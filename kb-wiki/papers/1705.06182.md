# Exploring the In-Medium Momentum Dependence of the Dynamical Quark Mass

> We review the two standard equations of states based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and the thermodynamic bag (tdBag) model for dense, cold quark matter from a perspective based on the Dyson-Schwinger (DS) formalism. A different, but technically not more complicated approximation reproduces the model of Munczek and Nemirovsky (MN) which accounts in a simplified way for chiral symmetry breaking and confinement as a dynamic process rooted in the momentum dependence of QCD model gap solutions. We review the mass gap solutions for the MN model in the chiral limit and sketch the behavior of mass gap solutions for finite bare quark masses at finite chemical potential.

# Exploring the In-Medium Momentum Dependence of the Dynamical Quark Mass

## Key claim
The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and thermodynamic bag (tdBag) model for dense, cold quark matter can be understood as simple truncations of the Dyson-Schwinger (DS) formalism, but neither has mass gap solutions with nontrivial momentum dependence; the Munczek-Nemirovsky (MN) model, by contrast, provides momentum-dependent gap solutions that can account for confinement and chiral symmetry breaking simultaneously, and its behavior at finite bare quark masses and finite chemical potential shows a richer structure than the chiral limit.

## Method
The authors derive gap equations from the Dyson-Schwinger formalism using a rainbow truncation (bare quark-gluon vertex) and compare two effective gluon propagator choices: a constant contact interaction (NJL model, regularized with a 3-momentum hard cutoff Λ) and a delta-function in momentum space (MN model, with interaction strength η). For the MN model, they solve the polynomial gap equation for the B-gap (scalar self-energy) at finite bare quark mass m and finite chemical potential μ, obtaining four complex solutions whose momentum dependence is examined at μ = p₄ = 0 and for varying p₄ and μ.

## Key numbers
- For the NJL model, the effective gluon propagator is parameterized as g²D^{ρσ}(p−q) = (1/m_G²) Θ(Λ² − q⃗²) δ^{ρσ}, with m_G a gluon mass scale defining the coupling strength.
- In the MN model, the confining solution (Nambu-Goldstone mode) for chiral quarks has a square-root branch point at Re(p̃²) < η²/4, where the scalar self-energy B = √(η² − 4p̃²); at μ ≠ 0, the branch point occurs at p₄ = 0, |p⃗|² = μ² + η²/4.
- Mass gap solutions are shown for bare masses of m = 0 (chiral), m = 3 MeV (up quark), m = 5 MeV (down quark), and m = 100 MeV (strange quark), all in units of [GeV/η].
- For m = 5 MeV, the non-zero mass solutions exhibit a sharp transition at high 3-momentum and finite energy that is not observed in the chiral limit or at zero energy.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the comparison of effective quark matter models within the Dyson-Schwinger framework, building on the NJL model of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) and the thermodynamic bag model of Farhi and Jaffe (1984). It extends the MN model of Munczek and Nemirovsky (1983) beyond the chiral limit studied by Klähn et al. (2009) and Blaschke et al. (1997), showing that finite bare quark masses smooth the discontinuous chiral transition and that the approximation of light quarks as massless becomes questionable for masses of order 0.1 GeV and above. The results motivate further study of the MN model as a confining, chirally symmetric equation of state for dense quark matter applicable to compact star astrophysics.
