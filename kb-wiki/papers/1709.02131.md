# Decay Constants of the Heavy-Light Mesons $D^{(*)}$ and $B^{(*)}$: Isospin Breaking

> QCD sum rules are analytic predictions of hadron observables from quantum chromodynamics. In the so-called local-duality limit of their Borel-transformed form, all nonperturbative contributions are encompassed by just a single quantity, an effective threshold beyond which, by assumption, all hadronic contributions are reliably described by perturbative QCD. Reconstructing its quark-mass dependence enables us to investigate the differences of the leptonic decay constants of heavy-light mesons effectuated by the tiny isospin-violating discrepancy of the masses of up and down quarks. By and large, our findings enjoy excellent agreement with the outcomes of earlier studies, the only exception being a lattice-QCD prediction for the $B$ mesons, where we find dramatic disagreement.

## Key claim

Using the local-duality limit of QCD sum rules, the authors predict isospin-breaking differences in heavy-light meson decay constants that agree well with earlier sum-rule and lattice-QCD results for D mesons, but find a dramatic disagreement with a lattice-QCD prediction for B mesons, where the lattice value is about four times larger.

## Method

The authors employ QCD sum rules in the local-duality limit, where all nonperturbative effects are subsumed into a single effective threshold \(s_{\rm eff}\). They reconstruct the quark-mass dependence of this threshold by matching to lattice-QCD results at the average light-quark mass \(m_{ud}\) and at the strange quark mass \(m_s\), using three parametrizations (constant, linear, and linear plus chiral logarithms). The decay constants are then computed from dispersion integrals of perturbative spectral densities up to \(O(\alpha_{\rm s}\,m_q^1)\) and \(O(\alpha_{\rm s}^2\,m_q^0)\), with input parameters defined in the modified minimal subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- For \(D^{\pm,0}\) mesons: \(f_{H_d} - f_{H_u} = 0.96 \pm 0.09\) MeV (local-duality limit), compared to \(0.94_{-0.12}^{+0.50}\) MeV from lattice QCD.
- For \(D^{*\pm,0}\) mesons: \(f_{H_d} - f_{H_u} = 1.18 \pm 0.35\) MeV (local-duality limit), compared to \(1.73 \pm 0.27\) MeV from the Borelized threshold method.
- For \(B^{0,\pm}\) mesons: \(f_{H_d} - f_{H_u} = 1.01 \pm 0.10\) MeV (local-duality limit), compared to \(3.8 \pm 1.0\) MeV from lattice QCD.
- For \(B^{*0,\pm}\) mesons: \(f_{H_d} - f_{H_u} = 0.89 \pm 0.30\) MeV (local-duality limit), compared to \(0.81 \pm 0.11\) MeV from the Borelized threshold method.
- Input parameters include: \(\alpha_{\rm s}(M_Z) = 0.1182 \pm 0.0012\); \(m_{ud}(2\ \text{GeV}) = (3.70 \pm 0.17)\) MeV; \((m_d - m_u)(2\ \text{GeV}) = (2.67 \pm 0.22)\) MeV; \(m_s(2\ \text{GeV}) = (93.9 \pm 1.1)\) MeV; \(m_c(m_c) = (1.275 \pm 0.025)\) GeV; \(m_b(m_b) = (4.247 \pm 0.034)\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum-rule studies of heavy-light meson decay constants (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2014-2017) by focusing on isospin breaking from the up-down quark mass difference. It agrees with their earlier Borelized threshold method (LMSIB, 2017) for most channels but finds a notable tension with lattice-QCD results for B mesons from the FLAG collaboration (2016) and specific lattice calculations (LQB, 2016). The results enable refined tests of isospin symmetry in heavy-meson decays and highlight the need for further lattice studies of B-meson decay constants.
