# On the shape of the $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry

> Using data from a recent reanalysis of neutron structure functions extracted from inclusive proton and deuteron deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), we re-examine the constraints on the shape of the $\bar d-\bar u$ asymmetry in the proton at large parton momentum fractions $x$. A global analysis of the proton-neutron structure function difference from BCDMS, NMC, SLAC and Jefferson Lab DIS measurements, and of Fermilab Drell-Yan lepton-pair production cross sections, suggests that existing data can be well described with $\bar d > \bar u$ for all values of $x$ currently accessible. We compare the shape of the fitted $\bar d-\bar u$ distributions with expectations from nonperturbative models based on chiral symmetry breaking, which can be tested by upcoming Drell-Yan data from the SeaQuest experiment at larger values of $x$.

## Key claim

Existing deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data can be well described with \(\bar d > \bar u\) for all accessible parton momentum fractions \(x\), and the apparent sign change in \(\bar d-\bar u\) at large \(x\) reported in some previous analyses is an artifact of leading-order approximations that neglect higher-order QCD corrections.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) using the CJ15 parton distribution functions, computing the isovector combination \(\Delta(x) = \frac12 [u_v(x)-d_v(x)] - \frac{3}{2x}[F_2^p(x)-F_2^n(x)]\) from proton-neutron structure function data from BCDMS, NMC, SLAC, and Jefferson Lab, together with Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan lepton-pair production cross sections. They compared the calculated \(\Delta(x)\) with the actual \(\bar d-\bar u\) difference from the CJ15 fit, and examined the effects of finite-\(Q^2\) power corrections, nuclear corrections in the deuteron, and different parametrization choices for the antiquark distributions.

## Key numbers

- The CJ15 fit to the E866 Drell-Yan absolute cross sections gives \(\chi^2\) per datum of \(284/250 \approx 1.14\) using a cut on dimuon masses of \(Q > 6\) GeV.
- The alternative fit CJ15-a, which parametrizes \(\bar d-\bar u\) instead of the ratio, gives \(\chi^2\) per datum of \(294/250 \approx 1.18\) for the same data.
- The CJ15-b fit, using a relaxed cut of \(Q > 4\) GeV (increasing data points by \(\sim 50\%\)), gives \(\chi^2\) per datum of \(593/375 \approx 1.58\).
- The E866 Drell-Yan data were taken at an average \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\) with center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} \approx 40\) GeV; the average \(Q\) ranges from 4.6 GeV at the highest \(x_F\) to 12.9 GeV at the lowest \(x_F\).
- The zero crossing of \(\Delta(x)\) occurs at \(x \approx 0.4\) in the CJ15 calculation at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The CJ15-b fit shows \(\bar d/\bar u\) dipping below unity at \(x \gtrsim 0.4\), while the standard CJ15 fit remains above unity for all \(x\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Peng et al. (2014), who used a leading-order approximation to extract \(\bar d-\bar u\) from the NMC \(F_2^p-F_2^n\) data and found a sign change at \(x \approx 0.3\). The present analysis shows that this sign change is an artifact of neglecting NLO QCD corrections, and that the CJ15 global fit (Accardi et al., 2016) with \(\bar d > \bar u\) at all \(x\) describes the same data well. The results contradict the interpretation of the E866 Drell-Yan data (Hawker et al., 1998; Towell et al., 2001) as requiring a sign change, and instead show that the measured cross section ratio is consistent with a positive \(\bar d-\bar u\) asymmetry. The upcoming SeaQuest experiment at Fermilab, extending to \(x \approx 0.45\), will test whether chiral symmetry breaking models (Salamu et al., 2015) can fully account for the asymmetry or whether additional mechanisms are needed.
