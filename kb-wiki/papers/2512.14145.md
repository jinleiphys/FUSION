# Impact of experimental mass of $^{70}$Kr on the $^{68}$Se waiting-point in $rp$-process

> The recent mass measurement of $^{70}$Kr using the $Bρ$-defined isochronous mass spectrometry yields a mass excess of $-41320(140)$ keV, indicating a 220-keV increase in binding energy compared to the AME2020 prediction. We utilize this experimental mass -- the last piece of information needed -- to model the potential waiting point $^{68}$Se in $rp$-process and quantitatively constrain the sequential $p$-capture reaction flow bypassing this waiting point. Our investigation shows that the more tightly bound nature of $^{70}$Kr enhances this reaction flow up to a factor of four. This enhancement reduces the effective half-life of $^{68}$Se. {A} one-zone X-ray burst model calculations reveal that the higher flow of $^{70}$Kr has distinct effects on the tail structure of light curve and the final SnSbTe abundances in the ashes due to a stronger $rp$-process heating.

## Key claim

The new direct mass measurement of $^{70}$Kr shows it is 220 keV more bound than the AME2020 prediction, and this more tightly bound nature enhances the sequential 2p-capture reaction flow bypassing the $^{68}$Se waiting point by up to a factor of four, reducing the effective half-life of $^{68}$Se. The abstract's claim that this is "the last piece of information needed" is qualified by the paper's acknowledgment that the 1$\sigma$ experimental uncertainty in the $^{70}$Kr mass still exerts a pronounced influence on the SeBr cycle and luminosity profile.

## Method

The authors used the newly measured mass excess of $^{70}$Kr ($-41320(140)$ keV) from $B\rho$-defined isochronous mass spectrometry ($B\rho$-IMS) to recalculate the $^{69}$Br($p,\gamma$)$^{70}$Kr reaction rate using the TALYS code. They performed a local small network calculation to determine the effective stellar half-life of $^{68}$Se as a function of temperature at a density of $10^6$ g/cm$^3$ and hydrogen abundance $Y_p=0.7$, then conducted one-zone X-ray burst model calculations with a network of 897 nuclei at constant ignition pressure $P_{\rm ign}=10^{23.03}$ dyn/cm$^2$ to study the impact on light curves and final abundances.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of $^{70}$Kr measured by $B\rho$-IMS: $-41320(140)$ keV, which is 220 keV more bound than the AME2020 value of $-41100\pm200$ keV.
- The reaction flow ratio of the 2p-capture on $^{68}$Se channel to the $^{68}$Se($\beta^+$) channel increases from about 3% (using AME2020 mass) to 12% (using the new experimental $^{70}$Kr mass).
- The effective half-life of $^{68}$Se is reduced by about 20% in the 1–2 GK temperature range when using the new $^{70}$Kr mass compared to the AME2020 mass.
- The X-ray burst luminosity increases by about 10% at $t\approx100$ s due to stronger $rp$-process heating from the higher flow through $^{70}$Kr.
- The impurity parameter $Q_{\rm imp}$ of the final ashes is $33.69^{+0.07}_{-0.04}$.

## Context

This work completes the modeling of the $^{68}$Se waiting point in the $rp$-process, following prior mass measurements of $^{68}$Se by Clark (2004) and Savory (2009), and predicted masses of $^{69}$Br and $^{70}$Kr via Coulomb displacement energy calculations by Schury (2007). The new direct mass measurement resolves the uncertainty that Del Santo (2014) identified as highly correlated with the impact of the $^{69}$Br proton separation energy on X-ray burst light curves. The results enable more accurate modeling of X-ray burst light curves and final ash compositions, particularly the SnSbTe cycle around $A\approx100$, and underscore the need for more precise future mass measurements of $^{70}$Kr to clarify the role of other reaction rates in this mass range.
