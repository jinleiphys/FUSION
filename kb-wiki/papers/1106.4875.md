# Upper limits on the observational effects of nuclear pasta in neutron stars

> The effects of the existence of exotic nuclear shapes at the bottom of the neutron star inner crust - nuclear `pasta' - on observational phenomena are estimated by comparing the limiting cases that those phases have a vanishing shear modulus and that they have the shear modulus of a crystalline solid . We estimate the effect on torsional crustal vibrations and on the maximum quadrupole ellipticity sustainable by the crust. The crust composition and transition densities are calculated consistently with the global properties, using a liquid drop model with a bulk nuclear equation of state (EoS) which allows a systematic variation of the nuclear symmetry energy. The symmetry energy J and its density dependence L at nuclear saturation density are the dominant nuclear inputs which determine the thickness of the crust, the range of densities at which pasta might appear, as well as global properties such as the radius and moment of inertia. We show the importance of calculating the global neutron star properties on the same footing as the crust EoS, and demonstrate that in the range of experimentally acceptable values of L, the pasta phase can alter the crust frequencies by up to a factor of three, exceeding the effects of superfluidity on the crust modes, and decrease the maximum quadrupole ellipticity sustainable by the crust by up to an order of magnitude. The signature of the pasta phases and the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the potential observables highlights the possibility of constraining the EoS of dense, neutron-rich matter and the properties of the pasta phases using astrophysical observations.

## Key claim
The paper estimates the maximal possible observational effects of nuclear pasta phases by comparing two extreme cases: pasta with the shear modulus of a crystalline solid versus pasta with a vanishing shear modulus (liquid). The authors demonstrate that within the experimentally acceptable range of the symmetry energy slope L (25-115 MeV), pasta phases can alter crustal torsional frequencies by up to a factor of three and decrease the maximum quadrupole ellipticity by up to an order of magnitude, but these are upper limits because the actual shear modulus of pasta is unknown and likely intermediate between these extremes.

## Method
The authors use a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) with a Skyrme-like interaction (MSL) to calculate crust composition and transition densities self-consistently with global neutron star properties via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. They vary the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy L over its experimentally constrained range (25-115 MeV) while holding J=32 MeV constant. For torsional oscillations, they use analytic approximations for fundamental frequencies and overtones (Samuelsson 2007), including superfluid entrainment effects via an effective mass parameter. For maximum quadrupole ellipticity, they use the stress-energy ratio estimate (Ushomirsky 2000, Haskell 2006) with a breaking strain of approximately 0.1 (Horowitz 2009).

## Key numbers
- The density range of pasta phases is largest at the smallest values of L, and disappears at L > 80 MeV
- The pasta layer thickness reaches about 10% of the crustal thickness and volume at intermediate L ≈ 70 MeV
- The pasta layers occupy the highest density region of the crust, with the fraction of crust mass in pasta approaching 50% (Lorenz 1993)
- For L < 70 MeV with solid pasta, maximum ellipticity is close to the canonical value of 10^-7 (σ_max/10^-2); with liquid pasta, maximum ellipticity is an order of magnitude lower, approximately 10^-8 (σ_max/10^-2)
- At higher L values, the maximum ellipticity falls by almost two orders of magnitude compared to the canonical value
- The fundamental torsional mode frequency (l=2, n=0) for a 1.4 M_sun star ranges from approximately 34 Hz at L=46 MeV (solid pasta, no superfluidity) to values a factor of 3 lower with liquid pasta
- The crust-core transition density ranges from approximately 0.05-0.07 fm^-3 (7×10^13 - 10^14 g cm^-3)
- The gravitational wave strain for a 300 Hz neutron star at 0.1 kpc is calculated using the formula h_0 ≈ 3.8×10^-25 (ε/10^-7)(σ_max/10^-2)(I/10^45 g cm^2)(ν/300 Hz)^2(d/0.1 kpc)^-1

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear pasta phases (Ravenhall 1983, Hashimoto 1984, Oyamatsu 1984, Watanabe 2001, Magierski 2002, Gogelein 2007) by estimating their observational consequences rather than just delineating their density range. It contrasts with Sotani (2011), which used a more sophisticated calculation of crustal frequencies but with inconsistent crust and core equations of state (using the Douchin-Haensel model with SLy4 EoS at L=46 MeV and treating the pasta transition density as a free parameter). The results enable future constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy from astrophysical observations of quasi-periodic oscillations in soft gamma-ray repeater giant flares and gravitational waves from rotating neutron stars, and motivate more microscopic treatments of pasta shear moduli and superfluid effects.
