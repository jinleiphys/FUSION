# Universality and scaling in the $N$-body sector of Efimov physics

> Universal behaviour has been found inside the window of Efimov physics for systems with $N=4,5,6$ particles. Efimov physics refers to the emergence of a number of three-body states in systems of identical bosons interacting {\it via} a short-range interaction becoming infinite at the verge of binding two particles. These Efimov states display a discrete scale invariance symmetry, with the scaling factor independent of the microscopic interaction. Their energies in the limit of zero-range interaction can be parametrized, as a function of the scattering length, by a universal function. We have found, using a particular form of finite-range scaling, that the same universal function can be used to parametrize the energies of $N\le6$ systems inside the Efimov-physics window. Moreover, we show that the same finite-scale analysis reconciles experimental measurements of three-body binding energies with the universal theory.

## Key claim

Using a finite-range scaling approach, the same universal function that parametrizes three-body Efimov energies can describe the ground- and excited-state energies of N-body systems with N ≤ 6 inside the Efimov-physics window, provided N-dependent scaling parameters and finite-range shift parameters are introduced.

## Method

The authors solved the N-body Schrödinger equation for identical bosons using the non-symmetrized hyperspherical harmonic expansion method with two different two-body potentials: an attractive Gaussian potential and a Pöschl-Teller potential. They computed ground-state and excited-state binding energies for N = 3, 4, 5, 6 as a function of the two-body scattering length, then applied a finite-range scaling procedure that replaces the scattering length a with a_B (derived from the two-body binding or virtual-state energy) and introduces N-dependent shift parameters Γ_n^N to the control variable κ_n^N a_B.

## Key numbers

- For the TBG potential, κ_0^3 r_0 = 4.88 × 10⁻¹, κ_0^4 r_0 = 1.18, κ_0^5 r_0 = 1.96, κ_0^6 r_0 = 2.77
- For the TBG potential, κ_1^3 r_0 = 2.12 × 10⁻², κ_1^4 r_0 = 5.11 × 10⁻¹, κ_1^5 r_0 = 1.24, κ_1^6 r_0 = 2.07
- The ratio κ_0^3/κ_1^3 = 23.0 from TBG potential (zero-range theory gives 22.7)
- The ratio κ_0^4/κ_0^3 = 2.42 from TBG potential (zero-range value 2.147 from Deltuva (2013))
- For ⁷Li experimental data, the shift parameter Γ_1³ = 4.95 × 10⁻² and κ³₁ = 1.61 × 10⁻⁴ a₀
- The three-body parameter a₋ ≈ -1.56/κ₊ from the universal function

## Context

This work extends the finite-range scaling approach previously introduced by Kievsky and Gattobigio (2013) for three-body systems to N-body systems up to N = 6. It builds on prior theoretical studies showing that each Efimov trimer has two attached four-body states (Platter 2004, von Stecher 2009, Deltuva 2013) and that similar structures exist for N = 5 and N = 6 (Gattobigio 2011, von Stecher 2011). The analysis reconciles experimental measurements of three-body binding energies in ⁷Li (Machtey 2012) with universal theory, and suggests a linear relation κ₀^N/κ₀³ = 1 + 1.147(N-3) in the scaling limit.
