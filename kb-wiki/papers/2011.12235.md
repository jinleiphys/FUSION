# Neutron skin $r_{\rm skin}^{48}$ determined from reaction cross section of proton+$^{48}$Ca scattering

> {\bf Background:} Using the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model with the densities calculated with GHFB+AMP, we determined $r_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.25$fm from the central values of $σ_{\rm R}$ of p+$^{208}$Pb scattering in $E_{\rm in}=40-81$MeV. The high-resolution $E1$ polarizability experiment ($E1$pE) yields $r_{\rm skin}^{48}(E1{\rm pE}) =0.14-0.20$fm. The data on $σ_{\rm R}$ are available as a function of $E_{\rm in}$ for $p$+$^{48}$Ca scattering. {\bf Aim:} Our aim is to determine $r_{\rm skin}^{48}$ from the central values of $σ_{\rm R}$ for $p$+$^{48}$Ca scattering by using the folding model. {\bf Results:} As for $^{48}$Ca, we determine $r_n(E1{\rm pE})=3.56$fm from the central value 0.17fm of $r_{\rm skin}^{48}(E1{\rm pE})$ and $r_p({\rm EXP})=3.385$fm of electron scattering, and evaluate $r_m(E1{\rm pE})=3.485$fm from the $r_n(E1{\rm pE})$ and the $r_p({\rm EXP})$ of electron scattering. The folding model with GHFB+AMP densities reproduces $σ_{\rm R}$ in $23 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 25.3$ MeV in one-$σ$ level, but slightly overestimates the central values of $σ_{\rm R}$ there. In $23 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 25.3$MeV, the small deviation allows us to scale the GHFB+AMP densities to the central values of $r_p({\rm EXP})$ and $r_n(E1{\rm pE})$. The $σ_{\rm R}(E1{\rm pE})$ obtained with the scaled densities almost reproduce the central values of $σ_{\rm R}$ when $E_{\rm in}=23-25.3$MeV, so that the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm GHFB+AMP})$ and the $σ_{\rm R}(E1{\rm pE})$ are in 1-$σ$ of $σ_{\rm R}$ there. In $E_{\rm in}=23-25.3$MeV, we determine the $r_{m}({\rm EXP})$ from the central values of $σ_{\rm R}$ and take the average for the $r_{m}({\rm EXP})$. The averaged value is $r_{m}({\rm EXP})=3.471$fm. Eventually, we obtain $r_{\rm skin}^{48}({\rm EXP})=0.146$fm from $r_{m}({\rm EXP})=3.471$fm and $r_p({\rm EXP})=3.385$fm.

## Key claim

The central value of the neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca is determined to be 0.146 fm from reaction cross section data of proton+⁴⁸Ca scattering, but this result is only valid in the narrow incident energy range of 23–25.3 MeV and relies on scaling the theoretical densities to match the proton radius from electron scattering and the neutron radius from the E1 polarizability experiment.

## Method

The authors used the chiral (Kyushu) g-matrix folding model with nuclear densities calculated from the Gogny-HFB plus angular momentum projection (GHFB+AMP) method. They compared the calculated reaction cross sections σ_R with experimental data for p+⁴⁸Ca scattering in the incident energy range 23–25.3 MeV, then scaled the GHFB+AMP densities to match the experimental proton radius r_p(EXP)=3.385 fm and the neutron radius r_n(E1pE)=3.56 fm derived from the E1 polarizability experiment. The matter radius r_m(EXP) was extracted from the central values of σ_R using the relation σ_R = c r_m², and the neutron skin was obtained as r_skin = r_m(EXP) - r_p(EXP).

## Key numbers

- r_skin⁴⁸(EXP) = 0.146 fm, determined from r_m(EXP) = 3.471 fm and r_p(EXP) = 3.385 fm
- r_n(E1pE) = 3.56 fm, derived from the central value 0.17 fm of r_skin⁴⁸(E1pE) and r_p(EXP) = 3.385 fm
- r_m(E1pE) = 3.485 fm, evaluated from r_n(E1pE) and r_p(EXP)
- r_m(EXP) = 3.471 fm, the averaged value from reaction cross section data in E_in = 23–25.3 MeV
- r_p(EXP) = 3.385 fm from electron scattering
- The E1 polarizability experiment yields r_skin⁴⁸(E1pE) = 0.14–0.20 fm, with central value 0.17 fm

## Context

This work extends the previous determination of the neutron skin for ²⁰⁸Pb (r_skin²⁰⁸ = 0.25 fm) by Tagami et al. (2020) using the same chiral g-matrix folding model, now applied to ⁴⁸Ca. The result is consistent with the E1 polarizability experiment by Birkhan et al. (2016) which gave r_skin⁴⁸ = 0.14–0.20 fm, and provides an independent determination from reaction cross section data by Carlson et al. (1994). This method enables extraction of neutron skin thickness from proton scattering data, complementing ongoing direct measurements such as the CREX experiment at Jefferson Lab.
