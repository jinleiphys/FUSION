# Exact Solutions to the Fermion Propagator Schwinger-Dyson Equation in Minkowski space with on-shell Renormalization for Quenched QED

> With the introduction of a spectral representation, the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) for the fermion propagator is formulated in Minkowski space in QED. After imposing the on-shell renormalization conditions, analytic solutions for the fermion propagator spectral functions are obtained in four dimensions with a renormalizable version of the Gauge Technique anzatz for the fermion-photon vertex in the quenched approximation in the Landau gauge. Despite the limitations of this model, having an explicit solution provides a guiding example of the fermion propagator with the correct analytic structure. The Padé approximation for the spectral functions is also investigated.

## Key claim

The authors obtain analytic solutions for the fermion propagator spectral functions in quenched QED in four-dimensional Minkowski space, but only after imposing a loop-renormalizable modification to the Gauge Technique anzatz for the fermion-photon vertex in the Landau gauge; the solutions are therefore model-dependent and do not represent a fully consistent truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equations.

## Method

The fermion propagator Schwinger-Dyson equation is formulated in Minkowski space using a Kallen-Lehmann spectral representation with two scalar spectral functions. The fermion-photon vertex is approximated by a renormalizable version of the Gauge Technique anzatz, which is linear in the spectral function and satisfies the Ward-Green-Takahashi identity. After imposing on-shell renormalization conditions, the spectral functions are solved analytically in the quenched approximation in the Landau gauge, and the Pade approximation for the spectral functions is also investigated.

## Key numbers

* The coupling parameter \(a = 3\alpha/(4\pi)\) appears in the spectral function solution from the original Gauge Technique (Eq. (20) of Delbourgo (1977)).
* The divergent parts of the fermion self-energy in the original Gauge Technique are \(\Sigma_1(p^2) = -\frac{3\alpha}{4\pi\epsilon}[1 - (1+\xi/3)/(Z_2\mathcal{F}(p^2))] + \mathcal{O}(\alpha\epsilon^0)\) and \(\Sigma_2(p^2) = -\frac{3\alpha}{4\pi\epsilon}(1+\xi/3)\mathcal{M}(p^2)/(Z_2\mathcal{F}(p^2)) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha\epsilon^0)\).
* The loop-renormalizability condition requires the divergent parts to take the form \(\Sigma_1(p^2) = \frac{\alpha\xi}{4\pi\epsilon}\frac{1}{Z_2\mathcal{F}(p^2)} + \overline{\Sigma}_1(p^2)\) and \(\Sigma_2(p^2) = -\frac{\alpha\xi}{4\pi\epsilon}\frac{\mathcal{M}(p^2)}{Z_2\mathcal{F}(p^2)} + \overline{\Sigma}_2(p^2)\).
* The minimal loop-renormalizable modification replaces the kernel \(K(p^2,s)\) with \(\overline{K}(p^2,s) = \frac{4}{3} + (1 - s/p^2)\ln[s/(s-p^2)]\).

## Context

This work extends the Gauge Technique approach of Delbourgo (1977) by imposing on-shell renormalization and a loop-renormalizability condition that the original Gauge Technique did not satisfy. The solutions provide a guiding example of a fermion propagator with correct analytic structure in Minkowski space, which can inform the construction of transverse vertex supplements for more consistent truncations of Schwinger-Dyson equations in QED and QCD. The results enable future studies of gauge covariance and the analytic structure of propagators beyond the quenched approximation.
