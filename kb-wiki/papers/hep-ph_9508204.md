# Low-energy expansion of the pion-nucleon Lagrangian

> The renormalized pion-nucleon Lagrangian is calculated to O(p^3) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. By suitably chosen transformations of the nucleon field, the Lagrangian is brought to a standard form.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the complete renormalized pion-nucleon Lagrangian to order O(p^3) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), but the specific form of the Lagrangian and its low-energy constants depend on the choice of the mesonic Lagrangian of O(p^4) and on the choice of pion fields, meaning the results are not unique.

## Method

Starting from the fully relativistic chiral Lagrangian for the pion-nucleon system, the authors perform a frame-dependent decomposition into velocity-dependent nucleon fields (N_v, H_v) characteristic of HBCHPT. They integrate out the heavy components H_v, expand the resulting non-local action in inverse powers of the nucleon mass, and then eliminate equation-of-motion terms through successive transformations of the nucleon field to bring the Lagrangian to a standard form where all one-particle-irreducible vertices can be read off directly.

## Key numbers

- The Lagrangian of O(p^2) contains 7 scale-independent low-energy constants (LECs) a_1 through a_7, with relations to the nucleon magnetic moments: a_6 = (mu_p - mu_n)/4 and a_7 = (mu_p + mu_n)/2.
- The Lagrangian of O(p^3) contains 24 renormalized, scale-dependent LECs b_i^r(mu), with their beta-functions listed in Table 1 as functions of g_A (the axial-vector coupling constant in the chiral limit).
- The beta-functions for three of the 24 LECs change when using the mesonic Lagrangian L_4^{GL} instead of L_4^{GSS}: beta_6^{GL} = -5(1-g_A^2)/24, beta_{19}^{GL} = g_A, beta_{20}^{GL} = -g_A/2.
- The spin-dependent nucleon Compton scattering amplitude in the forward direction at small photon energies is proportional to (kappa_p^2/4) for protons and (kappa_n^2/4) for neutrons, where kappa_p and kappa_n are the anomalous magnetic moments.

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) to O(p^3) for the pion-nucleon system, building on the relativistic Lagrangians of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and Krause (1990). It enables future calculations of one-nucleon processes at next-to-next-to-leading order, such as pion photoproduction and nucleon Compton scattering, and clarifies that the low-energy constants depend on the choice of the mesonic Lagrangian of O(p^4), a subtlety that must be accounted for in phenomenological applications.
