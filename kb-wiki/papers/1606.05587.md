# SU(2) low energy quark effective couplings in weak external magnetic field

> In this work corrections to the usual flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio coupling due to a weak external magnetic field are calculated by considering quark polarization in a (dressed) gluon exchange mechanism for quark interactions. The quark field is splitted into two components, one that condenses and another one that is a background field for interacting quarks, being the former integrated out. The resulting determinant is expanded for relatively large quark mass and small magnetic field, $(eB_0/{M^*}^2)<1$ by resolving magnetic field dependent low energy quark effective interactions. Besides the corrections for the NJL and vector NJL effective couplings, different $B_0-$dependent effective couplings that break isospin and chiral symmetry emerge.

## Key claim

Corrections to the usual flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) coupling due to a weak external magnetic field are calculated, but the expansion is valid only under the condition $(eB_0/{M^*}^2) < 1$, meaning the magnetic field is weak relative to the square of the quark effective mass, and the results are obtained in the large quark mass limit.

## Method

The author starts from the global color model (GCM) with a dressed gluon exchange mechanism, couples it minimally to a constant external magnetic field, and performs a Fierz transformation to obtain color-singlet quark interactions. The quark field is split into a background field (constituent quarks) and a sea quark field using the background field method, with auxiliary fields introduced to handle dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and produce the quark effective mass. The resulting determinant is expanded in powers of the magnetic field up to second order, keeping only the leading non-derivative terms, and the effective coupling constants are extracted in the long-wavelength (local) limit.

## Key numbers

- The expansion condition is $(eB_0/{M^*}^2) < 1$, where $M^*$ is the quark effective mass and $eB_0$ is the magnetic field strength.
- The Fierz transformation coefficient for SU(2) flavor is $\alpha = 8/9$.
- The isospin coefficients for the pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial channels are $c_1 = -4/9$, $c_2 = 4/9$, and $c_3 = 5/9$.
- The trace of $\hat{Q}^2 \sigma_i \sigma_j$ in flavor space yields $5/9 \delta_{ij} + (i/3) \epsilon_{ij3}$.
- The trace of $\hat{Q} \sigma_i \sigma_j$ in flavor space yields $1/3 \delta_{ij} + i \epsilon_{ij3}$.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop background field method previously applied to quark effective interactions in Refs. PRD2014 and piquark by incorporating a constant weak magnetic field. It builds on the known result that the NJL coupling may receive magnetic field contributions due to the QCD coupling constant dependence on $B_0$, as studied by G-B (2014), krein-etal, ayala-g2, and others. The results enable the investigation of magnetic field effects on chiral imbalance, vector condensation, and isospin-breaking phenomena in quark matter relevant to magnetars and relativistic heavy ion collisions.
