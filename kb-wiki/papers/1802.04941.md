# Axial Charge Fluctuation and Chiral Magnetic Effect from Stochastic Hydrodynamics

> The amount of axial charge produced in heavy ion collisions is one of the key quantities in understanding chiral magnetic effect. Current phenomenological studies assume large axial charge chemical potential $μ_5$ produced in Glasma phase and assume the conservation of axial charge throughout the evolution, which is valid in the long relaxation time limit. Based on the solution of stochastic hydrodynamics with phenomenological parameters, our study suggests that the situation of heavy ion collisions may be close to the opposite limit, in which axial charge fluctuation approaches thermodynamic limit. Using $μ_5$ set by the thermodynamic limit for chiral magnetic effect and a background from parity-even $v_1$, we obtain a reasonable description of the centrality dependence of charged particle correlation measured in experiment.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that in heavy ion collisions, axial charge may not be conserved as previously assumed, but instead approaches a thermodynamic limit due to rapid dissipation, with the relaxation time scale being comparable to or shorter than the QGP evolution time.

## Method

The authors solve stochastic hydrodynamic equations for axial charge evolution on top of a Bjorken flow background, incorporating both axial charge generation from topological fluctuations (Chern-Simons diffusion) and dissipation. They solve for the evolution of total axial charge and its rapidity dependence, using conformal scaling for transport coefficients and phenomenological parameters for the initial conditions, then apply the resulting thermodynamic-limit axial chemical potential to compute the chiral magnetic effect signal and compare with experimental charged particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- Relaxation time at initial time: τ_CS0 ≈ 2.3 fm (from Γ_0 = 30α_s⁴T_0⁴ with α_s = 0.3, T_0 = 350 MeV, χ_0 = 3T_0²)
- Initial proper time: τ_0 = 0.6 fm; freezeout time: τ_f = 7 fm
- Effective axial chemical potential at τ_0 ranges from 6.86 MeV (0-5% centrality) to 16.3 MeV (60-70% centrality)
- Induced electric chemical potential at freezeout eμ_e(τ_f) ranges from 0.76 MeV (0-5% centrality) to 4.33 MeV (60-70% centrality)
- Magnetic field parameters: eB_0 = 10m_π², τ_B = 3 fm
- Freezeout temperature: T_f ≈ 154 MeV (from T_0(τ_f/τ_0)^{-1/3})
- Diffusion constant D_0 ≈ 1.5T_0 from Einstein relation with σ_0 ≈ 1.5T_0
- Rapidity correlation width: Δη = √(6D_0/τ_0)

## Context

This work extends the stochastic hydrodynamic framework for axial charge developed by Iatrakis (2015) and applies it to heavy ion collisions, contradicting the common assumption of axial charge conservation used in event-by-event anomalous hydrodynamic simulations by Hirono (2014), Jiang (2016), and Shi (2017). The authors find that axial charge from topological fluctuations becomes comparable to initial Glasma-phase charge (from Fukushima 2010) at τ ≈ 5.0 fm for peripheral collisions and τ ≈ 9.8 fm for central collisions. The approach enables a quantitative treatment of CME signals using equilibrium axial charge fluctuations rather than relying on uncertain initial conditions, though the authors note caveats regarding omitted background sources and uncertainties.
