# Vorticity in the QGP liquid and hyperon polarization at the RHIC BES energies

> We calculate the polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in Au-Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan range $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7, \dots, 200$ GeV in a state-of-the-art 3+1 dimensional cascade + viscous hydro model vHLLE+UrQMD. We find that the polarization of $Λ$ in the out-of-plane direction decreases substantially with collision energy. We explore the connection between the polarization signal and thermal vorticity and discuss the feed-down and hadronic rescattering effects on the mean polarization of all produced $Λ$ hyperons.

# Vorticity in the QGP liquid and hyperon polarization at the RHIC BES energies

## Key claim
The mean polarization of Λ hyperons in Au-Au collisions decreases substantially with collision energy, reaching a maximal value of about 1.8% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, but the authors state that these values should serve only as an upper bound because hadronic rescattering after particlization will presumably randomize spin directions and thus decrease the estimated mean global polarization.

## Method
The authors used the vHLLE+UrQMD hybrid model, which combines an UrQMD cascade for the initial state and hadronic afterburner with a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic expansion. The initial fluidization hypersurface τ0 was set to the moment when the two nuclei have completely passed through each other, varying from 3.2 fm/c at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 0.4 fm/c at √sNN = 200 GeV. The mean spin vector of Λ hyperons was calculated using an extension of the Cooper-Frye formula incorporating thermal vorticity, defined as the antisymmetric part of the gradient of the four-temperature field.

## Key numbers
- Mean Λ polarization perpendicular to the reaction plane reaches a maximal value of about 1.8% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV for 20-50% central Au-Au collisions
- The duration of the pre-hydrodynamic stage changes from 3.2 fm/c at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 0.4 fm/c at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Feed-down contributions from Σ0 and Σ(1385) decrease the resulting mean polarization by only a few percent
- Extending the list of decays up to Σ(1670) results in about 15% suppression of the overall Λ polarization

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of Λ polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which had been performed for √sNN = 200 GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC using ideal hydrodynamics (Becattini 2013) and viscous hydrodynamics (Becattini 2015, Pang 2016), yielding polarization values from 0.2% to 9% depending on the model and transverse momentum range. The present study covers the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan range from 7.7 to 200 GeV and incorporates a realistic hybrid model tuned to reproduce basic hadron observables. The results enable comparison with preliminary STAR measurements showing several percent polarization at lower BES energies, and the authors identify that the steep decrease of polarization with collision energy arises from both higher initial thermal vorticity at lower energies and shorter hydrodynamic lifetimes that dilute the vorticity less.
