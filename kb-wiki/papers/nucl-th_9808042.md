# Scaling of particle production with number of participants in high-energy A+A collisions in the parton-cascade model

> In view of the recent WA98 data of pi0 spectra from central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS, we analyze the production of neutral pions for A+$ collisions across the periodic table at sqrt(s)=17 AGeV and 200 AGeV within the framework of the parton-cascade model for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The multiplicity of the pions (having pT > 0.5 GeV/c) in the central rapidity region, is seen to scale as \sim (N_part)^alpha, where N_part$ is the number of participating nucleons, which we have approximated as 2A for central collisions of identical nuclei. We argue that the deviation of α(\simeq 1.2) from unity may have its origin in the multiple scattering suffered by the partons. We also find that the constant of proportionality in the above scaling relation increases substantially in going from SPS to RHIC energies. This would imply that the (semi)hard partonic activity becomes a much cleaner signal above the soft particle production at the higher energy of RHIC, and thus much less dependent on the (lack of) understanding of the underlying soft physics background.

## Key claim

The observed scaling of neutral pion yield with number of participants as (N_part)^alpha with alpha approximately 1.3 in WA98 data is reproduced by the parton-cascade model with a slightly smaller alpha of approximately 1.2, and this deviation from linear scaling (alpha=1) originates from multiple parton scatterings rather than from thermal equilibration of the produced matter.

## Method

The authors used the event generator VNI, which implements the parton-cascade model for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, to simulate central A+A collisions across the periodic table at sqrt(s)=17 A GeV and 200 A GeV. The model evolves the multi-particle system from nuclear overlap through initial minijet production, parton cascading with multiple gluon emission and rescatterings, cluster hadronization, and final-state hadron cascading. The multiplicity of neutral pions with p_T > 0.5 GeV/c in the central rapidity region (-0.5 < y < 0.5) was extracted as a function of the number of participating nucleons (approximated as 2A for central collisions of identical nuclei).

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=17 A GeV (CERN SPS), the extracted scaling exponent alpha = 1.16 for the fit to simulated pi0 yields across systems from A=16 to A=197.
- At sqrt(s)=200 A GeV (BNL RHIC), the extracted scaling exponent alpha = 1.23 for the same range of nuclear systems.
- The WA98 experimental value for alpha is approximately 1.3 for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) approximately 17 A GeV.
- The simulated pi0 yield at RHIC energy shows an increase larger than a factor of 2 compared to the CERN SPS results at the same A.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of WA98 pi0 data (WA98 Collaboration, 1998) by comparing the observed scaling behavior to predictions of the parton-cascade model, in contrast to earlier analyses by Wang (1998) using perturbative QCD cross-sections and by Gyulassy and Levai (1998) using the HIJING event generator. The results demonstrate that multiple parton scatterings contribute significantly in heavy-ion collisions and that the scaling exponent alpha serves as a "collision-meter" for partonic multiple interactions, enabling future experimental discrimination between different dynamical scenarios at RHIC energies.
