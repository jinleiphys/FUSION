# The yield ratio of anti-nuclei and nuclei of relativistic nuclear collisions in the central rapidity region

> This article describes calculations of the yield ratio of anti-particles to the yield of particles ($\bar{p}/p$, $\bar{d}/d$,$\overline{^{3}\mathrm{He}}$/$^{3}$He) in proton-proton and nuclear-nuclear interactions using the self-similarity parameter in the central rapidity region. The used approach is based on the study of relativistic nuclear interactions in the four-velocity space. The results of the calculations are compared with the existing experimental data in a wide center-of-mass energy range ($SPS$, $RHIC$, $LHC$). Within this approach the inclusive spectra of pions and kaons and ratios of their yields in $pp$ collisions have been successfully described earlier.

# The yield ratio of anti-nuclei and nuclei of relativistic nuclear collisions in the central rapidity region

## Key claim
The self-similarity parameter approach, previously successful for pion and kaon spectra, can describe the yield ratios of anti-protons to protons, anti-deuterons to deuterons, and anti-helium-3 to helium-3 in the central rapidity region across a wide range of collision energies and nuclear sizes, but only if rapidity loss (baryon stopping) is taken into account for nucleus-nucleus collisions heavier than beryllium.

## Method
The authors apply the Baldin-Malakhov self-similarity parameter formalism, which expresses inclusive cross sections in terms of four-velocities and cumulative numbers, to compute the ratio of anti-baryon to baryon yields. For symmetric nuclei, the ratio reduces to an analytic expression involving the atomic number A, the particle mass, the rapidity Y, and a universal constant C2. The calculations are compared with experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC for pp, Be+Be, S+S, Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions, with a rapidity shift dY introduced to account for baryon stopping in heavier systems.

## Key numbers
- The universal constant C2 = 0.146 ± 0.003 describes pp and Be+Be data for the anti-proton to proton ratio.
- For nucleus-nucleus collisions from S+S to Pb+Pb, a different constant C2 = 0.088 ± 0.001 is required unless rapidity loss is included.
- After introducing a rapidity shift dY ≈ 0.5, all nucleus-nucleus data for anti-proton to proton ratios are described with the single constant C2 = 0.146.
- The constant C1 in the cross-section formula is 1.9 × 10^4 mb·GeV^{-2}·c^3·st^{-1}.
- The constant C2 in the cross-section formula is 0.125 ± 0.002.

## Context
This work extends the self-similarity approach previously applied by Baldin and Malakhov (1998) to inclusive cross sections of particles and nuclear fragments, and by the same authors to inclusive spectra of pions and kaons in pp collisions (LMZ 2021) and central AA collisions (ML 2018). The calculations are compared with experimental data from multiple collaborations including Aduszkiewicz, Rossi, Aamodt, Abelev, Adler, Acharya, Kornasa, Adare, Bearden, Aggarwal, and Adam. The introduction of rapidity loss follows the concept of baryon stopping discussed by Weber and Zhou. This approach enables a unified description of anti-nucleus to nucleus yield ratios across collision systems from pp to Pb+Pb at energies from SPS to LHC.
