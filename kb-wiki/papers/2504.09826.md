# Understanding baryon stopping at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider top energies

> The nucleon exhibits a rich internal structure governed by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), where its electric charge arises from valence quarks, while its spin and mass emerge from complex interactions among valence quarks, sea (anti-)quarks, and gluons. At the advent of QCD, an alternative hypothesis emerged suggesting, at high energies, the transport of a nucleon's baryon number could be traced by a non-perturbative configuration of gluon fields connecting its three valence quarks, forming a $Y$-shaped topology known as the gluon junction. Recent measurements by the STAR experiment are compatible with this scenario. In light of these measurements, this study aims to explore the mechanisms of baryon transport in high-energy nuclear collisions using the PYTHIA-8 framework, which incorporates a state-of-the-art hadronization model with advanced Color Flow (CF) and Color Reconnection (CR) mechanisms that mimic signatures of a baryon junction. Within this model setup, we investigate (i) the rapidity slope of the net-baryon distributions in photon-included processes ($γ$+p) and (ii) baryon over charge transport in the isobaric (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions. Our study highlights the importance of the CF and CR mechanisms in PYTHIA-8, which play a crucial role in baryon transport. The results show that the CF and CR schemes significantly affect the isobaric baryon-to-charge ratio, leading to different predictions for baryon stopping and underscoring the need to account for CF and CR effects in comparisons with experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that advanced Color Flow (CF) and Color Reconnection (CR) mechanisms in the PYTHIA-8 framework can produce baryon transport signatures consistent with the baryon-junction scenario, but the results depend critically on which specific CF and CR scheme is employed; the CF-2 CR-3 scheme, which explicitly includes non-planar color topologies (junctions), yields R(Isobar) values greater than unity across all centralities, while simpler schemes without junctions produce values below unity, indicating that the observed STAR data can be accommodated by models with or without explicit junction structures depending on the sophistication of the color reconnection treatment.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA-8 event generator with the Angantyr extension for heavy-ion collisions to simulate two types of processes: (i) photon-induced collisions on protons (gamma_e + p) with photon energies restricted to less than 2.0 GeV, and (ii) isobaric collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They implemented three combinations of Color Flow and Color Reconnection schemes: CF-1 CR-1 (leading color approximation with MPI-based CR), CF-1 CR-2 (leading color with gluon-move CR), and CF-2 CR-3 (full SU(3) color flow with advanced SU(3) CR including junction formation). For the gamma+p collisions, they extracted the exponential slope of the net-baryon rapidity distribution in the range y - Y_beam = -2.5 to -5.0. For the isobaric collisions, they computed the ratio R(Isobar) = (B x Delta Z/A) / Delta Q as a function of the number of participating nucleons (N_part).

## Key numbers

- The extracted rapidity slope alpha_B for gamma_e + p collisions: 2.253 +/- 0.05 for CF-1 CR-1, 2.231 +/- 0.06 for CF-1 CR-2, and 2.443 +/- 0.04 for CF-2 CR-3, over the rapidity range y - Y_beam = -2.5 to -5.0.
- The CF-1 CR-2 and CF-2 CR-3 schemes produce R(Isobar) values that are 25-30% above unity in central Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV.
- The photon energy in the gamma+p simulations was restricted to E_gamma < 2.0 GeV, with photons emitted by an electron of energy 5 GeV.
- The isobaric collisions were simulated for Ru-96 + Ru-96 and Zr-96 + Zr-96 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the recent STAR Collaboration measurements (STAR, 2024) of baryon stopping in isobar collisions and photon-induced processes at RHIC, which found results compatible with the baryon-junction picture originally proposed by Kharzeev (1996). The study provides PYTHIA-8 baseline calculations that can be compared with these STAR data, building on earlier theoretical work by Lewis (2022) and Frenklakh (2023) that proposed the R(Isobar) observable as a distinguishing test between valence-quark and junction-mediated baryon transport. The results show that even without explicit junction structures, sophisticated color reconnection (CR-2) can produce R(Isobar) > 1 in central collisions, complicating the interpretation of the STAR data and underscoring the need for more differential observables such as the rapidity dependence of net-proton density to further discriminate between models.
