# Coherent Pion Radiation From Nucleon Antinucleon Annihilation

> A unified picture of nucleon antinucleon annihilation into pions emerges from a classical description of the pion wave produced in annihilation and the subsequent quantization of that wave as a coherent state. When the constraints of energy-momentum and iso-spin conservation are imposed on the coherent state, the pion number distribution and charge ratios are found to be in excellent agreement with experiment.

## Key claim
A classical pion wave produced in nucleon-antinucleon annihilation, when quantized as a coherent state and constrained by energy-momentum and isospin conservation, reproduces the observed pion multiplicity distribution and charge ratios. The agreement requires the constraints; the unconstrained coherent state gives too broad a distribution (variance too large) and does not enforce the physical limits of 2 to 13 pions allowed by energy conservation.

## Method
The authors model the annihilation source as a spherically symmetric, localized pulse in space and time, with a p-wave form factor. They quantize the resulting classical pion field as a Glauber coherent state, then impose four-momentum conservation by projecting the coherent state onto states of fixed total four-momentum, and impose isospin conservation by integrating over a unit vector in isospin space with appropriate spherical harmonic weighting. The pion multiplicity distribution and charge ratios are computed from the projected state using a simple parameterization of the source (with range parameters α = γ = 2 in pion mass units).

## Key numbers
- Mean pion number in the unconstrained coherent state: \(\hat{N} = 6\) (chosen to roughly compensate for decay into resonant channels, versus the experimental ~5).
- Mean pion number after four-momentum constraint: \(\hat{N} = 6.4\); variance after constraint: \(\sigma = 0.88\) (experimental \(\sigma \sim 1\)).
- Energy released in annihilation at rest: 13.87 in units of the pion mass (\(\mu = 1\)).
- For the isospin I=1, I_z=0 state, the predicted charge ratio: \(\hat{N}_+ = \hat{N}_- = \hat{N}/5\), \(\hat{N}_0 = 3\hat{N}/5\).
- Using a recent estimate of I=0 vs I=1 population in pbar-p annihilation at rest, the predicted ratio \(\hat{N}_0/\hat{N}_+ = 1.53 \pm 0.15\), compared to the experimental value \(1.27 \pm 0.14\).

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model picture of rapid annihilation into a pion pulse, as studied by Sommerman and by Shao, Wambach, and Amado (early 1990s), by providing a full quantum coherent-state treatment with conservation laws. It contradicts the notion that a simple Poisson distribution describes annihilation; the constrained coherent state is necessary to match the narrow experimental multiplicity distribution. The approach enables unified treatment of all direct pion channels in pbar-p annihilation and suggests applications to other strongly interacting processes such as jets, Centauros, and high-energy heavy-ion collisions, where classical QCD descriptions may be quantized via coherent states (Greiner, Blaizot, and Rajagopal, early 1990s).
