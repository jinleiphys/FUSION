# Numerical evidence for fractional topological objects in SU(3) gauge theory

> The continued development of models that propose the existence of fractional topological objects in the Yang-Mills vacuum has called for a quantitative method to study the topological structure of $\mathrm{SU}(N)$ gauge theory. We present an original numerical algorithm that can identify distinct topological objects in the nontrivial ground-state fields and approximate the net charge contained within them. This analysis is performed for $\mathrm{SU(3)}$ colour at a range of temperatures crossing the deconfinement phase transition, allowing for an assessment of how the topological structure evolves with temperature. We find a promising consistency with the instanton-dyon model for the structure of the QCD vacuum at finite temperature. Several other quantities, such as object density and radial size, are also analysed to elicit a further understanding of the fundamental structure of ground-state gluon fields.

## Key claim

The authors present numerical evidence that the topological structure of SU(3) Yang-Mills ground-state fields at finite temperature is consistent with the instanton-dyon model, but the evidence is qualified as "promising consistency" rather than definitive proof, and the algorithm's charge assignments show a distribution of values rather than exact fractional charges due to overlapping objects and quantum fluctuations.

## Method

The authors developed an iterative algorithm that identifies topological objects by locating local extrema in the topological charge density, then grows each object outward by assigning neighboring lattice sites with the same sign and decreasing absolute charge density. A hypercube filter removes lattice-scale dislocations by discarding peaks that fail to assign all points within a 3^4 hypercube. The algorithm was applied to SU(3) pure gauge configurations at a range of temperatures crossing the deconfinement phase transition, after smoothing via gradient flow with an O(a^2)-improved action and a small Euler step size of epsilon = 0.005.

## Key numbers

- On five cooled 32^3 x 64 configurations at a = 0.1 fm, the algorithm identified objects with charges clustering near |Q| = 1, with action S/S0 and topological charge Q agreeing to at least one part in one thousand (e.g., configuration 011: S/S0 = 18.0004, Q = -18.0011; configuration 015: S/S0 = 8.0001, Q = 8.0002).
- The net topological charge on cooled configurations converged to within 1% of an integer in less than 75 cooling sweeps and remained stable thereafter.
- Gradient flow was performed with a step size of epsilon = 0.005, chosen to provide fine control over the smoothing level while minimizing distortion of topological features.

## Context

This work extends previous threshold-based clustering methods for analyzing topological structure (ThresholdAnalysisI through ThresholdAnalysisVII) by providing an unbiased algorithm that does not discard low-density regions or merge distinct objects connected above a threshold. The results are compared against the instanton-dyon model, which has successfully reproduced the second-order phase transition of SU(2) (SU2DyonConfinementI, SU2DyonConfinementII, SU2DyonConfinementIII) and first-order phase transition of SU(3) (SU3DyonConfinement) in numerical simulations. The algorithm enables direct quantitative study of how topological object charges, densities, and radial sizes evolve with temperature across the deconfinement transition.
