# Construction of a local barK N-pi Sigma-pi Lambda potential and composition of the Lambda(1405)

> A Kbar N-pi Sigma-pi Lambda coupled-channel potential is constructed on the basis of chiral SU(3) dynamics. Several matching conditions are introduced to formulate an equivalent local potential that reproduces the coupled-channel scattering amplitudes resulting from chiral SU(3)_L times SU(3)_R meson-baryon effective field theory. In contrast to a previously constructed effective single-channel Kbar N potential, the explicit treatment of the pi Sigma channel yields a natural description of the low-mass pole as part of the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The energy dependence of the potential can now be parametrized with a minimum of polynomial orders. To study the properties of the Lambda(1405) as a quantum-mechanical quasibound state, we derive the normalization condition of its wave function generated by the energy-dependent coupled-channel potential, using the Feshbach projection method. This framework provides an improved understanding of this system from the viewpoint of the compositeness of hadrons. With the properly normalized wave function, we demonstrate and confirm that the high-mass pole of the Lambda(1405) is dominated by the Kbar N component.

## Key claim

The authors construct a local coupled-channel $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$-$\pi\Lambda$ potential that reproduces the two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance, and they demonstrate that the high-mass pole is dominated by the $\bar{K}N$ component, while the explicit treatment of the $\pi\Sigma$ channel provides a natural description of the low-mass pole.

## Method

The authors start from chiral SU(3) dynamics with interaction kernels up to next-to-leading order, fitted to $K^-p$ scattering data, branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement. They construct an equivalent local potential in coordinate space by matching the Born approximation of the chiral amplitudes to the Schrödinger equation, using Gaussian spatial distributions with range parameters $b_i$ determined by minimizing deviations at channel thresholds. An energy-dependent adjustment term $\Delta V_{ij}$ is added to reproduce the original amplitudes over a wide energy range, and the energy dependence is parametrized as a polynomial in the nonrelativistic energy $E$. The Feshbach projection method is used to derive the normalization condition for the wave function of the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a quasibound state.

## Key numbers

- Range parameters for the $I=0$ potential: $b_{\pi\Sigma}^{I=0} = 0.80$ fm, $b_{\bar{K}N}^{I=0} = 0.43$ fm
- The $\chi^2$ fit to experimental data yields $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 0.96$
- Theoretical uncertainties of the subthreshold extrapolations of the amplitudes are estimated to be about 20%
- The integration region for the complex energy plane optimization is set as $1332\ \text{MeV} \leq \text{Re}\,z \leq 1450\ \text{MeV}$ and $-100\ \text{MeV} \leq \text{Im}\,z \leq 50\ \text{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the previous single-channel $\bar{K}N$ potential constructed by Miyahara and Hyodo (2015) to a full coupled-channel treatment including $\pi\Sigma$ and $\pi\Lambda$, which is necessary because the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold lies roughly 100 MeV below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. The framework builds on chiral SU(3) dynamics as developed by Kaiser, Oset, Oller, Lutz, and others in the 1990s and 2000s, and uses the SIDDHARTA constraint from Bazzi et al. (2011, 2012) to reduce theoretical uncertainties. The resulting potential enables more accurate few-body calculations of $\bar{K}$-nuclear systems and provides a quantum-mechanical framework for analyzing the compositeness of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance.
