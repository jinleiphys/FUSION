# Particle multiplicities in the central region of high-energy collisions from $k_T$-factorization with running coupling corrections

> Horowitz and Kovchegov have derived a $k_T$-factorization formula for particle production at small $x$ which includes running coupling corrections. We perform a first numerical analysis to confront the theory with data on the energy and centrality dependence of particle multiplicities at midrapidity in high-energy p+A (and A+A) collisions. Moreover, we point out a strikingly different dependence of the multiplicity per participant on $N_\text{part}$ in p+Pb vs.\ Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, and argue that the observed behavior follows rather naturally from the convolution of the gluon distributions of an asymmetric vs. symmetric projectile and target.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first numerical analysis of the Horowitz-Kovchegov $k_T$-factorization formula with running coupling corrections for particle production, and finds that the observed decrease of multiplicity per participant with increasing $N_\text{part}$ in p+Pb collisions at LHC energies follows naturally from the convolution of asymmetric gluon distributions, though the framework fails for p+A collisions at RHIC energy (200 GeV) where the multiplicity prediction is significantly too low.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the Horowitz-Kovchegov $k_T$-factorization formula (Eq. 1) which includes running coupling corrections via resummation of one-loop effects, using rcBK-evolved unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) from the MV model initial condition at $x_0=0.01$. They convolute the gluon production cross-section with a Monte-Carlo Glauber simulation to compute the dependence of charged particle multiplicity on the number of participants $N_\text{part}$ for p+Pb, Pb+Pb, and Xe+Xe collisions at various energies, fixing a single normalization factor to central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, a numerical fit to the multiplicity per participant curve for $15 \ge N_\text{part} \ge 5$ gives $\sim \ln^{1.25}(N_\text{part}) / N_\text{part}$.
- The ALICE collaboration found that the multiplicity per participant in NSD p+Pb collisions at 5 TeV (averaged over $N_\text{part}$) is 16% lower than in NSD p+p collisions interpolated to the same energy.
- For the two-component "soft+hard" model fit to peripheral Pb+Pb collisions ($N_\text{part} \le 34$) at 5 TeV, the hard fraction $f \approx 0.26 \pm 0.01$; for Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV with the same $N_\text{part}$ cut, $f \approx 0.34 \pm 0.01$.
- The collinear infrared cutoff $\Lambda_\text{coll}^2$ is set to match the fragmentation function scale $\mu^2_\text{FF} \simeq p_T^2$, and $\Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}} = 0.24$ GeV is used for the running coupling.
- The integration over gluon transverse momentum $k$ is performed from $k_\text{min} = 0.25$ GeV for the fixed-coupling comparison, and from $k_\text{min} = \Lambda_{\overline{\text{MS}}}$ for the running-coupling formula.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical derivation of Horowitz and Kovchegov (2010) for running coupling corrections to $k_T$-factorization, providing the first numerical confrontation with data on energy and centrality dependence of multiplicities. It contrasts with previous phenomenological applications such as Albacete et al. (2012) which used ad-hoc running coupling prescriptions, and with the KLN model of Kharzeev and Levin (2001, 2004). The results enable future studies of transverse energy per particle and bias effects from centrality selection, and highlight the need for improved understanding of the proton's unintegrated gluon distribution at $x \sim 0.01$ for RHIC energies.
