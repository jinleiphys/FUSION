# Electric polarizabilities of proton and neutron and the relativistic center-of-mass coordinate

> We argue that the relativistic correction $δ{\bf R}_{c.m.}$ to the center-of-mass vector can lead to the approximate equality of the proton and neutron electric polarizabilities in the quark model. The explicit form of $δ{\bf R}_{c.m.}$ depends only on the non-relativistic potential between quarks. In particular, this correction is the same for the potential generated by Lorentz-vector and -scalar interactions.

# Electric polarizabilities of proton and neutron and the relativistic center-of-mass coordinate

## Key claim
The relativistic correction to the center-of-mass vector in the constituent quark model can make the proton and neutron electric polarizabilities approximately equal, compensating the naive expectation that the proton's polarizability should be larger by the relativistic term Δα ≈ 3.8. The authors demonstrate this cancellation using a simplified harmonic-oscillator model with unequal quark masses, but state that the calculation serves only as an illustration and that a full treatment of all relativistic corrections remains a very complicated problem.

## Method
The authors derive the relativistic correction δR_c.m. to the center-of-mass vector for a system of spin-1/2 quarks interacting via Lorentz-vector and Lorentz-scalar potentials, showing that the spin-independent part depends only on the non-relativistic potential. They then compute the electric polarizabilities of proton and neutron in a constituent quark model using Jacobi coordinates, treating the difference between the masses of the identical quarks (m₁) and the third quark (m₃) as a proxy for relativistic effects. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the experimental charge radii of proton and neutron, and the resulting polarizability difference is evaluated to first order in the mass ratio x = m₁/m₃.

## Key numbers
- Experimental proton electric polarizability: ᾱ_p = 12.2 ± 0.3(stat) ± 0.4(syst) ± 0.3(model) (from Compton scattering, Olmos et al. 2001, Kossert et al. 2002)
- Experimental neutron electric polarizability from Coulomb scattering: ᾱ_n = 12.6 ± 1.5(stat) ± 2.0(syst) (Schmiedmayer 1991, with Schwinger-term correction of +0.62)
- Experimental neutron electric polarizability from quasi-free Compton scattering: ᾱ_n = 12.5 ± 1.8(stat) +1.1/−0.6(syst) ± 1.1(model) (Kossert et al. 2002)
- Experimental difference: ᾱ_p − ᾱ_n = −0.3 ± 1.8
- Naive non-relativistic prediction for the difference: Δα_p = 3.8 ± 0.1
- Proton charge radius squared: (r²_E)_p = 0.74 fm²
- Neutron charge radius squared: (r²_E)_n = −0.119 ± 0.003 fm²
- Best-fit quark mass ratio: x = m₁/m₃ = 0.8
- Oscillator parameter: m₁ω_ρ = 6 × 10⁴ MeV²
- Frequency ratio: ω_ρ/m₁ = 0.6
- Resulting ratio of corrections: (δα + Δα_p)/Δα_p = −0.1, indicating near cancellation

## Context
This work addresses a long-standing puzzle in the constituent quark model: the naive prediction that the proton electric polarizability should exceed the neutron's by Δα ≈ 3.8 (from the relativistic term proportional to charge and charge radius, derived by Petrunkin 1961, Baldin 1960, and Petrunkin 1964) contradicts the experimental finding that the two polarizabilities are equal within errors. The authors extend their earlier work (Lee, Milstein, Schumacher 2001) on relativistic center-of-mass corrections, showing that the correction to the electric dipole operator can compensate the naive difference. This removes the need for ad hoc mesonic current contributions in the constituent quark model. The result enables further refinement of quark-model calculations of nucleon polarizabilities and suggests that the relativistic center-of-mass coordinate must be treated consistently in any non-relativistic quark-model calculation of electromagnetic properties.
