# Electromagnetic response functions of few-nucleon systems

> Inclusive electromagnetic reactions in few-nucleon systems are studied basing on accurate three- and four-body calculations. The longitudinal 4He(e,e') response function obtained at q\le 600 MeV/c completely agrees with experiment. The exact 4He spectral function obtained in a semirealistic potential model is presented, and the accuracy of the quasielastic response calculated with its help is assessed, as well as the accuracy of some simpler approximations for the response. The photodisintegration cross section of 3He obtained with the realistic AV14 NN force plus UrbanaVIII NNN force agrees with experiment. It is shown that this cross section is very sensitive to underlying nuclear dynamics in the E_γ\simeq 70-100 MeV region. In particular, the NNN nuclear force clearly manifests itself in this region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that conventional non-relativistic few-body calculations with semirealistic NN potentials can reproduce the longitudinal \(^4\)He(e,e') response function at momentum transfers up to 600 MeV/c, but only because the right wing of the response probes low nucleon momenta (below about 0.7 fm\(^{-1}\)) where the wave function is insensitive to the tensor correlations missing in such potentials; the photodisintegration cross section of \(^3\)He is shown to be very sensitive to the choice of NN interaction and to the inclusion of a three-nucleon force in the \(E_\gamma \simeq 70\)–100 MeV region.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive electromagnetic response functions for \(^4\)He(e,e') and total photodisintegration cross sections for \(^3\)He and \(^3\)H using a method that avoids explicit construction of the complete set of final continuum states, requiring only bound-state-type calculations. For the \(^4\)He response, a non-relativistic four-body Hamiltonian with central NN potentials (the Trento potential) reproducing \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) phase shifts is used, with the conventional single-particle transition operator including Sachs form factors. For the trinucleon photodisintegration, realistic NN interactions (AV14 and TRSB) are employed, and the AV14 case is supplemented with the UrbanaVIII three-nucleon force; the unretarded E1 approximation is used for the transition operator.

## Key numbers

* Longitudinal \(^4\)He(e,e') response function calculated at \(q = 300\), 400, 500, and 600 MeV/c shows complete agreement with Bates and Saclay experimental data.
* At \(q = 600\) MeV/c, the quasielastic (PWIA) approximation overestimates the peak height of the longitudinal response by about 9%.
* For the \(^4\)He spectral function, the integrated values of the ground-state and inelastic contributions are \(n_{tp} = 0.89\) and \(n_{t^*p} = 0.11\), respectively.
* The \(^3\)H ground-state binding energy with the AV14 NN interaction alone is 7.71 MeV; adding the UrbanaVIII NNN force increases it to 8.52 MeV (compared to 7.67 and 8.46 MeV from Ref. chen).
* The \(^3\)H ground-state D-state probability with AV14 is 8.85%; the rms radius is 1.66 fm.
* At \(E_\gamma = 110\) MeV, the N–d relative momentum in the photodisintegration is about 1.9 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier rigorous calculations of trinucleon photodisintegration that used central separable or local potentials (e.g., Malfliet–Tjon) and approximate treatments of non-central forces (Vostrikov and Zhukov, 1991) by presenting the first calculations with realistic NN interactions (AV14, TRSB) and including a three-nucleon force (UrbanaVIII). The \(^4\)He spectral function calculation is the first exact one for \(A=4\) (previous spectral functions existed only for \(A=3\) and for nuclear matter). The results enable future experimental discrimination between NN interaction models and detection of NNN force contributions in the 70–100 MeV photon energy region, and suggest even greater sensitivity in exclusive experiments.
