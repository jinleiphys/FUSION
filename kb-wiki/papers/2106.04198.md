# From quarks and gluons to color superconductivity at supranuclear densities

> We study the emergence of color superconductivity in the theory of the strong interaction at supranuclear densities. To this end, we follow the renormalization group (RG) flow of dense strong-interaction matter with two massless quark flavors from the fundamental quark and gluon degrees of freedom at high energies down to the non-perturbative low-energy regime which is found to be governed by the dynamical formation of diquark states. With the strong coupling at the initial RG scale as the only input parameter, we compute the (chirally symmetric) scalar diquark condensate and analyze its scaling behavior over a wide range of the quark chemical potential. Approximations entering our computations are critically assessed. Since our approach naturally allows us to study the scale dependence of couplings, we also monitor the strength of couplings appearing in low-energy models of dense strong-interaction matter. The observed dependence of these couplings on the quark chemical potential may help to amend model studies in the future. Finally, we estimate the speed of sound of dense QCD matter. Our results indicate that the speed of sound exceeds the value of the noninteracting quark gas at high densities and even increases as the density is decreased, across a wide range, suggesting the existence of a maximum at supranuclear densities.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that starting from fundamental quark and gluon degrees of freedom at high energies, the renormalization group flow of dense QCD matter with two massless quark flavors leads to the dynamical formation of a chirally symmetric scalar diquark condensate at low energies, with the strong coupling at the initial RG scale as the only input parameter; however, the analysis relies on several approximations including the background field approximation and the restriction to a single diquark interaction channel, which are critically assessed but not fully quantified.

## Method

The authors employ the Wetterich functional renormalization group equation to study the RG flow of dense QCD matter from the perturbative high-momentum regime down to the non-perturbative low-energy regime. They use the dynamical hadronization technique to continuously map gluon-induced four-quark interactions onto a Yukawa-type quark-diquark interaction and diquark self-interactions, allowing them to follow the flow through the symmetry-breaking scale. The ansatz for the scale-dependent effective action includes quark fields, gluon fields, and complex-valued scalar diquark fields, with flow equations derived for the renormalized curvature of the effective potential, the four-diquark coupling, and the quark-diquark coupling.

## Key numbers

* The diquark channel becomes the most dominant interaction channel for quark chemical potentials μ ≳ 350 MeV, based on a Fierz-complete study of gluon-induced four-quark interaction channels.
* The initial RG scale Λ is chosen such that Λ ≫ μ to ensure the flow starts in the perturbative high-energy regime.
* The curvature εμ of the effective potential becomes zero at a finite scale kSB, signaling the onset of spontaneous U(1)V symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RG analysis of symmetry-breaking patterns in dense QCD (Braun, 2019) and the equation of state study (Leonhardt, 2019) by implementing dynamical hadronization to follow the flow below the symmetry-breaking scale. The approach is complementary to perturbative QCD studies at very high densities (Kurkela, 2009; Fraga, 2013) and chiral effective field theory calculations at low densities (Hebeler, 2013), aiming to bridge the intermediate density regime relevant for neutron-star physics. The results enable future first-principles computations of the equation of state and speed of sound of dense QCD matter with reduced systematic uncertainties compared to the authors' previous work.
