# Intersections of ultracold atomic polarons and nuclear clusters: How is a chart of nuclides modified in dilute neutron matter?

> Neutron star observations, as well as experiments on neutron-rich nuclei, used to motivate one to look at degenerate nuclear matter from its extreme, namely, pure neutron matter. As an important next step, impurities and clusters in dilute neutron matter have attracted special attention. In this paper, we review in-medium properties of these objects on the basis of the physics of polarons, which have been recently realized in ultracold atomic experiments. We discuss how such atomic and nuclear systems are related to each other in terms of polarons. In addition to the interdisciplinary understanding of in-medium nuclear clusters, it is shown that the quasiparticle energy of a single proton in neutron matter is associated with the symmetry energy, implying a novel route toward the nuclear equation of state from the neutron-rich side.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the polaron framework developed in ultracold atomic physics can be applied to nuclear impurity problems, specifically to describe alpha particles and protons immersed in dilute neutron matter, but the applicability depends on the assumption that the relevant interactions can be characterized by parameters such as the Fermi momentum of the medium and the scattering length between impurity and medium particles, largely independent of microscopic details.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical and experimental status of Fermi and Bose polarons in ultracold atomic gases, including S-wave and P-wave interactions, and then map these concepts onto nuclear systems by treating protons and alpha particles as impurities in a neutron medium. They use the polaron quasiparticle properties (energy, effective mass, residue) derived from the impurity self-energy and spectral function, and connect these to thermodynamic quantities such as the symmetry energy and the nuclear equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ (context: reference density for nuclear matter)
- The polaron-to-molecule transition in Fermi polarons is expected at $k_{\rm F} a_{\rm im} \simeq 1$, where $k_{\rm F}$ is the Fermi momentum of the medium (context: attractive Fermi polaron in three dimensions)
- The ground-state energy density expansion for attractive Fermi polarons: $E = E_{\rm FG} \left[1 + \frac{5}{3}\frac{E_{\rm P}}{E_{\rm F}}\left(\frac{\rho_{\rm i}}{\rho_{\rm m}}\right) + \frac{M}{M^*}\left(\frac{\rho_{\rm i}}{\rho_{\rm m}}\right)^{\frac{5}{3}} + \cdots\right]$, where $E_{\rm FG} = \frac{3}{5}\rho_{\rm m}E_{\rm F}$ (context: three-dimensional Fermi gas with fermionic impurities)
- The collective mode frequency for attractive Fermi polarons: $\omega^* = \omega \sqrt{\left(1 - \frac{E_{\rm P}}{E_{\rm F}}\right) \frac{M}{M^*}}$ (context: dipole oscillation in a trapped unitary Fermi gas)

## Context

This review extends the polaron concept from ultracold atomic experiments (e.g., Chevy (2007), Schirotzek et al. (2009), Kohstall et al. (2012), Koschorreck et al. (2012)) to nuclear systems, specifically addressing impurities in neutron matter relevant to neutron stars and neutron-rich nuclei. The work connects to prior studies of protons in neutron matter (Kutschera and Wójcik (1994), PhysRevC.47.1077, PhysRevC.91.024327) and to the nuclear symmetry energy problem. It enables a novel interdisciplinary route to constrain the nuclear equation of state from the neutron-rich side using polaron physics, and opens the possibility of studying in-medium clustering of alpha particles and protons in dilute neutron matter.
