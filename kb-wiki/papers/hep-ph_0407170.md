# Improving the ultraviolet behavior in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We introduce a new formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory which improves the ultraviolet behavior of propagators and can be interpreted as a smooth cutoff regularization scheme. It is equivalent to the standard approach, preserves all symmetries and therefore satisfies the Ward identities. Our formulation is equally well defined in the vacuum, one- and few-nucleon sectors of the theory. The equations (Bethe-Salpeter, Lippmann-Schwinger, etc.) for the scattering amplitudes of the few-nucleon sector are free of divergences in the new approach. Unlike the usual cutoff regularization, our 'cutoffs' are parameters of the Lagrangian and do not have to be removed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory that adds symmetry-preserving higher-derivative terms to the Lagrangian, which modifies the ultraviolet behavior of propagators and acts as a smooth cutoff regularization scheme; crucially, unlike standard cutoff regularization, these "cutoffs" are parameters of the Lagrangian that do not need to be removed, and the scheme preserves all symmetries including Ward identities.

## Method

The authors add higher-derivative terms to the standard BChPT Lagrangian that modify the pion and nucleon propagators by including factors of the form Λ²/(Λ² − p²) for each regulator parameter Λ. They then apply dimensional regularization to the remaining primitively divergent diagrams, subtract the 1/(n−4) poles via the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, and expand in powers of Λ to absorb Λ-dependent terms into redefinitions of the standard Lagrangian parameters. The approach is demonstrated by calculating the nucleon self-energy to O(q³), verifying the electromagnetic Ward identity at one loop, and applying the method to the NN scattering problem with contact interactions and one-pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass to O(q³): m_N = m − 4c₁M² − (3g_A²)/(32πF²) M³ + O(1/Λ⁴), where m is the chiral-limit nucleon mass, M is the pion mass, g_A is the axial-vector coupling constant, and F is the pion-decay constant in the chiral limit (F_π = 92.4 MeV)
- The renormalized on-mass-shell self-energy at O(q³): Σ^R|_{p/=m_N} = −(3g_A² M³)/(32πF²) + O(1/Λ⁴)
- For the HBChPT calculation with N_π=2 and Λ_π1=Λ_π2=Λ, the loop self-energy contains terms proportional to Λ³ and ωΛ² that violate power counting: Σ^(3)_loop(p) = −(3g_A²)/(64π²F²) [πΛ³ + 3ωΛ²] + O(Λ)

## Context

This work extends the higher-derivative regularization idea of Slavnov (1971) from the nonlinear sigma model to baryon chiral perturbation theory, providing a symmetry-preserving alternative to the cutoff regularization schemes used in few-nucleon calculations by Lepage (1997), Gegelia (1998), and Epelbaum (2004). The approach addresses the problem that standard cutoff regularization breaks symmetries, while dimensional regularization is difficult to apply to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with non-renormalizable potentials from BChPT. The new formulation enables consistent, symmetry-preserving calculations in the few-nucleon sector where previous methods required special care to maintain symmetries, and it is equally applicable to vacuum, one-nucleon, and few-nucleon sectors.
