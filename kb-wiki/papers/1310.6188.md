# Evidence for shape coexistence in $^{98}$Mo

> A $γγ$ angular correlation experiment has been performed to investigate the low-energy states of the nucleus $^{98}$Mo. The new data, including spin assignments, multipole mixing ratios and lifetimes reveal evidence for shape coexistence and mixing in $^{98}$Mo, arising from a proton intruder configuration. This result is reproduced by a theoretical calculation within the proton-neutron interacting boson model with configuration mixing, based on microscopic energy density functional theory. The microscopic calculation indicates the importance of the proton particle-hole excitation across the Z=40 sub-shell closure and the subsequent mixing between spherical vibrational and the $γ$-soft equilibrium shapes in $^{98}$Mo.

## Key claim

The paper presents experimental evidence for shape coexistence and configuration mixing in $^{98}$Mo, arising from a proton intruder configuration, with the caveat that the interpretation relies on a theoretical calculation within the proton-neutron interacting boson model with configuration mixing based on microscopic energy density functional theory, which reproduces the data but involves no adjustment to experimental results.

## Method

A $\gamma\gamma$ angular correlation experiment was performed using the $^{96}$Zr($\alpha$,2n)$^{98}$Mo reaction with a 16 MeV $\alpha$ beam from the ESTU tandem accelerator at Yale University, detecting $\gamma$ transitions with 10 Compton-suppressed HPGe Clover detectors of the YRAST Ball array. Spins, multipole mixing ratios, and lifetimes (via the Doppler-Shift Attenuation Method) were extracted from 1.2$\times 10^9$ coincidence events. The experimental results were compared to a theoretical calculation using the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) with configuration mixing, where the Hamiltonian parameters were determined solely by mapping the microscopic energy surface from a Skyrme energy density functional (SLy6) calculation onto the IBM-2 boson condensate.

## Key numbers

- Multipole mixing ratio $\delta_{1419} = 0.33 \pm 0.11$ for the $2^+_4 \xrightarrow{1419} 2^+_1$ transition, previously unknown.
- Multipole mixing ratio $\delta_{644} = +1.67(25)$ for the $2^+_2 \xrightarrow{644} 2^+_{1}$ transition, refuting the most recent value from Coulomb excitation.
- Effective lifetime $\tau = 0.30 (7)$ ps for the $2^+_4$ state, measured via DSAM.
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment $Q(2^+_1) = -0.245$ $e$b predicted by the IBM-2 calculation, consistent with a previous experimental value of $Q(2^+_1) = -0.25(9)$ $e$b but differing from a more recent value of $Q(2^+_1) = -0.05(2)$ $e$b.
- Mixing matrix element $\langle H_{mix}\rangle = 385$ keV between unperturbed $0^+_1$ states of normal and intruder configurations, consistent with a previous schematic two-level mixing calculation of 326 keV.
- The $0^+_{1,2}$ states after mixing contain 55.3% and 46.9% intruder configuration, respectively, indicating nearly equal mixing.
- $B$(E2; $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_2$) = 280 (40) W.u. (experimental) compared to 256 W.u. (theoretical), a strong transition testing configuration mixing.
- $B$(E2; $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1$) = 21.4 $^{+11}_{-10}$ W.u. (experimental) compared to 27 W.u. (theoretical).

## Context

This work extends studies of shape coexistence in the $A\sim 100$ mass region, particularly in Mo isotopes, where previous work on $^{94}$Zr (Chakraborty et al., 2013) and $^{92}$Zr (V. Werner et al., 2002) demonstrated similar phenomena. The results confirm and refine earlier suggestions of shape coexistence in $^{98}$Mo from Coulomb excitation (Zielinska et al., 2002) and investigations of $1^+$ states (Rusev et al., 2005), while resolving conflicting multipole mixing ratios in the literature. The theoretical approach, combining energy density functional theory with the IBM-2, provides a fully microscopic description that can be applied to other heavy exotic nuclei in the future.
