# Deuteron distribution in nuclei and the Levinger's factor

> We compute the distribution of quasideuterons in doubly closed shell nuclei. The ground states of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are described in $ls$ coupling using a realistic hamiltonian including the Argonne $v_{8}^\prime$ and the Urbana IX models of two-- and three--nucleon potentials, respectively. The nuclear wave function contains central and tensor correlations, and correlated basis functions theory is used to evaluate the distribution of neutron-proton pairs, having the deuteron quantum numbers, as a function of their total momentum. By computing the number of deuteron--like pairs we are able to extract the Levinger's factor and compare to both the available experimental data and the predictions of the local density approximation, based on nuclear matter estimates. The agreement with the experiments is excellent, whereas the local density approximation is shown to sizably overestimate the Levinger's factor in the region of the medium nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper computes the Levinger factor for \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca from first principles using correlated basis functions, finding excellent agreement with experimental photoreaction data, while showing that the local density approximation (LDA) sizably overestimates the Levinger factor for medium-mass nuclei.

## Method

The ground states of \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca were described in \(ls\) coupling using a realistic Hamiltonian with the Argonne \(v_{8}^\prime\) two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential. The nuclear wave function included central and tensor correlations, and correlated basis functions (CBF) theory was used to evaluate the momentum distribution of neutron-proton pairs having deuteron quantum numbers, from which the total number of quasideuteron pairs and the Levinger factor were extracted.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}\)O, the computed Levinger factor is \(\mathrm{L}_{f_6}(^{16}\mathrm{O}) = 4.36\), compared to a variational Monte Carlo estimate of \(\mathrm{L}_{\text{VMC}}(^{16}\mathrm{O}) = 4.70\) from Forest (1996).
- For \(^{40}\)Ca, the computed Levinger factor is \(\mathrm{L}_{f_6}(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}) = 5.48\).
- For nuclear matter at equilibrium density, the computed Levinger factor is \(\mathrm{L}_{f_6}(NM) = 10.83\), compared to the empirical estimate \(\mathrm{L}_\text{expt}(A=\infty) = 9.26\).
- The total number of quasideuteron pairs per nucleon is \(\mathcal{P}_D/A = 1.090\) for \(^{16}\)O, \(1.370\) for \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(2.707\) for nuclear matter (with the AU8\(^\prime\) interaction).
- The binding energy per nucleon obtained with the wave function used is \(B/A = 5.41\) MeV for \(^{16}\)O and \(6.64\) MeV for \(^{40}\)Ca.
- The root mean square radii are \(R = 2.67\) fm for \(^{16}\)O and \(R = 3.39\) fm for \(^{40}\)Ca.

## Context

This work extends the CBF approach previously applied to nuclear matter by Benhar et al. (2002) to finite doubly closed shell nuclei, providing an *ab initio* calculation of the Levinger factor. The results are compared to experimental photoreaction data from Ahrens (1975) and to the phenomenological Levinger and Laget models, as well as to variational Monte Carlo results for \(^{16}\)O by Forest (1996). The paper shows that LDA, based on nuclear matter estimates, overestimates the Levinger factor for medium nuclei, and that the Jastrow model (retaining only scalar correlations) underestimates it.
