# Odd-even mass differences of well and rigidly deformed nuclei in the rare earth region: A test of a newly proposed fit of average pairing matrix elements

> We discuss a test of a recently proposed approach to determine average pairing matrix elements within a given interval of single-particle states (sp) around the Fermi level $λ$ as obtained in the so-called uniform gap method (UGM). It takes stock of the crucial role played by the averaged sp level density $\tildeρ(e)$. These matrix elements are deduced within the UGM approach, from microscopically calculated $\tildeρ(e)$ and gaps obtained from analytical formulae of a semi-classical nature. Two effects generally ignored in similar fits have been taken care of. They are: (a) the correction for a systematic bias in choosing to fit pairing gaps corresponding to equilibrium deformation solutions as discussed by Möller and Nix [Nucl. Phys. A 476, 1 (1992)] and (b) the correction for a systematic spurious enhancement of $\tildeρ(e)$ for protons in the vicinity of $λ$, because of the local Slater approximation used for the treatment of the Coulomb exchange terms in most calculations (see e.g. [Phys. Rev C 84, 014310 (2011)]). This approach has been deemed to be very efficient upon performing Hartree-Fock + BCS (with seniority force and self-consistent blocking when dealing with odd nuclei) calculations of a large sample of well and rigidly deformed even-even rare-earth nuclei. The reproduction of their experimental moments of inertia has been found to be at least of the same quality as what has been obtained in a direct fit of these data [Phys. Rev C 99, 064306 (2019)]. We extend here the test of our approach to the reproduction, in the same region, of three-point odd-even mass differences centered on odd-$N$ or odd-$Z$ nuclei. The agreement with the data is again roughly of the same quality as what has been obtained in a direct fit, as performed in [Phys. Rev C 99, 064306 (2019)].

## Key claim

The newly proposed approach for determining average pairing matrix elements via the uniform gap method (UGM), which incorporates corrections for the Möller-Nix bias and the Slater approximation overestimation of proton level density, reproduces three-point odd-even mass differences for well and rigidly deformed rare-earth nuclei with roughly the same quality as a direct fit of these data performed in Phys. Rev C 99, 064306 (2019).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations (with seniority force and self-consistent blocking for odd nuclei) using the SIII Skyrme parametrization, with average pairing matrix elements determined from microscopically calculated Strutinsky-averaged single-particle level densities and semi-classical analytical gap formulas. The method incorporates two corrections: (a) the Möller-Nix (1992) correction for bias from selecting equilibrium deformation solutions, and (b) a correction factor R_p for the spurious enhancement of proton level density near the Fermi energy caused by the local Slater approximation for Coulomb exchange. Odd-even mass differences were extracted using a three-point formula centered on odd-N or odd-Z nuclei, and the calculated values were compared to experimental data for 13 odd-proton and 22 odd-neutron nuclei in the rare-earth region.

## Key numbers

* For 13 odd-proton nuclei, calculated three-point odd-even mass differences range from 438 keV (for 167Tb) to 777 keV (for 159Eu), compared to experimental values ranging from 466 keV to 602 keV.
* For 22 even-even nuclei, the ratio R_42 of first 4+ to 2+ excitation energies satisfies R_42 >= 3.29 for all nuclei, confirming they are well and rigidly deformed.
* The averaging width for Strutinsky level density is taken as gamma = 1.2 hbar-omega, where hbar-omega = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV.
* The energy interval for BCS active single-particle states is Omega = 6 MeV.
* The Möller-Nix average gap parameter r = 4.8 MeV is used for neutrons, while for protons a correction factor R_p = 0.0181 * E_cond^p + 0.781 is applied, where E_cond^p is the proton pairing condensation energy in MeV.

## Context

This work extends the test of the UGM-based approach previously validated on moments of inertia (Hao et al., 2020) to odd-even mass differences, comparing results against the direct fitting approach of Hafiza et al. (2019). The method addresses deficiencies in earlier fitting approaches that suffer from accidental local deficiencies of single-particle level distributions near the Fermi energy, and it corrects for the systematic bias in equilibrium-deformation selections noted by Möller and Nix (1992) and the Slater approximation overestimation of proton level density (Phys. Rev C 84, 014310, 2011). The approach enables parameter-free predictions of pairing properties for well-deformed nuclei without requiring direct fits to experimental data.
