# Possible hadronic molecules composed of the doubly charmed baryon and nucleon

> We perform a systematical investigation of the possible deuteron-like bound states with configuration $Ξ_{cc}N (\bar{N})$, where $N(\bar{N})$ denotes the nucleon (anti-nucleon), in the framework of the one-boson-exchange-potential model. In the spin-triplet sector we take into account both the ${}^3S_1$ and ${}^3D_1$ channels due to non-vanishing tensor force. There exist several candidates of the loosely bound molecular states for the $Ξ_{cc}N$ and $Ξ_{cc}\bar{N}$ systems, which lie below the threshold of $Λ_cΛ_c$ or $Λ_c{\barΛ}_c$. We also investigate the possible loosely bound states with configurations $Λ_cN(\bar{N})$ and $Σ_cN(\bar{N})$. These molecular candidates may be searched for at Belle II and LHC in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several loosely bound molecular states exist for the $\Xi_{cc}N$ and $\Xi_{cc}\bar{N}$ systems, but only for specific isospin and spin channels, and only when the cutoff parameter falls within a certain range; the existence of these states depends on whether the short-range delta interaction is included or removed.

## Method

The authors used the one-boson-exchange-potential (OBEP) model to calculate the interaction potentials for systems composed of a doubly charmed baryon ($\Xi_{cc}$) and a nucleon or anti-nucleon, as well as for systems with singly charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c$, $\Sigma_c$) and nucleons. They solved the Schrodinger equation for both spin-singlet (${}^1S_0$) and spin-triplet (${}^3S_1$ and ${}^3D_1$ coupled channels) states, including tensor forces and S-D mixing, and varied the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ from 0.80 to 2.50 GeV to identify loosely bound states with binding energies between 0.5 and 15 MeV and root-mean-square radii between 0.8 and 5 fm.

## Key numbers

- For the spin-singlet $[\Xi_{cc}N]^{(0,0)}$ system, a loosely bound state was found with binding energy $0.49 < \text{B.E.} < 10.39$ MeV for cutoff $2.20 < \Lambda < 2.80$ GeV.
- For the spin-singlet $[\Xi_{cc}\bar{N}]^{(0,0)}$ system, the binding energy was $4.98 < \text{B.E.} < 34.86$ MeV for cutoff $0.85 < \Lambda < 1.00$ GeV, with root-mean-square radius $0.97 < r_{rms} < 1.90$ fm.
- For the spin-singlet $[\Xi_{cc}\bar{N}]^{(0,1)}$ system, the binding energy was $1.98 < \text{B.E.} < 44.15$ MeV for cutoff $1.05 < \Lambda < 1.20$ GeV.
- For the spin-triplet $[\Xi_{cc}\bar{N}]^{(1,0)}$ system, the binding energy was $0.94 < \text{B.E.} < 64.58$ MeV for cutoff $0.95 < \Lambda < 1.10$ GeV, with D-wave contribution around $2\% - 5\%$.
- For the spin-triplet $[\Xi_{cc}\bar{N}]^{(1,1)}$ system, the binding energy was $0.31 < \text{B.E.} < 38.99$ MeV for cutoff $0.95 < \Lambda < 1.10$ GeV, with D-wave contribution around $0.2\% - 0.5\%$.
- The mass of the doubly charmed baryon $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ was taken as $3621.40 \pm 0.72 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.27 (\text{syst}) \pm 0.14 (\Lambda_c^+)$ MeV/$c^2$ from LHCb (Aaij 2017).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic molecules formed by heavy baryons, such as the investigation of $\Xi_{cc}\Xi_{cc}$ systems by Meng (2017) and earlier work on $\Xi_{cc}N$ systems by Julia-Diaz (2004). The new LHCb mass measurement of $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$ (Aaij 2017) places the $\Xi_{cc}N$ system about 13 MeV below the $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$ threshold, making molecular states possible without decay into that channel. The results suggest that these molecular candidates could be searched for at Belle II and LHC, and the formalism can be applied to other heavy baryon-nucleon systems.
