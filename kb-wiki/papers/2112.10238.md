# When cold, dense quarks in 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions are not a Fermi liquid

> We analyze the behavior of quarks coupled to a $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions. In the limit of strong coupling, the model reduces to a Wess-Zumino-Novikov-Witten (WZNW) model. At nonzero density, excitations near the Fermi surface form a non-Fermi liquid. With $N_f$ flavors, the finite density of quarks reduce to a free $U(1)$ field, which governs fluctuations in baryon number, together with a WZNW $SU(N_f)$ nonlinear sigma model at level $N_c$, from the pion/kaon modes. We compute the singularity in the charge susceptibility at the Fermi surface and the attendant power law correlations. We suggest that this is relevant to the quarkyonic regime of cold, dense QCD in 3+1 dimensions, in the limit that the Fermi surface is covered by many small patches, and the theory is effectively one dimensional. In this regime the dominant excitations near the Fermi surface are not baryons, but gapless bosonic modes.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in 1+1 dimensional QCD at nonzero density, excitations near the Fermi surface form a non-Fermi liquid (a "strange metal") rather than a Fermi liquid, and suggest this behavior is relevant to the quarkyonic regime of cold, dense QCD in 3+1 dimensions in the limit where the Fermi surface is covered by many small patches and the theory becomes effectively one dimensional.

## Method

The authors use non-Abelian bosonization to decompose the Hamiltonian of massless Dirac fermions with N_c colors and N_f flavors into three commuting Wess-Zumino-Novikov-Witten (WZNW) Hamiltonians describing the U(1), color, and flavor sectors. They project onto the color singlet ground state, integrate out the gapped color sector, and analyze the resulting low-energy effective theory at finite chemical potential. For N_f=1 and arbitrary N_c, they employ thermodynamic Bethe ansatz (TBA) equations to determine the Luttinger parameter and Fermi velocity; for general N_f and N_c, they use perturbative methods valid when the chemical potential far exceeds the mass gap.

## Key numbers

* For N_f=1 and N_c=3, the Luttinger parameter in the limit of large chemical potential approaches \(\tilde{K}(\mu) = N_c^{-1/2} = 1/\sqrt{3}\).
* The soliton mass m_s sets the threshold for the strange metal phase: when \(\mu > m_s\), the soliton spectrum becomes gapless while anti-soliton and breather gaps increase.
* The breather masses are given by \(m_n = 2 m_s \sin(\pi n \xi / 2)\) for n=1,...,N_c, with \(\xi = 1/(2N_c - 1)\).
* For N_f=1, as the Fermi surface shrinks (B → 0), the Luttinger parameter approaches unity as \(\tilde{K} = 1 - 2^{3/2} \mathcal{K}_{ss}(\theta=0) \delta_\mu^{1/2} + O(\delta_\mu)\) where \(\delta_\mu = \mu/m_s - 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in 1+1 dimensions studied by Azaria (2016), where a similar non-Fermi liquid behavior was found at nonzero density. The authors connect their 1+1 dimensional results to the quarkyonic regime of cold, dense QCD in 3+1 dimensions, building on prior work by Kojo (2010), Pisarski (2018, 2020, 2021), and Tsvelik (2021) which argued that the quarkyonic regime shares many aspects with systems in 1+1 dimensions. The results enable further study of transport properties of cold quarkyonic matter, where the dominant excitations near the Fermi surface are gapless bosonic modes rather than baryons.
