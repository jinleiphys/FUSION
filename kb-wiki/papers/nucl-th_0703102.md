# Ground-state properties of even-even N=Z nuclei within the Hartree-Fock-BCS and Higher Tamm-Dancoff approaches

> We calculate the ground-state properties of well deformed, even-even N=Z nuclei in the region between Ni-56 and Sn-100 within two different approaches, focusing on the binding energy and deformation and pairing properties. First, we employ the Hartree-Fock-BCS (HFBCS) approximation with the Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and discuss how the results depend on the parameterization of the interaction and on the pairing force parameters adjusted in various schemes to reproduce the experimental odd-even mass differences. Then, within the Higher Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (HTDA), which explicitly conserves the particle number, we calculate the same properties starting from the HFBCS solutions. The HTDA treatment of the ground-state correlations is converged within a n-particle-n-hole expansion using up to n=4 particle-hole excitations of the pair type (in the sense of Cooper pairs). We compare the ground-state properties calculated in these two descriptions of pairing correlations and deduce the importance of the particle-number conservation in weak pairing regimes. Finally, we extend the HTDA calculations so as to include the proton-neutron residual interaction and investigate the role of proton-neutron pairing on the above ground-state properties.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-BCS (HFBCS) and Higher Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (HTDA) approaches yield significantly different ground-state properties for even-even N=Z nuclei in the region between ⁵⁶Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn, particularly in weak pairing regimes, but the authors note that the results depend sensitively on the choice of Skyrme interaction parameterization and on the pairing force parameters adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences using either 3-point or 5-point formulas.

## Method

The authors performed HFBCS calculations using the Skyrme effective interactions SIII and SLy4 in the particle-hole channel with a seniority pairing force, adjusting the pairing strength G to fit experimental quasiparticle energies from 3-point and 5-point odd-even mass difference formulas. They then performed HTDA calculations on top of the HFBCS solutions, using a δ-force residual interaction and a many-body basis truncated to include up to 4-particle-4-hole (4p-4h) pair-type excitations, with the single-particle configuration space limited by a cut-off energy Δε=12 MeV. The HTDA calculations were extended to include proton-neutron residual interactions in the T=0 and T=1 isospin channels.

## Key numbers

- Optimal pairing strengths G_opt for SIII: 17.7 MeV (3-point fit) and 20.6 MeV (5-point fit), with root-mean-square errors σ_Δ of 0.120 MeV and 0.216 MeV respectively
- Optimal pairing strengths G_opt for SLy4: 17.2 MeV (3-point fit) and 19.9 MeV (5-point fit), with σ_Δ of 0.194 MeV and 0.260 MeV respectively
- For ⁷²Kr, the percentage difference in correlation energy between 4p-4h and 6p-6h solutions reaches 5% in the strong pairing region
- Correlation energies for ⁶⁴Ge with V₀=400 MeV·fm³: -4.763 MeV (2p-2h), -6.383 MeV (4p-4h), -6.556 MeV (6p-6h)
- Quadrupole moment Q₂₀ for ⁸⁰Zr with V₀=400 MeV·fm³: 8.453 b (2p-2h), 8.449 b (4p-4h), 8.448 b (6p-6h)
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A for ⁶⁴Ge with SIII and 5-point fit: 8.4485 MeV; experimental value: 8.5294 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier HTDA studies by Pillet et al. (2002) on ¹⁷⁸Hf and by Sieja et al. (2007) on ⁶⁴Ge, applying the method for the first time in systematic calculations of medium-mass, proton-rich N=Z nuclei. The results are compared with HFBCS calculations using the same Skyrme interactions, following the fitting procedure of Bonche et al. (1985) who performed similar calculations for ⁷⁶Sr and ⁸⁰Zr. The study enables future investigations of the role of particle-number conservation in weak pairing regimes and the influence of proton-neutron pairing on ground-state properties in the N=Z region between ⁵⁶Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn.
