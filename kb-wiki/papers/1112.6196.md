# Dynamical Hawking radiation and holographic thermalization

> Using gauge/gravity duality, we study the thermalization of strongly coupled N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma. We analyze the expectation value of the stress tensor and scalar correlation functions and the applicability of the fluctuation dissipation theorem. Via gauge/gravity duality, this maps into studying the equilibration of a black hole geometry and its Hawking radiation.

## Key claim
Using gauge/gravity duality, the authors study thermalization of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma by analyzing both the stress tensor and scalar correlation functions, finding that the effective temperature derived from the stress tensor (T₁) equilibrates before the effective temperature derived from two-point functions (T₂), with the caveat that during the dynamic stage of evolution the effective temperatures do not agree with each other and can become singular.

## Method
The authors create homogeneous but anisotropic non-equilibrium states by applying a time-dependent change to the spatial geometry of an initially equilibrium SYM plasma, which in the dual gravitational description creates a non-equilibrium black hole via gravitational radiation. They solve Einstein's equations numerically for the 5D metric in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, then compute bulk dilaton correlators on the time-dependent geometry using retarded Green's functions and a geometric optics approximation near the stretched horizon. The SYM stress tensor and correlation functions are extracted from the near-boundary behavior of the gravitational fields, from which effective temperatures T₁ (from energy density) and T₂ (from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem) are computed.

## Key numbers
- The SYM energy density changes by a factor of 50 over a time 1/πT_f, where T_f is the final temperature
- The energy density is within 2% of its final equilibrium value at time v = 1.5/πT_f
- The transverse and longitudinal pressures equilibrate near time v = 3/πT_f
- The width of the Gaussian smearing function for correlators is w = 1/3πT_f
- Changing the stretched horizon parameter ε by a factor of two yields a 3% change in the smeared correlators
- The transit time for thermal Hawking radiation to propagate from the stretched horizon to the boundary is Δv ≳ 2/πT_f
- For large momentum light-like modes, the thermalization time can be as large as Δv ~ (1/πT_f)(|q|/πT_f)^{1/3}

## Context
This work extends the holographic thermalization program of Chesler (2008) by studying not just the stress tensor but also correlation functions and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, providing a more complete measure of thermalization. The authors build on the bulk correlator calculation methods of Caron-Huot (2011) and find that high-frequency, small-momentum modes thermalize fastest while light-like modes with large momentum thermalize slowest, consistent with conclusions reached by Balasubramanian (2010). The results enable future semi-analytical studies of the regime ω ~ |q| ≫ πT_f and provide insight into the thermalization of quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions.
