# Gamow shell model description of $^4$He($d$,$d$) elastic scattering reactions

> Structure of weakly bound/unbound light nuclei is often related to the low-energy decay channels involving composite particles like deuteron or $α$-particle. These channels are essential to understand the appearance of light nuclei in the Big Bang nucleosynthesis or the helium fusion. We generalize the Gamow shell model (GSM) in coupled-channel (GSM-CC) representation to include reaction channels with the composite particles. In the core + valence particle formulation, this unified microscopic approach for structure and reactions involving weakly bound/unbound nuclei can be also applied to study low-energy properties of heavy nuclei. As the first application of this generalized GSM-CC approach, we describe the structure of 6Li and deuteron - $α$-particle elastic scattering using the same effective Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) type nucleon-nucleon interaction. Asymptotically, the deuteron structure including its continuum is described using the N3LO chiral force. The bulk of the data, including low-energy spectrum of 6Li, asymptotic normalization coefficients, and angular differential cross sections are satisfactorily described.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Gamow shell model in coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to include reaction channels with composite particles like deuterons, enabling a unified microscopic description of structure and reactions for weakly bound or unbound nuclei. The authors hedge that this approach is a first application and testing ground, and that the practical restriction to valence space effective interactions may be a convenient way to describe low-energy reactions microscopically, but the method's reliability is assessed by comparison with an *ab initio* NCSMC calculation for the same system.

## Method

The authors describe the $^6$Li nucleus as a $^4$He core plus two valence particles interacting with the finite-range Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) type nucleon-nucleon interaction. The coupled-channel equations of GSM-CC are solved using a Berggren basis expansion method, where the deuteron structure is described in a Berggren basis using the N$^3$LO chiral interaction, and the center-of-mass part of projectile states is generated by a finite-range potential derived from the cluster approximation of the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron intrinsic Hamiltonian is diagonalized on a Berggren basis of only bound and scattering states; the deuteron has no low-lying resonance states, so the Berggren basis contour is discretized with Gauss-Legendre quadrature.
- The center-of-mass Berggren basis states are generated by the Hamiltonian $\hat{H}_{CM} = \frac{\mathbf{P}_{CM}^2}{2M_p} + \hat{U}_{CM}$, where $M_p$ is the mass of the projectile.
- The coupled-channel equations are solved for $^4$He($d$,$d$) elastic scattering at low center-of-mass energies, with the bulk of data including low-energy spectrum of $^6$Li, asymptotic normalization coefficients, and angular differential cross sections satisfactorily described.

## Context

This work extends the GSM-CC approach, previously applied to one-nucleon reaction channels (Mercenne 2016), to cluster reaction channels involving composite particles like deuterons. It builds on prior RGM formalism (1970s) and more recent NCSM/RGM (Navratil 2011) and NCSMC (Hupin 2015) calculations for the same $^6$Li system, providing a phenomenological alternative to *ab initio* methods that can be applied to heavier systems. The comparison between the phenomenological GSM-CC and *ab initio* NCSMC for $^6$Li provides insight into the reliability of the former approach for future applications in medium-mass and heavy nuclei.
