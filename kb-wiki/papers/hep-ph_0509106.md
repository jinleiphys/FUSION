# The Problem of Mass: Mesonic Bound States Above Tc

> By extending the Bielefeld LGS (Lattice Gauge Simulation) color singlet interaction, we find that the masses of pi, sigma, rho and a_1 excitations, 32 degrees of freedom in all, go to zero (in the chiral limit) as T -> T_c. This result indicates a smooth phase transition at T_c, at which from above the masses and couplings of mesons vanish `a la Brown-Rho scaling. We discuss that our scenario successfully explains the STAR (STAR Collaboration) rho^0/pi^- ratio in Au-Au peripheral collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

By extending the Bielefeld lattice gauge color-singlet interaction to light quarks via a model-dependent magnetic interaction, the masses of mesonic bound states (π, σ, ρ, a₁) approach zero as T → T_c from above, but only in the chiral limit and with the specific assumption that the internal energy (not free energy) is used as the potential in the Klein-Gordon equation.

## Method

The authors extracted the color-singlet internal energy from Bielefeld lattice gauge simulation data for the heavy-quark free energy above T_c, using the relation V₁(r,T) = F₁(r,T) - T ∂F₁/∂T. They then transcribed the electromagnetic interaction Hamiltonian for fermions to QCD to obtain an effective light-quark potential, which at T ≈ T_c gives either 2V_heavy or 0 depending on the helicity product α₁·α₂. This potential was used in the Klein-Gordon equation to compute bound-state masses of chirally restored SU(2) quarks and antiquarks.

## Key numbers

- The STAR Collaboration measured ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio = 0.169 ± 0.003 (stat) ± 0.037 (syst) in Au-Au peripheral collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The proton-proton ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio is 0.183 ± 0.001 (stat) ± 0.027 (syst).
- The equilibrium expectation for ρ⁰/π⁻ at freezeout is approximately 4 × 10⁻⁴.
- The authors' prediction from their model is ρ⁰/π⁻ ≈ 3/21 ≈ 0.14.
- The width scaling near T_c ≈ 175 MeV follows Γ*_ρ/Γ_ρ ∼ (m*_ρ/m_ρ)⁵.
- The flash temperature T_flash ≈ T_freezeout ≈ 120 MeV is where soft glue condenses and triggers decays.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) to the temperature domain above T_c, and builds on the vector manifestation of Harada and Yamawaki (Harada and Yamawaki, Physical Review) where vector meson mass goes to zero approaching T_c from below. The existence of mesonic bound states above T_c was predicted by Kunihiro, Asakawa et al., and Brown et al. (2004). The results enable a natural explanation of the anomalously large ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio observed by STAR, which standard equilibrium scenarios (Peter et al.) underestimate by several orders of magnitude.
