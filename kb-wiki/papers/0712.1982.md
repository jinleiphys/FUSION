# Low mass dilepton radiation at RHIC

> In this work we discuss the emission of low mass dilepton radiation from a hydrodynamic evolution model of Au-Au collisions and make comparisons with recent PHENIX measurements. The dilepton emission rates from the hadronic phase are treated at finite temperature and baryon density and are completely constrained by broken chiral symmetry in a density expansion. The rates are expressed in terms of vacuum correlators which are measured in $e^+e^-$ annihilation, $τ$ decays and photo-reactions on nucleons and nuclei. We consider two possibilities for the hadronic phase: A chemical equilibrated an off equilibrium hadronic gas. We find that while chemical off-equilibrium helps explain part of the low mass (0.15 $\leq$ M GeV $\leq$ 0.7) enhancement seen in the data there is still a large discrepancy.

## Key claim

Chemical off-equilibrium in the hadronic phase helps explain part of the low-mass (0.15 ≤ M GeV ≤ 0.7) dilepton enhancement seen in PHENIX data, but a large discrepancy remains even after including these effects.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model with an ideal gas equation of state to simulate Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV. Dilepton emission rates from the hadronic phase were computed using a density expansion constrained by broken chiral symmetry, with rates expressed in terms of vacuum correlators measured in e+e- annihilation, τ decays, and photo-reactions. Two scenarios were considered: a chemically equilibrated hadronic gas and a chemically off-equilibrium hadronic gas where particle yields per entropy are conserved throughout the evolution.

## Key numbers

- The low-mass region studied is 0.15 ≤ M GeV ≤ 0.7, where PHENIX finds a factor of ~8 enhancement above the cocktail for most central collisions (Npart ~ 325)
- The critical temperature used is Tc = 180 MeV, with freezeout temperatures of 120 MeV (chemical equilibrium) and 90 MeV (off-equilibrium)
- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c, with freezeout occurring at τ ~ 14 fm/c at the center
- At T ≈ 100 MeV in the off-equilibrium scenario, μπ ≈ 90 MeV and μK ≈ 200 MeV
- At M ≈ 0.4 GeV, the off-equilibrium scenario shows about a 50% enhancement in yields compared to chemical equilibrium

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of NA60 data at SPS energies (Dusling, Teaney, and Zahed, 2007) to RHIC energies, using the same rate equations. The authors compare their results to recent PHENIX measurements (Afanasiev et al., 2007; Toia, 2007). The chemical off-equilibrium treatment follows the approach of Teaney (2002) and incorporates anti-baryon conservation as emphasized by Rapp (2002). The results are comparable to those of Rapp (2000, 2002) who used medium modifications to ρ, ω, and φ propagators. The remaining discrepancy suggests that further mechanisms beyond chemical off-equilibrium are needed to explain the PHENIX low-mass excess.
