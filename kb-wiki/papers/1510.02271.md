# Search for Long-Range Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions at SPS Energies

> Long range correlations are searched for by analyzing the experimental data on 16O-AgBr and 32S-AgBr collisions at 200A GeV/c and the results are compared with the predictions of a multi phase transport (AMPT) model. The findings reveal that the observed forward-backward (F-B) multiplicity correlations are mainly of short-range in nature. The range of F-B correlations are observed to extend with increasing projectile mass. The observed extended range of F-B correlations might be due to overall multiplicity fluctuations arising because of nuclear geometry. The findings are not sufficient for making any definite conclusions regarding the presence of long-range correlations.

## Key claim

The observed forward-backward multiplicity correlations in 16O-AgBr and 32S-AgBr collisions at 200A GeV/c are mainly short-range in nature, and the extended range seen with increasing projectile mass may be due to overall multiplicity fluctuations from nuclear geometry rather than dynamical long-range correlations; the findings are not sufficient for making any definite conclusions regarding the presence of long-range correlations.

## Method

Forward-backward multiplicity correlations were studied by dividing the pseudorapidity distribution of relativistic charged particles into forward and backward hemispheres relative to the center of symmetry, then calculating the correlation strength b from the slope of <n_b> versus n_f and from the ratio D_bf^2/D_ff^2. To isolate short-range from long-range contributions, a pseudorapidity gap was introduced between the forward and backward windows, and the dependence of b on gap size was examined. Experimental data from emulsion experiments (223 events for 16O-AgBr and 452 events for 32S-AgBr at 200A GeV/c) were compared with simulations from the AMPT model (version v1.21-v2.21) with matching event statistics.

## Key numbers

- Correlation strength b for 16O-AgBr: 1.21 ± 0.06 (linear fit) and 1.20 ± 0.03 (from dispersion ratio) for experimental data; 1.08 ± 0.06 and 1.07 ± 0.05 for AMPT model
- Correlation strength b for 32S-AgBr: 1.19 ± 0.03 (linear fit) and 1.18 ± 0.02 (from dispersion ratio) for experimental data; 1.03 ± 0.02 and 1.03 ± 0.03 for AMPT model
- For both projectiles, b remains essentially constant up to a pseudorapidity gap Δη ≈ 1.0, then gradually decreases to zero with increasing gap
- For 16O-AgBr, b stays constant at ~0.7 up to η_gap ~ 1.25; for 32S-AgBr, b remains constant up to η_gap ~ 1.75 before decreasing

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of forward-backward correlations in 16O-AgBr collisions at 14.5A, 60A, and 200A GeV/c by Ahmad et al. (2013) and Singh et al. (1989), and provides a systematic comparison with the AMPT model. The results establish a baseline for correlation studies at SPS energies, showing that correlation strength is independent of projectile mass but the range extends with increasing mass and beam energy. This baseline is necessary before searching for signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation, such as modifications of cluster characteristics or shortening of correlation length, as suggested by Shuryak (1980) and Konchakovski et al. (2009).
