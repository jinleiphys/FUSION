# Baryon Number Violation: From Nuclear Matrix Elements to BSM Physics

> Processes that violate baryon number, most notably proton decay and $n\bar n$ transitions, are promising probes of physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM) needed to understand the lack of antimatter in the Universe. To interpret current and forthcoming experimental limits, theory input from nuclear matrix elements to UV complete models enters. Thus, an interplay of experiment, effective field theory, lattice QCD, and BSM model building is required to develop strategies to accurately extract information from current and future data and maximize the impact and sensitivity of next-generation experiments. Here, we briefly summarize the main results and discussions from the workshop "INT-25-91W: Baryon Number Violation: From Nuclear Matrix Elements to BSM Physics," held at the Institute for Nuclear Theory, University of Washington, Seattle, WA, January 13-17, 2025.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes discussions from the INT-25-91W workshop, concluding that interpreting current and future experimental limits on baryon number violation requires an interplay of experiment, effective field theory, lattice QCD, and BSM model building, but the precision of nuclear matrix elements, especially for channels beyond the golden modes, remains a limiting factor.

## Method

The workshop brought together experts in experiment, effective field theory (EFT), lattice QCD (LQCD), and BSM model building to discuss strategies for extracting information from baryon-number-violating processes, primarily proton decay and neutron-antineutron transitions. Presentations covered ongoing and future experiments (Super-Kamiokande, Hyper-Kamiokande, DUNE, JUNO, HIBEAM/NNBAR), SMEFT analyses at one-loop and two-loop order, LQCD calculations of proton-decay matrix elements, nuclear EFT for neutron-antineutron conversion factors, and specific BSM realizations including leptoquark models.

## Key numbers

- Super-Kamiokande limit on $p\to e^+\pi^0$: $2.4\times 10^{34}$ years at 90% C.L.
- Super-Kamiokande limit on $p\to K^+\bar\nu$: $5.9\times 10^{33}$ years at 90% C.L.
- Super-Kamiokande limit on bound $n\to\bar n$ oscillation: $\tau_{n\bar n} > 4.7\times 10^8$ s at 90% C.L., corresponding to $3.6\times 10^{32}$ years for $\bar n$ appearance lifetime in ${}^{16}$O
- Free $n\to\bar n$ oscillation limit from ILL: $\tau_{n\bar n} > 0.86\times 10^8$ s at 90% C.L.
- Hyper-Kamiokande fiducial mass: 186 kton, expected to probe proton partial lifetimes up to $10^{35}$ years
- JUNO expected sensitivity for $p\to \bar\nu K^+$: $\tau/\text{Br} > 9.6\times 10^{33}$ years at 90% C.L. with 200 kton-years
- JUNO expected sensitivity for $n\to\text{inv}$: $\tau/\text{Br} > 5.0\times 10^{31}$ years at 90% C.L. after 10 years
- JUNO expected sensitivity for $nn\to\text{inv}$: $\tau/\text{Br} > 1.4\times 10^{32}$ years at 90% C.L. after 10 years
- NNBAR at ESS expects sensitivity to $n\to\bar n$ approximately 1000 times greater than ILL
- Unpublished Super-Kamiokande limits: $p\to e^+\gamma$ at $4.1\times 10^{34}$ years and $p\to\mu^+\gamma$ at $2.1\times 10^{34}$ years, exceeding official PDG limits by two orders of magnitude

## Context

This workshop summary extends the ongoing experimental program on baryon number violation, building on decades of Super-Kamiokande results (Super-Kamiokande 2020, 2014) and the theoretical framework of SMEFT analyses (Beneito 2023, Naterop 2024). It connects the nuclear matrix element calculations needed for interpreting proton decay and neutron-antineutron searches with the BSM model-building landscape, particularly grand unified theories (Pati and Salam 1973, Georgi and Glashow 1974, Fritzsch and Minkowski 1974) and leptoquark models. The work enables next-generation experiments, Hyper-Kamiokande, DUNE, JUNO, and NNBAR, to maximize their physics reach by identifying critical channels (such as $p\to e^+\eta$, $p\to\rho\ell^+$, and $p\to\ell^+\gamma$) that break degeneracies in EFT parameter space, and by motivating inclusive search strategies and improved LQCD calculations for resonance final states and disconnected diagrams.
