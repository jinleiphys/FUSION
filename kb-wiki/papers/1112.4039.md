# Transport coefficients of the second order hydrodynamics and the applicability of hydrodynamic model

> Based on the Nakajima-Zubarev type nonequilibrium density operator, we derive microscopic formulae of the transport coefficients in the second order hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper derives microscopic formulae for all transport coefficients in second-order (causal) hydrodynamics using the Nakajima-Zubarev nonequilibrium density operator, but the derivation is limited to small deviations from thermal equilibrium and assumes thermodynamical parameters change slowly compared to the microscopic relaxation length.

## Method

The author expands the nonequilibrium density operator around the local equilibrium operator up to linear order in the thermodynamical force, then performs a derivative expansion of the thermodynamical force terms (gradients of \(\beta U^\sigma\) and \(\beta\mu\)) around the position of the operator being evaluated. The expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) and current \(J^\mu\) are expressed as power series in gradients of thermodynamical parameters, with coefficients given by canonical correlation functions involving \(T^{\mu\nu}\), \(J^\mu\), and the spacetime displacement \((x'^\mu - x^\mu)\).

## Key numbers

- The first-order (Navier-Stokes) coefficients for viscosity and heat conductivity are obtained from the second terms in the expansion, following the well-known Kubo-formula approach as cited from KYN and NI.
- The second-order coefficient for the time-derivative term of heat current is identified as \(\kappa^{2} T^{3} n^{2} {\beta}_{1}/(\epsilon+P)^2\) in the Israel-Stewart equation (IS2).
- The second-order coefficients are expressed as current-weighted relaxation time \(\int d^3 \vex ' {\int^{t}}_{-\infty} dt' {\rm e} ^{-\zeta(t-t')} (\hat{J}^{\mu}(x),(t'-t)\hat{J}_{\rho}(x'))\) and current-weighted correlation length \(\int d^3 \vex ' {\int^{t}}_{-\infty} dt' {\rm e} ^{-\zeta(t-t')} (\hat{T}^{\mu\nu}(x), \Delta^{\sigma \lambda}({x_{\lambda}}'-x_{\lambda})\hat{J}_{\rho}(x'))\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological relativistic causal hydrodynamics introduced by Israel and Stewart (IS1, IS2) and the earlier Kubo-formula approach for first-order transport coefficients (KYN, NI). It provides a microscopic foundation for second-order hydrodynamics that can be evaluated using hadro-molecular simulation (MS, Xian) or lattice field theory, enabling a self-consistent check of the hydrodynamic picture (IMN) by comparing the computed coefficients to the scale of change of thermodynamical parameters in hydrodynamic solutions.
