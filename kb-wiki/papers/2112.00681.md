# Classification of quark and gluon jets in hot QCD medium with deep learning

> Deep learning techniques have shown the capability to identify the degree of energy loss of high-energy jets traversing hot QCD medium on a jet-by-jet basis. The average amount of quenching of quark and gluon jets in hot QCD medium actually have different characteristics, such as their dependence on the in-medium traversed length and the early-developed jet substructures in the evolution. These observations motivate us to consider these two types of jets separately and classify them from jet images with deep learning techniques. We find that the classification performance gradually decreases with increasing degree of jet modification. In addition, we discuss the predictive power of different jet observables, such as the jet shape, jet fragmentation function, jet substructures as well as their combinations, in order to address the interpretability of the classification task.

## Key claim

The classification performance of quark versus gluon jets in a hot QCD medium decreases as the degree of jet energy loss increases, meaning that more heavily quenched jets become harder to distinguish by their species.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 250,000 jets at √s = 5.02 ATeV for PbPb collisions at 0-5% centrality using the hybrid strong/weak coupling model, with vacuum parton showers simulated by PYTHIA 8.244. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (R=0.4) using FastJet 3.3.1, requiring |η|<2 and pTjet > 100 GeV. A convolutional neural network (CNN) was trained on jet images to classify quark and gluon jets, and a separate fully-connected neural network was trained on jet observables (jet shape, jet fragmentation function, and single-valued jet features) for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 250,000 jets were generated at √s = 5.02 ATeV for PbPb collisions at 0-5% centrality
- Jet selection required pTjet > 100 GeV and |η| < 2 with anti-kT algorithm R=0.4
- Classification accuracy using jet image input (33×33 pixels): 75.9%
- Classification accuracy using jet shape input (8 bins): 72.2%
- Classification accuracy using jet fragmentation function input (10 bins): 73.0%
- Classification accuracy using jet features input (7 features): 73.6%
- Classification accuracy using combined JFF, jet shape, and features (25 inputs): 75.8%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deep learning studies on jet energy loss estimation (Du 2020, du2021jet) by now classifying the initiating parton species (quark vs. gluon) of jets traversing hot QCD medium. The classification accuracy in medium is lower than in vacuum, consistent with prior work by Chien (2018). Successful classification would enable improved jet tomographic studies by disentangling the correlation between in-medium traversed length and energy loss for different jet species, and could test the universality of energy loss across different processes such as dijet events versus boson-jet events.
