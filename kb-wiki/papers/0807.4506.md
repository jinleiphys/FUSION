# Complementary descriptions of shape/phase transitions in atomic nuclei

> Shape/phase transitions in atomic nuclei have first been discovered in the framework of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model. Critical point symmetries appropriate for nuclei at the transition points have been introduced as special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian, stirring the introduction of additional new solutions describing wide ranges of nuclei. The complementarity of the IBA and geometrical approaches will be demonstrated by three examples. First, it will be shown that specific special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian correspond to the borders of the critical region of the IBA. Second, it will be demonstrated that the distinct patterns exhibited in different transitional regions by the experimental energy staggering in gamma-bands can be reproduced both by the IBA and by special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian. Third, a first attempt to obtain a IBA SU(3) level scheme from a special solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian will be presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that specific parameter-free solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian (X(3), X(5)-β², X(5)-β⁴) can be mapped onto the borders of the shape/phase transition region of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model, but the X(5) critical point symmetry itself cannot be exactly reproduced by any point in the two-parameter IBA space.

## Method

The authors compare the parameter-independent predictions of several geometric collective models (X(3), X(5)-β², X(5)-β⁴, E(5)-β²ⁿ, Z(4), Z(5)) to two-parameter IBA calculations, using energy level ratios and γ-band staggering patterns S(J) as structural indicators. They also examine whether the ES-D (exactly separable Davidson) solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian can reproduce the SU(3) degeneracy pattern of the IBA by minimizing the rms deviation between even levels of β₁ and γ₁ bands.

## Key numbers

- X(3) lies close to ζ_crit (the left border of the IBA shape/phase transition region, corresponding to two equal minima in IBA total energy curves)
- X(5)-β² lies near the right border ζ** (corresponding to IBA total energy curves with a single deformed minimum)
- A set of neighboring even-even nuclei exhibiting X(3), X(5)-β², and X(5)-β⁴ behaviors: ¹⁷²Os-¹⁷⁴Os-¹⁷⁶Os
- Additional candidate nuclei: ¹⁸⁶Pt (X(3)), ¹⁴⁶Ce (X(5)-β²), ¹⁵⁸Er (X(5)-β⁴)
- For ²³²Th, experimental rms deviation between even levels of β₁ and γ₁ bands is σ_exp(L_max=10)=0.593, while the theoretical value is σ_th(L_max=10)=1.142
- The ES-D model is applicable only to well deformed nuclei with R₄/₂ ≥ 3.0
- IBA critical point of the U(5)-O(6) transition for large boson numbers (N=1,000 and N=10,000) corresponds to the E(5)-β⁴ model

## Context

This work extends the earlier discovery of shape/phase transitions in the IBA model by Deans (1985) and the introduction of critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) by Iachello (2000-2001). It builds on the mapping between geometric and algebraic models by comparing parameter-free Bohr Hamiltonian solutions to IBA calculations, following the approach of McCutchan (2007) who showed X(5) cannot be exactly reproduced in IBA. The results enable identification of candidate nuclei for each geometric model and suggest that the ES-D model provides approximate SU(3) degeneracy but leaves the problem of reproducing a full SU(3) spectrum from the Bohr Hamiltonian conceptually open.
