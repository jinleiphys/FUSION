# A systematic study of neutron magic nuclei with N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126 in the relativistic mean field theory

> We perform a systematic study of all the traditional neutron magic nuclei with $N$ = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126, from the neutron drip line to the proton drip line. We adopt the deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) theory as our framework and treat pairing correlations by a simple BCS method with a zero-range $δ$-force. Remarkable agreement with the available experimental data is obtained for the binding energies, the two- and one-proton separation energies, and the nuclear charge radii. The calculated nuclear deformations are compared with the available experimental data and the predictions of the FRDM mass formula and the HFBCS-1 mass formula. We discuss, in particular, the appearance of sub-shell magic nuclei by observing irregular behavior in the two- and one-proton separation energies.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a simple BCS pairing treatment and the TMA parameter set can reproduce experimental binding energies, separation energies, and charge radii for all traditional neutron magic isotonic chains (N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) from the neutron drip line to the proton drip line, but the rms deviation for binding energies (2.873 MeV) remains about three to four times larger than that of the HFBCS-1 mass formula or the FRDM model.

## Method

The authors performed quadrupole-constrained deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the TMA parameter set, with pairing correlations treated by a BCS method employing a zero-range delta-force. The Dirac equations for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for mesons were solved by expansion on an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with N_f = N_b = 20 shells, and a cutoff energy of 8.0 MeV was applied to the pairing-active single-particle states. For odd-nucleon nuclei, a simple blocking method without breaking time-reversal symmetry was adopted, and the center-of-mass correction was approximated by E_cm = -(3/4)*41*A^{-1/3}.

## Key numbers

- Mass rms deviation sigma = 2.873 MeV for 107 nuclei with N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126 from the RMF+BCS calculation, compared to 0.957 MeV for HFBCS-1 and 0.774 MeV for FRDM.
- Pairing strength V_0 = 344.1 MeV fm^3 for N = 8, 20, 28, 50 isotonic chains; V_0 = 310 MeV fm^3 for N = 82, 126 isotonic chains.
- Cutoff energy E_cut = 8.0 MeV for the pairing-active single-particle states.
- Predicted two-proton drip-line nuclei include: ^20_12Mg_8; ^46_26Fe_20; ^58_30Zn_28; ^100_50Sn_50; ^156_74W_82; ^220_94Pu_126.
- Predicted one-proton drip-line nuclei include: ^20_12Mg_8; ^44_24Cr_20; ^56_28Ni_28; ^100_50Sn_50; ^152_70Yb_82; ^218_92U_126.
- Calculated Q_beta for ^130Cd: 8849 KeV, based on ground-state binding energies of ^130In (1080.966 MeV, beta_2 = 0.041) and ^130Cd (1072.897 MeV, beta_2 = 0.0).

## Context

This work provides the first systematic deformed RMF study covering all six traditional neutron magic numbers (N = 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) from the neutron drip line to the proton drip line, extending earlier RMF studies that focused on individual isotonic chains or proton magic nuclei (Geng 2003). The results are compared with the FRDM mass formula (Moller 1995) and the HFBCS-1 mass formula (Goriely 2001), and the authors identify new subshell closures at Z = 6 in the N = 8 chain and Z = 92 in the N = 126 chain. The failure to obtain deformed ground states for nuclei around Z = 12 in the N = 20 chain (e.g., ^32Mg) is attributed to broken rotational symmetry, motivating future angular momentum projection in the RMF framework.
