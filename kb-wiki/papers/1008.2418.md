# A consistent description of kinetic equation with triangle anomaly

> We provide a consistent description of the kinetic equation with triangle anomaly which is compatible with the entropy principle of the second law of thermodynamics and the charge/energy-momentum conservation equations. In general an anomalous source term is necessary to ensure that the equations for the charge and energy-momentum conservation are satisfied and that the correction terms of distribution functions are compatible to these equations. The constraining equations from the entropy principle are derived for the anomaly-induced leading order corrections to the particle distribution functions. The correction terms can be determined for minimum number of unknown coefficients in one charge and two charge cases by solving the constraining equations.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent description of the kinetic equation with a triangle anomaly that is compatible with both the second law of thermodynamics and charge/energy-momentum conservation, but only if an anomalous source term is introduced in the collision term and the distribution function corrections depend on momentum (they cannot be constants).

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation with an external electromagnetic field and add an anomalous source term to the collision integral. They expand the distribution function around equilibrium to first order in gradients, introducing correction terms proportional to vorticity and magnetic field with momentum-dependent coefficients. By imposing the entropy principle (positive divergence of the entropy current) together with charge and energy-momentum conservation equations, they derive constraining equations for these coefficients and solve them in the one-charge case (with and without antiparticles) and the two-charge (chiral) case.

## Key numbers

* Anomaly coefficient C appears in the charge conservation equation: ∂_μ j^μ = -C E·B, where E and B are electric and magnetic field 4-vectors.
* For massless fermions with one charge and antiparticles at small μ/T, the correction coefficients are λ₀ ≈ C G₁ μ/T³ and λ₁ ≈ -C G₂ μ/T⁴, where G₁ ≡ 6750π²ζ(5)/(d_g G₀) and G₂ ≡ 14/(d_g G₀) with G₀ ≡ -84π²ζ(3) + 1125ζ(5).
* For the two-charge (chiral) case at small μ, μ_A, the solutions are ξ ≈ 2C μ μ_A, ξ_A ≈ C μ², ξ_B ≈ C μ_A, ξ_AB ≈ C μ, DT = -C μ_A μ², and D_B T = -C μ_A μ.
* The anomalous source term integral ∫[dp] C_{A1} is given by Eq. (3.18) in terms of ξ, ξ_B, D, D_B, and the anomaly coefficient C.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of triangle anomalies by Son and Surowka (2009), who found that anomalous terms in the charge current require new vorticity and magnetic field terms in the entropy current to satisfy the second law. The authors provide a kinetic-theory foundation for those hydrodynamic results, deriving the momentum-dependent corrections to distribution functions that are required by consistency. The results are closely related to the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions studied by Kharzeev et al. (2007-2010) and Fukushima et al. (2008-2010), and enable microscopic calculations of anomalous transport coefficients for use in real-time simulations of the quark-gluon plasma.
