# Energy-loss of heavy quarks in the isotropic collisional hot QCD medium at a finite chemical potential

> The present article is the followup of {\color{blue} Eur.\ Phys.\ J.\ C {\bf 79}, 761 (2019)}, where we have studied the energy-loss of the heavy quarks traversing through the isotropic collisional hot QCD medium. Since, the exploration of QCD phase diagram is possible with the upcoming experimental facilities such as Anti-proton and Ion Research (FAIR) and Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA), at finite baryon density and moderate temperature, the inclusion of finite chemical potential is essential to study the hot QCD/QGP medium. Therefore, the aim is to develop a formalism to study the energy-loss of heavy quarks moving in the interacting collisional hot QCD medium having small but a finite quark chemical potential. To do so, the extended effective fugacity quasi-particle model has been employed \cite{chandra_quasi1, chandra_quasi2, Mitra:2017sjo} while considering the effective kinetic theory approach using the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel. Finally, the momentum dependence of the energy-loss for the charm and bottom quark has been investigated at different values of collisions frequency and chemical potential. It is observed that as compared to charm quark, bottom quark loses less energy at a particular momentum, collisional frequency and chemical potential. Also, the energy-loss is seen to decrease with increasing chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous formalism (Jamal, 2019) for heavy-quark energy-loss in a collisional hot QCD medium to include a small but finite quark chemical potential, finding that energy-loss decreases with increasing chemical potential and that the bottom quark loses less energy than the charm quark under the same conditions.

## Method

The authors employ an effective kinetic theory approach with the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel to compute the gluon self-energy and dielectric permittivity of the isotropic hot QCD medium. Medium interaction effects and finite chemical potential are incorporated via the extended effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM), using both (2+1)-lattice QCD and 3-loop HTL perturbative equations of state. The energy-loss per unit length is then obtained by numerically integrating the longitudinal and transverse components of the dielectric permittivity over the momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: 1.8 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.5 GeV.
- Temperature: T = 2 T_c, with T_c = 0.17 GeV.
- Chemical potential values considered: μ_q = 0.0 GeV and μ_q = 0.2 GeV.
- Collision frequency values considered: ν = 0.0 and ν = 0.3 m_D (where m_D is the Debye mass).
- Energy-loss of charm quark at μ_q = 0.2 GeV is lower than at μ_q = 0.0 GeV for the same momentum and collision frequency.
- Bottom quark loses less energy than charm quark at both μ_q = 0.0 and μ_q = 0.2 GeV for the same momentum and collision frequency.

## Context

This work is a direct follow-up of Jamal (2019) (Eur. Phys. J. C 79, 761), which studied heavy-quark energy-loss at vanishing chemical potential using the same BGK and RTA kernels. It extends that formalism to finite baryon density, motivated by upcoming experiments at FAIR and NICA that will probe the QCD phase diagram at moderate temperature and finite chemical potential. The results are consistent with partial findings in the literature, such as Han (2017) and Berrehrah (2015), and enable future studies of magnetic field effects, momentum anisotropy, and viscous effects in the interacting hot QCD medium, as well as the calculation of the nuclear modification factor for comparison with experimental data.
