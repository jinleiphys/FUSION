# Comment on "Time modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI experiments", arXiv:0908.0877

> According to GSI experiment Phys.Lett.B 664, 162 (2008), the rate of the daughter ion production by the K-shell electron capture (with neutrino emission) in H-like ions 142Pm, 140Pr and 152I is modulated with a period of 6 to 7 seconds. In PRL 103, 062502, 2009 by Ivanov and Kienle (also by Lipkin}) there is a claim that neutrino oscillations may explain this result. In arXiv:0811.2302 by Lambiase et al a different explanation is suggested which is based on the rotation of electron and nuclear spins. The aim of this Comment is to show why these explanations are not satisfactory and to discuss other possibilities.

# Comment on "Time modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI experiments"

## Key claim

The neutrino-oscillation explanation for the observed 6–7 second modulation of K-shell electron capture decay rates in H-like heavy ions at GSI is fundamentally flawed because the three neutrino mass eigenstates produced in the capture reactions are distinct final-state particles whose amplitudes cannot interfere. The alternative spin-rotation explanation by Lambiase et al. overestimates the modulation effect by many orders of magnitude and predicts a frequency of ~10^14 Hz rather than the observed ~0.1 Hz.

## Method

The author analyzes the quantum mechanical basis of the proposed explanations by examining the final-state structure of electron capture reactions in the neutrino mass eigenstate basis, showing that the three reactions produce different and orthogonal final states (ν1, ν2, ν3) which cannot interfere. For the spin-rotation explanation, the author calculates the hyperfine splitting energy in heavy hydrogen-like ions, which is enhanced by a factor Z^3 ~ 10^5, and compares the mixing between hyperfine states F=3/2 and F=1/2 induced by the storage ring magnetic field to the hyperfine energy interval.

## Key numbers

- The hyperfine interaction in heavy hydrogen-like ions is enhanced by a factor Z^3 ~ 10^5 relative to ordinary atoms, where Z is the proton number.
- The hyperfine period in such ions is h/E_Hf ~ 10^-14 seconds.
- The frequency of any hyperfine-induced oscillations would be E_Hf/h ~ 10^14 Hz, in contrast to the observed modulation frequency of ~10^-1 Hz.
- The mixing between hyperfine states F=3/2 and F=1/2 is proportional to μ_B B / E_Hf and is very small (where μ_B is the Bohr magneton).

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the neutrino-oscillation explanation proposed by Ivanov (2009) and Lipkin (2009) for the GSI experiment results reported in Physics Letters B 664, 162 (2008). It also refutes the alternative spin-rotation explanation by Lambiase et al. (2008), showing that their mechanism neglects the dominant hyperfine interaction. The analysis suggests that any atomic or nuclear explanation for the observed modulation is unnatural because all relevant energy scales in the ion and storage ring exceed 0.1 Hz by many orders of magnitude, and would require special experimental arrangements to produce observable Rabi oscillations.
