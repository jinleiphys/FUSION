# Charmed hadron production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We present a new model for the description of heavy-flavor hadronization in high-energy nuclear (and possibly hadronic) collisions, where the process takes place not in the vacuum, but in the presence of other color charges. We explore its effect on the charmed hadron yields and kinematic distributions once the latter is applied at the end of transport calculations used to simulate the propagation of heavy quarks in the deconfined fireball produced in nuclear collisions. The model is based on the formation of color-singlet clusters through the recombination of charm quarks with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell. This local mechanism of color neutralization leads to a strong space-momentum correlation, which provides a substantial enhancement of charmed baryon production -- with respect to expectations based on $e^+e^-$ collisions -- and of the collective flow of all charmed hadrons. We also discuss the similarities between our model and recently developed mechanisms implemented in QCD event generators to simulate medium corrections to hadronization in the presence of other nearby color charges.

# Charmed hadron production in high-energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The authors present a model for heavy-flavor hadronization in nuclear collisions where charm quarks recombine with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell at hadronization, producing a strong space-momentum correlation that enhances charmed baryon production relative to expectations from vacuum hadronization in e+e- collisions. The model's success depends critically on this local recombination mechanism; if space-momentum correlation is artificially removed, the charmed baryon enhancement disappears and yields revert to e+e- values.

## Method

The model interfaces a transport simulation of heavy-quark propagation through a deconfined fireball with a hadronization step at T_H = 155 MeV, where each charm quark recombines with a light antiquark or diquark sampled from a thermal distribution in the local rest frame of the same fluid cell. The resulting color-singlet clusters undergo isotropic two-body decay if their invariant mass is below approximately 4 GeV, or fragment as Lund strings for heavier clusters. The authors compare results using both weak-coupling (HTL) and lattice-QCD transport coefficients for charm quark propagation.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature: T_H = 155 MeV
- Cluster mass threshold for two-body decay: M_max ≈ 4 GeV
- In e+e- collisions, the expected Λc+/D0 ratio is approximately 0.1
- Central Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV are used for the primary results
- Results are also compared to STAR data for Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy-flavor hadrochemistry in heavy-ion collisions, addressing the unexpected enhancement of charmed baryon production observed by ALICE (2021) in Pb-Pb collisions and by ALICE (2020) in proton-proton collisions, which cannot be explained by hadronization models tuned to e+e- data. The mechanism shares features with Color Reconnection implemented in PYTHIA by Christiansen (2015) and with earlier work by Norrbin (1998) on D-meson asymmetries in hadronic collisions, where low invariant-mass strings explained production asymmetries. The authors note that extending this model to proton-proton collisions, assuming a small fireball is formed, is work in progress.
