# Proton-neutron pairing vibrations in N=Z nuclei: Precursory soft mode of isoscalar pairing condensation

> L=0 proton-neutron ($pn$) pair-addition and pair-removal strengths in $^{40}$Ca and $^{56}$Ni are investigated by means of the $pn$ particle-particle random-phase approximation employing a Skyrme energy-density functional. It is found that the collectivity of the lowest $J^π= 1^+$ state in the adjacent odd-odd nuclei becomes stronger as the strength of the isoscalar (T=0) pairing interaction increases. The results suggest the emergence of the T=0 $pn$-pairing vibrational mode as a possible critical phenomenon toward the T=0 pairing condensation.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the low-lying $J^\pi = 1^+$ state in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei can be a precursory soft mode of isoscalar ($T=0$) pairing condensation, but only when the strength of the $T=0$ pairing interaction exceeds a critical value ($f_c = 2.04$ in $^{40}$Ca and $f_c = 2.57$ in $^{56}$Ni); below these values the collectivity is weaker and the mode is not a precursor to condensation.

## Method

The author performed proton-neutron ($pn$) particle-particle random-phase approximation (ppRPA) calculations using a Skyrme energy-density functional (SGII interaction for the particle-hole channel) with density-dependent contact pairing interactions. The single-particle basis was obtained from self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) solutions in cylindrical coordinates with a mesh size of 0.6 fm and box boundary conditions at (14.7, 14.4) fm. The $T=0$ pairing strength was varied by a factor $f$ multiplying the interaction, while the $T=1$ pairing strength was fixed at $V_0 = -390$ MeV fm$^3$ to reproduce experimental $T=1$ pairing vibrational frequencies in $^{40}$Ca and $^{56}$Ni.

## Key numbers

- Critical $T=0$ pairing strength factor: $f_c = 2.04$ in $^{40}$Ca and $f_c = 2.57$ in $^{56}$Ni, above which the RPA breaks down and $T=0$ pairing gaps become nonzero.
- Pairing strength fixed as $V_0 = -390$ MeV fm$^3$ for the $T=1$ channel, producing $T=1$ pairing vibrational frequencies of 4.38 MeV in $^{40}$Ca and 4.10 MeV in $^{56}$Ni.
- In $^{42}$Sc with $f=1.7$, the lowest $1^+$ state has a summed forward-going matrix element of 6.63 and a sum of backward-going amplitudes squared of 0.17.
- In $^{58}$Cu with $f=1.7$, the lowest $1^+$ state has a summed forward-going matrix element of 4.25 and a sum of backward-going amplitudes squared of 0.03.
- Experimental energy differences $\Delta E_{\mathrm{exp}} = E_{1^+_1} - E_{0^+_1}$ are 0.328 MeV in $^{38}$K, 0.611 MeV in $^{42}$Sc, 0.937 MeV in $^{54}$Co, and -0.203 MeV in $^{58}$Cu.
- The deuteron transfer experiment on $^{40}$Ca($^3$He, p)$^{42}$Sc observed a cross section to the lowest $1^+$ state about 24 times larger than the pure $(f_{7/2})^2$ configuration calculation (Puhlhofer 1968).

## Context

This work extends the study of $T=0$ proton-neutron pairing correlations beyond the schematic two-level model used by Macchiavelli et al. (2000), who found no appreciable $T=0$ collectivity in $^{56}$Ni. The present microscopic framework using Skyrme EDF shows that collective $T=0$ pairing vibrations can emerge when the interaction is sufficiently strong, contradicting that earlier conclusion. The results enable future calculations of $pn$-transfer cross sections using microscopic transition densities and suggest that low-lying Gamow-Teller strengths in $N=Z$ nuclei, recently measured by Fujita et al. (2014), can serve as an alternative probe to fix the $T=0$ pairing strength.
