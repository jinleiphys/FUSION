# Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking on the Light Front I. DLCQ Approach

> Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the DLCQ method is investigated in detail using a chiral Yukawa model closely related to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. By classically solving three constraints characteristic of the light-front formalism, we show that the chiral transformation defined on the light front is equivalent to the usual one when bare mass is absent. A quantum analysis demonstrates that a nonperturbative mean-field solution to the ``zero-mode constraint'' for a scalar boson (sigma) can develop a nonzero condensate while a perturbative solution cannot. This description is due to our identification of the ``zero-mode constraint'' with the gap equation. The mean-field calculation clarifies unusual chiral transformation properties of fermionic field, which resolves a seemingly inconsistency between triviality of the null-plane chiral charge Q_5|0>=0 and nonzero condensate. We also calculate masses of scalar and pseudoscalar bosons for both symmetric and broken phases, and eventually derive the PCAC relation and nonconservation of Q_5 in the broken phase.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking can be realized in the light-front DLCQ formalism by identifying the zero-mode constraint for the scalar boson with the gap equation, and shows that a nonperturbative mean-field solution to this constraint yields a nonzero condensate while a perturbative solution cannot, thereby resolving the apparent contradiction between a trivial null-plane chiral charge and a nonzero condensate.

## Method

The authors analyze a chiral Yukawa model (closely related to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) using the DLCQ method, compactifying the longitudinal coordinate \(x^-\) into a circle of length \(2L\) with periodic boundary conditions for scalars and antiperiodic for fermions. They classically solve three constraints characteristic of light-front quantization (a fermionic constraint and two zero-mode constraints for the scalar and pseudoscalar bosons), then perform Dirac quantization before solving the constraints, treating the zero-mode constraints quantum mechanically with a mean-field approximation in the leading order of the \(1/N\) expansion.

## Key numbers

* The scalar condensate in the broken phase is given by \(\langle \sigma \rangle = -\frac{\lambda}{N}\langle \bar \Psi \Psi \rangle \neq 0\), where \(\lambda\) is the four-Fermi coupling and \(N\) is the number of fermion components.
* The boson masses in the chiral Yukawa model are determined by the gap equation derived from the zero-mode constraint; the pseudoscalar boson (pion) mass vanishes in the chiral limit \(m=0\) as a Nambu-Goldstone boson.
* The light-front chiral charge \(Q_5^{\rm LF}\) always annihilates the vacuum: \(Q_5^{\rm LF}|0\rangle = 0\), even in the broken phase where \(\langle \bar \Psi \Psi \rangle \neq 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of discrete chiral symmetry breaking in the 1+1 dimensional Gross-Neveu model (Itakura and Maedan, 1998) to continuous chiral symmetry in 3+1 dimensions, and generalizes earlier efforts to describe spontaneous symmetry breaking in scalar \(\lambda\phi^4\) models on the light front (SSB references). The paper enables future application of DLCQ methods to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and QCD, with a follow-up paper (Itakura, 1999) planned to address the fermionic constraint without auxiliary scalar fields.
