# On Non-Relativistic Conformal Field Theory and Trapped Atoms: Virial Theorems and the State-Operator Correspondence in Three Dimensions

> The field theory of nonrelativistic fermions interacting via contact interactions can be used to calculate the properties of few-body systems of cold atoms confined in harmonic traps. The state-operator correspondence of Non-Relativistic Conformal Field Theory (NRCFT) shows that the energy eigenvalues (in oscillator units) of N harmonically trapped fermions can be calculated from the scaling dimensions of N-fermion operators in the NRCFT. They are also in one-to-one correspondence with zero-energy, scale-invariant solutions to the N-body problem in free space. We show that these two mappings of the trapped fermion problem to free space problems are related by an automorphism of the SL(2,R) algebra of the conformal symmetry of fermions at the unitary limit. This automorphism exchanges the internal Hamiltonian of the gas with the trapping potential and hence provides a novel method for deriving virial theorems for trapped Fermi gases at the unitary limit. We also show that the state-operator correspondence can be applied directly in three spatial dimensions by calculating the scaling dimensions of two- and three-fermion operators and finding agreement with known exact results for energy levels of two and three trapped fermions at the unitary limit.

## Key claim

The state-operator correspondence of Non-Relativistic Conformal Field Theory (NRCFT) can be applied directly in three spatial dimensions, not only via epsilon expansion, by calculating scaling dimensions of few-fermion operators in the effective field theory and finding agreement with known exact energy levels of trapped fermions at the unitary limit. The two known mappings of the trapped fermion problem to free space problems are related by an automorphism of the SL(2,R) algebra that exchanges the internal Hamiltonian with the trapping potential.

## Method

The author uses a nonrelativistic effective field theory of two-component fermions interacting via S-wave contact interactions, with the Lagrangian containing a four-fermion interaction term and the coupling constant renormalized using dimensional regularization with the Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) scheme. The scaling dimensions of two- and three-fermion operators are computed from the anomalous dimensions obtained by evaluating Feynman diagrams (the matrix element of the composite operator between the vacuum and a two-particle state) and requiring the renormalized matrix element to be cutoff-independent. The SL(2,R) algebra formed by the Hamiltonian, dilatation operator, and conformal generator is used to derive the automorphism relating the trapped-to-free mappings and to obtain virial theorems.

## Key numbers

* The ground state energy of two trapped fermions at the unitary limit is 2, in oscillator units (where the fundamental oscillator energy is set to 1).
* The scaling dimension of the two-fermion operator ψψ is Δ_{ψψ} = 2, obtained from the anomalous dimension γ_{ψψ} = -1 in the a → ±∞ limit (naive dimension 2Δ_ψ = 3 plus γ_{ψψ} = -1).
* The virial theorem for trapped fermions at the unitary limit gives ⟨H_osc⟩ = 2⟨C⟩, where C is the trapping potential operator, for eigenstates and thermal ensembles.
* Higher moments of the trapping potential in the ground state follow ⟨C^n⟩ = Γ[E_0 + n] / (2^n Γ[E_0]), where E_0 is the ground state energy.

## Context

This work extends the state-operator correspondence developed by Nishida (2007) for NRCFT, which previously required epsilon expansion (in 2+ε or 4-ε dimensions) with Padé approximants to reach d=3, by showing the correspondence works directly in three dimensions for N=2 and N=3 fermions. The automorphism of the SL(2,R) algebra provides a group-theoretical derivation of virial theorems previously obtained by Thomas, Kinast, and Turlapov (2005) using thermodynamic arguments, by Werner and Castin (2006) using pseudopotential wavefunctions, and by Son (2007) using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem. The results enable direct calculation of trapped fermion energy levels from operator scaling dimensions in three dimensions and provide a systematic framework for extending the approach to larger N.
