# Collective flow effects on charge balance correlations and local parity-violation observables in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We study the effects of collective flow on charge dependent azimuthal correlations at LHC energies. We propose a series of correlations as a signature of the combined effects of azimuthal collective flow and local charge conservation and perform an analysis within a statistical freeze-out model. We find that present LHC measurements of charge dependent azimuthal correlations are consistent with local charge conservation on the kinetic freeze-out surface. In view of experimental searches for signatures of the chiral-magnetic effect, we provide an alternative explanation of the charge dependence of the observed signal and propose additional measurements to disentangle the effects.

## Key claim
The charge-dependent azimuthal correlations observed by ALICE in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, which have been interpreted as a signature of the chiral magnetic effect, can be fully explained by the combined effects of collective flow and local charge conservation on the kinetic freeze-out surface, without invoking parity violation.

## Method
The authors employed a statistical freeze-out (blast-wave) model that incorporates local charge conservation at kinetic freeze-out, using a filled ellipse parameterization of the freeze-out configuration. Model parameters (freeze-out temperature, flow parameters, eccentricity) were determined by fitting single-particle spectra and flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) from ALICE data. They calculated charge balance functions and mixed-harmonic azimuthal correlators \(\delta C(n,m;k)\) for six centrality classes in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV Pb+Pb collisions, using the same kinematic cuts (\(|\eta|<0.8\), \(0.2 < p_T < 5\) GeV/\(c\)) as the ALICE experiment.

## Key numbers
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{kin}\) ranges from 0.086 GeV (5-10% centrality) to 0.079 GeV (50-60% centrality).
- Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch}\) = 148 MeV, used in the canonical ensemble with fixed volume 100 fm\(^3\).
- The blast-wave model with perfectly local charge conservation (\(\sigma_\phi = 0\)) reproduces ALICE data for \(\delta\langle\cos(\Delta\phi)\rangle\) in central and mid-central collisions; for peripheral collisions, a non-zero separation \(\sigma_\phi\) of balancing charges on the freeze-out surface is needed to match data.
- The correlator \(\delta C(2,1;2)\), originally proposed as a chiral magnetic effect signature, is fully accounted for by local charge conservation at LHC energies, in analogy to RHIC results.
- Higher harmonic correlators \(\delta C(4,1;2)\) show a large signal from quadrangular anisotropy, while \(\delta C(6,1;2)\) is highly suppressed because \(v_6\{\Psi_2\}\) is very small.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Schlichting and Pratt (2011) from RHIC to LHC energies, showing that the same mechanism of local charge conservation combined with collective flow explains the charge-dependent correlations observed by ALICE. The results contradict interpretations of the \(\langle\cos(\phi_\alpha+\phi_\beta-2\Psi_{RP})\rangle\) correlator as a unique signature of the chiral magnetic effect, as proposed by Kharzeev et al. (2008). The authors propose measuring higher harmonic correlators (\(|n|>4\)) where the background from local charge conservation is significantly reduced, and suggest that balance functions of identified charged particles could disentangle hadronic and partonic contributions, following the approach of Pratt (2012).
