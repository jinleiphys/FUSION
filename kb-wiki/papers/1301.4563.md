# Parton Energy Loss in the Extremely Prolate Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The energy loss per unit path length of a highly energetic parton scattering elastically in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma is studied as an initial value problem. The approach is designed to study unstable plasmas but in the case of an equilibrium plasma the well known result is reproduced. As an example of an unstable system, an extremely prolate plasma, where the momentum distribution is infinitely elongated along one direction, is considered here. The energy loss is shown to be strongly time and directionally dependent and its magnitude can much exceed the energy loss in equilibrium plasma.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of a highly energetic parton in an extremely prolate quark-gluon plasma is not a constant but grows exponentially in time due to unstable chromomagnetic transverse modes, and its magnitude can much exceed the equilibrium value, but this result depends on treating the energy loss as an initial value problem rather than assuming a steady-state plasma.

## Method

The authors treat the parton as a classical particle with SU(N_c) color charge and solve the Wong equations together with linearized Yang-Mills equations in the Hard Loop approximation, using a one-sided Fourier transformation to handle the initial value problem. The chromodielectric tensor is computed for an extremely prolate momentum distribution that is infinitely elongated along one direction (the beam axis), and the energy loss formula is expressed in terms of the collective mode spectrum obtained from the poles of the inverted matrix Σ. The integrals over momentum are performed numerically in cylindrical coordinates with a finite cutoff k_max = 5 μ, using parameters g=1 and C_R = N_c = 3.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss per unit time in equilibrium plasma is 0.18 (in units of g²μ²) for k_max = 5 μ (Fig. 1).
- The Debye mass μ is defined as μ² ≡ g² ∫ d³p/(2π)³ f(p)/|p|.
- For the prolate distribution, a related mass parameter m satisfies m² = (1/2)μ².
- The instability condition for the Weibel mode is cos²θ < m²/(m² + k²), where θ is the angle between k and the anisotropy direction n.
- The energy loss is computed for three angles Θ between the parton velocity v and the z-axis: Θ = 0, π/12, and π/6, showing exponential growth in time for all three cases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier initial-value approach to energy loss (Carrington 2011, Carrington 2012) from two-stream instabilities to the case of a prolate plasma unstable due to transverse chromomagnetic modes. It contrasts with the equilibrium energy loss formula (lebellac) and with the earlier anisotropic calculation by Romatschke (2004) that did not account for time dependence of unstable modes. The collective excitations of the extremely prolate system were previously studied by Arnold (2003) using a different method. The formalism enables future calculations of energy loss in more realistic time-evolving anisotropic plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
