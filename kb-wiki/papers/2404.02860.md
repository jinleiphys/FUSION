# Spin alignment of $K^\ast$ induced by strange-baryon density inhomogeneity

> The difference between the spin alignments of $K^\ast$ and those of $ϕ$ at the low collision energies is a puzzle raised by the recent experiments. Unlike $ϕ$ meson, $K^\ast$, carrying a unit strange charge, should react to strange chemical potential $μ_S$. In this paper, we shall first convince you that $μ_S$ is not small in a brayon-rich medium for keeping strange neutrality, and then derive the spin alignment induced by the gradient of $μ_S$, and hence of baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, using linear response theory, with the transport coefficients expressed, without any approximation, in terms of the $K^\ast$'s in-medium spectral properties by employing Ward-Takahashi identity. It turns out that such an effect applies mainly to the particles whose longitudinal and transverse modes diverge, and induces only the local spin alignment in a static medium. The magnitudes of these coefficients will be further estimated under the quasi-particle approximation.

# Spin alignment of $K^\ast$ induced by strange-baryon density inhomogeneity

## Key claim

The difference between $K^\ast$ and $\phi$ spin alignments at low collision energies may arise from the gradient of strange chemical potential $\mu_S$ acting on $K^\ast$ (which carries strange charge) via diffusion-induced tensor polarization (DITP), but this effect contributes only to local spin alignment in a static medium and most probably is not the reason for the observed difference in global spin alignment between the two species.

## Method

The author derives the spin alignment of $K^\ast$ mesons induced by gradients of strange chemical potential $\mu_S$ using linear response theory, with the transport coefficients expressed non-perturbatively in terms of the meson's in-medium spectral properties via the Ward-Takahashi identity. The three-point Green's function connecting the Wigner function and the strange current is evaluated exactly, then simplified under the assumption that the vertex is time-reversal symmetric and the spectral functions are analytic in the upper half-plane. The coefficients are further estimated under the quasi-particle approximation.

## Key numbers

- In Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 GeV, produced kaons amount to 50 times the number of anti-kaons, implying a large strange chemical potential $\mu_S$ from the thermal model relation $N_K/N_{\bar K} \sim \exp(2\mu_S/T)$
- In the partonic phase, strange neutrality requires $\mu_S = \mu_B/3$ (neglecting electrical chemical potential)
- The spin alignment $\delta\rho_{00} \equiv \rho_{00} - 1/3$ was previously estimated via thermal model as $\mathcal O(\omega^2/T^2) \sim \mathcal O(10^{-4})$ in heavy-ion collisions, much smaller than later measurements at RHIC and LHC

## Context

This work extends the shear-induced tensor polarization (SITP) framework developed by Li (2022) and Dong (2023), which connected spin alignment to in-medium spectral properties of vector mesons. The author addresses the puzzle raised by STAR (2008, 2022) and ALICE (2019) measurements showing different spin alignments for $K^\ast$ and $\phi$ mesons at low collision energies. The DITP effect is proposed as a potential novel probe of QCD first-order phase transition, since $\partial(\beta\mu_S)$ is positively dependent on $\partial(\beta\mu_B)$, which is enhanced during spinodal decomposition as studied by Li (2015, 2016) and simulated by Sun (2020, 2022) at beam energy scan energies.
