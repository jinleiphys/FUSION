# Hard Photodisintegration of 3He into pd pair

> The recent measurements of high energy photodisintegration of the $^3He$ nucleus to the $pd$ pair at $90^0$ center of mass demonstrated an energy scaling consistent with the quark counting rule with unprecedentedly large exponent of $s^{-17}$. To understand the underlying mechanism of this process we extended the theoretical formalism of hard rescattering mechanism to calculate the $γ^3He\rightarrow pd$ reaction. In HRM the incoming high energy photon strikes a quark from one of the nucleons in the target which subsequently undergoes hard rescattering with the quarks from the other nucleons generating hard two-body system in the final state of the reaction. Within the HRM we derived the parameter free expression for the differential cross section of the reaction, which is expressed through the $^3He\rightarrow pd$ transition spectral function, cross section of hard $pd\rightarrow pd$ scattering and the effective charge of the quarks being interchanged during the hard rescattering process. The numerical estimates of all these factors resulted in the magnitude of the cross section which is surprisingly in a good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The hard rescattering mechanism (HRM), previously successful for deuteron photodisintegration, can be extended to the $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ reaction and yields a parameter-free expression for the differential cross section that agrees surprisingly well with experimental data, despite the unprecedented $s^{-17}$ scaling observed.

## Method

The authors extended the HRM formalism to the $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ reaction, where the incoming photon strikes a quark from one proton in the $^3\text{He}$ nucleus, and that struck quark then undergoes hard rescattering via quark interchange with quarks from the other nucleons (the "initial" deuteron system) to produce the final proton and deuteron with large relative momentum. The total scattering amplitude was factorized into a nuclear part (the $^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ transition spectral function, calculated using nonrelativistic wave functions) and a hard scattering part (the elastic $pd\rightarrow pd$ amplitude, taken from experiment), with the photon-quark interaction evaluated at the pole of the struck quark propagator using a peaking approximation at $\beta_1 = 1/3$.

## Key numbers

- The measured energy scaling of the $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ reaction at $\theta_{cm}=90^\circ$ follows $d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-17}$, consistent with the quark counting rule prediction for a reaction with 1 (photon) + 3 ($^3\text{He}$) + 3 (proton) + 3 (deuteron) = 10 fundamental constituents, giving exponent $n_a+n_b+n_c+n_d-2 = 10-2 = 8$? Wait, the abstract says $s^{-17}$, which corresponds to $n_a+n_b+n_c+n_d-2 = 1+3+3+3-2 = 8$, not 17. The abstract states "unprecedentedly large exponent of $s^{-17}$", this appears to be a direct quote from the abstract, so the paper claims the data shows $s^{-17}$ scaling.

- For the $\gamma d\rightarrow pn$ reaction, the HRM model reproduced the $s^{-11}$ scaling and absolute cross section magnitudes without free parameters at photon energies $\gtrsim 1$ GeV and large center-of-mass angles.

- At $E_\gamma = 1$ GeV, the invariant momentum transfer $-t \sim 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for $\theta_{cm}=90^\circ$, and the laboratory momenta of outgoing proton and deuteron exceed 1 GeV/c.

- At $E_\gamma = 1$ GeV, the mass of the intermediate state produced is $m_R \approx \sqrt{s} - M_d \approx 1.8$ GeV, close to the deep inelastic threshold of 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the HRM model previously developed for $\gamma d\rightarrow pn$ reactions (Sargsian, 2001; Sargsian, 2002) and applied to $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pp+n$ reactions (Sargsian, 2004) to the more complex $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ channel, following recent measurements by Pomerantz et al. (2013) that observed $s^{-17}$ scaling. The HRM approach contrasts with conventional mesonic pictures that failed to explain the observed energy scaling in deuteron photodisintegration, and it enables predictions for angular distributions and energy dependencies at other center-of-mass angles for the $^3\text{He}$ target, as well as further tests of quark degrees of freedom in nuclear systems.
