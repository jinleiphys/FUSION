# A Study of Quasi-parton Distribution Functions in the Diquark Spectator Model

> To facilitate lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structute, a set of quasi-parton distributions were recently introduced. These quasi-PDFs were shown to reduce to standard PDFs when the nucleon is boosted to high energies, $P_z\rightarrow \infty$. Since taking such limit is not feasible in lattice simulations, it is essential to provide guidance for what values of $P_z$ the quasi-PDFs are good approximations of standard PDFs. Within the framework of the spectator diquark model, we evaluate both the up and down quarks' quasi-PDFs and standard PDFs for all leading-twist distributions (unpolarized distribution $f_1$, helicity distribution $g_1$, and transversity distribution $h_1$). We find that, for intermediate parton momentum fractions $x$, quasi-PDFs are good approximations to standard PDFs (within $20-30\%$) when $P_z\gtrsim 1.5-2$ GeV. On the other hand, for large $x\sim 1$ much larger $P_z > 4$ GeV is necessary to obtain a satisfactory agreement between the two sets. We further find that the Soffer positivity bound does not hold in general for quasi-PDFs.

## Key claim

Within the spectator diquark model, quasi-PDFs approximate standard PDFs to within 20-30% for intermediate parton momentum fractions when the nucleon momentum is at least 1.5-2 GeV, but for large x near 1, much larger momenta above 4 GeV are required; the Soffer positivity bound does not generally hold for quasi-PDFs.

## Method

The authors computed both standard PDFs and quasi-PDFs for unpolarized (f1), helicity (g1), and transversity (h1) distributions in the spectator diquark model, which includes scalar and axial-vector diquark contributions. They used dipolar form factors with parameters fitted to reproduce standard PDFs from global analyses (ZEUS2002 and GRSV2000) at low factorization scales. The quasi-PDFs were evaluated at finite nucleon momenta Pz and compared to the standard PDFs by computing ratios and testing the Soffer inequality.

## Key numbers

- For intermediate x (0.1 to 0.4-0.5), quasi-PDFs agree with standard PDFs within 20-30% when Pz is greater than or equal to 1.5-2 GeV.
- For large x = 0.7, the ratio of quasi-PDF to standard PDF can be as large as 6-7 at Pz of 1-2 GeV; Pz greater than 4 GeV is needed for satisfactory agreement.
- The Soffer bound is violated for the u-quark quasi-PDF at Pz = 0.5 GeV, where the quantity S^u(x, Pz) becomes negative over the entire x range from 0 to 1.
- The standard PDFs were fitted at factorization scale mu^2 = 0.30 GeV^2 for unpolarized distributions and mu^2 = 0.26 GeV^2 for helicity distributions.

## Context

This work extends the spectator diquark model calculations of Gamberg et al. (2014) to systematically compare quasi-PDFs with standard PDFs, providing guidance for lattice QCD simulations that use the quasi-PDF approach introduced by Ji (2013). The study uses the same model parameters as Bacchetta et al. (2008) to ensure consistency with global PDF fits. The results enable lattice practitioners to estimate the required nucleon boost momenta for reliable PDF extraction and caution against using positivity bounds for quasi-PDFs in lattice calculations.
