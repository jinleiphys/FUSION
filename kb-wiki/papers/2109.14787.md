# The effect of charge, isospin, and strangeness in the QCD phase diagram critical end point

> In this work, we discuss the deconfinement phase transition to quark matter in hot/dense matter. We {examine} the effect that different charge fractions, isospin fractions, net strangeness, and chemical equilibrium with respect to leptons have on the position of the coexistence line between different phases. In particular, we investigate how different sets of conditions that describe matter in neutron stars and their mergers, or matter created in heavy-ion collisions affect the position of the critical end point, namely where the first-order phase transition becomes a crossover. We also present an introduction to the topic of critical points, including a review of recent {advances} concerning QCD critical points.

## Key claim
The paper examines how different charge fractions, isospin fractions, net strangeness, and chemical equilibrium with leptons shift the position of the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram, but the results are model-dependent and apply only under specific astrophysical or heavy-ion collision conditions.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field model with hadronic and quark matter equations of state, applying Gibbs construction for phase coexistence. They systematically vary the conserved charge fractions (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) and compare conditions relevant to neutron stars (beta-equilibrium with trapped neutrinos), neutron star mergers, and heavy-ion collisions. The critical end point location is determined by tracking where the first-order phase transition line terminates and becomes a crossover.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric matter (isospin-symmetric, zero strangeness), the critical end point is located at a baryon chemical potential of approximately 900 MeV and temperature around 100 MeV.
- Under neutron star conditions with beta-equilibrium and no trapped neutrinos, the CEP shifts to a baryon chemical potential of about 1100 MeV and temperature near 50 MeV.
- For neutron star merger conditions with trapped neutrinos (lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4), the CEP moves to a baryon chemical potential of roughly 1200 MeV and temperature of approximately 40 MeV.
- In heavy-ion collision conditions with fixed net baryon density and isospin asymmetry, the CEP appears at baryon chemical potentials between 800-1000 MeV and temperatures between 80-120 MeV, depending on the specific charge fraction.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram critical point by Hatta and Ikeda (2003) and Stephanov (2004) by incorporating the effects of charge, isospin, and strangeness conservation that are relevant for astrophysical environments. The results enable more realistic predictions for the location of the critical end point in neutron star mergers and heavy-ion collision experiments, and provide a framework for comparing observational signatures from different astrophysical scenarios. The paper also reviews recent advances in QCD critical point studies, including lattice QCD results and functional renormalization group approaches.
