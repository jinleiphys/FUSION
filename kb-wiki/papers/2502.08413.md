# A variation on "compositeness" (including higher partial waves)

> The ``spatial interpretation of compositeness'', presented and discussed in [1,2] in the context of non-relativistic potential scattering, is extended to higher partial waves. A particular set of basis states is used to arrive at a slightly different perspective on the derivation and interpretation of ``compositeness'' usually given in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper extends the "spatial interpretation of compositeness", previously developed for s-wave scattering, to higher partial waves, showing that the compositeness parameter derived from the bound-state pole residue is exactly related to the probability of finding the particle outside a sphere of radius R, but that for ℓ > 0 this relation is more complicated than in the s-wave case and is given by an exact expression involving spherical Hankel functions.

## Method

The author constructs a complete set of orthonormal basis states from a hard-sphere (impenetrable delta-shell) problem, consisting of discrete states confined inside a sphere of radius R and continuum scattering states outside. A bound state in a finite-range potential V(r) with range d < R is expanded in this basis, and the residue of the bound-state pole in the partial-wave scattering amplitude is computed analytically. The resulting exact relation between the compositeness parameter C_B^ℓ and the probability P(r > R) is then used to derive approximate expressions connecting threshold parameters (scattering length a_ℓ and effective range r_ℓ) to spatial probabilities, which are tested numerically for a spherical-well potential.

## Key numbers

* For ℓ = 0 with a spherical well of range d = 5 μ⁻¹ and κ_B = 0.1 μ: compositeness C_B^0 = 1.6885, scattering length a_0 = 12.589 μ⁻¹, effective range r_0 = 4.1893 μ⁻¹, exact P(r > d) = 0.6203, approximate P_a(r > d) = 0.6265, approximate P_r(r > d) = 0.6328.
* For ℓ = 1 with the same well: C_B^1 = 0.2794, a_1 = 456.950 μ⁻³, r_1 = -0.6167 μ⁻¹, exact P(r > d) = 0.5137, P_a(r > d) = 0.5451, P_r(r > d) = 0.5808.
* For ℓ = 2: C_B^2 = 0.0092, a_2 = 1743.991 μ⁻⁵, r_2 = -0.1236 μ⁻¹, exact P(r > d) = 0.3721, P_a(r > d) = 0.3393, P_r(r > d) = 0.3123.
* For ℓ = 3: C_B^3 = 0.0001, a_3 = 2011.072 μ⁻⁷, r_3 = -0.1034 μ⁻¹, exact P(r > d) = 0.2774, P_a(r > d) = 0.2671, P_r(r > d) = 0.2572.

## Context

This work extends the spatial interpretation of compositeness developed by Bruns (2019, 2022) from s-wave to arbitrary orbital angular momentum, building on the foundational compositeness framework of Weinberg (1965). The exact relations derived here connect the compositeness parameter to spatial probabilities without relying on short-range approximations, unlike the standard effective-range approximations used in Weinberg (1965) and reviewed by Sekihara et al. (2014). The results enable estimation of the interaction range and bound-state size from threshold parameters (scattering length and effective range) for higher partial waves, and the author notes that extension to relativistic dynamics, energy-dependent potentials, coupled channels, and resonances requires more elaborate methods.
