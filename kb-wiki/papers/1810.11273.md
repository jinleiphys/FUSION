# TMD factorization for dijets + photon production from the dilute-dense CGC framework

> We calculate the production of a photon and two jets at forward rapidity in proton-nucleus collisions, within the hybrid dilute-dense framework in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism. After obtaining the cross section for both the quark- and gluon-initiated channels, we consider the correlation limit, in which the vector sum of the transverse momenta of the three outgoing particles is small with respect to the individual transverse momenta. In this limit, the cross section simplifies considerably and can be written in a factorized form, sensitive to various unpolarized and linearly-polarized transverse-momentum-dependent gluon distribution functions (gluon TMDs). Thus, we demonstrate for the first time that the emergence of a TMD factorization formula in the correlation limit, from CGC expressions, holds beyond the previously-considered simpler $2\to2$ processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization formula emerges from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework for the 2→3 process of dijet plus photon production in proton-nucleus collisions, extending the previously established TMD factorization for simpler 2→2 processes, but only in the correlation limit where the vector sum of the transverse momenta of the three outgoing particles is small compared to the individual transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for forward-rapidity photon-plus-two-jet production in proton-nucleus collisions using the hybrid dilute-dense CGC formalism, treating the projectile proton via collinear parton distribution functions and the dense target nucleus via CGC Wilson lines. They derive the partonic cross sections for both the quark-initiated channel (where an incoming quark emits a photon and a gluon before scattering) and the gluon-initiated channel (where an incoming gluon splits into a quark-antiquark pair with photon emission from one of the quarks). They then take the correlation limit by performing a small dipole expansion, which corresponds to a leading-power expansion in the small ratio of the total transverse momentum imbalance to the hard scale, and show that the resulting cross section factorizes into a convolution of hard coefficients with various unpolarized and linearly-polarized gluon TMDs.

## Key numbers

- The correlation limit corresponds to a leading-power expansion in the small ratio \(p_t^2/Q^2\), where \(p_t = q_1 + q_2 + q_3\) is the vector sum of the transverse momenta of the three outgoing particles and \(Q\) is the hard scale.
- The quark-initiated channel involves coupling constants \(g_e\) (QED) and \(g_s\) (strong coupling) at order \(O(g_e g_s)\).
- The gluon-initiated channel involves splitting at order \(O(g_s)\) for the quark-antiquark pair and \(O(g_e g_s)\) for the photon emission.

## Context

This work extends the program of understanding gluon TMDs within the CGC framework, which was previously applied to simpler 2→2 processes such as dijet, heavy-quark pair, and photon-jet production in electron-nucleus or proton-nucleus collisions (Marquet 2016, Akcakaya 2012, Dumitru 2015, Kotko 2015, Boer 2016, Boer 2017, Marquet 2017). The derivation enables future studies of nonlinear evolution of the gluon TMDs via JIMWLK or BK equations, and allows the use of non-perturbative models such as the McLerran-Venugopalan or Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff model for analytical expressions.
