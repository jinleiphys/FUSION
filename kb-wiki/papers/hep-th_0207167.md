# Electrodynamic Limit in a Model for Charged Solitons

> We consider a model of topological solitons where charged particles have finite mass and the electric charge is quantised already at the classical level. In the electrodynamic limit, which physically corresponds to electrodynamics of solitons of zero size, the Lagrangian of this model has two degrees of freedom only and reduces to the Lagrangian of the Maxwell field in dual representation. We derive the equations of motion and discuss their relations with Maxwell's equations. It is shown that Coulomb and Lorentz forces are a consequence of topology. Further, we relate the U(1) gauge invariance of electrodynamics to the geometry of the soliton field, give a general relation for the derivation of the soliton field from the field strength tensor in electrodynamics and use this relation to express homogeneous electric fields in terms of the soliton field.

## Key claim
In the electrodynamic limit (where the soliton length scale \(r_0\) is sent to zero, corresponding to point-like charges), the model of topological fermions reduces to a theory with only two degrees of freedom described by a unit vector field \(\vec{n}(x)\), and the Lagrangian becomes that of the Maxwell field in dual representation. Coulomb and Lorentz forces are shown to be consequences of topology rather than being put in by hand.

## Method
The authors start from a previously proposed model of topological solitons (MTF) based on an SU(2) field \(Q(x)\) with a potential term that confines the field to an equatorial \(\mathrm{S}^2\) sphere. They take the limit \(r_0 \to 0\) where the soliton size vanishes, forcing the field to its asymptotic value \(Q = i\vec{\sigma}\vec{n}\) everywhere except at world-lines of charges. From this reduced \(\vec{n}\)-field they construct an Abelian field strength tensor via \(\vec{R}_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu\vec{n} \wedge \partial_\nu\vec{n}\) and identify it with the dual electromagnetic field. The equations of motion are derived by varying the action under the constraint \(\vec{n}^2 = 1\) using an \(x\)-dependent rotation of \(\vec{n}\).

## Key numbers
- The fine-structure constant \(\alpha_f = e_0^2 / (4\pi \varepsilon_0 \hbar c)\) appears in the Lagrangian prefactor.
- The total energy of a single soliton is \(E_1 = \alpha_f \hbar c \, \pi / (4 r_0)\), with \(\alpha_f \hbar c = 1.44\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}\).
- Comparing \(E_1\) to the electron mass \(m_e c^2 = 0.511\ \text{MeV}\) fixes \(r_0 = 2.21\ \text{fm}\), close to the classical electron radius \(\alpha_f \hbar / (m_e c) = 2.82\ \text{fm}\).
- The electric charge enclosed by a surface \(\mathcal{S}\) is \(Q_\mathrm{el}(\mathcal{S}) = -Z(\mathcal{S}) e_0\), where \(Z(\mathcal{S})\) is the topological winding number of the mapping \(\mathcal{S} \to \mathrm{S}^2_\mathrm{equ}\).

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model philosophy (Skyrme 1961; Witten; Adkins, Nappi, Witten; Manton, Ruback, Samols) to long-range electromagnetic forces, where the original Skyrme model fails because its energy density decays as \(r^{-6}\) and cannot describe Coulombic \(r^{-2}\) interactions. The authors build on their own earlier model (Faber 1999) of topological fermions with quantised electric charge. The electrodynamic limit connects this soliton model to standard Maxwell electrodynamics and to Dirac's theory of magnetic monopoles (Dirac 1948), but with the key difference that the magnetic currents here propagate at the speed of light and are not associated with massive magnetic charges. The results enable a topological understanding of charge quantisation, gauge invariance, and the origin of electromagnetic forces.
