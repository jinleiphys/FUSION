# Nuclear modification in the structure function $F_3$

> Nuclear shadowing in the structure function $F_3$ is investigated as a possible test of shadowing models. The $F_3$ shadowing is studied in two different theoretical models: a parton-recombination model with $Q^2$ rescaling and an aligned-jet model. We find that predictions in these models differ completely in the small $x$ region. It suggests that the $F_3$ shadowing could be used for discriminating among different models, which produce similar shadowing behavior in the structure function $F_2$.

# Nuclear modification in the structure function $F_3$

## Key claim
Nuclear shadowing in the structure function $F_3$ could discriminate between different shadowing models that produce similar behavior in $F_2$, because the two models studied predict completely opposite behavior at small $x$: one predicts antishadowing (ratio above unity) and the other predicts shadowing (ratio below unity). However, current experimental data are not accurate enough to rule out either model.

## Method
The authors computed the nuclear modification ratio $R_3 = F_3^A(x)/F_3^D(x)$ using two distinct theoretical approaches. The first model combined parton recombination with $Q^2$ rescaling, where nuclear structure functions are obtained by rescaling $Q^2$ in the nucleon structure function and adding parton recombination contributions. The second model was an aligned-jet model, an extension of vector-meson dominance, where the virtual photon transforms into a $q\bar q$ pair that interacts with the target nucleus. The authors then estimated experimental constraints by using $F_2^A/F_2^D$ data from SLAC for calcium at medium $x$ and applying baryon-number conservation to extrapolate into the small $x$ region.

## Key numbers
- The parton-recombination model with $Q^2$ rescaling predicts antishadowing at small $x$, with $R_3$ rising above unity, while the aligned-jet model predicts shadowing with $R_3$ below unity in the same region.
- The experimental constraint derived from SLAC $R_2^{Ca}$ data and baryon-number conservation allows about 6% antishadowing at $x = 0.1$ to $0.2$ as a possible lower bound.
- The shaded region in Fig. 1, representing the range of nuclear modification allowed by current data, spans from the upper bound (approximately the model 1 curve) to the lower bound (approximately the model 2 curve), with both models being acceptable within present knowledge.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear shadowing in $F_2$ (Kumano and collaborators, 1995) by proposing $F_3$ as a more discriminating observable. The authors note that various models, including vector-meson dominance and parton recombination approaches, produce similar $x$ dependence in $F_2$ and cannot be distinguished by existing data. The paper builds on earlier work by the same group on sea-quark and gluon distributions in nuclei (Kumano and Miyama, 1995) and on the aligned-jet model for $F_3$ (Ref. 4 in the paper). The results suggest that future measurements of $F_3^A/F_3^D$ at small $x$ could rule out one of the two extreme model classes, enabling progress in understanding nuclear modifications of valence-quark distributions.
