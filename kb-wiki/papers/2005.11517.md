# Froissart Bound, Diffraction Scattering of Hadrons and Scaling at Asymptotic Energies

> The Froissart bound on the total cross section is subjected to test against very high energy data. We have found no clear evidence for its violation. The scaling property of differential cross section in the diffraction region is investigated. It exhibits scaling in the ISR, SPS, Tevatron and LHC energy domain which had hitherto remained unexplored. The slope of the diffraction peak is fitted and the data are tested against the rigorous bounds.

## Key claim

The authors test the Froissart-Martin bound on total hadronic cross sections using high-energy data from ISR through LHC and cosmic rays, finding no clear evidence for its violation. The key hedge is that the fit exponent of the logarithm is determined to be α = 2.07 ± 0.04, which is consistent with the bound's log²(s) dependence within uncertainties, but the authors do not claim definitive proof of saturation.

## Method

The authors fit the combined pp and p̅p total cross section data from ISR energies (√s = 23.5 GeV) up to cosmic ray energies (95 TeV) using a three-parameter function σ_t = H log^α(s/s₀) + P, where the exponent α is left free to test the Froissart bound. They also investigate scaling of the differential cross section dσ/dt in the diffraction region using two scaling variables: τ₁ = |t|σ_t²/(16πσ_el) and τ₂ = |t|b(s), and fit the slope parameter b(s) with a log²(s) dependence.

## Key numbers

- Fitted exponent α = 2.07 ± 0.04 for the total cross section parameterization σ_t = H log^α(s/s₀) + P, with H = 0.22 ± 0.02 mb and P = 36.4 ± 0.3 mb (s₀ fixed at 16.00 GeV²)
- The ω-Regge pole contribution to σ_t at √s = 23.5 GeV is approximately 0.5 mb, while at √s = 8 TeV it is ≈ 0.001 mb (compared to total cross sections of ≈ 39 mb and ≈ 103 mb respectively)
- Scaling with τ₁ covers the range 0.02 ≤ τ₁ ≤ 3.6; scaling with τ₂ covers 0.05 ≤ τ₂ ≤ 9.0
- The lower bound on b^A(s) is given by (2/9)[σ_t²/(4πσ_el) − 1/k²] ≤ b^A(s)

## Context

This work extends the study of the Froissart-Martin bound (Froissart 1961, Martin 1963) and diffraction scaling to the full ISR-through-LHC energy range, which the authors note had remained unexplored for scaling phenomena. It addresses a prior claim of Froissart bound violation by Menon (year not specified), which was subsequently refuted by Block and Halzen (year not specified). The scaling analysis builds on the axiomatic field theory framework of Auberson, Kinoshita, and Martin (1971) and the group contraction approach of Singh and Roy (1970), as well as the rigorous scaling variable work of Cornille and Martin (1970). The slope parameter analysis tests the Martin-MacDowell lower bound (1970) and the Singh upper bound (year not specified).
