# Enhancement of octupole strength in near spherical nuclei

> The validity of the rotational formula used to compute E1 and E3 transition strengths in even-even nuclei is analyzed within the Generator Coordinate Method framework based on mean field wave functions. It turns out that those nuclei with spherical or near spherical shapes the E1 and E3 strengths computed with this formula are strongly underestimated and a sound evaluation of them requires angular momentum projected wave functions. Results for several isotopic chains with proton number equal or near magic numbers are analyzed and compared with experimental data. The use of angular momentum projected wave functions greatly improves the agreement with the scarce experimental data.

## Key claim

The rotational formula used to compute E1 and E3 transition strengths in even-even nuclei is not valid for spherical or near-spherical nuclei, where it systematically underestimates the strengths by factors of 2 to 4; a sound evaluation requires angular momentum projected wave functions, and the enhancement is crucial to improve agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The author used the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) intrinsic wave functions generated with the axially symmetric octupole moment Q30 as a constraint, employing the Gogny energy density functional with the D1S parametrization. Parity projection was applied to obtain states with good parity, and then angular momentum projection (AMP) was applied to the GCM wave functions to compute E1 and E3 transition strengths, comparing results with the standard rotational formula. Calculations were performed for isotopic chains of Fe, Ni, Zn, Cd, Sn, Te, Hg, Pb, and Po, with proton numbers at or near magic numbers 28, 50, and 82.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the B(E3, 3- → 0+) strength goes from 7.7 W.u. (Weisskopf units) with the rotational formula to 22 W.u. with angular momentum projection, compared to the experimental value of 34 W.u.
- For 64Zn, the B(E3) strength goes from 10^-4 W.u. (parity-projected RVAP) to 5.75 W.u. (GCM with AMP), compared to the experimental value of 20 ± 3 W.u.
- For 20Ne, the B(E3) strength is 12.31 W.u. with GCM+AMP, compared to the experimental value of 13 W.u.
- For 224Ra, the B(E3) strength is 61.51 W.u. with GCM+AMP, compared to the experimental value of 42 ± 3 W.u.
- For 158Gd, the B(E3) strength is 12.64 W.u. with GCM+AMP, compared to the experimental value of 12 W.u.
- In the Pb isotopic chain, the AMP results are a factor of about 3 larger than the rotational formula results across the whole chain.
- In the Sn chain near N=50, the enhancement factor grows up to nearly 5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of octupole correlations in even-even nuclei previously carried out using the GCM with the Gogny force (PhysRevC.84.054302), which had revealed a systematic overestimation of excitation energies and a pattern where the rotational formula worked well only for well-deformed nuclei. A prior analysis (PhysRevC.86.054306) had already noted that the rotational formula was invalid in spherical or weakly quadrupole-deformed systems, with a factor of up to 7 (2I+1 with I=3) needed. The present work enables more reliable theoretical predictions for E1 and E3 strengths in near-spherical nuclei and cautions against using the rotational formula in reverse to extract beta_3 deformation parameters from transition strengths in such systems.
