# ν\barν -Pair Synchrotron Emission in Neutron-Star Matter based on a Relativistic Quantum Approach

> We study the neutrino anti-neutrino pair synchrotron emission from electrons and protons in a relativistic quantum approach. This process occurs only under a strong magnetic field, and it is considered to be one of effective processes for neutron star cooling. In this work we calculate the luminosity of the neutrino anti-neutrino pairs emitted from neutron-star-matter with a magnetic field of about 10^{15} G. We find that the energy loss is much larger than that of the modified Urca process. The neutrino anti-neutrino pair emission processes in strong magnetic fields is expected to contribute significantly to the cooling of the magnetars.

## Key claim

The neutrino-antineutrino pair synchrotron emission process, calculated with a full relativistic quantum treatment that includes transitions between different Landau levels, yields an energy loss from neutron-star matter that is much larger than that of the modified Urca process for magnetic fields of about \(10^{15}\) G, but only in the low-density region (\(\rho_B \lesssim \rho_0\)); in the high-density region (\(\rho_B \gtrsim 3 \rho_0\)) the direct Urca process dominates.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for electrons and protons in a uniform magnetic field along the \(z\)-direction, including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for protons via a Pauli term. The weak interaction is treated with the standard \(V-A\) Lagrangian, and the differential decay width for the transition \(p (e^{-}) \rightarrow p (e^{-}) + \nu + \bar{\nu}\) is computed exactly, allowing transitions between different Landau levels (\(n_i \neq n_f\)) and spin states. The mean fields for protons are taken from relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory at zero temperature, and the neutrino luminosity per nucleon is evaluated for baryon densities from \(0.1 \rho_0\) to \(\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear matter density) and temperatures \(T \ll 1\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- At \(B = 10^{15}\) G and \(\rho_B = \rho_0\), the energy interval for the dominant proton transition is \(\Delta E \approx eB/E_F^* = 9.4\) keV, and the AMM contribution is \(e \kappa B/M = 11.30\) keV.
- For the spin-flip transition with \(\Delta n_{if} = 1\) at \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) and \(B = 10^{15}\) G, the neutrino-antineutrino pair energy is \(\Delta E \approx 21\) keV when \(s_i = -1 = -s_f\) and \(\Delta E \approx 7.5\) keV when \(s_i = +1 = -s_f\).
- At zero density and \(B = 10^{15}\) G, the energy interval for electrons is \(eB/m_e \approx 11.6\) MeV, while for protons it is \(eB/M \approx 6.3\) keV.
- The neutrino-antineutrino pair luminosity from protons without the AMM is about 500 to 1000 times larger than the spin non-flip contribution at \(B = 10^{15}\) G and \(\rho_B = \rho_0\).
- The surface temperature of magnetars is \(T \approx 0.28 - 0.72\) keV, compared to \(T \approx 0.01 - 0.15\) keV for normal neutron stars of similar age.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic quantum calculations of pion production via proton synchrotron radiation (Maruyama et al., 2018) and axion production (Maruyama et al., 2019) to the case of neutrino-antineutrino pair emission. It improves upon the semi-classical treatment of Landstreet (1967) for \(B \sim 10^{14}\) G and the work of van Dalen et al. (2000) for \(B \ge 10^{16}\) G, which only considered spin-flip transitions within the same Landau level. The results show that quantum calculations give much larger luminosities than semi-classical ones because the latter assume near-zero pair energies and cannot exactly account for momentum transfer from the magnetic field. The findings suggest that this process must be included in cooling models of magnetars, and that more careful calculations of neutrino transport including both heating and cooling effects are needed.
