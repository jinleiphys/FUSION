# Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction in a Quark Model

> A lecture given at the International School Seminar on {\sl Hadrons and Nuclei from QCD}, Tsuruga-Vladivostok-Sapporo, August-September, 1993. A realistic hyperon ($Y$)-nucleon ($N$) interaction based on the quark model and the one-boson-exchange potential is constructed. The Nijmegen potential model D with the SU(3) flavor symmetry is modified with a quark exchange interaction at the short-distance, which replaces the short-range repulsive core in the original model. The flavor-spin dependences of the short-range repulsion are qualitatively different from the original hard-core potential. We also study a two-body weak decay, $ΛN \to NN$, in the quark model. An effective weak interaction, where one-loop QCD corrections are explicitly taken into account, is employed. Differences from the conventional meson-exchange processes are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a realistic hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction by replacing the short-range repulsive core of the Nijmegen potential model D with a quark exchange interaction, but the resulting repulsion in two specific N-Sigma channels (S=0, I=1/2 and S=1, I=3/2) is as strong as the Nijmegen model F, which is known to provide insufficient binding for Sigma hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors combine a quark cluster model (QCM) using the resonating group method (RGM) for six valence quarks with the meson-exchange part of the Nijmegen potential model D, where the hard core is replaced by a quark exchange interaction derived from one-gluon exchange and the Pauli exclusion principle. The model parameters (quark mass, confinement strength, gluon coupling constant, and Gaussian size parameter) are fixed by fitting the N-N S-1S0 scattering phase shift, with the size parameter b=0.56 fm determined from that fit. For the weak decay study, an effective four-quark weak Hamiltonian with both |Delta I|=1/2 and |Delta I|=3/2 components is employed, including one-loop QCD corrections.

## Key numbers

- Light quark mass m_q = 383.7 MeV (one-third of the average octet baryon mass)
- Light/strange quark mass ratio m_q/m_s = 0.6 (fixed from Lambda-Sigma mass difference)
- Gluon coupling constant alpha_s = 1.85 (chosen to reproduce N-Delta mass difference)
- Confinement strength a = 20.8 MeV/fm
- Gaussian size parameter b = 0.56 fm (determined from NN S-1S0 scattering data)
- Normalization kernel eigenvalues: N-Sigma (S=0, I=1/2) e=1/9; N-Sigma (S=1, I=3/2) e=2/9; N-Lambda (S=0, I=1/2) e=1; N-Lambda (S=1, I=1/2) e=1; N-Sigma (S=0, I=3/2) e=10/9; N-Sigma (S=1, I=1/2) e=1
- Effective core radii: N-Sigma (S=0, I=1/2) 0.68 fm; N-Sigma (S=1, I=3/2) 0.67 fm; N-Lambda (S=0, I=1/2) 0.40 fm; N-Lambda (S=1, I=1/2) 0.34 fm; N-Sigma (S=1, I=1/2) 0.30 fm; N-Sigma (S=0, I=3/2) 0.48 fm
- Final relative momentum of NN from Lambda N decay at rest: about 420 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the quark cluster model approach of Oka and Yazaki (1980) and the Nijmegen potential models (Nijmegen group) by combining short-range quark exchange dynamics with long-range meson exchange, specifically modifying the Nijmegen model D. The weak decay study addresses discrepancies in the meson-exchange picture of nonmesonic hypernuclear decay, particularly the n-p ratio R_np predicted to be about 0.1 in meson-exchange models versus experimental values near 1 in light hypernuclei, and questions about the |Delta I|=1/2 rule raised by analysis of A=3 and 4 hypernuclei decays (Shoeb). The work enables future calculations of YN scattering and hypernuclear binding with a more microscopically motivated short-range interaction, and provides a framework for testing direct quark weak processes against conventional meson-exchange mechanisms.
