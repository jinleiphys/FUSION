# Spatial imaging of polarized deuterons at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We study diffractive vector meson production at small-$x$ in the collision of electrons and polarized deuterons $e+d^{\uparrow}$. We consider the polarization dependence of the nuclear wave function of the deuteron, which results in an azimuthal angular dependence of the produced vector meson when the deuteron is transversely polarized. The Fourier coefficients extracted from the azimuthal angular dependence of the vector meson differential cross-section exhibit notable differences between longitudinally and transversely polarized deuterons. The angular dependence of the extracted effective deuteron radius provides direct insight into the structure of the polarized deuteron wave function. Furthermore, we observe slightly increased gluon saturation effects when the deuteron is longitudinally polarized compared to the transversely polarized case. The small-$x$ observables studied in this work will be accessible at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The azimuthal angular dependence of exclusive vector meson production in electron-deuteron collisions at small-$x$ can image the spatial structure of polarized deuterons, with the Fourier coefficients and effective radius showing notable differences between longitudinally and transversely polarized deuterons; however, the observed gluon saturation effects are weak (within 3%) because the deuteron comprises only two nucleons.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent diffractive vector meson (J/ψ and ρ) photoproduction cross-sections in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using the dipole picture where a virtual photon splits into a quark-antiquark pair that scatters eikonally off the small-$x$ gluon field of the deuteron. The nucleon positions in the deuteron are sampled according to polarization-dependent squared wave functions (Eqs. 4-5) using S-wave and D-wave radial functions from the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential, with the D-wave weight around 6%. The color charge densities are obtained from the IP-Glasma initial state description, using the MAP parameter set from a Bayesian analysis constrained by HERA exclusive J/ψ data.

## Key numbers

- The D-wave part contributes approximately 6% of the probability distribution in the deuteron wave function parametrization
- At |t| = 0.1 GeV², the coherent J/ψ photoproduction cross-section with j_z = ±1 is approximately twice that with j_z = 0 for transversely polarized deuterons
- The effective transverse radius R is extracted by fitting the coherent t-spectra with dσ/dt ∼ e^{-B_D|t|} within 0 < |t| < 0.3 GeV²
- The longitudinal-to-transverse polarization ratio of the coherent ρ cross-section at |t| = 0 for j_z = ±1 is consistently below 1, with the magnitude within 3%
- The Fourier coefficients a₂ and a₄ are extracted as functions of |t|, with a₂ showing opposite signs for j_z = 0 and j_z = ±1 at low |t| < 0.5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends studies of polarized observables in small-$x$ electron-proton and proton-proton collisions (Kovchegov 2012, Brodsky 2013, Hatta 2016) to electron-deuteron collisions, building on previous hydrodynamic simulations of polarized deuteron-nucleus collisions (Bozek 2018, Broniowski 2019). The results demonstrate that exclusive vector meson production at the future Electron-Ion Collider can image the spatial distribution of small-$x$ gluons within polarized deuterons, complementing studies of deuteron Generalized Parton Distributions (Cosyn 2018) and providing essential inputs for models of deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC (STAR 2008, PHENIX 2017).
