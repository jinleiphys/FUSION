# Effect of the crust on neutron star empirical relations

> We analyze how the crust equation of state affects several neutron star properties and how it impacts on possible constraints inferred from astrophysical observations. Using three distinct crusts, we generate three sets of model-independent equations of state describing stellar matter from a Taylor expansion around saturation density. The equations of state are thermodynamically consistent, causal, and compatible with astrophysical observations. The relations between the tidal deformability $Λ$ and compactness $C$, Love number $k_2$ and radius of neutron star with mass $M$ are studied, and the effect of the crust equation of state on these relations analyzed. In most of the relations, the impact of the crust equation of state is not larger that 2\%. If, however, a fixed neutron star mass is considered, the relation between the tidal deformability and the radius depends on the crust. We have found that the relation $Λ_{M_i} = αR_{M_i}^β$ becomes almost exact and crust independent for massive neutron stars. It is shown that it is possible to determine the tidal deformability of an 1.4$M_\odot$ star from the GW179817 effective tidal deformability $\tildeΛ$ with an accuracy of at least $\approx 10\%$. A high correlation between $\tildeΛ$ and the radius of the most massive star of the neutron star binary was confirmed, however, it was demonstrated that the crust has an effect of $\approx 14\%$ on this relation. We have found that the relation $Λ_1/Λ_2=q^a$ depends on $M_{\text{chirp}}$ as $a\sim \sqrt{M_{\text{chirp}}}$.

## Key claim

The crust equation of state (EoS) affects most neutron star empirical relations by no more than 2%, but for fixed neutron star mass the relation between tidal deformability and radius depends on the crust; the relation Λ_{M_i} = α R_{M_i}^β becomes almost exact and crust-independent only for massive neutron stars.

## Method

The authors generated three sets of model-independent EoS by Taylor expanding the energy per particle of homogeneous nuclear matter around saturation density up to fourth order, using a multivariate Gaussian sampling of eight empirical parameters (E_sym, L_sym, K_sat, K_sym, Q_sat, Q_sym, Z_sat, Z_sym). Each core EoS was matched via Maxwell construction to one of three distinct inner crust EoS (SLy4, NL3, DDHd) requiring the crust-core transition below 0.10 fm^{-3}. The resulting EoS sets were filtered for thermodynamic stability, causality, and support of a maximum mass ≥ 1.97 M_⊙, yielding 2158 (SLy4), 5167 (NL3), and 1956 (DDHd) valid models.

## Key numbers

- For the relation Λ ∼ C^{-6}, the exponent deviates from -6 by less than 2% across all three crusts, with correlation coefficients of 0.998 (NL3 and SLy4) and 0.995 (DDHd)
- For Λ_{1.4 M_⊙} = α R_{1.4 M_⊙}^β, the exponent β = 7.25 (SLy4), 7.33 (NL3), and 5.98 (DDHd), showing crust dependence
- For M ≥ 1.8 M_⊙, the correlation between Λ and R reaches 0.99 for NL3 and SLy4 crusts, with Residual Standard Error on R of 0.05 km at 1.9 M_⊙ (NL3 crust)
- The tidal deformability of a 1.4 M_⊙ star can be determined from the GW170817 effective tidal deformability Λ̃ with accuracy of at least ≈10%
- The crust has an effect of ≈14% on the relation between Λ̃ and the radius of the most massive star in the binary
- For k_2 versus compactness C, the power-law exponent is -1.122 (NL3), -1.025 (DDHd), and -1.137 (SLy4) with correlations of 0.933, 0.860, and 0.941 respectively
- Mean radii for 1.4 M_⊙ stars: 12.24 km (NL3), 12.46 km (DDHd), 12.18 km (SLy4)
- Mean Λ_{1.4 M_⊙} values: 476.89 (NL3), 498.89 (DDHd), 469.76 (SLy4)

## Context

This work extends the Taylor-expansion approach of Margueron (2018a, 2018b) and the universal relation studies of De (2018) and Annala (2018) by systematically testing the impact of three different inner crust EoS on neutron star empirical relations. The authors use crust models from Douchin (2001) for SLy4 and from Avancini (2008) and Grill (2012, 2014) for NL3 and DDHd crusts, which have very different symmetry energy density dependences. The results enable more precise inference of neutron star properties from gravitational-wave events like GW170817, particularly showing that the Λ-R relation becomes nearly exact for massive stars (≥1.8 M_⊙) and that the crust EoS must be considered when interpreting fixed-mass relations for lower-mass stars.
