# New $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As reaction rates corresponding to the temperature regime of thermonuclear X-ray bursts

> We compute the $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As thermonuclear reaction rates using the latest experimental input supplemented with theoretical nuclear spectroscopic information. The experimental input consists of the latest proton thresholds of $^{64}$Ge and $^{65}$As, and the nuclear spectroscopic information of $^{65}$As, whereas the theoretical nuclear spectroscopic information for $^{64}$Ge and $^{65}$As are deduced from the full pf-shell space configuration-interaction shell-model calculations with the GXPF1A Hamiltonian. Both thermonuclear reaction rates are determined with known uncertainties at the energies that correspond to the Gamow windows of the temperature regime relevant to Type I X-ray bursts, covering the typical temperature range of the thermonuclear runaway of the GS 1826$-$24 periodic bursts and SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion bursts.

## Key claim

The authors compute thermonuclear reaction rates for 63Ga(p,γ)64Ge and 64Ge(p,γ)65As using the latest experimental proton thresholds and nuclear spectroscopic information, supplemented by full pf-shell configuration-interaction shell-model calculations with the GXPF1A Hamiltonian, but the rates are determined with known uncertainties only at energies corresponding to the Gamow windows of Type I X-ray bursts, not across all possible astrophysical conditions.

## Method

The resonant reaction rates were calculated by combining experimental proton thresholds for 64Ge and 65As with theoretical nuclear spectroscopic information for 64Ge and 65As obtained from full pf-shell space configuration-interaction shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian. For each resonance state, the authors computed the resonance energy, proton spectroscopic factors (C²S) for different orbital angular momenta, gamma width (Γγ), proton width (Γp), and resonance strength (ωγ). The rates were evaluated over the temperature range relevant to Type I X-ray bursts, specifically covering the thermonuclear runaway conditions of GS 1826-24 periodic bursts and SAX J1808.4-3658 photospheric radius expansion bursts.

## Key numbers

- The 63Ga(p,γ)64Ge resonant rate calculation includes states with excitation energies (Ex) ranging from 5.118 MeV to 7.014 MeV in 64Ge, with resonance energies (Eres) from 0.061 MeV to 1.957 MeV.
- Resonance strengths (ωγ) for individual states in the 63Ga(p,γ)64Ge reaction span from 4.192×10⁻⁴⁵ eV (for the 1⁺₈ state at Ex=5.118 MeV) to 2.801×10⁻¹ eV (for the 5⁺₃₉ state at Ex=6.958 MeV).
- The temperature regime covered corresponds to the typical range of thermonuclear X-ray bursts, specifically the periodic bursts of GS 1826-24 and the photospheric radius expansion bursts of SAX J1808.4-3658.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton capture reactions on neutron-deficient nuclei in the A≈60-70 mass region, which are important for understanding the rp-process in Type I X-ray bursts. The use of the GXPF1A Hamiltonian for full pf-shell calculations provides improved theoretical spectroscopic information compared to earlier approaches that relied on simpler nuclear models. The computed rates enable more accurate modeling of the thermonuclear runaway in X-ray burst systems such as GS 1826-24 and SAX J1808.4-3658, and can be used in future astrophysical simulations to compare with observational burst light curves and spectra.
