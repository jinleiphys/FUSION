# Empirical formula applied to the lowest excitation energies of the natural parity odd multipole states in even-even nuclei

> We applied our recently proposed empirical formula, a formula quite successful in describing essential trends of the lowest excitation energies of the natural parity even multipole states, to the lowest excitation energies of the natural parity odd multipole states in even-even nuclei throughout the entire periodic table. Even though the systematic behavior of the lowest excitation energies of odd multipole states is quite different from those of even multipole states, we have shown that the same empirical formula also holds reasonably well for the odd multipole states with the exception of a few certain instances.

# Empirical formula applied to the lowest excitation energies of the natural parity odd multipole states in even-even nuclei

## Key claim
The same empirical formula previously successful for natural parity even multipole states also describes the overall trend of lowest excitation energies for natural parity odd multipole states (1⁻ through 9⁻) in even-even nuclei across the periodic table, with the exception of two specific mass regions where octupole deformation dominates.

## Method
The authors applied a six-parameter empirical formula depending only on mass number A, valence proton number Nₚ, and valence neutron number Nₙ to fit measured lowest excitation energies of 1⁻, 3⁻, 5⁻, 7⁻, and 9⁻ states in even-even nuclei. Parameters were determined by minimizing the χ² value of the logarithmic error between calculated and experimental energies, using data from Raman et al. (2001), Kibédi et al. (2002), and the Table of Isotopes (Firestone et al., 1996), with additional validation against the ENSDF database.

## Key numbers
- The empirical formula Eₓ = αA⁻ᵞ + βₚ exp(−λₚ Nₚ) + βₙ exp(−λₙ Nₙ) was fitted with six parameters for each multipole.
- Two groups of nuclei deviate from the formula: those with mass numbers A = 220–232 (proton numbers 86–92) and A = 144–152 (neutron numbers 86–92).
- The dispersion σ of the logarithmic error for odd multipole states was comparable to that for even multipole states, as shown in Table 2 of the paper.
- The recalculated χ² values after including additional ENSDF data are listed in parentheses in Table 1.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier empirical formula (Jin, Ha, Kim, 2005–2007) from natural parity even multipole states (2⁺ through 10⁺) to odd multipole states, despite the qualitatively different systematic behavior of odd multipole excitation energies. The formula provides a landscape description analogous to the Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula (Weizsäcker, 1935) but for excited states. The two deviating mass regions coincide with nuclei known from prior work (Cottle, 1993; Nazarewicz, 1994; Ahmad, 1999) to exhibit octupole deformation, suggesting the formula can identify regions of strong octupole collectivity. The NₚNₙ scaling pattern (Casten, 1985) previously established for even multipole states is shown to hold for odd multipole states as well, except in octupole-deformed nuclei.
