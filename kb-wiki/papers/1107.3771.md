# Entanglement of Quasielastic Scattering and Pion Production

> The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters requires the determination of the neutrino energy from observations of the hadronic final state. Here we discuss the difficulties connected with this energy reconstruction for the ongoing experiments MiniBooNE and T2K. We point out that a lower limit to the uncertainty in the reconstructed energy from Fermi motion alone amounts to about 15%. The entanglement of very different elementary processes, in this case quasielastic scattering and pion production, in the actual observables leads to considerably larger errors. We discuss the sensitivity of the energy reconstruction to detection techniques and experimental acceptances. We also calculate the misidentification cross section for electron appearance in the T2K experiment due to neutral pion production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the intrinsic uncertainty in reconstructing neutrino energy from quasielastic-like events is at least 16% due to Fermi motion alone, and that the entanglement of quasielastic scattering with pion production in actual observables raises this uncertainty to 20-25%, which is larger than previously discussed values.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model, which treats the neutrino's first interaction with a bound nucleon via the impulse approximation and then simulates complex hadronic final-state interactions. They compared two experimental identification methods for quasielastic-like events: the Cherenkov detector method (requiring a muon and no pions) and the tracking detector method (requiring a muon, one proton, and no pions). They then reconstructed the neutrino energy using quasifree kinematics on a nucleon at rest, applied to both true quasielastic events and quasielastic-like event samples from GiBUU simulations.

## Key numbers

- Fermi motion alone gives a lower limit on the reconstructed energy uncertainty of about 16% (at 1 GeV neutrino energy).
- For a Cherenkov detector, the quasielastic-like cross section is about 20% higher than the true CCQE cross section; for a tracking detector, it is about 20% lower.
- For a tracking detector with a proton kinetic energy threshold of 175 MeV (SciFi detector), the ratio of quasielastic-like to true quasielastic events is about 50%; for a threshold of 75 MeV (T2K tracking detector), the ratio is about 70%.
- Including pion production events raises the rms energy-width to about 22% at a realistic pion detection threshold of about 100 MeV kinetic energy.
- The total cross section for misidentified neutral pion events in T2K is 0.09 x 10^-38 cm^2, which is 26% of the true pion events.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrino energy reconstruction challenges discussed by Harris (2004), Tanaka (2009), and Fernandez Martinez (2010), and follows closely the presentation of Leitner (2010). The paper shows that the impulse approximation may not describe the full total neutrino cross section in the quasielastic region, as recently shown by Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011), who found that MiniBooNE's identification method allows significant 2p-2h excitations that are not removed by event generators. The results imply that event generators must be reliable across a broad class of reactions, and that the inherent energy uncertainty of about 16% from Fermi motion alone sets a fundamental limit on neutrino oscillation parameter extraction.
