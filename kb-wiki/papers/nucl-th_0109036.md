# Fully Self-consistent RPA description of the many level pairing model

> The Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) equations in the particle-particle channel are solved without any approximation for the picket fence model. The results are in excellent agreement with the exact solutions found with the Richardson method. Particularly interesting features are that screening corrections reverse the sign of the interaction and that SCRPA yields the exact energies in the case of two levels with two particles.

## Key claim

The Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) equations in the particle-particle channel are solved without any approximation for the picket fence model, yielding results in excellent agreement with exact Richardson solutions; the method gives exact energies for the special case of two levels with two particles, and screening corrections reverse the sign of the interaction.

## Method

The SCRPA equations were derived from a variational principle minimizing the average two-particle excitation energy, leading to RPA-like equations for addition and removal phonon operators. The expectation values of occupation numbers and two-body correlation functions appearing in the RPA matrix were expressed exactly in terms of the forward and backward amplitudes X and Y, using the vacuum condition that the phonon operators annihilate the SCRPA ground state. A system of linear equations for the two-body expectation values ⟨M_i M_j⟩ was constructed and solved numerically, fully closing the SCRPA equations without any approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the picket fence model with Ω total levels, half-filled with N = Ω/2 pairs, the chemical potential is set to λ = ε(N + 1/2) − G/2 to preserve particle-hole symmetry.
- The single-particle energies are ε_i = i ε, with particle states ε_p = ε(N + p) and hole states ε_h = ε(N − h + 1), where p, h = 1, …, N.
- The SCRPA ground state energy is ⟨H⟩ = −ε N² + Σ_{p=h=1}^{N} [ε(p − 1/2) + G/2] (⟨M_p⟩ + ⟨M_h⟩) − G Σ_{pp′} ⟨Q_p† Q_{p′}⟩ − G Σ_{hh′} ⟨Q_h† Q_{h′}⟩ + G Σ_{ph} (⟨Q_p† Q_h† + Q_p Q_h⟩).
- The SCRPA correlation energy is E^{SCRPA}_{corr} = ⟨H⟩ + ε N².
- For Ω = 2, the SCRPA ground state coefficients are a_h = X / √(X² + Y²) and a_p = Y / √(X² + Y²), giving exact correlation and excitation energies.
- The sum rule Σ_{pp′} ⟨M_p M_{p′}⟩ = Σ_{hp} ⟨M_h M_p⟩ must be satisfied for number conservation.

## Context

This work extends the SCRPA approach previously applied to the many-level pairing model in Dukelsky et al. (1999), where the approximation ⟨M_i M_j⟩ ≈ ⟨M_i⟩⟨M_j⟩ was used; here that approximation is removed, providing the first fully self-consistent solution. The method is contrasted with the renormalized RPA (r-RPA) of Catara et al. (1994, 1996) and Toivanen et al. (1995), which uses simpler Pauli-principle corrections, and with standard RPA. The exact solution enables assessment of the accuracy of these simpler approximations and demonstrates that SCRPA can describe the smooth superfluid-to-normal transition without any abrupt phase transition, as previously shown by Dukelsky et al. (1999).
