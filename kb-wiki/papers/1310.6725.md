# Universal Properties of the Langevin Diffusion Coefficients

> We show that in generic isotropic holographic theories the longitudinal Langevin diffusion coefficient along the string motion is larger compared to that of the transverse direction. We argue that this is a universal relation and we derive the generic conditions in order to be satisfied. A way to violate the relation is to consider anisotropic gauge/gravity dualities. We give an explicit example of this violation where the noise along the transverse direction is larger than the noise occurring along the quark motion. Moreover, we derive the effective world-sheet temperature for any generic theory and then the conditions for negative excess noise. We argue that isotropic theories can not have negative excess noise and we additionally remark that these conditions are difficult to get satisfied, indicating positivity of the excess noise, even in a large class of anisotropic holographic theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for generic isotropic holographic theories, the longitudinal Langevin diffusion coefficient is universally larger than the transverse coefficient, but this inequality can be violated in anisotropic theories where the transverse noise may become larger than the longitudinal noise.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to study the stochastic motion of a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled plasma. They derive generic formulas for the Langevin diffusion coefficients from the fluctuations of a trailing string in a diagonal background metric, using the membrane paradigm to extract transport coefficients at the world-sheet horizon. The ratio of longitudinal to transverse diffusion coefficients is expressed in terms of the metric elements and analyzed for isotropic and anisotropic backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- For the space-dependent axion anisotropic theory, the ratio of diffusion coefficients is given by \(\frac{\kappa_\parallel}{\kappa_\perp} = \gamma^2 + \frac{a^2}{24\pi^2 T^2} B(\gamma) + \mathcal{O}(\frac{a^4}{T^4})\), where \(a\) is the anisotropy parameter, \(T\) is the temperature, and \(\gamma = (1-v^2)^{-1/2}\)
- The function \(B(\gamma)\) is defined as \(B(\gamma) = (\gamma-1)(4+\gamma+2\gamma^2) - 2\gamma^2(2+\gamma^2)\log(1+\gamma^{-1})\)
- The world-sheet temperature is given by \(T_{ws}^2 = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left| \frac{1}{G_{00} G_{uu}} (G_{00} G_{pp})' \left(\frac{G_{00}}{G_{pp}}\right)' \right|_{u=u_0}\)

## Context

This work extends observations from Gursoy (2010) that the inequality \(\kappa_\parallel > \kappa_\perp\) holds for particular classes of backgrounds, and generalizes this to a universal claim for all isotropic holographic theories including those with Lifshitz scalings. The violation of the inequality in anisotropic theories is analogous to the violation of the shear viscosity over entropy density bound studied by Rebhan (2011). The paper enables future studies of negative excess noise in anisotropic holographic systems, though the authors note that such negative noise is difficult to realize and is not found in the axion-deformed anisotropic theory of Mateos (2011).
