# Study of Transverse Spherocity and Azimuthal Anisotropy in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV using A Multi-Phase Transport Model

> Transverse spherocity is an event shape observable having a very unique capability to separate the events based on their geometrical shapes. Recent results from experiments at the LHC suggest that transverse spherocity is an important event classifier in small collision systems. In this work, we use transverse spherocity for the first time in heavy-ion collisions and perform an extensive study on azimuthal anisotropy of charged particles produced in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV using A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT). The azimuthal anisotropy is estimated using the 2-particle correlation method, which suppresses the non-flow effects significantly with an appropriate pseudorapidity gap of particle pairs. The results from AMPT are compared with estimations from PYTHIA8 (Angantyr) model and it is found that with the chosen pseudorapidity gap the residual non-flow effects become negligible. We found that the high spherocity events have nearly zero elliptic flow while low spherocity events contribute significantly to elliptic flow of spherocity-integrated events. Our studies indicate that using transverse spherocity in heavy-ion collisions, one can enhance and/or suppress the collective effects.

## Key claim

Transverse spherocity, previously used only in small collision systems, is shown for the first time in heavy-ion collisions to successfully separate events by geometrical shape, with high-spherocity events exhibiting nearly zero elliptic flow while low-spherocity events dominate the elliptic flow of spherocity-integrated events, but this conclusion is drawn from a specific transport model (AMPT) with default parameter settings, not from experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the String Melting version of the AMPT model (version 2.26t7) with default settings to generate Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, applying transverse spherocity as an event-shape classifier with 20% cuts on the spherocity distribution to define low-S0 and high-S0 event classes. Elliptic flow (v2) was calculated using the two-particle correlation method with a pseudorapidity gap of 2 < |Δη| < 4.8 to suppress non-flow effects, and results were compared with the PYTHIA8 (Angantyr) model, which lacks collective flow, to verify that residual non-flow contributions were negligible.

## Key numbers

- Transverse spherocity distributions were computed for charged particles with pT > 0.15 GeV/c and |η| < 0.8, requiring a minimum of 5 charged particles per event.
- For 40-50% centrality, the low-S0 cut was 0–0.716 and the high-S0 cut was 0.865–1.
- Two-particle elliptic flow coefficients v2,2 were calculated for particle pairs in the transverse momentum range 0.5 to 5 GeV/c.
- For spherocity-integrated events in 40-50% centrality, v2 reached its maximum value among centrality classes; high-S0 events showed nearly zero v2,2 across all centralities.
- Comparison with ALICE data (Adam 2016) for spherocity-integrated events showed that default AMPT settings give a good prediction for experimental v2(pT) within uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the use of transverse spherocity from small collision systems (pp and p-Pb), where it was introduced by Cuautle (2014, 2015) and Ortiz (2017), to heavy-ion collisions for the first time. It complements the event-shape engineering approach based on the flow vector used by ATLAS (Aad 2015) and provides a baseline for future experimental studies at RHIC and the LHC. The results enable experimentalists to use spherocity as a tool to enhance or suppress collective effects in heavy-ion collisions, similar to its demonstrated capability in pp collisions for strangeness enhancement studies by the ALICE collaboration (Tripathy 2020).
