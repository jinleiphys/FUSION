# Universal Scaling of $p_T$ Distribution of Particles in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> With the experimental data from STAR, PHENIX and BRAHMS on the centrality and rapidity dependence of the $p_T$ spectrum in Au+Au and d+Au collisions, we show that there exists a scaling distribution which is independent of the colliding system, centrality and rapidity. The parameter for the average transverse momentum $<p_T>$ increases from peripheral to central d+Au collisions. This increase accounts for the enhancement of particle production in those collisions. A non-extensive entropy is used to derive the scaling function.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a single scaling function \(\Psi(u)\) can describe the transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) distribution of charged particles and pions across Au+Au, d+Au, and p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, independent of colliding system, centrality, and rapidity. The hedge is that this universality holds only when the distribution is normalized by the average transverse momentum \(\langle p_T\rangle\), which itself varies with centrality and rapidity and encodes all dynamical differences.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental data from STAR, PHENIX, and BRAHMS for Au+Au and d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They defined a normalized probability distribution \(\Phi(p_T, y, \beta) = 2\pi E d^3N/dp^3 / (dN/dy)\) and a scaling variable \(u = p_T / \langle p_T\rangle\), then constructed the scaling function \(\Psi(u) = \langle p_T\rangle^2 \Phi(\langle p_T\rangle u, y, \beta)\). They fitted \(\Psi(u)\) to a parameterized form and tested its universality by collapsing data from different centralities, rapidities, and colliding systems onto a single curve. A non-extensive Tsallis entropy was used to derive a functional form for \(\Psi(u)\) from the principle of maximum entropy.

## Key numbers
- The scaling function is parameterized as \(\Psi(u) = 2.1 \times 10^4 (u^2 + 11.65)^{-4.8} [1 + 25 \exp(-1.864 u)]\).
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\eta=2.6\), the fitted \(\langle p_T\rangle\) ranges from 0.4340 GeV (0-10% centrality) to 0.4366 GeV (40-60% centrality).
- For d+Au collisions at mid-rapidity (PHENIX data), \(\langle p_T\rangle\) increases from 0.563 GeV (0-20% centrality) to 0.601 GeV (60-88% centrality).
- For d+Au collisions at \(y=3\) (\(\pi^-\)), \(\langle p_T\rangle\) decreases from 0.3228 GeV (0-20% centrality) to 0.3108 GeV (60-80% centrality).
- The ratio \(\langle p_T^n\rangle / \langle p_T\rangle^n\) is predicted to be constant: 1.65 for \(n=2\), 4.08 for \(n=3\), and 14.39 for \(n=4\).
- The Tsallis entropy parameter \(q\) is derived as \(q = 0.924\) from the fitted exponent \(n=13.2\).
- The enhancement factor \(Q_{\rm CP}\) for d+Au collisions reaches about 1.33, indicating a 30% enhancement of high-\(p_T\) pions.

## Context
This work extends the scaling behavior previously found for pions in mid-rapidity Au+Au collisions by Hwa and Yang (2003) to non-central rapidities and to d+Au and p+p collisions, demonstrating a universal scaling function. It contradicts the traditional two-component model of soft and hard processes by showing that both can be united in a single scaling function, implying that hard processes contribute coherently to \(\langle p_T\rangle\). The result enables model-independent definitions of suppression/enhancement measures like \(Q_{\rm CP}\) and predicts constant ratios of \(\langle p_T^n\rangle\) moments that can be tested experimentally. The derivation using Tsallis entropy suggests the produced particle system behaves as a non-extensive fractal system, independent of the colliding system.
