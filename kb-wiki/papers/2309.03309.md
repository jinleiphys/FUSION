# Tetraquark mass relations in quark and diquark models

> We present new linear relations among the masses of S-wave tetraquarks with either one flavour ($QQ \bar Q \bar Q$) or two ($QQ\bar q \bar q$). Because the relations are sensitive to the hidden-colour, spin, and spatial degrees of freedom, comparison to experimental data can help to reveal the internal structure of tetraquarks, and discriminate among different theoretical models. Depending on the model, the relations are either exact, or valid in perturbation theory, and a thorough comparison with existing literature confirms their validity at the MeV level. Additionally, we explore the connections among tetraquark models, and show how those with effective (quark or diquark) masses are related to dynamical potential models. We also show how the spectrum of diquark models is effectively a limiting case of (more general) quark models, and in particular, that the diquark concept is most relevant in the particular combination $QQ\bar q \bar q$, where $Q$ is much heavier than $\bar q$.

## Key claim

The paper presents new linear mass relations among S-wave tetraquark states that are either exact (in diquark models) or valid in perturbation theory (in quark models), with the caveat that the quark-model relations require an additional symmetry assumption about the spatial wavefunction being totally symmetric under quark-antiquark interchange.

## Method

The authors derive mass formulae for S-wave tetraquarks with one flavour ($QQ\bar Q \bar Q$) or two flavours ($QQ\bar q \bar q$) using the chromomagnetic interaction Hamiltonian, evaluating it in both the full colour basis (quark models, including colour sextet configurations) and the truncated colour-triplet basis (diquark models). They then show how these effective-mass models relate to dynamical potential models through perturbation theory, and extract linear relations among the masses of tensor, axial, and scalar states that are independent of or only weakly dependent on model parameters.

## Key numbers

- For diquark models, the linear relation $M_1 = \frac{1}{3}(2M_0 + M_2)$ holds independently of all model parameters.
- The dimensionless ratio $R = 2C_{Q\bar q}/(C_{QQ}+C_{qq})$ for two-flavour states takes values in the range $0 < R < 1$, while for one-flavour states $R = 1$.
- For one-flavour states with $R=1$, the mixing parameter $\Delta = \sqrt{232R^2+8R+1} = \sqrt{241}$.
- In the limit of small $R$ (heavy $Q$ much heavier than $\bar q$), the lighter scalar mass $M_0 \approx \mathcal{M} + \frac{8}{3}(C_{QQ}+C_{qq})(1-2R)$ reproduces the diquark model result.

## Context

This work extends the tradition of symmetry-based mass relations for hadrons, analogous to the Gell-Mann–Okubo formula for conventional mesons and baryons, and the linear relations among P-wave $Q\bar Q$ meson masses that are satisfied experimentally to less than 1 MeV (Voloshin 2007, Burns 2011). The relations can discriminate between quark models (which predict two scalar states) and diquark models (which predict one), and are directly applicable to recent experimental candidates for $cc\bar c \bar c$ states observed by LHCb (2020), CMS (2023), and ATLAS (2023), as well as to $bb\bar q \bar q'$ and $cc\bar q \bar q'$ tetraquarks studied in lattice QCD and models by Francis (2016), Bicudo (2016), Karliner (2017), and others.
