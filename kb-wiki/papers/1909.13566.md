# Effects of curvature of the symmetry energy in Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon

> Based on the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, the isotope Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon are investigated. It is shown that nucleon and meson observables in the Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon cannot effectively probe the high-density symmetry energy. These observables, however, are sensitive to the curvature of the symmetry energy. It thus sheds light on the study of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy by extracting the curvature of the symmetry energy through the interpretation of forthcoming RIBF/RIKEN related data.

## Key claim

Nucleon and meson observables in Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon cannot effectively probe the high-density symmetry energy directly, but they are sensitive to the curvature parameter \(K_{sym}\) of the symmetry energy at saturation density; if the slope \(L\) is already constrained, extracting \(K_{sym}\) from these observables can indirectly constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock initial density distributions and parameterized high-momentum tails. They fixed the symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)=30\) MeV and the slope \(L=40\) MeV (and later \(L=80\) MeV) while varying the curvature \(K_{sym}\) between 0 and -400 MeV using a density-dependent \(x\) parameter in the single-nucleon potential. They simulated semi-central and central collisions of neutron-rich \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn and neutron-deficient \(^{108}\)Sn+\(^{112}\)Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon and computed the squeezed-out free n/p ratio, the double n/p ratio, and the double \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio as functions of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)=31.7\pm3.2\) MeV, \(L=58.7\pm28.1\) MeV, and \(-400\text{ MeV}<K_{sym}<100\) MeV are the current constraints on symmetry energy parameters at saturation density.
- The simulations fix \(E_{sym}(\rho_0)=30\) MeV and \(L=40\) MeV (or \(L=80\) MeV) and compare \(K_{sym}=0\) MeV with \(K_{sym}=-400\) MeV.
- The squeezed-out n/p ratio in semi-central \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter \(b=4.5\) fm, azimuthal angle cuts \(60^{\circ}\leq\phi\leq120^{\circ}\) and \(240^{\circ}\leq\phi\leq300^{\circ}\), and rapidity cuts \(|(y/y_{beam})_{c.m.}|\leq0.5\) shows sensitivity to \(K_{sym}\) at high transverse momenta.
- The double ratio of squeezed-out n/p nucleons in \(^{132}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn and \(^{108}\)Sn+\(^{112}\)Sn reactions retains sensitivity to \(K_{sym}\) in both semi-central and central collisions.
- The double \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratio in central reactions also shows sensitivity to \(K_{sym}\), with greater sensitivity to the low-density part of the symmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of the high-density symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions, following the finding by Yong (2019) that Sn+Sn reactions at 270 MeV/nucleon cannot directly probe high-density symmetry energy. It builds on constraints of the slope parameter \(L\) from analyses by Oertel et al. (2017), Li et al. (2013), and others. The results provide a method to extract the curvature \(K_{sym}\) from forthcoming RIBF/RIKEN data, which then allows deduction of the high-density symmetry energy behavior relevant to neutron star properties such as radii, cooling rates, tidal deformability, and gravitational-wave signals.
