# Sigma terms from an SU(3) chiral extrapolation

> We report a new analysis of lattice simulation results for octet baryon masses in 2+1-flavor QCD, with an emphasis on a precise determination of the strangeness nucleon sigma term. A controlled chiral extrapolation of a recent PACS-CS Collaboration data set yields baryon masses which exhibit remarkable agreement both with experimental values at the physical point and with the results of independent lattice QCD simulations at unphysical meson masses. Using the Feynman-Hellmann relation, we evaluate sigma commutators for all octet baryons. The small statistical uncertainty, and considerably smaller model-dependence, allows a signifcantly more precise determination of the pion-nucleon sigma commutator and the strangeness sigma term than hitherto possible, namely σπN=45 \pm 6 MeV and σs = 21 \pm 6 MeV at the physical point.

## Key claim

The paper reports a determination of the pion-nucleon sigma term and the strangeness sigma term from a chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD data, claiming significantly smaller model-dependence than previous analyses. The quoted central values of sigma_piN = 45 +/- 6 MeV and sigma_s = 21 +/- 6 MeV at the physical point depend on the specific choice of finite-range regularisation regulator and on the set of phenomenological input parameters used in the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral extrapolation of octet baryon masses from the PACS-CS Collaboration's 2+1-flavor lattice QCD data using finite-range regularisation (FRR) to resum the chiral perturbation theory expansion. They fitted the resulting mass functions to the lattice data, then applied the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to extract sigma commutators for all octet baryons by differentiating the fitted mass functions with respect to quark masses. The analysis included corrections for finite-volume effects and tested systematic uncertainties by varying the regulator form (monopole, dipole, Gaussian, sharp cutoff) and by varying the phenomenological input parameters F, C, delta, and f by +/- 10%.

## Key numbers

- sigma_piN = 45 +/- 6 MeV at the physical point (nucleon, combined statistical and systematic uncertainty)
- sigma_s = 21 +/- 6 MeV at the physical point (nucleon, combined statistical and systematic uncertainty)
- sigma_KN = 300 +/- 40 MeV at the physical point (nucleon)
- y-value = 0.04 +/- 0.01 for m_l/m_s = 0.039(6) (nucleon)
- sigma_piN - sigma_0 = 1.64 +/- 0.53 MeV (nucleon)
- Optimal dipole regularization scale Lambda = 0.9 +/- 0.1 GeV
- Minimum chi^2_dof = 0.41 (6.1/(20-5)) for the dipole regulator
- Largest finite-volume correction: -0.022 +/- 0.002 GeV for the nucleon at the lightest pion mass
- Extrapolated nucleon mass: 0.959(24)(9) GeV (experimental: 0.939 GeV)
- Extrapolated Lambda mass: 1.129(15)(6) GeV (experimental: 1.116 GeV)
- Extrapolated Sigma mass: 1.188(11)(6) GeV (experimental: 1.193 GeV)
- Extrapolated Xi mass: 1.325(6)(2) GeV (experimental: 1.318 GeV)
- Dimensionless nucleon sigma terms: sigma_bar_Bl = 0.047(6)(5), sigma_bar_Bs = 0.022(6)(0)
- At (m_pi, m_K) = (281, 547) MeV: sigma_piN = 131(11)(5) MeV, sigma_s = 16(5)(1) MeV (from fit, compared to QCDSF direct calculation: sigma_piN = 106(11)(3) MeV, sigma_s = 12(-16)(+23) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the finite-range regularisation approach of Young (2009) and Shanahan (2011) to a new analysis of PACS-CS Collaboration lattice data, providing sigma terms with reduced model-dependence compared to traditional determinations that relied on dispersion relation analysis of piN scattering combined with SU(3) symmetry breaking estimates. The results are consistent with recent direct lattice QCD calculations by the QCDSF Collaboration (Bali 2011) at unphysical meson masses, and the fitted mass functions successfully predict independent UKQCD-QCDSF lattice data along a different trajectory in the m_l-m_s plane. The precise value of sigma_s = 21 +/- 6 MeV enables improved predictions for dark matter scattering cross-sections, which depend quadratically on the strange scalar matrix element.
