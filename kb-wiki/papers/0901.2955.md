# Longitudinal Rescaling and High-Energy Effective Actions

> Under a rescaling of longitudinal coordinates $x^{0,3}$ by a factor $λ$ which is taken to zero, the classical QCD action simplifies dramatically. This is the high-energy limit, as $λ$ is of order $s^{-1/2}$, where $s$ is the center-of-mass energy squared of a hadronic collision. We find the quantum corrections to the rescaled action at one loop, in particular finding the anomalous powers of $λ$ in this action, for $λ$ close to unity. The method is an integration over high-momentum components of the gauge field. This is a Wilsonian renormalization procedure, and counterterms are needed to make the sharp-momentum cut-off gauge invariant. Our result for the quantum action is found, assuming that the logarithm of $λ$ is small, which is essential for the validity of perturbation theory. If $λ$ is sufficiently small (so that its logarithm is large), then the perturbative renormalization group breaks down. This is due to uncontrollable fluctuations of the longitudinal chromomagnetic field.

## Key claim

Under a longitudinal rescaling of coordinates \(x^{0,3} \rightarrow \lambda x^{0,3}\) with \(\lambda \ll 1\), the classical QCD action simplifies, but the quantum one-loop corrections introduce anomalous powers of \(\lambda\) only when \(|\ln \lambda| \ll 1\); if \(\lambda\) is sufficiently small that \(|\ln \lambda| \gg 1\), perturbative renormalization group methods break down due to uncontrollable fluctuations of the longitudinal chromomagnetic field.

## Method

The authors perform a Wilsonian renormalization procedure by integrating out high-momentum components of the SU(\(N\)) Yang-Mills gauge field from a spherical momentum cut-off to an ellipsoidal cut-off, then rescaling longitudinal momenta to restore isotropy. They compute the one-loop effective action by expanding the action to quadratic order in the fast gauge field, evaluating the polarization tensor and field-strength-squared contributions using an O(2) \(\times\) O(2) symmetric integration over the momentum shell, and adding counterterms to restore gauge invariance broken by the sharp momentum cut-off.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop renormalization of the coupling for a spherical cut-off yields \(\frac{1}{\tilde{g}_0^2} = \frac{1}{g_0^2} - \frac{11 C_N}{96\pi^2} \ln \frac{\Lambda}{\tilde{\Lambda}}\), where \(C_N\) is the Casimir of SU(\(N\)).
* The polarization tensor at zero momentum for the spherical case gives \(\Pi_{\mu\nu}(0) = \frac{1}{128\pi^2}(\Lambda^2 - \tilde{\Lambda}^2)\delta_{\mu\nu}\).
* The gauge-invariant part of the polarization tensor for the spherical case is \(\hat{\Pi}_{\mu\nu}(p) = \frac{1}{192\pi^2} \ln \frac{\Lambda}{\tilde{\Lambda}} (\delta_{\mu\nu} - p_\mu p_\nu) + \cdots\).
* The ellipsoidal case yields a polarization tensor with an additional term proportional to \(\ln \tilde{b} / (64\pi^2)\) that violates gauge invariance and requires counterterms.

## Context

This work extends the classical longitudinal rescaling approach of Verlinde and Verlinde (1993), who argued that such rescaling yields the BFKL theory and gluon Reggeization, and the Color-Glass Condensate picture of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994). The paper provides the first perturbative quantum treatment of this rescaling, showing that the classical simplification is only valid when \(|\ln \lambda| \ll 1\), and that for extremely high energies (very small \(\lambda\)) the perturbative renormalization group breaks down. This result enables future work to determine whether the high-energy limit of QCD is justified and to include quark fields in the analysis.
