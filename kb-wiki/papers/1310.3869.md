# Nuclear Effects in Neutrino Interactions and their Impact on the Determination of Oscillation Parameters

> The quantitative description of the effects of nuclear dynamics on the measured neutrino-nucleus cross sections -- needed to reduce the systematic uncertainty of long baseline neutrino oscillation experiments -- involves severe difficulties. Owing to the uncertainty on the incoming neutrino energy, different reaction mechanisms contribute to the cross section measured at fixed energy and scattering angle of the outgoing lepton, and must therefore be consistently taken into account within a unified model. We review the theoretical approach based on the impulse approximation and the use of realistic nucleon spectral functions, allowing one to describe a variety of reaction mechanisms active in the broad kinematical range covered by neutrino experiments. The extension of this scheme to include more complex mechanisms involving the two-nucleon current, which are believed to be important, is also outlined. The impact of nuclear effects on the determination of neutrino oscillation parameters is illustrated by analyzing the problem of neutrino energy reconstruction.

## Key claim

The quantitative description of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus cross sections is essential to reduce systematic uncertainties in long-baseline oscillation experiments, but the uncertainty on the incoming neutrino energy means that different reaction mechanisms contribute to the measured cross section at fixed outgoing lepton energy and angle, requiring a unified theoretical model that consistently treats all active mechanisms.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical approach based on the impulse approximation (IA), in which the neutrino-nucleus cross section is factorized into a convolution of the nucleon spectral function, giving the probability distribution of nucleon momentum and removal energy in the target, and the elementary neutrino-nucleon cross section. The spectral function is constructed using the local density approximation (LDA), combining mean-field contributions from shell-model states with correlation contributions from nuclear matter calculations, and the formalism is extended to cover quasi-elastic scattering, resonance production, and deep inelastic scattering within a single framework.

## Key numbers

- The mean momentum transfer for quasi-elastic processes averaged over the MiniBooNE neutrino flux is approximately 640 MeV, and for the Miner$\nu$a flux it is approximately 880 MeV.
- The axial mass extracted from neutrino-deuteron scattering data is $M_A = 1.032 \pm 0.036$ GeV, while the MiniBooNE analysis using the relativistic Fermi gas model yields $M_A \approx 1.35$ GeV, significantly larger.
- The K2K collaboration reported $M_A \approx 1.2$ GeV from CCQE neutrino-oxygen cross section analysis, while NOMAD reported $M_A = 1.05$ GeV from CCQE neutrino- and antineutrino-carbon interactions at beam energies around 10 GeV.
- The mean-field contribution to the spectral function normalization accounts for approximately 80% of the integral, with the correlation strength located at large momentum and removal energy.
- The axial coupling constant is $g_A = -1.261 \pm 0.004$, obtained from neutron beta-decay.

## Context

This review extends the impulse approximation framework previously applied to electron-nucleus scattering (as reviewed in Ref. RMP) to neutrino-nucleus interactions, addressing the additional complexity that neutrino beams have broad energy distributions unlike the fixed-energy electron beams used in experiments such as those at JLab. The work contrasts with the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) of Moniz (1971) used in most oscillation analysis codes, and with the independent particle model approaches of Martini (2011) and Nieves (2012) that include two-nucleon currents but omit nucleon-nucleon correlations. The spectral function formalism enables a consistent treatment of all reaction mechanisms, quasi-elastic scattering, resonance production, and deep inelastic scattering, which is necessary to resolve the discrepancy between the MiniBooNE CCQE cross section data and predictions using the standard axial mass from deuteron data.
