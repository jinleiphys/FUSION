# Short-Range Correlations and Transverse Response Enhancement from Meson-Exchange Currents

> We investigate the role of short-range correlations (SRC) in the transverse nuclear response within the quasielastic peak region, focusing on the 1p1h channel. The calculation is performed in nuclear matter by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the realistic Granada 2013 nucleon-nucleon potential, including both one-body and two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC) of seagull, pion-in-flight, and $Δ$ types. We find that MEC produce a sizable enhancement of the transverse response in the 1p1h channel when acting on correlated nucleon pairs with high-momentum components generated by SRC. This contrasts with the uncorrelated case, where MEC, dominated by the $Δ$ current, can even yield a negative effect. The results are consistent with previous findings based on the correlated basis function approach, supporting the interpretation that SRC play a central role in the transverse response enhancement from MEC.

# Short-Range Correlations and Transverse Response Enhancement from Meson-Exchange Currents

## Key claim
Short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclear matter produce a sizable enhancement of the transverse nuclear response in the 1p1h channel when meson-exchange currents (MEC) act on correlated nucleon pairs with high-momentum components, in contrast to the uncorrelated case where MEC, dominated by the Δ current, can even yield a negative effect. The enhancement is predominantly produced by the ³S₁–³D₁ partial wave, indicating that tensor correlations drive the effect.

## Method
The calculation is performed in nuclear matter by solving the Bethe–Goldstone equation with the realistic Granada 2013 nucleon–nucleon potential, using the independent-pair approximation. The transverse response is computed including both one-body (magnetization) and two-body meson-exchange currents (seagull, pion-in-flight, and Δ-excitation types) in the non-relativistic limit, with the correlated wave functions replacing plane-wave states for nucleon pairs. The frozen-k approximation (setting the spectator nucleon momentum k=0) reduces the seven-dimensional numerical integration to a four-dimensional one with only ~6% variation in the correlated interference transverse response.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfers studied: q = 400 MeV/c and q = 550 MeV/c for the comparison with Fabrocini (1997) results
- Fermi momentum used for ¹²C: k_F = 225 MeV/c
- High-momentum integration cutoff: |p₁| ~ 800 MeV or less for convergence
- Frozen-k approximation introduces only ~6% variation in the correlated interference transverse response
- The uncorrelated Δ current interference is negative and dominates the three MEC contributions; correlations render it positive, particularly pronounced at q = 550 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the correlated basis function (CBF) approach of Fabrocini (1997), who found that correlations incorporated through explicit operators in the wave function led to significant enhancement of the 1p1h transverse response. The present calculation uses a completely different method, solving the Bethe–Goldstone equation with the Granada 2013 potential, and confirms that SRC play a central role in the transverse response enhancement from MEC. The results are consistent with earlier suggestions by Leidemann and Orlandini that tensor correlations drive the enhancement, and have implications for modeling nuclear effects in neutrino–nucleus scattering and Monte Carlo event generators.
