# Robust ab initio predictions for dimensionless ratios of E2 and radius observables. I. Electric quadrupole moments and deformation

> Converged results for E2 observables are notoriously challenging to obtain in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) approaches. Matrix elements of the E2 operator are sensitive to the large-distance tails of the nuclear wave function, which converge slowly in an oscillator basis expansion. Similar convergence challenges beset ab initio prediction of the nuclear charge radius. However, we exploit systematic correlations between the calculated E2 and radius observables to yield meaningful predictions for relations among these observables. In particular, we examine ab initio predictions for dimensionless ratios of the form Q/r^2, for nuclei throughout the $p$ shell. Meaningful predictions for electric quadrupole moments may then be made by calibrating to the ground-state charge radius, if experimentally known, or vice versa. Moreover, these dimensionless ratios provide ab initio insight into the nuclear quadrupole deformation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dimensionless ratios of the form \(Q/r^2\) (quadrupole moment over squared radius) converge more rapidly in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations than the individual observables, enabling meaningful ab initio predictions even when the separate E2 and radius observables are not adequately converged.

## Method

The authors performed no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations using the MFDn code for p-shell nuclei, computing ground-state quadrupole moments and point-proton radii in an oscillator basis truncated to maximum oscillator excitations \(\Nmax\) from 4 to 12, with oscillator parameters \(\hbar\omega\) ranging from 10 to 30 MeV. They constructed the dimensionless ratio \(Q/r_p^2\) from the calculated observables and examined its convergence properties using relative differences and ratios of successive differences as diagnostics, comparing results across three internucleon interactions: Daejeon16, JISP16, and LENPIC.

## Key numbers

* For \(^9\)Be ground state with the Daejeon16 interaction, the relative differences \(\Delta_{\text{rel}}[Q]\) for the quadrupole moment decrease from approximately 10% at \(\Nmax=6\) to approximately 4% at \(\Nmax=12\) for \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV.
* The Darwin-Foldy contribution to the charge radius correction is \(3/(4M_p^2)=0.033\) fm\(^2\), and the total proton correction used is \(R_p^2+3/(4M_p^2)=0.7401(7)\) fm\(^2\).
* The neutron mean-square charge radius correction used is \(R_n^2=-0.1155(17)\) fm\(^2\).
* For the Daejeon16 interaction, the ground state energy convergence for \(^9\)Be reaches the level of \(\lesssim 0.1\) MeV near the variational minimum, while LENPIC results still differ by approximately 5 MeV between the highest two \(\Nmax\) values shown.

## Context

This work extends the approach of observable correlations previously exploited for E2 matrix elements within rotational bands by Caprio et al. (2013, 2015, 2019, 2020) and for mirror states by Henderson (2019) and Caprio et al. (2021), and for calibration to ground-state quadrupole moments by Calci et al. (2016) and Caprio et al. (2022). The use of radius calibration overcomes the limitation that quadrupole moment calibration is not possible for even-even nuclei or odd-mass nuclei with \(J=1/2\) ground states. The results enable prediction of unknown quadrupole moments from measured charge radii (or vice versa) for p-shell nuclei, and provide ab initio insight into nuclear quadrupole deformation through the relation to the Bohr deformation parameter \(\beta\).
