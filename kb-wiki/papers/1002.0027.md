# Misfits in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock

> We address very briefly five critical points in the context of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) scheme: 1) the impossibility to consider it as an interaction, 2) a possible inconsistency of correlation corrections as, e.g., the center-of-mass correction, 3) problems to describe the giant dipole resonance (GDR) simultaneously in light and heavy nuclei, 4) deficiencies in the extrapolation of binding energies to super-heavy elements (SHE), and 5) a yet inappropriate trend in fission life-times when going to the heaviest SHE. While the first two points have more a formal bias, the other three points have practical implications and wait for solution.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) scheme, despite its enormous success in describing nuclear properties across the chart of isotopes, suffers from five specific unresolved problems: the density-dependent term cannot be interpreted as a genuine interaction, center-of-mass corrections are inconsistent for reactions changing particle number, the giant dipole resonance (GDR) cannot be described simultaneously in light and heavy nuclei, binding energy extrapolations to super-heavy elements (SHE) show systematic underbinding, and fission lifetime trends for the heaviest SHE are incorrectly reproduced by all current parameterizations.

## Method

The authors analyze the formal structure of the Skyrme energy functional, demonstrating that the density-dependent term (with exponent α) cannot be expressed as a standard quantum-mechanical many-body interaction operator. They perform Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) calculations of the GDR for multiple Skyrme forces (SkM* and SLy6) across a range of nuclei and compare to experimental data. For SHE, they compute binding energies using the SV-min parameterization fitted to spherical nuclei, then refit with 264Hs included to produce SV-def. Fission lifetimes are computed via quadrupole-constrained SHF with cranking collective masses, zero-point energy corrections, and WKB tunneling rates for several Skyrme forces (SkM*, SkP, SkI3, SV-bas, SV-min).

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass correction estimate: E_cm ≈ 30 MeV A^{-1/3}
- GDR experimental trend: E_GDR ∝ A^{-1/6}; RPA predictions show admixture of E_GDR ∝ A^{-1/3} trend
- SV-min binding energy errors: interpolation for nuclei with A < 210 shows acceptably small errors distributed on both sides of zero; extrapolation to SHE shows significant trend to increasing underbinding
- SV-def (with 264Hs added to fit): improves SHE predictions but sacrifices quality for many other nuclei, which become overbound
- Triaxial barrier-lowering in fission: typically 1 MeV, at most 2 MeV, insufficient to resolve the trend discrepancy

## Context

This paper extends the critical analysis of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework, building on reviews by Bender et al. (2003) and Stone & Reinhard (2007) that established SHF's successes. The formal inconsistency of the density-dependent term as an interaction was previously noted by Dobaczewski et al. (1990) regarding self-interaction errors. The center-of-mass correction problem was identified earlier by Bertsch (1980) and Skalski (2007), who proposed ad-hoc interpolation formulas. The GDR trend issue contradicts the common belief that SHF controls the GDR well, based on calibrations using heavy nuclei (Brack et al., 1985). The SHE binding energy extrapolation problem and fission lifetime trend issue are newly identified here, using the fit protocol of Klüpfel et al. (2009) and the fission lifetime method of Schindler et al. (2009). The work enables future efforts to reformulate the Skyrme functional to simultaneously describe light and heavy nuclei, and to develop a consistent treatment of collective correlations in reactions.
