# Bound State Calculations of the Three-Dimensional Yakubovsky Equations with the inclusion of Three-Body Forces

> The four-body Yakubovsky equations in a Three-Dimensional approach with the inclusion of the three-body forces is proposed. The four-body bound state with two- and three-body interactions is formulated in Three-Dimensional approach for identical particles as function of vector Jacobi momenta, specifically the magnitudes of the momenta and the angles between them. The modified three dimensional Yakubovsky integral equations is successfully solved with the scalar two-meson exchange three-body force where the Malfliet-Tjon-type two-body force is implemented. ## Key claim

The paper proposes and solves a three-dimensional (3D) formulation of the four-body Yakubovsky equations that includes three-body forces (3BFs), using a scalar two-meson exchange 3BF model with a Malfliet-Tjon-type two-body force, but only for identical bosons (neglecting spin and isospin degrees of freedom) as a first step toward a full four-nucleon calculation.

## Method

The authors formulate the four-body Yakubovsky equations in momentum space using vector Jacobi momenta (magnitudes and angles between them) instead of a partial-wave decomposition. They evaluate the matrix elements of the three-body force by inserting complete sets of states and performing sequential integrations over meson-exchange propagators, with coordinate transformations handled via five-dimensional interpolations using cubic Hermitian splines. The coupled three-dimensional integral equations are discretized on Gaussian quadrature grids and solved as a matrix eigenvalue problem using a Lanczos-like iterative scheme.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 3D approach for four-body bound states with only two-body forces (Hadizadeh, 2006) to include three-body forces, building on the 3D formulation of three-body forces developed by Liu (2004). It contrasts with traditional partial-wave decomposition methods used in Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations (Schellingerhout, 1993; Kamada, 1992; Nogga, 2004) and other approaches like CRCGV (Hiyama, 2000), HH (Viviani, 2005), GFMC (Viringa, 2000), and NCSM (Navratil, 2000), which require complicated angular momentum algebra. The method enables future four-nucleon bound state calculations with three-nucleon forces without performing partial-wave decomposition.
