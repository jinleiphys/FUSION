# Review of Recent Developments in the CGC

> I review recent developments in the CGC approach to high-energy collisions. The focus is on topics related to the Quark Matter conference, specifically on predictions for the p+Pb run at the LHC; as an added bonus some of these predictions are confronted with first data taken during the p+Pb machine performance test at the LHC performed in september 2012. I also highlight recent work related to particle production fluctuations, suppression of nuclear modification factors in p+A from rcBK evolution, (partial) NLO corrections in forward production, and photon-hadron as well as di-hadron correlations.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory can describe a broad range of high-energy collision observables from RHIC and LHC, but many predictions remain at leading-order accuracy and require full NLO corrections for reliable quantitative comparisons, particularly for forward particle production and high-transverse-momentum tails.

## Method

The paper reviews CGC calculations using the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling (rcBK) to evolve dipole scattering amplitudes, starting from either the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model or the AAMQS fit to HERA data. For particle production, the hybrid formalism combines DGLAP-evolved projectile parton distributions with small-x target fields from rcBK, while for correlations the JIMWLK evolution equation is solved numerically or approximated by a Gaussian ansatz. Multiplicity distributions are computed from classical color field fluctuations using the KLN model and negative binomial distributions.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity in p+Pb collisions at √s = 5 TeV predicted at midrapidity; deviations among different CGC models are ±15% from the central value
- Reference rapidity for BK evolution: Y₀ = log(1/x₀) ≈ log(100) = 4.6
- MV model initial condition uses γ = 1; AAMQS fit to HERA data finds γ ≈ 1.1
- p+p collisions at 7 TeV: AAMQS initial condition with rcBK evolution agrees with CMS spectrum; MV model initial condition is excluded by data
- Nuclear modification factor R_pPb(p_T) at η = 0 shows suppression at p_T = 1 GeV, approaching or exceeding unity at higher p_T
- Forward rapidity predictions: R_pPb at η = 2 and η = 4 show increasing suppression with rapidity
- Inelastic corrections to hybrid formalism are suppressed by one power of α_s but enhanced by log(p_T/Q_s); numerical evaluations use α_s = 0.1

## Context

This review extends the CGC framework from earlier work on d+Au collisions at RHIC (Arsene 2004) and HERA DIS fits (AAMQS) to predictions for the first p+Pb run at the LHC at √s = 5 TeV in September 2012. It confronts predictions with preliminary ALICE data on multiplicities and nuclear modification factors, and discusses how CGC initial-state fluctuations (Schenke 2012) affect angular flow harmonics measured by ATLAS (Jia 2012). The work enables quantitative tests of rcBK evolution speed and motivates full NLO calculations (Chirilli 2011) to replace the current partial NLO corrections (Altinoluk 2011).
