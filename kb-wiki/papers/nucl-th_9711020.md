# Nuclear Response in Electron Scattering at High Momentum Transfer

> The nuclear response functions for high energy electron scattering were calculated in the wide region of excitation energy. The three typical regions were studied: the quasi-elastic region, the $Δ$-excitation region and the intermediate region where the meson-exchange currents give significant contribution. In the quasi-elastic and the $Δ$ regions the response functions were found for finite size nucleus with account of relativistic kinematics. The contribution of the meson-exchange currents was calculated in the relativistic Fermi-gas model. The results were compared with the electron scattering data at high momentum transfer from $^{12}C$ and $^{16}O$.

## Key claim

The authors develop a "quasi-relativistic" model for nuclear response functions in electron scattering at high momentum transfer, where relativistic kinematics for outgoing nucleons and Delta isobars are incorporated into a finite-nucleus framework that otherwise uses non-relativistic Schrodinger-based nucleon-nucleus interactions. The key hedge is that this approach is "certainly not fully relativistic" but improves the energy-momentum relation for outgoing particles, stabilizing the quasi-elastic peak shape compared to purely non-relativistic calculations.

## Method

The authors calculated Coulomb and transverse response functions for finite-size nuclei (12C and 16O) using an independent-particle model with Woods-Saxon potentials, incorporating relativistic kinematics by substituting omega -> omega + omega^2/(2m) in the Green functions and redefining form factors. For the quasi-elastic and Delta-excitation regions, they computed particle-hole and Delta-hole response functions respectively, using a relativistic Fermi-gas model for the meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions in the intermediate region. The Delta-hole response was calculated with a Woods-Saxon optical potential for the Delta, and MEC contributions from pion-in-flight, contact, and propagation diagrams were included with a pion form factor cutoff Lambda = 1250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant f_{gamma N,Delta} = 3.6 was used for Delta production, found from reaction on a single proton (Ang96).
- The imaginary part of the optical potential for nucleons in the continuum was W = 13 MeV in the quasi-elastic peak region, consistent with elastic proton scattering parameters (Fe69).
- The pion form factor cutoff parameter was Lambda = 1250 MeV, giving the best agreement with data.
- The Delta-nucleus optical potential parameters were taken from pion-nucleus data (Horik80).
- Calculations were performed for incident electron energies of 961 MeV and 1108 MeV on 12C and 16O targets.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic calculations (Alb88) that produced overly broad quasi-elastic peaks at high momentum transfer, and contrasts with the fully relativistic treatment of HorP90 which used the Walecka model (Wal74) with specific nucleon-nucleon interactions. The authors' approach allows use of the full variety of nucleon-nucleus interactions developed for the Schrodinger equation while incorporating relativistic kinematics, similar to the method used by Amaro96 for parity-violating response functions. The MEC calculations follow the approach of Ris89 and Vod81, with the nonstatic omega-dependence treatment in the spirit of Tjon92. This work enables unified description of nucleon and Delta degrees of freedom in the overlapping region between quasi-elastic and Delta peaks at high momentum transfer.
