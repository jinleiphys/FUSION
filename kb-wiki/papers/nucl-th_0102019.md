# Application of Information Theory in Nuclear Liquid Gas Phase Transition

> Information entropy and Zipf's law in the field of information theory have been used for studying the disassembly of nuclei in the framework of the isospin dependent lattice gas model and molecular dynamical model. We found that the information entropy in the event space is maximum at the phase transition point and the mass of the cluster show exactly inversely to its rank, i.e. Zipf's law appears. Both novel criteria are useful in searching the nuclear liquid gas phase transition experimentally and theoretically.

# Application of Information Theory in Nuclear Liquid Gas Phase Transition

## Key claim
Information entropy (Shannon entropy) of the event-wise multiplicity distribution reaches a maximum at the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition point, and the fragment mass distribution obeys Zipf's law (mass inversely proportional to rank) at that same transition temperature, but both criteria are acknowledged as phenomenological rather than definitive proofs, and the transition temperature is first identified by conventional observables (τ, S₂, N_imf) before being cross-checked against the new signatures.

## Method
The isospin-dependent lattice gas model (LGM) and a classical molecular dynamical model (MD) were used to simulate the disassembly of ¹²⁹Xe nuclei at freeze-out densities of 0.18ρ₀, 0.38ρ₀, and 0.60ρ₀. For each temperature and density combination, 1000 events were generated via Monte Carlo Metropolis sampling (LGM) or classical propagation (MD). Information entropy was computed from the normalized probability distribution of total multiplicity per event, and the slope parameter λ of the relation A_n ∝ n^{-λ} was extracted by fitting the rank-ordered cluster masses for ranks 1 through 10.

## Key numbers
- The interaction energy between unlike nucleons (proton-neutron) is set to ε_pn = -5.33 MeV; like-nucleon interactions (nn, pp) are set to 0 MeV.
- Freeze-out densities studied: 0.18ρ₀ (9³ cubic lattice), 0.38ρ₀ (7³ cubic lattice), and 0.60ρ₀ (6³ cubic lattice).
- The MD potential minimum is -5.33 MeV at r₀ = 1.842 fm, with parameters p=2, q=1, a=1.3, B=0.924, C=1966 MeV.
- The slope parameter λ reaches approximately 1 (Zipf's law, A_n ∝ n^{-1}) at the phase transition temperature for all cases studied.
- The χ²/ndf for the Zipf-law fit shows minima at the respective transition temperatures across all calculations.

## Context
This work extends prior lattice gas model studies of nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions (Campi 1997, Pan 1996, Pan 1998) by introducing information-theoretic diagnostics from Shannon (1948) and Zipf (1949) that had previously been applied in linguistics, percolation (Watanabe), and high-energy multiparticle production (Brog). The paper directly addresses the ambiguity of conventional signatures: the power-law parameter τ can also show minima along the Kertesz line (Campi 1997) and at subcritical densities (Pan 1996), while caloric curve plateaus (Aladin collaboration) are complicated by spectator mass changes and side-feeding (Natowitz). The proposed entropy and Zipf criteria offer complementary, event-space-based tools for identifying the phase transition in both experimental data and theoretical simulations, and the author demonstrates that the same transition temperatures are identified for larger systems (A=274, 500, 830) in LGM.
