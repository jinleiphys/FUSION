# Cluster states in nuclei as representations of a U(n+1) group

> We propose a description of cluster states in nuclei in terms of representations of unitary algebras U(n+1), where n is the number of space degrees of freedom. Within this framework, a variety of situations including both vibrational and rotational spectra, soft and rigid configurations, identical and non-identical constituents can be described. As an example, we show how the method can be used to study alpha-clustering configurations in 12C with point group symmetry D(3h).

# Cluster states in nuclei as representations of a U(n+1) group

## Key claim

The authors propose that cluster states in nuclei can be described using representations of the unitary algebra U(n+1), where n is the number of space degrees of freedom, and that this algebraic framework can accommodate both vibrational and rotational spectra, soft and rigid configurations, and identical or non-identical constituents. The claim is hedged by the fact that the method is illustrated only for the specific case of three-alpha clustering in 12C with D(3h) symmetry, and the authors note that whether the observed excited states represent triangular or linear configurations remains an open question.

## Method

The authors construct the U(7) algebra for the three-body problem (after removing center-of-mass motion, n=6 space degrees of freedom) by introducing two vector bosons (b_rho, b_lambda) representing Jacobi coordinates and an auxiliary scalar boson s. The Hamiltonian is written as the most general operator preserving angular momentum, parity, and permutation symmetry for three identical particles, with up to quadratic (two-body) interactions. For the equilateral triangle configuration, the energy eigenvalues are obtained approximately using the method of intrinsic (coherent) states in the limit of large boson number N, yielding an analytic energy formula. Form factors for electron scattering are calculated from matrix elements of the algebraic image of the operator exp(iq*r_3z).

## Key numbers

- The three-body problem has n=6 space degrees of freedom, leading to the algebra U(7)

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to cluster states previously applied to two-body cluster configurations using U(4) by Iachello (1981) and to three-quark configurations in baryons by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994). The method is applied to the long-standing suggestion by Wheeler (1937) and Glassgold and Galonsky (1956) that 12C in its ground state can be viewed as three alpha particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle with D(3h) point group symmetry. The authors argue that the experimental spectrum of 12C is similar to that of a triangular configuration, but note that the rotational spectrum shows a spherical or slightly prolate top rather than the expected oblate top. The work enables future experimental reinvestigation of high-lying states in 12C via (alpha,alpha') and (e,e') inelastic scattering to settle whether the cluster structure is triangular or linear.
