# Centrality dependence of chemical freeze-out parameters from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations using hadron resonance gas model

> We estimate chemical freeze-out parameters in HRG and EVHRG model by fitting the experimental information of net-proton and net-charge fluctuations measured in Au + Au collisions by the STAR collaboration at RHIC. We observe that chemical freeze-out parameters obtained from lower and higher order fluctuations are though almost same for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 27$ GeV, tend to deviate from each other at lower $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. Moreover, these separations increase with decrease of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ and for a fixed $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ increase towards central collisions. Furthermore, we observe an approximate scaling behaviour of $(μ_B/T)/(μ_B/T)_{central}$ with $(N_{part})/(N_{part})_{central}$ for the parameters estimated from lower order fluctuations for 11.5 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}} \le$ 200 GeV. Scaling is violated for the parameters estimated from higher order fluctuations for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 11.5$ and 19.6 GeV. It is observed that the chemical freeze-out parameter, which can describe $σ^2/M$ of net-proton very well in all energies and centralities, can not describe the $sσ$ equally well and vice versa.

# Centrality dependence of chemical freeze-out parameters from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations using hadron resonance gas model

## Key claim

Chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from lower-order cumulant ratios (sigma^2/M) and higher-order cumulant ratios (S sigma) of net-proton and net-charge are nearly identical for sqrt(s_NN) > 27 GeV, but deviate significantly at lower energies, with the separation increasing for more central collisions and decreasing beam energy; this deviation may indicate incomplete thermalization or proximity to a critical end point, though the authors caution that statistical fluctuations in the data may overwhelm critical fluctuations.

## Method

The authors fitted chemical freeze-out temperatures and baryon chemical potentials using chi-squared minimization of STAR collaboration data on sigma^2/M, S sigma, and kappa sigma^2 for net-proton and net-charge in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV across multiple centralities, using both the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and the excluded-volume hadron resonance gas (EVHRG) model with a hard-core radius of 0.3 fm for all hadrons. Four sets of freeze-out parameters were extracted: CFO1 and CFO2 using only sigma^2/M of net-proton and net-charge in HRG and EVHRG respectively, and CFO3 and CFO4 using sigma^2/M of net-charge plus S sigma of both net-proton and net-charge in HRG and EVHRG respectively, with experimental acceptance cuts applied (mid-rapidity |y|<0.5, 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV for net-proton; |eta|<0.5, 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV for net-charge).

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperatures across all sqrt(s_NN) and centralities lie within 140-180 MeV
- Baryon chemical potentials increase by about two orders of magnitude as sqrt(s_NN) decreases from 200 GeV to 7.7 GeV
- For sqrt(s_NN) >= 27 GeV, freeze-out parameters from CFO1/CFO2 and CFO3/CFO4 are almost the same; deviations appear for sqrt(s_NN) < 27 GeV
- An approximate scaling of (mu_B/T)/(mu_B/T)_central with (N_part)/(N_part)_central holds for lower-order fluctuation parameters for 11.5 GeV <= sqrt(s_NN) <= 200 GeV, but is violated for higher-order fluctuation parameters at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 and 19.6 GeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from CFO1/CFO2 increases from sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV up to 39 GeV, then decreases at 27 GeV, becomes flat to 19.6 GeV, then decreases again

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chemical freeze-out parameters from particle yields (Cleymans 2005, Becattini 2005, Andronic 2005, Andronic 2009, Karsch 2010, Chatterjee 2015) by instead using cumulant ratios of conserved charge fluctuations, which are sensitive to higher-order susceptibilities and potentially to critical phenomena. The results are compared with Alba (2014), who extracted freeze-out parameters from sigma^2/M of net-proton and net-charge for central collisions, finding good agreement for CFO1/CFO2 at 0-5% centrality for sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6-200 GeV. The observed deviation between parameters from lower and higher cumulants at low energies suggests that the system may not be fully equilibrated when described by the HRG model, and the authors propose that critical slowing down near a possible critical end point could cause higher-order cumulants to carry information from farther from equilibrium, though they note that statistical fluctuations in the STAR data and the lack of subtraction of statistical fluctuations from variances make the sensitivity to dynamical effects ambiguous.
