# Luneburg-lens-like universal structural Pauli attraction in nucleus-nucleus interactions: origin of emergence of cluster structures and nuclear rainbows

> The Pauli exclusion principle plays an important role in many-body fermion systems preventing them from collapsing by repulsion. For example, the Pauli principle causes a repulsive potential at short distances between two $α$ particles. On the other hand, the existence of nuclear rainbows demonstrates that the inter-nuclear potential is sufficiently attractive in the internal region to cause refraction. The two concepts of repulsion and attraction are seemingly irreconcilable. Contrary to traditional understanding, it is shown that the Pauli principle causes a {\it universal structural Pauli attraction} between nuclei rather than a {\it structural repulsive core}. Through systematic studies of $α$+$α$, $α$+$^{16}$O, $α$+$^{40}$Ca and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O systems, it is shown that the emergence of cluster structures near the threshold energy at low energies and nuclear rainbows at high energies is a direct consequence of the Pauli principle.

# Luneburg-lens-like universal structural Pauli attraction in nucleus-nucleus interactions: origin of emergence of cluster structures and nuclear rainbows

## Key claim

Contrary to the traditional understanding that the Pauli exclusion principle causes a repulsive core at short distances between nuclei, the Pauli principle actually causes a universal structural Pauli attraction in the internal region, manifesting as a Luneburg-lens-like truncated harmonic oscillator potential. This attraction simultaneously explains the emergence of cluster structures near threshold energy at low energies and nuclear rainbows at high energies, provided that the outer region of the potential has a diffuse surface.

## Method

The author systematically analyzed four nuclear systems (α+α, α+¹⁶O, α+⁴⁰Ca, and ¹⁶O+¹⁶O) using global phenomenological potentials that reproduce elastic scattering angular distributions, including nuclear rainbow structures and anomalous large-angle scattering. The overlap of calculated wave functions for states with N < N₀ (where N₀ is the Pauli-forbidden quantum number) with redundant Pauli-forbidden harmonic oscillator wave functions was computed to demonstrate that the Pauli-forbidden states are embedded in the global potentials. The internal region of each potential was compared to a truncated harmonic oscillator (Luneburg lens) potential, and the theoretical connection was established through the resonating group method (RGM) formalism, showing that the local potential must resemble a deep harmonic oscillator in the internal region to satisfy the Pauli principle.

## Key numbers

- α+α system: global potential depth V₀ = 120 MeV (nuclear only) with truncation radius R = 2.5 fm; Luneburg lens potential depth V₀ = 116 MeV with R = 2.4 fm (including Coulomb); N₀ = 4; overlap of N=0 and N=2 states with Pauli-forbidden HO wave functions is 0.99
- α+¹⁶O system: global potential depth V₀ = 131 MeV with R = 3.75 fm (nuclear only); V₀ = 144 MeV with R = 3.9 fm (including Coulomb); N₀ = 8; overlaps for N=0 through N=6 range from 0.98 to 0.99
- α+⁴⁰Ca system: global potential depth V₀ = 167 MeV with R = 5.3 fm (nuclear only); V₀ = 146 MeV with R = 5.2 fm (including Coulomb); N₀ = 12; overlaps for N=0 through N=10 range from 0.93 to 1.00
- ¹⁶O+¹⁶O system: global potential depth V₀ = 310 MeV with R = 4.8 fm (nuclear only); V₀ = 266 MeV with R = 4.7 fm (including Coulomb); N₀ = 24; overlaps for N=0 through N=20 range from 0.74 to 0.98, with N=22 having overlap 0.43
- Theoretical Pauli-constrained depths at E=0: 121 MeV (α+α), 125 MeV (α+¹⁶O), 157 MeV (α+⁴⁰Ca), 305 MeV (¹⁶O+¹⁶O)
- α+⁴⁰Ca rainbow scattering at E_L = 61 MeV shows first-order Airy minimum A1 at θ ≈ 80°
- α+¹⁶O rainbow scattering at E_L = 49.5 MeV shows Airy minimum A1 at θ ≈ 75°

## Context

This work directly contradicts the traditional structural repulsive core picture established by Tamagaki (1962-1968), Hiura (1972), and Otsuki (1965) from microscopic RGM studies of α+α scattering, which found a repulsive core at short distances. It instead supports and unifies the deep potential approach that successfully described anomalous large-angle scattering in α+¹⁶O (Ohkubo 1977, Michel 1983) and α+⁴⁰Ca (Michel 1977, Delbar 1978), as well as the nuclear rainbow observed in ¹⁶O+¹⁶O scattering at E_L = 350 MeV (Stiliaris 1989, Khoa 2007). The work extends the threshold rule for cluster structures by providing a fundamental Pauli-principle explanation, and shows that the deep double folding potentials derived from the HNY force (Hasegawa 1971) and DDM3Y force (Kobos 1982) naturally incorporate this Pauli attraction. This enables a unified understanding of cluster structures near threshold, molecular resonances, and nuclear rainbows as manifestations of the same underlying Pauli principle, and suggests the effect may extend to nucleon-nucleon interactions and few-body systems.
