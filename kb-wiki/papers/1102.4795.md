# Vortons in dense quark matter

> At large baryon number density, it is likely that the ground state of QCD is a color-flavor-locked phase with a K0 condensate. The CFL+K0 phase is known to support superconducting vortex strings, and it has been previously suggested that it may also support vortons, which are superconducting vortex rings. We reexamine the question of the stability of vortons, taking into account electromagnetic effects, which make leading-order contributions to vorton dynamics but were not investigated in previous work. We find that current-carrying and electrically charged vortons can be stabilized either by their angular momentum, by Coulomb repulsion, or by a combination of both effects.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines the stability of vortons (superconducting vortex rings) in the CFL+K0 phase of dense quark matter, finding that current-carrying and electrically charged vortons can be stabilized by angular momentum, Coulomb repulsion, or a combination of both effects, but only when electromagnetic effects, which make leading-order contributions to vorton dynamics, are included. The authors note that their analysis is valid only for time scales shorter than those over which weak interactions would drive the electric charge to zero and cause collapse.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for the CFL+K0 phase using a non-linear sigma model for the meson fields, including full non-linear interactions and electromagnetic gauge fields. They approximate the vorton as a circular loop of radius R and divide space into three regions: a torus around the vortex (where the energy is approximated by 2πR times the straight-vortex tension), the region outside a sphere of radius 2R (treated via multipole expansion), and a neglected "apple-core" region between them. For the straight-vortex calculation, they use step-function ansätze for the K0 and K+ radial profiles and treat the string thickness δ and the K+ condensate magnitude as variational parameters.

## Key numbers

- The K0 condensate vacuum expectation value is given by cos(|K0|√2/f) = m_K0²/μ_sd², where μ_sd = (m_s² - m_d²)/(2μ) is the effective chemical potential
- The quark mass matrix is M = diag(m_u, m_d, m_s) ≈ diag(2, 5, 95) MeV
- The effective theory parameters at very large μ are: f² = (21-8ln2)/18 × μ²/(2π²), v² = 1/3, and A = 3/(4π²)Δ², where μ is the chemical potential and Δ is the gap
- The meson masses in the CFL phase are: m_π± = √(4A/f² (m_d+m_u)m_s), m_K± = √(4A/f² m_d(m_s+m_u)), m_K0 = √(4A/f² m_u(m_s+m_d))
- The K0 vortex thickness δ is expected to be approximately 20 fm, set by the mass splitting between the two kaon fields

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superconducting strings in the CFL+K0 phase by Kaplan (2001) and vorton stability analysis by Buckley et al. (2002), who did not fully explore electromagnetic effects. The paper also builds on the general mechanism of superconducting strings proposed by Witten (1984) in relativistic field theory. The analysis uses the full non-linear sigma model rather than the linear approximation used in prior work, and finds that electromagnetic effects make leading-order contributions to vorton stability. The results enable future estimates of the time scale for charge equilibration via weak interactions, which would determine the lifetime of these objects in neutron star interiors.
