# Extension of the Random Phase Approximation including the self-consistent coupling to two-phonon contributions

> A microscopic formalism is developed that includes the coupling to two particle-hole phonons in the particle-hole propagator by extending the dressed random phase approximation (DRPA) equation for a finite system. The resulting formalism is applied to study the low-lying excitation spectrum of 16O. It is observed that the coupling to two-phonon states at low energy generates excited states with quantum numbers that cannot be obtained in the DRPA approach. Nevertheless, the two-phonon states mix weakly with particle-hole configurations and participate only partially in the formation of the lowest-lying positive-parity excited states. The stability of the present calculation is tested vs. the truncation of model space. It is demonstrated that when single-particle strength fragmentation is properly considered, the present formalism exhibits convergence with respect to the chosen model space within the confines of the chosen approximation scheme.

# Extension of the Random Phase Approximation including the self-consistent coupling to two-phonon contributions

## Key claim

The coupling to two-phonon states generates excited states with quantum numbers not obtainable in the Dressed Random Phase Approximation (DRPA) alone, but these two-phonon states mix only weakly with particle-hole configurations and participate only partially in forming the lowest-lying positive-parity excited states of 16O. The formalism exhibits convergence with respect to model-space truncation only when single-particle strength fragmentation is properly considered.

## Method

The authors extended the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole (ph) propagator by including two-phonon exchange contributions to the irreducible ph interaction, following the Baym-Kadanoff procedure. They solved the resulting Extended RPA (ERPA) equations for 16O using a harmonic oscillator model space (four major shells plus the 1g9/2 orbital) with a Brueckner G-matrix derived from a Bonn-C potential, employing both an independent-particle model propagator and a fully dressed single-particle propagator from prior Faddeev-RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- The first 3⁻ and 0⁺ states in the independent-particle model ERPA calculation shifted down to approximately 0.5 MeV above their experimental energies.
- The lowest 0⁺ state with predominantly ph character rose to about 17 MeV when dressed propagators were used in DRPA.
- A triplet of states (0⁺, 2⁺, 4⁺) was found at approximately twice the energy of the first 3⁻ phonon (7.14 MeV in RPA), corresponding to an unperturbed energy near 14.3 MeV.
- The low-lying 3⁻ and 1⁻ levels remained at 9.23 and 10.90 MeV respectively in the dressed ERPA calculation, above their experimental energies.
- The 2⁺ giant quadrupole resonance appeared at 22.9 MeV (RPA) and 23.3 MeV (ERPA) in the independent-particle model, with about 20% of ph strength shifted to two-phonon configurations in ERPA.

## Context

This work extends the Dressed RPA approach of Barbieri and Dickhoff (2001, 2002) for 16O by incorporating two-phonon coupling, motivated by the Interacting Boson Model of Iachello and collaborators (1982, 1983) which described low-lying positive-parity states via coupling of 3⁻ and 1⁻ isoscalar phonons. The calculation addresses discrepancies between experimental spectroscopic factors for 16O and prior theoretical results from Muther (1994), Radici (1994), and others. The formalism enables future inclusion of particle-particle and hole-hole correlations, Pauli effects, and up to four-phonon configurations for a more complete microscopic description of nuclear spectra.
