# Quark-meson coupling model for antikaon condensation in neutron star matter with strong magnetic fields

> We study the effects of strong magnetic fields on antikaon condensation in neutron star matter using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The QMC model describes a nuclear many-body system as nonoverlapping MIT bags in which quarks interact through the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons in the mean-field approximation. It is found that the presence of strong magnetic fields alters the threshold density of antikaon condensation significantly. The onset of $K^-$ condensation stronger depends on the magnetic field strength, and it even shifts beyond the threshold of $\bar K^0$ condensation for sufficiently strong magnetic fields. In the presence of strong magnetic fields, the equation of state (EOS) becomes stiffer in comparison with the field-free case. The softening of the EOS by antikaon condensation also depends on the magnetic field strength, and it becomes less pronounced with increasing magnetic field strength. The results of the QMC model are compared with those obtained in a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, and we find there are quantitative differences between the results of the QMC and RMF models.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields significantly alter the threshold density of antikaon condensation in neutron star matter, with the onset of $K^{-}$ condensation shifting to higher density and even occurring beyond the threshold of $\bar{K}^0$ condensation for sufficiently strong fields; the softening of the equation of state by antikaon condensation becomes less pronounced with increasing magnetic field strength.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, describing nucleons and antikaons as nonoverlapping MIT bags that interact through self-consistent exchange of $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons in the mean-field approximation, with a constant external magnetic field along the $z$ axis. The effective masses of nucleons and kaons were obtained self-consistently at the quark level, and the coupled equations for meson fields, $\beta$-equilibrium, and charge neutrality were solved at given baryon densities for magnetic field strengths $B^* = B/B_c^e = 0$, $10^5$, and $10^6$, where $B_c^e = 4.414 \times 10^{13}$ G. Results were compared with those from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model using the TM1 parameter set, with kaon-meson couplings adjusted to match the antikaon optical potential from the QMC model.

## Key numbers

- Threshold densities for $K^{-}$ condensation in the QMC model: 0.513, 0.572, and 1.277 fm$^{-3}$ for $B^* = 0$, $10^5$, and $10^6$, respectively.
- Threshold densities for $\bar{K}^0$ condensation in the QMC model: 0.750, 0.728, and 0.934 fm$^{-3}$ for $B^* = 0$, $10^5$, and $10^6$, respectively.
- Threshold densities for $K^{-}$ condensation in the TM1 model: 0.514, 0.588, and 1.732 fm$^{-3}$ for $B^* = 0$, $10^5$, and $10^6$, respectively.
- Threshold densities for $\bar{K}^0$ condensation in the TM1 model: 0.905, 0.885, and 1.316 fm$^{-3}$ for $B^* = 0$, $10^5$, and $10^6$, respectively.
- Antikaon optical potential at normal nuclear matter density in the QMC model: $U_{\bar{K}}(\rho_0) = -123$ MeV.
- Maximum masses of neutron stars with antikaon condensation in the field-free case: about $1.85 M_\odot$ (QMC model) and $2.09 M_\odot$ (TM1 model).
- Direct URCA process critical density in the QMC model at $B=0$: $\rho_{\mathrm{URCA}} \approx 0.29$ fm$^{-3}$, corresponding to gravitational mass $M > 1.08 M_\odot$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars using the QMC model (Panda 2005) and investigations of strong magnetic field effects on $K^{-}$ condensation within a relativistic mean-field model (Kaon02). The results show quantitative differences between the QMC and RMF (TM1) models, with the QMC model predicting lower threshold densities for antikaon condensation in strong magnetic fields. The findings enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors with strong internal magnetic fields, which may reach $10^{18}$ G as estimated in theoretical works (Lattimer 2001, Mao 2003), and have implications for the maximum mass and cooling behavior of magnetized neutron stars.
