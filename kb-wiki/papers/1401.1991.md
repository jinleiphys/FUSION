## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly the ¤ë meson, where earlier models (Klingl 1998, Post 2000) reported in-medium widths around 40 MeV, while Riek (2004) found approximately 60 MeV. The present calculation yields a significantly larger width of 121 MeV at saturation density, similar to the more recent results of Cabrera (2013). The results are relevant for interpreting experimental data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Trnka 2005), which reported an experimental width of approximately 130-150 MeV at an average momentum of 1.1 GeV/c, and from the KEK E325 Collaboration (Ozawa 2000), CLAS (Wood 2010), and HADES (Agakishiev 2012). The methodology parallels the authors' previous work on the K╠à* meson in dense matter (Tolos 2010). No clear conclusion on the ¤ë mass shift could be drawn due to uncontrolled high-momentum components of the ¤Ç and ¤ü propagators.
