# The width of the $ω$ meson in dense matter

> We obtain the width of the $ω$ meson in dense nuclear matter by taking into account (i) the free decay of the $ω$ into three pions, which is dominated by $ρπ$ mode, (ii) the processes induced by a vector-baryon interaction dominated by vector meson exchange, and (iii) the $ω\to K \bar K$ mechanism in matter. The $ω$ meson develops an important width in matter, coming from the dominant $ω\to ρπ$ decay mode, with a value of $121 \pm 10$ MeV at normal nuclear matter density for an $ω$ at rest. At finite momentum, the width of the $ω$ meson increases moderately with values of 200 MeV at 600 MeV/c.

# The width of the ω meson in dense matter

## Key claim
The ω meson acquires a large in-medium width of 121 ± 10 MeV at normal nuclear matter density for an ω at rest, dominated by the ω → ρπ decay mode; this value is larger than earlier theoretical estimates (Klingl 1998, Post 2000, Riek 2004) but consistent with more recent calculations (Cabrera 2013) and experimental data from CBELSA/TAPS (Trnka 2005) when extrapolated to finite momentum.

## Method
The ω meson self-energy in dense nuclear matter was computed by including three contributions: (i) the free ω → 3π decay via the dominant ω → ρπ mechanism, with the ρ and π mesons dressed by their in-medium self-energies; (ii) the ω → K K̅ mechanism, incorporating medium modifications of the K and K̅ mesons from a chiral unitary approach for K̅ N interactions and low-density approximation for K N interactions; and (iii) s-wave ω N interactions with vector mesons and baryons constructed within the hidden gauge formalism in coupled channels, solved via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with medium modifications on intermediate states. Three different prescriptions for the ρ meson spectral function were employed, and the ω → ρπ coupling was adjusted to reproduce the complete free ω → 3π width.

## Key numbers
- Free ω meson width: Γ(0)ω = 8.49 ± 0.08 MeV, with 89.2% from the 3π decay channel
- In-medium ω width at rest at saturation density ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³: Γω(ρ0, mω) = 121 ± 10 MeV, composed of 7.6 MeV (free width) + 101.2 MeV (ω N → ππ N, ω N N → π N N) + 2.9 MeV (ω N → K Y) + 0.5 MeV (ω N → K* Y → ρ N) + 9 MeV (ω N → π N)
- In-medium width correction from ω → K K̅ at ρ0 and free ω mass: 2.9 MeV for ω at rest
- In-medium width correction from s-wave ω N → VB interaction at ρ0 and free ω mass: 0.5 MeV
- At finite momentum, the ω → 3π width reaches approximately 200 MeV at P = 600 MeV/c
- The ω → ρπ coupling adjusted to reproduce the free ω → 3π width: G = 15.7 GeV⁻¹ (or G = 11.9 GeV⁻¹ with contact term)

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly the ω meson, where earlier models (Klingl 1998, Post 2000) reported in-medium widths around 40 MeV, while Riek (2004) found approximately 60 MeV. The present calculation yields a significantly larger width of 121 MeV at saturation density, similar to the more recent results of Cabrera (2013). The results are relevant for interpreting experimental data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Trnka 2005), which reported an experimental width of approximately 130-150 MeV at an average momentum of 1.1 GeV/c, and from the KEK E325 Collaboration (Ozawa 2000), CLAS (Wood 2010), and HADES (Agakishiev 2012). The methodology parallels the authors' previous work on the K̅* meson in dense matter (Tolos 2010). No clear conclusion on the ω mass shift could be drawn due to uncontrolled high-momentum components of the π and ρ propagators.
