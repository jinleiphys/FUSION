# Alternate derivation of the Ginocchio--Haxton relation [(2j-3)/6]

> We have found an alternate way of deriving the Ginocchio-Haxton relation [(2j-3)/6], where the square brackets mean the largest integer that is less than or equal to what is inside them. Our derivation involves the calculation of the number of states with total angular momentum J=j for 3 identical particles (e.g., neutrons) in a j-shell.

# Alternate derivation of the Ginocchio–Haxton relation [(2j-3)/6]

## Key claim
The paper derives an alternate expression for the number of states with total angular momentum J=j for three identical fermions in a j-shell, showing that the maximum value of the integer n (denoted n̄) satisfies n̄ < (2j-1)/6, which is equivalent to the Ginocchio–Haxton relation n̄ = [(2j-3)/6] where the brackets denote the floor function.

## Method
The authors construct states of three identical particles in a j-shell with magnetic quantum numbers M1 > M2 > M3 and total M = M1+M2+M3. They first consider all states with M = j+1, then lower M3 by one to obtain states with J > j and M = j. The remaining states with M = j are those with J = j, which have the structure M1, M2, M2-1. The possible values of M1 and M2 are expressed as j-2n and 1/2+n respectively, where n = 0, 1, 2, ..., and the condition M1 > M2 yields the inequality for n̄.

## Key numbers
- The maximum integer n (denoted n̄) satisfies n̄ < (2j-1)/6, which is equivalent to n̄ = [(2j-3)/6] where the brackets denote the largest integer less than or equal to the argument.
- The total number of J=j states is N = n̄ + 1, and n̄ itself is the number of seniority 3 states.
- The inequality n̄ < (2j-1)/6 leads to N < (2j+5)/6.
- For the case where (2j-1)/6 equals an integer I, then n̄ = [(2j-3)/6] = [I-1/3] = I-1.

## Context
This work provides an alternative derivation of the Ginocchio–Haxton relation, originally presented by Ginocchio and Haxton (1993) and also discussed in Talmi's 1993 book. The derivation uses a direct counting approach based on the magnetic quantum numbers of three identical fermions in a j-shell, rather than more formal group-theoretic methods. The result also connects to the fact that the number of J=j states for the three-particle system equals the number of J=0 states for a four-particle system, which may enable further studies of seniority and pairing in nuclear shell models.
