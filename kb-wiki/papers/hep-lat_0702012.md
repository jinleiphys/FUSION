# The Delta-resonance in a finite volume

> We study the extraction of Delta-resonance parameters from lattice data for small quark masses, corresponding to the case of an unstable Delta. To this end, we calculate the spectrum of the correlator of two Delta-fields in a finite Euclidian box up-to-and-including O(epsilon^3) in the small scale expansion using infrared regularization. On the basis of our numerical study, we argue that the extraction of the parameters of the Delta-resonance (in particular, of the mass and the pion-nucleon-delta coupling constant) from the measured volume dependence of the lowest energy levels should be feasible.

## Key claim

The authors argue that extracting the Delta-resonance mass and the pion-nucleon-delta coupling constant from the volume dependence of the lowest energy levels in lattice QCD should be feasible, but caution that the Delta width is so large that the avoided level crossing is almost completely washed out from the energy spectrum, requiring an optimum fitting strategy rather than the simple procedure used for narrow resonances.

## Method

The authors calculate the self-energy of the Delta-resonance in the small scale expansion (SSE) at finite volume up to and including O(epsilon^3), using infrared regularization. They compute the poles of the Delta propagator in a finite Euclidean box of size L, which yields the parameterization of the energy spectrum as a function of the pion mass M_pi, box size L, and the physical masses m_Delta(M_pi), m_N(M_pi) and the pi N Delta coupling constant g_{pi N Delta}. The calculation involves evaluating pion-nucleon and pion-Delta loop functions in a finite volume, where loop integrals are replaced by infinite sums over discrete lattice momenta.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed up to and including O(epsilon^3) in the small scale expansion
- The pion mass is given by M_pi^2 = 2B m_hat + O(m_hat^2), where m_hat is the average light quark mass
- The Delta-nucleon mass difference in the chiral limit is denoted Delta_0 = m_Delta^0 - m_N^0
- The authors work in the isospin limit m_u = m_d = m_hat
- The lattice data from Zhou (2006) were performed down to quark masses corresponding to (M_pi/M_rho)^2 = 0.4, while the physical value is (M_pi/M_rho)^2 approximately 0.03

## Context

This work extends the Luscher method for extracting two-body scattering phase shifts from finite-volume energy levels to the specific case of the Delta-resonance, which is unstable at small quark masses. The approach is closely related to the work of Luscher (various papers) and DeGrand (who studied the rho-meson system), but the authors find that the Delta width is so large that the avoided level crossing characteristic of narrow resonances is almost completely washed out. This enables the development of an optimum fitting strategy for extracting resonance parameters from lattice data, which is needed because previous lattice calculations of the Delta mass (such as Zhou 2006) were performed at quark masses where the Delta is stable and require large chiral extrapolations to reach the physical point.
