# Importance of static quadrupole moments for determining collective aspects of nuclear structure: N=Z calculations with four and eight valence particles

> In this work we look at the low lying nuclear structure of several N=Z nuclei residing between the doubly magic nucei ^{40} Ca and ^{100} Sn. Using large shell model codes we calculate and discuus the systematics of enegies. We show energy levels, B(E2)'s, static quadrupule moments and g factors. In all cases we compare the results of 2 different interactions which yield significanly different occupation numbers. We compare with the simplest versions of the rotational and vibrational models. By examinnig B(E2)'s and static quadrupole moments we make associations with collective models find that in the model space here considered ,{}^{88} Ru is oblate . The quadruple moment of ^{92} Pd is very small consistent with the vibrational model.

## Key claim

By examining both B(E2) values and static quadrupole moments, the authors associate specific N=Z nuclei with collective models, finding that in the model space considered, 88Ru is oblate and the quadrupole moment of 92Pd is very small, consistent with the vibrational model; however, the overall situation is more complicated because the ratio of B(E2) values for 92Pd does not match the vibrational model prediction.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed for the N=Z even-even nuclei 44Ti, 48Cr, 52Fe, 88Ru, 92Pd, and 96Cd using two different interactions for each mass region: gxpf1 and FPD6 in the f-p shell, and jun45 and jj4b in the g9/2 shell region. The authors calculated excitation energies, B(E2) transition strengths, static quadrupole moments, and g factors for yrast states, and compared the results with the simplest versions of the rotational and vibrational collective models. Occupation percentages of the lowest configurations were also computed to trace differences between interactions.

## Key numbers

* 44Ti: B(E2,2→0) = 103 e²fm⁴ (gxpf1) and 139.8 e²fm⁴ (FPD6); Q(2⁺) = -5.1 e·fm² (gxpf1) and -21.7 e·fm² (FPD6)
* 48Cr: B(E2,2→0) = 243.9 e²fm⁴ (gxpf1) and 312.4 e²fm⁴ (FPD6); Q(2⁺) = -30.2 e·fm² (gxpf1) and -35.4 e·fm² (FPD6)
* 52Fe: B(E2,2→0) = 218.5 e²fm⁴ (gxpf1) and 291.2 e²fm⁴ (FPD6); experimental B(E2) = 164 e²fm⁴
* 88Ru: Q(2⁺) = +36.7 e·fm² (jun45) and +29.0 e·fm² (jj4b), indicating oblate deformation; B(E2,2→0) = 492.0 e²fm⁴ (jun45) and 578.3 e²fm⁴ (jj4b)
* 92Pd: Q(2⁺) = -3.5 e·fm² (jun45) and +4.6 e·fm² (jj4b), both very small; B(E2,2→0) = 304.5 e²fm⁴ (jun45) and 366.2 e²fm⁴ (jj4b)
* 96Cd: B(E2,2→0) = 151.9 e²fm⁴ (jun45) and 154.7 e²fm⁴ (jj4b); Q(2⁺) = -19.3 e·fm² (jun45) and -16.4 e·fm² (jj4b)
* In 48Cr, the static quadrupole moment drops dramatically from -41.5 e·fm² at 10⁺ to -8.0 e·fm² at 12⁺ (FPD6 interaction)
* The experimental g factor of 44Ti is 0.5(15); no other g factors in this work have been measured

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model calculations of B(E2) values in the f7/2 shell by Robinson, Escuderos and Zamick (2005) to include static quadrupole moments and heavier N=Z nuclei up to 96Cd. The study is partly motivated by the work of Cederwall and collaborators (2011) on 92Pd, who noted equally spaced energy levels but B(E2) values closer to the rotational model. The results enable future comparisons with more sophisticated collective models and highlight the need for experimental measurements of static quadrupole moments, as well as the importance of determining correct occupation numbers via transfer reactions as emphasized by Schiffer and collaborators (Kay et al., 2013).
