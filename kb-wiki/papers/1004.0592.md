# Investigation of meson masses for real and imaginary chemical potential

> We investigate chemical-potential ($μ$) and temperature ($T$) dependence of scalar and pseudo-scalar meson masses for both real and imaginary $μ$, using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with three-flavor quarks. A three-flavor phase diagram is drawn in $μ^2$-$T$ plane where positive (negative) $μ^2$ corresponds to positive (imaginary) $μ$. A critical surface is plotted as a function of light- and strange-quark current mass and $μ^2$. We show that $μ$-dependence of the six-quark Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction originated in $U_\mathrm{A}(1)$ anomaly can be determined from lattice QCD data on $η'$ meson mass around $μ=0$ and $μ= i πT/3$ with $T$ slightly above the critical temperature at $μ=0$ where the chiral symmetry is restored at $μ=0$ but broken at $μ=i πT/3$, if it is measured in future.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the mu-dependence of the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction coupling constant G_D can be determined from future lattice QCD data on the eta' meson mass at mu=0 and mu=i pi T/3, but only if the temperature is slightly above the critical temperature at mu=0 where chiral symmetry is restored at mu=0 but broken at mu=i pi T/3.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean field approximation to compute scalar and pseudo-scalar meson masses as functions of real and imaginary chemical potential. Meson masses are obtained from poles of the effective propagator, with the polarization function evaluated at finite temperature and chemical potential. The coupling constant G_D of the KMT determinant interaction is varied to study its effect on meson masses, particularly for the eta' meson.

## Key numbers

- The KMT determinant interaction coupling constant at T=mu=0 is G_D(0) = -12.36 Lambda^{-5}, where Lambda is the three-dimensional momentum cutoff.
- The RW endpoint in the three-flavor phase diagram occurs at mu^2 = -(pi T/3)^2 with T approximately 250 MeV.
- At T=200 MeV, chiral symmetry is restored at mu^2 greater than about 0.1 (GeV)^2 and broken at mu^2 less than about 0.1 (GeV)^2.
- At T=300 MeV, chiral symmetry is restored at mu^2 greater than about -0.02 (GeV)^2 and broken at mu^2 less than about -0.02 (GeV)^2 (corresponding to pi/6 less than or equal to theta less than or equal to pi/3).
- Pion mass shows dips at mu^2 approximately 0.06 (GeV)^2 at T=300 MeV and at mu^2 approximately -0.08 (GeV)^2 at T=200 MeV, attributed to threshold effects.

## Context

This work extends the imaginary chemical potential matching approach previously proposed by Kashiwa et al. (2009) and Sakai et al. (2009), where effective model interactions are determined from lattice QCD data at imaginary chemical potential to avoid the sign problem. The paper extends earlier two-flavor PNJL studies to three flavors, incorporating the KMT determinant interaction that accounts for the U_A(1) anomaly. The results enable future lattice QCD measurements of eta' meson mass at imaginary chemical potential to determine the chemical potential dependence of the KMT coupling, which would allow more reliable predictions of the three-flavor QCD phase diagram at real chemical potential.
