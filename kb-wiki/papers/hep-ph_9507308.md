# Strangelets with finite entropy

> Strangelets with non-zero entropy are studied within the MIT bag model. Explicit account is taken of the constraints that strangelets must be color neutral and have a fixed total momentum. In general, masses increase with increasing entropy per baryon, and the constraints work so as to increase masses further. This has an important destabilizing effect on strangelets produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Strangelets with finite entropy

## Key claim

Strangelet masses increase with entropy per baryon, and the constraints of color singletness and fixed total momentum further increase these masses; however, the calculations use massless quarks (the "most optimistic" case for production) and the multiple reflection expansion which includes only mean shell effects, so the quantitative results represent a lower bound on the destabilizing effect.

## Method

The authors use the multiple reflection expansion approach within the MIT bag model to compute thermodynamic potentials for spherical strangelets containing three flavors of massless quarks with equal chemical potentials. They impose color singlet and zero-momentum constraints via the group-theoretical projection method of Elze and Greiner (1986) in a saddle-point approximation, then solve the equations of mechanical equilibrium, fixed baryon number, and fixed entropy per baryon simultaneously for temperature, chemical potential, and volume.

## Key numbers

- For temperatures of 40 MeV (corresponding to roughly 4 units of entropy per baryon at high baryon numbers), the total increase in energy per baryon from constraints is of order 80 MeV/baryon
- The bag constant is chosen as B^{1/4} = 145 MeV throughout the calculations
- For sufficiently high temperature and low baryon number, the color singlet constraint is equivalent to lowering the temperature by as much as 10 MeV
- The constraints are only important for baryon numbers A < 100
- Shell effects are washed out at temperatures exceeding 10-20 MeV (based on preliminary results from Mustafa and Ansari)

## Context

This work extends the extensive literature on strangelet properties at zero temperature (Bodmer 1971, Chin and Kerman 1979, Witten 1984, Farhi and Jaffe 1984, and others) to the finite entropy regime relevant for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results demonstrate that finite entropy and the constraints of color singletness and fixed momentum significantly destabilize strangelets compared to zero-temperature calculations, which must be taken into account in models for strangelet production and detection in experiments at Brookhaven and CERN, as well as in astrophysical contexts such as proto-neutron stars.
