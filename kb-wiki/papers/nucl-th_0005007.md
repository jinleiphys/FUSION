# A Dynamical Model for the Resonant Multipoles and the $Δ$ Structure

> We show that recent experiment data for the ratios $E_{1^+}/M_{1^+}$ and $S_{1^+}/M_{1^+}$ can be explained in a dynamical model for electromagnetic production of pions, together with a simple scaling assumption for the bare $γ^* N Δ$ form factors. Within our model we find that the bare $Δ$ is almost spherical and the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 quadrupole excitations of the physical $Δ$ are nearly saturated by pion cloud contribution in $Q^2 \le 4.0 GeV^2$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recent experimental data for the ratios \(E_{1^+}/M_{1^+}\) and \(S_{1^+}/M_{1^+}\) in the \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition can be explained by a dynamical model for pion electroproduction, under the condition that a simple scaling assumption is adopted for the bare \(\gamma^* N \Delta\) form factors. The authors find that the bare \(\Delta\) is almost spherical and that the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 quadrupole excitations of the physical \(\Delta\) are nearly saturated by the pion cloud contribution for \(Q^2 \le 4.0\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors employ a dynamical model for electromagnetic pion production that explicitly includes final-state \(\pi N\) interactions via a \(t\)-matrix equation incorporating the \(\pi N\) scattering \(t\)-matrix and a principal-value integral. The transition potential is split into a background term (Born terms and vector meson exchange) and a bare \(\Delta\) contribution, with the resonant multipoles described by a Breit-Wigner form. The \(Q^2\) dependence of the bare magnetic dipole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta\) is parametrized using a dipole form factor with two free parameters, and the small electric and Coulomb amplitudes are assumed to scale identically.

## Key numbers

* Bare magnetic dipole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta(0) = 158 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) at \(Q^2=0\), compared to the dressed value \(289 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and the PDG value \(293 \pm 8 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\).
* Bare electric quadrupole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{E}}^\Delta(0) = 0.4 \pm 0.3 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) at \(Q^2=0\), compared to the dressed value \(-7 \pm 0.4 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and the PDG value \(-4.5 \pm 4.2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\).
* Bare helicity amplitudes at \(Q^2=0\): \(A_{1/2}^\Delta = -80 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}^\Delta = -136 \pm 3 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\); dressed values are \(-134 \pm 2\) and \(-256 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\), respectively.
* Fit parameters for the \(Q^2\) dependence of \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta\): \(\beta = 0.44\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and \(\gamma = 0.38\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The ratios \(R_{EM} = E_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) and \(R_{SM} = S_{1+}^{(3/2)}/M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) remain small and negative up to \(Q^2 = 4.0\) GeV\(^2\), in agreement with the data from Frolov et al. (1999).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Tanabe and Yang (1985) and Yang (1985) for pion photo- and electroproduction to explain recent experimental data from Frolov et al. (1999) at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and 4.0 GeV\(^2\), which contradicted earlier positive-sign results from Burkert (1995) at \(Q^2 = 3.2\) GeV\(^2\). The results support the conclusion that pion cloud (rescattering) effects dominate the quadrupole excitations, consistent with cloudy bag model calculations by Kalberman and collaborators (1983) and Bermuth and collaborators (1988), and with nonperturbative chiral chromodielectric model results by Fiolhais, Golli, and Sirca (1996). The work enables future searches for the \(Q^2\) region where perturbative QCD predictions (helicity conservation leading to \(R_{EM} \rightarrow 1\)) begin to apply.
