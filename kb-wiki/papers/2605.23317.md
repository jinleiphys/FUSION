# Enhanced Stellar Production of Weakly Interacting Slim Particles from Non-Thermal Nuclear Cascades

> Weakly interacting slim particles (WISPs) can be produced in stars through the conversion of non-thermal photons generated in nuclear reactions. Previous studies have generally treated these sources only at the level of their primary injection lines. We show that this picture is incomplete: repeated Compton scatterings redistribute the injected photons into a broad low-energy spectrum, while associated positrons can thermalize and annihilate into a 511~keV line. Together, these effects define a generic non-thermal photon reservoir and thus a broadly applicable source term for any photon-coupled WISP. We develop a general framework for this mechanism and illustrate its impact with the example of dark-photon production in the solar pp chain. Our results show that non-thermal stellar WISP production can be substantially underestimated if Compton reprocessing and positron annihilation are neglected.

## Key claim

Previous studies of stellar WISP production from nuclear reactions considered only the primary injection lines, but this picture is incomplete because repeated Compton scatterings redistribute injected photons into a broad low-energy spectrum and associated positrons thermalize and annihilate into a 511 keV line, creating a generic non-thermal photon reservoir that can substantially enhance WISP yields.

## Method

The authors developed a general framework treating the Compton cascade of non-thermal photons injected by nuclear reactions, where each successive Compton scattering provides an independent conversion opportunity for photon-coupled WISPs. They computed the cumulative spectral weight by summing the normalized energy distributions after each scattering iteration up to a maximum number determined by when inverse-bremsstrahlung absorption overtakes Compton scattering, and applied this to dark-photon production in the solar pp chain using a realistic solar model with local volumetric rates of pp and pep reactions.

## Key numbers

- The 5.49 MeV photon from radiative deuterium capture (p + d → ³He + γ) undergoes up to N_max = 57 Compton scatterings before cascade termination at r ≈ 0.1 R_⊙
- The 511 keV photons from positron annihilation undergo up to N_max = 55 Compton scatterings before cascade termination at r ≈ 0.1 R_⊙
- The plasma frequency ω_pl in the solar interior ranges from approximately 300 eV in the core to negligible values near the surface
- The Earth-Sun distance d_⊙ is approximately 1 AU

## Context

This work extends the analysis of D'Eramo et al. (2023), who studied dark-photon production from the 5.49 MeV solar pp-chain line but retained only the primary injection photon, by showing that the Compton cascade and 511 keV positron-annihilation line provide additional conversion channels that can dominate over primary-line estimates. The framework is applicable to any nuclear process in stars that injects energetic photons or charged particles, and to any WISP coupled to photons including axions, dilatons, and chameleons beyond the dark-photon case explicitly shown.
