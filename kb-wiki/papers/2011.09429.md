# Zilch Vortical Effect for Fermions

> We consider the notion of zilch current that was recently discussed in the literature as an alternative helicity measure for photons. Developing this idea, we suggest the generalization of the zilch for the systems of fermions. We start with the definition of the photonic zilch current in chiral kinetic theory framework and work out field-theoretical definition of the fermionic zilch using the Wigner function formalism. This object has similar properties to the photonic zilch and is conserved in the non-interacting theory. We also show that, in full analogy with a case of photons, the fermionic zilch acquires a non-trivial contribution due to the medium rotation - zilch vortical effect (ZVE) for fermions. Combined with a previously studied ZVE for photons, these results form a wider set of chiral effects parameterized by the spin of the particles and the spin of the current. We briefly discuss the origin of the ZVE, its possible relation to the anomalies in the underlying microscopic theory and possible application for studying the spin polarization in chiral media.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the concept of zilch currents, previously defined for photons, to systems of fermions, and shows that these fermionic zilch currents acquire a non-zero expectation value in a rotating medium (the zilch vortical effect, ZVE). This effect is presented as a family of chiral effects parameterized by an odd integer \(s\), with the usual chiral vortical effect (CVE) recovered as the special case \(s=1\).

## Method

The authors first define the fermionic zilch current within the chiral kinetic theory (CKT) framework, generalizing the photonic zilch phase-space density to fermions. They then derive a field-theoretical definition of the fermionic zilch using the Wigner function formalism for massless fermions, obtaining an expression in terms of Dirac fields and symmetrized derivatives. Finally, they verify the CKT result for the ZVE by computing the same effect via the Kubo formula for linear response to rotation, treating the rotation as an external gravitational field and summing the dynamical and contact contributions to the vortical conductivity.

## Key numbers

* For a rotating fermion gas at zero chemical potential (\(\mu = 0\)), the expectation value of the zilch current component \(\langle :\mathcal{Z}^{i0..0}: \rangle\) equals \(\frac{(-1)^{\frac{s-1}{2}}}{3\pi^2} \Omega^i T^{s+1} \left(1 - \frac{1}{2^{s}}\right) \frac{(s+2)(s+1)!}{s} \zeta(s+1)\), where \(\Omega^i\) is the vorticity, \(T\) is the temperature, and \(s\) is an odd integer.
* For a rotating fermion gas at zero temperature (\(T=0\)), the same expectation value equals \(\frac{(-1)^{\frac{s-1}{2}}}{6\pi^2} \frac{(s+2)}{s} \Omega^i \mu^{s+1}\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential.
* The total vortical conductivity \(\sigma\) for the zilch current, obtained from the Kubo formula, is \(\sigma = \frac{(-1)^{\frac{s-1}{2}}}{2\pi^2} \frac{(s+1)(s+2)}{3s} \int_0^\infty dq \, q^{s} [n_F(q-\mu) + n_F(q+\mu)]\), where \(n_F\) is the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the zilch vortical effect, previously introduced for photons by Chernodub (2018) and studied in chiral kinetic theory by Huang (2020), to fermionic systems. It provides a unified family of chiral transport phenomena parameterized by the spin of the current, with the standard chiral vortical effect (CVE) for fermions as the \(s=1\) case. The results enable future studies of the connection between the thermal part of the CVE and gravitational anomalies, and may have applications in understanding spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
