# Hybrid stars in light of the HESS J1731-347 remnant and the PREX-II experiment

> The recent analysis on the central compact object in the HESS J1731-347 remnant suggests interestingly small values for its mass and radius. Such an observation favors soft nuclear models that may be challenged by the observation of massive compact stars. In contrast, the recent PREX-II experiment, concerning the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb, points towards stiff equations of state that favor larger compact star radii. In the present study, we aim to explore the compatibility between stiff hadronic equations of state (favored by PREX-II) and the HESS J1731-347 remnant in the context of hybrid stars. For the construction of hybrid equations of state we use three widely employed Skyrme models combined with the well-known vector MIT bag model. Furthermore we consider two different scenarios concerning the energy density of the bag. In the first case, that of a constant bag parameter, we find that the resulting hybrid equations of state are strongly disfavored by the observation of $\sim2 M_\odot$ pulsars. However, the introduction of a Gaussian density dependence yields results that are compatible with the conservative $2 M_\odot$ constraint. The utilization of recent data based on the observation of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0952-0607 and GW190814 allows for the imposition of additional constraints on the relevant parameters and the stiffness of the two phases. Interestingly, we find that the derived hybrid equations of state do not satisfy the PSR J0030+0451 constraints in $1σ$ and only marginally agree with the $2σ$ estimations. In addition, we estimate that the observation of massive pulsars, like PSR J0952-0607, in combination with the existence of HESS J1731-347, may require a strong phase transition below $\sim 1.7n_0$. Finally, we show that the supermassive compact object involved in GW190814 could potentially be explained as a rapidly rotating hybrid star.

## Key claim

The authors find that hybrid stars combining stiff hadronic equations of state (favored by the PREX-II experiment) with a density-dependent bag parameter can simultaneously satisfy the small mass and radius constraints from the HESS J1731-347 remnant and the conservative 2 solar mass pulsar constraint, but only if the bag parameter has a Gaussian density dependence rather than a constant value.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining three Skyrme hadronic models (Ska, SkI3, SkI5) with the vector MIT bag model for quark matter, using Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition. They consider two scenarios for the bag parameter: a constant value and a Gaussian density-dependent parametrization, and vary the coupling constant G_v and bag parameters to explore the parameter space. The resulting mass-radius relations are compared against observational constraints from the HESS J1731-347 remnant, massive pulsars (PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0952-0607), and the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers

- The HESS J1731-347 remnant has mass M = 0.77 (+0.20, -0.17) solar masses and radius R = 10.4 (+0.86, -0.78) km at 1 sigma confidence (Doroshenko 2022)
- The PREX-II experiment measured the neutron skin thickness of Pb-208 as Delta r_np = 0.283 +/- 0.071 fm, corresponding to a symmetry energy slope L = 106 +/- 37 MeV (Reed 2021)
- For constant bag parameter, the maximum hybrid star mass is only 1.44 solar masses (Ska), 1.43 solar masses (SkI3), and 1.43 solar masses (SkI5), all below the 2 solar mass constraint
- For density-dependent bag with G_v = 0.25 fm^2, the maximum transition density is 0.280 fm^{-3} (Ska), 0.261 fm^{-3} (SkI3), and 0.222 fm^{-3} (SkI5)
- The GW190814 event involves a compact object of 2.5-2.67 solar masses that could potentially be explained as a rapidly rotating hybrid star

## Context

This work addresses the tension between the soft equation of state required by the HESS J1731-347 remnant (Doroshenko 2022) and the stiff equation of state favored by the PREX-II experiment (Reed 2021). Previous studies have examined strange star scenarios (Di Clemente 2023, Horvath 2023) and hybrid star explanations (Tsaloukidis 2023, Brodie 2023, Sagun 2023) for the HESS J1731-347 object, but the present study specifically investigates whether stiff hadronic models compatible with PREX-II can accommodate this light compact object through a hadron-quark phase transition. The results enable future work to constrain the density dependence of the bag parameter and the stiffness of both hadronic and quark phases using combined observations of low-mass and high-mass compact objects.
