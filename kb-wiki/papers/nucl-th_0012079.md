# Particle-Rotor Model Description of Doubly Odd Ta Isotopes

> The doubly odd Ta isotopes are studied within the framework of the particle-rotor model. The main aim of this study is to obtain information on the neutron-proton interaction in deformed nuclei, with particular attention focused on the role of the tensor force. To this end, both zero-range abd finite-range phenomenological interactions are considered. Comparison of the calculated results with experimental data provides evidence of the importance of the tensor-force effects. We have also performed calculations using a realistic two-body G matrix for the neutron proton interaction. Some preliminary results are presented here.

## Key claim
The tensor force in the effective neutron-proton interaction is essential for correctly reproducing the level order and odd-even staggering in doubly odd Ta isotopes, but only when using a finite-range Gaussian force with tensor terms; zero-range and pure central forces fail to match experimental data.

## Method
The authors employed the particle-rotor model, coupling an odd neutron and odd proton to an axially symmetric deformed core described by the Nilsson potential. They compared three forms of the neutron-proton interaction: a finite-range Gaussian force with central plus tensor terms, a Gaussian central-only force, and a zero-range delta force, with parameters from Boisson et al. (1976). Additionally, they performed preliminary calculations using a realistic bare G matrix derived from the Bonn-A free nucleon-nucleon potential, computed in a Nilsson basis via the Tsai-Kuo method.

## Key numbers
- For the lowest K^π = 0^- band in ¹⁸²Ta, the calculated Newby shift was 28 keV (Gaussian central plus tensor), 1 keV (Gaussian central only), and -6 keV (delta force), compared to the experimental value of 26 keV.
- The discrepancy between calculated and experimental excitation energies for the K^π = 0^- band in ¹⁸²Ta was at most about 20 keV when using the Gaussian force with tensor terms.
- For the Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting in ¹⁸²Ta, the largest discrepancy between the G-matrix calculation and experiment was 100 keV (for the 7/2[404] p + 3/2[512] n configuration: experimental 139 keV, calculated 374 keV).
- For the lowest K^π = 0^- band in ¹⁸²Ta, the G-matrix calculation gave a Newby shift of 46 keV, compared to the experimental value of 26 keV.
- For ¹⁸⁰Ta, the G-matrix calculation gave a GM splitting of -186 keV for the 7/2[404] p + 9/2[624] n configuration (experimental -100 keV) and -78 keV for the 7/2[404] p + 5/2[512] n configuration (experimental -88 keV).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous particle-rotor model studies of ¹⁷⁴,¹⁷⁶Lu (Itaco et al., prior works) to the heavier doubly odd Ta isotopes ¹⁸⁰Ta and ¹⁸²Ta, confirming the importance of the tensor force for describing K=0 bands and odd-even staggering. The results contradict the common neglect of tensor-force effects in most existing studies of deformed nuclei. The preliminary G-matrix calculations, using a realistic interaction with no adjustable parameters, open the possibility of relating the effective neutron-proton interaction in deformed nuclei to the free nucleon-nucleon potential, a direction not previously attempted in the unified model.
