# Analytical Calculation of the Neutrons Spectrum for Direct Measurement of N-N Scattering at Pulsed Reactor Yaguar

## Key claim
The coincidence scheme for direct n-n scattering measurement at the YAGUAR reactor is absolutely impractical because the estimated number of counts per pulse is only about 10^{-3}, making the background level irrelevant; this conclusion holds even though many researchers had previously assumed the coincidence scheme would have only a 20% loss of intensity with much higher background suppression.

## Method
The author performed analytical calculations using standard neutron scattering theory with the Fermi golden rule and Fermi pseudopotential, working directly in the laboratory reference frame without transformation to the center-of-mass system. The calculation integrates over initial Maxwellian momentum distributions of both neutrons and atoms (treated as identical particles with mass m) to obtain the momentum spectrum of scattered neutrons, then transforms to a time-of-flight spectrum for a single detector. For the coincidence scheme, the calculation restricts the final neutron and atom momenta to opposite directions (approximately 180 degrees) and integrates over a coincidence window.

## Key numbers
- Reactor pulse length: t_p = 0.68 ms
- Neutron flux density: Phi = 0.77 x 10^18 n/cm^2/s
- Collision volume: V = 1.13 cm^3
- Solid angle of collimators: Delta_Omega = 0.64 x 10^{-4}
- Neutron density: n = 3 x 10^12 cm^{-3}
- Singlet scattering amplitude: b_0 = 18 fm, giving |b|^2 = 8.1 x 10^{-25} cm^2
- Estimated neutrons per pulse from simple formula: N_e ~ 170
- Single-detector correction factor from analytical calculation: F = 0.705 (compared to Monte Carlo value F = 0.83)
- Coincidence scheme estimated counts per pulse: N_ec ~ 10^{-3}
- Additional suppression factor for coincidence scheme: F_c = 0.15
- Ratio of coincidence to single-detector rate: W ~ 0.1 dOmega (tau/t), where tau is the coincidence window (~0.5 ms) and t_T ~ 5 ms

## Context
This work extends the project described in prior publications (the author cites references [1] and [2] which proposed direct n-n scattering measurement at YAGUAR and presented Monte Carlo simulations). The analytical calculation confirms the Monte Carlo result (F=0.83) with F=0.705, with the difference attributed to the analytical calculation using only a Maxwellian spectrum while the Monte Carlo included an epithermal tail. The main conclusion contradicts the prevailing opinion among project participants that a coincidence scheme would be advantageous, showing instead that the coincidence scheme reduces the signal by a factor of about 10^{-5} relative to the single-detector scheme, making the experiment non-feasible regardless of background levels. The author notes that background from neutron scattering on residual gas molecules could also be calculated analytically.
