# Relativistic Mean-Field Theory and the High-Density Nuclear Equation of State

> The properties of high-density nuclear and neutron matter are studied using a relativistic mean-field approximation to the nuclear matter energy functional. Based on ideas of effective field theory, nonlinear interactions between the fields are introduced to parametrize the density dependence of the energy functional. Various types of nonlinearities involving scalar-isoscalar ($σ$), vector-isoscalar ($ω$), and vector-isovector ($ρ$) fields are studied. After calibrating the model parameters at equilibrium nuclear matter density, the model and parameter dependence of the resulting equation of state is examined in the neutron-rich and high-density regime. It is possible to build different models that reproduce the same observed properties at normal nuclear densities, but which yield maximum neutron star masses that differ by more than one solar mass. Implications for the existence of kaon condensates or quark cores in neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim

Different relativistic mean-field models that reproduce identical nuclear matter properties at equilibrium density can yield maximum neutron star masses that differ by more than one solar mass, because the quartic neutral vector meson (omega) interaction term, which is poorly constrained by equilibrium data, dominates the high-density behavior of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field energy functional for nuclear and neutron matter, including nonlinear self-interactions and mixed interactions among scalar-isoscalar (sigma), vector-isoscalar (omega), and vector-isovector (rho) meson fields up to fourth order in the fields. They calibrated model parameters to five equilibrium nuclear matter properties (density, binding energy, effective mass, compression modulus, and symmetry energy) and then extrapolated the resulting equations of state to high density, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses from different models that reproduce the same equilibrium nuclear matter properties differ by more than one solar mass.
- The high-density limit of the equation of state can range from the causal limit (p = E) to that of a free relativistic gas (p = E/3), depending on the choice of nonlinear couplings.
- The equilibrium nuclear matter properties used for calibration are five: equilibrium density (rho_0), binding energy (-e_0), nucleon effective mass at equilibrium (M*_0), compression modulus (K_0), and bulk symmetry energy (a_4).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analyses of Bodmer (1991) and Bodmer and Price (1989) on the sensitivity of the high-density equation of state to nonlinear meson interactions, and it generalizes the original Walecka model (1974) and its extensions by Boguta (1977) and Reinhard (1986) by including quartic self-interactions for the omega and rho mesons. The results imply that without better constraints on the quartic omega coupling, it will be impossible to determine whether exotic components such as kaon condensates (Politzer, 1991) or quark cores (Collins and Perry, 1975; Baym and Chin, 1976) exist in neutron stars, because the uncertainty in the purely nucleonic equation of state masks their signatures.
