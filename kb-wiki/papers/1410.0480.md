# Partial dynamical symmetry and odd-even staggering in deformed nuclei

## Key claim
Partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) is shown to be relevant for describing the odd-even staggering in the γ-band of ¹⁵⁶Gd while retaining solvability and good SU(3) symmetry for the ground and β bands, but only for specific classes of interacting boson model Hamiltonians where the symmetry-breaking terms are chosen to annihilate the ground and β band states.

## Method
The study constructs SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry Hamiltonians within the interacting boson model (IBM) by expanding in terms of tensors that annihilate prescribed sets of states, specifically the ground band members |[N](2N,0)K=0,L⟩ and β band members |[N](2N-4,2)K=0,L⟩. Two classes of Hamiltonians are surveyed: one (Ĥ_PDS) with solvable ground and β bands that mixes the γ band with higher-lying excited bands, and another (Ĥ'_PDS) with solvable γ^k (K=2k) band members. The calculated spectra and E2 transition rates are compared to experimental data for ¹⁵⁶Gd.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁵⁶Gd with N=12 bosons, the PDS Hamiltonian parameters are α₁=-7.6 keV, α₂=12.0 keV, α₃=0 keV, η₁=-0.23 keV, η₃=1.54 keV
- The PDS calculation yields 15% SU(3) admixtures into the dominant (2N-4,2) component for γ-band wave functions
- The E2 operator uses effective boson charge e_b=0.166 eb and χ=-0.168
- Observed B(E2) for 2⁺_γ→0⁺_1 is 0.0233(8) eb², compared to DS value 0.035 eb² and PDS value 0.030 eb²
- Observed B(E2) for 2⁺_γ→2⁺_1 is 0.0361(12) eb², compared to DS value 0.056 eb² and PDS value 0.048 eb²
- Observed B(E2) for 2⁺_γ→4⁺_1 is 0.0038(2) eb², compared to DS value 0.0037 eb² and PDS value 0.0031 eb²
- Observed B(E2) for 4⁺_γ→2⁺_β is 0.0214(80) eb², compared to DS value 0.0033 eb² and PDS value 0.0096 eb²

## Context
This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) introduced by Leviatan (2011) to describe odd-even staggering in deformed nuclei, specifically addressing the failure of the standard SU(3) dynamical symmetry of the interacting boson model (Iachello, 1987) to reproduce γ-band energies in ¹⁵⁶Gd. The PDS approach improves upon earlier attempts by Bonatsos (1988) coupling the γ band to the β band and by Minkov (2000) coupling to the ground band, which both failed to describe the odd-even staggering in this nucleus. The results enable systematic selection of higher-order terms in collective Hamiltonians and can be applied to beyond-mean-field methods for heavy nuclei.
