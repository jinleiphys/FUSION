# Probing the neutron-skin thickness via the double-charge exchange reactions in pion-nucleus collisions

> Within the framework of Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, we investigate the influence of the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei on the pion emission in the double-charge exchange reactions near the $\triangle$-resonance energy, in particular, the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra, charged pion ratios et al. The reactions induced by pions are associated with the multiple processes of pions, nucleons and $\triangle$-resonances, correlated with the neutron-skin thickness. It is found that the kinetic energy spectra of $π^{-}$/$π^{+}$ ratio are sensitive to the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei for extracting the subsaturation-density symmetry energy. The double-charge exchange reactions are influenced by the neutron-skin thickness, in-medium properties of resonances, pion-nucleon potential and pion-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The kinetic energy spectra of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in double-charge exchange reactions induced by pions near the Δ-resonance energy are sensitive to the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei, but this sensitivity depends on whether the reaction is initiated by π⁻ or π⁺ and on the kinetic energy region considered.

## Method

The authors employed the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model to simulate pion-nucleus collisions at an incident momentum of 300 MeV/c on targets of ⁴⁸Ca, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹³²Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb. The neutron-skin thickness was varied by adjusting the diffuseness parameter of the neutron density distribution in the target initialization, using Skyrme Hartree-Fock density profiles with a two-parameter Fermi form. The model self-consistently implements elastic and inelastic hadron-hadron collisions, production and decay of Δ(1232) and N*(1440,1535) resonances, and the pion-nucleon potential in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- Incident momentum of pions: 300 MeV/c for all reactions studied.
- Symmetry energy slope parameters used: L = 53 MeV (soft), 82 MeV (linear), and 139 MeV (hard).
- Maximum cross sections for Δ(1232) resonance formation: σ_max(π⁺ + p → Δ⁺⁺) = σ_max(π⁻ + n → Δ⁻) = 200 mb; σ_max(π⁰ + p → Δ⁺) = σ_max(π⁰ + n → Δ⁰) = 133.3 mb; σ_max(π⁻ + p → Δ⁰) = σ_max(π⁺ + n → Δ⁺) = 66.7 mb.
- For the (π⁻, π⁺) double-charge exchange reaction, the DCX cross-section is larger for targets with thinner neutron-skin thickness at kinetic energies greater than 30 MeV.
- The neutron skin significantly influences the DCX process at kinetic energies above 90 MeV.
- For the (π⁺, π⁻) reaction, the DCX cross-section is larger for targets with thicker neutron skin at low kinetic energies.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of neutron-skin thickness using pion-induced reactions, building on earlier GiBUU transport model calculations by Buss et al. (2006) that showed a 35% enhancement of DCX cross sections with inclusion of the neutron-skin profile for π⁺ + Pb → π⁻ + X. The study also connects to the use of charged pion ratios in heavy-ion collisions for constraining symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, as discussed by Ouzounov et al. (2011). The results suggest that DCX reactions and π⁻/π⁺ ratios in pion-induced reactions can serve as probes for extracting the neutron-skin thickness, which is relevant for future experiments at facilities such as HIAF and CiADS.
