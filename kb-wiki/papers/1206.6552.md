# Microscopic Study of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in Cd, Sn and Pb Isotopes

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in Cd, Sn and Pb isotopes has been studied within the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock+BCS and quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA). Three Skyrme parameter sets are used in the calculations, i.e., SLy5, SkM* and SkP, since they are characterized by different values of the compression modulus in symmetric nuclear matter, namely K=230, 217, and 202 MeV, respectively. We also investigate the effect of different types of pairing forces on the ISGMR in Cd, Sn and Pb isotopes. The calculated peak energies and the strength distributions of ISGMR are compared with available experimental data. We find that SkP fails completely to describe the ISGMR strength distribution for all isotopes due to its low value of the nuclear matter incompressibility, namely K=202 MeV. On the other hand, the SLy5 parameter set, supplemented by an appropriate pairing interaction, gives a reasonable description of the ISGMR in Cd and Pb isotopes. A better description of ISGMR in Sn isotopes is achieved by the SkM* interaction, that has a somewhat softer value of the nuclear incompressibility.

## Key claim

The authors find that no single Skyrme interaction can simultaneously reproduce the ISGMR in Cd, Sn, and Pb isotopes; the SLy5 force (with nuclear matter incompressibility K=230 MeV) works best for Pb and Cd, while the SkM* force (K=217 MeV) gives a better description for Sn isotopes, with pairing effects reducing but not eliminating the discrepancy in extracted incompressibility to about 5%.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations for ground states and quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) for excited states, using three Skyrme parameter sets (SLy5, SkM*, SkP) with different nuclear matter incompressibilities (230, 217, and 202 MeV respectively). They compared volume, surface, and mixed zero-range pairing interactions, fitting the pairing strength to reproduce empirical pairing gaps from odd-even mass differences in one representative nucleus per isotope chain (112Cd, 120Sn, 204Pb). The calculations included all terms of the interaction, including two-body spin-orbit and Coulomb parts, in both the mean-field and residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility values used: K=230 MeV (SLy5), 217 MeV (SkM*), 202 MeV (SkP)
- ISGMR centroid energy in 110Cd: 16.18 MeV (SkM* with mixed pairing), compared to experimental 16.3±0.1 MeV
- ISGMR centroid energy in 120Sn: 15.78 MeV (SkM* with mixed pairing), compared to experimental 15.7±0.1 MeV
- ISGMR centroid energy in 208Pb: 13.77 MeV (SLy5 with mixed pairing), compared to experimental 13.96±0.2 MeV
- Maximum difference in ISGMR centroid energy due to different pairing models: 280 keV in 110Cd, 110 keV in 120Sn
- Experimental peak energies for 204-208Pb isotopes: 13.98, 13.94, and 13.90 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the ISGMR in Sn isotopes (Li 2007, 2008) and Pb isotopes (Colo 2004) by including Cd isotopes and performing fully self-consistent QRPA calculations that incorporate two-body spin-orbit interactions, which earlier works (Sagawa 2007, Li 2008, Tse 2009) had omitted. The results confirm the puzzle that Sn isotopes require a softer incompressibility than Pb, as noted by Li (2008) and Pie (2007, 2009), and show that Cd data are consistent with Sn data. The findings enable future work to constrain the attractive particle-particle matrix elements in QRPA and to determine whether volume or surface pairing interactions should be preferred for describing giant resonances.
