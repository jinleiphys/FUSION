# 1/N_c and 1/n preasymptotic corrections to Current-Current correlators

> We obtain the corrections in $1/n$ and in $1/\ln n$ ($n$ is the principal quantum number of the bound state) of the decay constants of scalar and pseudoscalar currents in two and four dimensions in the large $N_c$. We obtain them from the operator product expansion provided a model for the large $n$ mass spectrum is given. In the two-dimensional case the spectrum is known and the corrections obtained in this paper are model independent. We confirm these results by confronting them with the numerical solution of the 't Hooft model. We also consider a model at finite $N_c$ and obtain the associated decay constants that are consistent with perturbation theory. This example shows that that the inclusion of perturbative corrections, or finite $N_c$ effects, to the OPE does not constrain the slope of the Regge trajectories, which remain a free parameter for each different channel.

## Key claim

The paper derives the preasymptotic corrections in \(1/n\) and \(1/\ln n\) to the decay constants of scalar and pseudoscalar currents in two and four dimensions, working in the large-\(N_c\) limit. The authors show that the inclusion of perturbative corrections or finite-\(N_c\) effects in the operator product expansion does not constrain the slope of Regge trajectories, which remains a free parameter for each channel.

## Method

The authors match the hadronic representation of current-current correlators (expressed as a sum over narrow resonances) to the operator product expansion (OPE) at large Euclidean momentum \(Q^2\), using an Euler-Maclaurin formula to convert the sum over principal quantum number \(n\) into an integral plus corrections. They assume a linear Regge trajectory for the mass spectrum at large \(n\) and expand both masses and decay constants in powers of \(1/n\) and \(1/\ln n\), then match order by order in \(1/Q^2\) to determine the decay constant coefficients. In two dimensions, they apply the same method to the 't Hooft model, where the mass spectrum is known analytically, and compare their analytical results to numerical solutions of the 't Hooft equation.

## Key numbers

- In four dimensions, the leading-order decay constant for scalar/pseudoscalar currents is given by \(F^2_{X,0}(n;\mu) = \frac{B_X^2}{8\pi^2} N_c \left[ \left( \frac{a(nB_X)}{a(\mu^2)} \right)^{\bar{\gamma}_0} \frac{c(a(nB_X))}{c(a(\mu^2))} \right]^2 \left( 1 + r_1 a(nB_X) + r_2 a(nB_X)^2 + r_3 a(nB_X)^3 \right)\), with \(r_1 = 17 N_c / 8\), \(r_2 = N_c^2 (7431 - 160\pi^2 - 1920\zeta(3))/768\), and \(r_3 = N_c^3 (25999999 - 1095264\pi^2 - 11200032\zeta(3) + 1607040\zeta(5))/497664\).
- In the 't Hooft model (two dimensions), the mass spectrum at leading order is \(M^2_{\text{LO}}(n) = B^{(1)} n\) with \(B^{(1)} = \pi^2 \beta^2\), and the next-to-leading order correction includes \(B^{(0)} = (m_{i,R}^2 + m_{j,R}^2) \ln n + \text{constant}\).
- The gluon condensate contribution in four dimensions is parameterized as \(\beta(\alpha_s(\mu)) \langle G^2(\mu) \rangle = -(352\ \text{MeV})^4\).
- Fits to experimental scalar meson masses yield \(B_S = 0.456\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(A_S = 1.262\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(C_S = -0.746\ \text{GeV}^2\); for pseudoscalar mesons: \(B_P = 1.040\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(A_P = 1.589\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(C_P = -0.926\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Mondejar and Pineda (2007) from vector and axial-vector correlators to scalar and pseudoscalar correlators, incorporating nontrivial anomalous dimensions. It provides the first analytical computation of \(1/n\) corrections to decay constants in the 't Hooft model, which are model-independent because the mass spectrum is known from 't Hooft (1974). The results enable future studies of duality violations in deep inelastic scattering and B decays in two dimensions, and they confirm that the slope of Regge trajectories remains unconstrained by the OPE, consistent with earlier findings by Cata et al. (2006) and Golterman et al. (2002).
