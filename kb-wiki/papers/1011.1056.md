# Microscopic Formulation of Interacting Boson Model for Rotational Nuclei

> We propose a novel formulation of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) for rotational nuclei with axially-symmetric strong deformation. The intrinsic structure represented by the potential energy surface (PES) of a given multi-nucleon system has a certain similarity to that of the corresponding multi-boson system. Based on this feature, one can derive an appropriate boson Hamiltonian as already reported. This prescription, however, has a major difficulty in rotational spectra of strongly deformed nuclei: the bosonic moment of inertia is significantly smaller than the corresponding nucleonic one. We present that this difficulty originates in the difference between the rotational response of a nucleon system and that of the corresponding boson system, and could arise even if the PESs of the two systems were identical. We further suggest that the problem can be cured by implementing $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ term into the IBM Hamiltonian, with coupling constant derived from the cranking approach of Skyrme mean-field models. The validity of the method is confirmed for rare-earth and actinoid nuclei, as their experimental rotational yrast bands are reproduced nicely.

# Microscopic Formulation of Interacting Boson Model for Rotational Nuclei

## Key claim

The longstanding problem that the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) yields a moment of inertia significantly smaller than the corresponding nucleonic one for strongly deformed nuclei can be cured by adding an $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ term to the IBM Hamiltonian, with its coupling constant derived from the cranking approach of Skyrme mean-field models. This prescription works specifically for nuclei with axially-symmetric strong deformation where the ratio $R_{4/2} > 3.2$, and the correction arises from the different rotational responses of fermion and boson systems even when their potential energy surfaces at rest are identical.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) calculations using the Skyrme SkM* interaction to obtain nucleonic intrinsic states and potential energy surfaces (PES) for rare-earth and actinoid nuclei. They constructed corresponding IBM-2 boson Hamiltonians by mapping the bosonic PES onto the nucleonic PES following the method of Nomura et al. (2008). To determine the $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ coupling constant $\alpha$, they computed the fermionic moment of inertia using the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula and the bosonic moment of inertia using a boson cranking formula, then set $\alpha$ so that the bosonic moment of inertia matched the fermionic one. The resulting IBM Hamiltonians were diagonalized using the NPBOS code to obtain yrast spectra.

## Key numbers

- The criterion for applying the $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ term is $R_{4/2} > 3.2$, where $R_{4/2} = E_x(4^+_1)/E_x(2^+_1)$.
- For $^{152-158}$Sm with $R_{4/2} > 3.2$, the experimental moment of inertia is $35 \sim 40$ MeV$^{-1}$ for $N \geq 92$.
- The $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ term lowers $E_x(2^+_1)$ by $>30\%$ for $^{92-96}$Sm and $>60\%$ for $^{140-144}$U.
- For $^{232-236}$U, the calculated moment of inertia with the LL term is about twice as large as that of $^{152-158}$Sm.
- The IBM parameters for U isotopes are $\epsilon \approx 0.100$ MeV, $\kappa \approx -0.18$ MeV, and $\chi_\pi \approx \chi_\nu \approx -1.0$.
- The $\hat{L} \cdot \hat{L}$ term contributes to the PES as a change of $d$-boson energy $\Delta \epsilon = 6\alpha$.

## Context

This work extends the PES-mapping method of Nomura et al. (2008) for deriving IBM Hamiltonians from Skyrme energy density functionals, which was previously validated for weakly to moderately deformed nuclei but failed for strongly deformed rotational nuclei. The authors address the criticism by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) regarding the sufficiency of $SD$-pair truncation for strongly deformed nuclei, and build upon earlier attempts to renormalize $g$-boson effects into the $sd$-boson sector by Mizusaki and Otsuka (1996) and others. The approach enables microscopic IBM calculations for rotational rare-earth and actinoid nuclei without phenomenological adjustment, and suggests that the IBM can now be placed on a microscopic basis for all deformation regimes at lowest order.
