# The Search for Gluon Saturation in pA Collisions

> In this talk, I reviewed the recent theoretical progress towards the exploration of the gluon saturation phenomenon in $pA$ collisions at both RHIC and the LHC. Two important pillars of this exploration are the single inclusive forward hadron productions and forward dihadron correlations, which have both been computed up to one-loop order within the small-$x$ factorization formalism. Furthermore, detailed numerical study for the single inclusive hadron production at next-to-leading order provides us good description of the low transverse momentum data measured in $dAu$ collisions at RHIC with significantly reduced uncertainty. In addition, the one-loop calculation for dihadron productions in $pA$ collisions shows that there is a new type of large logarithm, which is known as the Sudakov factor, arising in the back-to-back correlation limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that gluon saturation is an inevitable consequence of QCD dynamics in high-energy scatterings, but emphasizes that the remaining important tasks are to improve small-x physics calculations with more precise predictions and to look for experimental signatures of saturation at RHIC and the LHC, rather than claiming definitive discovery.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical progress on two key observables in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions: single inclusive forward hadron production and forward dihadron correlations. Both observables have been computed up to one-loop order within the small-x factorization formalism, using the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation and the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) resummation framework. For single inclusive hadrons, the SOLO numerical program incorporates next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections except for NLO BK evolution. For dihadron production, a one-loop calculation demonstrates the emergence of Sudakov double logarithms in the back-to-back correlation limit.

## Key numbers

* For single inclusive hadron production in dAu collisions at RHIC, the SOLO program gives decent agreement with experimental data when the transverse momentum p_perp is less than the saturation scale Q_s, with significantly reduced renormalization scale dependence.
* The Sudakov double logarithm factor for dijet production is given by S_sud = (alpha_s/pi) * C * integral from c_0^2/R_perp^2 to P_perp^2 of (d mu^2 / mu^2) * ln(P_perp^2 / mu^2), where c_0 = 2e^{-gamma_E} and R_perp = x_perp - y_perp.
* The color factor C for the Sudakov double logarithm is C = (1/2)C_a + (1/2)C_b, where C_a = C_F for incoming quarks and C_a = C_A for incoming gluons.

## Context

This work extends the saturation physics framework developed by Gribov (1984), Mueller (1985), and McLerran (1993), and builds on the BK evolution equation derived by Balitsky (1995) and Kovchegov (1999). The numerical SOLO program (Stasto, 2013) represents the first step toward precision tests of saturation beyond leading logarithmic approximation. The suppression of back-to-back dihadron correlations in dAu collisions at RHIC, first predicted by Marquet (2007) and with experimental evidence from Braidot (2010), Adare (2011), and Li (2012), provides a possible smoking gun measurement for gluon saturation. The one-loop calculation for dihadron production enables future unified descriptions of small-x evolution and CSS resummation for unintegrated gluon distributions.
