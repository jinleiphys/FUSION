# Chiral approach to antikaon s- and p-wave interactions in dense nuclear matter

> The properties of the antikaons in nuclear matter are investigated from a chiral unitary approach which incorporates the s- and p-waves of the ${\bar K}N$ interaction. To obtain the in-medium meson-baryon amplitudes we include, in a self-consistent way, Pauli blocking effects, meson self-energies corrected by nuclear short-range correlations and baryon binding potentials. We pay special attention to investigating the validity of the on-shell factorization, showing that it cannot be applied in the evaluation of the in-medium corrections to the p-wave amplitudes. In nuclear matter at saturation energy, the $Λ$ and $Σ$ develop an attractive potential of about -30 MeV, while the $Σ^*$ pole remains at the free space value although its width gets sensibly increased to about 80 MeV. The antikaon also develops a moderate attraction that does not support the existence of very deep and narrow bound states, confirming the findings of previous self-consistent calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the on-shell factorization of the kernel in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which is valid for s-wave in-medium amplitudes, cannot be applied to p-wave amplitudes in nuclear matter because it leads to a violation of causality by producing negative decay widths in some cases.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral unitary approach incorporating both s- and p-wave components of the antikaon-nucleon interaction, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with medium-modified loop functions. Medium effects were included self-consistently through Pauli blocking, meson self-energies corrected by nuclear short-range correlations, and baryon binding potentials, with the p-wave loop functions calculated using the full off-shell momentum dependence rather than the on-shell factorization.

## Key numbers

- The Λ and Σ hyperons develop an attractive potential of about -30 MeV in nuclear matter at saturation density.
- The Σ* pole remains at its free space value, but its width increases to about 80 MeV.
- The cut-off parameter used for regularizing the loop function is q_max = 630 MeV.
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The bare masses used for the hyperon poles are: Λ̃ = 1030 MeV, Σ̃ = 1120 MeV, Σ̃* = 1371 MeV.
- The nucleon binding potential is U = U₀ ρ/ρ₀ with U₀ = -70 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent chiral unitary calculations of Ramos (1999) by incorporating p-wave contributions to the antikaon-nucleus potential, following the p-wave studies of Jido (2002) within the same chiral framework. The results confirm the findings of previous self-consistent calculations that the antikaon develops only a moderate attraction in nuclear matter, contradicting the much stronger potentials proposed by Akaishi (2002) that predicted deeply bound kaon states, and instead supporting the interpretation of experimental peaks as arising from kaon absorption mechanisms rather than deeply bound states.
