# Analytical Evaluation Of An Infinite Integral Over Four Spherical Bessel Functions

> An infinite integral over four spherical Bessel functions is analytically evaluated for the special case when the arguments k_3=k_1 and k_4=k_2

## Key claim

The paper analytically evaluates an infinite integral over four spherical Bessel functions for the special case where the arguments satisfy \(k_3 = k_1\) and \(k_4 = k_2\), with the result expressed as a finite sum over an associated Legendre function of integer degree and half-integer order with a real argument greater than 1; the general case with arbitrary momenta would require a more complicated hypergeometric function.

## Method

The integral \(\int_0^\infty r^2 j_{\lambda_1}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_2}(k_2 r) j_{\lambda_3}(k_1 r) j_{\lambda_4}(k_2 r) dr\) is evaluated by first writing the product of four spherical Bessel functions as a double integral over two pairs using the closure relation for spherical Bessel functions. Each pair integral is expressed in terms of 3j and 6j symbols and Legendre polynomials, following the formalism of Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991). The resulting integral over the momentum \(K\) is transformed to an integral over \(\Delta = (k_1^2 + k_2^2 - K^2)/(2k_1 k_2)\), which is then evaluated analytically using a series expansion from Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (1994) involving associated Legendre functions.

## Key numbers

* For the special case \(\lambda_2 = \lambda_1 \equiv l_1\) and \(\lambda_4 = \lambda_3 \equiv l_2\) with \(L=0\), the integral reduces to \(\frac{\pi}{4} \sum_\mu \begin{pmatrix} l_1 & l_2 & \mu \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}^2 \frac{k_<^{\mu-1}}{k_>^{\mu+2}}\), where \(k_<\) and \(k_>\) are the smaller and larger of \(k_1\) and \(k_2\).
* The general result for the special case \(k_3=k_1, k_4=k_2\) involves the factor \(\frac{\sqrt{k_1 k_2}}{|k_1^2 - k_2^2|^L}\) multiplied by a sum over \(\mu\) of terms containing \(\Gamma(L+\mu+1/2)\) and the associated Legendre function \(P_L^{-\mu-1/2}\left(\frac{k_1^2+k_2^2}{|k_1^2-k_2^2|}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of integrals over spherical Bessel functions previously developed by Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991) and Mehrem (1992, 2011), which handled integrals over two or three spherical Bessel functions. The result enables closed-form evaluation of certain four-point integrals arising in nuclear physics reaction theory, particle physics (Chen and Su, 2004), and astrophysics (Hohenegger, 2009), where previous attempts required either numerical integration (Van Deun and Cools, 2006) or involved complicated hypergeometric functions (Crucean, 2009).
