# Spin Alignments in Heavy-ion Resonances

> Recent particle-particle-$γ$ coincident measurements on a $^{28}\rm Si+{}^{28}Si$ resonance have suggested ''vanishing spin alignments''. New analyses for spin alignments by using a molecular model are reported. Different aspects between di-nuclear systems with prolate deformed nuclei and those with oblate deformed nuclei are clarified.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "vanishing spin alignments" (misalignments) observed in the $^{28}\rm Si+{}^{28}Si$ resonance at $E_{\rm CM}=55.8$ MeV arise from a tilting mode (K-mixing) that is specific to oblate-oblate di-nuclear systems, in contrast to the spin alignments seen in prolate-prolate systems like $^{24}\rm Mg+{}^{24}Mg$.

## Method

The authors use a molecular model with seven degrees of freedom for the di-nuclear system, assuming constant axial deformation of the constituent nuclei. They solve for normal modes around the equilibrium configuration and introduce a rotation-vibration wave function with K-quantum number mixing, which is diagonalized in the high-spin limit to yield a Gaussian distribution of K values. The resulting wave function is used to calculate scattering amplitudes via the R-matrix formula and $\gamma$-ray angular distributions $W(\theta_\gamma)$ from polarized fragments, which are compared to experimental particle-particle-$\gamma$ coincidence data.

## Key numbers

- The resonance studied is at $E_{\rm CM}=55.8$ MeV for the $^{28}\rm Si+{}^{28}Si$ system.
- The angular distributions for elastic and inelastic channels are consistent with $L=38$ dominance, implying $L=J=38$.
- The width parameter for the K-mixing Gaussian is $b=1.3$, which is consistent with the di-nuclear configuration of $^{28}\rm Si+{}^{28}Si$.
- The total intrinsic spin of the system is in the range $30 \sim 40\hbar$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous molecular model studies of $^{24}\rm Mg+{}^{24}Mg$ and $^{28}\rm Si+{}^{28}Si$ resonances (Uegaki 1989, 1994) to explain recent experimental data from Nouicer and Beck at IReS Strasbourg. The results contrast with the spin alignments observed in $^{12}\rm C+{}^{12}C$ and $^{24}\rm Mg+{}^{24}Mg$ systems (CL88), and the proposed tilting mode represents a new facet in nuclear resonance phenomena that calls for further experimental investigation.
