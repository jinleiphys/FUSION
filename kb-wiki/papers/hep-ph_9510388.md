# Strange Twists in Neutral Pion Photo/Electro-Production

> I review the interesting tale of the electric dipole amplitude in neutral pion photoproduction and the resulting consequences. I also discuss why there is new life related to P--wave multipoles. Electroproduction is briefly touched upon.

## Key claim

The original low-energy theorem (LET) for the electric dipole amplitude in neutral pion photoproduction is modified at order mu^2 by chiral perturbation theory (CHPT), and the new term is so large that it cancels the leading term, making the original LET unusable for testing chiral dynamics; the new experimental value from Mainz (Fuchs et al., 1995) confirms the CHPT prediction and rules out the old low-energy guess by many standard deviations.

## Method

The author reviews the history of the electric dipole amplitude E0+ in threshold neutral pion photoproduction off protons, comparing the original low-energy theorem of de Baenst (1970) and Vainsthein and Zakharov (1970) with subsequent CHPT calculations at order p^3 (Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, Meissner, 1991) and p^4 (Bernard, Kaiser, Meissner, 1995). The p^4 calculation includes one-loop graphs with insertions from the dimension-two effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian and counterterms of the form e a1 omega M_pi + e a2 M_pi^3, with the low-energy constants a1 and a2 fitted to data or estimated by resonance exchange (Delta, rho^0, omega). For P-waves, novel LETs are derived for the slopes of the combinations P1 and P2 at threshold.

## Key numbers

- The original LET prediction for E0+ at threshold was -2.3 x 10^{-3} / M_pi^+ (in natural units).
- The reexamined Saclay data gave E0+ = -1.5 +/- 0.3 (with a guessed error after correcting for rescattering subtraction).
- The reexamined Mainz data gave E0+ = -2.0 +/- 0.2.
- The new Mainz data (Fuchs et al., 1995) give E0+^{exp}(s_thr) = -1.33 +/- 0.08.
- The CHPT prediction based on resonance exchange gives E0+(s_thr) approximately -1.2.
- For P-wave P1 at threshold: (1/|q|) P1^{pi^0 p}_{thr} = 0.480 GeV^{-2} from the LET, and 0.47 +/- 0.01 GeV^{-2} from the new Mainz analysis.
- For P-wave P2 at threshold: (1/|q|) P2^{pi^0 p}_{thr} = -0.512 GeV^{-2} from the LET.
- The low-energy constants from the free fit gave a1 approximately -a2 approximately 50 GeV^{-4} with a1 + a2 = 6.7 GeV^{-4}; resonance exchange gave a1 approximately a2 approximately 3 GeV^{-4} with almost the same sum.

## Context

This paper reviews the resolution of a long-standing puzzle in neutral pion photoproduction, where early experiments (Saclay, Mazzucato et al., 1986; Mainz, Beck et al., 1990) claimed large deviations from the original low-energy theorem, but reanalysis brought them into agreement. The CHPT calculation by Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) showed the original LET was incomplete, and the higher-order calculation by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) combined with new Mainz data (Fuchs et al., 1995) and SAL measurements (Bergstrom, 1995) confirmed the CHPT prediction. The work enables quantitative tests of chiral dynamics through P-wave multipoles rather than S-waves, and opens the field of neutral pion electroproduction where first measurements at NIKHEF (Welch et al., 1992; van den Brink et al., 1995) are being compared with relativistic CHPT and p^4 calculations.
