# Interaction effects on the conductance peak height statistics in quantum dots

> A random interaction matrix model is used to study the statistics of conductance peak heights in Coulomb blockade quantum dots. When the single-particle dynamics conserves time-reversal symmetry, the peak height statistics is insensitive to the interaction strength. But when the single-particle dynamics breaks time-reversal symmetry, the peak height statistics exhibits a crossover from unitary to orthogonal symmetry as the interaction strength increases. This crossover is driven by the time-reversal symmetry of the interaction. Our random interaction matrix model describes features of both the measured peak height and peak spacing statistics.

# Interaction effects on the conductance peak height statistics in quantum dots

## Key claim
When the single-particle dynamics conserves time-reversal symmetry, the conductance peak height statistics is insensitive to the interaction strength; but when time-reversal symmetry is broken, the statistics exhibits a crossover from unitary (GUE) to orthogonal (GOE) symmetry as the interaction strength increases, driven by the time-reversal symmetry of the two-body interaction. This crossover is partial: a time-reversal symmetry-breaking one-body field does not fully convert the statistics to GUE because the GOE-symmetric interaction competes with it.

## Method
The authors use a random interaction matrix model (RIMM) combining a random one-body Hamiltonian (GOE or GUE) with a random two-body interaction that preserves time-reversal symmetry (GOE in two-particle space). For each realization, they compute the ground states for n-1 and n electrons and evaluate the partial width for decay into a lead via the squared overlap between these ground states. The width and conductance peak height distributions are compared to analytic crossover distributions from random matrix theory parameterized by a crossover parameter λ.

## Key numbers
- For GOE one-body statistics, the width distribution is independent of the interaction strength U/Δ for values tested: U/Δ = 0, 2.4, 4, and 8, all matching the GOE Porter-Thomas distribution.
- For GUE one-body statistics, the crossover parameter λ extracted from fits to the width distribution takes values λ = 0.28, 0.17, and 0.08 for U/Δ = 2.4, 4, and 8 respectively (with m=12, n=4).
- In the Anderson model with Coulomb interactions and magnetic flux Φ = 0.14 Φ₀, the width distribution at U_c = 12 is described by the crossover distribution with λ = 0.17.
- The average width as a function of interaction strength follows the form Γ̄(U) - Γ̄(∞) = (Γ̄(0)-Γ̄(∞))/(1 + b U²/Δ²), where b is independent of m and depends only weakly on n.
- For U_eff ≲ 1, the statistics is essentially GUE; for U_eff ∼ 1–1.5, peak height statistics remains close to GUE while peak spacings already follow a Gaussian distribution.

## Context
This work extends the random interaction matrix model previously introduced by Alhassid, Jacquod, and Wobst (1999) for peak spacing statistics to the study of conductance peak height statistics. It addresses the puzzle that measured peak height distributions (Chang et al., 1996; Folk et al., 1996) agree with RMT predictions despite strong electron-electron interactions, while peak spacing distributions (Sivan et al., 1996; Simmel et al., 1997; Patel et al., 1998) show Gaussian-like shapes rather than Wigner-Dyson. The model explains both observations within a single framework and enables quantitative understanding of how interactions modify the symmetry classification of chaotic quantum dots. The results are compared with numerical simulations of an Anderson model with Coulomb interactions (Berkovits and Sivan, 1998).
