# Gravitational form factors of pion from top-down holographic QCD

> The gravitational form factors (GFFs) of pions are calculated from a top-down holographic quantum chromodynamics (QCD) approach with momentum transfer dependence for the first time. It is important because the GFFs of hadrons have information on the internal stress distribution that may provide insight into the mechanism of how QCD forms hadrons. The forward limit value of this GFFs, i.e. the D-term, was also obtained. Furthermore, in this approach, we observe the so-called glueball dominance, in which pions have gravitational interactions via infinite glueball spectra.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the momentum-transfer dependence of pion gravitational form factors (GFFs) from a top-down holographic QCD approach for the first time, but the results are valid only in the chiral limit (massless pions) and in the large \(N_c\) limit, with the expansion truncated at \(k=10\) in the momentum-transfer series.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic QCD model based on D4-D8 brane construction in type IIA supergravity. They solve the linearized Einstein equations in the 7-dimensional bulk spacetime with the pion field as the only matter source, using Green's function methods expanded in complete sets of glueball eigenfunctions (T4 and S4 modes). The boundary energy-momentum tensor expectation values are extracted via holographic renormalization, and the GFFs \(A(t)\) and \(D(t)\) are obtained from the asymptotic behavior of metric fluctuations at the boundary.

## Key numbers

* The D-term in the forward limit is \(D(0) = -1\), consistent with the low-energy theorem for Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the chiral limit.
* The model parameters are fixed as \(\kappa = 0.00745\) and \(M_{KK} = 949\ \mathrm{MeV}\), determined by the \(\rho\) meson mass \(776\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The expansion of the GFFs is truncated at \(k=10\) in the momentum-transfer series; \(A(t)\) converges well in the range \(0 < t < 0.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), while \(D(t)\) begins to deviate from around \(t \sim 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nucleon GFFs using the same top-down holographic approach by Fujita et al. (2022) to the pion case, and contrasts with bottom-up holographic QCD calculations by Abidin and Carlson (2008), which lack the S4 glueball mode contribution to \(D(t)\). The results are qualitatively compatible with lattice QCD data from Hackett et al. (2023), and the observed glueball dominance (pions interacting gravitationally via infinite glueball spectra) mirrors findings in the nucleon case. This work enables future studies of GFFs for other hadrons, beyond-chiral-limit analyses, and investigations of stress distributions under finite temperature, density, or magnetic fields.
