# Direct ab initio calculation of the $^{4}$He nuclear electric dipole polarizability

> The calculation of nuclear electromagnetic sum rules by directly diagonalizing the nuclear Hamiltonian in a large basis is numerically challenging and has not been performed for $A>2$ nuclei. With the significant progress of high performance computing, we show that calculating sum rules using numerous discretized continuum states obtained by directly diagonalizing the ab initio no-core shell model Hamiltonian is achievable numerically. Specifically, we calculate the $^{4}$He electric dipole ($E1$) polarizability, that is an inverse energy weighted sum rule, employing the Daejeon16 $NN$ interaction. We demonstrate that the calculations are numerically tractable as the dimension of the basis increases and are convergent. Our results for the $^{4}$He electric dipole polarizability are consistent with the most recent experimental data and are compared with those of other theoretical studies employing different techniques and various interactions.

# Direct *ab initio* calculation of the ⁴He nuclear electric dipole polarizability

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that calculating electromagnetic sum rules by directly diagonalizing the *ab initio* no-core shell model Hamiltonian and solving for numerous discretized continuum states is numerically achievable for A>2 nuclei, specifically for the ⁴He electric dipole polarizability using the Daejeon16 NN interaction. The results are consistent with the most recent experimental data, but the authors note that the calculation is restricted to excitation energies up to 100 MeV and that the uncertainty from this truncation is estimated via exponential extrapolation.

## Method

The authors use the *ab initio* no-core shell model (NCSM) in a harmonic oscillator basis to diagonalize the Hamiltonian with the Daejeon16 NN interaction plus Coulomb interaction between protons. They obtain the ⁴He ground state (0⁺) from even Nmax calculations and the 1⁻ excited states from odd Nmax calculations, using a Lagrange multiplier term λ(J² − 2) to suppress unwanted states with higher angular momentum. The E1 polarizability is computed as an inverse energy weighted sum rule over all 1⁻ states up to 100 MeV excitation energy, with convergence tested by increasing the basis truncation parameter Nmax.

## Key numbers

- The predicted ⁴He E1 polarizability is α_E = 0.0782^{+0.0006}_{-0.0003} fm³, obtained at Nmax = 15 with ℏΩ = 17.5 MeV.
- The uncertainty from excitation energy truncation (extrapolated contributions above 100 MeV) is estimated as 0.0003 fm³.
- The uncertainty from basis truncation (difference between ℏΩ = 17.5 and 25 MeV at Nmax = 15) is estimated as 0.0003 fm³.
- At Nmax = 11 with ℏΩ = 20 MeV, 2346 states (including 1⁻, 2⁻, etc.) are obtained below 100 MeV excitation energy, of which only 370 are 1⁻ states.
- The bin of 80–100 MeV excitation energy contributes less than 10⁻³ fm³ to the polarizability.

## Context

This work extends the direct diagonalization approach for sum rules, previously verified only for the deuteron (Bacca 2014, Friar 1997, Yin 2022), to A=4 nuclei for the first time. It contrasts with alternative techniques such as the Lorentz integral transform (Efros 1994, Gazit 2006, Bacca 2014) and the Lanczos sum rule method (Haxton 2005, Stetcu 2008, Baker 2020), which avoid solving for numerous eigenstates. The results show significant variations in ⁴He E1 polarizability across different *ab initio* approaches and interactions (ranging from 0.0655 to 0.084 fm³), indicating that this observable can provide constraints on nuclear interactions. The method can be extended to heavier nuclei such as ⁶He and to other sum rules, and the results can benchmark evaluations using other techniques.
