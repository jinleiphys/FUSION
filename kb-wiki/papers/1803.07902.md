# Analyses of multi-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations for the pion-emitting sources with Bose-Einstein condensation

> We calculate the three- and four-particle correlations of identical pions in an evolving pion gas (EPG) model with Bose-Einstein condensation. The multi-pion correlation functions in the EPG model are analyzed in different momentum intervals and compared with the experimental data for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. It is found that the multi-pion correlation functions and cumulant correlation functions are sensitive to the condensation fraction of the EPG sources in the low average transverse-momentum intervals of the three and four pions. The model results of the multi-pion correlations are consistent with the experimental data in a considerable degree, which gives a source condensation fraction between 16~--~47\%.

## Key claim

The multi-pion correlation functions and cumulant correlation functions in the evolving pion gas (EPG) model are sensitive to the condensation fraction of the source in low average transverse-momentum intervals of three and four pions, and the model results are consistent with experimental data for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, yielding a source condensation fraction between 16% and 47%.

## Method

The authors calculated three- and four-pion correlation functions using an evolving pion gas (EPG) model with Bose-Einstein condensation, where the pion gas is treated as a relativistic boson gas within a time-dependent harmonic oscillator mean-field and expands via relativistic hydrodynamics. The multi-pion correlation functions were analyzed in different transverse-momentum intervals (\(K_{T3}\) and \(K_{T4}\)) and compared with experimental data from the ALICE collaboration for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. The condensation fraction \(f_0 = N_0/N\) was determined from the fugacity parameter \(\mathcal{Z}\) and the particle number \(N\) using the harmonic oscillator energy levels.

## Key numbers

* The source condensation fraction extracted from the model is between 16% and 47%, consistent with the analysis of four-pion correlations measured in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.
* The model parameters include initial source radius \(R_0 = 6\) fm, initial temperature \(T_0 = 170\) MeV, \(\gamma = 1.627\), and expansion parameter \(\alpha = 0.62\).
* The characteristic length parameter \(C_1\) is taken as 0.35 and 0.40 in the model calculations.
* Three-pion correlation functions were calculated for sources with particle numbers \(N = 400, 800, 1200\) (for \(C_1 = 0.35\)) and \(N = 800, 1200, 1600\) (for \(C_1 = 0.40\)), at temperatures \(T = 80, 100, 120\) MeV.
* The transverse-momentum intervals used are \(K_{T3} < 0.16\) GeV/\(c\), \(0.16 < K_{T3} < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\), and \(K_{T3} > 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) for three-pion correlations, and \(K_{T4} < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) and \(K_{T4} > 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) for four-pion correlations.
* For the source with \(N = 400\) and \(C_1 = 0.35\), the intercept of the three-pion correlation function approaches the maximum value of 6 when extrapolated to \(Q_3 = 0\) for temperatures above 80 MeV, indicating nearly completely chaotic emission.

## Context

This work extends the earlier EPG model studies of two-pion HBT correlations by Liu, Ru, Zhang, and Wong (2014), which investigated the chaoticity parameter \(\lambda\) in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. It also builds on the theoretical framework of Bose-Einstein condensation in a harmonic oscillator potential developed by Wong and Zhang (2007). The results are compared with experimental three- and four-pion correlation data from the ALICE collaboration (2016), and the extracted condensation fraction is consistent with the ALICE analysis. This work enables further studies of partial coherence in heavy-ion collisions and the role of Bose-Einstein condensation in multi-pion femtoscopy.
