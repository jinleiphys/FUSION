# Probing the dissipative properties of a strongly interacting medium with dileptons

> We investigate the effects of the presence of a non-vanishing net baryon number density and its diffusion on dilepton production, within a hydrodynamical description of the medium created at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV collision energy. This energy value is explored within the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory. Particular attention is devoted to a new dissipative degree of freedom: the net baryon number diffusion ($V^μ$), and to the net baryon number conductivity ($κ$) | a transport coefficients governing the overall magnitude of $V^μ$. The effects of $κ$ on dilepton production are assessed, with an outlook on how future experimental dilepton data can be used to learn more about $κ$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that thermal dilepton elliptic flow ($v_2$) is sensitive to both net baryon chemical potential and baryon diffusion, while the invariant mass dilepton yield is not significantly affected by baryon diffusion under the current assumption of the baryon diffusion constant $\kappa$. This sensitivity could be used to constrain $\kappa$ from future experimental dilepton data.

## Method
The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV using a 2+1D dissipative fluid dynamics framework that includes net baryon number diffusion as a new dissipative degree of freedom. They initialized energy density and net baryon density profiles using MC-Glauber event averaging, with longitudinal profiles tuned to reproduce net proton rapidity distributions and charged hadron pseudorapidity spectra. The thermal dilepton production rates were computed including corrections from baryon diffusion, and the resulting invariant mass yields and elliptic flow were compared for cases with and without net baryon density and diffusion.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV, corresponding to the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC
- Initial thermalization time: $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c
- Freeze-out energy density: 0.1 GeV/fm$^3$
- Shear viscosity coefficient: $\frac{\eta T}{(\varepsilon+P)}=0.08$
- Baryon diffusion transport coefficient: $\kappa=0.2\frac{n_B}{\mu_B}$
- Relaxation time for baryon diffusion: $\tau_V=\frac{0.2}{T}$
- Centrality class: 0-80%

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of dilepton production (Vujanovic 2013) by incorporating finite net baryon chemical potential and baryon diffusion, which become important at lower collision energies explored by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The transport coefficients for baryon diffusion are taken from AdS/CFT calculations (Natsuume 2007) and the 14-moment approximation of the Boltzmann equation (Denicol 2012). The results suggest that future experimental dilepton elliptic flow data could provide constraints on the net baryon number conductivity $\kappa$, a transport coefficient that governs the magnitude of baryon diffusion in strongly interacting matter.
