# Classical and Quantal Descriptions of Small Amplitude Fluctuations Around Equilibriums in the Two-Level Pairing Model

> Various classical counterparts for the two-level pairing model in a many-fermion system are presented in the Schwinger boson representation. It is shown that one of the key ingredients giving the classical descriptions for quantal system is the use of the various trial states besides the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state, which may be natural selection for the two-level pairing model governed by the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-algebra. It is pointed out that the fictitious behavior like the sharp phase transition can be avoided by using the other states such as the $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$- and the $su(1,1)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent states, while the sharp phase transition appears in the usual Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and the quasi-particle random phase approximations in the original fermion system.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$- or $su(1,1)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent states in the Schwinger boson representation avoids the fictitious sharp phase transition that appears in the usual Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and quasi-particle random phase approximations, but only for certain interaction strength regimes; the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state (corresponding to the standard mean-field approach) gives better agreement with exact results at large coupling.

## Method

The authors treat the two-level pairing model, governed by an $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-algebra, using the Schwinger boson representation with four boson operators. They introduce three types of trial coherent states, $su(2)\otimes su(2)$, $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$, and $su(1,1)\otimes su(1,1)$, and compute the classical energy surface and small-amplitude oscillation frequency around the energy minimum for each. They then re-quantize the classical Hamiltonians to obtain the ground-state energy with quantum corrections and the excitation frequency, comparing these to the exact eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- For the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state (case iii) with $\Omega=5$ and $G\Omega < 1$: classical ground-state energy $E_c(c) = -2\Omega - G\Omega$, frequency $\omega(c) = 4\sqrt{1-G\Omega}$.
- For the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state with $\Omega=5$ and $G\Omega > 1$: classical ground-state energy $E_c(c) = G^{-1} - G\Omega(\Omega+1)$, frequency $\omega(c) = 2\sqrt{(G\Omega)^2 - 1}$.
- The fictitious phase transition in the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state occurs at $G\Omega = 1$, which for $\Omega=5$ corresponds to $G = 0.2$.
- The $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent state (case i) and $su(1,1)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent state (case ii) show no dip structure at $G=0.2$ in either the ground-state energy or the frequency, unlike the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of the two-level pairing model using the $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent state (Tsue et al., 2005, referred to as (I)) and the $su(1,1)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent state (Tsue et al., 2005, referred to as (I-1)). It contrasts the standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and quasi-particle random phase approximation approaches, which produce a sharp phase transition, with the alternative coherent-state descriptions that avoid this fictitious behavior. The results enable improved descriptions of small-amplitude fluctuations in pairing models, particularly in the weak-coupling regime where the $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$-coherent state reproduces the exact frequency well.
