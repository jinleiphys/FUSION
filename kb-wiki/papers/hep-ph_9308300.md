# Hydrodynamical Beam Jets in High Energy Hadronic Collisions

> A study of hadronic data up to TEVATRON energies in terms of relativistic hydrodynamics indicates an extended 1-dimensional stage of the expansion which suggests a jet like behaviour of the fireball along the collision axis.

## Key claim

A hydrodynamical analysis of hadronic data up to TEVATRON energies indicates that the Landau Hydrodynamical Model succeeds in describing hadronic reactions not because of local thermal equilibrium, but because its 1-dimensional solution effectively corrects for the absence of local equilibrium in these small, short-lived systems.

## Method

The authors compared 1-dimensional and 3-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic solutions for the rapidity distributions of antiproton-proton collisions at SPS (sqrt(s)=20 GeV) and ISR (sqrt(s)=53 GeV) energies, using the Khalatnikov analytical solution for the 1d case. They then extended the analysis to higher energies up to 1800 GeV by fitting pseudorapidity distributions while allowing the parameter a_{3d} (which controls the duration of the 1-dimensional expansion stage) to increase above the Landau geometrical limit a_L.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=20 GeV, the 1d solution used c_0^2=0.18 and inelasticity K=0.5; the 3d solution used K=0.35
- At sqrt(s)=53 GeV, the 1d solution used c_0^2=0.195 and K=0.5; the 3d solution used K=0.176
- The parameter a_{3d} was increased from sqrt(2)*a_L at sqrt(s)=53 GeV to sqrt(10)*a_L at 1800 GeV to fit the data
- The thermal contribution to the rapidity width sigma_therm is less than 1.65 for freeze-out temperature T_f below 0.2 GeV
- The upper limit for the rapidity distribution width sigma at LHC energies is less than 5

## Context

This work extends the Landau Hydrodynamical Model (LHM), originally developed by Landau (1956), to hadronic collisions at much higher energies than previously tested. It contradicts the conventional assumption that the 3-dimensional solution with local equilibrium is appropriate for hadronic reactions, and instead suggests that the 1-dimensional solution works better because local equilibrium may break down. The authors connect their findings to the two-component model proposed by Fowler (1986) and the UA5 multiplicity distribution analysis (1985), which distinguished chaotic (equilibrated) sources in the central region from coherent (non-equilibrated) sources from leading valence quarks. This work enables future studies of the pre-equilibrium to equilibrium transition in the fragmentation region at RHIC, LHC, and SSC.
