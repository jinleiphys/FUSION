# Fluctuation Phenomena in Chaotic Dirac Quantum Dots: Artificial Atoms on Graphene Flakes

> We develop the stub model for the Dirac Quantum Dot, an electron confining device on a grapheme surface. Analytical results for the average conductance and the correlation functions are obtained and found in agreement with those found previously using semiclassical calculation. Comparison with available data are presented. The results reported here demonstrate the applicability of Random Matrix Theory in the case of Dirac electrons confined in a stadium.

## Key claim

The authors develop a random matrix theory (RMT) based stub model for chaotic Dirac quantum dots that yields analytical expressions for average conductance and conductance correlation functions, applicable to all crossover regimes of magnetic flux, strain, and boundary confinement. The model recovers semiclassical results of Adagideli (2010) in the limit of high massive confinement, but the paper's central hedge is that the framework is valid only in the limit where the total number of open channels \(N_T \gg 1\) and the number of resonances \(M \gg N_T\), and where the stub's permanence time is much shorter than the escape time to the leads.

## Method

The authors generalize the stub model of quantum chaotic scattering to Dirac electrons by attaching a fictitious stub to the quantum dot and constructing the scattering matrix \(\mathcal{S}(\epsilon,\mathcal{B},m)\) from a random unitary matrix \(\mathcal{U}\) (drawn from the Circular Orthogonal Ensemble) and a reflection matrix \(\mathcal{R}\) that encodes energy, magnetic flux, and the effective Dirac Hamiltonian's symmetry-breaking parameters. They compute the average conductance and conductance covariance using diagrammatic expansions (diffuson and cooperon diagrams) in the limit \(M \gg N_T \gg 1\), then apply the formalism to a chaotic graphene quantum dot by parameterizing the effective Hamiltonian with terms for magnetic flux, strain, massive confinement, armchair edges, and zigzag edges.

## Key numbers

- Average conductance weak localization term: \(\langle G_{wl} \rangle / G_0 = -2/(1 + 2\mathcal{X}^2 + 2\mathcal{W}_{st}^2)\) for the case \(m = w_{zz} = 0\), where \(\mathcal{X}^2 = x^2/N_T\) and \(\mathcal{W}_{st}^2 = w_{st}^2/N_T\).
- In the limit \(m \to \infty\) (massive confinement), the weak localization term becomes \(\langle G_{wl} \rangle / G_0 = -1/(1 + 2\mathcal{X}^2 + 2\mathcal{W}_{st}^2) + 1/(1 + 2\mathcal{X}^2 + 2\mathcal{W}_{st}^2 + 2\mathcal{W}_{ac}^2)\), which reduces to Eq. (71) of Adagideli (2010) when \(\mathcal{W}_{st}=0\).
- Variance of conductance with all parameters zero: \(\mathbf{var}[G] = G_0^2 \times [4 \times 2] = 8 G_0^2\); with magnetic flux only (\(x \to \infty\)): \(\mathbf{var}[G] = G_0^2 \times [4 \times 1] = 4 G_0^2\).
- Variance in the limit \(w_{st} \to \infty\) (strong strain): \(\mathbf{var}[G]/G_0^2 = \sum_{i=0}^1 1/(1 + 2i\mathcal{W}_{ac}^2)^2\), giving \(\mathbf{var}[G] = 2 G_0^2\) for \(\mathcal{W}_{ac}=0\) and \(\mathbf{var}[G] = G_0^2\) for \(\mathcal{W}_{ac} \to \infty\).
- Correlation function takes the Lorentzian-squared form \(\mathcal{C}_F(\delta\epsilon,\delta\mathcal{X})/G_0^2 = C_\lambda / |1 + i\delta\epsilon + \delta\mathcal{X}^2|^2\), with \(C_0 = 8\) (all parameters zero), \(C_\mathcal{X} = 4\) (magnetic flux only), and \(C_\lambda = 4\) for \(\mathcal{M}, \mathcal{W}_{ac}, \mathcal{W}_{zz} \to \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of chaotic graphene quantum dots by Adagideli (2010) to a full RMT framework that handles arbitrary crossover regimes of magnetic flux, strain, and boundary conditions, and it generalizes the stub model previously applied to Schrodinger quantum dots by Brouwer (1995) and Halperin (1996). The results enable analytical calculation of conductance fluctuations and weak localization in Dirac quantum dots under combined symmetry-breaking fields, and the authors note that the suppression of the weak localization peak with increasing strain agrees with the experimental findings of Geim (2007).
