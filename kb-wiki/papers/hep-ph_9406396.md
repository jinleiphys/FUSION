# Treating Solar Model Uncertainties: A Consistent Statistical Analysis of Solar Neutrino Models and Data

> We describe how to consistently incorporate solar model uncertainties, along with experimental errors and correlations, when analyzing solar neutrino data to derive confidence limits on parameter space for proposed solutions of the solar neutrino problem. Our work resolves ambiguities and inconsistencies in the previous literature. As an application of our methods we calculate the masses and mixing angles allowed by the current data for the proposed MSW solution using both Bayesian and frequentist methods, allowing purely for solar model flux variations, to compare with previous work. We consider the effects of including metal diffusion in the solar models and also discuss implications for future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a consistent statistical framework for incorporating solar model flux uncertainties (parameterized via the flux covariance matrix) into analyses of solar neutrino data, resolving ambiguities in prior work. The authors caution that systematic solar model uncertainties (e.g., from new physics like metal diffusion) could shift the entire allowed parameter range, so their results are conditional on the assumption that only statistical flux variations are relevant.

## Method

The authors used the power-law approach, expressing neutrino fluxes as products of input parameters raised to logarithmic partial derivatives, to generate a flux covariance matrix via Monte Carlo. They then combined this theoretical covariance matrix with experimental errors (including correlations) to compute a chi-squared statistic for comparing predicted and observed rates in the Homestake, Kamiokande, and combined SAGE/GALLEX experiments. For MSW oscillations, they applied analytic survival probabilities (including adiabatic and non-adiabatic transitions) and modeled Earth regeneration effects using a five-shell Earth model, adding a day/night constraint from Kamiokande data.

## Key numbers

- The combined gallium rate used in the fit: SAGE at 74 ± 20 SNU and GALLEX at 79 ± 12 SNU, added in quadrature.
- The day/night asymmetry measurement from Kamiokande: ⟨(day−night)/(day+night)⟩ = −0.08 ± 0.11 (independent of solar model flux uncertainties).
- The 95% confidence level allowed region was defined by χ² < 9.49 (4 degrees of freedom), including both rate and day/night fits.
- Relative probability from the likelihood analysis: P(sin²2θ > 0.1) ≈ 0.3 × P(sin²2θ < 0.1), favoring the small mixing angle region over the large mixing angle region.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by White (1993) and Bahcall, Krastev, Lisi (1994) by properly accounting for both experimental correlations and neutrino flux correlations, which the Tc-parameterization approach (used by Bahcall, Krastev, Lisi) had overestimated. The paper also critiques the non-standard chi-squared definition used by Hata and Langacker (1994). The method enables future analyses to incorporate improved solar model covariance matrices directly, and the authors suggest that future solar model calculations should explicitly report the flux covariance matrix Vij.
