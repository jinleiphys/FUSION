# Directed flow in heavy-ion collisions at NICA: what is interesting to measure?

> We study the formation of the directed flow of hadrons in nuclear collisions at energies between AGS and SPS in Monte Carlo cascade model. The slope of the proton flow at midrapidity tends to zero (softening) with increasing impact parameter of the collision. For very peripheral topologies this slope becomes negative (antiflow). The effect is caused by rescattering of hadrons in remnants of the colliding nuclei. Since the softening of the proton flow can be misinterpreted as indication of the presence of quark-gluon plasma, we propose several measurements at NICA facility which can help one to distinguish between the cases with and without the plasma formation.

## Key claim

The softening (reduction to zero) and eventual sign reversal (antiflow) of the proton directed flow slope at midrapidity in peripheral collisions can be reproduced by a hadronic cascade model without any quark-gluon plasma formation, meaning this signal alone cannot be taken as evidence for a phase transition; the effect is instead caused by rescattering and absorption of hadrons in the baryon-rich spectator remnants.

## Method

The authors used the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), a Monte Carlo cascade model based on Gribov Reggeon field theory and string phenomenology, to simulate heavy-ion collisions at energies between AGS (11.6 AGeV) and SPS. They computed the directed flow coefficient \(v_1\) as a function of rapidity for nucleons and pions in both light (\({}^{32}\)S+\({}^{32}\)S) and heavy (\({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au) systems across multiple centrality bins defined by the reduced impact parameter \(\tilde{b} = b/b_{\text{max}}\).

## Key numbers

*   For light \({}^{32}\)S+\({}^{32}\)S collisions at 11.6 AGeV/\(c\), the nucleon directed flow slope at midrapidity becomes "not so steep" already at \(\tilde{b} = 0.3\) and develops antiflow at \(\tilde{b} \geq 0.7\).
*   For heavy \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at the same energy, the flatness of \(v_1^N\) at midrapidity with transition to antiflow builds up only at \(\tilde{b} \geq 0.7\).
*   In peripheral collisions at SPS energy, the directed flow of nucleons changes slope at midrapidity from antiflow to normal flow when selecting nucleons with transverse momenta \(p_T \geq 0.6\) GeV/\(c\).
*   The snapshot of S+S collisions at SPS energy with impact parameter \(b = 2.13\) fm (corresponding to \(\tilde{b} = 0.3\)) was taken at time \(t = 6\) fm/\(c\) for cells of volume \(V = 3\) fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier QGSM studies of directed flow from AGS to RHIC energies (Bravina 1995, Zabrodin 2004) and directly addresses the interpretation of the STAR Beam Energy Scan results (STAR Collaboration 2014), which observed a sign change in the proton flow slope between 7.7 and 11.5 GeV. The authors argue that the softening of directed flow, previously attributed to a softest point in the equation of state from a first-order QGP phase transition (Hung and Shuryak 1995, Csernai and Rodel 1999), can instead arise from purely hadronic rescattering. The paper proposes specific measurements at the NICA facility (comparing light vs. heavy systems, measuring over a wide centrality range up to 90%, extending to fragmentation regions, and studying high-\(p_T\) hadrons) to distinguish between hadronic and QGP interpretations.
