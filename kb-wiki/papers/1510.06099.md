# Hyperon-mixed neutron star with universal many-body repulsion

> Neutron stars with large masses $\sim 2M_{\odot}$ require the hard stiffness of equation of state (EoS) of neutron-star matter. On the other hand, hyperon mixing brings about remarkable softening of EoS. In order to solve this problem, a multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) is introduced as a model for the universal many-body repulsion in baryonic systems on the basis of the Extended Soft Core (ESC) baryon-baryon interaction. The strength of MPP is determined by analyzing the nucleus-nucleus scattering with the G-matrix folding model. The interactions in $Λ\!N$, $Σ\!N$ and $Ξ\!N$ channels are shown to be consistent with experimental indications. The EoS in neutron-star matter with hyperon mixing is obtained from ESC in addition of MPP, and mass-radius relations of neutron stars are derived. The maximum mass is shown to reach $2M_{\odot}$ even in the case of including hyperon mixing on the basis of model-parameters determined by terrestrial experiments.

# Hyperon-mixed neutron star with universal many-body repulsion

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a neutron star can reach 2 solar masses even when hyperon mixing is included, provided that a multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) provides universal many-body repulsion among all baryons, with the MPP strength parameters determined solely from terrestrial experiments (nucleus-nucleus scattering data) rather than fitted to neutron-star observations.

## Method

The authors construct a baryon-baryon interaction model combining the Extended Soft Core (ESC) two-body potentials with a multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) for universal three-body and four-body repulsion and a phenomenological three-baryon attraction (TBA). The MPP strength parameters are determined by fitting G-matrix folding model calculations to angular distributions of 16O+16O elastic scattering at E/A=70 MeV, while TBA parameters are adjusted to reproduce nuclear saturation properties. The equation of state for beta-stable neutron-star matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and hyperons (Λ, Σ⁻, Ξ⁻) is then derived using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Observed neutron star masses: J1614-2230 at (1.97±0.04) M⊙ and J0348-0432 at (2.01±0.04) M⊙
- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.154 fm⁻³ for all three parameter sets (MPa, MPa⁺, MPb)
- Incompressibility K = 283 MeV (MPa), 313 MeV (MPa⁺), and 254 MeV (MPb) at saturation
- Λ potential energy at normal density: UΛ(ρ₀) = −38.1 MeV (MPa), −36.9 MeV (MPa⁺), −38.3 MeV (MPb), −39.4 MeV (ESC only)
- Σ potential energy at normal density: UΣ(ρ₀) = 1.5 MeV (MPa), 3.4 MeV (MPa⁺), 1.3 MeV (MPb), 1.3 MeV (ESC only)
- MPP parameters: gP(3) = 2.34 and gP(4) = 30.0 (MPa); gP(3) = 1.31 and gP(4) = 80.0 (MPa⁺); gP(3) = 2.94 and gP(4) = 0.0 (MPb)
- Maximum neutron star mass reaches 2 M⊙ for all parameter sets including hyperon mixing

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars, where hyperon mixing softens the equation of state and prevents reaching observed 2 M⊙ masses. The authors extend their previous work (Yamamoto et al., 2013, 2014) by correcting overestimated stiffness from earlier numerical calculations and by including Ξ⁻ hyperons, which were not considered previously. The approach contrasts with the Urbana UIX three-body force model, which the authors find gives similar three-body repulsion strength to their MPb parameter set. The ESC model's S=−2 sector reproduces the recently measured Ξ⁻ binding energy from emulsion experiments (Nakazawa, 2015). This work enables future analyses of hypernuclear data using G-matrix folding potentials and provides a framework for determining high-density equation of state constraints from nucleus-nucleus scattering experiments.
