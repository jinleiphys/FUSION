# Final state interaction in semi-inclusive DIS off nuclei

> The final state interaction in DIS of leptons off a nucleus A, due to the propagation of the struck nucleon debris and its hadronization in the nuclear environment is considered. The effective cross section of such a partonic system with the nucleons of the medium and its time dependence are estimated on the basis of a model which takes into account the production of hadrons due to the breaking of the color string and to gluon radiation. It is shown that the details of the propagation of the partonic system in the nuclear environment can be thoroughly investigated by the semi-inclusive process A(e,e'(A-1))X, in which the scattered lepton is detected in coincidence with the nucleus (A-1) in low energy and momentum states. The rescattering of the struck nucleon debris with the medium damps and distorts the momentum distributions of (A-1) in a way which is very sensitive to the details of effective cross section of the debris. The total cross section of the process on ^4He, ^16O, and ^40Ca, related to the probability that after a target nucleon experiences a DIS process, the recoiling (A-1) nucleus remains intact, is evaluated, and the distorted momentum distribution of the recoiling (A-1) system is obtained. It is shown that both quantities are very sensitive to the details of the early stage of hadronization of the nucleon debris in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the semi-inclusive process \(A(e,e'(A-1))X\), in which the scattered lepton is detected in coincidence with a low-energy, low-momentum recoil nucleus \((A-1)\), is highly sensitive to the details of the early-stage hadronization of the struck nucleon debris in the nuclear medium, and that the final state interaction (FSI) distorts the momentum distribution of the recoil system in a way that depends on the time-dependent effective cross section of the partonic system.

## Method

The authors model the propagation and hadronization of the struck nucleon debris using a color string model with string tension \(\kappa \approx 1\ \text{GeV/fm}\), including spontaneous \(\bar qq\) pair creation via the Schwinger mechanism (rate \(w \approx 2\ \text{fm}^{-2}\)), and supplement it with perturbative QCD gluon bremsstrahlung. The effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}(t)\) of the partonic system with nucleons is computed as a function of time, combining contributions from the string and from radiated gluons. This FSI is incorporated into the Glauber formalism to compute the distorted spectral function and distorted momentum distribution for the recoil nucleus \((A-1)\) in the process \(A(e,e'(A-1))X\) on \(^4\text{He}\), \(^{16}\text{O}\), and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\).

## Key numbers

* String tension \(\kappa = 1/(2\pi \alpha_R^\prime) \approx 1\ \text{GeV/fm}\), with Regge slope \(\alpha_R^\prime \approx 0.9\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
* Probability rate for \(\bar qq\) pair creation: \(w \approx 2\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), estimated from the Schwinger formula, heavy resonance decay widths, and the mean Feynman \(x_F \approx 0.5\) of leading protons in \(pp \to pX\).
* Mean time of first string breaking: \(\Delta t \approx 1\ \text{fm}\).
* Infrared cut-off for gluon radiation: \(\lambda = 0.65\ \text{GeV}\), fixed from diffractive gluon radiation data.
* Total cross sections used: \(\sigma_{tot}^{NN} = 40\ \text{mb}\), \(\sigma_{tot}^{\pi N} = 20\ \text{mb}\); QCD scale \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 0.25\ \text{GeV}\).
* The effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}(t)\) grows logarithmically with time, reaching a maximum value \(\sigma_{max} \approx \sigma_{tot}^{NN} + \sigma_{tot}^{\pi N} \langle n_M \rangle\) where \(\langle n_M \rangle\) is the mean meson multiplicity.
* The total cross section for the process \(A(e,e'(A-1))X\) is evaluated for \(^4\text{He}\), \(^{16}\text{O}\), and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\); the ratio of distorted to undistorted total cross sections is presented in Fig. 4 (numerical values not given in the provided text).

## Context

This work extends the impulse approximation (IA) treatment of the process \(A(e,e'(A-1))X\) previously developed by Ciofi degli Atti, Kopeliovich, and others (2001) by including FSI from the propagating nucleon debris. It builds on the color string model of hadronization (Casher, Neuberger, Nussinov; Gurvich) and on earlier models of quark re-interaction in nuclei (Kopeliovich, Nemchik, Predazzi, et al.). The approach is intended to provide more direct information on hadron formation lengths and medium modifications of nucleon structure functions (EMC effect) than the less exclusive \(A(e,e'h)X\) measurements (Osborne, Ashman, HERMES). The results enable future experimental tests of the spectator mechanism and the space-time structure of hadronization by comparing the predicted distorted momentum distributions with coincidence data.
