# Phase Transitions: Summary of Discussion Session II of Camerino 2005

> A brief report of the topics which received attention during the discussion session II of the International Workshop on Symmetries and Low-Energy Phase Transitions in Nuclear-Structure Physics, held in Camerino on 9-11 October 2005, is given. These include special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian for various potentials, the study of triaxial shapes and of degrees of freedom other than the quadrupole one (octupole, scissors), as well as the search for experimental manifestations of the critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5), and of the recently proposed critical point supersymmetry E(5/4).

# Phase Transitions: Summary of Discussion Session II of Camerino 2005

## Key claim

The workshop discussion session surveyed progress on critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) and the newly proposed E(5/4) supersymmetry, but many candidate nuclei remain unconfirmed because experimental B(E2) measurements are still preliminary or unreliable, and the theoretical models involve drastic approximations made to maintain analytic solubility.

## Method

The session reviewed special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian for various potentials (infinite square well, finite well, sextic oscillator, Davidson potentials, β⁴, β⁶, β⁸ potentials) in the E(5) and X(5) frameworks, and extended these to triaxial solutions (Z(4), Z(5)) with γ confined around 30°. Participants also discussed the inclusion of octupole deformation and the scissors mode as additional degrees of freedom, and surveyed experimental searches for critical point symmetries in specific nuclei across multiple mass regions.

## Key numbers

- The critical point of the U(5)-O(6) transition occurs at η_C = 0.5, corresponding to a β⁴ potential.
- Best agreement between E(5) and the potential u(β) = (1-η)/2 β² + η/4 (1-β²)² is obtained for η ≈ 5, where the potential develops a bump at the center.
- In the Analytic Quadrupole Octupole Axially symmetric (AQOA) model, the β₁ bandhead is higher than the X(5) bandhead by a factor of almost two, in agreement with experiment for ²²⁶Th and ²²⁶Ra.
- ¹²⁸Ce has a P-factor of 4.8, close to 5, and with 8 valence protons and 12 valence neutron holes it matches ¹⁵²Sm (12 valence protons, 8 valence neutrons).
- ¹⁰²Pd was singled out as the best example of E(5) so far, with no backbending in its ground state band remaining in excellent agreement with parameter-free E(5) predictions up to high angular momenta.

## Context

This discussion session extended the work of Iachello (2000, 2001) on E(5) and X(5) critical point symmetries, and the recently proposed E(5/4) supersymmetry of Iachello (2005). It reviewed alternative potentials studied by Caprio (2005), Fortunato (2005), and others, and compared results with the interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello. The session identified open questions about whether N=90 isotones like ¹⁵⁰Nd and ¹⁵²Sm, known as X(5) examples, are also critical with respect to the octupole degree of freedom, and noted that no analytically soluble model for a shape phase transition of the scissors mode has been constructed.
