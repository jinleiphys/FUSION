# The nuclear shell effects near the r-process path in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> We have investigated the evolution of the shell structure of nuclei in going from the r-process path to the neutron drip line within the framework of the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. By introducing the quartic self-coupling of $ω$ meson in the RHB theory in addition to the non-linear scalar coupling of $σ$ meson, we reproduce the available data on the shell effects about the waiting-point nucleus $^{80}$Zn. With this approach, it is shown that the shell effects at N=82 in the inaccessible region of the r-process path become milder as compared to the Lagrangian with the scalar self-coupling only. However, the shell effects remain stronger as compared to the quenching exhibited by the HFB+SkP approach. It is also shown that in reaching out to the extreme point at the neutron drip line, a terminal situation arises where the shell structure at the magic number is washed out significantly.

# The nuclear shell effects near the r-process path in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

## Key claim

The shell effects at N=82 in the inaccessible region of the r-process path become milder when using a Lagrangian with both scalar self-coupling of the sigma meson and quartic vector self-coupling of the omega meson (forces NL-SV1 and NL-SV2) compared to using scalar self-coupling only (NL-SH), but they remain stronger than the quenching exhibited by the non-relativistic HFB+SkP approach. At the neutron drip line, the shell structure at the magic number is washed out significantly, reaching a terminal situation termed "terminus stratum."

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations for spherical nuclei using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion with 20 shells for both fermionic and bosonic wavefunctions. They employed the finite-range Gogny force D1S for the pairing channel and compared three Lagrangian parameter sets: NL-SH (scalar self-coupling only), NL-SV2, and NL-SV1 (both scalar and vector self-couplings), alongside non-relativistic HFB calculations with the Skyrme force SkP. The study examined two-neutron separation energies (S₂ₙ) and single-particle levels for isotopic chains from Z=36 (Kr) to Z=46 (Pd) across the magic neutron number N=82, from the r-process path to the neutron drip line.

## Key numbers

- The experimental S₂ₙ value for the waiting-point nucleus ⁸⁰Zn (N=50) is about 11 MeV.
- For N=82 Pd and Ru nuclei, the neutron separation energy from RHB results is about 3-4 MeV, indicating these nuclei lie on the r-process path.
- For Sr (Z=38) and Kr (Z=36) isotopes at N=82, the S₂ₙ value decreases from about 2.5 MeV for Sr to about 1.5 MeV for Kr, with the corresponding Sₙ value amounting to 1 MeV or less.
- The depth of the spin-orbit potential is reduced by about 10% for NL-SV2 compared to NL-SH, and reduced further with NL-SV1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of shell effects at the stability line (Sharma et al., 2000), where the vector self-coupling of the omega meson was introduced to remedy the overly strong shell effects produced by scalar-only Lagrangians like NL-SH. The paper directly addresses the conflicting predictions between the RMF theory (Sharma et al., 1994), which predicted strong shell effects at N=82 in Zr nuclei, and the HFB calculations using Skyrme force SkP (Dobaczewski et al., 1994), which suggested quenched shell effects. The results enable improved modeling of r-process nucleosynthesis by providing a relativistic framework that reproduces available data on shell effects in the waiting-point region while predicting the evolution of shell structure toward the neutron drip line.
