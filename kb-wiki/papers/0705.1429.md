# Scaling and isospin effects in quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering in the Relativistic Mean Field Approach

> The role of isospin in quasielastic electron scattering and charge-changing neutrino reactions is investigated in the relativistic impulse approximation. We analyze proton and neutron scaling functions making use of various theoretical descriptions for the final-state interactions, focusing on the effects introduced by the presence of strong scalar and vector terms in the relativistic mean field approach. An explanation for the differences observed in the scaling functions evaluated from $(e,e')$ and $(ν,μ)$ reactions is provided by invoking the differences in isoscalar and isovector contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed differences between scaling functions from electron scattering and charge-changing neutrino reactions arise from the different roles of isoscalar and isovector nucleon form factors, and that the longitudinal scaling function from electron scattering agrees with the neutrino-scattering scaling function better than the transverse scaling function from electron scattering does, contrary to naive expectation.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations for quasielastic electron scattering and charge-changing neutrino reactions on 12C, using relativistic mean field (RMF) potentials to describe final-state interactions. They compared the fully relativistic RMF results with the effective momentum approach (EMA), which lacks the dynamical enhancement of lower Dirac spinor components, and analyzed the separate contributions of protons and neutrons as well as the isoscalar and isovector components of the nuclear response.

## Key numbers

- For 12C at momentum transfer q = 1 GeV/c, the transverse scaling function f_T is about 20% larger than the longitudinal f_L for the CC2 current operator, and almost twice as large for the CC1 operator, within the RMF model.
- The transverse scaling function f_T from electron scattering overestimates the experimental longitudinal data by approximately 20% near the maximum (psi' approximately 0).
- The neutrino and antineutrino scaling functions were computed at incident lepton energy epsilon = 1 GeV and lepton scattering angle theta_mu = 45 degrees.
- The comparison of electron and neutrino scaling functions was performed at fixed incident lepton energy epsilon = 1 GeV and momentum transfer q = 0.7 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superscaling in quasielastic electron scattering (Day 1990, Donnelly and Sick 1999, Maieron et al. 2002) and the SuperScaling Analysis (SuSA) approach introduced for neutrino-nucleus cross sections (Barbaro et al. 2004). It builds on earlier RMF calculations (Caballero et al. 2005, 2006) that showed the RMF model produces the correct asymmetric shape of the scaling function, unlike non-relativistic mean field approaches. The paper provides theoretical support for the SuSA assumption that the longitudinal scaling function from electron scattering can be used to predict charge-changing neutrino cross sections, and identifies the need for more precise separated longitudinal and transverse data to disentangle isoscalar and isovector contributions.
