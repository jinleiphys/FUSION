# The dielectric function excited by $ρNN$ tensor coupling in nuclear matter

> The dielectric function of nuclear matter excited by $ρNN$ tensor coupling has been studied in the framework of Finite temperature field theory. The induced current mechanism has been introduced to explain the three extrema on the dielectric function curve, of which one is in the space-like region and the other two are in the time-like region. It points out that the tensor coupling contributes much more large amplitude than the vector coupling and plays a more important role on the time-like region compared with its effect on the space-like region.

## Key claim

The dielectric function of nuclear matter excited by the \(\rho NN\) tensor coupling exhibits three extrema (one in the space-like region and two in the time-like region) and one singularity, with the tensor coupling contributing a much larger amplitude than the vector coupling and playing a more important role in the time-like region; however, the singularity occurs when the mass-shell condition \(K_0^2 - k^2 = m_\rho^2\) is satisfied and is considered "trivial to some extent" because an in-medium meson cannot meet that condition.

## Method

The authors calculated the longitudinal part of the polarization tensor \(\Pi_L\) for the \(\rho NN\) tensor coupling using finite-temperature field theory in the real-time formalism, including both the vector and tensor coupling terms in the interaction Lagrangian. The thermal nucleon propagator was decomposed into Feynman (vacuum) and density-dependent (Fermi-Dirac) parts, and only the temperature-dependent part was retained after renormalization of the divergent Feynman part. The dielectric function was then obtained via \(\varepsilon = 1 - \Pi_L / (K^2 - m_\rho^2)\), and numerical results were plotted for fixed momentum \(k = 100\) MeV at temperatures \(T = 10\) and \(20\) MeV and chemical potentials \(\mu = 800\) and \(850\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The singularity on the dielectric function curve occurs at \(K_0/k \approx 7.8\) for the solid curve (T=10 MeV, \(\mu=800\) MeV).
- The two extrema in the time-like region appear at \(K_0/k \approx 16\) (i.e., \(K_0 = 1600\) MeV) for the solid curve, corresponding to the threshold \(2m_N^* \sim 1500\) MeV.
- The extremum in the space-like region occurs at \(K_0/k \approx 0.3\).
- The effective nucleon mass \(m_N^*\) varies with \(\mu\) and T: at T=10 MeV, \(\mu=800\) MeV, \(m_N^* = 767.7\) MeV; at T=10 MeV, \(\mu=850\) MeV, \(m_N^* = 830.8\) MeV; at T=20 MeV, \(\mu=800\) MeV, \(m_N^* = 780.3\) MeV.
- The tensor coupling constant used is \(\kappa_\rho = 6.1\), with \(g_{\rho NN}^2 = 6.91\) and the \(\rho\) meson mass \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dielectric functions in QED and QCD plasma using hard thermal loop approximations, and specifically builds on the dielectric function for the \(\omega\) meson studied with the hard nucleon loop approximation (Ref. d). It also complements earlier investigations of the \(\rho\) meson dispersion relation in medium (Refs. c, f) and the sensitivity of the \(\rho\) meson mass to the \(\rho NN\) tensor coupling (Ref. b). The results enable a more complete understanding of the in-medium properties of the \(\rho\) meson, which is crucial for interpreting dilepton signals from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the low-mass enhancement of lepton pairs observed at CERN/SPS.
