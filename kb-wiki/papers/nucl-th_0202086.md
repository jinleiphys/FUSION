# Phi meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Within a multiphase transport model we study phi meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions from both superposition of initial multiple proton-proton interactions and the secondary collisions in the produced hadronic matter. The yield of phi mesons is then reconstructed from their decaying product of either the kaon-antikaon pairs or the dimuon pairs. Since the kaon-antikaon pairs at midrapidity with low transverse momenta are predominantly rescattered or absorbed in the hadronic medium, they can not be used to reconstruct the phi meson and lead thus to a smaller reconstructed phi meson yield than that reconstructed from the dimuon channel. With in-medium mass modifications of kaons and phi mesons, the phi yield from dimuons is further enhanced compared to that from the kaon-antikaon pairs. The model result is compared with the experimental data at the CERN/SPS and RHIC energies and its implications to quark-gluon plasma formation are discussed.

# Phi meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Within a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, the phi meson yield reconstructed from dimuon pairs is about a factor of 1.7 higher than that from kaon-antikaon pairs for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy, but this factor can increase to about two when in-medium mass modifications of kaons and phi mesons are included; however, the factor of 2–4 enhancement observed in the NA50 data compared to NA49 data cannot be fully explained by hadronic rescattering and medium effects alone, suggesting that additional mechanisms such as quark-gluon plasma formation may be required.

## Method

The authors extended the AMPT model, which combines initial partonic interactions from HIJING with the ZPC parton cascade and final-state hadronic scatterings via the ART model, to include phi meson production and interactions in hadronic matter. They incorporated phi meson production from baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and kaon-antikaon channels, as well as phi meson scattering with mesons (π, ρ, ω, K, K*), using cross sections from one-boson-exchange models, effective Lagrangians, and detailed balance. The reconstruction of phi mesons from kaon-antikaon pairs and dimuon pairs was modeled by integrating the time evolution of phi meson abundance with in-medium decay widths, accounting for rescattering of kaons and the free escape of dimuons.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV (SPS), the AMPT model without medium effects gives a phi meson yield from the dimuon channel that is a factor of 1.7 larger than from the kaon-antikaon channel at midrapidity.
- With in-medium mass modifications, the phi meson yield from dimuons is about a factor of 2 larger than from kaon-antikaon pairs at midrapidity.
- The inverse slope parameter for phi mesons from the dimuon channel at SPS is T = 228 MeV (AMPT), compared to the NA50 value of T = 227 ± 10 MeV.
- The inverse slope parameter for phi mesons from the kaon channel at SPS is T = 267 MeV (AMPT), compared to the NA49 value of T = 305 ± 15 MeV.
- At SPS, the suppression factor R(m_T) at low transverse mass is about 40% without medium effects and about 33% with medium effects.
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy √s = 130A GeV, the AMPT model gives a phi meson yield from dimuons that is about 1.5 times larger than from kaon-antikaon pairs at midrapidity.
- The inverse slope parameter for phi mesons from the kaon channel at RHIC is T = 335 MeV (AMPT), compared to the STAR value of T = 379 ± 50 MeV.
- The phi meson elastic cross section with nucleons is taken as 0.56 mb from vector meson dominance model.
- The in-medium phi meson mass decreases as m*_φ/m_φ ≈ 1 − 0.0255 ρ/ρ_0.
- At density ρ = 2ρ_0, the in-medium phi meson width to K anti-K is about 45 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson production in heavy ion collisions, particularly the RQMD model calculations by Johnson et al. (2001) that found a 40–60% suppression factor for kaon reconstruction, and the schematic model by Filip et al. (2001) that included kaon mass modifications. The results are compared with experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration (kaon channel) and NA50 Collaboration (dimuon channel) at CERN/SPS, and from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC. The study enables future investigations of whether the discrepancy between the two experimental channels at SPS requires invoking quark-gluon plasma formation or color rope formation as suggested by Shor (1985) and Greiner et al. (1993), and motivates a dedicated experiment measuring both decay channels in the same kinematic range.
