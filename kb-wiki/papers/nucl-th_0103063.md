# p_T-fluctuations in high-energy p-p and A-A collisions

> The event-by-event p_T-fluctuations in proton-proton and central Pb-Pb collisions, which have been experimentally studied by means of the so-called Phi-measure, are analyzed. The contribution due to the correlation which couples the average p_T to the event multiplicity is computed. The correlation appears to be far too weak to explain the preliminary experimental value of Phi (p_T) in p-p interactions. The significance of the result is discussed.

# p_T-fluctuations in high-energy p-p and A-A collisions

## Key claim

The correlation between average transverse momentum and event multiplicity is far too weak to explain the preliminary experimental value of Phi(p_T) in proton-proton collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, unless the preliminary data overestimates the real value.

## Method

The authors computed the contribution to the Phi-measure of transverse momentum fluctuations arising from the multiplicity-dependent temperature (slope parameter) of the single-particle p_T distribution, using both analytical formulas (assuming massless particles and weak correlation) and Monte Carlo simulations with a thermal model. The finite detector acceptance was modeled by registering each generated pion with a fixed probability p, and the correlation strength Delta T was extracted by fitting the experimentally observed dependence of average p_T on charged particle multiplicity in p-p collisions at 205 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon in the laboratory rapidity window (4.0, 5.5), the measured Phi(p_T) = 4.6 ± 1.5 MeV; when short-range correlations are excluded, Phi(p_T) = 0.6 ± 1 MeV.
- The preliminary result for proton-proton interactions in the same acceptance is Phi(p_T) = 5 ± 1 MeV.
- The temperature parameter extracted from the data is T = 167 ± 1.5 MeV, with correlation strength Delta T = 1.25 ± 0.25 MeV for charged particle multiplicity ⟨N⟩ = 6.56.
- The Monte Carlo simulation for p-p collisions with Delta T = 1.25 MeV and acceptance probability p = 0.2 gives Phi(p_T) = 0.41 ± 0.07 MeV, significantly below the preliminary experimental value of 5 ± 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of event-by-event p_T fluctuations measured by the NA49 collaboration (App99) using the Phi-measure introduced by Gazdzicki (1992). The authors had previously shown (Mrowczynski, 1998, 1999) that Bose-Einstein correlations of pions modified by hadron resonances fully explain the observed Phi(p_T) in central Pb-Pb collisions. The present paper demonstrates that the correlation between average p_T and multiplicity cannot account for the preliminary p-p data, leaving the origin of dynamical long-range correlations in nucleon-nucleon interactions as an open question. The results enable future experimental and theoretical studies to focus on other sources of fluctuations, such as hadron resonance decays, and motivate measurements with enlarged acceptance.
