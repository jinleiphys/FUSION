# Octupole deformation properties of actinide isotopes within a mean field approach

> We discuss the octupole deformation properties of many even-even actinide isotopes. The analysis is carried out in the mean field framework (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation) by using the axially symmetric octupole moment as a constraint. A one-dimensional octupole collective Hamiltonian is used to obtain properties like excitation energies or $E1$ and $E3$ transition probabilities of the negative parity band-heads associated to the lowest lying $1^{-}$ and $3^{-}$ states. The evolution of these values with neutron number is discussed and a comparison with available experimental data is made. In order to minimize the uncertainties associated to the energy density functional used, the calculations have been carried out for an assorted set ranging from the BCP1 functional to the finite range Gogny interaction with the D1S, D1N and D1M parametrization.

## Key claim

The paper claims that octupole deformation effects persist in actinide isotopes beyond radium, with strong octupole correlations predicted for specific mass ranges of uranium, plutonium, curium, and californium isotopes, but the authors hedge that the numerical values of physical quantities oscillate within a range of plus or minus 40% and should be taken only as an indication of the order of magnitude to be expected, given the uncertainties associated with the different energy density functionals used.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the axially symmetric octupole moment Q3 as a constraint to generate potential energy curves for even-even actinide isotopes. They then constructed a one-dimensional octupole collective Hamiltonian within the Gaussian Overlap Approximation to the Generator Coordinate Method, using the ATDHF+ZPE set of collective parameters, to compute excitation energies of the lowest 1- and 3- states as well as E1 and E3 transition probabilities. The calculations were carried out with four different energy density functionals: the Gogny D1S, D1N, and D1M parametrizations, and the BCP1 functional.

## Key numbers

- For uranium isotopes, five nuclei (222U to 230U) show an octupole deformed minimum with a depth never exceeding 1.5 MeV
- For plutonium isotopes, the mean field octupole correlation energy reaches as large as 1 MeV in 226Pu
- For plutonium isotopes, B(E3) transition probabilities reach values of up to 60 W.u. for the octupole deformed isotopes
- For curium isotopes, B(E3) transition probabilities are around 50 W.u. in the mass range 226-232
- The zero point energy correction values are on the average of the order of 1.5 MeV across the uranium isotopes
- The theoretical values for physical quantities oscillate typically in a range of plus or minus 40%

## Context

This work extends a previous theoretical survey of K=0 octupole states (Robledo 2011) that used the Generator Coordinate Method for over 800 even-even nuclei but did not include many nuclei close to the proton drip line studied here. The paper also builds on earlier studies of octupole deformation in radium, thorium, and barium isotopes using the BCP functional (Robledo 2010). The results enable predictions for octupole correlations in heavy actinides near the proton drip line where experimental data are scarce, and the comparison across multiple functionals provides an estimate of theoretical uncertainties for future experimental searches.
