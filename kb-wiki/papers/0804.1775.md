# Analytical solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential

> Analytical solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian are obtained in the $γ$-unstable case, as well as in an exactly separable rotational case with $γ\approx 0$, called the exactly separable Morse (ES-M) solution. Closed expressions for the energy eigenvalues are obtained through the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM), the effectiveness of which is demonstrated by solving the relevant Bohr equations for the Davidson and Kratzer potentials. All medium mass and heavy nuclei with known $β_1$ and $γ_1$ bandheads have been fitted by using the two-parameter $γ$-unstable solution for transitional nuclei and the three-parameter ES-M for rotational ones. It is shown that bandheads and energy spacings within the bands are well reproduced for more than 50 nuclei in each case.

## Key claim

The paper claims that analytical solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Morse potential can be obtained for any angular momentum L, despite the Morse potential being exactly soluble only for L=0, by using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) combined with the Pekeris approximation; the solutions are limited to the γ-unstable case and the exactly separable rotational case with γ≈0, and the method is demonstrated to work well for more than 50 nuclei in each category, though it fails for critical point nuclei like those near the X(5) symmetry.

## Method

The authors solved the Bohr Hamiltonian by separating variables into a β-dependent radial equation and a γ-dependent equation, using a potential of the form u(β,γ)=u(β)+u(γ)/β² for the exactly separable rotational case with γ≈0, and a γ-independent potential u(β) for the γ-unstable case. The radial Schrödinger equation for the Morse potential was converted to a second-order differential equation and solved via the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM), applying the Pekeris approximation to handle the exponential terms. The γ equation was solved using a harmonic oscillator potential u(γ)=(3c)²γ², and the resulting energy eigenvalues were expressed in closed form with parameters βe, a, and the γ-stiffness parameter C.

## Key numbers

- For the γ-unstable Morse solution, 54 nuclei with mass A≥100 and R4/2=E(4)/E(2)<2.6 were fitted using two free parameters (βe, a); the quality measure σ remained below 1 in most cases.
- For the exactly separable rotational Morse solution (ES-M), 45 rare earths and 13 actinides with mass A≥150 and R4/2>2.9 were fitted using three free parameters (βe, a, C); the quality measure σ remained below 1 in most cases.
- The energy eigenvalues for the γ-unstable Morse solution are given by εn,τ = (ν c0/βe²) - [γ1²/(2βe γ2) - (n+1/2)(α/βe)]², where ν = τ(τ+3)+2, α = aβe, and c0, c1, c2 are defined in terms of α.
- The energy eigenvalues for the exactly separable rotational Morse solution (ES-M) are given by εn,L = (μ c0/βe²) - [γ1²/(2βe γ2) - (n+1/2)(α/βe)]², where μ = L(L+1)/3 + 2 + λ and λ = (3C)(nγ+1) - K²/3.

## Context

This work extends the family of exactly separable solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian, previously obtained for the Davidson potential (ES-D, Bonatsos et al. 2004), Kratzer potential (Fortunato 2005), and Coulomb potential (Fortunato 2005), to the Morse potential, which is known to be exactly soluble only for L=0. The solutions fill the region between vibrational [U(5)] and γ-unstable [O(6)] limits (γ-unstable case) and between vibrational and prolate deformed rotational [SU(3)] limits (ES-M case), avoiding the overprediction of beta band spacings by a factor of two that occurs in X(5)-type models (Iachello 2001). The extra parameter in ES-M compared to ES-D extends applicability to higher angular momenta, but the Morse potential cannot describe critical point nuclei such as 150Nd, 152Sm, 154Gd, and 178Os, which require flat potentials as in X(5).
