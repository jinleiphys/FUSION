# Geometric scaling in symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We show that the centrality dependence of the multiplicity is identical in Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC, up to a constant factor. This geometric scaling is revealed if one defines centrality according to impact parameter, as opposed to the usual experimental definition, which is in terms of multiplicity. We reconstruct the impact parameter dependence of the multiplicity from experimental data using a recently-developed inversion method, which turns out to describe ALICE Xe+Xe multiplicity data much better than usual Monte Carlo Glauber fits. The multiplicity as function of impact parameter extracted from ALICE data is compared to model calculations.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of the mean multiplicity in Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions at the LHC is identical up to a constant factor, but only when centrality is defined by impact parameter rather than by the usual experimental definition based on multiplicity.

## Method

The authors apply a Bayesian inversion method to reconstruct the impact parameter dependence of the multiplicity from experimental data, assuming that multiplicity fluctuations at fixed impact parameter follow a gamma distribution. They fit the observed multiplicity distribution from ALICE Xe+Xe data at 5.44 TeV using a convolution of the gamma fluctuation kernel with the impact parameter distribution, then compare the extracted mean multiplicity as a function of true centrality between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The gamma distribution fit to ALICE Xe+Xe data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV deviates by up to 10% for the Monte Carlo Glauber fit used by ALICE, while the inversion method fit ratio is very close to 1
- A polynomial of degree 6 is used for the mean multiplicity as a function of centrality in the fit
- The T_RENTo model with p=0 (sqrt(T_A T_B)) shows better agreement with data than p=1 (T_A + T_B, equivalent to wounded nucleon model)

## Context

This work extends the inversion method developed by Das (2017) and Rogly (2018) for reconstructing impact parameter from multiplicity without relying on specific microscopic models. It contradicts the standard Monte Carlo Glauber fitting approach used by the ALICE collaboration, showing that the inversion method provides a more accurate representation of data. The geometric scaling result enables model testing of particle production mechanisms, particularly constraining the T_RENTo model parameter p, and suggests that the multiplicity is proportional to the mass number of colliding nuclei at the same energy.
