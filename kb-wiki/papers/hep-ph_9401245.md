# Kaon-Nucleon Scattering from Chiral Lagrangians

> The s-wave $K^{\pm}N$ scattering amplitude is computed up to one-loop order corresponding to next-to-next-to-leading order (or N$^2$LO in short) with a heavy-baryon effective chiral Lagrangian. Constraining the low-energy constants by on-shell scattering lengths, we obtain contributions of each chiral order up to N$^2$LO and find that the chiral corrections are ``natural" in the sense of viable effective field theories. We have also calculated off-shell $s$-wave $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes relevant to kaonic atoms and $K^-$ condensation in ``nuclear star" matter including the effect of $Λ(1405)$. The $K^-p$ amplitude is found to be quite sensitive to the intermediate $Λ(1405)$ contribution, while the $K^-n$ amplitude varies smoothly with the C.M. energy. The crossing-even one-loop corrections are found to play an important role in determining the higher-order chiral corrections.

## Key claim

The paper computes s-wave kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding that chiral corrections are "natural" and that the attraction found at tree order remains unaffected by loop graphs, but the authors note that complications from increased terms and off-shell ambiguities at loop orders prevent as simple an analysis as at tree level.

## Method

The authors computed s-wave K±N scattering amplitudes using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to one-loop order (N²LO), including the Λ(1405) resonance as an independent heavy baryon field. They constrained the low-energy constants by fitting to empirical on-shell scattering lengths, then calculated off-shell K⁻N scattering amplitudes relevant to kaonic atoms and kaon condensation.

## Key numbers

- Empirical scattering lengths used as constraints: a₀^{K⁺p} = -0.31 fm, a₀^{K⁻p} = -0.67 + i 0.63 fm, a₀^{K⁺n} = -0.20 fm, a₀^{K⁻n} = +0.37 + i 0.57 fm
- Tree-order empirical parameters: (d̄_s - d̄_v)_{emp} ≈ (0.05~0.06) fm, (d̄_s + d̄_v)_{emp} ≈ 0.13 fm, ḡ²_{Λ_R} = 0.15 with m_{Λ_R} = 1.405 GeV
- One-loop crossing-even contributions: L_s M_K ≈ -0.109 fm, L_v M_K ≈ +0.021 fm
- Λ(1405) decay width from tree-level coupling: Γ_{Λ_R} ≈ 50 MeV
- At N²LO, required effective coupling: ḡ²_{Λ_R} ≈ 0.25
- Extracted KN sigma term: σ_{KN} ≈ 2.83 m_π

## Context

This work extends the tree-order (next-to-leading order) calculation of Brown, Lee, Rho and Thorsson (BLRT) (1992) by including one-loop contributions (N²LO), similar to the one-loop calculations Bernard et al. performed for s-wave pion-nucleon scattering. The paper addresses the question of whether chiral Lagrangians used for predicting kaon condensation (first proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986)) are consistent with empirical kaon-nucleon scattering data, enabling more reliable calculations of kaonic atom properties and kaon condensation in dense nuclear star matter.
