# The axially-deformed relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation based on point-coupling interactions

> Collective nuclear excitations, like giant resonances, are sensitive to nuclear deformation, as evidenced by alterations in their excitation energies and transition strength distributions. A common theoretical framework to study these collective modes, the random-phase approximation (RPA), has to deal with large dimensions spanned by all possible particle-hole configurations satisfying certain symmetries. This work aims to establish a new theoretical framework to study the impact of deformation on spin-isospin excitations, that can provide fast and reliable solutions of the RPA equations. The nuclear ground state is determined with the axially-deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model based on relativistic point-coupling energy density functionals (EDFs). To study the excitations in the charge-exchange channel, an axially-deformed proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle RPA (pnRQRPA) is developed in the linear response approach. After benchmarking the axially-deformed pnRQRPA in the spherical limit, a study of spin-isospin excitations including Fermi, Gamow-Teller (GT), and Spin-Dipole (SD) is performed for selected $pf$-shell nuclei. For GT transitions, it is demonstrated that deformation leads to considerable fragmentation of the strength function. A mechanism inducing the fragmentation is studied by decomposing the total strength to different projections of total angular momentum $K$ and constraining the nuclear shape to either spherical, prolate or oblate. A similar fragmentation is also observed for SD transitions, although somewhat moderated by the complex structure of these transitions, while the Fermi strength is almost shape-independent. The axially-deformed pnRQRPA introduced in this work opens perspectives for future studies of deformation effects on astrophysically relevant weak interaction processes, in particular beta decay and electron capture.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a new axially-deformed proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnRQRPA) based on relativistic point-coupling energy density functionals, demonstrating that deformation leads to considerable fragmentation of Gamow-Teller strength functions for selected pf-shell nuclei, while Fermi strength remains almost shape-independent.

## Method

The nuclear ground state is first determined using the axially-deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with point-coupling interactions. The pnRQRPA equations are then solved in the linear response formalism, where the interaction matrix is expressed as a sum of separable terms, allowing the response to be computed in a reduced space of interaction channels rather than the full two-quasiparticle space. The method is benchmarked against the spherical pnRQRPA in the spherical limit, then applied to study Fermi, Gamow-Teller (GT), and Spin-Dipole (SD) excitations in selected even-even pf-shell nuclei, decomposing the total strength by angular momentum projection K and constraining nuclear shapes to spherical, prolate, or oblate geometries.

## Key numbers

* For GT transitions in selected pf-shell nuclei, deformation leads to considerable fragmentation of the strength function, demonstrated by decomposing the total strength into different projections of total angular momentum K (K = 0, ±1).
* For SD transitions, similar fragmentation is observed but "somewhat moderated by the complex structure of these transitions," with modes K = 0, ±1, ±2 considered.
* Fermi strength is "almost shape-independent" across spherical, prolate, and oblate nuclear shapes.
* The dimension of the axially-deformed pnRQRPA equation is "much larger" than the spherical case; the reduced space of interaction channels is around 10^3, compared to the two-quasiparticle dimension of around 10^5 for a reasonable basis size.

## Context

This work extends the spherical pnRQRPA framework previously developed by the same group (Ravlić et al., 2021) for tin isotopes into the axial geometry required for deformed nuclei. It provides an alternative to finite-amplitude method (FAM) approaches used with non-relativistic EDFs (e.g., PhysRevC.90.024308, PhysRevC.93.014304) and to schematic separable interaction models for deformed nuclei (e.g., Sarriguren, 1999; PhysRevC.91.044304). The method enables future studies of deformation effects on astrophysically relevant weak interaction processes, particularly beta decay and electron capture in r-process nucleosynthesis and core-collapse supernovae.
