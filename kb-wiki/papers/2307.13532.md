# Quantum van der Waals theory meets quarkyonic matter

> We incorporate the empirical low-density properties of isospin symmetric nuclear matter into the excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter by including attractive mean field in the nucleonic sector and considering variations on the nucleon excluded volume mechanism. This corresponds to the quantum van der Waals equation for nucleons, with the interaction parameters fixed to empirical ground state properties of nuclear matter. The resulting equation of state exhibits the nuclear liquid-gas transition at $n_B \leq ρ_0$ and undergoes a transition to quarkyonic matter at densities $n_B \sim 1.5-2 ρ_0$ that are reachable in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions. The transition is accompanied by a peak in the sound velocity. The results depend only mildly on the chosen excluded volume mechanism but do require the introduction of an infrared regulator $Λ$ to avoid the acausal sound velocity. We also consider the recently proposed baryquark matter scenario for the realization of the Pauli exclusion principle, which yields a similar equation of state and turns out to be energetically favored in all the considered setups.

## Key claim

By incorporating an attractive mean-field into the excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter and fixing interaction parameters to empirical nuclear ground state properties, the resulting equation of state exhibits a transition to quarkyonic matter at densities of 1.5-2 times normal nuclear density, but the introduction of an infrared regulator Lambda is still required to avoid acausal sound velocity in the quarkyonic matter scenario.

## Method

The authors incorporate the quantum van der Waals equation for nucleons into the excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter, fixing the attractive mean-field parameter a and excluded volume parameter b to reproduce the nuclear ground state at density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}. They consider three different excluded volume prescriptions (van der Waals, Carnahan-Starling, and trivirial model) and two realizations of the Pauli exclusion principle between confined and deconfined quarks (quarkyonic matter and baryquark matter), then minimize the energy density at fixed baryon density to determine the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear ground state density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} (by construction)
- Binding energy at ground state: epsilon/n_B - m_N = -16 MeV at n_B = rho_0
- Quarkyonic matter transition density: n_B ~ 1.5-1.9 rho_0 across all excluded volume prescriptions
- Quarkyonic matter sound velocity peak density: 1.47 rho_0 (QvdW), 1.76 rho_0 (CS), 1.89 rho_0 (TVM)
- Quarkyonic matter sound velocity peak values: v_s^2 = 0.94 (QvdW), 0.71 (CS), 0.65 (TVM)
- Baryquark matter sound velocity peak density: 1.40 rho_0 (QvdW), 1.67 rho_0 (CS), 1.80 rho_0 (TVM)
- Baryquark matter sound velocity peak values: v_s^2 = 1.07 (QvdW), 0.93 (CS), 0.87 (TVM)
- Infrared regulator value used in quarkyonic matter: Lambda = 0.2 GeV
- van der Waals interaction parameters: a = 329 MeV fm^3, b = 3.42 fm^3 (vdW model)
- Carnahan-Starling parameters: a = 347 MeV fm^3, b = 4.43 fm^3
- Trivirial model parameters: a = 349 MeV fm^3, b = 4.28 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the excluded-volume model for quarkyonic matter developed by Jeong (2019) and the baryquark matter scenario proposed by Koch (2022) by incorporating realistic low-density nuclear matter properties through the quantum van der Waals framework of Vovchenko (2015). The results show that fixing nuclear interaction parameters to empirical ground state properties constrains the quark onset density to 1.5-2 rho_0, which is reachable in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions, and that baryquark matter is energetically favored over quarkyonic matter in all considered setups, extending the conclusion of Koch (2022). The work enables future studies incorporating density-dependent mean fields to improve the description of nuclear incompressibility and quark-nucleon interactions to address the acausal sound velocity issue.
