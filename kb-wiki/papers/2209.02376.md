# On the influence of Maxwell--Chern--Simons electrodynamics in nuclear fusion involving electronic and muonic molecules

> New results recently obtained (\textit{Annals of Physics} (New York) a.n.~168943) established some non-relativistic ground state solutions for three-body molecules interacting through a Chern--Simons model. Within this model, it was argued that Chern--Simons potential should not help improve the fusion rates by replacing electrons with muons, in the case of particular muonic molecules. This achievement motivated us to investigate quantitatively whether or not the Maxwell--Chern--Simons electrodynamics could influence positively, for example, the probability of having a muon-catalyzed fusion; its contribution to electronic molecules is also considered in this letter. The principal factors related to the probability of elementary nuclear fusion are therefore numerically calculated and compared with their analogs admitting other forms of interaction like $-1/ρ$ and $\ln (ρ)$. The analysis carried on here confirms that one should not expect a significant improvement in nuclear fusion rates in the case of muonic molecules, although, surprisingly, the same is not true for electronic molecules, compared with other theoretical predictions. Numerical predictions for the fusion rates for $ppe$, $ppμ$, $dde$ and $ddμ$ molecules are given as well as the predicted value for the tunneling rate for these molecules.

## Key claim

The Maxwell–Chern–Simons electrodynamics does not significantly improve nuclear fusion rates for muonic molecules compared to other potentials, but surprisingly it yields fusion probabilities for electronic molecules that are comparable to or better than those predicted by the logarithmic potential, suggesting a possible path to low-temperature fusion without replacing electrons with muons.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for three-body molecules (ppe, dde, ppμ, ddμ) in a planar (2D) geometry using the Maxwell–Chern–Simons potential, with the photon topological mass taken as a fraction of the electron mass (λ = 0.2×10⁻³ and λ = 0.2×10⁻⁵). They computed the probability of finding the two nuclei within 10 fm separation and the semiclassical tunneling coefficient for each molecule, and compared these results with those obtained using logarithmic (ln ρ) and Coulomb-like (−1/ρ) potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the ppe molecule, the probability of small internuclear separation under the Chern–Simons potential is 3.47×10⁻⁶ (λ = 0.2×10⁻³) and 2.68×10⁻⁵ (λ = 0.2×10⁻⁵), compared to 7.23×10⁻⁶ for the logarithmic potential and 2.4×10⁻⁸⁴ for the −1/ρ potential.
- For the ddμ molecule, the probability under Chern–Simons is 9.54×10⁻⁵ (λ = 0.2×10⁻³) and 8.97×10⁻⁴ (λ = 0.2×10⁻⁵), compared to 1.6×10⁻² for the logarithmic potential and 1.2×10⁻¹⁰ for the −1/ρ potential.
- The tunneling coefficient T for ppe under Chern–Simons is 8.54×10⁻³ (λ = 0.2×10⁻³) and 9.87×10⁻² (λ = 0.2×10⁻⁵), compared to 9.59×10⁻⁸ for the logarithmic potential and 6.89×10⁻⁷¹ for the −1/ρ potential.
- For ppμ, the tunneling coefficient under Chern–Simons is 9.46×10⁻³ (λ = 0.2×10⁻³) and 9.89×10⁻² (λ = 0.2×10⁻⁵), compared to 1.56×10⁻² for the logarithmic potential and 1.92×10⁻⁵ for the −1/ρ potential.
- The fusion rate for ppμ under Chern–Simons is about 10⁵ times greater than the predicted 3D result from Caruso (2019).

## Context

This work extends the planar (2D) fusion studies of Caruso (2019) and Felipe (2020), which showed that muon-catalyzed fusion can be significantly enhanced in two-dimensional geometries, and the Chern–Simons analysis of Felipe (2021) which found that the Chern–Simons potential alone does not improve muonic fusion rates. The present results confirm that Maxwell–Chern–Simons electrodynamics does not aid muon-catalyzed fusion, but unexpectedly predicts that electronic molecules (ppe, dde) under this potential could achieve fusion probabilities comparable to or better than those from the logarithmic potential, opening the possibility of low-temperature fusion without muons if the real planar interaction is of Maxwell–Chern–Simons type.
