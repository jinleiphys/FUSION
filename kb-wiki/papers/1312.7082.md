# Critical stability of few-body systems

> When a two-body system is bound by a zero-range interaction, the corresponding three-body system -- considered in a non-relativistic framework -- collapses, that is its binding energy is unbounded from below. In a paper by J.V. Lindesay and H.P. Noyes it was shown that the relativistic effects result in an effective repulsion in such a way that three-body binding energy remains also finite, thus preventing the three-body system from collapse. Later, this property was confirmed in other works based on different versions of relativistic approaches. However, the three-body system exists only for a limited range of two-body binding energy values. For stronger two-body interaction, the relativistic three-body system still collapses. A similar phenomenon was found in a two-body systems themselves: a two-fermion system with one-boson exchange interaction in a state with zero angular momentum J=0 exists if the coupling constant does not exceed some critical value but it also collapses for larger coupling constant. For a J=1 state, it collapses for any coupling constant value. These properties are called "critical stability". This contribution aims to be a brief review of this field pioneered by H.P. Noyes.

# Critical stability of few-body systems

## Key claim
Relativistic effects can prevent the three-body Thomas collapse for zero-range interactions, but only within a limited range of two-body binding energies; for stronger two-body interactions, the relativistic three-body system still collapses, and the two-fermion system with one-boson exchange also collapses when the coupling constant exceeds a critical value.

## Method
The authors solved the relativistic three-body equation for three equal-mass bosons with zero-range interaction using the explicitly covariant formulation of Light-Front Dynamics, reducing the Faddeev equation to a single integral equation for the vertex function. For the two-fermion system with Yukawa (one-boson exchange) interaction, they analyzed the asymptotic behavior of the kernel in the Light-Front dynamics equations for states with total angular momentum J=0 and J=1, applying the Smirnov collapse condition to determine critical coupling constants.

## Key numbers
- Three-body binding energy in the two-body zero binding limit: B3(c) ≈ 0.012 m (where m is the particle mass)
- Critical two-body mass below which the three-boson system no longer exists: M2(c) ≈ 1.43 m, corresponding to maximal two-body binding energy B2 = 0.57 m
- For the J=0 two-fermion system with Yukawa interaction in Light-Front dynamics: critical coupling constant αc = 3.723 (gc = 6.840)
- For the J=0 two-fermion system with Yukawa interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter approach: critical coupling constant αc = π (gc = 2π)
- For the J=1 two-fermion state: collapses for any value of the coupling constant

## Context
This work reviews and extends the field of "critical stability" pioneered by H.P. Noyes, building on the finding by Lindesay and Noyes that relativistic effects prevent the Thomas collapse (Thomas, 1935) in three-body systems with zero-range interactions. The authors show that this stabilization is limited, and that a similar collapse phenomenon occurs in two-fermion systems with Yukawa interaction, consistent with earlier work by Smirnov and with results from the Bethe-Salpeter equation approach (Carbonell, 2010; Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013). The findings establish universal critical quantities for bosonic systems and identify conditions under which relativistic few-body systems become unstable, enabling future studies of the limits of nuclear binding.
