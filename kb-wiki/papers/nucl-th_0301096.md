# Constraints on background contributions from K+ Lambda electroproduction

> Results for response functions for kaon electroproduction on the proton are presented. A tree-level hadrodynamical model is adopted and it is shown that some of the electroproduction response functions are particularly powerful with the eye on gaining control over the parameterization of the background diagrams. The existing data set for the p(e,e'K+)Lambda reaction appears to rule out the use of a g_{K+ Lambda p} coupling constant beyond the boundaries of softly broken SU(3) flavor symmetry. Also the use of soft hadronic form factors, which has been proposed as a valid alternative for a hadrodynamical description of the p(gamma,K+)Lambda data in the resonance region, seems to be disfavored by the magnitude of the measured p(e,e'K+)Lambda cross sections.

## Key claim

The existing $p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda$ data appear to rule out background models that use a $g_{K^+ \Lambda p}$ coupling constant beyond the boundaries of softly broken SU(3) flavor symmetry or that adopt soft hadronic form factors, even though those same models are compatible with the existing $p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda$ photoproduction data.

## Method

A tree-level hadrodynamical model was extended from $p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda$ photoproduction to $p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda$ electroproduction, using the same interaction Lagrangians, Born terms, $t$-channel $K^*(892)$ and $K_1(1270)$ mesons, $s$-channel nucleon resonances ($S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1895)$), and for model B, $u$-channel $\Lambda^*$ resonances ($S_{01}(1800)$ and $P_{01}(1810)$). The three background schemes (model A with soft hadronic form factors, model C with a strongly reduced $g_{K^+ \Lambda p}$ coupling, and model B with hyperon resonances) were compared against the longitudinal and transverse response functions and $\phi$-averaged cross sections from Jlab Hall C, Cornell, and Orsay data.

## Key numbers

- Model C uses $g_{K^+ \Lambda p}/\sqrt{4 \pi} \approx -0.4$, which is almost ten times smaller than the SU(3) flavor symmetry prediction.
- Model A adopts soft hadronic form factors with cutoff masses $\Lambda_h$ approaching the kaon mass.
- For $N^*$ and $\Lambda^*$ transition electromagnetic form factors, a dipole form with a universal cutoff mass of 0.84 GeV was used; for the $K_1$, a monopole form with cutoff mass of 0.6 GeV was used.
- The $N^*$ dipole cutoff mass was varied between 0.4 and 1.0 GeV to test sensitivity, with results found to be rather insensitive to those variations.
- The data comparisons are shown at $W$ = 1.84 GeV and $\langle W \rangle$ = 2.15 GeV for various $Q^2$ values.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadrodynamical analysis of $p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda$ photoproduction (Janssen, 2003) to the corresponding electroproduction process, where the additional $Q^2$ dependence of the response functions provides a discriminating tool that photoproduction alone could not offer. The results contradict the viability of soft hadronic form factor approaches (Haberzettl, 2000; Davidson, 2001) and strongly broken SU(3) coupling schemes (Hsiao, 2000) for describing kaon production, while supporting the hyperon resonance mechanism introduced by Janssen (2003) as a consistent description respecting SU(3) flavor symmetry. The findings enable future extraction of resonance parameters from combined photo- and electroproduction data with reduced background uncertainties, particularly as new data from Jlab become available.
