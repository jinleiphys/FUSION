# Multiple chiral doublets in four-$j$ shells particle rotor model: five possible chiral doublets in $^{136}_{~60}$Nd$_{76}$

> A particle rotor model, which couples nucleons in four single-$j$ shells to a triaxial rotor core, is developed to investigate the five pairs of nearly degenerate doublet bands recently reported in the even-even nucleus $^{136}$Nd. The experimental energy spectra and available $B(M1)/B(E2)$ values are successfully reproduced. The angular momentum geometries of the valence nucleons and the core support the chiral rotation interpretations not only for the previously reported chiral doublet, but also for the other four candidates. Hence, $^{136}$Nd is the first even-even candidate nucleus in which the multiple chiral doublets exist. Five pairs of chiral doublet bands in a single nucleus is also a new record in the study of nuclear chirality.

## Key claim

The paper claims that five pairs of nearly degenerate doublet bands in the even-even nucleus $^{136}$Nd can be interpreted as chiral doublets based on particle rotor model (PRM) calculations, but four of these pairs are considered only as "chiral candidates" because only one pair (D5/D5-C) has measured $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios to confirm chirality.

## Method

A particle rotor model coupling nucleons in four single-$j$ shells to a triaxial rotor core was developed, extending previous PRM versions with two or three single-$j$ shells. The unpaired nucleon configurations and quadrupole deformation parameters ($\beta$, $\gamma$) were obtained from constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations. The PRM Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a truncated basis space (reduced to ~5000-10000 dimensions) with a configuration energy cutoff, and the resulting wave functions were used to calculate energy spectra, $B(M1)/B(E2)$ transition probabilities, and angular momentum expectation values.

## Key numbers

- Energy splitting for D2: ~360 keV at $I=21\hbar$, decreasing to ~110 keV at $I=25\hbar$
- Energy splitting for D5/D5-C: ~410 keV at $I=18\hbar$, reducing to ~150 keV at higher spin
- Energy separations for D1, D3, and D4: approximately 70, 40, and 120 keV respectively, with little spin dependence
- Quadrupole deformation parameters for D1: $\beta=0.21$, $\gamma=21^\circ$; for D2: $\beta=0.22$, $\gamma=19^\circ$; for D5: $\beta=0.26$, $\gamma=23^\circ$
- Moments of inertia $\mathcal{J}_0$: 32.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV (D1), 35.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV (D2), 40.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV (D5)
- Basis space dimension for band D1: $864(2I+1)$, giving 18144 at $I=10\hbar$ and 35424 at $I=20\hbar$

## Context

This work extends previous PRM studies of chiral doublet bands in odd-odd nuclei (Frauendorf 1997) and odd-A nuclei (Qi 2009, Ayangeakaa 2013) by developing a four-single-$j$ shell version needed for even-even nuclei. It complements the tilted axis cranking CDFT calculations of Petrache (2018) that first reported the five doublet bands in $^{136}$Nd, but which could not describe energy splitting between chiral partners. The results suggest $^{136}$Nd as the first even-even candidate for multiple chiral doublets (M$\chi$D), a phenomenon previously predicted by CDFT (Meng 2006, Peng 2008) and observed in odd-odd nuclei. Further experimental measurements of $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios for the four candidate bands are needed for confirmation.
