# Sensitivity of electromagnetic spectra to equation of state and initial energy density in the Pb+Pb collisions at SPS

> We study Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c using a hydrodynamical approach. We test different equations of state (EoSs) and different initial conditions and show that there are more than one initial state for each EoS which reproduce the observed hadronic spectra. We also find that different equations of state favour different freeze-out temperature. Simultaneously we calculate the thermal dilepton and photon spectra for each EoS and initial state. We compare the dilepton mass spectrum to data measured by the CERES collaboration and find that the differences in spectra obtained using different EoSs and initial states are not resolvable within the current experimental resolution. However, at invariant masses over 2 GeV the difference in the yield due to various initial states is close to an order of magnitude. We also study the rapidity distribution of lepton pairs and find that for masses around 800 MeV the shape of the distribution depends strongly on the EoS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that multiple distinct initial conditions can reproduce the observed hadronic spectra for a given equation of state, and that different equations of state favor different freeze-out temperatures; however, the differences in thermal dilepton and photon spectra arising from these different equations of state and initial states are generally not resolvable within current experimental resolution, except at invariant masses over 2 GeV where the yield difference between initial states approaches an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors used a hydrodynamical model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c, testing three equations of state (EoS H with no phase transition, EoS D with T_c=200 MeV, and EoS A with T_c=165 MeV) and two different initial conditions (IS 1 with peaked energy distribution and IS 2 with wider energy distribution) for each EoS. They calculated thermal dilepton and photon spectra by folding emission rates from both the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases with the hydrodynamic flow, and compared the dilepton mass spectrum to CERES collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- Average initial energy densities in the central region: 10.2-10.3 GeV/fm^3 for IS 1 and 5.0-5.3 GeV/fm^3 for IS 2 across all EoSs
- Average initial temperatures at z=0: 255-258 MeV for IS 1 with EoS A/D, 234 MeV for IS 1 with EoS H; 213-220 MeV for IS 2 across all EoSs
- Phase transition temperatures: T_c=165 MeV for EoS A, T_c=200 MeV for EoS D
- Freeze-out energy densities: epsilon_f = 0.15 GeV/fm^3 giving T_f approx 140 MeV, and epsilon_f = 0.069 GeV/fm^3 giving T_f approx 120 MeV
- Inverse slope constant T for net proton p_T spectra at y=3.4: 318 MeV (EoS D, T_f approx 120 MeV), 275 MeV (EoS D, T_f approx 140 MeV), 277 MeV (EoS A, T_f approx 120 MeV), 243 MeV (EoS A, T_f approx 140 MeV)
- Photon yield differences between EoSs for IS 1: EoS D increases yield by factor 1.2-1.8, EoS H by factor 1.6-4 compared to EoS A
- At M=2 GeV, initial state IS 1 produces 20% more electron pairs than IS 2
- For 3 GeV electron pairs, yield differs by factor 3.5 between IS 1 and IS 2 when using EoS D

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamical studies of electromagnetic probes (Dumitru 1993, Srivastava 1994, Shuryak 1994, Arbex 1995, Neumann 1995, Tarasov 1996, Srivastava 1996, Steele 1996, Chaudhuri 1997, Hirano 1997, Cleymans 1997, Hung 1997, Sollfrank 1997, Sarkar 1998, Huovinen 1998) which were mostly performed for S+Au collisions, by concentrating on Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies. The results enable future work to use electromagnetic spectra as discriminators between different hydrodynamic scenarios, particularly through the rapidity distribution of dileptons around 800 MeV which shows strong dependence on the equation of state, and through high-mass dilepton production which can differentiate between initial conditions.
