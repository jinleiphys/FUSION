# Multiparticle-hole excitations in nuclei near N = Z = 20: $^{41}$K

> This experimental study of high-spin structure near N = Z = 20 nuclei was focused on $^{41}$K, but will also mention three newly observed $γ$ transitions in $^{41}$Ca observed in the same reaction. High-spin states were populated using the $^{26}$Mg($^{18}$O, $p2nγ$)$^{41}$K and $^{26}$Mg($^{18}$O, $3nγ$)$^{41}$Ca reactions. The experiment was carried out at an incident beam energy of 50 MeV at the Florida State University (FSU) John D. Fox Superconducting Linear Accelerator Laboratory and used the FSU high-purity germanium detector array. The $^{41}$K level scheme was extended to 12325 keV, possibly with J$^π$ = 25/2$^-$ or 27/2$^+$, by means of 25 new transitions and that of $^{41}$Ca to 9916 keV. Linear polarization and a measure of angular distribution results are also reported and used to provide information on the spins and parities of several states in the $^{41}$K decay scheme. The results have been compared to the $spsdpf$ cross-shell FSU shell model interaction calculations. The theoretical results from configurations involving no or one additional nucleon promoted from the $sd$ to the $fp$ shell agree relatively well with the energies of known states, while those that involve multi-particle excitations paint an interesting and complex picture of interplay between single-particle excitations, collective pairing, and deformation. This presents an interesting challenge for future theory.

## Key claim

The high-spin level scheme of $^{41}$K was extended to 12325 keV with 25 new transitions, but the spin-parity of the highest observed state is ambiguous (possibly $J^\pi = 25/2^-$ or $27/2^+$), and the theoretical FSU shell model calculations agree well only for configurations involving zero or one nucleon promoted across the $sd$-$fp$ shell gap, while multi-particle excitations present a more complex picture that challenges current theory.

## Method

High-spin states in $^{41}$K and $^{41}$Ca were populated via the $^{26}$Mg($^{18}$O, $p2n\gamma$) and $^{26}$Mg($^{18}$O, $3n\gamma$) fusion-evaporation reactions at a beam energy of 50 MeV, using the FSU high-purity germanium detector array with six HPGe clover detectors and two single-crystal HPGe detectors. Gamma-gamma coincidence data, linear polarization measurements from Compton scattering in clover detectors, and angular distribution ratios from detectors at 135$^\circ$, 45$^\circ$, and 90$^\circ$ were used to assign spins and parities to newly observed states. The experimental results were compared to the $spsdpf$ cross-shell FSU shell model interaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- The $^{41}$K level scheme was extended to 12325 keV, with the highest state possibly having $J^\pi = 25/2^-$ or $27/2^+$.
- 25 new $\gamma$ transitions and 21 new states were added to the $^{41}$K decay scheme.
- The $^{41}$Ca level scheme was extended to 9916 keV with three newly observed states and transitions.
- The 7348 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = 21/2^-$ based on the 2365 keV transition (M1/E2, mixing ratio $\delta \approx -0.8$).
- The 8460 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = 23/2^+$ based on the 1111 keV transition (pure E1 character).
- The 7758 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = 23/2^-$ based on the 2774 keV transition (nearly pure E2, $\delta \approx -0.03$).
- The 7841 keV state in $^{41}$K was tentatively assigned $J^\pi = (21/2^+)$.
- The 6073 keV state in $^{41}$K was tentatively assigned $J^\pi = (17/2^+)$.
- The 5934 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = 17/2^-$ or $21/2^-$ based on the 949 keV transition (M1/E2, $\delta \approx +0.21$ or $-0.27$).
- The 7200 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = 17/2^-$ or $21/2^-$ based on the 2216 keV transition (M1/E2, $\delta \approx -0.34$ or $+0.25$).
- The 6478 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = (17/2^+)$ based on the 2580 keV transition (mixed M1/E2).
- The 5224 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J = 15/2$ (no parity) based on the 2450 keV transition ($\delta \approx -0.11$).
- The 5459 keV state in $^{41}$K was assigned $J^\pi = (15/2^+)$ based on the 2683 keV transition (M1/E2).

## Context

This work extends previous high-spin studies of $^{41}$K by BNL (which identified states up to about 3.9 MeV with $15/2^+$ and about 5 MeV with possible $19/2$) and Utrecht (which confirmed spin and parity assignments using a Ge(Li)-NaI(Tl) Compton suppression spectrometer). For $^{41}$Ca, the most recent prior study used the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) with a $^{27}$Al($^{16}$O,$pn$) reaction at 34 MeV, extending the decay scheme to about 9 MeV. The present results provide new experimental data on multi-particle-hole excitations across the $N=Z=20$ shell gap, enabling future theoretical work to refine cross-shell interactions like the FSU $spsdpf$ interaction, which has been successful for $^{39}$Ar (PhysRevC.100.014310, 2019) and $^{38}$Cl (Lubna et al., 2019) but faces challenges in describing the complex interplay of single-particle excitations, collective pairing, and deformation in $^{41}$K.
