# Supplemental note for "Two-fermion emission from spin-singlet and triplet resonances in one dimension"

> Time-dependent calculation has been a suitable method to investigate the quantum dynamical processes. In Ref. [1] = [T. Oishi et al., J. of Phys. G 45, 105101 (2018)], we applied this method to the one-dimensional two-fermion tunneling in the nuclear-physics scale. Beside the specific results presented therein, some basic formalism and methods, which can be helpful for further discussions and developments to investigate time-dependent quantum systems, have been awaiting our description. This note is devoted to describe those supplemental contents. We do not limit the story to the nuclear physics, but keep it applicable to other scales and/or targets.

## Key claim

This supplemental note provides the formal time-dependent quantum-mechanical framework underlying the specific two-fermion tunneling results reported in Oishi et al. (2018), but the formalism is deliberately kept general and not limited to nuclear-physics scales or to the specific Hamiltonian used in that prior work.

## Method

The paper derives the time evolution of a meta-stable state by expanding it in the continuum eigenstates of a static Hermitian Hamiltonian, using the Krylov-Fock theorem to relate the survival probability to the Fourier transform of the energy spectrum. It then shows analytically that a Breit-Wigner (Cauchy-Lorentz) energy spectrum yields the exponential-decay rule, and extends this to multi-resonance cases via convolution of Lorentzians. Practical issues of energy cutoff, continuum discretization via a radial box, and the quantum recurrence theorem for discretized spectra are discussed, along with an appendix on handling non-static or state-dependent Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

* The Breit-Wigner spectrum is defined by center \(E_0\) and full width at half maximum \(\Gamma_0\); the resulting lifetime is \(\tau = \hbar / \Gamma_0\).
* For the numerical calculations in Oishi et al. (2018), the number of basis states was typically \(N_{\rm max} = 100\)–\(1000\).
* The survival probability for a two-component mixture state oscillates with frequency \(\Delta_{12} = (E_1 - E_2)/\hbar\), returning to unity at times \(t = 2N\pi / \Delta_{12}\).

## Context

This note extends the time-dependent computational method used in Oishi et al. (2018) for one-dimensional two-fermion tunneling, and in earlier works by the same group (Oishi 2014, 2017; Naru 2012) for nuclear meta-stable phenomena such as two-nucleon emission. The formalism is built on the Krylov-Fock theorem (Krylov 1947; Kuku 1989) and the confining-potential procedure for initial-state preparation (Serot 1994; Talou 1998, 2000; Gurv 2004). It enables future applications to time-dependent quantum systems across different scales, including cases with non-static Hamiltonians, and clarifies the conditions under which exponential decay is a good approximation versus when deviations occur (Winter 1961; Wilk 1997; Muga 2006; Campo 2011).
