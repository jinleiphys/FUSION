# Flavor Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea and the Five-Quark Components of the Nucleons

> The existence of the five-quark Fock states for the intrinsic charm quark in the nucleons was suggested some time ago, but conclusive evidence is still lacking. We generalize the previous theoretical approach to the light-quark sector and study possible experimental signatures for such five-quark states. In particular, we compare the $\bar d - \bar u$ and $\bar u + \bar d - s -\bar s$ data with the calculations based on the five-quark Fock states. The qualitative agreement between the data and the calculations is interpreted as evidence for the existence of the intrinsic light-quark sea in the nucleons. The probabilities for the $|uudu\bar{u}>$ and $|uudd\bar{d}>$ Fock states are also extracted.

## Key claim

The qualitative agreement between the measured $\bar d - \bar u$ and $\bar u + \bar d - s - \bar s$ distributions and calculations based on the BHPS five-quark Fock state model, after accounting for QCD evolution from a low scale, is interpreted as evidence for the existence of intrinsic light-quark sea in the nucleons, though the probabilities for the $|uudu\bar{u}\rangle$ and $|uudd\bar{d}\rangle$ states depend on the assumed probability for the strange five-quark component.

## Method

The authors generalized the Brodsky-Hoyer-Peterson-Sakai (BHPS) model for intrinsic charm five-quark Fock states to the light-quark sector, calculating the $x$ distributions of $\bar u$, $\bar d$, and $\bar s$ quarks in the $|uudQ\bar{Q}\rangle$ configurations using both the analytical heavy-quark limit and a Monte-Carlo algorithm solving the full probability distribution. They compared these predictions to the $\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)$ data from the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan experiment at $Q^2 = 54$ GeV$^2$ and to the $x(\bar u + \bar d - s - \bar s)$ data extracted from HERMES semi-inclusive DIS measurements at $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$, applying leading-order QCD evolution from a low starting scale $\mu$ to the experimental $Q^2$ values.

## Key numbers

- The integral of $\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)$ from the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan experiment is $0.118 \pm 0.012$ at $Q^2 = 54$ GeV$^2$.
- Using $\mu = 0.5$ GeV for evolution, the sum of probabilities for the $|uudu\bar{u}\rangle$ and $|uudd\bar{d}\rangle$ Fock states is $\Sigma {\cal P}^{\bar d \bar u}_5 = 0.471$, yielding ${\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.176$ and ${\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.294$ under the $1/m_Q^2$ assumption for the strange sea.
- In the limiting case of zero strange intrinsic sea (${\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0$), the extracted probabilities become ${\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.097$ and ${\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.215$.
- The ratio of strange to non-strange intrinsic sea probabilities is taken as $m_{\bar u}^2 / m_{\bar s}^2 \approx 0.36$, using $m_{\bar u} = 0.3$ GeV/c$^2$ and $m_{\bar s} = 0.5$ GeV/c$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the BHPS model (Brodsky, Hoyer, Peterson, and Sakai, 1980) for intrinsic charm to the light-quark sector, addressing the long-standing puzzle of the nucleon sea flavor asymmetry first indicated by the violation of the Gottfried Sum Rule (Gottfried, 1967; Amaudruz et al., 1991). The results contradict the expectation that perturbative QCD alone can explain the sea, and they complement the meson-cloud model (MCM) approach to flavor asymmetry. The extracted five-quark probabilities enable future searches for intrinsic strange and charm sea components and can guide extensions of the BHPS model to hyperons and mesons.
