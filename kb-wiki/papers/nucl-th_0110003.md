# Tensor representation of the nucleon-nucleon amplitude

> Many approaches to nucleon-nucleus elastic and inelastic scattering are based on the use of the free-space nucleon-nucleon transition amplitude. In calculations where the full spin-dependence of this amplitude is needed, its use is more tractable when it is expressed in terms of irreducible tensor operators of the spins of the interacting nucleons. We present general formulae for this representation which is particularly useful for inelastic scattering studies involving spin-flip transitions of a target nucleon.

# Tensor representation of the nucleon-nucleon amplitude

## Key claim
The paper presents a general method to express the free-space nucleon-nucleon (NN) transition amplitude as a linear combination of irreducible tensor operators of the spins of the two interacting nucleons, which is claimed to be more tractable than the standard Wolfenstein parametrization for applications requiring full spin dependence, particularly in inelastic scattering studies involving spin-flip transitions of a target nucleon. The authors note that the method is especially useful when the full spin dependence of the NN amplitude is needed, but they do not demonstrate that it yields improved numerical results over existing approaches.

## Method
The authors decompose the NN scattering amplitude into spin singlet (S=0) and triplet (S=1) components using partial wave transition amplitudes M^{JS}_{L'L}(K',K) obtained from realistic NN interaction models such as the Paris potential. They then re-express these components in terms of irreducible tensor operators of the projectile and struck nucleon spins, using 9-j coefficients to transform from the total spin S representation to the individual nucleon spin operators. The resulting tensor amplitudes M^{(ab)}_{kappa q} are evaluated from the angular momentum amplitudes using the NNAMP numerical program, with calculations performed at E_lab = 135 MeV using the Paris potential with a maximum of six partial waves.

## Key numbers
- Calculations performed at E_lab = 135 MeV using the Paris potential
- The tensor amplitudes are evaluated at momentum transfer q = 1 fm^{-1} for the phi-dependence study
- The on-shell condition satisfies q^2/4 + Q^2 = 2 mu omega / hbar^2, with phi = pi/2 on the energy shell
- The non-vanishing amplitudes identified are: M^{(00)}_{00}, M^{(01)}_{11}=M^{(10)}_{11}, M^{(11)}_{00}, M^{(11)}_{20}, M^{(11)}_{21}, and M^{(11)}_{22}
- The relation between tensor and Wolfenstein amplitudes is given by M^{(00)}_{00}=A and M^{(01)}_{11}=M^{(10)}_{11}=-iC/sqrt(2)

## Context
This work extends the standard Wolfenstein parametrization (Wolfenstein and Ashkin, 1952) of the NN amplitude, which uses scalar products of Pauli spin vectors with unit vectors defined by the scattering plane. The tensor representation is designed for use in multiple scattering formalisms such as the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) approach (Kerman, McManus, and Thaler, 1959) and few-body frameworks (Crespo and Johnson, 1999), where second and higher order terms require all spin components. The method contrasts with the Franey-Love representation (Love and Franey, 1981) which uses central, spin-orbit, and tensor components with Yukawa form factors but violates unitarity (Macfarlane and Redish, 1988). The work enables more tractable calculations of inelastic scattering and elastic scattering from non-spin-zero targets where the full spin dependence of the NN interaction must be treated.
