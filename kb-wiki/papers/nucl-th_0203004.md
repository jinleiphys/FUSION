# Can hadronic rescattering explain the large elliptic flow and small HBT radii seen at RHIC?

> Results from the data obtained in the first physics run of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have shown suprisingly large elliptic flow and suprisingly small HBT radii. Attempts to explain both results in a consistant picture have so far been unsuccessful. The present work shows that a thermal model + hadronic rescattering calculation can explain both elliptic flow and HBT results from RHIC. The calculation requires a very early hadronization time of about 1 fm/c after the initial collision of the nuclei.

## Key claim

A thermal model combined with hadronic rescattering can simultaneously explain both the large elliptic flow and small HBT radii observed at RHIC, but only if hadronization occurs very early, at about 1 fm/c after the initial nuclear collision, and if hadronic-like objects can exist during the high-energy-density phase of the collision.

## Method

The author performed semi-classical Monte Carlo simulations of hadronic rescattering in three stages: initialization and hadronization, rescattering and freeze-out, and calculation of experimental observables. Initial hadron momenta were sampled from a thermal transverse momentum distribution with temperature T=300 MeV and a Gaussian rapidity distribution, while initial space-time positions were determined from a hadronization proper time τhad=1 fm/c using relativistic equations. The rescattering stage evolved particles in time steps of 0.1 fm/c using isospin-averaged s-wave and p-wave cross sections for meson scattering from Prakash et al. (1993), running to 100 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization proper time τhad = 1 fm/c required to match both elliptic flow and HBT results
- Initial temperature T = 300 MeV used in the thermal transverse momentum distribution
- Maximum hadron number density at mid-rapidity at t=0 fm/c: 6.8 fm⁻³, dropping to about 1 fm⁻³ at t=4 fm/c
- Maximum energy density ρ > 1 GeV/fm³ during the early phase, reaching 8 GeV/fm³
- Extracted slope parameters from mT distributions: pions ~190 MeV, kaons ~300 MeV, nucleons ~565 MeV, close to preliminary STAR measurements of 190±10, 300±30, and 565±50 MeV respectively
- Rescattering calculation for b=8 fm produced v2 values in reasonable agreement with STAR measurements for pions and nucleons (STAR errors ≤0.002 for pions, 0.006 for protons+antiprotons)
- Effective pion volume estimated as 0.065 fm³ from π-π scattering cross section of about 0.8 fm²

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle that hydrodynamical models (Kolb 1999) could explain the large elliptic flow but disagreed with HBT radii, while relativistic quantum molecular dynamics calculations such as RQMD v2.4 (Sorge 1989) underpredicted elliptic flow but gave HBT radii comparable to data. A previous hydrodynamical model with hadronic afterburner (Soff 2000) produced HBT radii significantly larger than measurements. The present calculation extends the author's earlier SPS-energy rescattering calculations (Humanic 1998) to RHIC energies and shows that the same τhad=1 fm/c required at SPS also works at RHIC. The results suggest a modified collision scenario where the Color Glass Condensate (McLerran 2002, Kovner 1995) might be "shattered" directly into hadronic fragments rather than melting into quarks and gluons.
