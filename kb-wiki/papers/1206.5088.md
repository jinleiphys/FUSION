# Lattice QCD approach to Nuclear Physics

> We review recent progress of the HAL QCD method which was recently proposed to investigate hadron interactions in lattice QCD. The strategy to extract the energy-independent non-local potential in lattice QCD is explained in detail. The method is applied to study nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-hyperon, hyperon-hyperon and meson-baryon interactions. Several extensions of the method are also discussed.

## Key claim

The HAL QCD method defines an energy-independent non-local potential from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function in lattice QCD, which can then be used to calculate scattering phase shifts and bound states; the potential is scheme-dependent (depending on the choice of interpolating baryon operator) and is not unique, but its velocity expansion is expected to converge well at low energies.

## Method

The equal-time NBS wave function for two baryons is extracted from a four-point correlation function on the lattice, using wall sources with fixed quantum numbers (total spin, isospin, parity, and cubic-group representation). A non-local potential \(U(\bx,\by)\) is defined by requiring that the NBS wave function satisfies a Schrödinger-type equation \((E_k - H_0)\varphi^W = \int U \varphi^W\); this potential is shown to be energy-independent by construction for energies below the inelastic threshold. In practice, the non-local potential is expanded in a velocity (derivative) expansion, and the leading-order local potentials (central, spin-spin, tensor) are extracted from the wave function at a single energy.

## Key numbers

* The long-range part of the \(NN\) force is dominated by one-pion exchange for \(r > 2\) fm.
* The medium-range part (1 fm \(< r <\) 2 fm) receives contributions from two-pion exchange and/or heavy meson (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\sigma\)) exchanges, with a spin-isospin independent attraction of about 50–100 MeV.
* The short-range part (\(r < 1\) fm) is described by a phenomenological repulsive core.
* The spin-singlet central potential \(V_C(r)^{(S,I)=(0,1)}\) is obtained from the \((J^P,I)=(A_1^+,1)\) state as \(V_C = (E_k - H_0)\varphi^W / \varphi^W\).
* The spin-triplet central potential \(V_C(r)^{(1,0)}\) and tensor potential \(V_T(r)\) are extracted from the \((J^P,I)=(T_1^+,0)\) state using projection onto \(A_1\) and non-\(A_1\) components.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume method of Luscher (1990) for extracting scattering phase shifts from lattice QCD, but instead of using only the asymptotic region of the NBS wave function, the HAL QCD method defines a potential from the internal region. It enables direct calculation of nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-hyperon, hyperon-hyperon, and meson-baryon interactions from first-principles QCD, and can be generalized to many-body forces and inelastic scattering. The approach contrasts with phenomenological high-precision \(NN\) potentials (e.g., Bonn, Reid93, Argonne \(v_{18}\)) that are fitted to scattering data, and with the Luscher method which requires extracting multiple energy levels in a finite box.
