# Real-Time Kadanoff-Baym Approach to Nuclear Response Functions

> Linear response functions are calculated for symmetric nuclear matter of normal density by time-evolving two-time Green's functions with conserving self-energy insertions, thereby satisfying the energy-sum rule. Nucleons are regarded as moving in a mean field defined by an effective mass. A two-body effective (or residual) interaction, represented by a gaussian local interaction, is used to find the effect of correlations in a second order as well as a ring approximation. The response function S(e,q) is calculated for 0.2<q<1.2 fm^{-1}. Comparison is made with the nucleons being un-correlated, "RPA+HF" only.

# Real-Time Kadanoff-Baym Approach to Nuclear Response Functions

## Key claim
The real-time Kadanoff-Baym (two-time Green's function) method can be applied to calculate nuclear response functions in symmetric nuclear matter, automatically satisfying the energy-weighted sum rule through conserving self-energy approximations, without requiring separate vertex corrections. The method is illustrated for a simplified interaction model, and the results show no major differences between collisionless (HF+RPA), second-order, and full ring-diagram approximations for the chosen parameter range.

## Method
The authors time-evolve two-time Kadanoff-Baym equations for symmetric nuclear matter at normal density, starting from an uncorrelated Fermi distribution in a mean field defined by an effective mass m* = 0.7m. A local Gaussian residual interaction (η = 0.57 fm, V0 = -453 MeV) is switched on at t=0, and the system evolves until fully correlated at t = 10 fm/c. An external Gaussian perturbation (width 3 fm/c) is then applied, and the time-dependent density fluctuation δn(q,t) is recorded, Fourier-transformed to ω-space, and used to compute the response function S(ω,q) via the imaginary part of the lesser Green's function G<10. Three cases are compared: (I) mean field only (HF+RPA), (II) second-order self-energy insertions, and (III) ring diagrams to all orders in the self-energy.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter density: ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (normal density)
- External momentum transfer range: 0.2 < q < 1.2 fm⁻¹
- Effective mass: m* = 0.7 m
- Gaussian interaction parameters: range η = 0.57 fm, strength V0 = -453 MeV
- Initial temperature: T = 0 (zero temperature) in most calculations; some cases use T = 20 MeV
- Correlation time to reach equilibrium: t = 10 fm/c
- External perturbation width: 3 fm/c
- For q = 0.2 fm⁻¹ at T = 0 in HF+RPA: a sharp resonance is observed with a sharp cut-off on the high ω side
- Final equilibrium temperature after correlations: estimated to be 20 MeV when starting from T = 0

## Context
This work extends the real-time Kadanoff-Baym method previously applied to the electron gas by Kwong and Bonitz (2000) to nuclear matter response calculations. It contrasts with the traditional Bethe-Salpeter equation approach used by Bozek (2004, 2005) for nuclear matter response, which requires explicit construction of a consistent interaction kernel to satisfy sum rules. The authors note that the two-time method avoids numerical difficulties at zero temperature and small q that arise in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. The results enable future inclusion of spin-isospin dependence, tensor forces, pairing (as studied by Sedrakian, 2010, and others), and extension to neutron matter for astrophysical applications.
