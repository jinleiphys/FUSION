# Effects of clustered nuclear geometry on the anisotropic flow in O-O collisions at the LHC within a multiphase transport model framework

> To understand the true origin of flowlike signatures and applicability of hydrodynamics in small collision systems, effects of soft QCD dynamics, the sensitivity of jetlike correlations, and nonequilibrium effects, efforts are being made to perform \textit{p}-O and O-O collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies. It is equally interesting to look into the possible signatures of an $α$-clustered nuclear geometry in $^{16}$O-$^{16}$O collisions by studying the initial-state effects on the final-state observables. In this work, within a multiphase transport model, we implement an $α$-cluster tetrahedral density profile in the oxygen nucleus along with the default Woods-Saxon density profile. We study the eccentricity ($ε_2$), triangularity ($ε_3$), normalized symmetric cumulants [NSC(2,3)], elliptic flow ($v_2$), and triangular flow ($v_3$) in $^{16}$O-$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7~$TeV. The constituent quark number scaling of the elliptic flow is also reported. For the most central collisions, enhanced effects in $\langle ε_3 \rangle/ \langle ε_2 \rangle$ and $\langle v_3 \rangle/ \langle v_2 \rangle$ with a negative value of NSC(2,3), and an away-side broadening in the two-particle azimuthal correlation function [$C(Δϕ)$] of the identified particles are observed in the presence of an $α$-clustered geometry.

## Key claim
In O-O collisions at √sNN = 7 TeV simulated with the AMPT string melting model, an α-cluster tetrahedral nuclear density profile produces enhanced triangular flow relative to elliptic flow and a negative normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(2,3) in the most central collisions, which the authors propose as a possible signature of α-clustered structure in oxygen nuclei, but only if the medium exhibits hydrodynamic behavior.

## Method
The authors implemented an α-cluster tetrahedral density profile for ¹⁶O nuclei, with ⁴He nuclei placed at the vertices of a regular tetrahedron (side length 3.42 fm), alongside the default Woods-Saxon density profile, within the AMPT (a multiphase transport model) string melting mode (version 2.26t9b). They generated O-O collisions at √sNN = 7 TeV and computed eccentricity (ε₂), triangularity (ε₃), normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(2,3), and anisotropic flow coefficients v₂ and v₃ using the two-particle correlation method with a pseudorapidity gap of 1.0 < |Δη| < 4.8 and kinematic cuts |η| < 2.5, pT > 0.4 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The α-cluster tetrahedral structure has a tetrahedron side length of 3.42 fm, giving an rms radius for ¹⁶O of 2.699 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for oxygen are r₀ = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051; for the ⁴He clusters, r₀ = 0.964 fm, a = 0.322 fm, w = 0.517, yielding an rms radius of 1.676 fm for ⁴He.
- For the most central (0–5%) collisions, the α-cluster case shows a higher ⟨ε₃⟩/⟨ε₂⟩ ratio and a higher ⟨v₃⟩/⟨v₂⟩ ratio compared to the Woods-Saxon profile.
- NSC(2,3) is negative for the α-cluster case up to mid-central (20–30%) collisions, while it is positive for the Woods-Saxon profile at all centralities.
- The α-cluster structure yields larger ⟨v₂⟩/⟨ε₂⟩ and ⟨v₃⟩/⟨ε₃⟩ ratios than the Woods-Saxon profile across all centralities.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of α-clustered nuclear structure in ¹⁶O collisions using Glauber Monte Carlo (Rybczynski 2019), hydrodynamic models (Lim 2018, Schenke 2020), and AMPT simulations (Li 2020, Ding 2023) by systematically comparing initial-state eccentricities and final-state flow harmonics for both Woods-Saxon and α-cluster density profiles. The results suggest that future experimental O-O runs at the LHC and RHIC (proposed by Brewer 2021 and Katz 2019) could test whether enhanced ⟨v₃⟩/⟨v₂⟩ and negative NSC(2,3) in central collisions serve as signatures of α-clustering, while also probing the origin of collectivity in small systems and the validity of number-of-constituent-quark scaling at LHC energies.
