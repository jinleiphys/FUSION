# Quantum Mechanics Lecture Notes. Selected Chapters

> These are extended lecture notes of the quantum mechanics course which I am teaching in the Weizmann Institute of Science graduate physics program. They cover the topics listed below. The first four chapter are posted here. Their content is detailed on the next page. The other chapters are planned to be added in the coming months. 1. Motion in External Electromagnetic Field. Gauge Fields in Quantum Mechanics. 2. Quantum Mechanics of Electromagnetic Field 3. Photon-Matter Interactions 4. Quantization of the Schrödinger Field (The Second Quantization) 5. Open Systems. Density Matrix 6. Adiabatic Theory. The Berry Phase. The Born-Oppenheimer Approximation 7. Mean Field Approaches for Many Body Systems -- Fermions and Bosons

## Key claim

These are extended lecture notes from a graduate quantum mechanics course at the Weizmann Institute of Science, covering selected topics with an emphasis on gauge fields and their role in quantum mechanics; the author notes that the notes are incomplete, with only the first four chapters posted and additional chapters planned for future release.

## Method

The notes follow a pedagogical approach, beginning with the classical Lagrangian and Hamiltonian for a charged particle in an external electromagnetic field, then applying canonical quantization to obtain the Schrodinger equation with minimal coupling. The author derives the gauge transformation properties of wave functions and potentials, introduces the spin magnetic moment term via the gyromagnetic factor, and obtains the electric current density including both orbital and spin contributions. Specific solutions are presented for motion in a uniform electric field using the Airy function.

## Key numbers

- The gyromagnetic factor \(g\) is introduced as a dimensionless coefficient in the relation \(\vmu_{op} = g\;\frac{q}{2m}\; \vs_{op}\) for the spin magnetic moment operator
- The Airy function is defined as \(Ai(\xi) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} du \exp \left[i\left(\frac{u^3}{3} + \xi u\right)\right]\)
- The parameter \(\alpha = (2mF/\hbar^2)^{1/3}\) appears in the wave function solution for a uniform electric field, where \(F = q|\vE|\)

## Context

These lecture notes extend the standard treatment of quantum mechanics in electromagnetic fields found in textbooks such as Griffiths (Chapter 10) and provide a foundation for understanding gauge field theories. The discussion of the gauge principle as a heuristic for deriving interactions connects to the broader framework of non-abelian gauge theories and general relativity, with references to Che (Section 8.1) and Ryder (Chapter 3) for relativistic treatments. The notes enable further study of topics including the Berry phase, the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and mean-field approaches for many-body systems.
