# Exploring the nuclear pion dispersion relation through the anomalous coupling of photon to photon and neutral pion

> We investigate the possibility of measuring the pion dispersion relation in nuclear matter through the anomalous coupling in the reaction γ- γ' π_0. It is shown that this reaction permits the study of pionic modes for space-like momenta. If the pion is softened in nuclear matter due to mixing with the delta-hole state, significant strength for this reaction is expected to move into the space-like region. Competing background processes are evaluated, and it is concluded that useful insight can be obtained experimentally, but only through a difficult exclusive measurement.

## Key claim

The reaction γ → γ′ + π0, which is kinematically forbidden in free space, becomes allowed in nuclear matter because in-medium corrections can lower the pionic branch into the space-like region (|kπ| > ωπ); however, a gauge-invariance constraint forces the matrix element to zero at the space-like-to-time-like boundary (kπ2 = 0), making the cross sections small and requiring a difficult exclusive measurement to extract useful information.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for neutral pion production through the anomalous γγπ0 coupling in nuclear matter, using a Lorentzian form to incorporate a finite width for the pionic state. They then estimate two background processes: ordinary Compton scattering through an intermediate Δ resonance, and reactions producing a Δ-hole final state that decays into πN, using Rarita-Schwinger propagators and minimal substitution for electromagnetic couplings. Cross sections are evaluated for a lead nucleus with incoming photon energies between 400 and 600 MeV, and the feasibility of isolating the signal via polarization measurements and exclusive detection of the final π0 is assessed.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous coupling Lagrangian uses a pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV and the fine structure constant α.
- The momentum range where delta-hole mixing is strongest is near |kπ| = 300 MeV/c.
- For a 5 mm lead target, approximately 10^14 photons would be needed to investigate the peak region, requiring 100,000 final-state photons with pion momenta between 225 and 275 MeV and pionic energies within 75 MeV of the momentum.
- The pion width in the medium is estimated to be in the 10–30 MeV range for momenta below 300 MeV/c, corresponding to a mean free path of 2–5 fm.
- The first excited state of lead is at 2.6 MeV, requiring photon measurements to better than 1.0 MeV to eliminate excited states.
- The coupling constant G1 = 2.63 is used for the γNΔ vertex, determined from experiments.
- The coupling strengths assumed for the pion self energy are gπΔN = 2.0 and g′ = 0.8.

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts on in-medium pion dispersion relations, which were extensively reviewed by Migdal (1978) and later treated with self-consistent calculations by Xia, Siemens and Soyeur (1994) and Korpa and Malfliet (1995). The proposed photon-based probe addresses the limitations of previous hadronic charge-exchange measurements by Dmitriev (1994), where the hadronic probe must traverse the nuclear surface, and of heavy-ion collision studies that proved difficult to interpret. The paper enables a potential experimental path to directly measure the pion dispersion relation in nuclear matter, with implications for understanding pion condensation in neutron stars and chiral symmetry restoration.
