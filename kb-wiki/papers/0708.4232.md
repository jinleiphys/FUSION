# Heavy quark diffusion in perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order

> We compute the momentum diffusion coefficient of a nonrelativistic heavy quark in a hot QCD plasma, to next-to-leading order in the weak coupling expansion. Corrections arise at O(g); physically they represent interference between overlapping scatterings, as well as soft, electric scale ($p\sim gT$) gauge field physics, which we treat using the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory. In 3-color, 3-flavor QCD, the momentum diffusion constant of a fundamental representation heavy quark at NLO is $κ= \frac{16π}{3} α_s^2 T^3 (\ln \frac{1}{g} + 0.07428 + 1.8869 g)$. The convergence of the weak coupling expansion is poor.

## Key claim

The momentum diffusion coefficient of a nonrelativistic heavy quark in hot QCD plasma receives next-to-leading order corrections at O(g), which are positive and large; for realistic values of the strong coupling the correction is a factor of 2 already at α_s = 0.03, indicating that the weak coupling expansion converges poorly for dynamical quantities.

## Method

The authors compute the momentum diffusion coefficient κ from the time-integrated correlator of two electric field operators connected by fundamental Wilson lines along the static heavy quark trajectory, evaluated in the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. They treat soft (p ∼ gT) gauge field physics using the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory extended to the closed time path, working in strict Coulomb gauge. The NLO calculation involves diagrams with HTL-resummed propagators and vertices for soft momenta, including self-energy corrections, vertex corrections, and contributions from overlapping scatterings.

## Key numbers

- For 3-color, 3-flavor QCD, the NLO momentum diffusion constant is κ = (16π/3) α_s² T³ (ln(1/g) + 0.07428 + 1.8869 g)
- The NLO coefficient C in the expression κ = (C_F g⁴ T³)/(18π) [(N_c + N_f/2)(ln(2T/m_D) + ξ) + (N_f ln 2)/2 + (N_c m_D/T) C] is C = 1.4946 + C_{Eq.(locorrelator)}
- Individual diagram contributions to C: C_{re (A)} = 0.77199, C_{(C)} = -0.132916(1), C_{(D)} = 0.067526(1), C_{im (A)} = 0.9097(1), C_{re (B)} = -0.04829(1), C_{im (B)} = -0.07338(1)
- The leading-order result (strict LO) gives κ ≈ (C_F g⁴ T³)/(18π) [N_c (ln(2T/m_D) + ξ) + (N_f/2)(ln(4T/m_D) + ξ)] with ξ ≈ -0.64718

## Context

This work presents the first next-to-leading order calculation of any dynamical transport quantity in QCD involving large length or time scales, extending the leading-order calculation of Braaten and Thoma (1991) and Moore and Teaney (2005). The result enables comparison with experimental observations of heavy quark elliptic flow and energy loss at RHIC and LHC, and provides a benchmark for similar NLO calculations of other transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electric conductivity, and photon production. The authors note it would be interesting to extend the calculation to N=4 SYM theory.
