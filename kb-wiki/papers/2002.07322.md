# Critical fluctuations in a dynamically expanding heavy-ion collision

> For the discovery of the QCD critical point it is crucial to develop dynamical models of the fluctuations of the net-baryon number that can be embedded in simulations of heavy-ion collisions. In this proceeding, we study the dynamical formation of the critical fluctuations of the net-baryon number near the QCD critical point and their survival in the late stages in an expanding system. The stochastic diffusion equation with a non-linear free energy functional is employed for describing the evolution of conserved-charge fluctuations along trajectories in the crossover and first-order transition regions near the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-equilibrium dynamics in an expanding heavy-ion collision system can produce critical fluctuations of the net-baryon number that survive into the late stages, but only when non-linear terms are included in the free energy functional; the Gaussian approximation fails to capture the non-Gaussian cumulants that are essential for critical point searches.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the stochastic diffusion equation in Milne coordinates (space-time rapidity and proper time) with a non-linear free energy functional expanded to sixth order in the density fluctuation, using parameters mapped from the 3D Ising model universality class onto the QCD phase diagram. They evolve net-baryon number fluctuations along four trajectories in the crossover region near a critical point located at T=150 MeV and μB=390 MeV, and separately along a trajectory crossing a first-order phase transition modeled by a double-well free energy functional.

## Key numbers

- Critical point location: T = 150 MeV and μB = 390 MeV
- Initial proper time τ0 = 1 fm and initial temperature T0 = 200 MeV
- For the trajectory closest to the critical point, the second-order cumulant ⟨nB²⟩c peaks at τ − τ0 ≈ 1.5 fm, while the medium passes through the transition line at τ − τ0 ≈ 1.37 fm, showing a retardation effect of about 0.13 fm
- In the first-order transition simulation, the correlation function C(Δy, τ) shows a local maximum around Δy = 2.0 that survives to τ = 20 fm

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian treatment of fluctuations in expanding systems by Sakaida (2017) by incorporating non-linear terms that generate non-Gaussian cumulants, following the framework introduced by Nahrgang (2018). The results enable future comparisons with experimental fluctuation measurements from the RHIC beam-energy scan program and upcoming FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI experiments, particularly by showing that correlation functions can serve as signals for first-order phase transitions.
