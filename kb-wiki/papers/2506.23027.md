# Quarks in Hadrons and Nuclei Part 1: Quark Structure of Nucleon

> We propose a semi-empirical quark model of nucleon structure, so-called, Strongly Correlated Quark Model, SCQM, which possess the features of both non-relativistic and relativistic quark models. Based on SU(3) color symmetry it includes the main features of QCD: local gauge invariance, asymptotic freedom, and chiral symmetry breaking. This First Part of the paper is devoted to description of the model, SCQM, and in the forthcoming Second Part we will apply the model to built the nuclear structure. Applied to nuclei, it reveals emergence of the face-centered cubic (FCC) symmetry of the nuclear structure.This symmetry arise from quark-quark correlations leading, in turn, to nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semi-empirical Strongly Correlated Quark Model (SCQM) that unifies features of both non-relativistic and relativistic quark models, but the author explicitly states the model is "rather qualitative than quantitative" and serves primarily to construct nuclear structure in a semi-empirical way, with quantitative results limited to nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections.

## Method

The model treats valence quarks as oscillating between constituent (non-relativistic) and current (relativistic) states, with their dynamical mass arising from destructive interference of color fields in the physical vacuum, analogous to a breather solution of the Sine-Gordon equation. The author performs Monte Carlo simulations of nucleon-nucleon collisions using an Inelastic Overlap Function (IOF) in impact parameter representation, where inelastic interactions occur when the convolution of hadronic matter density distributions of quarks from projectile and target exceeds a threshold related to the pion transverse mass. Parameters of the model are adjusted by comparing calculated IOF with the "BEL" parametrization of experimental data for pp and pbar-p collisions.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at maximum displacement: 360 MeV (taken as one-third of the average of delta isobar and nucleon masses)
- Current (valence) quark mass: 5 MeV
- Maximum quark displacement x_max: 0.64 ± 0.02 fm
- Gaussian width parameters: sigma_xy = 0.24 ± 0.01 fm, sigma_z = 0.12 ± 0.01 fm
- Root mean square radius of proton: 0.85 ± 0.03 fm (compared to experimental value 0.84 fm)
- Predicted single diffractive cross section at LHC energy: sigma_SD = 8.30 ± 0.15 mb (compared to ATLAS result of 7.9 mb)
- Chiral condensate value: <psi-bar psi> approximately -(250 MeV)^3

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Strongly Correlated Quark Model (Musulmanbekov, earlier works cited as Mus1, Mus2, Mus3) and is presented as Part 1 of a two-part series, with Part 2 to apply the model to nuclear structure. The model attempts to address the long-standing dilemma in nuclear physics that mutually exclusive models (liquid-drop, shell, and cluster models) each work well for limited data sets but fail for others, and that realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions with three-body forces have not solved nuclear structure problems. The approach is contrasted with lattice QCD (which does not specify overall dynamics) and effective field theories (van Kolck, cited), and the author argues that quarks play an explicit role in arranging nucleons via color-charged quark-quark interactions leading to nucleon-nucleon correlations.
