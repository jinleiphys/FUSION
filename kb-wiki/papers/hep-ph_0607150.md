# Study of exclusive semileptonic and nonleptonic decays of $B_c^-$ in a nonrelativistic quark model

> We present results for different observables measured in semileptonic and non-leptonic decays of the $B_c^-$ meson. The calculations have been done within the framework of a nonrelativistic constituent quark model. In order to check the sensitivity of all our results against the inter-quark interaction we use five different quark--quark potentials. We obtain form factors, decay widths and asymmetry parameters for semileptonic $B_c^-\to c\bar c$ and $B_c^-\to \bar B$ decays. In the limit of infinite heavy quark mass our model reproduces the constraints of heavy quark spin symmetry. For the actual heavy quark masses we find nonetheless large corrections to that limiting situation for some form factors. We also analyze exclusive non-leptonic two--meson decay channels within the factorization approximation.

## Key claim

The authors compute form factors, decay widths, and asymmetry parameters for exclusive semileptonic and nonleptonic decays of the $B_c^-$ meson using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model, finding that while the model reproduces heavy quark spin symmetry constraints in the infinite heavy quark mass limit, large corrections to that limiting situation appear for some form factors when using actual heavy quark masses.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with five different quark-quark potentials (one from Bhaduri and collaborators, four from Silvestre-Brac and Semay) to evaluate hadronic matrix elements for $B_c^-$ decays. They compute form factors for semileptonic $B_c^-\to c\bar c$ and $B_c^-\to \bar B$ transitions, and analyze exclusive nonleptonic two-meson decay channels within the factorization approximation, using physical meson masses from the PDG and the recent CDF measurement of $m_{B_c}=6285.7$ MeV/$c^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $B_c$ decays using various theoretical approaches, including the relativistic constituent quark model of Ivanov (2005, 2006), the quasi-potential approach of Ebert (2003), and the Bethe-Salpeter equation treatments of Chang (1994, 2001, 2002) and Liu (1997). The authors compare their form factor results with those of Ivanov (2005) and Ebert (2003), finding excellent agreement with Ebert for the $B_c\to\eta_c$ and $B_c\to J/\Psi$ channels but large discrepancies with Ivanov for some form factors such as $F_-$ in the $\eta_c$ case. The use of five different inter-quark potentials provides a theoretical error estimate, and the results enable future comparisons with experimental data from CDF and other facilities studying $B_c$ meson weak decays.
