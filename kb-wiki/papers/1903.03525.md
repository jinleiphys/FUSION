# The Equation of State and Cooling of Hyperonic Neutron Stars

> We present two recent parametrizations of the equation of state (FSU2R and FSU2H models) that reproduce the properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, fulfill constraints on high-density matter stemming from heavy-ion collisions, produce 2$M_{\odot}$ neutron stars, and generate neutron star radii below 13 km. Making use of these equations of state, cooling simulations for isolated neutron stars are performed. We find that two of the models studied, FSU2R (with nucleons) and, in particular, FSU2H (with nucleons and hyperons), show very good agreement with cooling observations, even without including nucleon pairing. This indicates that cooling observations are compatible with an equation of state that produces a soft nuclear symmetry energy and, thus, generates small neutron star radii. Nevertheless, both schemes produce cold isolated neutron stars with masses above $1.8 M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim
The FSU2R (nucleonic) and FSU2H (hyperonic) equations of state produce cooling curves that agree with observed neutron star cooling data even without nucleon pairing, but only for cold isolated neutron stars with masses above 1.8 solar masses; the agreement relies on a soft nuclear symmetry energy that generates small neutron star radii.

## Method
The authors performed cooling simulations for isolated neutron stars using two new relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations (FSU2R and FSU2H) of the FSU2 model. They calculated the thermal evolution for stars of different masses, with and without hyperons, and with and without nucleon pairing (superfluidity/superconductivity), comparing the resulting surface temperatures as a function of stellar age against observed cooling data from a set of prominent neutron stars including the Cas A remnant.

## Key numbers
- FSU2R predicts symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0)= 30.7\) MeV and slope parameter \(L=46.9\) MeV
- FSU2H predicts \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0)= 30.5\) MeV and \(L=44.5\) MeV
- FSU2R produces neutron star radii in the range 11.5–13 km for masses between the maximum mass and \(M=1.4 M_\odot\)
- Nucleonic direct URCA threshold for FSU2R(nuc) is 0.61 fm\(^{-3}\); for FSU2H(nuc) it is also 0.61 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Hyperonic direct URCA threshold for FSU2R(hyp) is 0.37 fm\(^{-3}\); for FSU2H(hyp) it is 0.34 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Central density for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star: FSU2R(nuc) 0.39 fm\(^{-3}\), FSU2H(hyp) 0.34 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Central density for a \(2.0 M_\odot\) star: FSU2R(nuc) 0.72 fm\(^{-3}\), FSU2H(hyp) 0.71 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Both models produce neutron stars with maximum masses above \(2 M_\odot\) and radii below 13 km

## Context
This work extends the FSU2 RMF model family (Chen 2014) by introducing two new parametrizations that reconcile the \(2 M_\odot\) mass observations (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) with radius constraints below 13 km from gravitational-wave analysis of GW170817 (LIGO/Virgo 2017) and from X-ray observations (Lattimer 2014, Nattila 2015). The cooling simulations build on prior work by Page (2006) and Weber (2007) and show that a soft symmetry energy is compatible with observed cooling data, contrary to some earlier expectations. The results enable future studies of pairing scenarios and mass determinations for colder neutron stars with surface temperatures below \(10^6\) K.
