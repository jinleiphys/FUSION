# Photon bremsstrahlung from quark jet via transverse and longitudinal scatterings: single versus multiple scatterings

> We study the production of jet-bremsstrahlung photons through the scattering with the constituents of a dense nuclear matter within the framework of deep-inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. Applying a gradient expansion up to the second order for the exchanged three-dimensional momentum between jet and medium, we derive the single photon bremsstrahlung spectrum with the inclusion of the contributions from the transverse broadening as well as the longitudinal drag and diffusion of the hard parton's momentum. We also compare the medium-induced photon radiation spectra for single scattering and from the resummation of multiple scatterings. It is found that the coupling between different scatterings can give additional contribution to medium-induced photon radiation, while for small momentum exchange, the leading contribution from the drag and diffusions to the photon emission spectra remain the same for single and multiple scatterings.

## Key claim

The authors derive the single photon bremsstrahlung spectrum from a quark jet interacting with a dense nuclear medium, including contributions from both transverse momentum broadening and longitudinal momentum drag and diffusion. They find that while the coupling between multiple scatterings can give additional contributions to medium-induced photon radiation, for small momentum exchange the leading contributions from drag and diffusion to the photon emission spectra remain the same for single and multiple scatterings.

## Method

The calculation is performed within the framework of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus, using a gradient expansion up to second order for the exchanged three-dimensional momentum between the jet and the medium. The authors compute the hadronic tensor for photon bremsstrahlung at twist-four level (single scattering) and then resum all-twist contributions (multiple scatterings) in the light-cone gauge, applying contour integration techniques for the momentum fractions and factorizing the nucleus state into nucleon densities and gluon field correlators.

## Key numbers

- The leading twist contribution to the hadronic tensor is given by: $dW_0^{A\mu\nu}/dl_\perp^2 dy = A C_p^A \sum_q Q_q^4 (\alpha_e/2\pi) [P(y)/l_\perp^2] (-g_\perp^{\mu\nu}) (2\pi) f_q(x_B+x_L)$, where $P(y)=[1+(1-y)^2]/y$ is the quark-to-photon splitting function and $y=l^-/q^-$ is the fraction of forward momentum carried by the photon.
- The momentum fraction $x_L = l_\perp^2/[2p^+q^-y(1-y)]$ is related to the photon formation time as $\tau_{\rm form} = 1/(x_Lp^+)$.
- The hard part of the matrix element for the single scattering diagram (Fig. 11twist(a)) simplifies to $T_{(a)} = 2yP(y)/l_\perp^2$.
- The phase factor for the single scattering contribution approximates to $S_{(a)} \approx e^{-ix_Lp^+y_0^-} [2-2\cos(x_L p^+ Y_1^-)]$.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Qin (2014) which obtained the leading contribution to the parton-photon double differential spectrum with both transverse and longitudinal momentum exchanges. The authors note that to obtain the leading contribution from longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion to the single photon radiation spectrum, one must proceed one step further in the momentum gradient expansion compared to that prior work. The single photon emission spectrum obtained here can be directly used to evaluate the production of jet-medium photons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which are expected to provide significant contribution to direct photon production at intermediate transverse momentum, as discussed by Fries (2002) and Qin (2009).
