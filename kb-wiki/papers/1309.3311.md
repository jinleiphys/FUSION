# Description of 158Er at ultrahigh spin in nuclear density functional theory

> Rotational bands in $^{158}$Er at ultra-high spin have been studied in the framework of relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear density functional theories. Consistent results are obtained across the theoretical models used but some puzzles remain when confronted with experiment. Namely, the many-body configurations which provide good description of experimental transition quadrupole moments and dynamic moments of inertia require substantial increase of the spins of observed bands as compared with experimental estimates, which are still subject to large uncertainties. If, however, the theoretical spins assignments turned out to be correct, the experimental band 1 in $^{158}$Er would be the highest-spin structure ever observed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed triaxial superdeformed bands in $^{158}$Er are best described by TSD3 configurations with large quadrupole moments and positive $\gamma \approx 10-15^{\circ}$, but this interpretation requires the spins of the observed bands to be substantially higher than current experimental estimates; if the theoretical spin assignments are correct, band 1 would be the highest-spin structure ever observed.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent principal-axis cranking calculations using two complementary frameworks: relativistic (covariant) density functional theory with the NL1 and NL3* parametrizations (cranked relativistic mean field, CRMF) and non-relativistic Skyrme density functional theory with the SkM* functional (cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock, CSHF). Pairing correlations were neglected due to the very large angular momenta involved, and the calculations were carried out for configurations at or slightly above the collective yrast line in the spin range $I=30-90\hbar$.

## Key numbers

- Experimental transition quadrupole moments of TSD bands in $^{157,158}$Er are $\sim 11$ $e$b, ruling out TSD1 configurations which predict $Q_t\sim 7.5$ $e$b
- For TSD3(a) configuration in CRMF-NL3*, $Q_t$ changes from 10.5 $e$b at $I=42$ to 9.0 $e$b at $I=72$, with $\gamma$ increasing from $12^{\circ}$ to $16^{\circ}$
- For TSD3(c) configuration in CRMF-NL3*, $Q_t$ ranges from 11 to 10.4 $e$b in the spin range $I=34-66$
- For TSD3(b) configuration in CSHF-SkM*, $Q_t=10.7$ $e$b
- For TSD3(d) configuration in CSHF-SkM*, $Q_t\sim 10.6$ $e$b with $\gamma \sim 11^{\circ}$
- Experimental spin estimate for band 1 is $\sim 65\hbar$, with uncertainty possibly larger than $4\hbar$
- If assigned to TSD3(a), band 1 would be observed in spin range $I=35-77$; if assigned to TSD3(c), $I=29-71$; if assigned to TSD3(b) in CSHF, $I=31-73$
- Band 1 carries only $\sim 10^{-4}$ of the respective channel intensity, two orders of magnitude less than the superdeformed yrast band in $^{152}$Dy observed up to $68\hbar$

## Context

This work extends the study of triaxial superdeformed bands at ultrahigh spins in the $A\sim 154-160$ mass region, following observations in $^{154}$Er, $^{157,158,159,160}$Er, $^{157}$Ho, and $^{160}$Yb. It contradicts the earlier interpretation based on TSD1 minima (from CNS calculations) which was ruled out by transition quadrupole moment measurements in $^{157,158}$Er (Er-Qt, 2012), and also challenges the TSD2 scenario advocated in a recent CNS study (KRMR, 2012) by showing that TSD2 configurations appear as saddle points in tilted-axis cranking (SDFN, 2012). The results enable future experimental work to resolve the spin assignment puzzle through more precise measurements of transition quadrupole moments and reliable spin estimates.
