# Measurement of the nuclear symmetry energy parameters from gravitational wave events

> The nuclear symmetry energy plays a role in determining both the nuclear properties of terrestrial matter as well as the astrophysical properties of neutron stars. The first measurement of the neutron star tidal deformability, from gravitational wave event GW170817, provides a new way of probing the symmetry energy. In this work, we report on new constraints on the symmetry energy from GW170817. We focus in particular on the low-order coefficients: namely, the value of the symmetry energy at the nuclear saturation density, S_0, and the slope of the symmetry energy, L_0. We find that the gravitational wave data are relatively insensitive to S_0, but that they depend strongly on L_0 and point to lower values of L_0 than have previously been reported, with a peak likelihood near L_0 ~ 20 MeV. Finally, we use the inferred posteriors on L_0 to derive new analytic constraints on higher-order nuclear terms.

## Key claim

The gravitational wave data from GW170817 are relatively insensitive to the symmetry energy at saturation density ($S_0$) but depend strongly on the slope parameter ($L_0$), pointing to lower values of $L_0$ than previously reported, with a peak likelihood near $L_0 \sim 20$ MeV; however, these constraints are approximate and depend on a single-polytrope approximation of the nuclear equation of state.

## Method

The authors used a nuclear expansion formalism decomposing the equation of state into symmetric matter and symmetry energy components with six free parameters ($K_0, Q_0, S_0, L_0, K_{\rm sym}, Q_{\rm sym}$). They approximated this full six-parameter pressure with a single-polytrope model depending only on $S_0$, $L_0$, and the polytropic index $\Gamma$, validated against 1,000 test equations of state drawn from experimentally-constrained distributions. They then integrated augmented Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute stellar masses, radii, and tidal apsidal constants, mapping the measured effective tidal deformability $\widetilde{\Lambda}$ from GW170817 to posteriors on $L_0$ and deriving analytic constraints on higher-order nuclear parameters.

## Key numbers

- GW170817 constrained the effective tidal deformability to $\widetilde{\Lambda} = 300^{+420}_{-230}$ (90% highest posterior density) for a system with chirp mass $\mathcal{M}_c = 1.186^{+0.001}_{-0.001} M_\odot$ and low-spin priors
- For $\Gamma=3.5$, the 90% highest-posterior density interval on $L_0$ is $9.0 < L_0 < 65.4$ MeV, with peak likelihood at $L_0 \approx 22.5$ MeV
- The single-polytrope approximation introduces errors $\lesssim 15\%$ at $2 n_{\rm sat}$ for 90% of the test EOS sample
- For $\Gamma=3.5$, the most likely $L_0$ yields the constraint $K_{\rm sym} = 92.32 - 1.201 K_0$ (in MeV)
- Combining with $220 < K_0 < 315$ MeV gives $-375 \lesssim K_{\rm sym} \lesssim 45$ MeV at 90% confidence

## Context

This work extends prior analyses by Malik (2018) and Carson (2019) that set constraints on nuclear parameters from GW170817 but either limited $L_0$ to predetermined ranges or marginalized over priors on $L_0$. The authors find that those studies' priors may be in tension with the lower $L_0$ values inferred directly from gravitational wave data. The framework enables direct mapping from future gravitational wave detections to $L_0$ and combinations of higher-order nuclear parameters ($K_{\rm sym}$, $Q_0$, $Q_{\rm sym}$), independent of nuclear experiment priors.
