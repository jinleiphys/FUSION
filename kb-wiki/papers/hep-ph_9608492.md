# Color Transparency and Color Opacity in Coherent Production of Vector Mesons off Light Nuclei at small x

> Coherent electroproduction of vector mesons off $^2$H and $^4$He is considered in the kinematics of deep inelastic scattering. Special emphasis is given to the $-t\sim 0.8-1$ GeV$^2$ region where the cross section is dominated by the interaction of the $q\bar q$ configuration in the $γ^*$ with two nucleons. These kinematics provide the unique possibilities to study quantitatively in vector meson production the onset of Color Transparency with increasing $Q^2$ as well as its gradual disappearance at very small $x$, i.e., perturbative Color Opacity.

## Key claim

Coherent electroproduction of vector mesons off deuterium and helium-4 at momentum transfers \(-t \sim 0.8-1\) GeV\(^2\) provides a quantitative probe of both the onset of Color Transparency with increasing \(Q^2\) and its gradual disappearance at very small \(x\) (perturbative Color Opacity), but the analysis assumes double rescattering dominates and neglects leading-twist shadowing effects that may compete in certain kinematic ranges.

## Method

The authors calculate the coherent cross section for \(\gamma^*_L + A \rightarrow V + A\) (A=2,4) at small \(x\) and large \(Q^2\) using a three-stage model: the virtual photon fluctuates into a small transverse-size \(q\bar q\) pair (size \(b_{q\bar q} \approx 3/Q\)), the pair scatters off the target via two-gluon exchange with amplitude proportional to the gluon density \(xG_T(x,Q^2)\), and the pair forms the vector meson long after the target. For the deuteron and \(^4\)He, they include single and double rescattering of the \(q\bar q\) pair off nucleons, using the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors and the \(^4\)He charge form factor, while neglecting multi-scattering amplitudes beyond double scattering due to Mandelstam cancellation.

## Key numbers

- The \(q\bar q\) transverse size is \(b_{q\bar q}(Q^2 \sim 10\ \text{GeV}^2) \approx 0.4\) fm.
- The slope of the elementary production amplitude is \(B \approx 2.5\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The region where double rescattering dominates is \(-t \sim 0.8-1\ \text{GeV}^2\) for the deuteron and \(-t \sim 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\) for \(^4\)He (the diffractive minimum for \(^4\)He).
- The ratio \(R(t) = (d\sigma/dt)(t)/(d\sigma/dt)(t=0)\) at \(|t|>|t_0|\) scales as \(x^2 G_N^2(x,Q^2) e^{Bt}/Q^4\).
- The real part of the production amplitude is predicted to be \(ReF/ImF \sim 0.3-0.4\).
- The hard mechanism is expected to dominate already at \(Q^2 \ge 5\ \text{GeV}^2\) based on HERA data.

## Context

This work extends the QCD analysis of exclusive vector meson production from nucleons (Frankfurt et al., 1995; Brodsky et al., 1994) to light nuclei, specifically targeting the double-scattering regime where the \(q\bar q\) pair interacts with two nucleons. It contrasts with the Vector Dominance Model (VMD), where a pre-formed \(\rho\) meson scatters with a cross section similar to \(\pi N\) interactions. The results enable experimental tests of Color Transparency at HERA energies and the search for perturbative Color Opacity at very small \(x\) near the unitarity bound, though the authors note that leading-twist nuclear shadowing effects (Frankfurt et al., 1995) may compete and require further study.
