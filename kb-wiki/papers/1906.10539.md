# Ab initio Gamow in-medium similarity renormalization group with resonance and continuum

> We have developed a novel ab initio Gamow in-medium similarity renormalization group (Gamow IMSRG) in the complex-energy Berggren framework. The advanced Gamow IMSRG is capable of describing the resonance and nonresonant continuum properties of weakly bound and unbound nuclear many-body systems. As test grounds, carbon and oxygen isotopes have been calculated with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces from the effective field theory. Resonant states observed in the neutron-dripline 24O are well reproduced. The halo structure of the known heaviest Borromean nucleus 22C is clearly seen by calculating the density distribution in which the continuum s channel plays a crucial role. Furthermore, we predict low-lying resonant excited states in 22C. The Gamow IMSRG provides tractable ab initio calculations of weakly bound and unbound open quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops an *ab initio* Gamow in-medium similarity renormalization group (Gamow IMSRG) that extends the standard IMSRG to the complex-energy Berggren framework, enabling unified treatment of bound, resonant, and continuum states in weakly bound and unbound nuclei. The method reproduces observed resonant states in the neutron-dripline nucleus ²⁴O and reveals the halo structure of ²²C, but the authors note that the center-of-mass correction cannot be applied in the complex-energy basis and is instead estimated via an approximation from real-energy calculations.

## Method

The authors first perform a Gamow Hartree-Fock (GHF) calculation using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces (NNLO_opt and NNLO_sat) in a harmonic oscillator basis to generate a complex-energy Berggren single-particle basis. They then apply the IMSRG in this complex-momentum Berggren basis using the Magnus(2*) approximation and the White generator to decouple the Hamiltonian for closed-shell ground states, followed by equation-of-motion (EOM) calculations for excited states. Only neutron d₃/₂ and s₁/₂ partial waves are treated in the resonance and continuum Berggren basis, while other neutron channels and all proton channels are handled in the real-energy discrete HF basis.

## Key numbers

- Experimental root-mean-squared matter radius of ²²C: 3.44 ± 0.08 fm (Togano 2016) and 3.38 ± 0.10 fm (PhysRevC.97.054614)
- Gamow IMSRG with NNLO_opt gives matter radius of ²²C: 2.928 fm (with continuum s-wave) vs 2.798 fm (with discrete real-energy HF s-states)
- Gamow IMSRG with NNLO_sat gives matter radius of ²²C: 3.139 fm (with continuum s-wave) vs 2.983 fm (with discrete real-energy HF s-states)
- Optimized center-of-mass vibration frequency for ground state of ²⁴O: ℏΩ̃ ≈ 14.0 MeV; for ²²C: ℏΩ̃ ≈ 12.6 MeV
- 30 Gauss-Legendre mesh points for each continuum channel sufficient for converged energies; 36 mesh points needed for converged density tail in ²²C
- Predicted low-lying resonant states in ²²C at energies ~3.5–4.0 MeV with widths ~0.15–0.25 MeV

## Context

This work extends the real-energy IMSRG framework (Bogner 2007, Hergert 2016) to the complex-energy Berggren basis, following the approach of the Gamow shell model (Michel 2002, 2003) and complex coupled cluster (Hagen 2007, 2012). The method enables *ab initio* calculations of weakly bound and unbound open quantum systems, reproducing experimental results for ²⁴O and providing the first continuum-coupled *ab initio* calculation of the Borromean halo nucleus ²²C. The predictions for low-lying resonant excited states in ²²C offer guidance for future radioactive beam experiments.
