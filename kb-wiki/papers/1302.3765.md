# Nonlinear effects and the behavior of total hadronic and photonic cross sections

> In this work we use an eikonalized minijet model where the effects of the first nonlinear corrections to the DGLAP equations are taken into account. The contributions coming from gluon recombination effects are included in the DGLAP+GLRMQ approach for the free proton in the context of saturation models. The parameters of the model are fixed to fit total $pp$ and $\bar p p$ cross sections, including the very recent data from LHC, HiRes and P. Auger Collaborations. Glauber and multiple scattering approximations are then used to describe the inclusive inelastic proton-Air cross section. Photoproduction cross sections, without change of parameters fixed before, are also obtained from the model using Vector Meson Dominance and the Additive Quark Model. We show and discuss our main results as well as the implications of saturation effects in the behavior of total hadronic and photonic cross sections at very high energies.

# Nonlinear effects and the behavior of total hadronic and photonic cross sections

## Key claim

The inclusion of first nonlinear (GLRMQ) corrections to DGLAP evolution equations, via gluon recombination effects, produces a modest softening of the rise of total hadronic and photonic cross sections at very high energies compared to conventional linear-evolution eikonalized minijet models, but the effect is relatively small and only manifests at extremely high energies (above ~10 TeV).

## Method

The authors use an eikonalized minijet model where the hard component is computed from leading-order pQCD minijet cross sections, with parton distribution functions evolved using either linear DGLAP evolution (GRV98 and CTEQ6L sets) or the EHKQS set which includes the first nonlinear GLRMQ gluon recombination terms. The model's hard parameters (p_Tmin^2, mu_gg, mu_qq) are fixed by fitting total pp and pbar-p cross sections to LHC, HiRes, and Pierre Auger data; then, without changing parameters, the inelastic proton-Air cross section is obtained via the Glauber multiple scattering formalism, and photoproduction cross sections (gamma-p and gamma-gamma) are derived using vector meson dominance and the additive quark model.

## Key numbers

- Hard parameters for the nonlinear (EHKQS) case: p_Tmin^2 = 1.51 GeV^2, mu_gg = 2.00 GeV, mu_qq = 0.70 GeV; for linear CTEQ6L: p_Tmin^2 = 2.10 GeV^2, mu_gg = 2.03 GeV, mu_qq = 0.73 GeV; for linear GRV98: p_Tmin^2 = 1.32 GeV^2, mu_gg = 1.88 GeV, mu_qq = 1.00 GeV; mu^2_soft = 0.7 GeV^2 in all cases.
- At 10^3 TeV, the ratio of the total pp cross section to the PDG parametrization is ~0.86 for linear (CTEQ6L or GRV98) and ~0.79 for nonlinear (EHKQS); the ratio to the Block-Halzen parametrization is ~0.91 for linear and ~0.84 for nonlinear.
- The Froissart-type fit to the EHKQS results gives a ln^2(s) coefficient of 0.2014 ± 0.0035 mb (PDG-style fit) or 0.1445 ± 0.0026 mb (BH-style fit), compared to 0.2152 ± 0.0015 mb (PDG-style) or 0.2230 ± 0.0004 mb (BH-style) for the linear cases.
- The probability that the photon interacts as a hadron was fixed at P_had^gamma-p = 1/221.
- For proton-Air cross sections, the Woods-Saxon parameters used are A_Air = 14.5 and a_0 = 0.75 fm.

## Context

This work extends the eikonalized minijet model approach of Durand and Pi (1980s) and the formulation by Block, Pancheri, and collaborators, by incorporating the nonlinear GLRMQ gluon recombination terms originally calculated by Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin and later by Mueller and Qiu. The results are compared with linear-evolution calculations using GRV98 and CTEQ6L parton densities. The model's predictions for inelastic proton-Air cross sections favor a moderately slow rise consistent with Pierre Auger measurements, and the photoproduction results show similar limitations to other models in the literature (e.g., Luna and Natale, Grau et al.), suggesting the need for additional dynamical ingredients such as diffractive processes or an infrared gluon mass scale.
