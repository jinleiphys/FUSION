# The role of tensor force in nuclear matter saturation

> Using a relativistic Dirac-Brueckner analysis the pion contribution to the ground state energy of nuclear matter is studied. Evidence is presented that the role of the tensor force in the saturation mechanism is substantially reduced compared to its dominant role in a usual non-relativistic treatment. The reduction of the pion contribution in nuclear matter is due to many-body effects present in a relativistic treatment. In particular, we show that the damping of OPEP is actually due to the decrease of $M^*/M$ with increasing density.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic Dirac-Brueckner analysis, the pion (tensor force) contribution to nuclear matter saturation is substantially reduced compared to its dominant role in non-relativistic treatments, and this damping is specifically due to the decrease of the effective nucleon mass ratio \(M^*/M\) with increasing density.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic Dirac-Brueckner (D-B) calculation of nuclear matter, using a relativistic one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with \(\pi, \rho, \omega, \sigma, \delta\) and \(\eta\) mesons as the NN quasipotential. They solved the Dirac-Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone equation self-consistently for the G-matrix and nucleon self-energy at a given density, then computed the binding energy per nucleon \(E/A\). The pion contribution \(\langle NM | V_\pi | NM \rangle\) was extracted using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem, and its density dependence was compared to the non-relativistic result. To isolate the role of \(M^*/M\), they artificially scaled the scalar self-energy \(S\) by a factor \(\alpha\) and recalculated the pion contribution.

## Key numbers

* The non-relativistic pion contribution to nuclear matter is \(\langle NM | V_\pi | NM \rangle_{\text{non-relativistic}} \sim -34\,(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.45}\) MeV, where \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The relativistic pion contribution is \(\langle NM | V_\pi | NM \rangle_{\text{Relativistic}} \sim -20\,(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.16}\) MeV, showing a much weaker density dependence.
* In the relativistic D-B calculation, the scalar self-energy is \(S_{D-B} = -306\,(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.81}\) MeV and the vector self-energy is \(V_{D-B} = 233\,(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.97}\) MeV.
* In a mean field theory (MFT) using the same OBE interaction, the scalar field is \(S_{MFT} \sim -358\,(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.92}\) MeV and the vector field is \(V_{MFT} \sim 295\) MeV.
* The deuteron expectation value of \(V_\pi\) in a relativistic treatment is \(\langle D | V_\pi | D \rangle = -31\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner studies of nuclear matter by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and by Amado and Tjon (1995), and directly contradicts the long-standing non-relativistic picture established by Bethe (1956) where the tensor force from one-pion exchange is the dominant saturation mechanism. The finding that the tensor force contribution is suppressed in relativistic nuclear matter, while remaining large in the deuteron, resolves a tension between relativistic and non-relativistic many-body approaches and implies that no non-relativistic theory using NN forces that fit two-body data can be compatible with the relativistic theory. This enables future work to focus on the scalar-vector field mechanism as the primary saturation driver in relativistic treatments.
