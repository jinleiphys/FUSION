# Quark matter nucleation in neutron stars and astrophysical implications

> A phase of strong interacting matter with deconfined quarks is expected in the core of massive neutron stars. We investigate the quark deconfinement phase transition in cold (T = 0) and hot beta-stable hadronic matter. Assuming a first order phase transition, we calculate and compare the nucleation rate and the nucleation time due to quantum and thermal nucleation mechanisms. We show that above a threshold value of the central pressure a pure hadronic star (HS) (i.e. a compact star with no fraction of deconfined quark matter) is metastable to the conversion to a quark star (QS) (i.e. a hybrid star or a strange star). This process liberates an enormous amount of energy, of the order of 10^{53}~erg, which causes a powerful neutrino burst, likely accompanied by intense gravitational waves emission, and possibly by a second delayed (with respect to the supernova explosion forming the HS) explosion which could be the energy source of a powerful gamma-ray burst (GRB). This stellar conversion process populates the QS branch of compact stars, thus one has in the Universe two coexisting families of compact stars: pure hadronic stars and quark stars. We introduce the concept of critical mass M_{cr} for cold HSs and proto-hadronic stars (PHSs), and the concept of limiting conversion temperature for PHSs. We show that PHSs with a mass M < M_{cr} could survive the early stages of their evolution without decaying to QSs. Finally, we discuss the possible evolutionary paths of proto-hadronic stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that pure hadronic stars (neutron stars with no deconfined quark matter) become metastable to conversion into quark stars (hybrid or strange stars) once their central pressure exceeds a threshold value, but only if the first-order phase transition from hadronic to quark matter is assumed; the actual nucleation time depends critically on the poorly known surface tension at the hadron-quark interface, and the concept of a "critical mass" \(M_{cr}\) is defined as the mass for which the nucleation time equals one year, meaning stars above this mass are unlikely to be observed.

## Method

The authors calculated the quark deconfinement nucleation rate and nucleation time in cold (\(T = 0\)) and hot \(\beta\)-stable hadronic matter using a semi-classical WKB tunneling approach for quantum nucleation and a thermal nucleation mechanism. They employed the Glendenning–Moszkowski (GM1 and GM3) relativistic mean-field models for the hadronic phase and several quark matter equations of state (MIT bag model, extended bag model with perturbative QCD corrections, Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, and Chromo Dielectric model) to determine the phase equilibrium conditions via the Gibbs phase rule. The nucleation time was then used to define a critical mass \(M_{cr}\) for cold hadronic stars and proto-hadronic stars, and the energy released during the conversion of a hadronic star to a quark star was computed assuming baryonic mass conservation.

## Key numbers

* The maximum stellar central density predicted by hadronic equations of state is in the range of 4–8 times the saturation density of nuclear matter (\(\sim 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\)).
* The energy released in the conversion of a hadronic star to a quark star is \(E_{conv} = 0.5\)–\(4.0 \times 10^{53}\) erg.
* For the GM1 model with \(x_\sigma = 0.7\) and the extended bag model with \(\sigma = 10\) MeV/fm\(^2\), the critical mass \(M_{cr}\) ranges from 1.891 to 2.033 \(M_{sun}\) depending on the bag constant and \(a_4\) parameters, with corresponding released energies \(E_{conv}\) from 1.41 to 2.86 \(\times 10^{53}\) erg.
* For the same hadronic model with \(\sigma = 30\) MeV/fm\(^2\), the critical mass \(M_{cr}\) ranges from 1.958 to 2.083 \(M_{sun}\) and \(E_{conv}\) from 1.08 to 2.35 \(\times 10^{53}\) erg.
* The transition pressure \(P_0\) at \(T = 0\) for the GM1\(_{0.6}\)–B85 equation of state is 39.95 MeV/fm\(^3\) for neutrino-free matter and 113.77 MeV/fm\(^3\) for matter with trapped neutrinos.
* The specific latent heat \(\mathcal{Q}\) at \(T = 30\) MeV for the GM1\(_{0.6}\)–B85 equation of state is 25.44 MeV for neutrino-free matter and 29.88 MeV for trapped-neutrino matter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Bombaci et al., 2002–2013) on quark matter nucleation in neutron stars and provides a framework for understanding the delayed conversion of hadronic stars to quark stars as a possible energy source for gamma-ray bursts, as originally proposed by Bombaci and collaborators (2002, 2003). The results are consistent with the measured masses of PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{sun}\)) and PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{sun}\)), and the scenario suggests that the bimodal distribution of pulsar kick velocities (Arzoumanian et al., 2002) could be explained by a second kick imparted during the hadronic-to-quark star conversion (Bombaci and Popov, 2004). The paper enables future studies of the evolutionary paths of proto-hadronic stars and the observational signatures (neutrino bursts, gravitational waves) of such stellar conversions.
