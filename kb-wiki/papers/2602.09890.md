# Imaging two-body correlations in atomic nuclei via low- and high-energy processes

> Characterizing the correlated behavior of nucleons inside atomic nuclei constitutes a long-standing challenge, both experimentally and theoretically. It has recently been understood that two-particle correlations in the azimuthal distribution of final hadrons emitted in ultra-relativistic ultra-central ion-ion collisions can be used to quantify ground-state two-body correlations. Performing systematic ab initio nuclear structure calculations of light nuclei, we demonstrate that such an observable does provide a meaningful imaging of nuclear ground states, naturally leading to a robust interpretation of the various categories of two-nucleon correlations at play. This is at variance with the low-energy approach relying on Kumar operators whose traditional interpretation in terms of deformation parameters is shown to be inoperative. A future interesting development will consist of targeting specific three-particle correlations to isolate three-nucleon correlations in which additional nuclear structure information of interest leave their fingerprint.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-particle correlations in the azimuthal distribution of final hadrons from ultra-relativistic ultra-central ion-ion collisions can provide a meaningful imaging of nuclear ground-state two-body correlations, but only after isolating the two-body contribution from a trivial one-body term that dominates in light nuclei; this high-energy approach is shown to be superior to the low-energy approach using Kumar operators, whose traditional interpretation in terms of deformation parameters is inoperative for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed systematic ab initio nuclear structure calculations using the Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) and its reduced Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) approximation, based on the EM1.8/2.0 Chiral Effective Field Theory Hamiltonian. They computed the normalized mean-square eccentricity of multipolarity ell=2 and ell=3 for ground states of even-even nuclei with proton number 8 to 28, decomposing it into one-body and two-body contributions, and compared these results to the expectation value of the second-order quadrupolar Kumar operator reconstructed from electromagnetic transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For the PHFB ground state of 20Ne, the rescaled two-body quadrupole mean-square eccentricity B2_2(HE) is +0.13; the PGCM value increases to +0.21, consistent with the Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) value of +0.20.
- For 16O, the PHFB B2_2(HE) is -0.06, the PGCM value is -0.05, the NLEFT value is +0.04, and the Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) value is -0.01.
- For 20Ne, the PGCM octupole B2_3(HE) is +0.10, in perfect agreement with the NLEFT value of +0.10, while the PHFB value is -0.07.
- For 22Ne, the PHFB B2_2(HE) is +0.13 and the PGCM value is +0.18; the PHFB B2_3(HE) is -0.07 and the PGCM value is -0.01.
- For 12C, the PHFB B2_2(HE) is +0.07 and the PGCM value is +0.10; the PHFB B2_3(HE) is +0.00 and the PGCM value is +0.01.
- The one-body contribution to the normalized mean-square quadrupole eccentricity is equal to 1/A in the rigid rotor model and dominates in light nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the recent theoretical connection established by Duguet (2025) between the fluctuation of the ell-th Fourier component of azimuthal hadronic flow and the ground-state density-density correlation function of colliding nuclei. It contradicts the traditional low-energy approach using Kumar operators, whose interpretation in terms of deformation parameters was previously questioned by Poves (2020) and is shown here to be inoperative for light nuclei because the operator does not separate one-body and two-body contributions. The results enable future systematic PGCM calculations to quantify how shape fluctuations enlarge the two-body mean-square eccentricity as a function of both intrinsic deformation and mass number, and suggest targeting three-particle correlations to isolate three-nucleon correlations.
