# $ρ$-$ω$ mixing and spin dependent CSV potential

> We construct the charge symmetry violating (CSV) nucleon-nucleon potential induced by the $ρ^0$-$ø$ mixing due to the neutron-proton mass difference driven by the $NN$ loop. Analytical expression for for the two-body CSV potential is presented containing both the central and non- central $NN$ interaction. We show that the $ρ$$NN$ tensor interaction can significantly enhance the charge symmetry violating $NN$ interaction even if momentum dependent off-shell $ρ^0$-$ω$ mixing amplitude is considered. It is also shown that the inclusion of form factors removes the divergence arising out of the contact interaction. Consequently, we see that the precise size of the computed scattering length difference depends on how the short range aspects of the CSV potential are treated.

# ρ-ω mixing and spin dependent CSV potential

## Key claim

The paper constructs a charge symmetry violating (CSV) nucleon-nucleon potential from ρ⁰-ω mixing driven by the neutron-proton mass difference, and shows that including the ρNN tensor interaction and contributions from external nucleon legs can significantly enhance the CSV interaction even when momentum-dependent off-shell ρ⁰-ω mixing is used, countering earlier claims that off-shell mixing suppresses CSV. The precise size of the computed scattering length difference depends on how the short-range aspects of the CSV potential are treated, particularly the inclusion of contact terms and form factors.

## Method

The authors compute the ρ⁰-ω mixing amplitude from the difference between proton and neutron loop diagrams (N̄N excitations) using dimensional regularization, with the mixing driven by the neutron-proton mass difference. They then construct the two-body CSV potential in the one-boson exchange (OBE) model by taking the non-relativistic limit of the matrix element, including contributions from external nucleon legs and the ρNN tensor coupling. The potential is transformed to coordinate space, and form factors of the monopole type are introduced to remove divergences from contact interactions. The scattering length difference Δa = a_pp − a_nn in the ¹S₀ state is calculated using a zero-energy wave function.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell mixing amplitude values obtained are Π_{ρω}(m²_ω) = −4314 MeV² and Π_{ρω}(m²_ρ) = −4152 MeV², within the experimentally extracted range of ∼ −4520 ± 600 MeV².
- The experimentally observed CSV in the ¹S₀ state is Δa_CSV = a^N_pp − a^N_nn = 1.6 ± 0.6 fm (from Miller 1990, Howell 1998, Gonzalez 1999).
- Without the contact term ΔV^{NN}_{ρω}, the calculated scattering length difference is Δa = 0.31 fm (with C_ρ = 0) and Δa = 2.14 fm (with C_ρ = 6.1).
- With the contact term ΔV^{NN}_{ρω} included, the calculated scattering length difference becomes Δa = −0.06 fm (with C_ρ = 0) and Δa = −0.08 fm (with C_ρ = 6.1).
- The effective range used in the zero-energy wave function is r₀ = 2.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Piekarewicz (1992), who also used neutron-proton mass difference to drive ρ⁰-ω mixing but ignored CSV from external nucleon legs. The present paper shows that including external leg contributions and the ρNN tensor coupling can restore significant CSV strength even with off-shell mixing, contrary to the suppression found by Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (1992), Piekarewicz (1992), Krein (1993), and others. The results enable future calculations of other CSV observables such as binding energy differences in mirror nuclei (the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly) and further tests of the role of tensor interactions in charge symmetry violation.
