# The Spectrum of Diquark Composites in Cold Dense QCD

> The Bethe-Salpeter equations for spin zero diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of $N_f=2$ and $N_f=3$ cold dense QCD are studied. The explicit form of the spectrum of the diquarks with the quantum numbers of the (pseudo-) Nambu-Goldstone bosons is derived.

## Key claim

The paper derives the spectrum of diquark composites in cold dense QCD using Bethe-Salpeter equations, but the results are qualified by the leading-order approximation and the assumption that the left- and right-handed sectors are approximately decoupled; the massive radial excitations exist only for the singlet channel in the two-flavor case, not in the three-flavor case.

## Method

The author studies the Bethe-Salpeter equations for spin-zero diquark composites in the color superconducting phase of cold dense QCD with two and three quark flavors. The structure of the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for the (pseudo-) Nambu-Goldstone bosons is obtained from Ward identities, and the decay constants and velocities are derived in the Pagels-Stokar approximation. The analysis of massive radial excitations accounts for the Meissner effect, which is shown to be crucial for the binding of these states.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order mass formula for singlet diquark states in two-flavor QCD is \(M^{2}_{n} \simeq 4 |\Delta |^{2} \left(1-\frac{\alpha_{s}^{2}\kappa}{(2n+1)^{4}}\right)\), where \(|\Delta|\) is the dynamical Majorana mass of quarks, \(\alpha_{s}\) is the running coupling at the scale of the chemical potential \(\mu\), and \(\kappa\) is a constant of order 1.
- The decay constant of the (pseudo-) Nambu-Goldstone bosons is \(F = \mu/(2\sqrt{2}\pi)\) in the Pagels-Stokar approximation.
- The velocity of the (pseudo-) Nambu-Goldstone bosons is \(v = 1/\sqrt{3}\) in the Pagels-Stokar approximation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the color superconducting phase of dense QCD (first papers by various authors, and the work of Son and others) which focused mainly on Nambu-Goldstone bosons derived from symmetry arguments. The paper reveals for the first time the existence of five pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons in two-flavor dense QCD and shows that the spectrum contains an infinite tower of massive diquark singlets in that case, while no such massive radial excitations appear in the three-flavor color-flavor locked phase. This enables future work on thermodynamic properties of dense QCD that depend on the full diquark spectrum.
