# Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian within the formalism of deformation-dependent effective mass for Davidson potential

> In this work, we modify the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian describing the collective motion of a $γ$-rigid atomic nucleus by allowing the mass to depend on nuclear deformation. Exact analytical expressions are derived for energy spectra as well as normalized wave functions for Davidson potential. The model, called Z(4)-DDMD (Deformation Dependent Mass with Davidson potential), is achieved by using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM). The numerical calculations for energy spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities are compared to the experimental data of $^{192-196}$Pt isotopes. The obtained results show an overall agreement with the experiment and an important improvement in respect to other models.

## Key claim

The Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian for γ-rigid nuclei is modified by allowing the nuclear mass to depend on deformation, yielding exact analytical energy spectra and wave functions for the Davidson potential; the resulting Z(4)-DDMD model shows overall agreement with experiment and "an important improvement" over prior constant-mass models, though the improvement is conditional on the specific choice of deformation mass parameter and potential minimum fitted to each isotope.

## Method

The authors start from the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian for a γ-rigid nucleus (γ = π/6) and replace the constant mass parameter B with a deformation-dependent mass B(β) = B₀/f(β)², choosing f(β) = 1 + aβ² with a << 1. They separate the angular and β-vibrational parts, solve the angular equation using the results of Meyer-ter-Vehn (1975), and solve the radial Schrödinger equation for the Davidson potential v(β) = β² + β₀⁴/β² by applying the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) to obtain closed-form expressions for energy levels and normalized wave functions. The model parameters (a, β₀) are fitted to experimental energy spectra of ¹⁹²⁻¹⁹⁶Pt isotopes via least-squares minimization of the root-mean-square deviation.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁹²Pt: fitted parameters a = 0.002, β₀ = 1.32; the rms deviation σ = 0.526, compared to σ = 0.614 (Z(4)-sextic, Buganu 2015) and σ = 0.593 (Z(4)-sextic, Budaca 2016).
- For ¹⁹⁴Pt: fitted parameters a = 0.058, β₀ = 1.31; σ = 0.338, compared to σ = 0.543 and σ = 0.515 from the same prior models.
- For ¹⁹⁶Pt: fitted parameters a = 0.122, β₀ = 1.14; σ = 0.550, compared to σ = 0.682 and σ = 0.683.
- Ground-state band ratios R₀,₀,₄ (energy of 4⁺ relative to 2⁺): 2.374 (¹⁹²Pt), 2.445 (¹⁹⁴Pt), 2.362 (¹⁹⁶Pt); experimental values are 2.479, 2.470, 2.465 respectively.
- B(E2) ratio for 4g→2g relative to 2g→0g: 1.60 (¹⁹²Pt), 1.60 (¹⁹⁴Pt), 1.68 (¹⁹⁶Pt); experimental values are 1.56(12), 1.73(13), 1.48(3).

## Context

This work extends the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian framework for γ-rigid nuclei (originally developed by Davydov and Chaban, 1960) by incorporating the Deformation-Dependent Mass Formalism (DDMF) introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2010, 2011, 2013) and Chabab et al. (2016). It improves upon prior constant-mass treatments of the Davidson potential in the Z(4) geometry, specifically the Z(4)-sextic models of Buganu (2015) and Budaca (2016), by allowing the mass to vary with β deformation. The analytical solutions obtained via AIM enable systematic comparison with experimental data for ¹⁹²⁻¹⁹⁶Pt isotopes, and the approach can be extended to other collective potentials or nuclear regions where γ-rigid triaxial shapes are relevant.
