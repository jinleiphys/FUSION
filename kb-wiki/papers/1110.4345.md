# Theory of Soft Electromagnetic Emission in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A status report of utilizing soft electromagnetic radiation (aka thermal photons and dileptons) in the diagnosis of strongly interacting matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is given. After briefly elaborating on relations of the electromagnetic spectral function to chiral symmetry restoration and the transition from hadron to quark degrees of freedom, various calculations of electromagnetic emission rates in the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases of QCD matter are discussed. This, in particular, includes insights from recent thermal lattice QCD computations. Applications to dilepton and photon spectra in heavy-ion collisions highlight their role as a spectro-, thermo-, baro- and chrono-meter of extraordinary precision.

## Key claim

Soft electromagnetic radiation (thermal photons and dileptons) can serve as a spectro-, thermo-, baro- and chrono-meter for strongly interacting matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the extraction of medium properties depends on reliable knowledge of the in-medium electromagnetic spectral function, which remains a theoretical challenge especially in the nonperturbative low-mass regime.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations of the electromagnetic spectral function in both hadronic matter (using effective hadronic Lagrangians constrained by empirical decay widths and scattering data to compute in-medium selfenergies of vector mesons like the rho) and in the quark-gluon plasma (using hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory and lattice QCD computations of Euclidean current-current correlators). These spectral functions are then converted into thermal dilepton and photon emission rates via standard formulas involving the Bose-Einstein distribution and the electromagnetic coupling, and the rates are integrated over model fireball expansions to compare with experimental spectra from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperatures from lattice QCD are approximately T_c^chi = 150 MeV for chiral restoration and T_c^conf = 170 MeV for deconfinement, separated by about 20 MeV (Borsanyi 2010)
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV appears in the Weinberg sum rule f_{-1} = f_pi^2 relating vector and axialvector spectral functions to chiral symmetry breaking
- The rho meson dominates the low-mass electromagnetic correlator over the omega by a factor of approximately 11, based on empirical dilepton decay widths
- The chiral mixing parameter epsilon = T^2 / (6 f_pi^2) describes the leading-temperature effect on vector and axialvector correlators in a pion gas
- Lattice QCD results at T = 1.45 T_c show the electromagnetic spectral function in a gluon plasma, with a transport peak at low energies and a perturbative continuum at high energies

## Context

This status report extends the program of using electromagnetic probes to diagnose QCD matter, building on earlier reviews by Rapp (2009) and Gale (2009) and on lattice QCD computations by Borsanyi (2010) and Ding (2010). The work connects hadronic many-body calculations of the rho meson spectral function (Rapp 1999) with partonic calculations from hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (Braaten 1990) and lattice QCD, showing that the in-medium hadronic rate approaches the perturbative partonic rate near the phase transition, consistent with chiral restoration and deconfinement. The results enable more precise extraction of fireball temperatures, lifetimes, and collective flow properties from experimental dilepton and photon spectra at RHIC and LHC.
