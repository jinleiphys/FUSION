# Four-nucleon scattering with a correlated Gaussian basis method

> Elastic-scattering phase shifts for four-nucleon systems are studied in an $ab$-$initio$ type cluster model in order to clarify the role of the tensor force and to investigate cluster distortions in low energy $d+d$ and $t+p$ scattering. In the present method, the description of the cluster wave function is extended from a simple (0$s$) harmonic-oscillator shell model to a few-body model with a realistic interaction, in which the wave function of the subsystems are determined with the Stochastic Variational Method. In order to calculate the matrix elements of the four-body system, we have developed a Triple Global Vector Representation method for the correlated Gaussian basis functions. To compare effects of the cluster distortion with realistic and effective interactions, we employ the AV8$^{\prime}$ potential as a realistic interaction and the Minnesota potential as an effective interaction. Especially for $^1S_0$, the calculated phase shifts show that the $t+p$ and $h+n$ channels are strongly coupled to the $d+d$ channel for the case of the realistic interaction. On the contrary, the coupling of these channels plays a relatively minor role for the case of the effective interaction. This difference between both potentials originates from the tensor term in the realistic interaction. Furthermore, the tensor interaction makes the energy splitting of the negative parity states of $^4$He consistent with experiments. No such splitting is however reproduced with the effective interaction.

## Key claim

The tensor force in the realistic AV8' potential causes strong coupling between the \(t+p\) and \(h+n\) channels and the \(d+d\) channel in \(^1S_0\) scattering, whereas the central-only Minnesota potential shows only minor channel coupling; this difference explains why the tensor interaction is essential for reproducing the energy splitting of negative parity states of \(^4\)He.

## Method

The authors employed the microscopic \(R\)-matrix method with cluster wave functions expanded in correlated Gaussian basis functions using a newly developed Triple Global Vector Representation (TGVR) method. The intrinsic wave functions of the deuteron, triton, and helion clusters were determined by the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM) with realistic (AV8' plus a three-nucleon force) and effective (Minnesota) nucleon-nucleon interactions. Elastic-scattering phase shifts for \(d+d\) and \(t+p\) systems were then calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation in the internal region and matching to asymptotic Coulomb wave functions at a channel radius.

## Key numbers

*   Deuteron binding energy with AV8' potential: \(-2.18\) MeV (present calculation) versus \(-2.24\) MeV (literature); \(D\)-state probability \(P_D = 5.9\%\) (present) versus \(5.8\%\) (literature).
*   Triton binding energy with AV8' potential: \(-8.22\) MeV (present) versus \(-8.41\) MeV (literature); \(P_D = 8.4\%\) (present).
*   Helion binding energy with AV8' potential: \(-7.55\) MeV (present) versus \(-7.74\) MeV (literature); \(P_D = 8.3\%\) (present).

*   Deuteron binding energy with Minnesota potential: \(-2.10\) MeV (present) versus \(-2.20\) MeV (literature); \(P_D = 0\%\) (central-only interaction).
*   Triton binding energy with Minnesota potential: \(-8.38\) MeV (present) versus \(-8.38\) MeV (literature).
*   \(^4\)He binding energy with Minnesota potential: \(-29.94\) MeV (present, using 1140 basis functions) versus \(-29.94\) MeV (literature).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic cluster model beyond the conventional (0\(s\)) harmonic-oscillator description by incorporating realistic cluster wave functions with tensor forces, building on prior SVM calculations by Varga (1994, 1997) and Suzuki (1995, 1998) and on the Global Vector Representation method (GVR) developed for bound states. The TGVR method enables unified treatment of both H-type (for \(d+d\)) and K-type (for \(t+p\) and \(h+n\)) coordinate sets in scattering calculations. The results enable future studies of the \(d(d,\gamma)^4\)He radiative capture reaction, where the tensor-induced \(D\)-wave components are expected to contribute through \(E2\) transitions, and address puzzles in analyzing powers related to the \(A_y\) problem in three-nucleon systems.
