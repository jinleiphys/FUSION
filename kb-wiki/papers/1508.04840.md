# The Relativistic Three-Body Bound State in Three-Dimensions

> Studying of the relativistic three-body bound state in a three-dimensional (3D) approach is a necessary first step in a process to eventually perform scattering calculations at GeV energies, where partial-wave expansions are not useful. To this aim we recently studied relativistic effects in the binding energy and for the first time, obtained the relativistic 3B wave function \cite{Hadizadeh_PRC90}. The relativistic Faddeev integral equations for the bound state are formulated in terms of momentum vectors, and relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic effects in the three-body bound state can be treated using a three-dimensional (vector) formulation of the Faddeev equations, which avoids partial-wave expansions that become inefficient above about 300 MeV. The overall relativistic correction to the binding energy is small (about 3 percent), but this results from large cancellations among four distinct relativistic contributions.

## Method

The authors formulate the relativistic Faddeev integral equations for the three-body bound state in terms of momentum vectors, incorporating relativistic invariance within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics. The boosted two-body t-matrix is obtained from the non-relativistic t-matrix through a two-step process: first relating the relativistic right-half-shell t-matrix to the non-relativistic one via a multiplicative function F, then obtaining the fully off-shell t-matrix by solving a first resolvent type equation. The integral equations are solved using a Lanczos technique with spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon type potentials (MT-V and MT-Vc).

## Key numbers

- For the MT-V potential: relativistic binding energy E_r = -7.4825 MeV, nonrelativistic binding energy E_nr = -7.7382 MeV, giving a reduction of about 3.3 percent in the three-body binding energy
- For the MT-Vc potential: relativistic binding energy E_r = -7.4732 MeV, nonrelativistic binding energy E_nr = -7.7177 MeV, giving a reduction of about 3.2 percent
- Setting the Jacobian function N to one increases the binding energy by about 0.8 percent
- Setting the permutation coefficient C to one reduces the binding energy by about 0.6 percent
- Setting F=1 (replacing relativistic half-shell t-matrix with nonrelativistic) increases binding energy by about 2.4 percent
- Replacing the relativistic free propagator with the nonrelativistic one reduces binding energy by about 1.84 percent
- The net contribution of dynamic ingredients (N and C set to one) is a small increase of about 0.15 percent
- The net contribution of kinematic ingredients (F=1 and nonrelativistic propagator) is an increase of about 0.22 percent

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional vector formulation previously applied to non-relativistic three-body problems (Elster 2000, Liu 2003) and to relativistic one-channel scattering models (Lin 2008). The relativistic reduction in binding energy is consistent with earlier partial-wave calculations truncated to s-wave (Gloeckle 1993), which found about 2.70 percent reduction. The successful implementation of this three-dimensional approach for the bound state paves the way toward three-nucleon scattering calculations in the few-GeV energy range, where partial-wave expansions are not useful.
