# Antiquark Flavor Asymmetry with New Accelerator Facilities

## Key claim
The light antiquark distributions $\bar u$ and $\bar d$ are not equal, contradicting the naive perturbative QCD expectation; the asymmetry is a nonperturbative effect that cannot be explained by next-to-leading-order QCD corrections, which contribute only about 0.3% at $Q^2=4$ GeV$^2$ versus the observed ~30% deficit in the Gottfried sum.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental determinations of the $\bar u/\bar d$ asymmetry from the Gottfried sum rule in deep inelastic scattering (NMC), Drell-Yan dilepton production (NA51, Fermilab E866), and semi-inclusive charged-hadron production (HERMES). It then outlines future measurement strategies using Drell-Yan asymmetries at large $x_F$, W charge asymmetry in $p+p$ collisions at RHIC, and direct measurement of the Gottfried sum at low $x$ via an electron-deuteron collider at HERA.

## Key numbers
- NMC reanalysis (1994) Gottfried sum: $I_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026$, significantly below the symmetric expectation of $1/3$.
- NA51 Drell-Yan ratio at $x=0.18$: $\bar u/\bar d = 0.51 \pm 0.04\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.05\ (\text{syst.})$.
- Next-to-leading-order QCD correction to the Gottfried sum is only 0.03552 $(\alpha_s/\pi)$ for $n_f=3$, contributing about 0.3% at $Q^2=4$ GeV$^2$.
- HERMES semi-inclusive data in the range $0.1 < x < 0.3$ agree with the NMC flavor asymmetry.

## Context
This talk extends the NMC discovery (1991) of Gottfried sum rule violation and the NA51 confirmation (1994) of $\bar u/\bar d$ asymmetry at a single $x$ point. It discusses the meson-cloud model as the most promising theoretical explanation, where virtual mesons (e.g., $p \rightarrow \pi^+ n$) produce an excess of $\bar d$ over $\bar u$, and notes that Pauli-blocking effects appear insufficient. The paper enables future experimental programs at Fermilab E866, RHIC, and a possible HERA electron-deuteron collider to map the $x$ dependence of the asymmetry and test the small-$x$ contribution to the Gottfried sum down to $x=10^{-4}-10^{-5}$.
