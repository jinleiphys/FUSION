# Lattice QCD at Imaginary Chemical Potential in the Chiral Limit

> We report on an ongoing study on the interplay between Roberge-Weiss (RW) and chiral transitions in simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD with an imaginary chemical potential. We established that the RW endpoint belongs to the 3-$d$, $Z_2$ universality class when calculations are done with the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action in the RW plane with physical quark masses. We also have explored a range of quark masses corresponding to pion mass values, $m_π\geq40$~MeV and found that the transition is consistent with $Z_2$ universality class. We argue that observables that were usually used to determine the chiral phase transition temperature, e.g. the chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility, are sensitive to the RW transition and are energy-like observables for the $Z_2$ transition, contrary to the magnetic-like (order parameter) behavior at vanishing chemical potential. Moreover the calculations performed at $m_π\sim40$~MeV also put a stringent constraint for a critical pion mass at zero chemical potential for a possible first-order chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The Roberge-Weiss (RW) endpoint in (2+1)-flavor QCD with the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action is consistent with the 3-d, Z2 universality class down to pion masses of approximately 40 MeV, but the chiral and RW transitions may coincide in the chiral limit, potentially leading to a different universality class than either Z2 or O(N).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD with HISQ fermions and tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on Nτ=4 lattices, working on the first RW plane at imaginary chemical potential μ/T=π/3. They varied the light quark mass from ms/27 to ms/320 (corresponding to pion masses from 135 to 40 MeV) while keeping the strange quark mass fixed at its physical value. Finite-size scaling analyses of the Polyakov loop order parameter, its susceptibility, and the Binder cumulant were performed using 3-d, Z2 universal scaling functions, and chiral observables (the renormalization-group-invariant chiral condensate Δls and the disconnected chiral susceptibility χdisc) were examined for their sensitivity to the RW transition.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass values explored: mπ ≥ 40 MeV, corresponding to light quark masses from ms/27 to ms/320
- RW endpoint temperature TRW for ms/27: 201.05(93) MeV
- RW endpoint temperature TRW for ms/160: 195.80(11) MeV
- RW endpoint temperature TRW for ms/320: 194.97(17) MeV
- Critical exponents used from 3-d Z2 universality class: α=0.1088, β=0.3258, γ=1.2396, ν=0.6304
- Chiral pseudocritical temperature from χdisc peak at ms/320: 194.6(6) MeV (Nσ=32)
- Chiral pseudocritical temperature from Δls inflection point at ms/320: 195.9(7) MeV (Nσ=32)
- Upper bound on critical pion mass for possible first-order chiral transition at zero chemical potential: mcrit ≤ 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Goswami et al. (2018, 2019) investigating the chiral transition from the perspective of the RW plane, and is consistent with findings by Bonati et al. (2018). The results constrain the possible first-order chiral phase transition scenario in (2+1)-flavor QCD, which was suggested by earlier coarse lattice studies with unimproved staggered fermions (Karsch et al., 2001) but contradicted by more recent improved discretization results (Bazavov et al., 2017). The finding that chiral observables become energy-like (rather than magnetic-like) near the RW endpoint enables a new approach to studying the interplay between chiral and Roberge-Weiss transitions, and the upper bound on mcrit at zero chemical potential guides future searches for a possible first-order chiral transition.
