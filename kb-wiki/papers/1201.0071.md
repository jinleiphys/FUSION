# Fine structure of charge-exchange spin-dipole excitations in $^{16}$O

> The charge-exchange spin-dipole (SD) excitations for both $(p,n)$ and $(n,p)$ channels in $^{16}$O are investigated in the fully self-consistent random phase approximation based on the covariant density functional theory. The fine structure of SD excitations in the most up-to-date $^{16}$O($\vec p, \vec n$)$^{16}$F experiment is excellently reproduced without any readjustment in the functional. The characteristics of SD excitations are understood with the delicate balance between the $σ$- and $ω$-meson fields via the exchange terms. The fine structure of SD excitations for $^{16}$O($n,p$)$^{16}$N channel is predicted for future experiments.

# Fine structure of charge-exchange spin-dipole excitations in $^{16}$O

## Key claim
The fine structure of spin-dipole excitations in the $^{16}$O($\vec p, \vec n$)$^{16}$F experiment is excellently reproduced by the fully self-consistent random phase approximation based on covariant density functional theory without any readjustment of the functional, but the agreement depends on the inclusion of exchange terms from the $\sigma$- and $\omega$-meson fields; without these exchange terms, the mixture of $J^\pi=1^-$ and $2^-$ excitations at $E_x\approx9.5$ MeV is missing and the $J^\pi=1^-$ resonances are too high in energy.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) calculations built on the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory, using the PKO1 covariant density functional which was fitted to nuclear ground-state properties and contains no free parameters for excitations. The particle-hole residual interactions were derived from the second derivative of the same energy functional, including exchange terms from $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, and $\pi$ mesons. The radial Dirac equations were solved in coordinate space within a spherical box of radius 25 fm and mesh size 0.1 fm, with single-particle energy truncations from $-M$ to $M+100$ MeV where $M$ is the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- The low-lying SD triplets ($0^-_1$, $1^-_1$, $2^-_1$) are found at $E_x\approx0$ MeV in the $T_-$ channel, consistent with known states from Tilley (1993).
- The main SD resonance at $E_x\approx7.5$ MeV and a "shoulder" at $E_x\approx6$ MeV (which could not be described by shell model calculations in Wakasa (2011)) are both reproduced as $J^\pi=2^-$ states.
- Broad SD resonances are found at $E_x\approx9.5$ and $11\sim13$ MeV, where the former is dominated by $J^\pi=2^-$ and the latter by $J^\pi=1^-$ components.
- For the $T_+$ channel ($^{16}$O($n,p$)$^{16}$N), the main resonance dominated by $J^\pi=2^-$ appears at $E_x\approx8.3$ MeV with a shoulder at $E_x\approx6.5$ MeV, and a broad giant resonance at $E_x=11\sim15$ MeV is predicted.
- The $J^\pi=0^-$ resonances beyond $E_x=10$ MeV are predicted to be fragmented in the range $E_x=12\sim18$ MeV with a peak at $E_x\approx14.5$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the fully self-consistent RHF+RPA approach previously applied to Gamow-Teller resonances in $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb by Liang (2008) to the spin-dipole excitations in $^{16}$O. It resolves a discrepancy between experimental data from Wakasa (2011) and shell model calculations for the $J^\pi=2^-$ "shoulder" structure at $E_x\approx6$ MeV. The results demonstrate that explicit tensor interactions, as used in Skyrme HF+RPA calculations by Bai (2011), are not necessary to reproduce the data; instead the delicate balance between $\sigma$- and $\omega$-meson exchange terms suffices. The predictions for the $T_+$ channel provide guidance for future experiments with polarized beams.
