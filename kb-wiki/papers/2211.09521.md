# Quantification of the hadronic CP violation contribution to the atomic EDMs

> CP violating interactions are required to realize the matter abundance of our Universe. It is however known that the standard model of particle physics does not contain sufficient CP violation. The electric dipole moment (EDM) of atomic systems is a very sensitive experimental probe of CP violation beyond the standard model, and it is very actively studied in experiments. The atomic EDM has a significant sensitivity to hadronic CP violation, but its quantification has for long been obstructed by the nonperturbative physics of quantum chromodynamics. Quite recently, the contribution of the Weinberg operator (CP violating three-gluon interaction) to the atomic EDM has been analyzed, and we are almost attaining the quantification era of the CP violating hadronic interaction in the leading order of standard model effective field theory. In this proceedings contribution, we summarize the current attempt to quantify the hadronic CP violation contribution to the EDM of atoms.

# Quantification of the hadronic CP violation contribution to the atomic EDMs

## Key claim

The hadronic CP violation contribution to atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) is approaching a quantification era, but only for the leading order of standard model effective field theory; the Weinberg operator contribution has been recently analyzed, while the quark chromo-EDM matrix elements still have uncertainties of order 100% and the pion-nucleon sigma term shows a factor-of-two discrepancy between phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD.

## Method

The author reviews the current theoretical calculations of hadronic matrix elements of CP-odd quark-gluon operators that contribute to the nucleon EDM and to the CP-odd pion-nucleon interaction. For each operator (quark EDM, theta-term, CP-odd four-quark interaction, Weinberg operator, and quark chromo-EDM), the author summarizes the methods used to derive the hadron-level couplings, including lattice QCD, chiral perturbation theory, QCD sum rules, and factorization with vacuum saturation approximation. The review also covers the status of the pion-nucleon sigma term, which is a key input for these calculations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM from quark EDM: \( d_n \approx 0.8 d_d - 0.2 d_u \), with about 10% error (lattice QCD average).
- Neutron EDM from theta-term (chiral perturbation leading order): \( d_n \approx -0.0025 \theta e \, \text{fm} \); lattice QCD result: \( d_n = -0.00152 (71) \theta e \, \text{fm} \).
- Neutron EDM from Weinberg operator: \( d_n = (20 \pm 12) w \, e \, \text{MeV} \).
- Isovector CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling from chromo-EDM: \( \bar g_{\pi NN}^{(1)} \approx -1.5 \times 10^{-11} \frac{d_u^c - d_d^c}{10^{-26} \, \text{cm}} \), with O(100%) uncertainty.
- CP-odd four-quark interaction uncertainty from factorization: estimated at O(60%).
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: phenomenological extractions yield approximately 60 MeV; lattice QCD yields approximately 30 MeV.

## Context

This proceedings contribution reviews the quantification of hadronic CP violation for atomic EDM calculations, extending the work of Crewther (1979) on the theta-term contribution and Weinberg (1989) on the three-gluon operator. The author notes that nuclear and atomic level EDM calculations have uncertainties up to 30% for many systems (Yamanaka 2017, Chupp 2017), but the hadron-level matrix elements have much larger errorbars. The review identifies the quark chromo-EDM matrix elements as the most important remaining challenge, and notes that resolving the discrepancy in the pion-nucleon sigma term between phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD (FLAG 2021, Gupta 2021) is crucial for future progress.
