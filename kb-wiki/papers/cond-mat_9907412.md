# The rate of entropy increase at the edge of chaos

> Under certain conditions, the rate of increase of the statistical entropy of a simple, fully chaotic, conservative system is known to be given by a single number, characteristic of this system, the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate. This connection is here generalized to a simple dissipative system, the logistic map, and especially to the chaos threshold of the latter, the edge of chaos. It is found that, in the edge-of-chaos case, the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs-Shannon entropy is not appropriate. Instead, the non-extensive entropy $S_q\equiv \frac{1-\sum_{i=1}^W p_i^q}{q-1}$, must be used. The latter contains a parameter q, the entropic index which must be given a special value $q^*\ne 1$ (for q=1 one recovers the usual entropy) characteristic of the edge-of-chaos under consideration. The same q^* enters also in the description of the sensitivity to initial conditions, as well as in that of the multifractal spectrum of the attractor.

## Key claim

The rate of entropy increase at the edge of chaos is not given by the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs-Shannon entropy but by the non-extensive entropy \(S_q\) with a special entropic index \(q^* \neq 1\), and this same \(q^*\) also describes the power-law sensitivity to initial conditions and the multifractal spectrum of the attractor.

## Method

The authors numerically studied the logistic map \(x_{t+1} = 1 - a x_t^2\) for chaotic regimes (\(a=2\) and \(a=1.6\)) and at the edge of chaos (\(a_c = 1.40115519...\)). They partitioned the interval \([-1,1]\) into \(W\) equal cells, initialized \(N\) points in a single cell, and tracked the time evolution of the probability distribution across cells. They computed the generalized entropy \(S_q(t)\) for various \(q\) values and determined the entropic index \(q^*\) for which \(S_q(t)\) grows linearly with time, using a polynomial fit to quantify the deviation from linearity.

## Key numbers

- For the chaotic case \(a=2\), the Lyapunov exponent is \(\lambda_1 = \ln 2\) and the slope of \(S_1(t)\) equals the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate \(\ln 2\).
- For the chaotic case \(a=1.6\), the fitted slope of \(S_1(t)\) agrees with the predicted KS entropy rate \(0.3578\).
- At the edge of chaos \(a_c = 1.40115519...\), the special entropic index is \(q^* = 0.2445...\), determined from the linear growth of \(S_q(t)\).
- The attractor at the edge of chaos occupies only 844 cells out of a partition of \(W = 10^5\) cells.
- The averaging over initial conditions used 1251 initial cells (out of \(10^5\)) selected by a cutoff of 5000 integrated occupied cells over 50 time steps.

## Context

This work extends the connection between the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate and statistical entropy, previously established for conservative systems by V. Latora and M. Baranger (1999), to dissipative systems and specifically to the edge of chaos. It confirms and unifies earlier findings by Tsallis et al. (1997) that the non-extensive entropy \(S_q\) with \(q^* = 0.2445\) describes the sensitivity to initial conditions and the multifractal spectrum at the chaos threshold. The results enable a unified description of strong (exponential) and weak (power-law) sensitivity to initial conditions through a single parameter \(q\), and suggest that the foundation of statistical mechanics on microscopic dynamics depends on the nature of mixing (exponential vs. algebraic).
