# Large Amplitude Collective Motion in Nuclei and Metallic Clusters: Applicability of adiabatic theory for a pairing model

> A model Hamiltonian describing a two-level system with a crossing plus a pairing force is investigated using technique of large-amplitude collective motion. The collective path, which is determined by the decoupling conditions, is found to be almost identical to the one in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for the case of a strong pairing force. For the weak pairing case, the obtained path describes a diabatic dynamics of the system.

## Key claim

The adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion (ALACM) theory can describe a system even when the conventional Born-Oppenheimer adiabatic approximation completely fails; for weak pairing, the ALACM path yields a diabatic dynamics that qualitatively agrees with the exact solution, while for strong pairing the path matches the Born-Oppenheimer result.

## Method

The authors apply the theory of adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion (ALACM) to a model Hamiltonian consisting of a harmonic oscillator core coupled to a two-level valence space with a pairing interaction. They recast the quantum dynamics into a classical Hamiltonian form using time-dependent mean-field (BCS) theory, then solve the local harmonic equations (LHE) to determine the collective path on the two-dimensional potential surface defined by the core coordinate \(q\) and the valence distribution coordinate \(\xi\). The resulting collective paths are compared with the conventional Born-Oppenheimer approximation and with exact quantum solutions for both symmetric and asymmetric potential landscapes.

## Key numbers

- For the symmetric case (\(\epsilon_0=0\), \(\chi=1\)), two pairing strengths are studied: \(G=0.2\) (weak pairing) and \(G=1.9\) (strong pairing).
- For the asymmetric case, parameters are \(\epsilon=0.4\), \(\chi=0.7\), and \(G=0.1\).
- In the asymmetric case, the exact wave function of the second excited state consists of \(\ket{2\bar 2}\) (99%) and \(\ket{1\bar 1}\) (1%), and the Born-Oppenheimer wave function spreads over a much wider range of \(q\) than the exact solution.

## Context

This work extends the theory of adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion (ALACM) developed in Ref. [1] to a pairing model originally introduced in Ref. [2], addressing the role of pairing in level crossings during shape changes in nuclei and metallic clusters. The results show that ALACM can produce diabatic dynamics when the Born-Oppenheimer approximation fails, which is relevant for understanding phenomena such as nuclear spontaneous fission where pairing affects odd-A lifetimes. The authors plan to provide further technical details in a future publication [4] and envisage applications to fissioning clusters.
