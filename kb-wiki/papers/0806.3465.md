# Direct photon production from hadronic sources in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The low $p_T$ direct photon production from a variety of the hadronic sources is studied within the microscopic HSD transport approach for p+C, p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at 160 A GeV. The direct photon emission from elementary hadronic scatterings as well as meson-meson bremsstrahlung are incorporated. The influence of in-medium effects such as a collisional broadening of the vector-meson spectral functions on the photon emission rate is found to be hardly observable in the final spectra which are dominated by bremsstrahlung type processes. The uncertainties in the subtraction of the 'background' from the photon decay of hadronic resonances inside the hot and dense fireball is investigated, additionally. Our findings are relevant for the interpretation and extraction of experimental data on direct photon production at low $p_T$.

## Key claim

The authors find that in-medium effects such as collisional broadening of vector-meson spectral functions have only a small impact on direct photon production at low transverse momentum, which is dominated by meson-meson bremsstrahlung processes; this conclusion is conditional on the HSD transport model not including a phase transition from QGP to hadronic matter.

## Method

The authors extended the HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics) transport model to include direct photon production from elementary hadronic scatterings (ππ → ργ, πρ → πγ), meson-meson bremsstrahlung using the soft-photon approximation, and photon decays of hadronic resonances (π⁰, η, η′, ω, φ, a₁). They incorporated off-shell dynamics for vector mesons with density-dependent spectral functions and compared their calculations to WA98 experimental data for Pb+Pb, p+C, and p+Pb collisions at 160 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Central 158 A GeV ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions: WA98 data cover transverse momentum interval 0.5 ≤ p_T ≤ 4 GeV/c; HSD calculations extend up to 2.5-3 GeV/c due to limited statistics.
- For the ρ meson collisional broadening coefficient: α_coll ≈ 150 MeV; for the ω meson: α_coll ≈ 70 MeV.
- The a₁ → πγ branching ratio used: Br(a₁ → πγ) ∼ 1.5 × 10⁻³.
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For the dropping mass scenario: α ≈ 0.16 for the ρ meson and α ≈ 0.12 for the ω meson.
- Approximately 25% of ω mesons and ∼70% of a₁ mesons emit photons at densities ρ_N ≥ ρ₀/2 in central Pb+Pb collisions.
- The dominant contribution to meson-meson bremsstrahlung comes from π+π elastic scattering; a 10 mb elastic cross section was used for all meson-meson scatterings.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model studies of direct photon production at SPS energies by Dumitru (1995), the AMPT model, and Bratkovskaya (1997), which compared to WA98 upper limits for S+Au at 200 A GeV. The present calculations use the updated HSD model with off-shell vector meson dynamics and compare to the WA98 Pb+Pb data at 160 A GeV (2000) and preliminary p+C and p+Pb data. The results confirm the importance of meson-meson bremsstrahlung as found by Haglin and by Liu and Rapp (2007), and quantify uncertainties in experimental background subtraction from short-lived resonance decays inside the fireball.
