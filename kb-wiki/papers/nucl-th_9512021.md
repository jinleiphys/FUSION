# Inelastic nucleon contributions in $(e,e^\prime)$ nuclear response functions

> We estimate the contribution of inelastic nucleon excitations to the $(e,e^\prime)$ inclusive cross section in the CEBAF kinematic range. Calculations are based upon parameterizations of the nucleon structure functions measured at SLAC. Nuclear binding effects are included in a vector-scalar field theory, and are assumed have a minimal effect on the nucleon excitation spectrum. We find that for $q\lsim 1$ GeV the elastic and inelastic nucleon contributions to the nuclear response functions are comparable, and can be separated, but with roughly a factor of two uncertainty in the latter from the extrapolation from data. In contrast, for $q\rsim 2$ GeV this uncertainty is greatly reduced but the elastic nucleon contribution is heavily dominated by the inelastic nucleon background.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that for momentum transfers \(q \lesssim 1\) GeV, the elastic and inelastic nucleon contributions to nuclear \((e,e^\prime)\) response functions are comparable and can be separated, but with roughly a factor of two uncertainty in the inelastic contribution due to extrapolation from data; for \(q \gtrsim 2\) GeV this uncertainty is greatly reduced, but the elastic nucleon contribution is heavily dominated by the inelastic nucleon background.

## Method

The authors use a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with a Fermi gas momentum distribution for the nuclear target, folding in nucleon structure functions parameterized from SLAC data (Bodek et al. and Whitlow fits). Nuclear binding effects are included via a vector-scalar field theory (quantum hadrodynamics) that introduces an effective nucleon mass \(M^*\), and the off-shell extrapolation is chosen so that the longitudinal response vanishes at \(Q^2=0\). The inelastic nucleon background is computed for the longitudinal and transverse response functions at three-momentum transfers of 1, 2, and 4 GeV/c and compared to the quasielastic peak.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum \(p_F = 0.257\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding roughly to \({}^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\).
- Free nucleon mass \(M = 938.92\ \mathrm{MeV}\); reduced mass \(M^*/M = 0.648\) for bound nucleons.
- At \(|\mathbf{q}| = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\), the Bodek fit gives an inelastic background roughly half as large as the Whitlow fit; the two fits differ by nearly a factor of two for protons at this momentum.
- At \(|\mathbf{q}| = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\), the inelastic background is much larger compared to the quasielastic peak, and the two fits are in much closer agreement.
- The Bodek fit covers the kinematic range \(1.0 \le Q^2 \le 20\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(0.1 \le x \le 0.77\); the Whitlow fit covers \(0.6 \le Q^2 \le 30\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(0.06 \le x \le 0.9\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the nuclear \((e,e^\prime)\) response to higher momentum transfers relevant to the CEBAF facility, where relativistic effects and nucleon internal excitations become important. It builds on the Fermi smearing formalism of West (1975) and Bodek and Ritchie (1981), but uses a different off-shell prescription to maintain the correct behavior of the longitudinal response at \(Q^2=0\). The results enable quantitative estimates of the inelastic nucleon background that must be subtracted to extract nuclear structure information from inclusive electron scattering data at CEBAF, and highlight the need for better data on nucleon structure functions at lower \(Q^2\) and \(\omega\).
