# A Neutral Atom and a Charged Wire: From Elastic scattering to Absorption

> We solve the problem of a neutral atom interacting with a charged wire, giving rise to an attractive 1/r^2 potential in two dimensions. We show how a suitable average over all possible self-adjoint extensions of the radial Schroedinger Hamiltonian eventually leads to the classical formula for absorption of the atom, a formula shown to be in agreement with a recent experiment.

# A Neutral Atom and a Charged Wire: From Elastic Scattering to Absorption

## Key claim
The classical absorption formula for a neutral atom falling into a charged wire can be derived from the quantum mechanical elastic scattering solutions of the Schrödinger equation by averaging the S-matrix over all possible self-adjoint extensions of the radial Hamiltonian, rather than introducing ad hoc complex phase shifts. This derivation resolves the apparent difficulty of formulating the quantum mechanics of an attractive 1/r² potential in two dimensions.

## Method
The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation for a neutral atom of mass M and polarizability α interacting with a charged wire of line charge density λ, which produces an attractive 1/r² potential. They construct the partial wave S-matrix S_m(γ) for each self-adjoint extension characterized by a phase γ, then average S_m(γ) over all γ from 0 to 2π to obtain ⟨S_m⟩ = e^{-νπ}, where ν² = 4αλ²M - m². The absorption cross-section is computed from the averaged S-matrix using the standard partial wave expansion, and the classical limit is taken by converting the sum over angular momentum quantum numbers m into an integral.

## Key numbers
- The averaged partial wave S-matrix is ⟨S_m⟩ = e^{-νπ}, where ν² = 4αλ²M - m².
- The classical absorption cross-section derived is σ_abs = 4λ√(αM)/k = 2√(4α/M · λ²/v²), where k = Mv.
- The classical limit requires μ = 2λ√(αM) ≫ 1, where μ is the integer part of 2λ√(αM) that limits the sum over m.
- For a finite wire radius R_w, the classical absorption cross-section becomes σ_abs = 2√(R_w² + (4α/M)(λ²/v²)).

## Context
This work extends the experimental study of cold neutral atoms scattered by a thin charged wire reported in PRL_98, where the absorption was successfully described by classical equations of motion. The authors provide a rigorous quantum mechanical foundation for that classical result, contrasting with earlier approaches that introduced ad hoc complex phase shifts (DS_97, ASV_99) or claimed mathematical difficulties with the attractive 1/r² potential (Hagen). The averaging procedure follows a method suggested by Radin for Green functions, here applied to S-matrix elements in two dimensions, and the classical limit treatment follows Alliluev in three dimensions. The result enables a unified quantum-to-classical description of absorption by singular potentials and resolves questions about the physical interpretation of self-adjoint extensions.
