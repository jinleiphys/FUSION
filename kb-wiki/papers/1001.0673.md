# Breaking and restoring symmetries within the nuclear energy density functional method

> We review the notion of symmetry breaking and restoration within the frame of nuclear energy density functional methods. We focus on key differences between wave-function- and energy-functional-based methods. In particular, we point to difficulties to formulate the restoration of symmetries within the energy functional framework. The problems tackled recently in connection with particle-number restoration serve as a baseline to the present discussion. Reaching out to angular-momentum restoration, we identify an exact mathematical property of the energy density $E^{LM}(\vec{R})$ that could be used to constrain energy density functional kernels. Consequently, we suggest possible routes towards a better formulation of symmetry restorations within energy density functional methods.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while symmetry breaking and restoration are essential in nuclear energy density functional (EDF) methods, the multi-reference EDF (MR-EDF) extension suffers from fundamental formal difficulties compared to wave-function-based methods, because the energy kernel in EDF is not derived from a genuine Hamiltonian operator. The authors identify an exact mathematical property of the energy density \(E^{LM}(\vec{R})\) that could constrain EDF kernels for angular-momentum restoration, but this remains a suggestion rather than a proven solution.

## Method

The authors review the group-theoretic formulation of symmetry breaking and restoration for continuous compact groups (specifically \(U(1)\) for particle number and \(SO(3)\) for angular momentum), contrasting wave-function-based methods (where energy kernels are strict matrix elements of a state-independent Hamiltonian) with EDF methods (where energy kernels are functionals of one-body density matrices computed from symmetry-breaking product states). They analyze the difficulties that arise when extending the single-reference EDF (SR-EDF) functional to the non-diagonal energy kernel required for multi-reference EDF (MR-EDF) calculations, using particle-number restoration as a baseline and extending the discussion to angular-momentum restoration.

## Key numbers

* Correlation energy from spontaneous symmetry breaking and restoration in \(^{240}\)Pu and \(^{120}\)Sn ground states can reach up to 20 MeV out of about 2 GeV binding energy, i.e., about 2% of the total binding energy.
* Symmetry restoration adds a few MeV correlation energy to the ground-state binding energy of heavy nuclei, which is significant compared to the few hundred keV targeted accuracy on nuclear masses.
* Bulk correlations are estimated at \(\sim 8A\) MeV, static collective correlations at \(\lesssim 25\) MeV, and dynamical collective correlations at \(\lesssim 5\) MeV, where \(A\) is the mass number.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of symmetry breaking and restoration in nuclear EDF methods, building on prior studies of particle-number restoration pathologies and proposed cures by Lacroix (2009), Bender (2009), and Duguet (2009). It contrasts the EDF approach with wave-function-based methods such as symmetry-unrestricted and symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximations (Ring 1980). The paper suggests that the identified mathematical property of the energy density \(E^{LM}(\vec{R})\) could provide new constraints for MR-EDF calculations involving angular-momentum restoration, but notes that further work is needed to extend the Hohenberg-Kohn-like theorem (Engel 2007, Messud 2009) from translational symmetry to rotational and particle-number symmetries.
