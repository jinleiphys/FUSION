# Spin Response of the Nucleon in the Resonance Region

> I discuss recent results from Jefferson Lab on the measurement of inclusive spin structure functions in the nucleon resonance region using polarized electron beams and polarized targets. Results on the first moment of the spin structure function for protons and neutrons are discussed, as well as the Bjorken integral. I will argue that the helicity structure of individual resonances plays a vital role in understanding the nucleon's spin response in the domain of strong interaction QCD, and must be considered in any analysis of the nucleon spin structure at low and intermediate photon virtuality.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the helicity structure of individual nucleon resonances plays a vital role in understanding the nucleon's spin response in the domain of strong interaction QCD, and must be considered in any analysis of nucleon spin structure at low and intermediate photon virtuality, a nuance glossed over in the abstract's general claim.

## Method

Inclusive double polarization experiments were carried out at Jefferson Lab using polarized electron beams and polarized targets (NH3 for protons, ND3 and 3He for neutrons) with the CLAS and Hall A detectors. The experimental double polarization asymmetry was measured and used to extract the spin structure function g1(x,Q2) via a relation involving the unpolarized structure function F1 and the asymmetries A1 and A2. The first moment Γ1(Q2) was then computed by integrating g1 over x, using a model parameterization to extrapolate to x=0.

## Key numbers

*   The first moment Γ1p(Q2) for the proton shows a zero crossing near Q2 = 0.25 GeV2.
*   The helicity asymmetry A1 for the D13(1520) resonance transition changes from helicity 3/2 dominance at the real photon point to helicity 1/2 dominance at Q2 > 0.5 GeV2.
*   The asymmetry A1+ηA2 for protons reaches peak values of about 0.6 at Q2=0.8 GeV2 and W=1.5 GeV.
*   Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) predicts the Q2 evolution of the Bjorken integral with a Q4 coefficient of 1.31 (GeV2) for the proton-neutron difference, compared to 6.85 (GeV2) for the proton and 5.54 (GeV2) for the neutron separately.
*   The Bjorken sum rule has been verified at the 5% level at finite Q2 values using pQCD evolution.

## Context

This work extends measurements of polarized structure functions from the deep inelastic regime (pioneered by the EMC experiment at CERN and follow-up experiments) into the low Q2 regime and the domain of nucleon resonances, which is the true domain of strong QCD. The results test the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule at finite Q2 and the Bjorken sum rule at low Q2, and enable tests of Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) predictions by Ji and Osborne, as well as lattice QCD calculations of resonance contributions. The data also allow exploration of quark-hadron duality for the spin structure function g1, a phenomenon previously observed for the unpolarized structure function F1 by Bloom and Gilman.
