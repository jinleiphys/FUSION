# Insights into the gamma* N -> Delta transition

> The γ* N -> Δ(1232) transition is a window on hadron shape deformation, the applicability of perturbative QCD at moderate momentum transfers, and the influence of nonperturbative phenomena on hadronic observables. We explain that the Ash-convention magnetic transition form factor must fall faster than the neutron's magnetic form factor and nonzero values for the associated quadrupole ratios reveal the impact of quark orbital angular momentum within the nucleon and Δ(1232); and show that these quadrupole ratios do approach their predicted asymptotic limits, albeit slowly.

## Key claim

The Ash-convention magnetic transition form factor for the γ*N → Δ(1232) transition must fall faster than the neutron's magnetic form factor, contrary to widespread expectations; the quadrupole ratios do approach their predicted asymptotic limits, but only at truly large momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) treatment using a vector×vector contact interaction (CI) to compute the three Poincaré-invariant transition form factors (magnetic-dipole G_M*, electric quadrupole G_E*, and Coulomb quadrupole G_C*) for the γ*N → Δ transition. The calculation includes one-loop diagrams describing photon coupling to a dressed quark, to a diquark, and inducing scalar-to-axial-vector diquark transitions, with Faddeev amplitudes for the nucleon and Δ obtained from the same framework.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass M = 0.368 GeV, obtained from rainbow gap equation with current-quark mass m = 7 MeV
- Interaction strength α_IR = 0.93π, confinement scale Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV, ultraviolet cutoff Λ_uv = 0.905 GeV
- Direct result for reduced magnetic moment: μ̃*_NΔ = 1.13; ameliorated value: μ̃*_NΔ = 2.04
- Ratio of axial(Δ)-axial(N) to axial(Δ)-scalar(N) contributions to G_M*(0): 0.85/0.18 for CI, compared with 0.96/1.27 from momentum-dependent DSE kernels (Eichmann 2011)
- Meson-cloud effects provide more than 30% of the form factor for Q² ≲ 2m_ρ²

## Context

This work extends DSE-based studies of baryon structure (Wilson 2011, Chen 2012) to the γ*N → Δ transition, contradicting SU(6) symmetry expectations (Beg 1964) that the magnetic transition form factor should match the neutron's magnetic form factor. The results are compared with the Sato-Lee dynamical meson-exchange model (Julia-Diaz 2006) and data from CLAS (Aznauryan 2009). The analysis enables quantitative separation of dressed-quark core contributions from meson-cloud effects in resonance electroproduction, and demonstrates that helicity conservation arguments (Carlson 1985) for asymptotic quadrupole ratios are valid but require truly large Q² to become apparent.
