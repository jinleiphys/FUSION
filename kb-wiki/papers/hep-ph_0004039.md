# A consistent nonperturbative approach to thermal damping-rates

> We propose a nonperturbative scheme for the calculation of thermal damping-rates using exact renormalization group (RG)-equations. Special emphasis is put on the thermal RG where first results for the rate were given in M. Pietroni, Phys. Rev. Lett. 81 (1998), 2424. We point out that in order to obtain a complete result that also reproduces the known perturbative behaviour one has to take into account effects that were neglected in the work cited above. We propose a well-defined way of doing the calculations that reproduces perturbation theory in lowest order but goes considerably beyond perturbative results and should be applicable also at second order phase-transitions.

# A consistent nonperturbative approach to thermal damping-rates

## Key claim

The authors propose a nonperturbative scheme using exact renormalization group (RG) equations that reproduces two-loop perturbation theory as the leading term for thermal damping-rates in scalar field theory, while also being applicable at second-order phase transitions. This corrects an earlier approach by Pietroni (1998) that neglected certain contributions needed to recover the known perturbative behavior.

## Method

The authors formulate a thermal renormalization group (TRG) equation within the Schwinger-Keldysh (CTP) real-time formalism, introducing a momentum cutoff that suppresses soft thermal modes while leaving hard modes unchanged. They derive coupled flow equations for the imaginary parts of the two-point and four-point functions in massless phi^4 theory, making a quasiparticle approximation that neglects imaginary parts on the right-hand side of the flow equations. The system is solved numerically with boundary conditions that include the zero-temperature one-loop imaginary part of the four-point function, which was omitted in prior work.

## Context

This work extends the thermal RG approach of Pietroni (1998) by including the zero-temperature one-loop imaginary part of the four-point function as a boundary condition, which was previously neglected. The method reproduces the two-loop perturbative result of Wang and Heinz for the damping-rate while going beyond standard daisy and superdaisy resummations. The approach enables nonperturbative calculation of both linear-response and static quantities in the critical regime, and can be extended to theories with fermionic degrees of freedom as shown in prior work by the authors, with potential applications to nuclear matter near the chiral phase transition.
