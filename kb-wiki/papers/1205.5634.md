# Higher moment singularities explored by the net proton non-statistical fluctuations

> We use the non-statistical fluctuation instead of the full one to explore the higher moment singularities of net proton event distributions in the relativistic Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ from 11.5 to 200 GeV calculated by the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. The PACIAE results of mean ($M$), variance ($σ^2$), skewness ($S$), and kurtosis ($κ$) are consistent with the corresponding STAR data. Non-statistical moments are calculated as the difference between the moments derived from real events and the ones from mixed events, which are constructed by combining particles randomly selected from different real events. An evidence of singularity at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim$ 60 GeV is first seen in the energy dependent non-statistical $S$ and $Sσ$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a singularity in the energy dependence of net-proton higher moments is first seen at approximately 60 GeV, but only when using non-statistical fluctuations (the difference between real-event moments and mixed-event moments) rather than the full fluctuations that have been studied previously.

## Method
The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE to generate real events for Au+Au collisions at five beam energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV. They constructed mixed events by randomly combining particles from different real events while preserving the event multiplicity distribution. Non-statistical moments were then calculated as the difference between moments derived from real events and those from mixed events, isolating dynamical correlations from statistical fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: 11.5, 19.6, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Total number of generated real events: 3.0 × 10^5 at 11.5 and 19.6 GeV, 2.4 × 10^5 at 39 GeV, and 1.2 × 10^5 at 62.4 and 200 GeV
- The non-statistical skewness (S), kurtosis (κ), Sσ, and κσ² all change sign at approximately 60 GeV
- STAR acceptance cuts used: |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c
- The critical temperature from lattice QCD comparison with RHIC BES data is cited as T_c = 175^{+1}_{-7} MeV

## Context
This work extends the search for the QCD critical point by analyzing higher moments of net-proton distributions, following experimental efforts by the STAR collaboration (2010) and theoretical predictions by Stephanov (2009) and Asakawa (2000) that higher moments are sensitive to critical fluctuations. The PACIAE model results for full moments are consistent with STAR data, but the non-statistical approach reveals sign changes not visible in the full fluctuations. The authors note that the sign changes in skewness and Sσ may correspond to predictions from Csernai (2006) about skewness changing sign during hadronization, and the results need confirmation from STAR Beam Energy Scan non-statistical fluctuation data.
