# Local Duality and Charge Symmetry Violation in Quark Distributions

> We use local quark-hadron duality to calculate the nucleon structure function as seen by neutrino and muon beams. Our result indicates a possible signal of charge symmetry violation at the parton level in the very large x region.

## Key claim

Using local quark-hadron duality to calculate the nucleon structure function as seen by neutrino and muon beams, the ratio of the iso-scalar structure functions deviates from the value of 1 predicted by leading-order QCD with charge symmetry, indicating a possible signal of charge symmetry violation at the parton level in the very large x region, though the calculation should not be trusted for x below about 0.78 where Q^2 is less than 1 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors used local quark-hadron duality to relate the scaling structure functions F2 for neutrino-nucleon and muon-nucleon scattering to the elastic contributions from the nucleon pole, expressed in terms of nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors. They derived expressions for F2^nuN and F2^muN by taking derivatives of the duality integrals, and then evaluated the ratio 5F2^nuN/18F2^muN using a world data parametrization of the form factors. The resulting deviation from unity was interpreted in terms of charge symmetry violation parameters delta u and delta d for valence quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation should not be trusted for x = x_th less than approximately 0.78, where Q^2 is less than 1 GeV^2.
- At x = x_th approximately 0.9, Q^2 is about 2.5 GeV^2 and W^2 is about 1.25 GeV^2, a region where local quark-hadron duality has more chances of being respected.
- The experimental uncertainty on the elastic form factors is around 10 percent in the Q^2 approximately 2 GeV^2 region.
- The difference between F2^nuN and F2^muN normalized by their total contribution shows a signal of about 27 percent at x = x_th approximately 0.9, indicating possible charge symmetry breaking even allowing a 20 percent error from local duality uncertainty and form factor determination.

## Context

This work extends the study of local quark-hadron duality triggered by high quality JLAB data from Niculescu et al. (2000) on the proton structure function, building on the foundational ideas of Bloom and Gilman (1970) and the QCD justification by De Rujula, Georgi and Politzer (1977). The results enable further investigation of charge symmetry violation effects in the determination of the Weinberg angle as measured by the NuTeV collaboration, with Londergan and Thomas (2004) having analyzed the impact of such violation; the authors note that their result would make the NuTeV anomaly larger, making the need for physics beyond the standard model more pressing.
