# Amorphous state in the mixed phase of quark-hadron phase transition in protoneutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark mixed phase in protoneutron stars, where neutrinos are trapped and lepton number becomes a conserved quantity besides the baryon number and electric charge. Considering protoneutron-star matter as a ternary system, the Gibbs conditions are applied together with the Coulomb interaction. We find that there no crystalline ("pasta") structure appears in the regime of high lepton-number fraction; the size of pasta becomes very large and the geometrical structure becomes mechanically unstable due to the charge screening effect. Consequently the whole system is separated into two bulk regions like an amorphous state, where the surface effect is safely neglected. There, the local charge neutrality is approximately attained, so that the equation of state is effectively reduced to the one for a binary system. Hence, we conclude that there is no possibility for the density discontinuity to appear in protoneutron-star matter, which is a specific feature in a pure system. These features are important when considering astrophysical phenomena such as supernova explosions or radiation of the gravitational wave from protoneutron stars.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark mixed phase in protoneutron stars does not form crystalline ("pasta") structures at high lepton-number fraction; instead, the system separates into two bulk regions like an amorphous state, so that there is no density discontinuity in the equation of state, contrary to what a pure system would show.

## Method

The authors treat protoneutron-star matter as a ternary system with three conserved quantities (baryon number, electric charge, and lepton number) and apply the Gibbs conditions together with the Coulomb interaction. They use the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach for the hadronic phase (including hyperons) and a density-dependent bag model for the quark phase, solving the coupled equations for particle densities and the Coulomb potential within Wigner-Seitz cells for various pasta geometries.

## Key numbers

- The range of lepton-number fraction considered is \(0.05 < Y_l < 0.4\), and the temperature range is \(0 < T < 30\) MeV.
- For the quark phase, the strange quark current mass is taken as \(m_s = 150\) MeV.
- The surface tension parameter is set to \(\sigma_S = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\); pasta structures do not appear for \(\sigma_S > 70\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\).
- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The minimum point in the free energy as a function of cell size disappears beyond \(Y_l \sim 0.2\), indicating the onset of the amorphous state.
- The Wigner-Seitz cell radius is fixed at \(R_W = 100\) fm for evaluating the equation of state in the amorphous state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase with finite-size effects (Maruyama 2007, Yasutake 2009) to protoneutron stars, where neutrinos are trapped. It contradicts the assumption of Pagliara (2009) and Hempel (2009) that a bulk Gibbs calculation with local charge neutrality is sufficient, by showing that the finite-size effects lead to an amorphous state rather than pasta structures. The results are important for modeling supernova explosions and gravitational wave radiation from protoneutron stars, and they call for numerical studies of thermal and chemical evolution including neutrino transport (Fischer 2009).
