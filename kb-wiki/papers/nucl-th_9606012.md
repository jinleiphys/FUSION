# Perturbation of Embedded Eigenvalue by a Near-Lying Resonance

> The case of quantum-mechanical system (including electronic molecules) is considered where Hamiltonian allows a separation, in particular by the Faddeev method, of a weakly coupled channel. Width (i.e. the imaginary part) of the resonance generated by a discrete spectrum eigenvalue of the separated channel is studied in the case where main part of the Hamiltonian gives itself another resonance. It is shown that if real parts of these resonances coincide and, at the same time, a coupling between the separated and main channels is sufficiently small then the width of the resonance generated by the separated (molecular) channel is inversely proportional to the width of the main (nuclear) channel resonance. This phenomenon being a kind of universal law, may play an important role increasing the ``cold fusion'' probability in electronic molecules whose nuclear constituents have narrow pre-threshold resonances.

## Key claim

If the real parts of a "nuclear" resonance and a "molecular" eigenvalue coincide, and the coupling between the separated molecular channel and the main nuclear channel is sufficiently small, then the width of the resulting molecular resonance is inversely proportional to the width of the nuclear resonance, meaning a narrower nuclear resonance produces a wider molecular resonance.

## Method

The authors analyze abstract three-channel and two-channel Hamiltonian matrix models, where a main channel with continuous spectrum is coupled to one or two discrete channels representing nuclear and molecular subsystems. They solve the spectral problem for these Hamiltonians by deriving dispersion equations whose solutions in unphysical energy sheets correspond to resonances, and they examine the behavior of these solutions when the real parts of the nuclear and molecular resonances coincide and the coupling constants are small. The analysis is extended to a more general Hamiltonian of the form H = h₀ + v₁ + v₂, motivated by the Faddeev method for separating weakly coupled channels in molecular systems.

## Key numbers

- The molecular width Γ_R^(m) is approximately 4·|b₁₂|²/Γ_R^(1) when λ₂ coincides with E_R^(1), where Γ_R^(1) is the nuclear width and b₁₂ is the coupling between channels 1 and 2.
- The ratio Γ_R^(m)/Γ_R^(2) is approximately (C_a/c_𝒟)/Γ_R^(1), where Γ_R^(2) is the molecular width in absence of the nuclear resonance, C_a is a residue constant, and c_𝒟 is a lower bound on the imaginary part of the regularized resolvent matrix element.
- The condition for the effect to occur requires Γ_R^(2) ≪ c_𝒟^(2) (Γ_R^(1))², where c_𝒟^(2) is a positive constant depending on the domain 𝒟 and the structure functions.
- The coupling constant |b₁₂| is of order ‖b₀₂‖, and the condition C_𝒟‖b₀₁‖ < 1 ensures the nuclear resonances are narrow.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of resonance phenomena in coupled-channel quantum systems, building on the Faddeev method (Faddeev, 1963) and prior studies of embedded eigenvalues and resonances (Albeverio, 1972; Reed and Simon, 1978). The authors specifically reference earlier work by Belyaev, Motovilov, and collaborators (BMS) that estimated overlap integrals for molecules LiD and H₂O, and they note the well-known example of muon-catalyzed fusion in dtμ molecules (Breunlich et al.) where a near-threshold nuclear resonance plays a decisive role. The results suggest that narrow pre-threshold nuclear resonances in systems such as p p ¹⁶O and p ¹⁷O (the nuclear constituents of water molecules containing ¹⁷O) could significantly enhance the decay rate of molecular states, potentially increasing the probability of cold fusion in electronic molecules.
