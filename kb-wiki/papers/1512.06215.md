# The effect of momentum deposition during fireball evolution on flow anisotropy

> The highest LHC energies give rise to production of many pairs of hard partons which deposit four-momentum into the expanding fireball matter. We argue that it is necessary to include momentum deposition during fireball evolution into 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of the collision. This influence cannot be accounted for simply by modifying initial conditions of the simulation. The resulting contribution to flow anisotropy is correlated with the fireball geometry and causes an increase of the elliptic flow in non-central collisions. The results are presented for various scenarios with energy and momentum deposition to clearly demonstrate this effect.

# The effect of momentum deposition during fireball evolution on flow anisotropy

## Key claim

Momentum deposition from hard partons into the expanding fireball matter *during* hydrodynamic evolution produces measurable flow anisotropy that cannot be reproduced by depositing the same total energy and momentum entirely into the initial conditions, even when both energy and momentum are included in the initial state. The effect persists for ultra-central collisions where initial-state spatial anisotropies of all orders vanish.

## Method

The authors used an ideal event-by-event three-dimensional hydrodynamic model with the SHASTA algorithm, employing smooth optical Glauber initial conditions with a lattice-inspired nuclear equation of state. Hard parton pairs were generated back-to-back in transverse momentum using a parametrized gluon cross-section, with energy loss scaling with entropy density; source terms in the energy-momentum conservation equation described deposition of four-momentum during evolution, and freeze-out at T = 150 MeV was processed via the Cooper-Frye formula using THERMINATOR2 to generate hadrons and extract anisotropic flow coefficients by the two-particle cumulant method.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density ε₀ = 60 GeV/fm³ at proper time τ₀ = 0.55 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV set to 62 mb
- Binary collision fraction α = 0.16 in the initial energy density parametrization
- Longitudinal plateau width η_flat = 10 with σ_η = 0.5
- Reference energy loss dE/dx|₀ = 7 GeV/fm (with s₀ = 78.2/fm³ corresponding to energy density 20.0 GeV/fm³ and T = 324 MeV)
- Hard parton production parametrization at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV: B = 14.7 mb/GeV², p₀ = 6 GeV, n = 9.5
- Partons generated with p_t above 3 GeV; average ~10 hard partons per central event
- Gaussian width for energy-momentum deposition σ_i = 0.3 fm (tested at 0.15 fm and 0.6 fm with no major change)
- For dE/dx|₀ → 0 limit, value set to 0.1 GeV/fm; all anisotropic flow coefficients compatible with zero
- In non-central collisions (centrality 30–40%, b = 6–7 fm), v₂ with hard partons grows by about 50% relative to simulation with smooth initial conditions and no hard partons

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Schulc and Tomasik, 2015) which showed that many hard-parton-induced streams contribute to azimuthal anisotropies at LHC energies, and that the magnitude did not significantly depend on the value of dE/dx for the two tested values. The present paper explicitly tests whether the effect can be reproduced by putting all energy and momentum into initial conditions (the dE/dx → ∞ limit), and demonstrates it cannot. This breaks the linear mapping of initial-state spatial anisotropies onto final-state momentum anisotropies reported by Gardim (2012), Niemi (2013), and Gale (2013). The results imply that realistic hydrodynamic simulations must include momentum deposition during evolution, and that conclusions about initial-state fluctuations drawn from simulations omitting this mechanism may need re-evaluation. The authors suggest femtoscopy and Event Shape Sorting as complementary tools to distinguish the effects.
