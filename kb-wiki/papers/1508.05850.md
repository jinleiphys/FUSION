# $χ_{cJ}$ Polarization in Polarized Proton-Proton Collisions at RHIC

> We study inclusive $χ_{cJ}$ production with definite polarizations in polarized proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV and 500 GeV at RHIC by using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) color-octet mechanism. We present results of rapidity distribution of $χ_{c0}$, $χ_{c1}$ and $χ_{c2}$ production with specific polarizations in polarized p-p collisions at RHIC within the PHENIX detector acceptance range. We also present the corresponding results for the spin asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the NRQCD color-octet mechanism can predict the rapidity distributions and spin asymmetries for $\chi_{c0}$, $\chi_{c1}$, and $\chi_{c2}$ production with definite helicity states in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC, but this claim is conditional on the LO analysis considering only production in the forward direction at finite rapidity and neglecting parton fragmentation contributions, which are assumed to be very small at RHIC energies.

## Method

The author used the helicity decomposition method within the NRQCD color-octet mechanism to calculate partonic-level cross sections for the processes $q \bar q \rightarrow \chi_{cJ}(\lambda)$ and $gg \rightarrow \chi_{cJ}(\lambda)$, where $\lambda$ is the helicity of the produced charmonium state. These partonic cross sections were then folded with the GRSV polarized parton distribution functions and GRV unpolarized parton densities at LO, using a charm quark mass of 1.5 GeV and a mass factorization scale equal to $2m$, to obtain rapidity distributions and longitudinal spin asymmetries $A_{LL}$ for $\chi_{c0}$, $\chi_{c1}$, and $\chi_{c2}$ production within the PHENIX detector acceptance range.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass set to $m = 1.5$ GeV, with mass factorization scale equal to $2m$.
* Non-perturbative matrix element for $\chi_{c0}$: $<{\cal{O}}^{\chi_{c0}}_8(^3S_1)> = 0.0019$ GeV$^3$ and $<{\cal{O}}^{\chi_{c0}}_1(^3P_0)> = 0.089$ GeV$^5$, taken from Fermilab Tevatron fits by Kniehl.
* Matrix elements for $\chi_{c1}$ and $\chi_{c2}$ obtained via symmetry: $<{\cal{O}}^{\chi_{cJ}}_8(^3S_1)> = (2J+1) \times 0.0019$ GeV$^3$ and $<{\cal{O}}^{\chi_{cJ}}_1(^3P_J)> = (2J+1) \times 0.089$ GeV$^5$.
* LO four-flavor running coupling constant uses $\Lambda^{\rm LO}_4 = 175$ MeV, giving $\alpha_s^{\rm LO}(m_Z) = 0.121$.
* Cross sections and spin asymmetries computed at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s} = 500$ GeV, presented in the rapidity range $-3 < y < 3$, covering PHENIX central arm ($-0.5 < y < 0.5$) and forward arm ($1 < |y| < 2$).

## Context

This work extends previous NRQCD color-octet studies of $J/\psi$ and $\psi'$ production with definite polarizations in polarized p-p collisions at RHIC by Jack and Nayak, and the first study of double spin asymmetries in P-wave charmonium hadroproduction by Nowak. It provides LO predictions for $\chi_{cJ}$ polarization that can be compared with future PHENIX data at RHIC, which is particularly important because polarized heavy quarkonium production at Tevatron energies was not explained by the NRQCD color-octet mechanism in the work of Braaten and Kniehl. The results enable tests of spin transfer processes in pQCD and may help extract the polarized gluon distribution function inside the proton.
