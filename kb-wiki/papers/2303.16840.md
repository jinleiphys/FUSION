# Effects of finite volume and magnetic fields on thermodynamic properties of quark matter and fluctuations of conserved charges

> In the current work, we present the influence of finite volume and magnetic field on the thermodynamic properties of isospin asymmetric quark matter using the Polyakov loop extended chiral SU(3) quark mean field (PCQMF) model at finite chemical potential and temperature. Within the PCQMF model, we use the scalar and vector field values in mean-field approximation to obtain the thermodynamic properties: pressure density, entropy density and energy density. The susceptibilities of conserved charges for strongly interacting matter for different system sizes as well as for different values of the magnetic field have been studied. A sizable shift in phase boundary towards the higher values of quark chemical potential ($μ_q$) and temperature (T) has been observed for decreasing values of system volume as well as an opposite shift towards lower temperature and quark chemical potential for increasing magnetic field. We observe an enhancement in fluctuations of conserved charges in the regime of the transition temperature. These studies may have a significant role in understanding the thermodynamic observables extracted from heavy-ion collisions data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the Polyakov loop extended chiral SU(3) quark mean field (PCQMF) model, decreasing the system volume shifts the phase boundary toward higher quark chemical potential and temperature, while increasing the magnetic field shifts it toward lower temperature and quark chemical potential; these shifts are observed under the condition that the fermion vacuum term is not included and a lower momentum cutoff is used to simulate finite volume.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov loop extended chiral SU(3) quark mean field (PCQMF) model in mean-field approximation to study isospin asymmetric quark matter. Finite volume is incorporated by introducing a lower momentum cutoff p_min = pi/R, where R is the cubic system size, and a homogeneous magnetic field B along the z-direction is included by quantizing the quark energy into Landau levels. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta), vector fields (omega, rho, phi), the dilaton field chi, and the Polyakov loop variables Phi and anti-Phi to obtain field values, from which pressure, entropy density, energy density, and susceptibilities of conserved charges are computed via Taylor expansion in chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum constituent quark masses used are m_u = m_d = 313 MeV and m_s = 490 MeV, with m_{s0} = 29 MeV for the strange quark current mass.
* The lower momentum cutoff for finite volume is defined as p_min = pi/R, where R is the system size in femtometers.
* The magnetic field values considered are eB around 0.1 m_pi^2 for SPS, eB of order m_pi^2 at RHIC, and eB approximately 15 m_pi^2 at LHC, where m_pi is the pion mass.
* The Polyakov loop potential parameters are a_0 = 1.53, a_1 = 0.96, a_2 = -2.3, a_3 = -2.85, b_3 = 13.34, and b_4 = 14.88.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram using the PCQMF model by simultaneously incorporating finite volume and magnetic field effects, which are relevant for heavy-ion collisions where the fireball has a finite extent and strong magnetic fields are generated. It builds upon the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) and Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model calculations of finite-size effects by Magdy (2019) and magnetic field effects by Fu (2013), and it contrasts with lattice QCD results that show inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperature. The results are intended to help interpret thermodynamic observables and fluctuations of conserved charges measured in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, and future facilities like FAIR and NICA.
