# Fluctuations of electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

> We perform quantum calculations of fluctuations of the electromagnetic fields in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We find that in the quantum picture the field fluctuations are much smaller than predictions of the classical Monte-Carlo simulation with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density.

## Key claim

Quantum calculations of electromagnetic field fluctuations in heavy ion collisions show that these fluctuations are much smaller than predictions from classical Monte-Carlo simulations with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density, with the reduction factor depending on collision energy and impact parameter.

## Method

The author uses the fluctuation-dissipation theorem (FDT) in the zero-temperature limit to relate the correlators of the electromagnetic field to the retarded Green's function of Maxwell's equations. Each nucleus is treated as a point-like dipole described by its dipole polarizability, which is parametrized using a single giant dipole resonance (GDR) state fitted to experimental photoabsorption cross section data for 197Au and 208Pb nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 197Au: GDR parameters ω₁₀ ≈ 13.6 MeV, Γ ≈ 4.38 MeV, c ≈ 18.2 GeV⁻²; for 208Pb: ω₁₀ ≈ 13.3 MeV, Γ ≈ 3.72 MeV, c ≈ 18.93 GeV⁻²
- Quantum calculation gives ⟨0|d²|0⟩ ≈ 1.91 fm² for 197Au and ≈ 2.02 fm² for 208Pb; classical MC with Woods-Saxon gives ≈ 9.89 fm² and ≈ 10.39 fm² respectively (classical overestimates dipole moment squared by factor ~5)
- Ratio ⟨δB_x²⟩^{1/2}/⟨B_y⟩ from quantum treatment is smaller than classical by factor ~5-8 for Au+Au at √s = 0.2 TeV (RHIC) and by factor ~13-27 for Pb+Pb at √s = 2.76 TeV (LHC)

## Context

This work extends previous Monte-Carlo simulations of electromagnetic field fluctuations using classical Lienard-Weichert potentials with Woods-Saxon nuclear density (Skokov, Deng, Liao) by incorporating quantum collective dynamics of the nuclear ground state via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. The results are important for the chiral magnetic effect and charge separation in heavy ion collisions (Kharzeev), as smaller fluctuations preserve the correlation between magnetic field direction and reaction plane. The author notes that future work should include joint analysis with fluctuations of initial entropy deposition and other collective nuclear modes (giant monopole and quadrupole resonances) for event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations.
