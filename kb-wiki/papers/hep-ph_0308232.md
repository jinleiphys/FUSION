# $Θ^+$ in a chiral constituent quark model and its interpolating fields

> The recently discovered pentaquark $Θ^+$ is described within the chiral constituent quark model. Within this picture the flavor-spin interaction between valence quarks inverts the $(1s)^4$ and $(1s)^3(1p)$ levels of the four-quark subsystem and consequently the lowest-lying pentaquark is a positive parity, I=0, J=1/2 state of the flavor antidecuplet, similar to the soliton model prediction. Contrary to the soliton model, however, the quark picture predicts its spin-orbit partner with $J=3/2$. Different interpolating fields intended for lattice calculations of $Θ^+$ are constructed, which have a maximal overlap with this baryon if it is indeed a quark excitation in the 5Q system.

# Θ⁺ in a chiral constituent quark model and its interpolating fields

## Key claim

The recently discovered pentaquark Θ⁺ can be described within the chiral constituent quark model as a positive parity, I=0, J=1/2 state of the flavor antidecuplet, but only if the flavor-spin interaction between valence quarks inverts the (1s)⁴ and (1s)³(1p) levels of the four-quark subsystem. Contrary to the soliton model, the quark picture predicts a spin-orbit partner with J=3/2 that should be experimentally searchable in the 1400–1700 MeV region.

## Method

The author constructs the quantum numbers and wave function of the lowest pentaquark within the chiral constituent quark model, where the dominant residual interaction between valence quarks is a flavor-spin interaction of the form -λᶠᵢ·λᶠⱼ σᵢ·σⱼ. The four-quark subsystem is analyzed using Young diagram techniques for permutational symmetry in color, orbital, flavor, and spin spaces. Local interpolating fields for lattice QCD calculations are then constructed as products of diquark bilinears with specific color, flavor, and spin symmetries, using SU(3) color Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to ensure color-singlet pentaquark states.

## Key numbers

- The Θ⁺ resonance has a mass around 1540 MeV with strangeness +1 and minimal quark content uudd\bar{s}
- The spin-orbit splitting between the J=1/2 and J=3/2 partners is expected to be of order 100 MeV or less, based on typical baryon spectroscopy
- The predicted J=3/2, S=+1 pentaquark should be searched for in the region 1400–1700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska (1996) to the pentaquark sector, following earlier unpublished work by the same authors (1998) and Stancu (1998) showing that the lowest pentaquark should have positive parity. The prediction of identical quantum numbers (I=0, Jᴾ=1/2⁺) to the soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) is noted, but the quark picture additionally predicts a J=3/2 spin-orbit partner. The constructed interpolating fields are intended to distinguish in lattice calculations between this quark-excitation picture and the diquark clustering picture of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). The work also notes a potential difficulty: it is not clear whether the analog of Θ⁺ exists in the heavy quark region, so the signal may appear only below some critical current quark mass.
