# Dilepton production in pp and np collisions at 1.25 GeV

> The inclusive reactions $pp \rightarrow e^+ e^- X$ and $np \rightarrow e^+ e^- X$ at the laboratory kinetic energy of 1.25 GeV are investigated in a model of dominance of nucleon and $Δ$ resonances. Experimental data for these reactions have recently been reported by the HADES Collaboration. In the original model, the dileptons are produced either from the decays of nucleon and $Δ$ resonances $R \rightarrow N e^+ e^-$ or from the Dalitz decays of $π^0$- and $η$-mesons created in the $R \to Nπ^0$ and $R \to Nη$ decays. We found that the distribution of dilepton invariant masses in the $pp \rightarrow e^+ e^- X$ reaction is well reproduced by the contributions of $R \rightarrow N e^+ e^-$ decays and $R \rightarrow N π^0$, $π^0 \to γe^+e^-$ decays. Among the resonances, the predominant contribution comes from the $Δ(1232)$, which determines both the direct decay channel $R \rightarrow N e^+ e^-$ and the pion decay channel. In the collisions $np \rightarrow e^+ e^- X$, additional significant contributions arise from the $η$-meson Dalitz decays, produced in the $np \rightarrow npη$ and $np \rightarrow dη$ reactions, the radiative capture $np \rightarrow d e^+ e^-$, and the $np \rightarrow np e^+ e^-$ bremsstrahlung. These mechanisms may partly explain the strong excess of dileptons in the cross section for collisions of $np$ versus $pp$, which ranges from 7 to 100 times for the dilepton invariant masses of 0.2 to 0.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The resonance model, dominated by the Δ(1232), reproduces the dilepton invariant-mass distribution in pp collisions at 1.25 GeV, but in np collisions the observed 7-to-100-fold excess over pp is only partly explained by adding η-meson Dalitz decays, radiative capture np → d e⁺ e⁻, and bremsstrahlung.

## Method

The authors model inclusive pp → e⁺ e⁻ X and np → e⁺ e⁻ X at T_LAB = 1.25 GeV as a two-step process: first, nucleon and Δ resonances are produced in NN collisions; second, these resonances decay into a nucleon, mesons, and a dilepton pair. For np collisions, they additionally include η-meson Dalitz decays from np → npη and np → dη reactions, radiative capture np → d e⁺ e⁻ (scaled from deuteron photodisintegration data), and bremsstrahlung (taken from Shyam and scaled by a vector-meson-dominance form factor). The Fermi motion of the bound proton in the deuteron is accounted for using experimental electron-scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The excess of dilepton yield in np versus pp collisions ranges from 7 to 100 times for dilepton invariant masses of 0.2 to 0.5 GeV.
- The total cross section for np → d γ is approximately 5 μb, derived from the deuteron photodisintegration cross section σ(γ d → n p) = 7.5 μb at photon energy E_γ = 600 MeV.
- The radiative capture np → d e⁺ e⁻ dominates at dilepton invariant masses M ≳ 400 MeV.
- In pp collisions, the η-meson is not produced because the excess energy over threshold in the center-of-mass system is ε = −1 MeV.
- Near threshold, the np → dη cross section is greater by a factor of 3 to 4 than the np → npη cross section, which in turn is greater than the pp → ppη cross section by a factor of 6.5.

## Context

This work extends the resonance model of Martemyanov, Krivoruchenko, and Faessler (2005), previously applied to nucleon-nucleon collisions at 1 to 6 GeV, to describe HADES Collaboration data at 1.25 GeV. It addresses the large np/pp dilepton excess that conventional sources (including η-meson production) could not explain, and it contrasts with bremsstrahlung calculations by Kaptari and Kämpfer (2008) and Shyam and Mosel (2010). The results enable future exclusive measurements (e.g., pp → pp e⁺ e⁻) to constrain off-shell vector-meson couplings and suggest that a dibaryon resonance observed in np → d π⁰ π⁰ by Hans (2010) may be an additional source at high invariant masses.
