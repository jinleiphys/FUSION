# Chiral magnetic effect and anomalous transport from real-time lattice simulations

> We present a first-principle study of anomaly induced transport phenomena by performing real-time lattice simulations with dynamical fermions coupled simultaneously to non-Abelian $SU(N_c)$ and Abelian $U(1)$ gauge fields. Investigating the behavior of vector and axial currents during a sphaleron transition in the presence of an external magnetic field, we demonstrate how the interplay of the Chiral magnetic (CME) and Chiral separation effect (CSE) lead to the formation of a propagating wave. We further analyze the dependence of the magnitude of the induced vector current and the propagation of the wave on the amount of explicit chiral symmetry breaking due to finite quark mass.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that real-time lattice simulations with dynamical fermions can capture the formation of a chiral magnetic wave from a sphaleron transition in an external magnetic field, but the quantitative description within anomalous hydrodynamics shows minor deviations (e.g., a fraction of axial charge remains localized at the sphaleron center). The magnitude of the induced vector current and wave propagation depend on the amount of explicit chiral symmetry breaking from finite quark mass, with heavier quarks reducing the axial charge imbalance and even reversing the vector current direction immediately after the transition.

## Method
The authors perform classical-statistical real-time lattice gauge theory simulations with dynamical Wilson fermions coupled to both non-Abelian SU(2) and Abelian U(1) gauge fields in 3+1 dimensions. They construct a topologically non-trivial sphaleron configuration with winding number unity on a spatial lattice, evolve the fermion modefunctions using a leap-frog scheme, and compute vector and axial current densities in the presence of a constant external magnetic field along the z-direction, without including fermionic back-reaction on the gauge fields.

## Key numbers
- Lattice dimensions: \(24 \times 24 \times 64\) sites with spacing \(r_{\rm{sph}}/a = 6\)
- Sphaleron transition duration: \(t_{\rm{sph}} = 3/2 \, r_{\rm{sph}}\) (corresponding to \(\sim 0.6-1.5\) fm/c)
- Magnetic field strength: \(qB = 3.5 \, r_{\rm{sph}}^{-2}\) (corresponding to a few \(m_\pi^2\))
- Quark masses studied: \(m r_{\rm{sph}} = 3 \cdot 10^{-3}, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0\)
- For light quarks (\(m r_{\rm{sph}} \ll 1\)), the sphaleron transition induces two units of net axial charge
- For intermediate quark mass \(m r_{\rm{sph}} = 1/2\) (order of strange quark mass), the maximal axial charge imbalance is reduced by a factor of two compared to the light quark case

## Context
This work extends real-time lattice techniques previously applied to gauge field dynamics (Berges 2013, Mace 2016) and fermion production (Gelis 2005, Saffin 2011, Tanji 2016) to include dynamical fermions coupled to both non-Abelian and Abelian fields for the first time. It provides a first-principles confirmation of the chiral magnetic wave predicted by anomalous hydrodynamics (Newman 2005, Son 2009, Kharzeev 2010) and enables future quantitative studies of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, including fermionic back-reaction and realistic non-equilibrium gauge field ensembles.
