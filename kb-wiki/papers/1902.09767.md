# $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α,γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ reaction rates and the implication for Big Bang nucleosynthesis in the potential model

> The reaction rates of the direct astrophysical capture processes $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α,γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$, as well as the abundance of the $^{7}{\rm Li}$ element are estimated in the framework of a two-body potential model. The estimated $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ abundance ratio of $^{7}{\rm Li/H}=(5.07\pm 0.14 )\times 10^{-10}$ is in a very good agreement with the recent measurement $^{7}{\rm Li/H}=(5.0\pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}$ of the LUNA collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their two-body potential model yields a primordial $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ abundance ratio of $(5.07 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-10}$, which agrees with the LUNA collaboration measurement of $(5.0 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}$, but this result depends on including both the $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$ reaction rates; using only the $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ process gives lower values of $(4.81 \pm 0.12) \times 10^{-10}$ (model $V_{M1}^{a}$) and $(4.92 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{-10}$ (model $V_{D}^{a}$).

## Method

The authors calculated reaction rates for the direct astrophysical capture processes $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$ using two potential models ($V_{M1}^{a}$ and $V_{D}^{a}$) within a two-body potential framework. They then fitted the temperature-dependent reaction rates to an analytical polynomial formula and inserted these rates into the PArthENoPE public Big Bang nucleosynthesis code, adopting the Planck 2015 baryon density parameter $\Omega_b h^2 = 0.02229^{+0.00029}_{-0.00027}$ and neutron lifetime $\tau_n = 880.3 \pm 1.1$ s, to compute the primordial $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ abundance ratio.

## Key numbers

* $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ abundance ratio from combined reactions: $(5.07 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-10}$ (average of both models), compared to LUNA measurement $(5.0 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}$.
* $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ from $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ only, model $V_{M1}^{a}$: $(4.81 \pm 0.12) \times 10^{-10}$.
* $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ from $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ only, model $V_{D}^{a}$: $(4.92 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{-10}$.
* $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ from combined reactions, model $V_{M1}^{a}$: $(5.06 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{-10}$.
* $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ from combined reactions, model $V_{D}^{a}$: $(5.08 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{-10}$.
* Astrophysical S-factor $S_{34}$ at 23 keV from indirect method (Takacs 2015): $0.548 \pm 0.054$ keV b.
* Analytical approximation for $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ reaction rates reproduces tabulated values within 1.02% (model $V_{M1}^{a}$) and 0.98% (model $V_{D}^{a}$).
* Analytical approximation for $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$ reaction rates reproduces tabulated values within 0.61% (model $V_{M1}^{a}$) and 0.62% (model $V_{D}^{a}$).

## Context

This work extends the two-body potential model approach previously applied by Tursunov (2015, 2016, 2018) and Baye (2018) to the $^{6}{\rm Li}$ problem, now targeting the $^{7}{\rm Li}$ cosmological abundance. The results are compared with the NACRE 1999 and NACRE II 2013 compilations, as well as indirect measurements by Takacs (2015) and direct measurements by the LUNA collaboration. The study enables improved input for Big Bang nucleosynthesis calculations using the PArthENoPE code, and addresses the long-standing cosmological lithium problem by showing that a potential model can reproduce the LUNA measurement without invoking new physics.
