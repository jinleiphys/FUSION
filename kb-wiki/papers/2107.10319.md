# On the linear causality and stability of third-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics

> We analyze the linear causality and stability of third-order fluid dynamics considering perturbations around a global equilibrium state. We investigate the formulation derived from kinetic theory, using the Chapman-Enskog expansion, in PRC 88, 021903, which was shown to be in excellent agreement with solutions of the microscopic theory. From this analysis, we demonstrate that this theory is linearly acausal and unstable and that such instabilities cannot be corrected by tuning the transport coefficients. We then propose a modification of this theory, valid only in the linear regime, that can be constructed to be linearly causal and stable and obtain the conditions the transport coefficients must satisfy in order for this to be the case.

# On the linear causality and stability of third-order relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics

## Key claim

The third-order relativistic fluid dynamics formulation derived from kinetic theory via Chapman-Enskog expansion (Phys. Rev. C 88, 021903) is linearly acausal and unstable when perturbed around a moving global equilibrium state, and these instabilities cannot be corrected by tuning the transport coefficients. A modified version of this theory, valid only in the linear regime, can be constructed to be linearly causal and stable if the transport coefficients satisfy specific constraints.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis by perturbing the fluid-dynamical variables (energy density, fluid 4-velocity, and shear-stress tensor) around a global equilibrium state, then Fourier-transform the linearized equations and decompose them into transverse and longitudinal components with respect to the wave vector. They solve the resulting dispersion relations for both static and moving background fluids, analyzing the asymptotic behavior of the modes in the small and large wave number limits, and compare the results to Israel-Stewart theory.

## Key numbers

- The nonhydrodynamic transverse mode for perturbations on a moving fluid has a negative imaginary part at wave number k = 0, indicating instability: the new mode ω^shear_T,new has imaginary part proportional to 35i/(16γ τ̂_π² V²) times a square-root expression that is always positive, leading to a negative imaginary part for all background velocities V.
- For perturbations on a static fluid, the nonhydrodynamic transverse mode behaves as ω ∼ i k² in the large wave number limit (specifically ω̂^shear_T,+ = (8/35)i τ̂_π k̂² + ...), a behavior associated with parabolic (acausal) theories.
- The longitudinal nonhydrodynamic mode for static perturbations also behaves as ω̂^shear_L ∼ (9i/35) τ̂_π k̂² in the large wave number limit.
- The hydrodynamic longitudinal modes in the large wave number limit have asymptotic group velocity ±c_s (the speed of sound), which is subluminal.
- The analysis uses τ̂_π = 5 (from Denicol et al.) as a representative value for the scaled relaxation time.

## Context

This work extends the linear stability analysis framework developed for Israel-Stewart theory (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1983; Olson, 1990; Denicol et al., 2008; Rischke, 1998; Brito and Denicol, 2020) to third-order fluid dynamics. The third-order formulation analyzed was derived by El et al. (2010) and Amaresh et al. (2013) using the Chapman-Enskog expansion, and was previously shown to agree well with Boltzmann equation solutions for Bjorken flow (Amaresh et al., 2013). The paper demonstrates that unlike Israel-Stewart theory, which can be made causal and stable with appropriate transport coefficient constraints, the existing third-order theory suffers from the same fundamental issues as relativistic Navier-Stokes theory (Eckart, 1940; Landau and Lifshitz, 1959), namely acausality and instability for moving fluids. The proposed modified theory enables future work on causal and stable third-order formulations that may better describe the early stages of heavy-ion collisions where large gradients are present.
