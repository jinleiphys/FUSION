# Spin-parities of the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ in the One-Boson-Exchange Model

> The LHCb collaboration has recently observed three pentaquark peaks, the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$. They are very close to a pair of heavy baryon-meson thresholds, with the $P_c(4312)$ located $8.9\,{\rm MeV}$ below the $\bar{D} Σ_c$ threshold, and the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ located $21.8$ and $4.8\,{\rm MeV}$ below the $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ one. The spin-parities of these three states have not been measured yet. In this work we assume that the $P_c(4312)$ is a $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D} Σ_c$ bound state, while the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ are $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ bound states of unknown spin-parity, where we notice that the consistent description of the three pentaquarks in the one-boson-exchange model can indeed determine the spin and parities of the later, i.e. of the two $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ molecular candidates. For this determination we revisit first the one-boson-exchange model, which in its original formulation contains a short-range delta-like contribution in the spin-spin component of the potential. We argue that it is better to remove these delta-like contributions because, in this way, the one-boson-exchange potential will comply with the naive expectation that the form factors should not have a significant impact in the long-range part of the potential (in particular the one-pion-exchange part). Once this is done, we find that it is possible to consistently describe the three pentaquarks, to the point that the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ can be predicted from the $P_c(4312)$ within a couple of MeV with respect to their experimental location. In addition the so-constructed one-boson-exchange model predicts the preferred quantum numbers of the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ molecular pentaquarks to be $\tfrac{3}{2}^-$ and $\tfrac{1}{2}^-$, respectively.

## Key claim

Assuming the $P_c(4312)$ is a $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ bound state, the one-boson-exchange model can consistently describe all three pentaquarks only after removing the short-range delta-like contributions from the spin-spin component of the potential, which then predicts the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ quantum numbers as $\tfrac{3}{2}^{-}$ and $\tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$, respectively, within a couple of MeV of their experimental locations.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential for the $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ and $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ systems including $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ meson exchange, using heavy-quark spin symmetry to relate couplings. They determined the cutoff $\Lambda = 1119$ MeV by reproducing the $P_c(4312)$ as a $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ bound state with $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$, then predicted binding energies for the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ system in both $J^P = \tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$ and $\tfrac{3}{2}^{-}$ channels. The authors identified that the naive OBE model with monopolar form factors produces an unphysical short-range attraction from the regularized delta-function piece of the one-pion-exchange potential, and they removed these delta-like contributions by hand to restore consistency with the expectation that form factors should not distort the long-range potential.

## Key numbers

- $P_c(4312)$ binding energy: $8.9$ MeV below the $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ threshold
- $P_c(4440)$ binding energy: $21.8$ MeV below the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ threshold
- $P_c(4457)$ binding energy: $4.8$ MeV below the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ threshold
- Cutoff determined from $P_c(4312)$: $\Lambda = 1119$ MeV
- Naive OBE predictions (with delta contributions): $B_E(\tfrac{1}{2}^-) \simeq 74$ MeV and $B_E(\tfrac{3}{2}^-) \simeq 3$ MeV for $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$
- After removing OPE delta contribution only: $B_E(\tfrac{1}{2}^-) = 13.2$ MeV and $B_E(\tfrac{3}{2}^-) = 11.6$ MeV
- After removing all delta contributions (preferred result): $B_E(\tfrac{1}{2}^-) = 4.2$ MeV and $B_E(\tfrac{3}{2}^-) = 18.3$ MeV, within $1-3$ MeV of the experimental $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ binding energies
- Predicted quantum numbers: $P_c(4440)$ as $\tfrac{3}{2}^{-}$, $P_c(4457)$ as $\tfrac{1}{2}^{-}$

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquark peaks (Aaij 2019) by resolving a form-factor artifact in the one-boson-exchange model that previously prevented a consistent description of all three states with a single cutoff. The removal of delta-like contributions follows the renormalized OBE model ideas of Cordon (2009) and has been considered previously by Meng (2017). The results enable future experimental determination of the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ spin-parities to test the molecular hypothesis, and provide predictions for unobserved partner states in the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ system via heavy-quark spin symmetry relations.
