# Global analysis of polarized DIS & SIDIS data with improved small-$x$ helicity evolution

> We analyze the world polarized deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS) data at low values of $x < 0.1$, using small-$x$ evolution equations for the flavor singlet and nonsinglet helicity parton distribution functions (hPDFs). The hPDFs for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons are extracted and evolved to lower values of $x$ to make predictions for the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). We improve on our earlier work by employing the more realistic large-$N_c\, \& N_f$ limit of the revised small-$x$ helicity evolution, and incorporating running coupling corrections along with SIDIS data into the fit. We find an anti-correlation between the signs of the gluon and $C$-even quark hPDFs as well as the $g_1$ structure function. While the existing low-$x$ polarized DIS and SIDIS data are insufficient to constrain the initial conditions for the polarized dipole amplitudes in the helicity evolution equations, future EIC data will allow more precise predictions for hPDFs and the $g_1$ structure function for $x$ values beyond those probed at the EIC. Using the obtained hPDFs, we discuss the contributions to the proton spin from quark and gluon spins at small $x$.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first phenomenological analysis of polarized DIS and SIDIS data using the revised KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations, finding that existing low-$x$ data are insufficient to constrain the initial conditions for polarized dipole amplitudes, but future Electron-Ion Collider data will enable more precise predictions for helicity parton distribution functions and the $g_1$ structure function at smaller $x$ values.

## Method

The authors solved the large-$N_c \& N_f$ limit of the revised KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations with running coupling corrections, using the JAM Bayesian Monte Carlo framework to fit world polarized DIS and SIDIS data at $x < 0.1$. They extracted flavor singlet and nonsinglet helicity parton distribution functions (hPDFs) for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, employing existing JAM fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, and unidentified hadrons from Cocuzza (2022) to analyze the SIDIS data.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses data with $x < 0.1$ and $Q^2 > m_c^2$ (with $m_c$ the charm quark mass)
- The flavor singlet helicity intercept at fixed coupling was found to be $\alpha_h = 3.66 \sqrt{\frac{\as N_c}{2 \pi}}$ in the pure-gluon case and $\alpha_h = 3.45 \sqrt{\frac{\as N_c}{2 \pi}}$ for $N_f = 4$ (with $N_c = 3$)
- The KPS evolution intercept was $\alpha_h = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \sqrt{\frac{\as N_c}{2 \pi}} \approx 2.31 \sqrt{\frac{\as N_c}{2 \pi}}$ in the large-$N_c$ limit
- Running coupling corrections reduce the intercept $\alpha_h$ for $\Delta q^+$ and $\Delta G$ below 1
- The one-loop running coupling is given by $\alpha_s(Q^2) = \frac{12\pi}{11N_c-2N_f}\frac{1}{\ln (Q^2 /\Lambda_\textrm{QCD}^2)}$

## Context

This work extends the proof-of-principle fit by Adamiak (2021) that used only polarized DIS data with the earlier KPS version of small-$x$ helicity evolution, by incorporating the revised KPS-CTT evolution equations from Cougoulic (2022) and adding SIDIS data. The analysis enables predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider and provides estimates of quark and gluon spin contributions to the proton spin at small $x$, while the numerical disagreement with the Bartels-Ermolaev-Ryskin (BER) approach (Bartels 1995, 1996) at the 2-3% level requires further theoretical investigation.
