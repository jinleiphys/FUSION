# Demonstration of the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo approach for sd-shell nuclei

> We apply the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo approach to the nuclear shell model in the 1s-0d configuration space. The Hamiltonian was chosen to have isovector pairing and isoscalar multipole-multipole interactions, and the calculations were performed within the fixed-particle, canonical ensemble. The results demonstrate the feasibility of the method for $N\neq Z$ even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei. In particular, static observables for even-even Ne isotopes and Na-22 compare well with results obtained from exact diagonalization of the Hamiltonian. Response functions are presented for Ne-22 and compared with exact results, and the viability of cranked calculations for $N\neq Z$ even-even nuclei is addressed. We present methods for computing observables in the canonical ensemble using Fourier extraction, and for determining the nuclear shape.

# Demonstration of the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo approach for sd-shell nuclei

## Key claim

The auxiliary-field Monte Carlo method is demonstrated to be feasible for calculating static observables and response functions in the 1s-0d shell model space for N≠Z even-even nuclei and N=Z odd-odd nuclei, with results that compare well to exact diagonalization, but only for Hamiltonians with isovector pairing and isoscalar multipole-multipole interactions where the sign problem is controlled.

## Method

The authors applied the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to linearize two-body interactions in the imaginary-time propagator, introducing auxiliary fields that reduce the problem to a multi-dimensional integral evaluated by Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling. They used a density decomposition of the Hamiltonian into one-body operators, with the canonical trace for fixed particle number computed via a Fourier extraction method that avoids numerical instabilities of the activity expansion near mid-shell. Calculations were performed with approximately 2000 Monte Carlo samples and a time step Δβ=0.0625 on the Intel Touchstone Gamma and Delta parallel systems.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 2000 Monte Carlo samples were collected for each calculation, with Δβ=0.0625 used as the time step
- For A~60 nuclei in the 0f-1p shell, an unrestricted calculation would involve approximately 2×10^9 basis states with definite Jz, reduced to about 10^7 with angular momentum and isospin projection
- The activity expansion for the canonical trace was found stable only for Nv ≤ 4 or Nv ≥ Ns-4, motivating the Fourier extraction method
- Ground-state observables for Ne isotopes and 22Na were plotted at β=2.5
- Response functions for 22Ne were computed at β=2.0 using 16 and 32 time slices
- The maximum Jz available to 22Ne is 10, making ω=2 an extreme cranking limit
- Distribution functions f(β,γ)β⁴sin(3γ) for 22Ne were computed at temperatures T=0.5, 1.0, 2.0, and 4.0 MeV, smoothed with a Gaussian width of 0.01

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo method previously demonstrated for N=Z even-even nuclei in Johnson, Koonin, Lang, and Ormand (1992) and Lang, Johnson, Koonin, and Ormand (1993) to N≠Z even-even and N=Z odd-odd systems in the sd shell. The Fourier extraction method for the canonical trace is introduced to overcome the numerical instability of the activity expansion approach from the earlier formalism. The results enable future calculations for rare-earth nuclei with nearly 100,000 fields, and open the possibility of studying shape transitions as functions of temperature and angular momentum, as well as investigating the sign problem for more general interaction schemes.
