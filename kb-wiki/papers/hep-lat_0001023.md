# Insight into the Scalar Mesons from a Lattice Calculation

> We study the possibility that the light scalar mesons are (qbar qbar q q) states rather than (qbar q). We perform a lattice QCD calculation of pseudoscalar meson scattering amplitudes, ignoring quark loops and quark annihilation, and find indications that for sufficiently heavy quarks there is a stable four-quark bound state with J^{PC}=0^{++} and non-exotic flavor quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that, for sufficiently heavy quarks (pseudoscalar mass around 800 MeV), there may be a stable four-quark bound state with scalar quantum numbers in the non-exotic flavor channel, but the authors caution that the result is suggestive rather than conclusive and requires confirmation on larger lattices.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations of pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar scattering amplitudes using Symanzik-improved glue and D234 quark actions, ignoring quark loops and quark annihilation diagrams. They constructed four-quark correlation functions from direct (D) and crossed (C) quark contractions, then formed exotic (D minus C) and non-exotic (D plus half C) combinations, and extracted binding energies by fitting ratios of these correlators to single exponentials at large Euclidean time. The lattice volumes ranged from approximately 2 fm to 4 fm at lattice spacings of 0.25 fm and 0.40 fm.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar meson mass was 790 MeV on the coarser lattice (a=0.40 fm) and 840 MeV on the finer lattice (a=0.25 fm), with pseudoscalar-to-vector mass ratio m_P/m_V = 0.76.
* For the exotic channel (I=2), the fit to the scattering-state form gave T = 1.18(3) x 10^-5 MeV^-2 with chi^2/dof = 3.0, yielding 4 f_P^2 T = 1.03(3), consistent with the chiral perturbation theory prediction of 1.
* For the non-exotic channel (I=0), the fit to the scattering-state form gave T = 1.20(5) x 10^-5 MeV^-2 but with chi^2/dof = 17.0, indicating the data do not follow the expected 1/L^3 scaling for a scattering state.
* The lattice spacing was determined from charmonium P-S splitting to be 495(4) MeV for the coarser lattice and 790(10) MeV for the finer lattice.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of four-quark sources by Gupta et al. (1993) and Fukugita et al. (1995), who each examined only a single lattice volume and could not distinguish bound states from scattering states. The results support the four-quark interpretation of light scalar mesons originally proposed by Jaffe (1977), where the inverted mass spectrum of a qbar-qbar-qq nonet matches the observed pattern of the f0(980), a0(980), sigma(600), and kappa(900). The calculation enables future unquenched lattice studies to determine whether these bound states persist when quark annihilation diagrams are included and whether they survive at physical quark masses.
