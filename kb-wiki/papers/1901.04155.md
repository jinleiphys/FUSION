# Extracting jet transport coefficient via single hadron and dihadron productions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We study the suppressions of high transverse momentum single hadron and dihadron productions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions based on the framework of a next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD parton model combined with the higher-twist energy loss formalism.Our model can provide a consistant description for the nuclear modification factors of single hadron and dihadron productions in central and non-central nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC energies. We quantitatively extract the value of jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ via a global $χ^2$ analysis, and obtain ${\hat{q}}/{T^3} = 4.1 \sim 4.4$ at $T = 378$~MeV at RHIC and ${\hat{q}}/{T^3} = 2.6 \sim 3.3$ at $T = 486$~MeV at the LHC, which are consistent with the results from JET Collaboration. We also provide the predictions for the nuclear modification factors of dihadron productions in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV and in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 5.44 TeV.

## Key claim
The authors claim that a next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD parton model combined with the higher-twist energy loss formalism can provide a consistent description of both single hadron and dihadron nuclear modification factors across RHIC and LHC energies, but the extracted scaled jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}/T^3$ shows a temperature dependence that decreases with increasing temperature, a nuance not captured in the abstract's statement of consistency with JET Collaboration results.

## Method
The authors compute single hadron and dihadron production cross sections in proton-proton collisions at NLO in perturbative QCD using CT14 parton distribution functions and Kretzer fragmentation functions. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, they incorporate cold nuclear matter effects via EPPS16 nuclear shadowing and hot nuclear matter effects via medium-modified fragmentation functions calculated using the higher-twist formalism for parton energy loss, with the QGP medium evolution described by the OSU (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model (VISH2+1). A global $\chi^2$ analysis is performed by comparing model calculations to experimental data on nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}$ and $I_{AA}$ from RHIC and the LHC to extract the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}_0$.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=0.2$ TeV at RHIC: $\hat{q}_0 = 1.1 \sim 1.2$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T_0 = 378$ MeV, corresponding to $\hat{q}/T^3 = 4.1 \sim 4.4$ at $T = 378$ MeV.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC: $\hat{q}_0 = 1.5 \sim 1.9$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T_0 = 486$ MeV, corresponding to $\hat{q}/T^3 = 2.6 \sim 3.3$ at $T = 486$ MeV.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV: $\hat{q}_0 \approx 1.7$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T_0 = 516$ MeV, corresponding to $\hat{q}/T^3 \approx 2.5$ at $T = 516$ MeV.
- For Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=5.44$ TeV: $\hat{q}_0 \approx 1.8$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T_0 = 469$ MeV, corresponding to $\hat{q}/T^3 \approx 3.5$ at $T = 469$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the quantitative extraction of the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ performed by the JET Collaboration (Burke et al., 2013), which compared five theoretical models with single hadron data in central collisions at RHIC and the LHC and obtained $\hat{q}/T^3 = 4.6 \pm 1.2$ at $T \approx 370$ MeV and $\hat{q}/T^3 = 3.7 \pm 1.4$ at $T \approx 470$ MeV. The present study goes beyond by simultaneously fitting both single hadron and dihadron nuclear modification data, including non-central collisions, and providing predictions for dihadron suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV. The extracted temperature dependence of $\hat{q}/T^3$ enables further refinement of jet quenching models and tests of the temperature dependence of jet-medium interactions.
