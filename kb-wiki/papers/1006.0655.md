# A minimal quasiparticle approach for the QGP and its large-$N_c$ limits

> We propose a quasiparticle approach allowing to compute the equation of state of a generic gauge theory with gauge group SU($N_c$) and quarks in an arbitrary representation. Our formalism relies on the thermal quasiparticle masses (quarks and gluons) computed from Hard-Thermal-Loop techniques, in which the standard two-loop running coupling constant is used. Our model is minimal in the sense that we do not allow any extra ansatz concerning the temperature-dependence of the running coupling. We first show that it is able to reproduce the most recent equations of state computed on the lattice for temperatures higher than 2 $T_c$. In this range of temperatures, an ideal gas framework is indeed expected to be relevant. Then we study the accuracy of various inequivalent large-$N_c$ limits concerning the description of the QCD results, as well as the equivalence between the QCD$_{AS}$ limit and the ${\cal N}=1$ SUSY Yang-Mills theory. Finally, we estimate the dissociation temperature of the $Υ$-meson and comment on the estimations' stability regarding the different considered large-$N_c$ limits.

## Key claim

The authors propose a minimal quasiparticle model for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that uses only perturbative Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) thermal masses and a standard two-loop running coupling without any additional temperature-dependent ansatz, and they claim it reproduces lattice equations of state only for temperatures above 2 \(T_c\), not near \(T_c\) where the ideal gas framework breaks down.

## Method

The model computes the pressure of an ideal gas of gluons and quarks with thermal masses obtained from leading-order HTL formulas, using a two-loop running coupling constant with the temperature as the energy scale and a single fitted parameter \(\Lambda\). The trace anomaly \(\Delta = e - 3p\) is derived from the pressure via the thermodynamic relation \(\Delta = T^5 \partial_T(p/T^4)\), and results are normalized to the Stefan-Boltzmann pressure. The approach is applied to pure SU(\(N_c\)) gluon plasma, SU(3) QGP with light and heavy quarks at zero and nonzero chemical potential, and various large-\(N_c\) limits including the 't Hooft, Veneziano, and QCD\(_{\text{AS}}\) limits.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda = 0.88\, T_c\): the single fitted parameter for the pure glue case, obtained by fitting to SU(3) lattice trace anomaly data in the range \((2.0, 3.5) \times T_c\).
- \(T_c = 180\) MeV: the critical temperature used for QGP with quark flavors, taken as a typical value from lattice simulations.
- Bare quark masses used: \(\bar m_u = \bar m_d = 0\) MeV, \(\bar m_s = 150\) MeV, \(\bar m_c = 1270\) MeV, \(\bar m_b = 4200\) MeV, taken from the PDG.
- The model reproduces lattice trace anomaly data for pure glue SU(3) to SU(8) in the range \((2.0, 3.5) \times T_c\) and for SU(3) QGP with \(2+1\) light flavors at zero chemical potential above \(2\, T_c\).
- For nonzero chemical potential, the ratio \(\Delta p / \Delta p_{SB}\) varies by only 3% at \(2\, T_c\) for \(\mu\) between 100 and 530 MeV, and the difference vanishes as temperature increases.

## Context

This work extends quasiparticle models pioneered by Levai and Heinz (1998) and others, which required fitting thermal masses to lattice data, by instead using analytically computed HTL thermal masses from perturbative techniques (e.g., Weldon 1982, Thoma 1995). The model is compared to recent lattice results from Panero (2009) for pure glue and from Cheng (2008) and Bazavov (2009) for QGP with quarks. It enables systematic study of large-\(N_c\) limits (e.g., 't Hooft 1974, Veneziano 1976) and shows that the QCD\(_{\text{AS}}\) limit with \(N_f = 2+1\) fails to reproduce QCD due to a Banks-Zaks infrared fixed point, while the 't Hooft and Veneziano limits are excellent approximations. The approach also allows computation of the \({\cal N}=1\) SUSY Yang-Mills equation of state, which is predicted to be independent of \(N_c\).
