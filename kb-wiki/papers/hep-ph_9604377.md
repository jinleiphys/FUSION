# Scaling approach to higher-twist corrections

> Bjorken scaling is violetd. At large $x$-values ($0.7 \lesssim x \leq 1$) the violation is mainly attributed to $\propto 1/Q^2$ (higher-twist) corrections. We discuss how to incorporate such corrections by using a new scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$ which accounts for the non-perturbative effects due to the confining parton interaction in the final state. The approach can accomodate also the remaining QCD logarithmic corrections and the behaviour of the $F_2(x,Q^2)$ structure functions is reproduced, in a quite natural way, within a wide kinematical range.

# Scaling approach to higher-twist corrections

## Key claim

The paper claims that higher-twist (power-law) corrections to Bjorken scaling, which dominate at large $x$ ($0.7 \lesssim x \leq 1$), can be effectively incorporated by replacing the Bjorken variable $x$ with a new scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$ that accounts for non-perturbative effects from confining parton interactions in the final state, provided the spectator diquark mass $m_0$ is set to zero (current-quark spectators) rather than a constituent-quark value.

## Method

The authors introduce a scaling variable $\bar x(x,Q^2)$ derived from a Bethe-Salpeter analysis of final-state confining interactions, which depends on the target mass $M$ and an invariant spectator mass $m_s$ parameterized as $m_s^2 \simeq m_0^2 + C(1-x)$. They test scaling of SLAC proton and deuteron $F_2$ data ($5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 30$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $x \geq 0.7$) as a function of $\bar x$ for different $m_0$ values. They then combine the $\bar x$ variable with NLO QCD logarithmic evolution (using the DIS scheme) to reproduce the $Q^2$ dependence of $F_2$ over a wider kinematic range ($0.35 < x < 0.75$, $5 < Q^2 < 230$ (GeV/c)$^2$), evolving backward from $Q^2 = 230$ (GeV/c)$^2$ using a shifted Bjorken variable.

## Key numbers

- The SLAC data cover $5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 30$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for $x \geq 0.7$; BCDMS data extend to $Q^2 \lesssim 250$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for $x \leq 0.75$.
- Best scaling is achieved with spectator mass parameters $m_0 = 0$ and $C = 3$ (GeV)$^2$; scaling deteriorates for $m_0 = 600$ MeV.
- The ratio $r = F_2^{NS}(x,Q^2)/F_2(x,Q^2)$ is approximately 0.96 at $x = 0.35$ and exceeds 0.995 for $x \geq 0.55$ and $Q^2 \geq 5$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- The combined power-plus-logarithmic model reproduces data for both $\Lambda = 100$ MeV and $\Lambda = 200$ MeV, with slightly better agreement for $\Lambda = 100$ MeV.
- The deuteron structure function ratio $2F_2^d/(F_2^p+F_2^n)$ is about 1.13 at $x = 0.9$ and $Q^2 = 5$ (GeV/c)$^2$, and within 5% of unity for $x < 0.85$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gurvitz (1995), who proposed the $\bar x$ variable from a Bethe-Salpeter analysis of confining final-state interactions, and contrasts with the Nachtmann variable $\xi$ (Nachtmann, 1972) which accounts only for target mass effects. The authors show that $\bar x$ with $m_0 = 0$ provides significantly better scaling of SLAC data than either the Bjorken variable $x$ or the Nachtmann variable $\xi$. The combined treatment of power corrections via $\bar x$ and NLO QCD evolution enables a unified description of $F_2$ data from SLAC, BCDMS, and NMC across a wide $Q^2$ range, and allows determination of the spectator diquark mass parameter.
