# The deconfined phase near Tc and its decay into hadrons

> We sketch an effective theory for the deconfined state of QCD near Tc. This relates the behavior of the expectation value of the Polyakov loop, and its two-point functions, to the pressure. Defining the ``mass'' of three and two gluon states from the imaginary and real parts of the Polyakov loop, while this ratio is 3:2 in perturbation theory, at Tc it is 3:1. We also discuss the decay of the deconfined state into hadrons.

# The deconfined phase near Tc and its decay into hadrons

## Key claim

The ratio of the "mass" of three-gluon states to two-gluon states, defined from the imaginary and real parts of the Polyakov loop two-point functions, is 3:1 at Tc+ rather than the perturbative 3:2, and this ratio increases sharply from approximately 1.5 at high temperature to approximately 2.5 near Tc according to recent lattice studies.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for the Polyakov loop ℓ as an order parameter for deconfinement, with terms respecting global Z(3) symmetry and a cubic term ∼b3 ℓ³ that breaks U(1) to Z(3). They perform a mean-field analysis where the mass term b₂ changes sign to drive condensation near Tc, and relate the curvature of the effective potential to the masses m_r and m_i extracted from the exponential falloff of the real and imaginary parts of the Polyakov loop two-point functions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio m_i/m_r equals 3/2 in perturbation theory, but at Tc+ the effective theory predicts m_i/m_r = 3 (plus corrections from pentic and hexatic interactions)
- Lattice studies in SU(3) gauge theory find the mass ratio of a three-gluon versus two-gluon state increases from approximately 1.5 at high temperature to approximately 2.5 near Tc
- The mass of the Polyakov loop m_ℓ is approximately 400 MeV just below Tc, giving a correlation length ξ_ℓ ≈ 1/2 fm
- The K:π ratio from experiment is approximately 1:10, which the model reproduces when m_π < m_ℓ < m_K

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov loop model of Pisarski (2000) to relate the effective theory for the deconfined state to lattice observables such as the pressure and screening masses. The prediction that three-gluon states are three times heavier than two-gluon states at Tc+ deviates strongly from perturbative counting and is supported by lattice studies of Datta (2002). The model enables a dynamical description of hadronization where the decay of the Polyakov loop condensate determines chemical composition, contrasting with quasi-gluon decay models of Biro which would predict K:π near unity. The explosive decay scenario provides a possible explanation for the RHIC HBT result Rout ≈ Rside, as argued by Csorgo and Csernai.
