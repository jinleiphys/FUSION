# Color-flavor locked strange quark matter in a strong magnetic field

> The quark quasiparticle model is extended to study the properties of color-flavor locked strange quark matter at finite chemical potential and in a strong magnetic field. We present a self-consistent thermodynamic treatment by employing a chemical potential dependent bag function. It is found that the magnetized color-flavor-locked (MCFL) matter is more stable than other phases within a proper magnitude of magnetic field. The stability window is graphically shown for the MCFL matter compared with ordinate magnetized matter. The anisotropic structure of MCFL matter is dominated by the magnetic field and almost independent of the energy gaps. A critical maximum magnetic field of about $1.56\times 10^{18}$ G is found, under which MCFL matter is absolutely stable with respect to nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that magnetized color-flavor-locked (MCFL) strange quark matter is more stable than other phases (ordinary strange quark matter, magnetized unpaired matter, and unmagnetized CFL matter) within a proper magnitude of magnetic field, but this stability ordering depends on the specific values of the pairing gaps; if the CFL gap is large enough (approximately three times the MCFL gap Δ_H), the unmagnetized CFL phase can become more stable than the magnetized unpaired phase.

## Method

The authors extend the quark quasiparticle model, which introduces a medium-dependent effective quark mass derived from the hard dense loop approximation, to describe color-flavor locked (CFL) and magnetized CFL (MCFL) matter at zero temperature. They enforce thermodynamic self-consistency by deriving a chemical-potential-dependent and field-strength-dependent effective bag function B* through integration of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the effective mass. For MCFL matter, the thermodynamic potential includes Landau level quantization of charged quarks in a constant magnetic field along the z-axis, using the rotated charge basis appropriate for the CFL phase, and the anisotropic parallel and transverse pressures are computed including the Maxwell field contribution.

## Key numbers

- Critical maximum magnetic field for absolute stability of MCFL matter with respect to nuclear matter: about 1.56 × 10^18 G.
- For the numerical stability comparison, the adopted energy gaps are φ = 15 MeV and Δ_H = 20 MeV for MCFL matter.
- The constant term B_0 in the effective bag function is (145 MeV)^4.
- Quark current masses used: m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 10 MeV, m_s = 120 MeV.
- The threshold magnetic field for noticeable pressure anisotropy is about 3 × 10^17 G, common to both MCFL and magnetized unpaired matter.
- The stability window is defined by energy per baryon less than 930 MeV (the value for 56Fe nucleus) at zero pressure.

## Context

This work extends the quark quasiparticle model previously applied to strangelets (Wen 2009) and magnetized strange quark matter (Wen 2012) to the color-flavor locked phase in a strong magnetic field. It builds on studies of magnetized CFL matter in the NJL model (Ferrer 2005, 2006; Noronha 2007) and the bag model (Felipe 2005, 2011), and on the thermodynamic self-consistency approach developed by Schertler (1997) and Gorenstein (1995). The results enable future investigation of MCFL matter as a candidate phase in the interiors of neutron stars and pulsars, where both pairing and magnetic field effects may stabilize strange quark matter even when ordinary strange quark matter would not be allowed.
