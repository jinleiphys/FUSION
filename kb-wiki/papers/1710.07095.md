# Coupled kinetic equations for quarks and gluons in the relaxation time approximation

> Kinetic equations for quarks and gluons are solved numerically in the relaxation time approximation for the case of one-dimensional boost-invariant geometry. Quarks are massive and described by the Fermi-Dirac statistics, while gluons are massless and obey Bose-Einstein statistics. The conservation laws for the baryon number, energy, and momentum lead to two Landau matching conditions which specify the coupling between the quark and gluon sectors and determine the proper-time dependence of the effective temperature and baryon chemical potential of the system. The numerical results illustrate how a non-equlibrium mixture of quarks and gluons approaches hydrodynamic regime described by the Navier-Stokes equations with appropriate forms of the kinetic coefficients. The shear viscosity of a mixture is the sum of the shear viscosities of quark and gluon components, while the bulk viscosity is given by the formula known for a gas of quarks, however, with the thermodynamic variables characterising the mixture. Thus, we find that massless gluons contribute in a non-trivial way to the bulk viscosity of a mixture, provided quarks are massive. We further observe the hydrodynamization effect which takes place earlier in the shear sector than in the bulk one. The numerical studies of the ratio of the longitudinal and transverse pressures show, to a good approximation, that it depends on the ratio of the relaxation and proper times only. This behaviour is connected with the existence of an attractor solution for conformal systems.

## Key claim

The paper numerically solves coupled kinetic equations for a mixture of massive quarks and massless gluons in the relaxation time approximation, finding that the system approaches hydrodynamic behavior described by Navier-Stokes equations, with the key qualifier that massless gluons contribute nontrivially to the bulk viscosity only when quarks are massive.

## Method

The authors solved three coupled relativistic Boltzmann transport equations for quark, antiquark, and gluon phase-space distribution functions using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) with a constant relaxation time, assuming one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) geometry and transverse homogeneity. The Landau matching conditions for baryon number and energy-momentum conservation were used to determine the proper-time dependence of the effective temperature and baryon chemical potential, with the formal analytic solutions of the kinetic equations expressed as integrals over the time history of the system.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass is finite and constant (value not specified numerically in the text); gluons are treated as massless
- Two quark flavors (up and down) with equal mass are assumed, reflecting SU(2) isospin symmetry
- The degeneracy factors are: g_{Q±} = 3 × 2 × N_f for (anti)quarks and g_G = 8 × 2 for gluons
- The anisotropy parameters ξ_s vary in the range -1 < ξ_s < ∞, with ξ_s = 0 corresponding to isotropic momentum distribution
- The damping function for constant relaxation time is D(τ₂,τ₁) = exp[-(τ₂-τ₁)/τ_eq]

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-gluon mixtures (Florkowski 2012, Florkowski 2013, Florkowski 2014, Florkowski 2015) that were restricted to massless particles and done mostly in the context of anisotropic hydrodynamics. The paper restricts itself to kinetic-theory study, leaving anisotropic hydrodynamics for separate investigation, and uses first-order Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics results to demonstrate hydrodynamization (Heller 2011). The observed scaling behavior of the longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio is connected to the existence of an attractor solution for conformal systems (Heller 2015, Romatschke 2016, Spalinski 2016, Romatschke 2017, Spalinski 2017, Strickland 2017) and more recently for non-conformal systems (Romatschke 2017).
