# Coulomb corrections to density and temperature of bosons in heavy ion collisions

> A recently proposed method, based on quadrupole and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, is modified in order to take into account distortions due to the Coulomb field. This is particularly interesting for bosons produced in heavy ion collisions, such as $d$ and $α$ particles. We derive temperatures and densities seen by the bosons and compare to similar calculations for fermions. The resulting energy densities agree rather well with each other and with the one derived from neutrons. This suggests that a common phenomenon, such as the sudden opening of many reaction channels and/or a liquid gas phase transition, is responsible for the agreement.

## Key claim

The paper modifies a previously proposed method for extracting temperature and density from quadrupole and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions to include Coulomb distortions, and shows that for bosons (deuterons and alpha particles) the resulting energy densities agree with those derived from neutrons, suggesting a common phenomenon such as the sudden opening of reaction channels or a liquid-gas phase transition.

## Method

The authors use Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) simulations of 40Ca+40Ca collisions at impact parameter b=1 fm for beam energies from 4 to 100 MeV/nucleon, generating about 250,000 events per energy. They derive modified Bose-Einstein distribution functions that include a Coulomb correction term A'/(y V T^2) in the exponent, then solve coupled equations for quadrupole fluctuations, multiplicity fluctuations, and average multiplicity to extract temperature T, volume V, and chemical potential nu for deuterons and alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies range from 4 MeV/nucleon to 100 MeV/nucleon in the laboratory system for 40Ca+40Ca collisions at impact parameter b=1 fm
- About 250,000 events were generated for each beam energy
- The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition is about 4 MeV, where different particles experience a sudden increase of degrees of freedom
- The quantity A' = (1.44 x 4 pi hbar^2 Z_d Z_s)/(2m) defines the Coulomb correction strength, where Z_d and Z_s are the charges of the emitted particle and source respectively
- The b_j distribution from CoMD at 35 MeV/nucleon shows an average close to zero, indicating most final fragments are fermion-like

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fluctuation-based method (Zheng et al., 2013) by adding Coulomb corrections that were previously applied only to fermions (Zheng et al., 2013). The results contradict the need for classical or ergodic assumptions, showing that different particle types give similar temperatures and densities when Coulomb effects are included. The paper enables experimental selection of events based on the b_j parameter to search for possible Bose condensate signatures in nuclei, and suggests comparing d-like, alpha-like, and fermion-like events to further constrain the nuclear equation of state in the liquid-gas phase transition region.
