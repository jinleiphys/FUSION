# Shape coexistence in 153Ho

> The high-spin states in 153Ho, have been studied by 139 57 La(20Ne, 6n) reaction at a projectile energy of 139 MeV at Variable Energy Cyclotron Centre (VECC), Kolkata, India, utilizing an earlier campaign of Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) setup. Data from gamma-gamma coincidence, directional correlation and polarization measurements have been analyzed to assign and confirm the spins and parities of the levels. We have suggested a few additions and revisions of the reported level scheme of 153Ho. The RF-gamma time difference spectra have been useful to confirm the half-life of an isomer in this nucleus. From the comparison of experimental and theoretical results, it is found that there are definite indications of shape coexistence in this nucleus. The experimental and calculated lifetimes of several isomers have been compared to follow the coexistence and evolution of shape with increasing spin.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shape coexistence in 153Ho is indicated by a sudden transition from prolate to oblate deformation at a rotational frequency of about 0.25 MeV, as predicted by Total Routhian Surface calculations, with the experimental level scheme and isomer lifetimes supporting this interpretation.

## Method

High-spin states in 153Ho were populated via the 139La(20Ne, 6n) reaction at 139 MeV beam energy using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) with six Compton-suppressed Clover detectors. Gamma-gamma coincidence, directional correlation (DCO) and integrated polarization asymmetry (IPDCO) measurements were analyzed to assign spins and parities, while RF-gamma time difference spectra were used to determine isomer half-lives. Total Routhian Surface (TRS) and Particle Rotor Model (PRM) calculations were performed to interpret the experimental results.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of the isomer at 2772 keV: 251 +54 -38 ns, determined from RF-gamma TAC spectra gated by the 533 keV transition.
- Prompt time resolution of the TAC spectrum: 15.23 +/- 0.23 ns, determined from the 913 keV transition.
- The isomer at 9074 keV has a lifetime around 50 ns, instead of the previously reported approximately 300 ps, based on intensity ratios from 200 ns and 800 ns time-gated matrices.
- TRS calculations predict a near-prolate deformation of beta2 approximately 0.20 at low rotational frequencies (hbar omega = 0.20 - 0.24 MeV).
- The shape transition to oblate occurs at hbar omega = 0.25 MeV, with angular momentum increasing from J approximately 9 to 21 over a rotational frequency range of 0.25 to 0.28 MeV, contributing 12 units of angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 153Ho by Radford et al. (1990) and others, which had uncertainties in spin and parity assignments and suggested triaxial shapes. The paper also builds on systematic studies of holmium isotopes initiated by Dey et al. and others to understand structural differences from the neighboring dysprosium isotopes (Z=66). The results enable further investigation of shape coexistence and evolution in neutron-deficient rare-earth nuclei near the N=86 and N=88 isotones, particularly for odd-Z nuclei.
