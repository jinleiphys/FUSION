# Thermal description of transverse-momentum spectra at RHIC

> We show that the transverse-momentum spectra of all hadrons measured at RHIC, including hyperons, are described very well in a thermal model assuming the simultaneous chemical and thermal freeze-outs. The model calculation takes into account all hadronic resonances and uses a simple parametrization of the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple thermal model with simultaneous chemical and thermal freeze-out, using only four parameters (two thermodynamic and two geometric), reproduces the transverse-momentum spectra of all measured hadrons at RHIC, including hyperons, at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 130 GeV. The hedge is that the geometric parameters fitted to pions, kaons, and protons only are then used to predict the spectra of other particles, and the model's HBT radii are too small unless excluded-volume corrections are applied.

## Method

The authors combine a thermal model for hadron yields with a hydrodynamic expansion model at freeze-out, assuming a single freeze-out hypersurface of constant proper time \(\tau\) (generalized Bjorken model) and a Hubble-like flow. They use the Cooper-Frye formalism to compute transverse-momentum spectra, including all hadronic resonances, with thermodynamic parameters \(T = 165\) MeV and \(\mu_B = 41\) MeV fixed from earlier analysis of hadron multiplicity ratios. The geometric parameters \(\tau\) and \(\rho_{\rm max}\) (transverse size) are fitted to the spectra of pions, kaons, and protons for each centrality class.

## Key numbers

- Thermodynamic parameters: \(T = 165\) MeV and \(\mu_B = 41\) MeV, obtained from analysis of hadron multiplicity ratios at RHIC.
- For minimum-bias data: \(\tau = 5.55\) fm and \(\rho_{\rm max} = 4.50\) fm.
- For most central collisions: \(\tau = 7.66\) fm and \(\rho_{\rm max} = 6.69\) fm.
- The model reproduces the crossing of pion and antiproton spectra at \(p_\perp \sim 2\) GeV.
- The \(K^*(892)\) lifetime is \(\tau = 4\) fm/c, used to argue that rescattering time between chemical and thermal freeze-out is short.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach previously applied to hadron yields at SPS and RHIC (e.g., Rafelski, Braun-Munzinger, and collaborators) by adding a simple hydrodynamic expansion to describe transverse-momentum spectra. It contradicts the need for a separate thermal freeze-out stage, supported by STAR measurements of \(K^*(892)\) yields and the antibaryon puzzle (Rapp, 2002). The model enables further tests of thermalization at RHIC and challenges full hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., from the University of Helsinki group) to reproduce the specific freeze-out conditions, with first steps taken by Csorgo and collaborators.
