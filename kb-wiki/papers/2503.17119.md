# Many-Body Effects on Nuclear Short Range Correlations

> We reveal nuclear many-body effects on short range correlations by ab initio no-core shell model calculations of the scaling factor a2. The factor a2 characterizes the abundance of SRC pairs and is linearly related to the EMC effect. Our study employs the fifth-order N4LO chiral nuclear force without softening, enabling to distinguish the influences of nuclear states with different quantum numbers on SRC. It is striking to find that a2 is reduced and close in triplet isobaric analog states of neighboring nuclei, indicating that it is insufficient to estimate SRC abundances by considering only mean-field shell structures. This is explained as specific nuclear states suppress the formation of deuteron-like component, impacting our understandings of the link between high-energy partonic properties and low-energy nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper reveals that the scaling factor \(a_2\) (which characterizes short-range correlation pair abundance) is reduced and nearly equal in triplet isobaric analog states of neighboring nuclei, indicating that estimating SRC abundances by considering only mean-field shell structures is insufficient; the suppression of deuteron-like components in specific nuclear states is the cause.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the one-nucleon momentum distribution for \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, \(^4\)He, and states of \(^6\)Li using the fifth-order N\(^4\)LO chiral nucleon-nucleon force without softening. They unfolded the center-of-mass motion from the space-fixed densities using Fourier methods to obtain translationally invariant densities, then extracted the scaling factor \(a_2\) by integrating the high-momentum tail of the one-nucleon distribution relative to the deuteron. Shell model calculations with the Moshinsky transformation were used to extract the probability of the \(^3S_1\) (deuteron-like) component in the two-valence-nucleon configurations.

## Key numbers

*   The scaling factor \(a_2\) for \(^4\)He was calculated to be 3.4 by NCSM.
*   The calculated excited energies of the \(3^+\) and \(0^+\) states in \(^6\)Li are 2.68 MeV and 4.19 MeV, respectively, compared to experimental values of 2.186 MeV and 3.563 MeV.
*   The \(^3S_1\) component probabilities in \(^6\)Li are 36.63% for the \(1^+\) ground state, 50% for the \(3^+\) state, and 0% for the \(0^+\) state.
*   The integration threshold \(k_0\) for evaluating \(a_2\) was chosen as 2 fm\(^{-1}\), with the Fermi momentum \(k_F\) noted as approximately 1.4 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of short-range correlations beyond ground states to excited states, using a hard nonlocal chiral interaction (N\(^4\)LO from Entem et al. (2017)) that is not accessible to Variational Monte Carlo methods (Wiringa et al., 2014; Piarulli et al., 2023). The results contradict the simple expectation that SRC abundance scales only with the number of neutron-proton pairs, and instead show that the specific quantum state (angular momentum, parity, isospin) and the formation of deuteron-like \(^3S_1\) pairs are decisive. This enables future experimental studies of SRC in short-lived exotic nuclei and isomeric states using hadronic probes.
