# Elliptical Flow in Relativistic Ion Collisions at s^(1/2)= 200 A GeV

> A consistent picture of the Au+Au and D+Au, s^1/2 = 200 A GeV measurements at RHIC obtained with the PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS and BRAHMS detectors including both the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra was previously developed with the simulation LUCIFER. The approach was modeled on the early production of a fluid of pre-hadrons after the completion of an initial, phase of high energy interactions. The formation of pre-hadrons is discussed here, in a perturbative QCD approach as advocated by Kopeliovich, Nemchik and Schmidt. In the second phase of LUCIFER, a considerably lower energy hadron-like cascade ensues. Since the dominant collisions occurring in this latter phase are meson-meson in character while the initial collisions are between baryons, i.e. both involve hadron sized interaction cross-sections, there is good reason to suspect that the observed elliptical flow will be produced naturally, and this is indeed found to be the case.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the observed elliptical flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is produced naturally by the LUCIFER simulation without parameter adjustment, provided that a fluid of colour-neutral pre-hadrons with hadron-sized interaction cross-sections is present from an early time; the hedge is that this picture may be of less validity for the lowest transverse momentum charged particles but becomes increasingly accurate with rising transverse momentum.

## Method

The LUCIFER simulation is divided into two phases: Phase I tracks initial nucleon-nucleon interactions using measured hadron-hadron cross-sections and KNO scaling for multiplicities, while Phase II models a cascade of pre-hadrons (colour-neutral quark-antiquark pairs) that interact with hadron-sized cross-sections and decay after a formation time of about 1.0 fm. The pre-hadron production time is estimated using a perturbative QCD approach from Kopeliovich, Nemchik and Schmidt (2007), and the simulation is constrained by fitting to elementary nucleon-nucleon data and previous calculations of rapidity and transverse momentum spectra for D+Au and Au+Au.

## Key numbers

- The parton-parton cross-section must be made as large as 49 mb to yield measured ellipticities in parton cascade models, according to Molnar.
- The pre-hadron mass distribution is Gaussian with center m0 ~ 700 MeV for non-strange mesonic pre-hadrons and m0 ~ 950 MeV for strange pre-hadrons, with width w = m0/4.
- The pre-hadron decay time in the colliding frame is uniformly taken as τ_f ~ 1.0 fm.
- The NN pT spectrum at pT ~ 1 GeV has dropped by close to two orders of magnitude below the softest transverse momenta measured.
- Eliminating Phase II reduces the ellipticity v2 by some 25-30%, depending on centrality.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LUCIFER simulations of D+Au and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV (Kahana and Kahana, 2005; 2007) to the calculation of elliptical flow, and contrasts with parton cascade models studied by Molnar and Gyulassy (2001) which required either parton coalescence or cross-sections as large as 49 mb to produce sufficient flow. The approach is closest in spirit to RQMD and work by Gallmeister, Greiner and Xu, as well as Cassing and Wolschin, who separate initial parton-dominated processes from hadronic interactions. The results enable future work evolving the first stage with allowances for mutual crossing between parallel quark-gluon and hadronic simulations.
