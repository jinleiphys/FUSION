# From Complexity to Clarity: Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks in Nuclear Binding Energy Prediction

> This study explores the application of Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) in predicting nuclear binding energies, leveraging their ability to decompose complex multi-parameter systems into simpler univariate functions. By utilizing data from the Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME2020) and incorporating features such as atomic number, neutron number, and shell effects, KANs achieved a significant lower root mean square error (0.26~MeV), surpassing traditional models. The symbolic regression analysis yielded simplified analytical expressions for binding energies, aligning with classical models like the liquid drop model and the Bethe-Weizsäcker formula. These results highlight KANs' potential in enhancing the interpretability and understanding of nuclear phenomena, paving the way for future applications in nuclear physics and beyond.

## Key claim
Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) achieve a root mean square error of 0.26 MeV for nuclear binding energy predictions using data from AME2020, but this performance depends on including shell-structure and pairing features; with only neutron number and proton number as inputs the RMSE is 0.87 MeV, and the model shows larger errors for light nuclei compared to medium and heavy nuclei.

## Method
The authors trained shallow KANs with one hidden layer of 12 neurons on 3456 nuclei from AME2020 (2856 training, 600 test), using B-spline basis functions for the learnable activation functions. They evaluated four feature sets (KAN-2 through KAN-11) incorporating atomic number, neutron number, mass number, isospin asymmetry, surface term, pairing indicators, and shell-structure variables, with 3-fold cross-validation and L1 regularization. Symbolic regression was applied to extract analytical expressions from the trained networks.

## Key numbers
- KAN-11 model achieved RMSE of 0.26 MeV for the full dataset (training and test sets combined) using 11 features including shell and pairing terms
- KAN-2 model with only neutron number and proton number achieved RMSE of 0.87 MeV
- KAN-2 with architecture [2,5,1] contained 450 free parameters; KAN-11 with [11,12,1] contained 4320 free parameters
- Comparison models: WS4 achieved 0.285 MeV, FRDM2012 achieved 0.559 MeV, HFB-31 achieved 0.576 MeV; Bayesian neural networks achieved 84 keV (0.084 MeV)
- Gaussian process regression achieved errors of 0.14 to 0.96 MeV for training set and 0.26 to 1.08 MeV for test set against AME2020
- Support vector regression achieved errors of 0.39 to 2.55 MeV for test set and 0.23 to 2.40 MeV for training set against AME2020

## Context
This work extends machine learning approaches to nuclear mass prediction, comparing with mixture density networks (PhysRevC.106.014305), Gaussian processes and support vector regression (ml2024), and Bayesian neural networks (PhysRevC.106.L021303). The KAN approach provides interpretable symbolic expressions that align with the liquid drop model and Bethe-Weizsacker formula, unlike black-box neural networks. The method enables future extraction of analytical mass formulas from data and could be extended to heavier nuclei beyond the AME2020 dataset, though extrapolation beyond known regions remains challenging due to B-spline edge behavior.
