# Nuclear Matrix Elements of Axial-Charge Exchange Currents Derived in Heavy-Fermion Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We calculate shell-model matrix elements of the axial-charge exchange current operators that have been obtained up to the next-to-leading order from heavy-fermion chiral perturbation theory. It is found that loop corrections to the soft one-pion-exchange contribution are small (around 10 \%) and have no significant dependence on the nuclear mass number or on the valence-nucleon orbits. These results render further support to the chiral-filtering conjecture.

## Key claim

The loop corrections to the soft one-pion-exchange contribution to the axial-charge operator are small (around 10%) and show no significant dependence on nuclear mass number or valence-nucleon orbits, supporting the chiral-filtering conjecture; however, the results depend on the choice of short-range correlation function, which introduces model dependence particularly for the shorter-ranged loop contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated shell-model matrix elements of axial-charge exchange current operators derived up to next-to-leading order in heavy-fermion chiral perturbation theory, using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for closed-shell-plus-one nuclei. They decomposed the two-body operator into a tree-level soft-pion-exchange part and loop corrections (one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange parts), and evaluated the ratio of each contribution to the one-body impulse-approximation matrix element for various transitions across nuclei from A=16 to A=208, using three different short-range correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Loop correction relative to tree contribution (eta_loop) for the 1s1/2 to 0p1/2 transition in A=16: 10.2% with PMR parameters and step-function correlation with d=0.71 fm; ranges from 7.7% to 10.5% across all nuclei and transitions studied with that correlation
- Tree-level contribution (delta_tree) for the 1s1/2 to 0p1/2 transition in A=16: 0.407 with PMR parameters and d=0.71 fm correlation
- Total two-body contribution (delta_mec) for the 1s1/2 to 0p1/2 transition in A=16: 0.449 with PMR parameters and d=0.71 fm correlation
- Total two-body contribution (delta_mec) for the 1g9/2 to 0h9/2 transition in A=208: 0.602 with PMR parameters and d=0.71 fm correlation
- Pion-decay constant: 93.0000 MeV (PMR set) and 86.3982 MeV (OBEPR set)
- Pion mass: 139.5673 MeV (PMR) and 138.0300 MeV (OBEPR)
- Short-range correlation cut-off values: d1 = 0.71 fm (half the inverse pion mass), d2 = 0.5 fm, and qc = 3.93 fm^-1 for the 1 - j0(qc r) correlation

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Park, Min, and Rho (1993) from a simple Fermi-gas model to realistic shell-model wavefunctions, confirming that loop corrections remain small (around 10%) and mass-independent. The results support the chiral-filtering conjecture of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978), which states that soft-pion-exchange currents dominate over shorter-ranged contributions. The calculations also relate to the heavy-meson-exchange current approach of Kirchbach, Reinhardt, and Tegen (1992) and Towner (1992), where individually large short-range contributions tend to cancel; the present chiral perturbation theory framework provides a systematic expansion parameter that the heavy-meson approach lacks.
