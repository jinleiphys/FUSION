# N*(1535) electroproduction at high Q2

> A covariant spectator quark model is applied to study the gamma N -> N*(1535) reaction in the large Q2 region. Starting from the relation between the nucleon and N*(1535) systems, the N*(1535) valence quark wave function is determined without the addition of any parameters. The model is then used to calculate the gamma N -> N*(1535) transition form factors. A very interesting, useful relation between the A12 and S12 helicity amplitudes for Q2 > GeV^2, is also derived.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model can describe the $\gamma N \to N^\ast(1535)$ transition form factors at high $Q^2$ without additional parameters, but only the $F_1^\ast$ form factor agrees with data; the $F_2^\ast$ data require cancellation between valence quark and meson cloud effects, leading to $F_2^\ast \simeq 0$ for $Q^2 > 1.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The authors applied a covariant spectator quark model where baryons are described as three-valence quark systems with an on-shell quark-pair (diquark) of mass $m_D$. The $N^\ast(1535)$ valence quark wave function was derived from the nucleon wave function using the same scalar wave function parameters $\beta_1$ and $\beta_2$, with the $N^\ast(1535)$ state including a P-state in the relative quark-diquark configuration represented by a $\gamma_5$ factor. The $\gamma N \to N^\ast(1535)$ transition form factors $F_1^\ast$ and $F_2^\ast$ were calculated in a relativistic impulse approximation, and the helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$ were derived under the assumption $F_2^\ast = 0$.

## Key numbers

- The model is valid for $Q^2 > 2.3$ GeV$^2$, derived from the condition $Q^2 \gg |\mathbf{q}|^2 = 0.23$ GeV$^2$, where $|\mathbf{q}|$ is the photon 3-momentum in the $N^\ast(1535)$ rest frame at $Q^2=0$.
- The relation $S_{1/2} = - \frac{\sqrt{1+\tau}}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{M_S^2-M^2}{2 M_S Q} A_{1/2}$ holds for $Q^2 > 1.8$ GeV$^2$, where $\tau = Q^2/(M_S+M)^2$.
- Experimental data show $F_2^\ast \simeq 0$ for $Q^2 > 1.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The model and data for $A_{1/2}$ are consistent with a $1/Q^3$ asymptotic behavior, apart from small logarithmic corrections.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to the nucleon (Ramalho, 2010), $\Delta$ baryon (Ramalho, 2008), and higher resonances (Ramalho, 2010) to the $N^\ast(1535)$ resonance. The results contradict the pure valence quark core estimate from EBAC (2009) for $F_2^\ast$, supporting instead the interpretation that meson cloud effects cancel valence quark contributions, as suggested by effective chiral meson-baryon models (Jido, 2008; Jido, 2011). The derived relation between $A_{1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$ provides a testable prediction for future experiments at $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, and the work calls for further studies from quark models, dynamical coupled-channel models, chiral effective models, QCD sum rules (Braun, 2009), and lattice QCD.
