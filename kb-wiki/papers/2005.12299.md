# QCD thermalization: Ab initio approaches and interdisciplinary connections

> Heavy-ion collisions at BNL's Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and CERN's Large Hadron Collider provide strong evidence for the formation of a quark-gluon plasma, with temperatures extracted from relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations shown to be well above the transition temperature from hadron matter. How the strongly correlated quark-gluon matter forms in a heavy-ion collision, its properties off-equilibrium, and the thermalization process in the plasma, are outstanding problems in QCD. We review here the theoretical progress in this field in weak coupling QCD effective field theories and in strong coupling holographic approaches based on gauge-gravity duality. We outline the interdisciplinary connections of different stages of the thermalization process to non-equilibrium dynamics in other systems across energy scales ranging from inflationary cosmology, to strong field QED, to ultracold atomic gases, with emphasis on the universal dynamics of non-thermal and of hydrodynamic attractors. We survey measurements in heavy-ion collisions that are sensitive to the early non-equilibrium stages of the collision and discuss the potential for future measurements. We summarize the current state-of-the art in thermalization studies and identify promising avenues for further progress.

## Key claim
This review argues that the thermalization of quark-gluon matter in heavy-ion collisions can be understood through two complementary *ab initio* theoretical frameworks, weak-coupling Color Glass Condensate effective field theory and strong-coupling holographic approaches, but neither is directly applicable to real-world collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, so data-theory comparisons rely on phenomenological extrapolations beyond their strict regimes of validity.

## Method
The authors survey theoretical progress in QCD thermalization by examining weak-coupling effective field theories (the Color Glass Condensate EFT, which describes high-energy nuclei as coherent states of static color sources and dynamical gluon fields) and strong-coupling holographic approaches based on gauge-gravity duality (applied to N=4 superconformal Yang-Mills theory). They connect these frameworks to non-equilibrium dynamics in other systems, including inflationary cosmology, strong-field QED, and ultracold atomic gases, focusing on universal dynamics of non-thermal and hydrodynamic attractors, and they survey experimental measurements sensitive to early non-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s ≤ 0.2 (in natural units) for the quark-gluon plasma, close to the universal lower bound η/s = 1/(4π) from holography.
- Temperatures of approximately five trillion Kelvin attained at the highest LHC energies.
- The saturation scale Q_S emerges from the condition that gluon phase space density equals 1/α_S(Q_S), with the relation (1/α_S(Q_S)) = xG_A(x,Q_S^2) / [2(N_c^2-1) π R_A^2 Q_S^2].
- Unstable modes in the Glasma display quasi-exponential dynamical growth at very early proper times τ ≲ (1/Q_S) log²(1/α_S).

## Context
This review extends the theoretical understanding of QCD thermalization beyond earlier work on equilibrium properties of the quark-gluon plasma, building on the discovery of nearly perfect fluid behavior at RHIC and LHC (Romatschke, 2017) and the holographic bound on viscosity (Policastro, 2001; Kovtun, 2004). It connects to studies of non-thermal fixed points in scalar field theories (Berges, 2008) and hydrodynamic attractors (Heller, 2015; Romatschke, 2017), and it enables future work on constraining early-time dynamics through event engineering of final-state observables and through interdisciplinary comparisons with cold atom experiments (Prufer, 2018; Erne, 2018) and inflationary cosmology.
