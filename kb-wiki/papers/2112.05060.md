# Revisiting NLO QCD corrections to total inclusive J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction cross sections in lepton-proton collisions

> We revisit inclusive J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction at lepton-hadron colliders, namely in the limit when the exchanged photon is quasi real. Our computation includes the next-to-leading-order (NLO) alpha_s corrections to the leading-order contributions in v. Similarly to the case of NLO charmonium-hadroproduction processes, the resulting cross sections obtained in the MS-bar factorisation scheme are sometimes found to be negative. We show that the scale-fixing criteria which we derived in a previous study of eta(c) production successfully solves this problem from the EicC all the way up to the FCC-eh energies. We then elaborate on how to study a scale uncertainty akin to that derived by scale variations when one fixes a scale. In turn, we investigate where both J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction could be used to improve our knowledge of gluon content of the proton at scales as low as a couple of GeV.

## Key claim

The NLO QCD corrections to total inclusive J/psi photoproduction cross sections in the MS-bar factorisation scheme can become negative at high energies, but this unphysical behaviour is resolved by fixing the factorisation scale to mu_F = 0.86 M_Q, a prescription derived from requiring the partonic cross section to vanish in the high-energy limit to avoid oversubtraction by the Altarelli-Parisi counter terms.

## Method

The authors computed NLO alpha_s corrections to the leading-v contributions (Colour-Singlet Model) for inclusive J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction in lepton-proton collisions, using the scaling-function decomposition of Kramer (1995) and reproducing his results with FDC. They integrated over the quarkonium transverse momentum and elasticity z < 0.9, folded the partonic cross sections with CT18NLO, MSHT20nlo_as118, and NNPDF31_nlo_as_0118_hessian PDF sets, and applied a scale-fixing prescription where mu_F = M_Q exp(c^{(1)}_{gamma i}(s-hat to infinity)/(2 c-bar^{(1)}_{gamma i}(s-hat to infinity))) to cure negative cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The fixed factorisation scale for J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction is mu_F = 0.86 M_Q, derived from the high-energy limit of the scaling functions with A_{gamma i} = -0.29 for z < 0.9.
- For J/psi, the radial wave function squared at the origin is taken as |R(0)|^2 = 1.25 GeV^3, with m_c = 1.5 GeV and M_Q = 2 m_c = 3.0 GeV.
- For Upsilon(1S), |R(0)|^2 = 7.5 GeV^3, with m_b = 4.75 GeV and M_Q = 2 m_b = 9.5 GeV.
- The renormalisation scale range for J/psi is mu_R in [2.5, 10] GeV, with central value 5 GeV; for Upsilon(1S), mu_R in [8, 32] GeV, with central value 16 GeV.
- Feed-down contributions to J/psi from psi' decay are estimated at 20%; for Upsilon(1S), feed-down from Upsilon(2S) is 12.5% and from Upsilon(3S) is 2.2%.

## Context

This work extends the scale-fixing prescription previously applied to eta_c hadroproduction by Lansberg et al. (2020) to vector quarkonium photoproduction, solving the long-standing problem of negative NLO cross sections first encountered by Kramer (1994, 1995) in the same process. The results enable reliable predictions for total inclusive J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction at future lepton-hadron colliders from EicC to FCC-eh energies, and provide a framework for extracting gluon PDFs at scales as low as a few GeV, complementing earlier studies at HERA by the H1 and ZEUS collaborations.
