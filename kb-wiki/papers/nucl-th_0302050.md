# Low-energy scattering and photoproduction of $η$-mesons on three-body nuclei

> The optical potential approach for low-energy scattering of $η$-mesons on three-body nuclei is compared to an exact treatment of the $η3N$ system using four-body scattering theory with separable interactions in s-waves only. The higher-order terms including the interaction of the struck nucleon with the surrounding nuclear medium and virtual target excitations in between successive $η$-scatterings are found to cause important corrections. Effects of final state interaction in $η$-photoproduction on $^3$H and $^3$He are also studied and sizable contributions beyond the optical model approach are found.

## Key claim

The optical potential approach for low-energy $\eta$-meson scattering on three-body nuclei requires important corrections from higher-order terms including the interaction of the struck nucleon with the surrounding nuclear medium and virtual target excitations between successive $\eta$-scatterings; these corrections are sizable and the simplest impulse approximation significantly underestimates the interaction strength, contrary to what the scattering-length approximation would suggest.

## Method

The authors solved the $\eta\,3N$ four-body scattering problem exactly using separable interactions in $s$-waves only, employing the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method to reduce the four-body equations to numerically manageable form. They compared this exact treatment to the first-order optical potential (Kerman-McManus-Thaler formalism) in both the coherent approximation and the impulse approximation for $\eta$-$^3$H elastic scattering. For $\eta$-photoproduction on $^3$H and $^3$He, they included final-state interactions via the same four-body formalism and compared with the finite-rank-approximation (FRA) results of Shevchenko (2002).

## Key numbers

- $\eta N$ scattering length used: $a_{\eta N} = (0.50 + i0.32)$ fm
- $\eta$-$^3$H scattering length from exact four-body calculation: $a_{\eta^3\mathrm{H}} = (1.82 + i2.75)$ fm
- Pole position of the $\eta$-$^3$H scattering amplitude: $E_{\eta^3\mathrm{H}}^{\mathrm{pole}} \approx (1.53 + i3.59)$ MeV (virtual state on the nonphysical sheet)
- Triton binding energy from the separable $NN$ potential: $\varepsilon_{^3\mathrm{H}} = 8.64$ MeV
- Energy gap between coherent and incoherent thresholds for $\eta$-photoproduction on $^3$He: $\Delta E_\gamma = 7$ MeV
- Estimated energy shift of the $\eta N$ amplitude in the medium: $\Delta\omega \approx -15$ MeV
- Estimated probability for the two-step $\eta N \to \pi N \to \eta N$ process in $^3$H: $P \approx 1/10$

## Context

This work extends the earlier four-body treatment of the $\eta\,3N$ system by Fix and Arenhoevel (2002) and provides a systematic comparison with the optical model approach, which had been used previously for $\eta$-nuclear scattering by Wycech (1995) and others. The authors show that the impulse approximation fails for $\eta$-nuclear scattering due to the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance causing a non-negligible collision time ($\Delta t \approx 1.7 \times 10^{-23}$ s) that exceeds the pion-exchange time scale, unlike the pion-nuclear case where the impulse approximation works well (Ericson and Ericson, 1966). The results enable more reliable predictions for $\eta$-photoproduction experiments on $^3$He, such as the TAPS measurement at MAMI (Pfeiffer, 2002), and demonstrate that the $\eta\,3N$ system has a virtual state rather than a bound state, contradicting earlier speculations by Wilkin (1993).
