# A Detailed Analysis of the Special Points on $M-R$ Solutions of Hybrid (Twin) Stars

> Hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star cores is achieved in the present work with the help of Maxwell construction. For the purpose we employ six different and well-known hadronic models for the pure hadronic phase. The quark phase is described with the MIT Bag model in which the density dependence of the bag pressure $B(ρ)$ is invoked for different asymptotic values ($B_{as}$) of $B(ρ)$. The resulting hybrid star (HS) configurations exhibit twin star characteristics and distinct special points (SPs) on the mass-radius diagram of the HSs irrespective of the transition densities and the value of $B_{as}$. We find that for any particular value of $B_{as}$, the mass corresponding to SP ($M_{SP}$) and the maximum mass ($M_{max}$) of the HSs, obtained with different hadronic models, follow a nearly linear (fitted) relationship where the slope is independent of the value of $B_{as}$. The $M_{SP}-M_{max}$ dependence of the HSs is found to be consistent with any hadronic equation of state (EoS) chosen to obtain the hybrid EoS and thus such relations can be considered as universal relations in the context of formation of SPs. A change in the value of $B_{as}$ shifts the position of the fitted line in the $M_{SP}-M_{max}$ plane, with the linearity, however, retained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for hybrid stars formed via Maxwell construction with a density-dependent MIT Bag model, the mass at the special point (M_SP) and the maximum mass (M_max) follow a nearly linear relationship that is independent of the hadronic model and the asymptotic bag pressure value B_as, with only the intercept shifting when B_as changes.

## Method

The authors employed six different relativistic mean field (RMF) hadronic models (TM1, BSR2, BSR6, GM1, NL3ωρ4, and NL3) for the pure hadronic phase and the MIT Bag model with a Gaussian density-dependent bag pressure for the quark phase. Hadron-quark phase transition was achieved using Maxwell construction for three values of the asymptotic bag pressure B_as (30, 50, and 70 MeV fm^-3). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for the resulting hybrid star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with NL3 model and B_as=30 MeV fm^-3: 2.27 M_sun (highest among all models)
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with TM1 model and B_as=30 MeV fm^-3: 1.98 M_sun (lowest among all models)
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars with GM1 model: 2.03 M_sun for B_as=30 MeV fm^-3 and 1.80 M_sun for B_as=70 MeV fm^-3
- Hadron-quark transition densities for TM1 model: ρ_t^H/ρ_0 = 1.59 and ρ_t^Q/ρ_0 = 3.24 for B_as=30 MeV fm^-3; ρ_t^H/ρ_0 = 1.83 and ρ_t^Q/ρ_0 = 3.59 for B_as=70 MeV fm^-3
- Hadron-quark transition densities for NL3 model: ρ_t^H/ρ_0 = 1.31 and ρ_t^Q/ρ_0 = 3.22 for B_as=30 MeV fm^-3; ρ_t^H/ρ_0 = 1.51 and ρ_t^Q/ρ_0 = 3.53 for B_as=70 MeV fm^-3

## Context

This work extends prior studies on special points in hybrid star mass-radius diagrams, which were first identified by Yudin (2014) using the constant-speed-of-sound quark model and later studied by Cierniak (2018, 2019) who suggested SPs as a universal property of hybrid star models. The present work differs by using six different hadronic models with a density-dependent MIT Bag model and Maxwell construction, whereas Blaschke (2013) used the color superconducting nlNJL model and Ayriyan (2018) used interpolation procedures. The linear M_SP-M_max relation found here enables future observational constraints on the maximum mass of hybrid stars from the measured special point mass.
