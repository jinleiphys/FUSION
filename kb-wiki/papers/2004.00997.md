# Crucial role of neutron diffusion in the crust of accreting neutron stars

> Observed temperatures of transiently accreting neutron stars in the quiescent state are generally believed to be supported by deep crustal heating, associated with non-equilibrium exothermic reactions in the crust. Traditionally, these reactions are studied by considering nuclear evolution governed by compression of the accreted matter. Here we show that this approach has a basic weakness, that is that in some regions of the inner crust the conservative forces, applied for matter components (nuclei and neutrons), are not in mechanical equilibrium. In principle the force balance can be restored by dissipative forces, however the required diffusion fluxes are of the same order as total baryon flux at Eddington accretion. We argue that redistribution of neutrons in the inner crust should be involved in realistic model of accreted crust.

## Key claim

The traditional approach to modeling accreted neutron star crusts, which assumes all compositional changes arise solely from compression-driven nuclear reactions, is fundamentally inconsistent because it leads to strong violations of diffusion equilibrium in the inner crust; the required diffusion fluxes to restore mechanical balance are of the same order as the total baryon flux at Eddington accretion, meaning neutron redistribution must be included in realistic models.

## Method

The authors analyze force balance equations for electrons, nuclei, and free neutrons in the inner crust, assuming nonsuperfluid neutrons for simplicity. They compare the electron chemical potential profile as a function of pressure from multi-component accreted crust models (using several nuclear mass models: FRDM95+DZ31, FRDM12+DZ31, HFB21+DZ31, DZ31, and the compressible liquid drop model by Mackie and Baym (1977)) against the diffusion equilibrium condition derived from the force balance equations. They also estimate the neutron diffusion flux required to restore equilibrium using neutron-ion scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The neutron diffusion flux estimate: \(J_n \approx 3 \times 10^{28} g_{14} A_{70}^{1/3} \rho_{n,12}^{-1/3}\,\text{cm}^{-2}\,\text{s}^{-1}\), where \(g_{14}=g/10^{14}\,\text{cm s}^{-2}\), \(A_{70}=A/70\), and \(\rho_{n,12}=m_n n_n/10^{12}\,\text{g cm}^{-3}\)
- The total baryon flux at Eddington accretion: \(J^\mathrm{E}_\mathrm{acc} = \dot m^\mathrm{E} / m_\mathrm{U} = 6 \times 10^{28}\,\text{cm}^{-2}\,\text{s}^{-1}\) for \(\dot m^\mathrm{E}=10^5\,\text{g cm}^{-2}\,\text{s}^{-1}\)
- The neutron-ion scattering cross section used: \(\sigma_\mathrm{ni} \approx 10^{-23} (1+A^{1/3})^2\,\text{cm}^2\)
- In the pycnonuclear reaction region studied by Lau et al. (2018), the electron chemical potential increases by only 0.1 MeV over a density increase from \(\rho=1.7\times 10^{12}\,\text{g cm}^{-3}\) to \(\rho=2.4\times 10^{12}\,\text{g cm}^{-3}\)
- The fiducial gravitational acceleration assumed for column density estimates: \(g=2\times 10^{14}\,\text{cm s}^{-2}\)

## Context

This work extends the critique by Steiner (2012) that one-component models produce unphysical jumps in neutron number density, and shows that even multi-component models (such as those by Lau et al. (2018) and the authors' own previous work Shchechilin and Chugunov (2019)) violate diffusion equilibrium. The paper argues that neutron diffusion in the inner crust, previously considered for young neutron stars by Bisnovatyi-Kogan et al. (1976) and Bisnovatyi-Kogan and Chechetkin (1979), must be incorporated into accreting crust models, transforming the problem into a combined diffusion-nuclear burning calculation. The subsequent paper by Gusakov and Chugunov (2020) provides a diffusion equilibrium model accounting for neutron superfluidity.
