# Understanding long-range near-side ridge correlations in p$-$p collisions using rope hadronization at LHC energies

> The observation of long range ridge-like structure in the near-side region of the two particle $Δη-Δϕ$ correlations as measured by LHC experiments in high multiplicity p$-$p collisions indicated towards the presence of collective effects which are similar to that observed in p$-$A(nucleon-nucleus) and A$-$A (nucleus-nucleus) collisions. The two particle correlation between the charged particles in $Δη-Δϕ$ for p$-$p collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV and 13 TeV is studied using Pythia 8 event generator within the framework of final-state partonic color reconnection effects as well as the microscopic rope hadronization model. The rope hadronization relies on the formation of ropes due to overlapping of strings in high multiplicity events followed by string shoving. A near side ridge-like structure which is qualitatively similar to the observed ridge in data was observed for high-multiplicity events when the mechanism of rope hadronization (with shoving) was enabled.

# Understanding long-range near-side ridge correlations in p-p collisions using rope hadronization at LHC energies

## Key claim

The rope hadronization mechanism (with string shoving) implemented in Pythia 8 can produce a near-side ridge-like structure in two-particle correlations for high-multiplicity p-p collisions that is qualitatively similar to the ridge observed in LHC data, without requiring the formation of a deconfined, thermalized medium.

## Method

The authors generated 40 million p-p collision events at √s = 7 TeV and 13 TeV using the Pythia 8 event generator with the Monash 2013 tune, enabling multi-partonic interactions. They constructed two-dimensional Δη-Δϕ correlation functions for charged particle pairs in three multiplicity classes (0 < Ntrk < 20, 80 < Ntrk < 100, Ntrk > 100) with trigger and associated particles selected from pT ranges of 0.5-3 GeV/c and 1-3 GeV/c, and compared results with and without rope hadronization across three color reconnection modes (MPI-based CR0, QCD-based CR1, and gluon-move CR2).

## Key numbers

- 40 million events generated for p-p collisions at each of √s = 7 TeV and 13 TeV
- Trigger particles selected from pT range 0.5-3 GeV/c; associated particles from pT range 1-3 GeV/c
- Three multiplicity classes defined by Ntrk (charged particles within |η| < 0.5): 0 < Ntrk < 20, 80 < Ntrk < 100, and Ntrk > 100
- Long-range region defined as |Δη| > 2.0; short-range (jet) region defined as |Δη| < 1.0
- Mixed-event background constructed from 10 events
- Acceptance for charged particle selection: |η| < 2.4 and pT > 0.4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the DIPSY generator (which incorporated shoving in rope hadronization but used a biased sample of events with only long strings) by implementing the shoving mechanism in Pythia 8 for an unbiased study. It builds on prior observations by CMS (2010, 2016) and ATLAS of ridge-like structures in high-multiplicity p-p collisions at LHC, and on previous work by the authors and collaborators (Chakraborty and Dash, 2018; Bierlich et al., 2015, 2016) showing that rope hadronization could explain strangeness enhancement and resonance suppression in p-p collisions. The results suggest that microscopic partonic processes can mimic collective features without invoking hydrodynamic evolution (as in EPOS) or color glass condensate formation.
