# Fragmentation and systematics of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in the stable N=82 isotones

> The low-lying electric dipole (E1) strength in the semi-magic nucleus 136Xe has been measured which finalizes the systematic survey to investigate the so-called pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in all stable even N=82 isotones with the method of nuclear resonance fluorescence using real photons in the entrance channel. In all cases, a fragmented resonance-like structure of E1 strength is observed in the energy region 5 MeV to 8 MeV. An analysis of the fragmentation of the strength reveals that the degree of fragmentation decreases towards the proton-deficient isotones while the total integrated strength increases indicating a dependence of the total strength on the neutron-to-proton ratio. The experimental results are compared to microscopic calculations within the quasi-particle phonon model (QPM). The calculation includes complex configurations of up to three phonons and is able to reproduce also the fragmentation of the E1 strength which allows to draw conclusions on the damping of the PDR. Calculations and experimental data are in good agreement in the degree of fragmentation and also in the integrated strength if the sensitivity limit of the experiments is taken into account.

# Fragmentation and systematics of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in the stable N=82 isotones

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in the stable even N=82 isotones shows a fragmented resonance-like structure between 5 MeV and 8 MeV, with the degree of fragmentation decreasing toward proton-deficient isotones while total integrated strength increases, but the experimental results represent a lower limit because the finite sensitivity of nuclear resonance fluorescence (NRF) experiments misses weak states and the assumption of unity branching ratio for ground-state decay may overestimate extracted widths.

## Method

Nuclear resonance fluorescence (NRF) experiments using bremsstrahlung photons from the S-DALINAC at the Darmstadt High Intensity Photon Setup (DHIPS) were performed on 136Xe, with endpoint energies of 9.2 MeV and 8.0 MeV, using high-purity germanium detectors with BGO anti-Compton shields. The measured peak areas were converted to integrated cross sections using 11B calibration standards and GEANT4 simulations of the bremsstrahlung spectrum and detector efficiency, and angular distributions were used to assign J=1 to the excited states. The quasi-particle phonon model (QPM) calculations included configurations of up to three phonons built from phonons with multipolarities from 1± to 9± below 8.5 MeV, with all 1− one-phonon configurations below 20 MeV included in the wave function.

## Key numbers

- 76 newly observed states in 136Xe up to the neutron separation energy (Sn = 8.06 MeV), with excitation energies ranging from 3626 keV to 8093 keV
- Total integrated B(E1) strength in 136Xe increases compared to heavier isotones, with individual states reaching B(E1) values up to 36.3(19) × 10−3 e2fm2 (at 5888 keV)
- The QPM basis contains on average about 350 two-phonon and 900 three-phonon configurations per nucleus
- The 3−1 state energy systematically decreases from 136Xe to 144Sm while the 2+1 state energy remains nearly constant across all isotones
- The experimental sensitivity limit is approximately 2 × 10−3 e2fm2, below which states are systematically missed

## Context

This work completes a systematic survey of the PDR in all stable even N=82 isotones (136Xe, 138Ba, 140Ce, 142Nd, 144Sm), extending previous NRF studies on individual nuclei such as those by Volz (2006) and earlier QPM calculations for 138Ba by Herzberg (1999) and 140Ce by Herzberg (1997). The results enable quantitative comparison of PDR fragmentation patterns with microscopic theory, demonstrating that the damping mechanism arises from coupling of the collective mode to complex configurations, and provide a baseline for interpreting Coulomb excitation experiments on unstable neutron-rich nuclei such as those on Sn and Sb isotopes by Adrich (2005) and Klimkiewicz (2007) and on 68Ni by Wieland (2009).
