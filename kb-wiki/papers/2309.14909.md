# Coupled oscillator model of a trapped Fermi gas at the BEC-BCS crossover

> We address theoretically the puzzling discontinuity of the radial quadrupole mode frequency observed in a trapped Fermi gas across the BEC-BCS crossover. We apply the scaling transformation to a two-channel model of a resonant Fermi superfluid and argue that the frequency downshift in the crossover region is due to Feshbach coupling of the molecular Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) to the surrounding Fermi sea. The Bose and Fermi components of the gas act as coupled macroscopic oscillators. The frequency jump corresponds to the point where the closed-channel molecules are entirely converted into the Fermi sea. This implies linear scaling of the "critical" detuning between the scattering channels with the Fermi energy, which can be readily verified in an experiment.

# Coupled oscillator model of a trapped Fermi gas at the BEC-BCS crossover

## Key claim

The discontinuity in the radial quadrupole mode frequency across the BEC-BCS crossover arises because the molecular BEC and the BCS condensate of Cooper pairs act as two distinct macroscopic oscillators coupled via a coherent Feshbach link, and the frequency jump corresponds to the point where closed-channel molecules are entirely converted into the Fermi sea. The model predicts that the "critical" detuning between scattering channels scales linearly with the Fermi energy, which can be verified experimentally.

## Method

The author applies a scaling transformation to a two-channel model Hamiltonian of a resonant Fermi superfluid, treating the molecular condensate as a classical field and the fermions as second-quantized fields. The equations of motion for the scaling parameters are derived separately in the BEC limit (where the molecular condensate dominates) and the BCS limit (where fermions dominate), then coupled via the Feshbach interaction treated perturbatively. The resulting eigenvalue problem for the quadrupole mode frequency is solved using the local density approximation, with the anomalous average estimated from BCS theory.

## Key numbers

- In the BCS limit, the radial quadrupole mode frequency is $\omega_q^{(\mathrm{BCS})} = 2\omega_r$, where $\omega_r$ is the radial trapping frequency.
- In the BEC limit, the radial quadrupole mode frequency is $\omega_q^{(\mathrm{BEC})} = \sqrt{2}\omega_r$.
- The crossover range for the detuning parameter is $0 < \delta < 2\mu$, where $\mu$ is the Fermi energy in the BCS limit.
- The coupling parameters $\Gamma(\delta)$ and $\Lambda(\delta)$ in the perturbative result $\omega_q^{(\alpha)}(\delta) = \omega_r\sqrt{2[1+\Gamma(\delta)+\Lambda(\delta)]}$ are evaluated using the parameters of the Altmeyer (2007) experiment.
- The ratio of interaction strengths is $g_{FB}/g_{BB} \approx 3$ in 3D, following Petrov (2003, 2004).

## Context

This work addresses the puzzling discontinuity in the radial quadrupole mode frequency observed experimentally by Altmeyer (2007) and Vogt (2012), which contradicted the expectation of a smooth evolution between the hydrodynamic regime (BEC side) and the collisionless limit (BCS side). Previous theoretical attempts by Zhou (2008), Urban (2011, 2013), Bruun (2013), and Dong (2015) failed to explain the origin of this discontinuity. The model extends the two-channel approach of Gurarie (2007) and the author's own prior work (Andreev 2022) to collective dynamics, and predicts that the critical detuning scales as $\delta_c(N) = 2\mu(N)$, which can be tested in future experiments with varying particle number.
