# Hybrid neutron stars in the mass-radius diagram

> We present a systematic investigation of the possible locations for the special point (SP), a unique feature of hybrid neutron stars in the mass-radius. The study is performed within the two-phase approach where the high-density (quark matter) phase is described by the constant-sound-speed (CSS) equation of state (EoS) and the nuclear matter phase around saturation density is varied from very soft (APR) to stiff (DD2 with excluded nucleon volume. Different construction schemes for the deconfinement transition are applied: Maxwell construction, mixed phase construction and parabolic interpolation. We demonstrate for the first time that the SP is invariant not only against changing the nuclear matter EoS, but also against variation of the construction schemes for the phase transition. Since the SP serves as a proxy for the maximum mass and accessible radii of massive hybrid stars, we draw conclusions for the limiting masses and radii of hybrid neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that the "special point" (SP) in the mass-radius diagram of hybrid neutron stars is invariant not only against changes in the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS), but also against variations in the construction scheme used for the deconfinement phase transition (Maxwell construction, mixed phase construction, or parabolic interpolation).

## Method

The authors used a two-phase approach where the high-density quark matter phase is described by a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) equation of state, and the nuclear matter phase around saturation density is varied from very soft (APR) to stiff (DD2 with excluded nucleon volume). Different deconfinement transition constructions were applied: Maxwell construction, mixed phase construction via the replacement interpolation method (RIM), and parabolic interpolation. The location of the special point was systematically mapped across the mass-radius diagram by varying the CSS model parameters (A, c_s^2) and the hadronic EoS.

## Key numbers

- The minimal value of the CSS parameter A is A_min = 81 MeV/fm^3, derived from the condition that the onset density equals nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} with c_s^2 = 1 and ξ = 0.98.
- For c_s^2 = 0.40, the special point mass M_SP = 2.07 M_⊙, with predicted radius range for PSR J0740+6620 of R_min = 12.18 km and R_max = 12.29 km.
- For c_s^2 = 0.50, M_SP = 2.50 M_⊙, with R_min = 11.56 km and R_max = 13.91 km.
- For c_s^2 = 1.00, M_SP = 4.00 M_⊙, with R_min = 9.95 km and R_max = 14.84 km.
- The special point for a DD2p40 hadronic EoS with Maxwell construction and CSS parameters A = 91 MeV/fm^3, c_s^2 = 0.46 is located at M_SP = 2.17 M_⊙ and R_SP = 12.23 km, and remains unchanged when the Maxwell construction is replaced by a mixed phase construction with Δ_p = 4%.
- The lighter companion in GW190814 has mass M = 2.58^{+0.08}_{-0.09} M_⊙, which could be a hybrid star requiring c_s^2 ≳ 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of the special point concept introduced by Yudin (2014) and further developed by Cierniak (2020) and Blaschke (2020). It demonstrates that the special point invariance holds across different phase transition constructions, including the replacement interpolation method (RIM) from Abgaryan (2018) and Ayriyan (2017). The results enable systematic generation of hybrid star sequences that can masquerade as either soft or stiff hadronic stars, challenging conclusions from Pang (2021) that disfavored strong first-order phase transitions, and suggesting that very massive objects like the GW190814 companion could be hybrid stars.
