# Role of $Δ$s in determining the properties of Neutron Stars in parameterized hydrostatic equilibrium

> The possible existence of $Δ$ resonances is inspected in the cold dense matter of neutron star (NS) core in presence of the hyperons. The diverse effects of variation in $Δ$ mass on their formation and the equation of state (EoS) are studied in this work with an effective chiral model and the resultant NS properties are calculated with the help of parameterized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations (PTOV) to bring out the two important features of pressure in the context of massive NSs. The $Δ$ puzzle is re-explored and resolved taking into account the concept of modified/parameterized inertial pressure and self-gravity in case of massive pulsars like PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432. It is seen that although the presence of exotic matter like the hyperons and the $Δ$s softens the EoS considerably, their presence in massive NSs can be successfully explained with the theory of parameterized hydrostatic equilibrium conditions. The results of this work also satisfy the constraints on $R_{1.4}$ and $R_{1.6}$ from the gravitational wave (GW170817) detection of binary NS merger. The constraint on baryonic mass from PSR J0737-3039 is also satisfied with the solutions of the PTOV equations for all the $Δ$ masses considered.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the presence of Δ resonances and hyperons in neutron star cores, which softens the equation of state and normally prevents reaching high masses, can be reconciled with observations of massive pulsars (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432) by using parameterized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations that modify the treatment of inertial pressure and self-gravity, without invoking any specific modified gravity theory.

## Method

The author uses an effective chiral model with relativistic mean-field theory to compute the equation of state for neutron star matter containing nucleons, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and Δ resonances (Δ⁻, Δ⁰, Δ⁺, Δ⁺⁺) with varied Δ masses (1112, 1232, and 1352 MeV). The hyperon couplings are fixed using known potential depths (Λ at -28 MeV, Σ at +30 MeV, Ξ at -18 MeV), while Δ-meson couplings are chosen as (x_{σΔ}, x_{ωΔ}, x_{ρΔ}) = (1.35, 1.0, 1.0) based on QCD sum-rule prescriptions. Neutron star properties are then calculated using parameterized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with two free parameters β (inertial pressure contribution) and χ (self-gravity of pressure), while keeping other parameters at their general relativity values.

## Key numbers

- Δ resonance Breit-Wigner centroid mass: 1232 MeV with width of about 120 MeV, leading to varied masses of 1112, 1232, and 1352 MeV considered
- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Saturation properties of the model: binding energy B/A = -16.3 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32 MeV, incompressibility K = 303 MeV, slope parameter L₀ = 87 MeV, nucleon effective mass m*_N/m_N = 0.85
- For m_Δ = 1112 MeV: Δ⁻ appears first at 2.1 ρ₀, Δ⁰ at 4.9 ρ₀, Δ⁺ at 6.7 ρ₀, Δ⁺⁺ at 7.8 ρ₀
- For m_Δ = 1232 MeV: Λ appears first at 1.8 ρ₀, Ξ⁻ at 2.4 ρ₀, Ξ⁰ at 5.1 ρ₀, Δ⁻ at 8.0 ρ₀, Δ⁰ at 9.3 ρ₀
- For m_Δ = 1352 MeV: Λ appears at 1.8 ρ₀, Ξ⁻ at 2.4 ρ₀, Ξ⁰ at 5.1 ρ₀, Δ⁻ at 9.5 ρ₀, Δ⁰ at 10.5 ρ₀
- Observed masses used for constraints: PSR J1614-2230 at (1.928 ± 0.017) M_⊙, PSR J0348+0432 at (2.01 ± 0.04) M_⊙
- Radius constraints from GW170817: R_{1.4} < 13.76 km (Fattoyev) or 12.00 ≤ R_{1.4} ≤ 13.45 km (Most); R_{1.6} ≤ 13.3 km upper limit and R_{1.6} ≥ 10.68_{-0.04}^{+0.15} km lower limit

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hyperon puzzle and Δ puzzle in neutron stars by incorporating both hyperons and Δ resonances simultaneously within an effective chiral model, building on the author's prior work (Sen 2020) that studied Δ baryons alone. The parameterized TOV approach follows the formalism of Velten (2016) and is applied here to test whether modifications to general relativity's treatment of pressure can resolve the softening caused by exotic matter, similar to how the author's recent work (Sen 2020) constrained the β and χ parameters for hyperonic matter alone. The results satisfy constraints from gravitational wave event GW170817 on R_{1.4} and R_{1.6}, and the baryonic mass constraint from PSR J0737-3039, suggesting that the parameterized hydrostatic equilibrium approach can successfully explain massive neutron stars containing Δ resonances without requiring hadron-quark phase transitions or specific modified gravity theories.
