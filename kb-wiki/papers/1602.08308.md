# Thermodynamics and kinetics of Gribov-Zwanziger plasma with temperature dependent Gribov parameter

> A Gribov-type dispersion relation is treated as an effective description of interacting gluons forming a hot medium. Temperature dependence of the Gribov parameter is determined from the fit to the lattice Yang-Mills results describing thermodynamic functions. To maintain thermodynamic consistency of the approach, a temperature dependent bag pressure is introduced. The results obtained for equilibrium functions are generalised in the next step to non-equilibrium conditions. We derive formulas for the bulk and shear viscosity coefficients within the relaxation time approximation. We find evidence for largely enhanced bulk viscosity in the region of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The Gribov-Zwanziger plasma model, with a temperature-dependent Gribov parameter fitted to lattice Yang-Mills data and a thermodynamically consistent bag pressure, predicts a largely enhanced bulk viscosity near the phase transition temperature, but only when the temperature dependence of the Gribov parameter is included; the enhancement is absent if the Gribov parameter is treated as constant.

## Method

The authors fitted the temperature dependence of the Gribov parameter \(\gamma_\smallG(T)\) to lattice data for the entropy density from Borsanyi (2012), then determined the bag pressure \(B(T)\) from the condition of thermodynamic consistency (Eq. Delta1). Using this equilibrium model, they derived formulas for bulk and shear viscosities within the relaxation time approximation, generalizing earlier work by Florkowski (2015) where \(\gamma_\smallG\) was constant. The kinetic coefficients were obtained from linear-response solutions of the Boltzmann equation in a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature used for the pure Yang-Mills system is \(T_c = 260\ \mathrm{MeV}\), taken from the lattice data of Borsanyi (2012).
- The bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) shows a sharp peak near \(T_c\); the peak is absent when using a constant Gribov parameter \(\gamma_\smallG = 0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (dashed blue line in Fig. 6).
- In the high-temperature limit where \(\gamma_\smallG = \alpha T\) with constant \(\alpha\), the bulk viscosity vanishes.
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\) shows monotonic behavior for all three cases considered (Fig. 7), with no increase of \(\eta/s\) as \(T \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger plasma approach of Florkowski (2015) by introducing a temperature-dependent Gribov parameter fitted to modern lattice data from Borsanyi (2012), rather than treating it as constant. It contrasts with the quasiparticle model using a massive dispersion relation (Eq. EkM) as in Sasaki (2008), finding a significantly larger and narrower peak in bulk viscosity near \(T_c\). The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly regarding the possible cavitation effects from enhanced bulk viscosity suggested by Torrieri (2007).
