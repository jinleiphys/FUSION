# Triton binding energy calculated from the SU_6 quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Properties of the three-nucleon bound state are examined in the Faddeev formalism, in which the quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction is explicitly incorporated to calculate the off-shell T-matrix. The most recent version, fss2, of the Kyoto-Niigata quark-model potential yields the ground-state energy ^3H=-8.514 MeV in the 34 channel calculation, when the np interaction is used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The charge root mean square radii of the ^3H and ^3He are 1.72 fm and 1.90 fm, respectively, including the finite size correction of the nucleons. These values are the closest to the experiments among many results obtained by detailed Faddeev calculations employing modern realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models.

## Key claim

The Kyoto-Niigata quark-model potential fss2 yields a triton ground-state energy of -8.514 MeV in a 34-channel Faddeev calculation using the np interaction, which is closer to the experimental value than results from modern realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models, but still underbinds by 150-160 keV when charge dependence and charge asymmetry effects are included.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equation for the three-nucleon bound state by directly incorporating the off-shell T-matrix derived from the resonating-group method (RGM) exchange kernel of the quark-model nucleon-nucleon interaction. The energy dependence of the two-cluster RGM kernel was treated self-consistently through an iterative procedure. The continuous momentum variables were discretized using Gauss-Legendre quadrature formulas, and the Arnoldi-Lanczos algorithm was employed for diagonalization of the large non-symmetric matrix.

## Key numbers

- Triton ground-state energy E(3H) = -8.514 MeV for fss2 in the 34-channel calculation using the np interaction
- Triton ground-state energy E(3H) = -8.390 MeV for FSS in the 34-channel calculation using the np interaction
- Charge root mean square radius of 3H = 1.72 fm for fss2, including finite size correction of the nucleons
- Charge root mean square radius of 3He = 1.90 fm for fss2, including finite size correction of the nucleons
- Deuteron D-state probability P_D = 5.49% for fss2 and 5.86% for FSS
- Deuteron binding energy epsilon_d = 2.225 MeV for fss2
- Kinetic-energy contribution <H_0> = 43.95 MeV and potential-energy contribution <V> = -52.47 MeV for fss2 in the 34-channel calculation
- Energy gain from 5-channel to 34-channel calculation is about 330-360 keV

## Context

This work extends the Kyoto-Niigata quark-model approach (Fujiwara 1996, 2001, 2002) to three-nucleon systems by directly using the RGM exchange kernel rather than constructing effective local potentials. The results improve upon earlier quark-model Faddeev calculations by Takeuchi, Cheon and Redish (1992) and the Salamanca-Julich group (2001), and show that the quark-model potentials can reduce the missing binding energy beyond the two-nucleon force to less than half of the values found with one-boson exchange potential models such as the Bonn potentials (Machleidt 1989). The work enables further study of the off-shell behavior of the RGM T-matrix and its connection to short-range correlations in few-baryon systems, including hypertriton and other hypernuclear systems.
