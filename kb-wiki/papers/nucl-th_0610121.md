# Conical flow due to partonic jets in central Au+Au collisions

> In jet quenching, a hard QCD parton, before fragmenting into a jet of hadrons, deposits a fraction of its energy in the medium, leading to suppressed production of high-$p_T$ hadrons. The process can generate shock waves. We study the distortion of Mach shock waves due to jet quenching in central Au+Au collisions and its effect on particle production. Finite fluid velocity and inhomogeneity of the medium can distort the Mach shock front significantly such that the inside shock front disappear and the outside shock front is opened up. We also show that the STAR data on azimuthal distribution of background subtracted secondaries, associated with high $p_T$ trigger, are reasonably well explained by the excess pions produced due to partonic energy loss.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the azimuthal distribution of background-subtracted secondaries observed by STAR in central Au+Au collisions can be "reasonably well explained" by excess pions produced from partonic energy loss, but only after averaging over all possible jet trajectories and normalizing the calculated distribution; the match is qualitative rather than exact, and the calculation provides a "robust upper limit" due to the boost-invariance assumption.

## Method

The author numerically solves (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic equations with longitudinal boost invariance, modified by a source term representing a quenching parton jet that deposits energy and momentum into the medium. The jet energy loss rate is taken as proportional to the local entropy density, normalized to 14 GeV/fm at a reference entropy density of 140 fm⁻³, consistent with RHIC high-pT suppression data. The simulation is performed for central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=3 fm, with initial energy density ε₀=30 GeV/fm³ at τ₀=0.6 fm/c, using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition and hadronic freeze-out at Tc=164 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Jet energy loss rate: dE/dx|₀ = 14 GeV/fm at reference entropy density s₀ = 140 fm⁻³
- Initial peak energy density: ε₀ = 30 GeV/fm³ at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Critical temperature for phase transition: Tc = 164 MeV
- Squared speed of sound in hadronic phase: c_s² ≈ 0.15
- Impact parameter: b = 3 fm (corresponding to 0-5% centrality Au+Au collisions)
- Nuclear radius: R = 6.4 fm (for Au+Au collisions)
- Gaussian smoothing width for jet source: σ = 0.70 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: 120 MeV
- For off-diagonal jets, the outside Mach cone opening angle becomes ≈ 0.72 rad, compared to ≈ 0.4 rad for a static resonance hadron gas

## Context

This work extends the author's previous hydrodynamic simulation (Chaudhuri, 2005) that considered only diagonal jet trajectories in b=0 collisions and found Mach cone formation could not explain the detailed azimuthal correlation structure. It also corroborates the theoretical predictions of Satarov (2005) regarding distortion of Mach regions due to radial and longitudinal expansion, but finds the Mach surface is more complex than predicted, with inhomogeneity playing a significant role. The results suggest that the structures seen by STAR in azimuthal distributions of background-subtracted secondaries associated with high-pT triggers are closely linked to conical flow from partonic energy loss, enabling the possibility of measuring the speed of sound of the deconfined quark-gluon plasma.
