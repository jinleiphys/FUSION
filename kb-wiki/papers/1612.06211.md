# Effective particles in quantum field theory

> The concept of effective particles is introduced in the Minkowski space-time Hamiltonians in quantum field theory using a new kind of the relativistic renormalization group procedure that does not integrate out high-energy modes but instead integrates out the large changes of invariant mass. The new procedure is explained using examples of known interactions. Some applications in phenomenology, including processes measurable in colliders, are briefly presented.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a relativistic renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) that does not integrate out high-energy modes but instead integrates out large changes of invariant mass, using the size of effective particles as a free scale parameter. The authors claim this provides a Minkowski-space Hamiltonian formulation of quantum field theory that can connect parton and constituent quark descriptions, though the procedure is demonstrated only perturbatively to order g^3 for Yang-Mills theory.

## Method

The RGPEP defines a unitary transformation that relates quantum field theories with different effective particle sizes s, starting from the canonical theory at s=0. The transformation is governed by differential equations that generate vertex form factors exponentially suppressing interactions that change invariant mass by more than 1/s. The procedure is applied to the three-gluon interaction in Yang-Mills theory in the front form of Hamiltonian dynamics, and the resulting coupling constant evolution is computed perturbatively to order g^3.

## Key numbers

- The effective three-gluon coupling constant evolves as g_s = g_0 + (g_0^3 / 48 pi^2) * N_c * 11 * ln(s/s_0) for N_c=3 colors, reproducing the asymptotic freedom beta function
- For the pion constituent quark model: constituent quark mass m_c ~ 331 MeV, holographic potential parameter kappa ~ 436 MeV, and pion mass parameter M ~ -1.9 GeV
- Pion radius squared from the model is ~0.44 fm^2, compared to experimental value 0.45(1) fm^2
- Pion weak decay constant from the model is f_pi ~ 130.7 MeV, compared to experimental value 130.4 MeV
- The exponential form factor in the effective three-gluon vertex is e^{-s^4 * M_{12}^4}, where M_{12} is the invariant mass of gluons 1 and 2

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group procedure developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993) and Wegner (1994) by introducing a new formulation where particle size rather than momentum cutoff serves as the scale parameter. The RGPEP connects to the asymptotic freedom results of Gross and Wilczek (1973) and Politzer (1973) by reproducing their beta function in the Hamiltonian framework. The approach enables phenomenological applications including a description of the proton radius puzzle in muonic hydrogen (comparing to data from the CREMA collaboration) and jet production in pion-nucleus collisions (comparing to E791 Fermilab experiment data), though the authors note that effects of quark propagation through the nucleus and hadronization are not yet included.
