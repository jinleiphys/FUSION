# Octupole deformation for Ba isotopes in a reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field approach

> The potential energy surfaces of even-even $^{142-156}$Ba are investigated in the constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field approach with parameter set PK1. It is shown that for the ground states, $^{142}$Ba is near spherical,$^{156}$Ba well quadrupole-deformed, and in between $^{144-154}$Ba octupole deformed. In particular, the nuclei $^{148,150}$Ba with $N=92,94$ have the largest octupole deformations. By including the octupole degree of freedom, energy gaps $N=88$, $N=94$ and $Z=56$ near Fermi surfaces for the single-particle levels in $^{148}$Ba with $β_2\sim 0.26$ and $β_3\sim 0.17$ are found. Furthermore, the performance of the octupole deformation driving pairs ($ν2f_{7/2}$, $ν1i_{13/2}$) and ($π2d_{5/2}$, $π1h_{11/2}$) is demonstrated by analyzing the single-particle levels near Fermi surfaces in $^{148}$Ba.

# Octupole deformation for Ba isotopes in a reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field approach

## Key claim

The ground states of even-even $^{144-154}$Ba are octupole deformed, with $^{148,150}$Ba (N=92,94) having the largest octupole deformations; however, the paper qualifies that these octupole-deformed Ba isotopes (N=88-98) are more neutron-rich than those predicted in the finite-range droplet model by Moller (1995), which suggested N=86-91.

## Method

The constrained reflection-asymmetric relativistic mean-field (RAS-RMF) approach with parameter set PK1 was applied to calculate potential energy surfaces in the ($\beta_2$, $\beta_3$) plane for even-even $^{142-156}$Ba. The two-center harmonic-oscillator basis with 16 major shells for both fermions and bosons was used, and pairing correlations were treated by the BCS approximation with a constant pairing gap $\Delta=11.2/\sqrt{A}$ MeV. Single-particle levels near the Fermi surface were analyzed for $^{148}$Ba to identify octupole-driving orbital pairs.

## Key numbers

- $^{148}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 \sim 0.26$, $\beta_3 \sim 0.17$ (largest octupole deformation among isotopes studied)
- $^{150}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 \sim 0.28$, $\beta_3 \sim 0.17$ (tied for largest octupole deformation)
- $^{142}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.12$, $\beta_3 = 0.00$ (near spherical)
- $^{156}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.33$, $\beta_3 = 0.03$ (well quadrupole-deformed)
- Binding energies reproduced within 0.3% of experimental data from Audi (2003)
- $^{148}$Ba calculated binding energy: 1207.26 MeV (experimental: 1208.76 MeV)
- $^{144}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.20$, $\beta_3 = 0.12$
- $^{146}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.23$, $\beta_3 = 0.13$
- $^{152}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.30$, $\beta_3 = 0.12$
- $^{154}$Ba ground state: $\beta_2 = 0.32$, $\beta_3 = 0.08$

## Context

This work extends previous RAS-RMF calculations on $^{226}$Ra by Geng (2007) and on La isotopes by Wang (2009) to the Ba isotopic chain, confirming octupole deformation in a broader neutron-rich region than predicted by the finite-range droplet model of Moller (1995). The identification of energy gaps at N=88, N=94, and Z=56 in $^{148}$Ba, along with the demonstration of octupole-driving pairs ($\nu 2f_{7/2}$, $\nu 1i_{13/2}$) and ($\pi 2d_{5/2}$, $\pi 1h_{11/2}$), provides microscopic understanding that can guide future experimental searches for octupole bands in neutron-rich Ba isotopes and inform systematic studies of octupole correlations across the Z~56, N~88 region.
