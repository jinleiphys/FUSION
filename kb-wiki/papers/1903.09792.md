# A systematic study of the ground state properties of W, Os and Pt isotopes using HFB theory

> A systematic study of the ground state properties of transitional nuclei W, Os and Pt is conducted with the help of Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Different Skyrme interactions are employed in the study. Two different bases, harmonic oscillator and transformed harmonic oscillator, are used in our investigation. 2n-separation energy, charge radii, neutron and proton rms radii, neutron skin thickness and deformation parameter have been estimated. The results obtained are in good agreement with the available experimental values.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, using both harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) bases, reproduces ground state properties of W, Os, and Pt isotopes in good agreement with available experimental data, but the agreement is qualified by the specific Skyrme interaction used (UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 perform better than SIII, SKP, SLY6, and SKM* for binding energies).

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme HFB equations using an axially deformed cylindrical harmonic oscillator (HO) basis and a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, with 20 oscillator shells and a 60 MeV cut-off energy. Six Skyrme interactions (SIII, SKP, SLY6, SKM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) were used for the mean field, while a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) in mixed form was used for the pairing part. Ground state binding energies, 2n-separation energies, charge radii, neutron and proton rms radii, neutron skin thickness, and deformation parameters were computed for even-even isotopes from the 2-proton drip line to the 2-neutron drip line.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon (BE/A) for W, Os, and Pt isotopes show "very small deviation" from experimental values from AME2016; UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 give better agreement than other Skyrme forces.
- A sudden fall in 2n-separation energy is observed at neutron shell closure N=126 and at N=184 for all three isotopic chains.
- The 2n-shell gap shows sharp peaks at N=82, 126, and 184 for W isotopes, and at N=126 and 184 for Os and Pt isotopes.
- Deformation parameter is zero at and near N=126 and N=184, indicating spherical configuration at those neutron numbers.
- For normal nuclei near beta-stability, the difference between neutron and proton rms radii (neutron skin thickness) is found to be 1-2 fm.
- SIII force underestimates neutron skin thickness values, while UNEDF forces predict larger skin thickness for all nuclei studied.
- The saturation density used in the pairing interaction is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} with alpha = 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Ashok and Joseph) who applied Skyrme HFB theory to predict decay properties of these nuclei, and it builds on prior theoretical studies of transitional nuclei (Yb, Hf, W, Os, Pt) by various groups using experimental and theoretical models (referenced as alkh, whel, john, pt1, pt2 for experiments; ans, znaik, stev, sar2008, rob2009, rod2010, rmf for theory). The results enable predictions of ground state properties for nuclei far from stability, particularly the identification of N=184 as a potential next magic number, and support the formation of neutron skin in neutron-rich isotopes of W, Os, and Pt.
