# Kaon effective mass and energy from a novel chiral SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian

> A new chiral SU(3) Lagrangian is proposed to describe the properties of kaons and antikaons in the nuclear medium, the ground state of dense matter and the kaon-nuclear interactions consistently. The saturation properties of nuclear matter are reproduced as well as the results of the Dirac-Brückner theory. Our numerical results show that the kaon effective mass might be changed only moderately in the nuclear medium due to the highly non-linear density effects. After taking into account the coupling between the omega meson and the kaon, we obtain similar results for the effective kaon and antikaon energies as calculated in the one-boson-exchange model while in our model the parameters of the kaon-nuclear interactions are constrained by the SU(3) chiral symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper claims that, within a novel chiral SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian that self-consistently describes both kaon-nuclear interactions and nuclear matter ground-state properties, the kaon effective mass changes only moderately with density and saturates at high densities, never tending to zero. This result depends on the highly non-linear density effects of the scalar fields, which prevent kaon or antikaon condensation in this model.

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian that includes baryon interactions with scalar and vector meson multiplets, spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, and explicit symmetry breaking terms. They solved the field equations for scalar fields (σ, ζ) and the vector field ω in static nuclear matter, using two parameter sets (C1 and C2) fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties and the density dependence of Dirac-Brückner theory. The kaon effective mass and energy were then computed from the scalar field condensates and the vector field, including an omega-kaon coupling term.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ for both parameter sets C1 and C2.
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A(ρ₀) = -15.99 MeV (C1) and -15.93 MeV (C2).
- Incompressibility: K = 276.3 MeV (C1) and 266.2 MeV (C2).
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: m*/m_N = 0.612 (C1) and 0.641 (C2).
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93.3 MeV; kaon decay constant: f_K = 122 MeV.
- Kaon optical potential at normal density: U_opt^K = 21.5 MeV (repulsive).
- Antikaon optical potential at normal density: U_opt^K̄ = -98.7 MeV (attractive).

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and the one-boson-exchange model of Schaffner et al. (1994) by providing a self-consistent chiral SU(3) framework that simultaneously describes nuclear matter saturation and kaon properties. It contrasts with the coupled-channel model of Waas et al. (1996) and Oset et al. (1997), which includes the Λ(1405) resonance but does not account for nuclear matter saturation. The results enable future studies of kaon production in heavy-ion collisions and provide a chiral basis for comparing with empirical kaon-nucleus optical potentials from kaonic atom data.
