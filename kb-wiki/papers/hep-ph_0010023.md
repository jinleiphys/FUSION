# Searching for T-Violating, P-Conserving New Physics with Neutrons

> The observance of parity conserving time reversal violation in light quark systems could signal the presence of physics beyond the Standard Model. I discuss the implications of low-energy time reversal tests for the existence of such T-violating, P-conserving (TVPC) interactions. I argue that searches for permanent electric dipole moments (EDM's) and direct TVPC searches provide complementary information on P-conserving T-violation. EDM searches yield constraints only under the assumption that parity symmetry is restored at the scale Lambda associated with new TVPC physics. If parity remains broken at short distances, direct searches yield the least ambiguous bounds. In the latter case, improving the experimental precision of direct TVPC searches in neutron beta-decay and polarized epithermal neutron transmission at the Spallation Neutron Source could yield tighter bounds.

## Key claim

The paper argues that searches for permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) and direct T-violating, P-conserving (TVPC) searches provide complementary information, but that EDM constraints on TVPC interactions apply only under the specific assumption that parity symmetry is restored at the scale of new TVPC physics; if parity remains broken at short distances, direct searches yield the least ambiguous bounds.

## Method

The author uses effective field theory (EFT) to analyze the expansion of the Lagrangian containing new T-violating interactions in powers of 1/Λ_TVPC, where Λ_TVPC is the mass scale of new TVPC physics. Two scenarios are considered: (A) parity symmetry is restored at or below Λ_TVPC, causing lower-dimension T-violating, parity-violating (TVPV) operator coefficients to vanish; (B) parity remains broken at Λ_TVPC, allowing non-vanishing TVPV coefficients. The author compares how neutron and electron EDM limits and direct TVPC search bounds constrain the coefficients of dimension-7 TVPC operators under each scenario.

## Key numbers

- Under scenario (A), using the electron EDM bound |d_e| < 4 × 10⁻²⁷ e cm from the Berkeley group (Commins, 1994), the constraint on the TVPC scale is Λ_TVPC ≳ 270 κ²/³ TeV, where κ characterizes the coupling strength of new TVPC interactions.
- Under scenario (B), using direct TVPC search bounds α_T ≲ 10⁻³ (where α_T is the ratio of TVPC to strong nuclear matrix elements) and p ∼ 1 GeV/c, the bound is Λ_TVPC ≳ 20 κ²/³ GeV.
- The ratio of typical nuclear TVPC matrix elements to strong interaction matrix elements scales as α_T ∼ C_7 (p/Λ_TVPC)³, and with neutron EDM bounds under scenario (A) one expects α_T ≲ 10⁻¹³.
- Improving scenario (B) limits to the level of scenario (A) limits would require at least 12 orders of magnitude improvement in experimental precision; reaching the weak scale would require three or more orders of magnitude improvement.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Conti and Khriplovich (1992), who argued that EDM constraints on TVPC interactions far exceed those from direct searches, but the author shows that argument applies only under scenario (A) where parity is restored at the TVPC scale. The paper uses effective field theory methods analogous to chiral perturbation theory, where loop effects are suppressed by powers of m/Λ relative to tree-level terms. The results enable future experimental programs at the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS), including searches in neutron beta-decay (the D coefficient) and polarized epithermal neutron transmission, as well as charge symmetry-breaking TVPC terms in neutron-proton elastic scattering and proton-deuteron transmission.
