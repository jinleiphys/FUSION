# Chiral Perturbation Theory Approach to NN Scattering Problem

> It is shown that chiral perturbation theory (in its original form by Weinberg) can describe NN scattering with positive as well as negative effective range. Some issues connected with unnaturally large NN ^1S_0 scattering length are discussed.

# Chiral Perturbation Theory Approach to NN Scattering Problem

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory in Weinberg's original formulation can describe nucleon-nucleon scattering with positive effective range, contrary to claims by Cohen, Phillips, and others; the apparent failure arises from inconsistent renormalization, not from a fundamental problem with the effective field theory approach.

## Method
The author analyzes the two-nucleon scattering amplitude in the ^1S_0 channel using an effective non-relativistic Lagrangian with contact interactions (C_S, C_T, C_2) after pions are integrated out. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is iterated to obtain an on-shell T-matrix, and renormalization is performed by subtracting divergent integrals at a subtraction point μ rather than by removing a cutoff; the renormalized couplings C^R(μ) and C_2^R(μ) are then fixed by fitting to the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter for the ^1S_0 channel at μ = 0 is λ = a r_e p^2 / 2, where a ~ -1/(8 MeV) is the scattering length; this gives a radius of convergence p^2 ~ 1/(a r_e), which is problematic for large a.
- By choosing μ ~ 140 MeV, the expansion parameter becomes λ ~ -r_e p/2, giving a radius of convergence ~ 2/r_e ≈ 146 MeV for the ^1S_0 channel.
- With μ = 140 MeV, the renormalized coupling C^R = -1/(99 MeV)^2 and Λ^2 ≈ (147 MeV)^2, where Λ is the mass parameter in the next-to-leading order potential.
- For a → ∞, Λ^2 = -r_e/(2μ) ~ -(140 MeV)^2 when μ ~ 140 MeV.

## Context
This work directly responds to claims by Cohen, Phillips, Scaldeferri, and Beane (mid-1990s) that Weinberg's chiral perturbation theory approach to NN scattering has severe problems, particularly for positive effective range. The author argues these conclusions are misleading because they used an incorrect renormalization scheme that effectively imposes a zero-range potential, which Wigner's theorem (Wigner, 1955) then constrains to non-positive effective range. The solution to the unnaturally large ^1S_0 scattering length via choice of normalization point was independently suggested by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) using dimensional regularization with an "unusual subtraction scheme." This work enables consistent application of chiral perturbation theory to NN scattering without requiring explicit pionic degrees of freedom at very low energies.
