# Simultaneous Fitting of Neutron Star Structure and Cooling Data

> Using a model for the equation of state and composition of dense matter and the magnitude of singlet proton superconductivity and triplet neutron superfluidity, we perform the first simultaneous fit of neutron star masses and radii determined from observations of quiescent low-mass x-ray binaries and luminosities and ages determined from observations of isolated neutron stars. We find that the Vela pulsar strongly determines the values inferred for the superfluid/superconducting gaps and the neutron star radius. We find, regardless of whether or not the Vela pulsar is included in the analysis, that the threshold density for the direct Urca process lies between the central density of 1.7 and 2 solar mass neutron stars. We also find that two solar mass stars are unlikely to cool principally by the direct Urca process because of the suppression by neutron triplet superfluidity.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first simultaneous Bayesian fit of neutron star mass-radius data from quiescent low-mass x-ray binaries and cooling data from isolated neutron stars, finding that neutron triplet superfluidity suppresses the direct Urca process in two-solar-mass stars, but this conclusion depends strongly on whether the Vela pulsar is included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a 64-parameter Bayesian model combining a covariant field-theoretical Lagrangian for the equation of state (with a two-parameter polytrope added above twice nuclear saturation density), Gaussian parametrizations of the proton singlet and neutron triplet superfluid gaps, and a likelihood function that jointly fits nuclear structure data for 208Pb and 90Zr, mass-radius data from seven quiescent low-mass x-ray binaries, and luminosity-age data from 16 isolated neutron stars. To make the computation tractable, they build a library of exact calculations via MCMC, fit the likelihood to a 64-dimensional Gaussian, and use inverse-distance-weighted interpolation to sample from the emulator.

## Key numbers

* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.4 to 13.1 km (without Vela), 12.35 to 12.65 km (with Vela), both at 95% credible interval.
* 95% credible range for the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.15 to 0.20 fm (without Vela), 0.143 to 0.157 fm (with Vela).
* Threshold density for the direct Urca process lies between the central density of 1.7 and 2.0 solar mass neutron stars.
* Vela pulsar mass inferred: 1.25 ± 0.25 solar masses.
* Nuclear incompressibility constrained to 220 to 260 MeV; symmetry energy at saturation less than 36 MeV; slope of symmetry energy less than 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the separate analyses of neutron star mass-radius data (Steiner 2018) and isolated neutron star cooling (Beloin 2018) by performing the first combined Bayesian fit, going beyond the "minimal cooling" model of Page (2004) which assumed the direct Urca process does not occur. The results contradict models that rely on hyperonic direct Urca processes (Raduta 2018) and are incompatible with the cooling of the coldest transiently-accreting source SAX J1808.4-3658 (Heinke 2007, Han 2017), suggesting that simultaneous fitting of accreting and isolated neutron stars is needed next.
