# Isospin Dependence of the Spin-Orbit Force and Effective Nuclear Potentials

> The isospin dependence of the spin-orbit potential is investigated for an effective Skyrme like energy-functional suitable for density-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. The magnitude of the isospin dependence is obtained from a fit to experimental data on finite spherical nuclei. It is found to be close to that of relativistic Hartree models. Consequently the anomalous kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei is well reproduced.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the spin-orbit force in a non-relativistic Skyrme-like energy functional, when fitted to experimental data on finite spherical nuclei, is found to be close to that of relativistic Hartree models, and this isospin dependence is essential for reproducing the anomalous kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei, though the agreement for heavy Pb isotopes is not as good as in relativistic mean-field theory.

## Method

The authors constructed a Modified Skyrme Ansatz (MSkA) by modifying the conventional Skyrme force to include an isospin-dependent spin-orbit term while neglecting the exchange (Fock) term for the spin-orbit potential. The 11 parameters of the MSkA were determined by fitting to experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb) and additional nuclei (116Sn, 124Sn, 132Sn, 214Pb) to capture isospin variation. The resulting spin-orbit field was compared to that derived from a non-relativistic approximation of the Dirac equation in relativistic mean-field theory.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.1531 fm−3 for MSkA, matching the value from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM).
* Binding energy per particle: E/A = 16.006 MeV for MSkA.
* Compression modulus: K = 319.4 MeV for MSkA, compared to 216.7 MeV for SkM*.
* Asymmetry energy: J = 30.0 MeV for MSkA.
* Ratio W1/W2 in the spin-orbit field: 1.0005 for MSkA, compared to 1.13 for the relativistic parameter set NL1 and 1.10 for NL-SH.
* Parameter xw in the MSkA spin-orbit term: 0.0005, close to zero.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent Hartree-Fock approach by addressing a known failure of conventional Skyrme forces (e.g., SkM*) and Gogny forces (Egido, 1994) to reproduce the kink in Pb isotope shifts, which had been successfully described by relativistic mean-field theory (Sharma, Lalazissis, and Ring, 1993). The results also connect to the Finite Range Droplet Model (Moller, Nix, Myers, and Swiatecki, 1994), which uses an isospin-dependent spin-orbit term. The paper enables future investigations into including a density-dependent spin-orbit term in Skyrme theory to improve agreement with experimental charge radii for heavy Pb isotopes.
