# SU(2) higher-order effective quark interactions from polarization

> Higher order quark effective interactions are found for SU(2) flavor by departing from a non local quark-quark interaction. By integrating out a component of the quark field, the determinant is expanded in chirally symmetric and symmetry breaking effective interactions up to the fifh order in the quark bilinears. The resulting coupling constants are resolved in the leading order of the longwavelength limit and exact numerical ratios between several of these coupling constants are obtained in the large quark mass limit. In this level, chiral invariant interactions only show up in even powers of the quark bilinears, i.e. ${\cal O}(\barψ ψ)^{2 n}$ ($n=1,2,3,..$), whereas (explicit) chiral symmetry breaking terms emerge as ${\cal O}(\barψ ψ)^{n}$ being always proportional to some power of the Lagrangian quark mass.

# SU(2) Higher-order effective quark interactions from polarization

## Key claim

Higher-order quark effective interactions up to tenth order in quark bilinears can be derived from a dressed one-gluon exchange by integrating out one component of the quark field, but only in the large quark mass (or weak field) limit and at leading order in the long-wavelength (zero derivative) expansion. Chiral invariant interactions appear only in even powers of the quark bilinears, while explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms emerge at all powers and are always proportional to some power of the Lagrangian quark mass.

## Method

Starting from a nonlocal quark-quark interaction derived from a dressed one-gluon exchange (global color model), the quark field is split into two components using a background field method. The second component is integrated out, and the resulting quark determinant is expanded in powers of quark bilinears up to fifth order (tenth order in scalar-pseudoscalar bilinears). The effective coupling constants are resolved in the leading order of the long-wavelength limit (zero derivative expansion), and exact numerical ratios between coupling constants are obtained in the large quark mass limit by considering two limits for the gluon propagator components (transverse-only or longitudinal-only).

## Key numbers

- Fourth-order chiral invariant scalar-pseudoscalar coupling: $g_4 \simeq 1.2$ GeV$^{-2}$ (estimated using effective quark mass $m = 0.33$ GeV and coupling $g^2 = 17.8\pi/(10^3 N_c)$ from lattice QCD)
- Sixth-order symmetry breaking coupling: $g_6 \simeq -28.2$ GeV$^{-5}$ (same parameter set)
- Eighth-order chiral invariant coupling: $g_8 \simeq 4.1 \times 10^{4}$ GeV$^{-8}$ (same parameter set)
- Tenth-order symmetry breaking coupling: $g_{10}^{(1)} \simeq 2.2 \times 10^{8}$ GeV$^{-11}$ (same parameter set)
- Ratio of fourth-order couplings (transverse gluon limit): $|g_4/g_{v4}|^T \sim 3$
- Ratio of fourth-order couplings (longitudinal gluon limit): $|g_4/g_{v4}|^L \sim 1$
- Ratio of fourth-order chiral invariant to symmetry breaking coupling (kernel-independent): $|g_4/g_{4,sb}| \sim 1/2$
- Ratio of eighth-order to eighth-order symmetry breaking coupling (kernel-independent): $|g_8/g_{8sb}^{(2)}| \sim 1/32$

## Context

This work extends the approach of Braghin (2014) for deriving effective quark interactions from polarization effects in a global color model, providing explicit expressions for couplings up to tenth order in quark bilinears for SU(2) flavor. It complements the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model framework by deriving higher-order interactions that have been shown to affect the ground state (Osipov and collaborators, Andrianov and Andrianov), the chiral phase transition (Braghin and collaborators for SU(2) and SU(3)), and multiquark structures. The results enable systematic computation of effective coupling constants without extensive phenomenological fits to hadron masses, and the derived ratios between coupling constants provide constraints for phenomenological models such as those used by Osipov and collaborators for SU(3) flavor.
