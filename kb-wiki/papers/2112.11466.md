# The Piezoaxionic Effect

> Axion dark matter (DM) constitutes an oscillating background that violates parity and time-reversal symmtries. Inside piezoelectric crystals, where parity is broken spontaneously, this axion background can result in a stress. We call this new phenomenon "the piezoaxionic effect". When the frequency of axion DM matches the natural frequency of a bulk acoustic normal mode of the piezoelectric crystal, the piezoaxionic effect is resonantly enhanced and can be read out electrically via the piezoelectric effect. We explore all axion couplings that can give rise to the piezoaxionic effect -- the most promising one is the defining coupling of the QCD axion, through the anomaly of the strong sector. We also point our another, subdominant phenomenon present in all dielectrics, namely the "electroaxionic effect". An axion background can produce an electric displacement field in a crystal which in turn will give rise to a voltage across the crystal. The electroaxionic effect is again largest for the axion coupling to gluons. We find that this model independent coupling of the QCD axion may be probed through the combination of the piezoaxionic and electroaxionic effects in piezoelectric crystals with aligned nuclear spins, with near-future experimental setups applicable for axion masses between $10^{-11}\,\mathrm{eV}$ and $10^{-7}\,\mathrm{eV}$, a challenging range for most other detection concepts.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the oscillating axion dark matter background, which violates parity and time-reversal symmetries, can produce a mechanical stress inside piezoelectric crystals (the "piezoaxionic effect"), and that this effect is resonantly enhanced when the axion frequency matches a bulk acoustic normal mode of the crystal; the reach is forecast for axion masses between $10^{-11}\,\mathrm{eV}$ and $10^{-7}\,\mathrm{eV}$, but the signal depends on the presence of aligned nuclear spins in the crystal for the dominant QCD axion coupling to gluons.

## Method

The authors compute the piezoaxionic tensor by mapping the axion-induced Schiff moment of nuclei (generated via the QCD anomaly coupling) to atomic energy shifts and then to macroscopic stress in a piezoelectric crystal, using the nuclear shell model and octupole-deformed nuclei for enhanced Schiff moments. They also derive the electroaxionic effect, where the axion background produces an electric displacement field in any dielectric material with aligned nuclear spins. The experimental sensitivity is estimated by modeling a resonant-mass detector made from a piezoelectric crystal coupled to a SQUID readout, including noise sources from thermal mechanical noise, dielectric loss, and SQUID noise.

## Key numbers

- The rms effective theta angle amplitude from QCD axion dark matter is $\sqrt{\langle \overline{\theta}_a^2 \rangle} \approx 4\times 10^{-19} \sqrt{\rho_a/\rho_\mathrm{DM}}$, where $\rho_\mathrm{DM} \approx 0.4\,\mathrm{GeV}\,\mathrm{cm}^{-3}$ is the local dark matter energy density.
- The QCD axion mass is related to the decay constant as $m_a \approx (5.70 \pm 0.07) \times 10^{-10}\,\mathrm{eV} (10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}/f_a)$, corresponding to a frequency $f = 0.138\,\mathrm{MHz} (10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}/f_a)$.
- The axion coherence time is $\tau_\mathrm{coh} \simeq (1/f)(3.3 \times 10^6)$, using the local Milky-Way dark matter velocity dispersion $v_0 \approx 235\,\mathrm{km/s}$.
- The Schiff moment from a valence nucleon EDM is estimated as $\mathsf{S}_{\text{EDM}} \approx 2 \times 10^{-3} \, \overline{\theta}_a \,e\,\text{fm}^3 [(K+1)/(I+1)] (A/230)^{2/3}$, with $K\equiv(l-I)(2I-1)$.
- For the non-deformed nucleus $\ce{^{181}_{73}Ta}$, the Schiff moment from $\mathsf{P}$- and $\mathsf{T}$-violating nuclear forces is $\mathsf{S}_\mathrm{ch}(\ce{^{181}_{73}Ta}) \simeq 0.17 \, \overline{\theta}_a\, e \, \text{fm}^3$.
- For octupole-deformed nuclei, the Schiff moment can reach $\mathsf{S}_\mathrm{ch} \approx 5 \,\overline{\theta}_a\, e\,\mathrm{fm}^3 \, (\eta_a/0.1) (50\,\text{keV}/\Delta E_\pm) (\beta_2/0.12)(\beta_3/0.1)^2 (Z/88)(A/230)^{2/3}$.
- The target axion mass range for near-future experimental setups is between $10^{-11}\,\mathrm{eV}$ and $10^{-7}\,\mathrm{eV}$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of resonant-mass detectors previously used for gravitational wave detection (Weber, 1960s) and scalar dark matter searches (Arvanitaki, 2016; AURIGA collaboration, Branca 2016) to the detection of QCD axion dark matter via its parity- and time-reversal-violating couplings. It differs from solid-state electric dipole moment experiments (Lamoreaux 2002, Budker 2006) and nuclear-magnetic-resonance-based axion detection (Budker 2014) because it does not require an applied electric field or detection of magnetization changes. The combination of the piezoaxionic and electroaxionic effects in piezoelectric crystals with aligned nuclear spins could probe axion masses in a range challenging for other detection concepts, and the authors provide code and data for their results on GitHub.
