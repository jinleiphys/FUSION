# Evolutions of in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross sections and stiffness of dense nuclear matter from Bayesian analyses of FOPI proton flow excitation functions

> Within a Bayesian statistical framework using a Gaussian Process (GP) emulator for an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model simulator of heavy-ion reactions with momentum-independent Skyrme interactions, we infer from the proton directed and elliptical flow in mid-central Au+Au reactions at beam energies from 150 to 1200 MeV/nucleon taken by the FOPI Collaboration the posterior Probability Distribution Functions (PDFs) of the in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section (BBSCS) modification factor $X$ (with respect to their free-space values) and the stiffness parameter $K$ of dense nuclear matter. We find that the most probable value of $X$ evolves from around 0.7 to 1.0 as the beam energy $E_{beam}/A$ increases. On the other hand, the posterior PDF($K$) may have dual peaks having roughly the same height or extended shoulders at high $K$ values. More quantitatively, the posterior PDF($K$) changes from having a major peak around 220 MeV characterizing a soft EOS in the reaction at $E_{beam}/A$=150 MeV to one that peaks around 320 MeV indicating a stiff EOS in the reactions at $E_{beam}/A$ higher than about 600 MeV. The transition from soft to stiff happens in mid-central Au+Au reactions at beam energies around 250 MeV/nucleon in which $K=220$ MeV and $K=320$ MeV are approximately equally probable. Altogether, the FOPI proton flow excitation function data indicate a gradual hardening of hot and dense nuclear matter as its density and temperature increase in reactions with higher beam energies.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section modification factor \(X\) evolves from around 0.7 to 1.0 as beam energy increases from 150 to 1200 MeV/nucleon, while the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K\) transitions from a soft value around 220 MeV at low energies to a stiff value around 320 MeV at high energies, with the transition occurring around 250 MeV/nucleon where both values are approximately equally probable.

## Method

The authors used a Bayesian statistical framework with a Gaussian Process emulator for the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, employing momentum-independent Skyrme interactions. They inferred posterior probability distribution functions of the in-medium baryon-baryon scattering cross section modification factor \(X\) and the stiffness parameter \(K\) from FOPI Collaboration data on proton directed flow slope \(v_{11}\) and elliptic flow \(v_{20}\) at mid-rapidity in mid-central Au+Au reactions at beam energies from 150 to 1200 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The most probable value of \(X\) evolves from around 0.7 to 1.0 as beam energy increases from 150 to 1200 MeV/nucleon.
- At \(E_{beam}/A = 150\) MeV, the posterior PDF of \(K\) has a major peak around 220 MeV, characterizing a soft equation of state.
- At \(E_{beam}/A\) higher than about 600 MeV, the posterior PDF of \(K\) peaks around 320 MeV, indicating a stiff equation of state.
- The transition from soft to stiff occurs in mid-central Au+Au reactions at beam energies around 250 MeV/nucleon, where \(K = 220\) MeV and \(K = 320\) MeV are approximately equally probable.
- The prior ranges used were \(X = 0.3\) to 2.0 and \(K = 180\) to 400 MeV, with refined ranges of \(X = 0.3\) to 1.5 and \(K = 180\) to 380 MeV at 150 MeV/nucleon.
- Maximum central density increases from about \(1.7\rho_0\) at 150 MeV/nucleon to about \(2.7\rho_0\) at 1200 MeV/nucleon using a soft EOS (\(K = 204\) MeV) and approximately free-space cross sections (\(X \approx 1\)).

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses by Li and Xie (2024) of HADES proton flow data, which found an enhanced in-medium cross section (\(X = 1.32^{+0.28}_{-0.40}\)) and stiff EOS (\(K = 346^{+29}_{-31}\) MeV), to the broader FOPI excitation function data spanning 150 to 1200 MeV/nucleon. The results address the longstanding \(X-K\) degeneracy in describing collective flow observables, previously noted by Bertsch (1988), Xu (1991), Westfall (1993), and Danielewicz (2002), by showing that the degeneracy is broken as beam energy increases, with the EOS becoming harder at higher densities and temperatures. The findings enable future studies to use reduced prior ranges for both parameters and to investigate the density and temperature dependence of in-medium cross sections and nuclear matter stiffness more systematically.
