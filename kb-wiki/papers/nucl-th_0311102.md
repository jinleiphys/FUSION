# An indication for deconfinement in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> We present simultaneous, quality fits to final BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS and STAR data on particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations as measured in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV. Using the Buda-Lund hydro model, the best fit is achieved when the most central 1/8th of the particle emitting volume is superheated to $T > T_c =170$ MeV. In contrast, we find no indication for such a hot central region in a similar analysis of Pb+Pb data at CERN SPS.

## Key claim

The Buda-Lund hydro model provides a statistically acceptable simultaneous fit to final BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR data from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, and the best fit requires that the most central 1/8th of the particle emitting volume is superheated to a temperature above the lattice QCD critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV, which the authors interpret as an indication for quark deconfinement.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits to final single-particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlation data from all four RHIC experiments using the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model (version 1.5), which assumes a core-halo source structure with a three-dimensionally expanding, locally thermalized core. The fits were carried out using analytic expressions from the model and the CERN Minuit fitting package, yielding a well-defined minimum with an accurate error matrix.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature T_0 = 214 +/- 7 MeV for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, compared to T_c = 172 +/- 3 MeV from lattice QCD (Fodor and Katz, 2001), a more than 5 standard deviation effect
- Fit quality: chi^2/NDF = 158/180, corresponding to a confidence level of 88%
- Net baryochemical potential mu_B = 77 +/- 38 MeV for Au+Au at RHIC
- Transverse flow <u_t> = 1.0 +/- 0.1 for Au+Au at RHIC, compared to 0.55 +/- 0.06 for Pb+Pb at CERN SPS
- Source transverse size R_s|G = 8.6 +/- 0.4 fm for Au+Au at RHIC
- Mean emission time tau_0 = 6.0 +/- 0.2 fm/c for Au+Au at RHIC
- Central temperature for Pb+Pb at CERN SPS: T_0 = 139 +/- 6 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Buda-Lund model analyses of CERN SPS Pb+Pb data (Csorgo et al., 2002) and a previous three-standard-deviation indication for deconfinement at RHIC (Csorgo et al., 2002) by including pseudorapidity distributions from BRAHMS and PHOBOS, which increased the significance. The results contradict claims of an "HBT puzzle" at RHIC by showing that the Buda-Lund hydro model describes the transverse mass dependence of HBT radii as well at RHIC as at CERN SPS, and the finding that the fugacity becomes independent of transverse coordinates at RHIC explains why thermal and statistical models can be successful at these energies.
