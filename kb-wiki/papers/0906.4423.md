# Boost-invariant early time dynamics from AdS/CFT

> Boost-invariant dynamics of a strongly-coupled conformal plasma is studied in the regime of early proper-time using the AdS/CFT correspondence. It is shown, in contrast with the late-time expansion, that a scaling solution does not exist. The boundary dynamics in this regime depends on initial conditions encoded in the bulk behavior of a Fefferman-Graham metric coefficient at initial proper-time. The relation between the early-time expansion of the energy density and initial conditions in the bulk of AdS is provided. As a general result it is proven that a singularity of some metric coefficient in Fefferman-Graham frame exists at all times. Requiring that this singularity at tau = 0 is a mere coordinate singularity without the curvature blow-up gives constraints on the possible boundary dynamics. Using a simple Pade resummation for solutions satisfying the regularity constraint, the features of a transition to local equilibrium, and thus to the hydrodynamical late-time regime, have been observed. The impact of this study on the problem of thermalization is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that, unlike the late-time regime, no universal scaling solution exists for the early-time boost-invariant dynamics of a strongly-coupled conformal plasma; instead, the early-time boundary dynamics depends on initial conditions encoded in the bulk behavior of a Fefferman-Graham metric coefficient at initial proper-time, and requiring that a singularity at proper-time tau = 0 is only a coordinate singularity (without curvature blow-up) imposes constraints on the possible boundary dynamics.

## Method
The authors solve the 5-dimensional Einstein's equations with negative cosmological constant in Fefferman-Graham coordinates using a boost-invariant metric ansatz depending on proper time tau and the radial AdS coordinate z. They perform a perturbative expansion in proper time around tau = 0, treating the radial dependence exactly, and relate the coefficients of the early-time expansion of the energy density to the initial conditions encoded in the bulk metric coefficient a(tau=0, z). A Pade resummation is applied to solutions satisfying the regularity constraint to study the transition to the hydrodynamic late-time regime.

## Key numbers
- The late-time energy density scales as epsilon(tau) ~ constant / tau^(4/3) for the perfect fluid case, with the temperature scaling as T(tau) = Lambda / tau^(1/3) + ... where Lambda is the overall energy scale.
- The first viscous correction to the perfect fluid expansion appears at order tau^(-2/3) in the temperature expansion, with the shear viscosity contribution eta_0 appearing as T(tau) = Lambda / tau^(1/3) * {1 - [1/(Lambda * tau^(2/3))] * [eta_0/(sqrt(2) * 3^(1/4) * pi)] + ...}.
- The second order transport coefficients lambda_1^(0) and tau_Pi^(0) appear at order tau^(-4/3) in the temperature expansion.
- The only value of the power s (where epsilon ~ 1/tau^s) that yields a regular geometry in the late-time scaling limit is s = 4/3.
- For early times, the only possible scaling solution from a naive analysis would require s = 0 (epsilon ~ constant), but this is excluded by the full analysis showing no scaling solution exists.

## Context
This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to boost-invariant dynamics pioneered by Janik and Peschanski (2006) for the late-time regime, where a scaling solution exists and leads to hydrodynamic behavior. The paper contrasts with the early-time scaling analysis of Kovchegov and Taliotis (2007), which proposed a scaling variable w = z/tau for early times; the present work shows that this scaling fails because the terms leading to it vanish for the relevant case. The results enable future studies of the transition from early-time dynamics to local equilibrium and thermalization in strongly-coupled plasmas, providing a framework to connect initial conditions in the bulk to the boundary energy-momentum tensor evolution.
