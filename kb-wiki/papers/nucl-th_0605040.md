# Electromagnetic corrections to the dominant two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential

> We calculate at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory the electromagnetic corrections to the dominant two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction that is generated by the isoscalar $πN$ contact-vertex proportional to the large low-energy constant $c_3$. We find that the respective $2πγ$-exchange potential contains sizeable isospin-breaking components which amount to about -1% of the strongly attractive isoscalar central $2π$-exchange potential. The typical value of these novel charge-independence and charge-symmetry breaking central potentials is $0.3 $MeV at a nucleon distance of $r= m_π^{-1} = 1.4 $fm. Our analytical result for this presumably dominant $2πγ$-exchange interaction is in a form such that it can be easily implemented into phase-shift analyses and few-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that electromagnetic corrections to the dominant two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction produce isospin-breaking components of about -1% of the strongly attractive isoscalar central two-pion-exchange potential, with the caveat that this result applies specifically to the class of two-loop diagrams involving the isoscalar πN contact-vertex proportional to the low-energy constant c₃.

## Method

The author calculates at two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory the electromagnetic corrections (2πγ-exchange) to the dominant isoscalar central two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction generated by the ππNN contact-vertex proportional to the low-energy constant c₃. The spectral functions for 70 two-loop diagrams are evaluated analytically using the Cutkosky cutting rule and perturbative unitarity, with an infrared singularity from soft photon emission regularized via a "plus"-prescription analogous to that used for parton splitting functions. The coordinate-space potentials are then obtained through a modified Laplace transformation of the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- The charge-independence breaking central potential at r = 1.4 fm is 0.303 MeV (for c₃ = -3.3 GeV⁻¹)
- The charge-symmetry breaking central potential at r = 1.4 fm is 0.305 MeV (for c₃ = -3.3 GeV⁻¹)
- The isospin-conserving 2πγ-exchange central potential at r = 1.4 fm is 0.547 MeV
- The dominant isoscalar central 2π-exchange potential at r = 1.4 fm is -25.6 MeV
- The isospin-violating potentials are about -1% of the strongly attractive isoscalar central 2π-exchange potential
- For the c₂ vertex (c₂ = 3.3 GeV⁻¹) at r = 1.4 fm: isospin-conserving = -8.54 keV, charge-independence breaking = -23.4 keV, charge-symmetry breaking = -14.8 keV
- For the c₁ vertex (c₁ = -0.8 GeV⁻¹) at r = 1.4 fm: isospin-conserving = 23.4 keV, charge-independence breaking = 8.40 keV, charge-symmetry breaking = 9.78 keV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear isospin violation pioneered by Van Kolck et al. (2000s), who calculated the leading-order pion-photon exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction, and by Epelbaum et al. (2000s), who derived subleading isospin-breaking two-pion-exchange potentials. Previous long-range isospin-breaking potentials from pion-exchange were found to be rather weak (below 50 keV at r = 1.4 fm), so the 0.3 MeV potentials found here represent the largest long-range isospin-breaking nucleon-nucleon potentials obtained so far. The analytical result is presented in a form suitable for implementation into phase-shift analyses and few-body calculations, and the author notes that work on the remaining diagrams proportional to c₄ (generating spin-spin and tensor potentials) is in progress.
