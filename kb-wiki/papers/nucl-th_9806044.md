# Exchange Currents in Radiative Hyperon Decays

> A short overview of motivations and successes of two-body exchange currents between constituent quarks for electromagnetic hadron observables like charge radii, magnetic and quadrupole moments is given. We then predict and analyze exchange current effects on the radiative decay widths of decuplet hyperons, which are to be measured soon. In our chiral constituent quark model, exchange currents dominate the E2 transition amplitude, while they largely cancel for the M1 transition amplitude. Strangeness suppression of the radiative hyperon decays is weakened by exchange currents. The SU(F)_3 flavor symmetry breaking for the negatively charged hyperons is strong.

## Key claim

Exchange currents in the chiral constituent quark model dominate the E2 transition amplitude for radiative hyperon decays while largely canceling for the M1 transition amplitude, but the decay widths remain dominated by the impulse approximation M1 transition due to these cancellation effects and the smallness of the E2 amplitude.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic chiral constituent quark model with three quark flavors (u, d, s), including a quadratic confinement potential, pseudoscalar meson exchange (π, K, η), one-gluon exchange in Fermi-Breit form, and σ-exchange. They employed spherical (0s)³ oscillator states with SU_SF(6) spin-flavor wave functions and constructed electromagnetic two-body exchange currents by non-relativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams to satisfy the continuity equation, then calculated M1 and E2 transition amplitudes for decuplet hyperon radiative decays.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius: r_n² = -0.113(3) fm² (experimental value cited)
- Quark core size b_N = 0.61 fm from analytic relation r_n² = -(M_Δ - M_N)/M_N · b_N²
- Quark mass ratio m_u/m_s = 0.55
- Universal cutoff parameter Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹ for pseudoscalar meson regularization
- γN↔Δ transition magnetic moment: impulse approximation 2.828 μ_N, total with exchange currents 2.533 μ_N; experimental value μ_exp(Δ⁺→p) = 3.58(9) μ_N
- γN↔Δ E2 transition amplitude: Q_Tot = -0.089 fm², compared to experimental Q_exp(Δ⁺→p) = -0.085(13) fm²
- γN↔Δ decay width: Γ = 350 keV; experimental Γ_exp(Δ→γN) = 610-730 keV
- γN↔Δ E2/M1 ratio: -3.65%
- γΣ⁻↔Σ*⁻ decay width: Γ = 3.61 keV, E2/M1 ratio: -5.5%
- γΞ⁻↔Ξ*⁻ decay width: Γ = 6.18 keV, E2/M1 ratio: -2.8%
- γΞ⁰↔Ξ*⁰ total transition magnetic moment: 2.428 μ_N, essentially equal to γn↔Δ⁰ total of 2.428 μ_N

## Context

This work extends the exchange current approach previously applied to nucleon and octet baryon electromagnetic properties by Buchmann (1991), Wagner (1995), Buchmann (1997), and Meyer (1997) to the radiative decays of decuplet hyperons, which were to be measured in experiments by Russell (1995). The predictions differ from earlier SU_F(3) Skyrme model calculations by Abaev (1996), chiral bag models by Dlu98, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory by Butler (1993), and quenched lattice calculations by Leinweber (1993), particularly in the E2/M1 ratios and the degree of strangeness suppression. The results enable experimental discrimination between effective gluon versus pseudoscalar degrees of freedom in low-energy QCD through upcoming measurements of Σ* and Ξ* radiative decays.
