# Bayesian model averaging for nuclear symmetry energy from effective proton-neutron chemical potential difference of neutron-rich nuclei

> The data-driven Bayesian model averaging is a rigorous statistical approach to combining multiple models for a unified prediction. Compared with the individual model, it provides more reliable information, especially for problems involving apparent model dependence. In this work, within both the non-relativistic Skyrme energy density functional and the nonlinear relativistic mean field model, the effective proton-neutron chemical potential difference $Δμ^*_{\rm{pn}}$ of neutron-rich nuclei is found to be strongly sensitive to the symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ around $2ρ_0/3$, with $ρ_0$ being the nuclear saturation density. Given discrepancies on the $Δμ^*_{\rm{pn}}$-$E_{\rm{sym}}(2ρ_0/3)$ correlations between the two models, we carry out a Bayesian model averaging analysis based on Gaussian process emulators to extract the symmetry energy around $2ρ_0/3$ from the measured $Δμ^*_{\rm{pn}}$ of 5 doubly magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni, $^{88}$Sr, $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb. Specifically, the $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2ρ_0/3)$ is inferred to be $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2ρ_0/3) = 25.6_{-1.3}^{+1.4}\,\mathrm{MeV}$ at $1σ$ confidence level. The obtained constraints on the $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(ρ)$ around $2ρ_0/3$ agree well with microscopic predictions and results from other isovector indicators.

## Key claim

The effective proton-neutron chemical potential difference of neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei is strongly correlated with the symmetry energy around a subsaturation density of 2ρ₀/3, but the correlation exhibits systematic discrepancies between the non-relativistic Skyrme energy density functional and the nonlinear relativistic mean field model, requiring Bayesian model averaging to produce a unified, statistically reliable constraint.

## Method

The authors generated 50 parameter sets for each of the Skyrme energy density functional and the nonlinear relativistic mean field model via Latin hypercube sampling, with parameters uniformly distributed in empirical ranges. They calculated the effective proton-neutron chemical potential difference for five doubly magic nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ⁸⁸Sr, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) and the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities using each parameter set. Gaussian process emulators were trained on the predictions from each model to map the relationship between the chemical potential difference and the symmetry energy at 2ρ₀/3, and Bayesian model averaging was then performed over the two models using sequential Monte Carlo sampling to obtain the final posterior distribution.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at 2ρ₀/3 inferred from the Skyrme EDF alone is 25.8 MeV with −1.2 MeV and +1.3 MeV uncertainties at 68.3% confidence level.
- The symmetry energy at 2ρ₀/3 inferred from the nonlinear RMF model alone is 24.9 MeV ± 1.1 MeV at 68.3% confidence level.
- The Bayesian model averaging result gives E_sym(2ρ₀/3) = 25.6 MeV with −1.3 MeV and +1.4 MeV uncertainties at 1σ confidence level.
- The ratio of the GP-Sky evidence to the GP-RMF evidence is 3.3, indicating the measured data slightly favor the Skyrme EDF.
- The posterior model deficiency parameter σ is 0.4 MeV with −0.2 MeV and +0.4 MeV for GP-Sky, and 0.8 MeV with −0.3 MeV and +0.6 MeV for GP-RMF.

## Context

This work extends the use of the effective proton-neutron chemical potential difference, similar to the neutron-proton Fermi energy difference proposed by Wang (2013), as an isovector indicator for the symmetry energy. The Bayesian model averaging approach addresses the model dependence that has complicated previous extractions, such as the tension between PREX-2 and CREX results on neutron skin thickness (Reed 2021, Piekarewicz 2021, Reinhard 2021, Zhang 2022). The obtained constraints on E_sym(ρ) around 2ρ₀/3 agree with microscopic predictions from N3LO chiral many-body perturbation theory (Drischler 2020) and with results from other isovector indicators including giant dipole resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb (Trippa 2008), binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs (Zhang 2013), and the charge-weak form factor difference analysis of PREX-2 and CREX (Zhang and Chen 2022).
