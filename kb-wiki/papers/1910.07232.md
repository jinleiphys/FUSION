# $Δ$-mass dependence of the M-matrix in the calculation of $\mbox{N}Δ\to \mbox{NN}$ cross sections

> Within the one boson exchange model, $Δ$-mass dependent M-matrix and its influence on the calculation of $NΔ\to NN$ cross sections are investigated. Our calculations show that the $m_Δ$ dependence of $|\textbf{p}_{NΔ}|$ and $|\mathcal{M}|^2$ has effects on the calculations of $σ_{NΔ\to NN}$, especially around the threshold energy. We finally provide a table of accurate $σ_{NΔ\to NN}$ which can be used in the transport models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the commonly used approximate methods for calculating the $N\Delta\to NN$ cross section from the $NN\to N\Delta$ cross section via detailed balance, which neglect the $\Delta$-mass dependence of either the M-matrix or the center-of-mass momentum, introduce significant deviations (up to 50% or more) near the threshold energy, particularly when the $\Delta$ mass deviates from its pole mass of 1.232 GeV.

## Method

The authors employ the one boson exchange model (OBEM) with effective Lagrangians coupling nucleons and $\Delta$ baryons to $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\delta$, and $\pi$ mesons to calculate the M-matrix elements for $NN\to N\Delta$ and $N\Delta\to NN$ processes. They compute the exact $\sigma^{th}_{N\Delta\to NN}$ directly from the M-matrix and compare it to two approximate methods: the Wolf method (which ignores the mass dependence of $|\textbf{p}_{N\Delta}|$) and the Danielewicz method (which ignores the mass dependence of $|\mathcal{M}|^2$). The M-matrix parameters are fitted to two sets of experimental data for $pp\to n\Delta^{++}$ from CERN8401 and Landolt-Bornstein to cover the experimental uncertainty range.

## Key numbers

* The pole mass of the $\Delta$ resonance is $m_{0,\Delta}=1.232$ GeV, with a minimum mass of $m_{\Delta,\text{min}}=1.077$ GeV and a maximum mass of $m_\Delta=1.387$ GeV considered for heavy ion collisions at 1 GeV beam energy.
* For $m_\Delta=1.077$ GeV (near minimum mass), the Danielewicz method underestimates the $\Delta$ absorption cross section by less than 20% at $\sqrt{s}<2.2$ GeV, while the Wolf method underestimates it by more than 50%.
* For $m_\Delta=1.387$ GeV (large mass), both methods overestimate the absorption cross section near threshold: the Danielewicz method deviates by close to 50% and the Wolf method by less than 40% at $\sqrt{s}>2.5$ GeV.
* The measured cross section of $pp\to n\Delta^{++}$ has 3-5 mb uncertainties around $\sqrt{s}\sim 2.2$ GeV and above 3.0 GeV.
* In symmetric nuclear matter at $2\rho_0$, the effective masses are $m^*_{\Delta,\text{min}}=0.679$ GeV, $m^*_{0,\Delta}=0.834$ GeV, and $m^*_{\Delta}=0.989$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the one boson exchange model calculations of $NN\rightleftharpoons N\Delta$ cross sections by Cui et al. (2018) and addresses the approximations used in the detailed balance formulas of Danielewicz (1991) and Wolf (1992, 1993) for calculating $N\Delta\to NN$ cross sections. The study is motivated by ongoing debates about the constraints on symmetry energy at suprasaturation density from charged pion ratios in heavy ion collisions, as discussed by Yong (2019) and others. The authors provide a table of accurate $\sigma_{N\Delta\to NN}$ values and a sampling function for $\Delta$ mass that can be used in transport models, and suggest future work should evaluate the influence of these precise cross sections on heavy ion collision observables near threshold energy.
