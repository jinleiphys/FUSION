# Localised distributions and criteria for correctness in complex Langevin dynamics

> Complex Langevin dynamics can solve the sign problem appearing in numerical simulations of theories with a complex action. In order to justify the procedure, it is important to understand the properties of the real and positive distribution, which is effectively sampled during the stochastic process. In the context of a simple model, we study this distribution by solving the Fokker-Planck equation as well as by brute force and relate the results to the recently derived criteria for correctness. We demonstrate analytically that it is possible that the distribution has support in a strip in the complexified configuration space only, in which case correct results are expected.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically that for complex Langevin dynamics to yield correct results, the real and positive probability distribution sampled during the stochastic process must have support only within a strip in the complexified configuration space, rather than decaying as a power law; this condition is more specific than the abstract's general statement about "sufficiently localised" distributions.

## Method

The authors study a zero-dimensional toy model with a quartic action and complex quadratic coefficient, solving the Fokker-Planck equation by expanding the probability distribution in a truncated basis of Hermite functions and solving the resulting eigenvalue problem numerically. They compare these solutions to histograms obtained directly from complex Langevin simulations using an adaptive stepsize discretisation, for both real noise (N_I=0) and complex noise (N_I>0), and relate the distribution properties to the recently derived criteria for correctness.

## Key numbers

* For real noise (N_I=0) at σ=1+i and λ=1, the partially integrated distribution P_x(x) decays as exp(-a x^4) with a ≈ 0.295, slightly larger than λ/4 = 0.25.
* For complex noise (N_I=1) at σ=1+i and λ=1, the partially integrated distributions decay as power laws: P_x(x) ~ 1/|x|^5 and P_y(y) ~ 1/|y|^5.
* For real noise at σ=1+i and λ=1, the distribution has support only within the strip |y| < 0.3029, determined by the extrema of the central curve where K_y(x,y)=0.
* The repulsive fixed points of the classical flow are located at (x,y) = (±0.455, ∓1.10) for σ=1+i and λ=1.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Duncan and Niedermaier (2012), who solved the Fokker-Planck equation for the same model with complex noise but did not consider real noise. The paper connects the distribution properties to the criteria for correctness derived by Aarts, Seiler, and Stamatescu (2009, 2011), showing that power-law decay of the distribution (as seen with complex noise) leads to divergent higher moments and unreliable results, while exponential decay and strip-localised support (as seen with real noise) yield correct results. This enables practical diagnostics for complex Langevin simulations in lattice field theories with a sign problem, such as the relativistic Bose gas at nonzero chemical potential studied by Aarts (2008, 2009).
