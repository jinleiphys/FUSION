# Resonances, Chiral Symmetry, Coupled Channel Unitarity and Effective Lagrangians

> By means of a coupled channel non-perturbative unitary approach, it is possible to extend the strong constrains of Chiral Perturbation Theory to higher energies. In particular, it is possible to reproduce the lowest lying resonances in meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV using the parameters of the O(p^2) and O(p^4) Chiral Lagrangian. We report on an update of these results examining their possible relevance for meson spectroscopy.

## Key claim

By using a coupled-channel non-perturbative unitary approach (the Inverse Amplitude Method or a Bethe-Salpeter approximation), it is possible to extend the constraints of Chiral Perturbation Theory to reproduce the lowest-lying meson resonances up to 1.2 GeV, but the fitted chiral parameters cannot be directly compared with standard ChPT coefficients because tadpoles and crossed loops are neglected and absorbed into the parameters.

## Method

The authors unitarize the chiral Lagrangian by imposing exact unitarity via the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), where the ChPT expansion is used only for the real part of the inverse T-matrix. For scalar channels, they approximate the full O(p^4) calculation by adding the O(p^4) tree-level terms to the Bethe-Salpeter solution of the O(p^2) amplitudes, neglecting tadpoles and crossed loops. The resulting amplitudes are fitted to meson-meson scattering phase shifts and inelasticities up to 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The approach reproduces meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV, the scale of chiral symmetry breaking (4πv ≈ 1.2 GeV).
- The fitted cutoff for the Bethe-Salpeter approach is q_max = 673 MeV.
- The fitted IAM chiral constants (L_i values at scale μ = 800 MeV) are: L_1 = 0.88, L_2 = 1.54, L_3 = -3.66, L_4 = -0.27, L_5 = 1.09, 2L_6+L_8 = 0.68, L_7 = 0.10 (all ×10^{-3}).
- Standard ChPT values for comparison: L_1 = 0.4±0.3, L_2 = 1.3±0.3, L_3 = -3.5±1.1, L_4 = -0.3±0.5, L_5 = 1.3±0.5, 2L_6+L_8 = 0.42±0.5, L_7 = -0.4±0.2 (all ×10^{-3}).

## Context

This work extends earlier single-channel Inverse Amplitude Method results (Pelaez et al., 1990s) that reproduced the σ, ρ, and K* resonances from ππ and πK scattering, and the Bethe-Salpeter unitarization of lowest-order ChPT (Oller et al., 1990s) that generated scalar resonances. The updated coupled-channel fit simultaneously reproduces the σ, f_0, a_0, ρ, κ, K*, and ω_8 resonances, and supports the "Resonance Saturation Hypothesis" (Ecker, 1980s) by showing that scalar states around 1.3-1.4 GeV (octet) and 1 GeV (singlet) are needed to saturate chiral constants, while the lightest scalars (σ, κ, f_0, a_0) are generated independently of these preexisting heavier resonances.
