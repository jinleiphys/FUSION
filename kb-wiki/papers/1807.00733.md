# Irreducible nonlocality of optical model potentials based on realistic NN interactions

> We investigate the nonlocal structure of optical model potentials for nucleon-nucleus scattering based on microscopic approaches. To this purpose, \emph{in-medium} folding optical potentials are calculated in momentum space and their corresponding coordinate-space counterpart are examined, paying special attention to their nonlocal shape. The nucleon-nucleon effective interaction consists of the actual full off-shell $g$ matrix in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. The nonlocality of effective interactions is preserved throughout all stages in the the calculation. Argonne $v_{18}$ bare potential and chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order bare interaction are used as starting point. The study is focused on proton elastic scattering off $^{40}$Ca at beam energies between 30 and 800 MeV. We find that the gradual suppression of high-momentum contributions of the optical potential results in quite different-looking coordinate-space counterparts. Despite this non-uniqueness in their nonlocal structure, the implied scattering observables remain unchanged for momentum cutoff above a critical one, which depends on incident energy of the projectile. We find that coordinate-space potentials with momentum cutoffs at the critical value yield the least structured nonlocal behavior. Implications of these findings are discussed.

## Key claim

The nonlocal structure of microscopic optical model potentials in coordinate space is not unique; potentials with different nonlocal shapes can yield identical scattering observables as long as the momentum cutoff applied to the potential remains above a critical, energy-dependent value, with the least structured nonlocal behavior occurring at that critical cutoff.

## Method

The authors calculated in-medium folding optical potentials in momentum space for proton elastic scattering off \(^{40}\)Ca, using the full off-shell Brueckner-Hartree-Fock \(g\) matrix as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, preserving its nonlocality throughout. They started from either the Argonne \(v_{18}\) (AV18) bare potential or the chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) bare interaction, and transformed the momentum-space potentials to coordinate space while applying a smooth momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) to suppress high-momentum components. Scattering observables were then computed by solving the integral equation for the scattering wavefunction including Coulomb interaction, and the critical cutoff \(\Lambda_A\) was identified as the smallest \(\Lambda\) for which the reaction cross section became insensitive to further increases in \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

* For \(p+^{40}\)Ca at 200 MeV, all three cutoffs \(\Lambda = 12\), 7, and 4 fm\(^{-1}\) yielded practically identical differential cross section and analyzing power; the reaction cross section was \(\sigma_R = 540.0\) mb for AV18 and 524.9 mb for N3LO at these cutoffs, dropping to 505.0 mb (AV18) and 490.8 mb (N3LO) at \(\Lambda = 3.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* Volume integrals of the central optical potential at 200 MeV gave \(J/A = -(536.2 + i\,225.9)\) MeV fm\(^3\) in momentum space for AV18 and \(J/A = -(571.6 + i\,199.8)\) MeV fm\(^3\) for N3LO, with coordinate-space values agreeing to within about 0.1%.
* The critical cutoff \(\Lambda_A\) as a function of beam energy \(E\) was characterized by \(\Lambda_A(E) = \sqrt{\Lambda_0^2 + k_E^2}\) with \(\Lambda_0 = 2.4\) fm\(^{-1}\), where \(k_E\) is the relative momentum in the center-of-momentum frame.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding-model approach for nucleon-nucleus optical potentials developed by Arellano and collaborators (1995, 2008) and contrasts with coordinate-space folding models such as the Melbourne group (Amos, 2000) and the Hamburg group (Geramb, 1983), which localize the effective interaction before folding. The finding that a whole class of coordinate-space potentials with different nonlocal structures yield the same observables above a critical cutoff parallels the role of momentum-space regulators in renormalization group transformations (Bogner, 2010). The results enable a systematic way to compare nonlocal structures from different microscopic approaches on an equivalent footing, as demonstrated for the Perey-Buck-type parametrization (Tian, 2015) and the Nuclear Structure Method (Blanchon, 2015) at 30.3 MeV.
