# The equation of state of neutron matter, symmetry energy, and neutron star structure

> We review the calculation of the equation of state of pure neutron matter using quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods. QMC algorithms permit the study of many-body nuclear systems using realistic two- and three-body forces in a nonperturbative framework. We present the results for the equation of state of neutron matter, and focus on the role of three-neutron forces at supranuclear density. We discuss the correlation between the symmetry energy, the neutron star radius and the symmetry energy. We also combine QMC and theoretical models of the three-nucleon interactions, and recent neutron star observations to constrain the value of the symmetry energy and its density dependence.

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of pure neutron matter using realistic two- and three-body forces can constrain the symmetry energy and its density dependence, but the results become model-dependent at densities above about 0.24 fm⁻³, and the specific three-nucleon force models used must be viewed as minimal models whose validity can be tested by combining terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations.

## Method
The authors performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of 66 neutrons in a periodic box to compute the ground-state energy per neutron as a function of density. They used the Argonne AV8' two-body potential combined with various models of three-neutron forces, including the Urbana-IX (UIX) potential and several variants with different short-range and intermediate-range contributions, adjusting the three-body force strength to reproduce specific energies at saturation density. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Energy per neutron at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³: 14.47(2) MeV with AV8' two-body force alone; 19.10(2) MeV with AV8'+UIX three-body force
- Symmetry energy at saturation: ranges from 30.5 MeV (AV8' only) to 35.1 MeV (AV8'+UIX)
- Slope parameter L: ranges from 31.3 MeV (AV8' only) to 63.6 MeV (AV8'+UIX)
- Maximum neutron star radius implied by the modern neutron matter EoS: not larger than 13.5 km
- The AV8' Hamiltonian alone does not support a neutron star mass of 1.97(4) M⊙; adding a three-body force provides sufficient repulsion to be consistent with this observation

## Context
This work extends the QMC calculations of neutron matter by Gandolfi et al. (2009, 2012) and Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) by systematically exploring the uncertainties from different three-neutron force models. The results show a strong linear correlation between the symmetry energy and its slope L, and demonstrate that the three-pion ring contributions in Illinois-type forces (Pieper 2001, 2008) make the equation of state too soft to support observed neutron star masses. The findings enable future work to combine neutron star observations with chiral effective field theory calculations (Gezerlis 2013) and lattice QCD to better constrain three- and four-nucleon interactions at supranuclear densities.
