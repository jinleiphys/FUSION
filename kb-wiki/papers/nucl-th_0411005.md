# Study of dissipative dynamics in fission of hot nuclei using Langevin equation

> The fission of highly excited compound nuclei formed in heavy ion induced fusion reactions has emerged as a topic of considerable interest in the recent years. Dissipative dynamical models based on the Langevin equation were developed and were applied successfully for fission dynamics of highly excited heavy nuclei. However, Wall Friction(WF), the standard version of nuclear friction when incorporated in the Langevin dynamical model was not able to reproduce simultaneously experimental data for both prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability. Consequently, an empirical reduction in the strength of the wall friction was found necessary to reproduce the experimental numbers by many workers. Interestingly, a modification of the wall friction was proposed recently where the reduction was achieved microscopically. This modified version is known as the chaos weighted wall friction(CWWF) which takes into account non-integrability of single particle motion. The work in my thesis aims at using this strongly shape dependent version of friction (CWWF) in the Langevin dynamical model coupled with particle and gamma evaporation in order to verify to what extent it can account for the experimental data of fission of hot nuclei. The experimental data includes excitation functions of prescission neutron multiplicity, fission probability as well as evaporation residue cross-section of a number of nuclei. The importance of transients in nuclear fission is also discussed. The present endeavour is an effort to obtain a clear physical picture of nuclear dissipation which in turn will help in solving many open problems related to collective motion, and in particular, nuclear fission.

## Key claim
The thesis investigates whether the chaos-weighted wall friction (CWWF), a microscopically motivated modification of the standard wall friction that accounts for non-integrability of single-particle motion, can simultaneously reproduce experimental data for prescission neutron multiplicity, fission probability, and evaporation residue cross-section in fission of hot nuclei, where the standard wall friction alone had failed.

## Method
The author implemented a Langevin dynamical model coupled with particle and gamma evaporation, using the chaos-weighted wall friction (CWWF) which is strongly shape-dependent. The potential energy was obtained from the finite-range liquid drop model via double folding of a Yukawa-plus-exponential potential, and the chaos factor was calculated from Lyapunov exponents of classical trajectories for a wide range of nuclear shapes. The combined dynamical plus statistical code (DYSTCNF) followed Langevin trajectories for an ensemble of typically 20,000 to 100,000 trajectories, with Monte Carlo selection for particle emission, until each trajectory reached scission or formed an evaporation residue.

## Key numbers
- Chaos factor was calculated for c values from 0.6 to 2.1 in steps of 0.01, and h values from -1.5 to 1.5 in steps of 0.1.
- A typical calculation of chaos factor for 1000 trajectories at a particular (c,h) took about 2.22 minutes of computer time.
- A typical calculation of fission width for thorium (A=224) at angular momentum 30 hbar and temperature 2 MeV took about 4.72 hours of computer time.
- A typical calculation for 224Th with 20,000 trajectories at projectile energy 100 MeV took about 60 hours of computer time.
- The stability of the potential calculation by the double folding method was of the order of 1 in 10^8.
- The numerical errors in solving the Langevin equation for a free Brownian particle were within 1-2%, comparable with 1/sqrt(N) where N is the number of trajectories.

## Context
This work extends the dissipative dynamical models based on the Langevin equation that had been applied to fission of highly excited heavy nuclei, specifically addressing the failure of standard wall friction to simultaneously reproduce prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability. The approach builds on the chaos-weighted wall friction (CWWF) modification proposed by Blocki and collaborators, which reduces the friction strength microscopically rather than empirically. The thesis enables a more rigorous test of nuclear dissipation models and aims to clarify the physical picture of nuclear dissipation, which is relevant to open problems in collective motion and nuclear fission.
