# Effects of flow fluctuations and partial thermalization on $v_4$

> The second and fourth Fourier harmonic of the azimuthal distribution of particles, $v_2$ and $v_4$, have been measured in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The harmonic $v_4$ is mainly induced from $v_2$ as a higher-order effect. However, the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ is significantly larger than predicted by hydrodynamics. Effects of partial thermalization are estimated on the basis of a transport calculation, and are shown to increase $v_4/(v_2)^2$ by a small amount. We argue that the large value of $v_4/(v_2)^2$ seen experimentally is mostly due to elliptic flow fluctations. However, the standard model of eccentricity fluctuations is unable to explain the large magnitude of $v_4/(v_2)^2$ in central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, which is significantly larger than the ideal hydrodynamic prediction of 0.5, is mostly due to elliptic flow fluctuations rather than partial thermalization; however, the standard model of eccentricity fluctuations cannot explain the large magnitude of this ratio in central collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation numerically for massless particles in the transverse plane, tuning the Knudsen number \(K\) via the elastic scattering cross section to study partial thermalization. They also compute ideal hydrodynamic predictions using a Gaussian initial entropy density profile with an equation of state of a two-dimensional ideal gas. Flow fluctuations are modeled using the Monte-Carlo Glauber model for eccentricity fluctuations and a toy model of Gaussian \(v_2\) fluctuations, and the results are compared to experimental data from STAR and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- For ideal hydrodynamics with a Gaussian initial profile (\(\sigma_x = 2\) fm, \(\sigma_y = 3\) fm), the fit to \(v_4/(v_2)^2 = A + B \langle p_t \rangle / p_t\) over \(0.5 < p_t < 2.5\) GeV/\(c\) gives \(A = 0.557\) and \(B = 0.479\).
- For the Boltzmann transport calculation with Knudsen number \(K = 0.5\), \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) is larger than in ideal hydrodynamics; a linear extrapolation to \(K = 0\) gives \(A = 0.524 \pm 0.008\) and \(B = 0.508 \pm 0.012\).
- Realistic Knudsen numbers for semi-central collisions are in the range \(0.3 - 0.5\), yielding \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) at most 0.6, still below the experimental value of 0.9.
- For central collisions, eccentricity fluctuations give \(\langle \epsilon_{\rm PP}^4 \rangle / \langle \epsilon_{\rm PP}^2 \rangle^2 = 2\), while data suggest \(\langle (v_2)^4 \rangle / \langle (v_2)^2 \rangle^2 \simeq 3\).
- The nonflow error on \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) is estimated as \(\delta (v_4/(v_2)^2)_{\rm nf} = 2 \delta_{pp} / (N_{\rm part} (v_2)^2)\) with \(\delta_{pp} = 0.0145\).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic predictions by Borghini and Ollitrault (2005) that \(v_4 = \frac{1}{2} (v_2)^2\), which underestimated the experimental ratio. It builds on transport calculations by Bhalerao et al. (2005) and the AMPT model by Chen et al. (2004) that suggested partial thermalization increases \(v_4/(v_2)^2\). The paper also uses the Monte-Carlo Glauber model by Miller et al. (2007) and the PHOBOS collaboration (Alver et al., 2008) to model eccentricity fluctuations. The results enable future studies of the mechanism of flow fluctuations, particularly the need for a model that yields \(\langle (v_2)^4 \rangle / \langle (v_2)^2 \rangle^2 \simeq 3\) in central collisions, and suggest that the 4-particle cumulant analysis for central collisions could provide independent confirmation.
