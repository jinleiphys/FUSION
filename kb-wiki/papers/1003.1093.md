# Leptonic contribution to the bulk viscosity of nuclear matter

> For beta-equilibrated nuclear matter we estimate the contribution to the bulk viscosity from purely leptonic processes, namely the conversion of electrons to and from muons. For oscillation frequencies in the kiloHertz range, we find that this process provides the dominant contribution to the bulk viscosity when the temperature is well below the critical temperature for superconductivity or superfluidity of the nuclear matter.

## Key claim

For beta-equilibrated nuclear matter at oscillation frequencies in the kiloHertz range, purely leptonic processes (electron-muon conversion via the modified Urca process) provide the dominant contribution to the bulk viscosity when the temperature is well below the critical temperature for superconductivity or superfluidity of the nuclear matter, but only if the nucleonic bulk viscosity is suppressed by Cooper pairing.

## Method

The authors calculated the bulk viscosity arising from the processes e + ℓ ⇌ μ + ℓ + ν + ν̄ (where ℓ = e or μ) in beta-equilibrated nuclear matter. They derived a general expression for bulk viscosity in a two-species system (electrons and muons), computed the electron-muon conversion rate using Feynman diagrams for the weak interaction with electromagnetic scattering via photon exchange including plasma screening, and performed numerical integration of the resulting phase-space integrals. The calculation assumed the subthermal regime where density oscillations have small amplitude and bulk viscosity is independent of that amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For a lepton chemical potential μ_l ≈ 120 MeV and oscillation frequency ω ≈ 2π × 1000 Hz, the maximum possible leptonic bulk viscosity is ζ_max = 5.5 × 10^22 MeV^3 = 7.5 × 10^27 g s^{-1} cm^{-1}
- The effective equilibration rate γ_eff scales as T^7, with the largest value at T = 10 MeV and μ_l = 300 MeV being γ_eff ∼ 2 rad/s
- The crossover temperature where leptonic bulk viscosity becomes dominant over nucleonic bulk viscosity is of order 0.01 to 0.1 MeV (10^8 to 10^9 K) for kHz oscillation frequencies
- The dimensionless energy integrals for both processes are I^e_ω ≈ 78.86 (T/μ_l)^8 and I^μ_ω ≈ 78.62 (T/μ_l)^8
- For μ_l = 200 MeV, the peak of the leptonic bulk viscosity occurs at T ≈ 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity in npeμ nuclear matter that focused on nucleonic interconversion processes (Haensel 2000, Haensel 2001, Andersson 2004, Chatterjee 2007, Gusakov 2007). It addresses the regime where protons become superconducting or neutrons become superfluid (Dean 2002, Muther 2005, Gandolfi 2009), which suppresses the nucleonic bulk viscosity by a factor of order exp(-Δ/T). The results enable calculations of damping of neutron star oscillations, particularly radial pulsations (Cutler 1990, Gusakov 2006), in the low-temperature regime where shear viscosity from superfluid nuclear matter (Shternin 2008) may dominate r-mode damping but bulk viscosity could still affect modes with little shear flow.
