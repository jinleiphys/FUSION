# Heavy-flavor dynamics in event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds

> We investigate the effects of (2+1)d event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds on the nuclear modification factor and momentum anisotropies of heavy-flavor mesons. Using the state-of-the-art D and B mesons modular simulation code (the so-called DAB-mod), updated recently with heavy-light quark coalescence, we perform a systematic comparison of different transport equations, including two energy loss models and a relativistic Langevin model with two drag parametrizations. We present the resulting D$^0$ meson $R_{AA}$, $v_2$ and $v_3$, using the multiparticle cumulant method, in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV and compare them to the latest experimental data. We investigate the $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ ratio as a function of centrality for different initial conditions (MCKLN vs. Trento) and different system geometries and sizes (coming from Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, spherical and prolate Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.44$ TeV).

## Key claim

The paper shows that no single transport model can simultaneously describe both the nuclear modification factor and the azimuthal anisotropies of D⁰ mesons across all transverse momenta, but the M&T Langevin model with coalescence provides the best overall fit to experimental data, albeit still underpredicting \(v_n(p_T)\) at high \(p_T\).

## Method

The authors used the DAB-mod simulation code to evolve heavy quarks on top of (2+1)d event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds from v-USPhydro with \(\eta/s = 0.05\), comparing two energy loss models and a relativistic Langevin model with two drag parametrizations. Heavy-quark hadronization was performed either via fragmentation only (Peterson) or via heavy-light quark coalescence, with decoupling temperature varied between 120 and 160 MeV. The simulations were run for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.44\) TeV, using MCKLN and Trento initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- D⁰ meson \(R_{AA}\), \(v_2\), and \(v_3\) were computed for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV and compared to CMS data.
- The decoupling temperature \(T_d\) was varied between 120 and 160 MeV to assess hadronization uncertainties.
- The \(v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}\) ratio was integrated over \(8 < p_T < 40\) GeV for D⁰ mesons.
- Xe-Xe collisions were studied at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.44\) TeV for spherical and prolate nuclear geometries.
- Free parameters in the transport models were fixed to central \(R_{AA}\) data at \(p_T > 10\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous heavy-flavor studies by systematically comparing multiple transport models within the same event-by-event hydrodynamic background, building on the DAB-mod code developed by Prado (2016). It contrasts with earlier approaches that used fixed backgrounds or single transport models, and it enables future discrimination between energy loss and Langevin dynamics through the \(v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}\) ratio, which is shown to be sensitive to initial conditions (MCKLN vs. Trento) and nuclear geometry (size and deformation of Xenon). The results also connect to soft-sector studies by Giacalone (2017) that observed similar centrality trends in the cumulant ratio.
