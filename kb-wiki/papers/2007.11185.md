# Study of strange non-strange hadron ratios in pp and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies

> It has been observed that the yields of strange and multi-strange hadrons relative to pion increase significantly with the event charged-particle multiplicity. We notice from experimental data that yield ratios between non-strange hadrons, like p/$π$ or hadrons of same strange content, like $Λ$/K$_s^0$, show similar enhancement. We have studied this behavior within the ambit of a parton model (EPOS3) and A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model in pp and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. We investigate model predictions of yields and yield ratios of different identified hadron productions as a function of charged-particle multiplicity and compare them with published ALICE results. The string melting versions of AMPT and EPOS are found to establish enhancements in the particle yield ratios.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed enhancement of strange and multi-strange hadron yields relative to pions with increasing charged-particle multiplicity is not unique to strange particles; yield ratios between non-strange hadrons (p/π) and hadrons of the same strange content (Λ/Kₛ⁰) show similar enhancement, and this behavior can be qualitatively reproduced by the string melting versions of the AMPT and EPOS models, though with quantitative deviations from ALICE data.

## Method

The authors simulated pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV and p-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV using the AMPT model (versions 1.26t7/2.26t7, both default and string-melting) and the EPOS3 model (version 3.107). They calculated primary yields of π, p, Kₛ⁰, Λ, Ξ, and Ω as a function of charged-particle multiplicity density (⟨dN_ch/dη⟩) at mid-rapidity, and compared these yields and their ratios to published ALICE data. The models were run with default parameter sets, and statistical uncertainties were estimated from the simulations.

## Key numbers

- pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV and p-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV were simulated.
- The AMPT model hadronic interactions were terminated at a cut-off time t_cut = 40 fm/c.
- EPOS model is designed for energies from 100 GeV to 1000 TeV.
- ALICE data used multiplicity classes defined by V0A (2.8 < η < 5.1) and V0C (-3.7 < η < -1.7) detectors; mean charged-particle multiplicity densities were calculated in |η| < 0.5.
- EPOS describes most experimental data points within two standard deviations but highly overestimates Ω yields.
- AMPT default and AMPT SM describe experimental data for mesons but underestimate yields for strange baryons; the higher the strange baryon mass, the lower the AMPT model prediction from experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the ALICE observation (Nature, 2017) that strange and multi-strange hadron yields relative to pions increase with charged-particle multiplicity in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV. The authors test whether this enhancement is a general feature of hadron production by examining ratios of non-strange hadrons and same-strangeness hadrons, using the AMPT model (Z. Lin, 2005) and EPOS model (K. Werner, 2012). The results suggest the enhancement may be a mass-dependent phenomenon rather than purely strangeness-driven, and the authors call for more experimental data with improved uncertainties to clarify this.
