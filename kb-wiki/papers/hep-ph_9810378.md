# Inverse Slope Systematics in High-Energy p+p and Au+Au Reactions

> We employ the Monte-Carlo PYTHIA to calculate the transverse mass spectra of various hadrons and their inverse slopes T* at m_T-m=1.5-2 GeV in p+p reactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. Due to (multiple) minijet production T* increases as a function of the hadron mass. Moreover, the T*(m) systematics has a ``discontinuity'' at the charm threshold, i.e. the inverse slope of D-mesons is much higher than that of non-charmed hadrons and even of the heavier Lambda_C baryon. The experimental observation of this characteristic behaviour in Au+Au collisions would indicate the absence of c-quark rescattering. In contrast, the assumption of thermalized partons and hydrodynamical evolution would lead to a smoothly increasing T*(m), without discontinuity at the charm threshold. The degree of collective transverse flow, indicated by the slope of the T*(m) systematics, depends strongly on whether kinetic equilibrium is maintained for some time after hadronization or not.

## Key claim

In p+p reactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the inverse slope T* of transverse mass spectra shows a "discontinuity" at the charm threshold, with D-meson inverse slopes much higher than those of non-charmed hadrons and even the heavier Lambda_C baryon; experimental observation of this same pattern in Au+Au collisions would indicate the absence of c-quark rescattering, while a smoothly increasing T*(m) would indicate thermalized partons and hydrodynamical evolution.

## Method

The authors used the Monte-Carlo event generator PYTHIA (version 6.115) with default parameter settings to calculate transverse mass spectra of various hadrons at midrapidity in p+p reactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV, they employed a hydrodynamical model with initial conditions from the FRITIOF minijet/string fragmentation model, assuming local thermalization of partons and using the MIT bag model equation of state with a first-order phase transition. Inverse slopes T* were extracted by fitting the transverse mass spectra to a Boltzmann distribution in the range m_T-m between 1.5 and 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- p+p reactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: inverse slopes T* extracted from PYTHIA for various hadrons in the range m_T-m = 1.5-2 GeV
- Au+Au hydrodynamical calculation: initial energy density epsilon_0 = 17 GeV/fm^3, thermalization time tau_0 = 0.6 fm, nuclear radius R_T = 6 fm
- Initial transverse energy at midrapidity for central Au+Au: dE_T/dy = 1.2 TeV (from FRITIOF 7.02)
- On the hadronization hypersurface in Au+Au: dE_T/dy = 640 GeV
- Bag constant B = 380 MeV/fm^3, critical temperature T_C = 160 MeV
- For p+p hydrodynamics: R_T = 1.18 fm, dE_T/dy = 2.8 GeV (from PYTHIA)
- For Au+Au hydrodynamics with hadronic rescattering: T* approximately 400 MeV for charmed hadrons
- c-quark mass assumed: m_c = 1.5 GeV, Lambda_QCD = 300 MeV
- Tunnel probability for charm vs light quarks in Lund string model: approximately 1:10^{-11}

## Context

This work extends the study of inverse slope systematics from lower-energy p+p experiments at sqrt(s)=23-63 GeV (Alper 1975, Guettler 1976), where inverse slopes were found to be independent of hadron mass, to higher energies where minijet production becomes important. The paper contrasts two extreme scenarios for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies: superposition of minijet production without final-state interactions (as in p+p) versus local thermalization with hydrodynamical expansion (Geiger 1992, Dumitru and Rischke 1998). The predicted discontinuity in T*(m) at the charm threshold provides an experimental observable to determine the degree of heavy quark rescattering and equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, enabling tests of whether charm quarks thermalize with the quark-gluon plasma.
