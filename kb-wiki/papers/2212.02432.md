# Neutrino-tagged jets at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We explore the potential of jet observables in charged-current deep-inelastic scattering (CC DIS) events at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Tagging jets with a recoiling neutrino, which can be identified by the event's missing transverse momentum, will allow for flavor-sensitive measurements of Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs). We present the first predictions for transverse-spin asymmetries in azimuthal neutrino-jet correlations and hadron-in-jet measurements. We study the kinematic reach and the precision of these measurements and explore their feasibility using parameterized detector simulations. We conclude that jet production in CC DIS, while challenging in terms of luminosity requirements, will complement the EIC experimental program to study the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon encoded in TMDs.

## Key claim

Neutrino-tagged jets in charged-current deep-inelastic scattering at the EIC can provide flavor-sensitive measurements of transverse-spin asymmetries related to TMDs, but these measurements are challenging in terms of luminosity requirements and will complement rather than replace analogous neutral-current measurements.

## Method

The authors performed first calculations of unpolarized cross-sections and transverse-spin asymmetries for neutrino-jet correlations and hadron-in-jet measurements in CC DIS using TMD factorization at NLL accuracy. They used Pythia8 to simulate CC DIS events with 10 GeV electron and 275 GeV proton beams, and Delphes for fast detector simulations with a general-purpose EIC detector geometry covering up to |η|=4.0. The neutrino was identified via missing transverse momentum, and the Jacquet-Blondel method was used to reconstruct event kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momenta at the EIC reach up to approximately 40 GeV
- The standard deviation of the neutrino azimuthal angle reconstruction is less than 0.06 radians
- Approximately 30% of generated CC DIS events passed the selection cuts (p_T^ν > 15 GeV, V_AP/V_P < 0.35, δ < 30 GeV)
- Only 0.0005 ± 0.0002% of photoproduction events passed the same cuts, but the photoproduction cross section (58 nb) is three orders of magnitude larger than CC DIS (14 pb)
- About 8 ± 3% of the identified event sample would be background from photoproduction when using only kinematic cuts
- Values of Bjorken x up to 0.8 can be probed with jet/neutrino transverse momenta of 45 GeV
- The projected statistical precision for the Collins asymmetry in hadron-in-jet measurements is at the level of 1% or smaller for small z_h

## Context

This work extends the formalism of lepton-jet azimuthal correlation measurements in neutral-current DIS developed by Liu et al. (2018) to the charged-current channel at the EIC. It builds on previous studies of CC DIS at HERA by the H1 and ZEUS collaborations, and on feasibility studies of CC DIS at the EIC by Aschenauer et al. (2013) that focused on helicity PDFs. The proposed measurements would provide independent cross-checks of TMD extractions from neutral-current measurements and could test the consistency and universality of theoretical predictions, particularly regarding the chiral-odd nature of the Collins and transversity functions.
