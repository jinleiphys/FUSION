# Modified big bang nucleosynthesis with non-standard neutron sources

> During big bang nucleosynthesis, any injection of extra neutrons around the time of the $^7$Be formation, i.e. at a temperature of order $T \simeq 50$~keV, can reduce the predicted freeze-out amount of $^7$Be + $^7$Li that otherwise remains in sharp contradiction with the Spite plateau value inferred from the observations of Pop II stars. However, the growing confidence in the primordial D/H determinations puts a strong constraint on any such scenario. We address this issue in detail, analyzing different temporal patterns of neutron injection, such as decay, annihilation, resonant annihilation, and oscillation between mirror and standard model world neutrons. For this latter case, we derive the realistic injection pattern taking into account thermal effects (damping and refraction) in the primordial plasma. If the extra neutron supply is the sole non-standard mechanism operating during the BBN, the suppression of lithium abundance below Li/H~$\leq 1.9 \times 10^{-10}$ always leads to the overproduction of deuterium, D/H~$\geq 3.6 \times 10^{-5}$, well outside the error bars suggested by recent observations.

## Key claim

If extra neutron injection during big bang nucleosynthesis is the only non-standard mechanism operating, suppressing lithium abundance to Li/H less than or equal to 1.9 x 10^{-10} always leads to deuterium overproduction to D/H greater than or equal to 3.6 x 10^{-5}, which is well outside the error bars of recent observations.

## Method

The authors implemented four classes of neutron injection models in a BBN code: neutron-mirror-neutron oscillation (including thermal damping and refraction effects in the primordial plasma), particle decay, particle annihilation, and resonant particle annihilation. For each model, they scanned the parameter space and compared the predicted abundances of helium-4, lithium-7, and deuterium against observational constraints, with the baryon-to-photon ratio fixed to the Planck value.

## Key numbers

- Observed primordial D/H = (2.53 ± 0.04) x 10^{-5} from Cooke et al. (2013)
- Standard BBN prediction for 7Li/H = (4.89 +0.41 -0.39) x 10^{-10}
- Spite plateau value for 7Li/H = (1.23 +0.34 -0.16) x 10^{-10} from Ryan et al., and (1.58 ± 0.31) x 10^{-10} from Sbordone et al.
- Planck baryon-to-photon ratio η = 6.047 ± 0.074
- For n-n' oscillation, the mixing matrix element m_{12} is constrained to be less than about 2 x 10^{-7} eV from compositeness arguments, and experimental limits give m_{12}^2/Δm^2 less than about 3 x 10^{-6}
- The neutron damping rate Γ_eff(T) changes from electromagnetic to strong force scattering at T approximately 40 keV
- For the n-n' oscillation model with Δm = 10^{-10} eV and m_{12} = 10^{-13} eV, the oscillation rate exceeds the Hubble rate during 4He formation at T approximately 80 keV
- For the n-n' oscillation model with Δm = 10^{-11} eV and m_{12} = 3 x 10^{-15} eV, the oscillation rate exceeds the Hubble rate only briefly around T approximately 40 keV
- The allowed lithium-7 range used for constraints is 1.27 x 10^{-10} < Li/H < 1.89 x 10^{-10}
- The allowed deuterium range used for constraints is 2.49 x 10^{-5} < D/H < 2.57 x 10^{-5}
- The allowed helium-4 range used for constraints is 0.2368 < Y_p < 0.2562

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Jedamzik (2004) and others on neutron injection BBN, and specifically updates the n-n' oscillation model previously studied by three of the authors (Coc et al., 2013) to include thermal modification of oscillation effects. The paper demonstrates that the neutron injection mechanism alone cannot simultaneously solve the lithium problem and maintain consistency with the precise deuterium measurements from Cooke et al. (2013), suggesting that either astrophysical depletion mechanisms or other non-standard BBN scenarios must be invoked.
