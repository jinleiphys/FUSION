# Theory of doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions

## Key claim

The doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions are eigenstates of a Hamiltonian consisting of the kinetic energy plus the infinite-energy limit of the single-particle mass operator, which receives contributions only from Hartree diagrams with free-space nucleon-nucleon forces. The observed doorway state energies therefore carry information about both nuclear structure and the free-space nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the identification of peak energies with doorway state energies is reliable only for states with |ε_λ| > σ (where σ is about 20 MeV), because for weakly bound states the s-factors from two different reactions (pick-up and stripping) are required and their absolute values are measured with low accuracy.

## Method

The authors derive the Baranger theorem showing that the single-particle Hamiltonian formed from the static nuclear field (the infinite-energy limit of the mass operator) generates doorway states describing the beginning of the evolution of a suddenly created particle or hole. They calculate the two-particle contribution to the static field using two free-space NN interaction models (Bonn B and OSBEP) and parametrize the many-particle contributions as a power series expansion in nucleon density, with coefficients a₃ (three-particle repulsion) and a₄ (four-particle attraction) determined by fitting observed doorway state energies in ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb. Neutron density distributions are simultaneously refined using a Hermite function correction to the folded densities from 1 GeV proton-nucleus scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The dispersion σ_λ, identified with the largest observed width of peaks in quasielastic knockout reactions, is about 20 MeV in all nuclei (from Refs. [7,8] on (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions leading to 1s_{1/2} hole states).
- For nuclear matter at equilibrium density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the vector and scalar fields from the two-particle contribution are V = +284 MeV, S = −367 MeV for Bonn B, and V = +322 MeV, S = −404 MeV for OSBEP.
- The two-particle isoscalar contribution to nuclear matter potential is −83 MeV (Bonn B) or −82 MeV (OSBEP); the three-particle contribution is +96.5 MeV (Bonn B) or +97 MeV (OSBEP); the four-particle contribution is −104 MeV (Bonn B) or −107 MeV (OSBEP).
- The many-particle strength parameters for Bonn B are a₃ = 16.9296 fm⁵, a₄ = −107.6744 fm⁸, a⁻₃ = 25.5873 fm⁵, a⁻₄ = −128.5134 fm⁸; for OSBEP they are a₃ = 17.0011 fm⁵, a₄ = −110.3747 fm⁸, a⁻₃ = 26.9036 fm⁵, a⁻₄ = −130.1210 fm⁸.
- The "full" doorway state energies agree with observed ones within the experimental error of 3 MeV, except for the 2s_{1/2} states in ²⁰⁸Pb where the discrepancy is about 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Baranger theorem (Baranger, 1960s) connecting doorway states for one-nucleon transfer to eigenstates of the static nuclear field, and applies it to extract information about free-space many-particle NN forces from experimental data on quasielastic knockout reactions (Refs. [7,8] on (p,2p) and (p,pn)). The results are competitive with information from few-nucleon systems (Ref. [17]) but are obtained from a simpler one-body problem, and they show that three-particle repulsion and four-particle attraction are present in free-space NN forces, consistent with the signs found in relativistic mean-field calculations (Refs. [18,19]) where such forces might otherwise be considered artifacts of the approximation. The work enables future quasielastic knockout experiments with outgoing nucleon energies of 0.5–1 GeV to improve the determination of doorway state energies and neutron density distributions.
