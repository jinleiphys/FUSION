# Up to and beyond ninth order in opacity: Radiative energy loss with GLV

> A new examination of the GLV all-orders opacity result for radiative energy loss is presented. The opacity expansion is shown to be a Dyson expansion of a Schrodinger-like (or diffusion) equation, a form also found in BDMPS-Z-ASW, AMY and Higher Twist formalisms of radiative energy loss. A new, efficient numerical evaluation of the GLV all-orders result gives results up to and beyond ninth order in opacity. This presents a solution to this Schrodinger-like equation to almost arbitrary accuracy for lengths and densities applicable at RHIC and LHC. The first order is found to be most important, although higher orders implemented in a geometry integration will be needed for quantitative jet tomography.

## Key claim

The GLV opacity expansion is shown to be equivalent to a Dyson expansion of a Schrodinger-like (diffusion) equation, and a new numerical evaluation can compute results up to and beyond ninth order in opacity for lengths and densities applicable at RHIC and LHC; however, the first order in opacity remains the most important contribution, with higher orders being smaller and partially canceling each other.

## Method

The author rederives the GLV recursion relation for radiative energy loss, showing it can be expressed as a Schrodinger-like equation in impact parameter space with an imaginary (absorbing) potential. A Monte Carlo integration method is then used to numerically evaluate the all-orders GLV closed-form result for the single-gluon radiation spectrum, computing terms up to ninth order in opacity for a Gyulassy-Wang static color center model with fixed gluon mean free path λ_g = 1 fm and Debye mass μ_D = 0.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The gluon mean free path is fixed at λ_g = 1 fm and the Debye mass at μ_D = 0.5 GeV for all numerical evaluations.
- The q⊥ momentum integral is cut at √(6 T E) with T = 0.25 GeV.
- Results are computed for a light quark with p_T = 100 GeV and L = 6 fm, at x values of 0.025, 0.05, 0.2, 0.5, and 0.8.
- The average q² per collision is μ² log(E²/μ²), and the average q² per fm is μ²/λ log(E²/μ²) = q̂ log(E²/μ²).
- The opacity series is evaluated for L/λ in the range 1 to 6, where the first order remains the largest contribution.

## Context

This work extends the GLV formalism (Gyulassy, 2000) by providing a numerical solution to the all-orders opacity result that was previously limited to first, second, and third order calculations. The paper demonstrates that the GLV opacity expansion is formally equivalent to the Schrodinger-like equation approach used in BDMPS-Z-ASW (Baier, 1996; Zakharov, 1996), AMY (Arnold, 2002), and Higher Twist (Wang, 2001) formalisms, suggesting these different approaches share common foundations. This enables future quantitative jet tomography at RHIC and LHC by allowing comparison of different numerical solutions to the same underlying equation, and by providing a method that can incorporate improved medium models beyond the static Gyulassy-Wang potential, such as the dynamical medium calculations of Djordjevic (2007, 2008).
