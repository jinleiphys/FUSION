# Towards a theory of hadron resonances

> In this review, we present the current state of the art of our understanding of the spectrum of excited strongly interacting particles and discuss methods that allow for a systematic and model-independent calculation of the hadron spectrum. These are lattice QCD and effective field theories. Synergies between both approaches can be exploited allowing for deeper understanding of the hadron spectrum. Results based on effective field theories and hadron-hadron scattering in lattice QCD or combinations thereof are presented and discussed. We also show that the often used Breit-Wigner parameterization is at odds with chiral symmetry and should not be used in case of strongly coupled channels.

# Towards a theory of hadron resonances

## Key claim
The Breit-Wigner parameterization of pionic resonances is fundamentally inconsistent with chiral symmetry constraints of QCD, leading to systematically overestimated masses for states such as open charm mesons; a proper treatment requires energy-dependent modifications or, more systematically, unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT) coupled with lattice QCD.

## Method
The authors review the current state of lattice QCD and effective field theory approaches for determining the hadron spectrum, focusing on synergies between these methods. They analyze LHCb data on B meson decays (B- → D+π-π-, B- → D+π-K-, Bs0 → D0K-π+, B0 → D0π-π+, B0 → D0π-K+) using unitarized chiral perturbation theory amplitudes, fitting angular moments up to MDπ = 2.54 GeV and MDK = 2.65 GeV with a χ²/d.o.f. = 1.2. They also compile and compare lattice QCD results for scalar charmed mesons from multiple collaborations.

## Context
This review extends the program of understanding hadron resonances through combined lattice QCD and effective field theory approaches, building on prior work by Liu (2012) on UCHPT parameters, Du (2017) on the two-pole structure of the D0*(2300), and Albaladejo (2016) on the two-D0* scenario. The analysis demonstrates that the lightest charmed scalar meson is dynamically generated by meson-meson final-state interactions, similar to the f0(500) and K0*(700), contradicting simple quark model expectations. The results enable future lattice studies to properly account for chiral symmetry constraints and coupled-channel effects when extracting resonance parameters, and support the inclusion of the two-pole scenario in the Review of Particle Physics.
