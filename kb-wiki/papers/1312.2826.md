# Recent Developments on Hadron Interaction and Dynamically Generated Resonances

> In this talk I report on the recent developments in the subject of dynamically generated resonances. In particular I discuss the $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$ and $γn \to K^0 Σ^0$ reactions, with a peculiar behavior around the $K^{*0} Λ$ threshold, due to a $1/2^-$ resonance around 2035 MeV. Similarly, I discuss a BES experiment, $J/ψ\to ηK^{*0} \bar K^{*0}$ decay, which provides evidence for a new $h_1$ resonance around 1830 MeV that was predicted from the vector-vector interaction. A short discussion is then made about recent advances in the charm and beauty sectors.

# Recent Developments on Hadron Interaction and Dynamically Generated Resonances

## Key claim
The paper argues that the peculiar drop in the cross section of the γp → K⁰Σ⁺ reaction near the K*Λ threshold is caused by a dynamically generated 1/2⁻ resonance around 2030–2035 MeV, and that the J/ψ → ηK*⁰K̅*⁰ decay provides evidence for a new h₁ resonance around 1830 MeV, but these interpretations depend on fine-tuning subtraction constants in the meson-baryon loop functions.

## Method
The authors apply the chiral unitary approach, using interaction kernels from chiral Lagrangians extended to include vector mesons via local hidden gauge Lagrangians. For the photoproduction reactions, they model the process where the photon converts into a vector meson (vector dominance), the vector and nucleon interact via coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations, and the final K⁰Σ state is produced through pion exchange. For the J/ψ decay, they compute the K*⁰K̅*⁰ invariant mass distribution using the same vector-vector interaction framework and compare to BES experimental data.

## Key numbers
- A 1/2⁻ resonance around 2035 MeV (from γp → K⁰Σ⁺ data) or 2030 MeV (from pole analysis of the vector-baryon amplitude after parameter tuning)
- A new h₁ resonance around 1830 MeV from J/ψ → ηK*⁰K̅*⁰ decay, qualitatively agreeing with a prediction around 1800 MeV
- For γn → K⁰Σ⁰, a predicted peak in the cross section at 2075 MeV
- Hidden-charm baryon states: N* around 4260 MeV (coupling mostly to D̅Σc), Λ around 4200 MeV (coupling to D̅sΛc⁺ and D̅Ξc), and another Λ at 4400 MeV (coupling to D̅Ξ′c), with widths of about 50 MeV
- Beauty sector states predicted with masses around 11000 MeV

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously developed for light hadrons (reviewed in Oset et al.) to vector-vector and vector-baryon interactions, building on the local hidden gauge formalism of Bando et al. (1985). The photoproduction analysis follows the vector-baryon resonance predictions of Geng and Oset (2009) and the specific γp → K⁰Σ⁺ study of Albaladejo et al. (2013), while the J/ψ decay analysis uses the vector-vector interaction framework of Geng et al. (2009). The charm and beauty sector predictions extend the SU(3) results via heavy quark spin symmetry, consistent with the work of Wu et al. (2010) and Xiao et al. (2013). The results enable experimental searches for the predicted neutron-target peak in γn → K⁰Σ⁰ and for the predicted hidden-charm and hidden-beauty baryon states at facilities such as BESIII, LHCb, and future experiments.
