# Local $^4$He--p potentials from RGM phase shifts

> Phase shifts for $α$ + nucleon scattering generated by a multichannel RGM model of the five-nucleon system are subjected to iterative-perturbative ``mixed-case" inversion for energies below the reaction threshold. The resulting phase-equivalent potentials are compared with local potentials calculated from the inversion of empirical phase shifts. A strong similarity is revealed between the two sets of potentials, most notably in the description by a parity- and energy-dependent local potential. In particular, the RGM-derived and empirical potentials share a distinctive form of parity dependence in which the odd-parity component is of greater radial extent. Comparison with potentials representing single-channel RGM phase shifts exhibits the importance of the coupled channels in terms of local potentials. The relative wave functions derived from RGM are very different to those for the phase-equivalent local potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that local potentials derived from inverting multichannel RGM phase shifts for α+p scattering below the reaction threshold share a distinctive parity-dependent shape with empirical potentials, specifically that the odd-parity component has greater radial extent than the even-parity component, but the relative wave functions from RGM and the phase-equivalent local potentials are very different.

## Method

The authors used a multichannel resonating-group method (RGM) model of the five-nucleon system with α+p and ³He+d partitions to generate phase shifts for α+p scattering below the reaction threshold. They then applied iterative-perturbative "mixed-case" inversion to these RGM phase shifts to construct local phase-equivalent potentials, and compared these with local potentials obtained from inversion of empirical phase shifts. The calculations were performed for both coupled-channel (CC) and single-channel (SC) RGM models, and trivially equivalent local potentials (TELPs) were also constructed from the RGM wave functions.

## Key numbers

- RGM phase shifts were evaluated at intervals of 0.25 MeV for energies below the reaction threshold
- Inversion potentials were established for laboratory energies of 12 MeV and 18 MeV, using narrow energy bites of 12±0.03 MeV and 18±0.03 MeV
- The RGM calculation placed the h+d threshold approximately 2.5 MeV too high compared to experiment
- The matching radius for all inversion calculations was 8 fm
- The conventional charge radius used for the Coulomb potential was 1.3 × 4^(1/3) fm
- The spin-orbit force parameters used were V_s.o. = -224.8 MeV and a_s.o. = 0.707 fm
- The free space-exchange parameter u was set to 0.835

## Context

This work extends the two-step approach of fitting empirical data with phase shifts and then inverting those phase shifts to obtain potentials, previously developed by the same group (Cooper, Mackintosh, and collaborators, 1988-1992). It directly compares RGM-derived potentials with empirical inversion potentials from earlier work (Cooper, Mackintosh, and collaborators, 1992), establishing that the distinctive parity dependence seen empirically arises from the basic properties of the RGM theory. The paper also clarifies the relationship between microscopic RGM wave functions and local potential wave functions, showing that the two are very different in the interaction region despite being phase-equivalent, which has implications for using such potentials in structure calculations.
