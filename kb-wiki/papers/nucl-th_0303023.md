# Quadrupole correlations and inertial properties of rotating nuclei

> The contribution of quantum shape fluctuations to inertial properties of rotating nuclei has been analyzed for QQ-nuclear interaction using the random phase approximation (RPA). The different recipes to treat the cranking mean field plus RPA problem are considered. The effects of the dN=2 quadrupole matrix elements and the role of the volume conservation condition are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quantum shape fluctuations (RPA correlations) modify the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia, but the size and sign of this modification depend critically on whether the quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction includes \(\Delta N=2\) shell mixing and on whether the volume conservation constraint is applied; the authors state their investigations are "merely an exploratory approach" rather than a quantitative description of experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent cranking mean field calculations for rotating deformed harmonic oscillator nuclei using a separable quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) residual interaction, then added random phase approximation (RPA) correlations to include quantum shape fluctuations. They compared three versions of the QQ-model: (i) full N-mixing including \(\Delta N=0,\pm 2\) matrix elements, (ii) \(\Delta N=0\) only, and (iii) double-stretched QQ-forces with the volume conservation constraint. The total Routhian energy was calculated as a function of rotational frequency \(\Omega\), and the angular momentum and dynamical moment of inertia were obtained via numerical differentiation of the mean field and RPA contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the N=Z=14 system with full \(\Delta N=2\) QQ-forces, the RPA correlations reduce the dynamical moment of inertia by **16%** relative to the mean field value.
- For the N=Z=76 system with full \(\Delta N=2\) QQ-forces, the RPA correlations reduce the dynamical moment of inertia by **4%** relative to the mean field value.
- For the N=Z=10 system with QQ-forces, the rotational band starts with a delay at \(\hbar\Omega = 1.1\) MeV (where the calculated angular momentum becomes positive).
- The RPA contribution to the moment of inertia for N=Z harmonic oscillators with the volume conservation constraint was plotted as a function of mass number A, showing the relative contribution decreases with increasing A.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent cranking plus RPA approach of Nazmitdinov and Almehed (2002) from a simplified model to a more realistic treatment with a deformed oscillator potential, including the effects of \(\Delta N=2\) quadrupole matrix elements and volume conservation. The results contrast with earlier restricted-configuration studies by Egido and Mang (1980) that did not maintain self-consistency between the residual interaction and the mean field. The paper enables future studies of quantum shape fluctuations in rotating nuclei using more advanced effective interactions like Skyrme or Gogny forces, though the authors note that RPA correlation energy calculations with non-separable forces remain computationally prohibitive.
