# Parton Distribution Functions of Heavy Mesons on the Light Front

> The parton distribution functions (PDFs) of heavy mesons are evaluated from their light-front wave functions, which are obtained from a basis light-front quantization in the leading Fock sector representation. We consider the mass eigenstates from an effective Hamiltonian consisting of the confining potential adopted from light-front holography in the transverse direction, a longitudinal confinement, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with running coupling. We present the gluon and the sea quark PDFs which we generate dynamically from the QCD evolution of the valence quark distributions.

## Key claim

The parton distribution functions (PDFs) of heavy mesons (charmonium, bottomonium, and B_c mesons) are evaluated from light-front wave functions obtained via basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) in the leading Fock sector, with gluon and sea quark PDFs generated dynamically through QCD evolution rather than being present in the initial nonperturbative calculation.

## Method

The authors solve the mass eigenvalue equation for an effective light-front Hamiltonian that includes kinetic energy, a confining potential from light-front holography in the transverse direction, a longitudinal confining potential, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with running coupling. The valence-sector light-front wave functions are obtained in a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions (transverse) and Jacobi polynomials (longitudinal) with truncation parameters N_max and L_max. The valence quark PDFs at the initial hadronic scale are computed from these wave functions, then evolved to higher scales using the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) equations up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using the HOPPET toolkit, which dynamically generates the gluon and sea quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium (c\bar{c}) confining strength: kappa = 0.985 GeV; quark mass m_q = 1.570 GeV; basis truncation N_max = L_max = 8
- Bottomonium (b\bar{b}) confining strength: kappa = 1.389 GeV; quark mass m_q = 4.902 GeV; basis truncation N_max = L_max = 32
- B_c meson (b\bar{c}) confining strength: kappa = 1.196 GeV; quark masses m_b = 4.902 GeV, m_c = 1.603 GeV; basis truncation N_max = L_max = 32
- Initial scale (UV cutoff) for charmonium PDFs: mu_0 = 2.80 GeV (for N_max = 8)
- Initial scale (UV cutoff) for bottomonium PDFs: mu_0 = 7.90 GeV (for N_max = 32)
- Initial scale (UV cutoff) for B_c meson PDFs: mu_0 = 6.77 GeV (for N_max = 32)
- Running coupling at Q^2 = 0: alpha_s(0) = 0.6 for all systems
- Finite vector boson mass to regularize Coulomb singularity: mu_g = 0.02 GeV
- Final evolution scales shown: mu_1 = 20 GeV, mu_2 = 80 GeV, mu_3 = 1500 GeV

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia (Li et al., 2017) and B_c mesons (Tang et al., 2018) by computing not only valence quark PDFs but also gluon and sea quark distributions via DGLAP evolution. Unlike the parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of heavy mesons, which can be factorized perturbatively within nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) as studied by Ma (2006) and Bell (2008), the PDFs of heavy mesons have received limited theoretical attention. This study provides predictions for heavy meson structure at higher momentum scales accessible at the LHC and RHIC, and enables future comparisons with experimental data on heavy meson production and decay.
