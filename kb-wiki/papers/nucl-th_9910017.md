# Cherenkov-like shock waves associated with surpassing the light velocity barrier

> The effects arising from accelerated and decelerated motion of a point charge inside a medium are studied. The motion is manifestly relativistic and may be produced by a constant uniform electric field. It is shown that in addition to the bremsstrahlung and Cherenkov shock waves, the electromagnetic shock wave arises when the charge particle velocity coincides with the light velocity in the medium. For the accelerated motion this shock wave forming an indivisible entity with the Cherenkov shock wave arrives after the arrival of the bremsstrahlung shock wave. For the decelerated motion the above shock wave detaches from the charge at the moment when its velocity coincides with the light velocity in the medium. This wave existing even after termination of the charge motion of the charge propagates with the light velocity in the medium. It has the same singularity as the Cherenkov shock and is more singular than the bremsstrahlung shock wave. The space-time regions, where these shock waves exist, and conditions under which they can be observed are determined.

## Key claim

In addition to the well-known Cherenkov and bremsstrahlung shock waves, an electromagnetic shock wave arises when a charged particle's velocity coincides with the light velocity in the medium, both for accelerated and decelerated motion; this prediction is made under the simplifying assumption of a frequency-independent refractive index, which the authors acknowledge leads to divergences in integrals quadratic in field strengths.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the Lienard-Wiechert potentials for a point charge moving along a straight line with manifestly relativistic motion given by \(z(t)=\sqrt{z_0^2+c^2t^2}-z_0\), which corresponds to constant proper acceleration in a constant uniform electric field. They find the zeros of the denominators in the potential expressions to locate the singular surfaces (shock waves) for both accelerated motion (charge starting from rest at \(t=0\)) and decelerated motion (charge coming to rest at \(t=0\)), using dimensionless variables and a refractive index independent of frequency.

## Key numbers

* For \(\alpha = c_n/c = 0.8\) (the ratio of light velocity in medium to that in vacuum), the charge acquires the light velocity in the medium at times \(t_l = \pm \alpha/\sqrt{1-\alpha^2}\) for accelerated and decelerated motion, respectively.
* The position of the charge when its velocity coincides with the medium light velocity is \(z_l = 1/\sqrt{1-\alpha^2} - 1\).
* For accelerated motion, the Cherenkov cone dimension is zero for observation times \(t \le t_l\) and continuously rises for \(t > t_l\); the Cherenkov shock wave overtakes the bremsstrahlung shock wave at the moment \(t = 2\alpha/(1-\alpha^2)\).
* For the decelerated motion case, the shock wave \(C_M\) is approximated with good accuracy by the spherical surface \(\rho^2 + (z - z_l)^2 = (t + t_l)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the qualitative predictions of Tyapkin and Zrelov et al. regarding a shock wave associated with surpassing the medium light velocity, which were based on acoustic analogies without formulae or numerical results. It also improves upon the earlier quantitative study by Afanasiev and Kartavenko (1999) that used a singular electric field (diverging at finite distance) by instead employing a constant uniform electric field that is easier to create experimentally. The results enable experimental searches for these shock waves in gases, where the Vavilov-Cherenkov effect is observed and screening effects are less problematic than in solids.
