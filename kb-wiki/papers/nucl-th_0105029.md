# Role of dynamical particle-vibration coupling in reconciliation of the $d_{3/2}$ puzzle for spherical proton emitters

> It has been observed that decay rate for proton emission from $d_{3/2}$ single particle state is systematically quenched compared with the prediction of a one dimensional potential model although the same model successfully accounts for measured decay rates from $s_{1/2}$ and $h_{11/2}$ states. We reconcile this discrepancy by solving coupled-channels equations, taking into account couplings between the proton motion and vibrational excitations of a daughter nucleus. We apply the formalism to proton emitting nuclei $^{160,161}$Re to show that there is a certain range of parameter set of the excitation energy and the dynamical deformation parameter for the quadrupole phonon excitation which reproduces simultaneously the experimental decay rates from the 2$d_{3/2}$, 3$s_{1/2}$ and 1$h_{11/2}$ states in these nuclei.

## Key claim

The systematic quenching of proton-emission decay rates from the 2d₃/₂ single-particle state relative to predictions of a one-dimensional potential model can be reconciled by including dynamical particle-vibration coupling to quadrupole phonon excitations of the daughter nucleus, provided the phonon excitation energy is at least 0.6 MeV and the dynamical deformation parameter β₂ is around 0.18.

## Method

Coupled-channels equations were solved for the proton-plus-daughter-nucleus system, coupling the proton motion to vibrational excitations of the spherical daughter nucleus via a deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear potential (treated to all orders), a Coulomb term (linear coupling), and a spin-orbit term (linear coupling). The formalism was applied to the 2d₃/₂ state in ¹⁶⁰Re and the 3s₁/₂ and 1h₁₁/₂ states in ¹⁶¹Re, using the Becchetti-Greenless optical potential with depth adjusted to reproduce the experimental Q-value for each combination of β₂ and phonon energy ℏω. The Green's function method was used to obtain the resonance Gamow state and compute decay widths.

## Key numbers

- For the 2d₃/₂ state of ¹⁶⁰Re, the no-coupling calculation is off from the experimental data by about 30%, even when uncertainty in the decay Q-value is considered.
- With a quadrupole phonon excitation energy of 0.8 MeV, the dynamical deformation parameter range 0.18 ≤ β₂ ≤ 0.23 simultaneously reproduces the measured decay half-lives for the 2d₃/₂, 3s₁/₂, and 1h₁₁/₂ states.
- Simultaneous description of all three states is possible only when the quadrupole phonon excitation energy ℏω₂ is larger than 0.59 MeV.
- The minimum value of β₂ remains below 0.25 for ℏω₂ ≥ 0.6 MeV.
- Octupole phonon excitations (with ℏω₃ = 0.2 MeV) produce too small an enhancement of the half-life to account for the observed discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels approach previously applied to deformed proton emitters by Ferreira and Maglione (2000) and by Esbensen and Davids (2000) to the case of spherical or nearly-spherical nuclei, where vibrational rather than rotational excitations dominate. It addresses the "d₃/₂ puzzle" identified by Davids et al. (1997) and Bingham et al. (1999), where a spherical potential model with BCS spectroscopic factors systematically underestimated half-lives for 2d₃/₂ states while successfully describing s₁/₂ and h₁₁/₂ states. The results enable future work to consistently compute both the decay rate and the spectroscopic factor based on phonon-induced pairing mechanisms, as proposed by Barranco et al. (1999).
