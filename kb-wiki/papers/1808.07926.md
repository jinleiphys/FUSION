# Stationary and non-stationary solutions of the evolution equation for neutrino in matter

> We study solutions of the equation which describes the evolution of a neutrino propagating in dense homogeneous medium in the framework of the quantum field theory. In the two-flavor model the explicit form of Green function is obtained, and as a consequence the dispersion law for a neutrino in matter is derived. It is shown that there exist both the solutions describing the stationary states and the solutions describing the spin-flavor coherent states of the neutrino. The stationary states may be different from the mass eigenstates, and the wave function of a state with a definite flavor should be constructed as a linear combination of the wave functions of the stationary states with coefficients, which depend on the mixing angle in matter. In the ultra-relativistic limit the wave functions of the spin-flavor coherent states coincide with the solutions of the quasi-classical evolution equation. Quasi-classical approximation of the wave functions of spin-flavor coherent states is used to calculate the probabilities of transitions between neutrino states with definite flavor and helicity.

# Stationary and non-stationary solutions of the evolution equation for neutrino in matter

## Key claim
The paper derives both stationary states and spin-flavor coherent states (non-stationary) as solutions of the quantum field-theoretic evolution equation for neutrinos in dense homogeneous matter, but the stationary states in matter are not mass eigenstates; instead, a definite-flavor wave function must be constructed as a linear combination of stationary-state wave functions with coefficients that depend on the effective mixing angle in matter.

## Method
The authors start from a quantum field theory modification of the Standard Model in which fermions are combined into multiplets, and reduce the mass operator of the neutrino in a homogeneous medium (composed of neutrons, protons, and electrons) to obtain an effective evolution equation. In the two-flavor model, they compute the Green function in momentum representation, derive the dispersion law from its poles, and construct explicit stationary solutions for neutrinos in non-polarized matter at rest. They also construct spin-flavor coherent states using a resolvent operator and take the ultra-relativistic quasi-classical limit to compute transition probabilities between states of definite flavor and helicity.

## Context
This work extends the quantum field-theoretic description of neutrino oscillations proposed by Lobanov and collaborators (2017) to include matter effects, generalizing earlier results on the dispersion law for neutrinos in matter. The dispersion law derived here for the two-flavor case was previously written by Kiers (1997) but not consistently derived from a field-theoretic framework. The results enable a consistent quantum-field theoretical description of neutrino evolution in matter applicable to low-energy neutrinos, including relic neutrinos, and can be used to calculate spin-flip transition probabilities that depend on the relative motion of the neutrino and the medium.
