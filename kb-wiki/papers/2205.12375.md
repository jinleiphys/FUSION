# Designing Observables for Quantum Interference in Jets at Subleading Color

> The large-$N_c$ or topologically planar limit of gauge theories can be considered as a classical limit because all gauge bosons are distinguishable particles and therefore cannot exhibit interference. Quantum effects due to the flow of color therefore arise starting at subleading in $1/N_c$. We introduce kinematic observables explicitly sensitive to effects at subleading color formed from the ratio of interfering to squared color-ordered amplitudes. Such observables are in general not infrared and collinear safe, so we introduce angular observables defined from appropriate multi-point energy correlators motivated by the form of color-ordered amplitudes. We demonstrate that color interference effects are manifest as sinusoidal oscillation in the simplest system, a collinear jet with three particles, and show the limitations of predicting this observable in all-purpose, leading-color parton shower Monte Carlos.

## Key claim

Quantum interference due to color flow in QCD jets can be isolated by constructing the ratio of Abelian (QED-like) to large-Nc squared amplitudes, which manifests as a sinusoidal oscillation in the azimuthal angle between gluons in the collinear limit; however, this ratio is not infrared and collinear safe in general and must be embedded into multi-point energy correlators to be calculable in perturbation theory.

## Method

The observable is constructed as the likelihood ratio of the interference contribution to the non-interfering terms in the squared amplitude for e+e- → qggqbar, expressed in terms of color-ordered amplitudes. For the collinear limit, the observable reduces to the ratio of the Abelian splitting function to the large-Nc splitting function for 1→3 parton splitting. To achieve IRC safety, the ratio of pairwise angles (which emerges for identical gluon helicities) is embedded into multi-point energy correlators, with the jet axis defined by a heavy-flavor quark from a displaced vertex.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of color factors controlling the subleading color contribution is (2CF - CA)/CA = -1/Nc^2, which for QCD (Nc=3) gives approximately -1/9 ≈ -0.11
- The purity of the two-gluon density matrix in QCD is tr(ρ_QCD^2) = 1/2 + 1/(2Nc^4) + ..., expanding about the Nc→∞ limit where the purity is 1/2 for a completely random ensemble
- The color matrix traces for the e+e- → qggqbar process are tr(T1 T2 T1 T2) = -Nc + 1/Nc and tr(T2 T1 T1 T2) = Nc(Nc - 1/Nc)^2
- In the collinear limit with identical gluon helicities, the ratio of Abelian to large-Nc squared amplitudes becomes θ12^2/(θ1q^2 + θ2q^2), which equals 1 - (2θ1q θ2q/(θ1q^2 + θ2q^2)) cos φ

## Context

This work extends the concept of the jet color ring introduced by Buckley et al. (2020) for discriminating color-singlet resonance decays, and builds on the approach of Chen et al. (2020) for constructing observables sensitive to quantum interference of helicity states. The observable is designed to test parton shower Monte Carlos that include subleading color effects, such as those developed by Nagy and Soper (2012, 2015, 2019), Platzer and Sjodahl (2012, 2018, 2020), and others working on full-color showers. The paper demonstrates that leading-color parton showers like Pythia cannot reproduce the interference pattern, motivating development of subleading-color shower algorithms.
