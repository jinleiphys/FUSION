# Multiplicity fluctuation and correlation of mesons and baryons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC

> We study the multiplicity fluctuation and correlation of identified mesons and baryons formed at the hadronization by the quark combination mechanism in the context of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Based on the statistical method of free quark combination, we derive the two-hadron multiplicity correlations such as meson-meson and meson-baryon correlations, and take the effects of quark number fluctuation at hadronization into account by a Taylor expansion method. After including the decay contributions, we calculate the dynamical fluctuation observable $ν_{dyn}$ for $\text{K}π$, $pπ$ and $\text{K}p$ pairs and discuss what underlying physics can be obtained by comparing with the data in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV and the simulations from HIJING and AMPT event generators.

# Multiplicity fluctuation and correlation of mesons and baryons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC

## Key claim

The quark combination mechanism can reproduce the basic behavior of the dynamical fluctuation observable $\nu_{dyn}$ for Kπ, pπ, and Kp pairs in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, but only when quark number fluctuations and flavor conservation at hadronization are included; the authors caution that the comparison with data is "served only as a reference but not as a decisive test of QCM" because hadronic re-scattering effects are not negligible.

## Method

The authors derived analytic formulas for two-hadron multiplicity correlations (meson-meson, baryon-baryon, baryon-antibaryon, and baryon-meson) using a statistical quark combination model with constituent quark degrees of freedom, incorporating quark number fluctuations via a Taylor expansion method to second order. They then calculated the dynamical fluctuation observable $\nu_{dyn}$ for Kπ, pπ, and Kp pairs after including resonance decay contributions from the PDG, and compared results with ALICE data and simulations from HIJING and AMPT event generators for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV.

## Key numbers

- Quark numbers used for 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV in the unit rapidity interval: $\langle N_u \rangle = \langle N_d \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{u}} \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{d}} \rangle = 1137$, $\langle N_s \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{s}} \rangle = 478$
- Quark numbers used for the pseudorapidity interval $|\eta| \leq 0.8$ in 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions: $\langle N_u \rangle = \langle N_d \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{u}} \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{d}} \rangle = 1819$, $\langle N_s \rangle = \langle N_{\bar{s}} \rangle = 765$
- Parameters for hadron formation: $R_{V/P} = 0.45$ (ratio of vector to pseudoscalar mesons), $R_{O/D} = 2.5$ (ratio of octet to decuplet baryons)
- Variance of total baryons parameterized as $\overline{\sigma}_B^2 \approx 0.36 \overline{N}_B$
- Lattice QCD off-diagonal flavor susceptibilities cited: $\chi_{us}/\chi_{ss} \approx -0.05$, $\chi_{ud}/\chi_{uu} \approx -0.05$
- The observable $\nu_{dyn}$ is inversely proportional to hadronic multiplicity $\langle dN_{ch}/d\eta \rangle$ in the quark combination model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark combination model studies (Shao et al. 2009, Song et al. 2013) by deriving two-hadron multiplicity correlations and incorporating quark number fluctuations, building on the statistical method of free quark combination. It provides a framework to test the quark combination mechanism against ALICE measurements of $\nu_{dyn}$ (ALICE collaboration 2013) and simulations from HIJING and AMPT event generators, with the goal of extracting information about quark system fluctuations at hadronization. The results suggest that hadronic re-scattering effects, which are present in AMPT but not in HIJING or the current QCM calculation, are important for quantitative agreement with data, pointing to the need for future work incorporating these effects along with finite acceptance and nonzero baryon density at lower energies.
