# Complete plasmon spectrum of two-stream system

> The complete spectrum of plasmons of the two interpenetrating plasma streams is found in a closed analytic form. The orientation of the wave vector with respect to the stream direction is arbitrary and the plasmas, which are assumed to be collisionless and spatially homogeneous, can be nonrelativistic, relativistic or even ultrarelativistic. Our results apply to the electromagnetic plasma of electrons and passive ions and to the quark-gluon plasma governed by QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete analytic solution for the full plasmon spectrum of a two-stream plasma system, valid for arbitrary orientation of the wave vector relative to the stream direction and for nonrelativistic through ultrarelativistic streams. The authors hedge that the distribution function is an idealization of a two-peak structure where particles have momenta close to q or -q, but not necessarily exactly those values.

## Method

The authors start from the linearized Maxwell/chromodynamic equations and the general dispersion equation det[Σ(ω,k)] = 0 for the matrix Σ defined in terms of the dielectric tensor. They use a delta-function momentum distribution for two counter-streaming beams, compute the dielectric tensor in the collisionless limit, and instead of computing the determinant directly, they invert the matrix Σ and look for poles of Σ⁻¹. The matrix is decomposed in a basis of four projectors built from the wave vector k and the stream velocity u, leading to two dispersion equations: one giving the transverse mode ω²_a = μ² + k², and a cubic equation for the remaining modes solved in closed form using Viete's trigonometric formulas.

## Key numbers

* The complete spectrum consists of four pairs of modes of opposite sign (eight modes total), with one unstable mode for any orientation of the wave vector.
* For k perpendicular to u, the solutions are ω²_0 = μ², ω²_± = ½(λ² + k² ± √((λ² + k²)² + 4μ²u²k²)), where λ ≡ μ√(1 - u²).
* For k parallel to u, the longitudinal modes are ω²_± = k²u² + λ²/2 ± (λ/2)√(8k²u² + λ²), with ω²_- negative for k² < λ²/u².
* For u² = 1, the solutions simplify to ω²_0 = μ² + (k·u)² and ω²_± = ½(k² + (k·u)² ± √(k⁴ + (k·u)⁴ + 4μ²k² - 4μ²(k·u)² - 2k²(k·u)²)), with ω²_- negative for k < μ|tanθ|.
* The discriminant Δ of the cubic equation is nonnegative for 0 ≤ u² ≤ 1, but negative in some k-domain for u² > 1.

## Context

This work extends the analytic study of plasmons in anisotropic plasmas, methodologically close to Carrington (2014) who studied plasmons from stretched/squeezed isotropic distributions, but here the two-stream distribution requires a modified inversion technique. The results apply to both electromagnetic plasmas (electrons and passive ions) and quark-gluon plasma governed by QCD, and the authors note the two-stream system has been studied in the QGP context by Pokrovsky (1988) and Mrowczynski (1988). The analytic form enables practical calculations of plasma characteristics such as energy loss of high-energy partons, which the authors previously studied in Carrington (2012).
