# Proton Internal Pressure in $pp$ and $\bar{p}p$ Elastic Scattering

> In this work, one proposes the use of a damped confinement potential to mimic the proton internal energy. The internal pressure is calculated taking into account the occurrence of a phase transition in the $pp$ and $\bar{p}p$ total cross section. The model predicts the inversion of the positive and negative pressure regions depending on the squared energy $s_0$, where the total cross section achieves its minimum value. Considering energies below the phase transition the expected results are in accordance with the recent proton internal pressure measurement. For energies above $s_0$, the model predicts a negative pressure region near the center of the hadron surrounded by a positive pressure region.

## Key claim

The model predicts that the internal pressure distribution inside the proton inverts its positive and negative regions depending on whether the squared center-of-mass energy \(s\) is below or above the phase transition point \(s_0\) (where the total cross section reaches its minimum), with the inversion driven by a transition factor \(g = 1/(s/s_0 - 1)\) introduced into the pressure calculation.

## Method

The author models the proton internal energy using a damped confinement potential \(V_d(r) = n V(r) \exp(-kr)\), where \(V(r)\) is the standard Cornell potential (Coulomb-like term plus linear string tension) and \(k\) is a Debye-like wavenumber. The internal pressure \(p(r)\) is then derived from this potential, incorporating a phase transition factor \(g\) that depends on the squared energy \(s\) relative to the transition energy \(s_0\). The pressure distribution is calculated for fixed values of \(s\) and transferred momentum \(Q^2\), with the number density of quark-antiquark pairs assumed constant.

## Key numbers

* The string tension \(\sigma \approx 0.405\) GeV is used in the confinement potential.
* The Debye length is assumed to be in the range \(0.2 \lesssim \lambda \lesssim 0.5\) fm, corresponding to wavenumber \(2 \lesssim k \lesssim 5\) fm\(^{-1}\); the author adopts \(k = 3\) fm\(^{-1}\) for all figures.
* The phase transition squared energy is \(\sqrt{s_0} = 25\) GeV, where the total cross section achieves its minimum.
* For energies below the phase transition (e.g., \(\sqrt{s} = 6\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} = 24\) GeV), the negative pressure region is located between approximately 0.5 and 1.0 fm from the hadron center, consistent with the measurement by Burkert et al. (2018).
* For energies above the phase transition (e.g., \(\sqrt{s} = 52.8\) GeV, 1800 GeV, 14000 GeV, and 95000 GeV), the negative pressure region shifts to near the center of the hadron (between 0.5 and 1.0 fm), shrinking as \(Q^2\) increases.

## Context

This work extends the phase transition interpretation of \(pp\) and \(\bar{p}p\) total cross sections previously introduced by Borcsik and Campos (2016), who used fractal dimensions, and by Campos (2018), who studied Tsallis entropy in impact parameter space. The model directly addresses the recent measurement of proton internal pressure by Burkert, Elouadrhiri, and Girod (2018) using deeply virtual Compton scattering, and provides a prediction for the pressure inversion at energies above the transition. The results also relate to the hollowness effect observed by TOTEM (2013) and ATLAS (2014) at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV and \(\sqrt{s} = 8\) TeV, and to the analysis by Broniowski et al. (2017) based on the Barger-Phillips model.
