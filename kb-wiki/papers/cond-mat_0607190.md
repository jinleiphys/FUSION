# Thermodynamical limit in non-extensive and Renyi statistics

> Previous results on Renyi and Wang's formalism of the Tsallis thermostatics are founded by using an extensive variable z connected to the entropic parameter q. It is shown that in the thermodynamical limit both the Tsallis and Renyi entropies are extensive functions of state and the temperature of the system is intensive. In this limit Wang's incomplete nonextensive statistics resembles the Tsallis one, but the Renyi thermostatics is reduced to the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs one. The principle of additivity and the zeroth law of thermodynamics in the canonical ensemble are demonstrated on the particular example of the classical ideal gas of identical particles.

# Thermodynamical limit in non-extensive and Renyi statistics

## Key claim
If the entropic parameter q is connected to an extensive variable of state z (where z = q/(1-q) for Tsallis statistics or z = 1/(q-1) for Renyi statistics), then in the thermodynamical limit both the Tsallis and Renyi entropies become extensive functions of state and the temperature becomes intensive, thereby satisfying all requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics including the zeroth law and the principle of additivity.

## Method
The authors rewrite the expressions for Wang's formalism of Tsallis statistics and Renyi statistics in terms of a new extensive variable z related to the entropic parameter q, then apply the thermodynamical limit procedure (N → ∞, E → ∞, V → ∞ with ε = E/N = constant, v = V/N = constant, and z̃ = z/N = constant). They expand the functions of state in inverse powers of 1/N and examine the factorization properties of the statistical weight and distribution function for dynamically independent subsystems in both the microcanonical and canonical ensembles, using the classical ideal gas of identical particles as a specific test case.

## Key numbers
- The entropic parameter q is related to the extensive variable z via z = q/(1-q) for Tsallis statistics and z = 1/(q-1) for Renyi statistics.
- For the classical non-relativistic ideal gas in the microcanonical ensemble with z extensive, the temperature is T = (2/3)(ε/k) w^(1/z̃) and the entropy is S = k z̃ N [1 - w^(-1/z̃)], where w = v (mε e^(5/3) / (3πħ^2))^(3/2).
- For the classical non-relativistic ideal gas in the microcanonical ensemble with z as a universal constant, the temperature is T = (2/3)(ε/k) (z/(z+1)) w^(N/z) and the entropy is S = k(z+1)[1 - w^(-N/z)].
- The specific energy used for the ideal gas calculations is ε = 50 MeV and the specific volume is v = 3/ρ₀.
- The temperature used for the canonical ensemble distribution function plots is T = 100 MeV with specific volume v = 3/ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Botet et al. (2004) and Parvan (2005, 2006) who first defined the thermodynamical limit for Tsallis statistics by treating 1/(q-1) as an extensive variable. It resolves the violation of the zeroth law and additivity principle previously found in Wang's formalism of Tsallis statistics and Renyi statistics when q was treated as a universal constant (Abe, 2000; Wang, 2001; Abe, 2004). The results enable a consistent connection between nonextensive statistical mechanics and equilibrium thermodynamics, showing that in the thermodynamical limit Renyi statistics reduces to Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics while Tsallis statistics remains distinct but thermodynamically consistent.
