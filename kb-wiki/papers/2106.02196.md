# Effective Matrix Model for Gauge Theories at Finite Temperature and Density using Quantum Computing

> We study the effective matrix model for for gauge fields and fermions on a quantum computer. We use the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) using IBM QISKit for the effective matrix model for SU(2) and SU(3) including fermions in the fundamental representation. For SU(2) we study the effects of finite temperature and nonzero chemical potential. In all cases we find excellent agreement with the classical computation.

## Key claim

The Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) on IBM QISKit can reproduce classical ground-state energies for the effective matrix model of SU(2) and SU(3) gauge theories with fermions, including finite temperature and nonzero chemical potential, to within a relative error of about 10^-5 or better for SU(2) and about 0.9% for SU(3).

## Method

The authors derived one-loop effective potentials for SU(2) and SU(3) gauge fields coupled to fermions in the fundamental representation, compactified on an S^1 of radius L. They represented the Hamiltonian in a finite harmonic-oscillator basis (16x16 matrices for each Wilson line variable), mapped the resulting matrix to qubit operators via Pauli decomposition, and used the VQE algorithm with an R_y variational form, fully entangled circuit depth 3, and the SLSQP optimizer on a statevector simulator.

## Key numbers

- SU(2) with one fermion in fundamental representation: exact ground state energy 0.4425673, VQE result 0.4426310, using 4 qubits and 71 Pauli terms
- SU(2) at finite temperature (with V L / (L^3 beta) = 1): exact ground state energy 0.46617183, VQE result 0.46617228, using 4 qubits and 55 Pauli terms
- SU(2) at finite density (mu = pi/2): exact ground state energy -7.32051788, VQE result -7.32051782, using 4 qubits and 55 Pauli terms
- SU(3) with one fermion in fundamental representation: exact ground state energy -21.98808168, VQE result -21.793084965, using 8 qubits and 9137 Pauli terms

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study of SU(2) effective matrix models on quantum computers (Miceli 2019) to include finite temperature, finite density, and SU(3) gauge theory. The effective matrix model approach, originally developed for QCD applications (Korthals-Altes 1994, Dumitru 2012, Kashiwa 2012) and gauge-Higgs unification (Shiraishi 1986, Ho 1990, Hosotani 1983), requires fewer qubits than full lattice Hamiltonian methods such as those of Kogut and Susskind. The results enable future extensions to nonperturbative equation-of-state terms and inhomogeneous matrix models that may exceed classical simulation capabilities.
