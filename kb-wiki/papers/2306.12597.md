# Nuclear structure and elastic scattering observables obtained consistently with different NN interactions

> Nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions based on chiral effective theories are commonly used in ab initio calculations of light nuclei. Here we present a study based on three different NN interactions (up to next-to-next-to-leading order) for which structure and elastic proton scattering observables are consistently calculated for $^4$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O. The interactions are compared at the two-body level in terms of Wolfenstein amplitudes, and their predictions for ground state energies, point-proton radii, and charge form factors, as well as proton elastic scattering observables in the leading-order spectator expansion in the energy range between 65 and 160 MeV projectile energy are presented. To gain further insight into differences visible in elastic scattering observables, we investigate the behavior of the calculated effective nucleon-nucleus interactions for the $^{12}$C nucleus based on the different $NN$ interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three different chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (NNLO$_{\rm opt}$, EKM N2LO, and Daejeon16), when used consistently for both nuclear structure and elastic proton scattering calculations, produce observables that differ noticeably from each other and from experiment, with the differences becoming more pronounced at higher projectile energies (above 100 MeV) where the NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ interaction becomes considerably weaker in its central amplitude than the other interactions.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for $^4$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O using three different chiral NN interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order, then used the resulting nonlocal one-body densities as input to the leading-order spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory to compute elastic proton-nucleus scattering observables. The effective nucleon-nucleus interactions were constructed from the same NN interactions used for structure, with the NN amplitudes parameterized in terms of Wolfenstein amplitudes, and the Watson optical potential was obtained by solving an additional integral equation. The calculations were performed for projectile laboratory kinetic energies between 65 and 160 MeV, with the HO parameter varied between 16 and 24 MeV (15 to 25 MeV for Daejeon16) to assess convergence.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^4$He: Daejeon16 gives -28.372(1) MeV, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives -27.596(1) MeV, EKM N2LO gives -28.11(1) MeV (experiment: -28.296 MeV)
- Ground state energy of $^{12}$C: Daejeon16 gives -92.9(0.1) MeV, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives -88.4(1.3) MeV, EKM N2LO gives -93.8(3.3) MeV (experiment: -92.16 MeV)
- Ground state energy of $^{16}$O: Daejeon16 gives -131.3(0.3) MeV, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives -133.(3.) MeV, EKM N2LO gives -149.(5.) MeV (experiment: -127.62 MeV)
- RMS point-proton structure radius of $^4$He: Daejeon16 gives 1.514 to 1.513 fm, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives 1.440 to 1.436 fm, EKM N2LO gives 1.450 to 1.416 fm (experiment: 1.484(6) fm)
- RMS point-proton structure radius of $^{12}$C: Daejeon16 gives 2.30 to 2.27 fm, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives 2.30 to 2.14 fm, EKM N2LO gives 2.29 to 2.05 fm (experiment: 2.341(5) fm)
- RMS point-proton structure radius of $^{16}$O: Daejeon16 gives 2.40 to 2.32 fm, NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ gives 2.35 to 2.17 fm, EKM N2LO gives 2.28 to 2.05 fm (experiment: 2.58(1) fm)
- The first zero-crossing of the charge form factor for $^4$He occurs between 4.4 and 4.8 fm$^{-1}$ for all three interactions, whereas experimental data indicate a zero-crossing at or just above 3 fm$^{-1}$
- The NCSM calculations for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O were limited to $N_{\rm max}=10$ on current computational resources, while $^4$He calculations used $N_{\rm max}=18$

## Context

This work extends the program of consistent ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions pioneered by Burrows (2020), Baker (2021), and others, who developed rigorous folding nucleon-nucleus interactions based on the leading-order spectator expansion. The study directly compares three chiral NN interactions (NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ from Ekstrom (2013), EKM from Epelbaum (2014), and Daejeon16 from Shirokov (2016)) that were previously only compared individually to experiment. The results enable systematic investigation of truncation uncertainties in chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleus scattering, similar to what has been done for NN scattering by Furnstahl (2015) and Melendez (2017), and for structure observables by Binder (2018) and Maris (2020). The significant discrepancy in the $^4$He charge form factor above 2.5 fm$^{-1}$ raises open questions about the role of three-body forces, two-body currents, and the breakdown of the chiral expansion at higher momenta, noting that quantum Monte Carlo calculations with phenomenological potentials (AV18 plus UIX three-body forces) and local chiral interactions give much better agreement with experiment for $^4$He as shown by Lynn (2017).
