# Viscosities of the quasigluon plasma

> We investigate bulk and shear viscosities of the gluon plasma within relaxation time approximation to an effective Boltzmann-Vlasov type kinetic theory by viewing the plasma as describable in terms of quasigluon excitations with temperature dependent self-energies. The found temperature dependence of the transport coefficients agrees fairly well with available lattice QCD results. The impact of some details in the quasigluon dispersion relation on the specific shear viscosity is discussed.

# Viscosities of the quasigluon plasma

## Key claim
The temperature dependence of bulk and shear viscosities calculated from a quasiparticle model with temperature-dependent self-energies agrees fairly well with available lattice QCD results, but the specific shear viscosity minimum value and its location depend on the choice of parameters in the relaxation time ansatz and on details of the quasigluon dispersion relation.

## Method
The authors model the gluon plasma as composed of quasigluon excitations with a temperature-dependent self-energy Π(T) within a quasiparticle model (QPM). They derive expressions for shear viscosity η and bulk viscosity ζ using a relaxation time approximation to an effective Boltzmann-Vlasov type kinetic theory, where the relaxation time τ is parameterized by an ansatz inspired by Heiselberg (1994) with two adjustable parameters. The model parameters are fitted to reproduce thermal equilibrium lattice QCD results for the entropy density s/T³ from Boyd (1996) and Okamoto (1999).

## Key numbers
- Specific shear viscosity minimum (η/s)*_min_ = 0.096 at T = 1.22 T_c for Fit 2 parameters (a₁ = 3.85×10⁻², a₂ = 2(μ*/T)² with μ*/T = 2.765)
- For Fit 1 parameters: a₁ = 2.587×10⁻², a₂ = (μ*/T)²
- Using constant mass M = 200 MeV instead of temperature-dependent self-energy increases the minimal η/s by about 14% relative to (η/s)*_min_
- Using constant mass M = 1 GeV yields a minimal η/s 9% smaller than (η/s)*_min_

## Context
This work extends prior quasiparticle model descriptions of equilibrium lattice QCD thermodynamics (Bluhm 2005, 2007, 2008) to transport coefficients, generalizing earlier kinetic theory approaches by Hosoya (1985) and Gavin (1985) that used constant mass excitations. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations from Meyer (2007, 2008), Nakamura (2005), and Sakai (2007), as well as perturbative QCD results from Arnold (2000, 2003, 2006). The approach enables systematic study of how medium effects in the dispersion relation influence transport coefficients near the deconfinement transition temperature T_c.
