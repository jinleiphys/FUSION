# Beth-Uhlenbeck equation for the thermodynamics of fluctuations in a generalised 2+1D Gross-Neveu model

> We study a generalized version of the Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions. The model is inspired from Graphene, which shows a linear dispersion relation near the Dirac points. The phase structure and the thermodynamic properties in the mean field approximation have been studied before. Here, we go beyond the mean field level by deriving a Beth-Uhlenbeck equation for Gaussian fluctuations formulated in phase shift solutions, which we explore numerically, for the first time including their momentum dependence. We discuss the excitonic mass, fluctuation pressure, and phase shifts. The inclusion of momentum dependence in the phase shift shows a significant difference from the Lorentz-boosted version of the phase shift previously used in the literature. We find resurrection of the pseudoscalar bound states at large momentum above Mott temperature and show that the presence of Landau modes significantly contributes to the fluctuation pressure.

## Key claim

The paper goes beyond the mean-field approximation for a generalized 2+1D Gross-Neveu model by deriving and numerically evaluating a Beth-Uhlenbeck equation for Gaussian fluctuations that includes, for the first time, the full momentum dependence of the phase shifts, rather than approximating them via a Lorentz boost from the rest frame as done in prior work.

## Method

The authors start from the mean-field grand potential, renormalized with a momentum cutoff, and then include Gaussian fluctuations of the auxiliary scalar and pseudoscalar fields by expanding the partition function to second order in the fluctuations. They compute the polarization function at finite temperature and chemical potential, analytically continue it to real frequencies, and obtain the phase shifts for the scalar and pseudoscalar channels via the spectral representation of the inverse propagator. The phase shifts are decomposed into resonant and scattering parts, and the fluctuation pressure is evaluated from the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula using these momentum-dependent phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero chemical potential in the chiral limit is \(T_c(\mu=0) = M / (2 \ln 2)\), and the critical chemical potential at zero temperature is \(\mu_c(T=0) = M\), where \(M = \pi/|g|\) is the mass scale set by the renormalized coupling.
- The Mott temperature for the pseudoscalar channel, at which the bound state mass equals twice the constituent fermion mass, is \(T_{\rm Mott} = 0.955\, M\) for a vacuum pseudoscalar mass of \(M_\varphi(0,0) = 0.1 M\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 5M\).
- The squared speed of sound at high temperature approaches the conformal limit of \(c_s^2 = 1/2\), shown for \(\kappa = 0.01\).
- The pseudocritical line in the nonchiral case is shown for \(\kappa = 0.046\, M^2\).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field studies of the 2+1D Gross-Neveu model by Ebert, Zhukovsky, and collaborators (2015, 2018) and applies the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously developed for the 3+1D Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model by Blaschke et al. (2013). The inclusion of momentum-dependent phase shifts goes beyond the Lorentz-boost approximation used in earlier work and enables a more accurate calculation of the fluctuation pressure, including contributions from Landau damping. The results are relevant for understanding the semimetal-insulator transition in graphene, which has been studied via Dyson-Schwinger equations by Khveshchenko (2006) and lattice simulations by Drut and Lahde (2009), but has not yet been observed experimentally.
