# Matrix Elements of Twist-2 Operators in Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We compute the leading non-analytic quark mass dependence of the matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators between octet baryon states in quenched QCD using quenched chiral perturbation theory. There are contributions of the form m_q log m_q, in analogy with QCD, but there are also contributions of the form log m_q from hairpin interactions. The nucleon does not receive such hairpin contributions.

## Key claim

The leading quark mass dependence of isovector twist-2 operator matrix elements between octet baryon states in quenched QCD includes not only the standard \(m_q \log m_q\) terms found in full QCD, but also additional \(\log m_q\) contributions from hairpin interactions, except for the nucleon which receives no such hairpin contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop chiral corrections to forward matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators in quenched chiral perturbation theory (Q\(\chi\)PT), using the graded symmetry \(SU(3|3)_L \otimes SU(3|3)_R\) and the heavy baryon expansion for the octet and decuplet baryon fields. They separate the one-loop contributions into non-hairpin diagrams (giving \(m_q \log m_q\) terms) and hairpin diagrams (giving \(\log m_q\) terms), and present tree-level results including counterterms with coefficients \(b_i^{(n)}\) that must be determined from elsewhere.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant in QCD is taken as \(f \sim 132\ \text{MeV}\), which sets the scale \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi f\) for chiral symmetry breaking.
- For \(n=1\), the coefficients for the baryon twist-2 operators are fixed by isospin charge conservation: \(\alpha^{(1)} = +2\), \(\beta^{(1)} = +1\), \(\gamma^{(1)} = -3\), \(\sigma^{(1)} = 0\).
- The leading non-analytic contributions from non-hairpin diagrams are of the form \(m_q \log m_q\), with the explicit expression containing the factor \(\frac{1-\delta^{n1}}{8\pi^2 f^2}\).
- The meson mass relations in the isospin limit are \(m_{\eta_s}^2 = 2 m_K^2 - m_\pi^2\) and \(m_{\eta_u}^2 = m_{\eta_d}^2 = m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of twist-2 operator matrix elements from QCD (Arndt and Savage, 2000; Chen and Ji, 2000) to the quenched approximation used in lattice QCD simulations. It builds upon the formalism of quenched chiral perturbation theory developed by Sharpe (1990), Bernard and Golterman (1992), and Labrenz and Sharpe (1996). The results enable lattice practitioners performing quenched QCD computations to understand the quark mass dependence of moments of parton distribution functions, and provide a theoretical framework for connecting quenched lattice results to physical QCD through partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory.
