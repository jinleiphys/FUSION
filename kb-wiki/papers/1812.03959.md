# Computing the gluon Sivers function at small-$x$

> We compute the gluon Sivers function $f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp)$ of the transversely polarized nucleon at small-$x$ by exploiting the known connection between the dipole gluon Sivers function and the Odderon. We numerically solve the evolution equation for the Odderon both in the linear and nonlinear regimes. While we find that the $x$ and $k_\perp$ dependences of the Sivers function do not factorize as a result of the quantum evolution, factorization breaking is not numerically significant, and is much milder than what one expects in the case of unpolarized TMDs. We also point out the possibility that, due to the presence of a node in the Sivers function, single spin asymmetry for open charm production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering flips signs as the transverse momentum of D-mesons is varied. This can be tested at the future Electron-Ion Collider.

# Computing the gluon Sivers function at small-$x$

## Key claim

The gluon Sivers function at small-$x$ can be computed from the Odderon exchange, and while the $x$ and $k_\perp$ dependences do not factorize due to quantum evolution, the factorization breaking is numerically mild and much weaker than for unpolarized TMDs; the dipole gluon Sivers function has a node for all values of $x$, which may cause the single spin asymmetry for open charm production to flip signs as a function of D-meson transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors exploit the known connection between the dipole gluon Sivers function and the spin-dependent Odderon, then numerically solve the coupled Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equations for the Pomeron and Odderon amplitudes in both the linear and nonlinear regimes. They discretize the transverse coordinate space on a lattice uniform in $\ln|\tilde{r}_\perp|$ from $-12$ to $4$ with 400 sites and in azimuthal angle from $0$ to $2\pi$ with 100 sites, using $\bar{\alpha}_s = 0.2$ and evolving in rapidity $Y$ with step $\Delta Y = 0.4$ via a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method. The resulting Odderon amplitude is Fourier-transformed to obtain the gluon Sivers function $f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp)$.

## Key numbers

- The initial saturation scale $Q_{s0}$ is used to rescale transverse coordinates; the constant $\kappa$ in the initial Odderon condition $O = \kappa Q_{s0}^3 |r_\perp|^3 \sin\theta e^{-r_\perp^2 Q_{s0}^2}$ is set to $\kappa = 1/3$ for numerical results.
- The strong coupling is fixed at $\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c / \pi = 0.2$ throughout the calculation.
- The Pomeron $N$ develops a $\cos 2\theta$ dependence during evolution with oscillation magnitude of order $5 \times 10^{-5}$ at $Y=4$, estimated as $(\bar{\alpha}_s / 2\pi) Y O^2$ with $O \approx 0.02$.
- For the SSA calculation in open charm production, the charm quark mass is taken as $m_c = 1.3$ GeV and $Q_{s0} = 0.7$ GeV, with kinematic variables $y = z = 1/2$ and $Q^2$ values of 1 GeV and 5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical connection between the gluon Sivers function and the Odderon established by Zhou (2013), Szymanowski (2016), and Boer (2015), and builds on the small-$x$ evolution equations for the Odderon derived by Kovchegov (2003) and Hatta (2005). The numerical solution of the Odderon equation was previously performed by Lappi (2016) but without connection to spin physics. The results enable phenomenological predictions for single spin asymmetries in open charm production at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and complement studies of the Weiszacker-Williams gluon Sivers function sign change in $ep^\uparrow$ and $pp^\uparrow$ collisions by Boer (2016) and Zheng (2018).
