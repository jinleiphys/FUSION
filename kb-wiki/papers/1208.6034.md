# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in $A \le 9$ nuclei

> We present Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in $A\le 9$ nuclei which take into account contributions of two-body electromagnetic currents. The Hamiltonian utilized to generate the nuclear wave functions includes the realistic Argonne-{\it v}$_{18}$ two-nucleon and the Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions. The nuclear two-body electromagnetic currents are derived from a pionful chiral effective field theory including up to one-loop corrections. These currents involve unknown Low Energy Constants which have been fixed so as to reproduce a number of experimental data for the two- and three-nucleon systems, such as $np$ phase shifts and deuteron, triton, and $^3$He magnetic moments. This preliminary study shows that two-body contributions provide significant corrections which are crucial to bring the theory in agreement with the experimental data in both magnetic moments and M1 transitions.

## Key claim
Two-body electromagnetic currents derived from chiral effective field theory provide significant corrections that bring Quantum Monte Carlo predictions for magnetic moments and M1 transitions in nuclei up to A=9 into closer agreement with experimental data, though the study is preliminary and the Low Energy Constants are fixed using data from A=2-3 systems, meaning predictions for A>3 nuclei are the actual test.

## Method
The authors used Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) to solve the Schrodinger equation with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions. They computed magnetic moments and M1 transition matrix elements using one-body and two-body electromagnetic current operators derived from pionful chiral effective field theory up to N3LO (one-loop order), with the two-body currents regularized by a cutoff function exp(-Q^4/Lambda^4) with Lambda=600 MeV. The unknown Low Energy Constants in the two-body currents were fixed by fitting to experimental data for the deuteron, triton, and He-3 magnetic moments and np phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- Two-body current corrections provide up to approximately 40% of the total predicted magnetic moments for A=9 and T=3/2 nuclei
- The cutoff parameter used for regularization of two-body operators is Lambda=600 MeV
- The chiral expansion orders are counted as: LO at e Q^{-2}, NLO at e Q^{-1}, N2LO at e Q^0, and N3LO at e Q^1
- For A=6,7 M1 transitions, previous SNPA calculations found two-body contributions of 17-34%

## Context
This work extends earlier Quantum Monte Carlo studies by Marcucci et al. (2008) from A<=7 nuclei to A<=9 nuclei, and compares the chiral effective field theory current operators from Pastore et al. (2009) with the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach (SNPA) meson-exchange currents used in that prior work. The hybrid approach using chiral currents with realistic wave functions from the AV18+IL7 Hamiltonian shows improved agreement for isoscalar magnetic moments compared to the SNPA formulation, which had underpredicted isoscalar moments by up to 10% in A=7 systems. A full manuscript with detailed results is in preparation.
