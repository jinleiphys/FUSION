# Charge Transfer Fluctuations as a Signal for QGP

> In this work, the charge transfer fluctuation which was previously used for $pp$ collisions is proposed for relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a QGP probe. We propose the appearance of a local minimum at midrapidity for the charge transfer fluctuation as a signal for a QGP. Within a two-component neutral cluster model, we demonstrate that the charge transfer fluctuation can detect the presence of a QGP as well as the size of the QGP in the rapidity space. We also show that the forward-backward correlation of multiplicity can be a similarly good measure of the presence of a QGP. Further, we show that the previously proposed net charge fluctuation is sensitive to the existence of the second phase only if the QGP phase occupies a large portion of the available rapidity space.

## Key claim

The appearance of a local minimum at midrapidity in the charge transfer fluctuation ratio $\kappa(y) = D_u(y)/(dN_{\rm ch}/dy)$ is proposed as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the slope and size of the dip revealing the longitudinal extent of the QGP phase; this signal is sensitive to a QGP even when it occupies only a small rapidity region, unlike net charge fluctuations which require a large QGP phase to be detectable.

## Method

The authors use a two-component neutral cluster model where hadronic clusters have a large charge correlation length and QGP clusters have a much smaller correlation length, and calculate the charge transfer fluctuation $D_u(y) = \langle u(y)^2 \rangle - \langle u(y)\rangle^2$ where $u(y) = [Q_F(y)-Q_B(y)]/2$ is the forward-backward charge asymmetry. They also analyze 50,000 HIJING events for $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV Au-Au collisions as a hadronic baseline, and fit the net charge fluctuation data from STAR to extract the hadronic correlation length $\gamma \approx 1.5$ in rapidity units.

## Key numbers

- The proportionality constant $\kappa$ in $D_u(y) = \kappa dN_{\rm ch}/dy$ for $pp$ collisions at 205 GeV beam energy is approximately $0.62$.
- The charge correlation length $\gamma$ extracted from STAR net charge fluctuation data for $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV Au-Au collisions is $\gamma \approx 1.5$ in rapidity units.
- HIJING simulations for $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV Au-Au collisions yield $\kappa$ values in the range $0.63$ to $0.68$ for rapidity $y=0$ to $3$, corresponding to $\gamma = 2\kappa \approx 1.26$ to $1.36$.
- The corrected net charge fluctuation ratio $\delta Q^2/N_{\rm ch}$ measured by STAR for central collisions in the rapidity window $(-0.5,0.5)$ is around $0.8$ to $0.9$.
- The best fit to HIJING central events using Eq. (13) gives $\kappa = 0.72$ for the observation window $(-1.0,1.0)$.

## Context

This work extends the charge transfer fluctuation concept originally developed for $pp$ collisions by Quigg and Thomas (1973) and Chao and Quigg (1973) to relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a local probe of QGP formation. It addresses the limitation of net charge fluctuation signals proposed by Asakawa (2000) and Jeon (2000), which are insensitive to a QGP confined to a small rapidity region, consistent with negative experimental results from STAR (Adams, 2003) and PHENIX (Adcox, 2002). The approach enables future experimental searches for a local minimum in $\kappa(y)$ at midrapidity, with the expectation that this feature should disappear at lower energies or in peripheral collisions where QGP is not expected to form.
