# Nucleon Polarisabilities from Compton Scattering off the One- and Few-Nucleon System

> These proceedings sketch how combining recent theoretical advances with data from the new generation of high-precision Compton scattering experiments on both the proton and few-nucleon systems offers fresh, detailed insight into the Physics of the nucleon polarisabilities. A multipole-analysis is presented to simplify their interpretation. Predictions from Chiral Effective Field Theory with special emphasis on the spin-polarisabilities can serve as guideline for doubly-polarised experiments below 300 MeV. The strong energy-dependence of the scalar magnetic dipole-polarisability $β_{M1}$ turns out to be crucial to understand the proton and deuteron data. Finally, a high-accuracy determination of the proton and neutron polarisabilities shows that they are identical within error-bars. For details and a better list of references, consult the given references.

## Key claim

Combining recent theoretical advances with high-precision Compton scattering data on both the proton and few-nucleon systems offers fresh insight into nucleon polarisabilities, with the strong energy-dependence of the scalar magnetic dipole-polarisability β_M1 being crucial to understand both proton and deuteron data; a high-accuracy determination shows that proton and neutron polarisabilities are identical within error-bars.

## Method

The paper presents a multipole-analysis of Compton scattering amplitudes to extract dynamical (energy-dependent) polarisabilities, using Chiral Effective Field Theory (χEFT) as the low-energy theory of QCD. The χEFT amplitudes are fitted to proton Compton scattering data below 200 MeV to determine two short-distance parameters (δα, δβ), after which the energy-dependence of all six dipole-polarisabilities is predicted. For the neutron, deuteron Compton scattering data are analysed within the same χEFT framework.

## Key numbers

- Static proton electric dipole-polarisability from free fit: ᾱ^p = 11.5 ± 2.4_stat (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Static proton magnetic dipole-polarisability from free fit: β̄^p = 3.4 ± 1.7_stat (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- With Baldin sum-rule input: ᾱ^p = 11.0 ± 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin and β̄^p = 2.8 ∓ 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Dispersion theory values for comparison: ᾱ^p = 12.4 ± 0.6_stat ∓ 0.6_syst and β̄^p = 1.4 ± 0.7_stat ± 0.5_syst (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Short-distance parameters: δα = -5.9 ± 1.4, δβ = -10.7 ± 1.2 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Baldin sum-rule for the proton: ᾱ^p + β̄^p = 13.8 ± 0.4 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- At ω ≈ 90 MeV, β_M1 is already about 3 units larger than its static value
- Static iso-scalar dipole-polarisabilities from deuteron: ᾱ^s = 12.6 ± 1.4_stat ± 1.0_wavefu and β̄^s = 2.3 ± 1.7_stat ± 0.8_wavefu (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Chiral Effective Field Theory for Compton scattering, building on prior multipole-decomposition approaches and Dispersion Theory analyses (Olmos de Leon, 2001; Beane, 2004). The paper enables future extraction of spin-polarisabilities from doubly-polarised experiments below 300 MeV, particularly around the pion-production threshold, and provides a method to determine dynamical polarisabilities directly from the angular dependence of observables. The finding that proton and neutron polarisabilities are identical within error-bars resolves a long-standing uncertainty about neutron polarisabilities, which previously had reported values for ᾱ^n ranging from -4 to 19 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3).
