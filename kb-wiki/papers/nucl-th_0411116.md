# Particle Number Fluctuations in Statistical Model with Exact Charge Conservation Laws

> Even though the first momenta i.e. the ensemble average quantities in canonical ensemble (CE) give the grand canonical (GC) results in large multiplicity limit, the fluctuations involving second moments do not respect this asymptotic behaviour. Instead, the asymptotics are strikingly different, giving a new handle in study of statistical particle number fluctuations in relativistic nuclear reactions. Here we study the analytical large volume asymptotics to general case of multispecies hadron gas carrying fixed baryon number, strangeness and electric charge. By means of Monte Carlo simulations we have also studied the general multiplicity probability distributions taking into account the decay chains of resonance states.

# Particle Number Fluctuations in Statistical Model with Exact Charge Conservation Laws

## Key claim

Even though the first moments (ensemble averages) of the canonical ensemble converge to grand canonical results in the large multiplicity limit, the second moments (fluctuations) do not share this asymptotic behavior and remain strikingly different, providing a new tool for studying statistical particle number fluctuations in relativistic nuclear reactions. The key qualifier is that this difference persists for all system sizes due to the lack of a charge bath in the canonical ensemble, meaning the variance of multiplicity distributions always stays different from the grand canonical value.

## Method

The authors derived analytical large-volume asymptotic expansions for the scaled variance of particle multiplicities in a multispecies hadron gas with fixed baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, using saddlepoint methods to evaluate the canonical partition function. They diagonalized the Hessian matrix of the generating function to obtain closed-form expressions for the asymptotic scaled variances, and complemented these analytical results with Monte Carlo simulations that included resonance decay chains.

## Key numbers

- For a neutral system in the thermodynamic limit, the scaled variance for positively or negatively charged particles approaches 0.5, compared to the grand canonical Poissonian value of 1.0
- When charge density grows indefinitely, the limiting scaled variances are: ω_+ → 0, ω_- → 1, and ω_ch → 0
- Monte Carlo simulations were performed for a volume of V = 200 fm³, which was verified to be large enough to reproduce asymptotic results
- The full quantum hadron gas calculations were performed at temperature T = 160 MeV with charge-to-baryon ratio Q/B = 0.4, corresponding to PbPb and AuAu reactions
- All resonances up to mass ∼1.9 GeV were included in the calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gorega and Begun on canonical ensemble fluctuations in hadron gases, and builds on the projection method for canonical partition functions developed in prior work. The authors provide a general analytical framework for multi-charge systems (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) that goes beyond the single-charge or two-charge approximations studied previously. The results enable experimental comparisons of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, where exact charge conservation in the canonical ensemble produces measurable deviations from grand canonical predictions, and the inclusion of resonance decay chains makes the predictions directly comparable to experimental data.
