# Generalized parton distributions for dynamical equation models

> We show how generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can be determined in the case where hadrons are described in terms of their partonic degrees of freedom through solutions of dynamical equations. We demonstrate our approach on the example of two-quark bound states described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and three-quark bound states described by three- and four-dimensional Faddeev-like equations. Within the model of strong interactions defined by the dynamical equations, all possible mechanisms contributing to the GPDs are taken into account, and all GPD sum rules are satisfied automatically. The formulation is general and can be applied to determine generalized quark distributions, generalized gluon distributions, transition GPDs, nucleon distributions in nuclei, etc. Our approach is based on the gauging of equations method.

## Key claim

The paper shows that generalized parton distributions (GPDs) can be extracted from hadronic systems described by dynamical equations (Bethe-Salpeter for two-quark bound states and Faddeev-like equations for three-quark bound states) using the "gauging of equations method," with the condition that the resulting density matrix satisfies all GPD sum rules automatically only when the input quantities (gauged propagators and potentials) themselves obey corresponding sum rules.

## Method

The authors apply the "gauging of equations method" to bilocal operators, extending a technique previously used for electromagnetic currents. They start from the density matrix definition for GPDs and derive expressions for the (n+2)-point Green function by U-gauging the integral equations that define the bound state Green functions, cutting all bare propagators in the strong interaction model. The method is demonstrated for two-body bound states described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation and for three-body bound states described by both four-dimensional Faddeev-like equations and the three-dimensional spectator approach of Gross.

## Context

This work extends the "gauging of equations method" previously developed by the same authors (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1999-2004) for constructing charge- and current-conserving electromagnetic interactions in nonperturbative models, now applied to bilocal operators for GPDs. The approach directly addresses the problem of preserving GPD sum rules in models where hadrons are described by solutions of dynamical equations, which was not guaranteed by earlier methods. The formulation is general enough to be applied to generalized quark distributions, generalized gluon distributions, transition GPDs, and nucleon distributions in nuclei, enabling future calculations of GPDs in the spectator approach of Gross (1982) and in Bethe-Salpeter approaches previously explored by Miller and Vento.
