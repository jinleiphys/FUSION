# Effects of multi-scale jet-medium interactions on jet substructures

> We utilize event-by-event Monte Carlo simulations within the JETSCAPE framework to examine scale-dependent jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions. The reduction in jet-medium interaction during the early high-virtuality stage, where the medium is resolved at a short distance scale, is emphasized as a key element in explaining multiple jet observables, particularly substructures, simultaneously. By employing the MATTER+LBT setup, which incorporates this explicit reduction of medium effects at high virtuality, we investigate jet substructure observables, such as Soft Drop groomed observables. When contrasted with existing data, our findings spotlight the significant influence of the reduction at the early high-virtuality stages. Furthermore, we study the substructure of gamma-tagged jets, providing predictive insights for future experimental analyses. This broadens our understanding of the various contributing factors involved in modifying jet substructures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating an explicit reduction of jet-medium interactions at high parton virtuality (where the medium is resolved at a shorter distance scale) is essential for simultaneously describing jet substructure observables, inclusive jet spectra, and single particle spectra in heavy-ion collisions, whereas the same observables cannot be reproduced without this virtuality-dependent suppression.

## Method

The JETSCAPE framework was used to simulate PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02\) TeV with the MATTER+LBT multi-stage model, where MATTER handles high-virtuality vacuum-like shower evolution with reduced medium effects and LBT handles low-virtuality medium-induced radiation. The switching between modules occurs on a per-parton basis at a switching virtuality \(Q^2_\mathrm{sw}=4~\mathrm{GeV}^2\), with the effective jet quenching strength in the MATTER phase modulated by a virtuality-dependent factor \(f(Q^2)\) that suppresses interactions at high virtuality. Hard partons were generated by PYTHIA8, the medium profile was obtained from freestreaming plus viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNU) with TRENTo initial conditions, and final-state hadronization used the Lund string model in PYTHIA8.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) for reconstructed jets and single charged particles in 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02\) TeV; the setup with virtuality dependence used \(\alpha_{\mathrm{s}} = 0.3\), while the setup without used \(\alpha_{\mathrm{s}} = 0.25\) with \(f(Q^2)=1\) for all \(Q^2\)
- Switching virtuality between MATTER and LBT modules: \(Q^2_\mathrm{sw}=4~\mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- The virtuality-dependent modulation factor is parametrized as \(f(Q^2) = \frac{1+10\ln^{2}(Q^2_\mathrm{sw}) + 100\ln^{4}(Q^2_\mathrm{sw})}{1+10\ln^{2}(Q^2) + 100\ln^{4}(Q^2)}\) for \(Q^2 > Q_{\rm sw}^2\) and \(f(Q^2)=1\) for \(Q^2 \le Q_{\rm sw}^2\)
- Soft Drop groomed observables \(z_g\) and \(r_g\) for inclusive charged jets in 0-10% centrality at 5.02 TeV; the \(z_g\) distribution shows virtually no medium modification, while the \(r_g\) distribution shows differences between setups
- The \(r_{\mathrm{g}}\)-dependent \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) for inclusive full jets in 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV shows qualitative differences between setups, with experimental data favoring the virtuality-dependent formulation
- Predictions for photon-tagged jet \(z_g\) and \(r_g\) distributions for different gamma-jet asymmetry \(X_{\mathrm{J\gamma}} = p^{\mathrm{jet}}_{\mathrm{T}}/p^{\mathrm{\gamma}}_{\mathrm{T}}\) ranges at 0-10% centrality and 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multi-stage approach (Putschke 2019, JETSCAPE 2019-2023) by explicitly incorporating virtuality-dependent jet quenching strength, building on the theoretical insight from Kumar (2019) that high-virtuality partons resolve the QGP at a shorter distance scale. The results contradict the assumption that a single, virtuality-independent transport coefficient can simultaneously describe inclusive jet suppression and jet substructure modifications, and enable future experimental analyses of gamma-tagged jets to disentangle genuine medium-induced narrowing from energy-loss bias effects on jet substructure.
