# M1 Resonances in Unstable Magic Nuclei

> Within a microscopic approach which takes into account RPA configurations, the single-particle continuum and more complex $1p1h\otimes phonon$ configurations isoscalar and isovector M1 excitations for the unstable nuclei ${56,78}$Ni and ${100,132}$Sn are calculated. For comparison, the experimentally known M1 excitations in ${40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb have also been calculated. In the latter nuclei good agreement in the centroid energy, the total transition strength and the resonance width is obtained. With the same parameters we predict the magnetic excitations for the unstable nuclei. The strength is sufficiently concentrated to be measurable in radioactive beam experiments. New features are found for the very neutron rich nucleus ${78}$Ni and the neutron deficient nucleus ${100}$Sn.

## Key claim

The authors predict that M1 resonance strength in the unstable nuclei ⁵⁶Ni, ⁷⁸Ni, ¹⁰⁰Sn, and ¹³²Sn is sufficiently concentrated to be measurable in radioactive beam experiments, but the resonance widths show no simple A-dependence and depend delicately on the interplay between shell structure and low-lying vibrational modes.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic approach combining continuum RPA with more complex 1p1h⊗phonon configurations, formulated in coordinate space within Migdal's Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS). They employed a universal set of Landau-Migdal interaction parameters (with g' adjusted to 0.86 for M1 calculations) and a standard Woods-Saxon mean field, with single-particle energies corrected to avoid double-counting of particle-phonon coupling contributions. The strength function dB(M1)/dω was computed from the density response to an external M1 field, including both RPA and particle-phonon coupling terms.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca: calculated centroid energy 10.36 MeV (experimental 10.23 MeV)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb: calculated total B(M1↑) = 11.6 μ₀² (experimental 15.6 μ₀²); calculated centroid energy 7.74 MeV (experimental 7.3 MeV); experimental width ~1 MeV
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn: the g₉/₂(p) → g₇/₂(n) Gamow-Teller transition energy is 7.6 MeV
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn: the M1 resonance splits into two major peaks with centroid energies Ē₁ = 9.8 MeV and Ē₂ = 10.5 MeV
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn: proton binding energy Bₚ = 2.91 MeV
- The spin local charge values used: eᵖ_q = 0.64γᵖ and eⁿ_q = 0.74γⁿ, where γᵖ = 2.79 μ₀ and γⁿ = -1.91 μ₀

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations of M1 resonances in stable magic nuclei (e.g., Richter and Speth, 1985; Bortignon et al., 1985) by including 1p1h⊗phonon configurations and the single-particle continuum, following the formalism previously applied to giant electric resonances by Kamerdzhiev et al. (1991) and Kamerdzhiev et al. (1992). The predictions for unstable nuclei enable future experimental tests at radioactive beam facilities, and confirmation would establish the universality (A-independence) of the Migdal parameters, allowing generalization to non-magic nuclei far from stability.
