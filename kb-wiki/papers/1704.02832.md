# More quantum centrifugal effect in rotating frame

> The behaviour of quantum systems in non-inertial frames is revisited from the point of view of affine coherent state (ACS) quantization. We restrict our approach to the one-particle dynamics confined in a rotating plane about a fixed axis. This plane is considered as punctured due to the existence of the rotation center, which is viewed as a singularity. The corresponding phase space is the affine group of the plane and the ACS quantization enables us to quantize the system by respecting the affine symmetry of the true phase space. Our formulation predicts the appearance of an additional quantum centrifugal term, besides the usual angular momentum one, which prevents the particle to reach the singular rotation center. Moreover it helps us to understand why two different non-inertial Schrödinger equations are obtained in previous works. The validity of our equation can be confirmed experimentally by observing the harmonic oscillator bound states and the critical angular velocity for their existence.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using affine coherent state (ACS) quantization for a particle in a rotating punctured plane yields an additional repulsive quantum centrifugal term proportional to \(1/|\vec{x}|^2\) that is absent in canonical quantization, and that the differences between two previously published non-inertial Schrödinger equations arise from different choices of the fiducial vector in the ACS quantization scheme.

## Method

The authors apply affine coherent state (ACS) quantization to the classical Hamiltonian of a particle in a rotating frame, treating the punctured plane \(\mathbb{R}^2_*\) as the configuration space and identifying the phase space with the 2D affine group SIM(2). They construct a unitary irreducible representation of SIM(2) on \(L^2(\mathbb{R}^2_*, d^2\vec{x})\) and define quantum operators via the resolution of the identity provided by affine coherent states. The resulting Schrödinger equation for a harmonic oscillator potential is solved analytically for stationary states, and the energy eigenvalues and critical angular velocity for bound states are derived.

## Key numbers

- The additional quantum centrifugal term has strength \(\sigma = \frac{1}{c_\psi} \int_{\mathbb{R}^2_*} d^2\vec{q} \, |\nabla_{\vec{q}} \psi(\vec{q})|^2\), which is positive for any admissible differentiable fiducial vector \(\psi\).
- For the choice \(({\cal N}_0, {\cal N}_1) = (1, 1/2)\) reproducing previous approaches, the critical angular velocity for bound states is \(\Omega_{cri} = \sqrt{2}\,\omega\).
- For the choice \(({\cal N}_0, {\cal N}_1) = (2, 1/2)\) reproducing the Klink-Wickramasekara result, the critical angular velocity is \(\Omega_{cri} = \omega/\sqrt{2}\).
- The classical critical angular velocity is \(\Omega_{cri} = \omega\).
- The energy eigenvalues are \(E_{n,l} = \omega \sqrt{2{\cal N}_1} \left( 2n+1 + \sqrt{\sigma + (l+\varsigma)^2} - \frac{{\cal N}_0}{2\sqrt{{\cal N}_1}} \frac{\Omega}{\omega} l \right)\), with \(\varsigma \in \{0, 1/2\}\) under time-reversal symmetry.

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of non-inertial Schrödinger equations by Schmüdgen (1977), Mashhoon, Anandan, Takagi, and Koide, as well as the more recent approach by Klink and Wickramasekara (using the Galilei line group). The paper shows that the differences between these two families of results can be understood as different choices of the fiducial vector in ACS quantization. The predicted additional quantum centrifugal term and the modified critical angular velocity can be tested experimentally by observing the energy spectrum of harmonic oscillator bound states.
