# Initial eccentricity and constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow in ideal and viscous dynamics

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, we study the scaling properties elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions. Initial energy density of the fluid was fixed to reproduce STAR data on $ϕ$ meson multiplicity in 0-5% Au+Au collisions, such that irrespective of fluid viscosity, entropy at the freeze-out is similar in ideal or in viscous evolution. Initial eccentricity or constituent quark number scaling is only approximate in ideal or minimally viscous ($η/s=1/4π$) fluid. Eccentricity scaling become nearly exact in more viscous fluid ($η/s \geq$0.12). However, in more viscous fluid, constituent quark number scaled elliptic flow for mesons and baryons split into separate scaling functions. Simulated flows also do not exhibit 'universal scaling' i.e. elliptic flow scaled by the constituent quark number and charged particles $v_2$ is not a single function of transverse kinetic energy scaled by the quark number. From a study of violation of universal scaling, we obtain an estimate of QGP viscosity, $η/s=0.12 \pm 0.03$.

## Key claim

Initial eccentricity scaling of elliptic flow becomes nearly exact in more viscous fluid (η/s ≥ 0.12), while constituent quark number scaling splits into separate scaling functions for mesons and baryons; the abstract's claim of an estimate η/s = 0.12 ± 0.03 is qualified as statistical only, with systematic error potentially as large as 200%.

## Method

The paper uses Israel-Stewart's second-order dissipative hydrodynamics (code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, assuming boost-invariance and a baryon-free fluid with shear viscosity but no bulk viscosity or heat conduction. Initial energy density was tuned to reproduce STAR data on φ meson multiplicity in 0-5% central collisions, ensuring similar freeze-out entropy across ideal and viscous evolutions, with a lattice+hadronic resonance gas equation of state and freeze-out at T_F = 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial central energy density ε_i = 35.5 GeV/fm³ for ideal fluid (η/s = 0), decreasing to 20.8 GeV/fm³ for η/s = 0.16, with errors of ±5.0 and ±2.7 GeV/fm³ respectively
- φ meson multiplicity in 0-5% Au+Au collisions: predicted 7.96 (η/s=0) to 8.22 (η/s=0.12), compared to STAR measurement 7.95 ± 0.74
- φ meson mean p_T: predicted 1.019 GeV (η/s=0) to 1.174 GeV (η/s=0.16), compared to STAR measurement 0.977 ± 0.064 GeV
- At KE_T ≈ 2 GeV, v₂/ε for Ω in 50-60% centrality exceeds that for π in 0-10% by ~45% in ideal fluid, ~30% at η/s=0.08, ~15% at η/s=0.12, and ~7% at η/s=0.16
- Viscosity estimate from universal scaling violation: η/s = 0.12 ± 0.03 (statistical only)

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow scaling by Song and Heinz (2008) and Bhalerao et al. (2005), who found approximate eccentricity scaling in ideal fluids but larger scaling violation in viscous dynamics. The paper contradicts the expectation that viscous corrections worsen scaling, finding instead that eccentricity scaling improves with viscosity due to the use of a lattice-based crossover equation of state and reduced viscous corrections at freeze-out. The viscosity estimate agrees with prior phenomenological estimates from Drescher et al. (2007) (η/s = 0.11-0.19) and Lacey et al. (2006) (η/s ≈ 0.09 ± 0.015), but the author notes that systematic errors from uncertain initial conditions can be as large as 200%, as previously shown in Chaudhuri (2009).
