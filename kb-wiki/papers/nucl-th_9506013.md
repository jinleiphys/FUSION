# Intrinsic vs. laboratory frame description of the deformed nucleus 48Cr

> The collective yrast band of the nucleus $^{48}$Cr is studied using the spherical shell model and the HFB method. Both approaches produce basically the same axially symmetric intrinsic state up to the - accurately reproduced - observed backbending. Agreement between both calculations extends to most observables. The only significant discrepancy comes from the static moments of inertia and can be attributed to the need of a more refined treatment of pairing correlations in the HFB calculation.

## Key claim

The spherical shell model (SM) and the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) method produce essentially the same axially symmetric intrinsic state for the yrast band of ⁴⁸Cr up to the backbending at spin J=10, with the only significant discrepancy being in the static moments of inertia, which is attributed to an inadequate treatment of pairing correlations in the CHFB calculation rather than to the Gogny force itself.

## Method

The authors performed full pf-shell spherical shell model calculations using the KB3 effective interaction with effective charges qπ=1.5 and qν=0.5, diagonalized via the Lanczos method with the code ANTOINE. They also carried out self-consistent cranking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force (DS1 parameter set) expanded in a triaxial harmonic oscillator basis with ten oscillator shells. The CHFB wave functions were then projected onto a spherical basis to compute fractional shell occupancies, shell contributions to ⟨Jx⟩, and shell contributions to the quadrupole moment, allowing direct comparison with the SM results.

## Key numbers

- The CHFB gamma-ray energies Eγ(J)=E(J)-E(J-2) are shifted downward by approximately 0.5 MeV compared to the SM and experimental values
- The static moment of inertia from CHFB is on average a factor of 1.5 bigger than the SM and experimental values
- The ratio Q20/Q20pf(HO) (total quadrupole moment to valence harmonic oscillator contribution) ranges from 1.99 at J=0 to 1.83 at J=14
- The ratio Q20/Q20pf(HF) (using spherical HF orbits) ranges from 1.70 at J=0 to 1.63 at J=14
- The gyromagnetic factor g is close to the rotational limit gR=Z/A=0.50 in both calculations up to the backbending region
- For a pure f7/2 configuration, the g-factor is constant at 0.55
- The CHFB deformation parameter β stays constant at β≈0.3 with γ≈0 up to the backbend
- The overlap between SM wavefunctions with modified pairing and original SM wavefunctions exceeds 0.97 in all cases

## Context

This work extends the comparison between laboratory-frame and intrinsic-frame descriptions of deformed nuclei into the pf shell, where previous studies were limited by the lack of experimental data and computational constraints. The authors build on recent measurements by Cameron et al. (1994) showing that ⁴⁸Cr is a good rotor up to spin J=10, and on full pf-shell calculations by Caurier et al. (1994) that reproduce the properties of A=48 isobars. The paper demonstrates that ⁴⁸Cr behaves as an axially symmetric rotor (unlike ²⁴Mg in the sd shell which obeys Elliott's SU3 symmetry and is triaxial), and enables future combined use of CHFB simplicity with SM precision for studying deformed nuclei in the pf shell.
