# Measurements of Dihadron Correlations Relative to the Event Plane in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV

> Dihadron azimuthal correlations containing a high transverse momentum ($p_T$) trigger particle are sensitive to the properties of the nuclear medium created at RHIC through the strong interactions occurring between the traversing parton and the medium, i.e. jet-quenching. Previous measurements revealed a strong modification to dihadron azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions with respect to p+p and d+Au collisions. The modification increases with the collision centrality, suggesting a path-length or energy density dependence to the jet-quenching effect. This paper reports STAR measurements of dihadron azimuthal correlations in mid-central (20-60%) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV as a function of the trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the event plane, $ϕ_s=|ϕ_t-ψ_{\rm EP}|$. The azimuthal correlation is studied as a function of both the trigger and associated particle $p_T$. The subtractions of the combinatorial background and anisotropic flow, assuming Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM), are described. The correlation results are first discussed with subtraction of the even harmonic (elliptic and quadrangular) flow backgrounds. The away-side correlation is strongly modified, and the modification varies with $ϕ_s$, with a double-peak structure for out-of-plane trigger particles. The near-side ridge (long range pseudo-rapidity $Δη$ correlation) appears to drop with increasing $ϕ_s$ while the jet-like component remains approximately constant. The correlation functions are further studied with subtraction of odd harmonic triangular flow background arising from fluctuations. It is found that the triangular flow, while responsible for the majority of the amplitudes, is not sufficient to explain the $ϕ_s$-dependence of the ridge or the away-side double-peak structure. ...

## Key claim

The paper reports that the away-side dihadron correlation in mid-central (20-60%) Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV exhibits a double-peak structure for trigger particles oriented out-of-plane relative to the event plane, and that while triangular flow (v3) accounts for most of the correlation amplitude, it alone cannot explain the phi_s dependence of either the near-side ridge or the away-side double-peak structure.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured dihadron azimuthal correlations in 20-60% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using high-pT trigger particles (pT > 3 GeV/c) and associated particles (1-2 GeV/c). The trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the event plane (phi_s = |phi_t - psi_EP|) was used to bin the data. Combinatorial background and anisotropic flow contributions were subtracted using the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) method, first removing even harmonics (elliptic v2 and quadrangular v4) and then separately studying the effect of subtracting odd harmonic triangular flow (v3).

## Key numbers

*   The analysis uses mid-central (20-60%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
*   Trigger particles are selected with transverse momentum pT > 3 GeV/c; associated particles are selected with 1 < pT < 2 GeV/c.
*   The near-side ridge (long-range Delta eta correlation) is observed to drop with increasing phi_s, while the jet-like component remains approximately constant.
*   Triangular flow (v3) is found to be responsible for the majority of the correlation amplitudes, but is not sufficient to explain the phi_s-dependence of the ridge or the away-side double-peak structure.

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements that showed strong modification of dihadron correlations in Au+Au collisions relative to p+p and d+Au collisions, with the modification increasing with centrality (Adler et al., 2003; Adams et al., 2005). The measurement of correlations relative to the event plane allows separation of path-length dependent jet-quenching effects from bulk flow. The results enable future studies to disentangle the contributions of initial-state fluctuations and final-state medium response to the observed away-side modification, and provide constraints for models of parton energy loss and the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
