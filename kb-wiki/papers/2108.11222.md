# Role of quadrupole deformation and continuum effects in the "island of inversion'' nuclei $^{28,29,31}$F

> The properties of nuclei in the ``island of inversion'' (IOI) around Z=10 and N=20 are the focus of current nuclear physics research. Recent studies showed that $^{28}$F has a negative-parity ground state (g.s.) and thus lies within the southern shore of the IOI, and $^{29}$F presents a halo structure in its g.s., but it is unclear which effects, such as deformation, shell evolution due to tensor forces, or couplings to the continuum, lead to this situation. We investigate the role of quadrupole deformation and continuum effects on the single-particle (s.p.) structure of $^{28,29,31}$F from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach, and show how both phenomena can lead to a negative-parity g.s. in $^{28}$F and halo structures in $^{29,31}$F. We solve the Dirac equation in the complex-momentum (Berggren) representation for a potential with quadrupole deformation at the first order obtained from RMF calculations using the NL3 interaction, and calculate the continuum level densities using the Green's function method. We extract s.p. energies and widths from the continuum level densities to construct Nilsson diagrams, and analyse the evolution of both the widths and occupation probabilities of relevant Nilsson orbitals in $^{28}$F and find that some amount of prolate deformation must be present. In addition, we calculate the density distributions for bound Nilsson orbitals near the Fermi surface in $^{29,31}$F and reveal that for a quadrupole deformation $0.3 \leq β_2 \leq 0.45$ (prolate), halo tails appear at large distances. We also demonstrate that while in the spherical case the $pf$ shells are already inverted and close to the neutron emission threshold, a small amount of quadrupole deformation can reduce the gap between $fp$ shells and increase the role of the continuum, ultimately leading to the negative parity in the g.s. of $^{28}$F and the halo structures in $^{29,31}$F.

# Role of quadrupole deformation and continuum effects in the "island of inversion" nuclei 28,29,31F

## Key claim

While the spherical case already shows inverted pf shells near the neutron emission threshold, a small amount of quadrupole deformation (prolate or oblate) is required to dramatically reduce the gap between positive- and negative-parity states and increase the role of continuum states, ultimately leading to the negative parity ground state in 28F and halo structures in 29,31F. The authors find that some amount of prolate deformation must be present in 28F, and that quadrupole deformation between β2 = 0.3 and 0.45 is likely in 29,31F.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 interaction to generate spherical mean-field potentials for each fluorine isotope, then parametrized deformed Woods-Saxon potentials from these results. They solved the Dirac equation in the complex-momentum (Berggren) representation for potentials with quadrupole deformation at first order, and calculated continuum level densities using the Green's function method to extract single-particle energies and widths for constructing Nilsson diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 28F is a resonance at 199 keV with negative parity, with approximately 80% l=1 content from the p3/2 neutron shell (Revel et al., 2020)
- For 28F, the 1d3/2 shell lies about 1.5 MeV below the neutron emission threshold in the spherical case
- The 1f7/2 shell lies almost 5.0 MeV above the threshold in the spherical case for 28F
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: diffuseness a = 0.75 fm, radius R = 3.32 fm; vector and scalar depths V0 = 371 MeV and S0 = -439 MeV for 28F, and V0 = 337 MeV and S0 = -405 MeV for 29,31F (giving V0 + S0 = -68 MeV for both sets)
- For 29,31F, the quadrupole deformation range producing halo tails is 0.3 ≤ β2 ≤ 0.45 (prolate)
- For prolate deformation β2 = 0.3 in 28F, the 1/2[310] orbital has 77% 2p3/2 contribution, 15% 2p1/2 contribution, and about 8% 1f7/2 contribution
- For oblate deformation β2 = -0.3 in 28F, the 3/2[301] orbital is 97% 2p3/2, while the 1/2[310] orbital splits as 52% 2p3/2 and 45% 2p1/2
- The 1/2[310] orbital in 29F shows approximately 80% l=1 content that varies little with quadrupole deformation for β2 > 0.3

## Context

This work extends the complex-momentum representation Green's function method to the relativistic framework for deformed nuclei, building on prior RMF approaches by Fang (2017) and Li (2016). It addresses experimental findings by Revel (2020) showing 28F has a negative-parity ground state inside the island of inversion, and by Bagchi (2020) demonstrating a halo structure in 29F. The results are consistent with shell model calculations by Caurier (2014) and Poves (1987) showing deformation around 29F, and with Gamow shell model calculations by Michel (2020) suggesting a two-neutron halo in 31F. This work enables future experimental study of A > 29 fluorine isotopes to determine how quadrupole deformation develops with neutron number and affects single-particle structure with increasing continuum couplings.
