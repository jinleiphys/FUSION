# Generalised Bohr Hamiltonian for Gogny interactions

> Using a generalised Bohr Hamiltonian formalism together with the Gogny interaction to provide a microscopic input of the required mass parameters, we present the potential energy surface and the evolution of the first excited 2$^+$ state for the gadolinium isotopic chain. We observe that the energies and electromagnetic transitions of low-lying states are fairly similar for the various parametrisations and are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim
Using a generalised Bohr Hamiltonian with Gogny interactions, the energies and electromagnetic transitions of low-lying states in the gadolinium isotopic chain are fairly similar across four parametrisations and agree well with experimental data, but the best agreement on average is obtained with the Gogny D1S interaction, and a more detailed analysis is needed for robust conclusions about the role of the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using four Gogny effective interactions (D1S, D1N, D1M, D3G3M) to derive the collective potential and six mass parameters for the generalised Bohr Hamiltonian. The mass parameters were calculated using the perturbative cranking approximation with a global scaling factor of 1.3, and the collective Hamiltonian was expanded on a basis in the β,γ plane and diagonalised using a newly developed numerical code.

## Key numbers
- The potential energy surface of ¹⁵⁴Gd shows a well pronounced prolate minimum at β ≈ 0.3 for all selected Gogny interactions.
- The low-energy spectrum of ¹⁵⁴Gd is expressed in keV and electromagnetic transition strengths in Weisskopf units; the method reproduces the overall structure quite well.
- The evolution of the first excited 2⁺ level and B(E2;2⁺ → 0⁺) transition strength is shown for the isotopic chain ¹⁵²–¹⁶⁰Gd.

## Context
This work extends the generalised Bohr Hamiltonian approach developed in Refs. Kumar (1967), Libert (1999), and Prochniak (1999), which was applied systematically in Refs. Bertsch (2007), Delaroche (2010), and Delaroche (2024) to analyse lowest excited states of even-even nuclei. The study tests sensitivity to four Gogny parametrisations (D1S, D1N, D1M, D3G3M) for the gadolinium chain, finding robust results with little dependence on the interaction, and enables future systematic comparisons of effective interactions in collective nuclear structure calculations.
