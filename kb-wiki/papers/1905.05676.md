# Heavy quarkonium suppression beyond the adiabatic limit

> Many prior studies of in-medium quarkonium suppression have implicitly made use of an adiabatic approximation in which it was assumed that the heavy quark potential is a slowly varying function of time. In the adiabatic limit, one can separately determine the in-medium breakup rate and the medium time evolution, folding these together only at the end of the calculation. In this paper, we relax this assumption by solving the 3d Schrodinger equation in real-time in order to compute quarkonium suppression dynamically. We compare results obtained using the adiabatic approximation with real-time calculations for both harmonic oscillator and realistic complex heavy quark potentials. Using the latter, we find that, for the Upsilon(1s), the difference between the adiabatic approximation and full real-time evolution is at the few percent level, however, for the Upsilon(2s), we find that the correction can be as large as 18% in low temperature regions. For the J/Psi, we find a larger difference between the dynamical evolution and the adiabatic approximation, with the error reaching approximately 36%.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the adiabatic approximation, which assumes the heavy quark potential varies slowly enough to treat in-medium breakup and medium evolution separately, introduces errors that are small for the Upsilon(1s) but can reach 18% for the Upsilon(2s) and approximately 36% for the J/Psi when compared to full real-time evolution of the 3d Schrodinger equation.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the time-dependent 3d Schrodinger equation in real-time using a split-operator pseudospectral method, evolving wave functions under both a toy complex harmonic oscillator potential and a realistic complex Karsch-Mehr-Satz (CKMS) potential. For the CKMS potential, they used a radial reduction with Fourier sine transforms to enforce boundary conditions at the origin, and extracted time-dependent eigenstates via a modified point-and-shoot method with an adjustable parameter to handle the complex potential. They compared the resulting survival probabilities to the adiabatic approximation formula N_n(t) = N_n(0) exp(2 integral of Im[E_n(t)] dt) for bottomonium and charmonium states under boost-invariant Bjorken flow with initial proper time tau_0 = 1 GeV^{-1} and freeze-out temperature T_fo = 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the Upsilon(1s) at initial temperature T_0 = 0.6 GeV, the difference between adiabatic approximation and full real-time evolution was approximately 6% at final time
- For the Upsilon(2s), the correction from the adiabatic approximation can be as large as 18% in low temperature regions
- For the J/Psi, the error between dynamical evolution and adiabatic approximation reached approximately 36%
- The toy harmonic oscillator study used heavy quark mass m = 1 GeV with a box size L = 40 GeV^{-1} and 1024 grid points, with temporal step size Delta t = 5 x 10^{-4} GeV^{-1}
- The CKMS potential calculations used box size L = 40 GeV^{-1}, N = 1024 grid points, and temporal step size Delta tau = 1 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1}
- The bottom quark mass was taken as m_b = 4.7 GeV (pole mass from PDG)
- The initial proper time was fixed at tau_0 = 1 GeV^{-1} = 0.197 fm/c

## Context

This work extends prior phenomenological studies of quarkonium suppression by Strickland and collaborators (2011-2017) and Krouppa and collaborators (2015-2017) that implicitly used the adiabatic approximation, as well as earlier work by Karsch, Matsui, and Satz (1986-1987) on quarkonium melting. The paper directly addresses suggestions by Dutta (2012) that there may be important corrections beyond the adiabatic limit. The results indicate that future quantitative descriptions of heavy quarkonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma require solving the real-time Schrodinger equation coupled to realistic 3d hydrodynamical backgrounds, particularly for excited bottomonium states and charmonium states.
