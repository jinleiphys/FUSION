# Comment on "Regge Trajectories for All Flavors"

> We show that Regge trajectories for all flavors suggested recently by Filipponi et al. cannot combine both meson spectroscopy and additivity of intercepts. Other defects of these trajectories are also discussed.

## Key claim

The Regge trajectory formula proposed by Filipponi, Pancheri and Srivastava (1998) cannot simultaneously satisfy both meson spectroscopy and the theoretically required additivity of intercepts; fixing the quark mass parameters to reproduce known vector meson masses leads to violations of intercept additivity, and vice versa.

## Method

The authors test the proposed formula by extracting constituent quark masses from hidden-flavor vector meson masses (rho, phi, J/psi, Upsilon) using the condition alpha(M^2)=1, then applying the additivity of intercepts constraint to relate these masses to those in open-flavor trajectories (D*, D_s*, B*, B_s*). They compute the resulting vector meson masses from the formula and compare them to experimental values from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Using the proposed formula with intercept additivity gives M(D*) = 1882.5 MeV, while the measured value is 2008 +/- 2 MeV
- M(D_s*) = 2007.1 MeV from the formula versus measured 2112.4 +/- 0.7 MeV
- M(B*) = 4566.3 MeV from the formula versus measured 5324.8 +/- 1.8 MeV (discrepancy ~700 MeV)
- M(B_s*) = 4724.1 MeV from the formula versus measured 5416.3 +/- 3.3 MeV
- The formula gives alpha_rho(0) = 0.47, while Donnachie and Landshoff extracted alpha_rho(0) = 0.55 from pp and pbar-p scattering data
- The formula gives alpha_K*(0) = 0.29, while hypercharge exchange processes give alpha_K*(0) ≈ 0.40
- Simple constituent quark model relations give M(B*) = 5114 MeV and M(B_s*) = 5240 MeV, which are closer to experiment than the formula's predictions

## Context

This comment extends the authors' prior work (Burakovsky and Goldman, 1998) establishing that additivity of inverse Regge slopes is required by the heavy quark limit, and that additivity of intercepts is a firmly established theoretical constraint from two-dimensional QCD and many QCD-motivated models. The paper contradicts the claim by Filipponi, Pancheri and Srivastava (1998) that their flavor-dependent trajectory formula successfully describes meson spectroscopy and inclusive D meson production. The analysis enables future work by demonstrating that a trajectory form satisfying both additivity constraints, using constituent quark masses m_n=0.29 GeV, m_s=0.46 GeV, m_c=1.65 GeV, m_b=4.80 GeV, reproduces data more reliably.
