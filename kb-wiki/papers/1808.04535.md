# Microscopic optical potentials for calcium isotopes

> We construct nucleonic microscopic optical potentials by combining the Green's function approach with the coupled-cluster method for $\rm{^{40}Ca}$ and $\rm{^{48}Ca}$. For the computation of the ground-state of $\rm{^{40}Ca}$ and $\rm{^{48}Ca}$, we use the coupled-cluster method in the singles-and-doubles approximation, while for the A = $\pm 1$ nuclei we use particle-attached/removed equation-of-motion method truncated at two-particle-one-hole and one-particle-two-hole excitations, respectively. Our calculations are based on the chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction $\rm{NNLO_{sat}}$, which reproduces the charge radii of $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca, and the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction $\rm{NNLO_{opt}}$. In all cases considered here, we observe that the overall form of the neutron scattering cross section is reproduced for both interactions, but the imaginary part of the potential, which reflects the loss of flux in the elastic channel, is negligible. The latter points to neglected many-body correlations that would appear beyond the coupled-cluster truncation level considered in this work. We show that, by artificially increasing the parameter $η$ in the Green's function, practical results can be further improved.

# Microscopic optical potentials for calcium isotopes

## Key claim

The coupled-cluster Green's function approach reproduces the overall form of neutron scattering cross sections for 40Ca and 48Ca, but the imaginary part of the optical potential is negligible at the CCSD truncation level, indicating that many-body correlations beyond singles-and-doubles are needed to account for absorption; artificially increasing the parameter η in the Green's function can improve practical results.

## Method

The authors combine the Green's function approach with the coupled-cluster method, using CCSD for the ground states of 40Ca and 48Ca and particle-attached/removed equation-of-motion methods truncated at 2p-1h and 1p-2h excitations for the A±1 nuclei. They employ a complex Berggren basis generated from a Hartree-Fock potential to analytically continue the Green's function into the complex-energy plane, and compute the optical potential by inverting the Dyson equation. Calculations are based on the chiral NN+3N interaction NNLOsat and the chiral NN interaction NNLOopt.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-cluster Green's function approach previously introduced in Rotureau et al. (2017) (cited as gfccpap) from systems with only NN interactions to include three-nucleon forces via the NNLOsat interaction. It differs from the self-consistent Green's function approach of Dickhoff (2004) and Barbieri (2005, 2017) by obtaining the optical potential directly from inversion of the Dyson equation rather than iterative self-consistency. The results show that CCSD-level truncation is insufficient to generate the imaginary part of the optical potential needed for absorption, pointing to the need for including triples excitations and higher-order correlations; this enables future work to incorporate such correlations or to use finite η as a practical proxy for missing absorption channels.
