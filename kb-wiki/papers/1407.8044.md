# Nonextensive thermodynamics with finite chemical potentials and protoneutron stars

> We derive the nonextensive thermodynamics of an ideal quantum gas composed by bosons and/or fermions with finite chemical potentials. We find agreement with previous works when $μ\le m$, and some inconsistencies are corrected for fermions when $μ> m$. This formalism is then used to study the thermodynamical properties of hadronic systems based on a Hadron Resonance Gas approach. We apply this result to study the protoneutron star stability under several conditions.

## Key claim

The paper derives thermodynamically consistent expressions for nonextensive (Tsallis) quantum gases of bosons and fermions with finite chemical potentials, correcting previous works for fermions when the chemical potential exceeds the mass (μ > m). The authors find that while Tsallis statistics hardens the equation of state of hadronic matter, this effect is washed out by leptons in protoneutron star applications, so Tsallis statistics alone cannot explain the observed maximum stellar masses.

## Method

The authors start from the Tsallis entropy framework with entropic index q and define q-deformed exponential and logarithmic functions to construct the grand-canonical partition function for ideal quantum gases. They derive thermodynamic quantities including particle number, energy, and entropy, paying careful attention to a discontinuity at x = 0 (where p = (μ² - m²)^(1/2)) that produces a momentum-independent correction term C_{N,q} necessary for thermodynamic consistency when μ > m. They apply this formalism to a Hadron Resonance Gas model with 808 meson states and 1168 baryon states (plus anti-baryons), and then to protoneutron star matter composed of neutrons, protons, pions, electrons, and muons under charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- The entropic index used throughout is q = 1.14, which characterizes the nonextensive behavior of hadronic systems in the confined phase.
- For μ_B = 0, the effective temperature is τ_o = 45.6 MeV when q = 1.14, in agreement within 25% with values from p_T-distributions in high energy p+p collisions.
- The chemical freeze-out lines span the region 0 < μ_B < 1039.2 MeV.
- The maximum stellar masses and radii obtained lie around 0.7 M_⊙ and 8 Km.
- The constant temperature scenario for protoneutron star calculations uses τ ≈ 20 MeV.
- The hadronic states considered include mesons with masses below 11 GeV and baryons with masses below 5.8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of Tsallis statistics in high energy physics, such as the description of transverse momentum distributions by Bediaga (1999) and the nonextensive generalization of Hagedorn's theory by Marques (2012) which found q ≈ 1.14. It corrects thermodynamic inconsistencies in prior formulations by Conroy (2010) and Cleymans (2011) for the sector x < 0, which is important for protoneutron star studies but was irrelevant for ultra-relativistic collisions where T, m ≫ μ. The results enable future work (Menezes, 2014) incorporating nuclear interactions and fixed entropy scenarios to better explain the observed 2 M_⊙ neutron stars reported by Demorest (2010).
