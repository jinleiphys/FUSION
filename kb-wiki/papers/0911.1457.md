# Effective Range Expansion for the Interaction Defined on the Lattice

> The relation between the interaction parameters for fermions on the spatial lattice and the two-body $T$ matrix is discussed. The presented method allows determination of the interaction parameters through the relatively simple computational scheme which include the effect of finite lattice spacing. In particular the relation between the interaction parameters and the effective range expansion parameters is derived in the limit of large lattices.

# Effective Range Expansion for the Interaction Defined on the Lattice

## Key claim
The paper derives a practical computational scheme that relates the coupling constants of a two-body interaction defined on a spatial lattice to the effective range expansion parameters (scattering length and effective range), properly including finite lattice spacing effects in the limit of large lattice volume. The method bypasses the more complicated Lüscher formula by reducing the T-matrix calculation to traces of small matrices whose dimension equals the number of lattice interaction sites.

## Method
The authors start from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix and rewrite it as an infinite series of matrix traces, where each term involves the lattice interaction coupling constants and a free-particle propagator evaluated at lattice site separations. They then take the large-lattice limit (L → ∞) with fixed lattice spacing b, impose a spherical momentum cutoff p_cut = π/b, and analytically evaluate the propagator integral to obtain closed-form expressions. The resulting series is matched to the effective range expansion to extract the scattering length and effective range as functions of the coupling constants.

## Key numbers
- The momentum cutoff is set to p_cut = π/b, where b is the lattice spacing.
- For the on-site (Hubbard) interaction, the relation between the coupling constant g₀ and scattering length a is: 1/a = 4π/(m g₀) + 2 p_cut/π.
- For the on-site interaction, the effective range is r_eff = 4/(π p_cut), showing that a zero-range potential acquires a nonzero effective range due to finite lattice spacing.
- The effective range expansion is valid for momenta satisfying k_F ≲ 0.6 fm⁻¹ in the context of dilute neutron matter in the ¹S₀ channel.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Seki and Kolck for determining interaction parameters on large lattices using effective field theory power counting, and provides an alternative to the more involved Lüscher (1986) formula which requires solving for the exact two-particle energy spectrum in a box. The method is directly applicable to Monte Carlo simulations of dilute fermionic systems, such as trapped cold atoms (where only the scattering length matters) and dilute neutron matter, and can be used with extended Hubbard models (D=7) that include nearest-neighbor interactions.
