# Spin hydrodynamics -- recent developments

> After briefly touching on relativistic hydrodynamics, we provide a detailed description of recent developments in spin hydrodynamics. We discuss the theory of perfect spin hydrodynamics within two different approaches, which lead to identical generalized thermodynamic relations. We also indicate the applicability range of the theory, finding it compatible with the conditions existing in the late stages of heavy-ion collisions. Finally, we discuss the near-equilibrium dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a hybrid formulation of spin hydrodynamics, which combines classical and quantum (Wigner-function) approaches to local equilibrium, yields identical generalized thermodynamic relations and is compatible with conditions in the late stages of heavy-ion collisions, provided the spin polarization tensor components satisfy a specific convergence criterion involving the particle mass-to-temperature ratio.

## Method

The authors develop perfect spin hydrodynamics from two distinct local-equilibrium descriptions: a classical treatment using distribution functions in an extended phase space (with spin vector \(s^\alpha\)) and a quantum approach using a newly constructed local-equilibrium Wigner function for spin-1/2 particles. They compute the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor, and entropy current from both frameworks, then derive generalized thermodynamic relations of tensor form. The convergence of the scalar density integrals is analyzed in the local rest frame, yielding applicability criteria in terms of the electric- and magnetic-like components of the spin polarization tensor \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) and the fluid velocity. Dissipation is introduced via an Israel-Stewart-type expansion, with entropy production expressed in terms of gradients of the hydrodynamic variables.

## Key numbers

* The convergence criterion for perfect spin hydrodynamics in the local rest frame is \(C \sqrt{ {\bvp}^2 + {\evp}^2 + 2 |\evp \times \bvp|} < m/T\), where \(C = \sqrt{3/4}\) in the classical case and \(C = 1/2\) in the quantum case, with \(\evp\) and \(\bvp\) the electric- and magnetic-like components of \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) in that frame.
* A simpler bound using the maximum absolute component \(\omega_{\rm max}^\prime\) in the local rest frame is \(C \omega_{\rm max}^\prime (2 + \sqrt{2}) < m/T\).
* For a fluid element moving with speed \(v\) relative to the laboratory frame, the criterion becomes \(C \omega_{\rm max} (2 + \sqrt{2}) \sqrt{(1+v)/(1-v)} < m/T\).
* The spin eigenvalue used in the classical integration measure is \(\spin = \sqrt{3/4}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier kinetic-theory formulations of spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2017, 2018; Bhadury 2020, 2022) and reconciles them with the approach based on thermal vorticity and shear (Becattini 2013, 2021; Palermo 2024) and with Israel-Stewart-type frameworks (Hattori 2019; Fukushima 2020; Daher 2022). It also addresses the pseudogauge ambiguity by adopting the GLW pseudogauge (deGroot 1980). The derived convergence criteria enable quantitative checks of whether spin hydrodynamics is applicable in heavy-ion collision simulations, and the hybrid approach provides a foundation for future dissipative spin-hydrodynamic codes that can be tested against experimental spin-polarization data (STAR 2017; Adam 2018).
