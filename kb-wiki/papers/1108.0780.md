# Consistent interactions for high-spin baryons

> Consistent interactions for off-shell fermion fields of arbitrary spin are constructed from the gauge-invariance requirement of the interaction Lagrangians. These interactions play a crucial role in the quantum hadrodynamical description of high-spin baryon resonances in hadronic processes. We find that the power of the momentum dependence of a consistent interaction rises with the spin of the fermion field. This leads to unphysical structures in the energy dependence of the computed tree-level cross sections when the short-distance physics is cut off with standard hadronic form factors. A novel, spin-dependent hadronic form factor is proposed that suppresses the unphysical artifacts.

## Key claim

Consistent interaction Lagrangians for high-spin baryons, constructed from gauge invariance under the unconstrained Rarita-Schwinger gauge, introduce a momentum dependence whose power rises with spin; this causes unphysical structures in tree-level cross sections when standard hadronic form factors are used, and the authors propose a novel spin-dependent form factor to suppress these artifacts.

## Method

The authors construct gauge-invariant interaction Lagrangians for off-shell Rarita-Schwinger fields of arbitrary spin-\((n+1/2)\) by requiring invariance under the unconstrained Rarita-Schwinger gauge transformation. They then compute tree-level cross sections for the reaction \(\gamma p \rightarrow N^* \rightarrow K^+\Lambda\) using mock and real resonances (e.g., \(N(1680)\,F_{15}\)), and test the effect of standard dipole and Gaussian form factors versus their proposed multidipole-Gauss form factor on the energy dependence of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- For a mock resonance with \(J^P_R = 5/2^+\), \(m_R = 1700\) MeV, and \(\Gamma_R = 50\) MeV, the computed cross section shows divergent high-energy behavior.
- The \(N(1680)\,F_{15}\) resonance has a mass \(m_R = 1685\) MeV and a decay width \(\Gamma_R = 130\) MeV (four-star PDG rating).
- With the multidipole-Gauss form factor and an artificially lowered decay width \(\Gamma_R' = 20\) MeV, the deviation of the observed mass from the physical value is \(0.22\%\) and the deviation of the observed decay width is \(0.75\%\).

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant approach to high-spin interactions developed by Vrancx (2011) and builds on the Rarita-Schwinger formalism (Rarita 1941) and the spin-\((n+1/2)\) propagator derived by Huang (2005). It addresses the problem that standard hadronic form factors (dipole form from Pearce (1990) and Gaussian form from Corthals (2005)) produce artificial bumps in cross sections for high-spin resonances. The proposed multidipole-Gauss form factor enables a consistent description of high-spin baryon resonances in hadronic processes, particularly for resonances with large decay widths as listed by the Particle Data Group (Nakamura 2010).
