# Shell-Model Monte Carlo Simulations of Pairing in Few-Fermion Systems

> We study a trapped system of fermions with an attractive zero-range two-body interaction using the Shell-Model Monte Carlo method. The method provides {\em ab initio} results in the low $N$ limit where mean-field theory is not applicable. The energy and pairing properties are presented as functions of interaction strength, particle number, and temperature. In the interesting region where typical matrix elements of the two-body interaction are comparable to the level spacing of the trap we find large odd-even effects and signatures of shell structure. As a function of temperature, we observe the disappearance of these effects as in a phase transition.

## Key claim

The Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method provides exact (subject only to statistical uncertainties) finite-temperature results for few trapped fermions with an attractive zero-range interaction in the regime where typical two-body matrix elements are comparable to the trap level spacing, but the results are on the BEC side of the crossover and the relation between the interaction strength parameter \(g\) and the scattering length \(a\) is only approximate.

## Method

The authors map a two-component ultracold Fermi gas with an attractive zero-range \(s\)-wave interaction onto a single spin-1/2 nucleon species and apply the Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method, which uses a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to linearize the imaginary-time many-body propagator \(e^{-\beta H}\) and expresses observables as path integrals over one-body propagators in fluctuating auxiliary fields. Calculations are performed in a finite harmonic-oscillator model space of 4 major shells (with a check using 5 major shells for \(N=2\)), and the interaction strength is renormalized through continuum low-energy scattering to relate the dimensionless parameter \(g\) to the \(s\)-wave scattering length \(a\). Energies and a number-conserving BCS-like pair matrix (pairing strength) are computed as functions of particle number \(N\), temperature \(T\), and interaction strength \(g\).

## Key numbers

- For \(g=10\) (corresponding to \(a = 10.66\,b\), where \(b = \sqrt{\hbar/m\omega}\) is the oscillator length), the SMMC energy for \(N=2\) at \(T = 1/6\,\hbar\omega\) is \(2.60(1)\,\hbar\omega\), compared to the exact \(T=0\) ground-state value of \(2.4\,\hbar\omega\) for \(a = 10.66\,b\).
- Energies (in units of \(\hbar\omega\)) for \(g=10\) at \(T=1/6\,\hbar\omega\) range from \(2.60(1)\) for \(N=2\) to \(56.1(2)\) for \(N=20\); at \(T=1/5\,\hbar\omega\) they range from \(2.62(1)\) for \(N=2\) to \(56.4(1)\) for \(N=20\).
- The specific heat exhibits a peak around \(T \sim 0.3\,\hbar\omega\) for \(g=10\), shifting to higher temperature for \(g=20\); the peak height is larger for the closed-shell system \(N=8\) than for \(N=10\).
- Odd-even staggering in pairing strength vanishes around \(T \sim (1/3 - 1/2)\,\hbar\omega\) for \(g=10\), and persists to larger temperatures for \(g=20\).
- At \(g=1\) (deep BCS regime), no interesting pairing effects remain; for \(g>20\) the calculation becomes unstable.

## Context

This work extends the Shell-Model Monte Carlo method, previously used in nuclear physics for finite-temperature properties (Johnson 1992, Koonin 1997), to ultracold Fermi gases in traps, providing exact many-body results in the few-fermion regime where mean-field theory fails. It complements Green's function Monte Carlo studies of homogeneous and trapped systems (Carlson 2003, Bertsch 2007), no-core shell-model calculations (Stetcu 2007), and traditional shell-model approaches (Alhassid 2007), which have focused on the unitary limit and crossover regime. The results enable quantitative predictions for experiments with few fermions in tight traps or optical lattices (Jochim 2008, Stoferle 2006) and open the way to study deformation properties and higher angular momentum pairing in ultracold gases, analogous to nuclear shape transitions studied with SMMC (Koonin 1997).
