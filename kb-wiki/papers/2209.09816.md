# Systems Dominated by Exchange Particles

> As well known, the spectrum of a non-relativistic two-body system interacting by the Coulomb potential is the Balmer series $E_n=\frac{α^2m}{4n^2}$ produced by the Schrödinger equation. In 1954, Wick and Cutkosky have found, in the Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, that for $α>\fracπ{4}$ the relativistic effects result in new levels (in addition to the Balmer series). However, the physical nature of these new states remained unclear and therefore their existence was being questioned. We have recently shown that these extra states are dominated by the exchange (massless) particles, moving with speed of light. That's why they did not appear in the non-relativistic (Schrödinger) framework.

# Systems Dominated by Exchange Particles

## Key claim
The extra "abnormal" states found by Wick and Cutkosky (1954) in the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a relativistic two-body Coulomb system exist because they are dominated by massless exchange particles moving at the speed of light, not by the massive constituent particles; this explains why they disappear in the non-relativistic Schrödinger framework and why their existence was questioned for nearly 70 years.

## Method
The author solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a one-boson exchange kernel for massless exchange particles, obtaining integral representations for the S-wave amplitudes. The two-body Fock sector contribution to the full state vector normalization was computed from these amplitudes, and elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors were calculated to distinguish normal from abnormal states.

## Key numbers
- For coupling constant α=5, the binding energy |E| for the normal state (n=1, κ=0) is 0.999259 m, while for the abnormal state (n=1, κ=2) it is 3.51169 × 10⁻³ m.
- For the abnormal state (n=1, κ=2) at α=5, the two-body sector contribution to normalization N₂ = 0.094 (less than 10%); for (n=1, κ=4) it is 6.19 × 10⁻³ (less than 1%).
- The abnormal spectrum near threshold follows E_κ ≈ −m exp[−(κ−1)π / √(α/π − 1/4)] for α > π/4 and κ = 2,3,...
- The critical coupling constant for the appearance of abnormal states is α > π/4, corresponding to charge Z > 107.
- At Q² = 1, the elastic form factor for the abnormal state (n=1, κ=2) is approximately 10³ times smaller than the normal state form factor at Q² = 10.
- The transition form factor between normal and abnormal states is suppressed by a factor of 100 compared to transitions within states of the same nature.

## Context
This work extends the foundational results of Wick (1954) and Cutkosky (1954), who first found extra solutions to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Coulomb problem beyond the Balmer series. It resolves a 70-year debate about the physical nature of these "abnormal" states, which Nakanishi (1969) reviewed as potentially indicating a defect of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The author's recent papers (Karmanov, 2020, 2021) showed these states are many-body systems dominated by exchange particles. The results enable experimental searches for such states in strong-field systems (Z > 107) and suggest a conceptual connection to glueballs and hybrid states in QCD, though the latter involve colored gluons and self-interactions rather than scalar exchanges.
