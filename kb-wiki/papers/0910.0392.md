# Why is $v_4/(v_2)^2$ larger than predicted by hydrodynamics?

> The second and fourth Fourier harmonics of the azimuthal distribution of particles, $v_2$ and $v_4$, have been mesured in Au-Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ is significantly larger than predicted by hydrodynamics. Effects of partial thermalization are estimated on the basis of a transport calculation, and are shown to increase the ratio by a small amount. We argue that the large value of $v_4/(v_2)^2$ seen experimentally is mostly due to elliptic flow fluctuations. However, the standard model of eccentricity fluctuations is unable to explain the large magnitude of $v_4/(v_2)^2$ in central collisions.

## Key claim

The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) measured in Au-Au collisions at RHIC is significantly larger than the ideal hydrodynamic prediction of \(v_4 = \frac{1}{2}(v_2)^2\), and this discrepancy is mostly due to elliptic flow fluctuations rather than partial thermalization; however, the standard model of eccentricity fluctuations cannot explain the large magnitude of \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) in the most central collisions.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation to study systems with arbitrary numbers of collisions per particle, parameterized by the Knudsen number \(K \propto 1/n_{coll}\). They computed \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) as a function of \(K\) and extrapolated to the hydrodynamic limit \(K=0\). They then combined these transport results with a Monte-Carlo Glauber model of initial eccentricity fluctuations to predict the centrality dependence of the measured ratio \(\langle v_4 (v_2)^2 \rangle / \langle (v_2)^2 \rangle^2\).

## Key numbers

- The ideal hydrodynamic limit (\(K=0\)) yields \(v_4/(v_2)^2 = 0.52\), close to the predicted \(\frac{1}{2}\).
- For Au-Au collisions at top RHIC energy, the typical number of collisions per particle is \(n_{coll} \propto 3-5\), based on a prior study by Drescher (2007).
- The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) scales like \(1/n_{coll}\) in the limit of small \(n_{coll}\), meaning the farther from equilibrium, the larger the ratio.

## Context

This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic prediction of Borghini (2005) that \(v_4 = \frac{1}{2}(v_2)^2\), which was contradicted by experimental data from STAR (Abelev, 2007) and PHENIX (Huang, 2008). The authors build on their own prior transport study (Drescher, 2007) that showed deviations from local equilibrium are needed to reproduce the centrality dependence of elliptic flow. The results enable a more complete understanding of flow fluctuations and thermalization effects, but leave open the question of why central collisions show an even larger \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) than the model predicts.
