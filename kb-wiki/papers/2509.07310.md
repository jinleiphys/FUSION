# Quantum Monte Carlo calculation of $δ_{\rm NS}$ in $^{10}$C using an effective field theory approach

> We compute radiative corrections to the superallowed $β$ decay of $^{10}{\rm C}$ in an effective field theory approach using nuclear matrix elements obtained from quantum Monte Carlo calculations. These corrections are an important ingredient in the extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Masakawa quark mixing matrix element $V_{ud}$, and the role of this work is to illuminate the uncertainties arising from nuclear structure. Our results provide good agreement with both the traditional extraction of $V_{ud}$, as well as with a more recent evaluation performed using the no-core shell model and a dispersion formalism. The dominant uncertainty in this approach is the presence of two unknown low-energy constants that enter into the relevant nuclear matrix elements. Future determinations of these low-energy constants -- either from QCD or modeling them with two nucleon amplitudes -- would improve the precision of the extraction in this formalism.

## Key claim

The paper computes nuclear-structure-dependent radiative corrections to the superallowed beta decay of ¹⁰C using an effective field theory approach with quantum Monte Carlo nuclear wave functions, finding good agreement with both the traditional extraction of the CKM matrix element V_ud and a recent no-core shell model evaluation, but the dominant uncertainty comes from two unknown low-energy constants that parameterize short-range two-nucleon physics.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear-structure-dependent correction δ_NS for the ¹⁰C(0⁺) → ¹⁰B*(0⁺;1) transition using chiral effective field theory operators at leading order in the chiral expansion, with nuclear wave functions obtained from variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods. They evaluate matrix elements of two-body transition operators including Gamow-Teller, Fermi, tensor, and spin-orbit components using four different nuclear Hamiltonians: the phenomenological AV18+UX and AV18+IL7 models, and two versions of the Norfolk chiral interaction (NV2+3-Ia and NV2+3-Ia*).

## Key numbers

- The electron endpoint energy for ¹⁰C decay is E₀ = 1907.994 ± 0.067 keV (from Hardy and Towner 2020)
- The phase-space average factors are f̃_E = 1.105 and f̃_{m_e}^π = 0.23 for ¹⁰C → ¹⁰B
- The vector coupling constant at the renormalization scale μ_π = M_π is g_V(M_π) = 1.01494(12)
- The Fermi matrix element M_F = 7.07 for the NV2+3-Ia VMC wave function, consistent with the expected value √2(Z-1) ≈ 7.07 where Z=6
- The Gamow-Teller matrix elements from VMC with NV2+3-Ia are M_GT,p = -2.33 and M_GT,n = 0.29
- The dominant GFMC matrix elements for the NV2+3-Ia model are: M^mag_GT,p = -0.515, M^mag_GT,n = 0.082, M^mag_T = 0.002, M_LS,p = -0.005, M^CT_GT,p = 0.051, M^CT_GT,n = 0.025, M^+_F,p = -2.83, and M^E_F,p = -4.74

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory formalism developed by Cirigliano et al. (2023, 2024) for nuclear-structure-dependent radiative corrections in superallowed beta decays, applying it to ¹⁰C for the first time using quantum Monte Carlo methods. It complements the recent calculation by Gennari et al. (2024) that used the no-core shell model with a dispersive approach for the same nucleus. The results enable improved extraction of V_ud from superallowed decays, with the dominant remaining uncertainty being the two unknown low-energy constants that could be determined from QCD calculations or two-nucleon amplitude modeling.
