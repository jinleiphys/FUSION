# Testing Deconfinement at High Isospin Density

> We study the transition from hadronic matter to a mixed phase of quarks and hadrons at high baryon and isospin densities reached in heavy ion collisions. We focus our attention on the role played by the nucleon symmetry energy at high density.In this respect the inclusion of a scalar isovector meson, the δ-coupling, in the Hadron Lagrangian appears rather important. We study in detail the formation of a drop of quark matter in the mixed phase, and we discuss the effects on the quark drop nucleation probability of the finite size and finite time duration of the high density region. We find that, if the parameters of quark models are fixed so that the existence of quark stars is allowed, then the density at which a mixed phase starts forming drops dramatically in the range Z/A \sim 0.3--0.4. This opens the possibility to verify the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis on absolute stability of quark matter using ground-based experiments in which neutron-rich nuclei are employed. These experiments can also provide rather stringent constraints on the Equation of State (EoS) to be used for describing the pre-Supernova gravitational collapse. Consistent simulations of neutron rich heavy ion collisions are performed in order to show that even at relatively low energies, in the few AGeV range, the system can enter such unstable mixed phase. Some precursor observables are suggested, in particular a ``neutron trapping'' effect.

## Key claim

If the parameters of quark models are fixed so that the existence of quark stars is allowed (i.e., the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis is satisfied), then the density at which a mixed phase of hadrons and quarks starts forming drops dramatically for systems with proton fraction Z/A in the range 0.3-0.4, potentially reaching densities as low as nuclear matter saturation density.

## Method

The authors used a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach for hadronic matter, comparing the Glendenning-Moszkowski parameter sets (GM1, GM2, GM3) with a model including a scalar isovector delta-meson coupling (NLρδ). For quark matter they employed the MIT bag model (with and without first-order corrections in α_s) and the Color Dielectric Model (CDM). The deconfinement phase transition was described by imposing Gibbs equilibrium conditions for chemical potentials and pressure. Relativistic transport simulations of neutron-rich heavy ion collisions (132Sn+132Sn and 238U+238U at 1 AGeV) were performed using the same effective Lagrangians to check whether the mixed phase region could be reached in realistic collisions.

## Key numbers

- For the MIT bag model with B^{1/4}=148 MeV and the GM3 hadronic EoS, the transition density drops to very small values (indicated by arrows in figures) for Z/A ~ 0.3-0.4
- For B^{1/4}=150 MeV with the NLρδ hadronic EoS, the critical density reaches approximately ρ_0 (0.16 fm^{-3}) at Z/A ~ 0.3
- The bag constant range explored was between B=(140 MeV)^4 and B=(170 MeV)^4
- The symmetry energy at saturation for NLρδ is 31.3 MeV, with saturation density ρ_0=0.160 fm^{-3}
- The quark drop nucleation time scale is estimated to be approximately 10 fm/c
- The beam energy used in simulations is 1 AGeV for 132Sn+132Sn and 238U+238U collisions

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Mueller (1998) on the isospin dependence of deconfinement transition densities, but explores a wider range of model parameters and includes the crucial effect of a scalar isovector delta-meson coupling in the hadronic Lagrangian. The authors also incorporate finite-size and finite-time nucleation effects for quark drop formation, which were absent in previous studies. The paper enables experimental tests of the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis using ground-based experiments with neutron-rich nuclei at radioactive beam facilities, and can provide constraints on the Equation of State relevant for pre-Supernova gravitational collapse.
