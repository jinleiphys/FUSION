# A microscopic calculation of secondary Drell-Yan production in heavy ion collisions

> A study of secondary Drell-Yan production in nuclear collisions is presented for SPS energies. In addition to the lepton pairs produced in the initial collisions of the projectile and target nucleons, we consider the potentially high dilepton yield from hard valence antiquarks in produced mesons and antibaryons. We calculate the secondary Drell-Yan contributions taking the collision spectrum of hadrons from the microscopic model URQMD. The contributions from meson-baryon interactions, small in hadron-nucleus interactions, are found to be substantial in nucleus-nucleus collisions at low dilepton masses. Preresonance collisions of partons may further increase the yields.

## Key claim

Secondary Drell-Yan production from interactions of produced hadrons (especially meson-baryon collisions) can substantially enhance the dilepton yield in nucleus-nucleus collisions at low invariant masses (around 1.5-2 GeV), whereas this contribution is negligible in hadron-nucleus collisions; the enhancement depends on system size and could reach 45% in central Pb+Pb collisions, though the perturbative calculation is unreliable below 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic hadronic transport model URQMD to generate the collision spectrum of secondary hadrons in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies (200 A GeV for S+U, 160 A GeV for Pb+Pb). They computed the leading-order Drell-Yan cross section for each hadron-hadron collision using GRV LO parton distribution functions for nucleons (GRV 94) and pions (GRV 92), applying a constant K-factor (1.5 for nucleon-nucleon, 2 for pion-induced reactions). The secondary contributions from meson-baryon, meson-meson, and baryon-antibaryon interactions were summed and compared to the primary nucleon-nucleon Drell-Yan yield, with the hadron formation time set to approximately 1 fm/c in the default calculation.

## Key numbers

- In p+Pt collisions at 400 GeV/c, secondary collisions contribute less than 10% of the dilepton yield for masses above 1.5 GeV, and less than 1% at forward xF values (0.325 and 0.625).
- At masses around 1.5 GeV, secondary Drell-Yan enhances the yield by 25% in S+U collisions at 200 A GeV and by 45% in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 A GeV.
- For masses above 3 GeV, the additional production from secondary hadrons is less than 10% in both S+U and Pb+Pb interactions.
- Meson-baryon interactions account for at least 90% of the secondary yield at masses around 1 GeV, with meson-meson contributing about 10% and baryon-antibaryon less than 1%.
- Setting the formation time to zero (preresonance scattering) increases the secondary dilepton yield by a factor of approximately 5 at all masses compared to the default formation time of about 1 fm/c.
- The isospin correction factor for Pb+Pb interactions is 1.3 with the GRV 92 LO set and 1.03 with the GRV 94 LO set (which includes a flavour-asymmetric sea).

## Context

This work extends previous Drell-Yan calculations for nucleus-nucleus collisions (Vogt 1992) that considered only initial nucleon-nucleon interactions, and complements studies of thermal dilepton emission from quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas (Kapusta 1992, Kampfer 1992, Dumitru 1993, Winckelmann 1995, Geiger 1993, Gavin 1996). The results show that secondary hadron interactions provide a non-negligible source of dileptons at intermediate masses (around 2 GeV) that must be accounted for before thermal contributions can be reliably extracted from data such as those from NA38/NA50 and CERES. The finding that secondary yields scale linearly with pion multiplicity, while thermal sources scale quadratically, offers a potential experimental handle to distinguish these contributions at RHIC and LHC energies.
