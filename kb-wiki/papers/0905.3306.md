# N-d Elastic Scattering Using the Hyperspherical Harmonics Approach with Realistic Local and Non-Local Interactions

> The application of the hyperspherical harmonic approach to the case of the N-d scattering problem below deuteron breakup threshold is described. The nuclear Hamiltonian includes two- and three-nucleon interactions, in particular the Argonne v_{18}, the N3LO-Idaho, and the V_{low k} two-nucleon, and the Urbana IX and N2LO three-nucleon interactions. Some of these models are local, some are non-local. Also electromagnetic effects are included. Accurate calculations for many scattering observables at various center-of-mass energies are performed and the results are compared with the available experimental data. Furthermore, a χ^2 analysis of some of the Hamiltonian models has been performed to compare their capability to describe the scattering process.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method is extended to describe N-d elastic scattering below deuteron breakup threshold using both local and non-local two- and three-nucleon interactions, with the caveat that the method is applied only to the N3LO-Idaho and Vlow-k non-local potentials (not the CDBonn potential) because the Fourier transform of the momentum-space potential to coordinate space requires the potential to have no high-momentum tail.

## Method

The scattering wave function is split into a core part expanded on the HH basis (in either coordinate or momentum space) and an asymptotic part describing the relative N-d motion. The Kohn variational principle is applied to determine the linear coefficients of the core expansion and the reactance (K) matrix elements, from which phase shifts and scattering lengths are obtained. For non-local potentials expressed in momentum space, the Fourier transform of the potential to coordinate space is performed using spherical Bessel functions, and the projection onto specific particle pairs is handled via Raynal-Revai coefficients and recoupling of spin-isospin states.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy with the Vlow-k model (cutoff Lambda = 2.2 fm^-1) is 8.477 MeV when the complete electromagnetic interaction is used, and 8.519 MeV without electromagnetic effects.
- The non-linear parameter gamma in the hyperradial Laguerre functions is chosen in the interval 6-8 fm^-1 for the N3LO-Idaho potential.
- The regularization parameter b for the irregular Coulomb function is set to 0.25 fm^-1.
- Convergence tests use M=28 Laguerre polynomials in the hyperradial functions, with HH functions up to grand angular momentum G=20 for J^pi=1/2^+ and G=21 for J^pi=1/2^-.
- The UIX three-nucleon interaction used with N3LO-Idaho has its spin-isospin independent part rescaled by a factor of 0.384 to fit the triton binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the HH variational approach previously applied to A=3 bound states (Kievsky 1993, 1997) and to scattering states with local potentials (Kievsky 1994, 1995) to include non-local two-nucleon potentials (N3LO-Idaho and Vlow-k) and three-nucleon forces (Urbana IX and N2LO) for both n-d and p-d scattering. It enables systematic comparison of local versus non-local interaction models in the three-nucleon scattering regime below breakup, and provides a framework for testing modern chiral effective field theory potentials in few-nucleon scattering.
