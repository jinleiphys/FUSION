# Triangle diagram in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The pion-baryon triangle diagram is inspected for a special kinematic region where the squared momentum transfer $t$ is close to $4m_π^2$: $|t - 4m_π^2| \lesssim m_π^4/m_N^2$. Instead of arguing on the ground of anomalous threshold, we investigate possible impacts on power counting. The pion can have very small energies, as opposed to $\sim m_π$ in the physical region, which allows all three propagators to be extremely near their mass shell and contributes significantly to the loop integral. We find that in this narrow kinematic window the static-limit approximation for the baryon propagator is invalid and the resummation of the kinetic energy is necessary. Interestingly, in contrast to low-energy two-baryon processes, this resummation of baryon recoils does not lead to overall enhancement of power counting of the diagram.

# Triangle diagram in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

In the narrow kinematic window where the squared momentum transfer \(t\) is within \(m_\pi^4/m_N^2\) of \(4m_\pi^2\), the static-limit approximation for the baryon propagator fails and the recoil term \(\vec{k}^2/2m_N\) must be resummed; however, contrary to low-energy two-baryon processes, this resummation does **not** lead to an overall enhancement of the power counting of the triangle diagram, which remains \(\mathcal{O}(m_\pi^{-1})\).

## Method

The authors analyze the pion-baryon triangle diagram in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory by performing the \(k_0\) integral to obtain two residue contributions (\(\gamma_1\) and \(\gamma_2\)), then power-count the three-momentum integration in a small domain around \(\vec{k}^2 = -m_\pi^2\) where all three propagators are nearly on-shell. They evaluate the loop integral numerically with the first recoil term retained in the baryon propagator, using a Feynman parameter and an auxiliary integration to obtain a double-integral representation, and compare it to both the static-limit approximation and the fully relativistic result.

## Key numbers

* The kinematic window where the static limit fails is defined by \(|t - 4m_\pi^2| \lesssim m_\pi^4/m_N^2\), which for physical pion and nucleon masses gives \(|\frac{t}{m_\pi^2} - 4| \lesssim \xi^2 = 0.022\) with \(\xi = m_\pi/m_N = 0.149\).
* In the dome-shaped integration region, the thickness of the spherical shell is \(\Delta k = \mathcal{O}(\xi^2 m_\pi)\) and the solid angle is \(\Omega \sim \mathcal{O}(\pi \xi^2)\), giving an integration volume \(\sim \xi^4 m_\pi^3\).
* The baryon propagator in the dome region scales as \(1/(\xi m_\pi)\), the first pion propagator as \(\xi^{-1} m_\pi^{-1}\), and the second pion propagator as \(\xi^{-2} m_\pi^{-2}\), combining to give \(\gamma_1 \sim m_\pi^{-1}\).
* The static-limit approximation diverges at \(t = 4m_\pi^2\), while the recoil-resummed result \(\gamma^{(0)}\) and the relativistic result \(\gamma_{rel}\) remain finite and agree closely.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Becher (1999), who first examined the pion-baryon triangle diagram and argued that the static-limit approximation fails to reproduce the anomalous threshold expected from covariant diagrams. The authors provide a power-counting rationale for why the recoil term must be resummed in a narrow window around \(t = 4m_\pi^2\), and show that higher-order kinetic terms (e.g., \(\vec{k}^4/8m_N^3\)) remain subleading, so a fully covariant treatment is not essential for power counting. This contrasts with the two-nucleon case studied by Weinberg (1991), where resummation of kinetic energy leads to \(\mathcal{O}(m_N/m_\pi)\) enhancement. The results enable a controlled EFT treatment of processes near the two-pion cut without invoking anomalous thresholds as an input.
