# Partial-Wave Analysis of $γp \rightarrow K^+ Λ$ using a multichannel framework

> Results from a partial-wave analysis of the reaction $γp \rightarrow K^+ Λ$ are presented. The reaction is dominated by the $S_{11}(1650)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$ resonances at low energies and by $P_{13}(1900)$ at higher energies. There are small contributions from all amplitudes up to and including $G_{17}$, with $F_{17}$ necessary for obtaining a good fit of several of the spin observables. We find evidence for $P_{11}$(1880), $D_{13}$(2120), and $D_{15}$(2080) resonances, as well as a possible $F_{17}$ resonance near 2300 MeV, which is expected from quark-model predictions. Some predictions for $γn \to K^0 Λ$ are also included.

## Key claim

The reaction $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda$ is dominated by the $S_{11}(1650)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$ resonances at low energies and by $P_{13}(1900)$ at higher energies, with evidence for $P_{11}(1880)$, $D_{13}(2120)$, and $D_{15}(2080)$ resonances and a possible $F_{17}$ resonance near 2300 MeV; however, the authors note that despite many measured observables, major discrepancies remain between the amplitudes determined by different groups, and more than eight independent observables may be needed to reach a unique solution.

## Method

A multichannel energy-dependent partial-wave analysis was performed on the reaction $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda$ using four helicity amplitudes expanded in terms of electric and magnetic multipoles $E_{l\pm}$ and $M_{l\pm}$. Data were binned into 20-MeV wide bins near threshold and 40-MeV wide bins from 1850 to 2200 MeV center-of-mass energy, then an iterative procedure of single-energy fits followed by multichannel energy-dependent fits was used, with $\chi^2$ penalty terms to constrain the single-energy solutions. Final error bars on single-energy amplitudes were obtained by zero-iteration fits with penalty terms removed.

## Context

This work extends the multichannel partial-wave analysis approach of Shrestha and Manley (2012) to the $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda$ reaction, and compares results with the BnGa 2016 solution and the Julich-Bonn (JuBo) coupled-channel framework of Roenchen (2018). The analysis was carried out in conjunction with other photoproduction and hadronic reactions including $\gamma n$ and $K\Lambda$ states, enabling predictions for $\gamma n \rightarrow K^0 \Lambda$ that show reasonable agreement with CLAS data from Compton (2017) and Akondi (2018). The amplitudes from this work have been included in an updated multichannel energy-dependent partial-wave analysis that also incorporates single-energy amplitudes for $\gamma p \rightarrow \eta p$ and $\gamma n \rightarrow \eta n$, with resonance parameters to be presented in a companion paper.
