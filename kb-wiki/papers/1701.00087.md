# Anomaly induced transport in non-anomalous currents

> Quantum anomalies are one of the subtlest properties of relativistic field theories. They give rise to non-dissipative transport coefficients in the hydrodynamic expansion. In particular a magnetic field can induce an anomalous current via the chiral magnetic effect. In this work we explore the possibility that anomalies can induce a chiral magnetic effect in non-anomalous currents as well. This effect is implemented through an explicit breaking of the symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper claims that anomalies can induce a chiral magnetic effect in non-anomalous currents, but only when the symmetries are explicitly broken by a scalar field deformation; without such symmetry breaking, the non-anomalous current conductivity vanishes identically.

## Method

The author uses the Kubo formalism at zero frequency to compute chiral magnetic conductivities from retarded correlators, and implements a holographic Einstein-Maxwell model in 5 dimensions with a pure gauge Chern-Simons term to mimic the axial anomaly. An explicit breaking of the axial and non-anomalous symmetries is introduced via a scalar field with a tachyonic bulk mass, dual to a CFT operator of dimension 3, and the conductivities are extracted from the near-boundary expansion of fluctuation fields at order O(p_z).

## Key numbers

- The chiral magnetic conductivity for the non-anomalous current sigma_W(M) is zero at M=0: sigma_W(0)=0.
- In the limit of large symmetry breaking, sigma_W(infinity) = sigma_A(infinity) = (1/2) sigma_A(0).
- The numerical results are shown for chemical potentials mu_V=0.4, mu_A=0.5, mu_W=0.6 and temperatures T=1, T=0.5, and T=0.3 (units not specified but consistent with the holographic model).
- The scalar field bulk mass is m^2 = -3, corresponding to operator dimension Delta = 3.

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport coefficients, specifically the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE), which were previously derived for anomalous currents by Kharzeev (2009), Landsteiner (2011), and others using Kubo formulae and holography. The novel result is that explicit symmetry breaking, implemented via a scalar deformation, can generate a chiral magnetic effect in a non-anomalous current, which is not present in the symmetric limit. This opens the possibility of studying similar effects for the chiral vortical conductivity and mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies, as planned in a forthcoming publication by the same author.
