# Heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light weak decay form factors in the light-front approach: the exclusive $ 0^- $ to $ 0^- $ case

> Weak transition form factors among heavy pseudoscalar mesons are investigated within a relativistic quark model formulated on the light-front. It is shown that the light-front result derived in the time-like region for the matrix elements of the plus component of the weak vector current coincides with the spectator pole term of the quark triangle diagram. For the first time, the dependence of the form factors on the squared four-momentum transfer $q^2$ is calculated in the whole accessible kinematical region $0 \leq q^2 \leq q^2_{max}$. For the numerical investigations of the semileptonic $B \to D \ell ν_{\ell}$, $B \to π\ell ν_{\ell}$, $D \to K \ell ν_{\ell}$ and $D \to π\ell ν_{\ell}$, the equal-time wave functions corresponding to the updated version of the ISGW model are adopted. Our results for the form factors and the decay rates are presented and compared with available experimental data and predictions of different approaches. Moreover, the $K^0_L \to π^{\pm} ~ e^{\mp} ~ ν_e$ decay is briefly discussed. Our approach is consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim

The light-front (LF) result for the matrix elements of the plus component of the weak vector current, derived in the time-like region, coincides with the spectator pole term of the quark triangle diagram; the non-partonic contribution from quark pair creation is expected to be negligible for heavy-quark decays but may become more relevant for kaon decays.

## Method

The authors evaluate the quark triangle diagram for the weak vector current in a frame where the momentum transfer is purely longitudinal (appropriate for time-like values of q^2), performing the integration over the light-front energy k^- for the plus component of the current. They then compare this result with the Hamiltonian light-front dynamics approach, showing equivalence to the spectator pole term. For numerical calculations of semileptonic B to D, B to pi, D to K, and D to pi decays, they adopt equal-time wave functions from the updated ISGW2 model (Gaussian ansatz with constituent quark masses and harmonic oscillator lengths from that model) and compute form factors f_+(q^2) and f_-(q^2) across the full kinematical range 0 ≤ q^2 ≤ q^2_max.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter of the form factor h_+ for B to D decay: ρ^2 = 1.1 (obtained from the LF result ρ^2 = 1.3 after subtracting heavy-quark symmetry violating corrections of approximately 0.2)
- Extracted CKM matrix element |V_bc| = 0.036 ± 0.004 from B^0 to D^- decay
- Extracted CKM matrix element |V_bu| = 0.0032 ± 0.0004 from B^0 to pi^- decay
- Ratio |V_bu / V_cb| = 0.088 ± 0.015
- Form factor f_+^{D to K}(0) = 0.78 and f_+^{D to K}(q^2_max) = 1.56
- Extracted CKM matrix element |V_cs| = 0.89 ± 0.03 from D^0 to K^- decay
- Predicted decay rate Γ(D^0 to pi^- e^+ nu_e) = 7.8 × 10^{-3} ps^{-1} assuming |V_cd| = 0.221 ± 0.003
- Ratio of branching ratios R_0 = Br(D to pi l nu_l) / Br(D to K l nu_l) = 7.3%

## Context

This work extends the light-front formalism of Jaus (1991) from space-like momentum transfers to the full time-like region accessible in semileptonic decays, addressing a limitation of prior constituent quark models that calculated form factors at a single q^2 value and extrapolated (e.g., the BSW approach of Wirbel, Stech, and Bauer (1985) used monopole extrapolation from q^2=0; the ISGW model of Isgur, Scora, Grinstein, and Wise (1989) used exponential extrapolation from zero recoil). The approach enables direct calculation of form factors across the entire kinematical range for heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light decays, allowing extraction of CKM matrix elements from experimental decay rates. The results are compared with experimental data from CLEO and E687 collaborations, as well as with predictions from QCD sum rules and lattice QCD calculations.
