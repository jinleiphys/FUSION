# Comment on "New modes of halo excitations in the 6He nucleus"

> We try to explain the differences in the 6He dipole strength function in refs. [1] and [2]. We perform the full basis calculation of the strength function with the same renormalized interaction as in [1] and show that the size of the basis, needed for converged calculations of the 6He continuum spectrum, is much larger than that for the discrete spectrum. The renormalized interaction of [1] therefore cannot be used for the continuum spectrum calculations with the same basis as for the ground state.

## Key claim

The dipole strength function for 6He calculated by Danilin et al. (1997) using a renormalized n-alpha interaction with a limited hyperspherical basis (K ≤ 6) differs from the authors' own calculation because the renormalized interaction, while adequate for the bound ground state, is not appropriate for continuum spectrum calculations with the same basis size.

## Method

The authors performed a full basis calculation of the 6He dipole strength function using the same renormalized n-alpha interaction as Danilin et al. (1997), but with a much larger hyperspherical harmonics basis. They solved the Faddeev equations rather than the Schrodinger equation, computed eigenvalues of the angular part of the Faddeev operator as functions of the hyper-radius, and used analytic large-distance solutions of the Faddeev equations for short-range potentials to achieve fully converged calculations.

## Key numbers

- The converged calculation of the 6He continuum spectrum required a basis with maximum hyperspherical quantum number K_max ≈ 100, compared to K ≤ 6 used by Danilin et al. (1997) for the ground state
- The lowest effective potential for the J^π = 1^- continuum state, when converged, has a minimum value of about 0.15 MeV and a low barrier of 0.3 MeV extending into the asymptotic region beginning at about 25 fm
- With K_max = 9, the minimum and maximum values of the effective potential are 0.7 MeV and 1.4 MeV, respectively
- The convergence scale for the angular eigenvalue toward asymptotic behavior is around 20 fm

## Context

This comment explains the discrepancy between the dipole strength function calculations for 6He by Danilin et al. (1997) and by Cobis et al. (1997), both using a three-body nn-alpha model. The work extends the earlier Faddeev approach of Fedorov et al. (1994) and the analytic large-distance solutions of Jensen et al. (1997). It demonstrates that continuum spectrum calculations of halo nuclei require much larger basis sizes than bound state calculations, and that renormalization techniques compensating for limited bases in bound states become ambiguous when applied to continuum spectra.
