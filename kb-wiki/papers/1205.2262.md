# Beyond-mean-field study of the possible "bubble" structure of 34Si

> Recent self-consistent mean-field calculations predict a substantial depletion of the proton density in the interior of 34Si. In the present study, we investigate how correlations beyond the mean field modify this finding. The framework of the calculation is a particle-number and angular-momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov+Lipkin-Nogami states with axial quadrupole deformation. The parametrization SLy4 of the Skyrme energy density functional is used together with a density-dependent pairing energy functional. For the first time, the generator coordinate method is applied to the calculation of charge and transition densities. The impact of pairing correlations, symmetry restorations and shape mixing on the density profile is analyzed step by step. All these effects significantly alter the radial density profile, and tend to bring it closer to a Fermi-type density distribution.

## Key claim

Beyond-mean-field correlations (pairing, symmetry restoration, and shape mixing) significantly reduce the central depletion of the proton density in 34Si predicted by mean-field calculations, but do not eliminate it entirely; the charge density of the GCM ground state retains a depletion factor of only 0.09, much smaller than the mean-field value of 0.41.

## Method

The authors performed a particle-number and angular-momentum projected Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov+Lipkin-Nogami (HFB+LN) states with axial quadrupole deformation, using the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional and a density-dependent pairing functional. They successively added correlations: spherical HF, spherical HFB+LN, particle-number projection of the spherical HFB+LN state, and finally configuration mixing of J=0 projected states with different intrinsic quadrupole moments (GCM). For the first time, the GCM was applied to calculate charge and transition densities in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

*   Depletion factor F_max for point-proton density: 0.41 (HF), 0.32 (HFB+LN), 0.36 (particle-number projected HFB+LN), 0.30 (J=0 projected state from prolate minimum), and 0.21 (GCM 0+ ground state).
*   Depletion factor F_max for the charge density of the GCM 0+ ground state: 0.09.
*   Root-mean-square radius of the point-proton density: 3.127 fm (spherical HF), 3.133 fm (particle-number projected spherical HFB+LN), and 3.180 fm (GCM 0+ ground state).
*   Calculated B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) = 60.5 e^2 fm^4, compared to the experimental value of 16.6 e^2 fm^4 (Rotaru 2011).
*   Calculated electric monopole strength rho^2(E0; 0+2 -> 0+1) = 58.1 x 10^{-3}, compared to the experimental value of 13.0 (0.9) x 10^{-3} (Rotaru 2011).

## Context

This work extends mean-field predictions of a proton bubble in 34Si (Khan 2008, Grasso 2009) by including correlations that were previously neglected, such as pairing and shape fluctuations. It contradicts the earlier assumption that the bubble structure is robust, showing that correlations bring the density closer to a Fermi-type distribution. The results enable future experimental searches for the bubble signature via elastic electron scattering at facilities like SCRIT or ELISe, and provide a benchmark for beyond-mean-field calculations of charge densities in exotic nuclei.
