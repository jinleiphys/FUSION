# Quantum phase transitions in Bose-Fermi systems

> Quantum phase transitions in a system of N bosons with angular momentum L=0,2 (s,d) and a single fermion with angular momentum j are investigated both classically and quantum mechanically. It is shown that the presence of the odd fermion strongly influences the location and nature of the phase transition, especially the critical value of the control parameter at which the phase transition occurs. Experimental evidence for the U(5)-SU(3) (spherical to axially-deformed) transition in odd-even nuclei is presented.

# Quantum phase transitions in Bose-Fermi systems

## Key claim

The presence of a single odd fermion strongly influences the location and nature of quantum phase transitions in Bose-Fermi systems, particularly shifting the critical value of the control parameter at which the phase transition occurs; however, this effect depends on which single-particle state the fermion occupies, with some states washing out the transition and others enhancing it, and the critical point shifts are modest (e.g., from ξ~0.51 in the bosonic case to ξ~0.58 for state 6 in the U(5)-SU(3) transition).

## Method

The authors study the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) for a system of N s,d bosons coupled to a single fermion with angular momentum j=11/2, using both classical and quantum mechanical analyses. Classically, they construct the total energy surface by taking expectation values of the Hamiltonian in a boson condensate and diagonalizing the resulting fermion single-particle matrix, then minimize the total energy with respect to the deformation parameters β and γ to find equilibrium values as functions of the control parameter ξ. Quantum mechanically, they perform numerical diagonalization of the full Hamiltonian to study the correspondence with the classical results.

## Key numbers

- For the U(5)-SU(3) transition with Λ=0 and N=10, the critical point for the purely bosonic case is ξ_B^c ~ 0.51; for the odd system, state 4 has ξ_4^c ~ 0.50, state 5 has ξ_5^c ~ 0.53, and state 6 has ξ_6^c ~ 0.58.
- The single-particle eigenvalues of the quadrupole term are periodic in γ with period 2π/3, while the exchange term eigenvalues are periodic in γ with period π/3.
- The equilibrium deformation β_e,B for the bosonic part is calculated for N=10 bosons as a function of ξ for both χ = -√7/2 (SU(3)) and χ = 0 (SO(6)).
- The quadrupole coupling strength is parameterized as Γ₀ = -2ε₀(ξ/4N), and the exchange coupling as Λ = λε₀, with the specific combination Λ/Γ = 3 used for display in combined eigenvalue figures.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quantum phase transitions in even-even nuclei within the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) by Gilmore (1979), Feng (1981), and Iachello and Scholten (1980) to odd-even nuclei using the IBFM framework. It builds on explicit studies by Alonso et al. (2005, 2007) and B\"oy\"ukata et al. (2010), who suggested a simple form of the IBFM Hamiltonian with supersymmetric properties. The single-particle levels in a deformed β,γ field are compared to the earlier collective model results of Meyer-ter-Vehn (1979) and the Nilsson model (1955). The work enables experimental tests of quantum phase transitions in odd-proton nuclei such as Pm, Eu, and Tb, particularly in the unique parity h_{11/2} levels.
