# Nucleon and Delta in a covariant quark-diquark model

> We develop a formally covariant quark-diquark model of the nucleon. The nucleon is treated as a bound state of a constituent quark and a diquark interacting via a quark exchange. We include both scalar and axial-vector diquarks. The underlying Bethe-Salpeter equation is transformed into a pair of coupled Salpeter equations. The space- and timelike electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated in the Mandelstam formalism for squared momentum transfers up to 3 (GeV/c)^2 above threshold. The model is applied also to the Delta(3/2).

## Key claim

The paper develops a formally covariant quark-diquark model of the nucleon and Delta, but the abstract's claim of calculating form factors "up to 3 (GeV/c)^2 above threshold" is qualified by the finding that the threshold condition for the Sachs form factors is violated in the model due to the choice of the axial-vector diquark propagator.

## Method

The nucleon is treated as a bound state of a constituent quark and a diquark (scalar and axial-vector) interacting via an instantaneous quark exchange, with the Bethe-Salpeter equation transformed into a pair of coupled Salpeter equations. The model uses a Ritz variational principle to obtain bound state solutions for positive parity spin-1/2 amplitudes, and electromagnetic transition currents are calculated using the Mandelstam formalism. For the Delta(3/2), the model treats it as a bound state of a quark and an axial-vector diquark only, with a separate coupling parameter.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: 440 MeV; scalar and axial-vector diquark masses: 800 MeV (both sets)
- Quark-diquark coupling parameter for nucleon: g^N = 17.76; for Delta: g^Delta = 8.50
- Diquark extension parameter: lambda = 0.30 fm
- Proton charge radius: 0.79 fm (both sets); experimental: 0.847 fm
- Neutron charge radius squared: -0.110 fm^2 (both sets); experimental: -0.113 fm^2
- Proton magnetic moment: 2.45 mu_N (Set A), 2.44 mu_N (Set B); experimental: 2.793 mu_N
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.42 mu_N (Set A), -1.91 mu_N (Set B); experimental: -1.913 mu_N
- Proton magnetic radius: 0.72 fm (Set A), 0.74 fm (Set B); experimental: 0.836 fm
- Neutron magnetic radius: 0.86 fm (Set A), 0.75 fm (Set B); experimental: 0.889 fm
- E2/M1 ratio at threshold: -3.4% (Set B)
- Form factors calculated for squared momentum transfers up to 3 GeV^2 above threshold

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-diquark models based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Ishii, Huang, Reinhardt) and the Salpeter equation framework (Salpeter, Meyer) by including both scalar and axial-vector diquarks in a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach. The model enables calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in both space- and timelike regions, and N-Delta transition form factors including the E2/M1 and C2/M1 ratios, though the Delta magnetic form factor comes out a factor of 2 too low, indicating the need for further refinements such as including scalar to axial-vector diquark transitions.
