# Binary and ternary ionic compounds in the outer crust of a cold nonaccreting neutron star

> The outer crust of a cold nonaccreting neutron star has been generally assumed to be stratified into different layers, each of which consists of a pure body-centered cubic ionic crystal in a charge compensating background of highly degenerate electrons. The validity of this assumption is examined by analyzing the stability of multinary ionic compounds in dense stellar matter. It is thus shown that their stability against phase separation is uniquely determined by their structure and their composition irrespective of the stellar conditions. However, equilibrium with respect to weak and strong nuclear processes imposes very stringent constraints on the composition of multinary compounds, and thereby on their formation. By examining different cubic and noncubic lattices, it is found that substitutional compounds having the same structure as cesium chloride are the most likely to exist in the outer crust of a nonaccreting neutron star. The presence of ternary compounds is also investigated. Very accurate analytical expressions are obtained for the threshold pressure, as well as for the densities of the different phases irrespective of the degree of relativity of the electron gas. Finally, numerical calculations of the ground-state structure and of the equation of state of the outer crust of a cold nonaccreting neutron star are carried out using recent experimental and microscopic nuclear mass tables.

## Key claim

The paper shows that multinary ionic compounds in the outer crust of a cold nonaccreting neutron star are stable against phase separation solely based on their structure and composition, independent of stellar conditions; however, equilibrium with respect to weak and strong nuclear processes imposes very stringent constraints on their composition, making their formation unlikely except for binary compounds with cesium chloride structure at interfaces between adjacent crustal layers.

## Method

The authors analyzed the thermodynamic stability of a pure body-centered cubic (bcc) solid phase against transition to a multi-component solid phase by comparing Gibbs free energies per nucleon at zero temperature, expanding to first order in the fine-structure constant. They derived analytical expressions for the threshold pressure, densities, and density discontinuities at phase transitions, applicable for arbitrary electron relativity, and applied these to binary and ternary compounds with various cubic and noncubic lattices. Numerical calculations of the ground-state structure and equation of state of the outer crust were then carried out using recent experimental and microscopic nuclear mass tables.

## Key numbers

- The threshold electron Fermi energy for a transition between pure solid phases is given by \(\mu_e^{1\rightarrow2} \equiv [M'(A_2,Z_2)c^2/A_2 - M'(A_1,Z_1)c^2/A_1] (Z_1/A_1 - Z_2/A_2)^{-1} + m_e c^2\).
- The structure constant for bcc lattice is \(C_{\rm bcc}\), with a lower bound \(C_{\rm min} \equiv -9/10 (4\pi/3)^{1/3} \simeq -1.450793\) from the ion-sphere model.
- The electron Fermi temperature is approximately \(T_{{\rm F}e} \approx 5.93 \times 10^9 (\mu_e^{\rm thres}/m_e c^2)~{\rm K}\) for ultrarelativistic electrons.
- The crystallization temperature is approximately \(T_m \approx 1.29 \times 10^5 (\mu_e^{\rm thres}/m_e c^2) \overline{Z^{5/3}}~{\rm K}\) using \(\Gamma_m = 175\).
- The neutron-drip density is \(\rho_{\rm drip} \simeq 4 \times 10^{11}~{\rm g~cm}^{-3}\).
- The density at which atoms become fully ionized is \(\rho_{\rm eip} \simeq 11 AZ~{\rm g~cm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Dyson (1971) of FeHe compounds with rocksalt structure in neutron star crusts and the subsequent analysis by Jog and Smith (1982) showing such compounds are unstable against weak and strong nuclear processes, while binary compounds with CsCl structure could be energetically favored at interfaces. The paper generalizes the stability condition originally derived by Dyson (1971) for binary compounds under the approximation \(P \approx P_e\) to arbitrary multinary compounds including lattice pressure contributions, and provides analytical formulas for threshold pressures and densities applicable to any electron relativity regime. This enables future calculations of the outer crust ground-state structure allowing for ionic compounds, using modern nuclear mass tables.
