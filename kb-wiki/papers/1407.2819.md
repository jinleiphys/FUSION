# Beam Energy Dependence of Clan Multiplicity at RHIC

> In this paper, STAR's measurement of clan multiplicity is presented for AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV, for a variety of centrality classes. The mean number of particles per clan is found to decrease with decreasing centrality. Within the same centrality class, the mean number of particles per clan exhibits a reduction between 19.6 GeV and 62 GeV, with the minimum around 27 GeV. The structure is visible for most centralities, and most prominent for central collisions.

## Key claim

The mean number of charged particles per clan exhibits a reduction between 19.6 GeV and 62 GeV, with a minimum around 27 GeV, but this structure is not seen in peripheral collisions and becomes prominent only in central collisions; the apparent enhancement at 62 GeV may be affected by worse beam conditions at that particular energy and requires further investigation.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured charged-particle multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies, using particles in the pseudorapidity range |η| < 0.5 while defining centrality with particles at |η| > 0.5 to avoid autocorrelation. For each fine bin of reference multiplicity, the multiplicity distribution was fitted with a Negative Binomial Distribution, from which the variance and mean were extracted and transformed into the average number of clans and the average number of particles per clan. Protons were removed from the analysis because they are contaminated by transported protons, especially at low energies.

## Key numbers

- Average number of particles per clan shows a minimum around √s_NN = 27 GeV for central collisions, with a reduction observed between 19.6 GeV and 62 GeV.
- Data were taken at √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, with total event counts of 5, 7, 40, 71, 56, 73, and 25 million events respectively.
- The structure in mean particles per clan is most prominent for the top 5% most central collisions, and is not seen in peripheral collisions (50%-70% centrality).
- Assuming a 30% detecting efficiency with 5% year-to-year variation, the resulting variation in mean particles per clan is too small (comparable to symbol size in the figure) to explain the observed energy dependence.

## Context

This work extends the clan concept developed by Giovanni (1986) to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, applying the Negative Binomial Distribution parameterization previously used by UA5, EMC, NA22, E802, NA35, and PHENIX to search for phase transition signatures. The observed energy dependence of the mean number of particles per clan, with a minimum near 27 GeV, motivates further studies into whether this feature is connected to a phase transition in nuclear matter. The analysis uses data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and from 2010 runs at higher energies.
