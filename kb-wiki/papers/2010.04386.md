# Spectroscopic properties of $Δ$ Baryons

> The resonance state of $Δ$ baryon existing in four isospin ($I=\frac{3}{2}$) states, has been studied using Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) with a simple linear potential with added first order correction. The calculated data range for 1S-5S, 1P-5P, 1D-4D and 1F-2F with possible spin-parity assignments of all the states. The magnetic moments have also been obtained for all four configuration. The $Nπ$ decay channel width has been calculated for few states. The linear nature of the data has been verified through Regge trajectories.

# Spectroscopic properties of Δ Baryons

## Key claim

The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) with a simple linear potential plus a first-order correction term reproduces the known mass spectrum of Δ baryons for low-lying states, but systematically underpredicts higher-spin orbital excitations (e.g., 1P(1700) with J^P=3/2^- is underpredicted by 97 MeV from the lower PDG range, and the 1F 7/2^- state is 113 MeV below the PDG lower limit).

## Method

The authors solve the six-dimensional Schrödinger equation for a three-quark system using Jacobi coordinates and a hypercentral potential consisting of a Coulomb-like term (−τ/x), a linear confining term (αx), and a first-order correction term proportional to 1/(m x^2). Spin-dependent interactions (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) are added to lift degeneracies. The numerical solution is performed with Mathematica, using constituent quark masses m_u = m_d = 0.290 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state Δ(1232) mass: 1232 MeV (PDG range 1230–1234 MeV)
- 2S (J^P=3/2^+) mass: 1611 MeV (PDG range 1500–1640 MeV)
- 1P (J^P=1/2^-) mass: 1609 MeV (PDG range 1590–1630 MeV)
- 1P (J^P=3/2^-) mass: 1593 MeV (PDG range 1690–1730 MeV; underpredicted by 97 MeV)
- 1D (J^P=1/2^+) mass: 1905 MeV (PDG range 1850–1950 MeV)
- 1F (J^P=7/2^-) mass: 2037 MeV (PDG range 2150–2250 MeV; underpredicted by 113 MeV)
- Δ^{++} magnetic moment: 4.568 μ_N (experimental PDG value: 4.52 μ_N)
- Δ^{+} magnetic moment: 2.28 μ_N (experimental PDG value: 2.7 μ_N)
- Δ(1600) → Nπ decay width: 24.8% (PDG range 8–24%)
- Δ(1620) → Nπ decay width: 92% (PDG range 25–35%; overpredicted)
- Δ(1700) → Nπ decay width: 14.83% (PDG range 10–20%)

## Context

This work extends the hCQM approach previously applied to N* spectroscopy by Shah et al. (2019) to the Δ baryon decuplet, which has isospin I=3/2 and symmetric flavor wavefunctions distinct from the mixed-symmetry nucleon wavefunctions. The results are compared with the Isgur-Karl model (1978), the relativized quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986), and lattice QCD calculations by Edwards et al. (2011). The predicted masses for many unobserved higher radial and orbital states (up to 5S, 5P, 4D, 2F) provide targets for upcoming experimental facilities such as PANDA at GSI. The systematic underprediction of higher-spin states indicates the need for improved treatment of spin-dependent interactions or relativistic effects in the model.
