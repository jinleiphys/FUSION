# Structure of the $\mathbf{Λ(1670)}$ resonance

> We examine the internal structure of the $Λ(1670)$ through an analysis of lattice QCD simulations and experimental data within Hamiltonian effective field theory. Two scenarios are presented. The first describes the $Λ(1670)$ as a bare three-quark basis state, which mixes with the $πΣ$, $\bar{K}N$, $ηΛ$ and $KΞ$ meson-baryon channels. In the second scenario, the $Λ(1670)$ is dynamically generated from these isospin-0 coupled channels. The $K^-p$ scattering data and the pole structures of the $Λ(1405)$ and the $Λ(1670)$ can be simultaneously described well in both scenarios. However, a comparison of the finite-volume spectra to lattice QCD calculations reveals significant differences between these scenarios, with a clear preference for the first case. Thus the lattice QCD results play a crucial role in allowing us to distinguish between these two scenarios for the internal structure of the $Λ(1670)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while both a "bare three-quark" and a "dynamically generated" description of the Λ(1670) resonance fit experimental K⁻p scattering data equally well, lattice QCD finite-volume spectra strongly favor the interpretation where the Λ(1670) is a bare three-quark basis state mixing with meson-baryon channels, rather than a purely dynamically generated resonance.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) framework with coupled channels (πΣ, K̄N, ηΛ, KΞ for isospin 0; plus πΛ, ηΣ for isospin 1) and fit its parameters to K⁻p scattering cross sections for laboratory momenta from 0 to 800 MeV/c, including recent near-threshold data. They then used the fitted Hamiltonians to compute finite-volume energy spectra and compared these predictions to lattice QCD results from the BaSc collaboration at a pion mass of approximately 200 MeV, as well as to earlier lattice data.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1670) pole position in the bare-baryon scenario is 1674⁺²₋₄ − 11⁻⁴₊₃ i MeV; in the dynamically generated scenario it is 1676 − 17 i MeV.
- The two Λ(1405) poles are found at 1324⁻⁴₋₄ − 67⁺⁸₊₁₀ i MeV and 1428⁺⁴₊₂ − 24⁺⁴₊₀ i MeV in the bare-baryon fit.
- The bare baryon mass parameter is m_B⁰ = 1750⁺³⁹₋₂₉ MeV.
- The regulator parameter Λ is fixed at 1.0 GeV with an allowed variation of ±0.1 GeV.
- The K⁻p scattering data cover laboratory momenta up to 800 MeV/c, including the near-threshold cross sections measured in 2022.

## Context

This work extends earlier HEFT analyses of the Λ(1405) by the CSSM group (Hall 2014, Hall 2016, Liu 2016) and the study of two-pole structure by Jido (2003) and Oller (2000). It directly confronts the recent coupled-channel lattice QCD simulations from the BaSc collaboration (2023) at m_π ≈ 200 MeV. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations at physical pion masses to definitively resolve the internal structure of the Λ(1670) and test the quark-model picture of baryon excitations proposed by Veit (1984).
