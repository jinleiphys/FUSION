# Jet-medium interaction and conformal relativistic fluid dynamics

> A formalism to study the mode-by-mode response to the energy deposition of external hard partons propagating in a relativistic fluid is developed, based on a semi-analytical solution of conformal fluid-dynamics. The soft particle production resulting from the jet-medium interaction is calculated and the recoil of the viscous medium is studied for different orientations of the relativistic jets, and for different values of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$.

## Key claim

The paper develops a semi-analytical formalism for studying the mode-by-mode hydrodynamic response of a conformal relativistic fluid to energy deposition from hard partons (jets), and finds that the dominant viscous suppression of the resulting particle spectra comes from a "dynamical" reduction of the jet energy-loss rate in a more viscous medium, rather than from the viscous damping of the hydrodynamic modes themselves.

## Method

The authors use the Gubser-Yarom solution for conformal viscous hydrodynamics, which incorporates longitudinal and radial expansion with rotational symmetry, to linearize the equations of motion for perturbations induced by a light-like jet parton. The source term for the jet-medium interaction is taken as \(J^\mu = \hat e v^\mu_{\rm jet} n_{\rm jet}\), with a \(T^2\)-dependent energy loss rate \(\hat e = \kappa T^2\). The linearized hydrodynamic equations are solved mode-by-mode via spherical harmonic decomposition in the de Sitter coordinates, and the resulting medium response is particlized using the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at \(\tau_f = 6\) fm/c to compute pion spectra.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity values considered are \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and \(2/4\pi\).
- The background medium parameters are \(\bar T_0 = 7.3\) and \(1/q = 4.3\) fm, for ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The jet partons start from \(\vec x^{\rm jet}_{\perp} = (1.0, 0)\) at \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm/c.
- The mode summation converges up to \(l < 35\) for the viscous fluid cases considered.
- For the strongly coupled system estimate, a constant value of \(\kappa = 20\pi/3\) is used.
- The freeze-out temperature at \(\tau = 6\) fm/c is \(T \lesssim 130\) MeV.
- A lower cut of \(p_T \ge 1\) GeV is applied to the pion spectra.

## Context

This work extends the semi-analytical approach of Gubser and Yarom (2010) for conformal fluid dynamics to include jet-medium interactions, building on earlier studies of Mach cone formation in static media by Casalderrey-Solana (2004) and Chesler (2007). It provides a controlled framework to separate "dynamical" viscous suppression (from reduced jet energy loss at higher \(\eta/s\)) from "hydro" viscous suppression (from damping of hydrodynamic modes), enabling a potential new measure of medium transport coefficients from jet substructure observables. The results are compared with full numerical hydro simulations in a companion work by Singh (2014).
