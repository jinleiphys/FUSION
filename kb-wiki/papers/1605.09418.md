# Initial state fluctuations in collisions between light and heavy ions

> In high energy collisions involving small nuclei (p+p or x+Au collisions where x=p, d, or $^3$He) the fluctuating size, shape and internal gluonic structure of the nucleon is shown to have a strong effect on the initial size and shape of the fireball of new matter created in the collision. A systematic study of the eccentricity coefficients describing this initial fireball state for several semi-realistic models of nucleon substructure and for several practically relevant collision systems involving small nuclei is presented. The key importance of multiplicity fluctuations in such systems is pointed out. Our results show large differences from expectations based on conventional Glauber model simulations of the initial state created in such collisions.

## Key claim

The fluctuating size, shape, and internal gluonic structure of the nucleon have a strong effect on the initial size and shape of the fireball created in collisions involving small nuclei (p+p, p+Au, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au), with the authors showing that sub-nucleonic fluctuations produce large differences from expectations based on conventional Glauber model simulations, though the effects from quark subdivision and from fluctuating gluon field substructure are characteristically different.

## Method

The authors implemented a Monte Carlo Glauber model with three levels of quantum fluctuations: fluctuating nucleon positions within nuclei, quark subdivision of nucleons (with three valence quarks carrying Gaussian gluon clouds of width $\sigma_g$), and gluon field fluctuations based on the Color Glass Condensate picture. They computed the eccentricity coefficients $\epsilon_2$ and $\epsilon_3$ from the initial entropy density profiles for p+p, p+Au, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, incorporating multiplicity fluctuations via Gamma-distributed weights per wounded nucleon (or per valence quark) to reproduce measured p+p multiplicity distributions.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the nucleon width parameter $\sqrt{B}=0.408$ fm; for LHC collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV, $\sqrt{B}=0.516$ fm.
- The gluon cloud width $\sigma_g$ and valence quark position width $\sigma_q$ satisfy the constraint $\sigma_g^2 + \frac{2}{3}\sigma_q^2 = B$, with $\sigma_g$ values explored in the paper.
- The Gamma distribution shape parameter for nucleon multiplicity fluctuations is $k=1/\theta$, with scale parameter $\theta=0.61$ for RHIC at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and $\theta=0.75$ for LHC at $\sqrt{s}=5020$ GeV.
- For quark subdivision, the shape parameter per valence quark is $k_q = k_n/3$ with the same scale parameter $\theta_q = \theta_n$.
- The average transverse correlation length for gluon field fluctuations is $\bar{a} \sim 1/\bar{Q}_s$, of order 0.3 fm for top RHIC energies and 0.2 fm for top LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Monte Carlo Glauber model (Miller 2007) by incorporating sub-nucleonic degrees of freedom, building on the JIMWLK-based picture of gluon lumps around valence quarks from Schlichting (2014) and the Color Glass Condensate fluctuation approach from Muller (2011) and Moreland (2012). The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations to test whether the linear relation between $\epsilon_n$ and $v_n$ observed in large nuclei holds for small collision systems, and provide a framework for interpreting the surprising flow-like features observed in p+Pb collisions at the LHC (CMS 2013) and in p+p, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions at RHIC (Adare 2014).
