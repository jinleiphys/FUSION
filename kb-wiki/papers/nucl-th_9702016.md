# Properties of Hadrons in the Nuclear Medium

> This review is devoted to the discussion of hadron properties in the nuclear medium and its relation to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. Special attention is given to disentangle in-medium effects due to conventional many-body interactions from those due to the change of the chiral condensate. In particular, we shall discuss medium effects on the Goldstone bosons (pion, kaon and eta), the vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), and the nucleon. Also, for each proposed in-medium effect the experimental consequence and results will be reviewed.

## Key claim

This review argues that hadron properties in the nuclear medium are modified by both conventional many-body interactions and changes in the chiral condensate, and that disentangling these two sources of modification is an unsolved question in nuclear many-body physics. The authors emphasize that while some in-medium effects (such as the softened pion dispersion relation from p-wave interactions) arise from conventional many-body physics, others (such as kaon mass shifts) are directly linked to chiral symmetry and its partial restoration.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches including chiral perturbation theory, the delta-hole model for pion-nucleus interactions, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for kaon-nucleon interactions, QCD sum rules, and transport theory for heavy-ion collisions. They compare these theoretical predictions with experimental data from pionic atoms, kaonic atoms, charge-exchange reactions, subthreshold particle production measurements, and dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions at BEVALAC, SIS, AGS, and SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density, the quark condensate is estimated to be reduced by about 30% relative to vacuum, based on chiral perturbation theory calculations by Drukarev and Levin (1990), Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992), and others
- The pion mass shift at nuclear matter density is approximately 8%, derived from pionic atom measurements of the isoscalar s-wave optical potential parameter (b0)eff = -0.024 m_pi^-1
- The isoscalar s-wave pi-N scattering length is b0 = -0.010(3) m_pi^-1 and the isovector s-wave scattering length is b1 = -0.091(2) m_pi^-1
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term is between 370 and 405 MeV, depending on the strangeness content of the nucleon (y approximately 0.1-0.2)
- The K^- optical potential changes sign from repulsive to attractive at a density of about 1/4 of nuclear matter density, based on calculations by Koch (1994) and confirmed by Waas, Weise, and Kaiser (1996)
- The Migdal parameter g' is approximately 0.6, accounting for short-range correlations in the pion self-energy
- The pion-nucleon-delta coupling constant f_pi N Delta is approximately 2
- The form factor range parameter b is approximately 7 m_pi
- The delta resonance mass is 1230 MeV and the rho meson mass is 770 MeV

## Context

This review extends earlier work on in-medium hadron properties by Brown and Rho (1991), who argued that masses of nonstrange hadrons would scale with the quark condensate, and by Gale and Kapusta (1987), who first studied the effect of softened pion dispersion on dilepton yields. The review synthesizes results from chiral perturbation theory (Drukarev and Levin 1990; Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel 1992), the delta-hole model (Ericson and Weise 1988; Friman, Pirner, and Urban 1981), and transport theory (Bertsch and Das Gupta 1988; Cassing, Mosel, and collaborators 1990; Aichelin and collaborators 1991). The work enables systematic comparison of in-medium effects across different hadron species and experimental observables, and identifies open questions about the relationship between conventional many-body effects and chiral symmetry restoration.
