# Quark-Hadron Phase Transition with Finite-Size Effects in Neutron Stars

> We study the quark-hadron phase transition with the finite-size effects in neutron stars. The finite-size effects should be, generally, taken into account in the phase transition of multi-component system. The behavior of the phase transition, however, strongly depends on the models for quark and hadron matter, surface tension, neutrino fraction, and temperature. We find that, if the surface tension is strong, the EOS becomes similar to the case of a Maxwell construction for any hadron and/or quark model, though we adopt the Gibbs conditions. We also find that the mass-radius relations for that EOS are consistent with the observations, and our model is then applicable to realistic astrophysical phenomena such as the thermal evolution of compact stars.

## Key claim
The paper claims that when surface tension is strong (40 MeV fm\(^{-2}\)), the equation of state for the quark-hadron mixed phase in neutron stars becomes similar to that of a Maxwell construction, even though the Gibbs conditions are adopted. This behavior is stated to be general across hadron and quark models, but the result depends on the specific choices for surface tension, neutrino fraction, temperature, and the models for quark and hadron matter.

## Method
The authors adopt the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory with the Bonn B potential for hadronic matter and a non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector coupling (ratio of vector to scalar channel couplings \(\eta_V = G_V / G_S = 0.20\)) for quark matter. They assume a sharp boundary between quark and hadron phases and compute the mixed phase including finite-size effects (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble structures) for two values of the surface tension parameter: \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and \(\sigma = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\).

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star masses: \(M_{\text{max}} = 2.47\,M_\odot\) for \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and \(M_{\text{max}} = 2.49\,M_\odot\) for \(\sigma = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\), both with \(\eta_V = 0.20\).
- Onset density of the mixed phase: 0.67 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and 0.70 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\sigma = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) (with \(\eta_V = 0.20\)).
- End density of the mixed phase: 1.00 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\sigma = 10\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and 0.97 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\sigma = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) (with \(\eta_V = 0.20\)).
- Neutron star radii in the models: 12–14 km.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the quark-hadron phase transition in neutron stars by incorporating finite-size effects (inhomogeneous structures) within a Gibbs construction, building on earlier work by the same group using a simple bag model (Yasutake et al., previous studies). The results are consistent with observational mass constraints from Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013). The authors note that hyperons are not considered because quark appearance likely prevents their formation, and they plan to investigate different quark models and include hyperons in future work.
