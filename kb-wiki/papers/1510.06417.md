# Static Response of Neutron Matter

> We generalize the problem of strongly interacting neutron matter by adding a periodic external modulation. This allows us to study from first principles a neutron system that is extended and inhomogeneous, with connections to the physics of both neutron-star crusts and neutron-rich nuclei. We carry out fully non-perturbative microscopic Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the energy of neutron matter at different densities, as well as different strengths and periodicities of the external potential. In order to remove systematic errors, we examine finite-size effects and the impact of the wave function ansatz. We also make contact with energy-density functional theories of nuclei and disentangle isovector gradient contributions from bulk properties. Finally, we calculate the static density-density linear response function of neutron matter and compare it with the response of other physical systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the static response of neutron matter can be computed from first principles using Quantum Monte Carlo methods, and that this response provides a new constraint for nuclear energy-density functionals (EDFs). However, the abstract glosses over the key finding that the difference between Quantum Monte Carlo and EDF results in inhomogeneous neutron systems does not come solely from the isovector gradient term, but also from a bulk/homogeneous matter energy mismatch.

## Method

The authors used Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Auxiliary-Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) to calculate the ground-state energy of neutron matter under a periodic external sinusoidal potential. They employed the Argonne v8' two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, with trial wave functions consisting of a Jastrow factor multiplied by a Slater determinant of single-particle orbitals (plane-wave or optimized Mathieu functions). The static density-density response function was extracted by fitting the energy as a function of potential strength for several periodicities, using the expansion E_tot/N = E_0/N + chi(q) v_q^2 / n_0 + C_4 v_q^4 + ...

## Key numbers

* Energy per particle of neutron matter calculated at densities from 0.02 to 0.12 fm^{-3} using NN interactions and AFDMC (Fig. 1)
* One-body potential strengths used: 2v_q = 0.25, 0.30, 0.35, 0.50, 0.75 times the Fermi energy E_F
* At density n = 0.10 fm^{-3}, the difference between plane-wave and Mathieu orbital results was up to approximately 1 MeV; the beta parameter optimization effect was approximately 50 keV
* The isovector gradient coefficient C^{Delta n}_1 was modified from -16 to -28 MeV fm^5 to match AFDMC results at the lowest strength
* Periodicities studied: q = 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20 times pi/L, corresponding to 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10 periods of the potential
* Finite-size corrections were checked with up to approximately 100,000 particles for the non-interacting Fermi gas
* SLy4 thermodynamic-limit values were reproduced to less than 0.5% accuracy

## Context

This work extends ab initio studies of neutron matter from homogeneous systems (Friedman 1981, Akmal 1998, Gezerlis 2008) to inhomogeneous systems with periodic modulation, connecting to neutron-star crust physics and neutron-rich nuclei. The static response function provides a new constraint for Skyrme energy-density functionals (SLy4 from SLy, 1998) beyond the homogeneous equation of state, and the authors disentangle isovector gradient contributions from bulk properties. This enables improved descriptions of neutron-star crusts and nuclei, and opens the door to ab initio calculations of neutron pasta (Pethick 1995) and comparisons with other strongly correlated systems like liquid helium-4 (Moroni 1992) and the electron gas (Moroni 1995).
