# Theoretical modeling of approximate universality of tidally deformed neutron stars

> Quasi-universal relations are known to exist among various neutron star observables that do not depend sensitively on the underlying nuclear matter equations of state. For example, some of these relations imply that the tidally induced multipole moments are approximately characterized by the electric-type quadrupolar tidal deformability. Such relations can be used to reduce the number of independent tidal parameters in gravitational-waveform modeling, thereby allowing us to infer extreme nuclear matter properties more accurately and test General Relativity in an insensitive manner to uncertainties in nuclear physics. We present a comprehensive theoretical investigation into approximate universality of neutron stars. Our approach employs a semi-analytic relativistic stellar interior model, which extends the Tolman VII solution, thereby enabling a refined exploration of the tidal properties of nonrotating stars within a semi-analytic framework. The derived power-law relations among various tidal deformabilities -- referred to as the universal Love relations -- agree well with expressions in previous work found empirically. We elucidate how the equation-of-state dependence is suppressed in a particular combination of macroscopic physical parameters induced by perturbations and demonstrate that the relation between the moment of inertia and electric-type quadrupolar tidal deformability (I-Love relation) rests on the same underlying mechanism. Our findings indicate that the approximate universality of neutron stars can be attributed to low compressibility, consistent with some of the previous studies on the possible origin of the universality.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the approximate universality of neutron star tidal deformabilities (the "Love relations") can be attributed to low compressibility of nuclear matter, with the EoS dependence being suppressed in particular combinations of macroscopic physical parameters induced by perturbations, consistent with the incompressible limit acting as a stationary point.

## Method
The authors employ a semi-analytic relativistic stellar interior model called the modified Tolman VII solution, which extends the original Tolman VII solution by introducing a parameter alpha that controls the internal relation between energy density and pressure. They solve the tidal perturbation equations within a post-Minkowskian expansion (expansion in terms of compactness C) to derive analytic expressions for the electric-type and magnetic-type tidal deformabilities, then re-sum these using Pade approximants to improve accuracy. The power-law relations among tidal deformabilities are obtained by fixing alpha=1 (the original Tolman VII solution) and expressing compactness as a series in the inverse of the quadrupolar tidal deformability.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the leading coefficient C0 for the bar-lambda_2 - bar-sigma_2 relation at alpha=0 versus alpha=1.4 is approximately 0.96, indicating only 4% deviation across the alpha range
- The ratio of the leading coefficient C0 for the bar-lambda_2 - bar-lambda_3 relation at alpha=0 versus alpha=1.4 is approximately 0.92, indicating 8% deviation across the alpha range
- The deviation of the bar-lambda_2 - bar-sigma_2 relation in the modified Tolman VII model (4%) is much smaller than the 11% deviation found between polytropes with polytropic index n between 0 and 1 (Yagi 2013)
- The deviation of the bar-lambda_2 - bar-lambda_3 relation in the modified Tolman VII model (8%) is much smaller than the 20% deviation found between polytropes with n between 0 and 1 (Yagi 2013)
- The first-order coefficient in the Taylor expansion of C0 for the bar-lambda_2 - bar-sigma_2 relation around alpha=1 is 0.028 times the zeroth-order coefficient (2.8% correction)
- The first-order coefficient in the Taylor expansion of C0 for the bar-lambda_2 - bar-lambda_3 relation around alpha=1 is 0.066 times the zeroth-order coefficient (6.6% correction)

## Context
This work extends the empirical universal Love relations discovered by Yagi (2013) by providing a semi-analytic derivation using the modified Tolman VII model developed by Jiang (2019) and Posada (2022). The paper demonstrates that the I-Love relation (moment of inertia versus tidal deformability) rests on the same suppression mechanism as the universal Love relations. The findings support previous studies suggesting the incompressible limit as a stationary point (Sham 2014) and the approximate self-similarity of isodensity contours (Yagi 2014) as origins of universality. This enables improved gravitational-waveform modeling by reducing the number of independent tidal parameters, and allows for strong-field tests of gravity that are insensitive to uncertainties in nuclear physics.
