# Dynamical Coupled-Channels Model Analysis of π-N Scattering and Electromagnetic Pion Production Reactions

> The ability of the coupled-channels model (MSL) developed in recently in Ref. \cite{msl} to account simultaneously for the $πN$ scattering data and the $π$ photoproduction reactions on the nucleon is presented. An accurate description of $πN$ scattering has been obtained. A preliminary description of $π$ photoproduction is also discussed.

## Key claim

The MSL dynamical coupled-channels model can simultaneously describe both pi-N scattering data and pi photoproduction reactions on the nucleon, but the photoproduction results are preliminary and are being further improved.

## Method

The model starts from a set of Lagrangians describing interactions between mesons (including the photon) and baryons, then applies a unitary transformation method to derive an energy-independent effective Hamiltonian. Coupled-channels equations for pi-N and gamma-N reactions are derived using the projection operator technique, satisfying two-body (pi-N, eta-N, gamma-N) and three-body (pi-pi-N) unitarity conditions, with pi-Delta, rho-N, and sigma-N resonant components of the pi-pi-N continuum generated dynamically. Non-resonant parameters are fixed from pi-N scattering data, then bare gamma-N to N* vertex interactions are determined from photoproduction data.

## Key numbers

- The model describes pi-N amplitudes in S, P, D, and F partial waves compared to the SAID energy-independent extraction
- Differential cross sections and target polarization asymmetry are compared with data for elastic pi+ p -> pi+ p, pi- p -> pi- p, and charge-exchange pi- p -> pi0 n processes
- Photoproduction results focus on the region up to 1.6 GeV, extending previous works that only studied the Delta(1232) region
- Differential cross section for gamma p -> pi+ n is shown at theta = 90 degrees as a function of center-of-mass energy W
- Angular distributions for both pi+ n and pi0 p photoproduction are presented in the Delta(1232) region

## Context

This work extends the MSL coupled-channels model developed in Ref. msl to simultaneously describe pi-N scattering and photoproduction, building on earlier meson-exchange models of pion-nucleon scattering reviewed by Lee. The model enables extraction of nucleon resonance parameters from the extensive data from Jefferson Laboratory, Mainz, Bonn, GRAAL, and Spring-8. Future developments include simultaneous studies of meson-baryon, single meson electroproduction, and two-meson photoproduction, as well as inclusion of omega-N and kaon-hyperon channels.
