# Possible Assignment of Excited Light $^3S_1$ Vector Mesons

> We reanalyze the problems in the assignment of 3$^3S_1$ and 4$^3S_1$ light mesons, which have not yet been well established with the $q\bar{q}$ quark model. Regge trajectories and the $^3P_0$ decay model are used respectively to study the mass and width of the observed states and predict the missing ones. By comparing our calculations with the latest experiments, we suggest that the inconsistent data of $ρ(2150)$ may include two similar structures $ρ(4^3S_1)$ and $ω(4^3S_1)$. In addition, the problem of the $K^*(2^3S_1)$ assignment, with two observed states $K^*(1410)$ and $K^*(1680)$, is investigated, with several possible explanations.

# Possible Assignment of Excited Light \(^3S_1\) Vector Mesons

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the inconsistent experimental data for \(\rho(2150)\) may actually contain two similar structures, \(\rho(4^3S_1)\) and \(\omega(4^3S_1)\), rather than a single resonance, and that the problematic assignment of \(K^*(1410)\) as the \(2^3S_1\) kaon state may be resolved if the observed \(K^*(1680)\) structure consists of two separate \(1^{--}\) states (\(K^*(2^3S_1)\) and \(K^*(1^3D_1)\)), though this remains a speculative possibility without direct experimental evidence.

## Method

The authors used Regge trajectories to predict the masses of excited \(^3S_1\) vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\), and \(K^*\)) based on well-established lower states, and applied the \(^3P_0\) quark-pair-creation model to calculate OZI-allowed two-body strong decay widths for candidate states. The simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wavefunction was used with a scale parameter \(\beta = 0.23\) GeV for mother mesons (determined by fitting to the widths of \(2^3S_1\) mesons \(\rho(1450)\), \(\omega(1420)\), and \(\phi(1680)\)) and varying \(\beta\) values for lower-excitation daughter mesons following Close (2005). The theoretical total and partial widths were compared with experimental data from BaBar, BESIII, LASS, LHCb, and the PDG.

## Key numbers

- \(\rho(1900)\) as \(3^3S_1\): theoretical total width = 184 MeV; experimental width = 160 ± 20 MeV (BaBar 2006)
- \(\rho(2150)\) as \(4^3S_1\): theoretical total width = 116 MeV; experimental width = 127 ± 14 ± 4 MeV (BaBar 2019)
- \(\omega(1960)\) as \(3^3S_1\): theoretical total width = 217 MeV; experimental width = 195 ± 60 MeV (SPEC)
- \(\omega(2205)\) as \(4^3S_1\) candidate: theoretical total width = 141 MeV; experimental width = 350 ± 90 MeV (SPEC 2002)
- \(\omega(2290)\) as \(4^3S_1\) candidate: theoretical total width = 138 MeV; experimental width = 275 ± 35 MeV
- \(\phi(2170)\) as \(3^3S_1\): theoretical total width = 410 MeV; experimental width = 125 ± 65 MeV (PDG average)
- \(\phi(2050)\) as predicted \(3^3S_1\) (using slope 1.5 GeV²): theoretical total width = 277 MeV
- \(\phi(2400)\) as predicted \(4^3S_1\): theoretical total width = 241 MeV
- \(K^*(1410)\): experimental mass = 1414 ± 15 MeV, width = 232 ± 21 MeV; the \(^3P_0\) model with \(2^3S_1\) assignment or mixing with \(1^3D_1\) cannot reproduce the experimental width within 2σ
- \(K^*(1680)\): experimental mass = 1718 ± 18 MeV, width = 322 ± 110 MeV; a hypothesized \(K^*_\text{Low}\) at 1680 MeV as a mixture of \(2^3S_1\) and \(1^3D_1\) gives a total width consistent with experiment at mixing angle \(\theta = 45^\circ\)
- BESIII observed a structure in \(e^+e^- \to K^+K^-\) with mass = 2239.2 ± 7.1 ± 11.3 MeV and width = 139.8 ± 12.3 ± 20.6 MeV
- BESIII observed \(Y(2040)\) in \(\omega\pi^0\) with mass = 2034 ± 13 ± 9 MeV and width = 234 ± 30 ± 25 MeV
- BESIII observed a resonance in \(e^+e^- \to \eta'\pi\pi\) with mass = 2108 ± 46 ± 25 MeV and width = 138 ± 36 ± 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous spectroscopic studies of light vector mesons, addressing the poorly established \(3^3S_1\) and \(4^3S_1\) assignments that have been problematic in the quark model. It builds on Regge trajectory analyses by Anisovich (2000) and Masjuan (2012), and on \(^3P_0\) model calculations by Barnes (2002), Close (2005), and others. The paper specifically addresses inconsistencies in the \(\rho(2150)\) region highlighted by recent BESIII and BaBar measurements, and the long-standing ambiguity in kaon assignments from LASS (1987) and LHCb (2017). The results enable future partial-wave analyses to distinguish overlapping \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) structures around 2.1–2.2 GeV, and motivate searches for the predicted \(\phi(2400)\) and mixed \(K^*\) states.
