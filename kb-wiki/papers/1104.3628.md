# The study of fusion barriers of neutron-rich colliding nuclei using various isospin-dependent potentials

> A detailed study fusion of neutron-rich colliding nuclei is performed using various isospin dependent potentials. For present study, Three different series namely, Ne-Ne, Ca-Ca, and Zr-Zr are taken into account and N/Z ratio.A monotonous increase (decrease) in the fusion barrier positions (heights) using a unified second order nonlinear parametrization in the normalized fusion barrier positions and heights with (N/Z-1) is presented. These predications are in good agreement with the available theoretical as well as experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper claims a monotonous increase (decrease) in fusion barrier positions (heights) with neutron excess, described by a unified second-order nonlinear parametrization in the normalized barrier quantities as a function of (N/Z-1), but this pattern is derived from eight proximity-type models and is stated to be independent of the specific colliding series and model, with the caveat that some models (Bass 80, Ngô 80) show slight scattering for very neutron-rich and neutron-deficient nuclei.

## Method

The author calculated total ion-ion interaction potentials (nuclear plus Coulomb) for 150 symmetric collisions across three isotopic series (Ne-Ne, Ca-Ca, Zr-Zr) covering N/Z ratios from 0.5 to 2.0, using eight isospin-dependent proximity-type potentials (Prox 77, Prox 88, Prox 00, Prox 00N, Bass 80, AW 95, Ngô 80, and Denisov N). Fusion barrier heights V_B and positions R_B were extracted from the total potential by solving the conditions dV_T/dr = 0 and d^2V_T/dr^2 ≤ 0 at the barrier. Normalized percentage deviations ΔR_B(%) and ΔV_B(%) relative to the N=Z symmetric case were then fitted as quadratic functions of the asymmetry parameter A_s = (N/Z-1).

## Key numbers

- The N/Z ratio domain studied spans from 0.5 to 2.0 across all three series, corresponding to asymmetry parameter A_s = (N/Z-1) ranging from -0.5 to 1.0.
- For the Ne-Ne series, the barrier position R_B for ¹⁶Ne+¹⁶Ne ranges from 7.15 fm (Prox 77) to 7.89 fm (Denisov N), while for ³⁰Ne+³⁰Ne it ranges from 9.00 fm (Prox 00) to 10.07 fm (Denisov N).
- For the Ca-Ca series, the barrier height V_B for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca ranges from 54.87 MeV (Bass 80) to 57.73 MeV (Ngô 80), while for ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca it ranges from 47.41 MeV (Denisov N) to 50.59 MeV (Prox 00).
- For the Zr-Zr series, the barrier height V_B for ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr ranges from 181.55 MeV (Bass 80) to 191.55 MeV (Ngô 80), while for ¹²⁰Zr+¹²⁰Zr it ranges from 163.06 MeV (Bass 80) to 171.40 MeV (Prox 00).
- The surface energy coefficient γ in the original proximity potential (Prox 77) uses γ₀ = 0.9517 MeV/fm² and k_s = 1.7826; the Prox 88 version uses γ₀ = 1.2496 MeV/fm² and k_s = 2.3.
- All proximity-type potentials reproduce experimental fusion barriers within ±10% on average.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Puri et al. (year not given in text) which analyzed only Ca and Ni series, by adding Ne-Ne and Zr-Zr series to cover a wider mass range and N/Z ratios from 0.5 to 2.0. The study uses eight proximity-type potentials including the original Prox 77 (Blocki et al., 1977), Prox 88 (Möller and Nix), Prox 00, and modified versions (Prox 00N, Denisov N) that incorporate isospin dependence in radii and surface energy coefficients. The results enable systematic predictions of fusion barriers for neutron-rich and neutron-deficient nuclei far from stability, which is relevant for planning experiments with radioactive-ion beams and for testing the validity of isospin-dependent nuclear interaction models.
