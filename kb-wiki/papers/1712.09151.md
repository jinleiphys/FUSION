# Interplay between isoscalar and isovector correlations in neutron-rich nuclei

> The interplay between isoscalar and isovector correlations in the 1$^{-}$ states in neutron-rich (N$\neq$Z) even-even nuclei is studied, taking examples of the nuclei, $^{22}_{8}$O$_{14}$ and $^{24}_{8}$O$_{16}$. The excitation modes explored are isovector dipole and isoscalar compression dipole modes. The self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus the random-phase approximation with the Skyrme interaction, SLy4, is solved in coordinate space so as to take properly into account the continuum effect. The isovector peak induced by isoscalar correlation, the isoscalar peak induced by isovector correlation, and the possible collective states made by both isoscalar and isovector correlations, ("iS-iV pigmy resonance"), are shown. The strong neutron-proton interaction in nuclei can be responsible for controlling the isospin structure of normal modes. It is explicitly shown that in the scattering by isoscalar (isovector) particles on N$\neq$Z even-even nuclei isovector (isoscalar) strength in addition to isoscalar (isovector) strength may be populated.

# Interplay between isoscalar and isovector correlations in neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

In neutron-rich (N≠Z) even-even nuclei, isoscalar (IS) and isovector (IV) correlations can mutually induce strength in the opposite channel, and a new collective "iS-iV pigmy resonance" can appear at energies much lower than both the isovector dipole giant resonance and the isoscalar compression dipole giant resonance, provided that the ISCD and IVD peaks occur in the same energy region. The claim is conditional on the specific choice of nuclei (²²O and ²⁴O) where weakly-bound low-ℓ neutron orbits and the strong r-dependence of the ISCD operator produce large low-lying ISCD strength.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the Skyrme interaction SLy4, solved in coordinate space to properly include continuum effects. They computed the RPA strength functions for both the isovector dipole (IVD) operator (τz r Y₁μ) and the isoscalar compression dipole (ISCD) operator (r³ Y₁μ) in the spherical neutron-rich nuclei ²²O and ²⁴O, comparing results with only IS interaction, only IV interaction, and both interactions included simultaneously. The spurious center-of-mass component was removed by using modified operators: for IVD, the operator (2N/A)Σ_proton r Y₁μ + (2Z/A)Σ_neutron r Y₁μ, and for ISCD, the operator Σ (r³ − η r) Y₁μ with η = (5/3)⟨r²⟩.

## Key numbers

- In ²²O, the calculated neutron separation energy S_n = 7.25 MeV (experimental: 6.85 MeV) and proton separation energy S_p = 22.29 MeV (experimental: 23.26 MeV).
- In ²⁴O, the calculated neutron separation energy S_n = 4.88 MeV (experimental: 4.19 MeV) and proton separation energy S_p = 24.62 MeV (experimental: 27.11 MeV).
- The nuclear matter incompressibility from SLy4 is K₀ = 229.9 MeV.
- In ²²O, the IV dipole strength in the region Ex = 11–16.5 MeV consumes 11.4 percent of the energy-weighted sum-rule (EWSR) and 16.6 percent of the non-energy-weighted sum-rule (NEWSR).
- In ²²O, the ISCD strength in the same energy region (Ex = 11–16.5 MeV) consumes 18.0 percent of EWSR and 25.9 percent of NEWSR.
- In ¹⁶O (used as a benchmark), the model gives ISCD strength of about 2 percent of the ISCD EWSR as a sum of two bound RPA solutions at 10.4 and 10.8 MeV, compared to experimental reports of 4 percent (from α scattering) or about twice that (from electron scattering) for the 1⁻ T=0 state at 7.12 MeV.
- In ²⁴O, a huge calculated ISCD peak appears at 5.8 MeV with a width of a few MeV, whose main component is the neutron (p-h) = (2p₃/₂ 2s₁/₂⁻¹) configuration.
- In ²²O, the unperturbed neutron (1f₇/₂, 1d₅/₂⁻¹) configuration lies at 12.2 MeV with width 1.22 MeV, and the neutron (1d₃/₂, 1p₁/₂⁻¹) configuration lies at 16.8 MeV with width 83 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1997) that in ⁴⁸Ca, a sharp IV peak appears at the energy of the IS quadrupole giant resonance when both IS and IV interactions are included, whereas no such IV strength is found with only IS interaction. The present paper systematically studies the reverse phenomenon (IS peaks induced by IV correlations) and the new "iS-iV pigmy resonance" in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. The choice of ²²O and ²⁴O was stimulated by the photoneutron cross section measurements on ²²O reported by Leistenschneider et al. (2001) and the observation of bound IS and IV dipole excitations in ²⁰O reported by Nakatsuka et al. (2017). The results enable experimental searches for the iS-iV pigmy resonance using scattering of isoscalar particles (such as α particles) or isovector particles on neutron-rich nuclei, where the strong neutron-proton interaction controls the isospin structure of normal modes and may populate strength in the opposite isospin channel.
