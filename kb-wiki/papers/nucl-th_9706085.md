# Charm Quark Production in Non-central Heavy Ion Collisions

> The effect of gluon shadowing on charm quark production in large impact parameter ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is investigated. Charm production cross sections are calculated for a range of non-central impact parameters which can be accurately inferred from the global transverse energy distribution. We show that charm production is a good probe of the local parton density which determines the effectiveness of shadowing. The spatial dependence of shadowing can only be studied in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Charm quark production in non-central heavy ion collisions is sensitive to the spatial dependence of gluon shadowing, but only if the impact parameter can be accurately inferred from the transverse energy distribution; the paper shows that this method is feasible at RHIC energies despite the need to correct for the altered relationship between transverse energy and impact parameter when charm events are selected at low transverse energy.

## Method

The authors calculated leading-order charm quark pair production cross sections in 100 GeV per nucleon Au+Au collisions at RHIC, using two different nuclear shadowing parameterizations (a fit to deep-inelastic scattering data called S1, and a QCD-evolved parameterization called S2) and two models of the spatial dependence of shadowing (one following the Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution and one following the nuclear thickness). They related impact parameter to transverse energy using a Gaussian approximation that convolves hard (minijet) and soft components, and computed charm production rates and transverse momentum spectra for impact parameters greater than the nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

- Without shadowing, for impact parameter b > 1.2 R_A (R_A = 6.38 fm for gold), the charm cross section is 2.9 b; for b > 1.8 R_A, it is 200 mb.
- At the RHIC Au+Au design luminosity of 2 x 10^26 cm^-2 sec^-1, this yields 6300 and 430 million c-cbar pairs per year (3000 hours) for the two impact parameter ranges respectively.
- The NLO total c-cbar production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is approximately 350 microbarns for both parton density sets used.
- At transverse energy E_T = 200 GeV, the impact parameter distribution is centered at b = 1.27 R_A with a standard deviation of about 0.05 R_A, and approximately 90% of events fall in the range 1.15 R_A < b < 1.35 R_A.
- The hard minijet cross section at RHIC is sigma_H^pp(p_0) approximately 6.5 mb, with sigma_H^pp(p_0) times the mean transverse energy equal to 17 mb GeV in the rapidity interval |y| <= 0.5.
- For b > 1.2 R_A, both spatial forms of shadowing increase the cross section about 15% over the spatially-averaged shadowing result S1.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of charm production in central heavy ion collisions (GMRV, no year given) to non-central impact parameters, proposing a method to probe the spatial dependence of gluon shadowing that cannot be studied in proton-nucleus collisions. The approach relies on the relationship between transverse energy and impact parameter developed in earlier work (eskola2, no year given) and uses two shadowing parameterizations: a fit to nuclear deep-inelastic scattering data (EQC, no year given) and a QCD-evolved parameterization (KJE, no year given). The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and the LHC to map out parton localization within the nucleus by measuring charm production as a function of impact parameter.
