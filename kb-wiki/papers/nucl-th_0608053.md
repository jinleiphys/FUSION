# K0 photoproduction on the deuteron and the extraction of the elementary amplitude

> The photoproduction of K0 on the deuteron has been investigated with the inclusion of YN and KN final-state interaction (FSI), as well as the pion-mediated process gamma d --> pi NN --> KYN. The YN rescattering effects for the inclusive cross section are found to be large in the threshold regions. Polarization observables show sizable FSI effects at larger kaon and hyperon angles. It is shown that the extraction of the elementary gamma N --> KY amplitude is possible in the quasi-free scattering region where FSI effects are negligible. Furthermore, the cross sections in this region are large, indicating that measurements in this kinematical region are favored.

## Key claim

The elementary amplitude for the $\gamma N \rightarrow KY$ process can be extracted from the deuteron cross section in the quasi-free scattering region where final-state interaction (FSI) effects are negligible, but this extraction is only demonstrated for the $\gamma d \rightarrow K^0 \Lambda p$ channel and relies on the condition that FSI effects are small enough to be ignored.

## Method

The authors calculated $K^0$ photoproduction on the deuteron using a production operator that includes the impulse approximation (IA), hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) rescattering solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the Nijmegen $YN$ potential, kaon-nucleon ($KN$) rescattering using a rank-1 separable potential, and the pion-mediated process $\gamma d \rightarrow \pi NN \rightarrow KYN$. The elementary photoproduction operator was taken from the KAON-MAID isobar model, and the deuteron wave function was generated from the Nijmegen93 $NN$ potential. Cross sections and polarization observables were computed for inclusive and exclusive channels at photon energies of 1.1 and 1.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section peaks at $p_K \approx 690$ MeV/$c$ for the $\Lambda$ quasi-free scattering region at $E_\gamma = 1.1$ GeV and $\theta_K = 0^\circ$
- $YN$ rescattering effects are sizable in the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ threshold regions and on top of the $\Lambda$-peak around $p_K = 690$ MeV/$c$
- Exclusive cross sections calculated at $E_\gamma = 1.1$ GeV, $\theta_K = 0^\circ$, $\phi_Y = 0^\circ$ for two kaon momentum values (690 and 590 MeV/$c$ for $\Lambda$ channel; 520 and 420 MeV/$c$ for $\Sigma^0$ channel)
- Tensor asymmetries $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$, and $T_{22}$ show FSI effects at larger kaon angles for $E_\gamma = 1.1$ GeV
- Extraction of elementary amplitude demonstrated at $E_\gamma = 1.1$ and 1.3 GeV with spectator nucleon momenta of 0, 50, 100, and 150 MeV/$c$

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Yamamura et al. (1999) on $K^+$ channels to $K^0$ photoproduction, adding $KN$ rescattering and the $\pi N-KY$ process that were included in Salam et al. (2003). It addresses the need for neutron-channel kaon photoproduction data, which cannot be obtained from free neutron targets, by using the deuteron as an effective neutron target. The results enable future experiments at LNS Sendai and Jefferson Lab to extract elementary $\gamma n \rightarrow K^0 \Lambda$ amplitudes from deuteron measurements, provided data are taken in the quasi-free scattering region where FSI effects are negligible.
