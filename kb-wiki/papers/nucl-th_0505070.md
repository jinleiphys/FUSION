# Nuclear Electromagnetic Current in the Relativistic Approach with the Momentum-Dependent Selfenergies

> We define the new description of the electromagnetic current to hold the current conservation in the momentum-dependent Dirac fields from the Ward Takahashi identity. To describe the momentum-dependence we solve the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation by using the one-pion exchange. In addition we discuss on contribution from the one-pion exchange current and the core polarization. It is shown that this current can reduce the convection current in the isovector case, whose value has been too big due to the small effective mass in the usual relativistic Hartree approximation.

## Key claim

The paper defines a new electromagnetic current operator for momentum-dependent Dirac fields that satisfies current conservation via the Ward-Takahashi identity, and shows that this current reduces the isovector convection current that had been too large in the usual relativistic Hartree approximation due to the small effective mass.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation using one-pion exchange to generate momentum-dependent selfenergies, while treating the sigma- and omega-meson exchanges at the relativistic Hartree (RH) level. They derive the vertex correction from the Ward-Takahashi identity in the zero-momentum-transfer limit, obtaining a current proportional to the derivative of the single-particle energy with respect to momentum. The core polarization current is calculated separately for the Hartree and Fock contributions, and the total static current is evaluated for a system with one valence nucleon on the Fermi surface of saturated nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-dependent selfenergies vary by only 2.5% at most below the Fermi level.
- The Landau mass at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}) is M*_L/M = 0.85 with the momentum-dependent calculation (parameter set PF1), compared to M*_L/M = 0.74 in the momentum-independent calculation (PM1).
- The isovector current density is 10% less than the normal current density at about rho_B ≈ rho_0/4 (low density region).
- The Landau mass from PF1 agrees with the bare mass at rho_B ≈ 0.5 rho_0.
- The binding energy used for parameter fitting is BE = 16 MeV, with incompressibility K = 200 MeV and effective mass M*/M = 0.7 at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Maruyama, 2004) showing that one-pion exchange creates momentum dependence that enhances Fermi velocity and explains the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance energy. It addresses the problem of too-large isovector magnetic moments in relativistic approaches, which had been noted in earlier work (Magmom, 1990; Nishi, 1990). The result that the isovector current is suppressed by about 10% in low-density regions is consistent with experimental observations that isovector magnetic moments are 10% smaller than the Schmidt value. The paper contrasts its pseudo-vector coupling approach with the pseudo-scalar coupling used by Bentz (2000), which gave opposite sign for the momentum-dependent vector selfenergy.
