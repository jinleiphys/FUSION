# Electron capture rates on nuclei and implications for stellar core collapse

> Supernova simulations to date have assumed that during core collapse electron captures occur dominantly on free protons, while captures on heavy nuclei are Pauli-blocked and are ignored. We have calculated rates for electron capture on nuclei with mass numbers A=65-112 for the temperatures and densities appropriate for core collapse. We find that these rates are large enough so that, in contrast to previous assumptions, electron capture on nuclei dominates over capture on free protons. This leads to significant changes in core collapse simulations.

## Key claim
The authors calculate electron capture rates on nuclei with mass numbers A=65-112 and find that, contrary to the previous assumption that captures on heavy nuclei are Pauli-blocked for neutron numbers N>=40, these rates are large enough that electron capture on nuclei dominates over capture on free protons throughout core collapse. The Pauli-blocking is overcome by nuclear correlations and temperature effects, with the caveat that the rates become insensitive to detailed strength distribution at densities above about 10^11 g cm^-3.

## Method
The authors performed Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in the full pf-sdg shell using a residual pairing+quadrupole interaction to determine temperature-dependent occupation numbers of single-particle orbitals. These occupation numbers were then used as input in Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations of the capture rates, considering allowed and forbidden transitions up to multipoles J=4 including momentum dependence. The rates were combined with a Saha-like nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) calculation to obtain the composition of approximately 200 nuclei with A=45-112, and the resulting reaction rates were implemented in self-consistent neutrino radiation hydrodynamics simulations.

## Key numbers
- Electron capture on nuclei dominates over capture on free protons by roughly an order of magnitude throughout collapse when composition is considered.
- At densities above about 10^11 g cm^-3, the rates become well represented by an average Q-value dependence with B=4.6 and ΔE=2.5 MeV.
- Neutrinos from captures on nuclei have a mean energy 40-60% less than those produced by capture on protons.
- The velocity minimum, marking the eventual location of shock formation, is displaced by 0.1 solar masses when using the new rates compared to the Bruenn parameterization.
- The calculations cover nuclei with mass numbers A=65-112 at temperatures T>=0.8 MeV and densities appropriate for core collapse.
- At (ρY_e=7×10^9 g cm^-3, T=0.93 MeV), the rates show scatter around the mean Q-dependence.
- At ρ_11 Y_e >= 4, the rates become virtually independent of the strength distribution.

## Context
This work extends the improved weak interaction rates for presupernova models by Langanke, Martinez-Pinedo et al. (LMP) for nuclei with A=45-65 to heavier nuclei up to A=112, and contradicts the earlier assumption by Bruenn (1985) and Fuller (1982) that electron capture on nuclei with N>=40 is Pauli-blocked due to the independent particle model. The new rates enable more realistic core collapse simulations, as demonstrated by the Oak Ridge and Garching collaborations (Hix 2003, Janka 2003), and show that the many neutron-rich nuclei dominating the composition throughout collapse also dominate the electron capture rate, with strong impact on the collapse trajectory and core properties at bounce.
