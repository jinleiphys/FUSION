# Hydrodynamic evolution and jet energy loss in Cu+Cu collisions

> We present results from a hybrid description of Cu+Cu collisions using 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics (MUSIC) for the bulk evolution and a Monte-Carlo simulation (MARTINI) for the evolution of high momentum partons in the hydrodynamical background. We explore the limits of this description by going to small system sizes and determine the dependence on different fractions of wounded nucleon and binary collisions scaling of the initial energy density. We find that Cu+Cu collisions are well described by the hybrid description at least up to 20% central collisions.

## Key claim
The hybrid model (MUSIC hydrodynamics + MARTINI jet evolution) describes Cu+Cu collisions well for centralities up to 20%, but begins to fail for more peripheral collisions, indicating that the combination of ideal hydrodynamics and the McGill-AMY energy loss mechanism reaches its limits of applicability in smaller systems.

## Method
The soft (low-pT) sector of the collision was modeled using 3+1D ideal relativistic hydrodynamics (MUSIC) with a lattice QCD-based equation of state (s95p-v1), initialized with a Glauber model that mixes wounded nucleon and binary collision scaling. The hard (high-pT) sector was simulated with the Monte-Carlo code MARTINI, which evolves high-momentum partons through the hydrodynamic background using the McGill-AMY formalism for radiative and elastic energy loss, with hadronization performed by PYTHIA. The initial energy density distribution was varied by changing the fraction alpha of binary collision scaling (0.1, 0.5, or 1.0) while keeping the freeze-out energy density fixed at 0.14 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for Cu+Cu collisions
- Initial time for hydrodynamics: tau_0 = 0.55 fm/c for all parameter sets
- Initial energy density: 20 GeV/fm^3 (alpha=0.1), 26 GeV/fm^3 (alpha=0.5), 29 GeV/fm^3 (alpha=1.0)
- Freeze-out energy density: 0.14 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to T_FO approximately 140 MeV
- Strong coupling constant in MARTINI: alpha_s = 0.32
- For central Cu+Cu collisions, hydrodynamics alone describes spectra up to pT approximately 2 GeV, compared to 3-4 GeV for central Au+Au collisions
- For 20-30% central collisions, hydrodynamics begins to deviate from data at approximately pT greater than 1.5 GeV
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for pions agrees with data for 0-10% and 10-20% central bins, but deviates for 20-30% and 30-40% centralities
- Average impact parameters range from 1.61 fm (0-5% centrality) to 6.4 fm (30-40% centrality)

## Context
This work extends the hybrid MUSIC+MARTINI framework previously applied to Au+Au collisions (Schenke, 2010) to the smaller Cu+Cu system, testing the limits of both ideal hydrodynamics and the McGill-AMY energy loss formalism. The study finds that wounded nucleon scaling (alpha=0.1) best describes the bulk pseudorapidity distributions across all centralities, consistent with earlier SPS findings, while binary collision scaling (alpha=1) better matches the nuclear modification factor. The results enable future studies incorporating viscous hydrodynamics and event-by-event fluctuations (Schenke, 2010) to improve the description of peripheral collisions and small systems.
