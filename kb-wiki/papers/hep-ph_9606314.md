# Nucleon Parton Distributions at Low Normalization Point in the Large N_c Limit

> At large N_c the nucleon can be viewed as a soliton of the effective chiral lagrangian. This picture of nucleons allows a consistent nonperturbative calculation of the leading-twist parton distributions at a low normalization point. We derive general formulae for the polarized and unpolarized distributions (singlet and non-singlet) in the chiral quark-soliton model. The consistency of our approach is demonstrated by checking the baryon number, isospin and total momentum sum rules, as well as the Bjorken sum rule. We present numerical estimates of the quark and antiquark distributions and find reasonable agreement with parametrizations of the data at a low normalization point. In particular, we obtain a sizeable fraction of antiquarks, in agreement with the phenomenological analysis.

## Key claim

At large $N_c$, the nucleon can be treated as a classical soliton of the effective chiral Lagrangian, enabling a consistent nonperturbative calculation of leading-twist parton distributions at a low normalization point around 600 MeV, with the caveat that gluon distributions are neglected because they vanish at leading order in the instanton packing fraction.

## Method

The authors compute quark and antiquark parton distributions in the chiral quark-soliton model, derived from the effective chiral theory obtained by integrating out quarks in a background pion field. They evaluate the light-cone quark correlation functions in the nucleon rest frame using the mean-field approximation justified by large \(N_c\), summing over occupied and non-occupied eigenstates of the Dirac Hamiltonian in the self-consistent hedgehog pion field. The nucleon states are obtained by collective quantization of rotational zero modes, and the distributions are regularized using the Pauli-Villars method to preserve anticommutativity and positivity.

## Key numbers

- The ultraviolet cutoff of the effective chiral theory is \(\Lambda \approx 600\) MeV, identified with the inverse average instanton size.
- The packing fraction of instantons is \(f = \bar\rho^4 N/V \ll 1\), with the relation \(M^2/\Lambda^2 \sim f \ll 1\).
- In the large \(N_c\) limit, the singlet unpolarized distribution scales as \(\sum_f q_f(x) = N_c^2 \rho(N_c x)\), where the function \(\rho(y)\) is stable as \(N_c \to \infty\).
- The baryon number sum rule is satisfied: \(\int_{-1}^1 dx [u(x)+d(x)] = N_c B\), with \(B=1\) for the nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model developed by Diakonov, Petrov, and Pobylitsa (1988) for baryon properties to the calculation of parton distributions, contrasting with the Skyrme model of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) where quark and antiquark distributions are not clearly defined. The approach provides initial conditions at a low normalization point for perturbative QCD evolution, enabling comparison with phenomenological parametrizations such as those by Glück, Reya, and Vogt (1995); the results show reasonable agreement with data and yield a sizeable antiquark fraction, in contrast to bag models which violate general sum rules due to incomplete treatment of the surface.
