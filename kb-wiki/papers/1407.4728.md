# Nonmesonic weak decay spectra of light hypernuclei

> The nonmesonic weak decay spectra of light hypernuclei have been evaluated in a systematic way. As theoretical framework we adopt the independent particle shell model with three different one-meson-exchange transition potentials. Good agreement with data is obtained for proton and neutron kinetic energy spectra of $^4_Λ$He, and $^5_Λ$He, when the recoil effect is considered. The coincidence spectra of proton-neutron pairs are also accounted for quite reasonably, but it was not possible to reproduce the data for the neutron-neutron pair spectra. It is suggested that the $π+K$ meson-exchange model with soft monopole form factors could be a good starting point for describing the dynamics responsible for the decays of these two hypernuclei. The $^4_Λ$H~ spectra are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the soft π+K meson-exchange model with soft monopole form factors (SPKE) provides a good starting point for describing the nonmesonic weak decay dynamics of light hypernuclei, but only when the recoil effect is included; the model fails to reproduce neutron-neutron pair coincidence spectra for both ⁴_ΛHe and ⁵_ΛHe.

## Method

The author employs the independent particle shell model (IPSM) with three different one-meson-exchange (OME) transition potentials: the full pseudoscalar-vector (PSVE) potential including π, ρ, ω, η, K, and K* mesons; the π+K meson-exchange (PKE) model with standard cutoffs; and the soft π+K meson-exchange (SPKE) potential with reduced cutoffs. Short-range correlations are incorporated phenomenologically via Jastrow-like functions, and the recoil effect is included in the kinematics. The calculated single and coincidence spectra are compared with experimental data from BNL, KEK, and FINUDA for ⁴_ΛHe, ⁴_ΛH, and ⁵_ΛHe.

## Key numbers

- The size parameter b for the harmonic oscillator wave functions is: b(⁴_ΛH) = 1.57 fm, b(⁴_ΛHe) = 1.53 fm, and b(⁵_ΛHe) = 1.33 fm.
- The maximum proton kinetic energy E^{max}_p (including recoil) is: 0.117 GeV for ⁴_ΛH, 0.110 GeV for ⁴_ΛHe, and 0.115 GeV for ⁵_ΛHe.
- For ⁴_ΛHe, the theoretical SPKE proton transition rate is Γ_p = 0.179 (in units of Γ_W), compared to the BNL bare value of 0.189 ± 0.028.
- For ⁵_ΛHe, the theoretical SPKE proton transition rate is Γ_p = 0.281, compared to the KEK bare value of 0.296 ± 0.020.
- The ⁴_ΛHe spectrum S_{np}(P) for center-of-mass momentum goes up to P_p = 0.546 GeV, with its maximum at P_p^↑ = 0.165 GeV.
- The Λ-N mass difference is Δ = 0.1776 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of ⁴_ΛHe spectra (Barbero et al., 2009) by including ⁴_ΛH and ⁵_ΛHe, and by comparing spectra directly without normalization to experimental data. It contradicts the ability of the Tokyo group's hybrid model (Inoue et al., 1997; Sasaki et al., 2000) to describe neutron-neutron coincidence spectra, and suggests that the four- or five-body nature of these decays should be treated explicitly, as done in ab initio calculations of hypernuclear separation energies (Kuzmin et al., 2002; Filikhin et al., 2002) and four-nucleon scattering (Deltuva et al., 2012). The results enable future experimental measurements of ⁴_ΛH neutron spectra to test the model predictions.
