# Role of Sterile Neutrino Warm Dark Matter in Rhenium and Tritium Beta Decays

> Sterile neutrinos with mass in the range of one to a few keV are important as extensions of the Standard Model of particle physics and are serious dark matter (DM) candidates. This DM mass scale (warm DM) is in agreement with both cosmological and galactic observations. We study the role of a keV sterile neutrino through its mixing with a light active neutrino in Rhenium 187 and Tritium beta decays. We pinpoint the energy spectrum of the beta particle, 0 < T_e < (Q_{beta} - m_s), as the region where a sterile neutrino could be detected and where its mass m_s could be measured. This energy region is at least 1 keV away rom the region suitable to measure the mass of the light active neutrino, located near the endpoint Q_{beta} . The emission of a keV sterile neutrino in a beta decay could show up as a small kink in the spectrum of the emitted beta particle. With this in view, we perform a careful calculation of the Rhenium and Tritium beta spectra and estimate the size of this perturbation by means of the dimensionless ratio R of the sterile neutrino to the active neutrino contributions. We comment on the possibility of searching for sterile neutrino signatures in two experiments which are currently running at present, MARE and KATRIN, focused on the Rhenium 187 and Tritium beta decays respectively.

## Key claim

A sterile neutrino with mass in the 1-2 keV range, mixing with a light active neutrino, could be detected in Rhenium-187 and Tritium beta decays as a small kink in the beta particle energy spectrum, but only if the experimental relative error in the low-energy region (0 to Q_beta minus m_s) is smaller than the height of that step; the mixing angle required for cosmological dark matter production (zeta ~ 0.006 degrees, sin^2 zeta ~ 10^-8) is far smaller than current experimental upper bounds.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential beta decay rates for Rhenium-187 (a first-forbidden unique Gamow-Teller transition, 5/2+ to 1/2-) and Tritium (an allowed 1/2+ to 1/2+ transition) including both s-wave and p-wave electron emission, with the neutrino flavor state written as a two-state mixing of a light active neutrino and a keV-scale sterile neutrino. They introduced the dimensionless ratio R of the sterile neutrino contribution to the active neutrino contribution as a function of electron momentum, and evaluated the spectral distortion for sterile masses of 1, 1.5, and 2 keV and mixing angles of 0.001, 0.005, and 0.01 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Q_beta for Rhenium-187 is approximately 2.469 keV; for Tritium it is approximately 18.6 keV.
- For Rhenium-187 with m_s = 1 keV, the step in the spectrum occurs at T_e = Q_beta - m_s = 1.469 keV.
- For m_s = 2 keV, (p_e)_max = 21.9 keV; for m_s = 1 keV, (p_e)_max = 38.8 keV.
- The p-wave contribution to the Rhenium decay rate dominates over the s-wave by four orders of magnitude (10^11 vs 10^7 in units of C_Re keV^6).
- The dimensionless squared nuclear reduced matrix element for Rhenium-187 is B_Re approximately 3.6 x 10^-4, using a nuclear radius of 6.86 fm.
- The constant C in the decay rate formula is approximately 2 x 10^-36 (keV)^-4.
- Cosmological constraints suggest sin^2 zeta ~ 10^-8 (zeta ~ 0.006 degrees) for popular sterile neutrino dark matter models.
- Current experimental upper bound on the mixing angle from Rhenium-187 is zeta < 0.095 for 1 keV sterile neutrinos (Gal (2007)).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of sterile neutrino signatures in beta decay from earlier studies by Shrock (1981) and Dvorak (1983), and provides specific calculations for the two isotopes currently under study in the MARE experiment (Rhenium-187) and the KATRIN experiment (Tritium). The paper contrasts the keV-scale warm dark matter scenario with cold dark matter (GeV-scale WIMPs) and hot dark matter (eV-scale), noting that warm dark matter with particle mass between 1 and 10 keV reproduces observed galactic structures, cored density profiles, and the universal galaxy surface density of 120 (MeV)^3, while CDM simulations predict cusped profiles and surface densities 1000 times larger than observed. The results enable experimental searches for sterile neutrinos in the low-energy region of the beta spectrum, well separated from the endpoint region used for active neutrino mass measurements.
