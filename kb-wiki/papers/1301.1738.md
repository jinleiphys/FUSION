# Varying the light quark mass: impact on the nuclear force and Big Bang nucleosynthesis

> The quark mass dependences of light element binding energies and nuclear scattering lengths are derived using chiral perturbation theory in combination with non-perturbative methods. In particular, we present new, improved values for the quark mass dependence of meson resonances that enter the nuclear force. A detailed analysis of the theoretical uncertainties arising in this determination is presented. As an application we derive from a comparison of observed and calculated primordial deuterium and helium abundances a stringent limit on the variation of the light quark mass, $δm_q/m_q = 0.02 \pm 0.04$. Inclusion of the neutron lifetime modification under the assumption of a variation of the Higgs vacuum expectation value that translates into changing quark, electron, and weak gauge boson masses, leads to a stronger limit, $|δm_q/m_q| < 0.009$.

## Key claim

By combining chiral perturbation theory with non-perturbative methods to derive the quark mass dependence of meson resonances and nuclear forces, the authors obtain a limit on the variation of the light quark mass at Big Bang nucleosynthesis of δm_q/m_q = 0.02 ± 0.04 from deuterium and helium abundances alone, and a stronger bound |δm_q/m_q| < 0.009 when including neutron lifetime modification under the assumption of a varying Higgs vacuum expectation value.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) combined with the inverse amplitude method (IAM) to compute the quark mass dependence of the σ and ρ meson masses, expressed as K-factors K_H^f = (δM_H/δm_f) / (M_H/m_f). These meson mass dependences were then connected to the two-nucleon (NN) potential via resonance saturation, using the expressions of Ref. [ressat] to relate NN contact terms to meson masses and couplings. The resulting quark mass dependence of NN scattering lengths and light-element binding energies was combined with a response matrix from Ref. [berengut10plb] to compute the impact on primordial deuterium and helium abundances, yielding limits on δm_q/m_q.

## Key numbers

- K_N^q = 0.048^{+0.002}_{-0.006} for the nucleon mass dependence on the light quark mass, extracted from the πN sigma term σ_πN = 44.9^{+1.8}_{-5.4} MeV
- K_π^q = 0.494^{+0.009}_{-0.013} for the pion mass dependence on the light quark mass
- K_ρ^q = 0.058 ± 0.002 for the ρ meson mass dependence on the light quark mass
- K_σ^q = 0.081 ± 0.007 for the σ meson mass dependence on the light quark mass
- K_Fπ^q = 0.048 ± 0.012 for the pion decay constant dependence on the light quark mass
- Limit from primordial D and ^4He abundances: δm_q/m_q = 0.02 ± 0.04
- Stronger limit including neutron lifetime modification: |δm_q/m_q| < 0.009

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of quark mass variation effects on BBN first presented in Ref. [platterbedaque] by providing improved, systematically derived quark mass dependences for meson resonances, rather than relying on naturalness assumptions for contact interactions as in Ref. [chiralextrapol_bonn]. The authors find significantly smaller quark mass dependences than those assumed in earlier model-dependent estimates by Flambaum and Shuryak (2003) and others, particularly for the σ meson where they obtain K_σ^s = 0.01 ± 0.05 compared to the earlier value of 0.54. The results enable more reliable constraints on fundamental constant variation from BBN and can be related to spatial variations of the fine-structure constant under grand unification models.
