# Inhomogeneous SU(2) gluon matter under rotation

> In this work a rotating SU(2) gluon system have been studied with the dyon ensemble in dilute limit. By solving the rotation-modified Yang-Mills equation we have obtained rotational corrections to the so-called dyon solutions with arbitrary centers to $\mathcal{O}(ω^2)$ order and the corresponding semi-classical potential. The radial position dependent deconfinement temperature have been obtained by minimizing the semi-classical potential in both real and imaginary angular velocity cases. Although without the $ω$-dependent coupling constant the critical temperature behaves different from the lattice simulation at each radial position as the rotation goes faster, its radial dependence is qualitatively the same as the lattice. That is in the real velocity case the outer layer will deconfine more difficult while the reverse is true in the imaginary velocity case.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a rotating SU(2) gluon system modeled with a dyon ensemble in the dilute limit, the radial dependence of the deconfinement temperature qualitatively matches lattice simulation results, even though the critical temperature at each radial position behaves differently from lattice data when the omega-dependent coupling constant is omitted.

## Method

The authors solved the rotation-modified Yang-Mills equation to obtain rotational corrections to dyon solutions with arbitrary centers up to O(omega^2) order, then computed the corresponding semi-classical potential. They minimized the total effective potential (non-perturbative plus perturbative) at different radial positions and temperatures to find the critical deconfinement temperature for both real and imaginary angular velocity cases.

## Key numbers

- The system radius was set to R = 2(pi T_0)^{-1} with T_0 = 0.25 GeV and omega_max = 0.9 R^{-1}
- The model parameters used were c = 2 and Lambda = 0.28 GeV
- The radial position dependent factor alpha = 1 - omega^2 (y_1^2 + y_2^2) for real velocity and alpha = 1 + omega^2 (y_1^2 + y_2^2) for imaginary velocity
- The critical temperature showed a rising trend around omega = 0.8 R^{-1} at r_c = 0.5R in the real velocity case

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Jiang 2023) which obtained on-axis dyon solutions under rotation, by now solving for off-axis dyons to study the inhomogeneous properties of the rotating system. The results are compared with lattice simulations by Chernodub (2020, 2022) and Braguta (2021, 2023) that used imaginary angular velocity to avoid the sign problem. The approach enables future investigation of a rotation-dependent coupling constant, which the authors note is needed for quantitative agreement with lattice data.
