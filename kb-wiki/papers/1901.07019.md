# Centrality dependence of the direct photon multiplicity in heavy ion collisions

> Measurements indicate that the centrality dependence of the direct photon multiplicity scales approximately with the number of binary nucleon collisions. Importantly, these same measurements suggest that this scaling does not depend on the cutoff used to define the photon multiplicity. In this work we provide a theoretical perspective on these observations. We emphasize the importance of stronger experimental constraints, in particular for the cutoff independence of the centrality scaling. We highlight the importance of revisiting theoretical studies of jet-medium photons and of medium-modified prompt photons to clarify their contribution to the direct photon multiplicity, as well as the magnitude and direction of their momentum anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed approximate binary-collision scaling of the direct photon multiplicity in heavy ion collisions, and its apparent independence from the photon transverse momentum cutoff, lacks a clear theoretical explanation and may be challenged by improved experimental constraints, particularly regarding the cutoff dependence of the scaling exponent.

## Method

The authors provide a theoretical perspective by decomposing the direct photon multiplicity into contributions from thermal photons, medium-modified prompt photons, and jet-medium photons. They perform hydrodynamic simulations to calculate the thermal photon multiplicity as a function of the number of binary nucleon collisions for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at 5020 GeV, using three different transverse momentum cutoffs (0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 GeV). The centrality dependence of the thermal photon multiplicity is fitted to a power law in the number of binary collisions.

## Key numbers

* Thermal photon multiplicity scales as N_binary^alpha with alpha approximately 1.1-1.3 for Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5020 GeV, depending on the transverse momentum cutoff.
* The exponent alpha increases as the photon transverse momentum cutoff is increased, with cutoffs of 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 GeV shown in the figures.
* The thermal photon multiplicity calculations are performed for Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5020 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of direct photon production in heavy ion collisions prompted by observations from the PHENIX Collaboration (Adare 2018) that the centrality dependence of the direct photon multiplicity scales approximately with the number of binary nucleon collisions and appears independent of the photon momentum cutoff. The paper highlights the need for updated calculations of jet-medium photons and medium-modified prompt photons, noting that almost all recent comparisons with data rely on prompt photon calculations that neglect parton energy loss. The authors emphasize that improved experimental constraints on the cutoff independence of the centrality scaling could rule out the scenario where thermal photons dominate the direct photon multiplicity.
