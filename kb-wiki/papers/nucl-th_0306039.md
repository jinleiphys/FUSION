# Transverse momentum diffusion and broadening of the back-to-back di-hadron correlation function

> We extend the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev reaction operator approach to multiple elastic scattering of fast partons traversing dense nuclear matter to take into account the leading power corrections due to the medium recoil and to derive the change in the partons' longitudinal momentum.We employ a boost-invariant formalism to generalize previous treatments of the problem, which were specific to the target rest frame. We apply the transverse momentum diffusion results in a simple analytic model to evaluate the broadening of the back-to-back di-hadron correlation function in $d+Au$ reactions.

# Transverse momentum diffusion and broadening of the back-to-back di-hadron correlation function

## Key claim

The authors extend the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) reaction operator approach to include leading power corrections from medium recoil, deriving a longitudinal momentum shift of the fast parton that ensures 3D momentum conservation. The resulting broadening of the back-to-back di-hadron correlation function in d+Au reactions is predicted to be small and weakly centrality-dependent, distinctly different from the disappearance of back-to-back correlations in central Au+Au reactions.

## Method

The GLV reaction operator formalism for multiple elastic scatterings of fast partons in dense nuclear matter is extended by including the leading power corrections in O(1/P) due to medium recoil, which modifies the parton's "+" light-cone component. A boost-invariant formulation is introduced that generalizes the Gyulassy-Wang model of static scattering centers, using a color-screened Yukawa potential with Lorentz boost invariance. The opacity series is resummed in impact parameter space, and the resulting Gaussian transverse momentum distribution is applied to evaluate the nuclear-induced acoplanarity of hard-scattered parton pairs in d+Au reactions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum broadening from multiple elastic scattering is ⟨p⊥²⟩ = 2χμ²ξ, where χ = L/λ is the opacity, μ is the screening mass, and ξ = ln(2e^{-γ_E}/(b_⊥μ))
- The longitudinal momentum shift is -Δp_∥ = ⟨p⊥²⟩/2P, ensuring 3D momentum conservation to O(⟨p⊥²⟩²/P²)
- The gluon scattering dominated transport coefficient is μ²/λ ≈ 0.14 GeV²/fm, constrained from low energy p+A data
- The quark transport coefficient is μ²/λ_q = 0.047 ± 0.035 GeV²/fm
- The ratio λ_q/λ_g = C_A/C_R = 2.25
- The vacuum intrinsic transverse momentum per parton is ⟨|k_Ty|⟩_vac = 0.75 GeV
- For minimum bias d+Au reactions at ⟨|p_T|⟩ = 3-4 GeV, the far-side width σ_Far increases by approximately 25-30%
- For central d+Au reactions, the far-side width grows by approximately 35-40% relative to p+p
- The near-side di-hadron correlation width gives ⟨|j_Ty|⟩ ≈ 400 MeV, roughly independent of √s and trigger particle p_T

## Context

This work extends the GLV reaction operator approach (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2002) for multiple elastic scatterings to include longitudinal momentum conservation, generalizing the Gyulassy-Wang model (Gyulassy, Wang, 1993) of static scattering centers. The formalism is applied to predict the broadening of back-to-back di-hadron correlations in d+Au reactions at RHIC, using cold nuclear matter transport coefficients extracted from low energy p+A data (Cronin, 1975). The predicted small broadening contrasts with the reported disappearance of back-to-back correlations in central Au+Au reactions (Adler, 2002), which is understood in terms of strong final state radiative energy loss. The results enable quantitative comparison with RHIC data on di-hadron correlations in d+Au collisions, and the formalism can be extended to Au+Au collisions using a Bjorken expansion scenario for the quark-gluon plasma.
