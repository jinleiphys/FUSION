# Nuclear Enhanced Higher-Twist Effects in the Drell-Yan Process

> We calculate the Drell-Yan cross section, resolving the full kinematics of the lepton pair, at high transverse momentum for hadron nucleus collisions. We use the general framework of Luo, Qiu and Sterman to calculate double scattering contributions that are of twist-4 and demonstrate their nuclear enhancement. By comparing single and double scattering at RHIC energies we find that double scattering gives contributions of comparable size. We also show that the angular dependence of the Drell-Yan pair discriminates between the various double scattering contributions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that double-scattering (twist-4) contributions to the Drell-Yan cross section in hadron-nucleus collisions are comparable in size to single-scattering (twist-2) contributions at RHIC energies, but only when both the lepton pair mass Q and transverse momentum q_perp are of the same order, because the formalism cannot yet handle the region where these scales differ substantially.

## Method

The authors use the Luo-Qiu-Sterman (LQS) factorization framework to compute twist-4 double-scattering contributions to the fully differential Drell-Yan cross section dσ/dQ² dq_perp² dy dΩ for hadron-nucleus collisions. They distinguish between double-hard (DH) scattering, where a parton undergoes two independent hard scatterings in the nucleus, and soft-hard (SH) scattering, where a parton interacts with the collective soft gluon field before the final hard scattering. The calculation includes all partonic subprocesses (quark-antiquark annihilation and Compton processes) and evaluates the helicity amplitudes W_T, W_L, W_Δ, and W_ΔΔ in both the Collins-Soper and Gottfried-Jackson frames.

## Key numbers

- Double scattering contributions are of "comparable size" to single scattering at RHIC energies (no specific numerical cross section values given in the truncated text)
- The nuclear enhancement scales as A^(4/3) in certain kinematic regions, compared to the linear A scaling of single scattering
- The NA10 experiment measured pair masses Q ≈ 4–8.5 GeV and transverse momenta q_perp < 3 GeV for π+A scattering
- The formalism requires q_perp² not to be much smaller than Q² to neglect interference terms involving additional twist-4 matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the LQS factorization framework (Luo, Qiu, and Sterman, 1994) for twist-4 multiple scattering in hadron-nucleus collisions, building on earlier calculations by Guo (1998) for higher-twist effects in Drell-Yan at large transverse momentum. The authors show that the angular distribution of Drell-Yan pairs, specifically the helicity amplitude W_Δ and the Lam-Tung relation W_ΔΔ = W_L/2 (Lam and Tung, 1980), can discriminate between double-hard and soft-hard scattering contributions, enabling future experimental identification of twist-4 effects at RHIC where higher Q² makes perturbation theory more reliable. The work also aims to inform the understanding of multiple scattering in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC, where parton cascade models (Kajantie, Landshoff, 1989; Geiger, Müller, 1992) and random mean-field models (McLerran, Venugopalan, 1994) represent competing theoretical descriptions.
