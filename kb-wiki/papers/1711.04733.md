# The Charm and Beauty of Strong Interactions

> We briefly review common features and overlapping issues in hadron and flavor physics focussing on continuum QCD approaches to heavy bound states, their mass spectrum and weak decay constants in different strong interaction models.

# The Charm and Beauty of Strong Interactions

## Key claim
Continuum QCD approaches based on Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations can provide a unified description of heavy quarkonia and heavy-light meson spectra and weak decay constants, but the agreement with experimental data depends strongly on the interaction model and truncation scheme employed, with decay constants showing up to 30% variation across different models.

## Method
The authors review calculations of meson masses and weak decay constants using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the quark propagator coupled with the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for bound states, both in rainbow-ladder truncation. They compare results from finite-range QCD interaction models (such as the Maris-Tandy model) with a simpler contact interaction (CI) model that uses a momentum-independent gluon mass scale of approximately 800 MeV and a proper-time regularization scheme to emulate confinement. The bound-state spectrum is obtained by solving for eigenvalues λ_n(P²) = 1 at the on-shell mass M_n for each J^PC channel.

## Key numbers
- η_c(1S) mass: 2.950 GeV (CI model), 3.065 GeV (DSE), compared to PDG average 2.983 GeV
- J/ψ mass: 3.129 GeV (CI model), 3.114 GeV (DSE), compared to PDG average 3.097 GeV
- Υ(1S) mass: 9.460 GeV (CI model), 9.490 GeV (DSE), compared to PDG average 9.460 GeV
- η_b(1S) mass: 9.345 GeV (CI model), 9.414 GeV (DSE), compared to PDG average 9.399 GeV
- f_D (weak decay constant): 0.207 GeV (CI model), 0.204 GeV (DSE), compared to reference value 0.204 GeV
- f_η_c(1S): 0.360 GeV (CI model), 0.389 GeV (DSE), compared to reference value 0.361 GeV
- f_J/ψ: 0.291 GeV (CI model), 0.433 GeV (DSE), compared to reference value 0.416 GeV
- f_η_c(2S) (first radial excitation): 0.105 GeV (DSE), with no reference value available
- f_π(1300) (first radial excitation in chiral limit): -8.3 × 10⁻⁴ GeV
- The ratio ζ = σ_f / M^E_f equals 0.5 for m_q(19 GeV) ≈ 0.5 GeV, indicating the transition region where DCSB and Higgs mechanism contribute equally

## Context
This review extends the continuum QCD program of Jain and Munczek (1993) on quarkonia spectroscopy and the DSE-BSE framework developed by Maris, Tandy, and collaborators. It directly compares results from the contact interaction model of Bedolla, Raya, and Serna (2015-2017) with finite-range DSE calculations by Rojas, Mojica, and Hilger (2014-2017) and Fischer, Ding, and collaborators (2014-2015). The paper highlights that while mass spectra of lower-angular momentum quarkonia are relatively easy to reproduce across models, weak decay constants show significant model dependence (up to 30% for J/ψ), and that none of the BSE truncations currently provide solutions for B mesons. The work enables improved precision calculations of weak heavy-meson decays by advocating for the use of full distribution amplitudes from DSE-BSE calculations rather than asymptotic approximations, as demonstrated by Chang (2013).
