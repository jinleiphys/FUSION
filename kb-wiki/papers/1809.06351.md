# Few-body universality in the deuteron-alpha system

> We treat ${}^6$Li as an effective three-body ($n$-$p$-$α$) system and compute the $d$-$α$ $S-$wave scattering length and three-body separation energy of ${}^6$Li for a wide variety of nucleon-nucleon and $α$-nucleon potentials which have the same (or nearly the same) phase shifts. The Coulomb interaction in the $p$-$α$ subsystem is omitted. The results of all calculations lie on a one-parameter curve in the plane defined by the $d$-$α$ $S-$wave scattering length and the amount by which ${}^6$Li is bound with respect to the $n$-$p$-$α$ threshold. We argue that these aspects of the $n$-$p$-$α$ system can be understood using few-body universality and that ${}^6$Li can thus usefully be thought of as a two-nucleon halo nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors show that the three-body separation energy of ${}^6$Li and the $d$-$\alpha$ $S$-wave scattering length lie on a single universal correlation curve, even when the Coulomb interaction in the $p$-$\alpha$ subsystem is omitted. This correlation holds for a wide variety of nucleon-nucleon and $\alpha$-nucleon potentials that have the same (or nearly the same) phase shifts, suggesting that ${}^6$Li can usefully be thought of as a two-nucleon halo nucleus.

## Method

The authors treat ${}^6$Li as an effective three-body ($n$-$p$-$\alpha$) system and solve bound state Faddeev equations and momentum-space Faddeev-AGS equations for $d$-$\alpha$ scattering, using separable representations of the pairwise interactions. They generate families of phase-shift-equivalent $\alpha N$ potentials by applying a unitary transformation (UT) to the $P$-wave $\alpha N$ interaction, varying the range parameter $c$ from 35 fm$^{-1}$ down to values where ${}^6$Li becomes unbound, while also testing realistic $NN$ potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen-93, Nijmegen-II, Idaho-N4LO) and varying the Woods-Saxon strength of the Bang $\alpha N$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $d$-$\alpha$ separation energy of ${}^6$Li is 1.47 MeV, comparable to the deuteron binding energy of 2.22 MeV.
- Using the Bang $\alpha N$ interaction with central Woods-Saxon strength -44 MeV and the CD-Bonn $np$ potential, omitting Coulomb, the ${}^6$Li three-body separation energy is -3.78 MeV and the scattering length is 5.29 fm.
- The deuteron breakup threshold is reached at $c = 3.9$ fm$^{-1}$, at which point ${}^6$Li becomes unbound and $1/a_{d\alpha} \rightarrow 0$.
- The $\alpha N$ $S$- and $P$-wave phase shifts are fitted up to 10 MeV for the rank-1 separable interactions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Phillips line, originally demonstrated for the three-nucleon system by Phillips (1968) and later understood via Efimov physics, to the $n$-$p$-$\alpha$ system. It contrasts with the six-body No-Core Shell Model EFT calculation of Stetcu, Barrett, and van Kolck (2006), which found ${}^6$Li unbound with respect to the $d$-$\alpha$ threshold. The results are consistent with universal correlations expected for weakly-bound three-body systems as analyzed for ${}^6$He by Rotureau (2012) and Ji (2014), and support the identification of ${}^6$Li as a deuteron halo nucleus, in agreement with infrared extrapolations of No-core Shell Model calculations by Forssen (2017). Future work should include the Coulomb repulsion between the $\alpha$ particle and the proton, and investigate whether other low-energy $d$-$\alpha$ observables are correlated with the three-body separation energy.
