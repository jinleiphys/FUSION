# Allowed $β^-$ decay of bare atoms with A$\sim$ 60-80 in stellar environments

> We have calculated $β^-$ decay rates to the continuum and bound states of some fully ionized atoms in the stellar s-process environment having free electron density and temperature in the range $n_e = 10^{26} $ cm$^{-3} - 10^{27} $ cm$^{-3}$ and $T = 10^8$ K - $5 \times 10^{8}$ K, respectively. The presence of bare atoms in these particular situations has been confirmed by solving Saha ionization equation taking into account the ionization potential depression (IPD). At these temperatures, low lying excited energy levels of parent nuclei may have thermal equilibrium population and those excited levels may also decay via $β^-$ emission. The Nuclear Matrix Element (NME) of all the transitions of the set of 15 nuclei is calculated using nuclear shell-model. These NME are then used to calculate the comparative half-life ($ft_{1/2}$) of the transitions. Calculated terrestrial half-lives of the $β^-$ decays are in good agreement with the experimental results in most of the cases. Decay to bound and continuum states of bare atoms from ground/isomeric levels and excited nuclear levels have been calculated separately. The ratio of bound state to continuum state decay rates as a function of IPD modified $Q$-value reveals that bound state $β^-$ decay rate may compete and even dominate for $Q$-value $<$ 100 keV. The importance of the bound state $β^-$ decay in stellar situations has been shown explicitly. We have calculated total $β^-$ decay rates (bound state plus continuum state) taking into account IPD corrected neutral atom $Q$-value as a function of density and temperature. We have also presented results for the stellar $β^-$ half-lives and compared the ratio of neutral atom to bare atom half-lives for different density and temperature combinations. These results may be useful for s-process nucleosynthesis calculations.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that bound-state β⁻ decay can compete with and even dominate continuum-state β⁻ decay for fully ionized atoms in stellar s-process environments when the IPD-modified Q-value is less than 100 keV, but this dominance is conditional on the specific temperature (10⁸ K–5×10⁸ K) and free electron density (10²⁶–10²⁷ cm⁻³) ranges assumed for the s-process site.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations in the (1f₇/₂ 2p₃/₂ 1f₅/₂ 2p₁/₂) and (2p₃/₂ 1f₅/₂ 2p₁/₂ 1g₉/₂) valence spaces with ⁴⁰Ca and ⁵⁶Ni cores, using the OXBASH and NuShellX codes, to obtain Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements for allowed β⁻ transitions in 15 nuclei with A ≈ 59–81. They then computed the lepton phase-space factors for both continuum and bound-state decays, incorporating ionization potential depression via the Stewart-Pyatt model as parameterized by Takahashi and Yokoi (1983), and solved the Saha ionization equation to confirm the presence of bare atoms under the chosen stellar conditions. The total stellar β⁻ decay rate was obtained by summing over thermally populated excited nuclear levels using Boltzmann statistics.

## Key numbers

*   Free electron density range: nₑ = 10²⁶ cm⁻³ to 10²⁷ cm⁻³; temperature range: T = 10⁸ K to 5×10⁸ K.
*   Bound-state β⁻ decay rate may compete and even dominate for Q-value < 100 keV.
*   Experimental log ft values for allowed transitions in the mass range A = 59–81 range from 4.26 to 8.72; first-forbidden transitions have log ft values from 9.80 to 11.14.
*   Calculated terrestrial half-life for ⁶⁰Co → ⁶⁰Ni decay: T₁/₂(t) = 3.285 years (experimental: 5.275 years); for the isomeric 2⁺₁ state: calculated T₁/₂(t) = 5.41 days (experimental: 2.91 days).
*   Calculated terrestrial half-life for ⁶³Ni → ⁶³Cu decay: T₁/₂(t) = 44.69 years with q = 1, or about 75 years with q = 0.77 (experimental: 101.2 ± 1.5 years).
*   Decay rates with momentum-independent L₀ are within 1%–2% of the results presented with momentum-dependent L₀.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of bound-state β⁻ decay by Takahashi and Yokoi (1983, 1987) and the authors' own previous work (Gupta et al., 2019) by performing realistic nuclear shell-model calculations of comparative half-lives (ft values) for all relevant allowed transitions, rather than using average or estimated ft values. It also incorporates thermal population of excited nuclear levels, which Takahashi and Yokoi (1983, 1987) had considered but with less detailed ft values. The results are intended for use in s-process nucleosynthesis calculations, where the competition between bound-state and continuum-state decay can significantly affect stellar β⁻ half-lives and branching ratios.
