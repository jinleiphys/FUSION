# Variational Principle Directly on the Coherent Pair Condensate. I. the BCS Case

> We propose a scheme to perform the variational principle directly on the coherent pair condensate (VDPC). The result is equivalent to that of the so-called variation after particle-number projection, but now the particle number is always conserved and the time-consuming projection is avoided. This work considers VDPC+BCS. We derive analytical expressions for the average energy and its gradient in terms of the coherent pair structure. In addition, we give analytically the pair structure at the energy minimum, and discuss its asymptotic behavior away from the Fermi surface, which looks quite simple and allows easy physical interpretations. The new algorithm iterates these pair-structure expressions to minimize energy. We demonstrate the new algorithm in a semi-realistic example using the realistic $V_{low-k}$ interaction and large model spaces (up to 15 harmonic-oscillator major shells). The energy can be minimized to practically arbitrary precision. The result shows that the realistic $V_{low-k}$ interaction does not cause divergences in the pairing channel, although tiny occupation numbers (for example smaller than $10^{-5}$) contribute to the energy (by a few tens of keV). We also give analytical expressions for the gradient of energy with respect to changes of the canonical single-particle basis, which will be necessary for the next work in this series: VDPC+HFB.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a variational principle performed directly on the coherent pair condensate (VDPC) that is equivalent to variation after particle-number projection (VAP) but avoids the time-consuming numerical projection because particle number is always conserved; the work is limited to the BCS case (VDPC+BCS), where only the pair structure is varied on a fixed canonical basis.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions for the average energy and its gradient with respect to the pair structure \(v_\alpha\) of the coherent pair condensate, using Pauli-blocked normalizations and recursive relations. They then obtain the analytical form of \(v_\alpha\) at the energy minimum and its asymptotic behavior far from the Fermi surface, and implement an iterative algorithm that updates \(v_\alpha\) using these expressions to minimize the energy. The method is demonstrated for a semi-realistic system using the realistic \(V_{low-k}\) interaction in model spaces up to 15 harmonic-oscillator major shells.

## Key numbers

* The realistic \(V_{low-k}\) interaction does not cause divergences in the pairing channel, even when model spaces include up to 15 harmonic-oscillator major shells.
* Tiny occupation numbers smaller than \(10^{-5}\) contribute to the total energy by a few tens of keV.
* The energy can be minimized to practically arbitrary precision using the new iterative algorithm.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach to the coherent pair condensate (VAP+BCS) previously studied by Dietrich (1964), Dussel (2007), Dukelsky (2000), and Sandulescu (2008), but replaces the time-consuming gauge-angle integration with analytical expressions for the energy and its gradient. The new VDPC algorithm avoids the numerical sign problem that limited earlier VAP calculations to small model spaces or schematic Hamiltonians (Sandulescu 2008, Hupin 2012), and enables future extension to VDPC+HFB (the next paper in this series) where the canonical basis is also varied.
