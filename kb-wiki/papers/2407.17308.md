# Impact parameter dependence of anisotropic flow: Bayesian reconstruction in ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Peculiar phenomena have been observed in analyses of anisotropic flow ($v_n$) fluctuations in ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collisions: The fourth-order cumulant of the elliptic flow ($v_2$) distribution changes sign. In addition, the ATLAS collaboration has shown that cumulants of $v_n$ fluctuations of all orders depend significantly on the centrality estimator. We show that these peculiarities are due to the fact that the impact parameter $b$ always spans a finite range for a fixed value of the centrality estimator. We provide a quantitative determination of this range through a simple Bayesian analysis. We obtain excellent fits of STAR and ATLAS data, with a few parameters, by assuming that the probability distribution of $v_n$ solely depends on $b$ at a given centrality. This probability distribution is almost Gaussian, and its parameters depend smoothly on $b$, in a way that is constrained by symmetry and scaling laws. We reconstruct, thus, the impact parameter dependence of the mean elliptic flow in the reaction plane in a model-independent manner, and assess the robustness of the extraction using Monte Carlo simulations of the collisions where the impact parameter is known. We argue that the non-Gaussianity of $v_n$ fluctuations gives direct information on the hydrodynamic response to initial anisotropies, ATLAS data being consistent with a smaller response for $n=4$ than for $n=2$ and $n=3$, in agreement with hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The peculiar sign change of the fourth-order cumulant of elliptic flow and the centrality-estimator dependence of flow cumulants in ultracentral collisions are explained by the fact that the impact parameter always spans a finite range for a fixed value of the experimentally-defined centrality estimator, and this range can be quantitatively reconstructed using a simple Bayesian analysis.

## Method

The authors reconstruct the probability distribution of true centrality (impact parameter) at fixed experimental centrality estimator (charged multiplicity or transverse energy) using Bayes' theorem, assuming the distribution of the centrality estimator at fixed true centrality is Gaussian. They then fit experimental cumulants of anisotropic flow fluctuations measured by ATLAS and STAR by assuming that the probability distribution of the flow vector at fixed impact parameter is approximately Gaussian, with mean and variance that depend smoothly on centrality through low-order polynomial expansions.

## Key numbers

* The width of the centrality distribution at fixed experimental centrality estimator is σ_c ≈ 1.2% when using N_ch and σ_c ≈ 0.85% when using E_T for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV (ATLAS).
* The relative increase of the variance of flow fluctuations with centrality, d ln σ_vn²/dc at c=0, is in the range 13–22 per 1% centrality increase for ATLAS data, compared to only 4% for 1/⟨N⟩.
* The mean elliptic flow in the reaction plane overrides the fluctuation width σ_v2 at approximately 4% centrality for ATLAS data.
* The fourth-order cumulant nc_2{4} changes sign from negative to positive at a centrality c_exp ∝ (σ_c)^(2/3), with the positive values reaching up to about 1–2% in the normalized cumulant.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian centrality fluctuation framework of Das (2017) and Yousefnia (2021) to explain the "ultracentral flow puzzle" and the ATLAS observation that cumulants depend on the centrality estimator (ATLAS, 2019). The analysis enables future hydrodynamic calculations at fixed impact parameter for direct theory-to-data comparisons, and shows that non-Gaussianity of v_n fluctuations gives information on the hydrodynamic response coefficient v_n/ε_n, with ATLAS data consistent with a smaller response for n=4 than for n=2 and n=3, in agreement with hydrodynamic calculations.
