# Diquark Correlations in Hadron Physics: Origin, Impact and Evidence

> The last decade has seen a marked shift in how the internal structure of hadrons is understood. Modern experimental facilities, new theoretical techniques for the continuum bound-state problem and progress with lattice-regularised QCD have provided strong indications that soft quark+quark (diquark) correlations play a crucial role in hadron physics. For example, theory indicates that the appearance of such correlations is a necessary consequence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, viz. a corollary of emergent hadronic mass that is responsible for almost all visible mass in the universe; experiment has uncovered signals for such correlations in the flavour-separation of the proton's electromagnetic form factors; and phenomenology suggests that diquark correlations might be critical to the formation of exotic tetra- and penta-quark hadrons. A broad spectrum of such information is evaluated herein, with a view to consolidating the facts and therefrom moving toward a coherent, unified picture of hadron structure and the role that diquark correlations might play.

# Diquark Correlations in Hadron Physics: Origin, Impact and Evidence

## Key claim
Diquark correlations are a necessary consequence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in QCD, but the paper acknowledges that a rigorous proof within QCD is not known; the assertion is based on an accumulated body of evidence from three decades of studying two- and three-body bound-state problems, with no realistic counter examples known.

## Method
The paper provides a critical review of diquark correlations across multiple theoretical and experimental approaches: phenomenological quark models (including relativised potential models with one-gluon-exchange and linear confinement), continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs), and lattice-regularised QCD (lQCD) simulations. It evaluates experimental signals from electromagnetic form factor flavour-separation and exotic hadron spectroscopy, and compares predictions from diquark-based models against three-quark models and experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The length-scale associated with flux tube dissolution in QCD is approximately 1/3 fm.
- In light baryons, the scalar diquark is approximately 20% lighter than the axial-vector diquark based on one-gluon-exchange interaction estimates.
- Scalar–axial-vector diquark mass differences from baryon mass combinations: M_ud^av - M_ud^sc ≈ 210 MeV; M_us^av - M_us^sc = 152 MeV.
- Scalar diquark mass estimates from fitting to a0(980) and σ(480): M_ud^sc = 395 MeV; M_sq^sc = 590 MeV (with q = u or d); M_cq^sc = 1933 MeV from fitting X(3872).
- Relativised quark model diquark masses: qq scalar 691 MeV, axial-vector 840 MeV; qs scalar 886 MeV, axial-vector 992 MeV; qc scalar 2099 MeV, axial-vector 2138 MeV.
- Predicted ground-state mass of Ξ_cc (J^P = 1/2^+) from relativised quark+diquark model: 3620 MeV, compatible with experimental 3621.2 ± 0.7 MeV.
- Predicted ground-state masses: Ξ_bb 10202 MeV; Ω_bb 10359 MeV; Ω_cc 3778 MeV.
- Mass difference between observed pentaquarks P_c(4312)^+ and P_c(4440)^+/P_c(4457)^+ is ΔM ≈ 140 MeV, much smaller than orbital excitation energy O(300) MeV.

## Context
This review consolidates evidence from multiple approaches including the quark+diquark models of Santopinto (2004), Ferretti (2011), and Galata (2012) for baryon spectroscopy; the tetraquark models of Maiani (2004) and Anwar (2018) for XYZ states; and the pentaquark model of Maiani (2015) for LHCb P_c states. It extends earlier diquark concepts from Gell-Mann and Zweig (1964) and addresses whether modern dynamical diquark correlations are the same as the static pointlike diquarks used in early attempts by Anselmino (1992). The work enables a unified framework for interpreting experimental signals of diquark correlations across conventional baryons, exotic tetraquarks, and pentaquarks, while identifying open questions about whether diquarks are mathematical artifices or physical degrees of freedom.
