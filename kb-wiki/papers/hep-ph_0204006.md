# Chiral symmetry restoration and the string picture of hadrons

> QCD string picture of highly excited hadrons very naturally explains parity doubling once the chiral symmetry is restored high in the spectrum. In particular, the spin-orbit and tensor interactions of quarks at the ends of the string, related to dynamics of the string, vanish. High in the spectrum there appears higher degree of degeneracy, namely parity doublets with different angular momentum cluster around energy of the string in the given quantum state.

# Chiral symmetry restoration and the string picture of hadrons

## Key claim

The QCD string picture of highly excited hadrons naturally explains parity doubling once chiral symmetry is restored high in the spectrum, but this restoration is only approximate and the chiral symmetry breaking condensates of the vacuum remain present, they simply become irrelevant for sufficiently excited states. The spin-orbit and tensor interactions of quarks at the ends of the string vanish only in the idealized limit of exact chiral restoration with massless quarks of definite chirality=helicity.

## Method

The paper constructs a theoretical model where highly excited hadrons are described as rotating QCD strings with massless quarks at the ends that have definite chirality=helicity, belonging to parity-chiral multiplets of the SU(2)L × SU(2)R group. The Weyl equations for stationary motion of right- and left-handed quarks are solved under the condition that parity transforms one into the other, showing that the rotating string does not distinguish between right and left valence quarks and assigns them the same energy. The analysis is applied to both meson spectra (vector ρ and axial-vector a1 mesons as chiral partners in the (0,1)⊕(1,0) representation) and baryon spectra (N and Δ resonances forming parity doublets).

## Key numbers

- The onset of approximate chiral symmetry restoration in the nucleon spectrum occurs at M ≃ 1.7 GeV, where states systematically appear as approximate parity doublets
- For mesons, nearly perfect parity doublets appear systematically in the mass region of 1.8 GeV and above
- The ρ(1,1−−) state at 1970 ± 30 MeV can be paired with the a1(1,1++) state at 1930+30−70 MeV, and the ρ at 2265 ± 40 MeV with the a1 at 2270+55−40 MeV
- The same ρ states can alternatively be paired with h1(0,1+−) states at 1965 ± 45 MeV and 2215 ± 40 MeV
- The first cluster of parity doublets in the nucleon spectrum appears at M ≃ 1.7 GeV, consistent with intrinsic string angular momentum L=1 giving three doublets with J = 1/2, 3/2, 5/2
- A band of baryons in the N and Δ spectra in the mass region M ≃ 2 GeV contains parity doublets consistent with L=2 giving J = 1/2, 3/2, 5/2, 7/2
- Meson parity doublets group into two clusters around masses of ≃ 2 GeV and ≃ 2.2–2.3 GeV
- Inclusive electroproduction of resonances in the mass region 3.1 ≤ M² ≤ 3.9 GeV² is perfectly dual to deep inelastic data (JLAB, 2000)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Cohen and Glozman (2002) on chiral symmetry restoration in hadron spectra, which showed that operator product expansion and dispersion relations imply smooth chiral restoration at high energies. The string picture is contrasted with the constituent quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), which fails to describe highly excited a1 mesons because it cannot incorporate chiral symmetry restoration, the potential model predicts only two a1 states up to 2.4 GeV (at 1.24 and 1.82 GeV), while experimental data from the LEAR partial wave analysis of Anisovich et al. (2000-2001) shows states at 1.26, 1.64, 1.93, and 2.27 GeV. The paper enables future systematic phase shift analyses to unambiguously identify chiral partners in both baryon and meson spectra, and calls for evaluation of weak spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor corrections that arise from the approximate nature of chiral restoration and nonzero quark masses.
