# Nuclear Parity Violation from Lattice QCD

> The electroweak interaction at the level of quarks and gluons are well understood from precision measurements in high energy collider experiments. Relating these fundamental parameters to Hadronic Parity Violation in nuclei however remains an outstanding theoretical challenge. One of the most interesting observables in this respect is the parity violating hadronic neutral current: it is hard to measure in collider experiments and is thus the least constrained observable of the Standard Model. Precision measurements of parity violating transitions in nuclei can help to improve these constraints. In these systems however, the weak interaction is masked by effects of the seven orders of magnitude stronger non-perturbative strong interaction. Therefore, in order to relate experimental measurements of the parity violating pion-nucleon couplings to the fundamental Lagrangian of the SM, these non-perturbative effects have to be well understood. In this paper, we are going to present a Lattice QCD approach for computing the $ΔI{=}2$ parity violating matrix element in proton proton scattering. This process does not involve disconnected diagrams in the isospin symmetric limit and is thus a perfect testbed for studying the feasibility of the more involved calculation of the parity violating pion-nucleon coupling.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Lattice QCD approach for computing the $\Delta I{=}2$ parity violating matrix element in proton-proton scattering, but the result is preliminary: the bare finite-volume matrix element is reported without renormalization or infinite-volume extrapolation, and the error bar is purely statistical.

## Method

The authors compute the five-point correlation function $\langle pp(A_1^+)(t_f) | \mathcal{O}^{\Delta I{=}2}(t) | pp(A_1^-)(t_i) \rangle$ on isotropic Wilson clover lattices, using two-proton operators projected to the cubic irreducible representations $A_1^+$ and $A_1^-$ which overlap with the $^1\!S_0$ and $^3\!P_0$ states respectively. They form a ratio of correlation functions to eliminate dependence on source and sink operators and subtract the leading-order energy difference between the $P$- and $S$-wave states by also computing the reversed process. The operator $\mathcal{O}^{\Delta I{=}2}$ is chosen because it avoids disconnected diagrams in the isospin symmetric limit and does not mix with $\Delta I{=}0,1$ operators under renormalization.

## Key numbers

* Bare finite-volume matrix element $M_\mathrm{FV}^{\Delta I{=}2} = -1.0(7) \cdot 10^{-5}$ (statistical error only, no renormalization or infinite-volume matching applied)
* Lattice spacing $a \sim 0.145$ fm, pion mass $m_\pi \sim 800$ MeV, volume $V = 24^3 \times 48$
* Approximately 6400 measurements on approximately 320 configurations
* Source-sink separation fixed to $t_f - t_i = 12$ (in lattice units), with exploratory calculations at $t_f - t_i = 25$ yielding compatible results but larger errors
* Spatial displacement $\Delta = 6$ (in lattice units) for the two-nucleon operators gave best overlap with the $A_1^\pm$ ground states

## Context

This work extends the Lattice QCD approach to parity violation in nuclear systems, building on techniques for two-nucleon operators and phase shift calculations developed by Basak (2005), Dudek (2010), Luu (2011), and Berkowitz (2015). The $\Delta I{=}2$ channel serves as a testbed for the more challenging calculation of the parity violating pion-nucleon coupling, which is needed to connect experimental measurements from the NPDGamma collaboration and earlier $\vec{p}p$ scattering experiments (Nagle 1978, Balzer 1980, Kistryn 1987, Eversheim 1991) to the fundamental Standard Model Lagrangian. Future work requires computing the Lellouch-Luscher matching factor and improving statistics for the $A_1^-$ phase shift measurement to enable infinite-volume extrapolation.
