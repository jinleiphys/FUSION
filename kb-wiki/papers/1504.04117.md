# The recoil correction and spin-orbit force for the possible $B^* \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}$ states

> In the framework of the one-boson exchange model, we have calculated the effective potentials between two heavy mesons $B^* \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}$ from the t- and u-channel $π$-, $η$-, $ρ$-, $ω$- and $σ$-meson exchanges. We keep the recoil corrections to the $B^* \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}$ systems up to $O(\frac{1}{M^2})$, which turns out to be important for the very loosely bound molecular states. Our numerical results show that the momentum-related corrections are favorable to the formation of the molecular states in the $I^G=1^+$, $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ in the $B^* \bar{B}^{*}$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}$ systems.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that momentum-related recoil corrections up to \(O(1/M^2)\) are favorable to the formation of molecular states in the \(I^G=1^+\), \(J^{PC}=1^{+-}\) channels of both the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) and \(D^* \bar{D}^{*}\) systems, but the magnitude of these corrections is small for the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) system (e.g., \(-0.03\) to \(-0.07\) MeV) and can be either favorable or unfavorable depending on the quantum numbers, as seen in the \(I^G=0^-\), \(J^{PC}=1^{+-}\) case where the recoil correction is large and unfavorable.

## Method

The authors employed the one-boson exchange (OBE) model to derive effective potentials for the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) and \(D^* \bar{D}^{*}\) systems, including t- and u-channel exchanges of \(\pi\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\sigma\) mesons. They kept momentum-related terms explicitly in the relativistic Lagrangian to include recoil corrections and spin-orbit interactions up to \(O(1/M^2)\), then solved the coupled S-wave and D-wave Schrodinger equation using a variational method with a form factor cutoff \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- For the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) system with \(I^G=1^+\), \(J^{PC}=1^{+-}\) (candidate for \(Z_b(10650)\)), the binding energy with recoil correction ranges from \(0.97\) MeV (at \(\Lambda = 2.2\) GeV) to \(15.15\) MeV (at \(\Lambda = 2.8\) GeV); the recoil correction itself is \(-0.03\) MeV to \(-0.07\) MeV, and the spin-orbit contribution is \(0.001\) to \(0.02\) MeV.
- For the same \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) system at fixed \(\Lambda = 2.0\) GeV, varying the pionic coupling constant \(g\) by a factor of \(0.99\) to \(1.1\) yields binding energies with recoil correction from \(6.39\) MeV to \(36.81\) MeV.
- For the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) system with \(I^G=1^-\), \(J^{PC}=1^{++}\), a bound state appears only when the coupling constant \(g\) is multiplied by a factor of \(1.3\) or larger; at \(\Lambda = 2.0\) GeV and factor \(1.3\), the binding energy with recoil correction is \(0.82\) MeV, and at factor \(1.5\) it is \(19.25\) MeV.
- For the \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) system with \(I^G=0^-\), \(J^{PC}=1^{+-}\), the binding energy with recoil correction ranges from \(0\) MeV (no bound state at \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV) to \(41.69\) MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.3\) GeV); the recoil correction is \(0.3\) MeV to \(12.05\) MeV, and the spin-orbit contribution is up to \(4.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on the \(D \bar{D}^*\) and \(B \bar{B}^*\) systems (Zhao et al., 2013) by applying the same recoil correction and spin-orbit force formalism to the \(D^* \bar{D}^{*}\) and \(B^* \bar{B}^{*}\) systems, which had not been investigated with these corrections before. The results support the molecular interpretation of the experimentally observed \(Z_b(10650)\) and \(Z_c(4025)\) states, and they enable future studies to include higher-order momentum corrections when modeling loosely bound heavy-meson molecules.
