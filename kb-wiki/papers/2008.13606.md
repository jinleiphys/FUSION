# Cosmological particle creation in the little bang

> Particle production by expanding in the future light cone scalar quantum field is studied by assuming that the initial state is associated with the quasiequilibrium statistical operator corresponding to fluid dynamics. We calculate particle production from a longitudinally boost-invariant expanding quantum field designed as a simple but reliable model for the central rapidity region of a relativistic collision. Exact diagonalization of the model is performed by introducing a notion of quasiparticles.

# Cosmological particle creation in the little bang

## Key claim

Particle production from a longitudinally boost-invariant expanding scalar quantum field can be calculated by assuming the initial state is a quasiequilibrium statistical operator corresponding to fluid dynamics, but the ground state of this operator is time-dependent and does not coincide with the Minkowski vacuum, leading to nonthermal contributions to the momentum spectra even after sudden decoupling.

## Method

The author performs exact diagonalization of the quasiequilibrium statistical operator for a noninteracting boost-invariant scalar quantum field in Bjorken coordinates, using a Bogolyubov transformation to define instantaneous quasiparticle creation and annihilation operators. The field is expanded in Hankel functions on hypersurfaces of constant proper time tau, and the operator H^{[tau]} is diagonalized to obtain a thermal-like form in the quasiparticle representation. Particle production after sudden decoupling at a fixed proper time tau_f is then calculated by relating the quasiparticle operators at tau_f to plane-wave particle operators at asymptotic times through time-dependent Bogolyubov coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle energy is given by omega(p_T, mu, tau) = sqrt(m_T^2 + mu^2/tau^2), where m_T = sqrt(p_T^2 + m^2) is the transverse mass.
- The Bogolyubov coefficient |beta|^2 = (E - 1)/2, where E is defined in terms of Hankel functions and satisfies E^2 - |F|^2 = 1.
- The expectation value of the quasiparticle number operator in the quasiequilibrium state is 1/(exp(omega(p_T, mu, tau_f)/T(tau_f)) - 1).
- The one-particle momentum spectrum at asymptotic times contains a thermal term proportional to (1 + 2|beta|^2)/(exp(omega/T_f) - 1) plus a vacuum term |beta|^2.
- When vacuum particle production terms are neglected, the spectrum reduces to the Bose-Einstein local equilibrium distribution (2pi)^{-3} (exp(u^mu p_mu/T(tau_f)) - 1)^{-1} integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on relativistic hydrodynamics and particle production in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Zubarev nonequilibrium statistical operator formalism (Zubarev, 1974) and the information entropy approach (Jaynes, 1957). It differs from previous studies by Sinyukov (1993, 1994) and Akkelin (2015) by performing exact diagonalization of the model in a quasiparticle basis rather than using approximations. The model draws an analogy between particle creation in the "little bang" of relativistic collisions and cosmological particle creation in the "big bang" expansion (Parker, 1969; Birrell and Davies, 1982). The results enable calculation of nonthermal contributions to particle momentum spectra from the difference between the quasiparticle vacuum and the Minkowski vacuum, which could be tested against experimental data from ultrarelativistic nucleus collisions.
