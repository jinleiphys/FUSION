# Hadron properties in nuclear medium and their impacts on observables

> The effect of changes in hadron properties in a nuclear medium on physical observables is discussed. Highlighted results are, (1) hypernuclei, (2) meosn-nuclear bound states, (3) $K$-meson production in heavy ion collisions, and (4) $J/Ψ$ dissociation in a nuclear medium. In addition, results for the near-threshold $ω$- and $ϕ$-meson productions in proton proton collisions are reported.

# Hadron properties in nuclear medium and their impacts on observables

## Key claim
Changes in hadron properties in a nuclear medium, calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, significantly affect physical observables such as hypernuclei energy levels, meson-nuclear bound states, kaon production cross sections in heavy ion collisions, and J/Ψ dissociation rates; however, the quantitative predictions depend on phenomenological adjustments such as increasing the vector coupling strength for kaons and D mesons by a factor of 1.4^2 to reproduce empirical data.

## Method
The QMC model describes hadrons as bags of quarks interacting with mean-field σ, ω, and ρ mesons, with Dirac equations solved for quarks and antiquarks in nuclear matter. Hadron masses in medium are calculated from the bag energy including quark eigenfrequencies, bag constant, and center-of-mass corrections, with parameters fitted to nuclear saturation properties. For hypernuclei, the Klein-Gordon equation is solved for mesons with complex potentials including widths, and for J/Ψ dissociation, in-medium reaction amplitudes are computed including scalar and vector potentials for all participating hadrons.

## Context
This work extends the QMC model developed by Guichon (1988) and refined by Saito and Thomas (1995) to finite nuclei and hypernuclei, providing a unified quark-level description of in-medium hadron properties. The hypernuclei predictions build on earlier shell-model calculations by Millener (1990) and others, while the J/Ψ dissociation study addresses the QGP suppression signal proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), showing that in-medium effects can explain the NA38/NA50 data without requiring a large constant absorption cross section of 4.5 mb used in the Capella model (1997). The results enable systematic studies of meson-nuclear bound states as probes of partial chiral symmetry restoration, and the pp → ppω/φ measurements at COSY-TOF and DISTO provide constraints on the ωNN tensor coupling and OZI rule violation.
