# Three-body problem at finite temperature and density

> We derive practical three-body equations for the equal-time three-body Green function in matter. Our equations describe both bosons and fermions at finite density and temperature, and take into account all possible two-body sub-processes allowed by the underlying Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper derives practical three-body equations for the equal-time three-body Green function in matter that sum the *entire* infinite series for the effective pair interaction, unlike prior work that kept only the linear term; the key advance is showing that Faddeev's idea of reexpressing three-body equations in terms of two-body t matrices enables exact summation of this series even at finite temperature and density.

## Method

The authors start from the four-dimensional three-body Green function in the real-time formulation of perturbation theory with doubled degrees of freedom, then perform a three-dimensional reduction by equating times in initial and final states. To handle the non-invertibility of the equal-time free Green function (which acts as a projection operator in the many-body case), they introduce a modified Green function with an auxiliary disconnected term that can later be taken to zero. They then derive a convolution relation connecting the exact equal-time three-body t matrix (with one spectator) to the equal-time two-body t matrix and the spectator propagator, bypassing the need to sum the quasi-potential perturbation series directly.

## Key numbers

* The free equal-time three-body Green function for identical particles is given by:  
  $\langle G_0^f \rangle(E,\mathbf{p}_1\mathbf{p}_2\mathbf{p}_3) = \frac{\bar{n}(\mathbf{p}_1)\bar{n}(\mathbf{p}_2)\bar{n}(\mathbf{p}_3)}{E-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_1}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_2}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_3}+i\epsilon} + \frac{n(\mathbf{p}_1)n(\mathbf{p}_2)n(\mathbf{p}_3)}{E-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_1}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_2}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_3}-i\epsilon}$  
  where $\omega_\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{p}^2/2m - \mu$ and $n, \bar{n}$ are functions of the distribution $f(\omega) = 1/(e^{\beta\omega}\pm 1)$.

* The free equal-time two-body Green function is:  
  $\langle D_0^f \rangle(E,\mathbf{p}_1\mathbf{p}_2) = \frac{\bar{n}(\mathbf{p}_1)\bar{n}(\mathbf{p}_2)}{E-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_1}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_2}+i\epsilon} - \frac{n(\mathbf{p}_1)n(\mathbf{p}_2)}{E-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_1}-\omega_{\mathbf{p}_2}-i\epsilon}$.

* The central result relating the three-body and two-body t matrices is:  
  $\tilde{G}_0 \tilde{T}_i \tilde{G}_0 = \tilde{D}_{0i} \tilde{t}_i \tilde{D}_{0i} \otimes \langle d_i \rangle$,  
  where $\otimes$ denotes the convolution $a\otimes b(E) \equiv \frac{i}{2\pi} \int_{-\infty}^\infty a(E-z) b(z)\, dz$.

## Context

This work extends the equal-time Green function approach of Schuck, Villars, and Ring (1970s) and the finite-temperature formulation of Beyer and collaborators (1990s–2000s) by summing the full infinite series for the effective pair-interaction kernel rather than truncating at linear order. The prior state-of-the-art model (Beyer et al.) approximated the effective pair interaction by terms linear in the physical two-body potential, which is unsatisfactory for strong coupling where two-body bound states exist. The result enables practical three-body calculations at finite temperature and density, needed for studying three-nucleon bound states in nuclear matter, three-quark binding in quark matter, and cluster formation in nonequilibrium many-body systems, using exact two-body t matrices as input rather than approximate potentials.
