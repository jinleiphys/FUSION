# Hadron resonance gas with repulsive mean field interaction: Thermodynamics and transport properties

> We discuss the interacting hadron resonance gas model to describe the thermodynamics of hadronic matter. While the attractive interaction between hadrons is taken care of by including all the resonances with zero width, the repulsive interactions are included by considering density-dependent mean field potentials. The bulk thermodynamic quantities are confronted with the lattice quantum chromodynamics simulation results at zero as well as at finite baryon chemical potential. We further estimate the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients of hot and dense hadronic matter within the ambit of this interacting hadron resonance gas model.

## Key claim

The repulsive mean-field hadron resonance gas model (RMFHRG) can reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics up to temperatures of about 190 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential, but the interaction measure (epsilon-3P)/T^4 is poorly reproduced above T=145 MeV, indicating that heavy Hagedorn states missing from the model contribute significantly to the energy density.

## Method

The authors construct a hadron resonance gas model including all known hadrons and resonances up to a mass cutoff of 2.25 GeV, with attractive interactions accounted for by including all zero-width resonances. Repulsive interactions are incorporated via density-dependent mean-field potentials, with separate repulsion parameters for baryons (K_B = 450 MeV fm^3) and mesons (K_M = 50 MeV fm^3). The shear and bulk viscosity coefficients are then computed using the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, with thermally averaged cross sections derived from the mean-field parameters via the Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- Baryon repulsion parameter: K_B = 450 MeV fm^3
- Meson repulsion parameter: K_M = 50 MeV fm^3
- Hadron mass cutoff: Lambda = 2.25 GeV
- Chiral crossover temperature from lattice QCD: T_c ~ 156 MeV
- RMFHRG pressure agrees with lattice QCD up to T ~ 190 MeV at mu=0 (ideal HRG deviates above T ~ 160 MeV)
- Speed of sound minimum at T = 155 MeV for mu=0 and at T = 140 MeV for mu=300 MeV
- eta/s approaches the KSS bound (1/(4pi)) at T ~ 170 MeV
- Freeze-out parametrization constants: T_10 = -34.4 MeV, T_20 = 30.9 MeV/GeV, T_30 = -176.8 GeV MeV, T_0^lim = 161.5 MeV, a_0 = 1481.6 MeV, b_0 = 0.365 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of repulsive interactions in the hadron resonance gas model, previously applied to baryon fluctuations by Huovinen (2017), to include both mesons and baryons and to compute transport properties. The approach differs from Walecka-type mean-field models because the repulsive interactions are present even at zero baryon density. The results enable improved estimates of shear and bulk viscosities for use in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and the authors compare their transport coefficients with prior calculations using the excluded volume HRG model (Kadam 2015) and the scaled hadron mass and coupling model (Khvorostukhin 2010).
