# Energy-dependent Lorentz covariant parameterization of the NN interaction between 50 and 200 MeV

> For laboratory kinetic energies between 50 and 200 MeV, we focus on generating an energy-dependent Lorentz covariant parameterization of the on-shell nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering amplitudes in terms of a number of Yukawa-type meson exchanges in first-order Born approximation. This parameterization provides a good description of NN scattering observables in the energy range of interest, and can also be extrapolated to energies between 40 and 300 MeV.

# Energy-dependent Lorentz covariant parameterization of the NN interaction between 50 and 200 MeV

## Key claim
The authors present an energy-dependent Lorentz covariant parameterization of on-shell nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering amplitudes using Yukawa-type meson exchanges in first-order Born approximation, which provides a good description of NN scattering observables between 50 and 200 MeV laboratory kinetic energy and can be extrapolated to 40–300 MeV. The key advance over prior work is that the coupling constants follow an exponential energy-dependence, avoiding the non-systematic and unphysical trends that plagued earlier separate fits at discrete energies.

## Method
The authors fit the relativistic Horowitz-Love-Franey (HLF) model parameters to the empirical SP05 (Spring 2005) NN scattering amplitudes from the SAID facility, using 10 meson exchanges (sigma, omega, rho, pi, eta, delta, plus tensor and axial-vector mesons) with complex coupling constants. The nonrelativistic Bystricky amplitudes (a, b, c, d, e) for both T_NN = 0 and T_NN = 1 isospin channels were fitted simultaneously at 7 laboratory kinetic energies from 50 to 200 MeV in 25 MeV steps and centre-of-mass scattering angles from 5 to 175 degrees in 5 degree steps, using a Levenberg-Marquardt minimization of chi-squared. The coupling constants were parameterized with an exponential energy-dependence function, while meson masses were fixed at experimental values for pi, omega, and rho mesons.

## Key numbers
- Fits performed at 7 energies: 50, 75, 100, 125, 150, 175, and 200 MeV laboratory kinetic energy
- Centre-of-mass scattering angles from 5 to 175 degrees in 5 degree steps (35 angles per energy)
- Total data points per real or imaginary fit: 2450 (7 energies x 35 angles x 5 amplitudes x 2 isospins)
- Minimum chi-squared values: 10.203 for real amplitudes, 10.798 for imaginary amplitudes (relative chi-squared obtained by dividing by 2450)
- After reweighting amplitude a for T_NN=0, real chi-squared became 16.657
- Pion mass fixed at 138 MeV, omega mass at 782 MeV, rho mass at 770 MeV
- Real scalar sigma coupling: g0^2 = -8.379, with cutoff Lambda = 965.23 MeV
- Real pseudoscalar pion coupling: g0^2 = 11.95, with cutoff Lambda = 678.44 MeV
- Extrapolation range: 40 to 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Horowitz-Love-Franey (HLF) model, originally parameterized at discrete energies of 135, 200, and 400 MeV by Horowitz (1985) and Murdock (1987), and later given energy-dependent forms by Maxwell (1996, 1998) for 200–500 MeV and 500–800 MeV ranges. The present parameterization fills the gap below 200 MeV, motivated by planned experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities (RIKEN, GSI) studying proton-induced knockout reactions on exotic nuclei at energies below 200 MeV per nucleon. The resulting interaction is intended for use in the relativistic t-rho folding procedure to generate microscopic optical potentials for elastic proton scattering from unstable nuclei, where Pauli blocking and nuclear medium modifications will need to be incorporated in future work.
