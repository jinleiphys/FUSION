# Matching collinear factorization with color-glass condensate for inclusive and exclusive deep inelastic scattering

> Collinear factorization and color-glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory are generally treated as separate approaches for calculating scattering amplitudes, valid in different kinematic regimes. For deep inelastic scattering at high photon virtuality and high center-of-mass energy, however, both of these approaches should be applicable. By expressing collinear parton distributions and generalized parton distributions in the shockwave approximation, we show that the resulting collinear-factorization amplitudes exactly reproduce the large-$Q^2$ expansion of CGC amplitudes for inclusive deep inelastic scattering, deeply virtual Compton scattering, and deeply virtual meson production. The matching holds directly at the amplitude level and includes both logarithmically enhanced and finite contributions. Our results establish the consistency between collinear factorization and the CGC in their common region of validity, clarify the origin of large momentum logarithms within the CGC framework, and provide a path toward combining high-energy and collinear evolution in a unified description of hadronic structure at small $x$ and large momentum scales.

## Key claim
By expressing collinear parton distributions and generalized parton distributions in the shockwave approximation, the authors show that collinear-factorization amplitudes exactly reproduce the large-\(Q^2\) expansion of color-glass condensate (CGC) amplitudes for inclusive deep inelastic scattering, deeply virtual Compton scattering, and deeply virtual meson production, with the matching holding directly at the amplitude level including both logarithmically enhanced and finite contributions.

## Method
The authors write collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the shockwave approximation, where the nonperturbative degrees of freedom correspond to CGC Wilson lines. They then expand the CGC scattering amplitudes in the limit of large photon virtuality \(Q^2\), keeping only the leading-twist contribution, and compare the resulting expressions to those obtained directly from collinear factorization using the shockwave-limit parton distributions. The equivalence is demonstrated for three processes: inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS), deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), and deeply virtual meson production (DVMP).

## Key numbers
- The gluon GPD in the shockwave limit is given by \(F^g(\Deltat, x,\xi) = \frac{8 N_c}{\alpha_s} \sqrt{1 - \xi^2} \int d^2\mathbf{k}_t \, \widetilde{N}_{\lambda\lambda'}(\mathbf{k}_t + \frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\Delta}_t, -\mathbf{k}_t + \frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\Delta}_t)\).
- The quark PDF after renormalization in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme is \(x f^q(x, \mu_R) = \frac{4 N_c}{3\pi} \int d^2\mathbf{k}_t \, \frac{1}{2}\sum_\lambda \widetilde{N}_{\lambda\lambda}(-\mathbf{k}_t,\mathbf{k}_t) \left[ \log(\mu_R^2/\mathbf{k}_t^2) - \frac{1}{3} \right]\).
- For inclusive DIS, the structure function \(F_2\) in the shockwave limit becomes \(F_2 = \frac{8 N_c}{3\pi} \sum_f e_f^2 \int d^2\mathbf{k}_t \, \sum_\lambda \frac{1}{2} \widetilde{N}_{\lambda\lambda}(\mathbf{k}_t,-\mathbf{k}_t) \left[ \log(Q^2/\mathbf{k}_t^2) + \frac{1}{6} \right]\).
- For inclusive DIS, the longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) in the shockwave limit becomes \(F_L = \frac{8 N_c}{3\pi} \sum_f e_f^2 \int d^2\mathbf{k}_t \, \sum_\lambda \frac{1}{2} \widetilde{N}_{\lambda\lambda}(\mathbf{k}_t,-\mathbf{k}_t)\).
- The leading-twist expansion of the CGC DVMP amplitude uses \(\frac{z\bar{z} Q^2 - \mathbf{k}_t^2}{(\mathbf{k}_t^2 + z\bar{z} Q^2)^3} = \frac{1}{[z\bar{z} Q^2]^2} + \mathcal{O}(\mathbf{k}_t^2/Q^2)\).

## Context
This work extends previous matching efforts between collinear factorization and CGC, which were established at the cross-section level for transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization by Xiao (2017), Taels (2022), and Caucal (2022-2025), and for collinear factorization by Stasto (2014), Altinoluk (2014), Watanabe (2015), Hatta (2017), and Boussarie (2023). The key advance is demonstrating the matching directly at the amplitude level for inclusive DIS, DVCS, and DVMP, using parton distributions calculated in the shockwave limit by Bhattacharya (2025). This enables a path toward combining high-energy and collinear evolution in a unified description of hadronic structure at small \(x\) and large momentum scales.
