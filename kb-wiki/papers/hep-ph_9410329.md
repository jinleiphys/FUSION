# Back-reaction in a cylinder

> A system is studied in which initially a strong classical electric field exists within an infinitely-long cylinder and no charges are present. Subsequently, within the cylinder, pairs of charged particles tunnel out from the vacuum and the current produced through their acceleration by the field acts back on the field, setting up plasma oscillations. This yields a rough model of phenomena that may occur in the pre-equilibrium formation phase of a quark-gluon plasma. In an infinite volume, this back-reaction has been studied in a field-theory description, and it has been found that the results of a full calculation of this sort are well represented in a much simpler transport formalism. It is the purpose here to explore that comparison for a situation involving a cylindrical volume of given radius.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether the simpler transport formalism can replace the full field-theory calculation for back-reaction in a finite cylindrical volume, finding that the close quantitative agreement seen in infinite volume is lost, but a rough qualitative correspondence holds for quantities averaged over the transverse direction.

## Method

The author develops a field-theory formalism for scalar QED in an infinitely long cylinder of radius R, solving coupled Klein-Gordon and Maxwell equations with mode amplitudes expanded in Fourier-Bessel functions, and implements adiabatic regularization via a momentum cutoff and charge renormalization. Separately, a transport formalism is constructed using a Vlasov-type equation with a Schwinger source term for pair production, solved with a Lax method for the partial differential equation and Fourier-Bessel decomposition for the electromagnetic fields. Both approaches are solved numerically and compared for transversely-integrated and on-axis current and electric field values.

## Key numbers

- For unrenormalized charge e^2 = 10, initial on-axis electric field e(b=0, t=0) = 6, and cylinder radii R = 2 and 5, the transversely-integrated electric field shows similar behavior between field theory and transport, but the on-axis current differs by nearly two orders of magnitude
- For e^2 = 4, initial field e(b=0, t=0) = 7, and R = 2 and 5, field-theory and transport results eventually drift out of phase over times up to t = 30
- Numerical parameters: typically 500 longitudinal momentum points, n_max ~ 20 Fourier-Bessel components, time step dt ~ 10^{-4} m^{-1}, with computation times of several weeks on 100-MHz or 150-MHz processors
- For R = 10, the agreement between field theory and transport begins to break down, and the calculation becomes unreliable because n_max ≤ 20 limits the transverse momentum coverage to n_max*pi/R

## Context

This work extends the infinite-volume back-reaction studies of Kluger, Eisenberg, Svetitsky, Cooper, and Mottola (1991-1993) to a finite cylindrical geometry, motivated by modeling the pre-equilibrium formation phase of a quark-gluon plasma. The paper finds that the close quantitative agreement between field theory and transport formalism found in infinite volume is degraded in finite volume, but a rough qualitative correspondence remains for transversely-averaged quantities, suggesting that transport methods may still be useful for practical studies of parton production in finite systems.
