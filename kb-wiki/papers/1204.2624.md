# Highly-anisotropic hydrodynamics in 3+1 space-time dimensions

> Recently formulated model of highly-anisotropic and strongly dissipative hydrodynamics is used in 3+1 dimensions to study behavior of matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We search for possible effects of the initial high anisotropy of pressure on the final soft-hadronic observables. We find that by appropriate adjustment of the initial energy density and/or the initial pseudorapidity distributions, the effects of the initial anisotropy of pressure may be easily compensated and the final hadronic observables become insensitive to early dynamics. Our results indicate that the early thermalization assumption is not necessary to describe hadronic data, in particular, to reproduce the measured elliptic flow v_2. The complete thermalization of matter (local equilibration) may take place only at the times of about 1-2 fm/c, in agreement with the results of microscopic models.

## Key claim

By appropriately adjusting the initial energy density and/or the initial pseudorapidity distributions, the effects of the initial anisotropy of pressure on final soft-hadronic observables can be compensated, making the final observables insensitive to early dynamics. This indicates that the early thermalization assumption is not necessary to describe hadronic data, and complete local equilibration may occur only at times of about 1–2 fm/c.

## Method

The authors used the (3+1)D version of their highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics model (ADHYDRO), which couples energy-momentum conservation with an entropy production equation. The system is closed by a generalized equation of state relating energy density, transverse pressure, longitudinal pressure, entropy density, and an anisotropy parameter x, together with a postulated entropy source term Σ = (1−√x)² σ / (√x τ_eq). Initial conditions were set at τ₀ = 0.25 fm for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, using a tilted source density profile, and final observables were computed via the Cooper-Frye formalism with Monte-Carlo hadronization in THERMINATOR.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ₀ = 0.25 fm used for all calculations.
- For the reference case (x₀ = 1.0, τ_eq = 0.25 fm), initial central energy density ε_i = 107.5 GeV/fm³ yields theoretical charged multiplicity N_ch^theor = 5020, compared to experimental N_ch^exp = 5060 ± 250 for centrality 0–6%.
- For x₀ = 100 and τ_eq = 1.0 fm, ε_i was reduced to 41.8 GeV/fm³ (from 48.8 GeV/fm³) and Δη changed to 1.5 to match final multiplicity.
- Freeze-out entropy density σ_f = 1.79/fm³, corresponding to freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV.
- Three values of initial anisotropy parameter x₀ were used: 100, 1.0, and 0.032.
- Three values of time-scale parameter τ_eq were used: 0.25 fm, 0.5 fm, and 1.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier (2+1)D ADHYDRO framework (Ryblewski and Florkowski, 2010–2011) to full 3+1 dimensions, and is related to the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach developed independently by Martinez and Strickland (2010–2012). The results contradict the necessity of extremely fast thermalization (fractions of a fm/c) assumed in perfect-fluid hydrodynamics, and instead support the timescales of 1–2 fm/c predicted by microscopic models. The tilted source initial condition follows the work of Bozek and Wyskiel (2010). This enables future studies where the early pre-equilibrium phase can be treated without assuming immediate local equilibrium.
