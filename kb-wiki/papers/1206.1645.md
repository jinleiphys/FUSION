# A Role of the Quasiparticle in the Conservation of the Fermion Number

> The role of the "quasiparticle" introduced in last paper by the present authors is discussed. It serves as a guide for the deformation of the Cooper-pair in the BCS-Bogoliubov approach. As an illustrative example, a certain case is treated. This case produces the su(1,1)-like behavior in the su(2)-algebraic many-fermion system.

# A Role of the Quasiparticle in the Conservation of the Fermion Number

## Key claim

The "quasiparticle" operator introduced in the authors' prior work (Tsue et al., 2011) serves as a guide for deforming the Cooper-pair in the BCS-Bogoliubov approach, but only within the restricted space of fixed su(2) spin magnitude s = Omega_0; the deformation produces an su(1,1)-like algebraic behavior rather than the standard su(2) structure, and the resulting state is not intended to describe superconductivity directly.

## Method

The authors start from the BCS-Bogoliubov condensed Cooper-pair state and the "quasiparticle" operators defined in their prior paper (I). They replace the standard BCS condition (S_- acting on the state gives z times (Omega_0 - S_0)) with a modified condition where S_- acting on the state gives z times the square root of (Omega_0 - S_0)(Omega_0 + S_0 + 1). This modified condition is solved explicitly for the state coefficients in the basis of fixed-s su(2) states, yielding a normalized superposition with a geometric series structure. The resulting operators (T_+, T_-, T_0) are shown to satisfy commutation relations that reduce to the su(1,1) algebra when the boundary term involving the highest-weight state |2Omega_0> is neglected.

## Key numbers

- For the case N/2 = Omega_0 (half-filling), the expectation values of the pairing Hamiltonian H = -G S_+ S_- are: E_exact = -G Omega_0(Omega_0+1), E_BCS = E_exact + 2G Omega_0, and E_present = E_exact + (G/3) * Omega_0(Omega_0+1).
- The average fermion number N/2 as a function of |z|^2 satisfies N(0)/2 = 0, N(1)/2 = Omega_0, and N(infinity)/2 = 2 Omega_0.
- The normalization factor Gamma for the deformed state is Gamma = (1 - (|z|^2)^(2Omega_0+1))/(1 - |z|^2) for |z|^2 != 1, and Gamma = 2Omega_0+1 for |z|^2 = 1.
- The expectation value of the operator T_+ is given by T_+ = e^(i theta) * (1/sqrt(|z|^2)) * (N(|z|^2)/2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior paper (Tsue et al., 2011, referred to as (I)) which introduced the "quasiparticle" operator that conserves fermion number. It contrasts with the standard BCS-Bogoliubov approach by replacing the linear condition on S_- with a square-root condition, producing an su(1,1)-like algebraic structure previously studied by the same authors with Kuriyama in many-boson systems under the name "pseudo su(1,1)-algebra." The deformation is motivated by the possibility of describing thermal properties and dissipation for many-fermion systems, following the ideas of Celeghini et al. and the thermo field dynamics formalism of Umezawa et al., rather than superconductivity directly. The concrete treatment of such applications is left as a future problem.
