# Azimuthal asymmetries in unpolarized Drell-Yan events on nuclear targets

> We show that for Drell-Yan events by unpolarized hadronic projectiles and nuclear targets, azimuthal asymmetries can arise from the nuclear distortion of the hadronic projectile wave function, typically a spin-orbit effect occurring on the nuclear surface. The asymmetry depends on quantities that enter also the spin asymmetry in the corresponding Drell-Yan event on polarized free nucleonic targets. Hence, this study can be of help in exploring the spin structure of the nucleon, in particular the transverse spin distribution of partons inside the proton. All arguments can be extended also to antinucleon projectiles and, consequently, apply to possible future measurements involving nuclear targets at the foreseen HESR ring at GSI.

## Key claim

Azimuthal asymmetries in unpolarized Drell-Yan events on nuclear targets can arise from nuclear distortion of the hadronic projectile wave function via spin-orbit effects on the nuclear surface, even when no asymmetry would be produced with a free proton target; however, this requires that the corresponding Drell-Yan process with a polarized projectile on a free proton target has a non-vanishing transverse spin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors model the projectile propagation through the nucleus using an eikonal approximation with a complex momentum, where the imaginary part represents nuclear damping. They incorporate spin-orbit effects by adding a transverse component to the complex momentum that depends on the polarization state of the projectile. The scattering amplitude is expanded to first order in the damping parameters, and the resulting cross sections for different polarization states are combined to construct the azimuthal asymmetry observable for unpolarized collisions on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear damping parameter p_I is estimated at approximately 50 MeV/c, based on reproducing NE18 data for the 12C(e,e'p) reaction where the outgoing proton flux at p = 1.4 GeV/c is reduced by 40%.
- The ratio r_I (relative size of asymmetry from nuclear damping compared to genuine polarized Drell-Yan asymmetry) is estimated in the range 5-10%, based on p_I ~ 50 MeV/c and a hadron size scale R_h ~ 0.2-0.5 fm (corresponding to p_h ~ 0.4-1 GeV/c).
- The spin-orbit ratio r_so is estimated in the range 4-10%, based on nuclear analyzing power A_N measurements showing 10% at 10 GeV decreasing to 2-4% at 20 GeV for proton-12C systems.
- The center-of-mass energy range considered for the HESR ring at GSI is 5 to approximately 14 GeV, with antiproton beam energy of 15 GeV or proton beam energy around 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of azimuthal asymmetries observed in Drell-Yan experiments on nuclear targets (Falciano 1986, Guanziroli 1987, Conway 1989, Anassontzis 1987), where the Lam-Tung sum rule (Lam 1980) is violated with cos2phi coefficients nu ~ 30%. The authors propose that nuclear distortion effects can mimic or contribute to the same asymmetry that at the partonic level is described by the Boer-Mulders function h_1^perp (Boer 1999). This mechanism could be relevant for future antiproton-nucleus measurements at the HESR ring at GSI, and establishes a duality between hadronic and partonic interpretations of transverse spin effects.
