# Collective flow and hydrodynamics in large and small systems at the LHC

> In this article, we briefly review the recent progress on collective flow and hydrodynamics in large and small systems at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), which includes the following topics: extracting the QGP viscosity from the flow data, initial state fluctuations and final state correlations in 2.76 A TeV Pb--Pb collisions, correlations and collective flow in high energy p--Pb and p--p collisions.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the state of collective flow and hydrodynamic modeling in both large (Pb-Pb) and small (p-Pb, p-p) collision systems at the LHC, arguing that the observation of similar flow-like correlations in high-multiplicity small systems suggests the development of collective expansion, but that more detailed investigations are still needed to fully understand the underlying physics.

## Method

The paper reviews viscous hydrodynamic and hybrid model frameworks (e.g., VISHNU, iEBE-VISHNU) that solve relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics with the Israel-Stewart formalism, using equations of state such as s95p-PCE and various initial condition models (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, IP-Glasma, EKRT, TRENTo, AMPT). It describes the extraction of anisotropic flow harmonics via multi-particle azimuthal correlation methods, including the Q-cumulant method and the generic framework for evaluating all multi-particle correlations with corrections for non-uniform acceptance and efficiency.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 A TeV are the primary large-system reference energy discussed
- p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV show two-particle correlation structures surprisingly similar to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV with similar multiplicity cuts
- p-p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV and 13 TeV show flow-like observables with smaller magnitudes than in p-Pb
- The decoupling temperature T_dec for pure hydrodynamic simulations with s95p-PCE is generally set to 100-120 MeV
- The switching temperature T_sw for hybrid models is set to around 160 MeV
- The iEBE-VISHNU simulations for correlations between flow harmonics used 30000 CPU hours on the Tianhe-1A supercomputer

## Context

This review extends the line of work from RHIC-era discoveries of strong collective flow and the "perfect liquid" QGP (Gyulassy 2004, Muller 2006) to LHC-era studies of both large and small systems. It covers the extraction of QGP viscosity from flow data, initial state fluctuations and final state correlations in Pb-Pb collisions, and the unexpected observation of flow-like signals in p-Pb and p-p collisions that were originally intended as reference data. The paper enables future work by summarizing the current status of event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations and the open questions regarding the interpretation of small-system collectivity.
