# Nuclear structure properties of $^{193-200}$Hg isotopes within large-scale shell model calculations

> Large-scale shell-model calculations have been performed to study the nuclear structure properties of Hg isotopes with mass varying from $A=193$ to $A=200$. The shell-model calculations are carried out in the 50 $\leq Z \leq$ 82 and 82 $ \leq N \leq$ 126 model space using monopole-based truncation. We present detailed studies on low-energy excitation spectra, energy systematics, and collective properties of Hg isotopes, such as reduced transition probabilities, quadrupole, and magnetic moments along the isotopic chain. The evolution of wave function configurations with spin is analyzed in the case of even-$A$ Hg isotopes. The shell-model results are in reasonable agreement with the experimental data and predictions are made where experimental data are unavailable. The shapes of Hg isotopes are also investigated through the energy-surface plots.

# Nuclear structure properties of $^{193-200}$Hg isotopes within large-scale shell model calculations

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations using the KHHE interaction with monopole-based truncation can reproduce the low-energy excitation spectra, electromagnetic transition rates, and moments of Hg isotopes from $A=193$ to $A=200$ in reasonable agreement with experimental data, though the correct order of the $5^-$ and $7^-$ states is not reproduced in $^{194,196}$Hg, suggesting a limitation of the present interaction for those isotopes.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the 50 $\leq Z \leq$ 82 and 82 $\leq N \leq$ 126 model space using the KHHE effective interaction, which is based on the realistic Kuo-Herling interaction derived from the Hamada-Johnston potential. To manage the large $M$-scheme dimensions (up to $9.1 \times 10^7$ for $^{200}$Hg), the authors employed monopole-based truncation, selecting partitions of single-particle occupancies whose monopole Hamiltonian energies lie below a specified cutoff energy. No truncation was applied for $^{200}$Hg, while cutoff energies of 12 MeV were used for $^{198,199}$Hg and 10 MeV for the other isotopes.

## Key numbers

- $^{200}$Hg full $M$-scheme dimension: $9.1 \times 10^7$; monopole-based truncation at $E_\text{cutoff}=10$ MeV yields dimension $1.3 \times 10^7$ (approximately 1/7 of full space)
- Ground-state energy of $^{200}$Hg with monopole-based truncation at $E_\text{cutoff}=10$ MeV: -471.406 MeV with $1.4 \times 10^7$ dimension, compared to -471.081 MeV with conventional particle-hole truncation at $1.8 \times 10^7$ dimension
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ for $^{194}$Hg: 25.5 W.u. (experimental 30(2) W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ for $^{196}$Hg: 26.7 W.u. (experimental 36(7) W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ for $^{198}$Hg: 28.6 W.u. (experimental 28(4) W.u.)
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ for $^{200}$Hg: 26.9 W.u. (experimental 26(2) W.u.)
- $B(E2; 10^+_1 \to 8^+_1)$ for $^{200}$Hg: 0.6 W.u. (experimental not available), indicating a sharp configuration change due to $\nu 0i_{13/2}$ subshell closure
- $R_{4/2} = E(4^+)/E(2^+)$ ratios from shell model lie within approximately 2.25 to 2.45, compared to experimental values of 2.45 to 2.55
- $B_{4/2} = B(E2; 4^+_1 \to 2^+_1)/B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ ratio for $^{194}$Hg: 0.63, indicating a configuration change between $J=2$ and $J=4$; for $^{196-200}$Hg the ratios lie between 1.1 and 1.4

## Context

This work extends shell-model calculations to Hg isotopes below $A=200$, complementing prior studies on $^{206-208}$Hg by Suzuki et al. and $^{201-206}$Hg by Yoshinaga et al. using the same model space and interaction. The results are compared with experimental data from Olaizola et al. (2019) and Suman et al. (2021), as well as with Interacting Boson Model calculations with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) by Nomura et al. The calculations enable systematic testing of the KHHE effective interaction in this mass region and provide predictions for electromagnetic transition strengths and moments where experimental data are unavailable, particularly for $^{193,195}$Hg and for high-spin states in the even-even isotopes.
