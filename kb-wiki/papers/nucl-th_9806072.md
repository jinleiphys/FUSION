# Anomalous Band Structure in Odd-Odd Nuclei with the Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction

> We perform shell model calculations in odd-odd nuclei using a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and single-particle splittings chosen so as to obtain the SU(3) results. Elliott had shown that such an interaction gives rotational bands for which the energies go as I(I+1). This is certainly true for eve-even and for odd-even or even-odd nuclei with $K\neq 1/2$. We have looked at odd-odd nuclei e.g $^{22}Na$ and found somewhat different behaviour. In $^{22}Na$ the $I=1^+_1 T=0$ and $I=0^+_1 T=1$ states are degenerate, and a rotational band built on the $I=0^+_1 T=1$ state behaves in a normal fashion. For the $I+1^+_1 T=0$ band however, we find that the energy is given by $E(I)-E(1^+_1)=AI(I+1)$. This differs from the `normal' behaviour which would be $E(I)-E(1^+_1)=AI(I+1)-2A$.

# Anomalous Band Structure in Odd-Odd Nuclei with the Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction

## Key claim

For the T=0 band in the odd-odd nucleus 22Na, the energy spectrum follows E(I)-E(1+1)=AI(I+1) rather than the normal rotational formula E(I)-E(1+1)=AI(I+1)-2A, meaning the I=1 state is anomalously low by 2A compared to the expected rotational bandhead energy.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed with all possible configurations in a given major shell using the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction ∑Q(i)·Q(j) in r-space, with single-particle splittings chosen to reproduce Elliott's SU(3) results. For the 1s-0d shell, the splitting was set to ε0d-ε1s=18χ̄, and for the 1p-0f shell, ε0f-ε1p=30χ̄, where χ̄=5b4χ/32π. The calculations were applied to 19F, 43Sc, and the odd-odd nucleus 22Na.

## Key numbers

- In 22Na, the I=1+1 T=0 and I=0+1 T=1 states are degenerate at 0.000 MeV
- The 2+ T=1 state in 22Na lies at 1.588 MeV, giving A = E(2+)/6 = 0.265 MeV
- The 2+ T=0 state in 22Na lies at 1.588 MeV, 3+ at 3.177 MeV, 4+ at 5.293 MeV, 5+ at 7.941 MeV, up to 10+ at 29.117 MeV
- B(E2: 1+1 T=0 → 2+1 T=0) = 34.9 e2fm4 with bare charges (ep=1, en=0); would increase to approximately 140 e2fm4 with effective charges (ep=1.5, en=0.5)
- In 19F, the K=1/2 band shows degenerate pairs (3/2+,5/2+) at 1.588 MeV, (7/2+,9/2+) at 5.295 MeV, consistent with decoupling parameter a=+1
- In 43Sc, the K=1/2 band shows degenerate pairs (1/2+,3/2+) at 0 MeV, (5/2+,7/2+) at 0.679 MeV, consistent with decoupling parameter a=-1

## Context

This work extends Elliott's SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) for rotational bands in even-even and odd-A nuclei to the case of odd-odd nuclei, where the authors find a deviation from the standard I(I+1) rotational formula. The results for K=1/2 bands in 19F and 43Sc reproduce the asymptotic Nilsson wave function predictions noted by Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The anomalous band structure in 22Na suggests that the rotational model formula requires an additional term for odd-odd nuclei to be consistent with SU(3) results, and the degeneracy pattern indicates the presence of two overlapping bands: a K=2 band and a band with states of angular momentum 1,2,4,6,8 whose energies follow BL(L+1) rather than AI(I+1).
