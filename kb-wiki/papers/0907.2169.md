# A transport calculation with an embedded (3+1)d hydrodynamic evolution: Elliptic flow as a function of transverse momentum at SPS energies

> The transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow of pions calculated in a full (3+1)d Boltzmann approach with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage for heavy ion reactions for CERN-SPS energies is discussed in the context of the experimental data. At higher SPS energies, where the pure transport calculation cannot account for the high elliptic flow values, the smaller mean free path in the hydrodynamic evolution leads to larger elliptic flow. Due to the more realistic initial conditions and the incorporated hadronic rescattering the results are in line with the experimental data. Within this integrated dynamical approach different equations of state are applied without adjusting the initial state and the freeze-out conditions. We employ a hadron gas equation of state to investigate the differences in the dynamics and viscosity effects, a chiral equation of state with a moderate first order phase transition and a critical endpoint and a bag model equation of state with a large latent heat. The elliptic flow results are shown to be rather insensitive to changes in the equation of state.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of pions at SPS energies is shown to be rather insensitive to changes in the equation of state (hadron gas, chiral with a moderate first-order phase transition, or bag model with large latent heat) when calculated in a hybrid transport-plus-hydrodynamic approach, contrary to the expectation that the equation of state should strongly influence the flow.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate the initial non-equilibrium stage and final-state hadronic rescatterings, with a full (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic evolution (using the SHASTA algorithm) embedded for the hot and dense stage. The hydrodynamic fields were initialized after the two nuclei passed through each other, and when the energy density dropped below approximately 730 MeV/fm³ in a transverse slice, the fields were converted to particles via the Cooper-Frye equation for further cascade evolution. Three different equations of state were employed without adjusting the initial state or freeze-out conditions: a hadron gas equation of state, a chiral equation of state with a moderate first-order phase transition and a critical endpoint, and a bag model equation of state with a large latent heat.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow (v₂) of pions as a function of transverse momentum was calculated for mid-central (b = 5-9 fm) Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of E_lab = 40A GeV and E_lab = 160A GeV.
- The transition from hydrodynamics to particles was triggered when the energy density dropped below approximately 730 MeV/fm³ (five times the ground state energy density).
- The hydrodynamic evolution was performed for transverse slices of thickness Δz = 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid transport-plus-hydrodynamic approach previously developed by the same group (Steinheimer 2007, Petersen 2008) to study elliptic flow at CERN-SPS energies, where pure transport calculations (UrQMD-2.3) underpredict the experimental data from the NA49 collaboration (Alt 2003). The finding that elliptic flow is insensitive to the equation of state is surprising because the expansion times differ significantly between the three scenarios, as estimated from the R_O/R_S ratio (Li 2008). This result enables future studies to focus on viscosity and non-equilibrium effects rather than the equation of state when modeling elliptic flow at these energies, and it suggests that the softer equation of state's effect on reducing flow is cancelled by longer expansion times.
