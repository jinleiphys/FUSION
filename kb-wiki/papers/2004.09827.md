# Fusion hindrance of $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba reaction

> We have already measured the ER excitation function of $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba reaction around the Coulomb barrier. While a detailed report of measurement of ER excitation function of $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba is available in literature, the present paper compares it with other channels forming the same compound nucleus. The experiment was performed at IUAC, New Delhi for lab energies from 189.3 to 234.4 MeV, where the ER cross sections show an abrupt fall beyond 160.03 MeV. The decrease in ER cross section is attributed to non compound nuclear reaction such as quasi-fission (QF). A comparison with statistical model code PACE 4 also shows significant deviation of the experimental data from theoretical predictions. When this reaction is compared with $^{86}$Kr + $^{100}$Mo and $^{64}$Ni + $^{122}$Sn forming the same compound nucleus $^{186}Pt^{*}$, the symmetric systems show more ERs than the asymmetric one and it is against the general trend. Preliminary study predict the influence of deformation and isospin asymmetry in the abrupt fall of ER cross section than that of the symmetric systems. A detailed theoretical analysis of the systems are in progress and will be published shortly.

# Fusion hindrance of $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba reaction

## Key claim

The evaporation residue (ER) cross section for the $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba reaction shows an abrupt fall beyond a center-of-mass energy of 160.03 MeV, attributed to quasi-fission (QF) rather than fission competition; this suppression is against the general trend because the asymmetric system should have higher fusion probability than the more symmetric systems forming the same compound nucleus, but instead shows the strongest hindrance.

## Method

The ER excitation function was measured at the Inter-University Accelerator Centre (IUAC), New Delhi for laboratory energies from 189.3 to 234.4 MeV. The experimental ER cross sections were compared with statistical model predictions from the code PACE 4 and with capture cross sections calculated using the coupled-channels code CCFULL. The survival probability was extracted as the ratio of experimental ER cross section to the CCFULL-predicted capture cross section.

## Key numbers

- ER cross section peaks at 26.6 ± 4.0 mb at a center-of-mass energy of 160.03 MeV (excitation energy 61.14 MeV), then falls to 18.1 ± 2.7 mb at 173.9 MeV (excitation energy 75.01 MeV)
- The overall error in the calculated ER cross section is approximately 15%
- The fission barrier for the compound nucleus $^{186}$Pt$^{*}$ is 16.3 MeV, which prevents significant fission decay
- The isospin asymmetry parameter $\Delta (N/Z)$ for $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba is 0.28, compared to 0 for $^{86}$Kr + $^{100}$Mo and 0.16 for $^{64}$Ni + $^{122}$Sn
- The deformation parameter $\beta_2$ of $^{48}$Ti is 0.28, the largest among the three compared systems

## Context

This work extends measurements of the $^{48}$Ti + $^{138}$Ba reaction previously reported by the same group (Rajesh et al., reference [12.a] in the paper) and compares it with the more symmetric systems $^{86}$Kr + $^{100}$Mo and $^{64}$Ni + $^{122}$Sn, all forming the same compound nucleus $^{186}$Pt$^{*}$. The finding that the asymmetric system shows more fusion hindrance than symmetric ones contradicts the general expectation based on charge product arguments. The authors identify deformation alignment effects (as studied by previous authors for deformed nuclei) and isospin asymmetry (as studied by references [19,20] in the paper) as the likely causes. A detailed theoretical analysis of all three systems is reported to be in progress.
