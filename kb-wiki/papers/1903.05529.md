# High Precision Measurement of Compton Scattering in the 5 GeV region

> The cross section of atomic electron Compton scattering $γ+ e \rightarrow γ^\prime + e^\prime $ was measured in the 4.40--5.475 GeV photon beam energy region by the {\em PrimEx} collaboration at Jefferson Lab with an accuracy of 2\% and less. The results are consistent with theoretical predictions that include next-to-leading order radiative corrections. The measurements provide the first high precision test of this elementary QED process at beam energies greater than 0.1 GeV.

## Key claim

The Compton scattering cross section on atomic electrons was measured in the 4.400-5.475 GeV photon beam energy region with a precision of 1.7-2.0% (depending on target and running period), providing the first high-precision test of this QED process above 0.1 GeV. The results are consistent with theoretical predictions that include next-to-leading order radiative corrections, though the abstract's stated "accuracy of 2% and less" glosses over the fact that the total systematic uncertainty ranged from 1.5% to 2.0% depending on target and running period.

## Method

The PrimEx collaboration measured Compton scattering on atomic electrons in solid carbon-12 and silicon-28 targets using the Hall B photon tagger at Jefferson Lab, which provided incident photon energies from 4.4 to 5.5 GeV. Forward-scattered photons and recoiling electrons were detected in the hybrid calorimeter (HyCal) located approximately 7 m downstream of the target, and event selection required coincidence timing, coplanarity, vertex reconstruction, and energy-momentum conservation criteria. The Compton yields were extracted by fitting elasticity distributions to Monte Carlo simulated signal (generated using the BASES/SPRING package with NLO radiative corrections) and background distributions, with backgrounds from accidental coincidences and pair production modeled separately.

## Key numbers

- Cross section at 4.40 GeV on 5% r.l. carbon-12: 0.3080 barns with statistical uncertainty 0.0014 barns and systematic uncertainty 0.0040 barns
- Cross section at 5.47 GeV on 5% r.l. carbon-12: 0.2528 barns with statistical uncertainty 0.0022 barns and systematic uncertainty 0.0043 barns
- Total systematic uncertainty: 1.5% for the first running period (carbon-12 5% r.l.), and 1.6-2.0% for the second running period depending on target (lowest for 5% r.l. carbon-12, highest for 10% r.l. silicon-28)
- Radiative corrections are about 4% of the total cross section for beam energy of approximately 5 GeV
- Photon beam energy range: 4.400-5.475 GeV (first running period: 4.9-5.5 GeV; second running period: 4.4-5.3 GeV)
- Energy detection threshold per particle in HyCal: 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous Compton scattering measurements, which were mostly below 0.1 GeV or had 10-15% precision in the 0.1-1.0 GeV range (Coensgen 1953, Gittlman 1968), and only one bubble-chamber experiment (Goshaw 1978) reached up to 5.0 GeV with 20-70% uncertainties. The measurements provide the first experimental test of next-to-leading order QED corrections to the Klein-Nishina formula at GeV energies, and the same experimental apparatus and analysis techniques were used to verify systematic uncertainties for the PrimEx pi0 lifetime measurement. The results enable future precision tests of QED at higher energies and validate the theoretical framework for radiative corrections in electromagnetic processes.
