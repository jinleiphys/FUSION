# Final State Interaction within the Bethe-Salpeter Approach in Charge Exchange pD->n(pp) process

> The exclusive charge exchange reaction pD->n(pp) at intermediate and high energies is studied within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. The final state interaction in the detected at zero-near excitation energy pp-pair is described by the 1S0 component of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Results of numerical calculations of polarization observables and differential cross-section persuade that, as in the non relativistic case, this reaction can be utilized for a relativistic deuteron tensor polarimeter and as a source of information about the elementary nucleon-nucleon charge exchange amplitude.

## Key claim
The Bethe-Salpeter formalism with a one-iteration approximation for the final state interaction in the \(^1S_0\) channel substantially improves agreement with experimental cross-section data for the \(pD \to n(pp)\) reaction, but the tensor analysing power \(T_{20}\) is less sensitive to these final state interaction effects. The reaction can serve as a relativistic deuteron tensor polarimeter at intermediate and high energies, provided the excitation energy of the \(pp\)-pair is kept below about 5 MeV and the transferred momentum is below about 0.3–0.4 GeV/c.

## Method
The exclusive charge exchange reaction \(pD \to n(pp)\) was analyzed within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter (BS) formalism. The final state interaction of the detected \(pp\)-pair at low excitation energy was described by the \(^1S_0\) component of the BS amplitude, solved via a one-iteration approximation using a non-relativistic Paris wave function as the trial function and a one-boson exchange kernel with pseudo-scalar isovector (\(\pi\)-meson) exchanges. The elementary charge-exchange amplitude was taken from the SAID partial wave analysis and the Nijmegen partial wave analysis, and the deuteron bound state was described by a numerical BS solution with a realistic one-boson exchange potential.

## Key numbers
- The excitation energy \(E_x\) of the \(pp\)-pair was considered in three bins: \(0 \leq E_x \leq 1\) MeV, \(1 \leq E_x \leq 4\) MeV, and \(4 \leq E_x \leq 8\) MeV.
- The one-iteration BS solution coincides with the exact solution up to relative momentum \(p \sim 0.6\)–\(0.7\) GeV/c.
- The mechanism is valid for excitation energy \(E_x < 5\) MeV and transferred momentum \(|\mathbf{q}| \le 0.3\)–\(0.4\) GeV/c.
- The cross section decreases substantially with increasing initial energy but remains large enough for experimental investigation at COSY and Dubna energies.
- The tensor analysing power \(T_{20}\) remains positive in a large kinematical region at relativistic energies, in contrast to lower energies where it changes sign.

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic spectator mechanism proposed by Bugg and Wilkin (1990) and earlier impulse approximation calculations by the same authors (Semikh et al., 2001) to a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter treatment including final state interaction. It confirms that the charge exchange reaction \(pD \to n(pp)\) can be used as a relativistic deuteron tensor polarimeter at facilities like COSY and Dubna, and provides a framework for extracting information about the elementary nucleon-nucleon charge exchange amplitude. The results enable future experimental studies of polarization observables in this reaction at intermediate and high energies.
