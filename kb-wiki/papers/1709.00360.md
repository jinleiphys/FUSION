# Update on extraction of transversity PDF from inclusive di-hadron production

> The transversity was recently extracted from data on the production of hadron pairs in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering. This analysis can be conveniently performed in the framework of collinear factorization where the elementary mechanism is represented by the simple product of transversity and of a suitable chiral-odd function describing the fragmentation of a transversely polarized parton into a pair of hadrons inside the same current jet. The same elementary mechanism was predicted long ago to generate an asymmetry in the azimuthal distribution of the hadron pairs when they are produced in proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton. Recently, the STAR Collaboration has observed this asymmetry. We analyze the impact of these data on our knowledge of transversity and we present its first preliminary extraction from a global fit of all data in hard processes with inclusive di-hadron production.

## Key claim

The transversity parton distribution function (PDF) can be extracted from a global fit of inclusive di-hadron production data from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), electron-positron annihilation, and proton-proton collisions, all within the collinear factorization framework; the preliminary results show that including the STAR proton-proton collision data resolves a previous tension in the valence down quark component, which no longer prematurely saturates the lower Soffer bound.

## Method

The analysis uses the collinear factorization framework where the elementary mechanism for the azimuthal asymmetry in di-hadron production is the product of the transversity PDF \(h_1\) and the chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation function (DiFF) \(H_1^{\sphericalangle}\). The DiFFs were previously extracted from BELLE \(e^+ e^-\) data for \((\pi^+ \pi^-)\) pairs (Courtoy 2012). The transversity was then fitted to HERMES and COMPASS SIDIS data for \((\pi^+ \pi^-)\) pairs (Bacchetta 2011, Bacchetta 2012, Radici 2015). For the global fit including STAR proton-proton collision data, the transversity is rewritten via its Mellin anti-transform to pre-compute a "reduced" asymmetry, speeding up the repeated computation of the convolution in the hadronic collision asymmetry. The fit uses 200 replicas of data, each altered by random noise within experimental uncertainty, and the parametric form of \(h_1\) satisfies the Soffer bound at any scale.

## Key numbers

* The SIDIS data cover the range \(0.0065 \leq x \leq 0.34\) for the transversity extraction.
* The transversity results are shown at \(Q^2 = 2.4\) GeV\(^2\).
* The previous Pavia fit (Radici 2015) used 100 replicas; the new preliminary global fit uses 200 replicas.
* The central 68% of replicas from the new global fit form the uncertainty band, compared to the 68% band from the previous fit.

## Context

This work extends the previous extraction of transversity from SIDIS and \(e^+ e^-\) data (Radici 2015) by including the recently observed asymmetry from the STAR Collaboration in \(p p^\uparrow \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- X\) (Radici 2016). The result is a higher degree of compatibility with the extraction of transversity from the Collins effect, as performed by Anselmino (2015) and by Kang (2015) in the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization framework. The ability to include hadronic collision data is unique to the di-hadron approach because the collinear factorization framework is valid, unlike the single-hadron Collins effect where factorization is broken in hadronic collisions. The improved precision on the transversity, particularly for the valence up quark, directly impacts the determination of the tensor charge, which is relevant for searches of physics beyond the Standard Model.
