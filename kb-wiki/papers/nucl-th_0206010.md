# Systematic Study of Elliptic Flow at RHIC Energy

> We study the elliptic flow systematically from SIS to RHIC energies in a realistic dynamical cascade model. We compile our results with the recent data from STAR and PHOBOS experiments on elliptic flow of charged particles in Au + Au collisions at RHIC energy. From the analysis of elliptic flow as a function of different dynamical variables such as transverse momenta, pseudorapidity and centrality at RHIC energy, we found a good fitting with data at 1.5 times a scaling factor to our simulation model, which characterizes that the model is required to have extra pressure generated from the subsequent parton scatterings. In energy dependence of elliptic flow, we observe a re-hardening nature at RHIC energies, which may probably signal the possible formation of quark-gluon plasma.

# Systematic Study of Elliptic Flow at RHIC Energy

## Key claim

The hadron-string cascade model JAM systematically underestimates elliptic flow at RHIC energies by a factor of approximately 1.5 to 2 at mid-rapidity, and this discrepancy can be resolved by scaling the model results by 1.5, which the authors interpret as evidence that extra pressure from parton scatterings (and possibly quark-gluon plasma formation) is needed beyond what the model includes.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic transport model) dynamical hadron-string cascade simulation to calculate elliptic flow (v2) for Au+Au collisions across beam energies from SIS to RHIC. They compared their calculated v2 as functions of transverse momentum, pseudorapidity, and centrality against experimental data from the STAR and PHOBOS collaborations at RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV), and also compared with hydrodynamic model results from Kolb et al. (1999, 2001).

## Key numbers

- JAM under-predicts mid-rapidity elliptic flow at RHIC by a factor of 1/2 of STAR data (factor of 2 discrepancy) for minimum bias events
- Multiplying JAM charged particle results by a factor of 1.5 gives excellent agreement with data up to p_t ~ 2 GeV/c
- At SPS energies, JAM gives reasonable description of data while hydrodynamic models overestimate by a factor of more than 2
- JAM underestimates v2 magnitudes by a factor of 1.2-1.5 for pions and charged particles in minimum bias events at RHIC
- The scaling factor of 1.5 applied to JAM results describes data well in a wide range of impact parameters within error bars for centrality dependence
- At pseudorapidity |eta| > 2 (fragmentation region), JAM results agree with PHOBOS data without scaling; at mid-rapidities the 1.5 scaling factor is needed

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of elliptic flow from lower energies (SIS, AGS, SPS) up to RHIC, building on prior dynamical transport model work by Sahu et al. (2000) at AGS energies and the RQMD model by Sorge (1995). The authors compare their results with hydrodynamic model calculations by Kolb et al. (1999, 2001), which assume complete local thermalization and QGP formation. The finding that JAM works well at SPS and in fragmentation regions but fails at RHIC mid-rapidity suggests that partonic interactions (not included in JAM) are essential for early thermalization, and that a full 3D hydrodynamical model with QGP initial conditions is needed at mid-rapidities. The paper enables future work incorporating partonic interactions into dynamical cascade models, as begun by Lin et al. (2002).
