# Monopoles, spectra of overlap fermions, and eta-prime meson in external magnetic fields

> The effects of external magnetic fields on monopoles, spectra of the overlap Dirac operator, instantons, and the mass of the eta-prime meson are examined by conducting lattice QCD simulations. The uniform external magnetic fields are applied to gauge field configurations with $N_{f}$ = 2 + 1 flavor quarks. The bare quark masses are tuned in order to obtain the physical values of the pion mass and of the ratio $\frac{m_{s}}{m_{u, d}}$. Standard configurations and configurations with applied external magnetic fields are generated in the color confinement and deconfinement phases. The intensity of external magnetic fields varies from $e|B|$ = 0.57 to 1.14 [GeV$^{2}$]. To examine the influence of external magnetic fields on monopoles, we first calculate the monopole density, measure the lengths of the monopole loops and compare them with the absolute value of the Polyakov loops. Next, using the generated configurations, we compute the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the overlap Dirac operator, which preserves exact chiral symmetry. To investigate how external magnetic fields affect the spectra of the overlap Dirac operator, we compute spectral densities, compare fluctuations of the eigenvalues of the overlap Dirac operator with the predictions of random matrix theory, and estimate the number of instantons and anti-instantons from the topological charges. In addition, we analyze smearing effects on these observables and chiral symmetry breaking. Finally, we calculate the decay constant of the pseudoscalar meson, the chiral condensate, and the square mass of the eta-prime meson using the eigenvalues and eigenvectors. We then extrapolate the numerical results in the chiral limit and demonstrate the effects of external magnetic fields on the extrapolation results. This article presents preliminary results.

## Key claim

External magnetic fields in the range \(e|B| = 0.57\) to 1.14 GeV\(^2\) produce measurable but preliminary effects on monopole properties, the spectral density of the overlap Dirac operator, and the eta-prime meson mass in lattice QCD with \(N_f = 2+1\) dynamical quarks at physical pion mass, though the small lattice volume (\(8^3 \times 16\) and \(8^3 \times 4\)) and limited statistics (60 configurations per parameter set) render the findings inconclusive.

## Method

The author used gauge configurations generated by the Pisa group with \(N_f = 2+1\) stout-improved staggered fermions at physical quark masses (\(m_\pi \sim 135\) MeV, \(m_s/m_{u,d} = 28.15\)) on a coarse lattice (\(a = 0.2457\) fm) at temperatures \(T = 50\) MeV (confinement) and \(T = 200\) MeV (deconfinement). Uniform external magnetic fields were applied along the \(z\)-direction with intensities \(e|B| = 0.00, 0.57, 1.14\) GeV\(^2\). The author computed approximately 400 eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the massless overlap Dirac operator per configuration, then analyzed monopole densities and loop lengths after maximal Abelian gauge fixing, spectral densities, nearest-neighbor spacing distributions, spectral rigidity compared to Gaussian random matrix theory, topological charges, and correlation functions for the pseudoscalar density to extract the eta-prime meson mass.

## Key numbers

* Monopole density at low temperature (\(T = 50\) MeV): \(\rho_m = 5.24(3) \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\) at \(e|B| = 0.00\), increasing to \(5.70(3) \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\) at \(e|B| = 1.14\) GeV\(^2\).
* Monopole density at high temperature (\(T = 200\) MeV): \(\rho_m = 4.16(5) \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\) at \(e|B| = 0.00\), decreasing to \(3.95(10) \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^3\) at \(e|B| = 1.14\) GeV\(^2\).
* Length of the longest monopole loop at low temperature: \(L^{\text{long}} = 185.4(1.2)\) fm at \(e|B| = 0.00\), increasing to \(205.9(1.1)\) fm at \(e|B| = 1.14\) GeV\(^2\).
* Length of the longest monopole loop at high temperature: \(L^{\text{long}} = 28.1(9)\) fm at \(e|B| = 0.00\), decreasing to \(23.6(9)\) fm at \(e|B| = 1.14\) GeV\(^2\).
* Mean absolute Polyakov loop at high temperature: \(\langle |P| \rangle = 0.116(2)\) at \(e|B| = 0.00\), increasing to \(0.134(2)\) at \(e|B| = 1.14\) GeV\(^2\).
* Lattice spacing from static potential: \(a = 0.220(12)\) fm for \(Bz = 0\) and \(Bz = 3\), and \(a = 0.249(13)\) fm for \(Bz = 6\), consistent with the interpolated value \(a = 0.2457\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Pisa-Dubna collaboration studies of magnetic catalysis in lattice QCD (Bali et al., 2012; Massimo et al., 2015) by examining topological observables, monopoles, instantons, and the eta-prime meson, using overlap fermions that preserve exact chiral symmetry, unlike the staggered fermions used in the original configuration generation. The preliminary results suggest that external magnetic fields reduce the eta-prime meson mass in the chiral limit and alter monopole loop structures oppositely in confinement versus deconfinement phases, but the small volume and limited statistics prevent definitive conclusions; future work with larger lattices and higher statistics is needed to confirm these trends and connect them to the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev, 2008).
