# Coupling between collective modes in the deformed $^{98}$Zr nucleus: Insights from consistent HFB+QRPA calculations with the Gogny interaction

> The Zirconium isotopes exhibit structural properties that present multiple challenges to nuclear theory. Investigations of the coupling present within isoscalar modes and within isovector modes are scarce but important for advancing our understanding of the microscopic picture of nuclei. To explore some of these underlying coupling features, and to test the predictive power of a state-of-the-art nuclear structure approach, we provide a detailed analysis of the properties of $^{90,96,98}$Zr. This region includes a benchmarking case and offers insights into nuclear deformation phenomena. To investigate the coupling between collective modes in deformed nuclei, we focused our analysis on the ground and excited-state properties of these isotopes, employing a consistent approach with the axially-symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) framework, both using the Gogny D1M force. This approach effectively describes both low-lying and giant-resonance states. We devoted special attention to the deformed $^{98}$Zr nucleus, where we confirm the existence of coupling between monopole and quadrupole excitations through the $K^π = 0^{+}$ QRPA components and demonstrate an analogous dipole-octupole coupling through the $K^π = 0^{-}$ and $K^π = 1^{-}$ components. Intrinsic transition densities and associated radial projections illustrate the coupling. Our work complements and extends earlier studies carried out using density-functional-based methods and notably, we included the complete Coulomb interaction also in the pairing fields, i.e. we treat terms exactly that are approximated in typical calculations that use the Gogny D1 and D2 interaction families.

## Key claim
The paper confirms the existence of coupling between monopole and quadrupole excitations in deformed $^{98}$Zr through $K^\pi = 0^+$ QRPA components and demonstrates an analogous dipole-octupole coupling through $K^\pi = 0^-$ and $K^\pi = 1^-$ components, using a consistent HFB+QRPA framework that treats the Coulomb interaction exactly in the pairing fields, a refinement over typical Gogny D1 and D2 family calculations.

## Method
The authors performed axially-symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for $^{90,96,98}$Zr using the Gogny D1M interaction in a cylindrical harmonic oscillator basis spanning 11 major shells, with the Coulomb interaction treated exactly in both mean-field and pairing fields. Excited states were computed via the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) using the same interaction and basis, solving for each $K^\pi$ component and employing angular-momentum restoration techniques to obtain electromagnetic transition probabilities and radial transition densities. The analysis focused on intrinsic transition densities and their radial projections to visualize the coupling between collective modes.

## Key numbers
- $^{90}$Zr QRPA first $0^+_1$ excited state energy: 1.658 MeV (experimental: 1.760 MeV)
- $^{90}$Zr QRPA first $2^+_1$ excited state energy: 2.725 MeV (experimental: 2.186 MeV)
- $^{90}$Zr QRPA ISGMR mean energy: 18.6 MeV over energy range [9,36] MeV (systematic formula: 17.9 MeV; experimental: 19.17$^{+0.21}_{-0.20}$ MeV from Gupta 2018)
- $^{90}$Zr QRPA IVGDR mean energy: 19.2 MeV over energy range [0,50] MeV (systematic formula: 16.7 MeV; experimental: 16.83$^{+0.04}_{-0.04}$ MeV from Berman 1967)
- $^{90}$Zr QRPA ISGQR mean energy: 15.5 MeV over energy range [9,36] MeV (systematic formula: 14.1 MeV; experimental: 14.64$^{+0.22}_{-0.21}$ MeV from Gupta 2018)
- $^{96}$Zr QRPA first $0^+_1$ excited state energy: 1.054 MeV (experimental: 1.581 MeV)
- $^{96}$Zr QRPA first $2^+_1$ excited state energy: 1.815 MeV (experimental: 1.750 MeV)
- $^{96}$Zr QRPA first $3^-_1$ excited state energy: 0.553 MeV (experimental: 1.897 MeV; note: without complete Coulomb treatment this state is at 1.605 MeV)

## Context
This work extends earlier density-functional-based studies of Zr isotopes, including QRPA calculations by Deloncle (2017) that used the D1M Gogny interaction but were limited to spherical $^{90-94}$Zr, and complements semi-phenomenological approaches such as the interacting boson model study by Ramos (2019). The inclusion of the complete Coulomb interaction in the pairing fields goes beyond typical Gogny D1 and D2 family calculations, where the Coulomb exchange contribution was approximated. The results enable future direct-reaction calculations by providing transition densities that can be folded with effective projectile-nucleon interactions for DWBA or coupled-channels calculations, and support interpretation of data from radioactive beam facilities.
