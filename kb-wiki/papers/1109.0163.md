# Pion scattering and electro-production on nucleons in the resonance region in chiral quark models

> Pion scattering and electro-production amplitudes have been computed in a coupled-channel framework incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states, based on the Cloudy Bag model. All relevant low-lying nucleon resonances in the P33, P11, and S11 partial waves have been covered, including the Delta(1232), the N*(1440), N*(1535), and N*(1650). Consistent results have been obtained for elastic and inelastic scattering (two-pion, eta-N, and K-Lambda channels), as well as for electro-production. The meson cloud has been shown to play a major role, in particular in electro-magnetic observables in the P33 and P11 channels.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a coupled-channel framework incorporating quasi-bound quark-model states based on the Cloudy Bag model can consistently reproduce pion scattering and electro-production data for low-lying nucleon resonances, but only when the meson cloud contributions are included; the pion cloud contributes approximately 45% to the magnetic dipole amplitude and strongly dominates the electric quadrupole amplitude in the Delta(1232) region.

## Method

The authors compute meson-baryon scattering K-matrix elements and electro-production amplitudes in a coupled-channel formalism using the Cloudy Bag Model with SU(3)-extended quark-meson vertices. The K-matrix elements are constructed from principal-value states that include free meson-baryon channels, bare three-quark states (nucleon, N*(1440), N*(1710) for P11; N*(1535), N*(1650) for S11), and meson-cloud contributions around different isobars, with the T-matrix obtained by solving Heitler's equation. For electro-production, the gamma-N channel is included and the amplitude is separated into resonant and non-resonant background contributions, with the electro-excitation of resonances described by helicity amplitudes containing both quark and pion contributions.

## Key numbers

- Pion cloud contributes approximately 45% to the magnetic dipole amplitude in the Delta(1232) region
- Bag radius R_bag = 0.83 fm used in the Cloudy Bag Model
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 76 MeV, chosen to reproduce the experimental g_piNN coupling
- Kaon decay constant f_K = 1.2 f_pi
- Eta decay constant f_eta = 1.2 f_pi or f_eta = f_pi
- Electro-production amplitudes computed for Q^2 up to approximately 3 GeV^2/c^2
- Calculations cover center-of-mass energies W up to approximately 1700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel approach from Sirca et al. (1996), which first demonstrated the importance of the pion cloud in electro-production of pions in the Delta(1232) region, and their later formalism from Golli et al. (2008) for meson-baryon scattering. The results enable improved understanding of the interplay between quark and meson degrees of freedom in nucleon resonances, though the over-estimation of pion-induced K+ production and under-estimation of photo-production amplitudes for the K-Lambda channel remain open questions requiring further investigation.
