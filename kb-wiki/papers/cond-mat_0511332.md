# Ground state energy of spin-1/2 fermions in the unitary limit

> We present lattice results for the ground state energy of a spin-1/2 fermion system in the unitary limit, where the effective range of the interaction is zero and the scattering length is infinite. We compute the ground state energy for a system of 6, 10, 14, 18, and 22 particles, with equal numbers of up and down spins in a periodic cube. We estimate that in the limit of large number of particles, the ground state energy is 0.25(3) times the ground state energy of the free Fermi system.

## Key claim

The ground state energy of a spin-1/2 fermion system in the unitary limit is estimated to be 0.25(3) times the free Fermi gas ground state energy in the large-particle-number limit, based on lattice simulations of systems with 6 to 22 particles (3 to 11 per spin component) that are free from the fermion sign problem.

## Method

The author performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations at zero temperature and fixed particle number using an auxiliary Hubbard-Stratonovich field to represent the attractive contact interaction, with the scattering length tuned to infinity. The method combines endpoint correlation function importance sampling with non-local hybrid Monte Carlo updating to compute the ground state energy from the large-Euclidean-time limit of the correlation function for systems of 6, 10, 14, 18, and 22 particles (equal numbers of up and down spins) in a periodic cube. The ratio of the interacting to free ground state energy, denoted xi, was extracted by fitting the time-dependent ratio xi_{N,N}(t) to an exponential asymptotic form at large Euclidean time.

## Key numbers

- For the large-N limit, the ratio of interacting to free ground state energy is estimated as xi = 0.25(3).
- Measured ratios for individual particle numbers: N=3 (6 particles): 0.19(2); N=5 (10 particles): 0.24(2); N=7 (14 particles): 0.28(2); N=9 (18 particles): 0.23(2); N=11 (22 particles): 0.25(2).
- For the two-particle (1,1) system, the dimensionless ground state energy mL^2 E_{1,1}^0 approaches the unitary limit value 4 pi^2 d_1 approximately equal to -3.7860, with lattice results within a few percent of this value.
- The interaction coefficient in the unitary limit is C^{phys} = -1.203 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-2} for the standard lattice action with spatial lattice spacing a = (50 MeV)^{-1} and temporal spacing a_t = (24 MeV)^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends lattice simulations of spin-1/2 fermions in the unitary limit (Wingate 2005, Bulgac 2005, Lee 2005) to zero temperature and fixed particle number, using a method free from the fermion sign problem that avoids systematic errors from nodal constraints present in fixed-node Green's function Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson 2003, Astrakharchik 2004), which gave xi values of 0.44(1) and 0.42(1) respectively. The result xi = 0.25(3) is consistent with the earlier bound 0.07 <= xi <= 0.42 from Lee (2005) and with the requirement that fixed-node values provide variational upper bounds, but disagrees with those fixed-node results. The method enables future studies of superfluidity, long-range order, and pairing gaps in the unitary limit.
