# Photons from Jet - Plasma Interaction in collisional energy loss scenario

> We calculate photons from jet plasma interaction in a collisional energy loss scenario. It is shown that the PHENIX photon data is well reproduced when photons from initial hard collisions are taken into account.

# Photons from Jet - Plasma Interaction in collisional energy loss scenario

## Key claim
The authors calculate that PHENIX photon data is well reproduced when photons from initial hard collisions are included, but only for the measured transverse momentum domain at RHIC where collisional energy loss plays a vital role; at high $p_T$ the data is only marginally reproduced because radiative energy loss (not included in this calculation) would further deplete the yield.

## Method
The authors compute photon production from jet-plasma interaction using exact expressions for the photon rate from Compton and annihilation processes, with thermal masses introduced to shield infrared singularities. The jet phase-space distribution is evolved via a Fokker-Planck equation incorporating drag and diffusion coefficients derived from collisional energy loss in the small-angle scattering limit, using hard thermal loop (HTL) corrected propagators. The space-time integrated transverse momentum distribution is obtained assuming longitudinal Bjorken expansion, with the initial jet production probability distribution taken from Moore (2005) and the temperature profile from the same reference.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature $T_i = 446$ MeV used for calculations at RHIC energies.
- Photon yield from jet-plasma interaction is depleted when collisional energy loss is included, compared to the case without energy loss.
- The total photon yield (jet-photon, initial hard collisions, bremsstrahlung, and thermal photons) reproduces PHENIX photon data well, though at high $p_T$ the data is only marginally reproduced.
- For Compton process the overall degeneracy factor $\mathcal{N}_i = 320/3$ when summing over u and d quarks; for annihilation process $\mathcal{N}_i = 20$.
- The collisional energy loss scales as $dE/dx \sim \alpha_s^2 T^2 C_R \ln(E/g^2 T)$ for jet energy $E \gg T$.

## Context
This work extends the jet-conversion photon calculations of DKS (unidentified reference) and Fries (unidentified reference) by including collisional energy loss of the jet in the medium, motivated by the observation that non-photonic single electron data from PHENIX (2006) show larger suppression than expected, leading to renewed interest in collisional energy loss for both heavy and light quarks as demonstrated by Abhee (2005) and Roy (2006). The approach uses the Fokker-Planck equation framework developed by Moore (2005) and Rapp (unidentified reference) for jet evolution in a thermal bath. The results enable future inclusion of radiative energy loss to improve agreement at high $p_T$, which the authors identify as necessary for a complete description.
