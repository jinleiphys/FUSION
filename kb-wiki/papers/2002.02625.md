# Transition properties of low-lying states in $^{28}$Si probed via inelastic proton and alpha scattering

> $0^+$, $1^-$, $2^+$, and $3^-$ excitations of $^{28}\textrm{Si}$ are investigated via proton and $α$ inelastic scattering off $^{28}\textrm{Si}$. The structure calculation of $^{28}\textrm{Si}$ is performed with the energy variation after total angular momentum and parity projections in the framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). As a result of the AMD calculation, the oblate ground and prolate bands, $0^+$ and $3^-$ excitations, and the $1^-$ and $3^-$ states of the $K^π=0^-$ band are obtained. Using the matter and transition densities of $^{28}\textrm{Si}$ obtained by AMD, microscopic coupled-channel calculations of proton and $α$ scattering off $^{28}\textrm{Si}$ are performed. The proton-$^{28}\textrm{Si}$ potentials in the reaction calculation are microscopically derived by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the AMD densities of $^{28}\textrm{Si}$. The $α$-$^{28}\textrm{Si}$ potentials are obtained by folding the nucleon-$^{28}\textrm{Si}$ potentials with an $α$ density. The calculation reasonably reproduces the observed elastic and inelastic cross sections of proton and $α$ scattering. Transition properties are discussed by combining the reaction analysis of proton and $α$ scattering and structure features such as transition strengths and form factors. The isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions are focused.

## Key claim

The authors use microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) densities to show that the transition properties of low-lying states in \(^{28}\)Si can be consistently described across proton and alpha inelastic scattering, but the assignment of the calculated \(1^-_\text{IS1}\) state to either the experimental \(1^-_1\) (8.95 MeV) or \(1^-_2\) (9.93 MeV) state remains ambiguous because both have similar isoscalar dipole transition strengths and form factors.

## Method

The structure of \(^{28}\)Si was calculated using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with energy variation after total angular momentum and parity projections (VAP), yielding wave functions for the oblate ground band, prolate band, \(0^+\) vibration, and \(K^\pi=0^-\) band states. Microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations of proton and alpha scattering were performed by folding the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix \(NN\) interaction with the AMD matter and transition densities of \(^{28}\)Si to construct nucleon-nucleus potentials; alpha-nucleus potentials were obtained by folding the nucleon-nucleus potentials with an alpha density. The theoretical transition densities were renormalized to fit experimental electric transition strengths and charge form factors before being used in the reaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- The AMD calculation gives a binding energy of 213.3 MeV for \(^{28}\)Si, which underestimates the experimental value of 236.53 MeV.
- The calculated \(B(E0;0^+_\text{vib}\to 0^+_1)\) is 4.0 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared with the experimental value of 4.71 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) from \((e,e')\) data; the renormalization factor is \(f_\text{tr}=1.08\).
- The calculated \(B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)\) is 46 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared with the experimental \(\gamma\)-decay value of 67(3) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\); the renormalization factor is \(f_\text{tr}=1.21\).
- The calculated \(B(E3;3^-_1\to 0^+_1)\) is 366 \(e^2\text{fm}^6\), compared with the experimental \(\gamma\)-decay value of 615(70) \(e^2\text{fm}^6\); the renormalization factor is \(f_\text{tr}=1.30\).
- The calculated \(B(\text{IS}1)/4\) for \(1^-_\text{IS1}\to 0^+_1\) is 34 \(e^2\text{fm}^6\), compared with the experimental \((e,e')\) value of 18.7 \(e^2\text{fm}^6\) for the \(1^-_1\) state; the renormalization factor is \(f_\text{tr}=0.75\).
- The calculated \(B(E0;0^+_\text{pro}\to 0^+_1)\) is 0.7 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\), with no experimental value available for the \(0^+_3\) state.
- The calculated \(B(E2;2^+_\text{pro}\to 0^+_1)\) is 0.03 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\), compared with the experimental value of 1.87(0.76) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) for the \(2^+_2\) state; the calculation underestimates this by about two orders of magnitude.
- The calculated \(B(E3;3^-_2\to 0^+_1)\) is 76 \(e^2\text{fm}^6\), compared with the experimental \((e,e')\) value of 78(20) \(e^2\text{fm}^6\); the renormalization factor is \(f_\text{tr}=1.40\).
- The point-proton rms radius of the ground state from experiment is \(R=3.010(24)\) fm; the AMD calculation gives a matter radius of 3.17 fm for the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) approach previously applied to \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O (Kanada-En'yo 2019) to \(^{28}\)Si, combining AMD structure calculations with folding-model reaction calculations that use the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix interaction (Amos 2000). It addresses the long-standing problem of shape coexistence in \(^{28}\)Si, where earlier mean-field calculations (Sagawa 2005, Win 2008, Lu 2011) failed to describe the prolate \(0^+_3\) band, and where previous \((p,p')\) analyses at 65 MeV (Kato 1985) and 180 MeV (Chen 1990) could not reproduce the \(1^-_1\) cross sections. The results enable future combined analyses of \((p,p')\), \((\alpha,\alpha')\), and \((e,e')\) data to resolve the assignment of \(1^-\) states and to probe the prolate band mixing, particularly for the \(2^+_2\) state where the observed form factor is two orders of magnitude larger than the pure prolate calculation predicts.
