# Critical Hadronization Pressure

> We discuss the bulk properties of QGP produced at RHIC obtained at time of hadronization. We argue that hadronization of quark--gluon plasma occurs at a critical pressure of 82 MeV/fm3, obtained for SPS energy range.

# Critical Hadronization Pressure

## Key claim

The authors argue that quark-gluon plasma (QGP) hadronization occurs at a critical pressure near 82 MeV/fm³, a value obtained from fits to SPS data and then imposed as an additional constraint when fitting RHIC data, rather than emerging freely from the RHIC fits themselves.

## Method

The authors apply the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) using the SHARE program to fit measured hadron yields from AGS, SPS, and RHIC heavy-ion collisions, allowing the light-quark and strangeness phase-space occupancy parameters γq and γs to differ from unity. They extract the thermal pressure and energy per hadron from the fitted fireball properties, and at RHIC impose the SPS-derived pressure value P = 82 ± 2 MeV/fm³ and energy per hadron E/hp = 0.75 ± 10% as additional constraints to obtain reliable fits.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization pressure P = 82 ± 2 MeV/fm³ = (158 MeV)⁴, obtained from SPS data and imposed at RHIC
- Energy density E/V → 500 MeV fm⁻³ = (250 MeV)⁴ for high-energy reactions
- Entropy density S/V → 3.4 fm⁻³ for high-energy reactions
- Temperature at RHIC: T = 140.5 ± 0.5 MeV (200 GeV), 140.2 ± 0.2 MeV (130 GeV), 142.9 ± 0.3 MeV (62.4 GeV)
- Light-quark occupancy γq = 1.54 ± 0.14 (200 GeV), 1.56 ± 0.031 (130 GeV), 1.53 ± 0.10 (62.4 GeV)
- Strangeness occupancy γs = 2.30 ± 0.40 (200 GeV), 2.32 ± 0.31 (130 GeV), 1.74 ± 0.22 (62.4 GeV)
- Baryon chemical potential μB = 27.1 MeV (200 GeV), 31.2 MeV (130 GeV), 65.9 MeV (62.4 GeV)
- Fireball volume dV/dy = 1156 ± 88 fm³ (200 GeV), 1172 ± 93 fm³ (130 GeV), 1089 ± 74 fm³ (62.4 GeV)
- Energy per primary hadron E/hp = 0.75 ± 0.075 GeV, constant across top SPS and RHIC energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Rafelski and Letessier, 2005, 2009) of hadronization conditions in heavy-ion collisions, finding that the hadronization pressure is remarkably constant across SPS and RHIC energies. The authors contrast their approach with other groups that force γq = 1, which they argue tests the hypothesis that QGP was not formed. The result that P ≈ 82 MeV/fm³ provides a first estimate of the vacuum pressure that confines color charge, analogous to how atmospheric pressure defines the boiling point of water. This enables future studies to use hadronization pressure as a universal condition for QGP breakup across different collision energies and systems.
