# On the momentum broadening of in-medium jet evolution using a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> Following the non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian formalism developed in our preceding work [Phys.Rev.D 104 (2021) 5, 056014], we investigate the momentum broadening of a quark jet inside a SU(3) colored medium. We perform the numerical simulation of the real-time jet evolution in Fock spaces of a single quark, a quark-gluon state, and coupled quark- and quark-gluon states at various jet momenta $p^+$ and medium densities. With the obtained jet light-front wavefunction, we extract the jet transverse momentum distribution, the quenching parameter, and the gluon emission rate. We analyze the dependence of momentum broadening on $p^+$, medium density, color configuration, spatial correlation, and medium-induced gluon emission. For comparison, we also derive analytically the expectation value of the transverse momentum of a quark-gluon state in any color configuration and in an arbitrary spatial distribution in the eikonal limit. This work can help understand jet momentum broadening in the non-eikonal regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the momentum broadening of a quark jet in a colored medium can be studied non-perturbatively using light-front Hamiltonian simulations beyond the eikonal approximation, but the quenching parameter $\hat q$ is suppressed at finite longitudinal momentum $p^+$ and finite number of uncorrelated medium layers $N_\eta$, contrary to the eikonal-limit expectation of linear growth.

## Method

The authors simulate real-time evolution of a quark jet in Fock spaces of single quark ($\ket{q}$), quark-gluon ($\ket{qg}$), and coupled $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ states using the time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) approach. The medium is modeled as a classical SU(3) color background field via the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, discretized into $N_\eta$ uncorrelated layers along the light-front time $x^+$. The jet light-front wavefunction is evolved numerically by decomposing the time-evolution operator into small steps, treating kinetic energy, gluon emission/absorption, and medium interaction terms separately. For comparison, the authors also derive analytically the expectation value of transverse momentum for a quark-gluon state in any color configuration in the eikonal limit using four-point Wilson line correlators.

## Key numbers

* The "bare" quenching parameter for a quark is $\hat q_0 = C_F g^4 \tilde{\mu}^2 / (4\pi)$, where $C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)$ and $N_c=3$; the full eikonal $\hat q_{Eik} = 4\pi \hat q_0 \mathcal{G}_2$, with $\mathcal{G}_2$ containing a logarithmic divergence dependent on the infrared regulator $m_g a_\perp/\pi$.
* The saturation scale on the lattice is $Q_s^2 = C_F (g^2 \tilde{\mu})^2 L_\eta / (2\pi^2)$, where $L_\eta$ is the medium thickness (total evolution time).
* The asymptotic saturation value of $\braket{p_\perp^2}$ on the finite lattice is approximately $(2/3)(\pi/a_\perp)^2$, where $a_\perp$ is the transverse lattice spacing and $\lambda_{UV} = \pi/a_\perp$ is the ultraviolet cutoff.
* The coupling constant is set to $g=1$ throughout the simulations.
* The medium IR regulator $m_g$ must satisfy $1/N_\perp \ll m_g a_\perp/\pi \ll Q_s a_\perp/\pi$ for proper lattice coverage.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian formalism (Li et al., Phys.Rev.D 104, 2021) from the $\ket{q}$ and $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ Fock sectors to a detailed study of momentum broadening, including the quenching parameter $\hat q$ and gluon emission rates. It contrasts with perturbative QCD-based approaches such as BDMPS-Z (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff; Zakharov), GLV (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev), AMY (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe), and higher-twist (Guo, Wang) formalisms by working at the amplitude level rather than probability level, enabling relaxation of eikonal and collinear radiation approximations. The analytical derivation of the quark-gluon state momentum broadening in the eikonal limit, using full color space with finite $N_c=3$, fills a gap in existing literature where large $N_c$ approximations or specific truncations were previously used (e.g., Apolinario et al., 2014). This work enables future quantum simulation implementations of jet evolution and provides a benchmark for non-eikonal studies of jet quenching.
