# Large-scale shell-model calculations on the spectroscopy of $N<126$ Pb isotopes

> Large-scale shell-model calculations are carried out in the model space including neutron-hole orbitals $2p_{1/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $0i_{13/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$ and $0h_{9/2}$ to study the structure and electromagnetic properties of neutron deficient Pb isotopes. An optimized effective interaction is used. Good agreement between full shell-model calculations and experimental data is obtained for the spherical states in isotopes $^{194-206}$Pb. The lighter isotopes are calculated with an importance-truncation approach constructed based on the monopole Hamiltonian. The full shell-model results also agree well with our generalized seniority and nucleon-pair-approximation truncation calculations. The deviations between theory and experiment concerning the excitation energies and electromagnetic properties of low-lying $0^+$ and $2^+$ excited states and isomeric states may provide a constraint on our understanding of nuclear deformation and intruder configuration in this region.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations in a six-orbital neutron-hole model space reproduce the low-lying spherical states of Pb isotopes down to \(^{194}\)Pb with good agreement, but the calculations overestimate excitation energies of the first \(2^+\) state by 300 keV and the first excited \(0^+\) state by 600 keV in \(^{194}\)Pb, and fail to reproduce the inversion between those two states, indicating that neglected deformed (intruder) configurations become important for the lightest isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed full configuration-interaction shell-model calculations in the \(M\)-scheme using a model space of six neutron-hole orbitals (\(2p_{1/2}\), \(1f_{5/2}\), \(2p_{3/2}\), \(0i_{13/2}\), \(1f_{7/2}\), \(0h_{9/2}\)) above a \(^{208}\)Pb inert core, with single-hole energies taken from \(^{207}\)Pb and an effective interaction developed by the Stockholm group. For isotopes lighter than \(^{194}\)Pb, an importance-truncation approach based on the monopole Hamiltonian was employed, and the results were compared with generalized seniority (up to \(v=6\)) and nucleon-pair-approximation (NPA) truncation calculations.

## Key numbers

* The largest system diagonalized in the full model space had an \(M\)-scheme dimension of \(3.4 \times 10^{9}\), enabling calculations for all nuclei between \(^{194-206}\)Pb.
* For \(^{194}\)Pb, convergence of the lowest three positive-parity states was practically reached at \(d/D \sim 0.1\), i.e., by considering only 10% of the total \(M\)-scheme bases.
* For nuclei heavier than \(^{196}\)Pb, the difference between calculated and experimental shell-model correlation energies is less than 100 keV; the largest deviation is for \(^{194}\)Pb, where the calculation overestimates \(E^{\rm SM}\) by 300 keV.
* The empirical pairing gaps for mid-shell nuclei \(^{196-198}\)Pb are underestimated by the calculation by around 100 keV.
* The excitation energy of the \(13/2^+\) state in \(^{197}\)Pb is 319 keV experimentally, compared to the calculated value of 362 keV.
* The splitting between the first \(5/2^-\) and \(1/2^-\) states increases to 126.5 keV in \(^{203}\)Pb; the calculated gap is 143 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations for Pb isotopes that were limited to nuclei near \(^{208}\)Pb with few valence holes (Cor98, Sha12, Xu09, Lei10) and provides a microscopic benchmark for approximation methods such as the generalized seniority scheme (Talmi 1971, Allaart 1988) and the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA1, NPA2, NPA3). The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data on electromagnetic moments and B(E2) values, and the observed deviations for light Pb isotopes (\(^{194}\)Pb and below) constrain future studies of nuclear deformation and intruder configurations, which have been described by collective models such as the interacting boson model (Hell08, Nom12) and beyond-mean-field calculations (Nom12, Yao13, Egi, Rod04).
