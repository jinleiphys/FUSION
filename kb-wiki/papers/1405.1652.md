# Overview of Neutron-Proton Pairing

> The role of neutron-proton pairing correlations on the structure of nuclei along the $N=Z$ line is reviewed. Particular emphasis is placed on the competition between isovector ($T=1$) and isoscalar $(T=0$) pair fields. The expected properties of these systems, in terms of pairing collective motion, are assessed by different theoretical frameworks including schematic models, realistic Shell Model and mean field approaches. The results are contrasted with experimental data with the goal of establishing clear signals for the existence of neutron-proton ($np$) condensates. We will show that there is clear evidence for an isovector $np$ condensate as expected from isospin invariance. However, and contrary to early expectations, a condensate of deuteron-like pairs appears quite elusive and pairing collectivity in the $T=0$ channel may only show in the form of a phonon. Arguments are presented for the use of direct reactions, adding or removing an $np$ pair, as the most promising tool to provide a definite answer to this intriguing question.

# Overview of Neutron-Proton Pairing

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence for neutron-proton pairing correlations along the N=Z line and concludes that while an isovector (T=1) np condensate is clearly established from isospin invariance, a condensate of deuteron-like isoscalar (T=0) pairs remains elusive; pairing collectivity in the T=0 channel may only appear in the form of a phonon rather than a condensate, and direct np pair transfer reactions are argued to be the most promising tool to resolve this question.

## Method

The authors review theoretical frameworks including schematic models (one- and two-shell models with exact solutions), realistic Shell Model calculations in spherical and deformed bases, and mean-field approaches (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with isospin symmetry breaking). They compare these theoretical predictions with experimental data on binding energies, rotational motion, odd-odd N=Z nuclei, Gamow-Teller strengths, and pairing vibrations, using the pair transfer matrix element and correlation energy as quantitative measures of pairing collectivity.

## Key numbers

- The control parameter for the isovector pair condensate transition is y = 2ΩG/D, with the critical value y=1 separating the paired regime (y>1) from the vibrational regime (y<1); the gap parameter for the two-shell model at M=2Ω is Δ = √((2GΩ)² - D²)/2 = D√(y²-1)/2
- In the degenerate one-shell limit (D→0), the isorotational moment of inertia is θ = 1/G, and the energy of the v=0 ground state in even-even systems is E = -½G[(M-v/2)(2Ω+3-M-v/2) + t(t+1)] + ½GT(T+1)
- The reduced pair transfer matrix element between T=0 ground state of an even-even nucleus and T=1 ground state of its odd-odd neighbor in mean-field is ⟨1||P†||0⟩ = √2Δ/G = (Ω/√2)[(y²-1)/y²]^(1/2)
- In the two-shell model with Ω=10 and M=20, the isorotational pattern prevails down to G/Gc ~ 2
- The isoscalar pair condensate shift for odd-odd N=Z nuclei relative to even-even neighbors is S(S+1)/2J = 1/J = G/(1-1/y²), which at G=4Gc amounts to 1/7 of the shell distance D
- The cross-over from isoscalar to isovector regime in the one-shell model occurs around |x| > 0.2, where x = (G_IV - G_IS)/(G_IV + G_IS)
- The isoscalar np pair field persists to substantial |N-Z| values of 8 in the model with degeneracy 2Ω=24

## Context

This review extends the long-standing investigation of nuclear pairing initiated by Bohr, Mottelson and Pines (1958) and developed through BCS theory and its applications to nuclear structure. The work contrasts with earlier expectations of a deuteron-like T=0 condensate by showing that theoretical studies point instead to collective T=0 correlations of a vibrational type, consistent with the schematic model calculations of Dussel et al. (1970, 1986), Evans et al. (1981), and Engel et al. (1997). The paper enables future experimental searches using direct np pair transfer reactions, analogous to how (t,p) and (p,t) reactions elucidated nn pairing correlations, and provides a framework for interpreting data from radioactive beam facilities along the N=Z line up to A<100.
