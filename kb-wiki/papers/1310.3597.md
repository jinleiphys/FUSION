# Heavy vs. light flavor energy loss within a partonic transport model

> The full space-time evolution of gluons, light and heavy quarks in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied within the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). We discuss for light and heavy quarks the elastic and radiative energy loss with a running coupling. Radiative processes, in particular, are implemented through an improved version of the Gunion-Bertsch matrix element, which is derived from comparisons to the exact result, explicitly taking finite heavy quark masses into account. Subsequently, we present results with and without radiative processes and compare them to experimental data at LHC. A focus is put on the nuclear modification factor of charged hadrons and D mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that radiative energy loss for light and heavy quarks is of similar magnitude when a running coupling and an improved Gunion-Bertsch matrix element with finite mass corrections are used, but this result is preliminary and depends on an effective implementation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect that introduces a free parameter \(X\) to match experimental data.

## Method

The authors use the partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings) to solve the Boltzmann equation for on-shell partons with pQCD interactions, including all \(2\rightarrow 2\) and \(2\leftrightarrow 3\) processes for light partons and elastic plus radiative collisions for heavy quarks. Radiative processes are implemented via an improved Gunion-Bertsch matrix element generalized to finite heavy quark masses, with a running coupling and a screening mass \(\mu^2 = \kappa m_D^2\) where \(\kappa = 1/(2e) \approx 0.2\) for heavy quarks (or \(\kappa = 1\) for consistent treatment of light and heavy partons). The LPM effect is included via an effective cut-off parameter \(X\) that controls the number of allowed radiative interactions.

## Key numbers

- Elastic energy loss per unit length for a charm quark (mass \(M = 1.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) and a bottom quark (mass \(M = 4.6\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) in a static thermal medium at temperature \(T = 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) is shown, with radiative energy loss on the same order or slightly larger for all quark masses.
- The screening mass prefactor for heavy quarks is \(\kappa = 1/(2e) \approx 0.2\), determined by matching elastic energy loss to HTL calculations.
- A phenomenological factor \(K = 3.5\) multiplying elastic cross sections is tuned to RHIC heavy flavor electron elliptic flow data and simultaneously describes the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of all heavy flavor particles at LHC.
- The LPM parameter \(X = 0.3\) yields good agreement with experimental data for charged hadron and \(D\) meson nuclear modification factors at LHC, while \(X = 1\) underestimates the suppression.

## Context

This work extends previous BAMPS calculations (Uphoff 2010, 2011, 2012; Fochler 2011) that showed elastic processes alone cannot reproduce heavy flavor data, requiring a phenomenological \(K\) factor. The paper introduces radiative processes via an improved Gunion-Bertsch matrix element consistent with Aichelin (2013) and incorporates finite mass effects including the dead cone suppression from Dokshitzer (2001) and Abir (2011). The results enable future studies of heavy flavor correlations (Uphoff 2013) and heavy flavor tagged jets (Senzel 2013), while the need for the \(X\) parameter highlights the need for a more sophisticated LPM implementation.
