# Measurement of $\rm ^{6}H$ ground state energy in an electron scattering experiment at MAMI-A1

> For the first time the neutron-rich hydrogen isotope $\rm ^{6}H$ was produced in an electron scattering experiment in the reaction $\rm ^{7}Li(e,~e'pπ^{+})^{6}H$ using the spectrometer facility of the A1 Collaboration at the Mainz Microtron accelerator. By measuring the triple coincidence between the scattered electron, the produced proton, and $π^{+}$, the missing mass spectrum of $\rm ^{6}H$ was obtained. A clear peak above $^3$H+n+n+n energy threshold was seen resulting in a ground state energy of $\rm ^{6}H$ at $2.3\pm0.5({\rm stat.})\pm0.4({\rm syst.})$ MeV with a width of $1.9\pm1.0({\rm stat.})\pm0.4({\rm syst.})$ MeV. This work challenges the understandings of multi-nucleon interactions and presents a new method to study light neutron-rich nuclei with electron scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The ground state energy of the neutron-rich hydrogen isotope ⁶H was measured at 2.3 ± 0.5 (stat.) ± 0.4 (syst.) MeV above the ³H+n+n+n threshold, with a width of 1.9 ± 1.0 (stat.) ± 0.4 (syst.) MeV, using electron scattering for the first time. This result favors the smaller energy values near 2.7 MeV reported in some earlier experiments (Aleksandrov 1984, Belozyorov 1986) and contradicts the larger energies (around 6-10 MeV) found in pion-induced reactions and in the most recent theoretical calculation (Hiyama 2022).

## Method

The experiment was performed at the MAMI-A1 spectrometer facility using a 855 MeV electron beam on a natural lithium target (92.7% ⁷Li). The reaction ⁷Li(e, e'pπ⁺)⁶H was used, where the virtual photon excites a proton to a Δ⁺(1232) resonance which decays to a neutron and π⁺; the neutron then scatters off another proton, ejecting it and leaving a ⁶H nucleus. The momenta of the scattered electron, the produced proton, and the π⁺ were measured in triple coincidence using three high-resolution spectrometers (SpekA, SpekB, SpekC), and the missing mass spectrum of ⁶H was reconstructed.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ⁶H: 2.3 ± 0.5 (stat.) ± 0.4 (syst.) MeV above the ³H+n+n+n threshold.
- Width of the ⁶H ground state: 1.9 ± 1.0 (stat.) ± 0.4 (syst.) MeV.
- Significance of the signal: 5.2 sigma, calculated using the asymptotic formula with signal and background counts in the 0-6 MeV/c² region.
- Missing-mass resolution from Monte Carlo simulation: 1.2 MeV (FWHM).
- Beam energy: 855 MeV; beam current: 400 nA; total beam time: about 550 hours.
- Central momenta of spectrometers: SpekA at 417 MeV/c, SpekB at 421 MeV/c, SpekC at 273 MeV/c.
- Angular acceptances: SpekA 28 msr, SpekB 5.6 msr, SpekC 28 msr.
- Estimated production rate: about 1 count per day in the 0-10 MeV/c² missing mass region.

## Context

This work provides the first measurement of ⁶H using electron scattering, a new reaction mechanism compared to prior heavy-ion and pion-induced experiments. The result supports the smaller ground state energy values (around 2.7 MeV) reported by Aleksandrov (1984), Belozyorov (1986), and Caamano (2008), and contradicts the larger energies (6.6-7.3 MeV) found by Gurov (2003) using pion beams, as well as the most recent theoretical prediction of about 10 MeV by Hiyama (2022). The measurement enables future studies of other light neutron-rich nuclei (e.g., ⁵H, tetraneutron) and light hypernuclei using similar electron scattering reactions at MAMI.
