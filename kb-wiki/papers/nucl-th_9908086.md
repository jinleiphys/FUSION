# Interpretation of y-scaling of the nuclear response

> The behavior of the nuclear matter response in the region of large momentum transfer, in which plane wave impulse approximation predicts the onset of y-scaling, is discussed. The theoretical analysis shows that scaling violations produced by final state interactions are driven by the momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section. Their study may provide valuable information on possible modifications of nucleon-nucleon scattering in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The onset of y-scaling in the nuclear response at large momentum transfer does not necessarily indicate the approach to the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) regime; final state interactions (FSI) driven by the momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section can persist and produce scaling behavior that converges to a value different from the PWIA limit.

## Method

The author analyzes the nuclear matter response using a microscopic many-body approach where the spectral function is obtained from ab initio calculations with realistic nuclear Hamiltonians. FSI are treated within the high-energy approximation (eikonal and frozen approximations), where the quasielastic response is obtained by folding the PWIA response with a function derived from the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude. The analysis compares results using the total NN cross section, the elastic NN cross section, and a color transparency model to examine their effects on the scaling function at large momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers momentum transfers up to Q^2 ~ 7 (GeV/c)^2, corresponding to the kinematic range of TJNAF data on Carbon, Iron, and Gold targets
- Sizeable scaling violations are observed at y < -0.2 GeV/c and Q^2 < 3 (GeV/c)^2
- The measured NN total cross section is roughly constant in the momentum range 2-5 GeV/c
- The ratio sigma_el/sigma_tot drops from ~95% to ~30% over the range 1 < q < 4 GeV/c
- The color transparency model uses a formation length L = 2q/Delta m^2 with Delta m^2 = 0.7 GeV^2 and <k_t^2>^{1/2} ~ 0.35 GeV/c
- CT effects become significant at y = -0.4 GeV/c and q > 1.5 GeV/c, with the scaling function showing a steep fall in the range 1 < q < 3 GeV/c
- The repulsive core radius of the NN interaction is r_c ~ 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of y-scaling from earlier studies by Benhar et al. (1991, 1993, 1994) on FSI in the SLAC kinematic domain to the higher Q^2 regime accessible at TJNAF. The analysis contradicts the simple expectation that FSI become negligible at large momentum transfer, showing instead that inelastic channels with roughly momentum-independent cross sections produce persistent FSI effects. The results enable interpretation of the new TJNAF data from Arrington et al. (1999) and suggest that the scaling behavior may provide information on possible color transparency effects in inclusive processes, as modeled by Farrar et al. (1988).
