# Heavy Quark Diffusion in Strongly Coupled $\N=4$ Yang Mills

> We express the heavy quark diffusion coefficient as the temporal variation of a Wilson line along the Schwinger-Keldysh contour. This generalizes the classical formula for diffusion as a force-force correlator to a non-abelian theory. We use this formula to compute the diffusion coefficient in strongly coupled $\N=4$ Yang-Mills by studying the fluctuations of a string in $AdS_5\times S_5$. The string solution spans the full Kruskal plane and gives access to contour correlations. The diffusion coefficient is $D=2/\sqrtλ πT$ and is therefore parametrically smaller than momentum diffusion, $η/(e+p)=1/4πT$. The quark mass must be much greater than $T\sqrtλ$ in order to treat the quark as a heavy quasi-particle. The result is discussed in the context of the RHIC experiments.

## Key claim

The heavy quark diffusion coefficient in strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang Mills is computed via the AdS/CFT correspondence, yielding D = 2/(sqrt(lambda) pi T), which is parametrically smaller than the momentum diffusion coefficient eta/(e+p) = 1/(4 pi T); however, the quark mass must be much greater than T sqrt(lambda) for the heavy quasi-particle treatment to be valid.

## Method

The authors express the heavy quark diffusion coefficient as the temporal variation of a Wilson line along the Schwinger-Keldysh contour, generalizing the classical force-force correlator formula to non-abelian theories. They then compute this diffusion coefficient in strongly coupled N=4 Yang-Mills by studying the fluctuations of a string in AdS_5 x S_5, where the string solution spans the full Kruskal plane to access contour correlations. The computation utilizes the Nambu-Goto action for semi-classical strings, with the string endpoints following the type "1" and type "2" boundaries corresponding to the two branches of the real time partition function.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion coefficient: D = 2/(sqrt(lambda) pi T)
- Momentum diffusion (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio): eta/(e+p) = 1/(4 pi T)
- The diffusion coefficient D is parametrically smaller than the momentum diffusion coefficient
- The quark mass must satisfy M >> T sqrt(lambda) for the heavy quasi-particle treatment to be valid

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT correspondence approach to transport properties previously applied to shear viscosity by Policastro, Son, and Starinet (2001, 2002) and the viscosity bound conjecture by Kovtun, Son, and Starinet (2004). The computation provides a rigorous result for heavy quark diffusion in strongly coupled N=4 SYM that serves as a foil to weak-coupling extrapolations used in phenomenological models of RHIC heavy flavor data, such as those by Moore and Teaney (2005) and van Hees and Rapp (2005). The result enables comparison with lattice determinations of the diffusion coefficient, which remain difficult as noted by Petreczky and Teaney (2006) and Aarts and Martinez Resco (2003), and informs interpretation of heavy quark suppression and elliptic flow measurements at RHIC.
