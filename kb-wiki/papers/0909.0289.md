# QCD thermodynamics with effective models

> In this talk we extend the Polyakov-quark-meson model to N_f=2+1 quark flavors and study its bulk thermodynamics at finite temperatures in mean-field approximation. Three different Polyakov-loop potentials are considered. Our findings are confronted to recent QCD lattice simulations of the RBC-Bielefeld and HotQCD collaborations. Furthermore, the finite chemical potential expansion of the quark-number susceptibility in a Taylor series around vanishing chemical potential is analyzed. By means of a novel algorithmic differentiation technique, we have calculated Taylor coefficients up to 24th order in the model for the first time. This allows the systematic study of convergence properties of the Taylor series.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model with N_f=2+1 quark flavors, evaluated in mean-field approximation, can reproduce the bulk thermodynamics of QCD lattice simulations reasonably well, but only when the larger quark masses used on the lattice are explicitly considered in the model; the agreement is best for the Fukushima Polyakov-loop potential below 1.5 T_chi, while all potentials fail to match lattice data in the restored phase.

## Method

The authors extended the two-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model to three quark flavors (N_f=2+1) and computed the thermodynamic grand potential in mean-field approximation, coupling the chiral linear sigma model to the Polyakov loop via a covariant derivative. Three different parameterizations of the Polyakov-loop potential were compared: a polynomial Ginzburg-Landau ansatz, a logarithmic form motivated by the SU(3) Haar measure, and a two-parameter strong-coupling inspired form due to Fukushima. The model results for the Polyakov loop expectation value, subtracted chiral condensate, and normalized pressure were confronted with lattice data from the RBC-Bielefeld and HotQCD collaborations, using pion and kaon masses of m_pi=220 MeV and m_K=503 MeV to match the lattice simulations. For the Taylor expansion study, a novel algorithmic differentiation technique was employed to compute Taylor coefficients of the pressure up to 24th order around zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature T_0 for the pure Yang-Mills Polyakov-loop potential is 270 MeV; for N_f=2+1 flavors with a strange quark mass m_s=150 MeV, T_0 is estimated at 187 MeV.
- Pion and kaon masses used for lattice comparison: m_pi=220 MeV, m_K=503 MeV.
- The critical endpoint (CEP) in the model is located at (T_c, mu_c) approximately (185, 167) MeV, corresponding to mu_c/T_c approximately 0.9.
- Taylor coefficients were calculated up to 24th order in the PQM model for the first time.
- The Taylor expansion fails to reproduce the divergence of the quark-number susceptibility at the CEP even at 12th order; the peak seen in the expansion around T ~ T_chi is a truncation artifact.
- The convergence radius analysis shows that for temperatures close to T_chi, higher-order coefficients (n>8) are required to approach the CEP location.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model of Schaefer (2007) to three flavors and compares with lattice results from the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration (Cheng, 2008) and HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2009). The study contrasts with pure quark-meson models (Schaefer, 2008) that fail to describe lattice thermodynamics, and with lattice QCD simulations that are limited to zero or imaginary chemical potentials due to the sign problem. The novel algorithmic differentiation technique enables the first calculation of Taylor coefficients up to 24th order in an effective model, allowing systematic study of convergence properties that are relevant for lattice extrapolation methods used by Allton (2005) and Gavai (2009). The results suggest that lattice Taylor expansions with coefficients only up to n=8 are insufficient to locate a possible critical endpoint, and that higher-order coefficients are definitely required.
