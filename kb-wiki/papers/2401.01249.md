# Femtoscopy with Lévy sources from SPS through RHIC to LHC

> Femtoscopy is a unique tool to investigate the space-time geometry of the matter created in ultra-relativistic collisions. If the probability density distribution of hadron emission is parametrized, then the dependence of its parameters on particle momentum, collision energy, and collision geometry can be given. In recent years, several measurements came to light that indicated the adequacy of assuming a Lévy-stable shape for the mentioned distribution. In parallel, several new phenomenological developments appeared, aiding the interpretation of the experimental results or providing tools for the measurements. In this paper, we discuss important aspects of femtoscopy with Lévy sources in light of some of these advances, including phenomenological and experimental ones.

## Key claim

Lévy-stable source shapes provide a more adequate description of femtoscopic correlation functions than Gaussian assumptions across a wide range of collision energies, but the extracted Lévy exponent α and scale R are entangled, and the physical interpretation of α requires disentangling multiple contributing phenomena (anomalous diffusion, jet fragmentation, critical phenomena, resonance decays) whose relative importance may vary with system size and collision energy.

## Method

The paper reviews and synthesizes experimental femtoscopic measurements of two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations from the SPS (NA61/SHINE at 16.82 GeV), RHIC (PHENIX and STAR at 200 GeV), and LHC (CMS at 5.02 TeV), where correlation functions were fitted with Lévy-stable source parametrizations. Phenomenological comparisons are made between Lévy and Gaussian source assumptions, including calculations of half width at half maximum (HWHM) and half width at half integral (HWHI) as alternative size measures, and a toy-model study is performed to illustrate how the Lévy exponent α and scale R affect these measures.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX 200 GeV Au+Au 0-30% centrality: α ≈ 1.2, with negligible mT dependence (PHENIX 2017)
- CMS 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb: α values larger (closer to 2) for central collisions, distinctly different from STAR trend
- NA61/SHINE 16.82 GeV Ar+Sc: α values among the largest measured; Be+Be at same energy: significantly smaller α
- For a 3D Gaussian source with radii Rout=6 fm, Rside=4 fm, Rlong=8 fm, the angle-averaged correlation function differs distinctly from a spherically symmetric Lévy source with α=1.4 and R=6 fm
- At α=1.3, a Gaussian with same HWHM has R ≈ 1.94 Rα=1.3; with same HWHI, R ≈ 1.21 Rα=1.3
- HWHI for 3D Gaussian ≈ 1.538·R; HWHM for Gaussian ≈ 1.177·R
- Critical exponent η for 3D Ising model: 0.03631(3); for random external field 3D Ising model: 0.50±0.05

## Context

This work extends the Lévy-source femtoscopy program initiated by PHENIX (2017) and the theoretical framework of Csorgo (2003, 2005) connecting Lévy exponents to critical phenomena and anomalous diffusion. It synthesizes results from NA61/SHINE (2023), STAR preliminary results (Kincses 2023), and CMS (2023) to map the collision energy dependence of Lévy parameters from SPS through RHIC to LHC. The findings enable systematic searches for the QCD critical point via the collision energy dependence of α, and motivate further model calculations to understand why the mT-dependence of Lévy radii follows the same 1/√mT trend predicted for Gaussian radii by Makhlin and Sinyukov (1987) and Csorgo and Lorstad (1995).
