# General analysis of two--photon exchange in elastic electron--$^4He$ scattering and $e^++e^-\to ŌÄ^++ŌÄ^-.$

> Using a general parametrization of the spin structure of the matrix element for the elastic $e^-+^4He$ scattering and for the annihilation $e^++e^-\to ŌÄ^++ŌÄ^-$ reactions in terms of two complex amplitudes, we derive general properties of the observables in presence of two--photon exchange. We show that this mechanism induces a specific dependence of the differential cross section on the angle of the emitted particle. We reanalyze the existing experimental data on the differential cross section, for elastic electron scattering on $^4He$, in the light of this result.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange mechanism, if sizable, would induce a specific angular dependence in the differential cross section for elastic electron-4He scattering and for e+e- ‚Üí ŌÄ+ŌÄ-; however, an analysis of existing 4He data shows no statistically significant evidence for such a contribution, with the extracted two-photon term typically below 1% (excluding the factor of őĪ).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent formalism for two-photon exchange previously applied to elastic electron-nucleon scattering (Re04) and to proton-antiproton annihilation to e+e- pairs (Ga06) to the case of spinless targets (‚ĀīHe) and the time-like reaction e+e- ‚Üí ŌÄ+ŌÄ-. The analysis of 4He data finds no evidence for two-photon exchange, consistent with earlier null results for the deuteron (Re99) and the proton (ETG05). The paper enables future Rosenbluth experiments at larger Q¬≤ and measurements of single-spin asymmetry with transversely polarized electron beams to search for two-photon effects, while also emphasizing the need for higher-order radiative corrections (ETG07, By07).
