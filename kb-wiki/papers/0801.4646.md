# General analysis of two--photon exchange in elastic electron--$^4He$ scattering and $e^++e^-\to π^++π^-.$

> Using a general parametrization of the spin structure of the matrix element for the elastic $e^-+^4He$ scattering and for the annihilation $e^++e^-\to π^++π^-$ reactions in terms of two complex amplitudes, we derive general properties of the observables in presence of two--photon exchange. We show that this mechanism induces a specific dependence of the differential cross section on the angle of the emitted particle. We reanalyze the existing experimental data on the differential cross section, for elastic electron scattering on $^4He$, in the light of this result.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange mechanism, if sizable, would induce a specific angular dependence in the differential cross section for elastic electron-4He scattering and for e+e- → π+π-; however, an analysis of existing 4He data shows no statistically significant evidence for such a contribution, with the extracted two-photon term typically below 1% (excluding the factor of α).

## Method

The authors parametrize the spin structure of the matrix element for elastic e- + 4He → e- + 4He and for e+ + e- → π+ + π- using two complex invariant amplitudes (F1 and F2), based on Lorentz invariance, P-invariance, and crossing symmetry. They derive expressions for the unpolarized differential cross section and for the single-spin asymmetry (A_y) arising from a transversely polarized electron beam, isolating the contributions from one-photon and two-photon exchange. Existing experimental data on the 4He differential cross section from Frerejacque et al. (1967) are reanalyzed by fitting the reduced cross section as a function of cosθ at fixed Q² and by performing a two-dimensional fit in Q² and cosθ.

## Key numbers

- For elastic e- + 4He scattering, the measured transverse beam spin asymmetry A_y(⁴He) = -13.51 ± 1.34(stat) ± 0.37(syst) ppm at E = 2.75 GeV, θ = 6°, and Q² = 0.077 GeV² (K07), compared to a theoretical prediction A_y(⁴He) ≈ 10⁻¹⁰ assuming the target remains in its ground state (CH05).
- From the measured asymmetry, the extracted imaginary part of the spin-flip amplitude is Im F₁ ≈ -F(q²) for θ = 6°.
- Linear fits of the reduced cross section at fixed Q² values (0.5 to 8 fm⁻²) from Frerejacque et al. (1967) yield slopes b that are all compatible with zero; for example, at Q² = 8 fm⁻², b = -0.01 ± 0.03.
- A two-dimensional fit up to Q² = 20 fm⁻² gives an additional two-photon term lower than 1% (excluding the factor of α).
- The ⁴He charge form factor was measured up to Q² = 64 fm⁻² (Ar78), where the counting rate dropped to one event/week.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent formalism for two-photon exchange previously applied to elastic electron-nucleon scattering (Re04) and to proton-antiproton annihilation to e+e- pairs (Ga06) to the case of spinless targets (⁴He) and the time-like reaction e+e- → π+π-. The analysis of 4He data finds no evidence for two-photon exchange, consistent with earlier null results for the deuteron (Re99) and the proton (ETG05). The paper enables future Rosenbluth experiments at larger Q² and measurements of single-spin asymmetry with transversely polarized electron beams to search for two-photon effects, while also emphasizing the need for higher-order radiative corrections (ETG07, By07).
