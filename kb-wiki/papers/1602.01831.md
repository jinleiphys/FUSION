# Thermal conductivity and impurity scattering in the accreting neutron star crust

> We calculate the thermal conductivity of electrons for the strongly correlated multi-component ion plasma expected in the outer layers of neutron star's crust employing a Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) approach. This allows us to isolate the low energy response of the ions and use it to calculate the electron scattering rate and the electron thermal conductivity. We find that the scattering rate is enhanced by a factor 2-4 compared to earlier calculations based on the simpler electron-impurity scattering formalism. This findings directly impacts the interpretation of thermal relaxation observed in transiently accreting neutron stars and has implications for the composition and nuclear reactions in the crust that occur during accretion.

## Key claim

The authors find that the electron scattering rate in multi-component plasmas (MCPs) in neutron star crusts is enhanced by a factor of 2-4 compared to earlier calculations based on the simpler electron-impurity scattering formalism, with the enhancement being substantially larger at low temperatures where the simple impurity scattering formula underestimates the low-energy response.

## Method

The authors employed Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) simulations to calculate the Euclidean charge-charge correlation function of multi-component ion plasmas, extracting the low-energy response needed to compute electron thermal conductivity. They used the PIMC data to obtain the impurity contribution to the dynamic structure factor by evaluating the correlation function at large imaginary-time separation, where phonon contributions become negligible at low temperatures. The thermal conductivity was then calculated from the electron scattering rate using the Coulomb logarithm formalism, with results compared to the standard impurity model and to earlier molecular dynamics simulations.

## Key numbers

- Electron scattering rate enhancement factor of 2-4 compared to earlier calculations based on the simpler electron-impurity scattering formalism
- Impurity parameter correction factor L = 1.96(5) at T approximately 0.9 times the plasma frequency Omega_pl, asymptoting to L = 3.66(7) in the low temperature limit
- Calculations performed at mass density rho = 10^10 g/cm^3 for three MCP systems with impurity parameters Q_imp = 2.16, 15.34, and 33.75
- Temperatures studied: T = 0.02, 0.002, 0.0004 MeV, corresponding to T/Omega_pl approximately 0.9, 0.09, and 0.02
- Systems contained N_i = 2000 ions, with convergence checked for N_i ranging from 686 to 2662

## Context

This work extends earlier classical molecular dynamics studies of MCPs by Caballero et al. (2006) and Chuck et al. (2009) to lower temperatures relevant to neutron stars, incorporating quantum effects through PIMC. The results contradict the standard impurity scattering formalism developed by Flowers and Itoh (1976) and Itoh et al. (1993), which treats MCPs as a one-component plasma plus uncorrelated impurities. The findings directly impact the interpretation of thermal relaxation observed in transiently accreting neutron stars, as studied by Shternin et al. (2007), Brown and Cumming (2009), and Page and Reddy (2012, 2013), and enable more accurate modeling of crust composition and nuclear reactions during accretion.
