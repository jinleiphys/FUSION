# Heavy-Quark Diffusion, Flow and Recombination at RHIC

> We discuss recent developments in assessing heavy-quark interaction in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). While induced gluon radiation is expected to be the main energy-loss mechanism for fast-moving quarks, we focus on elastic scattering which prevails toward lower energies, evaluating both perturbative (gluon-exchange) and nonperturbative (resonance formation) interactions in the QGP. The latter are treated within an effective model for D- and B-meson resonances above T_c as motivated by current QCD lattice calculations. Pertinent diffusion and drag constants, following from a Fokker-Planck equation, are implemented into an expanding fireball model for Au-Au collisions at RHIC using relativistic Langevin simulations. Heavy quarks are hadronized in a combined fragmentation and coalescence framework, and resulting electron-decay spectra are compared to recent RHIC data. A reasonable description of both nuclear suppression factors and elliptic flow up to momenta of ~5 GeV supports the notion of a strongly interacting QGP created at RHIC. Consequences and further tests of the proposed resonance interactions are discussed.

## Key claim

Elastic resonance scattering of heavy quarks with light antiquarks, modeled via effective D- and B-meson resonances above T_c, can generate sufficient drag and diffusion to explain both the nuclear suppression and elliptic flow of single electrons up to p_T ~ 5 GeV in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, whereas perturbative QCD scattering alone cannot produce the observed elliptic flow of up to 10% without unrealistically large coupling constants.

## Method

The authors evaluate elastic heavy-quark scattering in the QGP using a Fokker-Planck equation for Brownian motion, computing drag and diffusion coefficients from both leading-order perturbative QCD (t-channel gluon exchange with Debye screening) and a nonperturbative resonance model based on an effective Lagrangian for D- and B-meson exchange in the Q-anti-q channel. These transport coefficients are implemented into relativistic Langevin simulations of charm and bottom quarks in an expanding fireball model for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, with hadronization treated via a combined delta-function fragmentation and heavy-light quark coalescence framework, and final electron-decay spectra are compared to RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- Thermal relaxation time for charm quarks at T=300 MeV: tau_c ~ 15 fm/c for LO pQCD with alpha_s=0.4, reduced by a factor ~3 (to ~5 fm/c) when resonance interactions are added
- Expected QGP lifetime at RHIC: tau_QGP <= 5 fm/c
- Maximal single-electron v_2 from pQCD energy-loss calculations: 2-3% in semicentral Au-Au, while experimental values reach up to 10% around p_T^e=2 GeV
- Crossing of charm and bottom contributions to electron spectra in p-p: at p_T ~ 5 GeV
- Resonance model reduces thermalization times by factor ~3 over pQCD scattering, with moderate sensitivity to the effective coupling at the c-anti-q-D vertex
- Resonance widths used in the model: Gamma_Phi = 0.4-0.75 GeV (for the uncertainty band)
- Form factor cutoff for finite-size vertices: Lambda = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy-quark energy loss at RHIC (Teaney 2004, Djordjevic 2005) by introducing nonperturbative resonance interactions motivated by lattice QCD calculations suggesting hadronic bound states survive up to ~2T_c (Asakawa and Hatsuda 2003, Karsch and Laermann 2003). The approach enables a consistent description of both R_AA and v_2 data that pQCD-based calculations (Armesto 2005) cannot achieve, and opens the door to further tests including excitation functions to vary the quark-to-gluon ratio, measurements of D vs anti-D meson asymmetries, and consistency checks with charmonium regeneration (Thews 2006, Grandchamp 2005).
