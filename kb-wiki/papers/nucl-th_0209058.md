# More about the comparison of local and non-local NN interaction models

> The effect of non-locality in the NN interaction with an off-energy shell character has been studied in the past in relation with the possibility that some models could be approximately phase-shifts equivalent. This work is extended to a non-locality implying terms that involve an anticommutator with the operator p^2. It includes both scalar and tensor components. The most recent ``high accuracy'' models are considered in the analysis. After studying the deuteron wave functions, electromagnetic properties of various models are compared with the idea that these ones differ by their non-locality but are equivalent up to a unitary transformation. It is found that the extra non-local tensor interaction considered in this work tends to re-enforce the role of the term considered in previous works, allowing one to explain almost completely the difference in the deuteron D-state probabilities evidenced by the comparison of the Bonn-QB and Paris models for instance. Conclusions for the effect of the non-local scalar interaction are not so clear. In many cases, it was found that these terms could explain part of the differences that the comparison of predictions for various models evidences but cases where they could not were also found. Some of these last ones have been analyzed in order to pointing out the origin of the failure.

# More about the comparison of local and non-local NN interaction models

## Key claim

The paper extends previous studies of non-locality in NN interactions by examining terms involving an anticommutator with the operator p², including both scalar and tensor components; the extra non-local tensor interaction can explain almost completely the difference in deuteron D-state probabilities between the Bonn-QB and Paris models, but conclusions for the non-local scalar interaction remain unclear, with some discrepancies between models remaining unexplained.

## Method

The authors consider non-local terms of the form {p²/M, W_S} and {p²/M, W_T S₁₂} and show how these can be transformed away by a unitary transformation at lowest order, solving integral equations numerically to determine the transformation functions V₀(r), V₁(r), and V₂(r). They then apply these transformations to compare deuteron wave functions and electromagnetic observables (form factors A(Q²), B(Q²), and tensor polarization T₂₀(Q²)) across multiple high-accuracy NN interaction models, including Nij1, Nij2, Paris, Bonn-QB, Argonne V18, and Bonn-CD.

## Key numbers

- The non-local tensor term of commutator type studied in earlier works could explain 2/3 of the difference in deuteron D-state probabilities between Paris and Bonn-QB models; the remaining part is addressed by the anticommutator tensor term studied here.
- The Nij1 and Nij2 models differ by a linearly p²-dependent term; Nij1 (non-local) requires 41 parameters while Nij2 (local) requires 47 parameters, both achieving χ² per datum of 1.03.
- The mixing angle ε₁ shows an 8% difference around 100 MeV between Nij1 and Nij2 models, which cannot be removed by unitary transformation.
- A monopole cut-off form factor (Λ²-μ²)/(Λ²+k²) with Λ=1.05 GeV is introduced to suppress short-range contributions.
- The correction to form factors from the perturbative treatment involves a factor q²r³/24 in the integrand, roughly of order q²r₀⁴/96 with r₀ ≈ 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Desplanques and Amghar (2001) and de Forest (2001) that examined non-local terms of commutator type i(p²U - Up²), and responds to results by Von Geramb et al. (2000) who derived local equivalent potentials for Paris and Bonn-QB models showing very close deuteron D-state probabilities. The analysis builds on the observation by Friar (1980) that different phase-shift equivalent potentials may represent the same physics via unitary transformations, and compares models including the high-accuracy Nijmegen models (Stoks et al., 1994), Argonne V18 (Wiringa et al., 1995), Paris (Lacombe et al., 1980), and Bonn models (Machleidt et al., 1987). The work enables discrimination between effects due to mathematical representation of models versus genuine physical effects such as two-pion-exchange contributions or the choice of πNN coupling constant.
