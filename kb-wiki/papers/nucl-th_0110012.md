# Nuclear polarization in hydrogenlike $^{208}_{~82}$Pb$^{81+}$

> We calculate nuclear-polarization energy shifts for hydrogenlike $^{208}_{~82}$Pb$^{81+}$. A retarded transverse part as well as the Coulomb part is taken into account as the electromagnetic interaction between an electron and the nucleus. With a finite charge distribution for the nuclear ground state and the random-phase approximation to describe the nuclear excitations, we obtain nuclear polarization energy of the $1s_{1/2}$ state as --38.2 (--37.0) meV in the Feynman (Coulomb) gauge. For the $2s_{1/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$ and $2p_{3/2}$ states, they are --6.7 (--6.4), --0.2 (--0.2) and +0.0 (+0.0) meV, respectively. The seagull term in the two-photon exchange diagrams is shown to be quite important to obtain the gauge invariance of the nuclear polarization energies.

## Key claim

Nuclear-polarization energy shifts for hydrogenlike $^{208}_{~82}$Pb$^{81+}$ are calculated to be small (tens of meV), but the result is contingent on including the seagull diagram in the two-photon exchange to restore gauge invariance; without it, the Feynman and Coulomb gauge results differ by 34.2 meV for the $1s_{1/2}$ state.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear-polarization energy shifts using second-order perturbation theory with two-photon exchange diagrams (ladder, cross, and seagull) in both Feynman and Coulomb gauges. Dirac electron wave functions were solved numerically in the Coulomb potential of a finite nuclear charge distribution (two-parameter Fermi form), and nuclear excitations were described by the random-phase approximation (RPA) using a Migdal force with a full $3\hbar\omega$ particle-hole configuration space. The electromagnetic interaction included both the Coulomb part and a retarded transverse part, and the calculations were performed in momentum space with double numerical integrals.

## Key numbers

*   Nuclear-polarization energy shift for the $1s_{1/2}$ state: --38.2 meV in the Feynman gauge and --37.0 meV in the Coulomb gauge.
*   Nuclear-polarization energy shift for the $2s_{1/2}$ state: --6.7 meV (Feynman) and --6.4 meV (Coulomb).
*   Nuclear-polarization energy shift for the $2p_{1/2}$ state: --0.2 meV in both gauges.
*   Nuclear-polarization energy shift for the $2p_{3/2}$ state: +0.0 meV in both gauges.
*   Without the seagull term, the $1s_{1/2}$ nuclear-polarization energy was +1.5 meV in the Feynman gauge and --32.7 meV in the Coulomb gauge, a gauge dependence of 34.2 meV.
*   The nuclear ground-state charge distribution used a two-parameter Fermi distribution with $R_0 = 6.6477$ fm and $a = 0.5234$ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear-polarization calculations for electronic atoms by Plunien et al. (1989, 1995) and Nefiodov et al. (1996), which considered only the Coulomb interaction, and by Yamanaka et al. (1999, 2001), who used a collective nuclear model in the Feynman gauge. The present calculation uses a more realistic RPA description of nuclear excitations for $^{208}$Pb and demonstrates that the transverse photon contribution, while individually large, nearly cancels between positive and negative electron energy intermediate states, yielding total shifts of order tens of meV. The results enable more precise comparisons between theory and experiment for high-Z hydrogenlike atoms, where nuclear polarization represents a background limitation to tests of quantum electrodynamics in strong fields.
