# New critical point for QCD in a magnetic field

> We provide a general argument for the possible existence of a new critical point associated with a deconfinement phase transition in QCD at finite temperature $T$ and in a magnetic field $B$ with zero chemical potential. This is the first example of a QCD critical point in a physical external parameter region that can be studied using lattice QCD simulations without suffering from a sign problem.

## Key claim

The paper argues for the possible existence of a new critical point associated with a deconfinement phase transition in QCD at finite temperature and in a magnetic field with zero chemical potential, based on the assumption that the leading-order effective pure-glue theory at large magnetic field has a first-order transition.

## Method

The authors provide a general theoretical argument based on the behavior of QCD in the regime where the magnetic field is much larger than the QCD scale (eB >> Λ_QCD^2). In this regime, quarks acquire a large dynamical mass through magnetic catalysis and decouple from gluons, leaving an effective anisotropic pure SU(3) gauge theory. The authors argue that this effective theory must have a first-order deconfinement transition, and since QCD at B=0 shows only a crossover, the first-order line must terminate at a critical point in the (T,B) plane.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical quark mass gap scales as M_dyn = C(α_s) √|e_q B|, where e_q are the quark charges (e_u = 2/3 e, e_d = -1/3 e)
- The critical magnetic field is estimated to satisfy √(e B_c) ~ T_c ~ Λ_QCD in the chiral limit
- In QCD with realistic quark masses, lattice studies up to √(e B) ≈ 1 GeV found no signal of the magnetic critical point, suggesting √(e B_c) ≳ 1 GeV
- For N_f=2 QCD with m_π ≈ 195 MeV on coarse lattices, preliminary evidence for first-order deconfinement was indicated at √(eB) ≈ 850 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD phase diagrams beyond the conventional (T, μ_B) plane into the (T, B) plane, which is accessible to lattice QCD without a sign problem. It contrasts with earlier model calculations by Agasian (2008) that suggested the first-order deconfinement transition turns into a crossover with increasing B, and with lattice results by Bali (2011) showing inverse magnetic catalysis at T ~ √(eB) ~ Λ_QCD. The argument enables future lattice studies to search for this magnetic critical point and distinguishes it from the conventional QCD critical point at finite chemical potential discussed by Stephanov (2004).
