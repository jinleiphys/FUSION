# Probing double hadron resonance by complex momentum representation method

> Resonances are ubiquitous phenomena in nature, and physicists have developed many methods to explore resonant states. Of particular note is the complex momentum representation(CMR) method, which has been developed and widely used in the study of resonant states in atomic, molecular and nuclear physics. Here, for the first time, we have developed this novel method to study hadron resonant states. The CMR method is applied to probe the bound and resonant states for the $Λ_cD(\bar{D})$ and $Λ_cΛ_c(\barΛ_c)$ systems, in which the resonant states are exposed clearly in complex momentum plane and the resonance parameters can be determined precisely without imposing unphysical parameters. The results show that the CMR method has achieving higher accuracy than other widely used methods. This method is not only very effective for narrow resonances, but also can be reliably applied to broad resonances.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the complex momentum representation (CMR) method, previously used in atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics, can be extended to hadron resonant states for the first time, and that it achieves higher accuracy than the complex scaling method (CSM) without requiring adjustment of unphysical parameters such as the complex rotation angle.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation in momentum space using the CMR method for the Lambda_c D (anti-D) and Lambda_c Lambda_c (anti-Lambda_c) systems, employing one-sigma-exchange and one-omega-exchange potentials derived from effective Lagrangians under heavy-quark symmetry. A monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Lambda = 1.1 GeV is introduced at each vertex to regularize off-shell effects. The momentum-space integral equation is discretized using Gaussian-Legendre quadrature, and the Hamiltonian matrix is symmetrized for numerical diagonalization, with resonant states identified by their positions in the complex momentum plane independent of the integration contour chosen.

## Key numbers

- For the Lambda_c D system with g_omega = 3.00, the S-wave bound state energy is -11.094 MeV; with g_omega = 3.45, the S-wave bound state energy is -14.406 MeV and the P-wave resonance energy is 0.272 - i46.513 MeV; with g_omega = 5.28, the S-wave bound state energy is -38.661 MeV and the P-wave resonance energy is 9.643 - i37.763 MeV.
- For the Lambda_c Lambda_c system with g_omega = 3.00, the S-wave bound state energy is -31.695 MeV and the P-wave resonance energy is 7.739 - i27.785 MeV; with g_omega = 3.45, the S-wave bound state energy is -22.746 MeV and the P-wave resonance energy is 5.577 - i30.215 MeV.
- For the Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c system with g_omega = 3.45, the S-wave bound state energy is -126.019 MeV, the P-wave resonance energy is 9.169 - i7.004 MeV, the D-wave resonance energy is 32.925 - i95.097 MeV, and the F-wave resonance energy is 10.806 - i218.898 MeV.
- The CMR results for the Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c system with g_omega = 3.45 agree with CSM results to within 0.01 MeV for bound states and 0.001-0.003 MeV for resonance real parts, with half-widths matching to within 0.006-0.002 MeV.
- A P-wave resonant state is located at k = 0.1538588 - i0.5204488 GeV on the complex momentum plane, independent of the integration contour.

## Context

This work extends the CMR method, previously established for resonant states in atomic and nuclear physics by authors such as Sukumar (1978) and Li (2016), to hadronic physics for the first time. It directly builds on prior CSM studies of the same Lambda_c D (anti-D) and Lambda_c Lambda_c (anti-Lambda_c) systems by Yu (2021) and Mei (2022), where Y(4630) was explained as a Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c resonant state. The CMR method enables more precise determination of resonance parameters without the need for multiple diagonalizations or adjustment of unphysical rotation angles required by CSM, and can be applied to both narrow and broad resonances in hadronic molecular systems.
