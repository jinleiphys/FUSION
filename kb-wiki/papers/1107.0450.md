# Particle Production in p-p and Heavy Ion Collisions at Ultrarelativistic Energies

> 13 pages, 11 figures, Kruger2010: Workshop on Discovery Physics at the LHC, December 5 - 10 2010 Kruger National Park, South Africa

# Particle Production in p-p and Heavy Ion Collisions at Ultrarelativistic Energies

## Key claim

The thermal model provides a reliable description of particle multiplicities in both p-p and heavy ion collisions across all energies, but the baryon chemical potential follows a different pattern in p-p collisions due to lower stopping power, with mu_B always lower in p-p than in heavy ion collisions at the same beam energy.

## Method

The authors used the THERMUS thermal model in the grand-canonical ensemble to calculate hadron densities at chemical freeze-out, with chemical potentials for baryon number, strangeness, and charge constrained by initial conditions. They extracted the energy dependence of the baryon chemical potential mu_B from fits to experimental data in p-p collisions, using a new parametrization mu_B = d / (1 + e sqrt(s)) with d = 0.4 GeV and e = 0.1599 GeV^{-1}, while keeping the same temperature dependence T(mu_B) as in heavy ion collisions. These parameters were then used to predict ratios of antibaryons to baryons, including strange and multi-strange baryons, as well as light nuclei, antinuclei, hypernuclei, and antihypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The baryon chemical potential at LHC is predicted to be mu_B approximately 1 MeV from the parametrization mu_B(sqrt(s_NN)) = 1.308 GeV / (1 + 0.273 GeV^{-1} sqrt(s_NN))
- The temperature parametrization for heavy ion collisions is T(mu_B) = a - b mu_B^2 - c mu_B^4 with a = 0.166 +/- 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 +/- 0.016 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.053 +/- 0.021 GeV^{-3}
- The new parametrization for mu_B in p-p collisions uses d = 0.4 GeV and e = 0.1599 GeV^{-1}
- The antiproton to proton ratio is given by n_pbar / n_p = exp(-2 mu_B / T) in the Boltzmann approximation
- The antideuterium to deuterium ratio is given by n_dbar / n_d = exp(-4 mu_B / T)
- The anti-helium-3 to helium-3 ratio is given by n_3He_bar / n_3He = exp(-6 mu_B / T)
- The antihypertriton to hypertriton ratio is given by n_3Lbar_Hbar / n_3L_H = exp(-(6 mu_B - 2 mu_S) / T)
- The hypertriton to helium-3 ratio is given by n_3L_H / n_3He = 3 exp(-(6 mu_B - mu_S) / T)

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses of Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2005) for heavy ion collisions to include p-p collisions, providing a new parametrization of the baryon chemical potential for p-p systems. The paper addresses the unexpectedly high yields of antihypernuclei observed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Abelev, 2010) and provides predictions for LHC energies at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV for both p-p and Pb-Pb collisions. The results enable future comparisons with data from NICA and FAIR, particularly in the beam energy region around 8-12 GeV where the highest freeze-out baryonic density is expected.
