# Effective Field Theory For Nuclei, Dense Matter And The Cheshire Cat

> In this talk I discuss three related topics based on some of the recent developments in hadron and nuclear physics: one, effective field theory approach to two-nucleon systems; two, an explanation of the flavor singlet axial charge in the proton (i.e., "proton spin problem") in terms of a Cheshire Cat phenomenon; and three, the quark-hadron duality in hadronic matter at high density and "qualitons" at high density ("superqualitons"). The principal common theme in these discussions will be the emergence of the generic feature of the Cheshire Cat Principle.

# Effective Field Theory For Nuclei, Dense Matter And The Cheshire Cat

## Key claim

The three topics discussed (two-nucleon effective field theory, the flavor-singlet axial charge of the proton, and quark-hadron duality at high density) are all connected by the Cheshire Cat Principle, which states that at low energy, physics involving hadrons should be independent of the bag size R; however, this principle is only approximate in four-dimensional QCD because exact bosonization is not possible, and the equivalence between skyrmionic and quark-gluonic descriptions becomes more prominent at high density rather than less.

## Method

For two-nucleon systems, the author adopts the Lambda-counting scheme of Weinberg, where systematic power counting is applied only to irreducible graphs in chiral perturbation theory, while reducible graphs are summed to all orders with irreducible graphs entering as vertices; this scheme is contrasted with the Q-counting approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise. For the proton spin problem, a chiral bag model is constructed with a quark-gluon interior and a hadronic exterior, connected by boundary conditions that include a Chern-Simons current on the surface to cancel color outflow. For dense matter, a superqualiton description is developed from a color-flavor locked phase where diquark condensation breaks color and chiral symmetry to the diagonal subgroup SU(3)_{C+L+R}.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass m_pi is approximately 140 MeV, defining the energy scale below which pions can be ignored in the two-nucleon EFT.
- The s-wave scattering amplitudes are accurately postdicted up to momentum p less than or equal to m_pi for order Q^2 in the Lambda counting.
- The proton fusion process p + p -> d + e+ + nu_e is calculated to an accuracy of 1 to 3 percent (uncertainty from exchange current at order Q^3).
- The unpolarized cross section for threshold np capture (n + p -> d + gamma) is computed to 1 percent accuracy in complete agreement with experiment.

## Context

This talk extends the effective field theory approach to two-nucleon systems developed by Weinberg (1990s) and applied by Park, Min, Rho (PKMR) and others, showing that realistic potential models (PM) represent the leading term in the EFT expansion with systematically calculable corrections. The Cheshire Cat Principle, originally formulated by Rho, Brown, and collaborators for the "little bag" model, is applied to resolve the proton spin problem via the chiral bag model of Lee et al. (1999) and to describe high-density matter through superqualitons, building on Kaplan's qualiton concept and the color superconductivity work of several groups (1998-1999). The high-density analysis suggests that color superconductivity in QCD may differ fundamentally from BCS superconductivity, a point also raised by Pisarski (1999).
