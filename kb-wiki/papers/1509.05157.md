# Neutrinoless double positron decay and positron emitting electron capture in the interacting boson model

> Neutrinoless double-$־²$ decay is of fundamental importance for determining the neutrino mass. Although double electron ($־²^-־²^-$) decay is the most promising mode, in very recent years interest in double positron ($־²^+־²^+$) decay, positron emitting electron capture ($EC־²^+$), and double electron capture ($ECEC$) has been renewed. We present here results of a calculation of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-$־²^+$ decay and positron emitting electron capture within the framework of the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) for $^{58}$Ni, $^{64}$Zn, $^{78}$Kr, $^{96}$Ru, $^{106}$Cd, $^{124}$Xe, $^{130}$Ba, and $^{136}$Ce decay. By combining these with a calculation of phase space factors we calculate expected half-lives.

## Key claim

The paper presents nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double positron decay and positron emitting electron capture in eight candidate nuclei, calculated within the interacting boson model (IBM-2), and combines them with phase space factors to predict half-lives; the authors note that the NMEs depend on assumptions about short-range correlations and that the Fermi matrix element carries a large error due to isospin violation in nuclei where protons and neutrons occupy the same major shell.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) to compute nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double positron decay and positron emitting electron capture, applying the closure approximation and the formulation of Simkovic et al. for the decay operators. They employed realistic wave functions taken from the literature or fitted to observed energies and electromagnetic properties, and used the Miller-Spencer parametrization for short-range correlations. The matrix elements were then combined with previously calculated phase space factors from Kotila et al. (2012) to obtain half-life predictions for light neutrino exchange with an assumed effective neutrino mass of 1 eV.

## Key numbers

- NME for the ground state transition in $^{64}$Zn $\to$ $^{64}$Ni is 6.13(116), the largest among the eight nuclei studied.
- NME for the ground state transition in $^{58}$Ni $\to$ $^{58}$Fe is 2.31(37), the smallest among the eight nuclei.
- The predicted half-life for $^{124}$Xe $0\nu EC\beta^+$ decay is $0.19 \times 10^{27}$ years for $\langle m_\nu \rangle = 1$ eV, the shortest among all modes and nuclei considered.
- The predicted half-life for $^{136}$Ce $0\nu\beta^+\beta^+$ decay is $174 \times 10^{27}$ years for $\langle m_\nu \rangle = 1$ eV, the longest among all modes and nuclei considered.
- The mass difference for the $^{58}$Ni $\to$ $^{58}$Fe transition is $1926.3 \pm 0.3$ keV, and the isotopic abundance of $^{58}$Ni is $68.077 \pm 0.009\%$.
- The mass difference for the $^{106}$Cd $\to$ $^{106}$Pd transition is $2775.39 \pm 0.10$ keV, and the isotopic abundance of $^{106}$Cd is $1.25 \pm 0.06\%$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of phase space factors for double positron decay modes (Kotila et al., 2012) by adding nuclear matrix element calculations, completing the ingredients needed for half-life predictions. The IBM-2 results are compared with quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations from Hirsch et al. (1994) and more recent QRPA calculations from Suhonen et al. (2011, 2012), showing that QRPA tends to give larger NME values, particularly for $^{96}$Ru and $^{106}$Cd where the two methods differ significantly. The predicted half-lives are much larger than those for neutrinoless double electron decay ($\beta^-\beta^-$) reported in Barea et al. (2012), due to smaller available kinetic energy and Coulomb repulsion of positrons; the results enable experimental searches to focus on the most promising candidate, $^{124}$Xe $EC\beta^+$ decay, which has the shortest predicted half-life.
