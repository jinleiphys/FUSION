# Hydrodynamic theories for a system of weakly self-interacting classical ultra-relativistic scalar particles: microscopic derivations and attractors

> We derive and investigate several hydrodynamic formalisms that emerge from a system of classical, ultra-relativistic scalar particles self-interacting via a quartic potential. The specific form of the total cross-section enables the analytical computation of all transport coefficients that appear in Navier-Stokes (NS), Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK), and second-order transient hydrodynamic theories. We solve all these formalisms in a Bjorken flow scenario and show that NS and BDNK theories display unphysical features when gradients become sufficiently large. This implies that these hydrodynamic approaches may not be suitable to describe the early stages of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Navier-Stokes (NS) and Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) hydrodynamic theories, when derived from a microscopic kinetic theory for weakly self-interacting scalar particles, display unphysical features in Bjorken flow when gradients become sufficiently large, implying these approaches may not be suitable for describing the early stages of heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a classical gas of massless scalar particles with a quartic self-interaction (λφ⁴), whose total cross-section σ_T(s) = λ²/(32πs) enables exact analytical computation of the linearized collision operator's eigensystem. They derive constitutive relations for Navier-Stokes theory via the Chapman-Enskog expansion, for BDNK theory via a modified moment-based perturbative expansion, and for second-order transient hydrodynamics, obtaining exact analytical expressions for all transport coefficients in each formalism. All three hydrodynamic theories are then solved in a Bjorken flow scenario to compare their predictions and domains of validity.

## Key numbers

- Total cross-section: σ_T(s) = λ²/(32πs) ≡ g/s, where g ≡ λ²/(32π)
- Shear viscosity coefficient: η = 96/(g β³), appearing in π^{μν} = (96/(g β³)) σ^{μν}
- Particle diffusion coefficient (for arbitrary matching parameter z): ν^μ = z × (3/(g β²)) ∇^μ α
- Energy diffusion coefficient (for arbitrary matching parameter z): h^μ = (z-1) × (12/(g β³)) ∇^μ α
- Eigenvalues of linearized collision operator: χ_{nℓ} = -(g/2) I_{0,0} × ((n+ℓ-1)/(n+ℓ+1) + δ_{n0}δ_{ℓ0})

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical results for the linearized collision operator eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the λφ⁴ scalar field theory obtained by Denicol (2022) to derive complete hydrodynamic theories. It provides the first exact analytical expressions for all transport coefficients in NS, BDNK, and second-order transient hydrodynamics for this system. The findings contradict the expectation that BDNK theory might be suitable for early-stage heavy-ion collision modeling, and instead support the use of second-order transient hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart theory) for such regimes, while also identifying that second-order theory breaks down when longitudinal pressure becomes initially negative.
