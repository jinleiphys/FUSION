# Parameterizing Smooth Viscous Fluid Dynamics With a Viscous Blast Wave

> Blast wave fits are widely used in high energy nuclear collisions to capture essential features of global properties of systems near kinetic equilibrium. They usually provide temperature fields and collective velocity fields on a given hypersurface. We systematically compare blast wave fits of fluid dynamic simulations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV with the original simulations. In particular, we investigate how faithful the viscous blast wave introduced in \cite{Yang:2022yxa} can reproduce the given temperature and specific shear viscosity fixed at freeze-out of a viscous fluid dynamic calculation, if the final spectrum and elliptic flow of several particle species are fitted. We find that viscous blast wave fits describe fluid dynamic pseudodata rather well and reproduce the specific shear viscosities to good accuracy. However, extracted temperatures tend to be underpredicted, especially for peripheral collisions. We investigate possible reasons for these deviations. We establish maps from true to fitted values. These maps can be used to improve raw fit results from viscous blast wave fits. Although our work is limited to two specific, albeit important, parameters and two collision systems, the same procedure can be easily generalized to other parameters and collision systems.

## Key claim

Viscous blast wave fits can reproduce the specific shear viscosity of fluid dynamic simulations to good accuracy (within 10% in most cases), but extracted freeze-out temperatures tend to be underpredicted, especially for peripheral collisions, with deviations up to 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code MUSIC to generate pseudodata for smooth Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV at various impact parameters, using an optical Glauber model for initial conditions and the s95p-v1.2 equation of state. They then fitted the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of pions, kaons, and protons from these simulations using a viscous blast wave model based on the Retiere-Lisa parameterization, employing Bayesian inference with Gaussian process emulators and Markov Chain Monte Carlo to extract freeze-out temperature and specific shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au with true T_fo=130 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=2.51, extracted values were T_fo=116.4 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=2.47
* For Au+Au with true T_fo=105 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=6.03, extracted values were T_fo=111.2 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=5.83
* For Au+Au with true T_fo=145 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=1.01, extracted values were T_fo=126.3 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=1.23
* For Pb+Pb with true T_fo=130 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=2.51, extracted values were T_fo=123.7 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=2.62
* For Pb+Pb with true T_fo=145 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=1.01, extracted values were T_fo=130.3 MeV and 4pi(eta/s)=1.18
* Pseudodata spectra were assigned 5% uncertainty and v2 data were assigned 2% uncertainty, with an additional pedestal of 0.002 for v2 at low P_T
* Fit ranges for spectra and v2 varied with freeze-out temperature, covering P_T from approximately 0.2 to 3.0 GeV/c depending on particle species and temperature

## Context

This work systematically quantifies the systematic biases in viscous blast wave fits compared to full fluid dynamic simulations, extending the approach of Retiere and Lisa (2003) for ideal blast waves and the viscous blast wave formalism introduced by Yang and Fries (2022). The study establishes maps from true to fitted values of freeze-out temperature and specific shear viscosity that can be used to correct raw blast wave fit results, enabling more precise extraction of hadronic matter properties from experimental data. The authors note that the same procedure can be generalized to other parameters and collision systems beyond the two specific cases studied here.
