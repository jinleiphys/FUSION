# Single inclusive particle production at high energy from HERA data to proton-nucleus collisions

> We study single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions in the CGC framework. The parameters in the calculation are determined solely by standard nuclear geometry and by electron-proton deep inelastic scattering data, which is fit using the running coupling BK equation. We show that it is possible to obtain a good fit of the HERA inclusive cross section also without an anomalous dimension in the initial condition. We argue that one must consistently use the proton transverse area as measured by a high virtuality probe in DIS also for the single inclusive cross section in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions. We show that this leads to a midrapidity nuclear modification ratio R_pA that approaches unity at high transverse momentum independently of sqrt(s), in contrast to most CGC calculations in the literature. We also present predictions for future forward R_pA measurements at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors show that by consistently using the proton transverse area measured by a high virtuality probe in DIS (σ₀/2) rather than the larger "soft" inelastic cross section (σ_inel) for the single inclusive cross section in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, the midrapidity nuclear modification ratio R_pA approaches unity at high transverse momentum independently of √s, contrary to most CGC calculations in the literature.

## Method

The authors fit the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation to combined HERA reduced cross section data for Q² < 50 GeV² and x < 0.01, using three different initial condition parametrizations (MV, MV^γ, and MV^e). They then extend the dipole-proton amplitude to dipole-nucleus scattering using the optical Glauber model with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density, with all parameters determined solely from the HERA fit and standard nuclear geometry. Single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions is computed using k_T-factorization and the hybrid formalism, with the proton transverse area σ₀/2 from the DIS fit used consistently for particle production, while the total inelastic cross section σ_inel is used separately for the Glauber modeling of nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- MV^γ model fit to HERA data yields χ²/d.o.f = 1.17, with σ₀/2 = 16.45 mb, Q_s0² = 0.165 GeV², γ = 1.135, and C² = 6.35
- MV^e model fit yields χ²/d.o.f = 1.15, with σ₀/2 = 16.36 mb, Q_s0² = 0.060 GeV², e_c = 18.9, and C² = 7.2
- Pure MV model fit yields χ²/d.o.f = 2.76, with σ₀/2 = 18.81 mb, Q_s0² = 0.104 GeV², and C² = 14.5
- The ratio (σ₀/2)/σ_inel ranges from approximately 0.2 to 0.3, using σ_inel = 42 mb at RHIC, 44.4 mb at Tevatron, and 70 mb at LHC energies
- The saturation scale of the lead nucleus falls below the proton saturation scale at |b| ≳ 6.3 fm, corresponding to centrality ≳ 70%
- The proton area from J/Ψ data gives σ₀ ≈ 19.5 mb from σ₀ = 4πB_D with B_D ≈ 4 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework by using HERA DIS data (Aaron 2009) to determine all parameters for single inclusive particle production, contrasting with earlier approaches such as Albacete (2012) where the area corresponding to σ₀/2 was set to 42 mb, or Rezaeian (2012) where the initial saturation scale was varied within a large range. The authors argue that the two-component picture of the nucleon transverse structure (a small core of small-x gluons surrounded by a larger soft edge) resolves the discrepancy in R_pA predictions at high p_T, and enables more controlled predictions for future forward R_pA measurements at the LHC.
