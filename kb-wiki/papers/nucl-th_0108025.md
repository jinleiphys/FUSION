# Extended M1 sum rule for excited symmetric and mixed-symmetry states in nuclei

> A generalized M1 sum rule for orbital magnetic dipole strength from excited symmetric states to mixed-symmetry states is considered within the proton-neutron interacting boson model of even-even nuclei. Analytic expressions for the dominant terms in the B(M1) transition rates from the first and second $2^+$ states are derived in the U(5) and SO(6) dynamic symmetry limits of the model, and the applicability of a sum rule approach is examined at and in-between these limits. Lastly, the sum rule is applied to the new data on mixed-symmetry states of 94Mo and a quadrupole d-boson ratio $nd(0^+_1)/nd(2^+_2) \approx 0.6$ is obtained in a largely parameter-independent way

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized M1 sum rule for orbital magnetic dipole strength from excited symmetric states to mixed-symmetry states within the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2), and shows that for the γ-soft nucleus ⁹⁴Mo the ratio of summed M1 strengths from the ground state and the 2⁺₂ state can be used to extract the relative d-boson content of these states in a largely parameter-independent way, provided the partial strengths to the identified mixed-symmetry states exhaust most of the total sum rule strength.

## Method

Analytic expressions for the total M1 strength S_J from a symmetric state with angular momentum J were derived within the IBM-2, generalizing the earlier ground-state sum rule of Ginocchio (1991). The partial strengths Σ_J to the set of experimentally identified mixed-symmetry states (1⁺₁,ₘₛ, 2⁺₁,ₘₛ, 2⁺₂,ₘₛ, 3⁺₁,ₘₛ) were calculated in the U(5) and SO(6) dynamic symmetry limits and along a U(5)-to-SO(6) transition path preserving SO(5) symmetry, using boson numbers N_π=4 and N_ν=1 appropriate for ⁹⁴Mo. The ratio of experimental summed M1 strengths from the 0⁺₁ and 2⁺₂ states was then compared to the sum rule expression to extract the relative d-boson content.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ratio of summed M1 strengths in ⁹⁴Mo: R_{2⁺₂}(0⁺₁)_{Expt} = S(0⁺₁)_{Expt} / S(2⁺₂)_{Expt} = 0.58^{+11}_{-14}
- Extracted relative d-boson content ratio for ⁹⁴Mo: n_d(0⁺₁)/n_d(2⁺₂) ≈ 0.58^{+11}_{-14}
- In the SO(6) dynamic symmetry limit with N=5, the ratio n_d(0⁺₁)/n_d(2⁺₂) = 2/3
- For the U(5) dynamic symmetry limit, n_d(0⁺₁)/n_d(2⁺₂) = 0
- For N=5 in the SO(6) limit, the fraction of total strength exhausted by the partial strength Σ_{2⁺₁} is Y(2⁺₁)=0.58, and for Σ_{2⁺₂} is Y(2⁺₂)=0.85
- For N=5 throughout the U(5)-to-SO(6) transition, the partial strength Σ_{2⁺₂} exhausts more than 85% of the total sum rule strength S_{2⁺₂}
- The shape invariant K_4^{appr.} for ⁹⁴Mo from E2 data is 1.16(17)

## Context

This work extends the ground-state M1 sum rule of Ginocchio (1991) to excited symmetric states, and applies it to the new experimental data on mixed-symmetry states in ⁹⁴Mo from Pietralla et al. (1999, 2000) and Fransen et al. (2001). The analysis enables a parameter-independent determination of the relative d-boson content of low-lying states, which is sensitive to the nuclear shape and can distinguish between the U(5) spherical vibrator and SO(6) γ-unstable rotor limits of the IBM-2. The results are consistent with previous SO(6) predictions for ⁹⁴Mo that relied on assumed boson g-factors, and the approach can be extended to other transitional nuclei where mixed-symmetry states have been identified.
