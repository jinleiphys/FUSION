# The pseudogap regime in the unitary Fermi gas

> We discuss the pseudogap regime in the cold atomic unitary Fermi gas, with a particular emphasis on the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) approach. We discuss possible signatures of the pseudogap, review experimental results, and survey analytic and quantum Monte Carlo techniques before focusing on AFMC calculations in the canonical ensemble. For the latter method, we discuss results for the heat capacity, energy-staggering pairing gap, spin susceptibility, and compare to experiment and other theoretical methods.

## Key claim

The authors review evidence for a pseudogap regime in the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) above the superfluid critical temperature \(T_c\), but emphasize that the existence and temperature range of such a regime remain debated, with different theoretical methods and experiments yielding conflicting conclusions about whether a pseudogap is present and at what temperatures it appears.

## Method

The paper surveys theoretical approaches to the pseudogap in the UFG, including non-self-consistent and self-consistent \(T\)-matrix theories, diagrammatic Monte Carlo, and auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) methods. The authors focus on their own canonical-ensemble AFMC calculations for both trapped and uniform gases, using particle-number projection to compute thermodynamic observables such as the heat capacity, energy-staggering pairing gap, and spin susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- The non-self-consistent \(T\)-matrix approximation predicts \(T_c = 0.24\,T_F\) at unitarity (Strinati 2002), while an extended version predicts \(T_c = 0.21\,T_F\) (Tajima 2014).
- The extended BCS-Leggett theory yields \(T_c = 0.26\,T_F\) at unitarity (Levin 2010).
- The self-consistent Luttinger-Ward theory gives \(T_c = 0.16(1)\,T_F\) at unitarity (Haussmann 2007, Enss 2012).
- AFMC calculations on an \(8^3\) lattice with \(N=50-55\) particles at \(T=0.15\,T_F\) (near the estimated critical temperature \(T_c \lesssim 0.15(1)\,T_F\)) yield a fitted effective gap parameter \(\Delta \approx 0.22\,\varepsilon_F\) (Magierski 2009).
- The same AFMC study estimates the pairing temperature scale \(T^* = 0.20-0.25\,T_F\) from the spin susceptibility suppression (Drut 2013-2).

## Context

This review extends the ongoing debate about pseudogap physics in the UFG, which connects to high-\(T_c\) superconductors (Randeria 1992, Trivedi 1995) and the BCS-BEC crossover (Eagles 1969, Leggett 1980, Nozieres and Schmidt-Rink 1985). The authors compare experimental results from photoemission spectroscopy (Gaebler 2010, Jin 2015) and thermodynamic measurements (Ku 2012) with multiple theoretical approaches, highlighting that the self-consistent Luttinger-Ward theory finds only weak pseudogap signatures while non-self-consistent \(T\)-matrix theories and some AFMC calculations show clear gapped structures above \(T_c\). Resolving the pseudogap question requires precise calculations with controllable errors, which the authors' canonical-ensemble AFMC methods aim to provide.
