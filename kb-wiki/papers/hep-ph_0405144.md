# Regge Description of pion-pion, pion-Kaon, pion-N, Kaon-N and NN Forward Scattering Above E_{kin}>1 Gev

> We review our recent description of high energy forward hadronic cross sections and in particular of pion scattering data by means of Regge theory. We obtain a prediction for the total proton-proton cross section at LHC. In addition, and contrary to some suggestions in the literature we show that standard Regge behavior can accommodate both crossing symmetry and factorization. Some consequences for low energy pion-pion scattering are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Standard Regge theory can simultaneously satisfy crossing symmetry and factorization for forward hadronic scattering, contrary to earlier suggestions in the literature that factorization might be "badly broken" for pion-pion scattering.

## Method

The author performed a Regge fit to total cross section data for \(NN\), \(\pi^\pm N\), \(K^\pm N\), and \(\pi\pi\) scattering from \(E_{kin} \simeq 1\) GeV up to about 16 GeV, using a simple Pomeron parametrization with \(\alpha_P(0) = 1\). The fit assumed factorization to relate different processes, and was extended to multi-TeV energies by adding a soft logarithmic term to the Pomeron channel.

## Key numbers

- Total \(pp\) cross section at LHC (14 TeV) predicted to be \(\sigma^{tot}_{pp}(14\text{ TeV}) = 108 \pm 4 \pm 4\) mb, in agreement with the most recent Regge analysis of \(111.5 \pm 1.2^{+4.1}_{-2.1}\) mb.
- \(\pi\pi\) total cross section at 20 GeV: \(\sigma^{tot}_{\pi\pi}(20\text{ GeV}) = 13.4 \pm 0.6\) mb, dominated by the Pomeron and consistent with the Regge parameterization of \(\simeq 12.3 \pm 0.3\) mb from the Review of Particle Properties 2004.
- Measured \(\pi^+\pi^-\) total cross section at \(32\text{ GeV}^2\): \(13.5 \pm 2.5\) mb.
- Earlier analysis gave \(\sigma^\infty \simeq 15\) mb from \(\pi N\) and \(NN\) data, while later suggestions gave \(\sigma^\infty = 6 \pm 5\) mb, later raised to at least 8.3 mb.

## Context

This work extends the Regge description of forward hadronic cross sections to include \(\pi\pi\) scattering, resolving a long-standing tension from the early 1970s where crossing symmetry sum rules appeared incompatible with factorization (Pennington, 1974; Martin, 1977). The results contradict the reanalysis used in recent dispersive studies of \(\pi\pi\) scattering (Ananthanarayan, 2000; Colangelo, 2001), which used \(\sigma^\infty = 5 \pm 3\) mb and were unaware of the existing high-energy \(\pi\pi\) data. The improved Regge description enables more precise dispersive evaluations of low-energy \(\pi\pi\) observables relevant for testing Chiral Perturbation Theory, and shows that the parameterizations of Colangelo (2001) fail the Olsson sum rule by 2.5 standard deviations and Froissart-Gribov representations by 4 to 5 standard deviations.
