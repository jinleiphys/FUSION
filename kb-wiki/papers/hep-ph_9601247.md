# Thermal Equilibration in an Expanding Parton Plasma

> Thermalization in an expanding parton plasma is studied within the framework of Boltzmann equation in the absence of any mean fields. In particular, we study the time-dependence of the relaxation time to the lowest order in finite temperature QCD and how such time-dependence affects the thermalization of an expanding parton plasma. Because of Debye screening and Landau damping at finite temperature, the relaxation time (or transport rates) is free of infrared divergencies in both longitudinal and transverse interactions. The resultant relaxation time decreases with time in an expanding plasma like $1/τ^β$, with $β<1$. We prove in this case that thermal equilibrium will eventually be established given a long life-time of the system. However, a fixed momentum cut-off in the calculation of the relaxation time gives rise to a much stronger time dependence which will slow down thermal equilibrium. It is also demonstrated that the ``memory effect'' of the initial condition affects the approach to thermal equilibrium and the final entropy production.

## Key claim

Thermal equilibrium in an expanding parton plasma will eventually be established only if the relaxation time increases with time slower than linearly (β < 1 in θ ∝ τ^β); a fixed momentum cut-off in the relaxation time calculation produces a much stronger time dependence that slows down thermal equilibrium, and the "memory effect" of the initial condition affects both the approach to equilibrium and the final entropy production.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a one-dimensionally expanding gluon plasma, using the Bjorken scaling flow. They compute the gluon damping rate and transport relaxation time to lowest order in finite-temperature QCD, including Debye screening and Landau damping via resummed gluon propagators, and then study the time evolution of the energy density for different power-law time dependences of the relaxation time (θ ∝ τ^β) and different initial rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- The gluon damping rate for a fast gluon is Γ^(gg) ≈ N_c α_s T [ln(1/α_s) − 0.1 + O(α_s)] (Equation 20).
- The relaxation time for a pure gluonic gas near local thermal equilibrium is 1/θ ≈ 0.92 N_c^2 T α_s^2 ln(1.6/(N_c α_s)).
- For an ideal gluon gas with one-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion, temperature decreases as T/T_0 = (τ_0/τ)^(1/3).
- The thermalization time τ_th is approximately 5 θ_0 for β = 1/3, when thermalization is defined as G(τ/τ_0) being within 10% of its hydrodynamic limit.
- For β < 1, the asymptotic solution gives G ∝ (τ_0/τ)^(1/3), corresponding to the one-dimensional hydrodynamic limit ε ∝ (τ_0/τ)^(4/3).
- For β > 1, the asymptotic solution gives G = constant, corresponding to the free-streaming limit ε ∝ τ_0/τ.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of thermalization in expanding parton plasmas by Baym (1984) and Gavin (1991), who studied constant and linear time dependences of the relaxation time, respectively. The authors show that the Debye screening and Landau damping, which naturally regularize infrared divergences in finite-temperature QCD, produce a weak time dependence of the relaxation time (β ≈ 1/3) that allows thermal equilibrium to be reached, unlike the fixed momentum cut-off used in parton cascade models such as those of Wang and Gyulassy (1991). The results enable more realistic modeling of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and demonstrate that numerical simulations must include dynamical screening rather than artificial cut-offs to correctly predict equilibration times.
