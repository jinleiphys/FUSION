# One-loop contributions in the EFT for the $ΛN \to NN$ transition

> We consider the $ΛN\to NN$ weak transition, responsible for a large fraction of the non-mesonic weak decay of hypernuclei. We follow on the previously derived effective field theory and compute the next-to-leading one-loop corrections. Explicit expressions for all diagrams are provided, which result in contributions to all relevant partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper computes the next-to-leading order (NLO) one-loop corrections to the effective field theory (EFT) for the weak $\Lambda N \to NN$ transition, but notes that the 18 new low-energy constants (LECs) introduced at NLO cannot be constrained by current hypernuclear decay data, making the fitting procedure intractable without approximations such as neglecting the initial momentum of the bound baryons.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with Weinberg power counting to compute all one-loop diagrams contributing to the $\Lambda N \to NN$ transition at NLO, including two-pion-exchange diagrams (ball, triangle, box, and crossed-box topologies), caramel diagrams, and contact terms with up to two derivative operators. They derive analytic expressions for each diagram using master integrals and the heavy baryon expansion, retaining explicit baryon mass differences ($M_\Lambda - M_N$, $M_\Sigma - M_\Lambda$) in propagators and spinors. The NLO contact potential is simplified by assuming the initial baryon momentum is negligible compared to the final nucleon momentum ($\vec{p} \approx 0$, $\vec{q} \approx \vec{p}\,'$), reducing the 18 general LECs to 6 NLO contact terms.

## Key numbers

- Weak Fermi coupling constant: $G_F m_\pi^2 = 2.21 \times 10^{-7}$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 138$ MeV; kaon mass: $m_K = 495$ MeV
- Weak coupling constants for $\Lambda \to N\pi$: $A = 1.05$, $B = -7.15$
- Weak coupling constants for $\Sigma \to N\pi$: $A_{\Sigma 1/2} = -0.59$, $A_{\Sigma 3/2} = 2.00$, $B_{\Sigma 1/2} = -15.68$, $B_{\Sigma 3/2} = -0.26$
- Kaon weak couplings from SU(3): $C_K^{PC} = -18.9$, $D_K^{PC} = 6.63$, $C_K^{PV} = 0.76$, $D_K^{PV} = 2.09$
- Strong coupling constants: $g_A = 1.290$, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $D_s = 0.822$, $F_s = 0.468$
- Weak chiral Lagrangian parameters: $h_{2\pi} = -10.13$ MeV, $h_{\Lambda N} = 81.02$ MeV
- Mass difference: $M_\Lambda - M_N = 116$ MeV
- Cross-section for the inverse reaction $np \to \Lambda p$ is of order $10^{-12}$ mb (Haidenbauer 1995, Parreno 1998, Inoue 2001)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order EFT for the $\Lambda N \to NN$ transition previously developed by the same group (Perez-Obiol et al. 2011, Jun 2000, Parreno et al. 2005) by computing the next-to-leading order one-loop corrections. The approach parallels the successful EFT treatment of the strong nucleon-nucleon interaction (Epelbaum 2012, Entem 2003) and weak parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions (nucPV05). The results enable future fits to hypernuclear decay data from s-shell (A=5) and p-shell (A=12, 16) hypernuclei, though the authors note that current experimental data are insufficient to constrain the 18 NLO LECs without approximations.
