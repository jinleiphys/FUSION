# Finite-volume energy spectrum of the $K^-K^-K^-$ system

> The dynamics of multi-kaon systems are of relevance for several areas of nuclear physics. However, even the simplest systems, two and three kaons, are hard to prepare and study experimentally. Here we show how to extract this information using first-principle lattice QCD results. We (1) extend the relativistic three-body quantization condition to the strangeness sector, predicting for the first time the excited level finite-volume spectrum of three kaon systems at maximal isospin, and (2) present a first lattice QCD calculation of the excited levels of this system in a finite box. We compare our predictions with the lattice results reported here and with previous ground state calculations and find very good agreement.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic three-body quantization condition to the strangeness sector and presents the first lattice QCD calculation of excited finite-volume levels of the \(K^-K^-K^-\) system, finding very good agreement between the predicted spectrum and the lattice results, with the caveat that the three-body force is set to zero and the two-body input relies on chiral extrapolations.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic three-body quantization condition (3bQC) from Mai et al. (2017) and Mai et al. (2019), extended to higher irreps and boosts, with the two-body \(K\)-matrix determined by the inverse amplitude method (IAM) using next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory. Lattice QCD correlation functions are computed on two \(N_f=2\) ensembles (GWUQCD) with nHYP-smeared clover fermions, and the finite-volume spectrum is extracted via a variational analysis of a matrix of three-kaon operators projected to irreducible representations of the cubic group.

## Key numbers

- Lattice ensemble \(\mathcal{E}_1\): \(48 \times 24^3\) lattice, \(a = 0.1210(2)(24)\) fm, \(a M_\pi = 0.1931(4)\), \(a M_K = 0.3236(3)\), 300 configurations.
- Lattice ensemble \(\mathcal{E}_4\): \(64 \times 24^3\) lattice, \(a = 0.1215(3)(24)\) fm, \(a M_\pi = 0.1378(6)\), \(a M_K = 0.3132(3)\), 400 configurations.
- The largest change in any energy eigenvalue when varying the spectator momentum cutoff from \(L|\bm{p}_{\text{max}}| = 2\pi\sqrt{5}\) to \(2\pi\sqrt{11}\) is \(\sim 3 \times 10^{-3}\%\) for the first excited level in \(A_{1u}\) at \(M_\pi = 315\) MeV.
- Replacing the subthreshold \(KK\) amplitude with a constant at \(s = 3M_K^2\) and then at \(s = 3.95M_K^2\) leads to a maximal change of any energy eigenvalue of 0.02\%.
- The use of modified IAM instead of IAM changes the scattering length by \(\lesssim 0.1\%\).

## Context

This work extends the three-body formalism previously applied to three-pion systems in maximal isospin (Mai et al., 2018; Mai et al., 2019; Culver et al., 2019; Blanton et al., 2019; Fischer et al., 2020; Hansen et al., 2020) to the strange sector, enabling chiral extrapolations along arbitrary \(M_K(M_\pi)\) trajectories. It provides the first lattice QCD calculation of excited three-kaon states, going beyond the ground-state calculations of the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2007; Detmold et al., 2008), and opens the door to extracting information relevant for strange resonances, kaon condensates in neutron stars, and heavy-ion collision correlations.
