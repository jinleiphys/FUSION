# Nuclear modification of transverse-longitudinal structure function ratio

> We investigate the nuclear effects on the transverse and longitudinal responses in the deep inelastic region due to the nuclear binding and nucleon Fermi motion. We display the role of the transverse-longitudinal admixture due to the transverse nucleon momentum. The mixing effect is appreciable at small Q^2 values, and gradually disappears at large Q^2 values. The nuclear modification is then dominated by the binding and Fermi-motion effects which are contained in the spectral function.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification of the transverse-longitudinal structure function ratio \(R(x,Q^2)\) arises primarily from a transverse-longitudinal admixture due to the nucleon's transverse momentum relative to the virtual photon, an effect that is appreciable at small \(Q^2\) and gradually disappears at large \(Q^2\), where binding and Fermi-motion effects dominate.

## Method

The authors used a convolution model with a spectral function for nucleons in a target nucleus, specifically \(^{14}\text{N}\), described by a density-dependent Hartree-Fock model (Bonche, Koonin, and Negele, 1976). They projected the nuclear hadron tensor onto transverse and longitudinal structure functions, deriving expressions that explicitly contain mixing terms proportional to \(\vec p_{N\perp}^2 / Q^2\). Numerical integrations were performed using shell-model wave functions and the MRST 1998 parton distributions for the nucleon, with the SLAC 1990 parametrization for the nucleon ratio \(R_N\).

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) and medium \(x \approx 0.5\), the transverse-longitudinal admixture produces about a 5% positive modification in the ratio \(R_{^{14}\text{N}} / R_N\).
- The mixing coefficient is estimated as \(\vec p_{N\perp}^2 / \pt_N^{\, 2} \approx 4 x_N^2 \langle \vec p_{N\perp}^{\, 2} \rangle / Q^2 \approx 0.04\) at \(Q^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- At \(Q^2 = 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(x_N = 0.5\), the ratio \(2 x_N F_1 / F_L^N\) is 14, and the overall admixture correction is about 3%.
- At \(Q^2 = 100\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(x_N = 0.5\), the ratio \(2 x_N F_1 / F_L^N\) is 53, and the overall admixture correction is about 1%.
- The Hartree-Fock model for \(^{14}\text{N}\) yields a binding energy of about 101.7 MeV (experimental: 104.66 MeV) and a root-mean-square radius of 2.54 fm (experimental: 2.45 fm).

## Context

This work extends the notion of transverse-longitudinal admixture, previously introduced by Chanfray et al. (1993) in the low-energy quasi-elastic regime, to the deep inelastic regime. It addresses the nuclear modification of \(R\) following the initial large effect reported by the HERMES collaboration (2000), which was later not confirmed by neutrino data from CCFR (2001) and a HERMES reanalysis (2002). The results provide a baseline for future experimental searches for nuclear modifications of \(R\) at medium and large \(x\), and the authors note that short-range nucleon correlations and off-shell structure functions, not included here, will be important for extending the work to large \(x\).
