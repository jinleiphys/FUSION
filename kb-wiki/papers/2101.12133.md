# A Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model for Heavy Nuclei

> An extension of the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) is proposed, based on the pseudo-SU(3) model. The Hamiltonian and the spectroscopic factor operator of the model are presented and a procedure of constructing the model space. Because a huge number of SU(3) irreducible representations (irrep) appear, one has to be careful in designing a practical, consistent path to reduce the Hilbert space. The concept of forbiddenness, taking into account excitations of the clusters, is introduced and applied. The applications are to two systems with a low forbiddenness, namely to 236U -> 210Pb + 26Ne and 224Ra -> 210Pb + 14C, and to 236U -> 146Xe + 90Sr, which appears in the fission of 236U, which requires a large forbiddenness. Energies, electromagnetic transitions and spectroscopic factors are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) to heavy nuclei using the pseudo-SU(3) model, but the abstract glosses over that the model space must be severely restricted due to an exploding number of SU(3) irreducible representations, and that the concept of forbiddenness (cluster excitation quanta) is essential for connecting cluster configurations to the ground state of the parent nucleus.

## Method

The authors construct the model space by treating protons and neutrons separately within the pseudo-SU(3) framework, coupling cluster irreps for each nucleon type with relative motion irreps, then combining proton and neutron results using only the linear (aligned) coupling. The forbiddenness is computed using a formula that determines the minimal number of excitation quanta that must be transferred from relative motion to cluster excitations to reach the ground state irrep of the united nucleus, and the Hamiltonian includes terms for the pseudo-oscillator quanta, Casimir operators of pseudo-SU(3), angular momentum, and K-splitting.

## Key numbers

- For 236U → 210Pb + 26Ne and 224Ra → 210Pb + 14C: low forbiddenness systems (no explicit numerical value given for the forbiddenness itself, but stated as "low")
- For 236U → 146Xe + 90Sr: requires a "large forbiddenness" (no explicit numerical value given)
- The effective quadrupole charge formula: e_eff(E2) = (Z/Ã)^2 (A/Ã)^(4/3), where A and Ã are total nucleons and nucleons in normal orbitals, Z is total protons
- The scaling factor for mapping SU(3) operators to pseudo-SU(3) operators: κ = (η + 3/2)/(η + 1/2), close to 1

## Context

This work extends the SACM previously developed for light nuclei by Cseh and collaborators (Cseh, 1990s) and builds on the pseudo-SU(3) model of Hecht and Arima (1970s). It addresses the problem that earlier attempts to extend SACM to heavy nuclei (Cseh and Algora, 2000s) did not treat clusters and the united nucleus in the same mean field. The concept of forbiddenness was introduced by Smirnov (1990s) and applied successfully in fission fragment predictions; this paper provides a formula from Huitz (2015) to compute it. The work enables systematic calculations of energies, electromagnetic transitions, and spectroscopic factors for heavy cluster systems, including those relevant to radioactive decay and fission.
