# Structure of the nucleon in chiral perturbation theory

> We discuss a renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory which provides a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams. The method involves finite subtractions of dimensionally regularized diagrams beyond the standard modified minimal subtraction scheme of chiral perturbation theory to remove contributions violating the power counting. This is achieved by a suitable renormalization of the parameters of the most general effective Lagrangian. As applications we discuss the mass of the nucleon, the $σ$ term, and the scalar and electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an "extended on-mass-shell" (EOMS) renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory that restores a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams, which the standard modified minimal subtraction scheme of chiral perturbation theory fails to provide in the one-nucleon sector.

## Method

The method involves performing additional finite subtractions of dimensionally regularized diagrams beyond the standard modified minimal subtraction scheme, where terms violating the power counting are identified by expanding integrands in small quantities and are then absorbed through a suitable renormalization of the parameters of the most general effective Lagrangian. The approach is applied to one-loop calculations of the nucleon mass, the sigma term, and the scalar and electromagnetic form factors, and is compared with the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and the heavy-baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1990) and Bernard et al. (1992).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit (fixed m_s ≠ 0): m = 882.8 MeV, obtained from m_N = 938.3 MeV with Δm = 55.5 MeV, using parameters c1 = -0.9 m_N^{-1}, c2 = 2.5 m_N^{-1}, c3 = -4.2 m_N^{-1}, c4 = 2.3 m_N^{-1}, g_A = 1.267, F_π = 92.4 MeV, M_π = 139.6 MeV
- Sigma term at O(q^4): σ = 40.5 MeV, compared with the dispersive analysis σ = (45 ± 8) MeV of Gasser et al. (1990)
- Delta_sigma (difference between scalar form factor at t = 2 M_π^2 and t = 0): Δ_σ = 16.9 MeV, compared with the dispersive analysis Δ_σ = (15.2 ± 0.4) MeV of Gasser et al. (1990)
- The O(q^4) results for electromagnetic form factors provide a decent description only up to Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory framework originally developed by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988), which suffered from a mismatch between the loop expansion and the chiral expansion in the one-nucleon sector. The EOMS scheme provides an alternative to the heavy-baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1990) and Bernard et al. (1992), which produces problems with analyticity (generating poles at v·q = 0 instead of the correct nucleon pole structure), and to the infrared regularization of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), which can be reformulated in a form analogous to the EOMS scheme. The approach enables consistent power counting in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory and can be extended to include vector and axial-vector mesons explicitly, as well as to multi-loop diagrams.
