# Lepton Flavor Violation: Constraints from exotic muon to electron conversion

> The exotic neutrinoless $μ^- - e^-$ nuclear conversion is studied within the conventional extensions of the standard model as well as in the minimal supersymmetric (SUSY) models with and without R-parity conservation. The dependence of the $μ^- - e^-$ conversion rates on the nucleon and nuclear structure is consistently taken into account. Using our calculated transition matrix elements and the available experimental data on the branching ratio $R_{μe^-}$ for $^{48}$Ti and $^{208}$Pb as well as the expected experimental sensitivity for $^{27}$Al employed as a target in the planned at Brookhaven $μ^--e^-$ conversion (MECO) experiment, we extract very severe constraints for the flavor violation parameters. We especially emphasize on the constraints resulting for SUSY R-parity violating parameters.

## Key claim

The paper extracts constraints on lepton flavor violating parameters from muon-to-electron conversion data, but these constraints depend on the assumption that only one component of the conversion Lagrangian dominates at a time, and the nuclear structure dependence of the quantity Q can be neglected because the isovector terms are small.

## Method

The authors calculated the transition matrix elements for coherent muon-to-electron conversion in 27Al, 48Ti, and 208Pb using proton densities from electron scattering data and neutron densities from pionic atom data, with the muon wave function obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with Coulomb potential and including vacuum polarization corrections. They then used these matrix elements together with experimental upper limits on the branching ratio R_mu_e for 48Ti and 208Pb, and the expected sensitivity for 27Al in the planned MECO experiment, to extract upper bounds on the quantities rho and Q that contain the flavor-violating parameters. For the R-parity violating SUSY models, they derived constraints on products of trilinear couplings lambda lambda' from the experimental limits.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on branching ratio R_mu_e for 48Ti target: R_mu_e < 7.0 x 10^-13 at 90% confidence level (from SINDRUM II at PSI)
- Upper limit on branching ratio R_mu_e for 208Pb target: R_mu_e < 4.6 x 10^-11 at 90% confidence level (from SINDRUM II at PSI)
- Expected sensitivity for 27Al target in MECO experiment at Brookhaven: R_mu_e < 2 x 10^-17
- Bound on scalar current coupling alpha^(0)_(+/-S) from 27Al target: |alpha^(0)_(+/-S)| < 7 x 10^-10

## Context

This work extends previous studies of muon-to-electron conversion rates (KVF98, Chiang) by providing updated nuclear matrix elements for 27Al, 48Ti, and 208Pb and using the most recent experimental limits from SINDRUM II (Dohmen 1998, Honec) and the projected sensitivity of the MECO experiment (Molzon). The constraints on R-parity violating SUSY parameters derived here are significantly more stringent than those previously known in the literature (Rp-rev, Huitu, Kos-Kov), and the authors note that if the ongoing PSI and planned MECO experiments reach their quoted sensitivities, muon-to-electron conversion will provide the best constraints on all products of R-parity violating parameters measurable in this process.
