# Nonperturbative solution of scalar Yukawa model in two- and three-body Fock space truncations

> The Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff method of finding the nonperturbative solutions in field theory is based on the Fock decomposition of the state vector, complemented with the sector-dependent nonperturbative renormalization scheme. We show in detail how to implement the renormalization procedure and to solve the simplest nontrivial example of the scalar Yukawa model in the two- and three-body Fock space truncations incorporating scalar "nucleon" and one or two scalar "pions".

## Key claim

The Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff method with Fock sector-dependent renormalization (FSDR) can provide nonperturbative solutions for the scalar Yukawa model in two- and three-body Fock space truncations, but the cancellation of divergences is not guaranteed by standard perturbative renormalization because diagrams of the same order in the coupling constant may correspond to different Fock sectors, and omitted higher Fock sectors remove divergent contributions needed to cancel those from included sectors.

## Method

The authors solved the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation for the scalar Yukawa model (a heavy scalar "nucleon" with mass m=0.94 GeV coupled to lighter scalar "pions" with mass μ=0.14 GeV) by truncating the Fock decomposition to include only one nucleon plus up to two pions. They implemented the FSDR scheme where bare coupling constants and mass counterterms are determined sector-by-sector: first in the two-body truncation (one nucleon plus one pion), then in the three-body truncation (one nucleon plus two pions), using the previously determined parameters from lower sectors. Regularization was performed by introducing a Pauli-Villars scalar pion field with large mass μ_PV.

## Key numbers

- Physical masses: scalar nucleon m = 0.94 GeV, scalar pion μ = 0.14 GeV (only the ratio μ/m matters)
- Dimensionless coupling constant defined as α ≡ g²/(16π m²), where g is the physical coupling constant
- The two-body vertex function on the energy shell (s₂ = m²) gives the on-shell condition k⊥*(x) = i√(m²x² + μ²(1-x))
- The renormalization condition requires √(Z_χ) × √(Z_χ) × √(Z_φ) × on-shell vertex = g, with Z_φ = 1 for point-like pions

## Context

This work extends the FSDR approach previously developed for the Yukawa model with spin-1/2 fermions (Karmanov, 2008) and applies it to the simpler scalar Yukawa model to illustrate the method without complications from spin structure. The paper fills a gap by providing the detailed derivation of bare coupling constants and mass counterterms used in earlier four-body truncation studies (Li, 2015a; Li, 2015b), and enables future relativistic bound state calculations up to four-body truncation (two heavy plus two light scalar bosons). The approach contrasts with that of Bernard (2001), who studied the same model in three-body truncation without FSDR, which the authors argue is not universal for divergence cancellation.
