# Rapid neutron star cooling triggered by dark matter

> We study the effect of asymmetric fermionic dark matter (DM) on the thermal evolution of neutron stars (NSs). No interaction between DM and baryonic matter is assumed, except the gravitational one. Using the two-fluid formalism, we show that DM accumulated in the core of a star pulls inwards the outer baryonic layers of the star, increasing the baryonic density in the NS core. As a result, it significantly affects the star's thermal evolution by triggering an early onset of the direct Urca process and modifying the photon emission from the surface caused by the decrease of the radius. Thus, due to the gravitational pull of DM, the direct Urca process becomes kinematically allowed for stars with lower masses. Based on these results, we discuss the importance of NS observations at different distances from the Galactic center. Since the DM distribution peaks towards the Galactic center, NSs in this region are expected to contain higher DM fractions that could lead to a different cooling behavior.

## Key claim

The presence of asymmetric fermionic dark matter (DM) that interacts with baryonic matter only through gravity can trigger an early onset of the direct Urca process in neutron stars, causing rapid cooling at lower stellar masses than would occur in purely baryonic stars.

## Method

The authors model neutron stars using a two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) formalism where baryonic matter and DM interact only gravitationally, with DM treated as a relativistic Fermi gas of non-interacting spin-one-half particles at zero temperature. They use two different baryonic equations of state (IST and FSU2R) and implement the resulting density profiles into the publicly available thermal evolution code NScool to compute cooling curves, accounting for nucleon pairing via the SFB and CCDK gap models.

## Key numbers

- For the FSU2R equation of state, the mass at the direct Urca onset drops from 1.92 M_sun (pure baryonic) to 1.83 M_sun, 1.79 M_sun, and 1.75 M_sun for DM fractions of 2%, 3%, and 4%, respectively
- For the IST equation of state, the direct Urca onset mass drops from 1.91 M_sun (pure baryonic) to 1.83 M_sun, 1.80 M_sun, and 1.76 M_sun for DM fractions of 2%, 3%, and 4%, respectively
- A DM fraction of only 0.161% (IST) or 0.378% (FSU2R) with DM particle mass m_DM = 1 GeV is sufficient to trigger the direct Urca process in a 1.9 M_sun star
- The baryonic radius of a 1.9 M_sun star modeled with the IST equation of state decreases from 10.58 km (pure baryonic) to 10.05 km with 4% DM fraction
- The baryonic radius of a 1.9 M_sun star modeled with the FSU2R equation of state decreases from 12.16 km (pure baryonic) to 11.47 km with 4% DM fraction

## Context

This work extends the two-fluid DM-admixed neutron star formalism developed by Giangrandi (2022) and Ivanytskyi (2019) to include thermal evolution, showing that gravitational-only DM interactions can significantly alter cooling behavior. The results suggest that neutron stars observed near the Galactic center, where DM density peaks, may exhibit different cooling curves than those farther out, providing a potential observational signature that could be tested with X-ray telescopes such as Chandra and future facilities like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
