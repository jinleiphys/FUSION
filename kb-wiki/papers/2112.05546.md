# Hybrid stars from a constrained equation of state

> We determine, within a meta-model, the properties of the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS) that allow for a phase transition to deconfinement matter. It is shown that the properties that define the isoscalar channel are the ones that are affected, in particular, a phase transition implies much larger values of the skewness and kurtosis. The effect of multi-quark interaction channels in the description of the quark phase in hybrid stars is also studied. NS properties, such as the mass and radius of the quark core, show an interplay dependence between the 8-quark vector and the 4-quark isovector-vector interactions. We show that low mass NS, $M\sim 1.4 M_\odot$, may already contain a quark core, and satisfy all existing NS observational constraints. We discuss the strangeness content of the quark core and its influence on the speed of sound.

# Hybrid stars from a constrained equation of state

## Key claim

A phase transition to quark matter in neutron stars requires a harder isoscalar channel for the nucleonic equation of state than expected from purely nucleonic degrees of freedom, specifically requiring much larger values of the skewness (Q_sat) and kurtosis (Z_sat). Low-mass neutron stars around 1.4 solar masses may already contain a quark core while still satisfying all existing observational constraints, depending on the interplay between 8-quark vector and 4-quark isovector-vector interactions.

## Method

The authors use a two-model approach connecting a hadronic phase to a quark phase through a first-order phase transition via the Maxwell construction. The hadronic phase is described either by a meta-model based on a Taylor expansion around saturation density (sampling an 8-dimensional parameter space from a multivariate Gaussian) or by the relativistic mean-field model DDME2. The quark phase uses a SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with multi-quark interaction channels, including vector-isoscalar, vector-isovector, and 8-quark vector interactions, with the bag constant B controlling the transition density.

## Key numbers

- For hybrid stars with a phase transition in the range 1.3 n_0 to 2.5 n_0, the mean isoscalar skewness Q_sat = 434.37 MeV and mean isoscalar kurtosis Z_sat = 557.83 MeV, compared to Q_sat = 58.62 MeV and Z_sat = -181.97 MeV for purely nucleonic EoS
- The quark core mass M_QC has a mean value of 0.08 solar masses and a maximum value of 1.29 solar masses across all hybrid EoS
- The quark core radius R_QC has a mean value of 1.99 km and a maximum value of 8.78 km
- The binary effective tidal deformability for hybrid EoS with chi_omega=0 and chi_rho=0 concentrates around 650-750 for mass ratios q between 0.8 and 1.0
- For chi_omega=0.1 and chi_rho=0, the tidal deformability is approximately 800, still compatible with GW170817 for q greater than 0.8
- The NJL model parameters are: cutoff Lambda = 623.58 MeV, light quark masses m_u,d = 5.70 MeV, strange quark mass m_s = 136.60 MeV, G_S * Lambda^2 = 1.67, G_D * Lambda^5 = 13.67, with constituent quark masses M_u,d = 332.2 MeV and M_s = 510.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using NJL-type models (Pagliara 2007, Benic 2014, Zacchi 2015, Camara Pereira 2016, Wu 2018) by systematically exploring the multi-quark interaction parameter space and its effect on neutron star observables. The authors build on their earlier work (Ferreira 2020, Ferreira 2021) to show that the 8-quark vector channel (chi_omega_omega) is essential for generating massive quark cores, while the 4-quark isovector-vector channel (chi_rho) controls strangeness content and reduces the speed of sound. The results enable future studies to identify which combinations of quark interaction channels can simultaneously satisfy the constraints from massive pulsars (PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 solar masses, MSP J0740+6620 at 2.08 solar masses), the GW170817 tidal deformability upper bound, and NICER radius measurements.
