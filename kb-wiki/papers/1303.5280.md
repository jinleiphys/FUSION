# Forward-backward multiplicity correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a superposition approach

> We analyze the multiplicity correlations between distant forward and backward rapidity regions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a superposition framework, where the particle production occurs through independent emission from correlated sources. This in principle allows for inferring information on the long-range forward-backward correlations of the sources in the earliest phase of the collision, based solely on the experimental information on the statistical features of the observed particle distributions. Our three-stage study incorporates the fluctuations of parton production in the early phase, the effect of intermediate hydrodynamic evolution, as well as fluctuations in the production of particles at freeze-out. We investigate the dependence of the results on the features of the overlaid distributions and hydrodynamics, as well as the centrality dependence. We analyze the existing data from the STAR Collaboration. Predictions for the forward-backward multiplicity correlations in Pb+Pb collisions to be analyzed at the LHC are also made.

## Key claim

The paper shows that forward-backward multiplicity correlations of final particles can be related to the correlations of initial sources through a single parameter that collects the effects of overlaid distributions and hydrodynamic evolution, but the extraction of source correlations from data requires complete experimental information including both the correlation coefficient and the variance at each centrality, which was not available from the STAR data.

## Method

The authors develop a three-stage superposition model where initial sources produce partons, hydrodynamic evolution converts these deterministically to freeze-out sources, and statistical hadronization produces final particles. They derive analytic relations linking the statistical moments (mean, variance, covariance) of the source distribution to those of the final particle distribution, with parameters capturing the effects of the overlaid distributions and hydrodynamics. The model is applied to STAR data for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, accounting for the reference bin correlation effect described by Lappi (2009) and Bzdak (2011), and predictions are made for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV using the Glauber model from GLISSANDO.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the STAR measured correlation coefficient rho*(n_F,n_B) = 0.58 for centrality 0-10% and 0 for centrality 40-50%; the scaled variance omega*(n_A) = 3.9 for 0-10% and 1.05 for 40-50%
- For Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, rho*(n_F,n_B) = 0.48 for centrality 0-10% and 0 for 40-50%; omega*(n_A) = 2.7 for 0-10% and 1.1 for 40-50%
- The ratio of mean multiplicities between peripheral and central bins is taken as <n_A>/<n_R> = 0.2
- For central Au+Au collisions, the estimate rho(n_F,n_B) approximately 0.72 from Bzdak (2011) implies rho(s_F,s_B) is limited between 0.72 and about 0.85, with rho(s_A,s_R) between 0.58 and 0.65
- The predicted ratio rho(n_F,n_B)/rho(s_F,s_B) for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV shows a gradual fall-off with centrality, with the lambda parameter expected in the range 1-2

## Context

This work extends the superposition model analysis of Brogueira and Dias de Deus (2006) and Bialas, Bzdak, and Zalewski (2011) by incorporating a three-stage framework with hydrodynamic evolution and statistical hadronization. The analysis accounts for the reference bin correlation effect identified by Lappi and McLerran (2009) and Bzdak (2011), which significantly affects the interpretation of STAR data from Tarnowsky (2008) and Abelev (2009). The results confirm the puzzling finding of Lappi (2009) and Bzdak (2011) that for central collisions, forward-backward source correlations are stronger than forward-central correlations. The predictions for LHC Pb+Pb collisions enable future experimental tests of early-phase production models.
