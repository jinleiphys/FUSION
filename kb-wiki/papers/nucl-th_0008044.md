# Thermal and Nonthermal Pion Enhancements with Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> The pion production by sigma decay and its relation with chiral symmetry restoration in a hot and dense matter are investigated in the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The decay rate for the process sigma -> 2pion to the lowest order in a 1/N_c expansion is calculated as a function of temperature T and chemical potential mu. The thermal and nonthermal enhancements of pions generated by the decay before and after the freeze-out present only in the crossover region of the chiral symmetry transition. The strongest nonthermal enhancement is located in the vicinity of the endpoint of the first-order transition.

# Thermal and Nonthermal Pion Enhancements with Chiral Symmetry Restoration

## Key claim

The thermal and nonthermal enhancements of pions from sigma decay occur only in the crossover region of the chiral symmetry transition, not in the first-order transition region; the strongest nonthermal enhancement is located in the vicinity of the endpoint of the first-order transition line, but contrary to earlier expectations the mean momentum of nonthermal pions is approximately constant at 0.26 GeV rather than being very low.

## Method

The authors calculate the sigma decay rate for the process σ → 2π to lowest order in a 1/N_c expansion within the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ, using the Hartree approximation for quarks and RPA approximation for mesons. They then apply scaling hydrodynamics with a μ-dependent expansion exponent to describe the space-time evolution of the hadronic system, defining freeze-out via the condition that the scattering time exceeds the expansion time scale. The thermal and nonthermal pion numbers are obtained by integrating the decay rate over time before and after freeze-out, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point P in the chiral limit is located at T_P = 79 MeV, μ_P = 280 MeV in the T-μ plane.
- The endpoint E of the first-order transition line (with explicit chiral symmetry breaking) is at T_E = 23 MeV, μ_E = 325 MeV.
- The vacuum sigma decay rate is approximately 0.45/fm, rising to a maximum of approximately 0.5/fm at T ≈ 0.17 GeV at zero baryon density.
- The σππ coupling strength in vacuum is about 2 GeV.
- For μ = 0.2 GeV, 48% of sigmas decay before freeze-out and 52% after, giving thermal enhancement of 32% and nonthermal enhancement of 30% relative to direct pions.
- The maximum total pion enhancement is 63%, agreeing with the value 2/3 estimated by simple statistics.
- The mean momentum of nonthermal pions is approximately constant at 0.26 GeV across the chemical potential range studied.
- The nonthermal pions are centralized in a narrow momentum region around p ≈ 0.26 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the idea proposed by Tricritical Point studies (1998-1999) that sigma decay near the critical endpoint could produce a nonthermal excess of low-momentum pions, by providing a systematic calculation in the NJL model across the full T-μ plane without assuming freeze-out occurs at the chiral transition. The authors build on earlier calculations of the sigma decay rate at zero density by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and on the NJL model framework for mesonic properties. The results enable experimental searches for the critical endpoint in relativistic heavy ion collisions by identifying the chemical potential region (μ ~ 0.25 GeV) where nonthermal enhancements are strongest, and provide a baseline prediction that the nonthermal pion peak should appear at p ≈ 0.26 GeV rather than at very low momentum as previously expected.
