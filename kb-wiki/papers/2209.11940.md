# Higher-Order Cumulants and Correlation Functions of Proton Multiplicity Distributions in $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 3 GeV Au+Au Collisions at the RHIC STAR Experiment

> We report a measurement of cumulants and correlation functions of event-by-event proton multiplicity distributions from fixed-target Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 3 GeV measured by the STAR experiment. Protons are identified within the rapidity ($y$) and transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) region $-0.9 < y<0$ and $0.4 < p_{\rm T} <2.0 $ GeV/$c$ in the center-of-mass frame. A systematic analysis of the proton cumulants and correlation functions up to sixth-order as well as the corresponding ratios as a function of the collision centrality, $p_{\rm T}$, and $y$ are presented. The effect of pileup and initial volume fluctuations on these observables and the respective corrections are discussed in detail. The results are compared to calculations from the hadronic transport UrQMD model as well as a hydrodynamic model. In the most central 5\% collisions, the value of proton cumulant ratio $C_4/C_2$ is negative, drastically different from the values observed in Au+Au collisions at higher energies. Compared to model calculations including Lattice QCD, a hadronic transport model, and a hydrodynamic model, the strong suppression in the ratio of $C_4/C_2$ at 3 GeV Au+Au collisions indicates an energy regime dominated by hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reports that in the most central 5% of Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV, the proton cumulant ratio C4/C2 is negative, which is drastically different from values observed at higher collision energies. This strong suppression indicates an energy regime dominated by hadronic interactions, though the authors note that pileup corrections and volume fluctuation effects are substantial and must be carefully accounted for in the interpretation.

## Method

The STAR experiment used a fixed-target configuration with a gold target and 3.85 GeV per nucleon gold beam to produce Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV. Protons were identified within rapidity -0.9 < y < 0 and transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) for dE/dx measurements and the Time-of-Flight detector (TOF) for mass-squared identification. Cumulants and correlation functions up to sixth order were calculated from event-by-event proton multiplicity distributions, with corrections applied for detector efficiency, pileup events (double collisions), centrality bin width, and initial volume fluctuations. Results were compared to the hadronic transport UrQMD model and a hydrodynamic model.

## Key numbers

- Total of 1.4 × 10^8 Au+Au events at √sNN = 3 GeV were analyzed
- Protons identified in kinematic acceptance: -0.9 < y < 0 and 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c
- Pileup fraction: 0.46% for minimum bias events, 2.10% for 0-5% central collisions
- Average number of participants ⟨Npart⟩ = 326 (systematic uncertainty 11) for 0-5% centrality
- Reference multiplicity threshold for 0-5% centrality: Nch ≥ 48 charged particles (excluding protons) within pseudo-rapidity -2 < η < 0
- Proton purity required to be higher than 95% at all rapidities and momenta
- TOF matching efficiency introduced 60% matching efficiency for proton candidates with p > 2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program to the lowest energy (√sNN = 3 GeV) of the STAR fixed-target program, following earlier BES-I measurements at √sNN = 7.7-200 GeV that showed a non-monotonic behavior of C4/C2 with 3.1σ significance (Adam 2020, Abdallah 2021). The results are compared to UrQMD (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999) and hydrodynamic model calculations, and the negative C4/C2 value contrasts with Lattice QCD predictions at lower baryochemical potential that yield positive C4/C2. The HADES Collaboration previously reported C4/C2 consistent with unity at √sNN = 2.4 GeV (HADES Collaboration), and this work provides higher-statistics measurements that enable quantitative interpretation of the energy dependence of proton fluctuations in the hadron-dominated regime.
