# Photoinduced Two-Nucleon Knockout: A Kinematical Study in Nuclear Matter

> The contributions of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations, meson exchange current (MEC) corrections and the influence of Delta isobar excitations (isobaric currents, IC) on photon induced two-nucleon knockout reactions are investigated. The nuclear structure functions are evaluated for the system of infinite nuclear matter. This allows a systematic investigation of the relative importance of the various contributions (MEC, IC and correlations) in different kinematical regions. Nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations derived from a realistic NN interaction by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation yield significant changes in the structure functions W_L and W_T of the (e,e'pn) and (e,e'pp) reactions. This is true in particular for the so-called 'super parallel' kinematics at momentum transfers which can be measured e.g. at MAMI in Mainz.

## Key claim

Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations, derived from a realistic NN interaction via the Bethe-Goldstone equation, produce significant changes in the longitudinal and transverse structure functions of (e,e'pn) and (e,e'pp) reactions, particularly in "super parallel" kinematics at momentum transfers accessible at MAMI in Mainz; however, the relative importance of correlations, meson exchange currents (MEC), and isobar currents (IC) depends sensitively on the kinematical setup, and the study is performed in infinite nuclear matter rather than for a specific finite nucleus.

## Method

The authors evaluated the nuclear matrix elements for photon absorption by a correlated pair of nucleons, MEC, and IC in a consistent scheme for infinite nuclear matter, using plane-wave states for the nucleons and a mean-field approximation for the final-state interaction of outgoing nucleons. The NN correlations were calculated from the Bonn A potential via the Bethe-Goldstone equation, with single-particle energies parametrized by an effective mass approximation (m* = 623 MeV, U = -86.8 MeV). The MEC operators for π and ρ exchange were derived from the continuity equation, while the IC operators were constructed explicitly including a Δ propagator with an energy-dependent decay width.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}
- Effective mass: m* = 623 MeV; single-particle potential: U = -86.8 MeV
- π-NN coupling constant: f_{πNN} = 1.005; f_{πNΔ} = 2 f_{πNN}; f_{γNΔ} = 0.12
- Cutoff mass for π-nucleon form factor: Λ_π = 1.3 GeV
- Photon energy ω = 215 MeV used for super parallel kinematics studies, with final kinetic energies of the two nucleons either asymmetric (T_{p,1}=156 MeV, T_{p,2}=33 MeV) or more symmetric (T_{p,1}=116 MeV, T_{p,2}=73 MeV)
- Structure functions for (e,e'pn) reactions are roughly 10 times larger than those for (e,e'pp) reactions; correlation contributions alone are enhanced by roughly a factor of 7 comparing (e,e'pn) with (e,e'pp)
- At ω = 300 MeV with symmetric kinematics (T_p = T_n = 100 MeV, θ_p' = θ_n' = 30°), the single-particle current (correlations) is almost negligible compared to the dominating MEC contribution
- The static Δ propagator overestimates the IC contribution by roughly a factor of three compared to the exact result; the propagator suggested by Ryckebusch (1997) overestimates by roughly a factor of two

## Context

This work extends studies of two-nucleon knockout reactions by treating correlations, MEC, and IC in a consistent framework using the Bonn A potential, going beyond earlier approaches that assumed local, state-independent correlation functions. It builds on the formalism of Giusti and Pacati (1991) and Ryckebusch et al. (1995) for the (e,e'2N) cross section, and on the treatment of isobar currents by Wilhelm et al. (1997). The nuclear matter approach enables systematic identification of kinematical regions where correlations dominate, which can guide experimental setups at facilities like MAMI; the authors note that future work should apply this scheme to finite nuclei and include direct two-nucleon rescattering after photon absorption.
