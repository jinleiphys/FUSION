# Derivation of second-order relativistic hydrodynamics for reactive multi-component systems

> We derive the second-order hydrodynamic equation for reactive multi-component systems from the relativistic Boltzmann equation. In the reactive system, particles can change their species under the restriction of the imposed conservation laws during the collision process. Our derivation is based on the renormalization group (RG) method, in which the Boltzmann equation is solved in an organized perturbation method as faithfully as possible and possible secular terms are resummed away by a suitable setting of the initial value of the distribution function. The microscopic formulae of the relaxation times and the lengths are explicitly given as well as those of the transport coefficients for the reactive multi-component system. The resultant hydrodynamic equation with these formulae has nice properties that it satisfies the positivity of the entropy production rate and the Onsager's reciprocal theorem, which ensure the validity of our derivation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equation for reactive multi-component systems from the Boltzmann equation using the renormalization group (RG) method, claiming that the resultant equation satisfies the positivity of the entropy production rate and Onsager's reciprocal theorem. The abstract glosses over the condition that the derivation is restricted to binary (2-to-2) collisions and that the particle numbers of each component need not be conserved individually, only the imposed conservation laws (energy-momentum and charges) are respected.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a multi-component system with N species and M conserved currents, considering only binary collisions. They apply the renormalization group (RG) method, solving the Boltzmann equation perturbatively with respect to a small parameter epsilon representing the Knudsen number (spatial inhomogeneity). The distribution function is expanded around the local equilibrium state, and secular terms are resummed by imposing the RG condition, yielding a second-order hydrodynamic equation. The linearized collision operator is decomposed into zero modes (collision invariants) and excited modes (doublet modes and higher), and the projection operators P0, P1, Q0, Q1 are used to separate slow and fast dynamics.

## Key numbers

* The linearized collision operator L has M+4 eigenvectors belonging to the zero eigenvalue, corresponding to the four energy-momentum components (p^mu) and M conserved charges (q^A).
* The metric matrix of the P0-space is defined as eta^{alpha beta} = <varphi^alpha, varphi^beta>, where the inner product is <psi, chi> = sum_k int dp_k (p_k . u) f^{eq}_{k,p_k} bar{f}^{eq}_{k,p_k} psi_{k,p_k} chi_{k,p_k}.
* The first-order solution includes terms proportional to (tau - tau_0) P0 F^{(0)} and (e^{L(tau - tau_0)} - 1) L^{-1} Q0 F^{(0)}.
* The initial value Psi for the first-order equation is expressed in terms of L^{-1} acting on the microscopic representations of bulk viscosity (hat{Pi}), charge diffusion (hat{J}_A^mu), and stress tensor (hat{pi}^{munu}), with denominators such as <hat{Pi}, L^{-1} hat{Pi}> and <hat{pi}^{rhosigma}, L^{-1} hat{pi}_{rhosigma}>.

## Context

This work extends the RG-based derivation of second-order hydrodynamics from single-component systems (Tsumura et al., 2012-2015) to reactive multi-component systems, which is necessary for realistic descriptions of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) composed of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons. It builds on earlier attempts by Prakash et al. (1993) for relativistic gas mixtures and by Monnai and Hirano (2010), who used a fourteen-moment method with positivity and Onsager conditions. The present derivation provides explicit microscopic formulas for relaxation times and transport coefficients, and the resulting equation is claimed to be causal and stable, enabling more accurate modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
