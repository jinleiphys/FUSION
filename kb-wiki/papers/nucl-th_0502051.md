# Inhomogeneous freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions

> Relative hadron abundances from high-energy heavy-ion collisions reveal substantial inhomogeneities of temperature and baryon-chemical potential within the decoupling volume. The freeze-out volume is not perfectly "stirred", i.e. the concentrations of pions, kaons, (anti-) nucleons etc are inhomogeneous. Such inhomogeneities in the late stages of the hydrodynamic expansion might be traces of a first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that event-averaged hadron abundances from heavy-ion collisions can be explained by an inhomogeneous freeze-out surface where temperature and baryon-chemical potential follow Gaussian distributions, rather than by a single homogeneous freeze-out condition. The authors explicitly hedge that such inhomogeneities could be traces of a first-order phase transition, but do not claim to have proven that connection.

## Method

The authors model the freeze-out surface by assuming that temperature \(T\) and baryon-chemical potential \(\mu_B\) are distributed according to independent Gaussian distributions with mean values \(\overline{T}\), \(\overline{\mu}_B\) and widths \(\delta T\), \(\delta\mu_B\). They compute average hadron densities by integrating the ideal-gas densities \(\rho_i(T,\mu_B)\) over these distributions, including feeding from strong and weak decays via branching ratios. A \(\chi^2\) fit is performed to midrapidity and \(4\pi\) data from NA49 for central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, with confidence intervals obtained from the \(\chi^2 \le \chi^2_{min} + 1\) criterion.

## Key numbers

- For midrapidity data: homogeneous fit gives \(\overline{T} = 155 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\overline{\mu}_B = 200 \pm 10\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 40.4/8\); inhomogeneous fit gives \(\overline{T} = 105 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\overline{\mu}_B = 230 \pm 15\) MeV, \(\delta T = 35 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\delta\mu_B = 80 \pm 40\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 11.2/6\).
- For \(4\pi\) data: homogeneous fit gives \(\overline{T} = 145 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\overline{\mu}_B = 210 \pm 15\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 40.0/11\); inhomogeneous fit gives \(\overline{T} = 100 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\overline{\mu}_B = 260 \pm 15\) MeV, \(\delta T = 30 \pm 5\) MeV, \(\delta\mu_B = 190 \pm 35\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 5.7/9\).
- Mean temperature for protons in midrapidity: \(\langle T \rangle = 157\) MeV; for antiprotons: \(\langle T \rangle = 170\) MeV.
- Mean baryon-chemical potential for protons in midrapidity: \(\langle \mu_B \rangle = 268\) MeV; for antiprotons: \(\langle \mu_B \rangle = 191\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard statistical model approach to hadron production (e.g., Becattini (2003)) by allowing for Gaussian fluctuations of temperature and chemical potential on the freeze-out surface, motivated by the possibility of a first-order phase transition line ending at a critical point as predicted by lattice QCD (Fodor (2001)). The results suggest that the decoupling surface is not well stirred, with different particle species originating from different temperature and density regions, which could enable future studies of event-by-event fluctuations and searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions at varying beam energies.
