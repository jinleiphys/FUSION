# Spin alignment of vector mesons by second-order hydrodynamic gradients

> Starting with the polarization dependent Wigner function of vector mesons, we derive an expression for the 00-component ($ρ_{00}$) of spin density matrix in terms of the second order gradients of the vector meson distribution functions. We further apply a thermal model to analyze the transverse momentum and the azimuthal angle dependence of $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$ mesons resulting from distribution gradients in Au-Au collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV at mid-rapidity. Our results for the transverse momentum dependence indicate that the deviations of $ρ_{00}$ from $1/3$ as the signal for spin alignment are greatly enhanced at large transverse momenta and have a strong centrality dependence while analysis of the azimuthal angle ($ϕ_q$) dependence suggest that such deviations have a $\cos(2ϕ_q)$ structure with opposite sign for $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$. Our finding may be considered as a baseline for probing spin-alignment mechanisms beyond hydrodynamic gradients.

## Key claim

The authors derive an expression for the spin density matrix component ρ₀₀ of vector mesons in terms of second-order spacetime gradients of the distribution functions, and estimate that deviations of ρ₀₀ from 1/3 are greatly enhanced at large transverse momenta and have a strong centrality dependence, but caution that their results should be considered as a baseline for probing spin-alignment mechanisms beyond hydrodynamic gradients rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

Starting from the polarization-dependent Wigner function of vector mesons expanded up to second order in ℏ (equivalent to second-order spacetime gradients), the authors derived an expression for ρ₀₀ in terms of the distribution functions of vector mesons. They then applied a thermal model with single-freeze-out, using the Jüttner distribution f_V(q,X) = exp[-q^μβ_μ(x) - ξ(x)], to numerically evaluate ρ₀₀ for φ and K*⁰ mesons in Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 130 GeV at mid-rapidity, analyzing both transverse momentum and azimuthal angle dependence.

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts to understand spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions, which have shown discrepancies between experimental measurements and predictions based on thermal equilibrium (Becattini, 2007) and the spin coalescence model (Liang, 2004; Yang, 2017). The STAR Collaboration (2022) found ρ₀₀ > 1/3 for φ mesons and ρ₀₀ ≈ 1/3 for K*⁰ mesons at RHIC energies, while the ALICE Collaboration (2019) observed ρ₀₀ < 1/3 for both mesons at LHC energies with small transverse momenta. The authors note that previous treatments of hydrodynamic gradients in this context were incomplete, with only negligible contributions estimated from vorticity-induced spin polarization of strange quarks in the coalescence model (Xia, 2020). This work provides a more rigorous estimation of hydrodynamic gradient effects, establishing a baseline for more exotic mechanisms such as fluctuating background fields from strong interactions (Sheng, 2019; Sheng, 2020; Xia, 2020; Muller, 2021; Yang, 2021; Kumar, 2022; Kumar, 2023).
