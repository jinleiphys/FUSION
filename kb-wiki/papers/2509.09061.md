# Study on the equation-of-state with light clusters and hypernuclei

> Heavy-ion collision experiments offer a unique opportunity to explore the early stages of the Universe by creating matter under extreme conditions of high temperature and baryon density. The properties of such matter are governed by the equation-of-state (EoS), which remains a central focus of investigation from both experimental and theoretical perspectives. Flow harmonics are among the most sensitive observables for probing the EoS, as they strongly reflect the underlying interactions and degrees of freedom of the system. In this article, we review the current status of our understanding of the EoS based on microscopic transport models, emphasizing comparisons with experimental data in the few GeV energy range.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current status of understanding the equation-of-state (EoS) of strongly interacting matter based on microscopic transport models, emphasizing that flow harmonics of light clusters and hypernuclei provide sensitive probes, but the conclusions depend on the specific cluster production mechanism and momentum-dependent potential parametrization used in the models.

## Method

The authors employ the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) transport approach, which combines baryon propagation from Quantum Molecular Dynamics with hadronic and partonic degrees-of-freedom from the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics approach. They compare calculations using three different equations-of-state (soft static, hard static, and soft momentum dependent) against experimental data from HADES, FOPI, and STAR collaborations for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 1.2 AGeV to sqrt(s_NN)=3 GeV, analyzing directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2 of protons, deuterons, tritons, and helium-3.

## Key numbers

* Compressibility modulus K = 200 MeV for the soft EoS and K = 380 MeV for the hard EoS, characterizing the curvature of the EoS at normal nuclear density rho_0
* The EoS can be probed at densities up to approximately 3 rho_0 and temperatures up to T = 120 MeV in the few-GeV energy range
* For the soft momentum dependent EoS, the PHQMD calculations agree with HADES data for v2 of protons, deuterons, and tritons within 10% at E_kin = 1.23 A GeV for 1.0 < p_T < 1.5 GeV/c
* The Gaussian width in PHQMD is taken as L = 4.33 fm^2 for the single-particle Wigner density
* The coalescence condition for deuteron formation uses thresholds of |r_1 - r_2| <= 3.575 fm and |p_1 - p_2| <= 285 MeV/c

## Context

This review extends earlier transport studies using QMD models by Aichelin (1987) and BUU-type models by Gale (1987) and Welke (1988), which established that nucleon flow depends strongly on momentum dependence of the potential. The paper builds on recent experimental data from HADES (2020, 2022), FOPI (2011), and STAR (2021-2024) collaborations, and relates PHQMD observations to results from UrQMD by Hillmann (2019) and Steinheimer (2024), as well as SMASH by Tarasovicova (2024) and Mohs (2024). The work enables systematic comparison of different cluster production mechanisms (potential, kinetic, and coalescence) and their influence on flow observables, which is necessary to distinguish between models using future experimental data.
