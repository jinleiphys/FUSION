# Universal behaviour, transients and attractors in supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

> Numerical simulations of expanding plasma based on the AdS/CFT correspondence as well as kinetic theory and hydrodynamic models strongly suggest that some observables exhibit universal behaviour even when the system is not close to local equilibrium. This leading behaviour is expected to be corrected by transient, exponentially decaying contributions which carry information about the initial state. Focusing on late times, when the system is already in the hydrodynamic regime, we analyse numerical solutions describing expanding plasma of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory and identify these transient effects, matching them in a quantitative way to leading trans-series corrections corresponding to least-damped quasinormal modes of AdS black branes. In the process we offer additional evidence supporting the recent identification of the Borel sum of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion with the far-from-equilibrium attractor in this system.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the late-time behavior of the pressure anisotropy in expanding strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma exhibits damped oscillations around the hydrodynamic attractor, and that these transient effects can be quantitatively matched to the leading trans-series corrections corresponding to the least-damped quasinormal modes of AdS black branes, with the caveat that this matching only works reliably for dimensionless proper time \(w > 3/2\).

## Method

The author analyzes numerical solutions of Bjorken flow in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma obtained via the AdS/CFT correspondence, evolving the asymptotically-AdS geometry to late times where only the least-damped pair of quasinormal modes is relevant. The pressure anisotropy \(\mathcal{A} = (\mathcal{P}_T - \mathcal{P}_L)/\mathcal{P}_{eq}\) is expressed as a function of the dimensionless variable \(w = \tau T(\tau)\), and the difference between pairs of numerical solutions is fitted to the known functional form of the leading trans-series correction, which involves a complex-conjugate pair of quasinormal mode frequencies. The Borel sum of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion is also compared to numerical solutions and to the attractor of conformal BRSSS hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The least-damped AdS black brane quasinormal mode frequencies (for an operator of conformal weight \(\Delta=4\)) are \(\Omega_R \approx 9.800\) and \(\Omega_I \approx 8.629\).
- The exponents associated with the leading trans-series correction are \(\beta_R \approx 0.6866\) and \(\beta_I \approx 0.7798\).
- The dimensionless second-order transport coefficients for N=4 SYM used in the BRSSS comparison are \(C_{\tau \Pi} = (2-\log 2)/(2\pi)\), \(C_{\lambda_1} = 1/(2\pi)\), and \(C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)\).
- The leading asymptotic behavior of the pressure anisotropy in rescaled variables is \(\mathcal{A}(\tilde{w}) = 2/(\pi\tilde{w}) + \dots\), where \(\tilde{w} = w/(4\pi C_\eta)\).

## Context

This work extends the program of understanding hydrodynamic attractors and transseries in relativistic plasma, building on the identification of the Borel sum of the gradient expansion as a far-from-equilibrium attractor by Spalinski (2017) and the transseries framework developed by Heller and Spalinski (2015) and Aniceto and Spalinski (2015). The numerical solutions analyzed were generated using methods from Jankowski et al. (2014) and Heller et al. (2011). The paper enables quantitative verification that the late-time approach to the attractor is governed by the least-damped quasinormal modes of AdS black branes, and shows that the attractor of conformal BRSSS hydrodynamics (Baier et al., 2007) with transport coefficients matched to N=4 SYM is essentially identical to the Borel-summed gradient expansion, in contrast to the attractor of kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation.
