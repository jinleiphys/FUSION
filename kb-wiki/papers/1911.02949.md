# Study of Some Aspects of Astronuclear System

> In this work we have studied the bulk properties of compact stellar object which is largely motivated by the recent work of Potekhin group who studied the structure and evolution of compact star on the basis of unified EoS of Brussels-Montreal group. Here we solved ToV equation of state numerically using three sets of parameter models to compute pressure, baryon density and bulk modulus etc over a wide range of matter density. The computed values of the observables like the stellar interior pressure, bulk modulus and baryon density have been presented as a function of mass density in the tabular form. Graphical analysis of computed observables and their comparison with reference data wherever available have been presented as representative cases.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their computed bulk modulus (B) as a function of mass density shows excellent agreement with reference data from Baym et al. (1971) and Pandharipande et al. (1998), but this agreement is presented only graphically and the paper provides no quantitative measure of the deviation between their results and the reference data.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations numerically using three sets of parameter models (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) based on the unified equations of state of the Brussels-Montreal group as presented by Potekhin et al. (2013). They computed pressure (P), baryon density (n_b), bulk modulus (B), and sound velocity (v_s) as functions of mass density (ρ) over a range from 10^6 g cm^{-3} to 10^16 g cm^{-3} for cold catalyzed nuclear matter at T=0 K.

- Pressure at ρ = 1.00E+06 g cm^{-3}: calculated 2.17E-15 MN fm^{-2}, reference 3.63E-15 MN fm^{-2}
- Bulk modulus at ρ = 1.00E+06 g cm^{-3}: calculated 3.44E-15 MN fm^{-2}
- Sound velocity at ρ = 1.00E+06 g cm^{-3}: calculated 0.0057c, reference 0.0063c
- Baryon density at ρ = 1.00E+15 g cm^{-3}: calculated 5.32E-01 fm^{-3}, reference 5.50E-01 fm^{-3}
- Pressure at ρ = 1.00E+15 g cm^{-3}: calculated 1.96E-02 MN fm^{-2}, reference 1.52E-02 MN fm^{-2}
- Bulk modulus at ρ = 1.00E+15 g cm^{-3}: calculated 5.63E-02 MN fm^{-2}
- Sound velocity at ρ = 1.00E+15 g cm^{-3}: calculated 0.5367c, reference 0.7667c
- A kink in the bulk modulus graph is observed around ρ ≃ 5×10^12 g cm^{-3}, interpreted as a possible phase change
- The standard neutron drip density from BPS EoS is ρ_{n-drip} ∼ 4×10^11 g cm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the unified EoS approach of Potekhin et al. (2013), who derived EoS tables from the Brussels-Montreal energy-density functional models BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21. The authors compute additional bulk properties (bulk modulus and sound velocity) not emphasized in the original Potekhin work, and compare their results graphically against the earlier BPS EoS of Baym et al. (1971) and the work of Pandharipande et al. (1998). The paper identifies a kink near 5×10^12 g cm^{-3} that may indicate a phase transition related to strange quark production, which the authors connect to recent work by Baym, but this interpretation remains speculative and requires further investigation.
