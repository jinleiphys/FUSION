# Bulk Nuclear Properties from Reactions

> Extraction of bulk nuclear properties by comparing reaction observables to results from semiclassical transport-model simulations is discussed. Specific properties include the nuclear viscosity, incompressibility and constraints on the nuclear pressure at supranormal densities.

# Bulk Nuclear Properties from Reactions

## Key claim
Extraction of bulk nuclear properties from heavy-ion reactions is possible by comparing reaction observables to semiclassical transport-model simulations, but the inferred properties (viscosity, incompressibility, and pressure at supranormal densities) carry significant uncertainties because the underlying Boltzmann equation has unknown inputs such as the density and momentum dependence of single-particle energies and the validity of free-space cross sections in dense matter.

## Method
Central collisions of heavy nuclei (Ar + Ag, Au + Au) were simulated using the Boltzmann equation with various parametrizations of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and nuclear equation-of-state (EOS) forms. Stopping observables (linear momentum transfer and ERAT) were compared to simulations to constrain viscosity; giant monopole resonance energies from alpha scattering were compared to Vlasov-equation calculations to extract incompressibility; and sideward and elliptic flow anisotropies in Au + Au collisions were compared to transport simulations to place constraints on pressure at supranormal densities.

## Key numbers
- In-medium cross sections in lower-energy reactions appear reduced by approximately 30% compared to free-space values, and nuclear viscosity appears increased by approximately 50% compared to calculations with free cross sections.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is extracted as K = 225 ± 15 MeV from giant monopole resonance data in spherical nuclei.
- Flow data constrain the nuclear pressure at T=0 for densities in the range 2 ≲ ρ/ρ₀ ≲ 5, eliminating extreme EOS models such as the relativistic NL3 model and models assuming a phase transition at relatively low densities.
- For the Ar + Ag system at 90 MeV/nucleon, the number of collisions for the Rostock in-medium cross sections is reduced by about 25% compared to free cross sections, while the number for cross sections screened by interparticle distance is reduced by a factor of 4; however, both yield similar weighted collision numbers when collisions are weighted with q⁴ sin²θ.
- In central Au + Au collisions at 250 MeV/nucleon, the ERAT observable is consistent with isotropy (ERAT = 2) and requires the screening parameter y ∼ 1 in the geometric cross-section reduction formula.

## Context
This work extends the program of extracting nuclear matter properties from heavy-ion reactions by using semiclassical transport theory, building on earlier studies of stopping by the Stony Brook group (1998) and the FOPI Collaboration (1997). The in-medium cross-section reduction is compared to calculations by the Rostock group (1998). The giant monopole resonance analysis uses precise measurements by Youngblood et al. (2001) and extends earlier time-dependent Hartree-Fock and random-phase-approximation approaches. The flow constraints on pressure at supranormal densities enable elimination of extreme EOS models and provide a benchmark for future microscopic calculations of dense nuclear matter.
