# Measurement of the isospin-filtering dd -> 4He K+ K- reaction at Q=39 MeV

> The total cross section for the dd -> 4He K+ K- reaction has been measured at a beam momentum of 3.7 GeV/c, corresponding to an excess energy of 39 MeV, which is the maximum possible at the Cooler Synchrotron COSY-Jülich. A deuterium cluster-jet target and the ANKE forward magnetic spectrometer, placed inside the storage ring, have been employed in this investigation. We find a total cross section of sigma(tot) < 14 pb, which brings into question the viability of investigating the dd -> 4He a0(980) reaction as a means of studying isospin violation.

## Key claim

The total cross section for the dd -> 4He K+ K- reaction at an excess energy of 39 MeV is surprisingly low (sigma_tot < 14 pb at 92% CL, or 5 +/- 2 pb from a signal of about five events after background subtraction), which brings into question whether the dd -> 4He a0(980) reaction can be a viable way to study isospin violation through a0/f0 mixing.

## Method

A deuterium cluster-jet target and the ANKE forward magnetic spectrometer at the COSY synchrotron were used to measure the dd -> 4He K+ K- reaction at a beam momentum of 3.7 GeV/c. Positively charged kaons were identified in the positive side detection system via time-of-flight and delayed-veto signals from K+ decay, while 4He nuclei were identified in the forward detector using energy-loss versus rigidity cuts. Two analysis approaches were used: a "background-free" method combining strict K+ and 4He selection criteria (including the delayed-veto criterion) that yielded zero events, and a relaxed-cut method that found approximately five signal events after background subtraction.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section upper limit: sigma_tot < 14 pb at 92% confidence level for dd -> 4He K+ K- at Q = 39 MeV
* Total cross section from signal extraction: sigma_tot = (5 +/- 2_stat +/- 1_syst) pb, with an additional overall uncertainty of about 50% from the luminosity determination
* Integrated luminosity: L_int = 35 pb^-1 over the course of the experiment
* Average luminosity: L = [2.6 +/- 0.1(stat) +/- 0.8(syst) +/- 0.3(syst)] x 10^31 s^-1 cm^-2
* Total acceptance for 4He K+ detection: A = 8.5%
* K+ detection efficiency using delayed-veto criterion: approximately 15%
* Efficiency of the stronger TOF cut on K+ mesons: epsilon_TOF(K+) = 90%
* Data acquisition system efficiency: epsilon_DAQ = 70%
* Side detector efficiency: epsilon_SD = 95%
* Forward detector efficiency: epsilon_FD = 70%

## Context

This measurement extends the program of isospin-filtering studies using the dd -> 4He X reaction, which previously provided evidence for charge symmetry breaking through the dd -> 4He pi0 measurement by Stephenson and collaborators. The work was motivated by theoretical proposals from Grishina, Hanhart (2003), and Achasov (1979) that a0/f0 mixing in the final state could enhance isospin violation, but the measured cross section is almost two orders of magnitude lower than a crude factorisation estimate based on pd -> 3He K+ K- data from the MOMO collaboration and dd -> 4He eta data from Budzanowski (2008). This result suggests that the dd -> 4He eta pi0 reaction may not be a useful channel for investigating isospin violation, and calls for theoretical work to understand why the dynamics of K+ K- pair production differ so dramatically from single meson production in these reactions.
