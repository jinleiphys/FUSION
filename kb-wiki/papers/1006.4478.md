# Knudsen number, ideal hydrodynamic limit for elliptic flow and QGP viscosity in $\sqrt{s}$=62 and 200 GeV Cu+Cu/Au+Au collisions

> Taking into account of entropy generation during evolution of a viscous fluid, we have estimated inverse Knudsen number, ideal hydrodynamic limit for elliptic flow and QGP viscosity to entropy ratio in $\sqrt{s}$=62 and 200 GeV Cu+Cu/Au+Au collisions. Viscosity to entropy ratio is estimated as $η/s=0.17\pm 0.10\pm 0.20$, the first error is statistical, the second one is systematic. In a central Au+Au collision, inverse Knudsen number is $\approx 2.80\pm 1.63$, which presumably small for complete equilibration. In peripheral collisions it is even less. Ideal hydrodynamic limit for elliptic flow is $\sim$40% more than the experimental flow in a central collision.

# Knudsen number, ideal hydrodynamic limit for elliptic flow and QGP viscosity

## Key claim
The inverse Knudsen number in central Au+Au collisions is approximately 2.80, which is too small for complete thermal equilibration, contrary to the ideal fluid approximation which would give a value near 8. The ideal hydrodynamic limit for elliptic flow is about 40% larger than the experimental flow in central collisions, but this estimate carries large uncertainties ($\pm$0.12 on a central value of 0.33).

## Method
The author generalizes the Knudsen number ansatz of Bhalerao (2005) by accounting for entropy generation during viscous fluid evolution, using an analytic integration of the Bjorken flow energy-momentum conservation equation with shear viscosity proportional to $T^3$. PHOBOS data on centrality-dependent eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=62 and 200 GeV are fitted with the modified relation to extract the ideal hydrodynamic limit and the viscosity parameter $\etas = (1/\tau_i T_i)(\eta/s)$.

## Key numbers
- Viscosity to entropy ratio: $\eta/s = 0.17 \pm 0.10$ (statistical) $\pm 0.20$ (systematic), corresponding to $4\pi\eta/s = 2.14 \pm 1.26 \pm 2.56$
- Inverse Knudsen number in 0-3% central Au+Au collisions: $K^{-1} = 2.80 \pm 1.63$ (viscous fluid) versus 7.91 (ideal fluid approximation)
- Ideal hydrodynamic limit for eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow: $\vih = 0.33 \pm 0.12$
- In the ideal fluid approximation ($\etas=0$): $\vih = 0.19 \pm 0.04$, with $\chi^2/N \approx 1.3$ (versus 0.1 for the viscous fit)
- Inverse Knudsen number decreases by a factor of 2-7 when entropy generation is accounted for, depending on centrality
- Parameter $\etas = 0.83 \pm 0.51$, with conversion $\eta/s = 0.2 \times \etas$

## Context
This work extends the Knudsen number approach of Bhalerao (2005) by correcting for the assumption of conserved particle number, which is violated in viscous evolution where entropy generation can reach 20-50% as shown in Chaudhuri (2009). The analysis uses PHOBOS data (Back 2004, Alver 2006, Alver 2008) and obtains a viscosity estimate consistent with the ADS/CFT lower bound of Policastro (2001) and the phenomenological upper bound of Luzum (2008) and Song (2008). The result enables more reliable extraction of QGP viscosity from elliptic flow data by properly accounting for entropy production during the early stages of the collision.
