# Thermal properties of a rotating nucleus in a fluctuating mean field approach

> The static path approximation to the path integral representation of partition function provides a natural microscopic basis to deal with thermal fluctuations around mean field configurations. Using this approach for one-dimensional cranking Hamiltonian with quadrupole- quadrupole interaction term we have studied a few properties like energy, level density, level density parameter($a$) and moment of inertia as a function of temperature and spin for $^{64}Zn$ taking it as an illustrative example. We have also investigated the effects of variation in interaction strength on the level density and the parameter $a$ as a function of temperature. The moment of inertia, $\cal I$ versus rotational frequency, $ω$ plot shows a sudden rise in the value of $\cal I$ due to rotation alignment of $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals at $ω\approx 1.0$ MeV for a small temperature T $\sim 0.5$ MeV. At high T $\sim$ 2.0 MeV about 40-45$\%$ of each angular momentum is generated by alignment of $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals with an interesting result that at $ω\sim 1.0$ MeV and spin J $\sim$ 16 the moment of inertia has almost a constant, temperature independent value.

## Key claim

The static path approximation (SPA) to the path integral representation of the partition function, applied to a one-dimensional cranking Hamiltonian with quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, can describe thermal fluctuations around mean-field configurations in a rotating nucleus, but the angular momentum is conserved only on average via a constraint, and the approach is not applicable at T=0.

## Method

The authors used the static path approximation (SPA) to evaluate the grand canonical partition function for a rotating nucleus described by a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian, with a cranking term for rotation about the intrinsic x-axis. The partition function was computed by numerical integration over deformation parameters β and γ (12-point Gaussian in β with β_max=0.5, 8-point Gaussian in γ from 0 to 60°) after diagonalizing the deformed single-particle Hamiltonian in a basis space of 30 orbitals (10 protons, 14 neutrons) with a ⁴⁰Ca inert core. Chemical potentials for protons and neutrons were determined from particle number equations, and the rotational frequency ω was adjusted to satisfy an average angular momentum constraint for each desired spin J.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Zn, the spherical basis single-particle energies used were -14.4, -10.2, -8.8, -8.3, and -4.4 MeV for the orbitals 0f₇/₂, 1p₃/₂, 0f₅/₂, 1p₁/₂, and 0g₉/₂, respectively.
- At T=0.5 MeV and J=2, the occupation of 0g₉/₂ orbitals is zero; at J=16 and T=0.5 MeV, the 0g₉/₂ orbitals contribute about 40% of the total angular momentum with less than two particles in them.
- At T=2.0 MeV, about 40-45% of each angular momentum is generated by alignment of 0g₉/₂ orbitals.
- At ω ≈ 1.0 MeV and spin J ≈ 16, the moment of inertia has an almost constant, temperature-independent value.
- For the level density parameter, the value of K_S (using S=2aT) is around 8 to 10, consistent with semi-empirical values.
- At T=1.9 MeV, ln ρ is practically independent of the interaction strength χ; at T=1.1 MeV, ρ increases by about 25% when the interaction strength parameter c is increased from 65 to 75.

## Context

This work extends the static path approximation (SPA) approach of Lauritzen, Alhassid, and Bertsch (1989) and Alhassid and Bush (1991) to include cranking for rotating nuclei, building on earlier SPA calculations without spin by Agrawal and Ansari (1992). The results for ⁶⁴Zn are compared with finite-temperature mean-field calculations by Egido et al. (1989) for ¹⁶⁴Er and with the temperature-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov work of Goodman (1984). The study enables future inclusion of full angular momentum projection to go beyond the average spin constraint used here.
