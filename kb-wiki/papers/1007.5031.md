# Reaction cross-section predictions for nucleon induced reactions

> A microscopic calculation of the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering has been performed by explicitly coupling the elastic channel to all the particle-hole (p-h) excitation states in the target and to all relevant pickup channels. These p-h states may be regarded as doorway states through which the flux flows to more complicated configurations, and to long-lived compound nucleus resonances. We calculated the reaction cross sections for the nucleon induced reactions on the targets $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr and $^{144}$Sm using the QRPA description of target excitations, coupling to all inelastic open channels, and coupling to all transfer channels corresponding to the formation of a deuteron. The results of such calculations were compared to predictions of a well-established optical potential and with experimental data, reaching very good agreement. The inclusion of couplings to pickup channels were an important contribution to the absorption. For the first time, calculations of excitations account for all of the observed reaction cross-sections, at least for incident energies above 10 MeV.

## Key claim

For the first time, a microscopic calculation of reaction cross-sections that explicitly couples the elastic channel to all particle-hole excitation states and all relevant pickup (deuteron formation) channels can account for all of the observed reaction cross-sections, at least for incident energies above 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculations for nucleon-induced reactions on targets ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ¹⁴⁴Sm, starting from a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field ground state using the SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functional. Excited states of the target were described using (quasi-particle) random phase approximation (QRPA), and the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was a Gaussian form matched to the volume integral and r.m.s. radius of the M3Y interaction at 40 MeV. The calculations coupled the ground state to all inelastic open channels (all QRPA states below the scattering energy) and to all transfer channels corresponding to deuteron formation, using the Johnson-Soper adiabatic prescription for the deuteron-target potential.

## Key numbers

- For n + ⁵⁸Ni at Elab = 20 MeV, coupling to excited states above the incident energy (20 MeV) produced no additional increment to the reaction cross section.
- For n + ⁹⁰Zr at Elab = 10 MeV, couplings between excited states produced small but noticeable differences in the reaction cross section; at higher energies the curves were almost indistinguishable.
- For p + ⁹⁰Zr, calculations were performed at Elab = 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60, and 70 MeV.
- For p + ⁴⁰Ca, p + ⁴⁸Ca, and p + ⁵⁸Ni, calculations were performed at Elab = 10, 20, 30, and 40 MeV.
- At Elab = 30 MeV, results were presented for all five targets (⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹⁴⁴Sm) normalized to the geometrical area of the target (using r0 = 1.2 fm).

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach projection-operator approach (Feshbach, 1958) and the coupled reaction channels framework by using microscopic nuclear structure input from the UNEDF (Universal Nuclear Energy Density Functional) project. It builds on prior work showing that pickup channels play an important role in nucleon-nucleus scattering (Mackintosh, 2007; Keeley, 2008; Coulter, 1977). The results are compared to the well-established Koning-Delaroche optical model (Koning, 2003) and to experimental data from Menet (1971), Carlson (1975, 1994), Turner (1964), Dicello (1970), and Eliyakut-Roshko (1995). This work enables future fully-consistent ab initio developments for a range of nuclei including unstable species.
