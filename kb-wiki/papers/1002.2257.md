# Exact Analytical Solutions in Three-Body Problems and Model of Neutrino Generator

> Exact analytic solutions are obtained in three-body problem for the scattering of light particle on two fixed centers in the case when pair potentials have a separable form. Solutions show an appearance of new three-body resonance states that depend on distance between two fixed centers. In the case when two-body neutron-nucleus amplitudes have the Breit-Wigner's form and heavy nuclei fixed in nodes of crystalline lattice widths of new resonance states can be equal to zero at certain values of the distance. It gives the possibility of transitions between three-body resonance states connected with process of electron capture by proton with formation of neutron and emission of neutrino. This exoenergic process leading to the cooling of star without nuclear reactions is discussed.

## Key claim

Exact analytic solutions for three-body scattering of a light particle on two fixed centers reveal that new resonance states appear whose widths can become exactly zero at specific distances between the centers, enabling a proposed neutrino generation mechanism via electron capture by proton.

## Method

The author solves the Faddeev equations in the limit where the light-to-heavy mass ratio ζ = m/M → 0 and pair t-matrices have separable form. For the case where two-body neutron-nucleus amplitudes take the Breit-Wigner resonance form, the three-body scattering amplitude is obtained analytically, and the zeros of the D-function (determinant of I - B) are analyzed to find resonance positions and widths as functions of the distance b between the two fixed heavy centers.

## Key numbers

- For n + α,α system: first quasi-bound state at E_R,3;1 = 0.88 MeV with b_1 = 18 fm; second at E_R,3;2 = 1.37 MeV with b_2 = 31 fm
- For p + α,α system: first quasi-bound state at E_R,3;1 = 1.78 MeV with b_1 = 14 fm; second at E_R,3;2 = 2.36 MeV with b_2 = 24 fm
- Two-body n,α P-wave resonance parameters: E_R,2 ≈ 0.9 MeV, Γ_2 ≈ 0.6 MeV
- Two-body p,α P-wave resonance parameters: E_R,2 ≈ 1.9 MeV, Γ_2 ≈ 1.5 MeV
- Two-body n,¹⁶O resonance: E_R,2 = 435 keV, Γ_2 = 40 keV
- Two-body n,²⁴Mg resonance: E_R,2 = 84 keV, Γ_2 = 13 keV
- Proton resonance states are situated more than 0.8 MeV above the neutron ones on the energy scale

## Context

This work extends the author's previous exact solution method for three-body scattering with separable potentials (Takibayev, 2008) to the case where two-body amplitudes have Breit-Wigner form, and applies it to neutron-nucleus systems. The results enable a proposed model of a neutrino generator based on electron capture by proton in three-body resonance states, which could provide a mechanism for stellar cooling without nuclear reactions. The approach contrasts with the Thomas effect and Efimov effect in three-particle systems, and the author suggests experimental investigation using monocrystals under high pressure and low temperature.
