# Transport, Aharonov-Bohm, and Topological Effects in Graphene Molecular Junctions and Graphene Nanorings

> The unique ultra-relativistic, massless, nature of electron states in two-dimensional extended graphene sheets, brought about by the honeycomb lattice arrangement of carbon atoms in two-dimensions, provides ingress to explorations of fundamental physical phenomena in graphene nanostructures. Here we explore the emergence of new behavior of electrons in atomically precise segmented graphene nanoribbons (GNRs) and graphene rings with the use of tight-binding calculations, non-equilibrium Green's function transport theory, and a newly developed Dirac continuum model that absorbs the valence-to-conductance energy gaps as position-dependent masses, including topological-in-origin mass-barriers at the contacts between segments. Through transport investigations in variable-width segmented GNRs with armchair, zigzag, and mixed edge terminations we uncover development of new Fabry-Perot-like interference patterns in segmented GNRs, a crossover from the ultra-relativistic massless regime, characteristic of extended graphene systems, to a massive relativistic behavior in narrow armchair GNRs, and the emergence of nonrelativistic behavior in zigzag-terminated GNRs. Evaluation of the electronic states in a polygonal graphene nanoring under the influence of an applied magnetic field in the Aharonov-Bohm regime, and their analysis with the use of a relativistic quantum-field theoretical model, unveils development of a topological-in-origin zero-energy soliton state and charge fractionization. These results provide a unifying framework for analysis of electronic states, coherent transport phenomena, and the interpretation of forthcoming experiments in segmented graphene nanoribbons and polygonal rings.

## Key claim
The paper claims that transport in segmented graphene nanoribbons (GNRs) and graphene nanorings can be unified through a Dirac continuum model with position-dependent masses, but this unification is conditional on the specific edge termination (armchair, zigzag, or mixed) and the width of the segments, which determine whether the carriers behave as massless relativistic, massive relativistic, or nonrelativistic particles.

## Method
The authors performed tight-binding non-equilibrium Green's function (TB-NEGF) calculations for segmented GNRs with armchair, zigzag, and mixed edge terminations, and developed a one-dimensional Dirac continuum model (Dirac-Fabry-Perot theory) that incorporates valence-to-conduction energy gaps as position-dependent masses, including topological mass-barriers at segment interfaces. For graphene nanorings, they used tight-binding calculations and a Dirac-Kronig-Penney (DKP) approach with alternating-sign position-dependent masses to analyze Aharonov-Bohm spectra and soliton formation.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of relativistic electron behavior in graphene nanostructures beyond the massless Dirac-Weyl picture familiar from two-dimensional graphene sheets (Castro Neto, 2009). It builds on prior tight-binding and DFT studies of Aharonov-Bohm effects in graphene nanoribbons (Hod, 2006; Ezawa, 2007; Recher, 2007; Bahamon, 2009; Wurm, 2010; Nguyen, 2013, 2014) and on the concept of soliton formation in polyacetylene (Heeger, 1988; Jackiw, 1981, 2012). The results enable interpretation of forthcoming experiments on coherent transport in atomically-precise graphene nanoribbons and polygonal rings, and provide a framework for analyzing topological effects such as zero-energy soliton states and charge fractionization in graphene nanorings.
