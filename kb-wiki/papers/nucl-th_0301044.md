# Homogeneity and Size Effects on the Liquid-Gas Coexistence Curve

> The effects of (in)homogeneity and size on the phase diagram of Lennard-Jones fluids are investigated. It is shown that standard multifragmentation scenarios (finite equilibrated systems with conserved center of mass position and momentum) are implying a strong radial inhomogeneity of the system strongly affecting the phase diagram. The homogeneity constraint is therefore necessary for finite systems in order to align to the ``meaning'' of infinite systems phase diagrams. In this respect, a method which deduces the equation of state of homogeneous finite systems from the one corresponding to bulk matter is designed. The resultant phase diagrams show a strong dependence on the system's size.

# Homogeneity and Size Effects on the Liquid-Gas Coexistence Curve

## Key claim

Standard multifragmentation scenarios that conserve center-of-mass position and momentum produce strong radial inhomogeneity in finite systems, which severely distorts the phase diagram relative to infinite matter; imposing a homogeneity constraint is necessary to make finite-system phase diagrams comparable to the infinite-system meaning, and even for systems as large as A=10^4 the phase diagram still deviates significantly from the bulk limit.

## Method

The authors performed Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations of Lennard-Jones 6-12 fluids in spherical containers, first with center-of-mass conservation (simulating multifragmentation conditions) and then with periodic boundary conditions to represent infinite homogeneous matter. For homogeneous finite systems, they derived a surface-correction formula for the virial energy term based on a spherical cap model, allowing the equation of state for any system size to be computed from bulk properties. Phase coexistence boundaries were obtained by performing Maxwell constructions on canonical P(V) isotherms, and the resulting coexistence curves were fitted with Guggenheim scaling relations to extract critical parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the truncated and shifted (TS) Lennard-Jones potential in the infinite system: the critical exponent β = 0.34, corresponding to the liquid-gas universality class.
- For the TS potential, important deviations from the infinite phase diagram are observed even for systems as large as A = 10^4.
- The liquid border density for A=50 systems with conserved center of mass is below 0.2 σ⁻³, compared to approximately 0.8 σ⁻³ for infinite homogeneous Lennard-Jones systems from Hansen and Verlet.
- The truncation radius used for the Lennard-Jones potential is r_c = 2.5 σ.
- For the temperature T = 1.05 ε, the backbending in the P(ρ) curve completely disappears for system sizes as small as A = 20.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition by Siemens and by Lee-Mekjian, and addresses finite-size effects previously examined within Hartree-Fock theories by Jaqaman and Satpathy. The authors show that the homogeneity constraint is essential for meaningful comparison with infinite-matter phase diagrams from Hansen and Verlet and from Guggenheim. The results demonstrate that nuclear multifragmentation experiments, where equilibrated systems are typically smaller than A=300, cannot approach the thermodynamic limit, and this size effect must be combined with the Coulomb effect studied in earlier work by the same group.
