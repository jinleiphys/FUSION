# Medium separation scheme effects on the magnetized and cold two-flavor superconducting quark matter

> We analyze the impact of the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS) on two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) dense quark matter under the influence of a constant external magnetic field. The effects of the proper treatment of the model divergences are examined through a comparison of different approaches, including the combined implementation of the Magnetic Field Independent Regularization (MFIR) and the MSS, as well as the standard use of smooth form factors. Our findings for the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model emphasize the critical role of properly separating medium effects from vacuum contributions in the model. The combined MFIR-MSS scheme suppresses spurious unphysical oscillations, often misinterpreted in the literature as de Haas--van Alphen oscillations, and ensures the correct high-density behavior of the diquark condensate. Furthermore, within the MSS framework, the magnetization remains positive across the explored parameter space, in sharp contrast with the behavior obtained in the traditional approach.

## Key claim

The combined Magnetic Field Independent Regularization (MFIR) and Medium Separation Scheme (MSS) suppresses spurious unphysical oscillations in the diquark condensate and magnetization that are often misinterpreted as de Haas–van Alphen oscillations, and ensures the correct high-density behavior of the diquark condensate, which increases with chemical potential rather than decreasing as in the traditional regularization scheme.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar-pseudoscalar and diquark interactions in mean-field approximation at zero temperature, under a constant external magnetic field. They compare three regularization approaches: the traditional regularization scheme (TRS) with a sharp cutoff, the non-magnetic field independent regularization (nMFIR) using smooth form factors, and the combined MFIR-MSS scheme which isolates both magnetic field and chemical potential dependencies from divergent vacuum integrals. The gap equations for the constituent quark mass M and the diquark condensate Δ are solved self-consistently using parameters fitted to vacuum observables (fπ = 93.2 MeV, mπ = 135 MeV, ⟨q̄q⟩¹/³ = −250 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are G_S = 4.75 GeV⁻², m_c = 4.99 MeV, Λ = 660 MeV, yielding vacuum quark mass M₀ ≈ 302 MeV; the diquark coupling G_D = 0.75 G_S is obtained via Fierz transformation.
- At μ = 0.4 GeV, the MFIR and MFIR+MSS schemes show both Δ and M decreasing with increasing magnetic field (inverse magnetic catalysis), while nMFIR shows both increasing with pronounced unphysical oscillations.
- A smoothness parameter α = 0.01 Λ is used for the Fermi-Dirac form factor in the nMFIR scheme.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on regularization in the NJL model, particularly the MSS approach first applied to color superconductivity by Farias (2005) and later to chiral imbalance by Farias (2016), finite isospin density by Avancini (2019), and two-color QCD by Lopes (2025). The MFIR method was proposed by Ebert (2003) to remove unphysical oscillations in magnetic field-dependent quantities. The combined MFIR-MSS treatment is applied here for the first time to color-superconducting quark matter, enabling physically reliable predictions for magnetized dense matter relevant to neutron stars and magnetars, and resolving discrepancies with lattice QCD results for N_c = 2 by Kogut (2001) and Braguta (2016) that show the diquark condensate increasing with chemical potential.
