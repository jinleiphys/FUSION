# Towards three-dimensional nucleon structures at the Electron-Ion Collider in China: A study of the Sivers function

> Transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) provide three-dimensional imaging of the nucleon in the momentum space. With its fundamental importance in understanding the spin structure of the nucleon, the precise measurement of TMDs is considered as one of the main physics topics of the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC). In this paper, we investigate the impact of future semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data from EicC on the extraction of TMDs. Taking the Sivers function as an example, we revisited the world SIDIS data, which serves as the input for the simulated EicC data. By performing a combined analysis of the world data and the EicC simulated data, we quantitatively demonstrate that the Sivers functions can be precisely determined for various quark flavors, especially for sea quarks, in the region, $x>0.005$, directly covered by the EicC.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that future semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data from the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC) will enable precise determination of the Sivers function for various quark flavors, particularly sea quarks, in the kinematic region \(x > 0.005\), but only when combined with existing world data in a global analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of existing world SIDIS transverse single-spin asymmetry data from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab, using TMD factorization with next-to-leading logarithmic resummation and the \(\zeta\)-prescription for TMD evolution. They then generated simulated pseudodata for charged pion and charged kaon production from both \(ep\) and \(e^3\)He collisions at EicC kinematics, and performed a combined fit of the world data plus the simulated EicC data to extract the Sivers function parameters for light quark flavors.

## Key numbers

- EicC is designed to deliver a 3.5 GeV electron beam colliding with a 20 GeV polarized proton beam, with 70% ion beam polarization and 80% electron beam polarization
- The instantaneous luminosity of \(ep\) collision at EicC can reach \(2 \times 10^{33} \, \text{cm}^{-2} \, \text{s}^{-1}\)
- The nonperturbative parameters for the TMD evolution were fixed using \(B_{\text{NP}} = 1.93 \, \text{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(c_0 = 0.0391 \, \text{GeV}^2\) from Scimemi and Vladimirov (2019)
- The world data set includes 10 data points from COMPASS (2008) on a \(^6\)LiD target, 10 from COMPASS (2014) on NH\(_3\), 104 from HERMES (2020) on H\(_2\), 4 from JLab Hall A (2011) on \(^3\)He, and 2 from JLab Hall A (2014) on \(^3\)He

## Context

This work extends the global extraction methodology for TMD PDFs, specifically the Sivers function, which was pioneered by analyses such as Echevarria et al. (2020) and Bury et al. (2021) that still had large uncertainties especially for sea quarks. The study is motivated by the EicC proposal (Anderle et al., 2021) and is designed to complement the JLab 12 GeV SIDIS program at large \(x\) and the EIC at BNL at small \(x\), enabling complete three-dimensional imaging of the nucleon across a wide kinematic range.
