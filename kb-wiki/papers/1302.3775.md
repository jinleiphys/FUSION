# Rotational constants of multi-phonon bands in an effective theory for deformed nuclei

> We consider deformed nuclei within an effective theory that exploits the small ratio between rotational and vibrational excitations. For even-even nuclei, the effective theory predicts small changes in the rotational constants of bands built on multi-phonon excitations that are linear in the number of excited phonons. In 166Er and 168Er, this explains the main variations of the rotational constants of the two-phonon gamma vibrational bands. In 232Th, the effective theory correctly explains the trend that the rotational constants decrease with increasing spin of the band head. We also study the effective theory for deformed odd nuclei. Here, time-odd terms enter the Lagrangian and generate effective magnetic forces that yield the high level densities observed in such nuclei.

## Key claim

For even-even deformed nuclei, the effective theory predicts that the rotational constants of bands built on multi-phonon excitations change linearly with the number of excited phonons, with the correction being of order ξ²/Ω (where ξ ≈ 100 keV is the rotational energy scale and Ω ≈ 1 MeV is the vibrational scale). For odd-mass nuclei, time-odd terms in the Lagrangian generate effective magnetic forces that yield the high level densities observed in such nuclei.

## Method

The authors extend an effective field theory for axially symmetric deformed nuclei, based on quadrupole degrees of freedom and a nonlinear realization of rotational symmetry, from next-to-leading order (NLO) to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They construct the NNLO Lagrangian by including all rotation-vibration coupling terms of order ξ²/Ω, perform a perturbative Legendre transformation using Fukuda's inversion method, and compute the expectation values of the NNLO correction in the NLO eigenstates. For odd-mass nuclei, they construct the Lagrangian at NLO including time-odd terms, gauge away total derivative terms, and obtain a Hamiltonian describing a particle in a constant magnetic field coupled to rotations via a Wess-Zumino term.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁸Er: ground-state band rotational constant A_g.s. = 13.17 keV; γ-band (K=2) A = 12.33 keV; two-phonon γ-band (K=4) A = 11.37 keV (experimental) vs. A_theo = 11.49 keV (prediction), with a_γ = 0.84 keV
- For ¹⁶⁶Er: ground-state band A_g.s. = 13.43 keV; γ-band (K=2) A = 12.25 keV; two-phonon γ-band (K=4) A = 10.56 keV (experimental) vs. A_theo = 11.07 keV (prediction), with a_γ = 1.18 keV
- For ²³²Th: ground-state band A_g.s. = 8.23 keV; γ-band (K=2) A = 7.38 keV; two-phonon γ-band (K=4) A = 7.27 keV (experimental) vs. A_theo = 6.53 keV (prediction), with a_γ = 0.85 keV
- Typical energy scales: rotational excitations ξ ≈ 100 keV, vibrational excitations Ω ≈ 1 MeV in rare earth nuclei; breakdown scale Λ = κΩ with κ ≈ 2 or 3

## Context

This work extends the effective theory for deformed nuclei developed by Papenbrock (2011) from NLO to NNLO, addressing the longstanding problem that rotational constants decrease with increasing band-head energy in well-deformed nuclei, which traditional collective models (Warner 1981, Kuyucak 1988, Caprio 2009, 2011) could not explain. The theory correctly predicts the rotational constants of two-phonon γ-vibrational bands in ¹⁶⁸Er and ¹⁶⁶Er and the decreasing trend in ²³²Th, using only three low-energy constants fitted to the ground-state, β, and γ bands. For odd-mass nuclei, the approach provides an alternative to the Kerman (1956) particle-rotor coupling model and boson-fermion models (Iachello 1979), treating the odd nucleon's spin as a static quantity rather than a dynamical degree of freedom.
