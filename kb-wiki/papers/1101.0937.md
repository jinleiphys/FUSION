# Dynamics of Quark Gluon Plasma and Interference of Thermal Photons

> The quantum statistical interference between identical particles emitted from a completely chaotic source is expected to provide valuable input for the space time description of the system. Intensity interferometry of thermal photons produced in heavy ion collisions is a very promising tool to explore the structure and dynamics of the collision fireball. Thermal photons having $K_T \, \le \, 2$ GeV/$c$ get competing contribution from both hadronic and quark matter phases and this competition gives rise to a rich structure in the outward correlation function, owing to the interference between the photons from the two sources. The temporal separation between the two sources provides the lifetime of the system and the correlation results are found to be sensitive to quark hadron phase transition temperature and the formation time of the plasma. The outward correlation function strongly depends on the equation of state of the strongly interacting matter and is seen to clearly distinguish between the lattice based and bag model equations of state.

## Key claim

The outward correlation function for thermal photons with transverse momentum $K_T \le 2$ GeV/$c$ in heavy ion collisions can distinguish between a bag model equation of state (first-order phase transition) and a lattice-based equation of state (rapid cross-over), provided the competing contributions from quark matter and hadronic matter phases are resolved through their interference pattern.

## Method

The authors performed ideal hydrodynamic simulations with boost-invariant longitudinal and azimuthally symmetric transverse expansion for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (200A GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (5.5A TeV). They computed the intensity correlation function for thermal photons using leading-order photon production rates from quark matter (Arnold et al.) and hadronic matter (Turbide et al.), then decomposed the correlation into outward, sideward, and longitudinal components. Two equations of state were compared: a bag model EOS (HHB) with a first-order phase transition at $T_C = 165$ MeV, and a lattice-matched EOS (HHL) incorporating hadron+Hagedorn resonance gas with volume correction matched to lattice p4 action results.

## Key numbers

- Initial formation time $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/$c$ for RHIC and $\tau_0 = 0.1$ fm/$c$ for LHC
- Bag model phase transition temperature set at approximately 180 MeV; freeze-out temperature taken as 100 MeV
- For $K_T \approx 2$ GeV/$c$ at RHIC, the temporal separation between quark and hadronic sources $\Delta R_o = 12.3$ fm, while $\Delta R_s$ and $\Delta R_\ell$ are approximately zero
- Charged particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity taken as 1260 for 200A GeV Au+Au at RHIC and 5625 for 5.5A TeV Pb+Pb at LHC
- Critical temperature for matching hadronic to lattice EOS set at $T = 165$ MeV; lattice EOS shows sharp cross-over for $180 < T < 190$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical framework for photon intensity interferometry developed by Srivastava et al. (2002) and the angular-dependent correlation study by Frodermann and Heinz (2009). It directly builds on the observation that thermal photons in the intermediate $K_T$ range receive competing contributions from quark and hadronic phases, as first highlighted in Srivastava et al. (2009). The key advance is demonstrating that the outward correlation function can discriminate between a bag model EOS (first-order transition) and a lattice-based EOS (cross-over), whereas particle spectra alone cannot. This enables future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to constrain the equation of state of strongly interacting matter through photon intensity interferometry, with the authors noting that a fully 3+1D hydrodynamic treatment (Nonaka, 2006) would be a valuable next step.
