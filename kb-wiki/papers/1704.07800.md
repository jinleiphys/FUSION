# A data-driven analysis of the heavy quark transport coefficient

> Using a Bayesian model-to-data analysis, we estimate the temperature dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficients by calibrating to the experimental data of $D$-meson $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ and $v_2$ in AuAu collisions ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV) and PbPb collisions ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV)~\cite{Xie:2016iwq}. The spatial diffusion coefficient $D_s2πT$ is found to be mostly constraint around $(1.3-1.5) T_c$ and is compatible with lattice QCD calculations. We demonstrate the capability of our improved Langevin model to simultaneously describe the $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ and $v_2$ at both RHIC and the LHC energies, as well as the feasibility to apply a Bayesian analysis to quantitatively study the heavy flavor transport in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s 2\pi T\) for charm quarks is constrained most strongly in the temperature range \((1.3-1.5) T_c\) and is compatible with lattice QCD calculations, but the slope parameter describing its temperature dependence remains relatively unconstrained, indicating either a missing piece in the parametrization or too large experimental data uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian model-to-data analysis using a Langevin model that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss, with the spatial diffusion coefficient parametrized as a linear function of temperature via two parameters (\(D_{\mathrm{min}}, D_{\mathrm{slope}}\)). They trained Gaussian process emulators on 80 Latin hypercube-sampled parameter sets evaluated with the full model, then used Markov chain Monte Carlo to calibrate the parameters to experimental \(D\)-meson \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) and \(v_2\) data from AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Key numbers
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s 2\pi T\) is most constrained in the temperature range \((1.3-1.5) T_c\).
- The analysis used 80 parameter sets uniformly sampled via a Latin hypercube algorithm to train the Gaussian process emulators.
- 200 input parameter values were randomly selected from the posterior distribution for validation.
- The study calibrated to experimental data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV (AuAu) and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV (PbPb).

## Context
This work extends Bayesian model-to-data analysis, previously applied successfully to shear and bulk viscosities by Bernhard (2016), to the heavy flavor sector for the first time. It addresses the challenge of simultaneously describing \(D\)-meson \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) and \(v_2\) at both RHIC and LHC energies, which most previous models could not achieve with manual parameter tuning. The results enable future improvements such as applying Boltzmann transport, including hadronic scattering, and extending the analysis to more collision systems and bottom quarks.
