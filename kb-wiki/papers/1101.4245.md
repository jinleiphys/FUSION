# Facets of the QCD Phase-Diagram

> In this contribution we will discuss two aspects of the matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. First we will attempt to define a universal measure for the fluidity of a substance, which will allow a correct comparison between the fluidity of a Quark Gluon Plasma and any well known substance. Second we will discuss current measurements of particle correlations and their implication for possible local parity violation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) is not a suitable universal measure for comparing fluidity across different substances, and proposes an alternative fluidity measure based on kinematic viscosity and inter-particle distance. The authors also critically examine whether the STAR collaboration's charge-dependent correlation measurements actually demonstrate the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and local parity violation, concluding that the evidence is inconclusive because the observed same-charge correlations are in-plane back-to-back rather than the out-of-plane same-side correlations predicted by the CME.

## Method

The authors derive a universal fluidity measure F = Lη/Ln, where Lη = η/(w·cs) for relativistic fluids (with enthalpy density w and speed of sound cs) and Lη = η/(ρ·cs) for non-relativistic fluids, and Ln = 1/n^(1/3) is the inter-particle distance. They compute this measure for fifteen different substances using data from the NIST webbook and compare it with estimates for the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) using a parametrization of viscosity from Hirano and Gyulassy (2005) and lattice results from Cheng et al (2007) for 2+1 flavor QCD. For the CME analysis, they decompose the STAR collaboration's measured three-particle correlator γ = ⟨cos(ϕi+ϕj-2ϕk)⟩ and two-particle correlator δ = ⟨cos(ϕi-ϕj)⟩ into separate in-plane and out-of-plane components.

## Key numbers

- The conjectured lower bound on η/s from gauge theories is 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- The fluidity measure for the strongly coupled AdS/CFT system is estimated as F = √3/(4π) ≈ 0.138
- The shear viscosity of the QGP is estimated as η_QGP ≈ 2×10^9 Pa·s, which is approximately 10^12 times larger than that of water
- The drag force on a particle moving through the QGP is estimated as F ≈ 5·R·u GeV/fm^2, giving dE/dx ≈ 1.25 GeV/fm for a particle with radius R = 0.5 fm and velocity u = 0.5
- The paper uses lattice results from Cheng et al (2007) for 2+1 flavor QCD with m_π ≈ 220 MeV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of fluidity in heavy ion collisions beyond the η/s ratio popularized by Policastro et al (2001) and Csernai et al (2006), proposing a measure applicable to both relativistic and non-relativistic systems. The fluidity analysis builds on the authors' prior work Liao (2009) and suggests that the QGP may be a super-critical fluid, analogous to super-critical water at high pressures. The CME analysis critically examines the STAR collaboration measurements from 2009 and follows the authors' earlier detailed studies in Bzdak (2009), Liao (2010), and Bzdak (2010), concluding that conventional physics explanations such as Coulomb interactions, transverse-momentum conservation, local charge conservation as discussed by Pratt (2010), and cluster decays as discussed by Wang (2009) may contribute to the observed correlations, and that alternative observables proposed in Liao (2010) and Ajitanand (2010) are needed to resolve the question of local parity violation.
