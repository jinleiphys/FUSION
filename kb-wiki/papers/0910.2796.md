# Dynamical properties of the crust of neutron stars derived from realistic NN interactions

> The mean free path of neutrino in charged and neutral current reactions is calculated for inhomogeneous nuclear matter which is expected to appear in the crust of neutron stars. The relevant cross section depends on Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength distributions, which are derived from the large-scale shell model calculations within the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach and in a relativistic mean-field model. The inhomogeneous nuclear matter is described in terms of cubic Wigner-Seitz cells, which allows for a microscopic description of the structures in the so-called pasta phase of nuclear configurations and provides a smooth transition to the limit of homogeneous matter. The influence of pasta phase, its microscopical structure and geometrical shapes on neutrino propagation is considered.

# Dynamical properties of the crust of neutron stars derived from realistic NN interactions

## Key claim
The neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in the crust of neutron stars is significantly affected by the inhomogeneous "pasta phase" structures, but only for charged current reactions at low neutrino energies; for neutral current reactions and at higher energies the effect of inhomogeneous structures is negligible. The authors find that the NMFP from charged current reactions in inhomogeneous matter can be *longer* than in homogeneous matter at the same global density, contrary to the expectation that scattering on quasi-nuclear structures would enhance the cross section.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent 3D Hartree-Fock calculations in cubic Wigner-Seitz cells using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction and a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) model, both with BCS pairing correlations. Single-particle wave functions and energies from these calculations were used to evaluate the neutrino mean free path for charged current (ν+n→p+e) and neutral current (ν+n→ν+n) reactions, averaging over three spatial orientations of the momentum transfer for non-spherical pasta structures (rods and slabs). The calculations were performed for β-stable matter at temperature T=1 MeV across a density range where droplet, rod, and slab pasta phases appear.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature for pasta phase stability in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach is around 5 MeV for slab structures and 10 MeV for rod structures, while droplet structures disappear above 15 MeV; in the relativistic approach droplet structures disappear at T=10 MeV
- For the rod structure at density 0.0625 fm⁻³, the NMFP for charged current reactions ranges from approximately 20 km for 10 MeV neutrinos down to 30 cm for 100 MeV neutrinos
- For low-energy neutrinos in the rod phase, the NMFP for momentum transfer parallel to the x-axis is larger by a factor of 2 compared to momentum transfer parallel to the z-axis
- The proton abundance in inhomogeneous matter is approximately constant around 3.2% in the density region from 0.03 to 0.08 fm⁻³, compared to 4% at 0.1 fm⁻³ for homogeneous matter in the Skyrme model
- At density 0.0165 fm⁻³ (droplet phase), the proton abundance in inhomogeneous matter is significantly larger than in homogeneous matter

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino opacities in homogeneous nuclear matter (Reddy 1998, 1999; Navarro 1999; Margueron 2003) by incorporating the microscopic description of pasta phase structures derived from 3D Hartree-Fock calculations (Goegelein 2006, 2007). It builds on previous studies of pasta phase ground-state structure using Thomas-Fermi approximations (Oyamatsu 1993), Quantum Molecular Dynamics (Horowitz 2004; Watanabe 2003; Sonoda 2007), and earlier investigations of dynamical properties including response functions and neutrino mean free path (Horowitz 2004; Reddy 2005; Burrows 2005). The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in neutron star crusts and supernova simulations, though the authors note that collective modes of weakly bound neutrons (Khan 2004) have not yet been included and should be addressed in future work.
