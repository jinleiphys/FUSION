# Roberge-Weiss phase transition and its endpoint

> The Roberge-Weiss (RW) phase transition in the imaginary chemical potential region is analyzed by the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. In the RW phase transition, the charge-conjugation symmetry is spontaneously broken, while the extended Z3 symmetry (the RW periodicity) is preserved. The RW transition is of second order at the endpoint. At the zero chemical potential, a crossover deconfinement transition appears as a remnant of the second-order RW phase transition at the endpoint, while the charge-conjugation symmetry is always preserved.

## Key claim

The Roberge-Weiss (RW) phase transition at imaginary chemical potential is of second order at its endpoint, and the crossover deconfinement transition at zero chemical potential appears as a remnant of this second-order RW phase transition, with charge-conjugation symmetry always preserved at zero chemical potential.

## Method

The two-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model was used to analyze the phase diagram in the imaginary chemical potential region, where the chemical potential is set to mu = i T theta. The thermodynamic potential was calculated in the mean-field approximation with a Polyakov potential fitted to reproduce lattice QCD results for pure gauge theory, and the stationary conditions were solved for the Polyakov loop, its conjugate, and the chiral condensate as functions of temperature T and the imaginary chemical potential parameter theta.

## Key numbers

* The endpoint temperature of the RW phase transition is T_E = 205 MeV, determined from the discontinuity in d psi/dT at theta = pi.
* The pseudocritical temperature of the crossover deconfinement transition at theta = 0 is T_C = 179 MeV.
* The ratio T_E / T_C = 1.15, showing the RW endpoint lies above the crossover transition temperature.
* The pure gauge deconfinement temperature used to fix the Polyakov potential is T_0 = 270 MeV.
* The NJL model parameters used are m_0 = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 0.6315 GeV, and G_s = 5.498 GeV^{-2}, reproducing f_pi = 93.3 MeV and M_pi = 138 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original analysis of Roberge and Weiss (1986) on the periodicity and phase structure of QCD at imaginary chemical potential, and is consistent with the speculation by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002) based on lattice QCD that the RW transition endpoint is second order. The PNJL model used here was developed by Fukushima (2004) and others to treat both chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, and the results enable future determination of the real-chemical-potential phase diagram by fitting PNJL parameters to lattice QCD data in the imaginary chemical potential region, as recently attempted by Sakai et al. (2009).
