# An amplitude analysis of the $\overline{N}N \to π^- π^+$ reaction

> A simple partial wave amplitude analysis of $\overline{p}p \rightarrow π^- π^+$ has been performed for data in the range p$_{\sl lab}$ = 360 -- 1000 MeV/c. Remarkably few partial waves are required to fit the data, while the number of required $J$ values barely changes over this energy range. However, the resulting set of partial wave amplitudes is not unique. We discuss possible measurements with polarized beam and target which will severely restrict and help resolve the present analysis ambiguities. New data from the reaction $\overline{p}p \rightarrow π^0 π^0$ alone, are insufficient for that purpose.

## Key claim

A partial wave amplitude analysis of $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^- \pi^+$ data in the range p$_{\rm lab}$ = 360–988 MeV/c finds that remarkably few partial waves (J ≤ 3) are sufficient to fit the data, but the resulting set of partial wave amplitudes is not unique; multiple distinct parameter sets yield equally good $\chi^2$ fits.

## Method

The two independent helicity amplitudes for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^- \pi^+$ were expanded in spin-triplet partial wave amplitudes with total angular momentum J. At each of ten antiproton laboratory momenta between 360 and 988 MeV/c, the amplitudes were parametrized as $f_{LJ} = R_{LJ} e^{i \delta_{LJ}}$ and a $\chi^2$ search was performed to fit both the differential cross section $d\sigma/d\Omega$ and the analyzing power $A_{\rm on}$. The analysis was repeated with maximum J values of 2, 3, and 4 to test the necessity of higher partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Data analyzed cover p$_{\rm lab}$ = 360–988 MeV/c, corresponding to an invariant mass region of 1910–2078 MeV.
- Data at ten momenta between 360 and 988 MeV/c were fitted; at p$_{\rm lab}$ = 783 MeV/c, $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom was 1.49 for J$_{\rm max}$ = 3, 1.47 for J$_{\rm max}$ = 4, and 1.50 for J = 1,2,3,4 (excluding J=0).
- The total cross section for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^- \pi^+$ computed from the fitted amplitudes agrees with the total cross section of Hasan et al. within a few percent.
- At p$_{\rm lab}$ = 360 MeV/c, the J = 2 contribution to the total cross section is considerable; the J = 3 contribution is small at 360 MeV/c but becomes substantial at 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This analysis extends earlier work by Carter (1965), Martin and P. (1970), Martin and M. (1970), Oades (1970), and Bowcock (1970) using pre-LEAR data, and the newer Durham analysis by Oakden and Pennington (1995) which used Barrelet zeros. It contrasts with the resonance-based approach of Hasan and Bugg (1995), who assumed each partial wave amplitude is a sum of up to four resonances. The authors find no compelling evidence for resonance behavior in the Argand plots over this energy range, unlike Oakden and Pennington, and conclude that measurements of spin-correlation observables such as A$_{\rm ss}$ or A$_{\rm ls}$ with polarized beam and target are necessary to resolve the amplitude ambiguities.
