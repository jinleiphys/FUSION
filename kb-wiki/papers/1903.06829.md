# Shape of the quark gluon plasma droplet reflected in the high-p_T data

> We show, through analytic arguments, numerical calculations, and comparison with experimental data, that the ratio of the high-p_T observables v_2/(1-R_AA) reaches a well-defined saturation value at high p_T, and that this ratio depends only on the spatial anisotropy of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. With expected future reduction of experimental errors, the anisotropy extracted from experimental data will further constrain the calculations of initial particle production in heavy-ion collisions and thus test our understanding of QGP physics.

## Key claim

The ratio \(v_2/(1-R_{AA})\) of high-\(p_\perp\) observables reaches a well-defined saturation value at high transverse momentum that depends only on the spatial anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma droplet, not on its material properties; this holds under the approximation that fractional energy loss is small and that the temperature and path-length anisotropies are linearly correlated.

## Method

The authors used their dynamical energy loss formalism (DREENA-B framework) which includes finite-size, finite-temperature QCD medium with dynamical partons, both radiative and collisional energy losses under the same theoretical framework, finite magnetic mass, running coupling, and multi-gluon fluctuations. They performed analytic derivations showing that \(v_2/(1-R_{AA})\) asymptotically equals \(0.57\varsigma\), where \(\varsigma = (\langle L_{out}\rangle - \langle L_{in}\rangle)/(\langle L_{out}\rangle + \langle L_{in}\rangle)\) is the path-length anisotropy, and then compared full numerical calculations with ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS data for 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at various centralities.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic scaling relation is \(v_2/(1-R_{AA}) \approx 0.57\varsigma\), where the prefactor \(0.57\) comes from \(a \approx 1.2\) and \(b \approx 1.4\) (temperature and path-length exponents) and \(c \approx 4.3\) (linear correlation between \(\Delta T/\langle T\rangle\) and \(\Delta L/\langle L\rangle\)).
- The asymptotic regime is reached from \(p_\perp \sim 20\)–30 GeV, with the asymptote matching Eq. (6) within \(\pm 5\%\) accuracy.
- The extracted \(\epsilon_{2L}\) from the DREENA-B results agrees with the initial \(\epsilon_2\) (binary collision scaling, optical Glauber model) within \(\sim 10\%\) accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Djordjevic et al., 2019; Zigic et al., 2019) on using high-\(p_\perp\) observables as tomographic probes of the QGP, and provides a complementary method to low-\(p_\perp\) approaches for extracting bulk QGP properties. The ratio \(v_2/(1-R_{AA})\) offers a way to directly infer the spatial anisotropy of the QGP droplet from high-\(p_\perp\) data, which traditionally were used mainly to study jet-medium interactions rather than bulk parameters. The extracted anisotropy parameter \(\varsigma\) (or \(\epsilon_{2L}\)) can be compared with initial-state models such as MC-Glauber (Loizides et al., 2018), EKRT (Eskola et al., 2018), IP-Glasma (Bernhard et al., 2016), and MC-KLN (Hirano et al., 2013), and with future high-luminosity LHC data will further constrain calculations of initial particle production.
