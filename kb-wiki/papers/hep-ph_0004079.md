# Proposal to observe the strong Van der Waals force in the low energy neutron-Pb scattering

> In the neutron-Pb scattering in the MeV. region, existence of the long range interaction has been known and people have hopefully expected to understand it as the effect of the electric polarization of the neutron. However the precise determination of $α_{n}$ in the very low energy region indicates that the polarizability of the neutron $α_{n}$ is around two order of magnitude smaller compared to what is expected in the phenomena in the MeV. region. We need another strong long range potential which decreases faster than $r^{-4}$. On the other hand, analysis of the low energy proton-proton data revealed the existence of the strong long range potential whose tail was $V(r) \sim -C/r^α$ with $α=6.08$. The neuton-nucleus scattering is a good place to see such a force, since the strength $C$ is magnified by factor $A$, the mass number. Using the long range parameters obtained in p-p, we predict the characteristic angular dependency of the n-Pb amplitude for fixed energy. Appearance of a cusp at $ν=0$, which points upward, in the once subtracted P-wave amplitude $a_{1}(ν)/ν$ is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the long-range interaction observed in neutron-Pb scattering in the MeV region, previously attributed to the electric polarizability of the neutron, actually arises from a strong Van der Waals force of nuclear origin, because the neutron polarizability determined from very low energy data is two orders of magnitude too small to explain the MeV-region effect.

## Method

The author uses parameters of the long-range tail of the nuclear potential (power-law exponent α = 6.08 and strength C = 0.196 in units of the neutral pion Compton wavelength) previously extracted from analysis of low-energy proton-proton S-wave phase shifts, and convolves this potential with a box-form nuclear density for Pb-208 to compute the neutron-Pb scattering amplitude. The singular behavior at t=0 arising from the long-range force is then used to predict characteristic anomalies in the angular distribution (a (1-z)^γ term near forward angles) and in the once-subtracted P-wave amplitude a₁(ν)/ν (a cusp at ν=0).

## Key numbers

- Neutron electric polarizability from Schmiedmayer et al.: α_n = (1.20 ± 0.15 ± 0.20) × 10⁻³ fm³
- Long-range potential tail from p-p analysis: V(r) ∼ -C/r^α with α = 6.08 and C = 0.196 (in units of neutral pion Compton wavelength)
- Spectral function parameters from p-p fit: γ = 1.543, C' = 0.1762, β = 0.06264 (in units of neutral pion mass), with χ = 0.441
- For n-Pb predictions, the nuclear radius parameter r₁ is taken as either 4.4 (standard) or 3.5 (to match the observed slope d(√ν cot δ₀)/dν = 1.9)
- Incident energies considered: T_lab = 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, and 1.0 MeV (corresponding to ν = 0.102123 at 1 MeV)
- The n-Pb cross section fit from Schmiedmayer: σ(ν) = 11.508(5) + 0.69(9)√ν - 448(3)ν + 9500(400)ν² (barn units, ν in fm⁻²)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior analysis of the strong Van der Waals force in proton-proton scattering (Sawada, using p-p phase shift data) to the neutron-nucleus system, where the strength is magnified by the mass number A = 208. It contradicts the interpretation that the long-range effect in MeV neutron-Pb scattering is due to neutron electric polarizability, as the polarizability measured by Schmiedmayer et al. (1990s) in the very low energy region (0.05-40 keV) is too small. The proposal enables experimental tests of the strong Van der Waals force in nuclear physics through precise angular distribution measurements of n-Pb scattering around 1 MeV, and could improve the determination of the long-range force parameters γ and C'.
