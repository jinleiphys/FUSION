# Holographic tetraquarks and the newly observed $T_{cc}^{+}$ at LHCb

> We describe a heavy and exotic tetraquark state as a holographic molecule, by binding the lightest heavy-light meson $(0^-, 1^-)$ multiplet to a flavored sphaleron, in the bulk of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. Bound tetraquark states emerge as Efimov states in the heavy quark limit, with a binding energy for charm tetraquark comparable to the $T_{cc}^+$ recently reported by the LHCb, but a substantially smaller width, for a large but finite $^\prime$t Hooft coupling. Fixing the parameters of the model at the empirical mass of $T_{cc}^+$ allows us to predict the bindings of the undiscovered so far bottom-charm and bottom tetraquarks, when interpreted as Efimov states.

# Holographic tetraquarks and the newly observed $T_{cc}^{+}$ at LHCb

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $T_{cc}^{+}$ tetraquark recently observed by LHCb can be described as a holographic Efimov state formed by binding a heavy-light meson multiplet $(0^{-}, 1^{-})$ to a flavored sphaleron in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, but only for a large but finite 't Hooft coupling $\lambda \sim 30$; the binding energy is comparable to the experimental value, while the predicted width is substantially smaller than the measured width of $48 \pm 2$ keV.

## Method

The authors extend their previous holographic framework for heavy-light baryons to tetraquarks by replacing the instanton (topological charge 1) with a sphaleron path (topological charge 0, Chern-Simons number 1/2) in the D8-D$\bar{8}$ brane configuration. The tetraquark emerges as a bound state of two heavy-light mesons $(0^{-}, 1^{-})$ to the sphaleron core, with the binding described by an Efimov-type radial equation in the moduli space. The decay width is computed through a two-step process: the tetraquark decays into a sphaleron core plus $D$ and $D^{*}$ mesons, followed by the core decaying into pions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $cc\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark: $-0.0016$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=1$ GeV) and $-0.019$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=0.475$ GeV)
- Binding energy of $bb\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark: $-0.056$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=1$ GeV) and $-0.035$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=0.475$ GeV)
- Binding energy of $bc\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark: $-0.024$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=1$ GeV) and $-0.027$ GeV (for $\lambda = 30$, $M_{KK}=0.475$ GeV)
- Ratio of sphaleron mass to instanton mass: $M_0/M_1 = 3\pi/8\sqrt{2} = 0.83$
- Predicted decay width: $\Gamma_T(S) \approx 1$ keV $/(2S+1)$ for $\lambda \sim 30$, $\xi \sim 5$, and $g_H \sim 0.67$
- Empirical binding energy of $T_{cc}^{+}$: $-360 \pm 40$ keV
- Empirical width of $T_{cc}^{+}$: $+48 \pm 2$ keV

## Context

This work extends the holographic Callan-Klebanov mechanism (Callan and Klebanov, 1985) from heavy baryons to tetraquarks, using the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) which provides a holographic description of QCD with confinement and chiral symmetry breaking. The authors previously developed the framework for heavy-light baryons (Liu, Nowak, and Zahed, 2019) and now apply it to tetraquarks by using a sphaleron path instead of an instanton. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations (MALT collaboration) predicting $bb\bar{q}\bar{q}$ binding of $-(0.15-0.2)$ GeV and quark model estimates (Karliner and Rosner, 2017) of $-(0.17)$ GeV for bottom tetraquarks. The work enables predictions for undiscovered bottom-charm and bottom tetraquark states, with binding energies tabulated as functions of the 't Hooft coupling.
