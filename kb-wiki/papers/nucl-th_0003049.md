# Invariant Manifolds and Collective Coordinates

> We introduce suitable coordinate systems for interacting many-body systems with invariant manifolds. These are Cartesian in coordinate and momentum space and chosen such that several components are identically zero for motion on the invariant manifold. In this sense these coordinates are collective. We make a connection to Zickendraht's collective coordinates and present certain configurations of few-body systems where rotations and vibrations decouple from single-particle motion. These configurations do not depend on details of the interaction.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a systematic method to construct Cartesian coordinate systems that separate collective and single-particle motion on invariant manifolds in interacting many-body systems, but the decoupling is exact only on the manifold itself and depends on the system's rotational and permutational symmetries, not on the details of the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors construct orthogonal transformations from single-particle Cartesian coordinates to new coordinates by projecting onto irreducible representations of a finite subgroup of the rotation group combined with particle permutations, using character projectors. The identical representation defines the invariant manifold coordinates (collective), while other representations span the transverse (single-particle) directions. They demonstrate the construction explicitly for four particles in two dimensions with a quartic potential, expanding the potential to second order in transverse coordinates, and compare their coordinates to Zickendraht's collective coordinates for several few-body configurations.

## Key numbers

- For four particles in two dimensions on the C2v invariant manifold, the potential expansion yields six eigenvalues for the transverse quadratic form, e.g., lambda_{1,2} = 10x^2 + 14y^2 +/- 2 sqrt(x^4 + 25y^4 + 54x^2y^2); all eigenvalues are non-negative and vanish at the origin (x=y=0).
- For the eight-body configuration with C4h symmetry, the moments of inertia are I1 = I2 = 4m(x^2 + y^2) + 8mz^2 and I3 = 8m(x^2 + y^2), with collective coordinates y1^2 = y2^2 = 4(x^2 + y^2) and y3^2 = 8z^2.
- For the four-particle configuration in two dimensions, the Zickendraht collective coordinates are y1 = 2x and y2 = 2y, with constant single-particle coordinates s_{11}=s_{12}=s_{21}=-s_{22}=-s_{31}=-s_{32}=-s_{41}=s_{42}=1/2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings (Papenbrock and Seligman, 1996) on low-dimensional invariant manifolds in rotationally invariant systems of identical particles, and connects to Zickendraht's (1970) collective coordinates for nuclear vibrations and rotations. The results enable further study of damping and dissipation of collective excitations in atomic nuclei, as well as multi-particle fragmentation of atoms at threshold (Wintgen, 1992; Pattard and Rost, 1998), by providing coordinate systems where collective motion on stable invariant manifolds can be analyzed separately from single-particle degrees of freedom.
