# Chiral hydrodynamics in strong external magnetic fields

> We construct the general hydrodynamic description of (3+1)-dimensional chiral charged (quantum) fluids subject to a strong external magnetic field with effective field theory methods. We determine the constitutive equations for the energy-momentum tensor and the axial charge current, in part from a generating functional. Furthermore, we derive the Kubo formulas which relate two-point functions of the energy-momentum tensor and charge current to 27 transport coefficients: 8 independent thermodynamic, 4 independent non-dissipative hydrodynamic, and 10 independent dissipative hydrodynamic transport coefficients. Five Onsager relations render 5 more transport coefficients dependent. We uncover four novel transport effects, which are encoded in what we call the shear-induced conductivity, the two expansion-induced longitudinal conductivities and the shear-induced Hall conductivity. Remarkably, the shear-induced Hall conductivity constitutes a novel non-dissipative transport effect. As a demonstration, we compute all transport coefficients explicitly in a strongly coupled quantum fluid via holography.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a general hydrodynamic description of (3+1)-dimensional chiral charged fluids in a strong external magnetic field, claiming that the magnetic field breaks rotational symmetry from O(3) to O(2) and, combined with the chiral anomaly, leads to 27 transport coefficients (8 thermodynamic, 4 non-dissipative hydrodynamic, 10 dissipative hydrodynamic, plus 5 dependent via Onsager relations). The hedge is that this description applies specifically to fluids with an axial U(1)_A symmetry in the regime where the magnetic field is strong enough to be treated as zeroth order in derivatives, and the explicit holographic computation is performed in N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory as a proof of existence.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory methods to derive constitutive equations for the energy-momentum tensor and axial charge current, including a generating functional for equilibrium response. They derived Kubo formulas relating two-point correlation functions to all transport coefficients, then computed 25 of these coefficients explicitly via holography using the gauge/gravity correspondence, specifically Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in five dimensions dual to N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory with an external axial magnetic field and axial chemical potential, solving for charged magnetic black brane solutions with spectral methods.

## Key numbers

- 27 total transport coefficients: 8 independent thermodynamic, 4 independent non-dissipative hydrodynamic, 10 independent dissipative hydrodynamic, plus 5 dependent via Onsager relations
- 25 transport coefficients computed explicitly in the holographic model, of which 20 are nonzero
- 5 Onsager relations verified in the holographic computation
- 4 novel transport effects: shear-induced conductivity (c_8), two expansion-induced longitudinal conductivities (c_4 and c_5), and shear-induced Hall conductivity (c_10)
- The shear-induced Hall conductivity c_10 is a novel non-dissipative transport coefficient
- In the holographic model, the Chern-Simons coupling gamma is treated as a free parameter, with specific values gamma=0 and gamma=2/sqrt(3) (supersymmetric case) investigated
- The transverse Hall viscosity tilde_eta_perp vanishes in the holographic model without any obvious reason
- M_3 (magneto-acceleration susceptibility) vanishes due to conformal invariance
- Bulk viscosities zeta_1 and zeta_2 vanish due to conformal invariance
- c_3 is zero in the holographic model

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic descriptions of chiral fluids by incorporating strong magnetic fields as a zeroth-order effect rather than a first-order derivative correction, building on the generating functional approach of Jensen (2012), Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015), and Haehl, Loganayagam, and Rangamani (2015-2018). The paper confirms a previous claim by Chernodub (2017) that the standard Nernst coefficient is proportional to the conformal anomaly and related to magnetic susceptibility chi_B. The results enable future applications to hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions, astrophysical fluids in strong magnetic fields, Weyl semimetals, and quantum Hall systems, and provide Kubo relations that facilitate comparison with lattice QCD results for magnetoresponse as studied by Endrodi (2018).
