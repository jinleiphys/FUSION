# Production of new superheavy Z=108-114 nuclei with $^{238}$U, $^{244}$Pu and $^{248,250}$Cm targets

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system (DNS) model, production cross sections of new superheavy nuclei with charged numbers Z=108-114 are analyzed systematically. Possible combinations based on the actinide nuclides $^{238}$U, $^{244}$Pu and $^{248,250}$Cm with the optimal excitation energies and evaporation channels are pointed out to synthesize new isotopes which lie between the nuclides produced in the cold fusion and the $^{48}$Ca induced fusion reactions experimentally, which are feasible to be constructed experimentally. It is found that the production cross sections of superheavy nuclei decrease drastically with the charged numbers of compound nuclei. Larger mass asymmetries of the entrance channels enhance the cross sections in 2n-5n channels.

# Production of new superheavy Z=108-114 nuclei with 238U, 244Pu and 248,250Cm targets

## Key claim
Within the dinuclear system (DNS) model, the production cross sections of new superheavy nuclei with Z=108-114 decrease drastically with increasing charge of the compound nucleus, but larger mass asymmetries in the entrance channels enhance the cross sections in 2n-5n evaporation channels. The paper identifies specific projectile-target combinations and optimal excitation energies that could produce new isotopes lying between those accessible via cold fusion and 48Ca-induced hot fusion reactions, though the calculations are model-dependent and the cross sections for the heaviest elements (Z=113-114) are predicted to be very small (below 0.1 pb).

## Method
The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model, solving microscopically derived master equations that distinguish proton and neutron transfer coupled to the relative motion of the colliding nuclei, with a barrier distribution included in the capture and fusion process. The evaporation residue cross section was calculated as a sum over partial waves, incorporating the transmission probability through the Coulomb barrier, the probability of compound nucleus formation (competing with quasifission and fission), and the survival probability of the excited compound nucleus against neutron evaporation versus fission. Calculations were performed for multiple projectile-target combinations based on 238U, 244Pu, and 248,250Cm targets with various projectiles (e.g., 26Mg, 27Al, 30Si, 31P, 36S, 37Cl, 40Ar, 41K, 45Sc, 48Ti, 50Ti) to predict production cross sections for Z=108-114.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction 26Mg+248Cm producing Z=108 (Hs), the maximal 4n channel cross section is 719.1 pb at an excitation energy of 42 MeV.
- For the reaction 27Al+248Cm producing Z=109 (Mt), the maximal 3n channel cross section is 27.83 pb at 44 MeV excitation energy.
- For the reaction 30Si+248Cm producing Z=110 (Ds), the maximal 4n channel cross section is 65.32 pb at 43 MeV excitation energy.
- For the reaction 31P+248Cm producing Z=111 (Rg), the maximal 4n channel cross section is 1.85 pb at 45 MeV excitation energy.
- For the reaction 36S+250Cm producing Z=112 (Cn), the maximal 4n channel cross section is 0.24 pb at 42 MeV excitation energy.
- For the reaction 37Cl+248Cm producing Z=113 (Nh), the maximal 3n channel cross section is 0.26 pb at 35 MeV excitation energy.
- For all systems calculated for Z=114, the maximal production cross sections are less than 0.1 pb; for example, 40Ar+244Pu gives 9.84×10^(-3) pb in the 4n channel at 44 MeV.
- For the reaction 26Mg+250Cm, the 5n channel cross section is 108.5 pb at 45 MeV, compared to 185.2 pb for the 4n channel at 42 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the DNS model calculations previously applied by Feng et al. (2006, 2007, 2009) to systematically predict production cross sections for superheavy nuclei in the Z=108-114 range, filling the gap between cold fusion reactions using 208Pb and 209Bi targets (as performed at GSI by Hofmann et al., 2000) and hot fusion reactions using 48Ca projectiles on actinide targets (as performed at FLNR Dubna by Oganessian et al., 2004, 2006, 2007). The calculations identify specific combinations and evaporation channels that could be tested experimentally at existing facilities such as HIRFL in Lanzhou, enabling the synthesis of new neutron-rich isotopes that may serve as stepping stones toward the predicted "island of stability" near Z=114-126 and N=184.
