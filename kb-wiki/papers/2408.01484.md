# Old neutron stars as a new probe of relic neutrinos and sterile neutrino dark matter

> We study the kinetic cooling (heating) of old neutron stars due to coherent scattering with relic neutrinos (sterile neutrino dark matter) via Standard Model neutral-current interactions. We take into account several important physical effects, such as gravitational clustering, coherent enhancement, neutron degeneracy and Pauli blocking. We find that the anomalous cooling of nearby neutron stars due to relic neutrino scattering might actually be observable by current and future telescopes operating in the optical to near-infrared frequency band, such as the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), provided there is a large local relic overdensity that is still allowed. Similarly, the anomalous heating of neutron stars due to coherent scattering with keV-scale sterile neutrino dark matter, could also be observed by JWST or future telescopes, which would probe hitherto unexplored parameter space in the sterile neutrino mass-mixing plane.

## Key claim

The paper claims that anomalous cooling of old neutron stars due to coherent scattering with relic neutrinos is too small to be observable, even with large local overdensities; however, anomalous heating of old neutron stars due to coherent scattering with keV-scale sterile neutrino dark matter could be detectable by current and future telescopes like JWST, provided the sterile neutrino mass and mixing angle lie in a specific, currently unexplored region of parameter space.

## Method

The authors model a neutron star as a homogeneous sphere of radius 12.6 km and mass 1.5 solar masses, using the BSk24 equation of state. They calculate the energy transfer from coherent neutrino-neutron scattering inside the neutron star, accounting for gravitational clustering, coherent enhancement from the large number of neutrons within a de Broglie wavelength, neutron degeneracy, Pauli blocking, and the weak potential that reflects antineutrinos. For sterile neutrino dark matter, they compute the heating rate from captured sterile neutrinos that scatter coherently off neutrons via mixing with active neutrinos, then compare the resulting surface temperature to the detection thresholds of JWST, ELT, and TMT.

## Key numbers

* Relic neutrino cross-section: approximately 1.7 × 10⁻⁵⁸ cm² for a neutrino energy of 0.1 eV (Equation 2).
* Coherent enhancement factor N_C: approximately 3 × 10³⁰ for momentum transfer |q| = 0.1 eV and neutron number density 4 × 10³⁸ cm⁻³ (Equation 3).
* Ratio of anomalous cooling rate to black-body photon cooling rate: approximately 6 × 10⁻¹⁰ × (10³ K / T_NS)³ × (0.1 eV / m_i)^{1/2} summed over non-relativistic neutrino mass eigenstates (Equation 4).
* Threshold cross-section for neutrino energy transfer: approximately 1.8 × 10⁻⁴⁵ cm² for a 10 km radius, 1.5 solar mass neutron star (Equation 5).
* Sterile neutrino DM local density: 0.4 GeV/cm³.
* Sterile neutrino relative velocity: 330.5 km/s, derived from a Maxwellian DM velocity distribution with mean 230 km/s and NS velocity 207 km/s.
* A neutron star at 10 pc from Earth with surface temperature 2000 K yields a spectral flux density of about 0.5 nJy at wavelength 2 μm, detectable by JWST.
* Sterile neutrino decay width to three active neutrinos: Γ = (G_F² m_νs⁵ / 96π³) sin²θ, with lifetime equal to the age of the Universe (13.8 Gyr) for m_νs = 50 keV and sin²θ = 1.1 × 10⁻⁷.

## Context

This work extends the old calculation by Dixit (1983) of neutron star cooling via relic neutrino scattering, correcting it by including coherent scattering, gravitational clustering, neutron degeneracy, and Pauli blocking, which yields orders of magnitude improvement but still a subdominant cooling rate. It also extends the extensive literature on dark kinetic heating of neutron stars by GeV-scale dark matter (Kouvaris 2007, Baryakhtar 2017, Bell 2018, and others) to the keV-scale sterile neutrino dark matter scenario for the first time. The projected sensitivities from neutron star heating are comparable to the most stringent astrophysical constraints from X-ray line searches (e.g., from ATHENA and eROSITA) and cosmological bounds from ΔN_eff, BAO, and H_0, and can probe sterile neutrino parameter space not yet excluded by Lyman-alpha or satellite galaxy constraints (DES 2020).
