# Variational Monte Carlo Calculations of n+3H Scattering

> A paramount goal in the field of nuclear physics is to unify ab-initio treatments of bound and unbound states. The position-space quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods have a long history of successful bound state calculations in light systems but so far have seen very little application to unbound systems. Here we introduce a numerical method to improve the efficiency and accuracy of unbound-state calculations in QMC, and as an initial application we compute scattering observables for the neutron-triton system using variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions. The method consists of inferring long-range amplitudes in the wave function from integrals over the short-ranged region where all the particles interact. This approach using integral relations is well established in the literature; here we develop it for the QMC framework. We validate our code with a consistency check between short-range spectroscopic overlap functions computed from direct evaluation and from the integral relations; scattering amplitudes are long-range asymptotics of those overlaps. Comparison against published benchmark calculations using the same potential demonstrates that the integral method, when applied to the current VMC wave functions, produces more accurate scattering observables than direct evaluation from the same variational wave function. However, it still differs noticeably from exact results. We then present phase shifts and mixing parameters for the neutron-triton system using various interactions. Application of the integral method here paves the way for its use in Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations. In GFMC the wave functions are more precise, but high-precision convergence of their tails is slow, and there are additional difficulties in reading out amplitudes. The integral methods will address both of those remaining problems.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an integral method for extracting scattering observables from variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions that is more accurate than direct evaluation from the same variational wave function, but the results still differ noticeably from exact benchmark calculations.

## Method

The method confines the scattering wave function to a spherical box with a logarithmic-derivative boundary condition, then infers long-range scattering amplitudes from integrals over the short-range region where all particles interact, using Green's theorem and Wronskian relations for Coulomb functions. The approach is validated by comparing short-range spectroscopic overlap functions computed from direct evaluation and from the integral relations, and then applied to compute phase shifts and mixing parameters for neutron-triton scattering using several interactions including the Norfolk family of local chiral potentials.

## Key numbers

- Box radius set to 9 fm for the n+3H scattering calculations
- The 4He ground state benchmark from Kamada (2001) found agreement within 1% on binding energy across seven many-body methods using the AV8' potential
- The triton core has mean radius 1.7 fm, smaller than the 9-fm box
- Eight angular-momentum channels are relevant at low energy in the n+3H system
- Ten different energies for a single Hamiltonian require eighty VMC wave functions for full calculation

## Context

This work extends the quantum Monte Carlo approach to unbound nuclear systems, which has previously seen only minimal application to scattering (Nollett 2007, Lynn 2016 for 5He). The integral method is related to the source-term technique of Pinkston (1965) and Kawai (1967), asymptotic normalization coefficient calculations (Mukhamedzhanov 1990, Timofeyuk 1998, Nollett 2011), and scattering calculations of Kievsky (2010) and Viviani (2020). The method paves the way for use in Green's function Monte Carlo calculations, where wave functions are more precise but convergence of their tails is slow and there are additional difficulties in reading out amplitudes.
