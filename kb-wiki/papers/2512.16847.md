# Exploring nuclear modification using one-point energy correlator at the electron-ion collider

> We study the one-point energy correlator (OPEC) at both the back-to-back and collinear limits in electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions. We provide the factorization formalism for the two types of OPEC and present phenomenological predictions in the kinematic region relevant for the future Electron-Ion Collider. Focusing on cold nuclear matter effects in electron-nucleus scattering, we demonstrate that the OPEC serves as a powerful probe of the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) physics and in characterizing the medium-induced transverse momentum broadening in cold nuclear matter.

# Exploring nuclear modification using one-point energy correlator at the electron-ion collider

## Key claim

The one-point energy correlator (OPEC) serves as a powerful probe of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) physics and medium-induced transverse momentum broadening in cold nuclear matter, but only when the observable is evaluated in the back-to-back limit (where the final hadron and incoming beam are almost opposite) or the collinear limit (where energy flow is measured inside a jet), and the nuclear modification factor depends nontrivially on both nuclear TMD parton distribution functions and nuclear TMD fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors derived TMD factorization formulas for the OPEC in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) at the back-to-back limit and for lepton-jet production at the collinear limit, incorporating cold nuclear matter effects by replacing vacuum TMD PDFs and TMD FFs with their nuclear counterparts. They modeled nuclear TMD PDFs using EPPS16 nuclear PDFs with a nuclear-broadened non-perturbative parameter, and nuclear TMD FFs using LIKEn 2021 nuclear FFs with a similar broadening parameter. Phenomenological predictions were generated for electron-proton and electron-gold collisions at EIC kinematics with center-of-mass energy 90 GeV and photon virtuality 20 GeV².

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √s = 90 GeV (highest envisioned for electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC)
- Photon virtuality: Q² = 20 GeV²
- Bjorken x range explored: x > 10⁻² (with full EIC range 0.01 ≤ y ≤ 0.95 giving 2.5×10⁻³ ≲ x ≲ 2.5×10⁻¹)
- Nuclear modification factor Rₑₐ at small τ: approximately 0.33 for e+Au at x = 0.05
- Non-perturbative broadening parameter for nuclear TMD PDFs: a_N = 0.016 GeV² (added to g₁^q = 0.106 GeV²)
- Non-perturbative broadening parameter for nuclear TMD FFs: b_N = 0.0097 GeV² (added to g₁^D = 0.042 GeV²)
- b_max = 1.5 GeV⁻¹ used in the b*-prescription
- Q₀² = 2.4 GeV² for the non-perturbative Sudakov factor
- g₂ = 0.84 for the non-perturbative Sudakov factor

## Context

This work extends the study of OPEC from electron-proton collisions (previously investigated by Li et al. (2021) and Kang et al. (2023)) to electron-nucleus systems, enabling exploration of cold nuclear matter effects at the future Electron-Ion Collider. The factorization formalism builds on established TMD factorization for SIDIS (Collins, 2011) and energy correlator techniques (Moult et al., 2018), while the nuclear modeling follows the approach of Alrashed et al. (2021) for nuclear broadening. The results enable future EIC measurements to disentangle initial-state (nTMD PDF) and final-state (nTMD FF) contributions to nuclear modification through the τ-dependence of the OPEC.
