# The nuclear matter density functional under the nucleonic hypothesis

> A Bayesian analysis of the possible behaviors of the dense matter equation of state informed through recent LIGO-Virgo as well as NICER measurements reveals that all the present observations are compatible with a fully nucleonic hypothesis for the composition of dense matter, even in the core of the most massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620. Under the hypothesis of a nucleonic composition, we extract the most general behavior of the energy per particle of symmetric matter and density dependence of the symmetry energy, compatible with the astrophysical observations as well as our present knowledge of low energy nuclear physics from effective field theory predictions and experimental nuclear mass data. These results can be used as a null hypothesis to be confronted with future constraints on dense matter to search for possible exotic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

All current LIGO-Virgo and NICER observations are compatible with a purely nucleonic composition of dense matter, even in the core of the most massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620, but this conclusion is conditional on the nucleonic hypothesis being used as a null hypothesis to be tested against future data that might reveal exotic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using a semi-agnostic meta-modelling technique that samples 10 independent empirical parameters characterizing the density dependence of the nuclear matter energy up to fourth order at saturation, complemented by 5 surface and curvature parameters optimized to the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016 (AME2016) nuclear mass table. Four posterior distributions were generated by sequentially applying filters: (1) nuclear mass data only (Prior), (2) chiral effective field theory (EFT) constraints at low densities (0.02 to 0.2 fm\(^{-3}\)), (3) high-density constraints from causality, stability, the PSR J0348+0432 mass measurement, and the GW170817 tidal deformability, and (4) all constraints including NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0348+0432 mass: \(M_{J03} = 2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}\) (used as likelihood constraint)
- PSR J0030+0451 mass and radius: \(M_{J00}=1.44^{+0.15}_{-0.14} M_{\odot}\), \(R_{J00}=13.02^{+1.24}_{-1.06}\) km (NICER measurement)
- PSR J0740+6620 mass and radius: \(M_{J07}=2.08 \pm 0.07 M_{\odot}\), \(R_{J07}=13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5}\) km (NICER and XMM-Newton data)
- Chirp mass of GW170817: \(\mathcal{M}_c=1.186\pm0.001 M_{\odot}\) (median with 90% confidence limits)
- Saturation density: \(n_{sat} \simeq 2.8\times10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)
- Prior median energy per particle in symmetric matter at saturation: \(E_{sat} = -16.25^{+0.61}_{-0.46}\) MeV
- Prior median symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{sym} = 32.6^{+3.5}_{-3.9}\) MeV
- Prior median symmetry energy slope: \(L_{sym} = 42^{+24}_{-22}\) MeV
- All-constraints median crust-core transition density: \(n_{CC} = 0.084^{+0.009}_{-0.010}\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- All-constraints median crust thickness for 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) NS: \(1.15^{+0.10}_{-0.08}\) km

## Context

This work extends the meta-modelling approach of Steiner (2013) and Carreau (2019) by including the most recent NICER results for PSR J0740+6620 (Miller et al., 2021) and using a fully unified equation of state approach that incorporates nuclear mass constraints from AME2016. The results provide a quantitative null hypothesis for purely nucleonic dense matter that can be confronted with future observations to search for exotic degrees of freedom such as deconfined quark matter or hyperons, addressing tensions previously noted between gravitational wave data favoring stiffer equations of state and ab-initio nuclear physics calculations pointing to softer density dependence (Guven et al., 2020).
