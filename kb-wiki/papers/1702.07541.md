# New approach to initializing hydrodynamic fields and mini-jet propagation in quark-gluon fluids

> We propose a new approach to initialize the hydrodynamic fields such as energy density distributions and four flow velocity fields in hydrodynamic modeling of high-energy nuclear collisions at the collider energies. Instead of matching the energy-momentum tensor or putting the initial conditions of quark-gluon fluids at a fixed initial time, we utilize a framework of relativistic hydrodynamic equations with source terms to describe the initial stage. Putting the energy and momentum loss rate of the initial partons into the source terms, we obtain hydrodynamic initial conditions dynamically. The resultant initial profile of the quark-gluon fluid looks highly bumpy as seen in the conventional event-by-event initial conditions. In addition, initial random flow velocity fields also are generated as a consequence of momentum deposition from the initial partons. We regard the partons that survive after the dynamical initialization process as the mini-jets and find sizable effects of both mini-jet propagation in the quark-gluon fluids and initial random transverse flow on the final momentum spectra and anisotropic flow observables. We perform event-by-event $(3+1)$-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with this new framework that enables us to describe the hydrodynamic bulk collectivity, parton energy loss, and interplay among them in a unified manner.

## Key claim

The authors propose a dynamical initialization framework for relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, where hydrodynamic fields are generated gradually from parton energy loss rather than being set at a fixed initial time; the key hedge is that this "would not be the actual thermalization process" but serves as a first step toward a unified description of soft and hard components.

## Method

The authors combine the Monte Carlo Glauber model with PYTHIA event generator to produce initial parton distributions, then solve (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations with source terms from parton energy loss (constant 5 GeV/fm from formation time τ₀₀=0.1 fm to hydrodynamic initial time τ₀=0.6 fm). After τ₀, surviving partons are treated as mini-jets propagating through the quark-gluon fluid with a temperature-dependent energy loss rate, while the fluid evolves under the same hydrodynamic equations with source terms. Final particle spectra are computed via the Cooper-Frye formula at a decoupling temperature of 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss rate during dynamical initialization: constant dE/dt = 5 GeV/fm for τ₀₀ < τ < τ₀
- Formation time τ₀₀ = 0.1 fm and hydrodynamic initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm
- Reference temperature for mini-jet energy loss: T₀ = 500 MeV, with reference energy-loss rate dE/dt|₀ = 5 GeV/fm
- Decoupling temperature: T_dec = 160 MeV
- Momentum cutoff parameter dividing soft and hard regions: p_T0 = 2 GeV, with crossover width Δp_T = 1 GeV
- Beam energy: √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Number of events: N_ev = 10² for each centrality class shown
- Impact parameters: b = 0.0 fm (central), b = 9.32 fm (30%-40% centrality), b = 10.58 fm (40%-50% centrality)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Tachibana (2014, 2015, 2017) for jet-medium interactions by incorporating multiple mini-jets and dynamical initialization of hydrodynamic fields, going beyond the optical Glauber model used by Schulc (2014) which treated mini-jets on a smooth background. The framework enables simultaneous treatment of bulk collectivity and jet energy loss, and the authors find that initial random transverse flow generated during dynamical initialization has a more significant effect on final p_T spectra than mini-jet propagation itself, while elliptic flow v₂ remains dominated by the shape of the participant region.
