# Neutrino-nucleus CCQE-like scattering

> RPA correlations, spectral function and 2p2h (multi-nucleon) effects on charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions without emitted pions are discussed. We pay attention to the influence of RPA and multi-nucleon mechanisms on the MiniBooNE and MINERvA flux folded differential cross sections, the MiniBooNE flux unfolded total cross section and the neutrino energy reconstruction.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE CCQE-like puzzle can be resolved by including both RPA correlations and multinucleon (2p2h) mechanisms, which together allow a description of the data using a standard nucleon axial mass around 1 GeV, rather than the anomalously large value of 1.35 GeV needed in the original analysis; however, the two main theoretical approaches (IFIC and Lyon) differ by about a factor of two in their estimation of the size of the multinucleon effects.

## Method

The authors compute charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for carbon targets using a microscopic many-body model that includes: (i) a relativistic Fermi gas description of the nucleus with RPA (random phase approximation) correlations to account for medium polarization effects, and (ii) 2p2h contributions evaluated from a full elementary model of the WN → πN reaction, including the Δ(1232) resonance and background terms required by chiral symmetry. The model is used to calculate flux-folded double differential cross sections for MiniBooNE and MINERvA, and to study the impact of 2p2h events on neutrino energy reconstruction from the measured muon kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE original analysis required a nucleon axial mass of M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV to describe their CCQE-like data using a relativistic global Fermi gas model.
- The IFIC model fit to the full MiniBooNE double differential data set yields M_A = 1.077 ± 0.027 GeV, or M_A = 1.007 ± 0.034 GeV when a momentum transfer threshold q_cut = 400 MeV is applied (removing 14 of 137 bins).
- The IFIC fit obtains a global normalization scale λ ≈ 0.9, consistent with the MiniBooNE quoted total normalization uncertainty of 10.7%.
- The deuterium and hydrogen CCQE measurements give M_A = 1.016 ± 0.026 GeV, in agreement with the pion electro-production result M_A = 1.014 ± 0.016 GeV.
- The NOMAD experiment reported M_A = 1.05 ± 0.02 (stat) ± 0.06 (syst) GeV from QE-like interactions on carbon with average neutrino energy 25.9 GeV.
- For neutrino energies up to 10 GeV, the 2p2h contribution saturates to approximately 30% of the QE cross section.

## Context

This work extends the debate on the MiniBooNE CCQE puzzle, first highlighted by Aguilar-Arevalo (2010), where the measured cross section exceeded predictions from the relativistic Fermi gas model with standard axial mass. The paper builds on prior work by Martini (2009, 2010) and Amaro (2010) that first explored 2p2h effects, and by Nieves (2011) who developed the microscopic model used here. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments, showing that the standard QE-based reconstruction formula becomes unreliable for 2p2h events, producing a systematic distortion of the extracted cross section shape that increases uncertainty on oscillation signals.
