# High-momentum components in the $^4$He nucleus caused by inter-nucleon correlations

> High-momentum components of nuclei are essential for understanding the underlying inter-nucleon correlations in nuclei. We perform the comprehensive analysis for the origin of the high-momentum components of $^4$He in the framework of Tensor-optimized High-momentum Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (TO-HMAMD), which is a completely variational approach as an $ab$ $initio$ theory starting from the bare nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction. The analytical derivations are provided for the nucleon momentum distribution of the Antisymmetrized Momentum Dynamics (AMD) wave functions, with subtraction of center-of-mass motion. The nucleon momentum distribution for $^4$He is calculated by applying a new expansion technique to our $ab$ $initio$ wave function, and agrees with the values extracted from experimental data up to the high-momentum region. Fine-grained analysis is performed for the high-momentum components in $^4$He with respect to different nucleon correlations. Contributions from tensor, central with short-range, and many-body correlations are extracted from the nucleon momentum distributions. The manifestation of tensor correlation around 2 fm$^{-1}$ region is explicitly confirmed by comparing the momentum distributions predicted using different types of $NN$ interactions with and without the tensor force.

## Key claim

The high-momentum components in the $^4$He nucleus arise primarily from tensor correlations around 2 fm$^{-1}$ and short-range central correlations around 4 fm$^{-1}$, with the tensor correlation being essential to reproduce the smooth momentum distribution extracted from experimental data; the many-body correlation contribution is comparably small for momenta below 4 fm$^{-1}$.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive analysis of the $^4$He nucleon momentum distribution using the Tensor-optimized High-momentum Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (TO-HMAMD) framework, a fully variational ab initio approach starting from the bare AV8$^\prime$ nucleon-nucleon interaction. They derived an analytical formulation for the nucleon momentum distribution of (HM-)AMD wave functions with complete subtraction of center-of-mass motion, and developed a new projection method to expand the TO-HMAMD wave function using HM-AMD basis states. The total wave function was decomposed into orthogonal components (AMD, central, tensor, and many-body channels) via a Gram-Schmidt process to isolate the contributions of each correlation type to the momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of $^4$He obtained with TO-HMAMD using AV8$^\prime$ interaction: -24.74 MeV; radius: 1.51 fm
- Overlap between the TO-HMAMD wave function and its expansion using Single HMAMD bases: 97.5%
- Overlap between the TOAMD wave function and its expansion using HM-AMD bases with shifts in both $z$- and $x$-directions: 99.8%
- Correlated terms contribute about 23% of the total wave function, consistent with the 20% estimate from Hen (2017)
- Tensor correlation term contributes about 12% of the total wave function; central correlation contributes about half that amount
- Many-body correlation contributes about 4% of the total wave function
- Paired imaginary shifts $D_z = \pm 5$ fm produce additional momentum peaks at $k_z = \pm 2.0$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the TOAMD and HM-AMD methods previously developed by Myo (2015, 2017a-d) and Lyu (2018b) for $s$-shell nuclei, providing a fine-grained decomposition of the momentum distribution that goes beyond earlier analyses. Prior studies by Morita (1988) using the Reid $NN$ force discussed $S$- and $D$-wave contributions but did not reproduce the $^4$He binding energy, while Wiringa (2014) compared central and central+tensor channels using Variational Monte Carlo. The present work enables future theoretical and experimental studies of high-momentum components in heavier nuclei by clarifying the distinct roles of tensor, short-range central, and many-body correlations in the momentum distribution.
