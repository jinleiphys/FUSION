# Electromagnetic transitions in the algebraic cluster model

> We study electromagnetic transition rates in the framework of the algebraic cluster model. The concept of shape-phase transitions is used to propose a mechanism that allows to have interband and intraband quadrupole transitions of comparable strength, as observed in 12C.

# Electromagnetic transitions in the algebraic cluster model

## Key claim

By mixing spherical and deformed phases in a simplified two-body algebraic cluster model with a control parameter near the critical value (χ ~ 0.6, just above χ_c = 1/2), interband and intraband quadrupole transitions can become comparable in strength while the energy spectrum remains approximately rotational, offering a mechanism to explain the observed electromagnetic decay of the Hoyle state in ¹²C, which the pure deformed (triangular) ACM cannot reproduce.

## Method

The authors study the transitional region between the U(3) (harmonic oscillator) and SO(4) (deformed oscillator) dynamical symmetries of the two-body algebraic cluster model (vibron model) using a schematic Hamiltonian that interpolates between the two limits via a control parameter χ. They derive closed analytic expressions for B(E2) ratios in both limits and numerically evaluate these ratios as functions of χ for boson numbers N = 6, 10, and 20, comparing to the experimental ratio B(E2;0₂⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 1.72 ± 0.25 measured in ¹²C.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ratio B(E2;0₂⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) in ¹²C: 1.72 ± 0.25 (from Ajzenberg compilation)
- Pure U(3) limit (leading order in N): B(E2;0₂⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 40/3
- Pure SO(4) limit: B(E2;0₂⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 0 (interband transition forbidden)
- For N = 10 bosons and χ = 0.59: energy ratio R_E = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 3.00 and B(E2) ratio R_B = 1.74
- Experimental energy ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) in ¹²C: 3.17
- Critical point of the shape-phase transition: χ_c = 1/2
- U(3) limit energy ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 2; SO(4) limit energy ratio = 10/3

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy in the algebraic cluster model (ACM) for ¹²C, introduced by Bijker (2000), where the Hoyle state is interpreted as a breathing vibration of an equilateral triangular α-particle configuration. In the pure deformed (triangular) phase, the interband quadrupole transition from the Hoyle state (0₂⁺) to the 2₁⁺ state is suppressed (B(E2) ratio ~0.15 in the three-body ACM), whereas experiments show it is comparable to intraband transitions. The authors extend earlier work on shape-phase transitions in algebraic models (Iachello, 1980s) to propose mixing spherical and deformed phases as a solution. This mechanism, demonstrated here for two-body clusters, is intended to be applied to the full three-body ACM for ¹²C in future work, potentially resolving the Hoyle state electromagnetic decay puzzle and enabling more accurate predictions for cluster states in light nuclei.
