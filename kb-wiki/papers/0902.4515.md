# Holographic Baryons

> We review baryons in the D4-D8 holographic model of low energy QCD, with the large $N_c$ and the large 't Hooft coupling limit. The baryon is identified with a bulk soliton of a unit Pontryagin number, which from the four-dimensional viewpoint translates to a heavily modified Skyrmion dressed by condensates of spin one mesons. We explore classical properties and find that the baryon in the holographic limit is amenable to an effective field theory description. We also present a simple method to capture all leading and subleading interactions in the $1/N_c$ and the derivative expansions. An infinitely predictive model of baryon-meson interactions is thus derived, although one may trust results only for low energy processes, given various approximations in the bulk. We showcase a few comparisons to experiments, such the leading axial couplings to pions, the leading vector-like coupling, and a qualitative prediction of the electromagnetic vector dominance that involves the entire tower of vector mesons. (This article is an expanded version of a proceeding contribution to "30 years of mathematical method in high energy physics," Kyoto 2008)

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryons in the D4-D8 holographic model are not simply Skyrmions made of pions, but rather five-dimensional instanton solitons that incorporate infinite towers of vector and axial-vector mesons, with the caveat that results are only trustworthy for low energy processes given the large 't Hooft coupling and large N_c approximations used in the bulk.

## Method

The authors construct baryons as instanton solitons with unit Pontryagin number in a five-dimensional U(N_F) gauge theory derived from D8 probe branes in the D4-D8 holographic background. They compute the classical energy of the soliton by balancing the Pontryagin energy (which favors shrinking) against the Coulombic energy from the Chern-Simons term (which favors expansion), then quantize the collective coordinates on the S^3 moduli space to obtain quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- Baryon size: ρ_baryon ≈ (2·3^7·π^2/5)^{1/4} / (M_KK √λ), which is ~ 1/(M_KK √λ) and much smaller than the Skyrmion size ~ 1/M_KK
- Classical baryon mass: m_B^classical = (λ N_c / 27π) M_KK × (1 + √(2·3^5·π^2/5)/λ + ...)
- Pion decay constant from the model: f_π^2 = (g_YM^2 N_c) N_c M_KK^2 / 54π^4
- Skyrme coupling: 1/e^2_Skyrme ≈ 61 (g_YM^2 N_c) N_c / 54π^7
- Fitted parameters: λ = g_YM^2 N_c ≈ 17, M_KK ≈ 0.94 GeV to match f_π and the first vector meson mass

## Context

This work extends the D4-D8 holographic QCD model developed by Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) and builds on earlier studies of baryons as solitonic objects by Hong, Hata, and collaborators (2007). It contrasts with the conventional Skyrmion picture of baryons from chiral perturbation theory (Skyrme 1961, Witten 1979), arguing that the Skyrmion truncation to pions alone is inadequate because baryons excite vector and axial-vector mesons. The holographic approach enables computation of all leading and subleading baryon-meson interactions in the 1/N_c and derivative expansions, with predictions for axial couplings to pions, vector-like couplings, and electromagnetic vector dominance involving the entire tower of vector mesons.
