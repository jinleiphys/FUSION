# Polyakov loop extended NJL model with imaginary chemical potential

> The Polyakov loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with imaginary chemical potential is studied. The model possesses the extended ${\mathbb Z}_{3}$ symmetry that QCD does. Quantities invariant under the extended ${\mathbb Z}_{3}$ symmetry, such as the partition function, the chiral condensate and the modified Polyakov loop, have the Roberge-Weiss (RW) periodicity. The phase diagram of confinement/deconfinement transition derived with the PNJL model is consistent with the RW prediction on it and the results of lattice QCD. The phase diagram of chiral transition is also presented by the PNJL model.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with imaginary chemical potential reproduces the Roberge-Weiss (RW) periodicity and phase structure of QCD, but only in the chiral limit (m0 = 0) where lattice QCD simulations are not available; the model predicts that the deconfinement transition at θ = (2k+1)π/3 is first order in the imaginary part of the modified Polyakov loop, while the chiral transition there is second order.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor PNJL model under the mean field approximation, introducing an imaginary chemical potential μ = iθ/β. They solve the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate σ, the Polyakov loop Φ, and its conjugate Φ* to obtain the thermodynamic potential Ω(θ). The Polyakov potential U is taken from Ratti et al. (2006), fitted to pure gauge lattice QCD results, with T0 = 270 MeV. A three-dimensional momentum cutoff Λ = 0.6315 GeV and coupling Gs = 5.498 GeV^{-2} are used in the NJL sector.

## Key numbers

- At θ = 0, the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is about 261 MeV, and that of the deconfinement transition is about 240 MeV (difference of about 20 MeV).
- Point E (endpoint of both transitions) is at T ≈ 269 MeV.
- Point F (bifurcation of the chiral transition line) is at T ≈ 328 MeV.
- Point D (crossover deconfinement transition) is at T ≈ 240 MeV.
- Point C (chiral transition at θ = 0) is at T ≈ 261 MeV.
- The Polyakov potential parameters are: a0 = 6.75, a1 = -1.95, a2 = 2.625, a3 = -7.44, b3 = 0.75, b4 = 7.5 (all dimensionless).

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model of Meisinger (2001), Fukushima (2004), and Ratti et al. (2006) to the case of imaginary chemical potential, directly testing the Roberge-Weiss (1983) prediction that the partition function has periodicity 2π/3 in θ. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002) and D'Elia and Lombardo (2003) for the deconfinement transition, but provide new predictions for the chiral transition in the chiral limit. The model enables future quantitative comparison with lattice data and suggests that adding scalar-type eight-quark interactions (as in Kashiwa et al., 2007) can reduce the gap between chiral and deconfinement critical temperatures.
