# Cluster Structures of the Ground and Excited States of 12Be Studied with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> The structures of the ground and excited states of 12Be were studied with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The ground state was found to be a state with a developed 2-alpha core with two neutrons occupying the intruder orbits. The energy levels of the newly measured spin-assigned states were described well, except for the $1^-_1$ state. The calculations indicated that many exotic cluster structures appear in the low-energy region. The widths concerning alpha and 6He decays were discussed by using reduced width amplitudes.

## Key claim

The ground state of 12Be is not a normal neutron-shell-closed state but a prolately deformed intruder state with a developed 2-alpha core and two neutrons occupying intruder orbits, and many exotic cluster structures appear in the low-energy region; however, the theoretical excitation energy of the 1−1 state is overestimated compared to the measured 1− state at 2.68 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations after spin-parity projection (VAP) within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework, using Gaussian wave packets for single-particle wave functions and the frictional cooling method for energy minimization. They obtained intrinsic states for various Jπ states by VAP, then superposed the spin-parity projected wave functions from all obtained intrinsic states and diagonalized the Hamiltonian and norm matrices to determine the final wave functions. The effective interactions used were the MV1 central force (case 3) with a zero-range three-body force and the G3RS spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical binding energy of 12Be: 61.9 MeV after diagonalization (experimental value 68.65 MeV)
- Calculated excitation energy of 0+2 state before diagonalization: 2.8 MeV (with m=0.65) and 3.0 MeV (with m=0.62)
- Theoretical matter radius of 0+1 state: 2.85 fm; experimental matter radius: 2.59 ± 0.06 fm
- Theoretical proton radius of 0+1 state: 2.67 fm; neutron radius: 2.94 fm; neutron-proton radius difference Δr ≈ 0.3 fm
- B(GT) for 12Be(0+1) → 12B(1+1): 0.9 after diagonalization (experimental B(GT) = 0.56)
- B(GT) for 12Be(0+2) → 12B(1+1): 2.1 after diagonalization
- B(E2;2+1 → 0+1): 14 e²fm⁴
- B(E2;2+2 → 0+2): 8 e²fm⁴
- B(E1;0+1 → 1−1): 2 × 10⁻² e²fm² (experimental B(E1) = 0.05 e²fm²)
- B(E0;0+2 → 0+1): 1.7 e fm²

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic AMD studies of Be isotopes by Kanada-En'yo and collaborators (2001-2002) and molecular-orbital model calculations by Itagaki et al. (2000-2001), which suggested cluster structures in 10Be and 11Be. The calculations contradict the expectation of a closed neutron p-shell for the 12Be ground state, supporting the vanishing of the N=8 magic number previously discussed by Barker (1966), Fortune (1978), and Tsuzuki (1999). The results enable systematic classification of rotational bands in 12Be and provide predictions for decay widths and transition strengths that can be tested by future experiments on cluster states, particularly the 6He+6He and 8He+4He breakup reactions studied by Freer (2001) and Saito (2002).
