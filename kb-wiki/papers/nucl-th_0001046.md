# Three-cluster nuclear molecules

> A three-center phenomenological model able to explain, at least from a qualitative point of view, the difference in the observed yield of a particle-accompanied fission and that of binary fission was developed. It is derived from the liquid drop model under the assumption that the aligned configuration, with the emitted particle between the light and heavy fragment is obtained by increasing continuously the separation distance, while the radii of the light fragment and of the light particle are kept constant. During the first stage of the deformation one has a two-center evolution until the neck radius becomes equal to the radius of the emitted particle. Then the three center starts developing by decreasing with the same amount the two tip distances. In such a way a second minimum, typical for a cluster molecule, appears in the deformation energy. Examples are presented for $^{240}$Pu parent nucleus emitting $α$-particles and $^{14}$C in a ternary process.

# Three-cluster nuclear molecules

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-center phenomenological model, derived from the liquid drop model, that can explain at least qualitatively the difference in observed yield between particle-accompanied (ternary) fission and binary fission. The model produces a second minimum in the deformation energy characteristic of a nuclear molecule, but the authors hedge that the explanation is qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method

The authors developed a shape parametrization based on two intersected spheres that evolves into three intersected spheres as the separation distance increases, with the radii of the light fragment and the emitted particle kept constant. The deformation energy was calculated using the liquid drop model with surface and Coulomb energy terms, plus a phenomenological shell correction that reproduces the experimental Q-value at the initial configuration. Numerical integration was performed for both surface and Coulomb energies, with the elliptic integrals computed using Chebyshev polynomial approximation.

## Key numbers

- Radii of fragments are given by \( R_j = 1.2249 \, A_j^{1/3} \) fm for \( j = 0, 1, 2, 3 \)
- Liquid drop model constants: \( a_s = 17.9439 \) MeV, \( \kappa = 1.7826 \), \( a_c = 3e^2/(5r_0) \), \( e^2 = 1.44 \) MeV·fm, \( r_0 = 1.2249 \) fm
- Examples are presented for \( ^{240} \)Pu parent nucleus emitting α-particles and \( ^{14} \)C in ternary fission, with the heavy fragment being the doubly magic \( ^{132}_{50} \)Sn\( _{82} \)
- For α-accompanied fission of \( ^{240} \)Pu: light fragments are \( ^{104} \)Mo (ternary) and \( ^{108} \)Ru (binary)
- For \( ^{14} \)C-accompanied fission of \( ^{240} \)Pu: light fragments are \( ^{94} \)Sr (ternary) and \( ^{108} \)Ru (binary)

## Context

This work extends the fission approach to cluster radioactivity and alpha decay developed over the previous two decades by Poenaru and collaborators, as an alternative to many-body theory. The model builds on earlier work on cold binary fission by Signer (1981), Pashkevich (1989), and Gonnenwein (1991), and on the discovery of multicluster accompanied fission recently envisaged by Poenaru and Greiner (1999). The results enable further theoretical study of ternary and multicluster fission processes, including the possibility of four-, five-, and six-fragment decays of heavy and superheavy nuclei such as \( ^{252} \)Cf and \( ^{262} \)Rf.
