# Polarization observables in electronuclear two-nucleon knockout

> Differential (e,e'pp) measurements are presently recognized as a way of studying short-range correlations in finite nuclei. The ($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p$) and ($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}n$) differential cross section and polarization observables are studied in a microscopic model that accounts for the short-range correlations, outgoing-nucleon distortions, meson-exchange and $Δ$-isobar currents. It is pointed out that polarization observables represent an attractive alternative for absolute electronuclear two-nucleon knockout measurements. In the polarization transfer $P'_t$ for ($\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p$), the effect of central short-range correlations is predicted to be large while at the same time the final-state interaction effects are small.

## Key claim

Polarization observables, particularly the polarization transfer \(P'_t\) in \((\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p)\), are predicted to show large sensitivity to central short-range correlations while remaining almost insensitive to final-state interaction effects, making them an attractive alternative to absolute cross section measurements for studying two-nucleon correlations in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic distorted-wave calculations within a shell-model framework for \(^{16}\text{O}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p)\) and \(^{16}\text{O}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}n)\) reactions leading to discrete states in \(^{14}\text{C}\) and \(^{14}\text{N}\). The model includes central Jastrow-type short-range correlations, meson-exchange currents, \(\Delta\)-isobar currents, and outgoing-nucleon distortions via partial-wave expansions of continuum mean-field eigenfunctions. Calculations were carried out in super-parallel kinematics (both nucleons detected along the three-momentum transfer \(\vec{q}\)) and in quasi-deuteron kinematics (\(\vec{P}=0\)), with the \(\Delta\)-propagator including an extra 40 MeV complex width to account for medium modifications.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{16}\text{O}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p)\) reaction to the \(0^+\) ground state of \(^{14}\text{C}\), the two-hole wave function is \(0.77\,|(1p_{1/2})^{-2};0^+\rangle + 0.18\,|(1p_{3/2})^{-2};0^+\rangle\).
- For the \(1^+\) state at \(E_x = 11.3\) MeV in \(^{14}\text{C}\), the wave function is \(0.77\,|(1p_{1/2})^{-1}(1p_{3/2})^{-1};1^+\rangle\).
- The \(\Delta\)-propagator includes an extra 40 MeV complex width beyond the standard \(\pi N\) decay width.
- In quasi-deuteron kinematics for \(^{12}\text{C}\), the missing-energy range for full p-shell integration is approximately \(28 \leq E_{2m} \leq 40\) MeV.
- For \(^{12}\text{C}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) at MIT-Bates, a non-zero induced polarization \(0 \leq P_n \leq 0.5\) was observed for missing energies above 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations in finite nuclei beyond inclusive (e,e') and semi-inclusive (e,e'p) reactions (Benhar 1997, Benhar 1994, Ciofi degli Atti 1994) by focusing on the triple-coincidence (e,e'pp) reaction, which Laget and others identified as a direct probe of two-nucleon correlations. The authors build on the model of Ryckebusch (1997) and the suggestion by Giusti (1998) that final-state structure can filter reaction mechanisms. The results enable future experimental investigations at TJNAF and MAMI to use polarization observables rather than absolute cross sections for isolating short-range correlation effects, and support the use of (e,e'pn) reactions to study tensor correlations via "quasi-deuteron" pairs.
