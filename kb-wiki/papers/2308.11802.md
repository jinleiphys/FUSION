# Effects of Quasiparticle-Vibration Coupling on Gamow-Teller Strength and $β$ Decay with the Skyrme Proton-Neutron Finite-Amplitude Method

> We adapt the proton-neutron finite-amplitude method, which in its original form is an efficient implementation of the Skyrme quasiparticle random phase approximation, to include the coupling of quasiparticles to like-particle phonons. The approach allows us to add beyond-QRPA correlations to computations of Gamow-Teller strength and $β$-decay rates in deformed nuclei for the first time. We test the approach in several deformed isotopes for which measured strength distributions are available. The additional correlations dramatically improve agreement with the data, and will lead to improved global $β$-decay rates.

## Key claim

The authors adapt the proton-neutron finite-amplitude method (pnFAM) to include quasiparticle-vibration coupling, enabling beyond-QRPA correlations in deformed nuclei for the first time; the improvement in Gamow-Teller strength distributions is dramatic, but the method is not yet efficient enough for global calculations of beta-decay rates in all neutron-rich isotopes without further automation and refitting of Skyrme functional parameters.

## Method

The authors extend the pnFAM equations by adding a spreading matrix W(ω) that incorporates four types of quasiparticle-phonon coupling diagrams: phonon-loop corrections to single-quasiparticle energies and phonon-exchange interactions between quasiparticles. They first solve the HFB equations with HFBTHO, then run the like-particle FAM to generate phonons excited by natural-parity multipole operators with L ≤ 6, identify peaks in the strength functions, extract quasiparticle-phonon vertices via contour integration, and finally solve the modified pnFAM* equations iteratively to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions. The subtraction procedure W̃(ω) = W(ω) − W(0) ensures the static response matches the unmodified pnFAM.

## Context

This work extends the pnFAM approach used in global QRPA calculations by Mustonen (2016) and Ney (2020) to include quasiparticle-vibration coupling, which was previously applied only in spherical nuclei by Niu (2016, 2018) using the time-blocking approximation. The method enables beyond-QRPA calculations in deformed nuclei for the first time, dramatically improving agreement with experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions from charge-exchange reactions. The approach will enable improved global beta-decay rate predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations once the Skyrme functional parameters are refit and the phonon extraction procedure is automated, following the roadmap outlined in the discussion section.
