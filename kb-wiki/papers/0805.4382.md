# Kaonic Nuclear Systems KbarN and KbarNN as Decaying States

> The formation spectra of model KbarN and KbarNN systems formed by (K-, n) reactions are investigated in order to obtain a theoretical basis for a proper interpretation of experimental data concerning kaonic nuclear quasi-bound states. It has been clarified that the experimentally observable kaonic nuclear state K-pp should be regarded as the decaying state introduced by Kapur-Peierls, which is different from the pole state solution of the Faddeev equation.

## Key claim

The experimentally observable kaonic nuclear state \(K^-pp\) should be regarded as a "decaying state" in the Kapur-Peierls sense, not as the pole-state solution of the Faddeev equation; the pole-state solution yields unphysical behaviors (e.g., a width that continues to grow below the \(\pi\Sigma\) threshold) and does not directly correspond to the missing-mass spectrum measured in experiment.

## Method

The authors constructed a solvable two-channel model (\(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\)) with separable Yukawa-type potentials, reproducing the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance (binding energy 27 MeV, width 40 MeV). They increased the attractive strength of the \(\bar{K}N\) channel interaction by a factor \(f\) and compared the pole-state solution (satisfying \(z - \Xi(z)=0\)) with the "intrinsic decaying state" (satisfying \(z - \operatorname{Re}\Xi(z)=0\)), the latter imposing on-shell conditions on the outgoing \(\pi\) and \(\Sigma\) particles. For the \(K^-pp\) system, they used the ATMS three-body method with Gaussian-simulated optical potentials derived from the two-channel model, adjusting the real part to reproduce a 75 MeV binding energy.

## Key numbers

* \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance: binding energy \(B_{\bar{K}} = 27\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 40\) MeV, reproduced with strength parameters \(s_{11}^{(0)} = -1.288\), \(s_{12} = 0.2783\), \(s_{22} = -0.660\) and \(\Lambda = 770\) MeV/\(\hbar c = 3.90\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For the two-body model at \(f=1.16\): pole state gives \(\operatorname{Re} E_{\rm pol} = -70\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\rm pol} = 74\) MeV; intrinsic decaying state gives \(\operatorname{Re} E_{\rm dec}^{\rm int} = -75\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\rm dec}^{\rm int} = 45\) MeV.
* For the two-body model at \(f=1.206\): pole state gives \(\operatorname{Re} E_{\rm pol} = -83\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\rm pol} = 97\) MeV; intrinsic decaying state gives \(\operatorname{Re} E_{\rm dec}^{\rm int} = -101\) MeV, \(\Gamma_{\rm dec}^{\rm int} = 18\) MeV.
* For \(K^-pp\) with 75 MeV binding: pole-state width = 100 MeV (setting value); intrinsic decaying state width = 54 MeV, obtained with Gaussian optical potentials \(v_1^{\rm opt} = (-669 - i120) \exp(-(r/0.66)^2)\) MeV (pole) and \(v_1^{\rm opt} = (-659 - i60) \exp(-(r/0.66)^2)\) MeV (decaying), range 0.66 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior predictions of deeply bound kaonic states (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, Dote 2004) based on an energy-independent complex \(\bar{K}N\) interaction, which had been criticized by Shevchenko, Gal, Mares and Revai (2007) for yielding a smaller width than their coupled-channel Faddeev calculations. The paper clarifies that the Faddeev pole-state solution does not correspond to the experimentally observable missing-mass spectrum, whereas the Kapur-Peierls decaying-state formalism does. It enables a proper theoretical framework for interpreting upcoming experiments at DA\(\Phi\)NE, GSI and J-PARC, and proposes the "intrinsic decaying state" as a practical tool for extracting lifetimes from measured spectra.
