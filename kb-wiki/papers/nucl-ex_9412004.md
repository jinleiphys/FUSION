# Relative spins and excitation energies of superdeformed bands in 190Hg: Further evidence for octupole vibration

> An experiment using the Eurogam Phase II gamma-ray spectrometer confirms the existence of an excited superdeformed (SD) band in 190Hg and its very unusual decay into the lowest SD band over 3-4 transitions. The energies and dipole character of the transitions linking the two SD bands have been firmly established. Comparisons with RPA calculations indicate that the excited SD band can be interpreted as an octupole-vibrational structure.

## Key claim

The excited superdeformed (SD) band in 190Hg decays entirely into the lowest SD band via three to four transitions, and the dipole character of these linking transitions has been firmly established, providing further evidence that the excited band is an octupole-vibrational structure rather than a two-quasiparticle band.

## Method

The experiment used the Eurogam Phase II gamma-ray spectrometer with 54 escape-suppressed Ge detectors, including 30 large-volume detectors at forward/backward angles and 24 "clover" detectors at side angles, to measure gamma-ray coincidences from the reaction 160Gd(34S,4n) at a beam energy of approximately 158 MeV. Directional Correlation from Oriented nuclei (DCO) ratios were extracted for the interband transitions by comparing counts in clover detectors versus large-volume detectors, calibrated against known E1 and E2 transitions. Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations based on the cranked Nilsson model were performed for comparison with the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The interband transitions linking the two SD bands have energies of 812, 864, and 911 keV, with a tentative fourth transition at 757 keV
- The excited SD band has an average dynamic moment of inertia of 123 hbar^2 MeV^{-1}, unusually large for an SD band in an even-even A~190 nucleus
- The 864 keV transition has a branching ratio of 0.35(4), partial half-life of 260(60) fs, and B(E1) of 1.2(3) x 10^{-3} Weisskopf units
- The 812 keV transition has a branching ratio of 0.29(4), partial half-life of 260(70) fs, and B(E1) of 1.5(4) x 10^{-3} Weisskopf units
- The 911 keV transition has a branching ratio greater than 0.5, partial half-life less than 200 fs, and B(E1) greater than 1.4 x 10^{-3} Weisskopf units
- RPA calculations predict the lowest octupole-vibrational state at 0.37 MeV above the lowest SD band at a rotational frequency of 0.4 MeV
- The gamma-ray intensities of the excited SD band ranged from 0.02 to 0.06 percent with respect to population of the ground state

## Context

This work confirms and extends the tentative observation of an excited SD band in 190Hg reported by Crowell et al. (1994), and is one of only a few cases in even-even nuclei where interband transitions between two SD bands have been proposed, alongside 150Gd and 152Dy reported by Fallon et al. (1994) and Dagnall et al. (1994). The results enable stringent tests of mean-field calculations for superdeformed nuclei, as the measured excitation energies, relative spins, and transition strengths provide more detailed constraints than the dynamic moments of inertia typically used for comparison.
