# Dynamics of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies

> Within the framework of the improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, the dynamics of strangeness (K$^{0,+}$, $Λ$ and $Σ^{-,0,+}$) production in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies is investigated systematically, in which the strange particles are considered to be mainly produced by the inelastic collisions of baryon-baryon and pion-baryon. The collisions in the region of supra-saturation densities of the dense baryonic matter formed in heavy-ion collisions dominate the yields of strangeness production. The total multiplicities as functions of incident energies and collision centralities are calculated with the Skyrme parameter SLy6. The excitation function of strangeness production is analyzed and also compared with the KaoS data for the K$^{+}$ production in the reactions $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au.

# Dynamics of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies

## Key claim
The improved isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, using the Skyrme parameter SLy6, reproduces the KaoS experimental data for K⁺ production in ¹²C+¹²C and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au reactions at near-threshold energies, with the caveat that calculations deviate from data at very low threshold energies due to limited statistics.

## Method
The authors extended the ImIQMD model to include strangeness production via inelastic baryon-baryon and pion-baryon collisions, incorporating channels such as BB → BYK, Bπ → YK, and associated antikaon and hyperon reactions. They simulated ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au and ¹²C+¹²C collisions at incident energies around 1A GeV, using the Skyrme force SLy6 (incompressibility ~230 MeV) and parameterized cross sections from prior work (Ts99, Ts94, Cu84). The time evolution of central density, pion, kaon, and hyperon yields was tracked, and total multiplicities were computed as functions of impact parameter and incident energy.

## Key numbers
- The Skyrme force SLy6 gives a modulus of incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter of about 230 MeV.
- In central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 1.5A GeV, pion yields saturate at approximately 30 fm/c, and kaons are produced at the early stage around 25 fm/c.
- The Bπ → YK channel contributes about 1/3 of the total kaon production.
- The calculated excitation functions for K⁺ production in ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au and ¹²C+¹²C were compared with KaoS data (Fo07) and reproduced well, except at very low threshold energies.

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies of strangeness as a probe of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) at supra-saturation densities, following earlier investigations by Ai85, Li97, Fu01, and Ha06. The K⁺ yield sensitivity to the EoS had previously favored a soft EoS at high densities, and the K⁰/K⁺ ratio was proposed by Fe06 and Pr10 as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy. The ImIQMD model had been previously applied to fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier (Fe05, Fe08) and to pion dynamics (Fe09, Fe10, Fg10); this work adds strangeness channels to enable comparisons with KaoS data and to constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, with implications for neutron star structure and supernova nucleosynthesis (St05).
