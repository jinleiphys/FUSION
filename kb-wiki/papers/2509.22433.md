# Cumulative proton production in $pd$ collisions

> The model of backward proton production in the exclusive reaction $pd \to ppn$ at proton beam momenta up to several GeV/c is constructed on the basis of Feynman diagrams for one- and two-step amplitudes. The latter include nucleon and $Δ(1232)$ resonance intermediate scattering states with propagators reduced to eikonal form using the generalized eikonal approximation. The model calculations are compared with available experimental data on the energy spectra of protons at backward polar angles in the deuteron rest frame. It is shown that inclusion of two-step amplitudes significantly increases the production of backward protons with energies above $\sim$ 50 MeV, thereby significantly improving the agreement with experiment. The remaining theoretical problem related to the description of the off-shell behavior of the elementary amplitudes of the $NN \to NN$ and $NN \leftrightarrow NΔ$ transitions is discussed. Partial discrepancies between different sets of experimental data do not allow for conclusion that exotic effects, such as proton interactions with density fluctuations and/or $6q$ clusters in the deuteron, are present.

## Key claim

The model shows that including two-step amplitudes with intermediate nucleon and Δ(1232) resonance states significantly increases backward proton production with energies above ~50 MeV in the exclusive reaction pd → ppn, improving agreement with experiment, but partial discrepancies between different experimental datasets prevent concluding that exotic effects like density fluctuations or 6q clusters in the deuteron are present.

## Method

The author constructed Feynman diagram amplitudes for one-step (impulse approximation) and two-step processes in the pd → ppn reaction, with two-step amplitudes including both nucleon and Δ(1232) resonance intermediate scattering states. Propagators of intermediate scattering states were reduced to eikonal form using the generalized eikonal approximation, with the integration over the longitudinal component of the intermediate spectator momentum performed analytically using the deuteron wave function from the Paris potential model. The NN → NΔ amplitudes were calculated using a pion exchange model, and the antisymmetry of the full amplitude with respect to interchange of outgoing nucleon quantum numbers and the isoscalar character of the deuteron wave function were explicitly taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Backward proton production increases significantly for energies above ~50 MeV when two-step amplitudes are included
- The Δ(1232) resonance mass is taken as mΔ = 1.232 GeV
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant fπNN = 1.008 and the pion-N-Δ coupling constant fπNΔ = 2.202
- The cutoff parameter Λ = 0.63 GeV was chosen for the πNN and πNΔ vertex form factors, following Dmitriev (1986)
- The slope parameter a for the u-channel term in pn elastic differential cross section is a = 1 for plab < 0.8 GeV/c and a = 0.64/plab^2 for 0.8 < plab (with plab in GeV/c)
- The ratio of real-to-imaginary part ρpp is set to 0 at plab < 2 GeV/c and ρpn is set to -0.5 at plab < 4.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cumulative proton production in pd collisions, particularly the calculations of Haneishi (1985) and Dakhno (1988), by improving the treatment of propagators of intermediate scattering states, placing them inside momentum integrals rather than using artificial constants or ignoring the real part. The model enables comparison with exclusive pd → ppn experimental data to test whether exotic effects (density fluctuations or 6q clusters in the deuteron) are needed to explain backward proton production, and the results suggest conventional rescattering mechanisms may be sufficient, though remaining discrepancies between different experimental datasets prevent a definitive conclusion.
