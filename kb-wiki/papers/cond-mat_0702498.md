# Pairing Gaps, Pseudogaps, and Phase Diagrams for Cuprate Superconductors

> We use a symmetry-constrained variational procedure to construct a generalization of BCS to include Cooper pairs with non-zero momentum and angular momentum. The resulting gap equations are solved at zero and finite temperature, and the doping-dependent solutions are used to construct gap and phase diagrams. We find a pseudogap terminating at a critical doping that may be interpreted in terms of both competing order and preformed pairs. The strong similarity between observation and predicted gap and phase structure suggests that this approach may provide a unified description of the complex structure observed for cuprate superconductors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a generalized BCS theory incorporating Cooper pairs with non-zero momentum and angular momentum, derived from an SU(4) symmetry-constrained variational procedure, can produce gap and phase diagrams for hole-doped cuprates that closely resemble experimental observations, but only if the effective interaction strengths are allowed to have a simple doping dependence in the low-doping region; with constant interaction strengths the agreement is only qualitative.

## Method

The authors construct an SU(4) coherent state formalism that generalizes BCS theory to include singlet D pairs (d-wave), triplet pi pairs, and antiferromagnetic (AF) correlations, with the SU(4) symmetry enforcing no double occupancy on the lattice. They derive temperature-dependent gap equations for the singlet pairing gap (Delta_d), triplet pairing gap (Delta_pi), and AF gap (Delta_q), and solve these equations at zero and finite temperature for varying hole doping x. The solutions are used to construct gap diagrams and phase diagrams, first with constant interaction strengths and then with doping-dependent strengths that include an onset of pairing at approximately 5% hole doping.

## Key numbers

* Critical doping point x_q = [(chi - G_0)/(chi - G_1)]^(1/2), where chi, G_0, and G_1 are the effective strengths of AF correlations, singlet pairing, and triplet pairing respectively; this separates two qualitatively distinct ground states.
* With doping-dependent interaction strengths, the pseudogap terminates at a critical doping P_q = 0.18 (with a dotted line variant shown at P_q = 0.16).
* The empirical T_c curve used for comparison is T_c = T_c,max [1 - 82.6(P - 0.16)^2].
* The onset of pairing strength is placed at P = 0.05 in the doping-dependent model.
* The empirical factor R = 0.6 is used in the finite-temperature quasiparticle number density formula.
* Constant interaction strengths used: G_1 = 3.74, G_0 = 8.2, and chi = 13 (units not specified in text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior SU(4) model of competing antiferromagnetism and d-wave superconductivity (Guidry 2001, Wu 2003, Guidry 2004, Sun 2006) by solving the gap equations at finite temperature and comparing with experimental data. The predicted pseudogap terminating at a critical doping is interpreted as unifying the competing-order picture (Tallon 2001) and the preformed-pair picture (Emery and Kivelson 1995). The results enable quantitative comparison with a wide range of cuprate data including NMR measurements (Zheng 2005), specific heat and transport data (Tallon 2004), Nernst effect measurements (Xu 2000, Ong 2004), and in-plane resistivity measurements (Ando 2004), and suggest that the general gap and phase structure will be a necessary consequence of any realistic theory of doped Mott insulators with competing d-wave pairing and antiferromagnetism.
