# Transverse momentum spectra of hadrons in pp, pA and AA collisions

> The transverse momentum spectra of the produced hadrons have been compared to a model which is based on the assumption that a nucleus-nucleus collision is a superposition of isotropically decaying thermal sources at a given freeze out temperature. The freeze-out temperature in nucleus-nucleus collisions is fixed from the inverse slope of the transverse momentum spectra of hadrons in nucleon-nucleon collision. The successive collisions in the nuclear reaction leads to gain in transverse momentum, as the nucleons propagate in the nucleus following a random walk pattern. The average transverse rapidity shift per collision is determined from the nucleon-nucleus collision data. Using these information we obtain parameter free result for the transverse momentum distribution of produced hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions. It is observed that such a model is able to explain the transverse mass spectra of produced pions at SPS energies. However it fails to satisfactorily explain the transverse mass spectra of kaons and protons. This indicates the presence of collective effect which cannot be accounted for by the initial state collision broadening of transverse momentum of produced hadrons, the basis of random walk model.

## Key claim

The random walk model, which attributes transverse momentum broadening in nucleus-nucleus collisions solely to initial-state successive collisions, can explain the transverse mass spectra of pions at SPS energies but fails to satisfactorily explain the spectra of kaons and protons, indicating the presence of collective effects (transverse flow) that cannot be accounted for by initial-state collision broadening alone.

## Method

The author models a nucleus-nucleus collision as a superposition of isotropically decaying thermal sources (fireballs) at a freeze-out temperature, where successive collisions impart transverse momentum gains following a Gaussian random walk pattern. The freeze-out temperature is fixed from the inverse slope of transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions, and the average transverse rapidity shift per collision is determined from pA collision data. Using these two parameters fixed from pp and pA data, the model produces parameter-free predictions for the transverse mass distributions of pions, kaons, and protons in AA collisions at SPS energies, which are then compared to experimental data from the NA49 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature for AA collisions is fixed at either 140 MeV or 150 MeV, based on fits to pp collision data at CMS energies from 23 GeV to 63 GeV.
- For p+Be collisions at 14.6 AGeV, the average transverse rapidity shift per collision (delta) is 0.05 for pions, 0.25 for kaons, and 0.25 for protons.
- For p+Pb collisions at 450 AGeV, delta is approximately 0.45 for all three hadron species (pions, kaons, protons) when using T = 150 MeV.
- For pion spectra in AA collisions at SPS energies (40-158 AGeV), the chi-squared per degree of freedom is less than 1.0 for both T = 140 MeV and T = 150 MeV.
- For kaon spectra in AA collisions, chi-squared per degree of freedom worsens from 3.5 at 40 AGeV to 10 at 158 AGeV.
- For proton spectra in AA collisions, chi-squared per degree of freedom lies between 3.5 and 7.5.
- When fitting kaon spectra with free parameters, a common temperature of 235 MeV and delta of 0.25 yields chi-squared per degree of freedom less than 1.0 for all three beam energies.
- When combining random walk model with transverse flow, a transverse flow velocity (beta_T) of 0.45 for kaons and 0.48 for protons is used, with relative contributions C1 = 0.5 and C2 = 0.5 for kaons, and C1 = 0.2 and C2 = 0.8 for protons.

## Context

This work extends the random walk model developed by Leonidov and Jane to systematically test whether initial-state transverse momentum broadening alone can explain the mass-dependent transverse momentum broadening observed in heavy-ion collisions, which had previously been attributed to radial flow in hydrodynamic models (Heinz; the radial flow reference). The study uses pp data from the ISR experiment, pA data from E802 (14.6 AGeV) and NA44 (450 AGeV), and AA data from NA49 at SPS energies. The failure of the model for kaons and protons enables future work to quantify the relative contributions of initial-state broadening versus collective transverse flow, and the author notes that systematic data from pp, pA, and AA collisions at a fixed CMS energy would help separate these effects.
