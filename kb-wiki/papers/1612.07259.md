# The Skyrme model and chiral perturbation theory

> A lagrangian which describes interactions between a soliton and a background field is derived for sigma models whose target is a symmetric space. The background field modifies the usual moduli space approximation to soliton dynamics in two ways: by introducing a potential energy, and by inducing a Kaluza-Klein metric on the moduli space. In the particular case of the Skyrme model, this lagrangian is quantised and shown to agree with the leading pion-nucleon term in the chiral effective lagrangian, which is widely used in theoretical nuclear physics. Thus chiral perturbation theory could be considered a low energy limit of the Skyrme model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the chiral effective lagrangian can be considered a low-energy limit of the Skyrme model, but this is demonstrated only to leading order in the pion-nucleon interaction, with the caveat that subleading terms are beyond the scope of the work.

## Method

The author derives a general formalism for sigma models whose target is a symmetric space, then obtains a worldline action describing a soliton interacting with a background field. For the Skyrme model specifically, this classical action is quantised and a Schrödinger equation for a nucleon is derived, which is then compared to the Schrödinger equation obtained from the leading pion-nucleon term in the chiral effective lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The measured pion decay constant is approximately 184 MeV and the pion mass is approximately 137 MeV.
- The Skyrme model lagrangian includes parameters \(F_\pi\) (pion decay constant), \(m_\pi\) (pion mass), and a dimensionless parameter \(e\).
- The skyrmion mass is given by \(M_S = 4\pi \frac{F_\pi}{4e} \mu[f]\), where \(\mu[f]\) is a dimensionless integral over the profile function \(f\).
- The moment of inertia tensor for the skyrmion is \(\Lambda(Z,W) = \frac{16\pi}{3}\frac{\hbar^2}{F_\pi e^3}\lambda[f] (Z^i-W^i)(Z^i-W^i)\), with \(\lambda[f]\) a dimensionless integral.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the relationship between the Skyrme model and chiral perturbation theory, two alternative models of nuclear physics. The author notes that baryons are treated differently in the two models: in chiral perturbation theory they are quantum excitations of fields, while in the Skyrme model they emerge as topological excitations (skyrmions). The result is presented as a counterpart to Coleman's duality between the quantised sine-Gordon and massless Thirring models (Coleman, 1975), though the author acknowledges that Coleman's result is an exact duality while this work is based on approximations and effective field theories. The methods developed for deriving the worldline action are expected to find applications in studies of skyrmion scattering and other soliton interactions.
