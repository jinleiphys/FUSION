# Low-energy E1 strength in select nuclei: Possible constraints on the neutron skins and the symmetry energy

> Correlations between low-lying electric dipole (E1) strength and neutron skin thickness are systematically investigated with the fully self-consistent random-phase approximation using the Skyrme energy functionals. The presence of strong correlation among these quantities is currently under dispute. We find that the strong correlation is present in properly selected nuclei, namely in spherical neutron-rich nuclei in the region where the neutron Fermi levels are located at orbits with low orbital angular momenta. The significant correlation between the fraction of the energy-weighted sum value and the slope of the symmetry energy is also observed. The deformation in the ground state seems to weaken the correlation.

## Key claim

The strong linear correlation between low-energy electric dipole (E1) strength and neutron skin thickness, which is currently under dispute, exists only in properly selected spherical neutron-rich nuclei where the neutron Fermi levels are located at orbits with low orbital angular momenta; the correlation is weak or absent in other nuclei and is weakened by ground-state deformation.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the Skyrme energy functional (primarily SkM*) for even-even nuclei with Z ≤ 40 and for ¹³²Sn. They systematically varied 10 Skyrme parameters to generate 21 parameter sets, computed the PDR strength (integrated B(E1) up to a cutoff energy ωc) and the neutron skin thickness Δrnp for each nucleus, and quantified correlations using the Pearson correlation coefficient r.

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient between PDR strength in ¹³²Sn and neutron skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb: r = 0.55 (weak correlation)
- Correlation coefficient for ⁶⁸Ni: r = 0.69; for ⁷⁸Ni: r = 0.76; for ⁸⁴Ni: r = 0.94 (strong linear correlation)
- Correlation coefficient for ⁵²Ca: r = 0.91; for ⁵⁴Ca: r = 0.96; for ⁴⁸Ca: r = 0.78 (weaker)
- Correlation coefficient for ²⁴O: r = 0.97 (strongest correlation observed)
- For deformed nuclei: ⁵⁸Cr (β₂=0.17) gives r = 0.80; ¹¹⁰Zr (β₂=0.36) gives r = 0.74
- Slope of linear fit for ⁸⁴Ni: dS_PDR/d(Δrnp) = 13-16 e²fm, universal across SkM*, SGII, and SIII functionals
- Using variable cutoff ωc, correlation between PDR fraction and slope parameter L: r(v) = 0.87 for ⁸⁴Ni, r(v) = 0.80 for ⁶⁸Ni

## Context

This work extends the systematic RPA study of PDR in even-even nuclei by Inakura (2011) using the finite amplitude method, and directly addresses the dispute between Piekarewicz (2006), who predicted a linear correlation for Sn isotopes using relativistic mean-field RPA, and Reinhard and Nazarewicz, who found a very weak correlation using covariance analysis of Skyrme functionals. The authors show that both conclusions can be reconciled: the correlation is strong only in specific neutron-rich spherical nuclei where the neutron Fermi level occupies low-ℓ orbits (s, p, d shells), specifically in the neutron number ranges 14 < N ≤ 16, 28 < N ≤ 34, and 50 < N ≤ 56. This provides guidance for future experiments to constrain the neutron skin thickness Δrnp and the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy by measuring PDR in selected nuclei such as ⁸⁴Ni, ⁵⁴Ca, and ²⁴O.
