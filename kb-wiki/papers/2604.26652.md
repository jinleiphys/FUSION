# Possible explanation of Hoehler's clustering: effective partial-wave mixing induced by truncation

> Hoehler noted that resonance poles obtained from different partial waves in $πN$ scattering appear to bunch together near a small set of common complex energies, and suggested that this could indicate mixing between angular momenta. Here, we examine whether at least part of this pattern could arise effectively from the extraction procedure itself. Exact partial-wave unitarity preserves the separation of angular momenta in the infinite problem, whereas practical pole extraction from bilinear observables requires truncation of the partial-wave series. Combined with the truncation-induced mixing mechanism established in Ref.~\cite{Svarc2026}, this provides a natural source by which fitted partial-wave coefficients can inherit overlapping pole-bearing content, thereby offering a plausible contribution to Hoehler-type clustering.

# Possible explanation of Höhler's clustering: effective partial-wave mixing induced by truncation

## Key claim
The paper argues that Höhler's clustering, the near-degeneracy of resonance pole positions across different partial waves in πN scattering, may arise not from genuine dynamical mixing of angular momenta in the exact amplitude, but rather as an effective artifact of the practical pole extraction procedure when bilinear observables are fitted with a truncated partial-wave series.

## Method
The author first demonstrates that exact partial-wave unitarity preserves angular-momentum separation in the infinite problem, ruling out genuine dynamical mixing as an explanation. Then, using the truncation-induced mixing mechanism established in Svarc (2026), the paper shows that when bilinear observables (which are quadratic in amplitudes) are fitted with a truncated Legendre expansion, the extracted lower-order partial-wave coefficients become coupled nonlinear functions of both retained and omitted higher partial waves, thereby inheriting mixed angular-momentum content.

## Key numbers
- Höhler observed resonance pole groups near (1670 − 50i) MeV and (2120 − 170i) MeV in πN scattering, with poles from different partial waves clustering within errors at these common complex energies.
- The truncation analysis considers an exact amplitude expanded to order N and a practical truncated ansatz to order M, with M < N, leading to bilinear coefficients up to order 2M that are fitted to the exact bilinear coefficients up to order 2N.

## Context
This work extends Höhler's original observation (Höhler, 1983) of pole clustering in πN partial-wave analyses, which was later interpreted by Kirchbach (1997) and Losanow and Kirchbach (1998) in terms of Lorentz multiplets as a genuine structural feature. The paper instead argues that the clustering can be explained by the truncation-induced mixing mechanism from Svarc (2026), and notes that the effect is less visually obvious in modern Particle Data Group compilations (PDG, 2024) due to broader data bases and stricter completeness criteria. This enables a reinterpretation of clustered pole positions as effective artifacts of practical extraction rather than fundamental properties of the baryon spectrum.
