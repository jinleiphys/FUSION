# QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential from Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We review results for the phase diagram of QCD, the properties of quarks and gluons and the resulting properties of strongly interacting matter at finite temperature and chemical potential. The interplay of two different but related transitions in QCD, chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement, leads to a rich phenomenology when external parameters such as quark masses, volume, temperature and chemical potential are varied. We discuss the progress in this field from a theoretical perspective, focusing on non-perturbative QCD as encoded in the functional approach via Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We aim at a pedagogical overview on the physics associated with the structure of this framework and explain connections to other approaches, in particular with the functional renormalization group and lattice QCD. We discuss various aspects associated with the variation of the quark masses, assess recent results for the QCD phase diagram including the location of a putative critical end-point for $N_f=2+1$ and $N_f=2+1+1$, discuss results for quark spectral functions and summarise aspects of QCD thermodynamics and fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and chemical potential remains largely speculative, with the existence and location of a critical end-point (CEP) still unconfirmed; the author emphasizes that while lattice QCD has established an analytic crossover at zero chemical potential, the situation at real chemical potential is hampered by the fermion sign problem, and functional methods like Dyson-Schwinger equations provide complementary access to larger chemical potentials but are restricted by the need to truncate infinite systems of equations.

## Method

The author reviews the application of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) to non-perturbative QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, focusing on truncation strategies for the infinite tower of Green's function equations. The review covers order parameters extracted from quark and gluon propagators, the determination of the chiral and deconfinement transition lines, and the calculation of thermodynamic quantities and fluctuations. Connections are made to results from lattice QCD, the functional renormalization group, and effective models such as the Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) and Polyakov-loop quark-meson (PQM) models.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition at zero chemical potential is \(T_c \approx 155\) MeV with an error margin below ten MeV, from lattice QCD results by Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2011).
- The curvature \(\kappa\) of the transition line from DSE by Fischer (2014) is \(0.0238 \pm 0.0100\), while lattice results range from \(0.0130 \pm 0.0030\) (Bonati, 2014) to \(0.0180 \pm 0.0040\) (Cea, 2014).
- A naive estimate based on the liquid-gas transition at \(\mu_B^{lg} \approx 922\) MeV gives an upper bound for the curvature \(\kappa \le 0.0141\).
- The coefficient \(\lambda\) in the expansion of the transition line is estimated as \(\lambda = \frac{1}{2} \kappa^2 \le 0.0001\).
- The spread of transition lines from lattice results corresponds to the range \(0.0120 < \kappa < 0.0141\), predicting a chiral transition at \(922 \lesssim \mu_B \lesssim 1000\) MeV.
- The deconfinement transition temperature for pure SU(3) gauge theory is around \(T_c \approx 270\) MeV, from Karsch (2003).
- The baryon density at the liquid-gas transition is \(\rho_0 = 0.17/\text{fm}^3\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Roberts and Schmidt (2000) on Dyson-Schwinger equations in QCD and provides a pedagogical overview of progress over nearly twenty years. It synthesizes results from lattice QCD (Aoki, 2006; Borsanyi, 2010; Bazavov, 2011) that established the crossover at zero chemical potential, and from effective models such as the PNJL model (Fukushima, 2003; Ratti, 2005) and PQM model (Schaefer, 2007) that suggest a critical end-point. The work enables future comparisons between functional methods and lattice extrapolations for chemical potentials \(\mu_B/T > 2\), and highlights open questions regarding inhomogeneous phases, the fate of the \(U_A(1)\) anomaly, and the location of the critical end-point for \(N_f=2+1\) and \(N_f=2+1+1\) quark flavors.
