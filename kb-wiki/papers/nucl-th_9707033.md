# Effects of gluon damping rate on the viscosity coefficient of the quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potential

> By considering the Debye screening and damping rate of gluons, the viscosity coefficient of the quark-gluon plasma was evaluated via real-time finite temperature QCD in the relaxation time approximation at finite temperature and chemical potential . The results show that both the damping rate and the chemical potential cause considerable enhancements to the viscosity coefficient of hot dense quark-gluon plasma.

# Effects of Gluon Damping Rate on the Viscosity Coefficient of the Quark-Gluon Plasma at Finite Chemical Potential

## Key claim
The shear viscosity coefficient of the quark-gluon plasma is enhanced considerably by both the gluon damping rate (finite width of transverse gluons) and the quark chemical potential, but the enhancement from the damping rate is larger than that from the chemical potential. The authors note their calculations are valid only in the weak coupling limit (g < 1), though they suggest results can be extrapolated to higher coupling at fixed temperature T = 300 MeV.

## Method
The shear viscosity coefficient was calculated using relativistic kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation, with transport interaction rates obtained from the imaginary parts of quark and gluon self-energies via Thermo Field Dynamics (real-time finite temperature QCD). The effective gluon propagator included Debye screening for the longitudinal part and a damping rate for the transverse gluon propagator, with the running coupling constant taken as a function of both temperature and chemical potential. The calculation assumed massless QGP with two quark flavors and hard quark momentum <p_q> ~ 3T.

## Key numbers
- Quark shear viscosity coefficient expression: η_q = [ (27/(5π)) ( (7π²/120) T³ (1 - 50α_s/(21π)) + (1/4) μ² T + (μ⁴/(8π² T)) (1 - 2α_s/(2π)) ) ] / [ α_s² (4/3 + μ²/(π² T²)) ( -ln α_s + ln( 1/(4π(4/3 + μ²/(π² T²))) + α_s ) + A(ν) ) ]
- Gluon shear viscosity coefficient expression: η_g = [ (16/(5π)) (π²/30) T³ (1 - 15α_s/(4π)) ] / [ α_s² (4/3 + μ²/(π² T²)) ( -ln α_s + ln( 1/(4π(4/3 + μ²/(π² T²))) + α_s ) + A(ν) ) ]
- At vanishing chemical potential and without damping, the leading-order result gives η = 0.98 T³ / (α_s² ln(1/α_s)), with coefficient c = 0.98 close to Thoma's c = 1.02 and Baym et al.'s result
- The running coupling constant used: α(T, μ) = 12π / [ (33 - 2 n_f) ln( (0.8 μ² + 15.622 T²) / Λ_s² ) ] with Λ_s = 0.1 GeV and n_f = 2
- Quark chemical potentials considered: μ = 0, 0.2, 0.4 GeV
- Fixed temperature for coupling constant study: T = 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in QGP by Hosoya and Kajantie (1985), Baym, Monien, Pethick, and Ravenhall (1990), and Thoma (1994) which were performed at finite temperature without baryon density and without including gluon damping effects. The authors note that at RHIC energies, μ/T ~ 1-2 may still be relevant, as discussed by Vija and Thoma (1995). The inclusion of finite width effects follows the treatment of damping rates in QGP by Pisarski (1989) and Braaten and Pisarski (1990). The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic descriptions of the expansion phase of QGP in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly for scenarios with non-zero baryon chemical potential.
