# Anisotropic Nature of Work Function in Strong Quantizing Magnetic Field

> Following an extremely interesting idea \cite{R1}, published long ago, the work function associated with the emission of ultra-relativistic electrons from magnetically deformed metallic crystal of astrophysical relevance is obtained using relativistic version of Thomas-Fermi type model. In the present scenario, surprisingly, the work function becomes anisotropic; the longitudinal part is an increasing function of magnetic field strength, whereas the transverse part diverges.

# Anisotropic Nature of Work Function in Strong Quantizing Magnetic Field

## Key claim

The work function for ultra-relativistic electron emission from magnetically deformed metallic iron crystals in neutron star crusts becomes anisotropic under strong quantizing magnetic fields: the longitudinal component increases as B^(1/2), while the transverse component diverges to infinity. This result is derived under the approximations of zero temperature, ultra-relativistic electrons (m_e → 0), and electrons occupying only the zeroth Landau level (valid for B ≥ 10^14 G).

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Thomas-Fermi type model within cylindrically deformed Wigner-Seitz cells to describe electron distributions around iron nuclei in the outer crust of strongly magnetized neutron stars. They solved the Poisson equation in cylindrical coordinates under the approximation that only the zeroth Landau level is occupied and electrons are ultra-relativistic, then applied continuity conditions for electric fields at the cell boundary to derive the secondary (image) potential. The work function was obtained by integrating the force on an electron at the verge of emission, separately for longitudinal (along magnetic field) and transverse (perpendicular to field) directions.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal work function is given by W_f = (1/3)(2B/(π B_c^(e)))^(1/2) m_e e^3, where B_c^(e) ≈ 4.43 × 10^13 G is the critical field strength for relativistic electron Landau level population.
- The longitudinal work function scales as B^(1/2), meaning it increases with magnetic field strength.
- For low magnetic field values, the longitudinal work function is a few eV in magnitude, consistent with experimentally known values for laboratory metals.
- The transverse work function integral diverges to infinity, independent of the transverse dimension R of the cylindrical cells (both for R → 0 and R → ∞ limits).
- The approximation of zeroth Landau level only is valid for magnetic fields B ≥ 10^14 G.

## Context

This work extends the idea of Schieber and Schachter (unpublished, cited as R1) for cold cathode emission to the astrophysical context of strongly magnetized neutron stars and magnetars. It contrasts with density functional theory (DFT) calculations by Lai et al. (md1, md2) which also obtained work function as a function of magnetic field for neutron star crustal matter; the authors note their simple model gives almost the same magnetic field dependence as the DFT results. The anisotropic work function is analogous to the anisotropic electrical conductivity in strong magnetic fields found by previous authors (pbh, po, py), where conductivity parallel to the field is high but perpendicular conductivity is severely inhibited. The results enable calculation of electron field emission currents at neutron star polar regions, which is relevant for understanding magnetospheric plasma formation in pulsars and magnetars.
