# Neutrino Scattering in Heterogeneous Supernova Plasmas

> Neutrinos in core collapse supernovae are likely trapped by neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering. Using molecular dynamics simulations, we calculate neutrino mean free paths and ion-ion correlation functions for heterogeneous plasmas. Mean free paths are systematically shorter in plasmas containing a mixture of ions compared to a plasma composed of a single ion species. This is because neutrinos can scatter from concentration fluctuations. The dynamical response function of a heterogeneous plasma is found to have an extra peak at low energies describing the diffusion of concentration fluctuations. Our exact molecular dynamics results for the static structure factor reduce to the Debye Huckel approximation, but only in the limit of very low momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutrino mean free paths are systematically shorter in heterogeneous supernova plasmas containing a mixture of different ion species compared to a single-component plasma, because neutrinos can scatter from concentration fluctuations in the weak charge density that are not screened by electrostatic forces. However, this enhancement is only fully captured by molecular dynamics simulations at moderate momentum transfers, while the Debye Huckel approximation reproduces the exact results only in the limit of very low momentum transfers (below approximately 0.055 fm\(^{-1}\) for the conditions studied).

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations using the Verlet algorithm with Yukawa potentials to model ion-ion interactions, including electron screening, for systems of 1000 to 40000 ions at supernova-relevant densities and temperatures. They calculated the static structure factor \(S(q)\) and the dynamical response function \(S(q,w)\) for both heterogeneous mixtures (using compositions from a microscopic nucleon model and from a statistical nuclear statistical equilibrium model) and for pure single-species plasmas with the same average neutron number and charge. The simulations used periodic boundary conditions and were run for warmup and measurement times ranging from \(1.6\times 10^5\) to \(1.3\times 10^7\) fm/c, with results compared to the Debye Huckel approximation.

## Key numbers

- At a density of \(1.66\times 10^{13}\) g/cm\(^3\), proton fraction \(Y_p=0.2\), and temperature \(T=1\) MeV, the microscopic model yields ions with average mass number \(\langle A \rangle = 105.2\) and average charge \(\langle Z \rangle = 32.1\), with an ion density of \(7.18\times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The statistical model at \(10^{13}\) g/cm\(^3\), \(Y_p=0.2\), and \(T=1\) MeV gives \(\langle A \rangle = 328.6\) and \(\langle Z \rangle = 75.7\), with ion density \(1.60\times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the statistical model at \(10^{12}\) g/cm\(^3\), \(Y_p=0.5\), and \(T=1\) MeV, \(\langle A \rangle = 56.8\) and \(\langle Z \rangle = 28.3\), with ion density \(1.06\times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The Debye Huckel approximation agrees with molecular dynamics results for \(S(q)\) in the mixture at \(1.66\times 10^{13}\) g/cm\(^3\) for momentum transfers \(q\) between 0.025 and 0.055 fm\(^{-1}\); for \(q > 0.055\) fm\(^{-1}\), the Debye Huckel result is smaller than the molecular dynamics result.
- The dynamical response function for the mixture at \(q=0.116\) fm\(^{-1}\) shows a large peak near \(w=0\) (zero energy transfer) that is absent in the pure system, corresponding to diffusion of concentration fluctuations.
- The pure system at \(1.66\times 10^{13}\) g/cm\(^3\) with \(Z=75.7\) has a Coulomb coupling parameter \(\Gamma = 335\), indicating the system is expected to be a solid (\(\Gamma > 180\) for a one-component plasma).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering in supernova plasmas that assumed a single-component plasma (Itoh, 1990s; Horowitz, 1997) and a first attempt at treating mixtures by Janka (1990s) using a simple prescription. It directly addresses the argument by Sawyer (2005) that composition fluctuations in ion mixtures could increase the neutrino cross section, but shows that Sawyer's Debye Huckel approximation is only valid at very low momentum transfers. The results enable more accurate calculations of neutrino mean free paths in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly during the infall phase where the plasma composition evolves due to electron capture and nuclear statistical equilibrium.
