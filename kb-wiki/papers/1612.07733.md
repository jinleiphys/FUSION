# Strong isospin violation and chiral logarithms in the baryon spectrum

> We present a precise lattice QCD calculation of the contribution to the neutron-proton mass splitting arising from strong isospin breaking, $m_n-m_p|_{QCD}=2.32\pm0.17$ MeV. We also determine $m_{Ξ^-} - m_{Ξ^0}|_{QCD} = 5.44\pm0.31$ MeV. The calculation is performed at three values of the pion mass, with several values of the quark mass splitting and multiple lattice volumes, but only a single lattice spacing and an estimate of discretization errors. The calculations are performed on the anisotropic clover-Wilson ensembles generated by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration. The omega-baryon mass is used to set the scale $a_t^{-1}=6111\pm127$ MeV, while the kaon masses are used to determine the value of the light-quark mass spitting. The nucleon mass splitting is then determined as a function of the pion mass. We observe, for the first time, conclusive evidence for non-analytic light quark mass dependence in lattice QCD calculations of the baryon spectrum. When left as a free parameter, the fits prefer a nucleon axial coupling of $g_A=1.24(56)$. To highlight the presence of this chiral logarithm in the nucleon mass splitting, we also compute the isospin splitting in the cascade-baryon system which is less sensitive to chiral dynamics. Finally, we update the best lattice QCD determination of the CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling that would arise from a non-zero QCD theta-term, $\bar{g}_0 / (\sqrt{2}f_π) = (14.7\pm1.8\pm1.4) \cdot 10^{-3} \barθ$. The original lattice QCD correlation functions, analysis results and extrapolated quantities are packaged in HDF5 files made publicly available including a simple Python script to access the numerical results, construct effective mass plots along with our analysis results, and perform the extrapolations of various quantities determined in this work.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the strong isospin contribution to the neutron-proton mass splitting, \(m_n-m_p|_{\text{QCD}}=2.32\pm0.17\) MeV, and claims the first conclusive evidence for non-analytic light quark mass dependence (chiral logarithms) in lattice QCD calculations of the baryon spectrum, though the calculation is performed at only a single lattice spacing with an estimate of discretization errors.

## Method

The calculation uses anisotropic clover-Wilson ensembles generated by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration at three values of the pion mass (241, 421, and 490 MeV), with several values of the quark mass splitting and multiple lattice volumes, but only a single lattice spacing. Strong isospin breaking is induced by spreading valence up and down quark masses symmetrically about the degenerate light quark mass, a partially quenched approach that suppresses unitarity-violating effects. The omega baryon mass sets the scale, kaon masses determine the light-quark mass splitting, and the nucleon mass splitting is determined as a function of the pion mass using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory fits.

## Key numbers

*   Neutron-proton mass splitting from strong isospin breaking: \(m_n-m_p|_{\text{QCD}} = 2.32 \pm 0.17\) MeV.
*   Cascade baryon mass splitting from strong isospin breaking: \(m_{\Xi^-} - m_{\Xi^0}|_{\text{QCD}} = 5.44 \pm 0.31\) MeV.
*   Lattice scale: \(a_t^{-1} = 6111 \pm 85 \pm 94\) MeV, determined from the omega baryon mass.
*   CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling from a non-zero QCD theta-term: \(\bar{g}_0 / (\sqrt{2}f_\pi) = (14.7 \pm 1.8 \pm 1.4) \cdot 10^{-3} \bar{\theta}\).
*   Nucleon axial coupling from chiral fits: \(g_A = 1.24(56)\) when left as a free parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of baryon masses by providing the first definitive evidence for chiral logarithms in the nucleon spectrum, building on suggestive evidence from Walker-Loud (2011) using linear combinations of octet and decuplet baryon masses. The results enable improved determinations of low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory and update the best lattice QCD determination of the CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling relevant for the QCD theta-term. The calculation uses ensembles from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration and provides publicly available HDF5 files with analysis scripts for reproducibility.
