# The $pd\to ^3H_ΛK^+$ reaction cross section

> The one- and two-step mechanisms of the $pd\to ^3H_ΛK^+$ reaction in the range of incident proton kinetic energy 1.13-3.0 GeV have been investigated. A remarkable peculiarity of the two-step mechanism which incorporates subprocesses $pp\to dπ^+$ and $π^+n\to K^+Λ$ is the so called velocity matching providing the presence of all intermediate particles nearly to the on-mass-shell. The differential cross section has been calculated using a realistic model for the hypertritium $^3H_Λ$ wave function. The maximum value of the cross section is estimated as $\sim $1nb/sr. The contribution of the one-step mechanism with the elementary process $pN\to NKΛ$ into the cross section has been found to be two - three orders of magnitude smaller in comparison with the two-step mechanism.

# The $pd\to ^3H_\Lambda K^+$ reaction cross section

## Key claim

The two-step mechanism, incorporating the subprocesses $pp\to d\pi^+$ and $\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$ with velocity matching that keeps intermediate particles nearly on-mass-shell, dominates the $pd\to ^3H_\Lambda K^+$ reaction cross section; the one-step mechanism via $pN\to NK\Lambda$ is two to three orders of magnitude smaller. The maximum cross section is estimated at approximately 1 nb/sr, but this prediction relies on the assumption that factorization of the amplitudes holds and that final-state interactions are negligible.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section using a three-dimensional diagram technique for the two-step mechanism, factoring the amplitudes of the elementary subprocesses $pp\to d\pi^+$ and $\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$ outside a nuclear formfactor integral. The nuclear formfactor was evaluated in the s-wave approximation for the deuteron wave function (RSC potential parametrization) and a $d+\Lambda$ cluster model for the $^3H_\Lambda$ wave function based on separable $\Lambda N$-interaction. For the one-step mechanism, the amplitude was computed using both S- and D-components of the deuteron wave function, with the elementary $pN\to N\Lambda K^+$ amplitude estimated from experimental total cross section data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum differential cross section of the $pd\to ^3H_\Lambda K^+$ reaction is approximately 1 nb/sr, about 50 times smaller than the corresponding $\eta$-meson production cross section in $pd\to ^3He\eta$.
- The one-step mechanism contribution is two to three orders of magnitude smaller than the two-step mechanism contribution.
- The squared amplitude $|A_3(pN\to N\Lambda K^+)|^2$ is estimated as 250 to 450 GeV$^{-2}$ in the initial proton energy range 1.6 to 3.0 GeV.
- The deuteron D-component contributes about 10% to the cross section.
- The reaction threshold is at incident proton kinetic energy $T_p = 1132$ MeV.
- The incident proton kinetic energy range investigated is $T_p = 1.13$ to 3.0 GeV.
- At $\theta_{c.m.}=180^\circ$, the momentum $P_0$ decreases from 0.5 to 0.1 GeV/c over the energy range $T_p = 1.1$ to 3.0 GeV.
- At $\theta_{c.m.}=0^\circ$ and $90^\circ$, both $E_0$ and $P_0$ increase from approximately 0.5 to 1.2 GeV over the same energy range.
- The $^3H_\Lambda$ binding energy is approximately 2.35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-step (three-body) mechanism previously applied to $pd\to ^3He\pi^0$ and $pd\to ^3He\eta$ reactions by Laget and collaborators, and by Kilian and collaborators, to the strangeness-production channel $pd\to ^3H_\Lambda K^+$. The velocity matching condition, which keeps intermediate pions, deuterons, and nucleons near on-mass-shell, is analogous to that in $\eta$-meson production but does not hold for heavier mesons ($\omega$, $\phi$, $\eta'$) or for target nuclei with $A\geq 3$. The calculation enables future experimental tests at facilities such as COSY, and provides a method to extract information about the $^3H_\Lambda$ wave function. The authors note that unlike $\eta$-production, where strong final-state interaction via the $N^*(1535)$ resonance complicates the threshold region, no such resonance coupling is known for $K^+$ mesons in the 1.2-2.0 GeV mass region, so final-state interactions are expected to be less important.
