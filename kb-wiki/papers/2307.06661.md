# The study of hadronic rescattering on $K^{*0}$ resonance yield in baryon-rich QCD matter

> The effect of hadronic rescattering on $K^{*0}$ resonance yield can be studied by measuring $K^{*0}/K$ ratio as a function of centrality or multiplicity. This study investigates how the size of the system and the chemical composition (meson-meson versus meson-baryon interaction) of the matter formed in heavy-ion collisions impact the process of hadronic rescattering. It is shown that existing calculation of $K^{*0}/K$ ratio, which considers the interaction of $K^{*0}$ and $K$ mesons with only light mesons in the hadronic medium (neglecting interactions with baryons), fails to explain the measured $K^{*0}/K$ ratio at RHIC BES energies. To understand the multiplicity dependence of the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio at RHIC BES and SPS energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $<$ 20 GeV), the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model is employed. UrQMD calculations suggest that for a given multiplicity, $K^{*0}/K$ is more suppressed in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ =7.7 GeV, compared to that in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ =200 GeV. This is possibly because of formation different type of QCD medium at 200 GeV and 7.7 GeV, and change in interaction cross-section between hadrons with change in particle type and energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio at low collision energies (below $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 20 GeV) cannot be explained by models that only include meson-meson interactions; the UrQMD model, which includes both meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions, suggests that meson-baryon interactions become dominant in the baryon-rich medium formed at these lower energies, leading to stronger rescattering effects.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model (version 2.3) in cascade mode to simulate minimum-bias Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at various beam energies. They reconstructed the $K^{*0}$ resonance from its decay daughters ($K^{\pm}\pi^{\mp}$) using invariant mass analysis with background subtraction via a track rotation technique, similar to experimental data analysis. The model was used to calculate the $p_T$-integrated yields of $K^{*0}$ and charged kaons at mid-rapidity ($|y| < 0.5$) and to track the time evolution of meson-meson versus meson-baryon interaction rates.

## Key numbers

* At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 GeV, the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio is more suppressed for a given multiplicity compared to that at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, as shown by UrQMD calculations.
* The pseudo-elastic cross-section for the formation of the $\Delta^{++}$ resonance in the $\pi^{+}+p$ system is about 200 mb, compared to approximately 120 mb for the $\rho$ meson in the $\pi+\pi$ system.
* The $K^{-}+p$ total cross-section can reach up to 120 mb at low kaon beam momentum, compared to only 20 mb at higher beam momenta.
* The ratio of reconstructed to true $K^{*0}$ resonances shows more significant suppression at 7.7 GeV than at 200 GeV as a function of $(dN_{ch}/d\eta)^{1/3}$.
* The $\phi$ meson lifetime is 42 fm/c, making it minimally influenced by hadronic rescattering compared to $K^{*0}$.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental measurements of the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio from the STAR, PHENIX, NA49, NA61, and ALICE collaborations, which showed suppression in central heavy-ion collisions compared to small systems. It directly challenges a prior model calculation (Pheno-Kstar 2021) that considered only meson-meson interactions and failed to explain data at RHIC BES energies below $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 20 GeV. The study enables future comparisons with high-statistics BES-II data from STAR to better understand the role of baryon-rich matter in hadronic rescattering, and suggests extending the work using hybrid UrQMD models with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage.
