# Nuclear signatures in high-harmonic generation from laser-driven muonic atoms

> High-harmonic generation from muonic atoms exposed to intense laser fields is considered. Our particular interest lies in effects arising from the finite nuclear mass and size. We numerically perform a fully quantum mechanical treatment of the muon-nucleus dynamics by employing modified soft-core and hard-core potentials. It is shown that the position of the high-energy cutoff of the harmonic spectrum depends on the nuclear mass, while the height of the spectral plateau is sensitive to the nuclear radius. We also demonstrate that $γ$-ray harmonics can be generated from muonic atoms in ultrastrong VUV fields, which have potential to induce photo-nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

High-harmonic generation (HHG) from muonic hydrogen isotopes in intense laser fields shows nuclear signatures: the position of the high-energy cutoff depends on the nuclear mass, while the height of the spectral plateau is sensitive to the nuclear radius. These effects are demonstrated numerically for muonic hydrogen and deuterium, with the caveat that the one-dimensional model likely overestimates the nuclear size influence compared to a full three-dimensional treatment.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the relative motion of the muon-nucleus system in one dimension, using both a soft-core potential (to isolate nuclear mass effects with a point-like nucleus) and a hard-core potential (to include finite nuclear size). The laser field was a 5-cycle trapezoidal pulse, and harmonic spectra were obtained by Fourier transforming the dipole acceleration. For the nuclear size study, laser parameters were scaled to compensate for the mass difference so that cutoff positions would coincide.

## Key numbers

- Muonic hydrogen ground-state binding energy: 2.5 keV; internal electric field: 1.8 x 10^14 V/cm, corresponding to intensity 4.2 x 10^25 W/cm^2.
- Over-barrier ionization threshold intensity for muonic hydrogen: approximately 1.6 x 10^23 W/cm^2.
- Minimum VUV photon energy satisfying both ionization and recombination conditions: approximately 27 eV; corresponding maximum harmonic energy: approximately 0.55 MeV.
- For the nuclear mass effect study: laser intensity 1.05 x 10^23 W/cm^2 and photon energy 59 eV; the hydrogen spectrum extends about 60 harmonics beyond the deuterium spectrum.
- Proton charge radius: approximately 0.875 fm; deuteron charge radius: approximately 2.139 fm.
- For the nuclear size effect study: muonic hydrogen at 1.05 x 10^23 W/cm^2 and 59 eV; muonic deuterium at scaled parameters 1.30 x 10^23 W/cm^2 and 62 eV; the hydrogen harmonic signal is larger by about 50 percent in the cutoff region.

## Context

This work extends the study of high-harmonic generation from electronic atoms (reviewed by Brabec and Krausz (2000)) to muonic atoms, where the large muon mass makes nuclear mass and size effects observable. It builds on prior work on muonic atoms for nuclear spectroscopy (Engfer and Jentschke (1970)) and on laser-nuclear physics (Schwoerer et al. (2006)). The results suggest that muonic atoms in strong laser fields could serve as a dynamic probe of nuclear ground-state structure and enable pump-probe experiments on excited nuclear levels, with potential applications to photo-nuclear reactions.
