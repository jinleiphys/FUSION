# Time-odd triaxial relativistic mean field approach for nuclear magnetic moments

> The time-odd triaxial relativistic mean field approach is developed and applied to the investigation of the ground-state properties of light odd-mass nuclei near the double-closed shells. The nuclear magnetic moments including the isoscalar and isovector ones are calculated and good agreement with Schmidt values is obtained. Taking $^{17}$F as an example, the splitting of the single particle levels (around $~0.7$ MeV near the Fermi level), the nuclear current, the core polarizations, and the nuclear magnetic potential, i.e., the spatial part of the vector potential, due to the violation of the time reversal invariance are investigated in detail.

## Key claim

The time-odd triaxial relativistic mean field approach reproduces the isoscalar magnetic moments of light odd-mass nuclei near double-closed shells in good agreement with Schmidt values, but the agreement is demonstrated specifically for nuclei where the single-particle picture is expected to hold because spin-orbit partners are absent on both sides of the Fermi surface.

## Method

The Dirac equation for nucleons and the Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields were solved self-consistently by iteration in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis expanded in Cartesian coordinates, using the PK1 effective interaction parameter set. The spatial components of the vector meson fields (the time-odd nuclear magnetic potential) were included explicitly, and the Dirac spinors were expressed as linear combinations of time-reversal conjugate basis states to account for the broken time-reversal invariance in odd-mass nuclei. The calculation assumed mirror symmetry with respect to the xy, xz, and yz planes and rotational symmetry for the currents to avoid complex coefficients in the basis.

## Key numbers

- The splitting of single-particle levels near the Fermi level in $^{17}$F is approximately 0.7 MeV due to the violation of time-reversal invariance.
- The nuclear magnetic field $\mathbf{B}_n$ in $^{17}$F is around $5 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-2}$, which leads to an estimated energy splitting of approximately 0.4 MeV for the $1s_{1/2}$ state.
- Each component of the nuclear magnetic potential in $^{17}$F has a peak value around 1.0 to 1.5 MeV.
- The expansion truncation at $n_f = 12$ and $n_b = 10$ gives an error less than 0.1% for the binding energy.
- The oscillator frequency used is $\hbar\omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the time-odd relativistic mean field approach from axial symmetry (Hofmann 1988, Furnstahl 1989) to triaxial deformation, enabling the study of nuclear magnetic moments in nuclei without axial symmetry. The approach incorporates the "back-flow" effect or RPA-type summation of particle-hole and particle-antiparticle bubbles through the self-consistent treatment of the spatial components of vector fields, which had previously been shown to be essential for describing magnetic moments in the relativistic framework. The method enables future investigations of ground-state properties, single-particle energy splittings, current distributions, and magnetic moments in odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei with triaxial shapes.
