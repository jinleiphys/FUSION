# What is the nature of the HESS J1731-347 compact object?

> Once further confirmed in future analyses, the radius and mass measurement of HESS J1731-347 with $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}~M_{\odot}$ and $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}~\rm km$ will be among the lightest and smallest compact objects ever detected. This raises many questions about its nature and opens up the window for different theories to explain such a measurement. In this article, we use the information from Doroshenko et al. (2022) on the mass, radius, and surface temperature together with the multimessenger observations of neutron stars to investigate the possibility that HESS J1731-347 is one of the lightest observed neutron star, a strange quark star, a hybrid star with an early deconfinement phase transition, or a dark matter-admixed neutron star. The nucleonic and quark matter are modeled within realistic equation of states (EOSs) with a self-consistent calculation of the pairing gaps in quark matter. By performing the joint analysis of the thermal evolution and mass-radius constraint, we find evidence that within a 1$σ$ confidence level, HESS J1731-347 is consistent with the neutron star scenario with the soft EOS as well as with a strange and hybrid star with the early deconfinement phase transition with a strong quark pairing and neutron star admixed with dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper finds that HESS J1731-347, with its unusually low mass and radius, is consistent within 1-sigma confidence level with four different scenarios: a neutron star with a soft equation of state, a strange quark star, a hybrid star with early deconfinement phase transition and strong quark pairing, or a dark matter-admixed neutron star; however, the authors note that further confirmation of the mass-radius measurement is required and that the neutron star and hybrid star scenarios are in tension with supernova simulations predicting a minimum neutron star mass of 1.17 solar masses.

## Method

The authors modeled nucleonic matter using the induced surface tension (IST) and BigApple equations of state, quark matter within a chirally symmetric relativistic density functional approach with self-consistent calculation of pairing gaps in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase, and dark matter as a relativistic Fermi gas of noninteracting spin-one-half particles. They performed joint analysis of mass-radius constraints and thermal evolution calculations, solving coupled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the dark matter-admixed neutron star scenario, and comparing cooling curves against the measured surface temperature and age of HESS J1731-347.

## Key numbers

- Mass of HESS J1731-347: M = 0.77 (+0.20, -0.17) solar masses
- Radius of HESS J1731-347: R = 10.4 (+0.86, -0.78) km
- Redshifted surface temperature of HESS J1731-347: T_s_infinity = 2.05 (+0.09, -0.06) MK
- Age of HESS J1731-347: approximately 27 kyr
- Bag constant range for strange quark star scenario: B^(1/4) = 134 (+12, -11) MeV, obtained by fitting the 1-sigma mass-radius constraint
- Dimensionless couplings for hybrid star quark matter: vector repulsion eta_V = 0.265, diquark pairing eta_D = 0.555
- For dark matter-admixed neutron star scenario: DM particle mass m_DM = 2.8 GeV with fraction f_DM = 4.75% reproduces the 2-sigma constraint; DM particles with mass above 1.15 GeV and fraction above 4.2% provide agreement for both stiff and soft baryonic equations of state
- Nuclear matter parameters for the IST equation of state: symmetry energy E_sym = 30.0 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 93.2 MeV, nuclear incompressibility K_0 = 201.0 MeV at normal nuclear density

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Doroshenko (2022), who provided the first simultaneous mass, radius, and surface temperature measurement of HESS J1731-347, and addresses the tension with supernova simulations by Suwa (2018) that predict a minimum neutron star gravitational mass of 1.17 solar masses. The study builds on previous investigations of HESS J1731-347 as a strange quark star candidate by Di Clemente (2022), Horvath (2023), and Das (2023), and on dark matter-admixed neutron star models by Ivanytskyi (2019), Sagun (2021), and Giangrandi (2022). The results enable future observational campaigns to distinguish between these scenarios by reducing uncertainties in distance, atmosphere composition, and surface temperature measurements, and will provide stringent constraints on strongly interacting matter in the 1-2 nuclear saturation density range.
