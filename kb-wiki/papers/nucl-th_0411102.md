# Parity nonconserving observables in thermal neutron capture on a proton

> We calculate parity nonconserving observables in the processes where a neutron is captured on a proton at the threshold energy radiating a photon. Various potential models such as Paris, Bonn and Argonne $v18$ are used for the strong interactions, and the meson-exchange description is employed for the weak interactions between hadrons. The photon polarization $P_γ$ in the unpolarized neutron capture process and photon asymmetry $A_γ$ in the polarized neutron capture process are obtained in terms of the weak meson-nucleon coupling constants. $A_γ$ turns out to be basically insensitive to the employed strong interaction models and thus can be uniquely determined in terms of the weak coupling constants, but $P_γ$ depends significantly on the strong interaction models.

## Key claim

The photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in polarized neutron capture on a proton is essentially insensitive to the choice of strong interaction model (Paris, Bonn, Argonne \(v18\)), making it a clean probe of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h^1_\pi\); however, the circular photon polarization \(P_\gamma\) in unpolarized capture depends significantly on the strong interaction model, particularly through the short-range behavior of the \(^1P_1\) channel.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the \(n+p\) system at threshold using the Paris, Bonn, Bonn-B, and Argonne \(v18\) potentials for the strong interaction, and the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange potential for the weak parity-nonconserving (PNC) interaction. Parity-admixed wave functions were constructed by including \(^3\tilde{P}_0\), \(^3\tilde{P}_1\), and \(^1\tilde{P}_1\) components generated by the weak potential acting on the dominant \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) states. The isovector M1 transition amplitude and the PNC E1 amplitudes were computed using one-body spin current and Siegert's theorem, respectively, and combined to obtain \(A_\gamma\) and \(P_\gamma\).

## Key numbers

- For the Bonn, Bonn-B, and Argonne \(v18\) potentials, \(A_\gamma = -0.117\,h^1_\pi - 0.001\,h^1_\rho + 0.002\,h^1_\omega\) (coefficients in units of \(10^{-7}\) for the coupling constants); the best value (using DDH "best" couplings) is \(-5.41 \times 10^{-8}\) for these three models.
- For the Paris potential, \(A_\gamma = -0.148\,h^1_\pi - 0.001\,h^1_\rho + 0.003\,h^1_\omega\), with a best value of \(-6.85 \times 10^{-8}\).
- The isovector M1 amplitude \(M^{3s1}_{1s0}\) is 0.184 for Paris and 0.233 for Argonne \(v18\) and Bonn potentials.
- For \(P_\gamma\), the best values (in units of \(10^{-8}\)) are: Paris 2.88, Bonn 12.01, Bonn-B 5.21, Argonne \(v18\) 2.64.
- The experimental value of \(A_\gamma\) is \(-(1.5 \pm 4.8) \times 10^{-8}\) (ILL 1988); the experimental value of \(P_\gamma\) is \((1.8 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-7}\).
- Using \(h^1_\pi\) from the \(^{18}\)F experiment gives \(A_\gamma = -1.52 \times 10^{-8}\); using \(h^1_\pi\) from the \(^{133}\)Cs anapole moment gives \(A_\gamma = -11.1 \times 10^{-8}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations from the 1970s (Desplanques 1975) that used older potentials (Hamada-Johnston, Reid-soft-core, Tourreil-Sprung) by employing modern high-precision potentials (Paris, Bonn, Argonne \(v18\)). It confirms the model-independent nature of \(A_\gamma\) found in those earlier studies, while revealing a much stronger model dependence for \(P_\gamma\) than previously appreciated. The results enable more precise extraction of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h^1_\pi\) from ongoing experiments at LANSCE (aiming at \(10^{-9}\) accuracy for \(A_\gamma\)) and from future measurements of the inverse process \(\vec{\gamma} + d \rightarrow n + p\), which is equivalent to \(P_\gamma\). A recent calculation by Schiavilla et al. (2004) including exchange currents gives \(A_\gamma = -4.98 \times 10^{-8}\), about 9% smaller than the present Bonn/Argonne \(v18\) result.
