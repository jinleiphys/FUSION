# Influence of the Pauli principle on two-cluster potential energy

> We study effects of the Pauli principle on the potential energy of two-cluster systems. The object of the investigation is the lightest nuclei of p-shell with a dominant $α$-cluster channel. For this aim we construct matrix elements of two-cluster potential energy between cluster oscillator functions with and without full antisymmetrization. Eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the potential energy matrix are studied in detail. Eigenfunctions of the potential energy operator are presented in oscillator, coordinate and momentum spaces. We demonstrate that the Pauli principle affects more strongly the eigenfunctions than the eigenvalues of the matrix and leads to the formation of resonance and trapped states.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle affects the eigenfunctions of the two-cluster potential energy operator much more strongly than its eigenvalues, and it leads to the formation of resonance and trapped states that are absent in the folding approximation which neglects antisymmetrization.

## Method

The authors employed an algebraic version of the resonating group method, constructing matrix elements of the two-cluster potential energy between cluster oscillator functions both with and without full antisymmetrization. They diagonalized the potential energy matrix to obtain eigenvalues and eigenfunctions, then analyzed the eigenfunctions in oscillator, coordinate, and momentum representations. The study covered six light p-shell nuclei (5He, 5Li, 6Li, 7Li, 7Be, 8Be) treated as two-cluster systems composed of an alpha particle and an s-shell nucleus, using three different nucleon-nucleon potentials (Volkov N2, modified Hasegawa-Nagata, and Minnesota).

## Key numbers

- Oscillator length b values ranged from 1.28 fm (5He, 5Li, 8Be with Minnesota potential) to 1.46 fm (6Li with Volkov N2 potential)
- For the 1- state of 7Be with the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential, the first 50 expansion coefficients Un^alpha of the exact potential eigenfunctions are suppressed to zero due to the Pauli principle
- For the 0+ state in 8Be with the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential, eigenfunctions phi_alpha(p) vanish in a large range of 0 < p < 10 fm^-1
- A "trapped" state appears in the Volkov N2 potential for cluster configurations with norm kernel eigenvalues Lambda_n > 1, occurring in states of normal parity in 5He, 5,6,7Li, and 7Be
- The Minnesota potential also produces a "trapped" state in 6Li

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for studying potential energy of two-cluster systems in discrete representation (Lashko 2019) and builds on investigations of the Pauli principle's influence on kinetic energy of relative motion (Filippov 2004, 2005; Lashko 2008, 2009). The results enable a quantitative comparison between exact nonlocal cluster-cluster potentials and the folding approximation commonly used in cluster models, showing that the folding approximation misses resonance and trapped states that arise from proper antisymmetrization.
