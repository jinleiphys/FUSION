# Taming nuclear size and shape effects in superallowed beta-decay

> We present the first combined analysis of the statistical rate function f in superallowed beta decays with ab initio calculations and data. We focus on C10 to 10B, 14O to 14N and 26mAl to 26Mg, all of which are important channels for the precise determination of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix element Vud. Nuclear charge form factors are obtained by combining experimental data on nuclear charge radii and theory calculations of ratios of moments with the in-medium similarity renormalization group, while the beta decay form factors are derived from exact isospin relations. This enables a rigorous study of the nuclear shape dependence in the statistical rate function f and the quantification of its uncertainties from both experiment and theory. The calculation leads to a more precise test for the first-row CKM unitarity with reduced theoretical uncertainties. This work demonstrates a reliable strategy for combining nuclear many-body calculations with high-precision nuclear data to describe beta decays at tree level for precision tests of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the statistical rate function \(f\) in superallowed beta decays becomes essentially model-independent once the three lowest charge-density moments \(\langle r^{2,4,6}\rangle\) are fixed, and that combining experimental charge radii with *ab initio* calculations of dimensionless moment ratios (V-factors) reduces nuclear-size uncertainties in \(f\) to below the 0.01% level, provided at least one charge radius in the isotriplet is experimentally known.

## Method

The authors combine experimental nuclear charge radii (primarily from muonic atoms) with *ab initio* valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations of charge form factors at low momentum transfer, using three different chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (1.8/2.0 (EM), 1.8/2.0 (EM7.5), and \(\Delta\)NNLO\(_\mathrm{GO}\)). They extract dimensionless moment ratios \(V_{2\,2n} \equiv \langle r^2\rangle^{1/2}/\langle r^{2n}\rangle^{1/2n}\) from the *ab initio* form factors, then combine these with experimental \(\langle r^2\rangle\) to obtain \(\langle r^{4,6}\rangle\). The charged weak density \(\rho_{\text{cw}}\) is obtained from the charge densities via exact isospin relations, and the statistical rate function \(f\) is computed by solving the Dirac equation for the positron in the static Coulomb potential.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{10}\text{C}\rightarrow {}^{10}\text{B}^*\): \(f = 2.30150(71)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}(43)_{\text{scr}}(6)_{\text{den}}\), with the density uncertainty (den) dominated by charge radii and the V-factor contribution at \(\mathcal{O}(0.001\%)\) or smaller.
* For \(^{14}\text{O}\rightarrow {}^{14}\text{N}^*\): \(f = 42.8010(77)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}(63)_{\text{scr}}(8)_{\text{den}}\).
* For \(^{26\mathrm{m}}\text{Al}\rightarrow {}^{26}\text{Mg}\): \(f = 478.029(101)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}(82)_{\text{scr}}(40)_{\text{den}}\).
* The difference in \(f\) between a three-parameter Fermi and a three-parameter exponential charge density model sharing the same \(\langle r^{2,4,6}\rangle\) is \(\le 0.001\%\) for both \(^{10}\text{C}\) (2.30160 vs. 2.30161) and \(^{26\mathrm{m}}\text{Al}\) (478.027 vs. 478.034).
* The updated \(|V_{ud}|\) from \(^{26\mathrm{m}}\text{Al}\) alone is 0.97394(27), implying \(\Delta_\text{CKM} = \{-1.58(58), -0.79(60)\} \times 10^{-3}\) when combined with \(V_{us}\) from semileptonic and leptonic kaon decays, corresponding to unitarity tensions of 2.7\(\sigma\) and 1.3\(\sigma\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the data-driven reanalysis of superallowed beta decays by Gorchtein et al. (2025) and Seng et al. (2022, 2023) by incorporating *ab initio* calculations to replace uncontrolled assumptions about nuclear charge density shapes. It builds on the finding by Yerokhin et al. (2025) that fixing three lowest moments makes observables in highly-charged ions largely model-independent, and applies this insight to the statistical rate function \(f\). The method applies to 21 of the 23 measured superallowed transitions and enables a more precise test of first-row CKM unitarity, while also opening directions for testing isospin symmetry (particularly in the \(A=26\) isotriplet) and for complementing the growing effective field theory program for electromagnetic effects in beta decays by Hill et al. (2023) and Crosas et al. (2025).
