# Helical Separation Effect and helical heat transport for Dirac fermions

> An ensemble of massless fermions can be characterized by its total helicity charge given by the sum of axial charges of particles minus the sum of axial charges of antiparticles. We show that charged massless fermions develop a dissipationless flow of helicity along the background magnetic field. We dub this transport phenomenon as the Helical Separation Effect (HSE). Contrary to its chiral cousin, the Chiral Separation Effect, the HSE produces the helical current in a neutral plasma in which all chemical potentials vanish. In addition, we uncover the Helical Magnetic Heat Effect which generates a heat flux of Dirac fermions along the magnetic field in the presence of non-vanishing helical charge density. We also discuss possible hydrodynamic modes associated with the HSE in neutral plasma.

## Key claim

The authors claim that charged massless fermions in a neutral plasma (with all chemical potentials vanishing) develop a dissipationless flow of helicity along a background magnetic field, an effect they call the Helical Separation Effect (HSE). The key qualifier is that this effect occurs specifically in neutral matter where the vector, axial, and helical chemical potentials are all zero, unlike the Chiral Separation Effect which requires a non-zero vector chemical potential.

## Method

The authors construct common eigenmodes of the Hamiltonian and helicity operator for Dirac fermions in a constant, uniform magnetic field, solving the Dirac equation for both the lowest Landau level and higher Landau levels. They then develop a finite-temperature field theory at finite helical density using second quantization, computing the expectation values of the vector, axial, and helical currents in the background magnetic field. The helical current and heat current are derived from the thermodynamic ensemble using the mode solutions and the Fermi-Dirac distribution with chemical potentials for vector, axial, and helical charges.

## Key numbers

* The Helical Separation Effect current is given by \( \mathbf{j}_H = \frac{T \ln 2}{\pi^2} q \mathbf{B} \) for neutral matter with \( \mu_V = \mu_A = \mu_H = 0 \), where the helical magnetic conductivity is proportional to temperature \( T \).
* The Helical Magnetic Heat Effect produces a heat flux \( \mathbf{j}_\epsilon = \frac{\mu_H T \ln 2}{\pi^2} q \mathbf{B} \) in the strong field limit \( |qB| \gg T^2 \), with the heat current proportional to the first power of temperature \( T \).
* The thermodynamic factor \( \kappa_\beta(\boldsymbol{\mu}) \) in the generalized HSE formula is normalized as \( \kappa_\beta(\mathbf{0}) = 1 \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies on fermion helicity in vortical backgrounds (Ambrus 2019, 2020) and introduces a magnetic-field counterpart to the helical vortical effects. The HSE is presented as a complement to the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) discovered by Son and Zhitnitsky (2004), but unlike the CSE, the HSE operates in neutral plasmas with vanishing chemical potentials. The Helical Magnetic Heat Effect contrasts with the \( T^2 \) heat current produced by the mixed axial-gravitational anomaly studied by Landsteiner, Megias, and Pena-Benitez (2011), as it is proportional to the first power of temperature. The results enable the study of new hydrodynamic modes in neutral plasmas, though the authors find that the helical degree of freedom does not generate a new hydrodynamic wave when all chemical potentials vanish.
