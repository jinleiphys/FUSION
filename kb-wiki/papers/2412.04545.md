# Ab initio calculations of overlap integrals for $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei

> The rate for $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei is set to provide the most stringent test of lepton-flavor symmetry and a window into physics beyond the Standard Model. However, to disentangle new lepton-flavor-violating interactions, in combination with information from $μ\to eγ$ and $μ\to 3e$, it is critical that uncertainties at each step of the analysis be controlled and fully quantified. In this regard, nuclear response functions related to the coupling to neutrons are notoriously problematic, since they are not directly constrained by experiment. We address these shortcomings by combining ab initio calculations with a recently improved determination of charge distributions from electron scattering by exploiting strong correlations among charge, point-proton, and point-neutron radii and densities. We present overlap integrals for $^{27}$Al, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{48}$Ti including full covariance matrices, allowing, for the first time, for a comprehensive consideration of nuclear structure uncertainties in the interpretation of $μ\to e$ experiments.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that strong linear correlations exist between charge radii and both proton and neutron overlap integrals for muon-to-electron conversion in nuclei, enabling the first fully quantified nuclear structure uncertainties for these integrals. The key hedge is that the correlations for neutron responses, while still strong, are "slightly more uncertain" than those for proton responses, and the final uncertainties depend on the relative size of propagated input uncertainties versus fit residuals.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio calculations using the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) with a large ensemble of 42 chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, varying in truncation order, regularization cutoff, and fitting strategy. They computed overlap integrals for the scalar, vector, and dipole responses for 27Al, 48Ca, and 48Ti, then exploited the observed linear correlations between these integrals and the charge radius squared to extract final values with uncertainties, using charge densities from a recent improved extraction from elastic electron scattering as reference.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kitano (2002) for overlap integrals by providing the first fully quantified nuclear structure uncertainties including full covariance matrices, addressing a shortcoming previously identified in the field. It builds on the improved charge distribution extraction from electron scattering by Noel (2024) and uses ab initio methods similar to those applied by Hagen (2015) for nuclear structure correlations. The results enable robust propagation of nuclear uncertainties in the interpretation of upcoming muon-to-electron conversion experiments Mu2e and COMET, and can also improve calculations for parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as Qweak and CREX, coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering, and dark matter direct detection.
