# Rotating Synchrotron Radiation (RoSyRa): photon emission from magnetized and rotating quark-gluon plasma

> This paper investigates the production of non-prompt photons originating from rotating synchrotron radiation (RoSyRa), specifically the emission of photons by a rigidly rotating quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium, in the presence of an external magnetic field. We compute the non-prompt photon spectrum and its elliptic flow ($v_2$) at mid-rapidity. In particular, we investigate the finite volume effects. We find that at low transverse momentum, the magnetic field induces a significant $v_2$, while the plasma rotation boosts the synchrotron radiation of negatively charged quarks. These effects account for both the observed excess of direct photons and their elliptic flow, contributing to the resolution of the "direct photon puzzle".

## Key claim

The paper proposes that rotating synchrotron radiation (RoSyRa) from a rigidly rotating quark-gluon plasma in a magnetic field can account for both the observed excess of direct photons at low transverse momentum and their unexpectedly large elliptic flow \(v_2\), but the authors emphasize that their results are obtained under idealized stationary conditions (constant homogeneous magnetic field and uniform rotation lasting 10 fm/c) and that comparisons to experimental data remain qualitative.

## Method

The authors compute the photon emission rate from the process \(q \to q + \gamma\) using exact solutions of the Dirac equation in a rotating reference frame with a constant magnetic field aligned along the rotation axis, employing Chandrasekhar-Kendall photon wave functions. The differential photon spectrum and elliptic flow \(v_2\) at midrapidity are obtained by summing and averaging over initial and final quark states using Fermi-Dirac thermal distributions, modeling the plasma as a rigidly rotating cylinder of radius \(R\) and height \(L\) with constant temperature \(T\) and angular velocity \(\Omega\).

## Key numbers

- Photon yields and \(v_2\) are computed for parameters \(eB = 18000\ \text{MeV}^2 \simeq m_\pi^2\), \(\Omega = 2\ \text{MeV}\), \(T = 200\ \text{MeV}\), \(R = 5\ \text{fm}\), \(L = 10\ \text{fm}\), and plasma lifetime \(\Delta t = 10\ \text{fm}/c\), compared to PHENIX Au-Au data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\ \text{GeV}\) for the 20%-40% centrality class.
- A second parameter set with \(eB = 18000\ \text{MeV}^2\), \(\Omega = 3\ \text{MeV}\), \(T = 300\ \text{MeV}\), \(R = 10\ \text{fm}\), \(L = 10\ \text{fm}\), \(\Delta t = 10\ \text{fm}/c\) shows an excess of photon production compared to experimental data.
- The effective synchrotron frequency for negatively charged quarks is \(\omega_{\text{Eff}} = |q eB|/E + \Omega\), while for positively charged quarks it is \(\omega_{\text{Eff}} = |q eB|/E - \Omega\), leading to enhancement of radiation from negative charges and suppression from positive charges.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical framework for synchrotron radiation in rotating magnetized plasmas (Buzzegoli 2022, Buzzegoli 2023) and addresses the "direct photon puzzle" identified by the PHENIX Collaboration (2012, 2014, 2016), where conventional hydrodynamic and transport models (Shen 2013, Chatterjee 2013, van Hees 2011) underpredict the observed photon yield and \(v_2\). The RoSyRa mechanism complements the multimessenger model (Gale 2021) by adding a previously neglected contribution, and the results suggest that future time-integrated calculations incorporating realistic decaying magnetic fields and nonuniform rotation could further resolve the puzzle.
