# Signature of smooth transition from diabatic to adiabatic states in heavy-ion fusion reactions at deep subbarrier energies

> We propose a novel extension of the standard coupled-channels framework for heavy-ion reactions in order to analyze fusion reactions at deep subbarrier incident energies. This extension simulates a smooth transition between the diabatic two-body and the adiabatic one-body states. To this end, we damp gradually the off-diagonal part of the coupling potential, for which the position of the onset of the damping varies for each eigen channel. We show that this model accounts well for the steep falloff of the fusion cross sections for the $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb, $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni reactions.

# Signature of smooth transition from diabatic to adiabatic states in heavy-ion fusion reactions at deep subbarrier energies

## Key claim

The authors propose that a smooth transition from diabatic two-body states to adiabatic one-body states, simulated by gradually damping the off-diagonal part of the coupling potential with a channel-dependent onset position, can account for the steep falloff (fusion hindrance) observed in deep subbarrier fusion cross sections for several heavy-ion systems.

## Method

The authors extend the standard coupled-channels (CC) framework by introducing a damping factor that gradually reduces the strength of nuclear coupling form factors inside the touching point, with the onset of damping varying for each eigen channel. They employ the Yukawa-plus-exponential (YPE) potential as the basic ion-ion potential and use the incoming wave boundary condition to simulate compound nucleus formation. The model is implemented in the computer code ccfull and applied to the 16O+208Pb, 64Ni+64Ni, and 58Ni+58Ni systems.

## Key numbers

- For the 64Ni+64Ni system: damping radius parameter r_d = 1.298 fm and damping diffuseness parameter a_d = 1.05 fm; YPE radius parameter r_0 = 1.205 fm
- For the 16O+208Pb system: damping radius parameter r_d = 1.280 fm and damping diffuseness parameter a_d = 1.28 fm; YPE radius parameter r_0 = 1.202 fm
- For the 58Ni+58Ni system: damping radius parameter r_d = 1.3 fm and damping diffuseness parameter a_d = 1.3 fm
- For the 64Ni+64Ni system, the logarithmic derivative with damping becomes saturated below E_cm = 86 MeV
- The Sommerfeld parameter eta is shifted by 75.23 for the 64Ni+64Ni system and by 49.0 for the 16O+208Pb system in the S-factor calculation

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic model previously proposed by Ichikawa et al. (2007) by replacing the sudden semi-classical treatment with a full quantum mechanical approach that smoothly joins the two-body CC treatment to the adiabatic one-body potential. It addresses the fusion hindrance phenomenon observed in deep subbarrier reactions, which was also studied by Misu and Esbensen (2006, 2007) using a sudden picture with a repulsive core from Pauli exclusion, and by Dasgupta et al. (2007) using quantum decoherence. The model achieves simultaneous reproduction of fusion cross sections, astrophysical S-factors, and logarithmic derivatives for multiple systems, and predicts a weaker energy dependence of the S-factor at extremely low energies compared to the sudden model of Misu and Esbensen.
