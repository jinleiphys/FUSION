# On a gauge-invariant interaction of spin-$\fth$ resonances

> We show that the gauge-invariant coupling suggested by Pascalutsa removes non-pole terms from a spin-$\fth$ propagator only for a specific choice of free parameter. For the general case the problem can be solved by including higher order derivatives of spin-$\fth$ fields or by modifying the original coupling. In the latter case the obtained Lagrangian depends on one free parameter pointing to the freedom in choosing an 'off-shell' content of the theory. However, the physical observables must not be affected by the 'off-shell' contributions and should not depend on the free parameter of the Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant coupling for spin-3/2 fields proposed by Pascalutsa removes non-pole (off-shell) contributions from the propagator only for the special Rarita-Schwinger choice of the free parameter A = -1; for general values of A the problem persists, but can be solved either by including higher-order derivatives of the spin-3/2 field or by modifying the original coupling with a parameter-dependent vertex operator.

## Method

The authors start from the general free Lagrangian for a spin-3/2 field, which contains one free parameter A (with the Rarita-Schwinger case corresponding to A = -1). They compute the s- or u-channel tree-level pi N scattering amplitude using the Pascalutsa gauge-invariant Delta N pi coupling and the general spin-3/2 propagator, showing that an unwanted spin-1/2 off-shell term survives for A not equal to -1. They then propose two alternative couplings: one using a projection operator with higher derivatives, and another that generalizes the Pascalutsa vertex by introducing a parameter-dependent tensor theta_{sigma eta}(A) = g_{sigma eta} - ((A+1)/2) gamma_sigma gamma_eta, which reduces to the Pascalutsa form at A = -1.

## Key numbers

- The free spin-3/2 propagator contains a free parameter A subject to the restriction A not equal to -1/2.
- The Rarita-Schwinger propagator corresponds to the special choice A = -1.
- The modified vertex operator includes the factor theta_{sigma eta}(A) = g_{sigma eta} - ((A+1)/2) gamma_sigma gamma_eta.
- The generalized electromagnetic coupling involves coupling constants g_M, g_E, and g_C, with the overall factor 3ie/(2 M_N (M_N + M_Delta)).

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant coupling approach for spin-3/2 fields developed by Pascalutsa (2000, 1998, 1999) and addresses a limitation: the original Pascalutsa coupling removes off-shell degrees of freedom only for the Rarita-Schwinger propagator (A = -1). The paper builds on the general spin-3/2 formalism reviewed by Benmerrouche (1989) and the projection operator decomposition of Van Nieuwenhuizen (1981). The results enable consistent treatment of Delta(1232) resonance interactions in pi N scattering and electromagnetic couplings without dependence on the off-shell parameter A, which is important for phenomenological models of baryon resonances.
