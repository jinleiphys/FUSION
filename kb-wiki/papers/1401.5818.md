# Neutrino Mixing and Oscillations in Astrophysical Environments

> A brief review of the current status of neutrino mixing and oscillations in astrophysical environments, with particular emphasis on the Sun and core-collapse supernovae, is given. Implications of the existence of sterile states which mix with the active neutrinos are discussed.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the current status of neutrino mixing and oscillations in astrophysical environments, particularly the Sun and core-collapse supernovae, while noting that the hierarchy of neutrino masses, the values of CP-violating phases, and the possible existence of sterile neutrinos remain open questions.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from solar neutrino detectors (SNO, SuperKamiokande, Borexino, KamLAND) and discusses the MSW effect in matter, including modifications for polarized backgrounds and density fluctuations. For supernova neutrinos, it describes collective oscillations driven by neutrino-neutrino interactions, the quantum kinetic equations governing flavor evolution, and the potential role of sterile neutrinos in explaining experimental anomalies and astrophysical phenomena.

## Key numbers

- Solar electron-neutrino survival probability is doubly degenerate in $\delta m^2_{21}$: one solution with $\delta m^2 \sim 7 \times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ (LMA) and another with $\delta m^2 \sim 10^{-7}$ eV$^2$ (LOW)
- Borexino measured day-night asymmetry for $E_{\nu} = 0.86$ MeV $^7$Be line neutrinos as A = 0.001 $\pm$ 0.012 (stat) $\pm$ 0.007 (syst), completely ruling out the LOW region
- Solar density fluctuations constrained to less than $\beta = 0.05$ at 70% confidence level from combined solar neutrino and KamLAND data
- Reactor antineutrino anomaly can be fitted with sterile neutrino states having mass splittings of order $\sim 1$ eV$^2$ and oscillation lengths of a few meters
- LSND and MiniBooNE interpretation in terms of active-sterile oscillations with $\sim 1$ eV$^2$ mass-difference scale requires mixing angle $\sin^2 2\theta \sim 10^{-2}-10^{-3}$
- Current best bound on neutrino magnetic moment from GEMMA spectrometer: $\mu_{\nu} < 2.9 \times 10^{-11} \mu_B$ at 90% C.L.
- Astrophysical limit from red-giant branch in globular cluster M5: $\mu_{\nu} < 4.5 \times 10^{-12} \mu_B$ (95% CL)
- A sterile state with mass $\sim 3.5$ MeV with a large enough radiative decay width could provide a solution to the $^7$Li abundance problem in Big Bang nucleosynthesis

## Context

This review extends the discussion of neutrino oscillations in astrophysical environments from earlier work by Wolfenstein (1977) and Mikheev and Smirnov (1986) on the MSW effect, and builds on recent reviews of collective neutrino oscillations by Duan (2010), Raffelt (2010), and others. The paper connects neutrino physics to solar modeling (Adelberger 1998, 2010; Asplund 2009; Serenelli 2011) and supernova nucleosynthesis, while also addressing the reactor antineutrino anomaly (Mention 2011) and its possible interpretation with sterile neutrinos. The discussion of sterile neutrino decay as a solution to the $^7$Li problem (Kusakabe 2013) points toward future experimental and observational tests.
