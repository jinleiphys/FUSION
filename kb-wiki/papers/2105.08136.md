# $α$-like quartetting in the excited states of proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonians

> Previous studies have shown that the ground state of systems of nucleons composed by an equal number of protons and neutrons interacting via proton-neutron pairing forces can be described accurately by a condensate of $α$-like quartets. Here we extend these studies to the low-lowing excited states of these systems and show that these states can be accurately described by breaking a quartet from the ground state condensate and replacing it with an "excited" quartet. # α-like quartetting in the excited states of proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonians

## Key claim

The authors show that low-lying excited states of N=Z systems with proton-neutron pairing interactions can be accurately described by a one-broken-quartet approximation, removing one α-like quartet from the ground-state condensate and replacing it with an "excited" quartet, but this description is limited to those excited states that can be formed by this procedure, with some exact eigenstates (e.g., certain intruder states in weak coupling) not reproduced.

## Method

The authors extend the quartet condensation model (QCM) to excited states by constructing trial states of the form (excited quartet) × (ground-state quartet condensate)^(n_q−1). For isovector pairing Hamiltonians, they test two levels of approximation: (A) where the ground-state quartets are general superpositions of two isovector pairs coupled to T=0, and (B) where the quartets are built from a single collective isovector pair. For isovector-isoscalar Hamiltonians, the ground-state condensate includes both isovector and isoscalar pairs, while the excited quartet is the most general T=0, J=J_z combination of two protons and two neutrons. The coefficients defining the excited quartet are obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the space of non-orthogonal basis states formed by replacing one quartet.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N=Z=6 particles on 6 equidistant levels with state-independent isovector pairing (strength g in units of level spacing Δε=2), the approximation (A) works well for all pairing strengths from weak to strong coupling; overlaps between exact and approximate eigenstates at g=1.0 are very large (shown in Fig. 2, with numerical values not explicitly given in text).
- For the same system at g=1.0, approximation (B) reproduces 5 low-lying excited states; for g ≳ 0.8 these coincide with the 5 lowest exact excited states, while for smaller g an intruder exact eigenstate crosses these states and is not reproduced.
- For ²⁸Si (N=Z=6 in the sd-shell) with an isovector pairing force extracted from the USDB interaction, the QCM approximation (17) reproduces the exact low-lying T=0 spectrum with overlaps close to unity for all low-lying states (Fig. 3, energies in MeV).
- For ²⁸Si with an isovector-isoscalar pairing force from the USDB interaction (J=0,T=1 and J=1,T=0 channels), the QCM approximation (24) shows good overall agreement; the first excited J=6 state has no corresponding QCM state, while the second J=6 exact state is well reproduced (Fig. 4, energies in MeV).

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation model (QCM) previously developed by the same authors for ground states of proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonians (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2020; Sambataro et al., 2017) to excited states. The one-broken-quartet approximation is analogous to the one-broken-pair approximation used for like-particle pairing systems (Talmi, 1993). The approach is tested against exact diagonalization for both schematic isovector Hamiltonians and realistic interactions (USDB) for ²⁸Si. The results demonstrate that α-like quartets are relevant degrees of freedom not only for ground states but also for excited states, and the method provides an interpretable tool for constructing approximate spectra of proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonians.
