# Single-neutron knockout from $^{20}\textrm{C}$ and the structure of $^{19}\textrm{C}$

> The low-lying unbound level structure of the halo nucleus $^{19}\textrm{C}$ has been investigated using single-neutron knockout from $^{20}\textrm{C}$ on a carbon target at 280 MeV/nucleon. The invariant mass spectrum, derived from the momenta of the forward going beam velocity $^{18}\textrm{C}$ fragment and neutrons, was found to be dominated by a very narrow near threshold ($E_\textrm{rel}$ = 0.036(1) MeV) peak. Two less strongly populated resonance-like features were also observed at $E_\textrm{rel}$ = 0.84(4) and 2.31(3) MeV, both of which exhibit characteristics consistent with neutron $p$-shell hole states. Comparisons of the energies, measured cross sections and parallel momentum distributions to the results of shell-model and eikonal reaction calculations lead to spin-parity assignments of $5/2^+_1$ and $1/2^-_1$ for the levels at $E_x$ = 0.62(9) and 2.89(10) MeV with $S_n$ = 0.58(9) MeV. Spectroscopic factors were also deduced and found to be in reasonable accord with shell-model calculations. The valence neutron configuration of the $^{20}\textrm{C}$ ground state is thus seen to include, in addition to the known $1s^2_{1/2}$ component, a significant $0d^2_{5/2}$ contribution. The level scheme of $^{19}\textrm{C}$, including significantly the $1/2^-_1$ cross-shell state, is well accounted for by the YSOX shell-model interaction developed from the monopole-based universal interaction.

## Key claim
The low-lying unbound level structure of the halo nucleus 19C was investigated via single-neutron knockout from 20C, with the invariant mass spectrum dominated by a very narrow near-threshold peak at E_rel = 0.036(1) MeV, and the valence neutron configuration of the 20C ground state includes a significant 0d²_5/2 component in addition to the known 1s²_1/2 component.

## Method
Single-neutron knockout from 20C on a carbon target at 280 MeV/nucleon was performed at the RIKEN RIBF facility. The invariant mass spectrum was reconstructed from the four-momenta of the forward-going beam-velocity 18C fragment and the decay neutron, detected using the SAMURAI spectrometer and NEBULA neutron array. Three single-level R-matrix lineshapes convoluted with the experimental resolution function and a Maxwellian-like continuum background distribution were fitted to the relative energy spectrum, and longitudinal momentum distributions were compared with eikonal reaction model calculations using the MOMDIS code.

## Key numbers
- Dominant resonance at E_rel = 0.036(1) MeV, corresponding to E_x = 0.62(9) MeV with S_n = 0.58(9) MeV, assigned as 5/2⁺_1 state with ℓ = 2 and cross section σ_{-1n} = 61(5) mb
- Second resonance at E_rel = 0.84(4) MeV with ℓ = 1 character and cross section σ_{-1n} = 4(1) mb
- Third resonance at E_rel = 2.31(3) MeV, corresponding to E_x = 2.89(10) MeV, assigned as 1/2⁻_1 state with ℓ = 1, width Γ = 0.20(7) MeV, and cross section σ_{-1n} = 15(3) mb
- Spectroscopic factor C²S_exp = 2.40(20) for the 5/2⁺_1 state and C²S_exp = 0.77(15) for the 1/2⁻_1 state
- Experimental resolution for momentum distributions σ ≈ 28 MeV/c in the beam rest frame

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 19C structure including in-beam γ-ray spectroscopy by Elekes (2005) and Stanoiu (2008), invariant mass measurements via (p,p') by Satou (2008), and inclusive two-neutron removal from 20C by Ozawa (2011) and Kobayashi (2012). The results confirm the unbound character of the 5/2⁺_1 state suggested by earlier work and provide the first observation of the 1/2⁻_1 cross-shell state in 19C. The YSOX shell-model interaction developed from the monopole-based universal interaction (VMU) by Otsuka (2010) best accounts for the observed level scheme, enabling tests of cross-shell components in neutron-rich p-sd-shell nuclei.
