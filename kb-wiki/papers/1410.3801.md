# Vector form factor of the pion in chiral effective field theory

> The vector form factor of the pion is calculated in the framework of chiral effective field theory with vector mesons included as dynamical degrees of freedom. To construct an effective field theory with a consistent power counting, the complex-mass scheme is applied.

## Key claim
The vector form factor of the pion can be described up to \(q^2\sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) using chiral effective field theory with vector mesons as dynamical degrees of freedom, provided the complex-mass scheme is used to maintain a consistent power counting; however, to describe data from \(e^+ e^-\to \pi^+ \pi^-\) (as opposed to \(\tau\) decay), the \(\rho^0\)-\(\omega\)-\(\gamma\) mixing must be included, and this mixing is only treated at tree level.

## Method
The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian including pions and \(\rho\) mesons, applied the complex-mass scheme (CMS) for renormalization, and computed the pion vector form factor at one-loop order. They performed simultaneous fits of the coupling constants and complex mixing parameters \(\Pi_{\rho\gamma}\) and \(\Pi_{\rho\omega}\) to \(\tau\) decay data (Fujikawa et al., 2008) and \(e^+ e^-\) scattering data (KLOE Collaboration, 2011), using the KSRF relation to eliminate the coupling \(g\) and setting \(D_x=0\).

## Key numbers
- Fit range: \(q^2\) up to 1.125 GeV\(^2\) for \(\tau\) decay data and up to 0.845 GeV\(^2\) for \(e^+ e^-\) data.
- Fit 1 (fixed \(\omega\) mass): \(\rho\)-meson pole mass \(M_\rho = 0.7621(3)\) GeV, width \(\Gamma = 0.1421(5)\) GeV, reduced chi-squared \(\chi^2_{\rm red} = 1.54\) (88 dof).
- Fit 2 (floating \(\omega\) mass): \(M_\rho = 0.7622(3)\) GeV, \(\Gamma = 0.1419(5)\) GeV, \(\omega\) mass \(M_\omega = 0.7838(2)\) GeV, reduced chi-squared \(\chi^2_{\rm red} = 1.32\) (88 dof).
- Input values: pion mass \(M_\pi = 0.1395\) GeV, pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 0.0922\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the complex-mass scheme approach previously applied to the \(\rho\) meson mass and width (Djukanovic et al., 2009) and to the Roper resonance (Djukanovic et al., 2010) to the calculation of the pion vector form factor. It addresses the power-counting problem for vector mesons in chiral EFT that had been tackled earlier with the heavy-particle approach (Jenkins et al., 1995; Bijnens et al., 1996-1998). The results enable a systematic EFT description of pion form factor data beyond the low-energy region of standard ChPT, with the next step being a more complete treatment of isospin-symmetry-breaking effects.
