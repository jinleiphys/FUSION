# Spectrum and Dynamics of the BCS-BEC crossover from a few-body perspective

> The spectrum of two spin-up and two spin-down fermions in a trap is calculated using a correlated gaussian basis throughout the range of the BCS-BEC crossover. These accurate calculations provide a few-body solution to the crossover problem. This solution is used to study the time-evolution of the system as the scattering length is changed, mimicking experiments with Fermi gases near Fano-Feshbach resonances. The structure of avoiding crossings in the spectrum allow us to understand the dynamics of the system as a sequence of Landau-Zener transitions. Finally, we propose a ramping scheme to study atom-molecule coherence.

# Spectrum and Dynamics of the BCS-BEC Crossover from a Few-Body Perspective

## Key claim
The spectrum of four trapped fermions (two spin-up, two spin-down) throughout the BCS-BEC crossover can be accurately calculated using a correlated Gaussian basis, and the dynamics of ramping through the resonance can be understood as a sequence of Landau-Zener transitions between a small number of diabatic states. The authors hedge that while the Landau-Zener model shows decent agreement with numerical results, it neglects many possible transitions and the molecule formation fraction does not follow a single Landau-Zener function but a combination of different terms.

## Method
The authors solved the time-independent Schrodinger equation for two spin-up and two spin-down fermions in a harmonic trap using a correlated Gaussian basis set with semi-randomly selected width parameters, with the two-body interaction modeled as a purely attractive Gaussian potential whose depth is tuned to produce the desired scattering length. A diabatization procedure was applied to reduce the system to a tractable number of relevant eigenfunctions, after which the time-dependent Schrodinger equation was solved using the diabatic representation. The Landau-Zener parameters were extracted from the P-matrix elements (nonadiabatic couplings) between adiabatic states, and the dynamics of unidirectional ramps were modeled as a sequence of Landau-Zener transitions between four partially-diabatic states.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy at unitarity: varies from 5.099 hbar omega to 5.027 hbar omega as the Gaussian width d0 is changed from 0.05 a_ho to 0.01 a_ho
- Fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo (FN-DMC) ground state energy at unitarity for a square well potential of range 0.01 a_ho: 5.069(9) hbar omega
- Landau-Zener parameters extracted from P-matrix analysis: kappa_{2,1} approximately 35, kappa_{5,1} approximately 43, kappa_{5,2} approximately 14, kappa_{14,5} approximately 90
- Landau-Zener parameter for molecule formation in the four-body system: kappa_mol^{(4)} approximately 59 plus or minus 6
- Two-body Landau-Zener prediction: kappa_mol^{(2)} approximately 42
- Experimental Landau-Zener parameter for potassium-40: kappa_mol^{exp} approximately 62 plus or minus 15 (from Regal et al. 2003)
- Estimated Landau-Zener parameter for lithium-6: kappa_mol^{exp} approximately 90 (from Strecker et al. 2003)
- Bohr frequencies in atom-molecule coherence oscillations: approximately 28 omega_0 (coherences between states differing in one broken dimer bond) and approximately 57 omega_0 (coherences between states differing in two broken dimer bonds)
- Average density for non-interacting four-particle Fermi gas: rho approximately 0.153 / a_ho^3

## Context
This work extends few-body calculations of trapped fermions beyond the two-body system studied experimentally in optical lattices (Esslinger group) and provides a microscopic perspective on the BCS-BEC crossover that has been explored in ultracold Fermi gas experiments (Regal et al. 2003, Jochim et al. 2003, Zwierlein et al. 2003, Strecker et al. 2003, Bourdel et al. 2004, Kinast et al. 2004). The results enable quantitative predictions for optical lattice experiments with four fermions per site and provide a testbed for Landau-Zener models of molecule formation in Fermi gases. The ground state energies agree with fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo calculations (von Stecher, unpublished), and the dimer-dimer scattering length extracted from the spectrum agrees with the prediction of Petrov et al. (2004). The proposed ramping scheme to study atom-molecule coherence extends previous work on atom-molecule oscillations in condensates (Donley et al. 2002) and quantum beats in Fermi systems near narrow Fano-Feshbach resonances (Andreev et al. 2004).
