# The Middle of the Spectrum

> There has been a great deal of attention to the low lying energy spectum in a nucleus becuse of the abundance of experimetal data. Likewise ,perhaps to a lesser extent but still significant the high end for a given configuratiion has been examined. Here ,using single j shell calculations as a guide we examine the middle part of the spectum resulting form single j shell calculations.Seniority arguments are used to partially explain the midshell behaviours even though in general seniority is not a good quantum number for mixed systems of neutrons and protons.

## Key claim

Using single-j shell calculations as a guide, the middle part of the nuclear spectrum shows a fairly wide gap that separates the lower part of the spectrum from the upper part; this gap can be partially explained by seniority arguments even though seniority is not a good quantum number for mixed systems of neutrons and protons.

## Method

Single-j shell model calculations were performed for even-even nuclei (44Ti, 52Fe, 96Cd) and odd-odd nuclei (44Sc, 52Mn, 96Ag) using several different interactions (INTa, INTb, INTd, Qi, Q.Q, and schematic pairing interactions). The energies of yrast states were computed and the differences between adjacent energy levels were examined to identify gaps in the spectrum. Seniority arguments from the pairing interaction of Flowers and Edmonds-Flowers were used to interpret the observed gaps.

## Key numbers

- For 44Ti with INTa interaction: E(6)-E(4) = 1.2718 MeV, E(8)-E(6) = 2.0244 MeV, E(10)-E(8) = 1.3007 MeV
- For 52Fe with INTb interaction: E(6)-E(4) = 1.4634 MeV, E(8)-E(6) = 1.7830 MeV, E(10)-E(8) = 1.0712 MeV
- For 96Cd with INTd interaction (even I): E(8)-E(6) = 0.0923 MeV, E(10)-E(8) = 1.2373 MeV
- For 96Cd with INTd interaction (odd I): gap between I=9 and I=11 of 1.4384 MeV
- Experimental gaps for 44Ti: E(6)-E(4) = 1.561 MeV, E(8)-E(6) = 2.493 MeV, E(10)-E(8) = 1.163 MeV
- Experimental gaps for 52Fe: E(6)-E(4) = 1.941 MeV, E(8)-E(6) = 2.035 MeV, E(10)-E(8) = 1.021 MeV
- For 52Fe, the 12+ state has a half-life of 45.9 s compared to 2.1 ns for 44Ti
- For the schematic pairing interaction with A=1: E(0)=0, E(I)=1 for I=2,4,6,8, E(I)=2.2 for I=10,12,14,16; for odd I: E(I)=1.4 for I=1,3,5,7,9, E(I)=2.2 for I=11,13,15

## Context

This work extends the author's previous single-j shell studies of the (2j-1) rule for odd-odd nuclei (Zamick, McCullen, and Bayman for the f7/2 shell) to even-even nuclei such as 44Ti and 96Cd. The results are compared with experimental data for nuclei in the f7/2 region (44Ti and 52Fe) where the midshell gap is confirmed qualitatively. The paper uses the pairing interaction formalism of Flowers (1952) and Edmonds and Flowers (1952) to explain the gaps via seniority changes, and notes that similar results are obtained with the interaction of Coraggio et al. The work calls for further experimental study of 96Cd and 96Ag spectra to test whether such gaps exist in heavier nuclei.
