# Fock space dualities

> Several cases of Fock space duality occurring in the theory of many-body systems in general and nuclei in particular are discussed. All of them are special cases of a general duality theorem proved in mathematics by Howe in the 1970s. Dualities on a fermion Fock space between orthogonal Lie algebras and related groups, including an o-Pin duality recently discovered by the author, present a nice, symmetric pattern.

# Fock space dualities

## Key claim
All known Fock space dualities in many-body physics, including the author's recently discovered $\lo$–$\gPin$ duality, are special cases of Howe's general duality theorem from the 1970s, but the $\lo$–$\gPin$ duality can be proven without a $\gPin(n)$ first main theorem (which is not known), using instead a character-comparison method restricted to fermion systems.

## Method
The paper reviews and unifies several Fock space dualities: the $\lgl(2l+1)$–$\lgl(2)$ duality for atomic shells (Wigner–von Neumann, 1928), Helmers's $\lsp(d)$–$\lsp(2k)$ duality (1961), the author's $\lo(d)$–$\lo(2k)$ duality (2019), and the new $\lo(d)$–$\gPin(2k)$ duality. For each, the decomposition of the fermion Fock space into tensor products of irreducible representations is stated, with the pairing of Young diagrams described by a geometric "rectangle-filling" rule. The $\lo(d)$–$\gPin(2k)$ duality is derived from the $\lo(d)$–$\lo(2k)$ duality by constructing a realisation of $\gPin(2k)$ within the Clifford algebra generated by single-kind creation and annihilation operators.

## Key numbers
- For $\lgl(2l+1)$–$\lgl(2)$ duality: the atomic shell Fock space decomposes over Young diagrams $\lambda$ with at most $2l+1$ rows and at most 2 columns.
- For $\lsp(d)$–$\lsp(2k)$ duality (Helmers): the rectangle filled by $\lambda$ and a rotated/reflected copy of $\mu$ has dimensions $k \times d/2$; example given for $d=12$, $k=4$ with $\lambda = (4,3,2,2,1,1)$ and $\mu=(5,4,2)$.
- For $\lo(d)$–$\lo(2k)$ duality: same $k \times d/2$ rectangle; example for $d=11$, $k=4$ with $\lambda = (4,3,2,2,1)$ and $\mu=(9/2,7/2,3/2,1/2)$.
- For $\lo(d)$–$\gPin(2k)$ duality: example for $d=12$, $k=4$ with $\lambda = (4,3,2,2,1,-1)$ and $\mu=(5,4,2,1,1)$.
- For $k=1$, $\lsp(2k)=\lsp(2)$ relates to Kerman's quasispin algebra (an $\lsu(2)$ algebra over the reals).
- For $k=2$, $\lsp(2k)=\lsp(4)$ is identical to $\lo(5)$ (Flowers and Szpikowski, 1964).
- For $k=4$, $\lo(2k)=\lo(8)$ was proposed as a quasispin algebra for $LS$ coupling (Flowers and Szpikowski, 1964).

## Context
This paper extends the line of work on Fock space dualities originating with Wigner and von Neumann (1928) and Helmers (1961), and continues the author's own recent results on $\lo(d)$–$\lo(2k)$ duality (Neergård, 2019). It unifies these with Howe's general duality theorem (1970s) and presents a new $\lo(d)$–$\gPin(2k)$ duality. The results enable a systematic labelling of nuclear many-body states using the representation theory of orthogonal and symplectic groups and their covering groups, with applications to quasispin formalisms (Kerman, 1961; Talmi, 1993), neutron–proton pairing (Flowers and Szpikowski, 1964a,b), and nuclear collective models (Rosensteel and Rowe, 1977).
