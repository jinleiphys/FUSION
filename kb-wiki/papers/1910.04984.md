# The viability of the 3+1 neutrino model in the supernova neutrino process

> Adopting the 3+1 neutrino mixing parameters by the IceCube and shortbase line experiments, we investigate the sterile-active neutrino oscillation effects on the supernova neutrino process. For the sterile neutrino ($őĹ_s$), we study two different luminosity models. First, we presume that the $őĹ_s$ does not interact with other particles through the standard interactions apart from the oscillation with the active neutrinos. Second, we consider that $őĹ_s$ can be directly produced by $őĹ_e$ scattering with matter. In both cases, we find that the pattern of neutrino oscillations can be changed drastically by the $őĹ_s$ in supernova environments. Especially multiple resonances occur, and consequently affect thermal neutrino-induced reaction rates. As a result, $^7$Li, $^7$Be, $^{11}$B, $^{11}$C, $^{92}$Nb, $^{98}$Tc and $^{138}$La yields in the $őĹ$-process are changed. Among those nuclei, $^7$Li and $^{11}$B yields can be constrained by the analysis of observed SiC X grains. Based on the meteoritic data, we conclude that the second model can be allowed while first model is excluded. The viability of the second model depends on the sterile neutrino temperature and the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Key claim
The 3+1 neutrino model with a non-interacting sterile neutrino (null luminosity model) is excluded at the 3ŌÉ level by meteoritic SiC X grain data, while a model where sterile neutrinos are directly produced by őĹ_e scattering with matter can be allowed depending on the sterile neutrino temperature and the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis (Woosley 1989, Heger 2003, Hayakawa 2018) by incorporating the 3+1 sterile neutrino model with parameters from Collin (2016) that reconcile IceCube and short-baseline experiment data. It contradicts the null L_őĹ_s model for sterile neutrinos but allows the direct production model, with viability depending on sterile neutrino temperature and mass hierarchy. The results enable future studies of sterile neutrino decoupling using Boltzmann transport equations to determine the actual sterile neutrino temperature and luminosity, which could provide clues about both sterile neutrino existence and the neutrino mass hierarchy.
