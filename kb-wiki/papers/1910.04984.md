# The viability of the 3+1 neutrino model in the supernova neutrino process

> Adopting the 3+1 neutrino mixing parameters by the IceCube and shortbase line experiments, we investigate the sterile-active neutrino oscillation effects on the supernova neutrino process. For the sterile neutrino ($ν_s$), we study two different luminosity models. First, we presume that the $ν_s$ does not interact with other particles through the standard interactions apart from the oscillation with the active neutrinos. Second, we consider that $ν_s$ can be directly produced by $ν_e$ scattering with matter. In both cases, we find that the pattern of neutrino oscillations can be changed drastically by the $ν_s$ in supernova environments. Especially multiple resonances occur, and consequently affect thermal neutrino-induced reaction rates. As a result, $^7$Li, $^7$Be, $^{11}$B, $^{11}$C, $^{92}$Nb, $^{98}$Tc and $^{138}$La yields in the $ν$-process are changed. Among those nuclei, $^7$Li and $^{11}$B yields can be constrained by the analysis of observed SiC X grains. Based on the meteoritic data, we conclude that the second model can be allowed while first model is excluded. The viability of the second model depends on the sterile neutrino temperature and the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Key claim
The 3+1 neutrino model with a non-interacting sterile neutrino (null luminosity model) is excluded at the 3σ level by meteoritic SiC X grain data, while a model where sterile neutrinos are directly produced by ν_e scattering with matter can be allowed depending on the sterile neutrino temperature and the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Method
The authors adopted global fitting parameters for the 3+1 neutrino model from IceCube and short-baseline experiments (Δm²₄₁ ≈ 1.75 eV², θ₁₄ = 9.44°, θ₂₄ = 6.93°, θ₃₄ = 0). They calculated neutrino flavor evolution in a supernova environment using a 4×4 mixing matrix and matter potentials, then computed neutrino-induced reaction rates for nucleosynthesis up to the He layer (1.6 M⊙ to 6 M⊙) based on a SN1987A model with LMC metallicity. Two sterile neutrino luminosity models were tested: one with zero initial sterile neutrino luminosity (null L_ν_s) and one with equivalent luminosity for all flavors (L_ν_s = L_ν/8) where the sterile neutrino temperature was treated as a free parameter from 7 MeV to 10 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Δm²₄₁ ≈ 1.75 eV², θ₁₄ = 9.44°, θ₂₄ = 6.93°, θ₃₄ = 0 (global fit parameters from IceCube and SBL experiments)
- For the null L_ν_s model: 3-flavor IH gives ⁷Li/¹¹B = 0.80 (allowed within 2σ limit of < 0.53); 3+1 flavor NH gives 1.27 and IH gives 1.04 (both excluded at 3σ limit of < 0.95)
- For the equivalent L_ν_s model at T_ν_s = 7 MeV: NH gives ⁷Li/¹¹B = 0.88, IH gives 0.92 (both allowed within 3σ); at T_ν_s = 10 MeV: NH gives 0.78, IH gives 1.15
- Constraint on interaction strength A ≤ 10⁻¹⁰ from SN1987A total neutrino luminosity
- Core temperature of proto-neutron star assumed as T_C = 50 MeV for the direct production model

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis (Woosley 1989, Heger 2003, Hayakawa 2018) by incorporating the 3+1 sterile neutrino model with parameters from Collin (2016) that reconcile IceCube and short-baseline experiment data. It contradicts the null L_ν_s model for sterile neutrinos but allows the direct production model, with viability depending on sterile neutrino temperature and mass hierarchy. The results enable future studies of sterile neutrino decoupling using Boltzmann transport equations to determine the actual sterile neutrino temperature and luminosity, which could provide clues about both sterile neutrino existence and the neutrino mass hierarchy.
