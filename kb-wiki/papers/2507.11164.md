# Do massive neutrino states really exist?

> In neutrino physics, while massive states define neutrino masses, the flavor states participating in weak interactions are governed by an off-diagonal mass matrix. This work examines the complete form of this mass matrix, both for a two-flavor toy model and for the general three-flavor case under two distinct mass hierarchies. Using the Monte Carlo method, we estimate the mass matrix parameters and demonstrate how its structure governs the dependence of the interaction cross section on the mass hierarchy (normal vs inverted). This formalism enables the treatment of processes involving neutrino exchange through a non-diagonal propagator, corresponding to a quantum field theory description. Numerical estimates for a local charged-lepton interaction via virtual neutrino exchange yield a ratio of electron-antimuon (lepton flavor violating) to electron-positron cross sections on the order of $10^{-51}$. Furthermore, the cross section exhibits a fundamental dependence on the lightest neutrino mass, which differs drastically between the two hierarchies. For macroscopic processes, this propagator formalism reproduces the standard neutrino oscillation probability by operating directly with the non-diagonal mass matrix, thereby circumventing the wave-packet formalism and confirming the validity of this approach.

# Do massive neutrino states really exist?

## Key claim

The paper argues that massive neutrino states and flavor states represent complementary aspects of neutrino physics, with no definitive answer to whether massive states "really exist" when only weak interactions are considered. The authors demonstrate that all observable phenomena can be described using a non-diagonal flavor-basis mass matrix and propagator, without ever diagonalizing into mass eigenstates, though gravitational interactions involving helicity flip may require definite massive states.

## Method

The authors analytically derive the complete three-flavor neutrino mass matrix in the Chau-Keung parametrization of the PMNS matrix, expressing it as a function of mixing angles, the CP-violating phase, and neutrino masses. They then apply a Monte Carlo method to generate distributions of mass matrix elements using input from the NuFIT 6.0 global fit and the joint T2K-NOvA oscillation analysis, obtaining numerical values and uncertainties for both normal and inverted hierarchies. Finally, they compute cross sections for the process $l_\alpha^- l_\beta^+ \to W^+ W^-$ using a non-diagonal neutrino propagator in the flavor basis, and reproduce the standard oscillation probability from macroscopic diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of lepton flavor-violating ($e^-\mu^+ \to W^-W^+$) to flavor-conserving ($e^+e^- \to W^+W^-$) cross sections: on the order of $10^{-51}$ for both mass hierarchies
- Cross section for same-flavor process $l_\alpha^- l_\alpha^+ \to W^+W^-$ via neutrino exchange: approximately $10^{-11}$ fbarn
- Cross section for different-flavor process $l_\alpha^- l_\beta^+ \to W^+W^-$ via neutrino exchange: approximately $10^{-62}$ fbarn
- Normal hierarchy mass matrix (with $m_1=0$), real part diagonal elements in units of $10^{-2}$ eV: $m_e = 0.372^{+0.010}_{-0.011}$, $m_\mu = 2.671^{+0.084}_{-0.071}$, $m_\tau = 2.837^{+0.070}_{-0.085}$
- Inverted hierarchy mass matrix (with $m_3=0$), real part diagonal elements in units of $10^{-2}$ eV: $m_e = 4.821^{+0.021}_{-0.021}$, $m_\mu = 2.292^{+0.080}_{-0.065}$, $m_\tau = 2.777^{+0.066}_{-0.079}$
- For inverted hierarchy, $\Re(m_{e\mu})$ values: $-4.07\times10^{-5}$ eV at $m_3=0$, $3.66\times10^{-5}$ eV at $m_3=2\times10^{-2}$ eV, $5.5\times10^{-5}$ eV at $m_3=4\times10^{-2}$ eV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Naumov (2020) and Grimus (2020), who treated neutrinos as virtual particles and computed propagators without diagonalizing the mass matrix. The authors provide the first explicit analytical expression for the complete three-flavor mass matrix in the Chau-Keung parametrization, which they note has not been previously reported. The formalism reproduces the standard oscillation probability of Kobzarev et al. (1982) while operating directly with the non-diagonal mass matrix, and the authors suggest the observed fine-tuning in the inverted hierarchy may favor the normal hierarchy. The results enable future treatment of processes where diagonalization is not straightforward, such as neutrino interactions with matter as studied by Dvornikov (2025), and open the question of whether gravitational interactions requiring definite massive states could resolve the complementarity between flavor and mass descriptions.
