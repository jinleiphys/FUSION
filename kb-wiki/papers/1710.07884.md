# Role of deformation in odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for $^{30,31,32}$Ne and $^{36,37,38}$Mg isotopes

> We discuss the role of pairing anti-halo effect in the observed odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for $^{30,31,32}$Ne and $^{36,37,38}$Mg isotopes by taking into account the ground state deformation of these nuclei. To this end, we construct the ground state density for the $^{30,31}$Ne and $^{36,37}$Mg nuclei based on a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, while for the $^{32}$Ne and $^{38}$Mg nuclei we also take into account the pairing correlation using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. We demonstrate that, when the one-neutron separation energy is small for the odd-mass nuclei, a significant odd-even staggering still appears even with finite deformation, although the degree of staggering is somewhat reduced compared to the spherical case. This implies that the pairing anti-halo effect in general plays an important role in generating the odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for weakly bound nuclei.

# Role of deformation in odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for $^{30,31,32}$Ne and $^{36,37,38}$Mg isotopes

## Key claim

The pairing anti-halo effect remains the dominant mechanism generating odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections for weakly bound deformed nuclei, although the degree of staggering is somewhat reduced compared to the spherical case due to admixture of several angular momentum components in the deformed single-particle wave function.

## Method

The authors constructed ground state densities for $^{30,31}$Ne and $^{36,37}$Mg using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential without pairing, while for $^{32}$Ne and $^{38}$Mg they included pairing correlations via the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with a zero-range pairing interaction. Reaction cross sections for collisions with a $^{12}$C target at 240 MeV/nucleon were computed using the Glauber theory with an adiabatic approximation for rotational motion, averaging over all orientation angles of the deformed projectile.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.3$ was used for all three Ne isotopes, with the valence neutron in $^{31}$Ne occupying the $K^\pi = 1/2^-$ orbit connected to the $1f_{7/2}$ spherical level.
- Deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.5$ was used for all three Mg isotopes, with the valence neutron in $^{37}$Mg occupying the $[321,1/2]$ orbit connected to the $2p_{3/2}$ spherical level.
- For $^{31}$Ne at $S_n = 0.3$ MeV, the occupation probability of the $p_{3/2}$ level was 46.3% in the deformed case (compared to full occupation in the spherical case).
- The one-neutron separation energy of $^{37}$Mg was measured as $0.22^{+0.12}_{-0.09}$ MeV (Kobayashi 2014).
- Reaction cross sections for $^{36,37,38}$Mg were well reproduced with $S_n = 0.32$ MeV for $^{37}$Mg, while $S_n = 1.5$ MeV gave monotonic increase inconsistent with experiment.
- The average pairing gap for $^{32}$Ne was set to $\bar{\Delta} = 12/\sqrt{32} = 2.1$ MeV, with tests at 1 and 3 MeV showing little sensitivity.
- The staggering parameter $\gamma_3$ for Ne isotopes increased as the single-particle energy approached threshold, eventually matching the central experimental value.

## Context

This work extends previous spherical analyses by Hagino and Sagawa (2011, 2012) that attributed odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections to the pairing anti-halo effect, a mechanism first discussed by Bennaceur, Dobaczewski, and Ploszajczak (2000) where pairing correlations quench the divergent radius of weakly bound $s$ and $p$ wave neutrons. The present study incorporates deformation, which had been suggested for $^{31}$Ne and $^{37}$Mg by Hamamoto (2010), Urata et al. (2011, 2012), and Minomo et al. (2011, 2012). The results enable future work using isotope-dependent deformation parameters from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations and blocked HFB treatments for odd-mass nuclei.
