# A Deep Learning Framework for Disentangling Triangle Singularity and Pole-Based Enhancements

> Enhancements in the invariant mass distribution or scattering cross-section are usually associated with resonances. However, the nature of exotic signals found near hadron-hadron thresholds remain a puzzle today due to the presence of experimental uncertainties. In fact, a purely kinematical triangle diagram is also capable of producing similar structures, but do not correspond to any unstable quantum state. In this paper, we report for the first time, that a deep neural network can be trained to distinguish triangle singularity from pole-based enhancements with a reasonably high accuracy of discrimination between the two seemingly identical line shapes. We also identify the type of triangle enhancement that can be misidentified as a dynamic pole structure. We apply our method to confirm that the $P_ψ^N(4312)^+$ state is not due to a triangle singularity, but is more consistent with a pole-based interpretation, as determined solely through pure line-shape analysis. Lastly, we explain how our method can be used as a model-selection framework useful in studying other exotic hadron candidates.

## Key claim

A deep neural network can be trained to distinguish triangle singularity from pole-based enhancements in invariant mass distributions with reasonably high accuracy, but the authors acknowledge that certain types of triangle enhancements can be misidentified as dynamic pole structures. The method confirms that the $P_\psi^N(4312)^+$ state is more consistent with a pole-based interpretation than a triangle singularity, as determined solely through pure line-shape analysis.

## Method

The authors generated training datasets of line shapes from two triangle diagrams (triangle loop A: $\Lambda_c^+D_s^{**-}\bar{D}^{*0}$ and triangle loop B: $\Lambda^*J/\psi p$) and from uniformized $S$-matrix pole configurations (one pole in [$bt$]-sheet, one pole in [$tb$]-sheet, and one pole each in [$bt$] and [$bb$]-sheets) for the $J/\psi p$ and $\Sigma_c^+\bar{D}^0$ coupled-channel system. A deep neural network with 150 input nodes (75 energy bins and 75 intensity values) was trained to classify these line shapes, then applied to the experimental $P_\psi^N(4312)^+$ data from LHCb (2019) to determine the most likely origin of the enhancement.

## Key numbers

- The $P_\psi^N(4312)^+$ signal is measured at $4311.9 \pm 0.7$ MeV, just below the $\Sigma_c^+\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold.
- The energy region of interest for training is restricted to 4200 MeV to 4350 MeV, consisting of 75 energy bins of 2 MeV each.
- For triangle loop B ($\Lambda^*J/\psi p$), a peak can be achieved within the energy region of [4035, 4366] MeV when the mass of $\Lambda^*$ is within [2151, 2523] MeV.
- For the triangle singularity demonstration, parameters of $\epsilon = 1$ MeV and $\Lambda = 3000$ MeV were used to regulate the integral.
- The pole-based amplitudes used pole energies at $4317.49 - 7j$ MeV (isolated [$bt$]-sheet) and $4311.90 - 7j$ MeV (both [$bt$] and [$bb$]-sheets).

## Context

This work extends the machine learning line-shape analysis framework first utilized by Sombillo (2020) for distinguishing bound and virtual states at the nucleon-nucleon threshold, and later applied to the pion-nucleon system by Sombillo (2021). It directly addresses the triangle singularity mechanism proposed by Landau (1959) and revived by Guo (2015) and Liu (2015) as an alternative explanation for exotic hadron signals like the $P_c(4450)^+$ observed by LHCb (2015). The method enables a model-selection framework for studying other exotic hadron candidates without requiring prior assumptions about the underlying mechanism, going beyond previous machine learning studies by Ng (2021) that focused only on conventional pole-based interpretations.
