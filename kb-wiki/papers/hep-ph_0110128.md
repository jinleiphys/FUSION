# Soliton Models for the Nucleon and Predictions for the Nucleon Spin Structure

> In these lectures the three flavor soliton approach for baryons is reviewed. Effects of flavor symmetry breaking in the baryon wave--functions on axial current matrix elements are discussed. A bosonized chiral quark model is considered to outline the computation of spin dependent nucleon structure functions in the soliton picture.

# Soliton Models for the Nucleon and Predictions for the Nucleon Spin Structure

## Key claim

Flavor symmetry breaking in the baryon wave-functions, which introduces a 23% contamination of the antidecuplet representation in the nucleon at the chosen symmetry breaking strength, still reproduces hyperon beta-decay parameters equally well as flavor symmetric fits, despite strong deviations from flavor covariance. The strange quark contribution to the nucleon axial charge decreases significantly (from -0.131 to -0.085) when symmetry breaking is turned on, providing a consistent explanation of the proton spin puzzle.

## Method

The three-flavor Skyrme model is extended with a Wess-Zumino-Witten term and flavor symmetry breaking terms parameterized by meson mass and decay constant differences. Collective coordinates are introduced to quantize the soliton, yielding a Hamiltonian whose eigenstates mix SU(3) representations; the mixing strength is controlled by a single parameter omega^2 derived from symmetry breaking. Axial current matrix elements are computed using a parameterized operator with coefficients c1, c2, c3, where c2 is fixed by the nucleon singlet axial charge Delta Sigma = 0.2, and c1 and c3 are determined from the empirical nucleon g_A and the Lambda-to-proton g_A/g_V ratio. For structure functions, a bosonized chiral quark model (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type) is used, where the soliton profile is obtained self-consistently from the Dirac Hamiltonian eigenvalues, and the Compton amplitude is evaluated in the Bjorken limit using double Pauli-Villars regularization.

## Key numbers

- The moment of inertia parameter e is fixed to approximately 4.0 by matching the empirical mass difference M_Delta - M_N = 300 MeV.
- At omega^2 = 6.0, the nucleon amplitude contains a 23% contamination from the antidecuplet representation.
- The effective symmetry breaking strength omega^2 is required to lie in the range 5 to 8 to obtain reasonable agreement with empirical baryon mass differences.
- The empirical g_A/g_V ratios for hyperon beta-decay: Lambda to p = 0.718 +/- 0.015, Sigma to n = 0.340 +/- 0.017, Xi to Lambda = 0.25 +/- 0.05, Xi to Sigma = 1.287 +/- 0.158, Sigma to Lambda g_A = 0.61 +/- 0.02.
- The flavor symmetric F and D fit predictions for these ratios are: 0.725, 0.339, 0.19, 1.258, and 0.65 respectively.
- At zero symmetry breaking, the strange quark component in the nucleon Delta S_N = -0.131; at omega^2 = 6.0 it decreases to Delta S_N = -0.085 (for Delta Sigma = 0.2).
- In the realistic vector meson model, the nucleon axial charge g_A predictions range from 0.88 (masses fit) to 0.98 (magnetic moments fit).
- The singlet axial charge of the Lambda hyperon is predicted to be 0.256 (masses fit), 0.238 (mag.mom. fit), and 0.233 (g_A fit).
- The strange quark polarization in the Lambda is predicted to be 0.567, 0.570, and 0.562 for the three parameter sets respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) to three flavors with explicit flavor symmetry breaking, following the formalism of Yabu and Ando (1988) and the author's own earlier work (Weigel 1996). It addresses the proton spin puzzle raised by the European Muon Collaboration and analyzed by Ellis and Karliner (1995) and Jaffe (1996). The structure function calculations build on the chiral quark soliton model of Alkofer, Reinhardt, and Weigel (1996) and the bosonization techniques of Ebert and Reinhardt (1986). The results enable quantitative predictions for polarized structure functions g1 and g2 that can be compared to data from SLAC-E143 (Abe et al. 1998), and provide a framework for computing spin-dependent parton distributions from the soliton picture.
