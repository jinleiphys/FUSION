# Relativistic magnetohydrodynamics

> We present the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics coupled to dynamical electromagnetic fields, including the effects of polarization, electric fields, and the derivative expansion. We enumerate the transport coefficients at leading order in derivatives, including electrical conductivities, viscosities, and thermodynamic coefficients. We find the constraints on transport coefficients due to the positivity of entropy production, and derive the corresponding Kubo formulas. For the neutral state in a magnetic field, small fluctuations include Alfven waves, magnetosonic waves, and the dissipative modes. For the state with a non-zero dynamical charge density in a magnetic field, plasma oscillations gap out all propagating modes, except for Alfven-like waves with a quadratic dispersion relation. We relate the transport coefficients in the "conventional" magnetohydrodynamics (formulated using Maxwell's equations in matter) to those in the "dual" version of magnetohydrodynamics (formulated using the conserved magnetic flux).

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete, systematic derivation of relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) including electric fields, polarization effects, and all transport coefficients at leading order in derivatives, under the specific counting scheme where the magnetic field is treated as order-one and the electric field as order-one in derivatives (i.e., \(B \sim O(1)\), \(E \sim O(\partial)\)), which is appropriate for conducting matter where electric screening suppresses the electric field.

## Method

The authors construct the hydrodynamic equations by starting from equilibrium thermodynamics in the presence of external electromagnetic and gravitational fields, using a derivative expansion of the free energy density. They then promote the thermodynamic variables to time-dependent fields, write constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and current, separate equilibrium contributions (derived from the generating functional) from non-equilibrium contributions, and impose constraints from the positivity of entropy production and Onsager relations. The analysis is performed in 3+1 dimensions for a parity-invariant conducting fluid in a strong magnetic field, using the "thermodynamic frame" as a natural generalization of the Landau-Lifshitz frame.

## Key numbers

* Eleven transport coefficients at one-derivative order for a parity-invariant conducting fluid in a magnetic field in 3+1 dimensions: one thermodynamic coefficient (the magneto-vortical susceptibility \(M_4\)) and ten non-equilibrium coefficients.
* Among the non-equilibrium coefficients: two electrical conductivities (transverse and longitudinal), two shear viscosities (transverse and longitudinal), three bulk viscosities, and three non-dissipative coefficients (two Hall viscosities and one Hall conductivity).
* The free energy density at leading order is \(p(T, \mu, B^2)\); at \(O(\partial)\) it contains five independent invariants \(s_n^{(1)}\) with coefficients \(M_n(T,\mu,B^2)\).
* For a neutral state in a magnetic field, small fluctuations include Alfven waves, magnetosonic waves, and dissipative modes; for a state with non-zero dynamical charge density, plasma oscillations gap out all propagating modes except Alfven-like waves with a quadratic dispersion relation.

## Context

This work extends the systematic derivative-expansion approach to relativistic hydrodynamics (as developed by Baier (2008), Bhattacharyya (2008), and others) to include dynamical electromagnetic fields, going beyond the "traditional" MHD described in the classic textbook by Landau and Lifshitz. It provides a complete enumeration of transport coefficients at leading order, resolving disagreements in the literature about the number of such coefficients, and enables applications to relativistic systems such as astrophysical plasmas, hot subnuclear matter, graphene, and Weyl semi-metals. The results are compared with the "dual" formulation of MHD based on conserved magnetic flux, and with previous studies of transport in relativistic fluids in magnetic fields.
