# Carbon Burning in Supernovae and Evolving Physical Constants

> The C(12)+C(12) => Ne(20)+He(4) fusion reaction rate is shown to be extremely sensitive to variations in the strong coupling constant. Connection with the strong coupling constant is established by using the one boson exchange potential for the nuclear forces and then relating the meson masses and coupling constants of this model to QCD. The implications of a cosmological evolution of these constants on carbon burning in Type 1a Supernovae are discussed.

## Key claim

The C(12)+C(12) → Ne(20)+He(4) fusion reaction cross section is extremely sensitive to variations in the strong coupling constant α_s, with a 2% change in α_s at the proton mass scale producing roughly a factor-of-two change in the cross section; however, this large effect could be compensated by a small (6.7%) change in temperature, making the net impact on Type 1a supernova luminosity difficult to quantify without a more complete supernova model.

## Method

The author models the C(12)+C(12) fusion cross section using the WKB approximation with a one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) for the nuclear force, retaining only the scalar σ and vector ω meson contributions. The OBEP parameters (meson masses and coupling constants) are related to QCD quantities, the quark condensate, gluon condensate, and pion decay constant, using sum rules, the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, and the dilute instanton gas approximation. The cross section and Q-value are then recalculated after independently varying α_em (electromagnetic fine structure constant) and α_s (strong coupling constant at the proton mass scale), with the proton radius and meson masses scaled accordingly.

## Key numbers

- For a 2% increase in α_s at the proton mass, the C(12)+C(12) fusion cross section decreases by 51%; for a 2% decrease in α_s, the cross section increases by 115% (both at a temperature of 1×10^9 K, the typical transition temperature from quiescent to explosive burning in supernovae).
- A 2% change in α_s results in approximately a 1.2% change in the proton radius (radius decreasing as coupling increases) and about a 2.5% change in meson masses.
- The fractional change in the Q-value for the reaction is ΔQ/Q ≈ 0.04 for a 2% change in α_s.
- Varying α_em by 1×10^(-5) (the Webb et al. bound at redshift z=1) produces a fractional cross section change of 5.4×10^(-4), a factor of 50 larger than the fractional change in α_em itself.
- A 6.7% increase in temperature would compensate for a 2% decrease in α_s in terms of the energy yield (cross section times Q-value).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Amendola et al. (1999), who studied the effect of a time-varying gravitational constant on the Chandrasekhar mass and cosmology, by instead focusing on the sensitivity of carbon fusion to variations in the electromagnetic and strong coupling constants. The paper uses the one-boson-exchange potential framework of Machleidt (1989) and QCD sum-rule techniques from Ioffe (1981), Brown and Adami (1991), and Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1978) to connect meson properties to the strong coupling constant. The results suggest that if α_s evolves cosmologically, Type 1a supernovae could exhibit luminosity evolution, but the extreme temperature sensitivity of carbon burning makes quantitative predictions difficult without more complete supernova models.
