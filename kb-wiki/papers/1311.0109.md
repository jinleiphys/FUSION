# Alternative Scenarios of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions: III. Transverse Momentum Spectra

> Transverse-mass spectra, their inverse slopes and mean transverse masses in relativistic collisions of heavy nuclei are analyzed in a wide range of incident energies 2.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$ 39 GeV. The analysis is performed within the three-fluid model employing three different equations of state (EoS's): a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with the first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition into deconfined state. Calculations show that inverse slopes and mean transverse masses of all the species (with the exception of antibaryons within the hadronic scenario) exhibit a step-like behavior similar to that observed for mesons and protons in available experimental data. This step-like behavior takes place for all considered EoS's and results from the freeze-out dynamics rather than is a signal of the deconfinement transition. A good reproduction of experimental inverse slopes and mean transverse masses for light species (up to proton) is achieved within all the considered scenarios. The freeze-out parameters are precisely the same as those used for reproduction of particles yields in previous papers of this series. This became possible because the freeze-out stage is not completely equilibrium.

## Key claim

The step-like behavior of inverse slopes and mean transverse masses as functions of incident energy is not a signal of deconfinement transition but rather results from the freeze-out dynamics, specifically the competition between the freeze-out front propagation and the supersonic expansion of the fluid. This behavior occurs for all three equations of state considered (purely hadronic, first-order phase transition, and smooth crossover), with the exception of antibaryons within the hadronic scenario.

## Method

The analysis was performed within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the collision process from the stage of cold nuclei up to freeze-out using three interacting fluids: two baryon-rich fluids (projectile and target) and a baryon-free fireball fluid. Three different equations of state were employed: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition into deconfined state, and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The freeze-out procedure is dynamical, where the freeze-out front propagates with respect to the fluid not faster than the speed of sound, and only matter in the surface layer gets frozen out during a single time step, while inner parts continue evolving hydrodynamically.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range analyzed: 2.7 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 39 GeV
- Freeze-out energy density εfrz = 0.4 GeV/fm³ for all incident energies except the lowest (2A GeV), where εfrz = 0.3 GeV/fm³
- Formation time τ = 2 fm/c for the hadronic scenario and τ = 0.17 fm/c for scenarios involving deconfinement transition
- Strangeness suppression factor γS is applied to single-strange particle spectra at Elab < 10A GeV; at Elab > 10A GeV no additional strangeness suppression is needed
- Mean freeze-out energy density ⟨εout⟩ saturates at approximately εfrz/2 at higher incident energies

## Context

This paper continues a series of simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions within different scenarios (Ivanov 2012, 2013). It extends earlier work by Gorenstein (2003) and Mohanty (2003) who associated the step-like behavior of kaon inverse slopes with onset of deconfinement, and contrasts with microscopic transport models (HSD, UrQMD, GiBUU) that initially failed to reproduce this behavior using hadronic degrees of freedom. The dynamical freeze-out explanation was previously demonstrated in the 3FD model (Russkikh 2006, Ivanov 2008) and later confirmed by the Hybrid Hydro-Kinetic Model (Petersen 2009). The results enable further investigation of the onset of deconfinement in the top-AGS to low-SPS energy region, where experimental baryon stopping shows signs of a deconfinement transition.
