# Opacity estimation of OO collision from CoMBolt-ITA hybrid

> Understanding the effect of system size on the applicability of the hydrodynamic description in heavy-ion physics remains unclear. Recent measurements of OO collisions at the LHC offer a new opportunity to refine our understanding of collectivity because of their intermediate size relative to heavy-ion and small-system collisions, as well as the relatively good control over their initial state. We use the CoMBolt-ITA hybrid model to describe recent OO measurements at the LHC. The model employs TrENTo for the initial state. A combination of the pre-equilibration and hydrodynamized medium stages is modeled consistently by CoMBolt-ITA, which evolves the Boltzmann distribution of massless collective excitations. The afterburner stage is included by employing UrQMD. Using this approach, we test whether the system lies in the regime where its spatial size approaches the mean free path, corresponding to low opacity, or in the opposite limit, where its size exceeds the mean free path sufficiently to enter the fluid-like evolution regime with high opacity. We find that, in light of the data-model comparison and considering the current status of the model, OO collisions with centralities larger than $60\%$ gradually leave the domain of fluid-like evolution.

## Key claim

OO collisions at the LHC with centralities larger than 60% gradually leave the domain of fluid-like evolution, while those with centralities below 60% lie within the fluid-like regime, based on the current status of the CoMBolt-ITA hybrid model and its comparison with ALICE data.

## Method

The authors used the CoMBolt-ITA hybrid model, which solves the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for massless collective excitations in 2+1 dimensions, with TrENTo initial conditions and an UrQMD hadronic afterburner. The model evolves the system from tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c through pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamized stages without a separate pre-equilibrium stage, then switches to hadron gas at T_sw = 0.155 GeV using the Cornelius algorithm. Two parameter sets were tested: one with eta/s = 0.1 and normalization 50 (case 1), and another with eta/s = 0.18 and normalization 70 (case 2), tuned to reproduce the elliptic flow v2{2} as a function of centrality.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature for freeze-out: T_sw = 0.155 GeV
- Initial evolution start time: tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c
- Two parameterizations tested: eta/s = 0.1 with normalization 50 (case 1) and eta/s = 0.18 with normalization 70 (case 2)
- Transverse momentum range for selected particles: 0.2 < p_T < 3.0 GeV
- Pseudorapidity subevent ranges: A in (-1.6, -1.4) and B in (1.4, 1.6)
- Approximately 3 x 10^3 minimum bias events, each oversampled 10^3 times
- Opacity threshold for fluid-like regime: hat(gamma) greater than approximately 4
- Opacity threshold for particle-like regime: hat(gamma) less than approximately 1-2

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Kurkela (2019) and the CoMBolt-ITA framework developed by Taghavi (2025) to the newly measured OO collisions at the LHC by the ALICE Collaboration (2025). The study addresses the open question of how far hydrodynamic descriptions can be applied to systems of intermediate size between heavy-ion collisions (Pb-Pb) and small systems (pp, p-Pb), complementing prior work by Ambrus (2024) on OO collisions. The results enable future systematic studies of even smaller systems such as He-He collisions and pp collisions with improved initial-state modeling, and highlight the need for including mass terms and a realistic QCD equation of state in the Boltzmann framework.
