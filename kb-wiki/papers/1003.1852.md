# Small x physics and RHIC data

> This is a review of applications of the Color Glass Condensate to the phenomenology of relativistic heavy ion collisions. The initial stages of the collision can be understood in terms of the nonperturbatively strong nonlinear glasma color fields. We discuss how the CGC framework can and has been used to compute properties of the initial conditions of AA collisions. In particular this has led to recent progress in understanding multiparticle correlations, which can provide a directly observable signal of the properties of the initial stage of the collision process.

## Key claim

This review argues that the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, assuming a small coupling constant but high gluon density, can describe the initial stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions, and that this description has led to recent progress in understanding multiparticle correlations which may provide a directly observable signal of the initial collision stage.

## Method

The paper reviews the CGC effective field theory approach, where a high-energy nucleus is described by classical color fields radiated by stochastic color charges representing large-x partons. The initial conditions for the glasma fields in the future light cone of a heavy ion collision are obtained by matching solutions from the individual nuclei, leading to longitudinal chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields. The resulting classical Yang-Mills equations are solved numerically or analytically, with the average over color charge configurations governed by the JIMWLK renormalization group equation.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale Qs is the transverse momentum scale characterizing the typical transverse momentum of gluons (~ Qs) and the correlation length of the system.
* In the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, saturation occurs at transverse momentum scales k_T less than or approximately equal to Qs ~ g^2 mu.
* The initial glasma field configuration has an energy-momentum tensor diagonal with components (epsilon, -epsilon/2, -epsilon/2, 0) at times tau greater than or approximately equal to 1/Qs.
* The lifetime of the purely longitudinal field configuration is approximately 1/Qs.
* Numerical calculations were performed on a 512^2 lattice with g^2 mu R_A = 67.7.

## Context

This review extends the CGC framework developed by Jalilian-Marian, Iancu, McLerran, Weigert, Leonidov, and Kovner (1997-2001) and applies it to RHIC phenomenology. It contrasts the CGC approach with strong coupling theories (gauge/gravity duality) and with models using a geometric final-state saturation criterion like the EKRT model (Eskola et al., 1999-2001) or parton percolation models (Armesto, Nardi, Satz, 1996-2003). The work enables further studies of isotropization via plasma instabilities and provides a framework for computing multiparticle correlations that can be tested against RHIC data.
