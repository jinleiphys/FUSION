# The QCD phase diagram

> Strongly interacting matter exhibits new phases under extreme conditions. Matter was exposed to such extremes not only in the Early Universe, but also today in the cores of neutron stars, as well as in laboratory experiments at a much smaller scale. We study the underlying theory, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) with the methods of statistical physics and explore the various phases we may encounter in experiment, such as the Quark Gluon Plasma. We briefly summarize the experimental evidence for the new forms of matter and review the theoretical efforts to embed these findings in the broader context of quantum field theory, with special attention to exact and broken symmetries and critical behaviour.

## Key claim

The QCD phase diagram is known to have a crossover transition at zero chemical potential, but the existence and location of a chiral critical point at finite baryon density remains unconfirmed; the paper emphasizes that "no reliable extrapolations exist, let alone continuum extrapolated results" for the critical endpoint, and recent data from the STAR collaboration show "no unambiguous critical signal" even in the latest update.

## Method

The paper reviews the QCD phase diagram by combining evidence from three complementary approaches: lattice QCD simulations (which use Monte Carlo methods on discretized spacetime to compute the partition function, but suffer from a sign problem at real chemical potential), heavy ion collision experiments (which probe the phase diagram by varying collision energy and measuring final-state hadrons, flow coefficients, and fluctuation observables), and theoretical methods including Taylor expansion and analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential to circumvent the sign problem.

## Key numbers

- The crossover transition at zero chemical potential occurs at T ≈ 157 MeV (from lattice simulations by Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2011))
- The chiral transition temperature in the chiral limit is T_c = 132^(+3)_(-6) MeV (Ding, 2019)
- The liquid-gas critical endpoint is located at T_c^(LG) = 17.9(4) MeV (Elliott, 2013)
- The liquid-gas transition occurs at baryo-chemical potential μ^(LG) ≈ 924 MeV
- The width of the transition is ΔT ≈ 15 MeV, and does not significantly change for μ_B < 300 MeV
- A recent continuum extrapolated result claims μ_(B,c) > 450 MeV with 2σ confidence (Borsanyi, 2025)
- Nuclear saturation density is n_0 = 0.17 fm^(-3)

## Context

This review extends the foundational lattice QCD results of Aoki (2006) establishing the crossover nature of the transition, and the Columbia plot framework for understanding chiral symmetry restoration. It summarizes the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which seeks to locate the hypothetical chiral critical point predicted by Stephanov (2006), and connects to multi-messenger astrophysics following the GW170817 neutron star merger observation. The paper enables future searches for the critical endpoint through improved experimental statistics from HADES and CBM, and through gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers.
