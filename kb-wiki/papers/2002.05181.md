# Early thermalization and shear viscosity to entropy ratio in heavy-ion collisions at energies of BES, FAIR and NICA

> Equilibration of highly excited baryon-rich matter is studied within the microscopic model calculations in A+A collisions at energies of BES, FAIR and NICA. It is shown that the system evolution from the very beginning of the collision can be approximated by relativistic hydrodynamics, although the hot and dense nuclear matter is not in local equilibrium yet. During the evolution of the fireball the extracted values of energy density, net baryon and net strangeness densities are used as an input to Statistical Model (SM) in order to calculate temperature $T$, chemical potentials $μ_B$ and $μ_S$, and entropy density $s$ of the system. Also, they are used as an input for the box with periodic boundary conditions to investigate the momentum correlators in the infinite nuclear matter. Shear viscosity $η$ is calculated according to the Green-Kubo formalism. At all energies, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio shows minimum at time corresponding to maximum baryon density. The ratio dependence on $T, μ_B, μ_S$ is investigated for both in- and out of equilibrium cases.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) in heavy-ion collisions at BES, FAIR, and NICA energies shows a minimum at the time of maximum baryon density (around 5-6 fm/c) only when the entropy density is calculated from the nonequilibrium momentum distributions of hadrons, not when using the equilibrium statistical model entropy.

## Method

Central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 10 to 40 AGeV were simulated using the UrQMD transport model. The central cell of volume 125 fm³ was analyzed at 20 time slices from 1 to 20 fm/c. For each time slice, the extracted energy density, net baryon density, and net strangeness density were used as input to initialize a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions (volume 1000 fm³) to compute shear viscosity via the Green-Kubo formalism, averaging over the plateau region between 200 and 800 fm/c. Entropy density was calculated both from the equilibrium statistical model of an ideal hadron gas and from the nonequilibrium momentum distribution functions of hadrons in the cell.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions were generated at four beam energies: Elab = 10, 20, 30, and 40 AGeV, with about 50,000 events per energy.
- The central cell volume was V = 125 fm³; the box for Green-Kubo calculations had volume V = 1000 fm³.
- Time slices were taken from t = 1 to 20 fm/c with step Δt = 1 fm/c.
- The η/s ratio shows minima at t ≈ 5-6 fm/c for all four beam energies when using nonequilibrium entropy.
- The η/s ratio decreases with decreasing bombarding energy from 40 to 10 AGeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shear viscosity in microscopic models (Muronga 2003, Demir 2008, Ozvenchuk 2012, Karpenko 2015, Rose 2017) that typically calculated η/s as a function of temperature at fixed baryochemical potential and zero strangeness chemical potential. The authors build on their earlier work (Teslyk 2019) and prior studies of equilibration in central cells (Bravina 1999, Bravina 2008). The results suggest that further measurements at lower energies (√s = 2-3 GeV) could reveal whether the η/s minimum stops decreasing, which would indicate a change in the equation of state due to formation of quark-gluon strings as a precursor to QGP formation.
