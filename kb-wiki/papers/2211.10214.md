# Dispersive formalism for the nuclear structure correction $δ_\mathrm{NS}$ to the $β$ decay rate

> We analyze the axial $γW$-box diagram for $I(J^P)=1(0^+)$ nuclei and provide a dispersion representation of the nuclear-structure correction $δ_\text{NS}$ including its energy-dependent part. We also summarize useful isospin rotation formula and representations in nuclear theory that could facilitate the calculation of the parity-odd nuclear structure function $F_3(ν,Q^2)$. They provide a rigorous theory framework for the future, high-precision calculation of the nuclear structure correction $δ_\text{NS}$ necessary for the extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element $|V_{ud}|$ from superallowed nuclear $β$ decays.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous, fully-relativistic dispersion relation framework for the nuclear-structure correction δ_NS to superallowed β decays, including its energy-dependent part, which was previously neglected or treated approximately. The authors argue that the commonly-adopted representation of δ_NS using quenched coupling constants disregards potentially important contributions from the quasi-elastic absorption peak and from the electron energy dependence, both of which are non-negligible at the 0.01% precision level.

## Method

The authors analyze the axial γW-box diagram for I(J^P)=1(0^+) nuclei and derive a dispersion representation of the nuclear-structure correction δ_NS in terms of the parity-odd nuclear structure function F_3(ν,Q^2). They split the invariant amplitudes T_i into even and odd components under ν→−ν crossing symmetry, separating the isoscalar and isovector electromagnetic current contributions, and show that the isovector part contributes to the energy-independent box diagram through a many-body effect specific to I=1 nuclei. They also summarize isospin rotation formulas and nuclear theory representations to facilitate calculation of F_3(ν,Q^2).

## Key numbers

* The master formula for |V_ud|^2 from superallowed decays uses the constant 2984.43 s in the numerator (Eq. 1).
* The outer correction δ_R' was calculated to Z^2α^3 and is well under control.
* The perturbative QCD correction not from the γW-box diagram is ᾱ_g ≈ −0.083.
* The resummation of O(α) QED logarithms and leading O(α^2) corrections gives δ_HO^QED ≈ 0.0013.
* For the free nucleon, energy-dependent corrections scale as (α/π)(E_e/M_π) ∼ 10^−5, well below current precision.
* The nuclear excitation spectrum features a characteristic scale Λ_nucl ∼ 10 MeV, comparable to the electron energy in the decay.
* Table 1 lists δ_NS values for 23 parent nuclei, ranging from −0.400(50)% for ¹⁰C to −0.016(82)% for ⁶²Ga, with uncertainties between 0.050% and 0.094%.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation treatment previously introduced for the single-nucleon axial γW-box diagram (Seng 2018, Seng 2018) to nuclear systems, and improves upon the approximate treatment in Gorchtein (2018) by providing the full E_e-dependence and correcting several approximations. The formalism enables future high-precision calculations of δ_NS using modern ab-initio methods, which together with ab-initio calculations of the isospin-symmetry breaking correction δ_C (Seng 2022) will allow controllable assessment of nuclear structure uncertainties in |V_ud| from superallowed β decays. The current δ_NS values used in global analysis (Hardy 2020) include inflated uncertainties from the new effects identified in Gorchtein (2018), making δ_NS the largest contributor to the |V_ud| error budget.
