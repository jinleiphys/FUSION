# Onset of scaling violation in pion and kaon elastic electromagnetic form factors

> Using a symmetry-preserving truncation of the quantum field equations describing hadron properties, parameter-free predictions are delivered for pion and kaon elastic electromagnetic form factors, $F_{P=π,K}$, thereby unifying them with kindred results for nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors. Regarding positive-charge states, the analysis stresses that the presence of scaling violations in QCD entails that $Q^2 F_P(Q^2)$ should exhibit a single maximum on $Q^2>0$. Locating such a maximum is both necessary and sufficient to establish the existence of scaling violations. The study predicts that, for charged $π$, $K$ mesons, the $Q^2 F_P(Q^2)$ maximum lies in the neighbourhood $Q^2 \simeq 5\,$GeV$^2$. Foreseeable experiments will test these predictions and, providing their $Q^2$ reach meets expectations, potentially also provide details on the momentum dependence of meson form factor scaling violation.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that for charged pion and kaon mesons, the quantity \(Q^2 F_P(Q^2)\) exhibits a single maximum in the neighborhood of \(Q^2 \simeq 5\,\text{GeV}^2\), and that locating this maximum is both necessary and sufficient to establish the existence of scaling violations in QCD. The hedge is that this prediction is delivered using a symmetry-preserving truncation of quantum field equations, and that foreseeable experiments with sufficient \(Q^2\) reach could test it.

## Method
The authors use a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, a symmetry-preserving continuum Schwinger function method (CSM). They compute the matrix element for the photon coupling to the meson’s valence quark, obtaining direct numerical results for the form factors up to a maximum \(Q^2\) (e.g., \(Q_{\pi^m}^2 = 3.2\,\text{GeV}^2\), \(Q_{K^m}^2 = 4.9\,\text{GeV}^2\)) beyond which complex conjugate poles in the dressed quark propagators prevent conventional evaluation. To extend predictions to larger \(Q^2\), they employ the Schlessinger point method (SPM), a multipoint Padé approximant approach, generating thousands of statistically independent extrapolations and reporting the mean and 68% confidence band.

## Key numbers
- The maximum in \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2)\) occurs at \(Q^2 \simeq 4.6(5)\,\text{GeV}^2\).
- The maximum in \(Q^2 F_{K}(Q^2)\) occurs at \(Q^2 \simeq 4.8(5)\,\text{GeV}^2\).
- The ratio \(F_K^{\bar s}/F_K^{u}\) has a maximum at \(Q^2 \simeq 5.9(2.6)\,\text{GeV}^2\) with peak height \(1.76(8)\).
- Pion charge radius squared: \(r_\pi^2 = [0.67]^2\,\text{fm}^2\) (compared to experimental \([0.663(6)]^2\,\text{fm}^2\)).
- Charged kaon charge radius squared: \(r_{K^\pm}^2 = [0.60]^2\,\text{fm}^2\) (compared to experimental \([0.56(3)]^2\,\text{fm}^2\)).
- Neutral kaon charge radius squared: \(r_{K^0}^2 = -[0.30]^2\,\text{fm}^2\) (compared to experimental \(-[0.277(18)]^2\,\text{fm}^2\)).
- Pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.14\,\text{GeV}\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.094\,\text{GeV}\), kaon mass \(m_K = 0.45\,\text{GeV}\), kaon decay constant \(f_K = 0.11\,\text{GeV}\) (all calculated).
- The asymptotic QCD prediction using the asymptotic distribution amplitude gives \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2) = 0.12\,\text{GeV}^2\) at \(Q^2 = 15\,\text{GeV}^2\).

## Context
This work extends the unified treatment of meson and nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors begun in Yao (2024) for nucleons, and builds on earlier CSM calculations of pion and kaon form factors by Chang (2013) and Gao (2017) using perturbation theory integral representations (PTIRs). It also contrasts with lattice QCD results (e.g., Ding 2023) which are currently insensitive to scaling violations at large \(Q^2\). The predictions are testable at Jefferson Laboratory experiments (E12-19-006, E12-09-011) and at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), enabling discovery of scaling violation in hard exclusive processes.
