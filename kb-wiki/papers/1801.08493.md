# The $l$ dependence of the helion optical model potential and the undularity of the $l$-independent equivalent

> The relationship between $l$-dependence and undularity of the optical model potential (OMP) is studied for the case of the elastic scattering of 33 MeV \nuc{3}{He} on $^{58}$Ni. The relationship that emerges follows the general features of the same relationship found for the proton OMP presented in arXiv:1705.07003. The general background is also presented in that reference.

# The $l$ dependence of the helion optical model potential and the undularity of the $l$-independent equivalent

## Key claim

The undularity (waviness) observed in the radial form of $l$-independent optical model potentials (OMPs) that are $S$-matrix equivalent to explicitly $l$-dependent potentials is a general feature that extends from proton scattering to helion scattering, and such undulations indicate an underlying $l$ dependency that should not be dismissed as unphysical. The relationship holds specifically for the case of 33 MeV $^{3}$He on $^{58}$Ni, where the $l$-dependent term only modifies partial waves with $l$ below a cutoff value $L$, yet induces potential changes at radii well beyond the semiclassical radius corresponding to $L$.

## Method

The author added an $l^2$-dependent term of Woods-Saxon form in $l$-space to a standard 7-parameter Woods-Saxon plus Woods-Saxon derivative $l$-independent central potential for the elastic scattering of 33 MeV $^{3}$He on $^{58}$Ni. The $S$-matrix $S_{lj}$ from this $l$-dependent potential was then used as input to an $S_{lj} \rightarrow V(r) + \mathbf{l}\cdot\mathbf{s}\, V_{\mathrm{SO}}(r)$ inversion procedure (the IP method) to determine the $l$-independent potential that exactly reproduces the same $S_{lj}$. The degree of undularity in the inverted potential was studied as a function of the inversion quality parameter $\sigma$.

## Key numbers

- The $l$-dependent term halved $\chi^2/N$ for the fit to the angular distribution of 33 MeV $^{3}$He on $^{58}$Ni, particularly improving the fit for $\theta \geq 120$ degrees.
- Inversion $\sigma$ values for the inverted potentials were $1.647 \times 10^{-3}$ and $5.432 \times 10^{-4}$ for the earlier iterations, and $7.64 \times 10^{-5}$ for the most refined iteration.
- The inverted potential with $\sigma = 7.64 \times 10^{-5}$ showed distinct emissivity (positive imaginary potential) at $r = 9$ fm and $r = 11$ fm.
- The $l$-dependent term was parameterized with a cutoff $L$ and diffusivity $\Delta$ in $l$-space, such that partial waves with $l > L + \Delta$ were essentially unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the same relationship between $l$-dependence and undularity previously established for the proton OMP by Mackintosh (2017, arXiv:1705.07003). The study was motivated by the finding that standard Woods-Saxon phenomenology could not fit the elastic scattering angular distribution of 33 MeV $^{3}$He on $^{58}$Ni, particularly between 120 and 180 degrees, while the same $l$-dependent approach did not improve fits for $^{3}$He scattering on $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca where data terminated below 120 degrees (Brummund, 1978). The results suggest that undulations found in model-independent fits to experimental data should be interpreted as evidence for $l$-dependence in the underlying potential, rather than being discarded as artifacts.
