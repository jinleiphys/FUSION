# Initial-State Bremsstrahlung versus Final-State Hydrodynamic Sources of Azimuthal Harmonics in p+A at RHIC and LHC

> Recent pT<2~GeV azimuthal correlation data from the Beam Energy Scan (BES) and D+Au runs at RHIC/BNL and, especially, the surprising similarity of azimuthal $v_n\{2m\}(p_T)$ ``transeverse flow'' harmonics in $p+Pb$ and $Pb+Pb$ at LHC have challenged the uniqueness of local equilibrium ``perfect fluid'' interpretations of those data. We report results at QM14 on azimuthal harmonics associated with initial-state non-abelian ``wave interference'' effects predicted by perturbative QCD gluon bremsstrahlung and sourced by Color Scintillation Arrays (CSA) of color antennas. CSA are naturally identified with multiple projectile and target beam jets produced in inelastic p+A reactions. We find a remarkable similarity between azimuthal harmonics sourced by initial state CSA and those predicted with final state perfect fluid models of high energy p+A reactions. The question of which mechanism dominates in $p+A$ and $A+A$ remains open at this time.

## Key claim

Initial-state non-abelian gluon bremsstrahlung from Color Scintillation Arrays (CSA) of multiple beam jets can produce azimuthal harmonics remarkably similar to those predicted by final-state perfect fluid hydrodynamic models, but the question of which mechanism dominates in p+A and A+A reactions remains open.

## Method

The authors apply the Vitev-Gunion-Bertsch (VGB) generalization of perturbative QCD gluon bremsstrahlung to all orders in opacity for p+A collisions. They analytically evaluate azimuthal harmonics from single and multiple color antennae, modeling the projectile and target beam jets as CSA sources, and compare the resulting harmonic scaling properties to those from perfect fluid hydrodynamic models.

## Key numbers

- The Gunion-Bertsch single inclusive gluon distribution depends on the Debye screening mass μ, with the elastic cross section given by dσ₀/d²q = σ₀ μ²/π(q²+μ²)²
- The single GB antenna azimuthal harmonics obey the scaling relation [v_n^{GB}(k,q,0)]^{1/n} = [v_m^{GB}(k,q,0)]^{1/m} for all even and odd moments n
- The single GB antenna harmonics are peaked near k ∼ q, vanish at k=0, and slowly decrease toward zero for k ≫ q
- The number of target beam jets N ∼ A^{1/3} < A, with 1 ≤ M ≤ N overlapping clusters that can radiate coherently

## Context

This work extends the Gunion-Bertsch (1981) perturbative QCD bremsstrahlung formula and the Vitev (2007) generalization to multiple interactions, applying them to the puzzle of azimuthal harmonics in small systems. It directly challenges the uniqueness of the "perfect fluid" interpretation of v_n data from RHIC and LHC, which was previously supported by hydrodynamic modeling by Romatschke (2007) and Heinz (2013). The paper enables future discrimination between initial-state interference and final-state flow through study of the rapidity dependence of multi-gluon azimuthal harmonics.
