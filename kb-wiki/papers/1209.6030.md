# Nuclear modification of high transverse momentum particle production in p+A collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We present results and predictions for the nuclear modification of the differential cross sections for inclusive light hadron and prompt photon production in minimum bias d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV and minimum bias p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5$ TeV at RHIC and LHC, respectively. Our calculations combine the leading order perturbative QCD formalism with cold nuclear matter effects that arise from the elastic, inelastic and coherent multiple scattering of partons in large nuclei. We find that a theoretical approach that includes the isospin effect, Cronin effect, cold nuclear matter energy loss and dynamical shadowing can describe the RHIC d+Au data rather well. The LHC p+Pb predictions will soon be confronted by new experimental results to help clarify the magnitude and origin of cold nuclear matter effects and facilitate precision dense QCD matter tomography.

## Key claim

A leading-order perturbative QCD formalism that separately implements isospin effect, Cronin effect, cold nuclear matter energy loss, and dynamical shadowing can describe the nuclear modification factor in d+Au collisions at RHIC rather well, but the predictions for p+Pb at LHC energies depend on whether the multiple-scattering parameters are held at RHIC values or scaled with the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method

The authors combine leading-order pQCD factorization for inclusive hadron and prompt photon production with four cold nuclear matter effects: isospin (weighting proton and neutron PDFs by Z/A), Cronin effect (initial-state parton transverse momentum broadening via Gaussian smearing), cold nuclear matter energy loss (implemented as a momentum-fraction shift in the PDFs using an effective fractional energy loss epsilon_eff = 0.7 times the mean value), and dynamical shadowing (power-suppressed coherent final-state corrections parameterized by a scale xi^2). The same parameters (mu^2 = 0.12 GeV^2, lambda_g = 1 fm, xi^2 = 0.12 GeV^2) are used for both Cronin broadening and energy loss at RHIC; for LHC predictions a second scenario scales xi^2 and mu^2/lambda_g by the ratio of inelastic cross sections (70 mb / 42 mb), giving an upper bound of xi^2 = 0.20 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the Cronin broadening parameters are mu^2 = 0.12 GeV^2 and lambda_g = 1 fm, with a Gaussian width in p+p of <k_T^2>_pp = 1.8 GeV^2.
* The dynamical shadowing parameter at RHIC is xi^2 = 0.12 GeV^2; for the LHC p+Pb scenario the upper limit is xi^2 = 0.20 GeV^2, a 67% increase based on sigma_in = 70 mb at LHC vs 42 mb at RHIC.
* For p+p baseline at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the K-factor is 1 for pi0 and 1.8 for photon production; at LHC energies the K-factor is 1 for charged hadrons at 2.76 TeV and 1.5 for photons at 7 TeV.
* At LHC p+Pb at sqrt(s) = 5 TeV, the predicted R_pPb for hadrons and photons at high p_T shows approximately 15% suppression at mid-rapidity, with stronger suppression at forward rapidities.

## Context

This work extends the multiple-parton-scattering approach of Vitev (2006) and Qiu (2004) for cold nuclear matter effects, which had previously been applied to Drell-Yan and hadron production at CERN SPS and RHIC. The predictions for p+Pb at the LHC are positioned between those from the EPS09 nuclear PDF global fit approach (de Florian, 2011) and the Color Glass Condensate hybrid formalism (Albacete, 2012; Arleo, 2011), enabling experimental data to discriminate among these competing descriptions of cold nuclear matter.
