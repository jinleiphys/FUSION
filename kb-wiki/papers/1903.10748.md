# Structure Functions of the Nucleon in a Soliton Model

> We study nucleon structure functions in the soliton picture of the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We focus on their vacuum contributions and examine whether they are outweighed by their valence quark counterparts.

## Key claim

The vacuum contributions to nucleon structure functions in the bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) soliton model are numerically shown to be negligible compared to the valence quark contributions, though the vacuum part has a small negative contribution slightly above x=1 that adversely affects the unpolarized structure function after DGLAP evolution.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic tensor from the absorptive part of the Compton tensor, which is obtained by introducing auxiliary source fields in the regularized bosonized NJL action and taking functional derivatives. The static soliton is constructed via the hedgehog ansatz, with the chiral angle F(r) determined by extremizing the classical energy functional that includes Pauli-Villars regularization. Nucleon states are generated by canonically quantizing the zero modes of the soliton, and the structure functions are projected from the hadronic tensor after applying infinite momentum frame (IMF) transformation and perturbative DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

- Gottfried sum rule S_G = 0.214 for m = 400 MeV, 0.225 for m = 450 MeV, and 0.237 for m = 500 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.235 ± 0.026 (Arneodo 1994)
- Vacuum contribution to Gottfried sum rule is only 0.000156 for m = 400 MeV, 0.000248 for m = 450 MeV, and 0.000356 for m = 500 MeV
- Axial charge g_A = 0.799 for m = 400 MeV, 0.766 for m = 450 MeV, and 0.733 for m = 500 MeV, compared to empirical value 1.2601 ± 0.0025 (Barnett 1996)
- Axial singlet charge g_A^0 = 0.345 for m = 400 MeV, 0.329 for m = 450 MeV, and 0.318 for m = 500 MeV, compared to empirical value 0.31 ± 0.07 (Ellis 1994)
- Model parameters: m_pi = 138 MeV, f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of valence quark contributions to nucleon structure functions in the NJL soliton model (Weigel 1996) by computing the vacuum contributions directly from the fully regularized action for the first time. The approach enables comparison with experimental data from the NMC collaboration (Arneodo 1994) and polarized data from E143 (Abe 1994, Abe 1995), and confirms that the valence dominance assumption used in prior work is justified. The results also show that the soliton model underestimates the axial charge g_A by 30-40%, a known limitation of soliton models, while the axial singlet charge agrees well with data.
