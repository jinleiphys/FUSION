# Fully self-consistent calculations of nuclear Schiff moments

> We calculate the Schiff moments of the nuclei 199Hg and 211Ra in completely self-consistent odd-nucleus mean-field theory by modifying the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov code HFODD. We allow for arbitrary shape deformation, and include the effects of nucleon dipole moments alongside those of a CP-violating pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results for 199Hg differ significantly from those of previous calculations when the CP-violating interaction is of isovector character.

## Key claim

The authors calculate nuclear Schiff moments for 199Hg and 211Ra using fully self-consistent mean-field theory that treats the CP-violating interaction on the same footing as the strong interaction and includes all nucleons equally, finding that results for 199Hg differ significantly from previous calculations when the CP-violating interaction is of isovector character, with the coefficient a1 sometimes changing sign.

## Method

The authors modified the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov code HFODD to include a CP-violating pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction (V_PT) and nucleon dipole moments in the self-consistent loop, allowing arbitrary shape deformation. They performed calculations in odd nuclei by treating all nucleons equally in mean-field theory, projecting states onto good angular momentum after variation, and using several Skyrme interactions (SLy4, SkM*, SV, SIII). For 199Hg, they extracted results from axially symmetric solutions even when weak triaxial instabilities prevented full convergence.

## Key numbers

- 199Hg: coefficient a1 = -0.006 e fm^3 (SLy4, projected HF), compared to a1 = 0.071 e fm^3 from Jesus (2005) and a1 = 0.055 e fm^3 from Dmitriev (2003)
- 199Hg: coefficient a0 = 0.013 e fm^3 (SLy4, projected HF), compared to a0 = 0.007 e fm^3 from Jesus (2005)
- 199Hg: coefficient b = 0.003 e fm^3 (SLy4, projected HF)
- 211Ra: coefficient a0 = 0.042 e fm^3, a1 = -0.018 e fm^3, a2 = 0.071 e fm^3, b = 0.016 e fm^3 (SLy4)
- 199Hg: deformation beta = -0.13 (SLy4, HF), with ground-state energy -1561.42 MeV (experimental binding energy 1573.19 MeV)
- 199Hg: projection reduced coefficients by a factor close to 3 (rigid-rotor factor)

## Context

This work extends previous Schiff moment calculations by Flambaum (1986), Dmitriev (2003, 2005), and Jesus (2005) by providing fully self-consistent mean-field treatment that includes all core-polarization effects and allows for deformation, whereas prior calculations used two-step approaches with spherical symmetry. The results enable more reliable interpretation of experimental EDM limits from 199Hg (currently the tightest limit) and future measurements in 211Ra at TRIUMF, though the authors note that generator-coordinate methods may be needed for soft nuclei like 199Hg.
