# Solvability of eigenvalues in jn configurations

> Eigenvalues of eigenstates in jn configurations (n identical nucle- ons in the j -orbit) are functions of two-body energies. In some cases they are linear combinations of two-body energies whose coe+/-cients are independent of the interaction and are rational non-negative num- bers. It is shown here that a state which is an eigenstate of any two-body interaction has this solvability property. This includes, in particular, any state with spin J if there are no other states with this J in the jn configuration. It is also shown that eigenstates with solvable eigenvalues have definite seniority v and thus, exhibit partial dynamical symmetry.

# Solvability of eigenvalues in j^n configurations

## Key claim

The paper proves that any state in a j^n configuration which is an eigenstate of *any* two-body (rotationally invariant) interaction has a solvable eigenvalue, meaning its energy is a linear combination of two-body energies with coefficients that are rational, non-negative numbers independent of the interaction. The converse also holds: any solvable eigenvalue belongs to a state that is an eigenstate of any two-body interaction, and such states necessarily have definite seniority v.

## Method

The author uses the m-scheme to express diagonal and non-diagonal matrix elements of two-body interactions in j^n configurations as linear combinations of two-body energies V(J) = <j^2 J M|V|j^2 J M>, with coefficients determined solely by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. By analyzing the eigenvalue equations for sub-matrices characterized by total M, and requiring that an eigenstate be an eigenstate of any two-body interaction, the author derives that the ratios of expansion coefficients (x_i a_i / x_k a_k) must be rational numbers, leading to rational coefficients in the energy expression. The seniority scheme and its selection rules (matrix elements vanish between seniorities differing by more than 4, and by 2 in mid-shell) are then used to identify specific states with solvable eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- For j=7/2, eigenvalues of all states in all j^n configurations are solvable but are not given by a closed formula.
- For j<7/2, any two-body interaction has dynamical symmetry and is exactly solvable.
- The highest possible M value in a j^n configuration is M_max = n j - n(n-1)/2.
- The pairing interaction eigenvalue for states with seniority v in the j^n configuration is given by (n-v)(2j+3-n-v).
- In the (9/2)^5 configuration, states with seniorities v=3 and v=5 cannot mix, yielding eigenstates for spins J=5/2, 7/2, 11/2, 13/2, 15/2, and 17/2.
- In the (11/2)^6 configuration, the J=0, v=6 state is an eigenstate of any two-body interaction; also J=3, v=4; J=5, v=6; and v=4 and v=6 states with J=11, J=13, and J=14.

## Context

This work extends the concept of solvability introduced in earlier studies by Zamick and Van Isacker (2008), who calculated solvable eigenvalues for certain states in the (9/2)^4 and (7/2)^4 configurations, and by previous work defining solvability in j^n configurations. The paper formalizes the conditions under which solvable eigenvalues occur, connecting them to the seniority scheme of Racah (1943) and Flowers (1952). It identifies such states as examples of partial dynamical symmetry, as discussed by Alhassid and collaborators, where states carry quantum numbers of the SU(2) seniority group even though the Hamiltonian does not possess that symmetry. The results enable systematic identification of solvable states in higher-j configurations and clarify the relationship between solvability, seniority, and the uniqueness of states with given spin J.
