# From Di-Nucleus to Mono-Nucleus - Neck Evolution in Fusion of Massive Systems -

> Dynamics of the neck degree of freedom during fusioning process between heavy ions is studied. Time scales of the three degrees of freedom (the relative distance, the neck and the mass-asymmetry) are studied, showing an early equilibration of the neck. This means that a di-nucleus formed by the incident combination of ions quickly forms a mono-nucleus with a superdeformation during the fusion process and that the other two degrees of freedom have to be solved in a coupled way. A brief introduction of Langevin approach and dissipation-fluctuation dynamics is also given and of the application to the synthesis of the superheavy elements.

# From Di-Nucleus to Mono-Nucleus - Neck Evolution in Fusion of Massive Systems

## Key claim

The neck degree of freedom in heavy-ion fusion equilibrates far more quickly than the radial fusion motion, meaning that the initial di-nucleus configuration (DNC) transforms into a superdeformed mono-nucleus before the radial fusion process begins; consequently, the radial and mass-asymmetry degrees of freedom must be solved in a coupled way, while the neck can be treated as already equilibrated.

## Method

The authors solve the Smoluchowski equation (a high-friction limit of the Langevin equation) for the time evolution of the neck parameter distribution, starting from a delta-function at the initial di-nucleus configuration. The liquid-drop model (LDM) potential for the neck is approximated as linear, with the slope parameter f, friction coefficient γ calculated from the one-body dissipation model, and diffusion coefficient D = T/γ. The time-dependent distribution is obtained analytically with a reflective boundary condition at ε = 0, and the time scale for equilibration is compared to that of the one-dimensional radial fusion problem solved via the Langevin equation for an inverted parabolic barrier.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹⁰⁰Mo system, typical parameter values are D = T/γ = 1/8 and C = f/γ = 20/8 in units of MeV/ℏ.
- The Boltzmann distribution in the neck coordinate space is established at several tenths of ℏ/MeV.
- The radial fusion current at the saddle has a peak structure around several ℏ/MeV, indicating the radial fusion time scale is longer than the neck equilibration time.
- The formation probability for compound nucleus formation is given by P_form(E_c.m.) = (1/2) erfc(√(B/T)), where B is the barrier height measured from the initial point.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based two-step model for fusion hindrance in massive systems previously developed by Abe, Shen, and collaborators (Abe 2000, Shen 2002, Boilley 2004) and applies it to the neck degree of freedom. The analysis uses the two-center parameterization of the liquid-drop model (TCP) and the one-body wall-and-window friction model (Blocki 1978). The finding that the neck equilibrates before radial fusion enables a simplified treatment of superheavy element synthesis calculations, where the neck can be assumed to be at its equilibrium value (ε ≈ 0) during the fusion process, while the mass-asymmetry and radial degrees of freedom must be treated as coupled (Abe 2008).
