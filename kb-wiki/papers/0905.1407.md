# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in Kadyshevsky Formalism: I Meson Exchange Sector

> In a series of two papers we present the theoretical results of $πN$/meson-baryon scattering in the Kadyshevsky formalism. In this paper the results are given for meson exchange diagrams. On the formal side we show, by means of an example, how general couplings, i.e. couplings containing multiple derivatives and/or higher spin fields, should be treated. We do this by introducing and applying the Takahashi-Umezawa and the Gross-Jackiw method. For practical purposes we introduce the $\bar{P}$ method. We also show how the Takashashi-Umezawa method can be derived using the theory of Bogoliubov and collaborators and the Gross-Jackiw method is also used to study the $n$-dependence of the Kadyshevsky integral equation. Last but not least we present the second quantization procedure of the quasi particle in Kadyshevsky formalism.

## Key claim

The Kadyshevsky formalism, when applied to meson exchange diagrams with derivative couplings or higher-spin fields, produces extra frame-dependent contact terms that cancel exactly when combined with additional terms from the Takahashi-Umezawa or Gross-Jackiw methods, yielding the same covariant, frame-independent results as the Feynman formalism.

## Method

The authors applied the Kadyshevsky formalism to tree-level meson exchange diagrams for pion-nucleon scattering, using interaction Lagrangians for scalar, vector, and pseudoscalar mesons coupled to baryons. They computed the amplitudes for scalar and simplified vector meson exchange, introducing the Takahashi-Umezawa and Gross-Jackiw methods to handle derivative couplings and higher-spin fields, and derived the Kadyshevsky integral equation in the center-of-mass system to study its n-dependence.

## Key numbers

- The scalar meson exchange amplitude with external quasi particle momenta set to zero yields the Feynman result: \( M_{00} = g_{PSS}g_S [\bar{u}(p's')u(ps)] \frac{1}{t-M_S^2+i\varepsilon} \)
- For simplified vector meson exchange, the naive Kadyshevsky amplitude with \(\kappa'=\kappa=0\) gives an extra n-dependent contact term: \(-g^2\bar{u}(p's')[\sln]u(ps) \frac{2Q\cdot n}{M_V^2}\)
- The Kadyshevsky integral equation uses a propagator factor \(\frac{1}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{1}{4\mathcal{E}_n E_n} \frac{1}{\sqrt{s}-\sqrt{s_n}+i\varepsilon}\)
- A phenomenological form factor \(F(\kappa)=\left(\frac{\Lambda^2_\kappa}{\Lambda^2_\kappa-\kappa^2-i\epsilon(\kappa)\varepsilon}\right)^{N_\kappa}\) is introduced to ensure the n-dependence integral vanishes

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's baryon-baryon models (Nagels 1978, Maessen 1989) and meson-baryon models (Polinder 2005) by applying the Kadyshevsky formalism to pion-nucleon scattering with formal treatment of pair suppression. It enables the construction of covariant, frame-independent meson-baryon scattering models that can be extended to other systems via SU_f(3) symmetry, and provides a foundation for the companion paper II which treats baryon exchange and pair suppression.
