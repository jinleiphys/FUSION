# The Subleading Term of the Strong Coupling Expansion of the Heavy-Quark Potential in a $\mathcal N=4$ Super Yang-Mills Plasma

> Applying the AdS/CFT correspondence, the expansion of the heavy-quark potential of the ${\cal N}$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at large $N_c$ is carried out to the sub-leading term in the large 't Hooft coupling at nonzero temperatures. The strong coupling corresponds to the semi-classical expansion of the string-sigma model, the gravity dual of the Wilson loop operator, with the sub-leading term expressed in terms of functional determinants of fluctuations. The contributions of these determinants are evaluated numerically.

## Key claim

The subleading term of the strong-coupling expansion of the heavy-quark potential in $\mathcal N=4$ super Yang-Mills theory at nonzero temperature is numerically small, with the temperature-dependent function $g_1(rT)$ reaching only 0.92 at the screening radius where the leading-order function $g_0(rT)$ vanishes, and the screening length correction is proportional to $1/(\sqrt{\lambda}T)$.

## Method

The authors apply the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the one-loop correction to the heavy-quark potential by expanding the string-sigma action to quadratic order in fluctuations around the classical worldsheet solutions for both a single static quark and a quark-antiquark pair. The functional determinants of bosonic and fermionic fluctuations are evaluated numerically using the Gelfand-Yaglom method, solving the resulting one-dimensional Schrödinger-like equations via fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration, with the temperature dependence entering through the Schwarzschild-AdS black hole metric.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order screening length is $r_0 \simeq 0.7541/(\pi T)$.
- The zero-temperature subleading coefficient is $\kappa \simeq -1.33460$.
- The screening radius to subleading order is $r_0' = r_0 - 0.267267/(\sqrt{\lambda}T)$.
- The function $g_1(rT)$ reaches 0.92 at $r_0$ where $g_0(r_0T)=0$.
- Low-lying eigenvalues at $s=1.5236$: $E_{11}=2.10070$, $E_{12}=3.34648$, $E_{21}=1.47031$, $E_{22}=3.13753$, $E_{31}=1.55800$, $E_{32}=2.80913$ (where $s=z_h/z_0$ is a temperature measure).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculation of the subleading strong-coupling correction to the heavy-quark potential by Chu (2009) and Forini (2010) to finite temperature, using the AdS/CFT framework previously applied to the leading-order potential by Maldacena (1998), Rey (1998), and Liu. The results enable comparison with lattice simulations of large-$N_c$ QCD, as reviewed by Marco (2009), and provide a benchmark for understanding how universal properties of strongly coupled plasmas deviate from the leading-order AdS/CFT predictions.
