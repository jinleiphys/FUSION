# Photoproduction of the Scalar Meson $f_0(500)$

> In the present talk, we report a recent investigation on photoproduction of the $γN \to f_0(500)N$ within a framework of the effective Lagrangian. We include the nucleon resonances with pin $1/2$ in the $s$ channel. The coupling constants have been etermined by assuming that the decay process $N^* \to (ππ)_{I=0,J=0}N$ can be regarded as $N^* \to f_0(500) N$. We discuss the numerical results for the total cross sections and possible extension of the present work.

# Photoproduction of the Scalar Meson $f_0(500)$

## Key claim
The photoproduction reaction $\gamma N \to f_0(500)N$ is dominated by the $N^*(1440)$ resonance, with the $\rho$-meson exchange in the $t$-channel being almost negligible; however, the results depend on the assumption that the decay $N^* \to (\pi\pi)_{I=0,J=0}N$ can be treated as $N^* \to f_0(500)N$, and the general features of the production mechanism are not much changed when the $f_0(500)$ mass is varied due to its very broad width.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach including nucleon resonances with spin $1/2$ ($N(1440,1/2^+)$, $N(1535,1/2^-)$, $N(1650,1/2^-)$, $N(1710,1/2^+)$) in the $s$-channel, $\rho$-meson exchange in the $t$-channel, and nucleon exchange in the $u$-channel. Coupling constants for the $N^*f_0(500)N$ vertices were determined by identifying the scalar-isoscalar $\pi\pi$ decay channel of the resonances with $f_0(500)$ decay, and cut-off parameters were fixed to 0.8 GeV to reduce ambiguity.

## Key numbers
- The $f_0(500)$ mass was taken to be 500 MeV for the calculation, with the cut-off parameter $\Lambda = 0.8$ GeV.
- Coupling constants for the strong vertices: $g_{f_0NN(1440)} = \pm 3.59$, $g_{f_0NN(1535)} = \pm 0.33$, $g_{f_0NN(1650)} = \pm 0.37$, $g_{f_0NN(1710)} = \pm 0.53$.
- Photon coupling constants: $f_{\gamma NN(1440)} = 0.47$, $f_{\gamma NN(1535)} = 0.81$, $f_{\gamma NN(1650)} = 0.28$, $f_{\gamma NN(1710)} = -0.24$.
- The $\rho NN$ coupling constant $g_{\rho NN} = 3.11$, and the $f_0 NN$ coupling constant $g_{f_0 NN} = 0.56$.
- Differential cross sections were computed at photon energies $E_\gamma = 1.0$ GeV and $E_\gamma = 1.8$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of scalar meson photoproduction, motivated by CLAS Collaboration measurements of $S$-wave $\pi^+\pi^-$ photoproduction (Battaglieri 2008) which showed a clear $f_0(980)$ signal but only a hint of $f_0(500)$ production. The approach follows earlier effective Lagrangian frameworks by Ryu (2012), Nakayama (2006), and Kim (2011). The results enable future combined treatments of $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ photoproduction on an equal footing, though the authors note that $f_0(980)$ photoproduction requires different $N^*$ resonances and may benefit from reggeized $\rho$-meson exchange due to its higher threshold energy near 2 GeV.
