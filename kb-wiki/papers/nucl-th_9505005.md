# QCD Sum Rules, Scattering Length and the Vector Mesons in Nuclear Medium

> Critical examination is made on the relation between the mass shift of vector mesons in nuclear medium and the vector-meson $-$ nucleon scattering length. We give detailed comparison between the QCD sum rule approach by two of the present authors (Phys. Rev. {\bf C46} (1992) R34) and the scattering-length approach by Koike (Phys. Rev. {\bf C51} (1995) 1488). It is shown that the latter approach is mortally flawed both technically and conceptually.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the claim by Koike (1995) that vector meson masses should increase in nuclear medium is "mortally flawed both technically and conceptually," and that the original QCD sum rule result by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) showing a 10-20% mass drop at nuclear matter density remains correct, provided one uses the proper correlation function and accounts for the momentum dependence of the forward scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors perform a critical comparison between two approaches to computing vector meson mass shifts in nuclear matter: the QCD sum rule method using the retarded current correlation function \(\Pi^R\) (as in Hatsuda and Lee 1992) and the scattering-length approach using the forward scattering amplitude \(T^R\) (as in Koike 1995). They analyze the operator product expansion up to dimension-6 operators in the Fermi gas approximation, including scalar condensates, twist-2 quark bilinear operators, and four-quark condensates, and examine the Borel sum rule stability for both \(\Pi^R\) and \(\Pi^R_{\mu\mu}\) at zero and nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

* The vector meson mass shift from the Borel sum rule using \(\Pi^R\) is \(m_V(n_B)/m_V(0) = 1 - (0.16 \pm 0.06) \cdot n_B/n_0\) at nuclear matter density \(n_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* The continuum threshold shift is \(\sqrt{S_0(n_B)/S_0(0)} = 1 - (0.15 \pm 0.05) \cdot n_B/n_0\).
* The residue shift is \(F(n_B)/F(0) = 1 - (0.24 \pm 0.07) \cdot n_B/n_0\).
* The Landau damping contribution at \(\mathbf{q}=0\) is \(\rho_{sc} = 2\pi^2 n_B / \sqrt{p_f^2 + m_N^2} \simeq 2\pi^2 n_B / m_N\).
* The Fermi momentum at nuclear matter density is \(p_f \simeq 270\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to \(x = p_f/m_N = 0.27\).
* The twist-4 contribution could change the mass shift slope from 0.16 to 0.10 for the rho meson and 0.075 for the omega meson.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for vector mesons in nuclear matter by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and directly contradicts the scattering-length approach by Koike (1995), which claimed vector meson masses should increase. The paper demonstrates that the scattering-length method fails because it neglects the momentum dependence of the V-N forward scattering amplitude due to s-channel resonances (N(1710), N(1720), N(1700), Delta(1700)) and because the sum rule for the V-N scattering amplitude cannot determine the scattering length without dimension-8 operators. The correct approach using \(\Pi^R\) enables reliable determination of in-medium vector meson properties, which is relevant for interpreting lepton-pair spectra in gamma-A, p-A, and A-A reactions and for testing the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho 1991).
