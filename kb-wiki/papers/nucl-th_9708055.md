# Transverse Energy production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Kinetic theory is used to check the applicability of parton cascade in 1 dimensional expansion. Using the information provided by 3 dimensional parton cascade, we model the transverse expansion by an effective area. With this model, kinetic theory is able to give prediction of the time development of transverse energy which is in good agreement with the parton cascade results.

# Transverse Energy production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Kinetic theory, when combined with a model of transverse expansion informed by three-dimensional parton cascade simulations, can reproduce the time development of transverse energy predicted by the full cascade, but only if the transverse expansion is modeled via an effective area derived from the cascade's weight function rather than from first principles.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation for a boost-invariant 1+1D expansion, using a Boltzmann distribution initial condition on a constant proper-time hyperbola. For the 3D expansion case, they extract a transverse area weight function from the ZPC parton cascade and use it to model the freezeout of transverse energy outside a rarefaction front, allowing a semianalytic kinetic theory calculation of the transverse energy per unit rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section used in the parton cascade is given by σ = 9πα²/(2μ²), where α is the coupling and μ is the screening mass.
- The transport cross section is defined as σ_θ = ∫ sin²θ (dσ/dΩ) dΩ, with the differential cross section dσ/d\hat{t} = (9πα²/2)(μ²/\hat{s} + 1)/(\hat{t} - μ²)².
- The transverse area weight function is parameterized as w(r,τ) = 1 for r < R - v_cτ, w(r,τ) = ((R + v_cτ - r)/(2v_cτ))⁴ for R - v_cτ < r < R + v_cτ, and w(r,τ) = 0 for r > R + v_cτ, where R is the initial radius and v_c is the rarefaction front speed.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach of Kajantie (1987) and Baym (1984) for energy density evolution in 1D expansion to include transverse dynamics, which is necessary for comparison with experimental transverse energy measurements at RHIC. The authors use the ZPC parton cascade as a benchmark and show that their semianalytic model reproduces the cascade results, enabling faster calculations of transverse energy production without running full 3D cascade simulations. The paper was presented at the Brookhaven Theory Summer Study (RHIC97) and is part of the OSCAR standard testing effort for cascade simulations.
