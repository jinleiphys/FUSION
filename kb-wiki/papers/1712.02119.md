# Amplitude analysis of the anomalous decay $η'\toπ^+π^-γ$

> In this paper we perform an amplitude analysis of $η'\toπ^+π^-γ$ and confront it with the latest BESIII data. Based on the final-state interaction theorem, we represent the amplitude in terms of an Omnés function multiplied by a form factor that corresponds to the contributions from left-hand cuts and right-hand cuts in the inelastic channels. We also take into account the isospin violation effect induced by $ρ-ω$ mixing. Our results show that the anomaly contribution is mandatory in order to explain the data. Its contribution to the decay width of $Γ(η'\toππγ)$ is larger than that induced by isospin violation. Finally we extract the pole positions of the $ρ$ and $ω$ as well as their corresponding residues.

## Key claim

The anomaly contribution (Wess-Zumino-Witten term) is mandatory to explain the BESIII data for η′→π+π−γ, but the tree-level amplitude from the WZW term receives a sizable 1/NC correction of about 29% when final-state interactions and ρ–ω mixing are included in a dispersive analysis.

## Method

The decay amplitude was constructed using the final-state interaction theorem, representing the P-wave amplitude as an Omnès function multiplied by a polynomial form factor that accounts for left-hand cuts and inelastic right-hand cuts. The isospin-violating ρ–ω mixing contribution was calculated within resonance chiral theory (RχT) at leading order in the 1/NC expansion, and the full amplitude was fitted to the BESIII di-pion invariant mass spectrum (about 9.7×10⁵ events) together with the PDG total decay width.

## Key numbers

- Anomaly value from Fit 3: 5.61 ± 0.29 GeV⁻³ (shifted by 29% from the tree-level WZW matching value of 14.37 GeV⁻³)
- Total decay width Γ(η′→π⁺π⁻γ) from Fit 3: 62.0 ± 5.9 keV (PDG value: 57.3 ± 1.0 keV)
- ρ pole position: 762.7 ± 2.3 − i (68.3 ± 5.5) MeV
- ω pole position: 782.56 ± 0.12 − i (4.24 ± 0.04) MeV
- |g_{η′ργ}| from Fit 3: 20.1 ± 0.9 MeV
- |g_{η′ωγ}| from Fit 3: 5.68 ± 0.74 MeV
- χ²_average for Fit 3: 1.74 (compared to 12.3 for Fit 1 without isospin violation)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of η′→π⁺π⁻γ beyond earlier treatments by Hanhart (2011) and Kubis (2015), and provides the first extraction of ρ and ω pole parameters from this decay using the high-statistics BESIII data (Ablikim 2017). The method combines dispersion theory with resonance chiral theory, similar to approaches used for γγ→ππ (Moussallam), η→3π (Guo 2015), and Bℓ4 decays (Kang). The results enable improved determinations of the pion vector form factor and the hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
