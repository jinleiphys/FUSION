# Amplitude analysis of the anomalous decay $О·'\toПҖ^+ПҖ^-Оі$

> In this paper we perform an amplitude analysis of $О·'\toПҖ^+ПҖ^-Оі$ and confront it with the latest BESIII data. Based on the final-state interaction theorem, we represent the amplitude in terms of an OmnГ©s function multiplied by a form factor that corresponds to the contributions from left-hand cuts and right-hand cuts in the inelastic channels. We also take into account the isospin violation effect induced by $ПҒ-Пү$ mixing. Our results show that the anomaly contribution is mandatory in order to explain the data. Its contribution to the decay width of $О“(О·'\toПҖПҖОі)$ is larger than that induced by isospin violation. Finally we extract the pole positions of the $ПҒ$ and $Пү$ as well as their corresponding residues.

## Key claim

The anomaly contribution (Wess-Zumino-Witten term) is mandatory to explain the BESIII data for О·вҖІвҶ’ПҖ+ПҖвҲ’Оі, but the tree-level amplitude from the WZW term receives a sizable 1/NC correction of about 29% when final-state interactions and ПҒвҖ“Пү mixing are included in a dispersive analysis.

## Method

The decay amplitude was constructed using the final-state interaction theorem, representing the P-wave amplitude as an OmnГЁs function multiplied by a polynomial form factor that accounts for left-hand cuts and inelastic right-hand cuts. The isospin-violating ПҒвҖ“Пү mixing contribution was calculated within resonance chiral theory (RПҮT) at leading order in the 1/NC expansion, and the full amplitude was fitted to the BESIII di-pion invariant mass spectrum (about 9.7Г—10вҒө events) together with the PDG total decay width.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of О·вҖІвҶ’ПҖвҒәПҖвҒ»Оі beyond earlier treatments by Hanhart (2011) and Kubis (2015), and provides the first extraction of ПҒ and Пү pole parameters from this decay using the high-statistics BESIII data (Ablikim 2017). The method combines dispersion theory with resonance chiral theory, similar to approaches used for ОіОівҶ’ПҖПҖ (Moussallam), О·вҶ’3ПҖ (Guo 2015), and Bв„“4 decays (Kang). The results enable improved determinations of the pion vector form factor and the hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
