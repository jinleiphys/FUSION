# Kadanoff-Baym approach to bound states in open quantum systems

> In this paper, we extend the method of Kadanoff-Baym equations for open quantum systems to arbitrary kinds of systems and heat baths, either fermionic or bosonic. This includes three spacial dimensions and different potentials for the system-bath interaction or external traps. We study the quantum-mechanical formation of bound states in one and also in three dimensions with the full Kadanoff-Baym equations and compare them to more simplified approaches with and without memory effects. An in-depth examination of the thermodynamics of open systems is performed, showing perfect equilibration of the system's degrees of freedom along with a comprehensive investigation of the influence of the heat bath on the system's wave functions. The formation time, decay time and regeneration of bound states and their dependence on the temperature and coupling strength is explored We evaluate the non-equilibrium Kadanoff-Baym equations for the system particles, assuming that interactions are elastic two-particle collisions with the heat-bath particles. Finally, we describe in detail the method used to numerically solve the corresponding spatially heterogeneous integro-differential equations for the set of one-particle Green's functions.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Kadanoff-Baym equation framework for open quantum systems to arbitrary combinations of fermionic or bosonic system and bath particles in one or three spatial dimensions, showing that the method guarantees thermalization to the bath temperature and preserves local conservation laws, unlike Markovian master equations which are restricted to high-temperature, weak-coupling limits.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the non-equilibrium Kadanoff-Baym equations for one-particle Green's functions in the energy eigenbasis of the system Hamiltonian, using a second-order (direct Born) sunset self-energy that couples system particles to a thermalized heat bath through elastic two-particle collisions. The system particles are confined by an external potential (harmonic or double square-well) to produce a discrete spectrum, and the resulting coupled integro-differential equations are propagated in the two-time plane using a predictor-corrector Heun method. The approach is compared to two simplified approximations: the diagonal Kadanoff-Baym equations (which preserve non-Markovian memory but neglect decoherence) and a quantum kinetic master equation (which is Markovian like the Lindblad or Boltzmann equation).

## Key numbers

- Bound-state formation and thermalization are studied in one dimension and three dimensions, with system particles coupled to a heat bath at temperature T_bath and chemical potential mu_bath
- The spectral function width Gamma_{n,m}(t,omega) = -2 Im(Sigma^{ret}_{n,m}(t,omega)) is computed from the self-energies, reflecting the inverse lifetime of each quantum state
- The equal-time anti-commutation relation for fermions and bosons fixes c^{>}_{n,m}(t,t) ∓ c^{<}_{n,m}(t,t) = delta_{n,m}
- The bath Green's functions use the Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distribution n_{B/F}(epsilon_n) = 1/(exp((epsilon_n - mu_bath)/T) ∓ 1)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-dimensional fermionic system study (Neidig et al., 2024) to arbitrary particle species and three spatial dimensions, and contrasts the Kadanoff-Baym approach with Lindblad master equations (Gorini, 1975; Lindblad, 1976) and the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983), which are only valid in the high-temperature, weak-coupling limit. The method enables systematic study of bound-state formation (such as deuterons or J/Psi) in hot media relevant to heavy-ion physics, including memory effects, decoherence, and the influence of the heat bath on wave functions and energy eigenvalues.
