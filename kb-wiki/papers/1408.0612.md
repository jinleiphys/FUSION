# Properties of Thermal Photons at RHIC and LHC

> We study the emission characteristics of thermal photons at RHIC and LHC as affected by both the space-time evolution of the bulk medium and the thermal emission rates. For the former we compare the results of two evolution models (expanding fireball and hydrodynamics). For the latter, we detail the influence of hadronic emission components and study a speculative scenario by upscaling the default QGP and hadronic rates around the pseudo-critical region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that state-of-the-art thermal photon emission rates, combined with a realistic space-time evolution that includes sequential freezeout and initial flow, can bring calculated direct-photon elliptic flow closer to PHENIX data than previous calculations, but the default rates still do not fully account for the observed photon enhancement at RHIC and LHC.

## Method

The authors computed thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow by integrating thermal emission rates over two different space-time evolution models: an expanding fireball model and a (2+1)-D ideal hydrodynamic model, both using the same lattice-based equation of state with a pseudo-critical temperature of 170 MeV and adjusted to reproduce light and multistrange hadron spectra and elliptic flow. For the emission rates, they employed complete leading-order QGP rates and hadronic rates from the zero-mass limit of the in-medium rho spectral function (including baryon-induced processes) plus meson-gas t-channel exchange reactions, and tested a speculative scenario where both QGP and hadronic rates were upscaled by up to a factor of 3 around the pseudo-critical region.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature used in both evolution models is T_pc = 170 MeV.
* The hadro-chemical freezeout temperature is set to T_ch = 160 MeV, and kinetic freezeout for multistrange hadrons is imposed at T_ch.
* The measured inverse slope of the excess radiation in 0-20% Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is T_slope = (239 +/- 26) MeV.
* In the hadronic phase at RHIC, the hydro emission falls below the fireball emission by up to about 50% at transverse momentum q_T = 2 GeV.
* The maximum total direct-photon elliptic flow from the hydro evolution is about 25% smaller than from the fireball evolution.
* Switching off the hadronic photon contributions from the in-medium rho spectral function reduces the maximum total direct-photon elliptic flow in the fireball calculation by about 35%.
* Switching off the strong-decay feeddown reduces the maximum total direct-photon elliptic flow by nearly 10%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of thermal photon emission from heavy-ion collisions by comparing two different space-time evolution models (fireball and hydrodynamics) using the same equation of state and state-of-the-art emission rates, building on earlier work by van Hees (2011) that used a schematic fireball model. The study is motivated by the large direct-photon enhancement and elliptic flow measured by PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at LHC, and the results are closely related to thermal dilepton studies by Rapp (2009, 2013) where the rho meson spectral function plays a critical role. The findings enable future work to determine whether a pseudo-critical enhancement of thermal rates can be substantiated by microscopic calculations, and what implications such an enhancement would have for dilepton observables where current theory and data agree.
