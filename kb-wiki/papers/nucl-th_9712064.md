# Radiative processes of nucleon interactions and possible existence of exotic dibaryons

> The cross section of the reaction $pp \to pp γγ$ is estimated and the exploration possibilities of this reaction to probe for a possible excitation of subnuclear degrees of freedom in two-nucleon systems, in particular, the production and decay of the NN-decoupled dibaryon resonances, are briefly discussed.Some arguments for and implications of the intermediate dibaryon resonance excitation are inferred from preliminary data on the two-photon yield and energy distribution observed by the DIBAR2$γ$ - Collaboration at the kinetic energy of the initial proton $T_{lab} \simeq 200 MeV$.

# Radiative processes of nucleon interactions and possible existence of exotic dibaryons

## Key claim
The reaction $pp \to pp \gamma\gamma$ at a proton kinetic energy of approximately 200 MeV shows a distinct enhancement in the photon energy spectrum near 42 MeV, which may be interpreted as evidence for a narrow NN-decoupled dibaryon resonance with mass near 1923 MeV, though the author notes that the statistics of the earlier experiment were insufficient to draw firm conclusions and the theoretical predictions for such states remain model-dependent.

## Method
The cross section for the reaction $pp \to pp \gamma\gamma$ was estimated using a phenomenological model where the radiative transition proceeds through an intermediate $N\Delta$ state with quantum numbers $IJ^P = 11^+$, using a Hulthen-type radial wave function for the $d_1\Delta N$ vertex and the long-wave approximation for the magnetic-dipole transition matrix element. The non-resonant double-bremsstrahlung background was estimated using a simplified model with spin- and angle-averaged T-matrices derived from total pp cross sections, with magnetic moment dominance assumed for the 40 MeV photon energy region. Experimental data were obtained using a 198 MeV proton beam from the JINR phasotron incident on a liquid hydrogen target, with two photon detectors placed at backward angles of 111° and 240° covering solid angles of 35 msr and 70 msr.

## Key numbers
- Observed photon peak energy: 42.0 ± 4.5 MeV with width (FWHM) 26.3 ± 4.0 MeV from the DIBAR2γ Collaboration data at $T_{lab} \simeq 198$ MeV
- Reconstructed dibaryon mass: 1923.5 ± 4.5 MeV with width (FWHM) 31.3 ± 5.0 MeV
- Statistical significance of the peak: exceeds 8σ
- Estimated resonance cross section in center-of-mass frame: approximately 40 nb/sr for the $pp \to \gamma d_1$ process
- Estimated experimental cross section from data: approximately 65 nb/sr for one photon registered in the solid angle element
- Estimated non-resonant double-bremsstrahlung cross section: approximately 1.3 pb/sr²
- Ratio of non-resonant to experimental cross section: less than $O(10^{-3})$
- Proton beam energy: 198 MeV with energy spread about 1.5%
- Photon energy range measured: 10 to 100 MeV
- Integrated luminosity: $L = 10^{37}$ cm⁻², yielding about 130 events

## Context
This work extends earlier searches for narrow dibaryon resonances, particularly those that are NN-decoupled (i.e., with quantum numbers forbidding direct decay to two nucleons), as proposed in QCD-inspired models by Kondratyuk (1987) and Kopeliovich (1995) and in alternative standard NN-interaction models by Pokrovsky. The author notes that previous claims for narrow structures by BES (1990), Bublik, Elder, and Tatischeff (1992) were not confirmed in later experiments by Aleshkin (1992). The present results are compared with the chiral soliton model prediction of a $IJ^P = 11^+$ state near 1940 MeV, though the model's predicted radiative width of order eV is noted as much too low. The work enables future searches for exotic dibaryons in related processes such as double radiative pion capture and radiative muon capture in deuterium mesoatoms, and is relevant to Hanbury-Brown and Twiss-type two-photon interferometry in nucleus-nucleus collisions below the pion threshold.
