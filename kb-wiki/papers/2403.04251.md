# Theoretical calculations of proton emission half-lives based on a deformed Gamow-like model

> In the present study, proton emission half-lives have been investigated for the deformed proton emitters with $53\leq Z \leq 83$ in the deformed Gamow-like model, where the deformation effect has been included in the Coulomb potential. The experimental half-lives of proton emitters can be reproduced within a factor of 3.45. For comparison, other results from the universal decay law and the new Geiger-Nuttall law are presented as well. Furthermore, the relevance of the half-lives to the angular momentum $l$ for $^{117}$La, $^{121}$Pr, $^{135}$Tb and $^{141}$Ho has been analyzed, and corresponding possible values of $l$ has been put forward: $l=$3, 3, 4, 4.

# Theoretical calculations of proton emission half-lives based on a deformed Gamow-like model

## Key claim
The deformed Gamow-like model (D-GLM), which includes deformation effects in the Coulomb potential, can reproduce experimental proton emission half-lives for 45 deformed emitters with Z between 53 and 83 within a factor of 3.45, but the model shows larger deviations for four well-deformed nuclei (117La, 121Pr, 135Tb, 141Ho) that may be resolved by reassigning their angular momentum values to l=3, 3, 4, 4 respectively rather than the originally assumed values.

## Method
The authors modified the Gamow-like model by incorporating deformation effects into the Coulomb potential using quadrupole, hexadecapole, and hexacontatetrapole deformation parameters (lambda=2,4,6) for the daughter nucleus. The total interaction potential combines a square well interior with a deformed Coulomb potential and centrifugal term, and the barrier penetrability is calculated via the WKB approximation averaged over all emission angles. The single adjustable parameter, the effective nuclear radius constant r0, was determined by fitting to 45 experimental half-lives.

## Key numbers
- Standard deviation sigma = 0.533 for the D-GLM when fitting 45 experimental proton emission half-lives
- Effective nuclear radius constant r0 = 1.20 fm, obtained by minimizing the standard deviation
- Experimental half-lives reproduced within a factor of 3.45
- For comparison: sigma(UDLP) = 0.471, sigma(NG-N) = 0.515, sigma(GLM-Xiao) = 0.601
- Proposed angular momentum values: l=3 for 117La, l=3 for 121Pr, l=4 for 135Tb, l=4 for 141Ho
- For 117La with l=3: log10(T1/2) calculated = -1.591 versus experimental = -1.664
- For 121Pr with l=3: log10(T1/2) calculated = -1.742 versus experimental = -1.921
- For 135Tb with l=4: log10(T1/2) calculated = -3.252 versus experimental = -2.996
- For 141Ho with l=4: log10(T1/2) calculated = -2.760 versus experimental = -2.387

## Context
This work extends the Gamow-like model originally proposed by Zdeb (2013) for alpha decay and later applied to spherical proton emitters by Zdeb (2016), by incorporating nuclear deformation effects. It builds on previous deformed-model approaches including the Coulomb and proximity potential model for deformed nuclei (CPPMDN) by Santhosh (2017), the deformed density-dependent model by Qian and Ren (2016), and the modified two-potential approach for deformed nuclei (D-TPA) by Zhang (2023). The results enable improved half-life predictions for deformed proton emitters and suggest revised angular momentum assignments for specific nuclei that can guide future experimental measurements and theoretical studies.
