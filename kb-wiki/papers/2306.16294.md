# Corrections to the Forward Limit Dispersion Relations for $γZ$-Exchange Contributions

> The weak charge of the proton $Q_{\textrm{w}}$ is one of the most fundamental quantities in physics. It can be determined by measuring the parity asymmetry $A_{\textrm{PV}}$ in elastic $ep$ scattering, where the $γZ$-exchange contributions are crucial. For the past fifteen years, dispersion relations (DRs) in the forward limit have been widely used as a model-independent method to estimate these contributions. In this work, we study corrections to these forward-limit DRs. We first estimate these corrections using pointlike interactions as an illustrative example. We then estimate the $γZ$-exchange contributions for the upcoming P2 experiment through both direct calculation and the forward-limit DRs, within the framework of low-energy effective interactions. The results indicate that the correction to the forward-limit DR for $\Box_{γZ}^{V}$ is around 47\% for the upcoming P2 experiment, which will significantly modify the extracted value of $Q_{\textrm{w}}$.

# Corrections to the Forward Limit Dispersion Relations for γZ-Exchange Contributions

## Key claim
The forward-limit dispersion relations (DRs) widely used over the past fifteen years to estimate γZ-exchange contributions in parity-violating elastic ep scattering can significantly underestimate the vector box contribution Re[□_{γZ}^{V}] for experiments at low electron energies and finite momentum transfer. For the upcoming P2 experiment, the correction to the forward-limit DR for □_{γZ}^{V} is around 47%, which will substantially modify the extracted value of the proton weak charge Q_w.

## Method
The authors first used pointlike proton interactions as an illustrative example to compute the γZ-exchange box diagrams directly and via forward-limit DRs, quantifying the differences between Re[□_{γZ}^{V,A}(E,Q²)], the forward-limit approximation Re[□_{γZ}^{V,A}(E,0)], and two alternative DR formulations (C and D) at finite Q². They then employed low-energy effective interactions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in momentum, with electromagnetic form factors F₁, F₂ and weak couplings g₁, g₂, g₃, to estimate the γZ-exchange contributions for the P2 experiment parameters (Q²=0.0045 GeV², E=0.155 GeV) through both direct calculation and the forward-limit DRs.

## Key numbers
- For the P2 experiment (Q²=0.0045 GeV², E=0.155 GeV): Re[□_{γZ}^{V}(P2)] = 0.004685, while the forward-limit approximation C_{γZ}^{V}(P2) = 0.002221, giving a ratio of 47.41%.
- For the P2 experiment: Re[□_{γZ}^{A}(P2)] = 0.007383, while C_{γZ}^{A}(P2) = 0.007370, giving a ratio of 99.82%, indicating negligible correction for the axial box.
- For the Qweak experiment (Q²=0.0248 GeV², E=1.15 GeV): C_{γZ}^{V}(E,0)/Re[□_{γZ}^{V}(E,Q²)] = 97.6% in the pointlike model.
- For the G0 experiment at Q²=0.22 GeV², E=0.362 GeV: C_{γZ}^{V}(E,0)/Re[□_{γZ}^{V}(E,Q²)] = 35.8% in the pointlike model.
- For the G0 experiment at Q²=0.63 GeV², E=0.687 GeV: C_{γZ}^{V}(E,0)/Re[□_{γZ}^{V}(E,Q²)] = 27.2% in the pointlike model.
- For the SAMPLE experiment (Q²=0.1 GeV², E=0.2 GeV): C_{γZ}^{V}(E,0)/Re[□_{γZ}^{V}(E,Q²)] = 25.0% in the pointlike model.
- The dimensionless parameter (E-E_min)M_N/Q² ranges from 0.12 (SAMPLE) to 432 (Qweak), correlating with the size of the forward-limit correction.

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation approach for γZ-exchange contributions developed by Gorchtein (2009), Sibirtsev (2010), Blunden (2011), Rislow (2011), Gorchtein (2011), Hall (2013), and Seng (2019), who applied forward-limit DRs as a model-independent method. The authors show that the forward-limit approximation fails when (E-E_min)M_N/Q² is not sufficiently large, contradicting the assumption that the simple continuation formula of Gorchtein (2011) is adequate. The results enable improved extraction of the proton weak charge Q_w from the upcoming P2 experiment and suggest that future analyses should use DRs at finite Q² (such as Eq. DR3) applied to the invariant amplitudes F_{γZ,i}^{V,A} rather than to the box functions □_{γZ}^{V,A} directly.
