# Electron Capture in a Fully Ionized Plasma

> Properties of fully ionized water plasmas are discussed including plasma charge density oscillations and the screening of the Coulomb law especially in the dilute classical Debye regime. A kinetic model with two charged particle scattering events determines the transition rate per unit time for electron capture by a nucleus with the resulting nuclear transmutations. Two corrections to the recent Maiani et al. calculations are made: (i) The Debye screening length is only employed within its proper domain of validity. (ii) The WKB approximation employed by Maiani in the long De Broglie wave length limit is evidently invalid. We replace this incorrect approximation with mathematically rigorous Calogero inequalities in order to discuss the scattering wave functions. Having made these corrections, we find a verification for our previous results based on condensed matter electro-weak quantum field theory for nuclear transmutations in chemical batteries.

# Electron Capture in a Fully Ionized Plasma

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Rome group (Maiani et al., 2014) incorrectly predicted higher electron capture rates in cold plasmas than in hot plasmas, contradicting experimental observations; the present work claims that when Debye screening is applied only within its proper domain of validity and the WKB approximation is replaced by rigorous Calogero inequalities, the authors' previous results for hot plasma electron capture rates are verified.

## Method

The authors derive a thermodynamic sum rule for the screening length using charge fluctuation theory, establishing that the screening length is determined by the thermodynamic identity κ² = 4πe² Σ z_a z_b (∂²P/∂μ_a ∂μ_b)_T. They then apply the Calogero variable phase formalism for s-wave potential scattering to compute the electron-nuclear correlation function, replacing the WKB approximation used by Maiani et al. (2014) with rigorous lower bounds on the scattering length and electron density at the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Effective Bohr radius: a = 2.65 × 10⁻¹⁰ cm (for β = 20, where β is the electron mass enhancement factor)
- Temperature: T = 5.0 × 10³ °K
- Landau length: l_T = 3.34 × 10⁻⁷ cm
- Mean particle spacing: L = 5.0 × 10⁻⁵ cm
- Debye screening length: Λ_D = 1.73 × 10⁻⁴ cm
- Thermal De Broglie wavelength: λ_T = 2.36 × 10⁻⁸ cm
- Lower bound on s-wave cross section: σ_s(k) ≥ 16π Λ⁴ / a², where Λ is the screening length
- Lower bound on electron density at nucleus: n̄ ≥ n exp(4Λ / a), exponentially larger than the Maiani et al. (2014) prediction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous electro-weak quantum field theory for nuclear transmutations in chemical batteries (Widom, 2006; Widom, 2007) and responds to objections from Ciuchi et al. (2012) and Maiani et al. (2014) regarding electron capture rates in water plasmas. The paper corrects two specific errors in the Maiani et al. (2014) calculation: the improper application of Debye screening outside its classical regime and the invalid use of the WKB approximation in the long De Broglie wavelength limit. The results enable further theoretical work on electron capture in fully ionized plasmas for nuclear transmutation studies.
