# First results from Hybrid Hadronization in small and large systems

> "Hybrid Hadronization" is a new Monte Carlo package to hadronize systems of partons. It smoothly combines quark recombination applicable when distances between partons in phase space are small, and string fragmentation appropriate for dilute parton systems, following the picture outlined by Han et al. [PRC 93, 045207 (2016)]. Hybrid Hadronization integrates with PYTHIA 8 and can be applied to a variety of systems from $e^++e^-$ to $A+A$ collisions. It takes systems of partons and their color flow information, for example from a Monte Carlo parton shower generator, as input. In addition, if for $A+A$ collisions a thermal background medium is provided, the package allows sampling thermal partons that contribute to hadronization. Hybrid Hadronization is available for use as a standalone code and is also part of JETSCAPE since the 2.0 release. In these proceedings we review the physics concepts underlying Hybrid Hadronization and demonstrate how users can use the code with various parton shower Monte Carlos. We present calculations of hadron chemistry and fragmentation functions in small and large systems when Hybrid Hadronization is combined with parton shower Monte Carlos MATTER and LBT. In particular, we discuss observable effects of the recombination of shower partons with thermal partons.

# First results from Hybrid Hadronization in small and large systems

## Key claim
Hybrid Hadronization, a Monte Carlo package that smoothly combines quark recombination and string fragmentation, can describe hadron chemistry and fragmentation functions in both small and large collision systems, but the presented calculations do not include purely thermal hadrons and therefore are not yet directly comparable to experimental data.

## Method
The authors implemented a hybrid hadronization algorithm that first splits gluons into quark-antiquark pairs, then computes recombination probabilities for quarks close in phase space using the Wigner formalism with Gaussian wave packets fitted to hadron charge radii, and finally fragments the remaining strings with PYTHIA 8. For heavy-ion collisions, thermal partons sampled from a quark-gluon plasma brick with variable size and flow velocity are added to the shower partons before recombination. The parton showers were generated using MATTER and LBT Monte Carlo codes for 20 GeV quark jets.

## Key numbers
- 20 GeV: fixed energy of the jet-initiating quark used for all calculations
- 4 fm: size of the QGP brick used in the spacetime structure study of 50 GeV quark jets
- 1 GeV: cutoff scale Q0 below which partons are considered ready for hadronization
- 0.8c: flow velocity applied to sampled thermal partons in one set of calculations
- 10 GeV/c: approximate momentum below which enhanced baryon production from recombination is observed

## Context
This work extends the hybrid hadronization approach proposed by Han et al. (2016) by implementing it as a Monte Carlo package integrated with PYTHIA 8 and the JETSCAPE framework (Kauder, 2018). It builds on Lund string fragmentation (Andersson, 1983) as implemented in PYTHIA 8 (Sjostrand, 2014) and quark recombination models (Fries, 2008). The results enable future direct comparisons to experimental data from e+e- and p+p collisions once tuning of MATTER and Hybrid Hadronization in small systems is completed, and provide a framework for studying in-medium jet hadronization effects such as hadron chemistry, momentum diffusion, and medium flow.
