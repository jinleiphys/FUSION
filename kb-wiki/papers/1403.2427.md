# Isospin-invariant Skyrme energy-density-functional approach with axial symmetry

> We develop the isospin-invariant Skyrme-EDF method by considering local densities in all possible isospin channels and proton-neutron (p-n) mixing terms as mandated by the isospin symmetry. The EDF employed has the most general form that depends quadratically on the isoscalar and isovector densities. We test and benchmark the resulting p-n EDF approach, and study the general properties of the new scheme by means of the cranking in the isospin space. We extend the existing axial DFT solver HFBTHO to the case of isospin-invariant EDF approach with all possible p-n mixing terms. Explicit expressions have been derived for all the densities and potentials that appear in the isospin representation. In practical tests, we consider the Skyrme EDF SkM* and, as a first application, concentrate on Hartree-Fock aspects of the problem, i.e., pairing has been disregarded. Calculations have been performed for the (A=78, T~11), (A=40, T~8), and (A=48, T~4) isobaric analog chains. Isospin structure of self-consistent p-n mixed solutions has been investigated with and without the Coulomb interaction, which is the sole source of isospin symmetry breaking in our approach. The extended axial HFBTHO solver has been benchmarked against the symmetry-unrestricted HFODD code for deformed and spherical states. We developed and tested a general isospin-invariant Skyrme-EDF framework. The new approach permits spin-isospin densities that may give rise to, hitherto, unexplored modes in the excitation spectrum. The new formalism has been tested in the Hartree-Fock limit. A systematic comparison between HFODD and HFBTHO results show a maximum deviation of about 10 keV on the total binding energy for deformed nuclei when the Coulomb term is included. Without this term, the results of both solvers agree down to a ~10 eV level.

## Key claim

The authors develop an isospin-invariant Skyrme energy-density-functional (pnEDF) method that includes all possible proton-neutron (p-n) mixed local densities, but the practical tests are limited to the Hartree-Fock level with pairing disregarded, using only the SkM* parametrization.

## Method

The authors extended the existing axial DFT solver HFBTHO to incorporate isospin-invariant Skyrme-EDF with all p-n mixing terms, deriving explicit expressions for all densities and potentials in the isospin representation. They performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations for isobaric analog chains using two-dimensional isocranking in isospin space, varying the isocranking tilting angle to control the isospin of the system. The extended axial solver was benchmarked against the symmetry-unrestricted HFODD code for deformed and spherical states, using the Skyrme EDF parametrization SkM* with a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis of up to 16 shells.

## Key numbers

- Maximum deviation between HFODD and HFBTHO results is about 10 keV on the total binding energy for deformed nuclei when the Coulomb term is included; without the Coulomb term, results agree down to approximately 10 eV.
- For the A=78, T approximately 11 isobaric analog chain without Coulomb, the isocranking parameter lambda' = 21 MeV and lambda_off = 0 MeV; with Coulomb, lambda' = 22.94 MeV and lambda_off = -10.92 MeV.
- The absolute difference between neutron and proton Fermi energies in 78Ni is 12.31 MeV and in 78Sn is 33.62 MeV.
- The energy difference between N_sh=16 and N_sh=14 calculations varies around 73 keV at theta'=0 degrees and 135 keV at theta'=180 degrees for the A=78 system.
- The expectation value of T^2 for the T=11 system should be exactly 132 in the absence of isospin mixing.

## Context

This work extends the generalized EDF approach proposed by Perlińska et al. (2004) and Rohoziński et al. (2010) that allows arbitrary mixing of protons and neutrons, implementing it in the axial DFT solver HFBTHO which serves as the backbone of the EDF optimization package. The development complements a parallel study by Sato et al. (2013) that implemented p-n mixed densities in the symmetry-unrestricted solver HFODD, enabling benchmarking between the two approaches. The new framework permits spin-isospin densities that may give rise to previously unexplored modes in the excitation spectrum, and the axial implementation will allow global surveys of nuclear properties to be extended to observables and decays related to isospin.
