# Gamow-Teller response in the configuration space of DFT-rooted no-core configuration-interaction model

> The atomic nucleus is a unique laboratory to study fundamental aspects of the electroweak interaction. This includes a question concerning in medium renormalization of the axial-vector current, which still lacks satisfactory explanation. Study of spin-isospin or Gamow-Teller (GT) response may provide valuable information on both the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant as well as on nuclear structure and nuclear astrophysics. We have performed a seminal calculation of the GT response by using the no-core-configuration-interaction approach rooted on multi-reference density functional theory (DFT-NCCI). The model treats properly isospin and rotational symmetries and can be applied to calculate both the nuclear spectra and transition rates in atomic nuclei, irrespectively of their mass and particle-number parity. The method is applied to compute the GT strength distribution in selected $N\approx Z$ nuclei including the $p$-shell $^8$Li and $^8$Be nuclei and the $sd$-shell well-deformed nucleus $^{24}$Mg. In order to demonstrate a flexibility of the approach we present also a calculation of the superallowed GT beta decay in doubly-magic spherical $^{100}$Sn and the low-spin spectrum in $^{100}$In. It is demonstrated that the DFT-NCCI model is capable to capture the GT response satisfactorily well by using relatively small configuration space exhausting simultaneously the GT sum rule. The model, due to its flexibility and broad range of applicability, may either serve as a complement or even as an alternative to other theoretical approaches including the conventional nuclear shell model.

## Key claim

The DFT-NCCI model can capture the Gamow-Teller response satisfactorily well using relatively small configuration spaces while simultaneously exhausting the GT sum rule, but the method currently lacks coupling to particle-emission channels, which significantly affects the comparison to experimental strength distributions, particularly for the GT resonance in light nuclei.

## Method

The DFT-NCCI calculation proceeds by first building a configuration space of (multi)particle-(multi)hole Slater determinants computed from self-consistent Hartree-Fock solutions using the SV_T Skyrme pseudopotential. Isospin and angular-momentum projections are then applied, followed by isospin- and K-mixing to construct a model space of linearly dependent states with good angular momentum. Finally, the projected states are mixed by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to obtain eigenstates and energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- The DFT-NCCI model predicts a 2+ ground state in 8Li at -41.9 MeV, approximately 0.6 MeV below the experimental value.
- The ground state energy of 8Be calculated using DFT-NCCI is -52.8 MeV, underestimating the experimental value by 3.7 MeV.
- The empirical excitation energy ratio R_{4/2} = E(4_1+)/E(2_1+) in 8Be equals 3.75; the DFT-NCCI model gives R_{4/2} = 3.77.
- For the 8Li 2+ ground state decay to 8Be: the 2_1+ state is at 2.698 MeV (experiment: 3.030 MeV) with log ft = 4.74 (experiment: 5.36); the 2_2+ state is at 11.869 MeV (experiment: 16.626 MeV) with log ft = 3.54 (experiment: 2.93).
- In 24Mg, the energies of excited proton and neutron 1p-1h HF configurations differ by 80 keV.
- With five configurations in 8Be, the calculated GT sum rule reaches 90% of the full value; the remaining 2p-2h configurations provide approximately 5% of the strength.

## Context

This work extends the DFT-NCCI framework previously applied to spectra and beta-decay rates in N≈Z nuclei (Satula 2014, 2015, 2016; Konieczka 2016) and to 25Mg (Bally 2014) by computing, for the first time, the full spin-isospin (Gamow-Teller) response. The method is complementary to the conventional nuclear shell model (Caurier 2005), coupled cluster (Ekstrom 2014), and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) approaches (Nakamura 1997, Sarriguren 1998, Engel 1999, Moller 2003, Paar 2004, Mustonen 2016), and can serve as an alternative for global studies of GT strength distribution. The results support the two-body current explanation for the quenching of the axial coupling constant rather than a model-space origin, consistent with Menendez (2011) and Ekstrom (2014).
