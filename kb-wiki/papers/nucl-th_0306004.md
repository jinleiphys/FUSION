# Simple solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for systems with ellipsoidal symmetry

> Simple, self-similar, analytic solutions of (1+3)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics are presented for ellipsoidally symmetric finite fireballs corresponding to non-central collisions of heavy ions at relativistic bombarding energies. The hydrodynamical solutions are obtained for a new, general family of equations of state with the possibility of describing phase transitions.

# Simple solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for systems with ellipsoidal symmetry

## Key claim
The paper presents exact analytic solutions of (1+3)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics for ellipsoidally symmetric fireballs, but only for a coasting (accelerationless) Hubble-type flow where the Euler equation is satisfied regardless of the equation of state parameters because the flow has zero acceleration and the pressure field depends only on proper time, not on spatial coordinates.

## Method
The authors assume a self-similar, ellipsoidally symmetric ansatz with a scaling variable s = r_x^2/X^2 + r_y^2/Y^2 + r_z^2/Z^2 and a Hubble-type velocity field v_i = (dX_i/dt)/X_i * r_i. They solve the relativistic continuity, Euler, and temperature equations for a perfect fluid with an equation of state epsilon = m n + kappa p and p = n T, where m and kappa are free parameters. The solutions are obtained by requiring that the comoving derivative of the scaling variable vanishes and that the flow is of the form u^mu = x^mu/tau, which guarantees zero acceleration.

## Key numbers
- The pressure solution has the form p = p_0 (tau_0/tau)^(3 + 3/kappa), where kappa > 0 is a free parameter from the equation of state.
- The temperature solution is T = T_0 (tau_0/tau)^(3/kappa) * 1/N(s), where N(s) is an arbitrary positive differentiable scaling function normalized to N(0) = 1.
- The number density solution is n = n_0 (tau_0/tau)^3 * N(s).
- For the quark matter phase, the pressure is p_Q = (p_0,Q + B) (tau_0/tau)^(3 + 3/kappa_Q) - B, where B is the bag constant and kappa_Q = lambda_epsilon/lambda_p.
- The critical temperature T_c is defined by n_H T_c = lambda_p n_Q T_c - B.

## Context
This work extends the earlier (1+1)-dimensional relativistic scaling solutions of Csorgo et al. (2001) and the cylindrically symmetric solutions of Csorgo et al. (2002) to full ellipsoidal symmetry, relevant for non-central heavy-ion collisions. It generalizes the non-relativistic ellipsoidal solutions of Csorgo et al. (2003) to the relativistic regime, and the non-relativistic spherical solutions of Zimanyi, Bondorf, and Garpman (1978). The solutions enable analytic treatment of phase transitions from a deconfined quark matter state to a hadron gas, following the approach of Gyulassy and Matsui (1984), and can be matched to sudden rehadronization models as described by Csorgo and Csernai (1994) and Csernai and Mishustin (1995).
