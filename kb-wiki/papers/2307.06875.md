# Simultaneous calculation of elastic scattering, fusion, and direct cross sections for reactions of weakly-bound projectiles

> Simultaneous analyses are performed for cross section data of elastic scattering, fusion, Coulomb breakup, and other direct yields for the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. The bare and dynamical polarization potentials are constructed microscopically from the structure of the colliding nuclei and they reproduce all the data well with only one adjustable parameter. This method of calculation can be successfully applied to the reactions of weakly-bound and exotic projectiles with heavy targets.

# Simultaneous calculation of elastic scattering, fusion, and direct cross sections for reactions of weakly-bound projectiles

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a single optical model potential with only two adjustable parameters can simultaneously reproduce elastic scattering, fusion, Coulomb breakup, and direct nuclear reaction cross sections for the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system at near-barrier energies, provided the bare potential is constructed from the São Paulo potential and the dynamical polarization potentials are built microscopically from the projectile structure. However, the method's success depends on fixing the nuclear dynamical polarization potential geometry from semiclassical theory using the separation energy, and the two adjustable parameters are the strengths of its real and imaginary parts.

## Method
The total optical potential combines a bare nuclear potential (real part from the São Paulo potential, imaginary part with fixed normalization factors $N_R=1.00$ and $N_I=0.78$), a Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP) derived from solving the Schrödinger equation for a two-body projectile under the adiabatic approximation, and a nuclear dynamical polarization potential (NDPP) of derivative Woods-Saxon form. The NDPP radius is set to the strong absorption radius $R_s=1.4(A_p^{1/3}+A_t^{1/3})$ and its diffuseness to $a_L=1/(2\kappa_0)$ where $\kappa_0=\sqrt{2\mu\varepsilon_0}/\hbar$, with only the real and imaginary strengths $V_L$ and $W_L$ varied to fit elastic scattering data. Cross sections for fusion, Coulomb breakup, and direct nuclear reactions are then calculated from the corresponding imaginary potential components using the distorted wave function.

## Key numbers
- For $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi at $E_{\text{c.m.}}=14.3$ MeV: fusion cross section $\sigma_F=0.4$ mb, Coulomb breakup $\sigma^C_{\text{bu}}=51$ mb, direct nuclear $\sigma_{\text{DN}}=162$ mb, total reaction $\sigma_{\text{Reac}}=214$ mb.
- For $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi at $E_{\text{c.m.}}=21.9$ MeV: fusion cross section $\sigma_F=398$ mb, Coulomb breakup $\sigma^C_{\text{bu}}=227$ mb, direct nuclear $\sigma_{\text{DN}}=578$ mb, total reaction $\sigma_{\text{Reac}}=1202$ mb.
- The NDPP diffuseness for $^{6}$He is $a_L=2.0$ fm using the actual $2n$ separation energy $\varepsilon=0.975$ MeV, or $a_L=1.565$ fm using the effective separation energy $\varepsilon=1.6$ MeV.
- The CDPP becomes purely real for large separation energies and has an imaginary-to-real ratio approaching $\sqrt{3}$ as the breakup energy tends to zero, independent of $\varepsilon_0$.
- The dipole polarizability from the CDPP is $\alpha_0 = \frac{1}{16} \frac{\hbar^2 \mu_p Z_P^2 e^2}{m_c^2 \varepsilon_0^2}$.

## Context
This work extends the extended optical model approach of Udagawa and collaborators (1984-1990) and the CDPP formalism developed by Maridi et al. (2021, 2022) to simultaneously describe all reaction channels for weakly-bound projectiles. It builds on prior analyses of the $^{6}$He+$^{209}$Bi system by Kim et al. (2002), Garcia et al. (2007), and others who studied subsets of these data. The method enables future application to other weakly-bound and exotic projectiles (e.g., $^{11}$Li, $^{11}$Be) with heavy targets, where the NDPP geometry can be predicted from the separation energy rather than fitted empirically.
