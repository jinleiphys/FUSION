# Suppression of high transverse momentum hadrons at RHIC by (pre-) hadronic final state interactions

> We investigate transverse hadron spectra from proton+proton, deuteron+Au and Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s}=200 GeV and 17.3 GeV within the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) approach which is based on quark, diquark, string and hadronic degrees of freedom as well as Pythia calculations for the high pT spectra. The comparison to experimental data on transverse mass spectra from p+p, d+Au and Au+Au reactions shows that pre-hadronic effects are responsible for both the hardening of the spectra for low transverse momenta (Cronin effect) as well as the suppression of high pT hadrons. The interactions of formed hadrons are found to be negligible in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s}=200 GeV for pT\geq 6 GeV/c, but have some importance for the shape of the ratio R_{AA} at lower pT values (\leq 6 GeV/c). The large suppression seen experimentally is attributed to the inelastic interactions of 'leading' pre-hadrons with the dense environment, which could be partly of colored partonic nature in order to explain the large attenuation seen in most central Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The suppression of high transverse momentum hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is attributed primarily to inelastic interactions of "leading" pre-hadrons (color-neutral objects containing quarks from the struck nucleons) with the dense environment, rather than to interactions of fully formed hadrons; the authors note that this pre-hadronic attenuation "could be partly of colored partonic nature" to explain the large suppression seen experimentally.

## Method

The authors employed the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach, which includes quark, diquark, string, and hadronic degrees of freedom, combined with Pythia v6.2 calculations for high pT spectra. They simulated proton+proton, deuteron+Au, and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and 17.3 GeV, tracking the propagation of high pT hadrons within a full background of strings and hadrons while computing their collisional history with baryons, mesons, quarks, and diquarks using fractional cross sections based on constituent quark counting rules.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the attenuation of leading pions, kaons, and vector mesons is roughly independent of pT and hadron type, giving R_AA approximately 0.09
- The interactions of formed hadrons are negligible for pT greater than or equal to 6 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- The fraction of leading pre-hadrons increases almost linearly with pT and approximately saturates above pT approximately 6 GeV/c for sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- The Cronin enhancement parameter alpha in the range 0.25 to 0.4 is fixed by comparison to d+Au collision data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- The default average kT^2 for pp collisions is kT^2_pp = 0.36 GeV^2
- The hadron formation time tau_f is estimated to be in the order of 0.4 to 0.8 fm/c based on studies of antiproton production and hadron attenuation in cold nuclear matter
- For pT greater than or equal to 6 GeV/c, reducing the energy density cut from 1 GeV/fm^3 to 0.7 GeV/fm^3 changes R_AA from approximately 0.4 to approximately 0.45 (a 10% modification)

## Context

This work extends the HSD transport approach previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions from AGS to SPS energies (Weber, 2002) and to RHIC energies (Bratkovskaya, 2003). The study directly addresses the interpretation of the large hadron suppression observed by the PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS collaborations at RHIC, which had been suggested as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation via partonic energy loss (Wang, Baier, Vitev). The authors argue that pre-hadronic final state interactions can explain both the Cronin enhancement at low pT and the suppression at high pT, and note that their approach is compatible with hadron attenuation measurements from the HERMES collaboration in deep inelastic lepton scattering off nuclei (Falter, 2004). The work enables quantitative separation of hadronic and partonic contributions to jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
