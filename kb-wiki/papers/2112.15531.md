# Hydrodynamic helicity polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study helicity polarization through the (3+1) dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic models at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$GeV Au+Au collisions. Similar to the local spin polarization, we consider the helicity polarization beyond global equilibrium and investigate the contributions induced by thermal vorticity, shear viscous tensor, and the fluid acceleration. We find that the local helicity polarization induced by thermal vorticity dominates over other contributions. It also implies that in the low-energy collisions, the the fluid vorticity as part of thermal vorticity may play the crucial role to the total helicity polarization. Such a finding could be useful for probing the local strength of vorticity in rotational quark gluon plasmas by measuring helicity polarization. Our simulation confirms the strict space reversal symmetry, whereas we also compare our numerical results with approximated relations derived from ideal Bjorken flow. Our studies also provide a baseline for the future investigation on local parity violation through the correlations of helicity polarization.

## Key claim

The authors find that local helicity polarization induced by thermal vorticity dominates over contributions from shear viscous tensor and fluid acceleration in (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations at √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption of zero axial chemical potential and applies specifically to mid-rapidity regions.

## Method

The authors implement the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code CLVisc with AMPT initial conditions at 20%-50% centrality to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They compute helicity polarization from the modified Cooper-Frye formula by decomposing the mean spin vector into contributions from thermal vorticity, shear viscous tensor, and fluid acceleration, then project the spatial spin vector onto the momentum direction of the final hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality: 20%-50%
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 1/(4π)
- Freeze-out temperature: T = 157 MeV
- Transverse momentum integration range: pTmin = 0 GeV to pTmax = 3 GeV
- Rapidity window: ΔY = 1, covering mid-rapidity [-1, +1]

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for helicity polarization proposed by Becattini (2020) and Gao (2021) by including off-equilibrium contributions from shear viscous tensor and fluid acceleration beyond the original thermal vorticity treatment. The study provides a baseline for future investigation of local parity violation through helicity-helicity correlations, as the axial chemical potential contribution was deliberately set to zero. The results also relate to the ongoing "sign problem" in spin polarization studies, where hydrodynamic simulations have disagreed with STAR collaboration measurements of local spin polarization.
