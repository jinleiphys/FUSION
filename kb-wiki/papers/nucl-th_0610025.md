# Photonuclear sum rules and the tetrahedral configuration of $^4$He

> Three well known photonuclear sum rules (SR), i.e. the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn, the bremsstrahlungs and the polarizability SR are calculated for 4He with the realistic nucleon-nucleon potential Argonne V18 and the three-nucleon force Urbana IX. The relation between these sum rules and the corresponding energy weighted integrals of the cross section is discussed. Two additional equivalences for the bremsstrahlungs SR are given, which connect it to the proton-neutron and neutron-neutron distances. Using them, together with our result for the bremsstrahlungs SR, we find a deviation from the tetrahedral symmetry of the spatial configuration of 4He. The possibility to access this deviation experimentally is discussed.

# Photonuclear sum rules and the tetrahedral configuration of $^4$He

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlungs sum rule (BSR) for $^4$He, calculated with the Argonne V18 two-nucleon potential plus the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, implies that the spatial configuration of the four nucleons deviates slightly from perfect tetrahedral symmetry, with the proton-neutron and neutron-neutron mean-square distances differing by about 6%. This symmetry breaking is accessible experimentally via combined measurements of the charge radius and the BSR.

## Method

The authors calculated three photonuclear sum rules (Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn, bremsstrahlungs, and polarizability) for $^4$He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with hyperspherical harmonics expansions (EIHH approach). They solved the many-body Schrödinger equation with the realistic AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. The sum rules were obtained both directly from Lanczos coefficients and by integrating the explicitly calculated total photoabsorption cross section up to 300 MeV. Two new equivalences for the bremsstrahlungs sum rule were derived, connecting it to the neutron-neutron and proton-neutron mean-square distances.

## Key numbers

- TRK enhancement factor: $\kappa^{\rm TRK} = 1.31$ for AV18 alone, and $\kappa^{\rm TRK} = 1.44$ for AV18+UIX.
- Bremsstrahlungs sum rule $\Sigma^{\rm BSR} = 2.41$ mb (AV18+UIX, from sum rule evaluation).
- Mean-square point nucleon radius: $\langle r^2 \rangle = 2.04$ fm$^2$ (AV18+UIX, coinciding with the experimental value corrected for proton charge radius).
- Extracted mean-square nucleon-nucleon distances: $\langle r^2_{pp} \rangle = \langle r^2_{nn} \rangle = 5.67$ fm$^2$ and $\langle r^2_{pn} \rangle = 5.34$ fm$^2$, a difference of about 6%.
- Ratios to tetrahedral value $Q_T = 8/3 \approx 2.67$: $Q_{pp} = 2.78$ and $Q_{np} = 2.62$.
- Distance between proton and neutron centers of mass: $R_{pn} = 1.58$ fm, versus 1.65 fm for the tetrahedral configuration.
- Electric dipole polarizability $\alpha_D = 0.0655$ fm$^3$ (AV18+UIX) and $0.0768$ fm$^3$ (AV18 alone), a 15% reduction from the three-nucleon force.
- The "tetrahedral" BSR value would be 2.62 mb, which is 9% larger than the calculated result of 2.41 mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier sum-rule studies on two-body systems (2bodySR, 2005) and three-body systems (ELO97pol, 1997) to the more compact $^4$He nucleus, which resembles heavier systems more closely. The authors use the same AV18+UIX potential model as in their prior calculation of the $^4$He total photoabsorption cross section (prl06, 2006). The new equivalences for the bremsstrahlungs sum rule (Eqs. BSR3 and BSR4) connect the sum rule to neutron-neutron and proton-neutron distances, enabling a test of the tetrahedral symmetry assumption originally made by Foldy (1957). The results enable experimental determination of two-body ground-state properties from photonuclear measurements at existing or planned low-intermediate energy facilities.
