# Pairing in nuclear systems with effective Gogny and V_low-k interactions

> The pairing properties of nuclear systems are a sensitive probe of the effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. We compare the 1S0 pairing gaps in nuclear and neutron matter derived from the phenomenological Gogny interaction and a renormalization group motivated low-momentum V_low-k interaction extracted from realistic interactions. We find that the pairing gaps predicted by these interactions are in an excellent agreement in a wide range of sub-nuclear densities. The close agreement between the predictions of the effective forces remains intact in the case where the single particle spectra in neutron and nuclear matter are renormalized within the Hartree-Fock theory.

## Key claim

The pairing gaps in the $^1S_0$ channel predicted by the phenomenological Gogny interaction and the renormalization-group-based $V_{\rm low~k}$ interaction agree to within about 10% over a wide range of subnuclear densities (Fermi momenta $0.4 \le k_F \le 1.0$ fm$^{-1}$), but this close agreement degrades at higher densities and depends on the renormalization of the single-particle spectrum.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for infinite, zero-temperature symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using two effective interactions: the finite-range Gogny D1S parameterization and the $V_{\rm low~k}$ interaction derived from the Nijmegen 93 potential with a momentum cut-off $\Lambda = 2.5$ fm$^{-1}$. They computed the pairing gaps both with a free single-particle spectrum and with spectra renormalized in the Hartree-Fock (Gogny) or Brueckner-Hartree-Fock ($V_{\rm low~k}$) approximations, and compared the diagonal matrix elements $V(k_F, k_F)$ and the momentum-dependent gap functions.

## Key numbers

* For symmetric nuclear matter with free single-particle spectrum at $k_F = 0.6$ fm$^{-1}$, the Gogny gap is 2.60 MeV and the $V_{\rm low~k}$ gap is 2.66 MeV, giving a ratio $R_\Delta = 0.978$.
* At $k_F = 1.0$ fm$^{-1}$, the Gogny gap is 3.47 MeV and the $V_{\rm low~k}$ gap is 3.06 MeV, with ratio $R_\Delta = 1.131$.
* At $k_F = 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$, the Gogny gap is 1.66 MeV and the $V_{\rm low~k}$ gap is 0.56 MeV, with ratio $R_\Delta = 2.964$.
* The diagonal matrix elements $V(k_F, k_F)$ for the Gogny interaction range from -39.5 MeV at $k_F = 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$ to -7.6 MeV at $k_F = 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$; for $V_{\rm low~k}$ they range from -49.1 MeV to -5.9 MeV over the same Fermi momenta.
* The $V_{\rm low~k}$ cut-off scale is $\Lambda = 2.5$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to a distance scale $\Lambda^{-1} \sim 0.4$ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons showing that Gogny interactions predict pairing properties close to those from bare realistic interactions (Gogny Pairing 1, 2001) and complements studies of $V_{\rm low~k}$ pairing by Schwenk (2002) and Kuckei (2003). The close agreement between the two effective forces, despite their very different origins, supports the use of either interaction in mean-field calculations of pairing in finite nuclei, but the authors note that extrapolation to finite nuclei may require systematic reductions of the pairing gap, as observed with the Argonne interaction in tin isotopes (Vigezzi, 2000).
