# Spontaneus pion emission during fission - a new nuclear radioactivity

> In this paper a short review of the theoretical problems of the pionic radioactivity is presented. The essential experimental results obtained in the 18 years of existence of the nuclear pionic radioactivity are reviewed. Moreover, using the recent results on the spontaneous fission half lives of the heavy nuclei with Z >= 100 new predictions on the pionic yields in the region of superheavy elements are established.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of nuclear pionic radioactivity (NPIR) and, using recent spontaneous fission half-life data for nuclei with Z ≥ 100, predicts that for certain superheavy nuclides the pionic decay branch can exceed the spontaneous fission branch, but these predictions depend on the choice of the parameter α (the fraction of pion mass taken from Coulomb energy) and are subject to the validity of a scaling property derived from fission systematics.

## Method

The authors extend a fission-like liquid-drop model for pionic radioactivity by defining a pionic fissility X_πF and a pionic fission barrier height E^πF(LD), then introduce a scaling property relating the logarithmic half-life function τ(θ) for spontaneous fission to an analogous function τ(θ_πF) for pionic fission. By fitting experimental spontaneous fission half-lives for even-even and A-odd parent nuclei to obtain τ(θ) = a₀ + a₁θ, they derive a formula for the relative pionic yield Γ_π/Γ_SF in terms of the ratio of spontaneous fission half-life to a reference half-life T_SF^C, raised to a power δ_α(A,Z) that depends on the parameter α.

## Key numbers

* For even-even parent nuclei, the fitted logarithmic half-life function is τ(θ) = 19.70 − 6.13θ (Equation 36).
* For A-odd parent nuclei, the fitted function is τ(θ) = 28.21 − 7.32θ (Equation 37).
* The reference spontaneous fission half-life T_SF^C is 10^−10.95 yr for even-even nuclei and 10^−8.39 yr for A-odd nuclei (Equations 42–43).
* The best experimental upper limit for π-yields from ²⁵²Cf is 3×10^−13 (Bellini et al.).
* An inferred pionic yield from ²⁵⁷Fm is Γ_π/Γ_SF = (1.2 ± 0.2)×10^−3 for π⁻/fission (Ion, Bucurescu and Ion-Mihai).
* A similar yield from ²⁵⁷Fm is Γ_π/Γ_SF = (7±)×10^−5 for π⁺/fission (Janko and Povinec).
* Predicted pionic yields for specific nuclides include: ²⁵⁰No (6.67), ²⁵⁸No (7.1×10^−2), ²⁶²No (1.2×10^−2), ²⁵⁸Sg (1.3×10^7), ²⁵⁹Sg (6.1×10^6), ²⁶⁰Sg (1.2×10^8), ²⁶¹Sg (1.3×10^11), ²⁶³Sg (5.2×10^27), and ²⁶⁴Hs (1.2×10^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' own 1985 proposal of nuclear pionic radioactivity (Ion, Ivascu and Ion-Mihai, 1985) and reviews experimental searches by Bellini et al., Wild et al., and Khryachkov et al. (2003). It also interprets the supergiant radiohalos of Grady and Walker and Laemmlein as signatures of pionic radioactivity, and connects the observed symmetrization of fragment mass-yields in spontaneous fission of nuclei with Z=98–104 to the onset of pionic decay. The predictions for superheavy elements (Sg, Hs) suggest that pionic radioactivity could become the dominant decay mode, motivating dedicated experiments with nuclides such as ²⁵⁸Fm, ²⁵⁹Fm, ²⁵⁸No, ²⁶⁰No, ²⁵⁴Rf, Sg isotopes, and ²⁶⁴Hs.
