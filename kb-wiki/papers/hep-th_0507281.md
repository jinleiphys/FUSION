# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation with Exact Propagators

> Consequent application of the instantaneous approximation to both the interaction and all propagators of the bound-state constituents allows us to forge, within the framework of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the description of bound states, an instantaneous form of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with exact (i.e., full) propagators of the bound-state constituents. This instantaneous equation generalizes the well-known Salpeter equation the derivation of which needs the additional assumption of free propagation of the bound-state constituents.

# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Equation with Exact Propagators

## Key claim
The authors derive an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that uses exact (full) propagators for the bound-state constituents, rather than the free-propagator approximation required by the standard Salpeter equation. This generalization is achieved by applying the instantaneous approximation not only to the interaction kernel but also to the propagators themselves, specifically by assuming the Lorentz-scalar functions characterizing each exact fermion propagator depend only on the spatial components of momentum.

## Method
Starting from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for a fermion-antifermion bound state, the authors impose the instantaneous approximation on the Bethe-Salpeter kernel (making it depend only on spatial momentum components) and also on the exact fermion propagators by taking the functions M_i(p^2) and Z_i(p^2) to depend only on the squared three-momentum. Using the partial fraction decomposition of the exact fermion propagator in terms of energy projection operators, they perform the contour integration over the time component of relative momentum to obtain an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. This equation is then recast as an eigenvalue problem for the Salpeter amplitude, with the bound-state energy as the eigenvalue, subject to two constraints that force half of the amplitude components to vanish.

## Key numbers
- The bound-state mass M_B appears as the eigenvalue in the rest-frame eigenvalue equation, where P_0 = M_B when the total three-momentum P = 0.
- The one-particle energy for each constituent is defined as E_i(p) = sqrt(p^2 + M_i^2(p^2)), where M_i(p^2) is the momentum-dependent mass function from the exact propagator.
- The free-propagator limit recovers the Salpeter equation by taking M_i(p^2) → m_i and Z_i(p^2) → 1, where m_i are the effective constituent masses.

## Context
This work extends the Salpeter equation (Salpeter, 1952) by removing the assumption of free-particle propagation for bound-state constituents, which has been a standard but conceptually problematic approximation in meson phenomenology studies (Lucha, 1991; Lucha, 1992). The authors note that in non-Abelian gauge theories like quantum chromodynamics, the simultaneous assumptions of free-particle propagation and confining interactions are intrinsically inconsistent, as argued by Boehm (1975), Alabiso (1976, 1977), and others. The derived formalism enables the use of propagators obtained from Dyson-Schwinger equations or lattice computations, potentially improving descriptions of mesons as quark-antiquark bound states and facilitating the embedding of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking mechanisms.
