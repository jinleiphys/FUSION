# Combining symmetry breaking and restoration with configuration interaction: extension to z-signature symmetry in the case of the Lipkin Model

> Background: Ab initio many-body methods whose numerical cost scales polynomially with the number of particles have been developed over the past fifteen years to tackle closed-shell mid-mass nuclei. Open-shell nuclei have been further addressed by implementing variants based on the concept of spontaneous symmetry breaking (and restoration). Purpose: In order to access the spectroscopy of open-shell nuclei more systematically while controlling the numerical cost, we design a novel many-body method that combines the merit of breaking and restoring symmetries with those brought about by low-rank individual excitations. Methods: The recently proposed truncated configuration-interaction method based on optimized symmetry-broken and -restored states is extended to the z-signature symmetry associated with a discrete subgroup of SU(2). The highly-truncated N-body Hilbert subspace within which the Hamiltonian is diagonalized is spanned by a z-signature broken and restored Slater determinant vacuum and associated low-rank excitations. Results: The proposed method provides an excellent reproduction of the ground-state energy and of low-lying excitation energies of various z-signatures and total angular momenta. In doing so, the successive benefits of (i) breaking the symmetry, (ii) restoring the symmetry, (iii) including low-rank particle-hole excitations and (iv) optimizing the amount by which the underlying vacuum breaks the symmetry are illustrated. Conclusions: The numerical cost of the newly designed variational method is polynomial with respect to the system size. The present study confirms the results obtained previously for the attractive pairing Hamiltonian in connection with the breaking and restoration of U(1) global gauge symmetry. These two studies constitute a strong motivation to apply this method to realistic nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed truncated configuration-interaction (TCI) method, which combines symmetry breaking and restoration with low-rank particle-hole excitations, to the z-signature symmetry associated with a discrete subgroup of SU(2) in the context of the Lipkin model; the method's accuracy is demonstrated across the full range of the two-body interaction strength, including the normal-to-deformed quantum phase transition, but the authors note that the Lipkin Hamiltonian is "too simplistic" to test the full generalized ansatz that mixes both horizontal (deformation) and vertical (particle-hole) expansions.

## Method

The method diagonalizes the Hamiltonian within a highly truncated N-body Hilbert subspace spanned by a z-signature broken and restored Slater determinant vacuum, plus associated low-rank (1p1h and 2p2h) excitations. The deformation parameter \(\alpha\) of the underlying symmetry-unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF) vacuum is optimized in the presence of both the symmetry restoration and the projected low-rank excitations. The approach is applied to the exactly solvable Lipkin model with \(N\) fermions distributed over two N-fold degenerate shells, using the quasi-spin algebra to express the Hamiltonian in terms of \(J_z\) and \(J_\pm\) operators.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy per particle error remains below \(1.6 \times 10^{-3} \varepsilon\) (relative error below \(0.3\%\)) for all particle numbers \(N=16,\dots,100\) over the full interval \(\chi \in [0,3]\) using the optimized TCI method including 1p1h and 2p2h configurations.
* The critical reduced interaction strength for the normal-to-deformed phase transition is \(\chi_c = 1.0\) (in units where \(\varepsilon=1\)).
* For \(N=10\) and \(\chi=3.0 \gg \chi_c\), the UHF minimum occurs at \(\alpha = \arccos(1/\chi)/2 = 0.615\) radians.
* The RHF ground-state energy for \(N=20\) is \(-N/2 = -10\) (in units of \(\varepsilon\)).

## Context

This work extends the TCI method previously applied to the attractive pairing Hamiltonian with U(1) global gauge symmetry breaking and restoration (Ripoche 2017) to the z-signature symmetry of the Lipkin model. The method is compared against exact solutions, the generator coordinate method (GCM), the random-phase approximation (RPA), and the self-consistent (second) random-phase approximation (SCRPA), with the TCI method outperforming the symmetry-restricted RPA and SCRPA in the strongly-interacting regime. The results motivate applying this approach to realistic nuclear Hamiltonians for ab initio description of mid-mass open-shell nuclei, building on concepts from the variation after mean-field projection (VAMPIR) method (Schmid 1984) and the projected shell model (Hara 1995).
