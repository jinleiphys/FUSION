# High-K Precession modes: Axially symmetric limit of wobbling motion

> The rotational band built on the high-K multi-quasiparticle state can be interpreted as a multi-phonon band of the precession mode, which represents the precessional rotation about the axis perpendicular to the direction of the intrinsic angular momentum. By using the axially symmetric limit of the random-phase-approximation (RPA) formalism developed for the nuclear wobbling motion, we study the properties of the precession modes in $^{178}$W; the excitation energies, B(E2) and B(M1) values. We show that the excitations of such a specific type of rotation can be well described by the RPA formalism, which gives a new insight to understand the wobbling motion in the triaxial superdeformed nuclei from a microscopic view point.

## Key claim

The rotational band built on a high-K multi-quasiparticle state can be interpreted as a multi-phonon band of the precession mode, which is the axially symmetric limit of the wobbling motion; the authors show that this precession mode is well described by the random-phase-approximation (RPA) formalism, provided the mean-field is axially symmetric about the rotation axis and the angular momentum is generated by quasiparticle alignment rather than collective rotation.

## Method

The authors applied the axially symmetric limit of the RPA formalism previously developed for nuclear wobbling motion, using the "minimal coupling" residual interaction that restores rotational invariance. They calculated the precession mode properties for ten high-K isomeric configurations in 178W, using the Nilsson potential with pairing, and computed excitation energies, B(E2) values, and B(M1) values for the one-phonon precession bands built on each isomer.

## Key numbers

- Experimental one-phonon precession energies for ten high-K isomers in 178W range from 150 keV (K=14+ isomer) to 621 keV (K=34+ isomer)
- The K=13- isomer has an experimental precession energy of 164 keV; the K=14+ isomer has 150 keV; the K=15+ isomer has 207 keV
- The K=18- isomer has an experimental precession energy of 184 keV; the K=21- isomer has 362 keV; the K=22- isomer has 373 keV
- The K=25+ isomer has an experimental precession energy of 288 keV; the K=28- isomer has 328 keV; the K=29+ isomer has 437 keV; the K=30+ isomer has 559 keV; the K=34+ isomer has 621 keV
- The RPA calculation reproduces the experimental precession energies within approximately 50-100 keV for most configurations

## Context

This work extends the RPA wobbling formalism of Marshalek (1979) and the authors' previous studies of wobbling in triaxial superdeformed nuclei (Shimizu, Matsuzaki, Matsuyanagi, 2004) to the axially symmetric limit, connecting it to the precession band formalism developed by Kurasawa (1975), Andersson (1976), and Skalski (1990). The results provide a microscopic foundation for understanding the recently observed wobbling motion in triaxial superdeformed nuclei (observed in Lu and Hf isotopes around 2001-2003) and enable future studies of the relationship between precession bands and chiral rotation bands.
