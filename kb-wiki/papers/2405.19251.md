# Dark matter admixed neutron stars with a realistic nuclear equation of state from chiral nuclear interactions

> We study the effects of dark matter on the structural properties of neutron stars. In particular we investigate how the presence of a dark matter component influences the mass-radius relation, the value of the maximum mass of a neutron star and other stellar properties. To model ordinary matter we use a state-of-the-art equation of state of $β$-stable nuclear matter obtained using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock quantum many-body approach starting from two-body and three-body nuclear interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. The dark matter component of the star is modeled as a non-self-annihilating system of spin $1/2$ fermions at zero temperature and its equation of state as an ideal relativistic Fermi gas. The equilibrium configurations of these dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANS) are calculated by solving a generalization of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to the case where the system consists of two perfect fluids interacting solely through gravity. We find that, depending on the dark matter particle mass $m_χ$, one can have somehow opposite effects on the stellar properties. In the case $m_χ= 1\, \mathrm{GeV}$, the stellar gravitational maximum mass $M_{max}$ decreases, whereas in the case $m_χ= 0.1\, \mathrm{GeV}$, $M_{max}$ increases with respect to the maximum mass of ordinary neutron stars. We also show that the presence of dark matter has indirect sizable effect on the proton fraction in the ordinary matter fluid and, in the case $m_χ= 1\, \mathrm{GeV}$, results in a decrease of the threshold gravitational mass $M_{tot}^{durca}$ for having direct URCA processes and fast stellar cooling. Finally we study the stability of dark matter admixed neutron stars with respect to radial perturbations.

## Key claim

The presence of dark matter can either increase or decrease the maximum gravitational mass of a neutron star depending on the dark matter particle mass: for a particle mass of 0.1 GeV the maximum mass increases, while for 1 GeV it decreases, with the transition occurring around 0.188 GeV where the maximum mass remains compatible with observed 2 solar mass neutron stars for any dark matter fraction.

## Method

The authors solve a generalized two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) system of equations for a spherically symmetric star composed of ordinary nuclear matter and dark matter that interact only gravitationally. The ordinary matter equation of state uses the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with chiral effective field theory two-body and three-body nuclear interactions (the BL EOS), while the dark matter is modeled as a zero-temperature ideal relativistic Fermi gas of spin-1/2 fermions with no self-annihilation. Equilibrium configurations are obtained by specifying central mass densities for both fluids and integrating the structure equations outward.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of ordinary neutron stars using the BL EOS: M_max = 2.08 solar masses, with central density 2.74 x 10^15 g/cm^3 and radius R(M_max) = 10.26 km
- For dark matter particle mass m_chi = 1 GeV and DM fraction f_chi = 6%, the observable radius R_OM of a 1.4 solar mass star decreases by approximately 0.6 km (about 5%)
- For m_chi = 0.4 GeV, DANS with total mass above 2 solar masses exist in two separate regions: low DM fraction (f_chi less than or equal to 3.27%) with a DM core, and high DM fraction (f_chi greater than or equal to 64.93%) with a DM halo
- The critical dark matter particle mass below which the maximum mass is always compatible with 2 solar mass measurements is m_chi = 0.188 GeV
- For m_chi = 0.559 GeV, the transition from two to one solution for the critical DM fraction occurs

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANS) by using a modern ab initio nuclear equation of state from chiral effective field theory (the BL EOS of Bombaci and Logoteta (2018)), rather than older phenomenological models such as the APR EOS of Akmal et al. (1998) or Skyrme-based interactions. The results enable constraints on dark matter particle mass and fraction by comparison with observed neutron star masses from pulsars PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses) and PSR J0740+6620 (2.08 solar masses), as well as radius measurements from NICER and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.
