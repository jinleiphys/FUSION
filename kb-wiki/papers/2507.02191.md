# Muon-induced fission as a probe of the underlying dynamics in nuclear fission

> Muon-induced fission could be utilized as a probe to study the underlying dynamics of nuclear fission. The probability of muon attachment to the light asymmetric fission fragment is sensitive to fission dynamics, such as the timescale and friction of the fission event, charge asymmetry, and possibly the shape of the fission fragments. We focus on muonic atoms that are formed with actinide nuclei. A relativistic approach is employed, solving the Dirac equation for the muonic wavefunction in the presence of a time-dependent electromagnetic field generated by the fissioning nucleus. Computations are carried out on a 3-D Cartesian lattice with no symmetry assumptions. The results show a strong dependence of the attachment probability on the fission charge asymmetry and a more modest dependence on friction.

## Key claim

Muon attachment probability to the light fission fragment shows a strong dependence on fragment charge asymmetry and a more modest dependence on nuclear friction, but the increase of light-fragment attachment probability for 240Pu from 5.2% to 6.5% when friction is included does not fit the expectation that increasing friction should decrease this probability, suggesting sensitivity to the constant friction model parameter.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for a muonic spinor wavefunction on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice with no symmetry assumptions, using basis-spline discretization and a time-dependent electromagnetic field generated by the fissioning nucleus. Constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations with BCS pairing and the SLy4d Skyrme interaction generate fission potentials, while density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) calculations evolve the system from the outer barrier to scission, with the collective dynamics described by a single coordinate R and a constant friction parameter f.

## Key numbers

- 240Pu: charge asymmetry 1.29, light-fragment muon attachment probability 5.2% without friction and 6.5% with friction (f = 456 MeV/fm·c, dissipated energy 21.94 MeV)
- 256Fm: charge asymmetry 1.15, light-fragment muon attachment probability 20.6% without friction and 16.6% with friction (f = 550 MeV/fm·c, dissipated energy 29.60 MeV)
- 258Fm: charge asymmetry 1.0 (symmetric fission), light-fragment muon attachment probability 50.0% both without and with friction (f = 562 MeV/fm·c, dissipated energy 18.41 MeV)
- Modification of fission barriers due to muon energy for 240Pu: 0.19 MeV at inner barrier and 0.98 MeV at outer barrier; for 256Fm and 258Fm: 0.24 MeV and 0.33 MeV respectively
- Excitation energy E* = 9.5 MeV corresponding to the 3d→1s transition with inverse internal conversion

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of muon-induced fission by Hadermann (1976), Maruhn (1980), and Oberacker (1992) from non-relativistic frameworks or crude fission potential models to a relativistic Dirac-equation approach coupled with modern microscopic fission calculations from Schunck (2016) and Bender (2020). The results enable future integration of the muon code with more sophisticated time-dependent fission codes and models, such as those by Qiang (2021), which allow for time-dependent friction calculations and could resolve the question of whether the muon prefers spherical versus deformed fragments.
