# Deformation effects in low-momentum distributions of heavy nuclei

> Momentum distributions (MD) of deep hole proton states in $^{238}$U are studied paying particular attention to the influence of deformation. Two essentially different mean-field models, Woods-Saxon (WS) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with the SkM$^*$ force, are used. Noticeable deviations between the WS and SkM$^*$ results are found. They are mainly due to the difference in effective nucleon mass. In particular, SkM$^*$ gives much weaker deformation effects at low momenta than WS. It is shown that, in spite of the deformation mixing, MD at low momenta can serve for identification of $K^π=1/2^+$ hole states originating from $s_{1/2}$ spherical sub-shells. Moreover, following the WS calculations, the deformation results in additional $K^π=1/2^+$ states with strong $l=0$ contributions. A possibility to probe such states in knock-out experiments is discussed.

## Key claim

Deformation effects in momentum distributions (MD) of deep hole proton states in heavy deformed nuclei are model-dependent: the Woods-Saxon (WS) potential predicts strong deformation-induced changes at low momenta, while the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with SkM* gives much weaker effects, primarily due to the difference in effective nucleon mass (m*/m = 0.79 for SkM* versus 1.0 for WS).

## Method

Momentum distributions for single-hole proton states in 238U were calculated using two mean-field models: a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential and a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with the SkM* force. The MD were obtained by Fourier transforming the spatial wavefunctions of the single-particle states, and the equilibrium ground-state deformations were determined by energy minimization for both models.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole moment Q2 = 11.0 b for WS and 11.1 b for SkM*, both within the experimental interval 11.1–11.3 b for 238U
- Effective nucleon mass m*/m = 0.79 for SkM* versus m*/m = 1.0 for WS
- Lowest proton hole state E0 = -33.7 MeV for WS and -39.8 MeV for SkM*
- Fermi energy EF = -7.5 MeV for WS and -6.2 MeV for SkM*
- Single-particle models are unreliable for momenta k > 1.3 fm^-1 (≈ 260 MeV/c)
- WS predicts 7 Kπ = 1/2+ states with appreciable l=0 strength (peak at k=0), while SkM* gives only 3 such states (from 1s, 2s, and 3s spherical subshells)
- Energy separations between relevant Kπ = 1/2+ levels in WS calculations are 3–6 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deformation effects in momentum distributions (Guerra and references therein) and addresses the scarcity of experimental data on deep hole states in heavy deformed nuclei, which are practically absent compared to spherical nuclei (Antonov review). The results suggest that low-momentum MD in heavy deformed nuclei can serve as a test to discriminate between different mean-field models, and that the sequence of Kπ = 1/2+ hole states predicted by WS could be observable in (e,e'p) knock-out experiments, building on the 208Pb(e,e'p) data from Heyde which observed 3s1/2 and 2s1/2 structures.
