# Scaling functions of two-neutron separation energies of $^{20}C$ with finite range potentials

> The behaviour of an Efimov excited state is studied within a three-body Faddeev formalism for a general neutron-neutron-core system, where neutron-core is bound and neutron-neutron is unbound, by considering zero-ranged as well as finite-ranged two-body interactions. For the finite-ranged interactions we have considered a one-term separable Yamaguchi potential. The main objective is to study range corrections in a scaling approach, with focus in the exotic carbon halo nucleus $^{20}C$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the exotic carbon halo nucleus $^{20}C$, the scaling function correlating two-neutron separation energies of successive three-body states is modified by finite-range effects in the first Efimov cycle, but these range corrections become negligible in the second cycle where the ratio of range to scattering length is very small.

## Method

The authors solved coupled s-wave Faddeev integral equations for a neutron-neutron-core ($n-n-^{18}C$) system, first using zero-range potentials and then finite-range separable Yamaguchi potentials with a range parameter $\beta$. They fixed the neutron-neutron virtual-state energy to $E_{nn}=-143$ keV and varied a three-body parameter to reproduce the $^{20}C$ ground-state energy, then computed the first excited-state energy to construct scaling plots of $\sqrt{(E^{(1)}_{^{20}C}-E_{^{19}C})/E^{(0)}_{^{20}C}}$ versus $\sqrt{E_{^{19}C}/E^{(0)}_{^{20}C}}$.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron virtual-state energy was fixed to $E_{nn} = -143$ keV for the realistic case.
- For the case $E_{nn}=0$, the scaling plots for zero-range and Yamaguchi potentials showed deviation in the first cycle ($N=0$), but in the second cycle ($N=1$) the Yamaguchi scaling plot approached the zero-range results.
- For the realistic case $E_{nn}=-143$ keV, the Yamaguchi potential scaling plot converged to zero-range results for large values of $\beta$ (low effective range).

## Context

This work extends the Efimov scaling approach previously developed for three-body systems near the unitary limit (Amorim 1997, Hammer 2007, Frederico 2012) by incorporating finite-range corrections through separable Yamaguchi potentials. The study focuses on the neutron-rich halo nucleus $^{20}C$, where the neutron-core subsystem is bound and the neutron-neutron subsystem is unbound. The results enable future model-independent analysis of range corrections in energy scaling plots and suggest further investigation of other properties such as sizes and momentum distributions.
