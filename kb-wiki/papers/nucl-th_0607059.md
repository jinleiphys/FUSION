# Tensor correlation in 4He with the tensor-optimized shell model

> We study the characteristics of the tensor correlation in $^4$He using a shell model type method. We treat the tensor force explicitly by performing a configuration-mixing calculation in the $2p2h$ basis and include single-particle states up to intermediately high angular momenta. We adopt the Gaussian expansion method for the quantitative description of the spatial shrinkage of the single-particle states to optimize the tensor correlation. We are able to describe the full strength of the tensor correlation for $^4$He in the shell model type method by realizing convergence. We call this model the tensor-optimized shell model. It is found that in $^4$He, three specific $2p2h$ configurations are strongly coupled with the $(0s)^4$ configuration due to the characteristic features of the tensor operator.

## Key claim

The tensor correlation in 4He can be described fully within a shell-model-type method (the tensor-optimized shell model) by including 2p2h configurations with single-particle states up to orbital angular momentum l=6, provided the radial wave functions of the excited states are allowed to shrink spatially. The convergence of the tensor force contribution requires higher partial waves up to at least l=4, and three specific 2p2h configurations dominate the tensor correlation.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-mixing calculations for 4He using a closed-shell (0s1/2)4 state as the 0p0h basis and adding 2p2h excitations with particle states up to lmax=6. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in Gaussian basis functions with optimized length parameters, allowing spatial shrinkage of the excited states, and the Hamiltonian included central, tensor, LS, and Coulomb terms using the phenomenological GA interaction (central part from GPT, tensor part from AK). The variational parameters (configuration mixing coefficients and Gaussian length parameters) were optimized to minimize the total energy.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of 4He converges to -44.85 MeV at lmax=6 (from -22.66 MeV at lmax=1)
- Tensor force contribution ⟨VT⟩ converges to -58.06 MeV at lmax=6 (from -21.65 MeV at lmax=1)
- D-state probability P(D) converges to 7.83% at lmax=6 (from 4.66% at lmax=1)
- Matter radius Rm converges to 1.24 fm for lmax=4 through lmax=6
- Optimized length parameters for excited particle states are approximately 0.6 times the 0s length parameter (b0s=1.16 fm at lmax=6)
- The three dominant 2p2h configurations contribute about 70% of the total tensor force matrix element: (0p1/2)2_{10} contributes -14.47 MeV, (1s1/2)(0d3/2)_{10} contributes -15.01 MeV, and (0p3/2)(0f5/2)_{10} contributes -12.00 MeV (lmax=6 case)
- The 0p0h-2p2h coupling term dominates ⟨VT⟩ at -61.22 MeV, while the 2p2h-2p2h term contributes only +3.16 MeV (lmax=6)

## Context

This work extends the shell-model approach to explicitly treat tensor correlations in 4He, building on earlier studies by Toki et al. (2002) and Myo et al. (2005) that used parity-mixing single-particle states and limited 2p2h configurations. The tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) addresses the difficulty of describing tensor correlations in the V-type (mean-field) basis, which contrasts with the T-type (relative coordinate) basis used in Green's function Monte Carlo methods by Wiringa et al. (2000) and variational calculations. The method enables future application to heavier nuclei where T-type basis calculations become impractical, and the authors plan to subsequently treat the short-range repulsion in the same framework.
