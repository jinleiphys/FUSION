# Quark Propagation in the Presence of a A_μ^aA_a^μ> Condensate

> There is a good deal of current interest in the condensate g^2A_μ^aA_a^μ> which has recently be shown to be the Landau gauge version of a more general gauge-invariant expression. In the present work we consider quark propagation in the presence of such a condensate which we assume to be present in the vacuum. We describe the vacuum as a random medium of gluon fields. We discuss quark propagation in that medium and show that the quark propagator has no on-mass-shell pole indicating that a quark cannot propagate over extended distances. That is, the quark is a nonpropagating mode in the gluon condensate.

## Key claim

The ensemble-averaged quark propagator in the presence of a gluon condensate has no on-mass-shell pole for real \(p^2\), implying that a quark cannot propagate over extended distances and is a nonpropagating mode; this result depends on modeling the QCD vacuum as a Gaussian random medium with a large correlation length and using the Kraichnan random-coupling approximation.

## Method

The authors separate the gluon field into a classical random condensate field \(\mathbb{A}_\mu^a(x)\) and a perturbative fluctuation field, then treat the vacuum as a Gaussian random medium with zero odd-point correlation functions and even-point functions expressed via the two-point function. They apply the Kraichnan random-coupling approximation (originally developed for classical wave propagation in random media) to compute the ensemble-averaged quark propagator and self-energy \(\Sigma(p)=A(p^2)\slr p+B(p^2)\) in momentum space, taking the condensate gluons to carry zero momentum in the large-correlation-length limit.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical gluon mass \(m_G = 649\) MeV obtained from the condensate in earlier work by the same authors.
- Dynamical quark mass from coupling to the gluon condensate: \(m_q^{Gl} = 432\) MeV (from earlier work).
- Predicted glueball mass: \(1.0 \pm 0.15\) GeV (from earlier work).
- Lattice result for gluon dynamical mass: \(m_G = 600 \pm 90\) MeV (from a lattice simulation of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge).
- Lattice-based value from Arriola, Bowman and Broniowski (2004): \(m_G = (625 \pm 33)\) MeV, obtained via the relation \(m_G^2 = (3/32)g^2\langle A^2\rangle\).
- Parameter \(\kappa^2 = (232\ \text{MeV})^2\) used in the quark self-energy calculations.
- Current quark masses used in calculations: up/down \(m_q^{cur} = 5\) MeV, strange \(m_q^{cur} = 125\) MeV, charm \(m_q^{cur} = 1375\) MeV, bottom \(m_q^{cur} = 4750\) MeV.
- For \(m_q^{cur}=0\), the chiral-symmetry-breaking solution gives \(A(p^2) = -1\) and \(B(p^2) = 2\sqrt{p^2+\kappa^2}\) for \(p^2 > -\kappa^2\), and the dynamical mass \(M(p^2) = (p^2+\kappa^2)^{1/2}\) for \(p^2 > -\kappa^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of the gluon condensate \(\langle g^2 A^2\rangle\) (Li and Shakin, earlier papers) and their previous analysis of the gluon propagator using a first-order smoothing approximation (Li and Shakin, earlier work). It parallels lattice studies of the gluon propagator in Landau gauge that found a dynamical gluon mass (lattice simulation, 2004) and the operator product expansion analysis of lattice quark propagator data by Arriola, Bowman and Broniowski (2004). The result that a single parameter \(g^2\phi_0^2\) governs both quark nonpropagation and chiral symmetry breaking is presented as a satisfactory feature for QCD, and the framework enables further study of confinement mechanisms via the random-medium description of the vacuum.
