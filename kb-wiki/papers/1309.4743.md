# Scattering lengths of Nambu-Goldstone bosons off $D$ mesons and dynamically generated heavy-light mesons

> Recent lattice QCD simulations of the scattering lengths of Nambu-Goldstone bosons off the $D$ mesons are studied using unitary chiral perturbation theory. We show that the Lattice QCD data are better described in the covariant formulation than in the heavy-meson formulation. The $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ can be dynamically generated from the coupled-channels $DK$ interaction without \textit{a priori} assumption of its existence. A new renormalization scheme is proposed which manifestly satisfies chiral power counting rules and has well-defined behavior in the infinite heavy-quark mass limit. Using this scheme we predict the heavy-quark spin and flavor symmetry counterparts of the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that lattice QCD scattering length data for Nambu-Goldstone bosons off D mesons are better described by covariant chiral perturbation theory than by the heavy-meson formulation, and that the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ resonance can be dynamically generated from coupled-channels $DK$ interactions without assuming its existence _a priori_, provided a new renormalization scheme is used that respects chiral power counting and has a well-defined infinite-heavy-quark-mass limit.

## Method

The authors constructed covariant chiral perturbation theory potentials up to next-to-leading order for $P^{(*)}\phi \rightarrow P^{(*)}\phi$ scattering, then iterated these potentials to all orders using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an on-shell approximation. They proposed a new "heavy-quark symmetry (HQS) inspired" renormalization scheme for the loop function that removes power-counting-breaking terms and restores proper $1/M_\mathrm{HL}$ scaling, and fitted the resulting amplitudes to 15 lattice QCD data points for scattering lengths of pions and kaons off D mesons from Liu et al. (2012).

## Key numbers

* The $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ mass is $M = 2317.8 \pm 0.6$ MeV, about 100 MeV lower than the lowest $c\bar{s}$ scalar state in the naive quark model.
* The coupling $\tilde g_{DD^*\phi} = (1177 \pm 137)$ MeV is determined from the $D^{*+}$ decay width $\Gamma_{D^{*+}} = (96 \pm 22)$ keV and branching ratio $BR_{D^{*+}\rightarrow D^0\pi^+} = (67.7 \pm 0.5)\%$.
* From mass splittings, $c_1 = -0.214$ for D mesons and $\tilde c_1 = -0.236$ for $D^*$ mesons; for B mesons, $c_1(B) = -0.513$ and $\tilde c_1(B) = -0.534$.
* Heavy-quark symmetry breaking is about $3\%$ between $D$ vs $D^*$ and $B$ vs $B^*$, and about $16\%$ between $D$ vs $B$ and $D^*$ vs $B^*$, based on ratios $c_1/\bar{M}_D = -0.113$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\tilde c_1/\bar{M}_{D^*} = -0.116$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_1(B)/\bar{M}_B = -0.097$ GeV$^{-1}$, and $\tilde c_1(B)/\bar{M}_{B^*} = -0.100$ GeV$^{-1}$.
* The constant $c_0 = 0.015$ is fixed by fitting NLO mass formulas to lattice QCD data from Liu et al. (2012).
* The covariant unitary chiral perturbation theory with the HQS scheme describes the 15 lattice QCD data points with a $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}$ closer to 1 than the heavy-meson formulation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy-light meson interactions with Nambu-Goldstone bosons using chiral perturbation theory, specifically building on the covariant formulation with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme applied to D meson decay constants by Geng et al. (2010) and Altenbuchinger et al. (2011). It directly addresses the lattice QCD simulations of scattering lengths by Liu et al. (2012) and Mohler et al. (2013), and provides a renormalization scheme that recovers heavy-quark spin and flavor symmetry up to $1/M_\mathrm{HL}$ corrections, which was identified as a problem by Cleven et al. (2010). The dynamically generated states predicted, including the $D^*_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ and their bottom-quark counterparts, can be tested by future experiments searching for these resonances in the predicted energy regions.
