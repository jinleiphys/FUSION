# Implications of hydrodynamic fluctuations on the minimum shear viscosity of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity

> We confirm and expand on work by Chafin and Schaefer on hydrodynamic fluctuations in the unitary Fermi gas. Using the result for the equation of state from a recent MIT experiment, we derive lower bounds for η/n and η/s as a function of temperature. Re-analyzing recent quantum Monte Carlo data for the shear-viscosity spectral function we point out a possible resolution for the tension between the viscosity bound η/n> 0.3 from Chafin and Schaefer and the quantum Monte Carlo results η/n<0.2$ from Wlazlowski et al. near the critical temperature

## Key claim

The paper confirms and expands on the work by Chafin and Schaefer (2012) on hydrodynamic fluctuations in the unitary Fermi gas, deriving a lower bound for the shear viscosity over density ratio that is consistent with the bound from Chafin and Schaefer but can be reconciled with quantum Monte Carlo results from Wlazlowski et al. (2012) if a narrow low-frequency peak in the spectral function is included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors use fluctuating hydrodynamics to calculate the one-loop correction to the shear viscosity spectral function from hydrodynamic fluctuations, then apply the Kubo relation to extract the physical shear viscosity. They re-analyze the Path Integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) data from Wlazlowski et al. (2012) for the imaginary-time correlation function at temperatures T/T_F = 0.355 and T/T_F = 0.15 using a simple chi-squared minimization with a five-parameter model for the spectral function, and compare the extracted low-frequency behavior to the hydrodynamic prediction.

## Key numbers

- The lower bound on the shear viscosity over density ratio is given by (eta/n) >= (eta/n)_min = 1.005 k^(1/3) sqrt(T/T_F) (P/(n T))^(1/6), where k is an order-unity constant.
- For the unitary Fermi gas at T/T_F = 0.355, the re-analysis yields a shear viscosity value of eta/n = 0.7.
- For the low-temperature case T/T_F = 0.15, the re-analysis yields eta/n = 0.35, consistent with the viscosity bound from Chafin and Schaefer (2012).
- The kinetic theory result for the shear viscosity is (eta/n)_KT = 2.768 (T/T_F)^(3/2).
- The phonon scattering result for the shear viscosity in the superfluid phase is (eta/n)_phonons = 1.18 x 10^(-7) (T_F/T)^5.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic fluctuation analysis of Chafin and Schaefer (2012) for the unitary Fermi gas, which derived a lower bound eta/n >= 0.32 at the critical temperature, and addresses the tension with the quantum Monte Carlo results of Wlazlowski et al. (2012) who found eta/n less than 0.2 near T_c. The authors propose that a narrow low-frequency peak in the spectral function, predicted by hydrodynamic fluctuations but potentially missed by the Maximum Entropy Method used by Wlazlowski et al. (2012), resolves the discrepancy. The results enable future quantum Monte Carlo calculations to properly account for low-frequency structure in spectral function reconstructions.
