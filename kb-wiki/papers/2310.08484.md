# Gravitational form factors of the proton from lattice QCD

> The gravitational form factors (GFFs) of a hadron encode fundamental aspects of its structure, including its shape and size as defined from e.g., its energy density. This work presents a determination of the flavor decomposition of the GFFs of the proton from lattice QCD, in the kinematic region $0\leq -t\leq 2~\text{GeV}^2$. The decomposition into up-, down-, strange-quark, and gluon contributions provides first-principles constraints on the role of each constituent in generating key proton structure observables, such as its mechanical radius, mass radius, and $D$-term.

# Gravitational form factors of the proton from lattice QCD

## Key claim

This work presents the first flavor decomposition of the proton's gravitational form factors (GFFs) into up-, down-, strange-quark, and gluon contributions from a first-principles lattice QCD calculation, but the results are obtained on a single lattice ensemble with a pion mass of approximately 170 MeV and have not been extrapolated to the continuum limit, meaning systematic uncertainties from finite lattice spacing, finite volume, and renormalization are not fully quantified.

## Method

The calculation uses a single ensemble of gauge configurations with N_f=2+1 flavors of clover-improved Wilson quarks at a lattice spacing of approximately 0.091 fm and volume 48^3 x 96, with quark masses tuned to yield m_pi ≈ 170 MeV. Bare matrix elements of the gluon and quark energy-momentum tensor (EMT) are extracted from ratios of three-point and two-point functions using the summation method, with the gluon EMT measured on Wilson-flowed gauge fields and disconnected quark contributions estimated stochastically using Z_4 noise with hierarchical probing. Renormalization is performed non-perturbatively using results from a companion study, and the GFFs are extracted by solving linear systems of equations across 34 t-bins in the range 0 ≤ -t ≤ 2 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the ensemble: m_pi ≈ 170 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.091 fm
- Lattice volume: L^3 × T = 48^3 × 96
- Total momentum fraction from dipole fit: A_total(0) = 1.011(37), satisfying the sum rule A(0)=1 within uncertainty
- Total spin from dipole fit: J_total(0) = 0.506(25), satisfying the sum rule J(0)=1/2 within uncertainty
- Total D-term from dipole fit: D_total(0) = -3.87(97)
- Gluon momentum fraction from dipole fit: A_g(0) = 0.501(27), several standard deviations above the global fit result of 0.414(8) from Hou (2019)
- Up-quark momentum fraction: A_u(0) = 0.3255(92) from dipole fit
- Down-quark momentum fraction: A_d(0) = 0.1590(92) from dipole fit
- Strange-quark momentum fraction: A_s(0) = 0.0257(95) from dipole fit
- Up-quark D-term: D_u(0) = -0.56(17) from dipole fit
- Down-quark D-term: D_d(0) = -0.57(17) from dipole fit
- Strange-quark D-term: D_s(0) = -0.18(17) from dipole fit
- Gluon D-term: D_g(0) = -2.57(84) from dipole fit
- m_pi L ≈ 3.8, below the typical rule-of-thumb bound m_pi L > 4 for limiting finite volume effects

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of proton GFFs by Shanahan (2018), Pefkou (2021), and Alexandrou (2019) by providing the full flavor decomposition into individual up, down, and strange quark contributions alongside the gluon contribution, which had not been achieved previously from either experiment or first-principles theory. The results for D_{u+d}(t) and D_g(t) are consistent with experimental extractions from Burkert (2018) and Duran (2022), while the t-dependence of A_g(t) favors the holographic QCD inspired analysis method of Duran (2022) over the GPD-inspired approach of Guo (2023). The computed mass and mechanical radii enable quantitative comparison of different measures of proton size, and the results set benchmarks for future measurements at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
