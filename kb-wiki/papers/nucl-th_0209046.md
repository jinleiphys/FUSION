# Spacelike and timelike response of confined relativistic particles

> Basic theoretical issues relating to the response of confined relativistic particles are considered including the scaling of the response in spacelike and timelike regions of momentum transfer and the role of final state interactions. A simple single particle potential model incorporating relativity and linear confinement is solved exactly and its response is calculated. The response is studied in common approximation schemes and it is found that final state interactions effects persist in the limit that the three-momentum transferred to the target is large. The fact that the particles are bound leads to a non-zero response in the timelike region of four-momentum transfer equal to about 10% of the total strength. The strength in the timelike region must be taken into account to fulfill the particle number sum rule.

## Key claim

Final state interaction effects in the response of confined relativistic particles persist even in the limit of large three-momentum transfer, and the binding of particles produces a non-zero response in the timelike region of four-momentum transfer that amounts to about 10% of the total strength, which must be included to satisfy the particle number sum rule.

## Method

The author solves exactly a single-particle potential model with a linear confining potential and relativistic kinetic energy for a massless scalar quark coupled to a scalar probe, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a spherical momentum basis. The exact response is compared with the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and the on-shell approximation (OSA), and analytic expressions for the first three moments of the response are derived for general spherically symmetric potentials.

## Key numbers

- The strength in the timelike region of four-momentum transfer is about 10% of the total response, independent of the string tension sqrt(sigma).
- The first inelastic peak in the model at small momentum transfer is 335 MeV above the ground state (n=1, l=1).
- The exact width Delta of the response at large momentum transfer is 409 MeV, while the PWIA width Delta_PWIA is 326 MeV.
- The integrated response over all nu at fixed q is unity (particle number sum rule) to within less than 0.02% error in the chosen basis.

## Context

This work extends studies of final state interaction effects in electron scattering from nuclear targets (BFFMPS 1991, BP 1993) and neutron scattering from liquid helium (SS 1989) to the case of hadrons with confined constituents, where constituents are confined in both initial and final states. The model is similar to one considered by Isgur et al. (2001) and uses the scaling variable tilde-y = nu - |q|, equivalent to the Nachtmann variable xi (ON, Jaffe 1985), which spans both spacelike and timelike regions. The results enable further study of quark-hadron duality and the role of timelike response in fulfilling sum rules for deep inelastic scattering.
