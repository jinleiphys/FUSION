# Finite-size effects on the Phase Structure of the Walecka Model

> In this work we investigate the finite-size effects on the phase structure of Walecka model within the framework of generalized Zeta-function, focusing on the influence of temperature as well as the number and length of compactified spatial dimensions. Here we concentrate on the situation of larger values of the coupling between the scalar and fermion fields, in which a phase transition of first order takes place. The phase transitions are analyzed and compared with the system in the situations of one, two and three compactified spatial dimensions. Our findings suggest that the thermodynamic behavior of the system depends on the length and number of spatial dimensions, with the symmetric phase being favored as the size of the system diminishes.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how finite-size effects alter the phase structure of the Walecka model, finding that the symmetric phase (where the effective fermion mass is small) is favored as the system size decreases, but only for the specific regime of large scalar-fermion coupling where a first-order phase transition occurs.

## Method

The authors use the generalized zeta-function regularization method within the imaginary-time formalism to compute the thermodynamic potential of the Walecka model in mean-field approximation, treating the scalar and vector fields as classical. They compactify one, two, or three spatial dimensions with antiperiodic boundary conditions and equal compactification lengths, then solve the gap equations for the effective fermion mass as a function of temperature at zero effective chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For the bulk system (no boundaries) with scalar mass \(m_{\sigma}=0.53\) (in units of the fermion mass \(m_{\psi}\)), the critical coupling for a first-order phase transition is \(g_{\sigma} > 9.8\); below this value the effective mass varies smoothly with temperature.
- For \(g_{\sigma}=26.00\) in the bulk, the first-order phase transition occurs at temperature \(T \approx 0.125\)–\(0.126\) (in units of \(m_{\psi}\)), where the effective mass drops from approximately \(0.98\) to approximately \(0.1\).
- For \(g_{\sigma}=16.00\) in the bulk, the first-order phase transition occurs at \(T \approx 0.158\)–\(0.159\), where the effective mass drops from approximately \(0.90\) to approximately \(0.20\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-size analysis previously applied to scalar Yukawa models by Abreu et al. (2014, 2015) to the Walecka model, which includes both scalar and vector meson fields. The results are compared with the bulk behavior described by J. Theis et al. (1983), and the formalism enables future studies of how quantum corrections (omitted here) might modify the finite-size phase diagram. The findings are relevant for understanding hadronic matter in finite reservoirs such as those encountered in heavy-ion collisions or neutron star crusts.
