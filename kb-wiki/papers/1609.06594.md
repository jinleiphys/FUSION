# Fragmentation Functions of neutral mesons $π^0$ and $k ^0$ with Laplace transform approach

> With an analytical solutions of DGLAP evolution equations based on the Laplace transform method , we find the fragmentation functions (FFs) of neutral mesons, $π^0$ and $k ^0$ at NLO approximation. We also calculated the total fragmentation functions of these mesons and compared them with experimental data and those from global fits. The results show a good agreements between our solutions and other models and also are compatible with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present an analytical solution to the DGLAP evolution equations using the Laplace transform method to compute the fragmentation functions of neutral pions and kaons at next-to-leading order (NLO), with the condition that the initial input fragmentation functions at the starting scale Q0 = 4.5 GeV are taken from the HKNS global fit code rather than being independently determined.

## Method

The authors solve the DGLAP evolution equations analytically by applying Laplace transforms in two variables (nu = ln(1/z) and tau) to convert the integro-differential equations into algebraic equations in s-space and U-space. They first compute the non-singlet, singlet, and gluon fragmentation functions for charged pions and kaons, then use charge conjugation symmetry to obtain the fragmentation functions for pi- and K-, and finally average the charged and neutral results to obtain the neutral meson fragmentation functions. The total fragmentation functions are computed by convolving the partonic fragmentation functions with Wilson coefficient functions at NLO.

## Key numbers

- Initial scale Q0 = 4.5 GeV used for the starting input fragmentation functions from HKNS code
- Best fit parameters a1 = 0.025 and b1 = 10.7 for the approximation a(tau) = alpha_s(tau)/(4 pi) approximately a0 + a1 exp(-b1 tau) at NLO
- Total fragmentation functions compared with experimental data from TASSO and ALEPH collaborations at Q = 44 GeV and at Q^2 = M_z^2 for neutral pions
- Total fragmentation functions compared with experimental data from TOPAZ and OPAL collaborations at Q = 44 GeV and at Q^2 = M_z^2 for neutral kaons

## Context

This work extends the Laplace transform method previously applied by the authors to charged pion and kaon fragmentation functions (Taghavi-Shahri, 2015) and to polarized parton distribution functions (Taghavi-Shahri, 2014a, 2014b). The method was originally developed by Block et al. (2010-2014) for solving DGLAP evolution equations. The results are compared with global fits from AKK, DSS, and HKNS (Hirai, 2007) and with experimental data from TASSO, ALEPH, TOPAZ, and OPAL collaborations. The analytical solutions enable the inclusion of neutral meson data in future global fits for determining fragmentation functions.
