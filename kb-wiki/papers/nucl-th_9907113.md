# Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation with inclusion of the Pauli Exclusion Principle

> Limitations of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) are studied within an exactly solvable model, with a two body interaction of Fermi type. A special attention is paid to the violation of the Pauli exclusion principle (PEP) in solving the QRPA equation. A comparison of the exact solution, obtained by the diagonalization of a schematic nuclear Hamiltonian and those obtained within the standard QRPA, the renormalized QRPA, the QRPA with pertubative treatment of the PEP and the QRPA with exact consideration of the PEP, is presented. The agreement quality is judged in terms of the quasiparticle number operator matrix elements in the ground state and in the first excited states, of the beta transition amplitudes, of the Ikeda sum rule and of the nuclear matrix element for the double beta decay. We have found that restoring the PEP, the QRPA solutions are considerably stabilized and a better agreement with the exact solution is obtained.

# Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation with inclusion of the Pauli Exclusion Principle

## Key claim

Restoring the Pauli exclusion principle (PEP) within the QRPA framework considerably stabilizes the solutions and yields better agreement with the exact solution of the nuclear Hamiltonian, but the improvement is limited by the simple linear form of the phonon operator; the EPP QRPA systematically overestimates the exact excitation energies, indicating that a more complex phonon operator may be necessary.

## Method

The authors study a solvable proton-neutron monopole Lipkin model with a schematic Hamiltonian containing single-particle terms, proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing, and a charge-dependent two-body interaction with particle-hole and particle-particle channels. They compare five approaches: standard QRPA (quasiboson approximation), renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), QRPA with perturbative treatment of the PEP (PP2 QRPA), QRPA with approximate PEP inclusion (PP QRPA), and QRPA with exact PEP inclusion (EPP QRPA), benchmarking all against the exact solution obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the space of states built from quasiparticle pair creation operators.

## Key numbers

- The standard QRPA collapses at approximately κ' ≈ 1.0 for χ' = 0.5, while the PP2 QRPA collapses at κ' ≈ 1.80, PP QRPA at κ' ≈ 2.50, and EPP QRPA at κ' ≈ 2.55 (for χ' = 0.5).
- The model parameters used are j = 9/2, Z = 4, N = 6, e = 1 MeV, with χ' values of 0.0 and 0.5, and κ' varied in the interval 0 ≤ κ' ≤ 2.
- For the double beta decay calculations, the parameters are j = 19/2, Z = 6, N = 14 transitioning to Z+2 = 8, N-2 = 12, with Δ = 0.5 MeV.
- The 2νββ matrix element M_F^(2ν) vanishes within the range 1.0 ≤ κ' ≤ 1.5 for all calculations including the exact ones.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the same solvable proton-neutron monopole Lipkin model previously studied by Hirata et al. (1996) and Sambataro et al. (1997, 1999), completing their analysis by presenting for the first time the QRPA solution with exact consideration of the Pauli exclusion principle. The paper addresses the well-known collapse of standard QRPA solutions and the violation of the Ikeda sum rule in RQRPA, as noted by Krmpotic et al. (1996) and Hirata et al. (1996). The findings enable improved treatment of beta and double beta decay transitions in realistic calculations by demonstrating that better PEP obedience shifts the instability to larger interaction strengths and stabilizes the nuclear matrix elements for double beta decay.
