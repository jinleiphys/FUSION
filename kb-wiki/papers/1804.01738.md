# Effects of Tensor Force in the Relativistic Scheme: A Case Study of Neutron Drops

> Tensor force is an important component in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, nevertheless, the role of the tensor force in the spin properties in finite nuclei is much less clear. In this report, we mainly focus on our recent progress on this topic about the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and the corresponding tensor effects on the spin-orbit splittings in neutron drops. This will serve as an important guide for future microscopic derivations of the relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear energy density functionals.

# Effects of Tensor Force in the Relativistic Scheme: A Case Study of Neutron Drops

## Key claim
The spin-orbit splitting in neutron drops shows a clear, repeating pattern of decreasing then increasing as neutron orbitals are filled, which is identified as a signature of the tensor force in the pure neutron (isovector T=1) channel within the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework. This pattern can be reproduced by relativistic Hartree-Fock theory only if the tensor coupling strength is adjusted by a factor lambda = 1.3 relative to the original PKO1 interaction, but cannot be reproduced by any relativistic mean-field functional that lacks tensor components.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations for neutron drops confined in an external harmonic-oscillator potential with hbar-omega = 10 MeV, using the relativistic bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn A. They computed spin-orbit splittings for the 1p, 1d, 1f, and 2p doublets as a function of neutron number N from 4 to 50, and compared these ab initio results against those from several relativistic mean-field (RMF) functionals (NL3, PK1, PKDD, DD-ME2, PC-PK1) and from the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) functional PKO1 with varied pion-nucleon coupling strengths.

## Key numbers
- Spin-orbit splitting of the 1d doublet decreases from N=20 to N=28 as neutrons fill the 1f_{7/2} shell, then increases above N=28 as neutrons fill the 2p_{1/2} and 1f_{5/2} shells.
- The pattern is observed for all neutron numbers in the range 4 ≤ N ≤ 50 under investigation.
- The RHF functional PKO1 with lambda = 1.3 (i.e., the pion-nucleon coupling multiplied by 1.3) reproduces the RBHF evolution pattern, while lambda = 0.7 and lambda = 1.0 produce too small tensor effects.
- The external harmonic-oscillator trap has hbar-omega = 10 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Otsuka (2005) finding of tensor-force-driven shell evolution between protons and neutrons to the pure neutron (isovector T=1) channel, demonstrated for the first time in a fully self-consistent relativistic framework. It contradicts the earlier conclusion from Lalazissis (2009) that the best global fit to nuclear masses is obtained with vanishing tensor forces. The results provide ab initio meta-data that can guide future fits of both relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear energy density functionals, as the neutron-drop results are free from complications such as particle-vibrational coupling (Litvinova 2011).
