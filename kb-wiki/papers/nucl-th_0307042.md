# Production of the exotic Theta baryon in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Recent experimental data appear to confirm the existence of the exotic Theta+ (uudd\bar{s}) baryon. Simple statistical considerations, which have generally proven rather successful, suggest that this particle should be produced in observable abundance in relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC, where it may be identifed through the p-K0_s invariant mass spectrum. The observation would not only add new evidence for its existence, but would also provide an additional means for probing the collision system, especially with regard to strangeness.

## Key claim

Simple statistical equilibrium considerations, which have been successful for other hadronic species, suggest that the exotic Theta+ baryon should be produced in observable abundance in relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC, with the yield estimate being rather insensitive to the precise values of the chemical potentials used.

## Method

The author employed a grand-canonical statistical model to calculate the equilibrium densities of hadronic species, using the full quantum-statistical expression for the partition function. Yield ratios of the Theta baryon relative to Lambda, proton, K+, and pi+ were computed for four different sets of chemical potentials (mu_B, mu_Q, mu_S) over a temperature range of 150-200 MeV, and double abundance ratios were constructed to further reduce sensitivity to chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The Theta yield is estimated to be about 12-14% of the Lambda yield at temperatures around 170-180 MeV, based on the single abundance ratios in Table R.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 100 GeV/A at RHIC, the estimated yield is about one Theta per unit rapidity at mid-rapidity, derived from the double ratio (n_Theta/n_p)/(n_K+/n_pi+) combined with BRAHMS multiplicities.
- The predicted mass of the Theta was about 1530 MeV with a decay width less than 15 MeV, as cited from the original chiral soliton model prediction by Diakonov.
- The double ratio (n_Theta/n_p)/(n_K+/n_pi+) ranges from 13.7% at T=150 MeV to 23.2% at T=200 MeV for chemical potential set A.

## Context

This work extends the successful statistical hadronization framework used by Heinz, Becattini, and Rafelski to predict yields of known hadrons, applying it to the newly claimed exotic Theta+ baryon. The paper encourages experimental searches using the p-K0_s invariant mass spectrum in existing RHIC data from the STAR TPC detector, and notes that if the Theta is actually an isotensor multiplet as hypothesized by Capstick, the Theta++ member could be identified via the p-K+ invariant mass spectrum.
