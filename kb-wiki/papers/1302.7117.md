# Spontaneous fission half-lives of heavy and superheavy nuclei within a generalized liquid drop model

> We systematically calculate the spontaneous fission half-lives for heavy and superheavy nuclei between U and Fl isotopes. The spontaneous fission process is studied within the semi-empirical WKB approximation. The potential barrier is obtained using a generalized liquid drop model, taking into account the nuclear proximity, the mass asymmetry, the phenomenological pairing correction, and the microscopic shell correction. Macroscopic inertial-mass function has been employed for the calculation of the fission half-life. The results reproduce rather well the experimental data. Relatively long half-lives are predicted for many unknown nuclei, sufficient to detect them if synthesized in a laboratory.

## Key claim
The generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) with quasimolecular shapes, proximity energy, and microscopic shell corrections reproduces experimental spontaneous fission half-lives for 47 nuclei from U to Fl within an average factor of about 10², though deviations larger than 10³ occur for seven nuclei, particularly near the N=152 sub-magic shell closure.

## Method
The spontaneous fission process was modeled using a semi-empirical WKB approximation to calculate barrier penetration, with the potential barrier obtained from a generalized liquid drop model that includes volume, surface, Coulomb, and nuclear proximity energies. The shape sequence used quasimolecular shapes evolving continuously from one sphere to two touching spheroids, and the macroscopic inertial-mass function was employed with a semi-empirical constant k=14.8. Microscopic shell corrections from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential (with Strutinsky method, smoothing parameter γ=1.15 ħω₀, order p=6) and shape-dependent pairing energies from the finite-range droplet model were added, and all possible mass and charge asymmetries were considered to obtain the total fission constant.

## Key numbers
- For 47 nuclei from ²³²U to ²⁸⁶Fl, the average logarithmic deviation S = Σ|log₁₀(T₁/₂(cal.)) − log₁₀(T₁/₂(exp.))|/47 = 1.61, meaning the average deviation is less than a factor of 10².
- 37 out of 47 experimental half-lives were reproduced within a factor of 10²; only 7 nuclei showed deviations larger than a factor of 10³.
- The seven nuclei with largest deviations and their deviation factors: ²⁴⁰Pu (10⁴), ²⁴⁸Cm (10⁴), ²⁵⁰Cf (10⁵), ²⁵²No (10³), ²⁵⁶Rf (10⁴), ²⁵⁸Rf (10³), and ²⁶⁴Hs (10⁵).
- Experimental half-lives span from 10²⁶ seconds (²³⁵U) to 10⁻³ seconds (²⁵⁶Rf); for example, ²³⁸U has T₁/₂(exp.) = 2.6×10²³ s and T₁/₂(the.) = 1.1×10²¹ s; ²⁸⁶Fl has T₁/₂(exp.) = 1.3×10⁻¹ s and T₁/₂(the.) = 6.9×10⁻² s.
- Predicted half-lives for unknown nuclei include ²⁹⁴Fl at 3.6×10⁸ s, ²⁹⁴Lv at 1.1×10⁵ s, and ²⁹⁴120 at 6.3×10⁻⁵ s.

## Context
This work extends the GLDM approach previously applied to alpha decay (Royer 2000), cluster emission (Royer 2001), and fusion (Royer 1985) to systematic spontaneous fission calculations for heavy and superheavy nuclei. The results improve upon earlier calculations by Moller et al. (1989) using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model, which systematically overestimated half-lives by up to six orders of magnitude, and are comparable to the phenomenological formula of Ren et al. (2005). The predictions for Z=114-120 isotopes provide guidance for future experiments synthesizing new superheavy nuclei, where relatively long half-lives may allow detection.
