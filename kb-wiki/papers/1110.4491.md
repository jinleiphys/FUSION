# Chiral Thermodynamic Model of QCD and its Critical Behavior in the Closed-Time-Path Green Function Approach

> By applying the closed-time-path Green function formalism to the chiral dynamical model based on an effective Lagrangian of chiral quarks with the nonlinear-realized meson fields as bosonized auxiliary fields, we then arrive at a chiral thermodynamic model for the meson fields with finite temperature. Particular attention is paid to the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and restoration from the dynamically generated effective composite Higgs potential of meson fields at finite temperature. It is shown that the minimal condition of the effective composite Higgs potential of meson fields leads to the thermodynamic gap equation at finite temperature, which enables us to investigate the critical behavior of the effective chiral thermodynamical model and to explore the QCD phase transition. After fixing the free parameters in the effective chiral Lagrangian at low energies with zero temperature, we determine the critical temperature of the chiral symmetry restoration and present a consistent prediction for the thermodynamical behavior of several physically interesting quantities, which include the vacuum expectation value $v_o(T)$, quark condensate $<\bar{q}q>(T)$, pion decay constant $f_π(T)$ and pion meson mass $m_π(T)$. In particular, it is also shown that the thermodynamic scaling behavior of these quantities becomes the same near the critical point of phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by applying the closed-time-path Green function formalism to a chiral dynamical model of QCD, one can derive a chiral thermodynamic model that predicts a critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration of approximately 200 MeV, with the thermodynamic scaling behavior of several quantities becoming identical near the critical point.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian of chiral quarks with nonlinear-realized meson fields as bosonized auxiliary fields, then integrate out the quark fields using the closed-time-path Green function formalism to obtain an effective chiral thermodynamic model for meson fields at finite temperature. The minimal condition of the dynamically generated effective composite Higgs potential yields a thermodynamic gap equation, which is solved numerically after fixing free parameters from zero-temperature low-energy QCD constraints. The temperature dependence of the vacuum expectation value, quark condensate, pion decay constant, and pion mass is then computed.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: \(T_c \simeq 200\) MeV, consistent with NJL model predictions.
- The characteristic energy scale \(M_c\) and sliding energy scale \(\mu_s\) are introduced via the loop regularization method, with \(\mu_s\) expected to be around the QCD scale \(\Lambda_{QCD}\).
- The effective action expansion retains only the first two terms (quadratically and logarithmically divergent integrals), regularized using the LORE method with integrals \(I_2\) and \(I_0\) yielding finite results proportional to \(M_c^2/(16\pi^2)\) and \(1/(16\pi^2)\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the chiral dynamical model (CDM) developed by Dai and Wu (2003) from zero temperature to finite temperature by incorporating the closed-time-path Green function formalism, which is more suitable for nonequilibrium statistical processes. The resulting critical temperature of about 200 MeV is consistent with predictions from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as reviewed by Klevansky (1992), Hatsuda (1994), Alkofer (1996), and Buballa (2005). The approach enables systematic investigation of the temperature dependence of chiral order parameters and meson properties near the QCD phase transition.
