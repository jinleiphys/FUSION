# Landau Hydrodynamics Reexamined

> We review the formulation of Landau hydrodynamics and find that the rapidity distribution of produced particles in the center-of-mass system should be more appropriately modified as dN/dy \exp[\sqrt{y_b^2-y^2}], where y_b=\ln[\sqrt{s_{NN}}/m_p] is the beam nucleon rapidity, instead of Landau's original distribution, dN/dy(Landau) \exp[\sqrt{L^2-y^2}], where L=\ln[\sqrt{s_{NN}}/2m_p]. The modified distribution agrees better with experimental dN/dy data than the original Landau distribution and can be represented well by the Gaussian distribution, dN/dy(Gaussian) \exp[-y^2/2L]. Past successes of the Gaussian distribution in explaining experimental rapidity data can be understood, not because it is an approximation of the original Landau distribution, but because it is in fact a close representation of the modified distribution. Predictions for pp and AA collisions at LHC energies in Landau hydrodynamics are presented.

# Landau Hydrodynamics Reexamined

## Key claim

The rapidity distribution of produced particles in the center-of-mass system should be modified to dN/dy ∝ exp[√(y_b² − y²)], where y_b = ln(√s_NN/m_p) is the beam nucleon rapidity, rather than Landau's original distribution dN/dy(Landau) ∝ exp[√(L² − y²)] with L = ln(√s_NN/2m_p); this modification arises from retaining a factor of 4 in the ratio of (ε+p) to pressure p in the transverse expansion equation, which Landau had taken as unity for an order-of-magnitude estimate.

## Method

The author reexamines Landau's hydrodynamical model for high-energy heavy-ion collisions by solving the one-dimensional longitudinal expansion with the relativistic equation of state p = ε/3, using approximate solutions for the energy density and flow rapidity in light-cone coordinates. The transverse expansion is treated via the Euler equation, and the condition for switching from the first stage (longitudinal expansion with simultaneous transverse expansion) to the second stage (conic flight with frozen rapidity) is set when the transverse displacement equals the initial transverse dimension. The final rapidity distribution is evaluated at this matching time t_FO(y) = 2a cosh y, which corresponds to a fixed proper time τ_FO = 2a.

## Key numbers

- Beam rapidity y_b = ln(√s_NN/m_p) versus Landau's L = ln(√s_NN/2m_p); for √s_NN = 200 GeV, y_b = 5.36 and L = 4.67.
- Total charged multiplicity per participant pair for central AuAu collisions: N_ch/(N_part/2) = 1.135 + 2.019 (√s_NN/GeV)^(1/2), with K = 2.019 determined from PHOBOS data.
- Predicted N_ch for pp collisions at √s_NN = 14 TeV with ξ = 0.5: 170 charged particles.
- Predicted N_ch/(N_part/2) for central PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV with full stopping (ξ = 1): 151.
- Predicted maximum dN/dy per participant pair at LHC: approximately 22 at midrapidity for AA collisions at 5.5 TeV, and approximately 24 at y = 0 for pp collisions at 14 TeV, with widths σ_y ∼ 3.
- The Gaussian distribution dN/dy(Gaussian) ∝ exp(−y²/2L) is a close representation of the modified distribution, explaining past successes of the Gaussian in fitting experimental data.

## Context

This work extends Landau hydrodynamics (Landau 1953, Belen'kii 1956) by correcting the rapidity distribution to resolve discrepancies with experimental data from RHIC (Murray 2004, Steinberg 2005, 2007). The modified distribution agrees better with data than the original Landau distribution, which was found to be significantly narrower. The analysis also reconciles the empirical success of the Gaussian rapidity distribution used by Carruthers (1973) and others, showing it approximates the corrected Landau distribution rather than the original one. The predictions for LHC energies enable future tests of Landau hydrodynamics at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV (PbPb) and 14 TeV (pp).
