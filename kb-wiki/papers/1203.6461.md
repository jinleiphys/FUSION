# A Derivation of the Fermi Function in Perturbative Quantum Field Theory

> We postulate that the Fermi function should be derived from the amplitude, not from the solution of the Dirac equation, in the quantum field theory. Then, we obtain the following results. 1, We give the amplitude and the width of the neutron beta decay, $n \to p + e^- + \bar ν_e $ to one loop order. It is carried out by the Feynman parameter integration. 2, As the result, we find the terms which can be interpreted as the Fermi function expanded to order $α$. 3, We also give the same result using complex analysis. 4, We check that there are no such terms in the similar process, $\bar ν_e + p \to e^+ + n$. 5, We perform the Fermi function expanded to order $α^2$ using complex analysis.

## Key claim

The Fermi function, which describes the Coulomb effect of the proton on the emitted electron in beta decay, should be derived from the perturbative loop amplitude of the decay process itself rather than from the solution of the Dirac equation in an external potential. The authors show that the terms proportional to \(\alpha/v\) and \((\alpha/v)^2\) appearing in the one-loop and two-loop beta decay amplitudes match the expansion of the Fermi function, but only under the condition that the electron and proton are present simultaneously in the final state.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude and decay width for neutron beta decay \(n \to p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e\) to one-loop order in perturbative quantum field theory using Feynman parameter integration. They extract the terms proportional to \(\alpha/v\) from the one-loop amplitude and verify the same result using complex analysis of the loop integral. They then extend the calculation to two-loop order using complex analysis to extract the \((\alpha/v)^2\) term. As a cross-check, they perform the analogous calculation for the scattering process \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \to e^+ + n\) to verify that no such \(\alpha/v\) terms appear when the two charged particles are not simultaneously present.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop decay width contains a term \(\frac{2\pi^2}{v}\) in the curly brackets of Eq. (Eq-width1), which is interpreted as the order-\(\alpha\) Fermi function term \(\pi\alpha/v\).
* The two-loop amplitude contains a term proportional to \(-\frac{\alpha^2}{v^2}\left(-\frac{1}{24}\pi^2 + \frac{1}{2}\log^2\frac{\bar{\mu}}{2}\right)\), which after taking the absolute square yields the order-\(\alpha^2\) Fermi function term \(\frac{\pi^2\alpha^2}{3v^2}\).
* The non-relativistic Fermi function expansion is given as \(F_{NR} = 1 + \frac{\pi\alpha}{v} + \frac{\pi^2\alpha^2}{3v^2} - \frac{\pi^4\alpha^4}{45v^4} + \cdots\) (Eq. Eq-Expanded-Fermi-Function), with the expansion parameter \(\alpha/v\) rather than \(\alpha\) alone.
* The scattering process \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \to e^+ + n\) yields exactly zero for the \(\alpha/v\) term, confirming the absence of the Coulomb potential effect.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of the Fermi function, which has traditionally been derived from the Dirac equation in an electromagnetic potential (ZPhys88-161, AmJP36-1150, ProcRoySoc133-381, NPA273-301) and factorized from the decay amplitude (NPA337-474). The authors build on the one-loop calculation of Sirlin (PLB598-67) and the bremsstrahlung treatment of Kinoshita and Sirlin (PR113-1652). The result enables a fully field-theoretic derivation of the Fermi function to all orders in perturbation theory, with potential application to beta decay of nuclei with larger atomic number \(Z\) by replacing \(\alpha \to Z\alpha\). The authors note that for \(\alpha/v \gtrsim 1\), perturbation theory fails and all orders must be summed to recover the full Fermi function \(F_{NR} = 2\pi\alpha/v / (1 - e^{-2\pi\alpha/v})\).
