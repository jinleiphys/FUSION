# Fluctuation-induced modifications of the phase structure in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> The low-energy sector of QCD with $N_f = 2\!+\!1$ dynamical quark flavors at non-vanishing chemical potential and temperature is studied with a non-perturbative functional renormalization group method. The analysis is performed in different truncations in order to explore fluctuation-induced modifications of the quark-meson correlations as well as quark and meson propagators on the chiral phase transition of QCD. Depending on the chosen truncation significant quantitative implications on the phase transition are found. In the chirally symmetric phase, the quark flavor composition of the pseudoscalar $(η,η^{\prime})$-meson complex turns out to be drastically sensitive to fluctuation-induced modifications in the presence of the axial $U(1)_A$ anomaly. This has important phenomenological consequences for the assignment of chiral partners to these mesons.

## Key claim

The chiral phase boundary of (2+1)-flavor QCD, including the location of the critical endpoint, is significantly modified when fluctuation-induced corrections to quark and meson propagators (wave function renormalizations) and to quark-meson Yukawa couplings are included beyond the classical mean-field and simple meson-fluctuation approximations, with the effect depending strongly on the chosen truncation scheme.

## Method

The authors employed a non-perturbative functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to a low-energy quark-meson model with two light and one strange quark flavor, including the full effective meson potential and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant for the axial U(1)_A anomaly. They solved the coupled flow equations for the effective potential, the running Yukawa couplings, and the quark and meson anomalous dimensions, using a two-dimensional fixed background Taylor expansion for the potential and comparing results across different truncation levels.

## Key numbers

* The pseudocritical temperature at vanishing chemical potential is found to be approximately 155 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results.
* The critical endpoint (CEP) in the truncation with running Yukawa couplings and wave function renormalizations is shifted to significantly smaller temperatures and larger chemical potentials compared to mean-field studies.
* The pseudoscalar mixing angle in the chirally symmetric phase shows a drastic sensitivity to fluctuation-induced modifications, with values differing by up to approximately 20 degrees between truncations.
* The light and strange constituent quark masses in the vacuum are determined from the mesonic vacuum expectation values and Yukawa couplings via m_l = (h_l/2) sigma_L and m_s = (h_s/sqrt(2)) sigma_S.

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of (2+1)-flavor QCD by Mitter and Schaefer (2013) and two-flavor studies by Pawlowski and Rennecke (2014) by including for the first time running Yukawa couplings and wave function renormalizations for both quarks and mesons in the three-flavor case. The results enable more reliable predictions for the chiral phase boundary at finite density and for the temperature-dependent quark composition of the eta-eta' meson complex, which has important consequences for identifying chiral partners in heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
