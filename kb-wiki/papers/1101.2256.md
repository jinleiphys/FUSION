# Microscopic description of large-amplitude shape-mixing dynamics with local QRPA inertial functions

> We introduce a microscopic approach to derive all the inertial functions in the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. Local normal modes are evaluated on the constrained mean field in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation in order to derive the inertial functions. The collective Hamiltonians for neutron-rich Mg isotopes are determined with use of this approach, and the shape coexistence/mixing around the N = 20 region is analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a fully microscopic method to compute all inertial functions in the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian using local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) normal modes on constrained mean fields, but the approach is demonstrated only for neutron-rich Mg isotopes and its accuracy depends on the validity of the local harmonic approximation for each constrained deformation point.

## Method
The authors evaluate local normal modes by solving the QRPA equations at each point on the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field energy surface. From these local QRPA modes, they extract the three vibrational mass parameters and three rotational moments of inertia that enter the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. The collective Hamiltonian is then constructed for neutron-rich Mg isotopes and solved to analyze shape coexistence and mixing in the N=20 island of inversion region.

## Key numbers
- The collective Hamiltonian is constructed for neutron-rich Mg isotopes, specifically around the N=20 region (N=20 corresponds to 32Mg, 34Mg, etc.)
- The approach uses the constrained HFB method with the Skyrme energy density functional and a zero-range pairing interaction

## Context
This work extends the standard five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian approach, which previously relied on phenomenological or approximate inertial functions (e.g., the cranking formula or the Gaussian overlap approximation), by providing a fully microscopic derivation using local QRPA. It enables more reliable predictions for shape coexistence and mixing in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly in the N=20 island of inversion region studied experimentally by Motobayashi et al. (1995) and theoretically by Caurier et al. (1998). The method can be applied to other mass regions where large-amplitude quadrupole shape dynamics are important.
