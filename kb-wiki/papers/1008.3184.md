# Delta and Omega electromagnetic form factors in a Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We investigate the electromagnetic form factors of the Delta and the Omega baryons within the Poincare-covariant framework of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The three-quark core contributions of the form factors are evaluated by employing a quark-diquark approximation. We use a consistent setup for the quark-gluon dressing, the quark-quark bound-state kernel and the quark-photon interaction. Our predictions for the multipole form factors are compatible with available experimental data and quark-model estimates. The current-quark mass evolution of the static electromagnetic properties agrees with results provided by lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for the electromagnetic form factors of the Delta and Omega baryons using a quark-diquark approximation within the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework, but these predictions represent only the three-quark core contributions and deliberately omit meson-cloud effects that are expected to be significant at low momenta.

## Method

The authors solve a quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Delta baryon, using dressed quark propagators from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and axial-vector diquark amplitudes from the diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation, all within a rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark-gluon interaction. The electromagnetic current is constructed by coupling the photon to the dressed quark, the axial-vector diquark, and the exchange kernel, with vertices constrained by Ward-Takahashi identities to preserve gauge invariance. The calculation employs an effective coupling with an infrared scale Lambda that is either fixed at 0.72 GeV or varied with current-quark mass to mimic quark-core behavior.

## Key numbers

- Delta mass from the fixed-scale model: M_Delta = 1.28 GeV, compared to the experimental value 1.232 GeV
- Delta mass from the core model: M_Delta = 1.73(5) GeV, where the bracket denotes sensitivity to the infrared width parameter eta
- Magnetic moment of Delta++: experimental estimate from the Particle Data Group is mu_Delta++ = 3.7 to 7.5 mu_N (nuclear magnetons)
- Magnetic moment of Delta+: MAMI result is mu_Delta+ = 2.7 (+5.5, -5.8) mu_N including experimental and theoretical errors
- Infrared scale for the u/d quark core model: Lambda = 0.98 GeV
- Fixed infrared scale for comparison: Lambda = 0.72 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger studies of the nucleon mass and form factors by Eichmann et al. (2008) and of the Delta mass by Nicmorus et al. (2008) to the electromagnetic form factors of the Delta and Omega baryons. The results are compared to unquenched lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou et al. (2009) at three different pion masses, and the current-quark mass evolution of static electromagnetic properties agrees with lattice results. The framework aims to provide a quark-core baseline that can later be augmented by meson-cloud effects from chiral effective field theory, and represents an intermediate step toward describing the N to Delta gamma transition.
