# Exotic Hadrons Of Minimal Pentaquark ($qqqq\Bar{Q}$) States

> It is shown that the exotic non-qqq hadrons of pentaquark $qqqq\bar{q}$ states can be clearly distinguished from the conventional qqq-baryon resonances or their hybrids if the flavor of $\bar{q}$ is different from any of the other four quarks. We suggest the physical process $p(e,e'K^-)Z(uuud\bar{s})$, which can be investigated at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab), as an ideal process to search for the existence or non-existence of the exotic hadron of minimal pentaquark state $Z(uuud\bar{s})$. (The search for the existence of $Z(uudd\bar{s})$ is also discussed in the paper. We added this sentence after the original published form of this paper.)

## Key claim

The paper argues that a pentaquark state of the form \(qqqq\bar{q}\) can be unambiguously distinguished from ordinary three-quark baryon resonances only if the flavor of the antiquark \(\bar{q}\) is different from any of the other four quarks; otherwise, such a state could be mistaken for a conventional baryon with a sea quark-antiquark pair. The authors propose that the specific state \(Z(uuud\bar{s})\) could be searched for via the process \(p(e,e'K^-)Z(uuud\bar{s})\) at JLab, but they hedge that the real production cross section may be lower than their optimistic estimate.

## Method

The authors use a baryon-meson fluctuation model of the nucleon sea, inspired by Brodsky (1996), to estimate the probability of finding the quark configuration \(uuud\bar{s}\) inside a proton. They then estimate the cross section for the electroproduction process \(p(e,e'K^-)Z(uuud\bar{s})\) by comparing it to the known process \(p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda\), using a convolution of the probabilities for the fluctuations \(p \to \Lambda K^+\) and \(\Lambda \to p K^-\). They also discuss the missing-mass technique to reconstruct the \(Z\) state from detected electrons and kaons.

## Key numbers

- The probability of finding the baryon-meson fluctuation \(p(uuds\bar{s}) = \Lambda(uds)K^+(u\bar{s})\) inside the proton is estimated to be on the order of 3%.
- The probability of finding the dominant baryon-meson fluctuation \(p(uudd\bar{d}) = n(udd)\pi^+(u\bar{d})\) inside the proton is estimated to be on the order of 15%.
- The probability of finding the baryon-meson fluctuation \(\Lambda(udsu\bar{u}) = p(uud)K^-(s\bar{u})\) inside \(\Lambda\) is estimated to be around 10%.
- The cross section for the process \(p(e,e'K^-)Z(uuud\bar{s})\) is estimated to be at most around 0.1 times that of the process \(p(e,e'K^+)\Lambda\), after removing kinematical factors.
- The mass of the \(Z(uuud\bar{s})\) state is predicted to be likely below or slightly above the threshold of a free proton and kaon, i.e., \(M = 1432\) MeV; if it is a partner of \(\Lambda(1405)\), its mass might be approximately 1405 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky (1996) to propose a clean experimental signature for pentaquark states, contrasting with earlier candidates such as \(\Lambda(1405)\) and the \(X(2050)\) and \(X(2000)\) states reported by Landua (1999), which all have hidden flavor and are difficult to distinguish from ordinary baryon resonances. The paper suggests that the proposed process at JLab could clarify the nature of \(\Lambda(1405)\) and enable a definitive search for the minimal pentaquark states \(Z(uuud\bar{s})\) and \(Z(uudd\bar{s})\), which would have no place in conventional three-quark baryon spectroscopy.
