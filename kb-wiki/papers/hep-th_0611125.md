# Schwinger Mechanism in the Presence of Arbitrary Time Dependent Background Electric Field

> We study, for the first time, the Schwinger mechanism for the pair production of charged scalars in the presence of an arbitrary time-dependent background electric field E(t) by by directly evaluating the path integral. We obtain an exact non-perturbative result for the probability of charged scalar particle-antiparticle pair production per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum (of the particle or antiparticle) from the arbitrary time dependent electric field E(t). We find that the exact non-perturbative result is independent of all the time derivatives d^nE(t)/dt^n, where n=1,2,....\infty. This result has the same functional dependence on E as the constant electric field E result with the replacement: E -> E(t).

## Key claim

The exact non-perturbative result for charged scalar pair production per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum from an arbitrary time-dependent electric field E(t) is independent of all time derivatives d^nE(t)/dt^n (n=1,2,...∞), and has the same functional form as the constant field result with the replacement E → E(t).

## Method

The authors directly evaluate the path integral for the one-loop effective action of charged scalar field theory in an arbitrary time-dependent background electric field E(t) along the z-axis, using the axial gauge A₃=0. They apply a recently derived shift theorem for path integrals and exploit the fact that the eigenvectors of a harmonic oscillator with time-dependent frequency ω(t) are the same as those of the constant frequency problem, with the replacement ω → ω(t). The s-contour integration is performed around the poles at s = inπ/|eE(t)| to extract the imaginary part of the effective action, which gives the pair production probability.

## Key numbers

- The probability per unit time per unit volume per unit transverse momentum is given by dW/(d⁴x d²p_T) = (|eE(t)|/(8π³)) Log[1 + exp(-π(p_T² + m²)/|eE(t)|)]
- For constant electric field E, the charged scalar result is dW/d⁴x = (e²E²/(8π³)) Σ_{n=1}^{∞} ((-1)^{n+1}/n²) exp(-nπm²/|eE|)
- For constant electric field E, the fermion result is dW/(d⁴x d²p_T) = -(|eE|/(4π³)) Log[1 - exp(-π(p_T² + m²)/|eE|)]

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger 1951) for pair production from constant electric fields to arbitrary time-dependent electric fields, and generalizes earlier WKB approximate methods by Casher et al. and path integral methods by Nayak. The result is consistent with numerical solutions of the semiclassical back-reaction problem studied by Cooper et al., where the constant-field Schwinger source term with E replaced by E(t) was used. The findings are relevant for early universe dynamics and for understanding quark-gluon plasma production and equilibration at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that Bose enhancement effects present in transport simulations are not included in their calculation and require further study.
