# Investigation of giant dipole resonance in Mo isotopes within TDHF theory

> The isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in the chain of even-even Mo isotopes is investigated within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) using the Skyrme force Sly6. The GDR calculated in $ ^{92-108}\text{Mo}$ are presented, and compared with the available experimental data. An overall agreement between them is obtained. Moreover, the dipole strength in $ ^{102-108}\text{Mo}$ is predicted. Shape phase transition from spherical to oblate as well as shape coexistence (A $\sim$ 100) in Mo isotopes are also investigated in this work. In addition, the correlation between the deformation splitting $ΔE$ and the quadrupole deformation parameter $β_{2}$ is studied. The results confirm that $ΔE$ is proportional to the deformation of nucleus. We also discuss the dependence of GDR strength on some nuclear properties of Skyrme forces, such as the asymmetry energy $a_{s}$. We find that $a_{s} = 32 MeV$, corresponding to SLy6, is quite consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method with the Skyrme force SLy6 reproduces the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in even-even Mo isotopes with "overall agreement" to experimental data, but the calculated GDR width is systematically underestimated because TDHF does not include the spreading width from 2p-2h and higher configurations.

## Method

The authors performed static Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations to obtain ground states of nine even-even Mo isotopes (92-108), then applied an instantaneous dipole boost to excite the isovector giant dipole resonance. The time evolution of the dipole moment was recorded and Fourier-transformed to obtain the GDR strength distribution, using the Sky3D code on a 24x24x24 Cartesian grid with 1 fm spacing and the SLy6 Skyrme parametrization.

## Key numbers

- For 92Mo (spherical), the calculated resonance energy is 16.63 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 16.90 ± 0.1 MeV (Beil 1974)
- For 100Mo (oblate minimum), the resonance energies are Ez ≈ 17.70 MeV along the short axis and Ex ≈ 14.70 MeV along the long axes
- For 108Mo (oblate), the resonance energies are Ez ≈ 17.30 MeV and Ex ≈ 14.15 MeV, with periods of 71.64 fm/c and 87.62 fm/c respectively
- The energy difference between the oblate and triaxial minima in 100Mo is only 0.05 MeV, indicating shape coexistence
- The asymmetry energy of SLy6 is as = 32 MeV, which the authors find consistent with experimental data

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of GDR in Sm isotopes by Mennana and Oulne (2021) to the Mo isotopic chain, and complements experimental photo-absorption measurements by Beil et al. (1974) on 92-100Mo and theoretical QRPA calculations by Colo et al. (2020) on ISGMR and ISGQR in Mo isotopes. The predictions for dipole strength in 102-108Mo provide guidance for future experimental investigations of neutron-rich molybdenum nuclei, and the shape coexistence found in 100Mo aligns with recent work by Shi et al. (2022) suggesting triaxial deformation in the ground state of that nucleus.
