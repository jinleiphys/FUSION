# Form factors in RQM approaches: constraints from space-time translations, extension to constituents with spin-1/2 and unequal masses

> Constraints related to transformations of currents under space-time translations have been considered for the relativistic quantum mechanics calculation of form factors of J=0 systems composed of scalar constituents with equal masses. Accounting for these constraints amounts to take into account many-body currents that restore the equality of the momentum transferred separately to the system and to the constituents, which holds in field-theory approaches but is not generally fulfilled in relativistic quantum mechanics ones. When this was done, discrepancies between results from different approaches could be found to vanish. The results are extended here to systems composed of spin-1/2 constituents with unequal masses. Moreover, as far as the equivalence of different approaches is concerned, some intermediate step could be skipped and the presentation of these results therefore slightly differs from the previous one. Due to the technical aspect of present results, this work is not aimed to be published but it could be useful for some applications like the form factors of the pion or kaon particles.

## Key claim

Constraints from space-time translations, which restore the equality of the squared momentum transferred to the constituents and to the whole system, can remove large discrepancies between different relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) calculations of form factors; the authors extend this treatment to systems of two spin-1/2 constituents with unequal masses, though the work is technical and not intended for publication but may be useful for applications like pion or kaon form factors.

## Method

The authors generalize previous work on scalar constituents with equal masses by deriving form factor expressions for a J=0 system of two constituents with unequal masses and spin-1/2, using the dispersion-relation approach, the front-form approach with q+=0, and generalized hyperplane (instant-form, front-form, and point-form) approaches. They incorporate constraints from space-time translations by introducing a factor α that multiplies the momentum transfer qμ, determined to enforce equality of the squared momentum transfer to constituents and the whole system, and they express the arguments of wave functions in terms of the spectator momentum p. The spin-1/2 case is handled by inserting extra factors from Dirac traces into the integrands of the scalar-constituent form factor expressions.

## Key numbers

- The function I(si, Q², sf) for unequal masses is derived as I(···) = θ(···) / (2Q√D), where D = 4s̄ + Q² + (si − sf)²/Q² and the θ function provides the condition θ((si sf / D) cΔm² − m₂²).
- The normalization integral for F₁(0) in the dispersion-relation approach for unequal masses yields F₁(0) = (8/N) ∫ dk k² φ̃²(k²) (e₁k + e₂k) / (2 e₁k e₂k), where e₁k = √(m₁² + k²) and e₂k = √(m₂² + k²).
- For the front-form approach with q+=0, the scalar form factor F₀(Q²) is given by (1/N) ∫ ds̄ d((si−sf)/Q) φ(si) φ(sf) θ(···) / (2√D), and the charge form factor F₁(Q²) by (1/N) ∫ ds̄ d((si−sf)/Q) φ(si) φ(sf) [2s̄ − 2Δm² + Q²] θ(···) / (D√D).
- For spin-1/2 constituents in the dispersion-relation approach, the scalar form factor integrand acquires the factor [2m₁(s̄ − (m₁−m₂)²) + m₂ Q²] / [2m₁ √(si−(m₁−m₂)²) √(sf−(m₁−m₂)²)], and the charge form factor integrand acquires the factor [2si sf − Δm²(2s̄+Q²) − (m₁−m₂)²(2s̄−2Δm²+Q²)] / [D√D √(si−(m₁−m₂)²) √(sf−(m₁−m₂)²)].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of constraints from space-time translations (Desplanques 2004, Desplanques 2008) from scalar constituents with equal masses to spin-1/2 constituents with unequal masses, and it generalizes results across multiple RQM approaches (instant-form, front-form, point-form) and the dispersion-relation approach. The results enable consistent calculation of form factors for physical systems such as the pion or kaon, and they resolve discrepancies previously noted between different RQM calculations (Dirac 1949, Keister and Polyzou) and dispersion-relation approaches (Anisovich 1992, Melikhov 2001, Krutov 2001). The authors note that the charge form factor expression for spin-1/2 constituents agrees with Anisovich (1992) and Melikhov (2001) but disagrees with Krutov (2001) for equal-mass constituents by a factor (si+sf+Q²)/(2√(si sf)).
