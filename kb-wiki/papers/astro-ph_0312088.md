# Resonance analysis of $^{147}$Sm(n,alpha) cross sections: Comparison to optical model calculations and indications of non-statistical effects

> We have measured the $^{147}$Sm(\textit{n},$α$) cross section from 3 eV to 500 keV and performed an $\mathcal{R}$-matrix analysis in the resolved region ($E_{n}$$<$ 700 eV) to extract $α$ widths for 104 resonances. We computed strength functions from these resonance parameters and compared them to transmission coefficients calculated using optical model potentials similar to those employed as inputs to statistical model calculations. The statistical model often is used to predict cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates. Comparing resonance parameters rather than cross sections allows more direct tests of potentials used in the model and hence should offer greater insight into possible improvements. In particular, an improved $α$+nucleus potential is needed for applications in nuclear astrophysics. In addition to providing a more direct test of the $α$% +nucleus potential, the $α$-width distributions show indications of non-statistical effects.

## Key claim
The measured alpha-width distributions for 147Sm(n,alpha) resonances show indications of non-statistical effects, as the alpha widths exhibit larger fluctuations than expected from statistical model predictions, contrary to the expectation that they should be intermediate between neutron and gamma fluctuations.

## Method
The 147Sm(n,alpha) cross section was measured from 3 eV to 500 keV using a compensated ionization chamber at the ORELA white neutron source, with a 95.3% enriched 147Sm2O3 sample. An R-matrix analysis using the SAMMY code was performed in the resolved resonance region below 700 eV to extract alpha widths for 104 resonances, and these widths were compared to optical model calculations using potentials similar to those employed in statistical model codes like NON-SMOKER.

## Key numbers
- Alpha widths were extracted for 104 resonances in the resolved region below 700 eV
- The overall normalization uncertainty of the cross section measurement is approximately 6%, dominated by a 4% uncertainty in the straggling correction and 3% uncertainty in sample sizes
- The s-wave neutron strength function was measured as (4.6 +/- 1.0) x 10^-4 for J=3 resonances and (4.3 +/- 0.9) x 10^-4 for J=4 resonances
- The alpha strength function for J=3 resonances over 0-600 eV is (0.116 +/- 0.025) x 10^-6, compared to optical model predictions of 1.0 x 10^-6 (P1) and 0.42 x 10^-6 (P2)
- The alpha strength function for J=4 resonances over 0-600 eV is (0.092 +/- 0.020) x 10^-6, compared to optical model predictions of 0.14 x 10^-6 (P1) and 0.063 x 10^-6 (P2)
- The average s-wave level spacing D0 is 5.7 +/- 0.5 eV for all s-wave resonances
- The Q-value for 147Sm(n,alpha) is 10.127 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous measurements by Koehler et al. (2000) and earlier studies by Kvitek (1970), Popov (1972), and Antonov (1980, 1984), which were hampered by the inability to measure alpha widths for most resonances. The improved detector and signal-to-noise ratio allowed extraction of alpha widths for most resonances in the resolved region, enabling more direct tests of the alpha+nucleus potential used in statistical model calculations for nuclear astrophysics, particularly for p-process nucleosynthesis in massive stars and supernovae as discussed by Hoffman (1999) and Rauscher (2002).
