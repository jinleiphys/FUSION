# $π_0$ pole mass calculation in a strong magnetic field and lattice constraints

> The $π_0$ neutral meson pole mass is calculated in a strongly magnetized medium using the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model within the random phase approximation (RPA) at zero temperature and zero baryonic density. We employ a magnetic field dependent coupling, $G(eB)$, fitted to reproduce lattice QCD results for the quark condensates. Divergent quantities are handled with a magnetic field independent regularization scheme in order to avoid unphysical oscillations. A comparison between the running and the fixed couplings reveals that the former produces results much closer to the predictions from recent lattice calculations. In particular, we find that the $π_0$ meson mass systematically decreases when the magnetic field increases while the scalar mass remains almost constant. We also investigate how the magnetic background influences other mesonic properties such as $f_{π_0}$ and $g_{π_0 q q}$.

## Key claim

The neutral pion pole mass systematically decreases with increasing magnetic field when a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant \(G(eB)\) is used, in contrast to the slight increase seen with fixed couplings; this running coupling, fitted to lattice QCD condensate data, yields quantitative agreement with lattice results for the pion mass, while the scalar meson mass remains almost constant.

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the random phase approximation (RPA) at zero temperature and zero baryonic density, with a magnetic-field-independent regularization (MFIR) scheme to avoid unphysical oscillations. They fit a magnetic-field-dependent coupling \(G(eB)\) to lattice QCD results for the average quark condensate at zero temperature, using a shifted Gaussian ansatz, and then solve the gap equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the neutral pion pole mass, the scalar meson mass, the pion decay constant, and the pion-quark coupling.

## Key numbers

* The fitted coupling parameters are \(\alpha = 1.44373\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\beta = 3.06\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), and \(\gamma = 1.31\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-4}\), giving \(G(0) = G_{II} = 4.50373\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
* The neutral pion mass decreases by about 30% at field strengths up to approximately \(1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\) when using the running coupling.
* With the running coupling, the scalar meson mass varies by less than 10% at intermediate field values, while with fixed couplings it reaches a value two and a half times higher than at \(B=0\) at \(eB \approx 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\).
* For heavy quark masses (set IV: \(m_0 = 50.16\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_{\pi_0}(0) = 417\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the running coupling yields quantitative agreement with lattice QCD results from Endrodi et al. (2015).
* The approximation \(I_{q,n}(0) \approx I_{q,n}(m_{\pi_0}^2)\) introduces an error of about 1% or less in the pion decay constant.

## Context

This work extends the RPA-MFIR formalism of Avancini et al. (2016) for the neutral pion mass in a magnetic field by introducing a running coupling \(G(eB)\) fitted to lattice QCD condensate data from Fodor et al. (2016), following the approach of Farias et al. (2014, 2016) for finite temperature. The results contradict the linear increase of the scalar mass found with fixed couplings and agree quantitatively with lattice QCD simulations of Endrodi et al. (2015) for the neutral pion mass, demonstrating that the simple NJL model with a running coupling can describe magnetized quark matter. The work enables further studies of mesonic properties in strong magnetic fields using effective models constrained by lattice data.
