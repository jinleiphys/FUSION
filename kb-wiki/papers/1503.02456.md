# A study of $d^*(2380)\to d ππ$ decay width

> The decay widths of the $\ds\to d π^0π^0$ and $\ds\to d π^+π^-$ processes are explicitly calculated in terms of our chiral quark model. By using the experimental ratios of cross sections between various decay channels, the partial widths of the $\ds\to pn π^0π^0$, $\ds\to pn π^+π^-$, $\ds\to pp π^0π^-$, and $\ds\to nn π^+π^0$ channels are also extracted. Further including the estimated partial width for the $\ds\to pn $ process, the total width of the $\ds$ resonance is obtained. In the first step of the practical calculation, the effect of the dynamical structure on the width of $\ds$ is studied in the single $ΔΔ$ channel approximation. It is found that the width is reduced by few tens of MeV, in comparison with the one obtained by considering the effect of the kinematics only. This presents the importance of such effect from the dynamical structure. However, the obtained width with the single $ΔΔ$ channel wave function is still too large to explain the data. It implies that the $\ds$ resonance will not consist of the $ΔΔ$ structure only, and instead there should be enough room for other structure such as the hidden-color (CC) component. Thus, in the second step, the width of $\ds$ is further evaluated by using a wave function obtained in the coupled $ΔΔ$ and CC channel calculation in the framework of the Resonating Group Method (RGM). It is shown that the resultant total width for $\ds$ is about 69 MeV, which is compatible with the experimental observation of about 75 MeV and justifies our assertion that the $\ds$ resonance is a hexaquark-dominated exotic state.

## Key claim

The total decay width of the $d^*(2380)$ resonance is calculated to be about 69 MeV using a wave function from a coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and hidden-color (CC) channel calculation, which is compatible with the experimental observation of about 75 MeV; this result supports the assertion that $d^*$ is a hexaquark-dominated exotic state, but only if the CC component (approximately 67% of the wave function) is included, as the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel approximation alone yields widths far exceeding the data.

## Method

The decay widths for $d^*(2380) \to d \pi^0\pi^0$ and $d^* \to d \pi^+\pi^-$ were calculated using the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with a non-relativistic quark-quark-pion effective Hamiltonian. The relative wave functions of the deuteron and $d^*$ were obtained by dynamically solving the Resonating Group Method (RGM) equation, first in a single $\Delta\Delta$ channel approximation and then in a coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and hidden-color (CC) channel calculation. Using the calculated widths for the two deuteron-plus-two-pion channels together with experimental cross-section ratios, the partial widths for the $pn\pi^0\pi^0$, $pn\pi^+\pi^-$, $pp\pi^0\pi^-$, $nn\pi^+\pi^0$, and $pn$ channels were extracted, and the total width was summed.

## Key numbers

- Total width of $d^*$ from the coupled $\Delta\Delta$+CC channel calculation: 69.1 MeV, compared to the experimental value of about 75 MeV.
- In the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel approximation, total widths are 167.6 MeV (for $M_{d^*}=2384$ MeV) and 125.8 MeV (for $M_{d^*}=2374$ MeV).
- Partial width for $d^* \to d \pi^0\pi^0$ in the coupled-channel calculation: 9.2 MeV (branching ratio 13.3%).
- Partial width for $d^* \to d \pi^+\pi^-$ in the coupled-channel calculation: 16.8 MeV (branching ratio 24.3%).
- Partial width for $d^* \to pn$ in the coupled-channel calculation: 8.3 MeV (branching ratio 12.0%).
- The calculated cross section for $d^* \to d\pi^+\pi^-$ is 1.83 times that for $d^* \to d\pi^0\pi^0$.
- Binding energy of $d^*$ in the coupled $\Delta\Delta$+CC calculation: approximately 84 MeV; in the single $\Delta\Delta$ channel: approximately 62 MeV.
- The coupling constant product $G = g_{qq\pi} I_o \sim 5.41$ GeV$^{-1}$ was fixed by fitting the measured width of 117 MeV for the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical RGM calculation of the $d^*(2380)$ mass by Huang et al. (2014), which predicted a binding energy of about 84 MeV and approximately 67% hidden-color configuration, by now computing the decay width. The results contradict the interpretation of $d^*$ as a pure $\Delta\Delta$ resonance (Wang et al., 2014), which predicted a width of about 150 MeV, and instead support the hexaquark-dominated exotic state hypothesis proposed by Bashkanov, Brodsky, and Clement (2013). The calculation enables future experimental searches for $d^*$ and its antiparticle $\bar{d}^*$ in processes at BaBar, Belle, CLEO, and BEPCII/BESIII.
