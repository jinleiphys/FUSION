# Convergence of electric quadrupole rotational invariants from the nuclear shell model

> Nuclei exhibit both single-particle and collective degrees of freedom, with the latter often subdivided into vibrational and rotational motions. Experimentally identifying the relative roles of these collective modes is extremely challenging, particularly in the face of possible shape coexistence. Model-independent, invariant quantities describing the deformation of a nucleus in the intrinsic frame have long been known but their determination potentially requires a large quantity of experimental data to achieve convergence. Through comparison with the nuclear shell model, the question of convergence will be addressed. Shell-model calculations performed in the $sd$- and $pf$-shell model spaces are used to determine electric-quadrupole matrix elements for a multitude of low-lying states. Relative contributions to the rotationally invariant quantities from multiple states can therefore be determined. It is found that on average, the inclusion of four intermediate states results in the leading-order invariant, $\left<Q^2\right>$, converging to within 10% of its true value and the triaxiality term, $\left<\cos{\left(3δ\right)}\right>$, converging to its true value, though some variance remains. Higher-order quantities relating to the softness of the nuclear shape are found to converge more slowly. The convergence of quadrupole rotationally invariant sum rules was quantified in the $sd$- and $pf$-shell model spaces and indicates the challenge inherent in a full determination of nuclear shape. The present study is limited to relatively small valence spaces. Larger spaces, such as the rare-earth region, potentially offer faster convergence.

## Key claim

Through shell-model calculations in the sd- and pf-shell model spaces, the inclusion of four intermediate states results in the leading-order invariant ⟨Q²⟩ converging to within 10% of its true value on average, and the triaxiality term ⟨cos(3δ)⟩ converging to its true value, though some variance remains; higher-order quantities relating to shape softness converge more slowly.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed with NuShellX@MSU using the USDB interaction in the sd-shell (with effective charges eπ = 1.36 and eν = 0.45) and the KB3G interaction in the full pf-model space (with effective charges eπ = 1.5 and eν = 0.5). Electric quadrupole matrix elements were determined for the first forty states of relevant spins across 34 nuclei, and these matrix elements were used to calculate rotationally invariant quantities (⟨Q²⟩, ⟨Q³cos(3δ)⟩, ⟨Q⁴⟩, and higher-order invariants) via intermediate-state expansions involving Wigner-6j symbols. Convergence was assessed by progressively increasing the number of states of each spin included (nI) and comparing to the nI = 40 values, with the deviation quantified as a percentage for ⟨Q²⟩ and σ(Q²) and as an absolute difference for cos(3δ) and σ(cos(3δ)).

## Key numbers

- With nominal effective charges and nI = 4, Δ⟨Q²⟩ = −9.4% with asymmetric uncertainties of +6.9% and −3.4%; Δcos(3δ) = 0.00 with uncertainties of +0.23 and −0.11.
- With nominal effective charges and nI = 1 (the approximation using only B(E2;0⁺₁→2⁺₁)), Δ⟨Q²⟩ = −21.2% with uncertainties of +12.6% and −9.2%; Δcos(3δ) = 0.26 with uncertainties of +0.42 and −0.37.
- With bare nucleon charges (eπ = 1, eν = 0) and nI = 4, Δ⟨Q²⟩ = −15.6% with uncertainties of +7.8% and −5.4%; Δcos(3δ) = 0.01 with uncertainties of +0.37 and −0.19.
- With nominal effective charges and nI = 20, Δ⟨Q²⟩ = −0.9% with uncertainties of +0.5% and −0.4%; Δσ(Q²) = −1.8% with uncertainties of +1.6% and −2.7%.
- The relative softness of Q² is well-localised at about 40% of the ⟨Q²⟩ expectation value for the full (nI = 40) calculations.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent quadrupole rotational invariant sum-rule method developed by Kumar (1972) and Cline (1986), which provides experimental access to nuclear quadrupole deformation in the intrinsic frame. It differs from theoretical approaches by Poves (2019) and Gilbreth (2018), which avoid the convergence issue and address full nuclear matter deformation, by instead employing the standard experimental method to inform experiment on electric quadrupole sum-rule convergence. The study is limited to sd- and pf-shell valence spaces, and the author notes that larger spaces such as the rare-earth region potentially offer faster convergence, while also enabling investigation of systematic deformation behaviours and the decoupling of proton and neutron shape distributions within the shell model.
