# Electromagnetic Gauge Invariance of the Cloudy Bag Model

> We examine the question of the gauge invariance of electromagnetic form factors calculated within the cloudy bag model. One of the assumptions of the model is that electromagnetic form factors are most accurately evaluated in the Breit frame. This feature is used to show that gauge invariance is respected in this frame.

# Electromagnetic Gauge Invariance of the Cloudy Bag Model

## Key claim

The cloudy bag model (CBM) respects electromagnetic gauge invariance in the Breit frame, meaning that no extra contact interaction terms need to be added for computing nucleon electromagnetic form factors in that frame. This result is limited to the Breit frame and relies on the inclusion of a vertex correction that was omitted in prior work by Musolf and Burkardt (1995) and Koepf and Henley (1995).

## Method

The authors evaluate the Ward identity for on-mass-shell nucleons in time-ordered perturbation theory, checking whether \( q_\mu \bar{u}(p') \Gamma^\mu(p',p) u(p) = \bar{u}(p') [S^{-1}(p') - S^{-1}(p)] u(p) \) holds in the Breit frame. They compute the nucleon self-energy \(\Sigma_{CBM}(p)\) using a loop integral formulation with the cloudy bag model form factor \( f(k) = 3 j_1(|\vec k|R)/|\vec k|R \), and separately evaluate the vertex contribution \( V^\mu \) (photon hitting the nucleon with an intermediate pion) and the direct pion term \(\Gamma_\pi^\mu\) (photon coupling to a charged pion). The Breit frame condition \( q^0 = 0 \) and \( |\vec p| = |\vec p'| = |\vec q|/2 \) is used throughout.

## Key numbers

- The cloudy bag model form factor is \( f(k) = 3 j_1(|\vec k|R)/|\vec k|R \), where \( R \) is the bag radius.
- In the Breit frame, the nucleon momentum magnitude is \( |\vec p| = |\vec q|/2 \) and the energy is \( E(p) = \sqrt{\vec{q}^2/4 + m^2} \).
- The self-energy difference \( \Sigma_{CBM}(p') - \Sigma_{CBM}(p) = 0 \) in the Breit frame, as shown by the integrals \( I_1 \) and \( I_2 \) which depend only on \( |\vec p| \).
- The contraction \( q_\mu V^\mu = (1/3)(\Sigma(p) - \Sigma(p')) = 0 \) in the Breit frame.
- The contraction \( q_\mu \Gamma_\pi^\mu = 0 \) in the Breit frame because \( \bar{u}(\vec q/2) \vec \gamma \cdot \vec q \, u(-\vec q/2) = 0 \).

## Context

This work addresses criticisms by Musolf and Burkardt (1995) and Koepf and Henley (1995), who argued that standard CBM computations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors were not gauge invariant and proposed adding contact interactions following Gross and Riska (1984) or Ohta (1989). The paper shows that within the Breit frame, the frame in which CBM form factors were originally evaluated, gauge invariance holds without extra terms, provided the vertex correction of Fig. 1(b) is included. The result is limited because the CBM is not covariant; the authors note that a fully covariant model involving quarks, mesons, and photons does not yet exist. This work enables continued use of the CBM for computing electromagnetic form factors of baryons, including strange quark content studies, without requiring ad hoc contact terms.
