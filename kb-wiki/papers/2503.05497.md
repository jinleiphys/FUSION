# $t$+$t$ cluster states in $^{6}$He

> The study of $t$+$t$ cluster states in $^{6}$He provides valuable insights into exotic nuclear structures and the behavior of fermionic cluster systems. This study shows rich cluster resonant state structures above the threshold, identified by experimental reconstruction and theoretical calculations. The excitation energy spectrum above the $t$+$t$ threshold in $^{6}$He is measured via the fragmentation excitation process during the breakup reaction of $^{9}$Li on a $^{208}$Pb target at an incident energy of 32.7 MeV/nucleon. The resonant states are reconstructed from the final state coincident particles $t$+$t$ using the invariant mass method, while the non-resonant background is estimated using the event mixing method. Two states of energy level peaks at $13.9\pm0.3$ and $15.0\pm0.3$ MeV are observed. Microscopic cluster model calculations exploring the $t+t$ resonant states in $^6\mathrm{He}$ yield theoretical energy spectra which are then compared with the current experimental results. The calculated reduced width amplitudes (RWA) of the $t+t$ channels further confirm the clustering structure of the identified $t+t$ resonant states.

## Key claim

The paper reports the observation of two \(t+t\) cluster resonant states in \(^{6}\)He at excitation energies of \(13.9\pm0.3\) MeV and \(15.0\pm0.3\) MeV, but notes that two candidate peaks around 16.9 MeV and 19.5 MeV could not be reliably fitted due to limited statistics, and that the 16.1 MeV and 18.3 MeV states reported by Povoroznyk et al. were not observed in this work.

## Method

The excitation energy spectrum of \(^{6}\)He above the \(t+t\) threshold was measured via the fragmentation excitation process during the breakup reaction of a \(^{9}\)Li beam at 32.7 MeV/nucleon incident on a \(^{208}\)Pb target. Resonant states were reconstructed from final-state coincident triton pairs using the invariant mass method, with non-resonant background estimated via the event-mixing method. Microscopic cluster model calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with \(t+t\) and \(\alpha+n+n\) configurations were performed, and reduced width amplitudes (RWA) were calculated to confirm the clustering structure.

## Key numbers

- Two resonant states observed at excitation energies \(13.93\pm0.26\) MeV (width \(0.89\pm0.94\) MeV) and \(14.95\pm0.30\) MeV (width \(0.15\pm1.21\) MeV), fitted with double Breit-Wigner functions using non-uniform binning.
- The \(^{9}\)Li secondary beam energy was 32.7 MeV/nucleon, with intensity approximately \(1.1 \times 10^{3}\) particles per second and purity exceeding 90%.
- The \(^{208}\)Pb target thickness was 526.9 mg/cm\(^2\); the \(^{9}\)Be production target thickness was 3038 \(\mu\)m.
- The detector spatial acceptance was approximately 43% at 13.5 MeV excitation energy; the mass resolution was about 0.4 MeV at 14.0 MeV excitation energy, increasing to about 0.8 MeV at 20.0 MeV.
- The GCM calculation used oscillator parameter \(b = 1.46\) fm and a radius cutoff \(R_{\text{cut}} = 5.0\) fm for the radius constraint method.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of \(^{6}\)He cluster states above the \(t+t\) threshold, which have shown discrepancies across different experiments: Ajzemberg-Selove and Tilley et al. compilations disagree on level energies, while Povoroznyk et al. (using the \(^{3}\)H(\(\alpha, tt\))\(^{1}\)H reaction) reported states at 14.0\(\pm\)0.4, 16.1\(\pm\)0.4, and 18.3\(\pm\)0.2 MeV. The present study achieves comparable precision for the lower two states but does not confirm the higher states, suggesting that different reaction mechanisms (fragmentation vs. three-body reactions) may excite different quantum states. The GCM calculations provide spin-parity assignments and RWA analysis that can guide future higher-statistics experiments and multi-reaction channel comparisons to resolve the resonance structure of \(^{6}\)He.
