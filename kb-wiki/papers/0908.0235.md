# Slowly rotating neutron stars and hadronic stars in chiral SU(3) quark mean field model

> The equations of state for neutron matter, strange and non-strange hadronic matter in a chiral SU(3) quark mean field model are applied in the study of slowly rotating neutron stars and hadronic stars. The radius, mass, moment of inertia, and other physical quantities are carefully examined. The effect of nucleon crust for the strange hadronic star is exhibited. Our results show the rotation can increase the maximum mass of compact stars significantly. For big enough mass of pulsar which can not be explained as strange hadronic star, the theoretical approaches to increase the maximum mass are addressed.

## Key claim

Rotation can increase the maximum mass of compact stars significantly, but even with rotation the maximum mass of a strange hadronic star in the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model (1.586 solar masses at the Kepler limit) is still insufficient to explain the observed mass of PSR J1903+0327 (1.67 solar masses), meaning this pulsar cannot be a strange hadronic star in this model.

## Method

The authors applied Hartle's method for slowly rotating stars to treat rotation as a small perturbation to the non-rotating structure, solving the perturbed TOV equations up to second order in angular velocity. They used equations of state from the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model for strange hadronic matter, non-strange hadronic matter, and neutron matter, with a nucleon crust EoS from Negele (1974) applied at baryon densities below 0.1 fm^-3. The Kepler limit for dynamical stability was imposed using an empirical formula.

## Key numbers

- Non-rotating maximum mass of strange hadronic star: 1.45 solar masses
- Maximum mass of strange hadronic star at the Kepler limit (6454 rad/s): 1.586 solar masses
- Non-rotating maximum mass of non-strange hadronic star: 1.7 solar masses
- Maximum mass of non-strange hadronic star at its Kepler limit (7039 rad/s): 1.877 solar masses
- Non-rotating maximum mass of neutron star: 1.8 solar masses
- Maximum mass of neutron star at its Kepler limit (6968 rad/s): 1.978 solar masses
- Radius increase of the maximum-mass strange hadronic star at 5000 rad/s compared to non-rotating: about 3.55 percent
- Crust contributes about 3.5 percent of the total moment of inertia at maximum mass for the strange hadronic star
- PSR J1903+0327 mass: 1.67 solar masses with rotation period 2.15 ms

## Context

This work extends the static (non-rotating) calculations of Wang (2005) for the same chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, which gave a maximum strange hadronic star mass of only 1.45 solar masses. The authors compare their rotating results with strange quark stars from the QMDTD model studied by Shen (2005). The paper shows that while rotation increases maximum masses by about 9-10 percent, the strange hadronic star still cannot reach the mass of PSR J1903+0327, unlike the non-strange hadronic and neutron star models. The authors suggest that differential rotation or a mixed hadron-quark phase (hybrid stars) could further increase the maximum mass, referencing work by Cook, Baumgarte (2000), and Alford (2007) on color-superconducting quark cores.
