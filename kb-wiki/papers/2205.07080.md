# Modeling survival probabilities of superheavy nuclei at high excitations

> This work investigated the first-chance survival probabilities of highly excited compound superheavy nuclei in the prospect of synthesizing new superheavy elements. The main feature of our modelings is the adoption of microscopic temperature dependent fission barriers in calculations of fission rates. A simple derivation is demonstrated to elucidate the connection between Bohr-Wheeler statistical model and imaginary free energy method, obtaining a new formula for fission rates. The best modeling is chosen with respect to reproducing the experimental fission probability of $^{210}$Po. Systematic studies of fission and survival probabilities of No, Fl, Og, and $Z$=120 compound nuclei are performed. Results show large discrepancies by different models for survival probabilities of superheavy nuclei although they are close for $^{210}$Po. We see that the first-chance survival probabilities of $Z$=120 are comparable to that of Fl and Og.

## Key claim

The first-chance survival probabilities of superheavy nuclei (Z=120) at high excitations are comparable to those of Fl and Og isotopes, but results show large discrepancies between different models for superheavy nuclei even though they are close for the benchmark nucleus 210Po.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations to obtain microscopic temperature-dependent fission barriers for superheavy nuclei. They then calculated fission rates using the Bohr-Wheeler statistical model, the imaginary free energy method, and a newly derived formula connecting these approaches, while neutron evaporation widths were computed using both the standard statistical model and a microscopic neutron gas model. The modeling was benchmarked against experimental fission probabilities of 210Po before being extrapolated to No, Fl, Og, and Z=120 compound nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The fission probability of 210Po was reproduced using energy-dependent fission barriers; a constant fission barrier height of 19.59 MeV gave much smaller fission widths at high excitations
- The ratio difference between Bohr-Wheeler and ImF results for 210Po was estimated as 26.3 at 65 MeV excitation energy, assuming saddle-point level density parameter a_sd = 1.1a
- For Fl, Og, and Z=120 isotopes, the curvature ω0 at the equilibrium point was found to be very small, indicating a soft fission valley that enhances stability against fission at high excitations
- The Bohr-Wheeler model results in fission widths of about 1-2 MeV at high excitations in the superheavy region
- The compound nuclei for synthesizing element 120 are 299120 and 302120 in attempted experiments

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fission barriers using finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods by Pei (2010) and earlier imaginary free energy calculations by Zhuyi (2016) that showed 278Cn in cold fusion cannot survive at high excitations while 292Fl in hot fusion still has considerable survival probability. The study enables more reliable theoretical guidance for experiments attempting to synthesize superheavy elements Z=119 and 120, which have been attempted at JINR, RIKEN, and GSI with no evidence of new elements observed so far, with the measured limit of residue cross section less than 65 fb.
