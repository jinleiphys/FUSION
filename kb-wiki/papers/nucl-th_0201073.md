# Gamow Shell Model Description of Neutron-Rich Nuclei

> This work presents the first continuum shell-model study of weakly bound neutron-rich nuclei involving multiconfiguration mixing. For the single-particle basis, the complex-energy Berggren ensemble representing the bound single-particle states, narrow resonances, and the non-resonant continuum background is taken. Our shell-model Hamiltonian consists of a one-body finite potential and a zero-range residual two-body interaction. The systems with two valence neutrons are considered. The Gamow shell model, which is a straightforward extension of the traditional shell model, is shown to be an excellent tool for the microscopic description of weakly bound systems. It is demonstrated that the residual interaction coupling to the particle continuum is important; in some cases, it can give rise to the binding of a nucleus.

## Key claim

This work presents the first continuum shell-model study of weakly bound neutron-rich nuclei involving multiconfiguration mixing, using the complex-energy Berggren ensemble as the single-particle basis. The residual interaction coupling to the particle continuum is shown to be important and, in some cases, can give rise to the binding of a nucleus, as demonstrated for the Borromean nucleus $^{6}$He where the ground state becomes particle-bound despite all basis states lying in the continuum.

## Method

The Gamow Shell Model (GSM) was formulated by diagonalizing a shell-model Hamiltonian, consisting of a one-body finite Woods-Saxon potential and a zero-range residual two-body interaction, in the complete Berggren basis of single-particle states. This basis includes bound states, narrow resonances (Gamow states), and a discretized non-resonant continuum along a complex contour in momentum space. The method was applied to two-neutron valence systems: $^{18}$O with an inert $^{16}$O core and $^{6}$He with an inert $^{4}$He core, using a $\delta$-force residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- For $^{18}$O, the single-particle $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ states are bound with energies -4.14 MeV and -3.27 MeV respectively; $0d_{3/2}$ is a resonance at 0.9 - i0.97 MeV.
- The $J^{\pi}=2_4^{+}$ state in $^{18}$O at $E_0$ = -2.61 MeV has a calculated neutron width of 57.04 keV (with 50 discretized scattering states), characterizing single-neutron emission.
- For $^{6}$He, the calculated ground state energy is $E$ = -0.951 - i0.01 MeV, reproducing the experimental ground state energy with respect to the two-neutron emission threshold.
- The first excited state $2_1^{+}$ in $^{6}$He is predicted at 2.25 MeV with a width $\Gamma$ = 700 keV (experimental width is 113 keV).
- The $0p_{3/2}$ resonance in $^{5}$He is found at $E$ = 0.745 - i0.32 MeV and the $0p_{1/2}$ resonance at $E$ = 2.13 - i2.94 MeV.
- Only about 10-20 vectors in the scattering continuum are sufficient to keep the error of calculated energies and widths at an acceptable level.

## Context

This work extends the continuum shell model (CSM) and the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) approach, which previously only allowed one particle in the continuum, to treat two particles in the continuum using the Berggren completeness relation. Prior approaches such as the hyperspherical harmonic method and coupled-channel approaches (Danilin 1998, Nielsen 2001, Esbensen and Bertsch 1999) could study three-body weakly bound nuclei but did not allow exact treatment of core excitations and antisymmetrization. The GSM enables microscopic description of Borromean systems like $^{6}$He and opens the door to further applications in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line.
