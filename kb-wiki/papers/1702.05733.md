# Energy loss of heavy quarks and $B$ and $D$ meson spectra in PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We study the production and evolution of charm and bottom quarks in hot partonic medium produced in heavy ion collisions. The heavy quarks loose energy in the medium which is reflected in the transverse momentum spectra of heavy mesons. The collisional energy loss of heavy quarks has been calculated using QCD calculations. The radiative energy loss is obtained using two models namely reaction operator formalism and generalized dead cone approach. The nuclear modification factors, $R_{AA}$ as a function of transverse momentum by including shadowing and energy loss are calculated for $D^{0}$ and $B^{+}$ mesons in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV and for $D^{0}$ mesons at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV and are compared with the recent measurements. The radiative energy loss from generalized dead cone approach alone is sufficient to produce measured $D^{0}$ meson $R_{AA}$ at both the LHC energies. The radiative energy loss from reaction operator formalism plus collisional energy loss gives good description of $D^{0}$ meson $R_{AA}$. For the case of $B^{+}$ meson, the radiative energy loss from generalized dead cone approach plus collisional energy loss gives good description of the CMS data. The radiative process is dominant for charm quarks while for the bottom, both the radiative process and the elastic collisions are important.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the radiative energy loss from the generalized dead cone approach alone is sufficient to reproduce the measured nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of $D^{0}$ mesons at both LHC energies, but for $B^{+}$ mesons the collisional energy loss must be added to the generalized dead cone radiative loss to match CMS data. This qualification is glossed over in the abstract's general statement about heavy quark energy loss.

## Method

The authors calculate leading-order pQCD production cross sections for charm and bottom quarks in pp collisions, then fragment them into $D^{0}$ and $B^{+}$ mesons using Peterson fragmentation functions. They model the quark-gluon plasma evolution with a simple isentropic cylindrical expansion using Lattice QCD and hadronic resonance equations of state, with initial temperatures derived from measured charged-particle multiplicities. The collisional energy loss is computed using the Peigne and Peshier (PP) formalism, while radiative energy loss is calculated using two independent models: the reaction operator formalism (DGLV) and the generalized dead cone approach; these energy losses are applied to modify the heavy quark transverse momentum spectra before computing $R_{AA}$ including nuclear shadowing via EPS09s parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass taken as 1.50 GeV; bottom quark mass taken as 5.0 GeV
- $k$ factor of 4 reproduces $D^{0}$ meson pp cross section data at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 TeV; $k$ factor of 5 reproduces $B^{+}$ meson pp cross section data at $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.02 TeV
- Initial temperature $T_0$ = 0.508 GeV for 0-10% central PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV; $T_0$ = 0.467 GeV for 0-10% central PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV
- Average path length $L$ = 5.74 fm for 0-10% central PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV; $L$ = 5.73 fm for 0-10% central PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV
- Initial and freeze-out times taken as 0.3 and 6 fm/$c$ respectively
- Strong coupling constant $\alpha_s$ = 0.3; number of quark flavors $N_f$ = 3
- Fragmentation fraction 0.557 for $D^{0}$ meson and 0.402 for $B^{+}$ meson
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: $\epsilon_c$ = 0.016, $\epsilon_b$ = 0.0012

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous simple hydrodynamic model (Saraswat 2015) for heavy quark energy loss by applying it to new LHC data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV from CMS, including both $D^{0}$ and $B^{+}$ meson measurements. It contrasts with more complex transport models such as BAMPS (Uphoff 2012), POWLANG (Beraudo 2014), the modified Langevin approach (Cao 2014), and PHSD (Bratkovskaya 2015), which require tuning of transport coefficients or inclusion of additional factors to match data. The results enable systematic comparison of two radiative energy loss formalisms (DGLV and generalized dead cone) against the same collisional energy loss baseline, showing that the choice of radiative model significantly affects whether collisional loss must be included to describe the data for charm versus bottom quarks.
