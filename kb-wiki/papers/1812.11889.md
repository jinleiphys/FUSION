# Hybrid equation of state with pasta phases and third family of compact stars

> The effect of pasta phases on the quark-hadron phase transition is investigated for a set of relativistic mean-field equations of state for both hadron and quark matter. The results of the full numerical solution with pasta phases are compared with those of an interpolating construction used in previous works, for which we demonstrate an adequate description of the numerical results. A one-to-one mapping of the free parameter of the construction to the physical surface tension of the quark-hadron interface is obtained for which a fit formula is given. For each pair of quark and hadron matter models the critical value of the surface tension is determined, above which the phase transition becomes close to the Maxwell construction. This result agrees well with earlier theoretical estimates. The study is extended to neutron star matter in beta equilibrium with electrons and muons and is applied to investigate the effect of pasta phases on the structure of hybrid compact stars and the robustness of a possible third family solution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the phenomenological interpolating construction used in previous works to mimic pasta-phase effects on the quark-hadron phase transition provides an adequate description of the full numerical solution, and establishes a one-to-one mapping between the free parameter of that construction and the physical surface tension of the quark-hadron interface. The robustness of a possible third family of compact stars depends on whether the relative pressure excess from pasta phases exceeds a critical threshold of about 5%.

## Method

The authors performed full numerical calculations of hybrid star matter with pasta structures (droplets, rods, slabs) using the Wigner-Seitz approximation and Thomas-Fermi method, solving the Poisson equation for the screened Coulomb potential with charge screening effects included. They used relativistic mean-field equations of state for hadron matter (KVORcut02 and KVORcut03 models) and quark matter (string-flip model with two parametrizations), and varied the surface tension parameter sigma over a wide range. For each pair of hadron and quark models, they fitted the numerical pressure-density results with a parabolic ansatz parameterized by a relative pressure excess Delta_P, and extracted the critical surface tension sigma_c above which the phase transition approaches the Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

- For the H1-Q1 model pair without muons: Delta_P(0) = 0.053 +/- 0.001, sigma_c = 52.3 +/- 1.27 MeV/fm^2, P_c = 82 MeV/fm^3, energy density jump Delta_E = 306 MeV/fm^3 at E_c = 433 MeV/fm^3
- For the H1-Q2 model pair without muons: Delta_P(0) = 0.053 +/- 0.002, sigma_c = 30.2 +/- 1.90 MeV/fm^2, P_c = 39 MeV/fm^3, Delta_E = 306 MeV/fm^3 at E_c = 309 MeV/fm^3
- For the H2-Q1 model pair without muons: Delta_P(0) = 0.048 +/- 0.001, sigma_c = 74.1 +/- 0.94 MeV/fm^2, P_c = 135 MeV/fm^3, Delta_E = 215 MeV/fm^3 at E_c = 585 MeV/fm^3
- For the H2-Q2 model pair without muons: Delta_P(0) = 0.060 +/- 0.001, sigma_c = 38.6 +/- 1.06 MeV/fm^2, P_c = 56 MeV/fm^3, Delta_E = 232 MeV/fm^3 at E_c = 401 MeV/fm^3
- The mean value of the beta parameter in the fit function S(x) is 0.68, with variation Delta_beta = 0.18
- The root-mean-square deviation of the fit for the H2-Q1 model ranges from 3.5% at sigma = 0 MeV/fm^2 to 1.0% at sigma = 40 MeV/fm^2 and 1.6% at sigma = 80 MeV/fm^2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pasta phases at the quark-hadron phase transition by Yasutake (2014) and the phenomenological mixed-phase construction by Ayriyan (2017, 2018). It provides the first direct numerical calibration linking the phenomenological parameter Delta_P to the physical surface tension sigma, confirming the earlier theoretical estimate of the critical surface tension by Voskresensky (2002). The results enable quantitative predictions for whether third-family compact star solutions (mass twins) survive when realistic pasta-phase effects are included, and allow calculation of observable properties such as moment of inertia and tidal deformability for hybrid stars with pasta phases.
