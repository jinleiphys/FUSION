# The su(1,1)-Algebraic Boson Model in the Deformed Boson Scheme

> Following the deformed boson scheme leading to the su(1,1)-algebra, a certain simple boson system is deformed in the framework of the second Holstein-Primakoff representation. With the aid of the MYT boson mapping, the second representation arrives at the first Holstein-Primakoff representation. The su(1,1)-algebraic model obtained in this procedure is compared with that in the Schwinger representation investigated by three of the present authors (A.K., Y.T. and M.Y.).

# The su(1,1)-Algebraic Boson Model in the Deformed Boson Scheme

## Key claim
The paper shows that an su(1,1)-algebraic boson model describing damped and amplified oscillations, previously formulated in the Schwinger boson representation, can be reproduced in the Holstein-Primakoff representation via a deformed boson scheme and the MYT boson mapping. However, the entropy expressions from the two representations differ in the third-order term, and the authors question whether this term can be neglected as widely as assumed in prior work.

## Method
The authors start with a two-boson system (c and d bosons) and deform the c-boson using a q-deformation scheme based on the second Holstein-Primakoff representation of the su(1,1)-algebra, with a positive integer parameter n0. The MYT boson mapping is then applied to transform the second Holstein-Primakoff representation into the first Holstein-Primakoff representation, where the boson number fluctuation is described by a new boson operator. The resulting Hamiltonian is compared with the Schwinger representation version from earlier work by three of the authors. The time-dependent variational method is used to derive classical equations of motion, and Tsallis entropy is calculated via power series expansion in (n0+1).

## Key numbers
- The Hamiltonian parameters are real parameters epsilon, omega, and chi, with no specific numerical values assigned.
- The deformation parameter n0 is a positive integer; the paper notes that cases n0=0 and n0=1 cannot be described.
- The entropy expansion is given to second order in (n0+1): sigma = sigma_0 + (n0+1)*sigma_1 + (n0+1)^2/2*sigma_2 + ...
- The exact value f(1/2) = pi^2/12 - (log 2)^2/2 = 0.5822 is given for the function f(x) = sum_{r=1}^{infinity} x^r * r^{-2}.
- The exact value f(1) = pi^2/6 = 1.6449 is given.
- The approximate formula with m=4 gives f(1/2) ~ 0.5833 and f(1) ~ 1.6458.
- The solution classification depends on the comparison between epsilon^2 and eta^2, where eta = chi|delta|/sqrt(n0).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous investigations of thermal effects in su(2)-algebraic models (Kuriyama, Tsue, Yamamura, 2002) and su(1,1)-algebraic models in the Schwinger representation (Kuriyama, Tsue, Yamamura, 2002). It also builds on the deformed boson scheme proposed by the same authors in earlier work (2002) and on the MYT boson mapping (Matsuo, Yamamura, Tsue, 1990s). The paper enables comparison between the Holstein-Primakoff and Schwinger representations for describing damped and amplified oscillations in many-boson systems with thermal effects, and raises open questions about the validity of neglecting higher-order entropy terms and about whether the wave packet used can properly represent a statistically mixed state.
