# Kaon-Nucleon Couplings for Weak Decays of Hypernuclei

> We investigate the weak kaon-nucleon (NNK) S-wave and P-wave interactions using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The leading 1-loop SU(3) breaking contributions to the $ppK$, $pnK$, and $nnK$ couplings are computed. We find that they suppress all NNK amplitudes by $30\%$ to $50\%$. The ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced hypernuclear decay widths is sensitive to such reductions. It has been argued that the discrepancy between the predicted and observed P-wave amplitudes in $Δs=1$ hyperon decay results from an accidental cancellation between tree-level amplitudes, and is not a fundamental problem for chiral perturbation theory. Agreement between experimentally determined NNK P-wave amplitudes and our estimates would support this explanation.

# Kaon-Nucleon Couplings for Weak Decays of Hypernuclei

## Key claim

The leading one-loop SU(3) breaking corrections suppress all NNK (kaon-nucleon) S-wave and P-wave amplitudes by 30% to 50%, but the authors stress that this computation is only an estimate because unknown counterterms also contribute at the same order; the results are scale-dependent and represent the leading nonanalytic chiral logarithms, not a complete prediction.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute the leading one-loop SU(3) breaking contributions (terms of the form m_s ln m_s) to the weak ppK, pnK, and nnK S-wave and P-wave amplitudes. They included loop graphs with octet and decuplet baryons and pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, etas), regularized in n-dimensions with modified minimal subtraction, and renormalized at the chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi = 1 GeV. The strong coupling constants D, F, C, and H were taken from one-loop computations of axial matrix elements, and the weak couplings h_D, h_F, h_C, and h_pi were determined from experimental hyperon and kaon decays.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level S-wave amplitudes: ppK = 2.0, pnK = 0.8, nnK = 2.8 (dimensionless); after one-loop corrections: ppK = 1.5 ± 0.1, pnK = 0.4 ± 0.1, nnK = 1.9 ± 0.4 (with Lambda_chi = 1 GeV)
- Tree-level P-wave amplitudes (in units of Lambda_chi/1 GeV): ppK = -2.4, pnK = 9.1, nnK = 6.7; after one-loop corrections: ppK = -1.7 ± 0.2, pnK = 7 ± 1, nnK = 6 ± 1
- The pnK S-wave amplitude is suppressed by approximately 50% from its tree-level value (0.8 to 0.4)
- The ppK and nnK S-wave amplitudes are suppressed by approximately 25-30% from their tree-level values
- Strong couplings used: D = 0.6 ± 0.1, F = 0.4 ± 0.1, C = -1.2 ± 0.1, H = -2.0 ± 0.2
- Weak couplings used (one-loop extracted): h_D = -0.35 ± 0.09, h_F = 0.86 ± 0.05, h_C = -0.36 ± 0.65
- Tree-level weak couplings: h_D = -0.58, h_F = 1.40, h_C ~ 1.4, h_pi = 1.4 MeV
- Kaon decay constant: f_K = 1.22 f_pi, with f_pi ~ 132 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of hypernuclear decay that either ignored SU(3) breaking or assigned an arbitrary 30% uncertainty to NNK couplings (e.g., the authors cite their own earlier work MS94). The computation addresses a long-standing problem in hyperon decay: the P-wave amplitudes for Delta s=1 hyperon decay show 100% SU(3 breaking corrections at one-loop (Jenkins and Manohar, 1992), which was suggested to result from accidental cancellations between tree-level amplitudes rather than a breakdown of chiral perturbation theory. Since NNK P-wave amplitudes have only one tree-level graph, no such cancellations occur, and the authors find the expected ~30% SU(3 breaking. The results are intended for incorporation into realistic hypernuclear models (e.g., Ramos et al., 1992) to improve predictions for the ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced decay widths of Lambda-hypernuclei such as 12_Lambda C, where current predictions disagree with experiment (Bennhold, 1995).
