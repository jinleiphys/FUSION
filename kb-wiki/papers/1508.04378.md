# Deviations of the Energy-Momentum Tensor from Equilibrium in the Initial State for Hydrodynamics from Transport Approaches

> Many hybrid models of heavy ion collisions construct the initial state for hydrodynamics from transport models. Hydrodynamics requires that the energy-momentum tensor $T^{μν}$ and four-currents $j^μ$ do not deviate considerably from the equilibrium ideal-fluid form, but the ones constructed from transport do not necessarily possess this property. In this work we investigate the space-time picture of $T^{μν}$ deviations from equilibrium in Au+Au collisions using a coarse-grained transport approach. The collision energy is varied in the range $E_{lab} = 5-160A$ GeV. The sensitivity of $T^{μν}$ deviations from equilibrium to collision centrality, and other parameters such as the switching criterion, the amount of statistics used to construct the initial state, and the smearing parameter $σ$ is investigated. For low statistics deviations of $T^{μν}$ from equilibrium are large and dominated by the effect of finite sampling. For large statistics the pressure anisotropy plays the most significant role, while the off-diagonal components of $T^{μν}$ are small in a large volume during the whole evolution. For all considered energies and centralities the pressure anisotropy exhibits a similar feature: there is a narrow interval of time, when it rapidly drops in a considerable volume. This allows us to introduce an "isotropization time," which is found to decrease with energy and slightly increase with centrality. The isotropization times are larger than times typically used for initializing hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies (Elab = 5-160A GeV), the energy-momentum tensor constructed from a transport model (UrQMD) deviates from the ideal-fluid form required for hydrodynamics, and these deviations are dominated by pressure anisotropy rather than off-diagonal components when sufficient statistics are used. The authors introduce an "isotropization time" which is found to be larger than the times typically used for initializing hydrodynamics in hybrid models.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hadronic transport approach to simulate Au+Au collisions at laboratory frame energies from 5 to 160A GeV. They coarse-grained the transport output onto a 2+1D space-time grid (event plane xz), constructed the energy-momentum tensor Tμν locally in each grid cell using Gaussian smearing with σ = 0.8 fm, and transformed to the Landau rest frame to compute pressure anisotropy X and off-diagonality Y as defined in the paper. They then studied the space-time evolution of these quantities and their dependence on collision energy, centrality, statistics, smearing parameter, and grid spacing.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range: Elab = 5-160A GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Pressure anisotropy criterion: X < 0.3 considered as limiting value for viscous hydrodynamics applicability
- Grid spacing: Δx = Δz = 0.6 fm used for final results
- Gaussian smearing: σ = 0.8 fm used for final results (with systematic errors assigned for σ in range 0.6-1.0 fm)
- Number of events: Nev = 1000 used for final results (saturation of X observed at this statistics for σ = 0.8 fm)
- For Y = 0.3 as acceptable level, the condition becomes Nev ρ σ³ > 100
- Isotropization time decreases with energy and increases with centrality; isotropization times are larger than times typically used for initializing hydrodynamics

## Context

This work extends studies of thermalization in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies using transport models, building on earlier work by Bravina (2008) that studied momentum distributions averaged over a central cell. The paper is particularly relevant for hybrid models that construct initial states for hydrodynamics from transport approaches, such as the UrQMD hybrid model by Petersen (2008), EPOS by Werner (2010), NeXSPheRIO by Andrade (2005), and the Karpenko hybrid model (2015). The results enable systematic verification of whether the weak consistency conditions for hydrodynamics initialization are satisfied, and suggest that fluidization times should be chosen based on isotropization rather than purely geometric criteria.
