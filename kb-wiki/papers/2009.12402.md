# A Novel Regularization Scheme for Nucleon-Nucleon Lattice Simulations with Effective Field Theory

> We propose a new regularization scheme to study the bound state of two-nucleon systems in Lattice Effective Field Theory. Inspired by continuum EFT calculation, we study an exponential regulator acting on the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) interactions, consisting of local contact terms. By fitting the low-energy coefficients (LECs) to deuteron binding energy and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) on a lattice simulation, we extract the effective range expansion (ERE) parameters in the $^3S_1$ channel to order $p^2$. We explore the impact of different powers of the regulator on the extracted ERE parameters for the lattice spacing $a=1.97$ fm. Moreover, we investigate how the implementation of the regularization scheme improves the predicted ERE parameters on the lattice spacing in the range of $1.4 \le a \le 2.6$ fm. Our numerical analysis indicates that for lattice spacing greater than $2$ fm, the predicted observables are very close to the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an exponential regulator for nucleon-nucleon contact interactions in lattice effective field theory simulations, and shows that for lattice spacing greater than 2 fm, the predicted effective range expansion parameters approach the experimental values, though the regulator does not fully correct the results for smaller lattice spacings.

## Method

The authors solve the discretized Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-nucleon bound states in the \(^3S_1\) channel on a cubic lattice using pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. They fit the low-energy coefficients \(C_0^L\) and \(C_2^L\) to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and asymptotic normalization coefficient for a given lattice spacing, then extract effective range expansion parameters from the lattice energy eigenvalues using the Luscher finite-volume formula.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy fixed to \(E_d = -2.224575\) MeV throughout
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient fixed to \(\text{ANC} = 0.249424\) fm\(^{-0.5}\) throughout
- For lattice spacing \(a = 1.97\) fm at NLO with regulator \(n=1, b=0.01\): scattering length \(a_t = 5.43(5)\) fm, effective range \(r_t = 1.74(4)\) fm
- For lattice spacing \(a = 2.6\) fm at NLO with regulator: scattering length \(a_t = 5.42(5)\) fm, effective range \(r_t = 1.79(4)\) fm
- Experimental values: scattering length \(a_t = 5.424(4)\) fm, effective range \(r_t = 1.759(5)\) fm
- Lattice spacing range studied: \(1.4 \le a \le 2.6\) fm
- Regulator parameter \(b\) defined by \(b \cdot a^3 = \mathcal{A}\) with \(\mathcal{A} = 7.645373 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^3\)

## Context

This work extends the lattice EFT studies of two-nucleon systems by Harada (2016) and the regularization studies by Klein (2015, 2018), who applied different regularization schemes at leading order in pionless and pionfull EFT. The proposed exponential regulator, inspired by continuum EFT calculations of Epelbaum (2000), is applied at next-to-leading order for the first time on a lattice, and enables improved extraction of scattering parameters for coarse lattices with spacing above 2 fm.
