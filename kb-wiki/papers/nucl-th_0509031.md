# Renormalisation-group analysis of repulsive three-body systems

> A coordinate space approach, based on that used by Efimov, is applied to three-body systems with contact interactions between pairs of particles. In systems with nonzero orbital angular momentum or with asymmetric spatial wave functions, the hyperradial equation contains a repulsive 1/r^2 potential. The resulting wave functions are used in a renormalisation group analysis. This confirms Griesshammer's power counting for short-range three-body forces in these systems. # Renormalisation-group analysis of repulsive three-body systems

## Key claim
The paper confirms Griesshammer's power counting for short-range three-body forces in repulsive three-body systems, with the exception of channels like the 4S channel for three nucleons where any derivatives needed in the interaction are already counted by the scaling with the cut-off, meaning no additional powers need to be added.

## Method
The author applies a coordinate-space approach based on Efimov's method, using hyperspherical coordinates to solve the free Schrodinger equation with boundary conditions representing zero-range two-body forces. The hyperradial equation yields a repulsive 1/r^2 potential for systems with nonzero orbital angular momentum or asymmetric spatial wave functions, and the resulting wave functions are used in a renormalisation group analysis to determine RG eigenvalues that give the power counting for short-range three-body forces.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric systems (lambda=+1) with l=0, the hyperangular eigenvalue equation has an imaginary solution for nu, leading to oscillatory wave functions and the Efimov effect
- For l >= 1 or systems with one antisymmetric pair (lambda=-1/2), the roots of the eigenvalue equation are real, making the 1/r^2 potential repulsive with no Efimov effect
- A term in the rescaled potential proportional to p^(2n) varies with the cut-off Lambda as Lambda^(2(n+nu)) p^(2n), giving RG eigenvalue rho = 2(n+nu)
- The leading term in each channel is of order 2nu, matching Griesshammer's Table 3 results except for the 4S and Wigner-antisymmetric 2S channels
- For two-body scattering with nonzero angular momentum L, setting nu = L + 1/2 gives the leading short-distance interaction of order 2L, with RG eigenvalue rho = 2L + 1

## Context
This work extends the coordinate-space RG approach developed in Birse (Refs. bmr, bb1, bb2) to repulsive three-body systems, confirming Griesshammer's momentum-space results from solving the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation. The analysis corrects Griesshammer's counting for the leading three-body force in the 4S channel for three nucleons, where additional derivatives are already accounted for by the RG eigenvalue. The results show that NDA (naive dimensional analysis) fails in these systems, with short-range three-body forces demoted to higher orders than naively expected in most low partial waves, making the standard LO/NLO classification inconvenient.
