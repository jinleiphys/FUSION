# The stopping of swift protons in matter and its implication for astrophysical fusion reactions

> The velocity dependence of the stopping power of swift protons and deuterons in low energy collisions is investigated. At low projectile energies the stopping is mainly due to nuclear stopping and charge exchange of the electron. The second mechanism dominates after Ep >=200 eV. A dynamical treatment of the charge exchange mechanism based on two-center electronic wavefunctions yields very transparent results for the exchange probability. We predict that the stopping cross sections vary approximately as v to the 1.35 power for projectile protons on hydrogen targets in the 1 keV energy region.

# The stopping of swift protons in matter and its implication for astrophysical fusion reactions

## Key claim
The stopping cross section for protons on hydrogen targets in the 1 keV energy region varies approximately as v_p^1.35, not as the linear v_p dependence assumed in extrapolations from the Andersen-Ziegler tables. This velocity dependence is derived from a two-center dynamical treatment of charge exchange, but the authors note their calculation is limited to the simplest p+H system and cannot be directly extended to helium targets without a much larger basis.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the electron in a dynamical two-center field, expanding the electronic wavefunction in terms of 1sσ and 2pσ two-center states from Teller's (1930) static solution. They solved coupled-channels equations for the time-dependent amplitudes, assuming the proton follows a classical trajectory with impact parameter b, and computed the charge-exchange probability and cross section. The stopping cross section was obtained by combining the charge-exchange energy loss (ΔE = m_e v_p^2/2) with nuclear stopping from Lindhard's theory.

## Key numbers
- Charge-exchange cross section at zero projectile energy: σ(E_p=0) = 37.88 × 10^{-16} cm^2
- Stopping cross section varies as S ∼ v_p^{1.35} for proton energies in the range 100 eV to 1 keV
- Charge-exchange stopping mechanism dominates over nuclear stopping for proton energies E_p ≥ 200 eV
- For the D(p,γ)^3He reaction in the sun, the most effective center-of-mass energy is E_cm = 6.5 ± 3.3 keV at T = 15 × 10^6 K

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the adiabatic model of atomic screening in low-energy fusion reactions and experimental data, which Langanke et al. (1996) and Bang et al. (1996) attributed to erroneous extrapolations of stopping power from the Andersen-Ziegler tables (1977). The authors extend the dynamical calculations of Grande and Schwietz (1993) to the simplest p+H system, showing that the stopping power does not follow a universal pattern and that charge-exchange effects must be treated system-by-system. The results call for improved theoretical studies and independent experimental verification of stopping powers for astrophysically important reactions such as D(p,γ)^3He and d+D reactions.
