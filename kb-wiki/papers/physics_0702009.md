# Comment on "Taleyarkhan et al. Reply:"

> In their Reply to my previous Comment, Taleyarkhan and coauthors measure their detectors' responses to a Cf-252 source, concluding that the resulting spectra differ substantially from the cavitation-fusion spectra published earlier in their Letter. On the contrary, I conclude that the two data sets are qualitatively consistent.

## Key claim

Taleyarkhan et al.'s cavitation-fusion spectra are qualitatively consistent with their own Cf-252 spectra, contrary to their claim that the two data sets differ substantially.

## Method

Naranjo cross-calibrated the detector gain between the Reply and the original Letter by comparing the calibrated and uncalibrated Cf-252 spectra, finding a 10% gain reduction in the Reply. He then compared the background-subtracted cavitation-fusion proton-recoil spectrum from the Letter's supplement with the Reply's Cf-252 spectrum after applying this cross-calibration. For the gamma spectra, he noted that the "cavitation on" spectrum was within approximately 2% of the "cavitation off" background, making the Reply's comparison of Cf-252 gamma spectra against the natural gamma background inappropriate.

## Key numbers

- Detector gain was approximately 10% less in the Reply than in the Letter, determined by comparing calibrated and uncalibrated Cf-252 spectra.
- The "cavitation on" gamma spectrum was within approximately 2% of the "cavitation off" background spectrum (from Fig. 15(a) of the Letter's supplement).
- In channel 14 of the gamma spectrum, there were approximately 970 "on" counts and 940 "off" counts, yielding a difference of 30 ± 40 counts.
- The Reply's Cf-252 source was placed only 30 cm from the detector.

## Context

This Comment extends Naranjo's previous Comment (2006) challenging Taleyarkhan et al.'s claims of cavitation fusion in their Letter (Taleyarkhan 2006). The Reply's Cf-252 spectra are shown to be consistent with the cavitation-fusion spectra, undermining the authors' defense. The work references prior experimental DD-fusion proton-recoil spectra from Zmeskal (1990) and Nagele (1989) for comparison. The analysis enables future scrutiny of whether the claimed cavitation-fusion signals are actually indistinguishable from Cf-252 neutron and gamma signatures.
