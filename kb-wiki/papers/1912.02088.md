# Heavy-Light Mesons on the Light Front

> We study the heavy-light mesons within basis light-front quantization. The resulting mass spectra of $D$, $D_s$, $B$, and $B_s$ agree reasonably well with experiments. We also predict states which could be measured in the near future. In the light-front formalism, we calculate the light-front wave functions and additional experimental observables, such as parton distribution functions, distribution amplitudes, and decay constants by means of integrations over light-front wave functions. We also provide ratios of decay constants for selected pseudoscalar meson decays ($D_s$ to $D$ and $B_s$ to $B$) as they may prove to be theoretically more robust and more reliably determined in experiments. We find that our ratios are systematically smaller than existing experiment and other approaches by $5-18\%$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach produces heavy-light meson mass spectra that agree reasonably well with experiment, but they note that their calculated ratios of decay constants for pseudoscalar meson decays (D_s to D and B_s to B) are systematically smaller than existing experiment and other approaches by 5-18%.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front eigenvalue problem for heavy-light mesons using an effective Hamiltonian within the valence quark-antiquark Fock sector, comprising a holographic QCD Hamiltonian augmented by massive quark kinematics and longitudinal confinement, plus an effective one-gluon-exchange potential with a running coupling. They expand the light-front wave functions in a basis of 2D harmonic oscillator functions for the transverse direction and Jacobi polynomials for the longitudinal direction, truncating the basis with parameters N_max and L_max set to 32. The confining strength and light quark masses (u/d and s) are fitted to reproduce the lowest pseudoscalar and vector state masses of D, D_s, B, and B_s mesons from experiment.

## Key numbers

- The confining strength for light mesons is set to kappa = 0.59 GeV, while for heavy-light systems the confining strength is computed as kappa_{cbar{u}/cbar{d}} = 0.800 GeV and kappa_{bbar{u}/bbar{d}} = 1.067 GeV.
- The fitted light quark masses are m_u/m_d = 0.553 GeV and m_s = 0.647 GeV; the charm quark mass is 1.603 GeV and the bottom quark mass is 4.902 GeV.
- The calculated pseudoscalar and vector masses for D (cbar{u}/cbar{d}) are 1.842 GeV and 2.050 GeV, with an r.m.s. deviation of 78 MeV over 5 compared states.
- The calculated pseudoscalar and vector masses for D_s (cbar{s}) are 1.944 GeV and 2.147 GeV, with an r.m.s. deviation of 40 MeV over 9 compared states.
- The calculated pseudoscalar and vector masses for B (bbar{u}/bbar{d}) are 5.291 GeV and 5.339 GeV, with an r.m.s. deviation of 21 MeV over 4 compared states.
- The calculated pseudoscalar and vector masses for B_s (bbar{s}) are 5.379 GeV and 5.428 GeV, with an r.m.s. deviation of 37 MeV over 4 compared states.
- The mean spread in masses over allowed m_J values (reflecting rotational symmetry violation) is 30 MeV (1.4%) for D, 25 MeV (1.1%) for D_s, 6.0 MeV (0.11%) for B, and 5.6 MeV (0.10%) for B_s.
- The resolution scales for PDFs are mu_{D/D_s} approximately 4.5 GeV and mu_{B/B_s} approximately 6.0 GeV, corresponding to N_max = L_max = 32.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia (Li et al., 2017; Li et al., 2018) to the heavy-light meson systems D, D_s, B, and B_s, providing a unified Hamiltonian framework with only two fitted parameters (light quark masses). The results are compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2018) and lattice QCD calculations from multiple groups (e.g., Lang et al., 2015; PhysRevD.83.014506, 2011). The calculated light-front wave functions enable predictions for parton distribution functions, distribution amplitudes, and decay constants that can be tested by ongoing experiments such as Belle II at SuperKEKB, and the authors note that incorporating chiral dynamics (as in PhysRevC.99.035206) would be needed to properly describe light meson properties.
