# Search for axion-like dark matter through nuclear spin precession in electric and magnetic fields

> We report on a search for ultra-low-mass axion-like dark matter by analysing the ratio of the spin-precession frequencies of stored ultracold neutrons and $^{199}$Hg atoms for an axion-induced oscillating electric dipole moment of the neutron and an axion-wind spin-precession effect. No signal consistent with dark matter is observed for the axion mass range $10^{-24}~\textrm{eV} \le m_a \le 10^{-17}~\textrm{eV}$. Our null result sets the first laboratory constraints on the coupling of axion dark matter to gluons, which improve on astrophysical limits by up to 3 orders of magnitude, and also improves on previous laboratory constraints on the axion coupling to nucleons by up to a factor of 40.

## Key claim

The paper reports no detection of axion-like dark matter in the mass range \(10^{-24}~\textrm{eV} \le m_a \le 10^{-17}~\textrm{eV}\), but the null result is conditional on the assumption that axions saturate the local cold dark matter energy density of approximately \(0.4~\textrm{GeV/cm}^3\); if axions constitute only a sub-dominant fraction of dark matter, the constraints shift accordingly.

## Method

The experiment analysed the ratio of spin-precession frequencies of stored ultracold neutrons and \(^{199}\)Hg atoms, using data from two separate apparatuses: the Sussex--RAL--ILL room-temperature nEDM experiment (1998-2002, at the Institut Laue-Langevin) for long time-base oscillations (periods longer than days), and the PSI nEDM experiment for short time-base oscillations (periods down to minutes). The search targeted two effects linear in the axion coupling constant: an axion-induced oscillating electric dipole moment of the neutron, and an axion-wind spin-precession effect arising from the derivative coupling of the axion field to nucleon spins. Periodograms were constructed from the time series of neutron EDM measurements to search for oscillatory signals in frequency space.

## Key numbers

* Axion mass range searched: \(10^{-24}~\textrm{eV} \le m_a \le 10^{-17}~\textrm{eV}\)
* First laboratory constraints on the axion-gluon coupling improve on astrophysical limits by up to 3 orders of magnitude
* Laboratory constraints on the axion-nucleon coupling improve on previous laboratory limits by up to a factor of 40
* The oscillating neutron EDM induced by the axion-gluon coupling is given by \(d_\mathrm{n}(t) \approx +2.4 \times 10^{-16} ~ (C_G a_0 / f_a) \cos(m_a t) ~ e \cdot \textrm{cm}\)
* The oscillating \(^{199}\)Hg atomic EDM is \(d_{\textrm{Hg}}(t) \approx +1.3 \times 10^{-19} ~ (C_G a_0 / f_a) \cos(m_a t) ~ e \cdot \textrm{cm}\)
* Local cold dark matter energy density assumed: \(\rho_{\rm DM}^{\rm local} \approx 0.4~\textrm{GeV/cm}^3\)
* Ratio of neutron to mercury magnetic moments used: \(\mu_\mathrm{n} / \mu_\textrm{Hg} = -3.8424574(30)\)

## Context

This work extends the search for axion-like dark matter beyond previous astrophysical constraints from Big Bang nucleosynthesis (Blum 2014, Stadnik 2015) and supernova energy-loss bounds (Raffelt 1990, Graham 2013), and improves on earlier laboratory magnetometry searches for axion-mediated spin-dependent forces (Romalis 2009). The analysis uses the same ultracold neutron and \(^{199}\)Hg co-magnetometer system previously employed for searches for new non-EDM physics (Altarev 2009, Altarev 2010, Afach 2015). The results enable future experiments to probe axion couplings at higher sensitivity and to extend the search to higher axion masses.
