# Bottomonia suppression in heavy-ion collisions

> Using the two-component model that includes both initial production from nucleon-nucleon hard scattering and regeneration from produced quark-gluon plasma (QGP), we study the effect of medium modifications of the binding energies and radii of bottomonia on their production in heavy-ion collisions. We find that the contribution to bottomonia production from regeneration is small and the inclusion of medium effects is generally helpful for understanding the observed suppression of bottomonia production in experiments carried out at both the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The two-component model (initial production plus QGP regeneration) can describe bottomonia suppression at RHIC and LHC, but the inclusion of medium-modified binding energies and radii is essential for matching data except in midcentral LHC collisions, where the model without medium effects fits better, a puzzle that improved data must resolve.

## Method

The authors used a two-component model that includes initial bottomonia production from nucleon-nucleon hard scattering and regeneration from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), with the expansion of the hot dense matter described by a schematic viscous hydrodynamics. They calculated thermal decay widths of bottomonia up to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, incorporating medium effects via a screened Cornell potential to obtain temperature-dependent binding energies and radii, then solved a rate equation for bottomonia numbers including both thermal dissociation and regeneration.

## Key numbers

- Initial maximum temperatures: 324 MeV (RHIC central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and 390 MeV (LHC central Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV); mean temperatures: 269 MeV (RHIC) and 311 MeV (LHC).
- Dissociation temperatures (in units of T_c): Upsilon(1S) 4.0, Upsilon(2S) 1.67, Upsilon(3S) 1.12, chi_b(1P) 1.51, chi_b(2P) 1.09.
- Vacuum masses and radii for bottomonia: Upsilon(1S) 9.5 GeV and 0.23 fm; Upsilon(2S) 9.97 GeV and 0.59 fm; Upsilon(3S) 10.19 GeV and 0.95 fm; chi_b(1P) 9.9 GeV and 0.46 fm; chi_b(2P) 10.25 GeV and 0.82 fm.
- Nuclear absorption cross section of bottomonium at RHIC: 4.3 mb (from R_dAu = 0.78 ± 0.28 in d+Au collisions).
- Regeneration contribution to bottomonia R_AA: less than 1% at both RHIC and LHC for all centralities.
- Bottom quark relaxation time: about 14 fm/c (compared to 4 fm/c for charm quarks).
- Initial thermalization times: 0.9 fm/c (RHIC) and 1.05 fm/c (LHC).

## Context

This work extends the two-component model previously applied to J/psi suppression by Song, Han, and Ko (2011) to the bottomonium system, using the same framework of initial production plus QGP regeneration with a rate equation. It contrasts with earlier studies that did not include medium modifications of bottomonia properties, and it provides a cleaner probe of QGP properties than charmonium because bottomonia are less affected by cold nuclear matter effects and regeneration is negligible. The results enable future experimental data to discriminate between models with and without medium effects, particularly for midcentral LHC collisions where the current data show a discrepancy.
