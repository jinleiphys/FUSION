# Improving the convergence of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces II: low phases and the deuteron

> Recently we have proposed a new cut-off scheme for pion loop integrals in the two-pion exchange potential. This method allows for a consistent implementation of constraints from pion-nucleon scattering and has been successfully applied to peripheral nucleon-nucleon partial waves. We now consider low partial waves in the non-perturbative regime, where the regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation has to be solved in order to generate the bound and scattering states. We observe an improved description of most of the phase shifts when going from next-to- to next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. We also find a good description of the deuteron properties. In addition, the new cut-off scheme allows to avoid the presence of unphysical deeply bound states. We discuss the cut-off dependence of the four-nucleon low-energy constants and show that their numerical values can be understood in terms of resonance saturation. This connects the effective field theory approach to boson exchange phenomenology.

# Improving the convergence of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces II: low phases and the deuteron

## Key claim

The spectral function regularization scheme for two-pion exchange potentials, previously applied to peripheral partial waves, also improves the description of low partial waves (S- and P-waves) and deuteron properties when solving the non-perturbative Lippmann-Schwinger equation, provided the cut-off parameters are chosen in the range of 450-650 MeV. The method avoids unphysical deeply bound states that appear in dimensional regularization, and the numerical values of the four-nucleon low-energy constants can be understood through resonance saturation, connecting chiral effective field theory to boson exchange phenomenology.

## Method

The authors apply spectral function regularization to the two-pion exchange potential by introducing a sharp cut-off (tilde-Lambda = 500-700 MeV) on the mass spectra in the dispersion integral representation, suppressing short-distance components. They then solve the regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation non-perturbatively for low partial waves, using an additional exponential regulator function f^Lambda(p) = exp[-p^6/Lambda^6] with Lambda varied between 450-600 MeV at NLO and 450-650 MeV at NNLO. The low-energy constants C_S, C_T and C_1...C_7 are fixed from fits to np S- and P-waves and the mixing parameter epsilon_1 for laboratory energies below 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off range in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation: Lambda = 450-600 MeV at NLO, 450-650 MeV at NNLO
- Spectral function cut-off range: tilde-Lambda = 500-700 MeV at both NLO and NNLO
- Pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV; charged pion mass: M_pi± = 139.570 MeV; neutral pion mass: M_pi0 = 134.977 MeV; nucleon mass: m = 938.918 MeV
- LECs from piN analysis: c_1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c_3 = -3.40 GeV^-1, c_4 = 3.40 GeV^-1
- 1S0 scattering length at NNLO with CR: a = -23.497 to -23.689 fm (Nijmegen PSA: -23.739 fm)
- 1S0 effective range at NNLO with CR: r = 2.62 to 2.67 fm (Nijmegen PSA: 2.68 fm)
- 3S1 scattering length at NNLO with CR: a = 5.424 to 5.430 fm (Nijmegen PSA: 5.420 fm)
- 3S1 effective range at NNLO with CR: r = 1.725 to 1.735 fm (Nijmegen PSA: 1.753 fm)
- Deuteron binding energy at NNLO with CR: E_d = -2.189 to -2.202 MeV (experimental: -2.225 MeV)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment at NNLO with CR: Q_d = 0.271 to 0.275 fm^2 (experimental: 0.286 fm^2)
- Deuteron asymptotic S-wave normalization at NNLO with CR: A_S = 0.874 to 0.879 fm^-1/2 (experimental: 0.885 fm^-1/2)
- Deuteron D-state probability at NNLO with CR: P_D = 3.53% to 4.93%

## Context

This work extends the spectral function regularization method introduced by Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2003, referred to as Paper I) from peripheral partial waves (l >= 2) to low partial waves in the non-perturbative regime. It addresses the problem of unphysically strong attraction in the isoscalar central part of chiral two-pion exchange at NNLO found in earlier approaches using dimensional regularization, which produced spurious deeply bound states. The results connect chiral effective field theory to boson exchange phenomenology by showing that the four-nucleon low-energy constants can be understood through resonance saturation, comparing with the Bonn-B potential of Machleidt (1989) and the Nijmegen 93 potential of Stoks and Rijken (1993). The work enables systematic improvement to N^3LO, where a new counter term appears that corrects the problematic 3P0 channel.
