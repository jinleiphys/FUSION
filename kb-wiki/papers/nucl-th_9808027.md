# Combined effects of nuclear Coulomb field, radial flow, and opaqueness on two-pion correlations

> Correlations of two like charged pions emitted from a hot and charged spherically expanding nuclear system are investigated. The motion of the pions is described quantum mechanically using the Klein-Gordon equation which includes Coulomb field and pion absorption. Flow modifies the radial distribution of the source function and rescales the pion wave functions. The radii extracted from the correlation functions are calculated in sideward and outward direction as a function of the pair momentum. Comparison with recent measurements at SIS and AGS energies is made.

# Combined effects of nuclear Coulomb field, radial flow, and opaqueness on two-pion correlations

## Key claim

The observed Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (HBT) radii extracted from two-pion correlation functions are modified by the combined and largely independent effects of the nuclear Coulomb field, radial flow, and source opaqueness; the Coulomb field increases sideward radii for negatively charged pions and decreases them for positively charged pions, while opaqueness can make the difference between outward and sideward radii negative, potentially compensating the positive contribution from finite emission time.

## Method

The author solves the Klein-Gordon equation for pions including a Coulomb potential from a Gaussian source and an imaginary absorptive potential parameterized by the pion mean free path, then computes the two-pion correlation function from a covariant formalism using a source function with a relativistic Boltzmann distribution and a radial flow velocity field. The correlation function is fitted to a Gaussian form in sideward and outward relative momentum components to extract the HBT radii \(R_{side}\) and \(R_{out}\) as functions of the average pair momentum. The distorted pion wave functions are expanded in partial waves and integrated along a complex rotation path to incorporate flow.

## Key numbers

- Source temperature \(T = 80\) MeV and flow velocity \(\langle \beta \rangle = 0.32\) used for Au+Au at 1 GeV per nucleon.
- Central charge \(Z = 120\) for nearly central Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV per nucleon; effective charge reduced to \(Z_{eff} = 60\) in comparison to data.
- Source hard-sphere radius \(R_h = 10\) fm (corresponding to Gaussian parameter \(R_0 = R_h/\sqrt{5}\)) and emission time \(\tau = 4\) fm/c used in calculations.
- Mean free paths at normal nuclear matter density: \(\lambda^0_{\pi^+} = 1.05\) fm, \(\lambda^0_{\pi^-} = 0.85\) fm.
- Ratio of measured sideward radii at AGS: \(R_{side}(\pi^-\pi^-) / R_{side}(\pi^+\pi^+) = (5.6 \pm 0.7) / (3.9 \pm 0.8) = 1.4 \pm 0.3\).
- Ratio of measured outward radii at AGS: \(R_{out}(\pi^-\pi^-) / R_{out}(\pi^+\pi^+) = (5.8 \pm 0.5) / (6.5 \pm 0.5) = 0.9 \pm 0.15\).
- Pion momenta below 300 MeV/c show significant Coulomb-induced changes; stationary approach not justified below 100 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Coulomb effects on HBT radii by Barz (1997) and Baym and collaborators, and incorporates the opaque-source concept of Heiselberg (1995) into a unified framework with radial flow. The model is compared to measurements at SIS energies by Pelte et al. (1997) and at AGS energies by Barette et al. (1997). The results enable more realistic extraction of source sizes from pion interferometry in heavy-ion collisions, but the author notes that a full dynamical treatment of the collision time evolution is needed for quantitative fits.
