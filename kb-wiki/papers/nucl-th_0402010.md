# Weak non-mesonic decay of Hypernuclei

> We review the mechanism of weak decay of hypernuclei, with emphasis on the non-mesonic decay channels. Various theoretical approaches are discussed and the results are compared with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the weak decay of hypernuclei, arguing that while theoretical models (particularly those using meson-exchange potentials) can reproduce the total non-mesonic decay widths, they systematically underestimate the experimentally measured ratio of neutron-induced to proton-induced decay widths (\(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\)), with the discrepancy remaining unresolved.

## Method

The author surveys two main theoretical frameworks for calculating hypernuclear decay widths: the wave function method, which uses explicit single-particle wave functions for the hyperon, nucleon, and pion; and the polarization propagator method, which evaluates the \(\Lambda\) self-energy in nuclear matter and separates the decay into contributions from particle-hole (\(\Gamma_1\)), \(\Delta\)-hole (mesonic), and two-particle–two-hole (\(\Gamma_2\)) excitations. The non-mesonic transition \(\Lambda N \to NN\) is modeled via one-meson exchange (pion, rho, kaon, etc.) with short-range correlations parameterized by Landau–Migdal parameters \(g'\) and \(g'_\Lambda\). The local density approximation is used to connect infinite nuclear-matter results to finite hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

* Free \(\Lambda\) decay branching ratios: \(\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\pi^-}/\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\Lambda}=0.639\) and \(\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\pi^0}/\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\Lambda}=0.358\); the experimental ratio \(\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\pi^-}/\Gamma^{\rm free}_{\pi^0}\simeq 1.78\) supports the \(\Delta I=1/2\) rule (which would predict 2.0).
* For the one-body induced non-mesonic decay (\(\Lambda N \to NN\)), the emitted nucleon momentum is \(p_N\simeq 420\) MeV/c; for the two-body induced decay (\(\Lambda NN \to NNN\)), \(p_N\simeq 340\) MeV/c.
* The weak coupling constant is \(G=2.211\times 10^{-7}/m_\pi^2\), with parity-violating amplitude \(A=1.06\) and parity-conserving amplitude \(B=-7.10\).
* The phenomenological 2p-2h polarization propagator at pion threshold uses \(C^*_0=(0.105+i0.096)/m_\pi^6\).
* The Landau parameter \(g'\) is set to 0.8 and \(g'_\Lambda\simeq 0.4\) to fit hypernuclear decay data, compared to the typical range \(g'=0.6\div 0.7\) from other nuclear responses.
* The theoretical \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) ratio in the one-pion-exchange (OPE) approximation is \(0.05\div 0.20\), while the experimental central values lie in the range \(0.5\lsim \Gamma_n/\Gamma_p \lsim 2\).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on hypernuclear weak decay, notably the reviews by Alberico and Garbarino (cited as ref. AlbGar) and the calculations by Nieves (Nieves), Motoba (Motoba), and Parreno (Parreno) for the wave function method, and by Oset and collaborators (OsSa, RaOsSa, ADEM, ADGR, ADGC) for the polarization propagator method. The paper highlights the persistent “\(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) puzzle,” where OPE-based models give ratios far below experiment, and discusses possible resolutions including heavy-meson exchange, direct quark contributions, two-nucleon induced processes, and final-state interactions. It enables future experimental work, particularly coincidence measurements of \(nn\) and \(np\) pairs and angular correlations from the FINUDA experiment (Bressani), to disentangle one- and two-nucleon induced decay channels.
