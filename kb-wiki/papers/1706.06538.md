# New cluster approach on properties of 8-11Be isotopes with isospin-dependent spin-orbit potential

> The nonlocalized clustering approach is generalized to 8-11Be isotopes with isospin dependent spin-orbit potential. A new form of the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function is introduced to provide a correct description for the σ-binding neutron in 11Be. Systematic calculations for 8-11Be isotopes are performed and results fit well with experimental values. The low energy spectrum of 11Be is also obtained, especially the correct spin-parity 1/2+ is reproduced for the intruder ground state. The exotic neutron halo structure of 11Be is studied by calculations of root-mean-square radii and density distribution. We obtain a large spatial distribution for the last valence neutron of 11Be, which fits the phenomenological extracted value from experimental data. The spectroscopic factor is also calculated and discussed for the 1/2+ ground state of 11Be.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new form of the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function with an isospin-dependent spin-orbit interaction strength can correctly reproduce the intruder ground state spin-parity \(1/2^{+}\) and the neutron halo structure of \(^{11}\)Be, but the calculated low-energy spectrum remains "quite a bit too diluted" compared with experiment, suggesting that explicit account of low-lying collective states of the \(^{10}\)Be core is needed.

## Method

The authors generalize the nonlocalized clustering approach to \(^{8-11}\)Be isotopes by introducing a new THSR wave function with a creation operator for the last valence neutron that includes an extra factor to describe the nodal structure of the \(\sigma\)-orbit wave function. They use Volkov No. 2 as the central nucleon-nucleon potential and the G3RS term for the two-body spin-orbit interaction, with an isospin-dependent spin-orbit strength \(V^{ls}_0 = 2400 + 200 \times |N-Z|\) MeV. Systematic calculations of binding energies, one-neutron separation energies, root-mean-square radii, density distributions, and spectroscopic factors are performed and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of \(^{11}\)Be: \(-64.6\) MeV (THSR) vs. \(-65.5\) MeV (experimental).
- Ground state binding energy of \(^{10}\)Be: \(-63.9\) MeV (THSR) vs. \(-65.0\) MeV (experimental).
- Ground state binding energy of \(^{9}\)Be: \(-56.5\) MeV (THSR) vs. \(-58.2\) MeV (experimental).
- Ground state binding energy of \(^{8}\)Be: \(-55.9\) MeV (THSR) vs. \(-56.5\) MeV (experimental).
- Spin-orbit interaction strength \(V^{ls}_0\) used: 2400 MeV for \(^{8}\)Be, 2600 MeV for \(^{9}\)Be, 2800 MeV for \(^{10}\)Be, and 3000 MeV for \(^{11}\)Be.
- Root-mean-square (RMS) radius of the last valence neutron in \(^{11}\)Be: 8.94 fm (THSR) vs. 7.98 fm (phenomenological extraction from experiment).
- Matter RMS radius of \(^{11}\)Be: 3.73 fm (THSR) vs. 2.91 fm (experimental).
- Proton RMS radius of \(^{11}\)Be: 2.42 fm (THSR) vs. 2.36 fm (experimental).
- Neutron RMS radius of \(^{11}\)Be: 4.25 fm (THSR) vs. 3.09 fm (experimental).
- Spectroscopic factor \(S_{2s}\) for the \(1/2^{+}\) ground state of \(^{11}\)Be: 0.907 (with \(V^{ls*}=3000\) MeV) and 0.933 (with \(V^{ls}=2000\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the nonlocalized clustering approach previously applied to \(^{9-10}\)Be isotopes by Lyu et al. (2015, 2016) to include \(^{8}\)Be and \(^{11}\)Be, introducing an isospin-dependent spin-orbit interaction. It reproduces the intruder ground state parity inversion in \(^{11}\)Be, a feature that has been addressed by shell model calculations (Otsuka 1993, Brown 2001) and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Enyo 2002), but the calculated spectrum remains more diluted than experiment, similar to the particle-vibration coupling model of Vinh Mau (1995). The results enable further application of the THSR approach to neutron-rich nuclei beyond the \(\beta\)-stable line and provide insights into the halo structure and spectroscopic factors of \(^{11}\)Be.
