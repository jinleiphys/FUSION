# Charged charm stars

> The study of the general properties and stability of charm stars with a net electric charge is performed within the MIT bag model framework. We consider two different models for the electric charge distribution and demostrate that both imply stellar configurations with larger masses and that satisfy the equilibrium condition. The dynamical stability against radial oscillations is investigated. Our results demonstrate that the eigenfrequencies are modified by the presence of a net electric charge, but the instability, previously demonstrated for the electrically neutral case, is also present in charged charm stars.

## Key claim

Charm stars with a net electric charge satisfy the hydrostatic equilibrium condition (∂M/∂εc > 0) and have larger masses and radii than neutral charm stars, but they remain unstable against radial oscillations regardless of the charge distribution or magnitude, confirming that the instability previously demonstrated for electrically neutral charm stars persists when electric charge is included.

## Method

The authors solve the Einstein-Maxwell field equations for a static, spherically symmetric charged fluid using the MIT bag model equation of state, which treats quark matter as a relativistic degenerate ideal Fermi gas of up, down, strange, and charm quarks plus electrons and muons, with a bag pressure accounting for confinement. Two electric charge distribution models are considered: one proportional to the cube of the radial coordinate (q(r) = βr³, with β ≡ Q/R³) and one where the charge density is proportional to the energy density (ρe = αε). Dynamical stability is assessed by solving the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem for radial oscillations and examining the sign of the fundamental eigenfrequency squared (ω₀²).

## Key numbers

- Charm quark production begins at a threshold central energy density of **153.7 GeV·fm⁻³**.
- For electrically neutral charm stars, the possible configurations have a mass of approximately **1.2 M⊙** and a radius of about **8 km**.
- For the β-distribution (q(r) = βr³), the presence of charge increases both mass and radius relative to the neutral case, with equilibrium configurations satisfying ∂M/∂εc > 0 for all β values considered.
- For the α-distribution (ρe = αε), equilibrium configurations exist for all α values considered, with masses and radii increasing with α.
- The fundamental eigenfrequency squared (ω₀²) is negative for all energy densities associated with charm stars (εc ≥ 153.7 GeV·fm⁻³) for both charge distributions, indicating instability.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electrically neutral charm stars by Kettner et al. (1995) in the MIT bag model and by Jiménez and Fraga (2019) using perturbative QCD, which both found neutral charm stars to be unstable. It also builds on investigations of charged strange stars by Negreiros et al. (2009), Arbanil et al. (2015), and Lazzari et al. (2020), which showed that electric charge modifies stellar properties and stability. The results rule out the possibility of stable charged charm stars in Nature, but leave open the question of whether charm stars could exist transiently, for example after neutron star or strange star mergers.
