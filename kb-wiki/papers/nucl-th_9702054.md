# The Origin of the Wigner Energy

> Surfaces of experimental masses of even-even and odd-odd nuclei exhibit a sharp slope discontinuity at N=Z. This cusp (Wigner energy), reflecting an additional binding in nuclei with neutrons and protons occupying the same shell model orbitals, is usually attributed to neutron-proton pairing correlations. A method is developed to extract the Wigner term from experimental data. Both empirical arguments and shell-model calculations suggest that the Wigner term can be traced back to the isospin T=0 part of nuclear interaction. Our calculations reveal the rather complex mechanism responsible for the nuclear binding around the N=Z line. In particular, we find that the Wigner term cannot be solely explained in terms of correlations between the neutron-proton J=1, T=0 (deuteron-like) pairs.

## Key claim

The Wigner energy (the cusp in nuclear masses at N=Z) originates primarily from the isospin T=0 part of the nuclear interaction, but it cannot be explained solely by deuteron-like (J=1, T=0) neutron-proton pairs; instead, it arises from a complex combination of T=0 pairs with various angular momenta, including stretched (high-J) configurations.

## Method

The authors extracted the Wigner energy coefficients W(A) and d(A) from experimental nuclear binding energies using third-order mass difference indicators (double-difference formulas) that filter out the smooth symmetry energy background. They performed shell-model calculations in the sd-shell (using the USD interaction) and fp-shell (using the KB3 interaction) for even-even N=Z nuclei, systematically switching off T=0 two-body matrix elements for different values of total angular momentum J to isolate the contributions of various neutron-proton pair types.

## Key numbers

- The extracted Wigner coefficient follows W(A) = a_W / A^α with α ≈ 0.95 and a_W ≈ 47 MeV, considerably larger than the 30 MeV used in the semi-empirical mass formula of Krappe et al. (1979) but consistent with the value a_W = 43 ± 5 MeV from Jensen et al. (1984).
- For N=Z odd-odd nuclei with T=0 ground states, the ratio d_{T=0}(A)/W(A) ≈ 1, in agreement with the simple estimate of Myers (1977).
- The residual neutron-proton interaction ε_np(A) in odd-odd nuclei near the N=Z line (|N-Z| ≤ 3) is approximately 500 keV, about 2-3 times smaller than the 20-40 MeV/A estimates from Krappe et al. (1979) and Jensen et al. (1984); for heavier nuclei with larger neutron excess it approaches ~300 keV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Wigner energy from earlier macroscopic-microscopic fits (Myers 1966, Krappe 1979) and mass formula studies (Jensen 1984) by developing a direct extraction method from experimental data. The shell-model calculations confirm the earlier suggestion by Brenner et al. (1990) that the T=0 part of the interaction causes the cusp in δV_np, but reveal that the mechanism is more complex than simple deuteron-like pairing. The results challenge models that consider only J=0, T=1 and J=1, T=0 neutron-proton pairs (e.g., Evans et al. 1981) and motivate the development of generalized quasiparticle theories that include high-J T=0 pairs, such as the extension of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method to neutron-proton pairing by Perera et al. (1996).
