# Knots and Particles

> Using methods of high performance computing, we have found indications that knotlike structures appear as stable finite energy solitons in a realistic 3+1 dimensional model. We have explicitly simulated the unknot and trefoil configurations, and our results suggest that all torus knots appear as solitons. Our observations open new theoretical possibilities in scenarios where stringlike structures appear, including physics of fundamental interactions and early universe cosmology. In nematic liquid crystals and 3He superfluids such knotted solitons might actually be observed.

## Key claim

Using high-performance computing, the authors found strong numerical evidence that stable, finite-energy knotlike solitons exist in a 3+1 dimensional model based on a unit three-component vector field, but the results for the trefoil knot remain tentative due to limited computational resources.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the parabolic gradient-flow equation derived from the Hamiltonian of a nonlinear O(3) sigma model with an additional fourth-order term, using the PDE2D finite element algorithm. They simulated the unknot (Hopf invariant Q_H = ±1) on a 9600 triangle mesh with axial symmetry, and the trefoil knot (Q_H = ±3) on a 21^3 cubic finite element lattice with tri-cubic Hermite basis functions, evolving initial knot configurations toward stable stationary solutions.

## Key numbers

- Unknot vortex simulation required over 50 hours of CPU time, with each time step taking about 9 minutes on a 9600 triangle mesh.
- During the unknot simulation, the time step could be increased by up to 8 orders of magnitude while keeping the relative change in energy intact.
- The numerically computed Hopf invariant for the final unknot configuration reached a slight local maximum with Q_H = 0.999996.
- Trefoil vortex simulation required almost 200 hours of CPU time on a 21^3 cubic finite element lattice, with each time iteration taking about 20 minutes.

## Context

This work extends the model proposed by Faddeev (1975) for constructing closed knotted vortices, which had resisted solution despite numerous attempts. The results suggest that all torus knots appear as solitons in this model, opening possibilities for experimental investigation in nematic liquid crystals and ³He superfluids, as well as theoretical applications in spontaneously broken Yang-Mills-Higgs theories and early universe cosmology.
