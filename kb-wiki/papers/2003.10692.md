# Propagation of non-linear waves in hot, ideal, and non-extensive quark-gluon plasma

> We study the propagation of energy density perturbation in a hot, ideal quark-gluon medium in which quarks and gluons follow the Tsallis-like momentum distributions. We have observed that a non-extensive MIT bag equation of state obtained with the help of the quantum Tsallis-like distributions gives rise to a breaking wave solution of the equation dictating the evolution of energy density perturbation. However, the breaking of waves is delayed when the value of the Tsallis q parameter and the Tsallis temperature T are higher.

## Key claim
The paper claims that using a non-extensive (Tsallis) MIT bag equation of state for hot, ideal quark-gluon plasma leads to a breaking wave solution for the first-order energy density perturbation, but the breaking is delayed when the Tsallis q parameter and the Tsallis temperature T are higher. This result is conditional on the use of an approximated version of the quantum Tsallis distribution (zeroth-order term in the factorization approximation).

## Method
The authors derived closed analytic expressions for thermodynamic variables (energy density, pressure, entropy density) of a non-extensive gas of massless bosons and massive fermions using quantum Tsallis distributions. They then combined the relativistic Euler equation with the entropy continuity equation, applied the reductive perturbation theory (RPT) with stretched coordinates, and obtained an inviscid Burgers equation for the first-order energy density perturbation. The equation was solved analytically for a linear initial condition using Chandrasekhar's solution and for a sech-squared initial condition using the method of characteristics.

## Key numbers
- The breaking wave solution becomes multiple-valued at around t = 10 fm for parameters A=2.5, B=0.5 fm, q=1.08, and T=140 MeV.
- For initial wave amplitudes beyond A=1 (at t=15 fm, B=0.5 fm, q=1.08, T=140 MeV), solutions become multiple-valued, indicating wave breaking.
- The reference energy density used is epsilon_0 = 0.16 GeV fm^{-3}.
- The quark mass used is m = 10 MeV, of the order of the down quark mass.
- For the Boltzmann-Gibbs limit (q=1), the coefficient reduces to the standard result with epsilon_{bag,1} = 37 pi^2 / 30 for massless fermions and bosons.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nonlinear wave propagation in QGP that used the standard MIT bag equation of state with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (Raha et al. 1991, nlwprc). Those earlier calculations did not account for the fluctuating environment that arises in high-energy nuclear collisions. The Tsallis non-extensive distributions used here are motivated by experimental observations that particle spectra from p-p and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC and RHIC are describable by Tsallis-like power law distributions (CMS, ALICE collaborations, 2010-2015; Biro 2009; Cleymans 2013). The results enable future studies of energy density perturbation evolution in fluctuating QGP environments and provide closed-form thermodynamic expressions applicable to other studies of quantum Tsallis gases.
