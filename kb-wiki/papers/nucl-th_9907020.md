# Photoproduction of Hybrid Mesons

> In this contribution I discuss prospects for photoproducing hybrid mesons at CEBAF, based on recent model results and experimental indications of possible hybrids. One excellent opportunity appears to be a search for the I=1, JPC=2+-, neutral "(b2)o" hybrid in (a2 pi)o through diffractive photoproduction. Other notable possibilities accessible through pi+ or pio exchange photoproduction are I=1, JPC=1-+, charged "pi1+" in f1 pi+, (b1 pi)+ and (rho pi)+; piJ(1770)+ in f2 pi+ and (b1 pi)+; pi(1800)+ in f0 pi+, f2 pi+, omega rho+ and (rho pi)+; a1 in f1 pi+ and f2 pi+; and omega in (rho pi)o, omega eta and (K1 K)o.

# Photoproduction of Hybrid Mesons

## Key claim

Diffractive and pion-exchange photoproduction at CEBAF offers an excellent opportunity to search for hybrid mesons, particularly the neutral I=1, JPC=2+- "b2o" hybrid in the (a2 pi)o channel, but the theoretical flux-tube model predictions for hybrid masses (around 1.9-2.0 GeV) are in striking disagreement with experimental candidates such as the pi1(1400) at 1.4 GeV, so the existence of the light exotics must first be confirmed or refuted.

## Method

The author lists accessible quantum numbers for hybrid mesons in photoproduction based on three mechanisms: diffractive vector-nucleon scattering (vector dominance followed by pomeron exchange), t-channel meson exchange (with pion giving the leading contribution at small t), and baryon resonance decay treated as background. The flux-tube model is used as a guide to predict masses, widths, and dominant decay modes for specific hybrid candidates, and these predictions are compared with experimental results from VES, E852, and Crystal Barrel.

## Key numbers

- The flux-tube model predicts the lightest hybrid meson multiplet mass at 1.9 GeV, with recent LGT studies finding the lightest exotic n nbar-hybrid (1-+) at about 2.0 GeV
- The I=1 1-+ exotic pi1(1400) has M=1.4 GeV and Gamma_tot=0.4 GeV, about 0.5 GeV below theoretical expectations
- A second I=1 1-+ exotic found by E852 near 1.6 GeV in rho pi has a width slightly below 0.2 GeV
- The b2 hybrid is predicted to have a width of 0.4 GeV with a 50% branch to a2 pi
- The s sbar 2+- exotic is predicted to have a width of 0.2 GeV
- The 2+- exotic from neutral pion exchange is predicted to have Gamma_tot=0.3 GeV, decaying mainly to b1 pi
- An a1-type hybrid is expected with Gamma_tot=0.5 GeV in f2 pi+ and f1 pi+
- The narrowest hybrid predicted by the flux-tube model is an omega state with Gamma_tot=0.1 GeV, decaying into K1K and omega eta

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of hybrid meson photoproduction by applying the flux-tube model of Isgur, Kokoski, and Paton (1985) and Close and Page (1995) to propose specific search channels at CEBAF. It directly addresses the disagreement between theoretical predictions (including LGT studies by the MILC Collaboration and UKQCD Collaboration) and experimental results from VES, E852, and Crystal Barrel, which have reported exotic candidates at masses significantly lower than predicted. The paper enables future experimental searches at CEBAF by identifying the most promising channels and decay modes, while cautioning that the flux-tube model may be inaccurate and that broad hybrids might lie far from quenched theoretical predictions due to mass shifts from coupling to meson continua.
