# Nuclear Schiff moment and soft vibrational modes

> The atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) currently searched by a number of experimental groups requires that both parity and time-reversal invariance be violated. According to current theoretical understanding, the EDM is induced by the nuclear Schiff moment. The enhancement of the Schiff moment by the combination of static quadrupole and octupole deformation was predicted earlier. Here we study a further idea of the possible enhancement in the absence of static deformation but in a nuclear system with soft collective vibrations of two types. Both analytical approximation and numerical solution of the simplified problem confirm the presence of the enhancement. We discuss related aspects of nuclear structure which should be studied beyond mean-field and random phase approximations.

# Nuclear Schiff moment and soft vibrational modes

## Key claim

The enhancement of the nuclear Schiff moment, and therefore the atomic electric dipole moment, can occur even in the absence of static quadrupole or octupole deformation, provided the nucleus possesses soft collective quadrupole and octupole vibrational modes whose interaction creates a coherent phonon condensate. The authors acknowledge that a previous microscopic calculation based on random phase approximation (RPA) gave essentially negative results, finding enhancement only in the unphysical limit of very low vibrational frequencies, but they argue that RPA is inappropriate for this situation of strong mode-mode coupling.

## Method

The authors construct a simplified model of an odd-A nucleus where an unpaired particle interacts with two coupled soft collective modes (quadrupole 2+ and octupole 3-) of a spherical even-even core. They develop both an analytical approximation using bosonic phonon operators with a mode-mode interaction term, and an exact numerical diagonalization of a two-level model with j=j'=17/2 for 6 and 7 particles, using a Hamiltonian that includes pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and octupole-octupole interactions. The Schiff moment operator is evaluated in the ground state of the odd system, with the weak interaction treated as a perturbation to mix opposite-parity states.

## Key numbers

- The minimum energy splitting between parity doublets (17/2+, 17/2-) in the 7-particle system is 0.15 at equal quadrupole and octupole strength parameters (varkappa_2 = varkappa_3 = 1), which is smaller than the single-particle spacing epsilon = 0.2.
- The effective quadrupole deformation parameter delta_2 is given by delta_2 = -sqrt(4/21) * x / omega_2, where x is the mode-mode coupling constant and omega_2 is the quadrupole phonon frequency.
- The octupole phonon excitation energy E_3 is shifted from its unperturbed value omega_3 by E_3 = omega_3 - (8/21) * x^2 / omega_2.
- In the degenerate limit (epsilon = epsilon'), the single-particle contribution of the weak interaction reaches a maximum value of |w|/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions that static quadrupole and octupole deformation enhance the Schiff moment by 2-3 orders of magnitude (Auerbach et al., 1996; Spevak et al., 1997; Engel et al., 2003), and the subsequent relaxation to static quadrupole deformation combined with a soft octupole vibrational mode (Engel et al., 2000; Zelevinsky and Flambaum, 1995). The authors directly address the negative result of a detailed RPA-based calculation (Auerbach et al., 2006) which found enhancement only in the unphysical limit of very low frequencies, arguing that RPA cannot treat the strong mode-mode coupling that creates a coherent phonon condensate. The work opens the possibility of searching for Schiff moment enhancement in nuclei such as light isotopes of radium and radon, where both soft quadrupole and octupole modes with strong dipole transitions are known experimentally (Cocks et al., 1997).
