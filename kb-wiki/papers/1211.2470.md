# BCS-BEC quantum phase transition and collective excitations in two-dimensional Fermi gases with p- and d-wave pairings

> It is generally believed that the BCS-BEC evolution in fermionic systems with s-wave pairing is a smooth crossover. However, for nonzero orbital-angular-momentum pairing such as p- or d-wave pairing, the system undergoes a quantum phase transition at the point where the chemical potential $μ$ vanishes. In this paper, we study the BCS-BEC quantum phase transition and the collective excitations associated with the order-parameter fluctuations in two-dimensional fermionic systems with p- and d-wave pairings. We show that the quantum phase transition in such systems can be generically traced back to the infrared behavior of the fermionic excitation at $μ=0$: $E_{\bf k}\sim k^l$, where $l=1,2$ is the quantum number of the orbital angular momentum. The nonanalyticity of the thermodynamic quantities is due to the infrared divergence caused by the fermionic excitation at $μ=0$. As a result, the evolution of the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode is not smooth: Its velocity is nonanalytical across the quantum phase transition.

## Key claim

The BCS-BEC evolution in two-dimensional Fermi gases with p- or d-wave pairing is not a smooth crossover but instead exhibits a quantum phase transition at the point where the chemical potential μ vanishes, driven by infrared divergences in momentum integrals that depend only on the orbital angular momentum quantum number l, not on the specific order-parameter symmetry.

## Method

The authors used a functional path integral formalism with a separable interaction potential for p-wave (spinless fermions) and d-wave (spin-1/2 fermions) pairings in two dimensions. They evaluated the mean-field thermodynamic potential and its derivatives to identify nonanalytic behavior at μ=0, then expanded the effective action to Gaussian order around the saddle point to compute the dispersion of the Anderson-Bogoliubov collective mode.

## Key numbers

- For p-wave pairing (l=1), the single-particle excitation spectrum at μ=0 behaves as E_k ~ k^1 in the infrared limit k→0.
- For d-wave pairing (l=2), the single-particle excitation spectrum at μ=0 behaves as E_k ~ k^2 in the infrared limit k→0.
- The momentum integral I_1(0) ~ ∫₀^ε dk/k² for p-wave pairing is infrared divergent, causing ∂²α(μ)/∂μ² to diverge at μ=0.
- The momentum integral I_2(0) ~ ∫₀^ε dk/k for d-wave pairing is infrared divergent, causing α(μ) itself to diverge at μ=0.
- The sound velocity of the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode is given by c_s = √(n/(Mα)), where n is the density and α = -∂²Ω/∂μ², and therefore behaves nonanalytically across the quantum phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies by Botelho and Sa de Melo (2010) who first identified nonanalyticities in thermodynamic quantities at μ=0 for 2D p- and d-wave superfluids using Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink type potentials. The present paper shows these nonanalyticities are generic, arising solely from the infrared behavior of the pairing interaction (Δ(k) ~ k^l) and independent of the order-parameter symmetry or interaction details. The results enable future studies of the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode velocity as an experimental probe of the BCS-BEC quantum phase transition in ultracold Fermi gases with nonzero orbital-angular-momentum pairing.
