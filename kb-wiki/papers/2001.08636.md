# Exploring Longitudinal Observables with 3+1D IP-Glasma

> We present a formulation of the initial state of heavy ion collisions that generalizes the 2+1D boost invariant IP-Glasma \cite{Schenke:2012wb} to 3+1D through JIMWLK rapidity evolution of the pre-collision Wilson lines. The rapidity dependence introduced by the JIMWLK evolution leads us to modify the initial condition for the gauge fields, and to solve Gauss' law iteratively in order to allow for temporal evolution on a 3-dimensional lattice. While the transverse physics of QGP has been studied nearly exhaustively, the effect of longitudinal fluctuations introduced by the JIMWLK evolution has yet to be studied in detail phenomenologically. Hence, we couple our 3+1D IP-Glasma model to MUSIC+UrQMD, for completely 3+1D simulations of heavy ion collisions. Specifically, we consider Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76\, {\rm TeV}$ and study the rapidity dependence of the charged hadron $v_n(η)$ via the $η$-dependent flow factorization ratios $r_n(η_a,η_b)$ as measured by CMS \cite{Khachatryan:2015oea}, as well as the charged hadron multiplicity $dN_{ch}/dη$.

## Key claim
The paper presents a 3+1D generalization of the IP-Glasma model that incorporates longitudinal fluctuations via JIMWLK rapidity evolution, but the model still under-predicts the decorrelation in the flow factorization ratios \(r_n(\eta_a,\eta_b)\) compared to CMS data, indicating that additional sources of decorrelation (such as thermal fluctuations or minijets) are needed.

## Method
The authors generalize the 2+1D boost-invariant IP-Glasma model to 3+1D by evolving pre-collision Wilson lines in rapidity using the JIMWLK Langevin formulation. They modify the initial gauge field condition from pure gauge in transverse directions to pure gauge in all three spatial dimensions and solve Gauss' law iteratively via a covariant Poisson equation to allow temporal evolution on a 3-dimensional lattice. The initial state is matched to the 3+1D MUSIC hydrodynamic code at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm with a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity \(\zeta/s(T)\) and \(\eta/s = 0.10\), evolved to a switching temperature of \(T_{sw} = 145\) MeV, and then coupled to UrQMD for the hadronic gas phase.

## Key numbers
- Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV are simulated.
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm.
- The switching temperature for hadronization is \(T_{sw} = 145\) MeV.
- The specific shear viscosity used is \(\eta/s = 0.10\).
- The rapidity profile smoothly brings energy density to zero for \(|\eta| > 2.5\) using a Gaussian exponential factor \(\exp[-(|\eta|-2.5)^2/2]\).
- The model achieves good agreement with ALICE data for charged hadron multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) across three centrality classes.
- The model agrees well with CMS data for \(v_2(\eta)\) across eight centrality classes.

## Context
This work extends the 2+1D boost-invariant IP-Glasma model of Schenke (2012) to 3+1D by incorporating JIMWLK rapidity evolution, following the numerical formulation of Lappi (2012) and the initial condition modifications of McDonald (2018). It enables the first phenomenological study of longitudinal fluctuations from JIMWLK evolution on final-state observables such as \(v_2(\eta)\) and the flow factorization ratios \(r_n(\eta_a,\eta_b)\) measured by CMS (2015). The results show that initial-state decorrelation of eccentricities \(\varepsilon_n(\eta)\) leads to larger final-state decorrelation in \(v_n\), but the model under-predicts the CMS data, leaving room for additional decorrelation sources such as thermal fluctuations in hydrodynamics or minijets.
