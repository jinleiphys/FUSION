# Phonons and gluons in the crystalline color superconducting phase of QCD

> The High Density Effective Theory formalism is used to calculate the low energy properties of the phonons and gluons in the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase of two-flavor QCD. The effective phonon Lagrangian for the cubic crystal structure, which is favored according to a recent study, depends, at the second order in the derivatives, on three parameters which we calculate in this paper. We also compute for soft momenta the effective lagrangian for the gluons of the unbroken SU(2)_c group, both for a single plane wave structure and for the cube. In both cases the Meissner gluon mass vanishes as in the case of complete isotropy; on the other hand there is a partial Debye screening due to the existence of blocking regions on the Fermi spheres. For the single plane wave structure the gluon dielectric tensor is non isotropic, while it is isotropic for the cubic crystal, in spite of the intrinsic anisotropy of the structure.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the low-energy effective Lagrangians for phonons and gluons in the crystalline color superconducting (LOFF) phase of two-flavor QCD, specifically for the face-centered cubic crystal structure that a prior study by Bowers and Rajagopal (2002) found to be favored. The authors compute the three parameters of the effective phonon Lagrangian at second order in derivatives and show that, for the cubic crystal, the gluon dielectric tensor becomes isotropic despite the intrinsic anisotropy of the structure, while the Meissner gluon mass vanishes as in the completely isotropic case.

## Method

The authors use the High Density Effective Theory (HDET) formalism, which is based on the limit of large quark chemical potential μ and employs velocity-dependent fields analogous to Heavy Quark Effective Theory. They introduce the LOFF condensate via a gap term in the Lagrangian, first for a single plane-wave ansatz and then for the face-centered cubic structure built from eight plane waves directed to the corners of a cube. The effective phonon Lagrangian is derived by integrating out the gapped quark degrees of freedom, computing self-energy and tadpole diagrams at second order in the momentum expansion, while the gluon effective Lagrangian is computed for soft momenta for both the single plane-wave and cubic structures.

## Key numbers

- The cubic crystal structure is built from eight plane waves with wave vectors of equal length q = π/a, where a is the lattice spacing.
- For a representative choice of parameters (μ = 400 MeV, δμ = 40 MeV, q/|δμ| ≈ 1.2), the parameter R characterizing the pairing region width is approximately R ≈ 18.
- The effective phonon Lagrangian at second order in derivatives depends on three parameters (a, b, c) as a consequence of the cubic symmetry and the general theory of elastic wave propagation in crystals.

## Context

This work extends the study of crystalline color superconductivity in QCD, which was initially proposed by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1965) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) in condensed matter physics and later applied to QCD by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2001). The paper builds on the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of Bowers and Rajagopal (2002), who found that a face-centered cubic crystal is favored over the single plane-wave structure, and on the earlier effective phonon Lagrangian calculation for the single plane-wave case by Casalbuoni et al. (2002). The results enable further study of the transport and mechanical properties of crystalline color superconducting matter, which may be relevant for the physics of compact stars, particularly the possible pinning of rotational vortices and the formation of glitches.
