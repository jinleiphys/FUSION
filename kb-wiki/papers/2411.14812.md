# Lattice gradient flows (de-)stabilizing topological sectors

> We investigate the stability of topological charge under gradient flow taking the admissibility condition into account. For the $SU(2)$ Wilson gauge theory with $β=2.45$ and $L^4=12^4$, we numerically show that the gradient flows with the Iwasaki and DBW2 gauge actions stabilize the topological sectors significantly, and they have qualitatively different behaviors compared with the Wilson and tree-level Symanzik flows. By considering the classical continuum limit of the flow actions, we discuss that the coefficient of dimension-$6$ operators has to be positive for stabilizing the one-instanton configuration, and the Iwasaki and DBW2 actions satisfy this criterion while the Wilson and Symanzik actions do not. Moreover, we observe that the DBW2 flow stabilizes the topological sectors at the very early stage of the flow ($\hat{t}\approx 0.5$--$1$), suggesting that a further systematic investigation of the DBW2 flow is warranted to confirm its computational efficiency in determining the gauge topology.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the stability of topological charge under lattice gradient flow depends on the sign of the coefficient of dimension-6 operators in the flow action: Iwasaki and DBW2 flows (with negative coefficient) stabilize topological sectors for long flow times, while Wilson and Symanzik flows (with non-negative coefficient) cause topological charge to jump, even for gauge configurations from Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of SU(2) pure Yang-Mills theory with the Wilson plaquette action at beta = 2.45 on a 12^4 lattice, generating 200 configurations. They applied four different gradient flows (Wilson, tree-level Symanzik, Iwasaki, and DBW2) using the third-order Runge-Kutta method with step size epsilon = 0.01, measuring the topological charge via an O(a^2)-improved clover-leaf operator and monitoring the admissibility condition through the maximal plaquette deviation P_adm. They also tested the flows on constructed one-instanton configurations of varying radii R = rho/a on the same lattice size.

## Key numbers

* For the one-instanton configuration with radius R = 3.0 on a 12^4 lattice, the Wilson flow shows the topological charge jumping from 1 to 0 at approximately hat-t = 18.5, while the Symanzik flow shows the jump at approximately hat-t = 31.
* For the one-instanton configuration, the DBW2 flow detects nonzero topological charge for instantons as small as R approximately 1.5, while other flows fail for R = 1.3.
* For Monte Carlo configurations at beta = 2.45 on a 12^4 lattice, the Iwasaki flow shows no topological sector jumps after hat-t approximately 6, and the DBW2 flow shows no jumps after hat-t approximately 1, up to the maximum flow time hat-t = 50.
* The admissibility measure P_adm for the DBW2 flow on the one-instanton with R = 3.0 falls below Luscher's bound of 0.015 around hat-t approximately 0.5 and stays approximately constant below 0.01.
* The lattice spacing corresponds to a approximately (2.1 GeV)^{-1} approximately 0.093 fm, giving a physical lattice size of L a approximately 1.1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of instanton stability under gradient flow from Garcia-Perez et al. (1993) and the earlier numerical investigations by Iwasaki (1983) to actual Monte Carlo gauge configurations. The study compares four flow actions (Wilson, tree-level Symanzik, Iwasaki, and DBW2) that are well-established as improved gauge actions in lattice QCD, with the Iwasaki and DBW2 actions originating from renormalization-group improved actions developed by Iwasaki (1983) and the QCD-TARO collaboration (1999). The results suggest that DBW2 flow may provide a computationally efficient method for determining gauge topology in lattice simulations, warranting further systematic investigation.
