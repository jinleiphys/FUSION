# Self-Consistent Separable Rpa Approach for Skyrme Forces: Axial Nuclei

> The self-consistent separable RPA (random phase approximation) method is formulated for Skyrme forces with pairing. The method is based on a general self-consistent procedure for factorization of the two-body interaction. It is relevant for various density- and current-dependent functionals. The contributions of the time-even and time-odd Skyrme terms as well as of the Coulomb and pairing terms to the residual interaction are taken self-consistently into account. Most of the expression have a transparent analytical form, which makes the method convenient for the treatment and analysis. The separable character of the residual interaction allows to avoid diagonalization of high-rank RPA matrices and thus to minimize the calculation effort. The previous studies have demonstrated high numerical accuracy and efficiency of the method for spherical nuclei. In this contribution, the method is specified for axial nuclei. We provide systematic and detailed presentation of formalism and discuss different aspects of the model.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a self-consistent separable random phase approximation (SRPA) method for Skyrme forces with pairing, specifically extended to axial nuclei, claiming that the separable character of the residual interaction avoids diagonalization of high-rank RPA matrices and minimizes calculation effort, though the method is presented as a systematic formalism rather than validated with numerical results for deformed systems.

## Method

The method starts from a time-dependent energy functional involving neutron and proton densities and currents, then factorizes the residual two-body interaction into a separable form using time-even and time-odd hermitian one-body operators derived self-consistently from the functional. The perturbed many-body wave function is obtained via a scaling transformation, and the coupling with the one-particle-one-hole (1ph) space is established through the Thouless theorem, leading to a dispersion equation for RPA eigenvalues. Pairing is included via the Bogoliubov transformation, and the strength function is formulated using a Lorentz smoothing function to avoid explicit calculation of individual RPA states.

## Key numbers

* The method reduces the RPA matrix rank from high values (e.g., ~400 in prior approaches) to a small rank determined by the number of separable terms K, where usually a few separable terms (or even one term) are sufficient for accurate reproduction of the residual interaction.
* The normalization condition for RPA operators yields the relation: sum over ph states of [(c^{nu s-}_{ph})^2 - (c^{nu s+}_{ph})^2] = 2.
* The pairing contribution to the strength matrix is given by [kappa^{(pair)}_{sk'sk}]^{-1} = G_s chi_{X, sk'} chi_{X, sk}, where chi_{X, sk} = 16 sum_{j} epsilon_j (u_j^2 - v_j^2) u^{(+)}_{jj} <j bar j|Q_{ks}|0>.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SRPA studies on spherical nuclei (Nesterenko et al., 2002) to axial deformed systems, where the 1ph configuration space becomes much larger and the computational savings of the separable method are more decisive. The approach builds on earlier self-consistent factorization schemes (e.g., the local RPA method of Reinhard, 1990, 1992) and addresses limitations of prior approaches that neglected Coulomb and time-odd density contributions to the residual interaction (e.g., Voronov and Severyukhin, 2000s). The formalism enables future studies of giant resonances and low-lying states in deformed and exotic nuclei, including systematic investigation of spin-orbit and Coulomb force contributions.
