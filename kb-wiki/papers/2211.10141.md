# Negative heat capacity for hot nuclei: confirmation with formulation from the microcanonical ensemble

> By using freeze-out properties of multifragmenting hot nuclei produced in quasifusion central $^{129}$Xe+$^{nat}$Sn collisions at different beam energies (32, 39, 45 and 50 AMeV) which were estimated by means of a simulation based on experimental data collected by the $4π$ INDRA multidetector, heat capacity in the thermal excitation energy range 4 - 12.5 AMeV was calculated from total kinetic energies and multiplicities at freeze-out. The microcanonical formulation was employed. Negative heat capacity which indicates a first order phase transition for finite systems is observed and confirms previous results using a different method.

## Key claim

Negative heat capacity, indicating a first-order phase transition in finite systems, is confirmed for hot nuclei using exact microcanonical formulae applied to freeze-out data reconstructed from a detailed simulation, correcting the earlier approximation of a single temperature for both internal excitation and thermal motion of fragments.

## Method

Quasifusion central 129Xe+natSn collisions at 32, 39, 45, and 50 AMeV were measured with the 4π INDRA multidetector. Freeze-out properties (multiplicities, total kinetic energies, fragment charges and masses) were reconstructed event-by-event via a detailed simulation constrained by experimental velocity spectra, calorimetry, and charge completeness (≥93% of total charge). The microcanonical heat capacity was then calculated directly from the statistical weight of configurations using formulae from Raduta (2002), which incorporate a limiting temperature for fragments (9 MeV) and separate kinetic and internal excitation temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Negative heat capacity observed in the thermal excitation energy range 6.0±1.0 to 10.0±1.0 AMeV (method 2, direct microcanonical formulae).
- Negative heat capacity observed in the range 5.5±1.0 to 9.0±1.0 AMeV when method 1 (partial energy fluctuations) was applied to the same freeze-out data from the detailed simulation.
- Microcanonical temperatures in the negative heat capacity region are approximately constant at 17–18 MeV.
- Average freeze-out volume varies from 3.9 to 5.9 times normal nuclear volume (V0) over the thermal excitation energy range, compared to a constant 3 V0 used in earlier method 1.
- Limiting temperature for excited fragments fixed at 9 MeV in the simulation.
- Beam energies: 32, 39, 45, and 50 AMeV.
- Thermal excitation energy range studied: 4–12.5 AMeV.
- Fragment charge selection restricted to Z = 80–100 (mass domain 194–238).

## Context

This work extends and corrects earlier observations of negative heat capacity by the MULTICS and INDRA collaborations (Borderie 2002, NicPHD) which used an approximate method (method 1) based on partial energy fluctuations and a single temperature assumption (Chomaz 1999, 2000). The present study applies exact microcanonical formulae from Raduta (2002) that treat internal excitation and thermal motion with two separate temperatures, a necessity demonstrated by simulations (Piantelli 2005, 2008). The results confirm the negative heat capacity signal for the liquid-gas phase transition in hot nuclei and enable future investigation of the bimodality signature, with the caveat that the effect of radial expansion must be understood (Borderie 2019, Frankland 2005, Gruyer 2013, Bonnet 2008).
