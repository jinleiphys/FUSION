# Effect of transverse momentum conservation and flow on symmetric cumulants $sc_{2,3} \left \{ 4 \right \}$ and $sc_{2,3,4} \left \{ 6 \right \}$

> Symmetric cumulants can improve our understanding of the joint probability distribution function $ P\left ( v_{m},v_{n},v_{k}, \dots,Ψ_{m},Ψ_{n},Ψ_{k},\dots \right )$, potentially offering new insights into the nature of the fluctuations of the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this work, the four-particle symmetric cumulants $sc_{2,3} \left \{ 4 \right \}$, six-particle symmetric cumulants $sc_{2,3,4} \left \{ 6 \right \}$, and the normalized cumulants $nsc_{2,3} \left \{ 4 \right \}$ and $nsc_{2,3,4} \left \{ 6 \right \}$ originating from transverse momentum conservation, collective flow, and the interplay between the two effects are calculated. Our results on $sc_{2,3} \left \{ 4 \right \}$ are consistent with the ATLAS data using the subevent cumulant method, facilitating a more profound understanding of the origins of the symmetric cumulant in small systems. Our results on $sc_{2,3,4} \left \{ 6 \right \}$ serve as theoretical predictions for future experimental measurements in small systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the four-particle symmetric cumulant \(sc_{2,3}\{4\}\) in small collision systems originates from the combined effects of transverse momentum conservation (TMC) and collective flow, with TMC dominating at low particle multiplicities and flow dominating at high multiplicities; the six-particle cumulant \(sc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) is presented as a theoretical prediction for future experiments, with the caveat that the calculations assume equal transverse momenta for all particles and expand the exponential term only to the order where the first nonzero TMC term appears.

## Method

The authors analytically compute the four-particle symmetric cumulant \(sc_{2,3}\{4\}\) and six-particle symmetric cumulant \(sc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) by first deriving the joint probability distribution of \(k\) particles under the constraint of transverse momentum conservation using a Gaussian approximation for the sum of momenta. They then incorporate collective flow by using a Fourier-expanded single-particle distribution including \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) harmonics, and perform Taylor expansions of the exponential TMC factor to the order where the first nonzero TMC contribution appears, assuming all particles have equal transverse momentum magnitude \(p\). The normalized cumulants \(nsc_{2,3}\{4\}\) and \(nsc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) are obtained by dividing by the appropriate products of two-particle cumulants \(v_n\{2\}\).

## Key numbers

- For TMC-only case, \(sc_{2,3}\{4\} \approx -\frac{5p^{10}}{6(N-4)^5\langle p^2\rangle_F^5} + \frac{5p^{12}}{2(N-4)^6\langle p^2\rangle_F^6} - \frac{827p^{14}}{144(N-4)^7\langle p^2\rangle_F^7} + \frac{p^{10}}{12(N-2)^5\langle p^2\rangle_F^5}\), where \(N\) is total particle multiplicity and \(\langle p^2\rangle_F\) is the mean squared transverse momentum.
- For TMC-only case, \(sc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) is given by a series expansion in powers of \(p^2/[(N-6)\langle p^2\rangle_F]\) with leading term \(-\frac{35p^{18}}{8(N-6)^9\langle p^2\rangle_F^9}\).
- The normalized cumulant \(nsc_{2,3}\{4\}\) is defined as \(sc_{2,3}\{4\} / (v_2\{2\}^2 v_3\{2\}^2)\).
- The normalized cumulant \(nsc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) is defined as \(sc_{2,3,4}\{6\} / (v_2\{2\}^2 v_3\{2\}^2 v_4\{2\}^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on TMC effects on multiparticle cumulants (Pei, Ma, Bzdak, prior works) and their recent calculation of \(sc_{2,4}\{4\}\) and \(ac_2\{3\}\) from TMC and flow, which agreed with ATLAS data in \(p+p\) collisions. The present results for \(sc_{2,3}\{4\}\) are compared with ATLAS measurements using the subevent method (ATLAS Collaboration, 2023) to suppress nonflow from jets, showing consistency and supporting the interpretation that TMC and flow together explain the observed correlations in small systems. The predictions for \(sc_{2,3,4}\{6\}\) await future experimental measurements, which could provide additional constraints on initial conditions and QGP properties in high-energy nuclear collisions.
