# A Higher-Order Calculation of $np$ Scattering in Cut-Off Effective Field Theory

> We report a next-to-leading-order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory calculation of the neutron-proton scattering cross section in the ${}^1S_0$ channel using a cut-off regularization. The inclusion of two-pion exchanges in the irreducible diagrams -- or potential -- figuring at NLO is found to be important in enlarging the domain of validity of the effective field theory. We are able to reproduce the {\it empirical} scattering phase shift up to p=300 MeV -- which is comparable to the cutoff scale involved -- with an agreement which is superior to results of other effective field theory approaches. We also discuss the role of the cutoff as a renormalization prescription and the importance of the explicit pion degree of freedom in scattering process.

# A Higher-Order Calculation of $np$ Scattering in Cut-Off Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The next-to-leading-order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory calculation of neutron-proton scattering in the ${}^1S_0$ channel, using a cut-off regularization with explicit two-pion-exchange potentials, reproduces the empirical scattering phase shift up to center-of-mass momentum $p=300$ MeV with agreement superior to other effective field theory approaches, though the authors acknowledge that the theory is expected to break down for $p \gtrsim \frac{3}{2} m_\pi$ and that missing subleading two-pion-exchange contributions are significant.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation in coordinate space using a momentum-space cutoff $\Lambda$ with a Gaussian regulator, employing potentials constructed from one-pion-exchange (OPEP), two-pion-exchange (TPEP), and contact interactions up to four-derivative order. The low-energy constants $C_0$, $C_2$, and $C_4$ were fit to reproduce the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (NPWA) values of the scattering length $a_0$, effective range $r_e$, and effective volume $v_2$ for the ${}^1S_0$ channel. The TPEP was included via a spectral representation with a spectral mass cutoff $m_0 = 2$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length $a_0 = -27.73$ fm and effective range $r_e = 2.677$ fm from NPWA, used to fix $C_0$ and $C_2$; the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential gives $a_0(v_{18}) = -27.732$ fm and $r_e(v_{18}) = 2.697$ fm.
- Effective volume $v_2 = -0.48$ fm$^3$ from NPWA, used to fix $C_4$.
- At $p=140$ MeV, the $1\pi C_2$ potential (OPEP plus contact terms up to $C_2$) differs from experiment by less than 3% for all cutoff $\Lambda \geq 300$ MeV.
- At $p=210$ MeV, the NLO potential (including TPEP) differs from experiment by less than 13% for all $\Lambda \geq 300$ MeV.
- The NLO potential with $\Lambda = 400$ MeV nearly coincides with NPWA for all momenta $p \leq 280$ MeV.
- The $2\pi C_4$ potential could not be fit for $\Lambda \geq 500$ MeV (with a tolerance of about 0.03 fm$^3$ in effective range, it could reach $\Lambda = 600$ MeV).
- TPEP is less than 3% of OPEP for momentum transfer $|\vec{q}| \leq m_\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the "$\Lambda$ counting scheme" of Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992), previously applied successfully to electroweak response functions by Park, Kubodera, Min, and Rho (1998), to the scattering problem. The authors compare their cut-off regularization approach to the "Q counting" scheme with power divergence subtraction (PDS) introduced by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), finding superior accuracy for the cut-off method. The results are consistent with contemporaneous work by Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (1999), who included subleading TPEP and Delta isobar contributions. The paper demonstrates that explicit inclusion of pion degrees of freedom is essential for extending the domain of validity beyond $p \sim m_\pi/2$, and that merely adding higher-order contact interactions without explicit pions cannot achieve comparable convergence.
