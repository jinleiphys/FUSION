# Appearance of peak in symmetry energy at N = 126 for Pb isotopic chain within relativistic energy density functional

> The newly derived relativistic energy density functional [\textcolor{blue}{ Phys. Rev. C \textbf{103}, 024305 (2021)}], which stems from the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) is employed to establish the appearance of peak/kink in the symmetry energy over the isotopic chain of Pb-nuclei. The coherent density fluctuation model parametrization procedure for finite nuclei is adopted here to obtain the relativistic energy density functional at local density. The relativistic energy density functional from E-RMFT takes precedence over the Brückner energy density functional as it accurately predicts the empirical saturation density and binding energy per nucleon $E/A$, so-called 'Coester Band Problem'. Interestingly, using the relativistic energy density functional, it is possible to predict the peak at $N=126$ for recently developed G3 and widely used NL3 parameter sets, which is not observed for Brückener's functional in-spite of using the E-RMF density. From the present analysis, the newly fitted energy density functional is found to be minutely sensitive to the choice of the parameter sets employed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a newly fitted relativistic energy density functional (EDF) derived from effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) theory can predict a peak in the symmetry energy at neutron number N=126 for the Pb isotopic chain, a result that was not achievable with the conventional Brückner energy density functional. The claim is hedged by noting that the relativistic EDF is "minutely sensitive to the choice of the parameter sets employed" and that the trend in the Pb isotopic chain is "not smooth enough," especially with the G3 parameter set.

## Method
The authors used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to convert nuclear matter quantities from momentum space to coordinate space via a local density approximation. They employed self-consistent E-RMF densities for Pb isotopes (even-even nuclei from N=98 to N=184) obtained with the NL3 and G3 parameter sets as input to calculate the weight function, which bridges nuclear matter and finite-nucleus properties. The symmetry energy S^A for each nucleus was then computed as the surface-weighted average of the nuclear matter symmetry energy, and its volume and surface components were separated using Danielewicz's liquid drop model.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy S^A for Pb isotopes using the relativistic EDF with the G3 force ranges between 29.4 MeV and 30.6 MeV over the isotopic chain; with the NL3 set, it ranges between 33.6 MeV and 35.7 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation for nuclear matter is 31.8 MeV for G3 and 37.4 MeV for NL3.
- At N=126, the symmetry energy from the relativistic EDF differs from the Brückner prediction by 1.73 MeV for G3 and by 7.40 MeV for NL3.
- The Brückner functional predicts binding energy per nucleon E/A = -14.9 MeV and saturation density rho ~ 0.2 fm^{-3}, while the relativistic EDF passes through the Coester band at E/A = -16 MeV and rho = 0.15 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends previous studies that used the Brückner energy density functional within the CDFM to examine symmetry energy peaks in Ca (N=20, 28) and Sn (N=82) but failed to reproduce a peak at N=126 for Pb (Bhuyan 2018, Quddus 2020, Pattnaik 2021). The authors resolve the "Coester Band Problem" (Coester 1970, Brockmann 1990) by using a relativistic EDF that correctly reproduces empirical saturation properties, enabling the prediction of the N=126 peak. This enables future studies of shell closures in heavy and superheavy nuclei using relativistic functionals, and suggests that the choice of parameter set (e.g., NL3 versus G3) affects the smoothness of the symmetry energy trend.
