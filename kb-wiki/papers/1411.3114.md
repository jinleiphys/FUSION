# A study of hyperons in nuclear matter based on chiral effective field theory

> The in-medium properties of a hyperon-nucleon potential, derived within chiral effective field theory and fitted to Lambda-N and Sigma-N scattering data, are investigated. Results for the single-particle potentials of the Lambda and Sigma hyperons in nuclear matter are reported, based on a conventional first-order Brueckner calculation. The Sigma-nucleus potential is found to be repulsive, in agreement with phenomenological information. A weak Lambda-nucleus spin-orbit interaction can be achieved by an appropriate adjustment of a low-energy constant corresponding to an antisymmetric Lambda-N -- Sigma-N spin-orbit interaction that arises at next-to-leading order in the effective field theory approach.

## Key claim

The chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon potential, fitted to scattering data, predicts a repulsive Σ-nucleus potential and can produce a weak Λ-nucleus spin-orbit interaction, but the latter requires adjusting a low-energy constant (the antisymmetric spin-orbit LEC C^(8_s8_a)) that could not be fixed by scattering data alone and was set to zero in the original fit.

## Method

The authors performed a conventional first-order Brueckner G-matrix calculation for Λ and Σ hyperons in nuclear matter, using the standard (gap) choice for the intermediate spectrum and angle-averaged Pauli operators. The hyperon single-particle potentials were computed self-consistently at fixed Fermi momentum, and the Scheerbaum factor S_Λ was evaluated from the G-matrix to quantify the Λ-nuclear spin-orbit strength. The LEC for the antisymmetric spin-orbit force (C^(8_s8_a)) was then adjusted to achieve a target S_Λ value of -3.7 MeV·fm⁵, while re-adjusting some ^1P_1 LECs to preserve the fit to Σ⁻p → Λn differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle potential depth U_Λ(p_Λ=0) at k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹: ranges from -28.2 MeV (NLO, Λ=500 MeV cutoff) to -22.4 MeV (NLO, Λ=600 MeV cutoff); LO results range from -38.0 MeV (Λ=550 MeV) to -34.4 MeV (Λ=700 MeV).
- Σ single-particle potential is repulsive; the isoscalar component U_Σ⁰ is positive (repulsive) for all NLO cutoffs, e.g., +16.1 MeV at Λ=600 MeV and k_F=1.35 fm⁻¹.
- Target Scheerbaum factor S_Λ = -3.7 MeV·fm⁵ was used to fix the antisymmetric spin-orbit LEC; empirical guidance from hypernuclear studies suggests S_Λ between -4.6 and -3.0 MeV·fm⁵.
- The LEC C^(8_s8_a) (in 10⁴ GeV⁻⁴) varies from -0.084 (Λ=450 MeV) to -0.048 (Λ=700 MeV).
- Total χ² for the 36 YN scattering data points in the original fit (without antisymmetric spin-orbit force) was 16–17; the new fit with the adjusted LEC maintains this quality.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon interaction of Haidenbauer et al. (2013) to nuclear matter, addressing two long-standing issues: the repulsive Σ-nucleus potential and the weak Λ-nucleus spin-orbit interaction. Prior meson-exchange models (e.g., Nijmegen NSC97f, Jülich '04) often failed to produce a repulsive Σ potential or overestimated the Λ spin-orbit force, while quark-model approaches (Fujiwara et al., 2007) had more success. The results enable future calculations of finite hypernuclei using the same chiral EFT interactions, and the authors note that a continuous-choice G-matrix calculation (planned for Pet15) would be needed to reliably compute the imaginary part of the Σ potential.
