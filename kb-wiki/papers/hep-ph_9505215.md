# Bound $q^2\bar q^2$ states in a constituent quark model

> We consider the existence of bound systems consisting of two quarks and two antiquarks ($q^2\bar q^2$) within the framework of a constituent quark model. The underlying quark dynamics is described by a linear confinement potential and an effective $q^2\bar q^2$ interaction which has its origin in instanton effects of QCD. We calculate the spectra and examine the internal structure of the states found.

# Bound $q^2\bar q^2$ states in a constituent quark model

## Key claim
Within a constituent quark model using a linear confinement potential and an instanton-induced effective interaction, all calculated $q^2\bar q^2$ states correspond to quasi-free two-meson states rather than tightly bound tetraquark systems; the model finds no evidence for bound $q^2\bar q^2$ states, although this does not rule out the possibility that the $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$ mesons are $K\bar K$ molecular states bound by long-range forces such as meson exchange.

## Method
The authors extended a non-relativistic constituent quark model, previously successful for meson and baryon spectra, to four-body $q^2\bar q^2$ systems with $u$, $d$, and $s$ quarks. The Hamiltonian includes a sum of constituent masses, kinetic energy for relative motion, a linear confinement potential (tested in two colour-dependence variants: a colour-singlet projector $V^1_{\rm conf}$ and a Gell-Mann matrix form $V^\lambda_{\rm conf}$), and a residual instanton-induced contact interaction regularized by a Gaussian of range $\Lambda$. Matrix elements were computed in a spin-flavour $SU(6)\times O(3)$ harmonic oscillator basis with up to $N=8$ total oscillator excitations, and the variational principle was applied to obtain mass spectra and eigenstates.

## Key numbers
- The confinement slope parameters satisfy $b_{qq} = 0.5493^{-1} b_{q\bar q}$, with $a_{q\bar q} = -2\sqrt{b_{q\bar q}}$.
- The residual interaction is regularized by replacing $\delta^3(\vec r)$ with $\frac{1}{\Lambda^3} \frac{1}{\pi^{3/2}} e^{-r^2/\Lambda^2} \delta_{L,0}$, where $\Lambda$ is the effective range of the pairing force.
- The single-quark density $\rho(z)$ shows a sharp maximum at $z=0$ and two significant shoulders at $z = \pm 1$ fm.
- The lowest $q^2\bar q^2$ state $0^0S^{--}$ lies below the $m_\pi + m_\pi$ threshold when calculated with the $V^1_{\rm conf}$ potential, but this is attributed to unphysical long-range attraction.
- Calculations with $N > 8$ oscillator excitations were limited by computational effort, but the lowest eigenvalues are estimated to have an accuracy of a few MeV based on comparison with meson calculations.

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model of Blask (1990) from meson and baryon sectors to $q^2\bar q^2$ systems without changing the interaction parameters. It directly addresses the long-standing debate over whether exotic mesons such as $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$ should be interpreted as $q^2\bar q^2$ states (Jaffe, 1977) or $K\bar K$ molecules (Isgur and Weinstein, 1982; Oka, 1985). The authors' conclusion that all calculated states are quasi-free mesons contradicts earlier suggestions of bound tetraquarks, but leaves open the possibility of molecular states bound by meson-exchange forces as discussed by Speth (1994). The analysis method based on single-quark density distributions provides a diagnostic tool for distinguishing genuine multiquark states from two-meson continuum states in future calculations with larger model spaces.
