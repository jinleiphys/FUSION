# Role of Neutron Transfer in Sub-Barrier Fusion

> Fusion excitation function of $^{35}$Cl + $^{130}$Te system is measured in the energy range around the Coulomb barrier and analyzed in the framework of the coupled-channels approach. The role of projectile deformation, nuclear structure, and the couplings of inelastic excitations and positive Q$-$value neutron transfer channels in sub-barrier fusion are investigated through the comparison of reduced fusion excitation functions of $^{35,37}$Cl +$^{130}$Te systems. The reduced fusion excitation function of $^{35}$Cl + $^{130}$Te system shows substantial enhancement over $^{37}$Cl + $^{130}$Te system in sub-barrier energy region which is attributed to the presence of positive Q-value neutron transfer channels in $^{35}$Cl + $^{130}$Te system. Findings of this work strongly suggest the importance of +2$n$ - transfer coupling in sub-barrier fusion apart from the simple inclusion of inelastic excitations of interacting partners, and are in stark contrast with the results presented by Kohley \textit{et al.}, [Phys. Rev. Lett. 107, 202701 (2011)].

# Role of Neutron Transfer in Sub-Barrier Fusion

## Key claim
The reduced fusion excitation function of the 35Cl + 130Te system shows substantial enhancement over the 37Cl + 130Te system in the sub-barrier energy region, which is attributed to the presence of six positive Q-value neutron transfer channels in the former system versus none in the latter. This finding contrasts with Kohley et al. (2011), who reported that positive Q-value neutron transfer channels have little influence on sub-barrier fusion, but the present work suggests that measurements extending to deeper sub-barrier energies (down to 10% below the barrier) reveal the effect that was not visible in the earlier work which only reached 3-4% below the barrier.

## Method
Fusion excitation functions were measured for the 35Cl + 130Te system using the Heavy Ion Reaction Analyser (HIRA) recoil mass separator at the Inter-University Accelerator Centre, New Delhi. Beams of 35Cl at center-of-mass energies from 94.04 to 121.65 MeV (10% below to 15% above the Bass barrier of 105.14 MeV) were bombarded onto a 130Te target of approximately 200 microgram/cm2 thickness. Evaporation residues were detected at the focal plane of HIRA using a multi-wire proportional counter, and fusion cross-sections were analyzed using coupled-channels calculations with the theoretical model code CCFULL, including couplings of inelastic excitations and the +2n transfer channel with a transfer form factor of 0.4 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Fusion cross-section at E_cm = 94.0 MeV (10% below barrier): 0.034 ± 0.010 mb
- Fusion cross-section at E_cm = 121.6 MeV (15% above barrier): 659 ± 83 mb
- Bass barrier V_B = 105.14 MeV for the 35Cl + 130Te system
- Six positive Q-value neutron transfer channels in 35Cl + 130Te: +1n = +0.61 MeV, +2n = +4.38 MeV, +3n = +1.71 MeV, +4n = +3.49 MeV, +5n = +0.21 MeV, +6n = +1.46 MeV
- Zero positive Q-value neutron transfer channels in 37Cl + 130Te (all Q-values negative: -2.31, -0.32, -3.27, -1.74, -5.18, -4.26 MeV)
- Transfer form factor F_tra = 0.4 MeV used in coupled-channels calculations to reproduce experimental cross-sections
- Deformation parameters: projectile 35Cl beta_2 = 0.24 (oblate), target 130Te beta_2 = 0.11

## Context
This work extends the study of sub-barrier fusion enhancement to the 35Cl + 130Te system and compares it with the 37Cl + 130Te system previously measured by the same group (Sahoo et al., 2019). The results contradict the conclusions of Kohley et al. (2011) for the 58,64Ni + 130Te systems, where no transfer effect was observed, and suggest that the discrepancy arises because the Ni measurements only reached 3-4% below the barrier while the present measurements extend to 10% below the barrier. The findings support the Stelson neutron flow model and enable future investigations of deep sub-barrier fusion dynamics, particularly for the 58,64Ni + 130Te systems to test whether transfer effects appear at deeper sub-barrier energies.
