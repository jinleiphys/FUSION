# Drag and diffusion coefficients of $B$ mesons in hot hadronic matter

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of a hot hadronic medium consisting of pions, kaons and eta using open beauty mesons as a probe have been evaluated. The interaction of the probe with the hadronic matter has been treated in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. It is observed that the magnitude of both the transport coefficients are significant, indicating substantial amount of interaction of the heavy mesons with the thermal bath. The results may have significant impact on the experimental observables like the suppression of single electron spectra originating from the decays of heavy mesons produced in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

# Drag and diffusion coefficients of $B$ mesons in hot hadronic matter

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of $B$ mesons in a hot hadronic medium (pions, kaons, eta) are significant, indicating substantial interaction, but the results are obtained under the assumption that inelastic channels can be ignored because chemical freeze-out occurs at about 170 MeV; the calculations use chiral perturbation theory up to NNLO, and the drag coefficient at NNLO increases by 22% compared to NLO at T=170 MeV.

## Method

The drag and diffusion coefficients of $B$ mesons (and $D$ mesons for comparison) were evaluated using elastic scattering amplitudes from Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory (HMχPT) at LO, NLO, and NNLO approximations. The transport coefficients were computed by thermally averaging momentum transfer weighted by squared invariant amplitudes for interactions with pions, kaons, and eta in the temperature range 100–170 MeV, using both momentum-dependent matrix elements and momentum-independent T-matrix elements derived from scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient of $B$ mesons evaluated at momentum 100 MeV increases with temperature from 100 to 170 MeV; NNLO result is 22% higher than NLO at T=170 MeV
- Diffusion coefficient from direct calculation and from Einstein relation differ by 6–7% at T=170 MeV for $B$ meson momentum p=100 MeV
- Temperature range considered for the hadronic medium: 100–170 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature for RHIC energies is about 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy meson diffusion in hadronic matter, which had focused on $D$ mesons using chiral perturbation theory (Laine, 2011) and empirical elastic scattering amplitudes (MinHe). The authors also compare with results from unitarized chiral effective $D\pi$ interactions (Abreu, 2011). The study is motivated by the need to subtract hadronic contributions from observables like the nuclear suppression factor ($R_{\mathrm AA}$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of single electrons from heavy meson decays at RHIC and LHC, where $B$ mesons dominate over $D$ mesons for $p_T > 5$ GeV (Djordjevic, 2009). The results enable more accurate extraction of quark-gluon plasma properties by accounting for hadronic phase effects on heavy flavor observables.
