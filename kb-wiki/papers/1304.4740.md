# Activation measurement of the 3He(a,g)7Be reaction cross section at high energies

> The astrophysically important 3He(a,g)7Be reaction was studied at high energies where the available experimental data are in contradiction. A thin window 3He gas cell was used and the cross section was measured with the activation method. The obtained cross sections at energies between Ec.m. = 1.5 and 2.5 MeV are compared with the available data and theoretical calculations. The present results support the validity of the high energy cross section energy dependence observed by recent experiments.

## Key claim

The present cross-section measurements at center-of-mass energies between 1.5 and 2.5 MeV support the validity of the high-energy cross-section energy dependence observed by recent experiments (ERNA and Carmona-Gallardo et al.), and do not reproduce the lower values and flatter energy dependence reported by Parker and Kavanagh (1963).

## Method

The cross section of the 3He(α,γ)7Be reaction was measured using the activation method with a thin-window 3He gas cell. A 4He++ beam from the MGC-20 cyclotron at ATOMKI irradiated the gas cell at five α-energies between 4.0 and 6.3 MeV; the produced 7Be nuclei were implanted into a removable copper catcher disk at the beam stop. After irradiation, the 478 keV γ-radiation from 7Be decay was counted off-line with a 100% relative efficiency HPGe detector, and the cross section was calculated from the measured activity, target thickness, beam intensity, and detection efficiency.

## Key numbers

- Cross section at Ec.m. = 1473 ± 15 keV: 2.96 ± 0.12 (stat) ± 0.17 (sys) μbarn; S-factor = 313 ± 12 (stat) ± 19 (sys) eV barn.
- Cross section at Ec.m. = 1791 ± 14 keV: 3.78 ± 0.13 (stat) ± 0.22 (sys) μbarn; S-factor = 327 ± 11 (stat) ± 19 (sys) eV barn.
- Cross section at Ec.m. = 2115 ± 14 keV: 4.68 ± 0.13 (stat) ± 0.28 (sys) μbarn; S-factor = 351 ± 10 (stat) ± 21 (sys) eV barn.
- Cross section at Ec.m. = 2338 ± 14 keV: 5.08 ± 0.18 (stat) ± 0.30 (sys) μbarn; S-factor = 354 ± 13 (stat) ± 21 (sys) eV barn.
- Cross section at Ec.m. = 2527 ± 14 keV: 6.06 ± 0.19 (stat) ± 0.36 (sys) μbarn; S-factor = 401 ± 12 (stat) ± 24 (sys) eV barn.
- Total systematic uncertainty on the cross section: 5.9%; γ-detection efficiency at 478 keV: (8.87 ± 0.44)%.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy between the high-energy data of Parker and Kavanagh (1963) and the more recent ERNA collaboration data (Di Leva et al., 2008) for the 3He(α,γ)7Be reaction, which is critical for solar neutrino physics and big-bang nucleosynthesis. The present results agree with the ERNA data and the independent measurements of Carmona-Gallardo et al. (2012), supporting the higher cross-section values and steeper energy dependence. These findings help constrain theoretical models (e.g., Kajino and Arima 1984, Nollett 2001, Neff 2011) used to extrapolate the S-factor to solar Gamow-window energies (15–30 keV), where the zero-energy S(0) value carries a 4% theoretical uncertainty according to Adelberger et al. (2011).
