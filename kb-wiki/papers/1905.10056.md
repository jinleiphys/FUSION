# A Study of Stress-Tensor Distribution around Flux Tube in Abelian-Higgs Model

> We study the stress-tensor distribution around the flux tube in static quark and anti-quark systems based on the momentum conservation and the Abelian-Higgs (AH) model. We first investigate constraints on the stress-tensor distribution from the momentum conservation and show that the effect of boundaries plays a crucial role to describe the structure of the flux tube in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory which has measured recently on the lattice. We then study the distributions of the stress tensor and energy density around the magnetic vortex with and without boundaries in the AH model, and compare them with the distributions in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory based on the dual superconductor picture. It is shown that a wide parameter range of the AH model is excluded by a comparison with the lattice results in terms of the stress tensor.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the stress-tensor distribution around the flux tube in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, measured on the lattice by Yanagihara (2018), is qualitatively inconsistent with the assumption of an infinitely-long flux tube based on momentum conservation; the authors then demonstrate that a wide parameter range of the Abelian-Higgs (AH) model cannot simultaneously reproduce the lattice results for the stress tensor, even when finite-length boundaries are included.

## Method

The authors first derive constraints on the stress-tensor distribution from momentum conservation in cylindrical coordinates, showing that for a translationally-invariant flux tube the integral of the azimuthal component must vanish. They then numerically solve the classical equations of motion for the magnetic vortex in the Abelian-Higgs model, both for infinitely-long vortices and for vortices with finite length between two magnetic monopoles, using an over-relaxation method on a discretized half-plane. The resulting energy-momentum tensor components are computed from the field configurations and compared with lattice results from Yanagihara (2018) for SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, with the physical scale set by matching the string tension.

## Key numbers

- String tension of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory: σ_YM = 1.132(10) GeV/fm, obtained from the large R behavior of the Q\bar{Q} potential at β=6.6 in Yanagihara (2018)
- The Bogomol'nyi bound occurs at κ = 1/√2, where the dimensionless string tension σ̂_AH = 1 analytically
- Numerical analysis covers κ range 0.1 ≤ κ ≤ 1.0 for finite-length vortices and 0.05 ≤ κ ≤ 3.0 for infinitely-long vortices
- Mesh size Δ = p × min(ξ_A, ξ_χ) with p chosen in range 0.02 < p < 0.1
- Spatial boundaries placed at least 3.4 times longer than max(ξ_A, ξ_χ)
- Convergence criterion: δE = (E_{n+1} - E_n)/E_n < 1.0 × 10^{-6}

## Context

This work extends the lattice measurement of stress-tensor distributions in static quark-antiquark systems by Yanagihara (2018) by providing a theoretical analysis using conservation laws and the Abelian-Higgs model. The dual superconductor picture, originally proposed by Nambu (1974), t Hooft (1975), Mandelstam (1974), and Nielsen (1973), connects the magnetic vortex in the AH model to the flux tube in Yang-Mills theory; previous comparisons by Suganuma (1993), Sasaki (1994), Ichie (1994), Monden (1997), Koma (1999, 2003), and Oxman (2017, 2018) used action density or field strength in specific gauges rather than the gauge-invariant stress tensor. The results enable future work to test whether modifications to the AH model potential or alternative dual descriptions can reproduce the lattice stress-tensor distributions.
