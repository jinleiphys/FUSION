# Linking partial and quasi dynamical symmetries in rotational nuclei

> Background: Quasi dynamical symmetries (QDS) and partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) play an important role in the understanding of complex systems. Up to now these symmetry concepts have been considered to be unrelated. Purpose: Establish a link between PDS and QDS and find an emperical manifestation. Methods: Quantum number fluctuations and the intrinsic state formalism are used within the framework of the interacting boson model of nuclei. Results: A previously unrecognized region of the parameter space of the interacting boson model that has both O(6) PDS (purity) and SU(3) QDS (coherence) in the ground band is established. Many rare-earth nuclei approximately satisfying both symmetry requirements are identified. Conclusions: PDS are more abundant than previously recognized and can lead to a QDS of an incompatible symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a previously unrecognized link between partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) and quasi dynamical symmetry (QDS) by showing that coherent mixing of SU(3) representations (QDS) can result in the partial conservation of a different, incompatible O(6) symmetry (PDS) in the ground band of the interacting boson model (IBM), with approximate validity in many deformed rare-earth nuclei.

## Method
The authors used quantum-number fluctuations of the O(6) quantum number sigma, calculated from wave-function expansions in the O(6) basis, to quantify the O(6) purity of ground-band states across the parameter space of the extended consistent-Q formalism (ECQF) Hamiltonian of the IBM. They analyzed the O(6) and SU(3) decompositions of yrast states for boson numbers up to N=60 using the ArbModel code, and compared results to the intrinsic state formalism with shape variables beta and gamma.

## Key numbers
- At the SU(3) dynamical symmetry limit (xi=1, chi=-sqrt(7)/2), the ground-state sigma fluctuation is Delta_sigma_gs ≈ 1.25 for N=14.
- At the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit (xi=0), the saturation value of Delta_sigma_gs ≈ 2.47 for N=14.
- In the newly identified valley of low fluctuations, Delta_sigma_gs values are two orders of magnitude lower than at saturation.
- For 160Gd (N=14, xi=0.84, chi=-0.53), the sigma=N components comprise more than 99% of the ground-state wave function at the bottom of the valley.
- For 162Dy (N=15, xi=0.92, chi=-0.31), the sigma fluctuation for the ground state is Delta_sigma_0 = 0.07 with 99.9% sigma=N fraction.
- For 166Er (N=15, xi=0.91, chi=-0.31), Delta_sigma_0 = 0.12 with 99.7% sigma=N fraction.
- The condition for the valley location for large N is given by xi = 1/(1 - sqrt(1/14)*chi + (1/14)*chi^2).

## Context
This work extends the concepts of partial dynamical symmetries (PDS), previously studied by Leviatan (2011) and others in specific nuclei and molecules, and quasi dynamical symmetries (QDS), discussed by Rowe (2004) and Macek (2009, 2010) in the context of nuclear models and quantum phase transitions. The paper connects these two previously unrelated symmetry concepts and identifies a region of the IBM parameter space similar to the "arc of regularity" found by Alhassid (1991). It enables the interpretation of many rare-earth nuclei, previously fitted by McCutchan (2004) to the ECQF, as simultaneously exhibiting O(6) PDS and SU(3) QDS, suggesting PDS are more abundant than previously recognized.
