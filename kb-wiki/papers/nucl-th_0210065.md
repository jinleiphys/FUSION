# CEM2k and LAQGSM as Event Generators for Space-Radiation-Shielding and Cosmic-Ray-Propagation Applications

> The CEM2k and LAQGSM codes have been recently developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory to simulate nuclear reactions for a number of applications. We have benchmarked our codes against most available measured data at incident particle energies from 10 MeV to 800 GeV and have compared our results with predictions of other current models used by the nuclear community. Here, we present a brief description of our codes and show illustrative results to show that CEM2k and LAQGSM can be used as reliable event generators for space-radiation-shielding, cosmic-ray-propagation, and other astrophysical applications. Finally, we show the use of our calculated cross sections together with experimental data from our LANL T-16 compilation to produce evaluated files which we use in the GALPROP model of galactic particle propagation to better constrain the size of the CR halo.

## Key claim

CEM2k and LAQGSM can serve as reliable event generators for space-radiation-shielding and cosmic-ray-propagation applications, but the authors acknowledge that the codes still have problems describing nuclides produced near the intersection of the spallation and fission regions, and that spectra of d, t, and 3He are reproduced reasonably but not as well as those of nucleons and 4He.

## Method

The authors benchmarked the CEM2k code (for nucleon-induced reactions up to 5 GeV) and the LAQGSM code (for particle- and nucleus-induced reactions up to 1000 GeV/nucleon) against measured data from 10 MeV to 800 GeV, comparing results with other models such as QMD, HIC, INCL, LAHET3, and YIELDX. They merged both codes with the GEM2 evaporation-fission model and used the resulting cross sections together with experimental data from the LANL T-16 compilation to produce evaluated excitation functions, which were then input into the GALPROP cosmic-ray propagation code.

## Key numbers

* CEM2k and LAQGSM were benchmarked against data at incident particle energies from 10 MeV to 800 GeV.
* LAQGSM describes reactions at energies up to about 1 TeV/nucleon (1000 GeV/nucleon).
* CEM2k describes nucleon-induced reactions at incident energies up to 5 GeV.
* Computing time for CEM2k decreased by up to a factor of 6 for heavy nuclei compared to earlier versions.
* For the reaction p(800 GeV) + Au, more than half of the measured products are described by LAQGSM+GEM2 with an accuracy of a factor of two or better.
* For the reaction 950 MeV/nucleon 238U on copper, LAQGSM+GEM2 describes most data with an accuracy of a factor of two or better.
* Using evaluated cross sections in GALPROP, the cosmic-ray halo size was restricted to zh ~ 4-6 kpc.
* The propagation region in GALPROP is bounded by R = Rh = 30 kpc and z = +/- zh.

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) and Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) lineage developed at Los Alamos, improving upon earlier versions CEM95 and CEM97, and upon the original QGSM by Amelin (1990). The codes are benchmarked against GSI inverse-kinematics data (Enqvist, 2001; Junghans, 1997) and compared with other models including INCL (Boudard, 2002), QMD (Aichelin, 1991), HIC (Bertini, 1974), and the phenomenological systematics of Webber (1990) and Silberberg (1998). The evaluated cross sections enable more precise constraints on the Galactic cosmic-ray halo size using the GALPROP model (Strong and Moskalenko, 1998), resolving previous large uncertainties that were mostly due to cross-section inaccuracies.
