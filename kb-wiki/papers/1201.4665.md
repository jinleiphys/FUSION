# Neutrinoless double beta decay

> The physics potential of neutrinoless double beta decay is discussed. Furthermore, experimental considerations are presented as well as the current status of experiments. Finally an outlook towards the future, work on nuclear matrix elements and alternative processes is given.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless double beta decay (0νββ) is a powerful laboratory probe of absolute neutrino mass and Majorana nature, but its interpretation depends critically on the underlying physics process (light or heavy Majorana neutrino exchange, right-handed currents, R-parity violating SUSY, or double charged Higgs bosons) and on the nuclear matrix elements, which introduce significant uncertainty into the extracted effective Majorana neutrino mass.

## Method

The paper reviews the physics potential, experimental status, and future prospects of neutrinoless double beta decay. It discusses the relation between the observed half-life and the effective Majorana neutrino mass via phase space factors and nuclear matrix elements, surveys the eleven candidate isotopes with Q-values above 2 MeV, and describes the experimental approaches (source-equals-detector using semiconductors, cryogenic bolometers, scintillators, liquid noble gas detectors, and tracking TPCs). The review also covers the status of running experiments (GERDA, EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen, CANDLES) and future projects (MAJORANA, COBRA, SNO+, CUORE, SuperNEMO, AMoRE, LUCIFER), as well as theoretical nuclear matrix element calculations and alternative processes involving positrons and electron capture.

## Key numbers

- Solar mass splitting: Δm⊙² = 7.59⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₈ × 10⁻⁵ eV²
- Atmospheric mass splitting (normal hierarchy): Δm_atm² = 2.49 ± 0.09 × 10⁻³ eV²
- Atmospheric mass splitting (inverted hierarchy): Δm_atm² = −2.343⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₉ × 10⁻³ eV²
- For effective Majorana mass ⟨m_ee⟩ larger than about 100 meV, neutrinos are almost degenerate; inverted hierarchy covers 10–50 meV; normal hierarchy is below 10 meV
- Half-lives for inverted hierarchy are beyond 10²⁶ years; half-lives for normal hierarchy are well beyond 10²⁸ years
- Claimed observation in ⁷⁶Ge: half-life of 2.23 ± 0.04 × 10²⁵ years
- GERDA Phase I: 17.7 kg of enriched germanium detectors; Phase II adds 35.5 kg of enriched Ge as BEGe detectors
- EXO-200: about 175 kg of liquid xenon (¹³⁶Xe)
- KamLAND-Zen: about 330 kg of enriched xenon in liquid scintillator
- CANDLES: 305 kg of CaF₂ scintillators for ⁴⁸Ca
- CUORE: 750 kg of TeO₂ crystals for ¹³⁰Te
- SuperNEMO: at least 100 kg of ⁸²Se in 20 TPC modules with 5 kg source mass per module
- SNO+: 1000 t of Nd-loaded scintillator with 0.1% loading giving 43 kg of ¹⁵⁰Nd (0.3% under investigation)
- ¹⁵²Gd identified as the most reliable candidate for resonant neutrinoless ECEC enhancement

## Context

This review summarizes the state of the field around 2012, extending earlier reviews such as those by Rodin (2011) and discussing the impact of recent oscillation results including a non-zero θ₁₃ from T2K (Abe, 2011), MINOS (Adamson, 2011), and Double Chooz (Kerr, 2011). The paper contextualizes the experimental program within the hierarchy of neutrino mass scenarios and emphasizes the need for a multi-isotope approach to compensate for nuclear matrix element uncertainties from shell model, QRPA, IBM, and GCM calculations. It enables future searches to target the inverted hierarchy region (⟨m_ee⟩ below about 50 meV) and, if no signal is found, the normal hierarchy region requiring ton-scale experiments with half-life sensitivities beyond 10²⁸ years.
