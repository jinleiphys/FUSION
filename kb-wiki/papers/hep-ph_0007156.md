# From Crepes to Pancakes in the MV Model

> The McLerran-Venugopalan model provides a framework which allows one to compute the gluon distribution function of a very large nucleus from the equations of QCD, provided that the longitudinal momentum fraction, xF, is sufficiently small. The source of color charge for this computation may be thought of as a crepe moving along the z axis at the speed of light. We refine the MV model by allowing for the presence of non-trivial longitudinal correlations between the color charges that comprise the nucleons. We find that a consistent treatment forces us to consider a pancake-like source which moves at slightly less than the speed of light. Our calculation allows us to consider larger values of xF than were allowed in the original MV model.

# From Crepes to Pancakes in the MV Model

## Key claim

The original McLerran-Venugopalan model, which treats the nucleus as an infinitely thin "crepe" of color charge moving at the speed of light, can be extended to larger longitudinal momentum fractions xF by incorporating non-trivial longitudinal correlations between color charges, forcing a "pancake" geometry with finite thickness moving at slightly less than the speed of light; however, the calculation is not reliable beyond xF ~ 0.25.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for a pancake-shaped distribution of color charge moving along the z axis at nearly the speed of light, obtaining a non-linear expression for the vector potential in terms of the charge density. They extract the gluon number density from the two-point correlation function of the vector potential using a classical average over an ensemble of nuclei, assuming Gaussian correlations and imposing a color neutrality condition on the charge-density correlator to render the integrals infrared finite. The resulting gluon number density is differential in both longitudinal momentum fraction xF and transverse momentum qt.

## Key numbers

- The original MV model is restricted to xF ≲ A^{-1/3}/(ma), where A is the number of nucleons, m is the nucleon mass, and a ~ Λ_QCD^{-1} is the nucleon radius.
- For uranium, the non-Abelian correction parameter Χ_∞ ~ 5 or 6 Λ_QCD^2.
- To obtain Χ_∞ = 20 Λ_QCD^2 requires of order 10^4 nucleons.
- The calculation is not reliable beyond xF ~ 0.25.
- At Q^2 = 2500 Λ_QCD^2, the effect of non-Abelian terms on the gluon distribution becomes negligible.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994; Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert, 1997) to larger values of xF by incorporating longitudinal structure, building on earlier work by Lam and Mahlon (2000) that introduced color neutrality to cure infrared divergences. The results show saturation behavior qualitatively similar to that obtained by Mueller (1990, 1999) from onium scattering, and enable calculations of the gluon distribution function for larger xF values than previously possible, with applications to nuclear dependence studies.
