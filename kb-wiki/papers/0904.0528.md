# Nonextensive thermal sources of cosmic rays?

> The energy spectrum of cosmic rays (CR) exhibits power-like behavior with a very characteristic "knee" structure. We consider a possibility that such a spectrum could be generated by some specific nonstatistical temperature fluctuations in the source of CR with the "knee" structure reflecting an abrupt change of the pattern of such fluctuations. This would result in a generalized nonextensive statistical model for the production of CR. The possible physical mechanisms leading to these effects are discussed together with the resulting chemical composition of the CR, which follows the experimentally observed abundance of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed cosmic-ray energy spectrum, including its characteristic "knee" structure, could be generated by nonstatistical temperature fluctuations in the source, with the knee reflecting an abrupt change in the fluctuation pattern; however, to keep the source temperature physically reasonable (around MeV rather than the 100 MeV required by earlier fits), the authors must additionally invoke an energy transfer mechanism from the surroundings parameterized by an effective temperature \(T_{eff}\).

## Method

The authors model cosmic-ray production using a generalized nonextensive thermal approach based on Tsallis statistics, where the power-law energy spectrum arises from fluctuations of the inverse temperature \(\beta\) described by a gamma distribution. They connect the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) to the heat capacity \(C_V\) of the source via \(C_V = 1/(q-1)\), and interpret the knee as a change in fluctuation pattern corresponding to an abrupt change in heat capacity, such as that occurring at a superfluid transition in neutron stars. To resolve the problem of requiring source temperatures around 100 MeV, they introduce an effective temperature \(T_{eff} = T_0 + (q-1)T_{visc}\) that incorporates energy transfer from the surrounding medium via viscosity effects.

## Key numbers

- The observed spectral index changes from \(\gamma_1 \simeq 2.7\) below the knee to \(\gamma_2 \simeq 3.1\) above it, corresponding to nonextensivity parameters \(q_1 = 1.213\) and \(q_2 = 1.196\).
- The ratio of heat capacities before and after the knee is \(C_2/C_1 = 1.09\).
- The temperature at which the fluctuation pattern changes is \(T_{cut} \approx 10^{15}\) eV \(\approx 10^{19}\) K.
- The effective temperature used in the double Tsallis fit is \(T = 100\) MeV.
- The average mass number \(\langle \ln(A) \rangle\) equals 1.82 below the knee and 1.78 above the knee.
- For the chemical composition model, the nucleon Fermi energy is \(E_F \simeq 30\) MeV, giving \(\varepsilon \simeq 22\) MeV per nucleon.
- The superfluid neutron heat capacity ratio \(C^{sf}_n / C_n \sim 1.1\) at \(T \sim 0.7 T_C\), corresponding to a spectral index change \(\Delta \gamma \sim 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of nonextensive statistics to cosmic rays by Tsallis (1988) and by previous authors (previous1, previous2) who attributed the knee to crossover between fractal-like thermal regimes or described the spectrum only up to the knee region, both requiring unphysically high source temperatures around 100-1000 MeV. The paper proposes a mechanism based on temperature fluctuations in neutron star crusts, specifically superfluid transitions and changes in effective mass of nucleons in Fermi liquids, to generate the observed fluctuation pattern. It enables future work incorporating energy-dependent chemical composition analysis and more detailed modeling of the effective temperature \(T_{eff}\) and its relation to transport properties of the surrounding medium.
