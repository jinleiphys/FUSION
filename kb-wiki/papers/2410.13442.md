# Heavy-light Pseudoscalar Mesons: Light-Front Wave Functions and Generalized Parton Distributions

> The internal structure of the lowest-lying pseudo-scalar mesons with heavy-light quark content is thoroughly studied using an algebraic model that has been successfully applied to similar physical observables of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with hidden-flavor quark content, ranging from light to heavy quark sectors. This model is based on constructing simple and evidence-based ansätze for the mesons' Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) and quark propagator, allowing the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF) to be computed algebraically. Its projection onto the light front yields the corresponding light-front wave function (LFWF), which provides easy access to the valence-quark Parton Distribution Amplitude (PDA) by integrating over the transverse momentum squared. We leverage our current knowledge of the PDAs of the lowest-lying pseudo-scalar heavy-light mesons to compute their Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) via the overlap representation of the LFWFs. From this three-dimensional information, various limits and projections allow us to deduce the related Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs), Electromagnetic Form Factors (EFFs), and Impact Parameter Space GPDs (IPS-GPDs). Whenever possible, we make explicit comparisons with available experimental results and previous theoretical predictions.

# Heavy-light Pseudoscalar Mesons: Light-Front Wave Functions and Generalized Parton Distributions

## Key claim

The algebraic model, previously successful for hidden-flavor mesons, can be extended to heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c) to compute their generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via the overlap representation of light-front wave functions (LFWFs), but the results depend on the specific choice of parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) computed using the Continuum Schwinger Function Method (CSM) with Rainbow-Ladder truncation and extrapolated via the Schlessinger point method (SPM).

## Method

The authors construct an algebraic model based on evidence-based ansätze for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) and quark propagator, allowing the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF) to be computed algebraically. The BSWF is projected onto the light front to obtain the LFWF, which is then integrated over transverse momentum squared to yield the valence-quark PDA. Using known PDAs for the five heavy-light mesons from Binosi et al. (2018), the LFWFs are computed and used to derive GPDs in the DGLAP region via the overlap representation. From these GPDs, the authors extract parton distribution functions (PDFs), electromagnetic form factors (EFFs), and impact-parameter space GPDs (IPS-GPDs).

## Key numbers

- Charge radius of D- meson: 0.680 fm
- Charge radius of D_s- meson: 0.372 fm
- Charge radius of B+ meson: 0.926 fm
- Charge radius of B_s0 meson: 0.345 fm
- Charge radius of B_c+ meson: 0.217 fm
- D-meson GPD nearly vanishes beyond momentum transfer -t approximately 1 GeV^2
- D_s-meson GPD approaches zero above -t approximately 2 GeV^2
- B_c-meson GPD remains significant for -t greater than approximately 4 GeV^2
- Light quark in D-meson located at approximately 0.6 times the meson charge radius from the center of transverse momentum
- Light quark in D_s-meson located at approximately 0.14 times the meson charge radius
- Light quark in B-meson located at approximately 0.65 times the meson charge radius
- Light quark in B_s-meson located at approximately 0.13 times the meson charge radius
- Light quark in B_c-meson located at approximately 0.035 times the meson charge radius

## Context

This work extends the algebraic model previously applied to hidden-flavor pseudoscalar and vector mesons (Albino et al., 2022) to heavy-light systems. The PDAs used are from Binosi et al. (2018), computed via CSM with RL truncation. The results are compared with available lattice QCD calculations (Li et al., 2017; Can et al., 2012), covariant constituent quark model (Moita et al., 2021), point-form model (Das et al., 2016), light-front quark model (Hwang et al., 2001), and contact interaction model (Hernandez-Pinto et al., 2023). The charge radii for D_s, B_s, and B_c mesons are consistent with lattice results, while D and B mesons show larger radii attributed to the significant mass difference between their valence quarks. This study enables future experimental and theoretical investigations of heavy-light meson structure.
