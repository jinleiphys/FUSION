# Deformed Hartree-Fock Calculation of Proton-Rich Nuclei

> We perform Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations for even-even nuclei with 2 <= Z <= 82 and N ranging from outside the proton drip line to the experimental frontier on the neutron-rich side. The ground state solutions are obtained for 737 nuclei, together with shape-coexistence solutions for 480 nuclei. Our method features the Cartesian-mesh representation of single-particle wavefunctions, which is advantageous in treating nucleon skins and exotic shapes. The results are compared with those of the finite-range droplet model of Moller et al. as well as the experimental values.

# Deformed Hartree-Fock Calculation of Proton-Rich Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors perform global Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations for even-even nuclei across a wide region of the nuclear chart, obtaining ground state solutions for 737 nuclei and shape-coexistence solutions for 480 nuclei. The method uses a Cartesian-mesh representation of single-particle wavefunctions, which is claimed to be advantageous for treating nucleon skins and exotic shapes, but the calculations are limited to nuclei with Z ≤ 82 and do not extend to the neutron drip line because the BCS method becomes unphysical there.

## Method
The authors use the Skyrme SIII interaction with a seniority pairing force in a Hartree-Fock+BCS framework, employing the code ev8 which represents single-particle wavefunctions on a Cartesian mesh with 1 fm spacing in a 13 × 13 × 14 fm³ box. The pairing strength is determined using a continuous spectrum approximation with the Thomas-Fermi single-particle level density and an average pairing gap of Δ = 12 MeV/√A. Solutions are obtained via the imaginary-time time-dependent-Hartree-Fock evolution method, with 500-2000 steps per nucleus using a time step of 1.5 × 10⁻²⁴ sec, and prolate or oblate solutions are searched by applying an external quadrupole potential to the initial wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- Ground state solutions obtained for 737 nuclei and shape-coexistence solutions for 480 nuclei, out of 752 even-even nuclei calculated with 2 ≤ Z ≤ 82
- Box dimensions: 13 × 13 × 14 fm³ with mesh size of 1 fm
- Mesh correction formula for total binding energy: ΔE(Z,N) = 0.00595·(Z+N) + 0.1090·(N-Z) + 1.24 MeV
- Average pairing gap adopted: Δ = 12 MeV/√A
- Cut-off energy for pairing interaction: λ_HF^τ + 7.3 MeV
- Proton skin thickness less than 0.3 fm for nuclei inside the proton drip line except some light (Z ≤ 20) nuclei
- For all nuclei with 50 < Z ≤ 82, no proton skin exists

## Context
This work extends global mean-field calculations of nuclear ground state properties, comparing results with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1993) and the extended Thomas-Fermi approach of Aboussir et al. (1992). Unlike these prior approaches which expand single-particle wavefunctions in a harmonic oscillator basis, the authors use a Cartesian-mesh representation from the code ev8 developed by Bonche, Flocard, and Heenen (1985). The method enables treatment of nucleon skins and exotic shapes but is limited to proton-rich nuclei; extension to the neutron drip line requires a deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov method. The results enable systematic comparison of binding energies, deformation parameters, and skin thickness across a wide region of the nuclear chart.
