# Bulk spectral functions in single and multi-scalar gravity duals

> We examine two point correlation functions involving the trace of the energy momentum tensor in five dimensional gravity dual theories supported by one or more scalar fields. A prescription for determining bulk channel spectral functions is developed. This prescription generalizes previous work which centered on one scalar field. As an application of these techniques, we investigate the bulk spectral function and corresponding sum rule in the Chamblin-Reall background. We show that, when expressed in terms of the beta function, the sum rule for the Chamblin-Reall background can be written in a form similar to the sum rule in Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The paper develops a prescription for computing bulk channel spectral functions (two-point correlation functions of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor) in five-dimensional gravity duals supported by multiple scalar fields, generalizing prior work that was limited to a single scalar field. The authors show that for the Chamblin-Reall background, the bulk sum rule can be expressed in terms of the beta function in a form similar to the Yang-Mills sum rule, but they also find that this sum rule does not agree with the bulk sum rule previously written down by Romatschke (2009), and they explain the reason for the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors work in a five-dimensional multi-scalar gravity theory with action containing the Ricci scalar, kinetic terms for scalar fields, a potential, and a Gibbons-Hawking boundary term. They employ the radial gauge (setting metric perturbations \(h_{05}=h_{55}=0\)) and construct gauge-invariant variables \(\mathcal{Z}_{\Phi i}\) for each scalar field. The on-shell action is expanded to quadratic order in perturbations, and the imaginary part is evaluated at the black hole horizon using incoming wave boundary conditions. The spectral density matrix is then obtained via the Son-Starinets prescription, yielding a formula for the bulk spectral function \(\rho^{\rm bulk}(w)\) in terms of the horizon-to-boundary ratios \(R_i(w,\epsilon)\) of the gauge-invariant perturbations.

## Key numbers

* The bulk spectral function for a multi-scalar theory is given by \(\rho^{\rm bulk}(w) = \frac{s w}{\pi} \lim_{\epsilon \to 0} \sum_{a=1}^n k_a(\epsilon)^2 R_a(w,\epsilon)\), where \(s\) is the entropy density and \(k_a(z) = \Phi_a'(z) g_{xx}(z)/g_{xx}'(z)\).
* For the Chamblin-Reall background, the ratio of bulk to shear spectral densities is a constant: \(\rho^{\rm bulk}(w)/\rho^{\rm shear}(w) = 4 \lim_{z \to 0} k(z)^2 = 12\delta\), where \(\delta = (\varepsilon - 3P)/(2\varepsilon) = (1-3v_s^2)/2\) ranges from \(0 \leq \delta \leq 1/2\).
* The bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio follows as \(\zeta/\eta = (4/3)\delta = 2(1/3 - v_s^2)\).
* The Chamblin-Reall background metric is \(ds^2 = b^2(z)[-f(z)dt^2 + d\mathbf{x}^2 + dz^2/f(z)]\) with \(b(z) = (L/z)^{1/(1-2\delta)}\) and \(f(z) = 1 - (z/z_h)^{2(2-\delta)/(1-2\delta)}\).

## Context

This work extends the single-scalar prescription for bulk spectral functions developed by Gubser (2008) to theories with multiple scalar fields, which are necessary to describe both conformal and chiral symmetry breaking in phenomenological holographic models (Erlich 2005, Batell 2008, Kapusta 2010). The method enables computation of the full frequency-dependent bulk spectral function, not just the zero-frequency limit that gives the bulk viscosity via the Kubo formula. The results for the Chamblin-Reall background complement prior studies of its thermodynamics and transport coefficients (Springer 2008, 2009; Gubser 2008; Romatschke 2009; Bigazzi 2010) and provide a consistency check with the alternative approach of Yarom (2009). The analysis of the bulk sum rule extends the shear sum rule study of Springer (2010) to the bulk sector and clarifies its relation to the sum rule proposed by Romatschke (2009).
