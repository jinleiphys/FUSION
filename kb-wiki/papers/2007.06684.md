# Charge radii of exotic neon and magnesium isotopes

> We compute the charge radii of even-mass neon and magnesium isotopes from neutron number N = 8 to the dripline. Our calculations are based on nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials from chiral effective field theory that include delta isobars. These potentials yield an accurate saturation point and symmetry energy of nuclear matter. We use the coupled-cluster method and start from an axially symmetric reference state. Binding energies and two-neutron separation energies largely agree with data and the dripline in neon is accurate. The computed charge radii have an estimated uncertainty of about 2-3% and are accurate for many isotopes where data exist. Finer details such as isotope shifts, however, are not accurately reproduced. Chiral potentials correctly yield the subshell closure at N = 14 and also a decrease in charge radii at N = 8 (observed in neon and predicted for magnesium). They yield a continued increase of charge radii as neutrons are added beyond N = 14 yet underestimate the large increase at N = 20 in magnesium.

## Key claim

Charge radii of neon and magnesium isotopes computed from chiral potentials that include delta isobars are accurate to within an estimated 2-3% for many isotopes where data exist, but finer details such as isotope shifts are not accurately reproduced, and the theory underestimates the large increase in charge radii at N=20 in magnesium.

## Method

The authors used the coupled-cluster method starting from an axially symmetric reference state built from natural orbitals, employing nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials from chiral effective field theory that include delta isobars (the DeltaNNLO_GO potentials with momentum cutoffs of 394 and 450 MeV/c). They truncated the cluster expansion at the 3-particle-3-hole level using the CCSDT-1 approximation, computed charge radii at the EOM-CCSD level, and used model spaces of up to 13 spherical harmonic oscillator shells with oscillator frequencies of 12 and 16 MeV to gauge model-space dependence.

## Key numbers

* Charge radii computed with an estimated uncertainty of about 2-3% for neon and magnesium isotopes from neutron number N=8 to the dripline.
* For magnesium isotopes, the DeltaNNLO_GO potentials agree with data within uncertainties for mass numbers 22 <= A <= 30.
* The dripline in neon is found at Ne-34, in agreement with data.
* For magnesium, the dripline is found at Mg-40, with Mg-42 being about 1.8 MeV less bound for the DeltaNNLO_GO(394) potential.
* The charge radius squared corrections used are: proton charge radius squared = 0.709 fm^2, neutron charge radius squared = -0.106 fm^2, and Darwin-Foldy term = 0.033 fm^2.
* For Mg-32, inclusion of triples via EOM-CCSDT-1 increases the radius by less than 1%.
* Angular momentum projection after variation of deformed Hartree-Fock states yields an energy gain from 3 to 6 MeV for all nuclei considered.

## Context

This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster calculations of charge radii to neutron-rich neon and magnesium isotopes in the island of inversion region, building on prior chiral EFT potentials such as NNLO_sat (Ekstrom 2015) and the DeltaNNLO_GO potentials (Payne 2019, Bagchi 2020, Jiang 2020). The calculations enable comparison with existing laser spectroscopy data for Ne-17-28 (Marinova 2011, Angeli 2013) and Mg-21-32 (Yordanov 2012), and predict charge radii trends for isotopes approaching the dripline that can be tested by proposed experiments (Vilen 2020).
