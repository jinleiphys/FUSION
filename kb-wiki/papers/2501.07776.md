# Generic framework for non-perturbative QCD in light hadrons

> This paper aims to serve as an introductory resource for disseminating the concept of instanton liquid model to individuals with interests in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) for hadrons. We discuss several topological aspects of the QCD vacuum and briefly review recent progress on this intuitive unifying framework for the lowlying hadron physics rooted in QCD by introducing the vacuum as a liquid of pseudoparticles. We develop systematic density expansion on the dilute vacuum with diagrammatical Feynman rules to calculate the vacuum expectation values (VEVs) and generalize the calculations to hadronic matrix element (charges), and hadronic form factors using the instanton liquid model (ILM). The ILM prediction are well-consistent with those of recent lattice QCD calculations. Thereby, the nonperturbative physics can be well-controlled by only a few parameters: instanton size $ρ$ and instanton density $n_{I+A}$, and current quark mass $m$.

## Key claim

The instanton liquid model (ILM) provides a unifying non-perturbative framework for low-lying light hadron physics rooted in QCD, where the vacuum is described as a dilute liquid of pseudoparticles (instantons and anti-instantons) whose effects are well-controlled by only three parameters: instanton size rho, instanton plus anti-instanton density n_{I+A}, and current quark mass m. The ILM predictions are well-consistent with recent lattice QCD calculations, though the framework is presented as an introductory resource rather than a definitive derivation.

## Method

The paper develops a systematic density expansion on the dilute instanton vacuum using diagrammatical Feynman rules to calculate vacuum expectation values (VEVs), hadronic matrix elements (charges), and hadronic form factors. The quark propagator in the multi-instanton background is computed via ensemble averaging with planar resummation in the large N_c limit, leading to a gap-like equation that yields a momentum-dependent constituent quark mass. The framework is extended from a canonical ensemble to a grand canonical ensemble to account for fluctuations in instanton and anti-instanton numbers, which capture the scale and U(1) anomalies.

## Key numbers

- Instanton plus anti-instanton density: n_{I+A} approximately 1/fm^4, with packing fraction kappa_{I+A} = pi^2 rho^4 n_{I+A} approximately 0.1
- Instanton size rho approximately 0.313 fm, with ratio rho/R approximately 1/3 where R = 1 fm is the hadronic scale
- At cooling time tau = 9 (low resolution mu = 520 MeV ~ 1/rho), the tunneling density is sparse; at tau = 0.6 (high resolution mu = 2 GeV), density reaches n_{I+A} ~ 7.46/fm^4
- Numerical fitting of cooling time evolution gives rate constant lambda = 0.1678 fm^4
- Constituent quark mass M(k) is compared with lattice QCD using instanton size rho = 0.313 fm and n_{I+A} = 1.056 fm^{-4}
- The chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic field strengths carried by pseudoparticles: sqrt(E) = sqrt(B) approximately 2.5/rho approximately 1.5 GeV
- The renormalization scale for ILM calculations is mu ~ 1/rho approximately 0.6 GeV

## Context

This paper extends the instanton liquid model framework originally developed by Diakonov (1985) and Shuryak (1981) to provide a comprehensive review of recent progress in calculating hadronic form factors and partonic observables using light-front wave functions. The work builds on lattice evidence for topological structure from Michael (1994), Leinweber (1999), and Athenodorou (2018), and connects the ILM to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model through the 't Hooft interaction. The framework enables systematic calculation of vacuum expectation values and hadron matrix elements at the intermediate scale mu ~ 1/rho, with future applications to non-perturbative renormalization group flow and connections to the dyon liquid model for finite-temperature QCD.
