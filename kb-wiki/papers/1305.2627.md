# Tsallis Fits to pT Spectra and Multiple Hard Scattering in pp Collisions at the LHC

> Phenomenological Tsallis fits to the CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE transverse momentum spectra of hadrons for pp collisions at LHC were recently found to extend over a large range of the transverse momentum. We investigate whether the few degrees of freedom in the Tsallis parametrization may arise from the relativistic parton-parton hard-scattering and related processes. The effects of the multiple hard-scattering and parton showering processes on the power law are discussed. We find empirically that whereas the transverse spectra of both hadrons and jets exhibit power-law behavior of 1/pT^n at high pT, the power indices n for hadrons are systematically greater than those for jets, for which n~4-5.

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution fits to CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE hadron transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions at LHC extend over a very large pT range (0.5 to 181 GeV/c at 7 TeV), but the authors argue that the power indices n for hadrons (n~7) are systematically greater than those for jets (n~4-5), and this difference likely arises from parton showering and hadronization rather than from multiple hard scattering alone.

## Method

The authors analytically evaluate the relativistic hard-scattering model integral using saddle point integration to obtain an approximate expression for the invariant differential cross section for parton production. They then extend the multiple hard-scattering formalism of Accardi (2001) to derive explicit power-law and logarithmic terms for N-fold parton scattering, incorporating the parton thickness function T(b) and summing over impact parameters. Finally, they compare the predicted power indices from the hard-scattering model with experimental jet and hadron spectra from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis/Hagedorn fit to CMS and ATLAS charged particle spectra extends from pT = 0.5 to 181 GeV/c in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, and from pT = 0.5 to 31 GeV/c at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV
- The power index n for hadron spectra is about 7 at LHC energies, while for jets n is about 4-5
- For single parton-parton hard scattering (N=1), the differential cross section scales as alpha_s^2 / pT^4
- For double parton scattering (N=2), the differential cross section scales as alpha_s^4 ln(pT/2p0) / pT^6
- For triple parton scattering (N=3), the differential cross section scales as alpha_s^6 [ln(pT/3p0)]^2 / pT^8
- The basic qq' -> qq' process at 90 degrees gives dsigma/dt = (4 pi alpha_s^2 / 9) * (5/16) / cT^4
- The basic gg -> gg process at 90 degrees gives dsigma/dt = (9 pi alpha_s^2 / 16) * (3/2)^3 / cT^4

## Context

This work extends the multiple hard-scattering formalism of Accardi (2001) and the earlier studies of Kalinowski (1987), Calucci and Treleani (1990-2001), and Gyulassy and collaborators (2001) to obtain explicit power-law and logarithmic dependence on the number of scatterers and centrality. The authors contrast their parton-based approach with earlier proposals by Blankenbecler, Brodsky, and Gunion (1975, 1977) and Arleo and Brodsky (2010, 2012) that invoked meson constituents to explain the hadron power index n~8. The results enable quantitative predictions for how parton showering and fragmentation modify the power index from the basic pQCD value of n=4 to the observed hadron value of n~7, and provide a framework for analyzing centrality-dependent multiple scattering in both hadron-hadron and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
