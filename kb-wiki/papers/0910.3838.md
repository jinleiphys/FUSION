# Mini-jet thermalization and diffusion of transverse momentum correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Transverse momentum correlation in azimuthal angle of produced hadrons due to mini-jets are studied first within the HIJING Monte Carlo model in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Jet quenching in the early stage of thermalization is shown to lead to significant diffusion (broadening) of the correlation. Evolution of the transverse momentum density fluctuation that gives rise to such correlation in azimuthal angle in the later stage of heavy-ion collisions is further investigated within a linearized diffusion-like equation and is shown to be determined by the shear viscosity of the evolving dense matter. Such a diffusion equation for the transverse momentum fluctuation is solved with initial values given by HIJING and together with the hydrodynamic equation for the bulk medium. The final transverse momentum correlation in azimuthal angle is calculated along the freeze-out hyper-surface and is found further diffused for larger values of shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s \sim 0.2-0.4$. Therefore the final transverse momentum correlation in azimuthal angle can be used to study the thermalization of mini-jets in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions and the viscous effect in the hydrodynamic evolution of the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma.

# Mini-jet thermalization and diffusion of transverse momentum correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The transverse momentum correlation in azimuthal angle, initially generated by mini-jets, is diffused (broadened) both by jet quenching in the early thermalization stage and by shear viscosity during the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution; the final correlation can therefore serve as a probe of both mini-jet thermalization and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma, provided the same-bin fluctuation at zero azimuthal separation is subtracted.

## Method

The authors first computed the transverse momentum correlation in azimuthal angle from the HIJING Monte Carlo model for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, both with and without jet quenching. They then derived a linearized diffusion-like equation for the fluctuation in transverse flow velocity from the viscous fluid equations, keeping only the off-diagonal shear stress component. This diffusion equation was solved numerically with initial conditions extracted from HIJING (with jet quenching) and with the space-time evolution of the bulk medium given by (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics, using a Glauber initial condition and an equation of state with a first-order phase transition (EOSQ). The final transverse momentum correlation was evaluated along the freeze-out hyper-surface for different values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature at the center of the overlap region: T = 359 MeV, corresponding to energy density ε₀ = 30.0 GeV/fm³, at initial proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV/n.
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 128 MeV (energy density 0.075 GeV/fm³); the calculated final transverse energy dE_T/dη = 667 GeV is consistent with the experimental value 606 ± 35 GeV for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio studied: η/s ~ 0.2–0.4, for which the diffusion of the correlation is as significant as that caused by jet quenching.
- HIJING result for total transverse energy without hydrodynamic evolution: dE_T/dη = 750 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-hadron correlations from mini-jets (Wang, 2001) and the use of transverse momentum fluctuations to constrain shear viscosity (Aziz and Gavin, 2004; Gavin and Abdel-Aziz, 2006; Wang, 2009) by explicitly connecting the early-stage jet quenching effect to the later viscous hydrodynamic diffusion of the correlation. The authors propose that experimental measurement of the transverse momentum correlation in heavy-ion collisions, compared with p+p and p+A collisions, can simultaneously probe mini-jet thermalization and the viscous properties of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, complementing existing autocorrelation measurements by the STAR collaboration (Adams et al., 2005).
