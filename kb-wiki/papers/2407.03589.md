# Systematic uncertainty of offshell corrections and higher-twist contribution in DIS at large x

> We study the systematic uncertainty and biases introduced by theoretical assumptions needed to include large-$x$ DIS data in a global QCD analysis. Working in the CTEQ-JLab framework, we focus on different implementations of higher-twist corrections to the nucleon structure functions and of offshell PDF deformations in deuteron targets and discuss how their interplay impacts the extraction of the $d$-quark PDF and the calculation of the neutron structure function at large $x$.

# Systematic uncertainty of offshell corrections and higher-twist contribution in DIS at large x

## Key claim

The choice between multiplicative and additive implementations of higher-twist (HT) corrections in global QCD analyses introduces a systematic bias in the extracted neutron-to-proton structure function ratio and offshell nucleon deformations, but this bias can be removed by allowing isospin-breaking HT terms rather than assuming isospin symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed global QCD analyses within the CTEQ-JLab (CJ22) framework, fitting deep-inelastic scattering data on proton and deuteron targets with different implementations of higher-twist corrections (multiplicative versus additive, and isospin-symmetric versus isospin-breaking) and a second-order polynomial parametrization of the parton-level offshell function δf. They compared the extracted neutron-to-proton F2 structure function ratios, offshell functions, and deuteron-to-proton ratios across these scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-to-proton F2 ratio at x→1 with isospin-symmetric additive HT corrections gives n/p ≈ 1/4 + 3H/(16uQ²), while multiplicative HT corrections give n/p ≈ 1/4, showing a systematic difference purely from implementation choice.
- With isospin-breaking HT corrections, the n/p ratio becomes n/p ≈ 1/4 + (4H̃_n − H̃_p)/(16uQ²) ≈ 1/4 + H̃_p/(16uQ²), removing the implementation bias.
- The extracted neutron HT function H_n(x) is approximately 1/2 of the proton HT function H_p(x) at large x, consistent with the assumption used in the derivation.
- The fits were performed at Q² = 10 GeV² for comparison of n/p and D/p ratios.

## Context

This work extends the CJ22 global QCD analysis framework (Accardi, 2023) by studying systematic uncertainties from higher-twist implementation choices. It contrasts with studies by Alekhin, Kulagin and Petti (AKP, 2022) who found no significant impact of HT implementation choice in isospin-symmetric fits, and with the JAM collaboration (Cocuzza, 2021) who considered isospin-breaking HT terms. The results enable more robust extraction of the d-quark PDF and neutron structure function at large x, and highlight the need for new Jefferson Lab tagged DIS data to further constrain offshell deformations.
