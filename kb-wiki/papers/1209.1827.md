# On the Langevin description of nonequilibrium quantum fields

> We consider the non-equilibrium dynamics of a real quantum scalar field. We show the formal equivalence of the exact evolution equations for the statistical and spectral two-point functions with a fictitious Langevin process and examine the conditions under which a local Markovian dynamics is a valid approximation. In quantum field theory, the memory kernel and the noise correlator typically exhibit long time power laws and are thus highly non-local, thereby questioning the possibility of a local description. We show that despite this fact, there is a finite time range during which a local description is accurate. This requires the theory to be (effectively) weakly coupled. We illustrate the use of such a local description for studies of decoherence and entropy production in quantum field theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, despite the fact that memory kernels and noise correlators in quantum field theory typically exhibit long-time power-law decays (making them highly non-local), there exists a finite time range during which a local Markovian Langevin description accurately approximates the exact nonequilibrium dynamics, provided the theory is effectively weakly coupled.

## Method

The authors first establish the formal equivalence between the exact Schwinger-Dyson equations for the statistical and spectral two-point functions and a fictitious Langevin process. They then examine the conditions under which the non-local memory integrals can be replaced by local damping terms, using a harmonic approximation that exploits a separation of scales between the oscillation frequency and the damping/nonequilibrium scales. To test the validity of this local approximation, they specialize to a solvable case where an out-of-equilibrium test field weakly interacts with a stationary thermal bath with negligible backreaction, allowing the exact solution to be expressed in terms of the equilibrium spectral function.

## Key numbers

* The time range for which the local Markovian description is valid is given by \(\gamma_p t \lesssim \ln \left\{ \left( \frac{\mu}{\gamma_p} \right)^{\!\nu} \frac{\gamma_p}{\epsilon_p^2 \mathcal{A}} \right\}\), where \(\gamma_p\) is the damping rate, \(\epsilon_p\) the quasiparticle energy, \(\mu\) a scale of the problem, \(\nu\) an exponent, and \(\mathcal{A}\) an amplitude proportional to a positive power of the coupling constant.
* The Breit-Wigner form of the spectral function near the pole is \(\tilde\rho_p(\omega \to \epsilon_p) \approx \frac{Z_p}{\epsilon_p} \frac{i\gamma_p}{(\omega - \epsilon_p)^2 + \gamma_p^2}\), with the field renormalization factor \(Z_p^{-1} = 1 - \frac{d}{d\omega^2} \text{Re} \tilde\Sigma^R_p(\omega) \big|_{\omega=\epsilon_p}\) and the quasiparticle width \(\gamma_p = -Z_p \frac{\text{Im} \tilde\Sigma^R_p(\epsilon_p)}{2\epsilon_p}\).
* The white noise correlator that reproduces the equilibrium state is \(\overline{\xi_p(t)\xi_p(t')} = Z_p^{-1} \alpha_p \delta(t-t')\) with \(\alpha_p = 4\gamma_p \epsilon_p \left( n(\beta\epsilon_p) + \frac{1}{2} \right)\), where \(n(x) = 1/(\exp(x)-1)\) is the Bose-Einstein distribution at temperature \(T = 1/\beta\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of deriving effective kinetic and Langevin descriptions from nonequilibrium quantum field theory, which includes the influence functional formalism of Feynman and Vernon (1963) and the gradient expansion approach to Kadanoff-Baym equations. The paper directly addresses the concern raised by Boyanovsky (1995, 1998) that memory kernels and noise correlators typically decay as power laws, questioning the possibility of a local Markovian description; the authors show that a finite time window for such a description exists for weakly coupled theories. This enables the use of local Langevin dynamics for studies of decoherence and entropy production in quantum field theory, particularly within the "incomplete description" picture recently advocated by Campo (2008), Giraud (2009), and Koksma (2009, 2010, 2011).
