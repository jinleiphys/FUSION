# Super-allowed beta-decay rates in 1d5/2 shell in Coriolis coupling model

> The expression for super-allowed beta-decay transition rates have been derived within the context of Coriolis coupling model. The derived expressions, valid for the beta-decay between any two mirror nuclei, has been applied to calculate super-allowed beta-decay transition rates of 21Na, 21Mg, 21Al, and 21Si. The calculated rates agree well with the data and the calculations done using the shell model with configuration admixture.

## Key claim

The Coriolis coupling model, which includes band mixing, can account for super-allowed beta-decay transition rates in the $1d_{5/2}$ shell, whereas the simpler Nilsson model (without band mixing) yields results that deviate significantly from observed data.

## Method

The authors derived expressions for super-allowed beta-decay transition rates within the Coriolis coupling model of Malik and Scholz, using a Hamiltonian that includes a rotating axially symmetric anharmonic core and a valence nucleon governed by the Nilsson Hamiltonian. The Gamow-Teller matrix elements were computed using wavefunctions that mix Nilsson bands via Coriolis coupling, with model parameters (moment of inertia, deformation, spin-orbit and orbit-orbit strengths, and Coriolis quenching factor) adjusted to reproduce observed low-lying level spectra and ground-state magnetic and quadrupole moments. The derived expressions were then applied to calculate $\log ft$ values for four mirror-pair transitions.

## Key numbers

*   For $^{21}_{11}$Na $\rightarrow ^{21}_{10}$Ne ($3/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$): calculated $\log ft = 3.65$ (Wilkinson parameters) and 3.63 (Landford-Wildenthal parameters), compared to observed 3.61.
*   For $^{23}_{12}$Mg $\rightarrow ^{23}_{11}$Na ($3/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$): calculated $\log ft = 3.65$ (Wilkinson) and 3.63 (Landford-Wildenthal), compared to observed 3.67.
*   For $^{25}_{13}$Al $\rightarrow ^{25}_{12}$Mg ($5/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+$): calculated $\log ft = 3.56$ (Wilkinson) and 3.54 (Landford-Wildenthal), compared to observed 3.57.
*   For $^{27}_{14}$Si $\rightarrow ^{27}_{13}$Al ($5/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+$): calculated $\log ft = 3.58$ (Wilkinson) and 3.51 (Landford-Wildenthal), compared to observed 3.62.
*   The ratio of coupling strengths used was $(g_A / g_V)^2 = 1.239 \pm 0.011$ (from free neutron decay) and 1.27 (from Wilkinson's analysis of nuclear data).
*   The constant $F$ in the $ft$ expression was taken as 6150 (Landford-Wildenthal) or $F = (6162 \pm 14) (1-3.67 \times 10^{-4} Z + 1.30 \times 10^{-5} Z^2)$ (Wilkinson).

## Context

This work extends the Coriolis coupling model, previously applied to level spectra and moments, to super-allowed beta-decay calculations for the first time in $1d_{5/2}$ nuclei. It directly addresses the discrepancy found by Kim (using the Nilsson model), whose calculations for $^{25}$Al $\rightarrow ^{25}$Mg and $^{27}$Si $\rightarrow ^{27}$Al yielded $\log ft$ values of 3.49, significantly lower than the observed values near 3.6. The results are consistent with the shell-model calculations of Landford and Wildenthal (using Kuo-Brown two-body matrix elements with $^{16}$O as a core), which gave $\log ft$ values of 3.56, 3.68, and 3.51 for three of the four transitions studied. The work enables further application of the Coriolis coupling model to beta-decay in other mass regions where band mixing is important.
