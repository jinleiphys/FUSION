# Impact of Magnetic field on neutron star properties

> We derive an equation of state for magnetized charge neutral nuclear matter relevant for neutron star structure. The calculations are performed within an effective chiral model based on generalization of sigma model with nonlinear self interactions of the sigma mesons along with vector mesons and a $ρ-σ$ cross-coupling term. The effective chiral model is extended by introducing the contributions of strong magnetic field on the charged particles of the model. The contributions arising from the effects of magnetic field on the Dirac sea of charged baryons are also included. The resulting equation of state for the magnetized dense matter is used to investigate the neutron star properties, like, mass-radius relation and tidal deformability. The dimensionless tidal deformability of $1.4~{M}_\odot$ NS is found to be $Λ_{1.4}=526$, which is consistent with recent observation of GW170817. The maximum mass of neutron star in presence of strong magnetic field is consistent with the observational constraints on mass of neutron star from PSR~ J0348 - 0432 and the radius at $1.4~{M}_\odot$ mass of the neutron star is within the empirical bounds.

## Key claim

The paper derives an equation of state for magnetized charge-neutral nuclear matter including the effects of strong magnetic fields on both the Fermi sea and the Dirac sea of charged baryons, and finds that the resulting neutron star properties (mass, radius, tidal deformability) are consistent with observational constraints from GW170817 and high-mass pulsars, but only when using a density-dependent magnetic field profile and including the Dirac sea contribution.

## Method

The authors use an effective chiral model based on a generalized sigma model with nonlinear self-interactions of sigma mesons, vector mesons, and a rho-sigma cross-coupling term. They extend this model by incorporating a constant external magnetic field in the z-direction, implementing Landau quantization for charged nucleons, and including the regularized Dirac sea contribution using dimensional regularization. The resulting anisotropic equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and compute the tidal deformability, assuming a density-dependent magnetic field that increases from the surface to the core.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is Lambda_{1.4} = 526, consistent with the GW170817 observation.
- The nuclear matter saturation density is rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}, with nucleon effective mass m* = 0.86 m, incompressibility K = 247 MeV, energy per particle e_0 = -16.0 MeV, symmetry energy J_0 = 32.5 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L_0 = 65 MeV.
- The surface magnetic field is taken as B_surf = 10^{15} Gauss, with a maximum core field B_0 and parameters beta = 0.003 and gamma = 3 for the density-dependent magnetic field profile.
- The empirical radius estimate for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is R_{1.4} = (11.9 +/- 1.22) km, and recent bounds from GW170817 suggest R_{1.4} < 13.76 km.

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral model previously developed by Malik et al. (2017) to include strong magnetic field effects, building on prior studies of magnetized neutron stars by Chakrabarty (1997), Broderick (2002), Huang (2010), Lopes (2012), Casali (2014), Gomes (2014, 2017), Gao (2015), Orsaria (2011), Dexheimer (2014), Franzon (2015), Chu (2015), Paulucci (2011), and Strickland (2012). The inclusion of the Dirac sea contribution in a magnetic field follows techniques used in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models by Menezes (2009) and Chatterjee (2011), as well as quark-meson models by Ferrari (2012), Andersen (2012), and Skokov (2012). The results enable future studies of magnetar structure and binary neutron star mergers where magnetic fields may become extremely large due to magneto-rotational instability.
