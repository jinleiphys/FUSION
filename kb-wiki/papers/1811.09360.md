# Extended optical model analyses of $^{11}$Be+$^{197}$Au with dynamic polarization potentials

> We discuss angular distributions of elastic, inelastic, and breakup cross sections for $^{11}$Be + $^{197}$Au system, which were measured at energies below and around Coulomb barrier. To this end, we employ Coulomb dipole excitation (CDE) and long-range nuclear (LRN) potential to take into account long range effects by halo nuclear system and break up effects by weakly-bound structure. We then analyze recent experimental data including 3-channes i.e. elastic, inelastic, and breakup cross sections, at $E_{\textrm{c.m.}}$=29.6 MeV and $E_{\text{c.m.}}$=37.1 MeV. From the extracted parameter sets using $χ^{2}$ analysis, we successfully reproduce the experimental angular distributions of the elastic, inelastic, and breakup cross sections for $^{11}$Be+$^{197}$Au system simultaneously. Also we discuss the necessity of LRN potential around Coulomb barrier from analyzed experimental data.

# Extended optical model analyses of $^{11}$Be+$^{197}$Au with dynamic polarization potentials

## Key claim
The authors successfully reproduce elastic, inelastic, and breakup angular distributions for the $^{11}$Be+$^{197}$Au system simultaneously using an extended optical model that includes Coulomb dipole excitation (CDE) and a long-range nuclear (LRN) potential. However, the necessity of the LRN potential is clearly demonstrated only through the breakup cross section data, not through the elastic or quasi-elastic scattering data alone.

## Method
The authors employed an extended optical model potential comprising a short-range nuclear (SRN) Woods-Saxon potential for the core-target interaction (taken from $^{10}$Be+$^{208}$Pb data), a surface-type Woods-Saxon LRN potential for the valence neutron-target interaction, and a CDE potential for Coulomb-induced excitation and breakup. They performed simultaneous $\chi^{2}$ analyses of elastic, inelastic, and breakup angular distribution data at two center-of-mass energies, using the CDE potential with a $B(E1)$ strength distribution and the LRN potential with adjustable parameters for radius, diffuseness, and potential depths.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energies analyzed: $E_{\text{c.m.}}=29.6$ MeV and $E_{\text{c.m.}}=37.1$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier for the system: $V_{B} \approx 37.9$ MeV
- First excitation energy of $^{11}$Be: $\varepsilon^{1st}_{x} = 0.32$ MeV, with Coulomb dipole strength $B(E1;\varepsilon^{1st}_{x})=0.115$ $e^{2}\text{fm}^{2}$
- Neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Be: $S_{n}=0.501$ MeV
- Normalization constant for the $dB(E1)/d\varepsilon$ distribution: $N=3.1$
- SRN potential parameters for $^{10}$Be+$^{208}$Pb: $V^{\text{sh}}_{0}=113$ MeV, $W^{\text{sh}}_{0}=169$ MeV, $a^{\text{sh}}_{0}=0.63$ fm, $a^{\text{sh}}_{W}=0.30$ fm, $r^{\text{sh}}_{0}=1.06$ fm, $r^{\text{sh}}_{W}=1.20$ fm
- LRN potential parameter set (A) at $E_{\text{c.m.}}=37.1$ MeV: $V^{\text{lo}}_{0}=-0.712$ MeV, $W^{\text{lo}}_{0}=0.0085$ MeV, $a^{\text{lo}}_{0}=a^{\text{lo}}_{W}=5.12$ fm, $r^{\text{lo}}_{0}=r^{\text{lo}}_{W}=1.5$ fm, $\chi^{2}=1.41$
- LRN potential parameter set (A) at $E_{\text{c.m.}}=29.6$ MeV: $V^{\text{lo}}_{0}=-0.203$ MeV, $W^{\text{lo}}_{0}=0.0094$ MeV, $a^{\text{lo}}_{0}=a^{\text{lo}}_{W}=7.58$ fm, $r^{\text{lo}}_{0}=r^{\text{lo}}_{W}=1.5$ fm, $\chi^{2}=0.73$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous optical model analysis of $^{11}$Be+$^{197}$Au (Heo et al., 2016) which only included inelastic channels without breakup cross sections. It builds on the dynamic polarization potential approach developed for halo nuclei reactions (PhysRevC.75.024610, PhysRevC.77.024609) and the CDE formalism from Andres, Gomez-Camacho, and Nagarajan (1994). The analysis uses recent experimental data from PhysRevLett.118.152502 (2017) which provided three-channel angular distributions. The results enable simultaneous description of elastic, inelastic, and breakup channels for weakly-bound halo projectiles near the Coulomb barrier, and demonstrate that a long-range nuclear potential is essential for reproducing breakup cross sections even when CDE alone is insufficient.
