# Hadron production in lepton-nuclei interactions at high energies: Monte Carlo generator HARDPING 2.0

> Hadron production in lepton-nucleus interactions at high-energies is considered in framework of developing Monte Carlo event generator HARDPING (HARD Probe INteraction Generator). Such effects as formation length, energy loss and multiple rescattering for produced hadrons and their constituents are implemented into the HARDPING 2.0. Available data from HERMES collaboration on hadron production in lepton-nucleus collisions are described by the present version of the HARDPING generator in a reasonable agreement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Monte Carlo event generator HARDPING 2.0 can describe HERMES data on hadron multiplicity ratios in lepton-nucleus collisions in "reasonable agreement," but this agreement is qualified by the fact that the model parameters (inelastic quark-nucleon cross-section and string tension) were fixed by fitting to those same data, and the comparison is shown only for krypton and xenon targets at a single beam energy.

## Method

The authors implemented a two-stage hadronisation model into the HARDPING 2.0 generator: a perturbative stage using PYTHIA parton shower with energy loss from gluon radiation, followed by a nonperturbative stage using the Lund string model. They incorporated formation length effects (split into pre-hadron and hadron formation lengths), energy loss during the perturbative stage, and multiple soft rescatterings of pre-hadrons and hadrons with intranuclear nucleons using a parameterised transverse momentum distribution and nuclear density profile. The generator was used to simulate positron-nucleus collisions at 27.6 GeV and compared to HERMES data on hadron multiplicity ratios.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic quark-nucleon (pre-hadron-nucleon) cross-section fixed at σ = 10 mb
- String tension fixed at k = 1.7 GeV/Fm
- Parameter C in the Lund-based formation length probability set to 0.3, following Accardi (2002)
- Soft rescattering parameter B = 2/⟨k_p⟩, where ⟨k_p⟩ is the mean quark transverse momentum
- Soft collisions defined by momentum transfers |t| < 1 GeV²
- Positron beam energy for all simulations: 27.6 GeV
- The first approach (Kopeliovich 2003) for formation length works only for leading hadrons with z_h > 0.5

## Context

This work extends the HARDPING generator, which previously described Drell-Yan reactions off nuclei (Berdnikov 2005, 2006), to hadron production in lepton-nucleus interactions. It combines and extends two prior approaches to hadronisation: the two-stage formation length model of Kopeliovich (2003) and the Lund string model approach of Accardi (2002), by including energy loss during the perturbative stage that both prior approaches neglected. The generator enables systematic Monte Carlo studies of nuclear effects in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, with the authors noting that comparisons to EMC data (Ashman 1991) and SLAC data (Benhar 1997, Degtyarenko 1994) also show good agreement and will be presented elsewhere.
