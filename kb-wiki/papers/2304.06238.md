# Enlarged deformation region in neutron-rich Zr isotopes by the second intruder orbit

> Nuclear deformations and density profiles of neutron-rich even-even Zr isotopes are investigated using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. Large quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations are predicted along with large enhancement of the total reaction cross sections at the neutron number $N=60$-74. Strong nuclear deformation starting at $N=60$ is induced by the occupation of the intruder orbit with the asymptotic quantum number $[nn_zΛ]Ω$ = [550]1/2 originating from the spherical $0h_{11/2}$ orbit. The deformation region is further enlarged from $N=72$ to 74 owing to the occupation of the next intruder orbit with [530]1/2 originating from the spherical $1f_{7/2}$ orbit. This characteristic nuclear deformation is crucially reflected in the systematic behavior of the nuclear radii and the density profiles near the nuclear surface.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the deformation region in neutron-rich Zr isotopes is enlarged from N=72 to N=74 due to the occupation of a "second" intruder orbit with asymptotic quantum number [530]1/2 originating from the spherical 1f7/2 orbit, in addition to the known deformation onset at N=60 from the [550]1/2 intruder orbit from 0h11/2.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the SkM* interaction and a standard mixed-type pairing interaction, implemented via the HFBTHO code assuming axial symmetry. They also performed Skyrme-HF plus BCS-type pairing (HF+BCS) in three-dimensional coordinate space and spherical-constrained HF calculations for comparison. The resulting intrinsic density distributions were averaged over angles to obtain laboratory-frame densities, which were then used as input to Glauber theory calculations of total reaction cross sections on a carbon target at 300 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 ≈ 0.4 for Zr isotopes from N=60 to N=74, with a sudden onset at N=60
- At N=76, the ground state deformation changes to oblate with β2 ≈ -0.2, and isotopes at N=78-82 become spherical again
- The total reaction cross section enhancement due to deformation is approximately 100 mb, corresponding to a relative change of about 4% (≈100 mb / 2500 mb)
- The quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.4283(317) deduced from E2 moment measurement for 101Zr (N=61) from Campbell (2002)
- Nuclear diffuseness a ≈ 0.7 fm in the deformed region, compared to the standard range of 0.5-0.6 fm for spherical nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deformation in the island-of-inversion regions around N=20 and N=40 (Warburton 1990, Lenzi 2010) to the Zr isotopic chain, where experiments have confirmed isotopes from 78Zr (Kienle 2001) to 114Zr (Sumikama 2021). The enlargement of the deformation region at N≈72 in Zr isotopes was seen in earlier calculations (Geng 2003, Miyahara 2018) but its mechanism via the second intruder orbit was not previously explained. The paper enables future systematic measurements of total reaction cross sections and elastic scattering cross sections to probe the characteristic behavior of nuclear deformation in heavy mass nuclei with A≳110.
