# Role of Coulomb-nuclear breakup of 6,7Li projectiles with heavy deformed 232Th target

> The significance of both Coulomb and nuclear couplings and their interference effects in the breakup processes of 6,7Li with a non-spherical nucleus 232Th has been evaluated. The continuum discretized coupled channel(CDCC) calculations are carried out in a nonstandard way, using short-range imaginary potentials for the fragment-target interaction at energies close to the Coulomb barrier. The present calculations employing short-range imaginary potentials exhibit better agreement with the experimental elastic scattering angular distributions than those using standard systematic value (0.78xWSPP ) used to describe elastic scattering. Including the excitation of the 232Th inelastic shows significant coupling effects on the elastic scattering below the barrier energies compared to higher incident energies. Subsequently, the CDCC framework was used to analyze the nuclear, Coulomb, and total breakup predictions separately. The breakup cross sections for the 6Li+232Th system are greater than those for the 7Li+232Th system across various energies. The present study predicts destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference in the breakup processes involving both 6Li and 7Li projectile nuclei with the deformed 232Th target. Additionally, the breakup reaction cross-sections are compared with experimentally measured fusion cross-sections near the barrier energies for both 6,7Li+232Th systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the breakup of weakly bound \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles on a deformed \(^{232}\)Th target, the Coulomb-nuclear interference is destructive, and this conclusion holds even for a non-spherical target, extending previous findings that were limited to spherical targets. The authors qualify that the standard CDCC method using a 0.78 scaling factor for the imaginary potential is less accurate than using short-range imaginary potentials for describing elastic scattering angular distributions.

## Method

Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations were performed using the FRESCO code, treating \(^{6,7}\)Li as two-cluster systems (\(\alpha\)+\(^{2}\)H and \(\alpha\)+\(^{3}\)H) with separation energies of about 1.47 MeV and 2.45 MeV, respectively. Two approaches were used for fragment-target interactions: a standard method (CDCC1) using the double-folding Sao Paulo Potential (SPP) with a 0.78 scaling factor for the imaginary part, and a nonstandard method (CDCC2) using short-range imaginary potentials with depth \(W_i=50\) MeV, radius \(r_i=1.06\) fm, and diffusivity \(a_i=0.2\) fm. The coupling potential was separated into Coulomb and nuclear components to isolate the Coulomb breakup cross-section (\(\sigma_{CBU}\)), nuclear breakup cross-section (\(\sigma_{NBU}\)), and the interference term (\(\sigma_{TBU} - (\sigma_{CBU} + \sigma_{NBU})\)).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{232}\)Th at \(E_{lab}=26\) MeV: \(\sigma_{CBU}=10.40\) mb, \(\sigma_{NBU}=0.64\) mb, \(\sigma_{TBU}=6.88\) mb, interference ratio \((\sigma_{TBU}-\sigma_{NBU})/\sigma_{CBU}=0.60\)
- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{232}\)Th at \(E_{lab}=44\) MeV: \(\sigma_{CBU}=54.58\) mb, \(\sigma_{NBU}=40.24\) mb, \(\sigma_{TBU}=74.21\) mb, interference ratio \((\sigma_{TBU}-\sigma_{NBU})/\sigma_{CBU}=0.62\)
- For \(^{7}\)Li+\(^{232}\)Th at \(E_{lab}=26\) MeV: \(\sigma_{CBU}=0.87\) mb, \(\sigma_{NBU}=0.004\) mb, \(\sigma_{TBU}=0.71\) mb, interference ratio \((\sigma_{TBU}-\sigma_{NBU})/\sigma_{CBU}=0.81\)
- For \(^{7}\)Li+\(^{232}\)Th at \(E_{lab}=44\) MeV: \(\sigma_{CBU}=12.88\) mb, \(\sigma_{NBU}=5.46\) mb, \(\sigma_{TBU}=13.3\) mb, interference ratio \((\sigma_{TBU}-\sigma_{NBU})/\sigma_{CBU}=0.61\)
- The quadrupole deformation parameter of \(^{232}\)Th is \(\beta_2=0.261\) (from Raman 2001)
- The short-range imaginary potential parameters used: \(W_i=50\) MeV, \(r_i=1.06\) fm, \(a_i=0.2\) fm
- Maximum continuum energy: 10 MeV for \(^{6}\)Li, 11 MeV for \(^{7}\)Li
- The ratio of Coulomb to nuclear breakup cross-sections exceeds unity for both systems and decreases with increasing incident energy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb-nuclear interference in breakup reactions, which were mostly performed on spherical targets (e.g., Otomar 2009, Otomar 2012), to the case of a deformed actinide target \(^{232}\)Th. The findings of destructive Coulomb-nuclear interference for \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles with a deformed target are consistent with earlier results for spherical targets such as \(^{208}\)Pb, but differ from observations for lighter targets like \(^{59}\)Co where the ratio of Coulomb to nuclear breakup falls below unity for \(^{7}\)Li. The study enables future theoretical work to incorporate both projectile breakup and target excitation simultaneously in CDCC calculations, which remains an open challenge.
