# Baryon Magnetic Moments in Alternate 1/N_c Expansions

> Recent work shows not only the necessity of a 1/N_c expansion to explain the observed mass spectrum of the lightest baryons, but also that at least two distinct large N_c expansions, in which quarks transform under either the color fundamental or the two-index antisymmetric representation of SU(N_c), work comparably well. Here we show that the baryon magnetic moments do not support this ambivalence; they strongly prefer the color-fundamental 1/N_c expansion, providing experimental evidence that nature decisively distinguishes among 1/N_c expansions for this observable.

## Key claim

The baryon magnetic moment spectrum strongly prefers the color-fundamental (F) \(1/N_c\) expansion over the two-index antisymmetric (AS) \(1/N_c\) expansion, providing experimental evidence that nature decisively distinguishes among \(1/N_c\) expansions for this observable, even though the baryon mass spectrum does not distinguish between them.

## Method

The authors computed matrix elements of a complete set of magnetic moment operators (up to 3-body level) for the ground-state baryon multiplet in both the \(1/N_c^{\rm F}\) and \(1/N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansions, using the single-photon ansatz where quarks couple proportionally to their electric charges. They then performed fits to the 9 measured magnetic moment observables (7 octet baryons, the \(\Omega^-\), and the \(\Sigma^0\Lambda\) transition moment, plus the \(\Delta^+ p\) transition moment and \(\Delta^{++}\) magnetic moment) and compared the naturalness of the fitted coefficients in each expansion.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta^+ p\) transition moment is \(\mu_{\Delta^+ p} = 3.51 \pm 0.09 \, \mu_N\) (extracted from \(\Delta \to N \gamma\) helicity amplitudes)
- The \(\Delta^{++}\) magnetic moment is \(\mu_{\Delta^{++}} = 6.14 \pm 0.51 \, \mu_N\) (from LopezCastro (2000))
- 9 magnetic moment observables are tabulated in the Review of Particle Physics (Nakamura (2010)): 7 of the 8 octet baryons (excluding \(\Sigma^0\)), the \(\Omega^-\), and the \(\Sigma^0\Lambda\) transition moment
- The \(1/N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansion generates effects too large to be consistent with the expansion, while the \(1/N_c^{\rm F}\) expansion produces coefficients of natural size \(O(1)\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cherman (2009) on baryon masses, which found that both the \(1/N_c^{\rm F}\) and \(1/N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansions fit the mass spectrum comparably well, to the magnetic moment observables. The operator basis for the \(1/N_c^{\rm F}\) expansion was established by Lebed (2004), and the present work adapts it to the \(1/N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansion by changing the 't Hooft scaling from \(1/N_c^n\) to \(1/N_c^{2n}\) and the combinatoric quark number from \(N_c\) to \(N_c(N_c-1)/2\). The result enables future work to use baryon magnetic moments as a discriminating observable between different large \(N_c\) generalizations of QCD.
