# Global optimization of harmonic oscillator basis in covariant density functional theory

> The present investigation focuses on the improvement of the accuracy of the description of binding energies within moderately sized fermionic basis. Using the solutions corresponding to infinite fermionic basis it was shown that in the case of meson exchange (ME) covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) the global accuracy of the description of binding energies in the finite $N_F=16-20$ bases can be drastically (by a factor ranging from $\approx 3$ up to $\approx 9$ dependent on the functional and $N_F$) improved by a global optimization of oscillator frequency of the basis. This is a consequence of the unique feature of the ME functionals in which with increasing fermionic basis size fermionic and mesonic energies approach the exact (infinite basis) solution from above and below, respectively. As a consequence, an optimal oscillator frequency $\hbarω_0$ of the basis can be defined which provides an accurate reproduction of exact total binding energies by the ones calculated in truncated basis. This leads to a very high accuracy of the calculations in moderately sized $N_F=20$ basis when mass dependent oscillator frequency is used: global rms differences $δB_{rms}$ between the binding energies calculated in infinite and truncated bases are only 0.025 MeV and 0.031 MeV for the NL5(Z) and DD-MEZ functionals, respectively. Optimized values of the oscillator frequency $\hbarω_0$ are provided for three major classes of CEDFs, i.e. for density dependent meson exchange functionals, nonlinear meson exchange ones and point coupling functionals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for meson exchange (ME) covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs), a global optimization of the harmonic oscillator frequency can drastically improve the accuracy of binding energies calculated in moderately sized fermionic bases (N_F = 16–20), reducing the difference from infinite-basis results by a factor of approximately 3 to 9 depending on the functional and basis size. This improvement relies on the unique feature of ME functionals where fermionic and mesonic energies approach the infinite-basis solution from above and below, respectively, allowing an optimal oscillator frequency to be defined.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using spherical and axially deformed computer codes, employing the DD-MEZ, NL5(Z), and PC-Z functionals as representatives of the density-dependent meson exchange (DDME), nonlinear meson exchange (NLME), and point coupling (PC) classes. They systematically varied the scaling factor f in the oscillator frequency formula ℏω₀ = f × 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV, comparing binding energies in finite fermionic bases (N_F = 10–40) against exact infinite-basis solutions obtained with N_F = 70–90 and N_B = 40 bosonic shells. The convergence patterns were analyzed for spherical nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb, ³⁰⁴120) and deformed nuclei (²⁴⁰Pu) across multiple functionals within each class.

## Key numbers

- Global rms differences δB_rms between binding energies in infinite and truncated N_F = 20 bases are 0.025 MeV for the NL5(Z) functional and 0.031 MeV for the DD-MEZ functional when using mass-dependent oscillator frequency.
- The improvement factor from global optimization ranges from approximately 3 up to approximately 9, dependent on the functional and N_F value.
- For the DD-MEZ functional, full convergence to infinite-basis binding energy in ²⁰⁸Pb is reached at N_F^conv = 52, 46, 38, 32, 36, 42, and 48 for scaling factors f = 0.8, 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, 1.5, 1.6, 1.8, and 2.0, respectively.
- The optimal scaling factor for NLME functionals is f = 1.2, providing best agreement with exact binding energies in ⁴⁸Ca (for N_F ≥ 12) and ²⁰⁸Pb (typically for N ≥ 18).
- For PC functionals, the difference between truncated (N_F = 20) and exact solutions is around 400 keV or more, with no optimal f value providing near-perfect reproduction at moderate N_F.

## Context

This work extends the recent extrapolation procedures to infinite fermionic and bosonic bases developed by the same group in Osei, Afanasjev, and Dalbah (2025) and Osei et al. (2024), which benchmarked the convergence of CDFT calculations. The standard oscillator frequency ℏω₀ = 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV, fixed since the work of Gambhir, Ring, and Thimet (1990) based on only ¹⁶O and ²⁰⁸Pb with the NL1 functional, is shown to be suboptimal for global calculations. The results enable highly accurate calculations in moderately sized N_F = 20 bases for ME functionals, reducing the need for computationally expensive larger bases or extrapolation procedures, and provide optimized oscillator frequencies for three major classes of CEDFs.
