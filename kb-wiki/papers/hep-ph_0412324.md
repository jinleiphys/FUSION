# NuTeV Anomaly & Strange-Antistrange Asymmetry

> The NuTeV Collaboration reported a value of $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$ measured in neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering, and found that the value is three standard deviations from the world average value of other electroweak measurements. If this result cannot be explained within conventional physics, it must imply some novel physics beyond the standard model. We report the correction from the asymmetric strange-antistrange sea by using both the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model and the chiral quark model, and show that a significant part of the NuTeV anomaly can be explained by the strange-antistrange asymmetry.

## Key claim
A significant part of the NuTeV anomaly, the three-standard-deviation discrepancy between the weak mixing angle measured in neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering and the world average, can be explained by a strange-antistrange asymmetry in the nucleon sea, without requiring new physics beyond the standard model. The correction depends on the choice of wave function model and the probability of the kaon-hyperon Fock state, reducing the discrepancy by 30–80% in the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model and by 60–100% in the chiral quark model.

## Method
The authors computed the second moment \(S^{-} = \int_0^1 x[s(x)-\bar{s}(x)]\,dx\) using two nonperturbative approaches: the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model (with Gaussian and power-law wave functions for the \(K^+\Lambda\) Fock state) and the chiral quark model. They then inserted \(S^{-}\) into the correction term \(\delta R^{-}_s\) to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, which shifts the extracted value of \(\sin^2\theta_w\). Valence quark distributions were obtained from a quark-diquark spectator model.

## Key numbers
- NuTeV reported \(\sin^2\theta_w = 0.2277 \pm 0.0013\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.0009\ (\text{syst})\), three standard deviations from the world average \(0.2227 \pm 0.0004\).
- In the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model, the second moment \(S^{-}\) ranges from 0.0042 to 0.0106 (Gaussian wave function) and from 0.0035 to 0.0087 (power-law wave function), for \(K^+\Lambda\) probabilities of 4% and 10%.
- The corresponding correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio is \(0.0017 < \delta R^{-}_s < 0.0041\) (Gaussian) and \(0.0014 < \delta R^{-}_s < 0.0034\) (power-law).
- The shift in \(\sin^2\theta_w\) reduces the discrepancy from 0.005 to 0.0033 (Gaussian, 4% probability) or to 0.0009 (Gaussian, 10% probability), and to 0.0036 or 0.0016 for the power-law wave function at the same probabilities.
- The chiral quark model explains 60–100% of the anomaly with cut-off parameters \(\Lambda_K = 900\) MeV and 1100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the debate over whether strange-antistrange asymmetry can resolve the NuTeV anomaly, directly contradicting the conclusion of Cao and Signal (2003) who used the meson cloud model and found the asymmetry too small to matter. It builds on the light-cone baryon-meson fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1997) and the chiral quark model of Weinberg (1979) and Manohar and Georgi (1984). The results suggest that future precision measurements of \(s(x)\) and \(\bar{s}(x)\) distributions could confirm the asymmetry and remove the need for beyond-standard-model explanations.
