# Constraints on quasinormal modes and bounds for critical points from pole-skipping

> We consider a holographic thermal state and perturb it by a scalar operator whose associated real-time Green's function has only gapped poles. These gapped poles correspond to the non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes of a massive scalar perturbation around a Schwarzschild black brane. Relations between pole-skipping points, critical points and quasinormal modes in general emerge when the mass of the scalar and hence the dual operator dimension is varied. First, this novel analysis reveals a relation between the location of a mode in the infinite tower of quasinormal modes and the number of pole-skipping points constraining its dispersion relation at imaginary momenta. Second, for the first time, we consider the radii of convergence of the derivative expansions about the gapped quasinormal modes. These convergence radii turn out to be bounded from above by the set of all pole-skipping points. Furthermore, a transition between two distinct classes of critical points occurs at a particular value for the conformal dimension, implying close relations between critical points and pole-skipping points in one of those two classes. We show numerically that all of our results are also true for gapped modes of vector and tensor operators.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for gapped quasinormal modes (QNMs) of a scalar operator in a holographic thermal state, the number of pole-skipping points constraining a mode's dispersion relation at imaginary momenta decreases by one for each higher level in the tower of QNMs, and that the radius of convergence of the derivative expansion about the lowest-lying gapped mode is bounded from above by the set of all pole-skipping points.

## Method

The authors consider a massive scalar field perturbation around a Schwarzschild black brane in AdS5, dual to a thermal state with a scalar operator whose retarded Green's function has only gapped poles. They compute the quasinormal mode spectrum by solving the scalar field equation of motion with ingoing boundary conditions at the horizon and Dirichlet conditions at the boundary, and identify pole-skipping points via a near-horizon expansion in ingoing Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates. The critical points of the spectral curve are found by solving the spectral curve equation and its derivative with respect to frequency simultaneously, and the radius of convergence of the derivative expansion about each gapped mode is determined by the distance to the nearest critical point.

## Key numbers

* For a scalar operator with conformal dimension Δ = 6, the pole-skipping points at frequencies ω = -i(2πT) and ω = -2i(2πT) are given by q*_{1,1} = (i/2)√(Δ² - 4Δ + 6) and q*_{j,2} = (i/2)√(Δ² - 4Δ + 12 + (-1)^j 2√2√(Δ² - 4Δ + 6)) for j = 1,2.
* A transition between two classes of critical points occurs at Δ_t ≈ 5.23 for the scalar operator, separating a regime of lowest-level-degeneracy (Re ω_c = 0) from a regime of level-crossing.
* The radius of convergence |q_c| for the derivative expansion about the lowest-lying QNM (n=1) and the second QNM (n=2) is computed for operator dimensions in the range 2 ≤ Δ ≤ 8, with |q_c^(2)| approximately linear in Δ and |q_c^(1)| showing a kink at Δ_t.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical points and convergence radii of derivative expansions from hydrodynamic (gapless) modes, studied by Withers (2018) and Grozdanov et al. (2019), to gapped (non-hydrodynamic) quasinormal modes in holographic systems. The results establish new relations between pole-skipping points, critical points, and the hierarchy of gapped QNMs, and show that the critical point is always enclosed within the circle passing through the nearest pole-skipping point in the Im ω - Im q² plane, providing an upper bound on the radius of convergence. The findings are numerically verified for vector and tensor operators as well, and the authors discuss implications for quantum chaos bounds proposed by Maldacena, Shenker, and Stanford (2015).
