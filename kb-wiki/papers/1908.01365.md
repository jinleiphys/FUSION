# Backward nucleon production by heavy baryonic resonances in proton-nucleus collisions

> The production of backward nucleons, $N(180^\circ)$, at $180^\circ$ in the nuclear target rest frame in proton-nucleus ($\mathrm{p}+A$) collisions is studied. The backward nucleons appearing outside of the kinematically allowed range of proton-nucleon ($\mathrm{p}+N$) reactions are shown to be due to secondary reactions of heavy baryonic resonances produced inside the nucleus. Baryonic resonances $R$ created in primary $\mathrm{p}+N$ reactions can change their masses and momenta due to successive collisions $R+N\rightarrow R +N $ with other nuclear nucleons. Two distinct mechanisms and kinematic restrictions are studied: the reaction $R+N\rightarrow N(180^\circ)+N$ and the resonance decay $R\rightarrow N(180^\circ)+π$. Simulations of $\mathrm{p}+A$ collisions using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model support these mechanisms and are consistent with available data on proton backward production.

## Key claim

Backward nucleons observed at 180 degrees in the nuclear target rest frame in proton-nucleus collisions, which are kinematically forbidden in binary proton-nucleon reactions, can be explained by secondary interactions of heavy baryonic resonances created in primary collisions, rather than requiring pre-existing multi-nucleon correlations or "grains" inside the nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed a kinematic analysis based on energy-momentum conservation to derive the maximal possible energy of backward nucleons produced via two mechanisms: (1) a reaction where a baryonic resonance R collides with a nucleon to produce a backward nucleon and another nucleon (R+N -> N(180 deg)+N), and (2) a resonance decay into a backward nucleon and a pion (R -> N(180 deg)+pi). They then simulated proton-nucleus collisions using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model for targets of He-4, C-12, and Pb-208 at projectile momenta of 6.9 GeV/c and 158 GeV/c, analyzing the contributions from different production sources.

## Key numbers

- The maximal kinetic energy of a backward nucleon produced with 2 nuclear nucleons involved (n=2) reaches an upper limit of approximately 0.24 GeV at infinite projectile momentum.
- The maximal kinetic energy of a backward nucleon produced with 3 nuclear nucleons involved (n=3) reaches an upper limit of approximately 0.63 GeV at infinite projectile momentum.
- The baryonic resonance masses required for backward nucleon production at the kinematic limit are in the region of 3-4 GeV for projectile momenta up to 10 GeV/c.
- The UrQMD simulations used 10^8 collision events for each proton-nucleus reaction at zero impact parameter.
- The backward proton spectra at kinetic energy E = E2* = 0.17 GeV in p+A collisions at p=6.9 GeV/c scale approximately as A^alpha with alpha = 2.46 for light nuclei (He to C) and alpha = 0.67 for heavy nuclei (C to Pb).
- The UrQMD results for p+Cu reactions at p=9.5 GeV/c show good agreement with experimental data from Frankel (1979).

## Context

This work extends the alternative model scenario proposed by Gorenstein (1976) and further developed in subsequent papers (Gorenstein 1977, Bogatskaya 1978, Bogatskaya 1979, Anchishkin 1981, Golubyatnikova 1984, Kalinkin 1989, Motornenko 2016), which attributes cumulative particle production to heavy hadronic states and their successive collisions with nuclear nucleons. This contrasts with earlier explanations based on short-range proton-neutron correlations in the nucleus (Amado 1976, Frankel 1976, Frankfurt 1977, Frankfurt 1981, Frankfurt 1988, Frankfurt 2008) or multi-nucleon "grains" (Baldin 1978, Burov 1976, Efremov 1976, Efremov 1987, Braun 2001). The results enable future experimental searches for new heavy hadron-like states by the NA61/SHINE collaboration at SPS CERN, GSI-HADES, and future FAIR-CBM and NICA-MPD experiments.
