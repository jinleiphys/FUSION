# Compact stars with a quark core within NJL model

> An ultraviolet cutoff dependent on the chemical potential as proposed by Casalbuoni {\it et al} is used in the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The model is applied to the description of stellar quark matter and compact stars. It is shown that with a new cutoff parametrization it is possible to obtain stable hybrid stars with a quark core. A larger cutoff at finite densities leads to a partial chiral symmetry restoration of quark $s$ at lower densities. A direct consequence is the onset of the $s$ quark in stellar matter at lower densities and a softening of the equation of state.

# Compact stars with a quark core within NJL model

## Key claim
Using a chemical-potential-dependent ultraviolet cutoff in the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, it is possible to obtain stable hybrid stars with a quark core, but only for a specific new cutoff parametrization (Λ₂) and only when the strange quark flavor is included; the stability depends on the parameter μ₀ being below approximately 360 MeV.

## Method
The authors introduce a chemical-potential-dependent ultraviolet cutoff into the su(3) NJL model with scalar-pseudoscalar and 't Hooft six-fermion interactions, using two parametrizations: Λ₁ from Baldo et al. (2007) and a new parametrization Λ₂ with a sigmoid-like increase. They enforce thermodynamic consistency via the Gorenstein-Yang prescription, impose β-equilibrium and charge neutrality for stellar quark matter, and construct hybrid star equations of state using a Maxwell construction with the GM1 hadronic model including hyperons. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain stellar configurations.

## Key numbers
- Standard NJL cutoff: Λ₀ = 631.4 MeV; current quark masses: m₀ᵤ = m₀ₔ = 5.6 MeV, m₀ₛ = 135.6 MeV
- Vacuum quark condensates: φᵥᵤ = φᵥₔ = (−246.7 MeV)³, φᵥₛ = (−266.9 MeV)³
- For Λ₂ with μ₀ = 350 MeV and a = 0.17: maximum gravitational mass = 1.456 M⊙, radius = 10.56 km, central energy density = 7.62 fm⁻⁴
- For Λ₂ with μ₀ = 400 MeV: maximum mass = 1.621 M⊙, radius = 12.00 km (maximum mass configuration lies in mixed phase)
- For Λ₁ with μ₀ = 350 MeV: maximum mass = 1.674 M⊙, radius = 11.64 km (mixed phase)
- For Λ₁ with μ₀ = 400 MeV: maximum mass = 1.701 M⊙, radius = 11.14 km (mixed phase)
- GM1 with hyperons (hadronic only): maximum mass = 1.705 M⊙, radius = 11.11 km, central density = 5.99 fm⁻⁴
- Onset density for quark phase with Λ₂ (μ₀ = 350 MeV): ρ_QP = 0.49 fm⁻³
- Onset density for quark phase with Λ₂ (μ₀ = 400 MeV): ρ_QP = 0.69 fm⁻³
- Effective bag pressure plateau in standard NJL: B_eff^(1/4) ≈ 161–163 MeV between 3ρ₀–5ρ₀

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Schertler et al. (1999) and Constança et al. (2003) which found that standard NJL models typically produce unstable hybrid stars, and by Baldo et al. (2007) who showed that a chemical-potential-dependent cutoff in su(2) NJL did not resolve the instability. The authors demonstrate that including the strange quark flavor with a rapidly-increasing cutoff (Λ₂) enables stable quark cores, unlike the Λ₁ parametrization. The results are compatible with most observed neutron star masses (≈1.45 M⊙) but cannot reproduce the highly massive pulsars PSR B1516+02B and PSR J1748-2021B if confirmed above 2 M⊙. The work enables future studies incorporating color superconductivity effects.
