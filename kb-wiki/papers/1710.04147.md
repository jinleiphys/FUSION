# Three-body scattering hypervolumes of particles with short-range interactions

> The low-energy scattering of three bosons or distinguishable particles with short-range interactions is characterized by a fundamental parameter, the three-body scattering hypervolume. Its imaginary part is directly related to the three-body recombination rate in a quantum gas consisting of such particles. We derive an analytical formula of it for weak interactions, and perform its first numerical calculations for bosons with a variable nonzero-range potential. For attractive interactions, we identify several three-body resonances at which the three-body scattering hypervolume becomes divergent or anomalously large.

## Key claim

The three-body scattering hypervolume \(D\) is a fundamental parameter for low-energy three-body scattering, and for attractive interactions it can become complex (with its imaginary part directly related to the three-body recombination rate) and exhibit divergent or anomalously large values at specific three-body resonances, which the abstract notes but does not specify are distinct from Efimov resonances and occur at finite scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical formula for \(D\) in the weak-interaction limit using the Born series expansion of the three-body wave function. They then perform the first numerical calculations of \(D\) for identical bosons interacting via a variable Gaussian potential \(V(r) = v_0 (\hbar^2 / m r_0^2) \exp(-r^2 / r_0^2)\), solving the zero-energy Schrodinger equation inside a large hypersphere and matching the wave function to asymptotic 111- and 21-expansions to extract \(D\) and the outgoing-wave coefficients \(C_{l u}\). For attractive potentials that support two-body bound states, the wave function includes outgoing dimer-plus-free-boson channels, leading to a complex \(D\).

## Key numbers

* For hard-sphere bosons in the strong-repulsion limit, the numerical results approach \(D_{\rm HS} \approx 1761.5 a^4\).
* For weak Gaussian interactions, the leading-order analytical formula is \(D / r_0^4 = (3/4) \pi^3 v_0^2 + O(v_0^3)\).
* A three-body resonance (simple pole) is found at \(v_0 \approx -2.1308\), where the scattering length is \(a \approx -4.38 r_0\) and the approximate formula near the pole is \(D / r_0^4 \approx [ -6.2 / (v_0 + 2.1308) + 56 ] \times 10^5\).
* Additional three-body resonances are identified at \(v_0 \approx -16.163\) and \(v_0 \approx -42.32\).
* At \(v_0 \approx -23.9\) (where \(a \approx 2.24 r_0\)), the imaginary part of \(D\) reaches a local minimum of \(|{\rm Im}(D)| \approx 0.194(10)\) in units of \(r_0^4\).
* Two-body s-wave bound states emerge at \(v_0 \approx -2.6840, -17.796, -45.574\); a d-wave bound state emerges at \(v_0 \approx -26.901\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of the three-body scattering hypervolume \(D\), originally introduced by Tan (2008) for hard-sphere bosons, to systems with finite-range interactions and to the complex plane where two-body bound states exist. It provides the first numerical calculations of \(D\) for a variable Gaussian potential, confirming the hard-sphere limit of Tan (2008) and the weak-interaction formula derived here. The identification of three-body resonances at finite scattering lengths (distinct from the Efimov effect, which occurs at diverging scattering lengths) enables future studies of Borromean states and metastable three-body states, and the method offers a novel route to compute three-body recombination rates channel by channel, complementing the effective field theory approach of Braaten, Hammer, and Mehen (2002).
