# Pasta Structures of Quark-Hadron Phase Transition in Proto-Neutron Stars

> We study the quark-hadron mixed phase in proto-neutron stars with the finite-size effects. In the calculations of pasta structures appeared in the mixed phase, the Gibbs conditions require the pressure balance and chemical equilibrium between two phases besides the thermal equilibrium. We find that the region of the mixed phase is limited due to thermal instability. Moreover, we study the effects of neutrinos to the pasta structures. As a result, we find that the existence of neutrinos make the pasta structures unstable, too. These characteristic features of the hadron-quark mixed phase should be important for the middle stage of the evolutions of proto-neutron stars.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark mixed phase in proto-neutron stars becomes mechanically unstable at high temperature (above about 60 MeV) and/or high neutrino fraction (above about 0.10), meaning that the "pasta" structures that appear in the mixed phase are suppressed under these conditions; the instability arises from different physical mechanisms for temperature versus neutrino trapping.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase using a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach including hyperons (Sigma- and Lambda), and the quark phase using the MIT bag model or a density-dependent bag model with u, d, s quarks. For the mixed phase, they assume non-uniform matter with various geometrical pasta structures (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) within a Wigner-Seitz cell, imposing Gibbs conditions for chemical equilibrium and pressure balance with a constant surface tension parameter of 40 MeV fm^-2, and compare the Helmholtz free energy among structures to determine the most favored configuration.

## Key numbers

- The range of temperature considered in proto-neutron stars is roughly 0 to 40 MeV, and the range of neutrino fraction is roughly 0 to 0.2 (citing Burrows 1986).
- The droplet structure is mechanically stable below T ~ 60 MeV, but the optimal radius shifts to larger values as temperature increases (for B = 100 MeV fm^-3, sigma = 40 MeV fm^-2).
- The droplet structure is mechanically stable below Y_nu_e ~ 0.10, with the optimal radius shifting to larger values as Y_nu_e increases (at n_B = 2.5 n_0, T = 10 MeV, using the density-dependent bag model).
- The s quark current mass is set to m_s = 150 MeV; the bag constant B is set to 100 MeV fm^-3 in the MIT bag model; in the density-dependent bag model, B_infinity = 50 MeV fm^-3, B_0 = 400 MeV fm^-3, and beta = 0.17.
- The baryon density for the temperature study is n_B = 2 n_0; for the neutrino study it is n_B = 2.5 n_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Maruyama et al. (2007, 2008) and Yasutake et al. (2009) on the finite-size effects in the hadron-quark mixed phase at zero temperature to include finite temperature and neutrino trapping relevant for proto-neutron stars. The results suggest that at the very first stage of proto-neutron stars (high temperature ~40 MeV and high neutrino fraction ~0.2), the quark-hadron phase transition is close to the Maxwell construction, while pasta structures appear only in the middle stage of evolution after cooling and deleptonization proceed; similar discussions appear in work by Pagliara (2009) and Hempel (2009) but without finite-size effects.
