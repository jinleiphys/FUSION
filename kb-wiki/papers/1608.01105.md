# Quartetting in odd-odd self-conjugate nuclei

> We provide a description of odd-odd self-conjugate nuclei in the sd shell in a formalism of collective quartets and pairs. Quartets are four-body structures carrying isospin T=0 while pairs can have either T=0 or T=1. Both quartets and pairs are labeled by the angular momentum J and they are chosen so as to describe the lowest states of 20Ne (quartets) and the lowest T=0 and T=1 states of 18F (pairs). We carry out configuration interaction calculations in spaces built by one quartet and one pair for 22Na and by two quartets and one pair for 26Al. The spectra that are generated are in good agreement with the shell model and experimental ones. These calculations confirm the relevance of quartetting in the structure of N=Z nuclei that had already emerged in previous studies of the even-even systems and highlight the role of J>0 quartets in the composition of the odd-odd spectra.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the low-lying spectra of odd-odd self-conjugate nuclei in the sd shell can be accurately described using a drastically reduced configuration space built from collective T=0 quartets and T=0 or T=1 pairs, provided that quartets with angular momentum J>0 (specifically J=2 and J=4) are included alongside the J=0 quartet.

## Method

The authors construct T=0 quartet creation operators from the lowest three states of 20Ne (J=0,2,4) and pair creation operators from the lowest three T=0 states (J=1,3,5) and T=1 states (J=0,2,4) of 18F, using the USDB interaction. They perform configuration interaction calculations in the m-scheme for 22Na using a basis of one quartet plus one pair, and for 26Al using a basis of two quartets plus one pair, with all building blocks fixed from the 20Ne and 18F calculations and not varied.

## Key numbers

- For 22Na, the ground state correlation energy (difference between ground state energies with and without interaction) increases progressively when adding J=2 and J=4 quartets to the J=0 quartet basis; the paper reports the correlation energy below each ground state in Fig. 2 and the relative error with respect to the shell model value in parentheses.
- For 26Al, the configuration space built by two quartets and one pair counts at most 238 states, compared to 63,094 states in the corresponding shell model m-scheme space.
- Excluding the J=1 T=0 pair from the 22Na calculation shifts the lowest J=1 state to an energy of 1.75 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quartet formalism for even-even N=Z nuclei (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2014-2016) to odd-odd systems, confirming the central role of quartetting in self-conjugate nuclei. The approach contrasts with full shell model calculations using USD/A/B interactions (Brown, 2006) by providing a much simpler and more intuitive description in drastically reduced spaces. The results enable future studies of odd N=Z+1 systems and highlight the crucial role of J>0 quartets that was already noted in even-even systems but proves essential for generating the correct odd-odd spectra.
