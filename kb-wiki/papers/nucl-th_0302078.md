# Effective Lagrangian and Dynamical Symmetry Breaking in the SU(2)XU(1) NJL Model

> Dynamical symmetry breaking and the formation of scalar condensates in the SU(2)XU(1) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with two coupling constants has been studied in the framework of the mean field approximation. The bosonization procedures of the model are performed using the functional integration method. The possibility of the spontaneous CP symmetry breaking in the model under consideration has been shown. The mass spectrum of the bound states of fermions, as well as the effective Lagrangian of interacting scalar and pseudoscalar mesons are obtained.

# Effective Lagrangian and Dynamical Symmetry Breaking in the SU(2)XU(1) NJL Model

## Key claim

Dynamical symmetry breaking and scalar condensate formation occur in the SU(2)XU(1) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with two coupling constants, and spontaneous CP symmetry breaking is possible when the pseudoscalar condensate \(\tilde{\Phi}_3\) acquires a nonzero vacuum expectation value, even in the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The author performs bosonization of the four-fermion NJL model using functional integration, introducing auxiliary scalar and pseudoscalar fields. Gap equations for the vacuum condensates are derived from the effective action in the mean-field approximation, and the mass spectrum of bosonic bound states is obtained by expanding the effective action to quadratic order in field fluctuations and diagonalizing the resulting mass matrix.

## Key numbers

- The gap equations possess nontrivial solutions (\(\Phi_0 \neq 0\), \(\tilde{\Phi}_3 \neq 0\)) when \(N_C F \Lambda > 2\pi^2\) and \(N_C G \Lambda > 2\pi^2\), where \(\Lambda\) is the momentum cutoff.
- The masses of the fields \(\tilde{\Phi}_1\) and \(\tilde{\Phi}_2\) are zero (Goldstone bosons), while the mass of the \(\tilde{\Phi}_3\) field is \(m_{33}^2 = 4g^2 \tilde{\Phi}_3^2\).
- The scalar field \(\Phi_0\) acquires mass \(m_{00}^2 = 4m_1^2 + \frac{2m_0 m^2}{m_0 - m_1}\), where \(m_1 = m_0 - f_0 \Phi_0\) and \(m^2 = m_1^2 + g_0^2 \tilde{\Phi}_3^2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu, 1961) by incorporating two independent coupling constants for scalar and pseudoscalar interactions, allowing for spontaneous CP violation through a nonzero pseudoscalar condensate. The approach follows the functional integration bosonization method of Pervushin and the mean-field techniques of Jona-Lasinio and Coleman. The results enable further study of CP-violating mechanisms in low-energy QCD and provide a framework for computing effective meson interactions, though the author notes that quark confinement requires additional model modifications beyond the four-fermion approximation.
