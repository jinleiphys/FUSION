# Pi-mesonic decay of the hypertriton

> The pi-mesonic decay of the hypertriton is calculated based on a hypertriton wavefunction and 3N scattering states, which are rigorous solutions of 3-body Faddeev equations using realistic NN and hyperon-nucleon interactions. The total pi-mesonic decay rate is found to be 92% of the free Lambda decay rate, which is close to the experimental data. Together with the nonmesonic decay the total life time of 3Lambda H is predicted to be 2.78 x 10^-10 sec which is 6 % larger than for the free Lambda particle. The differential decay rate is evaluated as a function of the pion momentum. The decay into the N+d+pi channel is stronger than in the 3N+pi channel in contrast to the situation for the nonmesonic decay. The ratio for the decay rate into 3He + pi^- to the decay rate into all channels including pi^- is found to be 0.40, which is close to the experimental value. We visualise the decay into the dominant channel p+d+pi^- in a Dalitz plot. Finally we compare the polarisation of the outgoing proton in free unpolarised Lambda-decay to the polarisation of 3He in unpolarised 3_Lambda H-decay and we compare the closely related asymmetry of pi^- emitted parallel and antiparallel with respect to the spin-direction for a polarised Lambda to the corresponding asymmetry for a polarised 3_Lambda H.

## Key claim

The total pi-mesonic decay rate of the hypertriton is found to be 92% of the free Lambda decay rate, which is close to experimental data; however, this result neglects the final state interaction of the pion and Coulomb forces, and the calculation uses a nonrelativistic reduction of the weak decay operator that introduces only a 2% shift for the free Lambda decay rate.

## Method

The authors solved 3-body Faddeev equations using the Nijmegen 93 NN potential and the Nijmegen hyperon-nucleon interaction to obtain rigorous hypertriton wavefunctions and 3N scattering states. They evaluated the pi-mesonic decay rates for three channels (3He + pi-, p + d + pi-, and p + p + n + pi-) using a nonrelativistic reduction of the weak Lambda to N + pi vertex operator, with the final state interaction among the three nucleons treated exactly but the pion-nucleon interaction neglected. The Faddeev equation for the 3N continuum states was solved in a partial wave representation with 102 channels for the hypertriton wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- Total pi-mesonic decay rate of the hypertriton is 92% of the free Lambda decay rate
- Total lifetime of 3Lambda H predicted to be 2.78 x 10^-10 sec, which is 6% larger than for the free Lambda particle
- Decay rate into 3He + pi- channel: Gamma^He = 9.36 x 10^8 sec^-1
- Ratio R = Gamma(3Lambda H -> pi- + 3He) / Gamma(3Lambda H -> all pi- meson modes) = 0.40
- Total mesonic decay rate: 3.5 x 10^9 sec^-1, corresponding to a mesonic-only lifetime of 2.9 x 10^-10 sec
- Total nonmesonic decay rate: 6.39 x 10^7 sec^-1 (1.7% of free Lambda decay rate)
- Pion momentum in the 3He channel: k_pi = 117.4 MeV/c
- Polarization of 3He in unpolarized hypertriton decay: P_3He = 0.134 (opposite sign to free Lambda decay polarization P_Lambda = -0.322)
- Free Lambda lifetime from relativistic calculation: 2.579 x 10^-10 sec; from nonrelativistic calculation: 2.624 x 10^-10 sec

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological calculations of hypertriton mesonic decay by Dalitz, Leon, and Kolesnikov and Kopylov, and more recently by Congleton, by using rigorous 3-body Faddeev solutions with modern baryon-baryon forces (Nijmegen 93 NN and Nijmegen YN interactions). The authors had previously investigated the nonmesonic decay channels (Kamada et al., 1996), and this paper completes the theoretical predictions for all decay channels of the hypertriton. The results enable comparison with experimental data on hypertriton lifetime and branching ratios, and the calculated polarization and asymmetry observables provide predictions that could be tested in future experiments.
