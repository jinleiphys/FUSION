# NRQCD Factorization and Velocity-dependence of NNLO Poles in Heavy Quarkonium Production

> We study the transition of a heavy quark pair from octet to singlet color configurations at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in heavy quarkonium production. We show that the infrared singularities in this process are consistent with NRQCD factorization to all orders in the heavy quark relative velocity v. This factorization requires the gauge-completed matrix elements that we introduced previously to prove NNLO factorization to order v ^2.

## Key claim
The infrared singularities in the transition of a heavy quark pair from a color-octet to a color-singlet state at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) are consistent with NRQCD factorization to all orders in the heavy quark relative velocity $v$, provided that gauge-completed matrix elements are used. This extends the previous proof of NNLO factorization to order $v^2$ to all powers in $v$, but only for the class of electric dipole transitions and without addressing spin-dependent operators.

## Method
The authors calculated the infrared poles of an eikonal infrared factor ${\cal E}^{(8\to 1)}$, which describes the long-distance evolution of a heavy quark pair from an octet to a singlet color configuration at NNLO ($\alpha_s^2$). They evaluated the relevant cut diagrams involving a three-gluon vertex (Fig. 2a) without expanding in the relative velocity $v$, using dimensional regularization in $4-2\varepsilon$ dimensions and an eikonal approximation for the heavy quarks and the light-like gauge-completing Wilson line.

## Key numbers
- The infrared pole for the velocity-dependent factor ${\cal I}^{(8\to 1)}(v,\varepsilon)$ is given by $\frac{\alpha_s^2}{4 \varepsilon} \left \{1- \frac{1}{2 f(|\vec{v}|)} \ln \left[\frac{1+ f(|\vec{v}|)}{1- f(|\vec{v}|)}\right] \right \}$, where $f(v) = 2v/(1+v^2)$ and $v$ is the relative velocity of the quark and antiquark in their center-of-mass frame.
- The color factor for the NNLO pole is $-\frac{N_c}{4} \left(N_c^2-1\right)$, where $N_c$ is the number of colors.
- The result at order $v^2$ (from a previous study) is ${\cal E}_2^{(8\to 1)}(v) = \frac{N_c}{4} \left(N_c^2-1\right) \alpha_s^2 \frac{1}{3 \varepsilon} v^2$, which is consistent with the expansion of the all-orders result.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous proof of NNLO factorization for NRQCD (Nayak, Qiu, Sterman, 2005) from order $v^2$ to all orders in the relative velocity $v$, specifically for the class of electric dipole transitions. It addresses a necessary condition for the NRQCD factorization theorem, which had not yet been fully proven. The result shows that the infrared poles are independent of the direction of the light-like Wilson line used to define gauge-completed matrix elements, which is a crucial step toward a complete proof of NRQCD factorization for heavy quarkonium production.
