# Weinberg Eigenvalues and Pairing with Low-Momentum Potentials

> The nonperturbative nature of nucleon-nucleon interactions evolved to low momentum has recently been investigated in free space and at finite density using Weinberg eigenvalues as a diagnostic. This analysis is extended here to the in-medium eigenvalues near the Fermi surface to study pairing. For a fixed value of density and cutoff Lambda, the eigenvalues increase arbitrarily in magnitude close to the Fermi surface, signaling the pairing instability. When using normal-phase propagators, the Weinberg analysis with complex energies becomes a form of stability analysis and the pairing gap can be estimated from the largest attractive eigenvalue. With Nambu-Gorkov Green's functions, the largest attractive eigenvalue goes to unity close to the Fermi surface, indicating the presence of bound states (Cooper pairs), and the corresponding eigenvector leads to the self-consistent gap function.

## Key claim

The pairing instability near the Fermi surface is signaled by Weinberg eigenvalues that increase arbitrarily in magnitude, and the pairing gap can be estimated from the largest attractive eigenvalue using a stability analysis with complex energies; however, this estimate is only a first approximation that works well in the weak-coupling limit where the gap is small compared to the Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors computed Weinberg eigenvalues for the kernel formed by the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential V_lowk multiplied by the in-medium two-particle and two-hole non-interacting Green's function, first using normal-phase propagators and then using Nambu-Gorkov propagators that incorporate pairing. For the normal-phase analysis, they solved the eigenvalue equation with complex energies to perform a stability analysis, extracting the pairing gap from the energy where the largest attractive eigenvalue crosses unity. For the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, they solved the eigenvalue equation at the Fermi surface and used the corresponding eigenvector as an initial guess for iterating the BCS gap equation to self-consistency.

## Key numbers

- For neutron matter in the 1S0 channel at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.0 fm^{-1} with cutoff Lambda = 2.0 fm^{-1}, the critical energy E_c where the largest attractive eigenvalue crosses unity gives an estimate of the BCS gap Delta_F.
- For the separable gaussian potential with coupling lambda = -1 fm and width alpha = sqrt(2) fm, the normal-phase stability analysis gave Delta_F approximately 0.604 MeV.
- The extracted gaps showed very weak cutoff dependence across the range Lambda = 1.6 fm^{-1} to 2.5 fm^{-1}, consistent with the BCS gap equation results of Hebeler and Schwenk (2006).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium Weinberg eigenvalue analysis of Bogner, Furnstahl, and collaborators (2006) from bulk nuclear matter properties to the pairing instability near the Fermi surface. The stability analysis method adapts the approach of Schrieffer (1964) for identifying bound states from the singularity structure of the in-medium T-matrix. The results confirm the weak cutoff dependence of the 1S0 pairing gap found by Hebeler and Schwenk (2006), suggesting that three-nucleon forces contribute little to pairing in this channel at the BCS level, and the method enables future investigation of particle-hole channel correlations and medium effects such as polarization.
