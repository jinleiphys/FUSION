# Robust correlations between quadrupole moments of low-lying $2^+$ states within random-interaction ensembles

> In the random-interaction ensembles, three proportional correlations between quadrupole moments of the first two $I^π=2^+$ states robustly emerge, including $Q(2^+_1)=\pm Q(2^+_2)$ correlations consistently with realistic nuclear survey, and the $Q(2^+_2)=-\frac{3}{7}Q(2^+_1)$ correlation, which is only observed in the $sd$-boson space. These correlations can be microscopically characterized by the rotational SU(3) symmetry and quadrupole vibrational U(5) limit, respectively, according to the Elliott model and the $sd$-boson mean-field theory. The anharmonic vibration may be another phenomenological interpretation for the $Q(2^+_1)=- Q(2^+_2)$ correlation, whose spectral evidence, however, is insufficient.

## Key claim
In random-interaction ensembles, three proportional correlations between quadrupole moments of the first two \(2^+\) states robustly emerge: \(Q(2^+_1)=\pm Q(2^+_2)\) correlations consistent with realistic nuclear survey, and a \(Q(2^+_2)=-\frac{3}{7}Q(2^+_1)\) correlation observed only in the \(sd\)-boson space. The paper shows that the \(\pm Q(2^+_1)\) correlations are microscopically characterized by rotational SU(3) symmetry, while the \(-\frac{3}{7}\) correlation corresponds to the vibrational U(5) limit, though the anharmonic vibration interpretation for the \(Q(2^+_1)=-Q(2^+_2)\) correlation lacks sufficient spectral evidence.

## Method
The study employed two-body random ensembles (TBRE) in both shell-model and interacting boson model (IBM1) frameworks. For the shell-model TBRE, four model spaces with four or six valence protons in either the \(sd\) or \(pf\) shell were considered (pseudo nuclei \(^{24}\)Si, \(^{26}\)S, \(^{44}\)Cr, \(^{46}\)Fe). For the IBM1 TBRE, \(sd\)-boson spaces were constructed for valence boson numbers \(N_b=12, 13, 14, 15\). For each pseudo nucleus, 1,000,000 sets of random interactions were generated, and quadrupole moment matrix elements of \(2^+_1\) and \(2^+_2\) states were calculated for those producing an \(I=0\) ground state. Statistical analysis was performed using the polar angle \(\theta = \arctan\{Q(2^+_2)/Q(2^+_1)\}\) to characterize proportional correlations, and the interaction signatures were compared with matrix elements of the SU(3) Casimir operator and the \(sd\)-boson mean-field theory.

## Key numbers
- The \(Q(2^+_2)=-\frac{3}{7}Q(2^+_1)\) correlation is observed in the IBM1 TBRE, with fitted peak positions around \(\theta^1 \approx -21^\circ\) and \(\theta^2 \approx -22^\circ\) for all boson numbers \(N_b=12\)–\(15\).
- For the shell-model TBRE, there is an 8% probability of \(|Q(2^+_2)/Q(2^+_1)|>10\) due to weak quadrupole collectivity.
- In the IBM1 TBRE, the \(Q(2^+_2)=\pm Q(2^+_1)\) peaks have fitted positions near \((\pm 45^\circ, \pm 45^\circ)\); for \(N_b=12\), the \(Q(2^+_2)=-Q(2^+_1)\) peak is at \((-42.05^\circ, -36.27^\circ)\) with intensity \(361 \times 10^2\) counts, and the \(Q(2^+_2)=Q(2^+_1)\) peak at \((40.19^\circ, 43.44^\circ)\) with intensity \(272 \times 10^2\) counts.
- The U(5) peak in IBM1 TBRE has intensity \(565 \times 10^2\) counts for \(N_b=12\), with peak position \((-21.27^\circ, -22.17^\circ)\).
- For the SU(3) limit of IBM1, the ratio \(Q^{\gamma}(2^+)/Q^{g}(2^+) = -(4N_b-3)/(4N_b+3)\) approaches \(-1\) as \(N_b \rightarrow \infty\).

## Context
This work extends previous random-interaction studies of nuclear collectivity, such as the predominance of \(I=0\) ground states (Johnson, 1998) and collective band structures (Bijker, 1999), by focusing specifically on quadrupole moment correlations. It is directly inspired by an experimental survey (Allmond, 2014) that demonstrated a global \(Q(2^+_2)=-Q(2^+_1)\) correlation across a wide range of masses and deformations. The paper provides a microscopic interpretation using the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and the \(sd\)-boson mean-field theory (IBM framework), and shows that the \(Q(2^+_2)=-\frac{3}{7}Q(2^+_1)\) correlation corresponds to the U(5) vibrational limit. The results enable future studies of how robust quadrupole collectivity emerges from random interactions, and clarify the conditions under which different \(Q\) correlations appear in fermionic versus bosonic model spaces.
