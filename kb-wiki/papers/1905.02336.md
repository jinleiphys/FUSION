# Charmonium-nucleon interactions from 2+1 flavor lattice QCD

> The charmonium-nucleon interaction is studied by the time-dependent HAL QCD method. We use a larger lattice volume and the relativistic heavy quark action for charm quark to obtain less systematic errors than those in our previous study. As a result, the sizable J/$ψ$N hyperfine splitting is observed, indicating that the spin-spin interaction is important to understand this system quantitatively. No J/$ψ$N or $η_c$N bound state is observed below the thresholds as in the previous results.

## Key claim
Using a larger lattice volume and the relativistic heavy quark (RHQ) action for the charm quark, the authors observe a sizable hyperfine splitting between the \(J=1/2\) and \(J=3/2\) \(J/\psi N\) interactions, which was not clearly seen in their previous study; however, no bound states are found for either \(J/\psi N\) or \(\eta_c N\) below threshold.

## Method
The time-dependent HAL QCD method was applied to compute the charmonium-nucleon four-point correlation function on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice with 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS collaboration. The effective central potentials for S-wave scattering were extracted using a derivative expansion truncated at lowest order, and the resulting potentials were fitted with two Gaussian functions to solve the radial Schrödinger equation for scattering phase shifts and low-energy parameters.

## Key numbers
- \(m_{J/\psi} = 3139(12)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_{\eta_c} = 3022(9)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_N = 1585(16)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(m_\pi = 700(2)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from two-point function fits on the \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a = 0.0907(13)\ \mathrm{fm}\) and spatial volume \(L = (2.90\ \mathrm{fm})^3\).
- Scattering length and effective range for \(J/\psi N\) (\(J=1/2\)): \(a = 0.656 \pm 0.071\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(r = 1.105 \pm 0.016\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Scattering length and effective range for \(J/\psi N\) (\(J=3/2\)): \(a = 0.380 \pm 0.048\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(r = 1.476 \pm 0.039\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Scattering length and effective range for \(\eta_c N\): \(a = 0.246 \pm 0.026\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(r = 1.703 \pm 0.045\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context
This work extends the previous HAL QCD study by Sugiura et al. (2017) by using a larger lattice volume (2.90 fm vs. 1.93 fm) and the RHQ action for the charm quark to reduce discretization errors, yielding a clearer hyperfine splitting. The results contradict the NPL collaboration (Beane et al., 2014), who reported a \(\eta_c N\) bound state with binding energy 19.7 MeV at a similar pion mass, and provide input for evaluating the QCD van der Waals framework applied to the \(P_c\) pentaquark states observed by LHCb. The next step is a coupled-channel analysis of the \(\bar{D}^{(*)} \Sigma_c^{(*)}\) channels to explore the hadronic molecular picture of the \(P_c\) resonances.
