# Two-body Photodisintegration of $^{4}$He with Full Final State Interaction

> The cross sections of the processes $^4$He($γ,p$)$^3$H and $^4$He($γ,n$)$^3$He are calculated taking into account the full final state interaction via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. This is the first consistent microscopic calculation beyond the three--body breakup threshold. The results are obtained with a semirealistic central NN potential including also the Coulomb force. The cross sections show a pronounced dipole peak at 27 MeV which lies within the rather broad experimental band. At higher energies, where experimental uncertainties are considerably smaller, one finds a good agreement between theory and experiment. The calculated sum of three-- and four--body photodisintegration cross sections is also listed and is in fair agreement with the data.

## Key claim
The cross sections for the two-body photodisintegration of 4He into p+3H and n+3He show a pronounced dipole peak at 27 MeV, favoring the strongly peaked experimental data sets over the flatter ones, but this result is obtained with a semirealistic central NN potential (MTI-III) and does not include a three-nucleon force.

## Method
The authors calculated the exclusive cross sections for 4He(γ,p)3H and 4He(γ,n)3He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, which avoids explicit calculation of continuum wave functions by solving localized Schrödinger-like equations. The four-nucleon dynamics were described with the MTI-III semirealistic central NN potential including the Coulomb force, and the wave functions were expanded in a basis of correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) with grand-angular quantum number up to Kmax=29. The transition matrix elements were evaluated using the Lanczos technique to handle the large Hamiltonian matrices.

## Key numbers
- The dipole peak in the cross sections occurs at 27 MeV photon energy.
- The calculation used a maximal grand-angular quantum number Kmax = 29 for the hyperspherical harmonic expansion.
- The Lorentz transform was computed with σI = 20 MeV at En,3He = 27.9 MeV for convergence testing.
- The cross sections were calculated up to 120 MeV photon energy (below the pion threshold).

## Context
This work extends the earlier total 4He photoabsorption calculation by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1997) and the reexamination by Barnea et al. (2001), which used the same MTI-III potential and LIT method but only computed the inclusive cross section. It is the first consistent microscopic calculation of the exclusive two-body breakup channels beyond the three-body breakup threshold, going beyond the earlier calculation by Ellerkmann et al. (1996) which was limited to energies below the three-body breakup threshold. The results enable separation of the total photoabsorption into two-body and three-plus-four-body breakup contributions, and provide a benchmark for future calculations with more realistic NN potentials and three-nucleon forces.
