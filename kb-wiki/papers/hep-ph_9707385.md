# Rho-Omega Mixing and the Pion Form Factor in the Time-like Region

> We determine the magnitude, phase, and $s$-dependence of $ρ$-$ω$ ``mixing'' in the pion form factor in the time-like region through fits to $e^+e^- \ra π^+ π^-$ data. The associated systematic errors in these quantities, arising from the functional form used to fit the $ρ$ resonance, are small. The systematic errors in the $ρ$ mass and width, however, are larger than previously estimated.

## Key claim

The magnitude, phase, and s-dependence of the effective rho-omega mixing matrix element extracted from e+e- -> pi+ pi- data are remarkably insensitive to the functional form used to parametrize the rho resonance, but the systematic errors in the rho mass and width are larger than previously estimated.

## Method

The authors fit the pion form factor F_pi(s) to e+e- -> pi+ pi- cross section data from threshold through sqrt(s) ≈ 923 MeV, using multiple parametrizations of the rho resonance (Gounaris-Sakurai and Heyn-Lang forms) that satisfy analyticity, unitarity, and the Fermi-Watson phase theorem. They extract the effective rho-omega mixing matrix element tilde-Pi_{rho omega}(s) by including it as a term proportional to 1/(s - m_omega^2 + i m_omega Gamma_omega) in the form factor, and test the sensitivity of extracted parameters to the choice of rho parametrization, to the inclusion of an s-dependent or complex mixing matrix element, and to the data set used.

## Key numbers

- Effective rho-omega mixing matrix element at s = m_omega^2: tilde-Pi_{rho omega}(m_omega^2) = -3500 ± 300 MeV^2, insensitive to rho parametrization
- s-dependence of mixing: tilde-Pi_{rho omega}' = 0.03 ± 0.04 (dimensionless, from expansion about m_omega^2)
- Imaginary part of mixing: Im[tilde-Pi_{rho omega}(m_omega^2)] = -300 ± 300 MeV^2, consistent with zero
- rho mass from selected fits (B, C, D): m_rho = 773 MeV (average)
- rho width from selected fits (B, C, D): Gamma_rho = 153 MeV (average)
- rho mass range across all fits: 763.1 to 774.2 MeV
- rho width range across all fits: 144.0 to 157.0 MeV
- chi^2/dof for fits to pi-pi phase shift data: 140/34 for fits B and C, 590/34 for fit E, 230/34 for fit F

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of rho-omega mixing by Barkov et al. (1985) and Heyn and Lang (1981), who extracted rho parameters from time-like pion form factor data, and by Maltman et al. (1996) and H. B. O'Connell et al. (1997), who discussed separating the mixing into direct omega -> 2pi and mixing-mediated contributions. The authors show that the effective mixing matrix element is robustly determined at -3500 ± 300 MeV^2, while the rho mass and width have systematic errors larger than previously estimated, and they demonstrate that fits constrained by the empirical pi-pi scattering length (a^1_1 = 0.038 ± 0.002 m_pi^{-3}) and by the measured l=1, I=1 phase shifts favor rho parameters near m_rho = 773 MeV and Gamma_rho = 153 MeV. This enables more reliable extraction of rho-omega mixing parameters for use in calculations of isospin violation in hadronic processes.
