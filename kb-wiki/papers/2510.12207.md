# Perturbative $χ$EFT calculations of the deuteron and triton up to N$^2$LO

> We extend previous studies of the deuteron and triton ground-state energies to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in chiral effective field theory, employing a power counting in which subleading interactions are treated perturbatively. Triton calculations are performed using the no-core shell model, and we demonstrate converged perturbative results for regulator cutoffs up to $Λ\approx 1200$ MeV. We analyze exceptional cutoffs in the $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1 \mathrm{-}^3D_1$ nucleon-nucleon channels and find a resulting cutoff dependence in the triton ground-state energy at N$^2$LO. The effect associated with the exceptional cutoff in the $^3P_0$ channel can be mitigated by redefining the leading-order wave function within the freedom allowed by the effective field theory. The same approach applied in the $^3S_1 \mathrm{-}^3D_1$ channel remedies the effect of this exceptional cutoff on the ground state energy of the deuteron, but not for the triton.

## Key claim

The authors extend perturbative chiral effective field theory calculations of the deuteron and triton ground-state energies to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) using the Long and Yang power counting, but find that exceptional cutoffs in the $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ nucleon-nucleon channels introduce cutoff dependence in the triton ground-state energy at N$^2$LO that cannot be fully mitigated by redefining the leading-order wave function.

## Method

The authors construct nucleon-nucleon potentials up to N$^2$LO in the Long and Yang power counting, where leading-order (LO) interactions are treated non-perturbatively and subleading interactions are treated perturbatively using distorted-wave perturbation theory. Triton ground-state calculations are performed using the no-core shell model (NCSM) formulated in Jacobi coordinates, with the LO Hamiltonian diagonalized exactly and subleading corrections computed via perturbation theory using the full LO spectrum. The low-energy constants (LECs) are calibrated to Nijmegen partial-wave analysis phase shifts, and exceptional cutoffs are analyzed by examining the determinant of the matrix relating LECs to renormalization conditions.

## Key numbers

- Triton calculations are performed for regulator cutoffs up to $\Lambda \approx 1200$ MeV, with convergence demonstrated for this range
- A limit-cycle-like divergence in the $^3P_0$ channel appears at $\Lambda = 679$ MeV
- An exceptional cutoff in the $^3P_0$ channel appears at $\Lambda = 670$ MeV
- An exceptional cutoff in the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel appears at $\Lambda = 930$ MeV and another at $\Lambda = 1340$ MeV
- The LO triton ground-state energy shows variation of approximately 1 MeV depending on whether S-wave LECs are calibrated to low-energy phase shifts versus the singlet scattering length and deuteron ground-state energy
- NCSM basis truncations up to $N_\mathrm{max} = 46$ are employed, with basis sizes of 19000 (partially antisymmetric) and 6336 (fully antisymmetric) states
- HO frequencies in the range $\omega \in [10\ \mathrm{MeV}, 125\ \mathrm{MeV}]$ are used

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative chiral EFT studies of the triton by Song (2016) at next-to-leading order (NLO) and of $^4$He, $^6$Li, and $^{16}$O by Yang (2020) also at NLO, now reaching N$^2$LO for the triton. The analysis of exceptional cutoffs builds on the theoretical framework developed by Gasparyan (2022), who identified that LECs for N$^2$LO contact interactions in the $^3P_0$ channel diverge at certain cutoff values, and on subsequent work by Peng (2024) and Yang (2024) showing that cutoff independence can be restored for NN scattering amplitudes by adjusting the LO wave function. The present work extends this analysis to the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel and to the three-nucleon system, finding that while the deuteron ground-state energy divergence at exceptional cutoffs can be mitigated by shifting the LO calibration phase shift, the same approach does not fully remedy the triton ground-state energy.
