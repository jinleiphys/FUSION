# Thermal production of sexaquarks in heavy-ion collisions

> We present new results on the thermal production yield of a hypothetical state made of six quarks $uuddss$ assuming its production in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN LHC. A state with this quark content and mass low enough to be stable against decay in timescales of the order of the age of the Universe, has been hypothesized by one of us (G.F.) and has been discussed as a possible dark matter candidate. In this work we address for the first time the thermal production rate that can be expected for this state in heavy-ion collisions at colliders. For this estimate we use a thermal model which has been shown to describe accurately the production of hadrons and nuclei in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energy. This estimate is of great relevance for sexaquark searches at colliders as well as for its consideration as a dark matter candidate and for the composition of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The thermal production yield of a hypothetical stable sexaquark (a six-quark state with quark content uuddss) in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC is estimated for the first time using a hadron resonance gas model with multicomponent hard-core repulsion, under the assumption that the sexaquark is produced thermally alongside other hadrons and nuclei.

## Method

The authors employed a hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with induced surface tension to account for hard-core repulsion among hadrons and light nuclei, solving a system of two coupled equations for pressure and surface tension. They calculated thermal particle number densities for each species using the grand canonical ensemble with vanishing chemical potentials (reflecting the near-perfect particle-antiparticle symmetry at LHC energies), then obtained total yields by accounting for strong decays via branching ratios. The sexaquark yield was computed for assumed masses of 1700 MeV and 1950 MeV and hard-core radii of 0.0 fm and 0.4 fm, and compared to thermal yields of deuterons and Omega hyperons as a function of temperature between 140 and 180 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For sexaquark mass of 1700 MeV at temperature 150 MeV: ratio of thermal sexaquark yield to thermal deuteron yield is about 1.45 for R=0 fm and about 1.00 for R=0.4 fm.
- For sexaquark mass of 1700 MeV at temperature 150 MeV: ratio of thermal sexaquark yield to thermal Omega hyperon yield is about 0.25 for R=0 fm and about 0.20 for R=0.4 fm.
- For sexaquark mass of 1950 MeV at temperature 150 MeV: ratio of thermal sexaquark yield to thermal deuteron yield is about 0.27 for R=0 fm and about 0.22 for R=0.4 fm.
- For sexaquark mass of 1950 MeV at temperature 150 MeV: ratio of thermal sexaquark yield to thermal Omega hyperon yield is about 0.05 for R=0 fm and about 0.04 for R=0.4 fm.
- The overall ratio of thermal sexaquarks to thermal light nuclei and strange hadrons varies between about 0.2 and 1.4 for sexaquark mass 1700 MeV, and between about 0.05 and 0.27 for sexaquark mass 1950 MeV, across the temperature range 140-180 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of a stable uuddss state (sexaquark) proposed by Farrar (2017, 2018) as a dark matter candidate, which differs from the H0 dibaryon originally discussed by Jaffe (1976) in that it is more compact (hard-core radius ~0.1-0.4 fm) and has a mass below 2 GeV, making it stable against decay on timescales of the age of the Universe. The thermal model used here has been previously applied to describe hadron and light nucleus production in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies by the same group (references KAB_Ref4, KAB_Ref5, KAB_Ref6new). The results enable future experimental searches for sexaquarks in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, and may inform considerations of the sexaquark's role as a dark matter candidate and in neutron star composition.
