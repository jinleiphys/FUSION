# Extended Optical Model Analyses of Elastic Scattering and Fusion Cross Sections for 6Li + 208Pb System at Near-Coulomb-Barrier Energies by using Folding Potential

> Based on the extended optical model approach in which the polarization potential is decomposed into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, simultaneous $χ^{2}$ analyses are performed for elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the $^{6}$Li+$^{208}$Pb system at near-Coulomb-barrier energies. A folding potential is used as the bare potential. It is found that the real part of the resultant DR part of the polarization potential is repulsive, which is consistent with the results from the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations and the normalization factors needed for the folding potentials. Further, it is found that both DR and fusion parts of the polarization potential satisfy separately the dispersion relation.

## Key claim
Using a double folding potential as the bare potential within the extended optical model, the authors simultaneously describe elastic scattering and fusion cross section data for the 6Li+208Pb system without needing the arbitrary normalization factor (N=0.5-0.6) required in conventional optical model analyses, and they show that the threshold anomaly appears in the fusion part of the polarization potential rather than in the total potential.

## Method
The authors performed simultaneous chi-squared analyses of elastic scattering angular distributions and fusion cross sections for 6Li+208Pb at five near-Coulomb-barrier energies (Elab=29-39 MeV). They decomposed the polarization potential into direct reaction (DR) and fusion parts, using a double folding M3Y potential as the bare potential, with the DR potential taken as a surface-derivative Woods-Saxon form and the fusion potential as a volume Woods-Saxon form. Semi-experimental DR cross sections were generated by subtracting experimental fusion cross sections from optical-model-derived total reaction cross sections, and the energy-dependent strength parameters were determined in a two-step fitting procedure that enforced the dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- Threshold energies: E0,D(semi-exp)=20.5 MeV (c.m.) for direct reactions and E0,F(exp)=26.0 MeV (c.m.) for fusion, indicating DR channels open at lower energies than fusion channels.
- At the strong absorption radius Rsa (12.27-12.75 fm), the ratio R=Vtot/V0 ranges from 0.19 to 0.37, compared to 0.51 from CDCC calculations and 0.59 from conventional folding model normalization.
- The DR imaginary strength WD(E) saturates at 1.25 MeV for E>29.0 MeV (c.m.), while the fusion imaginary strength WF(E) saturates at 3.40 MeV for E>30.5 MeV (c.m.).
- The inner imaginary potential WI contributes 14-25% of the total theoretical fusion cross section, ranging from 5 mb at 28.2 MeV to 100 mb at 37.9 MeV (c.m.).

## Context
This work extends the extended optical model approach previously applied by So et al. (2004) and Kim et al. (2002) to the 6Li+208Pb system, but now using a double folding potential as the bare potential instead of a shallow real potential. The results are consistent with CDCC calculations by Keeley and Rusek (1998) which showed a repulsive real polarization potential at the surface, and resolve two long-standing problems identified by Satchler and Love (1979) and Keeley et al. (1994): the need for arbitrary normalization of folding potentials and the absence of threshold anomaly for loosely bound projectiles. The approach enables future application to other systems such as 7Li+208Pb, where conventional analyses have been successful.
