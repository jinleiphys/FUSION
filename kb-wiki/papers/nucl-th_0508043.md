# Thermal Radiation from Au + Au Collisions at \sqrt{s} = 200 GEV/A Energy

> The transverse momentum distribution of the direct photons measured by the PHENIX collaboration in $Au + Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/A has been analyzed. It has been shown that the data can be reproduced reasonably well assuming a deconfined state of thermalized quarks and gluons with initial temperature more than the transition temperature for deconfinement inferred from lattice QCD. The value of the initial temperature depends on the equation of state of the evolving matter. The sensitivities of the results on various input parameters have been studied. The effects of the modifications of hadronic properties at non-zero temperature have been discussed.

## Key claim

The PHENIX direct photon data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV can be reproduced by assuming a deconfined state of thermalized quarks and gluons, but the extracted initial temperature depends on the equation of state (EOS) and on whether in-medium modifications of hadronic properties are included; the authors obtain a conservative lower limit of \(T_i = 300\) MeV using a lattice QCD EOS, while a bag-model-type EOS yields \(T_i \sim 400\) MeV.

## Method

The authors model the space-time evolution of the fireball using (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry. Photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are taken from the complete order-\(\alpha_s\) calculation of Arnold et al. (2001), and hadronic-phase photon rates are computed from an exhaustive set of reactions and decays (including \(\pi\pi \rightarrow \rho\gamma\), \(\pi\rho \rightarrow \pi\gamma\), and radiative decays of \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and other mesons) with in-medium mass reductions following Brown-Rho (BR) scaling. The initial temperature is varied to fit the PHENIX direct photon transverse momentum spectrum for central Au+Au collisions, using two different equations of state: a bag-model type (Type I) and a lattice QCD type (Type II).

## Key numbers

*   Initial temperature \(T_i = 400\) MeV with thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.2\) fm reproduces the PHENIX data when using Type I (bag-model) EOS and including in-medium hadronic mass modifications.
*   Initial temperature \(T_i = 300\) MeV with \(\tau_i = 0.5\) fm reproduces the data when using Type II (lattice QCD) EOS.
*   The freeze-out temperature required to reproduce pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra is \(T_f \sim 120\) MeV.
*   The average temperature extracted from the slope of the photon \(p_T\) spectrum in the range \(1.25 < p_T < 2.25\) GeV is \(T_{av} \sim 265\) MeV; when flow is ignored, the "true" average temperature is 215 MeV.
*   Photon production from \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory in the strong coupling limit is enhanced by about \(20\%\) compared to thermal QCD at \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV.
*   Increasing the transition temperature from 170 MeV to 190 MeV yields a \(20\%\) enhancement in total thermal photon production at \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of direct photon data from the PHENIX collaboration at RHIC, following earlier theoretical studies of photon spectra at SPS energies by Alam et al. (2001, 2002) and at RHIC by Peressounko et al. (2005). The authors show that the initial temperature required to fit the data is sensitive to the equation of state and to in-medium modifications of hadronic masses, with the BR scaling scenario yielding a lower \(T_i\) than the QHD model. The analysis enables future comparisons with dilepton spectra in the same framework and highlights the need for \(pp\) and \(p(d)+\)Au photon data to validate the NLO pQCD baseline.
