# Final state interactions and the extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries from SIDIS by a transversely polarized $^3$He target

> The semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off transversely polarized $^3$He, i.e. the process, $e + \vec{^3 {\rm He}} \to e' + h+X$, with $h$ a detected fast hadron, is studied beyond the plane wave impulse approximation. To this end, a distorted spin-dependent spectral function of a nucleon inside an A=3 nucleus is actually evaluated through a generalized eikonal approximation, in order to take into account the final state interactions between the hadronizing system and the (A-1) nucleon spectator one. Our realistic description of both nuclear target and final state is a substantial step forward for achieving a reliable extraction of the Sivers and Collins single spin asymmetries of the free neutron. To illustrate how and to what extent the model dependence due to the treatment of the nuclear effects is under control, we apply our approach to the extraction procedure of the neutron single spin asymmetries from those measured for $^3$He for values of the kinematical variables relevant both for forthcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab and, with an exploratory purpose, for the future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that final state interactions (FSI) between the hadronizing system and the spectator two-nucleon system in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off transversely polarized \(^3\)He can be accounted for using a generalized eikonal approximation, and that the resulting distorted spin-dependent spectral function enables a more reliable extraction of neutron Sivers and Collins single spin asymmetries than the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) alone, though the model dependence from nuclear effects must still be carefully controlled.

## Method

The authors evaluate a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for a nucleon inside \(^3\)He by incorporating final state interactions through a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), extending previous work that only included the deuteron spectator state to now include all possible two-nucleon spectator states (both two-body and three-body break-up channels). They then apply this formalism to the standard SIDIS process \(e + \vec{^3\mathrm{He}} \to e' + h + X\) with kinematical conditions relevant for Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV and for the future Electron Ion Collider, computing the nuclear hadronic tensor and the resulting single spin asymmetries for the extraction of neutron TMDs.

## Key numbers

* The \(^3\)He target is approximately 90% an effective neutron target, allowing access to neutron TMDs.
* The calculations use the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction for the realistic description of nuclear dynamics.
* The formalism is applied for kinematical variables relevant to experiments at Jefferson Lab with beam energy of 6 GeV (already performed) and after the 12 GeV upgrade, as well as for the future Electron Ion Collider.
* The distorted spin-dependent spectral function includes contributions from both the two-body break-up channel (with state density \(\rho_{2bbu} = 1/(2\pi)^3\)) and the three-body break-up channel (with state density \(\rho_{3bbu} = 1/(2\pi)^6 \cdot m_N\sqrt{m_N\epsilon^*_2}/2\)).

## Context

This work extends the PWIA analysis of SIDIS off transversely polarized \(^3\)He performed by the same group (Scopetta et al., 2016) by adding final state interactions, and builds upon the distorted spin-dependent spectral function approach introduced for spectator SIDIS with a detected deuteron (Del Dotto et al., 2016). The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron Sivers and Collins asymmetries from upcoming Jefferson Lab 12 GeV experiments and future EIC measurements, and the authors note that a comparison with results from a polarized \(^3\)H target (as proposed in the MARATHON experiment at JLab) could provide an experimental check of the nuclear effect description.
