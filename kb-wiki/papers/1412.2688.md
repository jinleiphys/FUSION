# QCD Factorization and PDFs from Lattice QCD Calculation

> In this talk, we review a QCD factorization based approach to extract parton distribution and correlation functions from lattice QCD calculation of single hadron matrix elements of quark-gluon operators. We argue that although the lattice QCD calculations are done in the Euclidean space, the nonperturbative collinear behavior of the matrix elements are the same as that in the Minkowski space, and could be systematically factorized into parton distribution functions with infrared safe matching coefficients. The matching coefficients can be calculated perturbatively by applying the factorization formalism on to asymptotic partonic states.

## Key claim

The authors propose that parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be extracted from lattice QCD calculations of single-hadron matrix elements using a QCD factorization approach, provided the probe's momentum transfer \(Q\) is large compared to \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) to ensure factorization validity; this is an approximation that neglects corrections suppressed by inverse powers of \(Q\), distinct from the \(1/P_z\) expansion in Ji's large-momentum effective field theory.

## Method

The method defines lattice "cross sections" as Fourier transforms of Euclidean-space hadronic matrix elements of quark-gluon operators, requiring that these quantities be calculable in lattice QCD, infrared safe in lattice perturbation theory, and that all collinear divergences of their continuum limit factorize into PDFs with perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. The factorization relation is applied to partonic states to derive the matching coefficients, and the PDFs are then extracted from lattice "data" via a global analysis analogous to traditional QCD global fits. As a case study, the authors examine whether Ji's quasi-PDFs satisfy the criteria for good lattice "cross sections," demonstrating factorization of collinear divergences and one-loop matching coefficients.

- The factorization scale \(\mu\) and the renormalization scale \(\tilde{\mu}\) appear in the coefficient function via a term \(\ln(\tilde{\mu}^2/\mu^2)\) multiplied by \((1+t^2)/(1-t)\).
- The hadron momentum \(P_z\) is required to be large, of order \(Q\) or larger, where \(Q \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) ensures the validity of the factorization approximation.

## Context

This work extends the standard QCD factorization framework used for extracting PDFs from high-energy scattering data (e.g., Collins (1989), Gao (2013), Alekhin (2013)) to lattice QCD calculations, replacing physical cross sections with Euclidean-space lattice "cross sections." It contrasts with Ji's (2013) quasi-PDF approach and large-momentum effective field theory (Ji, 2014), which rely on a \(1/P_z\) expansion rather than a large-virtuality expansion. The method enables extraction of PDFs for mesons or exotic baryons without performing high-energy scattering on those states, and could provide valuable information in the valence region, especially near \(x\to 1\) where experimental data are limited (Peng, 2014).
