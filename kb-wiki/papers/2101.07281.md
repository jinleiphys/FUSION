# Z_c -like spectra from QCD Laplace sum rules at NLO

> We present a global analysis of the observed Z_c, Z_cs and future Z_css-like spectra using the inverse Laplace transform (LSR) version of QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) within stability criteria. Integrated compact QCD expressions of the LO spectral functions up to dimension-six condensates are given. Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) factorized perturbative contributions are included. We re-emphasize the importance to include PT radiative corrections (though numerically small) for heavy quark sum rules in order to justify the (ad hoc) definition and value of the heavy quark mass used frequently at LO in the literature. We also demonstrate that, contrary to a naïve qualitative 1/N_c counting, the two-meson scattering contributions to the four-quark spectral functions are numerically negligible confirming the reliability of the LSR predictions. Our results are summarized in Tables III to VI. The Z_c(3900) and Z_cs(3983) spectra are well reproduced by the T_c(3900) and T_cs(3973) tetramoles (superposition of quasi-degenerated molecules and tetraquark states having the same quantum numbers and with almost equal couplings to the currents). The Z_c(4025) or Z_c(4040) state can be fitted with the D*_0D_1 molecule having a mass 4023(130) MeV while the Z_cs bump around 4.1 GeV can be likely due to the (D^*_s0D_1+ D^*_0D_s1) molecules. The Z_c(4430) can be a radial excitation of the Z_c(3900) weakly coupled to the current, while all strongly coupled ones are in the region (5634-6527) MeV. The double strange tetramole state T_css which one may identify with the future Z_css is predicted to be at 4064(46) MeV. It is remarkable to notice the regular mass-spliitings of the tetramoles due to SU(3) breakings M_{T_cs}-M_{T_c}= M_{T_css}-M_{T_cs= (73- 91) MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed Z_c and Z_cs exotic hadron spectra can be reproduced by "tetramoles" (superpositions of quasi-degenerate molecules and tetraquark states with the same quantum numbers and almost equal couplings to the currents), but this interpretation relies on the inclusion of Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) perturbative corrections to justify the heavy quark mass definition, and on the demonstration that two-meson scattering contributions to the four-quark spectral functions are numerically negligible despite qualitative large-N_c counting arguments suggesting otherwise.

## Method

The authors used the inverse Laplace sum rule (LSR) version of QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) within stability criteria, including LO spectral functions up to dimension-six condensates and NLO factorized perturbative contributions. They constructed QCD local currents of dimension-six for 1+ axial-vector molecules and tetraquarks, then extracted masses and couplings by analyzing τ-stability, t_c-stability, and μ-stability of the sum rules using a minimal duality ansatz (one resonance plus QCD continuum).

## Key numbers

- Z_c(3900) mass: reproduced by the T_c(3900) tetramole at 3900 MeV (used as input to fix coupling)
- Z_cs(3983) spectrum: reproduced by the T_cs(3973) tetramole
- D*_0D_1 molecule mass: 4023(130) MeV, fitting the Z_c(4025) or Z_c(4040) state
- Double strange tetramole state T_css (future Z_css candidate): predicted at 4064(46) MeV
- SU(3) breaking mass splittings: M_{T_cs} - M_{T_c} ≈ M_{T_css} - M_{T_cs} ≈ (73-91) MeV
- Strongly coupled radial excitations region: (5634-6527) MeV
- Optimal subtraction constant μ = (4.65 ± 0.05) GeV from μ-stability analysis
- t_c stability range: from 22 GeV² to 38 GeV², with mean at 30 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QSSR analyses of XYZ states (Albuquerque, Narison, 2018; Narison, 2016) by applying the same LSR methodology to the newly observed Z_cs candidate from BESIII (2020) and predicting the double-strange Z_css state. It contradicts the large-N_c argument of Lucha (2019) that non-factorized contributions dominate, demonstrating instead that such contributions are numerically negligible. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted Z_css state around 4064 MeV and provide a unified "tetramole" framework for interpreting the Z_c-like spectra.
