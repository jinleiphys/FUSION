# Excited $B_{c}$ States via Continuum QCD

> We study the most recently observed excited $B_{c}$ states with the Dyson-Schwinger equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation approach of continuum QCD. The obtained $M_{B^+_{c}(2S)}=6.813(16)\text{GeV}$, $M_{B^{*+}_{c}(2S)}=6.841(18)\text{GeV}$ and the mass splitting $M_{B_c^+(2S)}-M^{\text{rec}}_{B_c^{*+}(2S)} \approx 0.039 \text{GeV}$ agree with the observations very well. Moreover we predict the leptonic decay constant $f_{B^+_{c}(2S)}=-0.165(10)\text{GeV}$, $f_{B^{*+}_{c}(2S)}=-0.161(7)\text{GeV}$ respectively.

## Key claim

Using the Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach of continuum QCD with a flavor-dependent quark-gluon interaction kernel, the authors obtain masses of the first radial excited $B_c$ states that agree with recent CMS and LHCb observations to within about 1%, but the results rely on a Padé extrapolation scheme because the excited states lie outside the parabolic region accessible by direct calculation.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow-ladder truncated Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations for the $(c\bar{b})$ system, using a gluon propagator model with a flavor-dependent infrared part parameterized by $\omega_f$ and $D_f$ (three parameter sets from Chen (2019)). Since the radial excited states have masses larger than the contour border $M_{max}$ defined by singularities in the quark propagators, the authors extrapolate the Bethe-Salpeter eigenvalue $\lambda^{fg}(P^2)$ and the decay constant $f^{fg}(P^2)$ using Padé approximants of order [1,1] to reach the physical on-shell point $P^2 = -M^2$.

## Key numbers

- $M_{B^+_{c}(2S)} = 6.813(16)\text{ GeV}$ (computed; experimental value $6.872(2)\text{ GeV}$ from LHCb)
- $M_{B^{*+}_{c}(2S)} = 6.841(18)\text{ GeV}$ (predicted; no experimental value yet)
- Mass splitting $M_{B_c^+(2S)}-M^{\text{rec}}_{B_c^{*+}(2S)} \approx 0.039\text{ GeV}$ (computed; experimental value $0.031\text{ GeV}$ from LHCb)
- Leptonic decay constant $f_{B^+_{c}(2S)} = -0.165(10)\text{ GeV}$ (predicted)
- Leptonic decay constant $f_{B^{*+}_{c}(2S)} = -0.161(7)\text{ GeV}$ (predicted)
- Current quark masses used: $\check{m}_{c}=1.31\text{ GeV}$, $\check{m}_{b}=4.27\text{ GeV}$

## Context

This work extends the continuum QCD approach of Binosi (2018) and Chen (2019), which successfully described ground-state open-flavor mesons and quarkonia, to the first radial excited states of the $B_c$ system. It provides the first direct continuum QCD calculation of the masses and leptonic decay constants of $B_c^+(2S)$ and $B_c^{*+}(2S)$, complementing lattice QCD studies (Mathur 2018) which have not yet addressed excited-state decay constants. The results enable future experimental searches for the leptonic decays of these excited states and test the equal spacing rule for heavy meson masses.
