# Role of the crust on the tidal deformability of a neutron star within a unified treatment of dense matter

> The role of the crust on the tidal deformability of a cold nonaccreted neutron star is studied using the recent unified equation of state BSk24. This equation of state, which is based on the nuclear-energy density functional theory, provides a thermodynamically consistent description of all stellar regions. Results obtained with this equation of state are compared to those calculated for a putative neutron star made entirely of homogeneous matter. The presence of the crustal layers is thus found to significantly reduce the Love number $k_2$, especially for low-mass stars. However, this reduction mainly arises from the increase in the stellar radius almost independently of the equation of state. This allows for a simple analytic estimate of $k_2$ for realistic neutron stars using the equation of state of homogeneous matter only.

## Key claim

The crust reduces the Love number \(k_2\) of a neutron star, especially for low-mass stars, but this reduction arises mainly from the increase in the stellar radius and is almost independent of the equation of state; consequently, the tidal deformability parameter \(\Lambda\) can be accurately estimated using only the equation of state of homogeneous matter, with deviations from the full crust-included calculation never exceeding 0.75%.

## Method

The authors used the unified equation of state BSk24, based on nuclear-energy density functional theory, to compute the structure and tidal deformability of cold nonaccreted neutron stars with a full crust. They then constructed a second, artificial equation of state by extending the homogeneous core EoS to low densities using the same functional, and computed the same quantities for stars made entirely of homogeneous matter. By comparing the two sets of results, they isolated the effect of the crust on the Love number \(k_2\) and the tidal deformability \(\Lambda\), and derived an analytic approximation for \(k_2\) that uses only the homogeneous-matter EoS and the stellar radius.

## Key numbers

* For a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star, the relative deviation in the tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) between the full crust-included calculation and the homogeneous-matter-only calculation is 0.6%.
* The crust increases the stellar radius by at most 6% for a one-solar-mass star.
* The crust-core transition occurs at a density \(n_\textrm{cc} = 0.081\) fm\(^{-3}\) (or \(\rho_\textrm{cc} = 1.36 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)).
* The neutron-drip transition occurs at a density \(n_\textrm{nd} = 2.56 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) (or \(\rho_\textrm{nd} = 4.25 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)).
* For a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star, the analytic approximation using only the homogeneous-matter EoS yields relative errors on \(k_2\) and \(\Lambda\) of about -0.17% and -0.82%, respectively.
* The LIGO-Virgo constraint on the tidal deformability of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star is \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}\) at 90% confidence level, and the BSk24 EoS is consistent with this constraint.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Piekarewicz (2019), Kalaitzis (2019), and Ji (2019) that examined the role of the crust on tidal deformability, but those studies used ad hoc matching of different crust and core equations of state and reported widely different estimates for the crust's contribution to \(\Lambda\) (ranging from 0.1% to about 11% for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star). By using a thermodynamically consistent unified equation of state (BSk24) that avoids such matching uncertainties, the authors find a much smaller crust effect (0.6% for \(\Lambda\)) and provide an analytic method to compute \(k_2\) and \(\Lambda\) from homogeneous-matter EoS alone. This enables more reliable extraction of neutron-star radii from gravitational-wave observations and reduces systematic errors from crustal modeling.
