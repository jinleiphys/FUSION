# Chiral extrapolations of nucleon properties from lattice QCD

> We report on recent work about the study of quark mass dependence of nucleon magnetic moments and axial-vector coupling constant. We examine the feasibility of chiral effective field theory methods for the extrapolation of lattice QCD data obtained at relative large pion masses down to the physical values.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) that explicitly includes the Δ(1232) resonance as a degree of freedom can successfully extrapolate lattice QCD results for nucleon magnetic moments and the axial-vector coupling constant from pion masses above 0.6 GeV down to the physical pion mass, whereas truncated heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with only pion and nucleon degrees of freedom fails for these observables.

## Method

The authors computed the isovector and isoscalar magnetic moments of the nucleon and the axial-vector coupling constant g_A to one-loop order in two versions of chiral effective field theory: heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory restricted to pion and nucleon degrees of freedom, and an extended version incorporating the Δ(1232) with the Δ-N mass difference treated as a small scale. The calculations used leading-order NΔ transition Lagrangians with vector and axial-vector couplings, and the free parameters were fixed by fitting to quenched lattice QCD data at large pion masses, with additional constraints from πN → ππN data for the g_A analysis.

## Key numbers

- The empirical proton magnetic moment μ_p = 2.793 and neutron magnetic moment μ_n = -1.913 are reproduced by the χEFT extrapolation from lattice data at pion masses m_π ≳ 0.6 GeV.
- The empirical axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.267 is reproduced by the χEFT extrapolation including the Δ(1232), using lattice data from the QCDSF-UKQCD collaboration.
- The isovector anomalous magnetic moment expression contains a term -g_A^2 M/(4π f_π^2) m_π, where M is the nucleon mass and f_π is the pion decay constant.
- The isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment is analytic in the quark mass: κ_S = κ_S^0 - const · m_π^2.
- The g_A expression contains a term -(g_A^0)^3/(4π f_π)^2 m_π^2, where g_A^0 is the chiral limit value.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory to connect lattice QCD results obtained at unphysically large quark masses with physical observables, following earlier lattice studies by the QCDSF-UKQCD collaboration and others. The authors compare their approach with a Padé fit extrapolation method reported in prior lattice work, finding close agreement for magnetic moments. The explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance, motivated by the Adler-Weisberger sum rule which relates g_A to πN scattering cross sections dominated by Δ excitation, enables successful extrapolation of g_A where truncated chiral perturbation theory fails. This enables future lattice QCD calculations at moderate pion masses to be reliably connected to experimental nucleon properties.
