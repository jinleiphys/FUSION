# Method of unitary clothing transformations in the theory of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The clothing procedure, put forward in quantum field theory (QFT) by Greenberg and Schweber, is applied for the description of nucleon-nucleon (N-N) scattering. We consider pseudoscalar, vector and scalar meson fields interacting with fermion ones via the Yukawa-type couplings to introduce trial interactions between "bare" particles. The subsequent unitary clothing transformations (UCTs) are found to express the total Hamiltonian through new interaction operators that refer to particles with physical (observable) properties, the so-called clothed particles. In this work, we are focused upon the Hermitian and energy-independent operators for the clothed nucleons, being built up in the second order in the coupling constants. The corresponding analytic expressions in momentum space are compared with the separate meson contributions to the one-boson-exchange potentials in the meson theory of nuclear forces. In order to evaluate the T-matrix of the N-N scattering we have used an equivalence theorem that enables us to operate in the clothed particle representation (CPR) instead of the bare particle representation (BPR) with its large amount of virtual processes. We have derived the Lippmann-Schwinger type equation for the CPR elements of the T-matrix for a given collision energy in the two-nucleon sector of the Hilbert space of hadronic states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the unitary clothing transformation (UCT) method, applied to nucleon-nucleon (N-N) scattering with pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson fields, yields Hermitian and energy-independent quasipotentials for clothed nucleons in second order in the coupling constants, which can be compared to one-boson-exchange potentials; however, the work is limited to the second-order approximation and does not yet provide numerical phase-shift results, only the formal derivation of the integral equations.

## Method

The authors start from a quantum field theory Hamiltonian with Yukawa-type couplings between fermions (nucleons and antinucleons) and meson fields (pion, eta, rho, omega, delta, sigma). They apply unitary clothing transformations to eliminate "bad terms" that prevent bare vacuum and one-particle states from being Hamiltonian eigenstates, thereby obtaining a clothed-particle representation (CPR) where the interaction operator \(K_I\) contains only processes between physical particles. In the second order in coupling constants, they derive analytic momentum-space expressions for the N-N quasipotentials \(V_b\) for each meson type. They then derive a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation for the T-matrix in the CPR and reduce it to an R-matrix equation, performing an angular-momentum decomposition to obtain coupled integral equations for partial-wave matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The quasipotential for scalar mesons is given by \(v_s(1',2';1,2) = -\frac{g_s^2}{2} \bar u(\vec p'_1) u(\vec p_1) \frac{1}{(p_1 - p'_1)^2 - m_s^2} \bar u(\vec p'_2) u(\vec p_2)\) (Eq. 20).
- The quasipotential for pseudoscalar mesons is \(v_{ps}(1',2';1,2) = \frac{g_{ps}^2}{2}\bar u(\vec p'_1) \gamma_5 u(\vec p_1) \frac{1}{(p_1 - p'_1)^2 - m_{ps}^2} \bar u(\vec p'_2) \gamma_5 u(\vec p_2)\) (Eq. 21).
- The one-pion-exchange direct contribution includes the factor \(\frac12 \left\{ \frac{1}{(p_1 - p'_1)^2 - m_{\pi}^2} + \frac{1}{(p_2 - p'_2)^2 - m_{\pi}^2} \right\}\) (Eq. dir).
- The collision energy in the center-of-mass system is \(2E_p = 2\sqrt{\vec p^2 + m^2}\).
- The on-shell density factor is \(\rho(p) = pE_p/2\).
- The partial-wave R-matrix equation for uncoupled states is \(R_{JJ}^{JS=0T}(p',p) = V_{JJ}^{JS=0T}(p',p) + \frac{1}{2} {\rm P} \int_0^\infty \frac{q^2 dq}{E_p - E_q} V_{JJ}^{JS=0T}(p',q)R_{JJ}^{JS=0T}(q,p)\) (Eq. 55).

## Context

This work extends the clothing procedure of Greenberg and Schweber, previously developed by Shebeko and collaborators (Shebeko and Shirokov 2001; Korda, Canton, and Shebeko 2007), to the specific problem of nucleon-nucleon scattering. It provides a field-theoretic alternative to traditional one-boson-exchange potentials such as the Bonn potential (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster 1987; Machleidt 2001), Paris potential (Lacombe et al. 1980), and CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt 2001), as well as chiral effective field theory approaches (Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner 2000). The derived energy-independent quasipotentials in the CPR avoid the "unpleasant feature of being energy-dependent" that complicates applications to nuclear structure, and the formalism enables future numerical solution of the partial-wave R-matrix equations to compute N-N phase shifts and mixing parameters.
