# Form-factor dependence of the J/psi dissociation cross sections in meson exchange model

> The $J/ψ$ dissociation by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons is examined in meson exchange model and compared to the quark interchange model. We found that the main difference between the predictions of the two models could be resolved by the form factors and the contact interactions in meson exchange model calculations. By adopting covariant form factors and adjusting the four-point couplings, we found that the meson exchange model could give similar predictions with the quark interchange models not only for the magnitudes but also for the energy dependence of the low-energy dissociation cross sections of the $J/ψ$ by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons. Our finding suggests a way to understand one of the discrepancies among the existing model predictions on the $J/ψ$ dissociation cross sections by light hadrons.

# Form-factor dependence of the J/ψ dissociation cross sections in meson exchange model

## Key claim

By adopting covariant form factors and adjusting the four-point couplings, the meson exchange model can reproduce both the magnitudes and the energy dependence of the low-energy J/ψ dissociation cross sections by π and ρ mesons as predicted by the quark interchange model, resolving one of the main discrepancies between the two model approaches. The authors emphasize that this agreement requires the inclusion of dominant contact terms (four-point vertices) and that the SU(4) symmetry relations used in previous meson exchange calculations are strongly violated in this scenario.

## Method

The authors compute J/ψ dissociation cross sections for π + J/ψ and ρ + J/ψ using an effective meson Lagrangian with SU(4) flavor symmetry, including t- and u-channel D and D* meson exchange diagrams and four-point contact interactions. They replace the non-covariant form factors used in earlier work (Oh et al., 2001; Lin and Ko, 2000) with covariant form factors of the Pearce-Jennings type, which depend on the four-momentum squared of the exchanged particle and are normalized to unity on-shell. Two models are considered: model (I) with only three-point vertices, and model (II) which includes four-point contact terms with couplings treated as free parameters to fit the quark interchange model results of Wong et al. (2000-2002).

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_{D* D π} is determined from CLEO data as 12.6 ± 1.4, used to fix related couplings via heavy quark symmetry relations.
- For model (I) with covariant form factors, the cutoff parameter Λ = 1.8 GeV is used for π + J/ψ and Λ = 2.3 GeV for ρ + J/ψ.
- For model (II) with contact terms, the cutoff parameter Λ = 1.2 GeV is used for π + J/ψ and Λ = 1.0 GeV for ρ + J/ψ.
- The four-point couplings used in model (II) to match the quark interchange model include: g_{ψ D D π} = 23, g_{ψ D* D π} = 34, g_{ψ D* D* π} = h_{ψ D* D* π} = 7, g_{ψ D D ρ} = 39, g_{ψ D* D ρ} = h_{ψ D* D ρ} = 17, and g_{ψ D* D* ρ} = 27.
- The OZI-violating coupling g_{ψ ρ π} = 1.07 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1} gives cross sections at the order of pb or fb, which are negligible.

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange model calculations of Oh et al. (2001), Haglin (2000), and Lin and Ko (2000) for J/ψ dissociation by light hadrons, which previously showed significant discrepancies with the quark interchange model of Wong et al. (2000-2002) and with perturbative QCD results. The authors show that the choice of form factors is crucial because the range of heavy meson exchange is much smaller than the sizes of the initial hadrons, and that covariant form factors with dominant contact terms can reconcile the two approaches. This finding suggests a way to understand discrepancies among existing model predictions and enables more reliable calculations of J/ψ dissociation cross sections needed for interpreting J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions as a signal of quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors note that future work should include contributions from higher resonances such as the axial vector D₁(2420) in the meson exchange model for more detailed comparison.
