# Towards Quantum Transport for Central Nuclear Reactions

> Nonequilibrium Green's functions represent a promising tool for describing central nuclear reactions. Even at the single-particle level, though, the Green's functions contain more information that computers may handle in the foreseeable future. In this study, we explore slab collisions in one dimension, first in the mean field approximation and demonstrate that only function elements close to the diagonal in arguments are relevant, in practice, for the reaction calculations. This bodes well for the application of the Green's functions to the reactions. Moreover we demonstrate that an initial state for a reaction calculation may be generated through adiabatic transformation of interactions. Finally, we report on our progress in incorporating correlations into the dynamic calculations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for mean-field slab collisions in one dimension, only Green's function elements close to the diagonal in their arguments are practically relevant for reaction calculations, which bodes well for reducing the computational cost of applying nonequilibrium Green's functions to central nuclear reactions in three dimensions.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadanoff-Baym equations in the mean-field limit for one-dimensional slab collisions, using a split-operator method with Fast-Fourier-Transform to move between configuration and momentum representations. They prepare initial states through adiabatic switching of the Hamiltonian from a harmonic oscillator to a mean-field form, and test the importance of off-diagonal density matrix elements by applying an imaginary superoperator potential that suppresses elements away from the diagonal.

## Key numbers

- Discretization involving 50 values in any space-time direction would require 50^8 = 4 × 10^13 matrix elements, of order 100 TB of data, for every function.
- Slab collisions at c.m. energy 0.1 MeV/nucleon lead to fusion; at energies between 0.6 and 15 MeV/nucleon slabs pass through each other; at energies above 15 MeV/nucleon the system undergoes multifragmentation.
- For slab collisions at 25 MeV/nucleon, suppressing matrix elements at |x - x'| > 5 fm begins to produce subtle changes in density at late times, while suppression at |x - x'| > 20, 15, and 10 fm leaves no visible signs in density evolution.
- Adiabatic switching with τ = 40 fm/c produces a stationary interacting final state for a slab with N_s = 2 filled shells starting at t = -1000 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the application of nonequilibrium Green's functions, previously explored in Rios et al. (2011), toward central nuclear reactions where time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) becomes inadequate at higher energies due to nuclear transparency, and Boltzmann-equation models face theoretical difficulties with the quasiparticle picture as noted by Danielewicz (1984). The results enable progress toward practical three-dimensional reaction simulations by showing that far off-diagonal elements can be discarded, and by demonstrating that rotated numerical meshes explored in Barker (2013) can decouple momentum and position resolution scales.
