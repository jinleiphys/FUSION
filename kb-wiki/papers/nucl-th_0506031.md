# Two pion mediated scalar isoscalar NN interaction in the nuclear medium

> We study the modification of the nucleon nucleon interaction in a nuclear medium in the scalar isoscalar channel, mediated by the exchange of two correlated ($σ$ channel) or uncorrelated pions. For this purpose we use a standard approach for the renormalization of pions in nuclei. The corrections obtained for the $NN$ interaction in the medium in this channel are of the order of 20% of the free one in average, and the consideration of short range correlations plays an important role in providing these moderate changes. Yet, the corrections are sizable enough to suggest further studies of the stability and properties of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The medium modification of the scalar-isoscalar NN interaction from correlated and uncorrelated two-pion exchange is about 20% of the free interaction on average, but this moderate result depends critically on including short-range correlations; without them the renormalization would be huge.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian and compute the in-medium modification of the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) using a standard p-wave pion self-energy with particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations, including a Landau-Migdal parameter g'=0.6 to account for short-range correlations. They then extend this to the two-pion exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel, treating both the correlated (sigma channel) exchange via a unitarized pi-pi amplitude from chiral unitary approaches, and the uncorrelated two-pion exchange, with the pion propagators and vertex functions renormalized in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

* The corrections to the NN interaction in the scalar-isoscalar channel are of the order of 20% of the free interaction in average.
* The Landau-Migdal parameter used is g'=0.6.
* The sigma meson pole in vacuum is at mass and width M - i Gamma/2 approximately 450 - i 221 MeV.
* Normal nuclear matter density is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
* The monopole form factor cutoff is Lambda = 1 GeV.
* The pion decay constant is f_pi approximately 93 MeV.
* The axial coupling constant is g_A = F + D approximately 1.27, with F approximately 0.51 and D approximately 0.76.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of medium-modified two-pion exchange by Muther (1980) and by Rapp and collaborators (1995-1997), who investigated the role of chiral constraints and short-range correlations. It builds on the chiral unitary approach to pi-pi scattering (Oller, Oset, 1997-1998) which treats the sigma meson as a dynamically generated resonance rather than a genuine q-qbar state. The results motivate further calculations of nuclear matter binding energy and stability using these medium-modified interactions, and can be implemented in many-body schemes such as Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone or correlated basis functions.
