# Interplay between antimagnetic and collective rotation in Fe-58

> The self-consistent tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory based on the point- coupling interaction PC-PK1 is applied to investigate the possible existence of antimagnetic ro- tation in the nucleus Fe-58. The observed data for Band 3 and Band 4 are reproduced well with two assigned configurations. It is found that both bands correspond to a rotation of antimagnetic character, but, due to the presence of considerable deformation, the interplay between antimag- netic rotation and collective motion plays an essential role. In particular for Band 4, collective rotation is dominant in the competition with antimagnetic rotation. Moreover, it is shown that the behavior of the ratios between the dynamic moments of inertia and the B(E2) values reflects the interplay between antimagnetic and collective rotation.

## Key claim

Both Band 3 and Band 4 in Fe-58 exhibit antimagnetic rotation character, but due to considerable deformation (β = 0.28 and 0.34 at the bandhead, respectively), collective rotation provides a significant contribution to the total angular momentum; for Band 4, collective rotation is dominant over the antimagnetic rotation component.

## Method

The authors applied self-consistent tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) with the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1, solving the Dirac equation in three-dimensional harmonic oscillator bases with 10 major shells. Two valence nucleon configurations were assigned: π f⁻²₇/₂ ⊗ ν[g¹₉/₂(fp)³] for Band 3 (Config 1) and π f⁻²₇/₂ ⊗ ν[g²₉/₂(fp)²] for Band 4 (Config 2), with pairing correlations neglected. The self-consistent rotational angle was determined by requiring the rotational vector ω to be parallel with the total angular momentum J at fixed frequency.

## Key numbers

- Band 3 (Config 1): β = 0.28, γ = 14.3° at bandhead (ħω = 0.2 MeV); angular momentum range I ∼ 6–16 ħ above 5 MeV excitation energy
- Band 4 (Config 2): β = 0.34, γ = 1.4° at bandhead (ħω = 0.2 MeV); angular momentum range I ∼ 10–14 ħ above 8 MeV excitation energy
- For Config 1: neutron alignment contributes 3.35 ħ (55%) and the two-shears-like mechanism contributes 2.74 ħ (45%) to the total angular momentum increment
- For Config 2: collective rotation accounts for approximately 73% of the total angular momentum increment (neutron angular momentum jumps by more than 6 ħ from ħω = 0.2 to 1.1 MeV, while proton hole alignment provides less than 4 ħ)
- Calculated B(E2) values are very small (< 0.1 e²b²) and decrease smoothly with spin for both configurations
- Ground state deformation: β = 0.24, γ = 15.8°
- Energy splitting of time-reversal conjugate states reaches up to 1.75 MeV at ħω = 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of antimagnetic rotation from the A ∼ 100–110 region (Cd isotopes studied by Simons (2005), Choudhury (2010, 2013), and others) and Pd isotopes (Sugawara (2012, 2015), Rather (2014)) into the lighter A ∼ 60 mass region using Fe-58. The calculations build on the TAC-CDFT framework previously applied to antimagnetic rotation by Zhao (2011, 2012) and to magnetic rotation in the same nucleus by Steppenbeck (2012). The results enable future experimental measurements of B(E2) values in Fe-58 to validate the predicted interplay between antimagnetic and collective rotation, and suggest that the J⁽²⁾/B(E2) ratio can serve as a diagnostic for distinguishing the relative contributions of these two rotation modes.
