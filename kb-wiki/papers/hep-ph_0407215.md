# Two- and three-body color flux tubes in the Chromo Dielectric Model

> Using the framework of the Chromo Dielectric Model we perform an analysis of color electric flux tubes in meson-like $q\bar{q}$ and baryon-like $qqq$ quark configurations. We discuss the Abelian color structure of the model and point out a symmetry in color space as a remnant of the SU(3) symmetry of QCD. The generic features of the model are discussed by varying the model parameters. We fix these parameters by reproducing the string tension $τ=980 $MeV/fm and the transverse width $ρ=0.35 $fm of the $q\bar{q}$ flux tube obtained in lattice calculations. We use a bag constant $B^{1/4}=(240-260) $MeV, a glueball mass $m_g = (1000-1700) $MeV and a strong coupling constant $C_F α_s = 0.2-0.3$. We show that the asymptotic string profile of an infinitely long flux tube is already reached for $q\bar{q}$ separations $R\ge1.0 $fm. A connection to the Dual Color Superconductor is made by extracting a magnetic current from the model equations and a qualitative agreement between the two descriptions of confinement is shown. In the study of the $qqq$ system we observe a $\mathsfΔ$-like geometry for the color electric fields and a \textsf{Y}-like geometry in the scalar fields both in the energy density distribution and in the corresponding potentials. The resulting total $qqq$ potential is described neither by the $\mathsfΔ$-picture nor by the \textsf{Y}-picture alone.

## Key claim

Using the Chromo Dielectric Model (CDM) with parameters fixed to reproduce lattice results for the mesonic flux tube, the three-quark (baryonic) potential is described neither by the pure Δ-geometry nor by the pure Y-geometry alone, even though the color electric fields show a Δ-like geometry and the scalar fields show a Y-like geometry in the energy density and potentials.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled static equations of motion for two Abelian color electric potentials and the scalar confinement field σ in the CDM, treating quarks as static external sources with a finite Gaussian width (r₀ = 0.02 fm). They varied the model parameters (bag constant B, glueball mass m_g, strong coupling C_F α_s, and the dielectric function κ(σ)) to reproduce the lattice string tension τ = 980 MeV/fm and transverse width ρ = 0.35 fm of the qq̅ flux tube, then extended the calculation to three-quark configurations to study the geometry of the resulting flux tubes and potentials.

## Key numbers

- String tension τ = 980 MeV/fm and transverse width ρ = 0.35 fm of the qq̅ flux tube were used to fix model parameters.
- Bag constant B^(1/4) = 240–260 MeV, glueball mass m_g = 1000–1700 MeV, strong coupling C_F α_s = 0.2–0.3.
- Asymptotic string profile of an infinitely long flux tube is already reached for qq̅ separations R ≥ 1.0 fm.
- Quark source width r₀ = 0.02 fm was used for numerical calculations.
- Dielectric constant in the non-perturbative vacuum was set to κ_vac = 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends earlier CDM studies by Vetter (1995), Kalmbach (1993), Loh (1997), and Traxler (1998) that had produced flux tubes with radii up to 1 fm and linear potentials only for R > 1 fm, while lattice calculations by Bali (1995) and Trottier (1993) indicated much narrower flux tubes and linear potentials already for R > 0.2 fm. The authors also compare their three-quark results to lattice studies by Alexandrou (2001) and Takahashi (2002) that could not clearly distinguish between Δ-geometry and Y-geometry for baryonic flux tubes; the present work provides a model-based discrimination showing that neither geometry alone describes the total potential.
