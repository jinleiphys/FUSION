# Nuclear structure from random interactions

> Low-lying states in nuclei are investigated using an ensemble of random interactions. Both in the nuclear shell model and in the interacting boson model we find a dominance of $J^P=0^+$ ground states. It is shown that this feature is not due to time reversal symmetry. In the shell model, evidence is found for the occurrence of pairing properties, and in the interacting boson model for both vibrational and rotational band structures. Our results suggest that these features represent general and robust properties of the model space, and do not depend on details of the interactions.

# Nuclear structure from random interactions

## Key claim

Low-lying nuclear spectra show robust regular features (dominance of 0+ ground states, pairing, vibrational and rotational bands) even when the interactions are generated randomly, suggesting these features are general properties of the model space rather than consequences of specific realistic forces. The dominance of 0+ ground states is shown not to arise from time-reversal symmetry, as breaking that symmetry actually increases the percentage of 0+ ground states.

## Method

The authors used the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) in the nuclear shell model for identical nucleons in the sd shell (N=2, 4, 6 particles) and random one- and two-body (and later three-body) interactions in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) with N=16 bosons. For each random Hamiltonian, they diagonalized the many-body Hamiltonian, identified ground state spins, and computed energy ratios R = [E(4_1^+) - E(0_1^+)]/[E(2_1^+) - E(0_1^+)] and B(E2) transition ratios to classify spectral patterns. Time-reversal breaking was studied by interpolating between Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE, epsilon=0) and Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE, epsilon=1) for the two-body matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For N=6 identical particles in the sd shell (22O), 67.7% of random Hamiltonians produce a 0+ ground state, compared to only 9.9% of basis states having J=0.
- Breaking time-reversal symmetry increases the 0+ ground state percentage from 67.7% (epsilon=0) to 76.8% (epsilon=1) for N=6.
- In the IBM with random one- and two-body interactions (N=16), 63.4% of ground states are 0+, while only 3.3% of basis states have J=0.
- The probability distribution P(R) in the IBM shows two peaks: one at R ~ 1.95 (near the harmonic vibrator value of 2) and one at R ~ 3.35 (near the rotor value of 10/3).
- For N=4 identical particles in the sd shell (20O), 55.9% of ground states are 0+.
- Including three-body random interactions in the IBM gives 61.9% 0+ ground states, similar to the 63.4% with only one- and two-body interactions.

## Context

This work extends the surprising finding by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) that random two-body interactions in the shell model produce a predominance of 0+ ground states, and by Johnson et al. (2000) that pairing is a robust feature of the shell model space. The authors show that the shell model with random interactions favors seniority-type spectra (peak in R distribution around 1.3) but not rotational bands, while the IBM with random interactions produces both vibrational and rotational band structures. This connects to the empirical tripartite classification of Casten, Zamfir, and Brenner (1993, 1994) into seniority, anharmonic vibrator, and rotor regions. The results suggest that the selection of relevant degrees of freedom (nucleons vs. bosons) determines which collective structures emerge, and open the question of whether vibrational and rotational features can arise from random interactions in the shell model with appropriate restrictions on the parameter space.
