# Backbendings of Superdeformed bands in $^{36,40}$Ar

> Experimentally observed superdeformed (SD) rotational bands in $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ar are studied by the cranked shell model (CSM) with the paring correlations treated by a particle-number-conserving (PNC) method. This is the first time the PNC-CSM calculations are performed on the light nuclear mass region around $A=40$. The experimental kinematic moments of inertia $J^{(1)}$ versus rotational frequency are reproduced well. The backbending of the SD band at frequency around $\hbarω=1.5$ MeV in $^{36}$Ar is attributed to the sharp rise of the simultaneous alignments of the neutron and proton $1d_{5/2}[202]5/2$ pairs and $1f_{7/2}[321]3/2$ pairs, which is the consequence of the band crossing between the $1d_{5/2}[202]5/2$ and $1f_{7/2}[321]3/2$ configuration states. The gentle upbending at the low frequency of the SD band in $^{40}$Ar is mainly effected by the alignments of the neutron $1f_{7/2}[321]3/2$ pairs and proton $1d_{5/2}[202]5/2$ pairs. The PNC-CSM calculations show that besides the diagonal parts, the off-diagonal parts of the alignments play an important role in the rotational behavior of the SD bands.

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with particle-number-conserving pairing (PNC-CSM) reproduces the experimental kinematic moments of inertia for superdeformed bands in 36Ar and 40Ar, but the theoretical results for 40Ar systematically underestimate the data by about 1.2 hbar^2 MeV^-1 throughout the observed frequency range, possibly due to the omission of neutron-proton pairing.

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing correlations was diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space (Cranked Many-Particle Configuration space) within the N = 0 to 4 major shells for both neutrons and protons, using axially symmetric Nilsson parameters with deformations epsilon_2 = 0.48 for 36Ar and epsilon_2 = 0.5 for 40Ar, plus higher-order deformations epsilon_4 = 0.06 and epsilon_6 = -0.06. The effective monopole and quadrupole pairing strengths were set to G_0p = G_0n = 0.18 MeV and G_2p = G_2n = 0.08 MeV, with a configuration space dimension of about 500. The kinematic moment of inertia J^(1) was computed as the expectation value of the angular momentum alignment divided by the rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- For 36Ar: the backbending of the superdeformed band occurs at rotational frequency hbar-omega = 1.5 MeV, with the calculated backbending frequency in remarkably good agreement with experiment.
- For 40Ar: the theoretical kinematic moment of inertia J^(1) underestimates the experimental data by about 1.2 hbar^2 MeV^-1 throughout the whole observed frequency range.
- For 40Ar: the experimentally observed superdeformed band extends up to spin I^pi = 12^+, corresponding to hbar-omega = 1.35 MeV.
- For 40Ar: cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations give beta_2 = 0.57 at I = 0 hbar, decreasing to 0.45 at I = 12 hbar, with triaxiality gamma approximately 0 degrees.
- For 36Ar: the quadrupole deformation beta_2 = 0.46 +/- 0.03 was deduced from the B(E2) value for the 4^+ to 2^+ superdeformed transition.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to normal deformed nuclei in the A ~ 170 region and superdeformed nuclei in the A ~ 150 and 190 regions, to the light mass region around A = 40 for the first time. It provides a microscopic explanation for the backbending in 36Ar consistent with earlier projected shell model calculations by Long (2001), while also revealing the role of off-diagonal alignment contributions. The results enable future investigations of neutron-proton pairing effects in this mass region and suggest that shape evolution beyond the fixed axial deformation assumption may improve quantitative agreement with experiment.
