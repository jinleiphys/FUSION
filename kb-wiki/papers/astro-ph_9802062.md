# Kaons in Nuclear Matter

> The kaon energy in a nuclear medium and its dependence on kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon correlations is discussed. The transition from the Lenz potential at low densities to the Hartree potential at high densities can be calculated analytically by making a Wigner-Seitz cell approximation and employing a square well potential. As the Hartree potential is less attractive than the Lenz one, kaon condensation inside cores of neutron stars appears to be less likely than previously estimated.

## Key claim

The transition from the Lenz potential at low densities to the Hartree potential at high densities can be calculated analytically using a Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with a square well potential; because the Hartree potential is less attractive than the Lenz potential, kaon condensation inside neutron star cores appears less likely than previously estimated, with the attraction reduced by about a factor of 2-3.

## Method

The authors solved a recoil-corrected Klein-Gordon equation for kaons in neutron matter within the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation, using a square well potential to model the kaon-nucleon interaction. The kaon-neutron scattering length of -0.41 fm was used to relate the potential depth and range, and the resulting transcendental equation was solved analytically to obtain the kaon energy as a function of neutron density.

## Key numbers

- Kaon-neutron scattering length: \(a_{K^-n} \simeq -0.41\) fm
- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Kaon condensation previously estimated to occur at densities above \(\sim 3-4\rho_0\)
- Central density of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star: \(\sim 4\rho_0\)
- For ranges \(R = 0.4-1.0\) fm, the kaon-neutron potential depth ranges from \(V_0 = 463\) MeV down to \(V_0 = 49\) MeV
- Attraction reduction in neutron star cores: factor of 2-3

## Context

This work extends the discussion of kaon condensation originally suggested by Kaplan and Nelson, and addresses recent findings by Heiselberg and collaborators that kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon correlations reduce the \(K^-N\) attraction significantly. The results contradict earlier estimates that predicted kaon condensation at densities around 3-4\(\rho_0\), and enable more realistic assessments of neutron star structure, maximum masses, and cooling rates. The analysis builds on prior work by Wiringa, Fiks and Fabrocini on neutron star densities, and by Schaffner and collaborators on Coulomb energies in mixed phases.
