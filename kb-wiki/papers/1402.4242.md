# Constraints on the skewness coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter within the nonlinear relativistic mean field model

> Within the nonlinear relativistic mean field (NL-RMF) model, we show that both the pressure of symmetric nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities and the maximum mass of neutron stars are sensitive to the skewness coefficient $J_0$ of symmetric nuclear matter. Using experimental constraints on the pressure of symmetric nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities from flow data in heavy ion collisions and the astrophysical observation of a large mass neutron star PSR J0348+0432, with the former favoring a smaller $J_0$ while the latter a larger $J_0$, we extract a constraint of $-494 \mathrm{MeV}\leq J_0\leq -10 \mathrm{MeV}$ based on the NL-RMF model. This constraint is compared with the results obtained in other analyses.

## Key claim

Within the nonlinear relativistic mean field (NL-RMF) model, the skewness coefficient \(J_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter is constrained to \(-494\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq J_0 \leq -10\ \mathrm{MeV}\) by combining experimental flow data from heavy ion collisions (which favor a smaller \(J_0\)) and the astrophysical observation of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 (which favors a larger \(J_0\)). This is a conservative extraction that does not account for possible correlations among other macroscopic quantities, and the range could be narrowed further.

## Method

The authors used the nonlinear relativistic mean field (NL-RMF) model with seven macroscopic nuclear matter parameters (saturation density \(\rho_0\), binding energy \(E_0(\rho_0)\), incompressibility \(K_0\), skewness \(J_0\), Dirac effective mass \(M_{\mathrm{dirac}}^{\ast 0}\), symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_{\mathrm{c}})\), and slope parameter \(L(\rho_{\mathrm{c}})\)) as direct inputs, varying each individually while keeping others fixed at their FSUGold default values. They computed the pressure of symmetric nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the maximum mass of static neutron stars, using the flow data constraints from heavy ion collisions (Danielewicz 2002) and the mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) to set upper and lower limits on \(J_0\).

## Key numbers

- The extracted constraint on the skewness coefficient is \(-494\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq J_0 \leq -10\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- From flow data alone, the constraint is \(-1280\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq J_0 \leq -10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), using extreme values of other macroscopic parameters (set "S" with \(\rho_0 = 0.145\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0(\rho_0) = -15.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_{\mathrm{dirac}}^{\ast 0}/M = 0.57\), \(K_0 = 210\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the upper limit; set "H" with \(\rho_0 = 0.161\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0(\rho_0) = -16.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_{\mathrm{dirac}}^{\ast 0}/M = 0.65\), \(K_0 = 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the lower limit).
- From the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 (mass \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_{\odot}\)), the lower limit is \(J_0 \geq -494\ \mathrm{MeV}\), obtained using set "NS-H" (\(\rho_0 = 0.145\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0(\rho_0) = -16.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(K_0 = 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_{\mathrm{dirac}}^{\ast 0}/M = 0.65\), \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_{\mathrm{c}}) = 26\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(L(\rho_{\mathrm{c}}) = 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- The FSUGold interaction default values used as reference: \(\rho_0 = 0.148\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0(\rho_0) = -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M_{\mathrm{dirac}}^{\ast 0} = 0.61M\), \(K_0 = 230\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(J_0 = -522.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_{\mathrm{c}}) = 27.11\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(L(\rho_{\mathrm{c}}) = 49.97\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to constrain the skewness coefficient \(J_0\) (also denoted \(K'\) or \(Q_0\)), which is the third-order density derivative of the binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation, a higher-order bulk parameter about which little experimental information existed. The result is compared with constraints from other analyses: Farine (1997) obtained \(J_0 = -700 \pm 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from giant monopole resonances; Steiner (2010) obtained \(J_0 = -280^{+72}_{-410}\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (or \(-500^{+170}_{-290}\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) from neutron star data; Chen (2011) deduced \(J_0 = -355 \pm 95\ \mathrm{MeV}\) within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach; and Meixner (2013) deduced \(J_0 = -390 \pm 90\ \mathrm{MeV}\) using a similar method. The present constraint shows a remarkable overlap with these, and the authors note that including new degrees of freedom (hyperons or quark matter) in neutron stars would require a larger \(J_0\) value to reach \(1.97 M_{\odot}\), consistent with their lower bound.
