# Charmonium dissociation at high baryon chemical potential

> We study the charmonium dissociation in the hot medium with finite baryon chemical potential $μ_B$. Charmonium bound states are dissociated in the medium by the color screening effect and the random scatterings with thermal partons, which are included in the real and imaginary parts of the potential respectively. $J/ψ$ fraction in the $c\bar c$ pair defined to be the quantum overlap between the wave package and the wave function of $J/ψ$ eigenstate decreases with time due to the complex potentials. When $μ_B$ is large compared with the medium temperature, the Deybe mass is increased evidently. We consider $μ_B$-dependent Deybe mass in both real and imaginary parts of the potential to calculate the $J/ψ$ survival probability in the static medium and the Bjorken medium. $J/ψ$ survival probability is reduced evidently by the $μ_B$ effect at low temperatures available in the medium produced in Beam Energy Scan experiments, while this effect becomes not apparent at high temperatures.

# Charmonium dissociation at high baryon chemical potential

## Key claim

The paper claims that at low temperatures (around T=0.15 GeV) and high baryon chemical potential (μ_B up to 1.0 GeV), the J/ψ survival probability is reduced by up to 25% compared to the μ_B=0 case, but this reduction becomes smaller at higher temperatures (T=0.2 GeV) and is therefore most relevant for the Beam Energy Scan experiments where the medium has low temperature and high baryon density.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the radial wave function of a charm quark-antiquark pair in the center-of-mass frame, using a complex in-medium heavy quark potential. The real part of the potential is taken as the free energy with a Debye mass that depends on both temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B, while the imaginary part accounts for inelastic singlet-to-octet transitions from random scatterings with thermal partons. The wave package is initialized as the J/ψ eigenstate of the vacuum Cornell potential, and its time evolution is computed numerically using the Crank-Nicolson method in both static medium and Bjorken-expanding medium scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass increases by about 9% when μ_B=0.6 GeV compared to μ_B=0 at T=0.15 GeV
- At time t~3 fm/c in a static medium at T=0.15 GeV, J/ψ fraction with μ_B=1.0 GeV is suppressed by around 25% compared to the μ_B=0 case
- In the Bjorken medium with initial temperature T(t₀)=1.2 T_c and starting time t₀=0.6 fm/c, J/ψ fraction is reduced by around 15% with μ_B/T=6.0 compared to μ_B=0 at the end of the evolution
- Charm quark mass taken as m_c=1.27 GeV, D meson mass as m_D=1.87 GeV
- Cornell potential parameters: α=π/12 and σ=0.2 GeV²
- Numerical grid parameters: Δr=0.03 fm, Δt=0.001 fm/c
- Baryon chemical potential estimated as μ_B≈0.3 GeV for √s_NN=10 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium dissociation in hot QCD matter (Matsui 1986, Karsch 2005, Satz 2005) by incorporating finite baryon chemical potential effects that become important at Beam Energy Scan collision energies. The time-dependent Schrödinger equation approach with complex potentials follows the framework of Kajimoto (2017), Islam (2020), and Wen (2022), while the μ_B-dependent Debye mass is based on the work of Doring (2005). The results enable more accurate predictions for J/ψ suppression in baryon-rich medium produced at low collision energies, and the authors note that Friedel oscillations in the real potential (Kapusta 1988) and a consistent μ_B-dependent equation of state should be included in future work.
