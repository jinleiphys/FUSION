# The Color-Superconducting {'}t Hooft Interaction

> We consider the effect of a six-fermion interaction of the {'}t Hooft form in the quark-quark channel on the ground state of matter at finite density. The coupling constant for this new term is varied within the limits suggested by naturalness. The flavor-mixing effects of the additional term destabilize the color-flavor-locked (CFL) and, to a lesser extent, the two-color superconducting (2SC) phases of quark matter, especially for positive values of the coupling. For some values of the coupling, the critical density for the CFL phase is nearly larger than the maximum density in the neutron stars. We comment on the implications for neutron star evolution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding a six-fermion 't Hooft interaction in the quark-quark channel, with a coupling constant varied within naturalness bounds, destabilizes the color-flavor-locked (CFL) and two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phases of dense quark matter; for sufficiently positive values of the coupling, the critical density for the CFL phase can become nearly larger than the maximum density found in neutron stars.

## Method

The author extends the standard Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by including a six-fermion 't Hooft term in the quark-quark channel, with coupling constant \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}\) varied between \(-K\) and \(+K\) based on naturalness. The mean-field approximation is applied, retaining only the lowest-order terms in the \(1/N_c\) expansion, and the inverse propagator is numerically diagonalized to obtain the thermodynamical potential for charge- and color-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter at fixed baryon density and a temperature of 10 MeV. The gap equations and quark mass equations are solved self-consistently, and the resulting phase structure is compared for different values of \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}\).

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of the gaps when \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}=0\) is about 80 MeV (with dynamical quark mass included) or about 120 MeV (if dynamical mass is ignored).
- In the CFL phase at baryon density \(n_B = 1.6\) fm\(^{-3}\), the CFL phase has no solution for \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}} > 0.4\) (in units where \(K\) is the standard 't Hooft coupling).
- At a lower density of \(n_B = 1.2\) fm\(^{-3}\), the critical value of \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}\) above which the CFL phase disappears is less than 0.3.
- The critical density for the onset of the CFL phase shifts from about 1.0 fm\(^{-3}\) when \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}=0\) to about 1.42 fm\(^{-3}\) when \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}/K = 0.3\); for \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}/K = -0.5\), the critical density moves only to 0.96 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The maximum neutron star mass increases from 1.83 to 1.9 \(M_{\odot}\) when \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}/K = 0.3\) compared to \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}=0\).
- The strange quark mass can change by as much as 50% for different values of \(K_{\mathrm{DIQ}}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard NJL treatment of color superconductivity, where the quark-quark interaction is typically treated only at the four-fermion level, by including a six-fermion term in the quark-quark channel that shares the symmetries of the 't Hooft interaction. The results contradict the assumption that such higher-order terms are negligible, showing they can significantly alter the phase structure of dense matter. The findings enable future studies of neutron star cooling and proto-neutron star evolution, particularly regarding the possible two-stage enhanced cooling when critical temperatures for different gaps are surpassed, and motivate further work on the effect of this coupling on the surface tension of mixed phases (Reddy 2005).
