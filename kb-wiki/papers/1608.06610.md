# Challenges in flow background removal in search for the chiral magnetic effect

> We investigate the effect of resonance decays on the three-particle correlator charge separation observable in search for the chiral magnetic effect, using a simple simulation with realistic inputs. We find that resonance decays can largely account for the measured signal. We suppress the elliptic flow ($v_2$) background by using zero event-by-event $v_2$ (or via the mixed-event technique). We find that the background is suppressed, but not eliminated as naively anticipated. We identify the reason to be the non-identicalness of the resonance and final-state particle's $v_2$ and the induced correlation between the transverse momentum dependent resonance $v_2$ and decay angle. We make predictions for the charge separation signal due to resonance decays in 200~GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

Resonance decays can largely account for the measured charge separation signal in the three-particle correlator, and the elliptic flow background is suppressed but not eliminated by using zero event-by-event \(v_2\) or mixed-event techniques, contrary to naive expectations.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of resonance decays (\(\rho\), \(\eta\), \(\omega\)) with realistic inputs from 200 GeV Au+Au data, including transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) parameterizations, and particle yields. They computed the three-particle correlator \(\gamma \equiv \langle \cos(\alpha+\beta-2\psi_{\rm PP}) \rangle\) for same-sign and opposite-sign pion pairs, and studied its dependence on event-by-event \(v_2\) using both the true participant plane and reconstructed event plane.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(2\rho/\pi_{\rm inc} = 0.169\) was used for the \(\rho\) meson yield relative to inclusive pions, with \(\eta/\rho = 0.47\) and \(\omega/\rho = 0.59\).
- The \(\rho\) meson transverse momentum spectrum was fitted with an exponential slope parameter \(T = 0.317\) GeV from 200 GeV Au+Au data at 40-80% centrality.
- The mixed-event subtracted \(\Delta\gamma\) was reduced by approximately a factor of two from the inclusive signal across centralities.
- The Bose-Einstein fit temperature for primordial pions ranged from \(T_{\rm BE} = 0.171\) GeV (70-80% centrality) to \(T_{\rm BE} = 0.219\) GeV (0-10% centrality).

## Context

This work extends the study of flow backgrounds in the chiral magnetic effect search, following the STAR measurements (Abelev 2009) and the event-by-event method of Adamczyk (2013). It contradicts the earlier estimate by Voloshin (2004) that claimed negligible contributions from resonance decays, showing that those estimates missed a factor of \(v_2\). The results enable improved background subtraction strategies for ongoing CME searches at RHIC and LHC, while highlighting that residual correlations from the non-identicalness of resonance and final-state particle \(v_2\) prevent complete background elimination.
