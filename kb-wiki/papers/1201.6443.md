# Fragile Black Holes and an Angular Momentum Cutoff in Peripheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> In collisions of heavy ions at extremely high energies, it is possible for a significant quantity of angular momentum to be deposited into the Quark-Gluon Plasma which is thought to be produced. We develop a simple geometric model of such a system, and show that it is dual, in the AdS/CFT sense, to a rotating AdS black hole with a topologically planar event horizon. However, when this black hole is embedded in string theory, it proves to be unstable, for all non-zero angular momenta, to a certain non-perturbative effect: the familiar planar black hole, as used in most AdS/CFT analyses of QGP physics, is "fragile". The upshot is that the AdS/CFT duality apparently predicts that the QGP should always become unstable when it is produced in peripheral collisions. However, we argue that holography indicates that relatively low angular momenta delay the development of the instability, so that in practice it may be observable only for peripheral collisions involving favourable impact parameters, generating extremely large angular momenta. The result may be holographic prediction of a cutoff for the observable angular momenta of the QGP, or perhaps of an analogous phenomenon in condensed matter physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard planar AdS black hole used in holographic models of the Quark-Gluon Plasma is "fragile", it is unstable to a non-perturbative brane pair-production instability for any non-zero angular momentum, but that relatively low angular momenta delay the instability's development, so it may only be observable in peripheral collisions with extremely large angular momenta and favorable impact parameters.

## Method

The author develops a geometric model of a peripheral heavy ion collision using a flat two-dimensional space with a shearing motion parameterized by a function that vanishes along a neutral axis and increases away from it. This boundary geometry is shown to be dual, via the AdS/CFT correspondence, to a rotating asymptotically AdS black hole with a topologically planar event horizon, specifically the Klemm-Moretti-Vanzo (KMV₀) black hole. The stability of this black hole is then analyzed using the Seiberg-Witten criterion for brane pair-production instability in the Euclidean instanton formulation.

## Key numbers

- The maximum fraction of initial angular momentum deposited into the QGP is about 29% for the most favorable impact parameter, as discussed in Bec (reference in text).
- The black hole temperature is given by T = (2r_h³ − 4πM*L²)/(2πL²r_h²), where r_h is the horizon radius, M* is the energy density parameter, and L is the asymptotic curvature radius.
- Cosmic censorship imposes a strict inequality: a²/L² < 3 × (2πM*/L)^(4/3), where a = J*/M* is the ratio of angular momentum density to energy density.
- The Euclidean coordinate ψ must satisfy Ψ < √(L/a), with the natural choice Ψ = (1/3^(1/4)) (L/(2πM*))^(1/3) for a black hole with positive temperature.

## Context

This work extends the holographic modeling of the rotating Quark-Gluon Plasma beyond the topologically spherical AdS-Kerr black holes used by Atmaja and Schalm (reference in text), which are immune to the Seiberg-Witten instability as shown in McInnes (reference in text). The planar black hole geometry is more appropriate for peripheral collisions because it reproduces the flat spatial sections and shearing motion described by Liang (reference in text) and Bec (reference in text). The result suggests a holographic prediction of a cutoff on observable angular momenta in peripheral heavy ion collisions, and potentially analogous phenomena in condensed matter systems such as rotating superconductors modeled by Sonner (reference in text).
