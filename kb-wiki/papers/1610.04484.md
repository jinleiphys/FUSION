# $η$ bound state: theory and experiment

> The search for a quasi bound $η$ meson in atomic nuclei is reviewed. This tentative state is studied theoretically as well as experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the search for quasi-bound $\eta$ meson states in atomic nuclei, concluding that while theoretical predictions exist for binding in nuclei with mass number A ≥ 12, experimental evidence remains inconclusive; the only claim of a significant observation (by the GEM collaboration at about 5σ significance) has been questioned due to possible interference effects from non-resonant backgrounds.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical approaches that construct an optical potential for the η-nucleus interaction from the η-nucleon scattering length, then solve a wave equation (Klein-Gordon) to search for poles in the complex momentum plane. Experimentally, the review covers multiple search strategies: photoproduction on ³He at MAMI, the WASA collaboration's dd reaction, transfer reactions with recoil-free kinematics at GSI and COSY (including the GEM collaboration's use of the Big Karl spectrograph and ENSTAR detector), and final-state interaction studies of reactions such as d+p → ³He+η and d+d → ⁴He+η.

## Key numbers

- The η lifetime is τ = 5×10⁻¹⁹ s.
- For a binding energy B_η = 10 MeV, the decay η+N → π+N' yields T_π ≈ 317 MeV and T_N' ≈ 47.3 MeV.
- Deduced η-nucleon scattering lengths range from a(ηN) = 0.22 + i0.235 fm to a(ηN) = 1.14 + i0.31 fm.
- The GEM collaboration found a peak in the binding energy spectrum at E_B = -12.0 ± 2.2 MeV with Gaussian width σ = 4.7 ± 1.7 MeV, at about 5σ significance.
- For the d+p → ³He+η reaction, COSY-11 obtained a(η³He) = ±(2.9 ± 2.7) + i(3.2 ± 1.8) fm, corresponding to B_η = -0.2 ± 0.8 MeV and Γ/2 = 1.9 ± 0.4 MeV.
- For the d+d → ⁴He+η reaction, a fit to world data gave a(ηα) = [±(3.1 ± 0.5) + i(0.0 ± 0.5)] fm, corresponding to B_η = 3.71 ± 0.09 MeV and Γ/2 = 0.0 ± 0.2 MeV.
- For the p+⁶Li → η+⁷Be reaction, Upadhyay et al. obtained a(η⁷Be) = (-9.18 + i8.53) fm from a(ηN) = (0.88 + i0.41) fm.

## Context

This review extends earlier theoretical work by Bhalerao and Liu (1985) and Haider and Liu (1986), who first predicted η bound states in nuclei with A ≥ 12 based on the attractive η-nucleon interaction. It also updates the author's own previous review (Machner, 2014) and covers experimental efforts that build on techniques originally developed for hypernuclei production (Bruckner, 1976) and pionic atoms (Yamazaki, 1996). The review enables future experiments to focus on transfer reactions with recoil-free kinematics and final-state interaction studies, particularly for light nuclei where the η-nucleus scattering length can be extracted, and highlights the need for measurements closer to threshold to distinguish between models.
