# Quark-meson-diquark model for two-color QCD

> We introduce a two-flavor quark-meson-diquark model for two-color QCD and its extensions to include gauge-field dynamics as described by the Polakov loop. Grand potential and phase structure are being studied both in mean-field approximation and with the functional renormalization group. The model provides an explicit example for the importance of baryonic degrees of freedom: When they are omitted, the phase diagram closely resembles that of the corresponding (Polyakov)-quark-meson models for QCD, in particular including their critical endpoint. In order to reproduce the well established main features based on the symmetries and breaking patterns of two-color QCD, however, they must be included and there is no critical endpoint. The competing dynamics of collective mesonic and baryonic fluctuations is well described by the functional renormalization group equation in lowest order derivative expansion for the effective potential which we solve numerically on a two-dimensional grid in field space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that baryonic (diquark) degrees of freedom must be included in two-color QCD models to reproduce the correct phase structure based on symmetries and breaking patterns; when they are omitted, the phase diagram incorrectly resembles that of three-color QCD with a critical endpoint, whereas the full model shows no critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-flavor quark-meson-diquark (QMD) model for two-color QCD, extended with Polyakov-loop dynamics (PQMD), and studied the grand potential and phase structure using both mean-field approximation and the functional renormalization group (FRG) in lowest-order derivative expansion. The FRG flow equations for the effective potential were solved numerically on a two-dimensional grid in field space, allowing treatment of competing mesonic and baryonic fluctuations. The model was compared to the purely mesonic version to isolate the effect of baryonic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \( f_\pi = 76 \) MeV (scaled by \(\sqrt{N_c}\) for \(N_c=2\))
- Pion screening mass: \( m_\pi = 138 \) MeV
- Chiral transition temperature at \(\mu=0\): \( T_c \approx 183 \) MeV (used to fix sigma meson mass)
- Critical endpoint (CEP) in the purely mesonic model shifts toward larger chemical potentials as the vacuum-term cutoff \(\Lambda\) increases, approaching the dimensionally regularized result when \(\Lambda/\mu_c\) is sufficiently large

## Context

This work extends prior studies of two-color QCD using chiral effective field theory (Kogut 1999, 2000), random matrix theory (Splittorff 2001), lattice simulations (Hands 1999, 2000, 2006, 2010, 2011), and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model approaches (Ratti 2004, Brauner 2009, Andersen 2010). The model provides a relativistic analogue of the BEC-BCS crossover in ultracold fermionic quantum gases studied with FRG methods (Diehl 2009, Scherer 2010), and serves as a first step toward including baryonic degrees of freedom in quark-meson-baryon models for three-color QCD. The techniques developed are also applicable to QCD with two light flavors at finite isospin chemical potential and to color superconductivity studies.
