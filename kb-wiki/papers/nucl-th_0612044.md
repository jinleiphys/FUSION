# Isospin-dependent clusterization of Neutron-Star Matter

> Because of the presence of a liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, compact-star matter can present a region of instability against the formation of clusters. We investigate this phase separation in a matter composed of neutrons, protons and electrons, within a Skyrme-Lyon mean-field approach. Matter instability and phase properties are characterized through the study of the free-energy curvature. The effect of beta-equilibrium is also analyzed in detail, and we show that the opacity to neutrinos has an influence on the presence of clusterized matter in finite-temperature proto-neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within a Skyrme-Lyon mean-field approach, the addition of an electron gas completely suppresses the thermodynamic (macroscopic) spinodal instability of nuclear matter against liquid-gas phase separation, but that finite-size density fluctuations (clusterization) can still occur over a limited range of wave numbers and temperatures.

## Method

The free-energy curvature matrix was computed for homogeneous nuclear matter (neutrons and protons) and for nuclear matter with a neutralizing electron gas (NMe), using three Skyrme effective interactions (Sly230a, SGII, SIII). For finite-size fluctuations, a plane-wave density modulation was imposed on neutrons, protons, and electrons independently, and the total free-energy variation was evaluated including bulk, nuclear density-gradient, and Coulomb contributions within a Thomas-Fermi approximation.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the nuclear-matter spinodal instability is about 14 MeV (Sly230a interaction).
- For symmetric matter at saturation density, the electron susceptibility is 1052.45 MeV, while the nuclear free-energy second derivatives are 570.864 MeV (proton-proton) and -202.96 MeV (neutron-proton) at zero temperature.
- The instability interval for clusterization disappears at a limiting temperature of about 12 MeV for the realistic Skyrme forces (Sly230a, SGII) and almost 16 MeV for SIII.
- The instability interval for clusterization is maximal at zero temperature and goes to zero at the limiting temperature.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (Baran 2005, Muller and Serot 1995) to the astrophysical context of neutron-star matter, where electrons enforce charge neutrality. It contrasts with the relativistic mean-field results of Providencia (2006), who found a small thermodynamic instability region for nuclear matter with electrons using the NL3 parameter set. The results enable further study of clusterized "pasta phases" in neutron-star crusts and the role of density fluctuations in supernova dynamics via neutrino propagation.
