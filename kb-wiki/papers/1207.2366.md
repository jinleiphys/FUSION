# B_c meson enhancement and the momentum dependence in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy

> B_c meson production in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s}=2.76 A TeV is surveyed in both a statistical coalescence model and a transport model. The nuclear modification factor R_{AA} is predicted to be between 2 and 18 in the most central collisions, which can help to confirm the regeneration mechanism. In addition, the momentum dependence is also investigated as given by the transport model. A strong suppression of the transverse momentum is found in central collisions accompanying the enhancement in yield. The spectrum and elliptic flow of B_c are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for \(B_c\) mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) A TeV will be between 2 and 18, with the specific value depending on whether the heavy quark potential is taken as the internal energy or the free energy, and on whether excited states are included. This enhancement, rather than suppression, would serve as firm evidence for the regeneration (recombination) mechanism of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors calculate \(B_c\) meson production using two complementary approaches: a statistical coalescence model (SCM) that assumes chemical equilibrium at hadronization, and a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic transport model that traces the fireball evolution and includes both gluon dissociation of \(B_c\) mesons and the inverse regeneration process \(\bar{b}+c\rightarrow B_c+g\) via detailed balance. The transport model uses a Cornell potential with parameters fitted to charmonium and bottomonium spectra, and considers two limits for the finite-temperature heavy quark potential (internal energy \(V=U\) and free energy \(V=F\)) to determine dissociation temperatures. Initial heavy quark yields are derived from the Glauber model using cross sections from PYTHIA and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Predicted \(R_{AA}\) for \(B_c\) in most central Pb+Pb collisions: 3.5 (scalar state only, SCM), 11 (including vector and isospin partner, SCM), 13 (including 1P and 2S excited states, SCM), and 2.3–17.5 (transport model, both potential limits).
- Ratio of directly produced \(B_c\) to bottom quarks: \(\sigma_{B_c}^{dir}/\sigma_{b\bar{b}}^{dir} = 62\ \text{nb} / 20\ \mu\text{b} = 0.31\%\).
- In SCM, \(N_{B_c^+}/N_{b\bar{b}}^{dir} = 1.08\%\) (scalar only) and 3.30% (including vector and isospin partner).
- Dissociation temperatures scaled by \(T_c\): for \(V=U\), \(T_d/T_c = 3.27\) (1S), 1.59 (1P), 1.41 (2S); for \(V=F\), \(T_d/T_c = 1.51\) (1S only, 1P and 2S do not survive above \(T_c\)).
- Fireball parameters: \(T = 164\ \text{MeV}\), \(V_{\Delta y=1} = 4160\ \text{fm}^3\) for central collisions; maximum temperature 485 MeV at thermalization time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\ \text{fm}\).
- In transport model without regeneration, \(R_{AA}\) of initially produced \(B_c\) is 0.25 (\(V=U\)) and 0.01 (\(V=F\)).
- Final ratio \(F\) (defined as \(dN_{B_c}/dy\) divided by \(dN_c/dy \cdot dN_b/dy\)) in most central collisions: between \(6\times 10^{-4}\) and \(2\times 10^{-3}\) (SCM), and between \(7\times 10^{-5}\) and \(3\times 10^{-3}\) (previous literature at volume \(dV/dy = 600\)–\(800\ \text{fm}^3\) and \(T = 140\)–\(260\ \text{MeV}\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quarkonium regeneration, previously applied to \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) mesons, to the \(B_c\) meson, which requires both a charm and a bottom quark pair. The authors note that at RHIC energy, the statistical model (Kostyuk 2003, Braun-Munzinger 2000) did not predict enhancement of heavy quarkonia, and no enhancement was observed at RHIC or LHC for \(J/\psi\) or \(\Upsilon\). The unique flavor asymmetry of \(B_c\) makes its enhancement a cleaner signature of regeneration. The results enable experimental searches at LHC to distinguish between suppression and regeneration scenarios, and the momentum dependence (suppression of \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle\) in central collisions) provides a dynamical test of the model.
