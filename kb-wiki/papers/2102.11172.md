# In-medium similarity renormalization group with three-body operators

> Over the past decade the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) approach has proven to be a powerful and versatile ab initio many-body method for studying medium-mass nuclei. So far, the IMSRG was limited to the approximation in which only up to two-body operators are incorporated in the renormalization group flow, referred to as the IMSRG(2). In this work, we extend the IMSRG(2) approach to fully include three-body operators yielding the IMSRG(3) approximation. We use a perturbative scaling analysis to estimate the importance of individual terms in this approximation and introduce truncations that aim to approximate the IMSRG(3) at a lower computational cost. The IMSRG(3) is systematically benchmarked for different nuclear Hamiltonians for ${}^{4}\text{He}$ and ${}^{16}\text{O}$ in small model spaces. The IMSRG(3) systematically improves over the IMSRG(2) relative to exact results. Approximate IMSRG(3) truncations constructed based on computational cost are able to reproduce much of the systematic improvement offered by the full IMSRG(3). We also find that the approximate IMSRG(3) truncations behave consistently with expectations from our perturbative analysis, indicating that this strategy may also be used to systematically approximate the IMSRG(3).

## Key claim

The authors extend the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) to include three-body operators in the IMSRG(3) approximation, and demonstrate that this systematically improves over the standard IMSRG(2) truncation relative to exact results for small model spaces, but the full IMSRG(3) scales as O(N^9) and is therefore computationally prohibitive for large systems.

## Method

The authors derived the full set of fundamental commutators required for the IMSRG(3) truncation, where the Hamiltonian and generator each include normal-ordered one-body, two-body, and three-body parts. They performed a perturbative scaling analysis using Møller-Plesset perturbation theory to classify each commutator by its lowest-order contribution to the energy and its computational cost. Based on this analysis, they constructed several approximate IMSRG(3) truncation schemes (IMSRG(3)-MP4, IMSRG(3)-N^7, IMSRG(3)-N^8, IMSRG(3)-g^5) that include selected subsets of the three-body commutators to reduce computational cost while retaining key physics. The full IMSRG(3) and all approximate schemes were benchmarked against exact diagonalization results for 4He and 16O in small model spaces using different nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- The full IMSRG(3) scales as O(N^9) in the size of the single-particle basis N, due to the [3,3,3] commutator
- The IMSRG(3)-MP4 approximation scales as O(N^7) and is fourth-order complete in many-body perturbation theory
- The IMSRG(2) scales as O(N^6) and is third-order complete in many-body perturbation theory
- The IMSRG(3) is fourth-order complete in MBPT at the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) level
- The [2,2,3] and [2,3,2] commutators scale as O(N^7), while the [3,3,3] commutator scales as O(N^9)
- The [3,3,0] commutator scales as O(N^6) and contributes at order g^4 in perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG approach beyond the standard IMSRG(2) truncation, which was previously limited to normal-ordered two-body operators and had shown deficiencies for observables such as 2+ excited-state energies at closed shells (Hagen 2016), dipole polarizabilities (Miorelli 2018, Kaufmann 2020), and nuclear beta-decay matrix elements (Novario 2020). The perturbative analysis and approximate truncation schemes are analogous to developments in coupled-cluster theory, where methods like CCSDT-1 (Lee 1984, Watts 1995) provide fourth-order complete approximations at lower computational cost than full CCSDT. The IMSRG(3)-MP4 approximation is most similar to iterated coupled-cluster methods like CCSDT-1, as both are fourth-order complete, though CCSDT-1 scales like O(A^3 N^4) while IMSRG(3)-MP4 scales like O(N^7). This work enables future large-scale IMSRG calculations that approximately include three-body operator effects, potentially improving agreement with experiment for systems where IMSRG(2) is deficient.
