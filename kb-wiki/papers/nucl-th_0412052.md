# Low-momentum interactions for nuclei

> We show how the renormalization group is used to construct a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low k}, which unifies all potential models used in nuclear structure calculations. V_{low k} can be directly applied to the nuclear shell model or to nucleonic matter without a G matrix resummation. It is argued that V_{low k} parameterizes a high-order chiral effective field theory two-nucleon force. We use cutoff dependence as a tool to assess the error in the truncation of nuclear forces to two-nucleon interactions and introduce a low-momentum three-nucleon force, which regulates A=3,4 binding energies. The adjusted three-nucleon interaction is perturbative for small cutoffs. In contrast to other precision interactions, the error due to missing many-body forces can be estimated, when V_{low k} and the corresponding three-nucleon force are used in nuclear structure calculations and the cutoff is varied.

# Low-momentum interactions for nuclei

## Key claim

The renormalization group can be used to construct a "universal" low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low k} that unifies all potential models used in nuclear structure calculations, but the cutoff dependence of A=3,4 binding energies demonstrates that cutoff independence (and thus model independence) in nuclear physics requires consistent three-nucleon forces, and the error due to missing many-body forces can be estimated when V_{low k} and the corresponding three-nucleon force are used and the cutoff is varied.

## Method

Starting from a given high-precision nucleon-nucleon potential model, the author integrates out high-momentum modes above a cutoff Lambda using the renormalization group, solving the two-body Bloch-Horowitz equation in momentum space to resum high-momentum ladders into an energy-dependent effective interaction, then recasting the energy dependence as momentum dependence via the equations of motion (equivalent to the Lee-Suzuki basis transformation). The resulting low-momentum interaction V_{low k} is then augmented by adjusting the leading-order chiral effective field theory three-nucleon force (with long-range 2pi-exchange, intermediate-range 1pi-exchange, and short-range contact terms) to reproduce the triton and alpha particle binding energies for various cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff variation for all Lambda >= 1.0 fm^{-1} gives approximately 1 MeV for A=3 binding energy and 5 MeV for A=4 binding energy, compared to potential energies >30 MeV and >60 MeV respectively.
- Reference result for V_{low k} from Argonne v18 at Lambda=1.9 fm^{-1}: E(^3H) = 8.47 MeV and E(^4He) = 29.19 MeV.
- Reference result for V_{low k} from CD Bonn at Lambda=2.1 fm^{-1}: E(^3H) = 8.49 MeV and E(^4He) = 29.20 MeV.
- Average relative momentum k_rms in ^4He ranges from 0.55 to 0.80 fm^{-1} over cutoffs from 1.0 to 3.0 fm^{-1}.
- Ratio of maximum three-nucleon to V_{low k} contribution is 0.08 at Lambda=1.0 fm^{-1} and 0.23 at Lambda=3.0 fm^{-1}.
- For cutoffs Lambda <= 2.0 fm^{-1}, the adjusted three-nucleon forces are perturbative, with the ratio (Q/Lambda)^3 approximately 0.05 for Q ~ k_rms ~ m_pi and Lambda ~ 2.0 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to nuclear forces pioneered by the author's earlier work (Vlowk1, Vlowk2) and unifies precision potential models such as Argonne v18 and CD Bonn. It contrasts with the G matrix resummation approach used in conventional shell-model effective interactions, which introduces uncontrolled starting-energy dependence and lacks a corresponding three-nucleon force theory. The results enable direct application of low-momentum interactions to nuclear structure calculations (shell model, coupled cluster, nucleonic matter) with theoretical error estimates from cutoff variation, and the author recommends cutoffs near Lambda = 2.0 fm^{-1} as starting values while noting that three-nucleon forces never vanish.
