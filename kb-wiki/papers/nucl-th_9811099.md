# Transport Dynamics of Broad Resonances

> The propagation of short life time particles with consequently broad mass width are discussed in the context of transport descriptions. In the first part some known properties of finite life time particles such as resonances are reviewed and discussed at the example of the $ρ$-meson. Grave deficiencies in some of the transport treatment of broad resonances are disclosed and quantified. The second part addresses the derivation of transport equations which permit to account for the damping width of the particles. Baym's $Φ$-derivable method is used to derive a self-consistent and conserving scheme, which fulfils detailed balance relations even in the case of particles with broad mass distributions. For this scheme a conserved energy-momentum tensor can be constructed. Furthermore, a kinetic entropy can be derived which besides the standard quasi-particle part also includes contributions from fluctuations.

## Key claim

Standard transport treatments of broad resonances violate detailed balance and systematically underestimate the low-mass side of dilepton spectra; a self-consistent, conserving scheme derived from Baym's Phi-derivable method can account for the damping width of particles while preserving detailed balance relations even for broad mass distributions.

## Method

The paper first reviews known properties of finite-lifetime resonances using the rho-meson as an example, quantifying deficiencies in existing transport treatments. It then derives generalized kinetic equations using Baym's Phi-derivable functional method within the real-time non-equilibrium field theory (Schwinger-Kadanoff-Baym-Keldysh formalism), applying a first-order gradient approximation to obtain coupled equations for the slowly varying space-time part of phase-space distributions alongside retarded equations for the fast four-momentum dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Rho-meson lifetime is less than 2 fm/c, corresponding to damping rates above 100 MeV
- Typical mean kinetic energies in the system range between 70 to 150 MeV depending on beam energy
- The damping width of most constituents in dense matter can no longer be treated as a perturbation when collision rates are comparable in magnitude to these energies

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Schwinger, Kadanoff, Baym, and Keldysh (1960s) for non-equilibrium field theory, and builds on earlier investigations by Danielewicz on quantum effects in Boltzmann collision terms and by Landsmann on conceptual problems in quantum field theory. It addresses deficiencies in ad hoc recipes used for resonances like the Delta-33 resonance in transport simulation models, particularly the violation of detailed balance identified by Danielewicz and collaborators. The approach enables a thermodynamically consistent transport description of particles with finite mass width, including memory effects and a non-equilibrium kinetic entropy flow.
