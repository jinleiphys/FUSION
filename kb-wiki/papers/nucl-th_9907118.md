# Transport calculation of dilepton production at ultrarelativistic energies

> Dilepton spectra are calculated within the microscopic transport model UrQMD and compared to data from the CERES experiment. The invariant mass spectra in the region 300 MeV < M < 600 MeV depend strongly on the mass dependence of the $ρ$ meson decay width which is not sufficiently determined by the Vector Meson Dominance model. A consistent explanation of both the recent Pb+Au data and the proton induced data can be given without additional medium effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the CERES dilepton data for both p+Be and Pb+Au collisions can be explained by the UrQMD transport model without invoking in-medium effects, provided the mass dependence of the rho meson dilepton decay width is treated carefully; however, the authors note that the parameterization of this decay width is not constrained from first principles and the variation between different approaches can produce spectra that either clearly underestimate or slightly overshoot the data.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate dilepton production in p+Be collisions at 450 GeV and Pb+Au collisions at ultrarelativistic energies. Dileptons were produced perturbatively from hadronic decays (Dalitz decays of neutral mesons, direct decays of vector mesons, and incoherently summed pn bremsstrahlung), with the rho meson dilepton decay width treated differently depending on whether the rho came from hadronic sources (scaling as M^{-3}) or from string fragmentation (scaling as M). The model calculations were filtered through the CERES detector acceptance and momentum resolution for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass region of interest is 300 MeV < M < 600 MeV, where the spectra depend strongly on the mass dependence of the rho meson decay width.
- For comparison with CERES data, the detector acceptance requires lepton pseudorapidity 2.1 ≤ η_{e±} ≤ 2.65 and a cut on small opening angles Θ_{e+e-} > 0.035 mrad.
- Transverse momentum cuts are p_t > 50 MeV for proton-induced reactions and p_t > 200 MeV for ion-induced reactions.
- The bin width for the "naked" spectrum (ideal detector) is 8 MeV.
- The rho meson mass distribution shows a drop at invariant masses around 1.2 GeV due to a model limitation not to create resonances from string fragmentation with masses exceeding the pole mass by more than three widths (M < M_R + 3Γ_R).

## Context

This work extends previous transport model calculations of dilepton production at BEVALAC energies (Porter 1997, Bratkovskaya 1997, 1998, Ernst 1998) and at CERN SPS energies (Winckelmann 1996, Koch 1996) to the recent CERES Pb+Au data. The paper contrasts with approaches invoking in-medium effects such as Brown-Rho scaling (Brown 1991, Hatsuda 1992, Li 1996) or collisional broadening of spectral functions (Rapp 1997, Cassing 1997), showing that a conventional hadronic and string-based transport model can reproduce the data. The results enable further theoretical work to provide a microscopic scheme for the rho meson dilepton decay width and motivate higher-precision measurements to identify the omega peak in invariant mass spectra.
