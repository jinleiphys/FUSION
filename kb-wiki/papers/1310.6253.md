# Incompleteness of complete kaon photoproduction

> A possible roadmap for reaching a status of complete information in $γp \rightarrow K^ {+} Λ$ is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a possible roadmap for achieving "complete information" (i.e., unambiguous determination of all reaction amplitudes) in kaon photoproduction, but emphasizes that extracting the relative phases of the normalized transversity amplitudes from double-polarization observables is far more challenging than extracting the moduli, and that finite experimental errors frequently lead to imaginary or incorrect solutions for the phases.

## Method

The authors propose using normalized transversity amplitudes (NTA) \(a_i = b_i / \sqrt{\sum_i |b_i|^2}\) to analyze single- and double-polarization observables for the \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda\) reaction. They extract the moduli \(r_i\) of the NTA from the single-polarization observables \(\{\Sigma, T, P\}\) using linear equations, and then attempt to extract the relative phases \(\delta_i\) from double-polarization observables \(\{C_x, O_x, E, F\}\) using nonlinear equations. The analysis uses GRAAL collaboration data at 66 \((W, \cos\theta_{\text{cm}})\) combinations and compares results to predictions from the Regge-plus-Resonance (RPR-2011) model, which includes a Reggeized \(t\)-channel background and seven \(s\)-channel resonances.

## Key numbers

- GRAAL data for \(\{\Sigma, T, P\}\) are available at 66 \((W, \cos\theta_{\text{cm}})\) combinations in the ranges \(1.65 \lesssim W \lesssim 1.91\) GeV (with \(\Delta W \approx 50\) MeV) and \(-0.81 \lesssim \cos\theta_{\text{cm}} \lesssim 0.86\) (with \(\Delta \cos\theta_{\text{cm}} \approx 0.3\)).
- The RPR-2011 model predictions are shown at \(W = 1900\) MeV for various \(\theta_{\text{cm}}\).
- Pseudo-data studies used ensembles of 200 data sets, each containing samples of 50 events for the asymmetries \(\{\Sigma, P, T, C_x, O_x, E, F\}\), drawn from Gaussians with the RPR-2011 prediction as mean and a given \(\sigma_{\text{exp}}\) as standard deviation.

## Context

This work extends the program of complete measurements in pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction, previously applied to pion photoproduction, to the kaon channel \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda\). It builds on the transversity amplitude formalism developed by the authors in prior work (Ryckebusch and Vrancx, 2013) and uses the RPR-2011 model (Corthals et al., 2011; Corthals et al., 2010) as a benchmark. The paper enables future experimental efforts by identifying that over-complete sets involving more than seven polarization observables, as suggested by Tiator (2013), may be needed to reliably extract the relative phases from data with finite error bars.
