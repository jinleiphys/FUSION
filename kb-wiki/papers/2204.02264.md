# Constraints from $Λ$ hypernuclei on the $ΛNN$ content of the $Λ$-nucleus potential

> A depth of $D_Λ\approx -28$ MeV for the $Λ$-nucleus potential was confirmed in 1988 by studying $Λ$ binding energies deduced from $(π^+,K^+)$ spectra measured across the periodic table. Modern two-body hyperon-nucleon interaction models require additional interaction terms, most likely $ΛNN$ three-body terms, to reproduce $D_Λ$. In this work we apply a suitably constructed $Λ$-nucleus density dependent optical potential to binding energy calculations of observed $1s_Λ$ and $1p_Λ$ states in the mass range $12\leq A\leq 208$. The resulting $ΛNN$ contribution to $D_Λ$, about 14 MeV repulsion at symmetric nuclear matter density $ρ_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, makes $D_Λ$ increasingly repulsive at $ρ\gtrsim 3ρ_0$, leading possibly to little or no $Λ$ hyperon content of neutron-star matter. This suggests in some models a stiff equation of state that may support two solar-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper derives a repulsive three-body ΛNN contribution to the Λ-nucleus potential depth of about 14 MeV at symmetric nuclear matter density, which makes the potential increasingly repulsive above roughly three times nuclear matter density and may prevent Λ hyperons from appearing in neutron-star matter, thereby enabling a stiff equation of state that supports two-solar-mass neutron stars. This result is conditional on including Pauli correlations in the two-body part of the optical potential and using realistic nuclear densities constrained by charge radii.

## Method

The authors constructed a density-dependent Λ-nucleus optical potential with a linear term from two-body ΛN interactions and a ρ² term from three-body ΛNN interactions, including a Pauli correlation function in the two-body term. They fitted the two strength parameters b₀ and B₀ to the precisely measured 1sΛ and 1pΛ binding energies in ¹⁶ΛN from (e,e'K⁺) reactions, then calculated binding energies for 1sΛ and 1pΛ states across hypernuclei from A=12 to A=208 using nuclear densities constrained by charge radii. For nuclei with neutron excess beyond ⁴⁰Ca, they modified the ρ² term to exclude core-excess nucleon cross terms based on isospin arguments.

## Key numbers

- Λ-nucleus potential depth DΛ = −26.7 ± 1.7 MeV at nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Two-body contribution DΛ⁽²⁾ = −(40.6 ± 1.0) MeV at ρ₀
- Three-body contribution DΛ⁽³⁾ = (13.9 ± 1.4) MeV at ρ₀ (repulsive)
- Fitted two-body strength parameter b₀ = 1.85 fm in Model Y, close to the spin-averaged ΛN scattering length (1.65–1.78 fm)
- Fitted three-body strength parameter B₀ = 0.170 fm in Model Y
- 1sΛ binding energy in ¹⁶ΛN used as input: 13.76 ± 0.16 MeV from (e,e'K⁺) at JLab
- 1pΛ binding energy in ¹⁶ΛN used as input: 2.84 ± 0.18 MeV

## Context

This work extends the 1988 analysis by Millener, Dover, and Gal (1988) that first confirmed DΛ ≈ −28 MeV from (π⁺,K⁺) data, and addresses the overbinding problem in modern hyperon-nucleon interaction models such as the Nijmegen extended soft-core model ESC16 and chiral EFT models at leading order. The derived DΛ⁽³⁾ value is considerably smaller than the roughly 30 MeV repulsion found in the 1988 analysis and much smaller than the ∼100 MeV repulsion inferred from Quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Lonardoni, Pederiva, and Gandolfi (2013, 2014), a discrepancy the authors attribute to the QMC use of overly compact nuclear densities. The result supports the possibility that repulsive ΛNN interactions can expel Λ hyperons from neutron-star matter, providing a solution to the hyperon puzzle and enabling equations of state stiff enough to support two-solar-mass neutron stars, consistent with the decuplet-dominance chiral EFT approach of Gerstung, Kaiser, and Weise (2020).
