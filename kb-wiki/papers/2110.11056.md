# Reconciling multi-messenger constraints with chiral symmetry restoration

> We analyze the recent astrophysical constraints in the context of a hadronic equation of state (EoS), in which the baryonic matter is subject to chiral symmetry restoration. We show that with such EoS it is possible to reconcile the modern constraints on the neutron star (NS) mass, radius, and tidal deformability (TD). We find that the softening of the EoS, required by the TD constraint of a canonical $1.4~M_\odot$ NS, followed by a subsequent stiffening, required by the $2~M_\odot$ constraint, is driven by the appearance of $Δ$ matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry. Consequently, a purely hadronic EoS that accounts for the fundamental properties of quantum chromodynamics linked to the dynamical emergence of parity doubling with degenerate masses of nucleons and $Δ$ resonances can be fully consistent with multi-messenger data. Therefore, with the present constraints on the EoS, the conclusion about the existence of the quark matter in the stellar core may still be premature.

## Key claim
A purely hadronic equation of state that includes chiral symmetry restoration via parity doubling of nucleons and Delta resonances can simultaneously satisfy the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 and the 2-solar-mass neutron star constraint, without requiring a phase transition to quark matter. The softening at intermediate densities needed for the tidal deformability constraint is driven by the onset of Delta matter due to partial chiral symmetry restoration, not by deconfinement.

## Method
The authors use a parity doublet model with N_f=2, including nucleons (N(938) and N(1535)) and Delta resonances (Delta(1232) and Delta(1700)) as chiral partners, coupled to sigma, omega, and rho meson fields in the mean-field approximation. They solve for the equation of state under neutron star conditions of beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, varying the chirally invariant masses m_0^N (550-700 MeV) and m_0^Delta (with m_0^N <= m_0^Delta), and compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. The model parameters are fixed by nuclear saturation properties (n_0=0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy -16 MeV, compressibility K=240 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym=31 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass constraint: M_max = (2.08 +/- 0.07) solar masses from PSR J0740+6620 (Fonseca 2021)
- Tidal deformability constraint: Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120} from GW170817 (Abbott 2018)
- For m_0^N = 600 MeV with m_0^Delta = m_0^N: R_{1.4} = 12.0 km, Lambda_{1.4} = 394, M_max = 2.23 solar masses
- For m_0^N = 600 MeV purely nucleonic: R_{1.4} = 13.7 km, Lambda_{1.4} = 822, M_max = 2.28 solar masses
- For m_0^N = 700 MeV with m_0^Delta = m_0^N: R_{1.4} = 11.7 km, Lambda_{1.4} = 275, M_max = 1.89 solar masses
- For m_0^N = 700 MeV purely nucleonic: R_{1.4} = 13.1 km, Lambda_{1.4} = 610, M_max = 2.00 solar masses
- Radii of 1.4 solar mass neutron stars reduce by 1.4-2.0 km when Delta matter is included compared to purely nucleonic case
- Allowed chirally invariant masses lie in the range 550-680 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L_sym approximately 82 MeV at saturation

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet model approach of Takeda (2017) to neutron star phenomenology, directly addressing the tension noted by Fattoyev (2017) between the tidal deformability constraint requiring soft EoS at intermediate densities and the 2-solar-mass constraint requiring stiff EoS at high densities. Previous work by Annala (2019) and Li (2021) interpreted similar softening-stiffening behavior as evidence for a hadron-quark phase transition using constant-speed-of-sound models. The authors show that chiral symmetry restoration within hadronic matter can produce the same effect, making the conclusion about quark matter cores premature. The results enable future tests of chiral symmetry restoration signatures in gravitational wave emissions from neutron star mergers, as proposed by Bauswein (2018, 2020).
