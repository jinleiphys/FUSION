# Pasta structures in compact stars

> We review our recent works about ``pasta'' structures following the first-order phase transition in dense matter, which correspond to the structured mixed phases with geometrical symmetries. Three kinds of phase transitions at different density ranges are examined as the stages of pasta structures: liquid-gas phase transition at subnuclear density, kaon condensation and hadron-quark phase transition at high density. Charge density as well as particle density is non-uniform there. A consistent treatment of the Coulomb potential and the particle densities is presented and a peculiar role of the Coulomb potential is elucidated: the physical picture of the Maxwell construction will be effectively recovered. It largely influences the density regime of pasta structures by the charge screening effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the structured mixed phase ("pasta" structures) in compact stars is significantly limited by charge screening effects, which effectively recover the Maxwell construction picture and reduce the density range over which these geometrical structures exist, contrary to earlier bulk calculations that ignored these effects.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for three types of first-order phase transitions in dense matter: liquid-gas phase transition at subnuclear density, kaon condensation, and hadron-quark deconfinement transition at higher densities. They solved the coupled equations of motion for meson mean fields and the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential within Wigner-Seitz cells, using the local-density approximation for nucleons and electrons, and examined droplet, rod, slab, tube, and bubble geometries.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (saturation density)
- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.3 MeV per nucleon at ρ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility: K(ρ₀) = 240 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass: m_N*(ρ₀) = 0.78 m_N (with m_N = 938 MeV)
- Isospin-asymmetry coefficient: a_sym = 32.5 MeV
- Debye screening length for quarks: λ_D^q ≈ 5 fm at μ_B ≈ 1 GeV
- Sigma meson mass used: m_σ = 400 MeV
- Proton mixing ratio threshold for neutron drip: Y_p ≈ 0.26 in 3D calculation

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear pasta structures by Rav83, Has84, and others, which typically ignored the rearrangement of charged particle density profiles due to the Coulomb interaction. The authors specifically address the charge screening effect that was found to be of minor importance by Gen03 but is shown here to be significant for kaon condensation and hadron-quark transitions. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star crust properties, neutrino opacity calculations (relevant to cooling as studied by Horowitz), and glitch phenomena (as studied by MI95).
