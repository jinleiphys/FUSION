# Nuclear modification of heavy flavor decayed dielectrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Dielectrons from heavy flavor hadron decays not only constitute a crucial background to their thermal spectrum in high-energy nuclear collisions, from which the temperature of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is extracted, but also provide a valuable probe of heavy quark interactions with the QGP. Using a linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to describe heavy quark evolution inside the QGP and a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model for their hadronization, we find heavy quark energy loss softens the invariant mass spectrum of their decayed dielectrons and yields a higher value of the extracted QGP temperature, while coalescence hardens the spectrum and yields a lower value. Taking into account full medium effects leads to higher values of the extracted temperature than using vacuum baselines of heavy flavor decayed dielectrons in analyzing the experimental data. In addition, we find the angular correlations between dielectron pairs are sensitive to heavy quark interactions with the QGP: the radial flow of the QGP enhances the near-side correlations, and scatterings between heavy quarks and the QGP broaden the away-side correlations, with elastic and string interactions playing a dominant role.

# Nuclear modification of heavy flavor decayed dielectrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Heavy quark energy loss inside the quark-gluon plasma softens the invariant mass spectrum of their decayed dielectrons and yields a higher extracted QGP temperature, while coalescence during hadronization hardens the spectrum and yields a lower temperature; the net effect of full medium modifications leads to higher extracted temperatures than using vacuum baselines, but the magnitude depends on the fragmentation parameter and collision system.

## Method

The authors used the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to simulate heavy quark evolution inside a QGP medium generated by the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, with both elastic and inelastic scatterings including perturbative Yukawa and non-perturbative string interactions. Heavy quarks were converted into hadrons using a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model, with the Peterson fragmentation function for independent fragmentation, and hadron decays into dielectrons were simulated using Pythia. The invariant mass spectra of thermal dielectrons were obtained by subtracting the calculated heavy flavor decayed dielectron cocktail from experimental data, and the QGP temperature was extracted by fitting a Boltzmann function in the intermediate mass region.

## Key numbers

- In 0-80% Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 54.4 GeV, the extracted QGP temperature using vacuum fragmentation baseline was 302 +/- 65 MeV; using medium-modified charm quarks with pure fragmentation gave 334 +/- 55 MeV; using medium modification plus coalescence gave 316 +/- 51 MeV
- In 0-10% Isobar (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the extracted temperature using vacuum baseline was 271 +/- 22 MeV; using medium modification with pure fragmentation gave 313 +/- 20 MeV; using medium modification plus coalescence gave 306 +/- 19 MeV
- Heavy quark interactions with the QGP contribute about 32 MeV systematic uncertainty in the extracted temperature for Au+Au at 54.4 GeV, and about 42 MeV for Isobar collisions at 200 GeV; hadronization scheme contributes about 18 MeV and 7 MeV respectively
- The charm quark production cross section was taken as sigma_c\bar{c} = 72 microbarn for Au+Au at 54.4 GeV and 443 microbarn for Isobar at 200 GeV; bottom quark cross section was 3.7 microbarn at 200 GeV
- The Peterson fragmentation parameter epsilon_c was adjusted to 0.0003 (from default 0.05) to reproduce STAR's vacuum baseline

## Context

This work extends the experimental analysis by STAR (2024) of QGP temperature extraction from thermal dielectrons in Au+Au collisions at 54.4 GeV and preliminary Isobar results at 200 GeV, where heavy flavor decayed dilepton backgrounds were estimated using Pythia with only rough estimates of QGP effects. Prior theoretical work by Xu (2013) had rare studies of nuclear modification effects on heavy flavor decayed dileptons. The study enables more precise extraction of QGP temperature by quantifying systematic uncertainties from heavy quark energy loss and in-medium hadronization, and proposes angular correlations of dielectrons as a new observable for heavy quark interactions with the QGP, complementing existing measurements of single inclusive hadrons and their decayed leptons by PHENIX, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS.
