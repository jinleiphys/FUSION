# Asymptotics of the deuteron form factors in the nucleon model and JLab experiments

> Using the instant form dynamics of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics and the modified relativistic impulse approximation proposed previously we calculate asymptotics of electromagnetic form factors for the deuteron considered as two--nucleon system. We show that today experiment on the elastic $ed$-scattering has reached asymptotic regime. The possible range of momentum transfer when the quark degrees of freedom could be seen in future JLab experiments is estimated. The explicit relation between the behavior of deuteron wave function at $r=0$ and the form factors asymptotics is obtained. The conditions on wave functions to give the asymptotics predicted by QCD and quark counting rules are formulated.

# Asymptotics of the deuteron form factors in the nucleon model and JLab experiments

## Key claim
The authors show that existing JLab elastic electron-deuteron scattering data at momentum transfers up to approximately 6 (GeV/c)^2 have already reached the asymptotic regime predicted by a relativistic two-nucleon model of the deuteron, and that quark degrees of freedom are not yet visible in current experiments. The quark counting rule prediction (F_d ~ Q^{-10}) disagrees with the experimental data, which follow the relativistic two-nucleon model prediction (F_d ~ Q^{-8}) within fitting accuracy.

## Method
The authors use instant form dynamics of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics (PIQM) with a modified relativistic impulse approximation to compute asymptotic expansions of the deuteron electromagnetic form factors (charge, quadrupole, and magnetic dipole) at large momentum transfer. They derive the leading asymptotic terms analytically using deuteron wave functions of the standard analytic form (sums of poles in momentum space) and compare the resulting power-law behavior with experimental data from JLab, fitting the seven highest measured points in the range 3.040-5.955 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key numbers
- The experimental fit to the deuteron form factor F_d(Q^2) yields a power-law exponent of 3.76 ± 0.41 in 1/(Q^2)^n, with χ^2 = 3.93 × 10^{-9}, based on seven data points in the range Q^2 = 3.040 to 5.955 (GeV/c)^2.
- The nonrelativistic impulse approximation gives F_d^{NR}(Q^2) ~ Q^{-11}.
- The relativistic impulse approximation gives F_d^R(Q^2) ~ Q^{-8}.
- The QCD/quark counting rule prediction gives F_d(Q^2) ~ Q^{-10}.
- The asymptotic regime for the relativistic two-nucleon model is reached at Q^2 ≃ 6 (GeV/c)^2.
- The dipole fit parameter for nucleon magnetic form factors is a_d^2 = 0.71 GeV^2.
- Future JLab experiments are planned at Q^2 ≃ 10 (GeV/c)^2, where deviation from the two-nucleon model may reveal quark degrees of freedom.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics for the deuteron (Krutov and Troitsky, 2002-2007) and their theorem on asymptotic estimation of form factors (Krutov, Troitsky, and Tsirova, 2007). It directly addresses the JLab experimental program on elastic electron-deuteron scattering at high momentum transfer (Arrington et al., 2005; Abbott et al., 2000; Alexandrov et al., 1999). The paper contrasts with the relativistic approach of Arnold, Carlson, and Gross (1980), which predicts a faster asymptotic decrease than observed. The results enable future JLab experiments to search for the transition from nucleon-meson to quark-gluon degrees of freedom by looking for deviations from the Q^{-8} power law predicted by the relativistic two-nucleon model.
