# A systematic analysis of transverse momentum spectra of $J/ψ$ mesons in high energy collisions

> We aggregate the transverse momentum spectra of $J/ψ$ mesons produced in high energy gold-gold (Au-Au), deuteron-gold ($d$-Au), lead-lead (Pb-Pb), proton-lead ($p$-Pb), and proton-(anti)proton ($p$-$p(\overline{p})$) collisions measured by several collaborations at the Relativistic Heavy Ion collider (RHIC), the Tevatron Proton-Antiproton Collider, and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The collision energy (the center-of-mass energy) gets involved in a large range from dozens of GeV to 13 TeV (the top LHC energy). We consider two participant or contributor partons, a charm quark and an anti-charm quark, in the production of $J/ψ$. The probability density of each quark is described by means of the modified Tsallis--Pareto-type function (the TP-like function) while considering that both quarks make suitable contributions to the $J/ψ$ transverse momentum spectrum. Therefore, the convolution of two TP-like functions is applied to represent the $J/ψ$ spectrum. We adopt the mentioned convolution function to fit the experimental data and find out the trends of the power exponent, effective temperature, and of the revised index with changing the centrality, rapidity, and collision energy. Beyond that, we capture the characteristic of $J/ψ$ spectrum, which is of great significance to better understand the production mechanism of $J/ψ$ in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum spectra of J/psi mesons across a wide range of collision systems and energies (from dozens of GeV to 13 TeV) can be uniformly described by the convolution of two modified Tsallis-Pareto-type (TP-like) functions, where each function represents the probability density of a charm or anti-charm quark, provided that the azimuthal angles of the two quarks are assumed equal (p_T = p_t1 + p_t2).

## Method

The authors model J/psi production by assuming two contributor partons (a charm quark and an anti-charm quark, each with constituent mass 1.6 GeV/c^2) whose transverse momentum distributions follow a modified TP-like function that includes a revised index a_0 on p_T. The J/psi transverse momentum spectrum is then obtained as the convolution of these two TP-like functions, assuming the transverse momenta of the two quarks add linearly (p_T = p_t1 + p_t2). This convolution function is fitted to experimental data from Au-Au, d-Au, Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p(bar) collisions measured by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, CMS, ATLAS, LHCb, and CDF collaborations at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV measured by STAR (|y|<1, 0-60% centrality): n = 4.67 +/- 0.03, T = 0.169 +/- 0.001 GeV, a_0 = 0.221 +/- 0.001, with chi^2/ndof = 4/2.
* For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV measured by PHENIX (|y|<0.35, 0-20% centrality): n = 2.23 +/- 0.02, T = 0.227 +/- 0.001 GeV, a_0 = 0.208 +/- 0.001, with chi^2/ndof = 8/1.
* For d-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV measured by PHENIX (backward rapidity -2.2<y<-1.2, 0-20% centrality): n = 5.85 +/- 0.05, T = 0.310 +/- 0.001 GeV, a_0 = 0.191 +/- 0.001, with chi^2/ndof = 40/23.
* For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV measured by ALICE (2.5<y<4, 0-20% centrality): n = 2.70 +/- 0.02, T = 0.278 +/- 0.001 GeV, a_0 = 0.131 +/- 0.001 (from Table 1, as Table 3 is truncated; the text states fits were performed for Pb-Pb at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV).
* For p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV measured by PHENIX: the paper reports fits with parameters listed in Table 5 (values not shown in truncated text).
* The constituent mass of charm quark used is 1.6 GeV/c^2.

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model approach previously applied by the same group (Zhang, Liu, and collaborators, 2020-2021) to describe particle spectra via the convolution of two TP-like functions, now systematically applied to J/psi mesons across multiple collision systems and energies. The study enables a unified phenomenological description of J/psi production from small (p-p) to large (Au-Au, Pb-Pb) systems, and provides trends of the power exponent n, effective temperature T, and revised index a_0 with centrality, rapidity, and collision energy, which may inform understanding of the color confinement mechanism in QCD.
