# On the imaginary parts and infrared divergences of two-loop vector boson self-energies in thermal QCD

> We calculate the imaginary part of the retarded two-loop self-energy of a static vector boson in a plasma of quarks and gluons of temperature T, using the imaginary time formalism. We recombine various cuts of the self-energy to generate physical processes. We demonstrate how cuts containing loops may be reinterpreted in terms of interference between Order $α$ tree diagrams and the Born term along with spectators from the medium. We apply our results to the rate of dilepton production in the limit of dilepton invariant mass E>>T. We find that all infrared and collinear singularities cancel in the final result obtained in this limit.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the imaginary part of the retarded two-loop self-energy of a static vector boson in a thermal QCD plasma and demonstrate that all infrared and collinear singularities cancel in the final dilepton production rate, but only in the limit where the dilepton invariant mass \(E\) is much larger than the temperature \(T\) (\(E \gg T\)).

## Method

The calculation uses the imaginary time formalism (Matsubara sums) to evaluate the two-loop photon self-energy diagrams in the static limit (\(\vec{p}=0\)). The Matsubara sums are converted to contour integrals, and the residues at the various poles are evaluated. The resulting expressions are analytically continued to real energies to obtain the imaginary part of the retarded self-energy. The cuts of the self-energy are recombined and reinterpreted as physical processes, including tree-level diagrams with spectators from the medium, and the heavy-photon limit (\(E \gg T\)) is taken to study the infrared and collinear singularity structure.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed for a static vector boson in a plasma of quarks and gluons at temperature \(T\).
* The dilepton production rate is evaluated in the limit of dilepton invariant mass \(E \gg T\).
* The self-energy is computed to second order in the strong coupling constant \(g\) (order \(\alpha\)).
* All infrared and collinear singularities are found to cancel in the final result for the dilepton rate in the \(E \gg T\) limit.

## Context

This work revisits the problem of next-to-leading order dilepton production in a quark-gluon plasma, extending earlier calculations by Baier et al. (1988) and Aurenche et al. (1989) who found finite results after summing all processes. It directly addresses recent claims by Kapusta et al. (2000) of a remnant collinear divergence in a multiple scattering expansion, and the subsequent unresolved debate. The calculation demonstrates that the imaginary time formalism provides a clean framework for handling double poles and singularities, and it enables a systematic reinterpretation of loop cuts as interference between tree diagrams and the Born term with thermal spectators, following the spectator interpretation of Wong (2001).
