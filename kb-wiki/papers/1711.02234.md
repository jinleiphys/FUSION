# Shape transition with temperature of the pear-shaped nuclei in covariant density functional theory

> The shape evolutions of the pear-shaped nuclei $^{224}$Ra and even-even $^{144-154}$Ba with temperature are investigated by the finite-temperature relativistic mean field theory with the treatment of pairing correlations by the BCS approach. The free energy surfaces as well as the bulk properties including deformations, pairing gaps, excitation energy, and specific heat for the global minimum are studied. For $^{224}$Ra, three discontinuities found in the specific heat curve indicate the pairing transition at temperature 0.4 MeV, and two shape transitions at temperatures 0.9 and 1.0 MeV, namely one from quadrupole-octupole deformed to quadrupole deformed, and the other from quadrupole deformed to spherical. Furthermore, the gaps at $N=$136 and $Z=$88 are responsible for stabilizing the octupole-deformed global minimum at low temperatures. Similar pairing transition at $T\sim$0.5 MeV and shape transitions at $T$=0.5-2.2 MeV are found for even-even $^{144-154}$Ba. The transition temperatures are roughly proportional to the corresponding deformations at the ground states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the pear-shaped nucleus \(^{224}\)Ra, three distinct phase transitions occur with increasing temperature: a pairing (superfluid-to-normal) transition at 0.4 MeV, an octupole-to-quadrupole shape transition at 0.9 MeV, and a quadrupole-to-spherical shape transition at 1.0 MeV. The abstract glosses over the fact that these transition temperatures are obtained from a mean-field calculation without statistical fluctuations, which would likely smear the sharp discontinuities into a smoother behavior in a realistic description.

## Method

The authors employed the finite-temperature relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a point-coupling density functional (parameter set PC-PK1) and treated pairing correlations via the BCS approach with a delta pairing force. They performed constrained calculations in the \((\beta_2, \beta_3)\) deformation plane to obtain free energy surfaces as functions of temperature for \(^{224}\)Ra and even-even \(^{144-154}\)Ba. The global minimum properties (deformations, pairing gaps, excitation energy, specific heat) were extracted from these surfaces, and single-particle level evolution with temperature was analyzed.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{224}\)Ra: pairing transition at \(T = 0.4\) MeV; octupole-to-quadrupole shape transition at \(T = 0.9\) MeV; quadrupole-to-spherical shape transition at \(T = 1.0\) MeV.
- For \(^{224}\)Ra at zero temperature: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.184\), \(\beta_3 = 0.133\); neutron pairing gap \(\Delta_n(0) = 0.63\) MeV; proton pairing gap is zero.
- For \(^{224}\)Ra: the critical temperature for the pairing transition follows \(T_c = 0.6 \Delta_n(0)\), yielding \(T_c = 0.4\) MeV.
- For \(^{144}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.22\), \(\beta_3 = 0.13\); quadrupole transition temperature \(T_2 = 1.45\) MeV; octupole transition temperature \(T_3 = 0.85\) MeV.
- For \(^{146}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.23\), \(\beta_3 = 0.13\); \(T_2 = 1.70\) MeV; \(T_3 = 0.95\) MeV.
- For \(^{148}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.25\), \(\beta_3 = 0.14\); \(T_2 = 1.90\) MeV; \(T_3 = 0.95\) MeV.
- For \(^{150}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.27\), \(\beta_3 = 0.15\); \(T_2 = 2.05\) MeV; \(T_3 = 0.95\) MeV.
- For \(^{152}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.29\), \(\beta_3 = 0.12\); \(T_2 = 2.15\) MeV; \(T_3 = 0.75\) MeV.
- For \(^{154}\)Ba: ground state deformations \(\beta_2 = 0.30\), \(\beta_3 = 0.09\); \(T_2 = 2.20\) MeV; \(T_3 = 0.50\) MeV.
- For \(^{224}\)Ra: the calculated ground state binding energy is within 0.1% of the experimental value; the calculated deformations (\(\beta_2=0.184\), \(\beta_3=0.133\)) are slightly larger than experimental data (\(\beta_2=0.154\), \(\beta_3=0.097\)) from Nature (2013).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature RMF+BCS approach, previously applied to well-deformed and superdeformed nuclei (Agrawal 2000, 2001), to the octupole-deformed nucleus \(^{224}\)Ra and neutron-rich Ba isotopes where recent experiments (Bucher 2016, 2017) found evidence of octupole deformation. The results are compared with finite-temperature HFB calculations using the Gogny force (Egido 2000), which found a single shape transition at \(T=1.3\) MeV for \(^{224}\)Ra, whereas the present RMF calculation finds two separate transitions at 0.9 and 1.0 MeV. The lower transition temperatures in the RMF calculation are attributed to a smaller proton shell gap near \(Z=88\) compared to the Gogny D1S calculation (Robledo 2010). The work enables future studies incorporating statistical and quantal fluctuations beyond mean field, which would smear the sharp phase transitions into smoother behavior more consistent with experimental observations.
