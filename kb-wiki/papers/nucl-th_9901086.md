# A realistic study of the nuclear transparency and the distorted momentum distributions in the semi-inclusive process 4He(e,e'p)X

> The nuclear transparency and the distorted momentum distributions of 4He in the semi-inclusive process 4He(e,e'p)X are calculated within the Glauber multiple scattering approach using for the first time realistic four-body variational wave functions embodying central and non-central nucleon-nucleon (N-N) correlations. The contributions from N-N correlations and from Glauber multiple scattering are taken into account exactly to all orders. It is shown that non-central correlations significantly affect both the transparency and the distorted momentum distributions; as a matter of fact: i) the small (approx 3%) value of the effect of correlations on the transparency results from an appreciable cancellation between the short-range central repulsive correlations and the intermediate-range attractive correlations, whose magnitude is significantly affected by the non-central forces, and ii) the effect of Glauber final state interactions on the momentum distribution is reduced by the inclusion of tensor correlations.

## Key claim

The effect of realistic nucleon-nucleon correlations on the nuclear transparency of 4He in the semi-inclusive (e,e'p) process is only about 3%, but this small value results from a cancellation between short-range repulsive central correlations and intermediate-range attractive correlations whose magnitude is significantly affected by non-central (tensor) forces, not from a cancellation between hole and spectator correlation contributions as previously argued.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear transparency and distorted momentum distributions for 4He(e,e'p)X using the Glauber multiple scattering approach to treat final state interactions (FSI) exactly to all orders, combined with realistic four-body variational wave functions from the ATMS method that include central, tensor, spin, and isospin correlations. The wave functions were generated from the Reid soft core V8 nucleon-nucleon potential with no free parameters, and the calculations used a standard parametrization of the Glauber profile function with parameters corresponding to a struck proton kinetic energy of approximately 1 GeV. The results were compared with calculations using a mean-field harmonic oscillator wave function and a phenomenological Jastrow wave function with only central correlations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency with realistic ATMS wave function: T_ATMS = 0.778
- Nuclear transparency with mean-field wave function: T_0 = 0.754 (correlation effect ~3%)
- Nuclear transparency with Jastrow wave function: T_Jastrow = 0.806 (correlation effect ~7%)
- Nuclear transparency without D-wave component: T_S = 0.780
- Binding energy of ATMS wave function: E_4 = -21.2 MeV
- RMS radius of ATMS wave function: <r^2>^{1/2} = 1.57 fm
- Wave function probabilities: P_S = 87.94%, P_S' = 0.24%, P_D = 11.82%
- Double rescattering contribution to transparency: Delta G_2 = 0.0363, which is 14% of the single rescattering contribution Delta G_1 = -0.2566
- Glauber parameters used: alpha = -0.33, b_0 = 0.5 fm, sigma_tot = 43 mb (T_p ~ 1 GeV)
- Mean-field wave function parameters: beta = 0.76, R_0 = 1.22 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Bianconi et al. (1994-1996) that used phenomenological Jastrow wave functions with only central correlations, and by Seki et al. (1996) who used a local density approximation with realistic nuclear matter correlation functions. The authors show that the Jastrow approach overestimates FSI effects by a factor of 2-3 in parallel and antiparallel kinematics, while perpendicular kinematics show less difference. The results enable future calculations for complex nuclei using a linked cluster expansion approach being developed by the same authors, and demonstrate that the forward-backward asymmetry A_FB(k) can serve as a useful observable to isolate the S-wave component of the 4He wave function.
