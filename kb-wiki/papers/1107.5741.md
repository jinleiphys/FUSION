# NLO analysis of inclusive jet, tagged jet and di-jet production in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We present results and predictions at next-to-leading order for the recent LHC lead-lead run at a center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair. Specifically, we focus on the suppression the single and double inclusive jet cross sections and demonstrate how the di-jet asymmetry, recently measured by ATLAS and CMS, can be extracted from this general result. The case of jets tagged by an electroweak boson is exemplified by the $Z^0$+jet channel. We predict a signature transition from enhancement to suppression of the tagged jet related to the medium-induced modification of the parton shower. Finally, we clarify the relation between the suppression of inclusive jets, tagged jets and di-jets and the quenching of inclusive particles on the example of the recent ALICE charged hadron attenuation data.

# NLO analysis of inclusive jet, tagged jet and di-jet production in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
At next-to-leading order, the suppression of single and double inclusive jet cross sections in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair can be consistently described by combining final-state QGP-induced radiative energy loss with initial-state cold nuclear matter effects and non-perturbative hadronization corrections, provided that collisional diffusion of the parton shower energy is included to eliminate residual jet-radius dependence. For Z0-tagged jets, the model predicts a characteristic transition from suppression for jet transverse momenta above the Z0 momentum to strong enhancement for jet transverse momenta below the Z0 momentum.

## Method
The calculation starts from next-to-leading order perturbative QCD cross sections for nucleon-nucleon reactions (using EKS NLO results for inclusive jets and di-jets, and the MCFM code for Z0-tagged jets), then applies medium-induced modifications via a factorized convolution of energy-loss probability distributions and phase-space Jacobians for each jet. The analysis includes final-state QGP-induced radiative energy loss, initial-state cold nuclear matter effects, and non-perturbative hadronization corrections, with collisional interactions of the in-medium parton shower simulated by a parameter pTmin = 20 GeV to model energy diffusion away from the jet axis.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: 2.76 TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair for LHC lead-lead collisions
- Jet radii studied: R = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6 (defined as R = sqrt(δη² + δφ²))
- Three coupling values between jet and medium: g_med = 1.8, 2.0, 2.2 used for single inclusive jet suppression predictions
- Diffusion parameter: pTmin = 20 GeV used to model collisional energy redistribution away from the jet axis
- Radiative energy loss alone explains approximately 1/2 of the measured di-jet asymmetry broadening from ATLAS and CMS

## Context
This work extends the NLO treatment of jet quenching developed by Vitev (2008) and Ovanesyan (2011) from single jets to multiple-jet observables at LHC energies. It provides the first NLO calculation of di-jet suppression in the (ET1, ET2) plane and demonstrates compatibility with preliminary ATLAS data for inclusive jets and with ATLAS and CMS di-jet asymmetry measurements. The results enable quantitative comparison between jet quenching and leading-particle suppression as measured by ALICE charged hadron attenuation data, and provide predictions for future photon-tagged heavy meson production measurements.
