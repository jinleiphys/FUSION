# Strong-coupling effects of pairing fluctuations, and Anderson-Bogoliubov mode in neutron $^1$S$_0$ superfluids in neutron stars

> We investigate effects of thermal and quantum fluctuations of the superfluid order parameter in $^1S_{0}$ superfluids in neutron stars. We construct a separable potential to reproduce the $^1S_{0}$ phase shift reconstructed by using the partial wave analysis from nucleon scattering data. We include superfluid fluctuations within a strong-coupling approximation developed by Nozières and Schmitt-Rink and determine self-consistently the superfluid order parameter as well as the chemical potential. We show that the quantum depletion, which gives a fraction of noncondensed neutrons at zero temperature due to quantum pairing fluctuations, plays an important role not only near the critical temperature from superfluid states to normal states but also at zero temperature. We derive the dispersion relation of Anderson-Bogoliubov modes associated with phase fluctuations, and show also that there is a nonzero fraction of noncondensed components in the neutron number as a result of the strong-coupling effect. Our results indicate that superfluid fluctuations are important for thermodynamic properties in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors show that quantum pairing fluctuations (quantum depletion) significantly affect the neutron \(^1S_0\) superfluid in neutron stars not only near the critical temperature but also at zero temperature, contrary to the usual mean-field assumption that such fluctuations are negligible at \(T=0\). The claim is hedged by noting that in the high-density region (\(k_{\rm F} \gtrsim 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)) the enhancement of the superfluid order parameter and critical temperature may be an artifact of overestimating the fluctuation contribution by not subtracting the Hartree-Fock potential.

## Method

The authors construct a separable neutron-neutron potential with a numerically determined form factor \(F_c(k)\) that reproduces the \(^1S_0\) phase shift from partial-wave analysis of nucleon scattering data, restricting to the attractive region where the phase shift is positive (\(k \le 1.78\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)). They then apply the Nozières–Schmitt-Rink (NSR) strong-coupling scheme to the superfluid phase below \(T_c\), self-consistently solving the gap equation and the particle number equation (which includes the fluctuation contribution \(\delta N_{\rm fluct}\)) for the superfluid order parameter \(\Delta\) and chemical potential \(\mu\). The dispersion of the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode is derived from the pole of the many-body scattering matrix \(\hat{\Gamma}\).

## Key numbers

* The scattering length for the \(^1S_0\) interaction is \(a_s = -18.8 \pm 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}\) and the effective range is \(r_{\rm eff} = 2.75 \pm 0.11\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* The dimensionless pairing strength at typical Fermi momentum \(k_{\rm F} \simeq 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) is \((k_{\rm F} a_s)^{-1} \simeq -0.05\).
* The maximum of the superfluid order parameter \(\Delta(\tilde{k}_{\rm F})\) at \(T=0\) occurs around \(k_{\rm F} \simeq 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\); the order parameter vanishes at a critical Fermi momentum \(k_{\rm F} = 1.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
* The form factor cutoff momentum is \(k_{\rm max} = 1.78\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), above which the \(^1S_0\) phase shift becomes negative and the attractive potential is set to zero.
* The coupling constant is \(m U_S / |a_s| = 0.79\).

## Context

This work extends the NSR strong-coupling approach, previously applied to cold atomic Fermi gases and to nuclear systems above \(T_c\) (e.g., Stein (1995), PhysRevC.88.054315, and Tajima (2019)), into the superfluid phase below \(T_c\) for neutron \(^1S_0\) superfluids. It improves upon earlier effective-range-expansion (ERE) treatments (PhysRevA.97.013601) by using a separable potential that accurately reproduces the full phase shift, enabling description of the superfluid-to-normal transition at high density. The results enable future studies of thermodynamic properties (equation of state, specific heat, compressibility) dominated by the gapless Anderson-Bogoliubov mode in the low-temperature limit, which is relevant for neutron star cooling and transport.
