# Beyond-mean-field theories with zero-range effective interactions. A way to handle the ultraviolet divergence

> Zero-range effective interactions are commonly used in nuclear physics and in other domains to describe many-body systems within the mean-field model. If they are used within a beyond-mean-field framework, contributions to the total energy that display an ultraviolet divergence are found. We propose a general strategy to regularize this divergence and we illustrate it in the case of the second-order corrections to the equation of state (EOF) of uniform symmetric matter. By setting a momentum cutoff $Λ$, we show that for every (physically meaningful) value of $Λ$ it is possible to determine a new interaction such that the EOS with the second-order corrections reproduces the empirical EOS, with a fit of the same quality as that obtained at the mean-field level.

## Key claim

The authors propose a general strategy to regularize the ultraviolet divergence that appears when zero-range effective interactions are used in beyond-mean-field calculations; they show that for every physically meaningful value of a momentum cutoff Λ it is possible to determine a new interaction such that the equation of state (EOS) with second-order corrections reproduces the empirical EOS with a fit quality comparable to that obtained at the mean-field level.

## Method

The authors evaluate the second-order correction to the total energy of uniform symmetric nuclear matter using a simplified zero-range density-dependent interaction of the Skyrme type (the t0-t3 model). They introduce a momentum cutoff Λ to regularize the ultraviolet divergence in the second-order diagrams, then perform least-square fits to determine new parameter sets (SkPΛ) such that the second-order-corrected EOS matches the mean-field EOS obtained with the original SkP parameter set.

## Key numbers

- For Λ = 0.5 fm⁻¹, the refitted parameters are t0 = -2352.900 MeV fm³, t3 = 15379.861 MeV fm^(3+3α), α = 0.217, with χ²/N = 0.00004; the saturation density is 0.16 fm⁻³ and energy per particle is -15.96 MeV.
- For Λ = 1.0 fm⁻¹, the refitted parameters are t0 = -1155.580 MeV fm³, t3 = 9435.246 MeV fm^(3+3α), α = 0.572, with χ²/N = 0.00142; the saturation density is 0.17 fm⁻³ and energy per particle is -16.11 MeV.
- For Λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, the refitted parameters are t0 = -754.131 MeV fm³, t3 = 8278.251 MeV fm^(3+3α), α = 1.011, with χ²/N = 0.00106; the saturation density is 0.17 fm⁻³ and energy per particle is -16.09 MeV.
- For Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹, the refitted parameters are t0 = -632.653 MeV fm³, t3 = 5324.848 MeV fm^(3+3α), α = 0.886, with χ²/N = 0.00192; the saturation density is 0.16 fm⁻³ and energy per particle is -15.82 MeV.
- For the extreme unphysical value Λ = 350 fm⁻¹, the refitted parameters are t0 = -64.904 MeV fm³, t3 = 360.039 MeV fm^(3+3α), α = 0.425, with χ²/N = 0.00042; the saturation density is 0.16 fm⁻³ and energy per particle is -15.88 MeV.
- The original SkP mean-field EOS has saturation density 0.16 fm⁻³ and energy per particle -15.95 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the use of zero-range Skyrme-type interactions (Skyrme, 1959) beyond the mean-field level by addressing the ultraviolet divergence that appears in second-order perturbation theory, a problem previously studied with finite-range interactions by Euler (1937), Huby (1949), Thouless (1957), and Levinger (1958). The proposed regularization strategy, inspired by renormalization in quantum electrodynamics, enables future applications to beyond-mean-field models in nuclear physics where ultraviolet divergences appear, such as multiparticle-multihole configuration models (Quentin, 1972), second random-phase-approximation models (Gambacurta, 2010), and particle-phonon coupling models (Bertsch, 1980; Brown, 2005; Colo, 2001; Litvinova, 2007).
