# Radiative Corrections to Elastic Lepton-Proton Scattering with Focus on Two-Photon-Exchange Diagrams

> Lepton (electron and muon) scattering experiments are excellent tools to gain insight into the nucleon structure. Elastic electron-proton scattering probes the spatial distribution of charge and magnetization inside the proton, and comparing electron-proton and muon-proton scattering data tests lepton universality. The availability of a plethora of scattering data with increased precision and observed discrepancies such as the proton form factor puzzle and the proton radius puzzle motivated a renewed effort to improve the theoretical framework. Realizing that the one-photon-exchange approximation (OPE), i.e. the Born approximation, is not sufficient, radiative corrections in QED, especially the two-photon-exchange (TPE) diagrams, are under investigation. The TPE diagrams are of special interest among the radiative corrections, since they depend on the proton structure. In this work, we present a complete calculation of QED radiative corrections to elastic electron-proton and muon-proton scattering at next-to-leading order, taking into account loop-momentum-dependent form factors. In the discussion of their numerical impact on lepton-proton scattering cross sections, we pay special attention to the TPE diagrams and compare them with existing theoretical predictions and lepton-proton scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete calculation of next-to-leading-order QED radiative corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering that incorporates loop-momentum-dependent form factors, with the claim that the two-photon-exchange (TPE) diagrams are of special interest because they depend on the proton structure; the abstract's promise of comparison with data is conditional on the numerical impact being discussed with "special attention" to TPE diagrams, and the full text provided contains only the formal derivations and analytic expressions, not the numerical results or data comparisons.

## Method

The authors compute the proton vertex correction form factors \(F_{V,E}^{(p,\Lambda)}\) for both monopole (\(n=1\)) and dipole (\(n=2\)) form factor parameterizations, expressing them in terms of the IR-divergent scalar 3-point function \(C_0\), the finite function \(C_{0,\Lambda}\), and its first, second, and third derivatives with respect to the cutoff parameter \(L=\Lambda^2\). They separately evaluate the direct box diagram contribution by decomposing it into \(\Lambda^0\), \(\Lambda^2\), and \(\Lambda^4\) terms, which involve scalar 2-point functions \(B_0\), 3-point functions \(C_0\), and 4-point functions \(D_0\) along with their first and second derivatives with respect to \(\Lambda^2\), and provide the analytic coefficients \(c_{iab}, c_{ic}, c_{id}\) for these decompositions.

## Key numbers

- The proton vertex form factors \(F_{V,E}^{(p,\Lambda)}\) are provided for the dipole case (\(n=2\)) with explicit expressions containing factors such as \(48 \beta^2 \beta_\Lambda^5 L^2 M^2 t\) in the denominator for \(F_V^{(p,\Lambda)}\) and \(96 \beta^4 \beta_\Lambda^5 L^2 M^3 t^2\) for \(F_E^{(p,\Lambda)}\).
- For the monopole case (\(n=1\)), the form factors involve denominators such as \(128 \beta^2 \beta_\Lambda M^8 t\) for \(F_V^{(p,\Lambda)}\) and \(16 \beta^4 \beta_\Lambda (\beta_\Lambda^2-1) M^5 t^2\) for \(F_E^{(p,\Lambda)}\).
- The direct box contribution is expressed as \(\sum (M_{box} \cdot M_0^*) = \frac{Q_l^3 Z^2 4\pi \alpha^3}{t} \left(\frac{\Lambda^2}{\Lambda^2-t}\right)^n \sum_{i=0}^{2n-2} ({\rm box}_{B}(i)+{\rm box}_C(i)+{\rm box}_{D}(i))\), where the sum runs over \(i=0\) to \(2n-2\) (i.e., \(i=0,1,2\) for the dipole case and \(i=0\) for the monopole case).
- The first derivative of \(D_0^{(0145)}\) with respect to \(L\) is given by \(\frac{\partial D_0^{(0145)}}{\partial L} = \frac{2}{(4 s L^2-(t-4L)\Delta)} [\mathcal{F}_{\Delta} L_\Delta + \mathcal{F}_{t} L_t + \mathcal{F}(m,M) L_m + \mathcal{F}(M,m) L_M + \mathcal{F}_{D} D_0^{(0145)}]\), with explicit \(\mathcal{F}\) coefficients provided.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for QED radiative corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering beyond the one-photon-exchange (Born) approximation, motivated by the proton form factor puzzle and the proton radius puzzle that have emerged from high-precision scattering data. The calculation builds on the formalism of t'Hooft and Veltman (1979) and Maximon and Tjon (2000) for scalar loop functions, and provides analytic expressions for the derivatives of \(B_0\), \(C_0\), and \(D_0\) functions that can be evaluated using tools such as COLLIER (Denner et al., 2014). The results enable future numerical comparisons with existing theoretical predictions and lepton-proton scattering data, particularly to test lepton universality by comparing electron-proton and muon-proton scattering.
