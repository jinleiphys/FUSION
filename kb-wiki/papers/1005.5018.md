# D mesons and charmonium states in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures

> We investigate the in-medium masses of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in the isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures arising due to the interactions with the nucleons, the scalar isoscalar meson $σ$, and the scalar iso-vector meson $δ$ within a SU(4) model. The in-medium masses of $J/ψ$ and the excited charmonium states ($ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$) are also calculated in the hot isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in the present investigation. These mass modifications arise due to the interaction of the charmonium states with the gluon condensates of QCD, simulated by a scalar dilaton field introduced to incorporate the broken scale invariance of QCD within the effective chiral model. The change in the mass of $J/ψ$ in the nuclear matter with the density is seen to be rather small, as has been shown in the literature by using various approaches, whereas, the masses of the excited states of charmonium ($ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$) are seen to have considerable drop at high densities. The present study of the in-medium masses of $D$ ($\bar{D}$) mesons as well as of the charmonium states will be of relevance for the observables from the compressed baryonic matter, like the production and collective flow of the $D$ ($\bar D$) mesons, resulting from the asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments planned at the future facility of the FAIR, GSI. The mass modifications of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons as well as of the charmonium states in hot nuclear medium can modify the decay of the charmonium states ($Ψ^{'}, χ_{c}, J/Ψ$) to $D\bar{D}$ pairs in the hot dense hadronic matter. The small attractive potentials observed for the $\bar{D}$ mesons may lead to formation of the $\bar{D}$ mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of D and anti-D mesons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures are modified by interactions with nucleons and scalar mesons (sigma and delta) within an SU(4) model, but the SU(4) symmetry is used only to obtain interactions with the light hadron sector while observed heavy hadron masses and empirical decay constants are used, because chiral symmetry is explicitly broken for the SU(4) case due to the large charm quark mass.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(3) model in the mean field approximation to describe the light hadron sector (nucleons and scalar mesons) in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures, then generalize to SU(4) flavor symmetry to obtain the interactions of D and anti-D mesons with light hadrons. The in-medium masses of charmonium states (J/psi, psi(3686), psi(3770)) are calculated from the medium modification of the scalar dilaton field, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD through the broken scale invariance of the effective chiral model, using the leading order QCD formula. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta) and the dilaton field chi are solved to obtain density- and temperature-dependent values, with the dilaton field related to the scalar gluon condensate via the relation <(alpha_s/pi) G^a_{munu} G^{munu a}> = (8/9)(1-d) chi^4.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mass of the D meson is taken as 1869 MeV and the anti-D meson as 1864.5 MeV.
- The parameter d in the scale breaking terms is taken as 0.064, fitted to reproduce vacuum masses of eta and eta' mesons, a sigma meson mass around 500 MeV, and zero pressure at nuclear matter saturation density rho_0.
- For three flavors and three colors, the one-loop QCD beta function gives d = 6/33; for three colors and two flavors, d = 4/33.
- At nuclear matter density and zero temperature, the coupled channel approach generates the Lambda_c(2593) resonance 45 MeV below its free space position and the Sigma_c(2770) resonance 40 MeV below its free space position.
- At T = 100 MeV, the resonance positions shift to 2579 MeV for Lambda_c (I=0) and 2767 MeV for Sigma_c (I=1).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D meson properties in symmetric hot nuclear matter using the SU(4) model (Kumar and Mishra, 2010) and in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature (Kumar and Mishra, 2010) to include finite temperature effects. It also builds on the chiral SU(3) model framework used to study vector meson properties (Zschiesche et al., 1997; Mishra et al., 2004) and kaon/antikaon optical potentials (Mishra et al., 2004) in nuclear matter. The results are relevant for interpreting observables from compressed baryonic matter experiments at FAIR, GSI, particularly the production and collective flow of D and anti-D mesons, and may enable predictions for the formation of anti-D mesic nuclei due to the small attractive potentials observed for anti-D mesons.
