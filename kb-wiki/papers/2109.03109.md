# SU(3) flavor symmetry considerations for the $\bar{K}N$ coupled channels system

> We study the impact of SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking on the properties of dynamically generated $Λ^*$ states within an effective separable potential model describing the coupled channels $\bar{K}N$ system. The model is based on the chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian at next-to-leading order, and constitutes an improvement over a previous model developed by our group, with its applicability being extended to higher energies covering the $Λ(1670)$ resonance region. It is demonstrated that the ratios of channel couplings to the resonant states can vary dramatically when the flavor breaking is gradually switched off, tracing a path to the restored SU(3) symmetry. We conclude that the couplings determined from physical observables cannot be used to reliably relate a given resonance to a specific flavor multiplet.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ratios of channel couplings to dynamically generated $\Lambda^*$ resonances vary dramatically when SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking is gradually switched off, and concludes that couplings determined from physical observables cannot be used to reliably relate a given resonance to a specific flavor multiplet, even though the resonances can be traced back to well-defined SU(3) multiplets in the symmetric limit.

## Method

The authors construct a separable potential model for the coupled $\bar{K}N$ system based on the chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian at next-to-leading order, using Yamaguchi form factors with channel-dependent inverse ranges to regularize loop functions. They fit 12 parameters (6 inverse ranges, 4 d-couplings, $b_0$, and $b_F$) to experimental data including $K^-p$ total cross sections, threshold branching ratios, kaonic hydrogen characteristics, and $\eta\Lambda$ and $\eta\Sigma$ cross sections. They then introduce a scaling parameter $x$ that continuously varies hadron masses, decay constants, and inverse ranges from the SU(3) symmetric limit ($x=0$) to the physical limit ($x=1$), tracking the movement of resonance poles and their coupling ratios along this trajectory.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2/dof = 1.31$ for the fit to experimental data
- Pole positions in the physical limit: $z_1 = (1353, -43)$ MeV, $z_2 = (1429, -24)$ MeV (both assigned to $\Lambda(1405)$), $z_3 = (1677, -14)$ MeV (assigned to $\Lambda(1670)$)
- In the SU(3) symmetric limit ($x=0$), the singlet pole appears at $z_{\bm{(1)}} = 1482$ MeV, one octet pole at $z_{\bm{(8a)}} = 1536$ MeV, and the second octet pole at $z_{\bm{(8b)}} = 1561$ MeV
- The $z_3$ pole trajectory ends at $z_3(x=0) = 1551$ MeV, 10 MeV below the $\bm{(8b)}$ octet state and on a different Riemann sheet
- A shadow pole $z'_3 = (1676, 5.4)$ MeV is found on the $[+,-,-,+]$ Riemann sheet
- Kaonic hydrogen predictions: $\Delta E_N(1s) = 307$ eV, $\Gamma(1s) = 590$ eV (experiment: $283\pm42$ eV and $541\pm111$ eV)
- Threshold branching ratios: $\gamma = 2.36$, $R_c = 0.656$, $R_n = 0.207$

## Context

This work extends earlier SU(3) symmetry studies by Jido et al. (2003) that used only leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions, by including Born terms and next-to-leading-order contact terms necessary to describe data up to the $\Lambda(1670)$ region. The model improves upon the earlier Prague NLO30 model by Cieplý and Smejkal (2011) by fitting higher-energy cross sections to $\eta\Lambda$ and $\eta\Sigma$ channels, and shows agreement with the Kyoto-Munich model by Ikeda et al. (2012) for the $K^-p$ amplitude energy dependence. The results enable future studies of how flavor symmetry breaking affects resonance classification in coupled-channel systems, while cautioning against using physical coupling ratios to assign resonances to specific SU(3) multiplets.
