# Nuclear Hadronization: Within or Without?

> Nuclei are unique analyzers for the early stage of the space-time development of hadronization. DIS at medium energies is especially suitable for this task being sensitive to hadronization dynamics, since the production length is comparable with the nuclear size. This was the driving motivation to propose measurements at HERMES using nuclear targets, and to provide predictions based on a pQCD model of hadronization [1]. Now when the first results of the experiment are released [2,3], one can compare the predictions with the data. The model successfully describes with no adjustment the nuclear effects for various energies, zh, pT, and Q2, for different flavors and different nuclei. It turns out that the main source of nuclear suppression of the hadron production rate is attenuation of colorless pre-hadrons in the medium. An alternative model [4] is based upon an ad hoc assumption that the colorless pre-hadron is produced outside the nucleus. This model has apparent problems attempting to explain certain features of the results from HERMES. A good understanding of the hadronization dynamics is important for proper interpretation of the strong suppression of high-pT hadrons observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We demonstrate that the production length is even shorter in this case and keeps contracting with rising pT.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear suppression of hadron production in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) is primarily caused by attenuation of colorless pre-hadrons inside the nucleus, and that an alternative model assuming pre-hadrons are produced outside the nucleus has difficulty explaining HERMES data; the authors hedge that their perturbative QCD model successfully describes nuclear effects with no adjustment, but only for the specific kinematic range where Bjorken x is sufficiently large (x ~ 0.1) and valence quarks dominate.

## Method

The authors develop a perturbative QCD model of hadronization where gluon bremsstrahlung from the struck quark is treated with coherence time effects, and the leading hadron originates from a colorless q-qbar dipole formed when the last radiated gluon splits. They compute the production time distribution for pre-hadrons including Sudakov suppression, then calculate nuclear attenuation by modeling the absorption of these pre-hadrons as they propagate through the nuclear medium, using a dipole cross section that incorporates color transparency effects. The model predictions are compared to HERMES data on semi-inclusive hadron production from nuclear targets without any parameter adjustment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier predictions (Kopeliovich et al., 1995) for HERMES measurements of semi-inclusive hadron production on nuclear targets, now comparing with the first released data (HERMES collaboration, 2003). It contradicts the model of induced energy loss (Wang and Wang, 2002; Arleo, 2002) which assumes pre-hadrons are produced outside the nucleus and uses a simple shift of the fragmentation function variable. The results enable more reliable interpretation of high-p_T hadron suppression observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, as the authors demonstrate that production lengths are even shorter in that context and contract with rising p_T.
