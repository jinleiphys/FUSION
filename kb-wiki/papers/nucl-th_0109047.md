# Single particle properties of 16O and 40Ca

> We discuss some single particle properties of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca using correlated basis function theory and Fermi hypernetted chain equations with central and tensor correlations. In particular, we concentrate on one body density matrix, momentum distribution, natural orbits and quasi hole states. The correlations are variationally generated by a realistic hamiltonian containing the Argonne $v'_{8}$ two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The correlated momentum distributions show the well known enhancement at large momenta, with a relative importance of the different correlations (Jastrow and tensor) similar to that in nuclear matter. The natural orbits and their occupation numbers are obtained by diagonalization of the density matrix. The correlated first natural orbits occupations are depleted by more than 10%, whereas the first following ones are occupied by a few percent. The spectroscopic factors of the valence states are lowered by $\sim 8-12%$ with respect to unity by central and tensor correlations, confirming that short range correlations alone are not able to explain the values extracted from $(e,e'p)$ experiments.

# Single particle properties of 16O and 40Ca

## Key claim

Correlated basis function theory with central and tensor correlations, using the Argonne v'8 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, depletes spectroscopic factors of valence states by approximately 8-12% relative to unity, but this reduction is insufficient to explain the values extracted from (e,e'p) experiments (which give S_h ~ 0.6-0.7), indicating that short-range correlations alone cannot account for the experimental quenching.

## Method

The authors employed correlated basis function (CBF) theory with Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) integral equations to compute the one-body density matrix, momentum distributions, natural orbits, and quasihole overlap functions for 16O and 40Ca. The correlated wave function was constructed as a symmetrized product of two-body correlation operators (including central, spin-isospin, and tensor components) acting on a Slater determinant of Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions, with the correlation functions determined variationally using the Argonne v'8 two-nucleon potential plus Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- First natural orbit occupations in 16O: 1s state n=0.858 (f6 correlations), 1p state n=0.919 (f6 correlations); in 40Ca: 1s state n=0.864, 2s state n=0.780, 1d state n=0.956 (all f6 correlations)
- Jastrow-only first natural orbit occupations: 1s state n=0.960 in 16O, n=0.952 in 40Ca; 1p state n=0.980 in 16O
- Spectroscopic factor for 1p state in 16O with f6 correlations: S=0.90; with Jastrow only: S=0.98
- Spectroscopic factor for 1s state in 16O with f6 correlations: S=0.70; in 40Ca: S=0.55
- Spectroscopic factors for valence states in 40Ca with f6 correlations: 2s state S=0.86, 1d state S=0.87
- Experimental spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p): 16O p1/2 ground state S=0.61, p3/2 total strength S=0.61; 40Ca d3/2 ground state S=0.61±0.07
- Tensor correlations enhance the high-momentum tail of momentum distributions by a factor of 2-3 relative to Jastrow correlations

## Context

This work extends CBF/FHNC calculations previously applied to nuclear matter (Fabrocini 1998, 2000) to finite nuclei 16O and 40Ca, achieving comparable accuracy. The results are compared with Green function calculations by Polls, Muther, and Dickhoff (1995) using the Bonn B potential, which found higher occupation numbers for occupied shell model states (n1s=0.921, n1p=0.941) than the present CBF results, attributed to the stronger correlations induced by the Argonne v'8 plus Urbana IX interaction. The variational Monte Carlo estimate by Van Neck et al. (1998) for the 1p spectroscopic factor in 16O agrees with the present CBF value of 0.90. The remaining discrepancy with experimental (e,e'p) spectroscopic factors (0.6-0.7) is attributed to missing 2h-1p perturbative corrections and coupling to surface vibrations, analogous to findings in nuclear matter by Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni (1990) where second-order CBF corrections reduced the quasihole strength from ~0.88 to ~0.70.
