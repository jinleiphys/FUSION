# Regularized pseudopotential for mean-field calculations

> We present preliminary results obtained with a finite-range two-body pseudopotential complemented with zero-range spin-orbit and density-dependent terms. After discussing the penalty function used to adjust parameters, we discuss predictions for binding energies of spherical nuclei calculated at the mean-field level, and we compare them with those obtained using the standard Gogny D1S finite-range effective interaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary results showing that a finite-range regularized pseudopotential, when adjusted with a penalty function that includes infinite-nuclear-matter properties and binding energies of 17 spherical nuclei, can reproduce binding energies of 214 spherical even-even nuclei with root-mean-squared deviations comparable to or better than the standard Gogny D1S interaction, but only for effective masses above 0.75 and at higher orders (N²LO, N³LO).

## Method

The authors constructed a finite-range two-body pseudopotential with a Gaussian regulator of range *a* (varied from 0.8 fm to 1.6 fm), complemented by a zero-range spin-orbit term and a zero-range density-dependent term of the same form as in Gogny D1S. They adjusted 10 parameters at NLO, 14 at N²LO, and 18 at N³LO using a penalty function that includes infinite nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, compression modulus, effective mass, symmetry energy, and its slope), pairing gaps in infinite matter, binding energies of 17 spherical nuclei, proton radii, and constraints to avoid finite-size instabilities and unphysical vector pairing. The adjustment was performed in three steps: exploratory fits to fix canonical nuclear matter values, systematic determination of optimal regulator ranges, and final readjustment with narrow tolerances on nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density fixed to ρ_sat = 0.158 fm⁻³ with tolerance 0.003 fm⁻³.
* Symmetry energy coefficient fixed to J = 29.0 MeV with tolerance 0.5 MeV.
* Symmetry energy slope fixed to L = 15.00 MeV with tolerance 0.05 MeV; the authors note this is very low compared to current realistic values.
* Binding energy per nucleon in symmetric matter targeted to E/A = -16.0 MeV with tolerance 0.3 MeV.
* Compression modulus targeted to K_∞ = 230 MeV with tolerance 5 MeV.
* Effective mass m*/m varied from 0.70 to 0.90 with tolerance 0.001.
* For the 214 spherical even-even nuclei, the smallest average root-mean-squared deviation √(ΔE²)_all = 1.458 MeV was obtained at N³LO with m*/m = 0.85.
* For the 17 nuclei in the penalty function, the smallest √(ΔE²)_fit = 1.228 MeV was obtained at N³LO with m*/m = 0.90.
* At NLO with m*/m = 0.70, √(ΔE²)_all = 1.840 MeV and √(ΔE²)_fit = 1.899 MeV.
* At N²LO with m*/m = 0.70, √(ΔE²)_all = 2.028 MeV and √(ΔE²)_fit = 1.893 MeV.
* At N³LO with m*/m = 0.70, √(ΔE²)_all = 1.712 MeV and √(ΔE²)_fit = 1.587 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the class of regularized pseudopotentials introduced by Dobaczewski (2012), Raimondi (2014), and Bennaceur (2017) by complementing them with zero-range spin-orbit and density-dependent terms for use at the mean-field level. The results are compared to the standard Gogny D1S finite-range effective interaction (developed by Berger, Girod, and Gogny), which is typically used in beyond-mean-field approaches like the 5-dimensional Collective Hamiltonian. The study enables future systematic comparisons of binding energies, proton radii, single-particle energies, and deformed nuclei properties, with open questions remaining about which correlations can be incorporated into the coupling constants at the mean-field level.
