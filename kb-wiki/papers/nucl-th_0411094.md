# Description of resonances in light nuclei using a microscopic cluster model

> We investigate resonances in light halo nuclei using a fully microscopic cluster model and the complex scaling method. We make use of the hermitian representation of the complex scaling method. The general structure of the cluster model is that of a correlation operator acting on a starting function that describes a number of neutrons relative to an alpha-particle. The correlation operator is expanded in terms of a small non-orthogonal set of Gaussian basis states and we make use of a simplified, central but state-dependent, interaction. The many-body integrals required for the computation are evaluated by a variational Monte-Carlo algorithm. We show how to obtain resonant states for both $^5$He and $^6$He.

# Description of resonances in light nuclei using a microscopic cluster model

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a fully microscopic cluster model combined with the hermitian representation of the complex scaling method can successfully identify low-lying resonances in both $^5$He and $^6$He, even though the simplified central nucleon-nucleon interaction (S3) lacks spin-orbit force and therefore produces no bound states for these nuclei. The results are qualitative rather than quantitative, with resonance positions determined only within a range due to the approximate nature of the calculation.

## Method
The authors employ a fully microscopic cluster model where the wavefunction is constructed as a correlation operator acting on a reference function describing neutrons relative to an alpha-particle core. The correlation operator combines a multiplicative Jastrow factor for short-range correlations with an additive configuration-interaction term for long-range correlations, expanded in a small non-orthogonal set of Gaussian basis states (9 components). The complex scaling method is applied using the hermitian representation of Moiseyev and Bylicki, where the resonance energy $E_r$ and width $-2E_i$ become variational parameters, and the many-body matrix elements are evaluated via variational Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers
- Alpha-particle ground state energy: approximately -27.3 MeV from the variational approximation at $\theta=0$
- For $^5$He ($L=1$, $J^\pi=1/2^-,3/2^-$): resonance inferred at $E_r = -26 \pm 0.5$ MeV, $E_i = -23.2 \pm 0.5$ MeV for $\theta=0.35$, corresponding to 1.3 $\pm$ 0.5 MeV above the $^4$He threshold with a width of $2E_i = -46.4 \pm 1$ MeV
- Experimentally, $^5$He is unbound by 0.798 MeV and $^6$He ground state lies 0.973 MeV below the $^4$He + $2n$ threshold; the first $^6$He resonance ($J^\pi=2^+$) lies 1.797 MeV above the ground state
- The optimal width parameter $w$ for the $^5$He resonance lies in the range between 5 and 6 (arbitrary units)

## Context
This work extends the microscopic cluster model approach of Csoto (2000) for three-body resonances in $^6$He, $^6$Li, and $^6$Be using complex scaling in coordinate space, and contrasts with ab-initio Monte Carlo approaches such as Wiringa (1995) that have difficulties describing resonances. The hermitian representation of the complex scaling method, formulated by Moiseyev (1983) and simplified by Bylicki (1991), provides additional stability when working with small basis sets. The results enable future work with more realistic interactions including spin-orbit force, which should produce a bound state for $^6$He and more quantitative resonance parameters, as well as extension to heavier systems where the explicit antisymmetrization becomes the main computational challenge.
