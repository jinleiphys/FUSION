# Dirac Particles in Twisted Tubes

> We consider the dynamics of a relativistic Dirac particle constrained to move in the interior of a twisted tube by confining boundary conditions, in the approximation that the curvature of the tube is small and slowly varying. In contrast with the nonrelativistic theory, which predicts that a particle's spin does not change as the particle propagates along the tube, we find that the angular momentum eigenstates of a relativistic spin-1/2 particle may behave nontrivially. For example, a particle with its angular momentum initially polarized in the direction of propagation may acquire a nonzero component of angular momentum in the opposite direction on turning through 2 πradians. Also, the usual nonrelativistic effective potential acquires an additional factor in the relativistic theory.

## Key claim

For a relativistic Dirac particle confined to a twisted tube, the angular momentum eigenstates with helicity ±1/2 can mix as they propagate, in contrast to the nonrelativistic case where spin does not change. This mixing occurs because the Wigner-Eckart theorem does not forbid transitions between these states (their eigenvalues differ by 1), unlike the photon case where helicity ±1 states cannot mix.

## Method

The authors model a particle confined to the interior of a tube with circular cross-section using confining boundary conditions (−iγ·n̂ψ = ψ) on the tube surface. They adopt a parallel transport frame along the tube's central curve, perform a unitary transformation to a fixed spin basis, and expand the Dirac Hamiltonian to second order in small parameters (curvature κR ≪ 1 and slowly varying curvature). They then project the squared Dirac operator H² onto the four-dimensional adiabatic subspace of lowest transverse energy eigenstates with helicity ±1/2.

## Key numbers

- In the ultrarelativistic limit (m → 0), the depolarizing factor D ≈ 0.767, where the lowest root of J₀(qR) = J₁(qR) gives qR ≈ 1.434.
- In the nonrelativistic limit (m ≫ q), the depolarizing factor D → 1, and the boundary condition J₀(qR) = 0 reproduces the familiar nonrelativistic result.
- The adiabatic conditions require |κR| ≪ 1 and |(∂κʲ/∂s)R²| ≪ 1, where κ is the vector curvature and R is the tube radius.
- The curvature-induced effective potential acquires an additional factor −κ²/4 in the squared Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic twisted tube analysis of Takhtajan (1990s) and the confining potential approach reviewed by Maraner (1990s) to relativistic Dirac particles. It contrasts with the photon propagation in optical fibers studied by Chiao and Wu (1986), where helicity ±1 states do not mix due to gauge invariance. The results enable further study of spin transport in curved geometries and could be extended to other boundary conditions, such as the chiral generalization parameterized by a chiral angle θ.
