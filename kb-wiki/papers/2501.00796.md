# Stellar Weak Rates and Mass Fractions of 20 Most Important $fp$-shell Nuclei with $A < 65$

> This work presents stellar weak rates and mass fractions of 20 most important electron capture (ec) and beta decay (bd) nuclei with $A < 65$ according to a recent study during the presupernova evolution of massive stars. The mass fractions of these nuclei were calculated using the Sahas equation which assumes nuclear statistical equilibrium for a set of initial conditions ($T_9$, $ρ$ and $Y_e$) that represents the trajectory which a massive stars central region takes after its silicon core burns. Our computed mass fractions were found in decent comparison in most cases, and up to a factor 4 difference was noted when compared with the Independent Particle Model results. The weak interaction (ec and bd) rates were calculated in a totally microscopic fashion using the proton neutron quasiparticle random phase ap proximation model and without assuming the Brink Axel hypothesis. The rates were computed for a wide range of density ($10$-$10^{11}$) g/cm$^3$ and temperature (0.01-30) GK. In comparison with large scale shell model, our computed rates were found bigger at high values of core temperature. The current study may contribute in a more realistic simu lation of stellar evolution processes and modeling of core collapse supernovae.

# Stellar Weak Rates and Mass Fractions of 20 Most Important $fp$-shell Nuclei with $A < 65$

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA computed electron capture and beta-decay rates for the 20 most important $fp$-shell nuclei are generally larger than large-scale shell model rates at high core temperatures ($T_9 > 5$ GK), but the comparison is qualified by the fact that at low temperatures the pn-QRPA rates are sometimes smaller, though these low-temperature rates are too small in magnitude to affect simulation results.

## Method

The authors selected 20 $fp$-shell nuclei with $A < 65$ from a previously published list of the 50 most important electron capture and beta-decay nuclei for presupernova evolution. Weak interaction rates were calculated using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable particle-hole and particle-particle forces, without invoking the Brink-Axel hypothesis for excited-state Gamow-Teller strength distributions. Mass fractions were computed using Saha's equation under nuclear statistical equilibrium assumptions, with nuclear partition functions calculated using a discrete summation over excited states up to 10 MeV excitation energy and masses taken from the compilation of Audi et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

- Mass fractions were compared with Independent Particle Model (IPM) results of Aufderheide et al. (1994); differences up to a factor of 4 were noted between the two calculations.
- Weak rates were computed across a density range of $10$ to $10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$ and a temperature range of $0.01$ to $30$ GK.
- For the odd-odd nucleus $^{60}$Co (ranked 4th most important electron capture nucleus by Nabi et al. 2021), at low densities ($\rho Y_e = 10^7$ and $10^8$ g/cm$^3$) and low temperatures ($T_9 = 1$ and $3$ GK), LSSM electron capture rates were orders of magnitude larger than pn-QRPA rates; at $T_9 = 30$ GK, pn-QRPA rates were an order of magnitude larger.
- For $^{56}$Fe (ranked 21st by Nabi et al. 2021), pn-QRPA electron capture rates were up to an order of magnitude smaller than LSSM rates at $T_9 = 1$ GK across all $\rho Y_e$ values, but at $T_9 = 30$ GK, pn-QRPA rates were an order of magnitude larger.
- For $^{57}$Fe (a top-20 beta-decay nucleus), pn-QRPA beta-decay rates were smaller than LSSM rates at $T_9 = 1$, $3$, and $10$ GK for all $\rho Y_e$ values, but up to an order of magnitude larger at $T_9 = 30$ GK.
- For $^{59}$Mn, pn-QRPA beta-decay rates were comparable to LSSM rates at $T_9 = 1$, $3$, and $10$ GK, and an order of magnitude larger at $T_9 = 30$ GK for all $\rho Y_e$ values.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA rate calculations of Nabi et al. (2021), who compiled a list of 50 most important electron capture and beta-decay nuclei from a study of 728 nuclei with $A = 1$–100, and provides refined calculations for the 20 most important $fp$-shell nuclei with $A < 65$ using updated experimental data and optimized model parameters. The results are compared with the large-scale shell model (LSSM) rates of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000) for the mass range $45 \le A \le 65$, and with the Independent Particle Model mass fractions of Aufderheide et al. (1994). The authors attribute the generally larger pn-QRPA rates at high temperatures to the LSSM's use of the Brink-Axel hypothesis for excited-state contributions and limited Lanczos iterations (100 iterations, insufficient for convergence above 2.5 MeV excitation energy), whereas the pn-QRPA model uses a large model space (up to $7\hbar\omega$) and avoids the Brink-Axel hypothesis. The study aims to provide more reliable nuclear input for presupernova evolution simulations and core-collapse supernova modeling; a similar comparison for nuclei with $A > 65$ is reported as work in progress.
