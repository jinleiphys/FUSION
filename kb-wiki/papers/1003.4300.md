# Dispersion corrections to parity violating electron scattering

> We consider the dispersion correction to elastic parity violating electron-proton scattering due to \gammaZ exchange. In a recent publication, this correction was reported to be substantially larger than the previous estimates. In this paper, we study the dispersion correction in greater detail. We confirm the size of the disperion correction to be 6% for the QWEAK experiment designed to measure the proton weak charge. We enumerate parameters that have to be constrained to better than relative 30% in order to keep the theoretical uncertainty for QWEAK under control.

## Key claim

The dispersion correction to parity-violating electron-proton scattering due to γZ exchange is confirmed to be approximately 6% for the QWEAK experiment, substantially larger than previous estimates which had assumed it to be zero at non-zero energies. The theoretical uncertainty from hadronic structure can be kept under control if several isospin scaling parameters are constrained to better than relative 30%.

## Method

The authors compute the real part of the forward γZ dispersion correction Re δ_{γZ_A} using a dispersion relation that relates it to an integral over the imaginary part, which is expressed in terms of parity-violating deep inelastic scattering structure functions. They model the electromagnetic structure functions using three different approaches: Model I uses resonance parameters from Bianchi (2004) and Regge contributions from Cvetic (2008); Model II uses resonance and background fits from Bosted (2007); Model III combines the background from Model I with the resonance contributions from Model II. Isospin scaling factors ξ_{Z/γ} are then applied to convert electromagnetic cross sections into γZ interference contributions for each resonance and the non-resonant background.

## Key numbers

- The dispersion correction Re δ_{γZ_A} at the QWEAK energy E_lab = 1.165 GeV is (5.85 ± 0.45_mod ± 1.23_iso)%.
- Individual contributions to the dispersion correction at QWEAK energy (Model III): P_{33}(1232) = 1.27%, S_{11}(1535) = 0.44%, D_{13}(1520) = 0.21%, F_{15}(1680) = 0.06%, S_{11}(1665) = 0.05%, P_{11}(1440) = 0.10%, F_{37}(1950) = 0.50%, non-resonant background = 3.61%.
- The isospin scaling factor for the non-resonant background is ξ_{Z/γ}^{NR} = 1.075(1 ± 0.34), corresponding to a 100% uncertainty on the strange quark contribution.
- Constraining ξ_{Z/γ}^{P_{33}(1232)} to better than 20% would translate into 0.25% uncertainty on the dispersion correction; constraining ξ_{Z/γ}^{S_{11}(1535)} to relative 30% would lead to 0.13% uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation calculation of Gorchtein, Horowitz, and Ramsey-Musolf (2009) which first reported the large 6% correction, contradicting earlier assumptions that the axial-vector γZ box correction δ_{γZ_A} was negligible at non-zero energies (Sirlin, 1978). The result is critical for the QWEAK experiment at Jefferson Lab, which aims to measure the proton weak charge Q_W^p to 4% precision (combined 2% experimental and 2% theoretical uncertainties). The authors identify that the dominant theoretical uncertainty comes from the isospin decomposition of electromagnetic data, particularly for the high-energy non-resonant background, and outline the precision needed for future measurements or model constraints to enable the QWEAK physics reach.
