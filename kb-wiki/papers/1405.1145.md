# Theoretical study of the $Λ$(1520) photoproduction off proton target based on the new CLAS data

> Based on the high precision experimental data released by the CLAS Collaboration recently, the interaction mechanism of the photoproduction of $Λ$(1520) off a proton target is investigated within a Regge-plus-resonance approach. With the decay amplitudes predicted in the constituent quark model, the roles played by nucleon resonances are studied. It is found that $N(2120)$ provides the most important contribution among the nucleon resonances predicted in the constituent quark model. The $t$ channel contribution with Regge trajectories and the $Λ$ intermediate $u$ channel are responsible to the behaviors of the differential cross section at forward and backward angles, respectively.

## Key claim

Based on the new high-precision CLAS data, the photoproduction of Λ(1520) off a proton target is described within a Regge-plus-resonance approach, where the nucleon resonance N(2120) provides the most important contribution among the nucleon resonances predicted by the constituent quark model, but the contact term (Born term) is actually dominant at all energies considered.

## Method

The authors employ a Regge-plus-resonance approach, combining effective Lagrangians for Born terms (s-channel nucleon, t-channel K and K* exchange, u-channel Λ intermediate, and a contact term) with Regge trajectories for the t-channel meson exchanges to describe the high-energy behavior. Nucleon resonances are included using decay amplitudes and helicity amplitudes predicted by the constituent quark model, with a selection criterion λ = (A₁/₂² + A₃/₂²)(G(ℓ₁)² + G(ℓ₂)²)·10⁵ > 0.01, which yields six resonances. The model parameters are fitted to the CLAS13 and LEPS10 differential cross section data using the MINUIT code, with gauge invariance restored via a generalized contact term and a redefinition of amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the KNΛ* vertex is g_KNΛ* = 10.5, obtained from the PDG decay width of Λ(1520) → NK.
- The u-channel coupling constants for the Λ intermediate state are fᵘ₁ = -7.22 and fᵘ₂ = 9.48, derived from helicity amplitudes A₃/₂ = -122 and A₁/₂ = 13 (in units of 10⁻³/√GeV) and the PDG decay width Γ_γ = 133 keV.
- The coupling constant g_KNΛ = -13.24 is determined by flavor SU(3) symmetry.
- The best-fit Regge trajectory parameters include √s_Reg = 2.23 ± 0.08 GeV and √t_Reg = 1.71 ± 0.97 GeV².
- The reduced χ² for the full model fitting both CLAS13 and LEPS10 data is 3.05 (without systematic uncertainties) and 1.38 (with systematic uncertainties), compared to χ² = 2.5 in a prior work by Xie (2013).
- Turning off the u channel increases χ² to 4.10; turning off all nucleon resonances increases χ² to 4.82; turning off the Regge trajectory increases χ² to 8.39.
- The resonance N(2120) has the largest selection criterion value λ = 2.14 among the six considered resonances.
- The Breit-Wigner widths for all nucleon resonances are set to 330 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Λ(1520) photoproduction that used only LEPS10 data at forward angles (Xie, 2010; He, 2012) by incorporating the new CLAS13 data (Moriya, 2013) covering a large range of kaon production angles. It improves upon a recent analysis by Xie (2013) that found the u channel important at backward angles but did not reproduce forward-angle data well, by introducing Regge trajectories for the t-channel exchanges. The results enable future experimental checks of the predicted smooth behavior at extreme forward angles (cosθ = 0.95) and support assigning the [N 3/2⁻]₃ state to N(2120) rather than N(1875) as suggested in some PDG versions.
