# Nucleon-antinucleon annihilation at LEAR

> This report is a historical review of the salient results in low energy antiproton-proton and antineutron-proton annihilation obtained at the Low Energy Antiproton Ring (LEAR), which was operated at CERN between 1983 and 1996. The intention is to provide guidelines for future experiments at the CERN AD/ELENA complex and elsewhere. In the spirit of this workshop, hadron spectroscopy - one of the cornerstones at LEAR - is briefly mentioned, while emphasis is put on the annihilation mechanism on one and two nucleons, the final state multiplicity distributions and the contributions from quarks, in particular in annihilation channels involving strangeness.

## Key claim

This historical review of low-energy antiproton-proton and antineutron-proton annihilation at LEAR aims to provide guidelines for future experiments at the CERN AD/ELENA complex, but the author notes that many of the predicted narrow baryonium states were not confirmed by LEAR data, and the annihilation mechanism remains incompletely understood, particularly regarding dynamical selection rules whose origin is still open to discussion.

## Method

The paper reviews results from the LEAR facility (1983-1996) focusing on three major experiments: ASTERIX, which studied antiproton-proton annihilation at rest in gaseous hydrogen and measured x-rays from protonium cascades; CRYSTAL BARREL, which used a 1380-crystal CsI(Tl) calorimeter and jet drift chamber to study annihilation at rest in liquid hydrogen with high statistics for multiphoton final states; and OBELIX, which studied antiproton-proton and antiproton-neutron annihilations with large acceptance. The review covers protonium spectroscopy, final state multiplicity distributions, two-body annihilation branching fractions, and meson spectroscopy including searches for exotic states.

## Key numbers

- Fraction of P-wave annihilation in liquid hydrogen: (13 ± 4)%
- Fraction of P-wave annihilation in gas at NTP: (56 ± 7)%
- Branching fraction for antiproton-proton annihilation at rest into π⁰π⁰: (6.93 ± 0.43) × 10⁻⁴
- Branching fraction for antiproton-proton annihilation at rest into π⁺π⁻ from S-wave: (3.19 ± 0.20) × 10⁻³; from P-wave: (4.81 ± 0.49) × 10⁻³
- Branching fraction for antiproton-proton annihilation at rest into K⁺K⁻ from S-wave: (1.08 ± 0.05) × 10⁻³; from P-wave: (2.87 ± 0.51) × 10⁻⁴
- Mass and width of the f₂(1565) meson (formerly AX): 1565 ± 10 MeV and 170 ± 20 MeV
- Average charged pion multiplicity at 1.6 GeV/c: 3.46 ± 0.04; average π⁰ multiplicity: 1.92 ± 0.04
- Average total pion multiplicity at rest in liquid hydrogen: ⟨n⟩ = 5, with rms width σ = 1.04 ± 0.01
- Shift of the 1s protonium level from QED prediction: −0.72 ± 0.04 keV; width: 1.11 ± 0.07 keV

## Context

This review extends the earlier bubble chamber work of Armenteros and French (1969) and the Gargamelle measurements of Fett et al. (1977) by providing comprehensive data on neutral pion multiplicities and two-body annihilation channels that were inaccessible before LEAR. The results contradict the earlier claims of narrow baryonium states such as the S-meson reported by Bruckner et al. (1977) and others, which were not confirmed by LEAR measurements. The review enables future experiments at the CERN AD/ELENA complex to target specific open questions, particularly the nature of the f₂(1565) as a possible baryonium or tetraquark state, the origin of dynamical selection rules in nucleon-antinucleon annihilation, and the role of strangeness production in the annihilation mechanism.
