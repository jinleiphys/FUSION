# Nuclear surface properties in relativistic effective field theory

> We perform Hartree calculations of symmetric and asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter in the framework of relativistic models based on effective hadronic field theories as recently proposed in the literature. In addition to the conventional cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions, the extended models incorporate a quartic vector self-interaction, scalar-vector non-linearities and tensor couplings of the vector mesons. We investigate the implications of these terms on nuclear surface properties such as the surface energy coefficient, surface thickness, surface stiffness coefficient, neutron skin thickness and the spin-orbit force.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how additional non-linear meson interactions and tensor couplings, motivated by effective field theory and the principle of naturalness, affect nuclear surface properties; the authors find that these new terms have only a minor impact on surface energy and thickness when their coupling constants remain within the natural range, but can help to bring calculated surface properties into better agreement with empirical values.

## Method

The authors perform quantal Hartree calculations of symmetric and asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter, solving the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for the scalar and vector meson fields in a one-dimensional geometry. They systematically introduce each additional term from the effective Lagrangian (quartic vector self-interaction, scalar-vector couplings, gradient couplings, and tensor coupling) one at a time, fitting the remaining coupling constants to reproduce chosen nuclear matter saturation properties (density, energy per particle, incompressibility, effective mass). Surface properties are extracted from the resulting density profiles and energy densities.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy coefficient \(E_s\) and surface thickness \(t\) (90%-10% fall-off distance) are computed for nuclear matter with saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\), energy per particle \(a_v = -16.42\) MeV, incompressibilities \(K = 125\), 200, and 350 MeV, effective masses \(M^*_\infty/M = 0.6\) and 0.7, and scalar meson masses \(m_s = 490\) and 525 MeV.
- For the quartic vector self-interaction, the naturalness condition corresponds to \(\eta_0 \geq 2\); at \(\eta_0 = \infty\) (no quartic vector term) with \(K=200\) MeV, \(M^*_\infty/M=0.6\), and \(m_s=490\) MeV, the surface energy and thickness are approximately \(E_s \approx 18\) MeV and \(t \approx 2.2\) fm.
- The spin-orbit potential from the fully relativistic calculation is deeper than its non-relativistic Foldy-Wouthuysen limit by about 20% for \(M^*_\infty/M = 0.6\) and about 15% for \(M^*_\infty/M = 0.7\).
- For the scalar-vector couplings, the naturalness range for \(\eta_1\) and \(\eta_2\) (introduced separately) is roughly from \(-0.5\) to \(2.5\) when \(K=200\) MeV and \(M^*_\infty/M=0.6\).

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field studies of nuclear surface properties, which had been performed within the standard non-linear sigma-omega model (with only cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions) by Hofer and Stocker (1989), Von-Eiff et al. (1994, 1995), and Centelles et al. (1993, 1998). The paper incorporates the additional non-linear couplings and tensor terms recently proposed by Serot et al. (1997) and Furnstahl et al. (1996, 1997) on the basis of effective field theory and naturalness. The results enable a systematic assessment of which terms in the effective Lagrangian are most important for surface properties and provide guidance for constructing improved relativistic energy density functionals for finite nuclei.
