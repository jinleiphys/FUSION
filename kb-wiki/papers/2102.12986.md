# Role of tensor terms of the Skyrme energy-density functional on neutron deformed magic numbers in the rare-earth region

> The role of the tensor part of the nuclear interaction is actively investigated in recent years due to experimental advancement yielding new data in nuclei far from the $β$-stability line. In this article we study the effect of this part of the nuclear interaction on deformed neutron magic numbers in the rare-earth region within the Skyrme energy-density functional for various TIJ \cite{lesinski:2007} parametrizations. Two quantities signaling magic numbers are considered: two-neutron separation energies and single-particle energies. They are calculated in isotopic series involving well-deformed rare-earth nuclei ranging from $Z=64$ to $Z=72$ in the $N=100$ region. Obtained results show that, whereas the neutron-proton tensor contribution to binding energies is important to reproduce neutron sub-shell closure at $N = 104$ in heavier rare earths Yb ($Z=70$) and Hf ($Z=72$) isotopes, like-particle tensor also plays a role in the single-particle spectrum around Fermi level and is even favored in lighter Gd ($Z=64$) and Dy ($Z=66$) rare-earth isotopes.

# Role of tensor terms of the Skyrme energy-density functional on neutron deformed magic numbers in the rare-earth region

## Key claim

The neutron-proton tensor coupling is essential to reproduce the neutron sub-shell closure at N=104 in heavier rare-earth isotopes (Yb with Z=70 and Hf with Z=72), while like-particle tensor coupling is favored in lighter rare-earth isotopes (Gd with Z=64 and Dy with Z=66) and tends to remove the N=104 peak in heavier nuclei. The authors qualify that the situation in lighter rare-earth elements remains unclear and requires further work.

## Method

Skyrme Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations were performed for even-even rare-earth nuclei with proton numbers Z=64 to Z=72 in the neutron number N=100 region, using multiple TIJ parametrizations of the Skyrme energy-density functional (T22, T24, T26, T42, T62, T41, T44) as well as SIII, SIII+T, SLy5, and SLy5+T for comparison. The seniority force approximated residual pairing interactions, and single-particle wave functions were expanded on a deformed harmonic oscillator basis with optimized oscillator parameters. Two-neutron separation energies and their differentials, along with neutron single-particle energy spectra, were calculated to identify deformed magic numbers.

## Key numbers

- Two persistent peaks in the two-neutron separation energy differential were found at N=100 and N=104 across all considered nuclei with the T22 parameter set (where alpha=beta=0), except for Hf (Z=72)
- The like-particle tensor coupling constant alpha is defined as alpha = 60 (J-2) MeV fm^3, and the neutron-proton tensor coupling constant beta is defined as beta = 60 (I-2) MeV fm^3
- In the Gogny D1ST2a parametrization discussed for context, the like-nucleon tensor term strength was -20 MeV and the neutron-proton term strength was 115 MeV
- The experimental two-neutron separation energy differential shows a peak at N=104 in Er (Z=68), Yb (Z=70), and Hf (Z=72) isotopes from AME2016 data
- In Dy (Z=66) isotopes, experimental peaks are seen at N=98, N=102, and possibly N=106

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of deformed magic numbers in the rare-earth region, which have been experimentally contested by groups such as Asai (1999), Patel (2014), Wu (2017), and Hartley (2018, 2020). Previous theoretical work by Satpathy (2004), Ghorui (2012), and Kaur (2020) supported the magicity of N=100 in light rare-earth elements, while Yadav (2002) suggested N=102 as a magic number in Dy isotopes. The study builds on the TIJ parametrizations developed by Lesinski (2007) for the Skyrme energy-density functional, which include tensor terms fitted to nuclear properties. The results enable further investigation into the role of tensor interactions in explaining deformed shell closures, particularly the N=104 sub-shell closure in heavy rare-earth nuclei, and highlight the need for more experimental data in light rare-earth isotopes to resolve discrepancies between different experimental findings.
