# Unpolarized Transverse Momentum Distributions from a global fit of Drell-Yan and Semi-Inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering data

> We present an extraction of unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution and fragmentation functions based on more than two thousands data points from several experiments for two different processes: semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan production of lepton pairs. The baseline analysis is performed with a Monte Carlo replica method resumming large logarithms at N3LL accuracy. The resulting description of the data is very good ($χ^2/N_{\rm dat} = 1.06$). For semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, predictions for multiplicities are normalized by factors that cure the discrepancy with data introduced by higher-order perturbative corrections.

## Key claim

The MAP collaboration presents a global extraction of unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions (MAPTMD22) from 2031 data points, achieving a good fit quality of χ²/Ndat = 1.06, but the description of semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data required ad-hoc normalization factors to correct a discrepancy introduced by higher-order perturbative corrections beyond NLL.

## Method

The analysis uses TMD factorization at N³LL⁻ accuracy, combining TMD evolution at N³LL level with hard factors and matching coefficients at order αs², NNLO collinear PDFs, and NLO collinear FFs. The full TMDs are constructed from perturbative components regularized via the b* prescription and nonperturbative parts described as sums of Gaussians and weighted Gaussians, with 21 free parameters fitted using a Monte Carlo replica method with 250 replicas. Data from Drell-Yan production (484 points from Tevatron, LHC, RHIC, and fixed-target experiments) and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (1547 points from HERMES and COMPASS) are included, with kinematic cuts |qT| < 0.2 Q for DY and a more restrictive transverse momentum cut for SIDIS.

## Key numbers

* Total dataset: 2031 data points (484 DY + 1547 SIDIS) analyzed with χ²/Ndat = 1.06 for the central replica
* DY data: 251 points from collider experiments (Tevatron, LHC, RHIC) and 233 points from fixed-target experiments (E605, E772, E288)
* SIDIS data: 344 points from HERMES and 1203 points from COMPASS, with kinematic cuts Q > 1.4 GeV and 0.2 < z < 0.7
* Fit performed with 250 Monte Carlo replicas and 21 free parameters (11 for TMD PDF, 9 for TMD FF, 1 for Collins-Soper kernel)
* DY kinematic cut: |qT| < 0.2 Q; SIDIS cut: |PhT| < min[min(0.2 Q, 0.5 zQ) + 0.3 GeV, zQ]

## Context

This work extends previous global analyses of SIDIS and DY data by Bacchetta (2017) and Bacchetta (2019) by including new datasets from E772, PHENIX, CMS at 13 TeV, and ATLAS at 13 TeV, as well as the new COMPASS data release. The analysis achieves higher perturbative accuracy (N³LL⁻) compared to earlier fits and provides TMDs that deviate from simple Gaussian shapes, enabling improved predictions for future Electron-Ion Collider studies and precision measurements such as W mass determinations at hadron colliders.
