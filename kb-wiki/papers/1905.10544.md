# A microscopic resolution of the chiral conundrum with crossing twin bands in Ag-106

> The nuclear chiral conundrum with crossing twin bands is investigated with three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory in a fully self-consistent and microscopic way. The energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths for bands 1 and 2 in Ag-106 are well reproduced with two distinct configurations with two and four quasiparticles, respectively. For the four-quasiparticle configuration, a chiral vibrational band on top of band 2 is expected due to the soft Routhian curves. Therefore, it provides a microscopic and solid solution for the chiral conundrum in Ag-106. It also paves the way for understanding similar chiral structure in other nuclei in the future..

## Key claim

The crossing twin bands in Ag-106 are resolved by assigning band 1 to a two-quasiparticle configuration (πg₉/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂) and band 2 to a four-quasiparticle configuration (πg₉/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂(gd)²), with band 3 interpreted as a chiral vibrational partner of band 2 based on soft Routhian curves, but this interpretation for band 3 remains at the mean-field level and requires beyond-mean-field methods for confirmation.

## Method

Three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (3DTAC-CDFT) was applied to Ag-106, using the point-coupling Lagrangian PC-PK1 for the particle-hole channel and a finite-range separable pairing force for the particle-particle channel, with no adjustable parameters. The Dirac equation was solved in a three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells, and configurations were fixed during iteration by maximum overlap between block orbitals. Pairing correlations were treated via tilted axis cranking relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (TAC-RHB) equations.

## Key numbers

- The calculated energy difference between the bandhead of band 1 and the ground state is 0.75 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.87 MeV.
- For the four-quasiparticle configuration at rotational frequency ℏω = 0.25 MeV, the triaxial deformation at the energy minimum is γ ∼ 5°, with the energy rise less than 1.5 MeV when γ reaches ∼25°.
- The polar angle θ of the total angular momentum for the four-quasiparticle configuration varies from 46° to 69° with increasing rotational frequency.
- Convergent results for the two-quasiparticle configuration were obtained only up to around 14ℏ.
- The total energy at rotational frequency ℏω = 0.25 MeV changes by only 0.04% when using 12 major shells instead of 10.

## Context

This work extends the 3DTAC-CDFT approach previously applied to multiple chirality in Rh-106 (Zhao 2017) to resolve the chiral conundrum in Ag-106, where earlier phenomenological particle rotor model calculations (Lieder 2014) could not reproduce the bandhead energy separation. The results contradict the earlier interpretation by Joshi (2007) that the excited partner band arises from an axial shape transformation, and instead support the interpretation from Lieder (2014) and Rather (2014) that both bands have similar shapes. The work enables future beyond-mean-field calculations using random phase approximation or collective Hamiltonian methods to quantitatively describe the chiral vibrational band.
