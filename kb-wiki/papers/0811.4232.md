# Equation of state for dense supernova matter

> We provide an equation of state for high density supernova matter by applying a momentum-dependent effective interaction. We focus on the study of the equation of state of high-density and high-temperature nuclear matter containing leptons (electrons and neutrinos) under the chemical equilibrium condition. The conditions of charge neutrality and equilibrium under $β$-decay process lead first to the evaluation of the lepton fractions and afterwards the evaluation of internal energy, pressure, entropy and in total to the equation of state of hot nuclear matter for various isothermal cases. Thermal effects on the properties and equation of state of nuclear matter are evaluated and analyzed in the framework of the proposed effective interaction model. Since supernova matter is characterized by a constant entropy we also present the thermodynamic properties for isentropic case. Special attention is dedicated to the study of the contribution of the components of $β$-stable nuclear matter to the entropy per particle, a quantity of great interest for the study of structure and collapse of supernova.

## Key claim

The paper provides an equation of state for dense supernova matter using a momentum-dependent effective interaction model that includes temperature effects on both the kinetic and interaction parts of the energy density, but the results are limited to the specific choice of symmetry energy parametrization \(F(u)=u\) and assume chemical equilibrium rather than the dynamic conditions of an actual supernova explosion.

## Method

The authors apply a momentum-dependent effective interaction model (MDIM) to describe baryon interactions in hot asymmetric nuclear matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, and neutrinos under \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They solve the coupled equations for chemical equilibrium \(\mu_n + \mu_{\nu_e} = \mu_p + \mu_e\) and charge neutrality \(Y_p = Y_e\) to determine lepton fractions, then compute the total energy density, pressure, and entropy for both isothermal and isentropic cases. The model uses a parabolic approximation for the free energy \(F(n,T,I) = F(n,T,I=0) + (1-2Y_p)^2 F_{sym}(n,T)\) to simplify the calculation of chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) used as reference density throughout
- Incompressibility \(K = 240\) MeV for symmetric nuclear matter at saturation
- Symmetry energy at zero temperature parametrized as \(E_{sym}^{tot}(n,T=0) = 13 u^{2/3} + 17 u\) where \(u = n/n_0\)
- Lepton fractions considered: \(Y_l = 0.3\) and \(Y_l = 0.4\), characteristic of supernova matter
- Entropy per baryon \(S = 1-2\) (in units of \(k_B\)) characteristic of supernova matter
- For \(F(u)=u\) parametrization, muons appear at \(u \sim 4\) (density \(n \sim 4n_0\))
- Thermal effects on the momentum-dependent term \(V^C\) at \(n=0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) increase by \(3\%-20\%\) for \(T=10-30\) MeV compared to \(T=0\)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of hot \(\beta\)-stable nuclear matter without neutrino trapping (Moustakidis 2008) to include the case of supernova matter where neutrinos are trapped and the lepton fraction remains high (\(Y_l=0.3-0.4\)). The results confirm findings by Takatsuka (1994) that the proton fraction in supernova matter is remarkably constant with density and temperature (\(Y_p \simeq 0.3\) for \(Y_l=0.4\)), and that the equation of state is stiffer than that of cold neutron star matter. The model enables future studies of proto-neutron star structure and supernova collapse dynamics, though the authors note that the chemical composition changes dynamically during supernova evolution and their equilibrium assumption is a simplification.
