# The alternating-parity bands of \element{236,238}{U} and \element{238,240}{Pu} in a particle-number conserving method based on cranked shell model

> The particle-number conserving (PNC) method in the framework of cranked shell model (CSM) is developed to deal with the reflection-asymmetric nuclear system by applying the $S_x$ symmetry. Based on an octupole-deformed Nilsson potential, the alternating-parity bands in \element{236,238}{U} and \element{238,240}{Pu} are investigated. The experimental kinematic moments of inertia (MoI) and the angular momentum alignments of all studied bands are reproduced well in the PNC-CSM calculations. The striking difference of rotational behaviors between U and Pu isotopes can be linked to the strength of octupole correlations. The upbendings of the alternating-parity bands in\element{236,238}{U} are due to the alignments of pairs of nucleons occupying $νg_{9/2}$, $πf_{7/2}$ orbitals and $νj_{15/2}$, $πi_{13/2}$ high-$j$ intruder orbitals. Particularly, the interference terms of nucleon occupying the octupole-correlation pairs of $ν^2 j_{15/2} g_{9/2}$ and of $π^2 i_{13/2} f_{7/2}$ give a very important contribution to the suddenly gained alignments.

## Key claim

The particle-number conserving (PNC) method in the cranked shell model (CSM) is extended to reflection-asymmetric nuclear systems by applying the $S_x$ symmetry, and the alternating-parity bands in \element{236,238}{U} and \element{238,240}{Pu} are reproduced well; however, the octupole deformation parameters $\varepsilon_3$ are chosen by fitting the experimental yrast bands rather than predicted from first principles.

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with an octupole-deformed Nilsson potential is diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space using a good-simplex basis constructed from spherical harmonic oscillator states. Pairing correlations include both monopole and quadrupole pairing, treated via the particle-number conserving method where the particle number is conserved and Pauli blocking is handled spontaneously. The kinematic moments of inertia and angular momentum alignments are calculated from the resulting eigenstates, with parity splitting applied to separate positive- and negative-parity bands.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters used: $\varepsilon_3 = 0.110$ for \element{236}{U}, $0.130$ for \element{238}{U}, $0.025$ for \element{238}{Pu}, and $0.010$ for \element{240}{Pu}
- Effective pairing strengths: $G_{0p}=0.25$ MeV, $G_{2p}=0.03$ MeV for protons; $G_{0n}=0.25$ MeV, $G_{2n}=0.015$ MeV for neutrons
- Upbendings of alternating-parity bands occur at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.20$ MeV in \element{236}{U} and at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.25$ MeV in \element{238}{U}
- The CMPC space dimensions are about 1000 for both protons and neutrons, constructed in proton $N=5,6$ and neutron $N=6,7$ major shells
- Harris reference parameters used for alignment subtraction: $J_0=65\hbar^2$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $J_1=369\hbar^4$ MeV$^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to reflection-symmetric systems from $A\approx40$ (Xiang et al., 2018) to $A\approx250$ (Zhang et al., 2012; Li et al., 2016; He et al., 2020), to include octupole deformation for the first time. It addresses the experimentally observed striking difference in rotational behavior between U and Pu isotopes (Ward et al., 1996; Wiedenhoever et al., 1999; Wang et al., 2009; Zhu et al., 2010; Spieker et al., 2013), explaining that upbendings in U isotopes arise from octupole-correlation pairs ($\nu^2 j_{15/2} g_{9/2}$ and $\pi^2 i_{13/2} f_{7/2}$) while Pu isotopes are dominated by high-$j$ intruder orbitals. The approach enables microscopic investigation of alternating-parity bands in actinide nuclei with conserved particle number.
