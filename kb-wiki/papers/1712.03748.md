# The QCD Phase Diagram from Statistical Model Analysis

> Ideally, the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) freeze-out curve should reveal the QCD parton-hadron phase transformation line in the ($T$,$μ_B$) plane. We discuss the effects of various final state interaction phenomena, like baryon-antibaryon annihilation, core-corona effects or QCD critical point formation, which shift or deform the SHM freezeout curve. In particular, we present a method to remove the annihilation effects by quantifying them with the microscopic hadron transport model UrQMD. We further discuss the new aspects of hadronization that could be associated with the relatively broad cross-over phase transformation as predicted by lattice-QCD theory at low $μ_B$. That opens up the possibility that various observables of hadronization, e.g. hadron formation or susceptibilities of higher order (related to grand canonical fluctuations of conserved hadronic charges) may freeze out at different characteristic temperatures. This puts into question the concept of a universal \textit{(pseudo-)critical} temperature, as does the very nature of a cross-over phase transformation.

## Key claim

The Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) freeze-out curve, after correcting for final state baryon-antibaryon annihilation effects using the UrQMD transport model, reveals a hadronization line that is nearly horizontal up to a baryochemical potential of about 370 MeV, with a temperature of 164 ± 5 MeV, contradicting the assumption of a universal (pseudo-)critical temperature due to the broad crossover nature of the QCD phase transformation at low baryochemical potential.

## Method

The authors employed the hybrid UrQMD model with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic stage terminated by Cooper-Frye hadronization on either an iso-proper time or isothermal hypersurface, then compared hadronic multiplicities directly after hadronization with those after the full UrQMD afterburner cascade to extract species-dependent modification factors. These modification factors were then applied to experimental hadron multiplicity data from ALICE at LHC (2.76 TeV), NA49 at SPS (17.3, 8.7, and 7.6 GeV), and AGS experiments (4.85 GeV) before fitting with the grand canonical Statistical Hadronization Model to reconstruct the hadronization points in the (T, μ_B) plane.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature from UrQMD-modified SHM analysis: T = 164 ± 5 MeV, compared to T = 150 MeV from standard SHM analysis at top SPS energy (√s = 17.3 GeV)
- At top SPS energy (√s = 17.3 GeV), antiprotons suffer a net loss of about 50% due to final state annihilation, while Anti-Lambda and Anti-Xi multiplicities are reduced by 25 to 40%
- The SHM fit chi-square/dof at top SPS energy improved dramatically from about 2.7 (standard analysis) to about 1.0 (UrQMD-corrected analysis)
- The hadronization line remains nearly horizontal up to a baryochemical potential of about 370 MeV
- At LHC energy (2.76 TeV), the near-perfect particle-antiparticle symmetry means antibaryons are equally affected by annihilation as baryons

## Context

This work extends the SHM freeze-out curve methodology of Andronic et al. (2006) and Cleymans et al. (2006) by incorporating final state interaction corrections quantified through the UrQMD hybrid model, following the approach of Bass and Dumitru (2000) for modification factors. The results confirm lattice QCD predictions by Karsch and Laermann (2004) and Fodor and Katz (2010) that the phase boundary is almost horizontal up to high baryochemical potential, while also revealing a tension with recent analyses of higher order fluctuations by Ding (2014) that favor a lower transition temperature of about 150-155 MeV. The work enables future investigation of the QCD critical point and the onset of deconfinement at lower collision energies, and resolves the apparent non-thermal high pion-to-proton ratio puzzle in ALICE data at LHC.
