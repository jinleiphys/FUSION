# Precise dispersive determination of the f0(600) and f0(980) resonances

> We review our recent dispersive and model independent determination of the lightest two scalars, in terms of poles and residues - or mass, width and coupling- by means of once and twice subtracted Roy equations, using as input constrained fits to data, including the most recent ones from Kl4 decays. We find the f0(600) pole at (457+14-13)-i(279+11-7) MeV and that of the f0(980) at (996+-7)-i(25+10-6) MeV, whereas their respective couplings to two pions are 3.59+0.11-0.13 GeV and 2.3+-0.2 GeV.

## Key claim

Using once- and twice-subtracted Roy equations (GKPY and standard Roy equations) with constrained fits to data (including recent Kl4 decay data) but deliberately excluding Chiral Perturbation Theory input, the authors determine the pole positions of the f0(600) and f0(980) resonances with improved precision, settling the longstanding "dip" versus "no-dip" controversy for the S0 wave inelasticity above the K Kbar threshold.

## Method

The authors first construct unconstrained fits to data (UFD) for each ππ scattering partial wave, then check these against forward dispersion relations, Roy equations, and the newly derived once-subtracted GKPY equations. They then impose these dispersion relations as additional constraints to produce constrained fits to data (CFD) that satisfy analyticity, unitarity, and crossing. Using these CFD parametrizations, they analytically continue the partial waves to the complex plane and locate poles on the second Riemann sheet of the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- f0(600) pole from GKPY equations: (457 +14 -13) - i(279 +11 -7) MeV
- f0(980) pole from GKPY equations: (996 ± 7) - i(25 +10 -6) MeV
- f0(600) coupling to two pions: 3.59 +0.11 -0.13 GeV
- f0(980) coupling to two pions: 2.3 ± 0.2 GeV
- f0(980) width from pole position: 50 +20 -12 MeV
- f0(600) pole from standard Roy equations: (445 ± 25) - i(278 +22 -18) MeV
- f0(980) pole from standard Roy equations: (1003 +5 -27) - i(21 +10 -8) MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of ππ scattering by Garcia-Martin et al. (2011) using the newly derived GKPY equations, which provide smaller errors than standard Roy equations in the 0.45 to 1.1 GeV region. The results are consistent with the ChPT-based prediction of Caprini et al. (2005) for the sigma pole at (441 +16 -8) - i(272 +9 -14.5) MeV, but differ by about two standard deviations from the authors' earlier conformal expansion fit. The analysis enables model-independent resonance parameters for the two lightest scalar mesons and resolves the dip/no-dip ambiguity in the S0 wave inelasticity above the K Kbar threshold.
