# Space-time analysis: HBT at SPS and RHIC

> In this review talk, I first summarize the conclusions which can be drawn from a particle interferometric analysis of CERN SPS data and then I address related questions which are relevant for the upcoming experiments at RHIC.

# Space-time analysis: HBT at SPS and RHIC

## Key claim

The CERN SPS data on HBT radius parameters can be interpreted with a six-parameter model emission function that favors a box-shaped transverse density profile over a Gaussian one, but the model-dependence of the extracted parameters increases with transverse momentum, which is an important caveat for similar studies at RHIC.

## Method

The author reviews fits of a parametrized emission function $S(x,K)$ to NA49 one-particle and two-particle spectra for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/c per nucleon. The emission function is characterized by Gaussian widths in transverse, longitudinal, and temporal directions, a flow field with Bjorken scaling plus transverse flow, and a temperature parameter; the transverse density profile is tested in both Gaussian and box-shaped forms. The extracted model parameters are then used to compute the spatially averaged phase space density and to extrapolate HBT radius systematics to RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at 158 GeV/c, the box-shaped model fit yields transverse radius $R_B = 12.1 \pm 0.2$ fm (set b1), $11.5 \pm 0.2$ fm (b2), $10.7 \pm 0.2$ fm (b3); the Gaussian model gives $R_G = 6.5 \pm 0.1$ fm (g1), $5.9 \pm 0.2$ fm (g2), $5.6 \pm 0.1$ fm (g3).
- Emission time $\tau_0$ from box-shaped fits: $6.3 \pm 1.1$ fm/c (b1), $5.5 \pm 1.1$ fm/c (b2), $4.4 \pm 3.5$ fm/c (b3); from Gaussian fits: $7.8 \pm 0.8$ fm/c (g1), $6.6 \pm 0.9$ fm/c (g2), $5.5 \pm 0.9$ fm/c (g3).
- Temporal width $\Delta\tau$ from box-shaped fits: $3.6 \pm 0.6$ fm/c (b1), $3.2 \pm 0.7$ fm/c (b2), $2.6 \pm 2.0$ fm/c (b3); from Gaussian fits: $2.3 \pm 0.7$ fm/c (g1), $2.3 \pm 0.7$ fm/c (g2), $1.8 \pm 0.8$ fm/c (g3).
- Average transverse velocity $\bar{v}_\perp$: 0.5 (b1), 0.43 (b2), 0.33 (b3); 0.46 (g1), 0.39 (g2), 0.29 (g3).
- Extrapolation to RHIC: side radius $R_s \approx 9$ fm for $dN^-/dY = 600$ and $R_s \approx 11$ fm for $dN^-/dY = 1200$, based on $R_s \propto (dN/dY)^{1/3}$.
- Hydrodynamical extrapolation to RHIC: $\tau_0|_{\rm RHIC} \approx 2 \tau_0|_{\rm SPS}$, $R|_{\rm RHIC} \approx 1.3 R|_{\rm SPS}$, $\eta_f|_{\rm RHIC} \approx 1.3 \eta_f|_{\rm SPS}$, giving $R_s|^{\rm RHIC} \leq 1.2 R_s|^{\rm SPS} < 8$ fm and $R_\parallel|^{\rm RHIC} \approx 2 R_\parallel|^{\rm SPS}$.

## Context

This review talk summarizes the conclusions from particle interferometric analyses of CERN SPS data, particularly the NA49 collaboration results, and extends the discussion to the upcoming RHIC experiments. The work builds on prior analytical model studies by Wiedemann and Heinz (1999) and Tom\'a\v{s}ik (1999), and on the systematics of phase space density from Ferenc et al. (1999). The key open question is whether event generators can account for the observed strong transverse expansion without novel dynamical assumptions, and whether the universal scaling of freeze-out volume with multiplicity observed at SPS will hold at the higher multiplicities accessible at RHIC.
