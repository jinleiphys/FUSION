# The longitudinal response function of the deuteron in chiral effective field theory

> We use chiral effective field theory (EFT) to make predictions for the longitudinal electromagnetic response function of the deuteron, f_L, which is measured in d(e,e'N) reactions. In this case the impulse approximation gives the full chiral EFT result up to corrections that are of O(P^4) relative to leading. By varying the cutoff in the chiral EFT calculations between 0.6 and 1 GeV we conclude that the calculation is accurate to better than 10 % for values of q^2 within 4 fm^{-2} of the quasi-free peak, up to final-state energies E_{np}=60 MeV. In these regions chiral EFT is in reasonable agreement with predictions for f_L obtained using the Bonn potential. We also find good agreement with existing experimental data on f_L, albeit in a more restricted kinematic domain.

## Key claim

The longitudinal electromagnetic response function of the deuteron, \(f_L\), can be computed using chiral effective field theory with the impulse approximation giving the full result up to corrections of \(\mathcal{O}(P^4)\) relative to leading order, and the calculation is accurate to better than 10% for momentum transfers within 4 fm\(^{-2}\) of the quasi-free peak and final-state energies up to 60 MeV, provided the cutoff is varied between 0.6 and 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal response function \(f_L\) for deuteron electrodisintegration \(d(e,e'N)\) using chiral EFT potentials and wave functions up to \(\mathcal{O}(P^3)\) relative to leading order, with the NN charge operator given by single-nucleon contributions only (no two-body corrections up to \(\mathcal{O}(eP^4)\)). The matrix element was decomposed into a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) term and a final-state interaction (FSI) term, evaluated via partial-wave expansion and numerical integration over the intermediate momentum \(\mathbf{p}\). The NN t-matrix was obtained from chiral EFT potentials with subtractive renormalization, using cutoffs \(\Lambda\) between 0.6 and 1 GeV, and the phase shifts were fitted to the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis for laboratory energies \(T_{lab} \leq 100\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is accurate to better than 10% for values of \(\mathbf{q}^2\) within 4 fm\(^{-2}\) of the quasi-free peak, up to final-state energies \(E_{np} = 60\) MeV.
- The cutoff in the chiral EFT calculation was varied between 0.6 and 1 GeV to assess the accuracy.
- The NN t-matrix phase shifts were fitted to agree with the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis for \(T_{lab} \leq 100\) MeV, corresponding to \(E_{np} \leq 50\) MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy used is \(B = 2.24\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral EFT to electromagnetic reactions in few-nucleon systems, following earlier studies of charge form factors for \(A=2\) and \(A=3\) systems by Phillips (2003, 2007) and Piarulli et al. (2012). It uses the subtractive renormalization method developed by Yang and Phillips (2008, 2009) to handle NN potentials, and compares predictions to those obtained using the Bonn potential by Arenhövel et al., which have been successful in describing experimental data on \(f_L\) from van der Schaar et al. (1991), Ducret et al. (1994), and Jodice et al. (1996). The results enable further tests of chiral EFT in kinematic regions where cutoff dependence is controlled, and provide a foundation for extending such calculations to higher orders or to other response functions.
