# Electromagnetic and strong isospin breaking in light meson masses

> We study electromagnetic as well as strong isospin breaking effects in the isospin mass splittings of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons. To this end we employ a coupled system of quark Dyson-Schwinger and meson Bethe-Salpeter equations whose interaction kernels contain gluon, pion and photon exchange interactions. In bound states, QCD-induced isospin breaking is manifest on different levels. On the one hand, a different explicit up- and down-quark mass directly affects the propagators of the constituent quarks. On the other hand, it leads to different interaction kernels within the isospin multiplets. In addition, electromagnetic isospin breaking is induced via a photon exchange diagram. Using the kaon iso-doublet and the charged pion masses as input to determine the up, down and strange quark masses we find for the pion, kaon and rho meson mass splittings different patterns each. In particular, our results provide evidence that the effects from two sources of mass splittings, the different quark masses and the different quark charges, do not add up linearly.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that the effects from two sources of mass splittings in light mesons (different quark masses and different quark charges) do not add up linearly, with non-negligible mixed higher-order terms in the expansion, contrary to the traditional expectation that such effects are small.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled system of quark Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and meson Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) whose interaction kernels contain gluon, pion, and photon exchange interactions. The gluon interaction uses the rainbow-ladder truncation with a Maris-Tandy model for the effective coupling, while meson exchange diagrams introduce flavour mixing and photon exchange diagrams describe electromagnetic interactions. The up, down, and strange quark masses are determined by fitting to the experimental masses of the charged pion and the neutral and charged kaon.

## Key numbers

- Up quark mass: \(m_u = 6.3\) MeV, down quark mass: \(m_d = 8.1\) MeV, strange quark mass: \(m_s = 82.8\) MeV (in a MOM-scheme at renormalisation scale \(\mu = 19\) GeV)
- Strong isospin breaking contribution to kaon mass splitting: \((m_{K^0} - m_{K^\pm})^{SU(2)} = 5.4\) MeV
- Electromagnetic contribution to kaon mass splitting: \((m_{K^0} - m_{K^\pm})^{\gamma} = -2.0\) MeV
- Mixed higher-order contribution to kaon mass splitting: \((m_{K^0} - m_{K^\pm})^{SU(2),\gamma} = 0.6\) MeV
- Electromagnetic contribution to pion mass splitting: \((m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0})^{\gamma} = 3.6\) MeV
- Strong isospin breaking contribution to pion mass splitting: \((m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0})^{SU(2)} = 1.7\) MeV
- Mixed higher-order contribution to pion mass splitting: \((m_{\pi^\pm} - m_{\pi^0})^{SU(2),\gamma} = 1.0\) MeV
- Total calculated pion mass difference: 6.3 MeV (compared to experimental 4.6 MeV)
- Dynamically generated mass enhancement: \(M_u(0) - M_d(0) \approx 5 (m_u - m_d)\), giving approximately 9 MeV from a current mass difference of 1.8 MeV
- Electromagnetically induced kaon mass splitting ratio: \((m^2_{K^\pm} - m^2_{K^0})_{\mathrm em} \approx 2.0 (m^2_{\pi^\pm} - m^2_{\pi^0})_{\mathrm em}\)

## Context

This work extends previous continuum studies of isospin breaking using functional methods, notably the pioneering work of Jain and Munczek (1993) and improved ladder approximations by Harada et al. (2004, 2005), by including both meson exchange and photon exchange contributions. The results agree qualitatively with chiral perturbation theory (Bijnens et al., 1996; Amoros et al., 2001) and lattice QCD calculations (FLAG review by Aoki et al., 2021; Horsley et al., 2015; MILC Collaboration, 2018; Fodor et al., 2016; Giusti et al., 2017; Yong et al., 2021), and the findings do not support Dashen's theorem (Dashen, 1969) for the electromagnetically induced kaon mass splitting. The work enables more precise determinations of hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon and CKM matrix elements.
