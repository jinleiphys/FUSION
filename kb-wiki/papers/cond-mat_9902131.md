# Dilute Quantum Droplets

> In the limit when the two-body scattering length $a$ is negative and much larger than the effective two-body interaction radius the contribution to the ground state energy due to the three-body correlations is given by the Efimov effect. For particular values of the diluteness parameter $ρ|a|^3$ the three-body contribution can become the dominant term of the energy density functional. Under these conditions both Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac systems could become self-bound and either boson droplets or fermion ``designer nuclei'' of various sizes and densities could be manufactured.

## Key claim

In the limit where the two-body scattering length \(a\) is negative and much larger than the effective two-body interaction radius, the three-body correlation contribution to the ground state energy, governed by the Efimov effect, can become the dominant term of the energy density functional for particular values of the diluteness parameter \(\rho|a|^3\), potentially stabilizing self-bound quantum droplets.

## Method

The paper derives the three-body contribution to the ground state energy density of a dilute quantum gas using the zero-energy three-body scattering amplitude \(g_3\), which is expressed via a universal formula involving the Efimov effect parameters \(s_0\), \(d_1\), \(d_2\), and \(a_0\). For both Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac systems, the total energy density functional is constructed by adding the three-body term \(\mathcal{E}_3(\rho) = \frac{1}{6}g_3\rho^3\) to the standard two-body term, and the equilibrium density, surface tension, and droplet properties are derived in a mean-field approximation.

## Key numbers

- The universal constant \(s_0 \approx 1.0064\) governs the Efimov effect, with three-body bound state sizes changing by a factor \(\exp(\pi/s_0) \approx 22.68\) and energies changing by \(\exp(-2\pi/s_0)\) between successive states.
- The number of three-body bound states is \(N \approx (s_0/\pi) \ln(|a|/r_0)\), where \(r_0\) is the two-body interaction radius.
- The equilibrium density of a boson droplet (boselet) is \(\rho_0 = -3g_2/(2g_3)\), with chemical potential \(\mu_0 = -3g_2^2/(8g_3)\) and surface tension \(\sigma = g_3\rho_0^3/(12\kappa_0)\), where \(\kappa_0 = \sqrt{2m|\mu_0|/\hbar^2}\).
- The surface vibration frequency for a boselet with angular momentum \(l\) is \(\omega_l^2 = \sigma l(l-1)(l+2)/(m\rho_0 R^3) + \mathcal{O}(N^{-4/3})\), where \(R\) is the droplet radius.

## Context

This work extends the theory of dilute quantum gases beyond the standard two-body picture by incorporating the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) for three-body correlations, which was previously studied for three-body bound states and zero-energy scattering amplitudes (Braaten et al. 1999). The paper enables the theoretical prediction of self-bound boson droplets ("boselets") and fermion droplets ("fermilets" or "designer nuclei") with controllable sizes and densities, analogous to nuclear systems described by Skyrme energy density functionals (Skyrme 1959), and suggests a possible trimer phase analogous to the Cooper pair-BEC crossover in fermion systems.
