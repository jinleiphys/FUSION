# Mediating states in 180Ta

> Possible theoretical electromagnetic paths between the ground state and the isomeric state at 75.3 keV in 180Ta are discussed in the framework of the two-quasiparticle-plus-phonon model and the standard axially-symmetric rotor model including Coriolis mixing. Experimental transition rates from the isomeric state to the ground state via observed mediating states are compared to the theoretical ones.

# Mediating states in 180Ta

## Key claim

The two-quasiparticle-plus-phonon model combined with the standard axially-symmetric rotor model including Coriolis mixing can reproduce the main trend of experimental transition rates from the isomeric state to the ground state via mediating states, but the theoretical calculations still fail to account for 1 or 2 orders of magnitude in the summed transition rates.

## Method

The authors used the two-quasiparticle-plus-phonon model (TQPM) for intrinsic degrees of freedom and the standard axially-symmetric rotor model including Coriolis mixing to describe the deformed nucleus 180Ta. The model Hamiltonian included a deformed Nilsson potential, monopole pairing, and long-range multipole-multipole interaction, treated in the BCS approximation with RPA for one-phonon core excitations. Intrinsic wave functions were composed of one-neutron-quasiparticle plus one-proton-quasiparticle components with additional phonon admixtures, and theoretical energies were replaced by known experimental energies with internal conversion taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Isomeric state in 180Ta lies at 75.3 keV above the ground state, with a solar abundance of 2.48 normalized to 10^12 for Si.
- The lowest mediating state has an excitation energy of 1.085 MeV (above ground state), with experimental total integrated depopulation cross section I_D = (5.7 ± 1.2) eV fm^2.
- Higher mediating states below 2 MeV were found at: 1.30 MeV (I_D = 27 eV fm^2), 1.51 MeV (I_D = 24 eV fm^2), 1.63 MeV (I_D = 70 eV fm^2), and 1.93 MeV (I_D = 111 eV fm^2).
- In the theoretical calculations, the lowest mediating state lies at 670 keV, but with a very small transition rate from the isomer to the ground state.
- Effective charges used: e_p,eff(E1) = 1.2 e, e_n,eff(E1) = 0.8 e; e_p,eff(E2) = e, e_n,eff(E2) = 0.2 e; e_p,eff(E3) = e, e_n,eff(E3) = 0.2 e; g_s,red = 0.7 and g_R = 0.26.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Alexa (2002) and addresses the challenge of explaining the survival of the 180Ta isomer in nucleosynthesis, as discussed in the context of s-process thermal photon excitation. The experimental data from Belic et al. (1999, 2000) using the Stuttgart Dynamitron facility provided the mediating state energies and cross sections that the authors compare with their TQPM plus rotor model calculations. Previous TQPM calculations by Soloviev (1992) failed to reproduce mediating state energies and cross sections, finding mediating states only above 2.4 MeV, whereas the present improved model with 260 intrinsic two-quasiparticle states and one-phonon components finds low-lying mediating states for the first time, enabling future comparison with experimental data and refinement of nucleosynthesis models.
