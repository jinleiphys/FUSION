# Proton Cumulants and Correlation Functions in Au + Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$=7.7-200 GeV from UrQMD Model

> We studied the acceptance dependence of proton cumulants (up to fourth order) and correlation functions in 0--5\% most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$=7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV from UrQMD model. We found that high order proton cumulants show suppressions at large acceptance. By decomposing the proton cumulants into linear combination of multi-proton correlation functions, we observed the two-proton correlation functions always show negative values due to the effects of baryon number conservations. The three and four-proton correlation functions are close to zero and show negligible acceptance dependence. We further observed that the proton cumulants and correlation functions follow similar trends and show a scaling behavior when plotting the results versus mean number of protons. The comparisons between experimental data and the UrQMD calculations show that the non-monotonic energy dependence of proton correlation functions measured by STAR experiment cannot be described by the UrQMD model. The UrQMD calculations can provide us baselines for the experimental studies of the proton cumulants and correlation functions. Finally, we propose to measure the rapidity dependence of the reduced proton correlation functions to search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions.

# Proton Cumulants and Correlation Functions in Au + Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$=7.7-200 GeV from UrQMD Model

## Key claim

The UrQMD model cannot reproduce the non-monotonic energy dependence of the second and fourth order proton cumulants observed by the STAR experiment, particularly the minimum around 20 GeV and enhancement at lower energies; however, the authors note that deviations between UrQMD and experiment might be due to non-critical correlations not captured by the model, rather than definitively indicating critical point physics.

## Method

The authors performed UrQMD (Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) model simulations of 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, using 35 to 135 million minimum bias events per energy. They calculated proton cumulants up to fourth order ($C_1$ through $C_4$) and decomposed them into multi-proton correlation functions ($c_2$, $c_3$, $c_4$) via factorial cumulant relations, studying the dependence on rapidity acceptance ($\Delta y$) and transverse momentum cuts. Centrality was defined using charged pion and kaon multiplicity within $|\eta|<1.0$ to avoid auto-correlations, and statistical errors were estimated using the Delta theorem method.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Statistics per energy: 35 million (7.7 GeV), 113 million (11.5 and 19.6 GeV), 83 million (27 GeV), 135 million (39 and 62.4 GeV), and 56 million (200 GeV) minimum bias events
- Beam rapidities range from $y_\mathrm{beam}=2.09$ at 7.7 GeV to $y_\mathrm{beam}=5.36$ at 200 GeV
- Protons selected within $|y| < 0.5$ and $0.4 < p_\mathrm{T} < 2.0$ GeV/c for comparison with STAR data
- Two-proton correlation functions ($c_2$) are always negative and decrease monotonically with acceptance at all energies
- Three and four-proton correlation functions ($c_3$, $c_4$) are close to zero with negligible acceptance dependence
- Proton cumulant ratios deviate from Poisson expectation (unity) with deviations becoming larger at low energies

## Context

This work extends baseline calculations from the UrQMD transport model for proton cumulants and correlation functions, providing a reference for the STAR Beam Energy Scan (BES-I) program at RHIC. The study builds on prior theoretical work on conserved charge fluctuations near the QCD critical point by Kitazawa (2012), Asakawa (2015), and Bzdak (2016), and on baryon number conservation effects studied by Bzdak (2013) and Sakaida (2014). The results show that the non-monotonic energy dependence observed in STAR data for the fourth order proton cumulants is dominated by four-proton correlation functions, which the authors propose to study via rapidity dependence of reduced correlation functions to search for the QCD critical point.
