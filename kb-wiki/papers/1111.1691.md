# Shining a Gluon Beam Through Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We compute the energy density radiated by a quark undergoing circular motion in strongly coupled $\mathcal N = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma. If it were in vacuum, this quark would radiate a beam of strongly coupled radiation whose angular distribution has been characterized and is very similar to that of synchrotron radiation produced by an electron in circular motion in electrodynamics. Here, we watch this beam of gluons getting quenched by the strongly coupled plasma. We find that a beam of gluons of momenta $\sim q \gg πT$ is attenuated rapidly, over a distance $\sim q^{1/3} (πT)^{-4/3}$ in a plasma with temperature $T$. As the beam propagates through the plasma at the speed of light, it sheds trailing sound waves with momenta $\lesssim πT$. Presumably these sound waves would thermalize in the plasma if they were not hit soon after their production by the next pulse of gluons from the lighthouse-like rotating quark. At larger and larger $q$, the trailing sound wave becomes less and less prominent. The outward going beam of gluon radiation itself shows no tendency to spread in angle or to shift toward larger wavelengths, even as it is completely attenuated. In this regard, the behavior of the beam of gluons that we analyze is reminiscent of the behavior of jets produced in heavy ion collisions at the LHC that lose a significant fraction of their energy without appreciable change in their angular distribution or their momentum distribution as they plow through the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in these collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a beam of gluons produced by a quark in circular motion in strongly coupled plasma is attenuated rapidly without any broadening of its angular distribution or shift toward longer wavelengths, even as it loses energy completely; this behavior is qualitatively reminiscent of jet quenching observed at the LHC, where attenuated jets show no visible change in angular or momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors use gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) to compute the energy density radiated by a test quark undergoing circular motion in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma at nonzero temperature. They solve the Nambu-Goto action for the string profile describing the rotating quark, then linearize Einstein's equations around the AdS-Schwarzschild black hole geometry to compute the metric perturbation due to the string, from which they extract the boundary theory energy density via the holographic stress tensor relation. The calculation is performed numerically using pseudospectral methods with Chebyshev polynomials, solving the helicity-0 gauge invariant field equation for each Fourier mode and then transforming back to real space.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of synchrotron-like radiation from a quark in circular motion in vacuum (Athanasiou 2010) to the case of nonzero temperature, where the beam of gluons interacts with strongly coupled plasma. It builds upon prior calculations of the energy loss rate of a rotating quark in plasma (Fadafan 2008) and the stopping distance of light quarks (Gubser 2008, Chesler 2008). The results provide a strongly coupled benchmark for understanding jet quenching phenomena seen at RHIC and the LHC, particularly the observation that jets at the LHC lose significant energy without visible broadening or softening of their fragmentation functions (Chatrchyan 2011). The paper enables future work on how the wavelength of the gluon beam correlates with the production of trailing sound waves, potentially explaining differences between jet quenching at RHIC and LHC energies.
