# Collisional vs. Radiative Energy Loss of Heavy Quark in a Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter

> We study the heavy quark evolution in a quark-gluon plasma medium within the framework of Langevin equation coupled to a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model. We modify the current Langevin algorithm such that apart from quasi-elastic scattering, medium-induced radiative energy loss is incorporated as well by treating gluon radiation as an extra force term. We find a significant effect of gluon radiation on heavy quark energy loss at LHC energies. Our calculation provides a good description of the D meson suppression measured by ALICE experiment, and makes a prediction for B meson suppression and flow.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at LHC energies, medium-induced gluon radiation from heavy quarks becomes significant and can no longer be neglected due to the "dead-cone effect," and that a combination of collisional and radiative energy loss is required to describe D meson suppression data, whereas either mechanism alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors modified the Langevin equation for heavy quark Brownian motion in a quark-gluon plasma by adding a force term representing gluon radiation, with the radiation probability and momentum distribution sampled from the Higher-Twist calculation. The heavy quark evolution was simulated in a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic medium with MC-Glauber initialization, and the heavy quarks were fragmented to heavy mesons via Pythia 6.4 after traversing the medium.

## Key numbers

- The crossing point between collisional-dominated and radiative-dominated energy loss for charm quarks in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV is around 6 GeV.
- In a static medium at fixed temperature 300 MeV, charm quarks initialized with 15 GeV energy show collisional energy loss dominating before 2 fm/c, after which gluon radiation starts to dominate.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient was set at D = 6/(2 pi T), corresponding to a gluon transport coefficient hat(q) around 1.3 GeV^2/fm at 300 MeV.
- The lower limit of gluon energy was set to omega_0 = pi T, the balancing point between gluon emission and absorption.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin approach of He (2011), which described heavy flavor suppression and elliptic flow at RHIC using only collisional energy loss, to LHC energies where radiative energy loss becomes important. The authors note that the "dead-cone effect" of Dokshitzer (2001) had previously justified neglecting gluon radiation for heavy quarks, but this assumption breaks down at LHC energies. The calculation provides a good description of D meson suppression measured by the ALICE experiment and makes predictions for B meson suppression and flow, which can be tested by future LHC measurements.
