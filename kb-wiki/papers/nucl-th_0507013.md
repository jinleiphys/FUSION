# Complementary reaction analyses and the isospin mixing of the 4- states in 16O

> Data from the inelastic scattering of electrons, and of intermediate energy protons and pions leading to ``stretched'' configuration 4- states near 19 MeV excitation in 16O as well as from charge exchange (p,n) scattering to an isobaric analogue (4-) state in 16F have been analyzed to ascertain the degree of isospin mixing contained within those states and of the amount of d_{5/2}-p_{3/2}^{-1} particle-hole excitation strength they exhaust. The electron and proton scattering data have been analyzed using microscopic models of the structure and reactions, with details constrained by analyses of elastic scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper uses complementary reaction analyses (electron, proton, and pion scattering) to determine that the three 4- states near 19 MeV excitation in 16O exhibit isospin mixing, with the 18.977 MeV state being nearly pure isovector (sin(epsilon2)=0) while the 17.775 and 19.808 MeV states show small but nonzero isospin admixtures (sin(epsilon1)=0.2 and sin(epsilon3)=-0.17 respectively), though the authors caution that extracted spectroscopic factors from particle transfer reactions are "very model dependent" and such percentages should be considered "indicative at best."

## Method

The authors analyzed inelastic scattering data from electrons, 164 MeV pions, and intermediate-energy protons exciting 4- states in 16O, plus charge-exchange (p,n) data to the isobaric analogue state in 16F, using a three-component basis space model (one isovector and two isoscalar particle-hole states) with configuration and isospin mixing parameters. Electron scattering form factors were calculated with Woods-Saxon bound state wave functions using occupancies from a complete (0+2)h-bar-omega shell model calculation, while proton scattering was analyzed using a g-folding model with effective NN interactions from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g-matrices, and pion scattering used a conventional DWIA with a Kisslinger-type contact interaction.

## Key numbers

- The 17.775, 18.977, and 19.808 MeV states in 16O are the three strongly excited 4- states, with dominant isospin values of 0, 1, and 0 respectively
- The isovector (18.977 MeV) state contains 97% of the isovector particle-hole strength from particle transfer reactions; the summed isoscalar (17.775 and 19.808 MeV) states account for 92% of that strength
- The large basis shell model gave occupancies of 3.87 for the 0p3/2 shell and 0.14 for the 0d5/2 shell (as (2j+1)*sigma_j)
- The Woods-Saxon densities gave root mean square radii of 2.61 fm (proton), 2.65 fm (neutron), and 2.63 fm (total mass), compared to the experimental value of 2.712 +/- 0.015 fm
- The isospin mixing parameters determined were sin(epsilon1)=0.2 for the 17.775 MeV state, sin(epsilon2)=0 for the 18.977 MeV state, and sin(epsilon3)=-0.17 for the 19.808 MeV state
- Holtkamp et al. (1980) quoted a ratio of pi+ to pi- summed yields for the 18.977 MeV state as 0.964 +/- 0.08

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of 4- states in 16O by Hyde-Wright et al. (1987) and Clark (1988) for electron scattering, and by Holtkamp et al. (1980) and Bacher et al. (1981) for pion scattering, but improves upon earlier phenomenological proton scattering analyses by using a fully microscopic g-folding model with large basis shell model wave functions. The results enable future studies to use the determined isospin mixing parameters and particle-hole strength distributions as constraints for more sophisticated shell model calculations incorporating 3p-3h excitations, as predicted by Millener (1975), and for refining the specification of pi-N g-matrices in nuclear matter.
