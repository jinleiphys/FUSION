# Influence of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity on the azimuthal asymmetries of transverse momentum spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the influence of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$, different shear relaxation times $τ_π$, as well as different initial conditions on the transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons and identified particles. We investigate the azimuthal flow asymmetries as a function of both collision energy and centrality. The elliptic flow coefficient turns out to be dominated by the hadronic viscosity at RHIC energies. Only at higher collision energies the impact of the viscosity in the QGP phase is visible in the flow asymmetries. Nevertheless, the shear viscosity near the QCD transition region has the largest impact on the collective flow of the system. We also find that the centrality dependence of the elliptic flow is sensitive to the temperature dependence of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ at RHIC energies is dominated by the hadronic viscosity, while the impact of viscosity in the QGP phase becomes visible only at higher collision energies; however, the shear viscosity near the QCD transition region has the largest overall impact on collective flow, and the centrality dependence of $v_2$ is sensitive to the temperature dependence of $\eta/s$.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamics using Israel-Stewart theory with a temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$, parametrized in four ways that vary the viscosity in the hadron gas and QGP phases separately. They initialized the system at $\tau_0 = 1$ fm using optical Glauber model energy density profiles (BCfit and GLmix parameterizations), evolved the system with the SHASTA algorithm, and computed final hadron spectra and flow coefficients via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at $T_{\rm dec} = 100$ MeV with the 14-moment ansatz for the viscous correction $\delta f$.

## Key numbers

- Minimum $\eta/s = 0.08$ at $T_{\rm tr} = 180$ MeV, based on the AdS/CFT lower bound
- Freeze-out temperature $T_{\rm dec} = 100$ MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{\rm chem} = 150$ MeV
- Initialization time $\tau_0 = 1.0$ fm
- Relaxation time constant $c_\tau = 5$ (causality requires $c_\tau \geq 2$)
- Maximum initial temperatures: $T_{\rm max} = 313$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV (RHIC), $430$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (LHC), and $504$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV (LHC)
- Viscous entropy production during entire evolution ranges from $3-14\%$ depending on collision energy and $\eta/s$ parameterization; entropy production below $T_{\rm chem}=150$ MeV is less than $1\%$ of initial entropy
- At LHC, the binary collision contribution to initial conditions is $\sim 70\%$ (parameter $d_1 = 0.7$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Niemi et al., 2011) by providing a more detailed analysis of how temperature-dependent $\eta/s$ affects elliptic flow and higher harmonics across collision energies from RHIC to LHC. It contradicts the common assumption of constant $\eta/s$ used in earlier viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Song et al., 2010; Shen et al., 2010) and shows that the temperature region most relevant for flow suppression shifts from the hadronic phase at RHIC to the QGP phase at higher LHC energies. The results enable future extraction of the temperature dependence of $\eta/s$ from experimental data by comparing centrality and energy dependence of flow coefficients across RHIC and LHC.
