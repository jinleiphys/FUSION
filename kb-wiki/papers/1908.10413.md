# Gluonic Probe for the Short Range Correlation in Nucleus

> We investigate the gluonic probe to the nucleon-nucleon short range correlation (SRC) in nucleus through heavy flavor production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS). The relevant EMC effects of $F_2^{c\bar c}$ structure function will provide a universality test of the SRCs which have been extensively studied in the quark-channel. These SRCs can also be studied through the sub-threshold production of heavy flavor in $eA$ collisions at the intermediate energy electron-ion collider, including open Charm and $J/ψ$ ($Υ$) production.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that heavy flavor production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and sub-threshold electron-nucleus collisions can serve as a gluonic probe of nucleon-nucleon short range correlations (SRC), providing a universality test of SRC contributions that have been extensively studied in the quark channel. The authors hedge that their numerical calculations use leading-order perturbative results and the EPPS16 parameterization for illustration only, noting that the gluon EMC effects are not strongly constrained by previous experiments.

## Method

The authors compute the reduced cross section for charm structure functions \(F_2^{c\bar c}\) and \(F_L^{c\bar c}\) at leading order, using the EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution parameterization for gold nuclei to illustrate the nuclear modification factor \(R_A^{c\bar c}\). They then model sub-threshold \(J/\psi\) production in \(\gamma A\) collisions by applying a power-law parameterization of the near-threshold cross section, \((1-\chi_\gamma)^\beta\), and extending it to the two-nucleon SRC case by replacing the single-nucleon threshold variable \(\chi_\gamma\) with a two-nucleon version \(\tilde\chi_\gamma\). The universality prediction is tested by comparing the normalized nuclear modification factors \(\left[R_A^{c\bar c}-1\right]/a_2^A\) across different nuclei, where \(a_2^A\) is the SRC ratio relative to deuterium.

## Key numbers

- The reduced cross section for \(ep\) charm production at \(Q^2\sim 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(x\sim 0.1\) is about \(10^{-3}\), corresponding to a total cross section of \(10^4\ \text{fb/GeV}^2\) at the kinematics of an EIC in China (EicC).
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_A^{c\bar c}\) for gold ranges from 1.1 to 0.8 in the interval \(x_B=0.1-0.4\), based on the EPPS16 parameterization.
- The near-threshold \(J/\psi\) production cross section in \(\gamma p\) collisions is parameterized with \(\sigma_0=11.3\ \text{nb}\) and \(\beta=1.3\).
- The sub-threshold \(J/\psi\) production cross section is estimated for photon energies \(E_\gamma\sim 6-8\ \text{GeV}\) using \(\beta_2 = n_2\beta\) with \(n_2\) in the range 1–3.

## Context

This work extends the established connection between SRC and EMC effects in nuclear structure functions, previously studied by Frankfurt (1993), Weinstein (2010), Hen (2012), and Schmookler (2019), into the gluonic channel via heavy flavor production. The proposed measurements at a future intermediate-energy electron-ion collider (IEEIC) and JLab would provide a universality test of SRC contributions complementary to quark-channel studies, and could help constrain the poorly-known gluon distribution in nuclei. The sub-threshold heavy flavor production also connects to studies of the proton mass decomposition via near-threshold \(J/\psi\) production, as pursued by Brodsky (2000), Hatta (2018), and others.
