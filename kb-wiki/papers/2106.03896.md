# Orbital Angular Momentum Distribution of Gluons at Small-$x$ : Analytic interpolation between Ji and Jaffe-Manohar

> In this work, we first introduced a generalized Wilson line gauge link that reproduces both staple and near straight links along the light cone in different parameter limits. We then studied the gauge-invariant bi-local orbital angular momentum operator with such a general gauge link, in the framework of Chen et. al. decomposition of gauge fields. At the appropriate combination of limits, the operator reproduces both Jaffe-Manohar and Ji's operator structure and offers a continuous analytical interpolation between the two.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a generalized Wilson line gauge link that, through adjustable parameters, can reproduce both the staple-shaped gauge links (leading to Jaffe-Manohar OAM) and the near-straight gauge links (leading to Ji OAM), offering a continuous analytical interpolation between these two definitions of gluon orbital angular momentum at small-$x$.

## Method
The authors first construct a generalized Wilson line gauge link parameterized by two light-cone extent parameters, $\eta_1$ and $\eta_2$, which control the geometry of the link. They then study the gauge-invariant bi-local orbital angular momentum operator within the Chen et al. decomposition of gauge fields into pure and physical parts, deriving the transverse derivative of the generalized gauge link. Finally, they express the gluon OAM operator in terms of these parameters and analyze its behavior in the eikonal and first sub-eikonal limits, showing how different choices of $\eta_1$ and $\eta_2$ reproduce the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji operator structures.

## Key numbers
- The generalized gauge link is defined with two parameters $\eta_1$ and $\eta_2$; the limits $\eta = \pm\infty$ produce past-pointing and future-pointing staple links, while $\eta \rightarrow 0$ produces the near-straight link.
- The eikonal contribution to gluon OAM, ${\cal L}^{jk}_{g,\rm {0}}(x)$, vanishes for longitudinally polarized protons because $\epsilon^{kj}{\cal L}^{jk}_{g,\rm {0}} = 0$.
- Non-vanishing spin effects for longitudinally polarized targets first appear at the first sub-eikonal order, ${\cal L}^{jk}_{g,\rm {1}}(x)$, which is proportional to $xP^+$ and involves the $z^-$-moment of the physical gauge field.

## Context
This work extends the framework of Chen et al. (2008, 2009) for gauge-invariant decomposition of angular momentum, and builds on the distinction between Jaffe-Manohar OAM (involving staple gauge links with initial/final state interactions) and Ji OAM (involving straight gauge links). The interpolation between these two definitions is motivated by lattice QCD work by Engelhardt (2020) on quark OAM, and the results enable connections between quasi-PDFs measured on the lattice and TMDs measured in DIS and Drell-Yan processes, as well as between Weizsäcker-Williams and dipole TMD distributions at small-$x$.
