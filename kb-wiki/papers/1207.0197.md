# Yoctosecond metrology through HBT correlations from a quark-gluon plasma

> Expansion dynamics at the yoctosecond timescale affect the evolution of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy ion collisions. We show how these dynamics are accessible through Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry of direct photons emitted from the interior of the QGP. A detector placed close to the beam axis is particularly sensitive to early polar momentum anisotropies of the QGP. Observing a modification of the HBT signal at the proposed FoCal detector of the LHC ALICE experiment would allow to measure the isotropization time of the plasma and could provide first experimental evidence for photon double pulses at the yoctosecond timescale.

# Yoctosecond metrology through HBT correlations from a quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The authors show that Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry of direct photons from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), measured near the beam axis, can reveal early polar momentum anisotropies and allow measurement of the isotropization time, but they acknowledge that distinguishing such modifications from other sources of oscillations in the HBT correlation function will be experimentally challenging.

## Method
The authors calculate two-photon momentum correlation functions using a source function that incorporates polar momentum anisotropy via an anisotropy parameter ξ, with a time evolution that interpolates between ideal hydrodynamic expansion (δ=0) and free-streaming expansion (δ=2). Photon production rates from quark-Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation in an anisotropic plasma are computed numerically, and the HBT correlation function is evaluated for both collinear and non-collinear detector configurations relevant to the proposed FoCal detector at the LHC ALICE experiment.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T₀ = 845 MeV, plasma freezeout temperature T_c = 160 MeV, nucleus radius R = 7.1 fm, plasma formation time τ₀ = 0.088 fm/c
- For a non-collinear configuration with photon momentum k = 25 GeV/c at pseudorapidity η ≈ 4, the transverse momentum decreases from k_T ≈ 3.5 GeV/c at η ≈ 2.7 to k_T ≈ 0.9 GeV/c at η ≈ 4
- An assumed isotropization time of τ_iso = 2 fm/c leads to peaks in the correlation function separated by about 3° for the chosen parameters
- For a collinear configuration at θ = 25° (η ≈ 1.5) with k = 4 GeV/c (k_T ≈ 1.7 GeV/c), isotropization times τ_iso ≳ 1.5 fm/c produce a plateau-like structure in the HBT function
- Impact parameters b = 10 fm (collinear) and b = 12 fm (non-collinear) are used to avoid signal averaging by transverse plasma size
- An annual yield of at least 10⁶ prompt photons in the energy range k_T = 1 GeV/c to 4 GeV/c hitting the FoCal detector would yield a few hundred photon pairs within the same time frame

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of photon interferometry for central collisions (Bass 2004, Renk 2005) and non-central collisions (Frodermann 2009) that assumed isotropic photon emission even at very early QGP stages. It also builds on earlier work showing that polar momentum anisotropies can induce photon double pulses at the yoctosecond timescale (Ipp 2009). The proposed method would enable measurement of the isotropization time of the QGP and could provide first indirect experimental evidence for photon double pulses at the yoctosecond timescale, using the upcoming FoCal detector at the LHC ALICE experiment.
