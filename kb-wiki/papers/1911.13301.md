# Searching for dark photons in hyperon decays

> That massless dark photons could exist and have flavor-changing magnetic-dipole interactions with down-type light quarks is an attractive possibility which may be realized in various new-physics scenarios. It is potentially testable not only in kaon processes but also via two-body hyperon decays involving missing energy carried away by the massless dark photon. We explore the latter within a simplified model approach and take into account constraints from the kaon sector. We find that the branching fractions of some of these hyperon modes are allowed to be as high as a few times $10^{-4}$. Such numbers are likely to be within the sensitivity reaches of ongoing experiments like BESIII and future ones at super charm-tau factories.

# Searching for dark photons in hyperon decays

## Key claim
Massless dark photons with flavor-changing magnetic-dipole interactions to down-type light quarks can produce two-body hyperon decays with missing energy, and their branching fractions may reach a few times 10^{-4} while remaining consistent with kaon-sector constraints, but one of the two benchmark scenarios considered may already be in tension with indirect bounds from existing hyperon data.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian with dimension-five operators describing the $s \to d\overline\gamma$ transition via dipole-type couplings, parameterized by constants $\mathbb C$ and $\mathbb C_5$. They compute decay rates for six hyperon modes ($\Lambda \to n\overline\gamma$, $\Sigma^+ \to p\overline\gamma$, $\Xi^0 \to \Lambda\overline\gamma$, $\Xi^0 \to \Sigma^0\overline\gamma$, $\Xi^- \to \Sigma^-\overline\gamma$, $\Omega^- \to \Xi^-\overline\gamma$) using baryonic matrix elements from quark-model SU(6) wave functions taken from Gilman (1978). They then impose constraints from kaon mixing ($\Delta m_K$) on two specific simplified-model scenarios from Fabbrichesi et al. (2017, 2019) to derive maximal allowed branching fractions.

## Key numbers
- $\Lambda \to n\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $1.1 \times 10^{-5}$ (scenario I) and $6.7 \times 10^{-4}$ (scenario II)
- $\Sigma^+ \to p\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $6.0 \times 10^{-7}$ (scenario I) and $3.8 \times 10^{-5}$ (scenario II)
- $\Xi^0 \to \Lambda\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $1.9 \times 10^{-6}$ (scenario I) and $1.2 \times 10^{-4}$ (scenario II)
- $\Xi^0 \to \Sigma^0\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $4.3 \times 10^{-6}$ (scenario I) and $2.7 \times 10^{-4}$ (scenario II)
- $\Xi^- \to \Sigma^-\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $5.1 \times 10^{-6}$ (scenario I) and $3.3 \times 10^{-4}$ (scenario II)
- $\Omega^- \to \Xi^-\overline\gamma$: maximal branching fraction $2.0 \times 10^{-5}$ (scenario I) and $1.3 \times 10^{-3}$ (scenario II)
- Coupling constant bound in scenario I: $|\mathbb C| < 2.0 \times 10^{-9}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$
- Coupling constant bound in scenario II: $|\mathbb C| < 1.6 \times 10^{-8}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$
- Indirect upper limits from PDG data: $\Lambda$ decays $< 1.4\%$, $\Sigma^+$ decays $< 8.0 \times 10^{-3}$, $\Xi^0$ decays $< 3.4 \times 10^{-4}$, $\Xi^-$ decays $< 8.3 \times 10^{-4}$, $\Omega^-$ decays $< 1.6\%$

## Context
This work extends the study of massless dark photons from kaon processes (Fabbrichesi et al., 2017) to hyperon decays, which offer a complementary probe of the $s \to d\overline\gamma$ transition. The authors use the same simplified-model framework as Fabbrichesi et al. (2017, 2019) but apply it to baryonic final states, finding that hyperon modes can be more sensitive than kaon modes in some scenarios. The results are directly testable at BESIII, which has proposed sensitivity levels for three-body hyperon decays with missing energy (Li, 2016) and can achieve even better sensitivity for the two-body modes studied here. Future super charm-tau factories could further constrain or discover these decays.
