# Dark particle mass effects on neutron star properties from a short-range correlated hadronic model

> In this work we study a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model, with nucleonic short-range correlations (SRC) included, coupled to dark matter (DM) through the Higgs boson. We study different parametrizations of this model by running the dark particle Fermi momentum, and its mass in the range of $50$ GeV $\leqslant M_χ\leqslant 500$ GeV, compatible with experimental spin-independent scattering cross-sections. By using this RMF-SRC-DM model, we calculate some neutron star quantities, namely, mass-radius profiles, dimensionless tidal deformabilities, and crustal properties. Our findings show that is possible to construct RMF-SRC-DM parametrizations in agreement with constraints provided by LIGO and Virgo collaboration (LVC) on the GW170817 event, and recent observational data from the NICER mission. Furthermore, we show that the increase of $M_χ$ favors the model to attain data from LVC regarding the tidal deformabilities. Higher values of $M_χ$ also induce a reduction of the neutron star crust (mass and thickness), and cause a decrease of the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia ($I_{\rm{\tiny crust}}/I$). Nevertheless, we show that some RMF-SRC-DM parametrizations still exhibit $I_{\rm{\tiny crust}}/I>7\%$, a condition that explains the glitch activity in rotation-powered pulsars such as the Vela one. Therefore, dark matter content can also be used for describing such a phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that increasing the dark particle mass \(M_\chi\) (from 50 GeV to 500 GeV) in a relativistic mean-field model with short-range correlations and dark matter (RMF-SRC-DM) helps the model satisfy observational constraints from the GW170817 event on tidal deformabilities, while some parametrizations still maintain a crustal fraction of the moment of inertia above 7%, a condition needed to explain pulsar glitches.

## Method

The authors couple a relativistic mean-field hadronic model (with nucleonic short-range correlations included via a high-momentum tail in the nucleon momentum distribution) to dark matter through Higgs boson exchange, using the TFcmax parametrization for the hadronic sector. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with a piecewise equation of state (outer crust from Baym-Pethick-Sutherland, inner crust from a polytropic form) to compute mass-radius profiles, dimensionless tidal deformabilities, and crustal properties (mass, thickness, moment of inertia fraction) for dark particle masses \(M_\chi\) of 50, 200, and 500 GeV and dark Fermi momenta \(k_F^{\rm DM}\) of 0.02, 0.03, and 0.04 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Dark particle mass range: \(50\ \text{GeV} \leqslant M_\chi \leqslant 500\ \text{GeV}\), compatible with spin-independent scattering cross-sections from PandaX-II, LUX, and DarkSide.
- Higgs boson mass: \(m_h = 125\ \text{GeV}\); Higgs vacuum expectation value: \(v = 246\ \text{GeV}\); Higgs-dark particle coupling: \(\xi = 0.01\); nucleon-Higgs coupling: \(f = 0.3\).
- Hadronic bulk parameters at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.148\ \text{fm}^{-3}\): binding energy \(B_0 = -16.47\ \text{MeV}\), effective nucleon mass ratio \(M^*_0/M_{\text{nuc}} = 0.57\), incompressibility \(K_0 = 260\ \text{MeV}\), symmetry energy at \(2\rho_0/3\) \(\tilde{J} = 29.4\ \text{MeV}\), symmetry energy slope \(L_0 = 74\ \text{MeV}\).
- For a \(1.5 M_\odot\) star with \(k_F^{\rm DM} = 0.04\ \text{GeV}\), increasing \(M_\chi\) from 50 GeV to 500 GeV reduces the radius from 13.3 km to 9.9 km, the crustal thickness from 1.7 km to 0.50 km, and the crustal mass from \(4.3 \times 10^{-2} M_\odot\) to \(1.5 \times 10^{-2} M_\odot\).
- LVC constraint on tidal deformability: \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 190^{+390}_{-120}\) at 90% confidence from GW170817.
- Glitch condition: crustal fraction of moment of inertia \(I_{\text{crust}}/I > 7\%\) (including crustal entrainment effects).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Dutra et al. (2021, 2022) that fixed the dark particle mass at \(M_\chi = 200\ \text{GeV}\), now allowing \(M_\chi\) to vary across a broader range compatible with experimental limits. It builds on the RMF-SRC-DM model that incorporates short-range correlations (SRC) in nucleon momentum distributions, as developed by Cai and Li (2021) and others, and applies it to neutron star observables constrained by NICER (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620) and LIGO/Virgo (GW170817). The results enable further exploration of how dark matter content can simultaneously satisfy multiple astrophysical constraints and potentially explain pulsar glitch activity.
