# Chiral $p\pm ip$ superfluid on a sphere

> We consider a spinless fermionic $p\pm ip$ superfluid living on a two-dimensional sphere. Using superfluid hydrodynamics we show that the ground state necessarily exhibits topological defects: either a pair of elementary vortices or a domain wall between $p\pm ip$ phases. In the topologically nontrivial BCS phase we identify the chiral fermion modes localized on the topological defects and compute their low-energy spectrum.

# Chiral \(p \pm ip\) superfluid on a sphere

## Key claim

The ground state of a spinless fermionic \(p \pm ip\) superfluid confined to a two-dimensional sphere necessarily contains topological defects, either a pair of elementary (anti)vortices at the poles or a domain wall separating \(p+ip\) and \(p-ip\) phases at the equator, because the Gaussian curvature acts as an effective magnetic field on the chiral Cooper pairs, and the specific ground state realized depends on the equation of state and the sphere radius.

## Method

The authors use superfluid hydrodynamics, based on the low-energy effective theory developed in Hoyos (2013) and Moroz (2014a), to construct two candidate velocity fields on a unit sphere: one with a vortex-antivortex pair at the poles and one with a domain wall at the equator. They then solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation in the Andreev approximation for each candidate to compute the low-energy spectrum of chiral fermion modes bound to the topological defects, using an index theorem to predict the number of modes and degenerate perturbation theory or stereographic projection to obtain the dispersion.

## Key numbers

* Vortex winding number: \(\text{Winding number} = \frac{1}{\pi} \oint v = -2s\), where \(s = \pm 1/2\) for \(p \pm ip\) pairing, giving a pair of (anti)vortices at the poles.
* Vortex pair core energy scales as \(\sim \ln(\mathcal{R} / \xi_{\text{core}})\), where \(\mathcal{R}\) is the sphere radius and \(\xi_{\text{core}}\) is the vortex core size.
* Domain wall energy scales as \(E_{\text{DW}} \sim |\Delta_0|^2 \mathcal{R} / \xi_{\text{DW}}\), where \(\xi_{\text{DW}}\) is the domain wall thickness.
* For the vortex pair state, the chiral mode slope is \(\omega_0 = \frac{\int d\zeta |\psi_0(\zeta)|^2 |\Delta_0|(\zeta)/\sin\zeta}{\int d\zeta |\psi_0(\zeta)|^2}\), which is finite because \(|\Delta_0|\) vanishes linearly at the vortex cores.
* For the domain wall state with a strong repulsive barrier at the equator, the low-energy spectrum is \(E = -|\Delta_0| k / \mathcal{R}\), valid for \(|\Delta_0| \mathcal{R} \gg 1\), where \(k\) is a half-odd integer angular momentum quantum number.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral \(p \pm ip\) superfluids on curved surfaces, previously investigated in flat space by Read and Green (2000) and on a sphere with an artificial \(U(1)\) magnetic monopole by Kraus (2008, 2009) and Moller (2011). Unlike those earlier studies, the present paper does not introduce a monopole, so the curvature effects are uncompensated and force topological defects into the ground state. The results enable future experimental tests in \({}^{3}\text{He}\) thin films in nanofabricated geometries and cold-atom systems, and suggest that on a surface of genus \(g\) the ground state must have vortex number \(\mp (2-2g)\) by the Gauss-Bonnet theorem.
