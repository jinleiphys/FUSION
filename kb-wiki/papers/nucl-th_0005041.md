# Charmonium absorption by nucleons

> $J/Ψ$ dissociation in collisions with nucleons is studied within a boson exchange model and the energy dependence of the dissociation cross section is calculated from the threshold for $Λ_c{\bar D}$ production to high energies. We illustrate the agreement of our results with calculations based on short distance QCD and Regge theory. The compatibility between our calculations and the data on $J/Ψ$ photoproduction on a nucleon is discussed. We evaluate the elastic $J/Ψ{+}N$ cross section using a forward dispersion relation and demonstrate the overall agreement with the predictions from QCD sum rules. Our results are compatible with the phenomenological dissociation cross section evaluated from the experimental data on $J/Ψ$ production from $γ{+}A$, $p{+}A$ and $A{+}A$ collisions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the $J/\Psi$ dissociation cross section on nucleons using a boson exchange model and find that the total cross section approaches 5.5 mb at high invariant collision energy, but this result depends on the choice of coupling constants and cutoff parameters in the form factors, which are adjusted to match experimental data and other theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors compute the cross sections for the reactions $J/\Psi+N\to\Lambda_c+\bar{D}$, $J/\Psi+N\to\Lambda_c+\bar{D}^\ast$, and $J/\Psi+N\to N+D+\bar{D}$ using effective Lagrangian densities with coupling constants derived from SU(4) relations and the vector dominance model. They employ monopole form factors at the interaction vertices with cutoff parameters $\Lambda=3.1$ GeV for the $JDD$ and $JDD^\ast$ vertices and $\Lambda=2$ GeV for the $DN\Lambda_c$ and $D^\ast N\Lambda_c$ vertices, and sum the contributions from all channels to obtain the total dissociation cross section.

## Key numbers

- Total $J/\Psi+N$ cross section approaches 5.5 mb at high invariant collision energy
- $JDD$ coupling constant adopted as $g_{JDD}=7.64$
- $DN\Lambda_c$ coupling constant adopted as $g_{DN\Lambda_c}=14.8$, with QCD sum rules suggesting a smaller value of $6.7\pm2.1$
- $D^\ast N\Lambda_c$ coupling constant adopted as $g_{D^\ast N\Lambda_c}=-19$
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda=3.1$ GeV for form factors at $JDD$ and $JDD^\ast$ vertices
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda=2$ GeV for form factors at $DN\Lambda_c$ and $D^\ast N\Lambda_c$ vertices
- Total $\bar{D}+N$ cross section taken as constant $\sigma_{\bar{D}N}=20$ mb
- $J/\Psi$ photoproduction data at mean photon energy of 17 GeV indicate a $J/\Psi+N$ cross section of $3.5\pm0.9$ mb
- Combined analysis of $p+A$ collisions at beam energies from 200 to 800 GeV provides a $J/\Psi+N$ cross section of $7.3\pm0.6$ mb

## Context

This work extends previous hadronic models of $J/\Psi$ suppression in heavy ion collisions, where the dissociation cross section was treated as a free parameter ranging from 3.0 to 6.7 mb in calculations by Cassing, Capella, and others. The authors compare their results with short distance QCD calculations by Bhanot and Peskin, Regge theory predictions by Donnachie and Landshoff, and QCD sum rule predictions for the $J/\Psi$ mass shift in nuclear matter by Klingl, Hayashigaki, and Kim. The calculations enable a more consistent interpretation of NA50 data on $J/\Psi$ suppression in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at CERN-SPS without requiring quark-gluon plasma formation.
