# Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculation of ground state properties of neutron drops

> The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method has been applied to simulate droplets of 7 and 8 neutrons. Results for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, which include tensor, spin--orbit and three--body forces, plus a standard one--body confining potential, have been compared with analogous calculations obtained with Green's Function Monte Carlo methods. We have studied the dependence of the binding energy, the one--body density and the spin--orbit splittings of $^7n$ on the depth of the confining potential. The results obtained show an overall agreement between the two quantum Monte Carlo methods, although there persist differences in the evaluation of spin--orbit forces, as previously indicated by bulk neutron matter calculations. Energy density functional models, largely used in astrophysical applications, seem to provide results significantly different from those of quantum simulations. Given its scaling behavior in the number of nucleons, the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method seems to be one of the best candidate to perform {\sl ab initio} calculations on neutron rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method produces ground state energies for neutron drops of 7 and 8 neutrons that agree with Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) results to within about 2%, but the spin-orbit splitting of the 7-neutron drop computed with AFDMC is approximately half the GFMC value, indicating a persistent discrepancy in the treatment of spin-orbit forces.

## Method

The authors applied the AFDMC method to simulate droplets of 7 and 8 neutrons confined by a Wood-Saxon one-body potential, using the Argonne v'_8 two-body potential (which includes tensor, spin-orbit, and central terms) plus the Urbana IX three-body potential. The spin-dependent parts of the interaction were linearized via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, allowing Monte Carlo sampling of auxiliary fields and spinor rotations, with a path constraint to control the fermion sign problem. Results were compared with GFMC calculations and with energy density functional models (Skyrme M, Skyrme 1', FPS, FPS-21).

## Key numbers

- AFDMC ground state energy of $^8n(0^+)$ with AU8' interaction at $V_0=20$ MeV: $-37.8(2)$ MeV, compared to GFMC(AU8') value of $-38.3(1)$ MeV
- AFDMC ground state energy of $^7n(3/2^-)$ with AU8' at $V_0=20$ MeV: $-31.8(1)$ MeV, compared to GFMC(AU8') value of $-32.4(1)$ MeV
- AFDMC spin-orbit splitting of $^7n$ (energy difference between $^7n(3/2^-)$ and $^7n(1/2^-)$) with AU8': $0.7(2)$ MeV, compared to GFMC(AU8') value of $1.6(2)$ MeV
- AFDMC ground state energy of $^8n(0^+)$ with AU6' at $V_0=20$ MeV: $-36.82(5)$ MeV
- Energy density functional results for $^8n(0^+)$ at $V_0=20$ MeV: Skyrme M gave $-47.4$ MeV, FPS gave $-32.1$ MeV, Skyrme 1' gave $-47.03$ MeV, SKM gave $-50.6$ MeV
- AFDMC mixed estimate of spin-orbit expectation value for $^8n(0^+)$ at $V_0=20$ MeV: $-0.41(7)$ MeV
- AFDMC ground state energy of $^8n(0^+)$ with AU8' at $V_0=35$ MeV: $-111.0(2)$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of AFDMC from bulk neutron matter calculations (Sarsa 2003) to finite neutron droplets, providing a direct benchmark against GFMC results for the same systems studied by Pudliner (1996), Smerzi (1997), and Pieper (2001). The results confirm that AFDMC scales as $N^3$ operations per time step, making it more suitable than GFMC for studying larger neutron-rich systems. The significant deviations from energy density functional models (Skyrme and FPS families) highlight the need for ab initio methods in neutron-rich matter relevant to astrophysical applications such as neutron star structure and supernova mechanisms.
