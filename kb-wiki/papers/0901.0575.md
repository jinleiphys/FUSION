# The weak structure of the nucleon from muon capture on 3He

> I use a microscopic calculation of the weak capture process 3He(μ^{-},ν_μ)3H, to extract and constrain the weak form factors of the nucleon, particularly the induced pseudo-scalar, and the second-class currents. The induced pseudo-scalar form-factor is found to agree with the prediction of chiral perturbation theory. The constraint found on the conservation of vector current (CVC) hypothesis is the tightest to date.

## Key claim
The muon capture on ³He provides the tightest constraint to date on the conservation of vector current (CVC) hypothesis, with the extracted induced pseudoscalar form factor agreeing with chiral perturbation theory predictions, though the results depend on a hybrid approach combining realistic nuclear forces with chiral perturbation theory currents.

## Method
The author performed a parameter-free microscopic calculation of the ³He(μ⁻,ν_μ)³H capture process using bound states of the trinuclei calculated with the Argonne v18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana-IX (UIX) three-nucleon force, solved via the EIHH method. The dynamics of the capture were described using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to fourth order, with meson exchange currents appearing at third order and one unknown parameter calibrated using triton β-decay rate. The capture rate was then used to extract the weak form factors of the nucleon, including the induced pseudoscalar G_P, the second-class current g_t, and the CVC-breaking scalar form factor g_s.

## Key numbers
- Experimental capture rate: Γ_exp = 1496(4) Hz
- Theoretical capture rate: Γ = 1499(10)_NM(6)_RC Hz, consistent with experiment
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor: G_P(q² = -0.954 m_μ²) = 8.13 ± 0.6, compared to χPT prediction of 7.99(20) and experimental determination of 7.3 ± 1.2 at q² = -0.88 m_μ²
- Axial G-parity breaking term: g_t/g_A = -0.1 ± 0.68, compared to theoretical determination of -0.0152(53)
- CVC breaking form factor: g_s = -0.005 ± 0.040, compared to previous limit of 0.01 ± 0.27
- Constraint on CVC hypothesis: |g_s| < 0.045
- Binding energies reproduced within 20 keV of experimental values

## Context
This work extends previous attempts by Marcucci et al. to use muon capture on ³He for constraining nucleonic weak form factors, which were limited by the need for free parameters in the nuclear description. The calculation improves upon earlier studies by including electroweak radiative corrections (which increase the decay rate by 3%) and using a chiral perturbation theory framework that provides a direct connection to QCD. The results enable tighter constraints on second-class currents and the CVC hypothesis, and demonstrate the potential of ab initio nuclear calculations as quantitative tests of the weak structure of the nucleon.
