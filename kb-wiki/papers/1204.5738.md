# Thermodynamics of ultrasmall metallic grains in the presence of pairing and exchange correlations: Mesoscopic fluctuations

> We study the mesoscopic fluctuations of thermodynamic observables in a nanosized metallic grain in which the single-particle dynamics are chaotic and the dimensionless Thouless conductance is large. Such a grain is modeled by the universal Hamiltonian describing the competition between exchange and pairing correlations. The exchange term is taken into account exactly by a spin-projection method, and the pairing term is treated in the static-path approximation together with small-amplitude quantal fluctuations around each static fluctuation of the pairing field. Odd-even particle-number effects induced by pairing correlations are included using a number-parity projection. We find that the exchange interaction shifts the number-parity effects in the heat capacity and spin susceptibility to lower temperatures. In the regime where the pairing gap is similar to or smaller than the single-particle mean level spacing, these number-parity effects are suppressed by exchange correlations, and the fluctuations of the spin susceptibility may be particularly large. However, for larger values of the pairing gap, the number-parity effects may be enhanced by exchange correlations.

## Key claim

The exchange interaction shifts number-parity effects in the heat capacity and spin susceptibility to lower temperatures; when the pairing gap is similar to or smaller than the single-particle mean level spacing, these effects are suppressed by exchange correlations and spin susceptibility fluctuations may be particularly large, but for larger pairing gaps the number-parity effects may be enhanced by exchange correlations.

## Method

The authors model a chaotic nanosized metallic grain using the universal Hamiltonian, which includes both pairing and exchange correlations. The exchange term is treated exactly via a spin-projection method, while the pairing term is handled using the static-path approximation (SPA) plus random-phase approximation (RPA) corrections for small-amplitude quantal fluctuations around each static pairing field. Number-parity effects are included via a number-parity projection, and mesoscopic fluctuations are studied by generating approximately 1000 realizations of the single-particle spectrum following Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) random-matrix theory.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless Thouless conductance is assumed large: \(g_\Th = E_\Th/\delta \gg 1\), ensuring the universal Hamiltonian is valid.
- The number of single-particle levels \(N_{\text{sp}}\) is chosen between 30 and 60 to satisfy \(N_{\text{sp}} \delta \gg kT\) and \(N_{\text{sp}} \delta \gg \Delta\).
- The pairing gap relative to mean level spacing is studied in the regime \(\Delta/\delta \lesssim 5.0\).
- The exchange coupling satisfies \(J_s/\delta < 1\) to remain below the Stoner instability of macroscopic polarization.
- The SPA+RPA critical temperature \(T_*\) increases with the BCS gap \(\Delta\) and becomes of order \(\delta\) for \(\Delta/\delta \sim 5\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pairing correlations in ultrasmall metallic grains (Anderson 1959; Ralph, Black, and Tinkham 1995–1997) and of thermodynamic properties using SPA+RPA with number-parity projection (Falci 2002) and quantum Monte Carlo methods (Van Houcke 2010). It also builds on the universal Hamiltonian framework (Kurland 2000; Aleiner 2002) and spin-projection techniques (Alhassid 2003). The method enables efficient calculation of mesoscopic fluctuations of heat capacity and spin susceptibility for many realizations, which is less practical with quantum Monte Carlo or Richardson's solution (Schmidt 2007) at larger pairing gaps or higher temperatures.
