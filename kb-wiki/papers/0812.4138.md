# Memory Effects and Transport Coefficients for Non-Newtonian Fluids

> We discuss the roles of viscosity in relativistic fluid dynamics from the point of view of memory effects. Depending on the type of quantity to which the memory effect is applied, different terms appear in higher order corrections. We show that when the memory effect applies on the extensive quantities, the hydrodynamic equations of motion become non-singular. We further discuss the question of memory effect in the derivation of transport coefficients from a microscopic theory. We generalize the application of the Green-Kubo-Nakano (GKN) to calculate transport coefficients in the framework of projection operator formalism, and derive the general formula when the fluid is non-Newtonian.

# Memory Effects and Transport Coefficients for Non-Newtonian Fluids

## Key claim
The authors show that when memory effects are applied to extensive quantities (rather than current densities), the resulting relativistic hydrodynamic equations acquire a nonlinear term that prevents the bulk viscosity from becoming arbitrarily negative, thereby stabilizing the theory even in ultra-relativistic regimes where the truncated Israel-Stewart theory becomes unstable. The standard Green-Kubo-Nakano (GKN) formula implicitly assumes Newtonian (memoryless) fluids and may therefore give transport coefficients that are not directly applicable to relativistic non-Newtonian fluids.

## Method
The authors derive a causal dissipative hydrodynamic equation by applying a memory function to the ratio of irreversible current to the inverse fluid-cell volume (an extensive measure), yielding a nonlinear differential equation for the bulk viscosity that differs from the Maxwell-Cattaneo equation. They then generalize the GKN formula within the projection operator formalism to obtain expressions for the transport coefficient and relaxation time that account for memory effects, comparing these to the standard Newtonian GKN result.

## Key numbers
- The maximum propagation speed for a diffusive process with exponential memory is given by \(\sqrt{D/\tau_R}\), where \(D\) is the diffusion constant and \(\tau_R\) is the relaxation time.
- The bulk viscosity \(\Pi\) in the finite-size-effect theory satisfies the lower bound \(\Pi \geq -\zeta / \tau_R\), where \(\zeta\) is the bulk viscosity coefficient and \(\tau_R\) the corresponding relaxation time.
- Shock formation simulations are shown for an initial Lorentz factor \(\gamma = 5\) at time \(t = 0.75\) fm, comparing the unstable (without finite-size effect) and stable (with finite-size effect) cases.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies on memory effects in relativistic hydrodynamics (Kodama and Koide, 2008; dkkm1, dkkm2, dkkm3, dkkm4) and addresses the acausality problem of the relativistic Navier-Stokes (Landau-Lifshitz) theory. It contrasts the truncated Israel-Stewart theory (Maxwell-Cattaneo equation), which removes acausality but remains unstable in ultra-relativistic flows, with the proposed finite-size-effect formulation that is both causal and stable. The generalization of the GKN formula enables future calculation of transport coefficients for non-Newtonian fluids, relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical phenomena such as gamma-ray bursts where flow speeds reach \(\gamma \sim 100\).
