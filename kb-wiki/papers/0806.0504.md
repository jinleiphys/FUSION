# Modification of nucleon properties in nuclear matter and finite nuclei

> We present a model for the description of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, and at the same time, for the study of medium modifications of nucleon properties. The nucleons are described as nontopological solitons which interact through the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons. The model explicitly incorporates quark degrees of freedom into nuclear many-body systems and provides satisfactory results on the nuclear properties. The present model predicts a significant increase of the nucleon radius at normal nuclear matter density. It is very interesting to see the nucleon properties change from the nuclear surface to the nuclear interior.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model that predicts a significant increase of the nucleon radius in nuclear matter, but the magnitude of this swelling depends strongly on the parameter set chosen in the Friedberg-Lee model and on the center-of-mass correction approach used, with the nucleon rms radius increasing by about 10-16% at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors describe nucleons as nontopological soliton bags using the Friedberg-Lee model, where three quarks are bound by a scalar field with nonlinear self-interactions. They embed these soliton bags in nuclear matter and finite nuclei, with the quarks coupling to additional scalar (sigma), vector (omega), and rho meson fields treated in the mean-field approximation, solving the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently at both the quark and hadron levels.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon rms radius increases by about 10-16% at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3)
- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: 0.15 fm^-3, energy per nucleon: -16 MeV, symmetry energy: 35 MeV
- Set A: incompressibility K = 302 MeV; Set B: K = 384 MeV; Set C: K = 184 MeV (all at same saturation density and energy per particle)
- At normal nuclear matter density, (g_A/g_V)*/(g_A/g_V) = 0.98 for set A, 0.87 for set B, and 0.94 for set C
- Free nucleon properties: M = 939 MeV, r_c = 0.83 fm; Set A gives mu_p = 2.80, g_A/g_V = 0.87; Set B gives mu_p = 2.77, g_A/g_V = 0.90; Set C gives mu_p = 2.85, g_A/g_V = 0.80
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 500 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and the quark mean-field (QMF) model by replacing the MIT bag or constituent quark descriptions with the Friedberg-Lee nontopological soliton bag model. The predicted nucleon swelling of 10-16% is substantially larger than the 1-3% enhancement in the QMC model and the 5-9% increase in the QMF model, and also exceeds the less than 6% swelling constrained by quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering data. The model enables future studies of how nucleon properties change from the nuclear surface to the nuclear interior in finite nuclei.
