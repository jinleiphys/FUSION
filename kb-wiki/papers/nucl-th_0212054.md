# Freeze-out in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> I discuss the effects and quantities that influence the decoupling of particles from the fireball. The crucial role is played by the scattering rate. I show the results for the scattering rate at SPS and RHIC and discuss their implications.

## Key claim

The freeze-out of particles from the fireball in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is not a sharp, universal process occurring at a single three-dimensional hyper-surface; instead, it depends on particle momentum, with high-transverse-momentum particles decoupling earlier than low-momentum ones, and the scattering rate is the crucial quantity that determines decoupling.

## Method

The author calculates the scattering rate of pions in the fireball medium by summing contributions from scattering on pions, nucleons, and antinucleons, using thermal distributions of momenta and tuning abundances to reproduce observed phase-space densities and mid-rapidity yield ratios. The escape probability for a particle is computed via an opacity integral of the scattering rate along its expected straight-line trajectory, with the expansion dynamics determining the time dependence of the density. Results are compared for SPS and RHIC collision energies.

## Key numbers

- The pion mean free path at freeze-out is estimated to be of the order of 1 fm, possibly 2–3 fm.
- At a temperature of 120 MeV, the chance for a particle with momentum above 250 MeV to escape without further scattering is about 10%.
- To obtain a reasonable escape probability of 30–50%, the temperature must drop to about 100 MeV.
- The pion freeze-out phase-space density at RHIC shows an increase by a factor of 2 with respect to the SPS.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of freeze-out criteria, such as the constant freeze-out density observed in S-induced reactions at 200 AGeV (NA35 Collaboration, 1990s) and the critical mean free path approach (Adamova et al., 2002), showing that these universal criteria fail when applied across different collision energies. The momentum-dependent freeze-out mechanism builds on the escape-probability formalism of Grassi et al. (1994) and Hung and Shuryak (1997), and it enables more realistic modeling of hadronic spectra and HBT radii, particularly the time ordering of particle emission that affects the K_perp dependence of HBT radii.
