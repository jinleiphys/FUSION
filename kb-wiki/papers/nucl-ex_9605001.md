# Evidence for Neutrino Oscillations from Muon Decay at Rest

> A search for nu_bar_mu to nu_bar_e oscillations has been conducted at the Los Alamos Meson Physics Facility using nu_bar_mu from mu+ decay at rest. The nu_bar_e are detected via the reaction (nu_bar_e,p) -> (e+,n), correlated with the 2.2 MeV gamma from (n,p) -> (d,gamma). The use of tight cuts to identify e+ events with correlated gamma rays yields 22 events with e+ energy between 36 and 60 MeV and only 4.6 (+/- 0.6) background events. The probability that this excess is due entirely to a statistical fluctuation is 4.1E-08. A chi^2 fit to the entire e+ sample results in a total excess of 51.8 (+18.7) (-16.9) (+/- 8.0) events with e+ energy between 20 and 60 MeV. If attributed to nu_bar_mu -> nu_bar_e oscillations, this corresponds to an oscillation probability (averaged over the experimental energy and spatial acceptance) of 0.0031 (+0.0011) (-0.0010) (+/- 0.0005).

## Key claim

The LSND collaboration reports an excess of electron antineutrino-like events from a muon decay-at-rest source that cannot be explained by known backgrounds, with a statistical significance of 4.1 × 10⁻⁸ probability of being a fluctuation; however, the paper explicitly notes that any exotic process creating \(\bar\nu_e\) at production, in flight, or in detection could produce the same signal, not necessarily neutrino oscillations.

## Method

The experiment used \(\bar\nu_\mu\) from \(\mu^+\) decay at rest at LAMPF, searching for \(\bar\nu_\mu \to \bar\nu_e\) oscillations via the inverse beta decay reaction \(\bar\nu_e p \to e^+ n\) in a 167-ton liquid scintillator detector located 30 m from the neutrino source. The signature required a positron with energy between 20 and 60 MeV followed by a correlated 2.2 MeV gamma ray from neutron capture on free protons (\(np \to d\gamma\)), with cosmic ray backgrounds suppressed by a veto shield, particle identification cuts, and subtraction using beam-off data collected for about 14 times longer than beam-on data.

## Key numbers

- 22 events observed with positron energy between 36 and 60 MeV after tight cuts, compared to 4.6 ± 0.6 background events
- Probability that the excess is due to a statistical fluctuation: 4.1 × 10⁻⁸
- Total excess from \(\chi^2\) fit for positron energy 20–60 MeV: 51.8⁺¹⁸·⁷₋₁₆·₉ ± 8.0 events
- Oscillation probability averaged over experimental acceptance: 0.0031⁺⁰·⁰⁰¹¹₋₀·⁰⁰¹⁰ ± 0.0005 (or 0.31⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·¹⁰ ± 0.05%)
- Ratio of \(\bar\nu_e\) from \(\mu^-\) decay at rest to \(\bar\nu_\mu\) from \(\mu^+\) decay at rest: 7.8 × 10⁻⁴ from Monte Carlo simulation
- Neutron capture time constant from cosmic ray neutron sample: 188 ± 3 μs, consistent with expected 186 μs
- Efficiency for producing and detecting a correlated 2.2 MeV gamma within 2.5 m, 21–50 PMT hits, and within 1 ms: 63 ± 4%
- Accidental gamma rate over entire detector: 1.07 ± 0.01 kHz (1993), 1.19 ± 0.01 kHz (1994), 1.14 ± 0.01 kHz (1995)

## Context

This result extends the search for neutrino oscillations to the \(\Delta m^2\) range accessible with decay-at-rest neutrinos, following earlier hints from solar neutrino experiments (Bahcall, Davis, Kamiokande, gallium) and atmospheric neutrino experiments (Kamiokande, Soudan, IMB) that observed deficits compared to predictions. The paper notes that the same \(\Delta m^2\) might explain both the atmospheric neutrino effect and this LSND result, but states that more experimental data will be needed to firmly establish neutrino oscillations and clarify relationships among these indications.
