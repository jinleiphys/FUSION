# Formation of relativistic non-viscous fluid in central collisions of protons with energy 0.8 TeV with photoemulsion nuclei

> By the methods of mathematical statistics we test a qualitative prediction of the old theory of relativistic hydrodynamics non-viscous liquid which can be used as a part of the process of hadronization within the modern hydrodynamical approach for the description of the quark-gluon plasma. Experimental data on the interaction of protons with the energies of 0.8 TeV with emulsion nuclei are used. Results do not contradict the formation of relativistic ideal non-viscous liquid in rare central collisions.

# Formation of relativistic non-viscous fluid in central collisions of protons with energy 0.8 TeV with photoemulsion nuclei

## Key claim
The experimental data do not contradict the formation of a relativistic ideal non-viscous liquid in rare central collisions of 0.8 TeV protons with emulsion nuclei, but only for a very small fraction of events (eight out of 1685) that satisfy strict statistical selection criteria. The authors note that this energy is close to the minimum threshold of 1 TeV predicted by Landau (1953, 1952) for such a fluid to form.

## Method
The authors applied mathematical statistics methods to test whether the quasirapidity distribution of secondary charged particles in individual central proton-emulsion collisions follows a Gaussian form, as predicted by Landau's relativistic hydrodynamics of a non-viscous liquid. They computed the asymmetry (g1) and excess (g2) for each event, then constructed normalized variables d1 and d2 whose ensemble averages should follow a standard normal distribution if the underlying quasirapidity distribution is Gaussian. Events were selected using two criteria: a qualitative criterion requiring maximum weakly ionizing particles and minimum gray particles (to select central collisions), and a quantitative criterion requiring that the absolute values of the scaled averages of d1 and d2 be less than two.

## Key numbers
- Incident proton energy: 0.8 TeV
- Total number of collisions analyzed: 1685
- Average multiplicity of weakly ionizing particles: 20; average multiplicity of all charged particles: 25
- Events satisfying both selection criteria: 8 stars, each with multiplicity of relativistic singly charged particles n ≥ 55 and complete absence of gray particles
- For these 8 events: |d1̄ √N| = 2.0 and |d2̄ √N| = 0.4, both within the expected 95% confidence interval (absolute value less than 2)

## Context
This work tests the old Landau (1953, 1952) prediction that a relativistic non-viscous fluid can form in central hadron collisions at energies above approximately 1 TeV, using experimental data from the Baton Rouge-Krakow-Moscow-Tashkent Collaboration. The authors position this as a potential component of the hadronization stage within the modern hydrodynamic description of quark-gluon plasma, as reviewed by Dremin (2010) and Shuryak (2009). The results suggest that similar analyses at higher energies (such as those accessible at ATLAS) would be valuable to confirm whether the Landau approach can be incorporated into the modern hydrodynamic theory of QGP hadronization.
