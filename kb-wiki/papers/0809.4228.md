# Quark matter in compact stars: astrophysical implications and possible signatures

> After a brief non technical introduction of the basic properties of strange quark matter (SQM) in compact stars, we consider some of the late important advances in the field, and discuss some recent astrophysical observational data that could shed new light on the possible presence of SQM in compact stars. We show that above a threshold value of the gravitational mass a neutron star (pure hadronic star) is metastable to the decay (conversion) to an hybrid neutron star or to a strange star. We explore the consequences of the metastability of "massive" neutron stars and of the existence of stable compact "quark" stars (hybrid neutron stars or strange stars) on the concept of limiting mass of compact stars, and we give an extension of this concept with respect to the "classical" one given in 1939 by Oppenheimer and Volkoff.

## Key claim

The paper argues that above a threshold gravitational mass, a pure hadronic neutron star becomes metastable and can decay (convert) into a hybrid neutron star or a strange star, extending the classical concept of the limiting mass of compact stars originally given by Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939). The author presents this as a conditional possibility rather than a definitive prediction, noting that the existence of such metastability depends on the specific equation of state and the threshold mass value.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework for strange quark matter (SQM) in compact stars using the MIT bag model equation of state, with parameters including the bag constant B, strange quark mass m_s, and QCD structure constant α_c. The analysis considers the Gibbs' criterion for phase equilibrium in multicomponent systems with two conserved charges (electric charge and baryon number), and examines the layered structure of hybrid stars from the surface through the outer crust, inner crust, outer core, mixed hadron-quark layer, and quark matter core. The paper then discusses astrophysical observational data that could indicate the presence of SQM in compact stars, particularly focusing on pulsar braking index measurements.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of a hybrid star configuration: M_max = 1.448 M_⊙, with radius R = 10.2 km and central density ρ_c = 28.1 × 10^14 g/cm^3 (using GM3 EOS for hadronic phase and bag model EOS with B = 136.6 MeV/fm^3 and m_s = 150 MeV)
- Maximum mass of the corresponding pure hadronic star: M_max = 1.552 M_⊙, with R = 10.7 km and central density ρ_c = 25.4 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- Neutron drip density: ρ_n^drip ≃ 4.3 × 10^11 g/cm^3
- Nuclear matter density: ρ_NM ≃ 2.8 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- Outer core onset density: ρ_core = 0.5–0.8 ρ_NM
- Surface density of iron: ρ_Fe = 7.9 g/cm^3
- Energy per baryon of most bound nuclei: approximately 930.4 MeV (for ^62Ni, ^58Fe, ^56Fe)

## Context

This work extends the classical concept of the limiting mass of compact stars introduced by Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939), building on earlier studies of quark matter in stars by Ivanenko and Kurdgelaidze (1965), Bodmer (1971), and Witten (1984). The paper reviews recent advances in color superconductivity and its implications for neutron star physics, following the work of numerous groups including Rajagopal and Wilczek (2000), Nardulli (2001), Alford (2001), and others. The author discusses how pulsar timing properties, particularly deviations of the braking index from the canonical value n=3 predicted by the magnetic dipole model, could serve as observational signatures for quark deconfinement in neutron stars, while noting that such deviations could also arise from magnetic field decay or magnetic axis alignment as discussed by Tauris and Konar (2001).
