# Cold quark stars from hot lattice QCD

> Based on a quasiparticle model for βstable and electrically neutral deconfined matter we address the mass-radius relation of pure quark stars. The model is adjusted to recent hot lattice QCD results for 2 + 1 flavors with almost physical quark masses. We find rather small radii and masses of equilibrium configurations composed of cold deconfined matter, well distinguished from neutron or hybrid stars.

## Key claim

Pure quark stars constructed from a quasiparticle model adjusted to hot lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential have maximum masses around 0.5 solar masses and radii of about 3 km, well below canonical neutron star values, provided the extrapolation to non-zero chemical potential is sufficiently smooth.

## Method

The authors used a quasiparticle model with an effective coupling G² that runs with temperature and chemical potential, adjusted to reproduce the interaction measure (e-3p)/T⁴ from lattice QCD data for 2+1 flavors with almost physical quark masses at μ=0. They solved a flow equation to transport the effective coupling from the temperature axis to the chemical potential axis, imposing β-equilibrium and electric charge neutrality via five side conditions. The resulting zero-temperature equation of state was then used to integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for spherically symmetric equilibrium configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses of pure quark stars are about 0.5 M⊙ with radii of about 3 km, from integration of TOV equations using equations of state fitted to lattice QCD data from Baz09 and Che07.
- The vacuum energy density e₀¹/⁴ ranges from 366 MeV to 381 MeV across the four fits to p4 and asqtad lattice data with Nτ=6 and Nτ=8.
- The inverse squared velocity of sound v_s⁻² = ∂p/∂e ranges from 3.81 to 4.47 for the four fits.
- For v_s⁻² = 3 ± 1, the scaled maximum mass is 0.004 ± 0.001 in units of (G_N e₀)⁻¹/².
- The strange quark rest mass m_s,0 = 105 MeV was used, with m_u,0 = m_d,0 = m_s,0/10.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark stars using quasiparticle models (Pes01b, Pes03, Iva05, Fra01, Fra02, AS02, SLS99) but with more direct contact to first-principles lattice QCD results from Baz09 and Che07. The resulting mass-radius relations are well distinguished from canonical neutron stars (1.4 M⊙, 15 km radii) and from twin star scenarios discussed by Schertler (2002). The approach enables future construction of hybrid stars by combining this deconfined matter equation of state with a hadronic low-density equation of state, and can be extended to include full hard-thermal-loop quasiparticle effects with Landau damping and collective modes.
