# D-instantons in Real Time Dynamics

> Instanton is known to exist in Euclidean spacetime only. Their role in real time dynamics is usually understood as tunneling effect by Wick rotation. We illustrate other effects of instanton in holography by investigating 5d effective gravity theory of the black D3-brane-D-instanton system. The supergravity description of the D3-brane-D-instanton system is dual to the super Yang-Mills theory with topological excitations of the vacuum. We obtain Euclidean correlators in the presence of instantons by analyzing of the fluctuations of the bulk fields in the 5d effective theory. Furthermore, analytic continuation of Euclidean correlators leads to retarded correlators, which characterize real time dynamics. We find interestingly real time fluctuations of topological charge can destroy instantons and the lifetime of instanton is set by temperature. This implies instanton contribution to "real time dynamics" is suppressed at high temperature, which is analogous to classic field theory results that instanton contribution to "thermodynamics" is suppressed at high temperature.

## Key claim

The authors show that instantons, which are traditionally understood as Euclidean tunneling configurations, can be studied in real time dynamics via holography, and they find that real time fluctuations of topological charge can destroy instantons, with the instanton lifetime set by temperature. This implies that instanton contributions to real time dynamics are suppressed at high temperature, analogous to the known suppression of instanton contributions to thermodynamics at high temperature.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D effective gravity theory from the 10D type IIB supergravity description of the black D3-brane-D-instanton system, which is holographically dual to N=4 super Yang-Mills theory with topological vacuum excitations. They study fluctuations of the 5D metric, dilaton, and axion fields around this background, solve the linearized equations of motion with regularity boundary conditions at the horizon, and compute Euclidean two-point correlators of stress tensor components, gluon condensate, and topological charge density. They then analytically continue these Euclidean correlators to obtain retarded correlators that characterize real time dynamics.

## Key numbers

* The temperature of the dual field theory is given by T = r_T / (pi R^2).
* The instanton density parameter q represents the vacuum expectation value of the topological charge density, with <tilde O_E> = 4q (in units where the pressure P = pi^2 N^2 T^4 / 8).
* Euclidean correlators among stress tensor components are fixed by Ward identities: G_E^{00,00} = 3P, G_E^{00,ii} = 3P, G_E^{ii,jj} = -9P.
* Cross correlators between stress tensor and topological charge density are G_E^{00,tilde O} = -4q and G_E^{ii,tilde O} = 4q.
* Cross correlators between stress tensor and gluon condensate are G_E^{00,O} = 4q and G_E^{ii,O} = -4q.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of instantons in the D3-brane-D-instanton system, which were previously used to study chiral symmetry breaking (Gwak 2012, Zhang 2016, Zhang 2017) and thermodynamics of anisotropic instanton distributions (Mateos 2011, Cheng 2014). The authors build on the D-instanton background construction of Liu (1999) and Barbon (1999) and the finite-temperature extension by Ghoroku (2005). The key advance is moving beyond Euclidean thermodynamics to study real time dynamics via retarded correlators, enabled by the holographic approach which avoids the analytic continuation ambiguities present in lattice gauge theory. The result that instanton contributions to real time dynamics are suppressed at high temperature parallels classic field theory results by Pisarski (1980) and Shuryak (1994) showing suppression of instanton contributions to thermodynamics at high temperature.
