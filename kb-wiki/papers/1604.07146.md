# $\it{Ab}$ $\it{initio}$ nuclear many-body perturbation calculations in the Hartree-Fock basis

> Starting from realistic nuclear forces, the chiral N$^3$LO and JISP16, we have applied many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) to the structure of closed-shell nuclei, $^4$He and $^{16}$O. The two-body N$^3$LO interaction is softened by a similarity renormalization group transformation while JISP16 is adopted without renormalization. The MBPT calculations are performed within the Hartree-Fock (HF) bases. The angular momentum coupled scheme is used, which can reduce the computational task. Corrections up to the third order in energy and up to the second order in radius are evaluated. Higher-order corrections in the HF basis are small relative to the leading-order perturbative result. Using the anti-symmetrized Goldstone diagram expansions of the wave function, we directly correct the one-body density for the calculation of the radius, rather than calculate corrections to the occupation propabilities of single-particle orbits as found in other treatments. We compare our results with other methods where available and find good agreement. This supports the conclusion that our methods produce reasonably converged results with these interactions. We also compare our results with experimental data.

## Key claim

Many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) corrections in the Hartree-Fock (HF) basis, evaluated up to third order in energy and second order in radius, produce reasonably converged results for closed-shell nuclei when starting from realistic nuclear forces, provided the interactions are sufficiently soft (either via SRG renormalization or by construction as in JISP16) and higher-order corrections remain small relative to the leading-order perturbative result.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock calculations for \(^4\)He and \(^{16}\)O using two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: the chiral N\(^3\)LO potential softened by a similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation, and the unrenormalized JISP16 interaction. They then applied Rayleigh-Schrödinger many-body perturbation theory in the HF basis, evaluating energy corrections up to third order and radius corrections up to second order via anti-symmetrized Goldstone diagram expansions of the one-body density, using an angular-momentum-coupled scheme to reduce computational effort.

## Context

This work extends earlier MBPT calculations in the HF basis that used other renormalization schemes such as \(V_{\text{low-}k}\), Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS), and Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) (PhysRevC.68.034320, PhysRevC.69.034332, PhysRevC.73.044312). It differs from those treatments by using SRG-renormalized chiral N\(^3\)LO and the bare JISP16 interaction, and by directly correcting the one-body density via anti-symmetrized Goldstone diagrams for the radius rather than approximating corrections through occupation probabilities. The results enable systematic benchmarking of MBPT convergence for closed-shell nuclei and provide a foundation for extending such calculations to heavier systems or including three-nucleon forces.
