# Similarity renormalization group evolution of hypernuclear Hamiltonians

> Unitary transformations of a Hamiltonian generally induce interaction terms beyond the particle rank present in the untransformed Hamiltonian that have to be captured and included in a many-body calculation. In systems with strangeness such as hypernuclei, the three-body terms induced by the hyperon-nucleon interaction are strong, so their inclusion is crucial. We present in detail a procedure for computing hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interaction terms that are induced during a similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow. The SRG is carried out in a basis spanned by antisymmetric harmonic-oscillator states with respect to three-body Jacobi coordinates. We discuss basis construction, antisymmetrization, numerical evaluation of the flow equations, and separation of the genuine three-body terms. We then use the hypernuclear no-core shell model with SRG-evolved Hamiltonians, addressing the sensitivity of hypernuclear states and hyperon separation energies to changes in the nucleonic Hamiltonian by example of $^7_Λ$Li, $^9_Λ$Be, $^{11}_Λ$B, $^{13}_Λ$C, and the hyper-helium chain. We also present a survey of the hyper-hydrogen chain, exploring the structure of hypernuclei with extreme neutron-proton asymmetries.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of hypernuclear Hamiltonians induces strong hyperon-nucleon-nucleon (YNN) three-body interactions that must be explicitly computed and included in many-body calculations; the paper provides the detailed formalism for computing these induced YNN terms, which are crucial because the SRG evolution in two-body space alone produces too much attraction and massively overbinds hypernuclei compared to unevolved interactions.

## Method

The authors carry out the SRG flow equation in a basis of antisymmetric harmonic-oscillator states defined with respect to three-body Jacobi coordinates, constructing the basis, performing antisymmetrization via diagonalization of the antisymmetrizer, and numerically solving the matrix differential equation for the Hamiltonian in blocks of good strangeness, total angular momentum, parity, and isospin. The induced three-body terms are separated from the evolved Hamiltonian by subtracting the intrinsic kinetic energy and the two-body interaction evolved in two-body space to the same flow parameter, followed by a basis-frequency transformation from the SRG frequency to the many-body calculation frequency. The resulting Hamiltonians are then used in hypernuclear no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for a range of light hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The SRG evolution is performed with a flow parameter \(\alpha\) having units of fm\(^4\), using the generator \(\Gamma = m_N^2 T_{\text{int}}\) with the squared nucleon mass \(m_N^2\) setting the unit.
- The two-body basis truncation is set by \(e = 2n + l \le E_{\text{2,max}}\) and the three-body basis by \(E \le E_{\text{3,max}}\), with the choice \(E_{\text{2,max}} = E_{\text{3,max}}\) to minimize truncation artifacts.
- The transformation parameter for the harmonic-oscillator bracket connecting the two Jacobi coordinate sets used in antisymmetrization is \(D = m_b m_c / (m_a (m_a + m_b + m_c))\).
- The antisymmetrizer is block diagonal in the intrinsic energy quantum number \(E = 2n_1 + l_1 + 2n_2 + l_2\), allowing separate antisymmetrization for each finite-dimensional block.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the hypernuclear no-core shell model (Wirth 2014, Wirth 2018) and their earlier demonstration that SRG-induced YNN terms are strong and cannot be neglected (Wirth 2016). The paper provides the full formalism for computing these induced three-body terms, which was previously only presented in application-focused publications. The results directly impact the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics, as models that omit \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) conversion must include strong repulsive \(\Lambda\)NN forces (Chatterjee 2016, Lonardoni 2015). The new calculations use state-of-the-art nucleonic Hamiltonians with better saturation properties than those used in prior work.
