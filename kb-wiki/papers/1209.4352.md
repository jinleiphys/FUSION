# Electric dipole moment of the rho-meson

> At an hadronic scale the effect of CP-violating interactions that typically appear in extensions of the Standard Model may be described by an effective Lagrangian, in which the operators are expressed in terms of lepton and partonic gluon and quark fields, and ordered by their mass dimension, k .geq. 4. Using a global-symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, we compute the rho-meson's electric dipole moment (EDM), d_rho, as generated by the leading dimension-four and -five CP-violating operators and an example of a dimension-six four-quark operator. The two dimension-five operators; viz., quark-EDM and -chromo-EDM, produce contributions to d_rho whose coefficients are of the same sign and within a factor of two in magnitude. Moreover, should a suppression mechanism be verified for the theta-term in any beyond-Standard-Model theory, the contribution from a four-quark operator can match the quark-EDM and -chromo-EDM in importance. This study serves as a prototype for the more challenging task of computing the neutron's EDM.

## Key claim

Using a global-symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, the authors compute the rho-meson's electric dipole moment (EDM) as generated by the leading dimension-four and -five CP-violating operators and an example of a dimension-six four-quark operator, finding that the two dimension-five operators produce contributions whose coefficients are of the same sign and within a factor of two in magnitude, and that a four-quark operator can match these in importance if a suppression mechanism for the theta-term is verified.

## Method

The computation employs a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) using a vector×vector contact interaction, which is regularized in a Poincaré-covariant manner with infrared and ultraviolet regulators. The rho-meson's EDM is extracted from the dressed rho-gamma vertex at zero momentum transfer by applying projection operators to isolate the electric dipole form factor, with CP-violating effects introduced separately through modifications to the quark-photon vertex, the rho-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and the dressed-quark propagator.

## Key numbers

- The rho-meson magnetic moment computed in this framework is 2.11 in units of e/[2m_rho], compared to 2.01 from a renormalization-group-improved DSE treatment (Bhagwat 2006) and 2.14 from a light-front constituent quark model (deMelo 1997).
- The contact-interaction parameters used are: alpha_IR/pi = 0.93, m_G = 0.8 GeV, Lambda_ir = 0.24 GeV, Lambda_uv = 0.905 GeV, and current-quark mass m = 0.007 GeV.
- The computed rho-meson mass is 0.929 GeV and its leptonic decay constant is 0.129 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior DSE-based studies of hadron EDMs (Hecht 1997, Hecht 1999, Pospelov 1999) by considering a broader set of CP-violating operator structures within a single unifying framework, including for the first time the impact of a dimension-six four-quark operator on the rho-meson's EDM. The study serves as a prototype for the more challenging task of computing the neutron's EDM, and the results are connected to a vast array of hadron observables computed within the same framework using identical parameters (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2010, Roberts 2011, Wilson 2011, Chen 2012).
