# Theoretical study on the contributions of $ω$ meson to the $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ and $J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$ decays

> With the newly measurements of $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ from LHCb Collaboration, in addition to the dominant contribution from the $ρ^0$ meson, we perform a theoretical study on the contribution of $ω$ meson in the $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ and $X(3872)\to J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$ processes. It is found that the recent experimental measurements on the $π^+ π^-$ invariant mass distributions can be well reproduced, and the ratio of the couplings between $X(3872) \to J/ψρ^0$ and $X(3872) \to J/ψω$ is also evaluated. Within the parameters extracted from the $π^+ π^-$ invariant mass distributions of the $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-$ process, the branching fractions of the $X(3872) \to J/ψω$ channel relative to that of the $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$ channel and the $π^+ π^- π^0$ invariant mass distributions of the $X(3872) \to J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$ decay are calculated, which gives a hint for the further high-statistic experiment.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the recent LHCb measurements of the π⁺π⁻ invariant mass distributions in X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ can be reproduced by including both ρ⁰ and ω meson contributions, with the ω contribution being crucial at high π⁺π⁻ invariant masses, but the extracted branching fractions and coupling ratios have large uncertainties that require future high-statistics experiments for confirmation.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams where X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ proceeds via intermediate ρ⁰ and ω mesons, and X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻π⁰ proceeds via ω meson with subsequent ω → ρπ → π⁺π⁻π⁰ decay. They performed a four-parameter χ² fit (coupling ratio R_X = g_{X₁}/g_{X₂}, normalization factor Cg_{X₂}², cutoff parameter Λ, and relative phase φ) to the LHCb π⁺π⁻ invariant mass data, considering both energy-dependent and constant ρ meson width cases.

## Key numbers

- Coupling ratio R_X = g_{X₁}/g_{X₂} = 0.25 ± 0.01 (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 0.30 ± 0.01 (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Cutoff parameter Λ = 612 ± 18 MeV (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 598 ± 16 MeV (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Relative phase φ = 128.9 ± 8.0 degrees (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 134.5 ± 7.7 degrees (constant Γ_ρ case)
- χ²/d.o.f. = 0.6 for both fitting cases
- Extracted coupling g_{X₂} = 0.33 ± 0.06 (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 0.31 ± 0.06 (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Extracted coupling g_{X₁} = 0.08 ± 0.02 (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 0.09 ± 0.02 (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Fractional ω contribution to X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻: (4.7 ± 2.4)% (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and (4.0 ± 2.1)% (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Branching fraction B(X(3872) → J/ψω) = (11.5 ± 7.5)% (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and (9.9 ± 6.3)% (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Ratio R = B(X(3872) → J/ψω)/B(X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻) = 3.0 ± 2.2 (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and 2.6 ± 1.9 (constant Γ_ρ case)
- Branching fraction B(X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻π⁰) = (1.1 ± 0.5)% (energy-dependent Γ_ρ case) and (1.4 ± 0.6)% (constant Γ_ρ case)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hanhart et al. (2011) by using the new LHCb measurements (2022) of X(3872) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ to determine the ω meson contribution and the ratio of X(3872) couplings to J/ψρ⁰ and J/ψω. The results are consistent with previous determinations by LHCb (2022) and Hanhart et al. (2011), and the predicted π⁺π⁻π⁰ invariant mass distributions and branching fractions agree with available BESIII (2019) and BABAR (2010) data within uncertainties. The work enables future high-statistics experiments to test the predicted three-pion invariant mass distributions and reduce the uncertainties on the extracted couplings.
