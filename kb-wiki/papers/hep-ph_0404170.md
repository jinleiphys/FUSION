# Anti-charmed pentaquark $Θ_c (3099)$ from QCD sum rules

> We construct QCD sum rules for the anti-charmed pentaquark $Θ_c (3099)$, recently reported at HERA. The sum rules are constructed similarly with the $Θ^+ (1540)$ sum rules using the anti-charmed analogue of the $Θ^+$ interpolating field. The strange quark and quark-gluon mixed condensates, which were important in the $Θ^+$ sum rules, are replaced by the gluon condensates whose contribution to the OPE is suppressed due to the heavy quark mass. Our result suggests that the parity of $Θ_c$ is positive. We identify the difference from the $Θ^+$ sum rule, which leads to the positive parity in this heavy-light pentaquark system. The obtained mass is similar to the experimental value.

# Anti-charmed pentaquark Θ_c (3099) from QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The parity of the anti-charmed pentaquark Θ_c (3099) is positive, opposite to the negative parity previously found for the strange pentaquark Θ⁺(1540) in the same QCD sum rule framework, because the quark-gluon mixed condensate that drove the negative parity in the strange case is replaced by suppressed gluon condensates in the heavy-quark sector.

## Method

The authors construct QCD sum rules for the Θ_c using an interpolating field analogous to the Sugiyama-Doi-Oka (SDO) current for Θ⁺, with two scalar diquarks (one with γ₅, one without) and an anti-charm quark. The correlation function is computed in the operator product expansion (OPE) keeping the charm quark mass finite using momentum-space propagators, while light-quark parts are treated in coordinate space. The chiral-odd part of the sum rule determines the parity through its sign, and the mass is extracted by taking derivatives of the Borel-transformed sum rules with respect to 1/M².

## Key numbers

- The extracted Θ_c mass is around 3.06 GeV at continuum threshold √S₀ = 3.2 GeV, compared to the experimental value of 3.099 GeV.
- Varying the charm quark mass from 1.1 GeV to 1.5 GeV shifts the extracted mass from 3.02 GeV to 3.14 GeV at the stable Borel region.
- At a larger continuum threshold √S₀ = 3.6 GeV, the predicted mass rises to approximately 3.4 GeV.
- The gluon condensate used is ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩ = (0.33 GeV)⁴, and the triple-gluon condensate ⟨G³⟩ = 0.045 GeV⁶.
- The charm quark mass is taken as m_c = 1.26 GeV, with the quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.23 GeV)³.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Sugiyama, Doi, and Oka (2003) for the Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark to its anti-charmed analogue, motivated by the H1 Collaboration's report of Θ_c(3099) at HERA. The positive parity result agrees with the quenched lattice calculation of Chiu and Hsieh (2004) and with the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark-diquark-antiquark model prediction, in contrast to the negative parity found for Θ⁺ in the SDO sum rule. The crossover in parity between the strange and charm sectors demonstrates the suppression of the quark-gluon mixed condensate contribution as the heavy-quark mass increases, enabling a test of the constituent-quark picture in QCD sum rules.
