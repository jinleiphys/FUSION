# The nature and line shapes of charmonium in the $e^+e^- \to D\bar{D}$ reactions

> We explore the nature of sharp resonances with asymmetric line shape observed in cross section data, a general physical phenomenon produced by the interference of continuum background and resonances. A Fano scheme and the coupled-channel T-matrix approach are employed to this aim and their close relationship is present. As a typical example, we point out that the $ψ(3770)$ state observed in the $e^+e^-$ reactions with an anomalous line shape can be explained naturally as a resonance embedded in the $D\bar{D}$ continuum. From a coupled-channels analysis the background of $ψ(3770)$ resonance is found to originate from a pole at $\sqrt{s}=3716.0 \pm$ 30.0~MeV. As a by-product, the broad structure $X(3900)/G(3900)$ seen in the Belle data, is found to be the tail of the $ψ(3770)$ state, distorted by the opening of the $D^*\bar{D} + c.c$ channel and the onset of the $ψ(4040)$ spectral distribution, thus making the assignment as a genuine charmonium state unlikely.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalous line shape of the ψ(3770) resonance observed in e+e− → D\bar{D} reactions can be explained naturally as a Fano-type resonance embedded in the D\bar{D} continuum, where the asymmetric line shape arises from interference between the resonance and a continuum background, rather than requiring a separate exotic state interpretation.

## Method

The authors employ both a Fano formalism and a coupled-channel T-matrix approach to describe charmonium production in e+e− annihilation. The Fano approach separates the reaction amplitude into a production form factor containing a Fano q-parameter that controls the line shape, while the coupled-channel T-matrix analysis treats the c\bar{c} confinement sector and D\bar{D} continuum sector in a unified framework with configuration mixing interactions. The models are fitted to BES collaboration data for e+e− → D0\bar{D}0 and e+e− → D+D− cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The background of the ψ(3770) resonance originates from a pole at √s = 3716.0 ± 30.0 MeV
- Fitted ψ(3770) mass: 3782.1 ± 1.6 MeV (D0\bar{D}0 channel) and 3784.0 ± 2.0 MeV (D+D− channel)
- Fitted coupling constant g_{ψ'D\bar{D}}: 11.8 ± 0.9 (D0\bar{D}0) and 10.7 ± 1.3 (D+D−)
- Fano q-parameter: −2.1 ± 0.3 (D0\bar{D}0) and −1.6 ± 0.3 (D+D−)
- Electronic width Γ_{ψ' e+e−} = 0.265 keV used to determine photo-vector coupling constant g_{ψ'γ} = 56.35
- χ²/d.o.f. for Fano fits: 0.83 (D0\bar{D}0) and 0.90 (D+D−), compared to χ² = 2.72 and 3.27 for simple Breit-Wigner fits

## Context

This work extends the Fano formalism, originally developed for atomic auto-ionizing states (Fano 1961), to charmonium physics, building on the authors' previous work (Cao and Lenske 2014). It connects the Fano approach to standard coupled-channel methods pioneered by Eichten et al. (1980) and more recent studies of charmonium spectroscopy (Barnes 2008). The analysis shows that the broad X(3900)/G(3900) structure seen in Belle data is not a genuine charmonium state but rather the tail of ψ(3770) distorted by the opening of the D*\bar{D} channel and the onset of ψ(4040), enabling future experiments at e+e− facilities or PANDA@FAIR to probe configuration mixing effects through reaction-dependent line shapes.
