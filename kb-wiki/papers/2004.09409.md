# Heavy $Ω_c$ and $Ω_b$ baryons in the quark model

> In this contribution, we present a study of ground- and excited-state $Ω_c$ and $Ω_b$ baryons consisting of two strange quarks and a heavy charm or bottom quark. An analysis in the quark model shows that the recently observed excited $Ω_c$ and $Ω_b$ states can be interpreted in terms of $λ$-mode excitations.

# Heavy Ω_c and Ω_b baryons in the quark model

## Key claim

The five excited Ω_c states observed by LHCb and confirmed by Belle can be interpreted as λ-mode excitations in a harmonic oscillator quark model, but the spin and parity assignments remain model-dependent because neither LHCb nor Belle determined angular momenta and parities experimentally.

## Method

The authors used a harmonic oscillator quark model with spin, spin-orbit, isospin, and flavor-dependent terms, whose parameters were fitted to single-charm baryons (Σ_c, Λ_c, Ξ_c) and single-bottom baryons (Σ_b, Λ_b). They classified ssQ configurations into ground states (n_ρ,n_λ)=(0,0), λ-mode excitations (0,1), and ρ-mode excitations (1,0), then calculated masses from the Hamiltonian and strong decay widths using a ³P₀ decay model with the strength parameter fitted to the Ω_c(3065) decay.

## Key numbers

- Ω_c ground state mass: 2702 ± 12 MeV (theoretical) vs 2695.2 ± 1.7 MeV (experimental)
- Ω_c(3000) assigned as ²λ(Ω_c)_{1/2} with theoretical mass 3016 ± 9 MeV and decay width 0.48 MeV; experimental mass 3000.4 ± 0.2 MeV with total width 4.5 ± 0.6 ± 0.3 MeV
- Ω_c(3065) assigned as ²λ(Ω_c)_{3/2} with theoretical mass 3052 ± 15 MeV and decay width 3.5 MeV (fitted); experimental mass 3065.5 ± 0.3 MeV with total width 3.5 ± 0.4 ± 0.2 MeV
- Ω_c(3119) suggested as a molecular state, not a quark-model state; experimental mass 3119.1 ± 1.0 MeV with width < 2.6 MeV
- Ω_b ground state mass: 6061 ± 15 MeV (theoretical) vs 6046.1 ± 0.7 MeV (experimental)
- Ω_b(6316) assigned as ²λ(Ω_b)_{1/2} with theoretical mass 6305 ± 15 MeV and decay width 0.50 MeV; experimental mass 6315.6 ± 0.6 MeV with width < 2.8 ± 4.2 MeV
- ρ-mode excitations for both Ω_c and Ω_b have zero decay width into the Ξ_c⁺K⁻ and Ξ_b⁰K⁻ channels, respectively, due to selection rules
- Quark model parameters: m_s = 450 MeV, m_c = 1605 MeV, m_b = 4920 MeV; spin term A = 21.54 ± 0.37 MeV (charm) and 6.73 ± 1.63 MeV (bottom)

## Context

This work extends the quark model analysis of single-charm and single-bottom baryons from the same group (epjc) to the doubly-strange Ω_c and Ω_b systems. It provides a unified interpretation of the five Ω_c states discovered by LHCb (2017) and confirmed by Belle, and predicted five λ-mode Ω_b states before LHCb observed four of them (2019). The authors suggest a molecular interpretation for Ω_c(3119), following proposals by Huang (2018), Nieves (2017), and Debastiani (2017), rather than a quark-model assignment. The work enables future experimental determination of spin-parity quantum numbers and searches for the missing fifth λ-mode Ω_b state and the two ρ-mode states in both sectors.
