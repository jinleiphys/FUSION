# $νd \to μ^- Δ^{++} n$ Reaction and Axial Vector $N-Δ$ Coupling

> The reaction $νd \to μ^- Δ^{++} n$ is studied in the region of low $q^2$ to investigate the effect of deuteron structure and width of the $Δ$ resonance on the differential cross section. The results are used to extract the axial vector $N-Δ$ coupling $C^{A}_5$ from the experimental data on this reaction. The possibility to determine this coupling from electroweak interaction experiments with high intensity electron accelerators is discussed.

# νd → μ⁻ Δ⁺⁺ n Reaction and Axial Vector N-Δ Coupling

## Key claim

The axial vector N-Δ coupling C⁵_A is extracted from BNL data on the ratio of differential cross sections for νd → μ⁻ Δ⁺⁺ n and νd → μ⁻ p p, yielding C⁵_A = 1.22 ± 0.06, but this value depends on properly accounting for deuteron structure effects (which are small, under 10%) and the finite width of the Δ resonance, which the abstract does not specify as crucial for agreement with low-q² data.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for νd → μ⁻ Δ⁺⁺ n in the impulse approximation, using deuteron wave functions from Hulthen, Paris, and Bonn potentials, and compare with the free-nucleon case. They incorporate an energy-dependent P-wave width for the Δ resonance (Γ₀ = 120 MeV) and study the effect of nonzero muon mass at very low Q². The axial vector coupling C⁵_A is extracted by equating the theoretical ratio R(Q²=0) of the inelastic to quasielastic cross sections to the extrapolated experimental value 0.55 ± 0.05 from Kitagaki et al. (1990).

## Key numbers

- C⁵_A = 1.22 ± 0.06, extracted from the BNL data using the extrapolated ratio R(Q²=0) = 0.55 ± 0.05
- Deuteron structure effects on dσ/dq² do not exceed 8% even at Q² < 0.1 GeV², and are less than 7% in the region 0.05 < Q² < 0.10 GeV²
- The nonzero muon mass causes an additional reduction of about 5% in the cross section near Q² ≈ 0.05 GeV²
- The vector form factor C³_V(q²) = 2.05 / (1 − q²/0.54 GeV²)², with C⁵_V = 0 and C⁴_V = −(M/M′) C³_V
- The Δ resonance width is Γ₀ = 120 MeV, with an uncertainty of 10–15 MeV that does not substantially change the cross section
- For the charged current reaction e⁻ + p → Δ⁰ + ν at E_e = 4 GeV, the differential cross section near Q² = 0 is estimated at 2 × 10⁻³⁹ cm²/GeV², yielding about 72 events per hour for Δ⁰ production at an intensity of 2 × 10³⁸ cm⁻²/s with a Q² bin of 0.05 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hemmert, Holstein, and Mukhopadhyay (1995), who extracted C⁵_A from the same BNL and ANL data but neglected deuteron structure and used a zero-width approximation for the Δ. The authors show that including deuteron effects (using wave functions from Hulthen, Paris, and Bonn potentials) and an energy-dependent P-wave width improves agreement with the ANL data of Radecky et al. (1982). The extracted C⁵_A = 1.22 ± 0.06 is consistent with the PCAC prediction of 1.15 ± 0.01 (using g_ΔNπ = 28.6 ± 0.3) but is about 30% larger than most quark model predictions (e.g., 0.83–1.17 from various models). The paper also discusses the feasibility of determining C⁵_A from electron scattering experiments, concluding that charged current electroproduction of Δ is more promising than parity-violating asymmetry measurements, which suffer from the (1 − 4 sin²θ_W) suppression factor.
