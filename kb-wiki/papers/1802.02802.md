# Nuclear physics uncertainties of the astrophysical gamma-process studied through the 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu and 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga reactions

> In a recent work, the cross section measurement of the 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu reaction was used to prove that the standard alpha-nucleus optical potentials used in astrophysical network calculation fail to reproduce the experimental data at energies relevant for heavy element nucleosynthesis. In the present paper the analysis of the obtained experimental data is continued by comparing the results with the predictions using different parameters. It is shown that the recently suggested modification of the standard optical potential leads to a better description of the data.

# Nuclear physics uncertainties of the astrophysical gamma-process studied through the 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu and 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga reactions

## Key claim

The standard McFadden-Satchler alpha-nucleus optical potential, which was developed based on 25 MeV experimental data and has no energy dependence, strongly overestimates measured 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu cross sections at the lowest studied energies; however, an energy-dependent modification of the imaginary part of the potential using a Fermi-type function with a diffuseness parameter a_E between 1.5 and 5 MeV leads to a much better reproduction of the experimental data, with best agreement at a_E = 2.5 MeV.

## Method

The cross sections of 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu and 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga were measured using the activation method at the cyclotron accelerator of Atomki, where 64Zn targets were bombarded by a proton beam in the energy interval between 3.5 and 8 MeV. The number of reactions was determined by measuring the gamma-radiation following the decay of 61Cu and 65Ga using a HPGe detector. The measured cross sections were compared with statistical model calculations using the SMARAGD code (successor of NON-SMOKER) and the TALYS code (version 1.4), testing different alpha-nucleus optical potentials and, for the (p,gamma) channel, different combinations of level density and gamma-ray strength function options.

## Key numbers

- The standard SMARAGD calculation using the McFadden-Satchler potential overestimates the measured 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu cross section at the lowest studied energies, while agreement becomes fairly good as energy increases toward 8 MeV.
- For the 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga reaction, the standard SMARAGD calculation overestimates the measured data by about a factor of two but describes the energy dependence relatively well.
- TALYS calculations with different combinations of level density and gamma-strength function options produce cross sections that differ by up to a factor of 50 for the 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga reaction.
- The best TALYS result for 64Zn(p,gamma)65Ga is obtained using the microscopic level density of Goriely et al. and the gamma-strength function of Kopecky and Uhl (generalized Lorentzian form for the dominant E1 transition).
- For the modified alpha-nucleus optical potential, the best agreement with the 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu data is achieved with the diffuseness parameter a_E = 2.5 MeV, tested in the range between 1.5 and 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Gyürky et al. (2014) which first proved at astrophysical energies that the standard alpha-nucleus optical potential fails to reproduce measured cross sections. The results support the energy-dependent modification of the McFadden-Satchler potential recommended by Sauerwein et al. (2011), which has been tested in several recent experiments (e.g., Yalcin et al., 2015). The findings address uncertainties in gamma-process network calculations for heavy proton-rich isotope synthesis (Rauscher, 2013) and demonstrate that only experimental data can resolve the large divergences (up to a factor of 50) in calculated (p,gamma) cross sections arising from different choices of level density and gamma-strength function parameters.
