# Charmonium resonances and Fano line shapes

> Anomalous line shapes of quarkonia are explained naturally as an interference effect of a $c\bar c$ confined closed channel with the surrounding continua, well established in other fields of physics as Fano-resonances. We discuss a quark model coupled-channel analysis describing quarkonium as a mixing of closed $Q\bar Q$ and molecular-like $D\bar D$ open channels. The asymmetric line shapes observed in $ψ(3770)$ production cross sections in $e^+e^-$ annihilation to $D^0\bar{D}^0$ and $D^+ D^-$, respectively, are described very well. The method allows to extract directly from the data the amount of $Q\bar Q \leftrightarrow D\bar D$ configuration mixing.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that anomalous line shapes of charmonium states, such as the \(\psi(3770)\), can be explained as Fano resonances arising from the quantum interference between a confined \(c\bar c\) closed channel and surrounding \(D\bar D\) open channel continua, with the specific line shape depending on the production reaction and the Fano parameter \(q_{\alpha\beta}\).

## Method

The authors perform a coupled-channel analysis using the Cornell quark model to describe bare \(c\bar c\) states, coupled via the \(^3P_0\) model to molecular-like \(D\bar D\) open channels. The system is reduced to a solvable problem in three-dimensional momentum space, where the configuration mixing induces a dynamical self-energy for the quarkonium states, leading to a mass shift and width. The production cross section for \(e^+e^- \to D\bar D\) is then expressed in terms of a Fano line shape formula, with the Fano parameter \(q_{\alpha\beta}\) extracted from fits to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The bare \(1^3D_1\) charmonium state (the \(\psi(3770)\) precursor) has a calculated mass of 3.819 GeV and a leptonic width of \(7.917 \times 10^{-3}\) keV, compared to the experimental mass of 3.770 GeV and width of \(2.62 \pm 0.018\) keV.
- The Fano parameter extracted from fits to data is \(q_0 = -0.4\) for the \(e^+e^- \to D^0\bar{D}^0\) reaction and \(q_0 = -0.3\) for the \(e^+e^- \to D^+ D^-\) reaction.
- The Cornell model parameters used are \(\alpha_S = 0.55\), \(\sigma = 0.148\) GeV\(^2\) for the linear confinement potential (with \(\sigma = 0.088\) GeV\(^2\) for spin-singlet states), and a shift constant \(C = 0.014\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of bound states in the continuum (BIC), originally established in atomic physics by Fano (1961) and von Neumann and Wigner (1929), to charmonium physics. It provides a natural explanation for the asymmetric line shapes of \(\psi(3770)\) observed in experiments by BES, Belle, and CLEO, which had previously been analyzed by various theoretical approaches such as Achasov (2012) and Chen (2013). The method enables the direct extraction of \(Q\bar Q \leftrightarrow D\bar D\) configuration mixing from data and is applicable to other hadronic states, particularly the poorly understood XYZ states.
