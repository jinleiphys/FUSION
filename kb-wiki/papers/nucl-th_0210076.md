# Landau-Zener effect in superfluid nuclear systems

> The Landau--Zener effect is generalized for many-body systems with pairing residual interactions. The microscopic equations of motion are obtained and the $^{14}$C decay of $^{223}$Ra spectroscopic factors are deduced. An asymmetric nuclear shape parametrization given by two intersected spheres is used. The single particle level scheme is determined in the frame of the superasymmetric two-center shell. The deformation energy is computed in the microscopic-macroscopic approximation. The penetrabilities are obtained within the WKB approximation. The fine structure of the cluster decay analyzed in the frame of this formalism gives a very good agreement with the experimental ratio of partial half-lives obtained in special conditions

## Key claim

The Landau-Zener effect is generalized for many-body systems with pairing residual interactions, and the resulting formalism reproduces the experimental ratio of partial half-lives for the fine structure of the \(^{14}\)C decay of \(^{223}\)Ra, but only when the internuclear distance velocity is treated as a fitting parameter in the range \(v \approx 9\times 10^{4} - 3\times 10^{5}\) (units unspecified), which is about one order of magnitude lower than macroscopic model predictions.

## Method

The author derives coupled differential equations of motion from a variational principle using a Lagrangian that includes a time-dependent many-body Hamiltonian with pairing residual interactions and an additional interaction term \(H'\) responsible for avoided level crossings. The many-body state is expanded as a superposition of time-dependent BCS seniority-one diabatic wave functions, and the system is solved for the \(^{14}\)C decay of \(^{223}\)Ra using a nuclear shape parametrization of two intersected spheres, single-particle levels from the superasymmetric two-center shell model, deformation energy from the microscopic-macroscopic approximation (Yukawa-plus-exponential model with Strutinsky shell corrections), and WKB penetrabilities.

## Key numbers

* The experimental ratio \(R\) of partial half-lives for transitions to the first excited state and ground state in the \(^{14}\)C decay of \(^{223}\)Ra is approximately 0.218.
* The WKB action integrals \(K_m\) are \(2.10 \times 10^{27}\), \(1.27 \times 10^{29}\), and \(1.87 \times 10^{31}\) for transitions to the daughter ground state, first excited state, and second excited state, respectively.
* The internuclear distance velocity \(v\) that reproduces the experimental ratio \(R\) lies in the range \(v \approx 9\times 10^{4} - 3\times 10^{5}\) (units not specified in the text), approximately one order of magnitude lower than values from macroscopic models.
* Four avoided level crossings are identified at normalized elongations \((R-R_i)/(R_f-R_i) \approx 0.75, 1, 1.1,\) and \(1.2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach (Mirea, 2001-2002) that used the Landau-Zener promotion mechanism alone to describe fine structure in cluster and alpha decays, which could not describe fine structure in even-even nuclei. The inclusion of pairing residual interactions via time-dependent BCS equations generalizes earlier dissipation studies by Koonin (1976) that lacked the Landau-Zener term and were microscopically reversible. The model enables a description of fine structure in superasymmetric fission for both odd and even nuclei and is claimed to be the unique model explaining favored transitions to excited states in cluster decay.
