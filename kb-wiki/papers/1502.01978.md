# Estimation of the shear viscosity at finite net-baryon density from A+A collision data at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.7-200$ GeV

> Hybrid approaches based on relativistic hydrodynamics and transport theory have been successfully applied for many years for the dynamical description of heavy ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies. In this work a new viscous hybrid model employing the hadron transport approach UrQMD for the early and late non-equilibrium stages of the reaction, and 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics for the hot and dense quark-gluon plasma stage is introduced. This approach includes the equation of motion for finite baryon number, and employs an equation of state with finite net-baryon density to allow for calculations in a large range of beam energies. The parameter space of the model is explored, and constrained by comparison with the experimental data for bulk observables from SPS and the phase I beam energy scan at RHIC. The favored parameter values depend on energy, but allow to extract the effective value of the shear viscosity coefficient over entropy density ratio $η/s$ in the fluid phase for the whole energy region under investigation. The estimated value of $η/s$ increases with decreasing collision energy, which may indicate that $η/s$ of the quark-gluon plasma depends on baryochemical potential $μ_B$.

## Key claim
The effective shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (η/s) of the quark-gluon plasma increases with decreasing collision energy in the range √sNN = 7.7–200 GeV, which may indicate a dependence of η/s on baryochemical potential μB, but the extracted values are conditional on the specific choice of model parameters (Gaussian smearing radii, starting time, switching density) which are adjusted manually per energy.

## Method
A hybrid model was constructed combining the UrQMD hadron transport approach for the pre-equilibrium and post-hydrodynamic stages with a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code (vHLLE) for the quark-gluon plasma stage, including finite net-baryon density and using the chiral model equation of state. Event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions were generated by converting UrQMD particles at a constant proper time τ0 into hydrodynamic cells via Gaussian smearing with radii R⊥ and Rη, and the system was particlized at a constant switching energy density εsw = 0.5 GeV/fm³ using the Cooper-Frye prescription with Grad's 14-moment viscous correction. The model parameters (R⊥, Rη, τ0, η/s) were manually tuned per collision energy to reproduce experimental data on transverse momentum spectra, rapidity distributions, and elliptic flow from the NA49, PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations.

## Key numbers
- η/s = 0.2 gives a good estimate of rapidity and transverse momentum distributions at √sNN = 8.8 GeV (Elab = 40 A GeV) but overestimates dN/dη at midrapidity for other energies except √sNN = 200 GeV, where it underestimates PHOBOS results.
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, the optimal η/s required to fit elliptic flow data is η/s = 0.08, assuming Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions (Song 2010).
- The initial time τ0 is calculated as τ0 = 2R / √((√sNN / 2mN)² – 1), yielding values such as τ0 = 1.22 fm/c at √sNN = 19.6 GeV; for √sNN ≥ 27 GeV, τ0 is set to 1 fm/c.
- The switching energy density is εsw = 0.5 GeV/fm³, corresponding to T ≈ 175 MeV at μB = 0 in the chiral model EoS, which drops to T ≈ 165 MeV in the free hadron resonance gas EoS.
- The Gaussian smearing radii default values are R⊥ = Rη = 1.0 fm; varying these by 10% changes observables by less than 10%.

## Context
This work extends hybrid hydrodynamic+transport approaches previously applied at top RHIC and LHC energies (e.g., Song 2010, Gale 2012) to the lower collision energies of the RHIC beam energy scan (7.7–200 GeV) and SPS, where boost invariance and zero net-baryon density approximations break down. It contrasts with the standard UrQMD hybrid model (Petersen 2008, Auvinen 2013) by including shear viscosity in the hydrodynamic stage and event-by-event fluctuations. The extracted η/s values as a function of beam energy enable future studies of the temperature and baryochemical potential dependence of the QGP shear viscosity, and provide constraints for theoretical calculations of transport coefficients at finite μB.
