# Gluonia, Scalar and Hybrid Mesons in QCD

> For some experimental guidelines of the next millenium, I review the determinations of the masses, decays and mixings of the gluonia, scalar and hybrid mesons from QCD spectral sum rules and low-energy theorems, and compare them with the lattice.

## Key claim

This review argues that QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) and low-energy theorems (LET) can predict the masses, decays, and mixings of gluonia, scalar mesons, and hybrid mesons, and that these predictions agree well with lattice results in the quenched approximation, but that the simple quarkonium picture fails for the scalar sector without large mixings with gluonia.

## Method

The author uses QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) à la SVZ, analyzing two-point correlators built from hadronic currents with specific quantum numbers, employing exponential Laplace sum rules (unsubtracted and subtracted) and their ratios. The spectral function is parametrized by a "naïve" duality ansatz (a single resonance plus a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c), and the operator product expansion (OPE) is used to compute the QCD side including condensates up to dimension 6, with an additional tachyonic gluon mass term to mimic higher-order effects. Low-energy theorems based on Ward identities are also used to estimate couplings to meson pairs.

## Key numbers

*   Unmixed scalar gluonium G mass: 1.5 ± 0.2 GeV (from QSSR); lattice gives 1.60 ± 0.16 GeV.
*   Unmixed tensor gluonium T mass: 2.0 ± 0.1 GeV (from QSSR); lattice gives 2.26 ± 0.22 GeV.
*   Unmixed pseudoscalar gluonium P mass: 2.05 ± 0.19 GeV (from QSSR); lattice gives 2.19 ± 0.32 GeV.
*   Pseudoscalar gluonium P radiative decay width to γγ: 1.3 ± 0.1 keV.
*   Tensor gluonium T decay width to ππ+KK+ηη: ≤ 119 ± 36 MeV.
*   Low-mass scalar σ_B mass: ≈ 1 GeV; its decay width to ππ: ≈ 0.8 GeV.
*   Scalar gluonium G decay width to ηη': 5–10 MeV.
*   Scalar gluonium G decay width to σ_B σ_B → 4π: 60–140 MeV.
*   Light hybrid meson (1⁻⁺) mass M_ρ̃: ≈ 1.6 GeV; its decay width to ρπ: ≈ 274 MeV.
*   Heavy hybrid meson masses: M_ψ̃(cc̄g) ≈ 4.1 GeV; M_Υ̃(bb̄g) ≈ 10.6 GeV; M_B̃*(būg) ≈ 6.3 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of using QCD spectral sum rules (pioneered by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979)) to predict exotic hadron properties, and compares these predictions with lattice results from Teper and others (1990s). The work enables experimental searches for gluonia and hybrids at facilities like BES, and highlights the need for unquenched lattice calculations of mixing angles and widths to resolve the complex scalar meson spectrum, particularly the nature of states like the f₀(980), f₀(1370), f₀(1500), and f₀(1710).
