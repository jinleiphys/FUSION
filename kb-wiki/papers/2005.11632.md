# Nonlinear Constraints on Relativistic Fluids Far From Equilibrium

> New constraints are found that must necessarily hold for Israel-Stewart-like theories of fluid dynamics to be causal far away from equilibrium. Conditions that are sufficient to ensure causality, local existence, and uniqueness of solutions in these theories are also presented. Our results hold in the full nonlinear regime, taking into account bulk and shear viscosities (at zero chemical potential), without any simplifying symmetry or near-equilibrium assumptions. Our findings provide fundamental constraints on the magnitude of viscous corrections in fluid dynamics far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for Israel-Stewart-like theories of relativistic fluid dynamics, causality and well-posedness in the full nonlinear regime (with both shear and bulk viscosity, zero chemical potential, no symmetry assumptions) require specific algebraic inequalities involving the dissipative currents themselves, conditions that cannot be obtained from linearized analyses around equilibrium.

## Method

The authors write the full nonlinear system of conservation equations plus relaxation equations for the shear stress tensor and bulk scalar (the DNMR formulation) as a quasilinear system of 22 PDEs. They compute the characteristic determinant to find necessary conditions for causality (roots must be real and characteristic surfaces must be timelike or lightlike) and then derive sufficient conditions that also guarantee local existence and uniqueness of solutions via standard hyperbolic theory.

## Key numbers

- For a conformal fluid with \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) and \(\tau_\pi T = 5\eta/s\), the necessary conditions imply \(-4/5 < \Lambda_a/(\varepsilon+P) \leq 3/5\), where \(\Lambda_a\) are eigenvalues of the shear stress tensor.
- The linearized limit of the sufficient conditions reduces to \(\varepsilon+P > 0\), \(\varepsilon+P - \eta/\tau_\pi \ge 0\), and \(\frac{1}{\varepsilon+P}\left(\frac{4\eta}{3\tau_\pi} + \frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi}\right) \le 1 - c_s^2\), matching the known linearized causality/stability results from Hiscock and Lindblom (1983) and Olson (1989).
- The necessary condition for causality includes \((2\eta + \lambda_{\pi\Pi}\Pi) - \frac{1}{2}\tau_{\pi\pi}|\Lambda_1| \ge 0\) and \(\varepsilon+P+\Pi - \frac{1}{2\tau_\pi}(2\eta + \lambda_{\pi\Pi}\Pi) - \frac{\tau_{\pi\pi}}{4\tau_\pi}\Lambda_3 \ge 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis for bulk viscosity only (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, 2019) to include shear viscosity, which is essential for heavy-ion collision applications. It contradicts the common misconception that linearized analyses by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983) and Olson (1989) already established causality for Israel-Stewart theories, those works only considered linear disturbances around equilibrium where dissipative currents vanish. The results enable checking causality in numerical simulations of quark-gluon plasma (e.g., in heavy-ion codes using the DNMR formalism) and provide constraints on initial conditions and the magnitude of viscous corrections far from equilibrium, relevant for hydrodynamic attractor studies and small-system collisions.
