# Hyperons and $Θ_s^+$ in Holographic QCD

> We revisit the holographic description of strange baryons in the context of the Sakai-Sugimoto construction, by considering the strange quark mass as heavy. Hyperons are described by a massive $(K,K^*)$ multiplet, bound to a light-flavor instanton in bulk, much in the spirit of the Callan-Klebanov construction. The modular Hamiltonian maps onto the Landau problem, a charged particle in a 2-dimensional external magnetic field, induced by the bulk Chern-Simons interaction, plus spin-orbit coupling. The ensuing holographic hyperon spectrum compares fairly with the empirical one. The holographic strange pentaquark baryon $Θ^+_s$ is shown to be unbound.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hyperons can be described holographically as bound states of a massive kaon multiplet to a light-flavor instanton in bulk, with the strange quark mass treated as heavy rather than perturbatively. The holographic strange pentaquark baryon $\Theta_s^+$ is shown to be unbound, lying above the $nK^+$ threshold.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD construction, treating the strange quark as heavy and identifying the $(K,K^*)$ kaon multiplet as a heavy-light flavor field in bulk. The modular Lagrangian for kaons bound to a light-flavor instanton is derived and diagonalized via a Bogoliubov transformation that maps the problem onto the Landau problem of a charged particle in a 2-dimensional external magnetic field, plus spin-orbit coupling. The Gauss constraint contribution is evaluated in both the light and heavy kaon mass limits, and the Coulomb back-reaction is estimated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- $M_{\Lambda^0} - M_{N} = 0.237 M_{\rm KK}$ (with $M_{\rm KK}=475$ MeV, this gives about 112.7 MeV splitting, compared to empirical 177 MeV)
- $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{1}{2}^+)) - M_N = 302$ MeV (empirical: 254 MeV) for the small mass approximation with $\delta=1$
- $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{3}{2}^+)) - M_N = 501$ MeV (empirical: 444 MeV) for the small mass approximation
- Splitting $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{3}{2}^+)) - M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{1}{2}^+)) = 199$ MeV (empirical: 191 MeV)
- For the large mass approximation without Coulomb: $M(\Lambda) - M_N = 68.1$ MeV, $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{1}{2}^+)) - M_N = 289$ MeV, $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{3}{2}^+)) - M_N = 400$ MeV
- For the large mass approximation with Coulomb: $M(\Lambda) - M_N = 214$ MeV, $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{1}{2}^+)) - M_N = 368$ MeV, $M(\Sigma_{s}(1\frac{3}{2}^+)) - M_N = 460$ MeV
- $\Theta_s^+$ mass estimated at about 1600 MeV, above the $nK^+$ threshold of 1434 MeV
- $M_{\rm KK} = 475$ MeV fixed by the light baryon spectrum

## Context

This work extends the holographic description of strange baryons beyond the perturbative treatment of the strange quark mass in Liu and Zahed (2017), where the analysis was carried only to order $m_H^0$ without spin effects. The construction is the holographic dual of the Callan-Klebanov bound state approach for hyperons as kaon-Skyrmions (Callan 1985, Callan 1987), and enables the description of multiquark exotics with strangeness. The results show that the contentious $\Theta_s^+$ pentaquark state proposed by Jaffe (1976), Praszalowicz (1987), and Diakonov (1997) is unbound, consistent with the lack of experimental confirmation.
