# Feasibility of the finite amplitude method in covariant density functional theory

> Self-consistent relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) in the radial coordinate representation is established by using the finite amplitude method (FAM). Taking the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in spherical nuclei as example, the feasibility of the FAM for the covariant density functionals is demonstrated, and the newly developed methods are verified by the conventional RPA calculations. In the present relativistic RPA calculations, the effects of the Dirac sea can be automatically taken into account in the coordinate-space representation. The rearrangement terms due to the density-dependent couplings can be implicitly calculated without extra computational costs in both iterative and matrix FAM schemes.

## Key claim

The finite amplitude method (FAM) can be successfully implemented in covariant density functional theory for self-consistent relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations, with the Dirac sea effects automatically included via the coordinate-space representation and rearrangement terms from density-dependent couplings handled implicitly without extra computational cost in both iterative and matrix FAM schemes.

## Method

The authors implemented both iterative FAM (i-FAM) and matrix FAM (m-FAM) within the spherical density-dependent point-coupling relativistic mean-field theory using the DD-PC1 parametrization. The radial Dirac equation was solved in coordinate space by the fourth-order Runge-Kutta method within a spherical box, and the induced fields were calculated using finite differences with a small parameter eta. The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in spherical nuclei was used as a benchmark test, comparing FAM results against conventional RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- For J^pi = 0^+ excitations in ^208Pb, the number of particle-hole configurations N_ph was 1355 with a box radius R = 25 fm, of which 524 configurations involved particle states in the Dirac sea
- The mesh size was fixed at dr = 0.1 fm
- The single-particle energy truncation for constructing RPA matrices was [-M, M + 200 MeV]
- The parameter eta for m-FAM was taken as eta = 10^{-2}
- The Lorentzian smearing parameter for i-FAM was Gamma = 1 MeV
- The convergent criterion for i-FAM was ||A x - b||^2 / ||b||^2 < 10^{-6}, providing relative accuracy of 10^{-3} for transition strengths

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method, previously demonstrated in non-relativistic frameworks with Skyrme energy density functionals by Nakatsukasa (2007) and Inakura (2009, 2011), to covariant density functional theory. It addresses the computational challenge faced by conventional matrix-form RPA calculations in deformed nuclei, where the number of particle-hole configurations becomes huge. The method enables future self-consistent deformed relativistic RPA calculations without the need for explicit evaluation of complicated residual interaction terms, particularly the rearrangement terms that were previously derived explicitly by Niksic (2002).
