# Thermodynamical Relations in Function Space

> We formulate thermodynamical relations based on the field degrees of freedom by introducing a work induced by the volume change in the function space, in addition to the usual work associated with the spatial volume change. The first and second laws of thermodynamics are defined for such a work inherent to the field theory by generalizing stochastic energetics (stochastic thermodynamics) to the classical real scalar field in contact with a heat bath. We further discuss the local equilibrium ansatz in the function space and show that it is possible to introduce a model of functional ideal hydrodynamics, which is consistent with the derived thermodynamical laws.

## Key claim

The paper formulates first and second laws of thermodynamics for a classical real scalar field by introducing a work term associated with volume change in the function space (the space of field configurations), in addition to the usual work from spatial volume change. The authors show that this framework is consistent with a model of functional ideal hydrodynamics, but only under the assumption that the functional phase space distribution is approximately given by the equilibrium distribution in the quasi-static limit.

## Method

The authors generalize stochastic energetics (stochastic thermodynamics) to a 1+1 dimensional classical real scalar field coupled to a heat bath via a Langevin-type stochastic differential equation with thermal relaxation and noise. They derive a functional Fokker-Planck (Kramers) equation for the phase space distribution of the field configuration, then compute the expectation values of heat and work to obtain the first law. The second law is derived using Shannon information entropy and shown to be non-negative, with equality when the distribution is the canonical equilibrium distribution. A functional ideal hydrodynamics model is then constructed from a variational principle and shown to be consistent with the derived thermodynamical relations.

## Key numbers

- The heat bath temperature is denoted by \(T\), with \(\beta = (k_B T)^{-1}\).
- The relaxation coefficient \(\gamma\) characterizes the inverse of the relaxation time.
- The spatial discretization interval is \(\Delta x\) and the time interval is \(\ud t\); the continuum limit \(\Delta x, \ud t \rightarrow 0\) is taken at the final stage.
- The stationary equilibrium distribution is \(f_{*} (\{ \Pi, \phi\},a,b) = \frac{1}{Z(a,b)}e^{-\beta H(\{\Pi,\phi\},a,b)}\) with partition function \(Z(a,b) = \int [\ud \Pi] \int [\ud \phi] \, e^{-\beta H(\{\Pi,\phi\},a,b)}\).
- The thermodynamical potential is \(\Omega (T, a_t,b_t) = - \beta^{-1} \ln Z(a_t,b_t)\).
- The pressure associated with spatial volume change is \(P = - \frac{\partial \Omega(T,a_{t},0)}{\partial a_t} \frac{1}{(\ud V/\ud a_t)} = - \frac{\Omega(T,V(a_{t}),0)}{V(a_{t})}\).
- The pressure associated with functional volume change is \(P_F = - \frac{\partial \Omega(T,0,b_{t})}{\partial b_t} \frac{1}{(\ud V_F/\ud b_t)} = - \frac{\Omega(T,0,V_F (b_{t}))}{V_F(b_{t})}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of stochastic thermodynamics (Sekimoto 1998, Seifert 2012) from particle systems to field-theoretical systems, building on earlier proposals for functional hydrodynamics (Koide and Kodama, references cited as fhydro, svm-field, jackiw). The derived thermodynamical relations in function space enable the formulation of a functional ideal hydrodynamics that can incorporate quantum-field theoretical fluctuations into hydrodynamic evolution, with potential applications to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the collective behavior of photons. The authors note that the functional pressure and volume may relate to medium effects such as hadron mass shifts (Tsushima, Gubler) and to the Jarzynski relation in quantum field theory (Deffner 2016).
