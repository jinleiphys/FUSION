# Isospin breaking and $f_0(980)$-$a_0(980)$ mixing in the $η(1405) \to π^{0} f_0(980)$ reaction

> We make a theoretical study of the $η(1405) \to π^{0} f_0(980)$ and $η(1405) \to π^{0} a_0(980)$ reactions with an aim to determine the isospin violation and the mixing of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances. We make use of the chiral unitary approach where these two resonances appear as composite states of two mesons, dynamically generated by the meson-meson interaction provided by chiral Lagrangians. We obtain a very narrow shape for the $f_0(980)$ production in agreement with a BES experiment. As to the amount of isospin violation, or $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ mixing, assuming constant vertices for the primary $η(1405)\rightarrow π^{0}K\bar{K}$ and $η(1405)\rightarrow π^{0}π^{0}η$ production, we find results which are much smaller than found in the recent experimental BES paper, but consistent with results found in two other related BES experiments. We have tried to understand this anomaly by assuming an I=1 mixture in the $η(1405)$ wave function, but this leads to a much bigger width of the $f_0(980)$ mass distribution than observed experimentally. The problem is solved by using the primary production driven by $η' \to K^* \bar K$ followed by $K^* \to K π$, which induces an extra singularity in the loop functions needed to produce the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances. Improving upon earlier work along the same lines, and using the chiral unitary approach, we can now predict absolute values for the ratio $Γ(π^0, π^+ π^-)/Γ(π^0, π^0 η)$ which are in fair agreement with experiment. We also show that the same results hold if we had the $η(1475)$ resonance or a mixture of these two states, as seems to be the case in the BES experiment.

## Key claim

The authors find that the isospin-violating ratio of partial decay widths for η(1405) → π⁰ f₀(980) to η(1405) → π⁰ a₀(980) is much smaller than the 18% reported in the BES experiment when assuming a local (contact-like) primary production vertex, but can be brought into fair agreement with experiment by instead using a triangular mechanism driven by η′ → K* K̅ followed by K* → K π, which introduces an extra singularity in the loop functions.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral unitary approach, in which the f₀(980) and a₀(980) resonances appear as dynamically generated composite states from meson-meson interactions described by chiral Lagrangians. They compute the decay amplitudes for η(1405) → π⁰ π⁺ π⁻ and η(1405) → π⁰ π⁰ η using the Bethe-Salpeter equation in five coupled channels (K⁺K⁻, K⁰K̅⁰, π⁰η, π⁺π⁻, π⁰π⁰) with a cutoff regularization of q_max = 900 MeV. Two production mechanisms are compared: a local vertex for η(1405) → π⁰ K K̅ and a triangular loop involving η(1405) → K* K̅ followed by K* → K π, with the K K̅ pair then rescattering to form the f₀(980) or a₀(980).

## Key numbers

- The ratio Γ(π⁰, π⁺π⁻)/Γ(π⁰, π⁰η) obtained with the local vertex assumption is 0.015 (1.5%), far below the BES experimental value of 18%.
- Varying the SU(3) mixing parameter R between -1 and 1.2 gives a range for this ratio of [0.01 – 0.04] (1% to 4%).
- The width of the narrow peak in the π⁺π⁻ mass distribution from the local vertex is about 10 MeV, in agreement with the experimentally observed 9 MeV.
- The ratio of strengths at the peak for π⁺π⁻ (f₀) to π⁰η (a₀) production with the local vertex is of the order of 3%.
- The cutoff used to regularize the loop functions is q_max = 900 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to reactions such as J/ψ → φ π⁰ η and χc₁ → π⁰ ππ (BES experiments) to the η(1405) decay channel. It contradicts the interpretation of an unusually large isospin violation reported by BES for η(1405) → π⁰ f₀(980) (BES, 2012), showing that the large ratio cannot be explained by standard contact-like production or by an I=1 admixture in the η(1405) wave function. The triangular mechanism resolves the discrepancy and enables absolute predictions for the ratio of decay widths, improving upon earlier work by Wu and Zou (2012) which relied on an unknown cutoff. The results support the picture that f₀(980) and a₀(980) are predominantly K K̅ molecular states.
