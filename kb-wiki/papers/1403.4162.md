# Heavy dense QCD and nuclear matter from an effective lattice theory

> A three-dimensional effective lattice theory of Polyakov loops is derived from QCD by expansions in the fundamental character of the gauge action, u, and the hopping parameter, κ, whose action is correct to κ^n u^m with n+m=4. At finite baryon density, the effective theory has a sign problem which meets all criteria to be simulated by complex Langevin as well as by Monte Carlo on small volumes. The theory is valid for the thermodynamics of heavy quarks, where its predictions agree with simulations of full QCD at zero and imaginary chemical potential. In its region of convergence, it is moreover amenable to perturbative calculations in the small effective couplings. In this work we study the challenging cold and dense regime. We find unambiguous evidence for the nuclear liquid gas transition once the baryon chemical potential approaches the baryon mass, and calculate the nuclear equation of state. In particular, we find a negative binding energy per nucleon causing the condensation, whose absolute value decreases exponentially as mesons get heavier. For decreasing meson mass, we observe a first order liquid gas transition with an endpoint at some finite temperature, as well as gap between the onset of isospin and baryon condensation.

## Key claim
The authors derive a three-dimensional effective lattice theory of Polyakov loops from QCD via combined strong-coupling and hopping expansions, and find that in the cold and dense regime the nuclear liquid-gas transition occurs once the baryon chemical potential approaches the baryon mass, with a negative binding energy per nucleon whose absolute value decreases exponentially as mesons become heavier.

## Method
The effective action is obtained by expanding the QCD path integral in the fundamental character of the gauge action (u) and the hopping parameter (κ), keeping terms to order κ^n u^m with n+m=4. The spatial link variables are integrated out, leaving a three-dimensional spin model of Polyakov loops that includes static and kinetic quark determinants. The resulting effective theory is simulated using complex Langevin dynamics and, on small volumes, standard Monte Carlo, with the gauge coupling contributions neglected in the cold regime where Nτ ≥ 100 makes the gauge coupling λ ≤ 10^{-25}.

## Key numbers
- The effective action is correct to order κ^n u^m with n+m=4.
- For temperatures T ≤ 10 MeV, lattice parameters β ≤ 6 and Nτ ≥ 100 give λ ≤ 10^{-25}, so gauge interactions are safely neglected.
- The baryon mass to leading order is am_B = -3 ln(2κ) - 18κ^2 u/(1-u) + ...; the meson mass is am_M = -2 ln(2κ) - 6κ^2 - 24κ^2 u/(1-u) + ...
- In the static strong coupling limit at zero temperature, the baryon number density jumps from 0 to saturation density 2N_c = 6 per lattice site when μ exceeds the constituent quark mass m.
- The binding energy per nucleon is negative and its absolute value decreases exponentially as mesons get heavier.

## Context
This work extends the effective lattice theory approach previously developed for SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills (Langelage 2010) and for the deconfinement transition at imaginary chemical potential (Fromm 2011, Pinke 2012). It enables the study of cold and dense QCD matter that is inaccessible to standard lattice QCD due to the sign problem. The results provide a nuclear equation of state for heavy quarks and show a first-order liquid-gas transition with an endpoint at finite temperature, as well as a gap between the onset of isospin and baryon condensation, opening the way to controlled simulations of nuclear matter from first principles.
