# Limits on dark matter existence in neutron stars from recent astrophysical observations and mass correlation analysis

> Dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANSs) serve as a specific astrophysical laboratory for probing the features of dark matter (DM) and have emerged as a promising candidate for interpreting recent astrophysical observations (e.g., by NICER and LIGO/Virgo). Accurately constraining the internal DM content of DANSs is therefore of critical importance. In this work, we construct the equations of state (EoS) for DANS matter by employing twelve nuclear matter (NM) models within the covariant density functional (CDF) theory and a self-interacting fermionic model for DM. Using these EoSs as input, we solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to systematically investigate the influence of DM on the global properties of neutron stars (NSs). By incorporating recent observational constraints on NS properties, the maximum DM mass fraction $f_χ^{\mathrm{max}}$ in DANSs is determined for each NM EoS model. Our analysis reveals a strong linear correlation (Pearson coefficient $r=0.98$) between $f_χ^{\mathrm{max}}$ and the maximum mass of a pure NS, $M_{\rm{NS}}^{\mathrm{max}}$, described by $f_χ^{\mathrm{max}} = 0.22 M_{\mathrm{NS}}^{\mathrm{max}} - 0.44$. Leveraging this correlation and the observed NS maximum mass distribution, $P(M_{\text{NS}}^{\max} \mid \text{EM})$, we derive the probability distribution function (PDF) for the maximum DM mass, $P(M_χ^{\max} \mid \text{EM})$, in DANSs. We find that at the 68\% confidence level, $M_χ^{\mathrm{max}}=0.150^{+0.070}_{-0.051}\ M_{\odot}$. This quantitative constraint on the DM mass provides a critical prior for interpreting potential observational signatures of DANSs, such as anomalous tidal deformabilities and distinctive gravitational-wave signals.

## Key claim

The paper claims a strong linear correlation (Pearson coefficient 0.98) between the maximum dark matter mass fraction in dark matter admixed neutron stars and the maximum mass of a pure neutron star, which allows a model-independent constraint on the maximum dark matter mass at 0.150 solar masses at 68% confidence, but this result depends on the specific choice of a fermionic dark matter model with particle mass 1000 MeV and self-interaction strength y=0.1.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for dark matter admixed neutron star matter using twelve covariant density functional nuclear matter models (including DDRHF, DDRMF, and NLRMF approaches) combined with a self-interacting fermionic dark matter model. They solved the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute global properties of both pure neutron stars and dark matter admixed neutron stars, then applied observational constraints from NICER and LIGO/Virgo to determine the maximum allowed dark matter mass fraction for each nuclear matter model. A linear correlation was identified between this maximum dark matter fraction and the pure neutron star maximum mass, which was then used with the observed neutron star maximum mass distribution to derive a probability distribution for the maximum dark matter mass.

## Key numbers

- Maximum dark matter mass fraction correlation: \(f_{\chi}^{\mathrm{max}} = 0.22 M_{\mathrm{NS}}^{\mathrm{max}} - 0.44\) with Pearson coefficient \(r=0.98\)
- At 68% confidence level, maximum dark matter mass \(M_{\chi}^{\mathrm{max}} = 0.150^{+0.070}_{-0.051} M_{\odot}\)
- Pure neutron star maximum masses range from 2.125 \(M_{\odot}\) (TM1e model) to 2.775 \(M_{\odot}\) (NL3 model)
- Maximum dark matter mass fractions under MaxM constraints range from 0.011 (TM1e) to 0.161 (NL3)
- For the PKO3 model, the maximum dark matter fraction under MaxM constraints is 0.117, with dark matter core radius 4.320 km and total radius 11.610 km

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars by Ellis (2018), Das (2020, 2021, 2022), and Rutherford (2023) by providing a less model-dependent constraint on dark matter content through a correlation analysis across twelve nuclear matter models. The derived quantitative limit on dark matter mass enables more precise predictions for observational signatures such as anomalous tidal deformabilities studied by Sun (2024) and Liu (2024), and distinctive gravitational-wave signals investigated by Rafiei (2022) and Hong (2024).
