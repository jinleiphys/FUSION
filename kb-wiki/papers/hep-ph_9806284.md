# Energy Dependence of the Pomeron Spin-Flip

> There is no theoretical reason to think that the spin-flip component of the Pomeron is zero. One can measure the spin-flip part using Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI). Perturbative QCD calculations show that the spin-flip component is sensitive to the smallest quark separation in the proton, while the non-flip part probes the largest separation. According to HERA results on the proton structure function at very low x the energy dependence of the cross-section correlates with the size of the color dipole. Analysing the data from HERA we predict that the ratio of the spin-flip to non-flip amplitude grows with energy as $r(s)\propto (1/x)^{0.1-0.2}$, violating Regge factorisation of the Pomeron.

# Energy Dependence of the Pomeron Spin-Flip

## Key claim
The ratio of spin-flip to non-flip amplitude in Pomeron exchange grows with energy as \(r(s) \propto (1/x)^{0.1-0.2}\), violating Regge factorization of the Pomeron. This prediction depends on the assumption that the spin-flip amplitude probes the smallest quark separation in the proton (the diquark size of 0.2–0.4 fm), while the non-flip part probes the largest separation, and uses HERA data on the proton structure function to extract the effective Pomeron intercept as a function of dipole size.

## Method
The authors analyze HERA data on the proton structure function \(F_2^p(x,Q^2)\) using a color-dipole parametrization, extracting the effective Pomeron intercept \(\Delta(r_T)\) as a function of transverse dipole size via the relation \(\Delta(r_T=1/Q^2) = d/d\ln(1/x) \ln[d/d\ln(Q^2) F_2^p(x,Q^2)]\). They then apply this \(r_T\)-dependent intercept to the spin-flip amplitude, assuming the relevant scale is the diquark size in the proton, and compute the resulting energy dependence of the spin-flip to non-flip ratio \(r(s)\).

## Key numbers
- The effective Pomeron intercept for the spin-flip amplitude ranges within \(\Delta(r_T \approx 0.2-0.3\ \text{fm}) \approx 0.2 - 0.3\), based on HERA data at \(Q^2 > 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The predicted growth of the spin-flip fraction is \(\text{Im}\, r(s) \propto (s/s_0)^{0.1-0.2}\).
- Existing data from the E704 Collaboration at Fermilab at 200 GeV give an upper bound \(\text{Im}\, r < 0.15 \pm 0.2\).
- The planned RHIC energy range covers \(50\ \text{GeV}^2 < s < 250000\ \text{GeV}^2\), over which the value of \(r(s)\) more than doubles.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the proton is \(\mu-1 = 1.79\).
- The CNI polarization peak occurs at \(t_p(s) = 8\pi\sqrt{3}\alpha_{em}/\sigma^{pp}_{tot}\).

## Context
This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of the Pomeron spin-flip by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1990s), which showed that the spin-flip amplitude is nonzero only for asymmetric quark-diquark configurations in the proton and is sensitive to the diquark size. It contradicts the common assumption that the Pomeron has no spin-flip due to quark helicity conservation, and it violates Regge factorization. The prediction enables experimental tests with polarized proton beams at RHIC, specifically in the Coulomb-nuclear interference region via the planned pp2pp experiment, and builds on earlier analyses of polarization in \(\pi^{\pm}p\) elastic scattering by Kroll (1980, 1997) that placed upper bounds on the Pomeron spin-flip below 3%.
