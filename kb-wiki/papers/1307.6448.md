# Variational approach to the inhomogeneous chiral phase in quark matter

> The inhomogeneous chiral phase is discussed in QCD at finite temperature and/or density. We study the phase diagram on the density-temperature plane by taking into account the effect of the current mass by a variational method. It is demonstrated that our framework well describes the inhomogeneous phase over the whole phase region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a variational approach using a sine-Gordon equation for the chiral angle can describe the inhomogeneous chiral phase in quark matter even when a finite current quark mass is included, but the abstract's claim that the framework "well describes the inhomogeneous phase over the whole phase region" glosses over the fact that the method relies on a spatial averaging approximation for the derivative of the chiral angle and that the right phase boundary determination uses a correlation function method strictly valid only in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor, three-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in 1+3 dimensions at finite temperature and chemical potential, assuming no external magnetic field or isospin asymmetry. They propose a variational ansatz where the chiral condensates take the form of a dual chiral density wave (DCDW) with a spatially varying chiral angle, but allow the angle to deviate from the linear form of the original DCDW by deriving its equation of motion from a derivative expansion of the thermodynamic potential, yielding a sine-Gordon equation. The solution is given by the Jacobian amplitude function with a modulus parameter k, and the quark wave function is treated by replacing the derivative of the chiral angle with its spatial average, leading to a variational parameter q related to k.

## Key numbers

- The inhomogeneous phase appears in the moderate density region around chemical potential μ = 300-400 MeV, where the physical pion mass of 138 MeV cannot be neglected.
- For a current quark mass of m_c = 5 MeV, the inhomogeneous phase area decreases monotonously compared to the chiral limit (m_c = 0).
- The modulus parameter k increases monotonously with temperature along the phase boundaries, reaching values near k → 1 (kink-like solution) near the critical point, while in the low temperature region k is small and the deformation from original DCDW is small.
- The wave number q decreases monotonously as temperature increases, and near the critical point becomes smaller than the pion mass, causing large deformation of the condensates.

## Context

This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases in QCD, specifically the DCDW structure proposed by Nakano and Tatsumi (2005) and the real kink crystal (RKC) studied by Nickel (2009), by including the effect of a finite current quark mass which was previously neglected in the chiral limit. The paper shows that while the RKC-type structure changes only quantitatively with finite quark mass, the DCDW-type structure undergoes qualitative changes because the mass term deforms the chiral circle, making points on it inequivalent. The approach enables future studies of the inhomogeneous phase with more realistic conditions relevant to heavy ion collisions and compact star phenomena, and the authors note that the Lifshitz point (triple point) appears near the critical end point, though the exact coincidence predicted by generalized Ginzburg-Landau theory requires further investigation.
