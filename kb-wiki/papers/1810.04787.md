# Blind spots of probing the high-density symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions

> The nuclear symmetry energy, especially at suprasaturation densities, plays crucial roles in many astrophysical studies. However, nowadays the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy is still very controversial in nuclear community. To constrain the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy, neutron-rich nuclei collisions at medium energies are considered to be one of the most effective methods. While probing the high-density symmetry energy by using heavy-ion collisions, blind spots may exist. In the framework of the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, the blind spots of probing the high-density symmetry energy by the n/p ratio in the central Au+Au reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon are demonstrated. It is found that the nucleon observable neutron to proton ratio n/p in heavy-ion collisions cannot effectively probe the high-density symmetry energy when the high-density symmetry energy is less density-dependent.

# Blind spots of probing the high-density symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The neutron-to-proton ratio (n/p) in heavy-ion collisions cannot effectively probe the high-density symmetry energy when the symmetry energy is less density-dependent, because such a symmetry energy does not cause isospin fractionation in dense matter. This blind spot means that standard symmetry-energy-sensitive observables may fail to constrain the high-density symmetry energy if its value changes only weakly with density.

## Method
The study used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central Au+Au reactions at 300 MeV/nucleon. The authors varied the high-density symmetry energy by changing the stiffness parameter x (x = -1, 1, 2) in the single-nucleon mean-field potential, while keeping the low-density symmetry energy fixed with x = 1. They compared the time evolution of the n/p ratio in dense matter and the kinetic-energy-dependent free n/p ratio for each case against a reference simulation without any high-density symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 300 MeV/nucleon for central Au+Au reactions
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(ρ₀) = 30 MeV
- High-density symmetry energy parameter x values tested: -1, 1, 2
- The less density-dependent case (x = 1) produced n/p ratios in dense matter and free n/p ratios that were indistinguishable from the case without high-density symmetry energy

## Context
This work extends the ongoing effort to constrain the high-density symmetry energy, which remains controversial despite studies by Guo (2014) and others. Prior experimental programs at FOPI/GSI, RIBF-RIKEN, and HIRFL-CSR have used pion and nucleon yield ratios and flow measurements to probe the symmetry energy, as reported by Reisdorf (2007) and FOPI collaboration (2016). The paper identifies a fundamental limitation of the standard approach: when the symmetry energy is less density-dependent, the chemical equilibrium condition E_sym(ρ₁)δ₁ = E_sym(ρ₂)δ₂ prevents isospin fractionation, making the observable insensitive. The author suggests that one must first compare experimental free n/p data with simulations using only the constrained low-density symmetry energy to determine whether the high-density symmetry energy is density-dependent before using symmetry-energy-sensitive observables to extract its value.
