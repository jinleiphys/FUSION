# Heavy Ions at LHC: Theoretical Issues

> We give a brief overview of our current theoretical understanding of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision and the properties of super-hot nuclear matter. We focus on several issues that have been discussed in connection with experimental results from the CERN SPS and from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider RHIC. We give an extrapolation of our current knowledge to LHC energies and ask which physics questions can be addressed at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) may already be discovered at RHIC, the LHC will enable systematic study of its properties through plentiful hard probes; however, the extrapolation from RHIC to LHC energies is conditional on unresolved questions about saturation physics, medium-modified fragmentation, and the role of recombination versus perturbative QCD at intermediate transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors review theoretical issues in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions by comparing experimental results from CERN SPS and RHIC with models of QGP signatures, including latent heat, strangeness enhancement, J/Ψ suppression, and jet quenching. They then extrapolate these findings to LHC energies using parton recombination and fragmentation calculations, assuming a thermalized parton phase with specified temperature and radial flow, and compare the predicted transverse momentum spectra and baryon-to-meson ratios at RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The QCD phase transition temperature is \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV (from lattice QCD, Karsch (2001)).
- The critical point on the phase transition line is predicted at \(\mu_B \sim 700\) MeV (from Fodor (2001)).
- At RHIC, the \(p/\pi^0\) ratio is about 1 between 1.5 and 4.5 GeV/\(c\).
- For LHC, the crossover between recombination and pQCD domains shifts to 6 GeV/\(c\) for pions (from 4 GeV/\(c\) at RHIC) and to 8 GeV/\(c\) for protons (from 6 GeV/\(c\) at RHIC).
- At LHC, quenching factors of 10–30 are estimated for pions at \(P_T = 10\) GeV (from Vitev (2002)).
- The thermal parton phase used for LHC predictions assumes temperature \(T = 175\) MeV and radial flow velocity \(v_T = 0.75c\).
- LHC lead-lead collisions will have center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed from CERN SPS and RHIC experiments, particularly the work on jet quenching by Bjorken (1982) and Thoma (1990), and the recombination formalism by Fries (2003). It builds on lattice QCD results from Karsch (2001) and Fodor (2001) for the phase diagram, and on saturation physics from Gribov (1981) and McLerran (1993). The paper enables future LHC studies of medium properties via photon-tagged jets and jet-photon conversion, and identifies open questions about gluon saturation, heavy quark thermalization, and nuclear higher-twist effects.
