# Collinear Structure of Nonlinear Small-$x$ Evolution

> We introduce a novel approach to high-energy QCD factorization of cross-sections for processes involving a dilute projectile and a dense target. Our method preserves the factorization between "fast" and "slow" modes in the longitudinal momentum $k^+$ for a projectile moving along the positive light-cone direction, making it compatible with higher-order loop computations done within the standard formulation of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory. Moreover, it eliminates the anomalous double collinear logarithms that typically hinder the convergence of perturbation theory at small-$x$. Our scheme amounts to a change of basis in the space of CGC operators, introducing an arbitrary transverse scale dependence while leaving physical cross-sections invariant. Implementing this scheme requires a modification of the high-energy renormalization group equation for the CGC operators, which we explicitly derive at next-to-leading order (NLO) and in the non-linear regime for the case of the dipole operator and the DIS impact factor. This general framework provides a consistent and systematic prescription for computing observables in the small-$x$ regime of QCD, free from collinear instabilities.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a systematic first-principles framework for high-energy QCD factorization that eliminates the anomalous double collinear logarithms which cause numerical instabilities in NLO small-$x$ evolution, by exploiting factorization-scheme invariance to redefine the dipole operator and introduce an arbitrary transverse scale $\mu$, while leaving physical cross-sections invariant.

## Method
The authors modify the standard Color Glass Condensate (CGC) operator basis by defining a "composite dipole" operator $\bar S_{12}(\zeta,\mu^2)$ that includes a subtraction term proportional to $\ln(\br_{123}^2\mu^2)$, derived from the one-loop correction to a generalized dipole operator that captures information about the longitudinal size of the shockwave. They derive the modified NLO Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation for this composite dipole and the corresponding modified NLO DIS impact factor, showing that the double collinear logarithm $2\ln(\bx_{13}^2/\bx_{12}^2)\ln(\bx_{32}^2/\bx_{12}^2)$ is removed from the evolution kernel when the scheme choice $\br_{123}^2 = |\bx_{13}||\bx_{32}|$ is adopted.

## Key numbers
- The double collinear logarithm term in the NLO BK kernel is $2\ln(\bx_{13}^2/\bx_{12}^2)\ln(\bx_{32}^2/\bx_{12}^2)$, which generates numerical instabilities and must be resummed.
- The phase space integral for gluon radiation at double logarithmic accuracy yields $\bar{\alpha}_s \ln(Q^2/Q_0^2)\ln(1/x_{\rm Bj})$ when the kinematic constraint $k^+ \gg k_\perp^2/(2P^-)$ is enforced, compared to $\bar{\alpha}_s \ln(Q^2/Q_0^2)\ln(1/x_{\rm Bj}) - (\bar{\alpha}_s/2)\ln^2(Q^2/Q_0^2)$ without it.
- The composite dipole transformation at NLO is: $\bar S_{12}(\zeta,\mu^2) = S_{12}(\zeta) - \bar{\alpha}_s \int_3 K_{123} \ln(\br_{123}^2\mu^2)[S_{13}S_{32}-S_{12}](\zeta)$, where $\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi$.
- The scaling property $\bar S(\zeta,\mu^2) \equiv \bar S(\zeta/\mu^2)$ holds at LO, allowing the choice $\mu = Q$ and $\zeta = s$ to give $Y = \ln(s/Q^2) = \ln(1/x_{\rm Bj})$.

## Context
This work extends the shockwave formalism of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, addressing instabilities first identified in NLO BK evolution by Lappi and M\"antysaari (2015) and in NLO DIS impact factors by Beuf (2014). It provides a systematic alternative to the ad hoc kinematic constraint methods developed by Beuf (2014), Iancu et al. (2015), and Duclou\'e et al. (2019), which enforced ordering in both $k^+$ and $k^-$ to restore time ordering. The approach enables consistent higher-order computations in the small-$x$ regime, and together with the recent NNLO BK calculation by Brunello et al. (2025), paves the way for two-loop dipole evolution without collinear instabilities.
