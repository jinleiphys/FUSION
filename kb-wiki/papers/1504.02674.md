# TaN molecule as a candidate to search for New physics

> It is demonstrated that the TaN molecule is the best candidate to search for T,P-violating nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM), it also looks promising to search for other T,P-odd effects. We report results of coupled-cluster calculations of T,P-odd effects in TaN produced by the Ta nucleus MQM, electron electric dipole moment (EDM), scalar$-$pseudoscalar nucleus$-$electron interactions, also of the molecule-axis hyperfine structure constant and dipole moment. Nuclear calculations of $^{181}$Ta MQM are performed to express the T,P-odd effect in terms of the strength constants of T,P-odd nuclear forces, proton and neutron EDM, QCD parameter $θ$ and quark chromo-EDM.

# TaN molecule as a candidate to search for New physics

## Key claim

The TaN molecule in its ^3Δ_1 state is the most promising candidate among heavy-atom diatomic molecules studied to date for searching for the T,P-violating nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the ^181Ta nucleus, provided that the molecule can be fully polarized in a small electric field and systematic errors can be cancelled using Ω-doublet components.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled-cluster calculations including single, double, triple, and perturbative quadruple cluster amplitudes (CCSDT(Q)) to compute the electronic structure of the ^3Δ_1 state of TaN. They used a two-step procedure combining the generalized relativistic effective core potential (GRECP) method with subsequent restoration of the four-component wavefunction in the core region of Ta. Nuclear calculations of the ^181Ta MQM were performed using a deformed-nucleus model with occupation numbers from the Bohr model to express the MQM in terms of fundamental CP-violating parameters.

## Key numbers

- Effective electric field E_eff = 34.9 GV/cm for the ^3Δ_1 state of TaN (about 1.5 times larger than in HfF^+ and similar to PbF and WC, but about two times smaller than in ThO).
- Parameter of T,P-odd MQM interaction W_M = 1.08 × 10^33 Hz/(e·cm^2).
- Parameter of scalar-pseudoscalar nucleus-electron interaction W_SP = 31 kHz.
- Molecule-axis hyperfine structure constant A_|| = -3132 MHz for ^181Ta (nuclear magnetic moment μ_Ta = 2.371 μ_N).
- Equilibrium internuclear distance R_e = 3.19 a.u. (experimental value 3.20 a.u.).
- Harmonic vibrational wavenumber ω_e = 1028 cm^-1; vibrational anharmonicity ω_e x_e = 3.5 cm^-1.
- For the lowest rotational level (J=1, F=7/2), the MQM energy shift coefficient |C(J=1,F=7/2)| = 0.143.
- Current limits on proton EDM (|d_p| < 8.6 × 10^-25 e·cm), QCD θ parameter (|θ̃| < 2.4 × 10^-10), and quark chromo-EDM (|d̃_u - d̃_d| < 6 × 10^-27 cm) correspond to MQM energy shifts of 40 μHz, 18 μHz, and 28 μHz, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Flambaum, DeMille, and Kozlov (2014) that TaN is a promising candidate for MQM searches, providing the first reliable ab initio calculations of the relevant T,P-odd parameters. The TaN molecule offers advantages over other systems such as ThO (used in the ACME experiment), HfF^+, YbF, and WC because ^181Ta has nuclear spin I=7/2, a strongly enhanced collective MQM due to nuclear deformation, and the ^3Δ_1 state has Ω-doublets allowing full polarization in small electric fields with cancellation of systematic errors. The calculated parameters enable future experiments to extract limits on fundamental CP-violating quantities including the QCD θ parameter, quark chromo-EDMs, and nucleon EDMs from MQM measurements in TaN.
