# Chiral Symmetry Breaking and Dual Gluon Mass in the Confining Region of QCD

> The Dual Meissner Effect description of QCD in the confining region provides $\frac{1}{q^4}$ behaviour for the gluon propagator and involves the dual gluon mass $m$ as a parameter. This is used in the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quarks in the infrared region to exhibit chiral symmetry breaking for light quarks. Using the light quark condensate as input, the dual gluon mass is determined and its importance in showing the asymptotic free behaviour of the extrinsic curvature coupling in the rigid QCD string is discussed.

## Key claim

Using the dual Meissner effect description of QCD in the confining region, which provides a 1/q⁴ gluon propagator, chiral symmetry breaking is exhibited for light quarks through the Schwinger-Dyson equation in the rainbow approximation; the dual gluon mass is then determined from the light quark condensate, but this determination depends on the momentum scale through the running coupling.

## Method

The authors employed the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator in the rainbow approximation (with bare vertex and renormalization constants set to one) in Feynman gauge, using a 1/q⁴ gluon propagator derived from the dual Meissner effect description of QCD. They assumed a constant coupling g² in the infrared region following Gribov (1998) and used a Cauchy-Riemann representation for the quark mass function B(k²) to evaluate the integral equation. The resulting relation between the quark functions A(p²) and M(p²) was then used to compute the light quark condensate, which was matched to the lattice QCD estimate of Hemmert, Procura and Weise (2003) to determine the combination m²g², and the running coupling of Alkofer and Fischer (2003) was used to extract the dual gluon mass at various momenta.

## Key numbers

- The combination m²g²/(2π²) = 0.28 GeV², determined from the light quark condensate.
- The quark mass at zero momentum M(0) = 534 MeV, obtained from √D = 534 MeV, which compares with the lattice estimate of approximately 0.6 GeV from Maris, Raya, Roberts and Schmidt (2002).
- The dual gluon mass ranges from 0.384 GeV at p=0 GeV (α=2.972) to 0.828 GeV at p=1.0 GeV (α=0.641).
- For g² ≈ 8, the dual gluon mass is approximately 828 MeV, agreeing with the estimate of Baker, Ball and Zachariasen (1991).
- The string tension for light quarks is σ = 0.07 GeV², estimated from m²g²/(8π²) using the determined value of m²g²/(2π²)=0.28 GeV², agreeing with the estimate of Weda and Tjon (2004).
- The dimensionless extrinsic curvature coupling at p=0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 GeV is 10.24, 2.33, 0.977, and 0.401 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the dual Meissner effect description of QCD by Parthasarathy (2000), which provided confining configurations and a 1/q⁴ gluon propagator, by demonstrating that this propagator yields chiral symmetry breaking through the Schwinger-Dyson equation. The result is contrasted with the delta-function gluon propagator ansatz of Munczek and Nemirovsky (1983), which gives a similar constant relation for the quark functions but does not produce a linear potential. The determination of the dual gluon mass enables the calculation of the extrinsic curvature coupling in the rigid QCD string action derived by Parthasarathy (2001), showing that this coupling exhibits asymptotic freedom, consistent with earlier work by Viswanathan, Parthasarathy and Kay (1991).
