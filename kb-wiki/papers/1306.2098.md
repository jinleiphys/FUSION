# Weak decay constant of neutral pions in a hot and magnetized quark matter

> The directional weak decay constants of neutral pions are determined at finite temperature T, chemical potential μand in the presence of a constant magnetic field B. To do this, we first derive the energy dispersion relation of neutral pions from the corresponding effective action of a two-flavor, hot and magnetized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Using this dispersion relation, including nontrivial directional refraction indices, we then generalize the PCAC relation of neutral pions and derive the Goldberger-Treiman (GT) as well as the Gell--Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relations consisting of directional quark-pion coupling constant g_{qqπ^{0}}^{(μ)} and weak decay constant f_{π^{0}}^{(μ)} of neutral pions. The temperature dependence of g_{qqπ^{0}}^{(μ)} and f_{π^{0}}^{(μ)}, are then determined for fixed chemical potential and various constant background magnetic fields. The GT and GOR relations are also verified at finite T,μand eB. It is shown that, because of the explicit breaking of the Lorentz invariance by the magnetic field, the directional quark-pion coupling and decay constants of neutral pions in the longitudinal and transverse directions with respect to the direction of the external magnetic field are different, i.e. g_{qqπ^{0}}^{\|}\neq g_{qqπ^{0}}^{\perp} and f_{π^{0}}^{\|}\neq f_{π^{0}}^{\perp}. As it turns out, for fixed T,μand B, g_{qqπ^{0}}^{\|}> g_{qqπ^{0}}^{\perp} and f_{π^{0}}^{\|}< f_{π^{0}}^{\perp}.

## Key claim

The weak decay constant of neutral pions becomes directional (longitudinal vs. transverse) in the presence of a constant magnetic field, with the longitudinal component smaller than the transverse component, because the magnetic field explicitly breaks Lorentz invariance; this anisotropy is derived within a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature and chemical potential, and the modified Goldberger-Treiman and Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations are verified to hold with directional quantities.

## Method

The authors start from the effective action of a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in a constant external magnetic field, expanded to second order in derivatives around a constant chiral condensate. They derive the anisotropic energy dispersion relation for neutral pions, which includes directional refraction indices, and then generalize the partially conserved axial vector current (PCAC) relation to incorporate these directional indices. Using this modified PCAC relation, they derive directional versions of the Goldberger-Treiman and Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations, and compute the temperature dependence of the directional quark-pion coupling constants and weak decay constants for fixed chemical potential and various magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers

* For magnetic field strengths eB = 0.03, 0.2, and 0.3 GeV², the transverse refraction indices satisfy u_{\pi⁰}^{(1)} = u_{\pi⁰}^{(2)} > 1, while the longitudinal index u_{\pi⁰}^{(3)} = 1, independent of temperature and magnetic field.
* The directional weak decay constants satisfy f_{\pi⁰}^{(0)} = f_{\pi⁰}^{(3)} ≠ f_{\pi⁰}^{(1)} = f_{\pi⁰}^{(2)}, with f_{\pi⁰}^{(0)} < f_{\pi⁰}^{(1)} for fixed temperature, chemical potential, and magnetic field.
* The directional quark-pion coupling constants satisfy g_{qqπ⁰}^{(0)} = g_{qqπ⁰}^{(3)} ≠ g_{qqπ⁰}^{(1)} = g_{qqπ⁰}^{(2)}, with g_{qqπ⁰}^{(0)} > g_{qqπ⁰}^{(1)} for fixed temperature, chemical potential, and magnetic field.
* The magnetic fields considered correspond to eB ~ 1.5 m_π² (0.03 GeV²) for RHIC and eB ~ 15 m_π² (0.3 GeV²) for LHC, where m_π = 138 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of neutral pion mass and directional refraction indices in a hot magnetized quark matter (Fayazbakhsh and Sadooghi, 2012) by computing the weak decay constant and verifying low-energy theorems. It generalizes earlier work using first-order chiral perturbation theory (Agasian, 2001) that showed the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation remains valid at finite temperature and magnetic field, but now within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model framework. The results enable future studies of neutral pion properties in the freeze-out region of heavy-ion collisions, where strong transient magnetic fields are produced, and provide a theoretical basis for understanding how magnetic fields affect chiral symmetry restoration signatures.
