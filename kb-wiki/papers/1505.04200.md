# Clebcsh-Gordan coefficients in the symmric group $S_n$

> The coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP) or Clebcsh-Gordan coefficients of the outer product of representations of the symmetric group $S_n$ are evaluated using an build up algorithm defined in terms of the chain involving the chain $S_{n-1}\subset S_n$ and $S_{n-2}\subset S_n$ chains. Some applications in mathematical physics are considered by combining them with the Clebcsh-Gordan (C-G) coefficients of the inner product of representations of the symmetric group $S_n$

## Key claim

The paper presents a build-up algorithm for evaluating Clebsch-Gordan coefficients (coefficients of fractional parentage) for the outer product of representations of the symmetric group \(S_n\), using recursion based on the chains \(S_{n-1} \subset S_n\) and \(S_{n-2} \subset S_n\), but the construction is explicitly limited to cases where all particles occupy different states, and the eigenvectors for degenerate eigenvalues are chosen arbitrarily (any linear combination of degenerate states is accepted).

## Method

The algorithm constructs one-particle coefficients of fractional parentage (CFP) by starting from known representations of \(S_{n-1}\) and adding one particle, then diagonalizing the operator \(P = \sum_{i<j} p(i,j)\) (sum of all transpositions) in the product basis. The eigenvalues of \(P\) depend only on the Young tableau \([f]\) and serve to identify the irreducible representations of \(S_n\) that appear in the outer product. The method is applied explicitly for \(n=3,4,5\) to generate the reduction of outer products and the corresponding expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

* For \(S_3\): the operator \(P\) has eigenvalues 3 for \([3]\), 0 for \([2,1]\), and -3 for \([1^3]\).
* For \(S_3\): the product \([2,1] \otimes [2,1]\) yields \([3] + 2[2,1] + [1^3]\).
* For \(S_4\): the product \([3,1] \times [3,1]\) yields \([4] + [3,1] + [2^2] + [2,1^2]\).
* For \(S_4\): the product \([2^2] \times [2^2]\) yields \([4] + [2^2]\).
* For \(S_4\): the product \([3] \otimes 1\) yields \([4] + 3[3,1]\) with eigenvalues 6, 2, 2, 2.
* For \(S_4\): the product \([1,1,1] \otimes 1\) yields \([1^4] + 3[2,1^2]\) with eigenvalues -6, -2, -2, -2.
* For \(S_5\): the product \([4] \otimes [1]\) yields \([5] + 4[4,1]\).
* For \(S_5\): the product \([3,1] \otimes [1]\) yields \(4[4,1] + 5[3,2] + 6[3,1,1]\) with eigenvalues 5, 2, and 0 with multiplicities 5, 5, and 2 respectively.
* For \(S_5\): the product \([2^2] \otimes [1]\) yields \(5[3,2] + 5[2^2,1]\) with eigenvalues 2 and -2, each quadruply degenerate.

## Context

This work extends the known reduction formulas for inner and outer products of symmetric group representations (as summarized by Hamermesh and by Murganan) by providing explicit numerical coefficients for the Clebsch-Gordan series up to \(S_5\). The method enables construction of many-particle wavefunctions with specified permutation symmetry, with applications to quark models (e.g., the nucleon wavefunction in three-flavor SU(3) as discussed by Lightenberg and Perkins) and multiquark systems with color-spin locking as in the work of Buballa (2005). The paper also connects to the review by Liang Meng and Zhou (2015) on the role of symmetric groups in theoretical physics.
