# On-shell consistency of the Rarita-Schwinger field formulation

> We prove that any bilinear coupling of a massive spin-3/2 field can be brought into a gauge invariant form suggested by Pascalutsa by means of a non-linear field redefinition. The corresponding field transformation is given explicitly in a closed form and the implications for chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta (1232) isobar degrees of freedom are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proves that any bilinear coupling of a massive spin-3/2 field can be transformed into a gauge-invariant form (the form suggested by Pascalutsa) via a non-linear field redefinition, but only on-shell; the equivalence does not hold off-shell, where inconsistencies such as acausal propagation may still appear.

## Method

The authors employ a functional integral approach to show that a gauge-invariant interacting theory for spin-3/2 fields has the same constraints as the free theory. They then construct an explicit non-linear field redefinition, given in closed form in terms of operators X, S, and their conjugates, that maps a general bilinear interaction Lagrangian into a gauge-invariant form. The equivalence is verified algebraically and also interpreted diagrammatically via partial fraction decomposition of the free Feynman propagator.

## Key numbers

* The free massless Lagrangian is invariant under the gauge transformation psi_mu → psi_mu + partial_mu epsilon, where epsilon is an arbitrary spinor.
* The inverse of the mass operator in d dimensions is given by [1/m]^{mu nu} = -(1/m)(g^{mu nu} + (1/(1-d)) gamma^mu gamma^nu).
* The off-shell parameter contributions are projected out via the relation (Theta(Z_i) (1/m) i slash{partial} psi)_mu = [(1/m) i slash{partial} psi]_mu, using the field constraints gamma^nu psi_nu = 0 and partial^nu psi_nu = 0.
* In d dimensions, the relation gamma_alpha gamma^{alpha nu lambda} = (d-2) gamma^{nu lambda} is used in the projection of off-shell parameters.

## Context

This work extends the earlier result of Pascalutsa (2000), who showed that gauge non-invariant linear couplings of spin-3/2 fields can be brought into gauge-invariant form via a field redefinition, but left the case of bilinear couplings as a conjecture. The paper provides the explicit transformation for bilinear couplings, which is relevant for chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta(1232) isobar degrees of freedom. The result enables consistent calculations using naive Feynman rules in chiral effective field theory, and clarifies that off-shell parameters (related to unphysical spin-1/2 components) can be absorbed into counterterms, as previously discussed by Ellis and Tang (1996) and Fettes and Meissner (2000).
