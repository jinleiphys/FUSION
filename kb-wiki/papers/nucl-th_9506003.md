# Composite nucleons in scalar and vector mean-fields

> We emphasize that the composite structure of the nucleon may play quite an important role in nuclear physics. It is shown that the momentum-dependent repulsive force of second order in the scalar field, which plays an important role in Dirac phenomenology, can be found in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, and that the properties of nuclear matter are well described through the quark-scalar density in a nucleon and a self-consistency condition for the scalar field. The difference between theories of point-like nucleons and composite ones may be seen in the change of the $ω$-meson mass in nuclear matter if the composite nature of the nucleon suppresses contributions from nucleon-antinucleon pair creation.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which treats nucleons as composite objects rather than point-like particles, reproduces the momentum-dependent repulsive force of second order in the scalar field that is essential to Dirac phenomenology, but the origin of this force lies in the internal quark structure of the nucleon rather than in virtual nucleon-antinucleon pair creation.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which nuclear matter is treated as a collection of composite nucleons (MIT bags) interacting with scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mean-fields at the quark level. They expanded the effective nucleon mass in powers of the scalar field to second order, identifying the scalar density of quarks inside the nucleon bag as the response function to the external scalar field. A self-consistency condition for the scalar field was derived, relating the scalar mean-field to the quark-scalar density integrated over the Fermi sea.

## Key numbers

- The quark-scalar density \(C_N(\bar{\sigma})\) is much less than unity and decreases with increasing nuclear density, providing a suppression mechanism for the σ field at high density.
- The QMC model predicts a nuclear matter incompressibility of approximately 220 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value once binding energy and saturation density are fixed.
- The effective ω-meson mass in matter decreases as \((m_{\omega}^{\star}/m_{\omega}) \simeq 1 - 0.09 (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) at low density in the QMC model.

## Context

This work extends the Dirac phenomenology of Walecka's σ-ω model (QHD) by incorporating the composite nature of the nucleon, addressing criticisms by Brodsky, Kiritsis and Seki, and Cohen that virtual nucleon-antinucleon pair creation should be suppressed for composite objects. It builds on the earlier QMC model of Guichon and shows that the momentum-dependent repulsive force derived by Wallace, Gross, and Tjon and by Birse arises naturally from quark structure. The paper enables experimental tests of nucleon compositeness through measurements of the ω-meson mass in nuclear matter, where QHD and QMC models predict opposite trends if vacuum polarization is suppressed.
