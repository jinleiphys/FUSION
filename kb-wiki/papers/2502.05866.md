# Spin alignment of quarkonia in vortical quark-gluon plasma

> The spin alignment of $J/ψ$ with respect to event plane in relativistic heavy ion collisions exhibits a significant signal. We propose a possible mechanism for spin alignment through spin dependent dissociation of quarkonia in a vortical quark-gluon plasma. The spin dependent dissociation is realized through inelastic scattering between constituents of quarkonium and those of quark-gluon plasma polarized by the vorticity. The spin dependent dissociation rate is found to depend on the directions of vorticity, quantization axis, and quark momentum. We implement our results in a dissociation dominated evolution model for quarkonia in the Bjorken flow, finding the spin $0$ state is slightly suppressed compared to the average of the other two, which is consistent with the sign found in experiments. We also find absence of logarithmic enhancement in binding energy in the vortical correction to dissociation rate, which is understood from the requirement that a spin dependent dissociation can only come from quark coupling to a pair of chromomagnetic and chromoelectric field.

## Key claim

The authors propose that spin alignment of $J/\psi$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions can arise from spin-dependent dissociation in a vortical quark-gluon plasma, where the spin-0 state is slightly suppressed compared to the average of the other two spin states. This mechanism is presented as an alternative to the standard spin-vorticity coupling explanation, which predicts a much smaller signal because it is quadratic in the phenomenologically small vorticity.

## Method

The authors calculate the spin-dependent dissociation rate of spin-triplet S-wave quarkonia in a vortical quark-gluon plasma using the quasi-free picture, where heavy quark constituents scatter independently with light quarks and gluons. They compute the vortical correction to the heavy quark self-energy at leading logarithmic order in binding energy, restricting to Coulomb scattering processes, using hard thermal loop resummed gluon propagators and off-equilibrium propagators for light quarks and gluons modified by vorticity. The spin-dependent dissociation rate is then implemented in a dissociation-dominated evolution model for quarkonia in Bjorken flow to compute the spin alignment signal.

## Key numbers

* The vorticity produced in heavy ion collisions is approximately 10 MeV, much smaller than the typical QGP temperature of approximately 200 MeV.
* The binding energy is taken as 0.0233 GeV for an average temperature of 210 MeV, corresponding to the weak binding scenario.
* The numerical results show that |Gamma_2| is much larger than |Gamma_1|, indicating the angular dependence of the spin-dependent dissociation rate is largely set by the dot product of vorticity and quantization axis.
* The coupling constant alpha_s is taken as 0.3, with N_f = N_c = 3 and the heavy quark mass taken as the charm quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy ion collisions, following the measurement of significant spin alignment signals for the phi meson and the J/psi by the STAR Collaboration (STAR 2022). The proposed mechanism through spin-dependent dissociation differs from the standard spin-vorticity coupling mechanism proposed by Liang and Yang (2004, 2017), which gives the correct sign but much smaller magnitude. The authors focus on dissociation-dominated production in medium-high energy collisions, as opposed to recombination production which dominates at high energies, and note that the absence of logarithmic enhancement in binding energy in the vortical correction is understood from the requirement that spin-dependent dissociation can only come from quark coupling to a pair of chromomagnetic and chromoelectric fields.
