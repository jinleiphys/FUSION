# Effective field theory for lambda-sigma^0 mixing in nuclear matter

> We extend the effective field theory approach which successfully describes ordinary nuclei and nuclear matter to incorporate strangeness in nuclear structure. Central object is a chiral effective Lagrangian involving the baryon octet, the Goldstone boson octet, the vector meson octet and a light scalar singlet. According to the rules of effective field theory, we include all interaction terms (up to a given order of truncation) that are consistent with the underlying symmetries of QCD. We develop a mean-field approximation and study nuclear matter as a simple model for multi-strange systems. A D-type Yukawa coupling between baryons and vector mesons leads to lambda-sigma^0 flavor mixing in the nuclear medium. We study flavor oscillations in the nuclear matter ground state which are closely related to the phenomenon of neutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The paper extends the effective field theory (EFT) approach for ordinary nuclei to incorporate strangeness, and finds that a D-type Yukawa coupling between baryons and vector mesons leads to Λ–Σ⁰ flavor mixing in nuclear matter as a true many-body effect, which is considerably larger than the vacuum mixing arising from isospin violation.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian including the baryon octet, Goldstone boson octet, vector meson octet, and a light scalar singlet, truncated at order ν=4. They develop a mean-field approximation and compute the thermodynamic potential at one-loop level, solving the coupled Dirac equations for Λ and Σ⁰ in the presence of a nondiagonal vector self energy generated by the ρ meson mean field.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angle α is determined by tan²(α) − ((V⁰_Λ − V⁰_Σ⁰)/V⁰_m) tan(α) − 1 = 0, where V⁰_m = g^ρ_ΛΣ ρ⁰.
- The energy eigenvalues for the mass eigenstates 1 and 2 are E^±_i = V⁰_i ± √(p² + M*²) in the chiral limit M*_Λ = M*_Σ ≡ M*.
- The density-dependent part of the thermodynamic potential for Λ–Σ⁰ is ω⁰_ΛΣ⁰ = −2 Σ_{i=1,2} ∫ d³p/(2π)³ (U⁰_1 − E^+_i) Θ(U⁰_1 − E^+_i).

## Context

This work extends the EFT framework of Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) for ordinary nuclei to strange systems, and contrasts with earlier relativistic mean-field models (e.g., Rufa et al., 1990; Schaffner et al., 1993, 1994) that included only a restricted subset of allowed interaction terms. The Λ–Σ⁰ mixing is analogous to neutrino oscillations (Bilenky and Pontecorvo, 1978) and the MSW effect, but arises as a many-body effect from the medium rather than from vacuum mass differences. The approach enables systematic study of multi-strange systems and neutron star matter, and predicts that Λ–Σ⁰ mixing could produce deviations of magnetic moments from Schmidt values (Dover et al., 1995) in heavy, asymmetric hypernuclei.
