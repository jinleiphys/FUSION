# Pole analysis on the doubly charmed meson in $D^0D^0π^+$ mass spectrum

> In this paper we study the scattering amplitudes of $D^{0}D^{0}π^+$-$D^{*+}D^{0}$ coupled channels based on $K$-matrix within the Chew-Mandelstam formalism. The $D^{0}D^{0}π^{+}$ invariant mass spectrum of LHCb is fitted and the pole parameters of the $T_{cc}^+$ are extracted. The analysis of pole behavior suggests that the $T_{cc}^+$ may originate from a $D^{*+}D^{0}$ virtual state and is formed as a result of an interplay between an attractive interaction between $D^0$ and $D^{*+}$ and coupling to $D^{0}D^0π^+$ channel.

## Key claim

The $T_{cc}^+$ may not be a true bound state but rather an effect of a complicated interplay between a weak attractive interaction in the $D^0D^{*+}$ system and coupling to the $D^0D^0\pi^+$ channel; in the absence of channel coupling, the $D^0D^{*+}$ system alone may not be sufficiently attractive to produce a molecule, suggesting the $T_{cc}^+$ originates from a $D^{*+}D^0$ virtual state.

## Method

The authors construct coupled-channel scattering amplitudes for $D^0D^0\pi^+$ and $D^{*+}D^0$ using a $K$-matrix within the Chew-Mandelstam formalism, treating the $D^0\pi^+$ system as an isobar of spin-1 for $S$-wave amplitudes. They apply the Au-Morgan-Pennington method to describe final-state interactions and fit the resulting production amplitude to the LHCb $D^0D^0\pi^+$ invariant mass spectrum, convoluting with the experimental resolution function. The amplitudes are then analytically continued to the complex energy plane to extract pole parameters on the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

* Pole location of $T_{cc}^+$ on the second Riemann sheet: $3874.74^{+0.11}_{-0.04} - i\,0.30^{+0.05}_{-0.09}$ MeV.
* Residue coupling to $D^0D^0\pi^+$ channel: $|g_1| = 0.22^{+0.03}_{-0.04}$ GeV with phase $\varphi_1 = 9^{+11}_{-5}$ degrees.
* Residue coupling to $D^0D^{*+}$ channel: $|g_2| = 0.69^{+0.04}_{-0.02}$ GeV with phase $\varphi_2 = 10^{+11}_{-5}$ degrees.
* Fit quality: $\chi^2_{\rm d.o.f} = 0.92$.
* When including the $D^{*+}$ width explicitly, the pole shifts to $3874.76^{+0.08}_{-0.04} - i\,0.26^{+0.02}_{-0.09}$ MeV, with $|g_1| = 0.21^{+0.01}_{-0.04}$ GeV and $|g_2| = 0.71^{+0.02}_{-0.02}$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the $T_{cc}^+$ observed by LHCb (2021) in the $D^0D^0\pi^+$ invariant mass spectrum, following prior analyses of near-threshold exotic hadrons such as the $X(3872)$ near the $D\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold. The pole trajectory analysis, obtained by varying the coupling strength between channels, shows behavior similar to that found for the $P_c(4312)$ by Fernandez-Ramirez (2019), which was interpreted as an effect of weak interaction between $\Sigma_C^+ \bar D^0$ and coupling to $J/\psi p$. The results enable future precise line-shape measurements by LHCb, BelleII, or BESIII to further constrain the nature of the $T_{cc}^+$.
