# Collective Resonances in the Soliton Model Approach to Meson--Baryon Scattering

> The proper description of hadronic decays of baryon resonances has been a long standing problem in soliton models for baryons. In this talk I present a solution to this problem in the three flavor Skyrme model that is consistent with large-Nc consistency conditions. As an application I discuss hadronic pentaquark decays and show that predictions based on axial current matrix elements are erroneous.

# Collective Resonances in the Soliton Model Approach to Meson–Baryon Scattering

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pentaquark states are genuine predictions of chiral soliton models, not artifacts of the rigid rotator approach, by showing that the bound state approach and the rotation–vibration approach yield identical results for the kaon–nucleon S-matrix in the large-\(N_C\) limit. However, the resulting resonance mass is approximately 1.65 GeV (not 1.54 GeV as sometimes claimed), and the width is roughly 40 MeV only if one assumes the observed resonance to be the \(\Theta^+(1540)\).

## Method

The author compares two approaches to describing baryon resonances in the three-flavor Skyrme model: the bound state approach (BSA), where kaon fluctuations about the soliton yield bound states for hyperons, and the rigid rotator approach (RRA), where collective coordinates \(A(t) \in SU(3)\) are canonically quantized. To resolve the long-standing "Yukawa problem", that soliton models lack a linear meson–baryon coupling, the author introduces the rotation–vibration approach (RVA), which imposes constraints to exclude zero-modes from the fluctuations and derives a separable Yukawa potential \(V_Y\) from the interaction Hamiltonian. The resulting phase shifts from the BSA and RVA are shown to be identical in the large-\(N_C\) limit, and the \(R\)-matrix formalism extracts the resonance contribution to the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The bound state eigenenergies for \(P\)-wave fluctuations in the strangeness direction are \(\omega_{\pm} = \frac{1}{2}\left[\sqrt{\omega_0^2 + \frac{3\Gamma}{2\Theta_K}} \pm \omega_0\right]\), where \(\omega_0 = N_C/(4\Theta_K)\) and both \(\Theta_K\) and \(\Gamma\) are \(\mathcal{O}(N_C)\).
- In the flavor symmetric case (\(m_K = m_\pi\)), the mass difference between the \(\Theta^+\) and the nucleon increases by a factor of two from \(\omega_0\) to \((N_C+3)/4\Theta_K\) for \(N_C=3\).
- Assuming the observed resonance to be the \(\Theta^+(1540)\), the width read from the figure is roughly 40 MeV.
- The resonance is predicted to be about 200 MeV above threshold, corresponding to \(M_{\Theta^+} \approx 1.65\) GeV.

## Context

This work resolves two long-standing puzzles in chiral soliton models: the "Yukawa problem" of describing hadronic decays, and the apparent discrepancy between the bound state approach and rigid rotator approach for pentaquark predictions. It extends prior work by Itzhaki (2004), who argued that pentaquarks were artifacts of the RRA, and corrects earlier width calculations by Diakonov et al. (1997) and Ellis et al. (2004), which assumed \(SU(3)\) relations among hadronic decays that are invalid in soliton models due to symmetry breaking by the classical soliton embedding. The analysis enables a consistent framework for computing pentaquark properties in any chiral soliton model, with details given in Weigel (2005) and a review of \(SU(3)\) soliton models in Weigel (1996).
