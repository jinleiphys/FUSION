# Laser-assisted deformed $α$ decay of the ground state even-even nuclei

> In the present work, the influence of ultra-intense laser fields on the $α$ decay half-life of the deformed ground state even-even nucleus with the mass number $52 \leq Z \leq 118$ is systematically studied. The calculations show that the laser field changes the $α$ decay half-life by varying the $α$ decay penetration probability in a small range. Moreover, the analytical formulas for the rate of change of the $α$ decay penetration probability in the ultra-intense laser fields have been derived by the spherical approximation, which agrees well with the numerical solutions for nuclei with more significant proton numbers. This provides a fast way to estimate the rate of change of the $α$ decay penetration probability for superheavy nuclei. Furthermore, the relationship between laser properties and the average rate of change of the $α$ decay penetration probability is investigated. The calculations indicate that the shorter the wavelength of the laser pulse is, the larger the average rate of change of the penetration probability.

## Key claim

Ultra-intense laser fields with peak intensity \(10^{23}\ \mathrm{W/cm^{2}}\) can alter the \(\alpha\) decay half-life of deformed ground-state even-even nuclei by modifying the penetration probability, but the effect is small (rate of change between \(0.0009\%\) and \(0.135\%\)) and only occurs when the daughter nucleus and the \(\alpha\) particle have different charge-to-mass ratios.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent cluster model with a deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and a double-folding Coulomb potential to compute \(\alpha\) decay half-lives for 190 ground-state even-even nuclei with \(52 \leq Z \leq 118\). The laser field was treated in the quasistatic approximation, adding an electric dipole interaction term \(-Q_{eff} r E(t) \cos\theta\) to the total potential, and the penetration probability was averaged over the orientation angle \(\varphi\) of the daughter nucleus symmetry axis. Analytical formulas for the rate of change of the penetration probability were derived using a spherical Gamow-like approximation and compared with the full numerical solutions.

## Key numbers

- For a laser peak intensity of \(10^{23}\ \mathrm{W/cm^{2}}\), the rate of relative change of the \(\alpha\) decay penetration probability \(\Delta P\) ranges from \(0.0009\%\) to \(0.135\%\) across the studied nuclei.
- The most sensitive parent nucleus is \(^{144}\mathrm{Nd}\), with \(\Delta P = 1.35 \times 10^{-3}\) (i.e., \(0.135\%\)) at \(Q_{\alpha} = 1.901\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(^{108}\mathrm{Xe}\), both \(\Delta P\) and \(\Delta T\) are exactly 0 because the daughter nucleus and \(\alpha\) particle have the same charge-to-mass ratio (\(Q_{eff}=0\)).
- The standard deviation between calculated and experimental logarithmic half-lives is \(\sigma = 0.325\).
- The nuclear potential parameters used are \(r_0 = 1.06\), \(s = 0.88\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(V' = 161.95\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(S_{\alpha} = 0.43\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of laser-nucleus interactions (e.g., Mișicu (2013), PhysRevC.102.064629) by systematically including nuclear deformation and covering a wide range of even-even \(\alpha\) emitters from tellurium to oganesson. It provides a fast analytical estimate for superheavy nuclei using a spherical approximation, and identifies that shorter laser wavelengths produce larger average changes in penetration probability, which can guide future experimental designs at facilities such as ELI-NP or SULF.
