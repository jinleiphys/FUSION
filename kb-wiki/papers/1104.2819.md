# Thermodynamics of the in-medium chiral condensate

> The temperature dependence of the chiral condensate in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at varying baryon densities is investigated starting from a realistic free energy density of the correlated nuclear many-body system. The framework is thermal in-medium chiral effective field theory which permits a systematic calculation of the quark mass dependence of the free energy density. One- and two-pion exchange processes, virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobar excitations and Pauli blocking corrections are treated up to and including three-loop order. It is found that nuclear matter remains in the Nambu-Goldstone phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry at least in the range of temperatures $T\lesssim 100\,$MeV and baryon densities up to about twice the density of normal nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The paper finds that isospin-symmetric nuclear matter remains in the Nambu-Goldstone phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry at temperatures up to about 100 MeV and baryon densities up to roughly twice normal nuclear matter density, with no indication of a first-order chiral phase transition in this range.

## Method
The authors compute the temperature- and density-dependent chiral condensate via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, differentiating a free energy density constructed from thermal in-medium chiral effective field theory. One- and two-pion exchange processes, virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitations, and Pauli blocking corrections are treated up to and including three-loop order, with the free energy density built from one-body, two-body, and three-body kernels convolved with Fermi-Dirac distributions.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The chiral condensate at T = 100 MeV recovers an approximately linear decrease with density, characteristic of a free Fermi gas, once all one- and two-pion exchange contributions are included
- The nucleon sigma term used is σ_N = (45 ± 8) MeV at the physical pion mass m_π = 135 MeV, with the central value chosen for numerical calculations
- Input parameters in the chiral limit: f_π = 86.5 MeV, g_A = 1.224, c₁ = -0.93 GeV⁻¹, M_N = 882 MeV
- The calculation is reliable up to about twice normal nuclear matter density (ρ ≲ 2ρ₀) and temperatures up to T ∼ 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends a previous chiral effective field theory calculation by the same group (Fiorilla, Kaiser, Weise, 2011) of the density-dependent in-medium chiral condensate at zero temperature to finite temperatures. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as QCD sum rules (Drukarev, 1980s) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1990s; Klevansky, 1992) that do not properly account for nucleon many-body correlations. The results set nuclear physics constraints for the QCD equation of state relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that recent lattice QCD simulations place the chiral crossover transition at T_c ∼ 170 MeV.
