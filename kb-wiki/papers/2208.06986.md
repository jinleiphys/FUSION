# Relativistic correction to the dissociation temperature of B_c mesons in the hot medium

> By solving two body Dirac equations with potentials at finite temperature, we calculated the dissociation temperature T_d of B_c mesons in the quark-gluon plasma. It is found that the T_d becomes higher with the relativistic correction than the T_d from the Schroedinger equation. Both the short range interaction and the constant term of the potential at the long-range scale have a contribution to the shift of T_d, while the spin interaction is negligible.

# Relativistic correction to the dissociation temperature of B_c mesons in the hot medium

## Key claim

The dissociation temperature T_d of B_c mesons in the quark-gluon plasma increases from 1.45 T_c to 1.9 T_c when relativistic corrections from the two-body Dirac equation are included, compared to the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation result. The increase is mainly due to the square terms S^2 - A^2 in the spin-independent part of the potential, while the spin-dependent part and the difference in fitted quark masses between the two approaches contribute negligibly.

## Method

The authors solve the radial two-body Dirac equation (TBDE) for a pseudoscalar meson with screened Cornell potential inputs at finite temperature, where the potential is split into a vector part A (screened Coulomb) and a scalar part S (screened confining). Quark masses are fitted to experimental masses of η_c, η_b, and B_c mesons separately for the TBDE and the Schrödinger equation. The dissociation temperature T_d is defined as the temperature where the binding energy Δε vanishes, and the screening mass μ(T) is taken from a fit to lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature from Schrödinger equation: T_d = 1.45 T_c
- Dissociation temperature from TBDE (relativistic): T_d = 1.9 T_c
- Quark masses fitted in TBDE: charm quark m_1 = 1.27 GeV, bottom quark m_2 = 4.62 GeV
- Quark masses fitted in Schrödinger equation: charm quark m_1 = 1.19 GeV, bottom quark m_2 = 4.59 GeV
- Experimental B_c mass: 6.274 GeV; TBDE gives 6.254 GeV; Schrödinger gives 6.256 GeV
- If TBDE-fitted quark masses are used in the Schrödinger equation, T_d increases by only about 0.05 T_c
- At dissociation temperature, screening mass is roughly 0.6 GeV
- The mass coefficient modification to the vector part is (ε_w/m^S - 1) ≈ 0.3, giving roughly 15% modification to the potential

## Context

This work extends prior studies of relativistic effects in heavy quarkonium using two-body Dirac equations (Guo 2012, Shi 2013, Wu 2012) to the B_c meson system, which is a cleaner probe than charmonium or bottomonium due to its different-flavor heavy quarks and simpler spin structure. The non-relativistic treatment of heavy quarkonium dissociation (Satz 2005) is the baseline being corrected. The results enable more accurate extraction of the heavy quark potential at finite temperatures from experimental measurements of B_c nuclear modification factors, such as those from CMS (2022).
