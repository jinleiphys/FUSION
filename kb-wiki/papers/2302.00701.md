# Viewpoint: Vector meson spin alignment by the strong force field

> Observation of unexpectedly large global spin alignment of $ϕ$ vector mesons in non-central heavy-ion collisions by STAR experiment may reveal the non-perturbative nature of quark interaction in hot matter through fluctuating strong force field with short correlation length.

## Key claim

The unexpectedly large global spin alignment of φ vector mesons observed by STAR in non-central heavy-ion collisions may be explained by a quark polarization mechanism from a fluctuating strong force field with short correlation length, rather than by the vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma fluid that explains hyperon polarization.

## Method

The paper reviews the STAR measurement of φ and K*0 vector meson spin alignment in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC beam-scan energies, and discusses a theoretical model by Sheng, Oliva, Liang, Wang, and Wang (2022) that attributes the φ meson alignment to quark polarization by a fluctuating strong force field. In this model, quarks interact with the dense medium through a strong force field that is flavor-dependent and has a short-range correlation, so it contributes to spin alignment of flavor-singlet vector mesons (like φ) but not to hyperon global polarization or to spin alignment of flavor-nonsinglet mesons (like K*0).

## Key numbers

- The orbital angular momentum in non-central heavy-ion collisions is on the order of 10³ × (p_in/GeV) ħ, proportional to beam momentum per nucleon.
- The measured global Λ and anti-Λ polarization of 1–2% at RHIC BES energies indicates a vorticity ω ≈ 9 × 10²¹ per second.
- The measured φ meson spin alignment is 2–3 orders of magnitude larger than what would be caused by the fluid vorticity extracted from hyperon polarization.
- The strong field fluctuation strength extracted by STAR from the φ spin alignment measurement is about 2 times the pion mass squared m_π².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Liang and Wang (2004, 2005) who predicted global hyperon polarization and vector meson spin alignment from spin-orbital coupling in non-central heavy-ion collisions. The STAR observation of Λ and anti-Λ polarization (2017) confirmed the vortical nature of the quark-gluon plasma, but the new φ meson result contradicts expectations from that vorticity mechanism. The proposed strong force field model enables extraction of the correlation strength of non-perturbative quark-gluon interactions in hot dense matter, providing new information about the short-distance structure of the quark-gluon plasma and the nature of the QCD phase transition.
