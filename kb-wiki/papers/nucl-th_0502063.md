# Coordinate Space HFB Calculations for the Zirconium Isotope Chain up to the Two-Neutron Dripline

> We solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for deformed, axially symmetric even-even nuclei in coordinate space on a 2-D lattice utilizing the Basis-Spline expansion method. Results are presented for the neutron-rich zirconium isotopes up to the two-neutron dripline. In particular, we calculate binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, normal densities and pairing densities, mean square radii, quadrupole moments, and pairing gaps. Very large prolate quadrupole deformations (beta2=0.42,0.43,0.47) are found for the (102,104,112)Zr isotopes, in agreement with recent experimental data. We compare 2-D Basis-Spline lattice results with the results from a 2-D HFB code which uses a transformed harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a 2-D coordinate-space lattice using the Basis-Spline expansion method accurately reproduces ground-state properties of neutron-rich zirconium isotopes up to the two-neutron dripline, with the caveat that the agreement with experimental data is limited to the few isotopes for which data exist, and the dripline prediction (A=122) is a theoretical result from both the lattice and transformed harmonic oscillator basis codes.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-HFB equations for deformed, axially symmetric even-even nuclei on a 2-D cylindrical coordinate grid (r,z) using the Basis-Spline collocation and Galerkin methods, with a SLy4 Skyrme force in the particle-hole channel and a zero-range delta pairing force in the particle-particle channel. The lattice extended from 0 to 15 fm in the radial direction and from -15 to +15 fm in the symmetry axis direction, with a lattice spacing of about 0.8 fm in the central region, and angular momentum projections Omega = 1/2, 3/2, ..., 21/2 were included. Results were compared with those from a 2-D HFB code using a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis and with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron dripline for the zirconium isotope chain (Z=40) is predicted at mass number A=122 (neutron number N=82) by both the HFB-2D-LATTICE and HFB-2D-THO codes.
* Very large prolate quadrupole deformations beta2 = 0.42, 0.43, and 0.47 are found for the 102Zr, 104Zr, and 112Zr isotopes, respectively; the 112Zr isotope has the largest ground state deformation in the chain.
* The experimental proton rms radius of 102Zr is 4.54 fm, while the HFB codes predict 4.45 fm (HFB-2D-LATTICE) and 4.46 fm (HFB-2D-THO), a difference of about 2%.
* The pairing strength V0 = -187.1305 MeV was adjusted to reproduce the measured average neutron pairing gap of 1.245 MeV in 120Sn.
* For 120Sn, the HFB-2D-LATTICE code gives a binding energy of -1019.26 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -1020.54 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous HFB calculations on a 1-D spherical lattice (Dobaczewski and Nazarewicz, 1996) to deformed, axially symmetric nuclei using a 2-D coordinate-space approach, and it provides a benchmark comparison with the HFB-2D-THO code of Stoitsov et al. (2003). The results enable future studies of neutron-rich nuclei near the dripline, particularly for understanding neutron skins and pairing correlations in deformed systems, and they validate the Basis-Spline lattice method as a tool for describing loosely bound nuclei where continuum effects are important.
