# Baryon masses with dynamical twisted mass fermions

> We present results on the mass of the nucleon and the $Δ$ using two dynamical degenerate twisted mass quarks. The evaluation is performed at four quark masses corresponding to a pion mass in the range of 690-300 MeV on lattices of size 2.1 fm and 2.7 fm. We check for cutoff effects by evaluating these baryon masses on lattices of spatial size 2.1 fm with lattice spacings $a(β=3.9)=0.0855(6)$ fm and $a(β=4.05)=0.0666(6)$ fm, determined from the pion sector and find them to be within our statistical errors. Lattice results are extrapolated to the physical limit using continuum chiral perturbation theory. The nucleon mass at the physical point provides a determination of the lattice spacing. Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^3)$ we find $a(β=3.9)=0.0879(12)$ fm, with a systematic error due to the chiral extrapolation estimated to be about the same as the statistical error. This value of the lattice spacing is in good agreement with the value determined from the pion sector. We check for isospin breaking in the $Δ$-system. We find that $Δ^{++,-}$ and $Δ^{+,0}$ are almost degenerate pointing to small flavor violating effects.

## Key claim
The nucleon mass computed with two dynamical twisted mass quarks, when extrapolated to the physical pion mass using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at O(p^3), yields a lattice spacing of a(beta=3.9)=0.0879(12) fm that is consistent with the value determined from the pion sector, but the systematic error from the chiral extrapolation is estimated to be about the same size as the statistical error.

## Method
The collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate dynamical twisted mass quarks at maximal twist, using the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action. They computed nucleon and Delta baryon masses from two-point correlators using Gaussian smeared interpolating fields at four quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 300 to 690 MeV, on lattices of spatial size 2.1 fm and 2.7 fm, at two lattice spacings (beta=3.9 and beta=4.05). The lattice results were extrapolated to the physical limit using continuum heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at O(p^3) and O(p^4), as well as the small scale expansion and delta-scheme.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass range covered: 300 to 690 MeV across four quark masses
- Lattice spacing from pion sector: a(beta=3.9)=0.0855(6) fm and a(beta=4.05)=0.0666(6) fm
- Lattice spacing from nucleon mass using HBchiPT at O(p^3): a(beta=3.9)=0.0879(12) fm
- Lattice spacing from nucleon mass using HBchiPT at O(p^4): a(beta=3.9)=0.0883(9) fm
- Nucleon mass at the chiral limit from O(p^3) fit: m_N^0=0.875(10) GeV
- Low-energy constant from O(p^3) fit: c_1=-1.23(2) GeV^{-1}
- Nucleon mass at physical pion mass from O(p^3) extrapolation: 0.955(10) GeV (statistical error only)
- Delta^{++,-} mass at physical point from O(p^3) extrapolation: 1.265(26) GeV
- Finite volume effect at smallest quark mass: Delta m_N / m_N = 0.01 +/- 0.02 when increasing lattice size from 2.1 fm to 2.7 fm

## Context
This work extends the twisted mass fermion approach from the pion sector (where accurate determinations of low-energy constants l_3 and l_4 and pi-pi scattering lengths were obtained by the ETM Collaboration) into the light baryon sector. The results are consistent with dynamical staggered fermion calculations from the MILC Collaboration for N_F=2+1 and N_F=2. The chiral extrapolation uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory as developed by Bernard et al. and the small scale expansion of Procura et al., and the extracted low-energy constant c_1 is compared to values from partial wave analyses of elastic pi-N scattering data. The work enables future studies of baryon structure with twisted mass fermions and provides a cross-check of lattice spacing determinations from different physical quantities.
