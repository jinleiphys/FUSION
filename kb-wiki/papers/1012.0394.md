# A New Approach to Physics of Nuclei

> We employ the QCD sum rules method for description of nucleons in nuclear matter. We show that this approach provides a consistent formalism for solving various problems of nuclear physics. Such nucleon characteristics as the Dirac effective mass $m^*$ and the vector self-energy $Σ_V$ are expressed in terms of the in-medium values of QCD condensates. The values of these parameters at saturation density and the dependence on the baryon density and on the neutron-to-proton density ratio is in agreement with the results, obtained by conventional nuclear physics method. The contributions to $m^*$ and $Σ_V$ are related to observables and do not require phenomenological parameters. The scalar interaction is shown to be determined by the pion--nucleon $σ$-term. The nonlinear behavior of the scalar condensate may appear to provide a possible mechanism of the saturation. The approach provided reasonable results for renormalization of the axial coupling constant, for the contribution of the strong interactions to the neutron--proton mass difference and for the behavior of the structure functions of the in-medium nucleon. The approach enables to solve the problems which are difficult or unaccessible for conventional nuclear physics methods. The method provides guide-lines for building the nuclear forces. The three-body interactions emerge within the method in a natural way. There rigorous calculation will be possible in framework of self-consistent calculation in nuclear matter of the scalar condensate and of the nucleon effective mass $m^*$.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rules method can describe nucleons in nuclear matter by expressing the Dirac effective mass \(m^*\) and vector self-energy \(\Sigma_V\) in terms of in-medium QCD condensates, with the scalar interaction determined by the pion-nucleon \(\sigma\)-term; however, the approach requires a self-consistent calculation of the scalar condensate and nucleon effective mass for rigorous results, and the nonlinear behavior of the scalar condensate may provide a possible mechanism for saturation.

## Method

The authors apply the QCD sum rules method to nuclear matter by writing dispersion relations for the nucleon correlation function in the medium, separating singularities connected with the in-medium nucleon from those of the medium itself by fixing the variable \(s=(P+q)^2\). They express the nucleon characteristics \(m^*\) and \(\Sigma_V\) in terms of the in-medium vector condensate \(v(\rho)=\langle M|\sum_i\bar q^i\gamma_0 q^i|M\rangle\) and scalar condensate \(\kappa(\rho)=\langle M|\sum_i\bar q^i q^i|M\rangle\), with the scalar condensate expanded as \(\kappa(\rho)=\kappa(0)+\kappa_N\rho+S(\rho)\) where \(S(\rho)\) arises from nucleon-nucleon interactions. The four-quark condensates are calculated using the Perturbative Chiral Quark Model (PCQM), and the nonlinear contribution \(S(\rho)\) is determined mainly by the pion contribution to the nucleon self-energy, requiring renormalization of the pion propagator by particle-hole excitations.

## Key numbers

- Vector self-energy at saturation density: \(\Sigma_V \approx 200\) MeV
- Shift of Dirac effective mass at saturation density: \(m^*-m \approx -300\) MeV
- Vector condensate per nucleon: \(v_N = \langle N|\sum_i \bar q^i\gamma_0 q^i|N\rangle = 3\) (number of valence quarks)
- Vacuum scalar condensate: \(\langle 0|\bar uu+\bar dd|0\rangle = 2(-240\ \text{MeV})^3\) from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation
- Gluon condensate: \(\langle 0|\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}G^{a\mu\nu}G^a_{\mu\nu}|0\rangle \approx (0.33\ \text{GeV})^4\)
- Vacuum nucleon mass from sum rules: \(m = 0.93\) GeV with residue \(\lambda^2 = 1.8\) GeV\(^6\) and continuum threshold \(W^2 = 2.1\) GeV\(^2\)
- Borel mass interval for vacuum sum rules: \(0.8\ \text{GeV}^2 < M^2 < 1.4\ \text{GeV}^2\)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules method, originally developed for vacuum hadrons by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and applied to nucleons in vacuum by Ioffe (1981), to the description of nucleons in nuclear matter. The approach provides an alternative to Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and the Walecka model, which rely on phenomenological scalar \(\sigma\) and vector \(\omega\) meson exchanges, and addresses problems inaccessible to traditional nuclear physics methods such as the EMC effect in deep inelastic structure functions. The method enables calculation of the axial coupling constant renormalization, neutron-proton mass splitting in asymmetric matter, and provides guidelines for building nuclear forces where three-body interactions emerge naturally.
