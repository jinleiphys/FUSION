# Configuration mixing of angular momentum projected self-consistent mean-field states for neutron-deficient Pb isotopes

> We study the low-lying collective excitation spectra of the neutron-deficient lead isotopes Pb182-Pb194 by performing a configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected self-consistent mean-field states. The same Skyrme interaction SLy6 is used supplemented by a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. This study supports the interpretation of spectra made on the grounds of more schematic models in terms of coexisting spherical, oblate, prolate and superdeformed prolate structures. The model qualitatively reproduces the variation of the spectra with neutron number. Our results for E0 and E2 transition probabilities are compared with the few existing experimental data. Finally, we predict the presence of superdeformed bands at low excitation energy in the most neutron-deficient isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper supports the interpretation of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes (spherical, oblate, prolate, and superdeformed structures) using a fully microscopic method, but the agreement with experiment is only qualitative: the model overestimates the excitation energy of the oblate 0+ states and the vibrational 2+ state, and the trend with neutron number is too abrupt.

## Method
The authors performed a configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected self-consistent mean-field (HF+BCS) states, using the Skyrme interaction SLy6 and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The intrinsic states were generated with a constraint on the axial quadrupole moment, then projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, and finally mixed via a discretized generator coordinate method. Electric transition probabilities were computed directly in the laboratory frame without effective charges.

## Key numbers
- The pairing force strength used was -1250 MeV fm^3 for both neutrons and protons, with a soft cutoff at 5 MeV above and below the Fermi energy.
- The superdeformed minimum in the potential energy curve for 184Pb has a maximal depth of 1.5 MeV.
- The barrier heights between prolate and some superdeformed minima are slightly larger than 2 MeV; the barrier between spherical and oblate minima is always small.
- The total energy gain from rotational and vibrational correlations is between 3.5 and 4.5 MeV for all isotopes studied (182-194Pb).
- The rotational correlation energy is close to 2.5 MeV; vibrational correlations add approximately 1.0 MeV; particle-number projection contributes around 1.0 MeV.
- The deformation β2 of the prolate minimum varies from 0.30 for 190Pb to 0.35 for 182Pb.
- The two first excited 0+ states in 184-188Pb lie within 100 keV experimentally, but the calculation predicts near-degeneracy only in 188Pb.
- The calculated 2+ vibrational state on the spherical ground state has an energy larger than 2 MeV for all isotopes, compared to an experimental energy around 1 MeV (Dra03).

## Context
This work extends self-consistent mean-field studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes by Tajima (1993), Chasman (2001), and others, adding full symmetry restoration and configuration mixing. It contrasts with more schematic models such as the two-level mixing calculations of Dupont (1990) and the three-level mixing of Allatt (1998), and with the IBM calculations of Fossion (2003). The method enables direct calculation of E0 and E2 transition probabilities in the laboratory frame, and predicts superdeformed bands at low excitation energy in the most neutron-deficient isotopes, which can guide future experimental searches.
