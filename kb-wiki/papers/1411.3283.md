# Searching for P- and CP- odd effects in heavy ion collisions

> In this thesis we study the possibility that QCD breaks parity at high temperatures and densities, a scenario that may be tested in heavy ion collisions. Analytical studies with effective models suggest that QCD may break parity in dense systems. Besides, P- and CP- odd bubbles may appear in a finite volume due to local large topological fluctuations in a hot medium. The last effect may be treated in a quasi-equilibrium description by means of a non-trivial axial chemical potential. We consider the 'two flavour' Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in the presence of a vector and an axial external chemical potentials and study the phase structure of the model at zero temperature. These magnitudes could trigger a phase where parity could be broken in QCD. Analogies and differences with the expected behaviour in QCD are discussed besides the limitations of the model. We also consider the low energy realization of QCD in terms of mesons when an axial chemical potential is present. The eigenstates of strong interactions do not have a definite parity and interactions that would otherwise be forbidden compete with the familiar ones. We focus on scalars and pseudoscalars that are described by a generalized linear sigma model. Finally, we investigate how local parity breaking may affect vector physics. A modified dispersion relation is derived for the lightest vector mesons rho and omega. This effect predicts a natural overproduction of lepton pairs in the vicinity of the rho-omega resonance peak as well as a polarization asymmetry around this peak. The dilepton excess seems relevant to explain the anomalous dielectron yield quoted by PHENIX/STAR. We present a detailed analysis of the angular distribution associated to the lepton pairs created from these mesons searching for polarization dependencies. Two angular variables are found to carry the main information related to the parity-breaking effect.

## Key claim

This thesis investigates whether QCD can break parity at high temperatures and densities, a scenario that may be testable in heavy ion collisions. The work assumes topological fluctuations as the source of parity violation throughout, treating the effect via a quasi-equilibrium description using a non-trivial axial chemical potential, rather than claiming definitive evidence of parity violation.

## Method

The author studies the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with external vector and axial chemical potentials at zero temperature to examine the phase structure and possible parity-breaking phases. A generalized linear sigma model is used to describe scalar and pseudoscalar meson physics in the presence of an axial chemical potential, demonstrating that eigenstates of strong interactions no longer have definite parity. Finally, modified dispersion relations are derived for the lightest vector mesons rho and omega under local parity breaking, and the angular distribution of lepton pairs from these mesons is analyzed for polarization dependencies.

## Key numbers

- The constituent mass for u,d quarks from the dynamical chiral condensate is expected to be of order 300 MeV, in contrast with current quark masses of order 5 MeV.
- Lattice QCD suggests a phase transition from hadronic phase to quark-gluon plasma at T_c ~ 140-190 MeV.
- The conjectured bound on the ratio of viscosity to entropy density is eta/s >= 1/4 pi.
- A broadening of the rho meson spectral function of around Delta_Gamma_rho ~ 80 MeV is predicted at a temperature T = 150 MeV and pion density rho_pi ~ 0.75 rho_0, where rho_0 is nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends analytical studies using effective models that suggest QCD may break parity in dense systems due to condensation of pseudoscalar mesons in the isotriplet channel, and builds on lattice analyses attempting to understand the nature of the Aoki phase. The predicted dilepton excess from parity-breaking effects is presented as relevant to explaining the anomalous dielectron yield quoted by PHENIX and STAR experiments at RHIC. The results enable experimental searches for local parity breaking signatures in heavy ion collisions through polarization asymmetries around the rho-omega resonance peak.
