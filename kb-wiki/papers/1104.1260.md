# Observability of an induced electric dipole moment of the neutron from nonlinear QED

> It has been shown recently that a neutron placed in an external quasistatic electric field develops an induced electric dipole moment $\mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{IND}}$ due to quantum fluctuations in the QED vacuum. A feasible experiment which could detect such an effect is proposed and described here. It is shown that the peculiar angular dependence of $\mathbf{p}_{\mathrm{IND}}$ on the orientation of the neutron spin leads to a characteristic asymmetry in polarized neutron scattering by heavy nuclei. This asymmetry can be of the order of $10^{-3}$ for neutrons with epithermal energies. For thermalized neutrons from a hot moderator one still expects experimentally accessible values of the order of $10^{-4}$. The contribution of the induced effect to the neutron scattering length is expected to be only one order of magnitude smaller than that due to the neutron polarizability from its quark substructure. The experimental observation of this scattering asymmetry would be the first ever signal of nonlinearity in electrodynamics due to quantum fluctuations in the QED vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the induced electric dipole moment of the neutron arising from nonlinear QED vacuum fluctuations can be detected via a characteristic asymmetry in polarized neutron scattering by heavy nuclei, with the asymmetry reaching observable values of order 10^{-3} for epithermal neutrons and 10^{-4} for thermal neutrons, provided the momentum transfer is sufficiently large to avoid background from other known effects.

## Method

The authors compute the interaction Hamiltonian for a neutron (treated as a magnetic dipole) in external quasistatic electric and magnetic fields using the Euler-Heisenberg effective Lagrangian at one-loop order. They then derive the neutron-nucleus scattering amplitude in the Born approximation including the new NLQED-induced term, and evaluate the resulting differential cross section for scattering off heavy nuclei such as lead, accounting for the cutoff radius where the electric field reaches the critical QED field strength.

## Key numbers

- The Euler-Heisenberg coupling constant is ζ ≈ 1.3 × 10^{-52} J·m/V^4.
- The critical electric field for nonlinear QED effects is E_c ≈ 1.3 × 10^{18} V/m.
- The neutron magnetic moment is μ_n = -9.662 × 10^{-27} A·m^2.
- The lower bound on the effective source radius a from the constraint on field strength is a > 7.6 fm.
- For lead (Z=82), the cutoff radius where the critical field is reached is R ≈ 300 fm.
- The coherent bound scattering length of lead is b̄ = (9.401 ± 0.002) fm.
- The total bound scattering cross section of lead is σ_{s,b} = (11.187 ± 0.007) × 10^{-24} cm^2.
- The predicted scattering asymmetry is of order 10^{-3} for epithermal neutrons and of order 10^{-4} for thermal neutrons from a hot moderator.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of an induced electric dipole moment for a magnetic dipole in an external electric field due to QED vacuum nonlinearity, previously developed by Dominguez et al. (2009). It rules out detection via Larmor frequency changes (finding no effect for spin-1/2 particles) and instead proposes neutron-nucleus scattering as a feasible experimental approach. The observation of this scattering asymmetry would constitute the first experimental signal of nonlinearity in electrodynamics due to quantum fluctuations in the QED vacuum, complementing proposals using ultra-high intensity lasers such as vacuum birefringence (Heinzl et al., 2006; Adler, 2007) and pair production (Schwinger, 1951).
