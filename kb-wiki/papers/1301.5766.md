# Interplay of classical and quantal features within the coherent state model

> The classical and quantal features of a quadrupole coherent state and its projections over angular momentum and boson number are quantitatively analyzed in terms of the departure of the Heisenberg uncertainty relations from the classical limit. This study is performed alternatively for two choices of the pairs of conjugate coordinates. The role of deformation as mediator of classical and quantal behaviors is also commented. Although restoring the rotational and gauge symmetries makes the quantal features manifest dominantly for small deformation, these are blurred by increasing the deformation which pushes the system toward a classical limit.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively analyzes how restoring rotational and gauge symmetries of a quadrupole coherent state affects its classical or quantal character, measured by the departure of Heisenberg uncertainty products from the classical limit of 1/2. The authors find that while angular-momentum projection alone makes quantal features dominant for small deformation and increasingly classical for large deformation, simultaneous projection of both angular momentum and boson number reverses this trend: quantal features dominate at small deformation but the system approaches the classical limit as deformation increases.

## Method

The authors start from an axially deformed quadrupole coherent state \(|\Psi\rangle = e^{(d b^\dagger_{20} - d^* b_{20})}|0\rangle\) with real deformation parameter \(d\). They compute the Heisenberg uncertainty product \(\Delta\hat{\alpha}_{20}\Delta\hat{\pi}_{20}\) for the conjugate quadrupole coordinate and momentum operators, and the uncertainty product \(\Delta\hat{N} \cdot D\hat{P}\) (and related quantities) for the boson number operator and its conjugate phase operator, evaluating these on three types of states: the unprojected coherent state, the angular-momentum-projected states \(\phi^{(g)}_{JM}\), and the states \(|NJM\rangle\) with both angular momentum and boson number projected. Analytical expressions for expectation values and dispersions are derived using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, Legendre polynomials, and overlap integrals, and the results are plotted numerically as functions of the deformation parameter \(d\).

## Key numbers

* The classical limit of the uncertainty product for \((\hat{\alpha}_{20}, \hat{\pi}_{20})\) is \(1/2\) (in units where \(\hbar=1\)); the unprojected coherent state achieves exactly \(\Delta\hat{\alpha}_{20}\Delta\hat{\pi}_{20} = 1/2\).
* For angular-momentum-projected states, the \(J=0\) projected state behaves semiclassically for small deformation \(d \le 1.5\), while states with larger \(J\) show larger deviations from the classical limit.
* For angular-momentum-projected states at large deformation \(d > 3\), the deviation from the classical limit is an increasing function of \(d\) for all \(J\).
* For states with both angular momentum and boson number projected, the dispersion product \(\Delta\hat{\alpha}_{20}\Delta\hat{\pi}_{20}\) tends to the classical limit \(1/2\) for large values of \(d\) (the rotational limit).
* The dispersion of the boson number operator \((\Delta\hat{N})_J\) for angular-momentum-projected states is given by an expression involving \(|d|^4\), \(|d|^2\), and \(J(J+1)\); it vanishes in the spherical limit \(d \to 0\).
* For large \(|d|\), the unprojected coherent state satisfies \(\Delta\hat{N}_0 D\hat{\Phi}_0 = 1/2\) for the boson number and phase uncertainty relation.

## Context

This work extends the Coherent State Model (CSM) developed by Raduta (1982) and Raduta (1976), which uses angular-momentum-projected quadrupole coherent states to describe ground, beta, and gamma rotational bands in nuclei. The paper quantitatively addresses the question of how symmetry restoration affects the classical-to-quantum transition, complementing earlier semiclassical treatments using time-dependent variational principles (Baranger and Veneroni; Villars). The results enable future studies of how deformation mediates quantum decoherence in collective nuclear models and provide analytical tools for assessing the validity of semiclassical approximations in bosonic systems.
