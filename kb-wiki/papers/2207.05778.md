# Temperature and net baryochemical potential dependence of $η/s$ in a hybrid approach

> In this work, the qualitative impact of the net baryochemical potential dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$ in hydrodynamical simulations is studied. The effect of a predicted non-constant $η/s$($μ_B$) is largely unexplored in hydrodynamic simulations. Previous studies focus only on a temperature dependence or even only a constant effective shear viscosity. This work addresses this issue by studying qualitatively the effect of a generalized $η/s$($T,μ_B$) in the hybrid approach SMASH-vHLLE, composed of the hadronic transport approach SMASH and the (3+1)d viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE. In order to reduce the bias of the result on the equation of state used in the hydrodynamic part of the model, $η/s$ is parameterized directly in the energy density and net baryon number density. The parameterization takes into account the constraints of matching to the transport coefficients in the hadronic phase, as well as pQCD results. This work compares the impact of the density dependence for different system sizes and energies and compares the observables with experimental results in the RHIC - BES region $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ =7.7 - 39.0 GeV, as the effect of this generalization is especially relevant for intermediate collision energies, for which the system is in equilibrium for a relevant amount of time, but the net baryochemical potential does not vanish. It is shown that the effect of an explicit net baryon number dependence on the elliptic flow is negligible and only relevant in the early stages of the collision. Additionally, we find that the proposed parameterization could be a good proxy for the shear viscosity in the non-equilibrium hadronic transport stage.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including an explicit net baryon number density dependence in the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s has a negligible effect on elliptic flow and is only relevant in the early stages of the collision, despite theoretical predictions for a non-constant η/s(μ_B). The authors qualify that their study is qualitative and they did not attempt to fit other parameters of the approach simultaneously.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid approach SMASH-vHLLE, combining the hadronic transport code SMASH with the (3+1)d viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE, to simulate heavy-ion collisions at beam energies √s_NN = 7.7 - 39.0 GeV. They parameterized η/s directly in energy density ε and net baryon number density ρ rather than in temperature and baryochemical potential, with the parameterization constrained by matching to hadronic transport coefficients from SMASH box calculations and pQCD results at high temperature. The free parameter S_ρ controlling the net baryon density dependence was varied between -0.05 and 0.05 fm^3 to study its qualitative impact on observables including midrapidity yield, mean transverse momentum, and integrated elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- The switching energy density ε_switch was set to a default value of 0.3 GeV/fm^3, with variations to 0.1 and 0.5 GeV/fm^3 for sensitivity studies
- The kink position ε_kink (minimum of η/s at vanishing net baryochemical potential) was set to 1 GeV/fm^3 based on lattice QCD and experimental results
- The minimum value of η/s at the kink was set to the KSS bound (1/4π)
- The pQCD matching was performed at a temperature of 400 MeV and vanishing net baryochemical potential
- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 7.7, 17.3, and 39.0 GeV for AuAu and PbPb systems
- Temperature throughout hydrodynamic evolution ranged between 108 and 407 MeV
- Net baryochemical potential reached values between 0 and 583 MeV
- 100 event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics events were generated, each with 1000 sampled events for the afterburner
- The elliptic flow was found to be insensitive to the switching criterion in the range ε_switch = 0.1 - 0.5 GeV/fm^3 for the η/s(ε) parameterization

## Context

This work extends previous studies that focused only on temperature-dependent or constant effective shear viscosity in hydrodynamic simulations, such as the default constant η/s values in SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid (originally from UrQMD+vHLLE) and the Bayesian-extracted η/s(T) parameterization from JETSCAPE (2020). The study is motivated by theoretical predictions for a net baryochemical potential dependence of η/s from authors including Denicol (2013), Itakura (2008), Kadam (2015), and Demir (2009). The finding that η/s(ε) makes elliptic flow independent of the switching energy density suggests this parameterization could serve as a good proxy for shear viscosity in the non-equilibrium hadronic transport stage, enabling future studies to use a consistent viscosity description across the hybrid approach transition.
