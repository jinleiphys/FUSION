# Method to observe the $J^{P}=2^{+}$ partner of the $X_{0}(2866)$ in the $B^+ \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+} $ reaction

> We propose a method based on the moments of the $D^{-} K^{+}$ mass distribution in the $B^+ \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+} $ decay to disentangle the contribution of the $ 2^{+} $ state, partner of $X_{0}(2900)$ in the $ \bar D^{*} K^{*}$ picture for this resonance. Some of these moments show the interference patterns of the $X_{1}(2900)$ and $X_{0}(2900)$ with the $ 2^{+} $ state, which provide a clearer signal of the $ 2^{+} $ resonance than the $ 2^{+} $ signal alone. The construction of these magnitudes from present data is easy to implement, and based on these data we show that clear signals for that resonance should be seen even with the present statistics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $J^{P}=2^{+}$ partner of the $X_{0}(2866)$ resonance can be observed in existing $B^{+} \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+}$ data by analyzing moments of the $D^{-} K^{+}$ mass distribution, specifically because interference terms involving the $2^{+}$ state are linear in its amplitude and thus produce a stronger signal than the pure $2^{+}$ contribution alone.

## Method

The authors construct the differential decay width $\dfrac{d\Gamma}{dM_{\rm inv} d \tilde{\Omega}}$ for the $B^{+} \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+}$ reaction, including contributions from $0^{+}$, $1^{-}$, and $2^{+}$ resonances. They define moments of the distribution by projecting onto spherical harmonics $Y_{i0}(\cos\theta)$ for $i=0,1,2,3,4$, and derive linear combinations of these moments that isolate the $2^{+}$ contribution alone and the interference between the $2^{+}$ and $1^{-}$ resonances. The parameters for the $0^{+}$ and $1^{-}$ amplitudes are fixed using data from LHCb (2020), while the $2^{+}$ strength is chosen to be consistent with the non-observation of a clear $2^{+}$ peak in the original mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The predicted $J^{P}=2^{+}$ state has mass $M = 2775$ MeV and width $\Gamma = 38$ MeV, from fine-tuning the molecular $\bar{D}^{*}K^{*}$ model of Molina (2010).
* The $X_{0}(2866)$ has experimental mass $M = 2866 \pm 7$ MeV and width $\Gamma = 57.2 \pm 12.9$ MeV (LHCb 2020).
* The $X_{1}(2900)$ ($1^{-}$ state) has a bigger strength in the $D^{-} K^{+}$ distribution than the $X_{0}(2900)$ according to LHCb (2020).
* The moment $d\Gamma_{4}/dM_{\rm inv}$ isolates the $2^{+}$ contribution and is proportional to $|b|^{2}$; the interference term $d\Gamma_{3}/dM_{\rm inv}$ is proportional to $2\,\text{Re}(bc^{*})$ and is linear in the $2^{+}$ amplitude $b$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular $\bar{D}^{*}K^{*}$ picture of Molina (2010), which predicted $0^{+}$, $1^{+}$, and $2^{+}$ bound states, and the subsequent fine-tuning by Molina (2020) that matched the $X_{0}(2866)$ mass and width. The $1^{+}$ partner was already addressed in proposed reactions by Dai (2022), but the $2^{+}$ state remained unobserved because it requires $L=2$ production in the $B^{+} \to D^{+} D^{-} K^{+}$ decay. The method enables a search for the $2^{+}$ state using existing LHCb data without requiring a full partial wave analysis, and could confirm the molecular nature of the $X_{0}(2866)$ family.
