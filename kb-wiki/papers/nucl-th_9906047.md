# Shock-like Freeze-out in Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> We have formulated a self-consistent model of freeze-out on an arbitrary hypersurface. It conserves energy and momentum across the discontinuity between ideal fluid and the gas of free particles. Energy and momentum of those free particles have non-equilibrium values that could be a signal for the formation of hot and dense matter in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent freeze-out model for relativistic hydrodynamics that conserves energy and momentum across a discontinuity between an ideal fluid and a gas of free particles, but the energy and momentum of the free particles have non-equilibrium values that could serve as a signal for hot and dense matter formation in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The author derives the momentum distribution for a gas of free particles on a time-like freeze-out hypersurface, obtaining a modified Cooper-Frye formula that avoids negative particle numbers by including a theta-function cutoff based on the hypersurface derivative. Conservation laws are then imposed on the discontinuity between the fluid and the gas, yielding a determinant condition on the difference of their energy-momentum tensors and an equation relating the hypersurface velocity to the difference in tensor components. For baryon-rich matter, an additional conservation equation for baryonic charge is included, and the system of equations is closed by the equation of state and the freeze-out condition.

## Key numbers

* For massless noninteracting particles in the left hemisphere, the energy-momentum tensor components of the gas are: \(T^{00}_2(v_\sigma) = \epsilon(T^*) (1+v_\sigma)/2\), \(T^{01}_2(v_\sigma) = \epsilon(T^*) (v_\sigma^2 - 1)/4\), and \(T^{11}_2(v_\sigma) = \epsilon(T^*) (v_\sigma^3 + 1)/6\), where \(\epsilon\) is the usual energy density and \(v_\sigma\) is the derivative of the freeze-out hypersurface.
* For pions, the mean free-path depends on the fifth power of the inverse temperature: \(\lambda \approx \text{const}/T^5\).
* In the reference frame of the gas, the zero component of the baryonic charge flux for the left hemisphere is \(N^0 = n(T,\mu) (1+v_\sigma)/2\).

## Context

This work extends the Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) for particle spectra to time-like hypersurfaces, correcting the negative particle number problem. It contrasts with earlier ad hoc shock-like freeze-out models (Sinyukov, 1993; other works cited as references 3, 10, 11) by deriving the conservation laws from the equations of motion rather than postulating the shock. The model enables practical calculations of freeze-out hypersurfaces in relativistic hydrodynamics and suggests that measuring the ratio of longitudinal momentum density to baryonic charge flux in asymmetric heavy ion collisions (e.g., S + Au reactions studied in reference 7) could provide information about the freeze-out process.
