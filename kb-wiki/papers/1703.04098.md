# Towers of positive parity excited baryons and their mixing in the large $N_c$ limit

> We consider configuration mixing for the nonstrange positive parity excited baryons in the $[\mathbf{56'},0^+],[\mathbf{56},2^+], [\mathbf{70},0^+] $ and $[\mathbf{70},2^+]$ quark model $SU(6) \times O(3)$ multiplets contained in the $N=2$ band. Starting from the effective mass operator for these states we show by an explicit calculation that in the large $N_c$ limit they fall into six towers of degenerate states labeled by $K=0,1,1',2,2',3$. We find that the mixing of the quark model states is much simpler than what is naively expected. To leading order in $N_c$ only states carrying the same $K$ label can mix, which implies that for the spin-flavor states we started with configuration mixing can be parameterized by just two constants, $μ_{K=1}$ and $μ_{K=2}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the large \(N_c\) limit, configuration mixing among the \(N=2\) band positive parity nonstrange baryons (from the \([\mathbf{56'},0^+]\), \([\mathbf{56},2^+]\), \([\mathbf{70},0^+]\), and \([\mathbf{70},2^+]\) quark model multiplets) preserves the tower structure of the mass spectrum, and that mixing only occurs between states carrying the same \(K\) label, parameterized by just two constants \(\mu_{K=1}\) and \(\mu_{K=2}\).

## Method

The authors constructed a leading-order effective mass operator (up to \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^0)\)) that includes a generic spatial operator \(\xi\) to allow for transitions between different orbital angular momenta \(L\) and \(L'\). They computed the matrix elements of this operator between all nonstrange states in the \(S'_0\), \(MS_0\), \(S_2\), and \(MS_2\) spin-flavor representations for arbitrary \(N_c\), then diagonalized the resulting mass matrices in the large \(N_c\) limit to obtain the eigenvalues and eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The 30 nonstrange states (11 \(I=1/2\) and 19 \(I=3/2\)) in the \(N=2\) band fall into six degenerate towers labeled by \(K=0, 1, 1', 2, 2', 3\) in the large \(N_c\) limit.
- The \(K=0\) tower mass is \(m_0 = N_c \, c_1^{S'_0}\).
- The \(K=3\) tower mass is \(m_3 = N_c \, c_1^{MS_2} + c_2^{MS_2} - \frac{2}{7} c_3^{MS_2}\).
- The mixing between \(K=1\) and \(K=1'\) towers is governed by \(\mu_{K=1} = -\sqrt{2} c_3^{MS_0 MS_2}\).
- The mixing between \(K=2\) and \(K=2'\) towers is governed by \(\mu_{K=2} = -\sqrt{2} c_2^{S_2 MS_2}\).
- At \(N_c=3\), the physical states reduce to 19 states (11 \(N\)-states and 8 \(\Delta\)-states, with 11 \(\Delta\)-states being ghost states that decouple).

## Context

This work extends previous large \(N_c\) studies that verified tower degeneracies for the \(N=1\) band \([\mathbf{70},1^-]\) states (Pirjol and Schat, 2003; Cohen and Lebed, 2003) and the \(N=3\) band \([\mathbf{70},3^-]\) states (Matagne and Stancu, 2011), but which neglected configuration mixing. The authors show that configuration mixing, which is not \(N_c\) suppressed (Goity, 2004; Goity and Scoccola, 2005), preserves the tower structure and simplifies dramatically in the \(K\) basis. This enables future phenomenological studies of the \(N=2\) band baryons using the \(1/N_c\) expansion with a parameterization of mixing by only two constants.
