# Dilepton Spectra and Even Flow Harmonics in a Magnetized QGP: An Ideal Hydrodynamic Study

> We present the first comprehensive study of dilepton production from a hot, magnetized quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions (HIC), incorporating realistic, time-dependent, and spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field profiles within an analytically solvable Gubser flow background. This framework provides a significant improvement over previous static calculations with homogeneous fields and moves toward the long-term goal of full $3+1$D magnetohydrodynamic simulations. We explore the effects of impact parameter, electrical conductivity, and invariant mass on the dilepton spectra and anisotropic even flow coefficients. It is found that transverse momentum spectra increase with impact parameter, dominated by annihilation processes, while decay contributions remain sub-leading. Strikingly, the elliptic flow $v_2$ from decay channels is nonzero even in nearly central collisions, exhibiting a characteristic shape--positive at low $p_T$ and negative at high $p_T$--that is largely independent of impact parameter and conductivity. In contrast, $v_2$ from annihilation processes is smaller in magnitude but dominates the total flow in magnitude due to its larger yield. Higher harmonics, such as $v_4$, are an order of magnitude smaller as compared to $v_2$ along with distinctive zero-crossing patterns. Conductivity enhances both spectra and flow but leaves no unambiguous signature for its extraction. Varying the invariant mass reveals the strongest enhancements at low mass, with harmonic coefficients suppressed at higher masses. Overall, our results suggest that central and semi-central collisions can carry imprints of the background magnetic field, and that characteristic correlations in even flow harmonics may provide a robust probe of electromagnetic effects in HICs.

# Dilepton Spectra and Even Flow Harmonics in a Magnetized QGP: An Ideal Hydrodynamic Study

## Key claim

The authors present the first study of dilepton production from a magnetized quark-gluon plasma that incorporates realistic, time-dependent, and spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field profiles within an analytically solvable Gubser flow background, moving beyond previous static calculations with homogeneous fields; however, the results are obtained within an ideal hydrodynamic framework with a simplified Gubser flow that possesses azimuthal symmetry, which is at odds with the anisotropic nature of the electromagnetic field profile, allowing the authors to disentangle harmonic contributions originating from the rate itself from those induced purely by geometry.

## Method

The authors embed a semi-analytical magnetic field profile (derived from Maxwell's equations for colliding nuclei at given impact parameter and finite conductivity) into the Gubser analytic solution for relativistic conformal hydrodynamics, which describes boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and transverse flow of a circularly symmetric plasma. The dilepton production rate is computed using the general expression from Das et al. (2021) that includes quark-antiquark annihilation, quark decay, and antiquark decay channels without limiting the number of Landau levels, and the rate is integrated over the spacetime evolution of the fluid using a (2+1)-dimensional Milne grid covering the domain [-15 fm, 15 fm] x [-15 fm, 15 fm] x [0.4 fm/c, 6.0 fm/c] with 200 x 200 x 40 cells, retaining only the dominant B_y component of the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The Gubser flow parameters are set to hat_T_0 = 10.6 and q^{-1} = 6.4 fm to reproduce experimental pion and proton spectra at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for the 20-30% centrality class (impact parameters approximately 7-8 fm)
- The minimum temperature cutoff for dilepton emission is T_min = 0.15 GeV
- Three representative electrical conductivities are considered: 0.58 MeV, 5.8 MeV, and 10.6 MeV, with the central value 5.8 MeV corresponding to the LQCD value at T = T_C
- The magnetic field magnitude peaks around approximately 10^{-5} GeV^2 for most of the fireball region, with a mean value of B_y across all fluid cells around approximately 10^{-4} GeV^2
- A cutoff on magnetic field strength is applied requiring |eB| > 0.003 GeV^2; below this threshold the Born rate is used for annihilation and decay contributions are set to zero
- For invariant mass M in {0.1, 0.3, 0.5} GeV, the cutoff on the Laguerre polynomial argument xi is set to floor(xi_cf) in {120, 100, 50} respectively
- The higher harmonic v_4 is an order of magnitude smaller than v_2

## Context

This work extends the static, homogeneous-field calculations of Das et al. (2021), who first derived the general dilepton production rate in a magnetized medium without limiting Landau levels, and of Wang et al. (2022) who studied photon and dilepton anisotropy in such fields. It also builds on the Gubser hydrodynamic solution (Gubser, 2010) and the semi-analytical magnetic field profiles from Li et al. (2016) and Siddique et al. (2021, 2022). The results move toward the long-term goal of full 3+1D magnetohydrodynamic simulations and suggest that characteristic correlations in even flow harmonics may provide a robust probe of electromagnetic effects in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that conductivity enhances both spectra and flow but leaves no unambiguous signature for its extraction.
