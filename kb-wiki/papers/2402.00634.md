# High-resolution momentum distributions from low-resolution wave functions

> Nucleon momentum distributions calculated with a common one-body operator vary with the resolution scale (and scheme) of the Hamiltonian used. For high-resolution potentials such as Argonne $v_{18}$ (AV18) there is a high-momentum tail, reflecting short-range correlations in the nuclear wave function, which is reduced or absent for softer, lower-resolution interactions. We explore if the similarity renormalization group (SRG) can be used to quantitatively reproduce the high-resolution distributions from variational Monte Carlo at all momenta using SRG-evolved operators and empirically fit single-particle orbitals rather than a full RG evolution of many-body wave functions. The goal of this approach is to enable calculations of high-resolution distributions for a wider range of nuclei as well as for other interactions, and provides connections to phenomenological analyses of experiments.

## Key claim

The authors explore whether the similarity renormalization group (SRG) can quantitatively reproduce high-resolution nucleon momentum distributions from variational Monte Carlo calculations using SRG-evolved operators combined with empirically fit single-particle orbitals, rather than performing a full RG evolution of many-body wave functions; the approach sacrifices full unitary consistency for simplicity and wider applicability, with the caveat that the matrix element becomes dependent on the SRG scale parameter λ after replacing the evolved ground state with a single Slater determinant.

## Method

The authors compute single-nucleon momentum distributions by applying SRG transformations to the one-body momentum operator, truncating at the two-body operator level, and evaluating the resulting matrix elements using a single Slater determinant of phenomenologically fit Woods-Saxon orbitals rather than fully evolved many-body wave functions. The SRG-evolved operator contributions include linear terms (δU and δU†) that reduce the independent-particle model distribution and a quadratic term (δU δU†) that generates the high-momentum tail. These SRG results are benchmarked against variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations for light nuclei and cluster VMC (CVMC) calculations for 16O and 40Ca, using the Argonne v18 (AV18) interaction with and without three-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- SRG evolution scale λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ is used for all SRG calculations shown in the main results
- Proton momentum distributions are shown up to 2 fm⁻¹ for 16O in the Woods-Saxon parametrization comparison
- The δU + δU† contribution to the momentum distribution is negative up to approximately q ≈ 1.4 fm⁻¹ for 16O
- VMC wave functions reproduce experimental charge radii of 4He and 12C within approximately 1%
- VMC kinetic energy matches Green's function Monte Carlo calculations within approximately 2-8%
- CVMC radii for 16O and 40Ca are approximately 2-4% larger than experiment

## Context

This work extends the approach of Tropiano (2021), which demonstrated that a local density approximation could reproduce the high-momentum tail of nucleon momentum distributions but was not quantitative at lower momenta. The present study improves upon that method by using a Slater determinant with phenomenologically fit single-particle wave functions, enabling quantitative reproduction across all momenta. The approach builds on the generalized contact formalism of Weiss (2015, 2016, 2018, 2021) and the factorization properties of SRG transformations demonstrated by Bogner (2012) and Anderson (2010), and aims to enable calculations of high-resolution momentum distributions for a wider range of nuclei and interactions than currently accessible with VMC methods.
