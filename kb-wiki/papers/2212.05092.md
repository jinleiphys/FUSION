# Nuclear matter from the ladder resummation in terms of the experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes

> Infinite nuclear matter is studied by resuming the series of ladder diagrams based on the results developed by us in Ann. Phys. 437, 168741 (2022). The master formula for the energy density is explicitly solved for the case of contact interactions, within a pionless description of the nucleon-nucleon interactions. Renormalized results are obtained which are directly expressed in terms of the nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and mixing angles in partial-wave amplitudes up to an including $G$ waves, with convergence reached under the inclusion of higher partial waves. The energy per particle, density and sound velocity resulting from the ladder series are given for symmetric and neutron matter. This resummation of the ladder diagrams provides a rigorous result that may be used as low-density reference for other parameterizations of $\bar{\cal{E}}$ for higher densities.

## Key claim

The ladder resummation of nucleon-nucleon interactions in nuclear matter can be expressed directly in terms of experimental phase shifts and mixing angles up to G waves, yielding renormalized, cutoff-independent results for the energy per particle that serve as a rigorous low-density reference for higher-density parameterizations.

## Method

The authors resum the infinite series of ladder diagrams for infinite nuclear matter using the in-medium many-body formalism developed in Alarcón and Oller (2022). The in-medium T-matrix is obtained by solving an integral equation that incorporates Pauli blocking through mixed intermediate states with one Fermi-sea insertion and one vacuum propagator. The energy density is then computed from the trace of the logarithm of the operator (I - t_m L_d), where L_d is a unitary loop function containing two Fermi-sea insertions, and the result is expressed in a partial-wave expansion up to G waves using experimental nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.95 ± 0.40 fm
- For symmetric nuclear matter at k_F = 150 MeV, the energy per particle is approximately -3.5 MeV with an uncertainty of 1.5 MeV from cutoff variation
- The Gaussian cutoff scale Λ ≈ 350 MeV is inferred from the 1.5 MeV uncertainty in symmetric nuclear matter at k_F = 150 MeV
- Kinetic energy density for each nucleon species: ξ^5/(10mπ^2)

## Context

This work extends the ladder resummation approach of Kaiser (2011, 2012) from simple contact interactions with only scattering length and effective range to arbitrary partial waves including coupled channels, using the full experimental phase shifts. It builds on the vacuum ladder resummation formalism developed by the same authors in Alarcón and Oller (2022). The results provide a rigorous low-density benchmark that can be used to constrain higher-density parameterizations of the nuclear equation of state, particularly for applications to neutron stars and gravitational wave physics.
