# Establishing a theory for deuteron induced surrogate reactions

> The goal of this work is to establish on firm grounds a theory for deuteron induced neutron capture reactions. This includes formulating elastic and inelastic breakup in a consistent manner. We describe this process both in post and prior form distorted wave Born approximation, and we apply our method to the 53Nb(d,p)X at Ed =15 MeV and 25 MeV, obtaining a good description of the data. We look at the various partial wave contributions, as well as elastic versus inelastic contributions. We also connect our formulation with transfer to neutron bound states.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a consistent theoretical framework for deuteron-induced surrogate reactions by demonstrating that the post-form (Austern-Vincent) and prior-form (Udagawa-Tamura) distorted wave Born approximation formulations are equivalent when no further approximations are made, resolving a long-standing controversy through the proper inclusion of a non-orthogonality term that arises naturally in the prior formulation.

## Method

The authors derived both post-form and prior-form DWBA amplitudes for elastic and non-elastic breakup in deuteron-induced reactions on a target nucleus, using a spectator approximation for the proton. They applied the prior-form formalism to the reaction $^{93}$Nb(d,p)X at deuteron beam energies of 15 MeV and 25.5 MeV, using optical model potentials from the Perey and Perey compilation for the deuteron and proton channels and a modified Koning-Delaroche global optical potential for the neutron-target interaction, with partial waves up to $l_p=15$ and $l_p=20$ respectively.

## Key numbers

- The method was applied to $^{93}$Nb(d,p)X at $E_d=15$ MeV and $E_d=25.5$ MeV, obtaining good agreement with experimental data from Mastroleo et al. (1990)
- The deuteron ground state wavefunction used a Woods-Saxon potential with radius $R_d=0.4$ fm and diffusivity $a_d=0.6$ fm, producing a zero-range constant $D_0 = -122.5$ MeV.fm$^{3/2}$
- Maximum partial wave $l_p$ used was 15 for $E_d=15$ MeV and 20 for $E_d=25.5$ MeV; contributions from neutron states with $l\geq 8$ were found to be very small
- The non-orthogonality term in the prior formulation was shown to be significant and at the heart of the post-prior controversy

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatments of Kerman and McVoy (1979), Udagawa and Tamura (1980), and Austern and Vincent (1981), which had generated a decade-long controversy over the equivalence of post and prior formulations. The paper resolves this controversy by showing the two formulations are equivalent when the non-orthogonality term is properly included in the prior form. The theory enables practical calculations for surrogate neutron capture reactions, which are important for astrophysics and stewardship science, and the authors call for elastic breakup measurements to further constrain the model.
