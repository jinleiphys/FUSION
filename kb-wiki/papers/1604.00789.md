# Evaluation of the forward Compton scattering off protons: II. Spin-dependent amplitude and observables

> The forward Compton scattering off the proton is determined by substituting the empirical total photoabsorption cross sections into dispersive sum rules. In addition to the spin-independent amplitude evaluated previously [Phys. Rev. D 92, 074031 (2015)], we obtain the spin-dependent amplitude over a broad energy range. The two amplitudes contain all the information about this process, and we, hence, can reconstruct the nonvanishing observables of the proton Compton scattering in the forward kinematics. The results are compared with predictions of chiral perturbation theory where available. The low-energy expansion of the spin-dependent Compton scattering amplitude yields the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and relations for the forward spin polarizabilities (FSPs) of the proton. Our evaluation provides an empirical verification of the GDH sum rule for the proton, and yields empirical values of the proton FSPs. For the GDH integral, we obtain $204.5(21.4)$ $μ$b, in agreement with the sum rule prediction: $204.784481(4)$ $μ$b. For the FSPs, we obtain: $γ_0=-92.9(10.5) \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^4$, and $\bar{γ_0}=48.4(8.2) \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^6$, improving on the accuracy of previous evaluations.

## Key claim

The authors provide the first complete model-independent evaluation of the forward Compton scattering off the proton by combining empirical total photoabsorption cross sections with dispersive sum rules, but note that the existing database for the helicity-difference cross section is limited to data between 0.2 and 2.9 GeV from MAMI and ELSA experiments, requiring a subtraction using the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule to reduce the influence of the experimentally unknown high-energy behavior.

## Method

The authors performed a smooth fit of the experimental helicity-difference total photoabsorption cross section on the proton, dividing the integration domain into three regions: low energy (from pion threshold to double-pion threshold, using MAID partial-wave analysis), medium energy (from double-pion threshold to 2 GeV, fitting six nonrelativistic Breit-Wigner resonances to GDH and A2 collaboration data), and high energy (above 2 GeV, using a Regge form with fixed parameters). They then evaluated the spin-dependent forward Compton amplitude using a subtracted dispersion relation and reconstructed the forward Compton scattering observables from both the spin-independent amplitude obtained previously and the spin-dependent amplitude obtained here.

## Key numbers

* GDH integral: 204.5 ± 21.4 μb, in agreement with the sum rule prediction of 204.784481(4) μb
* Forward spin polarizability γ0: -92.9 ± 10.5 × 10⁻⁶ fm⁴
* Forward spin polarizability γ̄0: 48.4 ± 8.2 × 10⁻⁶ fm⁶
* GDH Collaboration integral in the interval 0.2 < ν < 2.9 GeV: 254 ± 5 ± 12 μb, while the authors' fit of the same data yields 246.4 ± 6.8 μb
* Low-energy contribution to GDH integral: 43.6 ± 6.0 μb; medium-energy contribution: 175.7 ± 3.7 μb; high-energy contribution: -14.8 ± 19.9 μb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous evaluation of the spin-independent forward Compton amplitude (Gryniuk et al., 2015) to include the spin-dependent amplitude, providing the first complete empirical determination of both forward Compton amplitudes for the proton. The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Lensky et al. (2015) in baryon chiral perturbation theory and by McGovern et al. (2012) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, showing remarkable agreement at low energies. The evaluation enables future multipole analysis of proton Compton scattering data using the stringent constraints of forward scattering, and provides improved empirical values for the forward spin polarizabilities compared to previous evaluations by Pasquini et al. (2010).
