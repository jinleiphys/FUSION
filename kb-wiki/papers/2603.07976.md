# Bayesian neural network with autoencoder for model-based description of $α$-particle preformation factor

> $α$ decay is an important probe for studying the structure of heavy and superheavy nuclei, in which the $α$-particle preformation ($P_α$) is a key physical quantity for describing decay half-lives. This work develops a hybrid framework that integrates Bayesian neural networks with autoencoder (BNN-Auto), combined with the cosh potential (CPT), to systematically optimize the constraint and prediction of $P_α$. The model employs variational inference for probabilistic modeling of network weights, naturally providing robust uncertainty quantification for predictions, and utilizes an autoencoder to enhance the robustness of feature representation. Based on experimental data from 535 nuclei, the BNN-Auto method achieves relative improvements in the root mean square deviation ($σ_{\rm{RMS}}$) of $P_α$ prediction by $61.14\%$ on the training set and $54.49\%$ on the validation set. Further analysis reveals that the $P_α$ and half-life extracted by the model exhibit pronounced odd-even staggering and shell effects in isotopic chains with $Z=86-90$ and isotones with $N=124-128$ and $N=150-154$. Moreover, we successfully predict the $α$ decay half-lives of nuclei with $Z=120$ and observe a significant increase in the half-life near $N=184$, which verifies the shell effect of the predicted 'stable island'. This study not only provides a high-precision theoretical description for $α$ decay, but also offers a new machine learning perspective for exploring the structure of superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim
The hybrid BNN-Auto framework, when combined with the cosh potential (CPT) model, achieves relative improvements in the root mean square deviation of α-particle preformation factor prediction by 61.14% on the training set and 54.49% on the validation set for even-even nuclei, but these improvements vary significantly by nuclear type (odd-A: 57.96% training, 35.48% validation; odd-odd: 56.80% training, 66.74% validation), indicating that the method's performance is conditional on nuclear parity class.

## Method
The authors extracted experimental preformation factors Pα from the half-lives of 535 nuclei (159 even-even, 295 odd-A, 81 odd-odd) using the cosh potential (CPT) model within a two-potential approach. They constructed an eight-feature input vector (mass number A, neutron number N, proton number Z, Qα, angular momentum l, and three derived features λ1=A^(1/3), λ2=Z/√Qα, λ3=√(l(l+1))) and trained a Bayesian neural network with an autoencoder architecture using variational inference, with 10-fold cross-validation repeated 5 times over 1000 epochs. The model was optimized by minimizing a loss function combining prediction error, autoencoder reconstruction error, and KL divergence regularization, with predictions obtained via 20 stochastic forward passes for uncertainty quantification.

## Key numbers
- Dataset: 535 nuclei total, comprising 159 even-even, 295 odd-A, and 81 odd-odd nuclei
- Relative improvement in RMS deviation of log10Pα for even-even nuclei: 61.14% on training set, 54.49% on validation set
- Relative improvement for odd-A nuclei: 57.96% on training set, 35.48% on validation set
- Relative improvement for odd-odd nuclei: 56.80% on training set, 66.74% on validation set
- Overall relative improvement across all nuclei: 30.75% on training set, 35.37% on validation set
- Standard deviation of half-life residuals reduced from 0.418 (CPT-LSM) to 0.280 (CPT-BNN-Auto), a reduction of 33%
- Mean residual improved from 0.204 to 0.136
- Nuclear potential parameters: diffuseness a0=0.5958 fm, depth V0=192.42+31.059(Nd-Zd)/Ad MeV
- Radius parameter: R=1.28A^(1/3)-0.76+0.8A^(-1/3) fm
- Training hyperparameters: learning rate 10^(-4), weight decay 10^(-6), batch size 32, 1000 epochs

## Context
This work extends the CPT-LSM approach of Luo (2024) by replacing the least-squares fitting method with a Bayesian neural network that provides uncertainty quantification, addressing the limitation that the prior method could not be extended globally to all nuclei. The BNN-Auto framework builds on Bayesian neural network applications in nuclear physics by Utama (2015, 2016) and Neufcourt (2018, 2019), and integrates an autoencoder for robust feature representation. The method enables prediction of α-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei with Z=120, where experimental data are scarce, and reveals odd-even staggering and shell effects in isotopic chains with Z=86-90 and isotones with N=124-128 and N=150-154, consistent with known nuclear structure phenomena.
