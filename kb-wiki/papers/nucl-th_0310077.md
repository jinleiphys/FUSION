# Dilute Multi Alpha Cluster States in Nuclei

> Dilute multi $α$ cluster condensed states with spherical and axially deformed shapes are studied with the Gross-Pitaevskii equation and Hill-Wheeler equation, where the $α$ cluster is treated as a structureless boson. Applications to self-conjugate $4N$ nuclei show that the dilute $Nα$ states of $^{12}$C to $^{40}$Ca with $J^π=0^+$ appear in the energy region from threshold up to about 20 MeV, and the critical number of $α$ bosons that the dilute $Nα$ system can sustain as a self-bound nucleus is estimated roughly to be $N_{cr}\sim10$. We discuss the characteristics of the dilute $Nα$ states with emphasis on the $N$ dependence of their energies and rms radii.

# Dilute Multi Alpha Cluster States in Nuclei

## Key claim

Dilute multi-alpha cluster condensed states with spherical and axially deformed shapes exist in self-conjugate 4N nuclei from 12C to 40Ca, appearing in the energy region from the N-alpha threshold up to about 20 MeV, but the critical number of alpha bosons that such a dilute system can sustain as a self-bound nucleus is only roughly estimated at Ncr ~ 10, beyond which the Coulomb barrier becomes too low to confine the system.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary theoretical approaches: the Gross-Pitaevskii equation (a nonlinear Schrodinger-type mean-field equation) and the Hill-Wheeler equation, both treating the alpha particle as a structureless boson. For the Gross-Pitaevskii approach, they solved self-consistently for the S-wave single-alpha wave function using either a density-dependent alpha-alpha potential or a phenomenological two-body plus three-body alpha potential. For the Hill-Wheeler approach, they constructed a superposition of Gaussian basis states with axial deformation parameters, projected onto good angular momentum, and solved the resulting Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain states free from center-of-mass motion.

## Key numbers

- The critical number of alpha bosons for a self-bound dilute N-alpha system is estimated roughly as Ncr ~ 10 (corresponding to 40Ca).
- The calculated resonant energy of the 8Be ground state relative to the 2-alpha threshold is 92 keV, matching the experimental value of 92 keV.
- For the 0_2+ state of 12C, the calculated energy relative to the 3-alpha threshold is 0.38 MeV and the rms radius is 4.14 fm (using the density-dependent potential fitted to reproduce the experimental energy of 0.38 MeV and the Tohsaki et al. rms radius of 4.29 fm).
- The rms radius of the dilute 3-alpha and 4-alpha condensate states is about 4 fm, compared to about 2.7 fm for the ground states.
- The height of the Coulomb potential barrier for the 2-alpha system (8Be) is estimated to be 1 to 2 MeV.
- The dilute N-alpha states of 12C to 40Ca with J^pi = 0+ appear in the energy region from threshold up to about 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of alpha-particle condensation, previously proposed by Tohsaki et al. (2001) for 12C and 16O and by Funaki et al. (2002) for 8Be, to heavier self-conjugate 4N nuclei up to 40Ca. The authors build on the Ikeda threshold rule (1968) for cluster states appearing near breakup thresholds and on earlier work by Ropke et al. (1998) and Beyer et al. (2000) suggesting alpha condensation in low-density nuclear matter below one-fifth of saturation density. The study enables systematic investigation of how many alpha particles can be bound in a dilute nuclear system and predicts that the Coulomb barrier, which confines the alpha gas, becomes too weak beyond N=10 to sustain a bound state.
