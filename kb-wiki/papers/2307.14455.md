# Spatial orientation of the fission fragment intrinsic spins and their correlations

> New experimental and theoretical results obtained in 2021 made it acutely clear that more than 80 years after the discovery of nuclear fission we do not understand the generation and dynamics of fission fragment (FF) intrinsic spins well, in particular their magnitudes, their spatial orientation, and their correlations. The magnitude and orientation of the primary FFs have a crucial role in defining the angular distribution and correlation between the emitted prompt neutrons, and subsequent emission of statistical (predominantly E1) and stretched E2 γ-rays, and their correlations with the final fission fragments. Here we present detailed microscopic evaluations of the FF intrinsic spins, for both even- and odd-mass FFs, and of their spatial correlations. These point to a well-defined 3D FF intrinsic spin dynamics, characteristics absent in semi-phenomenological studies, due to the presence of the twisting spin modes, which artificially were suppressed in semi-phenomenological studies.

## Key claim

The microscopic time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations show that fission fragment (FF) intrinsic spins are not perpendicular to the fission axis, contrary to the central assumption of the semi-phenomenological FREYA model; instead, the twisting and tilting spin modes are active, leading to a well-defined 3D spin dynamics with non-zero projections of the FF spins onto the fission direction.

## Method

The authors performed fully microscopic TDDFT simulations extended to superfluid systems using the LISE package with the SeaLL1 functional, for the spontaneous fission of 252Cf and the neutron-induced fission of 236U. They applied exact angular momentum projection techniques to evaluate the probability distributions P(S_F, K_F) for each FF and the triple distribution P(Λ, S_H, S_L) using Pfaffian overlaps, from which they extracted the distributions of the angle θ_F between each FF spin and the fission axis and the opening angle φ_HL between the two FF intrinsic spins.

## Key numbers

- For 252Cf, the sum P(-1/2) + P(0) + P(1/2) ≈ 0.33, meaning the probability to find a FF with |K| ≥ 1 is 0.67.
- For 236U, the sum P(-1/2) + P(0) + P(1/2) ≈ 0.49, meaning the probability to find a FF with |K| ≥ 1 is 0.51.
- The distribution P(φ_HL) is almost uniform in the interval φ ∈ (40°, 160°), with pronounced decays close to 0° and 180°.
- The probability that the opening angle φ_HL is larger than 90° is about 0.53, indicating a slight preference for bending over wriggling modes.
- The range of light FF intrinsic spins S_L is wider than the range of heavy FF intrinsic spins S_H.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic TDDFT studies of FF intrinsic spins reported by Bulgac (2021) and Bulgac (2022b, 2022e), which first showed that the heavy FF has a smaller average spin than the light FF, opposite to the prior consensus from Wilhelmy (1972) and the FREYA model (Randrup 2009, Vogt 2021). The present results starkly contradict the FREYA model assumption that FF spins are perpendicular to the fission axis (Randrup 2009, Vogt 2021, Randrup 2021, Randrup 2022), and instead confirm the activity of twisting and tilting modes conjectured by Nix (1965) and Moretto (1980, 1989). The predictions are expected to be tested by future experiments measuring the angular distribution of stretched E2 γ-rays relative to the fission direction, as discussed by Sobotka (2023).
