# A possible quantum fluid-dynamical approach to vortex motion in nuclei

> The essential point of Bohr-Mottelson theory is to assume a irrotational flow. As was already suggested by Marumori and Watanabe, the internal rotational motion, i.e., the vortex motion, however, may exist also in nuclei. So, we have a necessity of taking the vortex motion into consideration. In a classical fluid dynamics, there are various ways to treat the internal rotational velocity. The Clebsch representation, v(x) = -\nabla ϕ(x) + λ(x) \nabla ψ(x) (ϕ; velocity potential, λand ψ: Clebsch parameters) is very powerful and has an advantage deriving equations of fluid motion from a Lagransian. Making the best use of the advantage, Kronig-Thellung, Ziman and Ito obtained a Hamiltonian including the internal rotational motion, the vortex motion, through the term λ(x) \nabla ψ(x). Going to quantum fluid dynamics, Ziman and Thellung finally derived a roton spectrum of liquid Helium II postulated by Landau. Is it possible to apply such the manner to a description of the collective vortex motion in nuclei? The description of such a collective motion has never been treated in the Bohr-Mottelson model (BMM) for a long time. In this paper, we will investigate a possibility of describing the vortex motion in nuclei basing on the theories of Ziman and Ito together with Marumori's work.

## Key claim

This paper investigates whether the quantum fluid-dynamical methods developed by Ziman, Thellung, and Ito for describing vortex (internal rotational) motion in liquid helium can be applied to collective vortex motion in nuclei, a type of motion that has not been treated in the Bohr-Mottelson model (BMM) which assumes irrotational flow.

## Method

The authors extend the unitary transformation approach of Marumori to include Clebsch variables (phi, lambda, psi) that represent the velocity field with a rotational component, v = -nabla phi + lambda nabla psi. They transform an A-particle Hamiltonian into a collective Hamiltonian containing phonon (irrotational) and roton (vortex) parts plus their interaction, then apply the Ziman transformation to express the roton Hamiltonian in terms of boson operators b_lambda_mu and b*_lambda_mu. The angular momentum operator for the rotational flow is derived and expressed in spherical tensor form using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and Racah coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The roton Hamiltonian H_rot is expressed with a prefactor of hbar^2/(8 rho_0), where rho_0 is the equilibrium density
- The expansion of the Clebsch fields uses spherical harmonics with radial factors (r/R_0)^lambda, where R_0 is the nuclear equilibrium radius
- The rotational angular momentum operator hat J_k is derived with a prefactor of 1/2 and involves sums over angular momentum quantum numbers lambda, mu, L, M, L', M' with Racah coefficients W(lambda 1 L L'; lambda lambda')

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestions of Marumori and Watanabe (1955-1958) that internal rotational (vortex) motion may exist in nuclei alongside the irrotational flow assumed in the Bohr-Mottelson model (BMM) (Bohr 1952-1953). The authors build directly on the quantum fluid-dynamical methods of Ziman (1953), Thellung (1953-1956), and Ito (1955), who derived roton spectra for liquid helium II (Landau 1941). The approach aims to resolve the discrepancy noted by Bohr, Mottelson, and collaborators (1956) that the BMM gives moments of inertia smaller than empirical values, by incorporating vortex motion that could increase the moment of inertia through cooperation between rotational and irrotational motions.
