# Dynamical development of proton cumulants and correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV from a multiphase transport model

> Higher-order cumulants of the distributions of conserved charges, such as net-baryon number, are sensitive to the quantum chromodynamics(QCD) phase transition and the QCD critical point. We calculate the cumulants and correlation functions of proton, antiproton, and net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV using a multiphase transport model(AMPT). The AMPT model can basically describe the trends of cumulants, cumulant ratios, (normalized) correlation functions of the proton and net-proton measured by the STAR experiment. The multiproton (baryon) correlations in the AMPT model are consistent with the expectation from baryon number conservation. We demonstrate that multiproton (baryon) correlations suffer the dynamical evolution of heavy-ion collisions. Our results provide a baseline for searching for the possible critical behaviors at the critical end point in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The AMPT model can describe the trends of proton and net-proton cumulants and correlation functions measured by STAR, but the model does not include critical fluctuations at the QCD critical point, so its results serve as a baseline for searching for critical behavior rather than evidence for it.

## Method

The authors used the charge-conserving version of the AMPT model to simulate 70 million Au+Au collision events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV. They calculated cumulants \(C_n\) (n=1-4) and integrated multiparticle correlation functions \(\kappa_n\) for proton, antiproton, and net-proton multiplicity distributions within kinematic cuts of \(0.4 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|y| < 0.5\), applying a centrality bin width correction (CBWC) to suppress volume fluctuations. They compared results with STAR data and examined the evolution of these observables across four stages: initial partonic state, after parton cascade, after hadronization via quark coalescence, and final hadronic state after rescatterings and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Cumulant \(C_4\) for protons in the most central bin without CBWC was \(-500.31 \pm 8.33\), demonstrating that the centrality bin width effect contributes significantly to higher-order cumulants.
- The AMPT results for \(\kappa \sigma^2\) (\(C_4/C_2\)) of protons and net-protons were consistent with the Poisson baseline within large errors, showing no nonmonotonic energy dependence.
- The normalized two-proton correlation \(\kappa_2/\kappa_1\) was negative and decreased monotonically with increasing rapidity acceptance in 0-5% central collisions.
- The four-proton correlation \(\kappa_4\) from AMPT events was very small, consistent with zero, across all centralities.

## Context

This work extends previous viscous hydrodynamic simulations of proton and baryon cumulants by Vovchenko (2021) to a transport-model framework, specifically using the charge-conserving AMPT model. The results provide a non-critical baseline for interpreting the nonmonotonic energy dependence of \(\kappa \sigma^2\) observed by STAR (2021) with a significance of 3.1\(\sigma\), which may indicate critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point. The study enables future comparisons between transport-model baselines and experimental data to isolate possible critical behavior in heavy-ion collisions.
