# The S- and P-wave low-lying baryons in the chiral quark model

> The $1S$, $2S$, $1P$ and $2P$ states of light baryons are investigated within the chiral quark model, paying particular attention to the well-known order reverse problem of $1P$ and $2S$ states. Besides a nonperturbative linear-screened confining interaction and a perturbative one-gluon exchange between quarks, we incorporate the Goldstone-boson exchanges taking into account not only the full octet of pseudoscalar mesons but also the scalar one. The scalar meson exchange potential simulates the higher order multi-pion exchange terms that appear in the chiral Lagrangian and its omission has been already admitted as a deficiency of the original model in describing, for instance, the $ρ-ω$ splitting. The numerical approach to the three body bound state problem is the so-called Gaußian expansion method, which is able to get a precision as good as Faddeev calculations. With a set of parameters fixed to different hadron and hadron-hadron observables, we find that the chiral potential could play an important role towards the issue on the mass order reverse problem. We extend our calculation to the $qqQ$ and $qQQ$ sectors (with $q$ representing a light $u$-, $d$-, or $s$-quark and $Q$ denoting the charm quark or the bottom one) in which many new states have been recently observed. Some tentative assignments are done attending to the agreement between theoretical and experimental masses; however, we admit that other sources of information are needed in order to make strong claims about the nature of these states.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model, when including the full octet of scalar meson exchanges in addition to pseudoscalar Goldstone-boson exchanges, can invert the mass ordering of the nucleon's first radial excitation (2S, positive parity) below its first orbital excitation (1P, negative parity), but only when the chiral coupling constant and Goldstone-boson mass scales are slightly augmented (sets IV and V); with other parameter sets the ordering remains reversed relative to experiment.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for baryons using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM), which achieves precision comparable to Faddeev calculations. The Hamiltonian included a linear-screened confining potential, a perturbative one-gluon exchange (OGE) interaction, and Goldstone-boson exchange potentials comprising the full octet of pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) plus the full octet of scalar mesons (σ, a₀, κ, f₀). Five different parameter sets were used, with sets II, IV, and V employing a scale-dependent strong coupling constant αₛ(μ).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Garcilazo et al. (2001, 2003) that used a similar chiral quark model but omitted scalar meson exchanges and employed a linear confining potential instead of the linear-screened form. The inclusion of the full scalar octet (σ, a₀, κ, f₀) was previously shown to improve meson phenomenology, such as the ρ-ω splitting by Vijande et al. (2009). The results support the conclusion of Glozman et al. (1997) that Goldstone-boson exchange interactions are responsible for inverting the 1P-2S mass ordering in the nucleon spectrum, a problem unresolved in the earlier relativized quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986). The calculations also provide predictions for heavy-flavor baryons (qqQ and qQQ sectors) where many new states have been recently observed, though the authors note that additional information from widths and decay patterns is needed for definitive assignments.
