# On a test of the modified BCS theory performance in the picket fence model

> The errors in the arguments, numerical results, and conclusions in the paper "Test of a modified BCS theory performance in the picket fence model" [Nucl. Phys. A 822 (2009) 1] by V.Yu. Ponomarev and A.I. Vdovin are pointed out. Its repetitions of already published material are also discussed.

# On a test of the modified BCS theory performance in the picket fence model

## Key claim

The paper "Test of a modified BCS theory performance in the picket fence model" by Ponomarev and Vdovin contains incorrect statements, erroneous numerical results, and invalid conclusions, while largely duplicating already published material. The present comment demonstrates that the test paper's claim that the MBCS theory only works for the specific case of Omega = N+1 is false, as counterexamples with smooth MBCS gaps exist for many other choices of Omega.

## Method

The author reanalyzes the picket fence model (PFM) using exact canonical ensemble (CE) and grand canonical ensemble (GCE) calculations, comparing the chemical potential, thermodynamic entropy, and pairing gap from the modified BCS (MBCS) theory against exact results. The exact CE results are obtained by constructing the partition function from the exact eigenvalues of the pairing Hamiltonian for fixed particle number N, while the GCE results allow particle exchange with a heat bath. The author also presents new MBCS calculations for the PFM with N=10 particles and various numbers of levels Omega = N+k, using different single-particle energy spacings and pairing strengths G = 0.55 MeV and G = 0.6 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The chemical potential mu from exact CE calculations for the PFM with N=Omega=10 and G=0.4 MeV shows strong temperature dependence, contradicting the test paper's claim that it should remain at zero.
- The test paper's claimed "exact thermodynamic entropy" at T <= 0.1 MeV is wrong by more than one order of magnitude compared to the correct exact GCE results.
- For the half-filled PFM with level distance epsilon = 1 MeV, the maximal temperature T_M decreases from T_M ~ 24 MeV for N=Omega=100 to T_M ~ 0.7 MeV for N=Omega=6.
- Adding one extra level (Omega = N+1) extends the temperature region of applicability of MBCS to T <= 5-6 MeV for all N (with epsilon = 1 MeV and G = 0.4 MeV).
- Thirteen MBCS gaps shown for N=10 with Omega = N+k (k = 0 through 8 for G=0.6 MeV, and k = 0 through 3 for G=0.55 MeV) are all smooth functions of T from 0 to 5 MeV, providing counterexamples to the test paper's claims.

## Context

This comment extends the ongoing debate between the author and Ponomarev/Vdovin regarding the validity of the modified BCS (MBCS) theory, which was developed between 2001 and 2007 to restore the unitarity relation for the generalized single-particle density matrix at finite temperature. The MBCS theory had been previously tested in the exactly solvable picket fence model (Richardson model) by Dang (2007, 2008) and criticized by Ponomarev and Vdovin (2008, 2009). The present work points out that the test paper by Ponomarev and Vdovin (2009) repeats arguments from their earlier reply (2008) and contains errors in the chemical potential treatment and entropy calculations. The comment also notes that the Lipkin-Nogami method (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964) yields a temperature-dependent chemical potential, and that the self-consistent RPA (SCQRPA) can describe low excitation energies but not the splitting of high-lying levels due to configuration mixing (Yuz 2008).
