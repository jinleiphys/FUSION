# Three-body dynamics in one dimension: A test model for the 3-Nucleon system with irreducible pionic diagrams

> We formulate the three-body problem in one dimension in terms of the (Faddeev-type) integral equation approach. As an application, we develop a spinless, one-dimensional (1-D) model that mimics three-nucleon dynamics in one dimension. Using simple two-body potentials that reproduce the deuteron binding, we obtain that the three-body system binds at about 7.5 MeV. We then consider two types of residual pionic corrections in the dynamical equation; one related to the 2PI-exchange three-body diagram, the other to the 1PI-exchange three-body diagram. We find that the first contribution can produce an additional binding effect of about 0.9 MeV. The second term produces smaller binding effects, which are, however, dependent on the uncertainty in the off-shell extrapolation of the two-body t-matrix. This presents interesting analogies with what occurs in three dimensions. The paper also discusses the general three-particle quantum scattering problem, for motion restricted to the full line.

# Three-Body Dynamics in One Dimension: A Test Model for the 3-Nucleon System with Irreducible Pionic Diagrams

## Key claim

The authors develop a one-dimensional, spinless three-body model that mimics three-nucleon dynamics, finding that irreducible pionic corrections, specifically the 2π-exchange three-body diagram, produce an additional binding effect of about 0.9 MeV, while the 1π-exchange diagram produces smaller binding effects that depend on the uncertainty in the off-shell extrapolation of the two-body t-matrix, presenting analogies with what occurs in three dimensions.

## Method

The authors formulate the three-body problem in one dimension using the Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equation approach, reduced to a symmetrized Lovelace-type equation via a finite-rank (Unitary Pole Expansion) representation of the two-body potentials. They construct three different one-dimensional two-body potentials of exponential form that reproduce the deuteron binding, then add two types of irreducible pionic correction terms to the driving term of the AGS equation: the 2π-exchange (TPE3) diagram and the 1π-exchange (OPE3) diagram. The bound-state energies are obtained by solving the homogeneous version of the integral equation as a Sturmian eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- The three-body system binds at about 7.5 MeV using simple two-body potentials that reproduce the deuteron binding.
- The 2π-exchange three-body diagram produces an additional binding effect of about 0.9 MeV.
- The nucleon mass is set to m_N c^2 = 938.27 MeV and the pion mass to m_π c^2 = 134.98 MeV.
- The πN amplitude is parametrized with a scaling factor c_1 = -15, chosen to be approximately consistent with the ratio between observed scattering lengths of the πN and NN systems.
- The range parameter β of the attractive part of the two-body potentials is the inverse of the pion Compton wavelength: β = m_π c / ħ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Canton 1998, Canton et al. 2001) that reduces the field-theoretic problem of pion-three-nucleon dynamics to an approximate set of integral equations for the coupled πNNN-NNN system. The one-dimensional model serves as a simplified test environment for investigating irreducible three-nucleon-force (3NF) contributions that cannot be effectively described by standard nucleon-nucleon potentials, such as those from Fujita and Miyazawa (1957) and subsequent 3NF models. The results enable further study of the 1π-exchange 3NF term, which has been shown in realistic three-dimensional calculations (Canton and Schat 2001, Canton et al. 2002) to have the potential to address the A_y puzzle in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering.
