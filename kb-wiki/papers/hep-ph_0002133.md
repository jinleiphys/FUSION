# Spin polarisabilities of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

> We present a calculation of the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, and we give results for the four spin polarisabilities. No low-energy constants, except for the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleon, enter at this order. For forward scattering the fourth-order piece of the spin polarisability of the proton turns out to be almost twice the size of the leading piece, with the opposite sign. This leads to the conclusion that no prediction can currently be made for this quantity. For backward scattering the fourth-order contribution is much smaller than the third-order piece which is dominated by the anomalous scattering, and so cannot explain the discrepancy between the CPT result and the current best experimental determination.

# Spin polarisabilities of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

## Key claim

The fourth-order (NLO) contribution to the forward spin polarisability of the proton is almost twice the size of the leading third-order piece and has the opposite sign, leading to the conclusion that no prediction can currently be made for this quantity. For backward scattering, the fourth-order contribution is much smaller than the third-order piece dominated by anomalous scattering and cannot explain the discrepancy between the chiral perturbation theory result and the current best experimental determination.

## Method

The authors calculate the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) using the gauge \(A_0=0\) (equivalently \(v\cdot\epsilon=0\)). They work in the Breit frame and evaluate the one-loop diagrams contributing at NLO (Fig. 2), which involve pion-nucleon loops with vertices from the second-order Lagrangian. No undetermined low-energy constants enter at this order except for the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The leading (third-order) HBCPT result for the forward spin polarisability \(\gamma_0\) is \(4.5 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\) for both proton and neutron, coming entirely from \(\pi N\) loops.
- The NLO contribution to \(\gamma_0\) is \(-(6.9 + 1.5\tau_3) \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\), where \(\tau_3 = +1\) for the proton and \(\tau_3 = -1\) for the neutron.
- The experimental value for the backward scattering combination \(\gamma_\pi = \gamma_1 - \gamma_5\) is \(-27.1 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\) with experimental and theoretical errors of about 10% each.
- The HBCPT result for \(\gamma_\pi\) including the \(\Delta\) is \(-36.7 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\), of which \(-43.5\) is the anomalous contribution, 4.6 is the \(\pi N\) piece, and 2.2 comes from including the \(\Delta\).
- The NLO contribution to \(\gamma_\pi\) is \((2.7 - 1.1\tau_3) \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\).
- The electroproduction analysis of Sandorfi et al. gives \(\gamma_0^p = -1.3 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\) and \(\gamma_0^n = -0.4 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\); the more recent analysis of Drechsel et al. gives \(\gamma_0^p = -0.6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends previous HBCPT calculations of nucleon spin polarisabilities by Bernard et al. (1992) and Hemmert et al., who included an explicit \(\Delta\) resonance. The leading third-order calculation gave \(\gamma_0 = 4.5\) for both proton and neutron, while the \(\Delta\) contribution was estimated to change the sign. The present NLO calculation shows that even without the \(\Delta\), the chiral expansion for the polarisabilities has not converged, as the NLO contributions are disappointingly large. The results are in agreement with those of the Julich group (Gellas et al.) after including the one-particle-reducible diagram. The calculations enable comparison with ongoing experimental programs at MAMI (Mainz) and Bonn, and with dispersion relation analyses of electroproduction data.
