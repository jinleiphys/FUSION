# Simple function form for n+208Pb total cross section between 5 and 600 MeV

> The total cross section for neutron scattering from 208Pb with energies between 5 and 600 MeV has been analyzed extending a previously defined simple function of three parameters to reveal a Ramsauer-like effect throughout the whole energy range. This effect can be parametrized in a simple way so that it may be anticipated that the complete function prescription will apply for total cross sections from other nuclei.

## Key claim
The total neutron cross section for n+208Pb between 5 and 600 MeV can be reproduced by a simple three-parameter function form combined with a Ramsauer-like oscillatory correction, but the quality of fit depends on how the parameter l0(E) is adjusted to capture the Ramsauer structure below 100 MeV.

## Method
The authors used a previously defined three-parameter function form for partial total cross sections, with parameters l0, a, and epsilon, to fit g-folding optical potential calculations for n+208Pb. They then fixed a and epsilon as parabolic functions of energy and adjusted l0(E) to fit measured total cross-section data, incorporating a Ramsauer model correction term alpha(E) cos(beta(E)) to account for oscillatory structure.

## Key numbers
- The parameter a is given by a = 1.29 + 0.00250 E - 1.76 x 10^-6 E^2, with E in MeV.
- The parameter epsilon is given by epsilon = -1.47 - 0.00234 E + 4.16 x 10^-6 E^2.
- The l0 parameter for energies above 250 MeV is approximated as a straight line; the full energy-dependent form is l0^th(E) = 0.0384 E + 16.28 - 11.22 [1 - E/132.9] e^{-0.0164 E}.
- The Ramsauer correction parameter alpha(E) is bounded by +/- 0.2 and decreases monotonically with energy.
- The fractional deviation from Wick's limit never exceeds 2 percent.
- The Ramsauer effect has a sinusoidal variation with log10(E) and a wavelength of approximately 0.7 log10(E).

## Context
This work extends the simple function form previously used by the authors for proton reaction cross sections to neutron total cross sections for 208Pb, and incorporates the Ramsauer model used by Dietrich et al. (2003) to describe oscillatory structure. The approach contrasts with global optical potential parameter sets such as those of Koning and Delaroche (2003), and enables estimation of total reaction cross sections via Wick's limit without full optical potential calculations. The results suggest the method should be applicable to other nuclei given the similarity in shape of total cross section data.
