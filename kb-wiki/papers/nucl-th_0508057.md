# Dipole resonances in light neutron-rich nuclei studied with time-dependent calculations of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> In order to study isovector dipole response of neutron-rich nuclei, we have applied a time-dependent method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The dipole resonances in Be, B and C isotopes have been investigated. In $^{10}$Be, $^{15}$B, $^{16}$C, collective modes of the vibration between a core and valence neutrons cause soft resonances at the excitation energy $E_x=10-15$ MeV below the giant dipole resonance(GDR). In $^{16}$C, we found that a remarkable peak at $E_x=14$ MeV corresponds to coherent motion of four valence neutrons against a $^{12}$C core, while the GDR arises from the core vibration in the $E_x >20$ MeV region. In $^{17}$B and $^{18}$C, the dipole strengths in the low energy region decline compared with those in $^{15}$B and $^{16}$C. We also discuss the energy weighted sum rule for the $E1$ transitions.

## Key claim

The authors find that in neutron-rich Be, B, and C isotopes, soft dipole resonances appear at excitation energies of 10-15 MeV, below the giant dipole resonance (GDR), and that these soft resonances arise from collective vibrations between a core and valence neutrons; however, the calculated excitation energies are systematically overestimated by about 4 MeV compared to experimental data, likely due to the simple AMD wave function underestimating surface diffuseness.

## Method

The authors applied a time-dependent version of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to calculate the isovector dipole response of neutron-rich nuclei. They first obtained the ground state wave function by energy variation of the AMD wave function, then boosted the system instantaneously with an external dipole field and simulated the time evolution using the time-dependent variational principle. The dipole transition strength was obtained by Fourier transform of the time-dependent expectation value of the dipole operator, with a smoothing parameter introduced to simulate resonance widths.

## Key numbers

- In $^{16}$C, a remarkable peak at $E_x=14$ MeV corresponds to coherent motion of four valence neutrons against a $^{12}$C core
- In $^{10}$Be, $^{15}$B, and $^{16}$C, soft resonances appear at $E_x=10-15$ MeV below the GDR
- The GDR in $^{12}$C lies at $E_x=26$ MeV and in $^{18}$O at $E_x=28$ MeV, about 4 MeV higher than observed GDR peaks
- In $^{15}$B, the soft resonance in $E_x=10-17$ MeV exhausts 16% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule value
- The enhancement factor $\kappa$ for the energy-weighted sum rule is 0.71 for $^{12}$C, 0.74 for $^{18}$O, and 0.6-0.7 for neutron-rich Be, B, and C isotopes
- In $^{18}$O, the calculated strength $S(E1;E_x<15$ MeV)/$S(TRK)=3\%$ and $S(E1;E_x<17$ MeV)/$S(TRK)=6\%$
- In $^{10}$Be, a peak at $E_x<5$ MeV has strength $B(E1)=0.06$ $e^2$fm$^2$, corresponding to the known $1^-$ state at 5.96 MeV
- The GDR in $^{14}$Be splits into two peaks at $E_x=15$ MeV and $E_x>25$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of ground state properties in Be, B, and C isotopes (Kanada-En'yo 2003, 2004) to dipole excitations, and complements shell model calculations by Suzuki and collaborators (2000) that suggested coherent neutron transitions enhance strengths at $E_x=10-15$ MeV in C isotopes. The time-dependent AMD method is analogous to time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approaches but can describe cluster aspects important in light unstable nuclei. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from photonuclear reactions and with TDHF+absorbing boundary condition calculations by Nakatsukasa and collaborators (2004), which include continuum effects that the present bound-state approximation lacks.
