# A hint at quark deconfinement in 200 GeV Au+Au data at RHIC

> We give the emission function of the axially symmetric Buda-Lund hydro model and present its simultaneous, high quality fits to identified particle spectra, two-particle Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations and charged particle pseudorapidity distributions as measured by BRAHMS and PHENIX in 0-30 % central, $\sqrt{s_{\NN}} = 200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The best fit is achieved when the most central region of the particle emitting volume is superheated to $T_0 = 200 \pm 9$ MeV $ \ge T_c =172 \pm 3$ MeV, a preliminary, 3 $σ$ effect.

## Key claim
The central temperature of the particle-emitting source in 0-30% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV is found to be T0 = 200 ± 9 MeV, which exceeds the lattice QCD critical temperature Tc = 172 ± 3 MeV at the 3σ level, providing a preliminary hint at quark deconfinement rather than a definitive observation.

## Method
The authors performed simultaneous fits of the axially symmetric Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model to identified particle transverse mass spectra from PHENIX, two-particle Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlation radii from PHENIX, and charged particle pseudorapidity distributions from BRAHMS in 0-30% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The emission function includes a core-halo picture with a Gaussian freeze-out time distribution, a three-dimensional relativistic flow characterized by transverse and longitudinal Hubble constants, and spatial profiles for temperature and fugacity that are expanded to second order.

## Key numbers
- Central temperature T0 = 200 ± 9 MeV for 0-30% central Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Critical temperature from lattice QCD Tc = 172 ± 3 MeV (Fodor and Katz, 2004)
- Surface temperature Te = 127 ± 13 MeV for the 200 GeV data
- Net baryochemical potential μB = 61 ± 40 MeV for the 200 GeV data
- Geometrical radius RG = 13.2 ± 1.3 fm for the 200 GeV data
- Surface radius Rs = 11.6 ± 1.0 fm for the 200 GeV data
- Mean transverse flow at surface radius ⟨u_t′⟩ = 1.5 ± 0.1 for the 200 GeV data
- Mean freeze-out time τ0 = 5.7 ± 0.2 fm/c for the 200 GeV data
- Duration of particle emission Δτ = 1.9 ± 0.5 fm/c for the 200 GeV data
- Width of space-time rapidity distribution Δη = 3.1 ± 0.1 for the 200 GeV data
- Fit quality χ²/NDF = 132/208 for the 200 GeV data
- For comparison at √s_NN = 130 GeV (0-5% central): T0 = 214 ± 7 MeV, exceeding Tc by more than 5σ

## Context
This work extends the Buda-Lund hydro model analysis previously applied to 130 GeV Au+Au data (Ster et al., 2003) to the higher energy of 200 GeV at RHIC, using final published data from BRAHMS and PHENIX. The critical temperature is taken from lattice QCD calculations by Fodor and Katz (2004), which found Tc ≈ 172 ± 3 MeV independent of baryochemical potential in the 0 ≤ μB ≤ 300 MeV region. The results provide a preliminary indication for quark deconfinement at 200 GeV, though with lower significance than the 130 GeV case, and enable future studies that include STAR and PHOBOS data as well as centrality-dependent HBT measurements to clarify the picture.
