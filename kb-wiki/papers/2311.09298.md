# Scattering phase shifts from a quantum computer

> We calculate two-body scattering phase shifts on a quantum computer using a leading order short-range effective field theory Hamiltonian. The algorithm combines the variational quantum eigensolver and the quantum subspace expansion. As an example, we consider scattering in the deuteron $^3$S$_1$ partial wave. We calculate scattering phase shifts with a quantum simulator and on real hardware. We also study how noise impacts these calculations and discuss noise mitigation required to extend our work to larger quantum processing units. With current hardware, up to five superconducting qubits can produce acceptable results, and larger calculations will require a significant noise reduction.

## Key claim

The authors compute two-body scattering phase shifts on a quantum computer by combining the variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) with the quantum subspace expansion, but the method is limited to small systems: with current superconducting hardware, only up to five qubits produce acceptable results, and larger calculations require a significant noise reduction.

## Method

The authors use a leading-order short-range effective field theory Hamiltonian for the deuteron 3S1 partial wave, mapped onto a finite harmonic oscillator basis. The VQE algorithm finds the ground state, then the quantum subspace expansion generates excited states by applying single-particle excitation operators to the ground state and diagonalizing the resulting Hamiltonian matrix classically. The positive-energy eigenvalues are related to scattering phase shifts through a finite-volume method that imposes Dirichlet boundary conditions at an effective hard-wall radius L, which is determined from the kinetic energy operator eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant V0 = -5.68658111 MeV, adjusted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy in the infinite harmonic oscillator limit
- Harmonic oscillator frequency hbar-omega = 7 MeV used for the primary calculation
- Six additional interactions considered with oscillator frequencies hbar-omega = 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10 MeV, corresponding to momentum cutoffs Lambda = 128, 140, 152, 162, 172, 181 MeV respectively
- Three-qubit results on the ibmq_jakarta QPU agree with analytical results; four-qubit results are accurate at low energies; five-qubit results are inaccurate
- A transition occurs at approximately N = 5 qubits beyond which quantum computations become too limited by noise
- An order-of-magnitude reduction in noise is required for computations on more than six qubits

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume method for scattering phase shifts developed by Heller (1974) and More (2013) to quantum computing platforms, building on previous nuclear physics quantum computing studies such as Dumitrescu (2018) who calculated the deuteron binding energy. The approach contrasts with classical ab initio calculations of nuclear scattering phase shifts by Nollett (2007), Quaglioni (2008), Shirokov (2009), and Elhatisari (2015), which are computationally expensive. The results suggest that error-corrected quantum computing could be advantageous for such calculations in the future, but current hardware limitations require significant noise reduction before larger systems become tractable.
