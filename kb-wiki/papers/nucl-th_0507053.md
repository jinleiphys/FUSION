# Nuclear response functions in homogeneous matter with finite range effective interactions

> The question of nuclear response functions in a homogeneous medium is examined. A general method for calculating response functions in the random phase approximation (RPA) with exchange is presented. The method is applicable for finite-range nuclear interactions. Examples are shown in the case of symmetric nuclear matter described by a Gogny interaction. It is found that the convergence of the results with respect to the multipole truncation is quite fast. Various approximation schemes such as the Landau approximation, or the Landau approximation for the exchange terms only, are discussed in comparison with the exact results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for calculating nuclear response functions in homogeneous matter within the random phase approximation (RPA) with exchange, applicable to finite-range effective interactions like the Gogny force, and demonstrates that convergence of the multipole expansion is fast enough that only a few partial waves are needed for accurate results even at momentum transfers up to the Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors expand the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole Green's function onto a basis of spherical harmonics, transforming the integral equation into a set of coupled integral equations for the multipole components of the Green's function. They solve these equations by matrix inversion on a momentum grid, using the Gogny force D1S as the finite-range effective interaction in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature. The method is applied to calculate RPA response functions in the four spin-isospin channels, and the results are compared with the Landau-Migdal approximation and a proposed Landau Approximation For Exchange Term (LAFET) where only the exchange part of the particle-hole interaction is approximated.

## Key numbers

- For momentum transfer \(q = 27\) MeV (\(\simeq k_F/10\)), convergence of the response function \(\chi^{(0,0)}\) is reached for \(L_{max}=1\) (solid and dashed curves indistinguishable in the figure).
- For momentum transfer \(q = 270\) MeV (\(\simeq k_F\)), convergence is reached for \(L_{max}=3\).
- Symmetry parameters \(s_r\) and \(s_i\) (measuring deviation from expected \(\omega\)-symmetry) are below 1% for \(L_{max}=3\) at both \(q=27\) MeV (\(s_r=0.06\times10^{-2}\), \(s_i=0.12\times10^{-2}\)) and \(q=270\) MeV (\(s_r=0.94\times10^{-2}\), \(s_i=0.85\times10^{-2}\)).
- The dimensionless multipoles \(\tilde{V}_L^{(0,0)}\) span the range (-2:1) for \(L=0\), (-1:0) for \(L=1\), (-0.5:0) for \(L=2\), and (-0.3:0) for \(L=3\); beyond \(L=4\) the multipoles are practically negligible.
- The first Landau dimensionless parameters for the Gogny D1S interaction in the \((0,0)\) channel are \(F_0=-0.38\), \(F_1=-0.91\), and \(F_2=-0.33\).
- Numerical grid parameters: for \(q=k_F/10\), \(\Delta k=0.017\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(k_{max}=1.71\) fm\(^{-1}\); for \(q=k_F\), \(\Delta k=0.031\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(k_{max}=3.14\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA studies of nuclear matter with Gogny forces, which were limited to the long-wavelength limit (Gogny 1977) or used the Landau-Migdal approximation, by providing a method valid for all momentum transfers. The approach enables accurate calculations of response functions for processes such as neutrino propagation in nuclear matter or electron scattering, where finite momentum transfers are important. The proposed LAFET scheme offers a computationally efficient alternative to full RPA calculations for extensive studies, improving upon the standard Landau approximation while preserving its simplicity.
