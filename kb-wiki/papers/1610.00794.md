# Permanent Electric Dipole Moments of Single-, Two-, and Three-Nucleon Systems

> A nonzero electric dipole moment (EDM) of the neutron, proton, deuteron or helion, in fact, of any finite system necessarily involves the breaking of a symmetry, either by the presence of external fields (i.e. electric fields leading to the case of induced EDMs) or explicitly by the breaking of the discrete parity and time-reflection symmetries in the case of permanent EDMs. We discuss two theorems describing these phenomena and report about the cosmological motivation for an existence of CP breaking beyond what is generated by the Kobayashi-Maskawa mechanism in the Standard Model and what this might imply for the permanent electric dipole moments of the nucleon and light nuclei by estimating a window of opportunity for physics beyond what is currently known. Recent - and in the case of the deuteron even unpublished - results for the relevant matrix elements of nuclear EDM operators are presented and the relevance for disentangling underlying New Physics sources are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) measured in the window \(10^{-24} \, e\,\text{cm} > |d_{\text{N}}| \gtrsim 10^{-30} \, e\,\text{cm}\) would be a clear signal for new physics beyond the Kobayashi-Maskawa mechanism of the Standard Model, but this evidence is circumstantial and by no means compulsory, as it could arise from either genuine New Physics or a sufficiently large QCD \(\bar\theta\) term.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical foundations of permanent EDMs in finite quantum systems, establishing two theorems that link nonzero EDMs to the explicit breaking of parity (\(P\)) and time-reversal (\(T\)) symmetries. They then employ chiral effective field theory (EFT) to compute the leading \(P\)- and \(T\)-violating contributions to the EDMs of single-, two-, and three-nucleon systems, including the neutron, proton, deuteron, and helion, by matching dimension-six operators from beyond-the-Standard-Model physics to hadronic degrees of freedom at the chiral scale. The analysis incorporates the QCD \(\theta\) term, quark EDMs, quark chromo-EDMs, the Weinberg gluon term, and four-quark operators, with results for the deuteron presented as unpublished.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear magneton scale is \(\mu_{\text{N}} = e/(2 m_{\text{p}}) \sim 10^{-14} \, e\,\text{cm}\).
- The dimensionless product of Fermi's constant and the pion decay constant is \(G_{\text{F}} \cdot F_\pi^2 \sim 10^{-7}\).
- The ratio of \(K_{\text{L}}^0\) to \(K_{\text{S}}^0\) decay amplitudes into two pions is \(|\eta_{+-}| = (2.232 \pm 0.011) \cdot 10^{-3}\).
- The Standard Model prediction for the nucleon EDM is \(|d_{\text{N}}^{\text{SM}}| \sim 10^{-31} \, e\,\text{cm}\).
- The experimental bound on the neutron EDM is \(|d_n| < 2.9 \cdot 10^{-26} \, e\,\text{cm}\) from the Sussex/RAL/ILL group (Baker 2006).
- The bound on the proton EDM is \(|d_p| < 2.0 \cdot 10^{-25} \, e\,\text{cm}\), inferred from the \({}^{197}\text{Hg}\) atom bound \(|d_{\text{Hg}}| < 7.4 \cdot 10^{-30} \, e\,\text{cm}\) (Graner 2016).
- The most stringent bound on the electron EDM is \(|d_e| < 8.7 \cdot 10^{-29} \, e\,\text{cm}\) from the ThO measurement by the ACME group (Baron 2013).
- The bound on the muon EDM is \(|d_\mu| < 1.8 \cdot 10^{-19} \, e\,\text{cm}\) from \((g-2)_\mu\) storage ring measurements (Bennett 2008).
- The cosmic baryon-to-photon ratio from CMB measurements is \((n_B - n_{\bar B})/n_\gamma = (6.05 \pm 0.07) \cdot 10^{-10}\) (Ade 2015).
- The nucleon EDM induced by the QCD \(\theta\) term scales as \(|d_{\text{N}}^{\bar\theta}| \sim \bar\theta \cdot 10^{-16} \, e\,\text{cm}\), compatible with the window \(10^{-10} \gtrsim |\bar\theta| \gtrsim 10^{-14}\).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for EDMs from single nucleons to light nuclei, building on earlier chiral EFT calculations of the nucleon EDM induced by the QCD \(\theta\) term from the late 1970s (CDVW79) and more modern work by Pich (1991) and Ottnad. It contrasts the limited predictive power of chiral EFT for single-nucleon EDMs, which require lattice QCD input (e.g., Guo 2015, Shintani 2015), with the more robust tree-level two-nucleon contributions to deuteron and helion EDMs first noted by Flambaum (1984). The work enables future disentanglement of New Physics sources by comparing EDM measurements across different nuclear systems, using the operator basis of Dekens (2014) and the chiral Lagrangian of Mereghetti (2010) and de Vries (2011, 2012).
