# Excluded-volume model for quarkyonic Matter: Three-flavor baryon-quark Mixture

> The single-flavor excluded-volume model based on the effective size of baryons reproduces the hard-soft density evolution of the equation of state (EoS) required by the recent studies of GW170817. This phenomenological model basically realizes the concept of quarkyonic matter which is introduced from large-$N_{c}$ gauge theory for dense matter. Enhanced nucleon interactions and dynamically generated quark degrees of freedom can reproduce the hard-soft evolution of the EoS. In this paper, we extend the excluded-volume model to a three-flavor system by considering electromagnetic charge and possible weak equilibrium in order to obtain a proper description for the hard-soft behavior of the EoS inferred from the gravitational waves observations.

## Key claim

The three-flavor excluded-volume model with hard-core repulsion between baryons and dynamically generated quark degrees of freedom can reproduce the hard-soft density evolution of the equation of state required by GW170817 observations, but only if Pauli's exclusion principle is included to produce a shell-like baryon phase distribution; without this effect, the model cannot simultaneously support a 2 solar mass neutron star and satisfy the radius constraint R₁.₄ ≤ 13.5 km.

## Method

The authors extended the single-flavor excluded-volume model to a three-flavor system (neutrons, protons, and Λ hyperons) by introducing an effective hard-core density n₀ ≈ 5ρ₀ and a parameter α that controls the Λ hyperon's repulsive core size relative to nucleons. They imposed electromagnetic charge neutrality, β-equilibrium conditions (n ↔ p + e, n ↔ Λ), and baryon number conservation, then allowed quark degrees of freedom (u, d, s) to appear dynamically when the baryon chemical potential satisfies μ_B = N_c μ_Q − μ_e. The resulting density profiles and equation of state were computed, and the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Hard-core density assumed: n₀ ≈ 5ρ₀ (where ρ₀ denotes normal nuclear density), corresponding to a hard-core radius around 0.6 fm
- Λ hyperon repulsive core parameter α varied in the range −0.2 < α < 0.2
- Λ hyperon appears around n_B ≈ 6ρ₀ for α = 0.2, around n_B ≈ 4.8ρ₀ for α = 0, and around n_B ≈ 4.2ρ₀ for negative α
- Sound velocity squared v_s² reaches approximately 0.4 around the hard-core regime and converges to the ideal limit 1/3 at high density
- Maximum neutron star mass obtained is approximately 1.5 M⊙ for n₀ > 5ρ₀, with corresponding radius smaller than 10 km
- The trial parameter set for low-density interpolation (not used in the main calculation) reproduces normal nuclear matter properties: E/A ≈ −16 MeV, e_sym ≈ 30 MeV, K₀ ≈ 260 MeV, and L_sym ≈ 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the single-flavor excluded-volume model of Jeong (2019) to a three-flavor baryon-quark mixture, building on the quarkyonic matter concept from McLerran (2007) and subsequent developments by McLerran (2018) and Jeong (2019). The hard-core repulsive interaction is motivated by lattice QCD calculations of nucleon-nucleon and Λ-nucleon potentials from Inoue (2016), Nemura (2017), Hatsuda (2018), and Inoue (2018). The model aims to satisfy constraints from GW170817 analyses by Fattoyev (2017), Annala (2017), and Most (2018), but the authors find that without including Pauli's exclusion principle to generate a shell-like baryon distribution, the model cannot simultaneously achieve a 2 M⊙ maximum mass and the R₁.₄ ≤ 13.5 km constraint. The next step is to incorporate the shell-like phase distribution from Pauli blocking to produce a sufficiently hard equation of state at intermediate densities.
