# Equation of state at FAIR energies and the role of resonances

> Two microscopic models, UrQMD and QGSM, are used to extract the effective equation of state (EOS) of locally equilibrated nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions at energies from 11.6 AGeV to 160 AGeV. Analysis is performed for the fixed central cubic cell of volume V = 125 fm**3 and for the expanding cell that followed the growth of the central area with uniformly distributed energy. For all reactions the state of local equilibrium is nearly approached in both models after a certain relaxation period. The EOS has a simple linear dependence P/e = c_s**2 with 0.12 < c_s**2 < 0.145. Heavy resonances are shown to be responsible for deviations of the c_s**2(T) and c_s**2(mu_B) from linear behavior. In the T-mu_B and T-mu_S planes the EOS has also almost linear dependence and demonstrates kinks related not to the deconfinement phase transition but to inelastic freeze-out in the system.

## Key claim
The effective equation of state (EOS) of locally equilibrated nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions from 11.6 AGeV to 160 AGeV is approximately linear (P = c_s^2 epsilon) with a soft speed of sound, but the kinks observed in the T-mu_B and T-mu_S phase diagrams are not signatures of deconfinement; they are caused by the freeze-out of inelastic reactions (chemical freeze-out) in the expanding system.

## Method
The authors used two microscopic transport models, UrQMD (ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics) and QGSM (quark-gluon string model), to simulate central Au+Au collisions at bombarding energies from 11.6 AGeV to 160 AGeV. They extracted thermodynamic parameters (temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, strangeness chemical potential mu_S) by comparing particle yields and energy spectra from a fixed central cubic cell (V = 125 fm^3) and from an expanding cell that follows the region of uniformly distributed energy density to a statistical model of an ideal hadron gas in equilibrium. The pressure and energy density from the microscopic simulations were then used to determine the effective EOS and the speed of sound.

## Key numbers
- The square of the speed of sound c_s^2 ranges from 0.12 to 0.145 across all energies studied.
- In UrQMD, c_s^2 increases from 0.13 at E_lab = 11.6 AGeV to 0.146 at E_lab = 158 AGeV, saturating near 0.15 at RHIC energies.
- In QGSM, the averaged sound velocity is about 0.015 units smaller than in UrQMD.
- The nuclear compressibility changes from 140 MeV at AGS energies to 200 MeV at SPS and RHIC energies.
- The entropy per baryon ratio is conserved within 5% accuracy during the equilibrium phase of expansion.
- The PHENIX collaboration value c_s approx 0.35 +/- 0.05 (c_s^2 approx 0.12 +/- 0.03) for Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV is cited as consistent with these results.
- The Hagedorn model prediction for the EOS with resonance mass spectrum rho(m) proportional to m^{alpha'} (2 <= alpha' <= 3) gives 1/7 <= c_s^2 <= 1/6.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of equilibration in heavy-ion collisions using UrQMD and QGSM (UrQMD equilibrium studies and QGSM equilibrium studies, both cited by the authors) and a recent analysis using a non-fixed cell (Zabrodin et al. 2008). It directly compares two microscopic models with different resonance tables (UrQMD includes 55 baryon and 32 meson states; QGSM includes only octet and decuplet baryons and nonets of vector and pseudoscalar mesons, omitting heavy resonances) to isolate the role of heavy resonances in softening the EOS. The results enable more reliable hydrodynamic modeling at FAIR energies (20-40 AGeV) by providing a microscopic basis for the EOS and by warning that kinks in phase diagrams can mimic a QCD phase transition when they actually arise from chemical freeze-out.
