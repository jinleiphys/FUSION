# Nuclear fragmentation and charge-exchange reactions induced by pions in the $О”$-resonance region

> Dynamics of the nuclear fragmentations and the charge exchange reactions in pion-nucleus collisions near the $О”$(1232) resonance energies has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model. An isospin, momentum and density-dependent pion-nucleon potential is implemented in the model, which influences the pion dynamics, in particular the kinetic energy spectra, but weakly impacts the fragmentation mechanism. The absorption process in pion-nucleon collisions to form the $О”$(1232) resonance dominates the heating mechanism of target nucleus. The excitation energy transferred to the target nucleus increases with the pion kinetic energy and is similar for both $ПЂ^{-}$ and $ПЂ^{+}$ induced reactions. The magnitude of fragmentation of target nucleus weakly depends on the pion energy. The isospin ratio in the pion double charge exchange is influenced by the isospin ingredient of target nucleus.

## Key claim

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, including an isospin-, momentum-, and density-dependent pion-nucleon potential, shows that the pion-nucleon potential influences the kinetic energy spectra of pions but has a negligible contribution on the fragmentation process of the target nucleus; the magnitude of fragmentation depends only weakly on the pion energy.

## Method

The LQMD transport model was used to simulate pion-nucleus collisions near the О”(1232) resonance energies, implementing mean-field potentials for nucleons and resonances based on Skyrme forces and a pion optical potential evaluated from the in-medium energy of pions via the О”-hole model. All possible hadron-hadron reaction channels were included, with resonance formation cross sections (О”(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535)) calculated using a Breit-Wigner formula fitted to experimental data. The primary fragments formed at freeze-out (300 fm/c) were constructed via a coalescence model (relative momentum < 200 MeV/c, relative distance < 3 fm), and their de-excitation was treated with the GEMINI statistical decay code.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pion-nucleus collisions at meson facilities (LAMPF, PSI, TRIUMF, KEK-SKS) and theoretical approaches such as the GiBUU transport model (Buss 2012) by applying the LQMD model to pion-induced reactions for the first time. The results enable future investigations of hypernucleus production with high-energy pion beams at facilities like FAIR (GSI, Germany) and HIAF (IMP, China). The study also contrasts the IMF production in pion reactions with that in heavy-ion collisions, where multifragmentation dominates.
