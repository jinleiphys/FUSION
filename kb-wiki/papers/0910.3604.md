# Nonperturbative relativistic approach to pion form factor: predictions for future JLab experiments

> Some predictions concerning possible results of the future JLab experiments on the pion form factor F_pi(Q^2) are made. The calculations exploit the method proposed previously by the authors and based on the instant-form Poincare invariant approach to pion considered as a quark-antiquark system. Long ago, this model has predicted with surprising accuracy the values of F_pi(Q^2) measured later in JLab experiment. The results are almost independent from the form of wave function. The pion mean square radius <r^2_pi> and the decay constant f_pi also agree with experimental values. The model gives power-like asymptotic behavior of F_pi(Q^2) at high momentum transfer in agreement with QCD predictions.

# Nonperturbative relativistic approach to pion form factor: predictions for future JLab experiments

## Key claim
The authors predict that their nonperturbative constituent quark model, which already accurately reproduced JLab data up to Q² = 2.45 GeV² without parameter adjustment, will continue to describe the pion form factor in future JLab experiments up to Q² ≈ 6 GeV²; if deviations appear, they propose to attribute them to the onset of perturbative effects by introducing a Q²-dependent quark mass.

## Method
The authors use an instant-form Poincaré invariant constituent quark model (PICQM) based on a dispersion-relation approach, constructing the electromagnetic current operator via a modified impulse approximation (MIA). The pion form factor is computed as an integral over the Mandelstam variables s and s' of the pion wave function and a free two-particle form factor that includes quark electric and magnetic form factors with logarithmic corrections. Three different phenomenological wave functions are employed: harmonic oscillator, power-law (n=3), and a linear-confinement plus Coulomb-like form, all with a fixed constituent quark mass M = 0.22 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass fixed at M = 0.22 GeV, originally determined from data at Q² ≤ 0.26 GeV² (Amendolia et al., 1984).
- Pion mean square radius predicted within experimental uncertainty: ⟨r²_π⟩^{1/2}_{exp} = 0.657 ± 0.012 fm.
- Decay constant f_π = 0.1317 ± 0.0002 GeV (experimental value); model gives f_π = 127.4 MeV (harmonic oscillator) and 131.7 MeV (power-law and Tezuka-type wave functions).
- Predictions cover the range 2.45 ≤ Q² ≤ 6 (GeV)² for future JLab experiments.
- Asymptotic behavior at Q² → ∞ gives F_π(Q²) ∼ 14√2 b² / Q² (power-law matching pQCD) when M/b → 0.
- Quark anomalous magnetic moment sum s_q = 0.0268; quark mean square radius ⟨r²_q⟩ ≈ 0.3/M².

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier nonperturbative approach (Krutov and Troitsky, 2001) which successfully predicted the pion form factor values later measured in JLab experiments up to Q² = 2.45 GeV². The model contrasts with other constituent quark models such as those by Cardarelli et al. (1994), Hwang (2001), and the instant-form predictions of He and Jiang (2004), as well as with perturbative QCD calculations by Bakulev et al. (2004) and the dispersion approach of Donoghue and Na (1997). The paper proposes a scenario for detecting the transition from nonperturbative to perturbative regimes: if future JLab data deviate from the predictions, the quark mass dependence on Q² will be introduced as a signature of perturbative effects.
