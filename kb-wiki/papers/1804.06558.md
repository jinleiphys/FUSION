# Disappearance of nuclear deformation in hypernuclei: a perspective from a beyond-mean-field study

> The previous mean-field calculation [Myaing Thi Win and K. Hagino, Phys. Rev. C{\bf 78}, 054311 (2008)] has shown that the oblate deformation in $^{28,30,32}$Si disappears when a $Λ$ particle is added to these nuclei. We here investigate this phenomenon by taking into account the effects beyond the mean-field approximation. To this end, we employ the microscopic particle-rotor model based on the covariant density functional theory. We show that the deformation of $^{30}$Si does not completely disappear, even though it is somewhat reduced, after a $Λ$ particle is added if the beyond-mean-field effect is taken into account. We also discuss the impurity effect of $Λ$ particle on the electric quadrupole transition, and show that an addition of a $Λ$ particle leads to a reduction in the $B(E2)$ value, as a consequence of the reduction in the deformation parameter.

## Key claim

The previous mean-field result that oblate deformation in 28,30,32Si disappears completely when a Λ particle is added is modified when beyond-mean-field effects are included: for 30Si, the deformation does not completely disappear, though it is somewhat reduced, after a Λ particle is added.

## Method

The authors employ the microscopic particle-rotor model based on covariant density functional theory, using the PC-F1 parameter set for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and PCY-S4 for the nucleon-Λ interaction. Core wave functions for 30Si are constructed with the generator coordinate method (GCM) by superposing projected Slater determinants, including angular momentum and particle number projections. The coupled-channels equations for the Λ particle motion are then solved including core states up to n_max=2 and I_max=6.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field potential minimum for 30Si is at β = -0.22 (oblate), while the projected 0⁺ configuration minimum appears at β = -0.35.
- For the 31ΛSi hypernucleus, the projected energy minimum shifts from β = -0.35 to β = -0.30 when a Λ particle is added.
- The ratio R₄/₂ = E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) for 30Si is 3.083; for 31ΛSi the corresponding ratio E(9/2₁⁺)/E(5/2₁⁺) is 2.829.
- The B(E2) value for the 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺ transition in 30Si is 63.60 e²fm⁴; the corresponding cB(E2) values for 31ΛSi are 57.00 e²fm⁴ (3/2₁⁺ → 1/2₁⁺) and 57.06 e²fm⁴ (5/2₁⁺ → 1/2₁⁺), representing reductions of about 10.4%.
- The B(E2) for the 4₁⁺ → 2₁⁺ transition in 30Si is 103.59 e²fm⁴; the corresponding cB(E2) values for 31ΛSi are around 102.4 e²fm⁴, representing reductions of about 1.2%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier mean-field study by Myaing Thi Win and Hagino (2008) which found that deformation disappears in 28,30,32Si when a Λ particle is added, and is consistent with the anti-symmetrized molecular dynamics study by Isaka (2016) which also found a weaker polarization effect. The results show that beyond-mean-field effects, particularly shape fluctuation and angular momentum projection, prevent complete disappearance of deformation in soft hypernuclei, and the microscopic particle-rotor model provides a complementary tool to the GCM approach for the whole core-plus-Λ system.
