# Study of Early time attractor with Longitudinal Forces with Bjorken Symmetry

> We study the effect of external proper time-dependent longitudinal forces on the evolution of the distribution function using the Boltzmann Equation with a relaxation time collision kernel under Bjorken flow. We derive an exact solution and study the early time attractor behaviour of different components of energy-momentum tensor under Bjorken symmetry. We show that the arbitrary initial data approaches the attractor solution but is influenced by the external force with slightly broken Bjorken symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while arbitrary initial data approaches an attractor solution under Bjorken flow with an external longitudinal force, the attractor behavior is modified: for non-conformal systems, only a late-time attractor is present for scaled longitudinal pressure, whereas in the conformal (massless) limit an early-time attractor reappears even with the external force, provided the force has decayed before equilibration.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation with a relaxation time approximation (RTA) collision kernel under Bjorken flow, incorporating an external proper time-dependent longitudinal force. They derive an exact formal solution using a change of variables that transforms the force-modified Boltzmann equation into a form solvable by standard methods, then compute the energy-momentum tensor components (energy density, longitudinal pressure, transverse pressure, and off-diagonal \(T^{03}\)) via iterative techniques. The initial distribution uses the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic form, and two force parametrizations are studied: an exponentially decaying force (peaking at \(\tau_F\)) and a constant force, both treated as small perturbations to maintain approximate Bjorken symmetry.

## Key numbers

* Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm and initial temperature \(T_0 = 500\) MeV for all calculations.
* For non-conformal calculations, particle mass \(m = 200\) MeV.
* Force strength parameter \(\alpha\) values used: 0, 50, 100, 200 MeV (exponentially decaying force); 2, 5, 10 MeV (constant force).
* Decay time scale \(\tau_F = 1\) fm/c for the exponentially decaying force (with additional values 2.5 and 5 fm/c shown in a figure).
* Relaxation time conformal relation: \(\tau_R(\tau) = 5c/T(\tau)\), where \(c\) is a dimensionless number proportional to \(\eta_0/s_0\).
* Transverse electromagnetic field strengths estimated at \(10^{18}\) to \(10^{19}\) G at RHIC/LHC energies (cited from prior work).

## Context

This work extends studies of hydrodynamic attractors in non-conformal systems, specifically following Jaiswal (2022) who found that universal attractor behavior is absent for most hydrodynamic variables in non-conformal systems and only appears for scaled longitudinal pressure \(P_L/P\). The authors introduce an external longitudinal force to break symmetries further, motivated by the strong transient electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions estimated by Gursoy (2014), Voronyuk (2011), and Deng (2012). The results enable future studies of more realistic electromagnetic forces in non-Bjorken flows (e.g., Gubser flow) and self-consistent \(1+1\) dimensional expansions.
