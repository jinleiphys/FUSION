# Electromagnetic signatures of a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> In heavy-ion collisions, quark-gluon plasma is likely to be produced with sizable initial pressure anisotropy, which may leave an imprint on electromagnetic observables. In order to model a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma, we use the AdS/CFT correspondence to calculate the current-current correlator of a weakly gauged U(1) subgroup of R symmetry in an N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma with a (temporarily) fixed anisotropy. The dual geometry, obtained previously by Janik and Witaszczyk, contains a naked singularity which however permits purely infalling boundary conditions and therefore the usual definition of a retarded correlator. We obtain numerical results for the cases of wave vector parallel and orthogonal to the direction of anisotropy, and we compare with previous isotropic results. In the (unphysical) limit of vanishing frequency (infinite time) we obtain a vanishing DC conductivity for any amount of anisotropy, but the anisotropic AC conductivities smoothly approach the isotropic case in the limit of high frequencies. We also discuss hard photon production from an anisotropic plasma and compare with existing hard-loop resummed calculations.

## Key claim

Using the AdS/CFT correspondence with a stationary anisotropic geometry containing a naked singularity, the authors calculate that the DC conductivity vanishes for any nonzero anisotropy, but the anisotropic AC conductivities smoothly approach the isotropic case at high frequencies, suggesting the setup can model temporary anisotropies in a strongly coupled plasma on sufficiently short time scales.

## Method

The authors use the Janik-Witaszczyk dual geometry of a stationary anisotropic N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma to solve the equations of motion for a U(1) gauge field in the 5-dimensional background. They compute the current-current spectral functions by imposing purely infalling boundary conditions at the naked singularity, obtaining numerical results for wave vectors both parallel and orthogonal to the anisotropy direction.

## Key numbers

- For the isotropic limit (B=0), the dimensionful parameter A equals pi^2 T^2/2
- The anisotropy parameter B ranges from -sqrt(18) to sqrt(18), with physical pressures (both positive) in the range -sqrt(6) <= B <= sqrt(2)
- For B=0, the pressure ratio P_L/epsilon = P_T/epsilon = 1/3; for B=sqrt(2), P_L/epsilon = 0 and P_T/epsilon = 1/2; for B=-sqrt(6), P_L/epsilon = 1 and P_T/epsilon = 0
- For small anisotropies, the relation between anisotropy parameters is xi = (5/4)B + O(B^2)
- The characteristic exponent alpha in the differential equations near the singularity is alpha = (2 + sqrt(36 - 2B^2))/4, which is less than 2 for any nonzero B

## Context

This work extends the isotropic calculation of U(1) current-current spectral functions by Caron-Huot (2006) to the anisotropic case using the stationary anisotropic geometry previously obtained by Janik and Witaszczyk (2008). The results enable study of electromagnetic signatures such as hard photon and dilepton production from a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma, complementing weak-coupling hard-loop resummed calculations by Schenke (2006) and Martinez (2008). The vanishing DC conductivity reflects the fact that a stationary anisotropy can only approximate nonequilibrium dynamics at sufficiently short time scales.
