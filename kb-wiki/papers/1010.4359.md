# Bulk Viscosity and Relaxation Time of Causal Dissipative Relativistic Fluid Dynamics

> The microscopic formulae of the bulk viscosity $ζ$ and the corresponding relaxation time $τ_Π$ in causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics are derived by using the projection operator method. In applying these formulae to the pionic fluid, we find that the renormalizable energy-momentum tensor should be employed to obtain consistent results. In the leading order approximation in the chiral perturbation theory, the relaxation time is enhanced near the QCD phase transition and $τ_Π$ and $ζ$ are related as $τ_Π=ζ/[β\{(1/3-c_{s}^{2})(ε+P)-2(ε-3P)/9\}]$, where $ε$, $P$ and $c_{s}$ are the energy density, pressure and velocity of sound, respectively. The predicted $ζ$ and $% τ_Π$ should satisfy the so-called causality condition. We compare our result with the results of the kinetic calculation by Israel and Stewart and the string theory, and confirm that all the three approaches are consistent with the causality condition.

## Key claim

The authors derive microscopic formulas for the bulk viscosity ζ and its relaxation time τ_Π in causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics using the projection operator method, finding that in the leading order approximation of chiral perturbation theory for a pionic fluid, τ_Π is enhanced near the QCD phase transition and that the predicted values from this approach, from kinetic theory (Israel and Stewart), and from string theory all satisfy the causality condition.

## Method

The authors apply the Mori projection operator method to derive generalized Langevin equations for the bulk viscous pressure Π and the momentum density T^{0x}, then implement a time-convolutionless (TCL) coarse-graining approximation to extract the phenomenological Israel-Stewart-type equation τ_Π ∂_t Π + Π = -ζ ∂_μ u^μ. The resulting microscopic formulas express ζ and τ_Π as ratios of retarded Green functions of Π and T^{0x} in the low-momentum, low-frequency limit. These formulas are then evaluated for a hot pion gas using the renormalizable energy-momentum tensor θ^{μν} in the leading order of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time τ_Π/β for the pionic fluid is a decreasing function at low temperature, starts to increase around T = 100 MeV, and shows a maximum near the QCD phase transition temperature.
- The ζ-τ_Π ratio from the leading order result is given by ζ/[τ_Π(ε+P)] = [(1/3 - c_s^2)(ε+P) - (2/9)(ε-3P)]/(ε+P).
- The group velocity for signal propagation in causal dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics is v_g = sqrt(c_s^2 + ζ/[τ_Π(ε+P)]), and this must be less than the speed of light for causality.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous projection operator method applied to shear viscosity and its relaxation time (Huang et al., 2010) to the bulk viscosity channel. The results are compared with the kinetic calculation using Grad's 14-moment approximation from Israel and Stewart (1979) and with predictions from string theory, confirming that all three approaches yield values consistent with the causality condition. The derived microscopic formulas enable calculation of bulk viscosity and relaxation time for any system where the retarded Green functions can be computed, with applications to heavy-ion collision physics and the stability of rotating compact stars.
