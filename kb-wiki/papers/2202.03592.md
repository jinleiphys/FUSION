# Physical symmetries and gauge choices in the Landau problem

> Due to a special nature of the Landau problem, in which the magnetic field is uniformly spreading over the whole two-dimensional plane, there necessarily exist three conserved quantities, i.e. two conserved momenta and one conserved orbital angular momentum for the electron, independently of the choice of the gauge potential. Accordingly, the quantum eigen-functions of the Landau problem can be obtained by diagonalizing the Landau Hamiltonian together with one of the above three conserved operators with the result that the quantum mechanical eigen-functions of the Landau problem can be written down for arbitrary gauge potential. The purpose of the present paper is to clarify the meaning of gauge choice in the Landau problem based on this gauge-potential-independent formulation, with a particular intention of unraveling the physical significance of the concept of gauge-invariant-extension of the canonical orbital angular momentum advocated in recent literature on the nucleon spin decomposition problem. At the end, our analysis is shown to disclose a physically vacuous side face of the gauge symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the Landau problem, the three conserved quantities (two momenta and one orbital angular momentum) exist independently of gauge choice, and that the widely assumed connection between a specific gauge and a specific symmetry of the eigenfunctions is not an absolute demand of quantum theory. The authors claim that their analysis reveals a "physically vacuous side face" of gauge symmetry, particularly questioning the physical significance of the gauge-invariant extension of the canonical orbital angular momentum used in nucleon spin decomposition literature.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Landau problem using a gauge-potential-independent approach based on unitary transformations in standard non-relativistic quantum mechanics, without introducing nonlocal gauge links. They diagonalize the Landau Hamiltonian together with one of the three conserved operators (two conserved momenta or one conserved orbital angular momentum) for arbitrary gauge potentials, generating four types of eigenstates: those based on the symmetric gauge with either conserved OAM or conserved momentum as the diagonalized operator, and those based on the 1st Landau gauge with either conserved momentum or conserved OAM as the diagonalized operator. They then evaluate matrix elements of canonical, mechanical, and conserved momentum and OAM operators between these different eigenstate families.

## Key numbers

- The Landau energy levels are given by \(E_n = (2n + 1) \omega_L\) with \(\omega_L = eB / (2 m_e)\) being the Larmor frequency.
- The magnetic length is defined as \(l_B^2 = 1 / (eB)\).
- The conserved OAM eigenvalues are \(m\) (integer), and the conserved momentum eigenvalues are \(k_x\).
- In the \(\vert n, k_x \rangle\)-basis, the matrix element of the canonical momentum \(\hat{p}^{can}_x\) in the 1st Landau gauge is \(k_x \,\delta(k'_x - k_x)\), while in the symmetric gauge it is \(\frac{1}{2} k_x \,\delta(k'_x - k_x)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Wakamatsu, Kikuya, and Zhang (2018), who solved the Landau problem using a gauge-invariant but path-dependent formulation with Wilson lines, but did not fully elucidate relations between the two classes of solutions (Landau-gauge-based and symmetric-gauge-based). The paper directly addresses the nucleon spin decomposition problem, specifically critiquing the concept of gauge-invariant extension of the canonical orbital angular momentum advocated by Chen et al. (2008, 2009), Hatta (2011), Lorce (2013), and others. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of whether mechanical momentum and mechanical OAM expectation values coincide across different eigenstate families, and whether conserved (pseudo) momenta and OAM are truly gauge-invariant physical quantities.
