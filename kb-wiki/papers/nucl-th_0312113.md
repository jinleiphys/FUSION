# Clustering in stable and unstable nuclei in $p$-shell and $sd$-shell regions

> According to microscopic calculations with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, we studied cluster features in stable and unstable nuclei. A variety of structure was found in stable and unstable nuclei in the $p$-shell and $sd$-shell regions. The structure of excited states of $^{12}$Be was investigated, while in $sd$-shell nuclei we focused on molecular states and deformed states. The deformed states in $^{28}$Si and $^{40}$Ca were discussed in connection with the high-lying molecular states. Appealing molecular states in $^{36}$Ar and $^{24}$Mg were suggested. The results signified that both clustering of nucleons and mean-field formation are essential features in $sd$-shell nuclei as well as $p$-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

According to microscopic calculations with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, both clustering of nucleons and mean-field formation are essential features in *sd*-shell nuclei as well as *p*-shell nuclei, with the paper's results signifying that these two natures coexist, compete, interplay, or overlap even in heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with energy variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) for the excited states of ¹²Be, and the AMD plus generator coordinate method (AMD+GCM) for *sd*-shell nuclei. The wave functions are Slater determinants of Gaussian wave packets with complex variational parameters for the centers of Gaussians and intrinsic spin orientations, and the harmonic-oscillator quanta of the system were adopted as the generator coordinate for the GCM calculations.

## Key numbers

- In ¹²Be, three rotational bands with K^π = 0⁺₁, 0⁺₂, and 0⁺₃ were found in the positive-parity states; the ground band consists of intruder states (2ħω excited configurations) with a 2α core, while the third 0⁺ band predicts a ⁶He+⁶He molecule-like structure.
- In ²⁸Si, three K^π = 0⁺ bands dominated by O+C cluster states were found; the lowest band has minimum node number n₀ = 8 in Pauli allowed states, while the second and third bands have n₀ = 9 and n₀ = 10, respectively.
- In ⁴⁰Ca, the band-head state of the superdeformation lies at an excitation energy of 5.2 MeV, and the energy intervals between the superdeformed and second bands, and between the second and third bands, are predicted to be about 10 MeV.
- In ³⁶Ar, the rotational band of the linear 3-¹²C structure is predicted in the energy region E_x ∼ 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier AMD studies of cluster structure in light nuclei (Kanada-En'yo 1995, 1998) and addresses the long-standing issue of connecting molecular resonances observed in highly excited states of *sd*-shell nuclei (James 1976, Ohkubo 1998) with low-lying deformed states. The results enable further studies of unstable *sd*-shell nuclei, ²⁰Ne, and ³²S using extended AMD methods (Kimura 2003), and suggest that carbon clusters are a key structural element in the *sd*-shell region alongside the well-known alpha cluster.
