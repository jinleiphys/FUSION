# Complex Langevin dynamics at finite chemical potential: mean field analysis in the relativistic Bose gas

> Stochastic quantization can potentially be used to simulate theories with a complex action due to a nonzero chemical potential. We study complex Langevin dynamics in the relativistic Bose gas analytically, using a mean field approximation. We concentrate on the region with a Silver Blaze problem and discuss convergence, stability, fixed points, and the severeness of the sign problem. The real distribution satisfying the extended Fokker-Planck equation is constructed and its nonlocal form is explained. Finally, we compare the mean field results in finite volume with the numerical data presented in Ref. [1].

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics can correctly reproduce the expected physics of the relativistic Bose gas at finite chemical potential in the Silver Blaze region, provided that the condition \(A_p > 0\) (equivalent to \(4\sinh^2(\mu/2) < m^2\)) is satisfied, which ensures convergence and independence from initial conditions in the limit of infinite Langevin time.

## Method

The author performs an analytical mean field analysis of complex Langevin dynamics applied to a self-interacting complex scalar field (relativistic Bose gas) on a four-dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions. The complex Langevin equations are derived from the lattice action, the fields are complexified into real and imaginary parts, and the dynamics is studied both in continuous and discretized Langevin time. The mean field approximation factorizes interaction terms in the drift equations to obtain self-consistent fixed-point equations for the two-point correlation functions, and the stationary solution of the extended Fokker-Planck equation is constructed for the noninteracting case.

## Key numbers

* The stability condition for the free Bose gas at finite chemical potential is \(4\sinh^2(\mu/2) < m^2\), corresponding to \(\mu^2 < m^2\) in the formal continuum limit.
* For the discretized Langevin dynamics, the convergence condition is \(A_p - (\eps/2)(A_p^2 + B_p^2) > 0\), which at \(\mu=0\) yields the constraint \(\eps < 2/(4d + m^2)\) with \(d=4\).
* The average phase factor in the phase quenched theory decays as \(\langle e^{i\varphi}\rangle_{\rm pq} = e^{-\Omega \Delta f}\), where \(\Delta f = (1/2\Omega)\sum_p \ln[(A_p^2+B_p^2)/A_p^2]\).
* The stationary distribution coefficients for the noninteracting case are \(\alpha_p = A_p\), \(\beta_p = (A_p/B_p^2)(2A_p^2+B_p^2)\), and \(\gamma_p = A_p^2/B_p\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical study of Aarts (2008) on complex Langevin dynamics in the relativistic Bose gas by providing an analytical mean field analysis, complementing earlier mixed results from the 1980s reviewed by Damgaard and Huffel (1987). The analysis focuses on the Silver Blaze problem, originally described by Cohen (2003), where bulk observables are independent of chemical potential at zero temperature below onset. The results enable a deeper understanding of convergence criteria and the nonlocal nature of the real probability distribution in complex Langevin simulations, and provide a framework for comparing mean field predictions with full nonperturbative simulations on lattices of size \(N^4\) with \(N=4,6,8,10\).
