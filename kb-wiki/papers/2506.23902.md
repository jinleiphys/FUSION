# Nucleon mass: trace anomaly and $σ$-terms

> We give a pedagogical introduction to the origin of the mass of the nucleon. We first review the trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor, which generates most of the nucleon mass via the gluon fields and thus contributes even in the case of vanishing quark masses. We then discuss the contributions to the nucleon mass that do originate from the Higgs mechanism via the quark masses, reviewing the current status of nucleon $σ$-terms that encode the corresponding matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper provides a pedagogical review of how the nucleon mass arises from both the trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor (gluon fields) and quark-mass contributions via the Higgs mechanism, emphasizing that while the general hierarchy among contributions is robust, the numerical values for the light-quark σ-term still carry substantial uncertainties and the precise value of the light-quark contribution to the nucleon mass is not yet fully settled.

## Method

The authors review the trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor in QCD, deriving how the gluon field strength generates most of the nucleon mass even in the chiral limit. They then analyze nucleon σ-terms using the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem combined with Roy-Steiner dispersion relations for πN scattering, supplemented by constraints from pionic atoms, and compare these phenomenological determinations with lattice QCD calculations using both the Feynman-Hellmann theorem and direct three-point function methods.

## Key numbers

- σπN = 59.0(3.5) MeV from the combined analysis of Roy-Steiner equations and pionic atom data (Hoferichter 2023)
- The contribution from up and down quark masses to the nucleon mass, mN^ud ≃ 69 MeV, including higher-order chiral corrections beyond σπN
- The nucleon mass in the SU(2) chiral limit (removing u,d contributions) is m̃N^(2) ≃ 869.5 MeV
- The strange quark contribution to the nucleon mass, mN^s ≃ 43 MeV, from lattice QCD averages
- Lattice QCD results for σ̄πN: 42.2(2.4) MeV for Nf=2+1 and 60.9(6.5) MeV for Nf=2+1+1 (FLAG 2024)
- Lattice QCD results for σs: 44.9(6.4) MeV for Nf=2+1 and 41.0(8.8) MeV for Nf=2+1+1 (FLAG 2024)
- The third-order chiral correction to the nucleon mass from u,d quarks is approximately 8 MeV from the term 3gA^2 mπ^3/(64π Fπ^2)

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the foundational work on the trace anomaly by Collins (1976) and Nielsen (1977) and the nucleon σ-term program initiated by Gasser (1988, 1990). The phenomenological determination of σπN via Roy-Steiner equations builds on the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem (Cheng 1970, Brown 1971) and improves upon earlier partial-wave analyses by Koch (1980), Höhler (1984), and Pavan (2001). The paper enables future comparisons between lattice QCD calculations and phenomenological extractions, particularly to resolve the current 3.2σ tension between Nf=2+1 lattice results and the phenomenological value, and provides the σ-term inputs needed for dark matter direct detection, lepton flavor violation searches, and neutrino scattering phenomenology.
