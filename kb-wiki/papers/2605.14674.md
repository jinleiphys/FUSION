# Fourth order correlation of baryon number and electric charge as a better magnetometer of QCD

> This work focuses on the fourth order correlations $χ^{BQ}_{31}$, $χ^{QB}_{31}$, $χ^{BQ}_{22}$, $χ^{BS}_{31}$, $χ^{SB}_{31}$, $χ^{BS}_{22}$, $χ^{QS}_{31}$, $χ^{SQ}_{31}$, $χ^{QS}_{22}$, $χ^{BQS}_{211}$, $χ^{QBS}_{211}$, $χ^{SBQ}_{211}$ of baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$ at finite temperature, magnetic field and vanishing quark chemical potential. The study is carried out in the framework of a three-flavor PNJL model, considering both cases with and without inverse magnetic catalysis effect. We find that, fourth order correlations $χ^{BQ}_{31}$ at chiral restoration phase transition is more sensitive to the magnetic field than other second order and fourth order correlations and fluctuations, and can be served as a more effective magnetometer of QCD.

## Key claim

The fourth order correlation χ^{BQ}_{31} at the chiral restoration phase transition is more sensitive to the magnetic field than other second order and fourth order correlations and fluctuations, and can serve as a more effective magnetometer of QCD. This claim is conditional on the framework of a three-flavor PNJL model and holds for both cases with and without inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect.

## Method

The study uses a three-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model under external magnetic field at finite temperature and vanishing quark chemical potential. The thermodynamic potential is computed in the mean field approximation, and the fourth order correlations χ^{BQ}_{31}, χ^{QB}_{31}, χ^{BQ}_{22}, χ^{BS}_{31}, χ^{SB}_{31}, χ^{BS}_{22}, χ^{QS}_{31}, χ^{SQ}_{31}, χ^{QS}_{22}, χ^{BQS}_{211}, χ^{QBS}_{211}, and χ^{SBQ}_{211} are obtained by taking derivatives of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the chemical potentials. The IMC effect is introduced through magnetic field dependent coupling parameters G(eB), K(eB), and T_0(eB), fitted to reproduce the LQCD-reported decreasing pseudo-critical temperature of chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature of chiral restoration T^c_{pc} = 205 MeV at eB = 0, 219 MeV at eB = 10 m_π^2, and 225 MeV at eB = 20 m_π^2 (without IMC effect)
- Pseudo-critical temperature of deconfinement T^d_{pc} = 210 MeV at eB = 0, 215 MeV at eB = 10 m_π^2, and 219 MeV at eB = 20 m_π^2 (without IMC effect)
- Pseudo-critical temperature of strange quark chiral restoration T^s_{pc} = 260 MeV at eB = 0, 262 MeV at eB = 10 m_π^2, and 264 MeV at eB = 20 m_π^2 (without IMC effect)
- Among scaled correlations at T^c_{pc}, χ̂^{BQ}_{11} increases fastest at weak magnetic field eB < 10 m_π^2, while χ̂^{BQ}_{31} increases fastest at strong magnetic field eB > 10 m_π^2
- The double ratio R = χ̂^{BQ}_{31} / χ̂^{BQS}_{211} increases fastest with magnetic field among all double ratios considered

## Context

This work extends previous analytical studies of second order correlations between baryon number and electric charge as possible magnetometers of QCD, which were conducted in the hadron resonance gas model, PNJL model, and Polyakov loop extended chiral quark model by Ding et al. (2022, 2024, 2025) and Mao et al. (2023, 2024). The paper also addresses the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon observed in LQCD simulations by Bali et al. (2012) and Borsanyi et al. (2012), introducing IMC through magnetic field dependent parameters. The results enable using fourth order correlations, particularly χ^{BQ}_{31}, as a more sensitive probe of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, where the double ratio R can suppress volume effects and be analyzed as a function of centrality.
