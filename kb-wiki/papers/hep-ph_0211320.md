# How the nuclear Fermi motion plus a simple statistical model explains the EMC effect

> We present calculation of influence caused by nucleon Fermi motion on the parton distributions in nuclei. Our approach is based on the model where momenta of valence partons have some primordial distribution inside the hadron at rest, which is either provided by a statistical considerations or calculated using spherically symmetric Gaussian distribution with a width derived from the Heisenberg uncertainty relation. The sea parton contribution emerges from the similar Gaussian distribution with a width dictated by the presence of virtual pions in hadron. We show that the influence of Fermi motion changes substantially the nucleonic structure function inside the nucleus in the right direction and therefore should be considered seriously in all attempts devoted to explain the experimentally observed EMC effect for $x_{Bj} > 0.1$.

# How the nuclear Fermi motion plus a simple statistical model explains the EMC effect

## Key claim
The authors argue that nuclear Fermi motion of nucleons, contrary to some prior claims, provides a substantial contribution to the modification of nucleon parton distributions in the nuclear medium and therefore must be considered seriously in explanations of the EMC effect for Bjorken x > 0.1, but they note that for x < 0.2 the correction from single-nucleon Fermi motion alone is not valid and collective nuclear effects must be included.

## Method
The authors use two phenomenological approaches: a simple statistical model treating partons as a gas of noninteracting particles on the energy shell, and a Monte Carlo model with Gaussian primordial parton distributions (width 0.172 GeV for valence quarks). In both approaches, the Fermi motion of nucleons is subtracted from the parton energy in the nucleon rest frame, shifting the parton energy distribution to smaller values. For the sea quark contribution, the authors include a pionic component, and in the full Monte Carlo model they adjust the fraction of virtual pions assigned to the nucleon sea versus those mediating nucleon-nucleon interactions (93% to the nucleon sea, 7% to interactions).

## Key numbers
- The width of the Gaussian primordial distribution for valence quarks is 0.172 GeV.
- The nucleon chemical potential is given as mu = m - 8 MeV.
- The scalar and vector potentials in the Relativistic Mean Field approach are U_S = 300 MeV and U_V = 400 MeV.
- In the statistical model, the subtraction of the average Fermi energy changes the width of the parton energy distribution by approximately 7%.
- In the full Monte Carlo model, the width of the energy-momentum primordial distribution is changed by 10% (from 0.18 GeV to 0.165 GeV) to achieve a good fit to experimental data.
- The proportion of virtual pions assigned to the nucleon sea is 93%, with the remaining 7% responsible for nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model approach of Zou (1999) and the Monte Carlo model of Rozynek and Wilk (2001) to include nuclear Fermi motion effects on parton distributions. It contradicts the conclusions of the Relativistic Mean Field approach (MEANFIELD, 1998) and recent work by Miller (2002), which found that nuclear medium effects on the EMC ratio disappear when using standard nucleon primordial distributions. The results enable a unified description of the EMC effect across a broad range of x > 0.1, including the small-x region when the nuclear virtual pion cloud is properly treated.
