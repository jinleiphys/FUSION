# No-Core Shell Model Analysis of Light Nuclei

> The fundamental description of both structural properties and reactions of light nuclei in terms of constituent protons and neutrons interacting through nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces is a long-sought goal of nuclear theory. I will briefly present a promising technique, built upon the {\em ab initio} no-core shell model, which emerged recently as a candidate to reach such a goal: the no-core shell model/resonating-group method. This approach, capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei, complements a microscopic cluster technique with the use of two-nucleon realistic interactions, and a microscopic and consistent description of the nucleon clusters. I will discuss applications to light nuclei binary scattering processes and fusion reactions that power stars and Earth based fusion facilities, such as the deuterium-$^3$He fusion, and outline the progress toward the inclusion of the three-nucleon force into the formalism and the treatment of three-body clusters.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) can describe both bound and scattering states in light nuclei simultaneously, but the accuracy of its predictions for low-energy fusion reactions depends on the inclusion of three-nucleon forces and the choice of the similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolution parameter, which can compensate for missing higher-order effects.

## Method

The authors expand the many-body wave function of an A-nucleon system over a set of translationally-invariant cluster basis states describing two nuclei (target and projectile) separated by a relative coordinate, with the clusters described by eigenstates from the no-core shell model (NCSM). The continuous linear variational amplitudes are determined by solving orthogonalized resonating-group method equations that involve norm and Hamiltonian kernels computed from the underlying nuclear interaction and the NCSM eigenvectors of target and projectile. For the applications shown, the authors used similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon interactions, and in some cases included both induced and chiral three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

* $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B astrophysical S-factor at zero energy: $S_{17}(0)=19.4(7)$ MeV b, obtained with SRG-N$^3$LO NN interaction at $\Lambda=1.86$ fm$^{-1}$ and $N_{\rm max}=10$ HO basis.
* $^8$B ground-state separation energy: 136 keV from the NCSM/RGM calculation, compared to the observed 137 keV.
* $^3$H$(d,n)^4$He and $^3$He$(d,p)^4$He fusion reactions: experimental $Q$-value reproduced within $1\%$ using SRG-N$^3$LO NN interaction with $\Lambda=1.5$ fm$^{-1}$.
* $^4$He ground-state energy from NCSM/RGM with $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-28.22$ MeV, using SRG-N$^3$LO NN potential with $\Lambda=1.5$ fm$^{-1}$ and $\hbar\Omega=16$ MeV.
* $^6$He ground-state energy from NCSM/RGM with $N_{\rm max}=12$: $-28.72$ MeV, compared to the NCSM extrapolated value of $-29.80$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM approach developed by the same group (PhysRevLett.101.092501, PhysRevC.79.044606) to describe nucleon- and deuteron-nucleus collisions, and applies it to the $^7$Be$(p,\gamma)^8$B radiative capture (the final step in the nucleosynthetic chain leading to $^8$B and a key input for the Standard Solar Model) and to the $^3$H$(d,n)^4$He and $^3$He$(d,p)^4$He fusion reactions relevant for fusion energy and Big Bang nucleosynthesis. The inclusion of three-nucleon forces and three-cluster dynamics enables more accurate predictions for light-ion fusion reactions and exotic nuclei, with initial results showing that the three-nucleon force affects spin-orbit splitting in $^4$He$(n,n)^4$He scattering phase shifts and increases the binding energy of $^6$Li.
