# A New Approach to Large-Scale Nuclear Structure Calculations

> A new approach to large-scale nuclear structure calculations, based on the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG), is described. The method is tested in the context of a problem involving many identical nucleons constrained to move in a single large-j shell and interacting via a pairing plus quadrupole interaction. In cases in which exact diagonalization of the hamiltonian is possible, the method is able to reproduce the exact results for the ground state energy and the energies of low-lying excited states with extreme precision. Results are also presented for a model problem in which exact solution is not feasible.

## Key claim

The Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method can reproduce exact ground-state and low-lying excited-state energies with extreme precision for a pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction in a single large-j shell, provided a well-defined Fermi surface exists; the method's accuracy degrades if different intrinsic structures are present.

## Method

The DMRG method iteratively adds single-particle levels nearest the Fermi surface, diagonalizes the Hamiltonian for the enlarged system, constructs reduced density matrices for particles and holes from the ground-state wavefunction, and retains only the m eigenvectors with the largest eigenvalues for each subspace. This optimal truncation is repeated until all particle and hole levels are sampled, with calculations performed as a function of the number of retained states m.

## Key numbers

- For the picket-fence pairing model with Ω=400 levels and λ=0.224, the DMRG method with m=60 gave a ground-state correlation energy of -22.5168 in units of level spacing d, compared to the Richardson solution of -22.5183141 d (agreement better than 1 part in 10^4); the maximum Hamiltonian dimension treated was 3066.
- For 10 particles in a j=25/2 shell with χ=1, g=0, and ε=0.1, the DMRG ground-state energy at m=60 was off by only 1 part in 10^6; the largest matrix diagonalized was 398×398.
- For 10 particles in a j=25/2 shell with χ=1, g=0.05, and ε=0.1, the DMRG ground-state energy at m=80 was -16.00151 versus the exact Lanczos result of -16.00367; the maximum Hamiltonian dimension was 685.
- For 20 particles in a j=55/2 shell with χ=1, g=0.1, and ε=0.1, the exact calculation would involve a matrix of dimension 5.31064×10^13; DMRG results converged by m=60 to about six significant figures.

## Context

This work extends the DMRG method, originally developed for low-dimensional quantum lattice systems (White, 1992) and later applied to finite Fermi systems in a pairing problem for ultrasmall superconducting grains (Dukelsky and Sierra, 1999), to nuclear structure calculations. It contrasts with traditional shell-model truncation approaches based on weak-coupling considerations, symmetry considerations, or Monte Carlo sampling (e.g., the work on Barium isotopes by Langanke and collaborators). The method enables large-scale shell-model calculations for heavier nuclei or nuclei far from closed shells where exact diagonalization is infeasible, with the authors planning to extend it to problems involving both neutrons and protons in non-degenerate single-particle levels with general interactions.
