# Relativistic Equation of State of Nuclear Matter for Supernova and Neutron Star

> We construct the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter using the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory in the wide density, temperature range with various proton fractions for the use of supernova simulation and the neutron star calculations. We first construct the EOS of homogeneous nuclear matter. We use then the Thomas-Fermi approximation to describe inhomogeneous matter, where heavy nuclei are formed together with free nucleon gas. We discuss the results on free energy, pressure and entropy in the wide range of astrophysical interest. As an example, we apply the resulting EOS on the neutron star properties by using the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a complete equation of state (EOS) table for nuclear matter using relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, covering a wider density, temperature, and proton fraction range than previous non-relativistic tables, with the explicit condition that the non-uniform matter phase (heavy nuclei plus free nucleon gas) disappears above approximately 15 MeV temperature.

## Method
The authors first compute the EOS of homogeneous nuclear matter using RMF theory with the TM1 parameter set, which includes non-linear sigma and omega meson self-interactions. They then apply the Thomas-Fermi approximation within a Wigner-Seitz cell to describe inhomogeneous matter, where a single species of heavy spherical nucleus sits in a body-centered-cubic lattice surrounded by free neutron and proton gas. The free energy is minimized with respect to seven independent parameters (cell size, interior and exterior densities, radii, and surface diffuseness for neutrons and protons) at each density, temperature, and proton fraction to determine the thermodynamically favorable phase.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy: 36.9 MeV; incompressibility: 281 MeV (from the TM1 parameter set)
- Density range covered: baryon mass density from 10^5 to 10^15.5 g/cm^3
- Temperature range covered: 0 to 50 MeV
- Proton fraction range covered: 0.01 to 0.5
- Phase transition from non-uniform to uniform matter occurs around baryon mass density ~10^14 g/cm^3, nearly independent of temperature
- Non-uniform matter phase disappears when temperature exceeds ~15 MeV
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, central baryon density is about 1.8 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0) and central proton fraction is about 0.19
- Surface energy parameter F_0 = 70 MeV·fm^5, determined by fitting finite nuclei properties

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock EOS tables (e.g., Lattimer and Swesty 1991) by using a relativistic framework that provides stronger density-dependent repulsion at high densities, consistent with relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. The resulting EOS table is designed for supernova simulations and neutron star calculations, enabling consistent treatment of both homogeneous and inhomogeneous matter within the same RMF theory, which previous tables often lacked due to discontinuities or incomplete coverage.
