# Rare $β$p decays in light nuclei

> Beta-delayed proton emission may occur at very low rates in the decays of the light nuclei $^{11}$Be and $^8$B. This paper explores the potential physical significance of such decays, estimates their rates and reports on first attempts to detect them: an experiment at ISOLDE/CERN gives a branching ratio for $^{11}$Be of $(2.5 \pm 2.5) \cdot 10^{-6}$ and an experiment at JYFL a 95% confidence upper limit of $2.6 \cdot 10^{-5}$ for $^8$B.

# Rare βp decays in light nuclei

## Key claim

Beta-delayed proton emission may occur in the decays of ¹¹Be and ⁸B with branching ratios likely a few times 10⁻⁸, but the first experimental searches only set upper limits that do not yet reach the predicted sensitivity levels; the ¹¹Be measurement gives a branching ratio consistent with zero at (2.5 ± 2.5)·10⁻⁶, and the ⁸B measurement sets a 95% confidence upper limit of 2.6·10⁻⁵.

## Method

For ¹¹Be, the authors collected 5.2·10¹¹ atoms at ISOLDE/CERN by implanting a laser-ionized beam into a Be foil, quantified the source via gamma-ray spectroscopy of the 2124 keV line, and later measured the ¹⁰Be content of the foil using accelerator mass spectrometry (AMS) at the Uppsala Tandem Laboratory to infer the βp branching ratio. For ⁸B, the experiment at JYFL used four DSSSD detectors backed by thick Si detectors in close geometry to record 16 million decays, searching for a 337 keV proton peak from electron capture to the 17.640 MeV state in ⁸Be, with background suppression via anti-coincidence requirements.

## Key numbers

- ¹¹Be βp branching ratio: (2.5 ± 2.5)·10⁻⁶ (one sigma uncertainty), derived from (1.28 ± 1.29)·10⁶ ¹⁰Be atoms in the catcher foil and (5.2 ± 0.3)·10¹¹ ¹¹Be atoms collected.
- ⁸B βp branching ratio: 95% confidence upper limit of 2.6·10⁻⁵, based on an upper limit of 18 counts in a 337 keV peak after background suppression.
- ¹¹Be Q-value for βp decay: 280.7 ± 0.3 keV, leaving an energy window of 281 keV for proton emission.
- ¹¹Be source intensity during collection: 3.6·10⁶ ions/s average over approximately 40 hours.
- ¹¹Be gamma branching ratio for the 2124.47 keV line: 0.355 ± 0.018.
- ⁸B predicted βp branching ratio from scaling the ⁷Be ground-state decay: 2.3·10⁻⁸.
- ¹¹Be predicted βp branching ratio from a two-body potential model (Bay11): 3.0·10⁻⁸.

## Context

This work extends the search for beta-delayed proton emission to the lightest halo nuclei, ¹¹Be and ⁸B, where the decay mode is intimately connected to the halo structure through factorization of the wavefunction into core and halo parts (Nil00). The theoretical estimates use R-matrix sequential decay models, emission-before-decay formalisms (Bar94), and direct-to-continuum models (Rii90), with a recent two-body potential model calculation (Bay11) giving a branching ratio of 3.0·10⁻⁸ for ¹¹Be. The experimental results do not yet reach the predicted sensitivity; improvements by factors of 10² for ¹¹Be and 10³ for ⁸B are needed, potentially achievable through better sample handling (reducing stable Be carrier in AMS) and dedicated setups with particle identification.
