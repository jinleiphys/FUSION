# Low-Lying 2+ states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes in quasiparticle random phase approximation

> The properties of the low-lying, collective 2+ states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes are investigated in the framework of self-consistent microscopic models with effective Skyrme interactions. In RPA the excitation energies E2+ can be well described but the transition probabilities are much too small as compared to experiment. Pairing correlations are then accounted for by performing quasiparticle RPA calculations. This improves considerably the predictions of B(E2) values and it enables one to calculate more reliably the ratios Mn/Mp of neutron-to-proton transition amplitudes. A satisfactory agreement with the existing experimental values of Mn/Mp is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations with Skyrme interactions can achieve satisfactory agreement with experimental B(E2) values and neutron-to-proton transition amplitude ratios for low-lying 2+ states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, but only when pairing correlations are included; standard RPA without pairing fails to reproduce the transition probabilities, and QRPA actually worsens the agreement for excitation energies compared to RPA.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-BCS (HFBCS) calculations for the ground states of 18O, 20O, and 22O using three Skyrme interactions (SIII, SGII, SLy4), with a constant pairing gap of 12 A^{-1/2} MeV. They then solved the RPA and QRPA equations in configuration space to obtain excitation energies, B(E2) transition probabilities, and neutron-to-proton transition amplitude ratios for the first 2+ states, using the same effective interactions to determine both the mean field and the residual particle-hole interaction.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gap parameter: Delta = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV (constant gap approximation)
- Binding energy per particle for 18O with SIII: 7.74 MeV (HF), 7.43 MeV (BCS); with SGII: 8.30 MeV (HF), 7.95 MeV (BCS); with SLy4: 7.74 MeV (HF), 7.42 MeV (BCS)
- Neutron r.m.s. radius for 18O with SIII: 2.77 fm (HF), 2.79 fm (BCS); for 22O with SIII: 2.97 fm (HF), 3.03 fm (BCS)
- Proton r.m.s. radius for 18O with SIII: 2.65 fm (HF), 2.67 fm (BCS); for 22O with SIII: 2.69 fm (HF), 2.71 fm (BCS)
- Pairing correlations decrease binding energy per particle by about 4% in all cases
- QRPA results with SGII give Mn/Mp ratios approximately (1.8 - 2.0) times N/Z for the low-lying 2+ state

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent RPA calculations of giant resonances (Nguyen Van Giai and Sagawa, 1981) and low-lying collective states in closed-shell nuclei (Liu and Brown, 1976) to open-shell, neutron-rich oxygen isotopes by incorporating pairing correlations via HFBCS and QRPA. The calculations are motivated by recent proton scattering experiments on 20O (Jewell et al., 1999) and earlier combined analyses of electromagnetic and hadronic scattering data (Bernstein, Brown, and Madsen, 1983). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming proton scattering data on 18O and 20O (Khan et al., 1999) and establish a need for experimental measurements on more neutron-rich isotopes like 22O to test the predicted trend of Mn/Mp as a function of N/Z.
