# Single particle spectra from information theory point of view

> It is demonstrated how to obtain the least biased description of the single particle spectra measured in all multiparticle production processes by using information theory approach (known also as MaxEnt approach). The case of e+e- annihilation in hadrons process is discussed in more detail as an example. Comparison between MaxEnt approach and simple dynamical model based on the cascade process is presented as well.

# Single particle spectra from information theory point of view

## Key claim
The MaxEnt (maximum entropy) approach, using nonextensive Tsallis statistics with a single free parameter q, can describe single particle rapidity distributions in e+e- annihilation, but only when q < 1 (contrasting with the q > 1 needed for pT distributions in the same processes), and the cascade hadronization model cannot fit the same data with any reasonable choice of decay parameters.

## Method
The authors maximized information entropy (Shannon or Tsallis form) subject to energy conservation constraints to obtain the probability distribution p(y) = (1/N) dN/dy for single particle rapidity distributions, where the normalization constant Z_q and inverse temperature β_q are determined by the normalization condition and energy conservation rather than being free fit parameters. They compared this MaxEnt approach to a sequential decay cascade model in which particles decay via M_l → M_{l+1}^{(1)} + M_{l+1}^{(2)} with decay parameters k_i distributed according to P(k), producing a random-walk-like rapidity distribution. Both approaches were tested against e+e- annihilation data from ALEPH.

## Key numbers
- For e+e- annihilation data, the MaxEnt fit with total inelasticity K_q = 1 and q = 0.6 produces quite good agreement with data.
- The cascade model (CAS) produces a gaussian-like dN/dy shape, but no reasonable values of the decay parameters k_i can fit the data.
- For e+e- processes, K_q = 1 by definition (the whole energy W must be used for production, with no leading particles).
- The temperature parameter depends on energy as T = 1/β_q = T_0 - (1-q)E.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous MaxEnt analyses of pp and p\bar{p} collisions (Navarra, Utyuzh, Wilk, Wlodarczyk, 2002-2003) and nuclear collisions to the case of e+e- annihilation, where the condition K_q = 1 (no leading particles) forces q < 1 instead of the q > 1 values needed for pT distributions in the same processes (Bediaga, 2000). The approach enables a minimal-assumption description of multiparticle production data that can be systematically improved by incorporating additional information when discrepancies appear at small and large rapidities.
