# The basic K nuclear cluster K- pp and its enhanced formation in the p + p -> K+ + X reaction

> We have studied the structure of K- pp nuclear cluster comprehensively by solving this three-body system exactly in a variational method starting from the Ansatz that the Lambda(1405) resonance (Lambda*) is a K-p bound state. We have found that our original prediction for the presence of K-pp as a compact bound system with M = 2322$ MeV/c2, B = 48 MeV and Gamma = 60 MeV remains unchanged by varying the Kba-rN and NN interactions widely as far as they reproduce Lambda(1405). The structure of K- pp reveals a molecular feature, namely, the K- in Lambda* as an "atomic center" plays a key role in producing strong covalent bonding with the other proton. We have shown that the elementary process, p + p -> K+ + Lambda* + p, which occurs in a short impact parameter and with a large momentum transfer (Q ~ 1.6$ GeV/c), leads to unusually large self-trapping of Lambda* by the participating proton, since the Lambda*-p system exists as a compact doorway state propagating to K- pp (R{Lambda*-p} ~ 1.67 fm).

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $K^-pp$ nuclear cluster exists as a compact bound system with mass $M = 2322$ MeV/$c^2$, binding energy $B_K = 48$ MeV, and width $\Gamma = 60$ MeV, and that this result remains robust when varying the $\bar{K}N$ and $NN$ interactions widely, as long as they reproduce the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. The abstract glosses over the condition that this robustness holds specifically for the "decaying state" treatment rather than the "pole state" used in some Faddeev calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body $\bar{K}NN$ system exactly using a variational method called "Amalgamation of Two-body correlations into Multiple Scattering process" (ATMS), which constructs realistic wave functions with correlation functions for each constituent pair based on Watson's multiple scattering theory. They employed semi-empirically deduced $\bar{K}N$ and $NN$ interactions, starting from the Ansatz that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is a $K^-p$ bound state, and used energy-independent single-channel complex $\bar{K}N$ potentials derived from coupled-channel interactions via Feshbach's projection operator procedure.

## Key numbers

- $K^-pp$ bound state: mass $M = 2322$ MeV/$c^2$, binding energy $B_K = 48$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 61$ MeV (later refined to 54 MeV with energy-dependent imaginary part)
- $\Lambda(1405)$ parameters used: binding energy $B_K = 27$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 40$ MeV
- rms $p-p$ distance in $K^-pp$: 1.90 fm
- rms $N$-$(\bar{K}N)$ distance: 1.67 fm
- rms $\bar{K}$-$(NN)$ distance: 1.35 fm
- rms $K^-$-$p$ distance in $\Lambda(1405)$: 1.36 fm
- rms $K^-$-$N$ distance in $K^-pp$: 1.57 fm
- Momentum transfer in $p + p \rightarrow K^+ + \Lambda^* + p$: $Q \sim 1.6$ GeV/$c$
- $\bar{K}N$ interaction range parameter: $b = 0.66$ fm (Gaussian)
- $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$ potential strength: $V_0 = -595$ MeV, $W_0 = -83$ MeV
- $I=1$ $\bar{K}N$ potential strength: $V_0 = -175$ MeV, $W_0 = -105$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier prediction of $K^-pp$ as a kaonic dibaryon (Yamazaki 2002) and provides a comprehensive validation against varying interaction parameters. It contradicts the claim by Oset and Toki (2006) that the authors' scattering amplitudes are too large compared to chiral unitary approaches, showing instead that their interaction range is consistent with chiral dynamics results (Kaiser 1997, Cieply 2001, Borasoy 2005). The molecular interpretation of $K^-pp$ as a kaonic hydrogen molecule with a migrating real $K^-$ producing a "super strong nuclear force" revives the Heitler-London-Heisenberg scheme (Heitler 1927, Heisenberg 1932) and enables future experimental searches for $\bar{K}$ nuclear clusters via the $p + p \rightarrow K^+ + X$ reaction, as proposed for the FOPI detector at GSI.
