# Hypernuclei as chiral solitons

## Key claim

The identification of flavored multiskyrmions with the ground states of known hypernuclei is successful for several light systems (isodoublet \(_\Lambda^4H\)-\(_\Lambda^4He\), isoscalars \(_\Lambda^5He\) and \(_\Lambda^7Li\)), but for other cases agreement is only qualitative; the binding energy behavior with increasing baryon number follows data trends, and charmed or beauty hypernuclei are predicted to be bound more strongly than strange hypernuclei, a conclusion stable against variation of poorly known heavy flavor decay constants.

## Method

The author uses the rigid oscillator version of the bound state approach to quantize SU(2) multiskyrmions embedded into SU(3) configuration space, starting from the rational map ansatz for the chiral field configuration. Static properties (classical masses, flavor and isotopic moments of inertia, sigma-term) are computed for baryon numbers B = 1 through 16, and the mass formula (3) yields excitation energies and binding energy differences for states with strangeness, charm, or beauty relative to the unflavored ground state of the same baryon number.

## Key numbers

* Strangeness excitation energy for B=1: \(\omega_s = 306\) MeV; for \(_\Lambda^5He\): \(\omega_s = 287\) MeV, total binding energy \(\epsilon^{tot}_s = 33\) MeV (experimental: 31.4 MeV)
* For the isoscalar \(_\Lambda^7Li\): strangeness binding energy \(\epsilon^{tot}_s = 29\) MeV (experimental: 37.6 MeV); spin correction reduces the theoretical value by about 7 MeV
* For the isodoublet \(_\Lambda^4H\)-\(_\Lambda^4He\): strangeness binding energy \(\epsilon^{tot}_s = 5.3\) MeV (experimental: 10.52 and 10.11 MeV)
* Beauty excitation energy for B=1: \(\omega_b = 4501\) MeV (for \(r_b = F_B/F_\pi = 1.5\)) and 4805 MeV (for \(r_b = 2\))
* For \(_\Lambda^5He\) with beauty: total binding energy \(\epsilon^{tot}_b = 103\) MeV (\(r_b=1.5\)) and 81 MeV (\(r_b=2\))
* Charm excitation energy for B=1: \(\omega_c = 1535\) MeV (\(r_c=1.5\)) and 1673 MeV (\(r_c=2\))
* For \(_\Lambda^5Li\) with charm: total binding energy \(\epsilon^{tot}_c = 52\) MeV for both \(r_c=1.5\) and \(r_c=2\)
* The method underestimates strangeness binding beginning at B = A ~ 9; the overall uncertainty of the approach is about 30 MeV, as illustrated by the deuteron binding energy coming out as 30 MeV instead of the measured 2.225 MeV

## Context

This work extends the topological soliton approach to hypernuclei, building on the rigid oscillator quantization method of Kopeliovich (2000) and the rational map ansatz of Houghton, Manton, and Sutcliffe (1998). It provides binding energy predictions for strange, charmed, and beauty hypernuclei that can be compared with potential model calculations by Dover and Kahana (1977) and with experimental data compiled in standard hypernuclear reviews. The results enable further study of hypernuclei with flavor number \(|F| \geq 2\) and of systems with mixed flavors, and they support the applicability of chiral soliton models to realistic nuclear properties.
