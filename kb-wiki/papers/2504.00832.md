# Universal critical behavior of generaliezd susceptibilities of net-baryon number at small quark mass

> In the limit of small quark masses, the angle between the temperature axis and the applied magnetic field direction in the three-dimensional Ising model vanishes as $m_q^{2/5}$ when mapped onto the QCD $T-μ_B$ phase plane. By selecting two distinct small angles and projecting the Ising model results onto QCD, we have investigated the universal critical behavior of the sixth-, eighth-, and tenth-order susceptibilities of the net-baryon number. When considering only the leading critical contribution, the negative dip in the $μ_B$ dependence of the generalized susceptibilities is not universal, in contrast to the observation in the case where the angle is $90^{\circ}$. Its existence depends on the mapping parameters and the distance to the phase transition line. After incorporating the sub-leading critical contribution, the negative dip is enhanced to some extent but remains a non-robust feature. In contrast, the positive peak structure persists in all cases and represents a robust characteristic of generalized susceptibilities of the net-baryon number near the critical point.

## Key claim

In the limit of small quark masses, where the angle between the temperature axis and the magnetic field direction in the three-dimensional Ising model vanishes as \(m_q^{2/5}\) when mapped onto the QCD \(T-\mu_B\) phase plane, the negative dip in the \(\mu_B\) dependence of the sixth-, eighth-, and tenth-order generalized susceptibilities is not a universal feature; its existence depends on the mapping parameters and the distance to the phase transition line, whereas the positive peak structure remains robust.

## Method

The authors used the parametric representation of the three-dimensional Ising model to compute the magnetization, equation of state, and free energy density, then applied a linear mapping with six parameters (including angles \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\)) to project the Ising results onto the QCD \(T-\mu_B\) phase plane. They selected two distinct small angles (\(\alpha_2 = 1.8^\circ\) and \(\alpha_2 = 7.8^\circ\)) with \(\alpha_1 = 10.8^\circ\) fixed, and varied the scaling parameters \(w\) and \(\rho\) to compute the sixth-, eighth-, and tenth-order susceptibilities of the net-baryon number, both with only the leading critical contribution and with the sub-leading critical contribution included. The susceptibilities were evaluated along freeze-out curves parallel to the QCD phase transition line at different temperature shifts \(\Delta T\).

## Key numbers

* The angle between the temperature axis and the applied magnetic field direction vanishes as \(m_q^{2/5}\) in the limit of small quark masses.
* The critical exponents used are \(\beta \simeq 0.326\), \(\delta \simeq 4.8\), and \(\alpha \simeq 0.11\) for the three-dimensional Ising universality class.
* The normalization constants for the Ising model are \(m_0 = 0.605\) and \(h_0 = 0.364\).
* The QCD critical point is taken at \((T_C, \mu_{BC}) = (107, 635)\) MeV, with \(T_0 = 156.5\) MeV, \(\kappa = 0.015\), and \(\lambda = 0.000256\) for the phase transition line.
* For \(\alpha_2 = 1.8^\circ\) with \((w, \rho) = (0.5, 0.5)\) and \(\Delta T = 0.2\) MeV, the ratio of the depth of the negative dip to the height of the positive peak for \(\chi_6^{B,L}\) is 0.1294, for \(\chi_8^{B,L}\) is 0.222, and for \(\chi_{10}^{B,L}\) is 0.2922; after including sub-leading contributions, these ratios become 0.1742, 0.2779, and 0.3547, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal critical behavior of net-baryon number susceptibilities by Stephanov (2011) and earlier work by Pan et al. (2016, 2025), which assumed the Ising model \(h\) axis is orthogonal to the \(t\) axis (angle \(90^\circ\)). The authors instead adopt the mapping proposed by Stephanov (2020) where, in the limit of small quark masses, the angle between the \(t\) and \(h\) axes vanishes as \(m_q^{2/5}\), leading to \(0 < \alpha_2 < \alpha_1\). This enables a systematic investigation of whether the negative dip in high-order susceptibilities is a robust critical signature under more realistic mapping conditions, and it motivates future experimental searches for the QCD critical point using the positive peak structure rather than the negative dip.
