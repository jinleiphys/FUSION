# Proton-skins in momentum space and neutron-skins in coordinate space in heavy nuclei

> Neutron-skins in coordinate space and proton-skins in momentum space are predicted to coexist in heavy nuclei and their correlation is governed by Liouville's theorem and Heisenberg's uncertainty principle. An analysis of their correlation within a further extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF$^+$) approximation incorporating effects of nucleon short-range correlations reveals generally that protons move faster than neutrons in neutron-skins of heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

Neutron-skins in coordinate space and proton-skins in momentum space coexist in heavy nuclei, and their correlation is governed by Liouville's theorem and Heisenberg's uncertainty principle; within a further extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF+) approximation that incorporates nucleon short-range correlations, protons move faster than neutrons in neutron-skins of heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors extended the Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation by incorporating short-range correlation (SRC) effects through a parameterized single-nucleon momentum distribution featuring a high-momentum tail (HMT) with a 1/k^4 behavior, constrained by experimental data and microscopic many-body calculations. They applied this ETF+ framework to 208Pb using a 2-parameter Fermi distribution for nucleon density profiles, with proton parameters fixed by experiment and neutron parameters varied within known uncertainties, to compute local kinetic energy densities and average kinetic energies for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

* For the HMT-exp parameter set in isospin-asymmetric matter with delta=0.21: the SRC enhancement factor for protons is Phi_p=2.09 and for neutrons is Phi_n=1.60
* For the HMT-SCGF parameter set at the same asymmetry: Phi_p=1.21 and Phi_n=1.14
* The average kinetic energy of protons in 208Pb is approximately 41.9 MeV and of neutrons approximately 34.0 MeV using the HMT-exp parameter set (Delta_E_pn^kin approximately 7.9 +/- 2.7 MeV)
* The neutron-skin thickness Delta_r_np for 208Pb is explored in the range 0.01 to 0.43 fm, with a fiducial value of approximately 0.159 fm
* For the HMT-exp parameter set with Delta_r_np approximately 0.159 fm: the surface strength factor eta_p is approximately 26.7 and eta_n ranges from 7.2 to 9.2
* For the HMT-SCGF parameter set: eta_p is approximately 45.9 and eta_n ranges from 41.5 to 46.5

## Context

This work extends the understanding of neutron-skins in heavy nuclei, which have been studied using methods from photopion production to parity-violating electron scatterings (reviewed by Vinas et al. 2014, Tamii et al. 2014, Horowitz et al. 2014), by introducing the complementary concept of proton-skins in momentum space. It builds on experimental findings by Hen et al. (2014) showing that minority particles in isospin-asymmetric nuclei are more correlated, and on theoretical predictions from Variational Monte Carlo calculations (Wiringa et al. 2014) and the neutron-proton dominance model that protons have higher average kinetic energies than neutrons in neutron-rich nuclei. The correlation between the two types of skins provides a new combined constraint that can help improve nuclear models and potentially resolve discrepancies between different predictions of neutron-skin sizes.
