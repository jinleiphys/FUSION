# Fluctuations In The Inhomogeneous Chiral Transition

> Chiral pair fluctuation are considered near the phase boundary of the inhomogeneous chiral phase (iCP). The fluctuations are then bosonized and an effective action for the chiral pair fluctuation is basically constructed by considering the ring diagram of the polarization function. We can evaluate the self-energy and effective four point interaction among fluctuations in a consistent way. The peculiar dispersion of the fluctuation, reflecting the spatially inhomogeneous transition, gives rise to interesting and qualitative results. Thermal fluctuations prohibit the second-order transition, while the effect of the quantum fluctuations is rather modest. Quantum and thermal fluctuations changes the second-order transition to the first one by changing the sign of the effective four-point interaction between effective mesons. These features may be observed by relativistic heavy-ion collisions through the analysis of the thermodynamic observables. Distinct from the second-order phase transition, the first moment such as entropy production exhibits an anomalous behavior due to fluctuations, which is one of the signals of the phase transition to iCP. Some similar aspects are also remarked between iCP and the FFLO state in superconductivity.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations prohibit the second-order phase transition to the inhomogeneous chiral phase (iCP), while quantum fluctuations have a modest effect; both types of fluctuations change the second-order transition to a first-order one by altering the sign of the effective four-point interaction between effective mesons.

## Method

The authors start with the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit and introduce auxiliary fields representing quark bilinears. After integrating out the quark fields, they construct an effective action for chiral pair fluctuations by summing ring diagrams of the polarization function. They evaluate the self-energy and effective four-point interaction among fluctuations in a consistent way, separating the condensate from fluctuations and computing renormalized vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- The static polarization function exhibits an extremum at non-zero momentum \(q_c\), where \(\partial^2\Pi^0_{ps}(0,\mathbf{q})/\partial q^2|_{q=0} < 0\).
- The bare Green function for chiral pair fluctuations behaves as \(G_{ps}(i\nu_n,\mathbf{q})^{-1} \sim \tau + \gamma(|\mathbf{q}|^2 - q_c^2)^2 + \alpha|\nu_n|\) near the phase boundary.
- At finite temperature, the renormalized parameter \(\tau_R\) satisfies \(\tau \simeq \tau_R - \frac{\lambda T q_c}{4\pi\gamma^{1/2}\tau_R^{1/2}}\), preventing \(\tau_R\) from reaching zero.
- At zero temperature, the relation is \(\tau \simeq \tau_R - \frac{\lambda\Lambda}{48\alpha\pi^{5/2}\gamma^{3/2}q_c^3}\) with ultraviolet cut-off \(\Lambda\).
- The bubble sum \(L(0)\) at finite temperature behaves as \(L(0) \simeq \frac{T q_c}{8\pi\gamma^{1/2}}\frac{1}{\tau_R^{3/2}}\); at zero temperature, \(L(0) \simeq \frac{q_c}{4\alpha\pi^2\gamma^{1/2}}\frac{1}{\tau_R^{1/2}}\).
- The entropy density contribution from fluctuations scales as \(\Delta s \propto -1/\tau_R^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of the inhomogeneous chiral phase (iCP) in QCD, previously examined within the NJL model for configurations such as the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) by Tatsumi and collaborators and the real kink crystal (RKC) by Nickel. The authors build on their own earlier work (Lee and Tatsumi, 2016) on Nambu-Goldstone mode stability in iCP, and apply the Brazovskii-Dyugaev effect, originally developed for diblock copolymers and liquid crystals, to the chiral transition. The formalism parallels the Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink theory for superconducting phase transitions and may also apply to the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state in superconductivity. The results enable predictions for thermodynamic observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, such as anomalous entropy production as a signal of the iCP transition.
