# Rigorous proof of the Strutinsky energy theorem and foundations of nuclear density functional theory

> We have derived a rigorous theoretical proof of the Strutinsky energy theorem. This proof not only provides a proper interpretation of the shell-correction decomposition, resolving decades of confusion, but also lays a foundation for constructing nuclear density functionals.

# Rigorous proof of the Strutinsky energy theorem and foundations of nuclear density functional theory

## Key claim

The paper provides a formal proof of the Strutinsky energy theorem using density functional theory (DFT), showing that the total nuclear energy can be decomposed into a smooth functional of a reference density plus a first-order correction, without relying on the conventional (and conceptually flawed) interpretation involving a smoothed single-particle level density. The proof establishes that the separation is valid only when the reference density is variationally admissible and remains in the neighborhood of the true ground-state density, not for an arbitrary background.

## Method

The author derives the Strutinsky energy theorem by expanding the total energy functional E[ρ] as a Taylor series around a reference density ρ̃, yielding E[ρ] = E[ρ̃] + ∫ (δE/δρ)|ρ̃ (ρ − ρ̃) d³r + O(δρ²). The first-order correction δE⁽¹⁾ is expressed in terms of the effective single-particle Hamiltonian h̃ evaluated at the reference density, and is shown to equal the sum of single-particle energies from the Hamiltonian h̃[ρ̃] minus the expectation value of h̃ with respect to the reference orbitals. The smooth component E[ρ̃] is identified with the macroscopic liquid-drop energy, while the first-order term captures all oscillatory shell contributions.

## Context

This work resolves decades of conceptual confusion surrounding the Strutinsky shell-correction method, originally developed by Strutinsky (1967, 1968) and subsequently adopted in standard textbooks by Bohr and Mottelson (1998) and Ring and Schuck (1980). Prior attempts to justify the energy theorem relied on Hartree–Fock arguments (Strutinsky 1968, Bunatian 1972, Brack 1972) that did not provide a consistent derivation, while semiclassical approaches (Gross 1972, Brack 1973, 1975, Jennings 1973, 1975) operated only at the level of the single-particle density of states without establishing a variational decomposition of the total energy. The new DFT-based proof enables systematic construction of nuclear density functionals by constraining single-particle spectra at the first-order correction level, and can be extended to include pairing correlations within density matrix functional theory.
