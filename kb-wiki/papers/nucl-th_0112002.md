# Neutron stars and the fermionic Casimir effect

> The inner crust of neutron stars consists of nuclei of various shapes immersed in a neutron gas and stabilized by the Coulomb interaction in the form of a crystal lattice. The scattering of neutrons on nuclear inhomegeneities leads to the quantum correction to the total energy of the system. This correction resembles the Casimir energy and turns out to have a large influence on the structure of the crust.

## Key claim

The quantum correction to the total energy of the inner crust of a neutron star, arising from the scattering of unbound neutrons on nuclear inhomogeneities and termed the fermionic Casimir effect, has a large influence on the crust structure. This correction is crucial because the energy differences between various nuclear phases (spheres, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) are very small (of the order of a few keV/fm³), so the shell energy from unbound neutrons can easily reverse the phase transition order predicted by liquid-drop-based approaches.

## Method

The authors solved Hartree-Fock equations for neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter at fixed density in a cubic box with three symmetry planes, generating lattice geometries such as simple cubic, face-centered cubic, and body-centered cubic. The total energy functional included volume, surface, Coulomb, and shell terms, with the shell energy for unbound neutrons computed using a semiclassical approximation based on the Gutzwiller trace formula. The nuclear potential was approximately replaced by an effective repulsive potential of the same shape, and the leading shell energy contribution was derived for spherical, cylindrical, and planar obstacles.

## Key numbers

- The average density of a typical neutron star is 3-4 times larger than the density inside an atomic nucleus.
- In the neutron star crust, the nucleon density does not exceed 0.1 fm⁻³.
- The width of the crust layer reaches almost 10% of the star radius.
- The outer crust becomes relativistic at about 10⁶ g cm⁻³.
- Five nuclear phases are formed in the region where nucleon density varies from 0.03 to 0.1 fm⁻³.
- The neutron Fermi momentum kⁿF is of the order of 1 fm⁻¹, so kⁿF R > 1 where R is the size of an inhomogeneity.
- The energy differences between various nuclear phases are of the order of a few keV/fm³.
- The lattice constant used in one calculation was 26 fm (left subfigure) and 20.8 fm (right subfigure).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical efforts on neutron star crust structure (e.g., BBP, Oya2, PRA, DHM, DHA) which had either neglected shell effects entirely (as in liquid drop or Thomas-Fermi models) or included them only for bound nucleons. The authors show that the shell energy from unbound neutrons, analogous to the Casimir effect studied for simple geometries in earlier work (BMA1, BMA2, BMA3, BWI, BMA4), can reverse the phase transition order predicted by liquid-drop approaches and may increase the number of phase transitions. This enables future studies to consider distorted lattices or lattices with defects that decrease the shell energy, and suggests that the fermionic Casimir effect is responsible for determining the inner crust structure.
