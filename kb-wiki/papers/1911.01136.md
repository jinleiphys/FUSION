# The dipole picture and the non-relativistic expansion

> We study exclusive quarkonium production in the dipole picture at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy, using the non-relativistic expansion for the quarkonium wavefunction. This process offers one of the best ways to obtain information about gluon distributions at small $x$, in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions and in deep inelastic scattering. The quarkonium light cone wave functions needed in the dipole picture have typically been available only at tree level, either in phenomenological models or in the nonrelativistic limit. In this paper, we discuss the compatibility of the dipole approach and the non-relativistic expansion and compute NLO relativistic corrections to the quarkonium light-cone wave function in light-cone gauge. Using these corrections we recover results for the NLO decay width of quarkonium to $e^{+}e^{-}$ and we check that the non-relativistic expansion is consistent with ERBL evolution and with B-JIMWLK evolution of the target. The results presented here will allow computing the exclusive quarkonium production rate at NLO once the one loop photon wave function with massive quarks, currently under investigation, is known.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that NLO radiative corrections to exclusive quarkonium production in the dipole picture can be computed using only the leading non-relativistic quarkonium wave function, without needing gradient corrections (which are suppressed by \(v^2\)), provided the saturation scale and heavy quark mass are much larger than \(mv\) and \(\Lambda_{QCD}\).

## Method

The authors compute the NLO relativistic corrections to the quarkonium light-cone wave function in light-cone gauge, separating contributions into three types: relativistic particle components, wave function renormalization of non-relativistic particles, and corrections to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. They then use these corrections to compute the coefficient functions \(C_{q\bar{q}\gets q\bar{q}}^{0}\) and \(C_{q\bar{q}g\gets q\bar{q}}^{0}\) at order \(\alpha_s\), and verify consistency with ERBL evolution and B-JIMWLK evolution of the target.

## Key numbers

- The wave function renormalization constant is \(Z = 1 + \delta Z + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\), with \(\delta Z = -\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi}\left[\frac{1}{D-4}(4\log x_0+3) + 2\log x_0\left(\log\left(\frac{m^2}{4\pi\mu^2}\right)+1+\log x_0\right) + (4\log x_0+3)\frac{\gamma_E}{2} + \frac{3}{2}\log\left(\frac{m^2}{4\pi\mu^2}\right) - 2\right]\), where \(x_0\) is an infrared cutoff in the gluon momentum fraction.
- The binding energy satisfies \(M_{HQ} - 2m \sim mv^2 \ll m\), with the typical quark three-momentum of order \(mv\).
- The velocity expansion parameter satisfies \(v \sim \alpha_s(mv)\), making \(v^2 \ll \alpha_s(m)\) in the strict weak coupling power counting.

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture approach to exclusive quarkonium production (previously limited to tree-level wave functions in phenomenological models like the "boosted gaussian" or "gaussLC" from Kowalski (2006)) by incorporating NLO relativistic corrections. It bridges the dipole formalism with NRQCD factorization (Bodwin, 1994) and checks consistency with ERBL evolution (Efremov, 1979; Lepage, 1980) and B-JIMWLK evolution (Balitsky, 1995; Kovchegov, 1999). The results enable future NLO calculations of exclusive quarkonium production rates once the one-loop photon wave function with massive quarks becomes available.
