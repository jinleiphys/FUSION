# QCD Critical Point: Synergy of Lattice & Experiments

> The freeze-out curve, which describes a vast amount of precise experimental data in heavy ion collisions, provides a relation between the colliding energy and the thermodynamical parameters of the fireball. The variance, skew and kurtosis of the event distribution of baryon number are studied at several energies of interest through Pade resummations of our Lattice QCD results. A smooth behaviour is predicted for three ratios of these quantities at current RHIC and future LHC energies. Any deviations from these at the RHIC energy scan would signal the presence of a nearby critical point. Our lattice results on the critical point do show such a behaviour.

# QCD Critical Point: Synergy of Lattice & Experiments

## Key claim
Lattice QCD results predict a smooth, monotonic behavior for three ratios of baryon number fluctuation moments (variance, skew, kurtosis) along the freeze-out curve at current RHIC and future LHC energies; any deviation from this smooth behavior observed in the RHIC energy scan would signal the presence of a nearby QCD critical point. The hedge is that the lattice results themselves do show such critical behavior at the estimated critical point, but the numerical precision is limited and the actual location of the critical point in experiments remains to be confirmed.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of staggered fermions of mass m/T_c = 0.1 on N_t = 4 and N_t = 6 lattices with spatial volumes N_s = 8, 10, 12, 16, 24 (coarser) and N_s = 12, 18, 24 (finer). They constructed Taylor expansions of the QCD pressure in baryonic chemical potential up to 8th order, computed baryonic susceptibilities via stochastic estimators, and used ratio and root methods to estimate the radius of convergence as the location of the nearest critical point. They then used Padé resummations of the susceptibility series to compute the ratios m_1, m_2, and m_3 (related to variance, skew, and kurtosis of baryon number distributions) along the experimentally determined freeze-out curve as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers
- Critical point coordinates on the finer N_t = 6 lattice: T^E/T_c = 0.94 ± 0.01, μ_B^E/T^E = 1.8 ± 0.1
- Coarser N_t = 4 lattice result: μ_B^E/T^E = 1.3 ± 0.3 for similar volumes; infinite volume extrapolation gave μ_B^E/T^E = 1.1 ± 0.1
- Overall safe estimate: μ_B/T ∼ 1–2 for the critical point location
- Lattice parameters: Goldstone pion mass of 230 MeV, with m_π/m_ρ = 0.31 ± 0.01 and m_ρ/T_c = 5.4 ± 0.2
- Temperature range covered: 0.89 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 1.92
- Measurements on 50–200 independent configurations per parameter set

## Context
This work extends the Taylor expansion approach to finite-density lattice QCD, developed independently by the TIFR group and the Bielefeld group, to make experimentally testable predictions for heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the observation by statistical thermodynamical models (such as those describing hadron abundances) that the freeze-out curve maps collision energy to thermodynamic parameters. The results enable direct non-perturbative tests of QCD in hot, dense matter by comparing lattice predictions for baryon number fluctuation ratios with experimental data from the RHIC energy scan and future facilities such as FAIR at GSI and NICA at Dubna. The STAR collaboration has already compared their net proton fluctuation data with these lattice predictions, showing good agreement for m_1 and m_2.
