# Second RPA and correlated realistic interactions

> We examine the response of closed-shell nuclei using a correlated interaction, derived with the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) from the Argonne V18 potential, in second RPA (SRPA) calculations. The same correlated two-body interaction is used to derive the Hartree-Fock ground state and the SRPA equations. Our results show that the coupling of particle-hole states to higher-order configurations produces sizable effects compared with first-order RPA. A much improved description of the isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole resonances is obtained, thanks in part to the more fundamental treatment of the nucleon effective mass offered by SRPA. The present work suggests the prospect of describing giant resonance properties realistically and consistently within extended RPA theories. Self-consistency issues of the present SRPA method and residual three-body effects are pointed out.

## Key claim
Coupling particle-hole (ph) states to 2p2h configurations within second RPA (SRPA), using a correlated interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), produces sizable corrections to first-order RPA results and yields a much improved description of the isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole resonances, though the isoscalar monopole resonance energy remains underestimated, pointing to missing three-body effects.

## Method
The authors used the UCOM-correlated two-body interaction V_UCOM (derived from the Argonne V18 potential) to compute the Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state and then solved the SRPA equations in a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis for the closed-shell nuclei 16O and 40Ca. The SRPA matrix, which couples ph and 2p2h configurations, was solved using a Lanczos procedure for matrices up to dimension 10^6, employing a diagonal approximation for the 2p2h energies (Eq. Ea22) after verifying that inclusion of 2p2h couplings did not introduce significant corrections. Transition strength distributions for isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole resonances were computed and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For 16O and 40Ca, SRPA centroid energies for the isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole resonances are much lower than RPA values, bringing them into better agreement with experiment; the isoscalar quadrupole resonance centroid energies agree very well with experimental centroids from Refs. LCY2001 (16O) and YLC2001 (40Ca).
- The calculated width (sigma = sqrt(m2/m0 - (m1/m0)^2)) of the isoscalar quadrupole resonance is 3.8 MeV for 16O and 2.5 MeV for 40Ca, compared with experimental widths of 5.1 MeV and 2.9 MeV, respectively.
- The spurious center-of-mass state appears at about 5-8 MeV in the SRPA calculations.
- The single-particle basis included states with radial quantum number up to n_max=6 and orbital angular momentum up to l_max=6; convergence of the giant resonance sum rules m0 and m1 and centroids is within about 1-2 MeV for the centroids.

## Context
This work extends previous HF and RPA calculations using the UCOM interaction (Papakonstantinou et al., 2006; Roth et al., 2006) which showed that RPA overestimated the energies of the isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole resonances by several MeV, attributed to a small nucleon effective mass from missing long-range correlations. By incorporating SRPA, which couples ph states to 2p2h configurations and effectively dresses the HF single-particle states with self-energy insertions, the authors achieve a more realistic description of these resonances. The results suggest that UCOM-based SRPA can describe giant resonance properties consistently, but the underestimated isoscalar monopole resonance energy indicates the need for a three-body term to supplement the two-body V_UCOM, and the self-consistency issues of the present SRPA method (which uses the HF ground state rather than a correlated ground state) remain to be resolved.
