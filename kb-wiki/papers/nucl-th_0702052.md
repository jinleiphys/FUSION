# Isospin effect in the statistical sequential decay

> Isospin effect of the statistical emission fragments from the equilibrated source is investigated in the frame of statistical binary decay implemented into GEMINI code, isoscaling behavior is observed and the dependences of isoscaling parameters $α$ and $β$ on emission fragment size, source size, source isospin asymmetry and excitation energies are studied. Results show that $α$ and $β$ neither depends on light fragment size nor on source size. A good linear dependence of $α$ and $β$ on the inverse of temperature $T$ is manifested and the relationship of $α=4C_{sym}[(Z_{s}/A_{s})_{1}^{2}-(Z_{s}/A_{s})_{2}^{2}]/T$ and $β=4C_{sym}[(N_{s}/A_{s})_{1}^{2}-(N_{s}/A_{s})_{2}^{2}]/T$ from different isospin asymmetry sources are satisfied. The symmetry energy coefficient $C_{sym}$ extracted from simulation results is $\sim$ 23 MeV which includes both the volume and surface term contributions, of which the surface effect seems to play a significant role in the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The isoscaling parameters α and β extracted from GEMINI simulations of statistical sequential decay are independent of both light fragment size and source size, and show a good linear dependence on the inverse of temperature T, supporting the relationships α = 4C_sym[(Z_s/A_s)_1^2 - (Z_s/A_s)_2^2]/T and β = 4C_sym[(N_s/A_s)_1^2 - (N_s/A_s)_2^2]/T, but only when the surface contribution to the symmetry energy is included.

## Method

The authors used the GEMINI code, which implements statistical sequential binary decay via a Monte Carlo technique, to simulate the decay of equilibrated compound nuclei. They considered only the first decay step to ensure well-defined source size and temperature, and calculated yield ratios of fragments from pairs of sources with the same proton number Z_s but different mass numbers A_s (and thus different isospin asymmetries N_s/Z_s). Isoscaling parameters α and β were extracted from the exponential relationship R_21(N,Z) = C exp(αN + βZ) and studied as functions of excitation energy, source size, and source isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

*   Symmetry energy coefficient C_sym extracted from the slope of α·T versus Δ(Z_s/A_s)^2 for Z_s = 50 source pairs: 23.0 ± 0.7 MeV.
*   Symmetry energy coefficient C_sym extracted from the slope of α·T versus Δ(Z_s/A_s)^2 for Z_s = 30 source pairs: 23.8 ± 0.4 MeV.
*   Effective symmetry energy coefficient deduced from the derivative of the symmetry energy with respect to neutron number in the mass formula: approximately 22.63 MeV.
*   Free neutron chemical potential difference Δμ_n for the Z_s = 30, A_s = 60 to 63 source pair: 2.79 ± 0.21 MeV.
*   Free proton chemical potential difference Δμ_p for the Z_s = 30, A_s = 60 to 63 source pair: 3.42 ± 0.33 MeV.
*   Temperatures for the Z_s = 50, A_s = 100/105 source pair ranged from 2.8/2.9 MeV at E_ex = 1.0 MeV/nucleon to 5.3/5.4 MeV at E_ex = 3.0 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the isoscaling approach, previously observed in experimental data and other model calculations such as the expanding-emitting source model (Botvina 2002) and the statistical multifragmentation model (Tsang 2001), to the statistical sequential decay framework of GEMINI. The study enables a systematic extraction of the symmetry energy coefficient from isoscaling parameters by isolating the surface contribution, which the authors find plays a significant role in the symmetry energy term. The results are consistent with the "World Consensus Initiative" book on dynamics and thermodynamics with nuclear degrees of freedom (Colonna).
