# Charmonium Suppression and Regeneration from SPS to RHIC

> The production of charmonia is investigated for heavy-ion collisions from SPS to RHIC energies. Our approach incorporates two sources of $J/Ψ$ yield: (i) a direct contribution arising from early (hard) parton-parton collisions, subject to subsequent nuclear absorption, quark-gluon plasma and hadronic dissociation, and (ii) statistical production at the hadronization transition by coalescence of $c$ and $\bar{c}$ quarks. Within an expanding thermal fireball framework, the model reproduces $J/Ψ$ centrality dependencies observed at the SPS in Pb-Pb and S-U collisions reasonably well. The study of the $Ψ'/Ψ$ ratio at SPS points at the importance of the hadronic phase for $Ψ'$ interactions, possibly related to effects of chiral symmetry restoration. Predictions are given for the centrality dependence of the $N_{J/Ψ}/N_{c\bar{c}}$ ratio at full RHIC energy. We also calculate the excitation function of this ratio. The latter exhibits a characteristic minimum structure signalling the transition from the standard $J/Ψ$ suppression scenario prevailing at SPS to dominantly thermal regeneration at collider energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a two-component model for charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions that combines primordial production (subject to subsequent dissociation in quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter) with statistical recombination of charm and anticharm quarks at hadronization, and claims that the excitation function of the $N_{J/\Psi}/N_{c\bar{c}}$ ratio exhibits a characteristic minimum structure signaling the transition from suppression-dominated to regeneration-dominated scenarios, but this result depends on uncertain extrapolations of open-charm production cross sections and charm-quark thermalization assumptions.

## Method

The authors model heavy-ion collisions using an expanding thermal fireball framework with isentropic expansion, where the space-time evolution is parameterized for SPS (17-20 AGeV) and RHIC (200 AGeV) energies. Primordial charmonium dissociation rates are computed using quasifree parton scattering in the QGP phase (with in-medium binding energies from a screened potential) and SU(4)-effective Lagrangian interactions with pions and rhos in the hadronic phase, then convolved over the fireball evolution. Statistical charmonium production at hadronization is calculated using thermal weights at the critical temperature with exact canonical charm conservation, where the charm-quark fugacity is matched to the primordial $c\bar{c}$ abundance from PYTHIA extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear absorption cross section: $\sigma_{nuc}=6.4$ mb, used to fit SPS $p$-$A$ and light-ion data
- Normalization factors for $J/\Psi$ to Drell-Yan ratio: 52.8 for $Pb$-$Pb$ and 48.2 for $S$-$U$ systems at SPS
- Strong coupling constant: $g \simeq 1.7$, giving $J/\Psi$ binding energy of 250 MeV at $T=170$ MeV, decreasing to 100 MeV at $T=230$ MeV, vanishing around $T \simeq 360$ MeV
- QGP phase durations in central collisions: 1-2 fm/c at SPS, $\sim$3 fm/c at RHIC, with mixed phases lasting 3-4 fm/c
- Critical temperature: 170 MeV at SPS (with $\mu_B=260$ MeV) increasing to 180 MeV at RHIC (with $\mu_B=27$ MeV)
- Formation time $\tau_0$: 1 fm/c at SPS ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17$ GeV) decreasing to 1/3 fm/c at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV)
- Specific entropy $S/N_B$: 26 at SPS to 250 at RHIC
- Thermal freezeout times: $\tau_{fo}\simeq 10-14$ fm/c
- $J/\Psi$ survival probability in central collisions at SPS: approximately 25% of primordial $J/\Psi$ survive the evolution
- $\Psi'$ survival probability in central collisions at SPS: approximately 1/3 remaining at the end of the collision
- $J/\Psi$ survival probability in central collisions at RHIC: approximately 25% of primordial $J/\Psi$ survive
- PYTHIA extrapolation for $\sigma_{pp}^{J/\Psi}$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV: 8.75 $\mu$b (compared to 1.0 $\mu$b from Schuler parameterization)
- PYTHIA extrapolation for $\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}$ at RHIC: $\sim 570$ $\mu$b (compared to $\sim 350$ $\mu$b from NLO pQCD)
- Open-charm production at SPS: $N_{c\bar{c}}\simeq 0.2$ for central $Pb$-$Pb$
- Open-charm production at RHIC: $N_{c\bar{c}}\simeq 10-20$ for central $Au$-$Au$
- Feeddown fractions for primordial $J/\Psi$: 60% direct $J/\Psi$, 8% from $\Psi'$, 32% from $\chi$ states

## Context

This work extends the two-component model previously introduced by the same authors (Grandchamp and Rapp, 2001) by providing detailed centrality dependencies for $S$-$U$ and $Pb$-$Pb$ at SPS and predictions for $Au$-$Au$ at RHIC, including effects of higher charmonium states and incomplete charm-quark thermalization. It contrasts with purely statistical models (Gazdzicki and Gorenstein, 1999; Braun-Munzinger and Stachel, 2000; Gorenstein et al., 2001) that attribute all charmonium production to thermal recombination, and with pure suppression models (Matsui and Satz, 1986) that consider only dissociation mechanisms. The results enable predictions for the centrality dependence of the $N_{J/\Psi}/N_{c\bar{c}}$ ratio at RHIC and the excitation function from SPS to RHIC energies, which can be tested against upcoming PHENIX data.
