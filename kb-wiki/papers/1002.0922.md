# Short-distance and short-time structure of a unitary Fermi gas

> We consider the operator product expansions for unitarity fermions. We compute the dynamic structure factor S(q,w) at large frequency and wavenumber away from the one-particle peak. The overall normalization of S(q,w) is determined by Tan's contact parameter, and the dependence on q and w is obtained in closed analytic form. We also find energy deposited into the system by small, rapid variations of the inverse scattering length.

## Key claim

The dynamic structure factor \(S(\mathbf{q},\omega)\) of a unitary Fermi gas at large momentum and frequency (away from the single-particle peak) is proportional to Tan's contact parameter, with the functional dependence on \(\mathbf{q}\) and \(\omega\) obtained in closed analytic form via the operator product expansion; the result is strictly valid in the limit \(\omega\to\infty\), \(\mathbf{q}\to\infty\) with fixed \(x = q^2/2\omega \neq 1\).

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion (OPE) to the density-density correlation function in a nonrelativistic field theory of fermions with a contact interaction tuned to unitarity (infinite scattering length). They compute the OPE coefficients for the leading operators, the number density \(n\) (dimension 3) and \(\phi^*\phi\) (dimension 4, identified with Tan's contact parameter), by evaluating Feynman diagrams in vacuum. The imaginary part of the Fourier-transformed OPE yields the dynamic structure factor \(S(\mathbf{q},\omega)\) for \(\omega > q^2/4\), and the same method gives the energy deposition rate from rapid oscillations of the inverse scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The dynamic structure factor for \(\omega > q^2/4\) is given by Eq. (DSF):  
  \[
  \frac{S(\mathbf{q},\omega)}{\langle\phi^*\phi\rangle} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}\frac{\sqrt{\omega - q^2/4}}{(\omega - q^2/2)^2} + \frac{1}{2\pi^2\omega q}\ln\frac{\omega + q\sqrt{\omega - q^2/4}}{|\omega - q^2/2|} - \frac{1}{\pi^2 q^2\sqrt{\omega - q^2/4}}\left(\ln^2\frac{\omega + q\sqrt{\omega - q^2/4}}{|\omega - q^2/2|} - \pi^2\theta(q^2/2 - \omega)\right).
  \]
- In the small-\(q\) limit at fixed large \(\omega\): \(S(\mathbf{q},\omega) = \frac{4}{45\pi^2}\frac{q^4}{\omega^{7/2}}\langle\phi^*\phi\rangle\) (Eq. S-smallq).
- The energy deposition rate from a small oscillating inverse scattering length \(\alpha(t) = \alpha_0\cos\omega t\) is \(\frac{d\epsilon}{dt} = \frac{\alpha_0^2}{8\pi}\langle\phi^*\phi\rangle\sqrt{\omega}\).
- The two-body threshold for \(S(\mathbf{q},\omega)\) is \(\omega = q^2/4\); the single-particle peak lies at \(\omega = q^2/2\).
- The three-body contribution to \(S(\mathbf{q},\omega)\) is suppressed by \(\omega^{-3.77272}\) relative to the two-body term, using \(\Delta_3 \approx 4.27272\).

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach pioneered by Braaten and Platter (2008) for cold atoms, and builds on prior studies of the high-frequency RF tail by Strinati, Schneider, and Zwerger, which related the tail to Tan's contact parameter. The result for the dynamic structure factor at large \(\omega\) and small \(q\), the \(\langle\phi^*\phi\rangle q^4/\omega^{7/2}\) scaling, was also obtained independently by Randeria and Taylor (2010), though with a different numerical coefficient. The OPE method cleanly separates few-body from many-body physics, enabling systematic computation of high-momentum/high-frequency response in terms of Tan's contact parameter, and opens the door to analyzing multi-body contributions (e.g., three-body effects) at even larger momenta.
