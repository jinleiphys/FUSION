# Large scale shell model calculation for collectivity in nuclei beyond 78Ni

> A shell model effective interaction for nuclei beyond the double magic nucleus 78Ni is constructed. First, the single-particle evolutions for valence neutrons above the double magic 78Ni are systematically explored in the N = 51 isotones using the large scale shell model (LSSM) calculations based on the constructed effective interaction. Subsequently, we calculate the excitation energies of 2+1 states and reduced electric quadrupole transition probabilities B(E2; 2+ to 0+) for N = 52 isotones. Notably, our calculation gives the result most consistent with the trend of the B(E2) values observed in the N = 52 isotones, especially for 84Ge, a result that poses a serious challenge to the theoretical model. Furthermore, the collectivity in N = 52 isotones, as well as the roles of pseudo-SU(3) symmetry, are investigated via the calculated primary configurations of their ground states and the first excited states. Additionally, the low-lying structures and band characteristics of neutron-rich Ge and Se isotopes are investigated. The ground state and the γ-soft band are constructed in our LSSM calculations, aligning well with available experimental evidence. Finally, we present the calculated evolutions of low-lying states in neutron-rich Ge and Se isotopes. The predictions for the as-yet unobserved low-lying states in these nuclei provide a comprehensive dataset to guide and inform future experimental efforts to decipher the evolution of shell structures and collectivity.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new shell model effective interaction for nuclei beyond ⁷⁸Ni that reproduces the trend of B(E2) values in N=52 isotones better than previous interactions, particularly for ⁸⁴Ge, but the interaction required adjustment of monopole matrix elements to fit low-lying states of Ge, As, and Se isotopes with N=50, 51, and 52, meaning the predictive power is partially constrained by empirical tuning.

## Method

The authors derived an effective interaction from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential using the Vlow-k renormalization procedure with a cutoff momentum of 2.6 fm⁻¹, then applied open-shell many-body perturbation theory including Q-box vertex functions up to third order with folded diagram series summed to all orders. Monopole matrix elements were adjusted to reproduce low-lying states of Ge, As, and Se isotopes with N=50, 51, and 52. The Hamiltonian was exactly diagonalized using the KSHELL code in a valence space with protons in {1p₃/₂,₁/₂, 0f₅/₂, 0g₉/₂} orbitals and neutrons in {1d₅/₂,₃/₂, 2s₁/₂, 0g₇/₂, 0h₁₁/₂} orbitals above the ⁷⁸Ni core, with an enhanced polarization charge of 0.7e for quadrupole calculations and truncations up to 4ħω for the heaviest isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for protons: π0f₅/₂ at 0.00 MeV, π1p₃/₂ at 0.65 MeV, π1p₁/₂ at 2.21 MeV, π0g₉/₂ at 3.00 MeV
- Single-particle energies for neutrons: ν1d₅/₂ at 0.00 MeV, ν2s₁/₂ at 0.28 MeV, ν1d₃/₂ at 1.55 MeV, ν0g₇/₂ at 1.80 MeV, ν0h₁₁/₂ at 3.80 MeV
- The largest Hamiltonian dimension was about 1.0 × 10⁹·²²
- The Vlow-k cutoff momentum used was Λ = 2.6 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of nuclei beyond ⁷⁸Ni, specifically addressing limitations of the jj45 interaction (PhysRevLett.91.162503) and the Sieja interaction (PhysRevC.79.064310, PhysRevC.88.034327), which showed deficiencies in describing B(E2) values in N=52 isotones and low-lying states near ⁷⁸Ni. The new interaction enables systematic calculations of collectivity and γ-soft bands in neutron-rich Ge and Se isotopes, and provides predictions for unobserved low-lying states to guide future experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities.
