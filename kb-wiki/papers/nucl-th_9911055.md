# Multiboson effects in multiparticle production

> The influence of multiboson effects on pion multiplicities, single-pion spectra and two-pion correlation functions is discussed in terms of an analytically solvable model. The applicability of its basic factorization assumption is clarified. An approximate scaling of the basic observables with the phase space density is demonstrated in the low density (gas) limit. This scaling and also its violation at high densities due to the condensate formation is described by approximate analytical formulae which allow, in principle, for the identification of the multiboson effects among others. For moderate densities indicated by the experimental data, a fast saturation of multiboson effects with the number of contributing cumulants is obtained, allowing for the account of these effects in realistic transport code simulations. At high densities, the spectra are mainly determined by the universal condensate term and the initially narrow Poisson multiplicity distribution approaches a wide Bose-Einstein one. As a result, the intercepts of the inclusive and fixed-$n$ correlation functions (properly normalized to 1 at large relative momenta) approach 2 and 1, respectively and their widths logarithmically increase with the increasing phase space density. It is shown that the neglect of energy-momentum constraints in the model is justified except near a multipion threshold, where these constraints practically exclude the possibility of a very cold condensate production. It is argued that spectacular multiboson effects are likely to be observed only in the rare events containing sufficiently high density (speckle) fluctuations.

## Key claim

Multiboson effects on pion multiplicities, single-pion spectra, and two-pion correlation functions can be described by an analytically solvable model based on a factorization assumption, with approximate scaling of observables with phase space density in the low-density (gas) limit; however, the neglect of energy-momentum constraints in the model is justified only except near a multipion threshold, where these constraints practically exclude the possibility of a very cold condensate production.

## Method

The authors use an analytically solvable model assuming independent particle emission with a Gaussian ansatz for the single-boson emission function D(p,x), factorizing the n-particle emission function into products of single-particle ones. They derive recurrence relations for Bose-Einstein (BE) weights and cumulants, then compute the BE-modified multiplicity distributions, single-boson spectra, and two-boson correlation functions, comparing numerical calculations with analytical approximations that account for scaling behavior in the low-density limit and condensate formation at high densities.

## Context

This work extends the analytically solvable model introduced by Pratt and Zelevinsky (1993, 1994) for studying multiboson effects, and builds on earlier studies of Bose-Einstein correlations by Kopylov and Podgoretsky (1989) and others. The paper provides approximate analytical formulae that allow identification of multiboson effects among others, and demonstrates that for moderate densities indicated by experimental data (consistent with local thermal equilibrium as in Barz et al., 1997), a fast saturation of multiboson effects with the number of contributing cumulants occurs, enabling inclusion of these effects in realistic transport code simulations. The results enable future studies of rare events containing sufficiently high density (speckle) fluctuations at RHIC and LHC.
