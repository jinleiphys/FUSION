# Theoretical Review on QCD and Vector Mesons in Dileptonic Quark Gluon Plasma

> After the discovery of the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), many experimental and theoretical efforts have been made to investigate physics issues involved in ultra relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The fundamental goal of this work is to present a short guide of the underlying theory of strong interactions with emphasis on the light vector mesons based on the exceptional work of R.Rapp and J.Wambach. Today widely believed that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration probably takes place in a hot and dense hadronic medium as this produced at these collisions. We focus at the place where the examination of the new form of matter, so-called Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), can be done through the investigation of the dileptonic production coming from light vector mesons. Significant progress has been made since the last years, but still some open questions remain at the non-perturbative mass region.

## Key claim

This review argues that dilepton production from light vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) is a promising probe for studying the Quark-Gluon Plasma and chiral symmetry restoration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but open questions remain in the non-perturbative mass region where perturbation theory is not applicable.

## Method

The paper presents a theoretical review of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) as the underlying theory of strong interactions, focusing on the SU(3) gauge symmetry, chiral symmetry and its spontaneous breaking, and the role of quark and gluon condensates. It then derives the formalism for dilepton production rates from a thermal medium using the electromagnetic current correlator, decomposing the current into contributions from rho, omega, and phi mesons, following the framework established by Rapp and Wambach.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale parameter Lambda_QCD is approximately 200 MeV
- The critical temperature for deconfinement in a purely gluonic system is T_c approximately 150 MeV
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, which is the first order parameter for chiral symmetry breaking
- For an average quark mass m_bar = 6 MeV, the quark condensate is approximately -(240 MeV)^3 = -1.8 fm^{-3}
- The nucleon sigma-term is about 45 MeV
- The electromagnetic coupling constant alpha = 1/137
- For N_f = 2, the numerical value of Lambda_q is approximately 470 MeV

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Rapp and Wambach (2000) for understanding vector meson properties in hot and dense nuclear matter. It summarizes experimental results from the CERES collaboration (proton-Beryllium and proton-Gold at 450 GeV, Sulfur-Gold at 200 GeV), HELIOS-3 collaboration (Sulfur-Tungsten at 200 GeV), and HADES collaboration (carbon-carbon at 2 AGeV and proton-proton at 2.2 GeV). The work enables further investigation of the QCD phase diagram through dilepton measurements at future facilities such as CBM at GSI.
