# Positivity of High Density Effective Theory

> We show that the effective field theory of low energy modes in dense QCD has positive Euclidean path integral measure. The complexity of the measure of QCD at finite chemical potential can be ascribed to modes which are irrelevant to the dynamics at sufficiently high density. Rigorous inequalities follow at asymptotic density. Lattice simulation of dense QCD should be possible using the quark determinant calculated in the effective theory.

## Key claim

The Euclidean path integral measure of the high density effective theory (HDET) describing only low-energy modes near the Fermi surface in dense QCD is positive semi-definite, even though the full QCD measure at finite chemical potential is complex. This positivity holds for the leading-order effective theory, while higher-order corrections in the 1/μ expansion may reintroduce complexity.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for dense QCD by integrating out "fast" modes (ψ₋) that are far from the Fermi surface, keeping only quasiparticle modes (ψ₊) near the Fermi surface. They decompose the quark momentum into a large Fermi momentum μv_F and a residual momentum l, then derive the effective Lagrangian by eliminating ψ₋ using equations of motion. The leading term in the Euclidean effective action yields an anti-Hermitian Dirac operator M_eft = γ^E_∥·D(A) that anti-commutes with γ₅, ensuring a positive semi-definite determinant. The authors also develop a coordinate-space formulation using a velocity operator to avoid the velocity Fourier transform ambiguity.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass is M = √(N_f/(2π²)) g_s μ, where N_f is the number of flavors and g_s is the strong coupling constant.
- Corrections to the vacuum energy from higher-order terms are suppressed by powers of (α_s/(2π))(Λ/μ), where Λ is the effective theory cutoff and μ is the chemical potential.
- The effective theory describes quarks with energy E < μ, where the states ψ₊ near the Fermi surface (|p| ∼ μ) are easily excited while ψ₋ states in the Dirac sea are decoupled due to an energy gap of order μ.

## Context

This work extends the high density effective theory previously introduced by Hong (2000) and addresses the sign problem that has precluded lattice simulations of QCD at finite chemical potential, as reviewed by Hands (2002). The positivity result enables rigorous QCD inequalities at asymptotic density, similar to the Vafa-Witten (1984) theorem for zero-density QCD, though the authors note that the proof of non-breaking of vector symmetries must be modified because a quark mass term does not create a quasiparticle gap. The paper suggests that lattice simulation of dense QCD should be possible using the quark determinant calculated in the effective theory, potentially enabling first-principles studies of color superconductivity and neutron star matter.
