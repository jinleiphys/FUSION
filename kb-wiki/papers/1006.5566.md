# Fundamental Relativistic Rotator. Hessian singularity and the issue of the minimal interaction with electromagnetic field

> There are two relativistic rotators with Casimir invariants of the Poincaré group being fixed parameters. The particular models of spinning particles were studied in the past both at the classical and quantum level. Recently, a minimal interaction with electromagnetic field has been considered. We show that the dynamical systems can be uniquely singled out from among other relativistic rotators by the unphysical requirement that the Hessian referring to the physical degrees of freedom should be singular. Closely related is the fact that the equations of free motion are not independent, making the evolution indeterminate. We show that the Hessian singularity cannot be removed by the minimal interaction with the electromagnetic field. By making use of a nontrivial Hessian null space, we show that a single constraint appears in the external field for consistency of the equations of motion with the Hessian singularity. The constraint imposes unphysical limitation on the initial conditions and admissible motions. We discuss the mechanism of appearance of unique solutions in external fields on an example of motion in the uniform magnetic field. We give a simple model to illustrate that similarly constrained evolution cannot be determinate in arbitrary fields.

## Key claim

The two fundamental relativistic rotators (those with fixed Casimir invariants of the Poincaré group) are uniquely singled out from other relativistic rotators by the unphysical requirement that the Hessian matrix referring to the physical degrees of freedom be singular, and this singularity cannot be removed by minimal electromagnetic coupling, leading to a constraint that imposes unphysical limitations on initial conditions and admissible motions.

## Method

The author reduces the covariant action of relativistic rotators to a Lagrangian on the physical configuration space \(\mathbb{R}^3\times\mathbb{S}^2\) (position and a spatial direction) by eliminating auxiliary degrees of freedom and fixing the gauge \(\tau\equiv x^0\) and \(k^0\equiv1\). The Hessian determinant of this reduced Lagrangian is computed as a function of \(f(Q)\), and the condition for its vanishing yields a differential equation whose only nontrivial solutions give the two fundamental rotator Lagrangians. The Hessian null space is then used to derive constraints that appear when minimal electromagnetic interaction is added, and the resulting constrained dynamics is analyzed for free motion and in uniform magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The Hessian determinant is proportional to \(f(Q)^3 f'(Q)^2 \left(1+2Q\left(\frac{f'(Q)}{f(Q)}+\frac{f''(Q)}{f'(Q)}\right)\right)\), and vanishes identically only for \(f(Q)=\sqrt{1\pm\sqrt{Q}}\).
- The general free-motion solution gives the rotation frequency in the center-of-momentum frame as \(|\dot{\phi}(t)| = \frac{2}{\ell}\tanh\Psi\), where \(\Psi\) is the rapidity, with the bound \(\frac{\ell}{2}|\dot{\phi}(t)|<1\).
- The Hamilton action evaluated for the general solution is \(S(t)=S(0)-m t - m\frac{\ell}{2}\int |\dot{\phi}(t)|\,dt\).
- For the non-relativistic toy model, unique solutions in a magnetic-type field occur at fixed radii: for solution (a) \(\omega = \frac{\tilde{K}R}{m(R+\ell/2)}\) with \(R>0\); for solution (b) \(\omega = \frac{\tilde{K}R}{m(\ell/2-R)}\) with \(R<\ell/2\); for solution (c) \(\omega = \frac{\tilde{K}R}{m(R-\ell/2)}\) with \(R>\ell/2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the fundamental relativistic rotator originally proposed by Staruszkiewicz (2008) and independently discovered by Kuzenko, Lyakhovich, and Segal (1994), who also imposed strong conservation of mass and spin. The paper identifies a previously unnoticed defect: the Hessian singularity on the physical configuration space leads to indeterminate free motion and, in the presence of minimal electromagnetic coupling, produces a constraint \(F_{\mu\nu}k^{\mu}\dot{x}^{\nu}=0\) that limits initial conditions and admissible motions. This contrasts with regular (phenomenological) rotators, which have nonsingular Hessians and exhibit unique, unconstrained motion. The results imply that fundamental relativistic rotators with identically fixed mass and spin are unphysical, and the work enables future studies to focus on regular Lagrangians for spinning particle models.
