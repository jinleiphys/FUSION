# Many-body theory of nuclear and neutron star matter

> We present results obtained for nuclei, nuclear and neutron star matter, and neutron star structure obtained with the recent Argonne v18 two- nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions including relativistic boost corrections. These interactions predict that matter will undergo a transition to a spin layered phase with neutral pion condensation. We also consider the possibility of a transition to quark matter.

## Key claim

The Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, including relativistic boost corrections, predict that both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter will undergo a transition to a spin-layered phase with neutral pion condensation; the paper also considers a possible transition to quark matter, but notes that the calculations of the pion-condensed phase are still incomplete.

## Method

The authors used variational many-body methods with chain summation techniques to calculate the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter from a Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction, the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, and relativistic boost corrections. They also performed Green’s Function Monte Carlo calculations for light nuclei (A ≤ 8) to test the accuracy of the variational wave functions and interaction models. The neutron star structure was then obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using the calculated equations of state, and the possibility of a transition to quark matter was explored using a bag model with one-gluon exchange.

## Key numbers

- Triton ground state energy predicted by modern Nijmegen and Argonne models (without boost and three-nucleon forces): between -7.62 and -7.72 MeV; CD-Bonn gives -8.00 MeV.
- GFMC energy of ⁴He: -28.30 MeV, with ΔE_expt. = 0.00(2) MeV.
- GFMC energy of ⁸He: -25.8 MeV, with ΔE_expt. = -5.6(6) MeV.
- Phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter at a density of ~0.3 fm⁻³; in pure neutron matter at ~0.2 fm⁻³.
- For the most reliable model (with boost and three-nucleon interactions), normal matter at density 0.204 fm⁻³ and proton fraction 0.073 is in equilibrium with pion-condensed matter at 0.237 fm⁻³ and proton fraction 0.057.
- The proton fraction in neutron star matter remains below the critical value of 0.148 needed for direct Urca cooling.
- For the A18 + δv + UIX* model with bag constant B = 122 MeV/fm³, the transition to quark matter occurs over the density range ρ = 0.58 to 1.46 fm⁻³; the maximum neutron star mass is reduced to ~1.9 M⊙.
- For B = 200 MeV/fm³, the maximum mass becomes ~2 M⊙, with stars from 1.93 to 2.01 M⊙ having quark drops in their interior.

## Context

This work extends the variational calculations of Akmal and Pandharipande (1997) by including relativistic boost corrections and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, building on the Argonne v18 potential of Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995). The predicted phase transition to neutral pion condensation was first considered by Migdal, and the paper notes that this transition did not occur with the older Urbana v14 (1981) or Argonne v14 (1984) models. The results enable more realistic predictions of neutron star structure and maximum mass, and allow comparison with observations suggesting the existence of ~2 M⊙ neutron stars, which would rule out models without three-body forces.
