# Exclusive Production of Quarkonia in $pp$ and $p \bar p$ Collisions Far from the Threshold

> I discuss exclusive production of the $η'$, $J/ψ$, and $χ_c(0^+)$ mesons in $pp$ and $p \bar p$ collisions at high energies. QCD diffractive mechanisms as well as nondiffractive mechanisms are discussed. Different unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDF) are used. Some differential distributions are shown and discussed.

## Key claim

Exclusive production of eta', J/psi, and chi_c(0+) mesons in pp and p-pbar collisions at high energies can be described by QCD diffractive mechanisms using unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs), but the theoretical predictions depend strongly on the choice of UGDF model and are generally much smaller than existing experimental data at W = 29.1 GeV, suggesting either subleading reggeon contributions or a need to modify UGDFs in the nonperturbative small-transverse-momentum region.

## Method

The author applied the Kaidalov-Khoze-Martin-Ryskin formalism for diffractive double-elastic production, originally developed for Higgs boson production, to compute amplitudes for exclusive meson production using skewed unintegrated gluon distribution functions. For J/psi production, the author used a two-mechanism approach involving photon-pomeron and pomeron-photon fusion, with the gamma* p to J/psi p amplitude parametrized to describe HERA data from ZEUS and H1 collaborations, and included elastic rescattering corrections for pp and p-pbar interactions.

## Key numbers

- For eta' production at center-of-mass energy W = 29.1 GeV, the differential cross section d sigma / dx_F as a function of Feynman x_F was calculated with several UGDF models (KL, GBW, BFKL, Gaussian) and compared to WA102 collaboration data
- For chi_c(0+) production at Tevatron energy W = 1960 GeV, the rapidity distribution d sigma / dy was calculated for different UGDFs (KL, GBW, BFKL, KMR)
- For exclusive J/psi production, the accessible photon-proton center-of-mass energy range at Tevatron is 70 GeV < W_gamma p < 1500 GeV, and at LHC is 200 GeV < W_gamma p < 8000 GeV

## Context

This work extends the diffractive double-elastic formalism of Kaidalov, Khoze, Martin, and Ryskin (KMR) from Higgs boson production to lighter mesons (eta', J/psi, chi_c(0+)), and contrasts with the gamma* gamma* fusion mechanism which is found to be much smaller for eta' production and negligible for chi_c(0+) production. The J/psi exclusive production is suggested as a candidate for odderon exchange searches as proposed by Braun, M. et al. (2007), but the author notes that photon-pomeron fusion (Szczurek and Schaefer, 2007) is probably the dominant process and must be accounted for. The results enable future measurements at Tevatron and LHC to constrain the gamma p to J/psi p cross section at energies beyond those accessible at HERA.
