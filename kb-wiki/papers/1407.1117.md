# Deformation Dependence of Breathing Oscillations in Bose - Fermi Mixtures at Zero Temperature

> We study the breathing oscillations in bose-fermi mixtures in the axially-symmetric deformed trap of prolate, spherical and oblate shapes, and clarify the deformation dependence of the frequencies and the characteristics of collective oscillations. The collective oscillations of the mixtures in deformed traps are calculated in the scaling method. In largely-deformed prolate and oblate limits and spherical limit, we obtain the analytical expressions of the collective frequencies. The full calculation shows that the collective oscillations become consistent with the analytically-obtained frequencies when the system is deformed into both prolate and oblate regions. The complicated changes of oscillation characters are shown to occur in the transcendental regions around the spherically-deformed region. We find that these critical changes of oscillation characters are explained by the level crossing behaviors of the intrinsic oscillation modes. The approximate expressions are obtained for the level crossing points that determine the transcendental regions. We also compare the results of the scaling methods with those of the dynamical approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deformation dependence of breathing oscillations in Bose-Fermi mixtures at zero temperature can be described by four intrinsic oscillation modes (boson transverse, boson longitudinal, fermion transverse, fermion longitudinal) that mix in characteristic ways depending on trap shape, with the critical changes in oscillation character occurring in "transcendental regions" around spherical deformation that are explained by level crossing behaviors of the intrinsic modes.

## Method

The authors used the scaling method, an approximation of the random phase approximation (RPA), to calculate collective oscillation frequencies and amplitudes of Bose-Fermi mixtures in axially-symmetric deformed harmonic traps. They expanded the total energy functional to second order in the scaling parameters (collective coordinates for boson and fermion transverse and longitudinal breathing modes) and solved the resulting eigenvalue equation for the oscillation frequencies and eigenvectors. Analytical expressions were derived in the limits of spherical, largely prolate, and largely oblate trap deformations, and full numerical calculations were performed for intermediate deformations.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical trap with equal boson and fermion trap frequencies (omega_f = 1), the monopole oscillation frequencies are given by omega_M^2(+-1) = (1/2)[9 - v_B/2 - v_F/2 - v_2/2 +/- sqrt(D_M)], where D_M = (1 - v_B/2 + v_F/2 + v_2/2)^2 + v_B v_F
- For the spherical trap, the quadrupole oscillation frequencies are omega_Q^2(+-1) = (1/2)[2 - v_F/5 - v_B/5 + v_2 +/- sqrt(D_Q)], where D_Q = (2 + v_2 - v_F/5 + v_B/5)^2 + (4/25) v_B v_F
- In the largely prolate limit (omega_T >> omega_L), the in-phase transverse breathing (ITB) frequency is omega_ITB^2 approximately 4 omega_T^2, and the out-of-phase transverse breathing (OTB) frequency is omega_OTB^2 approximately [4 - (2/5)(v_B + v_F)] omega_T^2
- In the largely prolate limit, the boson transverse breathing (BTB) frequency is omega_BTB^2 approximately 4(1 - v_B/10) omega_T^2, and the boson axial breathing (BAB) frequency is omega_BAB^2 approximately (5/2)(1 - v_B/10) omega_L^2
- In the largely oblate limit (omega_L >> omega_T), the boson longitudinal breathing (BLB) frequency is omega_BLB^2 approximately 3(1 - v_B/10) omega_L^2, and the boson sidewards breathing (BSB) frequency is omega_BSB^2 approximately (10/3)(1 - v_B/10) omega_T^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of monopole (Maruyama et al., 2010), dipole (Maruyama et al., 2011), and quadrupole (Maruyama et al., 2012) oscillations in spherical traps and breathing oscillations in largely prolate deformed systems (Maruyama and Yabu, 2012) to the full deformation dependence between prolate, spherical, and oblate shapes. It also builds on the scaling approach previously applied to two-component Fermi gases (scalTF2, scalTF). The results enable experimental studies of Bose-Fermi mixtures of Yb isotopes (where five bosonic and two fermionic isotopes exist) to identify collective oscillation modes and their mixing as a function of trap deformation, and provide analytical expressions for level crossing points that determine the transcendental regions where mode character changes.
