# Contact-interaction Faddeev equation and, inter alia, proton tensor charges

> A confining, symmetry-preserving, Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction is used to formulate Faddeev equations for the nucleon and Delta-baryon in which the kernel involves dynamical dressed-quark exchange and whose solutions therefore provide momentum-dependent Faddeev amplitudes. These solutions are compared with those obtained in the static approximation and with a QCD-kindred formulation of the Faddeev kernel. They are also used to compute a range of nucleon properties, amongst them: the proton's sigma-term; the large Bjorken-x values of separate ratios of unpolarised and longitudinally-polarised valence u- and d-quark parton distribution functions; and the proton's tensor charges, which enable one to directly determine the effect of dressed-quark electric dipole moments (EDMs) on neutron and proton EDMs.

## Key claim

The authors show that solving the Faddeev equation for the nucleon and Delta-baryon with a full momentum-dependent treatment of the quark-exchange kernel, rather than the simpler static approximation, yields nearly identical baryon masses because the additional attraction from dynamical quark exchange is compensated by spin-orbit repulsion from the momentum-dependent Faddeev amplitudes.

## Method

The authors formulate Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for the nucleon and Delta-baryon using a confining, symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction, with the kernel involving dynamical dressed-quark exchange. They solve the resulting linear homogeneous integral equations numerically for the momentum-dependent scalar functions that comprise the Faddeev amplitudes, using a confining regularisation procedure with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs, and compare these solutions with those obtained using the static approximation where the exchanged quark propagator is replaced by a constant.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass from the gap equation: M = 0.37 GeV (with current-quark mass m = 0.007 GeV)
- Scalar diquark mass: m_{qq0+} = 0.78 GeV; axial-vector diquark mass: m_{qq1+} = 1.06 GeV
- Nucleon mass with full dynamical treatment (g_N=1): m_N = 1.30 GeV; with g_N=1.28: m_N = 1.14 GeV
- Delta-baryon mass with full dynamical treatment (g_Delta=1): m_Delta = 1.65 GeV; with g_Delta=1.73: m_Delta = 1.39 GeV
- Static-approximation nucleon mass from Ref. Chen (2012): m_N = 1.27 GeV (g_N=1) and 1.14 GeV (g_N=1.18)
- Static-approximation Delta mass from Ref. Chen (2012): m_Delta = 1.60 GeV (g_Delta=1) and 1.39 GeV (g_Delta=1.56)
- Gluon mass scale: m_G = 0.5 GeV; fitted coupling: alpha_IR = 0.36 pi
- Infrared cutoff: Lambda_ir = 0.24 GeV; ultraviolet cutoff: Lambda_uv = 0.905 GeV

## Context

This work extends the contact-interaction Faddeev equation approach of Roberts (2011) and Chen (2012) by removing the static approximation for the exchanged quark, which had been used to simplify the kernel. The results enable computation of nucleon properties including the sigma-term, large-x parton distribution function ratios, and proton tensor charges that relate dressed-quark electric dipole moments to nucleon electric dipole moments. The authors note that the computed masses are about 25% above empirical values, consistent with the omission of meson-baryon final-state interactions that are resummed in dynamical coupled-channels models such as those of Suzuki (2009), Kamano (2013), and Doring (2014).
