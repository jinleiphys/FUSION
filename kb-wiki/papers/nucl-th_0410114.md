# Effective Interaction Techniques for the Gamow Shell Model

> We apply a contour deformation technique in momentum space to the newly developed Gamow shell model, and study the drip-line nuclei 5He, 6He and 7He. A major problem in Gamow shell-model studies of nuclear many-body systems is the increasing dimensionality of many-body configurations due to the large number of resonant and complex continuum states necessary to reproduce bound and resonant state energies. We address this problem using two different effective operator approaches generalized to the complex momentum plane. These are the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method for complex interactions and the multi-reference perturbation theory method. The combination of these two approaches results in a large truncation of the relevant configurations compared with direct diagonalization. This offers interesting perspectives for studies of weakly bound systems.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a rotated-plus-translated contour in the complex momentum plane, combined with effective operator techniques (Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation and multi-reference perturbation theory), can significantly reduce the dimensionality of Gamow shell-model calculations for drip-line nuclei, while still capturing the effect of all particles moving in complex continuum states.

## Method

The authors constructed a single-particle Berggren basis by solving the momentum-space Schrödinger equation on a rotated and translated contour in the complex k-plane, using a Gaussian SBB (Sack, Biedenharn and Breit) potential fitted to reproduce neutron-⁴He scattering phase shifts. For the two-body interaction in ⁶He and ⁷He, they employed a phenomenological Gaussian interaction separable in coordinates, with strength fitted to reproduce the 0⁺ binding energy in ⁶He. They performed full diagonalizations of the many-body shell-model equations for ⁵He, ⁶He, and ⁷He, and then applied the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method and multi-reference perturbation theory generalized to complex interactions to truncate the model space.

## Key numbers

- The p₃/₂ single-particle resonance in ⁵He converges to Re[E] = 0.752476 MeV, Im[E] = -0.328033 MeV with 30 integration points along each contour segment
- The p₁/₂ single-particle resonance in ⁵He converges to Re[E] = 2.154147 MeV, Im[E] = -2.912162 MeV with 30 integration points along each contour segment
- The 0⁺₁ bound state in ⁶He converges to Re[E] = -0.979509 MeV (p₃/₂ only model space, 25 mesh points)
- The 2⁺₁ resonance in ⁶He converges to Re[E] = 1.216496 MeV, Im[E] = -0.267745 MeV (p₃/₂ only model space, 25 mesh points)
- The 0⁺₁ bound state in ⁶He (p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ model space) converges to Re[E] = -0.979148 MeV with 25 mesh points
- The 0⁺₂ resonance in ⁶He (p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ model space) converges to Re[E] = 4.286181 MeV, Im[E] = -3.882876 MeV with 25 mesh points
- The 2⁺₁ resonance in ⁶He (p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ model space) converges to Re[E] = 1.150527 MeV, Im[E] = -0.203060 MeV with 25 mesh points
- The 2⁺₂ resonance in ⁶He (p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ model space) converges to Re[E] = 2.372817 MeV, Im[E] = -2.123254 MeV with 25 mesh points
- The J^π = 3/2⁻ ground state resonance in ⁷He appears at E = -(0.12 + 0.12i) MeV in the full diagonalization
- Diagonalization in a reduced space (at most two particles in continuum states) gives E = -(0.14 + 0.16i) MeV for the ⁷He ground state
- The three-particle problem for ⁷He with 24 mesh points for p₃/₂ only has dimension d = 9224; including p₁/₂ would give roughly d ~ 40000 configurations

## Context

This work extends the Gamow shell model approach developed by Michel et al. (2002-2003) and Liotta et al. (2002) by introducing a rotated-plus-translated contour that better isolates physical resonances from complex continuum states, particularly for systems with more than two particles. The authors address the dimensionality problem previously noted by Witek et al. (2003), who used only a small number of continuum states, and by Michel et al. (2003), who allowed at most two particles in continuum states. The effective interaction scheme combining Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation and multi-reference perturbation theory enables future studies of heavier weakly bound nuclei with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, which the authors state will be considered in a forthcoming work.
