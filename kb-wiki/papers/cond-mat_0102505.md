# Generalized yrast states of a Bose condensate in harmonic trap for a universality class of repulsive interactions

> For a system of N bosons in a 2d harmonic trap with frequency omega, interacting via repulsive forces V<<omega, we develop supersymmetric method to find the lowest energy states of rotating Bose condensate as function of two quantum numbers, the total angular momentum and the angular momentum of internal excitations (generalized yrast states). The energies of these condensed vortex states are expressed through the single two-body matrix element of interaction V. A broad universality class of the repulsive interactions for which these results hold is described by a simple integral condition on V. It includes Gaussian, delta-function and log-Coulomb forces.

# Generalized yrast states of a Bose condensate in harmonic trap for a universality class of repulsive interactions

## Key claim

For a system of N bosons in a 2d harmonic trap with frequency ω, interacting via repulsive forces V much smaller than ħω, the lowest energy states at given total angular momentum L and internal excitation angular momentum v (generalized yrast states) have universal wavefunctions and energies expressed through simple moments of the interaction potential, provided the interaction satisfies an integral positivity condition that holds for any physically meaningful repulsive interaction including Gaussian, delta-function, and log-Coulomb forces.

## Method

The authors project the many-body Hamiltonian onto the lowest Landau level (single oscillator shell) and expand the projected interaction in powers of two-particle ladder operators. They then decompose the Hamiltonian into a simple part V0 and a remainder VS, and prove that VS is non-negative definite for interactions satisfying the integral condition, using an inertia theorem argument that reduces the problem to checking eigenvalues of the two-particle operator VS,12. The ground states in each (L,v) sector are identified as the states |L,v) = P_v|L⟩ obtained by projecting the simplest partition state [1,1,...,1] onto definite seniority v.

## Key numbers

- The condition for universality is λ_{2n} ≥ 0 for all 2n ≤ L, where λ_{l1} = ∫₀^∞ r dr V(r) f_{l1}(r) with f_{l1}(r) = e^{-r²/2}[r^{2l1}/(2^{l1} l1!) - 1 + (l1/4)(2 - r⁴/4)]
- For Gaussian interaction V(r) = U₀ e^{-r²/R²}/(πR²): λ_{2n} = U₀/[π(2+R²)] [(R²/(2+R²))^{2n} - 1 + 4n(1+R²)/(2+R²)²] ≥ 0 for any R
- For delta-function interaction (R→0 limit): λ_{l1} = U₀/(2π)[δ_{l1,0} + l1/2 - 1]; energies are E_min = L + N + (U₀/(8π))N(2N - L + v - 2)
- For 2d Coulomb interaction V = U₀ log(1/r): λ_{2n} = (U₀/4)[3n - 1/n + 2ψ(1) - 2ψ(2n)] ≥ 0; energies are E_min = L + N - (U₀N/4)[(log(2)-γ)(N-1) - (3/4)(v-L)] with γ = 0.57721 the Euler constant
- The first node of functions f_{2n}(r) occurs at r₀ ≈ 1.43 (in oscillator units ħ=m=ω=1)

## Context

This work provides the first rigorous analytical solution for yrast states of a rotating Bose condensate in a 2d harmonic trap, extending numerical results for delta-function interactions obtained by Wilkin, Mottelson, and others (1998). The method generalizes the approach of BP (numerical studies for delta-forces) to a broad universality class of repulsive interactions. The results enable analytical study of vortex states and their energetics, and the supersymmetric decomposition method developed here can be applied to fermions and particles with parastatistics, as well as to the three-dimensional case following the reduction scheme of 3d2d.
