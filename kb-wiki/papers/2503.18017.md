# Charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction at N$^3$LO in nuclear lattice effective field theory

> The nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) is an efficient tool for solving nuclear many-body problems, which takes high-fidelity lattice chiral interactions as input and computes nuclear low-energy observables via quantum Monte Carlo techniques. In this work, we present the first next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) chiral forces on the lattice with the isospin-breaking effects fully taken into account. We focus on both the charge-independence breaking (CIB) and charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) effects. Specifically, we include the isospin-breaking effect from the mass difference between the charged and neutral pions in the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP), the Coulomb force for the $pp$ interaction and the contribution of two additional charge-dependent contact operators. We also explicitly incorporate the two-pion-exchange potentials which was mostly neglected in previous NLEFT calculations. With these improvements, we are able to accurately reproduce the $np$ and $pp$ scattering phase shifts up to relative momentum $p \sim 200$ MeV as well as the deuteron properties. The construction of these charge-dependent lattice nuclear forces establishes a solid foundation for future high-precision nuclear ab initio calculations within the NLEFT framework.

## Key claim

The authors present the first N$^3$LO chiral forces on a lattice that fully incorporate isospin-breaking effects (charge-independence breaking and charge-symmetry breaking), including the pion mass difference in the one-pion-exchange potential, the Coulomb force for proton-proton interactions, and two additional charge-dependent contact operators, enabling accurate reproduction of nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts up to relative momentum approximately 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed nucleon-nucleon interactions on a cubic lattice using chiral effective field theory up to N$^3$LO, with the long-range part consisting of one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange potentials and the short-range part parameterized by contact terms with low-energy constants determined by fitting scattering data. They extracted scattering phase shifts from the asymptotic radial wave functions of the two-nucleon system on the lattice, using spherical Bessel functions for neutron-proton and neutron-neutron systems and spherical Coulomb wave functions for proton-proton systems. The fitting was performed separately for individual partial waves, with a joint fit for the $^1S_0$ channel that simultaneously determined the isospin-symmetric contact terms and the charge-dependent contact operators for proton-proton and neutron-neutron interactions.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass difference: $M_{\pi^\pm} - M_{\pi^0} \approx 4.593$ MeV, used as a source of isospin breaking in the one-pion-exchange potential
- Proton-proton scattering length: $a_{pp} = -17.3 \pm 0.4$ fm, with effective range $r_{pp} = 2.85 \pm 0.04$ fm
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: $a_{nn} = -18.95 \pm 0.40$ fm, with effective range $r_{nn} = 2.75 \pm 0.11$ fm
- Neutron-proton scattering length: $a_{np} = -23.740 \pm 0.020$ fm, with effective range $r_{np} = 2.77 \pm 0.05$ fm
- Charge-symmetry breaking quantified by $\Delta a_{\mathrm{CSB}} = a_{pp} - a_{nn} = 1.65 \pm 0.60$ fm
- Charge-independence breaking quantified by $\Delta a_{\mathrm{CIB}} = \frac{1}{2}(a_{pp} + a_{nn}) - a_{np} = 5.6 \pm 0.6$ fm
- Momentum cutoff for pion regulator: $\Lambda_{\pi} = 300$ MeV
- Momentum cutoff for Coulomb force: $\Lambda_{c} = 300$ MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: $g_A = 1.287$
- Pion decay constant: $F_{\pi} = 92.2$ MeV
- Neutral pion mass: $M_{\pi^0} = 134.98$ MeV
- Charged pion mass: $M_{\pi^\pm} = 139.57$ MeV
- Fine structure constant: $\alpha = 1/137$

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT calculations that assumed exact isospin symmetry, such as the N$^2$LO lattice chiral force with Fast Fourier Transform (PhysRevLett.128.242501) and the N$^3$LO chiral force for thermal nuclear matter (PhysRevLett.132.232502). It also builds on the approach of Ref. (arXiv2502.13565) which included leading-order charge-symmetry breaking contact terms, by additionally incorporating isospin-breaking corrections to the one-pion-exchange potential from pion mass splitting and explicitly including the two-pion-exchange potential that was mostly neglected in earlier NLEFT calculations. The construction enables future high-precision ab initio calculations within NLEFT that can address isospin-breaking phenomena such as energy differences between mirror nuclei and isobaric multiplets.
