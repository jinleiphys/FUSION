# Some applications of renormalized RPA in bosonic field theories

> We present some applications of the renormalized RPA in bosonic field theories. We first present some developments for the explicit calculation of the total energy in Phi^4 theory and discuss its phase structure in 1+1 dimensions. We also demonstrate that the Goldstone theorem is satisfied in the O(N) model within the renormalized RPA.

## Key claim

The renormalized RPA (r-RPA) cures the instability problem present in standard RPA for \(\Phi^4\) theory in 1+1 dimensions, and when non-covariant contributions are included, the effective potential yields a second-order phase transition consistent with the Simon-Griffiths theorem, with a critical coupling \(p_c = 1.6\) that approaches lattice and cluster results.

## Method

The authors apply the renormalized RPA formalism to \(\lambda\Phi^4\) theory in 1+1 dimensions, using a Green's function method with one-particle and two-particle excitation operators. They calculate the total energy via a charging formula that integrates over an auxiliary Hamiltonian \(H'(\rho)\), decomposing the correlation energy into covariant and non-covariant pieces from three-body and four-body interactions. For the O(N) linear sigma model, they derive the r-RPA pion propagator and demonstrate that the Goldstone theorem is satisfied in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling constant in r-RPA with non-covariant contributions: \(p_c = 1.6\) (reduced coupling constant \(p = b/24\mu^2\) with \(\mu = 1\))
- Critical coupling in standard RPA: \(p_c = 1.8\), compared to lattice result \(p_c = 2.55\) and cluster expansion result \(p_c = 2.45\)
- For \(s=0\) and \(p=2\), total energy in r-RPA with non-covariant term: \(-0.105\); without non-covariant term: \(-0.14\); cluster calculation: \(-0.11\)
- Critical coupling in r-RPA with only covariant pieces: \(p_c = 1.9\)
- Critical coupling in generalized mean field plus factorized term: \(p_c \simeq 2.3\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of r-RPA for \(\lambda\Phi^4\) theory (Hansen et al., 2002, referred to as I) and addresses the instability problem that plagues standard RPA in 1+1 dimensions. The standard RPA was previously applied to the O(N) model by Aouissat et al. (ACSW), where it restored the Goldstone theorem violated at the Gaussian approximation level (Stevenson, 1984; Kerman and Vautherin, 1989; Davesne et al., 1996). The r-RPA results improve upon standard RPA by yielding a second-order phase transition consistent with the Simon-Griffiths theorem, with critical couplings approaching lattice results (Jansen and Sexton, 1994; Loinaz and Willey, 1997; Marrero et al., 1999) and cluster expansion results (Haussling et al., 1995). The work enables further study of the chiral phase transition in the linear sigma model, potentially at finite temperature.
