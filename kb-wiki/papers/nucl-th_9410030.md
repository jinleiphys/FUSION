# Total Cross Sections for Neutron Scattering

> Measurements of neutron total cross-sections are both extensive and extremely accurate. Although they place a strong constraint on theoretically constructed models, there are relatively few comparisons of predictions with experiment. The total cross-sections for neutron scattering from $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are calculated as a function of energy from $50-700$~MeV laboratory energy with a microscopic first order optical potential derived within the framework of the Watson expansion. Although these results are already in qualitative agreement with the data, the inclusion of medium corrections to the propagator is essential to correctly predict the energy dependence given by the experiment.

## Key claim

The inclusion of medium corrections to the nucleon-nucleon propagator is essential for correctly predicting the energy dependence of neutron total cross-sections on $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca between 50 and 700 MeV, though the qualitative agreement with data is already present without these corrections.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron total cross-sections using a microscopic first-order optical potential derived from the Watson expansion, with the full Bonn potential as the free NN t-matrix for energies below 350 MeV and a high-energy extension above 350 MeV. Medium corrections to the propagator were incorporated using two different nuclear mean field potentials: one from a Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculation with the Gogny D1S interaction and one from a nonrelativistic reduction of Dirac-Hartree (DH) potentials based on the sigma-omega model. The calculations were performed in an off-shell t-rho framework using the optimum factorized form with diagonal nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- Neutron total cross-sections calculated for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca over the energy range 50–700 MeV laboratory energy
- For $^{16}$O, the effective number of nucleons for scaling is $A_{eff} \approx 12$; for $^{40}$Ca, $A_{eff} \approx 26$
- Measured total cross-sections for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are 25%–30% smaller than the upper limit given by $A \times \langle\sigma_T\rangle$
- The geometric black disk cross-section $2\pi R^2$ is exceeded below roughly 50 MeV for $^{16}$O and below about 70 MeV for $^{40}$Ca
- Medium corrections reduce calculated cross-sections in the 100–200 MeV region and raise them above approximately 300–400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous medium-corrected calculations (Chinn, Elster, and Thaler, 1993) from $^{40}$Ca alone to include $^{16}$O, confirming that the medium effects improve agreement with experimental data from Finlay et al. (1993) and Lisowski et al. (1982). The results demonstrate that first-order multiple scattering theory can accurately describe precision neutron total cross-section data in the appropriate energy regime, and the authors indicate that future work should include higher-order contributions in the spectator expansion.
