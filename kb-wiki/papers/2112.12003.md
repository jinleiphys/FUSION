# Testing the molecular nature of $ϕ(2170)$

> In this talk we show our recent results on the decay widths of $ϕ(2170)$ to final states formed by an anti-Kaon and a Kaonic resonance, in particular, $K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, considering a molecular description for $ϕ(2170)$. Branching fraction ratios are obtained and compared with the recent results found by the BESIII collaboration, finding compatible results.

## Key claim

The molecular description of $\phi(2170)$ as a $\phi f_0(980)$ bound state provides a fair description of the branching fraction ratios for decays to $\bar K$ plus a kaonic resonance ($K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$, $K_1(1400)$) when compared with BESIII data, but the compatibility depends strongly on which theoretical model is used for the internal structure of the kaonic resonances themselves.

## Method

The authors calculate partial decay widths of $\phi(2170)$ to $\bar K \mathbb{K}$ final states ($\mathbb{K} = K(1460), K_1(1270), K_1(1400)$) using triangular loop diagrams where $\phi(2170)$ decays first to $\phi$ and $f_0(980)$, then a kaon is exchanged between the two. The vertices are described by effective couplings obtained from three different models for the kaonic resonances: Model A treats $K_1(1270)$ as a dynamically generated vector-pseudoscalar molecule with a two-pole structure; Model B describes $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ as mixings of axial nonet states; Model C extracts couplings from experimental radiative decay data via vector meson dominance. The loop integrals are regularized with three different form factors (Heaviside theta, monopole, exponential) to check robustness.

## Key numbers

- Mass and width of $\phi(2170)$: $M = 2160 \pm 80$ MeV, $\Gamma = 125 \pm 65$ MeV.
- Coupling $\phi(2170) \to \phi f_0(980)$: $3123 \pm 561$ MeV.
- Coupling $K^+(1460) \to K^+ f_0(980)$: $4858 \pm 1337$ MeV.
- Partial decay width of $\phi(2170) \to K^+(1460) K^-$: $0.8 - 2.0$ MeV (range across form factors).
- Partial decay width of $\phi(2170) \to K^+_1(1400) K^-$: $1.5 - 3.1$ MeV (Model B); $8 - 19$ MeV (Model C).
- Partial decay width of $\phi(2170) \to K^+_1(1270) K^-$ (Model A, superposition of two poles): $0.8 - 1.5$ MeV.
- Ratio $B_1 = \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+(1460)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1400)K^-)$: $0.62 \pm 0.20$ (Model B); $0.11 \pm 0.04$ (Model C). Experimental values: $0.64 \pm 0.92$ (Solution 1), $0.03 \pm 0.04$ (Solution 2).
- Ratio $B_2 = \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+(1460)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1270)K^-)$: $1.3 \pm 0.4$ (Model A, two poles). Experimental values: $0.40 \pm 0.54$ (Solution 1), $0.02 \pm 0.03$ (Solution 2).
- Ratio $B_3 = \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1270)K^-) / \Gamma(\phi_R \to K^+_1(1400)K^-)$: $0.04 \pm 0.01$ (Model B); $0.09 \pm 0.02$ (Model C, Solution $\mathbb{S}_1$). Experimental values: $1.62 \pm 1.38$ (Solution 1), $1.55 \pm 0.19$ (Solution 2).

## Context

This work extends the molecular description of $\phi(2170)$ as a $\phi f_0(980)$ bound state, originally proposed by Martinez Torres et al. (2008) to explain the $e^+e^- \to \phi f_0(980)$ cross section, by calculating its decays to final states involving kaonic resonances. It directly tests the molecular hypothesis against recent BESIII data (2020) on $\mathcal{B}r \, \Gamma^{e^+e^-}_R$ for these channels. The results show that the molecular picture can accommodate the data, but the interpretation is complicated by the poorly understood internal structure of $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$; the authors note that higher-statistics experimental data are needed to distinguish between competing models for these resonances and to draw robust conclusions about the nature of $\phi(2170)$.
