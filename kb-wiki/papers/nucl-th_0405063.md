# Mesonic Content of the Nucleon and the Roper Resonance

> We investigate colorless (mesonic) 3q-q qbar components in the nucleon and the Roper resonance N*(1440). Starting from constituent quarks and gluons we estimate the excitation of quark-antiquark pairs in a gluon exchange model with the strong coupling constant extracted from a comparison with a non-perturbative resonating group calculation of the pi, eta, rho and omega content of the nucleon. Applying the same model to the Roper resonance, we find as the most striking result a very strong scalar-isoscalar sigma content of the N*(1440) with a strength of the 3q-q qbar configurations comparable to the 3q component itself.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the Roper resonance N*(1440) contains a very strong scalar-isoscalar sigma-meson component, with the strength of the 3q-q qbar configurations comparable to the 3q component itself, but this result depends on the specific model parameters and the principal value treatment of the energy denominator.

## Method

The authors start from a constituent quark model with one-gluon exchange, harmonic confinement, and q qbar pair creation. They first fit the strong coupling constant alpha_s to reproduce non-perturbative resonating group calculation results for the pion, eta, rho and omega content of the nucleon. They then apply the same perturbative one-loop model to estimate the mesonic admixtures in the Roper resonance, using a quark-diquark representation for baryons and Gaussian wave functions for all particles.

## Key numbers

- Admixture probabilities in the nucleon from the resonating group calculation: P_pi = 0.045, P_eta = 0.005, P_rho = 0.03, P_omega = 0.004
- Fitted strong coupling constant: alpha_s approximately 1.8
- Estimated sigma-content in the nucleon: P_sigma = 0.025
- For the Roper resonance: admixture probability of approximately 50 percent for the sigma meson and 10 percent for the eta meson
- Mass difference for sigma and eta with the Roper: m_sigma,eta + M - M* approximately 50 MeV
- Mass difference for rho and omega with the Roper: m_rho,omega + M - M* approximately 280 MeV
- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 330 MeV; gluon mass: m_g = 660 MeV (m_g = 2 m_q)
- Gaussian width parameters for mesons (in fm): pi = 0.5, eta = 0.5, sigma = 0.7, rho = 0.75, omega = 0.6

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model studies of baryon resonances (references 1-3) that predicted the Roper resonance mass several hundred MeV above its experimental value when restricted to three-quark configurations. The paper builds on non-perturbative resonating group calculations by Hoffmann (reference 19) for the nucleon's mesonic content and applies the same framework to the Roper resonance. The findings suggest that any quantitative treatment of baryon resonances in the continuum requires inclusion of at least the next-to-leading 3q(q qbar) component in the Fock expansion, and that the strong sigma content of the Roper resonance dominates hadron or photon induced double pion production in the N*(1440) regime.
