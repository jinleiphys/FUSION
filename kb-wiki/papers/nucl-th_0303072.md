# Cross sections for the excitation of isovector charge-exchange resonances in 208Tl

> The Glauber approximation for the treatment of heavy-ion scattering, has already been shown to give reliable predictions for the reaction cross section in the particular case of intermediate energy charge-exchange processes. In the present work, we couple a Glauber-type model to microscopic Random Phase Approximation calculations of the charge-exchange excitations of $^{208}$Pb. The aim is to solve the longstanding question whether the very elusive charge-exchange isovector monopole has been really identified in the past experiments, or other multipoles were prevalent in the observed spectra.

## Key claim
The authors show that the octupole (3-) cross section dominates over the monopole (0+) cross section in the energy region around 21 MeV in 208Tl, where previous experiments had identified a candidate for the isovector giant monopole resonance (IVGMR). This conclusion holds even when integrating over the small angular ranges (0-1 degree and 1-2.5 degrees) used in the experiment of Lhenry et al. (1995, 1996), although the monopole-to-octupole ratio increases at forward angles.

## Method
The authors coupled a Glauber-type reaction model to microscopic self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using the Skyrme SIII effective force. Transition densities for 0+, 1-, 2+, and 3- states in 208Tl were computed from the RPA and used as input to calculate charge-exchange cross sections for the (13C,13N) reaction on 208Pb at 60 MeV/A. The discrete RPA results were averaged with Lorentzian distributions of 2 MeV width to account for escape and spreading widths.

## Key numbers
- The discrete RPA peak for the IVGMR in 208Tl is at 19 MeV, shifted downward by about 2.8 MeV to 16.2 MeV when continuum coupling is included.
- The experimental candidate for the monopole excitation in 208Tl from the GANIL experiment (Berat et al., 1989, 1993) at 50 MeV/A appeared at 21 MeV excitation energy (referred to the ground state of 208Pb).
- The harmonic oscillator basis parameter used in the RPA calculation was hbar-omega = 6.2 MeV.
- The number of particle-hole configurations used in the RPA: 64 neutron p-proton h and 88 proton p-neutron h for 0+; 172 and 240 for 1-; 244 and 348 for 2+; 288 and 420 for 3-.
- The Lorentzian averaging width used for the cross section calculations was 2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model for charge-exchange reactions, previously applied to p-shell nuclei by Maqueda, Lenzi, Vitturi, and Zardi (1999), to the heavy target 208Pb. It addresses the longstanding discrepancy between self-consistent RPA calculations by Colo and Van Giai (1996), which predicted the IVGMR at 13-16 MeV depending on the Skyrme parametrization, and the experimental candidate at 21 MeV from the (13C,13N) experiments of Berat et al. (1989, 1993) and Lhenry et al. (1995, 1996). The results enable future experimental searches for the IVGMR and other charge-exchange multipoles by providing quantitative predictions of which multipolarities dominate in different energy and angular ranges.
