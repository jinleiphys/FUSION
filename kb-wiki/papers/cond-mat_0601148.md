# Surface Energy in Cold Asymmetrical Fermion Superfluids

> We derive the energy of the surface between the normal and superfluid components of a mixed phase of a system composed of two particle species with different densities. The surface energy is obtained by the integration of the free energy density in the interface between the two phases. We show that the mixed phase remains as the favored ground state over the gapless phase in weak coupling. We find that the surface energy effects emerge only at strong coupling.

# Surface Energy in Cold Asymmetrical Fermion Superfluids

## Key claim
The mixed phase (phase separation between normal and superfluid components) remains the energetically favored ground state over the homogeneous gapless superfluid phase in weak coupling, but surface energy effects emerge only at strong coupling. The surface energy vanishes in the weak-coupling BCS regime where phase separation exists, meaning the mixed phase is stable with no surface energy cost in that regime.

## Method
The author derives the surface energy between normal and superfluid components of a mixed phase in a two-species fermionic system by integrating the free energy density across the interface. A Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the effective action is performed near zero temperature, keeping terms up to fourth order in the gap parameter for strong coupling and up to sixth order for weak coupling, with the gradient coefficient calculated microscopically. The surface energy is obtained from the spatial profile of the gap parameter at the interface, solving the resulting differential equation for the order parameter with boundary conditions matching the normal and superfluid phases.

## Key numbers
- The surface energy in the strong coupling regime ($1/\bar{k}_F a > -4/\pi$) is given by $\sigma^{+} = \frac{25 \times \sqrt{2}}{48} |\alpha| \xi \Delta_0^2$, where $\alpha = N_0 (\pi/4\bar{k}_F|a| - 1)$, $N_0 = m\bar{k}_F/\pi^2$, and $\xi^2 = \gamma/|\alpha|$ with $\gamma = m/(4\pi)^2\bar{k}_F$.
- The weak coupling regime is defined by $1/\bar{k}_F a \leq -4/\pi$ (i.e., $a$ small and negative), where the surface energy vanishes for $1/k_F a \ll -1$.
- In the BEC regime ($a > 0$), the critical chemical potential asymmetry for the pairing gap is $h_c = E_b/2$, where $E_b = 1/ma^2$ is the molecular binding energy.
- For $h < |\mu|$ in the BEC regime, the pairing gap is $\Delta(h < |\mu|) = \sqrt{16/3\pi \cdot g(h/|\mu|)} \, |\mu|$, where $g(h/|\mu|) = (1+h/|\mu|)^{3/2} + (1-h/|\mu|)^{3/2}$.
- The surface energy in the weak coupling regime vanishes at $z_{max} = 0.924$, where $z = 4\alpha\delta/\beta^2$.

## Context
This work extends the mixed phase calculations of Caldas (2004) and Caldas (2005), which compared mixed phase and breached-pair superfluid energies but neglected surface terms as negligible in the thermodynamic limit. The derivation follows the approach of Reddy and Rupak (2004) for surface tension between normal and superconducting phases in quark matter, but calculates the gradient coefficients microscopically rather than matching to effective theory. The results are consistent with the Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of Carlson and Reddy (2005), which indicated that a homogeneous gapless superfluid would exist only at very strong coupling where $\Delta/\mu \gtrsim 1$, and the surface energy cost could help stabilize that phase over the mixed phase. The surface energy obtained here can be applied to studies of the BEC-BCS crossover in asymmetrical fermionic systems and to superfluid properties of neutron matter.
