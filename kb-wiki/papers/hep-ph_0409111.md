# Electromagnetic proton form factors in large $N_{c}$ QCD

> The electromagnetic form factors of the proton are obtained using a particular realization of QCD in the large $N_c$ limit (${QCD}_{\infty}$), which sums up the infinite number of zero-width resonances to yield an Euler's Beta function (Dual-${QCD}_{\infty}$). The form factors $F_1(q^2)$ and $F_2(q^2)$, as well as $G_M(q^2)$ agree very well with reanalyzed space-like data in the whole range of momentum transfer. In addition, the predicted ratio $μ_p G_E/G_M$ is in good agreement with recent polarization transfer measurements at Jefferson Lab.

# Electromagnetic proton form factors in large \(N_{c}\) QCD

## Key claim
The Dual-QCD\(_{\infty}\) framework, which sums infinite zero-width resonances into an Euler Beta function, reproduces the proton electromagnetic form factors \(F_1(q^2)\), \(F_2(q^2)\), and \(G_M(q^2)\) in the space-like region up to \(Q^2 \sim 30\) GeV\(^2\), but only after the experimental data are corrected for two-photon exchange effects to resolve the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements. The ratio \(\mu_p G_E/G_M\) is also well described, though the result is highly sensitive to the correlated pair of free parameters \(\beta_1\) and \(\beta_2\).

## Method
The Dirac and Pauli form factors \(F_1(q^2)\) and \(F_2(q^2)\) are expressed as sums over an infinite tower of zero-width vector-meson resonances with equally spaced squared masses \(M_n^2 = M_\rho^2 (1 + 2n)\). The couplings are chosen so that the sums close to Euler Beta functions \(B(\beta_{1,2} - 1,\; 1/2 - \alpha' s)\), with \(\alpha' = 1/(2 M_\rho^2)\) as the universal string tension. The free parameters \(\beta_1\) and \(\beta_2\) control the asymptotic Regge behaviour \(F_{1,2}(s) \sim (-\alpha' s)^{(1-\beta_{1,2})}\) and are fixed by fitting to a data base of \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) derived from Sachs form factors corrected for two-photon exchange (Brash et al., 2002). A combined fit to \(F_1\), \(F_2\), and the ratio \(\mu_p G_E/G_M\) yields a range for the parameters.

## Key numbers
- Fitted parameters: \(\beta_1 = 2.95 - 3.03\), \(\beta_2 = 4.13 - 4.20\) (combined fit to \(F_1\), \(F_2\), and \(\mu_p G_E/G_M\)).
- Predicted masses for the first three radial excitations: \(M_{\rho'} \simeq 1340\) MeV, \(M_{\rho''} \simeq 1720\) MeV, \(M_{\rho'''} \simeq 2034\) MeV (compared to experimental values \(1465 \pm 25\) MeV, \(1700 \pm 20\) MeV, \(2149 \pm 17\) MeV from the Particle Data Group).
- Root-mean-square radii from the fit: \(<r^2_1>^{1/2} = 0.72\) fm, \(<r^2_2>^{1/2} = 0.78\) fm; Sachs radii \(<r^2_E>^{1/2} = 0.81\) fm, \(<r^2_M>^{1/2} = 0.76\) fm.
- The form factors are fitted over the range \(0 \leq Q^2 \lesssim 30\) GeV\(^2\).
- The ratio \(\mu_p G_E/G_M\) is shown to deviate significantly from unity at Jefferson Lab up to \(Q^2 \simeq 6\) GeV\(^2\) (polarization transfer data from Jones et al., 2000; Punjabi et al., 2005).

## Context
This work extends the Dual-QCD\(_{\infty}\) approach previously applied successfully to the pion electromagnetic form factor (Dominguez, 2003) to the more complex case of the nucleon. It contrasts with naive Vector Meson Dominance (which corresponds to \(\beta_{1,2} = 2\)) and with purely perturbative QCD, neither of which reproduces the data as well. The analysis assumes that two-photon exchange corrections (Arrington, 2003; Blunden et al., 2003) resolve the long-standing discrepancy between Rosenbluth cross-section extractions and polarization transfer measurements of the Sachs form factors. The results support Dual-QCD\(_{\infty}\) as a viable non-perturbative realization of large-\(N_c\) QCD, and the predicted radii are in reasonable agreement with other determinations in the literature (Mergeil, 2001).
