# Is the strong-interaction proton-proton scattering length renormalization scale dependent in effective field theory?

> It is shown that the strong-interaction ${}^1S_0$ proton-proton scattering length in very low-energy effective field theory does not depend on the renormalization scale, if the electromagnetic interaction is "switched off" consistently.

## Key claim

The strong-interaction proton-proton scattering length in very low-energy effective field theory does not depend on the renormalization scale, provided the electromagnetic interaction is switched off not only in the explicit potential but also in the running of the strong coupling constant.

## Method

The paper analyzes the renormalization-scale dependence of the strong-interaction \( {}^1S_0 \) proton-proton scattering length using a non-relativistic effective field theory where all degrees of freedom except protons and photons are integrated out. The scattering amplitude is calculated from the effective Lagrangian containing a contact interaction \( C_0 \) and the static Coulomb repulsion, using dimensional regularization with the power divergent subtraction (PDS) scheme. The author then examines the running of the renormalized coupling constant \( C_0(\mu) \) and demonstrates that setting the fine structure constant \( \alpha \) to zero in the running equation yields a new coupling \( \tilde{C}_0(\mu) \) and a corresponding scattering length that is independent of the renormalization scale.

## Key numbers

- The effective strength of the Coulomb repulsion is given by \( \eta(p) = \alpha M / 2p \), where \( \alpha = 1/137 \) is the fine structure constant and \( M \) is the proton mass.
- The finite part of the dressed bubble integral in the PDS scheme is \( J_0^{\text{finite}}(p) = \frac{\alpha M^2}{4\pi} \left[ \ln\frac{\mu\sqrt{\pi}}{\alpha M} + 1 - \frac{3}{2}C_E - H(\eta) \right] - \frac{\mu M}{4\pi} \), where \( C_E = 0.5772\cdots \) is Euler's constant.
- The Coulomb-modified scattering length \( a_{pp}^C \) and the strong scattering length \( a_{pp} \) are related by \( \frac{1}{a_{pp}} = \frac{1}{a_{pp}^C} + \alpha M \left[ \ln\frac{\mu\sqrt{\pi}}{\alpha M} + 1 - \frac{3}{2}C_E \right] \).
- The running of the renormalized coupling constant is \( C_0(\mu) = \frac{C_0(\mu_0)}{1 - C_0(\mu_0) \left\{ -\frac{\alpha M^2}{4\pi} \ln\frac{\mu}{\mu_0} + \frac{M}{4\pi}(\mu - \mu_0) \right\}} \).

## Context

This work addresses a puzzle raised by Kong and Ravndal (1999), who found that the strong-interaction proton-proton scattering length defined in effective field theory depends on the renormalization scale. The author argues that this dependence arises because the Coulomb interaction was not fully switched off in the running of the strong coupling constant, and shows that consistently setting \( \alpha = 0 \) in the running removes the scale dependence. The result is consistent with the conclusion of Sauer (1975) that the \( {}^1S_0 \) proton-proton scattering amplitude cannot be divided into strong and electromagnetic parts in a model-independent way, and with independent analyses by Rusetsky (2002) and Gasser (2003). The work enables a consistent definition of the strong scattering length in effective field theory, though its numerical value must still be taken as input from other sources such as neutron-neutron scattering.
