# The partonic structure of the nucleon from generalized transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions

> We discuss the general formalism for the calculation in light-front quark models of the fully unintegrated, off-diagonal quark-quark correlator of the nucleon, parametrized in terms of generalized transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs). By taking specific limits or projections, these GTMDs yield various transverse-momentum dependent and generalized parton distributions, thus providing a unified framework to simultaneously model different observables. The corresponding distributions in impact-parameter space are the Wigner functions which provide multidimensional images of the quark distributions in phase space. We present results within a light-front constituent quark model, discussing some of the complementary aspects encoded in the different distributions and the relation to the quark orbital angular momentum of the proton.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified formalism for modeling generalized transverse momentum-dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) within light-front quark models, showing that these GTMDs reduce to all other parton distributions (TMDs, GPDs, PDFs, and form factors) under specific limits or projections, but the results are restricted to the three-quark Fock sector and do not include gluon contributions or higher Fock states.

## Method

The authors derive an overlap representation of the fully-unintegrated quark-quark correlator in terms of light-front wave functions (LFWFs) at twist-two level, restricted to the three-quark Fock sector. They apply this formalism to two specific models: the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) and the light-front chiral quark-soliton model (LFχQSM). The GTMDs are then Fourier-transformed to impact-parameter space to obtain Wigner distributions, and specific limits are taken to recover TMDs, GPDs, and form factors.

## Key numbers

- In the LFCQM, the anomalous magnetic moments are κ^u = 1.86 and κ^d = -1.57, compared to experimental values κ^u = 1.673 and κ^d = -2.033
- Total quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) from the Ji relation: L^q_z = 0.126 in LFCQM and 0.069 in LFχQSM
- Total quark OAM from Wigner distributions (Jaffe-Manohar): ℓ^q_z = 0.126 in LFCQM and 0.069 in LFχQSM
- Total quark OAM from the h_{1T}^⊥ TMD relation: ℒ^q_z = 0.126 in LFCQM and 0.069 in LFχQSM
- Individual u-quark OAM in LFCQM: L^u_z = 0.071, ℓ^u_z = 0.131, ℒ^u_z = 0.169
- Individual d-quark OAM in LFCQM: L^d_z = 0.055, ℓ^d_z = -0.005, ℒ^d_z = -0.042
- Individual u-quark OAM in LFχQSM: L^u_z = -0.008, ℓ^u_z = 0.073, ℒ^u_z = 0.093
- Individual d-quark OAM in LFχQSM: L^d_z = 0.077, ℓ^d_z = -0.004, ℒ^d_z = -0.023
- Wigner distribution distortion shown for fixed transverse momentum k_⊥ = 0.3 GeV
- In impact-parameter space, d-quark OAM flips sign at |b_⊥| > 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends the GTMD formalism developed by Meissner (2009) and Lorce (2011) by providing explicit light-front wave function overlap representations within constituent quark models. It unifies the description of TMDs, GPDs, and Wigner distributions, enabling simultaneous modeling of observables from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, deeply virtual Compton scattering, and elastic scattering. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations by Hagler (2007) and provide a framework for extracting quark orbital angular momentum from experimental data, as recently attempted by Bacchetta (2011) using the Sivers function to reconstruct the GPD E.
