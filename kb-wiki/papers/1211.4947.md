# Charged lepton induced one kaon production off the nucleon

> We study single kaon production off the nucleon induced by electrons (positrons) i.e. $e^-(e^+) + N \rightarrow ν_e (\bar ν_e) + \bar K (K) + N^\prime$ at low energies. The possibility of observing these processes with the high luminosity beams available at TJNAF and Mainz is discussed, taking into account that the strangeness conserving electromagnetic reactions have a higher energy threshold for $\bar K (K)$ production. The calculations are done using a microscopic model that starts from the SU(3) chiral Lagrangians, includes background terms and the resonant mechanisms associated to the lowest lying resonance $Σ^*(1385)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that single kaon production via charged-current weak interactions of electrons and positrons with nucleons, though having very small cross sections, could be observable at existing facilities like TJNAF and MAMI, provided that the high luminosity beams and the absence of competing electromagnetic background at low energies are exploited. The hedge is that the predicted event rates are modest and would require quite long runs, especially after accounting for detector acceptance and kaon survival probability.

## Method

The authors develop a microscopic model based on SU(3) chiral Lagrangians to compute the total and differential cross sections for the six charged-current reactions e⁻ + N → νₑ + K̄ + N' and e⁺ + N → ν̄ₑ + K + N' at electron/positron energies up to 1.5 GeV. The hadronic current includes background terms (contact term, meson-exchange, kaon pole, s-channel and u-channel hyperon exchanges) and the resonant contribution from the Σ*(1385) resonance, with a global dipole form factor (M_F = 1.05 GeV) applied to all background currents. Event rate estimates are obtained by folding the cross sections with the luminosities of the MAMI and TJNAF facilities.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for electron-induced weak K⁻ production on a proton is around 600 MeV, compared to 1.5 GeV for electromagnetic production.
- For the reaction e⁻ + p → νₑ + K⁻ + p at Eₑ = 1.5 GeV, the predicted event rate is 480 events per day at TJNAF (luminosity 5×10³⁸ s⁻¹ cm⁻²) and 48 events per day at MAMI (luminosity 5×10³⁷ s⁻¹ cm⁻²).
- For the reaction e⁻ + p → νₑ + K̄⁰ + n at Eₑ = 1.5 GeV, the predicted event rate is 320 events per day at TJNAF and 32 events per day at MAMI.
- The Σ*(1385) resonance contributes about 10% of the total cross section at Eₑ = 1 GeV and about 5% at Eₑ = 1.5 GeV.
- Changing the dipole mass M_F by 20% (up or down from 1.05 GeV) changes the cross section by about 10% for the neutron channel and about 28% for the two proton channels at Eₑ = 1.5 GeV.
- Varying the D coupling constant by 5% (while keeping g_A = D + F = 1.26 constant) changes the cross sections by about 5% (growing for proton processes, decreasing for the neutron process).
- The kaon momentum distribution for e⁻ n → νₑ K⁻ n and e⁻ p → νₑ K⁻ p peaks around 0.3 GeV, while for e⁻ p → νₑ K̄⁰ n it peaks around 0.6 GeV at Eₑ = 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier formalism for neutrino/antineutrino-induced single kaon production (Alam 2012, Rafi Alam 2010, Alvarez-Ruso 2012) to the case of electron and positron beams. It contrasts with the much larger cross sections of electromagnetic associated strangeness production (studied at MAMI, JLAB, ELSA, SPring-8, GRAAL by many groups), which requires a higher energy threshold because an additional kaon must be produced to conserve strangeness. The results open the possibility of using these weak reactions to study the hyperon/nucleon weak coupling constants D and F and their Q² dependence, which are less precisely known than the combination g_A = D + F from neutron beta decay (Cabibbo 2003).
