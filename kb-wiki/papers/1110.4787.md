# $pp \to pp K^{+}K^{-}$ reaction at high energies

> We evaluate differential distributions for the four-body $p p \to p p K^{+} K^{-}$ reaction at high energies which constitutes an irreducible background to three-body processes $p p \to p p M$, where $M=ϕ$, $f_{2}(1275)$, $f_{0}(1500)$, $f_{2}'(1525)$, $χ_{c0}$. We consider central diffractive contribution mediated by Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges as well as completely new mechanism of emission of kaons from the proton lines. We include absorption effects due to proton-proton interaction and kaon-kaon rescattering. We compare our results with measured cross sections for the CERN ISR experiment. We make predictions for future experiments at RHIC, Tevatron and LHC. Differential distributions in invariant two-kaon mass, kaon rapidities and transverse momenta of kaons are presented. Two-dimensional distribution in $(y_{K^+}, y_{K^-})$ is particularly interesting. The higher the incident energy, the higher preference for the same-hemisphere emission of kaons. We find that the kaons from the new mechanism of emission directly from proton lines are produced rather forward and backward but the corresponding cross section is rather small. The processes considered here constitute a sizeable contribution to the total proton-proton cross section as well as to kaon inclusive cross section. We consider a measurement of exclusive production of scalar $χ_{c0}$ meson in the proton-proton collisions via $χ_{c0} \to K^{+}K^{-}$ decay. The corresponding amplitude for exclusive central diffractive $χ_{c0}$ meson production is calculated within the $k_{t}$-factorization approach. The influence of kinematical cuts on the signal-to-background ratio is discussed.

## Key claim

The four-body $pp \to pp K^{+}K^{-}$ reaction constitutes an irreducible background to exclusive three-body processes $pp \to pp M$ (where $M = \phi, f_2(1275), f_0(1500), f_2'(1525), \chi_{c0}$), and the authors evaluate this background using central diffractive mechanisms mediated by Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges plus a new mechanism of kaon emission from proton lines, finding that the background is sizeable but that the signal-to-background ratio for $\chi_{c0}$ detection can be improved with appropriate kinematical cuts.

## Method

The authors calculate the Born amplitude for central diffractive $K^{+}K^{-}$ production using Pomeron and Reggeon ($f_2, a_2, \omega, \rho$) exchanges for the kaon-nucleon interactions, with kaon exchange treated via a reggeized propagator that interpolates between meson and Reggeon exchange. Absorption effects from proton-proton interactions and kaon-kaon rescattering are included via eikonal corrections and loop integrals over the $KK \to KK$ subprocess amplitude, which itself combines vector meson ($\rho, \omega, \phi$) exchanges at low energies and Regge exchanges at high energies. Additional diffractive mechanisms involving $\Lambda$ baryon exchange, kaon exchange from proton lines, and $\pi\pi \to KK$ rescattering via $K^{*}(892)$ exchange are also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The $\chi_{c0}$ branching fraction to $K^{+}K^{-}$ is $\mathcal{B} = (0.61 \pm 0.035)\%$, while the $\chi_{c2}$ branching fraction to $K^{+}K^{-}$ is $\mathcal{B} = (0.109 \pm 0.008)\%$ and the $\chi_{c1}$ does not decay to $KK$ at all.
- The $\chi_{c0}$ branching fraction to $J/\psi + \gamma$ is $\mathcal{B} = (1.16 \pm 0.08)\%$, compared to $(34.4 \pm 1.5)\%$ for $\chi_{c1}$ and $(19.5 \pm 0.8)\%$ for $\chi_{c2}$.
- The off-shell form factor parameter extracted from CERN ISR data at $\sqrt{s} = 62$ GeV is $\Lambda_{off}^{2} = 2$ GeV$^{2}$.
- The $\Lambda K N$ coupling constant used is $g^{2}_{\Lambda K N} = 14$.
- The Pomeron trajectory parameters are $\alpha_{I\!P}(t) = 1.0808 + (0.25$ GeV$^{-2}) t$, with $C_{I\!P}^{NN} = 21.7$ mb and $C_{I\!P}^{KN} = 11.82$ mb.
- The $\pi N N$ coupling constant is $g^{2}_{\pi N N}/4\pi = 13.5$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of exclusive $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ production in $pp$ collisions (Lebiedowicz and Szczurek, 2009, 2010) to the $K^{+}K^{-}$ channel, and provides the non-resonant background calculation needed for exclusive resonance production measurements. The analysis builds on the Donnachie-Landshoff Regge parametrization for total cross sections and on the CERN ISR data at $\sqrt{s} = 62$ GeV (ABCDHW89) and $\sqrt{s} = 63$ GeV (AFS85) for normalization. The results enable predictions for exclusive $\chi_{c0}$ meson detection via $K^{+}K^{-}$ decay at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC, complementing the authors' earlier work on $\chi_{c0} \to \pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ detection (Lebiedowicz, Pasechnik, and Szczurek, 2011).
