# Off-Shell Rho-Omega Mixing Through Quark Loops With Non-Perturbative Meson Vertex And Quark Mass Functions

> The momemtum dependence of the off-shell $ρ$-$ω$ mixing amplitude is calculated through a two-quark loop diagram, using non-perturbative meson-quark vertex functions for the $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons, as well as non-perturbative quark propagators. Both these quantities are generated self-consistently through an interlinked BSE-cum-SDE approach with a 3D support for the BSE kernel with two basic constants which are pre- checked against a wide cross section of both meson and baryon spectra within a common structural framework for their respective 3D BSE's. With this pre-calibration, the on-shell strength works out at -2.434$δ(m_q^2)$ in units of the change in "constituent mass squared", which is consistent with the $e^+e^-$ to $π^+π^-$ data for a u-d mass difference of ~4 MeV ,while the relative off-shell strength (0.99 $\pm$ 0.01) lies midway between quark-loop and QCD-SR results. We also calculate the photon-mediated $ρ$-$ω$ propagator whose off-shell structure has an additional pole at $q^2$=0. The implications of these results vis-a-vis related investigations are discussed.

## Key claim

The off-shell rho-omega mixing amplitude is calculated through a two-quark loop diagram using non-perturbative meson-quark vertex functions and quark propagators generated by a 3D Bethe-Salpeter equation cum Schwinger-Dyson equation approach, with the on-shell strength consistent with e+e- to pi+pi- data for a u-d mass difference of ~4 MeV, while the relative off-shell strength (0.99 ± 0.01) lies midway between quark-loop and QCD sum-rule results.

## Method

The authors compute the momentum-dependent rho-omega mixing amplitude via a two-quark loop diagram, employing non-perturbative meson-quark vertex functions and quark propagators generated self-consistently through an interlinked Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) cum Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) approach with a 3D support for the BSE kernel. The formalism uses the Covariant Instaneity Ansatz (CIA) to reduce the 4D BSE to a 3D form, with the hadron-quark vertex function depending only on the transverse component of the internal quark momentum. The quark mass function is obtained by relating it to the pion vertex function in the chiral limit via the Ward-Takahashi identity, and the loop integrals are evaluated analytically by differentiating with respect to the constituent quark mass squared.

## Key numbers

- On-shell mixing amplitude: θ(M²) = -1289 (MeV) · δ(m_q), where δ(m_q) is the u-d mass difference; with δ(m_q) ≈ 4 MeV, this gives θ(M²) = -5156 MeV².
- Off-shell parameter λ = 0.99 ± 0.01, defined by θ(q²) = θ(M²)[1 - λ(1 + q²/M²)].
- Photon-mediated rho-omega mixing on-shell value: θ_γ(M²) = +1316 (MeV)².
- Photon-mediated off-shell parameter μ = 0.7197, defined by g_V(P²) = f_V(M²)[1 - μ(1 + P²/M²)].
- BS normalizer for vector meson: N_V⁻² = 0.0502 (GeV)⁻⁶.
- Constituent quark mass m_q = 265 MeV, from spectroscopic fits.
- Beta parameter for rho-omega: β² = 0.0692 (GeV)².

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-loop calculations of rho-omega mixing (Mitra and collaborators, 1992; Piekarewicz and Williams, 1992) and QCD sum-rule analyses (Hatsuda et al., 1993) by using a 3D BSE-cum-SDE formalism that is pre-calibrated against both meson and baryon spectra (Mitra et al., 1992; 1993). The resulting off-shell parameter λ = 0.99 lies between the quark-loop values (near 0.5-0.7) and the QCD sum-rule range (1.43-1.85), and is surprisingly close to the nucleon-loop value of about unity (Krein et al., 1993). The calculation also provides the photon-mediated rho-omega propagator with an additional pole at q²=0, enabling improved estimates of charge symmetry violation in the nucleon-nucleon potential relevant to the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.
