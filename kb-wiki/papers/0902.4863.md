# New developments in nuclear supersymmetry

> We discuss several new developments in nuclear supersymmetry, in particular the identification of a new supersymmetric quartet of nuclei in the A~190 mass region, consisting of the 192,193 Os and 193,194 Ir nuclei, and a study of correlations between different transfer reactions.

# New developments in nuclear supersymmetry

## Key claim

The authors present evidence for a second supersymmetric quartet of nuclei in the A~190 mass region (192,193Os and 193,194Ir) using the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π supersymmetry framework, but note that the identification is conditional on the scarce experimental data for 193Os and that a more detailed study is in progress.

## Method

The authors analyze the energy spectra of the quartet using a single Hamiltonian expressed in terms of Casimir invariants of the subgroup chain of the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π supersymmetry, with parameters determined by fitting experimental excitation energies of 194Ir from polarized (d,α) transfer reactions. They then compare theoretical predictions for two-nucleon transfer spectroscopic strengths from the 196Pt(d,α)194Ir reaction with experimental data, and derive correlations between one-neutron and two-proton transfer reactions using a generalized F-spin formalism with SU(3) isoscalar factors.

## Key numbers

- The energy spectrum of 194Ir is calculated with parameters A+B = 35.0 keV, B' = -33.6 keV, C = 35.1 keV, D = 6.3 keV, and E = 4.5 keV
- For the one-neutron stripping reaction 194Pt → 195Pt (N=5), the predicted ratio of intensities R(ee→on) = 29.3, compared to the experimental value of 19.0 for j=5/2
- For the one-neutron stripping reaction 192Os → 193Os (N=6), the predicted ratio R(ee→on) = 37.8
- For the inverse pick-up reaction 195Pt → 194Pt (Nπ=1, Nν=4), the predicted ratio R(on→ee) = 1.96
- For the inverse pick-up reaction 193Os → 192Os (Nπ=2, Nν=4), the predicted ratio R(on→ee) = 3.24

## Context

This work extends the nuclear supersymmetry framework previously applied to the first supersymmetric quartet in the A~190 region (194,195Pt and 195,196Au) by Metz (1999), Groeger (2000), Wirth (2003), and Barea (2005, 2008). The new quartet (192,193Os and 193,194Ir) is characterized by Nπ=3 and Nν=5, and the parameter set obtained is close to that used for the 192Os-193Ir pair by Bijker (1981). The analysis enables predictions for 193Os that can be tested by future (d,p) stripping and (p,d) pick-up experiments, and establishes correlations between transfer reactions across different quartets.
