# Baryon interactions from lattice QCD with physical quark masses -- Nuclear forces and $ΞΞ$ forces --

> We present the latest lattice QCD results for baryon interactions obtained at nearly physical quark masses. $N_f = 2+1$ nonperturbatively ${\cal O}(a)$-improved Wilson quark action with stout smearing and Iwasaki gauge action are employed on the lattice of $(96a)^4 \simeq (8.1\mbox{fm})^4$ with $a^{-1} \simeq 2.3$ GeV, where $m_π\simeq 146$ MeV and $m_K \simeq 525$ MeV. In this report, we study the two-nucleon systems and two-$Ξ$ systems in $^1S_0$ channel and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ coupled channel, and extract central and tensor interactions by the HAL QCD method. We also present the results for the $NΩ$ interaction in $^5S_2$ channel which is relevant to the $NΩ$ pair-momentum correlation in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

At nearly physical quark masses (mπ ≈ 146 MeV), the HAL QCD method extracts baryon interactions from lattice QCD without requiring ground-state saturation, but the results for NN forces are preliminary and suffer from large statistical fluctuations, with further studies using larger statistics still underway.

## Method

The authors employed Nf = 2+1 nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action with stout smearing and Iwasaki gauge action on a (96a)4 ≈ (8.1 fm)4 lattice with a−1 ≈ 2.3 GeV. They computed Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlators and applied the time-dependent HAL QCD method to extract central and tensor potentials for two-nucleon (NN) and two-Ξ (ΞΞ) systems in the 1S0 channel and 3S1-3D1 coupled channel, as well as the NΩ interaction in the 5S2 channel, using a velocity expansion to handle the non-locality of the potentials.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a−1 ≈ 2.333 GeV (a ≈ 0.0846 fm)
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 146 MeV; kaon mass: mK ≈ 525 MeV
- Lattice volume: (96a)4 ≈ (8.1 fm)4
- Statistics for NN and ΞΞ systems: 414 configurations × 4 rotations × 72 wall sources, binned by 46 configurations
- Statistics for NΩ system: 200 configurations × 4 rotations × 48 wall sources, binned by 10 configurations
- ΞΞ (1S0) interaction: strongly attractive at low energies but not sufficient to form a bound state
- NN (1S0) repulsive core: more enhanced compared to that in ΞΞ (1S0)
- NΩ (5S2) scattering length: negative (in particle physics convention), implying a bound state relative to the NΩ threshold

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD collaboration's previous calculations at heavier quark masses (Aoki et al., 2012) to nearly physical quark masses, updating earlier results presented by Doi et al. (2017). The time-dependent HAL QCD method overcomes the fundamental problem of ground-state saturation that plagued earlier "direct" calculations (Yamazaki, 2015; Orginos, 2015; Berkowitz, 2015), which were shown to suffer from uncontrolled systematic errors by the sanity check proposed in Iritani et al. (2017). The results enable comparison with phenomenological potentials and chiral effective field theories, and the strong attractions observed in ΞΞ (1S0) and NΩ (5S2) can be tested through pair-momentum correlations in heavy-ion collision experiments as proposed by Morita et al. (2016).
