# Comment on Final State Interactions beyond the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation description of inclusive scattering

## Key claim

No set of reasonable approximations leads to a structure function that can be expressed as a convolution of the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and a Final State Interaction (FSI) contribution, contrary to the form proposed by Benhar et al. (1991, 1994). The claimed convolution form cannot be derived from a well-defined theoretical framework, even when using an optical potential or closure approximations.

## Method

The author analyzes the dominant incoherent part of the structure function S(q,ω) for a non-relativistic infinite system, starting from the exact Green's function formalism. Two approximation schemes are examined: case a) retains the kinetic energy of the recoiling particle in an eikonal form while neglecting the target Hamiltonian, suitable for large momentum transfer q; case b) retains the target Hamiltonian but neglects the residual interaction V₁, yielding the PWIA. The author compares the 1/q expansions of these approaches and demonstrates that the FSI terms in the Benhar et al. formulation contain diagonal two-particle density matrices instead of the semi-diagonal ones that appear in the correct expansion, and miss one of two potential terms present in the proper treatment.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic limit of the reduced response at large q is given by F₀(y) = (4π)⁻² ∫_{|y|}^{∞} dp p n(p), where n(p) is the momentum distribution
- The closure approximation reduces case b) to case a) with a small shift ⟨Δ⟩ relevant only around the quasi-elastic peak at y=0
- Numerical consequences have been discussed in a comparison of inclusive cross sections of electrons from Fe and Nuclear Matter (Rinat and Taragin, 1994, 1996), showing considerable differences in the relative magnitude of leading and FSI parts, particularly for low energy losses

## Context

This comment directly challenges the work of Benhar et al. (1991, 1994, 1995) who proposed a structure function as a convolution of PWIA and an FSI contribution expressed through diagonal two-particle density matrices and an eikonal phase. The analysis extends the formalism of Gersch, Rodriguez and Smith (1973) and Gersch and Rodriguez (1973), who derived the correct 1/q expansion with semi-diagonal density matrices. The paper also addresses the work of Silver (1988, 1989), noting that the Benhar et al. formulation differs from Silver's re-derivation of the Gersch results. The conclusion that no derivation of the convolution form exists from first principles closes a specific line of inquiry into FSI corrections to inclusive scattering, redirecting attention to the proper treatment using semi-diagonal density matrices and the full two-term eikonal phase.
