# Viewing the Chemical Evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Charge Balance Functions

> Correlations from charge conservation are affected by when charge/anticharge pairs are created during the course of a relativistic heavy ion collision. For charges created early, balancing charges are typically separated by the order of one unit of spatial rapidity by the end of the collision, whereas those charges produced later in the collision are far more correlated. By analyzing correlations from STAR for different species, I show that one can distinguish the two separate waves of charge creation expected in a high-energy collision, one at early times when the QGP is formed and a second at hadronization. Further, I extract the density of up, down and strange quarks at in the QGP and find agreement at the 20% level with expectations for a chemically thermalized plasma.

# Viewing the Chemical Evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Charge Balance Functions

## Key claim
By analyzing STAR balance functions for pions, kaons, and protons, the author shows that the two-wave nature of charge creation (one wave at QGP thermalization and a second at hadronization) can be distinguished, and extracts the density of up, down, and strange quarks in the QGP with agreement at the 20% level with expectations for a chemically thermalized plasma, though the ratio of total quarks to final-state hadrons was found to be about 20% higher than lattice expectations.

## Method
Charge balance functions for pion pairs, kaon pairs, and proton-antiproton pairs from STAR were compared to a model that assumes two Gaussian components for charge correlations: one from early QGP production and one from hadronization. The model parameters (diffusive widths, quark content ratios, and a baryon reduction factor) were varied through a Markov Chain Monte Carlo trace using a Gaussian-process emulator trained on 4096 Latin hyper-cube sampling runs, fitting only the shape (normalized area) of the balance functions.

## Key numbers
- The diffusive width for the first wave of charge creation, sigma_QGP, was constrained to a range of 0.6 to 1.2 units of spatial rapidity.
- The ratio sigma_had / sigma_QGP was constrained to be near or below 0.5, with a preference for being near zero.
- The ratio of strange quarks to up or down quarks in the QGP was found to be between 0.75 and 1.0, compared to lattice expectations of 0.9 to 0.95.
- The ratio of total quarks in the QGP to total final-state hadrons was found to be near 1.1, about 20% higher than lattice expectations.
- The blast wave parameters used for thermal smearing were a collective velocity of 0.732c and a temperature of 102 MeV.
- Hadron multiplicities were taken from chemical equilibrium at a temperature of 170 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for charge balance functions developed by Pratt (2012) and applies it to preliminary STAR data from Wang (2012). The analysis provides quantitative validation of the two-wave picture of quark production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where quarks are created first during QGP thermalization and again at hadronization, a picture supported by lattice calculations from Borsanyi (2011) and Ratti (2011). The results enable future studies with broader rapidity acceptance at LHC experiments (ATLAS, CMS, ALICE) and more sophisticated treatments of diffusion, non-Gaussian production mechanisms, and three-dimensional hydrodynamic evolution.
