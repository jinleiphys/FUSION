# Four-body system of ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms: Dimer-dimer scattering

> The strong short-range repulsion, characteristic to realistic interatomic potentials, complicates the description of weakly-bound few-body systems such as those of \He atoms. The present work proposes an approach for solving this problem and applies it to a realistic system of four ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms. The potential is gradually softened such that rigorous four-body equations for bound and scattering tates can be accurately solved in the momentum-space framework, and the results are extrapolated back to the limit of the original potential. Linear correlations between three- and four body quantities are observed, and the accuracy of the procedure is improved by extrapolating in one of the three-body quantities. Results for the ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ tetramer ground and excited state binding energies and atom-trimer scattering agree well with at least some of earlier determinations and shed light on the existing disagreements. An additional case of the Phillips correlation line is established for the dimer-dimer scattering length. The trimer production rate via the ultracold two-dimer collisions is estimated, it exhibits significant finite-range effects despite the weak binding of the dimer.

# Four-body system of ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms: Dimer-dimer scattering

## Key claim

The softening and extrapolation (SE) method, which gradually reduces the short-range repulsion of the realistic LM2M2 interatomic potential and then extrapolates results back to the original potential limit, yields accurate four-body observables for ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms; however, the extrapolation in the softening parameter $\rho$ is problematic, and reliable results require using one of the three-body quantities (such as the trimer ground state binding energy $B_3$) as the extrapolation variable instead.

## Method

The author defines an auxiliary potential $V(r,\rho)$ that reduces the short-range repulsion for distances $r < \rho$, then applies a momentum-dependent Gaussian suppression factor to produce a softened potential $v_L(\rho)$ that exactly reproduces the dimer binding energy $B_2 = 1.3094$ mK. Momentum-space Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for bound states and Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for scattering are solved for a series of softening parameters $\rho$ between 2.2 and 2.5 Å, and the results are extrapolated to the original LM2M2 potential limit using linear correlations between three- and four-body quantities.

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy fixed to $B_2 = 1.3094$ mK for all softened potentials.
- Atom-dimer scattering length $A_{12} = 115.39$ Å, trimer ground state binding energy $B_3 = 126.50$ mK, and excited trimer binding energy $B_3^* = 2.2784$ mK at $\rho \le 1.7$ Å, agreeing with the benchmark calculation of Roudnev (2011).
- Tetramer ground state binding energy $B_4 = 559.3(1)$ mK, in good agreement with the variational result $B_4 = 559.22$ mK from Hiyama (2012).
- Complex dimer-dimer scattering length $A_{22} = [100.5(5) - i\,0.75(5)]$ Å.
- Atom-trimer scattering length $A_{13} = 108.8(5)$ Å and effective range $R_{13} = 29.2(2)$ Å.
- Excited tetramer binding energy $B_4^* = 127.46(2)$ mK, corresponding to $B_4^* - B_3 = 0.96(2)$ mK.
- Trimer production rate via two-dimer collisions $\beta^0_{22\to31} = 3.0(2) \times 10^{-11}$ cm$^3$/s.

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space AGS method, previously successful for four-nucleon reactions (Deltuva, 2014), to the challenging case of four ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms with a realistic potential. It addresses disagreements in the excited tetramer binding energy between prior calculations: the coordinate-space Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach of Lazauskas (2009) gave $B_4^* - B_3 = 1.087$ mK, while the variational Gaussian expansion method of Hiyama (2012) gave 0.93 mK; the present result of 0.96(2) mK supports Hiyama (2012). The work establishes a new Phillips correlation line for the dimer-dimer scattering length and demonstrates that the universal zero-range theory fails to reproduce the imaginary part of $A_{22}$, highlighting the importance of finite-range corrections for trimer production rates in ultracold gases.
