# Extended triaxial projected shell model approach for odd-neutron nuclei

> In an effort to elucidate the rich band structures observed in odd-neutron systems, triaxial projected shell model approach is extended to include three-quasineutron and five-quasiparticle configurations. This extension makes it possible to investigate the high-spin states up to and including the second band crossing. Detailed investigation has been performed for odd-mass Xe isotopes with the extended basis, and it is shown that character of the band crossing along the yrast line changes with shell filling of the 1h11/2 orbital. Further, it is observed that the three-quasiparticle state that crosses the ground-state configuration, leading to the normal band crossing phenomenon along the yrast line, first crosses the γ band based on the ground-state configuration at an earlier spin value. This crossing feature explains the occurrence of the signature inversion observed in the γ bands for some of the studied isotopes.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach is extended to include three-quasineutron and five-quasiparticle configurations, enabling investigation of high-spin states up to and including the second band crossing in odd-neutron nuclei. The authors show that the character of the band crossing along the yrast line changes with shell filling of the 1h11/2 orbital, and that the three-quasiparticle state first crosses the gamma band based on the ground-state configuration at an earlier spin value than it crosses the ground-state band itself.

## Method

The TPSM basis space was expanded to include three-quasineutron and five-quasiparticle (three-neutron coupled to two-proton) configurations in addition to the previous one-neutron and one-neutron coupled to two-proton states. Angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle states were constructed from a triaxially deformed Nilsson potential, and the shell model Hamiltonian (containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing terms) was diagonalized using the Hill-Wheeler approach. Calculations were performed for eight odd-mass Xe isotopes (117-131Xe) using axial and triaxial deformation parameters adopted from earlier studies.

## Key numbers

- For 117Xe, the ground-state band is projected from a one-quasineutron configuration with K=3/2 and intrinsic energy of 1.25 MeV; the gamma and gamma-gamma bands built on this state have band heads at excitation energies of 1.01 MeV and 2.10 MeV, respectively.
- The three-quasiparticle band with K=3/2 configuration crosses the ground-state band at I=33/2 for 117Xe.
- For 119Xe, the band crossing at I=33/2 is due to alignment of neutrons rather than protons as for 117Xe.
- For 117Xe, the deviation between experimental and calculated energy for the highest observed spin state is about 0.15 MeV.
- The quadrupole pairing force strength G_Q is fixed as 0.16 times the monopole pairing strength G_M.
- Standard effective charges of 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons were used.
- Deformation parameters range from epsilon=0.234 (117Xe) to epsilon=0.150 (127Xe, 129Xe) and gamma from 25 degrees (117Xe) to 33 degrees (127Xe).

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach previously applied to even-even nuclei (Sheikh et al., 2018), odd-odd nuclei (Jehangir et al., 2020), and odd-proton systems (Jehangir et al., 2021). The axial version of the projected shell model has also been generalized to multi-quasiparticle states by other groups (PhysRevC.90.011303, PhysRevC.93.034322, PhysRevC.97.044302). The extended basis enables investigation of the interplay between neutron and proton alignments in odd-neutron Xe isotopes, where experimental data from groups studying 123Xe, 125Xe, and 127Xe have shown that the character of band crossing changes from proton to neutron with increasing mass number. The calculations reproduce observed band structures and predict that gamma bands built on three-quasiparticle states become favoured at high spin, providing guidance for future experimental identification of three-quasiparticle band structures.
