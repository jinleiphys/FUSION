# Conference Summary of QNP2018

> This report is the summary of the Eighth International Conference on Quarks and Nuclear Physics (QNP2018). Hadron and nuclear physics is the field to investigate high-density quantum many-body systems bound by strong interactions. It is intended to clarify matter generation of universe and properties of quark-hadron many-body systems. The QNP is an international conference which covers a wide range of hadron and nuclear physics, including quark and gluon structure of hadrons, hadron spectroscopy, hadron interactions and nuclear structure, hot and cold dense matter, and experimental facilities. First, I introduce the current status of the hadron and nuclear physics field related to this conference. Next, the organization of the conference is explained, and a brief overview of major recent developments is discussed by selecting topics from discussions at the plenary sessions. They include rapidly-developing field of gravitational waves and nuclear physics, hadron interactions and nuclear structure with strangeness, lattice QCD, hadron spectroscopy, nucleon structure, heavy-ion physics, hadrons in nuclear medium, and experimental facilities of EIC, GSI-FAIR, JLab, J-PARC, Super-KEKB, and others. Nuclear physics is at a fortunate time to push various projects at these facilities. However, we should note that the projects need to be developed together with related studies in other fields such as gravitational physics, astrophysics, condensed-matter physics, particle physics, and fundamental quantum physics.

## Key numbers
- 216 total participants at QNP2018 (45 students, 44 postdocs, 127 staff), with 89 young scientists (students and postdocs) among them
- 24 plenary talks, 128 parallel session talks (A:27, B:36, C:27, D:38), and 40 posters (A:6, B:9, C:7, D:16, other:2)
- Participants from 5 continents: Africa 1, Asia 162, Europe 32, North America 16, South America 5
- Energy spacing between spin-doublet states of $^4_\Lambda$He($1^+$, $0^+$) determined to be $1406\pm 2\pm 2$ keV, indicating charge-symmetry breaking in hypernuclei
- Binding energy $B_{\Xi^-}=4.38 \pm 0.25$ MeV in the $\Xi^-$-$^{14}$N system, showing $\Xi N$ interaction is attractive
- Super-KEKB peak luminosity $8 \times 10^{35} \text{cm}^{-2} \text{s}^{-1}$, 40 times larger than the KEKB collider
- JLab completed upgrade to beam energy of 12 GeV
- EIC center-of-mass energies span 22$-$63 GeV for polarized $ep$ collisions and 15$-$40 GeV for $eA$ collisions (or 45$-$141 GeV and 32$-$90 GeV for alternative design)
- Nuclear modification plateau ($\sigma_{^{56}\text{Fe}}/\sigma_{\text{D}}$) observed at $x=1.4 - 1.9$ for iron, indicating two-nucleon correlations
- GSI-FAIR will accelerate protons up to 29 GeV and heavy ions up to 11 GeV/u

## Context
This conference summary covers the QNP2018 meeting held in Tsukuba, Japan on November 13-17, 2018, continuing the series of QNP conferences previously held in Adelaide (2000), Juelich (2002), Bloomington (2004), Madrid (2006), Beijing (2009), Palaiseau (2012), and Valparaiso (2015). The paper reviews recent developments including the impact of gravitational wave observations (GW170817) on nuclear physics, lattice QCD predictions for baryon-baryon interactions beyond existing phenomenological models, and progress on exotic hadron candidates from BaBar, BES, Belle, and LHCb. The summary enables researchers to understand the current status and future directions of the field, particularly highlighting the need for interdisciplinary connections with gravitational physics, astrophysics, condensed-matter physics, particle physics, and fundamental quantum physics.
