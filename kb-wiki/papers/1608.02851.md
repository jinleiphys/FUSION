# Light cluster production at NICA

> Light cluster production at the NICA accelerator complex offers unique possibilities to use these states as "rare probes" of in-medium characteristics such as phase space occupation and early flow. In order to explain this statement, in this contribution theoretical considerations from the nuclear statistical equilibrium model and from a quantum statistical model of cluster production are supplemented with a discussion of a transport model for light cluster formation and with results from hydrodynamic simulations combined with the coalescence model.

## Key claim

Light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles) produced at the NICA accelerator complex can serve as "rare probes" of in-medium characteristics such as phase space occupation and early flow, but only if the simple law of mass action (LMA) description is tested against medium-modified approaches and transport models, because at NICA energies the conditions are close to Mott lines where bound states begin to dissolve.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares four theoretical approaches for light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions: the nuclear statistical equilibrium (law of mass action) model with pointlike and excluded-volume corrections; a quantum statistical model based on Green's function techniques that includes mean-field effects and Pauli blocking; transport models (time-dependent Green's function formalism and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics) that implement in-medium cross sections and cluster formation; and three-fluid hydrodynamics combined with a coalescence model for fragment formation. These approaches are applied to the NICA energy range (2-8 A GeV in fixed-target mode) for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out curve parametrization: T/GeV = 0.166 - 0.139 (mu_B/GeV)^2 - 0.053 (mu_B/GeV)^4, with mu_B/GeV = 1.308/(1 + 0.273 sqrt(s_NN)/GeV), where m_N = 0.939 GeV
- Cluster binding energies (in MeV): d = -2.22, t = -8.48, h (He-3) = -7.72, alpha = -28.3
- Hard-core radii: r_i = 1.1 A_i^(1/3) fm for clusters; pion radius r_pi = 0.8 fm; Delta baryon radius r_Delta = 0.6 fm; pion mass m_pi = 137 MeV; Delta mass m_Delta = 1232 MeV
- d/p ratio fit formula: d/p = 0.8 [sqrt(s_NN)/GeV]^(-1.55) + 0.0036
- Three-fluid hydrodynamics coalescence model originally fitted at incident energy 0.8 A GeV, extended to higher energies with a single scaling coefficient C_coal
- Directed flow of protons and deuterons shows antiflow (negative slope) for sqrt(s_NN) ~ 6-7 GeV only when a two-phase equation of state with first-order phase transition is used, not for crossover or purely hadronic equations of state

## Context

This contribution extends the line of work on light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions from lower-energy experiments at GANIL (Xe+Sn at 50 MeV/u) and GSI/FOPI to the NICA energy range. It builds on the statistical model fits of hadron production by Andronic (2010, 2011) and Cleymans (2011), the freeze-out parametrization of Begun (2012), and the Mott dissociation calculations of Schuck (1999) and Roepke (1983). The paper enables future NICA experiments at BM@N and MPD to test whether the simple law of mass action describes cluster yields or whether medium modifications (Pauli blocking, self-energy effects, Mott dissociation) are required, and to use cluster flow anisotropies as more sensitive probes of the dense-matter equation of state and the possible deconfinement phase transition than single-nucleon observables.
