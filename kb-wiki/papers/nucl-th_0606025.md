# Theoretical study of one-proton removal from 15O

> One-proton removal from 15O at intermediate energies (56 A MeV) is studied in the eikonal approximation of the Glauber model. The production of the 14N core fragment in the ground and excited states is regarded. The calculated proton removal cross section, the 15O interaction cross section, and the longitudinal momentum distribution of the 14N fragments are compared to recent experimental data [1]. [1] H. Jeppesen, R. Kanungo, B. Abu-Ibrahim it et al, Nucl. Phys. A 739, 57 (2004).

## Key claim

The one-proton removal cross section and longitudinal momentum distribution of 14N fragments from 15O at 56 A MeV on a Be target can be reproduced by the eikonal approximation of the Glauber model, but only when the production of the 14N core in its ground and excited states is included; the calculated total cross section of 95.9 mb and FWHM of 191 MeV/c agree with the experimental values of 80 ± 20 mb and 190 ± 10 MeV/c, respectively, but the theoretical curves must be shifted by 10 MeV/c to the left to match the data.

## Method

The authors used the eikonal approximation of the Glauber model with a core+proton (14N+p) description of 15O, where the 14N core can be in four bound states (ground state and excited states at 2.313, 3.948, and 7.029 MeV). The radial wave function of the core-proton relative motion was obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential fitted to reproduce the proton separation energy for each 14N state, and the profile functions for fragment-target interactions were generated from the free nucleon-nucleon interaction potential using fitted density parameters for 9Be and 14N. The proton removal cross section was calculated as the sum of stripping and diffraction contributions, weighted by spectroscopic factors from Cohen and Kurath (1967) and Saha et al. (1989).

## Key numbers

- Total calculated one-proton removal cross section from 15O on a Be target at 56 A MeV: 95.9 mb, compared to experimental value 80 ± 20 mb (Jeppesen 2004)
- Calculated FWHM of the total longitudinal momentum distribution of 14N fragments: 191 MeV/c, compared to experimental value 190 ± 10 MeV/c (Jeppesen 2004)
- Calculated 15O interaction cross section on Be at 56 A MeV: 1091 mb
- Calculated 14N interaction cross section on Be at 56 A MeV: 1061 mb
- Single-particle proton removal cross sections for each 14N state: 29.7 mb (ground state, 1+,0), 25.9 mb (0+,1 at 2.313 MeV), 23.3 mb (1+,0 at 3.948 MeV), 20.4 mb (2+,0 at 7.029 MeV)
- Proton removal cross sections weighted by spectroscopic factors (Cohen and Kurath 1967): 43.3 mb (ground state), 10.8 mb (0+,1), 16.2 mb (1+,0 at 3.948 MeV), 25.6 mb (2+,0 at 7.029 MeV)
- FWHM values for each 14N state: 178 MeV/c (ground state), 191 MeV/c (0+,1), 198 MeV/c (1+,0 at 3.948 MeV), 209 MeV/c (2+,0 at 7.029 MeV)
- Calculated 15O rms matter radius: 2.48 fm, consistent with Ozawa (2001) and Warner (2005)
- Calculated 9Be+12C interaction cross section at 790 A MeV: 818.7 mb, compared to experimental 806 ± 9 mb (Ozawa 2001)
- Estimated contribution of proton removal from the 15O core in 17Ne at 66 A MeV: 51 mb, leading to a total calculated proton removal cross section for 17Ne of 161 mb, compared to experimental 168 ± 17 mb (Kanungo 2003)

## Context

This work extends the eikonal Glauber model approach, previously applied by Hencken (1996), Esbensen (2000), and Parfenova (2000), to the specific case of one-proton removal from 15O, providing a benchmark for calculations of the two-proton halo candidate 17Ne. The results directly address the discrepancy noted by Grigorenko (2005) between cluster model predictions for 17Ne and experimental data from Kanungo (2003), showing that proton removal from the 15O core contributes significantly (about 51 mb) and must be included to reproduce the measured total proton removal cross section of 168 ± 17 mb. The calculated cross sections and momentum distributions enable more precise evaluations of the core contribution in future studies of proton-rich nuclei near the drip line.
