# Solving one-dimensional penetration problem for fission channel in the statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory

> We solve the Schrödinger equation for an arbitrary one-dimensional potential energy to calculate the transmission coefficient in the fission channel of compound nucleus reactions. We incorporate the calculated transmission coefficients into the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculation for neutron-induced reactions on $^{235,238}$U and $^{239}$Pu. The one-dimensional model reproduces the evaluated fission cross section data reasonably well considering the limited number of model parameters involved. A resonance-like structure appears in the transmission coefficient for a double-humped fission barrier shape that includes an intermediate well, which is understood to be a quantum mechanical effect in the fission channel. The calculated fission cross sections for the neutron-induced reactions on $^{235,238}$U and $^{239}$Pu all exhibit a similar structure.

## Key claim

The authors solve the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation for an arbitrary fission barrier shape to calculate transmission coefficients, and incorporate these into Hauser-Feshbach calculations for neutron-induced fission on ²³⁵,²³⁸U and ²³⁹Pu, finding that the simple model reproduces evaluated fission cross sections "reasonably well considering the limited number of model parameters involved", a hedge that acknowledges the model is not a precise fit but captures gross features.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the 1-D Schrödinger equation for a fission barrier parameterized by smoothly concatenated parabolas (representing inner barrier, class-II well, and outer barrier), using the Numerov or Fox-Goodwin method to obtain the wave function and transmission coefficient. They then compute a lumped transmission coefficient by summing over excited states, applying an empirical energy-dependent compression factor to the excitation energies to account for deformation effects on the eigenstate spectrum. This lumped transmission coefficient is fed into the CoH₃ statistical Hauser-Feshbach code with coupled-channels optical model calculations for neutron-induced reactions on ²³⁵,²³⁸U and ²³⁹Pu.

## Key numbers

- For ²³⁸U: barrier heights of V₁=6.0 MeV (inner), V₂=0.5 MeV (class-II well depth), and V₃=5.0 MeV (outer) reproduce evaluated fission cross sections; a V₃=5.5 MeV case produces more resonance-like structure below 1 MeV
- For ²³⁵U: barrier heights of V₁=5.9 MeV and V₃=5.7 MeV give a reasonable fit; changing V₁ by ±100 keV produces the dashed-curve sensitivity band
- For ²³⁹Pu: barrier heights of V₁=5.9 MeV and V₃=5.7 MeV with compression parameter f₀=0.55 (reduced from 0.8 used for uranium) give reasonable agreement; the ±100 keV band in V₁ covers the evaluated cross sections
- The trajectory compression parameters used are f₀=0.8 and f₁=0.2 MeV⁻¹ for uranium cases; for ²³⁹Pu, f₀ was reduced to 0.55
- The imaginary potential strength tested in the class-II well was W₂=0.5 MeV, which reduced the asymptotic transmission coefficient to approximately 0.5

## Context

This work extends the exact solution approach of Cramer and Nix (1970), who solved the Schrödinger equation analytically for double-humped parabolic barriers using parabolic-cylinder functions, to arbitrary 1-D potential shapes via numerical integration. It contrasts with the traditional approach using the Hill-Wheeler WKB approximation (Hill 1953) combined with the effective transmission formula T_f = T_A T_B/(T_A + T_B), which ignores the class-II well and its quantum interference effects. The model enables improved fission cross section calculations in the Hauser-Feshbach framework without requiring the phenomenological class-II resonance corrections often employed in codes such as those described by Talou and Vogt (2023) and in the RIPL-3 reference library.
