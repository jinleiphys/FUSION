# f [N pi N]: from quarks to the pion derivative coupling

> We study the N pi N coupling, in the framework of a QCD-inspired confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. A simple relativistic confining and instantaneous quark model is reviewed. The Salpeter equation for the nucleon and the boosted pion is solved. The f [n pi n] and f[n pi Delta] couplings are calculated and they turn out to be reasonably good. The sensibility of f[n pi n] and f[n pi Delta] to confinement, chiral symmetry breaking and Lorentz invariance is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the $f_{N\pi N}$ and $f_{N\pi\Delta}$ couplings using a QCD-inspired confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, finding that the results are "surprisingly good" compared to experiment, but only when using a specific value of the confining potential strength $K_0 = 247\ \mathrm{MeV}$; a larger $K_0 \simeq 400\ \mathrm{MeV}$ needed to explain K-n exotic phase shifts would yield only $60\%$ of the experimental values.

## Method

The authors solve the Salpeter equation for the nucleon (a three-quark bound state) and the boosted pion (a quark-antiquark bound state) within an instantaneous, confining quark model with a harmonic confining potential. They compute the $N\pi N$ and $N\pi\Delta$ couplings from overlap diagrams involving quark-antiquark annihilation, expanding the pion Salpeter amplitude to first order in the pion momentum $\mathbf{P}$ and using a variational solution for the nucleon wave function with a Gaussian ansatz parameterized by an inverse baryon radius $\alpha$.

## Key numbers

- $f_{n\pi n} = 1.0$ and $f_{n\pi\Delta} \simeq 2.1$ are the experimental values quoted for comparison.
- Theoretical results using $K_0 = 247\ \mathrm{MeV}$ yield $f_{n\pi n} = 1.0$ and $f_{n\pi\Delta} = 1.8$.
- The adimensional spin-flavor amputated overlap amplitude is ${\cal O}_{fs} = 0.54$, with ${\cal O}'_{fs} \simeq 0$.
- The constant $a$ in the pion normalization equals $0.16\ K_0$.
- With $K_0 \simeq 400\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (needed for K-n scattering), the couplings would be $60\%$ of the values obtained with $K_0 = 247\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of hadronic scattering within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio framework, particularly their analysis of exotic K-n scattering (Bicudo and Ribeiro, earlier work) which favored a small bare nucleon core and a larger $K_0$. The calculation is a preliminary step toward a Lorentz-invariant treatment of the $N\pi N$ coupling, and the authors note that including covariance and coupled channels (as in Horvat, earlier work) could improve the pion decay constant $f_\pi$ and the hadronic decay widths. The results enable future microscopic calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering, including the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) in the static approximation.
