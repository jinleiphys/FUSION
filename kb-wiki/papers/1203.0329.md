# High transverse momentum quarkonium production and dissociation in heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the yields of quarkonia in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC as a function of their transverse momentum. Based upon non-relativistic quantum chromodynamics, our results include both color-singlet and color-octet contributions and feed-down effects from excited states. In reactions with ultra-relativistic nuclei, we focus on the consistent implementation of dynamically calculated nuclear matter effects, such as coherent power corrections, cold nuclear matter energy loss, and the Cronin effect in the initial state. In the final state, we consider radiative energy loss for the color-octet state and collisional dissociation of quarkonia as they traverse through the QGP. Theoretical results are presented for J/psi and Upsilon and compared to experimental data where applicable. At RHIC, a good description of the high-pT J/psi modification observed in central Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions can be achieved within the model uncertainties. We find that measurements of J/psi yields in proton-nucleus reactions are needed to constrain the magnitude of cold nuclear matter effects. At the LHC, a good description of the experimental data can be achieved only in mid-central and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions. The large five-fold suppression of prompt J/psi in the most central nuclear reactions may indicate for the first time possible thermal effects at the level of the quarkonium wavefunction at large transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent treatment of cold nuclear matter effects and final-state QGP interactions within the NRQCD framework can describe J/ψ suppression at RHIC and in mid-central/peripheral LHC collisions, but the large five-fold suppression of prompt J/ψ in the most central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC may indicate thermal effects on the quarkonium wavefunction at high transverse momentum that are not captured by the current model.

## Method

The authors calculate quarkonium yields using leading-order NRQCD including both color-singlet and color-octet contributions, with feed-down from excited states. They implement cold nuclear matter effects (coherent power corrections, initial-state energy loss, and the Cronin effect via transverse momentum broadening) and final-state effects including radiative energy loss for color-octet states and collisional dissociation of formed quarkonia via rate equations as they traverse the QGP. The nuclear modification factor R_AB is computed for J/ψ and Υ at RHIC (√S=0.2 TeV) and LHC (√S=2.76 TeV) and compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD formalism of Bodwin (1994) and Cho and Leibovich (1995, 1996) for quarkonium production to heavy ion collisions, combining it with cold nuclear matter effects previously applied to light hadrons by Vitev (2003, 2006) and open heavy flavor by Vitev (2006, 2008). The approach contrasts with equilibrium models (such as those by Rapp (2009) and Strickland (2011)) that assume thermalized quarkonia; instead, it treats high-pT quarkonia as propagating through the medium with vacuum wavefunctions and dissociating via collisions. The results enable future constraints on cold nuclear matter effects from p+A measurements and suggest that differential high-pT data for both J/ψ and Υ can distinguish between dynamical dissociation and thermal wavefunction modification scenarios.
