# Progress in the Glauber model at collider energies

> We review the theoretical and experimental progress in the Glauber model of multiple nucleon and/or parton scatterings, after the last 10--15 years of operation with proton and nuclear beams at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and with various light and heavy colliding ions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The main developments and the state-of-the-art of the field are summarized. These encompass measurements of the inclusive inelastic proton and nuclear cross sections, advances in the description of the proton and nuclear density profiles and their fluctuations, inclusion of subnucleonic degrees of freedom, experimental procedures and issues related to the determination of the collision centrality, validation of the binary scaling prescription for hard scattering cross sections, and constraints on transport properties of quark-gluon matter from varying initial-state conditions in relativistic hydrodynamics calculations. These advances confirm the validity and usefulness of the Glauber formalism for quantitative studies of QCD matter produced in high-energy collisions of systems, from protons to uranium nuclei, of vastly different size.

## Key claim

The Glauber formalism remains valid and useful for quantitative studies of QCD matter produced in high-energy collisions across systems of vastly different size, from protons to uranium nuclei, but only when incorporating recent developments such as event-by-event fluctuations, subnucleonic degrees of freedom, and improved density profiles that were not present in earlier formulations.

## Method

The authors review theoretical and experimental progress in the Glauber model over the past 10-15 years of operation at the LHC and RHIC, covering measurements of inclusive inelastic proton and nuclear cross sections, advances in describing proton and nuclear density profiles and their fluctuations, inclusion of subnucleonic degrees of freedom, experimental procedures for collision centrality determination, validation of binary scaling for hard scattering cross sections, and constraints on transport properties of quark-gluon matter from varying initial-state conditions in relativistic hydrodynamics calculations.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at top RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=0.2 TeV) is sigma_NN = 41.3 +/- 1.2 mb, consistent with the STAR measurement of 43.82^{+1.39}_{-1.46} mb
- At top LHC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), sigma_NN = 68.3 +/- 1.2 mb
- At top FCC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=100 TeV), sigma_NN = 107.5 +/- 6.5 mb, in agreement with 105.1 +/- 2.0 mb from model predictions
- The Glauber calculation gives sigma_PbPb = 7.55 +/- 0.15 b at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, in good agreement with the measured value of 7.7 +/- 0.6 b
- The Glauber calculation gives sigma_pPb = 2.09 +/- 0.03 b at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, in good agreement with measured values of 2.06 +/- 0.08 b and 2.10 +/- 0.07 b
- The fit to sigma_NN energy dependence yields parameters a=28.84+/-0.52, b=0.0458+/-0.0167, n=2.374+/-0.123 with chi^2/N_dof=0.7

## Context

This review extends the previous summary of the Glauber formalism at RHIC energies by Miller (2007), now incorporating developments driven by the LHC's TeV-scale collisions where nucleon substructure becomes more important and particle multiplicities in small systems (pp, pPb) can reach values as large as those in CuCu collisions at 200 GeV. The advances enable improved initial conditions for hydrodynamic calculations of quark-gluon plasma evolution, better determination of collision centrality, and more reliable extraction of medium transport properties such as shear viscosity over entropy density ratio.
