# Pomeron exchange and exclusive electroproduction of rho-mesons in QCD

> A Pomeron-exchange model of exclusive electroproduction of $ρ$-mesons is examined using a dressed-quark propagator. It is shown that by representing the photon-$ρ$-meson-Pomeron coupling by a nonperturbative, confined-quark loop, one obtains predictions for $ρ$-meson electroproduction that are in good agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Pomeron-exchange model of exclusive rho-meson electroproduction, using a confined-quark loop to represent the photon-rho-meson-Pomeron coupling, yields predictions in good agreement with experiment without needing an ad hoc quark-Pomeron form factor, contrary to earlier approaches.

## Method

The authors calculate the exclusive rho-meson electroproduction cross section using a field-theoretic model in Euclidean space, where the photon-rho-meson-Pomeron vertex is computed via a dressed-quark loop integral. They employ a dressed-quark propagator from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (with no Lehmann representation, interpreted as confinement), a Ball-Chiu photon-quark vertex satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity, and a Gaussian Ansatz for the rho-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The Pomeron-nucleon coupling and Pomeron propagator are taken from the Donnachie-Landshoff model (1984, 1987), with parameters epsilon=0.08, alpha'=0.25 GeV^-2, beta_0=2.0 GeV^2, and the isoscalar nucleon form factor F_1(t).

## Key numbers

- The dressed-quark propagator parameters: bare quark mass m = 6.7 MeV, momentum scale lambda = 0.568 GeV; the rho-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude range parameter Lambda = 0.495 GeV (fixed by reproducing the rho -> e+ e- decay width).
- The Pomeron parameters used: epsilon = 0.08, alpha' = 0.25 GeV^-2, beta_0 = 2.0 GeV^2.
- The model predictions are compared with data in the energy range 5.5 < W < 16 GeV for electroproduction (Fig. 2) and 60 < W < 80 GeV for photoproduction (Fig. 3), at q^2 values from 0 to several GeV^2.
- The simplified dressed-quark propagator test used M = 0.5 GeV and Lambda = 0.465 GeV to reproduce the rho decay width.

## Context

This work extends the Donnachie-Landshoff Pomeron-exchange model (1984, 1987) by replacing the free constituent-quark propagator and on-shell approximation for the rho-meson with a confined-quark propagator and finite-width Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Roberts and collaborators, 1994). The model successfully describes data from the CHIO, EMC, NMC, and ZEUS Collaborations without a quark-Pomeron form factor, suggesting that such form factors in earlier work effectively modeled the quark substructure of the vertex. The approach enables further studies of exclusive vector-meson production and the role of confinement in diffractive processes.
