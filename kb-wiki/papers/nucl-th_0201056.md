# Proton-neutron quadrupole interactions: an effective contribution to the pairing field

> We point out that the proton-neutron energy contribution, for low multipoles (in particular for the quadrupole component), effectively renormalizes the strength of the pairing interaction acting amongst identical nucleons filling up a single-j or a set of degenerate many-j shells. We carry out the calculation in lowest-order perturbation theory. We perform a study of this correction in various mass regions. These results may have implications for the use of pairing theory in medium-heavy nuclei and for the study of pairing energy corrections to the liquid drop model when studying nuclear masses.

# Proton-neutron quadrupole interactions: an effective contribution to the pairing field

## Key claim

The proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, treated in lowest-order perturbation theory, renormalizes the strength of the monopole pairing force acting amongst identical nucleons, but only in regions near closed shells where the proton-neutron energy contribution is not the dominant part and where pairing dominates over the quadrupole interaction.

## Method

The authors start from a monopole pairing Hamiltonian for identical nucleons in a single-j shell (or degenerate many-j shells) and add a proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. They use first-order perturbation theory to compute the mixing of the seniority-zero ground state with states containing one broken proton pair and one broken neutron pair each coupled to J=2. The resulting energy correction is evaluated analytically using harmonic-oscillator wave functions and reduced matrix elements, yielding a closed-form expression for the total binding energy that takes the same functional form as the pure pairing energy but with a renormalized coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- The monopole pairing strength G for medium-mass and heavy nuclei is approximately 25/A MeV, ranging from G=19/A MeV for regions Z~40-50 to G=30/A MeV for Pb nuclei (Kisslinger and Sorensen, 1960).
- For 52Te isotopes (A≈106-152), the pairing strength is G≈0.15 MeV; for 42Mo isotopes (A≈84-128), G≈0.18 MeV.
- The energy denominator ΔE_α = -E(2+_π) - E(2+_ν) is approximately -2 MeV for the Z~50 region and approximately -2.5 MeV for the Z~40 region.
- The effective pairing correction C̅ from the proton-neutron quadrupole force is smaller than the monopole pairing constant G by a factor varying between 5 and 25, corresponding to a renormalization of 10-15% of the regular monopole pairing strength at maximum.

## Context

This work extends the study of local correlation effects in nuclear binding energies and two-neutron separation energies S2n, following earlier work by Fossion et al. (2001) and Schwarz et al. (2001) that used the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) to derive nuclear masses. The paper provides a microscopic justification for why the proton-neutron quadrupole interaction can be absorbed into an effective pairing force, which is relevant for applying pairing theory to lowest-order broken-pair excitations in medium-heavy nuclei and for improving the description of local binding energy variations on top of the liquid-drop model. The authors note that in open shells with both valence protons and neutrons active, both monopole pairing and low-multipole proton-neutron components must be treated on equal footing rather than through this renormalization.
