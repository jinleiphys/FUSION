# Short-range correlation physics at low RG resolution

> Recent experiments have succeeded in isolating processes for which short-range correlation (SRC) physics is dominant and well accounted for by SRC phenomenology. But an alternative and compelling picture emerges from renormalization group (RG) evolution to low RG resolution. At high RG resolution, SRCs are identified as components in the nuclear wave function with relative pair momenta greater than the Fermi momentum. Scale separation results in wave-function factorization that can be exploited with phenomenologies such as the generalized contact formalism or the low-order correlation operator approximation. Evolution to lower resolution shifts SRC physics from nuclear structure to the reaction operators without changing the measured observables. We show how the features of SRC phenomenology manifested at high RG resolution are cleanly identified at low RG resolution using simple two-body operators and local-density approximations with uncorrelated wave functions, all of which can be systematically generalized. We verify that the experimental consequences to date follow directly at low resolution from well-established properties of nucleon-nucleon interactions such as the tensor force. Thus the RG reconciles the contrasting pictures of the same experiment and shows how to get correct results using wave functions without SRC components. Our demonstration has implications for the analysis of knock-out reactions for which SRC physics is not cleanly isolated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that short-range correlation (SRC) physics, which is conventionally described using high-resolution wave functions with high-momentum components, can be equivalently reproduced at low RG resolution using simple two-body operators and uncorrelated wave functions, because RG evolution shifts SRC physics from nuclear structure to reaction operators without changing observables.

## Method

The authors apply unitary similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to transform high-resolution Hamiltonians (such as Argonne V18) to low-resolution forms, simultaneously evolving both the Hamiltonian and the reaction operators. They then demonstrate that at low RG resolution, the matrix elements of high-momentum operators factorize into a state-independent high-momentum part and a nucleus-dependent low-momentum part, enabling calculations using uncorrelated wave functions and local-density approximations. The approach is validated by comparing results from the phenomenological AV18 potential and a chiral EFT potential from Gezerlis et al. (2014).

## Key numbers

- The SRG resolution scale λ is taken to be of order kF or slightly higher, providing a dividing scale between mean-field and high-momentum physics.
- The ratio of |U(k0,q)U†(q,k0)| for coupled 3S1 to 1S0 channels shows dominance of the tensor-force channel around q = 2 fm⁻¹ (400 MeV), decaying to a combinatoric fraction at high momentum.
- For the deuteron momentum distribution at q = 2, 3, and 4 fm⁻¹, low-resolution results (λ = 1.35 fm⁻¹) are heavily dominated by the 3S1–3S1 channel for both AV18 and Gezerlis N2LO potentials.
- The factorization approximation is typically good at the 10–15% level with calculable corrections.

## Context

This work extends the program of applying renormalization group methods to nuclear physics, building on earlier RG studies by Anderson (2010), Bogner (2012), and Furnstahl (2013) that discussed contrasting high- and low-resolution pictures of SRC physics. The paper reconciles the high-resolution SRC phenomenology (embodied in the Generalized Contact Formalism of Weiss et al. and the low-order correlation operator approximation of Ryckebusch et al.) with low-resolution descriptions such as the shell model and ab initio methods using soft interactions. The results enable simplified calculations of knockout reactions using uncorrelated wave functions and local-density approximations, with implications for analyzing experiments where SRC physics is not cleanly isolated.
