# Fluctuations In ``BR-Scaled'' Chiral Lagrangians

> We develop arguments for "mapping" the effective chiral Lagrangian whose parameters are given by "BR scaling" to a Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory for nuclear matter in describing fluctuations in various flavor (e.g., strangeness) directions. We use for this purpose the effective Lagrangian used by Furnstahl, Tang and Serot that incorporates the trace anomaly of QCD in terms of a light-quark (quarkonium) degree of freedom with the heavy (gluonium) degree of freedom integrated out. The large anomalous dimension $d_{an}\approx 5/3$ for the scalar field found by Furnstahl et al to be needed for a correct description of nuclear matter is interpreted as an indication for a strong-coupling regime and the ground state given by the BR-scaled parameters is suggested as the background around which fluctuations can be rendered weak so that mean-field approximation is reliable. We construct a simple model with BR scaled parameters that provides a satisfactory description of the properties of matter at normal nuclear matter density. Given this, fluctuations around the BR scaled background are dominated by tree diagrams. Our reasoning relies heavily on recent developments in the study of nucleon and kaon properties in normal and dense nuclear matter, e.g., nucleon and kaon flows in heavy-ion processes, kaonic atoms, and kaon condensation in dense compact-star matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ground state of nuclear matter given by BR-scaled parameters can serve as a background around which fluctuations become weak enough that mean-field approximation is reliable, provided the large anomalous dimension \(d_{an}\approx 5/3\) for the scalar field is interpreted as an indication of a strong-coupling regime that requires redefining the vacuum.

## Method

The authors use the effective chiral Lagrangian of Furnstahl, Tang and Serot (FTS1) that incorporates the trace anomaly of QCD with a light quarkonium scalar field and a heavy gluonium scalar field integrated out. They analyze the dependence of nuclear matter properties on the anomalous dimension \(d_{an}=d-1\) of the scalar field, and then construct a hybrid model where the ground state is given by mean-field FTS1 while fluctuations around it are described by a BR-scaled chiral Lagrangian. The mapping between BR-scaled parameters and Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point parameters is developed using renormalization group flow arguments from condensed matter physics.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to nuclear matter and finite nuclei properties uses scalar scale dimension \(d=2.7\), corresponding to anomalous dimension \(d_{an}\approx 1.7\) (i.e., \(5/3\))
- For \(d=2.7\), the compression modulus \(K=196\) MeV, equilibrium Fermi momentum \(k_{eq}=257\) MeV, and binding energy \(B=16.4\) MeV
- For \(d=2.6\), \(K=309\) MeV, \(k_{eq}=279\) MeV, \(B=20.4\) MeV; for \(d=2.8\), \(K=184\) MeV, \(k_{eq}=241\) MeV, \(B=14.0\) MeV
- The scalar mass in free space \(m_S\sim 700\) MeV; the scalar vacuum expectation value \(S_0=90.6\) MeV for the T1 parameter set
- The T1 parameters include: \(g_s^2=99.3\), \(m_S=509\) MeV, \(g_V^2=154.5\), \(\xi=0.0402\), \(\eta=-0.496\)

## Context

This work extends the "BR conjecture" of Brown and Rho (1991) by connecting the FTS1 mean-field model of Furnstahl, Tang and Serot (1995) to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point theory as initiated by Friman and Rho (1996). The paper aims to provide a unified framework where the ground state of nuclear matter and fluctuations in flavor directions (such as strangeness for kaon condensation in compact stars) are treated consistently, addressing a defect in prior works on kaon-nuclear interactions and kaon condensation. The approach enables extrapolation from normal nuclear matter density to extreme conditions probed in heavy-ion collisions and compact-star matter.
