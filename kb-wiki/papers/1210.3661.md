# Tsallis Fits to p_T Spectra for pp Collisions at LHC

> Phenomenological Tsallis fits to the CMS and ATLAS transverse spectra of charged particles were found to extend for p_T from 0.5 to 181 GeV in pp collisions at LHC at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV, and for p_T from 0.5 to 31 GeV at \sqrt{s}=0.9 TeV. The simplicity of the Tsallis parametrization and the large range of the fitting transverse momentum raise questions on the physical meaning of the degrees of freedom that enter into the Tsallis distribution or q-statistics.

# Tsallis Fits to p_T Spectra for pp Collisions at LHC

## Key claim
The Tsallis distribution provides an excellent phenomenological fit to charged-particle transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions at LHC energies from p_T = 0.5 GeV up to 181 GeV at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV and up to 31 GeV at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, with the surprising result that the same three-parameter fit (overall magnitude, n, and T) works without deviation across the entire measured range. The authors raise the question of whether this success reflects a genuine statistical-thermodynamic origin or instead points to specific dynamical mechanisms, leaving the physical meaning of the Tsallis q parameter as an open issue.

## Method
The authors performed phenomenological fits using a Tsallis distribution in the form E d^3N/dp^3 = C (dN/dy) (1 + E_T/(nT))^{-n}, where E_T = sqrt(m^2 + p_T^2) - m, with pion mass m_pi = 0.14 GeV assumed. They numerically integrated over pseudorapidity windows (|eta| <= 2.4 for CMS, |eta| <= 2.5 for ATLAS) to obtain the averaged invariant yields, then fitted the CMS and ATLAS data for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV and 0.9 TeV using the parameters A = C dN/dy, n, and T.

## Key numbers
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the Tsallis fit to CMS data covers p_T from 0.5 GeV to 181 GeV with parameters A = 4.06, n = 6.6, and T = 0.147 GeV.
- At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, the fit to CMS data covers p_T from 0.5 GeV to 31 GeV with parameters A = 4.01, n = 7.65, and T = 0.128 GeV.
- The ATLAS data at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV extend from p_T = 0.5 GeV to 36 GeV and are consistent with the CMS data and the same Tsallis fit.
- The power index n decreases from 7.65 at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV to 6.6 at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, still far from the naive point-like counting rule value of n = 4.

## Context
This work extends earlier Tsallis fits to CMS data that had been limited to p_T up to 6 GeV (CMS Collaboration, 2011) and demonstrates that the same parametrization holds to much higher transverse momenta. The Tsallis distribution is equivalent to the QCD-inspired Hagedorn formula (Hagedorn, 1965; also earlier by Cline and Martin, 1973, and UA1 Collaboration) with n = 1/(q-1) and p_0 = T/(q-1). The observed power index n ~ 6.6-7.6 is discussed in relation to the constituent interchange model of Blankenbecler, Brodsky, and Gunion (1975), where a q+meson -> q+meson basic process would give n = 8, close to the observed values. The results enable further investigation into whether the Tsallis parametrization reflects nonextensive thermodynamics (as developed by Tsallis, 1988; Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2007; and others) or requires a dynamical explanation beyond statistical models.
