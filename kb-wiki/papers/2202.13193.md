# Transient Joule- and (ac) Josephson-like photon emission in one- and two- nucleon tunneling processes between superfluid nuclei: blackbody and coherent spectral functions

> Effective charged neutrons involved in one- and two- nucleon tunneling processes in heavy ion collisions between superfluid nuclei are expected to emit photons. Although the centroid, width and integrated energy area characterizing the associated gamma-strength functions are rather similar, the corresponding line shapes reflect the thermal equilibrated-like character of the quasiparticle transfer (1n-channel, blackbody spectral functional dependence), and the quantal coherent character of the Cooper pair transfer (2n-channel, Gaussian functional dependence) respectively. The predicted angular distributions, polarizations and analyzing power provide further insight into the profoundly different physics to be found at the basis of what can be considered a transient Joule-like and a (ac) Josephson-like nuclear processes

## Key claim
The gamma-strength functions for one- and two-nucleon tunneling between superfluid nuclei have similar centroid, width, and integrated area, but their line shapes are fundamentally different: the 1n-channel follows a blackbody spectral functional dependence (thermal, incoherent quasiparticle contributions) while the 2n-channel follows a Gaussian functional dependence (coherent Cooper pair transfer), reflecting transient Joule-like versus (ac) Josephson-like nuclear processes.

## Method
The authors calculated absolute double differential cross sections for gamma emission in one- and two-neutron transfer reactions between superfluid nuclei (116Sn+60Ni) at bombarding energies from above to well below the Coulomb barrier, using a T-matrix formalism that includes the dipole operator with effective neutron charges. For the 2n-channel, they computed a single coherent transition between BCS ground states; for the 1n-channel, they summed incoherent quasiparticle contributions (eleven total, four most important) with proper angular momentum coupling. They fitted the resulting gamma-strength functions with blackbody spectral radiance and Gaussian functions, and computed angular distributions, polarizations, and analyzing power.

## Key numbers
- Q-value for 2n transfer (116Sn+60Ni → 114Sn+62Ni): Q_{2n} = 1.307 MeV
- Q-value for 1n transfer (116Sn+60Ni → 115Sn+61Ni): Q_{1n} = -1.74 MeV
- Collision time: τ_{coll} ≈ 0.5 × 10^{-21} s
- Mean square radius (correlation length) of transferred Cooper pair: ξ ≈ 13.5 fm
- Barrier width: d ≈ 2.24 fm
- Center-of-mass energy: E_{cm} = 154.26 MeV; center-of-mass angle: θ_{cm} = 140°
- Chi-squared for blackbody fit to 1n gamma-strength function: χ²_{bb} = 3.7 × 10^{-3}
- Chi-squared for Gaussian fit to 2n gamma-strength function: χ²_G = 1.8 × 10^{-3}
- Effective neutron charge for 2n transfer: 2 × e_{eff} = -e × 0.89
- Characteristic temperature from fitting 1n gamma-strength function: T_{char} ≈ 0.69 MeV; average over four estimation methods: 0.74 ± 0.16 MeV
- Most probable photon energy for Cooper pair tunneling: 4 MeV; associated photon momentum: k_γ ≈ 0.02 fm^{-1} (negligible compared to k_i ≈ 1.76 fm^{-1})
- Experimental 1n transfer cross section: 7.25 mb/sr; theoretical sum renormalization factor: 1.2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions in heavy ion collisions between superfluid nuclei reported by Montanari (2014, 2016), and builds on the theoretical framework of Potel (2021) and Broglia (2021) for Josephson-like nuclear junctions. The predicted gamma-ray signatures provide a nuclear-physics analogue to condensed-matter Josephson junction phenomena (Josephson, 1962; Anderson, 1964) and Giaever tunneling (Giaever, 1973), and the authors suggest these predictions could be tested with modern 4π gamma-detector arrays, potentially providing microscopic insight into BCS superconductivity down to condensates of few (3-6) Cooper pairs.
