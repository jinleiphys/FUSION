# Exotic baryons with charm number $\pm1$ from Skyrme model

> We illustrate the exotic SU(3) baryon sub-multiplets in the SU(4) baryon multiplets predicted from the flavor SU(4) collective-coordinate quantization, and investigate the exotic states with charm number $C=\pm1$ up to leading order of $1/N_c$ under chiral $SU(3)_L\times SU(3)_R$ symmetry and the heavy quark limit from bound state approach. We find that there exist the $\bar{15}$-plets and 24-plets with C=1 and the $\bar{6}$-plets, 15-plets and $15^{\prime}$-plets with $C=-1$ bounded in this approach, qualitatively consistent with predictions from the flavor SU(4) collective-coordinate quantization. By fitting one unique parameter of leading SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking term with the mass difference between $Ξ_c$ and $Σ_c$ and up to $O(m_Q^0N_c^{-1})$, we give all the average masses of baryons in $\bar{6}$-plet and 15-plet. Several general relations of these masses without any parameter are also introduced.

# Exotic baryons with charm number $\pm1$ from Skyrme model

## Key claim

The authors claim that exotic baryon multiplets with charm number $C = \pm 1$ exist as bound states in the Skyrme model, but this result is obtained only up to leading order in $1/N_c$ and under the heavy quark limit ($m_Q \rightarrow \infty$), with the caveat that the mass predictions are qualitative rather than quantitative because the symmetry breaking treatment does not agree well with experiments in the light baryon sector.

## Method

The authors generalize the Callan-Klebanov bound state approach to three light flavors under chiral $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ symmetry and the heavy quark limit, treating heavy baryons as bound states of $SU(3)$ solitons and heavy mesons (or anti-heavy mesons). They compute binding energies from the interaction Hamiltonian up to leading order in $1/N_c$, then perform collective coordinate quantization of the chiral $SU(3)$ Lagrangian to obtain mass spectra including $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry breaking effects. A single parameter for the leading symmetry breaking term is fitted using the mass difference between $\Xi_c$ and $\Sigma_c$.

## Key numbers

- The average mass of $\Theta_c^0$ (with quark content $uudd\bar{c}$, $C = -1$) is estimated as 2704 MeV, using $gF^\prime(0) = 419$ MeV fitted from the $\Lambda_c$ mass and $m_H \equiv (3m_{D^*} + m_D)/4 = 1973$ MeV
- The average mass of $\Theta_c^*$ (with $I=1$, $s_l=1$) is estimated as 2802 MeV, including the $\Delta M$ mass difference between nucleon and $\Delta$
- The mass difference between $\Xi_c$ and $\Sigma_c$ used to fix the symmetry breaking parameter is $\Delta m = \bar{m}_{\Xi_c} - \bar{m}_{\Sigma_c} = 89$ MeV
- Predicted masses for $\bar{6}$-plet baryons: $N_{\bar{c}}$ at 2882 MeV, $\Sigma_{\bar{c}}$ at 3060 MeV
- Predicted masses for 15-plet baryons: $\Delta_{\bar{c}}$ at 3049 MeV, $N_{\bar{c}}^*$ at 2901 MeV, $\Sigma_{\bar{c}}^*$ at 3099 MeV, $\Lambda_{\bar{c}}$ at 3000 MeV, $\Xi_{\bar{c}}$ at 3148 MeV

## Context

This work extends the bound state approach of Callan and Klebanov (1985) and the subsequent heavy baryon treatments by Jenkins, Manohar, and collaborators (1993) and by Rho, Riska, and collaborators (1992) to include exotic states with charm number $\pm 1$ in the three-light-flavor case. The results are qualitatively consistent with predictions from the flavor $SU(4)$ collective-coordinate quantization of Walliser (1992) and the $SU(4)$ baryon multiplet analysis of Riska, Grigoryan, and collaborators. The paper enables future experimental searches for pentaquark states such as $\Theta_c^0$ and provides mass relations that can be tested against lattice QCD or future experimental data, though the authors caution that the quantitative predictions are limited by the treatment of $SU(3)$ symmetry breaking.
