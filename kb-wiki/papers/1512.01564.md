# SMWDs as SGRs/AXPs and the lepton number violation

> Possible nature of strongly magnetized white dwarfs (SMWDs) is studied. It is shown that for relatively low values of the equatorial surface magnetic field $B\,\sim\,10^9\,-\,10^{11}$ G they can be good candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars (SGRs/AXPs). For the case of iron SMWDs the influence of a neutrinoless electron to positron conversion on the SGRs/AXPs luminosity is estimated.

## Key claim

Strongly magnetized white dwarfs (SMWDs) with equatorial surface magnetic fields in the range of 10^9 to 10^11 G can serve as alternative candidates for soft gamma-ray repeaters and anomalous X-ray pulsars (SGRs/AXPs), but the neutrinoless electron-to-positron conversion process in iron SMWDs cannot produce enough energy to sizeably influence the observed luminosities if the effective Majorana neutrino mass is at most 0.8 eV.

## Method

The authors applied the pulsar spin-down model to three low-magnetic-field compact objects (SGR 0418+579, Swift J1822.6-1606, and 3XMM J185246.6+003317), calculating the rotational energy loss rate for three scenarios: neutron star (NS), standard white dwarf (WD), and strongly magnetized iron white dwarf (SMIWD) models. For the SMIWD case, they used masses of 2 solar masses and radii of 423 km and 186 km from their earlier work (Adam et al., 2015). They then compared the calculated spin-down luminosities with observed X-ray luminosities and separately estimated the luminosity contribution from the neutrinoless double charge exchange reaction e- + Fe-56 -> Cr-56 + e+ for two values of the effective Majorana neutrino mass.

## Key numbers

- For SGR 0418+579: observed X-ray luminosity L_X = 3.6 x 10^30 erg s^-1; SMIWD spin-down luminosity for R=423 km is 1.91 x 10^32 erg s^-1 and for R=186 km is 3.7 x 10^31 erg s^-1; neutron star spin-down luminosity is 7.5 x 10^28 erg s^-1 (too low to explain L_X)
- For Swift J1822.6-1606: observed X-ray luminosity L_X = 6.4 x 10^31 erg s^-1 (at distance 1.6 kpc); SMIWD spin-down luminosity for R=423 km is 5.0 x 10^33 erg s^-1 and for R=186 km is 9.6 x 10^32 erg s^-1
- For 3XMM J185246.6+003317: observed X-ray luminosity L_X < 4.0 x 10^33 erg s^-1 (at 7.1 kpc); SMIWD spin-down luminosity for R=423 km is < 3.2 x 10^33 erg s^-1 and for R=186 km is < 6.3 x 10^32 erg s^-1
- For the double charge exchange reaction at Fermi energy 20 m_e: (Delta L)_0.4 = 6.07 x 10^18 erg s^-1 for |<m_nu>| = 0.4 eV; (Delta L)_0.8 = 2.42 x 10^19 erg s^-1 for |<m_nu>| = 0.8 eV
- For the double charge exchange reaction at Fermi energy 46 m_e: (Delta L)_0.4 = 7.64 x 10^21 erg s^-1 for |<m_nu>| = 0.4 eV; (Delta L)_0.8 = 3.04 x 10^22 erg s^-1 for |<m_nu>| = 0.8 eV

## Context

This work extends the alternative hypothesis that low-magnetic-field magnetars can be described as massive rapidly rotating magnetized white dwarfs, as previously proposed by Malheiro et al. (2012), Boshkayev et al. (2013), and Coelho and Malheiro (2014). The authors had earlier applied the SMWD concept to the double charge exchange reaction in Adam et al. (2015), and here they improve those calculations by including a third compact object, 3XMM J185246.6+003317. The results show that current observational precision cannot provide conclusive information on the Majorana nature of the neutrino from this process, but the authors note that more realistic SMWD models from Das and Mukhopadhyay (2013, 2014) and Belyaev et al. (2015) exist, and new facilities like the Gaia satellite may enable future detection of the lepton-number-violating reaction in fainter compact objects.
