# Generalized Sum Rules for Spin-Dependent Structure Functions of the Nucleon

> The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov and Bjorken sum rules are special examples of dispersive sum rules for the spin-dependent structure function G_1(ν, Q^2) at Q^2=0 and \infty. We generalize these sum rules through studying the virtual-photon Compton amplitudes S_1(ν, Q^2) and S_2(ν,Q^2). At small Q^2, we calculate the Compton amplitudes at leading-order in chiral perturbation theory; the resulting sum rules can be tested by data soon available from Jefferson Lab. For Q^2>>Λ_{QCD}^2, the standard twist-expansion for the Compton amplitudes leads to the well-known deep-inelastic sum rules. Although the situation is still relatively unclear in a small intermediate-Q^2 window, we argue that chiral perturbation theory and the twist-expansion alone already provide strong constraints on the Q^2-evolution of the G_1(ν,Q^2) sum rule from Q^2=0 to Q^2=\infty.

## Key claim

The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov and Bjorken sum rules are special cases of a broader class of dispersive sum rules for the spin-dependent structure function \(G_1(\nu, Q^2)\) at \(Q^2=0\) and \(Q^2=\infty\), and the paper argues that chiral perturbation theory and the twist expansion together provide strong constraints on the \(Q^2\)-evolution of the \(G_1\) sum rule across the full range from \(Q^2=0\) to \(Q^2=\infty\), even though the intermediate-\(Q^2\) window between approximately 0.2 and 0.5 GeV\(^2\) remains theoretically unclear.

## Method

The authors study the virtual-photon Compton amplitudes \(S_1(\nu, Q^2)\) and \(S_2(\nu, Q^2)\) and relate them to the spin-dependent structure functions \(G_1(\nu, Q^2)\) and \(G_2(\nu, Q^2)\) through unsubtracted dispersion relations. At small \(Q^2\), they calculate the Compton amplitudes at leading order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT), including one-loop diagrams and nucleon-pole contributions. For large \(Q^2 \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\), they use the standard twist expansion via the operator product expansion (OPE) to recover the deep-inelastic sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule at \(Q^2=0\) gives \(\int^\infty_{\nu_{\rm in}} (d\nu/\nu)(\sigma_P(\nu)-\sigma_A(\nu)) = 2\pi^2\alpha_{\rm em}\kappa^2/M^2\), where \(\kappa\) is the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon.
- The Bjorken sum rule at \(Q^2\rightarrow\infty\) gives \(\lim_{Q^2\rightarrow\infty}\int_0^1 dx (g_1^{(p)}(x,Q^2)-g_1^{(n)}(x,Q^2)) = g_A/6\), where \(g_A\) is the neutron decay constant.
- The leading chiral contribution to the slope \(\overline S_1'(0,0)\) vanishes: \(a = 0\), meaning the correction to \(\overline S_1(0,Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 \sim m_\pi^2 \approx 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) is of order \(Q^2/(m_\pi M^3) \sim 0.15/M^2\), only a few percent.
- Chiral perturbation theory applies from \(Q^2=0\) to approximately 0.2 GeV\(^2\); the OPE applies from \(Q^2=\infty\) down to approximately 0.5 GeV\(^2\).
- The \(\Delta\)-resonance contribution to \(\overline S_2^{(1)}(0,Q^2)\) is less than 20% near \(Q^2=0\) in the real world with \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule (Drell, Hearn, Gerasimov) and the Bjorken sum rule (Bjorken) by providing a unified framework based on the Compton amplitudes \(S_1\) and \(S_2\). It contrasts with earlier generalizations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s) that used virtual-photon cross sections with a special flux factor, which the authors argue are neither unique nor theoretically appealing because they do not yield a well-defined sum rule. The paper enables experimental tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions using data from Jefferson Lab, and it highlights the intermediate-\(Q^2\) window (0.2 to 0.5 GeV\(^2\)) as a testing ground for parton-hadron duality and the transition between hadronic and partonic descriptions.
