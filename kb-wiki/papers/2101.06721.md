# Journey to the Bound States

> Guided by the observed properties of hadrons I formulate a perturbative bound state method for QED and QCD. The expansion starts with valence Fock states ($e^+e^-,\ q\bar q,\ qqq,\ gg$) bound by the instantaneous interaction of temporal gauge ($A^0=0$). The method is tested on Positronium atoms at rest and in motion, including hyperfine splitting at $O(α^4)$, electromagnetic form factors and deep inelastic scattering. Relativistic binding is studied for QED in $D=1+1$ dimensions, demonstrating the frame independence of the DIS electron distribution and its sea for $x_{bj} \to 0$. In QCD a homogeneous solution of Gauss' constraint in $D=3+1$ implies $O(α_s^0)$ confining potentials for $q\bar q,\ q\bar qg,\ qqq$ and $gg$ states, whereas $q\bar q\,q\bar q$ is unconfined. Meson states lie on linear Regge trajectories and have the required frame dependence. A scalar bound state with vanishing four-momentum causes spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking when mixed with the vacuum. These lecture notes assume knowledge of field theory methods, but not of bound states. Brief reviews of existing bound state methods and Dirac electron states are included. Solutions to the exercises are given in the Appendix.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a perturbative bound state method for QED and QCD that starts with valence Fock states bound by the instantaneous interaction in temporal gauge, arguing that a homogeneous solution of Gauss' constraint in QCD can produce confining potentials at order alpha_s^0, contrary to the standard view that confinement requires a scale arising only from renormalization.

## Method

The method quantizes the theory at equal time in temporal gauge (A^0=0) and expands around valence Fock states (e+e-, qqbar, qqq, gg) bound by the instantaneous gauge potential. For QCD, a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law is included for each color component of the state, introducing a boundary condition that gives a spatially constant color field energy density. The bound state equation is then solved for various Fock states, with the Schr\\"odinger equation derived for atoms at rest and in motion, and tested on Positronium including hyperfine splitting at O(alpha^4), electromagnetic form factors, and deep inelastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends the QED bound state methods developed for Positronium (Bodwin et al., 1985; Itzykson and Zuber, 1980) to QCD, motivated by the observed similarities between hadrons and atoms despite the conventional belief that QED methods are irrelevant for hadrons. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD studies (Particle Data Group, 2020; FLAG, 2019) and the Cornell potential phenomenology (Eichten et al., 1979, 2007), and aims to provide a perturbative framework that can reproduce confinement, linear Regge trajectories, and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, enabling analytic calculations of hadron structure that complement numerical approaches.
