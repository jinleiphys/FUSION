# Comparative study of quartet superfluid state: Quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory and generalized Nambu-Gor'kov formalism

> We theoretically investigate a quartet superfluid state in fermionic matter by using the quartet Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) variational theory and the Green's function method. We demonstrate that the quartet BCS theory with the multiple-infinite-product ansatz successfully reproduces an exact four-body result in a one-dimensional four-component Fermi gas at the dilute limit, in contrast to the single-infinite-product ansatz. To see the validity of the quartet BCS state, we derive the self-consistent equation for the quartet superfluid order parameter within the generalized imaginary-time Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, which is found to be consistent with the quartet BCS variational equation. Moreover, by numerically computing the momentum-resolved single-particle spectral function in a one-dimensional system, we discuss how the single-particle spectra evolve with increasing the strength of the four-body cluster formation. We show that a coherent BCS-like quasiparticle branch on the weak-coupling side evolves into a strongly damped, continuum-dominated spectrum in the strong-coupling side, while nonzero quartet superfluid order parameter persists throughout the crossover regime. Our results would be useful for understanding beyond-BCS pairing effects and four-body cluster formations in fermionic systems in an interdisciplinary way.

## Key claim

The quartet BCS theory with the multiple-infinite-product (MIP) ansatz successfully reproduces the exact four-body binding energy in a one-dimensional four-component Fermi gas at the dilute limit, while the single-infinite-product (SIP) ansatz gives a different renormalization condition and binding energy due to its restricted Hilbert space.

## Method

The authors compared two variational ansatzes within quartet BCS theory: the single-infinite-product (SIP) ansatz, which restricts quartet correlations to a BCS-like factorizable channel with momenta ±p, and the multiple-infinite-product (MIP) ansatz, which allows all momentum configurations. They derived self-consistent gap and density equations for each ansatz and benchmarked them against the exact four-body T-matrix solution. Separately, they derived the quartet gap equation within the generalized imaginary-time Nambu-Gor'kov formalism using four-component Nambu-Gor'kov spinors and showed its consistency with the MIP ansatz equation. Numerical self-consistent calculations of the order parameter and chemical potential were performed at fixed density for a one-dimensional system.

## Key numbers

- Exact four-body binding energy: \(E_{\rm b} = 1/(2ma^2)\), where \(a\) is the four-body scattering length.
- SIP ansatz binding energy: \(E_{\rm b}^{\rm SIP} = 9mg_0^2/(8L^4) \equiv 1/(2ma_{\rm eff}^2)\), which differs from the exact result.
- The unitary coupling constant: \(g_c = -3\sqrt{3}\pi/(m\Lambda)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the momentum cutoff.
- Numerical calculations used fixed density \(\rho = 4/\pi\), with \(k_{\rm F}=E_{\rm F}=1\), system size \(L=1\), and momentum cutoff \(\Lambda=12\).
- The chemical potential \(\mu\) shows a maximum around inverse scattering length \(1/a=0\) and decreases at larger \(1/a\).

## Context

This work extends the quartet BCS theory developed by Sen'kov (2011), Baran (2020), and Guo (2022) for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, and applies it to a one-dimensional four-component Fermi gas where exact four-body solutions are known from Bethe-ansatz methods (Takahashi, 1999; RevModPhys, 2013). The study also generalizes the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, originally introduced by Nambu (1960) and Gor'kov (1959), to quartet degrees of freedom following the approach of Sogo (2010). The results enable future studies of beyond-BCS pairing effects and four-body cluster formation in fermionic systems, including nuclear matter and ultracold atomic gases with SU(4) symmetry.
