# Drell-Yan Cross Section and J/psi Production in High-Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We use the Drell-Yan differential distribution dN_{DY}^{AB}/dE_T in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions to obtain a relation between the transverse energy E_T and the impact parameter b. Such a relation is then utilized to study the transverse-energy dependence of J/psi production in Pb-Pb collisions, using the J/psi absorption model presented previously. The anomalous Pb-Pb suppression data at 158A GeV can be explained if one assumes the occurrence of a new phase of strong J/psi absorption when the energy density exceeds 4.2 GeV/fm^3. The results are extended to make predictions for J/psi production at higher collision energies. It is found that J/psi survival probabilities reach the lowest survival limit when the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies is greater than about 35 GeV.

# Drell-Yan Cross Section and J/psi Production in High-Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The anomalous J/psi suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV can be explained if one assumes a new phase of strong J/psi absorption occurs when the local energy density exceeds 4.2 GeV/fm^3, but the discontinuity in the theoretical results is much smoother than the discontinuity in the preliminary NA50 data. The J/psi survival probability reaches the lowest survival limit when the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy exceeds about 35 GeV.

## Method

The author uses the Drell-Yan differential distribution dN_DY^AB/dE_T to establish a relation between transverse energy E_T and impact parameter b in nucleus-nucleus collisions, parameterizing E_T(b) with a sigmoid function and including E_T spreading via a Gaussian distribution. This relation is then applied to a previously developed J/psi absorption model (Wong 1996) that tracks space-time trajectories of J/psi precursors, baryons, and fireballs of produced hadrons, incorporating absorption by nucleons, produced hadrons (comovers), and a postulated new phase of matter with stronger absorption when the local energy density exceeds a critical threshold.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density for onset of new phase: epsilon_crit = 4.2 GeV/fm^3
- J/psi absorption cross section by nucleons: sigma_abs(J/psi-N) = 6.36 mb
- J/psi absorption cross section by produced hadrons: sigma_abs(J/psi-h) = 0.15 mb
- J/psi absorption cross section by new phase matter: sigma_abs(J/psi-x) = 3.7 mb
- psi' absorption cross section by produced hadrons: sigma_abs(psi'-h) = 11 mb
- Drell-Yan distribution parameters for Pb-Pb at 158A GeV: E_T0 = 135 GeV, b_0 = 6.7 fm, a = 3 fm
- E_T spreading parameters: sigma_0 = 11 GeV, b_sigma = 10 fm, a_sigma = 2 fm
- c-cbar production time: t_cbar = 0.06 fm/c
- Hadronization time: t_h = 1.2 fm/c
- Freezeout density: rho_freezeout^NN = 0.5 hadrons/fm^3
- For epsilon_crit = 4.2 GeV/fm^3, the break in the ratio B*sigma(J/psi)/sigma(DY) occurs at E_T ~ 40 GeV
- For epsilon_crit = 3.0 GeV/fm^3, the break occurs at E_T ~ 20 GeV
- For epsilon_crit = 5.5 GeV/fm^3, the break occurs at E_T ~ 90 GeV
- The lowest survival limit for J/psi is reached at sqrt(s) ~ 35 GeV with the new phase, or at sqrt(s) ~ 70 GeV without a phase transition
- The critical energy density from lattice gauge theory is epsilon_c ~ 4.17 GeV/fm^3 (Blum 1995)

## Context

This work extends the J/psi absorption model previously presented by Wong (1996) to explain the anomalous suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration (Gonin 1996, Ramello 1997, Romana 1998) in Pb-Pb collisions at 158A GeV, which showed a discontinuity in the differential J/psi survival probability as a function of transverse energy. The paper builds on the initial suggestion by Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/psi production is suppressed in a quark-gluon plasma, and uses the Drell-Yan distribution to establish the E_T-b relation needed for such analyses. The results enable predictions for J/psi production at higher collision energies, particularly for the RHIC collider at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, where the author notes it will be difficult to distinguish between absorption by the new phase and absorption by produced hadrons alone, motivating experiments at lower energies where such a distinction can be observed.
