# Symmetries of the Neutrino Interactions

> Symmetries in neutrino physics are explored using analogies to fermion pairing in many-body systems. In particular, the SO(5) symmetry of the most general neutrino mass Hamiltonian with both Dirac and Majorana mass terms as well as the invariants of certain limits of the collective neutrino Hamiltonian are discussed. The latter Hamiltonian finds applications in the Early Universe and core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the most general neutrino mass Hamiltonian containing both Dirac and Majorana mass terms possesses an SO(5) symmetry, and that the collective neutrino Hamiltonian describing self-interacting neutrino gases in astrophysical settings has a set of conserved quantities (invariants) that manifest dynamical symmetries, but these invariants do not eliminate the need for numerical stability analysis.

## Method
The author constructs the generators of an SO(5) algebra from the Dirac and Majorana mass terms for a single neutrino flavor, showing that the mass Hamiltonian sits in the SO(5)/SU(2)_PG x U(1)_chi coset. For the collective neutrino gas, the author writes the Hamiltonian in terms of SU(2) flavor isospin operators for two flavors, then derives conserved quantities by exploiting an analogy to the reduced BCS Hamiltonian and its Bethe ansatz solution by Richardson and Gaudin. The one-body reduction of the Hamiltonian is obtained via a stationary phase approximation to the path integral, equivalent to an RPA-like mean-field treatment.

## Key numbers
- The neutrino-neutrino interaction Hamiltonian contains the factor (1 - cos theta_pq) where theta_pq is the angle between neutrino momenta p and q, with normalization volume V.
- The single-angle collective neutrino Hamiltonian is written with the scaled time variable tau = mu t, where mu = sqrt(2) G_F / V, and scaled frequency omega_p = (1/mu) * (delta m^2 / 2p).
- For antineutrinos, the conserved quantities use omega_bar_p = -(1/mu) * (delta m^2 / 2 bar-p).

## Context
This work extends the analogy between neutrino physics and fermion pairing in many-body systems, building on the SO(5) symmetry framework introduced by Balantekin (2000) and the SU(2) flavor isospin formalism developed by Balantekin (2006). The collective neutrino Hamiltonian applies to core-collapse supernovae and the Early Universe, and the conserved quantities derived here are related to the spectral split or swapping phenomenon in neutrino energy spectra studied by Raffelt (2007) and Duan (2007). The paper notes that whether invariants exist for the more realistic multi-angle Hamiltonian remains an open question, and that direction-changing scattering outside the neutrinosphere may require modifications to the multi-angle picture as discussed by Cherry (2012).
