# K*Sigma photoproduction off the proton target with baryon resonances

> We investigate the photoproduction of K*0Sigma+ and K*+Sigma0 off the proton target, employing the effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation. In addition to the (s, t, u)-channel Born diagrams, we take into account various baryon-resonance contributions such as F15(2000), D13(2080), G17(2190), D15(2200), F35(2000), G37(2200), F37(2390), and Sigma*(1385, 3/2+) in a fully covariant manner. We present the numerical results for the energy and angular dependences for the cross sections in comparison to available experimental data. The single-polarization observables, i.e. the photon-beam, recoil and target baryon polarization asymmetries are computed as well for future experiments. We observe from the numerical results that the resonance contributions play a minor role in producing the strength of the cross sections, being different from the K*Lambda photoproduction. In contrast, it turns out that the Delta(1232)-pole contribution and strange-meson exchanges in the t-channel dominate the scattering process. On the other hand, the higher resonances influence the polarization observables such as the recoil and target asymmetries.

## Key claim

The authors find that, contrary to the case of K*Lambda photoproduction, baryon-resonance contributions play a minor role in producing the strength of the cross sections for K*Sigma photoproduction; instead, the Delta(1232)-pole contribution and K exchange in the t-channel dominate the scattering process, though higher resonances do influence the polarization observables such as the recoil and target asymmetries.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation to compute the photoproduction of K*0Sigma+ and K*+Sigma0 off the proton target. They included (s, t, u)-channel Born diagrams along with baryon-resonance contributions from F15(2000), D13(2080), G17(2190), D15(2200), F35(2000), G37(2200), F37(2390), and Sigma*(1385, 3/2+) in a fully covariant manner, using a gauge-invariant form factor prescription to preserve the Ward-Takahashi identity. The coupling strengths were determined from experimental data and theoretical models including the Nijmegen soft-core model (NSC97a) and quark-model predictions, with cutoff parameters for the form factors fitted to reproduce experimental cross-section data from the CBELSA/TAPS and CLAS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the charged gamma-K*-K vertex is g^charged_gamma K*K = 0.254 GeV^-1, and for the neutral vertex g^neutral_gamma K*K = -0.388 GeV^-1.
- The coupling constant for the charged gamma-K*-kappa vertex is g^charged_gamma K*kappa = -0.119 e GeV^-1, and for the neutral vertex g^neutral_gamma K*kappa = -2 g^charged_gamma K*kappa.
- The strong coupling constants from the Nijmegen soft-core model (NSC97a) are g_K*NSigma = -2.46, kappa_K*NSigma = -0.47, and g_kappa NSigma = -5.32.
- The strong coupling constant g_KNSigma is 3.58, and f_K*DeltaSigma = -12.8.
- The cutoff masses for the form factors are: Lambda_K* = 0.80 GeV, Lambda_K = 1.15 GeV, Lambda_kappa = 1.15 GeV, Lambda_N = 1.50 GeV, Lambda_Delta = 1.50 GeV, Lambda_N* = 1.00 GeV, Lambda_Delta* = 1.00 GeV, Lambda_Lambda = 0.70 GeV, Lambda_Sigma = 0.95 GeV, and Lambda_Sigma* = 0.95 GeV.
- The magnitude of the N* and Delta* resonance contributions to the total cross section is about 100 times smaller than that of the Born term contributions.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of K*Sigma photoproduction by Oh et al. (2006) by including various baryon-resonance contributions that were proven essential in K*Lambda photoproduction by Kim et al. (2011). The results contrast with those for K*Lambda photoproduction, where resonance contributions played important roles, and show that the Delta(1232)-pole and K exchange dominate the K*Sigma channel. The computed single-polarization observables (photon-beam, recoil, and target asymmetries) provide theoretical predictions for future experiments at facilities such as ELSA, CLAS, and LEPS.
