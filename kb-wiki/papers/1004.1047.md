# Semiclassical features of rotational ground bands

> A time dependent variational principle is used to dequantize a second order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian. The classical equations for the generalized coordinate and the constraint for angular momentum are quantized and then analytically solved. A generalized Holmberg-Lipas formula for energies is obtained. A similar $J(J+1)$ dependence is provided by the coherent state model (CSM) in the large deformation regime, by using an expansion in powers of $1/x$ for energies, with $x$ denoting a deformation parameter squared. A simple compact expression is also possible for the near vibrational regime. These three expressions have been used for 44 nuclei covering regions characterized by different dynamic symmetries or in other words belonging to the all known nuclear phases. Nuclei satisfying the specific symmetries of the critical point in the phase transitions $O(6)\to SU(3)$, $SU(5)\to SU(3)$ have been also considered. The agreement between the results and the corresponding experimental data is very good. This is reflected in very small r.m.s. values of deviations.

## Key claim
The paper derives three closed-form expressions for ground-band rotational energies that generalize the Holmberg-Lipas formula, claiming very good agreement with experimental data for 44 nuclei across all known nuclear phases (including critical-point nuclei in the O(6)→SU(3) and SU(5)→SU(3) transitions), but the agreement is qualified by the use of a least-mean-square fitting procedure with three free parameters per nucleus.

## Method
A time-dependent variational principle with a quadrupole-boson coherent state is used to dequantize a second-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian, yielding classical equations that are then quantized and solved analytically. Separately, angular momentum projection from a coherent state is performed, and the resulting energy expressions are expanded in the asymptotic (large deformation) and near-vibrational (small deformation) limits. The three resulting formulas are fitted to experimental ground-band energies for 44 nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For 44 nuclei, the root-mean-square deviation χ between theoretical and experimental excitation energies is reported; for example, χ = 0.56 keV for ²²⁸Th (Th(1) method) and χ = 0.66 keV (Th(2) method).
- The ratio E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) is given for each nucleus: e.g., 3.23 for ²²⁸Th, 3.28 for ²³²Th, 3.30 for ²³⁴U, 3.30 for ²³⁶U, 3.30 for ²³⁸U.
- For ²²⁸Th, the deformation parameter d = 2.7062 (dimensionless) and parameters A₁ = 182.8720 keV, A₂ = 4.0861 keV.
- The lower bound of the existence interval for the asymptotic formula (where G_J ≥ 0) is given for angular momenta J = 0 through 30; e.g., d_min = 1.55 for J=0, d_min = 0 for J=2 and J=4, d_min = 1.59 for J=12, d_min = 2.33 for J=30.

## Context
This work extends the Holmberg-Lipas formula (Holmberg and Lipas, 1970) for ground-band energies by providing a generalized square-root expression with an additional J²(J+1)² term. It also connects to the coherent state model (CSM) of Raduta (1982, 1983, 1984) and to the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) symmetries SU(3), SU(5), and O(6) as classified by Casten. The results enable systematic description of ground bands across all nuclear phases, including critical-point nuclei such as those with E(5) symmetry (e.g., ¹⁰⁴Ru, ¹⁰²Pd) and transitional nuclei like ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁴Gd, and ¹⁵⁶Dy.
