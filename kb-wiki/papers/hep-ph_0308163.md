# Single-Spin Beam Asymmetry in Semi-Exclusive Deep-Inelastic Electroproduction

> Recent measurements from Jefferson Lab show significant beam single spin asymmetries in deep inelastic scattering. The asymmetry is due to interference of longitudinal and transverse photoabsorption amplitudes which have different phases induced by the final-state interaction between the struck quark and the target spectators. We developed a dynamical model for a single-spin beam asymmetry in deep-inelastic scattering. Our results are consistent with the experimentally observed magnitude of this effect. We conclude that similar mechanisms involving quark orbital angular momentum (`Sivers effect') are responsible for both target and beam single-spin asymmetries.

## Key claim

The beam single-spin asymmetry in semi-exclusive deep-inelastic electroproduction arises from the same mechanism as target single-spin asymmetries, interference between longitudinal and transverse photoabsorption amplitudes with phases from final-state gluon exchange, and does not require the Collins fragmentation function, meaning it cannot be directly related to the Jaffe-Ji quark distribution \(e(x)\).

## Method

The authors model the proton as a bound state of a spin-1/2 quark and a scalar diquark, with the proton-quark-diquark vertex simplified to a scalar coupling. They calculate the beam single-spin asymmetry \(A_{LU}^{\sin\phi}\) from the interference of the tree-level diagram (Fig. 1a) and the one-loop gluon-exchange diagram (Fig. 1b), using Cutkosky rules to extract the absorptive part of the loop integral. The asymmetry is generated by the interference of longitudinal and transverse virtual photon absorption amplitudes, which acquire different phases from the final-state gluon exchange, and the calculation is performed in both covariant and light-cone gauges.

## Key numbers

- Beam single-spin asymmetry \(A_{LU}^{\sin\phi}\) for 6 GeV beam energy at \(y=0.7\) and \(r_T=0.4\) GeV/c is approximately constant at +0.08 in a simplified model with scalar partons.
- For 27 GeV beam energy (HERMES kinematics) at fixed \(x_{Bj}\) and \(y\), the beam asymmetry is suppressed by about a factor of two compared to the 6 GeV case, following a \(1/\sqrt{Q^2}\) dependence.
- The asymmetry changes sign around \(x_{Bj}=0.5\), where no experimental data are available.
- Model parameters used: quark mass \(m=0.3\) GeV, diquark mass \(\lambda=0.8\) GeV, and strong coupling \(\alpha_s=0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the model of Brodsky et al. (2002) for target single-spin asymmetries to the case of a polarized electron beam, using the same diquark spectator model originally studied by Ezawa, Drell, and Lee (1964). The results are consistent with Jefferson Lab CLAS measurements (2002) and show that beam single-spin asymmetries can arise from a "photon Sivers effect" without requiring the Collins fragmentation function, in contrast to analyses by Mulders and Tangerman (1996) and Efremov and Goeke (1998). The mechanism enables interpretation of target single-spin asymmetry measurements within a parton model and motivates extending experimental measurements to higher \(x_{Bj}\) to test the proposed mechanism.
