# Constraining the properties of dense matter and neutron stars by combining nuclear physics and gravitational waves from GW170817

> Gravitational waves from neutron-star mergers are expected to provide stringent constraints on the structure of neutron stars. At the same time, recent advances in nuclear theory have enabled reliable calculations of the low density equation of state using effective field theory based Hamiltonians and advanced techniques to solve the quantum many-body problem. In this paper, we address how the first observation of gravitational waves from GW170817 can be combined with modern calculations of the equation of state to extract useful insights about the equation of state of matter encountered inside neutron stars. We analyze the impact of various uncertainties and we show that the tidal deformability extracted from GW170817 is compatible, while less constraining, than modern nuclear physics knowledge.

## Key claim

The tidal deformability extracted from GW170817 is compatible with, but less constraining than, modern nuclear physics knowledge from chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron matter up to twice saturation density, provided the low-density equation of state is used to inform extrapolations to higher densities.

## Method

The authors generated thousands of neutron-star equations of state using two complementary models: a minimal model (MM) based on a smooth density expansion around nuclear saturation density using empirical nuclear-matter parameters, and a maximal model (CSM) that parametrizes the speed of sound at high densities with linear segments and allows for strong first-order phase transitions. Both models were constrained at low densities (up to a transition density n_tr between 1 and 2 times saturation density) by auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of pure neutron matter using local chiral effective field theory interactions, and were required to support a maximum mass of at least 1.9 solar masses. The resulting equations of state were then compared with the binary tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Chirp mass of GW170817: M_chirp = 1.186 ± 0.001 solar masses
- Binary tidal deformability from GW170817: 70 ≤ Λ̃ ≤ 720 at 90% confidence (LV collaboration reanalysis)
- For n_tr = n_sat (saturation density), the radius range for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 8.4–15.2 km (CSM) and 10.9–13.5 km (MM)
- For n_tr = 2 n_sat, the radius range for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star reduces to 8.7–12.6 km (CSM) and 10.9–12.0 km (MM)
- Saturation density: n_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³ = 2.7 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tews (2018) by combining chiral effective field theory constraints on neutron matter with gravitational-wave data from GW170817, using two complementary equation-of-state models (MM and CSM) introduced in earlier work by Margueron (2017) and Tews (2018). The approach enables distinguishing between smooth nuclear equations of state and those with strong phase transitions: a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with radius near 10 km would imply a softening phase transition and new phases of matter in the core, while the masquerade problem noted by Alford (2004) prevents identifying constituents where both models agree. The results provide a framework for interpreting future observations from NICER and additional gravitational-wave detections.
