# The $^4$He total photo-absorption cross section with two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory

> The total photo-absorption cross section of $^4$He is evaluated microscopically using two- (NN) and three-nucleon (NNN) interactions based upon chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). The calculation is performed using the Lorentz integral transform method along with the {\em ab initio} no-core shell model approach. An important feature of the present study is the consistency of the NN and NNN interactions and also, through the Siegert theorem, of the two- and three-body current operators. This is due to the application of the $χ$EFT framework. The inclusion of the NNN interaction produces a suppression of the low-energy peak and enhancement of the high-energy tail of the cross section. We compare to calculations obtained using other interactions and to representative experiments. The rather confused experimental situation in the giant resonance region prevents discrimination among different interaction models.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a consistent three-nucleon (NNN) interaction from chiral effective field theory produces a suppression of the low-energy peak and an enhancement of the high-energy tail of the $^4$He total photo-absorption cross section, but the rather confused experimental situation in the giant resonance region prevents discrimination among different interaction models.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic calculation of the $^4$He total photo-absorption cross section using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) approach. They employed the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) and the three-nucleon (NNN) interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) from chiral effective field theory, with the dipole operator including two- and three-body current contributions through the Siegert theorem. The NCSM expansions used harmonic oscillator basis states up to a maximum excitation of $N_{max}\hbar\Omega$, with effective interactions derived at the three-body cluster level to accelerate convergence.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^4$He with NN+NNN interaction: -28.60(3) MeV, compared to the experimental value of -28.296 MeV
- Point-proton root-mean-square radius with NN+NNN interaction: 1.458(2) fm, compared to the experimental value of 1.455(7) fm
- Total dipole strength with NN+NNN interaction: 0.868(1) fm$^2$, compared to 0.943(1) fm$^2$ with NN only (a 7.9% reduction)
- The NNN force provides 3.21 MeV additional binding to the $^4$He ground state
- The peak of the photo-absorption cross section occurs around $\omega=27.8$ MeV with a cross section of approximately 3 mb
- The NNN force produces a 9% suppression of the peak height and the NN and NN+NNN cross sections cross around $\omega=40$ MeV
- The relative difference between NN and NN+NNN cross sections varies from -10% at threshold to about +25% at $\omega=120$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $^4$He photo-disintegration that used phenomenological interactions such as the Argonne V18 (AV18) NN potential augmented by the Urbana IX (UIX) NNN force (Gazit, 2006) and the correlated AV18 potential within the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) (Bacca, 2007). The key advance is the use of chiral effective field theory interactions where the NN and NNN potentials are derived consistently, reducing the arbitrariness present in combining phenomenological NN potentials with NNN forces. The results enable future studies of photo-disintegration in $p$-shell nuclei, where differences in spin-orbit strength from NNN interactions are expected to have more substantial effects.
