# Instabilities in non-expanding glasma

> A homogeneous color magnetic field is known to be unstable for the fluctuations perpendicular to the field in the color space (the Nielsen-Olesen instability). We argue that these unstable modes, exponentially growing, generate an azimuthal magnetic field with the original field being in the z-direction, which causes the Nielsen-Olesen instability for another type of fluctuations. The growth rate of the latter unstable mode increases with the momentum p_z and can become larger than the former's growth rate which decreases with increasing p_z. These features may explain the interplay between the primary and secondary instabilities observed in the real-time simulation of a non-expanding glasma, i.e., stochastically generated anisotropic Yang-Mills fields without expansion.

## Key claim

The primary and secondary instabilities observed in real-time simulations of a non-expanding glasma can both be explained by the Nielsen-Olesen (N-O) instability mechanism, where the primary instability in a longitudinal color magnetic field generates an azimuthal magnetic field via induced currents, which then causes a secondary N-O instability with a growth rate that increases with longitudinal momentum \(p_z\).

## Method

The authors analyze the SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in the temporal gauge using an Abelian decomposed representation, treating a homogeneous color magnetic field \(B^z\) as a background U(1) field and the charged vector fields \(\phi_\mu\) as fluctuations. They solve the linearized equations of motion for the fluctuations to obtain eigenfrequencies and growth rates, first for the primary N-O instability in a uniform longitudinal field, then for the secondary instability in an azimuthal magnetic field \(B^\theta\) generated by the induced current, using both analytic approximations and numerical solution of the reduced radial Schrodinger-like equation.

## Key numbers

* Maximum growth rate of the primary N-O instability in a uniform longitudinal magnetic field: \(\gamma_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{gB}\) at \(p_z = 0\), with instability occurring for \(p_z^2 < gB\).
* Growth rate of the secondary instability in the azimuthal magnetic field \(B^\theta\): \(\gamma_\theta(p_z) \simeq \sqrt{gB^\theta} \sqrt{1 - \frac{1}{4\rho_0^2}}\) for large \(\rho_0 = p_z/\sqrt{gB^\theta}\), approaching \(\gamma_\theta^\infty = \sqrt{gB^\theta}\) as \(p_z \to \infty\).
* The secondary instability appears only for \(p_z/\sqrt{gB^\theta} \ge 0.755\) (from numerical solution), with no unstable modes at \(p_z = 0\).
* Estimated magnitude of the induced current: \(J^z \sim \mathcal{O}((gB)^{3/2}/g) \sim \mathcal{O}(Q_s^3/g)\), and the generated azimuthal magnetic field strength: \(B^\theta = \mathcal{O}(Q_s^2/g)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic studies of Nielsen-Olesen instabilities in the expanding glasma (Fujii and Itakura, 2008; Iwazaki, 2008) to the non-expanding case simulated by Berges, Scheffler, and Serreau (2007), who observed primary and secondary instabilities in classical SU(2) Yang-Mills simulations with an anisotropic initial condition inspired by the Color Glass Condensate. The analysis provides a theoretical explanation for the numerical observation that the secondary growth rate increases with \(p_z\), and it enables future non-perturbative numerical studies to verify the induced current mechanism and the role of azimuthal magnetic fields in glasma thermalization.
