# Glueballs and AdS/CFT

> I review the calculation of the glueball spectrum in non-supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (in 3 and 4 dimensions) using the conjectured duality between supergravity and large N gauge theories. The glueball masses are obtained by solving the supergravity wave equations in a black hole geometry. The masses obtained this way are in unexpectedly good agreement with the available lattice data, and are much better than strong-coupling expansion results. I also show how to use a modified version of the duality to calculate the glueball mass spectrum with some of the Kaluza-Klein states of the supergravity theory decoupled from the spectrum.

# Glueballs and AdS/CFT

## Key claim

The supergravity calculation of the glueball mass spectrum in non-supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (in 3 and 4 dimensions) agrees unexpectedly well with lattice data, despite the fact that the supergravity approximation and the pure gauge theory are valid in opposite limits of the 't Hooft coupling; the agreement is much better than strong-coupling expansion results, but the author notes there is a priori no reason to believe these two limits have anything to do with each other, and a phase transition could occur when the 't Hooft coupling is decreased.

## Method

The glueball masses are obtained by solving the supergravity wave equations (specifically the dilaton equation of motion for the 0++ glueball, and the two-form field equation for the 0-- glueball) in an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole geometry, using a numerical "shooting" technique to find eigenvalues of k² that yield regular, normalizable solutions. For the 4D case, the calculation is performed in a rotating M5-brane background with angular momentum parameter a, which allows some Kaluza-Klein states to decouple as a → ∞. The supergravity results are normalized so that the lightest 0++ state agrees with the lattice result, and then ratios of glueball masses are compared.

## Key numbers

- 3D 0++ glueball masses (in units of square root of string tension): supergravity gives 4.07 (input), 7.02, 9.92, 12.80; lattice (N→∞) gives 4.065 ± 0.055, 6.18 ± 0.13, 7.99 ± 0.22
- 3D mass ratio M(0--)/M(0++): supergravity = 1.50; lattice = 1.45 ± 0.08
- 4D 0++ glueball masses (in GeV): supergravity a=0 gives 1.61 (input), 2.55, 3.46; supergravity a→∞ gives 1.61 (input), 2.56, 3.48; lattice N=3 gives 1.61 ± 0.15, 2.48 ± 0.23
- 4D 0-+ glueball masses (in GeV): supergravity a=0 gives 2.00, 2.98, 3.91; supergravity a→∞ gives 2.56, 3.49, 4.40; lattice N=3 gives 2.59 ± 0.13, 3.64 ± 0.18
- The supergravity results for 4D glueball masses are within 4% of lattice results; strong-coupling expansion results (Kogut) are off by between 7% and 28%

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1998) proposal that non-supersymmetric gauge theories can be studied via the AdS/CFT correspondence by heating the theory and compactifying on a circle, which replaces the AdS metric with an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole metric. The numerical calculations follow the method of Csaki, Ooguri, Oz, and Terning (1999) for the glueball spectrum, and use the rotating brane construction of Russo (1998) and Csaki, Russo, and Terning (1999) to decouple some Kaluza-Klein states. The results are compared to lattice simulations by Teper (1998) and Morningstar (1999). The work enables further investigation into whether the unexpected agreement between supergravity and lattice results is a numerical coincidence or has a deeper explanation, and opens the question of how finite N corrections (string theory corrections) modify the ratios.
