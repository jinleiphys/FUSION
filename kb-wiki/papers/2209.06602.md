# Compact Star Twins with a Dark Matter Core

> We present a model of compact stars with a dark matter core. The hadronic equation of state is based on the parity doublet model and does not present a phase transition to quark matter. Instead, a strong first-order phase transition to dark matter described by a constant speed of sound model leads to the scenario of compact star mass twins. Compact star structural properties which obey state-of-the-art measurements and constraints are presented.

## Key claim
Compact stars with a dark matter core can produce mass twins (stars of similar gravitational mass but different radius) even when the hadronic equation of state does not feature a phase transition to quark matter, provided the first-order phase transition to dark matter is strong enough to satisfy the Seidov stability condition.

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid equation of state combining a hadronic phase described by the parity doublet model (with chiral symmetry restoration but no quark deconfinement) and a dark matter phase described by a constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each central density up to the maximum mass, and computed the moment of inertia and tidal deformability for the resulting compact star sequences.

## Key numbers
- The hadronic equation of state with parameter m0 = 700 MeV supports a 2 M_sun compact star, but if dark matter sets in its core it would collapse due to the softness of its nuclear matter.
- For the m0 = 550, 600, and 650 MeV models, the phase transition occurs at pressure p_trans = 30.95 MeV/fm^3, energy density e_trans = 281.32 MeV/fm^3, and baryon density n_trans = 0.286 fm^-3.
- For the m0 = 700 MeV model, the phase transition occurs at p_trans = 417.63 MeV/fm^3, e_trans = 1312.42 MeV/fm^3, and n_trans = 1.049 fm^-3.
- The energy density discontinuity at the transition (Delta e / e_trans) is 0.75 for m0 = 550, 600, and 700 MeV models, and 0.70 for the m0 = 650 MeV model.
- The dark matter speed of sound c_DM ranges from 0.80 c (m0 = 550 MeV) to 1.00 c (m0 = 700 MeV).
- The onset mass for the phase transition is 1.325 M_sun for m0 = 550, 600, and 650 MeV models, and 2.000 M_sun for the m0 = 700 MeV model.
- A Bayesian estimation of the moment of inertia reports I_* = 1.15^{+0.38}_{-0.24} x 10^45 g cm^2 (Landry 2018).

## Context
This work extends the mass twins scenario previously studied by Benic (2014), Dexheimer (2014), Alvarez-Castillo (2017, 2018), and others, where twins arise from a hadron-to-quark matter phase transition. Here the authors replace the quark matter core with a dark matter core, using a hadronic equation of state (parity doublet model) that explicitly avoids quark deconfinement. The results enable future tests of the dark matter hypothesis through observations of supernova explosions, neutron star mergers, neutron star cooling, and gravitational wave signals.
