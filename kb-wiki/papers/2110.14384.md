# The importance of multiple scatterings in medium-induced gluon radiation

> In this work we disentangle the underlying physical picture of the in-medium gluon radiation process across its different energy regimes by comparing the recently obtained fully-resummed -- without any further approximations -- BDMPS-Z in-medium emission spectrum with the extensively used analytical approaches. We observe that in the high-energy regime the radiation process is dominated by a single hard scattering, while in the intermediate-energy region coherence effects among multiple scatterings are crucial. Finally, we prove that in the low-energy regime the dynamics is again controlled by a single scattering but where one must include a suppression factor accounting for the probability of not having any further scatterings.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fully-resummed BDMPS-Z in-medium gluon radiation spectrum can be understood across different energy regimes: a single hard scattering dominates at high energies, coherence among multiple scatterings is crucial at intermediate energies, and at low energies the spectrum equals the single-scattering result multiplied by a suppression factor accounting for the probability of no further scatterings.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the full BDMPS-Z medium-induced gluon emission spectrum without any approximations, using a Yukawa parton-medium interaction potential. They compare this all-order result to the first opacity (GLV N=1) approximation, the Harmonic Oscillator (HO) approximation, and the Improved Opacity Expansion (IOE) at next-to-leading order (HO+NLO) for a brick of constant density \(n_0\) and length \(L\). The low-energy asymptotic limit is derived analytically from the all-order expression.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism by providing the first full numerical solution without approximations, building on the authors' prior work (Andres 2020). It clarifies the range of validity of the commonly used Harmonic Oscillator approximation and the opacity expansion (GLV), and resolves the physical interpretation of the low-energy (Bethe-Heitler) regime. The results enable more precise modeling of jet quenching in quark-gluon plasma by identifying which scattering processes dominate in each energy region.
