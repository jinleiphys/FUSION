# Bulk and shear viscosities of matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effects of the shear and bulk viscosities in the hadronic phase on the expansion of the fireball and on the particle production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Comparing simulation with or without viscosity in the hadronic matter we find that elliptic flow observables strongly dependent on dissipative effects in the late stage. On the other hand, interferometry radii are sensitive, through the early transverse flow, on the value of the viscosity at high temperatures. We present first calculations including the effects of bulk viscosity in the hadronic phase and in the hadron emission. We find them important in obtaining a small freeze-out temperature consistent with the measured transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of identified particles.

# Bulk and shear viscosities of matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The elliptic flow observables are strongly dependent on dissipative effects in the late hadronic stage, and the sensitivity of elliptic flow to the shear viscosity in the early QGP phase is strongly reduced if additional dissipation occurs below the transition temperature. Bulk viscosity in the hadronic phase is important for obtaining a small freeze-out temperature consistent with measured transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of identified particles.

## Method

The author solves 2+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations assuming boost invariance in the longitudinal direction, using second-order Israel-Stewart formalism for shear and bulk viscous corrections. Four scenarios are compared: ideal fluid, viscosity only in the hadronic phase (vHG, η/s=0.1), viscosity only in the QGP phase (vQGP, η/s=0.16), and viscosity in both phases (vQGP+vHG, η/s=0.16 in QGP and 0.1 in hadronic phase). The hadronic equation of state includes 371 known hadrons and resonances, and the freeze-out temperature is adjusted in each scenario to reproduce pion spectra in central collisions.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio in hadronic phase: η/s = 0.1 (vHG and vQGP+vHG scenarios)
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio in QGP phase: η/s = 0.16 (vQGP and vQGP+vHG scenarios)
- Bulk viscosity to entropy ratio in hadronic phase: ζ/s = 0.03–0.04 (vHG and vQGP+vHG scenarios)
- Transition temperature: T_c = 170 MeV
- Freeze-out temperatures: 140 MeV (ideal fluid), 150 MeV (vQGP), 130 MeV (vQGP), 135 MeV (vQGP+vHG)
- Relaxation time in hadronic phase: τ_HG ≈ 1 fm/c at T = 150 MeV
- Entropy production in QGP phase: ΔS/S ≈ 20%; in hadronic matter: 2–3%
- Initial time: τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Bulk viscosity at T ≈ 150 MeV: ζ/s ≈ 0.035
- Shear viscosity η/s = 0.24 in hadronic phase gives elliptic flow below experimental values

## Context

This work extends viscous hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, which previously mostly considered constant η/s throughout the evolution (Romatschke 2007, Song 2008). The paper addresses the role of dissipation specifically in the hadronic phase, which had been discussed qualitatively by Hirano (2005) but not systematically studied with both shear and bulk viscosities. The results enable more reliable extraction of QGP viscosity from elliptic flow data by accounting for hadronic dissipation, and show that reproducing the mass ordering of elliptic flow and proton yields requires bulk viscosity corrections at freeze-out.
