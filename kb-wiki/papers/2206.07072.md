# Semi-local nuclear forces from chiral EFT: State-of-the-art & challenges

> Recently, a new generation of nuclear forces has been developed in the framework of chiral EFT. An important feature of these potentials is a novel semi-local regularization approach that combines the advantages of a local regulator for long-range interactions with the convenience of an angle-independent nonlocal regulator for contact interactions. The authors discuss the key features of the semi-local two-nucleon potentials and demonstrate their outstanding performance in the two-nucleon sector by showing selected results up to fifth order in the EFT expansion. Also reviewed are applications to heavier systems, which are currently limited to third chiral order. This limitation reflects the conceptual difficulty in constructing a consistently regularized many-body forces and current operators and affects all currently available interactions. The authors outline possible ways to tackle this problem and discuss future directions in the field.

## Key claim
The semi-local momentum-space (SMS) regularized chiral potentials achieve a description of nucleon-nucleon scattering data that matches or exceeds the precision of the most sophisticated phenomenological potentials, but applications to heavier systems remain limited to third chiral order (N²LO) due to the conceptual difficulty of constructing consistently regularized many-body forces and current operators.

## Method
The authors derive nuclear forces from the heavy-baryon effective Lagrangian for pions and nucleons via a perturbative expansion in powers of Q (where Q is the typical momentum scale ~ M_π or M_π/Λ_b, with breakdown scale Λ_b ~ 650 MeV). They employ a semi-local regularization approach that uses a local regulator (preserving analytic structure) for long-range pion-exchange interactions and a nonlocal (angle-independent) regulator for contact interactions. The low-energy constants (LECs) for contact interactions are determined by fitting to neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data up to E_lab = 280 MeV, with the deuteron binding energy and coherent neutron-proton scattering length imposed as constraints, using the Nijmegen treatment of electromagnetic interactions and a 3σ-criterion for outlier data rejection.

## Key numbers
- Breakdown scale of chiral EFT in the two-nucleon sector: Λ_b ~ 650 MeV
- Cutoff values for SMS potentials: Λ = 400, 450, 500, and 550 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy constraint: B_d = 2.224575(9) MeV
- Coherent neutron-proton scattering length constraint: b_np = -3.7405(9) fm
- Database size: 2845 neutron-proton and 2081 proton-proton data points up to E_lab = 280 MeV
- χ²/datum for SMS N⁴LO⁺ potential at Λ = 450 MeV: 1.060 (np, 0-280 MeV) and 0.932 (pp, 0-280 MeV)
- χ²/datum for SMS N⁴LO⁺ at Λ = 500 MeV: 1.048 (np, 0-280 MeV) and 0.950 (pp, 0-280 MeV)
- χ²/datum for CD-Bonn potential on the same database: 1.042 (combined np+pp, 0-300 MeV)
- χ²/datum for Nijmegen I potential: 1.061 (combined np+pp, 0-300 MeV)
- Deuteron asymptotic S-state normalization from Granada 2013 PWA: A_S = 0.8829 fm^{-1/2}
- Fitting energy range for N³LO SMS potentials: up to E_lab = 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends the semi-local regularization approach first introduced by Epelbaum (2014) and developed through the SMS potentials of Reinert (2017, 2020), providing a comprehensive partial wave analysis comparable to the Nijmegen 1993 PWA (Stoks, 1993), the Granada 2013 PWA (Navarro Perez, 2013), and the Granada 2017 PWA (Navarro Perez, 2016). The results enable future development of consistently regularized three-nucleon forces and current operators beyond N²LO, which is the current limitation affecting all available chiral interactions when applied to heavier systems.
