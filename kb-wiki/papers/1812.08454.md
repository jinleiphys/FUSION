# A theoretical analysis of the doubly radiative decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0γγ$ and $η^\prime\toηγγ$

> The scalar and vector meson exchange contributions to the doubly radiative decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0γγ$ and $η^\prime\toηγγ$ are analysed within the Linear Sigma Model and Vector Meson Dominance frameworks, respectively. Predictions for the diphoton invariant mass spectra and the associated integrated branching ratios are given and compared with current available experimental data. Whilst a satisfactory description of the shape of the $η\toπ^{0}γγ$ and $η^\prime\toπ^{0}γγ$ decay spectra is obtained, thus supporting the validity of the approach, the corresponding branching ratios cannot be reproduced simultaneously. A first theoretical prediction for the recently measured $η^\prime\toηγγ$ by the BESIII collaboration is also presented.

## Key claim
The scalar and vector meson exchange contributions to the doubly radiative decays η(′)→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ are analyzed within the Linear Sigma Model and Vector Meson Dominance frameworks, respectively; while the shape of the η→π0γγ and η′→π0γγ decay spectra is satisfactorily described, the corresponding branching ratios cannot be reproduced simultaneously.

## Method
The chiral-loop contributions at O(p4) from charged kaon and pion loops were computed using large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory with the η0 as a ninth pseudo-Goldstone boson. Intermediate vector meson exchanges were calculated through the decay chain P0→Vγ→P0γγ using Vector Meson Dominance with full energy-dependent propagators, and scalar meson exchanges were treated within the Linear Sigma Model to provide an all-order estimate of scalar effects including the a0(980), σ(500), f0(980), and κ resonances.

## Key numbers
- For η→π0γγ, the PDG fit value is BR = (2.56 ± 0.22)×10−4 (PhysRevD.98.030001)
- For η→π0γγ, the A2 collaboration measured Γ = (0.330 ± 0.030) eV and BR = (2.54 ± 0.27)×10−4 (Nefkens:2014zlt)
- For η′→π0γγ, the BESIII collaboration measured BR = (3.20 ± 0.07 ± 0.23)×10−3 (Ablikim:2016tuo)
- For η′→ηγγ, the BESIII collaboration set an upper limit BR < 1.33×10−4 at 90% CL (Ablikim:2019wsb)
- The chiral-loop O(p4) contributions to η→π0γγ from pion loops alone gave Γπ(4) = 0.84×10−3 eV, from kaon loops alone gave ΓK(4) = 2.45×10−3 eV, and combined gave Γπ,K(4) = 3.89×10−3 eV (Ametller:1991dp)
- The all-order VMD estimate for η→π0γγ gave ΓVMD = 0.31 eV (Ametller:1991dp)
- The combined chiral plus VMD estimate for η→π0γγ gave Γχ+VMD = 0.42 eV (Ametller:1991dp)
- Including a0 and a2 exchanges gave Γχ+VMD+a0+a2 = 0.42 ± 0.20 eV (Ametller:1991dp)
- The chiral unitary approach gave decay widths of 0.47 ± 0.10 eV (Oset:2002sh) and 0.33 ± 0.08 eV (Oset:2008hp) for η→π0γγ

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of doubly radiative decays beyond the seminal ChPT and VMD calculations by Ametller (1991) for η→π0γγ, providing the first chiral-loop predictions for η′→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ. The analysis uses the Linear Sigma Model to fix the sign ambiguity of scalar meson contributions that was left unresolved in earlier work, and provides a first theoretical prediction for the η′→ηγγ decay recently measured by the BESIII collaboration (Ablikim:2019wsb). The results enable future experimental analyses of these decays at facilities such as BESIII and MAMI to compare with a unified theoretical framework that simultaneously treats vector and scalar resonance contributions.
