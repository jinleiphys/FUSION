# Towards nuclear energy density functionals from first principles

> We discuss the GUDE functionals which consist of pion exchanges derived from chiral effective field theory and a Skyrme-like part. Certain pion terms lead to significant improvements in the description of ground-state energies, indicating they might be useful ingredients for true ab initio energy density functionals. In addition, we present estimates of the statistical parameter uncertainties of the GUDE functionals.

## Key claim

The GUDE functionals, which combine chiral EFT pion exchanges with a Skyrme-like part, show significant improvement in ground-state energy reproduction only when pion contributions beyond NLO are included, but this improvement is contingent on the specific optimization setting and does not follow chiral EFT power counting because only mean-field contributions are considered.

## Method

The GUDE functionals are constructed by adding chiral EFT pion-exchange terms (up to N2LO, with and without Delta isobars and 3N forces) to a conventional Skyrme-type EDF, solved at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov level. Pion-exchange Hartree terms are approximated by sums of Gaussians, while Fock terms are included in quasi-local form using a density-matrix expansion (DME). For each of eight GUDE variants plus a no-chiral reference ("nochi") and a minimal-chiral ("minchi") variant, 14 Skyrme parameters are optimized by minimizing a chi-squared penalty function against 121 data points (binding energies, charge radii, odd-even mass staggerings, fission isomer excitation energies) for 81 even-even nuclei from Ca-40 to Hs-264, with bounds on infinite nuclear matter (INM) parameters.

## Key numbers

*   Chi-squared at optimum: class 0 (nochi) = 122, class 1 (LO, NLO) = 145, class 2 (higher-order variants) = 86-91.
*   Binding energy root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) for all even-even nuclei with Z >= 8 in AME 2020: class 0 = 2.11 MeV, class 1 = 2.09-2.13 MeV, class 2 = 1.41-1.56 MeV.
*   Saturation density for nochi: 0.1546(4) fm^-3; for minchi: 0.1583(9) fm^-3.
*   Saturation energy for nochi: -15.8 MeV (at bound); for minchi: -15.830(17) MeV.
*   Incompressibility for nochi: 260 MeV (at bound); for minchi: 224(7) MeV.
*   Symmetry energy for nochi: 29.9(8) MeV; for minchi: 28.6(3) MeV.
*   Slope parameter L_sym for nochi: 41(16) MeV; for minchi: 30 MeV (at bound, no uncertainty given).
*   Number of data points: n_d = 121; number of fitted Skyrme parameters: n_x = 14.

## Context

This work extends the approach first suggested by Drut et al. (2010) and Gebremariam et al. (2010) of using a density-matrix expansion to incorporate chiral EFT into EDFs, and builds on the GUDE functional family introduced by Zurek (2024). It contrasts with strategies that fit EDF volume terms to ab initio equations of state (e.g., Bulgac et al., 2018) or to neutron drop energies (e.g., Bonnard et al., 2018). The results suggest that specific pion-exchange terms (isoscalar NN Hartree at N2LO and isoscalar NN Fock at LO) are promising ingredients for constructing true ab initio EDFs, but the findings are limited to the specific optimization setting used and should be tested with other protocols.
