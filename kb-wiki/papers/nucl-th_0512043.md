# Parity Nonconservation in Elastic $\vec{p} p$ Scattering

> By looking at the parity-nonconserving (PNC) asymmetries at different energies in $\vec{p} p$ scattering, it is in principle possible to determine the PNC $ρNN$ and $ωNN$ couplings of a single-meson-exchange model of the PNC $NN$ force. The analysis of the experimental data at 13.6, 45 and 221 MeV simultaneously has been performed by Carlson \textit{et al.}, who concluded to an agreement with the original DDH estimates for the PNC meson-nucleon couplings. In this work, it is shown first that the comparison with updated hadronic predictions of these couplings rather suggests the existence of some discrepancy for the PNC $ωNN$ coupling. The effect of variations on the strong coupling constants and introduction of cutoffs in the one-boson-exchange weak potential is then investigated. As expected, it turns out that the resulting asymmetry is quite sensitive to these parameters regardless of the energy. However the above discrepancy persists. The dependence of this conclusion on various ingredients entering an improved description of the PNC $NN$ force is also examined. These include the two-pion resonance nature of the rho meson and some momentum dependence of the isoscalar PNC $ρNN$ vertex. It is found that none of these corrections is able to remove or even alleviate the above discrepancy. Their impacts on the theoretical determination of the vector meson-nucleon couplings, the description of the PNC force in terms of single-meson exchanges, or the interpretation of measurements, are finally examined.

## Key claim

By reanalyzing the parity-nonconserving (PNC) asymmetries in elastic proton-proton scattering at 13.6, 45, and 221 MeV, the authors find that the PNC omega-nucleon coupling derived from experiment disagrees with updated hadronic predictions, and that this discrepancy persists after accounting for variations in strong coupling constants, cutoff form factors, the two-pion resonance nature of the rho meson, and momentum dependence of the isoscalar PNC rho-nucleon vertex.

## Method

The authors calculated the PNC longitudinal asymmetry in elastic proton-proton scattering using the distorted-wave Born approximation, treating the PNC interaction as a first-order perturbation on wave functions solved from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the AV18 strong potential and Coulomb interaction. They systematically varied the strong coupling constants (including tensor couplings kappa_V and kappa_S), introduced monopole form factors at meson-nucleon vertices, replaced the rho-meson exchange with a two-pion exchange contribution incorporating the rho width and intermediate baryon resonances (N, Delta(1232), N(1440), N(1520)), and implemented a momentum-dependent PNC rho-nucleon vertex form factor from the chiral-soliton model. The weak coupling constants h_rho^pp and h_omega^pp were then extracted from a least-chi-squared fit to the experimental asymmetries at all three energies.

## Key numbers

- DDH "best-guess" weak coupling constants: h_rho^pp = -15.5 x 10^-7, h_omega^pp = -3.04 x 10^-7
- Fitted "adj." values from Carlson et al. (2002): h_rho^pp = -22.3 x 10^-7, h_omega^pp = +5.17 x 10^-7
- Strong coupling sets used: S1 (g_rhoNN=2.79, g_omegaNN=8.37, kappa_V=3.70, kappa_S=-0.12, no cutoffs); S2 (same but kappa_V=6.10, kappa_S=0); S3 (same as S1 with cutoffs Lambda_rho=1.31 GeV, Lambda_omega=1.50 GeV); S4 (g_rhoNN=3.25, g_omegaNN=15.58, kappa_V=6.10, kappa_S=0, cutoffs 1.31 and 1.50 GeV)
- Meson masses used: pi=139.0 MeV, rho=771.0 MeV, omega=783.0 MeV
- The measured asymmetry at 221 MeV is missed by a factor of about 2 when h_rho^pp is fixed to reproduce low-energy measurements

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Carlson et al. (2002), who fitted PNC meson-nucleon couplings to the same three experimental data points and found agreement with the original DDH estimates from Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1980). The authors show that comparison with updated hadronic predictions, particularly those from Kaiser and Meissner (1998) using the chiral-soliton model and the ranges from Desplanques (1980) and Feldman et al. (1991), reveals a discrepancy for the omega-nucleon coupling that cannot be resolved by improved treatment of the two-nucleon dynamics. The work enables future studies to focus on whether the discrepancy originates from the hadronic calculations of the weak couplings or from missing physics in the single-meson-exchange description of the PNC nuclear force.
