# Nuclear Binding Energies and NN uncertainties

> There is an increasing interest in quantifying the predictive power in nuclear structure calculations. We discuss how both experimental and systematic errors at the NN-level can be used to estimate the theoretical uncertainties by rather simple means and without solving the full nuclear many body problem. We emphasize the role of effective interactions defined by coarse graining the NN potential to length scales of the order of the minimal de Broglie wavelength probed between nucleons in nuclei. We find an a priori error of \DeltaB/A \sim 0.1 - 0.4MeV for the binding energy per particle throughout the periodic table for 2 \leq A \leq 208, and a linear growth of the error with density for nuclear matter \DeltaB/A \sim 3.75ρn.m. and neutron matter \DeltaB/N \sim 3.5ρn.. This suggests to limit the computational effort in solving the Nuclear Many Body Problem to such an accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that theoretical uncertainties in nuclear binding energies can be estimated a priori from NN-level errors alone, without solving the full many-body problem, by using coarse-grained interactions; the authors find that these NN-level uncertainties provide a lower bound on the total uncertainty, assuming two- and three-body force correlations can be ignored within errors.

## Method

The authors propagate uncertainties from NN potentials by constructing coarse-grained interactions fitted to scattering data up to a maximum CM momentum of about 200 MeV, using harmonic oscillator shell model wave functions for light doubly-closed-shell nuclei and a Skyrme-type effective interaction for heavier nuclei. They compare the spread among six high-quality NN potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Reid93, AV18, CD Bonn, and the covariant spectator model) that each achieve chi-squared per degree of freedom less than or equal to 1, finding that systematic discrepancies among these potentials exceed the statistical errors quoted in the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle uncertainty for triton: Delta B_3/3 = 0.08(1) MeV
- Binding energy per particle uncertainty for He-4: Delta B_4/4 = 0.12(1) MeV
- Binding energy per particle uncertainty for O-16: Delta B_16/16 = 0.28(2) MeV
- Binding energy per particle uncertainty for Ca-40: Delta B_40/40 = 0.34(2) MeV
- A priori error range for binding energy per particle across the periodic table (2 less than or equal to A less than or equal to 208): Delta B/A similar to 0.1-0.4 MeV
- Nuclear matter binding energy error linear with density: Delta B_n.m./A similar to 3.75 rho (with rho in fm^-3)
- Neutron matter binding energy error linear with density: Delta B_n/N similar to 3.5 rho_n (with rho_n in fm^-3)
- Coarse-grained S-wave volume integral t_0 = -0.92(1) GeV fm^3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous error analysis of NN potentials (Perez 2012) and coarse-grained interactions (Perez 2011) to binding energy predictions, providing a method to estimate uncertainties without large-scale ab initio calculations. The approach contrasts with the Trento group's Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Gandolfi 2009), whose quoted uncertainties reflect only many-body solution accuracy and are much smaller than the NN-level errors estimated here. The results suggest that computational effort in solving the nuclear many-body problem should be limited to match the a priori NN uncertainty, and that a more thorough analysis incorporating three-body coarse-grained interactions would provide a more definitive answer.
