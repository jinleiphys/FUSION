# Mass and width of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance using complex-mass renormalization

> We discuss the pole mass and the width of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance to third order in chiral effective field theory. In our calculation we choose the complex-mass renormalization scheme (CMS) and show that the CMS provides a consistent power-counting scheme. In terms of the pion-mass dependence, we compare the convergence behavior of the CMS with the small-scale expansion (SSE).

## Key claim

The complex-mass renormalization scheme (CMS) provides a consistent power-counting scheme for the Delta(1232) resonance at third order in chiral effective field theory, and the resulting pion-mass dependence of the mass and width shows improved convergence behavior compared to the small-scale expansion (SSE), particularly for pion masses below the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors calculated the complex pole position of the Delta(1232) propagator to order O(q^3) in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using the complex-mass renormalization scheme. They evaluated one-loop self-energy diagrams involving pions, nucleons, and Delta intermediate states, subtracted power-counting-violating terms, and solved for the complex pole position z = m_Delta_pole - i*Gamma/2. The results were compared with the small-scale expansion (SSE) and with an alternative Lagrangian formulation from Gegelia (2016).

## Key numbers

- The loop contribution to the Delta pole mass at the physical point: m_Delta_pole^loop = 13.8 MeV (12.2 MeV from diagram a, 1.6 MeV from diagram b)
- The loop contribution to the width at the physical point: Gamma^loop = 2.26 MeV (0.10 MeV from diagram a, 2.16 MeV from diagram b)
- Width in the chiral limit extracted from CMS: Gamma_chi = 149 MeV
- Imaginary part of the low-energy constant c_1^Delta: Im(c_1^Delta) = -0.313 GeV^{-1}
- For the full SSE result, the coupling g_ANDelta = 3.04 reproduces the experimental width at the physical pion mass
- For the LO-SSE result, g_ANDelta = 2.25 reproduces the experimental width at the physical pion mass
- The SSE width in the chiral limit: Gamma_chi = 161 MeV (full SSE) and 160 MeV (LO-SSE)
- The renormalized loop contribution to the real part of the Delta pole vanishes proportionally to M^3 as M approaches 0 in the CMS

## Context

This work extends the application of the complex-mass renormalization scheme, originally developed for electroweak gauge bosons by Stuart (1990) and Denner (1999), to the Delta(1232) resonance in chiral effective field theory. It builds on prior calculations of hadronic resonances using CMS by Djukanovic (2009) and Bauer (2012), and provides a comparison with the small-scale expansion approach of Hemmert (1997) and Hacker (2005). The results enable improved chiral extrapolations of Delta properties for lattice QCD calculations and provide a framework for systematic higher-order calculations of resonance properties in baryon chiral perturbation theory.
