# Collective T- and P- Odd Electromagnetic Moments in Nuclei with Octupole Deformations

> Parity and time invariance violating forces produce collective P- and T- odd moments in nuclei with static octupole deformation. Collective Schiff moment, electric octupole and dipole and also magnetic quadrupole appear due to the mixing of rotational levels of opposite parity and can exceed single-particle moments by more than a factor of 100. This enhancement is due to two factors, the collective nature of the intrinsic moments and the small energy separation between members of parity doublets. The above moments induce T- and P- odd effects in atoms and molecules. Experiments with such systems may improve substantially the limits on time reversal violation.

# Collective T- and P- Odd Electromagnetic Moments in Nuclei with Octupole Deformations

## Key claim

Parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear forces produce collective T- and P-odd electromagnetic moments in nuclei with static octupole deformation that can exceed single-particle moments by more than a factor of 100, but this enhancement depends critically on the existence of nearly degenerate parity doublets and the collective nature of intrinsic moments in the body-fixed frame.

## Method

The authors employ a particle-core model for reflection-asymmetric nuclei, using Nilsson potential calculations to determine mixing coefficients between opposite-parity rotational levels (parity doublets) induced by a T-, P-odd potential. Intrinsic Schiff moments, electric octupole and dipole moments, and magnetic quadrupole moments are calculated in the body-fixed frame using a two-fluid liquid drop model with deformation parameters, then projected into the laboratory frame via rotational wave functions that incorporate T-, P-odd mixing.

## Key numbers

- Schiff moments in the intrinsic system for reflection-asymmetric nuclei are in the range (22–29) e·fm³
- T-, P-odd Schiff moments in the laboratory frame are in the range (2–20) × 10⁻⁶ η e·fm³
- This is about two orders of magnitude larger than the largest single-particle estimate of 4 × 10⁻⁸ η e·fm³ given by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich for ²³⁷Np
- Mixing coefficients are in the range (0.6–7.0) × 10⁻⁷ η
- Octupole deformation β₃ ≈ 0.1 for all calculated isotopes (²²³,²²⁵Ra and ²²³Rn)
- The electric dipole moments of Ra and Rn atoms are estimated to be 10²–10³ times larger than those of Hg and Xe atoms

## Context

This work extends the study of T-, P-odd nuclear moments from single-particle mechanisms, as discussed by Haxton and Henley (1983) and Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984), to collective enhancement in octupole-deformed nuclei. The "spin hedgehog" mechanism for collective magnetic quadrupole moments was previously proposed by Flambaum (1994). The present mechanism enables substantial improvements in limits on time-reversal violation through experiments with atoms such as Ra and Rn, which are electronic structure analogs of Hg and Xe but have octupole-deformed nuclei, and the authors note that nuclei in the Ba-Sm region may also be suitable candidates.
