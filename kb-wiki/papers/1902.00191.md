# Multiple chiral doublet bands with octupole correlations in reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model

> A reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) with a quasi-proton and a quasi-neutron coupled with a reflection-asymmetric triaxial rotor is developed and applied to investigate the multiple chiral doublet (M$χ$D) bands candidates with octupole correlations in $^{78}$Br. The calculated excited energies, energy staggering parameters, and $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios are in a reasonable agreement with the data of the chiral doublet bands with positive- and negative-parity. The influence of the triaxial deformation $γ$ on the calculated $B(E1)$ is found to be significant. By changing $γ$ from 16$^\circ$ to 21$^\circ$, the $B(E1)$ values will be enhanced and better agreement with the $B(E1)/B(E2)$ data is achieved. The chiral geometry based on the angular momenta for the rotor, the valence proton and valence neutron is discussed in details.

## Key claim

The reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) can reproduce the multiple chiral doublet bands in \(^{78}\)Br, but the calculated \(B(E1)/B(E2)\) ratios systematically underestimate the experimental data unless the triaxial deformation \(\gamma\) is increased from \(16.3^\circ\) to \(21.3^\circ\), which enhances the \(B(E1)\) values and yields better agreement.

## Method

A reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) was developed by coupling a quasi-proton and a quasi-neutron to a reflection-asymmetric triaxial rotor. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a symmetrized strong-coupled basis with good parity and angular momentum, using Nilsson single-particle energies with quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.28\), \(\gamma = 16.3^\circ\), and octupole deformation \(\beta_3 = 0.02\) from the microscopic multidimensionally-constrained covariant density functional theory (MDC-CDFT) with PC-PK1. The model was applied to the positive-parity doublet bands (configurations \(\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu g_{9/2}\)) and negative-parity doublet bands (configuration \(\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu f_{5/2}\)) in \(^{78}\)Br, and the calculated excitation energies, energy staggering parameters \(S(I) = [E(I)-E(I-1)]/2I\), and electromagnetic transition ratios were compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.28\) and \(\gamma = 16.3^\circ\) for the \(\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu g_{9/2}\) configuration in \(^{78}\)Br, from MDC-CDFT with PC-PK1.
- Octupole deformation \(\beta_3 = 0.02\) adopted to include octupole correlations.
- Moment of inertia parameter \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 14\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\) and core parity splitting parameter \(E(0^-) = 3\ \text{MeV}\).
- For positive-parity doublet bands in spin region \(9\hbar \le I \le 13\hbar\), the average energy difference is 0.99 MeV, overestimating the data by \(\sim 0.5\) MeV.
- For negative-parity doublet bands in spin region \(6\hbar \le I \le 14\hbar\), the average energy difference is 0.35 MeV, overestimating the data by \(\sim 0.2\) MeV.
- Changing \(\gamma\) from \(16.3^\circ\) to \(21.3^\circ\) (from cranked-shell-model calculations by Landulfo (1996)) enhances the calculated \(B(E1)\) values and improves agreement with \(B(E1)/B(E2)\) data.

## Context

This work extends the triaxial particle rotor model (PRM) used by Liu (2016) to describe chiral doublet bands in \(^{78}\)Br, which could not calculate \(E1\) transitions because octupole degrees of freedom were omitted. The new RAT-PRM includes both triaxial and octupole deformations, enabling the calculation of \(E1\) transitions between positive- and negative-parity bands. The results support the interpretation of multiple chiral doublet bands with octupole correlations and open the possibility of studying chirality-parity quartet bands, as suggested by Frauendorf (2001) and Liu (2016). Future work could include three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) calculations with octupole deformation (Zhao, 2017) or \(g\)-factor measurements (Grodner, 2018).
