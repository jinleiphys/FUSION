# Level density and level density parameter in medium heavy nuclei including thermal and quantal fluctuation effects

> In a realistic application of the SPA + RPA theory for calculation of the nuclear level densities we find that quantal fluctuation corrections (RPA) are important even up to temperature $T = 2.0 MeV$. This leads to a good agreement between calculated numbers and the available experimental data for $^{104}Pd$ and $^{114}Sn$, particularly the excitation energy ($E^*$) dependence. Furthermore, we also argue that $a=S^2/4E^*$ is the only correct definition of the level density parameter in the present context which is also consistent with the Bethe like level density formula.

# Level density and level density parameter in medium heavy nuclei including thermal and quantal fluctuation effects

## Key claim
Quantal fluctuation corrections (RPA) are important for nuclear level density calculations up to temperature T = 2.0 MeV, and the level density parameter a must be defined as a = S²/4E* to be consistent with the Bethe-like level density formula, rather than using the simpler relations a = E*/T² or a = S/2T.

## Method
The authors applied the static path approximation plus random phase approximation (SPA+RPA) approach to compute the grand canonical partition function for a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction model Hamiltonian, using a spherical single-particle basis appropriate for A ≈ 100 nuclei. Level densities were extracted via the saddle point approximation to the partition function, and the level density parameter a was computed from the entropy S and excitation energy E* using the relation a = S²/4E*, then compared to values obtained from the alternative definitions a_e = E*/T² and a_s = S/2T.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁰⁴Pd, the ratio ρ_SPA/ρ_exp ≈ 10² at E* = 10 MeV, reducing to about 10 at E* = 24 MeV; the ratio ρ_SPA+RPA/ρ_exp varies from 1.0 to 1.2 over the entire experimental E* range.
- For ¹⁰⁴Pd at T = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0 MeV, the level density parameter a (from S²/4E*) equals 10.46, 12.92, 12.42, and 11.89 MeV⁻¹ respectively, while the fitted values a_fit are 11.04, 13.12, 12.49, and 11.94 MeV⁻¹.
- For ¹¹⁴Sn, the ratio ρ_SPA/ρ_SPA+RPA ≈ 4.5 at E* = 5.0 MeV and 2.6 at E* = 30 MeV.
- The ground state energy for ¹⁰⁴Pd is −310.603 MeV in SPA and −315.357 MeV in SPA+RPA.

## Context
This work extends the SPA+RPA approach (Puddu et al., Annals of Physics 1991; Lauritzen et al., Physics Letters B 1993) from simple model studies to realistic calculations for medium-mass nuclei, testing against experimental level densities extracted from proton yield measurements (Das et al., Physical Review C 1995) for ¹⁰⁴Pd and ¹¹⁴Sn. The results demonstrate that quantal fluctuations are essential for agreement with experiment up to E* ≈ 25 MeV, and clarify the correct definition of the level density parameter for use in Bethe's formula (Gilbert and Cameron, Canadian Journal of Physics 1965), resolving ambiguities between different definitions used in prior mean-field approaches.
