# $^{35}$Cl+$^{12}$C Asymmetrical fission excitation functions

> The fully energy-damped yields from the $^{35}$Cl+$^{12}$C reaction have been systematically investigated using particle-particle coincidence techniques at a $^{35}$Cl bombarding energy of $\sim$ 8 MeV/nucleon. The fragment-fragment correlation data show that the majority of events arises from a binary-decay process with rather large numbers of secondary light-charged particles emitted from the two excited exit fragments. No evidence is observed for ternary break-up events. The binary-process results of the present measurement, along with those of earlier, inclusive experimental data obtained at several lower bombarding energies are compared with predictions of two different kinds of statistical model calculations. These calculations are performed using the transition-state formalism and the Extended Hauser-Feshbach method and are based on the available phase space at the saddle point and scission point of the compound nucleus, respectively. The methods give comparable predictions and are both in good agreement with the experimental results thus confirming the fusion-fission origin of the fully-damped yields. The similarity of the predictions for the two models supports the claim that the scission point configuration is very close to that of the saddle point for the light $^{47}$V mass-asymmetry-dependent fission barriers needed in the transition-state calculation.

## Key claim
The fully energy-damped yields from the 35Cl+12C reaction at 8 MeV/nucleon arise from a binary fusion-fission process, with no evidence for ternary break-up events. The similarity between transition-state model and Extended Hauser-Feshbach method predictions supports the claim that the scission point configuration is very close to that of the saddle point for the light 47V mass-asymmetry-dependent fission barriers.

## Method
The authors used particle-particle coincidence techniques with ionization chamber telescopes and a Bragg Curve Spectrometer to measure fragment-fragment correlations at a 35Cl bombarding energy of 280 MeV. Inclusive measurements were also performed at several angles between 2.5 and 35 degrees. The experimental elemental excitation functions were compared with two statistical model calculations: the transition-state formalism (TSM) based on saddle-point phase space, and the Extended Hauser-Feshbach Method (EHFM+CASCADE) based on scission-point phase space, both including sequential light-particle evaporation from the fragments.

## Key numbers
- Total fusion-fission cross section: sigma_FF = 25.0 +/- 4.5 mb at E_lab = 280 MeV
- Total evaporation residue cross section: sigma_ER = 763 +/- 100 mb at E_lab = 280 MeV
- Critical angular momentum: L_crit = 27.5 +/- 2.5 hbar at E_lab = 280 MeV
- Average charge deficit: <Delta Z> = 1.74 at E_lab = 280 MeV, compared to <Delta Z> = 0.96 at E_lab = 200 MeV
- Linear relationship for charge deficit: <Delta Z> = 0.048 (E_cm - 37.12), with E_cm in MeV, giving an energy threshold of about 37 MeV for light-charged-particle emission
- Neck length parameter in EHFM: s = 3.0 +/- 0.5 fm

## Context
This work extends the known fusion-fission excitation functions for the 35Cl+12C reaction to higher bombarding energy (280 MeV), building on earlier studies by Beck (1989, 1992) and Djerroud (1992) at lower energies (150-200 MeV). The results confirm the fusion-fission origin of fully-damped yields in the 47V composite system, consistent with findings from other entrance channels such as 31P+16O studied by Ray (1991) and 23Na+24Mg studied by Beck (1993). The absence of ternary events below 10 MeV/nucleon supports the systematic trend established by Winkler (1981) and contradicts claims of three-body processes in 32S-induced reactions at lower energies by Vannini (1988) and Boccaccio (1993). The successful description by both TSM and EHFM enables further studies of mass-asymmetric fission barriers in light nuclear systems.
