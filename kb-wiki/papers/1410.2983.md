# The role of finite-size effects on the spectrum of equivalent photons in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> Photon-photon interactions represent an important class of physics processes at the LHC, where quasi-real photons are emitted by both colliding protons. These reactions can result in the exclusive production of a final state $X$, $p+p \rightarrow p+p+X$. When computing such cross sections, it has already been shown that finite size effects of colliding protons are important to consider for a realistic estimate of the cross sections. These first results have been essential in understanding the physics case of heavy-ion collisions in the low invariant mass range, where heavy ions collide to form an exclusive final state like a $J/Ψ$ vector meson. In this paper, our purpose is to present some calculations that are valid also for the exclusive production of high masses final states in proton-proton collisions, like the production of a pair of $W$ bosons or the Higgs boson. Therefore, we propose a complete treatment of the finite size effects of incident protons irrespective of the mass range explored in the collision. Our expectations are shown to be in very good agreement with existing experimental data obtained at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors present a complete treatment of finite-size effects of colliding protons for the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) that is valid for the full spectrum of invariant masses produced in exclusive photon-photon interactions at the LHC, including high-mass final states such as W boson pairs or the Higgs boson, whereas prior treatments were focused on the low invariant mass domain.

## Method

The authors derive an impact-parameter-dependent equivalent photon distribution \(n(b,\omega)\) for a fast-moving proton, incorporating the proton electric and magnetic form factors. They then compute the cross section for \(pp(\gamma\gamma)\to ppX\) by replacing the factorized photon distributions \(f(\omega_1)f(\omega_2)\) with a double integral over transverse distances \(\vec{b}_1\) and \(\vec{b}_2\) of the product \(n(\vec{b}_1,\omega_1)n(\vec{b}_2,\omega_2)\) multiplied by a survival probability \(P_{\text{non-inel}}(|\vec{b}_1-\vec{b}_2|)\) that accounts for the probability of no inelastic proton-proton interaction. The survival factor \(S_{\gamma\gamma}^2\) is defined as the ratio of the cross section with finite-size effects to that without.

## Key numbers

* The proton two-dimensional transverse radius is taken as \(r_p = 0.64 \pm 0.02\) from H1 experiment measurements.
* The survival factor at zero rapidity reaches values of \(0.3\) at \(W_{\gamma\gamma}=1\) TeV for \(\sqrt{s}=7\) or \(8\) TeV and \(0.43\) at \(W_{\gamma\gamma}=1\) TeV for \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV.
* For exclusive Higgs production (\(M_H=125\) GeV) at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the total cross section is 0.15 fb without survival factor and 0.11 fb with survival factor, giving an average survival factor of 0.74.
* For exclusive \(\mu^+\mu^-\) production with \(W_{\gamma\gamma}>40\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the cross section is 12 pb without and 10 pb with survival factor, average survival factor 0.8.
* For exclusive \(\mu^+\mu^-\) production with \(W_{\gamma\gamma}>160\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the cross section is 36 fb without and 25 fb with survival factor, average survival factor 0.7.
* For exclusive \(W^+W^-\) production at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the cross section is 82 fb without and 53 fb with survival factor, average survival factor 0.65.
* For exclusive \(\gamma\gamma\) production with \(W_{\gamma\gamma}>200\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV, the cross section is 0.06 fb without and 0.04 fb with survival factor, average survival factor 0.64.
* The CMS measurement of the survival factor for exclusive \(\mu^+\mu^-\) production for invariant masses above 11.5 GeV is \(0.83 \pm 0.15\), consistent with the authors' expectation of 0.84.
* The CMS measurement of the survival factor for exclusive \(W^+W^-\) production is about 10% smaller than that for muon pairs above 40 GeV, consistent with the authors' expectation of \(S_{\gamma\gamma}^2 = 0.76\) in that kinematic domain.

## Context

This work extends the impact-parameter-dependent EPA treatment previously encoded in the Starlight Monte Carlo (Klein, 2003), which was focused on the low invariant mass domain around the \(J/\Psi\) mass in heavy-ion collisions and neglected magnetic form factors. The authors provide a complete treatment valid for high-mass final states in proton-proton collisions, enabling accurate cross section predictions for exclusive production of W boson pairs and the Higgs boson at LHC energies. The results agree with existing CMS measurements of the survival factor for exclusive muon pair and W boson pair production (Chatrchyan, 2011; Chatrchyan, 2013).
