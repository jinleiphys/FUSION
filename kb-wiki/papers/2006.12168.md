# Modeling Nuclear Reactions for PET/MRI MultiModal Imaging: the innovative use of 52gMn

> MultiModal Imaging is an innovative technique that consists in the combination of diagnostic exams based on different physical processes, to obtain a unique image with more detailed clinical information. The possibility of a simultaneous use of PET and MRI could be achieved by using a particular radioisotope: 52gMn. The main route to produce this isotope is the reaction 52Cr(p,n)52gMn which is theoretically investigated in this study by means of three nuclear reaction codes. An analysis of the cross sections and a computation of the production rates and time evolution of the produced radioisotopes are performed, to identify the optimal production parameters. The procedure described for this reaction has a general validity and is applied also to other reactions, such as 52Cr(d,2n)52gMn and natV(alpha,x)52gMn.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the reaction 52Cr(p,n)52gMn is a promising route for producing high-purity 52gMn for combined PET/MRI imaging, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of theoretical nuclear reaction codes (TALYS, FLUKA, EMPIRE) and requires further experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors performed theoretical calculations of cross sections for the 52Cr(p,n)52gMn reaction using three nuclear reaction codes: TALYS (with 24 different model combinations), FLUKA, and EMPIRE. They then simulated an irradiation experiment with a 1 microampere beam current, 17 MeV incident energy, 200 micrometer target thickness, and 1 hour irradiation time to compute production rates, isotopic purity, and radionuclidic purity via Bateman equations. The same procedure was applied to other reactions including 52Cr(d,2n)52gMn and natV(alpha,x)52gMn.

## Key numbers

- 52gMn decays by emitting a 242 keV positron with a half-life of about 5.6 days
- The activity per unit of current at End-of-Bombardment for the 52Cr(p,n)52gMn reaction: 6.64 +/- 1.7 MBq/microAh for TALYS, 20.2 MBq/microAh for EMPIRE, and 23.2 MBq/microAh for FLUKA
- All codes predict an isotopic purity greater than 0.8 for at least 20 days after irradiation
- All codes predict a radionuclidic purity close to 1 for at least 150 days after irradiation
- 53Mn has a half-life of about 3.6 x 10^6 years
- The cross section maximum for 52Cr(p,n)52gMn occurs between 10 and 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of 52gMn production within the METRICS project, part of the LARAMED project at INFN-LNL. The study compares three nuclear reaction codes (TALYS, FLUKA, EMPIRE) and identifies the 52Cr(p,n)52gMn, 52Cr(d,2n)52gMn, and natV(alpha,3n)52gMn reactions as the most promising channels. The approach enables optimization of production parameters for 52gMn and can be applied to other radionuclides for multimodal imaging applications, though the authors note that further experimental data are needed to confirm the theoretical results.
