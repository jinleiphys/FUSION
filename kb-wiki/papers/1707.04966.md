# From hadrons to quarks in neutron stars: a review

> We review the equation of state of matter in neutron stars from the solid crust through the liquid nuclear matter interior to the quark regime at higher densities. We focus in detail on the question of how quark matter appears in neutron stars, and how it affects the equation of state. After discussing the crust and liquid nuclear matter in the core we briefly review aspects of microscopic quark physics relevant to neutron stars, and quark models of dense matter based on the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio framework, in which gluonic processes are replaced by effective quark interactions. We turn then to describing equations of state useful for interpretation of both electromagnetic and gravitational observations, reviewing the emerging picture of hadron-quark continuity in which hadronic matter turns relatively smoothly, with at most only a weak first order transition, into quark matter with increasing density. We review construction of unified equations of state that interpolate between the reasonably well understood nuclear matter regime at low densities and the quark matter regime at higher densities. The utility of such interpolations is driven by the present inability to calculate the dense matter equation of state in QCD from first principles. As we review, the parameters of effective quark models -- which have direct relevance to the more general structure of the QCD phase diagram of dense and hot matter -- are constrained by neutron star mass and radii measurements, in particular favoring large repulsive density-density and attractive diquark pairing interactions. We describe the structure of neutron stars constructed from the unified equations of states with crossover. Lastly we present the current equations of state -- called "QHC18" for quark-hadron crossover -- in a parametrized form practical for neutron star modeling.

## Key claim

This review argues that neutron star matter likely undergoes a smooth hadron-quark continuity (with at most a weak first-order transition) rather than a sharp first-order phase transition, and that unified equations of state interpolating between nuclear matter at low densities and quark matter at high densities can simultaneously explain the existence of ~2 solar mass neutron stars and current radius constraints, despite the present inability to calculate the dense matter equation of state from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The authors review the construction of unified equations of state by combining a low-density nuclear equation of state (adopting the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) model for nucleons at densities n_B less than about 2n_0) with a high-density quark matter equation of state based on the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (for n_B greater than about 5n_0), and interpolating in the intermediate density regime where neither description is reliable. They then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation of hydrostatic balance to compute neutron star mass-radius relations, and compare these to observational constraints from neutron star mass measurements and thermonuclear X-ray burst data.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation density is n_0 approximately 0.16 nucleons per fm^3, corresponding to a mass density of about 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3.
* The binary millisecond pulsar J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.928 +/- 0.017 solar masses (originally measured as 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses).
* The pulsar J0348+0432 has a mass of 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses.
* The typical scale of neutron star mass is given by M ~ m_p / alpha_G^(3/2) = 1.86 solar masses, and the scale of radii is (hbar / m_p c) alpha_G^(-1/2) = 17.2 km, where alpha_G = m_p^2 G_N / hbar c approximately 0.589 x 10^(-38).
* Perturbative QCD begins to become applicable for baryon chemical potentials mu_B greater than about 3-6 GeV, corresponding to baryon densities n_B greater than about 10-100 n_0.
* The intermediate density regime where hadron-quark continuity occurs is estimated as n_B between about 2n_0 and (4-7)n_0, based on geometric percolation theory.

## Context

This review extends and synthesizes the line of work on hadron-quark continuity (Schafer 1998, Hatsuda 2006, Masuda 2013) and unified equations of state (Fraga 2013, Kurkela 2014, Kojo 2014) for neutron star applications. It contrasts with the conventional hybrid star picture that assumes a first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark matter via a Maxwell construction (Buballa 2005, Benic 2014, Alvarez-Castillo 2016). The review enables future interpretation of neutron star mass and radius measurements from the NICER experiment and gravitational wave observations from LIGO and Virgo, and provides the QHC18 equation of state in parametrized form for neutron star modeling.
