# Meson resonance gas in a relativistic quark model: scalar vs vector confinement and semishort range correlations

> Smooth transitions from hadronic matter to hot and dense matter of quantum chromodynamics accompany continuous transformations in effective degrees of freedom. The microscopic descriptions should include relativistic quarks interacting inside of hadrons. In this work we construct a schematic constituent quark model with relativistic kinematics which captures the global trends of meson spectra in the light, strange, and charm quark sectors. We examine the roles of the scalar- and vector-confining potentials as well as semishort range correlations in estimating the strength of central, spin-spin, and spin-orbit interactions. The quark dynamics in low-lying mesons is very sensitive to relativistic kinematics and short range interactions, while in high-lying mesons are sensitive to the composition of scalar- and vector-confinement. After expressing mesons in terms of quark wave functions, we use them to describe the quark occupation probability in a meson resonance gas, and discuss how it can be related to its counterpart in a quark-gluon-plasma.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a schematic relativistic constituent quark model that captures global trends of meson spectra across light, strange, and charm sectors, but the authors explicitly state that their work does not have much improvement in reproducing hadronic quantities compared to previous works; instead, the focus is on demonstrating the sensitivity of hadronic spectra and structures to modeling choices in relativistic effects, short-range correlations, and confining potentials, particularly for applications to hadronic matter where hadron structure may change.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic two-body bound state equation for mesons at rest, using a Schrödinger-like equation derived after multiplying by a kinematic factor that depends on the meson mass. Quark kinematic factors (energies and spinor matrix elements) are replaced with average values that are determined self-consistently for each meson state. The effective potential includes scalar and vector confining potentials (with a mixing parameter f_S) and one-gluon-exchange semishort-range correlations, with form factors introduced to regularize ultraviolet singularities from the relativistic vertices.

## Key numbers

- String tension σ ≈ 0.18 GeV² (≈ 0.91 GeV/fm) is used as the standard value extracted from heavy quark spectra and lattice QCD Wilson loops.
- Constituent quark masses used: ~0.3 GeV for light quarks, ~0.5 GeV for strange quarks, and ~1.5 GeV for charm quarks.
- The running coupling α_s is found to be ~0.7–0.8 for light and strange quarks, and ~0.4 for charmed quarks, consistent with an infrared freezing parameterization with m_g ~0.5–0.9 GeV and Λ_IR ~0.2–0.3 GeV.
- The averaging procedure for quark momenta uses grids of 0.01 GeV² without demanding perfect self-consistency; typical |vP| is found to be ≥0.3 GeV for light quarks and ≥0.5 GeV for charm quarks.

## Context

This work follows the methodology of traditional constituent quark models such as De Rujula-Georgi-Glashow (1975), the Cornell model, Godfrey-Isgur (1985), Capstick-Isgur (1986), and the Ebert et al. series (1997–2011), which successfully reproduced meson spectra, widths, and couplings. The present model is intermediate between simple nonrelativistic quark models and more elaborated relativistic models, aiming for flexibility in studying how hadron structure changes in dense or hot matter. The application to a meson resonance gas at finite temperature is intended to connect to hadron resonance gas models (Venugopalan-Prakash 1992, Karsch et al. 2003) and to explore how quark contents in a hadron resonance gas approach those in a quark-gluon plasma across the crossover transition.
