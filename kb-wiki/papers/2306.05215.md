# Description of $^7$Be and $^7$Li within the Gamow Shell Model

> $^7$Li and $^7$Be play an important role in Big Bang nucleosynthesis and nuclear astrophysics. The $^3$H($^4$He,$γ$)$^7$Li radiative capture reaction is crucial for the determination of the primordial $^7$Li abundance. In nuclear astrophysics, lithium isotopes have attracted a great interest because of the puzzled abundance of $^6$Li and $^7$Li. Purpose: In this work we study spectra of $^7$Be, $^7$Li and elastic scattering cross sections $^4$He($^3$He, $^3$He), $^4$He($^3$H, $^3$H) within the Gamow shell model (GSM) in the coupled-channel formulation (GSM-CC). The evolution of channel amplitudes and spectroscopic factors in the vicinity of the channel threshold is studied for selected states. Methods: GSM provides the open quantum system formulation of nuclear shell model. In the representation of GSM-CC, GSM provides the unified theory of nuclear structure and reactions which is suited for the study of resonances in $^7$Be, $^7$Li and elastic scattering cross-sections involving $^3$H and $^3$He projectiles. Results: The GSM-CC in multi-mass partition formulation applied to a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with an effective finite-range two-body interaction reproduce well the spectra of $^7$Be, $^7$Li and elastic scattering reactions: $^4$He($^3$He, $^3$He), $^4$He($^3$H, $^3$H). Detailed analysis of the dependence of reaction channel amplitudes and spectroscopic factors on the distance from the particle decay threshold allowed to demonstrate the alignment of the wave function in the vicinity of the decay threshold. This analysis also demonstrates the appearance of clustering in the GSM-CC wave function in the vicinity of the cluster decay threshold.

## Key claim

The Gamow Shell Model in the coupled-channel formulation (GSM-CC) with multiple mass partitions can simultaneously reproduce the spectra of $^7$Be and $^7$Li and the elastic scattering cross sections $^4$He($^3$He,$^3$He) and $^4$He($^3$H,$^3$H), but only when using two different interactions: the FHT effective interaction for the composite system and the N$^3$LO realistic interaction for the internal structure of the $^3$He and $^3$H projectiles.

## Method

The authors formulated the GSM in a coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) using binary mass partitions: $^4$He + $^3$He and $^6$Li + $p$ for $^7$Be, and $^4$He + $^3$H and $^6$Li + $n$ for $^7$Li. They employed a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with an inert $^4$He core and an effective finite-range FHT two-body interaction optimized for $p$-shell nuclei, while the internal structure of the $^3$He and $^3$H projectiles was calculated separately using the N$^3$LO realistic interaction without three-body forces. The coupled-channel equations were solved using a Berggren basis expansion of the Green's function, and the evolution of channel amplitudes and spectroscopic factors was analyzed as a function of distance from the particle decay threshold.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^3$He calculated with N$^3$LO interaction: -6.35 MeV (experimental: -7.71 MeV)
- Ground state energy of $^3$H calculated with N$^3$LO interaction: -7.14 MeV (experimental: -8.48 MeV)
- Oscillator length used for $^3$He and $^3$H intrinsic states: $b = 1.65$ fm
- Woods-Saxon central potential depth for neutrons: $V_0 = 52.9$ MeV; for protons: $V_0 = 52.2$ MeV
- Woods-Saxon spin-orbit strength for neutrons: $V_{\ell s} = 3.39$ MeV fm$^2$; for protons: $V_{\ell s} = 3.77$ MeV fm$^2$
- Woods-Saxon radius: $R_0 = 2.0$ fm; diffuseness: $a = 0.65$ fm
- Charge radius for Coulomb potential: $R_{\rm ch} = 2.5$ fm
- Model space truncated to excitation energy of $8\hbar\omega$ with at most 2 nucleons in continuum-like states

## Context

This work extends the GSM-CC formalism, previously applied to one-nucleon projectiles (Jaganathen 2014) and deuteron projectiles (Mercenne 2019), to multi-mass partition calculations involving cluster projectiles ($^3$He and $^3$H). It contrasts with the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) approach of Baroni (2013) and the R-matrix theory of Descouvemont (2010) by using a core+valence nucleon picture that yields smaller model spaces while still treating bound states, resonances, and scattering states within a unified framework. The analysis of channel amplitudes and spectroscopic factors near threshold demonstrates the mechanism of wave function alignment with the decay threshold, as previously discussed by Okolowicz (2012, 2013), and enables future studies of radiative capture reactions such as $^3$H($^4$He,$\gamma$)$^7$Li and $^3$He($^4$He,$\gamma$)$^7$Be relevant to Big Bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino physics.
