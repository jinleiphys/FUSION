# High-spin structures as the probes of proton-neutron pairing

> Rotating $N=Z$ nuclei in the mass $A=58-80$ region have been studied within the framework of isovector mean field theory. Available data is well and systematically described in the calculations. The present study supports the presence of strong isovector $np$ pair field at low spin, which is, however, destroyed at high spin. No clear evidence for the existence of the isoscalar $t=0$ $np$ pairing has been found.

## Key claim

The study supports the presence of strong isovector (t=1) proton-neutron pair field at low spin in N=Z nuclei, which is destroyed at high spin, but finds no clear evidence for the existence of an isoscalar (t=0) np-pair condensate; however, due to limitations of the theoretical tools, one cannot completely exclude the possibility of t=0 np-pairing or that rotational properties are not sensitive to this type of pairing.

## Method

Rotating N=Z nuclei in the mass A=58-80 region were studied using isovector mean field theory, which assumes no isoscalar np-pairing but includes isovector np-pairing and isospin symmetry conservation. The cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with the NL3 parameterization and D1S Gogny force was used for low-spin states, while the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) and cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) approaches (assuming zero pairing) were used for high-spin states (I ≥ 15ℏ). Experimental data on excitation energies, transition quadrupole moments, and moments of inertia were compared with these calculations.

## Key numbers

- The [2,2] configuration (double S-band) in ⁷²Kr is well described by CNS and CRMF calculations up to the recently observed I=28ℏ state.
- The transition quadrupole moment Q_t of the [3,3]b configuration in ⁷²Kr is smaller than that of the [3,3]a configuration by 0.5-0.75 eb in the spin range of interest.
- CRHB calculations for configurations A and B in ⁷²Kr have energies approximately 0.7 MeV lower than their unpaired analogs.
- In ⁶⁸Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁶⁰Zn, and ⁷⁶Sr, the proton and neutron g₉/₂ paired band crossings take place simultaneously at ℏω = 0.6–1.0 MeV.
- The side band in ⁷²Kr extends from spin 7⁻ up to spin 29⁻, with configuration changes at I=11ℏ and I=17ℏ.
- In ⁶⁴Ge, the [0,0](α=0) configuration is characterized by deformation (ε₂ ~ 0.2, γ ~ -30°) in the spin range I=2-8ℏ; up to I~35ℏ, yrast lines have deformations ε₂ ≈ 0.25-0.35 and γ=26°-60°; superdeformed bands with ε₂ ~ 0.5, γ ~ 10° become yrast above I~35ℏ.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of rotating N≈Z nuclei published by Afanasjev (2005) and tests predictions that rotational properties (moments of inertia, band crossing frequencies, deformation) could signal isoscalar np-pair condensate, as suggested by Satula and Wyss (1997), Kaneko and Zhang (1998), and others. The study contradicts the prediction by Terasaki, Wyss, and Heenen (1998) that t=0 np-pairing would enhance quadrupole deformation in N=Z nuclei, finding no such enhancement needed to reproduce experiment. It also tests predictions of a Coulomb anti-pairing effect from Gogny-force calculations, finding that available data in even-even N=Z nuclei do not support its existence. The results enable future high-spin studies of ⁶⁴Ge, ⁶²Ga, ⁶⁶As, and ⁷⁸Yb, though the complicated structure of ⁶⁴Ge (γ-softness and terminating bands) may not allow reliable evidence for t=0 np-pairing even with extended data.
