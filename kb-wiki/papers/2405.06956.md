# Nuclear matter and finite nuclei: recent studies based on Parity Doublet Model

> In this review, we summarize recent studies on nuclear matter and finite nuclei based on parity doublet models. We first construct a parity doublet model (PDM), which includes the chiral invariant mass $m_0$ of nucleons together with the mass generated by the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. We then study the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the PDM, which shows that the symmetry energy is larger for smaller chiral invariant mass. Then, we investigate some finite nuclei by applying the Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory to the PDM. We present the root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) of the binding energies and charge radii, and show that $m_0$ = 700 MeV is preferred by the nuclear properties. Finally, we modify the PDM by adding the iso-vector scalar meson $a_0(980)$ and show that the inclusion of the $a_0(980)$ enlarges the symmetry energy of the infinite nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The review finds that a chiral invariant mass m0 of 700 MeV is preferred by nuclear properties when fitting binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei, and that the symmetry energy of infinite nuclear matter is larger for smaller values of m0 and is further enlarged by the inclusion of the iso-vector scalar meson a0(980).

## Method

## Context

This review extends previous work on parity doublet models by systematically studying both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei within a unified framework. The authors apply the Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory, which has been used in prior nuclear structure calculations, to the PDM for the first time. The finding that m0 = 700 MeV is preferred provides a specific constraint for future chiral effective field theory and lattice QCD studies of the nucleon mass decomposition, and the inclusion of the a0(980) meson opens a new direction for modeling the symmetry energy in dense matter relevant to neutron stars.
