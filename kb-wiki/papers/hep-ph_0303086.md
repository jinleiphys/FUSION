# From QFT to DCC

> A quantum field theoretical model for the dynamics of the disoriented chiral condensate is presented. A unified approach to relate the quantum field theory directly to the formation, decay and signals of the DCC and its evolution is taken. We use a background field analysis of the O(4) sigma model keeping one-loop quantum corrections (quadratic order in the fluctuations). An evolution of the quantum fluctuations in an external, expanding metric which simulates the expansion of the plasma, is carried out. We examine, in detail, the amplification of the low momentum pion modes with two competing effects, the expansion rate of the plasma and the transition rate of the vacuum configuration from a metastable state into a stable state.We show the effect of DCC formation on the multiplicity distributions and the Bose-Einstein correlations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified quantum field theoretical framework for the disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) that incorporates both the disorientation of the condensate and the expansion of the plasma, addressing the "DCC trouble list" posed by Bjorken. The authors claim that their background field analysis of the O(4) sigma model with one-loop quantum corrections can describe DCC formation, decay, and signals, but only for two specific cases: when the background field has no disorientation (theta=0) or when the vacuum is maximally misaligned (rho=pi/2).

## Method

The authors use a background field analysis of the O(4) linear sigma model, keeping quantum fluctuations to quadratic order, and couple the system to a Robertson-Walker expanding metric with scale factor a(t) to simulate plasma expansion. They decompose the fields into classical background components and quantum fluctuations, then quantize the resulting Hamiltonian using canonical methods and Bogolubov (squeezing) transformations. Two special cases are analyzed in detail: Case 1 with no vacuum disorientation (theta=0) and Case 2 with maximal misalignment (rho=pi/2), where the Hamiltonian shows explicit mixing between pion and sigma fields parameterized by an angle theta.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts at DCC modeling by Bjorken (1997), Kowalski and collaborators, Gavin, Cooper, Lampert, and Randrup, which the authors claim were incomplete because they did not incorporate disorientation of the condensate and plasma evolution in a unified picture. The paper directly addresses the "DCC trouble list" posed by Bjorken at Trento, including questions about coherent versus squeezed states, links to Bose-Einstein correlations, and quantum number conservation. The framework enables future calculation of multiplicity distributions and Bose-Einstein correlations from first principles in quantum field theory, with the diagonalized Hamiltonians providing the basis for analyzing parametric amplification of low-momentum pion modes.
