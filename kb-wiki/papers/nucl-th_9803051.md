# Toroidal collective motions in the atomic nucleus

> The work deals with one of the topics of collective motion. In the frame of Nuclear Fluid Dynamics, a model which portrays the nuclear matter as a quantum elastic body, the torus-like motions and their associated energies are computed using the thirteen moment approximation. The spin-dependent collective excitations, with toroidal electromagnetic structure, are studied by means of the Generalized Goldhaber-Teller model, with emphasize on the 1$^{-}$ spin-flip mode and its excitation in spherical nuclei by inelastic electron scattering. We discuss the importance of toroidal contributions in the inclusive electron scattering $(e, e')$ and exclusive coincidence electron scattering $(e, e'γ)$. In order to extract the toroidal multipole, we use the backscattering angles in the first mentioned reaction, and the separation of the longitudinal/transverse interference in the second case. The introduction of a quantity which accounts for the deviations from the Siegert theorem, shows the importance of toroidal quadrupole transitions at high-momentum transfer.

# Toroidal Collective Motions in the Atomic Nucleus

## Key claim

The paper argues that toroidal collective motions in nuclei, specifically the dipole torus mode (DTM) and spin-isospin (s-is) resonance, can be identified through electron scattering experiments, but only under specific kinematic conditions: backscattering angles for inclusive (e,e') reactions and longitudinal/transverse interference separation for exclusive (e,e'γ) reactions, with the caveat that toroidal effects are weak for small atomic number Z and become significant only at high momentum transfer where deviations from the Siegert theorem appear.

## Method

The author employs Nuclear Fluid Dynamics (NFD) in the thirteen moment approximation, treating the nucleus as an incompressible quantum elastic body, to derive the displacement fields and energies for toroidal collective modes. The dipole torus mode is obtained by solving the Helmholtz equation for stationary spherical waves and imposing center-of-mass correction, yielding a poloidal displacement field identical to the Hill vortex from classical hydrodynamics. For spin-dependent excitations, the Generalized Goldhaber-Teller model is used to compute the spin-isospin (s-is) resonance, and the Riemann rotational model is applied to study toroidal quadrupole transitions with a rigidity parameter r ranging from irrotational flow (r=0) to rigid rotation (r=1).

## Key numbers

- DTM energy: E(1⁻_tor) = √(21/5) ħω_F ≈ 2ħω, and using realistic Fermi distribution parameters: E(1⁻_tor) = 93.72 A^{-1/3} MeV
- The DTM is predicted to lie between the giant isovector resonance and the giant isoscalar octupole resonance
- For backscattering angles, the s-is differential cross-section exceeds that of the GT resonance at momentum transfer q > 0.5 fm^{-1} for ¹²C and ¹⁶O
- The transverse electric form factor for DTM is given by (1/c²)|F₁^el(k)|² = (1/(9π)^(1/3))√(35/6) (ω/(kc) j₃(kR))² Z²/A^(4/3)
- The critical (stagnation) points of the Hill vortex flow occur at r_c = R/√2 and θ_c = ±π

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Fluid Dynamics approach of previous studies (e.g., the author's own work cited as Misicu (1997) and references therein) to compute toroidal collective excitations, building on the classical concept of the Hill vortex from hydrodynamics. The paper connects to the earlier identification of the anapole moment by Zeldovich as the static toroidal dipole moment, and to the multipole parametrization framework of Dubovik and Cheshkov. The results enable experimental searches for toroidal modes in electron scattering facilities, particularly by measuring deviations from the Siegert theorem at high momentum transfer, and by using the quantity η₂(q) defined in the paper to quantify the importance of toroidal quadrupole transitions relative to the Siegert approximation.
