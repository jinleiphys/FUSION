# Self-bound quark stars with a first-order two-to-three flavor phase transition

> We investigate self-bound quark stars in a flavor-dependent quark-mass density-dependent model with an excluded-volume correction. We chart the parameter space at zero pressure to identify self-bound regimes, including parametrizations in which self-bound two-flavor matter undergoes a genuine first-order $ud \to uds$ transition at finite pressure. We construct cold, $β$-equilibrated stellar sequences and compute the corresponding global properties (mass-radius relation, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia). For a wide region of the model parameter space, we find that the onset of a $uds$ core occurs before the maximum-mass configuration is reached, yielding self-bound hybrid stars that follow the typical strange-quark-star sequence morphology but develop a characteristic kink at $p_c=p_{\rm tr}$ along the stellar curves. The excluded-volume parameter $κ$ controls the stiffness of the equation of state and thus masses, radii, tidal deformabilities, and moments of inertia; intermediate repulsion typically reconciles $M_{\max}\!\gtrsim\!2\,M_\odot$ with current astrophysical constraints. We further identify two equation-of-state-insensitive trends: dimensionless moment of inertia versus compactness and gravitational versus baryonic compactness. These results provide model-guided priors and tools for discriminating between hadronic and self-bound equations of state with multimessenger data.

## Key claim
Self-bound two-flavor quark matter can undergo a genuine first-order phase transition to three-flavor matter at finite pressure within a flavor-dependent quark-mass density-dependent model, producing "self-bound hybrid stars" that consist of a uds core surrounded by a ud mantle. The authors find that for a wide region of the model parameter space, the onset of the uds core occurs before the maximum-mass configuration is reached, yielding stellar sequences that follow the typical strange-quark-star morphology but develop a characteristic kink at the transition pressure.

## Method
The authors employ a flavor-dependent quark-mass density-dependent (QMDD) model with an excluded-volume correction, where each quark flavor's effective mass depends on its own number density as M_i = m_i + C/n_i^(a/3). They chart the parameter space at zero pressure to identify self-bound regimes, then construct cold, beta-equilibrated stellar sequences by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the perturbation equations for tidal deformability and moment of inertia.

## Key numbers
- Reference benchmark for self-bound matter: 930 MeV (energy per baryon of the most tightly bound nucleus, 62Ni)
- Transition pressures for benchmark set (a=1, C=135): 36.8 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.0), 25.8 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.3), 14.7 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.6)
- Energy-density jumps across transition for (a=1, C=135): 132.9 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.0), 93.0 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.3), 53.1 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.6)
- Transition pressures for benchmark set (a=2, C=70): 26.2 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.0), 18.4 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.3), 10.6 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.6)
- Transition pressures for benchmark set (a=3, C=60): 21.1 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.0), 14.9 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.3), 8.7 MeV fm^-3 (kappa=0.6)
- Seidov diagnostic gamma values range from 0.854 to 0.968 across all benchmark transitions, all below the instability threshold of 1.0
- Intermediate repulsion (kappa ~ 0.3-0.6) typically reconciles maximum masses greater than or approximately 2 solar masses with current astrophysical constraints

## Context
This work extends the flavor-dependent QMDD model introduced by Lugones (2022) and the exploratory study of Lugones (2023) that indicated strangeness onset could occur abruptly through a first-order transition. It contrasts with the flavor-blind QMDD variant used in previous astrophysical applications by Lugones (2023, 2024, 2025). The self-bound hybrid stars studied here differ from conventional hybrid stars based on hadron-to-quark phase transitions, as they contain only quark degrees of freedom at all pressures. The results provide model-guided priors for discriminating between hadronic and self-bound equations of state with multimessenger data, complementing recent work by Yang (2025) and Zhang (2025) who investigated similar quark-quark phase transitions but used highly parameterized piecewise prescriptions rather than a unified microphysical framework.
