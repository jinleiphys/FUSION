# Relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions in neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering at MiniBooNE kinematics

> The analysis of the recent neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections measured by the MiniBooNE Collaboration requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final state interactions. In this work we evaluate differential cross sections with the relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation and with the relativistic Green's function model to investigate the sensitivity to final state interactions. The role of the strange-quark content of the nucleon form factors is also discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model can describe the MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic (NCE) neutrino-nucleus cross section data without increasing the axial mass \(M_A\) beyond the world average value of 1.03 GeV/\(c^2\), but this agreement relies on phenomenological optical potentials whose imaginary parts recover strength from inelastic channels that cannot be microscopically disentangled.

## Method

The authors computed flux-averaged differential cross sections for neutral-current quasielastic neutrino scattering on a CH\(_2\) target using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) and the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, both based on Dirac-Hartree bound states from a relativistic mean field. Final-state interactions were treated with complex optical potentials (EDAI and EDAD1 parameterizations) fitted to elastic proton-\(^{12}\)C scattering data; the RDWIA uses the full complex potential in an exclusive knockout picture, while the RGF model reconstructs flux lost to inelastic channels via dispersion relations to produce inclusive cross sections.

## Key numbers

* The MiniBooNE NCE data cover a \(Q^2\) range up to approximately 1.65 (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\).
* The world average axial mass from deuterium data is \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/\(c^2\); the MiniBooNE best fit gives \(M_A = 1.39 \pm 0.11\) GeV/\(c^2\).
* The MiniBooNE extracted strangeness contribution to the axial form factor is \(\Delta s = 0.08 \pm 0.26\).
* RDWIA calculations with \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/\(c^2\) and the EDAI potential underestimate the NCE cross section except for \(Q^2 \ge 0.8\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\); with \(M_A = 1.39\) GeV/\(c^2\) the agreement improves for \(Q^2 \ge 0.6\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^2\).
* The RGF-EDAI calculation with \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/\(c^2\) and \(\Delta s = 0\) gives good agreement with the shape and magnitude of the experimental cross section.
* The NCE/CCQE ratio is enhanced by up to approximately 30% when \(M_A\) is increased from 1.03 to 1.39 GeV/\(c^2\).
* Varying the axial mass modifies the proton-to-neutron (p/n) ratio by up to approximately 10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comparisons of FSI treatments in relativistic models for electron scattering (Meucci 2009) and charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering (Meucci 2011) to the neutral-current channel at MiniBooNE kinematics. The RGF model had already described CCQE MiniBooNE data without increasing \(M_A\) (Meucci 2011), in contrast to models based on the relativistic impulse approximation (Butkevich 2010, 2011) or spectral functions (Benhar 2010, 2011) that require a larger axial mass. The results support the view that multinucleon contributions, as explicitly included in the models of Martini (2009, 2010, 2011) and Nieves (2011), may resolve the apparent discrepancy between deuterium and MiniBooNE axial mass values. The p/n ratio is identified as a sensitive probe of strangeness \(\Delta s\), but its experimental extraction remains challenging due to neutron detection difficulties.
