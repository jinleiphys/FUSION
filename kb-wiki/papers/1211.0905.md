# Heavy Flavor, Quarkonia, and Electroweak Probes at Quark Matter 2012

> We summarize and discuss some of the experimental and theoretical results on heavy flavor, quarkonia, and electro-weak probes presented at Quark Matter 2012.

## Key claim

This conference summary reports that the NLO correction to the photon production rate from the quark-gluon plasma is modest (a "K-factor" deviating from unity by at most 20-30% at \(k/T \sim 2\)), implying that existing phenomenology relying on LO rates need not be redone, but the abstract omits the caveat that this conclusion is conditional on the perturbative analysis being reliable.

## Method

The authors summarize and discuss selected experimental and theoretical results on heavy flavor, quarkonia, and electroweak probes presented at the Quark Matter 2012 conference. They review theory results including NLO calculations of photon emission rates, lattice QCD determinations of the static quark-antiquark potential and charmonium spectral densities, and Langevin simulations of heavy quark energy loss with both collisional and radiative contributions. They also survey experimental measurements of D and B meson nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow, J/psi and Upsilon suppression patterns, and thermal photon and dilepton spectra and flow from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The NLO/LO ratio for the photon production rate deviates from unity by at most 20-30% at \(k/T \sim 2\) (with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(N_f = 3\)).
- At LHC, \(R_{AA}(1S)\) is 0.4-0.5 while \(R_{AA}(2S)\) is 0.1 for Upsilon states in central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.
- At RHIC, \(R_{AA}(1S+2S+3S)\) is about 0.4 in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- The direct photon excess in 0-20% Au+Au at 200 GeV is exponential in \(p_T\) with slope parameter 221 MeV.
- The direct photon excess in 0-40% Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV is exponential in \(p_T\) with slope parameter 304 MeV.
- Charmonium dissolution is observed at \(T = 1.46\, T_c\) in the quenched lattice approximation.
- The initial temperature for QGP evolution is estimated as 300-600 MeV from thermal photon measurements.

## Context

This conference summary extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of the quark-gluon plasma, including NLO photon rate calculations by Arnold et al. (2001) and D. Teaney, and dilepton calculations by Gojko et al. It also reviews lattice QCD advances by Bazavov et al. on the static quark potential and by Ding et al. on charmonium spectral densities. The experimental results from PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, and CMS at RHIC and LHC enable future studies of color screening properties of the QGP, chiral symmetry restoration across a broad beam energy range from FAIR to LHC, and stringent tests of dynamical evolution scenarios in relativistic nuclear collisions.
