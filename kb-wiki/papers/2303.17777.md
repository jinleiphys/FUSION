# $α$ + $^{92}$Zr cluster structure in $^{96}$Mo

> In the evaluation of the half-life of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay ($0νββ$) of a doubly closed-subshell nucleus $^{96}$Zr, the structure of the nucleus $^{96}$Mo is essentially important. The $α$-clustering aspects of $^{96}$Mo are investigated for the first time. By studying the nuclear rainbows in $α$ scattering from $^{92}$Zr at high energies and the characteristic structure of the excitation functions at the extreme backward angle at the low-energy region, the interaction potential between the $α$ particle and the $^{92}$Zr nucleus is determined well in the double folding model. The validity of the double folding model was reinforced by studying $α$ scattering from neighboring nuclei $^{90}$Zr, $^{91}$Zr, and $^{94}$Zr. The double-folding-model calculations reproduced well all the observed angular distributions over a wide range of incident energies and the characteristic excitation functions. By using the obtained potential the $α$ +$^{92}$Zr cluster structure of $^{96}$Mo is investigated in the spirit of a unified description of scattering and structure. The existence of the second-higher nodal band states with the $α$+ $^{92}$Zr cluster structure, in which two more nodes are excited in the relative motion compared with the ground band, is demonstrated. The calculation reproduces well the ground-band states of $^{96}$Mo in agreement with experiment. The experimental $B(E2)$ value of the transition in the ground band is also reproduced well. The effect of $α$ clustering in $^{96}$Mo on the the half-life of the $0νββ$ double-$β$ decay of $^{96}$Zr is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that alpha clustering persists in the ground state of $^{96}$Mo, and that this clustering significantly quenches the half-life of the neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{96}$Zr, but the quantitative effect on the half-life depends on the energy dependence of the real potential due to the dispersion relation, which must be accounted for in the bound-state region.

## Method

The authors determined the interaction potential between an alpha particle and $^{92}$Zr using a double folding model with a density-dependent M3Y nucleon-nucleon force, analyzing nuclear rainbow scattering angular distributions at high energies (40-120 MeV) and backward-angle excitation functions at low energies (21-25 MeV) for $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr and neighboring nuclei $^{90}$Zr, $^{91}$Zr, and $^{94}$Zr. Using the obtained potential, they calculated bound and resonant states of the $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr system in a unified description of scattering and structure, identifying rotational bands by the nodal quantum number $N=2n+L$.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor $N_R$ for the real double folding potential is 1.26 at $E_\alpha$=120, 90, and 65 MeV, and 1.22 at $E_\alpha$=40 and 35.4 MeV for $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr scattering.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair $J_V$ for the real potential ranges from 280.5 MeV fm$^3$ at $E_\alpha$=120 MeV to 329.6 MeV fm$^3$ at $E_\alpha$=23 MeV for $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr.
- The dip in the back-angle excitation function for $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr is predicted at $E_\alpha$=22.7 MeV, following the linear trend $E_\alpha$=54.5 - 0.346$A$ observed for $^{90}$Zr, $^{91}$Zr, and $^{94}$Zr.
- The $N=20$ band (second-higher nodal band) has a bandhead $0^+$ state at $E$=14.4 MeV with rotational constant $k$=0.0492 MeV; the $12^+$ resonance at $E$=22.04 MeV is responsible for the characteristic dip.
- The calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ transition in the ground band is 20.7 W.u., matching the experimental value 20.7$\pm$0.4 W.u.
- The calculated $0^+$ ground state of $^{96}$Mo is overbound by 2.8 MeV (calculated at -5.56 MeV versus experimental -2.76 MeV from the $\alpha$ threshold) when using the potential from $E_\alpha$=23 MeV; using $N_R$=1.182 reproduces the experimental ground-state energy.

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster viewpoint previously applied to the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti by Ohkubo (2021) into the heavier mass region around $A=96$, where no cluster study had been attempted for $^{96}$Mo. It builds on earlier alpha-cluster studies of $^{94}$Mo by Ohkubo (1995), Buck (1995), and Michel (2000), and on the systematic analysis of backward-angle anomalies in alpha scattering. The results enable future experimental confirmation of the predicted dip in the $\alpha$+$^{92}$Zr excitation function and motivate re-evaluation of nuclear matrix elements for $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{96}$Zr using cluster-model wave functions, which may significantly reduce the calculated half-life compared to conventional shell-model, QRPA, and IBM approaches.
