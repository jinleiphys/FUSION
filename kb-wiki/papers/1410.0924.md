# Hadronic observables from a hadronic rescattering model in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We employ a simple kinematic model based on the superposition of $p+p$ collisions, relativistic kinematics, and final-state hadronic rescattering to calculate a number of hadronic observables in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV $Pb+Pb$ collisions. The current model is similar to those used in previous studies, but includes an additional procedure ("squeeze procedure") which modifies the model pseudorapidity distribution to better represent the experimental data. In addition, we vary the model hadronization time ($τ$ = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3 fm/c) to gain a better understanding of our systematic uncertainty. We find that the simple model describes the overall data reasonably well qualitatively, and in some cases quantitatively. Furthermore, the model is found to be robust in the sense that using the squeeze procedure and small variations of the hadronization proper time do not significantly affect our results.

## Key claim
A simple kinematic model based on superposed p+p collisions with final-state hadronic rescattering, including an ad hoc "squeeze procedure" to adjust pseudorapidity distributions, describes Pb+Pb collision data at 2.76 TeV reasonably well qualitatively and in some cases quantitatively, but the authors caution that the model includes only hadronic degrees of freedom and should be treated as a limiting case scenario.

## Method
The model generates p+p collisions using PYTHIA 6.409 at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, then superposes these collisions within the overlap volume of two Lorentz-contracted lead nuclei according to impact parameter. Hadrons are assumed to hadronize after a common proper time τ (varied as 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3 fm/c), after which final-state hadronic rescattering is simulated with a semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation using relativistic kinematics and binary collisions. An ad hoc "squeeze procedure" modifies the pseudorapidity distribution of particles before rescattering to better match experimental data, with the squeeze strength depending on impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- Hadronization proper time τ varied among 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3 fm/c for systematic uncertainty studies
- Scale factor of 1.35 applied to increase number of superposed p+p collisions to match experimental dNch/dη at midrapidity for most central events
- Squeeze procedure parameters: a0 = 0.2, η0 = 4.0, W = 2.0, bmax = 12.10
- 183,594 minimum bias events generated for √sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- Rescattering time step Δt = 0.5 fm/c, calculation carried out to 400 fm/c
- Model underestimates charged-hadron pT distributions in the range pT ~ 1-6 GeV/c for central (0-5%) collisions
- Model v2 results show too much elliptic flow at low pT compared to ALICE data

## Context
This work extends previous hadronic rescattering models applied to RHIC Au+Au collisions (Humanic, 2008), Tevatron p+pbar collisions (Humanic, 2006), and LHC Pb+Pb collisions (Humanic, 2010) by adding the squeeze procedure and varying hadronization time. The model resembles a simplified UrQMD model in cascade mode but with fewer particle species and isospin averaging. The results suggest that final-state hadronic rescattering can mimic some features typically attributed to hydrodynamic evolution, enabling further study of how much collective flow arises from hadronic versus partonic stages.
