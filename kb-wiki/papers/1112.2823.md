# Energy dependence of $K^-$-"$pp$" effective potential derived from coupled-channel Green's function

> We investigate the energy dependence of a single-channel effective potential between the $K^-$ and the "$pp$"-core nucleus, which can be obtained as an $K^-$-"$pp$" equivalent local potential from a coupled-channel model for $\bar{K}(NN)$-$π(ΣN)$ systems. It turns out that the imaginary part of the resultant potential near the $πΣN$ decay threshold can well approximate the phase space suppression factor of $K^-pp \to πΣN$ decay modes. The effects on the pole position of the $π(ΣN)$ state in the $πΣN$ channel are also discussed.

# Energy dependence of $K^-$-"$pp$" effective potential derived from coupled-channel Green's function

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the single-channel $K^-$-"$pp$" equivalent local potential, derived from a coupled-channel $\bar{K}(NN)$-$\pi(\Sigma N)$ model, approximates the phase space suppression factor of $K^-pp \to \pi \Sigma N$ decay modes near threshold only when no bound state exists in the $\pi(\Sigma N)$ channel; if a bound state exists in that channel, the energy dependence differs considerably due to modification of the phase volume via a nearby pole.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel Green's function formalism for $K^-(pp)$-$\pi(\Sigma N)$ systems with energy-independent Gaussian diagonal and coupling potentials. They derived an equivalent local potential $\tilde{U}_{11}^{\rm eff}$ for the $K^-$-"$pp$" channel by eliminating the $\pi(\Sigma N)$ channel, using the ratio of integrated Green's functions weighted by the initial bound-state wave function. The potential strength was extracted via volume integrals and compared to the phenomenological form $U^{\rm eff}(E; r) = (V_0 + i W_0 f(E)) \exp[-(r/b)^2]$.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi \Sigma N$ decay threshold $E_{\rm th}(\pi \Sigma N)$ lies approximately $-100$ MeV below the $K^-pp$ threshold.
- Potential parameters used in the coupled-channel model: $V_1 = -300$ MeV, $V_2 = -150$ MeV, $V_{\rm c} = -100$ MeV, $W_1 = W_2 = 0$ MeV, and range $b = 1.09$ fm.
- For the case with no bound state in the $\pi(\Sigma N)$ channel ($V_2 = -150$ MeV), the imaginary part $W^{\rm eff}(E)$ approximates the phase space factor $f(E)$ with $W_0 = -65$ MeV.
- For the case with a bound state in the $\pi(\Sigma N)$ channel ($V_2 = -300$ MeV), the energy dependence of $W^{\rm eff}(E)$ differs considerably from the phase space factor.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-channel phenomenological studies (Koike 2007, 2009) of the $K^-pp$ system, which used a "moving pole" picture to describe the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$, $n$) reaction spectrum planned for the J-PARC E15 experiment. The coupled-channel approach validates whether the energy-dependent phase space factor used in those earlier calculations is appropriate near the $\pi \Sigma N$ decay threshold. The results enable more refined DWIA calculations of the inclusive and semi-exclusive spectra, and the authors note that further investigation incorporating energy-dependent potentials from chiral $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ dynamics (Jido 2003) is in progress.
