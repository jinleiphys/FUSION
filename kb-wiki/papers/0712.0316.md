# Kaon production at subthreshold energies - what do we learn about the nuclear medium ?

> The Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics model is used to compare spectra and elliptic flow of kaons produced at subthreshold energies with data taken at the SIS accelerator at GSI. We find that temperatures of the spectra are dominated by the rescattering of the kaons. The study of elliptic flow observables indicates the influence of rescattering as well as of the optical potential of the kaons with increasing dominance of the optical potential at lower incident energies.

# Kaon production at subthreshold energies - what do we learn about the nuclear medium?

## Key claim

The temperatures of kaon spectra are dominated by rescattering with the nuclear medium, but the optical potential (repulsive for K+ and attractive for K-) provides a final push that leaves a trace on the spectra and elliptic flow, with the optical potential becoming increasingly dominant at lower incident energies.

## Method

The Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.48 AGeV incident energy, supplemented with cross sections for kaon production and annihilation and a density- and momentum-dependent KN optical potential parametrized from relativistic mean field calculations by Schaffner-Bielich. The authors compared simulated K+ and K- spectra, temperatures, and elliptic flow (v2) with data from the KaoS collaboration at GSI, analyzing the contributions of rescattering and the optical potential separately by tracking particles that collided frequently versus those that did not scatter.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 1.48 AGeV for Au+Au collisions
- Kaon spectra measured in the center-of-mass frame over the range of about 100-500 MeV
- K+ temperatures: directly after production show a "quite low temperature" (no specific value given); final temperatures after rescattering and potential are higher
- K- temperatures: absorption "eats up" low energy kaons, imitating higher temperatures; the attractive potential reduces the final temperature below that of K+
- v2 for K+ in semiperipheral Au+Au: calculations without potential nor rescattering yield v2 = 0; calculations with rescattering but without optical potential show less significant effect than calculations with potential but without rescattering
- The effect of the optical potential on v2 becomes more evident at lower incident energies (no specific energy values given for the excitation function)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the KaoS and FOPI collaborations (Barth, Menzel, Ritman, Crochet, Devismes, Laue, Sturm, Foerster, Uhlig) and theoretical work by Schaffner-Bielich, Cassing, and others on subthreshold kaon production as a probe of dense nuclear matter. The analysis demonstrates that while rescattering dominates the spectral temperatures, the optical potential leaves observable signatures in both the temperature difference between K+ and K- and in the elliptic flow, particularly for kaons that leave the system early. This enables future studies to disentangle rescattering and potential effects by comparing observables across different incident energies and system sizes, as planned in a forthcoming publication by the same authors.
