# Analysis of the $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α,γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ astrophysical direct capture reactions in a modified potential-model approach

> Astrophysical $S$ factors and reaction rates of the direct radiative capture processes $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ and $^{3}{\rm H}(α,γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$, as well as the primordial abundance of the $^{7}{\rm Li}$ element, are estimated in the framework of a modified two-body potential model. It is shown that suitable modification of phase-equivalent $α-^{3}{\rm He}$ potentials in the $d$ waves can improve the description of the astrophysical $S$ factor for the direct $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ radiative capture reaction at energies above 0.5 MeV. An estimated $^{7}{\rm Li/H}$ abundance ratio of $(4.89\pm 0.18 )\times 10^{-10}$ is in very good agreement with the recent measurement of $(5.0\pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}$ by the LUNA collaboration.

# Analysis of the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be and ³H(α,γ)⁷Li astrophysical direct capture reactions in a modified potential-model approach

## Key claim
By suitably modifying the depth and width parameters of phase-equivalent α-³He potentials specifically in the d₃/₂ and d₅/₂ partial waves, the potential model can simultaneously describe the astrophysical S factor for the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be direct capture reaction at both low energies (below 0.5 MeV) and intermediate energies (above 0.5 MeV), which previous potential-model calculations had underestimated.

## Method
The authors used a two-body potential model with Gaussian-form central potentials for the α-³He and α-³H interactions, solving the Schrödinger equation in the entrance and exit channels with the Numerov method. They adjusted the depth (V₀) and width (α₀) parameters of the d-wave potentials while keeping the potentials phase-equivalent to experimental scattering phase shifts, and applied the same modified potentials without further fitting to the mirror reaction ³H(α,γ)⁷Li. The E1, E2, and M1 transition contributions were calculated, and reaction rates were fed into the PArthENoPE code to estimate the primordial ⁷Li abundance.

## Key numbers
- For the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be reaction, the modified potential models V_D^n and V_M1^n improve the description of the astrophysical S factor at energies above 0.5 MeV, where previous calculations had underestimated the data.
- The V_D^n model yields asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) of C(3/2⁻)=4.34 fm⁻¹/² and C(1/2⁻)=3.71 fm⁻¹/² for ⁷Be bound states.
- The V_M1^n model yields ANC values of C(3/2⁻)=4.785 fm⁻¹/² and C(1/2⁻)=4.242 fm⁻¹/² for ⁷Be bound states.
- The estimated ⁷Li/H primordial abundance ratio is (4.930 ± 0.129)×10⁻¹⁰ within the V_D^n model and (4.842 ± 0.126)×10⁻¹⁰ within the V_M1^n model.
- The combined ⁷Li/H abundance ratio from both capture reactions is (4.89 ± 0.18)×10⁻¹⁰, in agreement with the LUNA collaboration measurement of (5.0 ± 0.3)×10⁻¹⁰.
- The binding energies reproduced for ⁷Li are E_b(3/2⁻)=2.467 MeV and E_b(1/2⁻)=1.990 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the potential-model approach of Tursunov et al. (2018) for the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be and ³H(α,γ)⁷Li reactions, which described low-energy data well but underestimated the S factor at intermediate energies. The modified d-wave potentials bring the calculations into agreement with experimental data from the LUNA collaboration and other groups across a wide energy range from tens of keV to a few MeV. The results are comparable to ab-initio calculations from Neff (2011) and Dohet-Eraly et al. (2016), and the reaction rates lie between the microscopic R-matrix results of Descouvemont (2004) and the NACRE II 2013 compilation. The improved reaction rates enable more reliable predictions of primordial ⁷Li abundance in Big Bang nucleosynthesis models, addressing the so-called lithium problem.
