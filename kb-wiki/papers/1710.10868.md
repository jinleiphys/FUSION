# $γγ\to γγ$ scattering in ultrarelativistic UPC

> We discuss diphoton semi(exclusive) production in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV (LHC) and in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s_{pp}}$ = 7 TeV (LHC) and $\sqrt{s_{pp}}$ = 100 TeV (FCC). The nuclear calculations are based on equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. The cross sections for elementary $γγ\to γγ$ subprocess are calculated including three different mechanisms: box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops, a VDM-Regge contribution with virtual intermediate hadronic excitations of the photons and the two-gluon exchange contribution. We get relatively high cross sections in heavy ion collisions. This opens a possibility to study the light-by-light (quasi)elastic scattering at the LHC. We find that the cross section for elastic $γγ$ scattering could be measured in the lead-lead collisions for the diphoton invariant mass up to $W_{γγ} \approx$ 15 - 20 GeV. Our Standard Model predictions are compared to a recent ATLAS experimental result. We present differential distributions for PbPb$\to$PbPb$γγ$ and pp$\to$pp$γγ$ reaction.

# γγ → γγ scattering in ultrarelativistic UPC

## Key claim

The cross section for elastic photon-photon scattering in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at LHC energies is large enough to be measured for diphoton invariant masses up to approximately 15–20 GeV, but the VDM-Regge contribution extends to rapidities around |y| ≈ 5, which lies outside the main detector coverage of ATLAS and CMS and below the acceptance of their zero-degree calorimeters.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear cross sections using the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space with a five-fold integral, incorporating a realistic nuclear charge form factor and an absorption factor. The elementary γγ → γγ subprocess cross section is computed from three mechanisms: box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops (using FormCalc and LoopTools), a VDM-Regge contribution with virtual hadronic excitations of the photons, and a two-gluon exchange contribution. For proton-proton collisions, the cross section is calculated in a parton-model form using elastic photon distributions from proton electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Lead-lead collisions at √s_NN = 5.5 TeV: the cross section for elastic γγ scattering could be measured for diphoton invariant mass up to W_γγ ≈ 15–20 GeV
- For the VDM-Regge mechanism in PbPb collisions, maxima of the cross section occur at |y_γ1|, |y_γ2| ≈ 5, which is below the ZDC acceptance for ATLAS (|η| > 8.3) and CMS (|η| > 8.5)
- With assumed integrated luminosity L_int = 1 nb⁻¹ typical for UPC at the LHC, the invariant mass distribution can be measured up to M_γγ ≈ 15 GeV
- The two-gluon exchange contribution dominates over the box contribution only above M_γγ > 50 GeV for 1 GeV < p_t < 2 GeV
- ATLAS measured fiducial cross section at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: 70 ± 20 (stat.) ± 17 (syst.) nb, compatible with the authors' predicted value of 49 ± 10 nb for ATLAS cuts

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier calculation (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2016) which gave an order-of-magnitude larger cross section than the earlier calculation by d'Enterria and da Silveira (2013, with erratum). The theoretical predictions motivated the ATLAS Collaboration to perform experimental studies, which recently observed 13 events for light-by-light scattering in Pb-Pb UPC. The results enable future measurements of elastic photon-photon scattering at the LHC and FCC, with identified phase-space regions where the two-gluon contribution is enhanced relative to the box contribution (large rapidity difference between photons and intermediate transverse momenta 1 GeV < p_t < 2–5 GeV).
