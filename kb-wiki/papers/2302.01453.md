# Studies of the equation-of-state of nuclear matter by heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energy in the multi-messenger era

> The study of the equation-of-state (EoS) describing the properties of nuclear matter away from the normal conditions is a relevant and intriguing topic of modern nuclear physics. The last decades have witnessed a substantial experimental progress in derivation of the symmetric matter term of the EoS and of the so-called symmetry energy for the asymmetric matter, especially at densities below the saturation point. But it is only in recent years that the opening of the multi-messenger astronomy era, triggered by detection of gravitational waves due to the neutron star mergers, has renewed and enlarged the interest in high-density EoS, being the main ingredient for determining the structure and properties of neutron stars. In this paper we review our knowledge obtained from heavy-ion collisions up to the 1 GeV/nucleon regime, on the EoS above nuclear saturation density. Special emphasis is given on the still few results on symmetry energy at high densities and their interconnections with multi-messenger astronomy findings.

## Key claim

This review argues that heavy-ion collision experiments at intermediate energies (up to about 2 GeV per nucleon) provide essential constraints on the nuclear equation-of-state above saturation density, particularly for the symmetry energy, and that these terrestrial constraints are now becoming complementary to multi-messenger astrophysical observations of neutron stars, though the number of high-density symmetry energy measurements remains limited and model-dependent.

## Method

The authors review experimental results from heavy-ion collisions at the GSI SIS18 accelerator and the RIKEN laboratory, focusing on observables sensitive to the equation-of-state above nuclear saturation density. For the isospin-symmetric part of the EoS, they examine constraints from kaon production, pion production, and collective flow measurements (directed and elliptic flow of protons and light clusters). For the symmetry energy, they analyze charged pion ratios and neutron-to-charged particle elliptic flow ratios, comparing these results to transport model simulations using Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) and Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) frameworks. The review also compares heavy-ion collision constraints on neutron star radii with results from gravitational wave detections (GW170817, GW190425) and NICER X-ray observations.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: approximately 32 MeV (the contribution to energy per nucleon for a neutron-rich Au nucleus is about 1.25 MeV, roughly 8% of the isospin-symmetric contribution)
- From the combined fit by Lynch and Tsang (2021) including low-density constraints, PREX-II, and pion results: symmetry energy magnitude S_01 = 24.0 ± 0.5 MeV and slope L_01 = 54 ± 6 MeV at density ρ_01 = 0.1 fm⁻³
- The tidal polarizability Λ scales approximately as neutron star radius to the fifth power (Λ ∼ R⁵)
- Heavy-ion collision energies reviewed range from several hundred MeV up to about 2 GeV per nucleon

## Context

This review extends and synthesizes decades of work on the nuclear equation-of-state, building on foundational studies such as Danielewicz et al. (2002) who used directed transverse flow to constrain symmetric matter EoS, and the topical issue on nuclear symmetry energy in European Physical Journal A (2014). The paper connects terrestrial heavy-ion collision results from GSI and RIKEN to the multi-messenger astronomy era opened by gravitational wave detections (LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2017) and NICER observations (Riley, 2019; Miller, 2019). The work enables future combined analyses that simultaneously use heavy-ion collision data, astrophysical observations, and theoretical calculations to constrain neutron star properties, as illustrated by the multiple source analysis discussed in the review.
