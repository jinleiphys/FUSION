# Model Comparisons of Transverse Energy and Charged-Particle Multiplicity in A+A Collisions at Midrapidity from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $=$ 7.7 to 200~GeV

> We present a comprehensive comparison of PHENIX measurements of transverse energy production ($dE_T/dη$) and charged-particle multiplicity ($dN/dη$) at midrapidity to simulations from PYTHIA-8, AMPT, HIJING, and SMASH. These comparisons span both small systems (d+Au, $^3$He+Au) and large systems (Cu+Cu, Cu+Au, Au+Au, and U+U) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ $\sim$ 200~GeV and Au+Au over a range of beam energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$--200~GeV. Using the Rivet framework, we assess the performance of these models. While general trends are captured, significant deviations persist, particularly in low-energy and peripheral collisions, underscoring the need for improved modeling of baryon stopping and energy deposition mechanisms.

## Key claim

While PYTHIA-8, AMPT, HIJING, and SMASH capture general trends of transverse energy and charged-particle multiplicity across collision systems and energies, significant deviations persist particularly in low-energy and peripheral collisions, indicating that all models require improved treatment of baryon stopping and energy deposition mechanisms.

## Method

The authors implemented Rivet framework analyses for PHENIX measurements of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse energy at midrapidity. They generated approximately 5.0 million minimum bias events per system using PYTHIA-8 Angantyr, AMPT (v2.26t9b), HIJING (v1.411), and SMASH (3.1) for Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, plus Cu+Cu, Cu+Au, U+U, d+Au, and 3He+Au at 200 GeV. Centrality was determined using a calibration analysis that records multiplicities in the PHENIX beam-beam counter region (3.1 < |eta| < 3.9) and defines centrality as a percentile of the total collision cross section.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV: both dE_T/dη and dN/dη show a monotonic decrease with decreasing beam energy across centrality selections 0-5%, 15-20%, and 35-40%
- For the correlation between dE_T/dη and dN/dη at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 200 GeV across systems from d+Au to U+U: slopes for data, PYTHIA, HIJING, and SMASH agree within 5%, while AMPT is lower than data by about 25%
- Deviations of about 10% are observed in the dE_T/dη versus dN/dη correlation ratios, particularly for small systems

## Context

This work extends PHENIX measurements of transverse energy and charged-particle multiplicity (PHENIX 2001, 2013, 2015) by providing systematic comparisons to four event generators using the standardized Rivet framework. The study spans both small systems (d+Au, 3He+Au) and large systems (Cu+Cu, Cu+Au, Au+Au, U+U) at 200 GeV and Au+Au from 7.7 to 200 GeV, enabling tests of models across the QCD phase diagram from high temperature/low baryon chemical potential to higher baryon chemical potential regimes. The results enable future Rivet-based studies of more refined observables such as particle correlations to further distinguish between theoretical approaches.
