# Meson-nucleon scattering and vector mesons in nuclear matter

> The properties of vector mesons in nuclear matter are discussed. I examine the constraints imposed by elementary processes on the widths of $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons in nuclear matter. Furthermore, results for the $ρ$- and $ω$-nucleon scattering amplitudes obtained by fitting meson-nucleon scattering data in a coupled-channel approach are presented.

# Meson-nucleon scattering and vector mesons in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The low-density theorem can be used to extract vector-meson self-energies in nuclear matter from elementary meson-nucleon scattering data, but the results are constrained by the limited availability of threshold data, particularly for the rho meson, where the large width makes identification close to threshold very difficult and introduces large uncertainties.

## Method
A coupled-channel approach was employed, including the channels pi N, rho N, omega N, pi Delta, and eta N, with only s-wave scattering considered in the rho N and omega N channels and s- and d-waves in the pi N and pi Delta channels. The model used an effective Lagrangian with 4-point meson-meson-baryon-baryon interactions, fitting data in the energy range 1.45 GeV to 1.8 GeV in sqrt(s). For the omega meson, the imaginary part of the forward-scattering amplitude was extracted from a fit to the pi- p -> omega n cross section data near threshold, expanded as a + b q_omega^2 + c q_omega^4.

## Key numbers
- The imaginary part of the omega-nucleon forward-scattering amplitude from the pi- p channel was fitted with coefficients a = 0.013 fm, b = 0.10 fm^3, and c = -0.08 fm^5, valid from threshold up to q = 120 MeV/c.
- At nuclear-matter density, the width of the omega meson is increased by 9 MeV due to the pi-nucleon channel alone.
- The rho-nucleon scattering length is (-0.2 + 0.7i) fm; the omega-nucleon scattering length is (-0.5 + 0.1i) fm.
- At nuclear matter density, the in-medium modifications are: Delta m_rho approximately 20 MeV, Delta m_omega approximately 50 MeV, Delta Gamma_rho approximately 140 MeV, and Delta Gamma_omega approximately 20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the low-density theorem approach of LDT to vector mesons in nuclear matter, building on earlier studies of rho-meson broadening in matter by HFN, CS, AK, and KKW, and the momentum dependence of the rho-meson self energy due to baryon-resonance-nucleon-hole states by FP and PLPM. The results support the dynamical scenario discussed by Brown, where vector-meson strength is split into a meson-like mode pushed up in energy and a resonance-hole-like mode pushed down, contributing to the low-mass enhancement in lepton-pair spectra observed in SPS nucleus-nucleus collisions by the Helios collaboration. The analysis also highlights the need for new threshold data on rho production, as the existing data from Manley and Saleski are afflicted with large uncertainties.
