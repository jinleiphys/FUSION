# Dilepton production in proton-proton collisions at top RHIC energy

> We study dielectron production in proton-proton collisions at top RHIC beam energy within an extended statistical hadronization model. The invariant mass spectrum of correlated dielectron pairs is evaluated in the low invariant mass region and calculated results are compared with the PHENIX experiment. The model is found to be able to describe the data very well up to invariant masses of 1 GeV with few adjustable parameters.

## Key claim

The extended statistical hadronization model (SHM) can describe the PHENIX dielectron invariant mass spectrum in proton-proton collisions at top RHIC energy up to 1 GeV, but only after introducing a two-dimensional Gaussian momentum distribution for clusters to account for acceptance effects not captured by the SHM itself.

## Method

The authors used a grand-canonical statistical hadronization model with three free parameters (temperature T, strangeness suppression factor gamma_S, and system volume V) fitted to STAR data for p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. They calculated primary yields of approximately 300 hadron resonances, sampled event-by-event Poisson distributions, then let unstable resonances decay into stable hadrons and vector mesons, which subsequently decay into dielectrons via direct, Dalitz, and other channels. To compare with PHENIX data uncorrected for acceptance, they modeled initial parton dynamics by assuming a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution for cluster momentum, fitting the widths to reproduce measured pion distributions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 170 MeV and strangeness suppression factor gamma_S = 0.6, fitted to STAR data for p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- The model describes PHENIX data well up to invariant masses of 1 GeV
- A Gaussian smearing filter with resolution of 10 MeV was applied to calculated curves to account for experimental mass resolution
- The open charm decay contribution is negligible at all invariant masses except between the omega and phi meson peaks

## Context

This work extends standard hadronic freeze-out cocktail calculations (which typically include only a few hadron species with yields taken from experiment or put in by hand) by implementing a statistical hadronization model with most known resonances, using only three free parameters. The approach builds on prior SHM applications to hadronic yields and rapidity densities in p+p collisions at SPS and RHIC by Bratkovskaya and collaborators (2004-2007). The results provide a baseline for understanding the unexplained dielectron excess observed in heavy-ion collisions by PHENIX, which the authors plan to address in a forthcoming publication.
