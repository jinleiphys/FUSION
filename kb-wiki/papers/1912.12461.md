# Hadron properties in a nuclear medium and effective nuclear force from quarks: the quark-meson coupling model

> We give a short review of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the quark-based model of finite nuclei and hadron interactions in a nuclear medium, highlighting on the relationship with the Skyrme effective nuclear forces. The model is based on a mean field description of nonoverlapping nucleon MIT bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of Lorentz-scalar-isoscalar, Lorentz-vector-isoscalar, and Lorentz-vector-isovector meson fields directly coupled to the light quarks up and down. In conventional nuclear physics the Skyrme effective forces are very popular, but, there is no satisfactory interpretation of the parameters appearing in the Skyrme forces. Comparing a many-body Hamiltonian generated by the QMC model in the zero-range limit with that of the Skyrme force, it is possible to obtain a remarkable agreement between the Skyrme force and the QMC effective interaction. Furthermore, it is shown that 3-body and higher order N-body forces are naturally included in the QMC-generated effective interaction.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, a quark-based description of finite nuclei and hadron interactions in a nuclear medium, can generate an effective many-body Hamiltonian whose zero-range limit shows remarkable agreement with the Skyrme effective force, while naturally including 3-body and higher-order N-body forces that are absent in the conventional Skyrme parametrization.

## Method

The QMC model describes nucleons and hyperons as nonoverlapping MIT bags whose light quarks (up and down) couple self-consistently to Lorentz-scalar-isoscalar (sigma), Lorentz-vector-isoscalar (omega), and Lorentz-vector-isovector (rho) mean meson fields. The coupling constants for these mesons to light quarks in any hadron are the same as those in the nucleon, fixed by nuclear matter saturation properties. The many-body Hamiltonian derived from the QMC model is expanded in a zero-range limit and compared term-by-term with the Skyrme effective force Hamiltonian, using the scalar polarizability of the nucleon (the density-dependent response of the nucleon mass to the sigma field) as the key ingredient that generates many-body forces.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation binding energy: 15.7 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear incompressibility from the QMC model: K approximately 280 MeV (contrasting with naive QHD which gives K approximately 500 MeV)
- Nucleon-level coupling constants: (g^N_sigma)^2/4pi = 3.12, g^2_omega/4pi = 5.31, g^2_rho/4pi = 6.93
- Quark-level coupling constants: g^q_sigma = 5.69, g^q_omega = 2.72, g^q_rho = 9.33
- Input meson masses: m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV
- Free nucleon mass: m_N = 939 MeV, with bag radius R_N = 0.8 fm and current quark mass m_q = 5 MeV
- Bag pressure: B_p^{1/4} = 170 MeV
- Slope parameter for the nucleon scalar polarizability: a_N = 9.1 x 10^{-4} MeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the QMC model originally developed by Guichon (1987) and reviewed by Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas (2005) and Krein, Thomas, and Tsushima (2017), by explicitly connecting it to the Skyrme effective force framework widely used in conventional nuclear physics. The QMC model provides a quark-level interpretation for the parameters of the Skyrme force, which previously lacked a satisfactory microscopic foundation. This connection enables future studies of finite nuclei and neutron star structure using the QMC model's density-dependent effective interaction, with the natural inclusion of many-body forces that are otherwise added phenomenologically in Skyrme-type calculations.
