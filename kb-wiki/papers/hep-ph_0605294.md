# The QCD collisional energy loss revised

> It is shown that to leading order the QCD collisional energy loss reads $dE/dx \sim α(m_D^2)T^2$. Compared to prevalent expressions, $dE^B/dx \sim α^2 T^2 \ln(ET/m_D^2)$, which could be considered adaptions of the (QED) Bethe-Bloch formula, the rectified result takes into account the running coupling, as dictated by renormalization. As one significant consequence, due to asymptotic freedom, the collisional energy loss becomes independent of the jet energy $E$. Some implications with regard to heavy ion collisions are pointed out.

## Key claim

The QCD collisional energy loss, when renormalization is properly accounted for, becomes proportional to \(\alpha(m_D^2)T^2\) rather than the previously assumed \(\alpha^2 T^2 \ln(ET/m_D^2)\). This revision implies that, due to asymptotic freedom, the collisional energy loss becomes independent of the jet energy \(E\) for sufficiently hard jets, a qualitative departure from the QED-like Bethe-Bloch formula.

## Method

The author re-examines Bjorken's (1982) derivation of the collisional energy loss for a jet propagating through a static quark-gluon plasma, replacing the fixed coupling \(\alpha\) with a running coupling \(\alpha(t)\) whose scale is set by the virtuality \(t\) of the exchanged gluon. Using renormalization group arguments and the Callan-Symanzik equation, the tree-level differential cross section is modified by substituting \(\alpha \rightarrow \alpha(t)\), which renders the integral over momentum transfer UV-finite and eliminates the logarithmic dependence on jet energy. The Debye mass \(m_D\) is determined self-consistently from an implicit equation involving the running coupling at the screening scale.

## Key numbers

* For a light quark jet, the revised collisional energy loss is \(dE_q/dx = \frac{2}{3} \cdot 2\pi (1 + \frac{1}{6} n_f) \frac{\alpha(m_D^2)}{b_0} T^2\), where \(b_0 = (11 - \frac{2}{3} n_f)/(4\pi)\) and \(n_f\) is the number of light flavors.
* For a gluon jet, the prefactor is multiplied by \(\frac{9}{4}\) relative to the quark case, giving \(dE_g/dx = \frac{3}{2} \cdot 2\pi (1 + \frac{1}{6} n_f) \frac{\alpha(m_D^2)}{b_0} T^2\).
* The Debye mass is given to leading order by \(m_D^2 = (1 + \frac{1}{6} n_f) 4\pi \alpha(m_D^2) T^2\), which agrees quantitatively with lattice QCD calculations down to \(T \approx 1.2 T_c\) with \(\Lambda = 205\) MeV for \(n_f = 2\).
* The critical temperature is assumed as \(T_c = 160\) MeV, with an uncertainty band for the IR cut-off of \(\mu^2 = [\frac{1}{2}, 2] m_D^2\).

## Context

This work revises the foundational collisional energy loss formula of Bjorken (1982) and the HTL-based calculation of Braaten and Thoma (1991), which both assumed a fixed coupling and produced a logarithmic dependence on jet energy. The rectified result, proportional to \(\alpha(m_D^2)\) rather than \(\alpha^2\), provides a consistent explanation for phenomenologically inferred cross sections of order 10 mb (Molnar 2001, Peshier 2005) and aligns with the concept of a strongly coupled QGP (sQGP). The approach also connects to lattice QCD calculations of the Debye mass (Peshier 2006) and the zero-temperature potential, enabling predictions for the temperature dependence of jet quenching near \(T_c\). A forthcoming study will extend these considerations to radiative energy loss.
