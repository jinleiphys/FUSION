# Elliptic Flow at Finite Shear Viscosity in a Kinetic Approach at RHIC

> Within a covariant parton cascade, we discuss the impact of both finite shear viscosity $η$ and freeze-out dynamics on the elliptic flow generated at RHIC. We find that the enhancement of $η/s$ in the cross-over region of the QGP phase transition cannot be neglected in order to extract the information from the QGP phase. We also point out that the elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ for a fluid at $η/s \sim 0.1-0.2$ is consistent with the one needed by quark number scaling drawing a nice consistency between the nearly perfect fluid property of QGP and the coalescence process.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the enhancement of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) in the cross-over region of the QGP phase transition significantly affects elliptic flow extraction, and that a fluid with η/s ~ 0.1–0.2 produces a parton-level v₂(pT) consistent with the quark number scaling observed in data, but only when both finite viscosity and a realistic freeze-out dynamics are included.

## Method
The authors used a covariant parton cascade (3+1D kinetic approach) with a local renormalization of the cross section to simulate finite shear viscosity, imposing an energy-density-dependent increase of η/s across the cross-over region and into the hadronic phase. They simulated Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV with Glauber initial conditions, a Boltzmann distribution for partons at pT < 2 GeV, and a minijet distribution at higher pT, then computed the elliptic flow v₂(pT) for different values of η/s in the QGP phase.

## Key numbers
- The enhancement of η/s across the cross-over region produces a damping of elliptic flow that introduces a systematic error of 40–50% if neglected.
- A fluid with η/s ~ 0.15–0.2 in the QGP phase produces a parton-level v₂(pT) consistent with the quark number scaling observed by PHENIX at √s = 200 AGeV.
- The authors note that including an equation of state with phase transition (as in hydrodynamics) reduces v₂ significantly, potentially shifting the agreement with data down to η/s ~ 0.1.

## Context
This work extends viscous hydrodynamic studies by Romatschke (2009) and Song (2008) that kept η/s constant during the hadronic phase, by implementing an energy-density-dependent increase of η/s across the QCD cross-over. It also addresses the breaking of v₂/εx scaling observed by PHENIX (2006, 2008), showing that both finite viscosity and freeze-out dynamics are necessary to reproduce this breaking while maintaining consistency with quark number scaling. The approach enables future inclusion of mean-field dynamics with phase transition, as noted by Plumari (2009).
