# Thermal Properties of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated in a relativistic mean- field approach. We start from free space NN-interactions and derive in-medium self-energies by Dirac-Brueckner theory. By the DDRH procedure we derive in a self-consistent approach density- dependent meson-baryon vertices. At the mean-field level, we include isoscalar and isovector scalar and vector interactions. The nuclear equation of state is investigated for a large range of total baryon densities up to the neutron star regime, the full range of asymmetries from symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter, and temperatures up to T~100 MeV. The isovector-scalar self-energies are found to modify strongly the thermal properties of asymmetric nuclear matter. A striking result is the change of phase transitions when isovector-scalar self-energies are included.

## Key claim

The authors find that including isovector-scalar (δ-meson) self-energies in a relativistic mean-field description of asymmetric nuclear matter strongly modifies the thermal properties, particularly changing the character of phase transitions from first-order to second-order in asymmetric systems, a result that depends on the density-dependent vertex formulation derived from Dirac-Brueckner theory.

## Method

The authors start from free-space nucleon-nucleon interactions and derive in-medium self-energies using Dirac-Brueckner theory, then apply the Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron (DDRH) procedure to obtain self-consistent density-dependent meson-baryon vertices. At the mean-field level, they include isoscalar (σ, ω) and isovector (δ, ρ) scalar and vector interactions, solving the field equations for infinite nuclear matter in the grand canonical ensemble at finite temperature. The nuclear equation of state is computed for total baryon densities up to the neutron star regime, proton fractions ξ = Z/A from 0.5 (symmetric matter) to 0 (pure neutron matter), and temperatures up to T ~ 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ_eq = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Temperature range investigated: up to T ~ 100 MeV (below the QCD phase transition at T_c^QCD ~ 150 MeV)
- Density range investigated: up to 6ρ_eq (approximately 0.96 fm⁻³), guided by neutron star observations of twice solar mass
- Critical temperature range for liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter cited from literature: T_C = 10-20 MeV, with phenomenological models favoring ~10 MeV and microscopic approaches predicting ~20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the DDRH field theory, previously developed by the Giessen group (Lenske, Fuchs, 1995) for cold nuclear matter, to finite temperature equilibrium thermodynamics. It builds on the earlier study of asymmetric nuclear matter thermodynamics by Müller and Serot (1995) using a non-linear Walecka model, but differs by using microscopically derived density-dependent vertices rather than phenomenological self-interactions. The inclusion of the isovector-scalar δ-meson channel, whose density-dependent NN vertex was first derived by the authors in prior work (Lenske, 2004), is a unique feature that enables the study of proton-neutron effective mass splitting and its thermodynamic consequences. The results enable future investigations of dynamical fluctuations beyond mean-field and applications to neutron star crust thermodynamics.
