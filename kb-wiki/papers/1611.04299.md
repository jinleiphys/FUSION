# Systematic effects in the measurement of the negatively charged pion mass using laser spectroscopy of pionic helium atoms

> The collision-induced shift and broadening of selected dipole transition lines of pionic helium in gaseous helium at low temperatures up to $T=12$ K and pressure up to a few bar are calculated within variable phase function approach. We predict blue shift of the resonance frequencies of the $(n,l)=(16,15) \rightarrow (16,14) $ and $(16,15) \rightarrow (17,14)$ unfavored transitions and red shift for the favored transition $(17,16) \rightarrow (16,15)$. The result may be helpful in reducing the systematic error in proposed future experiments for determination of the negatively charged pion mass from laser spectroscopy of metastable pionic helium atoms.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that collision-induced shifts and broadenings of specific dipole transition lines in pionic helium atoms immersed in gaseous helium are density-dependent and temperature-dependent, and that these systematic effects must be accounted for to achieve sub-ppm precision in future measurements of the negatively charged pion mass. The abstract's claim that the results "may be helpful in reducing the systematic error" is qualified by the finding that one transition line has a collisional broadening of 7.7 GHz, which would prevent determination of the transition wavelength to better than 1 ppm.

## Method
The collision-induced shift and broadening of dipole transition lines in pionic helium were calculated using the impact approximation of binary collision theory, with interatomic potentials obtained from ab initio quantum chemistry calculations for nearly 400 nuclear configurations. The elastic scattering phase shifts were computed via the variable phase function approach, and the density-dependent shift and broadening slopes were thermally averaged over a Maxwell velocity distribution for temperatures between 4 K and 12 K.

## Key numbers
- For the unfavored transition (16,15) → (16,14) at perturber density N = 10^21 cm^-3, the blue shift Δ ≈ 2.5 GHz and the collisional broadening Γ = 0.1 GHz.
- For the unfavored transition (16,15) → (17,14) at N = 10^21 cm^-3, the blue shift Δ = 18 GHz and the collisional broadening Γ = 7.7 GHz.
- For the favored transition (17,16) → (16,15) at N = 10^21 cm^-3, the red shift Δ ≈ -8 GHz.
- The slope of the density shift β for (16,15) → (16,14) at T = 6 K is 2.93 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3, and the slope of the density broadening α is 0.14 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3.
- The slope of the density shift β for (17,16) → (16,15) at T = 6 K is -7.55 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3, and the slope of the density broadening α is 1.41 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3.
- The slope of the density shift β for (16,15) → (17,14) at T = 6 K is 17.97 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3, and the slope of the density broadening α is 7.19 × 10^-21 GHz·cm^3.

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Hori (2011) for laser spectroscopy of pionic helium atoms as a method to determine the pion mass with fractional precision better than 1 ppm, and addresses systematic effects that could prevent achieving that precision. The calculations provide density-dependent shift and broadening values needed to extrapolate transition wavelengths to zero target density, which is essential for comparing experimental results to three-body QED calculations. The results are relevant to planned experiments at the Paul Scherrer Institute that aim to improve upon earlier X-ray spectroscopy measurements of pionic magnesium by Jeck (1980s), which achieved 3 ppm accuracy but encountered complications from electron screening corrections.
