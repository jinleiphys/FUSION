# Constraints on the dark photon from parity violation and the $W$ mass

> We present an analysis of the experimental data for parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) and atomic parity-violation, including the effects of a dark photon. We derive the favored region of dark photon parameter space, which provides a good description of the experimental data from the Qweak Collaboration and the Jefferson Lab PVDIS Collaboration and simultaneously relieves the tension between the neutron skin thickness determined in the PREX-II experiment and nuclear-model predictions. In addition, we extract the parameter region required to explain the latest W-boson mass anomaly. Our results indicate that a heavy dark photon with mass above the Z boson mass is favored, while other sources of new physics beyond the Standard Model in addition to the dark photon would also be expected.

# Constraints on the dark photon from parity violation and the $W$ mass

## Key claim
A heavy dark photon with mass above the Z boson mass is favored by a combined fit to parity-violating electron scattering data and atomic parity-violation data, and can simultaneously explain the CDF W-boson mass anomaly; however, other sources of new physics beyond the Standard Model in addition to the dark photon would also be expected.

## Method
The authors performed a chi-squared fit to parity-violating electron scattering data from the Qweak, PREX-II, and Jefferson Lab PVDIS Collaborations, together with atomic parity-violation data for cesium from the Particle Data Group, using a dark photon model with kinetic mixing with hypercharge. They also extracted the dark photon parameter region required to shift the unmixed Z boson mass to the physical Z mass to explain the CDF W-boson mass anomaly, using the relation between the W mass, Z mass, and electroweak parameters.

## Key numbers
- Qweak Collaboration weak charge of the proton: $Q_{\rm w}^p = 0.0719 \pm 0.0045$ at $Q^2 = 0.0248\ {\rm GeV}^2$
- PREX-II weak charge of $^{208}{\rm Pb}$: $Q_{\rm w}(^{208}{\rm Pb}) = -114.4 \pm 2.6$ at $Q^2 = 0.00616\ {\rm GeV}^2$
- PVDIS asymmetry at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 1.085\ {\rm GeV}^2$: $A^{{\rm exp}(1)}_{\rm PV} = [-91.1 \pm 3.1\ ({\rm stat.}) \pm 3.0\ ({\rm syst.})] \times 10^{-6}$
- PVDIS asymmetry at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 1.901\ {\rm GeV}^2$: $A^{{\rm exp}(2)}_{\rm PV} = [-160.8 \pm 6.4\ ({\rm stat.}) \pm 3.1\ ({\rm syst.})] \times 10^{-6}$
- Atomic parity-violation weak charge of $^{133}{\rm Cs}$ (PDG): $Q_{\rm w}(^{133}{\rm Cs})_{\rm PDG} = -72.82(42)$
- CDF W-boson mass: $m_W = 80.4335 \pm 0.0094\ {\rm GeV}$, a $7\sigma$ deviation from the Standard Model prediction
- Total chi-squared with dark photon fit: $\chi^2_{\rm total} = 2.179$, compared to $\chi^2_{\rm total} = 3.517$ without the dark photon
- The most promising dark photon mass region is between the Z boson mass and 110 GeV, where no CMS exclusion limits apply

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of dark photon effects in parity-violating electron scattering (Thomas 2022) and builds on earlier constraints from electroweak precision observables (Curtin 2014) and electron-proton deep inelastic scattering (Kribs 2020, Thomas 2021). The analysis simultaneously addresses the tension between the PREX-II neutron skin thickness determination and nuclear-model predictions (Piekarewicz 2012, Roca-Maza 2015, Hu 2021), and the CDF W-boson mass anomaly (CDF 2022). The results indicate that future PVES measurements at SoLID, P2, and MOLLER should provide more stringent constraints on the dark photon parameters.
