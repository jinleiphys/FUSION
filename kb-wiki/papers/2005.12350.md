# The fractal geometry and the mapping of Efimov states to Bloch states

> Efimov states are known to have a discrete real space scale invariance, working in momentum space we identify the relevant discrete scale invariance for the scattering amplitude defining its Weierstrass function as well. Through the use of the mathematical formalism for discrete scale invariance for the scattering amplitude we identify the scaling parameters from the pole structure of the corresponding zeta function, it's zeroth order pole is fixed by the Efimov physics. The corresponding geometrical fractal structure for Efimov physics in momentum space is identified as a ray across a logarithmic spiral. This geometrical structure also appears in the physics of atomic collapse in the relativistic regime connecting it to Efimov physics. Transforming to logarithmic variables in momentum space we map the three-body scattering amplitude into Bloch states and the ladder of energies of the Efimov states are simply obtained interms of the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule. Thus through the mapping the complex problem of three-body short range interaction is transformed to that of a non-interacting single particle in a discrete lattice.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the three-body Efimov scattering amplitude possesses a discrete scale invariance (DSI) that can be formally expressed as a Weierstrass function, and that by transforming to logarithmic momentum variables the Efimov problem maps exactly onto a single non-interacting particle in a one-dimensional periodic lattice, with the Efimov energy ladder obtained from the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule. The hedge is that this mapping relies on a specific condition (Eq. 13) relating the kernel of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation, and the scaling parameter \(a_e\) becomes complex rather than real, unlike standard DSI functions.

## Method

The author starts from the effective field theory (EFT) formulation of three-boson physics, writing the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation for the scattering amplitude. By imposing a condition (Eq. 13) that relates the iterated kernel to a scaled version of itself, the Neumann series solution is recast into the iterative form of a DSI function. The scaling parameters \(b_e = \sqrt{3}\pi/4\) and \(a_e = \exp(i \ln b_e / s_0)\) are extracted from the pole structure of the corresponding zeta function, with \(s_0 \simeq 1.0064\). Transforming to the logarithmic variable \(\tilde{x} = \ln p\), the scattering amplitude is rewritten as a Bloch wave \(\exp(i k \tilde{x}) G(\tilde{x}/L)\), and the Efimov energy spectrum is derived via the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rule.

## Key numbers

* The universal Efimov scaling factor for homonuclear systems is \(\lambda_0 = e^{\pi/s_0}\) where \(s_0 = 1.00624\) (from the introduction, citing prior work).
* The imaginary solutions to the Mellin-transform condition are \(s = \pm i s_0\) with \(s_0 \simeq 1.0064\) (from the EFT section).
* The DSI scaling parameter for the scattering amplitude is \(b_e = \sqrt{3} \pi / 4\) (from Eq. 19).
* The Efimov energy spectrum obtained from the Bohr-Sommerfeld rule is \(E_n = -(p_*^2 / m) e^{-2\pi n / s_0}\) (Eq. 30), where \(p_*\) is a constant set by boundary conditions.

## Context

This work extends the known connection between Efimov physics and discrete scale invariance, previously formalized through renormalization-group limit cycles (Wilson 1971, Albeverio 1981) and the inverse-square potential in hyperspherical coordinates (Efimov 1970, Bedaque 1999). The paper provides a new geometrical interpretation: the fractal structure underlying the Efimov scattering amplitude in momentum space is a ray across a logarithmic spiral, a geometry also appearing in relativistic atomic collapse (Ovdat 2017). The mapping to Bloch states enables a simplified single-particle lattice picture, which could facilitate further connections to solid-state systems such as topological semimetals and graphene (Ovdat 2017, Zhang 2018).
