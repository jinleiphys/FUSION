# Bayesian inference of the resonance content of p(gamma,K^+)Lambda

> A Bayesian analysis of the world's p(gamma,K^+)Lambda data is presented. From the proposed selection of 11 resonances, we find that the following nucleon resonances have the highest probability of contributing to the reaction: S11(1535), S11(1650), F15(1680), P13(1720), D13(1900), P13(1900), P11(1900), and F15(2000). We adopt a Regge-plus-resonance framework featuring consistent couplings for nucleon resonances up to spin J=5/2. We evaluate all possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances. The best model is selected from the 2048 model variants by calculating the Bayesian evidence values against the world's p(gamma,K^+)Lambda data.

## Key claim

Using Bayesian inference on the world's p(γ,K⁺)Λ data, the authors identify eight nucleon resonances with the highest probability of contributing to the reaction, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific Regge-plus-resonance framework and the proposed set of 11 candidate resonances; the analysis does not capture the full complexity of coupled-channels models.

## Method

The authors adopt a Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework with consistent couplings for nucleon resonances up to spin J=5/2, using a reggeized background constrained by CLAS data at 2.6 GeV < √s < 2.84 GeV and cosθ_K* > 0.35. They evaluate all 2048 possible combinations of 11 candidate resonances by calculating Bayesian evidence values against 6148 p(γ,K⁺)Λ data points (3455 differential cross sections, 2241 single, and 452 double polarization observables), using a genetic algorithm for global optimization, MINOS for covariance estimation, and the VEGAS algorithm for evidence integration.

## Key numbers

- 2048 model variants evaluated, corresponding to all combinations of 11 candidate resonances
- 6148 total data points used: 3455 differential cross sections, 2241 single polarization observables, 452 double polarization observables
- The best model (RPR-2011) has 14 N* parameters and features 8 resonances: S11(1535), S11(1650), F15(1680), P13(1720), D13(1900), P13(1900), P11(1900), and F15(2000)
- Evidence ratio Δln Z = 2.3 between RPR-2011 and the second-best model (12 N* parameters, no D13(1900)), corresponding to significant to strong evidence on Jeffreys' scale
- The reggeized background uses data with 2.6 GeV < √s < 2.84 GeV and cosθ_K* > 0.35
- Prior distributions for coupling strengths are uniform between -100 and +100, motivated by naturalness arguments (couplings of 100 produce total cross sections exceeding 25 μb)

## Context

This work extends previous Regge-plus-resonance analyses by Corthals et al. (2006, 2007) and De Cruz et al. (2010), applying Bayesian inference to resolve the long-standing ambiguity in the N* content of p(γ,K⁺)Λ, where the PDG lists only four resonances with fair evidence in the K⁺Λ decay channel (only S11(1650) has three-star status). The results enable future Bayesian analyses in more advanced coupled-channels models, though the authors note that the "dimensional curse" currently prevents such computations. The analysis contradicts some prior coupled-channels results (Shklyar 2005, Julia-Diaz 2006) regarding the P11(1710) resonance, finding no significant contribution, consistent with recent SAID analyses (Arndt 2006, Workman 2011) and the interpretation of P11(1710) as a ππN resonance (Khemchandani 2008).
