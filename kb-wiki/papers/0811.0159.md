# Efimov physics in a finite volume

> Three bosons with large scattering length show universal properties that do not depend on the details of the interaction at short distances. In the three-boson system, these properties include a geometric spectrum of shallow three-body states called "Efimov states" and log-periodic dependence of scattering observables on the scattering length. We investigate the modification of the Efimov states in a finite cubic box and calculate the dependence of their energies on the box size using effective field theory. We explicitly verify the renormalization of the effective field theory in the finite volume.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the finite-volume corrections to the Efimov trimer spectrum for three identical bosons in a cubic box, finding that the energy shift from the infinite-volume value becomes significant for box sizes comparable to the scattering length, but the effective field theory renormalization procedure remains valid as long as the finite-volume energy shift does not exceed 15–20% of the infinite-volume energy.

## Method
The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) for three bosons with short-range interactions at leading order in the large scattering length, extending the infinite-volume formalism of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) to a finite cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They derive a homogeneous integral equation for the atom-dimer bound-state amplitude in the box, where loop integrals become discrete sums over quantized momenta, and solve it numerically using a linear expansion of the kernel around the infinite-volume energy for small finite-volume shifts.

## Key numbers
- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\), with \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\).
- For state II (infinite-volume energy \(E_3^\infty = -5.04626/a^2\), \(\Lambda_\ast a = 1.66\)), proper renormalization fails for \(a/L \approx 0.6\) (i.e., \(L \approx 1.67a\)).
- For state III (\(E_3^\infty = -11.1322/a^2\), \(\Lambda_\ast a = 3.66\)), the linear approximation remains valid up to \(a/L \approx 0.85\) (i.e., \(L \approx 1.18a\)).
- For states Ia and Ib in the same system (\(\Lambda_\ast a = 5.66\)), the ratio of their energies decreases from 23.08 in infinite volume to 19.5 at \(L = 3a\).

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume studies of three-boson systems by Tan (2008) and Beane, Detmold, and Savage (2007) to the regime of large scattering length where Efimov states exist, and it provides the necessary formalism for interpreting future lattice QCD simulations of three-nucleon systems. The results enable tests of the conjecture by Braaten and Hammer (2003) that QCD could be tuned to exhibit the Efimov effect in the triton, and they could also be tested experimentally with ultracold atoms in optical lattices.
