# Polarization observables and spin-aligned fusion rates in 2H(d,p)3H and 2H(d,n)3He reactions

> Nucleon transfer reactions in low-energy deuteron-deuteron scattering are described by solving exact four-particle equations in momentum space. The Coulomb interaction between the protons is included using the screening and renormalization method. Various realistic potentials are used between nucleon pairs. The energy dependence of the differential cross section, analyzing powers, polarizations, spin-transfer coefficient, and the quintet suppression factor is studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that exact four-particle calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials show the quintet suppression factor for the $d+d \to n+\He$ reaction is only 20-25% smaller than for $d+d \to p+\Hh$, contradicting earlier hopes that spin-aligned deuteron fusion could strongly suppress neutron production; the suppression is significant at few MeV but not in the keV regime.

## Method

The authors solved exact Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) four-particle equations in momentum space for nucleon transfer reactions in deuteron-deuteron scattering, including the Coulomb interaction between protons via the screening and renormalization method. They used five different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, N3LO, CD Bonn, CD Bonn + Delta, and INOY04) and studied the energy dependence of differential cross sections, analyzing powers, outgoing nucleon polarization, spin-transfer coefficients, and the quintet suppression factor below the three-body breakup threshold.

## Key numbers

- The INOY04 potential gives $\Hh$ binding energy 8.49 MeV and $\He$ binding energy 7.73 MeV, closest to experimental values of 8.48 MeV and 7.72 MeV respectively (Table 1)
- The deuteron D-state probability $P_D(d)$ ranges from 3.60% (INOY04) to 5.78% (AV18) across the potentials used (Table 1)
- The deuteron to neutron spin transfer coefficient $K_y^y(0^\circ)$ at $E_d = 3$ MeV ranges from 0.622 (AV18) to 0.633 (INOY04) across potentials
- The quintet suppression factor for $d+d \to n+\He$ at $E_d = 3$ MeV ranges from 0.133 (AV18) to 0.219 (INOY04) across potentials
- The quintet suppression factor for both reactions is of order 0.25 above $E_d = 2$ MeV and increases more rapidly as energy decreases

## Context

This work extends exact four-particle calculations by Deltuva and Fonseca (2007) to include polarization observables and the quintet suppression factor for deuteron-deuteron transfer reactions. It contradicts earlier approximate calculations by Kulsrud (1982) that suggested strong suppression of neutron production with spin-aligned deuterons, and provides more converged results than the resonating group method calculations by Hofmann (1984) which included only $L \le 3$ states. The results enable precise predictions for future experiments measuring the quintet suppression factor directly, as planned by Schieck and Didelez.
