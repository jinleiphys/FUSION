# The extraction of hadronic parameters from experiments on pionium

> Experimental values of the lifetime of the 1s level of pionium and of the difference between the energies of the 2s and 2p levels yield values of the a(0c) and a(cc) elements of the s-wave scattering matrix for the 2-channel (pi+ pi-, pi0 pi0) system at the pi+ pi- threshold. We develop a method for obtaining the isospin invariant quanties a20 - a00 and 2a00 + a20 from a(0c) and a(cc). We emphasize that the isospin invariant scattering lengths a00 and a20 universally used in the literature cannot be considered to be purely hadronic quantities.

# The extraction of hadronic parameters from experiments on pionium

## Key claim

The isospin invariant scattering lengths a0^0 and a0^2 universally used in the literature cannot be considered to be purely hadronic quantities; the values of a_oc and a_cc obtained from pionium experiments are affected by the electromagnetic interaction and must be corrected to yield the isospin invariant quantities a0^2 - a0^0 and 2a0^0 + a0^2, which can then be compared with results from above-threshold experiments and chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors construct energy-independent double square well hadronic potentials V0^0 and V0^2 that reproduce the best available pion-pion s-wave phase shifts up to 500 MeV total energy in the center-of-mass frame, using the pion mass mu_c as a reference mass. These potentials are inserted into a two-channel relativistic Schrodinger equation that includes the physical charged and neutral pion masses and a Coulomb potential for extended charge distributions, and the coupled equations are integrated numerically to obtain the threshold scattering matrix a. The difference between the isospin-invariant matrix a-tilde (constructed from the scattering lengths a0^0 and a0^2) and the physical matrix a gives the electromagnetic corrections needed to relate pionium observables to hadronic parameters.

## Key numbers

- The conversion from the 1s level width Gamma to the scattering matrix element a_oc is: a_oc (fm) = 0.3802 [Gamma (eV)]^(1/2), with an uncertainty of about 0.02 percent.
- The conversion from the hadronic contribution to the 2s-2p energy difference Delta W^had to a_cc is: a_cc (fm) = -0.4168 Delta W^had (eV).
- The isospin invariant scattering lengths obtained from the double square well potentials are: a0^0 = 0.2883 fm, a0^2 = -0.0617 fm.
- The electromagnetic corrections to the threshold scattering matrix elements are: a-tilde_cc - a_cc = 0.024 +/- 0.002 fm, and the relative correction Delta_oc = 0.032 +/- 0.002.
- The expected hadronic contribution to the 2s-2p energy difference is close to -0.355 eV, giving a total Delta W of about -0.47 eV relative to the pure Coulomb n=2 level at 464.52 eV below threshold.
- The vacuum polarisation correction increases the 1s width by a factor (1 + 3.08 x 10^(-3)).

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach of Moor, Rasche and Woolcock (1995) by using energy-independent double square well potentials that reproduce chiral perturbation theory phase shifts up to 500 MeV, rather than the simple energy-dependent square wells used previously. The authors correct two problems in that earlier work: the inconsistent use of the neutral pion mass in chiral perturbation theory equations, and the neglect of energy dependence in the potentials when calculating electromagnetic corrections. The results enable the extraction of the isospin invariant quantities a0^2 - a0^0 and 2a0^0 + a0^2 from the upcoming CERN experiment (Adeva et al., 1994) measuring the pionium lifetime with 10 percent accuracy, allowing comparison with values from chiral perturbation theory and above-threshold scattering experiments.
