# Nuclear Transparency in Heavy Ion Collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon

## Key claim

The survival probability of a projectile nucleon traversing a target nucleus without interaction in central Si-Pb collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon is calculated to be 30 to 50% higher than the E814 experimental value when using Glauber theory, while the transport code UrQMD fails to reproduce the observed peak of beam-rapidity particles altogether, indicating that neither approach provides a fully satisfactory description of nuclear transparency in this regime.

## Method

The survival probability for a projectile nucleon to pass through the target without inelastic interaction was calculated using two independent approaches: (i) a Glauber-type approximation with straight-line trajectories and frozen nucleons, using Saxon-Woods parameterizations for nuclear densities with surface thickness 0.52 fm, and (ii) the cascade transport code UrQMD version 1.1. The calculations were compared to E814 data for Si-Pb collisions at 14.6 GeV/nucleon, with the transverse energy distribution used to relate impact parameter to experimental centrality bins, including a simulation of TCAL leakage effects in the UrQMD calculation.

## Key numbers

- Experimental survival probability for central Si-Pb collisions: $S_{exp} = 3.5 \times 10^{-3}$ at transverse energy $E_t^c = 15.5$ GeV
- Glauber calculation with $\sigma_{\rm eff} = 30$ mb yields $S(E_t^c, \sigma_{\rm eff}=30\ {\rm mb})/S_{exp} = 3.47$, i.e., a factor of 3.47 too high
- Fitting $S_G(b=0, \sigma_{\rm eff}) = S_{exp}$ gives $\sigma_{\rm eff} = 31.1 \pm 0.7$ mb when assuming $b=0$ for the highest $E_t$ bin
- Glauber predictions for central collisions are 30 to 50% higher than experiment
- Time between arrival of first and last projectile nucleons in target rest frame: $\Delta t \approx 0.5$ fm/c (with Lorentz factor $\gamma_p \approx 16$ and Si diameter $2R_p \simeq 7$ fm)
- Intrinsic excitation energy uncertainty from uncertainty principle: $\Delta E \geq (\Delta t)^{-1} \approx 0.4$ GeV
- Fit parameters for transverse energy distribution: $\epsilon_0 = 0.067$ GeV, $\omega = 7.8$ with $\sigma_{in}^{NN} = 30$ mb
- Mean impact parameters from UrQMD for three centrality bins: 3.6 fm, 2.1 fm, and 1.6 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the E814 collaboration (1990s) on nuclear transparency at the AGS energy of 14.6 GeV/nucleon, where earlier calculations using uniform density distributions had concluded that the effective nucleon-nucleon cross section equals the inelastic NN cross section within error bars. The paper directly compares Glauber theory and the UrQMD cascade code (Bass et al., 1990s) to assess whether wounded nucleon effects or effective cross sections can explain the observed transparency. The results show that neither approach fully reproduces the data, particularly the beam-rapidity peak in central collisions, leaving open the question of how to extract effective cross sections of wounded nucleons from transparency measurements.
