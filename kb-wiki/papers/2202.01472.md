# Lattice QCD calculation of the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift

> We develop a method for lattice QCD calculation of the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift. To demonstrate its feasibility, we present the first lattice calculation with a gauge ensemble at $m_π= 142$ MeV. By adopting the infinite-volume reconstruction method along with an optimized subtraction scheme, we obtain $ΔE_{\text{TPE}} = -28.9(4.9)~μ\text{eV} + 93.72~μ\text{eV}/\text{fm}^2 \cdot\langle r_p^2 \rangle$, or $ΔE_{\text{TPE}} = 37.4(4.9)~μ$eV, which is consistent with the previous theoretical results in a range of 20-50 $μ$eV.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift, obtaining a result consistent with previous theoretical estimates in the range of 20-50 μeV, but the calculation is performed at a single coarse lattice spacing and near-physical pion mass, so the dominant systematic uncertainty is from discretization effects that require future study with finer lattices.

## Method

The authors develop a master formula that expresses the two-photon exchange energy shift in terms of hadronic functions H₁(x,t) and H₂(x,t) calculable from lattice QCD, with an infinite-volume reconstruction method and optimized subtraction scheme to remove infrared divergences automatically. They compute four-point correlation functions on a single 2+1-flavor domain wall fermion ensemble at mπ = 142 MeV, using the random field sparsening technique, and evaluate both connected and disconnected quark contraction diagrams with multiple source-sink separations to control excited-state contamination.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the gauge ensemble: mπ = 142 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1943(8) fm
- Lattice spatial extent: L = 24a, temporal extent: T = 64a
- Number of configurations: Nconf = 131
- ΔE_lat (connected part) = 27.6(4.5) μeV
- ΔE_lat (disconnected part) = 2.1(2.1) μeV
- ΔE_lat (total contribution) = 29.7(4.9) μeV
- ΔE_TPE = -28.9(4.9) μeV + 93.72 μeV/fm² · ⟨r_p²⟩
- Using the muonic-hydrogen charge radius, ΔE_TPE = 37.4(4.9) μeV
- Extracted proton charge radius: √⟨r_p²⟩ = 0.84136(65) fm
- The third line of the master formula contributes -0.60 μeV to ΔE

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to the two-photon exchange correction, including dispersion relations (Pachucki 1999, Carlson 2011, Gorchtein 2013), baryon chiral perturbation theory (Alarcon 2013, 2020), heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Birse 2012, Peset 2015), non-relativistic QED (Hill 2011), and operator product expansion (Hill 2016), which all rely on model-dependent subtraction functions. The lattice QCD method provides a first-principles alternative that avoids such model assumptions, and the result is consistent with the range of 20-50 μeV from those prior calculations. This enables future lattice studies with controlled systematic errors to improve the extraction of the proton charge radius from muonic-hydrogen spectroscopy.
