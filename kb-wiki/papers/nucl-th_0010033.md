# On the f Sum Rule and its Extensions

> The $f$ sum rule is derived in a non-relativistic frame and connected, via Ward Identities, to the low energy Thomson scattering. A generalisation to isospin symmetry in the nuclear case is discussed and linked to the Meson Exchange Currents. The extension to a fully relativistic theory is then discussed and it is shown that the energy-weighted sum rule becomes a relation between the particle-hole and particle-antiparticle emission. Moreover the generalisation to isospin symmetry is also allowed and provides non-perturbative results.

## Key claim

The paper derives the $f$ sum rule in a non-relativistic frame and connects it via Ward Identities to the two-photon term of Compton scattering, but the key hedge is that in a fully relativistic theory the energy-weighted sum rule vanishes because it becomes a compensation between particle-hole creation and a reduction of particle-antiparticle creation due to Pauli blocking in the nuclear medium, with the vacuum subtraction playing a central role.

## Method

The author derives the $f$ sum rule from the double commutator of the density operator with the Hamiltonian, then links it to the asymptotic behavior of the polarization propagator via Ward Identities. For the relativistic case, the polarization propagator is computed for a relativistic free Fermi gas, and the vacuum contribution is subtracted to obtain a regularized result. The generalization to the isovector channel is performed by introducing an isospin-dependent Lagrangian with explicit meson exchange and evaluating the double commutator with the isovector density.

## Key numbers

* The non-relativistic $f$ sum rule gives $\Xi_1 = \frac{q^2}{2m} N$, where $N$ is the number of nucleons and $m$ is the nucleon mass.
* For the relativistic free Fermi gas, the particle-hole contribution to the sum rule is $\frac{{\bf q}^2}{2\sqrt{k_F^2+m^2}} N$ for small ${\bf q}$, and the particle-antiparticle contribution cancels it exactly, yielding $\Xi_1 = 0$.
* In the limit of small $k_F$ (compared to $m$) and without constraint over ${\bf q}$, the particle-hole contribution becomes $\frac{{\bf q}^2}{2\sqrt{{\bf q}^2+m^2}} N$, with the particle-antiparticle contribution providing the same result up to a sign.

## Context

This work extends the classic non-relativistic $f$ sum rule (NoPi-66-B; OrTr-91) to a fully relativistic framework, showing that the sum rule vanishes due to cancellation between particle-hole and particle-antiparticle contributions, a result derived via Ward Identities. The generalization to the isovector channel shows that explicit meson exchange dynamics (with the pion-in-flight term) cancels contributions from the interaction Hamiltonian, restoring the canonical value, while the free pion Lagrangian introduces a tadpole term that affects the sum rule via Ward Identities. This enables a non-perturbative treatment of sum rules in nuclear physics and clarifies the role of vacuum subtraction in relativistic calculations.
