# Spectroscopy of N=50 isotones with the valence-space density matrix renormalization group

> The recently proposed combination of the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) with the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) offers a scalable and flexible many-body approach for strongly correlated open-shell nuclei. We use the VS-DMRG to investigate the low-lying spectroscopy of N=50 isotones, which are characteristic for their transition between single-particle and collective excitations. We also study electromagnetic transitions and show the advantage of the VS-DMRG to capture the underlying physics more efficiently, with significantly improved convergence compared to state-of-the-art shell-model truncations. Combined with an analysis of quantum information measures, this further establishes the VS-DMRG as a valuable method for ab initio calculations of nuclei.

## Key claim

The VS-DMRG approach can capture the low-lying spectroscopy and electromagnetic transitions of N=50 isotones more efficiently than conventional shell-model truncations, but the method fails to reproduce the deformed excited-state band in 78Ni, indicating either increased many-body uncertainties or interaction deficiencies.

## Method

The authors first perform a VS-IMSRG decoupling to map the chiral NN+3N Hamiltonian onto a valence space built on a 60Ca core (proton pf shell and neutron sdg shell, 50 single-particle states total). They then use the DMRG with a matrix-product-state representation to variationally solve the valence-space problem, employing a truncation-error-based extrapolation scheme with bond dimensions up to M=10240. Electromagnetic transition operators are consistently transformed through the IMSRG evolution, and quantum information measures (single-orbital entropies) are computed from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- VS-DMRG extrapolated energies for 80Zn: E(0+) = -678.06(6) MeV, E(2+) = -676.20(7) MeV, E(4+) = -675.60(8) MeV
- VS-DMRG extrapolated B(E2) values for 80Zn: B(E2; 2+ → 0+) = 93.5(15) e² fm⁴, B(E2; 4+ → 2+) = 49.4(8) e² fm⁴
- Largest CI dimension at Tmax=7 for 80Zn: dim H_A = 8.8 × 10⁹
- Ratio E(4+)/E(2+) for 74Cr: 2.83 (approaching the rigid-rotor value of 3.33)
- 2+ excitation energy in 78Ni: approximately 3.0 MeV; in 74Cr: approximately 0.5 MeV
- B(E2) for 78Ni ground-state transition: 40 e² fm⁴ (VS-DMRG) vs. 34 e² fm⁴ (shell model)
- Experimental B(E2) for 80Zn 2+→0+: 144(29) e² fm⁴; VS-DMRG 2+ excitation energy: 1.90 MeV vs. experimental 1.49 MeV
- Total entropy S_tot = 10.4 for 78Ni vs. S_tot = 20.7 for 74Cr
- Tmax=0 component of ground state: |c₀|² ≈ 0.48 for 78Ni, ≈ 0.03 for 74Cr

## Context

This work extends the VS-DMRG approach previously introduced by Tichai (2023) and Legeza (2015) to electromagnetic transition operators, enabling comparison with phenomenological shell-model calculations by Nowacki (2016) for N=50 isotones. The results qualitatively reproduce the transition from single-particle excitations in 78Ni to collective rotational excitations in 74Cr observed by Nowacki (2016), but the failure to reproduce the deformed excited-state band in 78Ni mirrors similar difficulties in coupled-cluster calculations by Hagen (2016) using the same NN+3N interaction. The work enables future applications to heavier neutron-rich nuclei and valence spaces beyond current shell-model capacities.
