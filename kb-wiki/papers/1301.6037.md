# SIDDHARTA impact on $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes used in in-medium applications

> We have performed new fits of our chirally motivated coupled--channels model for meson-baryon interactions and discussed the impact of the SIDDHARTA measurement on the $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes in the free space and in nuclear medium. The kaon--nucleon amplitudes generated by the model are fully consistent with our earlier studies that used the older kaonic hydrogen data by the DEAR collaboration. The subthreshold energy dependence of the in-medium $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes plays a crucial role in $\bar{K}$--nuclear applications.

## Key claim

The SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen characteristics tightens constraints on chirally motivated coupled-channels models for low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions, but the resulting \(K^{-}p\) amplitudes are fully consistent with those obtained from older DEAR data because the CS30 model (fitted to DEAR) already produced kaonic hydrogen characteristics more compatible with SIDDHARTA than with DEAR.

## Method

The authors performed new fits of a chirally motivated separable potential model for meson-baryon interactions, matching effective potentials to chiral perturbation theory amplitudes up to second order in meson momenta and quark masses. They fitted model parameters (chiral Lagrangian couplings, low-energy constants, and inverse ranges defining off-shell form factors) to \(K^{-}p\) low-energy cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and kaonic hydrogen 1s level characteristics, using three variants: TW1 (only Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction), NLO30 (all LO and NLO terms fitted to SIDDHARTA data), and CS30 (older LO+NLO model fitted to DEAR data). The in-medium amplitudes were computed for symmetric nuclear matter at density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) including Pauli blocking and selfenergies of interacting particles.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density used for in-medium calculations: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), with proton and neutron densities \(\rho_p = \rho_n = \rho_0/2\).
- TW1 model pole positions: \(z_1 = (1371 - i\,54)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_2 = (1433 - i\,25)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_3 = (1383 - i\,53)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- NLO30 model pole positions: \(z_1 = (1355 - i\,86)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_2 = (1418 - i\,44)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_3 = (1410 - i\,38)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- CS30 model pole positions: \(z_1 = (1398 - i\,51)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_2 = (1441 - i\,76)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(z_3 = (1416 - i\,24)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The isovector pole \(z_3\) was previously reported at complex energy \(z = (1401 - i\,40)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in J. Oller and U. Meissner (2003).
- Selfconsistent treatment shifts the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance about 30 MeV below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold in nuclear medium.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Cieply and Smejkal, 2010, 2012) by incorporating the SIDDHARTA measurement (2011) and comparing with the older DEAR data (2005). The results confirm that the strong subthreshold energy dependence of the \(K^{-}p\) amplitude, driven by the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, is robust across model variants and is preserved in nuclear medium. This enables construction of deeper \(\bar{K}\)-nuclear optical potentials for analyzing kaonic atoms and quasi-bound \(\bar{K}\)-nuclear states, as previously investigated by Cieply et al. (2011), Friedman and Gal (2012), and Gazda and Mareš (2012).
