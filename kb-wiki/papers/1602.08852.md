# Antikaon-nucleon interaction and Lambda(1405) in chiral SU(3) dynamics

> The properties of the Lambda(1405) resonance are key ingredients for determining the antikaon-nucleon interaction in strangeness nuclear physics, and the novel internal structure of the Lambda(1405) is of great interest in hadron physics, as a prototype case of a baryon that does not fit into the simple three-quark picture. We show that a quantitative description of the antikaon-nucleon interaction with the Lambda(1405) is achieved in the framework of chiral SU(3) dynamics, with the help of recent experimental progress. Further constraints on the Kbar N subthreshold interaction are provided by analyzing pi Sigma spectra in various processes, such as the K-d -> pi Sigma n reaction and the Lambda_c -> pi pi Sigma decay. The structure of the Lambda(1405) is found to be dominated by an antikaon-nucleon molecular configuration, based on its wavefunction derived from a realistic Kbar N potential and the compositeness criteria from a model-independent weak-binding relation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a quantitative description of the antikaon-nucleon interaction and the Lambda(1405) resonance is achieved within chiral SU(3) dynamics, but the position of the lower-energy pole of the Lambda(1405) remains subject to ambiguities because experimental data are mainly accumulated at and above the Kbar N threshold, not in the subthreshold region where the pole resides.

## Method

The authors construct a nonperturbative coupled-channel scattering amplitude for s-wave Kbar N scattering using the chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO), solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation or using the N/D method. The low-energy constants and subtraction constants are determined by fitting to K- p total cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the kaonic hydrogen 1s shift and width measured by the SIDDHARTA collaboration. The resulting amplitude is then extrapolated into the subthreshold region to locate the poles of the Lambda(1405), and the pi Sigma invariant mass spectra from production reactions such as K- d -> pi Sigma n and Lambda_c -> pi pi Sigma decay are analyzed to further constrain the subthreshold interaction.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen 1s shift: Delta E = 283 +/- 36(stat) +/- 6(syst) eV; width: Gamma = 541 +/- 89(stat) +/- 22(syst) eV (SIDDHARTA collaboration, 2011)
- Best fit NLO analysis yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.96; Weinberg-Tomozawa-only fit yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.12
- Pole 1 (higher energy): z1 = (1424 +7/-23 - i 26 +3/-14) MeV from the NLO analysis constrained by SIDDHARTA data
- Pole 2 (lower energy): z2 = (1381 +18/-6 - i 81 +19/-8) MeV from the same analysis
- Comparison pole positions from other groups: Guo-Oller Fit II gives pole 1 at 1421 +3/-2 - i 19 +8/-5 MeV and pole 2 at 1388 +9/-9 - i 114 +24/-25 MeV; Mai-Meissner solution #2 gives pole 1 at 1434 +2/-2 - i 10 +2/-1 MeV and pole 2 at 1330 +4/-5 - i 56 +17/-11 MeV
- The E-dep. model used in the K- d -> pi Sigma n calculation yields poles at 1428.8 - i 15.3 MeV and 1344.0 - i 49.0 MeV; the E-indep. model yields a single pole at 1405.8 - i 25.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) dynamics framework previously developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001) by incorporating the precise kaonic hydrogen data from SIDDHARTA (Bazzi, 2011) to resolve inconsistencies found in earlier NLO analyses by Borasoy (2004, 2005) and Oller (2005, 2006). The two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405), originally identified by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003), is confirmed with reduced uncertainties. The analysis of pi Sigma spectra from photoproduction (CLAS collaboration, Moriya 2013-2014) and from the K- d -> pi Sigma n reaction (under study by J-PARC E31) enables future constraints on the subthreshold Kbar N interaction, while the Lambda_c -> pi pi Sigma decay studied by Belle (Zupanc, 2013) and BESIII (Ablikim, 2015) provides an additional isospin-filtered probe of the Lambda(1405).
