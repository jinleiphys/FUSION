# Spin asymmetries at RHIC and polarized parton distributions

> We discuss polarized parton distributions and their effects on spin asymmetries at RHIC. In particular, transversity distributions and transverse spin asymmetry are studied. First, we show the $Q^2$ evolution difference between a transversity distribution and a corresponding longitudinally polarized distribution. The difference could be an important test of perturbative QCD in high-energy spin physics. Then, the transverse spin asymmetry $A_{TT}$ is calculated with possible transversity distributions. Next, we study antiquark flavor asymmetry $Δ_{_T} \bar u/ Δ_{_T} \bar d$ in the transversity distributions by using a simple model. Its effects on the transverse spin asymmetry are also discussed.

## Key claim
The transversity distribution \(h_1\) evolves very differently from the longitudinally polarized distribution under \(Q^2\) evolution, and this difference could serve as a test of perturbative QCD in spin physics; however, the transversity distributions themselves have not yet been measured, so the authors rely on the assumption that input transversity distributions are nearly the same as longitudinal ones at low \(Q^2\).

## Method
The authors solve the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) DGLAP evolution equations for transversity distributions using the Euler method in \(Q^2\) and the Simpson method in \(x\), with the GS-A longitudinal distribution as the initial input at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\). They then compute the transverse spin asymmetry \(A_{TT}\) for the Drell-Yan process at RHIC energy \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, using both flavor-symmetric and flavor-asymmetric transversity distributions. The flavor asymmetry \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u - \Delta_{_T} \bar d\) is modeled via a Pauli exclusion principle estimate, with first moments \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u = -0.13\) and \(\Delta_{_T} \bar d = +0.05\), and evolved from \(Q^2 = 0.34\) GeV\(^2\) using the GRSV parametrization.

## Key numbers
- The singlet transversity distribution \(\Delta_{_T} q_s\) evolved from \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 = 200\) GeV\(^2\) is significantly smaller than the longitudinally polarized distribution \(\Delta q_s\) in the region \(x \sim 0.1\).
- The magnitude of \(\Delta_{_T} q_s\) is smaller than that of \(\Delta q_s\) at very small \(x\) (\(x < 0.07\)).
- In the flavor-symmetric case, the Drell-Yan transverse spin asymmetry \(A_{TT}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV is of order 0.5–1% in the dimuon mass region \(100 < M_{\mu\mu}^2 < 500\) GeV\(^2\).
- When flavor asymmetry is included, \(A_{TT}\) increases to 1–2% in the same mass region.
- The Pauli exclusion model yields first moments \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u = -0.13\) and \(\Delta_{_T} \bar d = +0.05\).
- The numerical evolution accuracy is better than 1% in the region \(10^{-5} < x < 0.8\) with more than fifty \(Q^2\) steps and more than five hundred \(x\) steps.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of transversity distributions and their \(Q^2\) evolution (Artru and Mekhfi 1990; Hino et al. 1997) and compares them with longitudinally polarized distributions (Hirai et al. 1997). It also builds on measurements of unpolarized antiquark flavor asymmetry from NMC, NA51, and E866 experiments, and on theoretical models of polarized flavor asymmetry (e.g., the Pauli exclusion model). The results enable future experimental tests of perturbative QCD via transversity at RHIC, and suggest that additional processes such as \(Z^0\) production, jet production, or semi-inclusive muon scattering may be needed to extract accurate transversity distributions.
