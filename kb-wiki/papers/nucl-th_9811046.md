# The Three-Boson System with Short-Range Interactions

> We discuss renormalization of the non-relativistic three-body problem with short-range forces. The problem is non-perturbative at momenta of the order of the inverse of the two-body scattering length. An infinite number of graphs must be summed, which leads to a cutoff dependence that does not appear in any order in perturbation theory. We argue that this cutoff dependence can be absorbed in one local three-body force counterterm and compute the running of the three-body force with the cutoff. This allows a calculation of the scattering of a particle and the two-particle bound state if the corresponding scattering length is used as input. We also obtain a model-independent relation between binding energy of a shallow three-body bound state and this scattering length. We comment on the power counting that organizes higher-order corrections and on relevance of this result for the effective field theory program in nuclear and molecular physics.

## Key claim

The three-body system with short-range interactions and a large two-body scattering length requires a three-body force at leading order to absorb cutoff dependence, but only one three-body parameter is needed to render observables cutoff independent, contrary to naive expectations that an infinite number of counterterms would be required.

## Method

The authors use effective field theory (EFT) with a non-relativistic boson field and a dummy "dimeron" field to rewrite two-body contact interactions as dimeron propagation. They derive an integral equation for particle/dimeron scattering, project onto S-wave, and study the ultraviolet behavior of the amplitude as a function of the momentum cutoff Lambda. The running of the three-body force with cutoff is computed by requiring that low-energy observables remain cutoff independent.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of the parameter lambda below which only real roots exist is lambda_c = 3*sqrt(3)/(4*pi) approximately 0.4135
- For lambda = 1 (bosonic case), the imaginary exponent is s_0 approximately 1.0064
- The discrete family of cutoffs that yield the same low-energy physics scales by a factor of exp(pi/s_0) approximately 22.7 between successive members
- The cutoff-independent phase delta in the running of the three-body force is delta = 0.76 plus or minus 0.01
- The amplitude coefficient C = -gamma / cos(s_0 ln(p_star a_2) + epsilon) with gamma = 1.50 plus or minus 0.03 and epsilon approximately 0.08

## Context

This work extends the EFT program from two-body systems with large scattering lengths (Weinberg 1990, van Kolck 1994) to three-body systems, resolving a puzzle where the J=1/2 nucleon-deuteron amplitude showed cutoff dependence despite being constructed from ultraviolet-finite diagrams. The authors derive a model-independent relation between the three-body binding energy and scattering length, known in nuclear physics as the Phillips line (Phillips 1968). This enables systematic calculation of three-body observables in systems such as the helium trimer and nucleon-deuteron scattering, with higher-order corrections organized by a power counting in the small parameter R/a_2.
