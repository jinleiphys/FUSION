# How High is High x?

> A general three quark bound state satisfying the Pauli principle, and conserving angular momentum and isospin, is used to investigate the spin structure of nucleons at high $x$. It is shown that, if a spin up quark dominates as $x$$\to$1 for a spin up proton, it must be a u quark. Then both $A_{1p}$ and $A_{1n}$, as well as the quark spin distribution $Δu/u$, will approach 1 as $x$$\to$1. The spin distribution $Δd/d$ does not approach 1, but is bound by the limits $-{1/3}\leΔd/d\le 0$ for any $x$. The ratio {$F_{1n}/F_{1p}$ will approach 1/4} (not3/7).

## Key claim
Using a general three-quark bound state that satisfies the Pauli principle and conserves angular momentum and isospin, the paper shows that if any single quark dominates as x→1 for a spin-up proton, it must be a spin-up u quark; this forces A₁p, A₁n, and Δu/u to approach 1, but Δd/d is bounded between -1/3 and 0 for all x, contradicting perturbative QCD predictions that Δd/d→1.

## Method
The author constructs the most general spin-1/2, isospin-1/2 three-quark bound state for a proton (uud) using a single three-body spatial function φ(123) that satisfies a constraint from isospin conservation. The wave function is then used to derive quark spin distributions u↑, u↓, d↑, d↓ by integrating over the coordinates of the two spectator quarks, with the struck quark's coordinate in boldface. The analysis is applied to deep inelastic scattering at high x (above 0.3), where sea quarks and explicit gluon effects are negligible.

## Key numbers
- The ratio F₁n/F₁p approaches 1/4 as x→1 (not 3/7).
- The spin distribution Δd/d is bounded by -1/3 ≤ Δd/d ≤ 0 for any x.
- For SU(6) symmetry, Δd/d = -1/3; the bound is model independent for any three-quark bound state satisfying the Pauli principle, angular momentum, and isospin conservation.
- Current measurements of Δd/d up to x = 0.6 show behavior consistent with approaching -1/3 (Zheng, 2004).

## Context
This work extends the general three-quark bound-state formalism developed by Franklin (2004) and applies it to high-x spin structure, contradicting perturbative QCD predictions by Farrar and Jackson (1975) and Brodsky (1995) that Δd/d→1 as x→1. The results provide model-independent constraints for upcoming experiments at higher x, such as those measuring A₁n beyond x=0.6 (Zheng, 2004), and challenge the SU(6) prediction of A₁p = 5/9 by requiring both A₁p and A₁n to approach 1 as x→1.
