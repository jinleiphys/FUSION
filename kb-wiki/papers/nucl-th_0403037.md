# Models of the Nonmesonic Weak Decay

> I review the current status of understanding the mechanism of the nonmesonic weak decays (NMWD) of hypernuclei. Long standing problem on the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio has been solved by considering short-range weak interactions properly. This leaves a few remaining problems, such as the asymmetry of the emitted proton with respect to the Lambda polarization and validity of the Delta I=1/2 in the nonmesonic weak decay.

# Models of the Nonmesonic Weak Decay

## Key claim

The long-standing problem of the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio in nonmesonic weak decays of hypernuclei has been solved by properly including short-range weak interactions, specifically through direct quark and heavy meson exchange mechanisms; however, the proton asymmetry parameter and the validity of the Delta I=1/2 rule remain unresolved issues.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical models of the nonmesonic weak decay (NMWD) of hypernuclei, comparing one-pion exchange (OPE), one-boson exchange (OBE) with heavy mesons including rho and correlated two-pion exchange, and direct quark (DQ) models based on four-quark weak vertices. Calculations are performed for s-shell hypernuclei, particularly A=4 and A=5 systems, and compared with experimental data from KEK E462 and E508 experiments using back-to-back coincidence measurements to isolate two-body decay events.

## Key numbers

- The Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio for Lambda-5 He: experimental value 0.44 ± 0.11 (KEK E462/E508)
- The Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio for Lambda-5 He: pi+K+DQ model gives 0.70, OBE (all mesons) gives 0.46, pi+K+omega+2pi/rho,sigma gives 0.39
- Total nonmesonic decay rate Gamma_NM for Lambda-5 He: experimental 0.41 ± 0.14 (arbitrary units), pi+K+DQ gives 0.52, OBE gives 0.32, pi+K+omega+2pi/rho,sigma gives 0.42
- Proton asymmetry parameter alpha_p(NM) for Lambda-5 He: experimental 0.09 ± 0.08, theoretical predictions range from -0.68 to +0.12
- Ratio alpha = Gamma_NM(Lambda-4 H)/Gamma_NM(Lambda-4 He) = (0.17 ± 0.11)/(0.17 ± 0.05)
- Ratio beta = Gamma_nn(Lambda-5 He)/Gamma_pn(Lambda-5 He) = 0.48 ± 0.10
- Expected cross section for np -> Lambda p weak production: order 10^-39 to 10^-40 cm^2

## Context

This review extends the earlier OPE model of Block and Dalitz (1963) and the heavy meson exchange work of Dubach et al. (1996) and Parreno et al. (1997), resolving the Gamma_n/Gamma_p discrepancy that persisted for decades. The direct quark approach introduced by Cheung et al. (1983) and developed by Inoue et al. (1994) and Sasaki et al. (2000) provides the short-range mechanism needed to bring theory into agreement with experiment. The remaining open questions concern the proton asymmetry parameter and the possible violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule in the two-baryon weak interaction, which can be tested through precise measurements of Lambda-4 H decay rates and pi+ emission from hypernuclei.
