# Impact of nuclear shape fluctuations in high-energy heavy ion collisions

> The shape of atomic nuclei is often interpreted to possess a quadrupole deformation that fluctuates around some average profile. We investigate the impact of nuclear shape fluctuations on the initial state geometry in heavy ion collisions, particularly its eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ and inverse size $d_{\perp}$, which can be related to the elliptic and radial flow in the final state. The fluctuation in overall quadrupole deformation enhances the variances and modifies the skewness and kurtosis of the $\varepsilon_2$ and $d_{\perp}$ in a controllable manner. The fluctuation in triaxiality reduces the difference between prolate and oblate shape for any observable, whose values, in the large fluctuation limit, approach those obtained in collisions of rigid triaxial nuclei. The method to disentangle the mean and variance of the quadrupole deformation is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that fluctuations in the quadrupole deformation parameters \((\beta, \gamma)\) of atomic nuclei significantly modify the variances, skewness, and kurtosis of the initial-state eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\) and inverse size \(d_{\perp}\) in heavy ion collisions, and that in the limit of large triaxiality fluctuations the observables become indistinguishable from those of rigid triaxial nuclei, complicating the extraction of the average nuclear shape.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo Glauber model simulations of \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions at zero impact parameter, sampling \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) from Gaussian distributions with varying means \((\bar{\beta}, \bar{\gamma})\) and standard deviations \((\sigma_{\beta}, \sigma_{\gamma})\). They calculated the eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\) and inverse transverse size \(d_{\perp}\) from the transverse positions of participating nucleons, and derived analytical expressions for the cumulants of these quantities in terms of the moments of \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\), including corrections for non-Gaussian fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- For \(\lr{\varepsilon_2^2}\) and \(\lr{(\delta d_{\perp}/d_{\perp})^2}\), the leading-order liquid-drop model estimates are \(\frac{3}{4\pi}\lr{\beta^2}\) and \(\frac{1}{32\pi}\lr{\beta^2}\), respectively.
- A twenty-degree fluctuation in triaxiality (\(\sigma_{\gamma} = \pi/9\) rad) reduces the three-particle correlation signal by nearly 40\%.
- The optimal combination \(\lr{\varepsilon_2^4} - k \lr{\varepsilon_2^2}^2\) that minimizes sensitivity to \(\sigma_{\beta}\) is found at \(k = 2.541\), yielding a best precision for determining \(\bar{\beta}\) of about 7\%.
- For the normalized correlator \(\rho\) in prolate nuclei with large \(\bar{\beta}\), the value converges to approximately \(-0.62\), nearly independent of \(\sigma_{\beta}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Jia 2021) by systematically incorporating fluctuations of both \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) into the initial-state geometry of heavy ion collisions, building on earlier efforts to constrain nuclear structure from isobar collisions (STAR 2021, Zhang 2022). It provides a framework to disentangle the mean quadrupole deformation from its variance using multi-particle correlations, which is essential for interpreting data from \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr collisions at RHIC and for future measurements at the LHC. The results also connect to low-energy nuclear structure studies of shape coexistence and \(\gamma\)-soft nuclei (Bender 2008, Rodriguez 2010).
