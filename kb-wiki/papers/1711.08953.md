# Individual low-energy toroidal dipole state in $^{24}$Mg

> The low-energy dipole excitations in $^{24}$Mg are investigated within the Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) for axial nuclei. The calculations with the force SLy6 reveal a remarkable feature: the lowest $I^πK=1^-1$ excitation (E = 7.92 MeV) in $^{24}$Mg is a vortical toroidal state (TS) representing a specific vortex-antivortex realization of well-known spherical Hill's vortex in a strongly deformed axial confinement. This is a striking example of an {\it individual} TS which can be much easier discriminated in experiment than the toroidal dipole resonance embracing many states. The TS acquires the lowest energy due to the huge prolate axial deformation in $^{24}$Mg. The result persists for different Skyrme parameterizations (SLy6, SVbas, SkM*). We analyze spectroscopic properties of the TS and its relation with the cluster structure of $^{24}$Mg. Similar TS could exist in other highly prolate light nuclei. They could serve as promising tests for various reactions to probe a vortical (toroidal) nuclear flow.

## Key claim

The lowest \(I^\pi K = 1^-1\) excitation in \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) (calculated at 7.92 MeV with the SLy6 Skyrme force) is an individual toroidal state, not a resonance; this result is conditional on the nucleus having an exceptionally large axial prolate deformation (\(\beta_\text{exp} = 0.605 \pm 0.008\)), and it persists across three different Skyrme parameterizations (SLy6, SVbas, SkM*).

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) calculations for axial nuclei using the Skyrme functional, with a 2D mesh in cylindrical coordinates (mesh size 0.4 fm, box extending to three times the nuclear radius). The single-particle spectrum included levels up to +55 MeV, and volume monopole pairing was treated at the BCS level. The toroidal and compression transition strengths were computed using current-dependent operators involving the curl and divergence of the nuclear current, respectively, with center-of-mass corrections for deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The lowest \(1^-1\) toroidal state in \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) is calculated at **E = 7.92 MeV** with the SLy6 force; with SVbas it appears at **7.26 MeV**, and with SkM* at **8.19 MeV**.
- The experimental axial prolate deformation of \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) is \(\beta = 0.605 \pm 0.008\) (from \(B(E2)\uparrow\) measurements); the QRPA equilibrium deformations are \(\beta = 0.536\) (SLy6), 0.525 (SVbas), and 0.493 (SkM*).
- The toroidal 7.92-MeV state has a collective downshift of **0.85 MeV** (difference between QRPA energy and the energy of its largest two-quasiparticle component).
- The state exhibits a strong E3 transition: \(B(E3, 0^+0_{\text{g.s.}} \to 3^-1) = 402\ \text{e}^2\text{fm}^6\) (11.7 W.u.).
- The isovector E1 decay to the ground state is \(B(E1, 1^-1 \to 0^+0_{\text{g.s.}}) = 2.52 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{e}^2\text{fm}^2\).
- The Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is exhausted by 100% (SLy6, SVbas) and 97% (SkM*); the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule is exhausted by about 97%.

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent studies of the toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) in spherical and deformed nuclei (e.g., Kv11, Rep13, Rei14, NePAN16, Rep17) by demonstrating that in a highly prolate light nucleus like \(^{24}\text{Mg}\), the toroidal mode can appear as an individual low-energy state rather than a broad resonance. The result enables experimental searches for such individual toroidal states in other strongly deformed light nuclei (e.g., \(^{10}\text{Be}\), \(^{20,22,24}\text{Ne}\), \(^{32,34}\text{Mg}\)), and provides a clean test case for reactions probing vortical nuclear flow, such as \((e,e')\) back scattering or \((\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)\).
