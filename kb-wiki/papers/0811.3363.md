# Resolving the HBT Puzzle in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision

> Two particle correlation data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider have provided detailed femtoscopic information describing the space-time structure of the emission of pions. This data had avoided description with hydrodynamic-based approaches, in contrast to the success of hydrodynamics in reproducing other classes of observables. This failure has inspired the term ``HBT puzzle'', where HBT refers to femtoscopic studies which were originally based on Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry. Here, the puzzle is shown to originate not from a single shortcoming of hydrodynamic models, but the combination of several effects: mainly including pre-thermalized acceleration, using a stiffer equation of state, and adding viscous corrections.

## Key claim

The HBT puzzle is resolved not by a single correction but by the combination of three effects: pre-thermalized acceleration, a stiffer equation of state consistent with lattice QCD, and viscous corrections, each of which increases the explosiveness of the collision and lowers the \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) ratio.

## Method

A hydrodynamic model coupled to a cascade code for the final breakup stage was used to generate the source function \(S({\bf P},{\bf r})\) from the times and positions of last collisions of pions. Correlation functions were computed via the Koonin equation and fitted to a Gaussian source to extract the femtoscopic radii \(R_{\rm out}\), \(R_{\rm side}\), and \(R_{\rm long}\) as functions of transverse momentum \(k_t\). The benchmark calculation used a first-order phase transition, no pre-thermal flow, and zero viscosity; sequential improvements added pre-thermal acceleration (starting expansion at 0.1 fm/\(c\)), a stiffer equation of state with a soft region of half the width and \(c_s^2=0.1\), and shear viscosity set at twice the KSS bound.

## Key numbers

* The benchmark calculation overstates the \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) ratio by \(\sim 40\%\) and overstates \(R_{\rm long}\) by \(\sim 25\%\) for 100 \(A\) GeV Au on 100 \(A\) GeV Au collisions at RHIC.
* The stiffer equation of state assumed a soft region with speed of sound \(c_s^2=0.1\) (rather than zero for a first-order transition) and a stiff region with \(c_s^2=0.3\); the benchmark first-order transition used a latent heat \(L=1.6\) GeV/fm\(^3\) and a lower mixed-phase bound at \(\epsilon_h\approx 500\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
* Adding shear viscosity at twice the KSS bound (\(\eta_{\rm min}=s/4\pi\)) lowered the \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) ratio by \(\sim 10\%\).
* The final calculation, including all three corrections and an improved relative wave function, matches the experimental radii within \(\approx 5\%\), which is less than the systematic experimental errors of 5-10\%.

## Context

This work resolves the long-standing HBT puzzle, where hydrodynamic models had failed to reproduce femtoscopic radii from RHIC despite successfully describing spectra and elliptic flow (Heinz, 2002; Teaney, 2001). Prior hydrodynamic studies (Soff, 2000; Hirano, 2002) used first-order phase transitions and no pre-thermal flow, while microscopic approaches (Petersen, 2008; Li, 2008) had more success due to stiffer effective equations of state. The resolution enables future work to test the same model against elliptic flow, non-identical particle correlations, non-Gaussian source features, and reaction-plane-dependent radii, and to perform a full statistical analysis of model parameters.
