# Neutrino Masses and Oscillations: Triumphs and Challenges

> The recent progress in establishing the existence of finite neutrino masses and mixing between generations of neutrinos has been remarkable, if not astounding. The combined results from studies of atmospheric neutrinos, solar neutrinos, and reactor antineutrinos paint an intriguing picture for theorists and provide clear motivation for future experimental studies. In this review, we summarize the status of experimental and theoretical work in this field and explore the future opportunities that emerge in light of recent discoveries.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the established evidence for finite neutrino masses and mixing, but notes that while the existence of oscillations is "strong and irrefutable," the absolute mass scale, the mass hierarchy (normal vs. inverted), the value of the mixing angle θ₁₃, the nature of neutrinos (Dirac vs. Majorana), and the possibility of CP violation in the lepton sector remain open questions.

## Method

The authors present a theoretical framework for neutrino masses and mixing, including the see-saw mechanism, the formalism of neutrino oscillations in vacuum and in matter (including the MSW effect), and the parametrization of the 3×3 Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata (PMNS) mixing matrix. They then review experimental results from solar neutrino experiments, atmospheric neutrino studies, reactor antineutrino experiments (KamLAND), and searches for neutrinoless double beta decay and tritium beta decay, and discuss the implications for cosmology.

## Key numbers

- The invisible width of the Z boson from LEP experiments gives the number of active neutrino flavors as N_ν = 2.984 ± 0.008
- The matter oscillation length in rock is L_0 ≈ 10⁴ km, and in the center of the Sun L_0 ≈ 200 km
- The effective potential for electron neutrinos in matter is V_C = 7.6 Y_e (ρ/10¹⁴ g/cm³) eV, where Y_e = N_e/(N_p + N_n)
- The ratio L_osc/L_0 = 0.22 [E_ν/1 MeV] [ρY_e/100 g/cm³] [7×10⁻⁵ eV²/Δm²] determines the transition between vacuum and matter oscillation regimes
- For the preferred solar solution, Δm² ∼ 7×10⁻⁵ eV² and sin²2θ ≃ 0.83; the pp neutrinos with 4E_ν/Δm² < 2.4×10¹⁰ eV⁻¹ and ⁷Be neutrinos with 4E_ν/Δm² = 4.9×10¹⁰ eV⁻¹ undergo vacuum oscillations, while ⁸B neutrinos with 4E_ν/Δm² > 2.55×10¹¹ eV⁻¹ undergo MSW matter oscillations

## Context

This review synthesizes the experimental and theoretical developments following the discoveries of atmospheric neutrino oscillations by Super-Kamiokande and solar neutrino oscillations confirmed by the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory and KamLAND. It extends the framework established by Pontecorvo (1957), Maki, Nakagawa and Sakata (1962), and the MSW effect (Wolfenstein 1978, Mikheyev and Smirnov 1986), and sets the stage for future experiments aimed at measuring θ₁₃, determining the mass hierarchy, searching for CP violation, and testing the Majorana nature of neutrinos through neutrinoless double beta decay.
