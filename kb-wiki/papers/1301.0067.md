# Hyperons and Condensed Kaons in Compact Stars

> Using the Callan-Klebanov bound state model for hyperons simulated on crystal lattice to describe strange baryonic matter, we argue that to ${\cal O} (1)$ in the large $N_c$ counting to which the theory is robust, hyperons can figure only when -- or after -- kaons condense in compact-star matter. We also discuss how the skyrmion-half-skyrmion topological transition affects the equation of state (EoS) of dense baryonic matter. The observations made in this note open wide the issue of how to theoretically access the EoS of compact stars.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, to leading order in the large \(N_c\) expansion (i.e., to \({\cal O}(N_c^0)\)), hyperons can appear in compact-star matter only at the same density at which s-wave kaons condense, or after kaon condensation has already occurred; this conclusion is robust only to next-to-leading order in \(N_c\), and higher-order corrections could reverse the order of appearance.

## Method

The authors use the Callan-Klebanov (CK) bound-state model for hyperons, in which anti-kaons are bound to \(SU(2)\) skyrmions, and simulate dense baryonic matter by placing multi-skyrmions on a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice. They compute the in-medium kaon mass \(m_K^*\) as a function of baryon density, and then use the CK mass formula to obtain the energy difference between hyperonic matter and non-strange matter to \({\cal O}(N_c^0)\), which reduces to the in-medium kaon mass. The skyrmion-half-skyrmion topological transition at density \(n_{1/2}\) is incorporated via the crystal lattice background.

## Key numbers

* The skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition occurs at \(n_{1/2}\sim (1.3-2.0)n_0\), where \(n_0\) is nuclear matter density.
* For a dilaton mass in vacuum \(m_\chi\approx 720\) MeV, the in-medium kaon mass shows an abrupt drop at \(n_{1/2}\) (see Fig. 1 of the paper).
* The hyperfine coefficient \(c\) in matter-free space is determined from the mass formula to be \(c\sim 0.7\).
* The symmetry energy of pure neutron matter in the absence of hyperons is \(E_{sym} = \frac{1}{8\tilde{\Omega}}\delta^2\), where \(\delta=(N-P)/(N+P)\) and \(\tilde{\Omega}\) is the moment of inertia of the unit cell.
* With hyperons included, the symmetry energy becomes \(E^{hyp}_{sym}\approx \frac{1}{8\tilde{\Omega}}(1-d^*)\delta^2\), where \(d^*\) is the in-medium analogue of \(c\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on whether hyperons or kaon condensation appears first in compact-star matter, a question previously addressed by the scenario of Brown, Thorsson, Kubodera, and Rho (1992) for kaon condensation and by standard hyperon models. The authors use the CK bound-state model (Callan and Klebanov, 1985) and the multi-skyrmion crystal approach (Park, Kim, and Rho, 2008) to unify the description of both strangeness channels. The result opens the issue of how to theoretically access the equation of state (EoS) of compact stars, particularly the interplay between the softening from strangeness and the stiffening needed to accommodate a 1.97 \(M_\odot\) neutron star.
