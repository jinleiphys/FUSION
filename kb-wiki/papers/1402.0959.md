# Neutrino Emissivities from Deuteron-Breakup and Formation in Supernovae

> Neutrino emissions from electron/positron capture on the deuteron and the nucleon-nucleon fusion processes in the surface region of a supernova core are studied. These weak processes are evaluated in the standard nuclear physics approach, which consists of one-nucleon and two-nucleon-exchange currents and nuclear wave functions generated by a high precision nucleon-nucleon potential. In addition to the cross sections for these processes involving the deuteron, we present neutrino emissivities due to these processes calculated for typical profiles of core-collapsed supernovae. These novel neutrino emissivities are compared with the standard neutrino emission mechanisms. We find that the neutrino emissivity due to the electron capture on the deuteron is comparable to that on the proton in the deuteron abundant region. The implications of the new channels involving deuterons for the supernova mechanism are discussed.

## Key claim
The neutrino emissivity from electron capture on the deuteron is comparable to that on the proton in regions where deuterons are abundant, but the cross section per bound proton is smaller than for a free proton, so the net neutrino emissivity per proton is reduced when deuterons are present.

## Method
The authors used the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) with nuclear wave functions from a high-precision nucleon-nucleon potential and one-nucleon plus two-nucleon meson-exchange currents (pion and rho exchange). They calculated cross sections for deuteron breakup via electron/positron capture and deuteron formation via nucleon-nucleon fusion, then integrated these over thermal distributions for two representative supernova profiles at 150 ms post-bounce to obtain neutrino emissivities.

## Key numbers
- The cross section for electron capture on the deuteron is smaller than that on the proton by a factor larger than 3 in the low-energy region (E_e < 50 MeV), mainly due to the higher threshold energy.
- At E_e ~ 150 MeV, the cross section for electron capture on a bound proton becomes almost comparable to that on a free proton.
- The deuteron mass fraction amounts to about 10% in the neutrino-emitting cooling regions at densities ~10^11-10^12 g/cm^3.
- The total neutrino emissivity from electron capture on protons is effectively reduced by up to 40% when deuteron abundance is included (Composition II vs Composition I).
- The neutrino emissivity from deuteron formation via pp and np fusion is orders of magnitude smaller than from electron capture on the deuteron, but becomes comparable to NN bremsstrahlung near 100 km in the cooling region.
- The anti-neutrino emissivity from positron capture on the deuteron is smaller than that on the neutron by a factor of 10^2-10^3.

## Context
This work extends the studies of Sumiyoshi and Ropke (2008) and Arcones et al. (2008), who showed that deuterons, tritons, and He-3 can appear copiously in supernova regions between the core and shockwave, and of Nakamura et al. (2009) who investigated neutrino absorption on deuterons as an additional heating mechanism. The paper provides the first evaluation of neutrino emissivities from deuteron breakup and formation processes, which had not been included in supernova simulations. These results enable more complete supernova modeling that accounts for light element abundances, potentially affecting the cooling mechanism and neutrino heating behind the shock wave.
