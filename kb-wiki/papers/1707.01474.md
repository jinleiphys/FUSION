# Pairing Theory of the Wigner Cusp

> Conclusions: (1) Calculations with an RPA correction added to the BCS pairing correction conventionally employed in Nilsson-Strutinskij calculations account well for the variation with A of the pattern of masses near N=Z. (2) The RPA correction is insignificant for reproducing the doubly even masses and hence for the shape of the Wigner cusp. (3) It is important, however, that the macroscopic (liquid drop) symmetry energy be proportional to T(T+1). (4) This form of the macroscopic symmetry energy is understood microscopically, in terms of the RPA, to result from the nuclear superfluidity. (5) The variation of the shape of the Wigner cusp is dominated by shell effects. (6) The RPA correction significally reduces the T=0 binding in doubly odd nuclei, thus reducing the required pair coupling constant.

# Pairing Theory of the Wigner Cusp

## Key claim

Calculations with an RPA correction added to the BCS pairing correction conventionally employed in Nilsson-Strutinskij calculations account well for the variation with A of the pattern of masses near N=Z, but the RPA correction is insignificant for reproducing the doubly even masses and hence for the shape of the Wigner cusp; it is important, however, that the macroscopic (liquid drop) symmetry energy be proportional to T(T+1) rather than T^2.

## Method

The author applies a Strutinskij renormalised independent nucleons plus pairing Hamiltonian to describe nuclei near N=Z, where the total energy is written as a microscopic term from BCS plus RPA minus a smooth microscopic term plus a liquid drop energy. The BCS and RPA equations are solved for neutron, proton, and neutron-proton channels separately using Woods-Saxon single-nucleon spectra, with the liquid drop symmetry energy taken proportional to T(T+1). The five liquid drop parameters and two pairing parameters (G = G_1 A^chi) are fitted alternately to empirical masses from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation for 24 ≤ A ≤ 100 and 0 ≤ N-Z ≤ 10, and to empirical values of the doubly odd doubly even mass difference and the T=1 to T=0 energy difference in doubly odd N=Z nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation of the fitted liquid drop parameters for doubly even masses is 0.875 MeV (compared to 0.950 MeV in earlier calculations by Bentley et al. (2014))
- The rms deviation of the fitted pairing parameters for the doubly odd doubly even mass differences and T=1 to T=0 energy differences is 0.708 MeV
- The fitted parameters are a_v, a_vt, a_s, a_st, a_c, G_1, chi = 15.07, 107.4, 16.04, 133.6, 0.6506, 7.232, -0.7604 (all except chi in MeV)
- The RPA correction in doubly even nuclei is largely constant with an average of about 0.7 MeV
- In doubly odd T=0 states, the RPA correction decreases from about 2.3 MeV to about 1.2 MeV over the range of the plot
- If the liquid drop symmetry energy is replaced by a T^2 proportionality, the rms deviation of doubly even masses increases from 0.875 MeV to 1.073 MeV, and E*(A,1) - E*(A,0) decreases by 1-2 MeV
- The RPA correction makes a contribution of about 1 MeV to the T=0 doubly odd doubly even mass differences
- The absolute value of the negative T^2 correction from the RPA remainder in the uniform case amounts to at most 3 MeV for T ≤ 0.2A, A ≥ 24

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies by Neergård (2002, 2003, 2009, 2016) on a microscopic pairing Hamiltonian with RPA corrections, and builds on the Strutinskij renormalised calculations by Bentley and Frauendorf (2013) and Bentley et al. (2014). The T(T+1) form of the symmetry energy was previously identified by Frauendorf and Sheikh (1999, 2000) as analogous to an axially symmetric rotor in isospace, with the superfluid pair gap acting as the deformation. The paper enables a microscopic understanding of the Wigner cusp in terms of shell effects dominating the variation of its shape, while the macroscopic T(T+1) symmetry energy arises from nuclear superfluidity via the RPA quasi-Nambu-Goldstone mode. The author notes that work by Negrea and Sandulescu based on quartet condensation addresses the same differential mass combinations and reproduces them equally well.
