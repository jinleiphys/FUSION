# Emergent chiral spin symmetry, non-perturbative dynamics and thermoparticles in hot QCD

> Several non-perturbative results for hot QCD are challenging some aspects of the phase diagram and its associated degrees of freedom which were previously believed to be well understood. With increasing temperature, the chiral crossover is followed by an intermediate region with an approximate chiral spin symmetry larger than chiral symmetry, in which pseudo-scalar mesons continue to exist as hadron-like excitations, before at some higher temperature the expected chiral symmetry is recovered. By testing general formal considerations against lattice data, it can be shown that thermally modified versions of stable vacuum particles, so-called thermoparticles, form the constituents of thermal quantum field theories, with properties quite different from what is expected perturbatively. This ``viewpoint'' aims to raise broader and, in particular, phenomenological interest in these directions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard perturbative picture of hot QCD, a smooth chiral crossover around 156 MeV followed by a deconfined quark-gluon plasma with partonic degrees of freedom, is challenged by non-perturbative lattice results showing an intermediate temperature region (up to roughly 2-3 times the chiral crossover temperature) where an approximate chiral spin symmetry larger than ordinary chiral symmetry is realized, confining dynamics persist, and pseudo-scalar mesons survive as thermally modified "thermoparticles."

## Method

The author reviews and synthesizes multiple lattice QCD simulation results, including spatial and temporal meson correlation functions computed with domain wall fermions (Rohrhofer 2019, Chiu 2023, Aoki 2025) and HISQ fermions (Bazavov 2019), as well as spectral function reconstructions using the rigorous Kallen-Lehmann-type representation of thermal correlators (Lowdon 2022). The thermoparticle formalism is tested by extracting damping factors from spatial correlators and using them to predict temporal correlators, which are then compared directly to lattice data without any ill-posed inversion. Additional comparisons are made with perturbation theory for both QCD entropy density and scalar phi^4 theory correlators.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature is T_ch ≈ 156 MeV
- The deconfinement-like transition temperature T_d is approximately 2-3 times T_ch, corresponding to roughly 500 MeV for N_f=2+1 QCD with physical quark masses
- Screening masses in 12 different quantum number channels show an abrupt change in T-dependence around T_d ~ 500 MeV, with m_scr/(2πT) changing from nearly vertical to almost horizontal behavior
- Even at T ~ 100 GeV, the perturbative series for the entropy density shows no sign of rapid convergence when compared to lattice results
- For the phi^4 theory, perturbation theory increasingly deviates from the full temporal correlator as the temperature rises from N_tau=8 to N_tau=2 (where N_tau = (aT)^{-1})

## Context

This viewpoint extends and challenges the prevailing picture established by lattice simulations (Aoki 2006) and perturbative calculations (Kajantie 2002, Braaten 1994) that QCD above T_ch is dominated by deconfined quarks and gluons. It builds on earlier work by Glozman (2022) identifying three distinct temperature regimes and by Rohrhofer (2019) demonstrating SU(4) chiral spin symmetry multiplets in spatial correlators. The thermoparticle concept, developed formally by Bros (1998, 2001) and Buchholz (1993), is shown to provide a consistent description of lattice data that standard perturbative approaches (including resummations and 2PI formalisms) fail to capture. The results enable new phenomenological investigations of experimental signatures for hadron-like excitations persisting above the chiral crossover and for the proposed band of chiral spin symmetry extending to finite baryon density, potentially connecting to the quarkyonic regime (McLerran 2007).
