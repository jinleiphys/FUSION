# Quantum Phase Transitions in the U(5)-O(6) Large N limit

> The U(5)-O(6) transitional behavior of the Interacting Boson Model in the large N limit is revisited. Some low-lying energy levels, overlaps of the ground state wavefunctions, B(E2) transition rate for the decay of the first excited energy level to the ground state, and the order parameters are calculated for different total numbers of bosons. The results show that critical behaviors of these quantities are greatly enhanced with increasing of the total number of bosons N, especially fractional occupation probability for d bosons in the ground state, the difference between the expectation value of n_d in the first excited 0^+ state and the ground state, and another quantity related to the isomer shift behave similarly in both the O(6)-U(5) large N and U(5)-SU(3) phase transitions.

# Quantum Phase Transitions in the U(5)-O(6) Large N limit

## Key claim
The critical behaviors of order parameters in the U(5)-O(6) transitional region of the Interacting Boson Model are greatly enhanced with increasing boson number N, and in the large N limit the order parameters ρ_d, v_1, and v_2 behave similarly in both the O(6)-U(5) and U(5)-SU(3) phase transitions, though the authors note that due to computational limitations the U(5)-SU(3) case cannot be calculated exactly for N ≥ 30, leaving the large N behavior of that transition as an open question.

## Method
The authors diagonalized a schematic U(5)-O(6) Hamiltonian H = (1-x) n_d + (x/f(N)) S^+ S^- in the U(6) ⊃ U(5) ⊃ O(5) ⊃ O(3) basis for boson numbers N = 10, 40, 120, 300, and 1000, with f(N)=N and control parameter x in [0,1]. They calculated low-lying energy levels, ground state wavefunction overlaps with the U(5) and O(6) limits, B(E2) transition rates for the first excited ν=1 level to the ground state, and the order parameters ρ_d (fractional d-boson occupation), v_1 (difference in n_d expectation between first excited 0^+ and ground state), and v_2 (difference in n_d between 2_1 and 0_1 states, related to isomer shift).

## Key numbers
- Energy level calculations were performed for N = 10, 40, 120, and 300, showing a minimum in low-lying excitation energy in the control parameter range 0.45 < x < 0.65, with the minimum growing sharper as N increases.
- Ground state wavefunction overlaps were calculated for N = 10, 40, 300, and 1000, with a sharp change in |⟨0_g;x|0_g;x=0⟩| around a critical point x_c ∼ 0.45 in the large N limit.
- B(E2) transition rates for decay of the first excited ν=1 level to the ground state were calculated for N = 10, 40, 300, and 1000, expressed in units where B(E2; 1→0) = 100 in the U(5) (x=0) limit.
- The critical point region becomes progressively narrower with increasing N, with a cusp around x ∼ 0.45, in agreement with Rowe (2004) who showed the N=40 case.

## Context
This work extends studies of the U(5)-O(6) transitional region in the Interacting Boson Model previously reported by Arias et al. (2003) and Rowe (2004), who examined the narrowing of the critical point region with increasing boson number and explained it via quasidynamical symmetry. The paper also builds on the order parameter analysis of Iachello and Zamfir (2004), who introduced v_1 and v_2 to distinguish first-order from second-order phase transitions in finite N systems. The results connect the finite-N IBM to the Bohr-Mottelson model in the large N limit, and suggest that the E(5) symmetry derived from the Bohr-Mottelson model can only be approximately realized in the IBM, consistent with Arias et al. (2003).
