# New lessons from the nucleon mass, lattice QCD and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> I will review heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for the nucleon delta degrees of freedom and then examine the recent dynamical lattice calculations of the nucleon mass from the BMW, ETM, JLQCD, LHP, MILC, NPLQCD, PACS-CS, QCDSF/UKQCD and RBC/UKQCD Collaborations. Performing the chiral extrapolations of these results, one finds remarkable agreement with the physical nucleon mass, from each lattice data set. However, a careful examination of the lattice data and the resulting extrapolation functions reveals some unexpected results, serving to highlight the significant challenges in performing chiral extrapolations of baryon quantities. All the N_f=2+1 dynamical results can be quantitatively described by theoretically unmotivated fit function linear in the pion mass with m_pi ~ 750 -190 MeV. When extrapolated to the physical point, the results are in striking agreement with the physical nucleon mass. I will argue that knowledge of each lattice datum of the nucleon mass is required at the 1-2% level, including all systematics, in order to conclusively determine if this is a bizarre conspiracy of lattice artifacts or rather a mysterious phenomenon of QCD.

## Key claim
All N_f=2+1 dynamical lattice results for the nucleon mass can be quantitatively described by a theoretically unmotivated fit function linear in the pion mass over the range m_pi ~ 750–190 MeV, and when extrapolated to the physical point they agree strikingly with the physical nucleon mass; however, this may be either a bizarre conspiracy of lattice artifacts or a mysterious phenomenon of QCD, and each lattice datum must be known at the 1–2% level (including all systematics) to decide.

## Method
The author reviewed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) for the nucleon and delta degrees of freedom, then collected and compared recent dynamical lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon mass from nine collaborations (BMW, ETM, JLQCD, LHP, MILC, NPLQCD, PACS-CS, QCDSF/UKQCD, RBC/UKQCD). Chiral extrapolations were performed using continuum SU(2) HBChPT formulae (with and without explicit delta degrees of freedom), a covariant baryon ChPT formula, and a simple linear fit in m_pi. The lattice results were plotted as a function of m_pi and compared across different lattice spacings, volumes, and scale-setting methods.

## Key numbers
- LHP mixed-action extrapolation using continuum SU(2) HBChPT with deltas gave M_N = 954 ± 42 ± 20 MeV (statistical and systematic errors).
- Linear fit to LHP results: M_N = alpha_0^N + alpha_1^N m_pi = 938 ± 9 MeV, with chi^2/dof = 1.46.
- LHP pion mass range: m_pi ~ 290–750 MeV.
- PACS-CS lightest pion mass: m_pi ~ 156 MeV, with m_pi L ~ 2.29.
- BMW lightest pion mass: m_pi ~ 190 MeV, with m_pi L >= 4.
- BMW lattice spacings: 0.125 fm, 0.085 fm, 0.065 fm.
- MILC lattice spacings: 0.125 fm, 0.09 fm, 0.06 fm.
- ETM lattice spacings: 0.083 fm, 0.0655 fm; volumes: 2.10 fm, 2.66 fm; m_pi L >= 3.3.
- JLQCD lattice spacing: 0.118 fm; volume: 1.9 fm; m_pi L >= 2.8.
- QCDSF/UKQCD lattice spacings: 0.085–0.067 fm; volumes: 1.5–2.6 fm; m_pi L >= 2.1.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Jenkins and Manohar, 1990) and covariant baryon ChPT (Becher and Leutwyler, 1999) to analyze the nucleon mass from multiple lattice QCD collaborations. It reveals that all N_f=2+1 lattice data exhibit an unexpectedly linear dependence on m_pi, contradicting the predicted chiral expansion (M_N = M_0 + c_1 m_q + c_{3/2} m_q^{3/2} + ...). This finding challenges the conventional understanding of chiral extrapolations for baryon masses and highlights the need for higher-precision lattice data (1–2% per datum) to distinguish between lattice artifacts and a genuine QCD phenomenon. The work also compares N_f=2 results (ETM, JLQCD, QCDSF/UKQCD) which show more variation and potential finite-volume effects.
