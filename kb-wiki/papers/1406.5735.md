# Low-energy R-matrix fits for the 6Li(d,a)4He S factor

> Background: The information about the 6Li(d,a)4He reaction rates of the astrophysical interest can be obtained by extrapolating direct data to the lower energies, or by indirect methods. The indirect Trojan Horse method, as well as various R-matrix and polynomial fits to direct data, estimate the electron screening energies much larger than the adiabatic limit. Calculations that include the subthreshold resonance estimate smaller screening energies. Purpose: Obtain the 6Li(d,a)4He reaction R-matrix parameters and the astrophysical S factor for the energies relevant to the stellar plasmas by fitting the R-matrix formulas for the subthreshold resonances to the S factor data above 60 keV. Methods: The bare S factor is calculated using the single and the two-level R-matrix formulas for the closest to the threshold 0+ and 2+ subthreshold states at 22.2, 20.2 and 20.1 MeV. The electron screening potential Ue is then obtained by fitting it as a single parameter to the low energy data. Results: The low energy S factor is dominated by the 2+ subthreshold resonance at 22.2 MeV. The influence of the other two subthreshold states is small. R-matrix fits result in the electron screening that is smaller than the adiabatic value. Neglecting the electron screening above 60 keV reduces the electron screening potential significantly. Calculations show a large ambiguity associated with a choice of the initial channel radius. Conclusions: The R matrix fits do not show a significantly larger Ue than predicted by the atomic physics models. The R-matrix best fit produces Ue=149.5 eV and Sb(0)=21.7 MeV b.

# Low-energy R-matrix fits for the 6Li(d,α)4He S factor

## Key claim

The R-matrix fits to the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction do not show a significantly larger electron screening potential Ue than predicted by atomic physics models, contrary to earlier claims of enhanced screening. The best fit produces Ue = 149.5 eV, which is below the adiabatic limit of 186 eV, but this value depends strongly on whether the screening potential is fitted simultaneously with all parameters or extracted after neglecting screening above 60 keV.

## Method

The bare astrophysical S factor was calculated using single-level and two-level R-matrix formulas for the three subthreshold states closest to the 8Be compound nucleus threshold: the 2+ state at 22.2 MeV (bound by 80 keV), the 0+ state at 20.2 MeV, and a second 2+ state at 20.1 MeV. The R-matrix fits used the s-wave approximation for the incoming deuteron and treated the reduced width amplitudes, asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs), and resonance energies as free parameters, with the electron screening potential Ue then fitted as a single parameter to low-energy data below 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit electron screening potential (three-level fit, all parameters varied simultaneously): Ue = 149.5 eV (with uncertainties +19.3 eV, -30.5 eV)
- Bare astrophysical S factor at zero energy from the same fit: Sb(0) = 21.7 MeV·b (with uncertainties +0.54 MeV·b, -0.06 MeV·b)
- Adiabatic limit for electron screening in this reaction: 186 eV (from difference in atomic binding energies between Li and Be+)
- When fitting only data above 60 keV and then extracting Ue as a single parameter, the screening potential dropped to Ue = 71.4 eV (three-level fit)
- Alpha partial width for the dominant 2+ subthreshold resonance at 22.2 MeV: Γα = 0.77 MeV (from the simultaneous fit), compared to the experimental total width of 0.8 MeV
- Single-level unrestricted fit gave Ue = 162.0 eV and Sb(0) = 21.6 MeV·b

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix analysis of the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction by including three subthreshold resonances, following earlier studies by Barker (2002) and Ruprecht et al. (2004) that used single-level or two-level fits. The Trojan Horse method measurement by Spitaleri et al. (2001) had extracted Ue = 340 ± 51 eV, and polynomial fits by Engstler et al. (1992) also gave large screening values. The present results support the conclusion of Ruprecht et al. (2004) that the lower screening energy is due to the influence of the 2+ subthreshold resonance, but find that the assumption that electron screening is negligible above 50 keV is not valid. The analysis enables improved reaction rate calculations for stellar plasmas and demonstrates the need for simultaneous fitting of screening and nuclear parameters in low-energy R-matrix analyses.
