# Factorization for jet production in heavy-ion collisions

> We develop an Effective Field Theory approach for jet observables in heavy-ion collisions, where the jet is treated as an open quantum system interacting with a hot and dense QCD medium. Within this framework, we derive a novel factorization formula for inclusive jet production, expressed as a series expansion with an increasing number of radiating subjet functions that encode forward scattering with the Quark-Gluon Plasma, convolved with perturbative matching coefficients. This work provides a systematic framework for computing jet observables at higher order and understanding their non-perturbative aspects, paving the way for future applications in heavy-ion phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors derive a factorization formula for inclusive jet production in heavy-ion collisions that, for the first time, separates vacuum and medium physics at all orders in perturbation theory, but only in the narrow-jet limit \(R \ll 1\) and under the specific hierarchy \(p_T \gg p_T R \gtrsim E_{\text{out}} \gg Q_{\text{med}} \gtrsim T \sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\), where the energy loss \(E_{\text{out}}\) is parametrically of order \(p_T R\).

## Method

The authors develop a Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) framework treating the jet as an open quantum system interacting with a Quark-Gluon Plasma. They perform a multi-stage factorization: first integrating out hard modes with virtuality above \(p_T R\) to obtain hard-scattering functions convolved with jet functions, then matching to an EFT at the medium scale \(Q_{\text{med}}\) where the jet function is refactorized into a series of collinear-soft subjet functions encoding forward scattering with the medium, convolved with perturbative matching coefficients. The medium correlator \(\varphi(\mathbf{k}, \mu, \nu, x^-)\) is separated from the process-dependent jet function via a rapidity factorization, and the collinear-soft function is expressed as a series in the number of jet-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

- The medium transverse momentum scale is defined as \(Q^2_{\text{med}} \equiv \langle k_\perp^2\rangle \sim \hat q L\), where \(\hat q\) is the jet transport parameter and \(L\) the medium length.
- The coherence angle is \(\theta_c \sim 1/(Q_{\text{med}} L)\), controlling color decoherence in the medium.
- The medium temperature at current collider experiments is in the range \(0.5-1\) GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor approximation is \(R_{AA} \sim (1 + E_{\text{out}}/p_T)^{-n}\) with \(n \sim 5\), implying \(E_{\text{out}} \lesssim p_T/n \ll p_T\).
- The expansion parameters are \(\beta \equiv E_{\text{out}}/p_T\) and \(\delta \equiv Q_{\text{med}}/E_{\text{out}}\), with \(\beta \sim R\) and \(\delta \sim R\) in the phenomenologically relevant regime.
- The one-loop result for the single-interaction collinear-soft function \({\bf F}_{1,q}^{(1)}\) agrees with GLV results from Gyulassy (2000) and Wiedemann (2000).

## Context

This work extends previous SCET applications to jets in heavy-ion collisions by Idilbi (2008), D'Eramo (2010), and Ovanesyan (2011), and builds on the vacuum factorization for inclusive jets in the narrow-jet limit developed by Dasgupta (2014), Kang (2016), and Dai (2016). The factorization accounts for both the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect and color decoherence phenomena identified by Mehtar-Tani (2010, 2012) and Casalderrey-Solana (2011, 2012). The framework enables systematic higher-order calculations and phenomenological studies of jet substructure observables in heavy-ion collisions, with future applications including the resolved case \(\theta_c \ll R\), connections to the BDMPS-Z framework, and non-linear evolution in dense media.
