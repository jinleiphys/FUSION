# Measurement of the Isolated Nuclear Two-Photon Decay in $^{72}\mathrm{Ge}$

> The nuclear two-photon or double-gamma ($2γ$) decay is a second-order electromagnetic process whereby a nucleus in an excited state emits two gamma rays simultaneously. To be able to directly measure the $2γ$ decay rate in the low-energy regime below the electron-positron pair-creation threshold, we combined the isochronous mode of a storage ring with Schottky resonant cavities. The newly developed technique can be applied to isomers with excitation energies down to $\sim100$\,keV and half-lives as short as $\sim10$\,ms. The half-life for the $2γ$ decay of the first-excited $0^+$ state in bare $^{72}\mathrm{Ge}$ ions was determined to be $23.9\left(6\right)$\,ms, which strongly deviates from expectations.

## Key claim

The half-life for the nuclear two-photon decay of the first-excited 0⁺ state in bare ⁷²Ge ions was measured to be 23.9(6) ms, which is approximately ten times shorter than expected from extrapolation of previous results on doubly-magic and semi-magic nuclei, indicating that the cancellation effect observed in those nuclides is not present in this mid-shell nucleus.

## Method

The experiment combined the isochronous mode of the Experimental Storage Ring (ESR) at GSI with non-destructive single-ion-sensitive resonant Schottky detectors (410 MHz and 245 MHz) in a technique termed combined Schottky plus Isochronous Mass Spectrometry (S+IMS). Fully ionized ⁷²Ge³²⁺ fragments were produced via few-nucleon removal reactions from a ⁷⁸Kr beam at 441 MeV/nucleon on a ⁹Be target, stored in the ESR tuned to γ ≈ γt ≈ 1.396, and their revolution frequencies were measured to resolve the isomeric state from the ground state and track the decay over time.

## Key numbers

- Half-life for the 2γ decay of the first-excited 0⁺ state in bare ⁷²Ge ions: T₁/₂ʳᵉˢᵗ = 23.9(6) ms
- Excitation energy of the isomeric state: ω₀ = 692.8(19) keV (measured via frequency difference between isomeric and ground state)
- Isomeric ratio: 3.4(2)% (initial number of isomers relative to ground state ions)
- Mass resolving power achieved: 9.1 × 10⁵
- The measured half-life is approximately a factor of 10 shorter than the extrapolation from previous results on ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ⁹⁰Zr
- Cumulative nuclear matrix element: Mγγ = 70(2) × 10⁻³ fm³

## Context

This work extends the storage-ring based non-destructive lifetime spectroscopy to shorter-lived species (millisecond regime) compared to previous experiments with electron-cooled stored beams (Litvinov, 2011). The technique enables direct measurement of 2γ decay in the low-energy regime below the electron-positron pair-creation threshold (1.022 MeV), where previous γ-ray spectroscopy had only observed 0⁺→0⁺ 2γ decays in ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ⁹⁰Zr (Schirmer, 1984; Kramp, 1987). The result enables future studies of low-lying 0⁺ isomers in exotic nuclei, including ⁹⁸Mo and ⁹⁸Zr, and may inform searches for shape coexistence and the role of such isomers in the rapid-proton capture process.
