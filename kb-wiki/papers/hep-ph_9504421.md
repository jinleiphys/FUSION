# BARYON-BARYON INTERACTIONS IN LARGE N_C CHIRAL PERTURBATION THEORY

> Interactions of two baryons are considered in large $N_C$ chiral perturbation theory and compared to the interactions derived from the Skyrme model. Special attention is given to a torus-like configuration known to be present in the Skyrme model.

# Baryon-baryon interactions in large N_C chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The paper argues that at leading order in the 1/N_C expansion, the interaction between two baryons in chiral perturbation theory produces the same torus-like meson cloud configuration at short distances as the Skyrme model does, but the author acknowledges that this conclusion relies on speculative higher-order terms in the effective action and on numerical methods with limited precision, so the quantitative agreement is not exact.

## Method

The author places two static baryon sources of fixed isospin orientation at positions ±X/2 and solves the classical Euler-Lagrange equations for the pion fields around them, using a non-unitary parametrization of the chiral fields on a three-dimensional lattice with randomly distributed points. For large separations, the linearized equations are sufficient and yield analytic results; for short separations (X = 0.7, 1.4, 2.1 fm), the full nonlinear equations including the fourth-order Skyrme stabilizing term are solved numerically, with the bare pion-nucleon coupling g_A° adjusted so that the single-baryon cloud matches the skyrmion profile.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic interaction between two baryons at large separation is given by V_asy = (9/(16π f_π^2)) g̃_A^2 D_bi(A) D_bj(B) ∂_i ∂_j (1/X) e^{-m_π X}, where f_π = 93 MeV and g̃_A is the physical πN coupling.
- For the numerical short-range study, the Skyrme parameter is set to e = 4, the bare πN coupling to g_A° = 1.72 for a sharp source of radius R_0 = 0.25 fm, and to g_A° = 2.01 for a smooth source.
- At separation X = 0.7 fm with relative isospin orientation A†B = iτ_2, the interaction energy for explicit sources is -290 MeV, compared to +780 MeV for A†B = iτ_3 and +625 MeV for A†B = 1.
- The exact mass of a single smooth source is M = -7568 MeV; the numerical lattice overestimates this by 8% at half-separation 0.35 fm, 15% at 0.7 fm, and 20% at 1.05 fm.
- For the topological B=1 soliton, the numerical mass is 1752 MeV versus the exact 1756 MeV; for the B=2 torus, the numerical mass is 3359 MeV versus the exact 3371 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Manohar (1994) that leading-order large N_C baryon-pion interactions reduce to classical equations for static sources, and it directly compares the resulting two-baryon system to the Skyrme model of Skyrme (1961). The paper shows that the long-range interaction reproduces the standard one-pion exchange potential, and that at short range the meson cloud around explicit sources forms a torus-like configuration identical in shape to the one found in the Skyrme model by Kopeliovich and Stern (1987) and Verbaarschot (1987). This enables future work to treat baryon-baryon interactions in chiral perturbation theory using the same topological structures as the Skyrme model, though the author notes that quantitative differences remain due to the finite source extension and that closer separations than 0.7 fm are beyond the numerical capabilities of this study.
