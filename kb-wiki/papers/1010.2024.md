# Effective-Range Expansion of the Neutron-Deuteron Scattering Studied by a Quark-Model Nonlocal Gaussian Potential

> The S-wave effective range parameters of the neutron-deuteron (nd) scattering are derived in the Faddeev formalism, using a nonlocal Gaussian potential based on the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. The spin-doublet low-energy eigenphase shift is sufficiently attractive to reproduce predictions by the AV18 plus Urbana three-nucleon force, yielding the observed value of the doublet scattering length and the correct differential cross sections below the deuteron breakup threshold. This conclusion is consistent with the previous result for the triton binding energy, which is nearly reproduced by fss2 without reinforcing it with the three-nucleon force.

## Key claim

The quark-model nonlocal Gaussian potential fss2 reproduces the observed doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length and low-energy differential cross sections without introducing a three-nucleon force, because its spin-doublet eigenphase shift is sufficiently attractive to match predictions from the AV18 potential plus the Urbana three-nucleon force.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation in the Faddeev formalism for neutron-deuteron elastic scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold, using a nonlocal Gaussian potential derived from the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. The energy dependence of the original resonating-group method kernel was eliminated via a renormalized off-shell transformation, and the S-wave effective-range parameters were extracted from the eigenphase shifts using single-channel effective-range formulas with a pole parameter for the doublet channel.

## Key numbers

- Doublet scattering length: \(^{2}a_{nd} = 0.65 \pm 0.04\) fm (experimental value cited from Di72)
- Triton binding energy deficiency from fss2 after charge dependence correction: 350 keV
- Difference in triton binding energy between fss2 and the nonlocal Gaussian potential: 15 keV
- Accuracy of NN phase shifts from the nonlocal Gaussian potential compared to fss2: less than \(0.1^{\circ}\)
- At \(E_n = 1.0\) MeV, the \(^{2}S_{1/2}\) eigenphase shift from fss2 is \(-14.8^{\circ}\), compared to \(-18.1^{\circ}\) for AV18 and \(-14.3^{\circ}\) for AV18+UR(3N)
- At \(E_n = 1.0\) MeV, the \(^{4}S_{3/2}\) eigenphase shift from fss2 is \(-46.6^{\circ}\), compared to \(-46.7^{\circ}\) for AV18

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the triton and hypertriton binding energies using the quark-model interaction fss2 (Fukukawa and Fujiwara, 2009; triton1, triton2) to the neutron-deuteron scattering regime. The results contradict the expectation that realistic NN potentials without three-nucleon forces cannot reproduce the doublet scattering length, as found with meson-exchange potentials such as AV18 (Wiringa et al., 1995). The approach enables testing nonlocal quark-model interactions against three-nucleon scattering observables without introducing three-nucleon forces, and provides a basis for future calculations including the Coulomb effect for proton-deuteron scattering.
