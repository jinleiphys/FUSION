# Gauge-invariant theory of two-pion photo- and electro-production off the nucleon

> A field-theoretical description of the photoproduction of two pions off the nucleon is presented that applies to real as well as virtual photons in the one-photon approximation. The Lorentz-covariant theory is complete at the level of all explicit Faddeev-type three-body final-state mechanisms of dressed interacting hadrons, including those of the nonlinear Dyson-Schwinger type. All electromagnetic currents are constructed to satisfy their respective (generalized) Ward-Takahashi identities and thus satisfy local gauge invariance as a matter of course. The Faddeev-type ordering structure results in a natural expansion of the full two-pion photoproduction current $\Mpp^μ$ in terms of multiple loops that preserve gauge invariance order by order in the number of loops, which in turn lends itself naturally to practical applications of increasing sophistication with increasing number of loops.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Lorentz-covariant field-theoretical framework for two-pion photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon that is gauge-invariant by construction, but the actual claim is conditional: the formalism achieves local gauge invariance order by order in a loop expansion only if each input current subamplitude individually satisfies its generalized Ward-Takahashi identity, which in practice may not be calculable exactly.

## Method

The authors apply the gauge-derivative formalism of Haberzettl (1997) to the fully dressed hadronic two-pion production processes, coupling the photon to all possible places in the underlying hadronic Green's functions. The resulting production currents are organized into diagram groups corresponding to no-loop (NL), one-loop (1L), and two-loop (2L) topologies, where each group is shown to satisfy independent gauge-invariance constraints. The hadronic interactions are treated via a Faddeev-type multiple scattering series based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations, amended by nonlinear Dyson-Schwinger-type driving terms that account for intermediate multi-meson production.

## Key numbers

- The formalism applies to real photons (photoproduction) and virtual photons (electroproduction) in the one-photon approximation, with no specific energy range or numerical precision given.
- The paper references experimental facilities at MAMI in Mainz, GRAAL in Grenoble, ELSA in Bonn, and the CLAS detector at JLab as sources of data for double-pion production reactions.
- No numerical results for cross sections, amplitudes, or observables are presented in this paper.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach of Haberzettl (1997) for single-pion photoproduction to the more complex case of two-pion production, where baryonic and mesonic degrees of freedom must be treated on equal footing. The formalism enables a microscopically consistent description of reactions such as γN → ππN, which is needed to extract properties of excited hadronic states from experimental data at facilities like MAMI, ELSA, and JLab. The full details of the approach, including treatment of three- or more-pion vertices omitted here, are promised in a forthcoming publication by the same authors.
