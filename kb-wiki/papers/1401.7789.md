# Improved description of the pion-nucleon scattering phenomenology in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We highlight some of the recent advances in the application of chiral effective field theory (chiral EFT) with baryons to the $πN$ scattering process. We recall some problems that cast doubt on the applicability of chiral EFT to $πN$ and show how the relativistic formalism, once the $Δ(1232)$-resonance is included as an explicit degree of freedom, solves these issues. Finally it is shown how this approach can be used to extract the $σ$-terms from phenomenological information.

## Key claim
The relativistic formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory, once the Δ(1232) resonance is included as an explicit degree of freedom, resolves the long-standing disagreement between chiral EFT and dispersive calculations of subthreshold coefficients, thereby restoring the reliability of the approach for low-energy πN scattering.

## Method
The author uses a Lorentz-invariant (covariant) baryon chiral perturbation theory framework that preserves the full analytic properties of the relativistic quantum field theory. The Δ(1232) resonance is included as a dynamical degree of freedom, introducing a new scale δ = m_Δ − m_N, to account for its strong coupling to the πN system near threshold. The Born-subtracted scattering amplitudes D(ν,t)^± and B(ν,t)^± are expanded around the soft point (ν=0, t=0) in terms of subthreshold coefficients, and the chiral expansion is compared to partial wave analyses and dispersive calculations.

## Key numbers
- σ_πN = 59(7) MeV, extracted from modern πN partial wave analyses using the relativistic formalism with explicit Δ(1232).
- σ_s = 16(80)(60) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic (estimation of O(p^4) corrections in the SU(3)_F breaking calculation).
- y ≡ 2⟨N|s̄s|N⟩ / ⟨N|(ūu + d̄d)|N⟩ = 0.02(13)(10), with the same error structure.
- δ ≡ m_Δ − m_N is the mass difference between the Δ(1232) and the nucleon, which sets the new scale in the theory.

## Context
This work extends the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and addresses the power-counting problem identified by Gegelia (1999). It directly contradicts the earlier conclusion of Becher and Leutwyler (2001) that chiral EFT could not connect the soft point to the physical region at one-loop accuracy, showing that omission of the Δ(1232) was the cause. The improved convergence enables extraction of the πN sigma term and strangeness content, relevant for dark matter detection and for predictions of proton polarizabilities in muonic hydrogen Lamb shift calculations (Nevado and Pineda, 2008; Alarcón et al., 2013).
