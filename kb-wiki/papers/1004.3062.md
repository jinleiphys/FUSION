# Structure functions for light nuclei

> We discuss the nuclear EMC effect with particular emphasis on recent data for light nuclei including 2H, 3He, 4He, 9Be, 12C and 14N. In order to verify the consistency of available data, we calculate the χ^2 deviation between different data sets. We find a good agreement between the results from the NMC, SLAC E139, and HERMES experiments. However, our analysis indicates an overall normalization offset of about 2% in the data from the recent JLab E03-103 experiment with respect to previous data for nuclei heavier than 3He. We also discuss the extraction of the neutron/proton structure function ratio F2n/F2p from the nuclear ratios 3He/2H and 2H/1H. Our analysis shows that the E03-103 data on 3He/2H require a renormalization of about 3% in order to be consistent with the F2n/F2p ratio obtained from the NMC experiment. After such a renormalization, the 3He data from the E03-103 data and HERMES experiments are in a good agreement. Finally, we present a detailed comparison between data and model calculations, which include a description of the nuclear binding, Fermi motion and off-shell corrections to the structure functions of bound proton and neutron, as well as the nuclear pion and shadowing corrections. Overall, a good agreement with the available data for all nuclei is obtained.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the recent JLab E03-103 data for nuclei heavier than $^3$He have an overall normalization offset of about 2% compared to previous data from NMC and SLAC E139, and that the E03-103 $^3$He/$^2$H data require a renormalization of about 3% to be consistent with the neutron/proton structure function ratio from NMC.

## Method
The authors performed a $\chi^2$ analysis to test the statistical consistency of nuclear EMC effect data from NMC, SLAC E139, HERMES, and JLab E03-103 experiments for light nuclei ($^2$H, $^3$He, $^4$He, $^9$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N). They then compared these data to a theoretical model that includes nuclear binding, Fermi motion, off-shell corrections to bound nucleon structure functions, nuclear pion corrections, and shadowing, using spectral functions for $^3$He from Faddeev calculations and a mean-field plus correlation model for $A\ge4$ nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The JLab E03-103 data for $^4$He/$^2$H, $^9$Be/$^2$H, and $^{12}$C/$^2$H require an overall normalization factor of $0.98^{+0.005}_{-0.003}$ to be statistically compatible with SLAC E139 data, lowering the total $\chi^2$ from 42.7/12 to 8.8/12.
- The E03-103 data on $^3$He/$^2$H require a renormalization of about 3% to be consistent with the $F_2^n/F_2^p$ ratio from the NMC experiment.
- The average bound nucleon energy $\langle \ceps \rangle$ for $^4$He is $-40.06$ MeV, compared to $-17.95$ MeV for $^3$He and $-11.46$ MeV for $^2$H.
- The average kinetic energy $\langle \bm p^2\rangle/2M$ for $^4$He is $25.01$ MeV, compared to $12.87$ MeV for $^3$He and $9.24$ MeV for $^2$H.

## Context
This work extends the model of Kulagin and Petti (2004) to analyze recent data from the JLab E03-103 experiment (Seely, 2009) and the HERMES experiment (Ackerstaff, 1999). The analysis identifies a normalization inconsistency in the E03-103 data relative to older NMC and SLAC E139 data, which is critical for the extraction of the neutron structure function from deuterium and $^3$He data. The findings enable a more reliable comparison of nuclear EMC effect data across experiments and inform future global QCD fits that use nuclear data to determine parton distribution functions.
