# Quest of $^{37}Mg$ halo structure using Glauber model and microscopic relativistic mean field densities

> We have studied the ground state properties (binding energy and charge radius) using relativistic mean filed formalism (RMF) for Mg-isotopes in the valley of stability to drip line region. The RMF densities have been analyzed in context of reaction dynamics. The calculated results of $^{24-40}$Mg+$^{12}$C reactions at projectile energy 240 AMeV using Glauber model with the conjunction of densities from relativistic mean field formalism are compared with experimental data. We found remarkable agreement of estimated values of reaction cross sections with experimental data except for $^{37}$Mg isotope. In view of this, the halo status of $^{37}$Mg is examined through higher magnitude of rms radius and small value of longitudinal momentum distribution. Finally, an effort is made to explore the structure of $^{37}$Mg halo candidate using Glauber few body formalism.

# Quest of $^{37}$Mg halo structure using Glauber model and microscopic relativistic mean field densities

## Key claim
The paper claims that $^{37}$Mg exhibits a halo structure, supported by a large root-mean-square radius and a narrow longitudinal momentum distribution, but only after using an unusually large diffuseness parameter (a = 3.5 fm) in a Wood-Saxon potential to fit the reaction cross section to the experimental value of 1536 mb, whereas standard RMF densities fail to reproduce the measured reaction cross section for this isotope.

## Method
The authors used relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set to compute ground state properties and densities for $^{24-40}$Mg isotopes. These densities were then used as input to the Glauber model to calculate reaction cross sections, angular elastic differential cross sections, one-neutron removal cross sections, and longitudinal momentum distributions for $^{24-40}$Mg + $^{12}$C reactions at 240 AMeV. For $^{37}$Mg specifically, a two-body Glauber formalism (core + neutron) was employed with Wood-Saxon single-particle wave functions, varying the diffuseness parameter to match the experimental reaction cross section.

## Key numbers
- Reaction cross section for $^{37}$Mg + $^{12}$C at 240 AMeV: calculated value 1537 mb at diffuseness parameter a = 3.5 fm, compared to experimental value 1536 ± 15 mb.
- Root-mean-square radius of $^{37}$Mg (core + neutron system) at a = 3.5 fm: 6.041 fm.
- Full width at half maximum (FWHM) of longitudinal momentum distribution for $^{36}$Mg core from $^{37}$Mg + $^{12}$C at 240 AMeV: 80.02 MeV/c.
- Glauber model parameters at 240 AMeV: σ_NN = 3.266868 fm², α_NN = 0.6800303, β_NN = 0.097843707 fm².
- FWHM for $^{18}$C from $^{19}$C one-neutron breakup: 44.3 ± 5.9 MeV/c (cited from Bazin 1995).

## Context
This work extends the experimental findings of Takechi (2015), who measured reaction cross sections for Mg isotopes at RIBF/RIKEN and identified $^{37}$Mg as a halo candidate based on its anomalously large cross section. The authors compare their Glauber model calculations using RMF densities to these data, finding agreement for all isotopes except $^{37}$Mg. The study also relates to earlier work on halo nuclei such as $^{11}$Li, $^{11,14}$Be (Tanihata 1992), $^{19}$C (Bazin 1995), $^{22}$C (Tanaka 2010, Kobayashi 2011), and $^{29,31}$Ne (Takechi 2012, Hama 2010). The paper enables further investigation of the $^{37}$Mg halo structure using few-body Glauber formalism and suggests that the unusually large diffuseness parameter needed to fit the data warrants further study of reaction dynamics for halo nuclei.
