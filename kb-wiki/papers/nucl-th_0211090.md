# Chiral dynamics and pionic 1s states of Pb and Sn isotopes

> We calculate shifts and width of 1s and 2p states of pions bound to heavy nuclei, using an optical potential with its s-wave interaction part based on chiral symmetry. This potential is constructed systematically in two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. A proper treatment of the explicit energy dependence of this potential in conjunction with gauge invariance turns out to be very important. Results are reported for deeply bound pionic states in Pb and Sn isotopes in comparison with the GSI data

# Chiral dynamics and pionic 1s states of Pb and Sn isotopes

## Key claim

The explicit energy dependence of the s-wave pion-nucleus polarization operator, treated consistently with gauge invariance via the Coulomb potential, resolves the long-standing "missing repulsion" problem in pionic atom calculations without invoking an artificially large repulsive real part in the ρ² terms of the optical potential. The in-medium reduction of the pion decay constant fπ, reflecting partial chiral symmetry restoration, provides the physical mechanism behind this extra repulsion, but the authors note that whether higher-order corrections permit this interpretation "needs still to be further explored."

## Method

The authors construct the pion self-energy (polarization operator) Π(ω, q; ρp, ρn) using two-loop in-medium chiral perturbation theory for the s-wave part, incorporating double-scattering (Pauli) corrections at that order, and add a phenomenological p-wave piece with standard parameterization. They solve the Klein-Gordon equation with the explicitly energy-dependent polarization operator in the local density approximation, introducing the Coulomb potential in a gauge-invariant way wherever the pion energy ω appears, and compare results to experimental data for pionic 1s and 2p states in ²⁰⁵Pb and a chain of Sn isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Kolomeitsev, Kaiser, and Weise (2002) and Kaiser and Weise (2001) to explain the "missing repulsion" problem in pionic atoms, which had previously been addressed only through phenomenological large repulsive real parts in the ρ² terms of the optical potential (Eriksson and Weise; Friedman). The calculations successfully reproduce the GSI data on deeply bound pionic 1s states in Pb (Gilg et al., 2002) and Sn isotopes (Suzuki et al., 2002), and the authors connect their results to the earlier hypothesis by Weise (2001) and Kaiser and Weise (2001) that the extra repulsion reflects chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter. The work enables systematic studies of neutron density distributions in heavy nuclei through pionic atom spectroscopy, as demonstrated by the sensitivity analysis for Sn isotopes.
