# Nuclear moments for the neutrinoless double beta decay

> A derivation of the neutrinoless double beta decay rate, specially adapted for the nuclear structure calculations, is presented. It is shown that the Fourier-Bessel expansion of the hadronic currents, jointly with the angular momentum recoupling, leads to very simple final expressions for the nuclear form factors. This greatly facilitates the theoretical estimate of the half life. Our approach does not require the closure approximation, which however can be implemented if desired. The method is exemplified for the $ββ$ decay $^{48}Ca \to ^{48}Ti$, both within the QRPA and a shell-model like model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a derivation of the neutrinoless double beta decay rate that avoids the closure approximation, using Fourier-Bessel expansion and angular momentum recoupling to produce simpler nuclear form factor expressions; the closure approximation can still be implemented if desired, but the formalism does not require it.

## Method

The authors expand the hadronic currents using Fourier-Bessel series and perform angular momentum recoupling to rewrite the nuclear matrix elements in terms of one-body spherical tensor operators. They then evaluate the electron s-wave and p-wave contributions to the transition amplitude, deriving expressions for the Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and other nuclear moments. The method is exemplified for the decay ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti using both the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and a shell-model-like approach.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff for the dipole form factor in finite nucleon size correlations is Λ = 850 MeV.
- The Compton wavelength parameter for short-range correlations is k_c = 3.93 fm⁻¹.
- The effective weak-magnetism coupling constant is f_W = 4.7.
- The nuclear radius R appears as a scaling factor in the matrix element definitions (e.g., M_R includes a factor R²/M_N).

## Context

This work extends earlier derivations by Krmpotic et al. (1992, 1994) for the s-wave matrix elements M_F, M_GT, and M_R, and adds the p-wave matrix elements relevant when right-hand lepton current admixture is considered. It differs from the neutrino potential formalisms of Haximac et al. (1984), Doi et al. (1985), and Tomoda (1986) by using one-body rather than two-body operators, and from Vergados et al. (1990) by not being limited to harmonic oscillator wave functions. The formalism enables simpler numerical calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, particularly within QRPA where summation over intermediate states is unavoidable.
