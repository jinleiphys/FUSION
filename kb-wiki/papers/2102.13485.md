# Bayesian analysis of multimessenger M-R data with interpolated hybrid EoS

> We introduce a family of equations of state (EoS) for hybrid neutron star (NS) matter that is obtained by a two-zone parabolic interpolation between a soft hadronic EoS at low densities and a set of stiff quark matter EoS at high densities within a finite region of chemical potentials $μ_H < μ< μ_Q$. Fixing the hadronic EoS as the APR one and choosing the color-superconducting, nonlocal NJL model with two free parameters for the quark phase, we perform Bayesian analyses with this two-parameter family of hybrid EoS. Using three different sets of observational constraints that include the mass of PSR J0740+6620, the tidal deformability for GW170817, and the mass-radius relation for PSR J0030+0451 from NICER as obligatory (set 1), while set 2 uses the possible upper limit on the maximum mass from GW170817 as an additional constraint and set 3 instead of the possibility that the lighter object in the asymmetric binary merger GW190814 is a neutron star. We confirm that in any case, the quark matter phase has to be color superconducting with the dimensionless diquark coupling approximately fulfilling the Fierz relation $η_D=0.75$ and the most probable solutions exhibiting a proportionality between $η_D$ and $η_V$, the coupling of the repulsive vector interaction that is required for a sufficiently large maximum mass. We used the Bayesian analysis to investigate with the method of fictitious measurements the consequences of anticipating different radii for the massive $2~M_\odot$ PSR J0740+6220 for the most likely equation of state. With the actual outcome of the NICER radius measurement on PSR J0740+6220 we could conclude that for the most likely hybrid star EoS would not support a maximum mass as large as $2.5~M_\odot$ so that the event GW190814 was a binary black hole merger.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that for hybrid neutron star equations of state constructed via two-zone parabolic interpolation between a soft hadronic APR model and color-superconducting nonlocal NJL quark matter, the quark phase must have a diquark coupling approximately fulfilling the Fierz relation (η_D = 0.75), and that with the actual NICER radius measurement for PSR J0740+6220, the most likely hybrid star EoS would not support a maximum mass as large as 2.5 M_⊙, implying that GW190814 was a binary black hole merger.

## Method
The authors constructed a family of hybrid equations of state by performing a two-zone parabolic interpolation between the APR hadronic EoS at low densities and a set of color-superconducting nonlocal NJL model quark matter EoS at high densities, with the hadronic and quark phases joined in a finite chemical potential region. They then performed Bayesian analyses with this two-parameter family (η_D and η_V) using three different sets of observational constraints: set 1 included the mass of PSR J0740+6620, the tidal deformability for GW170817, and the NICER mass-radius relation for PSR J0030+0451; set 2 added an upper limit on maximum mass from GW170817; set 3 replaced that with the possibility that the lighter object in GW190814 is a neutron star. The Bayesian analysis also used fictitious measurements to investigate consequences of anticipating different radii for the massive 2 M_⊙ PSR J0740+6220.

## Key numbers
- The mass of PSR J0740+6620 was updated to 2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙ (68.3% credibility interval) from Fonseca (2021).
- The tidal deformability for a 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star from GW170817 was constrained to 70 < Λ_1.4 < 580 from Abbott (2018).
- The radius of a 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star was constrained to R_1.4 = 11.0^{+0.9}_{-0.6} km from Capano (2019).
- The dimensionless diquark coupling η_D = 0.75 approximately fulfills the Fierz relation.
- The squared sound speed from the CSS fit of the nlNJL model ranges between c_s^2 = 0.45 and 0.54.
- The lighter component in GW190814 has mass 2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09} M_⊙ from Abbott (2020).
- The onset density for interpolation was set to n_H = n_0, and the matching density n_Q varied from 4.5 to 2.5 n_0 as η_V was incremented.

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach of Blaschke (2020) which used a strong first-order phase transition with pasta phases, by instead employing a continuous two-zone interpolation that avoids the pathologies of Maxwell constructions with soft hadronic EoS like APR. The method is inspired by the discussion in Baym (2017) on interpolating between hadronic and quark matter EoS, and it enables conclusions on the most favorable parameter set for the nonlocal NJL model of color-superconducting quark matter. The results contradict the possibility that GW190814 involved a neutron star, instead supporting a binary black hole interpretation, and they provide a framework for incorporating future NICER radius measurements into Bayesian EoS inference.
