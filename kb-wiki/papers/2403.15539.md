# From pole parameters to line shapes and branching ratios

> Resonances are uniquely characterized by their complex pole locations and the corresponding residues. In practice, however, resonances are typically identified experimentally as structures in invariant mass distributions, with branching fractions of resonances determined as ratios of count rates. To make contact between these quantities it is necessary to connect line shapes and resonance parameters. In this work we propose such a connection and illustrate the formalism with detailed studies of the $ρ(770)$ and $f_0(500)$ resonances. Based on the line shapes inferred from the resonance parameters along these lines, expressions for partial widths and branching ratios are derived and compared to other approaches in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a formalism to connect resonance pole parameters (complex pole location and residues) to experimentally observable line shapes and branching ratios, but the connection requires a two-potential framework with a background amplitude that must be parameterized explicitly to allow analytic continuation; the formalism reproduces pole parameters exactly by construction but the residue is predicted in terms of those parameters only in the simplest case without background.

## Method

The authors employ the two-potential formalism to decompose the scattering amplitude into a background part and a resonance part, constructing the vertex function and self energy via dispersion relations (Omnès function) that incorporate unitarity and analyticity constraints. For the ρ(770) and f0(500) resonances in ππ scattering, they fit the background amplitude parameters (using a conformal expansion for left-hand cuts) to reproduce the known pole residues, while enforcing the pole location through the Dyson equation for the dressed propagator. The formalism is extended to higher partial waves by including centrifugal barrier factors with regulator functions that ensure the spectral function remains normalized.

## Key numbers

- ρ(770) pole mass: M_ρ = 762.5(1.7) MeV, pole width: Γ_ρ = 2 × 73.2(1.1) MeV
- ρ(770) residue coupling: |g̃_{ρππ}| = 6.01(8) with phase -5.3(1.0) degrees
- ρ(770) coupling to photon: g̃_{ργ} = 5.01(7) with phase 1(1) degrees
- f0(500) residue coupling: |g̃_{σππ}| = 3.61(11) GeV with phase -74.0(2.2) degrees (scenario iii)
- f0(500) bare mass m = 0.82(3) GeV and bare coupling g = 4.7(3) (scenario iii)
- f0(500) coupling to K̅K: |g̃_{σK̅K}| = 2.0(1) GeV with phase -23(1)(1) degrees (scenario iv)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analyses of ππ scattering from Ananthanarayan (2000), Colangelo (2001), and Garcia-Martin (2011) that determined ρ(770) and f0(500) pole parameters via Roy equations, and the f0(500) analysis by Caprini (2005). It provides a framework to replace common prescriptions (such as the narrow-width formula for f0(500)→γγ from Morgan (1987, 1990)) with better-justified recipes for branching ratios derived from normalized spectral functions. The formalism enables future applications to more complicated multi-channel systems like the f0(980) and a0(980) where different Riemann sheets play a role.
