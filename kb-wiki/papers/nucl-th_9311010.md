# Exact, finite, and Hermitian fermion-pair-to-boson mapping

> We discuss a general, exact (in that matrix elements are preserved) mapping of fermion pairs to bosons, and find a simple factorization of the boson representation of fermion operators. This leads to boson Hamiltonians that are Hermitian and finite, with no more than two-body operators if the fermion Hamiltonian has at most two-body operators, and one-body boson transition operators if the fermion transition operator is one-body.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the exact Marumori-type mapping of fermion pairs to bosons, which normally produces boson Hamiltonians with infinite many-body expansions, can be factorized into a finite, Hermitian boson Hamiltonian times a norm operator; the infinite expansion resides entirely in the norm operator, not in the Hamiltonian itself.

## Method

The authors construct boson operators that preserve fermion matrix elements, following the Marumori philosophy, and introduce an explicit boson norm operator to account for the non-orthonormality of fermion-pair states. They demonstrate that the boson representation of any one- or two-body fermion operator factorizes as the product of the norm operator and a finite boson image, with the one-body fermion operator mapping to a one-body boson operator and the two-body fermion operator mapping to at most a two-body boson operator. The factorization is proven both by explicit normal-ordering of the full-space operators and by a direct algebraic argument using the action of fermion operators on pair-condensate states.

## Key numbers

* The norm operator has eigenvalue \((2N-1)!!\) on physical states constructed from \(N\) fermion pairs.
* For the pairing interaction example, the boson image in the full space is \(\hat{P}_B = \frac{1}{4\Omega} [2\hat{N} + \hat{C}_2 - 2\hat{G}_2]\), where \(\Omega\) is half the number of single-fermion states.
* In the truncated space of only \(b_0^\dagger\) bosons, the correctly factorized boson image is \(\tilde{P}_T = b^\dagger_0 b_0 - \frac{1}{\Omega} b^\dagger_0 b^\dagger_0 b_0 b_0\), which yields the correct ground state energy.

## Context

This work extends the Marumori mapping (Marumori 1964) and the Belyaev-Zelevinskii mapping (Belyaev and Zelevinskii 1962), which both produce infinite boson expansions, and the Dyson mapping (Dyson 1956) which gives finite but non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. The factorization result provides a rigorous microscopic foundation for the phenomenological Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello (1975) and for Otsuka et al.'s (1978) earlier attempts at a microscopic basis. The paper enables future work on truncating the boson space to a few collective degrees of freedom while preserving Hermiticity and finiteness, with the open question of convergence of many-body terms induced by such truncation.
