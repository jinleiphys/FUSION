# A supercircle description of universal three-body states in two dimensions

> We consider bound states of asymmetric three-body systems confined to two dimensions. In the universal regime, two energy ratios and two mass ratios provide complete knowledge of the three-body energy measured in units of one two-body energy. The lowest number of stable bound states is produced when one mass is larger than two similar masses. We focus on selected asymmetric systems of interest in cold atom physics. The scaled three-body energy and the two scaled two-body energies are related through an equation for a supercircle whose radius increases almost linearly with three-body energy. The exponents exhibit an increasing behavior with three-body energy. The mass dependence is highly non-trivial. We give a simple relation that predicts the universal three-body energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the universal three-body energy for asymmetric systems in two dimensions can be parametrized by a "supercircle" equation relating the scaled three-body energy to the two scaled two-body energies, with a radius that increases almost linearly with three-body energy and exponents that increase with three-body energy. The hedge is that this parametrization is based on numerical findings and the mass dependence is "highly non-trivial," meaning the simple linear relation is approximate and not an exact analytical result.

## Method

The authors numerically solved momentum-space integral equations for three particles with zero-range (contact) interactions in two dimensions, using two-body bound state energies instead of interaction strengths to reduce the parameter space. They surveyed the three-body energy as a function of four dimensionless parameters: two mass ratios and two two-body energy ratios, then fitted the numerical results to a supercircle (Lamé curve) parametrization. The fits were performed for specific mass sets corresponding to alkali atoms (e.g., ⁶Li, ⁴⁰K, ⁸⁷Rb) and the results were checked against known symmetric limits.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons in the universal limit, the ground state scaled three-body energy is approximately 16.52 (derived from the parametrization with t₀ ≈ 0.30).
- For three identical bosons, the first excited state scaled three-body energy is approximately 1.267 (derived from the parametrization with t₁ ≈ 2.93).
- The ground state radius function is approximately R₀(ε₃) ≈ 0.74 ε₃ − 2.5, essentially independent of masses.
- The first excited state radius function is approximately R₁(ε₃) ≈ ε₃, essentially independent of masses.
- The ground state exponent t₀ ranges from about 0.2 at ε₃ = 1 up to mass-dependent constants α₀ ≈ 0.3–0.5 at large energy, with parameters (p₀, α₀) ≈ (0.04–0.06, 0.3–0.5) and (β₀, γ₀) ≈ −(0.93–0.95), −(0.82–0.87).
- The first excited state exponent t₁ starts at about 1 at ε₃ = 1 and increases monotonically with ε₃.

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of three identical bosons in two dimensions (Tjon 1975, Adhikari 1988, Nielsen 1997, 2001) to fully asymmetric systems with different masses and different two-body energies. It provides the first systematic survey of the three-body bound state spectrum in two dimensions for general mass ratios, complementing the known Efimov effect in three dimensions (Efimov 1970) and the universal contact parameter approach (Tan 2008). The results enable experimental searches for universal three-body states in two-dimensional cold atom setups using Feshbach resonances (Chin 2010), and can serve as estimates of three-body energies and number of bound states for systems like ⁶Li–⁴⁰K–⁸⁷Rb and ⁴⁰K–⁸⁷Rb–¹³³Cs.
