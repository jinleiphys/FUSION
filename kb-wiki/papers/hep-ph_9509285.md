# Screening of initial parton production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Screening of initial parton production due to the presence of on-shell partons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is discussed. It is shown that the divergent cross sections in the calculation of parton production can be regulated self-consistently without an {\it ad hoc} cut-off, and that the resultant parton production and transverse energy production rate are finite. Consequences on the energy density estimates are discussed.

## Key claim

The divergent cross sections in perturbative QCD calculations of initial parton production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions can be regulated self-consistently by the screening effect of previously produced hard partons, without introducing an ad hoc infrared cut-off, provided that the saturation of the minijet cross section occurs in the perturbative region where \(p_T \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).

## Method

The authors compute the static electric screening mass \(\mu_D\) generated by the produced minijets, using the number distribution of minijets from lowest-order pQCD with a \(K=2\) factor, and assuming a formation time \(\tau_f = a/p_T\) for produced partons. They then iteratively compute the screened minijet cross section by replacing \(-\hat t \rightarrow -\hat t + \mu_D^2\) and \(-\hat u \rightarrow -\hat u + \mu_D^2\) in the divergent \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel sub-processes, starting from high \(p_T\) where screening is negligible and stepping downward until saturation is reached. The calculation uses MRSA parton distributions with scale \(Q = p_T\) and assumes a central \(AA\) collision with \(A=200\) at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\) GeV) and LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\,A\) TeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\,A\) TeV) with \(A=200\), the screened minijet cross section saturates in the perturbative region \(p_T \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), with the screening mass \(\mu_D\) reaching values comparable to \(p_T\) near saturation.
- At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\,A\) GeV) with \(A=200\), the screening mass \(\mu_D\) also regulates the cross section, though the saturation occurs at lower \(p_T\) values.
- For the formation time parameter \(a=1\) and \(a=2\), the resulting screening masses differ by more than a trivial factor \(\sqrt{2}\) due to the iterative procedure.
- At LHC energies, the average transverse energy from minijets with \(p_T \ge p_0\) practically saturates for \(p_0 \lesssim 1\) GeV when screening is included.
- The transverse energy density \(\varepsilon\) of the early minijet system is significantly reduced by screening compared to the unscreened Bjorken estimate, with the maximum energy density reached at earlier formation times.

## Context

This work extends the screening concept of Biro, Muller, and Wang (1992) by applying it self-consistently to the initial parton production itself, rather than only to soft interactions. It provides a dynamical alternative to the ad hoc infrared cut-off \(p_0\) used in earlier minijet models such as those by Kajantie, Landshoff, and Lindfors (1987) and the HIJING model of Wang and Gyulassy (1991). The results enable more reliable perturbative estimates of the energy density of the early parton plasma in heavy-ion collisions, and motivate further study of nuclear shadowing effects from initial-state interactions as discussed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Eskola (1993).
