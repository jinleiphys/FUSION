# Deep learning for exploring hadron-hadron interactions

> In this proceeding, we introduce deep learning technologies for studying hadron-hadron interactions. To extract parameterized hadron interaction potentials from collision experiments, we employ a supervised learning approach using Femtoscopy data. The deep neural networks (DNNs) are trained to learn the inverse mapping from observations to potentials. To link between experiments and first-principles simulations, we further investigate hadronic interactions in Lattice QCD simulations from the HAL QCD method perspective. Using an unsupervised learning approach, we construct a model-free potential function with symmetric DNNs, aiming to learn hadron interactions directly from simulated correlation functions (equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes). On both fronts, deep learning methods show great promise in advancing our understanding of hadron interactions.

## Key claim
Deep neural networks can learn the inverse mapping from femtoscopic correlation functions to hadron interaction potentials (supervised) and directly reconstruct non-local potentials from lattice QCD correlation functions (unsupervised), but the supervised approach fails when attempting to predict all five potential parameters simultaneously, and the unsupervised results still lack comparison to experimental scattering phase shifts.

## Method
For the supervised approach, the authors used CATS to generate correlation functions from a parameterized multi-Gaussian potential (Eq. 1) with a Gaussian source, then trained a DNN with 60 input nodes, three hidden layers of 64 nodes each, and ELU activations to predict potential parameters. For the unsupervised approach, they constructed a symmetric deep neural network (SDNN) with parameter-sharing architecture to represent the non-local potential \(U_\theta(r,r')\), minimizing the residual of the Schrödinger equation (for wave functions) or the time-dependent HAL QCD equation (for \(R\) correlators), with an asymptotic regularization loss enforcing zero potential at large distances.

## Key numbers
- Potential parameter ranges: \(b_1 \in [-2145.5, 1532.5]\ \text{MeV}\), \(b_2 \in [44.34, 103.46]\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(b_3 \in [-345.8, -186.2]\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(b_4 \in [0.702, 0.858]\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), with pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 135\ \text{MeV}\)
- Source radius range: \(r_0 \in [0.52, 4.16]\ \text{fm}\) for Gaussian source
- Training data: 6,400 samples per single-parameter prediction (60 \(k\)-measurements from 0 to \(300\ \text{MeV}/c\)); 10,000 samples for simultaneous \(b_1\) and \(b_3\) prediction
- \(R^2\) metric on test sets: 0.998–0.999 for single-parameter predictions (Model-1) and 0.998 for simultaneous \(b_1,b_3\) prediction (Model-2)
- Separable potential toy model: \(\mu=1.0\), \(\omega=-0.017 \mu^4\), \(m=3.30 \mu\), \(R=2.5/\mu\); wave functions prepared with \(k=[0.01,1.0]\) (\(N_k=10\)) and \(r=[0.01,5R]\) (\(N_r=100\)); training for 1000 epochs
- \(\Omega_{ccc}-\Omega_{ccc}\) system: lattice spacing \(a\approx 0.0846\ \text{fm}\) (\(a^{-1}\approx 2.333\ \text{GeV}\)), \(m_\pi\approx 146\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_K\approx 525\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_{\Omega_{ccc}}\approx 4796\ \text{MeV}\); \(R(t=26,r)\) data used, with \(t=25,27\) for derivatives; regularization cutoff \(\tilde{R}=3\ \text{fm}\); training for 5000 epochs

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2008) by replacing the leading-order local potential approximation with a model-free non-local potential represented by symmetric neural networks, and complements traditional femtoscopic analysis tools like CATS (Mihaylov 2018) and CRAB by providing an inverse-mapping capability. The approach enables direct extraction of hadron interaction potentials from both experimental correlation data and lattice QCD simulations without assuming a specific functional form, but future work must compute scattering phase shifts and compare with experimental observations to validate the method.
