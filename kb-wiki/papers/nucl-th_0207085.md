# High-density K nuclear systems with isovector deformation

> Using a phenomenological KN potential which reproduces $Λ$(1405) as an I=0 bound state of KN, we investigated deeply bound kaonic nuclei, ppnK$^-$ and $^8$BeK$^-$, with the method of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics. Our calculations show that strongly bound kaonic nuclear systems with unusual exotic structures are formed around the K$^-$, which attracts the surrounding nucleons to an extremely high-density assembly and induces a proton-neutron separation, ``isovector deformation''.

## Key claim

Using Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) with a phenomenological KN potential that reproduces Lambda(1405) as an I=0 bound state, the authors show that deeply bound kaonic nuclei ppnK- and 8BeK- form extremely high-density assemblies with a proton-neutron separation they term "isovector deformation," but the calculations rely on a bare NN interaction (Tamagaki potential) and a G-matrix effective interaction rather than a fully self-consistent many-body treatment.

## Method

The authors applied the AMD method, representing each nucleon as a superposition of two Gaussian wave packets and the kaon as a superposition of five Gaussian wave packets, with full antisymmetrization of nucleons and parity projection. They used the Tamagaki potential (OPEG) as a bare NN interaction and the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) KN interaction as a bare KN interaction, constructing effective central forces via the G-matrix method. The systems ppnK- and 8BeK- were treated as full (A+1)-body systems without prior assumptions about shell or cluster structure, with variational parameters determined by frictional cooling.

## Key numbers

- ppnK- binding energy: 113 MeV total (B_K = 105 MeV below 3He+K- threshold), with decay width Gamma_K = 24 MeV
- ppnK- central nucleon density: 1.39 fm^-3 (8.2 times normal nuclear density)
- ppnK- root-mean-square radius: 0.72 fm for the nucleon system
- ppnK- proton RMS radius: 0.70 fm; neutron RMS radius: 0.75 fm
- ppnK- proton kinetic energy per nucleon: 69.4 MeV; neutron kinetic energy per nucleon: 59.0 MeV
- ppnK- proton expectation value of KN interaction: -170.2 MeV per nucleon; neutron: -42.4 MeV per nucleon
- 8BeK- binding energy: 159 MeV (B_K = 104 MeV with respect to 8Be), with decay width Gamma_K = 43 MeV
- 8BeK- central nucleon density: 0.76 fm^-3 (4.5 times normal nuclear density)
- 8BeK- root-mean-square radius: 1.42 fm (compared to 2.46 fm for normal 8Be)
- 8BeK- width parameter nu: 0.52 fm^-2 (compared to 0.205 fm^-2 for normal 8Be)
- 8BeK- deformation parameter beta: 0.55 (compared to 0.63 for normal 8Be)

## Context

This work extends the earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) for ppnK- and 8BeK- using a phenomenological KN potential that reproduces Lambda(1405) as an I=0 KN bound state, and is consistent with chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangian results by Weise et al. (1996). The AMD approach reveals an "isovector deformation" (proton-neutron separation) that was not seen in the earlier alpha-cluster model calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki, because AMD imposes no a priori structural assumptions. The results enable experimental searches for deeply bound kaonic nuclei via (K-, pi-) reactions on proton-rich systems as proposed by Yamazaki and Akaishi, and could provide a cold dense environment for studying chiral symmetry restoration and possible kaon condensation, in contrast to hot dense states from heavy-ion collisions.
