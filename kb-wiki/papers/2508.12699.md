# Nonlocality parameters of microscopic optical potentials from Skyrme-based nuclear structure models at low energies

> We investigate the non-locality parameters $ β$ in nucleon-nucleus interactions for $ ^{16}\mathrm{O} $, $ ^{40}\mathrm{Ca} $, $ ^{48}\mathrm{Ca} $, and $ ^{208}\mathrm{Pb} $ using a microscopic optical potential (MOP) derived from nuclear structure models based on Skyrme self-consistent mean-field calculations at low incident energies. No ad hoc adjusted parameters were employed in the present calculations. The analysis reveals that the non-locality parameter exhibits a clear dependence on the radial position within the nucleus, the incident nucleon energy, and the choice of target nucleus. At incident energies below $30$ MeV, the $ β$ lies between $0.8$ fm and $1.2$ fm for all considered targets. For the light nucleus $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, the non-locality parameter in the interior is smaller than that on the surface, whereas for medium and heavy nuclei $( ^{40}\mathrm{Ca} $, $ ^{48}\mathrm{Ca} $, and $ ^{208}\mathrm{Pb} $ ), the values are comparable in both regions.

## Key claim

The non-locality parameter β in nucleon-nucleus interactions, extracted from a microscopic optical potential (MOP) based on Skyrme self-consistent mean-field calculations without any ad hoc adjusted parameters, shows a clear dependence on radial position within the nucleus, incident nucleon energy, and target nucleus, with values between 0.8 fm and 1.2 fm for incident energies below 30 MeV across all targets considered.

## Method

The authors derived a microscopic optical potential from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field calculations using the SLy5 Skyrme interaction, with particle-vibration coupling (PVC) corrections computed via fully self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) including all natural-parity excited states with multipolarities L = 0+, 1-, 2+, 3-, 4+, 5- and excitation energies below 50 MeV. The imaginary part of the non-local optical potential W(R, s) was calculated as a function of the average radial coordinate R and the non-locality coordinate s = r - r', and the non-locality parameter β was extracted by fitting the Gaussian form W(R, s) = W(R, s=0) × exp(-s²/β²) for neutron elastic scattering on 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb at incident energies below 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca, the volume non-locality parameter βv ≈ 1.0 fm, in agreement with Mau (1976), and remains nearly constant over the energy range considered.
- For 40Ca, the surface non-locality parameter βs ranges from 0.96 fm to 1.01 fm, significantly lower than the values reported in Mau (1976).
- For 208Pb, the surface non-locality parameter βs decreases from 1.18 fm to 1.08 fm with increasing incident energy, in agreement with Bernard (1979).
- For 208Pb, a significant volume component centered at R = 5.7 fm is characterized by βv = 0.98 fm.
- For 16O, the difference between βs and βv is approximately 0.2 fm, larger than for medium and heavy nuclei.
- At incident energies below 30 MeV, β lies between 0.8 fm and 1.2 fm for all considered targets.

## Context

This work extends the seminal approach of Bernard (1979), who extracted β only at the surface peak radius for 208Pb and could not extract β in the interior due to absence of absorptive strength, by using modern computational capabilities to include a complete set of RPA excited states and obtain significant absorption in the nuclear interior for all double closed shell nuclei. The results provide systematic guidance for non-local potential parametrizations beyond the constant β = 0.85 fm of the Perey-Buck potential (Perey and Buck, 1962), and enable improved modeling of nucleon-nucleus interactions for reactions such as (d,p) and (p,d) in inverse kinematics, particularly for exotic nuclei where experimental data are scarce.
