# Developments in lattice QCD for matter at high temperature and density

> A brief overview of the QCD phase diagram at nonzero temperature and density is provided. It is explained why standard lattice QCD techniques are not immediately applicable for its determination, due to the sign problem. We then discuss a selection of recent lattice approaches that attempt to evade the sign problem and classify them according to the underlying principle: constrained simulations (density of states, histograms), holomorphicity (complex Langevin, Lefschetz thimbles), partial summations (clusters, subsets, bags) and change in integration order (strong coupling, dual formulations).

## Key claim

Standard lattice QCD techniques cannot determine the QCD phase diagram at nonzero baryon density because the quark contribution to the Boltzmann weight becomes complex, ruling out importance sampling; this "sign problem" is severe when the expectation value of the phase factor in the phase-quenched theory decays exponentially with volume, and no definitive solution has yet been found.

## Method

The paper reviews four categories of lattice approaches that attempt to evade the sign problem: constrained simulations (density of states, histogram methods), holomorphicity-based methods (complex Langevin dynamics, Lefschetz thimbles), partial summations (clusters, subsets, fermion bags), and changes in integration order (strong-coupling expansions, dual formulations). Each approach is described in terms of its underlying principle, tested on simpler models, and assessed for applicability to QCD with light quarks.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is closer to 155 MeV than 170 MeV, as indicated by recent lattice studies (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011).
- Taylor expansion coefficients for the logarithm of the fermion determinant have been determined up to order (mu/T)^8 (Datta 2012).
- In the phase-quenched theory, the expectation value of the phase factor decays as exp(-V Delta f / T), where V is the three-volume and Delta f is the difference in free energy densities between the original and phase-quenched theories.
- For two quark flavours with equal mass, ignoring the complex phase (phase quenching) produces an incorrect onset at a baryon chemical potential of 3/2 times the pion mass, rather than around the nucleon mass.
- Complex Langevin dynamics with gauge cooling has been applied to QCD with two and four flavours of light quarks, describing the transition from zero density to saturation (the maximal density on the lattice) (Sexty 2013).
- In a one-dimensional QCD test, the subset construction eliminates the sign problem completely for five flavours or fewer (Bloch 2013).

## Context

This review extends earlier overviews of the sign problem in QCD at finite density (deForcrand 2010, Aarts 2013) and complements them by classifying approaches by underlying principle. The paper situates lattice QCD results against predictions from effective models such as Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005) and Polyakov-quark-meson models (Herbst 2010), as well as functional renormalization group (Braun 2009) and Schwinger-Dyson equations (Fischer 2012). The work enables future cross-validation between competing methods, with first results for heavy quarks appearing via histogram methods (Ejiri 2013) and strong-coupling expansions (Fromm 2012), and for light quarks via complex Langevin dynamics (Sexty 2013).
