# $ϕ$ meson self-energy in nuclear matter from $ϕN$ resonant interactions

> The $ϕ$-meson properties in cold nuclear matter are investigated by implementing resonant $ϕN$ interactions as described in effective approaches including the unitarization of scattering amplitudes. Several $N^*$-like states are dynamically generated in these models around $2$ GeV, in the vicinity of the $ϕN$ threshold. We find that both these states and the non-resonant part of the amplitude contribute sizably to the $ϕ$ collisional self-energy at finite nuclear density. These contributions are of a similar strength as the widely studied medium effects from the $\bar K K$ cloud. Depending on model details (position of the resonances and strength of the coupling to $ϕN$) we report a $ϕ$ broadening up to about $40$-$50$ MeV, to be added to the $ϕ\to\bar K K$ in-medium decay width, and an attractive optical potential at threshold up to about $35$ MeV at normal matter density. The $ϕ$ spectral function develops a double peak structure as a consequence of the mixing of resonance-hole modes with the $ϕ$ quasi-particle peak. The former results point in the direction of making up for missing absorption as reported in $ϕ$ nuclear production experiments.

# ϕ meson self-energy in nuclear matter from ϕN resonant interactions

## Key claim

The paper claims that resonant ϕN interactions, mediated by dynamically generated N*-like states around 2 GeV, contribute sizably to the ϕ meson collisional self-energy in nuclear matter, with a strength comparable to the widely studied medium effects from the \(\bar K K\) cloud. However, the magnitude of this contribution depends on model details such as the position of the resonances and the strength of their coupling to the ϕN channel, leading to a ϕ broadening up to about 40-50 MeV and an attractive optical potential at threshold up to about 35 MeV at normal matter density.

## Method

The authors compute the ϕ meson self-energy in cold symmetric nuclear matter by combining two contributions: (1) the in-medium \(\bar K K\) cloud, where kaon and antikaon propagators are dressed using a chiral unitary approach to the kaon-nucleon interaction in coupled channels including both s- and p-waves, and (2) direct resonant ϕN interactions, evaluated using two different unitarized coupled-channel models for vector-meson baryon scattering: the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) approach and an SU(6) spin-flavor extension of chiral perturbation theory. The ϕN scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, and the ϕ self-energy from ϕN interactions is computed by summing the scattering amplitude over the nucleon Fermi distribution, including Pauli blocking effects in the meson-baryon loop function.

## Key numbers

- ϕ broadening from resonant ϕN interactions: up to about 40-50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, to be added to the ϕ → \(\bar K K\) in-medium decay width
- Attractive optical potential at threshold: up to about 35 MeV at normal matter density
- ϕ width from the \(\bar K K\) cloud alone: about 28 MeV at normal nuclear matter density (from previous work by the authors)
- ϕ → \(\bar K K\) coupling constant: \(g_\phi = 4.57\), set to reproduce the experimental vacuum decay width \(\Gamma^{\text{exp}}_{\phi\to \bar K K} = 3.54\) MeV
- Kaon self-energy in the Tρ approximation: \(\Pi_K(\rho) = 0.13 \, m_K^2 \, \rho / \rho_0\)
- Attractive antikaon optical potential: -60 MeV at normal nuclear matter density for antikaons at rest
- Repulsive kaon mass shift: about 30 MeV at \(\rho = \rho_0\)
- Imaginary part of the ϕ optical potential from the SU(6) model: starts at -20 MeV at rest, reaches an extremum of -35 MeV around \(p_\phi \sim 200\) MeV/c
- Imaginary part of the ϕ optical potential from the HLS model: reaches an extremum of about -15 MeV at \(p_\phi \simeq 700\) MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Cabrera, 2002) of the ϕ meson self-energy from the \(\bar K K\) cloud, which yielded a ϕ width of about 28 MeV at normal density but proved insufficient to describe the ϕ transparency ratio measured in proton-induced and photoproduction experiments by the LEPS Collaboration (Ishikawa, 2004), CLAS Collaboration (Wood, 2010), and ANKE at COSY (Hartmann, 2012). The new resonant ϕN interaction mechanism is motivated by recent coupled-channel approaches to vector-meson baryon scattering (Oset, 2009; Gamermann, 2011) that dynamically generate N*-like states around 2 GeV. The results point toward making up for the missing absorption reported in ϕ nuclear production experiments, where inelastic widths as large as 23-100 MeV were extracted from CLAS data (Wood, 2010). The momentum-dependent optical potential and spectral function presented here are intended for use in transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions at HADES, GSI-FAIR, and NICA-JINR, and the extension to finite temperature is noted as future work.
