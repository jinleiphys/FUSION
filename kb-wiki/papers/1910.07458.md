# Deeply virtual Compton Scattering off $^4$He

> Deeply virtual Compton scattering is a fascinating process which can provide a tomographic view of nuclei and bound nucleons. The first experimental results for $^4$He targets, recently released at Jefferson Lab, have been analyzed here in a rigorous Impulse Approximation scenario. For both the coherent and incoherent channels of the process, the main experimental observables have been written in terms of state-of-the-art models of the nuclear spectral function and of the parton structure of the bound proton. A good overall agreement with the data is obtained. The calculation shows that a comparison of our conventional results with future precise data can expose novel quark and gluon effects in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first experimental data for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off $^4$He at Jefferson Lab can be described by a conventional Impulse Approximation calculation using realistic nuclear wave functions and nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), without requiring exotic nuclear medium modifications. The authors hedge that this agreement holds only at the current experimental precision, and that future more precise data may reveal novel quark and gluon effects in nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed a plane-wave Impulse Approximation analysis of both coherent ($^4$He$(e,e'\gamma)^4$He) and incoherent ($^4$He$(e,e'\gamma p)X$) DVCS channels. For the coherent channel, the nuclear GPD was constructed as a convolution of nucleon GPDs (using the GK model) with an off-diagonal spectral function of $^4$He, based on the Av18 nucleon-nucleon interaction including three-body forces. For the incoherent channel, the beam spin asymmetry was computed by integrating the squared amplitude over the diagonal spectral function of $^4$He, accounting for the kinematical off-shellness of the bound proton.

## Key numbers

- Beam spin asymmetry $A_{LU}(\phi=90^\circ)$ for coherent DVCS off $^4$He: results shown as red stars compared with data (black squares) from Hattawy et al. (2019) across experimental $Q^2$, $x_B$, and $t=\Delta^2$ bins; good agreement is found.
- Beam spin asymmetry $A_{LU}^{Incoh}$ for the proton in $^4$He at $\phi=90^\circ$: results (red dots) compared with data (black squares) from Hattawy et al. (2019); agreement is good except in the lowest $Q^2$ region corresponding to the first $x_B$ bin.
- The ratio $A_{LU}^{Incoh} / A_{LU}^p$ is shown as a function of $x_B$; the large nuclear effect persists even when using pointlike protons, indicating it arises from nuclear dynamics rather than medium modification of parton structure.

## Context

This work extends previous realistic calculations of nuclear DVCS for few-body systems, such as Cano and Pire (2004) for $^2$H and Scopetta (2009) for $^3$He, to the $^4$He target. It provides the first rigorous Impulse Approximation analysis of the recently released Jefferson Lab data from Hattawy et al. (2019) for both coherent and incoherent channels. The calculation establishes a conventional baseline that future high-luminosity measurements (Armstrong et al., 2012) can use to search for novel quark and gluon effects in nuclei, while also motivating improved treatments of the $^4$He spectral function including three-body scattering states.
