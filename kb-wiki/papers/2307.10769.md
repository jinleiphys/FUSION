# Fluid dynamics from the Boltzmann equation using a maximum entropy distribution

> Using the recently developed ``Maximum Entropy'' (or ``least biased'') distribution function to truncate the moment hierarchy arising from kinetic theory, we formulate a far-from-equilibrium macroscopic theory that provides the possibility of describing both free-streaming and hydrodynamic regimes of heavy-ion collisions within a single framework. Unlike traditional hydrodynamic theories that include viscous corrections to finite order, the present formulation incorporates contributions to all orders in shear and bulk inverse Reynolds numbers, allowing it to handle large dissipative fluxes. By considering flow profiles relevant for heavy-ion collisions (Bjorken and Gubser flows), we demonstrate that the present approach provides excellent agreement with underlying kinetic theory throughout the fluid's evolution and, especially, in far-off-equilibrium regimes where traditional hydrodynamics breaks down.

## Key claim
The paper formulates a far-from-equilibrium macroscopic theory, called Maximum Entropy (ME) hydrodynamics, that can describe both free-streaming and hydrodynamic regimes of heavy-ion collisions within a single framework by using the maximum entropy distribution to close the moment hierarchy from kinetic theory, without assuming small deviations from local equilibrium.

## Method
The authors truncate the infinite hierarchy of moment equations from the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) by using the maximum entropy (least-biased) distribution function, which maximizes the non-equilibrium entropy density subject to constraints from the instantaneous hydrodynamic variables (energy density, bulk viscous pressure, and shear stress tensor). At each time step, an equilibrium distribution and a maximum entropy distribution are matched to the energy-momentum tensor from the previous step, and the difference is used to evaluate the non-hydrodynamic moments that couple to the evolution equations for the dissipative fluxes. The approach is tested against exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation for Bjorken flow (with both massless and massive constituents) and Gubser flow (conformal).

## Key numbers
- For Bjorken flow with massless particles, the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is fixed at η/s = 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08, with relaxation time τ_R = 5η/(sT).
- For Bjorken flow with massive particles, the mass is set to m/T₀ = 1 (where T₀ is the initial temperature), with η/s = 1/(4π) and ζ/s = 0.02.
- For Gubser flow, the conformal system has η/s = 1/(4π) and the relaxation time τ_R = 5η/(sT).

## Context
This work extends the maximum entropy principle previously applied to particlization at freeze-out by Everett (2021) into a dynamical framework for general far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic evolution. It contrasts with traditional truncation schemes such as the Grad 14-moment approximation (Grad, Israel, Stewart), the Chapman-Enskog expansion (Chapman, Jaiswal), and anisotropic hydrodynamics using the Romatschke-Strickland ansatz (Romatschke, Florkowski, Martinez), all of which either assume proximity to equilibrium or are tailored to specific flow geometries. The approach enables a unified description of heavy-ion collisions from early free-streaming to late hydrodynamic stages without requiring additional assumptions about the microscopic kinetics.
