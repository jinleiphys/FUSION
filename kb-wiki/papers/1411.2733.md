# Electric Fields and Chiral Magnetic Effect in Cu + Au Collisions

> The non-central Cu + Au collisions can create strong out-of-plane magnetic fields and in-plane electric fields. By using the HIJING model, we study the general properties of the electromagnetic fields in Cu + Au collisions at 200 GeV and their impacts on the charge-dependent two-particle correlator $γ_{q_1q_2}=<\cos(ϕ_1+ϕ_2-2ψ_{RP})>$ (see main text for definition) which was used for the detection of the chiral magnetic effect (CME). Compared with Au + Au collisions, we find that the in-plane electric fields in Cu + Au collisions can strongly suppress the two-particle correlator or even reverse its sign if the lifetime of the electric fields is long. Combining with the expectation that if $γ_{q_1q_2}$ is induced by elliptic-flow driven effects we would not see such strong suppression or reversion, our results suggest to use Cu + Au collisions to test CME and understand the mechanisms that underlie $γ_{q_1q_2}$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the charge-dependent two-particle correlator Δγ in Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV may be strongly suppressed or even reversed in sign compared to Au+Au collisions, but only if the in-plane electric fields have a sufficiently long lifetime (e.g., τ_E ≥ 4 fm) and if the correlator is dominated by electromagnetic-field-induced effects rather than by elliptic-flow-driven effects.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING model to simulate Cu+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV on an event-by-event basis, calculating the electromagnetic fields from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials of all protons in both nuclei. They then constructed a single-particle distribution that includes directed flow, a chiral magnetic effect (CME) term along the magnetic field direction, and an electric conduction term along the electric field direction, and derived the two-particle correlator Δγ = (2γ_OS − γ_SS)/2. The CME conductivity was fixed by fitting Δγ to STAR Au+Au data with a magnetic field lifetime τ_B = 5 fm, and the electric field lifetime τ_E was treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The event-averaged magnetic field strength in Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV is comparable to that in Au+Au collisions (no explicit numerical value given for the field strength itself, but the comparison is stated).
- The in-plane electric field in Cu+Au collisions points from the Au nucleus to the Cu nucleus; its strength is smaller than the magnetic field but still "very large" (no explicit numerical value given).
- For τ_E = 2 fm, Δγ in Cu+Au is suppressed relative to Au+Au; for τ_E = 3 fm the suppression is clearer; for τ_E = 4 fm, Δγ becomes negative (sign reversal).
- The fitting to STAR Au+Au data used T = 350 MeV and τ_B = 5 fm; the fit is "surprisingly good" for centralities below 60%.
- The in-medium charge radius of the proton was taken as R_p = 0.8775 fm; varying R_p from 0.7 fm to 0.9 fm shifts numerical results by about 10%.
- The electric conductivity was taken from lattice QCD as σ = 0.4 C_EM T with C_EM = (2/3)e^2 for u,d,s quarks.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic fields in symmetric collisions (Au+Au at RHIC and Pb+Pb at LHC) by Skokov (2009), Voronyuk (2011), Bzdak (2011), and Deng (2012) to the asymmetric Cu+Au system. It directly addresses the ongoing debate about whether the two-particle correlator γ measured by STAR (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2014) and ALICE (Abelev 2012) is driven by the chiral magnetic effect or by elliptic-flow-driven effects such as transverse momentum conservation (Pratt 2010, Bzdak 2010) and local charge conservation (Schlichting 2010). The paper proposes Cu+Au collisions as a new test bed to distinguish these interpretations, complementing earlier proposals to use U+U collisions (Voloshin 2010).
