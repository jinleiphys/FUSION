# A Systematic Study Of Nuclear Matter: Finite Nuclei To Neutron Star

> The main aim of the thesis is to study the properties of nuclear matter, i.e., finite nuclei to infinite nuclear matter, at zero and finite temperature within effective field theory motived relativistic mean-field model by using some of the recent parameter sets. For this, we have chosen exotic, superheavy, and natural/neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei and studied ground as well as excited-state bulk and surface properties of nuclei. We have also tried to figure out the possible constraints on the DM variables by considering WIMP, a DM candidate, inside the NS core and using gravitational-wave data GW170817.

## Key claim

This thesis systematically studies nuclear matter properties from finite nuclei to infinite nuclear matter at zero and finite temperature using effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field models with recent parameter sets, and also explores possible constraints on dark matter variables by considering weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) inside neutron star cores using gravitational-wave data from GW170817.

## Method

The author employed relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with effective field theory motivated parameter sets to calculate ground and excited state bulk and surface properties of exotic, superheavy, and neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei. For neutron star studies, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved using the same RMF equations of state, and dark matter effects were incorporated by including WIMP particles in the neutron star core. The gravitational-wave data from the GW170817 event was used to constrain the dark matter variables.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon for finite nuclei were calculated and compared with experimental data from Audi et al. (2012), with typical deviations on the order of a few MeV
- Neutron star maximum masses were computed using RMF parameter sets, with results constrained by the GW170817 event (Abbott et al., 2017) which provided a tidal deformability constraint
- The symmetry energy and its density dependence were calculated for various RMF parameter sets, with values at saturation density typically around 30-35 MeV
- Nuclear matter saturation properties including binding energy per nucleon (around -16 MeV) and saturation density (around 0.15 fm^-3) were computed for different parameter sets

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of nuclear matter using RMF models previously developed by Walecka (1974), Serot and Walecka (1986), and later parameter sets such as those by Lalazissis et al. (1997) and Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005). The thesis connects finite nuclei properties to infinite nuclear matter and neutron star observations, enabling constraints on the nuclear equation of state from both terrestrial experiments and astrophysical observations including the GW170817 neutron star merger event (Abbott et al., 2017). The inclusion of dark matter in neutron stars builds on earlier work by Bergstrom et al. (2006) and others who considered WIMP accumulation in compact objects.
