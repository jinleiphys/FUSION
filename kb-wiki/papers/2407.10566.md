# Probing QGP droplets with charmonium in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions

> We study the hot medium effects in high-multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=13$ TeV via the charmonium probes. The hot medium is described with the hydrodynamic model, while charmonium evolutions in the medium are studied with a time-dependent Schrödinger equation. The hot medium dissociation on charmonium is considered with the temperature-dependent complex potential parametrized with the results from lattice QCD calculations. The ratio $σ_{ψ(2S)}/σ_{J/ψ}$ of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ production cross sections are calculated and compared with the LHCb experimental data in pp collisions. Our calculations explain the charmonium relative suppression in different transverse momentum and multiplicity bins. The suppression of this ratio is mainly affected by the effects of the deconfined medium. It is less affected by the initial effects before the generation of the heavy quark pair. We suggest this to be a clear signal of the small QGP droplets generated in high multiplicity pp collisions.

## Key claim

The suppression of the normalized ratio $\sigma_{\psi(2S)}/\sigma_{J/\psi}$ in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=13$ TeV is interpreted as a clear signal of final-state interactions with a deconfined medium (small QGP droplets), with the caveat that this conclusion holds despite significant uncertainties in the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential, because the ratio is less affected by these uncertainties than individual charmonium nuclear modification factors.

## Method

The authors model the hot medium in high-multiplicity pp collisions using the hydrodynamic model (MUSIC) to generate temperature profiles, with initial maximal temperatures treated as parameters (0.2, 0.25, and 0.3 GeV at $\tau_0 = 0.4$ fm/c). Charmonium evolution in the medium is studied with a time-dependent Schrödinger equation incorporating a complex heavy quark potential, where the real part is a Cornell potential (neglecting weak color screening) and the imaginary part is parametrized from lattice QCD results using two different functional forms ($V_I^{(A)}$ and $V_I^{(B)}$) to account for uncertainty. The survival probabilities of $J/\psi$ and $\psi(2S)$ are calculated by averaging over many wave packets with different initial positions and transverse momenta, and the normalized ratio $\sigma_{\psi(2S)}/\sigma_{J/\psi}$ is compared with LHCb experimental data across multiplicity and $p_T$ bins.

## Key numbers

- The charm quark mass is set to $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, with reduced mass $m_\mu = m_c/2$.
- The critical temperature for the medium is $T_c = 0.16$ GeV.
- The initial time for the Schrödinger equation evolution is $\tau_0 = 0.4$ fm/c.
- The proton radius parameter is $r_p = 0.9$ fm with diffuseness $a_p = 0.1$ fm; $\rho_0 = 0.29$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For $J/\psi$, the mean transverse momentum squared without hot medium effects at 13 TeV is $\langle p_T^2 \rangle = 15$ (GeV/c)$^2$, with $n = 3.2$.
- The parametrization of the ratio $\sigma_{\psi(2S)}/\sigma_{J/\psi}$ as a function of $p_T$ uses coefficients $a_r = -0.0103$, $b_r = 0.365$, $c_r = 1.107$.
- Three initial maximal temperatures are considered for the most central pp collisions: $T_0 = 0.2$, 0.25, and 0.3 GeV.
- The QGP lifetime is approximately 1-2 fm/c.
- The imaginary potential parametrization $V_I^{(A)}$ uses $a_1 = -0.040$ and $a_2 = 0.50$; $V_I^{(B)}$ uses $b_1 = 1.2$ and $b_2 = 0.54$.
- At T = 0.3 GeV, the magnitude of $V_I^{(B)}$ is around ten times larger than $V_I^{(A)}$ at the distance 0.5 fm (the $J/\psi$ mean radius).

## Context

This work extends the Schrödinger equation-based potential model previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Strickland 2011, Islam 2020, Wen 2022) and small collision systems like p-Pb (Song 2023) to high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV. It directly addresses the LHCb experimental observation (LHCb 2023) of charmonium suppression in pp collisions, which the authors interpret as evidence for small QGP droplets. The study enables quantitative constraints on the initial temperature of the QGP droplet in pp collisions (finding $T_0 \approx 0.25$ GeV best describes the data) and establishes the ratio $\sigma_{\psi(2S)}/\sigma_{J/\psi}$ as a clean probe of deconfined matter that is largely independent of cold nuclear matter effects and initial-state effects.
