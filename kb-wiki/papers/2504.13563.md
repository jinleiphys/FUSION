# Charge radii and their deformation correlation for even-$Z$ nuclei in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The systematics are investigated for the charge radii of the even-$Z$ nuclei with $8 \leqslant Z \leqslant 120$ calculated by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the functional PC-PK1, and their deformation correlation is explored. The available data of the charge radius are reproduced with a root-mean-square deviation $σ=0.033$ fm. In particular, for the nuclei between the closed shells, the descriptions of the charge radii are remarkably improved by including the deformation. Taking molybdenum isotopes as examples, both the evolutions of the charge radius and deformation are well reproduced. It is found that while the charge radius typically increases with the deformation, there also exist different cases. For example, in $^{346}$Sg, the charge radius of the deformed ground state is smaller than the one of the spherical state, and the largest binding energy does not necessarily correspond to the smallest charge radius. The increase or decrease of the charge radii with deformation is related to specific shell structures, particularly the key single-particle levels near the Fermi energy.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) reproduces available charge radii data for even-Z nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.033 fm, but the charge radius does not always increase with deformation; in 346Sg, the deformed ground state has a smaller charge radius than the spherical state, and the largest binding energy does not necessarily correspond to the smallest charge radius.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of charge radii for all even-Z nuclei with atomic numbers 8 to 120 using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional. The RHB equations were solved in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis to account for continuum effects, with densities and potentials expanded in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed nuclei. The charge radius was computed from the proton root-mean-square radius plus a 0.64 fm^2 correction, and results were compared to experimental data and to spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation between DRHBc charge radii and 620 experimental data points: sigma = 0.033 fm
- RMS deviation for even-even nuclei: sigma = 0.032 fm; for even-odd nuclei: sigma = 0.034 fm
- RMS deviation in RCHB calculations for comparison: sigma = 0.036 fm
- RMS difference between DRHBc and RCHB charge radii across 4609 nuclei: sigma = 0.055 fm
- For molybdenum isotopes, RMS deviation from data improved from 0.062 fm (RCHB) to 0.030 fm (DRHBc)
- In 346Sg, the deformed ground state charge radius is 6.622 fm, smaller than the spherical state value of 6.638 fm
- Energy difference between prolate and oblate minima in Mo isotopes with shape coexistence ranges from 0.1 to 2.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table project (Zhang 2022, Guo 2024) by systematically analyzing charge radii and their correlation with deformation, improving upon the spherical RCHB calculations of Xia (2018). The results demonstrate that including deformation significantly improves charge radius descriptions for nuclei between closed shells, as shown for molybdenum isotopes. The findings enable future inclusion of odd-Z nuclei in the DRHBc mass table and motivate experimental measurements that can distinguish prolate from oblate shapes to further constrain nuclear models.
