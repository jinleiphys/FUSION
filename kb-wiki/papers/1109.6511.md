# Multiple shape coexistence in the nucleus $^{80}$Zr

> We study the low-lying energy spectrum of the rp-process waiting point nucleus 80Zr with state-of-the-art beyond mean field methods with the Gogny D1S interaction. Symmetry restoration and configuration mixing of axial and triaxial shapes are included in the calculations. Five 0+ states corresponding to different nuclear shapes are obtained below 2.25 MeV and several rotational and γ- bands built upon them are identified. Nevertheless, these states do not modify the β-decay half-life having a negligible effect in the rp-process. A good agreement with the available experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that five distinct 0+ states corresponding to different nuclear shapes exist below 2.25 MeV in 80Zr, but these excited states do not modify the beta-decay half-life and have a negligible effect on the rp-process, contrary to the possibility that isomeric states could open additional decay channels.

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction, including particle number projection and triaxial angular momentum projection. They constructed a triangular mesh of 60 intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states in the (β2, γ) deformation plane and solved the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equations to obtain energy levels and B(E2) transition probabilities. The calculations go beyond previous work by including full triaxial angular momentum projection and shape mixing without assuming Gaussian overlaps for the collective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Five 0+ states are obtained below 2.25 MeV in 80Zr
- The ground state band head is at 0 MeV, with the first excited 0+ state at 0.76 MeV, the second at 1.4 MeV, the third at 1.5 MeV, and the fourth at 2.24 MeV
- The ground state band is built on an axial prolate configuration at (β2, γ) = (0.55, 0°)
- The first excited band is based on a triaxial configuration at (β2, γ) ≈ (0.4, 20°)
- The second excited band is built on a triaxial minimum at (β2, γ) = (0.5, 30°)
- The experimental ground state half-life of 80Zr is approximately 4.1 s
- Selected interband B(E2) values include: 0.039 e²b² for 0₂ → 2₁, 0.247 e²b² for 2₃ → 2₂, and 0.441 e²b² for 2₅ → 2₄

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in the A ≈ 80 region, which had been investigated using microscopic-macroscopic models (Moller, Naza, Heyde), Monte Carlo shell model (Langanke and Dean), and self-consistent mean field approximations with Skyrme, Relativistic, and Gogny interactions. The approach improves upon the collective Hamiltonian method of Gogny2 by including particle number projection and avoiding Gaussian overlap assumptions for the triaxial plane. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted excited 0+ states via Coulomb excitation studies once radioactive beam intensities are sufficient.
