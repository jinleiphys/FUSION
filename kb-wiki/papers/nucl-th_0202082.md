# Bethe-Salpeter equation and a nonperturbative quark-gluon vertex

> A Ward-Takahashi identity preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel can always be calculated explicitly from a dressed-quark-gluon vertex whose diagrammatic content is enumerable. We illustrate that fact using a vertex obtained via the complete resummation of dressed-gluon ladders. While this vertex is planar, the vertex-consistent kernel is nonplanar and that is true for any dressed vertex. In an exemplifying model the rainbow-ladder truncation of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations yields many results; e.g., pi- and rho-meson masses, that are changed little by including higher-order corrections. Repulsion generated by nonplanar diagrams in the vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel for quark-quark scattering is sufficient to guarantee that diquark bound states do not exist.

## Key claim

A Ward-Takahashi identity preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel can always be calculated explicitly from a dressed-quark-gluon vertex whose diagrammatic content is enumerable, and while the vertex obtained via complete resummation of dressed-gluon ladders is planar, the vertex-consistent kernel is necessarily nonplanar for any dressed vertex. In an exemplifying model, the rainbow-ladder truncation of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations yields results for pi- and rho-meson masses that are changed little by including higher-order corrections.

## Method

The authors employed a confining model defined by a contact interaction for the dressed-gluon propagator, which reduces integral equations to algebraic ones. They resummed the infinite set of dressed-gluon ladder diagrams contributing to the dressed-quark-gluon vertex via an algebraic recursion relation, solved the coupled gap and vertex equations simultaneously, and then constructed the vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel using functional differentiation of the quark self-energy. The resulting bound state equations were solved for meson and diquark channels.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit, the scalar part of the dressed-quark propagator at zero momentum is B0 = (1/2)*sqrt(15) ≈ 1.94 (in units of the model mass scale G), compared to B0^(0) = 2 for the bare vertex, a change of -3%.
- The vector part at zero momentum is A0 = 26/15 ≈ 1.73, compared to A0^(0) = 2 for the bare vertex, a change of -13%.
- The dressed-quark-gluon vertex form factors at zero momentum are: alpha1(0) = 15/16 ≈ 0.94 (cf. 1.0 for bare vertex), alpha2(0) = 221/1800 ≈ 0.12 (cf. 0.0), alpha3(0) = 1/sqrt(15) ≈ 0.26 (cf. 0.0).
- The constituent quark mass at zero momentum M(0) = B(0)/A(0) takes values: 1.000 (N=0), 1.105 (N=1), 1.115 (N=2), 1.117 (N=infinity), where N is the order of vertex dressing included.
- For a current-quark mass m = 0.023 (in units of G), corresponding to approximately 10 MeV when G ≈ 0.5 GeV, the dressed-quark propagator functions show little variation with vertex dressing order.

## Context

This work extends the systematic truncation scheme of the Dyson-Schwinger equations introduced by the same group (Ref. truncscheme in the paper), which provides a means to identify channels where the leading-order rainbow-ladder truncation is accurate. The paper provides a nonperturbative verification that the rainbow-ladder truncation is accurate for flavor-nonsinglet pseudoscalar and vector mesons, building on earlier applications to pi and rho mesons by Maris and Roberts (1997) and others. The finding that nonplanar diagrams in the vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel generate sufficient repulsion to guarantee that diquark bound states do not exist enables future studies of baryons as quark-diquark systems without the need to introduce ad hoc diquark confinement.
