# Complete $N_f^2$ contributions to four-loop pure-singlet splitting functions

> The scale evolution of parton distributions is determined by universal splitting functions. As a milestone towards the computation of these functions to four-loop order in QCD, we compute all contributions to the pure-singlet quark-quark splitting functions that involve two closed fermion loops. The splitting functions are extracted from the pole terms of off-shell operator matrix elements, and the workflow for their calculation is outlined. We reproduce known results for the non-singlet four-loop splitting functions and validate our new pure-singlet results against fixed Mellin moments.

## Key claim

The paper computes all contributions to the four-loop pure-singlet quark-quark splitting functions that involve two closed fermion loops (the $N_f^2$ terms), representing a milestone toward the complete four-loop splitting functions needed for N3LO QCD predictions; the results are validated against fixed Mellin moments and reproduce known non-singlet four-loop splitting functions as a cross-check.

## Method

The splitting functions are extracted from the pole terms of off-shell operator matrix elements (OMEs) using the operator product expansion (OPE) framework, where the renormalization of twist-two operators requires accounting for mixing with gauge-variant operators. The four-loop OMEs are computed by generating Feynman diagrams with QGRAF, summing non-standard $(\Delta \cdot p)^{n-1}$ terms into linear propagators via a tracing parameter $t$, performing integration-by-parts reductions using finite field sampling and rational reconstruction, and solving master integrals through differential equations cast into $\epsilon$-form. The bare OME results in parameter-$t$ space are transformed to $n$-space in terms of harmonic sums using the HarmonicSums package.

## Context

This work extends the OPE-based approach for splitting function calculations previously applied at lower loop orders by Gross (1974), Floratos (1978), Gonzalez-Arroyo (1979), Hamberg (1991), Blumlein (2021), and Gehrmann (2023), and builds on the novel procedure for handling gauge-variant operator counterterms proposed by Gehrmann (2023). The results provide the complete $N_f^2$ contributions to the four-loop pure-singlet splitting functions, which together with partial results for non-singlet splitting functions by Moch (2017) and fixed Mellin moments of singlet splitting functions by Moch (2021) and Falcioni (2023) move toward the full set of four-loop splitting functions required for fully consistent N3LO hadron collider predictions.
