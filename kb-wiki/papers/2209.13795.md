# Charmonium, $B_c$ and X(3872) Transport at the LHC

> We deploy a kinetic-rate equation to evaluate the transport of $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $B_c$ and X(3872) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and compare their production yields to experimental data from the Large Hadron Collider. The rate equation has two main transport parameters, i.e., the equilibrium limit and reaction rate for each state. The temperature-dependent equilibrium limits include charm- and bottom-quark fugacities based on their initial production. The reaction rates for charmonia, bottomonia and $B_c$ rely on charm- and bottom-quark masses as well as binding energies from a thermodynamic $T$-matrix approach. For the X(3872) particle, its internal structure information is encoded in reaction rates and initial conditions in the hadronic phase via two different scenarios: a loosely bound hadronic molecule vs. a compact diquark-antidiquark tetraquark.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the internal structure of the X(3872) particle, whether it is a loosely bound hadronic molecule or a compact diquark-antidiquark tetraquark, can be distinguished by its production yields in heavy-ion collisions, with the tetraquark scenario producing 2-5 times higher yields than the molecule scenario, but this conclusion depends on the assumed hierarchy of reaction rates and initial conditions in the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors solve a kinetic rate equation for each quarkonium state (J/ψ, ψ(2S), Bc, and X(3872)) in a cylindrically expanding fireball background simulating Pb-Pb collisions at 5 TeV. The rate equation depends on two transport parameters: the equilibrium limit, which includes charm- and bottom-quark fugacities from heavy-flavor conservation, and the inelastic reaction rate, computed via quasi-free approximation for charmonia, bottomonia, and Bc using binding energies from a thermodynamic T-matrix approach. For the X(3872), reaction rates are parameterized with a power-law form, with molecule rates taken as Γ0≈400±100 MeV and tetraquark rates as Γ0≈50-80 MeV at T0=180 MeV, and initial conditions set to zero for the molecule and chemical equilibrium for the tetraquark.

## Key numbers

- For J/ψ and ψ(2S) in forward-rapidity 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, the input charm cross section is dσ_{c\bar{c}}^{pp}/dy = 0.72±0.7 mb with 15-40% shadowing uncertainty.
- For Bc production, the pp cross section is estimated at dσ^{pp}/dy = 27-82 nb, with regeneration dominating the spectra up to pT≈2m_{Bc}.
- For the X(3872), molecule reaction rates at T0=180 MeV are Γ0^{mol}≈400±100 MeV, while tetraquark rates are Γ0^{tet}≈50-80 MeV.
- The X(3872) tetraquark yields are 2-5 times larger than molecule yields across centralities in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the rate-equation transport approach previously applied to charmonia by Grandchamp (2003), Zhao (2011), and Du (2015) to include Bc and the exotic X(3872) particle. The results for ψ(2S) are compared to recent ALICE data (Hushnud, 2022), and Bc results are compared to CMS data (2022). For the X(3872), the approach differs from most coalescence model predictions by encoding structure information through reaction rate hierarchies, enabling future experimental discrimination between the molecular and tetraquark scenarios at the LHC.
