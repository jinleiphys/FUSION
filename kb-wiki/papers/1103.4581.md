# The open string membrane paradigm with external electromagnetic fields

> We study the effective geometry felt by the fluctuations of open strings living on the worldvolume of probe D-branes in the presence of background electromagnetic fields. This is captured by an effective action consisting of a Maxwell term and a topological term, with the role of the metric played by the open string metric. Studying generalized Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates for stationary but non-static manifolds, we consider an open string membrane paradigm to obtain a generic formula for the DC transport coefficients, including the effect of external electromagnetic fields present on the worldvolume of the probe branes. We show that the previously studied singular shell, present when a critical electric field strength is turned on, behaves as a horizon for the open string degrees of freedom. The results of this analysis can be used to define a membrane paradigm for a very general class of spacetimes with non-diagonal metrics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the singular shell on probe D-branes, which appears when a critical electric field is turned on, behaves as a horizon for open string degrees of freedom, and this allows the definition of a membrane paradigm for a very general class of spacetimes with non-diagonal metrics, extending previous work by Iqbal and Liu (2009) that assumed diagonal metrics and no background electromagnetic fields.

## Method

The authors study the effective geometry felt by fluctuations of open strings on probe D7-branes in an AdS5 x S5 background by expanding the DBI action to second order in gauge field fluctuations, obtaining an effective action consisting of a Maxwell term and a topological term written in terms of the open string metric. They then analyze generalized Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates for stationary but non-static manifolds to extend the black hole membrane paradigm to the open string metric, deriving a generic formula for DC transport coefficients that includes the effect of external electromagnetic fields on the probe brane worldvolume.

## Key numbers

- The singular shell radius is given by r_s = 1/sqrt(1 + e_x^2) where e_x = E_x/(pi T L)^2, with the condition E_x = sqrt(-g_tt(r_s) g_xx(r_s))
- The DC conductivity for zero magnetic field is sigma_xx = (N_f N_c T)/(4 pi) sqrt(sqrt(1 + e_x^2)(1 - psi_s^2)^3 + n_q^2/(1 + e_x^2))
- The Hall conductivity is sigma_yx = -(N_f N_c T)/(4 pi) (n_q B_z)/(F + B_x^2 + B_z^2) where F is a function of electric and magnetic fields
- The ratio eta/s = 1/(4 pi) is cited as the celebrated result of the black hole membrane paradigm from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005)

## Context

This work extends the membrane paradigm formalism of Iqbal and Liu (2009), which was restricted to diagonal metrics and did not capture probe branes with non-trivial field strength components, to include background electromagnetic fields on probe D-branes. It also generalizes the DC conductivity formula from Mas, Shock, and Tarrio (2009) by providing a membrane paradigm interpretation. The results enable the calculation of transport coefficients for holographic systems modeling both the Quark-Gluon Plasma and condensed matter systems with external electromagnetic fields, and show that the open string horizon has an associated temperature that can be interpreted in the dual gauge theory as the effective temperature felt by fundamental matter moving in the electric field.
