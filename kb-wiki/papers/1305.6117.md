# Pion form factor and reactions e^+e^---> omega pi^0 and e^+e^---> pi^+ pi^- pi^+ pi^- at energies up to 2-3 GeV in the many-channel approach

> Using the field-theory-inspired expression for the pion electromagnetic form factor F_π, a good description of the data in the range -10<s<1 GeV^2 is obtained upon taking into account the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PP) loops. When the vector-pseudoscalar (VP) and the axial vector-pseudoscalar (AP) loops are taken into account in addition to the PP ones, a good description of the BABAR data on the reaction e^+e^--->pi^+ pi^- is obtained at energies up to 3 GeV. The inclusion of the VP and AP loops demands the treatment of the reactions e^+e^---> omega pi^0 and e^+e^---> pi^+ pi^- pi^+ pi^-. This task is also made, with the SND data on the omega pi^0 production and the BABAR data on the pi^+ pi^- pi^+ pi^- production, both in e^+e^- annihilation, upon taking into account ρ(770) and the heavier ρ(1450), ρ(1700), and ρ(2100) resonances. The problems with inclusion of the VP and AP loops are pointed out and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including vector-pseudoscalar (VP) and axial vector-pseudoscalar (AP) loops, in addition to pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PP) loops, allows a good description of the BABAR data on e+e- → π+π- up to 3 GeV, but only if the reactions e+e- → ωπ0 and e+e- → π+π-π+π- are also treated simultaneously; the authors point out and discuss problems that arise from including these additional loop contributions.

## Method

The authors used a field-theory-inspired expression for the pion electromagnetic form factor Fπ, incorporating PP, VP, and AP loop contributions to the diagonal and nondiagonal polarization operators of ρ-like resonances (ρ(770), ρ(1450), ρ(1700), ρ(2100)). They fitted the BABAR data on e+e- → π+π- up to 3 GeV, the SND data on e+e- → ωπ0, and the BABAR data on e+e- → π+π-π+π- separately in a scheme with four resonances and all three loop types, using a three-subtraction scheme for nondiagonal polarization operators instead of the two-subtraction scheme used in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The bare mass of ρ(770) from the e+e- → π+π- fit is 765.6 ± 0.1 MeV, with coupling constant gρ1ππ = 6.336 ± 0.004.
- The mass of ρ(1450) from the e+e- → π+π- fit is 1507 ± 3 MeV; from the e+e- → π+π-π+π- fit it is 1122 ± 1 MeV; from the e+e- → ωπ0 fit it is 1412 ± 6 MeV.
- The mass of ρ(1700) from the e+e- → π+π- fit is 1831 ± 4 MeV; from the e+e- → π+π-π+π- fit it is 1648 ± 1 MeV; from the e+e- → ωπ0 fit it is 1661 ± 5 MeV.
- The mass of ρ(2100) from the e+e- → π+π- fit is 2154 ± 15 MeV; from the e+e- → π+π-π+π- fit it is 2390 ± 5 MeV; from the e+e- → ωπ0 fit it is 1969 ± 7 MeV.
- The χ2 per degree of freedom for the e+e- → π+π- fit is 335/316; for e+e- → π+π-π+π- it is 400/59; for e+e- → ωπ0 it is 43/20.
- The ω meson mass from the e+e- → π+π- fit is 782.02 ± 0.10 MeV.
- The parameter ReΠ'ωρ (responsible for ωρ mixing) from the e+e- → π+π- fit is (4.38 ± 0.07) × 10-3 GeV2.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous field-theory-inspired approach (Achasov and Kozhevnikov, 2011, 2012, 2013) which described the pion form factor up to 1 GeV using only PP loops, to higher energies up to 3 GeV by adding VP and AP loops. It contrasts with the broken hidden local symmetry model of Benayoun et al. (2013, 2012, 2001) which did not extend analysis above 1 GeV, and with the work of Czyz et al. (2010) which included heavier ρ resonances but did not account for mixing among them. The results enable simultaneous description of three different e+e- annihilation channels within a single framework, though the authors note problems with the inclusion of VP and AP loops that require further study.
