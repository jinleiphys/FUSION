# Three loop anomalous dimension of the non-singlet transversity operator in QCD

> We calculate the three-loop anomalous dimension of the non-singlet transverse operator from N=1 to N=15. Using some guess we have reconstructed a general form of three-loop anomalous dimension for arbitrary Mellin moment N. Obtained result is transformed into Bjorken-x space by an inverse Mellin transformation. The final expressions are presented in both Mellin-N and Bjorken-x space.

## Key claim

The paper presents a reconstructed three-loop anomalous dimension for the non-singlet transversity operator in QCD for arbitrary Mellin moment \(N\), but the authors explicitly state that the reconstruction method is "mathematically non-rigorous" and relies on a guess based on the first fifteen fixed moments.

## Method

The authors computed the first fifteen fixed Mellin moments (N=1 to N=15) of the three-loop anomalous dimension for the non-singlet transversity operator using the MINCER program for three-loop diagrams in Feynman gauge, with the DIANA and QGRAF packages for diagram generation and the COLOR package for color traces. They then reconstructed a general form for arbitrary \(N\) by assuming that the transversity anomalous dimension differs from the known non-singlet anomalous dimension (Moch, Vermaseren, Vogt 2004) only by a small set of additional terms, using the maximal transcendentality principle and the LLL-algorithm to solve the resulting Diophantine system for the coefficients of harmonic sums.

## Key numbers

* The first fifteen Mellin moments of the three-loop anomalous dimension \(\gamma^{(2)\pm}_{\mathrm{TR,ns}}(N)\) were calculated for N=1 to N=15; the explicit numerical values for the reduced contributions are given in the text (e.g., for the \(\cf\n2f\) contribution the reduced values are \(\{4/9, 4/27, 2/27, 2/45, 4/135, 4/189, 1/63, 1/81, 4/405, 4/495, 2/297, 2/351, 4/819, 4/945, 1/270\}\)).
* The \(\cf\n2f\) contribution to the three-loop anomalous dimension is given by \(\gamma^{(2)}_{\mathrm{TR,ns},\cf\n2f}(N) = 16\,{\cf \n2f} \big( 17/144 - (1/27) S_1 - (5/27) S_2 + (1/9) S_3 - 1/(18 N(N+1)) \big)\).
* The \(\cf^3\) contribution includes a \(\zeta_3\) term: for even \(N\), \(\widetilde{\gamma}^{(2)+}_{\mathrm{TR,ns},\cf^3\z3}(N) = 96\,\cf^3\z3 / ((N-1)(N+2))\); for odd \(N\), \(\widetilde{\gamma}^{(2)-}_{\mathrm{TR,ns},\cf^3\z3}(N) = 288\,\cf^3\z3 / (N(N+1))\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the transversity anomalous dimension at leading order (Artru 1989, Baldracchini 1980, Shifman 1980, Bukhvostov 1985, Blumlein 2001, Mukherjee 2001) and next-to-leading order (Hayashigaki 1997, Kumano 1997, Vogelsang 1997), and complements known three-loop moments for N=1 to 8 (Gracey 2003, 2006) and partial results for N=9 to 14 (Blumlein 2009). The reconstructed result enables the full three-loop evolution of transversity distributions in Bjorken-x space, and the authors note that a subsequent calculation of the N=16 moment (Bagaev 2012) confirmed their prediction for even N.
