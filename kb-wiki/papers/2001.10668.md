# The impact of isomers on a kilonova associated with neutron star mergers

> We investigate the significance of isomers on a kilonova associated with neutron star mergers (NSMs) for the first time. We calculate the evolution of abundances and energy generation rates ($\dotε_{\rm nuc}$) of ejecta from NSMs, taking into account $β^-$ decay through isomers. We find that for ejecta with electron fraction ($Y_e$) of $0.2-0.3$, $\dotε_{\rm nuc}$ is appreciably changed from those without isomers, due to the large change in timing of $β^-$ decay through the isomeric states of ${}^{123, 125, 127} \rm Sn$ and ${}^{128} \rm Sb$. In particular, the effects of the isomers on $\dotε_{\rm nuc}$ are prominent for ejecta of $Y_e \sim 0.25$, which could emit a fraction of an early, blue component of a kilonova observed in GW170817. When the excitation from a ground state to its isomeric state is important, the isomers of ${}^{129} \rm Sb$ and ${}^{129, 131} \rm Te$ also cause additional and appreciable change in $\dotε_{\rm nuc}$. Furthermore, we demonstrate that larger amounts of lanthanide-free ejecta result in a better fit of an observed light curve of the kilonova in GW170817 if the isomers are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for neutron star merger ejecta with electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.2-0.3\), the nuclear energy generation rate \(\dot{\epsilon}_{\rm nuc}\) is appreciably changed from calculations without isomers, due to large changes in the timing of \(\beta^-\) decay through isomeric states of \({}^{123,125,127}\mathrm{Sn}\) and \({}^{128}\mathrm{Sb}\). The effect is most prominent for \(Y_e \sim 0.25\) ejecta, which could correspond to a fraction of the early blue kilonova component observed in GW170817, but the authors emphasize that their results are based on three limiting cases (ground states only, all isomers, or a subset of isomers) and a simplified dynamical model.

## Method

The authors used a nuclear reaction network containing 4070 nuclei from neutrons to fermium, with reaction rates theoretically evaluated using the FRDM mass model, and modified the \(\beta^-\) decay half-lives of nine selected nuclei (\({}^{121,123,125,127}\mathrm{Sn}\), \({}^{128,129}\mathrm{Sb}\), \({}^{129,131}\mathrm{Te}\), and \({}^{134}\mathrm{I}\)) to their isomeric-state values. They adopted a simplified uniformly expanding ejecta model with four parameters (ejecta mass \(M_{\rm ej}=0.03 M_\odot\), expansion velocity \(v_{\rm ex}/c = 0.05, 0.1, 0.2\), electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.1, 0.15, 0.2, 0.25, 0.3\), and entropy per baryon \(s = 10 k_{\rm B}\)), and compared three cases: (a) all nuclei always in ground states, (b) all nine nuclei always in isomeric states with 100% \(\beta^-\) branching ratios, and (c) only \({}^{123,125,127}\mathrm{Sn}\) and \({}^{128}\mathrm{Sb}\) always in isomeric states. They then fitted a two-component emission model to the bolometric light curve of GW170817.

## Key numbers

* For ejecta with \(Y_e = 0.25\) and \(v_{\rm ex} = 0.05c\), the energy generation rate \(\dot{\epsilon}_{\rm nuc}\) shows appreciable differences between cases (a) and (b) at early times (\(t = 0.1-3\) days) and later times (\(t = 10-100\) days).
* To fit the observed bolometric luminosity of GW170817, the required mass of high-\(Y_e\) (lanthanide-free) ejecta is \(M_{\rm ej, high} = 0.015 M_\odot\) for case (a), \(0.025 M_\odot\) for case (b), and \(0.018 M_\odot\) for case (c).
* The mass fractions of lanthanides in the two-component model are 0.019, 0.016, and 0.018 for cases (a), (b), and (c), respectively, slightly above an upper bound of \(\sim 0.01\) from Kasen et al. (2017).
* Excitation energies from ground state to isomeric state are 0.025 MeV for \({}^{123}\mathrm{Sn}\), 0.028 MeV for \({}^{125}\mathrm{Sn}\), 0.0047 MeV for \({}^{127}\mathrm{Sn}\), 0.02 MeV for \({}^{128}\mathrm{Sb}\), 1.85 MeV for \({}^{129}\mathrm{Sb}\), 0.11 MeV for \({}^{129}\mathrm{Te}\), 0.18 MeV for \({}^{131}\mathrm{Te}\), and 0.32 MeV for \({}^{134}\mathrm{I}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of kilonova light curves from neutron star mergers, which were previously modeled using only ground-state nuclear properties, by incorporating the effects of nuclear isomers for the first time. The paper builds on the two-component kilonova model for GW170817 developed by Cowperthwaite et al. (2017), Tanaka et al. (2017), and others, and uses the nuclear reaction network from Fujimoto et al. (2007, 2008). The results suggest that future multi-dimensional simulations of neutron star mergers, such as those by Just et al. (2015), Radice et al. (2016), and Sekiguchi et al. (2016), should include isomer effects to properly model the early blue kilonova component, and that more detailed network calculations including excited states (analogous to Iliadis et al. 2011 for \({}^{26}\mathrm{Al}\)) are needed for quantitative predictions.
