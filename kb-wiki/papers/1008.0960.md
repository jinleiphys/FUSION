# Low energy ($p,γ$) reactions in Ni and Cu nuclei using microscopic optical model

> Radiative capture reactions for low energy protons have been theoretically studied for Ni and Cu isotopes using the microscopic optical model. The optical potential has been obtained in the folding model using different microscopic interactions with the nuclear densities from Relativistic Mean Field calculations. The calculated total cross sections as well as the cross sections for individually low lying levels have been compared with measurements involving stable nuclear targets. Rates for the rapid proton capture process have been evaluated for astrophysically important reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a microscopic optical model, built by folding effective interactions (JLM and DDM3Y) with Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) densities, can describe low-energy proton capture cross sections on stable Ni and Cu isotopes, but only after visually normalizing the gamma-ray strength with a free parameter \(G_{norm}\) to match experimental data; the three RMF Lagrangian densities (NL3, TM1, FSU Gold) produce practically identical results.

## Method

The author performed folding-model calculations using two effective interactions (JLM and DDM3Y) with nuclear densities from RMF calculations (NL3, TM1, FSU Gold) to construct microscopic optical potentials. These potentials were then used in the TALYS 1.2 code with full Hauser-Feshbach calculations, including width fluctuation corrections and up to 25 discrete levels of the compound nucleus, to compute total and level-specific cross sections for (p,γ) reactions on stable Ni and Cu isotopes. The gamma-ray strength was visually normalized via a parameter \(G_{norm}\) (tabulated for each target) to match experimental cross sections, and the same method was then applied to predict rates for the rp process on unstable \(^{56}\)Ni and \(^{57}\)Cu.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for stable Ni and Cu isotopes: experimental values range from 506.46 MeV (\(^{58}\)Ni) to 569.21 MeV (\(^{65}\)Cu); theoretical values from FSU Gold range from 508.83 MeV to 569.43 MeV, with differences typically under 2 MeV.
- Charge radii: experimental values range from 3.775 fm (\(^{58}\)Ni) to 3.902 fm (\(^{65}\)Cu); theoretical values from FSU Gold range from 3.751 fm to 3.866 fm.
- Normalization parameter \(G_{norm}\) used for JLM and DDM3Y interactions: values range from 0.55 (for \(^{63}\)Cu) to 0.95 (for \(^{64}\)Ni and \(^{65}\)Cu), with both interactions sharing the same value for most isotopes.
- For the DDM3Y interaction, the real and imaginary parts of the potential were taken as 0.9 times and 0.1 times the DDM3Y potential, respectively.
- The Gaussian range parameters for the JLM interaction were \(t_{real}=1.25\) fm and \(t_{imag}=1.35\) fm.
- The density dependence constants for DDM3Y were \(C=2.07\) and \(\beta=1.624\) fm\(^2\).
- The spin-orbit potential depths were given by \(\lambda_{vso}=130\exp(-0.013E)+40\) and \(\lambda_{wso}=-0.2(E-20)\), with \(E\) in MeV.
- The Gamow window for these nuclei lies in the region 1.1 to 3.3 MeV; cross sections were compared up to 3.5 MeV proton energy.
- The calculation explained cross section values ranging over three orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the use of microscopic optical potentials, previously applied to elastic proton scattering and radioactivity (Gangopadhyay, 2009), to low-energy radiative capture reactions relevant to the astrophysical rp process. It compares results from two effective interactions (JLM and DDM3Y) and three RMF parametrizations (NL3, TM1, FSU Gold), finding that the microscopic approach outperforms default TALYS optical potentials above the neutron evaporation threshold. The calculated astrophysical rates for \(^{56}\)Ni and \(^{57}\)Cu are compared with those from the Hauser-Feshbach code NON-SMOKER (Rauscher and Thielemann, 2000) and shell-model calculations, noting that NON-SMOKER rates are much larger for proton-rich Ni isotopes.
