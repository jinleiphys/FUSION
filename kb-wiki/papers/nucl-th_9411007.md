# Probing Deformed Orbitals with \vec{A}(\vec{e}, e' N)B reactions

> We present results for response functions and asymmetries in the nuclear reactions 37\vec{Ar}(\vec{e},e'n)36Ar and 37\vec{K}(\vec{e},e' p)36Ar at quasifree kinematics. We compare PWIA results obtained using deformed HF wave functions with PWIA and DWIA results obtained assuming a spherical mean field. We show that the complex structure of the deformed orbitals can be probed by coincidence measurements with polarized beam and targets.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the most direct evidence for the existence of deformed orbitals in nuclei can be obtained by using polarized odd-A targets in coincidence electron scattering reactions, specifically by measuring response functions and asymmetries in \(\vec{A}(\vec{e}, e' N)B\) reactions for transitions to discrete states in the residual nucleus, rather than relying on even-even targets where the effect is associated with lack of resolution.

## Method

The authors performed density-dependent Hartree-Fock (DDHF) calculations for the reactions \(^{37}\vec{\mathrm{Ar}}(\vec{e}, e' n)^{36}\mathrm{Ar}\) and \(^{37}\vec{\mathrm{K}}(\vec{e}, e' p)^{36}\mathrm{Ar}\) at quasifree kinematics with momentum transfer \(q = 500\) MeV. They computed scalar and vector momentum distributions from deformed intrinsic wave functions using the rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson, then calculated response functions and asymmetries in the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and compared these with results obtained assuming a spherical mean field, also including some distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations to illustrate that deformation effects are distinct from final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- The dominant \(\ell j\)-wave component of the deformed odd-nucleon wave function is the \(d_{3/2}\) component at approximately 75%, with the \(s_{1/2}\) component at approximately 19%, the \(d_{5/2}\) at approximately 5%, and the \(g_{7/2}\) at approximately 0.7% for both \(^{37}\)K and \(^{37}\)Ar.
- The momentum transfer was fixed at \(q = 500\) MeV, with the energy transfer \(\omega\) set to the quasielastic peak value.
- The maximum value of the outgoing nucleon momentum \(p_N\) (equal to \(q\) at \(p=0\)) decreases at most 1.5% in going from \(p = p_{\mathrm{min}}\) to \(p = p_{\mathrm{max}}\) for \(q = 500\) MeV.
- The theoretical intrinsic quadrupole moment for \(^{36}\)Ar is compared to the experimental value \(Q_0^{\mathrm{exp}} = -38.5 \pm 21.0\) (units not specified in text).
- The theoretical moments of inertia give \(E \sim 2.85\) MeV for the \(5/2^+\) state in \(^{37}\)Ar and \(E \sim 2.86\) MeV for the \(5/2^+\) state in \(^{37}\)K, with the \(7/2^+\) states at \(E \sim 2.6\) MeV in both nuclei.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of deformation effects in \((e, e' p)\) reactions on even-even targets such as \(^{28}\)Si and \(^{146}\)Nd (discussed in references Si28 and Nd146), where the main effects were related to higher level density and fragmentation of spherical orbital strength. The authors show that using polarized odd-A targets allows direct observation of \(\ell\)-wave mixing in deformed orbitals through a single transition to a discrete state, which was not possible with even-even targets. This enables future experimental probes of the complex structure of deformed orbitals using coincidence measurements with polarized beams and targets.
