# A model prediction for polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry

> Polarized flavor asymmetry Delta u-bar/Delta d-bar is investigated in a meson-cloud model. A polarized nucleon splits into a rho meson and a baryon, then the polarized rho meson interacts with the virtual photon. Because of the difference between the longitudinally polarized distributions Delta u-bar and Delta d-bar in rho, the polarized flavor asymmetry is produced in the nucleon. In addition, we show that the g_2 part of rho contributes to the asymmetry especially at medium x with small Q^2.

## Key claim

The meson-cloud model predicts a polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry \(\Delta \bar d > \Delta \bar u\) (i.e., \(\Delta \bar u - \Delta \bar d\) negative), but this result depends on including new contributions from the \(g_2\) part of the \(\rho\) meson, which become significant only at medium \(x\) and small \(Q^2\).

## Method

The polarized nucleon is modeled as splitting into a \(\rho\) meson and a baryon (\(N \rightarrow \rho N\) and \(N \rightarrow \rho \Delta\)), after which the virtual photon interacts with the polarized \(\rho\) meson. The convolution formalism includes both the ordinary longitudinal meson-momentum distributions (\(1L\)) and new terms (\(2L\), \(1T\), \(2T\)) arising from the \(g_2\) structure function of the \(\rho\), approximated via the Wandzura-Wilczek relation. The valence quark distributions in the \(\rho\) are taken as \(\Delta V_\rho = 0.6 \, V_\pi\) based on a lattice QCD estimate, with the pion distributions from the GRS parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at \(Q^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Among the new terms, the \(2L\) contribution is the largest, becoming comparable in magnitude to the \(1L\) term at medium \(x\) (\(x > 0.2\)).
- The distributions from the \(N \rightarrow \rho \Delta\) process are "fairly small" compared to those from \(N \rightarrow \rho N\).
- The overall \(\Delta \bar u - \Delta \bar d\) distributions are "mostly negative," indicating \(\Delta \bar d\) excess over \(\Delta \bar u\).

## Context

This work extends meson-cloud model calculations that successfully explained the unpolarized flavor asymmetry \(\bar u / \bar d\) (Kumano and collaborators, 1990s) to the polarized case \(\Delta \bar u / \Delta \bar d\). It contrasts with earlier model predictions from Pauli-exclusion and chiral-soliton approaches. The results are intended to be tested by future \(W\) production experiments at RHIC and semi-inclusive measurements by the COMPASS collaboration, which will provide experimental constraints on the polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry.
