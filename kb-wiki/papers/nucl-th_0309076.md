# Charm- and Bottom- Baryons: A Variational Approach Using Heavy Quark Symmetry

> We evaluate masses of bottom and charmed baryons using several non-relativistic quark potentials which parameters have been adjusted to the meson spectra. Heavy Quark Symmetry leads to important simplifications of the three body problem, which turns out to be easily solved by a simple variational ansatz. The wave functions obtained can be readily used to compute further observables as mass densities or form factors. The quark-quark potentials explored so far, show an overall good agreement with the experimental masses.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS) simplifies the three-body problem for baryons containing one heavy quark (charm or bottom) and two light quarks, allowing a simple variational ansatz to produce masses in good overall agreement with experimental data, though the agreement is qualified by the use of several non-relativistic quark potentials whose parameters were adjusted to the meson sector rather than the baryon sector.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for heavy-light baryons using a variational ansatz with a Jastrow correlation function for the light-light quark distance, assuming the light quarks have zero relative orbital angular momentum. They employed several non-relativistic quark potentials (Bhaduri et al. 1981 and Silvestre-Brac & Semay 1996, including AL1, AL2, AP1, AP2) with parameters fitted to meson spectra, transformed to the quark-quark sector using the prescription V_qq = (1/2) V_q\bar{q} based on color dependence. Heavy Quark Symmetry was used to fix the quantum numbers of the light degrees of freedom, neglecting corrections of order Λ_QCD/m_c,b.

## Key numbers

- Λ_b mass: variational result 5640 MeV, compared to experimental 5624 ± 9 MeV and lattice 5640 ± 60 MeV
- Λ_c mass: variational result 2291 MeV, compared to experimental 2285 ± 1 MeV and lattice 2270 ± 50 MeV
- Σ_c mass: variational result 2453 MeV, compared to experimental 2452 ± 1 MeV and lattice 2460 ± 80 MeV
- Σ*_c mass: variational result 2542 MeV, compared to experimental 2518 ± 2 MeV and lattice 2440 ± 70 MeV
- Ω_c mass: variational result 2677 MeV, compared to experimental 2698 ± 3 MeV and lattice 2680 ± 70 MeV
- Λ_b mass radius: 0.045 fm^2 from variational calculation, matching the Faddeev result of 0.045 fm^2
- Λ_c mass radius: 0.095 fm^2 from variational calculation, compared to Faddeev result of 0.104 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic constituent quark model approach to heavy baryons using Heavy Quark Symmetry, building on the Faddeev calculations of Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1996) and lattice QCD results from Bowler et al. (1996). The variational method provides simpler and more portable wave functions than the Faddeev approach, enabling future calculations of additional observables such as mass densities, form factors, and semileptonic decays of bottom baryons into charmed baryons.
