# Quark matter revisited with non extensive MIT bag model

> In this work we revisit the MIT bag model to describe quark matter within both the usual Fermi-Dirac and the Tsallis statistics. We verify the effects of the non-additivity of the latter by analysing two different pictures: the first order phase transition of the QCD phase diagram and stellar matter properties. While, the QCD phase diagram is visually affected by the Tsallis statistics, the resulting effects on quark star macroscopic properties are barely noticed.

## Key claim

The Tsallis non-extensive statistics visually modifies the QCD phase diagram's first-order transition line when applied to the MIT bag model, but the resulting effects on quark star macroscopic properties (masses and radii) are barely noticed, with maximum mass increases of less than 1% even for the largest q values considered.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard Fermi-Dirac distribution in the MIT bag model with the Tsallis statistical distribution, characterized by the entropic index q (with q > 1 considered). They computed the equation of state for quark matter with three flavors (up, down, strange) and electrons under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar mass-radius relations for two finite-temperature snapshots of stellar evolution (entropy per baryon S/n_B = 1 with lepton fraction Y_l = 0.3, and S/n_B = 2 with zero neutrino chemical potential).

## Key numbers

- For the QCD phase diagram with B^{1/4} = 220 MeV and q = 1.05, the critical temperature T_c lies within the expected range of 150-200 MeV; with q = 1.14, the (pseudo) transition temperature approaches 155 MeV, the currently most accepted value.
- For stellar matter with S/n_B = 2 and mu_nu = 0, the maximum gravitational mass increases from 1.8344 M_sun (Fermi-Dirac) to 1.8494 M_sun (q = 1.14), a change of about 0.8%.
- For the same snapshot, the radius at maximum mass increases from 10.201 km (Fermi-Dirac) to 10.242 km (q = 1.14).
- For S/n_B = 1 and Y_l = 0.3, the maximum mass increases from 1.8545 M_sun (Fermi-Dirac) to 1.8597 M_sun (q = 1.14).
- At very high densities, the pressure difference between Tsallis and Fermi-Dirac statistics is smaller than 0.025%.
- The energy per particle in the QCD transition line varies between 624 MeV and 640 MeV for Fermi-Dirac statistics with B^{1/4} = 220 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of Tsallis statistics in hadronic matter models, specifically the Boguta-Bodmer (non-linear Walecka) model used by Lavagno (2011) and Menezes (2014) for neutron stars, where q > 1 produced slightly larger maximum masses. The authors use the simpler MIT bag model to isolate the effects of non-extensivity on the kinetic (free Fermi gas) part of the equation of state, finding that the QCD phase diagram is more sensitive to q than in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studied by Rozynek (2015). The results enable future work to separate the effects of non-additivity on interaction terms versus kinetic terms in more sophisticated relativistic models.
