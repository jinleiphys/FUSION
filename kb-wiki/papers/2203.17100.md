# Triangle singularities in the $T^+_{cc}\to D^{*+}D^0\toπ^+D^0D^0$ decay width

> The values of the masses of the particles involved in the decay of $T^+_{cc}\to D^{*+}D^0\toπ^+D^0D^0$ suggest that due to the final state interactions in the transition vertex $T^+_{cc}\to D^{*+}D^0 $ there may be triangle logarithmic singularities. We discuss their possible role and show that the tree approximation for calculating the decay widths $T^+_{cc}\to(D^{*+}D^0+D^{*0}D^+)\toπ^+D^0D^0$, $π^0D^0 D^+$, $γD^0D^+$ is quite sufficient at the current level of measurement accuracy.

## Key claim

The tree approximation for calculating the decay widths of \(T^+_{cc}\to(D^{*+}D^0+D^{*0}D^+)\to\pi^+D^0D^0\), \(\pi^0D^0 D^+\), and \(\gamma D^0D^+\) is sufficient at current measurement accuracy, because although triangle logarithmic singularities exist in the \(F_{D^{*0}D^+}(s_1,s)\) amplitude, their contribution to the decay width is suppressed by the small coupling constant \(g^2_{D^{*+}\pi^+D^0}/(16\pi)\approx 0.00289\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors used a scalar model treating all particles as spinless to calculate the decay width \(\Gamma_{T^+_{cc}\to D^{*+}D^0\to\pi^+D^0D^0}(s_1)\) in the tree approximation via Eq. (1), then introduced triangle-loop corrections via Eqs. (3) and (4) to account for final state interactions in the \(T^+_{cc}\to D^{*+}D^0\) vertex. They evaluated the amplitudes \(F_{D^{*+}D^0}(s_1,s)\) and \(F_{D^{*0}D^+}(s_1,s)\) using the method of 't Hooft and Veltman (1979) and Denner (2007), and compared the loop-corrected width to the tree-level result over the \(T^+_{cc}\) resonance region.

## Key numbers

- The Breit-Wigner mass of \(T^+_{cc}\) relative to the \(D^{*+}D^0\) threshold is \(\delta m_{BW} = -273 \pm 61\) keV, corresponding to a mass of approximately 3875 MeV (LHCb, Aa21a, Aa21b).
- The Breit-Wigner width of \(T^+_{cc}\) is \(\Gamma_{BW} = 410 \pm 165\) keV (statistical uncertainties only).
- The total width of \(D^{*+}\) is \(\Gamma^{tot}_{D^{*+}}(m^2_{D^{*+}}) = 83.4\) keV (PDG 2020), yielding \(g^2_{D^{*+}\pi^+D^0}/(16\pi) \approx 0.00289\) GeV\(^2\).
- The triangle singularity in \(F_{D^{*0}D^+}(s_1,s)\) occurs at \(\sqrt{s_1} \approx 3.87658\) GeV, within the interval \(3.87651\ \text{GeV} < \sqrt{s_1} < 3.92318\ \text{GeV}\) (corresponding to \(0 < \sqrt{s_1} - (m_{D^{*0}} + m_{D^+}) < 46.67\) MeV).
- The loop corrections increase the tree-level width by about 5% at \(\sqrt{s_1} = 3.874\) GeV, 6% at 3.875 GeV, 2% at 3.876 GeV, and 0.6% at 3.877 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the doubly charmed tetraquark \(T^+_{cc}\) discovered by LHCb (Muh21, Pol21, Aa21a, Aa21b) by examining whether triangle singularities, as discussed in the context of final state interactions by Guo (2020) and others, significantly modify the tree-level decay widths used in the LHCb fits. The authors conclude that the tree approximation is adequate, enabling future work to focus on spin effects and more precise measurements of the \(T^+_{cc}\) line shape without needing to incorporate loop corrections at the current experimental precision.
