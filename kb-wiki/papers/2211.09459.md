# Charge diffusion in relativistic resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics

> We study charge diffusion in relativistic resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics. In this theory, charge diffusion is not simply given by the standard Navier-Stokes form of Ohm's law, but by an evolution equation which ensures causality and stability. This, in turn, leads to transient effects in the charge diffusion current, the nature of which depends on the particular values of the electrical conductivity and the charge-diffusion relaxation time. The ensuing equations of motion are of so-called stiff character, which requires special care when solving them numerically. To this end, we specifically develop an implicit-explicit Runge-Kutta method for solving relativistic resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics and subject it to various tests. We then study the system's evolution in a simplified 1+1-dimensional scenario for a heavy-ion collision, where matter and electromagnetic fields are assumed to be transversely homogeneous, and investigate the cases of an initially non-expanding fluid and a fluid initially expanding according to a Bjorken scaling flow. In the latter case, the scale invariance is broken by the ensuing self-consistent dynamics of matter and electromagnetic fields. However, the breaking becomes quantitatively important only if the electromagnetic fields are sufficiently strong. The breaking of scale invariance is larger for smaller values of the conductivity. Aspects of entropy production from charge diffusion currents and stability are also discussed.

## Key claim

Charge diffusion in relativistic resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics is governed by an evolution equation rather than the standard Navier-Stokes form of Ohm's law, and the resulting transient effects depend on the damping ratio determined by the electrical conductivity and charge-diffusion relaxation time; the breaking of Bjorken scale invariance becomes quantitatively important only when electromagnetic fields are sufficiently strong and is larger for smaller conductivity values.

## Method

The authors developed an implicit-explicit Runge-Kutta (IMEX) method to solve the stiff system of equations comprising energy-momentum conservation, charge conservation, Maxwell's equations, and the evolution equation for the charge diffusion current in resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics. They applied this method to a simplified 1+1-dimensional scenario for heavy-ion collisions, assuming transverse homogeneity, and studied two cases: an initially non-expanding fluid and a fluid initially expanding according to Bjorken scaling flow. The electromagnetic fields were initialized from boosted electrostatic potentials of point-like charges representing the colliding nuclei, and the electrical conductivity and relaxation time were treated as free parameters.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of relativistic resistive second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics developed by Denicol (2018, 2019) and Panda (2020, 2021) to numerical simulations, representing the first such simulations of this theory. It is motivated by transport simulations by Wang (2021), Yan (2021), and Grayson (2022) showing that charge diffusion currents require finite time to reach the Navier-Stokes value of Ohm's law. The study enables future investigations of transient charge diffusion effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the chiral magnetic effect, and provides a numerical method applicable to more realistic 3+1-dimensional scenarios.
