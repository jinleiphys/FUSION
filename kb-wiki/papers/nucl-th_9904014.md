# Electromagnetic form factors of baryons in an algebraic approach

> We present a simultaneous analysis of elastic and transition form factors of the nucleon. The calculations are performed in the framework of an algebraic model of baryons. Effects of meson cloud couplings are considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simultaneous analysis of all four elastic nucleon form factors and transition form factors using a collective algebraic model of baryons, where deviations from simple dipole scaling at low momentum transfer are attributed to meson cloud couplings, but the model's success is qualified by the fact that the meson cloud parameters are fitted phenomenologically and the neutron form factors remain poorly constrained by data.

## Method

The authors employ a collective algebraic model in which baryon resonances are interpreted as vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate top, with electromagnetic form factors obtained by folding charge and magnetization distributions over the baryon volume in the Breit frame. The elastic form factors are expressed in terms of isoscalar and isovector components, and meson cloud effects are incorporated by coupling to vector mesons (omega, phi, rho) with a parametrized form that includes a finite width for the rho meson. The model parameters (meson coupling coefficients, dipole scale parameter gamma, and Lambda values) are determined by a simultaneous fit to all four nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the proton and neutron charge radii.

## Key numbers

- The neutron charge distribution shows a change in sign at 0.65 fm (from Fourier transform of the electric form factor).
- For the N(1535)S11 resonance, the ratio of photocouplings A^n_{1/2}/A^p_{1/2} = -0.84 ± 0.15 from experiment, compared to the collective algebraic model value of -0.81.
- For the N(1520)D13 resonance, the ratio A^p_{3/2}/A^p_{1/2} = -2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.4 from experiment, compared to the collective algebraic model value of -2.53.
- Under form factor scaling and for large Q^2, the ratio Q^2 F_2^p / F_1^p approaches 1.26 (GeV/c)^2.
- The elastic form factors are studied for the space-like region 0 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 10 (GeV/c)^2.
- The rho meson width is taken as Gamma_rho = 151 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic model of baryons previously introduced by Bijker and Leviatan (1998) to include meson cloud couplings via vector meson dominance, building on the approach of Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) and the hybrid model of Gari and Krumpelmann (1985). The simultaneous description of elastic and transition form factors enables comparison with new experimental data from facilities like ELSA, MAMI, and Jefferson Laboratory, particularly for neutron form factors and the helicity amplitudes of the N(1520)D13 and N(1535)S11 resonances from eta-photoproduction experiments.
