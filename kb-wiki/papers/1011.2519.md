# Non-equilibrium physics at a holographic chiral phase transition

> The D3/D7 system holographically describes an N=2 gauge theory which spontaneously breaks a chiral symmetry by the formation of a quark condensate in the presence of a magnetic field. At finite temperature it displays a first order phase transition. We study out of equilibrium dynamics associated with this transition by placing probe D7 branes in a geometry describing a boost-invariant expanding or contracting plasma. We use an adiabatic approximation to track the evolution of the quark condensate in a heated system and reproduce the phase structure expected from equilibrium dynamics. We then study solutions of the full partial differential equation that describes the evolution of out of equilibrium configurations to provide a complete description of the phase transition including describing aspects of bubble formation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the equilibrium phase structure of a first-order chiral phase transition in a holographic gauge theory can be reproduced by an adiabatic approximation in a time-dependent expanding or contracting plasma, but that the full partial differential equation solutions are necessary to describe the out-of-equilibrium dynamics such as bubble formation when the meta-stable vacuum is lost.

## Method

The authors place probe D7 branes with a world-volume magnetic field into the boost-invariant expanding or contracting plasma geometry of Janik and Peschanski (2005), which describes a time-dependent temperature via a moving black hole horizon. They first use an adiabatic approximation by power-expanding the D7 embedding in inverse powers of proper time, reducing the partial differential equation to ordinary differential equations. They then solve the full non-linear PDE numerically using Mathematica to study the time evolution of the quark condensate and the dynamics of the first-order phase transition, including the roll from super-heated chiral symmetry breaking configurations to the symmetric phase.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the first-order chiral phase transition in equilibrium occurs at \(\tilde{w}_H = 0.2516\) (dimensionless horizon size, with magnetic field \(B\) setting the scale).
* The quark condensate in the late-time expansion for the expanding plasma scales as \(\sim -\tau^{-8/3}\), with a viscous correction of \(\sim +\tau^{-10/3}\).
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi) + \mathcal{O}(\tau^{2/3})\) at large proper time.
* The temperature of the expanding plasma as a function of proper time is \(T(\tau) = (4\epsilon_0/3)^{1/4}/(\pi \tau^{1/3}) \left(1 - \eta_0/(2\epsilon_0^{1/4}\tau^{2/3})\right)\), where \(\epsilon_0\) is a free parameter related to energy density and \(\eta_0 = 1/(2^{1/2}3^{3/4})\).

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium analysis of the D3/D7 system with a magnetic field by Johnson (2008) and Evans (2009), which showed a first-order chiral phase transition at finite temperature, by introducing the time-dependent boost-invariant plasma geometry of Janik and Peschanski (2005). It enables the study of non-equilibrium aspects of the chiral phase transition in strongly coupled gauge theories, including bubble nucleation and the dynamics of the quark condensate during heating or cooling, which is relevant for understanding heavy ion collisions and early universe cosmology.
