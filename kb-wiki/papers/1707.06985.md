# DPS in CGC: Double Quark Production and Effects of Quantum Statistics

> We consider forward inclusive production of two quarks in the high energy p-A collisions in the CGC formalism. We demonstrate that the production cross-section is determined by the convolution of the proton generalized double transverse momentum-dependent distribution (2GTMD) functions with two independent eikonal scattering amplitudes: the product of two dipoles and a quadrupole. We explicitly demonstrate that the quadrupole amplitude term accounts for all the (initial and final state) effects of quantum statistics for identical fermions, and the correlations due to these effects. We also demonstrate that the effects due to quantum statistics (entirely encoded in the quadrupole) are parametrically leading contributions to the correlated particle production at large $N_c$. For non-identical quarks the quadrupole term also leads to correlated production which has characteristics similar to the HBT effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quadrupole amplitude term in the double inclusive quark production cross-section accounts for all effects of quantum statistics for identical fermions, and that these quantum statistics effects are parametrically leading contributions to correlated particle production at large $N_c$, contrary to the dipole term which is suppressed by a factor of $1/N_c$.

## Method

The authors derived the double inclusive quark production cross-section in forward p-A collisions using the Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) hybrid formalism, expressing it in terms of the proton generalized double transverse momentum-dependent distribution (2GTMD) functions convoluted with eikonal scattering amplitudes. They performed color algebra to separate the cross-section into contributions from the product of two dipoles and a quadrupole, then simplified the general expressions by assuming dominance of nucleon intermediate states, which allowed factorization of the 2GTMD into products of single quark GTMDs. The analysis was carried out separately for non-identical (ud) and identical (uu) quarks.

## Key numbers

* The quadrupole term accounts for quantum statistics effects that are parametrically leading at large $N_c$, while the dipole term is suppressed by a factor of $1/N_c$ relative to the quadrupole contribution.
* For non-identical quarks, the exchange term in the cross-section is suppressed by a single power of $1/N_c$ relative to the independent scattering term.
* The quadrupole correlator in the weak field Gaussian model factorizes into two contributions: $\mu^2(\x_1-\x_2)\mu^2(\x_3-\x_4)$ and $\mu^2(\x_1-\x_4)\mu^2(\x_2-\x_3)$, where the first corresponds to Pauli blocking and the second to HBT correlations.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum statistics effects in the CGC framework, following earlier work on Bose enhancement of gluons (Kovner and Rezaeian, 2016) and Pauli blocking for quark production at mid-rapidity (Kovner and Rezaeian, 2016). It also builds on the authors' previous study of the HBT effect for photon emission in forward p-A collisions (Kovner and Rezaeian, 2017). The paper explicitly notes that some recent calculations of particle correlations (Dusling and Venugopalan, 2013) neglect the quadrupole contribution and thus miss the leading $N_c$ contribution to correlated particle production. The results enable quantitative study of the balance between quantum statistics effects and other correlation mechanisms in multiple particle production.
