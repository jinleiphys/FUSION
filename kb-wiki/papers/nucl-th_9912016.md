# Properties of β-stable neutron star matter with hyperons

> We present results from many-body calculations for β-stable neutron star matter with nucleonic and hyperonic degrees of freedom, employing the most recent parametrizations of the baryon-baryon interaction of the Nijmegen group. It is found that the only strange baryons emerging in β-stable matter up to total baryonic densities of 1.2 fm^{-3} are Σ^- and Λ. The corresponding equations of state are thence used to compute properties of neutron stars such as the masses, moments of inertia and radii. We also study the possibility of forming a hyperon superfluid and discuss its implications for neutron stars.

# Properties of β-stable neutron star matter with hyperons

## Key claim

Using the most recent Nijmegen baryon-baryon interaction parametrizations (NSC97e), only Σ⁻ and Λ hyperons appear in β-stable neutron star matter up to total baryonic densities of 1.2 fm⁻³, but this result depends critically on the stiffness of the nucleonic equation of state and the attractiveness of hyperon-hyperon interactions; with the softer BHF nucleonic EoS, Λ does not appear at all within the density range considered.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body calculations for β-stable neutron star matter including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰), using the Nijmegen NSC97e baryon-baryon potential for all strangeness sectors. For the nucleonic part, two approaches were compared: a pure BHF calculation and a calculation where the nucleonic self-energies were replaced with results from Akmal et al. (1998) (APR98) which includes the Argonne V₁₈ interaction with relativistic boost corrections and a fitted three-body force. The G-matrix equations and single-particle potentials were solved self-consistently, and the β-equilibrium conditions were satisfied by interpolating between different nucleon and hyperon fractions at each total baryonic density.

## Key numbers

- Σ⁻ appears at n = 0.27 fm⁻³ for the APR98 equation of state and at n = 0.35 fm⁻³ for the BHF equation of state (nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Λ appears at n = 0.67 fm⁻³ for the APR98 equation of state; Λ does not appear in the BHF calculation up to 1.2 fm⁻³
- The mass difference m(Σ⁻) − m(Λ) = 81.76 MeV, which is smaller than the electron chemical potential, explaining why Σ⁻ appears at lower densities than Λ
- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons: M ∼ 1.3 M⊙ without rotational corrections, M ∼ 1.4 M⊙ with rotational corrections
- Maximum neutron star mass without hyperons (pure nucleonic APR98): M ∼ 1.9 M⊙ without rotational corrections, M ∼ 2.1 M⊙ with rotational corrections
- The reduction in maximum mass due to hyperons is ΔM ∼ 0.6−0.7 M⊙
- Matter becomes deleptonized at n = 0.85 fm⁻³ for the APR98 case
- Positrons appear at n = 1.18 fm⁻³ in the APR98 calculation

## Context

This work extends earlier many-body calculations by Schulze et al. (1998) which only included hyperon-nucleon interactions and could not determine Λ appearance thresholds, by employing the full baryon-baryon interaction of Stoks and Rijken (1999) that also accounts for strangeness S < −1. The results differ from relativistic mean field calculations by Prakash et al. (1997), Pons et al. (1995), and Muto and Tatsumi (1996), which predict all hyperon species appearing at the densities considered. The finding that Σ⁻ forms a superfluid while Λ does not (due to repulsive Λ-Λ interactions in the ¹S₀ channel) contrasts with Balberg and Barnea (1998) who used parametrized effective interactions and found Λ superfluidity. The maximum masses obtained with hyperons (∼1.3−1.4 M⊙) are comparable to those reported by Balberg et al. (1999). The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling, since Σ⁻ superfluidity would suppress the direct Urca process Σ⁻ → n + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ, and provide lower bounds for neutron star masses that can be tested against observations of binary pulsars and kilohertz QPO sources.
