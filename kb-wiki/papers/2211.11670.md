# QCD Equation of State of Dense Nuclear Matter from a Bayesian Analysis of Heavy-Ion Collision Data

> Bayesian methods are used to constrain the density dependence of the QCD Equation of State (EoS) for dense nuclear matter using the data of mean transverse kinetic energy and elliptic flow of protons from heavy ion collisions (HIC), in the beam energy range $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2-10 GeV$. The analysis yields tight constraints on the density dependent EoS up to 4 times the nuclear saturation density. The extracted EoS yields good agreement with other observables measured in HIC experiments and constraints from astrophysical observations both of which were not used in the inference. The sensitivity of inference to the choice of observables is also discussed.

## Key claim

Bayesian inference using proton mean transverse kinetic energy and elliptic flow data from heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2-10\) GeV constrains the QCD equation of state up to four times nuclear saturation density, but the shape of the posterior beyond \(3n_0\) depends on the choice of observables used, revealing a tension between the two data sets at collision energies around 4 GeV.

## Method

The authors embed a density-dependent potential energy parameterized as a seventh-degree polynomial for densities above \(2n_0\) into the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model. Gaussian Process emulators are trained on UrQMD simulations to act as fast surrogates, then Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling is performed to construct the posterior distribution of the polynomial coefficients using experimental data on proton elliptic flow \(v_2\) and mean transverse kinetic energy \(\langle m_T \rangle - m_0\) from Au-Au collisions. The extracted equation of state is cross-checked against directed flow data that were not used in the inference.

## Key numbers

- The beam energy range analyzed is \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2-10\) GeV.
- The equation of state is constrained up to \(4n_0\) (four times nuclear saturation density), with larger uncertainties from \(4n_0\) to \(6n_0\).
- The potential energy polynomial is fixed for densities up to \(2n_0\) using the Chiral Mean Field model; the constant \(h = -22.07\) MeV ensures continuity at \(2n_0\).
- Elliptic flow data are used at 10 beam energies: \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=\) 2.24, 2.32, 2.4, 2.42, 2.51, 3.0, 3.32, 3.84, 4.23, and 4.72 GeV.
- Mean transverse kinetic energy data are used at 5 beam energies: \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=\) 3.83, 4.29, 6.27, 7.7, and 8.86 GeV.
- The speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) at \(T=0\) from the 15-data-point inference shows a broad peak structure and agrees with constraints from binary neutron star merger observations (Altiparmak 2022), while the 13-data-point inference shows a drastic drop consistent with a strong first-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian analyses of the QCD equation of state from heavy-ion collisions, such as Pratt (2015) which constrained the EoS at vanishing baryon density using LHC and RHIC flow data, and Oliinychenko (2022) which used a piecewise constant speed-of-sound parameterization. The method complements astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations and binary neutron star merger signals (Altiparmak 2022), and the extracted speed of sound is compatible with functional renormalization group analyses (Leonhardt 2019) and conformality studies (Fujimoto 2022). The results enable future combined analyses incorporating higher-order flow coefficients and directed flow, and will benefit from upcoming data from the STAR-FXT program at RHIC, the CBM experiment at FAIR, and experiments at HIAF and NICA.
