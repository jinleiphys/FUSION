# Dineutron formation and breaking in $^8$He

> We discuss the correlation of the spin-singlet two-neutron pair, the dineutron correlation, in the ground and excited $0^+$ states of $^8$He by using a method to describe the dineutron correlation in neutron-rich nuclei, the dineutron condensate wave function. The shell-model configuration and the dineutron configuration compete with each other in the ground state of $^8$He. We compare the ground state structure of $^8$He with that of $^6$He and suggest that the dineutron correlation is weaker in the ground state of $^8$He than $^6$He. We also suggest the possibility of the dineutron condensation in an excited $0^+$ state of $^8$He. In addition, we investigate the formation mechanism of the dineutron condensation in $^8$He by using a newly developed framework, $α$ and dineutron condensate wave function, and conclude that the attraction from the core is essential for the formation of the dineutron condensation.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the dineutron correlation is weaker in the ground state of \(^8\)He than in \(^6\)He, and propose the possibility of dineutron condensation in an excited \(0^+\) state of \(^8\)He, with the attraction from the \(\alpha\) core being essential for the formation of that condensation.

## Method

The authors superposed extended \(^6\)He\(+2n\) cluster wave functions and \(^8\)He dineutron condensate (DC) wave functions to describe the \(0^+\) states of \(^8\)He. The extended cluster wave functions used complex Gaussian center parameters to account for dissociation of a \(2n\) cluster due to spin-orbit interaction, while the DC wave functions described one or two dineutrons in \(S\)-wave orbits around an \(\alpha\) core. The wave functions were projected onto \(0^+\) states and the coefficients were determined by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian with Volkov No.2 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force, using two parameter sets (m55 and m59) to test sensitivity to the \(\alpha\)-\(n\) and \(n\)-\(n\) interactions.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy of \(^8\)He: \(-31.13\) MeV (m55 set) and \(-30.98\) MeV (m59 set), measured from the \(\alpha+4n\) threshold at \(-27.61\) MeV (both sets); experimental value is \(-31.40\) MeV.
* Ground state energy of \(^6\)He: \(-27.95\) MeV (m55) and \(-28.62\) MeV (m59); experimental value is \(-29.27\) MeV.
* Matter rms radius of \(^8\)He(\(0^+_1\)): 2.37 fm (m55) and 2.49 fm (m59); experimental range is 2.49–2.52 fm.
* Neutron rms radius of \(^8\)He(\(0^+_1\)): 2.51 fm (m55) and 2.65 fm (m59); experimental range is 2.64–2.69 fm.
* Matter rms radius of \(^8\)He(\(0^+_2\)): 4.85 fm (m55) and 4.67 fm (m59).
* Neutron \(0p_{3/2}\) component in \(^8\)He(\(0^+_1\)): 73% (m55) and 55% (m59).
* Neutron \(0p_{3/2}\) component in \(^8\)He(\(0^+_2\)): 4% (m55) and 3% (m59).
* Neutron squared spin expectation \(\langle S_n^2 \rangle\) in \(^8\)He(\(0^+_1\)): 0.63 (m55) and 0.41 (m59); in \(^8\)He(\(0^+_2\)): 0.14 (m55) and 0.09 (m59).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dineutron correlation in He and H isotopes by Kanada-En'yo (2007), Itagaki (2008), and Aoyama (2009), which suggested the possibility of dineutron condensate states but did not clarify the formation mechanism. The present analysis uses a newly developed \(\alpha\) and dineutron condensate wave function to show that the core attraction is essential for dineutron condensation, and compares the ground state structure of \(^8\)He with that of \(^6\)He to quantify the weaker dineutron correlation in the heavier isotope. This enables future investigation of how dineutron condensation might appear in other neutron-rich nuclei and how cluster formation mechanisms relate to the BCS-BEC crossover.
