# Quark condensate in nuclear matter based on Nuclear Schwinger-Dyson formalism

> The effects of higher order corrections of ring diagrams for the quark condensate are studied by using the bare vertex Nuclear Schwinger Dyson formalism based on $σ$-$ω$ model. At the high density the quark condensate is reduced by the higher order contribution of ring diagrams more than the mean field theory or the Hartree-Fock .

## Key claim
The quark condensate in nuclear matter is reduced by higher-order ring diagram corrections beyond mean field theory (MFT) or Hartree-Fock (HF), but only at high densities; at normal nuclear density the reduction is about 35% in all three methods, and the condensate actually increases around 1.5 times normal density, contrary to expectations from chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method
The authors use the bare vertex Nuclear Schwinger-Dyson (BNSD) formalism based on the σ-ω (Walecka) model, solving the Dyson equations for the nucleon propagator and meson full propagators self-consistently. The energy density is decomposed into baryon, classical, and quantum parts, with the quantum parts including ring diagram corrections from particle-hole excitations. The quark condensate is then obtained via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, relating the shift in condensate to derivatives of the energy per nucleon with respect to the nucleon and meson masses.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩₀ ≈ -(225 ± 25 MeV)³ (from QCD sum-rule approach)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σπN = (45 ± 7) MeV (from Gasser et al.)
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- At normal density, the quark condensate is reduced by about 35% compared to vacuum value in BNSD, MFT, and HF
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 138 MeV; pion decay constant: fπ ≈ 93 MeV

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of the quark condensate in nuclear matter using MFT, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Cohen), and relativistic Brueckner Hartree-Fock (Li; Brockmann) by incorporating higher-order ring diagrams via the BNSD formalism. The results show that while all methods give similar condensate reduction at normal density, the BNSD predicts greater reduction at high densities due to ring diagram contributions. The finding that the condensate increases around 1.5ρ₀ contradicts expectations from chiral symmetry restoration and points to the need for including additional mesons (π, η, δ, ρ) or more sophisticated treatments.
