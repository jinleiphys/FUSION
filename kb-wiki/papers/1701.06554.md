# Rescattering effects in the hadronic-light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon

> We present a first model-independent calculation of $ππ$ intermediate states in the hadronic-light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$ that goes beyond the scalar QED pion loop. To this end we combine a recently developed dispersive description of the HLbL tensor with a partial-wave expansion and demonstrate that the known scalar-QED result is recovered after partial-wave resummation. Using dispersive fits to high-statistics data for the pion vector form factor, we provide an evaluation of the full pion box, $a_μ^{π\text{-box}}=-15.9(2)\times 10^{-11}$. We then construct suitable input for the $γ^*γ^*\toππ$ helicity partial waves based on a pion-pole left-hand cut and show that for the dominant charged-pion contribution this representation is consistent with the two-loop chiral prediction and the COMPASS measurement for the pion polarizability. This allows us to reliably estimate $S$-wave rescattering effects to the full pion box and leads to our final estimate for the sum of these two contributions: $a_μ^{π\text{-box}} + a_{μ,J=0}^{ππ,π\text{-pole LHC}}=-24(1)\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first model-independent calculation of two-pion intermediate states in the hadronic-light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment that goes beyond the scalar QED pion loop, but the result is limited to S-wave rescattering effects and relies on a pion-pole left-hand cut representation for the gamma*gamma* -> pi pi amplitudes, with the final estimate carrying an uncertainty of 1 x 10^{-11}.

## Method

The authors combine a dispersive description of the HLbL tensor with a partial-wave expansion, using a Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach decomposition into scalar functions free of kinematic singularities. They evaluate the pion-box contribution using dispersive fits to high-statistics data for the pion vector form factor, then construct input for the gamma*gamma* -> pi pi helicity partial waves based on a pion-pole left-hand cut and solve the unitarity relation using Muskhelishvili-Omnes methods with pi pi phase shifts from the modified inverse-amplitude method.

## Key numbers

- Full pion box contribution: a_mu^{pi-box} = -15.9(2) x 10^{-11}, evaluated using dispersive fits to pion vector form factor data
- S-wave rescattering correction: a_{mu,J=0}^{pi pi, pi-pole LHC} = -8(1) x 10^{-11}, dominated by isospin-0 contributions of about -9 x 10^{-11}
- Combined result for pion box plus S-wave rescattering: a_mu^{pi-box} + a_{mu,J=0}^{pi pi, pi-pole LHC} = -24(1) x 10^{-11}
- Charged pion dipole polarizability from the unitarized pion-pole left-hand cut: (alpha_1 - beta_1)^{pi^pm, pi-pole LHC} = (5.4 to 5.8) x 10^{-4} fm^3, consistent with the chiral two-loop prediction of 5.7(1.0) x 10^{-4} fm^3 and the COMPASS measurement of 4.0(1.2)_stat(1.4)_syst x 10^{-4} fm^3

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach to HLbL scattering developed by the same authors in a series of papers (Hoferichter 2013, Colangelo 2014-2015) by providing the first numerical evaluation of two-pion intermediate states beyond the scalar QED pion loop. The calculation settles the role of the pion polarizability, which had been suspected to produce sizable corrections in Engel (2013), and shows that the related rescattering corrections are of reasonable size, in agreement with a model approach by Bijnens (2016). The framework enables future extensions to higher partial waves, improved left-hand cuts in the gamma*gamma* -> pi pi subamplitudes, and higher-mass intermediate states, all necessary for a model-independent evaluation of the complete HLbL contribution to (g-2)_mu.
