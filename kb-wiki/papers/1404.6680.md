# Parton Physics from Large-Momentum Effective Field Theory

> Parton physics, when formulated as light-front correlations, are difficult to study non-perturbatively, despite the promise of light-front quantization. Recently an alternative approach to partons have been proposed by re-visiting original Feynman picture of a hadron moving at asymptotically large momentum. Here I formulate the approach in the language of an effective field theory for a large hadron momentum $P$ in lattice QCD, LaMET for short. I show that using this new effective theory, parton properties, including light-front parton wave functions, can be extracted from lattice observables in a systematic expansion of $1/P$, much like that the parton distributions can be extracted from the hard scattering data at momentum scales of a few GeV.

# Parton Physics from Large-Momentum Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

Parton properties, including light-front parton wave functions, can be extracted from lattice QCD observables through a systematic expansion in powers of \(1/P^z\), provided the hadron momentum \(P^z\) is large but finite (of order a few GeV), rather than requiring the infinite-momentum limit directly.

## Method

The author formulates Large-Momentum Effective Field Theory (LaMET) by analogy with Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET): a lattice-observable \(F(P^z/\Lambda)\) depending on large hadron momentum \(P^z\) is expanded as \(F = Z(P^z/\Lambda, \Lambda/\mu) f(\mu) + \mathcal{O}(1/(P^z)^2)\), where \(f(\mu)\) is the light-front parton observable defined at \(P^z \to \infty\). The matching coefficient \(Z\) is perturbatively calculable and captures the difference in ultraviolet physics between the finite-momentum lattice calculation and the infinite-momentum light-front limit. The approach is demonstrated for parton densities and extended to light-front wave functions by constructing Euclidean quasi-operators (e.g., replacing \(\gamma^+\) with \(\gamma^z\) and using equal-time correlations along the \(z\)-direction) that reduce to the light-cone operators under an infinite Lorentz boost.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is \(1/P^z\), with power corrections of order \(\mathcal{O}(1/(P^z)^2)\).
- The smallest parton momentum fraction \(x\) accessible is \(x \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}/P^z\); for \(x \sim 10^{-4}\), the hadron momentum must be around 3 TeV.
- For valence partons, the longitudinal correlation length scales as \(1/xP^z\), inversely proportional to the Lorentz factor \(\gamma\).
- The charm quark mass of 1.5 GeV is cited as an example where a heavy-quark treatment already works well; the bottom quark mass of 4.2 GeV is given as a case where HQET works fairly well.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposals (Ji 2013) for calculating parton distributions from Euclidean lattice QCD by placing them in a systematic effective field theory framework, analogous to HQET (Manohar 2000). It contrasts with the light-front quantization approach (Dirac 1949; Brodsky 1997), which has proven difficult for non-perturbative calculations in 3+1 dimensions, and with previous lattice methods that could only access the first few moments of parton distributions via operator product expansion (Negele). LaMET enables lattice QCD to serve as a complementary tool to experimental data (Gao 2013) for extracting parton physics, with the same parton observables extractable from different lattice operators forming a universality class.
