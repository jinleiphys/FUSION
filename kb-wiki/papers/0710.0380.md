# Fluctuations of particle ratios as a freeze-out probe

> We explain how event-by-event fluctuations of particle ratios can constrain and falsify the statistical model of particle production in heavy ion collisions, using $K/π$ fluctuations as an example. We define an observable capable of determining which statistical model, if any, governs freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We calculate this observable for $K/π$ fluctuations, and show that it should be the same for RHIC and LHC energies, as well as independent of centrality, if the Grand-Canonical statistical model is an appropriate description and chemical equilibrium applies. We describe what happens in case of deviations from this scenario, such as light quark chemical non-equilibrium, strange quark over-saturation and local conservation (canonical ensemble) for strange quarks. We also introduce a similar observable capable, together with the published $K^*/K$ measurement, of ascertaining if an interacting hadron gas phase governs the system between thermal and chemical freeze-out, and of ascertaining its duration and impact on hadronic chemistry

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations of particle ratios, specifically the scaled observable \(\Psi^{N_1}_{N_1/N_2} = \langle N_1 \rangle \nu_{dyn}^{N_1/N_2}\), should be strictly independent of centrality and multiplicity and identical between RHIC and LHC energies if the Grand-Canonical statistical model with chemical equilibrium is the correct description of freeze-out; deviations from this flat scaling would falsify the equilibrium statistical model or indicate alternative ensembles such as canonical or micro-canonical.

## Method

The author defines the dynamical fluctuation \(\nu_{dyn}^{N_1/N_2}\) as the difference between the measured event-by-event variance of a particle ratio and the variance from mixed events, which removes acceptance-cut effects. The scaled observable \(\Psi^{N_1}_{N_1/N_2} = \langle N_1 \rangle \nu_{dyn}^{N_1/N_2}\) is then calculated using the statistical hadronization code SHAREv2.X, which incorporates resonance decays and chemical (non)equilibrium parameters such as light-quark and strange-quark phase-space occupancies \(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\). The calculation is performed for \(K^-/\pi^-\) and \(K^+/K^-\) ratios at RHIC and LHC energies under equilibrium and non-equilibrium scenarios, and a second observable \(\Delta \Psi^{\pi^-}_{K/\pi}\) is introduced to compare with direct \(K^*/K\) measurements to probe the duration of the interacting hadron gas phase between chemical and thermal freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- For Cu-Cu 200 GeV collisions, the scaling relation predicts \(\left. \Psi^{\pi}_{K^-/\pi^-} \right|_{Cu-Cu} \simeq \left. \Psi^{\pi}_{K^-/\pi^-} \right|_{Au-Au}\) and \(\left. \nu^{dyn}_{K^-/\pi^-} \right|_{Cu-Cu} \simeq \frac{\left.\langle N_\pi \rangle \right|_{Cu-Cu}}{\left.\langle N_\pi \rangle \right|_{Au-Au}} \left. \nu^{dyn}_{K^-/\pi^-} \right|_{Au-Au}\).
- For \(\Psi^{\pi^-}_{K^+/K^-}\) in the canonical ensemble, the value should be lower by a factor of two compared to the Grand-Canonical expectation.
- Corrections from higher-lying resonances and finite baryochemical potential to \(\Delta \Psi^{\pi^-}_{K/\pi}\) make up a 10% effect, less than the expected experimental error.
- In the strong reinteraction limit, the observed \(K^*/K^-\) ratio is related to \(\Delta \Psi^{\pi^-}_{K/\pi}\) by \(\left. \frac{K^*}{K^-} \right|_{observed} \sim \Delta \Psi^{\pi^-}_{K/\pi} \exp \left[ \frac{\Delta m}{T_{therm}} - \frac{\Delta m}{T_{chem}} \right]\), with chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{chem} \sim 170\) MeV and thermal freeze-out temperature \(T_{therm} \sim 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model tests of Becattini (2006) and the fluctuation studies of Jeon and Koch (2004) by proposing a specific scaled observable that is robust against volume fluctuations and acceptance cuts. It directly addresses the ambiguity in interpreting resonance yields such as \(K^*(892)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\) measured by the STAR collaboration (Sevil, 2006), where chemical non-equilibrium fits can reproduce the abundances without requiring an interacting hadron gas phase. The proposed \(\Delta \Psi^{\pi^-}_{K/\pi}\) observable, combined with published \(K^*/K\) measurements, enables a direct determination of whether a reinteracting hadron gas phase exists between chemical and thermal freeze-out and, if so, its duration and impact on hadronic chemistry.
