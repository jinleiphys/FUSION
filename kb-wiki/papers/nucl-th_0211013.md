# Quenching of Weak Interactions in Nucleon Matter

> We have calculated the one-body Fermi and Gamow-Teller charge-current, and vector and axial-vector neutral-current nuclear matrix elements in nucleon matter at densities of 0.08, 0.16 and 0.24 fm$^{-3}$ and proton fractions ranging from 0.2 to 0.5. The correlated states for nucleon matter are obtained by operating on Fermi-gas states by a symmetrized product of pair correlation operators determined from variational calculations with the Argonne v18 and Urbana IX two- and three-nucleon interactions. The squares of the charge current matrix elements are found to be quenched by 20 to 25 % by the short-range correlations in nucleon matter. Most of the quenching is due to spin-isospin correlations induced by the pion exchange interactions which change the isospins and spins of the nucleons. A large part of it can be related to the probability for a spin up proton quasi-particle to be a bare spin up/down proton/neutron. We also calculate the matrix elements of the nuclear Hamiltonian in the same correlated basis. These provide relatively mild effective interactions which give the variational energies in the Hartree-Fock approximation. The calculated two-nucleon effective interaction describes the spin-isospin susceptibilities of nuclear and neutron matter fairly accurately. However $\geq$ 3-body terms are necessary to reproduce the compressibility. All presented results use the simple 2-body cluster approximation to calculate the correlated basis matrix elements.

## Key claim

The squares of the one-body Fermi and Gamow-Teller charge-current matrix elements in nucleon matter are quenched by 20 to 25% due to short-range correlations, with most of the quenching arising from spin-isospin correlations induced by pion exchange interactions; however, these results use only the simple two-body cluster approximation and neglect contributions from clusters with three or more nucleons, so the present findings have only qualitative significance.

## Method

The authors calculated correlated basis (CB) matrix elements for one-body Fermi, Gamow-Teller, vector neutral-current, and axial-vector neutral-current operators in nucleon matter at three densities (0.08, 0.16, and 0.24 fm⁻³) and proton fractions from 0.2 to 0.5. Correlated states were constructed by applying a symmetrized product of pair correlation operators, obtained from variational calculations using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, to Fermi-gas states. All matrix elements were evaluated in the two-body cluster approximation, with the cluster expansion carried out to second order in the correlation operators (F-1).

## Key numbers

- The squares of the Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements in correlated basis states are approximately 0.8 and 0.75 times those in Fermi-gas states at small momentum transfer q, corresponding to a quenching of 20 to 25%
- Calculations were performed at three densities: 0.08, 0.16, and 0.24 fm⁻³
- Proton fractions considered: 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5
- The variation of the Fermi quenching factor η_F with proton fraction is less than 3% at all densities calculated
- For small q (q ≲ 0.5 fm⁻¹), the Fermi quenching factor can be represented by η_F = η_F(q→0) + α_F(ρ) q²
- The dominant term F.2.d.a gives |1 + F.2.d.a|² = 0.7 for the Fermi matrix element at saturation density
- The probability for the active quasi-nucleon to be a neutron in the initial state (direct part) is P_I(a=n, d) = 1 + ρ_p ∫ d³r (-4 C¹¹_d)

## Context

This work extends variational correlated basis methods previously applied to electromagnetic responses and optical potentials in nucleon matter (Fantoni and Pandharipande, 1988; Fantoni, Friman, and Pandharipande, 1983) to weak interaction operators. The calculated quenching of 20-25% is consistent with the empirical factor of ~0.6 needed to bring pf-shell Gamow-Teller transition rates into agreement with experiment, as reviewed by Arima et al. (1987). The effective two-nucleon interaction obtained from the correlated basis describes spin-isospin susceptibilities of nuclear and neutron matter fairly accurately, but three-body terms are necessary to reproduce the compressibility; the authors note that realistic calculations of weak interaction rates in nucleon matter can presumably be carried out using the effective operators and interactions studied here.
