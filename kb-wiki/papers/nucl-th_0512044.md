# Meson Production on the Nucleon in the Giessen K-Matrix Approach

> Meson production on the proton is described in a coupled channels K-matrix approach. Both hadronic and photonic scenarios are taken into account on the same theoretical footing. At tree-level the Born amplitudes are obtained from an effective Lagrangian with phenomenologically adjustable coupling constants. The spectral distributions, resonances and their width and the background contributions are obtained within the same approach, thus accounting properly for interference effects. Applications to omega- and associated strangeness production on the proton are discussed.

## Key claim

The Giessen K-matrix approach provides a unified coupled-channels description of both hadronic and photonic meson production on the nucleon, but the quality of the description depends on including spin-5/2 resonances and on treating non-resonant background contributions separately for different data sets (CLAS vs. SAPHIR) when fitting associated strangeness production.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary coupled-channels K-matrix model using an effective Lagrangian with phenomenologically adjustable coupling constants at tree-level, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation by decomposing the Green function into principal value and pole parts. The model includes nucleons and hyperons from the SU(3) flavor octet, their excited states, pseudoscalar and vector meson octet states, and the photon with electromagnetic couplings, and is applied to both hadronic (πN) and photonic (γN) reactions up to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- χ²_γω = 4.5 for ω meson photoproduction with spin-5/2 resonances included, improving from χ²_γω = 6.25 in previous work (Penner 2002a, Penner 2002b)
- 115 data points total for ω-meson production in πN scattering, including differential and total cross sections, all measured before 1980
- The D₁₅(1675) state dominates the resonance part of the πN → ωN production amplitude
- The spin density matrix element ρ₀₀^GJ for forward directions in ω photoproduction was measured by SAPHIR to be in the range 0.2–0.3
- Two peak structures appear in γN → KΛ differential cross sections at 1.7 GeV and 1.9 GeV, with the first bump at 1.67 GeV produced by the S₁₁(1650) resonance

## Context

This work extends the Giessen model previously developed by Feuster (1998a, 1998b) and Penner (2002a, 2002b, 2002c) by including spin-5/2 resonance contributions (Shklyar 2004), which were previously restricted to spin J ≤ 3/2. The approach contrasts with simplified single-channel effective Lagrangian studies of ω production by Zhao (1998) and Titov (2002), and addresses the search for "missing resonances" predicted by quark models but not yet observed in pion- or photon-induced reactions. The results enable future precise measurements of spin density matrices in narrower energy bins to resolve interference effects between resonant and non-resonant contributions.
