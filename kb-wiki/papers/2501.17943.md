# The spatial string tension and its effects on screening correlators in a thermal QCD plasma

> We calculate the spatial Wilson line correlator for 2+1 flavor QCD using highly improved staggered quark discretization for fermions and in quenched QCD for a wide range of temperatures, from the chiral crossover temperature $\mathrm{T_{pc}\simeq 156}$ MeV or the deconfinement temperature $\simeq 300$ MeV respectively, up to $2$ GeV. Extracting the spatial string tension for different lattice cut-offs and by performing a continuum extrapolation of this observable, we show that the soft (magnetic) gluons interact non-perturbatively even at temperatures $\gtrsim 1$ GeV. We provide incriminating evidences to demonstrate that dimensionally reduced effective theories can describe these soft quark and gluon quasi-particles for both quenched and $2+1$ flavor QCD, at temperatures $\mathrm{T\gtrsim 5T_{pc}}$. We also show for the first time the imprints of the non-perturbative pseudo-potential in the properties of mesonic screening masses for temperatures ranging from $0.8$-$164$ GeV in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that non-perturbative magnetic gluon interactions, quantified by the spatial string tension, persist in QCD at temperatures above 1 GeV, and that the onset of dimensional reduction occurs at temperatures T ≳ 5 Tpc for 2+1 flavor QCD, rather than at the lower temperatures previously assumed.

## Method

The authors calculated spatial Wilson line correlators in modified-Coulomb gauge on 4-dimensional Euclidean lattices for both 2+1 flavor QCD (using highly improved staggered quark discretization) and quenched QCD, extracting the pseudo-potential V(R) from ratios of correlators at different Wilson line lengths. They performed continuum extrapolations of the spatial string tension σs using data at multiple lattice cutoffs (Nτ = 8,10 for QCD with dynamical quarks; Nτ = 8,12,16 for quenched QCD) over a temperature range from approximately 0.16 GeV to 2.7 GeV, and then used the extracted non-perturbative potential to solve a 2-dimensional Schrödinger equation for mesonic screening masses.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature for 2+1 flavor QCD is Tpc ≃ 156 MeV, and the deconfinement temperature for quenched QCD is Td r0 = 0.7457(45)
- For 2+1 flavor QCD in the temperature range 5-5.8 Tpc, the spatial string tension follows √σs = 0.566(15) gE²
- For quenched QCD in the temperature range 5-7.3 Tpc, √σs = 0.559(9) gE²
- The effective quark mass M extracted from screening mass data is parametrized as M/(πT) = 1 + 0.0159(15) g² - 0.0165(10) g⁴ with χ²/d.o.f = 0.53, using the coupling g estimated at scale 2πT with Λ(3)MS = 0.339(12)
- The zero-temperature string tension is √σ = 0.467 GeV from Ref. Brambilla (2022)
- The Luescher coefficient αL transitions from π/12 to π/24 at T ≳ 5 Tpc for 2+1 flavor QCD, indicating the onset of dimensional reduction

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the spatial string tension in quenched QCD by Boyd (1996) and in 2+1 flavor QCD with heavier-than-physical quarks by Cheng (2008), providing continuum-extrapolated results with physical quark masses. The authors show that the non-perturbative pseudo-potential explains the deviation of mesonic screening masses from perturbative EQCD predictions observed by Dalla Brida (2021) and Bazavov (2019), and quantifies the spin-splitting between vector and pseudo-scalar screening channels that leading-order EQCD cannot address. This enables future work on transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and baryon screening masses.
