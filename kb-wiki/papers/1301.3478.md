# Eta, Eta' and eLSM

> Prepared for Proceedings of Xth Conference on Quark Confinement and the Hadron Spectrum, October 8-12, 2012, TUM Campus Garching, Munich, Germany. 8 pages. v2 contains updated reference list

## Key claim
The Extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM), which includes only quark-antiquark states, can reproduce the eta-prime mass within experimental bounds but yields an eta mass below the experimental value, suggesting that non-quarkonium contributions may be needed to fully describe the pseudoscalar isosinglet states.

## Method
The authors constructed a globally U(3)L x U(3)R invariant Lagrangian containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector meson fields, with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking implemented via non-vanishing vacuum expectation values of the scalar isosinglet fields. They performed a global fit of 11 free parameters to 21 observables (masses and decay widths from the PDG) to determine the eta-eta-prime mixing angle and the masses of the physical eta and eta-prime states.

## Key numbers
- Fitted eta mass: m_eta = (509.4 ± 3.0) MeV, below the experimental value of (547.9 ± 27.4) MeV
- Fitted eta-prime mass: m_eta-prime = (962.5 ± 5.6) MeV, within the experimental interval of (957.8 ± 47.9) MeV
- Eta-eta-prime mixing angle: phi_eta = -44.6 degrees, close to maximal mixing
- Fitted pion decay constant: f_pi = (96.3 ± 0.7) MeV compared to experimental (92.2 ± 4.6) MeV
- Fitted kaon decay constant: f_K = (106.9 ± 0.6) MeV compared to experimental (110.4 ± 5.5) MeV

## Context
This work extends the three-flavor Linear Sigma Model by including vector and axial-vector degrees of freedom (eLSM), building on prior developments by Parganlija and collaborators (2013) and others. The model enables systematic study of meson spectroscopy including scalar and axial-vector mesons, and can be extended to finite temperatures and densities following the approach of Struber (2013). The underprediction of the eta mass suggests that glueball admixtures, as considered by Stani (2012), may be necessary to fully describe the pseudoscalar isosinglet wave functions.
