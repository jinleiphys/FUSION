# Low-momentum interactions with smooth cutoffs

> Nucleon-nucleon potentials evolved to low momentum, which show great promise in few- and many-body calculations, have generally been formulated with a sharp cutoff on relative momenta. However, a sharp cutoff has technical disadvantages and can cause convergence problems at the 10-100 keV level in the deuteron and triton. This motivates using smooth momentum-space regulators as an alternative. We generate low-momentum interactions with smooth cutoffs both through energy-independent renormalization group methods and using a multi-step process based on the Bloch-Horowitz approach. We find greatly improved convergence for calculations of the deuteron and triton binding energies in a harmonic oscillator basis compared to results with a sharp cutoff. Even a slight evolution of chiral effective field theory interactions to lower momenta is beneficial. The renormalization group preserves the long-range part of the interaction, and consequently the renormalization of long-range operators, such as the quadrupole moment, the radius and 1/r, is small. This demonstrates that low-energy observables in the deuteron are reproduced without short-range correlations in the wave function.

## Key claim

Smooth momentum-space regulators for low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions eliminate the convergence problems at the 10–100 keV level in deuteron and triton binding energies that arise when using sharp cutoffs, but the choice of regulator form and hermitization scheme introduces freedom that shifts contributions to three-body forces.

## Method

The authors construct low-momentum interactions with smooth cutoffs using two approaches: a three-step method starting from an energy-dependent renormalization group (RG) equation that preserves the fully-off-shell T-matrix, followed by conversion to energy independence and hermitization via Gram-Schmidt, Okubo, or Andreozzi transformations; and an alternative energy-independent RG equation derived from half-on-shell T-matrix equivalence. They apply exponential and Fermi-Dirac regulator functions to evolve chiral effective field theory potentials at N³LO and the Argonne v₁₈ potential to lower cutoffs, then test convergence in harmonic oscillator basis calculations of the deuteron and triton.

## Key numbers

- Sharp cutoff causes convergence problems at the 10–100 keV level in deuteron and triton binding energies when using harmonic oscillator bases
- For a cutoff Λ = 2 fm⁻¹, the converged triton binding energy with a sharp cutoff is 50 keV less than with a particular Fermi-Dirac regulator
- The exponential regulator with n = 3 corresponds to what is used in N³LO chiral potentials; n = 10 is the practical upper limit for numerical calculations
- Low-momentum interactions from different starting potentials collapse to nearly the same potential as the cutoff is lowered below 3 fm⁻¹
- Fermi-Dirac regulator with ε = 0.5 fm⁻¹ and exponential regulator with n = 8 produce minimal phase shift distortion

## Context

This work extends the Vlowk program of Bogner, Furnstahl, and collaborators (2003–2005) which showed that nucleon-nucleon potentials evolved to low momentum with sharp cutoffs simplify few- and many-body calculations. The sharp cutoff caused cusp-like behavior in the interaction and wave functions, leading to slow convergence in harmonic oscillator basis calculations observed by Bogner (2005). The smooth-cutoff approach resolves these technical disadvantages while preserving the long-range part of the interaction, demonstrating that low-energy deuteron observables (quadrupole moment, radius, 1/r) are reproduced without short-range correlations in the wave function. This enables more efficient variational and coupled-cluster calculations and provides a framework for systematically including three-nucleon forces that differ depending on regulator choice.
