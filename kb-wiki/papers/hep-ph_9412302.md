# Heavy-Quark Symmetry and Skyrmions

> We review recent development on combining heavy-quark symmetry and chiral symmetry in the skyrmion structure of the baryons containing one or more heavy quarks, c (charmed) and b (bottom). We describe two approaches: One going from the chiral symmetry regime of light quarks to the heavy-quark symmetry regime which will be referred to as ``bottom-up" approach and the other going down from the heavy-quark limit to the realistic finite-mass regime which will be referred to as ``top-down." A possible hidden connection between the two symmetry limits is suggested. This review is based largely on the work done -- some unpublished -- by the authors since several years.

## Key claim

This review argues that the bound-state Skyrme model approach to heavy baryons (containing charm or bottom quarks) can be made compatible with heavy-quark symmetry by constructing an effective Lagrangian that incorporates both chiral symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry simultaneously, resolving the problem that the traditional Callan-Klebanov approach gives hyperfine splittings that are too large and inconsistent with the heavy-quark limit.

## Method

The authors review two complementary approaches: a "bottom-up" approach starting from the chiral symmetry regime of light quarks and moving toward the heavy-quark symmetry regime, and a "top-down" approach starting from the infinite heavy-quark mass limit and adding 1/m_Q corrections. They construct an effective Lagrangian for heavy mesons (pseudoscalar P and vector P*) interacting with pions that respects both SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetry, then solve for bound states of this Lagrangian in the soliton background of the Skyrme model.

## Key numbers

* The D*-D splitting is approximately 145 MeV, while the B*-B splitting is approximately 50 MeV, consistent with the expected 1/m_Q behavior in the heavy-quark limit.
* The mass ratio of pseudoscalar to vector meson is 0.56 for strange (K/K*), 0.93 for charm (D/D*), and 0.99 for bottom (B/B*), showing that the approximation of integrating out heavy vector mesons becomes unreliable for charm and bottom.
* The hyperfine constant c is 0.60 for strange baryons, 0.14 for charmed baryons, and 0.03 for bottom baryons, decreasing toward zero in the infinite heavy meson mass limit.
* The bound-state eigenenergies used are omega_{B,s} = 223 MeV (fitted), omega_{B,c} = 1418 MeV (fitted), and omega_{B,b} = 4599 MeV (from experimental meson masses).
* The soliton mass M_sol = 866 MeV and moment of inertia 1/I = 196 MeV are fitted to nucleon and Delta masses.

## Context

This review extends the Callan-Klebanov bound-state approach (Callan and Klebanov, 1985) originally developed for strange baryons, which was first applied to charm and bottom baryons by Rho, Riska and Scoccola (1992). The work addresses a key difficulty: the traditional Skyrme model treatment of heavy vector mesons via the approximation K*_mu ~ (sqrt(2)/m_K*) A_mu K becomes invalid for heavy quarks because the vector meson mass is not much larger than the pseudoscalar mass. The resolution follows the approach of Jenkins, Manohar and Wise (1993) who applied the bound-state method to heavy meson effective Lagrangians incorporating both chiral symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry. This enables systematic inclusion of 1/m_Q corrections and connects to the non-abelian Berry potential interpretation of hyperfine splitting (Byung-Yoon Park and collaborators).
