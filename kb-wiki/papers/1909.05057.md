# Quenching of gamma0 transition results from p-wave neutron inducing doorway mechanism

> Gamma-strength function essentially distinguishes the reaction mechanisms of charged particle inelastic and neutron capture reactions, reflecting from the ratios of transition of neutron capture to low-lying states. The extraordinary quenching of gamma_0 transition of p-wave neutron resonance reaction in 3s-region nucleus 57Fe is observed, for the first time, due to the non-forming 2p-1h doorway state activation which raises the strong intensities of gamma_1 and gamma_2. The enhancement of low energy gamma-strength function doesn't emerge in the neutron capture reaction resulting from the population of cascade transitions of low-lying states rather than primary transitions. The astrophysical reaction rates that are extracted from total and partial neutron capture cross sections of 57Fe might be adopted to constrain the abundance of the successive heavier isotopes in s-process.

## Key claim

The observed quenching of the gamma_0 transition in p-wave neutron resonance on 57Fe is attributed to the non-formation of a 2p-1h doorway state, which instead raises the intensities of gamma_1 and gamma_2; this effect is specific to p-wave resonances in the 3s-region nucleus and does not appear for s-wave resonances.

## Method

Neutron capture on a 57Fe enriched sample was measured using the time-of-flight method with a pulsed neutron beam from the 3 MV Pelletron at the Tokyo Institute of Technology, covering neutron energies from 2 to 90 keV. Gamma rays were detected with an anti-Compton NaI(Tl) spectrometer placed at 125 degrees relative to the proton beam direction, and the pulse height weighting method was used to obtain detection efficiency proportional to deposited energy. The ratio of primary transition intensities to low-lying states (ground state, first and second excited states) was extracted from unfolded gamma-ray spectra and used to infer the gamma-strength function ratio R, distinguishing s-wave and p-wave neutron resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

- The primary transition energies observed in 57Fe(n,gamma)58Fe are: E_gamma0 = 10.09 MeV (to ground state), E_gamma1 = 9.28 MeV (to first excited state at 0.811 MeV), and E_gamma2 = 8.42 MeV (to second excited state at 1.675 MeV).
- The averaged width ratio in the 3s region is <Gamma_gamma(s)> ~ 3 <Gamma_gamma(p)>.
- The astrophysical reaction rates deviate from BRUSLIB calculations by factors of two to three in the low temperature region (T9 < 0.8 GK), but agree well near T9 = 1 GK.
- The neutron capture sample thickness was 0.373 mm; the main NaI(Tl) detector had dimensions 15.2 cm diameter by 30.5 cm, with the spectrometer placed 78.5 cm from the sample center.

## Context

This work extends studies of non-statistical effects in neutron capture, particularly the doorway mechanism with 2p-1h configurations first identified by Lane and Lynn (1960) and the pygmy resonance observed at E_gamma ~ 5.5 MeV. The observed quenching of gamma_0 for p-wave neutrons in the 3s-region nucleus 57Fe contrasts with the enhancement of low-energy gamma-strength function seen in charged-particle induced reactions such as (3He,3He'gamma) by Voinov et al. (2004) and (d,p) reactions, which does not appear in neutron capture experiments. The results enable improved constraints on s-process nucleosynthesis calculations for isotopes between Fe and Sr, particularly for the weak s-process component in massive stars.
