# Isospin and momentum dependence of liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The liquid-gas phase transition in hot neutron-rich nuclear matter is investigated within a self-consistent thermal model using different interactions with or without isospin and/or momentum dependence. The boundary of the phase-coexistence region is shown to be sensitive to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy as well as the isospin and momentum dependence of the nuclear interaction.

## Key claim

The boundary of the liquid-gas phase-coexistence region in hot neutron-rich nuclear matter is sensitive to both the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and the isospin and momentum dependence of the nuclear interaction, but the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential itself has little influence on the phase transition at pressures below a limiting pressure.

## Method

The authors used a self-consistent thermal model with three different nuclear effective interactions: the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction (constrained by isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions), a momentum-independent MID interaction, and an isoscalar momentum-dependent eMDYI interaction. At zero temperature, all three interactions give the same equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter. The phase coexistence was determined by solving the Gibbs conditions (equal chemical potentials and pressures) using a geometrical construction method on chemical potential isobars at fixed temperature.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV, incompressibility K0 = 211 MeV, and symmetry energy of 31.6 MeV for cold symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ (MDI interaction).
- At T = 10 MeV, critical pressures for MDI interaction: 0.265 MeV/fm³ (x=0, softer symmetry energy) and 0.195 MeV/fm³ (x=-1, stiffer symmetry energy).
- At T = 10 MeV, critical pressures for MID interaction: 0.230 MeV/fm³ (x=0) and 0.154 MeV/fm³ (x=-1).
- At T = 10 MeV, limiting pressures for eMDYI interaction: 0.205 MeV/fm³ (x=0) and 0.175 MeV/fm³ (x=-1).
- The isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU constrained the parameter x to be between 0 and -1 for nuclear matter densities less than about 1.2ρ0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter (Muller 1995, Liko 1997, Su 2000) by systematically comparing interactions with and without isospin and momentum dependence. The MDI interaction used is constrained by recent isospin diffusion data (Chen 2005, Li-Chen 2005) that placed stringent constraints on the symmetry energy at sub-normal densities. The results enable better predictions for the phase diagram of neutron-rich matter relevant to both heavy-ion collisions with radioactive beams and astrophysical applications.
