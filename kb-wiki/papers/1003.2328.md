## Key numbers
* The strong interaction energy shift of the 1s level in kaonic hydrogen is calculated to be between 195 eV and 289 eV depending on the pion-nucleon sigma term, compared to the experimental value of 193(43) eV (Cieplý & Smejkal).
* The calculated decay width of the 1s level in kaonic hydrogen ranges from 664 eV to 763 eV, significantly larger than the experimental value of 249(150) eV (Cieplý & Smejkal).
* The binding energy of the kaonic nuclear cluster $\bar{K}NN$ is predicted to be 48 MeV with a width of 61 MeV in the model of Akaishi and Yamazaki (Faber et al.).
* The DISTO Collaboration reports a binding energy for the $\bar{K}NN$ state of 105(2) MeV and a width of 118(8) MeV (Faber et al.).
* The estimated value for the 2p strong interaction width of $K^{-}p$ atoms is 0.105 +/- 0.002 meV (Kalantari & Raeisi G.).
* The optimum target density range for detecting higher x-ray yields is 0.03-0.06 of liquid hydrogen density (LHD) for $K^{-}p$ atoms and 0.06-0.2 of LHD for $K^{-}d$ atoms (Kalantari & Raeisi G.).
* At an incident pion energy of 1.17 GeV, simulations show about 65% of omega mesons and 90% of rho mesons decay inside the nucleus (Fabbietti).
* Secondary pion beams at GSI SIS18 can deliver maximal intensities of 10^4 particles per second to FOPI and 10^6 particles per second to HADES for a beam momentum of 1.3 GeV/c (Fabbietti).

## Context
This workshop proceedings summarizes the state of the field in 2009, extending the work of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) on deeply bound kaonic states and the chiral dynamics approach to the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance. It presents new calculations for kaonic hydrogen properties relevant to the SIDDHARTA experiment, and discusses future experimental programs at GSI with pion beams. The volume enables a direct comparison between different theoretical models (e.g., single-pole vs. double-pole nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$) and provides a roadmap for upcoming experiments to resolve open questions in antikaon-nucleon interactions and kaonic nuclei.
