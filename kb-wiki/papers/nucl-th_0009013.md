# Sub-barrier fusion of drip-line nuclei

> We discuss the role of break-up process of a loosely-bound projectile in subbarrier fusion reactions. Coupled-channels calculations are carried out for $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb and $^{4,6}$He + $^{238}$U reactions by discretizing in energy the particle continuum states. Our calculations show that the coupling to the break-up channel has two effects, namely the loss of flux and the dynamical modulation of fusion potential. Their net effects differ depending on the energy region. At energies above the Coulomb barrier, the former effect dominates over the latter and cross sections for complete fusion are hindered compared with the no coupling case. On the other hand, at below the barrier, the latter effect is much larger than the former and complete fusion cross sections are enhanced consequently.

## Key claim

The net effect of coupling to break-up channels on complete fusion cross sections depends on the energy region: at energies above the Coulomb barrier, the loss of flux dominates and hinders complete fusion, while at sub-barrier energies, the dynamical modulation of the fusion potential (the real part of the polarization potential) dominates and enhances complete fusion cross sections relative to the no-coupling case.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed for the reactions \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{4,6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U, discretizing the particle continuum states into energy bins. The coupling form factors were computed microscopically by folding single-particle transition densities (promoting the last weakly-bound nucleon to continuum states) with the nuclear and Coulomb fields of the target, using Woods-Saxon potentials for the nuclear part. The coupled-channels equations were solved with incoming wave boundary conditions inside the Coulomb barrier, and complete fusion cross sections were defined using the flux in the non-continuum (ground-state) channel.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb: the last neutron in \(^{11}\)Be occupies the \(2s_{1/2}\) state bound by about 500 keV; continuum states up to 2 MeV were included with a step of 200 keV (10 effective excited channels).
- For \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb: the nuclear form factor dominates over the Coulomb form factor up to an internuclear distance of about 25 fm (at continuum energy \(E_c = 0.9\) MeV).
- For \(^{6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U: continuum states up to \(E_c = 5\) MeV were included with an energy step of 0.25 MeV; the \(L=2\) potential depth was adjusted to reproduce the correct resonance state at 1 MeV.
- For \(^{4}\)He + \(^{238}\)U: the bare Woods-Saxon potential used \(V_0 = -174.05\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.01\) fm, and \(a = 0.67\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier coupled-channels calculations of Dasso and Vitturi (1994), which treated break-up channels as a single discrete state, by using a larger number of continuum states and microscopic form factors. It addresses the controversy between the polarization-potential approach of Hussein et al. (1992) and Takigawa et al. (1993), which predicted only hindrance, and the Dasso-Vitturi result of enhancement. The findings are consistent with experimental observations by Dasgupta et al. (1999) for \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb (hindrance above barrier) and by the SACLAY group (2000) for \(^{4,6}\)He + \(^{238}\)U (enhancement below barrier). The work enables more realistic predictions for fusion of drip-line nuclei and clarifies the need to separate complete and incomplete fusion contributions in experiments.
