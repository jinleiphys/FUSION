# Static correlation lengths in QCD at high temperatures and finite densities

> We use a perturbatively derived effective field theory and three-dimensional lattice simulations to determine the longest static correlation lengths in the deconfined QCD plasma phase at high temperatures (T\gsim 2 Tc) and finite densities (μ\lsim 4 T). For vanishing chemical potential, we refine a previous determination of the Debye screening length, and determine the dependence of different correlation lengths on the number of massless flavours as well as on the number of colours. For non-vanishing but small chemical potential, the existence of Debye screening allows us to carry out simulations corresponding to the full QCD with two (or three) massless dynamical flavours, in spite of a complex action. We investigate how the correlation lengths in the different quantum number channels change as the chemical potential is switched on.

## Key claim

The paper uses a perturbatively derived effective field theory combined with three-dimensional lattice simulations to determine static correlation lengths in the deconfined QCD plasma phase, but the method is limited to temperatures above roughly \(T \gsim 2 T_c\) and chemical potentials \(\mu \lsim 4 T\), and relies on the assumption that higher-order perturbative corrections to the dimensional reduction parameters are small.

## Method

The authors perform dimensional reduction of hot QCD to a three-dimensional SU(3)+adjoint Higgs model, then simulate this effective theory on the lattice using a combination of heat bath and over-relaxation updates for gauge fields and heat bath with reflection steps for scalar fields. Correlation matrices are measured for operators in various quantum number channels, and screening masses are extracted by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem and identifying stable plateaus in effective mass plots. For finite chemical potential, the complex term in the effective action is handled via a reweighting technique, validated against simulations with imaginary chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass at \(T=2T_c\) and \(N_f=0\): \(m_D/g_3^2 = 1.70(5)\), corresponding to \(m_D/T = 4.6(2)\)
- Debye mass at \(T\sim 10^{11}T_c\) and \(N_f=0\): \(m_D/g_3^2 = 3.82(12)\), corresponding to \(m_D/T = 0.96(3)\)
- Lightest \(0^{++}_+\) state at \(T=2T_c\) and \(N_f=0\): \(M/g_3^2 = 0.994(19)\), corresponding to \(M/T = 2.71(6)\)
- Non-perturbative coefficient \(c_3\) in the Debye mass expansion: \(c_3 = 1.65(6)\)
- \({\cal O}(g^3T)\) correction to the Debye mass at \(T=2T_c\): \(-0.46(6) g_3^2\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of screening masses in the SU(2)+adjoint Higgs model (Hart, Laine, Philipsen, 2000) to SU(3) Yang-Mills theory and to two- and three-flavour QCD in the massless limit. It improves on the accuracy of the Debye screening length determination compared to earlier SU(3) results (Laine, Philipsen, 1998; Arnold, 1997) and measures screening lengths in additional quantum number channels. The method enables simulations with finite chemical potential that are otherwise difficult in four-dimensional lattice QCD due to the complex action problem, and the results can be compared with four-dimensional lattice simulations (Datta, Gupta, 1999) to validate the dimensional reduction approach.
