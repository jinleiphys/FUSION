# Three particle quantization condition in a finite volume: 2. general formalism and the analysis of data

> We derive the three-body quantization condition in a finite volume using an effective field theory in the particle-dimer picture. Moreover, we consider the extraction of physical observables from the lattice spectrum using the quantization condition. To illustrate the general framework, we calculate the volume-dependent three-particle spectrum in a simple model both below and above the three-particle threshold. The relation to existing approaches is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives a three-body quantization condition in a finite volume using an effective field theory in the particle-dimer picture, arguing that the finite-volume spectrum is determined solely by on-shell three-body S-matrix elements, and that off-shell terms in the three-body potential (such as the coupling \(D_4''\)) do not affect the on-shell T-matrix or the spectrum.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic effective field theory for three identical scalar particles, introducing a dimer field to describe two-body interactions. They classify the three-body interaction terms in the Lagrangian up to next-to-next-to-leading order in momentum expansion, identify redundant off-shell couplings that can be eliminated via equations of motion, and derive a particle-dimer scattering equation (Lippmann-Schwinger type) for the three-body T-matrix. The quantization condition in a finite volume is then obtained from this formalism, and the volume-dependent three-particle spectrum is calculated numerically both below and above the three-particle threshold.

## Key numbers

* The two-body T-matrix on the mass shell is given by \(T = \frac{8\pi/m}{p\cot\delta(p)-ip}\), where \(p\cot\delta(p)\) is expanded as \(p\cot\delta(p)=-\frac{1}{a}+\frac{r_e}{2}p^2+\sum_{n=2}^\infty b_{2n}p^{2n}\) (effective-range expansion).
* The three-body Lagrangian at leading order contains the coupling \(D_0\); at next-to-leading order, the coupling \(D_2\) multiplies the operator \(\sum_{i=1}^3 ({\bf p}_i^2+{\bf q}_i^2)\); at next-to-next-to-leading order, three couplings \(D_4, D_4', D_4''\) appear, with \(D_4''\) shown to be redundant.
* The particle-dimer scattering equation (Eq. (4.1)) includes an integral over the three-momentum \({\bf k}\) with a cutoff \(\Lambda\): \({\cal M}({\bf p},{\bf q};E)=Z({\bf p},{\bf q};E)+8\pi\int^\Lambda\frac{d^3{\bf k}}{(2\pi)^3}\,Z({\bf p},{\bf k};E)\tau({\bf k};E){\cal M}({\bf k},{\bf q};E)\).

## Context

This work extends the formal three-body quantization conditions derived by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015) and by Briceno, Hansen, and Sharpe (2017) by providing a simpler parameterization using the particle-dimer formalism, which is more directly applicable to lattice QCD data analysis. It also builds on the numerical studies of Kreuzer and collaborators (2008-2012) who used the particle-dimer picture to compute finite-volume spectra. The framework enables the extraction of three-body observables from lattice spectra, addressing challenges such as the Roper resonance decay into a nucleon and two pions, and is intended to be generalized to include spin, relativity, and coupled two- and three-particle sectors.
