# Nucleon off-shell structure and the free neutron valence structure from A=3 inclusive electron scattering measurements

> Understanding the differences between the distribution of quarks bound in protons and neutrons is key for constraining the mechanisms of SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry breaking in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). While vast amounts of proton structure measurements were done, data on the structure of the neutron is much more spars as experiments typically extract the structure of neutrons from measurements of light atomic nuclei using model-dependent corrections for nuclear effects. Recently the MARATHON collaboration performed such an extraction by measuring inclusive deep-inelastic electron-scattering on helium-3 and tritium mirror nuclei where nuclear effects are expected to be similar and thus be suppressed in the helium-3 to tritium ratio. Here we evaluate the model dependence of this extraction by examining a wide range of models including the effect of using instant-form and light-cone nuclear wave functions and several different parameterizations of nucleon modification effects, including those with and without isospin dependence. We find that, while the data cannot differentiate among the different models of nuclear structure and nucleon modification, they consistently prefer a neutron-to-proton structure function ratio of at $x_B \rightarrow 1$ of $\sim 0.4$ with a typical uncertainty ($1σ$) of $\sim0.05$ and $\sim0.10$ for isospin-independent and isospin-dependent modification models, respectively. While strongly favoring SU(6) symmetry breaking models based on perturbative QCD and the Schwinger-Dyson equation calculation, the MARATHON data do not completely rule out the scalar di-quark models if an isospin-dependent modification exist.

## Key claim

The MARATHON data consistently prefer a neutron-to-proton structure function ratio at x_B approaching 1 of approximately 0.4, but the exact value and its uncertainty depend on whether the bound nucleon off-shell modification is assumed to be isospin-independent (uncertainty of about 0.05) or isospin-dependent (uncertainty of about 0.10), and the data cannot differentiate among different models of nuclear structure and nucleon modification.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous fits of a nuclear convolution model to inclusive deep-inelastic electron scattering data from BONuS (deuterium), Seely (helium-3 on deuterium), and MARATHON (helium-3 on tritium), using both spectral-function and light-cone formulations of the nuclear wave function. They varied the functional form of the bound nucleon off-shell modification function f^off, including constant, linear, isospin-dependent, and fixed forms from the CJ and KP global analyses, and extracted the free neutron-to-proton structure function ratio F2^n/F2^p at x_B approaching 1 as a fit parameter.

## Key numbers

* For isospin-independent off-shell modification, the extracted F2^n/F2^p at x_B approaching 1 is 0.43 plus or minus 0.04 (1 sigma), consistent across constant and linear modification models.
* For isospin-dependent constant-in-x modification including MARATHON data, the extracted ratio is 0.40 plus or minus 0.07 (1 sigma).
* For isospin-dependent linear-in-x modification including MARATHON data, the extracted ratio is 0.43 plus or minus 0.10 (1 sigma) for the spectral function approach and 0.49 plus or minus 0.07 (1 sigma) for the light-cone approach.
* Using the fixed CJ off-shell parameterization, the extracted ratio is 0.42 plus or minus 0.04 (1 sigma); using the fixed KP parameterization, it is 0.43 plus or minus 0.04 (1 sigma).
* The isospin-independent results reject the scalar diquark prediction by 4.5 standard deviations; the isospin-dependent results reject it by only 2 to 3 standard deviations.

## Context

This work extends the MARATHON collaboration's extraction of F2^n/F2^p (Abrams 2021), which used only the single Kulagin-Petti model for nucleon off-shell modification, by examining a wide range of alternative models for the off-shell function and nuclear wave functions. It builds on previous studies by Segarra (2020) that indicated potential systematic model uncertainties of up to 20 percent in such extractions. The results show that inclusive DIS data alone cannot constrain the off-shell modification function, motivating future measurements using spectator-tagged deep-inelastic scattering from experiments such as BONuS12 and BAND/LAD.
