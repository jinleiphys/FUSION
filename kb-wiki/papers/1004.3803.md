# Shock waves in strongly coupled plasmas

> Shock waves are supersonic disturbances propagating in a fluid and giving rise to dissipation and drag. Weak shocks, i.e., those of small amplitude, can be well described within the hydrodynamic approximation. On the other hand, strong shocks are discontinuous within hydrodynamics and therefore probe the microscopics of the theory. In this paper we consider the case of the strongly coupled N=4 plasma whose microscopic description, applicable for scales smaller than the inverse temperature, is given in terms of gravity in an asymptotically $AdS_5$ space. In the gravity approximation, weak and strong shocks should be described by smooth metrics with no discontinuities. For weak shocks we find the dual metric in a derivative expansion and for strong shocks we use linearized gravity to find the exponential tail that determines the width of the shock. In particular we find that, when the velocity of the fluid relative to the shock approaches the speed of light $v\to 1$ the penetration depth $\ell$ scales as $\ell\sim (1-v^2)^{1/4}$. We compare the results with second order hydrodynamics and the Israel-Stewart approximation. Although they all agree in the hydrodynamic regime of weak shocks, we show that there is not even qualitative agreement for strong shocks. For the gravity side, the existence of shock waves implies that there are disturbances of constant shape propagating on the horizon of the dual black holes.

## Key claim

Shock waves in strongly coupled N=4 plasma are described by smooth metrics with no discontinuities in the gravity dual, but for strong shocks (where the fluid velocity relative to the shock approaches the speed of light) the penetration depth scales as ℓ ∼ (1−v²)^{1/4}, and neither second-order hydrodynamics nor the Israel-Stewart approximation shows even qualitative agreement with the gravity result in this regime.

## Method

The authors construct the dual gravitational metric for weak shocks using a derivative expansion in the fluid-gravity correspondence, reproducing the hydrodynamic results. For strong shocks, they use linearized gravity in an asymptotically AdS₅ space to compute the exponential tail that determines the shock width, solving for the complex frequency ω(k) of sound modes from the gravity equations. They compare these results with first-order viscous hydrodynamics, second-order hydrodynamics, the Israel-Stewart theory, and effective hydrodynamics approaches.

## Key numbers

- The penetration depth ℓ scales as ℓ ∼ (1−v²)^{1/4} when the fluid velocity relative to the shock approaches v → 1
- The speed of sound in the conformal N=4 plasma is v_s = 1/√3 ≈ 0.57735
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is η/s = 1/(4π)
- Second-order hydrodynamics fails for velocities v > √((5−2 ln 2)/(12−6 ln 2)) ≈ 0.678871
- The velocity of discontinuity propagation in second-order hydrodynamics and Israel-Stewart theory is v_disc = √(1/(2(2−ln 2))) ≈ 0.618546
- The Israel-Stewart theory has a pole at v ≈ 0.92 for the N=4 plasma parameters
- The effective viscosity coefficients from fifth-order expansion include: η₀,₁ = 2−ln 2, η₀,₂ ≈ −1.379, η₂,₁ ≈ −2.275, η₀,₃ ≈ −0.082, η₄,₀ ≈ 0.565, η₀,₄ ≈ 2.9, η₂,₂ ≈ 1.1

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT correspondence to study shock waves in strongly coupled N=4 plasma, building on the fluid-gravity correspondence developed by Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) and the second-order hydrodynamic calculations of Baier et al. (2007). The results contradict the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1976, 1979) for strong shocks, showing that even with parameter tuning the Israel-Stewart theory predicts q ∝ γ (where γ = 1/√(1−v²)) while gravity predicts q ∝ γ^{1/2} as v → 1. The effective hydrodynamics approach of Lublinsky et al. (2009) gives reasonable approximation on the subsonic side but fails to capture the v → 1 asymptotics. This work enables future numerical solutions of the full Einstein equations in the bulk to obtain the complete shock profile T^{tt}(x).
