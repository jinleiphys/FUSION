# Dynamical grooming of QCD jets

> We propose a new class of infrared-collinear (IRC) and Sudakov safe observables with an associated jet grooming technique that removes dynamically soft and large angle branches. It is based on identifying the hardest branch in the Cambridge/Aachen re-clustering sequence and discarding prior splittings that occur at larger angles. This leads to a dynamically generated cut-off on the phase space of the tagged splitting that is encoded in a Sudakov form factor. In this exploratory study we focus on the mass and momentum sharing distributions of the tagged splitting which we analyze analytically to modified leading logarithmic accuracy and compare to Monte-Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new jet grooming technique called "dynamical grooming" that identifies the hardest splitting in a Cambridge/Aachen re-clustering sequence based on a pseudo energy correlation variable, removing the need for external grooming parameters like the cut on momentum fraction or angular exponent found in SoftDrop. The authors claim this procedure generates a dynamical phase-space cutoff encoded in a Sudakov form factor, but hedge that this is an "exploratory study" with analysis performed only to modified leading logarithmic accuracy and compared to Monte-Carlo simulations, not to experimental data.

## Method

The authors define a hardness variable $\kappa^{(a)} = z(1-z)(\theta/R)^a$ for each splitting in the Cambridge/Aachen re-clustering sequence, where $z$ is the momentum sharing fraction, $\theta$ the splitting angle, $R$ the jet radius, and $a$ a free parameter. They then construct a Sudakov form factor that vetoes emissions with larger $\kappa^{(a)}$ than the tagged splitting, and compute normalized probability distributions for the tagged splitting's mass ($b=2$) and momentum sharing ($b=0$) at modified leading logarithmic accuracy. The analytical results are compared to PYTHIA8 simulations of di-jet events in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV with anti-$k_T$ jets of $R=0.8$ and jet $p_T > 450$ GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For $a=2$ (TimeDrop), the average position of the tagged branching in the C/A de-clustering sequence is approximately $i \approx 2$; for $a=1$ ($k_t$Drop), $i \approx 3$; for $a=0.1$ ($z$Drop), $i \approx 5$.
- The dynamically generated cutoff in momentum fraction $z$ scales as $z_{\rm cut} \approx e^{-\sqrt{a/\bar\alpha}}$, where $\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha_s C_F/\pi$.
- The critical line boundary on the Lund plane is given by $\ln k_{t,\text{cut}}(\theta) = (1-a)\ln(R/\theta) - \sqrt{a\ln 2/\bar\alpha}$, with the intercept at $\ln k_{t,\text{cut}}(R) \sim -\sqrt{a}$.
- The tagged mass distribution for $a=2$ (TimeDrop) reproduces the plain mass distribution at leading logarithmic accuracy, given by $\frac{\rho}{\sigma}\frac{d\sigma}{d\rho} = -\bar\alpha(\ln\rho + \frac{3}{4}(1-\rho)) \exp\{-\bar\alpha[\frac{1}{2}\ln^2\rho + \frac{3}{4}(\ln\rho+1-\rho)]\}$.

## Context

This work extends the family of jet grooming techniques that includes modified Mass Drop (mMDT) grooming by Dasgupta et al. (2013) and SoftDrop (SD) grooming by Larkoski et al. (2014), which require two external parameters ($z_{\rm cut}$ and $\beta$) to determine the first perturbative branching. The dynamical grooming approach instead uses a single parameter $a$ that defines what constitutes the "hardest" emission, with the cutoff emerging naturally from the Sudakov form factor. This enables future studies of jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions, where grooming techniques have been applied by the CMS and ALICE collaborations, and allows probing the splitting function down to low $z$ values without external parameter tuning.
