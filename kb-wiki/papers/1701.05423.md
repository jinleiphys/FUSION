# Baryon-to-pion ratios within generic (non)extensive statistics

> The successive stages of a high-energy collision are conjectured to end up with chemical and thermal freezeout of the produced particles. We utilize generic (non)extensive statistics which is believed to determine the degree of (non)extensivity through two critical exponents due to possible phase-space modifications. This statistical approach likely manifests various types of correlations and fluctuations and also possible interactions among the final-state produced particles. We study the baryon-to-pion ratios at top RHIC and LHC energies including the so-called proton anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper claims that baryon-to-pion ratios at top RHIC and LHC energies, including the so-called proton anomaly, can be reproduced by a hadron resonance gas model using generic (non)extensive statistics characterized by two critical exponents \((c,d)\), rather than by assuming either extensive Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics or Tsallis nonextensive statistics as special cases.

## Method

The authors applied a generic (non)extensive statistical approach based on an equivalence class \((c,d)\) of generalized entropies to a hadron resonance gas model. They fitted the model to measured baryon-to-pion ratios (\(\bar{\mathrm{p}}/\pi^-\), \(\mathrm{p}/\pi^+\), \(\Lambda/\pi^-\), \(\Xi/\pi^-\), \(\bar{\Xi}/\pi^+\), \(\Omega/\pi^-\), \(\bar{\Omega}/\pi^+\)) from most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV, extracting the freezeout temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu\), and the scaling exponents \(c\) and \(d\).

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV (RHIC), the nonextensive fit gave \(T = 156.24\) MeV, \(\mu = 22.30\) MeV, \(c = 0.972\), \(d = 0.95\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 1.35\); the extensive fit gave \(T = 160.65\) MeV, \(\mu = 24.95\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 1.51\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV (LHC), the nonextensive fit gave \(T = 161.71\) MeV, \(\mu = 2.52\) MeV, \(c = 1.011\), \(d = 1.0323\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 3.18\); the extensive fit gave \(T = 153.09\) MeV, \(\mu = 2.52\) MeV, \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 3.16\).

## Context

This work extends the standard hadron resonance gas model (Tawfik 2014) by replacing the assumption of extensive Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics with a generic (non)extensive framework that includes both BG and Tsallis statistics as special cases. The approach addresses the proton anomaly at top RHIC and LHC energies, which had previously motivated explanations involving chemical and thermal out-of-equilibrium freezeout (Petran 2013) or excluded volume corrections. The results suggest that the degree of nonextensivity in particle production is not captured by Tsallis statistics alone, and that the extracted \((c,d)\) values imply the generalized entropy is no longer maximal for equi-distribution when \(c>1\) and \(d>1\).
