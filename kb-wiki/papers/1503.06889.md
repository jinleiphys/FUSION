# Jet asymmetry and momentum imbalance from $2 \to 2$ and $2 \to 3$ partonic processes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study momentum imbalance as a function of jet asymmetry in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. To implement parton production during the collision, we include all Leading Order (LO) $2\to 2$ and $2\to 3$ parton processes in pQCD. The produced partons lose energy within the quark gluon plasma and hadronize collinearly when they leave it. The energy and momentum deposited into the plasma is described using linear viscous hydrodynamics with a constant energy loss per unit length and a total energy loss given by a Gaussian probability centered around a mean value $\bar{\mathcal{E}}$ and a half-width $Δ{\mathcal{E}}$. We argue that the shape of the asymmetry observed by the CERN-CMS Collaboration can indeed be attributed to parton energy loss in the medium and that a good description of data is achieved when one includes a slight enhancement coming from the contribution of $2\to 3$ parton processes that modifies the asymmetry distribution of the dijet events. We compare our results to CMS data for the most central collisions and study different values for $\bar{\mathcal{E}}$ and $Δ{\mathcal{E}}$.

## Key claim

The shape of the dijet asymmetry distribution observed by CMS in central Pb-Pb collisions can be attributed to parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma, and a better description of the data is achieved when a 20% contribution from 2-to-3 partonic processes is included alongside the dominant 2-to-2 processes.

## Method

The authors generated 2-to-2 and 2-to-3 parton scattering events using Madgraph, then let one parton hadronize in vacuum as the leading hadron while the other partons travel through a medium of length L=10 fm and lose energy according to a Gaussian probability distribution with mean energy loss and half-width. The energy and momentum deposited into the medium was described using linearized viscous hydrodynamics with a constant energy loss per unit length of dE/dx = 5 GeV/fm, and the resulting soft particle production was computed via the Cooper-Frye formalism. The simulated asymmetry distributions and missing transverse momentum were compared to CMS data for the 0-10% centrality bin.

## Key numbers

* The combined sample used 80% 2-to-2 and 20% 2-to-3 AA events to describe CMS data for Pb-Pb collisions at 0-10% centrality.
* The medium length was taken as L = 10 fm with the hard scattering occurring at ΔL = 5/6 L, corresponding to an average energy loss of 50 GeV from dE/dx = 5 GeV/fm.
* The characteristic freeze-out time extracted from the hydrodynamic calculation was Δτ ≈ 8.5 fm.
* For the missing transverse momentum comparison with CMS data, the Gaussian energy loss parameters used were (a) average energy loss 30 GeV with half-width 3 GeV and (b) average energy loss 50 GeV with half-width 5 GeV.
* The 2-to-3 contribution to the average missing transverse momentum reached up to 30% effect on the most asymmetric bins (AJ > 0.5) in the AA sample.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of 2-to-3 parton processes in azimuthal angular correlations (Ayala 2013, 2014) and energy-momentum deposition into the medium using linearized viscous hydrodynamics (Ayala 2014). It directly compares to CMS measurements (CMS 2011, 2012) of dijet asymmetry in Pb-Pb collisions, and complements studies by Renk (2012), Majumder (2013), and Hirano (2013) on jet energy loss and jet-bulk interplay. The results suggest that a mixed two- and three-jet sample with a realistic energy-loss profile can improve characterization of jet momentum imbalance.
