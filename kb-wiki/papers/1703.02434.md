# Correlations and fluctuations of the gauge topology at finite temperatures

> Instanton-dyons are topological solitons -- solutions of Yang-Mills equations -- which appear at non-trivial expectation value of $A_0$ at nonzero temperatures. Using the ensembles of those, generated in our previous work, for 2-color and 2-flavor QCD, below and above the deconfinement-chiral phase transition, we study the correlations between them, as well as fluctuations of several global charges in the sub-volumes of the total volume. The determined correlation lengths are the finite-$T$ extension of hadronic masses, such as that of $η'$ meson.

## Key claim

The correlation lengths extracted from instanton-dyon ensembles provide the finite-temperature extension of hadronic masses such as the eta-prime meson, but the screening mass extracted from the L-anti-L dyon correlator at temperatures near T_c is only about half of the zero-temperature eta-prime mass, suggesting partial U(1)_a restoration.

## Method

The authors used previously generated ensembles of 64 instanton-dyons on a 3-sphere S^3 for SU(2) gauge theory with 2 massless quark flavors, at ten temperatures from 0.66 T_c to 1.44 T_c. They computed pair correlation functions for L-anti-L, M-anti-M, and M-L dyon channels by histogramming distances between dyons and normalizing to randomly placed dyons. They also computed fluctuations of topological charge Q, magnetic charge M, electric charge E, and action S in subvolumes defined by cutting the S^3 sphere with a plane at varying angles.

## Key numbers

* The screening mass extracted from the L-anti-L dyon correlator is m/T_c approximately 3, compared to the zero-temperature eta-prime mass m_eta'/T_c = 958/155 approximately 6.
* The M-anti-M dyon correlator shows a screening mass m/T_c approximately 2 from the large-distance asymptotics.
* The ensemble contains 64 dyons per configuration for N_c=2 and N_f=2 massless QCD.
* Temperatures studied: T/T_c = 0.66, 0.72, 0.78, 0.85, 0.92, 1.0, 1.09, 1.18, 1.28, 1.44.
* The zero-temperature eta-prime mass is approximately 958 MeV, corresponding to a screening length of approximately 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-dyon ensemble simulations previously reported by Larsen and Shuryak (2015, 2016) for 2-color 2-flavor QCD, which demonstrated deconfinement and chiral phase transitions. The correlation functions studied here provide a semiclassical counterpart to lattice measurements of topological susceptibility by Bonati, Borsanyi, and Petreczky (2016), and to the subvolume fluctuation method proposed by Shuryak (2017). The results enable quantitative comparison between semiclassical instanton-dyon ensembles and lattice QCD in the temperature range 0.5 T_c to 2 T_c, particularly for determining where the transition from instanton gas to instanton-dyon plasma occurs.
