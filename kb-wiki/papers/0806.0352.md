# Causal relativistic hydrodynamics for viscous fluids

> We report on recent results from VISH2+1, a code that solves the relativistic Israel-Stewart equations for causal viscous hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions with longitudinal boost invariance. We find that even ``minimal'' shear viscosity eta/s=hbar/(4pi) leads to a large reduction of elliptic flow compared to ideal fluid dynamics. We explore systematically the sensitivity of this reduction to the equation of state, system size, initial conditions, and the microscopic relaxation time in different formulations of the Israel-Stewart equations.

# Causal relativistic hydrodynamics for viscous fluids

## Key claim

Even the "minimal" shear viscosity of η/s = ħ/(4π) causes a large reduction of elliptic flow compared to ideal fluid dynamics, but the magnitude of this reduction depends strongly on system size, equation of state, and the specific formulation of the Israel-Stewart equations used, with the "full" Israel-Stewart formalism showing weaker sensitivity to the poorly constrained relaxation time τπ than simplified versions.

## Method

The authors used VISH2+1, a numerical code that solves the relativistic Israel-Stewart equations for causal viscous hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions assuming longitudinal boost invariance and two transverse spatial dimensions. They systematically varied the equation of state (including a first-order phase transition and a smooth cross-over), system size (Au+Au vs. Cu+Cu), initial conditions, and the microscopic relaxation time τπ in both "full" and "simplified" formulations of the Israel-Stewart equations, comparing the resulting elliptic flow and entropy production.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity set to η/s = ħ/(4π) (the "minimal" value) leads to large reduction of elliptic flow compared to ideal fluid dynamics
- For b=7 fm Cu+Cu collisions at pT=2 GeV/c, pion v2 reduction from ideal fluid value was about 63% using simplified Israel-Stewart
- For b=7 fm Au+Au collisions at pT=2 GeV/c, the reduction was about 25% using full Israel-Stewart with a smooth cross-over equation of state
- Going from Cu+Cu to Au+Au at the same impact parameter cut the viscous reduction of v2 almost in half, to 39%
- Replacing a first-order phase transition equation of state (SM-EOS Q) with a smooth cross-over (EOS L) reduced the viscous suppression of v2 by about another third, to 28%
- A final 5-10% relative reduction of the suppression resulted from replacing simplified Israel-Stewart with full Israel-Stewart
- For τ0=0.6 fm/c and EOS L, entropy production fractions ranged from about 10% at high multiplicity densities to about 12-13% at low multiplicities

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart formalism (Israel 1979) for causal viscous hydrodynamics to heavy-ion collisions, resolving acausality and numerical instability problems of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation. The results reconcile apparent disagreements between earlier calculations by the authors' group (Song 2007) and Romatschke (2007) by showing that differences in system size, equation of state, and Israel-Stewart formulation account for the discrepancies. The findings enable systematic comparison with experimental elliptic flow data and multiplicity scaling observations from Voloshin and collaborators, and show that approximate multiplicity scaling of v2/εx and viscous entropy production holds across system sizes, though with scale breaking that increases with η/s.
