# Observing short-range correlations in nuclei through $ρ^0$ photo-production

> Short-range correlations (SRCs) are a universal feature of nuclear structure. A wide range of measurements, primarily using electron scattering, have revealed SRC properties, such as their abundance in different nuclei, as well as the strong preference for proton-neutron pairing over proton-proton or neutron-neutron pairing. Despite the inherent complexity of many-body systems, a number of the salient features of electron scattering measurements are described by a simple, factorized theory called Generalized Contact Formalism. A key element of this theory, the factorization of the interaction with a hard probe, has yet to be tested. An experiment conducted at Jefferson Lab in 2021 collected data from scattering a tagged photon beam, with an energy up to 10 GeV, from several nuclear targets, measuring final state particles in the large-acceptance GlueX spectrometer. In this paper, we propose a test of probe factorization by measuring cross section ratios sensitive to proton-proton pair prevalence and relative SRC abundances in $^4$He and $^{12}$C. We present GCF predictions of the observables and make projections of the expected precision the experiment can achieve.

# Observing short-range correlations in nuclei through ρ⁰ photo-production

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring cross section ratios in ρ⁰ photo-production from nuclear targets can test the probe factorization assumption of Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) and validate properties of short-range correlations (SRCs) observed in electron scattering, provided that systematic uncertainties can be kept at a level comparable to the projected statistical precision.

## Method

The authors used Generalized Contact Formalism to generate Monte Carlo predictions for A(γ, ρ⁰ p) and A(γ, ρ⁰ pp) events on deuterium, helium-4, and carbon-12 targets, assuming an 8 GeV photon beam. They applied event selection criteria including cuts on Mandelstam variables (|t|>1.5 GeV², |u|>1.5 GeV²) and missing momentum (p_miss>0.350 GeV) to isolate SRC-dominated kinematics. The predictions were compared to preliminary data from the 2021 Jefferson Lab SRC/CT Experiment collected with the GlueX spectrometer, using sideband subtraction and a two-dimensional cut on y₃π and M²₂N to remove background from ρ⁰π⁺ production with misidentified pions.

## Key numbers

- Photon beam energy range: approximately 6–10 GeV, with predictions made at 8 GeV (the approximate coherent peak energy)
- Missing momentum threshold for SRC selection: p_miss > 0.350 GeV (simulation) and p_miss > 0.4 GeV/c (data analysis)
- Recoil proton momentum range for two-proton events: 0.3 GeV/c < p_recoil < 0.8 GeV/c
- ρ⁰ mass selection window: 0.57 GeV/c² < M_π⁺π⁻ < 0.97 GeV/c²
- Projected p_miss resolution: 30–50 MeV/c over the full kinematic range
- Data collected: 16.4 billion triggers on deuterium (4 days), 29.5 billion on helium (10 days), 46.7 billion on carbon (14 days)
- GCF predictions for the ratio σ[C(γ, ρ⁰ pp)]/σ[C(γ, ρ⁰ p)] show clear separation between tensor-inclusive potentials (AV18, χEFT N2LO) and the tensor-less AV4' potential, with statistical uncertainties small enough to distinguish the models

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Contact Formalism framework, previously applied to electron scattering data from CLAS (2020) and Jefferson Lab Hall A (2014), to photo-production reactions for the first time. It directly tests the probe-independence prediction of GCF, which has otherwise only been compared to proton-carbon scattering results from Patsyuk (2021). The approach enables validation of np-dominance in SRCs using a different probe than the electron scattering measurements from Subedi (2008) and the Jefferson Lab Hall A collaboration (2007, 2014), and avoids the technical challenge of neutron detection by using the two-proton to one-proton event ratio. The experiment also enables searches for Axion-Like Particles and studies of gluonic form factors, as noted by Pybus (2023, 2024).
