# Parton correlations effects in double parton distribution functions

> Double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), measurable in hadron-hadron collisions and encoding information on how partons inside a proton are correlated among each other, could represent a new tool to explore the three dimensional partonic structure of hadrons. In the present contribution, results of the calculations of dPFDs are presented. Phenomenological calculations of experimental observables, sensitive to dPDFs are also discussed showing how double parton correlations could be estimated in the next LHC run.

## Key claim

Double parton correlations (DPC) in double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) survive perturbative QCD evolution to high momentum transfer and remain sizable even in the small-\(x\) region for gluon-gluon distributions, contrary to the common factorized ansatz that neglects all correlations; the authors demonstrate that a clear signature of DPC could be measured in the next LHC run via the dependence of \(\sigma_{eff}\) on the product of muon rapidities.

## Method

The authors calculate dPDFs at a low hadronic scale using a Light-Front (LF) constituent quark model (CQM), then evolve them to high momentum transfer (\(Q^2 = 250\) GeV\(^2\)) using perturbative QCD (pQCD) evolution equations for dPDFs. They define ratios \(ratio_{ab}\), \(ratio_{ab}^P\), and \(ratio_{ab}^{NP}\) to disentangle perturbative from non-perturbative correlations, and compute the effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}\) and the DPS cross section for same-sign \(W\) pair production, fixing the initial scale \(Q_0^2\) by matching single \(W\) production cross sections to the DYNNLO code with MSTW PDFs.

## Key numbers

* The total same-sign \(W\) pair DPS cross section (sum over both charges, decaying to same-sign muons) is \(\sigma^{++}+\sigma^{--} = 0.69 \pm 0.18 (\delta \mu_F)^{+0.12}_{-0.16}(\delta Q_0^2)\) fb, calculated at a central initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.26\) GeV\(^2\).
* The mean effective cross section for this process is \(\langle \widetilde{\sigma}_{eff} \rangle = 21.04 \pm 0.07 (\delta Q^2_0)^{+0.06}_{-0.07}(\delta \mu_F)\) mb.
* The constant value \(\sigma_{eff} \simeq 15\) mb is compatible with data within errors under the fully uncorrelated ansatz for dPDFs.
* With a luminosity of \(\mathcal{L} \sim 1000\) fb\(^{-1}\), the departure of \(\sigma_{eff}\) from a constant value can be measured at 68% confidence level in the next LHC run.

## Context

This work extends earlier CQM calculations of dPDFs by Rinaldi et al. (2014, 2015) and Traini et al. (2015) that showed correlations at the hadronic scale, and contradicts the fully factorized ansatz (Eq. 1) commonly used in experimental analyses. The results enable experimental searches for double parton correlations at the LHC, specifically through binning \(\sigma_{eff}\) in the product of muon rapidities for same-sign \(W\) pair production, and are consistent with calculations using the AdS/QCD correspondence and with the prescription of Blok et al. (2011).
