# A model-free procedure to correct for volume fluctuations in E-by-E analyses of particle multiplicities

> We develop an innovative and unbiased procedure, based on event mixing, to account for unavoidable contributions from volume (or system size) fluctuations to experimentally measured moments of particle multiplicity distributions produced in relativistic nuclear collisions. Within the wounded-nucleon model they are characterized by fluctuations of the number of wounded nucleons, the latter usually referred to as participants. For the first time we extract participant fluctuations directly from the data used for the fluctuation analysis, i.e., without involving model calculations. To achieve this we constructed a dedicated event-mixing algorithm that eliminates all possible correlations between produced particles while preserving the volume fluctuations. The procedure provides direct access to the cumulants of wounded-nucleon distributions, which can be used to account for non-critical contributions to the experimentally measured cumulants of multiplicity distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-free procedure to extract participant (wounded-nucleon) fluctuations directly from experimental data using event mixing, eliminating the need for Glauber Monte Carlo or transport model calculations that introduce biases, particularly at low collision energies.

## Method

The authors construct mixed events by taking the total charged-particle multiplicity from a real event, then randomly selecting one charged track from that event and taking each remaining particle from a different subsequent event, one per event. This procedure preserves the original wounded-nucleon fluctuations because the total number of charged particles is kept identical between real and mixed events, while eliminating all correlations between particles. The cumulants of the wounded-nucleon distribution are then extracted from the mixed-event covariances between different particle species (Eq. mix_3) and from single-particle cumulants (Eq. mix_4).

## Key numbers

- The method is tested on IQMD transport model events for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV
- Analysis is performed in four centrality classes: 0-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, and 30-40%
- Charged particles are selected within kinematic region |y| < 0.4 and 0.4 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c
- Five particle species are used: proton (p), pion (pi), deuteron (d), triton (t), and He-3
- Covariances are reconstructed for 10 different particle pairs: [p, He-3], [p, d], [p, t], [p, pi], [He-3, d], [He-3, t], [He-3, pi], [d, t], [d, pi], [t, pi]
- The strongly intensive fluctuation measure Sigma[A,B] becomes unity for all particle pairs in mixed events, confirming elimination of correlations
- Normalized second-order cumulants of participants kappa_2(N_W)/<N_W>^2 extracted from covariances and single-particle cumulants show reasonable agreement across particle species

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of volume fluctuations in event-by-event analyses of particle multiplicities in relativistic nuclear collisions, which prevents direct comparison of experimental cumulants with theoretical susceptibilities from Eq. chi. Prior approaches relied on model-dependent input: Braun-Munzinger (2016) used Glauber Monte Carlo to obtain participant distributions, Gorenstein (2011) introduced strongly intensive quantities assuming independent particle production, and Esumi (2020) used unfolding algorithms also requiring model calculations. The method is essential for low-energy collisions at GSI/SIS and BNL/RHIC where Glauber models are known to introduce biases (STAR 2021), but is also applicable at LHC energies. It enables direct extraction of any higher-order cumulant of the participant distribution from data alone.
