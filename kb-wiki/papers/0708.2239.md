# Extension of thermonuclear functions through the pathway model including Maxwell-Boltzmann and Tsallis distributions

> The Maxwell-Boltzmannian approach to nuclear reaction rate theory is extended to cover Tsallis statistics (Tsallis, 1988) and more general cases of distribution functions. An analytical study of respective thermonuclear functions is being conducted with the help of statistical techniques. The pathway model, recently introduced by Mathai (2005), is utilized for thermonuclear functions and closed-form representations are obtained in terms of H-functions and G-functions. Maxwell-Boltzmannian thermonuclear functions become particular cases of the extended thermonuclear functions. A brief review on the development of the theory of analytic representations of nuclear reaction rates is given.

## Key claim

The paper extends the standard Maxwell-Boltzmannian theory of nuclear reaction rates to cover Tsallis statistics (Tsallis, 1988) and more general distribution functions by using the pathway model introduced by Mathai (2005), with the condition that the Maxwell-Boltzmann case is recovered when the pathway parameter α approaches 1.

## Method

The authors replace the exponential factor e^{-ax} in the standard thermonuclear function integrals with the pathway model expressions [1+a(α-1)x]^{-1/(α-1)} for α > 1 and [1-a(1-α)x]^{1/(1-α)} for α < 1, then evaluate the resulting integrals using Mellin transform and Mellin convolution techniques. Closed-form representations of the extended thermonuclear functions are obtained in terms of Fox's H-functions and Meijer's G-functions for the standard non-resonant case and the high-energy cut-off case.

## Key numbers

- The pathway parameter α > 1 yields the generalized type-2 beta model; α < 1 yields the generalized type-1 beta model with right tail cut-off; α → 1 recovers the Maxwell-Boltzmann exponential form e^{-ax}.
- For the standard non-resonant thermonuclear function with Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, the collision probability integral is I_1(ν,1,b,1/2) = ∫_0^∞ x^ν e^{-x-bx^{-1/2}} dx, where b = (μ/(2kT))^{1/2} (z_i z_j e^2)/ℏ.
- For the high energy cut-off case, the integral is I_2^{(d)}(ν,1,b,1/2) = ∫_0^d x^ν e^{-x-bx^{-1/2}} dx with cut-off d.
- The extended integral I_{1α} for α > 1 is expressed as (1/(ρ[a(α-1)]^γ Γ(1/(α-1)))) H_{1,2}^{2,1}(a(α-1)b^{1/ρ} |^{(1-1/(α-1)+γ,1)}_{(γ,1)(0,1/ρ)}).
- When 1/ρ = m is an integer, I_{1α} reduces to (√m (2π)^{(1-m)/2} / ([a(α-1)]^γ Γ(1/(α-1)))) G_{1,m+1}^{m+1,1}(a(α-1)b^m/m^m |^{1-1/(α-1)+γ}_{0,1/m,...,(m-1)/m,γ}).
- For the thermonuclear function with ρ = 1/2 (the Gamow peak case), I_{1α} becomes (1/([(α-1)]^{ν+1} Γ(1/(α-1)))) G_{1,3}^{3,1}((α-1)b^2/4 |^{1-1/(α-1)+ν+1}_{0,1/2,ν+1}).

## Context

This work extends the prior development of analytic representations of nuclear reaction rates by Haubold and Mathai (1984, 1998) and Anderson, Haubold, and Mathai (1994), and builds on the initial attempt to extend reaction rate theory to Tsallis statistics by Saxena, Mathai, and Haubold (2004). The paper addresses recent questions about possible deviations from Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions in solar fusion plasmas due to memory effects and long-range forces, raised by Coraddu et al. (1999), Lavagno and Quarati (2002), and Lissia and Quarati (2005). The results enable closed-form evaluation of thermonuclear functions for Tsallis statistics and more general distributions characterized by the pathway parameter, which can be applied to stellar nucleosynthesis, cosmological nucleosynthesis, and controlled fusion studies.
