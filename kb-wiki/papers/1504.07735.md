# Resonant states of neutron-rich $Λ$ hypernucleus $^7_Λ$He

> The structure of neutron-rich $Λ$ hypernucleus, $^7_Λ$He is studied within the framework of an $α+Λ+n+n$ four-body cluster model. We predict second $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states, corresponding to a $0s$ $Λ$ coupled to the second $2^+$ state of $^6$He, as narrow resonant states with widths $Γ\sim 1$ MeV to be at 0.03 MeV and 0.07 MeV respect to the $α+Λ+n+n$ threshold. From an estimation of the differential cross section for the $^7{\rm Li} (γ,K^+) ^7_Λ$He reaction, there is a possibility to observe these state at JLab in the future. We also calculate the second $2^+$ state of $^6$He as resonant state within the framework of an $α+n+n$ three-body cluster model. Our result is $2.81$ MeV with $Γ=$4.63 MeV with respect to the $α+n+n$ threshold. This energy position is $\sim 1$ MeV higher, and with a much broader decay width, than the recent SPIRAL data. It is suggested that an experiment at JLab to search for the second $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states of $^7_Λ$He would provide an opportunity to confirm the second $2^+$ state of the core nucleus $^6$He.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the second $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states of $^7_\Lambda$He exist as narrow resonant states with widths of about 1 MeV, located just 0.03 MeV and 0.07 MeV above the $\alpha+\Lambda+n+n$ threshold, but this prediction depends on the existence and properties of the second $2^+$ state of the core nucleus $^6$He, which the authors calculate to be at 2.81 MeV with a width of 4.63 MeV, about 1 MeV higher and much broader than the recent SPIRAL experimental data.

## Method

The authors employed an $\alpha+n+n$ three-body cluster model for $^6$He and an $\alpha+\Lambda+n+n$ four-body cluster model for $^7_\Lambda$He, using Gaussian basis functions with ranges in geometric progression. They applied the complex scaling method (CSM) to obtain resonant energies and decay widths by solving the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation, where the boundary condition for many-body outgoing waves is automatically satisfied. The two-body interactions (AV8 $NN$, $\alpha$-$N$, $\alpha$-$\Lambda$, and $\Lambda$-$N$) were chosen to reproduce the observed properties of all subsystems composed of two and three subunits, without including any charge-symmetry breaking component in the $\Lambda N$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- Second $2^+$ state of $^6$He: resonance energy $E_r = 2.81$ MeV above the $\alpha+n+n$ threshold with decay width $\Gamma = 4.63$ MeV
- First $2^+$ state of $^6$He: $E_r = 0.96$ MeV with $\Gamma = 0.14$ MeV
- $1^+$ state of $^6$He: $E_r = 3.00$ MeV with $\Gamma = 5.22$ MeV
- Second $3/2^+$ state of $^7_\Lambda$He: $E_r = 0.03$ MeV above the $\alpha+\Lambda+n+n$ threshold with $\Gamma/2 = 0.56$ MeV (total width $\sim 1$ MeV)
- Second $5/2^+$ state of $^7_\Lambda$He: $E_r = 0.07$ MeV above the $\alpha+\Lambda+n+n$ threshold with $\Gamma/2 = 0.51$ MeV (total width $\sim 1$ MeV)
- Ground state ($1/2^+$) of $^7_\Lambda$He: binding energy $E = -6.39$ MeV relative to the $\alpha+\Lambda+n+n$ threshold, corresponding to $\Lambda$-separation energy $B_\Lambda = 5.36$ MeV
- First $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states of $^7_\Lambda$He: bound states at $E = -4.73$ MeV and $E = -4.65$ MeV, respectively, with average $\Lambda$-separation energy $B_\Lambda = 3.66$ MeV
- Predicted differential cross sections for $^7{\rm Li}(\gamma,K^+)^7_\Lambda$He at $E_\gamma^{\rm Lab}=1.5$ GeV and $\theta_K^{\rm Lab}=7$ deg: 49.0 nb/sr for the ground state, 21.6 nb/sr for the first doublet ($3/2^+_1+5/2^+_1$), and 7.7 nb/sr for the second doublet ($3/2^+_2+5/2^+_2$)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-body cluster model calculations for $^7_\Lambda$He (Hiyama 2009) from the bound-state region into the resonant region using the complex scaling method. The predicted second $2^+$ state of $^6$He at 2.81 MeV with $\Gamma=4.63$ MeV is about 1 MeV higher and much broader than the SPIRAL experiment result (Mougeot 2012) which suggested a state at 1.63 MeV with $\Gamma=1.6$ MeV, and is consistent with earlier COSM calculations by Myo (2011) that gave 2.52 MeV with $\Gamma=3.87$ MeV. The predicted narrow resonant states of $^7_\Lambda$He, if confirmed by future JLab experiments, would demonstrate the gluelike role of the $\Lambda$ particle in stabilizing neutron-rich hypernuclei and provide an opportunity to confirm the controversial second $2^+$ state of $^6$He.
