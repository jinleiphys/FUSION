# Pion-induced Drell-Yan processes and the flavor-dependent EMC effect

> Pion-induced Drell-Yan processes are proposed as a potential tool to measure the flavor dependence of the EMC effect, that is, the flavor-dependent modification of quark distributions in the nuclear medium. Existing pionic Drell-Yan data are compared with calculations using a recent model for nuclear quark distributions that incorporates flavor-dependent nuclear effects. While no firm conclusions can yet be drawn, we demonstrate that existing Drell-Yan data seem to imply a flavor dependence of the EMC effect. We highlight how pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments on nuclear targets can access important new aspects of the EMC effect, not probed in deep inelastic scattering, and will therefore provide very stringent constrains for models of nuclear quark distributions. Predictions for possible future pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments are also presented.

## Key claim

Existing pion-induced Drell-Yan data seem to imply a flavor dependence of the EMC effect, but no firm conclusions can yet be drawn because the data are not sufficiently accurate to definitively distinguish between flavor-dependent and flavor-independent nuclear parton distribution models.

## Method

The authors compared existing pion-induced Drell-Yan cross-section data from the NA10, NA3, and Omega collaborations with calculations using the Cloet-Bentz-Thomas (CBT) model, which incorporates flavor-dependent nuclear quark distributions via the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with isovector-vector mean fields. They computed three Drell-Yan cross-section ratios (π⁺/π⁻ on a nucleus, π⁻ on nucleus relative to deuteron, and π⁻ on nucleus relative to hydrogen) using the full leading-order Drell-Yan cross-section formula with nuclear PDFs from the CBT model and GRV-P LO pion PDFs, then compared these predictions to existing data and made predictions for future COMPASS experiments.

## Key numbers

- NA10 data for σ^{DY}(π⁻+W)/σ^{DY}(π⁻+D) were collected at P_beam = 286 GeV and 140 GeV, with calculations performed at P_beam = 286 GeV and Q² = 25 GeV² (the approximate mean Q² of the experiment)
- NA3 data for σ^{DY}(π⁻+Pt)/σ^{DY}(π⁻+H) were collected using a 150 GeV π⁻ beam over Q² range 16.8 ≤ Q² ≤ 70.6 GeV², with calculations at P_beam = 150 GeV and Q² = 25 GeV²
- Omega collaboration data for σ^{DY}(π⁺+W)/σ^{DY}(π⁻+W) were collected with 39.5 GeV pions over Q² ranges 5.3 ≤ Q² ≤ 7.3 GeV² and 16 ≤ Q² ≤ 25 GeV², with calculations at Q² = 7 GeV² and Q² = 20 GeV²
- Predictions for COMPASS are made for a 160 GeV pion beam at Q² = 25 GeV², with calculations shown as a function of x₂ for fixed dimuon mass of 5 GeV and as a function of dimuon mass at fixed x₁ = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the study of the EMC effect, first discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (1983) in deep inelastic scattering, into the flavor-dependent regime using Drell-Yan processes. The CBT model (Cloet, Bentz, and Thomas, 2005) predicts distinct nuclear modifications for u and d quarks in N≠Z nuclei due to isovector-vector mean fields, unlike the Q²-rescaling model (Close, Roberts, and Ross, 1985) which predicts no flavor dependence, or the pion-excess model which predicts flavor dependence from different isospin composition. The paper shows that pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments can probe aspects of the EMC effect not accessible in inclusive DIS, and that existing global analyses of nuclear PDFs (Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado, 1999; Kumano, 1998) have not included pion-induced Drell-Yan data. Future precise measurements at COMPASS could distinguish between flavor-dependent and flavor-independent models and help identify the origins of the EMC effect.
