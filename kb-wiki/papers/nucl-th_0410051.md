# Three-Body Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. In its simplest form the Faddeev equation for identical bosons, which we are using, is a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables, magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. This equation is solved through Pade summation. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon-type potential, the numerical feasibility and stability of the algorithm for solving the Faddeev equation is demonstrated. Special attention is given to the selection of independent variables and the treatment of three-body break-up singularities with a spline based method. The elastic differential cross section, semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections and total cross sections of both elastic and breakup processes in the intermediate energy range up to about 1 GeV are calculated and the convergence of the multiple scattering series is investigated in every case. In general a truncation in the first or second order in the two-body t-matrix is quite insufficient.

## Key claim

The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering can be solved directly in momentum space using vector variables without partial wave decomposition, and the numerical algorithm is feasible and stable for intermediate energies up to about 1 GeV, though the authors note that a truncation of the multiple scattering series in the first or second order in the two-body t-matrix is quite insufficient.

## Method

The Faddeev equation for three identical bosons was formulated as a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables (magnitudes of relative momenta and angles) and solved through Padé summation. A Malfliet-Tjon-type potential was employed, and the three-body break-up singularities were treated using a spline-based method with cubic Hermite splines for quasi-analytic integration of logarithmic singularities. The elastic differential cross section, semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections, and total cross sections for both elastic and breakup processes were calculated, and the convergence of the multiple scattering series was investigated.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energy range: 0.2 to 1 GeV (intermediate energy regime)
- Total energy in the center of mass frame: \( E = E_d + \frac{3}{4m}q^2_0 = E_d + \frac{2}{3}E_{lab} \)
- The break-up singularity region in the \( q \)-\( q'' \)-plane is bounded by \( q_+ = \frac{q}{2} + \sqrt{Q^2_0 - \frac{3}{4}q^2} \) and \( q_- = -\frac{q}{2} + \sqrt{Q^2_0 - \frac{3}{4}q^2} \) for \( q < Q_0 \), where \( Q_0 = \sqrt{mE} \)
- The maximum \( q \) for which singularities occur is \( q_{max} = \sqrt{\frac{4m}{3}E} \)
- The five-fold differential break-up cross section in the c.m. frame: \( \frac{d^{5}\sigma_{br}}{d\Omega_{p}d\Omega_{q}dq} = \frac{(2\pi)^{4}m^{2}}{3q_{0}}q^{2}\sqrt{mE-\frac{3}{4}q^{2}} \; |U_0|^2 \)
- The optical theorem: \( \sigma_{tot} = \sigma_{el} + \sigma_{br} = -\frac{4m(2\pi)^{3}}{3q_{0}} \mathrm{Im} U(q_0, 1) \)

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space Faddeev approach beyond the low-energy regime (below about 200 MeV) where partial-wave-decomposed calculations have been highly successful for three-nucleon scattering with modern two- and three-nucleon forces, as summarized in reviews by Glöckle et al. (1996) and Witała et al. (1998). The authors had previously demonstrated the vector-variable approach for bound states (Liu et al., 2003) and for the first-order term of the Faddeev equation in neutron-deuteron breakup up to 500 MeV (Elster et al., 2003). This work enables full three-body scattering calculations at intermediate energies without the proliferation of partial waves, and the authors note that no comparable work at higher energies existed for comparison at the time of publication.
