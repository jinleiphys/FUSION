# Fission and cluster decay of $^{76}$Sr nucleus in the ground-state and formed in heavy-ion reactions

> Calculations for fission and cluster decay of $^{76}Sr$ are presented for this nucleus to be in its ground-state or formed as an excited compound system in heavy-ion reactions. The predicted mass distribution, for the dynamical collective mass transfer process assumed for fission of $^{76}Sr$, is clearly asymmetric, favouring $α$-nuclei. Cluster decay is studied within a preformed cluster model, both for ground-state to ground-state decays and from excited compound system to the ground-state(s) or excited states(s) of the fragments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the superdeformed nucleus $^{76}$Sr, while naturally stable (negative Q-value) against only light clusters with masses $A_2<12$, has calculated cluster decay half-lives for $A_2\ge 12$ that are also large enough ($T_{1/2}>10^{80}$ s) to term the nucleus as stable against all cluster decays; this stability is attributed to stable deformed shell closures at Z=N=38.

## Method

The authors apply the quantum mechanical fragmentation theory (QMFT) to treat fission as a collective mass transfer process, solving a stationary Schrödinger equation in the mass asymmetry coordinate $\eta$ at the touching configuration of two nuclei. For cluster decay, they use the preformed cluster model (PCM) where the decay half-life is given by $\lambda = P_0 \nu P$, with $P_0$ the cluster preformation probability from the QMFT solution, $\nu$ the assault frequency, and $P$ the WKB tunnelling probability. Calculations are performed for $^{76}$Sr in its ground-state and as an excited compound system, with nuclear temperature effects included via a Boltzmann-like function and temperature-dependent shell corrections.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation of $^{76}$Sr: $\beta_2 = 0.44$
- Fissility parameter for $^{76}$Sr: $x = 0.38$ (compared to $x_{BG}=0.396$ for $\ell=0$)
- Calculated cluster decay half-lives for $A_2\ge 12$: $T_{1/2} > 10^{80}$ s
- Excitation energy studied for fission: $E^* \approx 32$ MeV (corresponding to nuclear temperature $\theta = 2$ MeV)
- Coulomb barrier for $^{36}$Ar + $^{40}$Ca system: $V_c = 53.6$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the QMFT and PCM approaches previously applied to light systems (Gupta and collaborators, 1984) and to the superdeformed nucleus $^{80}$Zr (Puri and Gupta, 1992). The study is motivated by experiments on $^{78}$Sr, $^{80}$Zr, and $^{83}$Kr (Sobotka et al., 1984; Evans et al., 1989) which show asymmetric or symmetric mass distributions depending on fissility relative to the Businaro-Gallone transition point (Businaro and Gallone, 1955). The calculations suggest that to obtain complete fission products for $^{76}$Sr, experiments should use at least double the Coulomb barrier energy and possibly inverse kinematics, enabling future comparison with data and helping distinguish between fission and cluster decay mechanisms.
