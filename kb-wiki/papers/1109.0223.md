# Charge radii and neutron correlations in helium halo nuclei

> Within the complex-energy configuration interaction framework, we study correlations of valence neutrons to explain the behavior of charge radii in the neutron halo nuclei $^{6,8}$He. We find that the experimentally observed decrease of the charge radius between $^6$He and $^8$He is caused by a subtle interplay between three effects: dineutron correlations, a spin-orbit contribution to the charge radius, and a core swelling effect. We demonstrate that two-neutron angular correlations in the $2^+_1$ resonance of $^6$He differ markedly from the ground-state correlations in $^{6,8}$He.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed decrease of the charge radius between $^6$He and $^8$He is caused by a subtle interplay between three effects: dineutron correlations, a spin-orbit contribution to the charge radius, and a core swelling effect; the reduction is not due to a more "democratic" arrangement of neutrons around the core but rather to the reduction of the amplitude of the dineutron configuration in the ground-state wave function.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), a complex-energy configuration interaction framework using a Berggren single-particle basis consisting of the $0p_{3/2}$ Gamow resonance and non-resonant $psd$ scattering continua. They assumed a $^4$He core with valence neutrons interacting via a modified finite-range Minnesota (MN) central potential, with Gaussian strengths adjusted to reproduce experimental ground-state energies of $^{6,8}$He. Two-neutron angular correlations were assessed through the two-body density $\rho_{nn}(r,r',\theta)$, and charge radii were computed by combining GSM results for valence neutron distributions with core swelling corrections taken from Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Charge radius of $^6$He: 2.059(7) fm (experimental, from revaluated isotopic shift measurements); GSM prediction: 2.026 fm
- Charge radius of $^8$He: 1.959(16) fm (experimental); GSM prediction: 1.961 fm
- Charge radius of $^4$He: 1.681(4) fm (experimental)
- Spin-orbit contribution to charge radius: $\langle r^2 \rangle_{so} = -0.0718$ fm$^2$ for $^6$He and $-0.158$ fm$^2$ for $^8$He
- $2^+_1$ resonance in $^6$He: predicted energy 851 keV (experimental 822 keV) and width $\Gamma = 109$ keV (experimental 113 keV)
- $3/2^-$ ground-state resonance of $^7$He: predicted energy $-0.707$ MeV (experimental $-0.528$ MeV)
- Average opening angle between valence neutrons: $68^\circ$ in $^6$He, $78^\circ$ in $^8$He
- Core swelling: GFMC predicts 4.58% increase of $\alpha$-particle proton point radius in $^6$He and 6.66% increase in $^8$He

## Context

This work extends earlier Gamow Shell Model calculations of helium halos (Michel et al., various years) and provides a physical interpretation of charge radius measurements from Wang and Mueller (2004) and Brodeur (2011). The results are consistent with ab initio GFMC calculations (Pieper and Wiringa, 2001) and No-Core Shell Model calculations (Navratil, 2007). The study demonstrates the Pairing-Anti Halo (PAH) effect in a configuration-interaction framework for the first time, building on prior work by Karim (1997), Yamagami (2005), and Hagino (2007). The findings enable future studies of how continuum coupling and pairing correlations affect nuclear sizes near particle thresholds.
