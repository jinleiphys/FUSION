# Model-independent aspects of the reaction $\bar{K} + N \to K + Ξ$

> Various model-independent aspects of the $\bar{K} N \to K Ξ$ reaction are investigated, starting from the determination of the most general structure of the reaction amplitude for $Ξ$ baryons with $J^P=\frac12^\pm$ and $\frac32^\pm$ and the observables that allow a complete determination of these amplitudes. Polarization observables are constructed in terms of spin-density matrix elements. Reflection symmetry about the reaction plane is exploited, in particular, to determine the parity of the produced $Ξ$ in a model-independent way. In addition, extending the work of Biagi $\mathrm{\textit{et al. } [Z. Phys.\ C \textbf{34}, 175 (1987)]}$, a way is presented of determining simultaneously the spin and parity of the ground state of $Ξ$ baryon as well as those of the excited $Ξ$ states.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent framework for determining the spin and parity of ground-state and excited \(\Xi\) baryons produced in the \(\bar{K} N \to K \Xi\) reaction, using reflection symmetry about the reaction plane and spin-density matrix elements, without relying on any particular dynamical model.

## Method

The authors derived the most general spin structure of the reaction amplitude for \(\Xi\) baryons with \(J^P = \frac12^\pm\) and \(\frac32^\pm\) using the method of Nakayama and Lee (2005). They expressed polarization observables in terms of spin-density matrix elements and exploited reflection symmetry about the reaction plane to relate observables to the parity of the produced \(\Xi\). The spin-density matrix elements were then linked to helicity amplitudes and to measurable moments from the weak decay of the \(\Xi\) into \(\Lambda \bar{K}\).

## Key numbers

* The yield of \(\Xi\) in the hadronic \(\bar{K}\)-induced reaction is of the order of \(\mu\)b, whereas the yield in photoproduction is of the order of nb (from the introduction, citing FMMR83 and CLAS07b).
* For a \(\Xi\) with \(J^P = \frac12^\pm\), the spin-transfer coefficient \(K_{yy}\) equals the parity \(\pi_\Xi\) of the produced \(\Xi\) (i.e., \(K_{yy} = \pi_\Xi\)), providing a model-independent parity determination.
* For a \(\Xi\) with \(J^P = \frac12^\pm\), the single-polarization observables satisfy \(T_y = \pi_\Xi \, P_y\).
* For a \(\Xi\) with \(J^P = \frac12^\pm\), the double-polarization observables satisfy \(K_{xx} = \pi_\Xi \, K_{zz}\) and \(K_{xz} = -\pi_\Xi \, K_{zx}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Biagi et al. (1987) by providing a method to determine simultaneously the spin and parity of both the ground-state and excited \(\Xi\) baryons. It is motivated by upcoming experimental programs at JLab (CLAS Collaboration with the 12-GeV machine), J-PARC, and FAIR (PANDA), which will produce new data on \(\Xi\) and \(\Omega\) baryons. The model-independent analysis complements existing model-dependent effective-Lagrangian studies by some of the same authors (Nakayama et al., 2006; Oh et al., 2011) and provides tools to analyze forthcoming data without theoretical bias.
