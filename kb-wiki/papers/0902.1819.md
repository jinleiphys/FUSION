# Neutral pion photoproduction at high energies

> A Regge model with absorptive corrections is employed in a global analysis of the world data on the reactions gamma p -> pi0 p and gamma n -> pi0 n for photon energies from 3 to 18 GeV. In this region resonance contributions are expected to be negligible so that the available experimental information on differential cross sections and single- and double polarization observables at -t < 2 GeV^2 allows us to determine the non-resonant part of the reaction amplitude reliably. The model amplitude is then used to predict observables for photon energies below 3 GeV. A detailed comparison with recent data from the CLAS and CB-ELSA Collaborations in that energy region is presented. Furthermore, the prospects for determining the pi0 radiative decay width via the Primakoff effect from the reaction gamma p -> pi0 p are explored.

## Key claim

A Regge model with absorptive corrections, comprising pole and cut amplitudes for ρ, ω, and b₁ exchanges, can reliably determine the non-resonant part of the neutral pion photoproduction amplitude at photon energies from 3 to 18 GeV, and then predict observables below 3 GeV where resonance contributions may appear; the authors note that the model's applicability at lower energies is phenomenological rather than rigorously derived.

## Method

The authors constructed a gauge-invariant Regge model with absorptive corrections, parameterizing t-channel parity-conserving helicity amplitudes F₁, F₂, and F₃ (neglecting F₄) for ρ, ω, and b₁ exchanges, including both Regge pole and cut amplitudes. Free parameters (coupling constants, trajectory intercepts, and cut-off parameters) were fixed by a global fit to world data on differential cross sections and single- and double-polarization observables for γp → π⁰p at photon energies 3–18 GeV and −t ≤ 2 GeV², achieving χ²/dof = 1.4. The fitted amplitude was then used to predict observables at photon energies below 3 GeV, compared with CLAS and CB-ELSA data, and extended to explore the Primakoff effect for determining the π⁰ radiative decay width on a proton target.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range for the global fit: 3 to 18 GeV, with restriction −t ≤ 2 GeV².
- χ²/dof of the global fit: 1.4.
- Intercept of the ω-trajectory from the fit: α_ω = 0.641 ± 0.003.
- ρ-trajectory parameters: α_ρ = 0.53 + 0.8 t (intercept 0.53, slope 0.8 GeV⁻²).
- b₁-trajectory parameters: α_{b₁} = 0.51 + 0.8 t (intercept 0.51, slope 0.8 GeV⁻²).
- Pomeron slope used for cut amplitudes: α'_P = 0.2 GeV⁻².
- Dip in differential cross section observed at t ≈ −0.5 GeV², becoming more pronounced with increasing photon energy.
- Polarized photon asymmetry Σ drops to ≈ 0.7 at t ≈ −0.5 GeV².
- The Primakoff interference region (|t| < 0.04 GeV²) was omitted from the fit to fix the F₁ amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Regge-model analysis of charged pion photoproduction (Sibirtsev 2007) to neutral pions, where ρ, ω, and b₁ exchanges contribute rather than pion exchange. The model predictions are compared with recent data from the CLAS Collaboration (Dugger) and CB-ELSA Collaboration (Bartholomy, Pee) at energies 2–3 GeV, and with the GWU partial-wave analysis (Arndt, Arndt1, Arndt2, Arndt08) from SAID. The study re-examines the prospects for determining the π⁰ radiative decay width via the Primakoff effect on a proton target, following earlier work by Laget, and addresses complications from pion absorption and interference effects that affect nuclear-target experiments such as PrimEx at JLab (Gasparian).
