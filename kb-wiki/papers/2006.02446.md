# Stellar Signatures of Inhomogeneous Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We evaluate abundance anomalies generated in patches of the universe where the baryon-to-photon ratio was locally enhanced by possibly many orders of magnitude in the range $η= 10^{-10} - 10^{-1}$. Our study is motivated by the possible survival of rare dense regions in the early universe, the most extreme of which, above a critical threshold, collapsed to form primordial black holes. If this occurred, one may expect there to also be a significant population of early-forming stars that formed in similar but subthreshold patches. We derive a range of element abundance signatures by performing BBN simulations at high values of the baryon-to-photon ratio that may be detectable in any surviving first generation stars of around a solar mass. Our predictions apply to metal-poor galactic halo stars, to old globular star clusters and to dwarf galaxies, and we compare with observations in each of these cases.

## Key claim

The paper argues that rare patches of the early universe with locally enhanced baryon-to-photon ratios (η = 10⁻¹⁰ – 10⁻¹) could produce element abundance anomalies in surviving first-generation stars of around a solar mass, but only if such patches existed as subthreshold fluctuations that failed to collapse into primordial black holes.

## Method

The authors used the public BBN code AlterBBN, updated to include the full REACLIB nuclear reaction grid, to compute element abundances for baryon-to-photon ratios η ranging from 10⁻¹⁵ to 10¹. They reduced the nuclear grid from several thousand nuclei to approximately 800 by removing nuclei whose abundance relative to hydrogen never exceeded 10⁻¹² during BBN for any η value, then further identified 132 nuclei (54 chemical elements) with abundances [X]/[H] ≥ 10⁻¹⁰ for some η. Results were limited to η < 0.1 due to numerical convergence issues at higher values.

## Key numbers

- The standard (Planck) baryon-to-photon ratio is η₀ = (6.104 ± 0.058) × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The explored η range corresponds to η = 1 – 10⁹ η₀.
- Enhanced ⁴He abundance occurs in the range Yp ≈ 0.3 – 0.5, corresponding to η ≈ 10⁻⁷ – 10⁻¹.
- At η = 10⁻⁴, overproduced elements have atomic number Z ≈ 60; at η = 10⁻¹, overproduction concerns Z ≈ 25.
- The baryon content within the particle horizon at BBN onset is approximately 100 M☉.
- The required baryon overdensity for an isocurvature fluctuation at BBN is at least (1+z_BBN)/(1+z_eq) ≈ 10⁵ η₀.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous BBN, which were limited to η ≲ 10⁻³ (Matsuo et al., 2004; Nakamura et al., 2010, 2013), by exploring much higher η values motivated by primordial black hole formation scenarios (Carr, 2018). The predictions are compared with observations of metal-poor halo stars (Marino, 2019), dwarf spheroidal galaxies (Reichert, 2020), and r-process enhanced ultrafaint dwarfs Reticulum II (Ji, 2016) and Tucana III (2019). The results enable searches for primordial abundance anomalies that could distinguish inhomogeneous BBN signatures from conventional stellar nucleosynthesis in the oldest stellar populations.
