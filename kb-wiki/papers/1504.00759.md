# Random Matrix Theory for Transition Strength Densities in Finite Quantum Systems: Results from Embedded Unitary Ensembles

> Embedded random matrix ensembles are generic models for describing statistical properties of finite isolated interacting quantum many-particle systems. For the simplest spinless systems, with say $m$ particles in $N$ single particle states and interacting via $k$-body interactions, we have EGUE($k$) and the embedding algebra is $U(N)$. A finite quantum system, induced by a transition operator, makes transitions from its states to the states of the same system or to those of another system. Examples are electromagnetic transitions (same initial and final systems), nuclear beta and double beta decay (different initial and final systems), particle addition to/removal from a given system and so on. Towards developing a complete statistical theory for transition strength densities, we have derived formulas for lower order bivariate moments of the strength densities generated by a variety of transition operators. For a spinless fermion system, using EGUE($k$) representation for Hamiltonian and an independent EGUE($t$) representation for transition operator, finite-$N$ formulas for moments up to order four are derived, for the first time, for the transition strength densities. Formulas for the moments up to order four are also derived for systems with two types of spinless fermions and a transition operator similar to beta decay and neutrinoless beta decay operators. Moments formulas are also derived for transition operator that removes $k_0$ number of particles from $m$ fermion system. Numerical results obtained using the exact formulas for two-body ($k=2$) Hamiltonians (in some examples for $k=3,4$) and the asymptotic formulas clearly establish that in general the smoothed form of the bivariate transition strength densities take bivariate Gaussian form for isolated finite quantum systems. Extensions of these results to bosonic systems and EGUE ensembles with further symmetries are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives, for the first time, exact finite-\(N\) formulas for bivariate moments up to order four of transition strength densities in spinless fermion systems, using independent embedded Gaussian unitary ensembles (EGUE) for both the Hamiltonian (with \(k\)-body interactions) and the transition operator (with \(t\)-body interactions). The smoothed form of these strength densities is shown to be bivariate Gaussian for isolated finite quantum systems, but this conclusion is established numerically for specific examples (primarily \(k=2\), with some \(k=3,4\)) and relies on the asymptotic limit formulas.

## Method

The authors employ the \(U(N)\) Wigner-Racah algebra of the embedding Lie algebra to derive exact finite-\(N\) formulas for the ensemble-averaged bivariate moments \(M_{PQ} = \overline{\langle \mathcal{O}(t) H^Q(k) \mathcal{O}(t) H^P(k) \rangle^m}\) for \(P+Q=2\) and \(4\). The Hamiltonian \(H(k)\) is taken as EGUE(\(k\)) and the transition operator \(\mathcal{O}(t)\) as an independent EGUE(\(t\)), both represented by \(U(N)\) irreducible tensors. The moments are computed by applying the Wigner-Eckart theorem, Racah transforms, and orthonormal properties of \(U(N)\) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, yielding explicit expressions in terms of \(\Lambda\) functions and \(U(N)\) Racah coefficients. Asymptotic (dilute limit) formulas are also derived, and numerical evaluations for two-body (\(k=2\)) Hamiltonians (and some \(k=3,4\)) confirm the bivariate Gaussian form of the smoothed strength densities.

## Key numbers

* For a spinless fermion system with \(m\) particles in \(N\) single-particle states, the ensemble-averaged second moment of the Hamiltonian is \(\overline{\langle H^2(k) \rangle^m} = V_H^2 \, \Lambda^0(N,m,k)\), where \(\Lambda^0(N,m,k) = \binom{m}{k} \binom{N-m+k}{k}\).
* The bivariate correlation coefficient \(\xi(m)\) between initial and final energies is given by \(\xi(m) = \frac{\sum_{\nu=0}^{\min(t,m-k)} \Lambda^\nu(N,m,k) \Lambda^\nu(N,m,m-t) d(N:\nu)}{\binom{N}{m} \Lambda^0(N,m,t) \Lambda^0(N,m,k)}\), where \(d(N:\nu) = \binom{N}{\nu}^2 - \binom{N}{\nu-1}^2\).
* The moment \(M_{11}(m) = \overline{\langle \mathcal{O}(t) H(k) \mathcal{O}(t) H(k) \rangle^m}\) equals \(V_\mathcal{O}^2 V_H^2 \binom{N}{m}^{-1} \sum_{\nu=0}^{\min(k,m-t)} \Lambda^\nu(N,m,t) \Lambda^\nu(N,m,m-k) d(N:\nu)\).
* The moment \(M_{31}(m) = \overline{\langle \mathcal{O}(t) H(k) \mathcal{O}(t) H^3(k) \rangle^m}\) is \(2 \overline{\langle H^2(k) \rangle^m} M_{11}(m) + \frac{V_\mathcal{O}^2 V_H^4}{\binom{N}{m}} \sum_{\nu=0}^{\min(k,m-k,m-t)} \Lambda^\nu(N,m,t) \Lambda^\nu(N,m,k) \Lambda^\nu(N,m,m-k) d(N:\nu)\).

## Context

This work extends the embedded random matrix ensemble (EE) framework beyond eigenvalue densities to transition strength densities, which probe eigenfunction structure and are crucial for applications such as nuclear beta decay and nucleosynthesis. Prior results for state densities (Mon and French, 1975) and some dilute-limit formulas for transition strengths (FKPT; Ma-arx) are generalized to exact finite-\(N\) formulas for arbitrary \(k\)-body Hamiltonians and \(t\)-body transition operators. The derived moments enable statistical spectroscopy analysis of transition strengths in nuclei and atoms, and the bivariate Gaussian form can be used to compute transition strengths needed for astrophysical applications and neutrinoless double beta decay studies.
