# Equal-time hierarchies for transport descriptions of fermionic fields

> A transport theory which is not restricted to the gradient and quasi-particle approximations is presented which is formulated in terms of the energy moments, or equivalently the equal-time derivatives of the one-particle Green functions. A set of infinite hierarchies of kinetic and constraint equations for equal-time quantities for the spectral and the kinetic part of the one-particle Green function are derived. The hierarchies for the spectral function truncate automatically as in the mean field approximation. The possibility of a systematic truncation of the hierarchies for the kinetic part is discussed. The effects the quantum corrections are illustrated in a simple one-dimensional model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a transport theory for fermionic fields that goes beyond the gradient and quasi-particle approximations by formulating equations in terms of energy moments (equal-time derivatives) of one-particle Green functions, but the hierarchies for the kinetic part require a systematic truncation scheme and the spectral function hierarchies truncate automatically only in the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors start from the Dyson equation for real-time Green functions on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour, perform a Wigner transform in space coordinates only (keeping the relative time variable), and derive infinite hierarchies of kinetic and constraint equations for the equal-time derivatives of the spectral and kinetic Green functions. The collision integrals are approximated by expanding the integrands around zero relative time and introducing a cutoff parameter Δ, yielding polynomial approximations that include memory effects through time derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The zeroth equal-time derivative of the spectral function satisfies g^{A(0)} = -i from the equal-time commutation relation (sum rule for the spectral function)
- The first constraint equation gives g^{A(1)} = -ε, where ε = p²/(2m) + σ_δ(T) is the mean-field energy
- The second constraint equation gives g^{A(2)} = i cos(½◇)(ε·ε) - σ^{A(0)}, where ◇ is the Poisson operator
- The collision integrals S_±^{K(0)} are approximated to second order in the cutoff parameter Δ, with terms proportional to Δ and Δ²
- The collision integrals S_±^{K(1)} are approximated to first order in Δ, with terms proportional to Δ

## Context

This work extends the equal-time approach of the ETH group (1990s) for particles in external fields to interacting fermionic systems, going beyond the first-order gradient approximation used in approaches by Casas (1990s), Leupold (1990s), and Knoll (1990s). The method enables systematic inclusion of off-shell effects and nonlocal quantum corrections in transport descriptions of heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant for processes like subthreshold particle production and the treatment of broad resonances such as the Δ-resonance. The truncation scheme provides an alternative to test-particle methods in 8-dimensional phase space that were being developed by Leupold (1990s) and others.
