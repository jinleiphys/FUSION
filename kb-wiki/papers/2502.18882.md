# Implications of σ-cut potential on Antikaon condensates in neutron stars

> We investigate the properties of neutron stars with antikaon condensation in the framework of the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) model with a $σ$-cut potential. The well-known RMF models, TM1 and TM1e, are used to analyze the structure and composition of neutron stars. The antikaon condensation part of the equation of state (EoS) is constrained from the experimental data of K$^{-}$ atomic and kaon-nucleon scattering. The $σ$-cut potential, which is known to make the EoS stiffer at high densities, is modulated by a free parameter $f_{s}$. Our present analysis suggests that one can obtain neutron star configurations heavier than 2$M_{\odot}$ with antikaon condensates in most cases for $f_{s}$ = 0.6. The antikaon phase transition is a second-order for $f_{s}$ = 0.6 for both TM1 and TM1e parameter sets. The calculated global properties of neutron stars with antikaon condensates i.e., mass and radius seem to be in resonable agreement with other theoretical and observational data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron star configurations with masses exceeding 2 solar masses can be obtained with antikaon condensates in most cases when the sigma-cut potential parameter is set to f_s = 0.6, but this result is conditional on using this specific parameter value and applies only to the TM1 and TM1e relativistic mean-field models.

## Method

The authors use the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) model with TM1 and TM1e parameter sets to describe nucleonic matter, incorporating a logarithmic sigma-cut potential U_cut(sigma) that stiffens the equation of state at high densities. The antikaon condensation is included via a Lagrangian with scalar and vector couplings to mesons, where the scalar coupling g_sigmaK is determined from experimental K- optical potential depths ranging from -100 MeV to -160 MeV at saturation density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- The sigma-cut potential parameter is set to f_s = 0.6, chosen because previous work (Zhang 2018) showed f_s must be higher than 0.55 in the TM1 model to maintain finite nuclei properties
- Antikaon optical potential depths considered: U_K- = -100 MeV, -120 MeV, -140 MeV, and -160 MeV at saturation density n_0 = 0.145 fm^-3 for TM1
- The onset density for antikaon condensation occurs at approximately 3.7 n_0 for TM1 and 3.6 n_0 for TM1e with U_K- = -120 MeV
- The TM1e model has symmetry energy slope parameter L = 40 MeV, while TM1 has L = 110.8 MeV, with the additional coupling parameter Lambda_v = 0.0429
- The PREX-2 measurement gives neutron skin thickness of 208Pb as (0.283 +/- 0.071) fm, with symmetry energy E_sym = (38.1 +/- 4.7) MeV and slope L = (106 +/- 37) at saturation density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars using RMF models (Glendenning and Schaffner 1998, Banik 2002, Guo 2003) by incorporating a sigma-cut potential that stiffens the equation of state at high densities. The authors previously studied pure nucleonic and hyperon-rich neutron star matter (Thakur 2024) and now investigate how the sigma-cut potential affects neutron star properties through the parameter f_s and symmetry energy through Lambda_v coupling in the presence of antikaon condensation. The results are compared with observational constraints from pulsar mass measurements (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620), NICER data, and the GW170817 event.
