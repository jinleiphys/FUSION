# Global performance of covariant density functional theory in description of charge radii and related indicators

> A short review of existing efforts to understand charge radii and related indicators on a global scale within the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) is presented. Using major classes of covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs), the global accuracy of the description of experimental absolute and differential charge radii within the CDFT framework has been established. This assessment is supplemented by an evaluation of theoretical statistical and systematic uncertainties in the description of charge radii. New results on the accuracy of the description of differential charge radii in deformed actinides and light superheavy nuclei are presented and the role of octupole deformation in their reproduction is evaluated. Novel mechanisms leading to odd-even staggering in charge radii are discussed. Finally, we analyze the role of self-consistency effects in an accurate description of differential charge radii.

## Key claim

The paper claims that covariant density functional theory (CDFT) provides a reasonably good description of charge radii and related indicators at the mean-field level, but the accuracy is limited by deficiencies in single-particle state energies and the neglect of correlations beyond mean field; the global rms deviation between calculated and experimental charge radii is at the level of 0.028 fm, corresponding to an average precision of 0.58%.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with separable pairing interaction using seven covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs: NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, DD-PC1, PC-PK1, NL5(C), NL5(D), NL5(E)) representing major classes of CDFT models. They compared calculated absolute and differential charge radii against experimental data across the nuclear chart, evaluated statistical and systematic theoretical uncertainties, and analyzed the impact of octupole deformation in actinides and light superheavy nuclei. For odd-even staggering (OES) in charge radii, they employed two procedures (LES and EGS) for treating odd-N nuclei and introduced a schematic model accounting for fragmentation of the single-particle wavefunction via spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Global rms deviation Δr_ch^rms = 0.0283 fm for NL3*, 0.0230 fm for DD-ME2, 0.0329 fm for DD-MEδ*, 0.0253 fm for DD-PC1, 0.0284 fm for NL5(C), 0.0277 fm for NL5(D), and 0.0288 fm for NL5(E)
- Average precision of charge radius prediction: 0.58% (given average experimental rms charge radius around 4.8 fm)
- Systematic uncertainty spread Δr_ch for most medium-mass and heavy nuclei: approximately 0.01 fm; for Z ≤ 40 nuclei: exceeds 0.02 fm in most cases
- Statistical errors σ(r_ch) are on average a factor of approximately 6 smaller than global rms deviations between theory and experiment
- Accounting for octupole deformation in low-N Th and U isotopes increases δ⟨r²⟩^{N,N'} by approximately 0.1 fm²
- In the Sn isotopes, the magnitude of OES is underestimated on average by a factor of approximately two even when using the EGS procedure

## Context

This work extends the systematic CDFT studies of charge radii from Refs. AARR.14, AA.16, and AAT.19 to include new results on differential charge radii in deformed actinides and light superheavy nuclei, and to evaluate the role of octupole deformation. It challenges the conventional explanation of OES in charge radii (gradient terms in surface and pairing interactions, as in FTTZ.94 and FTTZ.00) by proposing a mechanism based on scattering of single-particle state occupations between even-even and odd nuclei due to particle-vibration coupling. The paper also compares CDFT results with non-relativistic Skyrme DFT calculations (UNEDF1 and SV-min functionals from Ref. No) and analyzes why spherical shell models and microscopic+macroscopic models fail to describe differential charge radii due to lack of self-consistency effects.
