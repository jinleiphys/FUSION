# Dilepton Production in Transport-based Approaches

> We investigate dilepton production in transport-based approaches and show that the baryon couplings of the $ρ$ meson represent the most important ingredient for understanding the measured dilepton spectra. At low energies (of a few GeV), the baryon resonances naturally play a larger role and affect already the vacuum spectra via Dalitz-like contributions, which can be captured well in an on-shell-transport scheme. At higher energies, the baryons mostly affect the in-medium self energy of the $ρ$, which is harder to tackle in transport models and requires advanced techniques.

## Key claim

The paper argues that baryon couplings of the rho meson are the most important ingredient for understanding measured dilepton spectra, but this claim is qualified by the energy regime: at low energies (a few GeV) baryon resonances affect vacuum spectra via Dalitz-like contributions that on-shell transport captures well, while at higher energies baryons affect the in-medium self energy of the rho, which requires advanced techniques beyond standard transport models.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU hadronic transport model with strict vector-meson dominance (VMD) to simulate dilepton production from baryon resonance decays (N*, Delta*) in pp and AA collisions. For the Delta(1232) resonance, they assumed a p-wave decay into rho N with an on-shell branching ratio of 5e-5, treating Dalitz decays R -> e+e- N as a two-step process: R -> rho N followed by rho -> e+e-. The branching ratios for R -> rho N decays were taken from the partial-wave analysis by Manley et al. (1992).

## Key numbers

- On-shell branching ratio of Delta -> rho N set to 5e-5, chosen to produce dilepton yields roughly equivalent to the radiative decay for small Delta masses and compatible with HADES data at low energies.
- In the VMD model for the Delta, a bump appears around m_Delta - m_N ≈ 300 MeV from on-shell Deltas.
- Best agreement with HADES data achieved in the CC system at 2 AGeV, where the spectrum above the pion mass is dominated by eta Dalitz and baryonic VMD channels.
- At 1 AGeV in the CC system, the model shows some underestimation at intermediate masses around 300 MeV despite inclusion of OBE Bremsstrahlung according to Shyam et al. (2010).

## Context

This work extends previous GiBUU transport investigations by Weil et al. (2012) that showed baryonic N* and Delta* resonances give important contributions to dilepton spectra at SIS energies. It directly addresses the controversy over the Delta(1232) dilepton contribution claimed by Bratkovskaya et al. (2013) to be particularly significant at SIS energies, which the authors cannot reproduce. The results enable future work using the "coarse-graining" approach for proper dynamic treatment of in-medium spectral functions, as noted by Endres et al. (2013), to improve descriptions of heavy systems and connect to higher energies.
