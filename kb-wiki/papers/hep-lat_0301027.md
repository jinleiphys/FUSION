# Lattice QCD at non-vanishing density: phase diagram, equation of state

> We propose a method to study lattice QCD at non-vanishing temperature (T) and chemical potential (μ). We use n_f=2+1 dynamical staggered quarks with semi-realistic masses on L_t=4 lattices. The critical endpoint (E) of QCD on the Re(μ)-T plane is located. We calculate the pressure (p), the energy density (ε) and the baryon density (n_B) of QCD at non-vanishing T and μ.

# Lattice QCD at non-vanishing density: phase diagram, equation of state

## Key claim

The paper proposes an overlap-improving multi-parameter reweighting method to study lattice QCD at non-vanishing temperature and chemical potential, and locates the critical endpoint of QCD on the Re(μ)-T plane. The hedge is that the method is applicable only for chemical potentials up to approximately μ ≲ T, and the endpoint location (T_E ≈ 160 MeV, μ_E ≈ 700 MeV) is obtained on L_t=4 lattices with semi-realistic quark masses that are still larger than physical values.

## Method

The authors generate an ensemble of QCD configurations at μ=0 and at the transition temperature T_c using n_f=2+1 dynamical staggered quarks with bare quark masses m_{u,d}=0.025 and m_s=0.2 on L_t=4 lattices with spatial sizes L_s=4,6,8. They then determine Boltzmann weights of these configurations at non-zero μ and at temperatures lowered to the transition temperature at that μ, using multi-parameter reweighting in both β and μ. The critical endpoint is located by analyzing the volume scaling of complex Lee-Yang zeros of the partition function in β as a function of μ, extrapolating to the infinite volume limit.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint baryon chemical potential: μ_E = 725 ± 35 MeV (quark chemical potential μ_{end} = 0.375(20) in lattice units)
- Critical endpoint temperature: T_E = 160 ± 3.5 MeV
- Transition temperature at μ_B=0: T_c = 172 ± 3 MeV
- Bare quark masses: m_{u,d}=0.025, m_s=0.2 (giving m_π/m_ρ ≈ 0.66 and R_0·m_π = 0.73(6))
- Lattice spacing at T_c: approximately 0.25–0.30 fm
- Number of configurations used: 14000 on L_s=4, 3600 on L_s=6, and 840 on L_s=8 lattices

## Context

This work extends the Glasgow method (Glasgow group, 1990s) by improving the overlap problem through reweighting transition configurations to transition configurations rather than hadronic to transition ones. It provides the first ab initio lattice QCD determination of the critical endpoint, which had previously only been estimated using crude models with m_s=∞ (e.g., critical point model studies from the 1990s) suggesting μ_E ≈ 700 MeV. The results enable calculation of the equation of state (pressure, energy density, baryon density) at non-vanishing temperature and chemical potential, and were subsequently confirmed by analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential (deForcrand and Philipsen, 2002; D'Elia and Lombardo, 2002).
