# Phenomenological hadron form factors: shape and relativity

> The use of relativistic quark models with simple parametric wave functions for the understanding of the electromagnetic structure of nucleons together with their electromagnetic transition to resonances is discussed. The implications of relativity in the different ways it can be implemented in a simple model are studied together with the role played by mixed symmetry s-state and D-state deformations of the rest frame wave functions of the nucleon and Delta resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic quark models with simple parametric wave functions can describe the electromagnetic structure of nucleons and their transitions to the Delta resonance, but the specific implementation of relativity (instant, front, or point form) and the inclusion of mixed symmetry s-state and D-state deformations in the rest frame wave functions significantly affect the predicted form factors.

## Method

The author constructs a mass operator in its spectral representation, building ground state wave functions for the nucleon, Delta, and Roper resonance within the SU(6) symmetric quark model. The spatial wave functions are written in momentum space with two parameters plus a constituent quark mass, which are fitted to reproduce the proton magnetic form factor separately for each of three forms of relativistic kinematics (instant, front, and point form). The electromagnetic current matrix elements are then computed using the appropriate kinematic current for each form, and the effects of adding mixed symmetry s-state and D-state components to the rest frame wave functions are studied.

## Key numbers

*   Constituent quark mass fitted to proton magnetic form factor: 350 MeV (point form), 250 MeV (front form), 140 MeV (instant form).
*   Radial wave function parameter b: 640 MeV (point form), 500 MeV (front form), 600 MeV (instant form).
*   Radial wave function exponent a: 9/4 (point form), 4 (front form), 6 (instant form).
*   Parameter r0: 0.19 fm (point form), 0.55 fm (front form), 0.63 fm (instant form).

## Context

This work extends the long history of quark model studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors and nucleon-to-resonance transitions, referencing prior work by Chung, Simula, Eli, Boffi, and Mauro as well as the author's own earlier papers (Julia-Diaz, 2004). The paper systematically compares three different Dirac forms of relativistic kinematics (instant, front, and point form) within a single simple model framework, which enables a direct assessment of how relativity implementation choices affect predictions. This enables future work to better constrain the role of relativistic effects and non-symmetric wave function components when comparing quark models to experimental data and lattice QCD results.
