# Failure of Breit-Wigner and success of dispersive descriptions of the П„^-\to K^-О·ОЅ_П„decays

> The П„^-\to K^-О·ОЅ_П„decays have been studied using Chiral Perturbation Theory extended by including resonances as active fields. We have found that the treatment of final state interactions is crucial to provide a good description of the data. The Breit-Wigner approximation does not resum them and neglects the real part of the corresponding chiral loop functions, which violates analyticity and leads to a failure in the confrontation with the data. On the contrary, its resummation by means of an OmnГЁs-like exponentiation of through a dispersive representation provides a successful explanation of the measurements. These results illustrate the fact that Breit-Wigner parametrizations of hadronic data, although simple and easy to handle, lack a link with the underlying strong interaction theory and should be avoided. As a result of our analysis we determine the properties of the K*(1410) resonance with a precision competitive to its traditional extraction using П„^-\to (KПЂ)^-ОЅ_П„decays, albeit the much limited statistics accumulated for the П„^-\to K^-О·ОЅ_П„channel. We also predict the soon discovery of the П„^-\to K^-О·'ОЅ_П„decays.

## Key claim

The Breit-Wigner (dipole) parametrization fails to describe the $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$ decays because it does not resum final state interactions and neglects the real part of chiral loop functions, violating analyticity; in contrast, dispersive representations (OmnГЁs-like exponentiation or three-times subtracted dispersion relations) successfully explain the measurements, provided the finite width of resonances is included as a subleading effect in the large-$N_C$ expansion.

## Method

The vector and scalar form factors for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^{(\prime)}\nu_\tau$ were derived within Resonance Chiral Theory ($R\chi T$), including the $K^\star(892)$, $K^\star(1410)$, and $K^\star_0(1430)$ resonances, with short-distance constraints from the operator product expansion. Three treatments of final state interactions were compared: a Breit-Wigner (dipole) model replacing $M_R^2-s$ by $M_R^2-s-iM_R\Gamma_R(s)$; an exponential OmnГЁs-like parametrization (JPP) multiplying the Breit-Wigner form by the exponential of the real part of the chiral loop function; and a three-times subtracted dispersive representation (BEJ) using a phase shift defined from the vector form factor and resumming the full complex loop function in the denominator.

## Key numbers

- BaBar branching fraction for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$: $(1.58\pm0.05\pm0.09)\cdot10^{-4}$
- Belle branching fraction for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$: $(1.42\pm0.11\pm0.07)\cdot10^{-4}$
- Predicted branching fraction from dipole model (BW): $(0.78^{+0.17}_{-0.10})\cdot10^{-4}$ with $\chi^2/dof=8.3$
- Predicted branching fraction from JPP approach: $(1.47^{+0.14}_{-0.08})\cdot10^{-4}$ with $\chi^2/dof=1.9$
- Predicted branching fraction from BEJ approach: $(1.49\pm0.05)\cdot10^{-4}$ with $\chi^2/dof=1.5$
- Experimental average branching fraction: $(1.52\pm0.08)\cdot10^{-4}$
- Best fit $K^\star(1410)$ mass from BEJ: $1327^{+30}_{-38}$ MeV (pole value)
- Best fit $K^\star(1410)$ width from BEJ: $213^{+72}_{-118}$ MeV (pole value)
- Best fit $K^\star(1410)$ mass from JPP (converted to pole): $1332^{+16}_{-18}$ MeV
- Best fit $K^\star(1410)$ width from JPP (converted to pole): $220^{+26}_{-24}$ MeV
- Predicted branching fraction for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^\prime\nu_\tau$ from BEJ: $(1.03^{+0.37}_{-0.29})\cdot10^{-6}$
- BaBar upper limit for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^\prime\nu_\tau$: $<4.2\cdot10^{-6}$ at $90\%$ confidence level
- $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing angle: $\theta_P=(-13.3\pm1.0)^\circ$
- $|V_{us}f_+^{K^-\pi^0}(0)|=0.21664\pm0.00048$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\tau^-\to (K\pi)^-\nu_\tau$ decays by the BaBar and Belle Collaborations (BfactKpi) and theoretical analyses using $R\chi T$ (Kpi, OurWork), which precisely determined the $K^\star(892)$ and $K^\star(1410)$ parameters from $K\pi$ data. The paper demonstrates that the $K\eta$ channel provides a complementary and competitive determination of the $K^\star(1410)$ properties, despite much lower statistics. The results enable improved hadronic currents for the TAUOLA Monte Carlo generator and predict the soon discovery of $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^\prime\nu_\tau$ at Belle-II, which would test the unitarized scalar form factor from Jamin, Oller, and Pich (JOP).
