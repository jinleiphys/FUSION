# New Modes of Nuclear Excitations

> We present a theoretical approach based on density functional theory supplemented by a microscopic multi-phonon model which is applied for investigations of pygmy resonances and other excitations of different multipolarities in stable and exotic nuclei. The possible relation of low-energy modes to the properties of neutron or proton skins is systematically studied in isotonic and isotopic chains. The fine structure of nuclear electric and magnetic response functions is analyzed and compared to experimental data. Their relevance to nuclear astrophysics is discussed.

# New Modes of Nuclear Excitations

## Key claim
The paper presents a theoretical approach combining density functional theory with a microscopic multi-phonon model (quasiparticle-phonon model, QPM) that can describe pygmy resonances and other low-energy nuclear excitations, but the agreement with experimental data is specifically demonstrated for stable and near-stable nuclei, with predictive power claimed for exploratory investigations of exotic nuclei.

## Method
The authors use a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov (HFB) description of the nuclear ground state, linked to density functional theory, and then apply the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) for excited states. The QPM diagonalizes the model Hamiltonian on a basis of one-, two-, and three-phonon configurations built from QRPA phonons, allowing multi-configuration mixing. Electromagnetic transition matrix elements are calculated with operators that include quasiparticle-phonon interactions and satisfy exact commutation relations to respect the Pauli principle.

## Key numbers
- For the N=50 isotones (⁸⁸Sr, ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹²Mo, ⁸⁶Kr), the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) total B(E1) strength below 9 MeV smoothly decreases with increasing Z number, closely correlated with neutron skin thickness.
- For ⁸⁶Kr, the experiment identifies 42 levels up to an excitation energy of 10.1 MeV.
- For ¹³⁸Ba, the electric dipole character of the pygmy mode was experimentally verified for excitations from 4.0 to 8.6 MeV.
- For ⁹⁰Zr, the M1 giant resonance has a centroid energy of 9 MeV and sum strength of 4.5(4) μ²_N, with the orbital part of the magnetic moment contributing about 22% of the total M1 strength below threshold.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical predictions of the pygmy quadrupole resonance (PQR) by Tsoneva and Lenske (2011) and systematic studies of the pygmy dipole resonance in N=50 isotones by Schwengner et al. (2013). The approach builds on earlier QPM developments by Soloviev (1976) and the density functional theory framework of Hohenberg and Kohn (1964). The results enable improved calculations of neutron-capture reaction rates for nuclear astrophysics, as demonstrated in work by Raut et al. (2013), and can be extended to experimentally inaccessible mass regions.
