# A QCD Analysis of Double Parton Scattering: Color Correlations, Interference Effects and Evolution

> We derive a factorization formula for the double Drell-Yan cross section in terms of double parton distribution functions (dPDFs). Diparton flavor, spin and color correlations and parton-exchange interference terms contribute, even for unpolarized beams. Soft radiation effects are nontrivial for the color correlation and interference contributions, and are described by non-perturbative soft functions. We provide a field-theoretic definition of the quark dPDFs and study some of their basic properties, including discrete symmetries and their interpretation in a non-relativistic quark model. We calculate the renormalization group evolution of the quark dPDFs and of the soft functions. The evolution receives contributions from both ultraviolet and rapidity divergences. We find that color correlation and interference effects are Sudakov suppressed, greatly reducing the number of dPDFs needed to describe double parton scattering at high energy experiments.

## Key claim

The paper derives a QCD factorization theorem for double Drell-Yan production that includes diparton flavor, spin, and color correlations as well as parton-exchange interference terms, but finds that color-correlated and interference double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) are Sudakov suppressed at high energies, leaving only the color-summed unpolarized and longitudinally-polarized dPDFs as the dominant contributions in high-energy experiments.

## Method

The authors systematically derive a factorization formula for the double Drell-Yan cross section in terms of double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) by extending the standard single Drell-Yan factorization approach. They classify the quark dPDFs according to their spin and color structures, including color-summed (1⊗1) and color-correlated (T^A⊗T^A) operators, and calculate the renormalization group evolution of both the dPDFs and the associated soft functions using the rapidity renormalization group to handle rapidity divergences. The evolution equations are solved to determine the Sudakov suppression factors for the color-correlated and interference contributions.

## Key numbers

- The effective cross section σ_eff measured in DPS experiments ranges from 5 to 15 mb.
- The color-correlated dPDF F^T_qq satisfies the inequality -4F^1_qq/3 ≤ F^T_qq ≤ 2F^1_qq/3 based on positivity of the color 6 and color 3-bar diquark distributions.
- The Sudakov suppression factor at leading-logarithmic accuracy is exp(-α_s C_A/(2π) ln^2(Q^2/Λ^2)), where Λ is taken as 1.4 GeV in the numerical analysis.
- The μ anomalous dimension for the color-correlated dPDF F^T contains a modified color factor of -1/6 rather than 4/3 for the x-dependent splitting function piece.

## Context

This work extends the earlier DPS formalism of Paver (1982) and the color-correlation analysis of Mekhfi (1985, 1988) by providing a complete QCD factorization theorem with explicit treatment of soft functions and rapidity divergences. It builds on the transverse-momentum-dependent dPDF analysis of Diehl (2011) but focuses on the case where transverse momenta are integrated over, leading to different evolution equations. The results enable simplified phenomenological analyses of DPS at the LHC by showing that only the first line of the factorization formula (color-summed unpolarized and longitudinally-polarized dPDFs) survives at high energies, greatly reducing the number of functions needed to describe double parton scattering.
