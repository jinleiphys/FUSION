# Is $^{40}$Mg a Borromean halo nucleus? A case built on the electric-dipole response

> We investigate the low-energy electric-dipole response of $^{40}$Mg using a $^{38}$Mg$+n+n$ three-body model. This model is implemented using a three-body hyperspherical formalism with an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator basis. In this study, two different neutron-neutron interactions are considered: a scalar Gaussian density-dependent central potential and a more realistic finite-range potential which includes central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. We examine how electric-dipole response is affected by the choice of the interaction.

# Is $^{40}$Mg a Borromean halo nucleus? A case built on the electric-dipole response

## Key claim

$^{40}$Mg exhibits electric-dipole response characteristics consistent with a two-neutron Borromean halo nucleus, but only if its actual two-neutron separation energy lies near the lower end of the evaluated range (0.670 ± 0.710 MeV); the claim is conditional on the unmeasured $S_{2n}$ value and on the orbital ordering scenario (Normal, Degenerate, or Inverted) assumed for the valence neutrons.

## Method

The authors model $^{40}$Mg as a $^{38}$Mg core plus two valence neutrons using a three-body hyperspherical formalism with an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis. They solve coupled hyperradial equations for the $0^+$ ground state and $1^-$ continuum pseudostates, using two types of neutron-neutron interactions: the finite-range Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) potential (with central, spin-orbit, and tensor components) and four density-dependent Gaussian (ddG) central potentials fitted to the $n$-$n$ scattering length. The $^{38}$Mg+$n$ interaction is described by a Woods-Saxon potential with $l$-dependent central depth and spin-orbit term, and the three-body force strength is adjusted to reproduce selected $S_{2n}$ values within the evaluated uncertainty band.

## Key numbers

- Evaluated two-neutron separation energy of $^{40}$Mg: $S_{2n} = 0.670 \pm 0.710$ MeV (from systematics, not measured)
- Integrated $B(E1)$ strength up to 6 MeV for GPT interaction at $S_{2n}=0.670$ MeV: 1.44 $e^2$fm$^2$ (Normal scenario), 1.82 $e^2$fm$^2$ (Degenerate), 1.94 $e^2$fm$^2$ (Inverted); these exhaust 76%, 84%, and 86% of the respective dipole cluster sum rules
- For the Inverted scenario with $S_{2n}=0.200$ MeV (shallowest): integrated $B(E1)$ strength up to 6 MeV is 3.10 $e^2$fm$^2$, exhausting 93% of the cluster sum rule
- Matter radii for Inverted scenario at $S_{2n}=0.670$ MeV: 3.767 fm (GPT), 3.708 fm (ddG-1), 3.757 fm (ddG-2), 3.780 fm (ddG-3), 3.772 fm (ddG-4)
- $(2p_{3/2})^2$ occupancy for Inverted scenario at $S_{2n}=0.670$ MeV: 50.01% (GPT), 24.93% (ddG-1), 41.20% (ddG-2), 51.15% (ddG-3), 51.77% (ddG-4)
- Comparison with known halo nuclei: $^{6}$He integrated $B(E1)$ up to 6 MeV is 1.1(1) $e^2$fm$^2$ (Sun 2021); $^{11}$Li up to 3 MeV is 1.42(0.18) $e^2$fm$^2$ (Nakamura 2006); $^{19}$B up to 6 MeV is 1.64(0.06) $e^2$fm$^2$ (Cook 2020)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body calculations for $^{39}$Na and $^{40}$Mg (Singh 2024, Singh 2025) that predicted enhanced matter radii and reaction cross sections near the weakened $N=28$ shell closure, following the identification of $^{29}$F as a Borromean halo nucleus (Bagchi 2020, Casal 2020). The study builds on the established connection between low-lying electric-dipole strength and halo structure in light nuclei such as $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li, and uses the hyperspherical formalism developed by Zhukov (1993) and Casal (2013). The results enable experimental searches via Coulomb dissociation at rare-isotope facilities, where the predicted $B(E1)$ enhancement provides a clearer halo signature than matter radius measurements alone; future work requires explicit treatment of core excitations and improved $^{38}$Mg+$n$ spectroscopy.
