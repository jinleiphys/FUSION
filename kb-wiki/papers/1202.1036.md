# Nucleon Resonances with Hidden Charm in Coupled-Channel Models

> The model dependence of the predictions of nucleon resonances with hidden charm is investigated. We consider several coupled-channel models which are derived from relativistic quantum field theory by using (1) a unitary transformation method, and (2) the three-dimensional reductions of Bethe-Salpeter Equation. With the same vector meson exchange mechanism, we find that all models give very narrow molecular-like nucleon resonances with hidden charm in the mass range of 4.3 GeV $ < M_R < $ 4.5 GeV, in consistent with the previous predictions.

## Key claim

All four coupled-channel models considered predict very narrow molecular-like nucleon resonances with hidden charm in the mass range 4.3 GeV < M_R < 4.5 GeV, but the resonance pole positions are highly sensitive to the cutoff parameter and to approximations made in the potential, particularly whether the momentum of the exchanged vector meson is neglected.

## Method

The authors solved three-dimensional coupled-channel equations for meson-baryon scattering in the hidden-charm sector, using the same vector meson exchange mechanism derived from SU(4) hidden gauge Lagrangians. They compared four models: one based on a unitary transformation method, and three based on different three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Kadyshevsky, Blankenbecler-Sugar, and Thompson). The channels considered were $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$, $\bar{D}\Lambda_c$, $\eta_c N$ for pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) systems, and $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$, $\bar{D}^*\Lambda_c$, $J/\psi N$, $\rho N$ for vector-baryon (VB) systems, with resonance pole positions extracted via analytic continuation.

## Key numbers

- For the unitary transformation model with cutoff $\Lambda = 1500$ MeV and $J^P = 1/2^-$ in the PB system, the resonance pole is at $M = 4314.531$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma/2 = 1.448$ MeV, giving a binding energy $\Delta E = 6.259$ MeV relative to the $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$ threshold at 4320.79 MeV.
- For the same model with $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV, the binding energy increases to 19.68 MeV and the half-width to 5.835 MeV in the PB system; in the VB system the binding energy reaches 23.90 MeV with half-width 7.115 MeV.
- The four models at $\Lambda = 1500$ MeV give binding energies differing by up to 11.30% and widths differing by up to 40.95% relative to the unitary transformation model.
- Neglecting both baryon spin and the momentum of the exchanged vector meson (the approximation used in the Valencia model) shifts the resonance mass to 4229.362 MeV with binding energy 91.43 MeV, a 1361% change from the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends the predictions of hidden-charm nucleon resonances from the Beijing-Valencia collaboration (Oset et al., 2009) by systematically testing the model dependence of those predictions. The authors find that the earlier Valencia model overestimated binding energies and widths because it neglected the momentum dependence of the exchanged vector meson in the potential. The results are consistent with later calculations using a chiral constituent quark model (Wang et al., 2011) and a Schrodinger equation approach (Liu et al., 2011), which also find bound states of $\bar{D}\Sigma_c$ with binding energies of 0-16 MeV. The work enables experimental searches for these narrow resonances in reactions such as $ep \to e J/\psi p$, $pp \to p \eta_c p$, and $p\bar{p} \to p \eta_c \bar{p}$.
