# Physics implications of correlation data from the RHIC and LHC heavy-ion programs

> Two-dimensional angular correlation data from the STAR experiment at RHIC and from the LHC experiments provide critical information about dynamical processes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The principal correlation structures of interest are a broad jet-related peak at small relative azimuth ($ϕ$) extending to large relative pseudorapidity ($η$), the dijet ridge at large relative azimuth, and an independent double ridge on $ϕ$ represented by a quadrupole function. The broad peak at small relative azimuth has been attributed, in large part, to initial-state fluctuations and hydrodynamic flow which produce higher-order harmonics on $ϕ$. That conjecture is challenged in this paper. It is shown that the net effect of additional higher harmonic model elements is to describe small, non-Gaussian (NG) shapes in the broad jet-related peak. The quadrupole correlation, which is also conventionally attributed to hydrodynamic flow, is considered within the Balitsky - Fadin - Kuraev - Lipatov (BFKL) Pomeron framework. Preliminary results using this model for the quadrupole correlation for particle production from 200 GeV $p+p$ collisions are shown to be consistent with recent data from STAR.

## Key claim

The paper challenges the conventional attribution of the broad same-side jet-related peak in two-particle angular correlations to initial-state fluctuations and hydrodynamic flow producing higher-order harmonics, showing instead that such higher harmonic model elements merely describe small non-Gaussian (NG) shapes in that peak. The quadrupole correlation, conventionally attributed to elliptic flow, is argued to be consistent with a perturbative QCD (pQCD) mechanism based on the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) Pomeron framework, with preliminary results for 200 GeV p+p collisions shown to be consistent with recent STAR data.

## Method

The analysis uses two-particle angular correlation data from the STAR experiment at RHIC and LHC experiments, constructing sibling and mixed-event pair histograms for all charged particles within the STAR TPC acceptance (p_t > 0.15 GeV/c, |η| < 1, 2π azimuth) without trigger particles. The standard fitting model includes a same-side 2D Gaussian, an away-side dipole, a quadrupole, and a same-side 2D exponential; additional NG fitting models (sextupole, SSG, NG exponents, η_Δ polynomial, quartic) were tested against 200 GeV minimum-bias Au+Au data from STAR. For the quadrupole, the BFKL Pomeron model of Levin and Rezaeian (2010) is applied, using fits to the 200 GeV p+p minimum-bias multiplicity frequency distribution to extract one- and two-Pomeron shower probabilities.

## Key numbers

* The measured 200 GeV p+p minimum-bias quadrupole amplitude reported by STAR is 0.002, corresponding to azimuth asymmetry parameter v_2 = 0.072.
* The BFKL predicted quadrupole amplitude for 200 GeV p+p minimum-bias collisions is between 0.0003 and 0.003, depending on the assumed gluon saturation model.
* Mean p_t^2 estimated from spectrum data equals 0.19 (GeV/c)^2.
* The saturation scale Q_S^2 is assumed to be 0.6 (GeV/c)^2; the dipole cut-off mass m^2 was assumed between 0.8 and 1.6 GeV^2.
* Fits to the 200 GeV p+p minimum-bias multiplicity distribution obtain P_1 = 0.91, P_2 = 0.09, and P_{n>2} = 0 for the one- and two-Pomeron shower probabilities.
* The average charged particle multiplicity per unit η at midrapidity for p+p at √s = 200 GeV is 2.5.
* The hard-to-soft multiplicity ratio parameter α = 0.005 from the relation n_h/n_s = α n_ch.

## Context

This work extends the critical evaluation of the triangular flow (sextupole) conjecture by Alver and Roland (2010), showing that the sextupole derives from the same-side 2D peak structure rather than from independent dynamics, consistent with earlier analyses by Ray (2011) and others. The quadrupole analysis builds on the BFKL Pomeron model of Levin and Rezaeian (2010) and contrasts with conventional elliptic flow interpretations, suggesting instead that the quadrupole originates from initial-state pQCD processes. The results motivate further study of NG structure in the same-side 2D peak based on minimum-bias jet fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, and enable application of the BFKL model to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions via Glauber superposition.
