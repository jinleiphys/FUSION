# Neutrino-pair emission in a strong magnetic field

> We study the neutrino emissivity of strongly magnetized neutron stars due to the charged and neutral current couplings of neutrinos to baryons in strong magnetic fields. The leading order neutral current process is the one-body neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung, which does not have an analogue in the zero field limit. The leading order charged current reaction is the known generalization of the direct Urca processes to strong magnetic fields. While for superstrong magnetic fields in excess of $10^{18}$ G the direct Urca process dominates the one-body bremsstrahlung, we find that for fields on the order $10^{16}-10^{17}$ G and temperatures a few times $10^9$ K the one-body bremsstrahlung is the dominant process. Numerical computation of the resulting emissivity, based on a simple parametrization of the equation of state of the $npe$-matter in a strong magnetic field, shows that the emissivity of this reaction is of the same order of magnitude as that of the modified Urca process in the zero field limit.

# Neutrino-pair emission in a strong magnetic field

## Key claim

The paper claims that for magnetic fields in the range $10^{16}-10^{17}$ G and temperatures of a few times $10^9$ K, the one-body neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung (a neutral current process with no zero-field analogue) becomes the dominant neutrino emission mechanism, but only under the condition that the magnetic field is not so strong that the direct Urca process (which dominates for fields above $10^{18}$ G) takes over.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrino emissivity of magnetized neutron star matter using the finite-temperature Matsubara Green's functions technique to evaluate the imaginary part of the polarization function for neutrons and protons in a strong magnetic field. For the neutral current one-body bremsstrahlung, they derive the emissivity from the spin-flip polarization function, which becomes finite only when the magnetic field splits the Fermi surfaces of spin-up and spin-down neutrons. For the charged current direct Urca process, they treat the Landau level quantization of protons and electrons, considering both the superstrong field limit (only the lowest Landau level occupied) and the weak field limit where many Landau levels contribute. The equation of state of $npe$-matter in a magnetic field is parametrized using a non-relativistic energy density decomposition with kinetic, magnetic, mass, potential, and lepton contributions, with parameters from Lattimer and Swesty (1991).

## Key numbers

- For magnetic fields $B = 10^{16}$ G and $B = 10^{17}$ G at saturation density $n_0 = 0.155$ fm$^{-3}$, the one-body neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung competes with the modified Urca process; the emissivity of the one-body bremsstrahlung scales as $\propto T^7$ while the modified Urca scales as $\propto T^8$.
- The modified Urca process emissivity in the zero-field limit is given by $\epsilon_{\nu \overline{\nu}} = 1.8 \times 10^{21} (n/n_0)^{2/3} T_9^8$ ergs cm$^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$, where $T_9$ is the temperature in units of $10^9$ K.
- For weak magnetic fields ($|\mu_n| B \ll T$), the one-body bremsstrahlung emissivity scales as $\epsilon_{\nu \overline{\nu}} \propto B^6$ for fixed temperature.
- For superstrong magnetic fields in excess of $10^{18}$ G, the direct Urca process dominates the one-body bremsstrahlung.
- At density $n = 3 n_0$ and $B = 10^{16}$ G, the triangular condition $p_{Fp} + p_{Fe} > p_{Fn}$ is satisfied, allowing the direct Urca process to dominate cooling.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino emission in magnetized neutron stars by identifying a new neutral current channel (one-body neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung) that has no zero-field analogue, complementing the known generalization of the direct Urca process to strong magnetic fields studied by Leinson and Perez (1998) and Baiko and Yakovlev (1999). The paper also builds on the equation of state work by Broderick, Prakash and Lattimer (2000) showing that strong magnetic fields increase the proton fraction. The results enable more accurate cooling simulations of magnetars and soft gamma-ray repeaters, particularly for fields in the $10^{16}-10^{17}$ G range where the one-body bremsstrahlung can compete with or dominate the standard modified Urca process.
