# Photons from jet - plasma interaction in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We expose the role of collisional energy loss on high $p_T$ photon data measured by PHENIX collaboration by calculating photon yield in jet plasma interaction. The phase space distribution of the participating jet is dynamically evolved by solving Fokker-Planck equation. It is shown that the data is reasonably well reproduced when contributions from all the relevant sources are taken into account. Predictions at higher beam energies relevant for LHC experiment have been made.

# Photons from jet - plasma interaction in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors show that the PHENIX high-$p_T$ photon data in the range $4 \leq p_T \leq 14$ GeV can be reasonably reproduced when collisional energy loss of the jet is included via a Fokker-Planck evolution, but they note that the assumption made in prior work (DKS) that photons are predominantly emitted at $p_\gamma \sim p_q$ may be valid at RHIC energies yet fails at LHC energies, where the difference between calculations with and without energy loss reaches a factor of 2–3.

## Method

The photon yield from jet-plasma interaction is calculated by dynamically evolving the phase-space distribution of the quark jet via a Fokker-Planck equation with drag and diffusion coefficients derived from hard thermal loop (HTL) screened matrix elements in the small-angle scattering limit. The evolved jet distribution is then used in the photon production rate for Compton scattering and annihilation processes, and the space-time integration is performed using Bjorken hydrodynamics with a centrality-dependent nuclear overlap function. Contributions from initial hard parton-parton collisions and jet fragmentation are also included and summed to obtain the total photon $p_T$ spectrum.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the photon yield from jet-plasma interaction is lowered by a factor of approximately 1.5 at $p_T = 4$ GeV and 1.7 at $p_T = 14$ GeV when collisional energy loss is included.
- For 10% most central collisions, the nuclear overlap factor $\mathcal{R}$ is about 215 fm$^{-2}$ at RHIC and 235 fm$^{-2}$ at LHC.
- At LHC, the difference between the jet-photon yield with and without energy loss is a factor of 2–3.
- Initial conditions for RHIC: $T_i = 0.446$ GeV, $\tau_i = 0.147$ fm/c; for LHC: $T_i = 0.897$ GeV, $\tau_i = 0.073$ fm/c.
- For an alternative RHIC initial condition with $T_i = 0.350$ GeV and $\tau_i = 0.25$ fm/c, the data are also reproduced reasonably well.
- The plasma lifetime $(t_c - t_i)$ is about 1.9 fm/c for $N_F = 2$ and 2.6 fm/c for $N_F = 2.5$.

## Context

This work extends the jet-photon calculations of DKS (Dutta, K., et al.) by relaxing the assumption that the photon momentum equals the quark momentum and by including collisional energy loss computed via HTL transport coefficients following Thoma (1995) and Abhee et al. (2005). It contrasts with earlier thermal photon calculations by Kapusta et al. and shows that jet-plasma interaction photons are important for describing PHENIX data in the $4 < p_T < 14$ GeV range. The results enable predictions for LHC photon yields and motivate future inclusion of both collisional and radiative energy loss in a unified framework, as recently done for neutral pion spectra by Roy et al. (2008).
