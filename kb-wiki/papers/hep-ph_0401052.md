# Dynamic universality class of the QCD critical point

> We show that the dynamic universality class of the QCD critical point is that of model H and discuss the dynamic critical exponents. We show that the baryon diffusion rate vanishes at the critical point. The dynamic critical index $z$ is close to 3.

## Key claim

The QCD critical point belongs to the dynamic universality class of model H (liquid-gas phase transition), not model C as previously argued by Berdnikov and Rajagopal, because the chiral order parameter mixes with the conserved baryon density and couples to momentum density via non-zero Poisson brackets.

## Method

The authors identify the set of hydrodynamic modes near the QCD critical point: energy density, momentum density, baryon number density, and the chiral condensate. They first analyze a simplified system without energy-momentum coupling, writing linearized stochastic hydrodynamic equations for the chiral condensate and baryon density from a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional. They then restore coupling to energy-momentum density and compare the resulting mode structure to the known classification of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977), using renormalization group and mode-coupling calculations to determine the dynamic critical exponents.

## Key numbers

- Dynamic critical index \(z \approx 3\) for the QCD critical point (model H)
- In model B (without energy-momentum coupling): \(z = 4 - \eta\) with \(\eta \approx 0.04\) (3d Ising model value)
- In model H, the shear viscosity exponent \(x_\eta \approx 0.065\) from \(4-\epsilon\) expansion, or \(x_\eta = 0.054\) from mode-coupling calculations in \(d=3\)
- In model H, the conductivity exponent \(x_\lambda \approx 0.916\) from \(4-\epsilon\) expansion, or \(x_\lambda = 0.946\) from mode-coupling calculations in \(d=3\)
- The relation \(x_\lambda + x_\eta = 4 - d - \eta\) holds for model H
- The baryon diffusion constant vanishes as \(D \sim \xi^{-2+\eta+x_\lambda}\) at the critical point

## Context

This work resolves a debate between Son and Stephanov (2004) and Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1998) over the dynamic universality class of the QCD critical point. The authors show that the earlier argument for model C by Berdnikov and Rajagopal incorrectly assumed vanishing Poisson brackets between the order parameter and momentum density, and neglected mixing between the chiral condensate and baryon density. The result places the QCD critical point in the same dynamic class as the liquid-gas phase transition studied by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) and Siggia, Halperin, and Hohenberg (1976). The larger value of \(z \approx 3\) compared to the model C value \(z \approx 2.17\) implies stronger constraints on the maximum correlation length achievable in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that revising the estimate of Berdnikov and Rajagopal yields a change of less than 10%.
