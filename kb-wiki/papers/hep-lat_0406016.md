# N(N*) and Delta(Delta*) on the lattice

> We investigate the mass spectrum of Nucleon and Delta (and its counterparts with strange and charm), and their excited states, in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry. For each light baryon, we use 23 masses to determine the coefficients of the mass formula in quenched chiral perturbation theory. By chiral extrapolation to m_π=135 MeV, we obtain M_N=958(26) MeV, M_{N*}=1553(42) MeV, M_{Delta}=1216(32) MeV and M_{Delta*}=1611(17) MeV, which are identified with N(939)P_{11},N(1535)S_{11}, Delta(1232)P_{33} and Delta(1620)S_{31} respectively. Further, we directly measure the masses of Omega^{-}, M_{Omega}=1648(60) MeV, and its excited state, M_{Omega*}=1935(48) MeV; as well as the triply charmed baryon Omega_{ccc}^{++}, M_{Omega_{ccc}^{++}}=4931(22) MeV, and its excited state, M_{{Ω^{++}_{ccc}}^*}=5185(35) MeV.

## Key claim
The paper reports lattice QCD masses for nucleon and Delta ground and excited states that agree with experimental values, but the chiral extrapolation required setting the linear term \(c_1 m_\pi\) to zero for all fits because including it produced unacceptably large errors; for the \(\Delta^*\) the cubic term \(c_3\) also had to be set to zero.

## Method
Quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry (optimal domain-wall fermion) was used on a \(20^3 \times 40\) lattice at \(\beta = 6.1\) with 100 gauge configurations. Baryon masses were computed for 30 quark masses in the range \(0.03 \le m_q a \le 0.8\); for light baryons, 23 masses were fitted to the quenched chiral perturbation theory mass formula \(M = c_0 + c_1 m_\pi + c_L m_\pi^2 \ln(m_\pi) + c_2 m_\pi^2 + c_3 m_\pi^3\) to extract coefficients, then extrapolated to the physical pion mass \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV. Heavy baryons (\(\Omega^-\), \(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\)) were measured directly at fixed strange and charm quark masses tuned to reproduce the \(\phi(1020)\) and \(J/\psi(3097)\) vector meson masses.

## Key numbers
- \(M_N = 958(26)\) MeV, identified with \(N(939)P_{11}\), from chiral extrapolation to \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
- \(M_{N^*} = 1553(42)\) MeV, identified with \(N(1535)S_{11}\), from chiral extrapolation to \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
- \(M_{\Delta} = 1216(32)\) MeV, identified with \(\Delta(1232)P_{33}\), from chiral extrapolation to \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
- \(M_{\Delta^*} = 1611(17)\) MeV, identified with \(\Delta(1620)S_{31}\), from chiral extrapolation to \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
- \(M_{\Omega^-} = 1648(60)\) MeV, directly measured at \(m_q a = 0.06\) (strange quark mass tuned to \(\phi(1020)\)).
- \(M_{\Omega^*} = 1935(48)\) MeV, directly measured at \(m_q a = 0.06\).
- \(M_{\Omega_{ccc}^{++}} = 4931(22)\) MeV, directly measured at \(m_q a = 0.80\) (charm quark mass tuned to \(J/\psi(3097)\)).
- \(M_{{\Omega_{ccc}^{++}}^*} = 5185(35)\) MeV, directly measured at \(m_q a = 0.80\).
- Inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} = 2.21(3)\) GeV, determined from the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 132\) MeV.
- Strange quark mass in \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(m_s^{\overline{\text{MS}}}(2\text{ GeV}) = 114(9)\) MeV.
- Charm quark mass in \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(m_c^{\overline{\text{MS}}}(2\text{ GeV}) = 1520(38)\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends quenched lattice QCD studies of nucleon and Delta baryons (e.g., Lee (1998), Brommel (2003)) by using exact chiral symmetry and a large set of quark masses to determine quenched chiral perturbation theory coefficients. It provides the first lattice QCD determination of the \(\Delta(1620)S_{31}\) mass and the first lattice predictions for the triply charmed baryon \(\Omega_{ccc}^{++}\) and its excited state. The results enable future unquenched calculations and experimental searches for the predicted charmed baryons.
