# Quantum fluctuations in the shape of exotic nuclei

> Quantum fluctuations concerning the shape of nuclei are treated within the framework of covariant density functional theory. Long range correlations beyond mean field are taken into account by configuration mixing of wave functions with triaxial shapes and the restoration of spontaneously broken rotational symmetries through three-dimensional angular momentum projection. The "controversial" nucleus 16C is treated as an example and it is found that its ground state has a triaxial shape but with large shape fluctuations. They are of crucial importance for a proper description of the spectroscopic properties of such nuclei.

## Key claim

The ground state of the controversial nucleus 16C has a triaxial shape with large shape fluctuations along the gamma degree of freedom, and this picture is essential to reproduce quantitatively the experimental B(E2: 2+1 → 0+1) value from the recoil distance method measurement, which the abstract presents as a finding but does not specify that the agreement depends on including triaxial states in the configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations using the PC-F1 parameter set to generate intrinsic wave functions with quadrupole deformations in the Hill-Wheeler coordinates (beta, gamma). They then applied three-dimensional angular momentum projection to restore rotational symmetry and used the Generator Coordinate Method to mix configurations with triaxial shapes across the full beta-gamma plane. For comparison, they also performed a restricted calculation using only axially symmetric shapes (1DAMP+GCM).

## Key numbers

- The B(E2: 2+1 → 0+1) value from 3DAMP+GCM calculations including triaxial states is 6.50 e2fm4, compared to the experimental value of 4.15(73) e2fm4 from the recoil distance method (Wiedeking 2008) and 2.6(9) e2fm4 from the recoil shadow method (Ong 2008)
- The 1DAMP+GCM calculation with only axially symmetric shapes gives a B(E2) value of only 0.11 e2fm4, demonstrating the crucial role of triaxial configurations
- The ground state (0+1) has average deformation parameters ⟨beta⟩ = 0.44 and ⟨gamma⟩ = 24.27 degrees from 3DAMP+GCM calculations
- The predicted ratio R4/2 = E4/E2 = 2.63, compared to the experimental value of 2.35 and the typical gamma-soft value of 2.50
- The K=0 component exhausts 93.1% of the norm for the 2+1 state
- The strength parameters of the zero-range pairing force are Vn = 308 and Vp = 321 MeV·fm3

## Context

This work extends covariant density functional theory beyond mean-field by including triaxial shape fluctuations and angular momentum projection, which previous non-relativistic calculations using Skyrme functionals (Valor 2000, Bender 2003) and Gogny functionals (Guzman 2002) had neglected. The results contradict earlier interpretations from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada 1997) that suggested oblate proton and prolate neutron shape coexistence, and show that three-body models (Hagino 2007) and shell model calculations (FMO 2007) requiring effective charge adjustments are not necessary. The method enables future studies of spectroscopic properties in other transitional and exotic nuclei far from stability.
