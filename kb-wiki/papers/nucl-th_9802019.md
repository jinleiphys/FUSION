# Strangeness production in proton-proton collisions

> The cross sections of the reaction $pp \to N K Y$ for $K^+$ or $K^0$ mesons and $Λ$ or $Σ$ hyperons are calculated within the boson exchange model including pion and kaon exchange diagrams. We analyze the dependence of the results on the accuracy of the input $πN$ amplitude. By fixing the $πNN $ coupling constant and the cut-off parameter $Λ_π$ at the $πNN$ vertex we calculate the contribution from the kaon exchange diagram and obtain the ratio of the $KN Λ$ and $KN Σ$ coupling constants by a fit to the experimental data. This ratio is in a good agreement with the SU(6) prediction. Our calculated total cross sections for the different reaction channels are fitted by simple expressions and compared with other parameterizations used in the literature. Furthermore, the gross features of the production cross section close to threshold are discussed.

## Key claim

The boson exchange model, including both pion and kaon exchange diagrams, can reproduce the experimental cross sections for strangeness production in proton-proton collisions, but the results depend strongly on the accuracy of the input πN amplitude and the kaon exchange contribution is necessary to account for the factor of 3 discrepancy between the one-pion exchange model and the data for pp → pΛK⁺.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross sections for pp → NYK reactions (with K⁺ or K⁰ mesons and Λ or Σ hyperons) using the one-boson exchange formalism with pion and kaon exchange diagrams. They fixed the πNN coupling constant and the cut-off parameter Λ_π at the πNN vertex from the Bonn model, then calculated the pion exchange contribution using parameterizations of the πN → YK cross section from Tsushima et al. (1997) and Cugnon et al. (1996). The difference between the experimental data and the one-pion exchange results was fitted by the kaon exchange mechanism to extract the KNΛ and KNΣ coupling constants and the kaon cut-off parameter Λ_K, neglecting interference terms and assuming isotropy of the KN elastic cross section near threshold.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion exchange model underestimates the experimental cross section for pp → pΛK⁺ by a factor of 3
- Fitted kaon cut-off parameter: Λ_K = 0.81 ± 0.14 GeV
- Fitted coupling constants: f²_NΛK = 14.6 ± 3.1, f²_NΣK = 0.88 ± 0.26
- Ratio of coupling constants g²_NΛK / g²_NΣK = 15 ± 6, in agreement with the SU(6) prediction of 27
- Pion cut-off parameter fixed at Λ_π = 1.0 GeV
- πNN coupling constant f²_NNπ = 1.0 (corresponding to g²/4π = 14.4)
- For the reaction pp → pΛK⁺, the momentum transfer at threshold is t ≈ -0.63 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the boson exchange approach to strangeness production previously applied by Ferrari (1960), Laget (1990), and Li and Ko (1996), and addresses the uncertainties in model parameters that had led to contradictory conclusions about the relative importance of pion versus kaon exchange. The authors compare their results with the resonance model calculations of Fäldt and Wilkin (1997) and Tsushima et al. (1997), noting that both approaches can describe the data but with different underlying mechanisms. The fitted coupling constants and their ratio provide input for heavy-ion collision simulations at GSI, SATURNE, CELSIUS, and COSY, where strangeness production is used to study hot and dense nuclear matter.
