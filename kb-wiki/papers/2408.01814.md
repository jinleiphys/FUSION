# Calculation of Some Integrals over Gaussian Measure with Nuclear Covariance Operator in Separable Hilbert Space

> The main purpose of this paper is to construct convergent series for the approximate calculation of certain integrals over the Gaussian measure with a nuclear covariance operator, nonlocal propagator, in separable Hilbert space. Such series arise, for example, in the model with the interaction Lagrangian $\sinh^{2(p+1)}φ$, where $p \in \mathbb{N}$ and $φ$ is the scalar field, although the problem can be solved in general form for a fairly wide class of Lagrangians: an even, strictly convex, continuous, non-negative function with a single zero value for $φ=0$ and for $|φ|\rightarrow +\infty$ growing faster than $φ^{2}$. We strictly define the scattering matrix, $\mathcal{S}$-matrix, at the zero value of the classical field, argument of the $\mathcal{S}$-matrix, of such a theory in terms of the corresponding integral, find the iterated expansion for the integrand (the Gaussian measure doesn't expand) over two orthonormal bases of functions, prove the validity of summation and integration interchange and thus find the expansion of the $\mathcal{S}$-matrix at the zero value of the classical field into the iterated series in powers of the interaction action. The individual terms of the resulting series have the form of a canonical partition function (CPF), and the methods of statistical physics are applicable to them. In particular, we express them in terms of Bell polynomials. It is important to note that such iterated series cannot be reduced to the perturbation theory (PT) series, since in the proposed model the latter diverges as $e^{n^{2}}$, where $n \in \mathbb {N}$ is the PT order. Along the way, we provide detailed mathematical background, including Beppo Levi's monotone convergence theorem (MCT) and Henri Lebesgue's dominated convergence theorem (DCT), without which the presented calculation would be significantly more complex.

## Key claim

The authors construct convergent iterated series for the S-matrix in a scalar field theory with interaction Lagrangian \(\sinh^{2(p+1)}\phi\) (or any even, strictly convex, continuous, non-negative Lagrangian growing faster than \(\phi^2\)), where the standard perturbation theory series diverges as \(e^{n^2}\) with \(n\) the perturbation order, and the proposed series cannot be reduced to perturbation theory.

## Method

The S-matrix at zero classical field is expressed as an integral over a Gaussian measure with a nuclear covariance operator in a separable Hilbert space. The integrand is expanded over two orthonormal bases of functions, and the validity of interchanging summation and integration is proved using Beppo Levi's monotone convergence theorem and Henri Lebesgue's dominated convergence theorem. The resulting iterated series is expressed in powers of the interaction action, with individual terms taking the form of a canonical partition function, which are then expressed in terms of Bell polynomials.

## Key numbers

* The perturbation theory series for the proposed model diverges as \(e^{n^{2}}\), where \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) is the perturbation theory order.
* The integral \(\int_\mathcal{H}e^{\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\Vert\varphi\Vert_{\mathcal{H}}^{2}}\mathcal{N}_{a,G}(d\varphi)\) equals \([\operatorname{Det}(1-\varepsilon G)]^{-1/2}e^{-\frac{\varepsilon}{2}\langle(1-\varepsilon G)^{-1}a|a\rangle}\) for \(\varepsilon < 1/\lambda_1\) (where \(\lambda_1\) is the largest eigenvalue of \(G\)), and \(+\infty\) otherwise.
* The integral \(\int_\mathcal{H}e^{\langle\psi|\varphi\rangle}\mathcal{N}_{a,G}(d\varphi)\) equals \(e^{\langle a|\psi\rangle+\frac{1}{2}\langle G\psi|\psi\rangle}\) for any \(\psi \in \mathcal{H}\).

## Context

This work extends the mathematical framework for non-perturbative calculations in non-local quantum field theory (NQFT) with nuclear covariance operators, building on the measure-theoretic foundations of Gaussian measures in infinite-dimensional Hilbert spaces as developed by Bogachev and Da Prato. The approach enables the calculation of S-matrix elements for Lagrangians where standard perturbation theory fails due to factorial divergence, and opens the possibility of applying methods from statistical physics (canonical partition functions, Bell polynomials) to quantum field theory calculations.
