# Pairing and Isospin Symmetry in Proton-Rich Nuclei

> Unlike their lighter counterparts, most odd-odd N=Z nuclei with mass A > 40 40 have ground states with isospin T=1, suggesting an increased role for the isovector pairing interaction. A simple SO(5) seniority-like model of this interaction reveals a striking and heretofore unnoticed interplay between like-particle and neutron-proton isovector pairing near N=Z that is reflected in the number of each kind of pair as a function of A and T. Large scale shell-model calculations exhibit the same trends, despite the simultaneous presence of isoscalar pairs, deformation, and other correlations.

# Pairing and Isospin Symmetry in Proton-Rich Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed ground-state isospin T=1 in most odd-odd N=Z nuclei with mass A > 40 is a signature of increased isovector pairing, and that a simple SO(5) seniority model captures a subtle competition between like-particle (nn, pp) and neutron-proton (np) isovector pairing that persists even when isoscalar pairing, deformation, and other correlations are included in large-scale shell-model calculations.

## Method

The authors first analyze an exactly solvable SO(5) seniority model with a pure isovector pairing interaction in a single j-orbital or degenerate set of 2Ω m-substates, deriving analytic expectation values for the numbers of nn, pp, and np pairs as functions of particle number N and isospin T. They then compare these predictions to large-basis Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in the fp-shell using the modified Kuo-Brown interaction KB3, which includes multiple orbits, isoscalar pairing, spin-orbit splitting, and deformation.

## Key numbers

- For even-even nuclei with T=0 (N=Z), all three pair numbers (nn, pp, np) are equal; adding a single pair of neutrons so that T=Tz=1 causes the number of np pairs to drop by about 40%, while the number of pp pairs increases by about 7%.
- The SMMC calculations cover Cr and Fe isotopes in the fp-shell for nuclei heavier than A=50, with results scaled by a factor of 0.5 relative to the SO(5) model to account for reduction of single-particle level density near the Fermi level.
- The quantity δVnp(N,Z), defined from binding energies, is enhanced for N=Z in nuclei with 40 ≤ A ≤ 64, extending the range of validity of an earlier SU(4) symmetry argument by Van Isacker, Warner, and Brenner (1995).

## Context

This work extends earlier isovector pairing theory by Camiz, Covello, and Jean (1965) and the review by Goodman (1979), which rarely exhibited coexistence between like-particle and np isovector pairs. The authors show that standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) methods break isospin symmetry and fail to mix np pairs with nn and pp pairs, producing three separate degenerate or near-degenerate states instead. The results enable future isospin-projected HFB treatments and motivate experimental study of proton-rich nuclei at the edge of stability, where binding energies and lifetimes are the most systematically measured quantities.
