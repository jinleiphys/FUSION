# Exact and approximate ensemble treatments of thermal pairing in a multilevel model

> A systematic comparison is conducted for pairing properties of finite systems at nonzero temperature as predicted by the exact solutions of the pairing problem embedded in three principal statistical ensembles, as well as the unprojected (FTBCS1+SCQRPA) and Lipkin-Nogami projected (FTLN1+SCQRPA) theories that include the quasiparticle number fluctuation and coupling to pair vibrations within the self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The numerical calculations are performed for the pairing gap, total energy, heat capacity, entropy, and microcanonical temperature within the doubly-folded equidistant multilevel pairing model. The FTLN1+SCQRPA predictions agree best with the exact grand-canonical results. In general, all approaches clearly show that the superfluid-normal phase transition is smoothed out in finite systems. A novel formula is suggested for extracting the empirical pairing gap in reasonable agreement with the exact canonical results.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares exact pairing solutions averaged within three thermodynamic ensembles (grand-canonical, canonical, and microcanonical) with approximate theories (FTBCS1+SCQRPA and FTLN1+SCQRPA) that include quasiparticle number fluctuation and coupling to pair vibrations, finding that the FTLN1+SCQRPA predictions agree best with exact grand-canonical results, but the authors note that the Lipkin-Nogami projection only removes quantal particle-number fluctuations, not thermal ones inherent to the grand-canonical ensemble.

## Method

The authors exactly diagonalize the pairing Hamiltonian for a doubly-folded equidistant multilevel model (Richardson's model) with N particles distributed over Ω = N doubly-degenerate levels, then compute thermodynamic quantities by averaging the exact eigenvalues within the grand-canonical ensemble (GCE), canonical ensemble (CE), and microcanonical ensemble (MCE). They compare these exact results with predictions from the FTBCS1+SCQRPA theory (which includes quasiparticle number fluctuation and coupling to self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation vibrations) and its Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projected version (FTLN1+SCQRPA), calculating the pairing gap, total energy, heat capacity, entropy, and microcanonical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for macroscopic superconductors is T_c ≈ 0.57 Δ(0), where Δ(0) is the pairing gap at T = 0.
- The full width at half maximum (FWHM) for the Gaussian distribution used in MCE smoothing is Γ = 2σ√(2 ln 2) ≈ 2.36σ, while for Breit-Wigner and Lorentz distributions it is 2σ.
- The condition for the modified three-point odd-even mass difference formula to have real solutions is S' ≤ G/4, where S' is defined in terms of total energies and the uncorrelated single-particle contribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kubo (1960s) on thermodynamic effects in small metal particles and by Denton et al. on GCE versus CE differences for heat capacity in spinless equidistant level models, as well as recent work by Sumaryada et al. comparing thermodynamic quantities across three ensembles without reporting pairing gaps. The paper enables future application of the proposed modified odd-even mass difference formula to extract empirical pairing gaps from experimental data at finite temperature, and provides a benchmark for testing approximate thermal pairing theories against exact solutions in finite systems.
