# Classification of Equation of State in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions Using Deep Learning

> Convolutional Neural Nets, which is a powerful method of Deep Learning, is applied to classify equation of state of heavy-ion collision event generated within the UrQMD model. Event-by-event transverse momentum and azimuthal angle distributions of protons are used to train a classifier. An overall accuracy of classification of 98\% is reached for Au+Au events at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11$ GeV. Performance of classifiers, trained on events at different colliding energies, is investigated. Obtained results indicate extensive possibilities of application of Deep Learning methods to other problems in physics of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Convolutional Neural Networks can classify the equation of state (hard vs. soft) of heavy-ion collision events on an event-by-event basis using only proton transverse momentum and azimuthal angle distributions, achieving 98% accuracy for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 11 GeV, but only for the discrete binary choice between two pre-defined equations of state rather than a continuous parameter space.

## Method

The authors generated 5000 Au+Au minimum-bias collisions at each of three energies (sqrt(s) = 7, 11, and 14 GeV) using the UrQMD model with two different mean-field potentials corresponding to hard and soft equations of state. For each event, they binned the proton transverse momentum (0-1 GeV/c) and azimuthal angle (-pi to pi) into 10x10 histograms, scaled bin values to [0,1], and used these as input to a convolutional neural network with two convolutional layers, two max-pooling layers, and two fully-connected layers trained with binary cross-entropy loss. The network was trained using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) with learning rate 0.01 over 800 epochs, with 80% of the 10000-event dataset used for training and 20% for validation.

## Key numbers

- Overall classification accuracy of 98% for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 11 GeV (both hard and soft potentials)
- Classification accuracy of 98% for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 GeV
- Classification accuracy of 94% for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 GeV
- For the classifier trained at sqrt(s) = 11 GeV: 77% accuracy on 7 GeV events, 98% on 11 GeV events, 90% on 14 GeV events
- For the classifier trained at sqrt(s) = 14 GeV: 74% accuracy on 7 GeV events, 96% on 11 GeV events, 98% on 14 GeV events
- For the classifier trained at sqrt(s) = 7 GeV: 94% accuracy on 7 GeV events, 55% on 11 GeV events, 50% on 14 GeV events
- Minimum training data needed for accuracy above 96%: only 100 images (1% of dataset) for sqrt(s) = 11 and 14 GeV
- With Adam optimizer and 4% training fraction at sqrt(s) = 7 GeV: 92% accuracy reached in approximately 30 epochs (versus 2500 epochs with SGD)
- With Adam optimizer and 1% training fraction at sqrt(s) = 11 GeV: rapid accuracy increase after 30 epochs

## Context

This work extends prior efforts applying deep learning to heavy-ion collision problems, specifically building on studies that used neural networks for equation of state identification (EoSdl, QCDtransitdl, spinoclumpml). The authors note that traditional methods, such as directed flow (v1) slope measurements, require about one million events per data point to distinguish between hard and soft equations of state, whereas their CNN approach works on an event-by-event basis. The paper enables future work on continuous parameter spaces, where the model would learn to extract fractions of soft and stiff equations of state from combined EoS calculations, and ultimately multi-dimensional continuous parameter extraction from fixed functional forms of the equation of state.
