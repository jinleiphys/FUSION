# Longitudinal asymmetry in heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> The longitudinal asymmetry arises in relativistic heavy ion collisions due to fluctuation in the number of participating nucleons. This asymmetry causes a shift in the center of mass rapidity of the participant zone. The rapidity shift as well as the longitudinal asymmetry have been found to be significant at the top LHC energy for collisions of identical nuclei. We study the longitudinal asymmetry and its effect on charged particle rapidity distribution and anisotropic flow parameters at relatively lower RHIC energies using a model calculation. The rapidity shift is found to be more pronounced for peripheral collisions, smaller systems and also for lower beam energies due to longitudinal asymmetry. A detailed study has been done by associating the average rapidity shift to a polynomial relation where the coefficients of this polynomial characterize the effect of the asymmetry. We show that the rapidity shift may affect observables significantly at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that longitudinal asymmetry (rapidity shift of the participant zone) significantly affects charged particle rapidity distributions and anisotropic flow parameters at RHIC energies, with the effect being more pronounced for peripheral collisions, smaller collision systems (Cu+Cu vs Au+Au), and lower beam energies (62.4 GeV vs 200 GeV), but the magnitude of the effect depends on the specific observable and centrality.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string melting (AMPT-SM) to simulate Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 62.4 GeV. Event-by-event rapidity shifts y0 and spectator asymmetries alpha_spec are calculated from a Monte Carlo Glauber initial state. The authors then compare shifted and unshifted charged particle rapidity distributions and anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 across different centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The maximum rapidity shift y0 for 0-5% central collisions is y0 = +/-0.22 for Cu+Cu and y0 = +/-0.14 for Au+Au at 200A GeV
- The linear coefficient c1 from polynomial fitting of the ratio of symmetric to asymmetric rapidity distributions is larger for central Au+Au collisions than for peripheral Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV
- The c1 values for Cu+Cu collisions are slightly smaller than Au+Au results at the same centrality bin
- The quadratic coefficient c2 is found to be two orders of magnitude smaller than c1, and c3 is even smaller
- For 62.4 GeV Au+Au collisions, c1 is larger compared to the 200A GeV results
- The spread in y0 is slightly larger for lower beam energy (62.4 GeV) compared to 200A GeV for a given centrality bin

## Context

This work extends studies of longitudinal asymmetry previously performed at LHC energies by the ALICE Collaboration (2013) for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV, and by Raniwala et al. using model frameworks. It also builds on theoretical work by Bzdak and Krishnan on rapidity correlations from fireball fluctuations, and by fluid dynamical simulations of longitudinal fluctuations on azimuthal anisotropy. The results enable experimental analysis at RHIC to classify events by longitudinal asymmetry and test initial state effects on final state observables, particularly for smaller systems and lower energies where fluctuations are larger.
