# Primordial lithium abundance problem of BBN and baryonic density in the universe

> Prediction of the primordial abundances of elements in the big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) is one of the three strong evidences for the big bang model. Precise knowledge of the baryon-to-photon ratio of the Universe from observations of the anisotropies of cosmic microwave background radiation has made the Standard BBN a parameter-free theory. Although, there is a good agreement over a range of nine orders of magnitude between abundances of light elements deduced from observations and calculated in primordial nucleosynthesis, there remains a yet-unexplained discrepancy of $^7$Li abundance higher by a factor of $\sim 3$ when calculated theoretically. The primordial abundances depend on the astrophysical nuclear reaction rates and on three additional parameters, the number of light neutrino flavours, the neutron lifetime and the baryon-to-photon ratio in the universe. The effect of the modification of thirty-five reaction rates on light element abundance yields in BBN was investigated earlier by us. In the present work we have incorporated the most recent values of neutron lifetime and the baryon-to-photon ratio and further modified $^3$He($^4$He,$γ$)$^7$Be reaction rate which is used directly for estimating the formation of $^7$Li as a result of $β^+$ decay as well as the reaction rates for t($^4$He,$γ$)$^7$Li and d($^4$He,$γ$)$^6$Li. We find that these modifications reduce the theoretically calculated abundance of $^7$Li by $\sim 12\%$.

## Key claim

Using the most recent values for neutron lifetime (880.3 ± 1.1 s) and baryon-to-photon ratio (η = 6.0914 ± 0.0438 × 10⁻¹⁰), together with updated reaction rates for ³He(⁴He,γ)⁷Be, t(⁴He,γ)⁷Li, and d(⁴He,γ)⁶Li, reduces the theoretically calculated abundance of ⁷Li by only ~12%, which is insufficient to resolve the factor-of-~3 discrepancy between standard BBN predictions and observed primordial lithium.

## Method

The authors used the Kawano/Wagoner BBN code, incorporating the most recent experimental neutron lifetime and the baryon-to-photon ratio from WMAP/Planck, and replaced the Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates for ³He(⁴He,γ)⁷Be, t(⁴He,γ)⁷Li, and d(⁴He,γ)⁶Li with new parametrizations from Dubovichenko (2017) valid up to 5T₉. They compared the resulting light-element abundance yields (⁴He, D, ³He, ⁷Li) with their previous 2012 calculations and with other recent BBN calculations by Coc et al. (2014, 2015) and Cyburt et al. (2016).

## Key numbers

- ⁷Li/H theoretical yield in this work: (4.447 ± 0.067) × 10⁻¹⁰, compared to observed value of (1.58⁺⁰·³⁵₋₀·₂₈) × 10⁻¹⁰ from Sbordone et al. (2010)
- ⁷Li theoretical abundance reduced by ~12% relative to standard BBN, but still overestimated by a factor of 2.27 when comparing lower theoretical limit to upper observational limit
- Neutron lifetime used: τₙ = 880.3 ± 1.1 s (Olive et al. 2014)
- Baryon-to-photon ratio used: η = (6.0914 ± 0.0438) × 10⁻¹⁰
- ⁴He mass fraction from this work: 0.2467 ± 0.0003; observed value: 0.2449 ± 0.0040 (Aver et al. 2015)
- D/H relative abundance from this work: (2.623 ± 0.031) × 10⁻⁵; observed value: (2.53 ± 0.04) × 10⁻⁵ (Cooke et al. 2014)
- ³He/H relative abundance from this work: (1.067 ± 0.005) × 10⁻⁵; observed value: (1.1 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁵ (Bania et al. 2002)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Mishra et al. 2012) which modified thirty-five reaction rates in the BBN network, and is compared with recent calculations by Coc et al. (2014, 2015) and Cyburt et al. (2016) who used the same τₙ and η values with the PARTHENOPE code. The paper confirms that conventional nuclear physics adjustments to reaction rates and fundamental constants cannot resolve the primordial lithium problem, and suggests that non-standard scenarios such as decaying massive particles, negatively charged relic particles, photon cooling, or modified gravity may need to be considered.
