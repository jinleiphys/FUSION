# Study of deeply virtual Compton scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> This study presents the impact of future measurements of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) with the ePIC detector at the electron-ion collider (EIC), currently under construction at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The considered process is sensitive to generalized parton distributions (GPDs), the understanding of which is a cornerstone of the EIC physics programme. Our study marks a milestone in the preparation of DVCS measurements at EIC and provides a reference point for future analyses. In addition to presenting distributions of basic kinematic variables obtained with the latest ePIC design and simulation software, we examine the impact of future measurements on the understanding of nucleon tomography and DVCS Compton form factors, which are directly linked to GPDs. We also assess the impact of radiative corrections and background contribution arising from exclusive $π^0$ production.

## Key claim
This study demonstrates that future DVCS measurements with the ePIC detector at the EIC will provide unprecedented access to nucleon tomography and Compton form factors, but the analysis is restricted to certain observables and relies on the collinear approximation for radiative corrections, meaning the results should be interpreted within those model considerations.

## Method
The authors used the EpIC Monte Carlo event generator to simulate DVCS and Bethe-Heitler events for three beam energy configurations (5 GeV x 41 GeV, 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 18 GeV x 275 GeV), processed through the full ePIC detector simulation. They assessed detector acceptance and kinematic distributions, evaluated radiative corrections using the collinear approximation up to order alpha^2, estimated the pi0 background contribution, and performed extraction of nucleon tomography via b-slope analysis and Compton form factors from beam spin asymmetry pseudo-data using artificial neural network techniques.

## Key numbers
- Integrated luminosity assumed for all distributions: 10 fb^-1
- Acceptance probabilities for reconstructing all three final-state particles: 0.49 for 5 GeV x 41 GeV, 0.48 for 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 0.29 for 18 GeV x 275 GeV
- Scattered electron reconstruction probability: 0.90 for 5 GeV x 41 GeV and 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 0.87 for 18 GeV x 275 GeV
- Scattered proton reconstruction probability: 0.76 for 5 GeV x 41 GeV, 0.90 for 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 0.81 for 18 GeV x 275 GeV
- Photon reconstruction probability: 0.72 for 5 GeV x 41 GeV, 0.59 for 10 GeV x 100 GeV, 0.46 for 18 GeV x 275 GeV
- Pi0 background contribution to DVCS sample does not exceed approximately 1% at the lowest beam energy configuration (5 GeV x 41 GeV), dropping by up to an order of magnitude at higher energies
- Kinematic cuts applied: 0.01 less than y less than 0.85

## Context
This work extends the earlier EIC feasibility study by Aschenauer et al. (2013) and the comprehensive yellow report by AbdulKhalek et al. (2021), using the latest ePIC detector design and simulation software. The study provides a new reference point for future DVCS phenomenology at the EIC, covering kinematic domains previously probed by HERA at low xB and by COMPASS, HERMES, and future JLab experiments at intermediate xB. The results enable improved constraints on generalized parton distributions and the total angular momentum of partons via the Ji sum rule, while also highlighting the need for future studies of higher-twist contributions and alternative kinematic reconstruction methods.
