# Initial state in relativistic nuclear collisions and Color Glass Condensate

> In this talk, I discuss recent works related to the pre-hydrodynamical stages of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework provides a QCD-based description of the pre-hydrodynamical stages of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, but fixed-order calculations do not match smoothly onto hydrodynamical behavior; the classical statistical approximation (CSA) may bridge this gap, though results depend on the choice of initial conditions and the CSA suffers from non-renormalizability when vacuum fluctuations are used.

## Method

The paper reviews the CGC framework, where fast partons are treated as a color current and soft gluons as gauge fields, with the JIMWLK renormalization group equation governing the probability distribution of color charge density. At leading order, observables are obtained from classical Yang-Mills equations solved numerically; at next-to-leading order, instabilities from gluon pair production cause exponential growth of longitudinal pressure. The classical statistical approximation is then introduced, solving fully non-linear classical field equations with fluctuating initial conditions, using either vacuum-like fluctuations (centered on the LO field with variance from linearized Yang-Mills) or particle-like fluctuations (with large occupancy and zero central value).

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum \(Q_s\) is an \(x\)-dependent momentum scale; roughly \(\alpha_s^{-2} Q_s^2\) is the gluon density per unit transverse area, and \(\alpha_s^{-2} Q_s^2 / k_\perp^2\) is the gluon occupation number.
* For QCD at leading log accuracy, \(\eta/s \approx 5.1 / (g^4 \ln(2.4/g))\).
* In the strong coupling limit of SUSY \(N=4\) Yang-Mills, \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\).
* With particle-like initial fluctuations, the ratio \(P_L/P_T\) follows a self-similar evolution \(P_L/P_T \sim \tau^{-2/3}\), intermediate between free streaming (\(\sim \tau^{-2}\)) and constant anisotropy.
* In the CGC, the dimensionless ratio \(n/\epsilon^{3/4}\) is initially \(g^{-1/2}\) (large at weak coupling), while it is of order unity in thermal equilibrium.

## Context

This talk reviews recent works extending the CGC description of heavy ion collisions beyond leading order, addressing the long-standing puzzle of why hydrodynamical models work so well despite the CGC predicting a very different initial state. The paper discusses the JIMWLK equation (Balitsky, 1996; Iancu et al., 2001) and its NLO extensions (Lappi and Mantysaari, 2015; Grabowski, 2014; Kovner et al., 2014), the classical statistical approximation (Aarts and Smit, 1999; Epelbaum and Gelis, 2013), and comparisons with the color flux tube model (Ryblewski and Florkowski, 2011). The results enable future work on matching CGC calculations to hydrodynamics and studying Bose-Einstein condensation in heavy ion collisions.
