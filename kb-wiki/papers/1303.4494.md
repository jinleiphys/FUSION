# Polarization of the neutron induced from hadronic weak interactions in the photo-disintegration of the deuteron

> New observables with which we can study the two-nucleon weak interactions at low energies are considered. In the breakup of the deuteron by photons, polarization of outgoing neutrons can depend on the parity-violating component of two-nucleon interactions. We express the parity-violating polarization in general forms, and perform numerical calculations with a pionless effective field theory. The theory has unknown parity-violating low energy constants, and the results are expressed in linear combination of them. We discuss the results and their implication to the understanding of the hadronic weak interactions.

## Key claim

The parity-violating (PV) component of two-nucleon weak interactions can induce non-zero neutron polarization along the radial (\(P_{z'}\)) and colatitude (\(P_{x'}\)) directions in the photo-disintegration of the deuteron (\(d\gamma \to \vec{n}p\)), which are free from parity-conserving (PC) contamination; the magnitude of \(P_{z'}\) may be larger than the well-studied asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in \(\vec{n}p \to d\gamma\) by an order of magnitude, but the numerical values depend on unknown PV low-energy constants (LECs) and are expressed as linear combinations of them.

## Method

The authors used a pionless effective field theory (EFT) with dibaryon fields as auxiliary fields for the two-nucleon \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) states. They constructed the parity-violating (PV) Lagrangian at leading order (LO) in terms of three unknown PV LECs (\(h^{0t}_d\), \(h^{0s}_d\), \(h^1_d\)) representing \(dNN\) vertices that couple \(S\)-wave states to \(P\)-wave states. The amplitudes for \(d\gamma \to \vec{n}p\) were calculated at LO including both PC and PV contributions, and the neutron polarizations \(P_{x'}\) and \(P_{z'}\) were expressed as linear combinations of the three PV LECs with calculable coefficients \(c^T_x\) and \(c^T_z\) as functions of photon energy and scattering angle.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient \(c^1_z\) (for the iso-vector PV LEC \(h^1_d\)) in \(P_{z'}\) reaches a magnitude greater than \(2\times 10^{-2}\) at forward or backward angles at \(E^{lab}_\gamma = 2.75\) MeV, dominating over \(c^{0t}_z\) and \(c^{0s}_z\) which do not exceed \(7.5\times 10^{-3}\) in the energy range considered.
- The asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in \(\vec{n}p \to d\gamma\) is related to \(h^1_d\) as \(A_\gamma = -3.2\times 10^{-3} h^1_d\), and its measured value is \(-(1.5\pm 4.8)\times 10^{-8}\) (Alberi 1988).
- The PV polarization \(P_\gamma\) in \(np \to d\gamma\) at threshold is \(P_\gamma = -(2.59 h^{0t}_d - 1.01 h^{0s}_d) \times 10^{-2}\).
- At \(\theta \simeq 22^\circ\) and \(E^{lab}_\gamma = 2.75\) MeV, \(c^{0t}_x \simeq -8.8\times 10^{-3}\), \(c^{0s}_x \simeq -2.0\times 10^{-3}\), and \(c^1_x \simeq 2.0\times 10^{-4}\).
- At \(\theta = 90^\circ\) and \(E^{lab}_\gamma = 2.75\) MeV, \(c^1_x \simeq 4.4\times 10^{-3}\), while \(c^{0t}_x\) and \(c^{0s}_x\) are of order \(10^{-4}\) or less.
- At \(\theta \simeq 157^\circ\) and \(E^{lab}_\gamma = 2.75\) MeV, \(c^1_x \simeq 1.15\times 10^{-2}\), with ratios \(c^{0t}_x / c^1_x \simeq 0.76\) and \(c^{0s}_x / c^1_x \simeq 0.27\).

## Context

This work extends the study of parity-violating (PV) observables in two-nucleon systems beyond the previously calculated asymmetry in \(\vec{n}p \to d\gamma\) (Savage 2001) and the polarization in \(np \to d\gamma\) (Shin 2010), using a pionless EFT with dibaryon fields. The new observables \(P_{x'}\) and \(P_{z'}\) in \(d\gamma \to \vec{n}p\) are complementary to existing PV measurements such as the longitudinal asymmetry in \(\vec{p}p\) scattering (Everett 1991) and the ongoing NPDGamma experiment at SNS (Gericke 2011), and they offer a pathway to determine the three PV LECs (\(h^{0t}_d\), \(h^{0s}_d\), \(h^1_d\)) in the \(np\) system. The results should be cross-checked with calculations using the DDH meson-exchange potential (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein 1980), which is work in progress (Liu 2013).
