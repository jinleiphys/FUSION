# New dynamical pair breaking effect

> A dynamical pair breaking effect is evidenced at very low excitation energies. For this purpose, a new set of time-dependent coupled channel equations for pair-breaking in superfluid systems are deduced from the variational principle. These equations give the probability to destroy or to create a Cooper pair under the action of some perturbations or when the mean field varies in time. The odd-even effect in fission is investigated within the model as an example. For this purpose, the time-dependent probability to find the system in a seniority-one or in a seniority-two state is restricted in the sense that the perturbations are considered only in the avoided crossing regions.

## Key claim

A dynamical pair breaking effect is evidenced at very low excitation energies, where the system does not possess enough energy to break a pair by statistical means. The effect is restricted to perturbations produced only in avoided crossing regions, which does not affect the essential features of the model but leads to a considerable simplification of the mathematical apparatus.

## Method

A new set of time-dependent coupled channel equations for pair-breaking in superfluid systems were deduced from the variational principle, using a many-body Hamiltonian with pairing residual interactions that depends on time-dependent collective parameters. The equations give the probability to destroy or to create a Cooper pair under the action of perturbations or when the mean field varies in time, and were applied to the odd-even effect in fission by considering only the radial coupling in avoided crossing regions between levels with the same spin projection Omega=1/2.

## Key numbers

* The inter-nuclear velocities tested ranged from 10^4 to 10^6 m/s, corresponding to barrier penetration times in the interval [1.4 x 10^-18, 1.4 x 10^-20] s.
* At the elongation R=20 fm, the probabilities of seniority-zero and seniority-two states were computed as a function of excitation energy.
* The even-even fragmentation dominates at fragment excitation energies above 4-6 MeV.
* 17 levels with spin projection Omega=1/2 were selected from the N=60 single-particle energies above and below the Fermi level for the 234U fission calculation.
* 16 seniority-two configurations were constructed, considering pair creation or annihilation possible only between adjacent levels.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Zener effect formalism (Landau 1932, Zener 1932) to include pairing residual interactions, generalizing previous time-dependent equations for molecular potentials (Mirea 2008). The model provides a dynamical alternative to statistical explanations of the odd-even effect in fission fragment yields (Rejmund 2000, Avrigeanu 2001), which previously required modifications of level densities for odd-even and even-even partitions. The formalism can be adjusted for other processes, including condensed matter systems where the collective variables could be the amplitude of an external applied field.
