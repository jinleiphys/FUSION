# Revisiting the centrality definition and observable centrality dependence of relativistic heavy-ion collisions in PACIAE model

> We improve the centrality definition in impact parameter in PACIAE model responding the fact reported by the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations that the maximum impact parameter in heavy ion collisions should be extended to 20 $fm$. Meanwhile the PACIAE program is updated to a new version of PACIAE 2.2.2 with convenience of studying the elementary nuclear collisions, proton-nucleus collisions, and the nucleus-nucleus collisions in one unified program version. The new impact parameter definition together with the optical Glauber model calculated impact parameter bin, $N_{part}$, and $N_{coll}$ in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energies are consistent with the improved MC-Glauber model ones within the error bar. The charged-particle pseudorapidity and the transverse momentum distributions in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV simulated by PACIAE 2.2.2 well reproduce the ALICE experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors improve the centrality definition in the PACIAE model by resetting the coefficient \(f\) in the impact parameter formula to 4 for nucleus-nucleus collisions and 2 for proton-nucleus collisions, responding to the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS observation that the maximum impact parameter should be extended to 20 fm; the new definition yields optical Glauber results consistent with improved MC-Glauber model results within error bars, except in the most peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors updated the PACIAE model to version 2.2.2, modifying the impact parameter formula \(b_{max}=R_A+R_B+f\times d\) (with \(d=0.546\) fm) by increasing the coefficient \(f\) from 1 to 2 for nucleus-nucleus collisions and from 2 to 4 for proton-nucleus collisions, and slicing the last \(b\) bin up to a \(b_{cut}\) rather than extending to 20 fm. They then used the optical Glauber model within PACIAE 2.2.2 to calculate impact parameter bins, \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle\), and \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle\) for Pb-Pb, p-Pb, Xe-Xe, Au-Au, and Cu-Cu collisions at various energies, and compared these with improved MC-Glauber model results from Loizides (2018). Finally, they simulated charged-particle pseudorapidity and transverse momentum distributions in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV and compared them with ALICE data.

## Key numbers

* For Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the optical Glauber model in PACIAE gives \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle = 1871.8\) and \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle = 375.4\) for the 0-5% centrality bin, compared to MC-Glauber values of \(1762\pm147\) and \(384.3\pm16.6\).
* For p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the optical Glauber model gives \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle = 13.1\) and \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle = 11.2\) for the 0-5% centrality bin, compared to MC-Glauber values of \(13.68\pm3.51\) and \(14.68\pm3.51\).
* For Xe-Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.44\) TeV, the optical Glauber model gives \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle = 992.5\) and \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle = 234.4\) for the 0-5% centrality bin, compared to MC-Glauber values of \(942.5\pm92.1\) and \(236.5\pm10.0\).
* For Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=0.2\) TeV, the optical Glauber model gives \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle = 1075.5\) and \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle = 345.5\) for the 0-5% centrality bin, compared to MC-Glauber values of \(1053\pm92.2\) and \(351\pm17.8\).
* For Cu-Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=0.2\) TeV, the optical Glauber model gives \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle = 217.9\) and \(\langle N_{\rm part}\rangle = 105.3\) for the 0-5% centrality bin, compared to MC-Glauber values of \(203.6\pm24.9\) and \(106.5\pm6.21\).
* The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross sections used are 41.6 mb at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=0.2\) TeV, 67.6 mb at 5.02 TeV, and 68.4 mb at 5.44 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the PACIAE model (Sa, 2012; Zhou, 2014) by updating its centrality definition to match the improved MC-Glauber model of Loizides (2018), which was developed in response to ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS observations that the maximum impact parameter in heavy-ion collisions should be extended to 20 fm. The updated PACIAE 2.2.2 now provides a unified framework for studying elementary nuclear collisions, proton-nucleus collisions, and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and its simulated charged-particle distributions in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV reproduce ALICE data well, enabling future studies of centrality-dependent observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
