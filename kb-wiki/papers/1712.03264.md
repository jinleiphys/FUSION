# SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model: Magnetic properties of QCD matter in thermal and dense medium

> The linear-sigma model, in which information about confining gluons is included through the Polyakov-loop potential (PLSM), is considered in order to perform a systematic study for various magnetic properties of QCD matter under extreme conditions of high temperatures and densities and finite magnetic field strengths. The introduction of magnetic field to the PLSM Lagrangian requires suitable utilization of Landau quantization, modification in the dispersion relations, and momentum-space dimension-reduction. We observed that increasing the magnetic field leads to filling-up lower Landau levels first and decreasing the number of occupied levels. We conclude that the population of Landau levels is most sensitive to the magnetic field and to the quark charges. The influences of finite magnetic field on the temperature dependence of chiral and deconfinement order-parameter(s) are studied. We present estimations for the magnetization, the magnetic susceptibility, the permeability and the catalysis properties of QCD matter as functions of temperature. The dependences of the resulting freezeout parameters, temperatures and baryon chemical potentials on the corresponding magnetic field strengths have been analyzed, as well. These calculations are compared with recent lattice QCD simulations, whenever available. We conclude that the QCD matter seems to have paramagnetic property at temperatures greater than the critical one. There is an evidence for weak diamagnetic property at low temperatures. Last but not least, we observe that the magnetic catalysis is inverse, namely the critical temperatures decrease with increasing the magnetic field.

## Key claim
The paper claims that QCD matter exhibits inverse magnetic catalysis, meaning the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition decreases with increasing magnetic field strength, contrary to the usual expectation of magnetic catalysis. This result is conditional on the maximum occupation of Landau levels and the specific implementation of the Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) with Landau quantization.

## Method
The authors extended the SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) to include finite magnetic fields by implementing Landau quantization, modifying the quark dispersion relations, and reducing the momentum-space dimension from three to one. The free energy was minimized globally to obtain the chiral quark condensates (sigma fields) and deconfinement order parameters (Polyakov loops) as functions of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and magnetic field strength. Magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and permeability were then derived from the free energy density.

## Key numbers
- At eB = 0.2 GeV² and vanishing baryon chemical potential, the normalized chiral condensates were analyzed for maximum Landau levels (MLL) of 14, 51, and infinity, showing that increasing Landau levels sharpens the phase transition and decreases the critical temperature.
- At eB = m²_π and μ = 0 MeV, up-quarks occupy up to 62 Landau levels, while down- and strange-quarks occupy 124 levels each.
- At eB = 10 m²_π and μ = 0 MeV, up-quarks have MLL = 3, while down- and strange-quarks have MLL = 6.
- At T = 50 MeV and eB = m²_π, the up-quark has 31 MLL, while each down- and strange-quark has 62 MLL.
- At T = 100 MeV and eB = 15 m²_π, MLL for up-quark is 2 and 4 for each down- and strange-quark.
- The vacuum light chiral condensate was taken as σ_l0 = 92.5 MeV and the strange condensate as σ_s0 = 94.2 MeV, with sigma meson mass m_σ = 800 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous PLSM studies by Tawfik (2014) and others, where opposite results for magnetic catalysis were obtained, and compares with lattice QCD simulations by Bruckmann (2013) and Preis (2011). The paper enables mapping of the QCD phase diagram (temperature vs. baryon chemical potential) under varying magnetic field strengths and provides estimates for magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and permeability that can be tested against future lattice simulations. The finding of inverse magnetic catalysis (critical temperature decreasing with magnetic field) contradicts the standard magnetic catalysis picture and suggests a remapping of the boundaries between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases.
