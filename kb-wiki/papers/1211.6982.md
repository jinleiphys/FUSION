# Highly excited states of baryons in large $N_c$ QCD

> The masses of highly excited negative parity baryons belonging to the $N$ = 3 band are calculated in the $1/N_c$ expansion method of QCD. We use a procedure which allows to write the mass formula by using a small number of linearly independent operators. The numerical fit of the dynamical coefficients in the mass formula show that the pure spin and pure flavor terms are dominant in the expansion, like for the $N$ = 1 band. We present the trend of some important dynamical coefficients as a function of the band number $N$ or alternatively of the excitation energy.

## Key claim

The masses of highly excited negative parity baryons in the N = 3 band are calculated using the 1/N_c expansion method, and the numerical fits show that pure spin and pure flavor terms dominate the expansion, similar to the N = 1 band; however, the analysis is limited by scarce data for hyperons in this band, with only one four-star hyperon (Lambda(2100)7/2-) included.

## Method

The authors used a procedure where all identical quarks are treated on the same footing with an exact wave function in orbital-flavor-spin space, as opposed to the standard Hartree-type core-plus-excited-quark approach. They constructed a mass operator with a small number of linearly independent operators, including spin-orbit, spin-spin, flavor, and tensor terms, and performed numerical fits to determine the dynamical coefficients c_i and d_i for the N = 3 band resonances. The fits were carried out with different combinations of operators to check the stability of the results.

## Key numbers

* Coefficient c_1 (the dominant mass term proportional to N_c) = 672 +/- 8 MeV from Fit 1, and 673 +/- 7 MeV from Fit 2, for the N = 3 band baryons.
* Coefficient c_2 (spin-orbit term) = 18 +/- 19 MeV from Fit 1, and 17 +/- 18 MeV from Fit 2, for the N = 3 band baryons.
* Coefficient c_3 (spin term) = 121 +/- 59 MeV from Fit 1, and 115 +/- 46 MeV from Fit 2, for the N = 3 band baryons.
* Coefficient c_4 (flavor/isospin term) = 202 +/- 41 MeV from Fit 1, and 200 +/- 40 MeV from Fit 2, for the N = 3 band baryons.
* Coefficient d_1 (strangeness breaking term) = 108 +/- 93 MeV from Fit 1, and 108 +/- 92 MeV from Fit 2, for the N = 3 band baryons.
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom values: 1.23 (Fit 1), 0.93 (Fit 2), 0.93 (Fit 3), 0.75 (Fit 4).

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of baryon resonances in the 1/N_c expansion method from previous studies of the N = 0, 1, 2, and 4 bands (Matagne 2005, Goity 2003, Matagne 2004) by completing the picture with the previously missing N = 3 band. The results show compatibility between the quark-shell picture and the meson-nucleon scattering picture from chiral soliton models, as demonstrated earlier by Cohen and Lebed and by Pirjol and Schat for low excited states with l = 1. The nearly linear behavior of c_1 as a function of band number N suggests a Regge trajectory that can be compared with standard constituent quark model results (Semay 2007, Buisseret 2008).
