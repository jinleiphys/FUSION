# Tensor Correlations Measured in 3He(e,e'pp)n

> We have measured the 3He(e,e'pp)n reaction at an incident energy of 4.7 GeV over a wide kinematic range. We identified spectator correlated pp and pn nucleon pairs using kinematic cuts and measured their relative and total momentum distributions. This is the first measurement of the ratio of pp to pn pairs as a function of pair total momentum, $p_{tot}$. For pair relative momenta between 0.3 and 0.5 GeV/c, the ratio is very small at low $p_{tot}$ and rises to approximately 0.5 at large $p_{tot}$. This shows the dominance of tensor over central correlations at this relative momentum.

## Key claim

The ratio of pp to pn spectator pair cross sections in 3He(e,e'pp)n, integrated over pair relative momenta between 0.3 and 0.5 GeV/c, is very small at low pair total momentum and rises to approximately 0.5 at large pair total momentum, demonstrating the dominance of tensor over central correlations at this relative momentum. This conclusion is conditional on the spectator pair selection requiring each spectator nucleon to carry less than 20% of the transferred energy and the leading nucleon's perpendicular momentum to be less than 0.3 GeV/c.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab was used to measure the 3He(e,e'pp)n reaction with a 4.7 GeV electron beam incident on a liquid 3He target. Events were selected with one leading nucleon and a spectator correlated NN pair by requiring that the two spectator nucleons each have less than 20% of the transferred energy and that the leading nucleon's momentum perpendicular to the momentum transfer vector be less than 0.3 GeV/c. The relative and total momentum distributions of the spectator pp and pn pairs were measured, and the ratio of pp to pn pair cross sections was computed as a function of pair total momentum.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy: 4.7 GeV
- Momentum transfer Q^2 peaks at approximately 1.5 (GeV/c)^2
- Ratio of pp to pn spectator pair integrated cross sections is about 1:4
- For pair relative momenta between 0.3 and 0.5 GeV/c, the pp to pn ratio is very small at pair total momentum below 0.1 GeV/c and rises to approximately 0.5 at pair total momentum above 0.2 GeV/c
- The pp to pn ratio at large pair total momentum is consistent with simple pair counting
- The pp pair prel distribution peaks at approximately 0.4 GeV/c with a tail extending to approximately 0.7 GeV/c
- The ptot distributions peak at approximately 0.25 GeV/c
- Systematic uncertainty is 15%, primarily due to low momentum proton detection efficiency

## Context

This measurement extends earlier work on 3He(e,e'pp)n at lower Q^2 (approximately 0.7 GeV^2) by Niyazov (2003) and provides a cleaner measurement at higher momentum transfer. The results are compared to one-body calculations by Golak (1995) and Laget (1987, 1988, 2004), as well as to direct two-nucleon knockout measurements in 12C(e,e'pN) by Subedi (2008) which found a pp to pn pair ratio of 1:18 at low pair total momentum. The observed increase in the pp to pn ratio with pair total momentum provides a signal for tensor correlation dominance and enables improved understanding of short-range correlations in nuclei, with implications for cold dense nuclear matter and neutron star physics.
