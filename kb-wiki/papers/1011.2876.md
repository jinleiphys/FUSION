# Phase diagram and critical endpoint for strongly-interacting quarks

> We introduce a method based on the chiral susceptibility, which enables one to draw a phase diagram in the chemical-potential/temperature plane for strongly-interacting quarks whose interactions are described by any reasonable gap equation, even if the diagrammatic content of the quark-gluon vertex is unknown. We locate a critical endpoint (CEP) at (μ^E,T^E) ~ (1.0,0.9)T_c, where T_c is the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at μ=0; and find that a domain of phase coexistence opens at the CEP whose area increases as a confinement length-scale grows.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method based on the chiral susceptibility that allows one to draw a phase diagram for strongly-interacting quarks using any reasonable gap equation, even when the diagrammatic content of the quark-gluon vertex is unknown. The critical endpoint (CEP) is located at approximately (1.0, 0.9) times the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration at zero chemical potential, but this location and the size of the phase coexistence region depend on the confinement length-scale and the vertex choice.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger gap equation for dressed quark propagators at nonzero temperature and chemical potential, using a simplified form of the Maris-Tandy gluon propagator model. They compare results from the rainbow truncation (leading-order vertex) with those from the Ball-Chiu dressed vertex Ansatz. The phase diagram is constructed by analyzing the chiral susceptibility in both the Nambu (chirally broken) and Wigner (chirally symmetric) phases, identifying regions of stability, metastability, and phase coexistence.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint location with Ball-Chiu vertex and sigma=0.5 GeV: (mu^E, T^E)/T_c = (1.13, 0.89), with T_c = 0.124 GeV and coexistence width Delta_C = 0.026 GeV on the mu-axis.
- Critical endpoint location with rainbow vertex and sigma=0.5 GeV: (mu^E, T^E)/T_c = (0.98, 0.90), with T_c = 0.133 GeV and coexistence width Delta_C = 0.220 GeV.
- At T=0 and mu=0, the chiral condensate is (-0.258 GeV)^3.
- At T=0, the upper bound on the domain of analyticity for the gap equation kernel is mu_a = 0.30 GeV, with the Nambu phase completely vanishing at mu_c^N = 0.314 GeV and the Wigner phase becoming stable at mu_c^W = 0.286 GeV.
- With mu=0, the Nambu phase is completely replaced by the Wigner phase at T = 124 MeV (using the Ball-Chiu vertex).

## Context

This work extends prior Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of the QCD phase diagram, which had used simpler truncations and had not described a region of coexisting phases. It contrasts with lattice-QCD results (Fodor 2001, Gavai 2004, deForcrand 2006, Li 2009) that give mu^E/T_c = 1.0-1.4 and T^E/T_c approximately 0.93, and with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Sasaki 2007, Costa 2008) that yield mu^E/T_c approximately 1.7 and T^E/T_c approximately 0.4. The method enables future use of sophisticated dressed-quark-gluon vertices in continuum studies of the phase diagram, and suggests that the CEP may be accessible to modern heavy-ion collider experiments.
