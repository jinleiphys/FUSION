# Quark Models of Baryon Masses and Decays

> The description of baryon resonance masses and decays in the constituent quark model is outlined, with emphasis on the potential-model approach combined with the 3P0 pair-creation model of strong decays. This approach allows the estimation of branching fractions for baryon states missing in pion nucleon elastic scattering analyses but expected to be present in electromagnetic production. Prospects for the discovery of hybrid baryons are discussed. A subjective list of the most important corrections required to this approach is presented.

## Key claim
The constituent quark model, when combined with the $^3P_0$ pair-creation model of strong decays, can estimate branching fractions for baryon states that are missing in pion-nucleon elastic scattering analyses but may be observable in electromagnetic production; however, the approach is not covariant and ignores Fock-space components beyond simple $qqq$ configurations, which is an important missing ingredient given that none of these states are narrow.

## Method
The author reviews the potential-model approach to baryon masses and decays, where constituent quarks move in two- and three-body potentials derived from flux-tube confinement (string tension $b\simeq 1.0$ GeV/fm) and one-gluon-exchange short-distance interactions. The dynamical problem is solved by expanding wave functions in a large harmonic-oscillator basis and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix. Strong decays are modeled using the $^3P_0$ pair-creation model, where a quark-antiquark pair with vacuum quantum numbers is created, and branching fractions are calculated for missing baryon states.

## Key numbers
- The string tension used in the confining potential is $b\simeq 1.0$ GeV/fm.
- The one-gluon-exchange interaction is responsible for the approximately 300 MeV splitting of the $\Delta(1232)$ and the nucleon.
- The lightest hybrid baryon states are predicted at roughly 1870 MeV for $N\frac{1}{2}^+$ and $N\frac{3}{2}^+$, with a model error of about $\pm 100$ MeV; $\Delta$ hybrid states are at about 2075 MeV with the same estimated error.
- The Roper resonance $N\frac{1}{2}^+(1440)$ has a width of roughly 350 MeV.
- Table 1 lists total widths and branching fractions for missing nonstrange baryons; for example, the $[N\frac{1}{2}^+]_4 (1880)$ state has a total width of 150 MeV and a $N\pi$ branching fraction of 0.05.

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Isgur (1978) and Capstick (1986) by combining potential-model mass calculations with the $^3P_0$ decay model of LeYaouanc (1978) to predict branching fractions for missing baryon states. It contrasts with quark-diquark models (Goldstein, 1988) and collective models (Bijker), which have fewer degrees of freedom and thus fewer excitations. The results enable experimental searches for missing states in electromagnetic production channels at facilities like Jefferson Laboratory, and the discovery of additional positive-parity excited states could rule out diquark-clustering models.
