# Spectral change of sigma and omega mesons in a dense nuclear medium

> The spectra of the scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) mesons in nuclear matter are studied in detail using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. It is shown that above normal nuclear matter density the effects of $σ$-$ω$ mixing and the decay of the $σ$ into $2π$ change the spectra of $σ$ and $ω$ mesons considerably. As in Quantum Hadrodynamics we find a remarkable spectral change in the $σ$ meson and the longitudinal mode of the $ω$ meson, namely a two-peak structure.

# Spectral change of sigma and omega mesons in a dense nuclear medium

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the authors show that above normal nuclear matter density the sigma-omega mixing and the decay of the sigma into two pions produce a two-peak structure in the sigma meson spectrum and the longitudinal mode of the omega meson, but this effect is moderated compared to earlier Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) calculations because the in-medium omega width and the density-dependent sigma-nucleon coupling constant weaken the mixing.

## Method

The authors calculated the spectral functions of the sigma and omega mesons in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature using the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) within the QMC model, where nucleons are described by the MIT bag model and mesons couple to confined quarks rather than point-like nucleons. They included sigma-omega mixing, a pion-loop contribution to the sigma self-energy to account for its decay into two pions, and an in-medium omega width that increases linearly with density. The sigma-nucleon coupling constant was made density-dependent through the QMC self-consistent treatment of the nuclear ground state, and monopole form factors were introduced at the meson-nucleon vertices.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Saturation energy: E/rho_B - M = -15.7 MeV at rho_0
- Effective nucleon mass at rho_0: M*/M = 0.81
- Incompressibility: K = 281 MeV
- Sigma-nucleon coupling constant at zero density: g_s^2(0) = 61.85
- Omega-nucleon coupling constant: g_v^2 = 62.61
- At rho_0, the sigma-nucleon coupling constant decreases by about 9%
- Free sigma width: Gamma_sigma^0 = 300 MeV, giving g_{sigma pi} = 18.33
- Free omega width: Gamma_omega^0 = 9.8 MeV
- In-medium omega width: Gamma_omega^* = Gamma_omega^0 + 40 MeV x (rho_B/rho_0)
- Free sigma mass: m_sigma = 550 MeV
- Free omega mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Free nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV
- Cutoff parameter for monopole form factors: Lambda_N = Lambda_pi = 1.5 GeV
- Bag radius: R_0 = 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier QHD calculation of Saito, Tsushima, Thomas, and Williams (1998) which first reported the two-peak structure in the sigma and longitudinal omega spectra, by incorporating the QMC model's density-dependent coupling and finite nucleon size effects. The QMC model itself was developed by Guichon (1988) and extended by Guichon, Saito, Rodionov, and Thomas (1996) and Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas (1997). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that detecting a clear signal of the spectral change in cold dense nuclear matter may be difficult but remains an important challenge for future experiments.
