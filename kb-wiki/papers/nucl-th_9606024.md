# Electromagnetic Production of Kaons on the Nucleon

> Kaon photo- and electroproduction in all six isospin channels are investigated by means of an isobaric model. It is found that existing models can only partially describe the current experimental data. Moreover, the extracted coupling constants are mostly inconsistent with the prediction of SU(3) symmetry or the information from hadronic reactions. In order to further analyze this problem, we introduce a form factor at the hadronic vertices $KNY$ (with $Y=Λ~ \textrm{or}~ Σ$) and study its behavior qualitatively. Our result suggests the need of more accurate experiments, especially in the charged $Σ$ and the neutral $K^0$ channels.

## Key claim

Existing isobaric models can only partially describe kaon photo- and electroproduction data, and extracted coupling constants are mostly inconsistent with SU(3) symmetry predictions; introducing a hadronic form factor at the KNY vertices can improve the model, especially at higher energies, but the results are qualitative and suggest the need for more accurate experiments.

## Method

The authors used an isobaric model with standard Born terms, intermediate K*-exchange, and selected N* resonances (S11(1650), P11(1710)) for KΣ and KΛ production plus Δ resonances (S31(1900), P31(1910)) for KΣ channels, applied to all six isospin channels of kaon photo- and electroproduction. To study the effect of a hadronic form factor, they multiplied the entire amplitude by a monopole form factor F(Λ_c, t) = (Λ_c² - m_K²)/(Λ_c² - t) and performed fits to all available KΣ and KΛ data, including preliminary data.

## Key numbers

- SU(3) prediction for g_{KΣN}/√(4π) = 1.09; fit to all KΣ and KΛ data with form factor (set IV) gave g_{KΣN}/√(4π) = 1.13
- SU(3) prediction for g_{KΛN}/√(4π) = -3.74; set IV gave g_{KΛN}/√(4π) = -3.09
- Cutoff parameter Λ_c = 0.859 GeV in set IV
- χ²/N for set IV = 5.94, compared to 7.18 for set III (fit without form factor) and 3.15 for fit to K⁺Σ⁰ data only (set I)
- Number of data points N = 754 in set IV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Mart, Bennhold, and Alfieri, 1995) on KΣ channels by including all six isospin channels simultaneously and introducing a hadronic form factor. It contradicts earlier models such as those by Saghai (1992) and Cota (1982) that overestimate cross sections at higher energies, and the model by Bennhold (1989) that overpredicts charged Σ channels by up to two orders of magnitude. The results enable future work to refine the isobaric model with more accurate experimental data, especially in charged Σ and neutral K⁰ channels.
