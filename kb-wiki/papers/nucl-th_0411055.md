# Quasi-elastic barrier distribution as a tool for investigating unstable nuclei

> The method of fusion barrier distribution has been widely used to interpret the effect of nuclear structure on heavy-ion fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier. We discuss a similar, but less well known, barrier distribution extracted from large-angle quasi-elastic scattering. We argue that this method has several advantages over the fusion barrier distribution, and offers an interesting tool for investigating unstable nuclei.

# Quasi-elastic barrier distribution as a tool for investigating unstable nuclei

## Key claim
The quasi-elastic barrier distribution, extracted from the first derivative of the ratio of quasi-elastic to Rutherford cross sections at backward angles, is an excellent analogue of the fusion barrier distribution and offers several experimental advantages that make it particularly suitable for studying unstable nuclei with low-intensity radioactive beams, although the quasi-elastic distribution may be somewhat smeared and thus less sensitive to nuclear structure effects than the fusion counterpart.

## Method
The authors derive a semi-classical formula for backward-angle elastic scattering that includes nuclear distortion effects to leading order, showing that the ratio of elastic to Rutherford cross sections at 180 degrees is proportional to the reflection probability of the Coulomb barrier multiplied by a nuclear distortion factor. They then demonstrate that the quasi-elastic barrier distribution, defined as the negative energy derivative of this ratio, can be expressed as a weighted sum of eigenchannel test functions analogous to the fusion barrier distribution, and validate the method using coupled-channels calculations with the code CCFULL for the $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm and $^{32}$Mg + $^{208}$Pb systems.

## Key numbers
- The quasi-elastic test function has a width of approximately $0.56 \hbar\Omega$, where $\hbar\Omega$ is the curvature of the Coulomb barrier.
- For the $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Sm system, the fusion and quasi-elastic barrier distributions are compared using a Woods-Saxon potential with surface diffuseness parameter $a = 0.65$ fm and $a = 1.05$ fm.
- The effective energy scaling for different scattering angles is given by $E_{\rm eff} = 2E \sin(\theta/2) / (1 + \sin(\theta/2))$.
- For the $^{32}$Mg + $^{208}$Pb system, coupled-channels calculations include quadrupole excitations in $^{32}$Mg up to the first $4^+$ state (rotational) or double phonon state (vibrational), and the single octupole phonon excitation at 2.615 MeV in $^{208}$Pb.

## Context
This work extends the fusion barrier distribution method pioneered by Rowley et al. (1991) and the quasi-elastic barrier distribution concept proposed by Timmers et al. (1995), building on earlier suggestions by Andres et al. (1988). The paper addresses the need for experimental techniques suitable for low-intensity radioactive beams, as fusion cross-section measurements require specialized recoil separators with low acceptance, while quasi-elastic measurements need only simple charged-particle detectors. The method enables future experiments at new-generation radioactive beam facilities to probe the structure of exotic neutron-rich nuclei, such as discriminating between rotational and vibrational quadrupole collectivity in $^{32}$Mg, which remains experimentally unresolved.
