# Evidence of effective axial U(1) symmetry restoration at high temperature QCD

> We study the axial U(1) symmetry at finite temperature in two-flavor lattice QCD. Employing the Mobius domain-wall fermions, we generate gauge configurations slightly above the critical temperature Tc with different lattice sizes L = 2-4 fm. Our action allows frequent topology tunneling while keeping good chiral symmetry close enough to that of overlap fermions. This allows us to recover full chiral symmetry by an overlap/domain-wall reweighting. Above the phase transition, a strong suppression of the low-lying modes is observed in both of overlap and domain-wall Dirac spectra. We, however, find a sizable violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation in the Mobius domain-wall Dirac eigenmodes, which dominates the signals of the axial U(1) symmetry breaking near the chiral limit. We also find that the use of overlap fermion only in the valence sector is dangerous since it suffers from the artifacts due to partial quenching. Reweighting the Mobius domain-wall fermion determinant to that of the overlap fermion, we observe the axial U(1) breaking to vanish in the chiral limit, which is stable against the changes of the lattice volume and lattice spacing.

## Key claim

The axial U(1) symmetry breaking signal in two-flavor lattice QCD slightly above the critical temperature vanishes in the chiral limit when the Möbius domain-wall fermion determinant is reweighted to that of the overlap fermion, but only if the reweighting is applied to both valence and sea quarks; using overlap fermions only in the valence sector suffers from partial quenching artifacts.

## Method

The authors generated gauge configurations using Möbius domain-wall fermions with two flavors at temperatures between 170 and 220 MeV, with spatial lattice sizes L = 2–4 fm and bare quark masses 2–25 MeV. They computed low-lying eigenvalues of both the Möbius domain-wall and overlap Dirac operators, then performed a reweighting of the Möbius domain-wall fermion determinant to that of the overlap fermion to achieve exact chiral symmetry. The axial U(1) breaking was quantified via the difference of flavor non-singlet meson susceptibilities Δπ−δ, which was extrapolated to the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature estimated as Tc = 175(5) MeV from the inflection point of the Polyakov loop
- Residual mass of Möbius domain-wall fermion ≤ 1 MeV around Tc, and smaller at higher temperatures
- Lattice spacing range: 0.074 fm (β = 4.30) to 0.127 fm (β = 4.05)
- Spatial lattice sizes L = 2–4 fm
- Bare quark mass range: 2–25 MeV
- Threshold for exact treatment of low eigenmodes of kernel operator HM: λMth = 400–600 MeV depending on parameters
- Violation of chiral symmetry in the overlap operator after reweighting kept at order ∼ 1 eV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of axial U(1) symmetry at finite temperature, including the authors' own work with overlap fermions (Cossu 2013) and work by the TWQCD collaboration (Chiu 2013), which supported the argument that ρ(0) vanishes above the chiral transition (Aoki 2012). It directly addresses the discrepancy with studies using Wilson fermions (Brandt 2016) and other lattice calculations (Bazavov 2012, Buchoff 2013, Dick 2015) that found a peak in ρ(λ) near zero, by demonstrating that such signals can arise from violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation in domain-wall fermions even when the residual mass is O(1 MeV). The results enable future studies of the order and critical exponents of the two-flavor QCD chiral phase transition and provide constraints for axion dark matter scenarios.
