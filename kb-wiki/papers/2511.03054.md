# Studying the thermoelectric properties of an anisotropic QGP medium

> We have studied how the thermoelectric properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are affected by a weak-momentum anisotropy arising from the asymptotic expansion of matter in the initial stages of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The highly energetic medium produced in such collisions exhibits a notable temperature difference between its central and peripheral regions. This temperature gradient induces an electric field whose magnitude per unit temperature gradient, in the limit of vanishing electric current, defines the Seebeck coefficient of the medium. We have calculated the Seebeck coefficient for both individual quark flavors and the entire QGP medium in the presence of expansion-induced anisotropy by solving the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within the kinetic theory framework. The partonic interactions are incorporated through their effective thermal masses within the quasiparticle model for an anisotropic QGP medium. We have observed that the magnitude of the Seebeck coefficient for each quark flavor as well as for the entire QGP medium increases in the presence of expansion-induced anisotropy, indicating a stronger induced electric field in the anisotropic medium compared to the isotropic case. Given that an increase in the Seebeck coefficient may lead to observable signatures such as charge asymmetries in particle distributions and to modifications in the transport behavior of the QGP, these results may provide useful input for future phenomenological studies investigating the internal structure and phase properties of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the Seebeck coefficient for each quark flavor and for the entire QGP medium increases in the presence of weak expansion-induced momentum anisotropy (ξ < 1), indicating a stronger induced electric field in the anisotropic medium compared to the isotropic case; this result is obtained within the relaxation time approximation and a quasiparticle model where the effective thermal masses of partons depend self-consistently on temperature, chemical potential, and the anisotropy parameter.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation to compute the electric current density induced by a temperature gradient in a weakly anisotropic QGP medium. The anisotropy was characterized by a parameter ξ quantifying the deviation from isotropy in momentum space, and the partonic interactions were incorporated through effective thermal masses within a quasiparticle model that depends on temperature, chemical potential, and ξ. The Seebeck coefficient was obtained by imposing the condition of vanishing electric current, separately for individual quark flavors (u, d, s) and for the combined QGP medium.

- The thermal mass of a quark in the anisotropic medium is given by m_{fTξ}^2 = m_{fT}^2 + (ξ g^2)/(3π^2) ∫ dp p [f_g + 1/2 (f_f + \bar{f}_f)] - (ξ β g^2)/(9π^2) ∫ dp p^2 f_g(1+f_g) - (ξ β g^2)/(18π^2) ∫ dp (p^3/ω_f) [f_f(1-f_f) + \bar{f}_f(1-\bar{f}_f)], where m_{fT}^2 = (g^2 T^2)/6 (1 + μ_f^2/(π^2 T^2)).
- The one-loop running coupling used is g^2 = 48π^2 / [(11N_c - 2N_f) ln(Λ^2/Λ_{\overline{MS}}^2)] with Λ_{\overline{MS}} = 0.176 GeV, Λ = 2π √(T^2 + μ_f^2/π^2) for quarks and Λ = 2π T for gluons.
- The relaxation time for quarks and antiquarks is τ_{f(\bar{f})} = 1 / [5.1 T α_s^2 log(1/α_s) [1 + 0.12(2N_f+1)]].

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Seebeck effect in hot QCD matter by focusing on expansion-induced momentum anisotropy as the primary source of anisotropy, in contrast to earlier works that considered magnetic field-induced anisotropy (Dey 2020, Dey 2021, Zhang 2021, Kurian 2021, Khan 2023) or hadronic matter (Bhatt 2019). Unlike prior approaches that treated anisotropy only through deformed distribution functions with fixed masses within a magnetized framework (Zhang 2021), the present analysis incorporates the anisotropy parameter self-consistently into the quasiparticle description, modifying effective thermal masses and dispersion relations. The results may provide input for future phenomenological studies investigating charge asymmetries and transport behavior of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions.
