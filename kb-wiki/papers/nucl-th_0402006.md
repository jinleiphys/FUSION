# Limiting temperature from microscopic equation of state

> The limiting temperature $T_{lim}$ of a series of nuclei is calculated employing a set of microscopic nuclear Equations of State (EoS). It is shown that the value of $T_{lim}$ is sensitive to the nuclear matter Equation of State used. Comparison with the values extracted in recent phenomenological analysis appears to favour a definite selection of EoS' s. On the basis of this phenomenological analysis, it seems therefore possible to check the microscopic calculations of the nuclear EoS at finite temperature, which is hardly accessible through other experimental informations.

# Limiting temperature from microscopic equation of state

## Key claim

The limiting temperature T_lim of a series of nuclei is sensitive to the nuclear matter Equation of State used, and comparison with phenomenological values extracted from experimental data appears to favour non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations over Dirac-Brueckner or chiral perturbation theory results, but this conclusion is conditional on the interpretation of the plateau in the caloric curve as marking the onset of mechanical instability.

## Method

The authors calculated the limiting temperature T_lim for finite nuclei by applying the liquid drop model with Coulomb and surface tension corrections to a set of microscopic nuclear matter Equations of State at finite temperature. The EoS were obtained from non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the Bonn B and Argonne v14 potentials (with three-body forces), from Dirac-Brueckner calculations using a simplified finite-temperature procedure based on the effective mass at zero temperature, and from chiral perturbation theory up to three-loop level. For each EoS, the coexistence point between liquid droplet and vapour was determined from chemical potential versus pressure plots, and T_lim was identified as the temperature at which this intersection disappears.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 18 MeV for the Bonn B potential (non-relativistic BHF), slightly below the value of T_c ≈ 20 MeV obtained for the Argonne v14 potential
- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 12 MeV for the Dirac-Brueckner EoS (compared to 10 MeV reported in prior work by Malfliet and Weber)
- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 25 MeV for the chiral perturbation theory EoS
- Surface tension at zero temperature α_0 = 1.14 MeV fm^{-2}, with temperature dependence α(T) = α_0 (1 + 3/2 T/T_c) (1 - T/T_c)^{3/2} using T_c = 20 MeV
- The ratio T_lim/T_c is close to 1/3 for non-relativistic BHF and chiral EoS, but closer to 1/4 for the Dirac-Brueckner case

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Levit and Bonche (1985) who first related the limiting temperature to the nuclear EoS, and the analysis by Song and Su (1991) who established a simple relationship between T_lim and T_c using Skyrme forces. The authors previously showed in Baldo and Song (2001) that the relationship is less systematic for microscopic EoS. The comparison with phenomenological values extracted by Natowitz et al. (2002) from multifragmentation data allows the authors to discriminate between different microscopic EoS, suggesting that non-relativistic BHF calculations are favoured over Dirac-Brueckner or chiral perturbation theory results. This opens the possibility of testing microscopic nuclear matter EoS at finite temperature, which is otherwise difficult to access experimentally.
