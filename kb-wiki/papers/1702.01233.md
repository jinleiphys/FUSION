# World-line construction of a covariant chiral kinetic theory

> We discuss a novel world-line framework for computations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Starting from the fermion determinant in the QCD effective action, we show explicitly how its real part can be expressed as a supersymmetric world-line action of spinning, colored, Grassmanian particles in background fields. Restricting ourselves for simplicity to spinning particles, we demonstrate how their constrained Hamiltonian dynamics arises for both massless and massive particles. In a semi-classical limit, this gives rise to the covariant generalization of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation; the derivation of the corresponding Wong equations for colored particles is straightforward. In a previous letter we outlined how Berry's phase arises in a non-relativistic adiabatic limit for massive particles. We extend the discussion here to systems with a finite chemical potential. We discuss a path integral formulation of the relative phase in the fermion determinant that places it on the same footing as the real part. We construct the corresponding anomalous world-line axial vector current and show in detail how the chiral anomaly appears. Our work provides a systematic framework for a relativistic kinetic theory of chiral fermions in the fluctuating topological backgrounds that generate the CME in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma. We outline some further applications of this framework in many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops a world-line framework for constructing a Lorentz-covariant chiral kinetic theory that makes no requirement of adiabaticity, in contrast to prior approaches that rely on Berry phase arguments valid only near the Fermi surface or in non-relativistic limits. The authors demonstrate that the real part of the fermion determinant gives the quasi-particle dynamics while the imaginary part (the relative phase) generates the chiral anomaly, showing these are distinct topological effects.

## Method

The authors start from the fermion determinant in the QCD effective action and express its real part as a supersymmetric world-line action of spinning, colored, Grassmanian particles in background fields using a coherent state formalism that enlarges the Dirac matrix structure from 4x4 to 8x8 dimensions. They employ a path integral formulation with Schwinger's proper-time representation, splitting the effective action into real and imaginary parts, and derive the constrained Hamiltonian dynamics for both massless and massive spinning particles. For the imaginary part (the relative phase), they use the D'Hoker-Gagné construction to rewrite it as a path integral with a regulating parameter that breaks chiral symmetry explicitly, then derive the anomalous axial vector current and the chiral anomaly equation using a non-perturbative variational method.

## Context

This work extends the world-line approach of D'Hoker and Gagné (1995) and connects to prior work on chiral kinetic theory by Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), and Chen (2013, 2014, 2015), which relied on Berry phase arguments in adiabatic limits. The framework provides a systematic method to construct a relativistic kinetic theory of chiral fermions in fluctuating topological backgrounds that generate the Chiral Magnetic Effect in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma, enabling matching between classical-statistical Glasma simulations at early times and anomalous hydrodynamics at late times. The authors explicitly address the distinction between topological effects from the chiral anomaly and those from geometric phases, following the critical discussion by Fujikawa and collaborators (2005).
