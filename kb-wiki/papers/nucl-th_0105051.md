# Chiral Effective Field Theories with explicit Spin 3/2 Degrees of Freedom---A Status Report

> Recent developments in calculations of low energy nucleon properties utilizing effective chiral field theories with explicit spin 3/2 matter fields are presented. In particular, the role of Delta resonances in microscopic calculations of the anomalous magnetic moments of the Nucleon is discussed and a comparison to lattice data is performed. Furthermore, the impact of Deltas on the isovector Pauli form factor of the Nucleon and the problems one faces in calculations of the isovector Nucleon-Delta transition form factors are addressed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that rescaling the leading-order gamma-N-Delta transition vertex (by placing the small Delta-N mass splitting in the denominator) substantially reorders the chiral expansion, enabling a leading-order fit to quenched lattice QCD data for the isovector anomalous magnetic moment that correctly extrapolates to the physical value at the physical pion mass, despite the fit being performed only at high pion masses.

## Method
The author compares three chiral effective field theory calculations of nucleon properties: SU(2) heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with only pion and nucleon degrees of freedom; the Small Scale Expansion (SSE) with pion, nucleon, and Delta(1232) degrees of freedom; and a modified SSE in which the leading gamma-N-Delta vertex is rescaled by the Delta-N mass splitting. Leading one-loop order results are computed for the isovector anomalous magnetic moment, the isovector Pauli form factor, and the isovector Nucleon-Delta transition form factors, with parameters fitted to quenched lattice QCD data from Leinweber et al.

## Key numbers
- The physical isovector anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon is kappa_v = 3.7 nucleon magnetons at m_pi = 140 MeV.
- The leading-order modified SSE fit to three quenched lattice QCD data points (at pion masses 600 to 950 MeV) yields a chiral limit value kappa_v^0 ~ 5.7 nucleon magnetons, more than 50% larger than the finite-quark-mass value.
- The leading-order SSE calculation gives an isovector Pauli radius of 0.61 fm^2, arising solely from the pion cloud, which amounts to more than 75% of the physical isovector Pauli radius.
- The isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment at leading order in SSE behaves as kappa_s^LO = kappa_s^(0) - (E_2 M_N)/(4 pi^2 F_pi^2 Delta) m_pi^2 + O(epsilon^4), with the physical value kappa_s = -0.12 nucleon magnetons at m_pi = 140 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier HBChPT calculations by BKKM and SSE calculations by BFHM and GHKP, which did not include the rescaled gamma-N-Delta vertex. The modified SSE provides a qualitatively better description of the isovector Pauli form factor than LO HBChPT and enables chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD data from Leinweber et al. (Adelaide) at high pion masses down to the physical point. The approach also offers new hope for calculating pion-electroproduction cross-sections in the resonance region, a longstanding problem in chiral effective field theory.
