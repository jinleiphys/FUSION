# Construction of gauge invariant effective nucleonic theories: functional approach

> Starting from relativistic quantum field theories, describing interacting nucleons and pions coupled to the dynamical electromagnetic field, the pion degrees of freedom are eliminated by means of functional integration. Apart from taking into account some operators perturbatively in $e$, e.g. the vacuum polarization, this procedure is exact, giving effective theories for nucleons and photons. The subsequent nonrelativistic reduction yields the corresponding nonrelativistic quantum field theory. The latter is unique, irrespective of the precize form of the original nucleon-pion interaction. Nucleonic potentials and electromagnetic interactions are mutually consistent. Local gauge invariance is satisfied at any stage of the formal developments.

## Key claim

Starting from a relativistic quantum field theory of nucleons, pions, and photons, the pion degrees of freedom can be eliminated exactly (apart from perturbative treatment of some operators in the electromagnetic coupling \(e\)) via functional integration, yielding an effective nucleon-photon theory that is unique regardless of the precise form of the original nucleon-pion interaction and that satisfies local gauge invariance at every stage.

## Method

The author begins with a locally gauge-invariant Lagrangian describing nucleons, pions (neutral and charged), and the dynamical electromagnetic field, using either pseudoscalar, pseudovector, or mixed pion-nucleon couplings. The pion fields are integrated out exactly via Gaussian functional integrals, producing nonlocal effective interactions for nucleons and photons; the charged-pion propagator in an external electromagnetic field \(G_A\) is defined via a differential equation and constructed perturbatively in \(e\). For dynamical photons, the determinant arising from the charged-pion integration is evaluated perturbatively to order \(e^2\) using dimensional regularization and on-shell renormalization, yielding the vacuum polarization contribution. A subsequent nonrelativistic reduction produces the corresponding nonrelativistic quantum field theory.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum polarization contribution to the effective photon action is computed to order \(e^2\); the first-order term in \(e\) vanishes identically.
- The renormalized effective photon action is given by \(S_A^{ef} = -\frac{1}{4} \int \frac{d^4 k}{(2 \pi)^4} F_{\mu \nu}(-k) F^{\mu \nu}(k) \left[ 1 + \frac{k^2}{6 m^2} \frac{e^2}{(4 \pi)^2} \int_0^1 dv \, \frac{v^4}{1-\frac{k^2}{4 m^2}(1-v^2)-i\epsilon} \right]\), where \(m\) is the pion mass.
- The vacuum polarization result is finite and gauge invariant; the denominator can vanish for \(k^2 \ge 4 m^2\), corresponding to pion pair production.

## Context

This work extends the functional-integral approach to eliminating meson degrees of freedom, previously done perturbatively via unitary transformations (e.g., Friar (1977) and the \(S\)-matrix approach of Adam), by providing a nonperturbative elimination of pions while maintaining local gauge invariance. It contrasts with approaches that break local gauge invariance despite conserving the global current, such as those discussed by Hyuga (1980) and the Siegert hypothesis work of Naus (1991). The method enables the construction of mutually consistent nucleonic potentials and electromagnetic currents, including exchange currents, and can be applied to other models; the explicit vacuum polarization result reproduces the classic result of Schwinger (1951) for spin-zero charged fields.
