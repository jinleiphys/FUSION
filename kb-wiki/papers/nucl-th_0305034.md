# Structure of positive energy states in a deformed mean-field potential

> We investigate the properties of single-particle resonances in a non-spherical potential by solving the coupled-channels equations for the radial wave functions. We first generalize the box discretization method for positive energy states to a deformed system. As in the spherical case, we find that the discretized energy is stabilized against the box size when a resonance condition is met. Using the wave functions thus obtained, we then discuss the energy and the radial dependences of scattering wave functions in the vicinity of an isolated resonance. In the eigenchannel basis, where the $S$-matrix is diagonal, we propose a generalized expression for the factorization formula for the multi-channel wave function. We find that the factorized wave function agrees well with the exact solution inside the centrifugal barrier when the energy distance from the resonance is less than the resonance width.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the box discretization method for positive-energy single-particle states to deformed (non-spherical) nuclear potentials, finding that discretized energies stabilize against box size when a multi-channel resonance condition is met, and they propose a generalized factorization formula for the multi-channel wave function in the eigenchannel basis that works well inside the centrifugal barrier when the energy distance from the resonance is less than the resonance width.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channels equations for radial wave functions in a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, expanding the total wave function in spin-angular components. They impose a box boundary condition by requiring the determinant of the radial wave function matrix to vanish at the box radius, generalizing the spherical node concept to multi-channel systems. They then construct eigenchannel wave functions by diagonalizing the S-matrix and derive a factorization formula for the energy and radial dependence of scattering wave functions near an isolated resonance, using first-order perturbation theory around the box-discretized states.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy \(E_R = 3.44\) MeV for neutron single-particle levels with \(K^\pi = 5/2^+\) at deformation \(\beta = 0.2\) in a potential simulating \(^{44}\)S
- Total resonance width \(\Gamma = 0.46\) MeV for the same system
- Woods-Saxon parameters: \(V_0 = 42.0\) MeV, \(R = R_{\text{so}} = 1.27 \times 44^{1/3}\) fm, \(a = a_{\text{so}} = 0.67\) fm, \(V_{\text{so}} = 14.9\) MeV/fm\(^2\)
- Eigenphase shifts at resonance: 0.559, \(2.53 \times 10^{-3}\), \(4.12 \times 10^{-2}\), \(1.06 \times 10^{-5}\), and \(2.79 \times 10^{-5}\) (in units of \(\pi\)) for the five eigenchannels
- Box radius \(R_{\text{box}} = 20\) fm used for evaluating integrals in the factorization formula
- Factorization works well for \(r \leq 4.23\) fm (inside the potential barrier) and for energy distances up to approximately \(\Gamma\) from resonance

## Context

This work extends the box discretization method for spherical potentials (DeFacio and collaborators, 1984; Hazi and Taylor, 1970; Maier and collaborators, 1993) to deformed systems, where angular momentum components are coupled. It builds on the eigenchannel approach used in electron-molecule scattering (Hazi, 1979) and the factorization formula of Unger (1967) for spherical systems. The method enables practical construction of scattering wave functions near multi-channel resonances in deformed nuclei, which is relevant for describing continuum effects in weakly bound drip-line nuclei and for applications such as proton emission decay studies (Esbensen and Davids, 2000; Delion and collaborators, 2001).
