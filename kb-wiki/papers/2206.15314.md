# Equation of State of Neutron-Rich Matter in $d$-Dimensions

> Nuclear systems under constraints, with high degrees of symmetries and/or collectivities may be considered as moving effectively in spaces with reduced spatial dimensions. We first derive analytical expressions for the nucleon specific energy $E_0(ρ)$, pressure $P_0(ρ)$, incompressibility coefficient $K_0(ρ)$ and skewness coefficient $J_0(ρ)$ of symmetric nucleonic matter (SNM), the quadratic symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$, its slope parameter $L(ρ)$ and curvature coefficient $K_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ as well as the fourth-order symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym,4}}(ρ)$ of neutron-rich matter in general $d$ spatial dimensions (abbreviated as "$d$D") in terms of the isoscalar and isovector parts of the isospin-dependent single-nucleon potential according to the generalized Hugenholtz-Van Hove (HVH) theorem. The equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter in $d$D can be linked to that in the conventional 3-dimensional (3D) space by the $ε$-expansion which is a perturbative approach successfully used previously in treating second-order phase transitions and related critical phenomena and more recently in studying the EOS of cold atoms. The $ε$-expansion of nuclear EOS in $d$D based on a reference dimension $d_{\rm{f}}=d-ε$ is shown to be effective with $-1\lesssimε\lesssim1$ starting from $1\lesssim d_{\rm{f}}\lesssim3$ in comparison with the exact expressions derived using the HVH theorem. Moreover, the EOS of SNM (with/without considering its potential part) is found to be reduced (enhanced) in lower (higher) dimensions, indicating in particular that the many-nucleon system tends to be deeper bounded but saturate at higher densities in spaces with lower dimensions. The links between the EOSs in 3D and $d$D spaces from the $ε$-expansion provide new perspectives to the EOS of neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytical expressions for the equation of state of neutron-rich matter in general d spatial dimensions using the generalized Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, and show that the epsilon-expansion linking d-dimensional and 3-dimensional equations of state is effective for perturbations with epsilon between -1 and 1 starting from reference dimensions between 1 and 3, but the qualitative behavior of the symmetry energy in lower dimensions depends strongly on the momentum-dependence of the nucleon isovector potential.

## Method

The authors first generalize the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem to d spatial dimensions, deriving analytical expressions for the nucleon specific energy, pressure, incompressibility coefficient, skewness coefficient, quadratic symmetry energy, its slope parameter and curvature coefficient, and fourth-order symmetry energy in terms of the isoscalar and isovector parts of the isospin-dependent single-nucleon potential. They then apply the epsilon-expansion perturbative approach, using a reference dimension d_f = d - epsilon, to link the equation of state in d dimensions to that in conventional 3-dimensional space, and test its effectiveness by comparing with exact expressions from the HVH theorem. A toy model for the single-nucleon potential is constructed to reproduce empirical 3D equation of state knowledge, and the effects of short-range-correlation-induced high-momentum tails are incorporated into the kinetic equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The epsilon-expansion is shown to be effective with -1 less than or approximately equal to epsilon less than or approximately equal to 1, starting from 1 less than or approximately equal to d_f less than or approximately equal to 3
- The crust thickness of neutron stars is approximately t = 1 km, compared with the typical neutron star radius R = 10 km, giving t/(4 pi R^2) approximately 10^{-3}
- The kinetic symmetry energy in d dimensions is E^{kin}_{sym}(rho) = (1/d) E_F, where E_F = k_F^2/(2M) is the nucleon Fermi energy
- The kinetic fourth-order symmetry energy in d dimensions is E^{kin}_{sym,4}(rho) = (d^2 - 3d + 2)/(6d^3) E_F
- The kinetic energy per nucleon of symmetric nucleonic matter in d dimensions is E^{kin}_0(rho) = d/(d+2) E_F

## Context

This work extends the application of the epsilon-expansion method, previously used successfully in treating second-order phase transitions (Wilson 1974, Ma 1976, Wallace 1976) and more recently in studying the equation of state of cold atoms (Nishida 2006), to nuclear matter in reduced spatial dimensions. The formalism enables future studies of nuclear systems that may effectively move in lower dimensions, such as toroidal or bubble-shaped nuclei (Wheeler, Wong 1972, 1973), quasi-1D subsystems in heavy-ion reactions (Li 2008), and quasi-2D treatments of neutron star crusts, potentially providing new perspectives on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter for applications in nuclear physics and astrophysics.
