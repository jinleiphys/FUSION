# Multifragmentation and Symmetry Energy Studied with AMD

> The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations suggest that the isospin composition of fragments produced dynamically in multifragmentation reactions is basically governed by the symmetry energy of low-density uniform nuclear matter rather than the symmetry energy for the ground-state finite nuclei. After the statistical secondary decay of the excited fragments, the symmetry energy effect still remains in the fragment isospin composition, though the effect in the isoscaling parameter seems a very delicate problem.

## Key claim
AMD simulations suggest that the isospin composition of fragments produced dynamically in multifragmentation reactions is governed by the symmetry energy of low-density uniform nuclear matter at about half normal density, rather than by the symmetry energy of ground-state finite nuclei; however, the symmetry energy effect in the isoscaling parameter for final fragments after secondary decay is a very delicate problem due to cancellation effects.

## Method
The author performed AMD simulations of central collisions of calcium isotopes (⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, and ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca) at 35 MeV/nucleon, using the Gogny force and the Gogny-AS force which differ in the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Primary fragments were identified at t=300 fm/c by a spatial distance condition of 5 fm, and their yields were analyzed to extract the function K(N,Z) and the parameters ζ(Z), Δ(Z/A)², and the isoscaling parameter α. Statistical secondary decay of the excited primary fragments was then calculated using a sequential binary decay model based on Pühlhofer's approach.

## Key numbers
- The density at which the symmetry energy governs fragment isospin composition is ρ ∼ ½ ρ₀ (half normal nuclear matter density).
- The temperature extracted from the absolute values of ζ and the symmetry energy at half density is T ∼ 3.4 MeV.
- Primary fragments have typical excitation energy of about 3 MeV/nucleon.
- Fragments are recognized at t = 300 fm/c with a spatial distance condition of less than 5 fm.
- The level density parameter used in the statistical decay code is a = A/(8 MeV).
- Excited states of composite particles up to excitation energy E* ≤ 40 MeV are included in the decay code.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous AMD analyses (Ono, Refs. ONOk, ONOl) of fragment yields in multifragmentation reactions of Ca isotopes at 35 MeV/nucleon. It contradicts the expectation that the symmetry energy of finite nuclei (with volume and surface contributions) would govern fragment isospin composition, instead finding that the volume symmetry energy of low-density uniform matter is directly responsible. The results enable experimental extraction of the density-dependent symmetry energy from fragment isospin observables, though the isoscaling parameter α for final fragments requires careful treatment due to cancellation between effects in ζ(Z) and Δ(Z/A)².
