# From $J/ψ$ to LHCb pentaquarks

> The two exotic $P_c^+(4380)$ and $P_c^+(4450)$ discovered in $2015$ by the LHCb Collaboration, together with the four resonances $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$, reported in $2016$ by the same collaboration, are described in a constituent quark model which has been able to explain the properties of charmonium states from the $J/ψ$ to the $X(3872)$. Using this model we found a $\bar DΣ_c^*$ bound state with $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^-$ that may be identified with the $P_c^+(4380)$. In the $\bar D^*Σ_c$ channel we found three possible candidates for the $P_c^+(4450)$ with $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$, $\frac{3}{2}^-$ and $\frac{3}{2}^+$ with almost degenerated energies. The $X(4140)$ resonance appears as a cusp in the $J/ψϕ$ channel due to the near coincidence of the $D_{s}^{\pm}D_{s}^{\ast\pm}$ and $J/ψϕ$ mass thresholds. The remaining three $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ resonances appear as conventional charmonium states with quantum numbers $3^{3}P_{1}$, $4^{3}P_{0}$ and $5^{3}P_{0}$, respectively; and whose masses and widths are slightly modified due to their coupling with the corresponding closest meson-meson thresholds.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the LHCb pentaquark candidates \(P_c^+(4380)\) and \(P_c^+(4450)\) can be described as meson-baryon molecular states within a constituent quark model, but the identification is conditional: the \(P_c^+(4380)\) corresponds to a single \(\bar D\Sigma_c^*\) bound state with \(J^P=3/2^-\), while the \(P_c^+(4450)\) has three nearly degenerate candidates with different quantum numbers (\(J^P=1/2^-\), \(3/2^-\), \(3/2^+\)), leaving its exact assignment ambiguous.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method for quark-antiquark (\(c\bar c\)) states and a coupled-channel formalism that mixes these bare charmonium states with meson-meson or meson-baryon configurations. The transition between the two- and four-quark sectors is mediated by a \(^3P_0\) quark-pair creation operator, and the meson-baryon interaction is computed using a constituent quark model with chiral-symmetry-breaking, one-gluon exchange, and screened confinement potentials, with no new parameters introduced beyond those previously fitted to the charmonium spectrum from \(J/\psi\) to \(X(3872)\).

## Key numbers

*   \(P_c^+(4380)\) candidate: a \(\bar D\Sigma_c^*\) bound state with \(J^P=3/2^-\) at mass 4385.0 MeV/\(c^2\), with predicted widths of 10.0 MeV (\(J/\psi p\)) and 14.7 MeV (\(\bar D^*\Lambda_c\)).
*   \(P_c^+(4450)\) candidates: three \(\bar D^*\Sigma_c\) states with masses 4458.9 MeV/\(c^2\) (\(J^P=1/2^-\)), 4461.3 MeV/\(c^2\) (\(J^P=3/2^-\)), and 4462.7 MeV/\(c^2\) (\(J^P=3/2^+\)); their \(J/\psi p\) widths are 5.3, 0.8, and 0.2 MeV respectively.
*   \(X(4274)\): identified as a \(3^3P_1\) charmonium state; coupled-channel calculation gives mass 4242.4 MeV and total width 25.9 MeV, with 48.7% \(c\bar c\) and 43.5% \(D_s D_s^*\) components.
*   \(X(4500)\) and \(X(4700)\): assigned as conventional charmonium states \(4^3P_0\) (theory mass 4497.2 MeV vs. experiment 4506\(\pm\)11\({}^{+12}_{-15}\) MeV) and \(5^3P_0\) (theory mass 4697.6 MeV vs. experiment 4704\(\pm\)10\({}^{+14}_{-24}\) MeV).
*   \(X(4140)\): no pole found; the structure near 4140 MeV is interpreted as a cusp from the \(D_s D_s^*\) threshold coinciding with the \(J/\psi\phi\) threshold.

## Context

This work extends the same constituent quark model previously applied to charmonium states from \(J/\psi\) to \(X(3872)\) (Ortega 2010) and to the \(D_{s0}(2317)\) (Ortega 2016) to the newly discovered LHCb pentaquarks and \(X\) resonances. It contradicts a pure compact-pentaquark interpretation for the \(P_c\) states by favoring a molecular \(\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}\) structure, and it contradicts earlier CDF claims of a resonance near 4140 MeV by explaining the \(X(4140)\) as a threshold cusp rather than a genuine state. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted \(\bar D^*\Lambda_c\) decay channel of the \(P_c(4450)\) candidates, which the authors calculate to have widths up to 63.6 MeV, much larger than the \(J/\psi p\) widths.
