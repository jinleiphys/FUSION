# In-medium NN cross sections determined from stopping and collective flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> In-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections are explored by comparing results of quantum molecular dynamics simulations to data on stopping and on elliptic and directed flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. The comparison points to in-medium cross sections which are suppressed at low energies but not at higher energies. Positive correlations are found between the degree of stopping and the magnitudes of elliptic and directed flows.

# In-medium NN cross sections determined from stopping and collective flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
In-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections are suppressed at low energies but not at higher energies, with the extracted cross sections depending on both density and energy; however, the results do not represent the last word on in-medium cross sections as the medium-variation in the parameterization has been clearly oversimplified, and the examined observables do not strongly differentiate between like and unlike nucleon cross sections.

## Method
The authors employed the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD05) model with SkP and SLy7 Skyrme mean fields to simulate collisions of symmetric or near-symmetric systems (Au+Au, Ni+Ni, Ru+Ru, Xe+Cs) at beam energies up to 400 AMeV. Three phenomenological forms of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections were tested: a standard density-dependent suppression with η=0.2 (σ*(1)), cross sections from the closed time path Green's function approach using QHD-II Lagrangian (σ*(2)), and an ad hoc parameterization guided by CTPGF results (σ*(3)) where the suppression parameter η varies with beam energy. The calculated stopping ratio vartl and flow observables (elliptic flow v2 and directed flow p_xdir^(0)) were compared to experimental data from the FOPI and INDRA collaborations.

## Key numbers
- The stopping observable vartl changed by 0.22 (32%) when switching from σ*(1) to σ*(3) cross sections in 400 AMeV Au+Au collisions at b/b_max<0.15
- Switching from SkP to SLy7 mean field changed vartl by only 0.015 (relative 2%)
- The σ*(3) parameterization uses η=0.2 for E_beam < 150 AMeV, η=0 for 150 AMeV < E_beam < 200 AMeV, η=-0.2 for 200 AMeV < E_beam < 400 AMeV, and η=-0.4 for E_beam > 400 AMeV
- The CTPGF cross sections σ_np*(2) change little with density and are nearly the same as in free space, while σ_nn,pp*(2) are suppressed at lower energies (√s ≲ 2.05 GeV) and enhanced at higher energies
- The vartl value for σ*(2) at 400 AMeV was 0.805
- The incompressibility for both SkP and SLy7 interactions is K ~ 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends prior studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections from relativistic Brueckner approaches (LiGQ 1993, Fuchs 2001) and closed time path Green's function approaches (Mao 1994, QFLi 2000, QFLi 2004), which found suppression at low relative momenta and less suppression at higher momenta. The results contradict the common phenomenological parameterization with η=0.2 (Klakow 1993, Persson 2002, Danielewicz 2000, Fuchs 2001) at higher energies, showing that cross sections need to be enhanced rather than suppressed. The findings enable improved constraints on the nuclear equation of state by reducing uncertainties from cross sections in transport models, and demonstrate that stopping observables (vartl) are strongly sensitive to cross sections while flow observables depend comparably on both cross sections and mean fields.
