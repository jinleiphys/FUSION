# Constraints, limits and extensions for nuclear energy functionals

> In the present contribution, we discuss the behavior of Skyrme forces when they are employed to study both neutron stars and giant resonance states in 208Pb within the fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA). We point out that clear correlations exist between the results for the isoscalar monopole and isovector dipole resonances (ISGMR and IVGDR), and definite quantities which characterize the equation of state (EOS) of uniform matter. We propose that the RPA results or, to some extent, the mentioned EOS parameters, are used as constraints when a force is fitted. This suggestion can be valid also when the fit of a more general energy density functional is envisaged. We use our considerations to select a limited number of Skyrme forces (10) out of a large sample of 78 interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the properties of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in 208Pb can be used as constraints to select physically reasonable Skyrme forces, but this selection is not straightforward to extend to open-shell nuclei because pairing correlations produce a systematic downward shift of the monopole energy that is not constant across isotope chains.

## Method

The author performed fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the ISGMR and IVGDR in 208Pb using 78 Skyrme parameter sets, first screening them against neutron star properties following the criteria of Stone (2007). The surviving 18 forces were then tested against the experimental IVGDR energy (13.46 MeV) within ±1 MeV, reducing the set to 12 forces, and finally against the experimental ISGMR energy (14.17 MeV) within ±1 MeV, yielding 10 selected forces. For open-shell Sn isotopes, fully self-consistent quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) was employed with three types of pairing forces (volume, surface, and mixed) to study the effect of pairing on the monopole strength distribution.

## Key numbers

- Experimental IVGDR energy in 208Pb: E_{-1} = 13.46 MeV (Dietrich, year not given)
- Experimental ISGMR energy in 208Pb: E_{-1} = 14.17 MeV (Youngblood, year not given)
- Symmetry energy at ρ=0.1 fm^{-3} constrained to S(0.1) = 24.1 ± 2.4 MeV from the IVGDR analysis
- Nuclear matter incompressibility constrained to K_∞ = 240 ± 20 MeV from the ISGMR analysis
- K_τ extracted from Sn isotope data: 550 ± 100 MeV
- 10 Skyrme forces selected: Gs, Rs, SLy230a, SLy4, SLy5, SkMP, SkO', SK255, SK272, LNS
- SkM* force has K_∞ = 217 MeV; discrepancy between incompressibility extracted from Sn isotopes and from 208Pb is about 10%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Stone (2007) on neutron star properties of Skyrme forces by adding giant resonance constraints, and builds on the correlation between IVGDR energy and the symmetry energy parameter f(0.1) established by Trippa (year not given). The paper enables a systematic selection of Skyrme parameter sets for use in nuclear structure calculations and highlights the need for improved functionals that can simultaneously describe closed-shell and open-shell nuclei, particularly addressing the "tin softness" puzzle raised by Piekarewicz (year not given). The results also provide guidance for the construction of universal energy density functionals such as the UNEDF project.
