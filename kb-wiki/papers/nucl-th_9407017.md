# Antiproton production in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.85 GeV/nucleon

> Antiproton production in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.85 GeV/nucleon is studied in the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model. The self-energies of the antiproton are determined from the nucleon self-energies by the G-parity transformation. Also, the final-state interactions of the antiproton including both rescattering and annihilation are explicitly treated. With a soft nuclear equation of state, the calculated antiproton momentum spectrum is in good agreement with recent experimental data from the heavy-ion synchrotron at GSI. The effect due to the reduced nucleon and antinucleon masses in a medium is found to be more appreciable than in earlier Bevalac experiments with lighter systems and at higher energies.

## Key claim

The calculated antiproton momentum spectrum from Ni+Ni collisions at 1.85 GeV/nucleon, using a soft nuclear equation of state and including medium effects via reduced nucleon and antinucleon masses, agrees with experimental data from the SIS at GSI; however, the authors caution that given uncertainties in antiproton production and annihilation cross sections in a medium, it is premature to conclude that the soft equation of state is favored by the data.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) model extended to include antiproton degrees of freedom. Antiproton self-energies were obtained from nucleon self-energies via G-parity transformation, and antiprotons were produced from baryon-baryon collisions (B1B2 → NNp\(\bar p\)), with final-state interactions including propagation in the mean-field potential, elastic rescattering, and annihilation explicitly treated.

## Key numbers

- Primordial antiproton abundance in a head-on (b=0 fm) Ni+Ni collision at 1.85 GeV/nucleon is about 1.7×10⁻⁵, reduced to about 2.2×10⁻⁷ after annihilation, meaning only about 1.3% of primordial antiprotons escape.
- With a soft equation of state (incompressibility 200 MeV), the calculated antiproton production cross section using a stiff equation of state (incompressibility 380 MeV) is about a factor of 2-3 smaller than with the soft equation of state.
- The antiproton production cross section is enhanced by about two orders of magnitude due to the reduction of baryon masses in a medium, compared to the case with bare nucleon and antiproton masses.
- When only the nucleon effective mass is used (threshold 3m*+m), the cross section is enhanced by about a factor of 12 over the bare-mass case; when both nucleon and antiproton effective masses are used (threshold 4m*), the cross section is further enhanced by about a factor of 8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RVUU study of antiproton production in Si+Si collisions at 2.1 GeV/nucleon (Li and Ko, 1994) to a heavier system (Ni+Ni) at a lower incident energy (1.85 GeV/nucleon), where medium effects are more appreciable. The results are compared to experimental data from the SIS at GSI and to a similar study by Teis et al. (1994), who required only a moderate attractive antiproton potential due to a stronger attractive scalar potential at high densities in their model. The work enables further theoretical study of whether the antiproton has a strong attractive potential in dense medium, which remains an open question.
