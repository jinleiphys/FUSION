# Study of in-medium $Î·^\prime$ properties in the ($Îł$, ${Î·^\prime}p$) reaction on nuclei

> We study the near-threshold photoproduction of $Î·^\prime$ mesons from nuclei in coincidence with forward going protons in the kinematical conditions of the Crystal Barrel/TAPS experiment, recently performed at ELSA. The calculations have been performed within a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function. The model accounts for both the primary $Îłp \to {Î·^\prime}p$ process and the two-step intermediate nucleon rescattering processes as well as the effect of the nuclear $Î·^\prime$ mean-field potential. We calculate the exclusive $Î·^\prime$ kinetic energy distributions for the $^{12}$C($Îł$, ${Î·^\prime}p$) reaction for different scenarios of $Î·^\prime$ in-medium modification. We find that the considered two-step rescattering mechanism plays an insignificant role in ${Î·^\prime}p$ photoproduction off the carbon target. We also demonstrate that the calculated $Î·^\prime$ kinetic energy distributions in primary photon--proton ${Î·^\prime}p$ production reveal strong sensitivity to the depth of the real ${Î·^\prime}$ potential at normal nuclear matter density (or to the ${Î·^\prime}$ in-medium mass shift) in the studied incident photon energy regime. Therefore, such observables may be useful to help determine the above ${Î·^\prime}$ in-medium renormalization from the comparison of the results of our calculations with the data from the CBELSA/TAPS experiment. In addition, we show that these distributions are also strongly influenced by the momentum-dependent optical potential, which the outgoing participant proton feels inside the carbon nucleus. This potential should be taken into account in the analysis of these data with the aim to obtain information on the ${Î·^\prime}$ modification in cold nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The exclusive $^{12}$C($\gamma$, $\eta^\prime p$) reaction cross section as a function of $\eta^\prime$ kinetic energy is strongly sensitive to the depth of the real $\eta^\prime$ nuclear potential at saturation density (i.e., the in-medium mass shift), but only when the momentum-dependent optical potential felt by the outgoing proton is also properly accounted for; the two-step nucleon rescattering mechanism contributes negligibly.

## Method

The author extended a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, previously used for inclusive $\eta^\prime$ photoproduction, to treat exclusive $(\gamma, \eta^\prime p)$ coincidence measurements. The model incorporates the primary $\gamma p \to \eta^\prime p$ process with in-medium modifications of both the $\eta^\prime$ mass (via a scalar potential depth $V_0$) and the outgoing proton mass (via a momentum-dependent Schroedinger equivalent potential), plus a separate calculation of two-step $\gamma N \to \eta^\prime N$ followed by $pn \to pn$ rescattering. The differential cross section was averaged over the incident photon energy ranges and proton angular acceptance ($1^\circ$â€“$11^\circ$) of the CBELSA/TAPS experiment.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta^\prime$ scalar potential depth $V_0$ at saturation density $\rho_0$ was varied over seven options: 0, -25, -50, -75, -100, -125, and -150 MeV.
- The average nucleon density in carbon was taken as $\langle \rho_N \rangle = \rho_0/2$.
- The $\eta^\prime N$ absorption cross section used was $\sigma_{\eta^\prime N} = 11$ mb; the total $pN$ cross section was $\sigma_{pN}^{\rm tot} = 40$ mb.
- The free $\gamma p \to \eta^\prime p$ threshold is approximately 1.446 GeV; incident photon energy ranges studied were 1.5â€“2.2 GeV, 1.5â€“2.6 GeV, and 1.3â€“2.6 GeV.
- The proton detection threshold was $E_{\rm kin}^{N,G} = 50$ MeV, within polar angles $1^\circ$â€“$11^\circ$.
- The average Fermi momentum of carbon was $\bar p_F = 210$ MeV/c, corresponding to an average Fermi energy of $\bar E_F \simeq 24$ MeV.
- The nuclear potential depth for slow nucleons in the two-step mechanism was $U_N = -31$ MeV.
- The elastic $pn \to pn$ total cross section was fitted as $\sigma_{pn \to pn}(|{\bf p}_N^{'}|) = 27.15 + (1.8/E_{\rm kin}^{'}) + (0.24/E_{\rm kin}^{'2})$ mb for $0.04 \le E_{\rm kin}^{'} \le 1.0$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the collision model of Paryev (2014) from inclusive $\eta^\prime$ photoproduction to exclusive $(\gamma, \eta^\prime p)$ coincidence measurements, motivated by the CBELSA/TAPS experiment at ELSA. The CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration (2014) previously extracted an $\eta^\prime$ optical potential of $V_{\rm real}(\rho_0) = -(37 \pm 10 \pm 10)$ MeV from inclusive data, consistent with the Quark Meson Coupling model prediction of -37 MeV but disfavoring larger mass shifts of 80â€“200 MeV from other theoretical approaches. The calculated exclusive distributions enable a more direct determination of the $\eta^\prime$ in-medium mass shift at lower $\eta^\prime$ momenta, and the results can be compared with forthcoming CBELSA/TAPS data to resolve the size of the $\eta^\prime$ modification in cold nuclear matter.
