# Quasifission and difference in formation of evaporation residues in the $^{16}$O+$^{184}$W and $^{19}$F+$^{181}$Ta reactions

> The excitation functions of capture, complete fusion, and evaporation residue formation in the $^{16}$O+$^{184}$W and $^{19}$F+$^{181}$Ta reactions leading to the same $^{200}$Pb compound nucleus has been studied theoretically to explain the experimental data showing more intense yield of evaporation residue in the former reaction in comparison with that in the latter reaction. The observed difference is explained by large capture cross section in the former and by increase of the quasifission contribution to the yield of fission-like fragments in the $^{19}$F+$^{181}$Ta reaction at large excitation energies. The probability of compound nucleus formation in the $^{16}$O+$^{184}$W reaction is larger but compound nuclei formed in both reactions have similar angular momentum ranges at the same excitation energy. The observed decrease of evaporation residue cross section normalized to the fusion cross section in the $^{19}$F+$^{181}$Ta reaction in comparison with the one in the $^{16}$O+$^{184}$W reaction at high excitation energies is explained by the increase of hindrance in the formation of compound nucleus connected with more quick increase of the quasifission contribution in the $^{19}$F induced reaction. The spin distributions of the evaporation residue cross sections for the two reactions are also presented.

## Key claim

The observed difference in evaporation residue yields between the two reactions is not due to different decay dynamics of the compound nuclei formed, but rather to different quasifission contributions in the entrance channel, which contaminate the measured fission fragments and cause an apparent suppression of fusion in the \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta reaction at excitation energies above 67 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system model to calculate capture, complete fusion, quasifission, fast fission, and fusion-fission cross sections for both reactions. An advanced statistical model was then employed to compute evaporation residue and fusion-fission excitation functions. The theoretical results were compared with experimental data from Shidling et al. (2008) and Forster et al. (1985) to analyze the ratio of evaporation residue cross section to fusion cross section as a function of excitation energy and angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio \(R^{(\rm exp)} = \sigma_{ER}/\sigma_{fus}\) for both reactions is approximately equal up to an excitation energy \(E^*_{\rm CN}\) of about 67 MeV; above this energy, the ratio for the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{184}\)W reaction becomes larger than that for the \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta reaction.
- The maximum contribution of fast fission fragments is about 15% of the observed yield of fission-like fragments only at large excitation energies \(E^*_{\rm CN} > 85\) MeV.
- The angular momentum value separating complete fusion and fast fission regions is \(L_f = 80\hbar\).
- The yield of fusion-fission and quasifission fragments become comparable at a beam energy corresponding to \(E^*_{\rm CN}\) of about 62 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Shidling et al. (2008), who observed a deficiency in evaporation residue cross sections for the \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta system compared to the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{184}\)W system leading to the same \(^{200}\)Pb compound nucleus. The present theoretical analysis contradicts the earlier interpretation that the reduction was due to suppression of fusion at higher orbital angular momenta, instead attributing it to quasifission contamination in the measured fission cross sections. The results enable more accurate determination of true fusion cross sections in heavy-ion reactions by accounting for quasifission and fast fission contributions.
