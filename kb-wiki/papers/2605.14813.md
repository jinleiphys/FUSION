# Skyrme SV density-functional analysis of the $2νββ$ decay in $^{76}$Ge

> We present a theoretical study of the two-neutrino $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ double beta decay of $^{76}$Ge within the No-Core Configuration-Interaction framework based on the Skyrme SV density functional. We analyze three allowed decay scenarios distinguished by the $[n,m] \equiv [(νg_{9/2})^n, (πg_{9/2})^m]$ occupancy of the $0g_{9/2}$ intruder orbital, which remains conserved to high precision, as well as by the triaxiality of the daughter nucleus. The resulting $2νββ$ nuclear matrix element is found to depend strongly on the scenario. For the energetically favored $[4,2]$ occupancy, we obtain $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}| = 0.069(7)$~MeV$^{-1}$. For the $[6,0]$ occupancy, the matrix element further depends on the triaxiality parameter $γ$ of the two coexisting, closely lying minima in $^{76}$Se, yielding $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}| = 0.040(4)$~MeV$^{-1}$ at $γ= 17.7^\circ$ and $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}| = 0.22(2)$~MeV$^{-1}$ at $γ= 41.9^\circ$. The latter result is consistent with the empirical value reported by A. S. Barabash, $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}| = 0.204(14)$~MeV$^{-1}$, while the two former results are comparable to existing calculations based on energy-density-functional frameworks. Our calculations reveal challenges in the precise determination of the $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}|$ for the $^{76}$Ge decay. The structural complexity, triaxiality, and shape coexistence identified in the analyzed nuclei imply a strong sensitivity to fine details of the interaction and configuration mixing. This, in turn, explains the difficulties in theoretical modeling of the $|\mathcal{M}^{2ν}|$ matrix elements for the $^{76}$Ge decay, which vary by almost an order of magnitude in the available literature.

## Key claim

The two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge depends strongly on the specific decay scenario (defined by the conserved occupancy of the 0g₉/₂ intruder orbital and the triaxiality of the daughter ⁷⁶Se), and the authors treat multiple scenarios as equally plausible because the Skyrme SV functional cannot uniquely determine the ground-state configurations.

## Method

The authors employed the No-Core Configuration-Interaction framework based on the Skyrme SV density functional (DFT-NCCI). They constructed configuration spaces of projected Slater determinants for the parent ⁷⁶Ge, intermediate ⁷⁶As, and daughter ⁷⁶Se nuclei, then solved the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain eigenstates. The 2νββ nuclear matrix element was computed using the Fermi golden rule of second order, summing over virtual 1⁺ states in ⁷⁶As, with the Gamow-Teller operator evaluated between angular-momentum-projected states using an additional isospin rotation.

## Key numbers

* For the C42 decay path ([6,0] → [5,1] → [4,2] occupancy), the fully correlated calculation yields |ℳ²ν| = 0.069(7) MeV⁻¹.
* For the C60 decay path ([6,0] → [6,0] → [6,0] occupancy) with the less-triaxial daughter minimum at γ = 17.7°, |ℳ²ν| = 0.040(4) MeV⁻¹.
* For the C60 decay path with the more-triaxial daughter minimum at γ = 41.9°, |ℳ²ν| = 0.22(2) MeV⁻¹.
* The empirical value reported by Barabash is |ℳ²ν| = 0.204(14) MeV⁻¹.
* The two coexisting minima in ⁷⁶Se for the [6,0] configuration differ in energy by approximately 200 keV, with deformation parameters β = 0.30, γ = 41.9° and β = 0.29, γ = 17.7°.
* The experimental Q-value Q_ββ = 2039.1 keV and mass difference ΔM = 410.9 keV were used in the calculation.

## Context

This work extends the DFT-NCCI framework previously applied to the 2νββ decay of ⁴⁸Ca by Miskiewicz (2025), where the simpler shell structure yielded consistent results across models. The present study reveals that the A=76 region exhibits pronounced triaxiality, shape coexistence, and strong sensitivity to fine details of the interaction, explaining why literature values for the ⁷⁶Ge matrix element vary by almost an order of magnitude. The results enable future work on the C40 decay path and studies of the effective axial-vector coupling constant (quenching) in this mass region, and may inform calculations of the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element for the LEGEND experiment.
