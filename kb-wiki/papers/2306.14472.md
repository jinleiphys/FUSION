# Pion condensation in dense QCD, the dilute Bose gas, and speedy Goldstone bosons

> We consider pion condensation in QCD at finite isospin density $μ_I$ and zero temperature using two-flavor chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT). The pressure is calculated to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the low-energy expansion. In the nonrelativistic limit, we recover the classic result by Lee, Huang, and Yang for the energy density of a dilute Bose gas with an $s$-wave scattering length that includes loop corrections from $χ$PT. In the chiral limit, higher-order calculations are tractable. We calculate the pressure to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the low-energy expansion, which is an expansion in powers of $μ_I^2/(4π)^2f^2$, where $f$ is the (bare) pion decay constant. The spontaneous breakdown of the global internal symmetry $U(1)_{I_3}$ gives rise to a massless Goldstone boson or phonon. We discuss the properties of the low-energy effective theory describing this mode. Finally, we compare our results for the pressure and the speed of sound with recent lattice simulations with 2+1 flavors. The agreement is very good for isospin chemical potentials up to 180-200 MeV, depending on the physical quantity.

## Key claim

The paper shows that pion condensation in QCD at finite isospin density reproduces the classic dilute Bose gas results of Lee, Huang, and Yang in the nonrelativistic limit, and that chiral perturbation theory agrees very well with lattice simulations for isospin chemical potentials up to 180-200 MeV, depending on the physical quantity.

## Method

The authors used two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to calculate the pressure of pion-condensed QCD at zero temperature and finite isospin chemical potential to next-to-leading order (NLO) and, in the chiral limit, to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the low-energy expansion. They expanded the chiral Lagrangian around the pion-condensed ground state, computed the one-loop functional determinant and the static part of the NLO Lagrangian, renormalized the low-energy constants in the MSbar scheme, and compared the resulting pressure and speed of sound with lattice QCD simulations from Brandt et al. (2022).

## Key numbers

- The pressure from chiral perturbation theory agrees with lattice simulations for isospin chemical potentials up to 180-200 MeV, depending on the physical quantity.
- The tree-level pion mass is m_pi,0 = 136.50 ± 1.2 MeV and the bare pion decay constant is f = 88.35 ± 1.9 MeV, obtained using the low-energy constants l1 = -0.4 ± 0.6, l2 = 4.3 ± 0.1, l3 = 2.9 ± 2.4, and l4 = 4.4 ± 0.2 from Colangelo (2001).
- The physical pion mass used for comparison is m_pi = 135 MeV and the pion decay constant is f_pi = 133/sqrt(2) MeV.
- The nonrelativistic energy density reproduces the Lee-Huang-Yang result: E(n) = (2 pi n^2 a / m) [1 + (128/(15 sqrt(pi))) sqrt(n a^3)].
- The nonrelativistic chemical potential as a function of density is mu_NR = (4 pi n_I a / m_pi) [1 + (32/(3 sqrt(pi))) sqrt(n_I a^3)].

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion condensation at finite isospin density by Andersen et al. (2018) to include the pressure and speed of sound, and connects these relativistic results to the classic nonrelativistic dilute Bose gas results of Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957). The comparison with lattice simulations by Brandt et al. (2022) with 2+1 flavors validates the effective theory approach and enables its use in modeling pion stars as proposed by Carignano et al. (2017). The paper also uses Son's construction of the effective field theory for phonons to describe the Goldstone mode from U(1)_I3 breaking, with the phonon damping rate scaling as p^5 as obtained by Beliaev (1958) for weakly interacting Bose gases.
