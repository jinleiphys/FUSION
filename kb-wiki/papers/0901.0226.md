# Bulk viscosity-driven freeze-out in heavy ion collisions

> We give an review the HBT puzzle, and argue that its resolution requires the introduction of new physics close to the phase transition scale. We argue that a candidate for this new physics is bulk viscosity, recently postulated to peak, and even diverge, close to the phase transition temperature. We show that such a viscosity peak can force the system created in heavy ion collisions to become unstable, and filament into fragments whose size is weakly dependent on the global size of the system, thereby triggering freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a sharp peak in bulk viscosity near the QCD phase transition temperature can trigger hydrodynamic instabilities that fragment the expanding fireball into pieces, thereby providing a dynamical freeze-out mechanism that could resolve the HBT puzzle. The authors hedge that this scenario requires the bulk viscosity peak to be large enough (parameter `z0` of order 1) for the instability to grow to significance, and that second-order hydrodynamics might stop the instabilities once they become comparable to the background.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of boost-invariant solutions to the Navier-Stokes equations (both 1D and 3D cases), using a parameterized bulk viscosity that peaks near the critical temperature Tc. They evolve the system from an initial temperature of 0.3 GeV and comoving time 0.6 fm, computing the eigenvalues of the perturbation matrix to determine whether unstable modes grow or are suppressed by the time-dependent rotation of eigenvectors.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: 0.3 GeV, initial comoving time: 0.6 fm
- Bulk viscosity peak width parameter: sigma = 0.01 Tc
- Perturbative bulk viscosity baseline: zpQCD ~ 10^-3
- Bulk viscosity peak height parameters tested: z0 = 0, 0.1, 1 (in units of Tc)
- Instability growth follows power law: x(tau) ~ (tau/tau_ini)^lambda, with lambda positive for unstable modes
- Perturbation growth factor Z = X(tau)/X(tau_ini) reaches Z >= 1 for large z0 a few fm after T ~ Tc

## Context

This work addresses the HBT puzzle in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, where hydrodynamic simulations fail to describe HBT radii despite successfully describing flow observables. The authors extend prior work on bulk viscosity near Tc from lattice calculations and sum rules (bulkvisc, 2007) and perturbative QCD (amybulk, 2006), and build on stability analysis methods from Bjorken (1983) and subsequent work (stability, 2005). The proposed fragmentation mechanism is phenomenologically similar to supercooling-induced fragmentation in first-order phase transitions (firstorder, 2000), but originates from hydrodynamic rather than thermodynamic instabilities. The scenario enables future ab initio HBT calculations that include fragment size as an additional parameter beyond critical temperature.
