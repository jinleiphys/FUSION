# Microscopic Calculation of Heavy-Ion Potentials Based on TDHF

> We discuss the implementation and results of a recently developed microscopic method for calculating ion-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross-sections. The method uses the TDHF evolution to obtain the instantaneous many-body collective state using a density constraint. The ion-ion potential as well as the coordinate dependent mass are calculated from these states. The method fully accounts for the dynamical processes present in the TDHF time-evolution and provides a parameter-free way of calculating fusion cross-sections.

## Key claim

The DC-TDHF method provides a parameter-free way to calculate ion-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross-sections that fully accounts for dynamical processes such as neck formation, mass exchange, and internal excitations, but the method's applicability to superheavy systems is limited to capture cross-sections because TDHF lacks quantum decay processes needed to describe complete fusion or break-up at long times.

## Method

The method uses the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) evolution to obtain the instantaneous many-body collective state, then applies a density constraint to perform a static Hartree-Fock minimization while holding neutron and proton densities fixed to the instantaneous TDHF densities. The ion-ion potential is extracted as the density-constrained energy minus the binding energies of the two separate nuclei, and a coordinate-dependent mass parameter is computed from energy conservation using the collective velocity from the TDHF evolution. Fusion cross-sections are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the coordinate-dependent mass and potential, using the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC) method as implemented in the coupled-channel code CCFULL.

## Key numbers

- For the 16O+208Pb system, numerical accuracy of static binding energies and deviation from point Coulomb energy in the initial state is on the order of 50-150 keV.
- Density constraint calculations were performed at every 10-20 fm/c interval during TDHF evolution.
- For the 48Ca+238U superheavy system, the alignment probability dP/dOmega is in the range 0.46-0.52 and does not vary appreciably with energy.
- For the 64Ni+132Sn system, experimental energies that appear sub-barrier relative to the spherical barrier are above the barrier for the beta=90 degree orientation potential.

## Context

This work extends the density-constraint concept first introduced by Cusson and Reinhard (1985) for calculating collective states from TDHF time-evolution, and used for nuclear molecular resonances by Umar and Strayer (1985). The DC-TDHF method improves upon phenomenological approaches such as the Bass model (1980), proximity potential (Blocki and Swiatecki, 1977), and double-folding method (Satchler and Love, 1979; Rowley, 1983), as well as semi-microscopic methods like constrained Hartree-Fock (Zint and Mosel, 1976; Bonche and Flocard, 2004), by avoiding the frozen-density or sudden approximation and instead allowing full dynamical rearrangements. The method enables parameter-free calculation of fusion cross-sections for spherical and deformed systems, and for superheavy systems it provides capture cross-sections that can be compared with experimental data such as those from Oganessian (2007) for 48Ca+238U.
