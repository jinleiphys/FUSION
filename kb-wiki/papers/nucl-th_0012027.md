# Effect of nuclear structure on Type Ia supernova nucleosynthesis

> The relationship among nuclear structure, the weak processes in nuclei, and astrophysics becomes quite apparent in supernova explosion and nucleosynthesis studies. In this brief article, I report on progress made in the last few years on calculating electron capture and beta-decay rates in iron-group nuclei. I also report on applications of these rates to Type-Ia nucleosynthesis studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that using realistic shell-model calculations for Gamow-Teller strength distributions (rather than the older Fuller-Fowler-Newman parameterization) systematically reduces electron-capture rates on iron-group nuclei, and that incorporating these corrected rates into Type Ia supernova models resolves previously problematic overproduction of certain isotopes like \(^{54}\)Cr and \(^{54}\)Fe.

## Method

The author used shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) and full \(pf\)-shell diagonalization with the KB3 effective interaction to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for iron-group nuclei. These strengths were then folded with stellar electron energy distributions to obtain electron-capture and \(\beta\)-decay rates, which were implemented into Type Ia supernova evolution codes by scaling the older FFN rates by empirically determined factors (0.2 for even-even, 0.1 for odd-A, 0.04 for odd-odd nuclei for electron capture; 0.05, 0.025, and 1.7 for \(\beta\)-decay respectively). The resulting nucleosynthetic yields were compared to solar abundances.

## Key numbers

- Electron-capture rates on even-even nuclei were multiplied by 0.2 relative to FFN rates; on odd-A nuclei by 0.1; on odd-odd nuclei by 0.04.
- \(\beta\)-decay rates were scaled by 0.05 (even-even), 0.025 (odd-A), and 1.7 (odd-odd) relative to FFN rates.
- The overproduction of \(^{54}\)Cr and \(^{54}\)Fe seen in FFN-based models was cured when shell-model rates were used in the WS15 model.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model Monte Carlo studies (Dean 1998) that first identified systematic deviations from the Fuller-Fowler-Newman (FFN) parameterization of Gamow-Teller strength, and confirms those trends using full diagonalization (Gabriel 2000). It directly contradicts the FFN assumption that Gamow-Teller strength could be modeled as a single matrix element carrying the non-interacting shell-model total strength. The results enable more reliable Type Ia supernova nucleosynthesis predictions by removing one major nuclear-physics uncertainty, and the author notes that similar work is needed for Type II supernovae, where very neutron-rich nuclei require going beyond the \(pf\)-shell.
