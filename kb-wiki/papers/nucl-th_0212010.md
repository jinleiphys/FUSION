# $p+^{4,6,8}He elastic scattering at intermediate energies

> Using a relativistic nuclear optical potential consisting of a Lorentz scalar, $V_{s}$, and the time-like component of a four-vector potential, $V_{0}$, we calculate elastic scattering differential cross sections and polarizations for $p+^{4}$He at intermediate energies for which experimental data are available. We also calculate the differential cross sections and analyzing powers for $p+^{6,8}$He at intermediate energies and compare with the few available experimental data.

# p+⁴,⁶,⁸He elastic scattering at intermediate energies

## Key claim

A relativistic Dirac optical potential with Gaussian form factors can fit p+⁴He elastic scattering data at intermediate energies where the Relativistic Impulse Approximation (RIA) fails at higher momentum transfers, but the parameters extracted from the ⁴He fit cannot be straightforwardly extended to p+⁶,⁸He scattering with satisfactory results.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation with a Lorentz scalar potential V_s and time-like vector potential V_0, each having real and imaginary parts with Gaussian radial dependence. For p+⁴He, they perform two parameter fits to experimental differential cross sections and polarizations at three laboratory energies (0.561, 0.800, and 1.029 GeV), with strengths varying linearly with energy. For p+⁶,⁸He at 0.721 and 0.678 GeV respectively, they extend the second ⁴He fit by scaling effective strengths with the appropriate nuclear densities from Gaussian harmonic oscillator parameters, and compare with RIA calculations using the Love-Franey NN t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- RMS matter radius of ⁴He used in RIA calculations: 1.49 fm (from Alkhazov 1997)
- Laboratory energies for p+⁴He scattering: 0.561, 0.800, and 1.029 GeV
- Laboratory energies for p+⁶He and p+⁸He scattering: 0.721 GeV and 0.678 GeV respectively
- First fit imaginary strengths required a NN total cross section about 2.5 times the physical value to be consistent with RIA interpretation
- Second fit radii set to r_0 = 1.22 fm, corresponding to an RMS radius of 1.49 fm
- Real potential radii in first fit found to be about 10% larger than the matter radius

## Context

This work extends the relativistic optical model approach of Arnold, Clark, and Mercer (1979) and the RIA framework of McNeil, Shepard, and Wallace (1983) to exotic helium isotopes. It builds on experimental measurements of p+⁴,⁶,⁸He scattering by Alkhazov (1997) and Egelhof (2001) that determined matter densities of exotic nuclei via inverse kinematics. The study shows that while RIA works well at low momentum transfer for all three systems, it fails for p+⁴He at higher momentum transfers, and that simple parameter extrapolation from stable to exotic isotopes is unreliable. The authors identify the need to account for boost effects between target rest frame and center-of-mass frame as a possible improvement for future work.
