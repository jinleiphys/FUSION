# Microscopic description of fission in neutron-rich plutonium isotopes with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> The most recent parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional is used to describe fission in the isotopes $^{232-280}$ Pu. We resort to the methodology introduced in our previous studies [Phys. Rev. C \textbf{88}, 054325 (2013) and Phys. Rev. C \textbf {89}, 054310 (2014)] to compute the fission paths, collective masses and zero point quantum corrections within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. The systematics of the spontaneous fission half-lives t$_{SF}$, masses and charges of the fragments in Plutonium isotopes is analyzed and compared with available experimental data. We also pay attention to isomeric states, the deformation properties of the fragments as well as to the competition between the spontaneous fission and $α$-decay modes. The impact of pairing correlations on the predicted t$_{SF}$ values is demonstrated with the help of calculations for $^{232-280}$Pu in which the pairing strengths of the Gogny-D1M energy density functional are modified by 5 $\%$ and 10 $\%$, respectively. We further validate the use of the D1M parametrization through the discussion of the half-lives in $^{242-262}$Fm. Our calculations corroborate that, though the uncertainties in the absolute values of physical observables are large, the Gogny-D1M Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework still reproduces the trends with mass and/or neutron numbers and therefore represents a reasonable starting point to describe fission in heavy nuclear systems from a microscopic point of view.

## Key claim

The Gogny-D1M Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework reproduces the trends of spontaneous fission half-lives, fragment masses, and charges along the plutonium isotopic chain, but the authors explicitly state that uncertainties in the absolute values of physical observables are large, and the calculated half-lives should be taken as lower bounds because the gamma degree of freedom and the path connecting one-fragment and two-fragment curves are neglected.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional for the isotopes 232-280Pu, computing one-fragment (1F) and two-fragment (2F) fission paths with constraints on quadrupole (Q20), octupole (Q30), and necking operators. Collective masses were obtained within both the Adiabatic Time-Dependent HFB (ATDHFB) and Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GCM) schemes, and spontaneous fission half-lives were computed via the WKB formalism with zero-point rotational and vibrational corrections. The pairing strengths of the Gogny-D1M functional were artificially increased by 5% and 10% to study the impact of pairing correlations on the predicted half-lives.

## Key numbers

* For 246Pu, the first fission isomer lies at Q20 = 44 b with an excitation energy of 3.90 MeV, and the inner barrier height is 9.98 MeV at Q20 ≈ 28 b.
* For 246Pu, the second fission isomer lies at Q20 = 94 b with an excitation energy of 4.29 MeV above the ground state.
* For 246Pu with E0 = 1.5 MeV, the spontaneous fission half-life predicted within the ATDHFB scheme is 4.544 × 10^32 s, while the GCM scheme gives 2.581 × 10^25 s.
* For the very neutron-rich isotope 280Pu, the basis with Mz,MAX = 17 provides an error (relative to Mz,MAX = 18) always smaller than 0.81 MeV, even at quadrupole moments around 200 b.
* The two-fragment solution for 246Pu at Q20 = 140 b consists of a spherical 132Sn fragment and an oblate 114Ru fragment with β2 = -0.23 and β3 = 0.02, with a Coulomb repulsion of 205.81 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic fission studies of uranium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2013, 2014) to the plutonium chain, using the Gogny-D1M parametrization which improves upon the D1S parametrization by better reproducing nuclear masses and neutron matter equation of state. The calculations predict very high barrier heights for N=184 isotones, consistent with Extended Thomas-Fermi calculations, and reveal that the minimal-energy two-fragment configurations systematically yield a heavy fragment near 132Sn rather than the experimentally observed A ≈ 140, indicating that a more sophisticated dynamical approach is needed to reproduce the broad experimental mass distribution.
