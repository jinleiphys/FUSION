# Multiloop Diagrams in Non-Relativistic Field Theories and the Deuteron Quadrupole Moment

> A new method is developed to calculate multiloop Feynman diagrams in non-relativistic field theories. A consistent scheme for regularizing and renormalizing loop integrals is established and shown to reproduce the results of dimensional regularization (DR) and modified minimal subtraction MS^bar or DR and power divergence subtraction (PDS) up to next-to-leading order (NLO). However, significantly less effort is required to evaluate the integrals and the methods are easily generalized to higher order graphs. Thus, even the most complicated multiloop graph can be expressed in terms of analytic functions. These techniques are then used to calculate the quadrupole moment of the deuteron to three loops in the Kaplan, Savage, and Wise effective field theory. A new unfixed direct S->D wave counterterm occurs at this order and its value is determined.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new coordinate-space parameter subtraction (CPS) method for regularizing and renormalizing multiloop Feynman diagrams in non-relativistic effective field theories, which reproduces the results of dimensional regularization with PDS or MSbar up to next-to-leading order but requires significantly less effort; the method is then applied to calculate the deuteron quadrupole moment to three loops in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise effective field theory, where a new unfixed direct S-to-D wave counterterm appears at this order and its value is determined by fitting to the experimental quadrupole moment.

## Method

The author transforms loop integrals from momentum space to coordinate space, where divergences become divergences in a parameter with units of energy (labeled μ for linear divergences and ν for artificial infrared divergences), and treats them consistently by labeling the divergences as renormalization scales. The CPS method is applied to bound state graphs with zero and finite momentum transfer, as well as above-threshold scattering graphs, by evaluating 1-, 3-, 4-, and 5-propagator integrals for the two-pion exchange graph contributing to the deuteron quadrupole moment at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). ## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_π = 138 MeV
- Pion decay constant f = 132 MeV
- Previously determined counterterms at renormalization scale μ = m_π: C_0 = -5.51 fm^2, C_2 = 9.91 fm^4, D_2 = 1.32 fm^4
- NLO deuteron quadrupole moment: μ_Q^{NLO} = 0.418 fm^2 (more than 40% larger than the experimental value of 0.2859 fm^2)
- The 1-propagator integral I_γ(1) = (μ - γ)/(4π)
- The 3-propagator integral J(1) = -1/(16π^2) log[(2γ + m_π)/μ]
- The 4-propagator integral K(1) = 1/(64π^3)[2(m_π+γ)(log(2(m_π+γ)/μ)-1)+μ]
- The 5-propagator integral L(1) = 1/[(4π)^3γ][Polylog_2(-m_π/(m_π+2γ)) + π^2/12]
- The 5-propagator integral LO(1) = 2 log 2 / [(4π)^3(m_π+γ)]
- The 5-propagator integral LZ(1) = 1/[(4π)^3(m_π+γ)^3][ν(m_π+γ) - (2 log 2 + 1)/3]

## Context

This work extends the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory approach (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) by providing a new calculational method (CPS) that simplifies the evaluation of higher-order loop integrals that become intractable with standard dimensional regularization and Feynman parameter techniques. The calculation of the deuteron quadrupole moment to NNLO provides the first test of the KSW expansion at this order, where previous NLO calculations (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1999) gave a value 40% above experiment, and the NNLO result is expected to have errors within 10%, rivaling potential model calculations that are consistently about 7% below the experimental value. The determination of the new C_2^{S→D} counterterm from the quadrupole moment enables future NNLO calculations of low-energy N-N scattering in the spin triplet channel, particularly the mixing parameter ε_1.
