# Production and in-medium modification of $ϕ$ mesons in proton-nucleus reactions from a transport approach

> Production and in-medium modification of hidden strange $ϕ$ mesons are studied in proton-nucleus reactions - p+C, p+Cu and p+Pb - at 12 GeV/c, partially measured by the KEK E325 collaboration via the dilepton decay mode. This work is based on the off-shell microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which provides the dynamical description of strongly interacting hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom in dense matter, and especially makes it possible to simulate in-medium off-shell dynamics of the $ϕ$ meson in the reactions studied. Different in-medium scenarios for the modification of the $ϕ$ meson spectral function in nuclear matter are investigated: i) dropping pole mass, ii) collisional broadening and iii) simultaneous dropping pole mass and collisional broadening. Even though the $ϕ$ meson production in p+A reactions occurs only at densities around or below normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0$, we find visible modifications of the generated dilepton spectra for the in-medium scenarios compared to the vacuum distribution.

## Key claim
Even though phi meson production in p+A reactions occurs only at densities around or below normal nuclear matter density rho_0, visible modifications of the generated dilepton spectra are found for in-medium scenarios compared to the vacuum distribution, with the magnitude of the effect depending on the target nucleus and the specific modification scenario.

## Method
The authors employed the off-shell Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach in its hadronic (HSD) implementation to simulate 12 GeV/c proton collisions on carbon, copper, and lead targets. They implemented three in-medium modification scenarios for the phi meson spectral function: dropping pole mass, collisional broadening, and a combination of both, using a Breit-Wigner parametrization with density-dependent mass and width terms. The phi meson production included contributions from baryon-baryon strings, meson-baryon strings, kaon-antikaon pair annihilation, and low-energy pion-baryon scattering, with novel meson-baryon production channels calculated from a T-matrix coupled channel approach based on an extended SU(6) chiral effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers
- Beam momentum: 12 GeV/c for p+C, p+Cu, and p+Pb reactions
- Mass shift reported by KEK E325 experiment: -35 +/- 7 MeV at normal nuclear matter density rho_0
- Broadening factor from KEK E325: 3.6^{+1.8}_{-1.2} at normal nuclear matter density rho_0
- ALICE measurement of phi-N scattering length real part: Re(a_phiN) = 0.85 +/- 0.34 (stat.) +/- 0.14 (syst.) fm
- ALICE measurement of phi-N scattering length imaginary part: Im(a_phiN) = 0.16 +/- 0.10 (stat.) +/- 0.09 (syst.) fm
- Lattice HAL QCD result for spin 3/2 scattering length: Re(a_phiN^{3/2}) = 1.43 +/- 0.23 (stat.) +/- 0.14 (syst.) fm
- CLAS photoproduction result for absolute scattering length: |a_phiN| = 0.063 +/- 0.010 fm
- Maximum density reached in carbon target: 0.25 rho_0
- Momentum cut for dilepton analysis: beta gamma < 1.25

## Context
This work extends the PHSD transport approach previously applied to phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions by Song and Bratkovskaya (2022) to the study of proton-nucleus reactions at 12 GeV/c, partially measured by the KEK E325 collaboration. It builds on earlier BUU transport studies of rho and omega meson in-medium modifications in pA reactions by Bratkovskaya et al. (2001), and addresses the ongoing discrepancy between the KEK E325 mass shift result, the large phi-N scattering lengths from ALICE femtoscopy measurements and lattice QCD calculations by the HAL QCD Collaboration, and the much smaller value from CLAS photoproduction data. The results enable future comparisons with the KEK E325 experimental data and provide predictions for the upcoming J-PARC E16 experiment, which is expected to have sufficient precision to study the momentum dependence of the dilepton spectrum from pA reactions.
