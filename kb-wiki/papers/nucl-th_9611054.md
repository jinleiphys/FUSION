# E2 component in subcoulomb breakup of ^{8}B

> We calculate the angular distribution and total cross section of the ^{7}Be fragment emitted in the break up reaction of ^{8}B on ^{58}Ni and ^{208}Pb targets at the subCoulomb beam energy of 25.8 MeV, within the non-relativistic theory of Coulomb excitation with proper three-body kinematics. The relative contributions of the E1, E2 and M1 multipolarities to the cross sections are determined. The E2 component makes up about 65% and 40% of the ^{7}Be total cross section for the ^{58}Ni and ^{208}Pb targets respectively. We find that the extraction of the astrophysical S-factor, S_{17}(0), for the ^{7}Be(p,γ)^8B reaction at solar energies from the measurements of the cross sections of the ^{7}Be fragment in the Coulomb dissociation of ^{8}B at sub-Coulomb energies is still not free from the uncertainties of the E2 component.

# E2 component in subcoulomb breakup of ^8B

## Key claim

The extraction of the astrophysical S-factor S_{17}(0) for the ^7Be(p,γ)^8B reaction from Coulomb dissociation measurements at sub-Coulomb energies remains subject to uncertainties from the E2 component, even when using proper three-body kinematics; no angular regime of the ^7Be fragment distribution is completely free from E2 contributions, contrary to earlier suggestions.

## Method

The authors calculated the angular distribution and total cross section of the ^7Be fragment from the breakup of ^8B on ^58Ni and ^208Pb targets at 25.8 MeV beam energy using the non-relativistic theory of Coulomb excitation (Alder-Winther theory) with proper three-body kinematics. They related the triple differential cross section for Coulomb breakup to the Coulomb excitation cross section via a Jacobian transformation that correctly accounts for the difference between the ^7Be emission angle and the ^7Be-p center-of-mass angle, and integrated over the unobserved proton solid angle and ^7Be energy to obtain ^7Be angular distributions for E1, E2, and M1 multipolarities.

## Key numbers

- E2 component makes up about 65% of the ^7Be total cross section for the ^58Ni target at 25.8 MeV
- E2 component makes up about 40% of the ^7Be total cross section for the ^208Pb target at 25.8 MeV
- The ratio of experimental total breakup cross section of ^7Be to Rutherford elastic scattering of ^7Be is reported as (8.1 ± 0.8 ±_{0.5}^{2.0}) × 10^{-3} for the ^58Ni target
- Using three-body kinematics, the calculated ratio of total ^7Be cross section to Rutherford elastic scattering of ^8B is 4.1 × 10^{-2} for the ^58Ni target, about an order of magnitude larger than the experimental value
- The model of Typel and Baur gives a ratio of 2.6 × 10^{-2} for the same quantity
- Beyond 50 degrees on the ^58Ni target, E2 cross sections are about 3 to 5 times larger than the corresponding E1 cross sections
- In the angular region of 30-40 degrees on the ^208Pb target, E2 cross sections still contribute up to 40%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Coulomb dissociation method for determining the ^7Be(p,γ)^8B reaction rate, which is crucial for understanding the solar neutrino puzzle. The authors improve upon the analysis of von Schwarzenberg et al. (1996) by using proper three-body kinematics instead of approximating the final state as a two-body system, and by directly calculating Coulomb excitation functions rather than interpolating from tables of Alder and Winther (1975). The results contradict the suggestion in von Schwarzenberg et al. (1996) that using a heavy target would allow clean determination of S_{17}, and instead support the earlier conclusion of Shyam et al. (1996) regarding the GSI experiments at higher beam energies being more promising for reducing E2 uncertainties.
