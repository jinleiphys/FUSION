# QCD phase diagram at finite isospin and baryon chemical potentials with the self-consistent mean field approximation

> The self-consistent mean field approximation of two-flavor NJL model with introducing a free parameter $α$ to reflect the competition between "direct" channel and the "exchange" channel, is employed to study QCD phase structure at finite isospin chemical potential $μ_I$, finite baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ and finite temperature $T$, especially the location of the QCD critical point. It is found that, for fixed isospin chemical potentials the lower temperature of phase transition is obtained with $α$ increasing in the $T-μ_I$ plane, and the largest difference of the phase transition temperature with different $α$'s appears at $μ_I \sim 1.5m_π$. At $μ_I=0$ the temperature of the QCD critical end point (CEP) decreases with $α$ increasing, while the critical baryon chemical potential increases. At high isospin chemical potential ($μ_I=500$ MeV), the temperature of the QCD tricritical point (TCP) increases with $α$ increasing, and in the regions of low temperature the system will transit from pion superfluidity phase to the normal phase as $μ_B$ increases. At low temperatures, the critical temperature of QCD phase transition with different $α$'s rapidly increases with $μ_I$ at the beginning, and then increases smoothly around $μ_I>300$ MeV. In high baryon density region, the increase of the isospin chemical potential will raise the critical baryon chemical potential of phase transition.

# QCD phase diagram at finite isospin and baryon chemical potentials with the self-consistent mean field approximation

## Key claim
The location of the QCD critical endpoint and tricritical point depends on the free parameter alpha, which reflects the competition between direct and exchange interaction channels in the NJL model; the results with alpha=0.5 best reproduce lattice QCD data for isospin density and energy density, but the value of alpha cannot be fixed by the self-consistent mean field approximation itself and must ultimately be constrained by experiment.

## Method
The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a self-consistent mean field approximation that includes both the original NJL Lagrangian and its Fierz-transformed form, weighted by a free parameter alpha. They introduce finite isospin chemical potential mu_I and baryon chemical potential mu_B, derive the thermodynamic potential and gap equations for the quark condensate, pion condensate, and number densities, and solve these coupled integral equations numerically for three values of alpha (0, 0.5, 0.9) to map the phase diagram in the T-mu_B plane at fixed mu_I and in the T-mu_I plane at mu_B=0.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass used for parameter fitting: m_pi = 131.7 MeV, with current quark mass m_0 = 4.76 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 659 MeV, and coupling G = 4.78 x 10^-6 MeV^-2.
- At mu_I=0, the critical endpoint (CEP) for alpha=0 is at (T=72 MeV, mu_B=813 MeV); for alpha=0.5 at (T=72 MeV, mu_B=841 MeV); for alpha=0.9 at (T=69 MeV, mu_B=971 MeV).
- At mu_I=200 MeV, the tricritical point (TCP) for alpha=0 is at (T=49 MeV, mu_B=761 MeV); for alpha=0.5 at (T=49 MeV, mu_B=769 MeV); for alpha=0.9 at (T=30 MeV, mu_B=823 MeV).
- At mu_I=500 MeV, the TCP for alpha=0 is at (T=111 MeV, mu_B=612 MeV); for alpha=0.5 at (T=120 MeV, mu_B=646 MeV); for alpha=0.9 at (T=123 MeV, mu_B=821 MeV).
- The largest difference in phase transition temperature among different alpha values in the T-mu_I plane occurs at mu_I approximately 1.5 m_pi.
- At zero temperature and mu_B=0, the critical isospin chemical potential for pion condensation is mu_I^C = m_pi for all alpha values.

## Context
This work extends previous NJL model studies of the QCD phase diagram (e.g., Lian, S.P. Klevansky) by incorporating the self-consistent mean field approach of Fei Wang, which systematically includes Fierz-transformed interaction channels. The results with alpha=0.5 agree with lattice QCD data from Brandt for isospin density and energy density. The study enables future comparison with neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision experiments, as the parameter alpha could potentially be constrained by astronomical data from neutron star mergers (as suggested by Tong Zhao and Q). The phase diagrams at finite isospin chemical potential are relevant for understanding pion superfluidity and the behavior of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter.
