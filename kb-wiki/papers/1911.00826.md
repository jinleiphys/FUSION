# Probing the partonic degrees of freedom in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV

> We investigate the role of partonic degrees of freedom in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV carried out at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) by studying the production and collective flow of identified hadrons at intermediate $p_T$ via the coalescence of soft and hard partons generated from viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) and the energy loss model (LBT), respectively. We find that combining the intermediate $p_T$ hadrons from the coalescence with the low $p_T$ hadrons from hydrodynamics and high $p_T$ hadrons from the jet fragmentation, our Hydro-Coal-Frag model provides a nice description of the measured $p_T$-spectra and the differential elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ of pions, kaons and protons over the $p_T$ range from 0 to 6 GeV. We further demonstrate the necessity of including the quark coalescence contribution to reproduce the experimentally observed approximate number of constituent quark scaling of hadron $v_2$ at intermediate $p_T$. Our results thus indicate the importance of partonic degrees of freedom and also hint at the possible formation of quark-gluon plasma in high multiplicity p+Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the observed approximate number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow \(v_2\) at intermediate \(p_T\) in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions can be explained only if quark coalescence is included in the hadron production model, thereby providing a strong indication for partonic degrees of freedom and the possible formation of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in these small systems. The claim is hedged by noting that the results "hint at" rather than prove QGP formation, and that the study is the first quantitative demonstration of this kind for p-Pb collisions.

## Method
The authors developed a hybrid model called Hydro-Coal-Frag that combines three components: (1) low-\(p_T\) hadrons from the 2+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 with TRENTo initial conditions and s95-PCE equation of state; (2) intermediate-\(p_T\) hadrons from quark coalescence of thermal partons (from the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface at \(T_c=150\) MeV) and hard partons (from the LBT energy loss model with \(\alpha_s=0.15\)); and (3) high-\(p_T\) hadrons from jet fragmentation of remnant hard partons via PYTHIA8. The coalescence model includes thermal-thermal, thermal-hard, and hard-hard parton recombinations with Wigner functions for mesons and baryons up to excited states \(n=10\) and \(n_1+n_2=10\), respectively. The hadronic afterburner stage uses UrQMD.

## Key numbers
- The three tunable parameters in the Hydro-Coal-Frag model are set to \(p_{T1}=1.6\) GeV (minimum \(p_T\) for thermal partons in coalescence), \(p_{T2}=2.6\) GeV (minimum \(p_T\) for hard partons in coalescence), and \(m_{\rm max}=1.5\) GeV (maximum virtuality for hard gluons).
- The average charged particle multiplicity for the 0-20% centrality class is \(\left<dN_{ch}/dy\right>= 35.6\pm0.8\) (\(|\eta_{lab}|<0.5\)).
- The thermal quark masses at \(T_c=150\) MeV are set to \(m_{u,d}=0.25\) GeV and \(m_{s}=0.43\) GeV.
- The strong coupling constant in LBT is fixed to \(\alpha_s=0.15\).
- The model describes data over the \(p_T\) range from 0 to 6 GeV.
- The NCQ scaling of \(v_2\) is approximately reproduced in the range \(1.4 < p_T/n < 2.2\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of collective flow and partonic degrees of freedom from heavy-ion collisions (Au-Au at RHIC, Pb-Pb at LHC) to small systems such as high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at the LHC. It directly addresses the debate on whether the observed collectivity in small systems originates from final-state effects (hydrodynamics or transport models) or initial-state effects (color glass condensate, IP-Glasma). The paper builds on prior hydrodynamic studies of p-Pb collisions by Werner (2013), Bozek (2013), and Shen (2016), and on coalescence models used in heavy-ion collisions by Greco (2003) and Han (2016). The results enable future work to test the sensitivity of the NCQ scaling to the model parameters and to extend the approach to other small systems such as p-p, p-Au, d-Au, and \(^3\)He-Au collisions.
