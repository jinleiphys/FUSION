# Consistent hydrodynamic description of one- and two-particle observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC

> We show that a consistent hydrodynamic description of soft-hadronic one- and two-particle observables (the HBT radii) studied in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC may be obtained if one uses the Gaussian energy density profile as the initial condition. The transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$, and the pionic azimuthally sensitive HBT radii are successfully reproduced, which hints that the long standing HBT puzzle has been solved.

# Consistent hydrodynamic description of one- and two-particle observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

A consistent hydrodynamic description of both one-particle observables (transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow v2) and two-particle observables (HBT radii) at RHIC is achieved only when the initial energy-density profile is taken as a Gaussian rather than the traditional optical Glauber profile, suggesting the long-standing HBT puzzle has been solved. The authors hedge that other ingredients (semi-hard equation of state, early starting time) are also important but alone insufficient, and that the Gaussian profile leads to faster development of initial transverse flow and shorter system evolution.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 boost-invariant inviscid hydrodynamics with initial energy-density profiles obtained from Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations (GLISSANDO) approximated by Gaussians, followed by statistical hadronization with the THERMINATOR event generator assuming a single freeze-out scenario at final temperature Tf = 145 MeV. The model has two additional parameters: initial temperature Ti (fixing normalization) and initial time τ0 = 0.25 fm; widths of the initial Gaussian energy distributions for each centrality class were obtained directly from GLISSANDO simulations.

## Key numbers

- Final freeze-out temperature: Tf = 145 MeV
- Initial time for start of hydrodynamics: τ0 = 0.25 fm
- Ratio Rout/Rside from traditional Glauber initial conditions exceeds experimental values by 20-50%
- Centrality classes studied: c = 0-5%, c = 20-30% for spectra; c = 20-40% for v2; central collisions for HBT radii
- Azimuthal HBT radii results shown for kT bins: 0.15-0.25 GeV, 0.25-0.35 GeV, and 0.35-0.6 GeV
- In the free-streaming plus sudden equilibration variant, hydrodynamic evolution delayed to τ = 1 fm with free-streaming from 0.25 fm ≤ τ ≤ 1 fm

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, which had successfully reproduced one-particle observables (spectra and v2) but failed on two-particle HBT radii, a discrepancy known as the HBT puzzle. The authors contrast their approach with that of Pratt (2008), who emphasized very early hydrodynamic start (τ0 = 0.1 fm) and viscous effects to increase transverse acceleration, but note that Pratt's studies were restricted to cylindrically symmetric cases and did not simultaneously describe elliptic flow. The results enable further investigation into the relative importance of initial energy profile, initial pre-equilibrium flow, and viscous effects, with the authors noting that a free-streaming plus sudden equilibration variant (Broniowski 2008) using Gaussian initial conditions describes the data equally well, suggesting universality of the Gaussian initial conditions.
