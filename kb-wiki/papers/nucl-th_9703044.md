# ``Soft'' transverse expansion and flow in a multi-fluid model without phase transition

> We study transverse expansion and directed flow in Au(11AGeV)Au reactions within a multi-fluid dynamical model. Although we do not employ an equation of state (EoS) with a first order phase transition, we find a slow increase of the transverse velocities of the nucleons with time. A similar behaviour can be observed for the directed nucleon flow. This is due to non-equilibrium effects which also lead to less and slower conversion of longitudinal into transverse momentum. We also show that the proton rapidity distribution at CERN energies, as calculated within this model, agrees well with the preliminary NA44-data.

# Soft transverse expansion and flow in a multi-fluid model without phase transition

## Key claim

The paper shows that non-equilibrium effects in a three-fluid dynamical model can produce a slow increase of transverse velocities and reduced directed flow in Au(11AGeV)Au collisions, mimicking the signals that one-fluid hydrodynamical models attribute to a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, even though no phase transition is included in the equation of state.

## Method

The authors construct a three-fluid hydrodynamical model in which projectile nucleons, target nucleons, and produced particles (fireball) are treated as separate fluids that exchange energy, momentum, and baryon charge via parametrized source terms derived from nucleon-nucleon collision rates. The baryonic fluids use a non-relativistic ideal gas equation of state with compression energy but no phase transition, heavy resonances, or attractive interactions. At late times, when relative velocities become comparable to thermal velocities, the two nucleonic fluids are merged into a single fluid.

## Key numbers

- Maximum baryon density in the one-fluid limit is twice the compression in the three-fluid model and is reached earlier (Fig. 3, no explicit numerical value given for the density ratio beyond "twice").
- In the one-fluid limit, the transverse velocity profile reaches speed of light at 4.8 fm/c (central collision, b = 0 fm).
- Mean in-plane momentum per nucleon in the one-fluid limit reaches approximately 300 MeV/c.
- Mean in-plane momentum per nucleon in the three-fluid model does not exceed 120 MeV/c.
- Normal nuclear matter density used in the compression energy ansatz: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the multi-fluid approach of the two-fluid model (2fluid) and the three-fluid model (3fluid, Dum95a) to study non-equilibrium effects in heavy-ion collisions. It directly contradicts the interpretation of earlier one-fluid hydrodynamical calculations (Yaris, otherflow, Hung) that attributed a minimum in the excitation function of directed flow and a prolonged system lifetime to a first-order phase transition. The results suggest that predictions of the one-fluid model for phase transition signals may need revision if non-equilibrium effects are significant. The model successfully reproduces preliminary NA44 data (na44) for proton rapidity distributions at CERN energies (Pb 160 GeV per nucleon).
