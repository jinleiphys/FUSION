# Quasielastic K+ scattering in nuclei

> The quasielastic scattering kaon-nucleus experiment performed at BNL is analyzed in a finite nucleus continuum random phase approximation framework, treating the reaction mechanism in Glauber theory up to two-step inelastic processes. A good description of the data is achieved, also providing a useful constraint on the strength of the effective particle-hole interaction in the scalar-isoscalar channel at intermediate momentum transfers.

## Key claim
The paper argues that quasielastic K+ scattering data from BNL can be well described by a non-relativistic continuum RPA model combined with Glauber theory, but only if the scalar-isoscalar effective particle-hole interaction is reduced by approximately 50% relative to the bare G-matrix strength; the abstract's claim of "good description" glosses over this necessary reduction of the interaction strength.

## Method
The author calculated K+-nucleus quasielastic cross sections using a non-relativistic finite-nucleus continuum random phase approximation (RPA) framework, with nuclear dynamics described by a Woods-Saxon mean field and a phenomenological complex particle-hole self-energy for spreading width. The reaction mechanism was treated via Glauber theory up to two-step inelastic processes, using a G-matrix based effective particle-hole interaction, and the one-step contributions were calculated without resorting to the effective number approximation.

## Key numbers
- The effective number of nucleons (N_eff) obtained from integrating experimental QE cross sections without background subtraction is ~30% higher than Glauber theory predictions.
- The G-matrix interaction in the scalar-isoscalar channel requires a reduction of ~50% to reproduce the data.
- Using Glauber predictions for N_eff in relativistic calculations would underestimate the data by ~30%.

## Context
This work extends earlier analyses of the BNL quasielastic K+ scattering experiment reported by Kor (1993) and Kor (1995), which used relativistic nuclear structure models (mean field, Hartree, RPA) with a simple effective number approximation for the reaction mechanism. The present calculation improves upon those by using a non-relativistic continuum RPA with a proper Glauber treatment of distortion, avoiding the overestimation of collectivity inherent in the effective number approximation. The results provide a constraint on the effective particle-hole interaction in the scalar-isoscalar channel at intermediate momentum transfers and suggest the presence of an unresolved background in the high-energy tail, similar to that seen in (e,e') scattering.
