# Possible Studies of Parton Distribution Functions at JHF

> We discuss possible studies of parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the nucleon and nuclei at the Japan Hadron Facility (JHF). First, the PDFs could be investigated by the 50 GeV primary proton facility. The distributions at medium x are determined, for example, by Drell-Yan measurements. Second, there are feasibility studies to propose a neutrino factory within the 50 GeV proton ring. If such an intensive high-energy neutrino facility is built, neutrino reactions should be able to provide valuable information on the PDFs, whereas the current structure functions have been measured mainly for neutrino-iron reactions.

## Key claim

The Japan Hadron Facility (JHF) could contribute to parton distribution function (PDF) studies through two possible avenues: a 50 GeV primary proton beam for Drell-Yan measurements at medium and large x, and a potential neutrino factory within the 50 GeV proton ring for neutrino scattering measurements; however, the neutrino factory feasibility is still at an early stage and its realization is not certain.

## Method

The paper surveys the kinematical reach of a 50 GeV proton beam at JHF for Drell-Yan processes, estimating that the center-of-mass energy of 10 GeV limits the target momentum fraction to x2 > 0.1, thus probing medium and large x regions. It then reviews the theoretical formalism for polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan reactions to access tensor structure functions and polarized antiquark flavor asymmetries. Finally, it examines the potential of a 30 GeV neutrino factory for measuring unpolarized and polarized structure functions, including the parity-violating structure functions g3, g4, and g5 that are inaccessible in electron and muon scattering.

## Key numbers

- The JHF primary proton beam energy is 50 GeV, giving a center-of-mass energy of sqrt(s) = 10 GeV for Drell-Yan processes.
- For Drell-Yan at JHF, the target momentum fraction x2 is limited to x2 > 0.1 based on the requirement that the dimuon mass squared Q^2 be larger than a few GeV^2.
- For a 30 GeV neutrino factory, the minimum Bjorken x accessible in deep inelastic scattering is x_min = 0.017, derived from x_min = 1/(2 * 1 * 30).
- The E866/NuSea data cover the antiquark flavor asymmetry in the x region x = 0.04 to 0.2, where d-bar is larger than u-bar.
- The extracted quark spin content Delta Sigma ranges from 5% to 30% depending on the analysis model, as shown by the LSS, SMC, and AAC parametrizations.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of PDF studies from existing facilities like Fermilab (E866/NuSea, E772) and CERN (NA51, NMC) to the proposed JHF, noting that unpolarized PDFs are well established by the CTEQ, GRV, and MRST groups but polarized PDFs and nuclear PDFs remain poorly determined. The work builds on the EMC discovery of the proton spin crisis and the NMC finding of Gottfried-sum-rule violation, and it enables future experimental programs at JHF that could determine antiquark flavor asymmetries (u-bar/d-bar, Delta u-bar/Delta d-bar, Delta_T u-bar/Delta_T d-bar), nuclear antiquark distributions at medium x, and the quark spin content directly from neutrino scattering without relying on low-energy semi-leptonic data.
