# Electric Conductivity from the solution of the Relativistic Boltzmann Equation

> We present numerical results of electric conductivity $σ_{el}$ of a fluid obtained solving the Relativistic Transport Boltzmann equation in a box with periodic boundary conditions. We compute $σ_{el}$ using two methods: the definition itself, i.e. applying an external electric field, and the evaluation of the Green-Kubo relation based on the time evolution of the current-current correlator. We find a very good agreement between the two methods. We also compare numerical results with analytic formulas in Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) where the relaxation time for $σ_{el}$ is determined by the transport cross section $σ_{tr}$, i.e. the differential cross section weighted with the collisional momentum transfer. We investigate the electric conductivity dependence on the microscopic details of the 2-body scatterings: isotropic and anisotropic cross-section, and massless and massive particles. We find that the RTA underestimates considerably $σ_{el}$; for example at screening masses $m_D \sim \,T$ such underestimation can be as large as a factor of 2. Furthermore, we study a more realistic case for a quark-gluon system (QGP) considering both a quasi-particle model, tuned to lQCD thermodynamics, as well as the case of a pQCD gas with running coupling. Also for these cases more directly related to the description of the QGP system, we find that RTA significantly underestimate the $σ_{el}$ by about a $60-80\%$.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) systematically underestimates the electric conductivity of a quark-gluon plasma compared to full solutions of the Relativistic Boltzmann Equation, with the discrepancy reaching a factor of 2 for screening masses near the temperature and 60-80% for realistic QGP models, contrary to the common assumption that RTA provides a reasonable approximation.

## Method
The authors solved the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport equation numerically in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions using a stochastic collision algorithm for 2-to-2 elastic scatterings. They computed the electric conductivity using two independent methods: the direct E-field method (applying an external electric field and measuring the resulting current) and the Green-Kubo relation (evaluating the time-correlation function of the electric current in thermal equilibrium). The study systematically varied the microscopic scattering details including isotropic versus anisotropic cross-sections, massless versus massive particles, and employed a quasi-particle model tuned to lattice QCD thermodynamics as well as a pQCD gas with running coupling.

## Key numbers
- For massless particles with isotropic cross-section (sigma_tot = 3 mb), RTA overestimates sigma_el/T by about 30% at T = 0.6 GeV
- For anisotropic scattering with screening mass m_D ~ T, RTA underestimates sigma_el by as large as a factor of 2
- For the quasi-particle QGP model, RTA underestimates sigma_el by an average factor of 1.8 in the temperature range explored
- For the pQCD case with running coupling, RTA underestimates sigma_el by a factor of 1.6
- For the realistic QGP cases, RTA underestimates sigma_el by about 60-80%
- Quasi-particle model predictions for sigma_el/T are about a factor of 4 greater than recent lattice QCD calculations from Amato et al.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the shear viscosity studied by Plumari et al. (2012) using the same transport code, and provides the first systematic comparison of electric conductivity from full Boltzmann solutions versus RTA for both isotropic and anisotropic scatterings. The results enable more accurate modeling of electromagnetic responses in heavy-ion collisions, including the Chiral Magnetic Effect and soft photon emission, and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations of electric conductivity from groups such as Gupta, Ding, Brandt, Aarts, Amato, and Buividovich. The finding that RTA significantly underestimates sigma_el for anisotropic cross-sections suggests that previous RTA-based estimates of electric conductivity in QGP may need revision.
