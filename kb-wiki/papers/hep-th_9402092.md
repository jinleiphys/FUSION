# Two-dimensional SU(N) Gauge Theory on the Light Cone

> Two-dimensional SU($N$) gauge theory is accurately analyzed with the light-front Tamm-Dancoff approximation, both numerically and analytically. The light-front Einstein-Schrödinger equation for mesonic mass reduces to the 't Hooft equation in the large $N$ limit, $g^2N$ fixed, where $g$ is the coupling constant. Hadronic masses are numerically obtained in the region of $m^2 \ll g^2N$, where $m$ is the bare quark (q) mass. The lightest mesonic and baryonic states are almost in valence. The second lightest mesonic state is highly relativistic in the sense that it has a large 4-body ($ {\rm qq} \bar{\rm q} \bar{\rm q} $) component in addition to the valence (${\rm q} \bar{\rm q}$) one. In the strong coupling limit our results are consistent with the prediction of the bosonization for ratios of the lightest and second lightest mesonic masses to the lightest baryonic one. Analytic solutions to the lightest hadronic masses are obtained, with a reasonable approximation, as $\sqrt{2Cm}(1-1/N^2)^{1/4}$ in the mesonic case and $\sqrt{CmN(N-1)}(1-1/N^2)^{1/4}$ in the baryonic case, where $C=(g^2Nπ/6)^{1/2}$. The solutions well reproduce the numerical ones. The $N$- and $m$-dependences of the hadronic masses are explicitly shown by the analytical solutions.

## Key claim

The light-front Einstein-Schrodinger equation for mesonic mass in two-dimensional SU(N) gauge theory reduces to the 't Hooft equation in the large N limit with g^2N fixed, but for finite N the 2-body and 4-body sectors couple through terms of order 1/sqrt(N), and the second lightest mesonic state acquires a large 4-body (qq qbar qbar) component that is not captured by the 't Hooft equation alone.

## Method

The authors applied the light-front Tamm-Dancoff (LFTD) approximation to two-dimensional SU(N) gauge theory, truncating the mesonic Fock space to 2-body (q qbar) and 4-body (qq qbar qbar) components and the baryonic state to the N-body (q^N) component. They derived the light-cone Hamiltonian from the Lagrangian, solved the Einstein-Schrodinger equation numerically by diagonalizing P^- within a finite basis of functions, and also obtained approximate analytic solutions for the lightest hadronic masses under the assumption that these states are predominantly valence.

## Key numbers

- Lightest mesonic mass analytic solution: sqrt(2Cm) * (1 - 1/N^2)^(1/4), where C = (g^2 N pi / 6)^(1/2)
- Lightest baryonic mass analytic solution: sqrt(C m N (N-1)) * (1 - 1/N^2)^(1/4), where C = (g^2 N pi / 6)^(1/2)
- Ratios of the two mesonic masses to the baryonic mass at small m agree with bosonization predictions within approximately 7% error
- Masses of the three bound states tend to zero as m^(0.5) in the massless limit (m -> 0)
- The next-to-leading order in 1/N for the mesonic mass is O(N^(-2)), not O(N^(-1))

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-dimensional QCD beyond the large N limit studied by 't Hooft (1974), who derived the 't Hooft equation for mesonic masses by summing planar diagrams. The authors compare their results in the strong coupling limit with predictions from bosonization (Author, year not specified in text) and note that previous lattice calculations for SU(2) by the same group (Author, year not specified in text) had poor accuracy. The LFTD approach is presented as an alternative to discretized light-cone quantization (DLCQ) methods (Author, years 4-8 in reference list), which showed slow convergence for strong coupling. The analytic solutions enable explicit study of N-dependence and m-dependence of hadronic masses across the full range of N.
