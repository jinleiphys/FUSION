# Constraints on the curvature of nuclear symmetry energy from recent astronomical data within the KIDS framework

> We investigate the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy $S(ρ) $ in the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) framework for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and energy-density functional (EDF). The aim is to constrain the value of the curvature parameter ($K_{\rm sym}$) based on recent astronomical data. First, assuming a standard saturation point, we calculate bulk nuclear properties within KIDS-EDF for different values of the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter ($K_0$) and of the leading-order symmetry energy parameters, i.e., the symmetry energy ($J$) and slope ($L$) at saturation density, each within a broadly accepted range, as well as $K_{\rm sym}$. All of the above EoS parameters are varied independently of each other. The skewness parameter ($Q_{\rm sym}$) is presently kept fixed at 650 MeV. For all EoS parameter sets which describe the selected nuclear data within better than $0.3\%$, we calculate the neutron-star equation of state and mass-radius relation and analyze the results in terms of Pearson correlation coefficients $r$. We find that the value of $K_{\rm sym}$ is strongly correlated with the radius of both a canonical and a massive star ($|r|>0.9$). If we impose that all known astronomical constraints on the neutron star radii must be satisfied, we deduce $-150 < K_{\rm sym}<0$. As a result, the symmetry energy as a function of the density is consistently found to have an inflection point at $ρ_0<ρ<2ρ_0$. We take the opportunity to report that the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb shows no correlation at all with the neutron star radii ($|r|<0.1$), in contrast with studies which focus on the role of $L$ only.

## Key claim

The curvature parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, \(K_{\rm sym}\), is constrained to \(-150 < K_{\rm sym} < 0\) MeV by imposing that all known astronomical constraints on neutron star radii must be satisfied simultaneously, a result that depends on the fixed value of the skewness parameter \(Q_{\rm sym}=650\) MeV and the assumption of a standard saturation point.

## Method

The authors used the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) energy-density functional framework, which allows independent variation of the equation-of-state parameters \(K_0\), \(J\), \(L\), and \(K_{\rm sym}\) (with \(Q_{\rm sym}\) fixed at 650 MeV). They generated approximately 4000 parameter sets spanning accepted ranges, filtered to about 380 sets that describe 13 bulk nuclear properties (binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei) with an average deviation per datum below 0.3%, then calculated neutron-star mass-radius relations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and analyzed Pearson correlation coefficients between parameters and observables.

## Key numbers

- The curvature parameter \(K_{\rm sym}\) is constrained to \(-150 < K_{\rm sym} < 0\) MeV when imposing that the canonical neutron star radius satisfies \(11.8 \leq R_{1.4} \leq 12.5\) km.
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between \(K_{\rm sym}\) and the radius of a canonical (1.4 solar mass) neutron star is \(|r| > 0.9\) (specifically \(r = 0.90\) for \(R_{1.4}\) and \(r = 0.91\) for \(R_{2.0}\)).
- The correlation coefficient between the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb and neutron star radii is \(|r| < 0.1\) (specifically \(r = 0.09\) for \(R_{1.4}\) and \(r = -0.05\) for \(R_{2.0}\)).
- The dimensionless tidal deformability for a canonical neutron star satisfying the radius constraints falls in the range \(315 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 486\).
- The symmetry energy consistently shows an inflection point at densities between \(\rho_0\) and \(2\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)).

## Context

This work extends the KIDS framework studies of Gil et al. (2021) and Papakonstantinou et al. (2018) by simultaneously varying \(K_0\), \(J\), \(L\), and \(K_{\rm sym}\) independently, whereas prior KIDS work fixed \(K_0\) at 240 MeV. The result \(-150 < K_{\rm sym} < 0\) MeV is consistent with the earlier KIDS range \(-200 \leq K_{\rm sym} \leq 0\) MeV from Gil et al. (2021) and with Bayesian analyses of neutron star tidal deformability by Malik et al. (2018) and Raithel et al. (2019). The finding that neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb shows no correlation with neutron star radii contradicts studies that focus only on the slope parameter \(L\), such as those reviewed by Piekarewicz (2021). The work enables tighter constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy above saturation density when combined with precise astronomical data and neutron skin measurements.
