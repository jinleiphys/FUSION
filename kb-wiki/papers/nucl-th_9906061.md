# Relativistic description of J/ψdissociation in hot matter

> The mass spectra and binding radii of heavy quark bound states are studied on the basis of the reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation. The critical values of screening masses for $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$ bound states at a finite temperature are obtained and compared with the previous results given by non-relativistic models.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation yields larger critical screening masses for charmonium and bottomonium dissociation in hot matter, indicating that the magnitude of the screening mass is model-dependent and that a relativistic treatment is necessary for a finer calculation of J/ψ dissociation at finite temperatures.

## Method

The authors solved the spin-independent reduced Salpeter equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) in momentum space for the radial wave functions of c̄c and b̄b bound states, using a color-screened potential that mixes scalar and vector confinement with a Debye screening mass μ. They computed mass spectra and root-mean-square radii for the 1S, 2S, 3S, and 1P states of both systems, then determined the critical screening mass μc for each state by finding where the dissociation energy vanishes.

## Key numbers

- For J/ψ (c̄c 1S): critical screening mass μc = 0.900 GeV (screening length 0.219 fm), compared to 0.600 GeV in Ref.[10] and 0.700 GeV in Ref.[9]
- For c̄c 2S (ψ'): μc = 0.470 GeV; for c̄c 1P (χc): μc = 0.450 GeV
- For Υ (b̄b 1S): μc = 1.640 GeV; for b̄b 2S: μc = 0.690 GeV; for b̄b 1P (χb): μc = 0.640 GeV
- Computed J/ψ mass at zero screening: 3.067 GeV (experimental spin-averaged value 3.068 GeV)
- Computed r.m.s. radius of J/ψ: 0.2868 fm, smaller than 0.4453 fm (Ref.[10]) and 0.4490 fm (Ref.[9])
- χ² fit quality: χ = 0.0066 GeV for this work, versus 0.0223 GeV (Ref.[10]) and 0.0511 GeV (Ref.[9])
- Parameters used: σ = 0.22 GeV², μ₀ = 0.06 GeV, m_c = 1.474 GeV, m_b = 4.762 GeV, α_s(c̄c) = 0.47, α_s(b̄b) = 0.38

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic studies of J/ψ dissociation in hot matter by Matsui and Satz (1986) and the subsequent non-relativistic potential-model calculations of Refs.[9] and [10] to a fully relativistic framework using the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The larger critical screening masses obtained imply that J/ψ may survive to higher temperatures in a relativistic treatment than previously estimated, which has implications for interpreting the anomalous J/ψ suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation. The results also show that relativistic corrections are more significant for charmonium than for bottomonium, as expected from the smaller charm quark mass relative to Λ_QCD.
