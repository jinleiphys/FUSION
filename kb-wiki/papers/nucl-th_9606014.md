# Phase Transition in Warm Nuclear Matter with Alternative Derivative Coupling Models

> An analysis is performed of the liquid-gas phase transition of nuclear matter obtained from different versions of scalar derivate coupling suggested by Zimanyi and Moszkowski (ZM) and the results are compared with those obtained from the Walecka model. We present the phase diagram for the models and one of them, the ZM3 model, has the lowest critical temperature $T_c=13.6$ MeV with the lowest critical density $ρ_c=0.037$ f$m^{-3}$ and pressure $p_c=0.157$ MeV f$m^{-3}$. These results are in accord with recent observations from energetic heavy-ion collisions, which suggest a small liquid-gas phase region.

# Phase Transition in Warm Nuclear Matter with Alternative Derivative Coupling Models

## Key claim
The ZM3 model, a variant of the Zimanyi-Moszkowski derivative scalar coupling model with the strongest nonlinear vector coupling, predicts the lowest critical temperature (13.6 MeV) and the smallest liquid-gas coexistence region among the models studied, which the authors argue is consistent with experimental observations from heavy-ion collisions suggesting a small phase region. However, this agreement is qualitative and the paper does not directly compare to specific experimental critical parameters beyond noting the trend.

## Method
The authors compute the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature using relativistic mean-field theory for four models: the linear Walecka model and three variants of the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) derivative scalar coupling model (ZM, ZM2, ZM3). The coupling constants for each model are fitted to reproduce the binding energy and saturation density of cold nuclear matter at zero temperature. The thermodynamic functions (energy density, pressure, entropy density) are obtained from the mean-field energy-momentum tensor, and the liquid-gas phase coexistence boundary is determined by applying Gibbs criteria (equal temperature, chemical potential, and pressure) to the calculated isotherms.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for ZM3 model: T_c = 13.6 MeV
- Critical density for ZM3 model: ρ_c = 0.037 fm^{-3}
- Critical pressure for ZM3 model: p_c = 0.157 MeV fm^{-3}
- Flash temperatures (highest temperature for self-bound equilibrium): Walecka model 14.1 MeV, ZM model 12.9 MeV, ZM2 model 12.2 MeV, ZM3 model 11.0 MeV
- The Walecka model exhibits a first-order phase transition at zero density at T ~ 185 MeV, while none of the ZM models show this transition
- For comparison, the authors note that including nonlinear terms in the Walecka model reduces its critical temperature to T_c = 14.2 MeV (citing Furs, year not specified in text)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of ZM models in cold, dense nuclear matter (Malheiro, Delfino, Coelho, 1995) to finite temperature, and compares the liquid-gas phase transition predictions with the standard Walecka model (Walecka, 1974). The results are motivated by experimental efforts to observe the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, including the EOS Collaboration's extraction of critical exponents from Au + C collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon and the ALADIN/LAND Collaboration's caloric curve from Au + Au at 600 MeV/nucleon. The ZM3 model's softer equation of state and smaller coexistence region suggest it may be more suitable for describing warm nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, and the density-dependent coupling approach underlying these models connects to relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock methods used for finite nuclei.
