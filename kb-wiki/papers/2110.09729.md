# Quiescent luminosities of transiently accreting neutron stars with neutrino heating due to charged pion decay

> We study the quiescent luminosities of accreting neutron stars by a new mechanism as neutrino heating for additional deep crustal heating, where the neutrino heating is produced by charged pions decay from the nuclear collisions on the surface of neutron star during its active accretion. For low mass neutron star($\lesssim1.4~M_{\odot}$), as the neutrino heating is little($\lesssim1$ MeV per accreted nucleon) or there would be no neutrino heating, the quiescent luminsoity will be not affected or slightly affected. While for massive neutron star ($\gtrsim2~M_{\odot}$), the quiescent luminosity will be enhanced more obviously with neutrino heating in the range 2-6 MeV per accreted nucleon. The observations on cold neutron stars such as 1H 19605+00, SAX J1808.4-3658 can be explained with neutrino heating if a fast cooling and heavy elements surface are considered. The observations on a hot neutron star such as RX J0812.4-3114 can be explained with neutrino heating if the direct Urca process is forbidden for a massive star with light elements surface, which is different from the previous work that the hot observations should be explained with small mass neutron star and the effect of superfluidity.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that neutrino heating from charged pion decay in nuclear collisions on the neutron star surface during active accretion can significantly enhance quiescent luminosities for massive neutron stars (greater than about 2 solar masses), but only if the neutrino heating per accreted nucleon reaches 2-6 MeV; for low-mass neutron stars (less than about 1.4 solar masses) the effect is negligible because the neutrino heating is less than 1 MeV per accreted nucleon or zero.

## Method
The authors used a spherically symmetric general relativistic stellar evolution code to calculate quiescent luminosities of transiently accreting neutron stars, incorporating standard deep crustal heating plus an extra neutrino heating term from charged pion decay. The pion production yields for alpha-iron collisions were estimated using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model at beam energies from 300 to 600 MeV per nucleon, and the resulting neutrino energy deposition in the inner crust (10^12-10^13 g cm^-3) was computed assuming half the neutrinos from charged pion decay move into the crust. Calculations were performed for neutron stars of 1.4 and 2.0 solar masses using three equations of state (Togashi, Togashi+pi condensation, and BSK24) with both heavy (Ni) and light (pure helium) surface compositions.

## Key numbers
- For a 2 solar mass neutron star with radius 10 km, an accreted proton gains kinetic energy of 585 MeV before reaching the surface; for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with radius 10 km, the kinetic energy is 286 MeV
- Neutrino energy from charged pion decay: 1.16 MeV per accreted nucleon at beam energy 300 MeV/nucleon, 6.55 MeV per accreted nucleon at beam energy 600 MeV/nucleon (for alpha-iron collisions)
- For a 2 solar mass neutron star with Togashi equation of state and light element surface, the logarithm of redshifted quiescent luminosity (in erg s^-1) at accretion rate 10^-10 solar masses per year increases from 33.41 (no neutrino heating) to 33.76 (neutrino heating 6 MeV per nucleon)
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with Togashi equation of state, the logarithm of redshifted quiescent luminosity at accretion rate 10^-10 solar masses per year increases from 33.59 (no neutrino heating) to only 33.69 (neutrino heating 1 MeV per nucleon)
- The source RX J0812.4-3114 has accretion rate (5.82_{-2.29}^{+9.16} x 10^-12 solar masses per year) and quiescent luminosity (1.23_{-0.65}^{+1.66} x 10^33 erg s^-1)

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Fujimoto et al. (2018) that charged pion decay from nuclear collisions on neutron star surfaces during accretion provides a new neutrino heating source in the deep crust. The authors apply this mechanism to quiescent luminosity calculations for soft X-ray transients, which previous studies (Yakovlev et al. 2003, Potekhin et al. 2019, Beznogov et al. 2021) had explained using only standard deep crustal heating (1-2 MeV per accreted nucleon) combined with fast neutrino cooling or superfluidity. The results suggest that hot neutron star observations like RX J0812.4-3114, previously requiring low-mass neutron stars with minimal cooling, could instead be explained by massive neutron stars with neutrino heating if the direct Urca process is forbidden. The work enables future studies of how neutrino heating affects crust cooling observations and the interpretation of neutron star mass and equation of state from quiescent luminosity data.
