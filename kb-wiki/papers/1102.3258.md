# Soft ridge in proton-proton collisions

> It is shown that the soft mechanism of multiparticle production by Lund hadronic strings formed by colliding constituent degrees of freedom generates a shape of angular correlations similar to the ridge structure observed in the pp collisions at 7 TeV at the LHC.

# Soft ridge in proton-proton collisions

## Key claim
The authors show that the standard soft mechanism of multiparticle production via Lund hadronic string breaking, with all hard partonic showers switched off in PYTHIA 6.4, generates a ridge-like angular correlation structure similar to that observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions at 7 TeV, but only for a much lower multiplicity cut (n_part > 12) compared to the experimental cut (n_part > 110).

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA 6.4 Monte Carlo generator with the parameter MSTP(82) set to zero, which switches off all non-soft mechanisms and leaves only two hadronic strings stretching between valence degrees of freedom as the source of multiparticle production. They computed the two-particle correlation function C(Δη, Δφ) for pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV with cuts on pseudorapidity 2.0 < |η| < 4.0, trigger transverse momentum p_trig_perp = 1.0–3.0 GeV, away-side transverse momentum p_as_perp = 0.6–1.5 GeV, and multiplicity n_part > 12, then compared the result to CMS experimental data normalized at the away-side peak Δφ = π.

## Key numbers
- The ridge is filled mostly by particles with transverse momenta in the range 1 < p_T < 3 GeV/c, emitted at polar angles |η| < 2.4.
- Longitudinal momenta of these particles are less than 17 GeV, and the share of initial momentum x is less than 5 × 10^{-3}.
- The simulation used 5.5 million events with cuts: 2.0 < |η| < 4.0, p_trig_perp = 1.0–3.0 GeV, p_as_perp = 0.6–1.5 GeV, n_part > 12.
- The experimental CMS data used for comparison had cuts: 2.0 < |η| < 4.8, p_trig_perp = 1.0–3.0 GeV, p_as_perp = 1.0–3.0 GeV, n_part > 110, with 354k events.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of ridge-like angular correlations from heavy ion collisions at RHIC at √s = 200 GeV (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS collaborations) to pp collisions at the LHC, where the CMS collaboration first observed such a structure. The authors contrast their soft string-based mechanism with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach (Dumitru et al., 2010; Kovchegov and Levin, 2010) and glasma flux tube descriptions (Dumitru et al., 2008; Lappi and McLerran, 2010; Dusling et al., 2010), noting that those CGC calculations did not present quantitative comparison with CMS data. The result raises the question of whether the soft ridge from valence strings can be rescaled to the high-multiplicity regime observed experimentally, possibly through enhanced chromoelectric fields in glasma flux tubes or color ropes (Biro et al., 1984; Topor Pop et al., 2010).
