# Cross Section for $γn \to π^0 n$ measured at Mainz/A2

> The $γn \to π^0 n$ differential cross section evaluated for 27 energy bins span the photon-energy range 290-813 MeV (W = 1.195-1.553 GeV) and the pion c.m. polar production angles, ranging from 18 deg to 162 deg, making use of model-dependent nuclear corrections to extract pi0 production data on the neutron from measurements on the deuteron target. Additionally, the total photoabsorption cross section was measured. The tagged photon beam produced by the 883-MeV electron beam of the Mainz Microtron MAMI was used for the 0-meson production. Our accumulation of 3.6 x 10^6 $γn \to π^0 n$ events allowed a detailed study of the reaction dynamics. Our data are in reasonable agreement with previous A2 measurements and extend them to lower energies. The data are compared to predictions of previous SAID, MAID, and BnGa partial-wave analyses and to the latest SAID fit MA19 that included our data. Selected photon decay amplitudes $N^* \to γn$ at the resonance poles are determined for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper presents new, precise differential cross sections for the reaction γn → π⁰n, but these are extracted from measurements on a deuteron target using model-dependent nuclear corrections (final-state interaction corrections), meaning the reported cross sections are not direct free-neutron measurements but rather rely on theoretical input to account for deuteron binding and final-state interactions.

## Method

The A2 Collaboration at MAMI used a tagged bremsstrahlung photon beam from an 883-MeV electron beam incident on a liquid deuterium target, with the Crystal Ball (CB) calorimeter detecting the π⁰ meson (via its two-photon decay) and the recoil neutron. Events from the competing γp → π⁰p reaction were rejected using the particle identification detector to veto charged protons. The GWU-ITEP group applied a model-dependent final-state interaction (FSI) correction factor, calculated using SAID phenomenological amplitudes for πN and NN elastic scattering and the full Bonn potential for the deuteron, to extract the γn → π⁰n cross section from the measured γd → π⁰np data.

## Key numbers

* Differential cross sections were evaluated for 27 energy bins spanning photon energies from 290 to 813 MeV (center-of-mass energy W = 1.195 to 1.553 GeV) and pion center-of-mass polar angles from 18° to 162°.
* A total of 3.6 × 10⁶ γn → π⁰n events were accumulated for the analysis.
* The tagged photon beam was produced by an 883-MeV electron beam from the Mainz Microtron MAMI.
* The total photoabsorption cross section was measured and compared to previous DAPHNE data (MacCormick 1996, 1997), with agreement within 2.5–3.0% in the Δ(1232) region and a difference reaching 6% at 700 MeV.
* The background from multi-pion production reached up to approximately 40% at 800 MeV photon energy and was nearly zero at 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous A2 measurements of π⁰ photoproduction on the neutron (Dieterle 2018, 2017) to lower energies and nearly doubles the world γn → π⁰n database below 2.7 GeV. The data are compared to predictions from previous SAID, MAID, and BnGa partial-wave analyses and to the latest SAID fit MA19 that included these data. The results enable the first determination of selected photon decay amplitudes N* → γn at the resonance poles, which is critical for disentangling the isoscalar and isovector electromagnetic couplings of nucleon resonances and for improving the poorly known neutron couplings compared to the better-determined proton couplings.
