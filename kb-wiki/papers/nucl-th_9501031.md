# The Weak Parity-Violating Pion-Nucleon Coupling

> We use QCD sum rules to obtain the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant $f_{πNN}$. We find that $f_{πNN}\approx 3\times 10^{-8}$, up to an order of magnitude smaller than the ``best estimates" based on quark models. This result follows from the cancellation between perturbative and nonperturbative QCD processes not found in quark models, but explicit in the QCD sum rule method. Our result is consistent with the experimental upper limit found from $^{18}$F parity-violating measurements.

## Key claim

The weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant \(f_{\pi NN}\) is approximately \(3 \times 10^{-8}\), up to an order of magnitude smaller than the "best estimates" based on quark models, due to a cancellation between perturbative and nonperturbative QCD processes that is explicit in the QCD sum rule method but absent in quark models.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules with a two-point function for the nucleon in an external pionic field, keeping terms in the operator product expansion up to dimension 5. They evaluated the correlator for the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon vertex using the standard model weak Hamiltonian, and eliminated the unknown vacuum susceptibility \(\chi_\pi\) by taking derivatives with respect to the Borel mass and taking the ratio of the weak to the strong sum rule. The strong coupling constant \(g_{\pi NN}\) was used as input to determine the susceptibility, and the unknown parameter \(m_0^\pi\) was varied over a range of 0.0 to 0.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

* \(f_{\pi NN} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{-8}\) for \(m_0^\pi = 0\) GeV, the central value quoted by the authors.
* \(f_{\pi NN} \approx (2.3 \text{ to } 5.8) \times 10^{-8}\) for \(m_0^\pi\) in the range 0.0 to 0.8 GeV.
* The first term in the sum rule, corresponding to a quark model type calculation, gives \(f_{\pi NN} \approx 2 \times 10^{-7}\), similar to the quark model value.
* The constant \(c_w = G_F \sin^2\theta_W (11/3 - \gamma)/(12\pi^2) = 6.7 \times 10^{-8} \text{ GeV}^{-2}\) appears in the sum rule for the ratio \(f_{\pi NN}/g_{\pi NN}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of QCD sum rules to predict an unknown hadronic property, the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling, which had previously been estimated using quark models by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and by Dubovik and Zenkin (1986). The result is consistent with the experimental upper limit from \(^{18}\)F parity-violating measurements and agrees with the chiral soliton model of Kaiser and Meissner (1989, 1990), but disagrees with the quark model calculations and a previous QCD sum rule calculation by Khatsimovskii (1985). The small value obtained implies that the neutral current contribution cannot be separated from the charged current contribution experimentally.
