# Nucleon to Pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes in a Light-Cone Quark Model

> We present a general representation for the nucleon distribution amplitudes and for the nucleon to pion transition distribution amplitudes in terms of light-cone wave functions. We apply our formalism to a light-cone constituent quark model giving some numerical results for both the classes of observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general light-cone wave function (LCWF) representation for nucleon distribution amplitudes (DAs) and, for the first time in the literature, for nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), but the TDA results are obtained only by considering a five-parton Fock state (three valence quarks plus a quark-antiquark pair) to describe the meson cloud surrounding the nucleon, and the numerical TDA predictions are preliminary, with full details postponed to a forthcoming paper.

## Method

The authors perform a Fock-state decomposition of the nucleon state in terms of light-cone wave functions, deriving the general LCWF representation for the leading-twist nucleon DAs from the three-quark valence sector. For the nucleon-to-pion TDAs, they extend the formalism to a five-parton configuration using a convolution model where two LCWFs (one for the 3-quark structure and one for the q-qbar pair) are combined with a splitting function that weights different possible configurations of the pion cloud.

## Key numbers

- Moments of the nucleon DA Φ from the light-cone quark model: Φ^(1,0,0) = 0.346, Φ^(0,1,0) = 0.331, Φ^(0,0,1) = 0.323, Φ^(2,0,0) = 0.152, Φ^(0,2,0) = 0.142, Φ^(0,0,2) = 0.137
- The TDA results are shown at Δ² = -1 GeV² and ξ = 0.9 for V₁^{pπ⁰}, A₁^{pπ⁰}, and T₁^{pπ⁰} (numerical values not given in the text)
- The nucleon DA Φ from the model is compared with the data fit of Bolz and Kroll (1999), showing good agreement

## Context

This work extends the LCWF formalism previously applied to form factors (Drell, 1970), transversity distributions, GPDs, and TMDs to the new domain of nucleon-to-pion TDAs, which were initially proposed as "skewed distribution amplitudes" and later established as TDAs by Pire and collaborators (2005). The nucleon-to-pion TDAs are presented as the most direct observables to test the pion-cloud contribution to nucleon structure, with experimental study ongoing at JLab or planned at GSI and CERN. The results for the DAs are compared with several prior model calculations (COZ, KS, DF, SB, BK) and lattice predictions (LAT), with the model showing closest agreement to the BK data parameterization and recent lattice results.
