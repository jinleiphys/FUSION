# Test particle description of transport processes for states with a continuous mass spectrum

> Aiming at a description of transport processes where the dynamically generated width of the states is potentially large a transport equation beyond the quasiparticle approximation is derived in first order gradient expansion. An effective particle number is identified which is exactly conserved by the coarse grained transport equation. Using a test particle ansatz for this conserved quantity allows to rewrite the transport equation into equations of motion for test particles. The two-body collision terms are formulated in terms of the test particles which gain non-trivial renormalization factors due to the coarse graining process.

## Key claim

A transport equation beyond the quasiparticle approximation can be derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equations in first order gradient expansion, but the particle number \(N\) defined for the full quantum field theory is not exactly conserved by this transport equation; instead, an effective particle number \(\tilde N\) is exactly conserved, and a test particle ansatz must be applied to \(\tilde S^<\) (the density corresponding to \(\tilde N\)) rather than to \(S^<\) itself.

## Method

Starting from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for the two-point functions, the author performs a Wigner transformation with respect to the difference variable, expands to first order in gradients of the center-of-mass coordinate, and separates the resulting complex equations into real and imaginary parts. By introducing the spectral function \(\mathcal{A}\) and the width \(\Gamma\), the algebraic equations are solved to obtain \(\mathcal{A}\) and the real part of the retarded propagator in closed form. The remaining equations are then combined into a single transport equation for \(S^<\), the generalized phase space distribution. A test particle ansatz is made for the conserved quantity \(\tilde S^< = \frac{1}{2} \Gamma \mathcal{A} S^< (1-K)\), where \(K\) is a renormalization factor, yielding equations of motion for the test particle coordinates that include off-shellness \(\Delta E_i\). The two-body collision terms (Born terms) are rewritten in terms of \(\tilde S^<\) and the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- The spectral function is given by \(\mathcal{A}(X,p) = \Gamma(X,p) / \left[ \left( p_0 - \frac{\vec p^2}{2m} - \text{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\text{ret}}(X,p) \right)^2 + \frac{1}{4} \Gamma^2(X,p) \right]\).
- The real part of the retarded propagator is \(\text{Re}\bar D^{\text{ret}}(X,p) = \left( p_0 - \frac{\vec p^2}{2m} - \text{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\text{ret}}(X,p) \right) / \left[ \left( p_0 - \frac{\vec p^2}{2m} - \text{Re}\bar\Sigma^{\text{ret}}(X,p) \right)^2 + \frac{1}{4} \Gamma^2(X,p) \right]\).
- The off-shellness evolves as \(\frac{d}{dt} \Delta E_i = \frac{\Delta E_i}{\Gamma} \frac{d}{dt}\Gamma\), ensuring that test particles return to mass shell as \(\Gamma \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the traditional Boltzmann-type transport equation (Equation 1), which assumes on-shell quasiparticles and is commonly solved via a test particle ansatz for the on-shell distribution \(f(t,\vec x,\vec p)\), to systems where the dynamically generated width \(\Gamma\) is large, such as in heavy-ion collisions with broad resonances or collisional broadening. The derivation starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equations, following the standard gradient expansion approach, and identifies a conserved effective particle number \(\tilde N\) that enables a consistent test particle method. The author notes that the test particle ansatz for \(\tilde S^<\) is conceptually distinct from Klimontovich's microscopic phase space density (Klimontovich, 1967), which describes a single microscopic realization rather than the ensemble-averaged distribution. This work enables numerical simulations of transport processes with off-shell effects, where the violation of the exact particle number \(N\) provides a diagnostic for the accuracy of the gradient expansion.
