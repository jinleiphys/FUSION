# A global potential constrained by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition for $α$-decay half-lives of even-even nuclei

> The $α$ decay provides valuable constraints on nuclear structure and plays an essential role in identifying heavy and superheavy nuclei. We study $α$-decay half-lives of 178 even-even nuclei within a semi-classical WKB framework using a phenomenological Woods-Saxon $α$-nucleus potential. The potential depth is determined by imposing the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition (BSQC), ensuring a physically consistent description of the quasibound $α$-daughter system. To facilitate large-scale calculations, a global parametrization of the BSQC-constrained potential depth is constructed. The resulting half-lives reproduce experimental data with comparable accuracy for both the direct BSQC approach and the fitted prescription, providing a first step toward a global and computationally efficient description of $α$ decay.

## Key claim
The authors show that a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential depth constrained by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition (BSQC) can be replaced by a fitted global parametrization that reproduces α-decay half-lives with comparable accuracy (rms deviation χ_Fit = 0.250 vs χ_BSQC = 0.253), providing a computationally efficient alternative for large-scale calculations while maintaining physical consistency at the wave-function level.

## Method
The authors calculated α-decay half-lives for 178 even-even nuclei using the WKB semi-classical approximation with a Woods-Saxon α-nucleus potential. The potential depth V0 was first determined by imposing the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition (BSQC) for each nucleus individually, then a global fitted parametrization of V0 was constructed as a function of mass number A, proton number Z, shell-correction energy E_sh, decay energy Q_α, and global quantum number G. The preformation factor S_α was taken from an existing analytical parametrization by Deng (2021), and the radius and diffuseness parameters were fixed at r0 = 1.27 fm and a0 = 0.64 fm.

## Key numbers
- 178 even-even nuclei were studied, restricted to global quantum numbers G = 18, 20, and 22
- The rms deviation for the BSQC-based calculation was χ_BSQC = 0.253; for the fitted parametrization it was χ_Fit = 0.250
- For G = 22 nuclei (76 nuclei), the fitted parametrization reduced the rms deviation from χ_BSQC = 0.237 to χ_Fit = 0.226
- For G = 18 (65 nuclei): χ_Fit = 0.216, χ_BSQC = 0.218; for G = 20 (37 nuclei): χ_Fit = 0.341, χ_BSQC = 0.339
- The mass range covered was 144 ≤ A ≤ 294
- The radius parameter was R0 = 1.27 × A_d^(1/3) fm and diffuseness a = 0.64 fm

## Context
This work extends the semi-classical WKB approach to α decay using phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials, following the methodology of Chien (2022) who demonstrated that BSQC-constrained microscopic double-folding potentials improve half-life predictions. The fitted parametrization follows the functional form proposed by Tian (2024). The results are comparable to those from semi-microscopic double-folding potentials using the CDM3Y3 interaction (Chien 2022), which gave χ_DF = 0.259 for 50 nuclei. This enables efficient large-scale α-decay calculations without solving the BSQC integrals individually for each nucleus.
