# A reanalysis of Finite Temperature SU(N) Gauge Theory

> We revise the $SU(N_c)$, $N_c=3,4,6$, lattice data on pure gauge theories at finite temperature by means of a quasi-particle approach. In particular we focus on the relation between the quasi-particle effective mass and the order of the deconfinement transition, the scaling of the interaction measure with $N^2_c -1$, the role of gluon condensate, the screening mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a quasi-particle model with a temperature-dependent effective gluon mass can successfully describe the lattice data for the interaction measure and other thermodynamic quantities of pure SU(N) gauge theories, but only if the mass ansatz accounts for the weakly first-order nature of the deconfinement transition via a shifted critical temperature. The abstract's claim of a "revision" glosses over the key condition that the scaling of the interaction measure with \(N_c^2-1\) is violated near \(T_c\) and only holds above the peak temperature.

## Method

The authors fit a quasi-particle partition function with two transverse polarization states and a temperature-dependent effective mass \(m(t)\) (where \(t = T/T_c\)) to lattice data for SU(3), SU(4), and SU(6) pure gauge theories. The mass is parametrized as \(m(t) = a/(t-\delta)^c + b t\), where the shift \(\delta\) accounts for the finite correlation length in weakly first-order transitions, and the parameters \(a, b, c, \delta\) are determined by fitting the computed interaction measure \(\Delta = (\epsilon-3p)/T^4\) and pressure to the lattice results of Boyd et al. and Gupta et al.

## Key numbers

* For SU(3) with \(c=0.5\) fixed, the effective mass at the critical temperature is \(m(T_c)/T_c = 6.33\) (or 6.64 with \(c\) free), with a shift \(\delta = 0.940\) (or 0.952 free), and \(\chi^2_{dof} = 2.6\) (or 2.0 free).
* For SU(4) with \(c=0.5\) fixed, \(m(T_c)/T_c = 6.02\) (or 7.68 free), \(\delta = 0.944\) (or 0.982 free), and \(\chi^2_{dof} = 7.7\) (or 0.8 free).
* For SU(6) with \(c=0.5\) fixed, \(m(T_c)/T_c = 4.33\) (or 5.91 free), \(\delta = 0.888\) (or 0.969 free), and \(\chi^2_{dof} = 6.5\) (or 5.8 free).
* The interaction measure \(\Delta\) scales approximately with \(N_c(N_c^2-1)\) near \(T_c\) and with \((N_c^2-1)\) for temperatures above the peak (above about \(1.1 T_c\)).
* The effective mass \(m(t)\) becomes independent of \(N_c\) for temperatures above the peak in the interaction measure.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach previously applied to SU(3) by Peshier et al., Mannarelli et al., and Redlich et al., and directly confronts the lattice data of Boyd et al. and Gupta et al. for larger \(N_c\). The analysis enables a systematic study of how the deconfinement transition order (weakly first-order for \(N_c \ge 3\)) affects the effective gluon mass, and it sets the stage for extending the same quasi-particle strategy to full QCD with fermions, as noted in the conclusions. The paper also discusses the role of the gluon condensate, arguing against a purely condensate-based explanation for the interaction measure, in contrast to suggestions by Pisarski (2006).
