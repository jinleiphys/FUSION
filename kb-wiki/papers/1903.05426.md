# Non-relativistic conformal symmetry in nuclear structure and relations with the unitarity limit

> The Interacting Boson Model of nuclear structure is introduced to the unitarity limit. Non relativistic conformal symmetry creates a scale invariant state from which the stationary states of atomic nuclei are obtained. A brief discussion of the nature of the conformal field theory is included along with the BCS-BEC crossover and hydrodynamic properties.

# Non-relativistic conformal symmetry in nuclear structure and relations with the unitarity limit

## Key claim
The Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of nuclear structure can be connected to the unitarity limit of fermionic scattering, where the O(6) dynamical symmetry limit of the IBM corresponds to a scale-invariant state of fermions at infinite scattering length. This connection holds specifically for the O(6) quantum number, with the claim that the unitarity limit does not distinguish between light and heavy nuclei due to universality.

## Method
The author compares the Schrödinger equation of the U(6) ⊃ O(6) limit of the IBM with the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for N particles in hyperspherical coordinates confined in a harmonic oscillator trap. By setting the trapping frequency ω=0 and energy E=0 in the hyperspherical equation, the unitarity limit is introduced, and the two equations are shown to coincide for N=2 fermions. The SO(2,1) conformal algebra is then revealed in the IBM by expressing the generators H, K, and D in their Cartesian form using the six-dimensional harmonic oscillator coordinates of the IBM.

## Key numbers
- For N=2 fermions at unitarity, the scaling exponent ν equals σ, where σ(σ+4) are the eigenvalues of the O(6) Casimir operator.
- For N>2 fermions at unitarity, the scaling exponent is ν = (1-3N)/2 + p, where p(p+4) = σ(σ+4) + (3N-1)(3N-3)/4.
- The spectrum of the trapped IBM is E_{σ,J} = (σ + 2J + 6/2) ħω, with J = (N_b - σ)/2 constrained by the total number of bosons N_b.
- The ladder operators L_+ and L_- create states separated by 2ħω in the trapped system.

## Context
This work extends the application of the unitarity limit, previously used in light nuclei within the No Core Shell Model framework (Barrett), to the collective states of the Interacting Boson Model. It connects with the E(5) symmetry that emerges at the critical point of a second-order quantum phase transition between the U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetry limits of the IBM (E(5) symmetry). The paper enables a new interpretation of nuclear collective stationary states as trapped states of a scattering problem at unitarity, and opens connections to non-relativistic conformal field theory (Son), the BCS-BEC crossover, and hydrodynamic properties of nuclear matter.
