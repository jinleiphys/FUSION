# Photon-Photon Luminosities in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC Energies

> Effective photon-photon luminosities are calculated for various realistic hadron collider scenarios. The main characteristics of photon-photon processes at relativistic heavy-ion colliders are established and compared to the corresponding photon-photon luminosities at electron-positron and future Photon Linear Colliders (PLC). Higher order corrections as well as inelastic processes are discussed. It is concluded that feasible high luminosity Ca-Ca collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are an interesting option for photon-photon physics up to about 100 GeV photon-photon CM energy.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that feasible high-luminosity Ca-Ca collisions at the LHC are an interesting option for photon-photon physics up to about 100 GeV photon-photon center-of-mass energy, but note that for very high invariant masses (above about 200 GeV) the proton-proton option becomes better due to the harder form factor of the proton compared to the softer nuclear form factor.

## Method

The authors compute effective photon-photon luminosities using the semiclassical impact-parameter-dependent equivalent photon approximation, with a cutoff at impact parameter b = 2R to exclude central collisions where strong interactions occur. They compare unrestricted and restricted (in rapidity Y) luminosities for Ca-Ca and Pb-Pb beams at the LHC against those for proton-proton, electron-positron, and future Photon Linear Collider scenarios, using beam luminosities from Eggert and Morsch (1994). They also evaluate higher-order electromagnetic processes and inelastic photon emission, particularly giant dipole resonance excitation, using the double equivalent photon approximation generalized to inelastic processes.

## Key numbers

- Ca-Ca collisions at LHC can reach photon-photon center-of-mass energies up to about 100 GeV
- The giant dipole resonance excitation energy is given by E_GDR = 80 MeV / A^(1/3)
- At impact parameter b = 2R, the GDR excitation probability is about 40% for Pb and about 1% for Ca
- The total probability for excitation of at least one nucleus is about 2% for Ca and about 65% for Pb
- Elastic-inelastic processes contribute about 1% to the total luminosity
- For very high invariant masses (W greater than about 200 GeV), the proton-proton option becomes better than heavy-ion options

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Baron and Baur (1994) by comparing unrestricted and restricted photon-photon luminosities for heavy-ion beams at the LHC with those at electron-positron colliders and future Photon Linear Colliders. The authors also investigate higher-order corrections and inelastic processes, finding that for high-Z systems like Pb-Pb most photon-photon collisions are accompanied by nuclear excitation (predominantly GDR), while for lighter systems like Ca-Ca such effects are negligible. The results enable experimental planning for photon-photon physics at the LHC, particularly for the ALICE detector, and suggest that muon pair production could serve as a luminosity monitor for both proton-proton and heavy-ion colliders.
