# Phase Diagram of Cold Polarized Fermi Gas in Two Dimensions

> The superfluid phase diagrams of a two-dimensional cold polarized Fermi gas in the BCS-BEC crossover are systematically and analytically investigated. In the BCS-Leggett mean field theory, the transition from unpolarized superfluid phase to normal phase is always of first order. For a homogeneous system, the two critical Zeeman fields and the critical population imbalance are analytically determined in the whole coupling parameter region, and the superfluid-normal mixed phase is shown to be the ground state between the two critical fields. The density profile in the presence of a harmonic trap calculated in the local density approximation exhibits a shell structure, a superfluid core at the center and a normal shell outside. For weak interaction, the normal shell contains a partially polarized cloud with constant density difference surrounded by a fully polarized state. For strong interaction, the normal shell is totally in fully polarized state with a density profile depending only on the global population imbalance. The di-fermion bound states can survive in the whole highly imbalanced normal phase.

# Phase Diagram of Cold Polarized Fermi Gas in Two Dimensions

## Key claim

In two dimensions, the BCS-Leggett mean field theory predicts that the transition from unpolarized superfluid to normal phase is always first order, and the Sarma (polarized superfluid) phase is always unstable for any coupling strength, contrary to the three-dimensional case where it becomes stable in the strong-coupling BEC region.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the BCS-Leggett mean field equations for a two-dimensional homogeneous Fermi gas with s-wave attraction, using an effective 2D Hamiltonian with a renormalized interaction characterized by a two-body binding energy. They determined the equation of state for the unpolarized superfluid (SF), polarized normal (N), and Sarma phases, then constructed the phase diagram by comparing free energies and applying Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions to identify the SF-N mixed phase region. For trapped systems, they used the local density approximation to calculate density profiles.

## Key numbers

- Critical Zeeman field for weak coupling: \(h_c = \Delta_0 / \sqrt{2}\) for \(\eta < 1\), where \(\eta = \epsilon_{\text B} / \epsilon_{\text F}\)
- Critical Zeeman field for strong coupling: \(h_c = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \eta)\epsilon_{\text F}\) for \(\eta > 1\)
- Lower critical field at strong coupling: \(h_{c1} = \epsilon_{\text F}(\sqrt{2} - 1 + \eta/2)\) for \(\eta > \eta_1\) with \(\eta_1 = 2 - \sqrt{2} \simeq 0.586\)
- Upper critical field at strong coupling: \(h_{c2} = \epsilon_{\text F}(2 - \sqrt{2} + \eta/2)\) for \(\eta > \eta_2\) with \(\eta_2 = 2(\sqrt{2} - 1) \simeq 0.828\)
- Splitting between critical fields at strong coupling: constant \((3 - 2\sqrt{2})\epsilon_{\text F} \simeq 0.172\epsilon_{\text F}\) for \(\eta > \eta_2\)
- Critical polarization: \(P_c = \sqrt{\eta(1 + \eta/4)}\) for \(\eta < \eta_2\), and \(P_c = 1\) for \(\eta > \eta_2\)
- Chemical potential in balanced case: \(\mu_0 = \epsilon_{\text F} - \epsilon_{\text B}/2\)
- Gap in balanced case: \(\Delta_0 = \sqrt{2\epsilon_{\text B}\epsilon_{\text F}}\)

## Context

This work extends the mean-field phase diagram studies of polarized Fermi gases from three dimensions (Sheehy 2006) to two dimensions, where the equations become analytically solvable across the entire BCS-BEC crossover. It contrasts with the three-dimensional case where the Sarma phase can be stable in the BEC region (Sheehy 2006, Parish 2007). The analytical results enable direct comparison with quasi-2D ultracold Fermi gas experiments (2Dexp, Zhang 2007) and provide a benchmark for future quantum Monte Carlo calculations in two dimensions. The finding that di-fermion bound states survive in the highly imbalanced normal phase suggests that pairing correlations persist even above the critical polarization.
