# A Deep Learning Based Estimator for Light Flavour Elliptic Flow in Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC Energies

> We developed a deep learning feed-forward network for estimating elliptic flow ($v_2$) coefficients in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC energies. The success of our model is mainly the estimation of $v_2$ from final state particle kinematic information and learning the centrality and the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) dependence of $v_2$ in wide $p_{\rm T}$ regime. The deep learning model is trained with AMPT-generated Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV minimum bias events. We present $v_2$ estimates for $π^{\pm}$, $\rm K^{\pm}$, and $\rm p+\bar{p}$ in heavy-ion collisions at various LHC energies. These results are compared with the available experimental data wherever possible.

# A Deep Learning Based Estimator for Light Flavour Elliptic Flow in Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC Energies

## Key claim

The deep learning feed-forward network can estimate elliptic flow ($v_2$) coefficients for identified light-flavor hadrons from final-state particle kinematics, learning centrality and transverse momentum dependence across a wide $p_{\rm T}$ regime; however, the DNN predictions agree well with AMPT simulations only up to $p_{\rm T} \lesssim 4.0-6.0$ GeV/c, beyond which limited training statistics cause the DNN to deviate from the AMPT values.

## Method

The authors trained a deep neural network (DNN) with one input layer, four hidden layers (128, 256, 256, 256 nodes), and one output layer using AMPT-generated minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV. Inputs were "pictures" of normalized transverse momenta, mass, and energy values on the pseudorapidity-azimuthal plane (3072 features), mapped through dense layers with ReLU activation and a linear output node using the Adam optimizer with mean squared error loss. The elliptic flow was calculated event-by-event using the event plane method with the reaction plane angle fixed to $\psi_{\rm R} = 0$, giving $v_2 = \langle\cos(2\phi)\rangle$.

## Key numbers

- Training data: AMPT-generated Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV minimum bias events with all charged particles having $p_{\rm T} > 0.15$ GeV/c in pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 0.8$
- For comparison with ALICE data, tracks were selected with $p_{\rm T} > 0.5$ GeV/c and midrapidity $|y| < 0.5$
- The DNN predictions agree with AMPT values up to $p_{\rm T} \lesssim 4.0-6.0$ GeV/c; beyond $p_{\rm T} \gtrsim 6.0$ GeV/c, mismatch appears due to limited statistics
- The magnitude of $v_2(p_{\rm T})$ for all particle species reaches a maximum around $p_{\rm T} \approx 2.0$ GeV/c, then decreases
- AMPT underestimates ALICE data above $p_{\rm T} \approx 2$ GeV/c, and the DNN follows this same trend
- Results were presented for three collision systems: Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV, and Xe-Xe at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.44$ TeV, in three centrality bins (0-10%, 40-50%, and 60-70%)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DNN-based machine learning approach for evaluating $v_2$ event-by-event for identified hadrons (Mallick 2022, Mallick 2023) to comparisons with experimental ALICE data (ALICE 2018). The DNN estimator correlates well with AMPT simulations up to $p_{\rm T} \lesssim 3$ GeV/c, where training statistics are sufficient, and reproduces the AMPT trend of underestimating data above $p_{\rm T} \approx 2$ GeV/c. The results suggest that training the DNN on original experimental data or Monte Carlo simulations with better high-$p_{\rm T}$ agreement with data could yield more accurate predictions for elliptic flow across different collision systems and energies.
