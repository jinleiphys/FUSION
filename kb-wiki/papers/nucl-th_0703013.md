# Exotic fission properties of highly neutron-rich Uranium isotopes

> The series of Uranium isotopes with $N=154 \sim 172$ around the magic number N=162/164 are identified to be thermally fissile. The thermal neutron fission of a typical representative $^{249}$U of this region amenable to synthesis in the radioactive ion beam facilities is considered here. Semiempirical study of fission barrier height and width shows this nucleus to be infinitely stable against spontaneous fission due to increase in barrier width arising out of excess neutrons. Calculation of probability of fragment mass yields and microscopic study in relativistic mean field theory, show this nucleus to undergo a new mode of thermal fission decay termed {\it multifragmentation fission} where a number of prompt scission neutrons are simultaneously released along with the two heavy fission fragments.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a series of highly neutron-rich Uranium isotopes (N=154–172) as thermally fissile, and predicts that a representative nucleus, \(^{249}\)U (which becomes \(^{250}\)U after neutron capture), will exhibit a new fission mode called "multifragmentation fission" where 2–3 prompt scission neutrons are emitted simultaneously with the two heavy fragments, in addition to the normal neutron multiplicity. This claim is hedged by the reliance on semiempirical extrapolations of fission barrier widths from known actinides and on mass models (Howard-Möller, FRDM, INM, RMF) that may have uncertainties for such neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the Howard-Möller (HM) fission barrier systematics and three mass models (HM, FRDM, and infinite nuclear matter) to identify Uranium isotopes with neutron separation energy \(S_n\) exceeding the fission barrier \(B_f\), the condition for thermal neutron fission. For \(^{250}\)U, they extracted the barrier curvature energy \(\hbar\omega = 0.225\) MeV from a linear fit of \(\hbar\omega\) versus \(B_f\) for known actinides, then constructed a parabolic barrier to compute the spontaneous fission half-life. Fragment mass yields were estimated from \(Q\)-value distributions using binding energies from RMF, FRDM, and INM mass models, and the neck composition at scission was studied via relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with the NL3 interaction for increasing quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\).

## Key numbers

- The chain of Uranium isotopes with neutron numbers N = 154 to 172 are identified as thermally fissile.
- For \(^{250}\)U, the fission barrier height is \(B_f = 4.3\) MeV (from Howard-Möller) and the barrier curvature energy is \(\hbar\omega = 0.225\) MeV.
- The spontaneous fission half-life of \(^{250}\)U is calculated to be \(5.7 \times 10^{24}\) years.
- For comparison, \(^{236}\)U has \(\hbar\omega = 0.357\) MeV and \(B_f = 5.74\) MeV.
- Normal actinides like \(^{230,232}\)Th or \(^{235,238}\)U have \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.4\)–\(0.5\) MeV.
- The neck density at scission for \(^{250}\)U is \(\rho = 0.02383\) fm\(^{-3}\) with neutron-to-proton density ratio \(\rho_n/\rho_p = 3.54703\); for \(^{236}\)U the neck density is \(\rho = 0.02440\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(\rho_n/\rho_p = 2.73089\).
- The estimated number of prompt multifragmentation neutrons is 2 to 3 per fission, in addition to the normal multiplicity of about 2.5 for \(^{233}\)U and \(^{235}\)U.

## Context

This work extends the study of thermal fission to highly neutron-rich actinides beyond the known thermally fissile isotopes \(^{233}\)U, \(^{235}\)U, and \(^{239}\)Pu, motivated by upcoming radioactive ion beam facilities. It builds on the fission barrier systematics of Howard and Möller (1980) and the finite-range droplet model calculations of Möller et al. (1995), and uses mass predictions from FRDM, INM, and RMF models. The predicted multifragmentation fission mode, if confirmed, would have implications for the \(r\)-process nucleosynthesis in stellar evolution and potentially for energy production, and the authors note that the phenomenon should be more prominent in heavier Uranium isotopes.
