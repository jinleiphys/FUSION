# Energies and widths of Efimov states in the three-boson continuum

> Three-boson Efimov physics is well known in the bound-state regime, but far less in the three-particle continuum at negative two-particle scattering length where Efimov states evolve into resonances. They are studied solving rigorous three-particle scattering equations for transition operators in the momentum space. The dependence of the three-boson resonance energy and width on the two-boson scattering length is studied with several force models. The universal limit is determined numerically considering highly excited states; simple parametrizations for the resonance energy and width in terms of the scattering length are established. Decreasing the attraction, the resonances rise not much above the threshold but broaden rapidly and become physically unobservable, evolving into subthreshold resonances. Finite-range effects are studied and related to those in the bound-state regime.

## Key claim

The paper establishes universal relations for the energy and width of three-boson Efimov resonances in the continuum regime of negative two-boson scattering length, but with the important qualifier that these resonances rise only slightly above the threshold and broaden rapidly, becoming physically unobservable when the width exceeds the energy by a factor of 4 or more.

## Method

The author solved rigorous Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three-body transition operators in momentum-space partial wave representation, using several S-wave two-boson interaction models with no bound dimers. The resonance position and width were extracted by fitting the energy dependence of on-shell and off-shell transition matrix elements to a pole expansion near the complex energy pole. Highly excited Efimov states (up to n=3) were used to approach the universal limit where finite-range effects become negligible.

## Key numbers

- The universal product of binding momentum and threshold scattering length is \(k_n^u |a_n^-| = -1.50763\) (analytical result from Ref. PhysRevLett.100.140404) and \(-1.5077(1)\) (numerical from Deltuva (2012))
- The ratio \(r_s/|a_n^-|\) for the potential with \(\beta=0\) is 0.372, 0.0187, \(8.45\times10^{-4}\), and \(3.73\times10^{-5}\) for n=0, 1, 2, and 3 respectively
- The product \(k_n^u a_n^-\) is \(-2.0629\), \(-1.5642\), \(-1.5116\), and \(-1.5079\) for n=0, 1, 2, and 3 respectively
- For n=3 (universal limit), the LO fit parameters are \(c_1^\varepsilon = 0.8582 \pm 0.0042\), \(c_2^\varepsilon = 0.3911 \pm 0.0079\), and \(c_1^\gamma = 0.9766 \pm 0.0057\)
- The ratio \(\sqrt{B_2^{(1)}/B_2^{(2)}} = 21.74\) and \(\sqrt{B_2^{(2)}/B_2^{(3)}} = 22.63\) in this work, approaching the universal limit of 22.694

## Context

This work extends the study of Efimov physics from the well-known bound-state regime into the three-particle continuum, where trimers become resonant states. It improves upon the earlier study by Bringas et al. (2004), which did not reach the strictly universal regime, and provides systematic quantification of finite-range effects that were previously missing. The results enable future studies of four-particle Efimov resonances, which present a more challenging problem due to finite width at threshold from lower trimers.
