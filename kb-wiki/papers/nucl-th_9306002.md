# Hamiltonian Dynamics of Yang-Mills Fields on a Lattice

> We review recent results from studies of the dynamics of classical Yang-Mills fields on a lattice. We discuss the numerical techniques employed in solving the classical lattice Yang-Mills equations in real time, and present results exhibiting the universal chaotic behavior of nonabelian gauge theories. The complete spectrum of Lyapunov exponents is determined for the gauge group SU(2). We survey results obtained for the SU(3) gauge theory and other nonlinear field theories. We also discuss the relevance of these results to the problem of thermalization in gauge theories.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that classical Yang-Mills fields on a lattice exhibit universal chaotic behavior, with the maximal Lyapunov exponent scaling approximately linearly with energy density. The authors hedge that this classical analysis is valid primarily for long-wavelength modes at high temperature, where quantum corrections are small, and caution that the classical limit may break down for classically chaotic systems due to wave packet delocalization.

## Method
The authors discretize SU(2) gauge fields on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using quaternion representation for link variables and fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration for the equations of motion. They study the exponential divergence of initially neighboring field configurations by computing a gauge-invariant distance based on plaquette traces, and determine the full Lyapunov spectrum using a rescaling method with orthogonalization of separation vectors.

## Key numbers
- Maximal Lyapunov exponent scales as λ₀ a ≈ (1/6) g² E a, where a is lattice spacing, g is coupling constant, and E is average energy per plaquette.
- For a 20³ lattice with energy per plaquette of order unity, the distance D(t) between trajectories grows exponentially as D(t) = D₀ exp(h t) almost immediately.
- Total energy conserved to better than 8 significant digits with time step of 0.01 dimensionless units; quaternion length conserved to better than 12 significant figures per unit time.
- Single time-step integration for N=10 lattice takes about 30 ms CPU time for SU(2) and 220 ms for SU(3) on a Cray-Y-MP processor.
- For SU(3), determinant of unitary link matrices deviates from unity by order 10⁻⁸ over total time interval T=20 to 30.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of chaotic dynamics in spatially constant Yang-Mills potentials by Matinyan, Savvidy, and others (1981) and by Luescher (1983) to the full (3+1)-dimensional classical field theory on a lattice. It provides a non-perturbative method for calculating quantities like the gluon damping rate and thermalization time, which are relevant to understanding equilibration in the early universe and in relativistic nuclear collisions. The results enable estimation of thermalization times from the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy, supporting the view that thermalization of long-wavelength modes is essentially a classical process.
