# Anomaly constraint on massless QCD and the role of Skyrmions in chiral symmetry breaking

> We discuss consequences of the 't Hooft anomaly matching condition for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) with massless fundamental quarks. We derive the new discrete 't Hooft anomaly of massless QCD for generic numbers of color $N_\mathrm{c}$ and flavor $N_\mathrm{f}$, and an exotic chiral-symmetry broken phase without quark-bilinear condensate is ruled out from possible QCD vacua. We show that the $U(1)_\mathrm{B}$ baryon number symmetry is anomalously broken when the $(\mathbb{Z}_{2N_\mathrm{f}})_\mathrm{A}$ discrete axial symmetry and the flavor symmetry are gauged. In the ordinary chiral symmetry breaking, the Skyrmion current turns out to reproduce this 't Hooft anomaly of massless QCD. In the exotic chiral symmetry breaking, however, the anomalous breaking of $U(1)_\mathrm{B}$ does not take the correct form, and it is inconsistent with anomaly matching. This no-go theorem is based only on symmetries and anomalies, and thus has a wider range of applicability to the QCD phase diagram than the previous one obtained by QCD inequalities. Lastly, as another application, we check that duality of $\mathcal{N}=1$ supersymmetric QCD with $N_\mathrm{f}\ge N_\mathrm{c}+1$ satisfies the new anomaly matching.

## Key claim

Massless QCD with generic numbers of colors \(N_\mathrm{c}\) and flavors \(N_\mathrm{f}\) possesses a new discrete 't Hooft anomaly involving the discrete chiral symmetry \((\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{f}})_\mathrm{L}\), the baryon-number symmetry \(U(1)_\mathrm{B}\), and the projective nature of the vector-like flavor symmetry; this anomaly rules out an exotic chiral-symmetry broken phase without quark-bilinear condensate from possible QCD vacua, based only on symmetries and anomalies rather than QCD inequalities.

## Method

The author introduces background gauge fields for the subgroup \(G^{\mathrm{sub}} = [SU(N_\mathrm{f})_\mathrm{V} \times U(1)_\mathrm{V}] / [\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{c}} \times (\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{f}})_\mathrm{V}] \times (\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{f}})_\mathrm{L}\) of the full symmetry group of massless QCD, including one-form and two-form gauge fields. Using the Stora-Zumino descent procedure, the gauge invariance of the partition function is examined, and the 't Hooft anomaly is characterized by a five-dimensional topological action involving the discrete axial gauge field, the baryon-number gauge field, and the flavor two-form gauge field. The anomaly matching condition is then applied to both the ordinary chiral symmetry breaking scenario (with quark-bilinear condensate) and the exotic scenario (without quark-bilinear condensate), checking whether the low-energy effective theories reproduce the same anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The discrete axial symmetry surviving the \(U(1)_\mathrm{A}\) anomaly is \((\mathbb{Z}_{2N_\mathrm{f}})_\mathrm{A}\), with the generator quantized to \(\alpha_\mathrm{A} = 2\pi/(2N_\mathrm{f})\).
- The topological charge of the \(SU(N_\mathrm{c})\) gauge field becomes fractional in the presence of the \(\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{c}}\) two-form gauge field \(B^{(2)}_\mathrm{c}\), taking values in \((1/N_\mathrm{c})\mathbb{Z}\).
- The baryon-number gauge field is identified as \(A_\mathrm{B} = N_\mathrm{c} A_\mathrm{V} + B^{(1)}_\mathrm{c}\), with its flux quantized as \((1/2\pi)\int \mathrm{d} A_\mathrm{B} \in \mathbb{Z}\).
- The target space for the ordinary chiral symmetry breaking is \(G/H \simeq SU(N_\mathrm{f})\), with \(\pi_3(G/H) = \mathbb{Z}\) corresponding to the baryon number.
- The target space for the exotic chiral symmetry breaking is \(G/G^{\mathrm{sub}} \simeq PSU(N_\mathrm{f}) = SU(N_\mathrm{f})/\mathbb{Z}_{N_\mathrm{f}}\).

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft anomaly matching approach to massless QCD by deriving a new discrete anomaly that applies for generic \(N_\mathrm{c}\) and \(N_\mathrm{f}\), removing the previous constraint \(\mathrm{gcd}(N_\mathrm{c},N_\mathrm{f}) > 1\) required in studies by Tanizaki (2017), Shimizu (2017), Gaiotto (2017), and Cherman (2017). The result rules out the exotic chiral symmetry breaking phase proposed by Stern (1997, 1998) and Kogan (1998) without relying on QCD inequalities, thus applying to finite-density zero-temperature QCD where sign problems prevent the use of such inequalities. The anomaly matching is also checked against Seiberg duality for \(\mathcal{N}=1\) supersymmetric QCD with \(N_\mathrm{f} \ge N_\mathrm{c}+1\), providing a consistency check for the new anomaly.
