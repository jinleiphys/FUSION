# Holographic equation of state matched with hadron gas equation as a tool for the study of the quark-gluon plasma evolution

> In this paper, we discuss the matching of the holographic equation of state with the equation of Hadron Resonance Gas for studying the nuclear matter properties within the framework of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Machine learning methods are applied to the calibration of model's free parameters using the lattice QCD results for the physical values of quark masses. One of the most advanced procedures for matching is used with the function that approximate behavior of both models on particular limit adopted from NEOS equation. Final hadronic spectra are obtained within multi-staged numerical approach of the iEBE-MUSIC and SMASH-vHLLE packages. The code of relativistic hydrodynamics is modified by implementing a tabulated holographic equation of state, enabling simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution with dynamically generated initial conditions via the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber Model and SMASH. Hybrid iSS+UrQMD and Hadron Sampler+SMASH approaches are utilized at the freeze-out stage.

# Holographic equation of state matched with hadron gas equation as a tool for the study of the quark-gluon plasma evolution

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that matching a holographic equation of state (EoS) with a hadron resonance gas EoS using a switching function adapted from the NEOS approach enables consistent multi-stage hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution, but they explicitly state that at this stage they do not assume significant agreement of their predictions with experimental results, as full calibration of initial conditions and viscosity parameters remains for later studies.

## Method
The authors calibrate a soft-wall bottom-up holographic model (with isotropic and anisotropic metric parameters) to lattice QCD results for physical quark masses using machine learning, then match the resulting EoS to a hadron gas EoS from the Thermal-FIST package via a tanh switching function dependent on temperature and baryon chemical potential. The matched EoS is implemented as a tabulated input into the relativistic hydrodynamic codes MUSIC and VHLLE, with initial conditions generated by the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber model and SMASH, and final hadron spectra obtained using hybrid iSS+UrQMD and Hadron Sampler+SMASH approaches.

## Key numbers
- The matching temperature function uses a width parameter Delta T_c = 0.1 T_c(0), where T_c is obtained from chemical freeze-out studies.
- The anisotropic model uses parameter nu = 4.5, which was shown in prior work (2016) to best reproduce the dependence of charged hadron multiplicity density on collision energy.
- The matching procedure is applied for temperatures below 180 MeV, which roughly corresponds to thermodynamics after the phase transition.
- Final hadron spectra are computed for the kinematic region of the NA49 experiment at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 8.9 GeV for the most central collisions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Anufriev and Kovalenko, 2025) by implementing a more advanced matching procedure adopted from the NEOS equation (2019) to ensure conservation laws in hydrodynamics, addressing problems noted in earlier matching attempts (2010). The approach enables the use of holographic EoS in standard multi-stage simulation packages (iEBE-MUSIC, SMASH-vHLLE) that previously relied on lattice-based EoS, opening the possibility to study the QCD phase diagram at non-zero baryon chemical potential where lattice QCD faces the sign problem. The results are compared against the NEOS reference EoS and the AZ Hydro reference (2012) for validation.
