# Suppression of elliptic anisotropy inside jets: A new perspective for jet quenching

> Particle azimuthal anisotropies inside jets, defined within the momentum plane perpendicular to the jet axis, carry the information of the QCD cascade process for jet formation. In this work, we propose to measure the medium-induced modifications of the elliptic anisotropy inside jets in relativistic heavy-ion collisions to provide novel insight into the jet quenching phenomenon. By simulating the jet propagation in the hot and dense nuclear medium with a Linear Boltzmann Transport model, we observe a de-correlation in the two-particle azimuthal angular distribution for inclusive jet production in AA collisions relative to that in pp collisions, which results in significant suppression of the in-jet elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v_2$. This phenomenon arises from the stochastic and strong interactions with the thermal QGP medium undergone by the jet particles. Furthermore, the nuclear modifications of the in-jet $v_2$ are found to be sensitive to the medium properties in the model study, which provide a potential probe for the jet tomography of nuclear matter.

# Suppression of elliptic anisotropy inside jets: A new perspective for jet quenching

## Key claim
The authors propose that measuring the medium-induced suppression of the elliptic anisotropy coefficient v2 inside jets in heavy-ion collisions can provide a novel probe of jet quenching, but the claim is conditional on the Linear Boltzmann Transport model's ability to capture the relevant physics: the suppression arises from stochastic interactions with the quark-gluon plasma that de-correlate the two-particle azimuthal angular distribution, and the effect is sensitive to medium parameters such as temperature, propagation time, and coupling strength.

## Method
The authors generate inclusive jet events in proton-proton collisions at 5.02 TeV using PYTHIA8 with the CP5 tune, then simulate jet propagation through a hot QGP medium using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic scatterings and medium-induced radiative processes for jet shower partons and recoiled medium partons. For realistic heavy-ion collisions, the evolving QGP background is provided by a 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamic calculation with initial conditions from the AMPT model, with the effective strong coupling set to alpha_s = 0.15. The elliptic anisotropy coefficient v2 is computed using the Q-cumulant method from two-particle correlations with a pseudorapidity gap |Delta eta*| > 2.0.

## Key numbers
- In pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, the PYTHIA8 model reproduces CMS data for v2 as a function of charged-particle multiplicity inside jets for N^j_ch < 80, where a downtrend is observed; for N^j_ch > 80 the data show an uptrend not captured by the model.
- For inclusive jets with pT^jet > 100 GeV in a static medium at T = 0.4 GeV with propagation time 1.0 fm/c and alpha_s = 0.15 including both elastic and inelastic scatterings, the v2 value is 0.126 compared to 0.169 for pp (no medium).
- Increasing the static medium temperature to T = 0.8 GeV (same propagation time 1.0 fm/c, alpha_s = 0.15) reduces v2 to 0.096.
- Increasing the propagation time to 5.0 fm/c (T = 0.4 GeV, alpha_s = 0.15) reduces v2 to 0.039.
- Increasing the coupling to alpha_s = 0.30 (T = 0.4 GeV, propagation time 1.0 fm/c) reduces v2 to 0.092.
- With elastic scatterings only (T = 0.4 GeV, propagation time 1.0 fm/c, alpha_s = 0.15), v2 is 0.147.
- For partonic jets with pT^parton > 5 GeV in the static medium setting A, v2 is 0.259 compared to 0.316 for pp.
- The elliptic anisotropy evaluated from the jet-plane method gives v2 = 0.33 for pp and v2 = 0.16 for central PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of two-particle azimuthal correlations inside jets, previously measured by CMS (2023) and modeled by Zhao et al. (2024) for pp collisions, into the heavy-ion collision regime where jet quenching occurs. The authors use the LBT model, which has been applied by Cao et al. (2017) and He et al. (2018) to describe various jet quenching observables, and the CLVisc hydrodynamic model by Pang et al. (2012, 2014). The results suggest that in-jet v2 suppression could serve as a new tomographic probe of medium properties, complementing existing jet substructure observables such as jet shapes and fragmentation functions studied by CMS (2013), Vitev (2008), and Kang et al. (2017).
