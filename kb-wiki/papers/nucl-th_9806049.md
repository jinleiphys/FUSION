# Pion Interferometry Using Wavepackets

> The conditions for the HBT interferometry from stars are compared to those encountered in heavy ion reactions. As a consequence the formalism using wavepackets is developed. The modifications relative to the standard formalism with plane waves are presented, and the effects of the residual Coulomb interactions between charged pions and with the charged source are calculated.

# Pion Interferometry Using Wavepackets

## Key claim

The standard plane-wave formalism for HBT interferometry is inadequate for pions emitted in heavy-ion reactions because the source size, coherence length, and de Broglie wavelength are all comparable (R ≈ Λ ≈ λ), unlike the stellar case where R ≫ Λ ≫ λ; a wavepacket formalism is therefore required, and the residual Coulomb interactions between charged pions and with the charged source produce modifications that are generally small at SIS energies but become significant when a dimensionless parameter ξ reaches order unity.

## Method

The authors develop a Gaussian wavepacket formalism for pions, parametrizing the source by a spatial Gaussian of radius R_s and a momentum distribution with temperature T, and introducing a localization parameter σ_0 related to the pion coherence length. They calculate single-pion observables and the two-pion correlation function C_2, obtaining an effective source radius R_eff and a chaoticity parameter λ. The Coulomb interactions are treated using two time-dependent Schrödinger equation methods: a multiconfigurational method and a molecular dynamics method, applied to both pion-source and pion-pion Coulomb forces.

## Key numbers

- Source size for a star: R ≈ 10^9 m; for a nucleus: R ≈ 10^{-14} m
- Correlation area for a star: ΔΩ ≈ 3 × 10^{-32} sr; for a nucleus: ΔΩ ≈ 3 × 10^{-2} sr
- Coherence length for pions: Λ ≈ λ²/Δλ ≈ 10^{-14} m
- Wavepacket localization: σ_0 ≈ Λ / 4π ≈ 1 fm
- Under SIS conditions, σ_0 is bounded by 0.8 fm < σ_0 < 4 fm
- Adopted value for calculations: σ_0 = 1.8 fm, giving quantum temperature contribution T_qm = 21 MeV
- Source temperature used in calculations: T = 50 MeV
- Source radius used in Coulomb calculations: R_s = 5.5 fm
- Escape radius for Coulomb calculations: R_esc = 20 fm
- For σ_0 = 1.8 fm and Z = 1: ξ = 1.4 × 10^{-2}
- For hypothetical Z = 20: ξ = 5.6
- Like-charge pion multiplicity at SIS energies: m_π ≈ 20

## Context

This work extends the HBT interferometry formalism originally developed for astronomy by Hanbury Brown and Twiss (1957) and applied to pions by Goldhaber et al. (1960). The authors compare stellar and nuclear conditions, showing that plane-wave descriptions fail for nuclear sources where R ≈ Λ ≈ λ. The wavepacket approach builds on prior work by Merlitz (1995, 1997) and others (Wiechula 1997, 1998; Zimanyi 1997; Csorgo 1997), and addresses the unresolved issue of Coulomb distortions in charged-pion interferometry. The results enable future experimental exploration of the dependence of HBT signals on pion localization and source temperature, particularly at higher energies where long-lived resonance decays produce pions with larger coherence lengths.
