# Collisional energy loss of heavy quarks

> We develop a transport approach for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma, which is based on improved binary collision rates taking into account quantum statistics, the running of the QCD coupling and an effective screening mass adjusted to hard-thermal loop calculations. We quantify the effects of in-medium collisions by calculating the heavy flavor nuclear modification factor and the elliptic flow for RHIC energies, which are comparable to radiative effects. We also derive an analytic formula for the mean collisional energy loss of an energetic heavy quark in a streaming quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper develops a transport approach for heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma that predicts collisional energy loss comparable to radiative effects, but the authors hedge that this conclusion depends on using an improved infrared regulator (with a specific small prefactor kappa = 0.184) and running coupling, rather than the naive Debye mass screening used in prior work.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark collisional energy loss using a transition matrix approach based on QCD Born cross sections with an effective screening mass that depends on momentum transfer via the running coupling, adjusted to match hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations. They implement a Monte Carlo simulation treating heavy quark interactions as a Markov chain, using Juttner distribution functions to account for QGP flow, and calculate the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 for charm and bottom quarks at RHIC energies, with hadronization via the Peterson fragmentation function and subsequent decays to electrons via PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass M_c ~ 1.3 GeV, bottom quark mass M_b ~ 4.6 GeV
- Effective screening mass prefactor kappa = 1/(2e) ~ 0.184, determined to match the NLL analytic formula
- For the nuclear modification factor, scaling the elastic cross section by K ~ 2-3 reproduces experimental data; for elliptic flow, K^{v2} ~ 3 is needed
- Critical temperature T_c = 200 MeV used for hadronization
- Initial thermalization times tau_0 = 0.6 to 1.0 fm/c tested, with R_AA found insensitive to this range
- For the Bjorken model, two parameter sets used: set-1 with T_0 = 0.42 GeV, tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, R = 5.0 fm; set-2 with T_0 = 0.30 GeV, tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c, R = 6.6 fm
- The analytic NLL formula for mean energy loss in a static medium includes a constant c(n_f=3) ~ 0.146*3 + 0.050 ~ 0.488

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark collisional energy loss by Braaten and Thoma (1991) and Peigne and Peshier (2007-2008) by incorporating running coupling, quantum statistics, and an improved infrared regulator, and generalizing to a flowing QGP medium. It contrasts with findings by Qin (2007) and Zakharov (2007) that collisional loss should be only about 20% of radiative loss, attributing the difference to their use of fixed coupling and naive infrared regulation. The approach enables more reliable baseline calculations for interpreting heavy flavor quenching data from RHIC, and the authors note that the inability to simultaneously fit R_AA and v_2 with a single K factor points to the need for improved understanding of radiative energy loss and low-momentum elastic scattering.
