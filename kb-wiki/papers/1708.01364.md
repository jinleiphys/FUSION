# Effective interactions of hyperons and mass-radius relation of neutron stars

> We examine the role of hyperons in a neutron star based on the relativistic mean field approach. For nuclear matter below 1.5 times the normal nuclear density we constrain the model parameters by using the symmetric nuclear matter properties and theoretical investigations for neutron matter in the literature. We then extend the model to higher densities by including hyperons and isoscalar vector mesons that contain strangeness degree of freedom. We confirm that the $ϕ$ meson induces a $Λ$ repulsive force and hardens the equation of state. The hardening arising from the $ϕ$ meson compensates the softening from the existence of hyperons. The flavor SU(3) and spin-flavor SU(6) relations are examined as well. We found that the coupling constants fitted by neutron matter properties could yield high enough maximum mass of a neutron star and the obtained results satisfy both the mass and radius constraints. The onset of the hyperon direct Urca process in neutron stars is also investigated using our parametrization.

## Key claim

The authors find that coupling constants fitted to neutron matter properties from quantum Monte Carlo (Gandolfi, Carlson, and Reddy, 2013) and chiral effective field theory (Drischler, Soma, and Schwenk, 2013) can yield neutron star maximum masses high enough to satisfy the observed 2 solar mass constraint, provided the phi meson repulsion among Lambda hyperons is included to compensate the softening from hyperon presence.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean field (RMF) model for nucleonic matter, fitting parameters to symmetric nuclear matter properties (saturation density 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy 16 MeV, incompressibility 240-245 MeV) and to neutron matter equations of state from Gandolfi, Carlson, and Reddy (2013) (GCR5) and Drischler, Soma, and Schwenk (2013) (DSS2). They then extend the model to include the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and the strange mesons phi(1020) and f0(980), using flavor SU(3) and spin-flavor SU(6) relations to determine hyperon-meson coupling constants, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B = 16 MeV
- Incompressibility coefficient: K = 240-245 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 32.9 MeV (RGCR), 30.7 MeV (RDSS)
- Symmetry energy slope at saturation: L = 46.8 MeV (RGCR), 42.2 MeV (RDSS)
- Neutron star radius estimate from Guillot et al. (2013): R_NS = 9.1_{-1.5}^{+1.3} km (90% confidence)
- Neutron star radius estimate from Steiner et al. (2010): R_NS = 12^{+0.5}_{-1.0} km
- Observed neutron star masses: PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 at approximately 2.0 solar masses
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 491.5 MeV
- Omega meson mass: m_omega = 782.5 MeV
- Rho meson mass: m_rho = 775.3 MeV
- f0(980) meson mass: m_sigma* = 975 MeV
- phi meson mass: m_phi = 1020 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long history of hyperon studies in neutron stars dating to Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and Glendenning (1982), addressing the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperons soften the equation of state and reduce maximum mass. The authors build on RMF approaches by Fattoyev et al. (2010) (IU-FSU) and Steiner et al. (2013) (SFHo), and compare with models like GM1 (Glendenning and Moszkowski, 1991) and NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1996). The results enable future tests against NICER (Neutron star Interior Composition ExploreR) observations of neutron star masses and radii, and allow investigation of the hyperon direct Urca process for neutron star cooling.
