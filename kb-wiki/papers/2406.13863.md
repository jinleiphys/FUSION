# Revealing initial state properties through ultra-central symmetric heavy-ion collisions

> Heavy-ion experiments provide a new opportunity to gain a deeper understanding of the structure of nuclei. To achieve this, it is crucial to identify observables under circumstances that are minimally affected by the process that leads to the initial state of heavy-ion collisions from nuclear wavefunction. In this study, we demonstrate that when assuming scale-invariance, the effect of this stage on the initial energy or entropy density moments in ultra-central symmetric collisions is negligible for nucleon sizes of approximately 0.7 fm or larger for large nuclei. By borrowing cluster expansion method from statistical physics and using scale-invariance assumption, we calculate the average ellipticity of initial density at the presence of short-range correlation. We compare our calculations to Monte Carlo studies and assess the accuracy of various methods of short-range correlation sampling. Additionally, we find that the isobar ratio can constrain the initial state parameters, in addition to deformation. Our study indicates that the isobar ratios in ultra-central collisions are especially sensitive to the fluctuation in the weight of the nuclei constituents and the two-body correlation among nucleons. This insight is crucial for drawing conclusions about nuclear deformations based on isobar ratios.

## Key claim

Assuming scale-invariance of the reduced thickness function, the effect of the entropy production stage on the initial energy or entropy density moments in ultra-central symmetric collisions is negligible for nucleon sizes of approximately 0.7 fm or larger for large nuclei, but this convergence does not hold for non-central or asymmetric collisions.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTO event generator to simulate Au-Au and Au-Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, implementing various reduced thickness functions including the TRENTO model with different p parameters, the MC-Glauber model, a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model, and a generalized TRENTO model with variable q parameter. They borrowed the cluster expansion method from statistical physics to analytically calculate the average ellipticity of initial density in the presence of short-range correlations, and compared these analytical results to Monte Carlo studies of different short-range correlation sampling methods.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon size threshold for convergence: approximately 0.7 fm for large nuclei such as Pb, Au, Ru, and Zr
- For point-like sources in spherical nuclei, the average ellipticity squared epsilon_2^2{2} = 5 / (7 A)
- The minimum distance between nucleons d_min = 0.6 fm was used in the TRENTO simulations
- The generalized TRENTO model parameter q = 1.34 (+0.14, -0.18) from Bayesian analysis in Nijs (2023) is marginally compatible with the scale-invariant value q = 3/2
- The CGC-based reduced thickness function scales as T_R^energy -> c^{3/2} T_R^energy under scaling of thickness functions

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear structure through heavy-ion collisions, building on the TRENTO model (Moreland, 2014) and the observation that ultra-central uranium-uranium collisions at RHIC (Pandit, 2013; Wang, 2014) support scale-invariance. The authors provide an analytical framework using cluster expansion from statistical mechanics (Huang, 1987) to connect initial state fluctuations to nuclear structure parameters, complementing Bayesian analysis studies (JETSCAPE, 2020; Nijs, 2023). This enables more precise constraints on nuclear deformation from isobar ratio measurements by the STAR collaboration (2021), particularly for Ru and Zr nuclei.
