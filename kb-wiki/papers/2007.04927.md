# Strong CP violation in nuclear physics

> Electric dipole moments of nuclei, diamagnetic atoms, and certain molecules are induced by CP-violating nuclear forces. Naive dimensional analysis predicts these forces to be dominated by long-range one-pion-exchange processes, with short-range forces entering only at next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. Based on renormalization arguments we argue that a consistent picture of CP-violating nuclear forces requires a leading-order short-distance operator contributing to ${}^1S_0$-${}^3P_0$ transitions, due to the attractive and singular nature of the strong tensor force in the ${}^3P_0$ channel. The short-distance operator leads to $\mathcal O(1)$ corrections to static and oscillating, relevant for axion searches, electric dipole moments. We discuss strategies how the finite part of the associated low-energy constant can be determined in the case of CP violation from the QCD theta term by the connection to charge-symmetry violation in nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to naive dimensional analysis, a consistent renormalized chiral effective field theory (χEFT) of CP-violating nuclear forces requires a leading-order (LO) short-distance operator for \(^1S_0\)-\(^3P_0\) transitions, because the attractive and singular strong tensor force in the \(^3P_0\) channel produces regulator-dependent mixing angles that cannot be absorbed without this operator. This short-distance operator leads to order-one (\(\mathcal{O}(1)\)) corrections to both static and oscillating electric dipole moments (EDMs), rather than the next-to-next-to-leading-order (N\(^2\)LO) suppression previously assumed.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the strong nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, consisting of one-pion-exchange (OPE) and contact interactions in the \(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\), and \(^3P_0\) partial waves, using a momentum-space regulator with cutoff \(\Lambda\). They then inserted the CP-odd OPE potential from the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term (proportional to the low-energy constant \(\bar g_0\)) in perturbation theory and computed the \(^1S_0\)-\(^3P_0\) mixing angle \(\epsilon^0_{\rm SP}\) as a function of \(\Lambda\). To restore cutoff independence, they promoted the short-distance CP-odd contact operator \(\bar C_0\) (which projects onto \(^1S_0\)-\(^3P_0\) transitions) to leading order and fitted its value at a single kinematic point.

## Key numbers

* \(\bar g_0 = -(14.7 \pm 2.3)\times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\): the CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant for the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term, determined from the quark-mass-induced part of the proton-neutron mass splitting \(\delta m_N^{\rm str}\) and the quark mass ratio \(\varepsilon = (m_u-m_d)/(m_u+m_d)\).
* The strong \(^3P_0\) phase shifts show a "dramatic limit-cycle-like" cutoff dependence when only OPE is used, requiring promotion of the \(P\)-wave contact term \(C_P\) to leading order; the LECs \(C_{s,t,P}\) are fitted to phase shifts at a center-of-mass energy \(E_{\rm CM} = 5\) MeV.
* The \(^1S_0\)-\(^3P_0\) mixing angle \(\epsilon^0_{\rm SP}\) (in units of \(\bar g_0\)) shows oscillatory behavior and changes sign as a function of \(\Lambda\) without the short-distance counter term; after promoting \(\bar C_0\) to LO and fitting \(\epsilon^0_{\rm SP,fit} = 0.01\,\bar g_0\) at \(E_{\rm CM}=5\) MeV, the resulting \(\epsilon^0_{\rm SP}\) becomes regulator independent for a wide range of energies (e.g., \(E_{\rm CM}=25\) and \(50\) MeV).
* The mixing angles \(\epsilon^1_{\rm SP}\) and \(\epsilon_{\rm DP}\) (for \(^3S_1\)-\(^1P_1\) and \(^3D_1\)-\(^1P_1\) transitions) converge quickly and show no regulator dependence, even without a short-distance counter term.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT framework for CP-violating nuclear forces developed by Maekawa et al. (2011) and Gnech et al. (2019), which used Weinberg's power counting and predicted short-range CP-odd operators to enter only at N\(^2\)LO. The authors show that renormalization arguments, following the earlier observation by Nogga et al. (2005) of limit-cycle behavior in the strong \(^3P_0\) channel, force a leading-order short-distance operator for \(^1S_0\)-\(^3P_0\) transitions. This result directly impacts the interpretation of EDM experiments (e.g., for \(^{199}\)Hg, \(^{225}\)Ra, and the deuteron) and axion searches via oscillating EDMs. The paper proposes to determine the finite part of the associated low-energy constant \(\bar C_0\) for the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term by connecting it to charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) data in few-body systems such as \(dd \rightarrow \alpha \pi^0\), building on the chiral-symmetry relation between \(\bar g_0\) and \(\delta m_N^{\rm str}\) from de Vries et al. (2015).
