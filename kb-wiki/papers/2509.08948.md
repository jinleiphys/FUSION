# Estimation of deuteron binding energy with renormalization group-based effective interactions using the variational quantum eigensolver

> We have obtained the binding energy of the deuteron on a quantum simulator using the variational quantum eigensolver for renormalization group (RG)-based low-momentum effective interactions. The binding energy (BE) has been calculated in the truncated harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, using the Qiskit-Aer simulator in both noise-free and noisy cases. The noise models have been taken from the actual IBM quantum hardware, and the results obtained have been extrapolated to the zero noise limit. The number of HO basis states (hence qubits) needed for computing the BE to within 1 percent of the experimental value in the quantum simulator, decreases with decreasing RG parameter $λ$. The $λ$-dependence of the extent of entanglement between the oscillator modes has been analysed.

# Estimation of deuteron binding energy with renormalization group-based effective interactions using the variational quantum eigensolver

## Key claim

The deuteron binding energy computed on a quantum simulator using the variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) with similarity renormalization group (SRG)-evolved interactions agrees with the experimental value to within 1 percent, but only when the RG cutoff parameter lambda is sufficiently small; for bare interactions or large lambda, the qubit requirement and error rates are substantially higher.

## Method

The authors mapped the deuteron Hamiltonian in a truncated harmonic oscillator (HO) basis to qubits using the Jordan-Wigner transformation, then applied the VQE algorithm with a unitary coupled-cluster ansatz built from single excitation operators. They used the Qiskit-Aer simulator with noise models from actual IBM quantum hardware (ibm_brisbane and ibm_kyiv), performed zero-noise extrapolation with polynomial fitting, and compared results to exact diagonalization of the same Hamiltonian in the HO basis. The SRG-evolved chiral N4LO interaction was used as the primary input, with the Argonne V18 interaction verified to give equivalent results.

## Key numbers

- For N=1 (2 qubits), lambda=0.5 fm^-1, hbar-omega=5.0 MeV: exact diagonalization gives BE = -2.2112 MeV, noisy VQE with ZNE gives -2.2094 MeV, error approximately 0.05 percent
- For N=2 (4 qubits), lambda=0.5 fm^-1, hbar-omega=6.1 MeV: exact diagonalization gives BE = -2.2137 MeV, noisy VQE with ZNE gives -2.1856 MeV, error approximately 0.6 percent
- For N=3 (6 qubits), lambda=0.1 fm^-1, hbar-omega=0.1 MeV: exact diagonalization gives BE = -2.2251 MeV, noisy VQE with ZNE gives -2.2220 MeV, error approximately 0.2 percent
- For N=3 with bare interaction (lambda ~ 3.0 fm^-1), hbar-omega=18.3 MeV: exact diagonalization gives BE = -1.0508 MeV, noisy VQE with ZNE gives -0.8640 MeV, error approximately 32 percent
- The experimental deuteron binding energy is approximately -2.2246 MeV (implied by the convergence criterion |E - E_exp| <= 0.5 MeV)
- Convergence in BE for the bare chiral N4LO interaction requires N ~ 6 and 15 MeV < hbar-omega < 40 MeV; for lambda = 0.5 fm^-1, convergence is achieved even for N=1

## Context

This work extends prior quantum computing calculations of the deuteron binding energy using pionless effective field theory by Dumitrescu (2018), Shehab (2019), and Gu (2025), which achieved similar 1 percent accuracy with 2-3 qubits. The key advance is demonstrating that SRG-evolved realistic interactions (chiral N4LO from Entem (2017) and Argonne V18) reduce the qubit requirement compared to bare interactions, connecting the well-established classical advantage of RG-based interactions (reviewed by Bogner and collaborators) to quantum computing platforms. The work also analyzes mode entanglement via concurrence as a function of the RG cutoff, extending prior entanglement studies in two-nucleon systems by Kruppa (2021) and Robin (2021). This enables future extensions to many-body systems such as the triton and alpha-nucleus using RG-evolved interactions on quantum computers.
