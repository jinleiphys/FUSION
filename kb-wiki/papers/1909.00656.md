# Hermitizing the HAL QCD potential in the derivative expansion

> A formalism is given to hermitize the HAL QCD potential, which needs to be non-hermitian except the leading order (LO) local term in the derivative expansion as the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for different energies are not orthogonal to each other. It is shown that the non-hermitian potential can be hermitized order by order to all orders in the derivative expansion. In particular, the next-to-leading order (NLO) potential can be exactly hermitized without approximation. The formalism is then applied to a simple case of $ΞΞ(^{1}S_{0}) $ scattering, for which the HAL QCD calculation is available to the NLO. The NLO term gives relatively small corrections to the scattering phase shift and the LO analysis seems justified in this case. We also observe that the local part of the hermitized NLO potential works better than that of the non-hermitian NLO potential. The hermitian version of the HAL QCD potential is desirable for comparing it with phenomenological interactions and also for using it as a two-body interaction in many body systems.

# Hermitizing the HAL QCD potential in the derivative expansion

## Key claim

The HAL QCD potential, which is inherently non-hermitian beyond the leading local term because Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions at different energies are not orthogonal, can be made hermitian order by order to all orders in the derivative expansion; for the specific case of ΞΞ(¹S₀) scattering, the next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to the scattering phase shift are relatively small, justifying the leading-order (LO) analysis.

## Method

The authors formulate a transformation that converts the non-hermitian Hamiltonian, expressed as a derivative expansion of the potential, into a hermitian one by applying a change of wave function ψ = R φ with a local function R determined order by order. For the NLO case (n=1), the transformation is exact and yields a hermitian potential containing only even-derivative terms; for higher orders (n≥2), the hermitization proceeds via mathematical induction, removing non-hermitian odd-derivative terms sequentially by solving linear first-order partial differential equations for the components of R. The formalism is applied to lattice QCD data for ΞΞ(¹S₀) scattering, using two NBS wave functions (wall source and smeared source) from a previous calculation, with two separate NLO analyses: one omitting the first-derivative term (NLO_A) and one omitting the second-derivative term (NLO_B).

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: a = 0.09 fm on a 64⁴ lattice; m_π = 0.51 GeV, m_K = 0.62 GeV, m_N = 1.32 GeV, m_Ξ = 1.46 GeV
- The hermitized local potential Ṽ₀^(NLO_A) describes the scattering phase shift well in the range 0 ≤ (k/m_π)² ≤ 0.6, with small deviations around (k/m_π)² ≈ 0.1
- The NLO_A second-derivative term V₂^(NLO_A)(r) is non-zero only at r < 1 fm
- The NLO_B first-derivative term V₁^(NLO_B)(r) is shown for r ≤ 3.5 fm
- The LO phase shift from the wall-source NBS wave function is very similar to the NLO phase shift at low energies and slightly larger at higher energies

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD potential method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009, Aoki 2012) by addressing the non-hermiticity issue that arises because NBS wave functions at different energies are not orthogonal. The HAL QCD method has been successfully applied to many two- and three-hadron systems at heavy pion masses (Inoue 2010, Murano 2011, Doi 2011, Ikeda 2013) and at nearly physical masses (Gongyo 2017, Sasaki 2017, Ishii 2017), but the non-hermitian nature of higher-order terms has been a known subtlety. The hermitized potential enables direct comparison with phenomenological interactions and use as a two-body interaction in many-body systems, which was previously problematic. The specific lattice data for ΞΞ(¹S₀) used here come from Iritani (2018).
