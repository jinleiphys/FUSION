# Shell model Monte Carlo calculations for Dy-170

> We present the first auxiliary field Monte Carlo calculations for a rare earth nucleus, Dy-170. A pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian is used to demonstrate the physical properties that can be studied in this region. We calculate various static observables for both uncranked and cranked systems and show how the shape distribution evolves with temperature. We also introduce a discretization of the path integral that allows a more efficient Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key claim

The first auxiliary field Monte Carlo calculations for a rare-earth nucleus (Dy-170) are presented, using a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian to demonstrate feasibility; the authors caution that the schematic nature of the Hamiltonian precludes reading too much into the results, but the method can be extended to more realistic interactions.

## Method

The authors perform shell model Monte Carlo calculations for Dy-170 using a pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian, with protons in the Z=50-82 shell (32 single-particle states) and neutrons in the N=82-126 shell (44 single-particle states). The imaginary-time path integral is evaluated via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, and a novel discretization of the auxiliary fields using a 3-state Gaussian quadrature (with sigma values of 0 and +/- sigma_0) replaces the continuous integral to reduce autocorrelation times. Both grand-canonical and canonically projected observables are computed for uncranked and cranked (H -> H - omega J_z) systems at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Dy-170 has 16 valence protons and 22 valence neutrons, requiring approximately 10^21 m-scheme determinants.
- The discretization of auxiliary fields reduces the Metropolis autocorrelation length from over 200 sweeps to only 5 sweeps, increasing efficiency by a factor of 40.
- The time step used is Delta_beta = 0.0625 MeV^-1, with N_t = 8 to 64 time slices.
- Canonical observables are calculable with good precision because fluctuations in the ratio zeta_c/zeta_g are less than 10%.
- The pairing strengths used are g_p = +0.160 MeV and g_n = +0.131 MeV; the quadrupole strength is chi = +0.054 MeV fm^4.
- Shape distributions are shown at inverse temperatures T^-1 = 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 MeV^-1, with a prolate minimum on the gamma=0 axis appearing at the lowest temperature.

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary field Monte Carlo method, previously developed for sd- and fp-shell nuclei by Johnson, Koonin, Lang, and Ormand (1992) and Lang et al. (1993), to the rare-earth region for the first time, using different major shells for protons and neutrons. The results enable future calculations with more realistic shell-model Hamiltonians, as demonstrated by Alhassid et al. (1993), and can be used to check various approximation schemes such as mean-field models of rare-earth nuclei by Goodman, Vary, and Sorensen (1976).
