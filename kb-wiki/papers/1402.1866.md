# Axial asymmetry in the IVBM

> The dynamical symmetry limit of the two-fluid Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), defined through the chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(3,3) \supset U_{p}(3) \otimes \overline{U_{n}(3)} \supset SU^{\ast}(3) \supset SO(3)$, is considered and applied for the description of nuclear collective spectra exhibiting axially asymmetric features. The effect of the introduction of a Majorana interaction to the $SU^{\ast}(3)$ model Hamiltonian on the $γ$-band energies is studied. The theoretical predictions are compared with the experimental data for $^{192}Os$, $^{190}Os$, and $^{112}Ru$ isotopes. It is shown that by taking into account the full symplectic structures in the considered dynamical symmetry of the IVBM, the proper description of the energy spectra and the $γ$-band energy staggering of the nuclei under considerations can be achieved. The obtained results show that the potential energy surfaces for the following two nuclei $^{192}Os$ and $^{112}Ru$, possess almost $γ$-flat potentials with very shallow triaxial minima, suggesting a more complex and intermediate situation between $γ$-rigid and $γ$-unstable structures. Additionally, the absolute $B(E2)$ intraband transition probabilities between the states of the ground state band and $γ$ band, as well as the $B(M1)$ interband transition probabilities between the states of the ground and $γ$ bands for the two nuclei $^{192}Os$ and $^{190}Os$ are calculated and compared with experiment and for the $B(E2)$ values with the predictions of some other collective models incorporating the $γ$-rigid or $γ$-unstable structures. The obtained results agree well with the experimental data and reveal the relevance of the used dynamical symmetry of IVBM in the description of nuclei exhibiting axially asymmetric features in their spectra.

## Key claim

The dynamical symmetry limit of the two-fluid Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) with the chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(3,3) \supset U_{p}(3) \otimes \overline{U_{n}(3)} \supset SU^{\ast}(3) \supset SO(3)$ can describe nuclear collective spectra exhibiting axially asymmetric features, but only when a Majorana interaction is added to the $SU^{\ast}(3)$ Hamiltonian; the resulting potential energy surfaces for $^{192}Os$ and $^{112}Ru$ are almost $\gamma$-flat with very shallow triaxial minima, representing an intermediate situation between $\gamma$-rigid and $\gamma$-unstable structures rather than a pure limit.

## Method

The authors used the symplectic IVBM with $Sp(12,R)$ as the dynamical symmetry group, constructing basis states labeled by $U(3,3)$, $U_{p}(3) \otimes \overline{U_{n}(3)}$, and $SU^{\ast}(3)$ quantum numbers. They added a Majorana interaction term to the $SU^{\ast}(3)$ Hamiltonian and diagonalized it numerically, mapping experimental ground-state band (GSB) and $\gamma$-band states onto stretched $SU^{\ast}(3)$ irreps of the type $(\lambda,\mu) = (k,k)$ for the GSB and $(\lambda_0+k,\mu_0+k)$ with $\lambda_0=2$, $\mu_0=4$ for the $\gamma$ band. The model parameters were determined by fitting the energies of the GSB and $\gamma$ bands for $^{192}Os$, $^{190}Os$, and $^{112}Ru$ to experimental data using a $\chi^2$ procedure, and the resulting energy surfaces were obtained via the coherent state method.

## Key numbers

- For $^{192}Os$: model parameters $a_1 = 0.1034$ MeV, $b = -0.0027$ MeV, $a_3 = -0.0011$ MeV, $b_3 = 0.0209$ MeV, and $a = -0.0150$ MeV.
- For $^{190}Os$: model parameters $a_1 = 0.1127$ MeV, $b = 0.0004$ MeV, $a_3 = -0.0002$ MeV, $b_3 = 0.0251$ MeV, and $a = 0$ MeV.
- For $^{112}Ru$: model parameters $a_1 = 0.1144$ MeV, $b = -0.0056$ MeV, $a_3 = -0.0014$ MeV, $b_3 = 0.0212$ MeV, and $a = -0.0209$ MeV.
- The triaxial minima in the energy surfaces for $^{192}Os$ and $^{112}Ru$ occur at $\rho_0 \approx 1$ and $\theta_0 = 90^\circ$, which corresponds to $\gamma_{eff} = 30^\circ$.
- For $a = 0$ MeV (no mixing of $SU(3)$ irreps), the model yields a $\gamma$-unstable-like structure; for $a \simeq -0.005$ MeV, an intermediate situation with more regular spacing of energy levels is obtained.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry limit of the IVBM introduced in Ganev (2013) (Ref. [TSIVBM]) by providing detailed calculations of energy spectra, $\gamma$-band staggering, and transition probabilities for $^{192}Os$, $^{190}Os$, and $^{112}Ru$. It addresses the long-standing debate about whether these nuclei are $\gamma$-unstable (as in the Wilets-Jean model) or $\gamma$-rigid (as in the Davydov rigid triaxial rotor model), showing that the IVBM can reproduce the intermediate staggering patterns observed experimentally. The results are compared with predictions from the IBM-1 with a cubic quadrupole term (Ref. [6Q]) and the sextic and Mathieu approach (Ref. [SMA]), and the energy surfaces are contrasted with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy4 forces (Robledo, 2010) and IBM-2 calculations (Nomura, 2011). The approach enables future studies of transitional nuclei where the potential is neither purely $\gamma$-rigid nor $\gamma$-unstable.
