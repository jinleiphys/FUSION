# Exotic baryons from a heavy meson and a nucleon - Positive parity states -

> We study heavy baryons with exotic flavor quantum numbers formed by a heavy meson and a nucleon (DbarN and BN) with positive parity. One pion exchange interaction, providing a tensor force, dominates as a long range force to bind the DbarN and BN ystems. In the heavy quark mass limit, pseudoscalar meson and vector meson are degenerate and the binding mechanism by the tensor force analogous to that in the nuclear systems becomes important. As a result, we obtain the DbarN and BN resonant states in the J^P=1/2^+, 3/2^+ and 5/2^+ channels with I=0.

## Key claim

The authors find that the one-pion-exchange tensor force, which mixes the heavy pseudoscalar meson (P) and heavy vector meson (P*) channels, generates resonant states in the DbarN and BN systems with positive parity, but no bound states exist, unlike the negative parity case where bound states were previously found.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for DbarN and BN systems using one-pion-exchange and vector-meson-exchange (rho and omega) potentials derived from Lagrangians satisfying heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry, with monopole form factors at each vertex. They solve the scattering problem for the positive parity channels J^P = 1/2^+, 3/2^+, and 5/2^+ with isospin I=0 and I=1, identifying resonances by analyzing the phase shift behavior (inflection point for resonance energy, derivative for width).

## Key numbers

- For (I,J^P) = (0,1/2^+): DbarN resonance at 26.8 MeV with width 131.3 MeV; BN resonance at 5.8 MeV with width 6.0 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (0,3/2^+): Dbar*N(^4P_{3/2}) resonance at 148.2 MeV with width 10.1 MeV; BN resonance at 31.8 MeV with width 28.7 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (0,5/2^+): Dbar*N(^4P_{5/2}) resonance at 176.0 MeV with width 174.8 MeV; B*N(^4P_{5/2}) resonance at 58.4 MeV with width 49.6 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- No bound states found in any positive parity channel; no resonant structures found in the isotriplet (I=1) channel

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of negative parity DbarN and BN states (Yasui 2009, Yamaguchi 2011) to the positive parity sector, completing the investigation of these exotic baryon systems with minimal quark content Qbar qqqq. The results show that the tensor force from one-pion exchange, analogous to its role in nuclear binding (deuteron), dominates over vector meson exchange in forming these resonances. The positive parity states are all resonances rather than bound states, with mechanisms including shape resonances from p-wave centrifugal barriers and a Feshbach resonance in the BN (0,3/2^+) channel, enabling future experimental searches for these exotic states near the DbarN and BN thresholds.
