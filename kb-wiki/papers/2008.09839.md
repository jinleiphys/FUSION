# Gravitational wave asteroseismology for low-mass neutron stars

> The fundamental ($f$-) mode gravitational waves from cold low-mass neutron stars are systematically studied with various equations of state (EOSs) characterized by the nuclear saturation parameters, especially focusing on the phenomena of the avoided crossing with the first pressure ($p_1$-) mode. We find that the $f$-mode frequency and the average density for the neutron star at the avoided crossing can be expressed as a function of the parameter, $η$, which is the specific combination of the nuclear saturation parameters. Owing to these relations, we can derive the empirical formula expressing the $f$-mode frequency for a low-mass neutron star, whose central density is larger than that for the neutron star at the avoided crossing, as a function of $η$ and the square root of the stellar average density, $x$. On the other hand, we also derive the empirical formula expressing the $f$-mode frequency for a neutron star, whose central density is less than that for the neutron star at the avoided crossing, as a function of $x$ independently of the adopted EOS. Furthermore, adopting the empirical formula of $x$ as a function of $η$ and $u_c$, which is the ratio of the stellar central density to the saturation density, we can also rewrite our empirical formula for the $f$-mode frequency to a function of $η$ and $u_c$. So, by observing the $f$-mode gravitational wave from a low-mass neutron star, whose mass or gravitational redshift is known, one could evaluate the values of $η$ and $u_c$, which enables us to severely constrain the EOS for neutron star matter.

# Gravitational wave asteroseismology for low-mass neutron stars

## Key claim

The f-mode gravitational wave frequency from low-mass neutron stars can be expressed as an empirical function of the nuclear saturation parameter η and the stellar central density ratio u_c, but this methodology works well only for η in the range of approximately 90 to 140 MeV; for lower η values the fitting formulae become unreliable because the central density exceeds 2ρ_0 where the underlying assumptions break down.

## Method

The author systematically computed f-mode oscillation frequencies for cold low-mass neutron stars (central density ≤ 2ρ_0) constructed with multiple equations of state (EOSs) from the Oyamatsu-Iida family and the Shen EOS, characterized by different values of the nuclear saturation parameters K_0 and L. Linear perturbation analysis was performed in the relativistic Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) to solve for the eigenfrequencies of the ℓ=2 fluid modes, focusing on the avoided crossing phenomenon between the f-mode and the first pressure (p_1-) mode as a function of central density. The f-mode frequencies at and around the avoided crossing were fitted with cubic functions of u_c, and the resulting empirical formulae were expressed in terms of the combined saturation parameter η = (K_0 L^2)^{1/3} and the square root of the normalized average density x = (M/1.4M_⊙)^{1/2}(R/10 km)^{-3/2}.

## Key numbers

- The f-mode frequency at the avoided crossing is given by f_{f,AC} (kHz) = 1.9926 − 0.8095 η_{100} + 0.1856 η_{100}^2, where η_{100} ≡ η/100 MeV.
- For neutron stars with central density less than that at the avoided crossing, the f-mode frequency follows f_f (kHz) = 0.018687 + 4.2621x + 15.1014x^2 − 26.8770x^3, independent of the adopted EOS.
- For neutron stars with central density greater than that at the avoided crossing, the empirical formula is f_f (kHz) = 1.5834x + 0.1306η_{100}^2 − 0.6051η_{100} + 1.5477.
- The empirical formula predicts f-mode frequencies with less than 5% relative deviation across the parameter space studied.
- The maximum neutron star mass for EOSs characterized by η is given by M_max/M_⊙ = 2.856 + 0.1511 η_{100}.
- The square root of the normalized average density x is expressed as x = c_0 η_{100}^{-1} + c_1 + c_2 η_{100}, with coefficients c_0, c_1, c_2 given as functions of u_c = ρ_c/ρ_0.

## Context

This work extends the gravitational wave asteroseismology program initiated by Andersson and Kokkotas (1998), who showed that f-mode frequency scales linearly with the square root of stellar average density with weak EOS dependence. The author builds on prior findings by Sotani et al. (2014) and Sotani, Sekiguchi, and Bamba (2016) that low-mass neutron star properties (mass, gravitational redshift, moment of inertia) can be expressed as functions of the combined saturation parameter η and central density ratio u_c. The empirical formulae derived here enable constraining the nuclear saturation parameters K_0 and L through future observations of f-mode gravitational waves from low-mass neutron stars, complementing constraints from the GW170817 event (which limited the radius of a 1.4M_⊙ neutron star to less than 13.6 km) and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451.
