# Bottomonium evolution with in-medium heavy quark potential from lattice QCD

> The static properties and dynamic evolution of bottomonium states in a hot QCD medium are investigated through the Schrödinger equation with complex heavy quark potentials, which are presented recently in lattice QCD study and with three different extractions. This approach builds a direct connection between the in-medium heavy quark potentials from the lattice QCD to the experimental observables. The yields and nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}$ of bottomonium in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~\rm TeV$ are calculated in this work. Our results show a large suppression of the bottomonium yield in heavy ion collisions due to the large imaginary potential. To understand bottomonium $R_{AA}$ based on lattice QCD potentials, we propose a fomration time for bottomonium states and find that experimental data can be well explained with the heavy quark potential extracted by the Padé fit, which shows no color screening in the real part potential.

# Bottomonium evolution with in-medium heavy quark potential from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of bottomonium in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~\rm TeV$ can be well explained by the heavy quark potential extracted via the Padé fit from lattice QCD, but only when a formation time of $\tau_\Upsilon = 3~\rm fm/c$ is introduced; the real part of this potential shows no color screening even at high temperatures up to $T \le 3T_c$.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with complex heavy quark potentials extracted from lattice QCD using three different spectral function extraction methods: Gaussian fits, HTL fits, and Padé fits. The dynamical evolution of bottomonium wave functions is computed in a hydrodynamic background simulated with the MUSIC package for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~\rm TeV$, with initial conditions determined from charged hadron multiplicity. The final yields and nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}$ for $\Upsilon(1S,2S,3S)$ are obtained by projecting the evolved wave package onto vacuum eigenstates and averaging over spatial and momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial temperature of the medium: $T_0(\tau_0=0.6, {\bf x}_T=0) = 510$ MeV at the center of the medium in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- Critical temperature: $T_c = 170$ MeV
- Bottom quark mass: $m_b = 4.7$ GeV
- Parameters for the Padé fit potential: $\alpha_3 = 0.41$, $\sigma_3 = 0.24$ GeV$^2$, $\gamma_3 = 60$
- Parameters for the Gaussian fit potential: $\alpha_1 = 0.3805$, $\sigma_1 = 0.22$ GeV$^2$, $\beta_1 = 1.2$, $\gamma_1 = 0.54$
- Parameters for the HTL fit potential: $\alpha_2 = 0.3805$, $\sigma_2 = 0.22$ GeV$^2$, $\gamma_2 = 100$
- Direct production cross sections in p-p collisions: $\sigma_{pp}(1s,1p,2s,2p,3s) = (37.97, 44.20, 18.27, 37.68, 8.21)$ nb
- Mean transverse momentum square in p-p collisions: $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp} = 80$ (GeV/c)$^2$ at 5.02 TeV
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section for Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV: $\sigma_{\rm inel} = 68$ mb
- Effective shear viscosity: $\eta/s = 0.08$

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the heavy quark potential by Bala et al. (2021) and Bazavov et al. (2023), which found no color screening in the real part potential and a large imaginary part, into phenomenological predictions for heavy ion collisions. It contrasts with previous transport approaches by Islam et al. (2020) and Wen et al. (2022) that used potentials with clear screening effects, and with the HTL-based perturbative potential of Laine et al. (2006). The framework enables direct comparison between first-principles lattice QCD potentials and experimental observables from the CMS collaboration, and opens the question of whether octet-to-singlet transitions (regeneration) need to be included for a complete description.
