# Pigmy resonances, transfer, and separable potentials

> In this contribution we make a short review of recent progress on topics of current interest in nuclear physics and nuclear astrophysics. In particular, we discuss a re-analysis of the extraction of the dipole response of the pigmy resonance in $^{68}$Ni based on a continuum discretized coupled-channels calculation in relativistic Coulomb excitation experiments. We also discuss the forthcoming progresses made by our group on the Alt-Sandhas-Grassberber approach to (d,p) reactions and future expectations. The role of separable potentials in solving such equations with a test case based on applications of such potentials to phase-shift analysis is also presented.

# Pigmy resonances, transfer, and separable potentials

## Key claim
The extraction of the dipole strength of the pigmy dipole resonance (PDR) in ⁶⁸Ni from relativistic Coulomb excitation experiments requires a careful treatment of the dynamical coupling with giant resonance states, as first-order perturbation theory underestimates the cross section by 14–18% depending on the target and beam energy, implying that previously reported PDR strengths may need to be reduced by roughly that amount.

## Method
The authors performed continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations for relativistic Coulomb excitation of ⁶⁸Ni on ¹⁹⁷Au and ²⁰⁸Pb targets, using Lorentzian forms for the response functions adjusted to a predetermined fraction of the sum rule and discretized into energy bins. For transfer reactions, they formulated generalized Alt-Sandhas-Grassberger (AGS) integral equations in momentum-space Coulomb distorted partial-wave representation, incorporating target excitations and separable potentials for the two-body interactions. Separable potentials of modified-Yukawa type were fitted to experimental phase shifts for n-⁴He, p-⁴He, n-¹⁶O, and p-¹⁶O scattering.

## Key numbers
- PDR in ⁶⁸Ni found at E_PDR ≈ 11 MeV with width Γ_PDR ≈ 1 MeV, exhausting approximately 5% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule in one experiment; a second experiment gave centroid energy 9.55 MeV, width 0.5 MeV, exhausting 2.8% of the TRK sum rule.
- For ⁶⁸Ni + ¹⁹⁷Au at 600 MeV/nucleon: first-order perturbation theory cross section = 80.9 mb; coupled-channels cross section = 92.2 mb (a 14% increase).
- For ⁶⁸Ni + ²⁰⁸Pb at 503 MeV/nucleon: first-order cross section = 58.3 mb; coupled-channels cross section = 71.2 mb (an 18.1% increase).
- Dipole polarizability α_D extracted from experiment = 3.40 fm³; value needed with higher-order calculations = 3.16 fm³.
- Neutron skin reduction from 0.17 fm (as reported in Ref. Ros13) to 0.16 fm when using coupled-channels analysis.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of PDR excitations in neutron-rich nuclei, which were first observed in radioactive beam facilities in the 1980s–1990s for light projectiles like ¹¹Be and ¹¹Li, and later confirmed in heavier nuclei via gamma and neutron emission (Lei01). The coupled-channels corrections mirror earlier findings on the coupling of giant and double giant dipole resonances (BB88, ABE98, BP99). The generalized AGS equations with explicit Coulomb treatment and separable potentials aim to provide a more complete description of (d,p) transfer reactions than the standard distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) used for over 50 years, enabling more reliable extraction of neutron capture cross sections for nuclear astrophysics.
