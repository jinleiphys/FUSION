# The 7/11 rule: an estimate of m_rho/f_pi

> We derive an estimate for the ratio of the rho mass and the pion decay constant from an analysis of vector and axial-vector two-point functions using large-Nc, lowest-meson dominance and the operator product expansion, in the chiral limit. We discuss the extension of this analysis to the scalar and pseudoscalar sector. Furthermore, this leads to a successful parameter-free determination of the L_i couplings of the chiral Lagrangian if an improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio ansatz for Green functions is assumed at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new sum rule (the "7/11 rule") relating the rho meson mass, its decay constant, and the pion decay constant, under the assumptions of large-Nc, lowest-meson dominance, and the operator product expansion in the chiral limit. The authors hedge that the relation depends on higher-order perturbative corrections not fully known, and that the numerical success relies on combining this sum rule with an additional relation from form factor dispersion relations.

## Method

The authors analyze vector and axial-vector two-point functions using a large-Nc approximation where the spectral function is described by a single narrow resonance plus a perturbative continuum starting at a scale s0. They match this low-energy ansatz to the operator product expansion at high Euclidean momenta, obtaining sum rules for the moments of the spectral functions. By eliminating the gluon and quark condensates between the vector and axial-vector sum rules, and using the Weinberg sum rules to eliminate the a1 parameters, they derive a relation between m_rho, f_rho, and f_pi.

## Key numbers

- The ratio m_rho/f_pi = 8.8 from the analysis, compared to the experimental value 8.3
- f_rho = 0.16 from the analysis, compared to the experimental value 0.20
- Using f_pi = 87 MeV (chiral limit value), the predicted m_rho = 765 MeV and m_a1 = 1082 MeV
- The onset scale for perturbation theory sqrt(s0) = 4*pi*f_pi = 1.09 GeV
- The gluon condensate estimate: <alpha_s G^2> = 0.014 GeV^4
- The four-quark condensate estimate: pi*alpha_s <psi-bar psi>^2 = 13 x 10^{-4} GeV^6
- The chiral condensate at scale sqrt(s0): <psi-bar psi>(sqrt(s0)) = -(319 +/- 13 MeV)^3
- In the scalar sector, the scalar mass is found between 0.7 and 1.2 GeV, with a central value m_S = 0.86 GeV

## Context

This work extends the classic Weinberg sum rules (Weinberg, 1967) by adding a third sum rule from dimension-six operators in the operator product expansion, following the approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and earlier work by Peris, Perrottet, and de Rafael (1998). The analysis enables a parameter-free determination of the Gasser-Leutwyler L_i couplings of the chiral Lagrangian at order p^4, which the authors compare favorably to experimental values. The scalar sector analysis highlights limitations of the one-resonance-plus-continuum approximation, particularly for the chiral condensate, and suggests the existence of a scalar resonance in the 0.7-1.2 GeV range in the large-Nc and chiral limits.
