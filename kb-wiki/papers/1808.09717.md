# Bound and continuum state $β^-$ decay of bare atoms: enhancement of decay rate and changes in $β^{-}$ decay branching

> We have calculated rates of $β^{-}$ decay to both continuum and bound states separately for some fully ionized (bare) atoms in the mass range A $\approx$ 60-240. One of the motivations of this work is that the previous theoretical calculations were very old and/or informatically incomplete. Probably no theoretical study on this subject has been done in the last three decades. For the calculation, we have derived a framework from the usual $β^{-}$ decay theory used by previous authors. Dependence of the calculated rates on the nuclear radius and neutral atom Q-value have been examined. We have used the latest experimental data for nuclear and atomic observables, such as $β^{-}$ decay Q-value, ionization energy, neutral atom $β^{-}$ decay branchings, neutral atom half-lives etc. Results of $β^{-}$ decay rates for decay to continuum and bound states and the enhancement factor due to the bound state decay for a number of nuclei have been tabulated and compared with the previously calculated values, if available. The effective rate or half-life calculated for bare atom might be helpful to set a limit for the maximum enhancement due to bound state decay. Finally, $β^{-}$ decay branching for bare atom has been calculated. The changes in branching in bare atom compared to that in the neutral atom and for the first time branching flip for a few cases have been obtained. Reason for this branching change has been understood in terms of Q-values of the transitions in the neutral and bare atoms. Verification of this branching change / flip phenomenon in bare atom decay might be of interest for future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that for fully ionized (bare) atoms, the total β⁻ decay rate is enhanced compared to neutral atoms due to the opening of bound-state decay channels, and for the first time they report that the ordering of branching ratios to different daughter levels can flip between neutral and bare atoms, driven by the dependence of the bound-to-continuum decay ratio on the neutral-atom Q-value.

## Method

The authors derived a framework from standard β⁻ decay theory, using the lepton phase volume formalism of Takahashi and Yokoi, to compute separate transition rates for continuum-state and bound-state β⁻ decay of bare atoms. They applied this to allowed, first-forbidden non-unique, and first-forbidden unique transitions for nuclei in the mass range A ≈ 60–240, using modern experimental data for Q-values, ionization energies, neutral-atom branchings, and half-lives from the National Nuclear Data Center and NIST. They examined the dependence of the calculated rates on three different choices of nuclear radius (phenomenological 1.2A¹/³ fm, charge radius, and half-density radius) and compared their results with the earlier theoretical calculations of Takahashi et al.

## Key numbers

* For bare ²⁰⁷Tl decaying to the [0.0, 1/2⁻] state of ²⁰⁷Pb, the calculated bound-state decay rate λ_B = 4.15 × 10⁻⁴ sec⁻¹ and continuum-state decay rate λ_C = 2.54 × 10⁻³ sec⁻¹, compared to experimental values of λ_B = 4.29(29) × 10⁻⁴ sec⁻¹ and λ_C = 2.29(12) × 10⁻³ sec⁻¹ from Ohtsubo et al.
* For the transition of ²²⁷Ac [0.0, 3/2⁻] to ²²⁷Th [37.9, 3/2⁻] with neutral-atom Q_n = 6.9 keV, the continuum Q-value Q_c becomes −13.1 keV, closing the continuum channel so that the total decay is governed solely by the bound-state channel.
* The ratio λ_B/λ_C for transitions in ¹¹⁵Cd, ¹²³Sn, ¹³⁶Cs, and ¹⁵²Eu follows a power-law fit λ_B/λ_C = a × (Q_n)^b, with exponents b ranging from −1.48 ± 0.02 to −1.73 ± 0.03 depending on the nucleus.
* For bare ¹³⁴Cs, the maximum λ_Bare occurs at Q_n = 88.8 keV (transition to the 1969.9 keV level of ¹³⁴Ba), whereas the maximum λ_Neutral occurs at Q_n = 658.1 keV (transition to the 1400.6 keV level), demonstrating a branching flip.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical studies of Takahashi and Yokoi (1980s) and Takahashi et al., which were based on older nuclear data and often omitted contributions from all available daughter energy levels. The authors provide updated calculations for a wider set of nuclei using modern experimental Q-values and branching data, enabling more reliable predictions for future storage-ring experiments such as those previously performed for ¹⁶³Dy by Jung et al. (1992) and ¹⁸⁷Re by Bosch et al. The identification of branching flips in bare atoms is a new result that may guide experimental verification and has implications for astrophysical s-process nucleosynthesis models where ionization states affect β⁻ decay rates.
