# Polarized proton+$^{4,6,8}$He elastic scattering with breakup effects in the eikonal approximation

> We study the elastic scattering of polarized protons from He isotopes. The central and spin-orbit parts of the optical potential are derived using the Glauber theory that can naturally take account of the breakup effect of the He isotopes. Both the differential cross section and the vector analyzing power for $p+^{4,6,8}$He scattering at 71 MeV are in reasonable agreement with experiment. Scattering observables at 300 MeV are predicted. The Pauli blocking effect is examined at 71 MeV.

## Key claim

The Glauber/eikonal model, using variational Monte Carlo wave functions for the He isotopes and a parametrized nucleon-nucleon profile function, can reproduce both differential cross sections and vector analyzing powers for p+⁴,⁶,⁸He elastic scattering at 71 MeV, but only after including a phenomenological Pauli-blocking correction with an adjusted strength parameter γ = 0.5 (rather than the theoretical value of unity) to bring the differential cross sections into agreement with experiment at forward angles.

## Method

The central part of the optical potential was derived from the eikonal phase shift function, which was calculated using the Glauber theory with ground-state wave functions of the He isotopes obtained from variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations using the Argonne v₁₈ two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. The spin-orbit potential was constructed as a derivative of the central potential with a strength parameter V_so that was adjusted to fit the vector analyzing power data. The nucleon-nucleon profile function was parametrized using free-space total cross sections, slope parameters, and real-to-imaginary ratios, with a phenomenological Pauli-blocking correction applied by reducing the total cross section according to a local-density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Proton root-mean-square radii from VMC wave functions: 1.447 fm for ⁴He, 1.928 fm for ⁶He, 1.884 fm for ⁸He; neutron rms radii: 1.447 fm for ⁴He, 2.871 fm for ⁶He, 2.901 fm for ⁸He
- Spin-orbit real potential strength V_so at 71 MeV: 0.125 for ⁴He, 0.025 for ⁶He, 0.05 for ⁸He; imaginary potential strength: −0.075 for ⁴He, 0.00 for ⁶He and ⁸He
- At 300 MeV for p+⁴He: V_so = 0.1 (real) and −0.05 (imaginary); at 550 MeV: V_so = 0.9 (real) and −0.025 (imaginary)
- Pauli-blocking correction used γ = 0.5 for all He isotopes, with the correction formula σ̅_pN^tot = σ_pN^tot (1 − γ × (7/5) × (E_F/E))

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of p+⁶,⁸He elastic scattering that failed to reproduce the vector analyzing powers, including the single-scattering approximation of Crespo (2005), the eikonal model analysis of Chulkov (2005), and the full-folding optical model of S. P. Weppner (2000). The present approach improves upon these by incorporating breakup effects of the weakly-bound He isotopes through the Glauber theory without explicit continuum discretization, and by using realistic VMC wave functions. The results enable predictions of scattering observables at 300 MeV for p+⁶He and p+⁸He, and demonstrate that the Pauli-blocking correction is essential at 71 MeV, while the knock-on exchange effect was found to be negligible for improving the p+⁴He cross section beyond 70 degrees.
