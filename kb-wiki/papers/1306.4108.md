# Quark mass dependence of the X(3872) binding energy

> We explore the quark-mass dependence of the pole position of the X(3872) state within the molecular picture. The calculations are performed within the framework of a nonrelativistic Faddeev-type three-body equation for the $D\bar{D}π$ system in the $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ channel. The $πD$ interaction is parametrised via a $D^*$ pole, and a three-body force is included to render the equations well defined. Its strength is adjusted such that the X(3872) appears as a $D\bar{D}^*$ bound state 0.5 MeV below the neutral threshold. We find that the trajectory of the X(3872) depends strongly on the assumed quark-mass dependence of the short-range interactions which can be determined in future lattice QCD calculations. At the same time we are able to provide nontrivial information on the chiral extrapolation in the $X$ channel.

## Key claim

The trajectory of the X(3872) binding energy as a function of quark mass depends strongly on the assumed quark-mass dependence of the short-range interactions, which is currently unknown and can only be determined by future lattice QCD calculations; the authors find that the pion mass dependence is highly nontrivial and can range from rapid disappearance of the bound state to increased binding as the pion mass increases.

## Method

The authors solve a nonrelativistic Faddeev-type three-body equation for the D Dbar pi system in the JPC = 1++ channel, treating the pi D interaction via a D* pole and including a three-body contact interaction C0 to render the equations well defined. The strength of the contact term is adjusted to produce the X(3872) as a D Dbar* bound state at 0.5 MeV below the neutral threshold at the physical pion mass. All masses, decay constants, and coupling constants are expressed as functions of the pion mass ratio xi = m_pi / m_pi^ph, and the contact interaction is expanded as C0(Lambda, xi) = C0^ph(Lambda) [1 + f(Lambda) (m_pi^2 - (m_pi^ph)^2) / M^2] where M ~ 800 MeV is the hard scale, with the function f(Lambda) determined by requiring the slope of the binding energy at the physical point to be cut-off independent.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) binding energy is fixed at 0.5 MeV below the neutral D0 Dbar*0 threshold at the physical pion mass
- Physical pion mass m_pi^ph = 134.98 MeV (neutral) and 139.57 MeV (charged)
- Physical D meson masses: m_0^ph = 1864.84 MeV, m_c^ph = 1869.62 MeV; D* meson masses: m_*0^ph = 2006.97 MeV, m_*c^ph = 2010.27 MeV
- The D*0 to D0 pi0 decay width at the physical point is Gamma_*^ph = 42 keV, giving the dimensionless coupling g_c^ph = 0.62
- The vertex coupling g^ph = 1.29 x 10^-5 MeV^-3/2
- The pion decay constant at the physical point f_pi^ph = 92.4 MeV, and in the chiral limit f = 85 MeV
- The D*0 to D0 gamma decay width at the physical point is Gamma_gamma^ph = 21 keV
- The allowed range for the slope of the binding energy at the physical point is (partial E_B / partial m_pi) at m_pi = m_pi^ph = (-2.2 to 0.7) x 10^-2
- The cut-off Lambda is varied in the range 400 to 700 MeV, with f(Lambda) considered natural in the range -5 to +5

## Context

This work extends the three-body Faddeev-type formalism developed by Baru et al. (2011) for the X(3872) to study its quark-mass dependence, providing the first systematic chiral extrapolation for this state. The authors contrast their approach with that of Wang et al. (2013), which claimed a parameter-free prediction for the pion mass dependence but which the authors argue is regularization-scheme dependent. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to distinguish between different binding mechanisms for the X(3872) by comparing the measured pion mass dependence of the binding energy with the scenarios presented here, which range from short-range-dominated to pion-exchange-dominated dynamics.
