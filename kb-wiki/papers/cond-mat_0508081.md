# Temperature-dependent gap equations and their solutions in the SU(4) model of high-temperature superconductivity

> Temperature-dependent gap equations in the SU(4) model of high-Tc superconductivity are derived and analytical solutions are obtained. Based on these solutions, a generic gap diagram describing the features of energy gaps as functions of doping P is presented and a phase diagram illustrating the phase structure as a function of temperature T and doping P is sketched. A special doping point P_q occurs naturally in the solutions that separates two phases at temperature T = 0: a pure superconducting phase on one side (P > P_q) and a phase with superconductivity strongly suppressed by antiferromagnetism on the other (P < P_q). We interpret P_q as a quantum phase transition point. Moreover, the pairing gap is found to have two solutions for P < P_q: a small gap that is associated with competition between superconductivity and antiferromagnetism and is responsible for the ground state superconductivity, and a large gap without antiferromagnetic suppression that corresponds to a collective excited state. A pseudogap appears in the solutions that terminates at P_q and originates from the competition between d-wave superconductivity and antiferromagnetism. Nevertheless, this conclusion does not contradict the preformed pair picture conceptually if the preformed pairs are generally defined as any pairs formed before pairing condensation.

## Key claim

The SU(4) model predicts a quantum phase transition point \(P_q\) that separates two distinct phases at zero temperature: a pure superconducting phase for \(P > P_q\) and a phase where superconductivity is strongly suppressed by antiferromagnetism for \(P < P_q\), with the pseudogap originating from competition between d-wave superconductivity and antiferromagnetism rather than from preformed pairs.

## Method

The authors derived temperature-dependent gap equations within the SU(4) dynamical symmetry model of high-temperature superconductivity using a generalized coherent-state method, which implements a Bogoliubov-type transformation constrained by SU(4) symmetry. They obtained analytical solutions for the gap equations at zero temperature and finite temperature, assuming the interaction strengths satisfy \(\chi > G_0 > G_1 > 0\) (antiferromagnetic correlation strongest, spin-triplet pairing weakest). The solutions were used to construct generic gap diagrams and phase diagrams as functions of doping \(P\) and temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- Critical doping point: \(x_q = \sqrt{(\chi - G_0)/(\chi - G_1)}\), which separates two doping regimes at \(T=0\)
- Maximum doping fraction consistent with SU(4) symmetry: \(P_{\mbox{\scriptsize f}} = \Omega/\Omega_e = 1/4\) (0.25), with empirical maximum doping for cuprates found to be 0.23 to 0.27
- For \(x \le x_q\) (all-gap solution): \(\Delta_q = (\chi\Omega/2)\sqrt{(x_q^{-1} - x)(x_q - x)}\), \(\Delta_d = (G_0\Omega/2)\sqrt{x(x_q^{-1} - x)}\), \(\Delta_\pi = (G_1\Omega/2)\sqrt{x(x_q - x)}\)
- For pure spin-singlet pairing solution (valid for \(0 \le x \le 1\)): \(\Delta_0 \equiv \Delta_d = (G_0\Omega/2)\sqrt{1 - x^2}\)

## Context

This work extends the SU(4) dynamical symmetry model previously developed by the same authors (Gu 1999, lawu99, wu03) to include temperature-dependent gap equations and analytical solutions. The model provides a unified description of antiferromagnetism and d-wave superconductivity consistent with Mott-insulator properties, and the results enable construction of phase diagrams for cuprate superconductors. The authors note that the pseudogap termination at \(P_q\) does not contradict the preformed pair picture (Timusk 1999) if preformed pairs are defined generally as any pairs formed before pairing condensation.
