# Nonresonant explanation for the Y(4260) structure observed in the $e^+e^-\to J/ψπ^+π^-$ process

> In this work, we proposed a nonresonant explanation for the Y(4260) structure observed in the $e^+e^-\to J/ψπ^+π^-$ process, i.e., Y(4260) is not a genuine resonance. Our result indicates that the Y(4260) structure can be reproduced by the interference of production amplitudes of the $e^+e^-\to J/ψπ^+π^-$ processes via direct $e^+e^-$ annihilation and through intermediate charmonia $ψ(4160)/ψ(4415)$. Besides describing Y(4260) line shape in $e^+e^-\to J/ψπ^+π^-$ well, such a nonresonant explanation for the Y(4260) structure naturally answers why there is no evidence of Y(4260) in the exclusive open-charm decay channel and $R$-value scan.

# Nonresonant explanation for the Y(4260) structure observed in the e+e- → J/ψ π+π- process

## Key claim

The Y(4260) structure is not a genuine resonance but can be reproduced by the interference between the direct production amplitude of e+e- → J/ψ π+π- via e+e- annihilation and the amplitudes from intermediate charmonia ψ(4160) and ψ(4415). This nonresonant explanation naturally accounts for why Y(4260) is observed only in hidden-charm decay channels and not in open-charm decays or R-value scans.

## Method

The authors constructed the total production amplitude for e+e- → J/ψ π+π- as the coherent sum of a direct production term (e+e- annihilation directly into J/ψ π+π-) and terms from intermediate ψ(4160) and ψ(4415) charmonia decaying via hadronic loops of charmed mesons (D, D*, D*D, D*D*) into J/ψ plus a scalar meson (σ or f0(980)). The hadronic loop contributions were calculated using effective Lagrangians for charmonium-charmed-meson interactions and vector meson dominance for the γ → c-cbar coupling, with monopole form factors and s-dependent form factors to regulate the loop integrals. The free parameters (phase angles φ1, φ2, φs, coupling gNoR, parameter a, and β1, β2) were fitted to the BaBar experimental data using a binned maximum likelihood fit with the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers

- Mass of ψ(4160): 4191.7 MeV; width: 71.8 MeV (input resonance parameters from Ablikim (2007))
- Mass of ψ(4415): 4415.1 MeV; width: 71.5 MeV (input resonance parameters from Ablikim (2007))
- Mass of σ: 526 MeV; width: 302 MeV (input resonance parameters)
- Mass of f0(980): 980 MeV; width: 61 MeV (input resonance parameters)
- Coupling constant gσππ = -0.02 ± 0.002 MeV^-1 (from Hooft (2008))
- Coupling constant gf0ππ = -0.00354 ± 0.0017 MeV^-1 (from Hooft (2008))
- Fitted parameter gNoR = 0.0967 ± 0.0280 GeV
- Fitted parameter a = 0.7341 ± 0.0678 GeV^-2
- Fitted phase φ1 = 0.4545 ± 0.3535 Rad
- Fitted phase φ2 = -0.9789 ± 0.5146 Rad
- Fitted phase φs = 1.5983 ± 1.0922 Rad
- β1 = β2 = 1 (fixed in fit)
- J/ψ-DD coupling constant: 7.71
- ψ(4160)-DD coupling constant: 1.86
- ψ(4415)-DD coupling constant: 0.21
- αψ(4160) for DD channel: 0.70 GeV^-2
- αψ(4415) for DD channel: 0.45 GeV^-2

## Context

This work extends the nonresonant explanation for Y(4260) suggested by van Beveren (2009, 2010), who proposed Y(4260) as a phenomenon connected with the Ds* Ds* threshold opening and coupling to J/ψ f0(980) and J/ψ σ channels. The paper contrasts with prior exotic explanations (charmonium hybrid by Zhu (2005), diquark-antidiquark state by Maiani (2005), molecular states by Liu (2005) and Yuan (2005), baryonium by Qiao (2005)) and conventional charmonium assignments (4S-3D mixing by Llanes-Estrada (2005), 2^3D1 c-cbar by Eichten (2005)). The key advantage claimed is that the interference mechanism naturally explains the absence of Y(4260) in open-charm channels and R-value scans, which posed a serious challenge to both exotic and conventional charmonium explanations. The authors suggest extending this model to other charmoniumlike X, Y, Z states observed in hidden-charm decay channels from e+e- annihilation.
