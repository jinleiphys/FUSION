# Quasi-stationary sequences of hyper massive neutron stars with exotic equations of state

> In this work, we study the effect of differential rotation, finite temperature and strangeness on the quasi stationary sequences of hyper massive neutron stars (HMNS). We generate constant rest mass sequences of differentially rotating and uniformly rotating stars. The nucleonic matter relevant to the star interior is described within the framework of the relativistic mean field model with the DD2 parameter set. We also consider the strange $Λ$ hyperons using the BHB$Λϕ$ equation of state (EoS). Additionally, we probe the behaviour of neutron stars (NS) with these compositions at different temperatures. We report that the addition of hyperons to the EoS produces a significant boost to the spin-up phenomenon. Moreover, increasing the temperature can make the spin-up more robust. We also study the impact of strangeness and thermal effects on the T/W instability. Finally, we analyse equilibrium sequences of a NS following a stable transition from differential rotation to uniform rotation. The decrease in frequency relative to angular momentum loss during this transition is significantly smaller for EoS containing hyperons, compared to nucleonic EoS.

## Key claim

The addition of $\Lambda$ hyperons to the equation of state produces a significant boost to the spin-up phenomenon in hyper massive neutron stars, and increasing the temperature can make the spin-up more robust; however, these effects are conditional on the specific differential rotation law (j-constant law with parameter $a=0.2$) and the particular relativistic mean field models (DD2 and BHB$\Lambda\phi$) used.

## Method

The authors generated constant rest mass sequences of differentially rotating and uniformly rotating neutron stars using the LORENE library, solving for equilibrium configurations with a self-consistent-field method. The nucleonic matter was described by the relativistic mean field model with the DD2 parameter set, and strange matter included $\Lambda$ hyperons via the BHB$\Lambda\phi$ equation of state, with both cold (T=0) and finite temperature (constant entropy per baryon s=2) variants. The j-constant rotation law was employed with differential rotation parameter $a=0.2$, restricting calculations to spheroidal class (Type A) solutions that possess a mass-shedding limit.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for cold DD2 differentially rotating Kepler star: $3.05 M_{sol}$, with central density $9.99 \rho_{nuc}c^2$, rotation frequency $1783.25$ Hz, and T/W = 0.16
- Maximum gravitational mass for cold BHB$\Lambda\phi$ differentially rotating Kepler star: $2.63 M_{sol}$, with central density $10.37 \rho_{nuc}c^2$, rotation frequency $1573.40$ Hz, and T/W = 0.14
- For a cold DD2 star with baryon mass $3.11 M_{sol}$ transitioning from differential to uniform rotation: frequency change $\Delta f_c = 79.58$ Hz and angular momentum change $\Delta J = 0.45 GM_{sol}^2/c$
- For a cold BHB$\Lambda\phi$ star with baryon mass $2.89 M_{sol}$ transitioning from differential to uniform rotation: frequency change $\Delta f_c = 7.73$ Hz and angular momentum change $\Delta J = 0.42 GM_{sol}^2/c$
- For a hot (s=2) DD2 star with baryon mass $3.11 M_{sol}$ transitioning from differential to uniform rotation: frequency change $\Delta f_c = 43.61$ Hz and angular momentum change $\Delta J = 0.33 GM_{sol}^2/c$
- Maximum T/W ratio for cold DD2 differentially rotating star: 0.16; for cold BHB$\Lambda\phi$ differentially rotating star: 0.14

## Context

This work extends previous studies of supramassive and hyper massive neutron stars by Cook (1994) and Zdunik (2004) to include the combined effects of differential rotation, finite temperature, and strangeness. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star merger remnants, where temperatures can reach $\sim 50$ MeV and densities 3-5 times nuclear saturation density, as studied by Perego (2019) and Bernuzzi (2020). The finding that hyperons boost spin-up and that the quasi-stationary stability limit can be fit with a polynomial function provides input for future simulations of post-merger evolution and gravitational wave signatures.
