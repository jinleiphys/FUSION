# Role of Deuteron NN^* -components in proceses pd - dp and pd- dN^*

> The contribution of nucleon isobar $N^*$ exchanges to backward elastic pd-scattering is calculated on the basis of deuteron 6q-model and found to be negligible in comparison with the neutron exchange. It is shown that the pole amplitude of neutron pickup from the deuteron $nN^*$-component is favoured in the reaction $pd\to dN^*$ for backward going $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1710)$ at kinetic energy of incident proton of 1.5--2 GeV whereas the triangular diagram with subprocess $pp\to dπ^+$ related to the usual $pn-$component of deuteron is considerable suppressed.

## Key claim

The contribution of nucleon isobar $N^*$ exchanges to backward elastic $pd\to dp$ scattering, calculated using the deuteron 6-quark model, is negligible compared to the neutron exchange mechanism; however, the pole amplitude of neutron pickup from the deuteron $nN^*$-component is favored in the reaction $pd\to dN^*$ for backward going $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1710)$ at incident proton kinetic energies of 1.5-2 GeV, provided the triangular diagram with subprocess $pp\to d\pi^+$ (related to the usual $pn$-component of the deuteron) is kinematically suppressed.

## Method

The author calculated the contribution of $N^*$ exchanges to backward elastic $pd\to dp$ scattering using the 6-quark model of the deuteron for $dNN^*$ vertices, with the Paris wave function for the $np$-component and its RGM modification for the $NN^*$-component. For the $pd\to dN^*$ reaction, the neutron exchange (NE) pole diagram and the one-pion exchange (OPE) triangular diagram were evaluated, with rescatterings in the initial and final states accounted for in the eikonal approximation. The spin-averaged squared amplitudes were computed using a phenomenological relativistic approach for the three-body problem, and the tensor polarization $T_{20}$ of the final deuteron was derived for the NE mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The total contribution of $N^*$-states (positive and negative parity) to the $pd\to dp$ cross section is smaller than the neutron exchange contribution by a factor of more than 30.
- In the energy interval $T_p = 0.5 - 1$ GeV, the p-wave $N^*$ contribution increases the OBE cross section by a factor of approximately 1.3 due to interference with the neutron exchange amplitude.
- The effective number for the $NN^*(1710)$ component in the deuteron is $2\,N_{NN(1710)}^d = 6.75 \times 10^{-3}$; for the $NN^*(1440)$ component it is $2\,N_{NN(1440)}^d = 10^{-3}$.
- The tensor polarization $T_{20}$ of the final deuteron in the $pd\to dN^*$ reaction at $T_p = 1-3$ GeV is approximately 0.6-0.7 for both $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1710)$.
- The OPE mechanism predicts a deep minimum in the cross section of $pd\to dN^*(1440)$ at the proton energy $T_p = 1.876$ GeV, corresponding to the rest point of $N^*(1440)$.
- The rest point in the laboratory system for $N^*(1440)$ is at $T_p = 1.876$ GeV; for $N^*(1535)$ at 2.75 GeV; for $N^*(1710)$ at 6.86 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the 6-quark model approach to deuteron structure developed by Glozman et al. (1990s) and Kobuchkin et al. (1990s), which previously explained inclusive deuteron disintegration data via $n+N^*$-exchange. The calculations contradict earlier suggestions by Kerman and Kisslinger (1960s) and others based on Regge phenomenology that $NN^*$-components were essential to explain $pd\to dp$ data near 1 GeV. The results enable experimental searches for the $NN^*$-component of the deuteron via the $pd\to dN^*$ reaction at SATURNE, where the NE mechanism can be isolated from the OPE background in the 1.5-2 GeV energy range for $N^*(1440)$ and $N^*(1710)$.
