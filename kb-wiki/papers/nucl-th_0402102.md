# Dilepton production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions

> We present a unified description of the vector meson and dilepton production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions. The production of vector mesons ($ρ,ω,ϕ$) is described via the excitation of nucleon resonances ($R$). The theoretical framework is an extended vector meson dominance model (eVMD) for resonance decays $R\longmapsto NV$ with arbitrary spin which is covariant and kinematically complete. The eVMD includes thereby excited vector meson states in the transition form factors. The model has successfully been applied to $ω$ and $ϕ$ production in $p+p$ reactions. The same model is used to describe the dilepton production in elementary reactions where corresponding data are well reproduced. However, when the model is applied to heavy ion reactions in the BEVALAC/SIS energy range the experimental dilepton spectra measured by the DLS Collaboration are significantly underestimated at small invariant masses. In view of this fact we discuss further medium effects: One is a substantial collisional broadening of the $ρ$ and in particular of the $ω$ meson in the vicinity of the $ρ/ω$-peak. The second medium effect is the destruction of quantum interference in a dense medium. A decoherent dilepton emission through vector mesons decays enhances the corresponding low mass dilepton yield in heavy ion reactions and improves the agreement with existing data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the DLS puzzle (the underestimation of low-mass dilepton yields in heavy ion reactions at BEVALAC/SIS energies) can be at least partially resolved by two medium effects: collisional broadening of the rho and omega mesons, and destruction of quantum interference in dense matter, but the authors acknowledge that the comparison with data is "not completely conclusive" and that definitive conclusions require more precise data from HADES.

## Method

The authors use an extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model for resonance decays R -> N V with arbitrary spin, including excited vector meson states in the transition form factors. Vector meson production in elementary reactions is described via excitation of nucleon resonances, and the reaction dynamics of heavy ion collisions is simulated using the QMD transport model with a complete set of baryonic resonances (Delta and N*) with masses below 2 GeV. The model is applied to C+C and Ca+Ca reactions at 1.04 AGeV, with acceptance filter functions from the DLS Collaboration applied to the results.

## Key numbers

* The DLS dilepton spectra in C+C and Ca+Ca at 1.04 AGeV are underestimated by a factor of 2-3 in the low mass region (M = 0.1-0.5 GeV) without medium effects
* The omega meson vacuum width is 8.4 MeV, while in-medium values up to 400 MeV are tested; best fits require an in-medium omega width above 50 MeV
* Best agreement with DLS data for Ca+Ca is obtained with total rho width of 300 MeV and total omega width of 100-300 MeV
* The average nuclear density in minimal bias 1 AGeV Ca+Ca reactions is about 1.5 times saturation density, corresponding to <rho_B> = 0.24 fm^{-3} for Ca+Ca and 0.20 fm^{-3} for C+C
* The experimental resolution for the DLS data is Delta M = 35 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ernst et al. and Bratkovskaja et al. that also found underestimation of DLS data, but the present approach includes significantly more decay channels and an improved description of baryonic resonance decays using the eVMD framework. The paper addresses the DLS puzzle, which contrasts with the situation at CERN SPS energies where CERES and HELIOS Collaborations observed low-mass dilepton enhancement that could be explained by dropping rho mass or in-medium spectral functions. The results enable future comparisons with more precise data from the HADES experiment, and the extracted in-medium widths can be used to constrain models of vector meson properties in nuclear matter.
