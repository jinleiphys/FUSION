# Realistic Implementation of Massive Yang Mills for rho and a1 Mesons

> We revisit the massive Yang-Mills approach to implement axial-/vector mesons into the chiral pion Lagrangian. Employing the non-linear realization of chiral symmetry, we compute vector and axial-vector spectral functions. This includes, for the first time, a resummation of the $ρ$ propagator in the $a_1$ selfenergy while maintaining a partially conserved axial-vector current (PCAC). A realistic $ρ$ propagator, combined with a recent idea of a chirally invariant continuum in the vector and axial-vector channels, turns out be critical to obtain a quantitative description of hadronic $τ$-decay data which has not been achieved before in local-gauge approaches to the chiral lagrangian. The thus obtained model provides a realistic basis to rigorously address the long-standing question of chiral symmetry restoration in the context of dilepton data in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the massive Yang-Mills (MYM) approach can quantitatively describe hadronic tau-decay data for vector and axial-vector spectral functions, contrary to previous claims, but only when a resummed rho propagator in the a1 selfenergy and a chirally invariant continuum are both included.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-minimal massive Yang-Mills Lagrangian with nonlinear chiral symmetry, computed vector and axial-vector spectral functions at one-loop order, and implemented a resummation of the dressed rho propagator in the a1 selfenergy while preserving partial conservation of the axial-vector current (PCAC) through specifically identified vertex correction diagrams. They supplemented the hadronic model with a chirally invariant perturbative continuum parameterized by a Fermi-type function, and fitted the resulting spectral functions to experimental tau-decay data from ALEPH.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of peak heights between axial-vector and vector spectral functions from tau-decay data is approximately 1/3; the bare MYM prediction is too low by a factor of about sqrt(2)
- Fitted parameters: g_{rho pi pi} = 6.01, g_{rho pi pi}^{(3)} = 0.02 GeV^{-2}, bare rho mass M_rho = 0.86 GeV, bare a1 mass M_{a1} = 1.20 GeV
- Continuum parameters: alpha_s = 0.5, E_0 = 1.5 GeV, delta = 0.2 GeV
- Calculated radiative a1 decay width Gamma_{a1 -> rho gamma} = 244 keV, compared to experimental value 640 +/- 246 keV from proton-nucleus collisions
- D/S ratio for a1 decay = -0.131 evaluated at sqrt(s) = 1.23 GeV
- PCAC satisfied at the sub-0.1% level, with discrepancy scaling with M_pi^2

## Context

This work extends the massive Yang-Mills approach of Gomm (1984) and Song (1993) by resolving long-standing problems in describing axial-vector spectral functions from tau-decay data (Barate 1998, Ackerstaff 1998). Previous attempts either abandoned the local gauge implementation in favor of global approaches (Urban 2001, Parganlija 2010) or treated the a1 as dynamically generated from rho-pi interactions (Wagner 2008). The model provides a realistic basis for studying chiral symmetry restoration in dilepton data from heavy-ion collisions, building on the phenomenological continuum idea of Hohler (2012).
