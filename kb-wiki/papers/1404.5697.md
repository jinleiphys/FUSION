# A Novel Mechanism for $J/ψ$ Disintegration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper we discuss the possibility of $J/ψ$ disintegration due the $Z(3)$ domain walls that are expected to form in QGP medium. These domain walls give rise to localised color electric field which disintegrates $J/ψ$, on interaction, by changing the color composition and simultaneously exciting it to higher states of $c\bar{c}$ system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that $J/\psi$ mesons can be disintegrated by interaction with $Z(3)$ domain walls expected to form in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium, where the domain wall's localized color electric field excites the $c\bar{c}$ system to a color octet state that is unbound. The authors note that this mechanism is presented as an additional source of $J/\psi$ suppression beyond conventional Debye screening, and they acknowledge that the existence of $Z(3)$ domains in the presence of dynamical quarks remains a non-trivial issue, with the paper ignoring explicit symmetry breaking effects from dynamical quarks for simplicity.

## Method

The authors model the interaction of a $J/\psi$ meson with a $Z(3)$ domain wall by treating the wall's Polyakov loop profile as a background gauge field $A_0$ in the diagonal gauge, using Gell-Mann matrices $\lambda_3$ and $\lambda_8$. They apply first-order time-dependent perturbation theory in the $J/\psi$ rest frame, computing transition amplitudes from the initial color singlet $J/\psi$ (with $l=0$) to color octet $\chi$ states (with $l=1$), using a non-relativistic potential model for the $c\bar{c}$ system with Coulomb plus linear confining terms. The radial wave functions are obtained numerically via the Numerov method and energy minimization, and the transition probability is integrated over the domain wall profile at a temperature of 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass $m_c = 1.28$ GeV, strong coupling $\alpha_s = \pi/12$, string tension $\sigma = 0.16$ GeV$^2$.
- Bound state energy (excluding rest mass) for $J/\psi$ ($l=0$): $E_0 = 0.447$ GeV; for $\chi$ state ($l=1$): $E_0 = 0.803$ GeV.
- Radius of $J/\psi$ wave function: approximately $0.5$ fm; radius of $\chi$ state: approximately $0.8$ fm.
- Domain wall thickness at $T = 400$ MeV: approximately $1.5$ fm; Debye radius in QGP at $T = 200$ MeV: $r_d \sim 0.6$ fm.
- Largest velocity considered for $J/\psi$ before first-order perturbation becomes unreliable: $v/c = 0.20$ to $0.24$.
- Suppression factor for $A_3'$ perturbation relative to $A_0'$ perturbation: of order $10\%$ for $r_{J/\psi} \simeq 0.4$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of $Z(3)$ domain walls in QGP, previously discussed in the context of baryon inhomogeneity generation (Layek 2005) and spontaneous CP violation in quark/antiquark scattering (Atreya 2011), to the specific case of quarkonia disintegration. The mechanism is presented as an alternative to the conventional Debye screening suppression of $J/\psi$ proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), which has not provided a clean QGP signal due to other contributing factors. The authors suggest that the strong $P_T$ dependence of the disintegration probability could distinguish this mechanism from conventional suppression, and note that the same mechanism would apply to Upsilon mesons at higher temperatures, to be studied in future work.
