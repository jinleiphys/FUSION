# The Kohn Variational Principle for Elastic Proton--Deuteron Scattering above Deuteron Breakup Threshold

> The Kohn variational principle is formulated for calculating elastic proton-deuteron scattering amplitudes at energies above the deuteron breakup threshold. The use of such a principle with an expansion of the wave function on the (correlated) hyperspherical harmonic basis is discussed.

## Key claim

The Kohn variational principle (KVP) can be formulated for calculating elastic proton-deuteron scattering amplitudes at energies above the deuteron breakup threshold, provided the trial wave functions satisfy "simplified" boundary conditions that account for the long-range Coulomb distortion between the two protons in the asymptotic three-nucleon outgoing wave.

## Method

The authors derive the asymptotic behavior of the proton-deuteron scattering wave function by analyzing the Faddeev-Noble equations in three distinct asymptotic regions: the breakup region (1+1+1), the transition regions, and the 2+1 regions where the deuteron is intact. They then prove that the Kohn variational principle remains valid when the trial wave function is expanded on a pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis, with the boundary conditions modified to include Coulomb distortion effects through functions that smoothly interpolate between the breakup and elastic asymptotic forms.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb parameter for the elastic channel is defined as \(\eta_0 = me^2/(2\hbar^2 q_0)\), where \(q_0\) is the wave number satisfying \(-B_d + (1/\kappa^2)(\hbar^2/m) q_0^2 = E\).
- The characteristic distance below which the two-proton Coulomb repulsion suppresses the breakup amplitude is \(\rho_c' \approx 60\) fm, derived from \(2\hbar^2/(me^2)\).
- The transition region exponent \(\mu\) for the p-d case is approximately \(3/4\), chosen in accordance with the condition \(x_i \gg \rho_c \equiv (m^2 e^4 \rho^3 / (4\hbar^4 Q^3))^{1/4}\).
- For the n-d case, the transition region exponent is \(\mu \approx 1/2\), based on the condition \(x_i \gg \rho_n \equiv (\rho/Q)^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Kohn variational principle, previously applied successfully to n-d scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold by Kievsky, Rosati, and Viviani (1994, 1996), to the p-d case above breakup where Coulomb repulsion between the two protons complicates the asymptotic wave function. The approach provides an alternative to solving Faddeev equations with Coulomb corrections as done by Alt, Sandhas, and collaborators (1978, 1994, 1996) and by Papp (1999) or Chen (1999), and enables accurate calculations of p-d elastic observables at energies where high-precision experimental data exist, particularly for polarization observables like \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) that show disagreements with theoretical predictions.
