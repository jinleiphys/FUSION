# $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O molecular structures of positive- and negative-parity superdeformed bands in $^{34}$S

> The structures of excited states in $^{34}$S are investigated using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics and generator coordinate method (GCM). The GCM basis wave functions are calculated via energy variation with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $β$. By applying the GCM after parity and angular momentum projections, the coexistence of two positive- and one negative-parity superdeformed (SD) bands are predicted, and low-lying states and other deformed bands are obtained. The SD bands have structures of $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O + two valence neutrons in molecular orbitals around the two $^{16}$O cores in a cluster picture. The configurations of the two valence neutrons are $δ^2$ and $π^2$ for the positive-parity SD bands and $π^1δ^1$ for the negative-parity SD band. The structural changes of the yrast states are also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the coexistence of two positive-parity and one negative-parity superdeformed (SD) bands in $^{34}$S, interpreted as $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O + two valence neutrons in molecular orbitals, but the prediction is based on theoretical calculations using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics and the generator coordinate method, not on experimental observation.

## Method
The authors performed energy variation with a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta$ using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave functions with the Gogny D1S effective interaction. They then applied parity projection and angular momentum projection followed by the generator coordinate method (GCM) to superpose the optimized wave functions, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain energy spectra and wave functions for $^{34}$S.

## Key numbers
- The $B(\mathrm{E2})$ value for the $8^+_1$ (10.650 MeV) $\rightarrow$ $6^+$ (8.503 MeV) transition is $27 \pm 15~B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ experimentally, where $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2}) = 6.54~\mathrm{e}^2\mathrm{fm}^4$
- Theoretical in-band $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the $K^\pi = 0_\mathrm{SD1}^+$ band range from 110.63 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($2_\mathrm{SD1}^+ \rightarrow 0_\mathrm{SD1}^+$) to 168.96 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($6_\mathrm{SD1}^+ \rightarrow 4_\mathrm{SD1}^+$)
- Theoretical in-band $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the $K^\pi = 0_\mathrm{SD2}^+$ band range from 152.02 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($2_\mathrm{SD2}^+ \rightarrow 0_\mathrm{SD2}^+$) to 238.99 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($6_\mathrm{SD2}^+ \rightarrow 4_\mathrm{SD2}^+$)
- Theoretical in-band $B(\mathrm{E2})$ values for the $K^\pi = 4_\mathrm{SD}^-$ band include 175.51 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($5_\mathrm{SD}^- \rightarrow 4_\mathrm{SD}^-$) and 181.14 $B_{\mathrm{Wu}}(\mathrm{E2})$ ($6_\mathrm{SD}^- \rightarrow 5_\mathrm{SD}^-$)
- The particle-hole configurations change at $\beta = 0.36$, 0.52, and 0.7 for positive-parity states, and at $\beta = 0.4$ and 0.6 for negative-parity states

## Context
This work extends the study of superdeformation and clustering in sulfur isotopes, building on prior mean-field calculations by Inakura (2003) and AMD studies of $^{32}$S by Kimura (2005) and Taniguchi (2008). The paper draws an analogy to $^{10}$Be, where structures of $\alpha$ + $\alpha$ + valence neutrons in molecular orbitals are known from work by Seya (1981), Kanada-En'yo (1999), and Ito (2004). The predicted SD bands in $^{34}$S with $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O + valence neutron configurations enable future $\gamma$ spectroscopy experiments on high-spin states ($J > 12$) to test the coexistence of positive- and negative-parity SD bands and the dramatic structural changes in yrast states.
