# A relativistic quark model with infrared confinement and the tetraquark state

> We explore the consequences of treating the X(3872) meson as a tetraquark bound state. As dynamical framework we employ a relativistic constituent quark model which includes infrared confinement in an effective way. We calculate the hadronic decay widths of the observed channels. For reasonable values of the size parameter of the X(3872) we find consistency with the available experimental data.

## Key claim
The X(3872) meson can be consistently described as a tetraquark bound state within a relativistic constituent quark model that includes infrared confinement, provided the mixing angle between isospin states is approximately ±18.4° and the size parameter Λ_X is varied between 3 and 4 GeV.

## Method
The authors employed a relativistic constituent quark model with infrared confinement implemented via a cut-off parameter on the Schwinger parameter integration, which removes all quark production thresholds. They calculated the hadronic decay widths for X(3872) → J/ψ + 2π (3π) via intermediate off-shell ρ and ω mesons, and X(3872) → D⁰D̄⁰π⁰ via intermediate off-shell D and D* mesons, using a nonlocal four-quark interpolating current with Gaussian vertex functions. The coupling constant was determined from the compositeness condition Z_X = 0, and the mixing angle between X_u and X_d tetraquark states was fitted to the experimental ratio of three-pion to two-pion decay modes.

## Key numbers
- The ratio Γ(X_u → J/ψ + 3π) / Γ(X_u → J/ψ + 2π) ≈ 0.25, stable under variations of Λ_X
- Mixing angle θ ≈ ±18.4° for X_l ("+") and X_h ("-"), respectively
- Decay width Γ(X → J/ψ + nπ) decreases from 0.30 MeV to 0.07 MeV as Λ_X varies from 3 to 4 GeV
- Decay width Γ(X_l → D⁰D̄⁰π⁰) decreases from 1.88 MeV to 0.41 MeV as Λ_X varies from 3 to 4 GeV
- Theoretical ratio Γ(X → D⁰D̄⁰π⁰) / Γ(X → J/ψπ⁺π⁻) = 6.0 ± 0.2, compared to experimental ratio 10.5 ± 4.7

## Context
This work extends the tetraquark interpretation of X(3872) originally proposed by Maiani et al. (2004) by providing a dynamical calculation within a relativistic quark model with infrared confinement, previously developed by Branz et al. (2009). The results are consistent with the experimental bound Γ(X(3872)) ≤ 2.3 MeV and with the mixing angle values found by Maiani et al. (2004) (≈ ±20°) and Navarra et al. (2006) (≈ ±23.5°). The approach enables further calculations of tetraquark properties and decay widths within a framework that avoids unphysical quark production thresholds.
