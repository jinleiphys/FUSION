# Bayesian inference of the path-length dependence of jet energy loss

> A simple model for medium modification of the jet function can be used to extract the jet energy loss distribution through a parameterized form. We carry out a comprehensive Bayesian analysis of the world data on single inclusive jet spectra in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. We extract the average jet energy loss $\langle ΔE\rangle$ as a function of jet transverse momentum $p_T$ for each collision system and centrality independently. Assuming jet energy loss is proportional to the initial parton density $ρ\sim dN_{\rm ch}/dη/πR_{\rm eff}^2$ as estimated from the pseudorapidity density of charged hadron multiplicity $dN_{\rm ch}/dη$ and the effective system size $R_{\rm eff}\sim N_{\rm part}^{1/3}$ given by the number of participant nucleons $N_{\rm part}$, the scaled average jet energy loss $\langle ΔE\rangle/ρ\sim R_{\rm eff}^{0.59} p_T^{0.13}\ln p_T $ for jet cone-size $R=0.4$ is found to have a momentum dependence that is slightly stronger than a logarithmic form while the system size or length dependence is slower than a linear one. The fluctuation of jet energy loss is, however, independent of the initial parton density or the system size.

## Key claim

The average jet energy loss extracted from single inclusive jet suppression data, when scaled by the initial parton density, shows a momentum dependence slightly stronger than logarithmic (∝ p_T^{0.13} ln p_T) and a system-size dependence slower than linear (∝ R_eff^{0.59}) for jet cone-size R=0.4, while the fluctuation of jet energy loss is independent of the initial parton density or system size.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to extract the jet energy loss distribution from experimental data on the nuclear modification factor R_AA of single inclusive jet spectra. They assumed a parameterized form for the average jet energy loss ⟨ΔE⟩ = β_i (p_T/p_Tref)^{γ_i} ln(p_T/p_Tref) and a Gamma distribution for the energy loss fluctuations, then independently calibrated these parameters for each collision system and centrality class using data from ATLAS, ALICE, and STAR experiments at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The scaled average jet energy loss for jet cone-size R=0.4 follows ⟨ΔE⟩/ρ ∼ R_eff^{0.59} p_T^{0.13} ln p_T
- The jet energy loss fluctuation parameter α ≈ 5.0 ± 3, independent of system size and colliding energy
- The analysis covers jet transverse momentum ranges: 15-30 GeV/c at RHIC (√s=200 GeV) and 50-800 GeV/c at LHC (√s=2.76 and 5.02 TeV)
- The system size dependence exponent is 0.59, extracted from data excluding peripheral collisions with ⟨N_part⟩^{1/3} < 3.3
- The power-law fit to the scaled energy loss versus system size gives exponent 0.557 for the same peripheral exclusion

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference approach of He (2018) to a comprehensive analysis of world data on single inclusive jet suppression across multiple collision systems and centralities at both RHIC and LHC energies. The extracted weaker-than-linear system size dependence (exponent 0.59 versus the linear dependence predicted by Gyulassy (2001) for single parton energy loss in expanding systems) is consistent with linear Boltzmann transport model simulations by He (2018) that account for energy recovery from radiated gluons and medium response within the jet cone. The results enable systematic tests of jet energy loss models and their scaling with medium density and path length.
