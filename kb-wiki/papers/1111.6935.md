# On the Origin of the Trigger-Angle Dependence of the Ridge Structure

> The origin of the trigger-angle dependence of the ridge structure in two-hadron long-range correlations, as observed at RHIC, is discussed as due to an interplay between the elliptic flow caused by the initial state global geometry and flow produced by fluctuations.

## Key claim

The trigger-angle dependence of the ridge structure in two-hadron long-range correlations arises from an interplay between elliptic flow from the initial-state global geometry and flow produced by fluctuations, specifically because multiplicity fluctuations cause the background elliptic flow to contribute to the correlation in a way that depends on the trigger angle.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic code, which combines event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from the NEXUS event generator with the SPheRIO hydrodynamical code, to compute two-particle correlations for non-central collisions (20-60% centrality) as a function of the trigger angle relative to the event plane. They also developed a simplified boost-invariant one-tube analytical model where a peripheral tube is placed on top of an elliptically-shaped background energy-density distribution, parameterizing the particle distribution with Fourier coefficients and computing proper and mixed-event correlations to isolate the mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The ridge structure changes continuously from one-broad-peak structure at trigger angle phi_S = 0 (in-plane trigger) to double-peak structure at phi_S = pi/2 (out-of-plane trigger), with the change particularly manifest for larger transverse momentum of the associated particle
- The analytical model uses Fourier coefficients v_2^b for the background and v_n^t (n=2,3) for the tube contribution, with the background contribution in the correlation having opposite sign for in-plane versus out-of-plane triggers due to the change in trigger angle from 0 to pi/2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamic studies of the ridge structure (Hama et al., 2009-2011) which used the NeXSPheRIO code to reproduce centrality dependence, trigger-direction dependence, and p_T dependence of the ridge. The paper provides a mechanistic explanation for the in-plane/out-of-plane effect observed at RHIC (reported by the STAR collaboration in 2009-2010), showing that the effect arises from the combination of background elliptic flow and multiplicity fluctuations rather than from the tube contribution alone. This enables future studies to disentangle the contributions of global geometry and fluctuations in two-particle correlations at both RHIC and LHC.
