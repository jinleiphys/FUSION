# The baryon spectrum and the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> The description of the baryon spectrum is performed using the hypercentral Consituent Quark Model (hCQM), mainly in comparison with the harmonic oscillator (h.o.). Recentlly many new states, at various levels of confidence have been observed, leading to a softening of the missing resonance problem in the case of positive parity states. However, the number of negative states is higher that predicted by the commonly used h.o. scheme and therefore one is forced to take into account also the higher energy shells, which contain an overall number of states much greater than the observed one. It is shown that, thanks to the peculiar level scheme of the hCQM, the recently observed negative parity states can be considered as belonging to the lower shells, keeping the missing resonance problem within more acceptable limits.

## Key claim
The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) can accommodate all recently observed negative parity baryon resonances within its first three shells, unlike the harmonic oscillator (h.o.) model which requires higher shells for these states; this keeps the "missing resonance problem" within more acceptable limits, though the model still predicts more positive parity states than observed.

## Method
The authors compare the baryon spectrum predicted by the hCQM, which uses a hypercentral potential depending only on the hyperradius (a Coulomb-plus-linear form), with the harmonic oscillator classification and with experimental data from the 2010 and 2014 PDG editions. They classify states by SU(6) multiplets and grand angular quantum number, fitting the three free parameters of the hCQM Hamiltonian (quark mass fixed at one-third the nucleon mass, plus parameters for the potential and hyperfine interaction) to reproduce the 4-star and 3-star resonances from the 1994 PDG, then compare predicted numbers and energies of states with observed ones.

## Key numbers
- The h.o. model predicts 7 negative parity N and Δ states in the first three shells (N_h.o. ≤ 2), while the 2014 PDG lists 13 such states (including 2-star and above), creating a deficit.
- The hCQM predicts 10 negative parity N states and 4 negative parity Δ states within its first three shells (N_hC ≤ 2), totaling 14 negative parity states, closely matching the observed 13.
- For positive parity, the h.o. predicts 23 states (14 N⁺ and 9 Δ⁺) in the first three shells; the 2014 PDG lists 20 positive parity states (12 N⁺ and 8 Δ⁺) when including all star ratings.
- The hCQM predicts 25 positive parity states (15 N⁺ and 10 Δ⁺) within the first three shells, exceeding the observed count.
- The hCQM fitted spectrum gives a theoretical mass of 1899 MeV for the N(1875) 3/2⁻ state (observed as a 3-star resonance), and 1525 MeV for the N(1520) 3/2⁻ state (observed at 1520 MeV, 4-star).

## Context
This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (Giannini and Santopinto, 2004) and addresses the missing resonance problem highlighted by the Isgur-Karl (1978) harmonic oscillator model and the Capstick-Isgur (1986) relativized model. The hCQM's level scheme, where positive and negative parity states coexist in the same shell, contrasts with the alternating parity pattern of the harmonic oscillator and allows the new negative parity states (e.g., N(1875) 3/2⁻ from PDG 2012/2014) to be assigned to lower shells. This enables a more natural classification of the baryon spectrum and provides predictions for further hyperradial excitations, such as a second Roper-like N(2100) 1/2⁺ candidate, to be tested by future experiments.
