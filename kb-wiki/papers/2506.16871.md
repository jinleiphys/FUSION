# Impact of $Ξ$-Hypernuclear Constraints on Relativistic Equation of State and Properties of Hyperon Stars

> Significant uncertainties persist in describing the equation of state and internal structure of hyperon stars due to the limited understanding of the mechanisms underlying hyperon interactions. Constraining the interaction parameter space through a combination of the latest astronomical observations and hypernuclear physics experiments is therefore essential. In this study, we incorporate experimental constraints from $Ξ$ hypernuclear physics on top of $Λ$ hyperons considered in \citet{Sun2023APJ942.55}. Specifically, based on updated measurements of hyperon separation energies from $Ξ$ hypernuclear experiments, sets of $ΞN$ effective interactions are constructed and a linear correlation between their scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) coupling strength ratios is proposed as a constraint derived from $Ξ$ hypernuclear physics. Together with experimental correlations and astronomical observational data, four types of analyses are performed to constrain hyperon-nucleon interactions and the properties of hyperon stars. Compared to the vector $ω$ meson-hyperon coupling, the introduction of linear correlations in hypernuclear physics imposes a more substantial constraint on the scalar $σ$ meson-hyperon coupling, significantly enhancing its coupling strength and thereby ensuring the stiffness of the equation of state, highlighting the crucial role of hypernuclear studies in solving the hyperon puzzle problem. Consequently, a maximum mass of around $2M_{\odot}$ can be achieved with all five interactions considered in this study under the combined constraints from astronomical observations and nuclear physics. With more reliably estimated hyperon-nucleon contributions, the uncertainties in both the fractions and the threshold densities at which hyperons appear inside neutron stars are notably reduced, along with those in the mass-radius predictions.

## Key claim

Incorporating experimental constraints from Ξ hypernuclear physics, specifically a linear correlation between the scalar (σ) and vector (ω) coupling strength ratios derived from updated measurements of Ξ separation energies, significantly enhances the scalar σ meson-hyperon coupling strength and ensures the stiffness of the equation of state, enabling a maximum neutron star mass around 2 solar masses under combined astronomical and nuclear physics constraints.

## Method

The authors constructed three sets of ΞN effective interactions within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework by fitting to experimental Ξ⁻ separation energies from three different hypernuclear states: the 1s state of ¹⁵Ξ⁻C (6.46 MeV), the 1p state of ¹⁵Ξ⁻C (1.13 MeV), and the 1p state of ¹³Ξ⁻B (0.82 MeV). A linear correlation between the scalar coupling ratio R_σΞ and vector coupling ratio R_ωΞ was established from these interactions and incorporated as a likelihood in a Bayesian analysis, which also included mass-radius observations from NICER (PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620), tidal deformability from GW170817, and Λ hypernuclear constraints from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The linear correlation between coupling ratios is given by R_ωΞ = 1.243 R_σΞ - 0.064, derived from fitting data across six RMF effective Lagrangians.
- The statistical uncertainty on R_σΞ is estimated as σ_R_σΞ = 0.08, conservatively accounting for model and experimental uncertainties.
- The Ξ⁻ hyperon mass is taken as M_Ξ⁻ = 1321.7 MeV.
- The fitting targets for ΞN interactions are: B_Ξ⁻ = 6.46 MeV (1s state of ¹⁵Ξ⁻C), B_Ξ⁻ = 1.13 ± 0.14 MeV (1p state of ¹⁵Ξ⁻C), and B_Ξ⁻ = 0.82 ± 0.17/0.14 MeV (1p state of ¹³Ξ⁻B).
- A maximum neutron star mass of around 2 solar masses (2M_⊙) is achieved with all four interactions considered under combined constraints.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference framework of Sun (2023), which previously constrained Λ hyperon couplings using astronomical observations and Λ hypernuclear data, by now incorporating Ξ hypernuclear constraints. The study addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the contradiction between observed 2 solar mass pulsars and the softening of the equation of state caused by hyperon appearance, by showing that Ξ hypernuclear constraints stiffen the equation of state. The results reduce uncertainties in hyperon fractions, threshold densities, and mass-radius predictions for hyperon stars, and build on experimental observations of Ξ hypernuclei including the first conclusive identification of ¹⁵Ξ⁻C by Nakazawa (2015) and subsequent measurements by Yoshimoto (2021) and Hayakawa (2021).
