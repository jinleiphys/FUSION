# Finite-size effects on the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars

> We study the finite-size effects, like the surface and Coulomb energies, on the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars. The equilibrium conditions for coexisting hadronic and quark phases are derived by minimizing the total energy including the surface and Coulomb contributions, which are different from the Gibbs conditions without finite-size effects. We employ the relativistic mean-field model to describe the hadronic phase, while the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector interactions is used for the quark phase. It is found that finite-size effects can significantly reduce the region of the mixed phase, and the results lie between those of the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions. We show that a massive star may contain a mixed-phase core and its size depends on the surface tension of the hadron-quark interface and the vector coupling between quarks. The repulsive vector interaction in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model can stiffen the equation of state of quark matter, and therefore, delay the phase transition and increase the maximum mass of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects (surface and Coulomb energies) significantly reduce the density range of the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars, placing results between the limiting cases of the Gibbs construction (zero surface tension) and the Maxwell construction (infinite surface tension), with the actual outcome depending on the poorly-known surface tension of the hadron-quark interface and the strength of repulsive vector interactions in the quark phase.

## Method

The authors employed the Wigner-Seitz approximation to model the hadron-quark mixed phase, assuming a sharp interface between phases inside charge-neutral cells. They derived equilibrium conditions by minimizing the total energy including surface and Coulomb contributions, using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set for hadronic matter and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector interactions for quark matter, treating the surface tension sigma and the vector coupling G_V as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.145 fm^-3, binding energy per nucleon: 16.3 MeV, symmetry energy: 36.9 MeV, incompressibility: 281 MeV (from the TM1 parameter set)
- Pure hadronic EOS with TM1 predicts a maximum neutron-star mass of 2.18 solar masses
- For G_V=0, the droplet or bubble phase becomes energetically unfavorable for surface tension sigma > 80 MeV/fm^2; for G_V=0.4 G_S, the threshold is sigma > 200 MeV/fm^2
- With G_V=0.4 G_S and the Gibbs construction, the maximum neutron-star mass is 2.13 solar masses; with G_V=0 and the Gibbs construction, it is 1.91 solar masses
- For the Maxwell construction with G_V=0, a pure quark core radius of 0.82 km appears; with G_V=0.2 G_S, the pure quark core radius is 0.38 km; no quark core appears for G_V=0.4 G_S

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase by Heiselberg et al. (1993) and Endo et al. (2006) by deriving equilibrium conditions from energy minimization rather than imposing Gibbs conditions, and by using the NJL model with vector interactions instead of the MIT bag model. The results enable more realistic neutron-star structure calculations that can be compared to the mass constraints from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010; Fonseca et al., 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013), and highlight the need for better theoretical estimates of the hadron-quark surface tension and vector coupling strength.
