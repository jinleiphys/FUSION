# The Entanglement Entropy between Short Range Correlations and the Fermi Sea in Nuclear Structure

> We calculate the nuclear structure orbital entanglement entropy of short range correlations (SRC) based on the nuclear scale separation. Specifically, the entanglement between the SRC orbitals and the rest of the system. It should be stressed that this is a single nucleon not a pair entanglement entropy between the proton and neutron. The entanglement arises from the probability for a nucleon to occupy a momentum state above the Fermi momentum. We separate the momentum space of the nucleus into two parts such that nucleons can occupy the meanfield part of the wave function, i.e. Fermi sea (FS) and separately the high-momentum SRC part. The orbital entropy we obtain is between these two parts where we essentially define two momentum subspaces, one containing all the low momentum FS states and the other the high-momentum part as a SRC "orbital" state. For the calculation we employ the decoupling of low and high-momenta which was established by the similarity normalization group the SRC is viewed as a further "orbital" which can be multiply occupied. Since the probability of the occupation of a single SRC is given by the nuclear contact we are able to obtain a simple general expression of the orbital entanglement entropy for SRC by employing the generalized contact formalism. This general formula for the SRC orbital entanglement entropy of a nuclear structure in terms of the nuclear contact, allows us to obtain the scaling of the entropy in terms the mass number, $A$. We find that, unlike the entanglement entropy of many quantum systems which scales with the surface area, the orbital entanglement entropy associated with the SRC in large nuclei is linearly dependent on $A$, i.e., it is shown to be extensive.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the orbital entanglement entropy associated with short range correlations (SRC) in nuclei scales linearly with mass number A (i.e., is extensive), unlike the area-law scaling typical of many quantum systems. This result depends on treating all high-momentum states above the Fermi momentum as a single multiply-occupiable "SRC orbital" and using the generalized contact formalism, with the caveat that the normalized contact is assumed approximately independent of A for large nuclei.

## Method

The author divides the nuclear momentum space into two subspaces: low-momentum Fermi sea (FS) states and high-momentum SRC states, treating the latter as a single effective "orbital" that can be multiply occupied. Using the generalized contact formalism (GCF), the occupation probability of this SRC orbital is expressed in terms of the nuclear contact, and the von Neumann entropy of the resulting 2x2 reduced density matrix is computed analytically. The total SRC entanglement entropy is then obtained by multiplying the single-SRC entropy by the number of nucleon pairs available to form SRC, which is A/2 for symmetric nuclei.

## Key numbers

* The normalized contact for symmetric nuclei in the Fermi gas approximation is given as c_pn^FG = 0.146 ± 0.002, which is used to show the SRC entanglement entropy is linear in A.
* Variational Monte Carlo calculated reduced contact values range from c_pn ≈ 0.125 for 4He, 8Be, 9Be, 10B to c_pn = 0.106 for 6Li, 7Li and c_pn = 0.168 for 12C.
* Experimental reduced contact values: c_pn^exp ≈ 0.149 for 4He and c_pn^exp ≈ 0.168 for 12C.
* The SRC entanglement entropy calculated for 4He using the author's formula gives S^SRC(4He) = 0.74 for c_pn = 0.12 and S^SRC(4He) = 0.86 for c_pn^exp = 0.15, compared to the total single-orbital entanglement entropy S_tot^(1)(4He) = 1.006 from Robin (2021).
* The ratio of proton-neutron SRC pairs to proton-proton SRC pairs found experimentally is approximately 20.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear entanglement entropy beyond few-body numerical calculations such as those by Robin (2021) for 4He and 6He and by Legeza (2015) for 28Si, 56Ni, and 64Ge using DMRG. It also connects to the one-body entropy approach of Bulgac (2022) for a Fermi system with mean-field and correlated parts. The paper enables a simple analytic estimate of SRC entanglement entropy for any nucleus using only the nuclear contact, and suggests that the extensive scaling of this entropy may have implications for computational model-space truncation and quantum computing resource estimation in nuclear structure.
