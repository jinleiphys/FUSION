# Weakly bound nuclei and realistic NN interactions

> Nuclei close to the neutron drip line are described employing an interaction model which is based on the low-momentum interaction $V_{lowk}$. This effective two-body interaction which is determined to reproduce the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering data at energies below the pion thresh-hold is supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction fitted to reproduce the saturation properties of infinite nuclear matter within the Hartree-Fock approach. It is demonstrated that corresponding calculations for closed shell-nuclei using this interaction model reproduce the bulk properties of these nuclei, independent whether the wave functions are expanded in terms of harmonic oscillator waves or in a basis of plane waves discretized in a spherical box of appropriate size. This plane wave basis, however, is more appropriate to describe weakly bound nuclei and the transition from discrete nuclei to homogeneous matter which is supposed to occur e.g. in the crust of neutron stars. Properties of exotic nuclei are studied within a Hartree-Fock plus BCS approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction ($V_{lowk}$) supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction can describe both stable and weakly bound neutron-rich nuclei within a Hartree-Fock plus BCS framework, but only when the contact term is included to compensate for the missing saturation behavior that $V_{lowk}$ alone fails to produce in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations for closed-shell nuclei using the $V_{lowk}$ interaction derived from the CD Bonn potential with a cutoff $\Lambda = 2$ fm$^{-1}$, supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction fitted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy per nucleon of -16.0 MeV at density 0.16 fm$^{-3}$) and the symmetry energy at saturation density. The single-particle wave functions were expanded in two different basis sets: harmonic oscillator states and plane waves discretized in a spherical box, with the latter intended for describing weakly bound nuclei and the transition to homogeneous matter. For open-shell nuclei, a BCS pairing treatment was added using a density-dependent zero-range interaction.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter: -16.0 MeV at saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, reproduced by $V_{lowk}$ plus contact interaction
- Compressibility modulus of $V_{lowk}$ plus contact interaction at saturation density: $K = 258$ MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 33 MeV for $V_{lowk}$ plus contact interaction (compared to 21 MeV for bare $V_{lowk}$)
- Parameters of the contact interaction: $t_0 = 584.1$ MeV fm$^3$, $x_0 = 0.0$, $t_3 = 8330.7$ MeV fm$^{3+3\alpha}$, $x_3 = -0.5$, with $\alpha = 0.5$
- For $^{16}O$ using bare $V_{lowk}$ in harmonic oscillator basis: calculated energy per nucleon around -7.7 MeV (experimental value -7.98 MeV)
- Box radius for $^{16}O$ plane wave calculations: $R = 15$ fm; for $^{208}Pb$: $R = 30$ fm

## Context

This work extends the approach of Coraggio (2003, 2006) who used $V_{lowk}$ in harmonic oscillator bases for light nuclei, and addresses the known problem that $V_{lowk}$ alone fails to produce nuclear matter saturation (Bogner 2005, Kuckei 2003). The method enables calculations for neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line and for the pasta phase in neutron star crusts, where plane wave bases in Wigner-Seitz cells are more appropriate than harmonic oscillator bases. The results are compared with Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock calculations (van Dalen 2004, 2007) and Brueckner Hartree Fock plus three-body force calculations (Lejeune 2000).
