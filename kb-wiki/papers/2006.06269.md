# Turning up and down strong magnetic fields in relativistic nuclear collisions

> I show that the average transverse momentum, $\langle p_t \rangle$, of the hadrons emitted in relativistic nuclear collisions can be used as a ``knob'' to control the strength of the magnetic field induced by the spectator and the participant protons over the overlap region. I thus argue that any observable sensitive to this magnetic field is nontrivially correlated with $\langle p_t \rangle$ at a given collision centrality.

## Key claim

The average transverse momentum of hadrons, ⟨p_t⟩, can serve as an experimental "knob" to control the strength of the magnetic field in relativistic nuclear collisions at a given collision centrality, because selecting events by ⟨p_t⟩ at fixed multiplicity corresponds to selecting events by system size and thus by impact parameter and spectator number.

## Method

The author uses the TRENTo model of initial conditions, tuned to simulate ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions at LHC energy and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au, ²³⁸U+²³⁸U, and ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru collisions at RHIC energy. The relative variation of ⟨p_t⟩ is approximated from the initial energy per rapidity and entropy per rapidity in the quark-gluon plasma using a hydrodynamic relation, with a constant κ₀ chosen to reproduce experimental dynamical fluctuations of ⟨p_t⟩. Observables are then studied as functions of ⟨p_t⟩ at fixed centrality, defined by the produced entropy.

## Key numbers

- In central ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions (1-2% centrality), the number of spectator nucleons increases by as much as a factor 4 moving from low to high ⟨p_t⟩.
- In central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at RHIC, the number of spectators increases by roughly a factor 7.
- In peripheral ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions (39-40% centrality), the overall relative increase of spectator number is less than 20%.
- In peripheral collisions at RHIC (39-40% centrality), the spectator number increases by about 60 units in all systems studied.
- For central ²⁰⁸Pb+²⁰⁸Pb collisions at low ⟨p_t⟩, the impact parameter is about 1 fm and spectator number N_s = 10; at high ⟨p_t⟩, the impact parameter is about 2.8 fm and N_s = 40.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic understanding that ⟨p_t⟩ at fixed multiplicity is proportional to system size (Broniowski 2009, Mazeliauskas 2015, Bozek 2017, Schenke 2020, Giacalone 2020). It provides a new event-shape selection method that is stronger than the commonly used elliptic flow (v₂) selection for controlling spectator number, with the correlation between ⟨p_t⟩ and spectator number being twice as strong as that between ε₂ and spectator number. The method enables experimental investigations of chiral magnetic effect (CME) signals and other magnetic-field-sensitive observables, particularly through the proposed correlation coefficient ρ(⟨p_t⟩, v₁^±), which can enhance CME signal while keeping background fixed.
