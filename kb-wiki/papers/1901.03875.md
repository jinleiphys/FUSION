# Neutron radii and neutron skin of neutron-rich nuclei deduced from proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections

> A new method is proposed to deduce the neutron radii of neutron-rich nuclei. This method requires measuring the reaction cross sections of both the neutron-rich nucleus and its stable isotope at the same energy on a proton target. Using this method and the available experimental data of $^{12,22}$C + $p$ and $^{9,14}$Be + $p$ at 40$A$ MeV, the neutron radii of $^{22}$C and $^{14}$Be have been deduced for the first time.

# Neutron radii and neutron skin of neutron-rich nuclei deduced from proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method to deduce neutron radii of neutron-rich nuclei by measuring the difference in reaction cross sections between the neutron-rich nucleus and its stable isotope on a proton target at the same energy, under the assumption that the proton density distributions of the two isotopes are nearly identical. The method is demonstrated for 22C and 14Be, but the authors note that the approximation works best at energies below 300A MeV and that the deviation between the approximate and exact calculations does not exceed 7% for the isotopes considered.

## Method

The method uses the Optical Limit Approximation of Glauber theory to relate the reaction cross section shift between a neutron-rich nucleus and its stable isotope to the difference in their neutron density distributions. The authors assume that the contribution from the proton density difference is negligible, based on the observation that charge radii and proton radii vary by less than 10% among isotopes of the same element (except for He, Li, and B). They then apply this approximation to experimental reaction cross section data for 12,22C + p and 9,14Be + p at approximately 40A MeV, using harmonic oscillator density distributions for the stable isotopes and varying the neutron radius of the neutron-rich isotope to match the measured cross section shift.

## Key numbers

- For 22C: neutron radius = 5.2 ± 0.95 fm, deduced from the reaction cross section shift δσR22,12[Exp] = 967 ± 285 mb between 22C and 12C on a proton target at 40A MeV.
- For 22C: neutron skin thickness = 2.87 ± 0.95 fm, assuming proton radius equal to that of 12C (2.33 fm).
- For 14Be: neutron radius = 3.36 ± 0.11 fm, deduced from the reaction cross section shift δσR14,9[Exp] = 314 ± 26 mb between 14Be and 9Be on a proton target at approximately 40A MeV.
- For 14Be: neutron skin thickness = 0.95 ± 0.17 fm, using the proton radius of 2.41 ± 0.06 fm from charge-changing cross section measurements (Terashima 2014).
- The approximation used in the method (Eq. 7) deviates from the exact calculation by no more than 7% for all Be, B, C, O, Ne, and Mg isotopes considered, at energies from 40A MeV to 800A MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine neutron radii of neutron-rich nuclei, which have relied on combining matter radii from reaction cross sections on carbon targets (Tanihata 1985) with charge radii from isotope-shift measurements (Suzuki 1995) or charge-changing cross sections (Yamaguchi 2011). The proposed method avoids the need for charge radius measurements, which are difficult for nuclei near the neutron drip line due to low beam intensities. The results for 22C are compared to earlier measurements by Tanaka (2010) giving a matter radius of 5.4 ± 0.9 fm from proton target data, and Togano (2016) giving 3.44 ± 0.08 fm from carbon target data; the authors note that simultaneous reproduction of both measurements is not feasible. For 14Be, the deduced neutron radius is consistent with results from Enyo (2015) and Lataz (2004). The method enables future determination of neutron radii for other neutron-rich isotopes where only proton-target reaction cross section data exist.
