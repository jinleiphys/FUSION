# Application of the coherent state formalism to multiply excited states

> A general expression is obtained for the matrix element of an m-body operator between coherent states constructed from multiple orthogonal coherent boson species. This allows the coherent state formalism to be applied to states possessing an arbitrarily large number of intrinsic excitation quanta. For illustration, the formalism is applied to the two-dimensional vibron model [U(3) model], to calculate the energies of all excited states in the large-N limit.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for the matrix element of an m-body operator between coherent states built from multiple orthogonal boson species, enabling the coherent state formalism to be applied to states with an arbitrarily large number of intrinsic excitation quanta, not just one or two as previously possible.

## Method

The author first reviews the standard condensate coherent state for a bosonic realization of U(n) and derives the expectation value of an m-body operator using commutation relations and falling factorials. The method is then extended to multi-species coherent states constructed from S orthogonal linear combinations of boson creation operators, yielding a general matrix element formula (Eq. 13) involving sums over species indices and products of falling factorials and coefficient products. The formalism is illustrated by applying it to the two-dimensional vibron model (U(3) model) in the SO(3) dynamical symmetry limit, where the energies of all excited states and transition strengths for dipole and quadrupole operators are calculated in the large-N limit.

## Key numbers

- For the SO(3) dynamical symmetry of the two-dimensional vibron model, the exact energy eigenvalues are \(E(N,v,l) = -N(N+1) + 4v[(N+1/2)-v]\), with \(v = 0,1,\ldots,\lfloor N/2 \rfloor\) and \(l = -(N-2v), -(N-2v)+1, \ldots, +(N-2v)\).
- The coherent state estimate for the energy is \(E(N,N_x) = -N(N+1) + 4N_x[N-N_x]\), which differs from the exact expression only by a term of order \(1/N\).
- For the dipole transition operator \(\hat{D}_\pm\) within an intrinsic excitation, the coherent state expectation value is \(\langle NN_x;r| \hat{D}_\pm |NN_x;r\rangle = -2(N-2N_x) \frac{r}{1+r^2}\).
- For the dipole transition between successive intrinsic excitations, the matrix element is \(\langle N(N_x-1);r| \hat{D}_\pm |NN_x;r\rangle = -\sqrt{(N-N_x+1)N_x} \frac{1-r^2}{1+r^2}\).
- For the quadrupole transition operator \(\hat{Q}_\pm\) within an intrinsic excitation, the expectation value is \(\langle NN_x;r| \hat{Q}_\pm |NN_x;r\rangle = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{N_x + (N-N_x)r^2}{1+r^2}\).
- For the quadrupole transition between successive intrinsic excitations, the matrix element is \(\langle N(N_x-1);r| \hat{Q}_\pm |NN_x;r\rangle = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{(N-N_x+1)N_x} \frac{r}{1+r^2}\).
- Quadrupole transition strengths from the coherent state estimate are accurate to within about 5% for low angular momentum members of the representations, except at the highest intrinsic excitation quantum numbers (for \(N=100\)).

## Context

This work extends the coherent state formalism for algebraic models, previously limited to ground states or states with one or at most two intrinsic excitation quanta (as in Garcia-Ramos (1998) for two-phonon states in the IBM), to states with an arbitrary number of excitation quanta. The general matrix element formula enables the study of excited states in transitional Hamiltonians where analytic results are not otherwise available, and provides a basis for investigating the evolution of a system's properties with excitation energy. The formalism is applicable to models such as the interacting boson model (IBM) for nuclei (Iachello 1987) and the vibron model for molecules (Iachello 1995), and the specific illustration uses the two-dimensional vibron model (Iachello 1996).
