# Decay Widths of X(1835) as Nucleon-Antinucleon Bound State

> Partial decay widths of various decay channels of the X(1835) are evaluated in the 3P0 quark model, assuming that the X(1835) is a nucleon-antinucleon bound state. It is found that the decays to rho+rho, omega+omega and pion+a0(1450) dominate over other channels, and that the product branching fractions of J/psi to pion+pion+eta and J/psi to pion+pion+eta' are in the same order. We suggest that the X(1835) may be searched in the pion+a0(1450) channel.

# Decay Widths of X(1835) as Nucleon-Antinucleon Bound State

## Key claim
Assuming the X(1835) is a ¹¹S₀ nucleon-antinucleon bound state with quantum numbers I^G(J^PC)=0⁺(0⁻⁺), the decays to ρρ, ωω and πa₀(1450) dominate over other channels, and the product branching fractions Br(J/ψ→γX)Br(X→ππη) and Br(J/ψ→γX)Br(X→ππη') are estimated to be of the same order. The authors hedge that the theoretical results are very sensitive to the effective coupling constants and size parameters, so they compute only relative partial decay widths normalized to the ρρ channel to avoid these uncertainties.

## Method
The authors evaluate partial decay widths of the X(1835) in the ³P₀ quark model using the A2 quark line diagram, treating the X(1835) as a ¹¹S₀ N̅N bound state. They compute transition amplitudes for two-meson final states by convolving the N̅N bound state wave function with the N̅N→M₁M₂ annihilation amplitude in momentum space, using harmonic oscillator wave functions for baryons and mesons. For the three-body final states ππη and ππη', they estimate the joint contributions from the πa₀(1450) and ηf₀(1370) intermediate channels using experimental branching ratios of the intermediate mesons.

## Key numbers
- The product branching fraction Br(J/ψ→γX(1835))Br(X(1835)→π⁺π⁻η') = (2.2 ± 0.4(stat.) ± 0.4(syst.)) × 10⁻⁴ from BES Collaboration (2005)
- Mass of X(1835): 1833.7 ± 6.1 (stat) ± 2.7 (syst) MeV; width: 67.7 ± 20.3 (stat) ± 7.7 (syst) MeV from BES (2005)
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→ωω)/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.32
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→πa₀(1450))/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.25
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→ηf₀(1370))/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.057
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→η'f₀(1370))/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.052
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→πa₂(1320))/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.055
- Relative partial decay width Γ(X→ηf₂(1270))/Γ(X→ρρ) = 0.008
- Estimated Γ(X→ηπ⁺π⁻)/Γ(X→ρρ) ∼ 0.1
- Estimated Γ(X→η'π⁺π⁻)/Γ(X→ρρ) ∼ 0.05
- Meson size parameter b = 3.24 GeV⁻¹ (rms radius 0.39 fm) from ρ→e⁺e⁻
- Baryon size parameter a = 2.0 GeV⁻¹ used for the relative width calculation
- Phenomenological phase space constant a_s = 1.2 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of the X(1835) as a nucleon-antinucleon bound state, building on earlier studies of N̅N annihilations and bound states in the ³P₀ quark model by Maruyama (1987), Dover (1990), and others. The authors specifically adopt the ¹¹S₀ assignment because previous work (Dover 1990, Yan 1997) found no such bound state in microscopic or phenomenological potentials, and because BES observations of the X(1835) in ππη but not in ππ, 3π, ηη or πη channels rule out the ³¹S₀, ¹³P₀ and ³³P₀ assignments. The results enable experimental searches for the X(1835) in the πa₀(1450) channel, and suggest that measuring product branching fractions in KK* and K*K* channels could distinguish the N̅N bound state interpretation from the radial excitation of η' interpretation (etap4, 2011).
