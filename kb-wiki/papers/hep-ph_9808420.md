# On the treatment of confinement in three-quark calculations

> The treatment of confining interactions in non-relativistic three-quark systems is revised. Usually in the Faddeev equations the Faddeev components are coupled by the total potential. In the new treatment the Faddeev components are coupled only by the non-confining short-range part of the potential, allowing thus its channel-by-channel investigation. The convergence in angular momentum channels is much faster.

# On the treatment of confinement in three-quark calculations

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified Faddeev approach for non-relativistic three-quark systems in which the confining (long-range) and non-confining (short-range) parts of the quark-quark interaction are treated separately, with the Faddeev decomposition applied only to the short-range part. This modification yields much faster convergence in angular momentum channels compared to the conventional Faddeev treatment, provided the splitting is performed such that the confining part contains a repulsive core to remove spurious bound states from the energy region of interest.

## Method

The author splits the quark-quark potential into a long-range confining term (including constant and linear confinement plus a repulsive Gaussian core) and a short-range non-confining term (the Goldstone-boson-exchange part minus the Gaussian). The modified Faddeev equations couple the Faddeev components only through the short-range non-confining potential, while the confining parts are treated on equal footing with the kinetic energy in the left-hand side of each equation. The equations are solved by expanding the Faddeev components in a basis of Coulomb-Sturmian functions, and convergence is tested by increasing the number of angular momentum channels.

## Key numbers

- Confining force strength in one parametrization: C = 0.474 fm^{-2} (from Ref. gpp, 1997)
- Confining force strength in the alternative parametrization: C = 0.77 fm^{-2} (from Ref. frasc, 1997)
- Gaussian parameters used in the splitting: r_g = 1.0 fm, V_g = 3.0 fm^{-1}
- Angular momentum channels up to l = 2 are completely sufficient for convergence in the new scheme for both parametrizations

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier approach for atomic three-body systems with Coulomb interactions (Papp, 1997) to the case of confining potentials in three-quark baryon calculations. It addresses a known limitation of conventional Faddeev calculations for three-quark systems (as performed by Silvestre-Brac, 1996; Gloeckle et al., 1997; and the Frascati group, 1997), namely the need for many angular momentum channels. The new method enables channel-by-channel investigation of the non-confining part of the interaction, which was not possible with the conventional Faddeev equations or with non-Faddeev variational methods. The converged results are cross-checked against the stochastic variational method of Kálmán (1997).
