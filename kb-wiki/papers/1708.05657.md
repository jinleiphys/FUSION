# Bulk viscosity from hydrodynamic fluctuations with relativistic hydro-kinetic theory

> Hydro-kinetic theory of thermal fluctuations is applied to a non-conformal relativistic fluid. Solving the hydro-kinetic equations for an isotropically expanding background we find that hydrodynamic fluctuations give ultraviolet divergent contributions to the energy-momentum tensor. After shifting the temperature to account for the energy of non-equilibrium modes, the remaining divergences are renormalized into local parameters, e.g. pressure and bulk viscosity. We also confirm that the renormalization of the pressure and bulk viscosity is universal by computing them for a Bjorken expansion. The fluctuation-induced bulk viscosity reflects the non-conformal nature of the equation of state and is modestly enhanced near the QCD deconfinement temperature.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations in a non-conformal relativistic fluid give ultraviolet divergent contributions to the energy-momentum tensor that, after a temperature shift to account for the energy of non-equilibrium modes, can be renormalized into local parameters including the bulk viscosity; the fluctuation-induced bulk viscosity is positive, proportional to scale symmetry breaking factors in the equation of state, and modestly enhanced near the QCD deconfinement temperature.

## Method

The authors apply hydro-kinetic theory, previously developed for conformal fluids, to a non-conformal relativistic fluid under isotropic expansion. They derive kinetic equations for the two-point correlation functions of hydrodynamic fluctuations (sound modes and transverse diffusive modes) around an expanding background, solve these equations perturbatively, and compute the nonlinear contributions of fluctuations to the statistically averaged energy-momentum tensor. The ultraviolet divergences are handled by introducing a cutoff Lambda, shifting the background temperature to satisfy the Landau constraint (that the energy of non-equilibrium modes is zero), and then renormalizing the pressure and bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

* The fluctuation-induced bulk viscosity renormalization is given by zeta(T) = zeta_0(T;Lambda) + (T Lambda)/(18 pi^2) [ C_zeta^2 / gamma_zeta + 4 C_eta^2 / (2 gamma_eta) ], where C_zeta = 1 + (3T/2)(d c_s^2/dT) - 3 c_s^2 and C_eta = 1 - 3 c_s^2.
* Using the lattice QCD equation of state and eta/s = 1/(4 pi), the bulk viscosity reaches zeta/s ~ 0.03-0.06 at the pseudocritical temperature T_c ~ 150 MeV.
* The UV cutoff Lambda for hydrodynamic fluctuations is varied between 2T and 4T in the numerical estimate.
* The non-equilibrium distribution of fluctuations at high k gives corrections proportional to (nabla dot u)/K^2, with coefficients involving C_zeta and C_eta.

## Context

This work extends the hydro-kinetic theory of Akamatsu, Mazeliauskas, and Teaney (2016) from conformal to non-conformal relativistic fluids, where the speed of sound c_s^2 differs from 1/3 and bulk viscosity is finite. The renormalization of bulk viscosity is consistent with diagrammatic calculations around a non-expanding background by Kovtun, Moore, and Romatschke (2003, 2011). The approach enables estimation of a lower bound for the bulk viscosity of QCD using lattice equation of state data from Borsanyi et al. (2016), and has recently been applied to estimate bulk viscosity in nonrelativistic cold Fermi gases by Martinez and Schafer (2017).
