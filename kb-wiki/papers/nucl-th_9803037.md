# Study of Superdeformation in Non-rotating States using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Method

> The superdeformation (SD) in non-rotating states is studied with the HF+BCS method using the Skyrme interaction. In applying the BCS theory, the seniority pairing force is employed, of which strengths are determined in order to reproduce the empirical pairing gap formula, $\barΔ = 12 A^{-1/2}$ MeV, through a smooth level density obtained in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Properties of superdeformation are investigated by calculating potential energy surfaces (PES) for various sets of the pairing force strengths and the Skyrme force parameter for 194Hg and 236,238U. The best results are obtained using both the SkM* force and the pairing force strength determined in this paper. By making use of this set of forces, a systematic calculation of SD states is carried out extensively for even-even nuclei for 20 <= Z <= 82. From our calculation, the barriers preventing the decay into the normally deformed states are about twice as high as those predicted by Krieger et al., who used the same Skyrme interaction but a pairing force stronger than ours. The differences of the present results from the Nilsson-Strutinsky calculation are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that superdeformed (SD) states exist in non-rotating (zero-spin) even-even nuclei across a wide region of the nuclear chart, but this conclusion is conditional on using the SkM* Skyrme force together with pairing force strengths that are weaker than those used in prior work by Krieger et al. (1992). The authors find that with their weaker pairing strengths, the barrier heights preventing decay into normally deformed states are about twice as high as those predicted by Krieger et al., while excitation energies are not very different.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with the Skyrme interaction, combined with BCS pairing using a seniority force, to compute potential energy surfaces (PES) for even-even nuclei. Single-particle wave functions were represented on a three-dimensional Cartesian mesh with 1 fm spacing and a box size of 15x15x16 fm, assuming D2h symmetry. The pairing force strengths were determined by requiring that the Thomas-Fermi averaged level density reproduce the empirical pairing gap formula Δ̄ = 12 A^{-1/2} MeV, and the systematic search covered 642 even-even nuclei with 20 ≤ Z ≤ 82.

## Key numbers

- For 194Hg, the experimental SD band-head excitation energy extrapolated to I=0 is 6.017 MeV; the SkM* force with the authors' pairing strengths gives E* = 6.3 MeV (from Fig. 1, left panel, value in parentheses).
- For 194Hg, the barrier height EB = 3.5 MeV with the authors' pairing strengths, compared to EB = 1.8 MeV from Krieger et al. (1992) using stronger pairing.
- The half-life of the SD band head in 194Hg is estimated as 1 × 10^{-13} sec with the authors' pairing strengths, versus 6 × 10^{-17} sec for Krieger et al.'s strengths, a factor of about 10^3 difference.
- For 236U and 238U, the enhancement factor fB needed to reproduce experimental half-lives is 13.7 and 10.4, respectively.
- The systematic search found SD minima in 118 out of 642 even-even nuclei with 20 ≤ Z ≤ 82.
- For 188Hg, 190Hg, and 192Hg, the excitation energies are 3.7, 4.1, and 4.9 MeV, with barrier heights of 1.1, 2.4, and 3.6 MeV, respectively.
- For 208Pb, the SD state has the largest excitation energy (23 MeV) and barrier height (6.5 MeV) in the A ~ 190 group.

## Context

This work extends the earlier systematic study by Krieger et al. (1992), who used the same SkM* Skyrme force but stronger pairing force strengths, and finds that the weaker pairing strengths significantly increase barrier heights while leaving excitation energies similar. The results also differ from the Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations, which the authors performed for comparison and which show a different distribution of SD minima, particularly in the A ~ 150 region where only two nuclei (154Gd and 156Gd) have zero-spin SD minima versus many high-spin SD bands observed experimentally. The study enables future experimental searches for zero-spin SD isomers, especially in the Hg isotopes around 188-192Hg and in the Pb region near N=126, and motivates more sophisticated treatments such as angular momentum projection and the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method for neutron-rich nuclei.
