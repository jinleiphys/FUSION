# Event-by-Event Multiplicity Correlations in $^{252}$Cf(sf)

> Excited nuclear fragments are emitted during nuclear fission. The de-excitation of these fission fragments takes place as sequential emission of neutrons followed by photons. A correlation between neutron and photon multiplicities accompanying fission is thus expected. Fission event generators based on established statistical nuclear physics models predict a negative event-by-event correlation in neutron-photon multiplicity. A survey of published experimental results of an event-by-event covariance between the neutron and photon multiplicities emitted following the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf is presented. Analytic unfolding expressions are developed in this work to determine the bias introduced by background sources, particle misclassification, pulse pileup, and inelastic photon production. The published experimental data are re-analyzed using these unfolding techniques and are found to be in qualitative agreement with the predictions of model-based calculations. In particular, we have concluded that there exists a significant event-by-event neutron-photon emission competition following the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf.

## Key claim
After applying novel analytic unfolding techniques to correct for experimental biases (background, particle misclassification, pulse pileup, and inelastic photon production), the previously inconsistent published experimental results for the event-by-event neutron-photon multiplicity covariance in the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf are all found to be in qualitative agreement with each other and with the negative correlations predicted by the fission event generators CGMF and FREYA.

## Method
The authors developed analytic unfolding expressions based on a binomial-response model to correct experimentally determined event-by-event neutron-photon multiplicity covariances for biases from background sources, particle misclassification, neutron-photon pileup, and inelastic photon production. They applied these corrections to re-analyze published experimental data from three independent experiments: Glassel et al. (1989) using the Darmstadt-Heidelberg Crystal Ball, Bleuel et al. (2010) using the LiBerACE detector array, and Marcath et al. (2018) using the Chi-Nu array. For the Marcath et al. data, a forward unfolding model using MCNPX-PoliMi simulations with FREYA events was also performed to check energy-dependent efficiency effects.

## Key numbers
- CGMF model prediction for the covariance: cov(N_n, N_gamma) = -0.839 +/- 0.004 (statistical uncertainty only)
- FREYA model prediction for the covariance: cov(N_n, N_gamma) = -0.8200 +/- 0.0004 (statistical uncertainty only)
- Re-analyzed Glassel et al. result: cov(N_n, N_gamma) = -0.71 +/- 0.17, after correcting for approximately 14% pileup bias and neutron efficiency of about 40%
- Re-analyzed Bleuel et al. result: cov(N_n, N_gamma) = -0.69 +/- 0.10, after correcting for inelastic photon production probability xi_n = 0.35 determined from MCNPX-PoliMi simulation
- Re-analyzed Marcath et al. result: cov(N_n, N_gamma) = -0.58 +/- 0.06, after corrections where pileup bias represented approximately 67% of the determined covariance and background covariance represented approximately 15%
- MCNPX-PoliMi simulation using FREYA events for the Chi-Nu array gave a covariance of -0.57 +/- 0.01, in good agreement with the corrected Marcath et al. result
- Mean neutron multiplicity for $^{252}$Cf(sf): 3.75 +/- 0.01 (from Santi et al.)
- Neutron multiplicity variance used for conversions: 1.59 +/- 0.02 (from Santi et al.)
- Photon multiplicity variance used for conversions: 13.4 +/- 3.2 (from Oberstedt et al.)

## Context
This work resolves long-standing inconsistencies between experimental determinations of the neutron-photon multiplicity correlation in $^{252}$Cf(sf) from Glassel et al. (1989), Bleuel et al. (2010), and Marcath et al. (2018), which had previously reported covariances ranging from -0.35 to 0.0. The corrected results provide experimental validation for the fission event generators CGMF (Talou et al.) and FREYA (Vogt et al.), which predict negative correlations from statistical nuclear physics models of fragment de-excitation. This enables future work on neutron-photon emission competition in other spontaneous fission isotopes such as $^{240}$Pu, and in neutron-induced fission, as well as studies of correlations between momenta and multiplicities.
