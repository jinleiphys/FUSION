# Differential and total cross sections of high energy proton-proton scattering in holographic QCD

> The analysis on the high energy proton-proton scattering is presented, focusing on the Regge regime and considering the Pomeron exchange to describe the involved nonperturbative dynamics in the framework of holographic QCD. We combine the Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator and the proton gravitational form factor, which is obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model, and calculate the differential and total cross sections. We explicitly demonstrate the comparison between our calculations and the currently available experimental data including the recent ones measured at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV by the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. It is shown that our results are consistent with the data, which implies that the present framework works in the considered TeV scale.

## Key claim
The holographic QCD framework, combining a Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator with the proton gravitational form factor from a bottom-up AdS/QCD model, reproduces the differential and total cross sections of high-energy proton-proton scattering up to \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV, but only in the kinematic regime where \(|t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\sqrt{s} \ge 546\) GeV, and without including saturation effects.

## Method
The author calculates the differential and total cross sections for elastic proton-proton scattering in the Regge regime (\(s \gg |t|\)) by combining a Reggeized spin-2 particle (Pomeron) propagator with the proton gravitational form factor \(A(t)\), obtained from both the hard-wall and soft-wall bottom-up AdS/QCD models. The three adjustable parameters (\(\lambda\), \(\alpha_c(0)\), and \(\alpha'_c\)) are determined by a simultaneous numerical fit to experimental data for both differential and total cross sections, selecting data with \(\sqrt{s} \ge 546\) GeV and \(|t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\) to avoid the diffractive minimum region.

## Key numbers
- The fit yields \(\chi^2 / \text{d.o.f.} = 1.317\) for the soft-wall model and \(1.355\) for the hard-wall model.
- The differential cross section calculations are compared with data from various collaborations, including TOTEM at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV, for \(|t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\).
- The total cross section calculation is consistent with the empirical fit by the COMPETE collaboration and with collider data, but cosmic-ray pp data from PDG2010 were not used in the fit due to their large uncertainties.

## Context
This work extends the preceding study by Domokos (2009), which used a dipole form factor with a free parameter, by instead employing the proton gravitational form factor from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model of Abidin (2009), reducing the number of adjustable parameters from four to three. The results are consistent with data from the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC and with the COMPETE empirical fit, but the model does not include saturation effects, Reggeon exchange, multi-Pomeron exchange, or Odderon exchange, which are needed for further improvements and for testing the model's reliability at higher energies.
