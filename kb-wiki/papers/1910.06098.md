# Analyses of multi-pion Bose-Einstein correlations for granular sources with coherent pion-emission droplets

> The ALICE Collaboration measure the three- and four-pion Bose-Einstein correlations (BECs) in Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). It is speculated that the significant suppressions of multi-pion BECs are due to a considerable degree of coherent pion emission in the collisions. In this paper, we study the multi-pion BEC functions in a granular source model with coherent pion-emission droplets. We find that the intercepts of multi-pion correlation functions at the relative momenta near zero are sensitive to droplet number in the granular source. They decrease with decreasing droplet number. The three-pion correlation functions for the evolving granular sources with momentum-dependent partially coherent pion-emission droplets are in basic agreement with the experimental data in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC. However, the model results of four-pion correlation function are inconsistent with the experimental data. The investigations of the normalized multi-pion correlation functions of the granular sources indicate that there is an interesting enhancement of the normalized four-pion correlation function in moderate relative-momentum region.

## Key claim

The three-pion correlation functions from an evolving granular source model with momentum-dependent partially coherent pion-emission droplets are in basic agreement with ALICE experimental data for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the model results for the four-pion correlation function are inconsistent with the experimental data, indicating that more considerations of partially coherent pion emission are needed.

## Method

The authors model the particle-emitting source as a granular source consisting of hot droplets that expand in viscous hydrodynamics, with droplet centers distributed within a cylinder along the collision axis. Pions emitted from each droplet are assumed to be either completely coherent or partially coherent with a momentum-dependent threshold \(p'\), where pions with momenta below \(p'\) are coherent and those above are chaotic. The three- and four-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions and normalized correlation functions \(r_3\) and \(r_4\) are calculated for static and evolving granular sources and compared to ALICE data from central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- The average droplet number \(\langle n \rangle\) for the simulation events with granular source parameters determined by experimental data is 8.
- The three-pion correlation functions for the evolving granular source with \(p' = 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) are approximately in agreement with experimental data in transverse-momentum intervals \(0.16 < K_{T3} < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) and \(0.3 < K_{T3} < 1\) GeV/\(c\).
- The normalized three-pion correlation intercept \(r_3(Q_3 = 10\) MeV/\(c)\) for \(p' = 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) is \(1.64 \pm 0.13\) in the low \(K_{T3}\) interval and \(1.67 \pm 0.14\) in the high \(K_{T3}\) interval.
- The normalized four-pion correlation intercept \(r_4(Q_4 = 30\) MeV/\(c)\) for \(p' = 0.5\) GeV/\(c\) is \(2.45 \pm 0.21\) in the low \(K_{T4}\) interval and \(2.07 \pm 0.24\) in the high \(K_{T4}\) interval.
- The normalized four-pion correlation function shows an enhancement at approximately \(Q_4 \sim 100\) MeV/\(c\) for the wide transverse-momentum interval \(0.3 < K_{T4} < 1\) GeV/\(c\).
- The static granular source calculations use a source radius \(R_G = 6.0\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the granular source model previously developed by Zhang et al. (2004, 2006, 2009, 2011) to explain the RHIC HBT puzzle and later used by Yang, Ren, and Zhang (2015, 2017) to reproduce pion transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and two-pion interferometry radii. It also builds on the authors' prior investigations of multi-pion BECs for spherical evolving sources with identical boson condensation (Bary, Ru, Zhang 2018, 2019). The study addresses the open question raised by the ALICE Collaboration (2014, 2016) and by Gangadharan (2015) regarding how to consistently explain the suppressions of three- and four-pion BECs in a partially coherent source model, finding that the four-pion correlation puzzle remains unresolved in the current framework.
