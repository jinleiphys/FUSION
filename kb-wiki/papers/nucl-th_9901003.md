# Effective Field Theory for Nuclear Physics

> I summarize the motivation for the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics, and highlight some of its recent accomplishments. The results are compared with those computed in potential models.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) can provide a systematic, improvable expansion for nuclear physics, but only if the theory is tuned to a nontrivial fixed point corresponding to infinite scattering length, making the leading contact interaction marginal rather than irrelevant and requiring nonperturbative treatment.

## Method

The author develops the KSW expansion for nucleon-nucleon scattering, starting with a nonrelativistic nucleon field interacting via a contact term \(C_0 (N^\dagger N)^2\). The amplitude is computed by iterating bubble diagrams using dimensional regularization with the PDS subtraction scheme, revealing a renormalization group equation with a UV fixed point at \(\hat C_0 = 1\). Higher-order corrections include derivative contact interactions \(C_2 p^2 + C_4 p^4 + \ldots\) and perturbative pions, with power-counting rules assigning \(m_\pi \sim Q\), \(\nabla \sim Q\), and loop integrations scaling as \(Q^5\) or \(Q^4\). The method is tested on a toy model with delta-shell potentials at radii \(1/m_\rho\) and \(1/m_\pi\), then applied to real two-nucleon and three-nucleon systems.

## Key numbers

- The low scales in nucleon-nucleon interactions are \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV and the nucleon momentum in nuclear matter \(p_F \simeq 280\) MeV; the high scale from the \(\Delta\) resonance is \(\sqrt{2 M_N (M_\Delta - M_N)} = 750\) MeV.
- In the \(^1S_0\) channel, the \(np\) scattering length is \(a = -1/(8\ \mathrm{MeV})\); in the \(^3S_1\) channel, \(a = 1/(40\ \mathrm{MeV})\).
- In the toy model with \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV, \(a = -23\) fm, and \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV, the KSW expansion converges up to \(p \sim m_\rho\).
- For the deuteron charge radius, the EFT prediction is off by \(28\%\) at LO and by \(11\%\) at NLO; the deuteron quadrupole moment is off by \(39\%\) at LO.
- For the deuteron scalar electric polarizability, the EFT calculation differs from potential model results by \(\sim 39\%\) at LO and \(6\%\) at NLO.
- The operative expansion parameter in the KSW expansion is \(\sim 1/3\), with LO results accurate to the \(30\%\) level and NLO to the \(10\%\) level.

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's (1990) proposal to apply EFT to nuclear physics, correcting its inconsistency with a new approach (KSW, 1998) that treats the contact interaction nonperturbatively. It contrasts with traditional potential models (e.g., Nijmegen partial wave analysis) that fit phase shifts with many parameters but lack a systematic expansion, leading to dependence on arbitrary off-shell amplitudes. The KSW expansion enables analytic calculations of two-nucleon processes (deuteron form factors, Compton scattering, parity-violating \(np\) capture) and reduces three-nucleon Fadeev equations to one-dimensional integral equations, but extending it to many-body systems and achieving NNLO accuracy (expected \(\sim 3\%\)) remains an open challenge.
