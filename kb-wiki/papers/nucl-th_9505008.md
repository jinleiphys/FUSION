# Spherical Shell Model description of rotational motion

> Exact diagonalizations with a realistic interaction show that configurations with four neutrons in a major shell and four protons in another -or the same- major shell, behave systematically as backbending rotors. The dominance of the $q\cdot q$ component of the interaction is explained by an approximate form of SU3 symmetry. It is suggested that these configurations are associated with the onset of rotational motion in medium and heavy nuclei.

# Spherical Shell Model description of rotational motion

## Key claim
Exact diagonalizations with a realistic interaction show that configurations with four neutrons and four protons in a major shell (or two different major shells) systematically behave as backbending rotors, but this behavior is restricted to the subspace spanned by the sequence of Δj=2 orbits that come lowest under spin-orbit splitting, and the dominance of the q·q component of the interaction is explained by an approximate form of SU3 symmetry (quasi-SU3) rather than exact SU3.

## Method
The authors performed exact shell-model diagonalizations in restricted single-major-shell spaces for four specific configurations: (fp)^8 T=0, (fp)_π^4(gds)_ν^4, (gds)^8 T=0, and (gds)_π^4(hfp)_ν^4, using both the KLS realistic interaction and a pure quadrupole force with ℏω=9 MeV and uniform single-particle spacing ε=1 MeV. They compared the resulting yrast bands, quadrupole moments Q_0(J), and wavefunction overlaps to establish that the quadrupole force alone reproduces the structure of the full Hamiltonian eigenstates. They introduced a "quasi-SU3" operator defined in the Δj=2 space by replacing LS coupling matrix elements with jj coupling equivalents.

## Key numbers
- The four diagonalizations had m-scheme dimensionalities: (fp)^8 T=0 at 2×10^4, (fp)_π^4(gds)_ν^4 at 1.1×10^5, (gds)^8 T=0 at 6×10^5, and (gds)_π^4(hfp)_ν^4 at 1.9×10^6.
- Q_0 values remained constant to within 5% up to a critical J value at which the bands backbend.
- At e_2=3.2, the overlap of each state with the full KLS interaction eigenstate was always better than (0.95)^2.
- For the eight-particle blocks, the intrinsic Q_0 formula gives Q_0=68 (dimensionless oscillator coordinates) for p_π=5, p_ν=6 with effective charges e_π=1.5, e_ν=0.5.
- The B(E2)↑ from this Q_0 is 1.4 e^2b^2 for A=166.
- Q_0 values with the q·q interaction at e_2=9.6 saturated the predicted value within 2%, while at e_2=2.4 they reached 80% of this limit.
- The mismatch between quasi-SU3 and exact SU3 was less than 1%.

## Context
This work extends the SU3 model of Elliott (1958) and the unified model of Bohr and Mottelson (1953) by showing that rotational motion can emerge from spherical shell-model calculations in restricted spaces, specifically configurations with four protons and four neutrons in major shells. It builds on earlier demonstrations that (ds)^4 describes 20Ne (Arima) and that (ds)_π^3,4(fp)_ν^2 configurations explain deformation in 31Na and 32Mg (Poves and Retamosa), as well as the full (pf)^8 calculation for 48Cr (Caurier et al., 1994). The quasi-SU3 symmetry introduced here provides a bridge between the spherical shell model and deformed mean-field descriptions, and the authors suggest that rotational motion sets in when eight particles are promoted to the (pfh)_π^4(sdgi)_ν^4 configuration, analogous to 4p-4h excitations in 16O and 40Ca. The work enables a shell-model approach to rotational nuclei in medium and heavy systems, though the authors note that the B(E2) estimate is a factor four smaller than the largest observed values in the region (Raman), indicating that lower orbits must supply additional quadrupole coherence through pseudo-SU3 symmetry.
