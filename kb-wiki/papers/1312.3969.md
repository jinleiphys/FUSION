# Background Electromagnetic Fields and NRQED Matching: Scalar Case

> The low-energy structure of hadrons can be described systematically using effective field theory, and the parameters of the effective theory can be determined from lattice QCD computations. Recent work, however, points to inconsistencies between the background field method in lattice QCD and effective field theory matching conditions. We show that the background field problem necessitates inclusion of operators related by equations of motion. In the presence of time-dependent electromagnetic fields, for example, such operators modify Green's functions, thereby complicating the isolation of hadronic parameters which enter on-shell scattering amplitudes. The particularly simple case of a scalar hadron coupled to uniform electromagnetic fields is investigated in detail. At the level of the relativistic effective theory, operators related by equations of motion are demonstrated to be innocuous. The same result does not hold in the non-relativistic effective theory, and inconsistencies in matching are resolved by carefully treating operators related by equations of motion. As uniform external fields potentially allow for surface terms, the problem is additionally analyzed on a torus where such terms are absent. Finite-size corrections are derived for charged scalar correlation functions in uniform electric fields as a useful byproduct.

## Key claim

The authors show that matching between relativistic and non-relativistic effective field theories in background electromagnetic fields requires retaining operators normally removed by equations of motion, because such operators modify Green's functions in the absence of an on-shell condition; for the specific case of uniform fields, however, the relativistic theory is immune to this complication, while the non-relativistic theory is not.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic effective field theory for a charged composite scalar coupled to uniform electromagnetic fields, including non-minimal photon couplings up to dimension-eight operators. They then formulate the corresponding non-relativistic theory using HQET (heavy quark effective theory) power counting, and compute charged scalar propagators in both uniform electric and magnetic fields using Schwinger's proper-time method. Matching between the relativistic and non-relativistic theories is performed by comparing Green's functions and by expanding the relativistic propagator in inverse powers of the scalar mass, demonstrating that an additional operator related by the non-relativistic equations of motion must be retained to resolve inconsistencies in the energy shift.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy shift of a charged scalar in a uniform electric field from the relativistic theory is \(\Delta E = -\frac{1}{2} 4\pi \alpha_E \mathcal{E}^2\), where \(\alpha_E\) is the electric polarizability and \(\mathcal{E}\) is the Euclidean electric field.
- The incorrect NRQED result (without the equation-of-motion operator) gives \(\Delta E = -\frac{1}{2} \left[ 4\pi \alpha_E - \frac{Z}{3M} \langle r^2 \rangle \right] \mathcal{E}^2\), where \(Z\) is the charge and \(\langle r^2 \rangle\) is the charge radius.
- The correct non-relativistic initial energy shift after proper matching is \(\Delta E = -\frac{1}{2} \left[ 4\pi \alpha_E + \frac{Z^2}{2M^3} \right] \mathcal{E}^2\).
- The Landau level energy for a charged scalar in a uniform magnetic field is \(E_n = \left[ M^2 + |ZB|(2n+1) - 4\pi \beta_M M B^2 \right]^{1/2}\), where \(\beta_M\) is the magnetic polarizability.
- The low-energy constants are related to observables as: \(C_0 = \pi M^3 (\alpha_E - \beta_M)\) and \(C_2 = 4\pi M^3 (\alpha_E + \beta_M)\).
- The charge radius is related to the coefficient \(C_1\) by \(\frac{C_1}{M^2} = \frac{1}{3!} \langle r^2 \rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the background field method in lattice QCD, previously used to compute hadronic properties such as electromagnetic polarizabilities (Detmold 2009, Lee 2005, Shintani 2006), by resolving inconsistencies between lattice QCD matching conditions and effective field theory. The authors build on the NRQED matching approach of Hill (2012) and show that operators related by equations of motion, which are ordinarily dropped in effective field theories (Arzt 1993), must be retained when matching Green's functions in external fields. This enables correct extraction of hadronic parameters like electric polarizabilities and charge radii from lattice QCD correlation functions in uniform electromagnetic fields, and provides finite-size corrections for charged scalar correlation functions on a torus.
