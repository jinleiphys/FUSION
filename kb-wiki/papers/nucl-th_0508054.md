# Pairing schemes for HFB calculations of nuclei

> Several pairing schemes currently used to describe superfluid nuclei through Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations are briefly reviewed. We put a particular emphasis on the regularization recipes used in connection with zero-range forces and on the density dependence which usually complement their definition. Regarding the chosen regularization process, the goal is not only to identify the impact it may or may not have on pairing properties of nuclei through spherical 1D HFB calculations but also to assess its tractability for systematic axial 2D and 3D mean-field and beyond-mean-field calculations.

## Key claim

The paper compares several pairing schemes for HFB calculations, finding that the choice of regularization recipe for zero-range forces has a relatively minor impact on physical observables (e.g., two-neutron separation energies and average pairing gaps), whereas the spatial character and isovector nature of the density dependence are more critical, especially for neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

Spherical 1D Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for even-even tin isotopes using the SLy5 Skyrme force in the particle-hole channel, while six different pairing schemes (ULB, DFTS, DFTM, RDFTM, ZFR, and FR) were compared in the particle-particle channel. The schemes differed in range (zero-range vs. finite-range), density dependence (surface-type, half-volume/half-surface, or microscopic form derived from the bare NN force), and regularization method (phenomenological energy windows, quasi-particle energy cutoffs, or microscopic regularization via effective coupling constants or occupation-number factors). Convergence of binding energy and occupation probabilities was studied as a function of the maximum single-particle energy included in the canonical basis.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{120}$Sn converged to 10 keV accuracy at $E_{\text{max}} \approx +10$ MeV for the finite-range force FR, versus $E_{\text{max}} \approx +80$ MeV for zero-range forces ZFR and RDFTM.
- For ULB (phenomenological window regularization), convergence to much better accuracy was achieved at $E_{\text{max}} \approx +5$ MeV.
- The converged binding energy of $^{120}$Sn differed by about 300 keV between DFTM and RDFTM (same density form factor, different regularization).
- DFTM and RDFTM provided identical two-neutron separation energies $S_{2N}$ for all spherical nuclei studied.
- Average neutron gaps $<\Delta^n(N)>_\kappa$ in tin isotopes were fitted to 1.245 MeV for $^{120}$Sn for DFTM, RDFTM, and DFTS.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of phenomenological Density-Dependent Delta Interactions (DDDIs) such as those used by Dobaczewski et al. (2002) and Duguet et al. (2002) with a microscopic pairing force (FR and ZFR) derived from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction by Duguet et al. (2004). It shows that surface-peaked DDDIs (e.g., DFTS) give too strong pairing in neutron-rich nuclei compared to half-volume/half-surface types (DFTM) or the microscopically derived ZFR, and that the regularization method matters less than the density dependence. The results enable systematic axial 2D and 3D mean-field and beyond-mean-field calculations by identifying which pairing schemes are tractable on a 3D mesh (ULB, FR, and ZFR being the most preferable).
