# Light mesons within the basis light-front quantization framework

> We study the light-unflavored mesons as relativistic bound states in the nonperturbative Hamiltonian formalism of the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach. The dynamics for the valence quarks of these mesons is specified by an effective Hamiltonian containing the one-gluon exchange interaction and the confining potentials both introduced in our previous work on heavy quarkonia, supplemented additionally by a pseudoscalar contact interaction. We diagonalize this Hamiltonian in our basis function representation to obtain the mass spectrum and the light-front wave functions (LFWFs). Based on these LFWFs, we then study the structure of these mesons by computing the electromagnetic form factors, the decay constants, the parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), and the parton distribution functions (PDFs). Our results are comparable to those from experiments and other theoretical models.

## Key claim

The authors extend their basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) effective Hamiltonian, previously successful for heavy quarkonia, to light-unflavored mesons by adding a pseudoscalar contact interaction inspired by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model; this addition is necessary to produce the pion-rho mass splitting, which the one-gluon exchange and confining potentials alone cannot generate.

## Method

The authors diagonalize an effective Hamiltonian containing light-front kinetic energy, transverse and longitudinal confinement potentials, one-gluon exchange with a running coupling, and a pseudoscalar contact interaction, within the valence quark-antiquark Fock sector. The Hamiltonian is represented in a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions for transverse modes and Jacobi polynomials for longitudinal modes, with truncation parameters N_max = 8 and L_max = 24. Model parameters (confining strength kappa, quark mass m_q, and pseudoscalar coupling lambda) are determined by fitting the masses of rho(770), rho'(1450), and pi(140) from the Particle Data Group. From the resulting light-front wave functions, the authors compute mass spectra, decay constants, electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, parton distribution amplitudes, and parton distribution functions.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia by Li, Vary et al. (2015, 2017) and to mesons with unequal mass quarks by Tang et al., now targeting light-unflavored mesons. It differs from the earlier BLFQ-NJL model by Jia et al. in that the mass spectroscopy is dominated by gluon dynamics rather than NJL interactions. The results are compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2018), with AdS/QCD holographic models, and with Bethe-Salpeter equation calculations by Fischer et al. (2014) and Williams et al. (2015). The computed observables (decay constants, charge radii) are systematically larger or smaller than experiment, which the authors attribute to the valence-only truncation and the treatment of quarks as point-like particles, suggesting that incorporating higher Fock sectors would improve agreement.
