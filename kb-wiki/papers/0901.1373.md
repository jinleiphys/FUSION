# Justifying the exotic Theta+ pentaquark

## Key claim
The existence of a light S=+1 baryon resonance follows from Quantum Field Theory applied to baryons, but the Skyrme model predicts a Theta+ resonance that is too strong (width overestimated), while a new mean field approach shows Theta+ arises as a necessary consequence of three known resonances: Lambda(1405), N(1440), and N(1535).

## Method
The author uses two theoretical frameworks: the Skyrme model (where KN scattering phase shifts are computed to extract the resonance pole) and a large-Nc mean field approach where baryons are treated as bound states of Nc quarks in a mean field that breaks SU(3) flavor symmetry. In the mean field picture, one-quark excitations of the filled s-quark and u,d-quark shells are identified with known resonances, and the Theta+ pentaquark emerges as a particle-hole excitation where an s quark is excited to an available u,d-quark level.

## Key numbers
- The Skyrme model gives a KN scattering amplitude pole at m_res - i Gamma/2 = 1449 - i 44 MeV for the standard parameters.
- The mean field estimate yields m_Theta ≈ 1440 + 1535 - 1405 = 1570 MeV, with an uncertainty of a few tens of MeV.
- The Theta+ width is estimated as Gamma_Theta ≤ 1 MeV, based on prior experimental work (Dolgolenko).
- Naive constituent quark counting would place a pentaquark uudd\bar s state at ≥ 1900 MeV, about 400 MeV heavier than the estimated Theta+ mass.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior theoretical prediction of a light and narrow exotic resonance (Diakonov, Petrov, Polyakov 1997) and responds to the Callan-Klebanov approach (Klebanov 1990s) which claimed no S=+1 kaon bound states or resonances in the Skyrme model. The paper argues that the Skyrme model does predict a strong Theta+ resonance but overestimates its width, while the new mean field picture shows the pentaquark is unavoidable given the existence of Lambda(1405), N(1440), and N(1535). The work enables experimental searches via interference with known resonances, as the interference cross sections are linear in the small coupling constants (Amarian).
