# Predictions for the LHC: an Overview

> I present an overview of predictions for the heavy ion program at the Large Hadron Collider. It is mainly based on the material presented during the workshop 'Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC - Last Call for Predictions', held in the frame of the CERN Theory Institute from May 14th to June 10th 2007. Predictions on both bulk properties and hard probes are reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a compilation of pre-LHC predictions for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV, based on the 2007 CERN workshop "Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC - Last Call for Predictions," with the caveat that many predictions carry large uncertainties and that the control of the benchmark (e.g., nuclear parton densities) is under poor control.

## Method

The author reviews predictions for bulk properties (multiplicities, azimuthal asymmetries, hadronic flavor observables, low-pT correlations) and hard/electromagnetic probes (high-pT observables and jets, quarkonium and heavy quarks, leptonic probes and photons) as presented at the workshop. Predictions are classified by theoretical approach (saturation physics, hydrodynamics, pQCD energy loss, statistical hadronization, etc.) and compared to each other and to RHIC results. For multiplicities, predictions are re-scaled to a common observable (dN_ch/deta at eta=0) and centrality class (mean N_part = 350) using the model of Amelin (2001).

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity predictions for central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC lie in the range 1000 to 2000 charged particles per unit pseudorapidity at eta=0, with the lowest values from extreme saturation models and data-driven predictions.
- For the nuclear suppression factor R_PbPb at pT=20 GeV/c and eta=0 in central collisions, pQCD-based models predict R^pi0_PbPb(pT=20 GeV/c, eta=0) approximately 0.1 to 0.2, increasing with increasing pT.
- The re-scaling table for central Pb-Pb collisions gives dN_ch/dy at y=0 = 3149 and dN_ch/deta at eta=0 = 2633 for the 0-3% centrality class (mean N_part = 390, mean b = 1.9 fm).
- For the 0-8.5% centrality class (mean N_part = 350, mean b = 3.1 fm), dN_ch/dy at y=0 = 2686 and dN_ch/deta at eta=0 = 2245.
- Net baryon number p-pbar at mid-rapidity is generically predicted to be less than 4 across a wide variety of models.
- The uncertainty in nuclear effects for the gluon distribution in Pb at x ~ 10^-4 is approximately a factor of 10.

## Context

This overview extends the pre-RHIC predictions compiled by Bass (1999) and the pre-LHC multiplicity predictions by Armesto (2000), now incorporating RHIC-tested models extrapolated to LHC energies. The paper enables direct comparison of theoretical predictions with upcoming LHC data, with the expectation that major deviations from the generic expectations (multiplicity below 2000, v2 similar or smaller than at RHIC, R_PbPb ~ 0.1-0.2) would enlarge understanding of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The work also highlights where naive data-driven extrapolations (Borghini, 2007) disagree with successful RHIC models, and identifies open questions requiring further theoretical effort, particularly for jet reconstruction and higher quarkonium states.
