# Anomalous decays of pseudoscalar mesons

> In this thesis we discuss the decays of the pseudoscalar mesons (the neutral pion, the eta and eta-prime) which are governed by the chiral anomaly. Some of these decays are of special interest because their study permits a deep insight into aspects of modern physics. The decays can be classified according to the type of the anomaly (triangle or box) that governs the corresponding amplitude when it is extrapolated to the chiral point. In order to take the momentum dependencies of the form factors into account, the actual calculations are performed in the frame work of vector meson dominance (VMD) models, namely in terms of the original hidden gauge model and modern generalized models. The results of the triangle-anomaly sector show that the hidden gauge model and the modern modifications can describe the data equally well. In the box-anomaly sector the data have a slight preference to the modern modified VMD formulation. The results of the latter also agree very well with the predictions of unitarized chiral perturbation theory. In the case of the box-anomaly decays, we especially analyze the contributions of an unconventional untested CP-violating electric dipole form factor that is sensitive to the strange-quark/antiquark-content of the eta and eta-prime mesons. We verify the claim that it could still be of natural size by comparing with the new KLOE data for eta-> pi+ pi- e+ e-.

## Key claim

The thesis tests whether vector meson dominance (VMD) models, specifically the hidden gauge model and modern generalized formulations, can describe the anomalous decays of pseudoscalar mesons (π⁰, η, η′), with the caveat that in the box-anomaly sector the data show a slight preference for the modern modified VMD formulation, and that an untested CP-violating electric dipole form factor in η → π⁺π⁻e⁺e⁻ could still be of natural size when compared to new KLOE data.

## Method

The author calculated decay rates and branching ratios for triangle-anomaly decays (e.g., π⁰ → γγ, η → γγ, η → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻γ) and box-anomaly decays (e.g., η → π⁺π⁻γ, η → π⁺π⁻ℓ⁺ℓ⁻) using two VMD frameworks: the original hidden gauge model and the modern modified VMD model of Benayoun et al. (2007, 2009). For box-anomaly decays, the analysis included contributions from a CP-violating electric dipole form factor sensitive to the strange-quark content of η and η′ mesons. The theoretical predictions were compared to experimental data from η-factories such as KLOE@DAΦNE and WASA@COSY.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of anomalous pseudoscalar meson decays beyond the Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian (Wess 1971, Witten 1983) by incorporating vector meson dominance models, specifically the hidden gauge model of Bando et al. (1984) and Fujiwara et al. (1984) and the modern modified VMD of Benayoun et al. (2007, 2009). The results for the box-anomaly sector agree well with predictions from unitarized chiral perturbation theory. The analysis enables future tests of CP-violating form factors in η and η′ decays, particularly through asymmetry measurements of decay planes in η → π⁺π⁻ℓ⁺ℓ⁻, and provides a handbook for students working on anomalous decays.
