# Global Ab initio Neutrino Mass Limits from Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay

> We present global limits for Majorana neutrino masses by combining latest results from neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay searches and ab initio nuclear theory. Limits are derived in a Bayesian framework utilizing likelihood functions from a suite of $0νββ$-decay experiments in conjunction with nuclear matrix elements calculated from nuclear and electroweak forces derived from chiral effective field theory and implemented in the in-medium similarity renormalization group many-body approach. In contrast to nuclear models, ab initio results indicate that the current generation of $0νββ$-decay experiments have likely \textit{not} yet reached sensitivities required to probe the mass regime allowed by neutrino-oscillation data, where the combined bounds are notably stronger than those given by individual experiments. Finally, from predicted sensitivities of next-generation searches, we show that, while no one individual experiment fully covers the inverted mass ordering, this can be achieved from combined contributions from the four key isotopes: $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, when using ab initio nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) rather than phenomenological nuclear models, current-generation neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments have likely *not* yet reached the sensitivity needed to probe the Majorana neutrino mass range allowed by neutrino-oscillation data, and that no single next-generation experiment will fully cover the inverted mass ordering, but a combination of experiments across four key isotopes can achieve this.

## Method

The authors combine likelihood functions from six current-generation experiments (GERDA, LEGEND-200, CUPID-Mo, CUORE, EXO-200, KamLAND-Zen) and nine next-generation projected experiments (nEXO, LEGEND-1000, CUPID, CUPID-1T, SNO+, AMoRE-II, NEXT-HD, PandaX-xT, DARWIN, XLZD) in a Bayesian framework. Nuclear matrix elements for the isotopes ⁷⁶Ge, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹³⁰Te, and ¹³⁶Xe are computed using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) ab initio method, with nuclear and electroweak forces derived from chiral effective field theory. The short-range contact contribution to the NME, required for renormalization, is included with the coupling adjusted to synthetic data.

## Key numbers

* Combined current-generation 90% credible interval upper limit on the effective Majorana neutrino mass using ab initio NMEs and a Γ⁰ν uniform prior: mββ ≤ 77–132 meV.
* Combined current-generation 90% credible interval upper limit using phenomenological NMEs and a Γ⁰ν uniform prior: mββ ≤ 30–76 meV.
* Combined next-generation projected 90% exclusion sensitivity using ab initio NMEs: mββ ≤ 7.4–13.1 meV (or mββ ≤ 5.6–10.8 meV with CUPID-1T included).
* Combined next-generation projected 90% exclusion sensitivity using phenomenological NMEs: mββ ≤ 2.9–7.8 meV (or mββ ≤ 2.3–4.1 meV with CUPID-1T included).
* VS-IMSRG NME ranges: ⁷⁶Ge 2.09–3.09, ¹⁰⁰Mo 1.80–3.96, ¹³⁰Te 1.52–2.40, ¹³⁶Xe 1.08–1.90 (dimensionless).
* IMSRG with uncertainty quantification for ⁷⁶Ge: NME = 2.76⁺¹·²⁶₋₁·₄₄.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian combination approach introduced by Biller (2021) and subsequently used by Jokiniemi (2021) and Pompa (2023), now applying it with ab initio NMEs from the VS-IMSRG method rather than phenomenological models such as QRPA, nuclear shell model, IBM, DFT, or GCF. The results contradict the suggestion from phenomenological NMEs that current xenon-based experiments have partially probed the inverted mass ordering. The work enables a quantitative assessment of the combined reach of next-generation experiments, showing that a worldwide effort across multiple isotopes is necessary to fully cover the inverted mass ordering, and provides a framework for incorporating future ab initio uncertainty quantification across all relevant isotopes.
