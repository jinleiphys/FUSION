# Baryon superfluidity and neutrino emissivity of neutron stars

> For neutron stars with hyperon-mixed core, neutrino emissivity is studied under the equation of state, obtained by introducing repulsive three-body force universal for all baryons so as to assure the maximum mass compatible with the observation. By paying attention to the density-dependence of critical temperatures of baryon superfluids, which reflect the nature of baryon-baryon interaction and control neutron star cooling, we show what neutrino emission processes are efficient in the regions with and without hyperon mixing and remark its implications related to neutron star cooling.

## Key claim
The paper shows that hyperon direct URCA neutrino emission can dominate cooling in neutron stars with hyperon-mixed cores, but only if the Lambda hyperon critical temperature is not too large; specifically, the observed colder class neutron star data are reproduced only when the Lambda superfluidity suppresses the direct URCA process with a critical temperature around 10^9 K.

## Method
The authors adopt the TNI6u equation of state, which includes a repulsive three-body force universal for all baryons, to compute the composition of neutron star matter including Lambda and Sigma- hyperons. They calculate critical temperatures for baryon superfluids using realistic NN potentials (AV18, OPEG) and a YY potential (ND-soft), then compute neutrino emissivities from three processes: hyperon direct URCA, nucleon modified URCA, and the Cooper-pair process, as functions of density at a fixed internal temperature of 10^8 K.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star mass for TNI6u-EOS: M_max = 1.71 solar masses
- Central density for M = 1.44 solar masses: rho_c = 4.5 rho_0 (nuclear density)
- Lambda and Sigma- mixing onset density: rho = 4 rho_0
- Lambda critical temperature: T_c^(Lambda) ~ 10^9 K (0.1 MeV), existing only in the density range rho = (4.0-5.3) rho_0
- Sigma- critical temperature: T_c^(Sigma-) ~ 10^9 - 10^10 K
- Neutron 3P2 critical temperature peak values: (6-8) x 10^8 K
- Hyperon direct URCA emissivity without suppression: ~ 10^20 T_8^6 erg/cm^3·sec, where T_8 = T/10^8 K
- Nucleon modified URCA emissivity without suppression: ~ 10^13 erg/cm^3·sec at T_8 = 1
- Colder class NS data: L_obs ~ 10^33 to 10^31.5 ergs/sec for ages 10^3 to 10^5.5 years
- Hotter class NS data: L_obs ~ 10^34 to 10^32 ergs/sec for ages 10^3 to 10^6 years

## Context
This work extends the equation of state studies by Nishizaki, Yamamoto and Takatsuka (2002) who introduced the universal three-body force TNI6u to resolve the hyperon softening problem that previously gave maximum masses below the observed 1.44 solar masses. The results enable comparison with thermal evolution calculations by Tsuruta and collaborators (2003), showing that the colder class neutron stars require hyperon-mixed cores with Lambda superfluidity at T_c ~ 10^9 K, while hotter class stars without hyperon mixing can be explained by Cooper-pair cooling balanced by vortex creep heating. The paper also corrects a mistaken statement from Maxwell (1979) and Yakovlev et al. (1999, 2001) that neutral currents do not couple to Lambda and Sigma- hyperons.
