# Estimate of Background Baseline and Upper Limit on the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Isobar Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=200$ GeV at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider

> For the search of the chiral magnetic effect (CME), STAR previously presented the results from isobar collisions (${^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}}+{^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}}$, ${^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}}+{^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}}$) obtained through a blind analysis. The ratio of results in Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr collisions for the CME-sensitive charge-dependent azimuthal correlator ($Δγ$), normalized by elliptic anisotropy ($v_{2}$), was observed to be close to but systematically larger than the inverse multiplicity ratio. The background baseline for the isobar ratio, $Y = \frac{(Δγ/v_{2})^{\text{Ru}}}{(Δγ/v_{2})^{\text{Zr}}}$, is naively expected to be $\frac{(1/N)^{\text{Ru}}}{(1/N)^{\text{Zr}}}$; however, genuine two- and three-particle correlations are expected to alter it. We estimate the contributions to $Y$ from those correlations, utilizing both the isobar data and HIJING simulations. After including those contributions, we arrive at a final background baseline for $Y$, which is consistent with the isobar data. We extract an upper limit for the CME fraction in the $Δγ$ measurement of approximately $10\%$ at a $95\%$ confidence level on in isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200$ GeV, with an expected $15\%$ difference in their squared magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the background baseline for the isobar ratio \(Y = \frac{(\Delta\gamma/v_{2})^{\text{Ru}}}{(\Delta\gamma/v_{2})^{\text{Zr}}}\) is consistent with the measured isobar data after accounting for genuine two- and three-particle correlations, and extracts an upper limit on the chiral magnetic effect (CME) fraction in the \(\Delta\gamma\) measurement of approximately 10% at a 95% confidence level, conditional on an expected 15% difference in the squared magnetic fields between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed data from Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=200\) GeV, using the same event and track selections as the previous blind analysis. They decomposed the background contributions to the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator \(\Delta\gamma\) into two-particle (2p) and three-particle (3p) nonflow correlations, and estimated the isobar ratio of these backgrounds using the measured relative excess of opposite-sign over same-sign pion pairs (\(r = (N_{\text{OS}} - N_{\text{SS}})/N_{\text{OS}}\)), the nonflow fraction in the elliptic flow measurement (\(\epsilon_{\text{nf}}\)), and the three-particle correlation terms (\(C_{\text{3p}}\)). The analysis utilized both the isobar data and HIJING simulations to quantify these contributions.

## Key numbers

- The isobar ratio of the relative excess of opposite-sign over same-sign pion pairs, \(\delta r / r\), was measured as a function of centrality; for example, in the 20-50% centrality range, the ratio of \(r\) in Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr deviates from the inverse multiplicity ratio.
- The nonflow fraction \(\epsilon_{\text{nf}} = v_{2,\text{nf}}^{2}/v_{2}^{2}\) was quantified using the two-particle cumulant method; the value of \(\epsilon_{\text{nf}}\) contributes to the background baseline via the term \(-\delta\epsilon_{\text{nf}}/(1+\epsilon_{\text{nf}})\) in Eq. (4).
- The upper limit on the CME fraction in the \(\Delta\gamma\) measurement is approximately 10% at a 95% confidence level, based on an expected 15% difference in the squared magnetic fields between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions.

## Context

This work extends the STAR isobar collision analysis (STAR, 2021) which reported the ratio of \(\Delta\gamma/v_{2}\) in Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr to be close to but systematically larger than the inverse multiplicity ratio. The present paper provides a rigorous estimate of the background baseline by incorporating contributions from two- and three-particle correlations, using both data and HIJING simulations, and enables a quantitative upper limit on the CME signal. It builds on prior studies of nonflow backgrounds in \(\Delta\gamma\) measurements (Voloshin, 2004; Wang, 2009; Bzdak, 2009; Schlichting, 2010; STAR, 2022) and the theoretical expectation of a stronger magnetic field in Ru+Ru due to its larger proton number (Kharzeev, 2022).
