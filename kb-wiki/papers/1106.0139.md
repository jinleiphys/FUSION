# Quark Wigner Distributions and Orbital Angular Momentum

> We study the Wigner functions of the nucleon which provide multidimensional images of the quark distributions in phase space. These functions can be obtained through a Fourier transform in the transverse space of the generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions. They depend on both the transverse position and the three-momentum of the quark relative to the nucleon, and therefore combine in a single picture all the information contained in the generalized parton distributions and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions. We focus the discussion on the distributions of unpolarized/longitudinally polarized quark in an unpolarized/longitudinally polarized nucleon. In this way, we can study the role of the orbital angular momentum of the quark in shaping the nucleon and its correlations with the quark and nucleon polarizations. The quark orbital angular momentum is also calculated from its phase-space average weighted with the Wigner distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon. The corresponding results obtained within different light-cone quark models are compared with alternative definitions of the quark orbital angular momentum, as given in terms of generalized parton distributions and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quark Wigner distributions, obtained via Fourier transform of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs), provide a unified phase-space picture of the nucleon that contains all information from both generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), and that the quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) can be computed from a phase-space average of the Wigner distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon, though this definition may differ from the GPD-based Ji sum rule when gauge-field degrees of freedom are included.

## Method

The authors define five-dimensional Wigner distributions (two transverse position and three momentum coordinates) as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of GTMDs in the infinite momentum frame, focusing on the twist-two sector with longitudinal polarizations. They compute the first x-moment (transverse Wigner distributions) in two light-cone quark models: the light-cone constituent quark model (LCCQM) and the light-cone version of the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) restricted to the three-quark sector, using an overlap representation of the quark-quark correlator in terms of light-cone wave functions without gauge-field contributions. The quark OAM is then evaluated as the phase-space average of the cross product of transverse position and momentum weighted by the Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- For unpolarized quarks in an unpolarized proton, the average quadrupole distortion Q_b(k_perp) in the LCCQM for u quarks is -0.04 at k_perp = 0.1 GeV, -0.14 at k_perp = 0.2 GeV, and -0.30 at k_perp = 0.3 GeV; for d quarks the corresponding values are -0.03, -0.13, and -0.27.
- The average quadrupole distortion Q_k(b_perp) in the LCCQM for u quarks is -0.15 at b_perp = 0.2 fm, -0.24 at b_perp = 0.4 fm, and -0.29 at b_perp = 0.6 fm; for d quarks the values are -0.10, -0.19, and -0.27.
- The valence-quark number for the proton is N^u = 2 and N^d = 1.
- The fixed transverse momentum used in the impact-parameter space plots is k_perp = 0.3 GeV; the fixed impact parameter used in the transverse-momentum space plots is b_perp = 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Wigner distributions in QCD first explored by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003), who used a nonrelativistic three-dimensional Fourier transform in the Breit frame, by instead working in the infinite momentum frame to avoid relativistic corrections. The paper connects to the GTMD formalism of Meissner (2009) and provides a phenomenological study using light-cone quark models previously applied to GPDs by Boffi (2002) and Pasquini (2005) and to TMDs by Pasquini (2008) and Boffi (2009). The results enable future comparisons between the Wigner-based definition of quark OAM and alternative definitions from GPDs (Ji sum rule, 1996) and TMDs (pretzelosity relation from Avakian, 2008), and can be extended to include gauge-field contributions from Wilson lines.
