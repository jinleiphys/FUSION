# Fast nucleon emission as a probe of the isospin momentum dependence

> In this article we investigate the structure of the non-local part of the symmetry term, that leads to a splitting of the effective masses of protons and neutrons in asymmetric matter. Based on microscopic transport simulations we suggest some rather sensitive observables in collisions of neutron-rich (unstable) ions at intermediate ($RIA$) energies. In particular we focus the attention on pre-equilibrium nucleon emissions. We discuss interesting correlations between the N/Z content of the fast emitted particles and their rapidity or transverse momentum, that show a nice dependence on the prescription used for the effective mass splitting.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the isospin content (N/Z ratio) of fast pre-equilibrium nucleons emitted in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies (50-100 AMeV) is a sensitive observable for determining the sign of the neutron/proton effective mass splitting in asymmetric nuclear matter, which remains an open and controversial problem.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic transport simulations of the BNV (Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov) type for central collisions of 132Sn + 124Sn at 50 and 100 AMeV with an impact parameter of 2 fm. They used an Iso-MD effective interaction derived from an asymmetric extension of the GBD force, with parameters adjusted to produce the same density dependence of the symmetry energy but with two opposite signs of the n/p effective mass splitting (m*n > m*p versus m*n < m*p). The N/Z content of the nucleonic gas phase was analyzed as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity at different times during the reaction.

## Key numbers

- The absolute value of the effective mass splitting between neutrons and protons is approximately 10% at normal nuclear density for an asymmetry of I=0.2
- The compressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter used in the simulations is K_NM(rho_0) = 215 MeV
- Simulations were performed at beam energies of 50 AMeV and 100 AMeV
- The initial average asymmetry of the combined system is N/Z = 1.56
- The local low density selection for the gas phase uses a threshold of rho < rho_0/8
- The impact parameter used is b = 2 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the momentum dependence of the symmetry term and the effective mass splitting, which had been investigated in Skyrme forces (such as SIII, SKM*) and Lyon parametrizations (SLy4, SLy7), as well as in microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) and non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations. The paper enables experimental tests at radioactive beam facilities by proposing specific observables (N/Z versus transverse momentum or rapidity) that can distinguish between the two competing predictions for the sign of the effective mass splitting, complementing earlier work by Bao et al. (2004) at 400 AMeV.
