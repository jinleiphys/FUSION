# ANCs of the bound states of $^{16}$O deduced from elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering data

> Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of the $0_1^+$, $0_2^+$, $1_1^-$, $2_1^+$, $3_1^-$ ($l_{i th}^π$) bound states of $^{16}$O are deduced from the phase shift data of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies. $S$ matrices of elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering are constructed within cluster effective field theory (EFT), in which both bound and resonant states of $^{16}$O are considered. Parameters in the $S$ matrices are fitted to the precise phase shift data below the $p$-$^{15}$N breakup energy for the partial waves of $l=0,1,2,3,4,5,6$, and the ANCs are calculated by using the wave function normalization factors of $^{16}$O propagators for $l=0,1,2,3$. We review the values of ANCs, which are compared with other results in the literature, and discuss uncertainties of the ANCs obtained from the elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering data in cluster EFT.

## Key claim

Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the bound states of 16O are deduced from elastic α-12C scattering phase shift data using cluster effective field theory, but the values show significant model dependence and the ANC for the 21+ state obtained from elastic scattering is about 6-7 times smaller than those from α-transfer reactions, indicating a persistent discrepancy.

## Method

S-matrices for elastic α-12C scattering were constructed within cluster effective field theory for partial waves l=0 through 6, incorporating both bound and resonant states of 16O. The effective range parameters in the S-matrices were fitted to the precise phase shift data from Tischhauser et al. (2009) for α energies between 2.6 MeV and 6.62 MeV, below the p-15N breakup threshold. The ANCs were then calculated using the wave function normalization factors of the dressed 16O propagators for l=0,1,2,3 via the formula of Iwinski, Rosenberg, and Spruch (1984).

## Key numbers

* ANC of the 11- state of 16O: |Cb|1 = 1.727(3) × 1014 fm-1/2 from elastic α-12C scattering in this work
* ANC of the 21+ state of 16O: |Cb|2 = 0.31(6) × 105 fm-1/2 from elastic α-12C scattering in this work
* ANC of the 01+ ground state of 16O: |Cb|01 = 45.5 ± 0.3 fm-1/2 from elastic α-12C scattering in this work
* ANC of the 02+ state of 16O: |Cb|02 = 621 ± 9 fm-1/2 from elastic α-12C scattering in this work (including 01+ state); 370 ± 25 fm-1/2 without 01+ state
* ANC of the 31- state of 16O: |Cb|3 = 113 ± 8 fm-1/2 from elastic α-12C scattering in this work
* The phase shift data used cover α energies from 2.6 MeV to 6.62 MeV in the laboratory frame
* The Gamow-peak energy for helium burning is EG = 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous cluster EFT studies of elastic α-12C scattering (Ando 2016, 2018, 2022) and the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction (Ando 2019, 2021). The results are compared with ANCs from α-transfer reactions reported by Brune (1999), Belhout (2007), Oulebsir (2012), Avila (2015), and Adhikari (2017), as well as R-matrix analyses by deBoer (2013, 2017) and others. The discrepancy for the 21+ state ANC between elastic scattering and transfer reactions remains unresolved, though a recent re-evaluation by Hebborn (2023) using a new ANC for 6Li brings the 11- ANC from transfer reactions into better agreement with the present cluster EFT result. The ANCs are essential inputs for extrapolating the 12C(α,γ)16O reaction rate to the Gamow-peak energy, which determines the C/O ratio in stellar helium burning.
