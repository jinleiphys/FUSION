# Linearly stable and causal relativistic first-order spin hydrodynamics

> We derive equations of motion for dissipative spin hydrodynamics from kinetic theory up to first order in a gradient expansion. Choosing a specific form of the matching conditions, relating the change in the spin potential to the spin diffusion and spin energy, we then show that the equations of motion, linearized around homogeneous global equilibrium, are causal and stable in any Lorentz frame, if certain sufficient conditions on the transport coefficients are fulfilled.

## Key claim

The paper derives a first-order relativistic spin hydrodynamics that is causal and stable in any Lorentz frame when linearized around homogeneous global equilibrium, provided certain sufficient conditions on the transport coefficients are fulfilled; this result depends on a specific choice of matching conditions that relate the spin potential to spin diffusion and spin energy, which is a sufficient but not necessary condition for stability and causality.

## Method

The author starts from the Boltzmann equation in extended phase space and expands the distribution function in dissipative spin moments up to first order in a gradient expansion. The equations of motion for the spin potential are obtained from the conservation of the spin tensor, with non-equilibrium contributions expressed in terms of gradients of thermodynamic potentials. Matching conditions are chosen that relate the dissipative components of the spin tensor (spin energy and spin diffusion) to time-like derivatives of the spin potential, and the resulting equations are linearized around homogeneous, non-rotating, unpolarized global equilibrium to obtain dispersion relations for longitudinal and transverse spin modes.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal spin modes yield dispersion relations from the determinant condition: $(-i\Omega-(\mathfrak{c}_1+\mathfrak{c}_2) k^{2}+\mathfrak{c}_0 \Omega^{2})(-i\Omega-(\mathfrak{d}_1+\mathfrak{d}_2) k^{2}+\mathfrak{d}_0 \Omega^{2})=0$.
* The transverse spin modes yield a quartic dispersion relation: $\mathfrak{c}_0\mathfrak{d}_0\Omega^4 -i(\mathfrak{c}_0+\mathfrak{d}_0)\Omega^3-(1+\mathfrak{c}_{(0} \mathfrak{d}_{2)}k^2 )\Omega^2+(i\mathfrak{d}_2k^2+i\mathfrak{c}_2k^2)\Omega+\mathfrak{c}_2 \mathfrak{d}_2 k^4-\mathfrak{c}_3\mathfrak{d}_3 k^2 =0$.
* The stability condition requires $\text{Im}\, \Omega(k) \geq 0$ for all modes, and the causality condition requires $\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} \left|\text{Re}\, \frac{\partial\Omega(k)}{\partial k}\right|<1$.

## Context

This work extends the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) framework for causal and stable first-order hydrodynamics to include spin degrees of freedom, addressing the known instability and acausality of Navier-Stokes-type spin hydrodynamics found in earlier work by Hattori (2019) and Hu (2021) and confirmed by Sarwar (2022), Daher (2022), Biswas (2022), and Xie (2023). It provides a first-order alternative to the second-order spin hydrodynamics derived from kinetic theory by Weickgenannt (2022) and from entropy-current analysis by Biswas (2023), enabling simpler causal and stable descriptions of spin dynamics in systems sufficiently close to local equilibrium, such as those studied in heavy-ion collision polarization phenomena.
