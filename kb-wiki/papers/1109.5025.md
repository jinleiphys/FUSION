# Medium-induced color flow softens hadronization

> Medium-induced parton energy loss, resulting from gluon exchanges between the QCD matter and partonic projectiles, is expected to underly the strong suppression of jets and high-$p_T$ hadron spectra observed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Here, we present the first color-differential calculation of parton energy loss. We find that color exchange between medium and projectile enhances the invariant mass of energetic color singlet clusters in the parton shower by a parametrically large factor proportional to the square root of the projectile energy. This effect is seen in more than half of the most energetic color-singlet fragments of medium-modified parton branchings. Applying a standard cluster hadronization model, we find that it leads to a characteristic additional softening of hadronic spectra. A fair description of the nuclear modification factor measured at the LHC may then be obtained for relatively low momentum transfers from the medium.

## Key claim

The first color-differential calculation of parton energy loss shows that color exchange between the QCD medium and the projectile enhances the invariant mass of energetic color-singlet clusters in the parton shower by a parametrically large factor proportional to the square root of the projectile energy, and this effect is seen in more than half of the most energetic color-singlet fragments of medium-modified parton branchings. A fair description of the nuclear modification factor measured at the LHC may then be obtained for relatively low momentum transfers from the medium.

## Method

The authors performed a first-order opacity expansion calculation of medium-induced gluon radiation from a quark projectile, separating the color-differential contributions to the radiation spectrum into virtual corrections, low-invariant-mass clusters, and high-invariant-mass clusters. They then applied a standard cluster hadronization model (similar to that in the HERWIG event generator) to translate the modified color flow into hadronic spectra, parametrizing the resulting suppression of the nuclear modification factor R_AA.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass of the most energetic color-singlet cluster in the high-mass channel is M^2_ct ~ p^+ Q_T, where Q_T = sqrt(2) E_th and E_th is the typical thermal energy of the target component; this is parametrically larger than the vacuum case by a factor sqrt(p^+).
- For a medium temperature T = 200 (500) MeV, the thermal energy scale Q_T is about 760 (1900) MeV, corresponding to a critical parton energy p_cr^+ = 21.0 (8.4) GeV above which additional cluster decays occur.
- The cluster hadronization model uses a hadronic scale Q_0 = 600 MeV and a critical cluster mass M_cr = 4 GeV.
- The color-induced suppression factor F(P^+) is plotted for cluster energies above P_cr, persisting up to cluster energies well above 100 GeV.
- Supplementing a baseline R_AA^fact (from a model with quenching parameter hat{q} = 1 GeV^2/fm) with color-flow suppression in f_t = 50% (100%) of fragmentation patterns reduces R_AA by about 0.1 (0.2).

## Context

This work extends standard parton energy loss calculations (reviewed by Armesto (2011)) by adding the first color-differential treatment, which had been neglected in prior models such as those of Chen (2011), Arleo (2011), Horowitz (2011), Lokhtin (2011), Zakharov (2011), and Fochler (2011). It enables future full microscopic simulations of parton energy loss that include medium-modified color flow, which could give insight into changes in hadrochemistry, jet fragmentation patterns, and possible differences between the R_AA of reconstructed jets and leading hadrons.
