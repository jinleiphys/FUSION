# Photoproduction of Theta^+(1540,1/2^+) reexamined with new theoretical information

> We reinvestigate the photoproduction of the exotic pentaquark baryon Theta^+(1540,1/2^+) from the gamma N -> Kbar Theta^+ reaction process within the effective Lagrangian approach, taking into account new theoretical information on the K-N-Theta and K*-N-Theta coupling strengths from the chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM). We also consider the crossing-symmetric hadronic form factor, satisfying the on-shell condition as well. Due to the sizable vector and tensor couplings for the vector kaon, g_{K* N Theta} and f_{K* N Theta}, which are almost the same with the vector coupling g_{K N Theta} ~ 0.8 for the pseudoscalar kaon, the K*-exchange contribution plays a critical role in the photon beam asymmetries.

## Key claim

The photoproduction cross sections of the exotic pentaquark baryon Theta^+(1540,1/2^+) are predicted to be tiny (less than about 1 nb), consistent with the null results from CLAS and the small signals from LEPS and DIANA, provided that the K-N-Theta and K*-N-Theta coupling constants from the chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM) are used without adjustable parameters.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach in the Born approximation to compute the gamma N -> Kbar Theta^+ reaction, incorporating s-channel, u-channel, t-channel K-exchange, and t-channel K*-exchange diagrams. They used coupling constants and transition form factors for the K-N-Theta and K*-N-Theta vertices derived from the chiral quark-soliton model (chiQSM) with SU(3) symmetry breaking effects, and applied a crossing-symmetric hadronic form factor that satisfies the on-shell condition and the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- The K-N-Theta coupling constant from chiQSM is g_{KNTheta} = 0.83 (with m_s = 180 MeV, M = 420 MeV), leading to a Theta^+ decay width Gamma_{Theta -> NK} = 0.71 MeV.
- The K*-N-Theta vector coupling constant is g_{K*NTheta} = 0.81 and the tensor coupling constant is f_{K*NTheta} = 0.84 (with m_s = 180 MeV, M = 420 MeV).
- The total cross section for gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ is about 30% larger than that for gamma p -> Kbar^0 Theta^+, with both being less than about 1 nb.
- The K*-exchange contribution accounts for approximately 30% of the total cross section for the neutron target, and essentially the entire cross section for the proton target (where K-exchange is absent).
- The differential cross section for the LEPS experiment was estimated to be (12 +/- 2) nb/sr in the photon energy range 2.0-2.4 GeV in the LEPS angular range.
- The 95% confidence level upper limits on total cross sections from CLAS lie mostly in the range 0.3-0.8 nb.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian studies of Theta^+ photoproduction by Nam (2005), Liu (2003), Oh (2003), and Nam (2004), which suffered from unknown coupling constants and cut-off masses. The authors incorporate new chiQSM results from Ledwig (2008) and Ledwig (2009) that provide parameter-free predictions for the K-N-Theta and K*-N-Theta couplings. The results help explain why the Theta^+ is so elusive experimentally, as the predicted cross sections are below the CLAS upper limits, and they enable future experimental searches to focus on photon beam asymmetry measurements, where the K*-exchange contribution produces distinctive signatures.
