# Weak Hypernuclear Decay

> Because of Pauli suppression effects the $Nπ$ decay of the free $Λ$ is not of importance in hypernuclei with $A\geq 10$. Rather the decay of such hypernuclei proceeds via the nucleon-stimulated mode $ΛN\rightarrow NN$, analysis of which presents a considerable theoretical challenge and about which there exists only a limited amount of experimental information. Herein we confront existing data wih various theoretical analyses which have been developed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei (ΛN → NN) is the dominant decay mode for A ≥ 10 due to Pauli suppression of the free Λ's pionic decay, but that theoretical predictions remain uncertain because the required weak couplings for exchanged mesons beyond the pion cannot be measured directly and must be estimated using models.

## Method
The author reviews existing experimental data on hypernuclear lifetimes and decay ratios, then compares them with theoretical calculations performed in a nuclear matter (Fermi gas) model and in finite nuclei. The theoretical framework uses a one-meson-exchange picture (π, ρ, ω, η, K, K*) with weak vertices evaluated via SU(6)w symmetry, current algebra, and pole models, including short-range correlation effects.

## Key numbers
- Free Λ decay rate: Γ_Λ = 1/(263 ps); branching ratios: pπ⁻ 64.1%, nπ⁰ 35.7%
- Pionic decay suppression in hypernuclei: Γ_π/Γ_Λ ~ 1/20 for A=10, ~ 1/120 for A=25
- BNL experimental nonmesonic decay rates (Γ_NM/Γ_Λ): ⁵_ΛHe = 0.41 ± 0.14, ¹¹_ΛB = 1.25 ± 0.16, ¹²_ΛC = 1.14 ± 0.20
- BNL experimental p/n stimulation ratios: ⁵_ΛHe = 1.07 ± 0.58, ¹¹_ΛB = 0.96^{+0.8}_{-0.4}, ¹²_ΛC = 0.75^{+1.5}_{-0.35}
- Delayed fission measurements: Γ(²³⁸_ΛU) = (1.0^{+1.0}_{-0.5}) × 10⁻¹⁰ s, Γ(²⁰⁹_ΛBi) = (2.5^{+2.5}_{-1.0}) × 10⁻¹⁰ s
- Nuclear matter pion-only calculation (with correlations): Γ_NM/Γ_Λ = 1.82 (UMass), 2.31 (McK-Gib), 2.1 (Oset-Sal)
- Full meson exchange calculation in nuclear matter (π,ρ,ω,η,K,K*): Γ_NM/Γ_Λ = 1.23, p/n ratio = 2.9, PV/PC ratio = 0.90
- Proton asymmetry parameter α: -0.078 (π no corr.), -0.192 (π corr.), -0.443 (all exchanges)
- For ¹²_ΛC with full exchange (UMass): Γ_NM/Γ_Λ = 0.2, p/n = 1.2, PV/PC = 0.8
- For ⁵_ΛHe with full exchange (UMass): Γ_NM/Γ_Λ = 0.5, p/n = 2.1
- Experimental ratios for light hypernuclei: γ_4 = 0.27 ± 0.14, γ_5 = 0.93 ± 0.55, γ = 0.73^{+0.71}_{-0.22}

## Context
This paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of weak hypernuclear decay as of 1995, extending earlier work on nuclear parity violation by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) to the hypernuclear case. It compares calculations by Adams (1983), McKellar and Gibson (1984), Oset and Salcedo (1985), and the UMass group, and highlights discrepancies between nuclear matter predictions and finite-nucleus calculations for ¹²_ΛC and ⁵_ΛHe. The work identifies the need for reliable finite-nucleus calculations, clarification of the ΔI=1/2 rule's validity in hypernuclear decay (questioned by Schumacher, 1994), and inclusion of two-nucleon stimulated decay and direct quark mechanisms as open problems for future experiments at DAΦNE.
