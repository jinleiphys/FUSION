# Non-Gaussian Correlations in the McLerran-Venugopalan Model

> We argue that the statistical weight function W[rho] appearing in the McLerran-Venugopalan model of a large nucleus is intrinsically non-Gaussian, even if we neglect quantum corrections. Based on the picture where the nucleus of radius R consists of a collection of color-neutral nucleons, each of radius a<<R, we show that to leading order in alpha_s and a/R only the Gaussian part of W[rho] enters into the final expression for the gluon number density. Thus, the existing results in the literature which assume a Gaussian weight remain valid.

## Key claim

The statistical weight function W[ρ] in the McLerran-Venugopalan model is intrinsically non-Gaussian even without quantum corrections, but to leading order in α_s and a/R (where a is nucleon radius and R is nuclear radius) only the Gaussian part contributes to the gluon number density, so existing Gaussian-based results remain valid.

## Method

The authors model a large nucleus of radius R as a collection of color-neutral nucleons of radius a ≪ R, and analyze the color charge density correlation functions in a three-dimensional extension of the MV model developed in earlier work (Lam and Mahlon, 2000). They compute the light-cone gauge vector potential from the covariant gauge source using a diagrammatic expansion, then evaluate the gluon number density via the two-point correlation function of the vector potential, systematically comparing contributions from Gaussian versus non-Gaussian terms in the statistical weight.

## Key numbers

- The ratio a/R is small for a large nucleus, with a ∼ Λ_QCD⁻¹ and R ≫ a; non-Gaussian contributions are suppressed by additional factors of a/R
- Non-Gaussian contributions are also suppressed by additional factors of α_s relative to the leading Gaussian terms
- The longitudinal coordinate x_∥ is defined via x_∥ ≡ (1/ε)x⁻ − (ε/2)x⁺, where ε ≡ √[2(1−β)/(1+β)] measures how close the source is to the light cone
- The propagator (Green's function) connecting source points is G(x_∥−ξ_∥; x_t−ξ_t) = −(1/4π) × 1/√[(x_∥−ξ_∥)² + (x_t−ξ_t)²]

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) by addressing the long-standing assumption of a Gaussian weight function. It contradicts the naive expectation from the central limit theorem that W[ρ] should become Gaussian for large A, showing instead that longitudinal correlations within nucleons violate the theorem's independence condition. The result reconciles the MV model with renormalization group equation approaches (Jalilian-Marian et al., 1997-1999) where Gaussian distributions are not solutions, and with the authors' earlier three-dimensional treatment (Lam and Mahlon, 2000) that incorporated color neutrality. This enables continued use of Gaussian-based calculations for the gluon number density in large nuclei while acknowledging the fundamentally non-Gaussian nature of the underlying weight function.
