# Color Non-Singlet Spectroscopy

> Study of the spectrum and structure of color non-singlet combinations of quarks and antiquarks, neutralized by a non-dynamical compensating color source, may provide an interesting way to address questions about QCD that cannot be addressed by experiment at the present time. These states can be simulated in lattice QCD and the results can be used to improve phenomenological models of hadrons. Here these ideas are applied to color triplet states of qqqq and qq bar q.

## Key claim

Studying color non-singlet combinations of quarks and antiquarks neutralized by a non-dynamical compensating color source can provide insight into QCD quark correlations that cannot be addressed experimentally, but the approach explicitly abandons comparison with experiment and instead compares phenomenological models directly with lattice calculations, which may use unphysical quark masses.

## Method

The paper constructs color triplet states of four quarks ($qqqq$) and two quarks plus one antiquark ($qq\bar q$) using the constituent quark model, with all quarks in the same $j=1/2$ orbital of a mean field generated by a pointlike, static, infinitely massive $\overline 3_c$ source. States are classified by irreducible representations of $SU(3)_c \times SU(3)_f \times SU(2)_s$ and $SU(6)_{cs}$ (colorspin), and their spectra are computed using two models: the colormagnetic (one-gluon exchange) Hamiltonian and the schematic diquark model. The matrix elements of the colormagnetic interaction are expressed in terms of quadratic Casimir operators of color, spin, and colorspin.

## Key numbers

* The "good" diquark ($\alpha$) has colormagnetic interaction energy $\Delta(\alpha) = -2\mathcal{M}_{qq}$, while the "bad" diquark ($\beta$) has $\Delta(\beta) = +\frac{2}{3}\mathcal{M}_{qq}$, giving a mass difference of approximately 212 MeV for $u$ and $d$ quarks, implying $\mathcal{M}_{qq} \approx 80$ MeV.
* For $[qqqq]^{3_c}$ states, the colormagnetic interaction energies $\Delta$ range from $-4\mathcal{M}_{qqqq}$ (for the $[210]$ colorspin, spin-0, flavor triplet) to $+\frac{14}{3}\mathcal{M}_{qqqq}$ (for the $[\overline{15}]$ colorspin, spin-1, flavor $\mathbf{15'}_f$).
* For $[qqqq]^{3_c}$ states, the $[210]$ colorspin, spin-0, flavor $\mathbf{3}_f$ multiplet has $\Delta = -4\mathcal{M}_{qqqq}$, and the $[210]$ colorspin, spin-1, flavor $\mathbf{3}_f$ multiplet has $\Delta = -10/3\mathcal{M}_{qqqq}$.
* For $[qqqq]^{3_c}$ states, the $[105']$ colorspin, spin-1, flavor $\mathbf{\overline{6}}_f$ multiplet has $\Delta = -\frac{4}{3}\mathcal{M}_{qqqq}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of multiquark hadrons initiated by Mulders, Aerts, and de Swart (1978), who cataloged quantum numbers and evaluated colormagnetic matrix elements for color $3_c$ and $\overline 3_c$ combinations of light quarks and antiquarks, and by Lipkin (1977), who first proposed that $qq\bar q \bar c$ states might be anomalously stable. The paper proposes that lattice QCD simulations of these color non-singlet states, compared with phenomenological models, can probe quark correlations (such as diquark versus triquark structures) in ways that are impractical experimentally, and suggests using unphysical quark masses as an additional degree of freedom to stabilize states that would otherwise decay.
