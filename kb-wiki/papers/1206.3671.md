# Comment on "Taming the pion cloud of the nucleon"

> In a recent Letter, Alberg and Miller presented a calculation of the pion contributions to the self-energy of the nucleon computed from a pseudoscalar (PS) pion-nucleon interaction, with the claim that the result would be equivalent to that with the pseudovector (PV) form. We demonstrate that the PV and PS pion-nucleon couplings do in fact lead to different results for the self-energy. In particular, for the model-independent, leading nonanalytic behavior of the self-energy, the PV theory yields the well-known m_π^3 dependence in the chiral limit, while the PS interaction involves an additional, lower-order term ~ m_π^2 log(m_π^2).

# Comment on "Taming the pion cloud of the nucleon"

## Key claim

The pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) pion-nucleon couplings lead to different results for the nucleon self-energy, contrary to the claim of Alberg and Miller (2012). Specifically, the PS interaction produces an additional lower-order term ~ m_π^2 log(m_π^2) that is absent in the PV theory, which instead yields the well-known m_π^3 dependence in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon self-energy Σ for both PS and PV πN couplings using light-front, time-ordered, and covariant perturbation theory. They expressed the total PS self-energy as Σ^PS = Σ^PV + Σ^PS_end-pt, where the end-point contribution arises from the k^+ = 0 region in the k^- integration. Using the Goldberger-Treiman relation to relate the πNN coupling to g_A and f_π, and a dipole parametrization for the πNN form factor with cutoff Λ, they evaluated the end-point contribution explicitly and expanded it in the chiral limit to extract the leading nonanalytic (LNA) terms.

## Key numbers

- The PV theory yields LNA behavior ~ m_π^3 in the chiral limit.
- The PS theory yields an additional LNA term ~ m_π^2 log(m_π^2), which is of lower order than the PV LNA term.
- The full nonanalytic expansion for the PS self-energy is: Σ^PS_nonanal. = (3 g_A^2)/(32 π f_π^2) [ (M/π) m_π^2 log(m_π^2) - m_π^3 - (m_π^4)/(2π M^2) log(m_π^2/M^2) + O(m_π^5) ].
- The moment of the pion momentum distribution f_π^N (the pion loop contribution to vertex renormalization Z_1^π) in PS theory gives a LNA term 4/3 larger than in PV theory.

## Context

This Comment corrects the claim by Alberg and Miller (2012) that PS and PV πN couplings yield equivalent results for the nucleon self-energy. The work extends earlier analysis by Ji (2009) showing the end-point contribution is essential. The result is independent of the short-distance πNN form factor and can be verified using multiple perturbation theory approaches. The finding implies that PS coupling cannot be used to ensure consistency with the chiral properties of QCD, which are naturally respected by the PV coupling as embodied in chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1967). This has implications for calculations of the d̄ - ū asymmetry in the proton sea and related quantities such as the Sullivan process (Sullivan, 1972).
