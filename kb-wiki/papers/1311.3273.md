# Baryon properties in meson mediums from lattice QCD

> We present results for the ground-state mass shifts of octet baryons due to the presence of a medium of pions or kaons from a lattice QCD calculation performed at a single value of the quark mass, corresponding to a pion mass of $m_π$ ~ 390 MeV, and a spatial volume V ~ (4fm)^3. We use a canonical approach in which correlators are formed using a single baryon propagator and a fixed number of meson propagators, up to n=9. From the ground-state energies we calculate two- and three-body interaction parameters. We also extract combinations of low-energy constants by comparing our results to tree level chiral perturbation theory at non-zero isospin/kaon chemical potential.

# Baryon properties in meson mediums from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The ground-state mass shifts of octet baryons in a medium of pions or kaons are computed from lattice QCD at a single quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 390 MeV, and the extracted meson-baryon scattering lengths show significant momentum dependence when compared to previous smaller-volume results, indicating that short-distance physics such as the effective range or inelasticities may be relevant at these momenta.

## Method

The authors use a canonical approach in which correlation functions are formed from a single baryon propagator and a fixed number of meson propagators (up to n=9), employing a matrix-based contraction method that reduces the intractable number of index contractions to traces of matrix products. The calculations are performed on gauge configurations from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration using a 2+1-flavor anisotropic dynamical tadpole-improved clover fermion action, with a spatial volume of approximately (4 fm)^3 and a large temporal extent of T=256 to eliminate thermal effects. Ground-state energies are extracted and used to compute two-body meson-baryon and meson-meson scattering parameters via a generalized Luscher formula, as well as three-body meson-meson-baryon interaction parameters, and the results are compared to tree-level heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at non-zero isospin or kaon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 386 MeV; kaon mass: m_K ~ 543 MeV
- Spatial lattice spacing: b_s = 0.1227(8); anisotropy parameter xi = b_t/b_s ~ 3.5
- Spatial volume: V ~ (4 fm)^3, using 32^3 lattice sites with temporal extent T = 256
- Systems studied: Xi^0(pi^+)^n, Sigma^+(pi^+)^n, p(K^+)^n, and n(K^+)^n with n up to 9
- The chemical potentials of the systems are very near the condensation point, mu_I,K approximately equal to m_pi,K

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of many-hadron systems beyond pure mesonic systems (Detmold 2008, Beane 2007) to include a single baryon in a meson medium, addressing the challenge of finite baryon density calculations where signal-to-noise issues are severe (Lepage 1989, Barbour 1986). The results enable future exploration of the momentum dependence of meson-baryon phase shifts and possible modifications of baryon dispersion relations in medium, and are relevant to understanding pion condensation (Migdal, Sawyer 1972) and kaon condensation (Kaplan 1986) proposed to occur in neutron star cores.
