# Instantons and Magnetic Monopoles on $R^3\times S^1$ with Arbitrary Simple Gauge Groups

> We investigate Yang-Mills theories with arbitrary gauge group on $R^3\times S^1$, whose symmetry is spontaneously broken by the Wilson loop. We show that instantons are made of fundamental magnetic monopoles, each of which has a corresponding root in the extended Dynkin diagram. The number of constituent magnetic monopoles for a single instanton is the dual Coxeter number of the gauge group, which also accounts for the number of instanton zero modes. In addition, we show that there exists a novel type of the $S^1$ coordinate dependent magnetic monopole solutions in $G_2,F_4,E_8$.

# Instantons and Magnetic Monopoles on $R^3\times S^1$ with Arbitrary Simple Gauge Groups

## Key claim

Instantons on $R^3\times S^1$ with maximally broken gauge symmetry are composites of $r+1$ distinct fundamental magnetic monopoles, one for each root in the extended Dynkin diagram, provided the Wilson loop parameter lies within a specific "fundamental cell" where each monopole carries exactly four zero modes. For the exceptional groups $G_2$, $F_4$, and $E_8$, the monopole associated with the lowest negative root is genuinely $x_4$-dependent and cannot be made time-independent by any gauge transformation.

## Method

The author considers pure Yang-Mills theory on $R^3\times S^1$ with an arbitrary simple gauge group $G$ of rank $r$, where the Wilson loop along $S^1$ breaks the gauge symmetry maximally to $U(1)^r$. For each simple root $\beta_i$ and the lowest negative root $\beta_0$ (which together form the extended Dynkin diagram), the author constructs explicit BPS monopole solutions by embedding $SU(2)$ monopole solutions along the corresponding $SU(2)$ subgroups. For groups with nontrivial center, the $\beta_0$ monopole is obtained by a large gauge transformation from a time-independent solution; for $G_2$, $F_4$, $E_8$ (which have trivial center), the $\beta_0$ monopole is constructed directly as an $x_4$-dependent solution using the $SU(2)$ embedding. Zero mode counting is performed via a Dirac-type fluctuation equation, analyzing the isospin and hypercharge parameters for fluctuations in each root multiplet.

## Key numbers

- The number of constituent magnetic monopoles for a single instanton equals the dual Coxeter number $c_2(G)$ of the gauge group, which also equals the number of instanton zero modes divided by 4.
- For $SU_{n+1}$, the dual Coxeter number is $n+1$ and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,...,1)$.
- For $G_2$, the dual Coxeter number is $4$, the dimension is $14$, and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,2)$.
- For $F_4$, the dual Coxeter number is $9$, the dimension is $52$, and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,2,2,3)$.
- For $E_8$, the dual Coxeter number is $30$, the dimension is $248$, and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,2,2,3,3,4,4,5,6)$.
- For $E_6$, the dual Coxeter number is $12$, the dimension is $78$, and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,1,2,2,2,3)$.
- For $E_7$, the dual Coxeter number is $18$, the dimension is $133$, and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,2,2,2,3,3,4)$.
- For $SO_{2n+1}$ ($n\ge 3$), the dual Coxeter number is $2n-2$ and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,1,2,2,...,2)$.
- For $SO_{2n}$ ($n\ge 4$), the dual Coxeter number is $2n-2$ and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,1,1,2,2,...,2)$.
- For $Sp_{2n}$ ($n\ge 2$), the dual Coxeter number is $n+1$ and the Dynkin numbers are $(1,1,...,1)$.
- The topological charge of the $\beta_0$ monopole is $m_0 = 1 + {\bf h}\cdot \beta_0^*$, where ${\bf h}$ is the asymptotic value of $A_4$ along the Cartan subalgebra.
- The fundamental cell where each of the $r+1$ fundamental monopoles has exactly four zero modes is defined by ${\bf h}\cdot \beta_i^* > 0$ for all simple roots and ${\bf h}\cdot (-\beta_0^*) < 1$ (with $S^1$ circumference $2\pi$).

## Context

This work generalizes the earlier result for $SU(n)$ gauge groups (Lee, 1998; referred to as "piljin" in the text) to arbitrary simple gauge groups, showing that the instanton-monopole correspondence holds universally. The construction extends the known BPS monopole solutions for simple roots (Weinberg, 1980; referred to as "erick" in the text) by adding the monopole for the lowest negative root. The zero mode counting matches the known result that a single instanton on $R^4$ has $4c_2(G)$ zero modes (Bernard, 1979; referred to as "bernard" in the text). The work enables further study of finite-temperature QCD, supersymmetric Yang-Mills theories on $R^3\times S^1$ (Seiberg, 1994; referred to as "seiberg" in the text), and D-brane dynamics in string theory.
