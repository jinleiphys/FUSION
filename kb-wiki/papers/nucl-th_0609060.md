# Cylindrically asymmetric hydrodynamic equations

> We show that the boost-invariant and cylindrically asymmetric hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free matter may be rewritten as only two coupled partial differential equations. In the case where the system exhibits the cross-over phase transition, the standard numerical methods may be applied to solve these equations. An example of our results describing non-central gold on gold collisions at RHIC energies is presented.

# Cylindrically asymmetric hydrodynamic equations

## Key claim

The boost-invariant and cylindrically asymmetric relativistic hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free perfect fluid can be reduced to only two coupled partial differential equations, provided the equation of state is incorporated via a temperature-dependent sound velocity that satisfies the stability condition against shock formation. The reduction works for systems exhibiting a cross-over phase transition, not only for constant sound velocity.

## Method

The author starts from the standard relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free matter in a boost-invariant, cylindrically asymmetric geometry, expressed in polar coordinates (t, r, phi) in the transverse plane. By introducing a potential Phi(T) derived from the temperature-dependent sound velocity and substituting the fluid rapidity theta and the combinations a_plus = exp(Phi + theta) and a_minus = exp(Phi - theta), the three original equations are reduced to two coupled partial differential equations: one for a(t,r,phi) (constructed from a_plus and a_minus via a symmetry ansatz) and one for the flow-angle function alpha(t,r,phi). The boundary conditions at the center are automatically satisfied by extending the radial coordinate to negative values and defining a(t,-r,phi) = a_minus(t,r,phi). Initial conditions are set at t0 = 1 fm using a temperature profile proportional to the cube root of the participant nucleon density and a small initial transverse flow parameter H0 = 0.001 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: T0 = 2.5 Tc, where Tc is the critical temperature, for collisions with impact parameter b = 7.6 fm (centrality class c = 0-80%).
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at t0 = 1 fm.
- Initial transverse flow parameter: H0 = 0.001 fm^{-1}.
- Temperature profiles are shown for times t = 1, 4, 7, 10, 13, 16 fm.
- Isotherms are plotted from T = 1.8 Tc down to T = 0.2 Tc in steps of 0.2 Tc.

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric approach of Baym, Friman, Blaizot, Soyeur, and Czyz (1983) to cylindrically asymmetric systems, enabling the description of non-central heavy-ion collisions. The formalism is designed to be combined with a statistical freeze-out model such as the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski and collaborators (2001) or the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo code of Kisiel (2005) to compute physical observables like the azimuthal asymmetry coefficient v2. The results shown are for gold-on-gold collisions at RHIC energies.
