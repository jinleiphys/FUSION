# A Poincaré covariant cascade method for high-energy nuclear collisions

> We present a Poincaré covariant cascade algorithm based on the constrained Hamiltonian dynamics in an $8N$-dimensional phase space to simulate the Boltzmann-type two-body collision term. We compare this covariant cascade algorithm with traditional $6N$-dimensional phase-space cascade algorithms. To validate the covariant cascade algorithm, we perform box calculations. We examine the frame dependence of the algorithm in a one-dimensionally expanding system as well as the compression stages of colliding two nuclei. We confirm that our covariant cascade method is reliable to simulate high-energy nuclear collisions. Furthermore, we present Lorentz-covariant equations of motion for the $N$-body system interacting via potentials, which can be efficiently solved numerically.

## Key claim

The authors present a Poincaré covariant cascade algorithm based on constrained Hamiltonian dynamics in an 8N-dimensional phase space that avoids the causality violations and frame-dependence issues of traditional 6N-dimensional cascade methods, but the algorithm's cluster separability is only approximate because the timekeeper vector is constant after specifying the computational frame rather than being reassigned to each separated cluster.

## Method

The method extends the phase space from 6N to 8N dimensions by treating four-positions and four-momenta as dynamical variables, imposing mass-shell constraints and time-fixation constraints that equate all particle times in the global center-of-mass frame. The equations of motion yield constant velocities for each particle, and the collision time is determined by the closest-distance condition in the two-body center-of-mass system using the evolution parameter tau, which provides a frame-independent ordering of collisions. The algorithm is implemented in the JAM event generator and compared with traditional 6N-dimensional cascade schemes (CS-A, CS-B, CS-G, CS-O) and the Poincaré covariant parton cascade (PCPC) approach.

## Key numbers

- The weight function in Sorge's constraints uses a length scale L = 8 fm^2 for the Gaussian term exp(q_ij^2/L)/(q_ij^2/L)
- The impact parameter condition for collisions is b ≤ sqrt(sigma/pi), where sigma is the total cross section
- The squared impact parameter is given by b^2 = -q_c^2 + (q_c·P)^2/P^2, where q_c is the relative distance at collision and P is the total momentum of the two-body system

## Context

This work extends the constrained Hamiltonian dynamics framework previously used in relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) models by Sorge (1989) and Maruyama (1991, 1996) to develop a purely cascade method without mean-field potentials. It addresses causality problems in cascade models discussed by Kodama (1983), Kortemeyer (1995), Zhang (1996, 1998), and Zhao (2020), and provides an alternative to the full-ensemble (subdivision) method of Welke (1989) and Molnar (2000) that preserves event-by-event fluctuations. The method enables Lorentz-covariant simulations of high-energy nuclear collisions across different computational frames, which is necessary for reliable extraction of nuclear matter properties from transport model comparisons such as those in the TMEP collaboration studies (2016-2022).
