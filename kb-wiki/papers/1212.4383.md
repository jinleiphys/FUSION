# Heavy hadron spectroscopy: a quark model perspective

> We present recent results of hadron spectroscopy and hadron-hadron interaction from the perspective of constituent quark models. We pay special attention to the role played by higher order Fock space components in the hadron spectra and the connection of this extension with the hadron-hadron interaction. The main goal of our description is to obtain a coherent understanding of the lowenergy hadron phenomenology without enforcing any particular model, to constrain its characteristics and learn about low-energy realization of the theory.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a coherent description of low-energy hadron phenomenology requires incorporating higher-order Fock space components (many-quark configurations) beyond the naive quark model, and that spectroscopy and hadron-hadron interaction must be treated within the same theoretical scheme to avoid spurious conclusions about the existence of compact tetraquark states versus molecular states.

## Method
The authors solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for $QQ\bar n\bar n$ systems (double charm and double bottom) using both the hyperspherical harmonic formalism and a variational approach with generalized gaussians. For meson-meson scattering, they solve the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation via the Fredholm determinant method, deriving meson-meson potentials from the same quark-quark interaction used in the four-quark calculations. Heavy baryon spectra are obtained by solving the three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space.

## Key numbers
- For $cc\bar n\bar n$ four-quark states: only one bound state found with quantum numbers $(I)J^P = (0)1^+$, with $\Delta_E = -76$ MeV relative to the $DD^*$ threshold
- For $bb\bar n\bar n$ four-quark states: bound states found with $\Delta_E = -217$ MeV for $(0)1^+$ and $-153$ MeV for $(0)0^+$
- The $X(3872)$ is reproduced as a $(I)J^{PC}=(0)1^{++}$ bound state with binding energy in the range $0-1$ MeV from the coupling between $D\bar D^*$ and $J/\Psi \omega$ channels
- $\Lambda_b$ ground state mass: 5624 MeV (calculated) vs 5619 MeV (experimental)
- $\Sigma_b$ ground state mass: 5807 MeV (calculated) vs 5811 MeV (experimental)
- Double charmed baryon $\Xi_{cc}$ ground state predicted at 3579 MeV
- Double bottom baryon $\Xi_{bb}$ ground state predicted at 10189 MeV

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Vijande et al. (2005) and Valcarce et al. (2005) to systematically include higher Fock space components in hadron spectroscopy. It directly addresses the missing resonance problem and the observation of states like the $X(3872)$ that challenge the naive $q\bar q$ classification. The results enable future experimental searches at LHC and RHIC for tetraquark states and provide parameter-free predictions for double heavy baryons that can be tested against lattice QCD calculations (Mathur et al., 2002) and potential future SELEX confirmations.
