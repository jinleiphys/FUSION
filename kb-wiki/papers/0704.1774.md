# Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculation of nuclei with A<40 with tensor interactions

> We calculate the ground-state energy of 4He, 8He, 16O, and 40Ca using the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method in the fixed phase approximation and the Argonne v6' interaction which includes a tensor force. Comparison of our light nuclei results to those of Green's function Monte Carlo calculations shows the accuracy of our method for both open and closed shell nuclei. We also apply it to 16O and 40Ca to show that quantum Monte Carlo methods are now applicable to larger nuclei.

## Key claim

The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method, using the fixed-phase approximation and the Argonne v6' interaction (which includes the tensor force), can accurately calculate ground-state energies of nuclei up to A=40, overcoming previous difficulties with the tensor force in the isosinglet channel that made sampling spin-isospin states problematic for nuclei with both proton-proton and proton-neutron interactions.

## Method

The authors used AFDMC with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to sample spin-isospin degrees of freedom via auxiliary fields, rather than summing them explicitly as in Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC). They employed the fixed-phase approximation to control the fermion sign problem, using a simplified trial wave function with only a central Jastrow correlation and Slater determinants of single-particle orbitals from a Hartree-Fock calculation with Skyrme interactions. The Argonne v6' interaction, which includes the six operators (central, spin, isospin, spin-isospin, tensor, and tensor-isospin), was used for all calculations.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground-state energy: -27.13(10) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -26.93(1) MeV (GFMC) and -26.85(2) MeV (EIHH), all with Argonne v6' interaction
- 8He ground-state energy: -23.6(5) MeV (AFDMC) compared to -23.6(1) MeV (GFMC) with Argonne v6'
- 16O ground-state energy: -100.7(4) MeV with Argonne v6' (experimental: -127.619 MeV)
- 40Ca ground-state energy: -272(2) MeV with Argonne v6' (experimental: -342.051 MeV)
- Nuclear matter energy at equilibrium density (0.16 fm^-3): -12.8(1) MeV per nucleon from AFDMC
- Surface energy coefficient from Weizsacker formula comparison: 20.5 MeV (experimental: 18.6 MeV)

## Context

This work extends AFDMC, previously applied to pure neutron matter (Fantoni 2001, Sarsa 2003), neutron drops (Pederiva 2004), and valence neutrons of neutron-rich nuclei (Gandolfi 2006), to finite nuclei with tensor interactions. The results for light nuclei agree with GFMC calculations by Wiringa (2002) and EIHH calculations by Orlandini (2006), while for 16O the AFDMC energy (-90.8 MeV with Argonne v14 truncated to six operators) is about 10% lower than variational Monte Carlo (-83.2 MeV, Pieper 1992) and FHNC/SOC (-84.0 MeV, Fabrocini 2000). This enables future calculations of heavier nuclei and asymmetric nuclear matter with more realistic Hamiltonians including three-body forces and spin-orbit terms.
