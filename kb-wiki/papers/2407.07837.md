# Probe and Prejudice: Classification of compact objects and model comparison using EOS knowledge

> Nuclear theory and experiments, alongside astrophysical observations, constrain the equation of state (EOS) of supranuclear-dense matter. Conversely, knowledge of the EOS allows an improved interpretation of nuclear or astrophysical data. In this article, we use several established constraints on the EOS and the new NICER measurement of PSR J0437-4715 to comment on the nature of the primary companion in GW230529 and the companion of PSR J0514-4002E. We find that, with a probability of $\gtrsim 84\%$ and $\gtrsim 68\%$, respectively, both objects are black holes. These likelihoods increase to above $95\%$ when one uses GW170817's remnant as an upper limit on the TOV mass. We also demonstrate that the current knowledge of the EOS substantially disfavors high masses and radii for PSR J0030+0451, inferred recently when combining NICER with XMM-Newton background data and using particular hot-spot models. Finally, we also use our obtained EOS knowledge to comment on measurements of the nuclear symmetry energy, finding that the large value predicted by the PREX-II measurement displays some mild tension with other constraints on the EOS.

## Key claim

Using established equation-of-state (EOS) constraints together with the new NICER measurement of PSR J0437-4715, the primary companion in GW230529 and the companion of PSR J0514-4002E are classified as black holes with probabilities of at least 84% and 68% respectively, rising above 95% when the GW170817 remnant is used as an upper limit on the maximum mass of a non-spinning neutron star (TOV mass).

## Method

The authors constructed a set of 100,000 model-agnostic EOS candidates using a nucleonic metamodel around nuclear saturation density with speed-of-sound extrapolation to higher densities, then applied Bayesian inference to obtain posterior estimates under three different constraint sets. Set 1 (high confidence) included chiral effective field theory, perturbative QCD, heavy radio pulsar masses, NICER observations of three pulsars, and GW170817; Set 2 added more model-dependent constraints including the GW170817 remnant mass as a TOV mass upper limit; Set 3 used the same constraints as Set 2 but assumed the GW170817 remnant was a hypermassive neutron star above the Kepler limit, providing a stricter upper bound.

## Key numbers

- Primary companion in GW230529 classified as black hole with probability at least 84%
- Companion of PSR J0514-4002E classified as black hole with probability at least 68%
- Both classification probabilities increase to above 95% when using GW170817 remnant as an upper limit on the TOV mass
- Nuclear saturation density taken as 0.16 fm^-3
- Crust EOS attached at density 0.076 fm^-3
- Fixed crust EOS may impact radii of lighter neutron stars up to approximately 0.5 km

## Context

This work extends the multimessenger EOS inference framework NMMA previously developed by Dietrich (2020) and Pang (2022), and builds on the comprehensive EOS constraint compilation of Koehn (2024). The classification of compact objects in the lower mass gap follows similar approaches by Golomb (2024), Essick (2020), and Tews (2020). The analysis demonstrates tension between the large symmetry energy value from the PREX-II measurement and other EOS constraints, and disfavors the high masses and radii for PSR J0030+0451 inferred from combined NICER and XMM-Newton data with particular hot-spot models, as also noted by Breschi (2024) and Miao (2024).
