# Projected shell model study of odd-odd f-p-g shell proton-rich nuclei

> A systematic study of 2-quasiparticle bands of the proton-rich odd-odd nuclei in the mass A ~ 70-80 region is performed using the projected shell model approach. The study includes Br-, Rb-, and Y-isotopes with N = Z+2, and Z+4. We describe the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths in terms of the configuration mixing of the angular-momentum projected multi-quasiparticle states. Signature splitting and signature inversion in the rotational bands are discussed and are shown to be well described. A preliminary study of the odd-odd N = Z nucleus, 74Rb using the concept of spontaneous symmetry breaking is also presented.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) can successfully describe the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths of proton-rich odd-odd nuclei in the mass A ~ 70–80 region through configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected multi-quasiparticle states, but the model fails to reproduce the signature inversion observed at low spins in several nuclei, indicating that the schematic Hamiltonian employed lacks the interaction needed to bring self-inverting bands sufficiently low to affect the yrast states.

## Method

The authors performed projected shell model calculations for the positive-parity yrast bands of six odd-odd nuclei (72,74Br, 76,78Rb, 80,82Y) and separately for the N = Z nucleus 74Rb. They constructed a basis of 2-quasiparticle states from a deformed Nilsson + BCS calculation using three major shells (N = 2, 3, 4) for both neutrons and protons, with basis states within 3.5 MeV of the Fermi surface. Angular-momentum projection was applied to restore rotational symmetry, and the shell model Hamiltonian (quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions) was diagonalized in the projected basis to obtain energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- Basis deformation ε₂ = 0.36 was used for 74Rb, taken from neighboring even-even N = Z nuclei 72Kr and 76Sr
- For 72Br, B(M1)/B(E2) values for the 11⁽⁺⁾ and 13⁽⁺⁾ levels are approximately 10 μₙ²/(eb)², while for the 10⁽⁺⁾ level the value is only 0.03(1) μₙ²/(eb)²
- The monopole-pairing force constants used are G_M^ν = [20.25 – 16.20 (N−Z)/A] A⁻¹ and G_M^π = 20.25 A⁻¹
- The quadrupole pairing strength G_Q = 0.16 G_M
- Effective charges of 1.6e for protons and 0.6e for neutrons were used in B(E2) calculations
- The g-factor for spin was damped by a factor of 0.85 from free values: g_s^π = 5.586 × 0.85, g_s^ν = −3.826 × 0.85
- Excited states have been populated experimentally up to spins I ≈ 20 ħ in some of these nuclei
- Signature inversion appears at spin I ≈ 10 in several nuclei, and self-inversion in unperturbed bands appears at I = 6 for one band and I = 10 for another

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model, previously applied successfully to even-even Kr, Sr, and Zr isotopes in the same mass region (Palit et al., 2001), to odd-odd proton-rich nuclei for the first time. It also provides the first PSM calculations of electromagnetic transition strengths in odd-odd nuclei. The study builds on earlier theoretical work using the 2-quasiparticle rotor model for 76Br (Kreiner et al., 1979) and the axially symmetric rotor plus quasiparticle model for signature inversion (Reinhardt et al., 2001), as well as Excited-VAMPIR calculations. The results enable future extensions of the PSM to include neutron-proton pairing and isospin degrees of freedom for a proper description of N = Z nuclei like 74Rb, where the current model cannot treat the interplay between T = 0 and T = 1 bands.
