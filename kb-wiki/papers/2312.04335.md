# The charge density and neutron skin thickness of Skyrmions

> Motivated by recent parity violating electron scattering experiments, we compute the Neutron Skin Thickness (NST) of nuclei modelled as (quantized) Skyrmions, the topological solitons of the Skyrme model. We show how in a certain approximation, the result for the NST is oblivious to the fine details of the (generally very complicated) quantum state of the soliton and only depends on the total baryon number and the isospin number. Moreover, in the leading order, the linear dependence on the asymmetry parameter is recovered, as expected both from experimental data and other models of nuclei such as the liquid drop model.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness (NST) of nuclei in the Skyrme model, computed under the rigid rotor quantization and three simplifying approximations (iso-sphericity of the inertia tensor, small mixed iso-term, and large spatial inertia relative to isospin inertia), depends only on the total baryon number and isospin number, not on the detailed quantum state; at leading order in small isospin asymmetry, the NST depends linearly on the asymmetry parameter, consistent with experimental data and the liquid drop model.

## Method

The authors compute the electric charge density of Skyrmions via the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula, using the rigid rotor quantization of classical Skyrmion solutions to obtain the expectation value of the third isospin Noether current in a given quantum state. For large nuclei, they apply three approximations, iso-sphericity of the isospin inertia tensor (\(u_{ab} = u \delta_{ab}\)), small mixed inertia (\(|w_{ab}| \ll u\)), and large spatial inertia (\(u \ll v\)), which allow the NST to be expressed solely in terms of classical Skyrmion properties (baryon number, isospin, and the second moment of the isospin inertia density). They generate new classical Skyrmion solutions up to baryon number \(B=256\) using arrested Newton flow on a cubic lattice and compute the relevant quantities numerically.

## Key numbers

- For the \(B=3\) Skyrmion (tritium/helium-3 isodoublet), the neutron skin thickness of \(^3\)H is \(\Delta R_{np}(^3{\rm H}) \approx 0.23\,{\rm fm}\), compared to the experimental value \(\Delta R_{np}(^3{\rm H})|_{\rm exp} \approx 0.2\,{\rm fm}\).
- The rational map approximation for the \(B=3\) Skyrmion gives \(u_{\rm RM}^{(2)} = 770.55\,{\rm MeV\,fm}^4\) and \(w_{\rm RM}^{(2)} = -466.36\,{\rm MeV\,fm}^4\); full field numerics give \(u^{(2)} = 722.57\,{\rm MeV\,fm}^4\) and \(w^{(2)} = -424.43\,{\rm MeV\,fm}^4\).
- The experimental linear relation for NST as a function of isospin asymmetry \(\delta\) is \(\Delta R_{np} = 1.32\,\delta - 0.024 \pm 0.026\,{\rm fm}\) (for baryon numbers \(16 < B < 238\)).
- The iso-sphericity approximation error is below \(8\%\) for \(B>4\) and averages around \(3\%\) for the 409 Skyrmion solutions with \(B=1-16\).
- The quantity \(\left(u^{(2)} / u r^2_{\rm rms} - 1\right) r_{\rm rms}\) changes by about \(10\%\) from \(B=64\) to \(B=240\), showing a nearly constant leading-order behavior.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to charge densities beyond the lightest nuclei (e.g., \(B=1\) nucleons studied by Braaten (1988) and \(B=3\) triton/helium-3 studied by Carson (1991)), where previous computations became prohibitively complex for larger baryon numbers. The results are motivated by recent parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX on \(^{208}\)Pb and CREX on \(^{48}\)Ca) and the COHERENT collaboration's observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. The linear dependence on the asymmetry parameter recovers the behavior seen experimentally and in the liquid drop model, and the Skyrme model predicts a small correction inversely proportional to baryon number. The work enables future calculations of NST for arbitrary nuclei using only classical Skyrmion properties, without needing the full quantum wavefunction.
