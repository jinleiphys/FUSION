# An introduction to mesic nuclei

> There is much speculation and a modest amount of evidence that certain mesons might form quasi-bound states with nuclei to produce really exotic states of matter. For this to be a practical possibility, the interaction between the meson and nucleons at low energies must be strong and attractive and the production rates "healthy". The conditions for this are surveyed for the light mesons. How this might lead to quasi-bound states is then discussed in a few typical cases.

# An introduction to mesic nuclei

## Key claim

While there is speculation and some evidence that certain mesons (particularly the η and K⁻) may form quasi-bound states with nuclei, the search for experimental signals in bound state regions has generally been frustrating, with positive claims only made for the η and the K⁻. The paper surveys conditions for mesic nucleus formation across the K̄, η, ω, η′, and φ mesons, noting that strong, attractive low-energy meson-nucleon interactions and healthy production rates are both required.

## Method

The paper reviews near-threshold production cross sections for η, η′, ω, φ, and K⁻ mesons in elementary reactions such as pp → ppX and γp → pX, comparing data to a simple S-wave final state interaction (FSI) parameterization. It then examines inclusive photoproduction scaling with nuclear mass number A (σ ∼ A^α) to infer meson absorption strength, and discusses production rates in pd → ³HeX reactions. Finally, it evaluates binding energy estimates from optical potential models and reviews experimental searches for mesic nuclei using both above-threshold extrapolation and direct sub-threshold decay measurements.

## Key numbers

- For η′ production in pp → ppη′, the best fit pole position ε = 0.75^(+0.20)_(-0.15) MeV (COSY-11, Czerwinski 2014)
- The pp → ppη cross section is about a factor of twenty larger than pp → ppη′ near threshold
- Natural width of the ω meson: Γ_ω ≈ 8.5 MeV/c²
- From γp → pω near threshold, σ_t/q ≈ 0.044 μb/MeV/c; vector-dominance model estimate gives a_ωp = (0.82 ± 0.03) fm
- Coupled-channel analysis gives a_ωp = (−0.026 + i0.28) fm (Shklyar 2005)
- The pp → ppφ cross section is about a factor of 30 lower than that for ω production
- Effective K⁻p scattering length from pp → K⁺K⁻pp data at Q = 24 MeV: a_(K⁻p) = 2.45i fm (Ye 2013)
- Average squared amplitudes near threshold for pd → ³HeX: |f(pd → ³Heη)|² ≈ 2500 nb/sr, |f(pd → ³Heω)|² ≈ 30 nb/sr, |f(pd → ³Heη′)|² ≈ 0.9 nb/sr, |f(pd → ³Heφ)|² ≈ 2.3 nb/sr
- Pole in η³He scattering amplitude at p_η = (−5 ± 7) ± i(19 ± 3) MeV/c, corresponding to Q = (−0.30 ± 0.15) ± i(0.21 ± 0.06) MeV
- For a potential model of ¹²_ηC, M(¹²_ηC(2⁺)) − M(¹²_ηC(0⁺)) ≈ 4.4 MeV
- DISTO data (Yamazaki 2010) gave a Λp invariant-mass peak at M ≈ 2267 MeV/c² with Γ ≈ 118 MeV/c², interpreted as X(2267)

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Liu and Haider (1986) on bound η-mesic nuclei using single-channel optical potentials, and surveys subsequent experimental and theoretical developments for multiple meson species. The paper discusses the strong s-wave couplings at the N*(1535):ηp and Λ(1405):K⁻p thresholds as drivers of attraction, while noting the absence of firm evidence for η′, ω, or φ attraction to nucleons. It enables future searches by clarifying that above-threshold experiments cannot distinguish bound from virtual states, and that direct sub-threshold searches face overwhelming backgrounds unless quasi-free meson decay can be identified. The controversy over deeply bound K⁻pp systems (X(2267)) is highlighted, with criticism by Epple and Fabbietti (2015) of the analysis procedure used by Yamazaki (2010).
