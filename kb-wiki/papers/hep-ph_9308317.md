# Electromagnetic Moments of the Baryon Decuplet

> We compute the leading contributions to the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the baryon decuplet in chiral perturbation theory. The measured value for the magnetic moment of the $Ω^-$ is used to determine the local counterterm for the magnetic moments. We compare the chiral perturbation theory predictions for the magnetic moments of the decuplet with those of the baryon octet and find reasonable agreement with the predictions of the large--$N_c$ limit of QCD. The leading contribution to the quadrupole moment of the $Δ$ and other members of the decuplet comes from one--loop graphs. The pionic contribution is shown to be proportional to $I_z$ (and so will not contribute to the quadrupole moment of $I=0$ nuclei), while the contribution from kaons has both isovector and isoscalar components. The chiral logarithmic enhancement of both pion and kaon loops has a coefficient that vanishes in the $SU(6)$ limit. The third allowed moment, the magnetic octupole, is shown to be dominated by a local counterterm with corrections arising at two loops. We briefly mention the strange counterparts of these moments.

## Key claim

The leading contributions to the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the baryon decuplet are computed in chiral perturbation theory, with the measured magnetic moment of the Omega- used to fix the local counterterm; the pion-loop contribution to the quadrupole moment is proportional to the third component of isospin (I_z) and thus vanishes for I=0 baryons, while the chiral logarithmic enhancement of both pion and kaon loops has a coefficient that vanishes in the SU(6) limit.

## Method

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory was used to compute one-loop graphs for the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the decuplet baryons, including intermediate decuplet and octet baryon states with pion and kaon loops. The measured magnetic moment of the Omega- (mu_Omega = -1.94 +/- 0.22 mu_N) was used to determine the leading SU(3)-invariant local counterterm for the magnetic moments. The magnetic octupole moment was shown to be dominated by a dimension-seven local counterterm with corrections arising at two loops.

## Key numbers

*   Omega- magnetic moment: mu_Omega = -1.94 +/- 0.17 +/- 0.14 mu_N (used as input to fix the counterterm).
*   Delta++ magnetic moment (one-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction): mu_Delta++ = 4.0 +/- 0.4 mu_N.
*   Delta++ magnetic moment (tree-level result): mu_Delta++ = 5.8 +/- 0.7 mu_N.
*   Delta++ magnetic moment (model-dependent extraction from pion bremsstrahlung): mu_Delta++ = 4.5 +/- 0.5 mu_N.
*   Delta++ quadrupole moment (one-loop, neglecting local counterterm): Q_Delta++ = -0.8 +/- 0.5 x 10^{-27} e-cm^2.
*   Delta+ quadrupole moment (one-loop, neglecting local counterterm): Q_Delta+ = -0.3 +/- 0.2 x 10^{-27} e-cm^2.
*   Delta0 quadrupole moment (one-loop, neglecting local counterterm): Q_Delta0 = 0.12 +/- 0.05 x 10^{-27} e-cm^2.
*   Delta- quadrupole moment (one-loop, neglecting local counterterm): Q_Delta- = 0.6 +/- 0.3 x 10^{-27} e-cm^2.
*   Ratio of isovector magnetic moments (one-loop): (mu_Delta++ - mu_Delta-)/(mu_p - mu_n) = 1.35 +/- 0.15.
*   Ratio of isovector magnetic moments (one-loop): (mu_Delta+ - mu_Delta0)/(mu_p - mu_n) = 0.45 +/- 0.05.

## Context

This work extends the application of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, previously used for the baryon octet (Jenkins et al., 1993), to the decuplet baryons. It provides model-independent predictions that can be compared with quenched lattice QCD results (Leinweber, Draper, and Woloshyn, 1992; Nozawa and Leinweber, 1990), quark model calculations, and the large-N_c limit of QCD (Dashen and Manohar, 1993). The results enable tests of the contracted SU(2N_f) algebra for baryons and suggest that the intrinsic quadrupole moment of Delta baryons contributes negligibly to the quadrupole moment of I=0 nuclei like the deuteron, a result that could be tested at CEBAF.
