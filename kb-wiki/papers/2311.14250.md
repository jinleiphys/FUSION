# Nuclear level density from relativistic density functional theory and combinatorial method

> Nuclear level density is calculated with the combinatorial method based on the relativistic density functional theory including pairing correlations. The Strutinsky method is adopted to smooth the total state density in order to refine the prediction at low excitation energy. The impacts of pairing correlations and moments of inertia on the nuclear level density are discussed in detail. Taking $\mathrm{^{112}Cd}$ as an example, it is demonstrated that the nuclear level density based on the relativistic density functional PC-PK1 can reproduce the experimental data at the same level as or even better than the previous approaches.

## Key claim

The combinatorial method for nuclear level density, when based on relativistic density functional theory with pairing correlations and using the Strutinsky method for smoothing, can reproduce experimental data for ¹¹²Cd at the same level as or even better than previous approaches, but only when pairing correlations are included; without pairing, the predicted level densities are about one order of magnitude too high.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the PC-PK1 density functional and a finite-range separable pairing force to compute single-particle levels, deformations, and moments of inertia for ¹¹²Cd. They then applied the combinatorial method to count incoherent particle-hole states and folded them with collective vibration states, using the Strutinsky method (with smoothing range γ₀ = 0.2 MeV and polynomial order M₀ = 1) to smooth the total state density at low excitation energy instead of the conventional linear-interpolation smoothing. Three formulas for the moment of inertia (rigid rotor, irrotational fluid, and microscopic Inglis-Belyaev) were tested, and the Inglis-Belyaev formula was adopted for the final results.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state deformation of ¹¹²Cd from the RHB calculation is β₂ = 0.145.
- The energy discretization unit used in the combinatorial method is ε₀ = 0.01 MeV.
- The cut-off excitation energy is 10 MeV and the cut-off angular momentum is 49ħ.
- Calculations without pairing correlations produce nuclear level densities about one order of magnitude higher than those with pairing correlations.
- The result based on PC-PK1 reproduces fluctuations at low excitation energy (≲ 2 MeV) and the level density at high excitation energy, while the D1M result fails at low excitation energy for the fluctuations, and the MSk7 and BSk14 results both significantly overestimate the nuclear level density at high excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends the combinatorial method for nuclear level density from non-relativistic frameworks (Goriely 2008, Hilaire 2012) to relativistic density functional theory, and improves upon a prior relativistic combinatorial calculation (Geng 2023) that neglected pairing correlations. It also introduces the Strutinsky method as a more mathematically appropriate smoothing technique than the conventional method (Hilaire 2001), which gave poor results at low excitation energy. The successful description of ¹¹²Cd enables future systematic calculations across the nuclear chart for applications to neutron capture rates in r-process nucleosynthesis.
