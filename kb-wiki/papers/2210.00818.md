# Preformation Probability and Kinematics of Clusters Emission yielding Pb-daughters

> In the present study, the newly established preformation formula is applied for the first time to study the kinematics of the cluster emission from various radioactive nuclei, especially those decaying to the double-shell closure $^{208}$Pb nucleus and its neighbours as daughters. The recently proposed universal cluster preformation formula has been established based on the concepts that underscore the influence of the mass and charge asymmetry ($η_A$ and $η_Z$), cluster mass $A_c$ and the Q-value, paving the way to quantify the energy contribution during the preformation as well as the tunnelling process separately. The cluster-daughter interaction potential is obtained by folding the relativistic mean-field (RMF) densities with the recently developed microscopic R3Y using the NL$3^*$ and the phenomenological M3Y NN potentials to compare their adaptability. The penetration probabilities are calculated from the WKB approximation. With the inclusion of the new preformation probability $P_0$, the predicted half-lives from the R3Y and M3Y interactions are in good agreement with the experimental data. Furthermore, a careful inspection reflects slight differences in the decay half-lives, which arise from their respective barrier properties. The $P_0$ for the systems with the double magic shell closure $^{208}$Pb daughter are found to be relatively higher with an order of $\approx 10^2$ than those with neighbouring Pb-daughter nuclei. By exploring the contributions of the decay energy, the recoil effect of the daughter nucleus is appraised, unlike several other conjectures. Thus, the centrality of the Q-value in the decay process is demonstrated and re-defined within the preformed cluster-decay model. Besides, we have introduced a simple and intuitive set of criteria that governs the estimation of recoil energy in the cluster radioactivity.

## Key claim

The newly proposed universal cluster preformation formula, which explicitly accounts for mass and charge asymmetry, cluster mass, and Q-value, is applied for the first time to study cluster emission kinematics; the Q-value is re-defined within the preformed cluster-decay model to separately quantify energy contributed during cluster formation, cluster emission (tunnelling), and daughter recoil, unlike previous studies where recoil energy was assumed negligible.

## Method

The cluster-daughter interaction potential was obtained by folding relativistic mean-field (RMF) densities with the microscopic R3Y NN potential (using the NL3* parameter set) and the phenomenological M3Y NN potential. Penetration probabilities were calculated using the WKB approximation, and decay half-lives were computed within the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) using the new preformation probability formula. The Q-values were calculated from RMF (NL3*), the macroscopic-microscopic WS3 mass table, and experimental binding energies from AME2016 (Wang et al., 2017).

## Key numbers

* Preformation probability \(P_0\) for systems with double magic shell closure \(^{208}\)Pb daughter is higher by an order of \(\approx 10^2\) compared to those with neighbouring Pb-daughter nuclei.
* For \(^{222}\)Ra \(\rightarrow\) \(^{14}\)C + \(^{208}\)Pb: experimental \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = 11.01\), M3Y prediction = 11.12, R3Y prediction = 11.94; \(P_0 = 8.82 \times 10^{-16}\); recoil energy \(E_d = 2.08\) MeV.
* For \(^{232}\)U \(\rightarrow\) \(^{24}\)Ne + \(^{208}\)Pb: experimental \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = 21.08\), M3Y prediction = 20.58, R3Y prediction = 20.94; \(P_0 = 2.92 \times 10^{-24}\); recoil energy \(E_d = 6.45\) MeV.
* For \(^{228}\)Th \(\rightarrow\) \(^{20}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb: experimental \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = 20.72\), M3Y prediction = 20.54, R3Y prediction = 22.28; \(P_0 = 3.78 \times 10^{-21}\); recoil energy \(E_d = 3.92\) MeV.
* For \(^{242}\)Cm \(\rightarrow\) \(^{34}\)Si + \(^{208}\)Pb: experimental \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = 23.24\), M3Y prediction = 23.70, R3Y prediction = 25.46; \(P_0 = 3.80 \times 10^{-30}\); recoil energy \(E_d = 13.56\) MeV.
* The R3Y barrier height is relatively lower than M3Y by about 3.6 MeV for the representative case \(^{228}\)Th \(\rightarrow\) \(^{20}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Context

This work extends the preformed cluster-decay model (PCM) of Gupta and collaborators by introducing a new preformation formula that goes beyond the earlier cluster-mass dependent formula of Blendowske and Walliser (1988). It contradicts the assumption of Levinger (1953) that recoil energy is negligible for heavy-ion emission, and also differs from studies by Xu and Ren (2016) and Ropke et al. (2014) that focused on kinetic energy while ignoring recoil. The results enable systematic estimation of recoil energy in cluster radioactivity and provide a framework to separate the energy contributions of cluster formation, tunnelling, and daughter recoil, with implications for understanding shell effects in cluster decay.
