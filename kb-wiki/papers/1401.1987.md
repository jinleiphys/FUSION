# Illusory Flow in Radiation from Accelerating Charge

> In this paper we analyze the classical electromagnetic radiation of an accelerating point charge moving on a straight line trajectory. Depending on the duration of accelerations, rapidity distributions of photons emerge, resembling the ones obtained in the framework of hydrodynamical models by Landau or Bjorken. Detectable differences between our approach and spectra obtained from hydrodynamical models occur at high transverse momenta and are due to interference.

## Key claim

The rapidity distributions of photons radiated by a single accelerating point charge on a straight-line trajectory can resemble those obtained from hydrodynamical models (Landau or Bjorken), but detectable differences occur at high transverse momenta due to interference effects, meaning that a flowing source medium is not necessarily required to produce such spectra.

## Method

The authors compute the classical electromagnetic radiation spectrum of a point charge undergoing constant proper deceleration along a straight line, using the Fourier transform of the source current with careful treatment of finite acceleration intervals to avoid spurious contributions from constant-velocity segments. The photon yield is expressed as an integral over the charge's proper time, and the resulting rapidity distributions are analyzed for varying acceleration durations and transverse momenta, with the deceleration parameter set to \(g=1\) for shape comparisons.

## Key numbers

- For small transverse momenta (\(\ell k_{\perp} \ll 1\)), the quantity \(k_{\perp}^2 \, dN/(k_{\perp} dk_{\perp} d\eta)\) approaches a constant value that depends on the rapidity gap \(\xi_2 - \xi_1\) during deceleration.
- In the infrared limit (\(k_{\perp} \to 0\)), the scaled yield is given by \(k_{\perp}^2 \, \phott = 2\alpha |v_2 - v_1|^2\), where \(v_i = \tanh(\xi_i - \eta)\).
- For small rapidity differences, the rapidity distribution at low \(k_{\perp}\) takes the form \(k_{\perp}^2 \, \phott = 2\alpha (\xi_2 - \xi_1)^2 / \cosh^4(\eta - \xi_{\text{mid}})\).
- For large rapidity differences, the distribution approaches a plateau: \(k_{\perp}^2 \, \phott \propto 8\alpha / (1 + \varepsilon \sinh^2(\eta - \xi_{\text{mid}}))^2\) with \(\varepsilon = \exp(-|\xi_2 - \xi_1|)\).
- The transition between short and long acceleration behavior occurs around \(g\tau \approx \pi\), when the phase under the integral takes a whole period of interference.
- Fitting gamma spectra at RHIC gave an equivalent Unruh temperature of 135 MeV, corresponding to a deceleration length \(\ell = \hbar c/(2\pi T_U) \approx 0.22\) fm, with an exponential slope parameter \(T = \pi T_U \approx 424\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of pseudothermal effects in gamma spectra from RHIC experiments (Biro, Schram, and Gyulassy, 2013) and connects to the Unruh effect (Unruh, 1976; Hawking, 1975), where constant acceleration produces thermal-like radiation. The results show that the rapidity distributions mimic those from Landau's hydrodynamical model (Landau, 1953) for short accelerations and the Hwa-Bjorken scenario (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1983) for long accelerations, but the presence of interference patterns at high transverse momenta offers a potential experimental test to distinguish classical radiation from true collective flow. The authors caution that flat or bell-shaped rapidity distributions of photons should not be taken as evidence for a flowing source medium, and they note that similar caution may apply to massive particle spectra when transverse momenta exceed the rest mass.
