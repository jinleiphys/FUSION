# Quenching of photon and pion spectra at intermediate RHIC energy

> Single prompt photon and pion spectra in p-p and Au-Au collisions at intermediate RHIC energy, s^1/2 = 62.4 GeV, are computed at large transverse momentum in perturbative QCD. Next-to-leading order calculations in p-p scattering are first presented. The quenching of the prompt photon and pion yield due to energy loss processes in central Au-Au with respect to p-p collisions is then predicted. At this energy, the small phase-space available to produce hard partons makes the pion quenching almost as pronounced as at s^1/2 = 200 GeV, despite the smaller gluon density of the produced medium. In the photon sector, energy loss effects prove small because of this very phase-space restriction, which favours the direct production channel. A significant suppression of high-pt photons is however predicted, because of a strong isospin effect together with the depletion of nuclear parton densities at large x.

## Key claim

At intermediate RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV), the small phase-space available to produce hard partons makes the pion quenching almost as pronounced as at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, despite the smaller gluon density of the produced medium; in the photon sector, energy loss effects prove small because of this very phase-space restriction, which favours the direct production channel, but a significant suppression of high-pT photons is predicted due to a strong isospin effect together with the depletion of nuclear parton densities at large x.

## Method

Single-inclusive pion and photon hadroproduction cross sections in p-p collisions were computed in perturbative QCD at next-to-leading order (NLO) using the CTEQ6M parton distribution functions, KKP fragmentation functions for pions, and BFG fragmentation functions for photons. For Au-Au collisions, nuclear effects were incorporated by replacing proton PDFs with nuclear PDFs including isospin corrections and EKS nuclear shadowing parametrisation, and by modelling partonic energy loss through a shift in the fragmentation variable z using the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff medium-induced gluon spectrum with a characteristic scale omega_c.

## Key numbers

- Pion quenching factor in central Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV: roughly a factor of 5 (R ~ 0.2) over the pT range, comparable to the quenching at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV despite omega_c = 10-15 GeV (half the value at 200 GeV)
- Photon quenching factor at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV: decreases from R ~ 0.85 at pT = 4 GeV to R ~ 0.65 at pT = 15 GeV due to isospin corrections alone; with nuclear PDF corrections, R ~ 1 at low pT and R ~ 0.5 at the highest pT bin
- Energy loss effects on photons at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV: roughly 20% for all pT
- The transverse momentum above which pion quenching is larger at 62.4 GeV than at 200 GeV (R_62.4/200 <= 1) is pT ~ 8 GeV when including nPDF corrections
- Pion cross section uncertainty in p-p at 62.4 GeV: factor of 2 at low pT up to factor of 3 at pT ~ 20 GeV
- Photon cross section uncertainty in p-p at 62.4 GeV: factor of 2 over the whole pT range

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (Arleo 2006) to intermediate RHIC energy, providing predictions for the quenching of both pions and photons in a unified model. The pion quenching predictions are compared with earlier calculations by Vitev (2004), Adil and Gyulassy (2004), Wang (2004), and Eskola, Honkanen, Salgado, Wiedemann (2004), all of which predicted slightly larger quenching factors (R ~ 0.3-0.4) in better agreement with preliminary PHENIX results. The double ratio of quenching factors at 62.4 and 200 GeV was introduced by Adil and Gyulassy (2004) to reduce uncertainty in the initial gluon rapidity distribution. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC Run-6 and provide a missing step between SPS and top RHIC energy measurements.
