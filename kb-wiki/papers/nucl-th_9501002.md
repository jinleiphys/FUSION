# Azimuthal correlations of pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucl.

> Triple differential cross sections of pions in heavy ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucl. are studied with the IQMD model. After discussing general properties of $Δ$ resonance and pion production we focus on azimuthal correlations: At projectile- and target-rapidities we observe an anticorrelation in the in-plane transverse momentum between pions and protons. At c.m.-rapidity, however, we find that high $p_t$ pions are being preferentially emitted perpendicular to the event-plane. We investigate the causes of those correlations and their sensitivity on the density and momentum dependence of the real and imaginary part of the nucleon and pion optical potential.

# Azimuthal correlations of pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions at 1 GeV/nucl.

## Key claim

The anticorrelation between pion and proton in-plane transverse momentum at projectile and target rapidities is dominated by pion scattering (rescattering) rather than pion absorption, contrary to earlier claims by Lib91a for asymmetric systems; at midrapidity, high transverse momentum pions are preferentially emitted perpendicular to the event plane, an effect that is stronger for π⁺ than for π⁻.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon incident beam energy. Pion production was treated via the delta resonance channel, including inelastic reactions (NN → ΔN, Δ → Nπ, ΔN → NN, Nπ → Δ) with experimental cross sections and a modified detailed balance formula for delta absorption. The real part of the nucleon optical potential was implemented using Skyrme-type parametrizations for hard (compressibility 380 MeV) and soft (compressibility 200 MeV) equations of state, with momentum-dependent interactions included; pions propagated on curved trajectories under Coulomb forces between collisions.

## Key numbers

- Total pion multiplicity for central Au+Au collisions at b=0 fm is approximately 55; average minimum bias multiplicity is about 19 (8 π⁻, 6 π⁰, 5 π⁺)
- The IQMD model overpredicts π⁰ yield for Ca+Ca by approximately 60% compared to TAPS data; for Au+Au the π⁺ multiplicity is approximately 20% above KaoS measurements
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio at low transverse momentum reaches 2.5, considerably higher than the Δ-isobar model prediction of 1.8; at high pₜ the ratio decreases toward 1
- At 10 fm/c up to 55 delta resonances are present in the total reaction volume; a delta multiplicity greater than 40 can be sustained for an interval of 10 fm/c
- The mean delta density experienced by deltas is about 0.25 ρ₀; less than 1% of deltas experience delta densities around 0.5 ρ₀
- For central collisions (b ≤ 3 fm) the pion in-plane transverse momentum pₓ is correlated with nucleon flow; at semiperipheral impact parameters a sign reversal to anticorrelation is observed

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collective flow effects in heavy ion collisions, specifically the bounce-off of nuclear matter in the reaction plane predicted by Stoecker (1980) and the squeeze-out perpendicular to the reaction plane predicted by Stoecker (1982). The authors compare their IQMD results to experimental data from the TAPS collaboration (Ven93) and the KaoS collaboration (Brill93a), and contrast their finding that pion scattering dominates the anticorrelation with the earlier conclusion by Lib91a that pion absorption was responsible. The results enable future experimental determination of the nuclear equation of state through high-statistics measurements of pion azimuthal correlations, though the authors note that very high statistics and high precision impact parameter classification are necessary to exploit this sensitivity.
