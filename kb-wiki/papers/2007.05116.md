# Neutron star equation of state: QMF modeling and applications

> Because of the development of many-body theories of nuclear matter, the long-standing, open problem of the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter may be understood in the near future through the confrontation of theoretical calculations with laboratory measurements of nuclear properties \& reactions and increasingly accurate observations in astronomy. In this review, we focus on the following six aspects: 1) providing a survey of the quark mean-field (QMF) model, which consistently describes a nucleon and many-body nucleonic system from a quark potential; 2) applying QMF to both nuclear matter and neutron stars; 3) extending QMF formalism to the description of hypernuclei and hyperon matter, as well as hyperon stars; 4) exploring the hadron-quark phase transition and hybrid stars by combining the QMF model with the quark matter model characterized by the sound speed; 5) constraining interquark interactions through both the gravitational wave signals and electromagnetic signals of binary merger event GW170817; and 6) discussing further opportunities to study dense matter EOS from compact objects, such as neutron star cooling and pulsar glitches.

## Key claim

This review argues that the quark mean-field (QMF) model, which self-consistently describes nucleons from a quark potential, can be systematically extended to hyperon matter, hybrid stars, and multimessenger constraints, but the authors acknowledge that the model's predictions depend on the chosen nucleon radius and that a unified description of all nuclear matter data remains a demanding task.

## Method

The authors survey the QMF model, which starts from a confinement potential for constituent quarks and solves the Dirac equation for quarks in nuclear medium to obtain the effective nucleon mass. This effective mass is then inserted into a relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian with sigma, omega, and rho mesons, and the equations of motion are solved self-consistently to generate the energy density and pressure of nuclear matter. The model is extended to hypernuclei and hyperon matter by incorporating strange baryons, and to hybrid stars by combining QMF with a quark matter model characterized by sound speed. The authors also discuss how gravitational wave and electromagnetic signals from GW170817 constrain interquark interactions.

- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: \(E/A = -16.00\) MeV for the QMF model.
- Incompressibility at saturation: \(K = 240.00\) MeV for the QMF model.
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\rm sym} = 31.00\) MeV for the QMF model.
- Symmetry energy slope at saturation: \(L = 40.0\) MeV for the QMF model.
- Nucleon radius in free space: \(r_N = 0.87\) fm, used as the intermediate value, with variations to 0.80 fm and 1.00 fm.

- Empirical ranges for saturation properties: \(\rho_0 = 0.16 \pm 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(E/A = -16.0 \pm 0.1\) MeV, \(K = 240 \pm 20\) MeV, \(E_{\rm sym} = 31.7 \pm 3.2\) MeV, \(L = 58.7 \pm 28.1\) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model developed by Guichon (1988) and Thomas and collaborators, and the QMF model constructed by Toki and collaborators (1998) using a quark potential model instead of the MIT bag model. It compares QMF predictions with other approaches such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (BCPM EOS), Skyrme effective forces (BSk20 and BSk21), and phenomenological RMF models (TM1, GM1), as well as the microscopic APR EOS. The work enables future studies of neutron star cooling, pulsar glitches, and the use of multimessenger observations from events like GW170817 to constrain the dense matter equation of state.
