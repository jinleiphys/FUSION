# Twist-4 Matrix Elements of the Nucleon from Recent DIS Data at CERN and SLAC

> We analyse the recent precision measurements of the lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering at CERN and SLAC to extract model independent constraints among the nucleon matrix elements of the twist-4 operators. We also study a parameterization of these matrix elements and point out the possibility that the matrix elements of the quark-gluon mixed operator has a negative value of the order of $- (400 \pm 100\ {\rm MeV})^2$ at 5 GeV$^2$ renormalization scale.

# Twist-4 Matrix Elements of the Nucleon from Recent DIS Data at CERN and SLAC

## Key claim

The nucleon matrix element of the quark-gluon mixed twist-4 operator is likely large and negative, of order $-(400 \pm 100\ {\rm MeV})^2$ at a renormalization scale of 5 GeV$^2$, but this result depends on a specific parameterization ansatz relating flavor-dependent matrix elements to parton momentum fractions; the model-independent constraints only give bands relating the three twist-4 matrix elements $A^1$, $A^2$, and $A^g$, not individual values.

## Method

The authors analyzed existing precision deep inelastic scattering data from the NMC, BCDMS, and SLAC collaborations to extract the twist-4 contributions to the spin-averaged structure functions $F_2$ and $F_L$ for proton and neutron targets. They performed polynomial fits to the function $C(x)$ that parameterizes the ratio $F_2^{\tau=4}(x)/F_2^{LT}(x)$ and used the operator product expansion to relate the integrated structure functions to the nucleon matrix elements $A^1$, $A^2$, and $A^g$ of the spin-2, twist-4 operators. They then introduced a flavor-structure ansatz assuming that the ratio of $d$-quark to $u$-quark matrix elements equals the ratio of their momentum fractions, reducing the number of unknowns and allowing extraction of individual matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Integrated twist-4 contribution to $F_2$ at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$: $0.005 \pm 0.004$ GeV$^2$ (proton), $0.011 \pm 0.004$ GeV$^2$ (neutron)
- Integrated twist-4 contribution to $F_L$ at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$: $0.035 \pm 0.012$ GeV$^2$ (proton), $0.023 \pm 0.008$ GeV$^2$ (neutron)
- Transverse momentum scale from $F_L$ analysis: $\kappa^2 = 0.03 \pm 0.01$ GeV$^2$ from SLAC data in the range $0.2 < x < 0.6$
- Model-independent constraints: $A^1 = -A^g + (-0.165 \pm 0.061)$ GeV$^2$ (proton) and $A^1 = -A^g + (-0.093 \pm 0.041)$ GeV$^2$ (neutron); $A^2 = (3/2)A^g + (0.280 \pm 0.096)$ GeV$^2$ (proton) and $A^2 = (3/2)A^g + (0.184 \pm 0.064)$ GeV$^2$ (neutron)
- Quark-gluon mixed operator matrix element $A^g$ from parameterization: between $-(540\ {\rm MeV})^2$ and $-(340\ {\rm MeV})^2$ for proton, between $-(440\ {\rm MeV})^2$ and $-(280\ {\rm MeV})^2$ for neutron at 5 GeV$^2$
- MIT bag model predictions (with $\alpha_s \sim 0.5$ and bag radius 1 fm) give incorrect signs: $\int F_2^{\tau=4} dx = -0.027 \times \alpha_s$ GeV$^2$ (proton) and $\int F_L^{\tau=4} dx = -0.022 \times \alpha_s$ GeV$^2$ (proton)

## Context

This work extends earlier operator product expansion analyses of higher twist effects by Jaffe and Soldate (1982) and Shuryak and Veinstein (1982), who first identified the twist-4 operators contributing to deep inelastic scattering. The authors use the recent high-precision data from the NMC, BCDMS, and SLAC collaborations to provide the first model-independent constraints among the three twist-4 matrix elements, going beyond earlier qualitative estimates. The results enable tests of nucleon models such as the MIT bag model (which fails to reproduce the sign of the data) and provide input for QCD sum rule calculations of hadron propagation in nuclear matter as developed by Hatsuda, Koike, and Lee (1993). The extracted matrix elements are also relevant for analyzing higher twist effects in other hard scattering processes such as neutrino-induced reactions and Drell-Yan processes.
