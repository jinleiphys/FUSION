# Simulating spin dynamics with spin-dependent cross sections in heavy-ion collisions

> We have incorporated the spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections into a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model for the first time, using the spin-singlet and spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections extracted from the phase-shift analyses of nucleon-nucleon scatterings in free space. We found that the spin splitting of the collective flows is not affected by the spin-dependent cross sections, justifying it as a good probe of the in-medium nuclear spin-orbit interaction. With the in-medium nuclear spin-orbit mean-field potential that leads to local spin polarization, we found that the spin-averaged observables, such as elliptic flows of free nucleons and light clusters, becomes smaller with the spin-dependent differential nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections.

## Key claim

The spin splitting of collective flows (the spin up-down differential transverse flow) is not affected by spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections, justifying it as a clean probe of the in-medium nuclear spin-orbit interaction; however, spin-averaged observables such as elliptic flows of free nucleons and light clusters become slightly smaller when both the spin-dependent mean-field potential and spin-dependent cross sections are used together, due to local spin polarization.

## Method

The authors incorporated spin-singlet and spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering cross sections into a spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SIBUU) transport model for the first time. The cross sections were extracted from energy-dependent phase-shift analyses of free-space nucleon-nucleon scatterings (Arndt et al., 1977) for incident energies between 1 and 500 MeV, and parameterized as functions of energy and scattering angle. Heavy-ion collisions of Au+Au at 100 MeV/nucleon were simulated with and without the spin-dependent mean-field potential, comparing results from spin-averaged versus spin-dependent cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Spin-singlet and spin-triplet total cross sections for neutron-proton and proton-proton elastic scatterings were parameterized for incident nucleon energies between 1 and 500 MeV (e.g., \(\sigma_{np}^{0} = 9302.64/E^{3} - 982.187/E^{2} - 1.32\times10^{2} + 1.03E - 3.06\times10^{-3}E^{2} + 4.85\times10^{-6}E^{3} - 3.19\times10^{-9}E^{4}\) in mb with \(E\) in MeV).
- The spin up-down differential transverse flow \(F_{ud}\) in Au+Au collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 12 fm was found to be essentially identical when using spin-averaged versus spin-dependent cross sections, both with the spin-orbit potential present.
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of free nucleons and deuterons in Au+Au collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 12 fm showed a slight reduction with spin-dependent cross sections compared to spin-averaged ones, only when the spin-orbit mean-field potential was included.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SIBUU transport model studies (Xu et al., 2013; Xia et al., 2014, 2016) which had incorporated spin-dependent mean-field potentials but used spin-averaged nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections. The finding that spin splitting of collective flows is unaffected by spin-dependent cross sections strengthens the case for using these flows as probes of the in-medium nuclear spin-orbit interaction, as proposed earlier by the same group. The complete framework now enables further exploration of spin dynamics in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that spin-dependent inelastic cross sections and in-medium modifications of cross sections remain open challenges.
