# Nuclear forces with Delta-excitations up to next-to-next-to-leading order I: peripheral nucleon-nucleon waves

> We study the two-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-leading order in a chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta degrees of freedom. Fixing the appearing low-energy constants from a next-to-leading order calculation of pion-nucleon threshold parameters, we find an improved convergence of most peripheral nucleon-nucleon phases compared to the theory with pions and nucleons only. In the delta-full theory, the next-to-leading order corrections are dominant in most partial waves considered.

## Key claim

Including explicit Delta degrees of freedom in a chiral effective field theory for the two-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-leading order leads to improved convergence of most peripheral nucleon-nucleon phase shifts compared to the theory with only pions and nucleons, with the next-to-leading order corrections being dominant in most partial waves considered.

## Method

The authors constructed the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential at next-to-next-to-leading order in the small scale expansion, which treats the Delta-nucleon mass splitting as an additional small parameter. They determined the dimension-two low-energy constants (the pion-nucleon LECs c_i and the combination of pion-nucleon-Delta LECs b_3+b_8) from a next-to-leading order fit to S- and P-wave pion-nucleon threshold parameters. The resulting potential was then used in Born approximation to calculate peripheral D-, F-, and G-wave phase shifts and mixing parameters, which were compared to results from the theory without explicit Deltas and to phase shift analyses.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.27 was used at leading order; two values for the N-Delta axial coupling h_A were employed: h_A = 1.05 and h_A = 3g_A/(2*sqrt(2)) = 1.34 from SU(4) or large N_c.
- The fitted LECs from the Q^2 fit with explicit Deltas (fit 1, h_A = 1.34) are: c_1 = -0.57 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = -0.25 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -0.79 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 1.33 GeV^{-1}, b_3+b_8 = 1.40 GeV^{-1}.
- The fitted LECs from the Q^2 fit with explicit Deltas (fit 2, h_A = 1.05) are: c_1 = -0.57 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 0.83 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -1.87 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 1.87 GeV^{-1}, b_3+b_8 = 2.95 GeV^{-1}.
- The spectral function regularization cutoff was set to tilde-Lambda = 700 MeV for the potential calculations.
- The isovector central potential at NNLO with explicit Deltas was nearly twice as strong as at NNLO without explicit Deltas.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral effective field theory studies of the two-nucleon force by Ordonez et al. (1996) and Kaiser et al. (1998) by systematically including Delta-excitations beyond leading order in the small scale expansion. The improved convergence demonstrated here for peripheral waves enables future calculations of lower partial waves and three-nucleon forces within the same framework, and provides a foundation for comparing the Delta-full and Delta-less formulations of nuclear forces at higher orders.
