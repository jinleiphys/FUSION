# Parton Energy Loss in an Unstable Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The energy loss of a fast parton scattering elastically in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma is formulated as an initial value problem. The approach is designed to study an unstable plasma, but it reproduces the well known result in the case of an equilibrium plasma. Contributions to the energy loss due to unstable modes are shown to exponentially grow in time. An unstable two-stream system is considered as an example.

## Key claim

The paper formulates the collisional energy loss of a fast parton in a quark-gluon plasma as an initial value problem, which allows the treatment of unstable plasmas where the energy loss is not constant but grows exponentially in time due to unstable collective modes. The abstract's claim that "contributions to the energy loss due to unstable modes are shown to exponentially grow in time" glosses over the condition that after a sufficiently long time the energy loss is dominated by the fastest unstable mode, and that the result depends on a ultraviolet cut-off parameter \(k_{\rm max}\) when computed numerically.

## Method

The energy loss is derived from the Wong equations for a classical parton with SU(3) color charge, using a one-sided Fourier transformation to treat the problem as an initial value problem. The chromoelectric field is obtained from the linearized Yang-Mills equations, expressed through the chromodielectric tensor \(\varepsilon^{ij}(\omega, {\bf k})\) and the matrix \(\Sigma^{ij}(\omega, {\bf k})\). For unstable plasmas, the poles of \(\Sigma^{-1}(\omega, {\bf k})\) in the upper half-plane produce exponentially growing contributions, and the initial values of the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields cannot be neglected. The method is applied to a two-stream system with a delta-function momentum distribution, where the longitudinal dielectric function \(\varepsilon_L(\omega, {\bf k})\) is computed analytically and the energy loss integral is evaluated numerically with a finite momentum cut-off.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss per unit length is shown for a parton flying along the streams with parameters \(g=1\), \(|{\bf v}| = 1\), \(|{\bf u}| = 0.9\), \(C_R = 3\), and cut-off values \(k_{\rm max} = 20, 50, 100\) (dimensionless units scaled by \(\mu\)).
- Choosing \(\mu = 200\) MeV as a rough estimate of the Debye mass in QGP from nuclear collisions, the units on the horizontal and vertical axes in the figure become fm and 200 MeV/fm, respectively.
- The parton's energy loss in QGP observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is of order 1 GeV/fm, indicating the magnitude of the energy loss discussed here is of phenomenological interest.

## Context

This work extends the study of collisional energy loss in anisotropic quark-gluon plasma by Romatschke (2004) by explicitly accounting for the time dependence of unstable chromomagnetic modes, which were neglected in that prior work. The approach is similar to the method used by Majumder (2009) to study momentum broadening \(\hat{q}\) in anisotropic plasma, and to the method used by Mrowczynski (2008) to study chromodynamic fluctuations of an unstable plasma. The formalism enables future quantum approaches to parton energy loss in unstable plasmas, as the classical results show ultraviolet sensitivity that signals the need to combine classical contributions at small wave vectors with quantum contributions at higher ones.
