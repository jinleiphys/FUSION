# Jet quenching in QCD matter: from RHIC to LHC

> The current experimental and theoretical status of hadron and jet production at large transverse momentum in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions is summarised. The most important RHIC results are compared to theoretical parton energy loss predictions providing direct information on the (thermo)dynamical properties of hot and dense QCD matter. Prospects for the LHC are also outlined.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-transverse-momentum hadrons and dihadron correlations in central AuAu collisions at RHIC is quantitatively consistent with parton energy loss models in a very dense QCD plasma, but the extracted transport coefficient $\hat{q} \approx 13$ GeV$^2$/fm is 3–4 times larger than leading-order perturbative estimates, supporting the interpretation of a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$ for single hadrons (pions, etas, charged hadrons, and direct photons) and the azimuthal dihadron correlation function $C(\Delta\phi,\Delta\eta)$ in AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, comparing them to theoretical parton energy loss calculations (GLV, HT, PQM models). The extracted medium properties (gluon density $dN^g/dy$, transport coefficient $\hat{q}$, temperature $T$) are derived by fitting the suppression patterns, and the path-length, centrality, centre-of-mass energy, colour-factor, and heavy-quark mass dependences are examined.

## Key numbers

- $R_{AA} \approx 0.2$ (factor of 5 suppression) for $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and charged hadrons at $p_T > 5$ GeV/c in central AuAu at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
- Initial gluon rapidity density $dN^g/dy \approx 1400$ from GLV model fit to RHIC data.
- Transport coefficient $\hat{q} \approx 13$ GeV$^2$/fm from PQM model fit to RHIC data.
- Plasma temperature $T \approx 0.4$ GeV from model fits.
- For an equilibrated gluon plasma at $T = 0.4$ GeV with $\alpha_s \approx 0.5$, the perturbative estimate gives $\hat{q} \simeq 2.2$ GeV$^2$/fm.
- $I_{AA}^{away} \approx 0.2$–0.3 for the most central AuAu events from dihadron correlations.
- Best-fit energy loss parameter $\epsilon_0 \approx 1.9$ GeV/fm from combined $R_{AA}$ and $I_{AA}$ fits.
- Path-length dependence exponent $\alpha = 0.56 \pm 0.10$ from $R_{AA}$ vs. $N_{part}$ fit.
- At SPS ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 20$ GeV), $R_{AA}$ is consistent with unity for $\pi^0$ in central PbPb.
- For central CuCu at 200 GeV, $R_{AA}$ is a factor of $(A_{Au}/A_{Cu})^{2/3} \approx 2$ higher than in central AuAu.
- At LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV), predicted $R_{AA}$ for $\pi^0$ in central PbPb from GLV ($dN^g/dy = 2000$–4000) and PQM ($\langle \hat{q} \rangle \approx 30$–80 GeV$^2$/fm) are given as yellow bands.
- Expected underlying event transverse energy $E_T^{UE} \approx 80$ GeV in a cone of radius $R = 0.4$ for central PbPb at LHC.
- Jet $p_T$ reach at LHC in PbPb at 5.5 TeV is up to $p_T \approx 500$ GeV/c.

## Context

This review summarises the experimental and theoretical status of jet quenching from RHIC to LHC, extending the earlier work of d'Enterria (2006) and the parton energy loss formalisms of Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff (BDMPS), Gyulassy-Lévai-Vitev (GLV), and the higher-twist (HT) approach. The results contradict the expectation of a weakly-coupled perturbative QGP and instead support the strongly-coupled QGP picture advocated by Gyulassy and McLerran (2004). The heavy-flavour puzzle, where $R_{AA}$ for electrons from $D$ and $B$ decays is similar to light hadrons, contrary to dead-cone predictions, motivates models including in-medium meson dissociation (Adil and Vitev, 2006). At LHC, the analysis of fully reconstructed jets, jet shapes, and $\gamma$-jet fragmentation functions (Loizides, 2008) will enable precise tests of the energy-loss mechanisms.
