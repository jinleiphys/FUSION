# Charmonium - Pion Cross Section from QCD Sum Rules

> The $J/ψπ\to \bar{D} D^*, \bar{D} D$, ${\bar D}^* D^*$ and ${\bar D} D^*$ cross sections as a function of $\sqrt{s}$ are evaluated in a QCD sum rule calculation. We study the Borel sum rule for the four point function involving pseudoscalar and vector meson currents, up to dimension four in the operator product expansion. We find that our results are smaller than the $J/ψπ\to {charmed mesons}$ cross sections obtained with models based on meson exchange, but are close to those obtained with quark exchange models.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculation yields J/ψπ → charmed meson cross sections that are smaller than those from meson-exchange models but close to quark-exchange model results; however, the authors caution that their numbers should be regarded as upper bounds because the calculation may implicitly include contributions from ψ'π processes and because the soft-pion limit and single Borel transform introduce uncertainties.

## Method

The authors evaluate the four-point correlation function involving pseudoscalar and vector meson currents using the Borel sum rule technique, up to dimension four in the operator product expansion (OPE). They take the soft-pion limit (p1 → 0) and perform a single Borel transform in the squared momenta of the J/ψ, D, and D* mesons, introducing a parameter A to account for nondiagonal pole-continuum transitions. The hadronic amplitudes Λ for the channels J/ψπ → D̅D*, D̅D, and D̅*D* are extracted by extrapolating the QCD sum rule results to M² = 0, and the cross sections are then computed from these amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- ΛDD* ≈ 17.71 GeV⁻² for the J/ψπ → D̅D* amplitude.
- ΛDD ≈ 12.25 GeV⁻¹ for the J/ψπ → D̅D amplitude.
- ΛD*D* ≈ 11.39 GeV⁻³ for the J/ψπ → D̅*D* amplitude.
- In the fiducial region 4.1 ≤ √s ≤ 4.3 GeV, the total J/ψπ dissociation cross section is 2.5 ≤ σ ≤ 4.0 mb.
- Input parameters: m_c = 1.5 GeV, m_D = 1.87 GeV, m_D* = 2.01 GeV, m_ψ = 3.097 GeV, ⟨q̅q⟩ = -(0.23)³ GeV³, F_π = 131.5 MeV.
- Decay constants from two-point sum rules: f_D = 155 ± 5 MeV, f_D* = 195 ± 5 MeV, f_ψ = 225 ± 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule technique to charmonium-pion dissociation, a process previously studied via meson-exchange effective Lagrangians (e.g., Matinyan and Müller (1998)) and nonrelativistic quark exchange models (e.g., Wong (2000)). The results are smaller than those from meson-exchange models without form factors but agree in order of magnitude with quark exchange calculations. The approach differs from the short-distance OPE method of Bhanot and Peskin (1979) and later updates by Kharzeev et al. and Oh et al., which assume a nearly pointlike J/ψ; the larger cross sections found here suggest that finite-size effects are important. The work enables future in-medium calculations by modifying the quark condensate, which is expected to reduce the cross section further in nuclear matter.
