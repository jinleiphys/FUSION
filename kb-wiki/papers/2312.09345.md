# Superfluid fraction in the slab phase of the inner crust of neutron stars

> An analysis of the slab phase as it is expected in the innermost layer of neutron-star crusts is performed within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. We take the periodicity of the slabs into account using Bloch boundary conditions, in order to well describe the interplay between the band structure and superfluidity. We introduce a relative flow between the slabs and the surrounding neutron gas in a time-independent way. This induces a non-trivial phase of the complex order parameter, leading to a counterflow between neutrons inside and outside the slabs. With the resulting current, we compute the actual neutron superfluid fraction. For the latter our results are slightly larger than previous ones obtained in normal band theory, suggesting that normal band theory overestimates the entrainment effect.

## Key claim

The authors compute the neutron superfluid fraction in the slab phase of the inner crust using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with Bloch boundary conditions, finding results slightly larger than those from normal band theory; this suggests that normal band theory overestimates the entrainment effect, but the result is conditional on the specific choice of pairing interaction and the restriction to one-dimensional slab geometry.

## Method

The authors perform static HFB calculations for neutrons and Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for protons in a one-dimensional periodic slab (lasagna) phase, using the SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functional for the mean field and a separable Gaussian pairing interaction fitted to the Vlow-k potential. They introduce a relative flow between the slabs and the surrounding neutron gas by applying a Galilean transformation to a frame where the superfluid neutrons carry zero average momentum, adding a term −ℏk·v to the mean-field Hamiltonian. The neutron superfluid fraction is then extracted from the linear relation between the average neutron current and the slab velocity.

## Key numbers

- Pairing interaction parameters: coupling constant g = 853 MeV fm³, Gaussian width k₀ = 1.365 fm⁻¹.
- Cell extensions L = 24 fm and L = 40 fm are considered for neutron chemical potentials μₙ = 10 MeV and μₙ = 12 MeV.
- The estimated relative flow velocity between superfluid and normal components in the Vela pulsar is v_N ≈ 4 × (10⁻⁷ – 10⁻⁶) c, based on δΩ ≈ 10⁻² – 10⁻¹ s⁻¹ and a neutron star radius R_NS ≈ 12 km.
- The coherence length condition for LDA validity is ξ ≪ L, with ξ = ℏ² k_F / (π Δ m*).

## Context

This work extends the band-structure calculations of Chamel (2005, 2006, 2012) and the hydrodynamical approach of Martin and Urban (2016) by using HFB theory, which includes both band structure and superfluid dynamics. The authors note that previous BCS approximations (Chamel, 2010) miss the dynamics of the order parameter, while the hydrodynamical approach assumes Cooper pairs are small compared to the lattice spacing, which is not valid in the inner crust. The results enable future extension to two-dimensional (rod) and three-dimensional (crystal) phases, and may help reconcile microscopic calculations with observed pulsar glitches.
