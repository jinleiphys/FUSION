# Faddeev treatment of long-range correlations and the one-hole spectral function of O16

> The Faddeev technique is employed to study the influence of both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons on the one-hole spectral function of O16. Collective excitations are accounted for at a random phase approximation level and subsequently summed to all orders by the Faddeev equations to obtain the nucleon self-energy. An iterative procedure is applied to investigate the effects of the self-consistent inclusion of the fragmentation in the determination of the phonons and the corresponding self-energy. The present results indicate that the characteristics of hole fragmentation are related to the low-lying states of O16.

## Key claim

The Faddeev treatment of long-range correlations (LRC) in O16, including both particle-particle and particle-hole phonons at the random phase approximation (RPA) level, reduces the discrepancy with experimental spectroscopic factors by about 4% compared to Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) calculations, but the authors note that the RPA description of the phonon spectrum itself is incomplete and further improvements may close the remaining gap.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function within a harmonic oscillator model space (first four major shells plus 1g9/2, oscillator parameter b=1.76 fm) using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn-C potential. They computed the irreducible self-energy by solving Faddeev equations for 2p1h and 2h1p propagation, where the vertices were constructed from RPA (or TDA) phonons for both particle-hole and particle-particle (hole-hole) channels. An iterative procedure was applied: after solving the Dyson equation, the resulting fragmented propagator was used as input for a dressed RPA calculation and then a new Faddeev solution, repeated for a few iterations.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for p1/2 hole: 0.775 (TDA), 0.745 (RPA with IPM input), 0.774 (after third iteration with fragmentation)
- Spectroscopic factor for p3/2 hole: 0.766 (TDA), 0.725 (RPA with IPM input), 0.722 (after third iteration, plus a secondary fragment at -26.3 MeV with 2.6% strength)
- Experimental spectroscopic strength for proton knockout from p1/2 and p3/2 shells: about 60%, with a 5-10% error estimate
- Predicted spectroscopic factor for d5/2 fragment at -15.6 MeV: 0.1% (experimental: 1.9%)
- Predicted spectroscopic factor for s1/2 fragment at -15.8 MeV: 3.0% (experimental: 1.8%)
- Total number of particles from summed hole strength: about 15 (IPM input), about 14.6 (after iteration with fragmentation)
- G-matrix fixed starting energy for Faddeev equations: -25 MeV; for the unstable 0+ state: -110 MeV

## Context

This work extends the TDA-level calculations of Geurts (2001) and Muether (2001) for LRC in O16 by incorporating RPA-level collective excitations, which were previously inaccessible due to the difficulty of simultaneously treating ph and pp(hh) excitations beyond TDA. The formalism follows the Faddeev approach developed in earlier work by the same group (Barbieri and Dickhoff, 2001) for including RPA phonons to all orders. The results enable future self-consistent calculations that could resolve the missing low-lying states in the residual nuclei N15 and C14, which are relevant for interpreting (e,e'p) and (e,e'pp) experiments.
