# Electromagnetic structure and weak decay of pseudoscalar mesons in a light-front QCD-inspired model

> We study the scaling of the $^3S_1-^1S_0$ meson mass splitting and the pseudoscalar weak decay constants with the mass of the meson, as seen in the available experimental data. We use an effective light-front QCD-inspired dynamical model regulated at short-distances to describe the valence component of the pseudoscalar mesons. The experimentally known values of the mass splittings, decay constants (from global lattice-QCD averages) and the pion charge form factor up to 4 [GeV/c]$^2$ are reasonably described by the model

# Electromagnetic structure and weak decay of pseudoscalar mesons in a light-front QCD-inspired model

## Key claim
The light-front QCD-inspired model with a short-distance regulator can describe the scaling of pseudoscalar weak decay constants and mass splittings across meson masses, but the naive linear scaling of decay constants with vector meson mass (f_ps proportional to M_v) fails for heavy mesons; instead the regulated model produces a saturation and decrease of f_ps for heavy-light systems, consistent with lattice-QCD and potential model expectations that f_D > f_B.

## Method
The authors solve an effective squared mass operator equation for the valence component of the light-front wave function of pseudoscalar mesons, including a Coulomb-like one-gluon-exchange interaction and a regulated short-range hyperfine interaction in separable form. The eigenvalue equation is transformed to instant-form momentum basis and solved numerically using a resolvent method. The valence wave function is then used to compute electromagnetic form factors via the impulse approximation of the plus component of the current in the Drell-Yan frame, and weak decay constants from the matrix element of the axial current.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses derived from vector meson masses: m_u = 384 MeV (from M_rho/2), m_s = 508 MeV (from M_K* - M_rho/2), m_c = 1623 MeV (from M_D* - M_rho/2), m_b = 4941 MeV (from M_B* - M_rho/2)
- Pion charge radius experimental value: 0.67 ± 0.02 fm
- Pion decay constant from the model: f_pi = 110 MeV (compared to experimental value 92.4 ± 0.07 ± 0.25 MeV)
- Kaon weak decay constant from the model: f_K = 126 MeV (varies less than 1 MeV for alpha between 0.1 and 0.5)
- Regulator parameters for alpha = 0.5: beta^(a) = -(634.5 MeV)^2 and beta^(b) = (1171 MeV)^2
- Mass splitting differences between the two regulator forms are less than 1 MeV
- Soft-pion limit with alpha = 0, g(M_0^2) = 1 and m_pi = 0 gives charge radius sqrt(<r^2_pi>) = 0.58 fm

## Context
This work extends the renormalized light-front QCD-inspired model of Pauli et al. (1990s) and the earlier unregulated model of Tobpauli by introducing a short-distance regulator in separable form. The model contradicts the naive scaling f_ps proportional to M_v proposed by Khoplov and by Luiz (2004), which fails for heavy mesons; instead the regulated model reproduces the behavior found in lattice-QCD simulations (Flynn, 2000s) and Dyson-Schwinger approaches (Maris and Roberts, 2000s) where f_ps decreases as 1/sqrt(M_ps) in the heavy-quark limit. The results enable systematic study of correlations between meson observables across different quark flavors using a few-parameter effective theory.
