# Extended soft-wall model for the QCD phase diagram

> The soft-wall model, emerging as bottom-up holographic scenario anchored in the AdS/CFT correspondence, displays the disappearance of normalisable modes referring to vector mesons at a temperature $T_{\dis}$ depending on the chemical potential $μ$, $T_{\dis}(μ)$. We explore options for making $T_{\dis}(μ)$ consistent with the freeze-out curve $T_{\rm f.o.}(μ)$ from relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the cross-over curve $T_{\rm c}(μ)$ from QCD at small values of $μ$.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a modified soft-wall holographic model can be tuned so that the temperature at which vector meson states disappear as normalisable modes, \(T_{\rm dis}(\mu)\), matches both the chemical freeze-out curve from heavy-ion collisions and the QCD crossover curve at small baryochemical potential, but only up to a certain critical value of \(\mu\) beyond which the agreement breaks down.

## Method
The authors start from the soft-wall model action for a U(1) vector field in a five-dimensional AdS-like spacetime with a dilaton field \(\Phi(z) = (cz)^p\) and a warp factor \(A(z) = \ln(L^2/z^2 + \tilde\mu^2)\). They construct a modified blackness function \(f(z)\) that generalises the Reissner-Nordstrom form, from which the Hawking temperature \(T(z_H)\) is derived. The equation of motion for vector mesons is transformed into a Schrodinger-type equation whose normalisable solutions yield the meson masses; the disappearance of these solutions defines \(T_{\rm dis}\). The parameters \(p\), \(\tilde\mu\), \(c\), \(\tilde T_{\rm min}\), \(\tilde z_{\rm min}\), and \(\gamma\) are adjusted to make \(T_{\rm dis}(\mu)\) coincide with the parametrised freeze-out/crossover curve \(T_{\rm f.o.}(\mu) \cong T_c(\mu) = T_0(1 - \kappa(\mu/T_0)^2)\).

## Key numbers
* The crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is set to \(T_0 = 155\) MeV.
* The curvature parameter \(\kappa\) in the freeze-out/crossover curve is taken from Bazavov (2016) as \(\kappa = 0.005\) to \(0.01\).
* The model parameters at \(T=\mu=0\) are \(p=1.99\), \(\tilde\mu=0.5\), and \(c=443\) MeV, producing a Regge trajectory with \(\alpha=0.71\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\beta=0.75\) GeV\(^2\).
* For \(\tilde T_{\min}=155\) MeV and \(c\tilde z_{\min}=2\), the ground state disappearance matches the freeze-out curve up to \(\mu=620\) MeV (for \(\kappa=0.005\)) or \(\mu=440\) MeV (for \(\kappa=0.01\)).
* The parameter \(\gamma\) is set to \(7.85\) (for \(\kappa=0.005\)) or \(5.55\) (for \(\kappa=0.01\)) to achieve the matching.

## Context
This work extends the soft-wall model of Karch, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2006) and the finite-temperature treatment of Colangelo, De Fazio, Giannuzzi, Nicotri, and Scardino (2011) to include chemical potential dependence. It builds on the earlier zero-chemical-potential study by the same group (ich, 2015) that tuned the model to reproduce the lattice QCD crossover temperature. The results enable a holographic description of the QCD phase diagram at small \(\mu\) that is consistent with both lattice QCD crossover calculations (Bazavov, 2016) and the chemical freeze-out systematics from heavy-ion experiments (Stachel, 2014; Becattini, 2014), but the authors caution that extrapolation to larger \(\mu\) is premature due to the leading-order nature of the parametrisation and unsettled options for the blackness function.
