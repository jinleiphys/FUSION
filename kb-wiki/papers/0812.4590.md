# Hard Break-Up of Two-Nucleons from the 3He Nucleus

> We investigate a large angle photodisintegration of two nucleons from the $^3$He nucleus within the framework of the hard rescattering model (HRM). In the HRM a quark of one nucleon knocked out by an incoming photon rescatters with a quark of the other nucleon leading to the production of two nucleons with large relative momentum. Assuming the dominance of the quark-interchange mechanism in a hard NN scattering, the HRM allows to express the amplitude of a two-nucleon break-up reaction through the convolution of photon-quark scattering, $NN$ hard scattering amplitude and nuclear spectral function. The photon-quark scattering amplitude can be explicitly calculated in the high energy regime, whereas for $NN$ scattering one uses the fit of the available experimental data. The HRM predicts several specific features for the hard breakup reaction. First, the cross section will approximately scale as $s^{-11}$. Secondly, the $s^{11}$ weighted cross section will have the shape of energy dependence similar to that of $s^{10}$ weighted $NN$ elastic scattering cross section. Also one predicts an enhancement of the $pp$ breakup relative to the $pn$ breakup cross section as compared to the results from low energy kinematics. Another result is the prediction of different spectator momentum dependencies of $pp$ and $pn$ breakup cross sections. This is due to the fact that same-helicity $pp$-component is strongly suppressed in the ground state wave function of $^3$He. Because of this suppression the HRM predicts significantly different asymmetries for the cross section of polarization transfer $NN$ breakup reactions for circularly polarized photons. For the $pp$ breakup this asymmetry is predicted to be zero while for the $pn$ it is close to ${2\over 3}$.

## Key claim

The hard rescattering model (HRM) predicts that for photon energies above approximately 2 GeV, the cross section for two-nucleon photodisintegration from 3He will approximately scale as s⁻¹¹, and that the s¹¹-weighted cross section will mirror the energy dependence of s¹⁰-weighted NN elastic scattering cross sections, with the caveat that the model's validity may extend to lower energies (≳1 GeV) if a smooth transition from hadronic to quark-gluon degrees of freedom occurs.

## Method

The authors applied the hard rescattering model (HRM) to the reaction γ + ³He → (NN) + Ns, where two nucleons are produced at large angles in the γ-NN center-of-mass frame. They expressed the scattering amplitude as a convolution of photon-quark scattering, hard NN scattering amplitude (using the quark-interchange mechanism), and the ³He nuclear spectral function derived from a nonrelativistic wave function, with the α-integration performed via a peaking approximation at α = 1/2. The quark-interchange NN amplitudes were substituted with actual NN helicity amplitudes from experimental data, and the charge factors for the struck quarks were calculated using the quark-interchange model of NN interactions.

## Key numbers

- The cross section approximately scales as s⁻¹¹ for the hard breakup reaction.
- The condition for validity of the HRM requires photon energy Eγ ≥ 2 GeV and transferred momenta -t, -u ≥ 2 GeV².
- The intermediate mass after photon absorption must exceed W ≈ 2.2 GeV, corresponding to Eγ ≥ 2 GeV.
- The relative momentum of the two outgoing nucleons must satisfy krel ≥ 1 GeV/c.
- For spectator momenta ps ≤ 100 MeV/c, the peaking approximation introduces approximately 20% uncertainty in the calculated cross section.
- The asymmetry for pp breakup with circularly polarized photons is predicted to be zero, while for pn breakup it is close to 2/3.

## Context

This work extends the hard rescattering model previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration (Sargsian, 2001; Sargsian, 2002) to the case of ³He targets, as suggested by Sargsian (2003) to test competing models. It contrasts with the reduced nuclear amplitude formalism (Brodsky, 1984; Brodsky, 1985), the quark-gluon string model (Kaidalov, 1994), and point-form relativistic quantum mechanics (Melnitchouk, 2005), which all describe deuteron photodisintegration but diverge significantly in their predictions for ³He. The analysis enables experimental tests currently underway at Jefferson Lab (Sargsian, 2003 proposal) and provides new observables such as spectator-neutron momentum distributions and polarization asymmetries to distinguish between theoretical approaches.
