# A discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich decomposition for general, fermionic two-body interactions

> A scheme is presented to decompose the exponential of a two-body operator in a discrete sum over exponentials of one-body operators. This discrete decomposition can be used instead of the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation in auxiliary-field quantum Monte-Carlo methods. As an illustration, the decomposition is applied to the Hubbard model, where it is equivalent to the discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduced by Hirsch, and to the nuclear pairing Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The paper presents a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich decomposition that is exact for a special class of two-body operators, but when applied to a general two-body Hamiltonian via the Suzuki-Trotter formula, the total decomposition is no longer exact due to non-commutativity of the operators, with an error of order O(β³/Nt²).

- For the Hubbard model with interaction strength U, the decomposition yields χ↑ = -χ↓ = √(1 - e^{-βU}) for βU > 0, or χ↑ = χ↓ = √(e^{-βU} - 1) for βU < 0.
- For the nuclear pairing Hamiltonian with G = 20/56 MeV (using A=56), the inverse temperature β was split into slices of length 0.05 MeV⁻¹ to make systematic error smaller than statistical error.
- The decomposition parameter for the pairing Hamiltonian is χ² = N_S (e^{βG/2} - 1), where N_S = 10 for the test system with 10 particles in 20 degenerate single-particle states.
- The specific heat curve shows a peak around a temperature of 0.8 MeV, associated with the breakup of J^π = 0⁺ pairs.

## Context

This work extends the discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduced by Hirsch (1983) for the Hubbard model to a general class of fermionic two-body interactions, and provides an alternative to the continuous Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation used in auxiliary-field quantum Monte-Carlo methods. The decomposition is applied to the nuclear pairing Hamiltonian, where it enables sign-problem-free calculations for systems with an even number of particles, analogous to the sign rule for the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian using the Hubbard-Stratonovich transform as discussed by Lang and collaborators. The method enables faster matrix multiplications and reduced memory requirements compared to the discretized continuous Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation.
