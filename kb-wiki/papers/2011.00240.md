# Conserved charge fluctuations in the chiral limit

> We study the signs of criticality in conserved charge fluctuations and related observables of finite temperature QCD at vanishing chemical potential, as we approach the chiral limit of two light quarks. Our calculations have been performed on gauge ensembles generated using Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) fermion action, with pion masses ranging from 140 MeV to 55 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that conserved charge fluctuations at finite lattice spacing are consistent with the chiral phase transition belonging to the O(2) universality class (and therefore O(4) in the continuum limit), but the authors hedge that these are preliminary results at a single lattice spacing (Nτ=8) and that more statistics and continuum extrapolations are needed for definitive conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) fermion action with tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action, on gauge ensembles generated by the HotQCD collaboration. They computed second, fourth, and sixth order conserved charge fluctuations (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) and mixed observables (light quark mass derivatives of fluctuations and condensates) at zero chemical potential, using four light quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 140 MeV down to 55 MeV, all at a single lattice spacing with temporal extent Nτ=8.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 140 MeV, 110 MeV, 80 MeV, and 55 MeV, corresponding to light quark masses m_l = m_s/27, m_s/40, m_s/80, and m_s/160 respectively
- Chiral phase transition temperature at Nτ=8: T_c = 144 MeV (from Ref. Ding:2019prx)
- Singular contribution to χ₂^B at T_c at physical quark mass: approximately 50%
- Singular contribution to χ₂^Q at T_c at physical quark mass: approximately 30%
- Singular contribution to χ₂^S at T_c at physical quark mass: approximately 20%
- Ratio of curvature coefficients κ₂^Q/κ₂^B: 2.6 (preliminary)
- Ratio of curvature coefficients κ₂^B/κ₂^S: 1.0 (preliminary)
- Expected ratio of peak heights for χ₆^Q between H=1/27 and H=1/40 from O(2) singular parts: approximately 1.26

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition by Ding (2019) and Clarke (2020) to conserved charge fluctuations, testing whether these observables show the universal scaling behavior predicted by 3d O(4) spin models (Pisarski, 1983). The results enable future quantitative scaling analyses following the approach of Clarke (2020), and will allow determination of curvature coefficients of the chiral critical line. The authors note that the alternative scenario of a Z(2) critical point at intermediate light quark mass (Philipsen, 2016) is not favored by their data, though they do not rule it out definitively.
