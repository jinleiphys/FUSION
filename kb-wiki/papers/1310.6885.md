# Quasielastic contribution to antineutrino-nucleus scattering

> We report on a calculation of cross sections for charged-current quasielastic antineutrino scattering off $^{12}$C in the energy range of interest for the MiniBooNE experiment. We adopt the impulse approximation (IA) and use the nonrelativistic continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) to model the nuclear dynamics. An effective nucleon-nucleon interaction of the Skyrme type is used. We compare our results with the recent MiniBooNE antineutrino cross-section data and confront them with alternate calculations. The CRPA predictions reproduce the gross features of the shape of the measured double-differential cross sections. The CRPA cross sections are typically larger than those of other reported IA calculations but tend to underestimate the magnitude of the MiniBooNE data. We observe that an enhancement of the nucleon axial mass in CRPA calculations is an effective way of improving on the description of the shape and magnitude of the double-differential cross sections. The rescaling of $M_{A}$ is illustrated to affect the shape of the double-differential cross sections differently than multinucleon effects beyond the IA.

## Key claim

The CRPA calculations reproduce the gross shape of MiniBooNE's measured double-differential cross sections for charged-current quasielastic antineutrino scattering off carbon-12, but they tend to underestimate the data magnitude; an enhancement of the nucleon axial mass \(M_A\) can improve the description, and this rescaling affects the cross-section shape differently than multinucleon effects beyond the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors adopt the impulse approximation and model nuclear dynamics using a nonrelativistic continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) with Green's functions to solve the CRPA equations. They use an effective Skyrme-type nucleon-nucleon residual interaction (SkE2 parametrization) and obtain bound and continuum single-nucleon wave functions from a mean-field potential. The computed cross sections are folded with the MiniBooNE antineutrino flux and compared to the experimental data and to other theoretical calculations.

## Key numbers

- The CRPA cross sections with \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV (tuned to deuterium bubble chamber data) underestimate the magnitude of the MiniBooNE data.
- Sensitivity of the computed cross sections to the residual nucleon-nucleon force reaches almost 15% at higher muon kinetic energies \(T_{\mu}\) for \(E_{\bar{\nu}_{\mu}} = 700\) MeV.
- Sensitivity to the choice of bound-state single-nucleon wave functions (Woods-Saxon vs. Hartree-Fock) yields differences up to 20% at higher \(T_{\mu}\) for \(E_{\bar{\nu}_{\mu}} = 700\) MeV.
- Multipoles up to \(J = 12\) are included; contributions from \(J > 12\) are negligible for all considered kinematic conditions.
- The MiniBooNE antineutrino flux peaks near 700 MeV, and the neutrino flux peaks at higher energies than the antineutrino one.

## Context

This work extends earlier CRPA studies of photo-induced and electro-induced quasielastic processes (Jan1, Jan2) and inclusive neutrino scattering at supernova energies (neut, char) to antineutrino-nucleus interactions at intermediate energies. It confronts the CRPA predictions with MiniBooNE antineutrino data and compares them with alternate calculations by Martini et al. (2010) and Nieves et al. (2012), which use local Fermi gas models with RPA correlations and multinucleon effects. The paper highlights that the antineutrino cross sections are more sensitive to low-energy nuclear dynamics than neutrino cross sections, and that the differing shapes of multinucleon contributions versus an enhanced axial mass could help disentangle these effects in future analyses.
