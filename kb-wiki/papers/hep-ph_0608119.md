# Can One Distinguish Tau Neutrinos from Antineutrinos in Neutral-Current Pion Production Processes?

> A potential way to distinguish tau-neutrinos from antineutrinos, below the tau-production threshold, but above the pion production one, is presented. It is based on the different behavior of the neutral current pion production off the nucleon, depending on whether it is induced by neutrinos or antineutrinos. This procedure for distinguishing tau-neutrinos from antineutrinos neither relies on any nuclear model, nor it is affected by any nuclear effect (distortion of the outgoing nucleon waves, etc...). We show that neutrino-antineutrino asymmetries occur both in the totally integrated cross sections and in the pion azimuthal differential distributions. To define the asymmetries for the latter distributions we just rely on Lorentz-invariance. All these asymmetries are independent of the lepton family and can be experimentally measured by using electron or muon neutrinos, due to the lepton family universality of the neutral current neutrino interaction. Nevertheless and to estimate their size, we have also used the chiral model of hep-ph/0701149 at intermediate energies. Results are really significant since the differences between neutrino and antineutrino induced reactions are always large in all physical channels.

# Can One Distinguish Tau Neutrinos from Antineutrinos in Neutral-Current Pion Production Processes?

## Key claim

Neutral-current pion production off the nucleon can distinguish tau neutrinos from antineutrinos below the tau-production threshold (approximately 3.5 GeV in the lab frame) but above the pion production threshold (approximately 0.15 GeV), because neutrino-antineutrino asymmetries appear in both total cross sections and pion azimuthal differential distributions; however, the asymmetries are independent of lepton family and can be measured using electron or muon neutrinos, and the authors estimate their size using a chiral model valid up to 2 GeV neutrino/antineutrino energies.

## Method

The authors derived the unpolarized differential cross section for neutral-current pion production off the nucleon (ν_l + N → ν_l + N + π) in the lab frame, expressing the hadronic tensor in terms of 19 Lorentz scalar structure functions. They showed that parity-violating terms in the hadronic tensor produce sin φ_π and sin 2φ_π contributions in the azimuthal differential cross section, which vanish upon pion solid angle integration, and that the leptonic tensor for antineutrinos is the transpose of that for neutrinos. To estimate the size of the asymmetries, they used the chiral model of Hernandez, Nieves, and Valverde (2007), which includes Delta(1232) resonance excitation and chiral background terms within a SU(2) nonlinear sigma model, applying an invariant pion-nucleon mass cut of W ≤ 1.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Tau-production threshold: approximately 3.5 GeV in the lab frame for production off the nucleon
- Pion production threshold: approximately 0.15 GeV in the lab frame
- Fermi constant used: G = 1.1664 × 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}
- Weinberg angle used: sin^2 θ_W = 0.231
- Invariant pion-nucleon mass cut applied: W ≤ 1.4 GeV
- Differences between neutrino and antineutrino induced reactions are always large, about a factor of two, in all physical channels
- The ratio (N_abv - N_blw)/(N_abv + N_blw) for the azimuthal asymmetry is of order 10^{-2} or smaller

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Jachowicz and collaborators (2004) that helicity-related differences between neutrinos and antineutrinos induce asymmetries in the polarization of ejected nucleons in neutrino-nucleus scattering. The present method offers an alternative that does not rely on any nuclear model and is not affected by nuclear effects such as distortion of outgoing nucleon waves. The chiral model used for numerical estimates is the same as that developed by Hernandez, Nieves, and Valverde (2007) for weak pion production off the nucleon at intermediate energies, which gives a fair description of available charged-current and neutral-current data. The results enable experimental determination of tau neutrino versus antineutrino beam composition below the tau-production threshold, using either total cross section measurements (which provide better statistical significance) or pion azimuthal distributions.
