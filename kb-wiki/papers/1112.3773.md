# Two charged strangeonium-like structures observable in the $Y(2175) \to ϕ(1020)π^{+} π^{-}$ process

> Via the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, we study the $ϕ(1020)π^{+}$ invariant mass spectrum distribution of $Y(2175) \to ϕ(1020)π^{+} π^{-}$. Our calculation indicates there exist a sharp peak structure ($Z_{s1}^+$) close to the $K\bar{K}^\ast$ threshold and a broad structure ($Z_{s2}^+$) near the $K^\ast\bar{K}^\ast$ threshold. In addition, we also investigate the $ϕ(1680) \to ϕ(1020)π^{+} π^{-}$ process due to the ISPE mechanism, where a sharp peak around the $K\bar{K}^\ast$ threshold appears in the $ϕ(1020)π^{+}$ invariant mass spectrum distribution. We suggest to carry out the search for these charged strangeonium-like structures in future experiment, especially Belle II, Super-B and BESIII.

## Key claim

Via the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, the authors predict two charged strangeonium-like structures in the $\phi(1020)\pi^{+}$ invariant mass spectrum of $Y(2175) \to \phi(1020)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$: a sharp peak ($Z_{s1}^{+}$) near the $K\bar{K}^{\ast}$ threshold and a broad structure ($Z_{s2}^{+}$) near the $K^{\ast}\bar{K}^{\ast}$ threshold, with the caveat that the lineshapes depend on the specific Lorentz structure of the $Y K^{\ast}K^{\ast}\pi$ interaction Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors applied the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, previously used for bottomonium and charmonium dipion decays, to the hidden-strange dipion decay $Y(2175) \to \phi(1020)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$. They constructed effective Lagrangians from $SU(3)$ symmetry and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, including interactions for $K^{\ast}K\pi$, $\phi KK$, $\phi K^{\ast}K^{\ast}$, and $Y K^{(\ast)}K^{(\ast)}\pi$ vertices, and computed the loop amplitudes for intermediate $K^{\ast}\bar{K}+h.c.$ and $K^{\ast}\bar{K}^{\ast}$ states with a dipole form factor to regularize the loop integrals. They also studied the $\phi(1680) \to \phi(1020)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ process using the same mechanism with only the $K^{\ast}\bar{K}+h.c.$ intermediate state.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant $g$ was determined as the average of two values: $g=14.9$ from the $\phi(1020) \to K^{+}K^{-}$ width (total width 4.26 MeV, branching ratio 48.9%) and $g=12.5$ from the $K^{\ast} \to K\pi$ width (46.2 MeV), yielding an adopted value of $g=13.7$.
- The mixing angle between $\omega$ and $\phi$ was taken as $\sin\theta = -0.761$.
- The pion decay constant was set to $F_{\pi}=132$ MeV.
- The QCD scale was set to $\Lambda_{QCD}=220$ MeV, with the dipole form factor cutoff parameterized as $\Lambda = m_{E} + \beta \Lambda_{QCD}$, using $\beta=1$ for the numerical results.
- The $Y(2175)$ mass was reported as $M=2175\pm10\pm15$ MeV with width $\Gamma=58\pm16\pm20$ MeV from BaBar.

## Context

This work extends the ISPE mechanism, previously applied to explain the charged bottomonium-like structures $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ in $\Upsilon(10860)$ decays (Chen 2011) and to predict structures in hidden-charm dipion decays of higher charmonia (Chen 2011), to the strange sector via $Y(2175)$ decays. The predictions of $Z_{s1}^{+}$ and $Z_{s2}^{+}$ are accessible at Belle II, Super-B, and BESIII, and the authors also suggest searching for similar structures in $\phi(1680) \to \phi(1020)\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ and in $\omega(1420)$ and $\omega(1650)$ decays to test the universality of the ISPE mechanism.
