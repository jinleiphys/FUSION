# Projection of Good Quantum Numbers for Reaction Fragments

> In reactions the wave packets of the emerging products typically are not eigenstates of particle number operators or any other conserved quantities and their properties are entangled. I describe a particle projection technique in parts of space, which eschews the need to evaluate Pfaffians in the case of overlap of generalized Slater determinants or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov type of vacua. The extension of these formulas for calculating either angular momentum or particle projected energy distributions of the reaction fragments are presented as well. The generalization to simultaneous particle and angular momentum projection of various reaction fragment observables is straightforward.

## Key claim

The paper describes a particle number projection technique for reaction fragments that avoids the need to evaluate Pfaffians for overlaps of generalized Slater determinants or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov type vacua, with the caveat that the method applies specifically when fragments are well separated in space and the overlap between them is vanishingly small.

## Method

The author develops projection operators for particle number in a half-space (or arbitrary spatial partition) by introducing a gauge rotation of the quasiparticle wave functions, where only the v-components are rotated by a phase factor \(e^{2i\eta\Theta(z)}\) while the u-components remain unchanged. The overlap between the original and rotated wave functions is expressed as a determinant (or square root of a determinant) of a matrix involving the overlap of v-components restricted to the spatial region of interest, which can be diagonalized to yield a product form. The method is extended to angular momentum projection by replacing the spatial projection operator \(\Theta(z)\) with a rotation operator \(\hat{R}(\eta)\), and to simultaneous projection of particle number and angular momentum for reaction fragment observables.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalues \(\beta_l\) of the overlap matrix \(\mathcal{O}_{kl} = \langle \textrm{v}_l | \Theta(z) | \textrm{v}_k \rangle\) satisfy \(0 \le \beta_l \le 1\).
- The average particle number in the right half-space is given by \(N_R = \sum_{l=1}^{2\Omega} \beta_l\).
- For the BCS case with a uniform single-particle spectrum, the author presents a numerical example with chemical potential \(\mu = 34\) and pairing gap \(\Delta = 2.5\) (units not specified in the text).
- The integration interval for particle number projection in the generalized Slater determinant case is \([-\pi/2, \pi/2]\) for even particle numbers, and \([-\pi, \pi]\) when odd particle numbers are allowed.

## Context

This work extends the particle number projection technique for reaction fragments previously applied in the Hartree-Fock approximation by Simenel (2010) to the case of superfluid nuclei with pairing correlations treated via Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. The method builds on the canonical basis formalism of Bloch (1962), Balian (1969), and Ring (2004), and uses the Onishi-Yoshida formula (1966) for norm overlaps, with recent clarifications by Mizusaki (2018) regarding sign ambiguities. The technique enables calculation of particle number distributions, angular momentum distributions, and particle-projected energies for fission fragments and other reaction products within density functional theory approaches.
