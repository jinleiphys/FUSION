# Bulk properties of light deformed nuclei derived from a medium-modified meson-exchange interaction

> Deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for finite nuclei are carried out. As residual interaction, a Brueckner G-matrix derived from a meson-exchange potential is taken. Phenomenological medium modifications of the meson masses are introduced. The binding energies, radii, and deformation parameters of the Carbon, Oxygen, Neon, and Magnesium isotope chains are found to be in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim
A density-dependent modification of the sigma and omega meson masses in the BonnA one-boson exchange potential, with only three free parameters, is sufficient to simultaneously reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the bulk properties (binding energies, radii, deformation parameters) of light deformed nuclei, although the specific density dependence found is acknowledged to be rather model-dependent.

## Method
Deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed for finite nuclei using a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the BonnA meson-exchange potential. The meson masses were modified phenomenologically with a density dependence: the omega meson mass scales linearly with density, and the sigma meson mass scales with the cube root of density. The G-matrix was calculated for infinite nuclear matter at constant density, then transformed to a finite spherical harmonic oscillator basis (up to N=3 shells), and the HFB equations were solved self-consistently with respect to the Fermi momentum and starting energy.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per particle of nuclear matter: 14.6 MeV for BonnA* (medium-modified), compared to 16.3 MeV for the bare BonnA potential.
- Fermi momentum of nuclear matter: 1.29 fm^-1 for BonnA*, compared to 1.71 fm^-1 for BonnA.
- Compression modulus of nuclear matter: 240 MeV for BonnA*, compared to 200 MeV for BonnA.
- Deviation from experimental binding energies per nucleon: less than 0.6 MeV for all C, O, Ne, and Mg isotopes considered.
- For 26Mg, the prolate solution (beta2 = 0.339) has total energy -220.71 MeV, while the oblate solution (beta2 = -0.272) has total energy -219.63 MeV.
- The finite size of the proton was folded in using r_nucleon = 0.8 fm.
- Nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 was used to make the modification parameters dimensionless.
- Bare meson masses: m_omega^0 = 782.6 MeV, m_sigma1^0 = 550 MeV, m_sigma0^0 = 710 MeV.
- Modification parameters: f_omega = 0.036, f_sigma1 = 0.025, f_sigma0 = 0.070.

## Context
This work extends earlier deformed HFB calculations using G-matrix interactions (Goodman 1970s, Faessler 1970s) by introducing medium-modified meson masses following the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho 1991). It contrasts with relativistic mean field (RMF) models like NL-SH (Sharma 1993) which adjust coupling constants directly to data, and with Skyrme-type HFB calculations (Dobaczewski 1984, 1994). The results enable application to heavier mass isotope chains and justify further microscopic study of medium modifications of low-mass mesons.
