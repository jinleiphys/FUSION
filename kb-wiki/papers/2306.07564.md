# Causality Criteria from Stability Analysis at Ultra-High Boost

> In this work, we have exclusively employed the linear stability analysis at ultra-high boost on two well-known stable-causal theories - second-order MIS and first-order BDNK, to identify the region of parameter space over which they are frame-invariantly stable and obey causal signal propagation. It has been shown that at near-luminal boost, stability criteria alone can provide the causality constraints on transport coefficients, which are identical to the asymptotic causality conditions, without actually going to the asymptotic limit of the theories. Thus, we present an alternative approach to derive the causality constraints, which is more appropriate for low-energy effective theories like relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors show that for the MIS and BDNK theories, performing linear stability analysis at ultra-high boost (velocity approaching the speed of light) alone is sufficient to identify the region of parameter space where the theory is frame-invariantly stable and causal, without needing to analyze the asymptotic high-wavenumber limit. This provides an alternative approach to derive causality constraints that is more appropriate for low-energy effective theories like relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors linearize the conservation equations for small perturbations around hydrostatic equilibrium in a Lorentz-boosted frame, then perform a leading-order stability analysis at the spatially homogeneous limit (k → 0) for both the second-order MIS theory and the first-order BDNK theory in the conformal, chargeless limit. For each theory, they derive the stability criteria in the shear and sound channels as functions of boost velocity, then take the v → 1 limit to obtain the strictest bounds on transport coefficients. They compare these bounds with the asymptotic causality conditions obtained from a large-k analysis using Schur stability of the polynomial for asymptotic group velocity.

## Key numbers

- For MIS shear channel at v → 1: τ_π/η̃ > 1, which gives asymptotic group velocity (v_g²)⊥ = η̃/τ_π satisfying 0 < v_g² < 1
- For MIS sound channel at v → 1: τ_π/(2η̃) > 1, which gives (v_g²)∥ = 4η̃/(3τ_π) + 1/3 satisfying 0 < v_g² < 1
- For BDNK shear channel at v → 1: θ/η > 1, giving v_g² = η/θ satisfying 0 < v_g² < 1
- For BDNK sound channel at v → 1: the stability inequalities reduce to θ/η > 4/3 · v²/(1 - v²/3)² and E/η > 4/9 · v⁴/(1 - v²/3)², which at v → 1 give bounds that reproduce the asymptotic causality conditions

## Context

This work extends the argument from Gavassino (2021, 2023) that frame-invariant stability is possible only if the theory respects causality, and builds on the observation from Biswas (2022) that this identification appears in kinetic theory derivations of stable-causal first-order theories. The authors demonstrate that for MIS theory (Israel 1976, Israel 1979, Muller 1967) and BDNK theory (Bemfica 2017, Bemfica 2019, Kovtun 2019, Hoult 2020, Rocha 2022), stability analysis at ultra-high boost reproduces the asymptotic causality conditions of Denicol (2008), Brito (2020), and Mitra (2021) without requiring the large-k limit. This enables a more physically appropriate causality check for low-energy effective theories where the gradient expansion has zero radius of convergence (Heller 2013, Heller 2015). The authors also identify a region of parameter space (region IB in their Fig. 2) that is causal and stable but was missed in the large-k analysis of Hoult (2020).
