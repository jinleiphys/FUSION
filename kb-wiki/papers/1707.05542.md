# Probing short-range correlations in asymmetric nuclei with quasi-free pair knockout reactions

> Short-range correlations (SRC) in asymmetric nuclei with an unusual neutron-to-proton ratio can be studied with quasi-free two-nucleon knockout processes following the collision between accelerated ions and a proton target. We derive an approximate factorized cross section for those SRC-driven $p(A,p^{\prime} N_1 N_2)$ reactions. Our reaction model hinges on the factorization properties of SRC-driven $A(e, e^\prime N_1 N_2)$ reactions for which strong indications are found in theory-experiment comparisons. In order to put our model to the test we compare its predictions with results of $^{12}\text{C}(p,p^{\prime} pn)$ measurements conducted at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) and find a fair agreement. The model can also reproduce characteristic features of SRC-driven two-nucleon knockout reactions, like back-to-back emission of the correlated nucleons. We study the asymmetry dependence of nuclear SRC by providing predictions for the ratio of proton-proton to proton-neutron knockout cross sections for the carbon isotopes $^{9-15}$C thereby covering neutron excess values $(N-Z)/Z$ between -0.5 and +0.5.

# Probing short-range correlations in asymmetric nuclei with quasi-free pair knockout reactions

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) in asymmetric nuclei can be studied via quasi-free two-nucleon knockout reactions in inverse kinematics using a factorized cross-section model, but the model is approximate and plane-wave-based, ignoring initial- and final-state interactions (IFSI) which cause a reduction factor of order 0.05–0.1 in the cross sections.

## Method

The authors derive a factorized differential cross section for SRC-driven p(A,p' N1 N2) reactions by separating the transition matrix element into a nuclear-structure part (removal of a bound SRC NN pair from the projectile) and a nuclear-reaction part (proton scattering from an SRC NN pair). The model uses harmonic oscillator single-particle states, a correlation operator with central, tensor, and spin-isospin terms, and free nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections from the SAID code or parametrizations. Predictions are compared with 12C(p,p'pn) data from BNL's EVA spectrometer, and the ratio of pn to pp knockout cross sections is computed for carbon isotopes 9-15C.

## Key numbers

- The IFSI reduction factor for plane-wave-based cross sections with carbon beams is of order 0.05–0.1.
- For 12C, the predicted fraction f_pn of SRC pn pair removals (integrated between k_F and 3 k_F) is approximately 93%.
- The ratio of pn to pp SRC pair removal probabilities is computed for carbon isotopes covering neutron excess values (N-Z)/Z between -0.5 and +0.5, with f_pn changing by less than 10% over that range.
- The model uses laboratory momenta p_A = 2.5 A GeV and p_f = 1.5 GeV for the 10C(p,p'pn) cross section calculations, with nucleon initial momenta p_m = p_2 = 400 MeV in the projectile frame.

## Context

This work extends the factorized cross-section formalism previously developed for electro-induced A(e,e'N1N2) reactions (Colle 2015, Ryckebusch 1996) to proton-induced reactions in inverse kinematics, enabling SRC studies with radioactive beams. The model reproduces characteristic features of SRC-driven two-nucleon knockout such as back-to-back emission of correlated nucleons, consistent with findings from electro-induced reactions (Hen 2014, Subedi 2008). The results enable planned experiments at radioactive-beam facilities to probe the asymmetry dependence of SRC, which is important for understanding neutron star physics and the nuclear equation of state.
