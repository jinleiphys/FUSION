# How transverse thermal fluctuations disorder a condensate of chiral spirals into a quantum spin liquid

> For a scalar theory with a global $O(N)$ symmetry, when $N=2$ a spatially inhomogeneous condensate arises when the term in the Lagrangian with two spatial derivatives has a negative coefficient. If the condensate for such a chiral spiral includes only one mode, characterized by a momentum $k_0 \hat{z}$, then in perturbation theory at nonzero temperature the propagator for the static mode has a double pole when $\vec{k}^2 = k_0^2$. We conjecture that since chiral spirals spontaneously break both global and spacetime symmetries, that such double poles are a universal property of their static transverse modes. Fluctuations from double poles generate linear infrared divergences in any number of spatial dimensions and disorder the condensate of chiral spirals, analogous to a type of quantum spin liquid. The characteristic feature of this region is that over large spatial distances the two point function is the usual exponential times an oscillatory function. We prove this at large $N$ and suggest this occurs for all $N > 2$. Implications for four-fermion models and the phase diagram of QCD are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that for scalar field theories with global O(N) symmetry and N > 2, static transverse thermal fluctuations around a chiral spiral condensate produce double poles in the propagator at nonzero momentum, leading to linear infrared divergences that disorder the condensate into a quantum spin liquid phase; this is proven at large N and suggested to hold for all N > 2, but the analysis is limited to the single-mode ansatz for the condensate.

## Method

The authors consider a scalar field theory with O(N) symmetry in d+1 dimensions at nonzero temperature, using an effective Lagrangian with a negative coefficient Z for the two-spatial-derivative term and higher-derivative stabilizing terms. They analyze the single-mode chiral spiral ansatz where the field rotates in internal space along a fixed spatial direction, compute the static transverse propagator perturbatively, and then solve the theory at large N using constraint fields to integrate out the N-2 transverse components. The large-N solution is obtained by introducing constraint fields for both the magnitude and derivative-squared of the transverse fluctuations and solving the resulting saddle-point equations.

## Key numbers

- The inverse transverse propagator at nonzero temperature takes the form Δ_χ^{-1}(0, k) = (1/M^2)(k^2 - k_0^2)^2, where k_0 is the characteristic momentum of the chiral spiral condensate, with the mass term vanishing due to the equation of motion.
- The tadpole diagram involving transverse fluctuations is proportional to ∫ d^d k 1/((k^2 - k_0^2)^2/M^2) ~ (M^2/k_0^2) ∫_{k~k_0} dk/(k - k_0)^2, producing a linear infrared divergence in any number of spatial dimensions.
- The solution for k_0 from the single-mode ansatz is k_0^2 = (-Z - φ_0^2/M_1) M^2/2, where φ_0 is the condensate magnitude, Z is the negative coefficient of the two-derivative term, and M, M_1 are mass scales from higher-derivative couplings.

## Context

This work extends the study of spatially inhomogeneous condensates in O(N) scalar theories, which arise when the coefficient of the two-spatial-derivative term becomes negative (Pisarski, 2018). It contrasts with Kleinert (1981), who introduced a nonanalytic term generating double poles for all N ≥ 1, whereas the present authors find disorder only for N > 2, consistent with condensed matter systems exhibiting quasi-long-range order for N = 1 and 2 (Chaikin and Lubensky, 2010; Fradkin, 1991). The analysis enables future study of four-fermion models and the QCD phase diagram at nonzero density, and suggests that the quantum spin liquid region includes the entire parameter space where mean field theory predicts a chiral spiral condensate plus a larger region.
