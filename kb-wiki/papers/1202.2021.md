# Symmetry and degeneracy of the curved Coulomb potential on the S3 ball

> The "curved" Coulomb potential on the S3 ball, whose isometry group is SO(4), takes the form of a cotangent function, and when added to the four-dimensional squared angular momentum operator, one of the so(4) Casimir invariants, a Hamiltonian is obtained which describes a perturbance of the free geodesic motion that results peculiar in several aspects. The spectrum of such a motion has been studied on various occasions and is known to carry unexpectedly so(4) degeneracy patterns despite the non-commutativity of the perturbance with the Casimir operator. We here suggest an explanation for this behavior in designing a set of operators which close the so(4) algebra and whose Casimir invariant coincides with the Hamiltonian of the perturbed motion at the level of the eigenvalue problem. The above operators are related to the canonical geometric SO(4) generators on S3 by a non-unitary similarity transformation of the scaling type. In this fashion, we identify a complementary option to the deformed dynamical so(4) Higgs algebra constructed in terms of the components of the ordinary angular momentum and a related Runge-Lenz vector.

## Key claim

The paper explains the unexpected SO(4) degeneracy patterns in the spectrum of a particle on the S3 ball perturbed by a cotangent (curved Coulomb) potential, despite the non-commutativity of the perturbance with the Casimir operator. The explanation is achieved by constructing a set of operators that close the so(4) algebra through a non-unitary similarity transformation of the scaling type, rather than through the deformed dynamical Higgs algebra used previously.

## Method

The authors solved the eigenvalue problem for the Hamiltonian (K - 2b cot χ) on S3, where K is the squared four-dimensional angular momentum operator (a Casimir invariant of so(4)). They expanded the exact solutions of the perturbed motion in the basis of hyper-spherical harmonics describing free geodesic motion, using Romanovski polynomials and Gegenbauer polynomials. From these expansions, they constructed invertible matrices A_K(θ, φ) that connect the representation spaces of the perturbed and free motions, and showed that the cotangent-perturbed Hamiltonian equals a similarity-transformed Casimir operator shifted by a constant.

## Key numbers

- The energy spectrum of the perturbed motion is given by ε_K + 1 = (K+1)^2 - b^2/(K+1)^2, where b is the dimensionless strength of the cotangent potential and K is the quantum number determining the four-dimensional angular momentum.
- The free geodesic motion on S3 has eigenvalues K(K+2) for the Casimir operator K, with a (K+1)^2-fold degeneracy of states.
- The parameter α_K = 2b/(K+1) appears in the exponential damping factor e^{-α_K χ/2} applied to the hyper-spherical harmonics.
- For K=0: ψ^0_0(χ) = S^0_0(χ)
- For K=1: ψ^0_1(χ) = S^0_1(χ) + b S^1_1(χ); ψ^1_1(χ) = S^1_1(χ)
- For K=2: ψ^0_2(χ) = S^0_2(χ) + b S^1_2(χ) + (2b/3)^2 S^2_2(χ); ψ^1_2(χ) = S^1_2(χ) + (2/3)b S^2_2(χ); ψ^2_2(χ) = S^2_2(χ)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of degeneracy phenomena for cotangent potentials on spheres, previously studied by Higgs (1979) and Leemon (1979) who constructed deformed dynamical algebras using angular momentum and Runge-Lenz vector components. The paper provides a complementary approach to the Higgs algebra by constructing a closed so(4) algebra via a non-unitary similarity transformation, which works at the level of the eigenvalue problems. The method can be extended to hyperbolic spaces through complexification of the polar angle χ → iχ and b → -ib, connecting to the Coulomb problem on a hyperboloid and to Dirac's work on the electron wave equation in the De-Sitter universe (Dirac, 1935).
