# Particle Ratios From Strongly Interacting Hadronic Matter

> We calculate the particle ratios for a strongly interacting hadronic matter matter using non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) in relativistic mean-field approximation. A comparison with Hadron Resonance Gas Model is made.

## Key claim
The paper claims that interactions among hadrons, modeled via the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) in relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation, modify the \(K^+/\pi^+\) and \(\Lambda/\pi^-\) particle ratios, but only at high baryon chemical potential (\(\mu_B = 450\) MeV) and especially in the critical region of a first-order quark-hadron phase transition; the \(K^-/\pi^-\) ratio is found to be insensitive to these interactions under all conditions studied.

## Method
The authors calculate particle ratios for a strongly interacting hadronic matter composed of the full baryon octet (p, n, \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\), \(\Lambda\)) and bosons (pions and kaons) using the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation. Baryons and bosons are both described via meson-exchange Lagrangians, with interactions mediated by \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma^*\), and \(\phi\) mesons. The thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain field equations, and the particle ratios are evaluated as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential, with strangeness conservation enforced. Results are compared to the non-interacting Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model.

## Key numbers
- For \(\mu_B = 0\) MeV, the effect of interactions on the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio is negligible up to a temperature of about \(T = 150\) MeV.
- For \(\mu_B = 450\) MeV, interactions increase the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio, with a significant rise only in the critical region (CR) of the first-order quark-hadron phase transition, calculated with a bag constant in the range \(B^{1/4} = 165-200\) MeV.
- For \(\mu_B = 450\) MeV, interactions also increase the \(\Lambda/\pi^-\) ratio, with a significant rise in the same critical region (CR) with \(B^{1/4} = 165-200\) MeV.
- The \(K^-/\pi^-\) ratio is found to be insensitive to interactions even at \(\mu_B = 450\) MeV, remaining the same as in the non-interacting HRG model.
- The hyperon potential depths used to fix scalar couplings are: \(U_{\Lambda}^{(N)} = -28\) MeV, \(U_{\Sigma}^{(N)} = +30\) MeV, and \(U_{\Xi}^{(N)} = -18\) MeV.
- The hyperon-hyperon interaction uses a square well potential with depth \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} = -20\) MeV.
- The strange quark mass in the QGP phase is set to \(m_s = 150\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of particle ratios in heavy-ion collisions, motivated by experimental observations from the NA49 collaboration which showed a sudden rise in \(K^+/\pi^+\), \(K^-/\pi^-\), and \(\Lambda/\pi^-\) ratios for a specific range of center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\). The authors use the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) to model the hadronic phase, contrasting with the simpler Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, and they incorporate both baryons and bosons via a consistent meson-exchange framework, addressing an inconsistency noted in earlier work by Ellis (1995) where kaons were treated with chiral perturbation theory while baryons used the Walecka model. The results enable a more detailed analysis of the sensitivity of these ratios to the critical region of the quark-hadron phase transition, particularly at high baryon chemical potential.
