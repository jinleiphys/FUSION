# The nuclear symmetry energy from neutron skins and pure neutron matter in a Bayesian framework

> We present an inference of the nuclear symmetry energy magnitude $J$, the slope $L$ and the curvature $K_{\rm sym}$ by combining neutron skin data on Ca, Pb and Sn isotopes and our best theoretical information about pure neutron matter (PNM). A Bayesian framework is used to consistently incorporate prior knowledge of the PNM equation of state from chiral effective field theory calculations. Neutron skins are modeled in a Hartree-Fock approach using an extended Skyrme energy-density functional which allows for independent variation of $J$, $L$ and $K_{\rm sym}$ without affecting the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state. We discuss the choice of neutron skin data sets, and combining errors in quadrature we obtain 95\% credible values of $J=31.3\substack{+4.2 \\ -5.9}$ MeV, $L=40\substack{+34 \\ -26}$ MeV and $K_τ = L - 6K_{\rm sym}= -444\substack{+100 \\ -84}$ MeV using uninformative priors in $J$, $L$ and $K_{\rm sym}$, and $J=31.9\substack{+1.3 \\ -1.3}$ MeV, $L=37\substack{+9 \\ -8}$ MeV and $K_τ = -480\substack{+25 \\ -26}$ MeV using PNM priors. The correlations between symmetry energy parameters induced by neutron skin data is discussed and compared with the droplet model. Neutron skin data alone is shown to place limits on the symmetry energy parameters as stringent as those obtained from chiral effective field theory alone, and when combined the 95\% credible intervals are reduced by a factor of 4-5. Ahead of new measurements of lead and calcium neutron skins from parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab and Mainz Superconducting Accelerator, we make predictions based on existing data on neutron skins of tin for the neutron skins of calcium and lead of 0.166$\pm$0.008 fm and $0.169 \pm 0.014$ fm respectively, using uninformative priors, and 0.167$\pm$0.008 fm and $0.172 \pm 0.015$ fm respectively, using PNM priors.

## Key claim

The authors infer the nuclear symmetry energy parameters (magnitude J, slope L, and curvature K_sym) by combining neutron skin data from calcium, lead, and tin isotopes with pure neutron matter (PNM) constraints from chiral effective field theory within a Bayesian framework, finding that combining these two independent sources reduces 95% credible intervals by a factor of 4-5 compared to either source alone.

## Method

The authors model neutron skins using a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with an extended Skyrme energy-density functional that allows independent variation of J, L, and K_sym without affecting symmetric nuclear matter properties. They perform Bayesian inference using Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo simulations, incorporating two types of priors: uninformative uniform priors over conservative ranges (J=24-36 MeV, L=-10 to 130 MeV, K_sym=-440 to 120 MeV) and PNM priors derived from chiral effective field theory calculations parameterized through Fermi liquid parameters. Neutron skin data from multiple experimental probes (exotic atoms, scattering, collective motion, giant resonances, coherent pion photoproduction, and parity-violating electron scattering) are combined either as full ranges or by adding errors in quadrature.

## Key numbers

- Using uninformative priors with errors combined in quadrature: J = 31.3 (+4.2 / -5.9) MeV, L = 40 (+34 / -26) MeV, K_tau = L - 6K_sym = -444 (+100 / -84) MeV at 95% credible intervals
- Using PNM priors with errors combined in quadrature: J = 31.9 (+1.3 / -1.3) MeV, L = 37 (+9 / -8) MeV, K_tau = -480 (+25 / -26) MeV at 95% credible intervals
- Predicted neutron skin for Ca-48 using uninformative priors: 0.166 +/- 0.008 fm
- Predicted neutron skin for Pb-208 using uninformative priors: 0.169 +/- 0.014 fm
- Predicted neutron skin for Ca-48 using PNM priors: 0.167 +/- 0.008 fm
- Predicted neutron skin for Pb-208 using PNM priors: 0.172 +/- 0.015 fm
- Combining neutron skin data with PNM priors reduces 95% credible intervals by a factor of 4-5 compared to either source alone

## Context

This work extends the meta-modeling approach of Chen (2010) by using a Bayesian framework that consistently incorporates PNM knowledge from chiral effective field theory calculations, and by using an extended Skyrme model that allows independent variation of K_sym alongside J and L. The results enable predictions for upcoming parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab (PREX-II and CREX) and Mainz Superconducting Accelerator (MREX), which should obtain total error ranges of Delta L approximately 50 MeV and Delta K_tau approximately 240 MeV for uninformative priors, and Delta L approximately 30 MeV and Delta K_tau approximately 100 MeV for PNM priors at 67% credible bounds.
