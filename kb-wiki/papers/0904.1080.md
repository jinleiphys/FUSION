# Anomalous delta-type electric and magnetic two-nucleon interactions

## Key claim
Anomalous delta-type electric and magnetic interactions between the overlapping peripheral structures of nucleons may explain why the spin-triplet deuteron state is bound while all other nucleon-nucleon states (n-p singlet, n-n, p-p) are unbound, assuming the strong nuclear interaction is equal for all four states.

## Method
The author introduces two phenomenological delta-type interaction energies, one electric (E_e) and one magnetic (E_m), acting between the peripheral charge and magnetic moment distributions of overlapping nucleons. Using the known binding energy of the deuteron (−2.22 MeV) and the scattering energies of the unbound singlet states (+0.07 MeV for n-p singlet, +0.15 MeV for n-n and p-p singlets), a system of three linear equations is solved to extract E_e, E_m, and the nuclear energy E_N.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: −2.22 MeV for the n-p triplet state
- Singlet n-p state energy: +0.07 MeV (unbound)
- Singlet n-n and p-p state energy: +0.15 MeV (unbound, after removing point-like Coulomb repulsion for p-p)
- Extracted electric delta-interaction energy: E_e = 1.19 MeV
- Extracted magnetic delta-interaction energy: E_m = 1.15 MeV
- Extracted nuclear energy (strong force minus kinetic): E_N = 0.11 MeV (slightly positive, meaning all states are unbound by nuclear force alone)
- Estimated pion contribution to nucleon magnetic moments: +1 μ_N for proton and −1 μ_N for neutron, arising in the peripheral cloud
- Proton magnetic moment: +2.79 μ_N; neutron magnetic moment: −1.91 μ_N
- Distance between nucleon cores in deuteron: 2 fm
- Approximate deuteron diameter: 4 fm
- Negative charge in neutron extends from 1 fm to 2–3 fm

## Context
This work proposes an alternative to the standard spin-dependent nuclear potential (which assigns a greater triplet potential than singlet potential) to explain the bound deuteron and the absence of other nucleon-nucleon bound states. The magnetic delta-interaction is compared to the pairing interaction in nuclei (Author, key-1), which couples like nucleons to spin zero, and its extracted strength of 1.15 MeV is noted to be comparable to the ~1 MeV pairing interaction. The peripheral distribution of anomalous magnetic moments is referenced to estimates of pion contributions (Author, key-3; Author, key-4). The spatial charge distributions of nucleons are cited from prior work (Author, key-2; Author, key-3; Author, key-7). The analysis enables a unified electromagnetic explanation for the pattern of two-nucleon bound states without requiring different strong interaction strengths for triplet and singlet channels.
