# Crossing symmetric potential model of pion-nucleon scattering

> A crossing symmetric $πN$ scattering amplitude is constructed through a complete attachment of two external pions to the dressed nucleon propagator of an underlying $πN$ potential model. Our formulation automatically provides expressions also for the crossing symmetric and gauge invariant pion photoproduction and Compton scattering amplitudes. We show that our amplitudes are unitary if they coincide on-shell with the amplitudes obtained by attaching one pion to the dressed $πNN$ vertex of the same potential model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a crossing symmetric pion-nucleon scattering amplitude by attaching two external pions to the dressed nucleon propagator of an underlying potential model, but this approach preserves crossing symmetry and gauge invariance at the expense of unitarity; the amplitudes satisfy exact unitarity only if they coincide on-shell with amplitudes obtained by attaching one pion to the dressed pi-N-N vertex of the same potential model.

## Method

The authors apply a "gauging" technique (attachment of external pions or photons) to the dressed nucleon propagator g of a standard pi-N potential model, first obtaining single-gauged amplitudes (hybrid amplitudes) and then double-gauged amplitudes for pi-N scattering. The double-gauged amplitude T^{mu nu} is expressed in pole-plus-non-pole form as T^{mu nu} = Gamma^mu g Gamma^nu + T_b^{mu nu}, where the non-pole part satisfies a Bethe-Salpeter-like equation. The same formalism yields crossing symmetric and gauge invariant amplitudes for pion photoproduction and Compton scattering when one or both gaugings involve photons.

## Key numbers

* The paper presents no explicit numerical results; it is a formal development of the theoretical framework.

## Context

This work extends the standard potential model description of pi-N scattering (Morioka 1981, Gross 1992, Pascalutsa 2000) which preserves unitarity but lacks crossing symmetry, and the standard photoproduction description (Nozawa 1989, Surya 1995, Pascalutsa 2004) which lacks both crossing symmetry and manifest gauge invariance. The approach uses the gauging of equations method previously developed by the authors (Kvinikhidze 1999) for attaching photons to strongly interacting systems. The formalism enables future model calculations where parameters are adjusted so that single- and double-gauged amplitudes coincide on-shell, thereby achieving exact two-body unitarity while preserving crossing symmetry.
