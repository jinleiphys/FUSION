# Why is the J/$ψ$ suppression enhanced at large transverse energy ?

> We study the ratio of $J/ψ$ over minimum bias in $Pb$ $Pb$ collisions at SPS energy. The NA50 data exhibit a sharp turn-over at $E_T \sim 100$ GeV (close to the knee of the $E_T$ distribution) followed by a steady, steep decrease at larger $E_T$. We show that this behaviour can be explained by the combined effects of a small decrease of the hadronic $E_T$ in the $J/ψ$ event sample (due to the $E_T$ taken by the $J/ψ$ trigger), together with the sharp decrease of the $E_T$ distributions in this $E_T$ region (tail). This phenomenon does not affect the (true) ratio $J/ψ$ over $DY$ (obtained by the NA50 standard analysis), but does affect the one obtained by the so-called minimum bias analysis. A good agreement is obtained with the data coming from both analysis -- as well as with the ratios of $J/ψ$ and $DY$ over minimum bias -- in the whole $E_{T}$ region.

# Why is the J/ψ suppression enhanced at large transverse energy?

## Key claim
The sharp turn-over and steep decrease of the J/ψ over minimum bias ratio at ET ~ 100 GeV in Pb Pb collisions at SPS energy can be explained by a small hadronic ET loss (about 0.5% of total ET) in the J/ψ event sample due to the ET taken by the J/ψ trigger, combined with the steep tail of the ET distribution, without requiring a phase transition. This effect does not affect the true J/ψ over Drell-Yan ratio from the standard analysis but does affect the ratio obtained via the minimum bias analysis.

## Method
The authors compute the ET distributions of minimum bias, Drell-Yan, and J/ψ event samples using the Dual Parton Model (DPM) for comover densities, with nuclear absorption and comover interaction survival probabilities. They introduce fluctuations in the comover density at large ET by scaling the density by the factor F(b) = ET/ET(b), and implement a hadronic ET loss in the J/ψ event sample by removing the contribution of one participant pair (or one binary collision) from the hadronic multiplicity in the rapidity region of the calorimeter and dimuon trigger. The ET-b correlation is parametrized as a Gaussian, and the ET distributions are obtained by folding the impact-parameter distributions with this correlation.

## Key numbers
- The turn-over in the J/ψ over minimum bias ratio occurs at ET ~ 100 GeV, close to the knee of the ET distribution.
- The hadronic ET loss in the J/ψ event sample at b ~ 0 (where nA ~ 200) is about 0.5% of total ET for the maximal ansatz, corresponding to ET ~ 5 GeV; the minimal ansatz gives about 0.25% or ET ~ 2.5 GeV.
- The ratio of minimum bias to J/ψ probabilities at ET = 130 GeV and b = 0 is approximately 1.3, calculated from the Gaussian ET-b correlation with parameters q = 0.62 GeV and a = 0.60 (for 1998 data).
- The charged multiplicity per participant pair in the rapidity region -1.8 < y* < 1.8 is 6.6, used to estimate the ET loss.
- Nuclear absorption cross-section σabs = 4.5 mb and comover interaction cross-section σco = 1 mb are used in the calculations.

## Context
This work extends the comover interaction model of Capella, Ferreiro, and Kaidalov (2000) and Capella and Sousa (2001) for J/ψ suppression in Pb Pb collisions. It addresses the discrepancy between the NA50 data at large ET and models based on either deconfinement or comovers interaction, which give a practically constant J/ψ over Drell-Yan ratio beyond the knee. The paper shows that the minimum bias analysis, introduced by Capella et al. (1990), is sensitive to small ET differences between event samples at large ET, and that the true J/ψ over Drell-Yan ratio from the standard analysis remains unaffected. The results enable a consistent description of NA50 data from both analyses without invoking a phase transition, and predict a similar turn-over in the Drell-Yan over minimum bias ratio that could be tested with future measurements.
