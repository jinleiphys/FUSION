# Factorization of the soft gluon divergence from the dipole picture deep inelastic scattering cross sections at next-to-leading order

> We use a factorization scheme analogous to one proposed for single inclusive forward hadron production to factorize the soft gluon divergence present in the deep inelastic scattering cross sections in the dipole picture at next-to-leading order (NLO). We show numerically that in this carefully constructed scheme it is possible to obtain meaningful results for the DIS cross sections at NLO, and so we are able to quantitatively study the recently derived NLO corrections to the DIS cross sections. We find that the NLO corrections can be significant and sensitive to the details of the factorization scheme used for the resummation of the large logarithms into the BK evolution equation. In the case of an approximative factorization scheme we observe a problematic behavior of the DIS cross sections similar to what has been seen with analogously factorized single inclusive cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a carefully constructed factorization scheme, analogous to one proposed for single inclusive forward hadron production, can successfully factorize the soft gluon divergence in NLO dipole picture DIS cross sections, but the NLO corrections are sensitive to the details of the factorization scheme and an approximative scheme leads to unphysical negative cross sections at high \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors computed the NLO DIS structure functions \(F_L\) and \(F_T\) in the dipole picture using the unsubtracted form of the NLO cross section from Beuf (2017), which separates into an initial condition term, a quark-gluon contribution \(\sigma^{qg}\), and a dipole contribution \(\sigma^{\text{dip}}\). They constructed a subtraction scheme where the large logarithmic divergence in the \(z_2\) integral of the quark-gluon contribution is resummed into the BK evolution equation by setting the target momentum fraction \(X(z_2) = x_{Bj}/z_2\) with a lower cutoff \(z_2 > x_{Bj}/x_0\), and compared this to an approximative "\(x_{Bj}\)-subtracted" scheme where \(X(z_2) = x_{Bj}\) and the lower limit is taken to zero. The LO BK equation with an MV initial condition was used for target evolution, with both fixed coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.2\) and parent dipole running coupling.

## Key numbers

* At \(x_{Bj} = 10^{-3}\) with fixed \(\alpha_s = 0.2\), the NLO corrections in the working subtraction scheme are moderate, of order a few tens of percent in the \(Q^2\) range of interest.
* In the approximative \(x_{Bj}\)-subtraction scheme at \(x_{Bj} = 10^{-3}\) with fixed \(\alpha_s = 0.2\), the full NLO structure functions become negative at \(Q^2 \gtrsim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
* With running coupling, the sign of the NLO correction to \(F_T\) changes compared to the fixed coupling case, demonstrating sensitivity to the coupling choice.

## Context

This work extends the NLO dipole picture formalism for DIS structure functions, building on the NLO impact factors calculated by Beuf (2016, 2017) and verified in the four-dimensional helicity scheme by Hanninen (2017). The factorization scheme follows the principle proposed by Iancu (2016) for single inclusive particle production, which was shown to be effective by Ducloue (2017) for that process. The paper demonstrates that the same approximative "CXY" scheme that led to problematic results for single inclusive production at high transverse momenta also fails for DIS at high \(Q^2\), and identifies a transient effect near the initial condition that will require careful treatment for fits to HERA data.
