# $Œ©_c$ excited states within a ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS model

> We have reviewed the renormalization procedure used in the unitarized coupled-channel model of Phys. Rev. D85 114032 (2012), and its impact in the $C=1$, $S=-2$, and $I=0$ sector, where five $Œ©_c^{(*)}$ states have been recently observed by the LHCb Collaboration. The meson-baryon interactions used in the model are consistent with both chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries and lead to a successful description of the observed lowest-lying odd parity resonances $Œõ_c(2595)$ and $Œõ_c(2625)$, and $Œõ_b(5912)$ and $Œõ_b(5920)$ resonances. We show that some (probably at least three) of the states observed by LHCb will also have odd parity and spins $J=1/2$ and $J=3/2$, belonging two of them to the same ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm light-spin-flavor}\times$HQSS multiplets that the latter strangenessless heavy$-Œõ$ baryons.

## Key claim

Some (probably at least three) of the five $\Omega_c^{(*)}$ states observed by LHCb will have odd parity and spins $J=1/2$ or $J=3/2$, with two of them belonging to the same ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm light-spin-flavor}\times$HQSS multiplets as the $\Lambda_c(2595)$, $\Lambda_c(2625)$, $\Lambda_b(5912)$, and $\Lambda_b(5920)$ resonances, provided the renormalization scheme is adjusted from the original choice used in Romanets (2012).

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation using an ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS-extended Weinberg-Tomozawa meson-baryon interaction as the kernel, within the on-shell approximation. The loop functions are renormalized using two different schemes: a one-subtraction scheme at scale $\mu = \sqrt{\alpha (m_{th}^2+M_{th}^2)}$ with adjustable parameter $\alpha$, and a sharp-cutoff regularization with common UV cutoff $\Lambda$ for all channels. The pole positions of the $T$-matrix are examined on the first and second Riemann sheets to identify bound states and resonances in the $C=1$, $S=-2$, $I=0$ sector.

## Key numbers

- Five $\Omega_c^{(*)}$ states predicted in Romanets (2012) using $\alpha=1$: masses at 2810.9 MeV (J=1/2), 2814.3 MeV (J=3/2), 2884.5 MeV (J=1/2), 2941.6 MeV (J=1/2), and 2980.0 MeV (J=3/2), all with zero width
- Using $\alpha=1.16$, states shift upward: pole **d** at 2999.9 MeV (J=1/2, width 0.06 MeV) and pole **e** at 3036.3 MeV (J=3/2, width 0 MeV), near the experimental $\Omega_c(3000)$ and $\Omega_c(3050)$
- Using a common cutoff $\Lambda=1090$ MeV: pole **c** at 2994.26 MeV (J=1/2, width 1.85 MeV) identified with $\Omega_c(3000)$; pole **b** at 3048.7 MeV (J=3/2, width 0.0 MeV) identified with $\Omega_c(3050)$; pole **d** at 3116.81 MeV (J=1/2, width 3.72 MeV) identified with $\Omega_c(3119)$ or $\Omega_c(3090)$
- For $\Lambda=1200$ MeV, two $J=3/2$ states appear at 3000 MeV and 3090 MeV, while a $J=1/2$ state appears at 3050 MeV

## Context

This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel model of Romanets (2012) and Garcia-Recio (2008), which successfully described the odd-parity $\Lambda_c(2595)$, $\Lambda_c(2625)$, $\Lambda_b(5912)$, and $\Lambda_b(5920)$ resonances within the same ${\rm SU(6)}_{\rm lsf}\times$HQSS framework. The original predictions placed five $\Omega_c^{(*)}$ states below 2.98 GeV, making identification with the LHCb states (all above 3 GeV) impossible. By adjusting the renormalization scheme, the authors show that at least three states can be identified with the observed $\Omega_c(3000)$, $\Omega_c(3050)$, and $\Omega_c(3119)$ or $\Omega_c(3090)$. The results differ from those of Montana (2017) and Debastiani (2017), who used different regularization schemes and interaction matrices, leading to different mass assignments for the molecular states. The work enables future experimental determination of the spins and parities of the LHCb states to discriminate between competing theoretical interpretations.
