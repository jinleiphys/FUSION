# Correlating $h\toμ^+μ^-$ to the Anomalous Magnetic Moment of the Muon via Leptoquarks

> Recently, both ATLAS and CMS measured the decay $h\toμ^+μ^-$, finding a signal strength with respect to the Standard Model expectation of $1.2\pm0.6$ and $1.19\substack{+0.41+0.17\\-0.39 -0.16}$, respectively. This provides, for the first time, evidence that the Standard Model Higgs couples to second generation fermions. This measurement is particularly interesting in the context of the intriguing hints for lepton flavor universality violation, accumulated within recent years, as new physics explanations could also be tested in the $h\toμ^{+}μ^{-}$ decay mode. Leptoquarks are prime candidates to account for the flavor anomalies. In particular, they can provide the necessary chiral enhancement (by a factor $m_t/m_μ$) to address $a_μ$ with TeV scale new physics. In this letter we point out that such explanations of $a_μ$ also lead to enhanced effects in $h\toμ^+μ^-$ and we examine the correlations between $h\toμ^+μ^-$ and $a_μ$ within leptoquark models. We find that the effect in the branching ratio of $h\toμ^+μ^-$ ranges from several percent up to a factor three, if one aims at accounting for $a_μ$ at the $2\,σ$ level. Hence, the new ATLAS and CMS measurements already provide important constraints on the parameter space, rule out specific $a_μ$ explanations and will be very important to test the flavor anomalies in the future.

# Correlating $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ to the Anomalous Magnetic Moment of the Muon via Leptoquarks

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that leptoquark (LQ) models which explain the muon anomalous magnetic moment ($a_\mu$) via a chiral enhancement factor of $m_t/m_\mu \approx 1700$ also produce enhanced effects in $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$, with branching ratio modifications ranging from several percent up to a factor of three, but only if one aims to account for $a_\mu$ at the $2\sigma$ level; the current ATLAS and CMS measurements already disfavor specific explanations, particularly the scenario involving $S_1$ mixing with $S_3$.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop contributions to both $a_\mu$ and $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ from three scalar leptoquark representations ($S_1$, $S_2$, and $S_1+S_3$ mixing) that can generate $m_t/m_\mu$ enhanced effects, using the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann formalism for self-energies and expanding in $m_h^2/m_{1,2,3}^2$ and $v^2/m_{1,2,3}^2$. They then incorporate SMEFT renormalization group evolution effects from the LQ scale to the electroweak scale, which modifies the leading-order predictions by approximately 50% for $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ and 20-30% for $a_\mu$.

## Key numbers

* The experimental deviation in the muon anomalous magnetic moment: $\delta a_\mu = a_\mu^{\rm exp} - a_\mu^{\rm SM} = (251 \pm 59) \times 10^{-11}$, corresponding to a $4.2\sigma$ deviation.
* ATLAS measured $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ signal strength relative to Standard Model: $1.2 \pm 0.6$; CMS measured $1.19^{+0.41+0.17}_{-0.39 -0.16}$.
* The chiral enhancement factor from leptoquarks is $m_t/m_\mu \approx 1700$, enabling TeV-scale new physics explanations.
* For scenario 3 ($S_1+S_3$ mixing), the effect in $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ is large enough that explaining the central value of $a_\mu$ is already disfavored by ATLAS and CMS measurements, with LQ masses fixed at 1.5 TeV and varied between 1.5 and 3 TeV.
* For scenarios 1 and 2 with LQ masses of 1.5 TeV, the predicted effects in $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ are at the several percent level, not yet constrained by current data.

## Context

This work extends the study of correlations between $a_\mu$ and $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$ previously considered in effective field theory by Feruglio (2018) and in the context of vector-like leptons by Kannike (2011), Dermisek (2013, 2014), and Crivellin (2018). The paper specifically focuses on leptoquarks as they are prime candidates for explaining the flavor anomalies observed in $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ and $b\to c\tau\nu$ transitions, which show up to approximately $6\sigma$ and $3\sigma$ deviations from the Standard Model respectively. The results enable future colliders (HL-LHC, ILC, FCC-ee, FCC-hh) to test these leptoquark explanations through precision measurements of $h\to\mu^+\mu^-$, with the $S_1$ and $S_2$ scenarios producing opposite signs of interference that can be distinguished experimentally.
