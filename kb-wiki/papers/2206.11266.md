# Equation of state for hot hyperonic neutron star matter

> The FSU2H equation-of-state model, originally developed to describe cold neutron star matter with hyperonic cores, is extended to finite temperature. Results are presented for a wide range of temperatures and lepton fractions, which cover the conditions met in protoneutron star matter, neutron star mergers and supernova explosions. It is found that the temperature effects on the thermodynamical observables and the composition of the neutron star core are stronger when the hyperonic degrees of freedom are considered. An evaluation of the temperature and density dependence of the thermal index leads to the observation that the so-called $Γ$ law, widely used in neutron star merger simulations, is not appropriate to reproduce the true thermal effects, specially when hyperons start to be abundant in the neutron star core. To make finite temperature equations of state easily accessible, simple parameterizations of the thermal index for nucleonic and hyperonic $β$-stable neutrino-free matter are provided.

## Key claim

The FSU2H equation-of-state model, originally developed for cold neutron star matter with hyperonic cores, is extended to finite temperature, and the authors find that the so-called Gamma law, widely used in neutron star merger simulations, is not appropriate to reproduce the true thermal effects, especially when hyperons start to be abundant in the neutron star core.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic mean-field FSU2H model to finite temperature by incorporating the sigma* meson field and adjusting the coupling of the sigma field to the Xi baryon to reproduce a deeper Xi nuclear potential of -24 MeV. They solved the coupled equations for baryon, lepton, and meson fields under beta-equilibrium conditions with charge neutrality and fixed lepton fractions, computing the composition, energy density, pressure, entropy density, and thermal index for temperatures from 5 to 50 MeV and lepton fractions of 0.2 and 0.4, as well as for neutrino-free matter.

## Key numbers

- The Xi nuclear potential was set to U_Xi = -24 MeV, as recently determined by Friedman (2021).
- The onset density for hyperon appearance at low temperature is around 2 rho_0, where rho_0 is approximately 0.16 fm^-3.
- Calculations were performed for temperatures T = 5 MeV and T = 50 MeV, and lepton fractions Y_l = 0.2 and Y_l = 0.4.
- The baryon density range covered is rho_B = rho_0/2 to 8 rho_0, and temperature range is T = 0 to 100 MeV.
- At the highest density considered, rho_B = 1.0 fm^-3, partial densities of baryons from the same isospin family tend to converge to an isospin degeneracy point.

## Context

This work extends the FSU2H model of Tolos (2016, 2017) to finite temperature, improving upon the cold EoS by incorporating the sigma* field for better description of the Lambda-Lambda bond energy from Takahashi (2001) and Ahn (2013). It addresses the scarcity of finite-temperature EoS models with exotic degrees of freedom, which was previously limited to models such as those by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen (1998) that only considered nucleons. The results enable more realistic simulations of protoneutron star evolution, neutron star mergers, and supernova explosions by providing parameterizations of the thermal index for nucleonic and hyperonic beta-stable neutrino-free matter.
