# Applicability of the Wong formula for fusion cross sections from light to heavy systems

> We discuss the applicability of the Wong formula for fusion cross sections in a single-channel problem. To this end, we carry out a systematic study and compare the approximate fusion cross sections with the exact results in a wide mass region of reaction systems. We show that the deviation of the approximate results from the exact cross sections is large for light systems, even though the Wong formula provides a reasonable approximation for heavy systems. We also discuss the energy dependence of the deviation, and show that for a given projectile nucleus the critical energy, at which the deviation exceeds 5\% of the exact cross sections, increases as a function of the mass number of the target nucleus.

## Key claim

The Wong formula provides a reasonable approximation for fusion cross sections in heavy systems, but its deviation from exact results is large for light systems; the critical energy at which the deviation exceeds 5% increases with the mass number of the target nucleus for a given projectile.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrödinger equation with the incoming wave boundary condition to obtain exact fusion cross sections for a single-channel potential model, using the Akyüz-Winther potential for the nuclear part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction. They compared these exact results to the Wong formula, which approximates the Coulomb barrier as an inverted parabola with s-wave barrier parameters, for a systematic set of projectile-target combinations ranging from light to heavy systems. The deviation was quantified by an integrated measure over energies from 0.9 to 1.1 times the barrier height, and by an energy-dependent measure at each incident energy.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O system, the critical energy (where deviation exceeds 5%) is 3.02 MeV above the barrier.
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{238}$U system, the critical energy is 30.37 MeV above the barrier.
- The deviation $\Delta\sigma$ for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{20}$Ne projectiles saturates at around target mass number $A \sim 50$, corresponding to $Z \sim 20$.
- For $^4$He projectile, the deviation remains relatively large even for heavy targets, unlike for heavier projectiles.
- The integration range for the deviation measure $\Delta\sigma$ was set from $E_{\rm min} = 0.9 V_B$ to $E_{\rm max} = 1.1 V_B$.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the Wong formula's limitations beyond the earlier generalization by Ref. [RH15], which introduced energy-dependent barrier parameters to correct the overestimation in light systems. The study systematically quantifies for which systems and at which energies the original Wong formula (Wong 1973) becomes inadequate, showing that for projectiles heavier than $^4$He, the formula works well for target masses $A \sim 50$ or larger. The results enable practitioners to determine when the simpler Wong formula suffices and when the generalized version from Ref. [RH15] or exact numerical solutions are required, particularly for light systems and for $^4$He-induced reactions.
