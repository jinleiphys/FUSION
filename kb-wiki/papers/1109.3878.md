# Modeling global event properties using hydrodynamics from RHIC to LHC

> The relativistic hydrodynamic model is applied to describe the expansion of the dense matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The hydrodynamic expansion of the fluid, supplemented with the statistical emission of hadrons at freeze-out gives a satisfactory description of the observables for particles emitted with soft momenta. Experimental data for the transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow and interferometry radii give constraints on the properties of the fluid, its equation of state and viscosity coefficients. The role of the fluctuations of the initial profile of the energy density is discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic hydrodynamic model, supplemented with statistical hadron emission at freeze-out, provides a satisfactory description of soft-momentum observables in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC energies, but the description relies on specific choices for the equation of state, viscosity coefficients, and initial conditions, and some discrepancies remain for the momentum dependence of elliptic and triangular flow of identified particles.

## Method

The author applies relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics to model the expansion of the fireball formed in heavy-ion collisions, using the energy-momentum tensor with shear and bulk viscosity corrections and dynamical equations for the stress tensors. The hydrodynamic evolution is supplemented with statistical hadron emission at freeze-out, and calculations are performed in both 2+1-D boost-invariant and 3+1-D geometries using a cross-over equation of state inspired by lattice QCD. The model is compared to experimental data on transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, directed flow, and Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry radii from RHIC (Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV).

## Key numbers

- RHIC Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV; LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Pion spectra described for soft momenta p_T < 1.5 GeV in a perfect fluid 3+1-D model
- Extracted shear viscosity to entropy ratio lies between eta/s=0.08 and eta/s=0.2 depending on initial density profile model (Glauber or KLN)
- Viscous hydrodynamic calculations use eta/s=0.08 for Glauber initial conditions and zeta/s=0.04 below T_c
- AdS/CFT reference value for shear viscosity to entropy ratio: eta/s=1/(4pi)
- HBT radii ratio R_out/R_side used to distinguish between cross-over and first-order phase transition equations of state

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling from RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) to LHC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), building on prior perfect fluid calculations by Teaney (2000), Kolb (2003), Hirano (2002), Hama (2005), Broniowski (2008), Huovinen (2006), and Bozek (2009), as well as viscous hydrodynamics by Teaney (2009), Romatschke (2009), Song (2008), and Schenke (2010). The paper shows that the same hydrodynamic parameters used at RHIC can describe LHC data when only the initial energy density is increased, but it identifies remaining discrepancies in the mass splitting of elliptic and triangular flow for identified particles, pointing to the need for improved treatment of initial state fluctuations and the hadronic rescattering stage.
