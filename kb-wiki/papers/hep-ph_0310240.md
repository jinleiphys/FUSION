# On the pion cloud of the nucleon

> We evaluate the two--pion contribution to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors by use of dispersion analysis and chiral perturbation theory. After subtraction of the rho--meson component, we calculate the distributions of charge and magnetization in coordinate space, which can be interpreted as the effects of the pion cloud. We find that the charge distribution of this pion cloud effect peaks at distances of about 0.3 fm. Furthermore, we calculate the contribution of the pion cloud to the isovector charges and radii of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The two-pion contribution to the nucleon's electromagnetic form factors, interpreted as the pion cloud, produces charge and magnetization densities that peak at about 0.3 fm and fall off smoothly, contradicting the much longer-range (approximately 2 fm) pion cloud claimed by Friedrich and Walcher (2003) based on a phenomenological fit to form factor data.

## Method

The authors used dispersion analysis to express the isovector spectral functions of the nucleon form factors in terms of the pion charge form factor and P-wave pi-N partial waves, valid from the two-pion threshold up to about 1 GeV. They then subtracted the rho-meson component from these spectral functions using three different methods (two-loop chiral perturbation theory, setting the pion form factor to unity, and a Breit-Wigner subtraction) to isolate the uncorrelated two-pion contribution, and Fourier-transformed the resulting spectral functions to obtain coordinate-space densities.

## Key numbers

- The charge distribution of the pion cloud effect peaks at distances of about 0.3 fm.
- At r = 1.5 fm, the isovector electric density from the pion cloud is smaller by a factor of 3 compared to the result of Friedrich and Walcher (2003).
- The two-pion contribution to the isovector electric charge G_E^V(0) is in the range 0.1 to 0.2 (20% of the total isovector charge).
- The two-pion contribution to the isovector magnetic moment G_M^V(0) is in the range 0.4 to 1.0 (10% to approximately 25% of the total isovector magnetic moment).
- The radii normalized to physical charges for the isovector electric and magnetic Sachs form factors are both in the range 0.2 to 0.3 fm^2.
- The upper limit for the pion cloud radius, assuming all spectral strength at threshold, is sqrt(3/2) * M_pi^{-1} approximately 1.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-analytic and chiral perturbation theory approach to nucleon form factors developed by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and others, and directly contradicts the much longer-range pion cloud interpretation of Friedrich and Walcher (2003) based on a global phenomenological fit. The results are consistent with earlier chiral soliton model calculations by Meissner (1988) and provide a model-independent benchmark for separating short-range (rho-meson) from long-range (pion cloud) contributions in nucleon structure, enabling future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and experimental data from Jefferson Lab.
