# $Δ$-baryon axialvector and pseudoscalar form factors, and associated PCAC relations

> A quark+diquark Faddeev equation treatment of the baryon bound state problem in Poincaré-invariant quantum field theory is used to deliver parameter-free predictions for all six $Δ$-baryon elastic weak form factors. Amongst the results, it is worth highlighting that there are two distinct classes of such $Δ$-baryon form factors, $(g_1, g_3, G_{πΔΔ})$, $(h_1, h_3, H_{πΔΔ})$, the functions within each of which are separately linked via partial conservation of axial current (PCAC) and Goldberger-Treiman (GT) relations. Respectively within each class, the listed form factors possess qualitatively the same structural features as the nucleon axial, induced pseudoscalar, and pion-nucleon coupling form factors. For instance, the $Δ$-baryon $g_1$ axial form factor can reliably be approximated by a dipole function, characterised by an axial charge $g_A^{Δ^+}=0.71(9)$ and mass-scale $m_A^Δ=0.95(2)m_Δ$. Moreover, the two distinct $Δ$-baryon PCAC form factor relations are satisfied to a high degree of accuracy on a large range of $Q^2$; the associated GT relations present good approximations only on $Q^2/m_Δ^2 \simeq 0$; and pion pole dominance approximations are reliable within both classes. There are two $πΔΔ$ couplings: $g_{πΔΔ} = 10.46(1.88)$; $h_{πΔΔ}= 35.73(3.75)$; and the associated form factors are soft. Such couplings commonly arise in phenomenology, which may therefore benefit from our analyses. A flavour decomposition of the axial charges reveals that quarks carry $71$\% of the $Δ$-baryon spin. The analogous result for the proton is $\approx 65$\%.

## Key claim
The paper delivers parameter-free predictions for all six Δ-baryon elastic weak form factors using a quark+diquark Faddeev equation treatment, but the results are embedded in uncertainty bands reflecting a ±5% variation in the axialvector diquark mass, and the predictions are only qualitatively consistent with existing lattice QCD results due to large lattice uncertainties.

## Method
The authors employed a Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation treatment of the baryon bound state problem, using only isovector-axialvector diquark correlations (neglecting isovector-vector diquarks as negligible). They constructed symmetry-preserving axialvector and pseudoscalar currents appropriate for baryons described by this framework, with six distinct diagrammatic contributions (though Diagram (3) does not contribute for Δ-baryons). The form factors were obtained by spin-summed matrix projection operations on the current vertices.

## Key numbers
- Δ-baryon axial charge for Δ⁺: g_A^{Δ⁺} = 0.71(9); for Δ⁺⁺: g_A^{Δ⁺⁺} = 2.13(27) = 3 × g_A^{Δ⁺}
- Axial dipole mass: m_A^Δ = 0.95(2) m_Δ
- Two πΔΔ coupling constants: g_{πΔΔ} = 10.46(1.88); h_{πΔΔ} = 35.73(3.75)
- Quarks carry 71% of the Δ-baryon spin (compared to ≈65% for the proton)
- h_1(0) = 2.35(17)
- Axialvector diquark mass: m_{axialvector} = 0.89 GeV

## Context
This work extends the continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) previously applied to nucleon weak form factors by Chen (2020, 2021, 2022) to the Δ-baryon, providing a unified treatment of nucleon and Δ-baryon electroweak properties. It is the first parameter-free CSM calculation of all six Δ-baryon elastic weak form factors, complementing lattice QCD studies by Alexandrou (2011, 2013) which used unphysically large pion masses and had significant uncertainties, and a chiral quark-soliton model calculation by Jun (2020) that computed only the four axial form factors. The results can serve as inputs for effective field theories in low-energy hadron physics and enable reliable predictions for weak N→Δ transitions relevant to neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments.
