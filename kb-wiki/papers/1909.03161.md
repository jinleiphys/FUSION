# Finite-size scaling phenomenon of nuclear liquid--gas phase transition probes

> Based on the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, finite-size scaling effects on nuclear liquid--gas phase transition probes are investigated by studying the de-excitation processes of six thermal sources of different sizes with the same initial density and similar $N/Z$. Using several probes including the total multiplicity derivative ($dM_{tot}/dT$), second moment parameter ($M_2$), intermediate mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity ($N_{IMF}$), Fisher's power-law exponent ($τ$), and Ma's nuclear Zipf's law exponent ($ξ$), the relationship between the phase transition temperature and the source size has been established. It is observed that the phase transition temperatures obtained from the IMF multiplicity, Fisher's exponent, and Ma's nuclear Zipf's law exponent have a strong correlation with the source size. Moreover, by employing the finite-size scaling law, the critical temperature $T_c$ and the critical exponent $ν$ have been obtained for infinite nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The phase transition temperatures extracted from the IMF multiplicity, Fisher's exponent, and Ma's nuclear Zipf's law exponent show a strong correlation with source size and follow the finite-size scaling law, whereas those from the total multiplicity derivative and second moment parameter do not; the critical temperature for infinite nuclear matter is approximately 13.32 ± 1.22 MeV, which is lower than the experimentally determined value of 16.6 ± 0.86 MeV reported by Natowitz et al.

## Method

The authors used a single-thermal-source version of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ThIQMD) model to simulate the de-excitation of six finite-size nuclear sources with mass numbers A = 36, 52, 80, 100, 112, and 129, all at the same normal density and similar N/Z ≈ 1.3. The sources were initialized at temperatures from 0 to 20 MeV using a Fermi-Dirac momentum distribution, and the final-state fragments were identified via a coalescence mechanism with a relative distance cutoff of 3.5 fm and relative momentum cutoff of 300 MeV/c. Phase transition temperatures were extracted from five probes: total multiplicity derivative (dMtot/dT), second moment parameter (M2), intermediate mass fragment multiplicity (NIMF), Fisher's power-law exponent (τ), and Ma's nuclear Zipf's law exponent (ξ).

## Key numbers

- Six source systems with mass numbers A = 36, 52, 80, 100, 112, and 129 and corresponding charges Z = 15, 24, 33, 45, 50, and 54, all with N/Z ≈ 1.3
- Critical temperature for infinite nuclear matter: Tc = 13.32 ± 1.22 MeV (average from NIMF, τ, and ξ probes)
- Critical exponent for correlation length: ν = 0.37 ± 0.07
- Fisher's exponent τ reaches a minimum value between 2 and 3 for all source sizes
- Ma's nuclear Zipf's law exponent ξ = 1 defines the phase transition temperature
- The incompressibility used in the model is K = 200 MeV (soft MDI EOS)
- Gaussian width L = 2.16 fm²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition probes by systematically examining finite-size scaling effects, building on the Fisher droplet model (Fisher 1967) and Ma's nuclear Zipf's law (Ma 1999). The extracted critical temperature of 13.32 ± 1.22 MeV is lower than the experimentally determined value of 16.6 ± 0.86 MeV from Natowitz et al. (2002) and the first-principles prediction of 16 ± 2 MeV from Rios using chiral interactions. The critical exponent ν = 0.37 ± 0.07 is close to the 3D Ising or liquid-gas universality class value of 0.6 rather than the 3D percolation class value of 0.9, suggesting the nuclear disassembly belongs to the 3D liquid-gas universality class. The results enable future studies to use NIMF, τ, and ξ as reliable probes for extracting infinite-matter critical parameters from finite nuclear systems.
