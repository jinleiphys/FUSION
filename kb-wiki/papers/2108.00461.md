# Improved quark coalescence model for spin alignments of vector mesons

> We propose an improved quark coalescence model for spin alignment of vector mesons by spin density matrix with phase space dependence. Within this model we propose an understanding of spin alignments of vector mesons $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$ in the static limit: a large positive deviation of $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons from $1/3$ may come from the electric part of the vector $ϕ$ field, while a negative deviation of $ρ_{00}$ for $K^{*0}$ mesons may come from the electric part of vorticity fields. In the low-$p_T$ region, $ρ_{00}$ for $K^{*0}$ mesons is proportional to $p_T^2$, which is qualitatively agree with experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the experimentally observed large positive deviation of the spin alignment parameter ρ₀₀ from 1/3 for φ mesons may originate from the electric part of the vector φ field, while the negative deviation for K*⁰ mesons may come from the electric part of vorticity fields, but only in the static limit and with the caveat that these explanations require testing by detailed simulations.

## Method

The authors construct an improved quark coalescence model based on a spin density matrix with phase-space dependence, generalizing earlier momentum-space formulations. They derive the spin density matrix elements for vector mesons from the direct product of quark and antiquark density matrices, using Gaussian wave functions for the meson bound states. The spin polarizations of quarks and antiquarks are obtained from the Wigner function approach, incorporating contributions from thermal vorticity fields and vector meson mean fields, which are then substituted into the coalescence formalism to compute the spin alignment parameter ρ₀₀ for φ and K*⁰ mesons.

## Key numbers

- For nearly static φ mesons (p ≈ 0), the spin alignment ρ₀₀ receives negative contributions from vorticity fields (ω and ε) and positive contributions from vector meson fields (E_φ and B_φ), with the electric part E_φ proposed as the dominant source of positive deviation from 1/3.
- For K*⁰ mesons, the spin alignment ρ₀₀ is proportional to p_T² in the low-p_T region, which qualitatively agrees with experimental results from ALICE and STAR.
- The contribution from the electric vorticity field ε for ρ₀₀ of K*⁰ is amplified by about 2.1 to 2.3 compared to that for φ mesons, making it sizable for K*⁰ but less important for φ.

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence model of Yang (2017) by incorporating spatial dependence into the spin density matrix, enabling treatment of inhomogeneous spin polarizations in heavy-ion collisions. It proposes explanations for the spin alignment measurements of φ and K*⁰ mesons reported by the ALICE and STAR collaborations (ALICE 2019, Kundu 2021, Singha 2020), which previously lacked a theoretical understanding. The results enable future tests through detailed simulations of vorticity tensor fields and vector meson fields in heavy-ion collisions, as the authors explicitly note their work should be validated by such comprehensive modeling.
