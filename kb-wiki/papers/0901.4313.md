# Covariant spectator theory for the electromagnetic three-nucleon form factors: Complete impulse approximation

> We present the first calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of $^3$He and $^3$H within the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST). This first exploratory study concentrates on the sensitivity of the form factors to the strength of the scalar meson-nucleon off-shell coupling, known from previous studies to have a strong influence on the three-body binding energy. Results presented here were obtained using the complete impulse approximation (CIA), which includes contributions of relativistic origin that appear as two-body corrections in a non-relativistic framework, such as "Z-graphs", but omits other two and three-body currents. We compare our results to non-relativistic calculations augmented by relativistic corrections of ${\cal O}(v/c)^2$.

# Covariant spectator theory for the electromagnetic three-nucleon form factors: Complete impulse approximation

## Key claim

The first calculations of the electromagnetic form factors of ³He and ³H within the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) are presented, but only in the complete impulse approximation (CIA), which omits two- and three-body currents; therefore the results cannot be expected to provide a quantitative description of the data over the entire momentum transfer range, and the study is exploratory, focusing on sensitivity to the scalar meson-nucleon off-shell coupling strength.

## Method

The electromagnetic current for the three-nucleon bound states was evaluated in the CST framework using the complete impulse approximation (CIA), which includes all one-body currents (including relativistic contributions such as "Z-graphs" that appear as two-body corrections in non-relativistic frameworks) but omits two- and three-body currents. The three-nucleon vertex functions were obtained by solving the CST Faddeev-type bound state equation using nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction models of one-boson-exchange form with different fixed values of the scalar meson-nucleon off-shell coupling strength. The form factors were extracted from the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current in the laboratory frame after boosting from the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The experimental triton bound state energy is \(E_t = -8.48\) MeV, which was reproduced by CST NN potential models with scalar off-shell coupling when the value that gave the best fit to NN observables was used.
- The two new high-precision OBE models fit the world data on np scattering below 350 MeV with \(\chi^2/N_\mathrm{data} \approx 1\).
- The charge form factor is given by \(F_C = F_1 - \tau F_2\) with \(\tau = Q^2/4M_t^2\), where \(M_t\) is the trinucleon mass.
- The magnetic form factor \(F_M = F_1 + F_2\) appears in the spatial components of the current, with the relation \(\langle M'| J^1_{3N} |M \rangle = 2M' \delta_{M',-M} (Q F_M)/(2M_t)\).

## Context

This work extends the CST approach previously applied to NN scattering and the deuteron (Gross, 1969-2008) and to the three-nucleon binding energy (Stadler, 1997), where scalar off-shell couplings were found to strongly influence the triton binding energy. The calculations are compared to non-relativistic calculations with relativistic corrections of order \((v/c)^2\) (Mar, 1998). The results enable future inclusion of two- and three-body currents in CST and provide a test of whether the relativistic off-shell dynamics captured by CST can explain the electromagnetic structure of three-nucleon systems without additional new physics.
