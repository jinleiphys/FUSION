# Ward Identities in Non-equilibrium QED

> We verify the QED Ward identity for the two- and three -point functions at non-equilibrium in the HTL limit. We use the Keldysh formalism of real time finite temperature field theory. We obtain an identity of the same form as the Ward identity for a set of one loop self-energy and one loop three-point vertex diagrams which are constructed from HTL effective propagators and vertices.

## Key claim

The paper verifies that the QED Ward identity for two- and three-point functions holds in the hard thermal loop (HTL) limit even when the system is out of equilibrium, using the Keldysh real-time formalism; the identity has the same structural form as in equilibrium, but the equilibrium thermal distribution functions are replaced by non-equilibrium Wigner functions.

## Method

The authors used the Keldysh formulation of real-time finite-temperature field theory to compute the retarded, advanced, and symmetric components of the electron self-energy and the 1PI three-point vertex in QED. They applied the HTL approximation, in which loop momenta are taken to be hard (of order the temperature T) while external momenta are soft (of order gT), and contracted the resulting integral expressions with the external photon momentum to verify the Ward identities. They also constructed a second-order effective theory using HTL-resummed propagators and vertices and checked that a similar identity holds for a subset of one-loop diagrams in that theory.

## Key numbers

* The HTL approximation requires loop momenta S to satisfy S >> P, Q, where P and Q are external fermion momenta, with the hard scale set by the temperature T.
* The splitting identity used for propagators is D_R/A(P1) D_R/A(P3) = 1/(P3^2 - P1^2) (D_R/A(P1) - D_R/A(P3)), which relies on the denominators (s0 +/- i epsilon)^2 - s^2 for the retarded and advanced propagators.
* In the HTL limit, the factor (Q+S)^2 - (P+S)^2 simplifies to 2 S dot (Q-P), and the gamma matrix structure reduces to 2 S^phi S slash, with the gamma^phi S^2 term dropping out by power counting.

## Context

This work extends the Braaten-Pisarski resummation scheme (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990), originally developed for equilibrium finite-temperature field theory, to non-equilibrium systems. It builds on the authors' earlier proof (Carrington, Defu, Thoma, 1997) that pinch singularities do not occur in the non-equilibrium HTL effective propagator. The result enables systematic perturbative calculations for soft external momenta in non-equilibrium QED and suggests that the HTL effective action remains gauge invariant out of equilibrium, with potential applications to heavy-ion collisions and electroweak phase transitions.
