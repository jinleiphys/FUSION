# Negative-parity Baryon Masses using an O(a)-improved Fermion Action

> We present a calculation of the mass of the lowest-lying negative-parity J=1/2- state in quenched QCD. Results are obtained using a non-perturbatively O(a)-improved clover fermion action, and a splitting is found between the mass of the nucleon and its parity partner. The calculation is performed on two lattice volumes and at three lattice spacings, enabling a study of both finite-volume and finite lattice-spacing uncertainties. A comparison is made with results obtained using the unimproved Wilson fermion action.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the O(a)-improved clover (SW) fermion action and the unimproved Wilson fermion action can resolve the mass splitting between the positive- and negative-parity nucleon states in quenched QCD, with the splitting obtained using the two actions being entirely consistent. The authors caution that systematic uncertainties of order 10% arise from finite-volume effects, chiral extrapolation, and discretisation uncertainties.

## Method

The calculation uses quenched QCD with a non-perturbatively O(a)-improved clover (Sheikholeslami-Wohlert) fermion action on lattices generated by the UKQCD and QCDSF collaborations at three lattice spacings (beta = 6.0, 6.2, 6.4) and two lattice volumes. Masses of the lowest-lying J=1/2+ and J=1/2- states are obtained from simultaneous four-parameter fits to positive- and negative-parity correlators constructed from local and fuzzed or smeared sources and sinks, with chiral extrapolations performed using a linear fit in m_pi^2.

## Key numbers

* Mass ratio of negative- to positive-parity nucleon in the quenched approximation: M_N(1/2-)/M_N(1/2+) = 1.50(3) (statistical error only), compared with the physical ratio of 1.63
* Continuum-extrapolated masses in units of r_0: M_N(1/2+) r_0 = 2.74(4), M_N(1/2-) r_0 = 4.1(1), where r_0 = 0.5 fm
* At beta = 6.0, the negative-parity mass on the smaller lattice (16^3 x 32) is a m_N(1/2-) = 0.97(1) versus 1.01(1) on the larger lattice (24^3 x 32) at a pseudoscalar mass a m_pi = 0.3
* At beta = 6.2, the negative-parity mass on the smaller lattice (24^3 x 48) is a m_N(1/2-) = 0.664(9) versus 0.69(4) on the larger lattice (32^3 x 64) at a pseudoscalar mass a m_pi = 0.2
* Chiral extrapolated masses in lattice units at beta = 6.4: a M_N(1/2+) = 0.285(4), a M_N(1/2-) = 0.46(1)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of negative-parity baryon masses using the highly-improved D_234 fermion action (Lee and Leinweber, 1999) and domain-wall fermions (Sasaki, 2000; Sasaki, Blum and Ohta, 2001), confirming the existence of a clear mass splitting between parity partners. The use of the computationally cheaper clover action enables more extensive studies of the excited nucleon spectrum, and the comparison with Wilson fermion results shows that the splitting is not a lattice artifact. The paper enables future calculations with non-degenerate quark masses and for I=3/2 states, as well as more detailed studies of quenched chiral behaviour near the N* to N pi threshold.
