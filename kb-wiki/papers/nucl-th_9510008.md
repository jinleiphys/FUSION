# Contribution of the NN inelasticity into photodisintegration of the deuteron

> The technique of dispersion integration over the mass of composite particle is used to describe the reaction of the deuteron photodisintegration. The influence of the final state interaction (FSI) on the total cross section, calculated with and without inelasticity is investigated. Numerical results depend on the choice of the vertex function for the isobar photoproduction.

# Contribution of the NN inelasticity into photodisintegration of the deuteron

## Key claim
The influence of NN inelasticity on the deuteron photodisintegration total cross section depends significantly on the choice of the vertex function for isobar photoproduction; the partial cross sections calculated with different vertex operators (Gourdin-Salin vs. Bjorken-Walecka type) differ considerably, particularly for the $^3F_3$ wave.

## Method
The dispersion relation approach developed by Anisovich et al. was used to construct relativistic and gauge invariant amplitudes for deuteron photodisintegration. The NN scattering amplitude was treated via the dispersion N/D method, with G-functions constructed by fitting phase shift data from Arndt et al. (1995). For waves showing resonance-type behavior ($^3P_2$, $^1D_2$, $^3F_3$), two-channel scattering amplitudes including the $N\Delta$ intermediate state were built; for non-resonant inelastic waves ($^1S_0$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$), two-channel amplitudes with various second channel choices ($N\Delta$, $NN^*(1440)$, $\pi N$-pair) were used.

## Key numbers
- Inelasticity was observed in waves $^1S_0$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$, $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$, $^3F_3$ at proton kinetic energy $T < 1$ GeV.
- The largest partial cross sections for the waves $^1S_0$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$ occur for photon energy $E_\gamma < 100$ MeV.
- The wave $^3F_3$ gives the largest contribution in the resonance region among the waves $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$, $^3F_3$.
- The $^1D_2$ wave contribution in the resonance region corresponds to the $M1$ transition from nucleon to isobar.

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Anisovich et al. for deuteron photodisintegration by explicitly incorporating NN inelasticity through two-channel scattering amplitudes. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic approach of Leidemann and Arenhovel (1993), where the main contribution in the resonance region comes from dipole magnetic $M1$ transitions corresponding to the $^1D_2$ final state, whereas this calculation finds the $^3F_3$ wave dominant. The results enable further investigation of the appropriate form for the isobar photoproduction vertex, with the Gourdin-Salin (1964) operator giving different results from the Bjorken-Walecka type operator.
