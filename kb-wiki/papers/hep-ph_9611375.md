# Why Hybrid Meson Coupling to Two S-wave Mesons is Suppressed

> We introduce strong interaction selection rules for the two-body decay and production of hybrid and conventional mesons coupling to two S-wave hybrid or conventional mesons. The rules arise from symmetrization in states in the limit of non-relativistically moving quarks. The conditions under which hybrid coupling to S-wave states is suppressed are determined by the rules, and the nature of their breaking is indicated.

## Key claim

The paper introduces strong interaction selection rules for the two-body decay and production of hybrid and conventional mesons coupling to two S-wave mesons, showing that the connected coupling of a hybrid to two S-wave states is suppressed under specific conditions involving non-relativistically moving quarks, identical S-wave meson spatial wave functions, and equal quark and antiquark constituent masses in the hybrid.

## Method

The author derives selection rules from symmetrization of the two final-state mesons B and C under exchange of their momenta, factoring the helicity amplitude into colour, flavour, spin, and remaining overlaps. The analysis focuses on the connected decay topology (topology 1) where a quark-antiquark pair is created or annihilated with spin S_QQ, and the conditions for vanishing amplitude are found by requiring that the product of the flavour and spin exchange factors equals (-1)^(L+1) where L is the partial wave between B and C. The rules are then applied to specific flavour categories including Q\bar{Q} decays and isospin-symmetric u,d quark decays.

## Key numbers

* For decays of c\bar{c} and b\bar{b} adiabatic hybrids to S-wave mesons via spin 1 pair creation, predicted widths are 1-10 MeV and 1-4 MeV respectively.
* Breaking of selection rules due to differing spatial wave functions from spin-dependent forces is estimated as (R_B^2 - R_C^2)^2 / (R_B^2 + R_C^2)^2, ranging from approximately 20% for rho pi to 4% for D* \bar{D} and 1% for B* \bar{B}.
* For u,d quark hybrid 1^{-+}, QCD sum rule calculations give a width of 10-600 MeV to rho pi and 8-300 MeV to K* K.
* For u,d quark hybrid 1^{-+}, QCD sum rule calculations give an eta pi width of 0.3 MeV and an eta' pi width of 3-4 MeV.
* Non-connected hybrid decays are expected to be less than O(10^{-1} MeV) for c\bar{c} and O(10^{-2} MeV) for b\bar{b} states.

## Context

This work extends earlier model calculations by Kalashnikova (1995) that noted vanishing decays of transverse electric (TE) hybrids and adiabatic hybrids in the flux-tube model by Page (1995), providing a more general and model-independent statement of the selection rules. The rules unify findings from both strong and weak coupling limits of QCD, and the paper indicates that for heavy quark systems (c\bar{c} and b\bar{b}) the suppression of hybrid decays to S-wave mesons may lead to narrow hybrid states below the (L=0)+(L=1) threshold, as predicted by Perantonis and Michael (1990).
