# Models for photo- and electro-production of K^+ in view of new data

> Predictions of isobaric and Regge models are compared with the latest experimental data (Bonn,JLab) to select among the models those providing a satisfactory description of the data. Only the Saclay-Lyon and MAID models are in a reasonable agreement with both photo- and electro-production data ranging up to about 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

Only the Saclay-Lyon (SLA) and MAID models are in reasonable agreement with both photo- and electro-production data up to about 2.5 GeV, but the Regge model is suitable only for electro-production data in this energy region, while older models (AS1, AS2, W1, W2, AB2) can reliably predict only photo-production cross sections up to 1.5 GeV.

## Method

Predictions of isobaric models (AB2, AW2, AW3, AW4, AS1, AS2, W1, W2, C3, C4, SL, SLA) and a Regge model were compared with experimental data from Bonn (SAPHIR) and Jefferson Lab (E93-018, E98-108) for the reactions gamma p -> Lambda K+ and p(e,e'K+)Lambda. The isobaric models use effective hadronic Lagrangians with Feynman diagram tree-level amplitudes including Born terms and resonant exchanges, while the Regge model uses t-channel exchanges of particle families with dipole form factors. The MAID model additionally incorporates hadron form factors in a gauge-invariant way and includes the "missing" D13(1895) resonance.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of comparison: up to E_gamma_lab = 2.5 GeV for photo-production; electro-production data at W = 1.84 GeV and Q^2 up to 2.4 GeV^2
- Photo-production total cross section data from SAPHIR have relative errors of approximately 8%, rising to 15% near threshold and up to 40% above 1.5 GeV
- Unseparated electro-production cross sections have relative errors less than 5%
- For the unseparated electro-production cross section, chi^2 values (normalized to AW3 = 1.00): AW3 = 64.1 (1.00), Regge = 89.7 (1.40), C4_4 = 70.5 (1.10), SLA = 117.2 (1.83), AW4 = 116.9 (1.82)
- For the photo-production total cross section, chi^2 values: MAID = 0.32, C3 = 2.47, SLA = 5.17, AB2 = 5.10, C4 = 11.6
- The Regge model cut-off mass: Lambda_K^2 = 1.5 GeV^2
- SU(3) symmetry limits for main coupling constants: -4.4 <= g_KLambdaN/sqrt(4pi) <= -3.0 and 0.8 <= g_KSigmaN/sqrt(4pi) <= 1.3
- The MAID model at Q^2 approximately 0.2 GeV^2 shows a pronounced bump structure in sigma_L

## Context

This work extends comparisons of isobaric and Regge models for kaon photo- and electro-production, building on earlier analyses by Adelseck and Saghai (1990), Williams, Ji, and Cotanch (1991, 1992), and the Saclay-Lyon group (1996, 1998). The new Jefferson Lab data from Niculescu (1998) and Mohring (2002) provide more stringent tests than previously available. The results enable selection of reliable models (SLA, MAID, Regge, AW4) for analyzing data from the E98-108 experiment at Jefferson Lab, which extends separated cross section measurements to higher energies and larger Q^2 (1.9-2.4 GeV^2).
