# Quantum Phase Transitions in Odd-Mass Nuclei

> Quantum shape-phase transitions in odd-even nuclei are investigated in the framework of the interacting boson-fermion model. Classical and quantum analysis show that the presence of the odd fermion strongly influences the location and nature of the phase transition, especially near the critical point. Experimental evidence for the occurrence of spherical to axially-deformed transitions in odd-proton nuclei Pm, Eu and Tb (Z=61, 63, 65) is presented.

## Key claim

Quantum shape-phase transitions in odd-even nuclei are strongly influenced by the presence of the odd fermion, which acts as a catalyst for some states and as a retardative for others, shifting the critical point and altering the sharpness of the transition relative to the purely bosonic case.

## Method

The authors study quantum phase transitions in odd-even nuclei using the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM) with a single-j fermion (j=11/2) coupled to N bosons. They perform both a classical analysis by constructing the combined Bose-Fermi potential energy surface and minimizing it with respect to deformation variables (beta, gamma), and a quantal analysis by numerically diagonalizing the Hamiltonian for finite N, using the expectation value of the d-boson number operator as a quantum order parameter.

## Key numbers

- The critical point for the purely bosonic U(5)-SU(3) transition occurs at xi_c approximately 1/2 (xi_B^c approximately 0.51)
- For pure quadrupole coupling (Lambda=0), the critical point for state 4 is at xi_4^c approximately 0.50, for state 5 at xi_5^c approximately 0.53, and for state 6 at xi_6^c approximately 0.58
- With exchange interaction Lambda = -0.287, the modifications to the phase transition are more dramatic, with state 6 moving considerably to the right of xi_B^c
- The deformation contribution to two-neutron separation energies in odd-even nuclei (Pm, Eu, Tb) is enhanced by approximately 300 keV at neutron number 92 compared to even-even nuclei (Nd, Sm, Gd)
- The linear background subtraction parameters for odd-even nuclei are A_2n = -15.185, -16.37, -17.672 MeV for Pm-Eu-Tb respectively, with B_2n = 0.670 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum phase transitions in even-even nuclei within the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) by Iachello and collaborators (Iachello 2001, Casten 2011, Iachello 2011) to odd-even nuclei using the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM) of Iachello (1987). The results enable experimental identification of spherical to axially-deformed transitions in odd-proton nuclei Pm, Eu, and Tb (Z=61, 63, 65), showing that the odd fermion enhances the signature of the phase transition in two-neutron separation energies compared to the even-even neighbors.
