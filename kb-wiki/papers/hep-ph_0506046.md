# Shape of the $f_0(980)$ in $╬│╬│\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$

> The Belle Collaboration results on the observation of the $f_0(980)$ resonance in the reaction $╬│╬│\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ are analyzed. It is argued that they point to the presence of mechanisms which give rise to a strong distortion of the $f_0(980)$ resonance shape in comparison with the shape of a solitary Breit-Wigner resonance. It is shown that the main factors responsible for the formation of the specific, steplike, shape of the $f_0(980)$ resonance in the $╬│╬│\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ reaction cross section are the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism of the $f_0(980)$ coupling to the $╬│╬│$ system and the destructive interference between the background and $f_0(980)$ resonance contributions in the $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ invariant mass region below the $K^+K^-$ threshold.

## Key claim

The Belle Collaboration data on the $f_0(980)$ resonance in $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ point to a strong distortion of the resonance shape relative to a solitary Breit-Wigner, with the main factors being the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism of the $f_0(980)$ coupling to $\gamma\gamma$ and destructive interference between background and resonance contributions below the $K^+K^-$ threshold; the resulting shape is a step-like structure rather than a clear peak.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for the $S$-wave amplitude of $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ in the 1 GeV region, incorporating the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism for $f_0(980)\to\gamma\gamma$ decay, the Born amplitude for $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$, and final-state interactions via $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar K$ scattering amplitudes. The model used the $f_0(980)$ propagator satisfying the Kallen-Lehmann representation and parametrized the $S$-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering with phase shifts and inelasticities from prior data, with no free parameters beyond those constrained by other reactions. The resulting cross sections were compared to Belle data for $\sigma(\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-)$ in the region $|\cos\theta^*|<0.6$.

## Key numbers

- The Belle Collaboration determined the $f_0(980)$ peak position as $m_{f_0}=981.2\pm0.5$ MeV and total width $\Gamma=21.7\pm2.1$ MeV from fitting to an incoherent sum of resonance and background.
- The $f_0(980)$ manifests as a step with width about 15 MeV and height about 11 nb in the Belle $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ cross section data.
- For the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism, the factor $m\Gamma^{Born}_{f_0\to K^+K^-\to\gamma\gamma}(m)$ falls relative to its maximum at $m=2m_{K^+}\approx0.9873$ GeV by factors of 1.69, 2.23, 2.75, 3.27, and 6.33 at $m=0.98$, 0.97, 0.96, 0.95, and 0.9 GeV, respectively.
- The $f_0(980)$ contribution via the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism yields a cross section at the level of about 15.5-17.5 nb at maximum (after multiplying by factor 0.6 for the angular cut).
- The averaged two-photon width $\langle\Gamma^{Born}_{f_0\to K^+K^-\to\gamma\gamma}\rangle_{\pi\pi}$ for set A parameters is approximately 0.114 keV (integration 0.93-1.03 GeV) and 0.191 keV (full range $2m_\pi$ to $\infty$); for set D it is 0.152 keV and 0.377 keV, respectively.
- The total averaged two-photon width $\langle\Gamma^{Born}_{f_0\to K^+K^-\to\gamma\gamma}\rangle$ for set A is about 0.14 keV (0.93-1.03 GeV) and 0.359 keV (full range); for set D it is 0.189 keV and 1.094 keV, respectively.
- The fit to Belle data and $\pi\pi$ scattering data (set E) gave $m_{f_0}=0.9676$ GeV, $g^2_{f_0\pi\pi}/16\pi=0.07017$ GeV$^2$, $g^2_{f_0K\bar K}/16\pi=0.3442$ GeV$^2$ (ratio $R=4.9$).
- The fit with fixed $g^2_{f_0K\bar K}/16\pi=1.6$ GeV$^2$ (set F) gave $m_{f_0}=0.968$ GeV, $g^2_{f_0\pi\pi}/16\pi=0.2438$ GeV$^2$ (ratio $R=6.56$).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the $f_0(980)$ resonance shape in $\gamma\gamma$ collisions, building on the $K^+K^-$ loop mechanism previously applied to $\phi\to f_0(980)\gamma$ decays (Achasov and Ivanchenko, 1989) and to $\gamma\gamma\to a_0^0(980)\to\eta\pi^0$ (Achasov and Shestakov, 1990). The analysis contradicts the simple Breit-Wigner parametrization used by the Belle Collaboration (2005) and shows that the $f_0(980)$ shape in $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ is a step rather than a peak, due to destructive interference with background. The results enable future partial-wave analyses of $\gamma\gamma\to\pi\pi$ data to extract the $S$-wave contribution, and suggest that $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0$ may show a clearer $f_0(980)$ peak, though with insufficient resolution in prior Crystal Ball (1990) and JADE (1986) experiments.
