# Lifetime of a Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> The lifetime of a disoriented chiral condensate formed within a heat bath of pions is calculated assuming temperatures and densities attainable at present and future heavy-ion colliders. A generalization of the reduction formula to include coherent states allows us to derive a formula for the decay rate. We predict the half-life to be between 4 and 7 fm/c, depending on the assumed pion density. We also calculate the lifetime in the presence of higher resonances and baryons, which shortens the lifetime by at most 20%.

## Key claim

The half-life of a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formed in the hadronic (chirally broken) phase is between 4 and 7 fm/c depending on the assumed pion density, but this range can be shortened to about 3-4 fm/c if thermal distributions are enhanced by a factor of three as predicted by some event generators.

## Method

The authors derive a decay rate for a DCC defined as a coherent state of low-momentum pions by generalizing the LSZ reduction formula to include coherent states. They then evaluate the rate using experimental $\pi\pi$ scattering phase shifts parameterized from data, and extend the calculation to include $\pi\rho$ scattering via an $a_1(1240)$ resonance model and $\pi N$ scattering using a relativistically improved $\Delta(1232)$ isobar model, with thermal weighting factors accounting for Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distributions.

## Key numbers

- Half-life from $\pi\pi$ scattering alone: $\tau = 8.9$ fm/c at $T = 150$ MeV and $\tau = 5.6$ fm/c at $T = 170$ MeV
- With three times the number of thermal pions at $T = 150$ MeV: $\tau = 3.8$ fm/c
- Including $\pi N$ scattering with a 5:1 pion-to-nucleon ratio at $T = 150$ MeV and $\mu = 260$ MeV: $\tau = 7.2$ fm/c (about 20% reduction)
- With factor-of-three enhancement of thermal distributions: half-life between 3 and 3.5 fm/c for all pion-to-nucleon ratios considered
- The $\rho$ channel ($P$-wave) contribution is a few percent effect

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates by Anselm and Ryskin (1991) who gave a half-life of about 3 fm/c, and by Biro and Greiner (1997) who found a damping rate of about 1 (fm/c)$^{-1}$ corresponding to a half-life of about 1 fm/c using the $O(4)$ sigma model in the symmetric phase. The present calculation operates in the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry phase where $S$-wave scattering among pions is suppressed, producing longer lifetimes than these prior estimates. The results enable assessment of whether DCC signals could be detected at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, with the conclusion that detection from hadronic observables may be difficult if thermal distributions are enhanced, but more promising for large DCC domains formed in the absence of nucleons at future colliders.
