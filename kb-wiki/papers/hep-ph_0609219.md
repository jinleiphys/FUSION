# Glueball enhancement by color de-confinement

> High energy heavy ion collisions lead to the formation of a strong coupling de-confined phase in which the lightest glueballs are numerous and stable. We analyze how their properties manifest themselves in experimental spectra and show that they provide a good signature for color de-confinement.

# Glueball enhancement by color de-confinement

## Key claim

The scalar glueball $g$, which in the cold world is mixed with the $\sigma$ meson and appears as part of the broad $f_0(600)$, becomes numerous and stable in the strong coupling de-confined phase (SCP) above $T_C$, providing a clean experimental signature for color de-confinement, provided that the OZI approximation holds and the mixing between $g$ and $\sigma$ vanishes near $T_C$.

## Method

The author uses a phenomenological model based on $1/N_C$ physics and the OZI approximation, in which the physical scalar glueball $g$ and the $\sigma$ meson arise from mixing between degenerate OZI-conserving fields $g_0$ and $\sigma_0$. The temperature dependence of the gluon condensate, taken from lattice results and model calculations, is combined with the scale anomaly relation $m_g^2 f_g^2 = -4 \langle 0| \frac{\beta(\alpha_s)}{4\alpha_s} G^2 |0\rangle$ to infer the behavior of $g$ across the de-confinement transition. The chiral partner relation between $\sigma$ and $\pi$ is used to track the $\sigma$ mass as a function of temperature, and the resulting decay widths for $g \to 2\pi$ and $g \to 2\gamma$ are computed as functions of the mixing angle and the particle masses.

## Key numbers

- The de-confinement transition temperature $T_C$ lies in the range $150\ \mathrm{MeV} < T_C < 300\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The gluon condensate dilutes (evaporates) into gluons at $(2-3) T_C$.
- The $\sigma \to 2\pi$ decay width is $\Gamma_{\sigma\to 2\pi} \sim 1.5 \cos^2(\theta/2) \left( m_\sigma(\mathrm{GeV}) / 1\ \mathrm{GeV} \right)^3\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
- The $\sigma \to 2\gamma$ decay width is $\Gamma_{\sigma\to 2\gamma} \sim 10.5 \cos^2(\theta/2) \left( m_\sigma(\mathrm{GeV}) / 1\ \mathrm{GeV} \right)^3\ \mathrm{eV}$.
- The $g \to 2\pi$ decay width is $\Gamma_{g\to 2\pi} \sim 1.5 \sin^2(\theta/2) \left( m_g(\mathrm{GeV}) / 1\ \mathrm{GeV} \right)^3\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
- The $g \to 2\gamma$ decay width is $\Gamma_{g\to 2\gamma} \sim 10.5 \sin^2(\theta/2) \left( m_g(\mathrm{GeV}) / 1\ \mathrm{GeV} \right)^3\ \mathrm{eV}$.
- The ratio of glueball channels to meson channels changes from 1:8 below the phase transition to 1:2 above.

## Context

This work extends the author's own earlier proposal (Vento, 2006) of a low-lying scalar glueball mixed with the $\sigma$ meson, and applies it to the hot medium produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It builds on the description of the strong Coulomb phase (SCP) above $T_C$ by Shuryak (2004), where binary color-bound states persist despite de-confinement, and on lattice results for the gluon condensate (Lattice, Langfeld) and model calculations (Heinz, Drago, Wambach). The paper proposes that the PHOS detector at LHC, with its capability for $\pi_0$ detection (Kharlov, 2006), could observe both the $\sigma$ and the $g$ in two-photon invariant mass spectra, providing a signature for the SCP distinct from the quark-gluon plasma phase at higher temperatures.
