# Evidence for Multiple Chiral Doublet Bands in $^{133}$Ce

> Two distinct sets of chiral-partner bands have been identified in the nucleus $^{133}$Ce. They constitute a multiple chiral doublet (M$χ$D), a phenomenon predicted by relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations and observed experimentally here for the first time. The properties of these chiral bands are in good agreement with results of calculations based on a combination of the constrained triaxial RMF theory and the particle-rotor model.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first firm experimental evidence for multiple chiral doublet (MχD) bands in a single nucleus, specifically in \(^{133}\)Ce, confirming a prediction from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations. The claim is hedged by noting that while two distinct sets of chiral-partner bands were identified, lifetime measurements, which provided crucial confirmation for chiral bands in the similar nucleus \(^{135}\)Nd, were not feasible for the bands reported here.

## Method

High-spin states in \(^{133}\)Ce were populated via the \(^{116}\)Cd(\(^{22}\)Ne,5n)\(^{133}\)Ce reaction at 112 MeV using the ATLAS facility at Argonne National Laboratory. Two experiments were performed with different target backings, accumulating approximately \(4.1\times10^{9}\) four- and higher-fold prompt \(\gamma\)-ray coincidence events measured with the Gammasphere array. The data were analyzed using the RADWARE suite, and spin/parity assignments were made via DCO ratio measurements and angular-distribution analyses. The experimental results were compared with calculations combining constrained triaxial RMF theory and the triaxial particle-rotor model (TPRM).

## Key numbers

- Approximately \(4.1\times10^{9}\) four- and higher-fold coincidence events were accumulated across two experiments.
- The positive-parity chiral doublet (Bands 2 and 3) shows an energy separation nearly constant at \(\sim\)100 keV.
- The negative-parity chiral doublet (Bands 5 and 6) shows an energy separation of about 400 keV at \(I=29/2\), decreasing to about 200 keV at \(I=41/2\).
- RMF calculations yield deformation parameters: ground state A at \((\beta=0.20, \gamma=11.1^\circ)\); state a (Band 2) at \((\beta=0.22, \gamma=17.5^\circ)\) with excitation energy 1.71 MeV; state b (Band 5) at \((\beta=0.23, \gamma=15.2^\circ)\) with excitation energy 3.78 MeV.
- Experimental bandhead energies: Band 2 at \(I^\pi=19/2^+\) with \(E_x=2.378\) MeV; Band 5 at \(I^\pi=29/2^-\) with \(E_x=3.734\) MeV.
- Angular-distribution \(A_2/A_0\) ratios for interconnecting transitions between Bands 5 and 6: \(-(0.622\pm 0.013)\) for the 711-keV transition and \(-(0.7312\pm 0.032)\) for the 779-keV transition.

## Context

This work extends the search for chiral doublet bands, previously observed in the \(A\sim190\), \(A\sim130\), \(A\sim100\), and \(A\sim80\) mass regions, to the first observation of multiple such pairs in a single nucleus, a phenomenon termed MχD predicted by RMF calculations for Rh isotopes (Meng 2006; Peng 2008; Li 2011). The two configurations observed in \(^{133}\)Ce are analogous to those proposed for \(^{105}\)Rh (Timar 2004; Joshi 2004). The results confirm triaxial shape coexistence in \(^{133}\)Ce and enable future studies of chiral vibration and tunneling between left- and right-handed configurations in nuclei with multiple chiral partner bands.
