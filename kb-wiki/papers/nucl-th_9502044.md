# QUARK MODEL DESCRIPTION OF THE N-N SYSTEM: MOMENTUM DISTRIBUTIONS, STRUCTURE FUNCTIONS AND THE EMC EFFECT

> We employ a relativistic quark bag picture, the chromo-dielectric soliton model, to discuss the quarks' symmetry structure and momentum distribution in the $N$-$N$ system. Six-quark clusters are constructed in a constrained mean-field calculation. The corresponding Hamiltonian contains not only an effective interaction between the quarks and a scalar field, which is assumed to parameterize all non-perturbative effects due to the non-linearity of QCD, but also quark-quark interactions mediated through one-gluon-exchange. We also evaluate the quark light-cone distribution functions, characterizing inclusive deep-inelastic lepton scattering, for the nucleon as well as for the six-quark structures. We find a competition between a softening of the quarks' momenta through the increase of the confinement volume, and a hardening via the admixture of higher symmetry configurations due to the color-electrostatic one-gluon-exchange. These findings suggest an unexpected absence of many-nucleon, multi-quark effects, even though six-quark structures should represent a non-negligible part of the nuclear ground state.

## Key claim

The authors find that in six-quark clusters representing overlapping nucleons, the expected softening of quark momenta from increased confinement volume is nearly counterbalanced by a hardening from admixture of higher-symmetry configurations induced by color-electrostatic one-gluon-exchange, suggesting an unexpected absence of many-nucleon multi-quark effects even if six-quark structures are a non-negligible part of the nuclear ground state.

## Method

The authors employ the chromo-dielectric soliton model (CDM), a relativistic quark bag picture, to construct six-quark clusters via a constrained mean-field calculation for various inter-nucleon separations. The Hamiltonian includes an effective quark-scalar field interaction parameterizing non-perturbative QCD effects, plus quark-quark interactions mediated through one-gluon-exchange (OGE) treated in the Coulomb gauge. Quark light-cone distribution functions for deep-inelastic scattering are evaluated for both the nucleon and six-quark structures using a Peierls-Yoccoz projection to form momentum eigenstates, with the calculated distributions evolved to experimental momentum scales via non-singlet Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The quark's single particle energy is epsilon = 340 MeV for parameter set f=3 and epsilon = 335 MeV for f=infinity
- The bag model scale for evolution was mu = 0.33 GeV for the CDM and mu = 0.38 GeV for the MIT bag model
- The maximum repulsive core in the N-N potential varied between 200 and 350 MeV for the two parameter sets
- The normalization violation for the quark distribution function was approximately 2% for the CDM versus approximately 17% for the MIT bag model
- QCD evolution used Lambda_QCD = 213 MeV for four flavors at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of the N-N interaction using the CDM (Koepf 1994), where an adiabatic nucleon-nucleon potential was found to be repulsive at short distances due to color-electrostatic OGE. The study directly tests the hypothesis of Pirner (1981) that six-quark clustering can account for the EMC effect through softened quark momenta, and the formalism follows the Adelaide group approach (Signal 1988) for calculating structure functions from bag models. The results challenge the Noble (1981) suggestion of nucleon swelling in nuclear medium and the Pirner (1981) multi-quark explanation of the EMC effect, while enabling future work on whether deep-inelastic scattering probes only a "small-size" component of the nucleon wave function rather than the average nucleon.
