# Bolstering up the existence of $P_s(2080)$

> We present a detailed study of the partial decay widths of a spin-parity resonance $J^P=3/2^-$ $N^*$ with a mass of $\simeq$ 2070 MeV obtained from the coupled channel s wave vector-baryon $ρN$, $ωN$, $ϕN$, $K^*Λ$ and $K^*Σ$ dynamics. This state, which couples strongly to the $K^*Σ$ channel, corresponds to a nucleon with a hidden strange quark content, in analogy to the $P_c$ states discovered by the LHCb collaboration, and we denote it as $P_s(2080)$. A state with such a nature can decay to vector-baryon, pseudoscalar-baryon, and pseudoscalar-baryon resonance channels, involving triangular loops in the latter two cases. As we will show, the partial decay widths to pseudoscalar-baryon resonance channels, like $πN^*(1535)$, $πN^*(1650)$, $KΛ(1405)$, are comparable to those related to ground state baryons in the final state, like $πN$, $ηN$, $KΛ$. In this way, reactions involving such lighter baryon resonances in the final state can be used as an alternative source of information on the properties of a $N^*$ with hidden strangeness.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a nucleon resonance with spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$ and mass around 2070 MeV, denoted $P_s(2080)$, exists as a hidden-strangeness partner of the $P_c$ pentaquarks, and that its partial decay widths to pseudoscalar-baryon resonance channels (such as $\pi N^*(1535)$, $\pi N^*(1650)$, $K\Lambda(1405)$) are comparable to those to ground-state baryons, providing alternative experimental signatures. The hedge is that the state is not listed in the latest Review of Particle Physics (PDG), and the experimental data are still scarce to draw clear conclusions.

## Method

The authors calculate partial decay widths of the $P_s(2080)$ state, which was previously predicted from coupled-channel $s$-wave vector-baryon ($\rho N$, $\omega N$, $\phi N$, $K^*\Lambda$, $K^*\Sigma$) dynamics using effective Lagrangians based on hidden local symmetry. They determine coupling constants from the residues of the $t$-matrix in the complex energy plane, then evaluate decay amplitudes to vector-baryon channels directly and to pseudoscalar-baryon and pseudoscalar-baryon resonance channels via triangular loop diagrams, using Passarino-Veltman decomposition and numerical integration with a cutoff varied between 600 and 850 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Mass of the $P_s(2080)$ state: $\simeq 2070$ MeV (from the abstract) or $\simeq 2071$ MeV (from the full text), with a width of $\simeq 60-70$ MeV from the original prediction.
* The $P_s(2080)$ state lies $\simeq 8$ MeV below the $K^*\Sigma$ threshold (2085 MeV), analogous to $P_c(4450)$ being $\simeq 8$ MeV below the $\bar D^*\Sigma_c$ threshold.
* Coupling constant of $P_s(2080)$ to $K^*\Sigma$: $2.313 - i0.856$ (dimensionless, largest among all channels).
* Coupling constant to $K^*\Lambda$: $0.112 + i0.553$; to $\rho N$: $-0.231 - i0.284$; to $\omega N$: $-0.175 + i0.038$; to $\phi N$: $0.285 + i0.01$.
* In the original prediction (Khemchandani 2011), the pole was found at $\simeq 2071 - i35$ MeV (mass $\simeq 2071$ MeV, half-width $\simeq 35$ MeV, full width $\simeq 70$ MeV).
* The cutoff for loop regularization was varied in the range $600-850$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction of a $J^P = 3/2^-$ $N^*$ resonance from vector-baryon dynamics by Khemchandani (2011), which found a pole at $\simeq 2071$ MeV with large coupling to $K^*\Sigma$, analogous to the $P_c(4450)$ state discovered by LHCb. It contrasts with the work of Oset (2010) and Ramos (2013), which found spin-degenerate $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states at different masses (1977 MeV and $\simeq 2035$ MeV, respectively). The calculated decay widths to channels involving baryon resonances ($\pi N^*(1535)$, $\pi N^*(1650)$, $K\Lambda(1405)$) are shown to be comparable to ground-state channels ($\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$), suggesting that experiments measuring these final states (e.g., in $\gamma p$ or $\pi^- p$ reactions) could provide alternative evidence for the $P_s(2080)$ state.
