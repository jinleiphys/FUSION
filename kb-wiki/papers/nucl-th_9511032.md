# Signal for supernova $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos in water Čerenkov detectors

> We suggest that photons with energies between 5 and 10 MeV, generated by the ($ν,ν'pγ$) and ($ν,ν'nγ$) reactions on $^{16}$O, constitute a signal which allows a unique identification of supernova $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos in water Čerenkov detectors. We calculate the yield of such $γ$ events and estimate that a few hundred of them would be detected in Superkamiokande for a supernova at 10 kpc distance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that photons with energies between 5 and 10 MeV, produced by neutral-current neutrino reactions on oxygen-16 followed by proton or neutron emission to excited states in nitrogen-15 or oxygen-15, can serve as a unique signal for supernova muon and tau neutrinos in water Cerenkov detectors, provided the neutrino spectrum follows a Fermi-Dirac distribution with zero chemical potential and temperature of 8 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross sections for neutral-current neutrino scattering on oxygen-16 using the continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) with a finite-range residual interaction based on the Bonn potential. They then applied the statistical model code SMOKER to determine branching ratios for the decay of the excited oxygen-16 states into proton and neutron channels, including subsequent gamma emission from excited states in nitrogen-15 and oxygen-15. The neutrino flux was weighted using Fermi-Dirac distributions with two parameter sets: (T=8 MeV, mu=0) and (T=6.26 MeV, mu=3T), and the resulting gamma spectra were compared to the expected background from antineutrino-proton interactions.

## Key numbers

- Total gamma-producing cross section for each flavor of nu_x plus antinu_x in neutral current reactions on oxygen-16: 3.2 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (for FD1 spectrum with T=8 MeV, mu=0)
- For a supernova at 10 kpc, expected gamma events in Superkamiokande: approximately 360 (FD1 spectrum) or 190 (FD2 spectrum) in the energy window 5-10 MeV
- Background positron events from antinu_e + p reaction in the same energy window: approximately 270 events
- Total cross section for antinu_e + p reaction: 47 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (FD1 with T=5 MeV, mu=0) or 44 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (FD2 with T=4 MeV, mu=3T)
- Expected positron events from antinu_e + p reaction for a supernova at 10 kpc: about 4000
- Expected events from nu_x-electron scattering: about 80 events

## Context

This work extends the detection capabilities for supernova neutrinos beyond the antineutrino-electron scattering events observed from SN1987A by Kamiokande (11 events) and IMB (8 events). The proposed gamma signal provides a more unambiguous identification of muon and tau neutrinos than the previously suggested method of separating nu_x-electron scattering events by angular distributions and energy spectra (Totsuka). The calculation builds on earlier CRPA studies of neutrino reactions on oxygen-16 (Kolbe 1992) and muon capture on oxygen-16 (Kolbe 1994), and the results depend on the assumed neutrino temperature hierarchy from supernova models (Qian, Janka, Woosley).
