# $d$-dimensional Lüscher's formula and the near-threshold three-body states in a finite volume

> We study two particles colliding in a $d$-dimensional finite volume and generalize Lüscher's formula to arbitrary $d$ spatial dimensions. We obtain the $s$- and $p$-wave approximations of the generalized Lüscher's formula. For resonant $s$- or $p$-wave interactions, we analytically determine the energies of the low-lying states at large box size $L$. At $s$-wave resonance, we discover two low-lying states with nearly opposite energies, which are proportional to $\pm 1/L^{d/2}$ for $d\ge 5$, or $\pm 1/L^{2}\sqrt{\ln L}$ for $d=4$. This provides important insights into the near-threshold states of three bosons at a three-body resonance in a 2- or higher-dimensional finite volume.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Lüscher's formula to arbitrary \(d\) spatial dimensions for two particles in a finite volume, and discovers that at an \(s\)-wave resonance in \(d \ge 4\) dimensions, there exist two low-lying states with nearly opposite energies (proportional to \(\pm 1/L^{d/2}\) for \(d\ge 5\) or \(\pm 1/L^{2}\sqrt{\ln L}\) for \(d=4\)), which the abstract presents as a discovery but which is conditional on the system being at exact \(s\)-wave resonance and the box size \(L\) being large.

## Method

The authors study two particles with short-range interactions in a \(d\)-dimensional cubic box of side length \(L\) with periodic boundary conditions, using nonrelativistic quantum mechanics. They introduce a pseudo wave function satisfying the Helmholtz equation with a periodic delta-function source, formulate the problem in momentum space with a regularization factor \(\exp(-\epsilon k^2)\), and derive a quantization condition by equating the partial-wave expansion of the periodic wave function to the known free-space form. The resulting infinite-dimensional determinant condition (Eqs. (spectrum) and (matm)) is the generalized Lüscher's formula; the \(s\)-wave and \(p\)-wave approximations are obtained by truncating to a single partial wave.

## Key numbers

* For \(s\)-wave resonance in \(d=4\): \(E_{\pm} = \pm \frac{2\sqrt{2}\pi}{L^2 \ln^{1/2}(L/ 2\pi \widetilde{r}_{4,0}^{})} - \frac{\alpha_{4,1}^{}}{L^2 \ln(L/ 2\pi \widetilde{r}_{4,0}^{})} + O(1/L^2\ln^{3/2}L)\), with \(\widetilde{r}_{4,0}^{} \equiv R_{4,0}^{}\exp(\pi r_{4,0}^{}/4)\) and \(\alpha_{4,1}^{} \approx -5.54518\).
* For \(s\)-wave resonance in \(d\ge 5\): \(E_{\pm} = \pm \frac{u_{d}^{1/2}}{(-r_{d,0}^{})^{1/2}}\frac{1}{L^{d/2}} + \frac{u_{d}^{}\alpha_{d,1}^{}}{8\pi^2 r_{d,0}^{}}\frac{1}{L^{d-2}} + \cdots\), where \(u_d = 2^{d+1}\pi^{d/2-1}\Gamma(d/2)\) and \(r_{d,0}^{}<0\) for \(d\ge 5\); \(\alpha_{6,1}^{} \approx -3.37968\).
* For \(p\)-wave resonance in \(d=2\): \(E = \frac{2\pi}{L^2 \ln(L/ 2\pi \widetilde{r}_{2,1}^{})} - \frac{\alpha_{2,1}^{}}{L^2 \ln^{2}(L/ 2\pi \widetilde{r}_{2,1}^{})} + O(1/L^2\ln^{3}L)\), with \(\alpha_{2,1}^{} \approx 2.58498\).
* For \(p\)-wave resonance in \(d\ge 3\): \(E = \frac{u_{d}^{}}{-r_{d,1}^{}L^d} - \frac{u_{d}^{2}\alpha_{d,1}^{}}{4\pi^2 r_{d,1}^{2} L^{2d-2}} + \cdots\), with \(r_{d,1}^{}<0\) for \(d\ge 3\).
* For off-resonant \(s\)-wave interactions, the low-lying state energy is \(E = \frac{u_{d}^{} a_{d,0}^{}}{2 L^d}\) for \(d\ge 3\), and \(E = \frac{2\pi}{L^2\ln (L/2\pi \widetilde{a}_{2,0}^{})}\) for \(d=2\).

## Context

This work extends the original Lüscher's formula (Luscher 1986a, 1986b, 1991) from three spatial dimensions to arbitrary \(d\) dimensions, and improves upon the earlier \(s\)-wave approximation by Beane (2010) which was not convergent for \(d\ge 4\). The authors also build on the mapping suggested by Guo and Gasparian (2017) that a multiple-body problem can be mapped to a two-body problem in a higher dimension. The results enable finite-volume simulations of high-dimensional systems (e.g., in string theory) and provide analytical predictions for near-threshold three-boson states at a three-body resonance in 2D and 3D finite volumes, including Efimov trimers (Efimov 1970, 1970a), with energies scaling as \(1/L^3\) (3D) or \(1/L^2\sqrt{\ln L}\) (2D).
