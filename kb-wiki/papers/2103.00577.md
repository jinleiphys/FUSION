# Multi-particle systems on the lattice and chiral extrapolations: a brief review

> The extraction of two- and three-body hadronic scattering amplitudes and the properties of the low-lying hadronic resonances from the finite-volume energy levels in lattice QCD represents a rapidly developing field of research. The use of various modifications of the Lüscher finite-volume method has opened a path to calculate infinite-volume scattering amplitudes on the lattice. Many new results have been obtained recently for different two- and three-body scattering processes, including the extraction of resonance poles and their properties from lattice data. Such studies, however, require robust parametrizations of the infinite-volume scattering amplitudes, which rely on basic properties of $S$-matrix theory and -- preferably -- encompass systems with quark masses at and away from the physical point. Parametrizations of this kind, provided by unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory, are discussed in this review. Special attention is paid to three-body systems on the lattice, owing to the rapidly growing interest in the field. Here, we briefly survey the formalism, chiral extrapolation, as well as finite-volume analyses of lattice data.

## Key claim

The extraction of two- and three-body hadronic scattering amplitudes from finite-volume lattice QCD energy levels requires robust parametrizations of infinite-volume scattering amplitudes that rely on basic properties of S-matrix theory and preferably encompass systems with quark masses at and away from the physical point, with unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory providing such parametrizations.

## Method

The authors review the modified Inverse Amplitude Method (mIAM), which combines leading-order and next-to-leading-order chiral amplitudes with dispersion relations to construct unitary scattering amplitudes that obey chiral constraints. For three-body systems, they survey three equivalent formalisms, Relativistic Field Theory (RFT), Non-Relativistic Effective Field Theory (NREFT), and Finite Volume Unitarity (FVU), each of which derives a quantization condition relating the finite-volume three-particle spectrum to infinite-volume scattering observables by re-summing on-shell intermediate states while neglecting exponentially suppressed finite-volume corrections.

## Key numbers

* Pion-pion scattering phase shifts were analyzed at pion masses of approximately 224 MeV and 315 MeV using GWUQCD lattice data, with the mIAM global fits producing results consistent across all isospin channels.
* The three-body quantization condition in NREFT includes a hard cutoff Λ that regulates the ultraviolet behavior of spectator momenta, with the three-body kernel containing a term H₀(Λ)/Λ².
* The energy window for elastic scattering narrows when approaching lower pion masses, with simulations at physical quark masses in two- and three-particle sectors having become feasible as of the review date.
* The leading-order chiral amplitude depends on the Goldstone-boson mass M² = B(m_u + m_d) and the pion decay constant in the chiral limit f₀, with four low-energy constants l̄₁, l̄₂, l̄₃, l̄₄ entering at next-to-leading order.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Lüscher (1985, 1986, 1990) on finite-volume methods for two-body scattering and the subsequent generalizations to moving frames and coupled channels by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995), Kim, Sachrajda, and Sharpe (2005), and others. The three-body quantization condition developments build on the pioneering work of Polejaeva and Rusetsky (2012) and the three independent frameworks of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015), Hammer, Pang, and Rusetsky (2017), and Mai and Döring (2017, 2018). The review enables future combined analyses of lattice data at multiple pion masses using chiral extrapolations, particularly for three-body systems where the field is rapidly growing.
