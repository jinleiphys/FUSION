# Delbrück scattering in combined Coulomb and laser fields

> We study Delbrück scattering in a Coulomb field in the presence of a laser field. The amplitudes are calculated in the Born approximation with respect to the Coulomb field and exactly in the parameters of the laser field having arbitrary strength, spectral content and polarization. The case of high energy initial photon energy is investigated in detail for a monochromatic circularly polarized laser field. It is shown that the angular distribution of the process substantially differs from that for Delbrück scattering in a pure Coulomb field. The value of the cross section under discussion may exceed the latter at realistic laser parameters that essentially simplify the possibility of the experimental observation of the phenomenon. The effect of high order terms in the quantum intensity parameter $χ$ of the laser field is found to be very important already at relatively small $χ$.

# Delbrück scattering in combined Coulomb and laser fields

## Key claim

The cross section for Delbrück scattering in a combined Coulomb and laser field can exceed that of ordinary Delbrück scattering in a pure Coulomb field at realistic laser parameters, but only when the calculation includes high-order terms in the quantum intensity parameter χ, which are shown to be important already at relatively small χ. The angular distribution also differs substantially from the pure-Coulomb case.

## Method

The authors calculate the Delbrück scattering amplitude in the Born approximation with respect to the Coulomb field (linear in Zα) and exactly in the parameters of the laser field, using an operator technique that avoids explicit construction of the electron propagator in the external field. They derive general expressions for an arbitrary laser field, then specialize to a monochromatic circularly polarized laser field with the laser propagation direction anti-parallel to the initial photon momentum. The high-energy regime (initial photon energy ω₁ ≫ m) is analyzed in detail, with the cross section summed over final photon polarization and over the number n of exchanged laser photons.

## Key numbers

- Critical electric field: E_c = m²/|e| = 1.3 × 10¹⁶ V/cm
- For an optical laser (ω₀ = 1 eV) with intensity ~10²³ W/cm², initial photon energy ω₁ = 500 MeV, and Z = 47 (silver): χ²/(Zα)² ≈ 1.8
- At χ ≪ 1: 𝒜(χ) = 139/4050 + (7904/42525)χ²
- At χ ≫ 1: 𝒜(χ) = [3^(1/3) · 5 · Γ⁶(2/3)] / [4^(1/3) · 378 · Γ²(4/3)] · χ^(-8/3)
- 𝒜(0) = 139/4050
- Born-approximation amplitudes at χ = 0: ℱ₁(0) = 11/90, ℱ₂(0) = 1/15, ℱ₃(0) = 0
- Screening radius: r_scr ∼ Z^(-1/3)/(mα)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of photon splitting in a laser field (Di Piazza and Milstein, 2007) to the related process of Delbrück scattering in combined Coulomb and laser fields. The calculation builds on the operator technique developed by Baier, Milstein, and collaborators (1975) for polarization and mass operators in external fields. The results enable experimental observation of Delbrück scattering at realistic laser parameters, which is otherwise difficult; the authors note that photon splitting in constant or laser fields also remains challenging to measure. The Born-approximation amplitudes for n = -1 are shown to agree with prior results for photon splitting in a Coulomb field from Lee, Milstein, and collaborators (2003).
