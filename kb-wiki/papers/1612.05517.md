# Bulk viscous corrections to photon production in the quark-gluon plasma

> Photons radiated in heavy-ion collisions are a penetrating probe, and as such can play an important role in the determination of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) transport coefficients. In this work we calculate the bulk viscous correction to photon production in two-to-two scattering reactions. Phase-space integrals describing the bulk viscous correction are evaluated explicitly in order to avoid the forward scattering approximation which is shown to be poor for photons at lower energies. We furthermore present hydrodynamical simulations of AA collisions focusing on the effect of this calculation on photonic observables. Bulk corrections are shown to reduce the elliptic flow of photons at higher $p_T$.

## Key claim
Bulk viscous corrections to photon production from two-to-two scattering in the quark-gluon plasma reduce the elliptic flow of photons at higher transverse momentum, but the forward scattering approximation used in prior estimates is poor for photons at lower energies, necessitating a full phase-space integration.

## Method
The authors calculated the bulk viscous correction to the photon production rate from two-to-two scattering (Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation) by evaluating the phase-space integrals explicitly, avoiding the forward scattering approximation. They used hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed propagators for soft loop momenta and included the viscous correction to the resummed propagator, with a cutoff scale \(q_{\mathrm{cut}}\) separating hard and soft loops. The bulk viscous correction to the momentum distribution was derived using kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation for particles with thermal masses. The rates were then folded with hydrodynamic simulations using MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV in the 0-40% centrality class.

## Key numbers
- The bulk viscous correction creates a ~20% decrease in the yield for photons from 2-to-2 processes with \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV.
- The bulk viscosity peaks sharply at a temperature of \(T_c = 180\) MeV.
- At realistic values for heavy-ion collisions, \(g = 2\), the cutoff dependence of \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{bulk}}\) was minimized at roughly \(q_{\mathrm{cut}}/T = \sqrt{g}\).
- The hydrodynamic simulations used Au-Au collisions at \(200\,\mathrm{GeV}\) in a \(0-40\%\) centrality class.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of viscous corrections to photon production, complementing a previous study involving shear viscosity (Shen 2014) and improving upon rough estimates using the forward scattering approximation (Dusking 2009). The presence of bulk viscosity in QCD is demanded by heavy-ion data (Ryu 2015). This calculation enables more consistent extraction of QGP transport coefficients from electromagnetic observables, and the authors indicate that viscous corrections to all leading-order QGP channels (including bremsstrahlung, inelastic pair annihilation, and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) remain to be computed in a future publication.
