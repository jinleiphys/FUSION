# Nucleon Resonance Effects in $pp\to ppπ^0$ near Threshold

> The role of the low lying nucleon resonances beyond the $Δ(1232)$ in the reaction $pp\to ppπ^0$ near threshold is shown to be numerically significant by a calculation, which takes into account the pion re-scattering contribution described by chiral perturbation theory and the short-range mechanisms that are implied by the nucleon-nucleon interaction model. The intermediate N(1440) (P$_{11}$) resonance is excited by the short-range exchange mechanisms, while the N(1535) ($S_{11}$) and N(1520) ($D_{13}$) resonances are excited by $η$ and $ρ$ meson exchange, respectively. The $P_{11}$ increases the calculated cross section, whereas the $S_{11}$ and $D_{13}$ resonances decrease it. The calculation takes full account of the initial and final state interactions.

## Key claim

The net effect of the low-lying nucleon resonances N(1440), N(1535), and N(1520) on the cross section for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) near threshold is small, but the individual resonance contributions are not small; the small net result depends on the sign of the \(\rho NN(1520)\) coupling constant, which the data favor to be negative.

## Method

The calculation used the Bonn-B boson-exchange potential model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with full account of initial and final state interactions and exact kinematics (not the frozen kinematics approximation). The pion rescattering contribution was described by chiral perturbation theory, and the short-range mechanisms implied by the nucleon-nucleon interaction were included as axial exchange charge operators. The N(1440) resonance was excited by effective scalar and vector exchange, the N(1535) by \(\eta\)-meson exchange, and the N(1520) by \(\rho\)-meson exchange; all resonance coupling constants were determined from experimental decay widths and branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- The pseudovector coupling constant for the \(\pi NN(1440)\) vertex: \((f_{\pi NN^*}^{1440})^2/4\pi \simeq 0.01\).
- The scalar coupling constant for the \(N(1440)\): \((g_{\sigma NN^*}^{1440})^2/4\pi \simeq 0.1\), giving a ratio \(g_{\sigma NN^*}^{1440}/g_{\sigma NN} = 0.11\) (with \(g^2_{\sigma NN}/4\pi = 8.28\) in Bonn B).
- The \(\eta NN(1535)\) coupling constant: \((f_{\eta NN^*}^{1535})^2/4\pi = 0.24\), from the empirical partial decay width of 67 MeV for \(N(1535)\to N\eta\).
- The \(\pi NN(1535)\) coupling constant: \((f_{\pi NN^*}^{1535})^2/4\pi = 0.0021\), from the empirical partial decay width of 67.5 MeV for \(N(1535)\to N\pi\).
- The \(\pi NN(1520)\) coupling constant: \((f_{\pi NN^*}^{1520})^2/4\pi = 0.19\).
- The \(\rho NN(1520)\) coupling constant: \((g_{\rho NN^*}^{1520})^2/4\pi = 0.43\) (magnitude 2.3), estimated from the partial width of about 24 MeV for \(N(1520)\to N\rho\) with a kinematic probability factor of 0.076 and an effective \(\rho\) mass of 480 MeV.
- The calculation covers the near-threshold region up to about 300 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of short-range mechanisms in \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) near threshold, which had considered the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance (Van Kolk et al., Niskanen) and the N(1440) resonance (Van Rensburg). It adds the N(1535) and N(1520) resonances, excited by \(\eta\) and \(\rho\) exchange respectively, which had not been included before. The results support the view that the dominant effects are the single nucleon operator, the short-range exchange mechanisms from the nucleon-nucleon interaction (Lee et al., Mariana), and the pion exchange term from chiral perturbation theory, while the net resonance contribution is small. The calculation enables a more complete description of the reaction and highlights the need to resolve the sign disagreement between chiral perturbation theory and phenomenological meson field theory for the pion exchange amplitude.
