# Dynamics of Strangeness Production and Strange Matter Formation

> The dynamics of a hadronizing quark matter drop is investigated with a hybrid-like model, which takes into account equilibrium as well as non-equilibrium features of the process. We study the the particle rates from the hadronizing plasma, which have to be put in relation to the whole time evolution of the system. The impact of finite-size effects on the distillation process is considered. $H^0$-Dibaryon yields are estimated. Microscopic calculations of hypercluster formation at AGS and SPS are presented.

## Key claim

The dynamics of a hadronizing quark matter drop are investigated with a hybrid-like model that assumes both equilibrium (Gibbs conditions) and nonequilibrium (time-dependent freeze-out) features, but the authors note that the model relies on "oversimplifying (and to some extent controversial) assumptions," particularly that the plasma sphere is permanently surrounded by a thin hadron gas layer in perfect equilibrium.

## Method

The authors model a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplet undergoing a first-order phase transition to hadron gas, using a hybrid approach that simultaneously solves Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions and differential equations for baryon number, entropy, and net strangeness. The equation of state combines the bag model for QGP with a mixture of relativistic Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac gases of hadrons up to 2 GeV, including Hagedorn's eigenvolume correction. For hyper-cluster formation, the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD 1.0 beta) transport model is used, with Lambda pair phase space projected onto a dilambda wave function via the Wigner-function method.

## Key numbers

- For initial conditions A_B^init = 30, S/A^init = 200, f_s^init = 0.7, a cold strangelet emerges for bag constants B^(1/4) < 180 MeV, while the droplet completely hadronizes for B^(1/4) >= 180 MeV (for B^(1/4) = 210 MeV, specific entropy S/A decreases from 200 to 100).
- For initial conditions S/A^init = 200, f_s^init = 0, B^(1/4) = 160 MeV, the baryon density increases by more than one order of magnitude; the chemical potential rises from mu^i = 16 MeV to mu^f > 200 MeV, and the strange quark chemical potential from mu_s^i = 0 to mu_s^f ≈ 400 MeV.
- For a bag constant B^(1/4) = 145 MeV with finite-size curvature corrections, a quark blob with initial net baryon number A_B^init = 30 completely hadronizes, in contrast to calculations without such corrections.
- The H^0 Dibaryon yields are of the same order as anti-Omega yields when the H^0 mass is m_H0 ≈ 2020 MeV (for B^(1/4) = 160 MeV).
- Multiplicities of Lambdas plus Sigma^0 in inelastic p+p reactions are 0.088 ± 0.003 at 14.6 GeV/c and 0.234 ± 0.005 at 200 GeV/c.
- The Lambda/Lambda-Lambda ratio is approximately 100 in central heavy-ion collisions at AGS and SPS energies.

## Context

This work extends the strangelet distillation mechanism proposed by Greiner and collaborators (1994) and the concept of metastable exotic multi-strange objects (MEMO's) from Schaffner et al. (1992). It also addresses finite-size effects on the distillation process, which were previously neglected, and provides microscopic calculations of hyper-cluster formation using URQMD that can be compared to experimental searches by the E864, E878, and NA52 collaborations at AGS and SPS. The results challenge the assumption that final particle yields reflect thermodynamic properties at a single stage of evolution, and suggest that time-dependent particle rates (e.g., Lambda vs. anti-Lambda emission at different stages) must be considered when interpreting experimental data.
