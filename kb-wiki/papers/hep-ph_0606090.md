# Chemical composition of the decaying glasma

> The the initial stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision can be described by a classical color field configuration known as the Glasma. The production of quark pairs from this background field is then computed nonperturbatively by numerically solving the Dirac equation in the classical background. The result seems to point towards an early chemical equilibration of the plasma.

## Key claim

The numerical computation of quark pair production from the classical glasma background field suggests that a large number of quark pairs may be present already in the initial state of a heavy ion collision, potentially leading to early chemical equilibration of quark and gluon degrees of freedom; however, the author notes that this conclusion requires a computation of gluon production to the same order in the coupling, which has not yet been done.

## Method

The author computes quark pair production nonperturbatively by numerically solving the 3+1-dimensional Dirac equation in a classical color field background obtained from the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The classical background fields are generated by solving the Yang-Mills equations on a lattice with stochastic Gaussian color charge densities as sources. The Dirac equation is solved forward in time from an analytically derived initial condition at proper time tau=0, and the amplitude for producing a quark pair is obtained by projecting the evolved solution onto positive energy plane wave states at late times.

## Key numbers

- For a classical color charge density of g^2 mu = 2 GeV (corresponding to approximately 1000 gluons per unit rapidity), the result suggests that these 1000 particles could consist of 400 gluons, 300 quarks, and 300 antiquarks (multiplying by N_f = 3), which is close to the thermal ratio N_q/N_g = 9 N_f/32.
- The antiquark transverse momentum spectrum is shown for different values of the classical color charge density g^2 mu with quark mass m = 0.3 GeV, with the spectrum following approximately e^{-q_T/(g^2 mu)}.
- The antiquark spectrum as a function of transverse mass sqrt(q_T^2 + m^2) is shown for different masses and g^2 mu = 1 GeV.
- The amplitude is shown as a function of time for g^2 mu = 2 GeV and g^2 mu = 1 GeV, demonstrating that the amplitude is close to its final value for very early times close to the light cone.

## Context

This work extends the classical field description of the initial stage of heavy ion collisions (the Glasma, introduced by Lappi (2006)) by computing quark pair production beyond the lowest-order gluon production calculations of Krasnitz et al. (1998-2001) and Lappi (2003). The method follows the approach of Gelis et al. (2004) for solving the Dirac equation in a classical background, and the results point toward a scenario where chemical equilibration between quarks and gluons may occur much earlier than conventionally assumed. The author notes that a full understanding requires a consistent computation of gluon pair production to the same order, which has not yet been formulated, and that the numerical method does not yet allow extension to large quark masses or transverse momenta.
