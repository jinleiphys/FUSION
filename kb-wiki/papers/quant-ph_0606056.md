# Application of a renormalization algorithm in Hilbert space to the study of many-body quantum systems

> We implement an algorithm which is aimed to reduce the number of basis states spanning the Hilbert space of quantum many-body systems. We test the efficiency of the procedure by working out and analyzing the spectral properties of strongly correlated and frustrated quantum spin systems. The role and importance of symmetries are investigated

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Hilbert-space renormalization algorithm, which reduces basis size while renormalizing coupling constants, can preserve ground-state and low-lying excited-state energies of frustrated quantum spin ladders, but its efficiency depends strongly on the relative coupling strengths and the chosen symmetry representation of the basis.

## Method

The authors implement an iterative reduction algorithm that eliminates one basis state at a time, ordered by increasing diagonal matrix elements, and renormalizes a single coupling constant (the rung coupling \(J_t\)) to keep the ground-state energy fixed. At each step, the ground-state energy and eigenvector are obtained via the Lanczos algorithm, and the new coupling constant is determined from a second-order algebraic equation derived from the Feshbach projection formalism. The procedure is tested on frustrated two-leg spin-1/2 ladders with Hamiltonians containing rung, leg, and diagonal couplings, using both an \(SU(2)\) basis (product states of individual spins with total magnetization \(M_{tot}=0\)) and an \(SO(4)\) basis (states built from rung singlet-triplet pairs).

## Key numbers

* For a ladder with \(L=6\) sites per leg, \(J_t=15\), \(J_l=5\), \(J_c=3\) in the \(SU(2)\) basis: the ground state remains stable down to a reduced space dimension of \(n \sim 50\) from an initial dimension \(N=924\).
* For the same system (\(L=6\), \(J_t=15\), \(J_l=5\), \(J_c=3\)) in the \(SU(2)\) basis: the percentage deviation \(p(1)\) for the ground-state energy per site is about \(0.8\%\) when the reduced space dimension ratio is \(n/N \sim 0.07\).
* For a larger ladder with \(L=8\) sites per leg, \(J_t=15\), \(J_l=5\), \(J_c=3\) in the \(SU(2)\) basis: \(p(1) \sim 0.8\%\) when \(n/N \sim 0.007\) (initial dimension \(N=12870\)), showing improved stability with larger initial space.
* For \(L=6\), \(J_t=5.5\), \(J_l=5\), \(J_c=3\) in the \(SU(2)\) basis: the ground state is stable down to \(n \sim 100\) from \(N=924\).
* For \(L=6\), \(J_t=2.5\), \(J_l=5\), \(J_c=3\) in the \(SU(2)\) basis: destabilization of excited states occurs at higher reduced dimensions than for larger \(J_t\), with entropy per site larger at \(n=N\) and decreasing more rapidly.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed non-perturbative Hilbert-space reduction method (Khalil and Richert, 2004) by testing it on frustrated quantum spin ladders, a system where perturbative approaches fail. It contrasts with methods like the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) of White (1992) and the Real Space Renormalization Group (RSRG), which operate in ordinary space and are primarily suited to one-dimensional lattice systems, whereas the present algorithm works directly in Hilbert space and is claimed to be applicable to any microscopic quantum system. The results enable future application to two-dimensional systems, finite-temperature systems, and the identification of critical points via the renormalization flow.
