# Holographic cold dense matter constrained by neutron stars

> The equation of state (EoS) for cold dense matter inside neutron stars is investigated by using holographic QCD models in the framework of the Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) system and the improved Karch-Katz-Son-Stephanov (KKSS) action for matter part. This method of describing holographic nuclear matter in the EMD$+$KKSS framework is different from that by using the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action and the Chern-Simons (CS) terms. Combining with the Hebeler-Lattimer-Pethick-Schwenk (HLPS) intermediate equation of state (EoS), the hybrid EoS inside the neutron stars is constructed. The obtained hybrid EoS is located in the range that is defined by the low-density chiral effective theory, the high-density perturbative QCD, and the polytropic interpolations between them, and is constrained by the astrophysics observations. The square of the sound velocity reaches a maximum value larger than $0.8$ in the region of $2-5$ times the saturation baryon number density and approaches the conformal limit at the high baryon density range. The mass-radius relation and the tidal deformability of the neutron stars are in agreement with astrophysical measurements. The possible maximum mass for the neutron star is about $2.5 M_{\odot}$ and the radius is about $12 \mathrm{km}$ then. It is noticed that the holographic quark matter branch in the mass-radius relation is always unstable and the holographic nuclear matter can produce a stable branch. These results indicate that even in the core of the NS, the matter is still in the confinement phase and the quark matter is not favored.

## Key claim

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state (EoS) for cold dense matter inside neutron stars using a holographic QCD framework that treats hadrons via the improved Karch-Katz-Son-Stephanov (KKSS) action rather than the topological soliton description of baryons, and they find that the holographic quark matter branch in the mass-radius relation is always unstable while the holographic nuclear matter branch is stable, indicating that even in the neutron star core the matter remains in the confinement phase and quark matter is not favored.

## Method

The authors solve the Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) system in five dimensions to derive the background thermodynamics and confinement-deconfinement phase diagram, then introduce the improved KKSS action as a probe to calculate the EoS of holographic nuclear matter. They combine this with the Hebeler-Lattimer-Pethick-Schwenk (HLPS) intermediate EoS to construct a hybrid EoS for cold dense matter, and use this hybrid EoS to compute the mass-radius relation and tidal deformability of neutron stars, comparing with astrophysical observations.

## Key numbers

- The square of the sound velocity reaches a maximum value larger than 0.8 in the region of 2-5 times the saturation baryon number density
- The possible maximum mass for the neutron star is about 2.5 solar masses (2.5 M⊙)
- The radius at maximum mass is about 12 km
- The baryon number density in the neutron star core can reach as high as 5 nS to 10 nS, where nS is the nuclear saturation density

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to neutron star matter by using the improved KKSS action for hadrons rather than the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action and Chern-Simons (CS) terms used in prior works by Ishii (2019), Jokela (2020), Jarvinen (2021), and Kovensky (2021). The hybrid EoS is constrained by the low-density chiral effective theory, high-density perturbative QCD, and polytropic interpolations between them, and is compared with astrophysical observations including mass measurements from Shapiro delay (Demorest 2010, Fonseca 2016, Antoniadis 2013, NANOGrav 2019, Fonseca 2021), radius measurements from NICER (Miller 2019, Riley 2019, Miller 2021, Riley 2021), and tidal deformability from gravitational wave events GW170817 (LIGO Scientific 2018) and GW190814 (LIGO Scientific 2020). The results enable further investigation of whether quark matter can exist inside neutron star cores.
