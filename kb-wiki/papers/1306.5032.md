# ψ^\prime Production and B Decay in Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC

> In comparison with J/ψ, the excited charmonium state ψ^\prime is loosely bounded and its yield is dominantly from the B-hadron decay. Based on the transport approach, we study the double ratio of N(ψ^\prime)/N(J/ψ) from A+A collisions to that from p+p collisions at LHC energy. We found that the primordial production in the initial stage and the regeneration in the hot medium are not significant for ψ^\prime production in heavy ion collisions at LHC and the double ratio in semi-central and central collisions is controlled by the B decay.

## Key claim

The double ratio of ψ′ to J/ψ in semi-central and central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC is controlled by B-hadron decay contributions rather than by the Debye screening picture that would predict a monotonic drop with centrality; the prompt ψ′ production and regeneration are not significant at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors solved a Boltzmann-type transport equation for prompt charmonia in a hydrodynamically evolving QGP, including gluon dissociation (loss term) and regeneration from thermalized charm quarks (gain term) with detailed balance. They then added the non-prompt (B-decay) contribution using the ratio of non-prompt to prompt yields extracted from p+p data at √sNN = 1.96 and 2.76 TeV, and a bottom-quark quench factor Q extracted from CMS data or estimated from model calculations. The inclusive double ratio was computed as a function of the number of participant nucleons Np for several transverse momentum and rapidity bins.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark production cross section in p+p collisions: dσppc̄c/dy = 0.38 mb in forward rapidity and 0.62 mb in central rapidity at LHC energy.
- Non-prompt to prompt ratios (rBJ/ψ, rBψ′) used: (0.27, 0.38) for 6.5 < pt < 30 GeV/c, |y| < 1.6; (0.16, 0.26) for 3 < pt < 8 GeV/c, 2.5 < y < 4; (0.1, 0.2) for 0 < pt < 3 GeV/c, 2.5 < y < 4; (0.17, 0.28) for 3 < pt < 30 GeV/c, 1.6 < y < 2.4.
- Bottom quark quench factor Q: 0.35 for 6.5 < pt < 30 GeV/c, |y| < 1.6 (extracted from CMS data); Q = 1 for intermediate pt regions; Q = 1.4 for low pt region 0 < pt < 3 GeV/c.
- Nuclear absorption cross sections at SPS energy: σabs = 4.3 mb for J/ψ, 4.3 mb for χc, and 7.9 mb for ψ′.
- The prompt double ratio in central and semi-central collisions is a small value around 0.1–0.2.
- For the region 3 < pt < 30 GeV/c and 1.6 < y < 2.4, the model with maximum B decay contribution (Q = 2.3) gives RAAψ′/RAAJ/ψ = 1.3 at Np = 300, still below the data lower limit.

## Context

This work extends the transport approach previously applied to J/ψ at RHIC and LHC (Yan et al., Liu et al., Zhou et al.) to the excited state ψ′, which has a lower dissociation temperature (only slightly above Tc) and negligible feed-down from heavier states. The calculation contradicts the simple Debye screening expectation (Matsui and Satz, 1986) that the double ratio should vanish in central collisions; instead, the LHC data from ALICE and CMS show a non-zero, centrality-independent double ratio that the authors attribute to B-decay feed-down. The model successfully describes the double ratio at SPS energy (NA50 data) and at RHIC (where regeneration is important but B decay is small), but fails to reproduce the CMS data in the region 3 < pt < 30 GeV/c and 1.6 < y < 2.4, indicating the need for further theoretical study and more precise measurements.
