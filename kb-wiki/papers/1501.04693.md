# Fluctuating Heavy Quark Energy Loss in Strongly-Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Results from an energy loss model that includes thermal fluctuations in the energy loss for heavy quarks in a strongly-coupled plasma are shown to be qualitatively consistent with single particle data from both RHIC and LHC. The model used is the first to properly include the fluctuations in heavy quark energy loss as derived in string theory and that do not obey the usual fluctuation-dissipation relations. These fluctuations are crucial for simultaneously describing both RHIC and LHC data; leading order drag results without fluctuations are falsified by current data. Including the fluctuations is non-trivial and relies on the Wong-Zakai theorem to fix the numerical Langevin implementation. The fluctuations lead to surprising results: B meson anisotropy is similar to that for D mesons at LHC, and the double ratio of D to B meson nuclear modification factors approaches unity more rapidly than even predictions from perturbative energy loss models. It is clear that future work in improving heavy quark energy loss calculations in AdS/CFT to include all energy loss channels is needed and warranted.

# Fluctuating Heavy Quark Energy Loss in Strongly-Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
When thermal fluctuations in heavy quark energy loss (derived from string theory and not obeying the usual fluctuation-dissipation relations) are correctly included via the Wong-Zakai theorem, the strongly-coupled AdS/CFT energy loss model becomes qualitatively consistent with single particle data from both RHIC and LHC, whereas leading order drag results without fluctuations are falsified by current data. The model is the first to properly include these fluctuations, but the agreement holds only within the regime of applicability where the heavy quark speed remains below a critical limit set by the setup.

## Method
The author implemented a fully Monte-Carlo energy loss model solving the Langevin equation for heavy quarks (charm and bottom) propagating through 2+1D VISHNU hydrodynamics backgrounds. The stochastic differential equation was solved numerically using the Euler-Maruyama scheme with the Itô interpretation, converted from the Stratonovich form required by the Wong-Zakai theorem for colored noise approximated as white noise. Heavy quark production spectra were sampled from FONLL calculations, fragmentation to D mesons, B mesons, and electrons was handled through numerical integration of analytic expressions, and the model used two parameter mappings from QCD to N=4 SYM: one with alpha_s = 0.3 and T_SYM = T_QCD, and one with lambda = 5.5 and T_SYM = T_QCD / 3^(1/4).

## Key numbers
- Drag coefficient: mu = (pi sqrt(lambda) T^2) / (2 M_Q), where M_Q is heavy quark mass, T is plasma temperature, lambda is 't Hooft coupling
- Transverse momentum diffusion coefficient: kappa_T = pi sqrt(lambda) T^3 gamma^(1/2)
- Longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficient: kappa_L = gamma^2 kappa_T
- Critical speed from fluctuations: gamma less than or approximately equal to M_Q^2 / (4 T^2)
- Speed limit from setup: gamma less than (1 + 2 M_Q / (sqrt(lambda) T))^2, approximately 4 M_Q^2 / (lambda T^2)
- Heavy quark masses used: m_c = 1.5 GeV/c^2, m_b = 4.75 GeV/c^2
- Time step used: dt = 1/150 x mu_max, where mu_max is drag coefficient at fireball center at thermalization time
- Cutoff for unphysical quarks: 1000 GeV/c, with lost quarks at O(10^{-4}%)
- Drag-only model oversuppressed D meson R_AA at LHC by a factor of approximately 5 compared to data
- Double ratio of D to B meson R_AA predicted to exceed 1 at p_T approximately 7 GeV/c when fluctuations are included

## Context
This work extends the leading-order heavy quark drag calculations from AdS/CFT by Gubser (2006), Herzog (2006), and Casalderrey-Solana (2006), which oversuppressed high-p_T particles compared to data from RHIC and LHC. It corrects the earlier fluctuation treatment by Akamatsu (2008) that used fluctuation-dissipation theorem relations, which are not valid for the string-theory-derived fluctuations shown by Gubser (2006) to be non-Markovian and colored. The results enable future work to include additional energy loss channels such as Larmor-like radiation and full autocorrelations in fluctuations, and suggest that B meson R_AA(p_T) alone may be the best observable to distinguish between strong-coupling and pQCD-based energy loss mechanisms.
