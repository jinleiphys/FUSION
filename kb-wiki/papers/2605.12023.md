# Mass radius and D-term of atomic nuclei in relativistic mean field theory

> Based on relativistic mean field theory for atomic nuclei, we compute the mass radius and other radii associated with the energy momentum tensor for dozens of spin-0 nuclei across the nuclear chart. We also compute the D-term of these nuclei, the forward limit of the gravitational form factor $D(t=0)=D$. The dependence on the neutron number $N$ is systematically studied for calcium (Ca), nickel (Ni), zirconium (Zr), tin (Sn) and lead (Pb) isotopes. Remarkably, $|D|$ does not monotonically increase with $N$. Instead, it exhibits local maxima and minima when $N$ equals a magic number and even a sub-magic number. This results in characteristic kinks in the mass, scalar, tensor and shear radii of these isotopes. Our work for the first time elucidates the strong sensitivity of the various mechanical properties of nuclei to the nuclear shell structure.

# Mass radius and D-term of atomic nuclei in relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The D-term magnitude |D| does not monotonically increase with neutron number N across isotopic chains; instead it exhibits local maxima and minima when N equals a magic number or even a sub-magic number, revealing strong sensitivity of mechanical properties to nuclear shell structure that contradicts the simple liquid-drop scaling D ∝ A^β.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) code DIRHBS with the DD-ME2 parameter set to solve the Dirac equation and meson field equations self-consistently for spherical even-even nuclei. From the resulting nucleon wave functions and meson fields, they constructed the Belinfante-improved energy-momentum tensor and computed its various spatial moments, including the mass radius, scalar radius, tensor radius, shear radius, and the D-term via the pressure integral D = M ∫ d³r r² p(r). The Coulomb field was omitted to avoid infrared divergences in the radii and D-term integrals.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb: r_em = 5.323 fm, r_m = 5.459 fm, r_shear = 5.926 fm, D = -25374.209 (dimensionless)
- For ¹⁶O: r_em = 2.685 fm, r_m = 2.574 fm, r_shear = 2.886 fm, D = -90.723
- For ⁴⁰Ca: r_em = 3.387 fm, r_m = 3.303 fm, r_shear = 3.766 fm, D = -930.453
- For ⁶⁸Ni: r_em = 3.800 fm, r_m = 3.874 fm, r_shear = 4.323 fm, D = -1903.734
- For ⁹⁰Zr: r_em = 4.153 fm, r_m = 4.170 fm, r_shear = 4.586 fm, D = -4123.965
- For ¹³²Sn: r_em = 4.591 fm, r_m = 4.739 fm, r_shear = 4.973 fm, D = -4192.501
- The ordering of radii for all nuclei studied is r_B < r_t < r_m < r_s < r_shear, with r_shear approximately 10% larger than the others
- The D-term of ⁹⁰Zr and ¹³²Sn differ by less than 2% despite their mass numbers differing by almost 50%

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of gravitational form factors for nuclei using the Skyrme model by Garcia-Martin-Caro et al. (2023) and the original Walecka model by Guzey and Siddikov (2005). The RMF approach works better for heavier nuclei (A ≳ 16) than the Skyrme model, which becomes numerically difficult for A ∼ O(100). The results reveal that the simple power-law scaling D ∝ A^β (with β ≈ 7/3 in the liquid drop model, β ≈ 2.26 in the Walecka model, and β ≈ 1.9 in the Skyrme model) fails to capture the strong shell-structure dependence. The kinks observed in the mass radius and shear radius at magic numbers are analogous to well-known kinks in charge radius isotope shifts, but this is the first observation of such structure in mechanical radii. Future work could explore the microscopic explanation of why maxima and minima in |D| alternate from one magic number to another.
