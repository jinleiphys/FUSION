# Wavelet-Harmonic Integration Methods

> A new integration method drastically improves the efficiency of the dark matter direct detection calculation. In this work I introduce a complete, orthogonal basis of spherical wavelet-harmonic functions, designed for the new vector space integration method. This factorizes the numeric calculation into a ``vector'' that depends only on the astrophysical velocity distribution; a second vector, depending only on the detector form factor; and a scattering matrix defined on the basis functions, which depends on the details of the dark matter (DM) particle model (e.g.~its mass). For common spin-independent DM--Standard Model interactions, this scattering matrix can be evaluated analytically in the wavelet-harmonic basis. This factorization is particularly helpful for the more complicated analyses that have become necessary in recent years, especially those involving anisotropic detector materials or more realistic models of the local DM velocity distribution. With the new method, analyses studying large numbers of detector orientations and DM particle models can be performed more than 10~million times faster. This paper derives several analytic results for the spherical wavelets, including an extrapolation in the space of wavelet coefficients, and a generalization of the vector space method to a much broader class of linear functional integrals. Both results are highly relevant outside the field of DM direct detection.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a complete, orthogonal basis of spherical wavelet-harmonic functions that factorizes the dark matter direct detection scattering rate calculation into independent components, enabling analyses of large numbers of detector orientations and DM particle models to be performed more than 10 million times faster, but only for spin-independent DM–Standard Model interactions where the scattering matrix can be evaluated analytically in this basis.

## Method

The author projects the DM velocity distribution and the detector response function onto separate bases of orthogonal spherical wavelet-harmonic functions, then evaluates the scattering integral analytically for every pair of basis functions to generate a scattering matrix that depends only on the DM particle model parameters. The scattering rate is then computed by multiplying the vectorized velocity distribution coefficients by the scattering matrix and then by the vectorized detector form factor coefficients, with detector rotations implemented via matrix multiplication on the spherical harmonic components.

## Key numbers

- The new method enables analyses to be performed more than 10 million times faster than standard approaches
- The width of the nth spherical wavelet is proportional to 1/n in the large n limit
- The Earth's velocity changes by approximately ±15 km/s over the course of a year
- Standard Halo Model parameters: dispersion velocity vσ ≈ 220–240 km/s, escape velocity vesc ≈ 544–600 km/s
- For nuclear scattering, the reference momentum transfer q0 is typically set to mχv0 with v0 ≈ 220–240 km/s

## Context

This work extends the vector space integration method introduced in a companion paper (Lillard 2025) by developing the specific spherical wavelet-harmonic basis functions that make the approach practical. The method addresses the computational bottleneck identified in prior work on anisotropic detector materials (Catena 2015, Hochberg 2016, Budnik 2017, Coskuner 2019, Griffin 2020, Blanco 2021, Boyd 2022) and enables rigorous uncertainty propagation from astrophysics (Wu 2019, Radick 2020, Maity 2020, Buckley 2022) and detector physics (Knapen 2021, Griffin 2021) into rate predictions, as well as halo-independent analyses of DM particle model parameters (Fox 2010, Frandsen 2011, Gondolo 2012, Kavanagh 2013).
