# Confronting hydrodynamic predictions with Xe-Xe data

> Comparing collision systems of different size, at near the same collision energy, offers us the opportunity to probe the scaling behavior and therefore the nature of the system itself. Recently, we made predictions for Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV using viscous hydrodynamic simulations, noting that the scaling from the larger Pb-Pb system is rather generic, and arguing that robust predictions can be made that do not depend on details of the model. Here we confront our predictions with measurements that were subsequently made in a short Xe-Xe run at the LHC by the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations. We find that the predictions are largely confirmed. Of particular interest is a strong indication of a non-spherical shape for the $^{129}$Xe nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors' hydrodynamic predictions for Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV are largely confirmed by subsequent ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS measurements, with the strong increase of elliptic flow in central collisions providing a distinct indication of a non-spherical (deformed) shape for the 129Xe nucleus, though the authors note that their model has a ratio v2{4}/v2{2} that is slightly too small in both systems.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the v-USPhydro code with TRENTo initial conditions (parameters p=0, k=1.6, σ=0.51 fm), assuming longitudinal boost invariance, shear viscosity η/s=0.047, and a lattice QCD equation of state with freeze-out at 150 MeV. They computed flow harmonics v2, v3, v4 and their cumulants for both Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe systems, then compared the ratios of these observables between the two systems to subsequent experimental measurements from the LHC collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Predicted increase in triangular flow v3 in Xe-Xe collisions of approximately 15% in central collisions and a decrease of approximately 10% in collisions at 50-60% centrality, validated by subsequent measurements
- Expected increase in fluctuations scaling as (129/208)^{-1/2} ~ 1.27 due to the smaller Xe system size compared to Pb
- Mean transverse momentum expected to change by less than 2% between Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb systems
- Collision energy for Xe-Xe beams: √sNN = 5.44 TeV, compared to Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic scaling predictions made by Giacalone et al. (2017) for Xe-Xe collisions and confronts them with subsequent measurements from the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations at the LHC. The results validate the standard hydrodynamic picture across different system sizes and provide the first experimental evidence for a deformed 129Xe nucleus, which was previously expected from interpolation of nearby isotopes and theoretical calculations by Moller et al. (2015). Additional predictions and comparisons using another hydrodynamic model were made by Eskola et al. (2017) and Niemi et al. (2018).
