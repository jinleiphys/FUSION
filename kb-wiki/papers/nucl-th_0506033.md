# Electromagnetic Form Factors and the Hypercentral CQM

> New results about the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are obtained with a semirelativistic version of the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM) and a relativistic current. The complex structure of the constituent quarks is taken into account implicitly by means of phenomenological constituent quark form factors. We obtain a detailed reproduction of the experimental data up to $5 GeV^2$, moreover our findings about constituent quark root mean square radii are of the same order than the recent ones obtained analyzing the proton structure functions.

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), when extended with a semirelativistic Hamiltonian and a relativistic current, can reproduce nucleon electromagnetic form factor data up to 5 GeV², but only if phenomenological constituent quark form factors are introduced to account for the composite nature of the quarks and residual relativistic effects not explicitly included.

## Method

The authors solved a semirelativistic Hamiltonian with relativistic kinetic energy and a linear-plus-Coulomb hypercentral potential using a variational method in the hyperspherical formalism. They constructed nucleon states in a Bakamjian-Thomas point-form approach, applied canonical boosts to the Breit frame, and calculated elastic form factors from the matrix elements of a single-quark current in impulse approximation. Phenomenological constituent quark form factors (monopole and dipole combinations) were then fitted to reproduce the experimental data for G_M^p, G_M^n, G_E^n, and the ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m = 100 MeV (from the semirelativistic Hamiltonian fit)
- Potential parameters: α = 1.61 fm⁻² and τ = 4.59 (from the nonrelativistic spectrum fit)
- Data reproduction achieved up to Q² = 5 GeV²
- The nonrelativistic hCQM calculation predicted a zero in the proton electric form factor at Q² ≈ 6 GeV² (citing Frank et al., 1996)
- The Skyrme soliton model predicted a zero in G_E^p at Q² ≈ 10 GeV² (citing Holzwarth, 1996)
- The MIT Bag model predicted a sign change in the ratio G_E^p/G_M^p at Q² = 1.5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonrelativistic hCQM calculations (De Sanctis et al., 1999) by introducing a semirelativistic Hamiltonian and exact boosts, improving upon the earlier expansion in quark momenta. It builds on the approach of Iachello et al. (1973) who used vector meson dominance with an intrinsic form factor, and parallels the light-cone constituent quark model of Cardarelli and Simula (2000) and the point-form dynamics of the Pavia-Graz group (Wagenbrunn et al., 2000; Boffi et al., 2001). The results enable further investigation of the internal structure of constituent quarks and the role of relativistic effects in nucleon form factors, with the constituent quark form factors providing a phenomenological bridge to more fundamental descriptions.
