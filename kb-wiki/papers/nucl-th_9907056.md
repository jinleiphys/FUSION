# Generalized polarizabilities and electroexcitation of the nucleon

> Generalized nucleon polarizabilities for virtual photons can be defined in terms of electroproduction cross sections as function of the 4-momentum transfer $Q^2$. In particular, the sum of the generalized electric and magnetic polarizabilities $Σ=α+β$ and the spin polarizability $γ$ can be expressed by virtual photon absorption cross sections integrated over the excitation energy. These quantities have been calculated within the framework of the recently developed unitary isobar model for pion photo- and electroproduction on the proton, which describes the available experimental data up to an excitation energy of about 1 GeV. Our results have been compared to the predictions of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim
The generalized electric+magnetic polarizability Σ(Q²) and the spin polarizability γ(Q²) can be calculated from virtual-photon absorption cross sections integrated over excitation energy, using the Unitary Isobar Model (UIM) for pion electroproduction on the proton up to about 1 GeV. The UIM predicts that γ(Q²) does **not** cross zero for Q² ≤ 1.0 (GeV)², in contrast to the zero-crossing at Q² ∼ 0.4 (GeV)² predicted by relativistic chiral perturbation theory (ChPT).

## Method
The authors applied the Unitary Isobar Model (UIM), an effective phenomenological Lagrangian with Born terms, t-channel vector-meson exchange, and the dominant nucleon resonances (P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535), F15(1680), D33(1700)) up to the third resonance region. They computed the transverse cross section σ_T and the helicity-difference cross section (σ_{1/2} − σ_{3/2}) for single-pion, η, and multipion electroproduction, then integrated these over excitation energy to obtain Σ(Q²) and γ(Q²) as functions of the four-momentum transfer Q².

## Key numbers
- At the real-photon point (Q²=0), the UIM gives for the proton: α^p+β^p ≈ 14.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, consistent with the existing sum-rule analysis of (14.3 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- For the neutron at Q²=0: α^n+β^n ≈ 15.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, compared to the literature value (15.8 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- The UIM spin polarizability at Q²=0: γ^p ≈ −0.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ and γ^n ≈ 0 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴.
- The UIM predicts that γ^p changes sign at Q² ∼ 1.4 (GeV)² and γ^n at Q² ∼ 2 (GeV)², whereas relativistic ChPT predicts a zero-crossing at Q² ∼ 0.4 (GeV)².
- The background contribution to Σ^p at Q²=0 is 50% larger than the pion-loop contribution of ChPT, while the Δ(1232) contribution (after interference) is only 25% of the ChPT Δ-pole value.

## Context
This work extends the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and Baldin’s sum rule from real photons (Q²=0) to virtual photons (Q²>0), using the UIM previously developed for electroproduction (Drechsel et al., 1999). It directly contradicts the predictions of relativistic chiral perturbation theory (Edelmann, Kaiser, Piller, and Weise, 1997) regarding the sign and slope of γ(Q²) at low Q², and it disagrees with the heavy-baryon ChPT result for γ^p (which has opposite sign). The results provide testable predictions for upcoming polarized electroproduction experiments at Jefferson Lab, ELSA, and MAMI, and they constrain models of the nucleon’s low-energy spin structure in the nonperturbative QCD regime.
