# Folding model analysis of proton scattering from $^{18,20,22}$O nuclei

> The elastic and inelastic proton scattering on $^{18,20,22}$O nuclei are studied in a folding model formalism of nucleon-nucleus optical potential and inelastic form factor. The DDM3Y effective interaction is used and the ground state densities are obtained in continuum Skyrme-HFB approach. A semi-microscopic approach of collective form factors is done to extract the deformation parameters from inelastic scattering analysis while the microscopic approach uses the continuum QRPA form factors. Implications of the values of the deformation parameters, neutron and proton transition moments for the nuclei are discussed. The p-analyzing powers on $^{18,20,22}$O nuclei are also predicted in the same framework.

# Folding model analysis of proton scattering from $^{18,20,22}$O nuclei

## Key claim

The folding model using DDM3Y effective interaction with continuum Skyrme-HFB ground state densities and QRPA transition densities satisfactorily reproduces elastic and inelastic proton scattering data on $^{18,20,22}$O, but the extracted deformation parameters and neutron-to-proton transition moment ratios depend on the choice of collective versus microscopic form factors and show that $^{20}$O has a disproportionately high neutron contribution to the $2^+_1$ excitation relative to its $N/Z$ ratio.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic proton scattering angular distributions for $^{18,20,22}$O at 43, 43, and 46.6A MeV respectively were analyzed using a single folding model with the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Ground state densities were obtained from continuum Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations, and transition densities from continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). Both a semi-microscopic collective vibrational model (deforming the optical potential) and a fully microscopic approach using QRPA form factors were employed within the DWBA formalism, with distorted waves generated from best-fit optical potentials; coupled channel calculations were also performed using the code CHUCK3.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter $\beta$ for $^{18}$O $2^+_1$ (1.98 MeV): 0.33 at 43A MeV
- Deformation parameter $\beta$ for $^{20}$O $2^+_1$ (1.67 MeV): 0.46 at 43A MeV
- Deformation parameter $\beta$ for $^{22}$O $2^+_1$ (3.17 MeV): 0.26 at 46.6A MeV
- $B(E2)$ for $^{18}$O: 45.1 $\pm$ 2.0 e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2)$ for $^{20}$O: 28.1 $\pm$ 2.0 e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2)$ for $^{22}$O: 21.0 $\pm$ 8.0 e$^2$fm$^4$
- $M_n/M_p$ ratio for $^{18}$O $2^+_1$: 1.12 (experimental: 1.10 $\pm$ 0.24)
- $M_n/M_p$ ratio for $^{20}$O $2^+_1$: 3.34 (experimental: 3.25 $\pm$ 0.80)
- $M_n/M_p$ ratio for $^{22}$O $2^+_1$: 2.00 (experimental: 2.50 $\pm$ 1.00)
- Volume integral per nucleon of the real folded potential: -470.3 MeV fm$^3$ for $^{18}$O, -457.6 MeV fm$^3$ for $^{20}$O, -371.8 MeV fm$^3$ for $^{22}$O
- Renormalization factors: $N_R$ = 0.90 ($^{18}$O), 0.88 ($^{20}$O), 0.73 ($^{22}$O); $N_I$ = 0.08 for all three nuclei

## Context

This work extends earlier folding model analyses of proton scattering on oxygen isotopes by Khan (2000) and Khoa (2003), resolving a previous discrepancy of about 50% at the first diffraction minimum for $^{20}$O elastic scattering. The results confirm the strong neutron contribution to the $2^+_1$ state in $^{20}$O reported by Jewell (1999) and provide predictions for analyzing powers on $^{18,20,22}$O at these energies. The study enables future comparisons with experimental analyzing power data and demonstrates that microscopic QRPA transition densities can be used alongside collective models to extract neutron and proton transition moments in neutron-rich nuclei approaching the drip line at A=24 (Tarasov 1997, Sakurai 1999).
