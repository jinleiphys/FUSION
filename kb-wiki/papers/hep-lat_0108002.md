# Parton distributions from lattice QCD

> We extract the x dependence of the nonsinglet u-d distribution function in the nucleon from the lowest few moments calculated on the lattice, using an extrapolation formula which ensures the correct behavior in the chiral and heavy quark limits. We discuss the implications for the quark mass dependence of meson masses lying on the J(PC)=1(--) Regge trajectory.

## Key claim

The authors extract the Bjorken-x dependence of the valence nonsinglet u-d parton distribution from lattice QCD moments, using an extrapolation formula that enforces correct behavior in both the chiral limit and the heavy quark limit, rather than the linear extrapolation in quark mass that had previously produced results 50% larger than experimental values.

## Method

The authors reconstruct the x-dependence of the valence u_v-d_v distribution by fitting a standard five-parameter PDF form (with two parameters fixed from global fits) to the lowest three nontrivial moments (n=1,2,3) of the u-d distribution computed on the lattice. They extrapolate these moments from the lattice quark mass region (30-190 MeV) to the physical pion mass using a formula that incorporates the leading nonanalytic chiral logarithm (m_pi^2 log m_pi) and the heavy quark limit where the distribution becomes a delta function at x=1/3, with the mass parameter mu fixed at 550 MeV from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The improved chiral extrapolation yields moments at the physical pion mass: <x^1> = 0.149, <x^2> = 0.0461, <x^3> = 0.0200 for the total u-d distribution; the linear extrapolation gives <x^1> = 0.262, <x^2> = 0.0843, <x^3> = 0.0340.
- The best-fit exponent b governing small-x behavior of the valence u_v-d_v distribution is b = 0.351 from the improved chiral extrapolation, compared to b = 3.51 from the linear extrapolation; the average global fit gives b = 0.476.
- The reconstructed distribution from the improved extrapolation agrees with phenomenological parameterizations, while the linear extrapolation produces a distribution that is roughly half the phenomenological value at small x and peaks near x ~ 1/3.
- At m_pi = 5 GeV, the valence distribution parameters become a = 125000, b = 6.20, c = 15.4, approaching the heavy quark delta-function limit.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation method of Detmold, Melnitchouk, Negele, Renner and Thomas (2001), which resolved the long-standing discrepancy between lattice moments and experiment by including pion cloud effects, to reconstruct the full x-dependence of PDFs rather than just their moments. The analysis connects the small-x exponent b to the intercept of the J^PC = 1^{--} Regge trajectory, enabling predictions for the quark-mass dependence of excited meson masses (rho, a2, rho3) that are in qualitative agreement with lattice calculations by the UKQCD Collaboration (1996). This enables future lattice studies to test the relation between PDF moments and the spectrum of excited hadrons at varying quark masses.
