# Fixed points of the Similarity Renormalization Group and the Nuclear Many-Body Problem

> The Similarity Renormalization Group reduces the off-shellness by driving the evolved interaction towards a diagonal band. We analyze the infrared limit and the corresponding on-shell interactions and its consequences for light nuclei. Using a harmonic oscillator shell model we obtain a Tjon line B(4He)= 4B(3H)-3B(2H) which can be understood from a combinatorics counting of nucleon pairs and triplets in the triton and alpha-particle and compares favorably with realistic calculations.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) drives the nuclear interaction to a unique infrared fixed point where the potential becomes on-shell, and the Tjon line correlation between alpha-particle and triton binding energies can be explained by simple combinatorial counting of nucleon pairs and triplets, provided that three-body forces are included at the infrared limit.

## Method

The authors evolve two-body nuclear potentials using the SRG with the Wilson generator (G_s = T) down to the infrared limit (lambda -> 0), where the potential becomes diagonal and on-shell. They then perform variational harmonic oscillator shell model calculations for the deuteron, triton, and alpha-particle binding energies using the evolved two-body potentials, initially setting three- and four-body forces to zero at each SRG cutoff scale. The binding energies are minimized with respect to the harmonic oscillator parameter b, and the results are analyzed in terms of the number of correlated nucleon pairs and triplets in each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Triton variational binding energy at lambda = infinity: B_t^Var = 5.9661 MeV (compared to exact Faddeev result B_t = 6.65543 MeV)
- Alpha-particle variational binding energy at lambda = infinity: B_alpha^Var = 32.1054 MeV
- In the infrared limit (lambda -> 0) with no three- or four-body forces: E_d = -B_d, E_t = -(3/2) B_d, E_alpha = -3 B_d
- Slope of Tjon line in two regimes: Delta B_alpha / Delta B_t ~ 2 (lambda -> 0) and Delta B_alpha / Delta B_t ~ 4 (lambda ~ 1 fm^-1)
- Using the combinatorial formula B_alpha = 4 B_t - 3 B_d with experimental values: B_alpha = 4 x 8.482 - 3 x 2.225 = 27.53 MeV (experimental: 28.296 MeV)
- For the on-shell limit with three-body forces: B_alpha = 4 B_t - 3 B_d

## Context

This work extends the analysis of SRG fixed points previously discussed by Timoteo et al. (2011) and provides a framework for understanding the Tjon line correlation observed in ab initio calculations using high-quality two-body interactions (reviewed by Hammer et al., 2012). The results show that the SRG infrared limit completely removes off-shell ambiguities in two-body forces, but requires significant three-body forces at the on-shell point, while four-body forces remain moderate (less than about 1 MeV). This enables a systematic program where only on-shell two-body information is needed, with three-body forces determined from triton binding energy.
