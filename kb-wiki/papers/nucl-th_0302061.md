# An analysis of the reaction pp --> ppηnear threshold

> It is shown that most of the available data on the $pp\to ppη$ reaction, including the invariant mass distributions in the $pp\to ppη$ reaction recently measured at COSY, can be understood in terms of the partial-wave amplitudes involving final $pp$ $S$ and $P$ states and the $η$ meson s-wave. This finding, together with the fact that results within a meson--exchange model are especially sensitive to the details of the excitation mechanism of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance, demonstrates the possibility of investigating the properties of this resonance in $NN$ collisions. The spin correlation function $C_{xx}$ is shown to disentangle the $S$- and $P$-wave contributions. ## Key claim
Most available data on the pp → ppη reaction, including the recently measured invariant mass distributions at COSY, can be understood in terms of only three partial-wave amplitudes involving final pp S and P states and the η meson s-wave. However, the relative strength of these amplitudes depends crucially on the details of the excitation mechanism of the S₁₁(1535) resonance, and a model-independent extraction of the amplitudes requires spin correlation measurements not yet available.

## Method
The authors performed a partial-wave analysis of the pp → ppη reaction near threshold, restricting the final state to pp S and P waves with the η meson in s-wave. They developed a relativistic meson-exchange model within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation, including both the NN final state interaction (using the Paris NN T-matrix) and initial state interaction, with the S₁₁(1535) resonance as the dominant production mechanism. The coupling constants at the ρNN* and ωNN* vertices were adjusted to reproduce the available data, including total cross sections and the newly measured pp invariant mass distributions at excess energies Q = 15 and 41 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Excess energies studied: Q = 15 MeV and Q = 41 MeV for the pp invariant mass distributions measured at COSY.
- Coupling constants obtained: g_ρNN*/(m_N* + m_N) = 9.5 [fm], κ_ρ = -5.3; g_ωNN*/(m_N* + m_N) = 6.0 [fm], κ_ω = 3.8.
- Pseudoscalar-pseudovector mixing parameter at the πNN* and ηNN* vertices: λ = 0.7.
- The spin correlation function C_xx is predicted to take a constant value of C_xx = 1 from the ³P₀ → ¹S₀ s partial wave alone, dropping to C_xx ∼ -0.3 when the ¹S₀ → ³P₀ s contribution is added.
- Normalization factors applied to theoretical differential cross sections to match data: approximately 2.7 at Q = 15 MeV and approximately 0.9 at Q = 41 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of meson production in NN collisions, particularly the meson-exchange model of Nakayama et al. (2001) which described total cross sections but failed to reproduce the newly measured pp invariant mass distributions from the TOF and COSY-11 collaborations at COSY (Roderburg, Moskal). The analysis shows that the bump in the pp invariant mass distribution at higher m_pp values arises from pp P-wave contributions, specifically the ¹S₀ → ³P₀ s amplitude, rather than from ηN final state interactions as previously considered by Baru et al. The results enable future experimental measurements of spin correlation functions (C_xx or C_yy) to disentangle the S- and P-wave contributions and to constrain the properties of the S₁₁(1535) resonance, which cannot be studied as cleanly in simpler reactions such as γ + N → η + N or M + N → η + N.
