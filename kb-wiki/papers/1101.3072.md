# Ratio of bulk to shear viscosity in a quasigluon plasma: from weak to strong coupling

> The ratio of bulk to shear viscosity is expected to exhibit a different behaviour in weakly and in strongly coupled systems. This can be expressed by its dependence on the squared sound velocity. In the high temperature QCD plasma at small running coupling, the viscosity ratio is uniquely determined by a quadratic dependence on the conformality measure, whereas in certain strongly coupled and nearly conformal theories this dependence is linear. Employing an effective kinetic theory of quasiparticle excitations with medium-modified dispersion relation, we analyze the ratio of bulk to shear viscosity of the gluon plasma. We show that in this approach, depending on the temperature, the viscosity ratio exhibits either of these dependencies found by means of weak coupling perturbative or strong coupling holographic techniques. The turning point between the two different dependencies is located around the maximum in the scaled interaction measure.

## Key claim
The ratio of bulk to shear viscosity in a gluon plasma, as described by an effective kinetic theory of quasiparticles with medium-modified dispersion, exhibits either a quadratic or a linear dependence on the conformality measure (the deviation of squared sound velocity from 1/3) depending on temperature, with the transition between these regimes occurring near the maximum of the scaled interaction measure.

## Method
The authors employed an effective kinetic theory of Boltzmann-Vlasov type for quasigluon excitations with a temperature-dependent self-energy, using a relaxation time approximation assumed equal for both bulk and shear viscosities. The quasiparticle model (QPM) was calibrated to reproduce lattice QCD results for the equation of state of pure SU(3) gauge theory, with the gluon self-energy expressed in terms of a temperature-dependent effective coupling. Bulk and shear viscosity coefficients were then computed from momentum integrals involving the Bose distribution function and the medium-modified dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- The ratio ζ/η reaches about 0.78 as T approaches 1.02 Tc.
- For T greater than approximately 1.5 Tc, the viscosity ratio is entirely determined by the perturbative QCD-like quadratic behaviour 15(Δv_s^2)^2.
- The transition from quadratic to linear dependence on Δv_s^2 occurs around T ≈ 1.15 Tc, where Δv_s^2 ≈ 0.17.
- The linear fit to ζ/η in the regime 0.17 ≤ Δv_s^2 ≤ 0.3 gives ζ/η = 3.78 Δv_s^2 - 0.305.
- The QPM describes lattice data with χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.4 × 10^(-2) for data from Boyd et al. and χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.5 × 10^(-3) for data from Okamoto et al.

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle model (QPM) approach, previously shown to describe equilibrium thermodynamics of SU(3) gauge theory, to transport properties of the gluon plasma. It bridges the gap between weak-coupling perturbative QCD results from Arnold et al. (2006), which give a quadratic dependence ζ/η = 15(Δv_s^2)^2, and strong-coupling holographic results from Buchel et al. (2005, 2008) and Benincasa et al. (2006), which yield a linear dependence ζ/η ∼ Δv_s^2. The results are consistent with available lattice QCD calculations from Meyer and provide a systematic interpolation between these regimes, with implications for viscous hydrodynamic simulations at LHC energies where a larger temperature range is probed.
