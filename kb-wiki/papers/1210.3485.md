# Meson-baryon reactions with strangeness -1 within a chiral framework

> We study meson-baryon scattering with strangeness -1 in unitary chiral perturbation theory. Ten coupled channels are considered in our work, namely $π^0 Λ$, $π^0 Σ^0$, $π^- Σ^+$, $π^+ Σ^-$, $K^- p$, $\bar{K}^0 n$, $ηΛ$, $ηΣ^0$, $K^0 Ξ^0$ and $K^+ Ξ^-$. A large amount of experimental data are analyzed, including the recent precise measurement by the SIDDHARTA Collaboration of the energy shift and width of the $1s$ state of kaonic hydrogen. This leads to a strong constraint on the free parameters in our theory and of the resulting meson-baryon scattering amplitudes. We also analyze the uncertainty that stems by using several different strategies to perform the fits to data. It is found that large uncertainties in the subthreshold extrapolation of the $K^-p$ scattering amplitude arise by employing either only one common weak pseudoscalar decay constant or distinguishing between $f_π$, $f_K$ and $f_η$. However, in both cases a good reproduction of experimental data is obtained. We also discuss the pole content of the resulting $S$-wave amplitudes, particularly in connection with the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while both a common pseudoscalar decay constant and distinct decay constants for pions, kaons, and eta can reproduce experimental data well, the choice between these two strategies leads to large uncertainties in the subthreshold extrapolation of the K- p scattering amplitude, which is larger than estimated in previous studies that also reproduced the SIDDHARTA measurement.

## Method

The authors study meson-baryon scattering with strangeness -1 using unitary chiral perturbation theory (UChPT) with ten coupled channels. They fit the free parameters of the chiral Lagrangian at leading order (O(p)) and next-to-leading order (O(p^2)) to a large set of experimental data including cross sections, event distributions, threshold ratios, and the SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen, using two different strategies for the pseudoscalar decay constants (Fit I with a common decay constant, Fit II with distinct f_pi, f_K, f_eta).

## Key numbers

- SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen 1s state: energy shift Delta E and width Gamma are reproduced, with fits preferring somewhat larger central values for Gamma
- K- p scattering length from SIDDHARTA data: a_{K^- p} = (-0.65 +/- 0.10) + i (0.81 +/- 0.15) fm
- Pseudoscalar decay constants used in Fit II: f_pi = 92.2 MeV, f_K = 110.0 MeV, f_eta = 120 MeV
- Subtraction constants a_i have natural size of O(1) in all fits
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for Fit I and Fit II is lower than 1; for the O(p)-Fit it is near 2, dropping to 1.23 if the K- p to eta Lambda cross section data are removed
- The two poles of the Lambda(1405) are characterized: one pole near the Kbar N threshold with imaginary part around 10-30 MeV, the second wider pole with imaginary part around 50-200 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Kbar N scattering in coupled channels based on chiral perturbation theory, including those by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), Oller (2000, 2005, 2006), Jido (2003), Borasoy (2004, 2005), and more recent works by Ikeda (2011, 2012), Cieply (2011), and Mai (2012). It improves upon these by including additional data such as the pi- Sigma+ event distribution from Hemingway (1985), the K- p to eta Lambda cross section from Starostin (2001), and the pi Lambda phase shift at the Xi- mass. The results confirm the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance and provide important constraints for studies of Kbar few-nucleon systems and possible bound states of Kbar with heavy nuclei.
