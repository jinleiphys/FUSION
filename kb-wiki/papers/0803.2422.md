# BCS-BEC crossover in dense relativistic matter: Collective excitations

> We study the relativistic BCS-BEC crossover within a class of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models, including arbitrary Lorentz-scalar pairing channels. Using the mean-field approximation we investigate spectral properties of the collective bosonic excitations in the superfluid phase, with particular attention to the Nambu--Goldstone bosons of the broken symmetry. This is a first step towards a systematic improvement of the mean-field approximation by including the fluctuation effects. The general results are illustrated on pairing in dense two-flavor quark matter - the two-flavor color superconductor.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that within the mean-field approximation, the relativistic BCS-BEC crossover in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models produces Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the superfluid phase whose spectral properties depend on whether they couple to gapped or gapless fermions, with type-II Goldstone bosons appearing due to nonzero density of the unbroken charge.

## Method
The author uses the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to bosonize a class of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models with Lorentz-scalar pairing channels, then computes the inverse propagator of collective bosonic modes from the second derivative of the effective action. The general formalism is applied to the specific case of two-flavor color superconductivity, where the pair correlation functions are evaluated analytically at zero temperature and numerically with a sharp three-momentum cutoff.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum set to \(k_{\text F}=0.2m\) and cutoff \(\Lambda=5m\) for numerical calculations
- Normalization factor \(N=4\) for the two-flavor color superconductor model
- At \(G_c/G_R\approx0.08\) the chemical potential decreases below the fermion mass, marking the onset of the BEC regime
- The coefficient \(a\) in the low-momentum expansion of \(\chi_{11}^{\Delta\Delta^*}\) equals \(1/\Delta^2\) times the density difference between gapped and gapless colors
- The coefficient \(b\) contains a term \(\theta(\mu-m)(\mu^2-m^2)^{3/2}/(3\pi^2\Delta^2\mu)\)

## Context
This work extends the mean-field analysis of the BCS-BEC crossover in relativistic matter, building on the formalism of Abuki (2006) who studied fluctuation effects in the normal phase using the Gaussian approximation. The paper provides the first systematic investigation of bosonic collective mode spectra in the superfluid phase, enabling future inclusion of fluctuation effects beyond mean-field. It connects to prior work on color superconductivity by Ebert et al. (2004, 2007) and to the nonrelativistic crossover studies of Eagles (1969) and Leggett (1980).
