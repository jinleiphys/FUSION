# Jets, Mach cone, hot spots, ridges, harmonic flow, dihadron and $γ$-hadron correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Within the AMPT Monte Carlo model, hot spots in the initial transverse parton density are shown to lead to harmonic flows which all contribute to dihadron azimuthal correlation. The net back-to-back dihadron correlation after subtraction of harmonic flows still has a double-peak that is independent of the initial geometric triangularity at a fixed impact-parameter and unique to the structure of jet-induced Mach cone and expanding hot spots dragged by radial flow. The longitudinal structure of hot spots also leads to a near-side ridge in dihadron correlation with a large rapidity gap. By successively randomizing the azimuthal angle of the transverse momenta and positions of initial partons, one can isolate the effects of jet-induced medium excitation and expanding hot spots on the dihadron azimuthal correlation with both small and large rapidity gap. The double-peak in the net dihadron correlation is quantitatively different from that in $γ$-hadron that is free of the contributions from harmonic flow and hot spots and caused only by jet-induced Mach cone.

## Key claim

Within the AMPT model, the double-peaked back-to-back dihadron azimuthal correlation observed after subtracting harmonic flow contributions is independent of the initial geometric triangularity at a fixed impact-parameter and is unique to the combined structure of jet-induced Mach cone and expanding hot spots distorted by radial flow, but the double-peak in dihadron correlation is quantitatively different from that in γ-hadron correlation, which is caused only by jet-induced Mach cone.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting and parton recombination for hadronization to simulate central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV with fixed impact-parameter b = 0. They calculated harmonic flow coefficients v_n (n = 1-6) from the final hadron spectra and subtracted the harmonic flow contributions from dihadron azimuthal correlations using the ZYAM (zero yield at minimum) scheme. To isolate different mechanisms, they successively randomized the azimuthal angle of jet shower partons (to switch off dijet correlations) and the polar angle of transverse coordinates of soft partons (to eliminate hot spots), and compared dihadron correlations with γ-hadron correlations from direct photon-triggered jets.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV with impact-parameter b = 0 were simulated.
- Initial parton density fluctuations have the smallest transverse size of about 1 fm, confined to the size of parent nucleons r_N ∼ 1 fm.
- In a typical AMPT event, the initial transverse parton distribution has ellipticity ε_2 = 0.02 and triangularity ε_3 = 0.02.
- Dihadron correlations were studied for trigger hadrons with p_T^trig > 2.5 GeV/c and associated hadrons with 1 < p_T^asso < 2 GeV/c.
- The double-peak in the away-side dihadron correlation has a maximum opening angle of Δφ ≈ 1 rad relative to the back-side of the trigger.
- When jet shower parton azimuthal angles were randomized, the magnitude of the double-peak in the away-side correlation was reduced by about 40% compared to the full simulation.
- For γ-hadron correlation, trigger photons had p_T^trig(γ) ≥ 15 GeV/c and trigger hadrons had p_T^trig(h) > 4 GeV/c, with associated hadrons in the range 1 < p_T^asso < 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where experimental data (Adams 2005, Adler 2005) showed a double-peaked back-to-back azimuthal correlation suspected to arise from Mach-cone excitation (Casalderrey-Solana 2004, Stoecker 2004). The authors build on hydrodynamic studies (Betz 2009) and parton transport studies (Li 2010) showing that radial flow deflection of jet showers and medium excitation produces the double-peak, as well as work on expanding hot spots (Takahashi 2009, Shuryak 2009) and triangular flow (Alver 2010, Schenke 2010). The paper enables experimental separation of jet-induced medium excitation from hot spot contributions by comparing dihadron and γ-hadron correlations, and demonstrates that the ridge structure with large rapidity gap arises from longitudinally extended hot spots rather than jets.
