# Squeezed back-to-back correlations of $K^+$$K^-$ in d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV

> We investigate the squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of $K^+$$K^-$, caused by the mass modification of the particle in the dense medium formed in d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV. Considering some kaons may be not affected by the medium, we further study the BBC functions of $K^+$$K^-$ when parts of all kaons have a mass-shift. Our results indicate that the BBC functions of $K^+$$K^-$ can be observed when only about 10$\%$ of all kaons have a mass-shift in d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and the peripheral collisions of Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV. Since the BBC function is caused by the mass-shift due to the interaction between the particle and the medium, the successful detection of the BBC function indirectly marks that the dense medium has formed in these collision systems. We suggest to measure the BBC function of $K^+$$K^-$ experimentally in d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and peripheral collisions of Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV.

## Key claim

The squeezed back-to-back correlation (BBC) function of \(K^+K^-\) can be observed in d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) GeV even when only about 10% of all kaons experience a mass shift due to the dense medium, provided the temporal distribution of freeze-out points is sufficiently narrow.

## Method

The authors used the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 with event-by-event MC-Glb initial conditions at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.08 to simulate the particle-emitting sources. They computed the BBC function \(C(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k})\) from the chaotic and squeezed amplitudes for kaon pairs, treating the in-medium kaon mass \(m_*\) as a parameter. To account for the possibility that some kaons are unaffected by the medium, they introduced a Gaussian factor \(e^{-\sigma_r^2 / 2\sigma_c^2}\) to describe the probability of particles without mass shift, where \(\sigma_r\) is the spatial width of the freeze-out points and \(\sigma_c\) is a spatial width cut parameter.

## Key numbers

- The average freeze-out time \(\langle \bar{\tau} \rangle\) for 0-5% central d+Au collisions at 200 GeV is 3.02 fm/\(c\), with an average temporal width \(\langle \sigma_\tau \rangle\) of 0.66 fm/\(c\).
- The average freeze-out time \(\langle \bar{\tau} \rangle\) for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV is 6.27 fm/\(c\), with an average temporal width \(\langle \sigma_\tau \rangle\) of 1.87 fm/\(c\).
- For 50-60% peripheral Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV, the average freeze-out time \(\langle \bar{\tau} \rangle\) is 2.87 fm/\(c\) and the average temporal width \(\langle \sigma_\tau \rangle\) is 0.84 fm/\(c\).
- The average probability of kaons with mass shift in 0-5% d+Au collisions at 200 GeV is 9.9% for a spatial cut parameter \(\sigma_c = 2\) fm.
- The average probability of kaons with mass shift in 50-60% Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV is 11.8% for \(\sigma_c = 2\) fm.
- The BBC function is shown as a function of in-medium kaon mass \(m_*\) at momenta \(k = 200\) MeV and \(k = 800\) MeV for various collision systems and centralities.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of squeezed back-to-back correlations by Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1996, 1999) and Padula (2006, 2010), and follows the hydrodynamic simulations of Zhang (2015, 2016) which found that the BBC function is suppressed in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV due to wide temporal distributions. The present paper is motivated by recent experimental observations of long-range angular correlations and elliptic flow in small systems such as d+Au collisions by the PHENIX Collaboration (2013, 2015), suggesting that a hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma may be created there. The results suggest that experimental measurement of the \(K^+K^-\) BBC function in d+Au collisions at 200 GeV and peripheral Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV could serve as an indirect signature of dense medium formation in these systems.
