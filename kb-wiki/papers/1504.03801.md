# Semi-microscopic modeling of heavy-ion fusion reactions with multi-reference covariant density functional theory

> We describe low-lying collective excitations of atomic nuclei with the multi-reference covariant density functional theory, and combine them with coupled-channels calculations for heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier. To this end, we use the calculated transition strengths among several collective states as inputs to the coupled-channels calculations. This approach provides a natural way to describe anharmonic multi-phonon excitations as well as a deviation of rotational excitations from a simple rigid rotor. We apply this method to subbarrier fusion reactions of $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni, $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni and $^{40}$Ca+$^{58}$Ni systems. We find that the effect of anharmonicity tends to smear the fusion barrier distributions, better reproducing the experimental data compared to the calculations in the harmonic oscillator limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating anharmonic quadrupole vibrations, deviations from the harmonic oscillator limit, into coupled-channels calculations for heavy-ion fusion reactions improves agreement with experimental fusion barrier distributions, though the effect on fusion cross sections is more modest and system-dependent.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) calculations with the PC-PK1 interaction to obtain low-lying collective states, transition strengths, and coupling matrix elements for ⁵⁸Ni and ⁶⁰Ni. They then used these microscopic inputs, scaled to match the experimental B(E2) value for the ground-state to first 2⁺ transition, as coupling strengths in phenomenological Woods-Saxon based coupled-channels calculations for subbarrier fusion of ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni, ⁵⁸Ni+⁶⁰Ni, and ⁴⁰Ca+⁵⁸Ni.

## Key numbers

* For ⁵⁸Ni, the MR-CDFT calculation yields B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 150 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 126(8) e²fm⁴ and the previous non-relativistic DFT value of 248 e²fm⁴.
* The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the first 2⁺ state is calculated as Q(2₁⁺) = +7.96 e fm² for ⁵⁸Ni and +10.4 e fm² for ⁶⁰Ni; experimental values are −10±6 e fm² and +3±5 e fm², respectively.
* The Coulomb coupling strength for the 0₁⁺→2₁⁺ transition in ⁵⁸Ni is β = 0.223, estimated from the measured B(E2) value with a radius parameter r₀ = 1.06 fm.
* For the ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni system, the Woods-Saxon potential parameters are V₀ = 170.2 MeV, r₀ = 1.0 fm, and a = 0.9 fm; experimental excitation energies used are 0, 1.454, 2.459, 2.775, and 3.531 MeV for the 0₁⁺, 2₁⁺, 4₁⁺, 2₂⁺, and 0₃⁺ states, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic approach previously applied to ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴,¹⁴⁸Sm using the interacting boson model (Hagino et al., 1997; Hagino et al., 1998) to a broader mass region using a fully microscopic description of nuclear structure. It also complements non-relativistic MR-DFT calculations for ⁵⁸Ni (Yao et al., 2015) by using a relativistic framework. The method enables systematic studies of anharmonic effects in subbarrier fusion without relying on the harmonic vibrator or rigid rotor approximations, and provides a natural truncation scheme for the phonon spectrum in coupled-channels calculations.
