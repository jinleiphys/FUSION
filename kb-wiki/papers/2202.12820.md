# In-beam $γ$-ray spectroscopy of $^{32}$Mg via direct reactions

> Background: The nucleus $^{32}$Mg ($N=20$ and $Z=12$) plays a central role in the so-called "island of inversion" where in the ground states $sd$-shell neutrons are promoted to the $fp$-shell orbitals across the shell gap, resulting in the disappearance of the canonical neutron magic number $N=20$. Purpose: The primary goals of this work are to extend the level scheme of $^{32}$Mg, provide spin-parity assignments to excited states, and discuss the microscopic structure of each state through comparisons with theoretical calculations. Method: In-beam $γ$-ray spectroscopy of $^{32}$Mg was performed using two direct-reaction probes, one-neutron (two-proton) knockout reactions on $^{33}$Mg ($^{34}$Si). Final-state exclusive cross sections and parallel momentum distributions were extracted from the experimental data and compared with eikonal-based reaction model calculations combined with shell-model overlap functions. Results: Owing to the remarkable selectivity of the one-neutron and two-proton knockout reactions, a significantly updated level scheme for $^{32}$Mg, which exhibits negative-parity intruder and positive-parity normal states, was constructed. The experimental results were confronted with four different nuclear structure models. Conclusions: In some of these models, different aspects of $^{32}$Mg and the transition into the island of inversion are well described. However, unexplained discrepancies remain, and even with the help of these state-of-the-art theoretical approaches, the structure of this key nucleus is not yet fully captured.

## Key claim

The paper extends the level scheme of \(^{32}\)Mg and provides spin-parity assignments for excited states using one-neutron and two-proton knockout reactions, but acknowledges that even with state-of-the-art theoretical models, the structure of this key nucleus in the island of inversion is not yet fully captured and unexplained discrepancies remain.

## Method

In-beam \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy of \(^{32}\)Mg was performed using one-neutron knockout from \(^{33}\)Mg and two-proton knockout from \(^{34}\)Si at incident energies of 99.6 and 94.8 MeV/nucleon, respectively. Final-state exclusive cross sections and parallel momentum distributions were extracted and compared with eikonal-based reaction model calculations combined with shell-model overlap functions from four different nuclear structure models (SDPF-M, SDPF-U-MIX, EEdf1, and IMSRG). \(\gamma\)-\(\gamma\) coincidence relationships measured with the GRETINA array were used to construct an updated level scheme.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive one-neutron knockout cross section: \(\sigma_{1n}^\mathrm{incl} = 104(5)\) mb for \(^{33}\)Mg beam at 99.6 MeV/nucleon on a \(^{9}\)Be target.
- Inclusive two-proton knockout cross section: \(\sigma_{2p}^\mathrm{incl} = 0.96(8)\) mb for \(^{34}\)Si beam at 94.8 MeV/nucleon on a \(^{9}\)Be target.
- \(2^+_1 \to 0^+_1\) transition energy: 885 keV, with a half-life of 11.4(20) ps for the \(2^+_1\) state.
- New state at 2288 keV built on the \(2^+_1\) state, established via the 1403 keV transition.
- New state at 2846 keV feeding the \(2^+_1\) state, established via the 1961 keV transition.
- \(4^+_1\) state at 2322 keV with a \(4^+_1 \to 2^+_1\) transition energy of 1437 keV.
- Theoretical inclusive cross section with SDPF-M interaction: 107(10) mb, consistent with the measured 104(5) mb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(^{32}\)Mg, which plays a central role in the island of inversion around \(N=20\) where \(sd\)-shell neutrons are promoted to \(fp\)-shell orbitals. It builds on earlier \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) spectroscopy (Detraz 1979, Guillemaud-Mueller 1984, Klotz 1993, Matsui 2007, Tripathi 2008), proton inelastic scattering (Takeuchi 2009), secondary fragmentation (Crawford 2016), and Coulomb breakup (Date 2016) measurements. The updated level scheme with negative-parity intruder and positive-parity normal states enables more stringent tests of shell-model interactions including SDPF-M (Utsuno 1999), SDPF-U-MIX (Caurier 2014), EEdf1 (Tsuruta 2017, 2020), and IMSRG (Miyagi 2020), and helps differentiate between models of shape coexistence and the role of 4p4h configurations in the island of inversion.
