# Complete sets of observables in pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction

> Pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction is characterized by four complex reaction amplitudes. A complete set is a minimum theoretical set of observables that allow to determine these amplitudes unambiguously. It is studied whether complete sets remain complete when experimental uncertainty is involved. To this end, data from the GRAAL Collaboration and simulated data from a realistic model, both for the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ reaction, are analyzed in the transversity representation of the reaction amplitudes. It is found that only the moduli of the transversity amplitudes can be determined without ambiguity but not the relative phases.

## Key claim
Theoretical complete sets of eight observables (or seven in the transversity representation) can determine the four complex reaction amplitudes of pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction only with infinite precision; with finite experimental uncertainty, only the moduli of the transversity amplitudes can be determined unambiguously, but not the relative phases.

## Method
The authors analyzed GRAAL Collaboration data for the γp → K⁺Λ reaction and simulated data from the RPR-2011 model, using the transversity representation of the reaction amplitudes. They extracted the four normalized transversity moduli from the three single asymmetries Σ, T, and P, and then studied the extraction of relative phases from a complete set of seven observables (three single plus four double asymmetries) by generating simulated data sets with Gaussian noise at two experimental resolutions (σ_exp = 0.1 and σ_exp = 0.01). The fraction of simulated data sets that yielded incorrect or imaginary solutions was quantified as the "insolvability" η.

## Key numbers
- The four normalized transversity moduli r_i are expressed as r_1 = ½√(1 + Σ + T + P), r_2 = ½√(1 + Σ – T – P), r_3 = ½√(1 – Σ – T + P), r_4 = ½√(1 – Σ + T – P).
- At each of 90,375 kinematic points, 1000 simulated data sets were generated with the RPR-2011 model to compute average insolvabilities.
- For σ_exp = 0.1, the insolvability η (fraction of incorrect or imaginary solutions) can become "quite substantial," with the largest contribution from imaginary solutions.
- Increasing experimental resolution by a factor of ten (to σ_exp = 0.01) "clearly improves the overall solvability."

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of complete sets in pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction, originally developed by Barker (1975) and Chiang (1996), by testing whether such sets remain complete under realistic experimental conditions. The authors use the transversity representation introduced in their own prior work (Vrancx 2013) and apply it to the γp → K⁺Λ reaction using GRAAL data (Lleres 2007, 2009) and the RPR-2011 model (Corthals 2006, De Cruz 2012). The finding that theoretical completeness does not imply experimental completeness motivates future investigation of "overcomplete" sets containing additional double asymmetries to resolve spurious phase ambiguities.
