# Bayesian refinement of covariant energy density functionals

> The last five years have seen remarkable progress in our quest to determine the equation of state of neutron rich matter. Recent advances across the theoretical, experimental, and observational landscape have been incorporated in a Bayesian framework to refine existing covariant energy density functionals previously calibrated by the properties of finite nuclei. In particular, constraints on the maximum neutron star mass from pulsar timing, on stellar radii from the NICER mission, on tidal deformabilities from the LIGO-Virgo collaboration, and on the dynamics of pure neutron matter as predicted from chiral effective field theories, have resulted in significant refinements to the models, particularly to those predicting a stiff symmetry energy. Still, even after these improvements, we find challenging to reproduce simultaneously the neutron skin thickness of both ${}^{208}$Pb and ${}^{48}$Ca recently reported by the PREX/CREX collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors use a Bayesian framework to refine existing covariant energy density functionals (FSUGold2 and FSUGarnet) by incorporating constraints from neutron star observations and chiral effective field theory, but they find that even after these refinements it remains challenging to simultaneously reproduce the neutron skin thickness of both \({}^{208}\)Pb and \({}^{48}\)Ca as reported by the PREX and CREX collaborations.

## Method

The authors start with covariance matrices from earlier calibrations of the FSUGold2 and FSUGarnet energy density functionals as prior distributions, then combine these with a likelihood function that includes constraints from the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from GW170817, mass and radius measurements of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451 from NICER, and predictions for the equation of state of pure neutron matter from chiral effective field theory. They sample the posterior distribution of model parameters using a Markov Chain Monte Carlo method with a Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, mapping the seven coupling constants to six bulk properties of infinite nuclear matter plus the quartic vector coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- FSUGold2 predicts a symmetry energy slope of L = 112.8 MeV with an uncertainty of 16.1 MeV from earlier calibration
- FSUGarnet predicts a symmetry energy slope of L = 50.96 MeV
- PSR J0740+6620: radius R = 12.35 ± 0.75 km, mass M = 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses (1-sigma errors)
- PSR J0030+0451: radius R = 12.45 ± 0.65 km, mass M = 1.44 ± 0.15 solar masses (1-sigma errors)
- GW170817 tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: Lambda = 190 with +390 and -120 at 90% confidence level
- Nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 = 0.1505 fm^{-3} for FSUGold2 and 0.1531 fm^{-3} for FSUGarnet
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A_0 = -16.28 MeV for FSUGold2 and -16.23 MeV for FSUGarnet

## Context

This work extends the calibration of covariant energy density functionals originally developed by Chen and Piekarewicz (2014) for FSUGold2 and FSUGarnet, which were fitted to finite nuclei properties and maximum neutron star mass known at that time. The Bayesian refinement incorporates recent observational constraints from NICER (Riley 2019, Miller 2019, Miller 2021, Riley 2021), LIGO-Virgo (Abbott 2017, Abbott 2018), and pulsar timing (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2019, Fonseca 2021), as well as chiral EFT predictions for pure neutron matter (Drischler 2021). The results enable improved predictions for neutron star properties and highlight the tension between the PREX measurement of a thick neutron skin in \({}^{208}\)Pb and the CREX measurement of a thin neutron skin in \({}^{48}\)Ca, suggesting that the simple isovector sector of these models may need extension.
