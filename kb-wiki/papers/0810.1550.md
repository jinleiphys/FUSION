# Elliptic flow from final state interactions in the distorted-wave emission-function model

> The effect of final state interactions on the elliptic flow coefficient measured in relativistic heavy ion collisions is investigated within the DWEF formalism established by Miller and Cramer [Phys. Rev. Lett. 94, 102302 (2005), J. Phys. G34, 703 (2007), Phys. Rev. C78, 054905 (2008)]. It is found that the optical potential previously found to give the best fit of particle multiplicity and Hanbury Brown and Twiss radii in RHIC events has a moderate effect on the resulting elliptic flow coefficient v_2. This indicates that final state interactions should be taken into account to confidently predict v_2 to better than ~20% accuracy.

## Key claim

Final state interactions in the distorted-wave emission-function (DWEF) model have a moderate effect on the elliptic flow coefficient v₂, reducing it by approximately 10–25% relative to the zero-interaction value, indicating that these interactions must be included to predict v₂ to better than ~20% accuracy.

## Method

The authors used the DWEF formalism with an analytical parametrization of the freezeout surface and an optical potential representing final state interactions, solving coupled differential equations for distorted pion wavefunctions and performing five-dimensional integrals to compute v₂. They fixed all parameters to best-fit values from prior fits to particle multiplicity and Hanbury Brown–Twiss radii from central RHIC collisions (Miller and Cramer, 2005; Luzum, 2008), then adjusted the spatial eccentricity and elliptic flow parameter a₂ to approximate a peripheral collision with impact parameter ~7 fm.

## Key numbers

- The optical potential decreases v₂ by 10–25% of its zero-interaction value, with a slightly smaller effect as momentum increases.
- For Fit 1 parameters, a₂ = 0.11 was required to roughly match experimental v₂; for Fit 2 parameters, a₂ = 0.10 was sufficient.
- The spatial eccentricity at freezeout was fixed to ε = 0.035, corresponding to a peripheral collision with impact parameter ~7 fm.
- The best-fit parameter sets include: Fit 1 with T = 156.58 MeV, η_f = 1.310, Δτ = 2.0731 fm/c, R_WS = 11.867 fm, a_WS = 1.277 fm, w₀ = 0.0693 fm⁻², w₂ = 0.856 + i0.116, τ₀ = 9.04 fm/c, Δη = 1.047, μ_π = 139.57 MeV; Fit 2 with T = 121 MeV, η_f = 1.05, Δτ = 0, R_WS = 11.7 fm, a_WS = 1.11 fm, w₀ = 0.495 fm⁻², w₂ = 0.762 + i0.0001, τ₀ = 9.20 fm/c, Δη = 70.7, μ_π = 139.57 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DWEF formalism previously applied to central collisions (Miller and Cramer, 2005; Luzum, 2008) to non-central collisions and the elliptic flow observable v₂, which is a key indicator of thermalization at RHIC. The results show that final state interactions produce a ~20% effect on v₂, consistent with other investigations of final state interactions such as Teaney (2001), and must be accounted for in hydrodynamic simulations aiming for quantitative precision. The authors suggest that a full parameter search using all relevant experimental data, or adding final state interactions directly into hydrodynamic simulations as an afterburner, would be needed to determine the precise size of these effects.
