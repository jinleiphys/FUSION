# Heavy Quarks and Meson Production in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> We report on our recent investigations in photonuclear production of heavy quarks and vector meson in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. In particular, our theoretical predictions are compared with the recent experimental measurements on coherent rho (STAR) and J/Psi (PHENIX) photoproduction at RHIC and estimates for LHC are given.

## Key claim

The authors show that theoretical predictions for coherent ρ and J/Ψ photoproduction in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC are consistent with the experimental measurements from STAR and PHENIX within the quoted uncertainties, and they provide estimates for LHC energies; however, the comparison is limited by the large experimental error bars and the model dependence of the dipole and saturation approaches used.

## Method

The photonuclear cross sections for heavy quark (charm and bottom) and vector meson (ρ, ω, φ, J/Ψ) production in ultraperipheral collisions are computed by convolving the equivalent photon flux from one nucleus with the photon-nucleus cross section, using the impact parameter restriction b > 2R_A to ensure no hadronic interaction. For heavy quarks, four approaches are compared: collinear factorization, semihard (k⊥-factorization) formalism, the color dipole saturation model, and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach. For vector mesons, the color dipole formalism is used with the DGKP model for meson light-cone wavefunctions, including corrections from the real part of the amplitude and skewedness effects for heavy mesons, and the nuclear form factor is computed from a hard-sphere Woods-Saxon distribution.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at LHC (√S_NN = 5500 GeV), the total charm photoproduction cross section ranges from 633 mb (CGC) to 2079 mb (semihard with GRV94), with the saturation model giving 862 mb.
- For bottom photoproduction at LHC, the cross section ranges from 8.9 mb (CGC) to 20.1 mb (collinear), with the saturation model giving 10.75 mb.
- Coherent ρ photoproduction at RHIC (√s = 130 GeV): theoretical prediction σ_theory(|y| ≤ 3) = 410 mb, compared to STAR measurement σ_STAR = 370 ± 170 (stat) ± 80 (syst) mb.
- Coherent J/Ψ photoproduction at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): theoretical dσ/dy at y=0 = 58 μb, compared to PHENIX measurement dσ_PHENIX/dy = 48 ± 16 (stat) ± 18 (syst) μb.
- For PbPb at LHC, predicted integrated cross sections: J/Ψ = 41.5 mb, φ = 998 mb, ω = 1131 mb, ρ = 10069 mb.
- For CaCa at LHC, predicted integrated cross sections: J/Ψ = 436 μb, φ = 12 mb, ω = 14 mb, ρ = 128 mb.
- The Lorentz boost for LHC is γ_L = 2930, giving maximum γA center-of-mass energy W_γA ≤ 950 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole and saturation model approaches previously applied to electron-proton collisions at HERA (Nikolaev, 1991; Armesto, 2002) to the nuclear environment of ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. It provides the first systematic comparison of collinear, semihard, saturation, and CGC formalisms for heavy quark production in UPCs, and shows that the dipole formalism successfully describes the STAR ρ data (2002) and PHENIX J/Ψ data (2005) at RHIC. The predictions for LHC enable future experimental tests of parton saturation and the QCD dynamics at small x in nuclear targets, complementing the information from electron-nucleus colliders that are not yet operational.
