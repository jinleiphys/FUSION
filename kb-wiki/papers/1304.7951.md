# Saturated symmetric nuclear matter in strong magnetic fields

> Strongly magnetized symmetric nuclear matter is investigated within the context of effective baryon-meson exchange models. The magnetic field is coupled to the charge as well as the dipole moment of the baryons by including the appropriate terms in the Lagrangian density. The saturation density of magnetized, symmetric nuclear matter was calculated for magnetic fields of the order of 10^17 gauss. For the calculated range of saturation densities the binding energy, symmetry energy coefficient and compressibility of nuclear matter were also calculated. It is found that with an increasing magnetic field the saturation density increases, while the system becomes less bound. Furthermore, the depopulation of proton Landau levels leaves a distinct fluctuating imprint on the symmetry energy coefficient and compressibility. The calculations were also performed for increased values of the baryon magnetic dipole moment. By increasing the dipole moment strength the saturation density is found to decrease, but the system becomes more tightly bound while the fluctuations in the symmetry energy coefficient and compressibility persist.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for magnetic fields of order 10^17 gauss, symmetric nuclear matter becomes less bound and its saturation density increases, but this conclusion depends on including the coupling of the magnetic field to both the charge and the magnetic dipole moment of baryons; when the dipole moment strength is increased, the saturation density decreases and the system becomes more tightly bound.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation within quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) with four parameter sets (QHD1, NL3, FSU, and IU-FSU) to calculate the properties of cold, saturated symmetric nuclear matter in strong magnetic fields. The magnetic field was coupled to both the proton charge (producing Landau quantization) and the magnetic dipole moments of protons and neutrons via the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) coupling term in the Lagrangian density. The saturation density was found by minimizing the binding energy per nucleon as a function of density for each magnetic field strength, and the compression modulus and symmetry energy coefficient were then evaluated at that saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density at B=0 is 0.148 fm^-3 with binding energy about -16.3 MeV for all QHD parameter sets
- At B approximately 3 x 10^17 G, the system becomes unbound (binding energy becomes positive) for g_b = g_b^(0), corresponding to saturation densities between 1.2 and 1.4 times the zero-field value
- The normal proton magnetic dipole moment is 2.793 mu_N and the neutron dipole moment is -1.913 mu_N (in units of nuclear magneton mu_N)
- For g_b = 30 g_b^(0), the last lambda = -1 proton Landau level depopulates at B approximately 3 x 10^17 G

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Chakrabarty et al. (1996) who first noted the increase in saturation density with magnetic field but found the system became more tightly bound, a difference attributed to the inclusion of the AMM coupling. It builds on the work of Broderick et al. (2000) who emphasized the importance of including baryon dipole moment coupling, and on studies by Dong et al. and Casali et al. who investigated magnetized nuclear matter with QHD parameter sets. The results enable future investigations of magnetar interior structure, particularly regarding how the equation of state and composition are affected by magnetic fields up to 10^17 G and by possible density-dependent baryon magnetic dipole moments.
