# Generalized parton distributions of sea quarks in the proton from nonlocal chiral effective theory

> We calculate the spin-averaged generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of sea quarks in the proton at zero skewness from nonlocal covariant chiral effective theory, including one-loop contributions from intermediate states with pseudoscalar mesons and octet and decuplet baryons. A relativistic regulator is generated from the nonlocal Lagrangian where a gauge link is introduced to guarantee local gauge invariance, with additional diagrams from the expansion of the gauge link ensuring conservation of electric charge and strangeness. Flavor asymmetries for sea quarks at zero and finite momentum transfer, as well as strange form factors, are obtained from the calculated GPDs, and results compared with phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper calculates spin-averaged generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of sea quarks in the proton at zero skewness using nonlocal covariant chiral effective theory, including one-loop contributions from intermediate states with pseudoscalar mesons and octet and decuplet baryons, with the caveat that the results are obtained without fine-tuning parameters and are compared with phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors derive the nonlocal chiral effective Lagrangian by introducing a gauge link to guarantee local gauge invariance, generating a relativistic regulator that makes loop integrals convergent and provides momentum-dependent form factors at tree level. They compute one-loop splitting functions for nucleon to meson plus octet and decuplet baryon processes from rainbow, Kroll-Ruderman, tadpole, and bubble diagrams, then convolve these splitting functions with the GPDs of the bare hadrons to obtain the unpolarized electric (H) and magnetic (E) GPDs for sea quarks. The magnetic Lagrangian at O(q²) is included for the octet, decuplet, and octet-decuplet transition interactions, with additional next-to-leading order baryon-meson interaction terms involving two derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar decay constant is f = 93 MeV.
- The coefficients for the next-to-leading order baryon-meson interaction are b₉ = 1.36 GeV⁻¹, b₁₀ = 1.24 GeV⁻¹, and b₁₁ = 0.46 GeV⁻¹.
- The electromagnetic and strange form factors of the nucleon obtained in this approach agree with experiment up to values of four-momentum transfer squared of ≈ 1 GeV².
- The chiral EFT scale Λχ ∼ 1 GeV is the scale associated with the chiral effective theory.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal chiral effective theory approach, previously applied to the d̄-ū flavor asymmetry in the proton (Salamu, 2014), the strange-antistrange PDF asymmetry s-s̄ (Wang, 2016; Salamu, 2018; Salamu, 2019), and the sea quark Sivers function (He, 2019), to the calculation of sea quark GPDs for the first time. The results are compared with phenomenological extractions and lattice QCD, and the study is timely given the ongoing experimental program of deeply-virtual Compton scattering and hard exclusive meson production measurements at Jefferson Lab with the 12 GeV CEBAF accelerator and plans for future studies at the Electron-Ion Collider.
