# A new dynamical mechanism of incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collision

> The incomplete fusion has been proved as the formation and emission of the $α$ particle by the increase in the rotational energy of the very mass-asymmetric dinuclear system. The results of the dinuclear system model have confirmed that the incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collisions occurs at a large orbital angular momentum ($L > 30 \hbar$) due to the strong increase of the intrinsic fusion barrier.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that incomplete fusion in heavy-ion collisions is not caused by projectile breakup (as assumed in prior models), but rather by a very mass-asymmetric quasifission of the dinuclear system (DNS) formed after full momentum transfer, occurring specifically at large orbital angular momentum (L > 30 hbar) due to the strong increase of the intrinsic fusion barrier.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate the potential energy surface, intrinsic fusion barrier, quasifission barrier, and excitation energy of the DNS as functions of charge asymmetry and orbital angular momentum. They solved a transport master equation to obtain the time evolution of the charge distribution and the yield of alpha particles (Z=2) as quasifission fragments, then computed partial cross sections for incomplete fusion and used the statistical model KEWPIE2 to calculate evaporation residue cross sections for neutron emission channels.

## Key numbers

- Incomplete fusion occurs at orbital angular momentum L > 30 hbar, with favorable ranges L = 35-60 hbar for the 16O+130Te reaction and L = 25-40 hbar for the 18O+93Nb reaction.
- The mean angular momentum values <L> = 40 hbar (from the 14N+159Tb reaction by Inamura (1977)) and <L> = 30 hbar (from the 16O+146Nd reaction by Barker) are consistent with the calculated favorable angular momentum ranges.
- The DNS excitation energy for incomplete fusion in the 18O+93Nb reaction spans the range E*_Z = 10-50 MeV for angular momenta L = 35-50 hbar.
- For the 16O+130Te reaction at E_c.m. = 78.4 MeV and L = 40 hbar, the evolution of the charge distribution and alpha-particle yield was calculated with orientation angles alpha_1 = 45 degrees and alpha_2 = 30 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model previously developed by Nasirov (2005) and Kayumov (2022) to describe incomplete fusion as a quasifission process, contradicting the breakup fusion model of Udagawa (1980) and the sum-rule model of Wilczynski (1980, 1982) which assumed projectile breakup as the mechanism. The new mechanism explains the plateau in excitation functions of evaporation residues for 3n-4n channels observed in reactions studied by Singh (2014) and Agarwal, and enables improved theoretical reproduction of measured cross sections compared to standard statistical models ALICE-91 and PACE4.
