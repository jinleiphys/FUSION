# Amplitudes and Resonances from an Energy-Dependent Analysis of pbar+p -> pi+pi

> The amplitudes at a series of discrete energies obtained from a previuos analysis of pbar+p -> pi+pi have been used as input to a global energy- dependent analysis of data in the momentum range 360 - 1550 MeV/c. The results confirm the previous analysis and yield refined values for meson resonance parameters in this energy region.

# Amplitudes and Resonances from an Energy-Dependent Analysis of pbar+p -> pi+pi

## Key claim

The energy-dependent analysis confirms the previous single-energy analysis (Martin and Oades, 1997) and yields refined meson resonance parameters, but the authors caution that the quality of fits over a range of energies is considerably worse than at single energies, and that they are not attempting a systematic search of the entire parameter space but rather testing the compatibility of the energy dependence of their previous solution with the whole data set.

## Method

The authors used the output amplitudes from their previous single-energy analysis (Martin and Oades, 1997) as starting values for a global energy-dependent fit to differential cross-section and polarization data for pbar+p -> pi+pi in the momentum range 360-1550 MeV/c. The partial-wave helicity amplitudes in the JL basis were parametrized with a Breit-Wigner resonance term plus a polynomial background, and robust estimation was used to reduce the influence of isolated discrepant data points. The fit included 2524 data points from the pi-pi+ and pi0pi0 channels, supplemented by 3304 invariant amplitude values from hyperbolic dispersion relations (Martin and Oades, 1988).

## Key numbers

- Momentum range of the analysis: 360-1550 MeV/c
- Total data points used: 2524 experimental data points plus 3304 invariant amplitude values
- Average chi-squared per data point: 2.05 (pi-pi+ channel), 0.61 (pi0pi0 channel), 0.11 (invariant amplitudes)
- Renormalizations of experimental dcs data were typically less than 10%
- Removing 69 of 1973 pi-pi+ data points reduced the average chi-squared per data point from 2.05 to 1.45
- Resonance parameters for J=0: mass 1.95 GeV, width 0.17-0.18 GeV, product of branching ratios B0 = 0.13-0.15
- Resonance parameters for J=1: mass 1.96 GeV, width 0.15-0.17 GeV, B1 = 0.059-0.064
- Resonance parameters for J=2: mass 1.93 GeV, width 0.14-0.15 GeV, B2 = 0.011
- Resonance parameters for J=3: mass 2.02 GeV, width 0.23 GeV, B3 = 0.002-0.006
- Resonance parameters for J=4: mass 2.00 GeV, width 0.16-0.18 GeV, B4 = 0.0022-0.0024
- Resonance parameters for J=5: mass 2.19 GeV, width 0.22 GeV, B5 = 0.0011-0.0018

## Context

This work extends the single-energy analysis of Martin and Oades (1997) by incorporating correlations between amplitudes at different energies and between different partial waves, which were not included in the previous analysis. The results confirm the earlier analysis and rule out other published solutions from earlier works such as Martin and Pennington (1979, 1980), Hassan and Bugg (1994), and Oakden and Pennington (1994). The refined resonance parameters, particularly for the J=4 wave where a smaller width closer to the established f4(2050) is found, enable more reliable comparisons with the established meson resonance spectrum and provide constraints for theoretical models of the nucleon-antinucleon interaction.
