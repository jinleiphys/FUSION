# Calculations of bound-state $β^-$-decay half-lives of highly ionized $^{163}$Dy$^{66+}$, $^{187}$Re$^{75+}$ and $^{205}$Tl$^{81+}$

> We propose a theoretical method to calculate the bound-state $β$-decay half-lives of highly-ionized atoms, which is based on the combination of the Takahashi-Yokoi model and our recently-developed projected shell model that can take into account both allowed and first-forbidden transitions of nuclear $β$ decay. Three examples that are of much experimental interests, $^{163}$Dy$^{66+}$, $^{187}$Re$^{75+}$ and $^{205}$Tl$^{81+}$ are taken for calculations. The ground and low-lying states of related nuclei are described reasonably. The bound-state $β$-decay half-lives of $^{163}$Dy$^{66+}$ and $^{187}$Re$^{75+}$ are described within a factor of two and four by our calculations without and with the quenching factors in allowed and first-forbidden transitions. The bound-state $β$-decay half-life of the last $s$-process branching point $^{205}$Tl$^{81+}$ is predicted to be 58 and 305 days for cases without and with the quenching factors in calculations. The presented method provides a theoretical way to calculate systematically the bound-state $β$-decay half-lives of nuclei from light to heavy ones including odd-mass and even-mass cases for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors propose a theoretical method combining the Takahashi-Yokoi model with their recently-developed projected shell model (PSM) to calculate bound-state β-decay half-lives, claiming this is the first systematic microscopic approach for nuclei from light to heavy including odd-mass and even-mass cases. The method reproduces measured half-lives within a factor of two (without quenching factors) and within a factor of four (with quenching factors) for the two cases with experimental data.

## Method

The method combines the Takahashi-Yokoi model for the lepton phase-space factor with the projected shell model (PSM) that calculates nuclear transition matrix elements for both allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden transitions. The PSM uses exact angular-momentum projection to obtain nuclear wave functions in the laboratory frame from intrinsic quasiparticle configurations, and the bound-state β-decay rate is computed as λ = Σ_F (ln2 / (ft)_IF) × f*_IF, where f* is the lepton phase-space function for electron capture into bound orbitals (K, L, etc.) of the daughter atom.

## Context

This work extends the Takahashi-Yokoi model (Takahashi and Yokoi 1983) by replacing empirical nuclear transition strengths with microscopic calculations from the projected shell model, which was recently developed to handle both allowed GT transitions (Wang 2018) and first-forbidden transitions (Wang 2024). The three nuclei studied are s-process branching points of experimental interest: ¹⁶³Dy was first observed to undergo β_b-decay by Jung (1992), ¹⁸⁷Re was measured by Bosch (1996), and ²⁰⁵Tl (the last s-process branching point) was recently measured at GSI with results pending (Litvinov 2023). The method enables systematic calculation of β_b-decay half-lives for the many s-process related nuclei listed in the Takahashi-Yokoi table (1987), and future work will combine this with the Saha equation to update effective stellar β-decay rates under realistic stellar conditions.
