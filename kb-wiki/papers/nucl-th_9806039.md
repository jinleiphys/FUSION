# Probing Chiral Symmetry Restoration with Heavy Ions

> It is discussed how chiral symmetry restoration manifests itself through mixing of vector and axial-vector correlators. The vector correlator is directly accessible in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Within models of the vector correlator its implications for low-mass dilepton spectra are reviewed.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry restoration in hot/dense hadronic matter can be signaled by the mixing of vector and axial-vector correlators, which become identical at the phase boundary; however, the observation of such mixing is difficult because only the vector correlator is directly accessible via electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors review hadronic models for the in-medium vector correlator, focusing on the rho meson, using the Vector Dominance Model (VDM) combined with chiral dynamics. They incorporate medium modifications from a pion gas (via thermal broadening and mixing with the axial-vector correlator) and from baryonic effects, including resonant rho-nucleon scattering through baryon resonances such as N(1520), N(1720), and Delta(1905), as well as pion cloud modifications from in-medium pion propagation. The resulting spectral functions are constrained by photoabsorption data on nucleons and nuclei, and are then used to compute dilepton production rates in a fireball model for comparison with CERN-SpS data.

## Key numbers

* Transition temperature from lattice QCD: T_c = 150 ± 20 MeV, at which the chiral condensate decreases sharply.
* Vacuum rho-meson mass: 770 MeV; vacuum a1-meson mass: 1260 MeV.
* In the low-temperature limit, the mixing parameter epsilon = T^2 / (6 F_pi^2), where F_pi is the pion decay constant; chiral symmetry is restored when epsilon = 1/2.
* The hadronic form factor at the pi-N-N vertex requires Lambda_{piNN} ≈ 300–400 MeV, constrained by pi-N to rho-N production data.
* In the dilepton invariant mass spectrum, the major part of the enhancement for 0.2 GeV < M_ee < 0.6 GeV is ascribed to small pair momenta q_t ≤ 0.7 GeV.
* The meson/baryon ratio at SpS energies is 5–7.

## Context

This paper reviews theoretical work extending the in-medium Weinberg sum rules derived by Kapusta and Shuryak (1990s) and the low-temperature mixing formalism of Eletsky and Ioffe (1990s) to heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the "Brown-Rho scaling" hypothesis (1991) for dropping vector meson masses and on chiral perturbation theory results by Gasser and Leutwyler (1980s). The models discussed, particularly those by Rapp, Wambach, and collaborators, provide quantitative predictions for dilepton spectra that can be tested against data from the CERES and NA50 collaborations at CERN-SpS, and enable further calculations with hydrodynamical simulations or transport models such as HSD.
