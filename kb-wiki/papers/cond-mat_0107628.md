# Low energy Collective Modes of a Superfluid Trapped atomic Fermi Gas

> We consider the low energy collective mode spectrum of a superfluid Fermi gas in a spherical trap in the collisionless regime. Using a self-consistent random-phase approximation, the effects of superfluidity on modes of dipole and quadrupole symmetries are systematically examined. The spectrum is calculated for varying pairing strength and temperature and we identify several spectral features such as the emergence of Goldstone modes that can be used to detect the onset of superfluidity. Our analysis is relevant for present experiments aimed at observing a superfluid phase transition in trapped Fermi gases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that superfluidity in a trapped atomic Fermi gas produces qualitative, experimentally detectable changes in the low-energy collective mode spectrum of dipole and quadrupole symmetry, specifically the emergence of a low-frequency quadrupole response and the damping of the spin-dipole mode, but these effects depend on the pairing strength relative to the trap frequency and on temperature.

## Method

The authors use a self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) generalized to superfluid systems, starting from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method to obtain quasiparticle energies and wave functions from the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for a spherically symmetric harmonic trap. They then construct the full RPA response matrix, including density-density, density-pairing, and pairing-pairing correlation functions, and compute the strength function for operators of dipole and quadrupole symmetry. Ultraviolet divergences in the pairing correlation functions are removed via a pseudopotential regularization procedure.

## Key numbers

- For weak pairing: \(\Delta(r=0) \simeq 0.69 \hbar \omega_T\) at \(T=0\) for \(\sim 1.4 \times 10^4\) atoms, with the chemical potential at \(\mu_F = 32 \hbar \omega_T\); the low-energy quadrupole response appears at \(\omega \sim 0.4 \omega_T\), corresponding to pair-breaking excitations with energy \(\sim 2\Delta\).
- For strong pairing: \(\Delta(r=0) = 6 \hbar \omega_T\) at \(T=0\) for \(\sim 1.6 \times 10^4\) atoms; the quadrupole Goldstone mode appears at \(\omega \simeq 1.4 \omega_T\), consistent with the hydrodynamic prediction of \(\omega = \sqrt{2} \omega_T\).
- The critical temperature for the strong-pairing case is \(k_B T_c \simeq 2.8 \hbar \omega_T\); for the weak-pairing case, \(k_B T_c \simeq 0.9 \hbar \omega_T\).
- The lowest quasiparticle energies for strong pairing are \(E_{\eta=0l} \gtrsim \hbar \omega_T\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collective modes in normal trapped Fermi gases (Bruun, 2000) and homogeneous superconductors (Minguzzi, 2000) to the trapped superfluid case, and it builds on proposals for detecting the superfluid transition using collective properties (Zambelli, 2000). The results enable experimental detection of the BCS transition in ultracold Fermi gases by measuring shape oscillations of the atomic cloud, analogous to techniques used in Bose-Einstein condensation (Edwards, 1996). The paper also connects to hydrodynamic theory for superfluid modes (Martin, 1967; Baranov, 2000) and to the concept of Goldstone modes in neutral superfluids.
