# Charm quarks in medium and their contribution to di-electron spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the dynamics of charm quarks in the partonic medium and its implication to the di-electron spectra in high energy heavy ion collisions. The charm quarks traversing a thermal medium is simulated by the relativistic Langevin equation for elastic scatterings of charm quarks by thermal partons in an expanding fireball. The transport coefficients of charm quarks are calculated by the in-medium T-matrix method, where a static heavy quark potential is used with parameters fitted by the lattice QCD results. The di-electron invariant mass spectra are computed in most central collisions and are compared to the STAR data. The angular correlations of di-electrons are almost the same in $p+p$ and Au+Au collisions in the mass range $1.1<M<2.5~\mathrm{GeV/c^2}$ with the back-to-back feature. This means that the angular correlation is intact even with medium interaction at the RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The angular correlation of di-electrons from semi-leptonic decays of open charm hadrons remains almost intact in Au+Au collisions compared to p+p collisions in the mass range 1.1 < M < 2.5 GeV/c², meaning the back-to-back feature survives medium interaction at RHIC energy; the suppression of the charm background in the intermediate mass region is dominated by charm quark energy loss rather than modification of angular correlations.

## Method

The authors simulated charm quark propagation through a partonic medium using a relativistic Langevin equation for elastic scatterings by thermal partons, with the fireball expansion described by a (2+1)-dimension ideal hydrodynamic model. Transport coefficients for charm quarks were calculated via the in-medium T-matrix method using a static heavy quark potential with parameters fitted to lattice QCD results for the color-averaged free energy. Charm quark pairs were generated by PYTHIA, evolved through the medium via the Hydro-Langevin simulation, then returned to PYTHIA for hadronization and decay to produce di-electron spectra, which were compared to STAR data for most central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy density at the center of the overlapping region at impact parameter b = 0 was set to ε₀ = 55 GeV/fm³ with equilibrium time τ₀ = 0.4 fm, determined by fitting transverse momentum spectra of long-lived hadrons.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature was set to T_chem = 150 MeV and kinetic freeze-out temperature to T_f = 106 MeV; the charm quark freeze-out (transition) temperature was T_c = 184 MeV.
- The time interval for position and momentum updates in the Langevin equation was set to dτ = 0.01 fm in the lab frame.
- The integrated yield of di-electrons per event from heavy-flavor decays in the mass range [1.1, 2.5] GeV/c² within PHENIX acceptance was (4.21 ± 0.28 ± 1.02) × 10⁻⁸.
- The default Gaussian k_T width of primordial partons in PYTHIA 6.4 was ⟨k_T⟩ = 2 GeV; a comparison was made with ⟨k_T⟩ = 1 GeV (default of PYTHIA 6.3).
- The lower freeze-out temperature tested was T_c = 159 MeV, corresponding to energy density e_c = 0.445 GeV/fm³.
- The width parameter for the coalescence model was ω = 0.33 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by some of the authors (Xu et al., 2012) that used a naive model for medium modification of charm quarks and neglected angular correlation modifications. It employs the in-medium T-matrix method (Mannarelli, 2005; van Hees, 2008; Riek, 2010; Huggins, 2012) for non-perturbative heavy quark transport coefficients, which yields relaxation rates much larger than perturbative QCD predictions due to Feshbach resonance structures in heavy-light quark scattering. The results enable more realistic modeling of the charm background to thermal dilepton signals in the intermediate mass region at RHIC, and the approach can be extended to LHC energies where charm quark regeneration and next-to-leading order processes become important.
