# Three-body Thomas-Ehrman shifts of analog states of $^{17}$Ne and $^{17}$N

> The lowest-lying states of the Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne ($^{15}$O+$p$ + $p$) and its mirror nucleus $^{17}$N ($^{15}$N+$n$ + $n$) are compared by using the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion. Three-body resonances are computed by use of the complex scaling method. The measured size of $^{15}$O and the low-lying resonances of $^{16}$F ($^{15}$O+$p$) are first used as constraints to determine both central and spin-dependent two-body interactions. The interaction obtained reproduces relatively accurately both experimental three-body spectra. The Thomas-Ehrman shifts, involving excitation energy differences, are computed and found to be less than 3% of the total Coulomb energy shift for all states.

## Key claim

The Thomas-Ehrman shifts for analog states of the Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne and its mirror nucleus $^{17}$N are computed to be less than 3% of the total Coulomb energy shift for all states, but the computed excitation energies of $^{17}$N are systematically higher than the measured values, with discrepancies that depend on the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion method to solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space for the three-body systems $^{15}$O+$p$+$p$ ($^{17}$Ne) and $^{15}$N+$n$+$n$ ($^{17}$N). Three-body resonances were computed using the complex scaling method. Two-body nucleon-core interactions were first constrained by fitting the measured size of $^{15}$O and the low-lying resonances of $^{16}$F ($^{15}$O+$p$), then applied to the mirror system by switching off the Coulomb interaction.

## Key numbers

- Ground state two-proton separation energy of $^{17}$Ne: $-944$ keV (bound)
- Computed root mean square radius of $^{17}$Ne ground state: 2.8 fm, consistent with experimental value $2.75\pm0.07$ fm (Ozawa 1994)
- Excitation energies of $^{17}$Ne: $3/2^-$ state at $1288\pm8$ keV, $5/2^-$ state at $1764\pm12$ keV, $7/2^-$ state at $2997\pm11$ keV, $9/2^-$ state at $3548\pm20$ keV (Gui 1998)
- $^{17}$N ground state binding energy: $-8.54$ MeV (computed) vs. $-8.374$ MeV (experimental, Ajzenberg-Selove 1986)
- Thomas-Ehrman shifts for all states: less than 3% of the total Coulomb energy shift
- For the $3/2^-$ state, experimental Thomas-Ehrman shift $\Delta_e = 0.08$ MeV, computed shift $\Delta_{th} = 0.62$ MeV (G+WS calculation)
- For the $5/2^-$ state, experimental Thomas-Ehrman shift $\Delta_e = 0.15$ MeV, computed shift $\Delta_{th} = 0.46$ MeV (G+WS calculation)

## Context

This work extends the study of Thomas-Ehrman shifts to three-body Borromean systems, following earlier investigations by Grigorenko et al. (2002) on $^{12}$O and $^{16}$Ne. The authors resolve a controversy from prior three-body models of $^{17}$Ne by Grigorenko et al. (2003) and Timofeyuk et al. (1996), which had reversed the ordering of the $3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ levels; the present calculation reproduces the correct ordering using only two-body forces that properly describe the $^{16}$F spectrum. The results enable future studies of how Coulomb effects modify nuclear structure in weakly-bound proton-rich systems near the dripline.
