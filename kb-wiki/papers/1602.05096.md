# Neutrino induced 1-pion production

> Neutrino-induced pion production constitutes an important contribution to neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections at intermediate energies. A deep understanding of this process is mandatory for a correct interpretation of neutrino-oscillation experiments. We aim at contributing to the ongoing effort to understand the various experimental results obtained by different collaborations in a wide range of energies. In particular, in this work we analyze recent MiniBooNE and MINERvA charged-current neutrino 1-pion production data. We use a relativistic theoretical approach which accounts for resonant and non-resonant 1-pion production contributions.

## Key claim

The paper's relativistic model, which includes resonant (Delta and D13) and non-resonant contributions, reproduces the shape of MiniBooNE and MINERvA charged-current neutrino 1-pion production data but systematically underpredicts the absolute cross sections when medium modifications of the Delta resonance are applied.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) framework where the W+ boson couples to a single off-shell nucleon in the nucleus. The hadronic current includes nine diagrams: Delta pole, crossed Delta pole, D13 pole, crossed D13 pole, nucleon pole, crossed nucleon pole, contact term, pion pole, and pion-in-flight term. Medium modifications of the Delta resonance width and mass are implemented using the Oset and Salcedo formalism, with the nuclear density fixed at 0.75 times saturation density.

## Key numbers

- The Delta decay constant is set to f_pi N Delta = 2.21, derived from the free Delta decay width Gamma_free(W = M_Delta) = 120 MeV
- The axial form factor parameters used are C5^A(0) = 1.2 and M_A = 1.05 GeV, tuned to BNL data for the nu_mu + p -> mu- + p + pi+ channel
- Medium modifications reduce the Delta cross sections by approximately 40-50% and reduce the full-model cross sections by approximately 20%
- The real part of the Delta self-energy is set to Re(Sigma_Delta) = 40 MeV * (rho/rho_0), with rho_0 = 17 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the relativistic approach of Hernandez (2007) and Hernandez (2013) for neutrino-induced pion production by implementing in-medium modifications of the Delta resonance using the Oset and Salcedo (1987) formalism. The model is compared against recent MiniBooNE (2011) and MINERvA (2015) data, and the results are notably lower than those of Hernandez (2013) and Sobczyk (2013) when medium modifications are included. The authors suggest that exploring different implementations of medium modifications, as well as including additional resonances, coherent pion production, and multi-pion final states, may improve agreement with data.
