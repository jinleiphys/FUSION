# "Explosive regime" should dominate collisions of ultra-high energy cosmic rays

> Since the launch of LHC experiments it has been discovered that the high multiplicity trigger in pp, pA collisions finds events behaving differently from the typical (minimally biased) ones. In central pPb case it has been proven that those possess collective phenomena known as the radial, elliptic and triangular flows, similar to what is known in heavy ion (AA) collisions. In this paper we argue that at the ultra-high energies, E_lab ~ 10^{20} eV, of the observed cosmic rays this regime changes from a small-probability fluctuation to a dominant one. We estimate velocity of the transverse collective expansion for the light-light and heavy-light collisions, and find it comparable to what is observed at LHC for the central PbPb case. We argue that significant changes of spectra of various secondaries associated with this phenomenon should be important for the development of the cosmic ray cascades.

## Key claim
At ultra-high energies (E_lab ~ 10^20 eV) of observed cosmic rays, the "explosive regime" characterized by collective flow should change from a small-probability fluctuation (P ~ 10^-6 in pp collisions at LHC) to the dominant behavior with probability O(1), based on extrapolation of multiplicity growth with energy.

## Method
The authors use Gubser's analytic hydrodynamic solution to model transverse collective expansion in light-light (e.g., pO) and heavy-light (e.g., FeO) collisions at cosmic-ray energies. They estimate initial system sizes from nuclear radii (R_O ≈ 3 fm, R_Fe ≈ 4.8 fm) and scale multiplicities by a factor of 3 from LHC to GZK energies. They apply the Cooper-Frye formula to obtain particle spectra and mean transverse momenta, and also consider a "spaghetti collapse" scenario where the initial radius is reduced by ΔR = -1 fm.

## Key numbers
- Maximum cosmic-ray laboratory energy: E_lab ≲ 10^20 eV, corresponding to √s_max ≈ 450 TeV for pp collisions
- Multiplicity enhancement factor from LHC to GZK energy: dN^GZK/dy / dN^LHC/dy ≈ 3
- pp cross section at ultra-high energies: ~150 mb, giving typical impact parameter b ≈ 2 fm
- Light nucleus (oxygen) radius: R_O ≈ 3 fm; iron radius: R_Fe ≈ 4.8 fm
- Mean p_T for pO collisions (compressed case): pions 0.76 GeV/c, kaons 0.96 GeV/c, protons 1.17 GeV/c
- Mean p_T for FeO collisions (compressed case): pions 0.69 GeV/c, kaons 0.88 GeV/c, protons 1.09 GeV/c
- Mean p_T for central PbPb at LHC (benchmark): pions 0.73 GeV/c, kaons 0.92 GeV/c, protons 1.13 GeV/c
- Number of participant nucleons in heavy-light collisions: N_p^FeO ~ 70; in light-light collisions: ⟨N_p⟩ ≈ 16

## Context
This work extends the observation of collective flow phenomena (radial, elliptic, triangular flows) in high-multiplicity pPb collisions at LHC, discovered by the CMS collaboration (Chatrchyan et al., 2013), to the ultra-high energy regime of cosmic rays observed by the Pierre Auger Observatory. The authors argue against the "Chiral Symmetry Restoration" toy model of Farrar and Allen (2013), which proposed suppressed pion production, instead emphasizing that radial flow (not just elliptic flow) significantly modifies transverse momenta of secondaries, especially nucleons. This work enables improved modeling of cosmic-ray air showers by replacing extrapolated pp spectra with hydrodynamically computed spectra from collective explosions.
