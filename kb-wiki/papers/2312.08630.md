# Pseudo-neutrino versus recoil formalism for 4-body phase space and applications to nuclear decay

> It is well-known that the traditional treatment of radiative corrections that utilizes the "true" neutrino momentum $\vec{p}_ν$ in the differential decay rate formula could lead to a $\sim α/π$ systematic error in certain observables due to the mistreatment of 4-body kinematics. We investigate the theory structure of one of the proposed solutions, the "$ν'$-formalism", in the non-recoil limit appropriate for neutron and nuclear beta decays. We derive an elegant master formula for the 4-body phase space and use it to re-analyze the spectrum-dependent "outer" radiative corrections to the beta decay of a polarized spin-half nucleus; a complete set of analytic expressions is provided for readers to straightforwardly obtain the final numerical results. We compare it to the "recoil formalism" where the energy of the recoil nucleus is fixed.

# Pseudo-neutrino versus recoil formalism for 4-body phase space and applications to nuclear decay

## Key claim

The traditional "true neutrino" formalism for radiative corrections in beta decay introduces a systematic error of order α/π in observables dependent on the neutrino direction, because it incorrectly treats 4-body kinematics when an undetected bremsstrahlung photon is present; the pseudo-neutrino (ν') formalism resolves this issue while retaining analytic elegance and experimental measurability, provided one works in the non-recoil limit appropriate for neutron and nuclear decays.

## Method

The author derives a master formula for the 4-body phase space in the ν'-formalism, where the pseudo-neutrino momentum is defined as p_ν' ≡ -p_e - p_f (an experimentally measurable quantity even when an extra photon is emitted). The bremsstrahlung squared amplitude is split into an infrared-divergent piece and a regular piece; the former is combined with the virtual one-loop correction to yield an analytic, infrared-finite expression, while the regular piece is reduced to a one-fold numerical integral over the pseudo-neutrino momentum magnitude. The full outer radiative correction to the differential decay rate of a polarized spin-1/2 nucleus is expressed in terms of the measurable variables E_e, Ω_e, and Ω_ν'.

## Key numbers

- The systematic error from using the true neutrino formalism is of order α/π, where α is the fine-structure constant.
- The difference between the approximate 4-body phase space formula in the ν'-formalism and the exact recoil-formalism expression is at the level of 0.1% for neutron decay parameters, consistent with the expected recoil correction size ∼(m_n - m_p)/m_n.
- The electron end-point energy is defined as E_m ≡ (m_i² - m_f² + m_e²)/(2m_i) ≈ m_i - m_f in the non-recoil limit.
- The recoil correction on top of a radiative correction gives a contribution ∼(α/π)(m_i - m_f)/m_i ≲ 10⁻⁵ to the total decay rate.
- The function δ_an(E_e, c') is given by an analytic expression involving dilogarithms and hyperbolic functions, with c' ≡ cosθ_{eν'} = p̂_e·p̂_ν'.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-neutrino formalism previously developed for unpolarized and polarized neutron and hyperon decays by Toth (1988), Gluck (1989, 1992, 1998), and others, providing for the first time a complete set of analytic expressions for the outer radiative corrections to polarized spin-1/2 nuclear beta decays in the non-recoil limit. The author notes that some numerical results in earlier papers are not completely accurate, and that existing computer packages (e.g., Gluck 2022) are effectively black boxes. The formalism enables straightforward cross-checking with the traditional ν-formalism of Sirlin (1967), Shann (1971), and Garcia-Maya (1978), and is applicable to a wide range of processes including free neutron decay, superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ transitions (Hardy 2020), and spin-half mirror transitions such as ¹⁹Ne→¹⁹F (Naviliat-Cuncic 2008).
