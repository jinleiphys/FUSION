# Chiral effective-field theory in the Delta(1232) region: II. radiative pion photoproduction

> We present a theoretical study of the radiative pion photoproduction on the nucleon ($γN \to πN γ'$) in the $\De$-resonance region, with the aim to determine the magnetic dipole moment (MDM) of the $Δ^+(1232)$. The study is done within the framework of chiral effective-field theory where the expansion is performed (to next-to-leading order) in the $δ$ power-counting scheme which is an extension of chiral perturbation theory to the $Δ$-resonance energy region. We present the results for the absorptive part of the $Δ$ MDM, as well as perform a sensitivity study of the dependence of $γN \to πN γ'$ observables on the real part of the $Δ$ MDM. We find that an asymmetry for circular polarization of the photon beam may provide a model-independent way to measure the $Δ$ MDM.

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moment (MDM) of the Δ⁺(1232) resonance can be extracted from radiative pion photoproduction (γN → πN γ′) using chiral effective-field theory, but only if the calculation includes the soft-photon expansion to next-next-to-leading order (where the MDM first appears) combined with the δ-expansion to next-to-leading order; the circular polarization asymmetry of the photon beam is identified as a model-independent observable for this extraction.

## Method

The authors computed the γN → πN γ′ amplitude within chiral effective-field theory using the δ-power-counting scheme, which treats the Δ-resonance excitation energy (Δ ≡ M_Δ − M_N ≈ 0.3 GeV) and the pion mass as distinct light scales. They performed a soft-photon expansion of the amplitude to next-next-to-leading order in the outgoing photon energy ω′ and a chiral expansion to next-to-leading order in the δ-counting, including chiral-loop corrections to the Δ self-energy, the γNΔ vertex, and the γΔΔ vertex. The absorptive (imaginary) part of the Δ MDM was computed from the chiral loops, while the sensitivity of various observables to the real part of the Δ MDM was studied.

## Key numbers

* The Δ⁺(1232) MDM from the Particle Data Group (2006) is μ_Δ⁺ = 2.7 (+1.0/−1.3) (stat.) ± 1.5 (syst.) ± 3 (theor.) μ_N, where μ_N = e/2M_N is the nuclear magneton.
* The absorptive part of the Δ⁺ MDM is predicted as Im μ_Δ⁺ = 1.62 in units of (e/2M_Δ), computed from chiral loops with h_A ≈ 2.85 (determined from Γ_Δ^(exp) ≈ 115 MeV).
* The absorptive parts for other charge states are: Im μ_Δ⁺⁺ ≈ 3.74, Im μ_Δ⁰ ≈ −0.14, and Im μ_Δ⁻ ≈ −2.63, all in units of (e/2M_Δ).
* The πNΔ coupling constant is h_A ≈ 2.85, and the M1 and E2 N→Δ transition couplings are g_M = 2.97 and g_E = −1, respectively.
* The Δ-resonance excitation energy is Δ ≡ M_Δ − M_N ≈ 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous next-to-leading order calculation of pion photoproduction (Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen, 2005) to the radiative process, providing a more systematic framework than the phenomenological effective Lagrangian approach of Drechsel et al. (2001) used by the TAPS Collaboration at MAMI to extract the Δ⁺ MDM. The calculation enables a model-independent extraction of the Δ⁺ MDM from upcoming high-statistics data from the Crystal Ball detector at MAMI, and the predicted absorptive MDMs differ significantly from the earlier chiral EFT calculation of Hacker et al. (2006) due to different expansions and coupling values.
