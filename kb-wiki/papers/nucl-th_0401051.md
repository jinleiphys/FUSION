# Photoproduction of $K^*$ for the study of $Λ(1405)$

> The photo-induced $K^*$ vector meson production is investigated for the study of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. This reaction is particularly suited to the isolation of the second pole in the $Λ(1405)$ region which couples dominantly to the $\bar K N$ channel. We obtain the mass distribution of the $Λ(1405)$ which peaks at 1420 MeV, and differs from the nominal one. Combined with several other reactions, like the $π^- p \to K^0 πΣ$ which favours the first pole, this detailed study will reveal a novel structure of the $Λ(1405)$ state.

## Key claim

The reaction \(\gamma p \to K^* \Lambda(1405) \to \pi K \pi \Sigma\) is particularly suited to isolate the second (higher-mass) pole of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, which couples dominantly to the \(\bar K N\) channel; the mass distribution of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) obtained in this reaction peaks at 1420 MeV, differing from the nominal 1405 MeV peak, and this shape is clearly distinct from that seen in the \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma\) reaction which favours the first (lower-mass) pole.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for \(\gamma p \to K^* \Lambda(1405) \to \pi K \pi \Sigma\) using a model where the \(t\)-channel exchange is dominated by a kaon, with the \(\gamma \to K^* K\) vertex described by effective Lagrangians with coupling constants fixed from the empirical partial decay widths of the \(K^*\) (0.05 MeV for \(K^{*\pm} \to K^{\pm}\gamma\) and 51 MeV for \(K^{*\pm} \to K\pi\)). The \(K^- p \to MB\) scattering amplitude is taken from a chiral unitary model that generates the \(\Lambda(1405)\) dynamically, with an explicit \(\Sigma(1385)\) pole term added because the \(\Sigma(1385)\) is a \(p\)-wave resonance not generated by the \(s\)-wave chiral model. The total cross section and invariant mass distributions are obtained by integrating the squared amplitude over four-body phase space using Monte-Carlo methods.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants used are \(|g_{\gamma K^{*\pm} K^{\pm}}| = 0.252\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(g_{VPP} = -6.05\), determined from the empirical partial decay widths \(\Gamma_{K^{*\pm} \to K^{\pm}\gamma} = 0.05\) MeV and \(\Gamma_{K^{*\pm} \to K\pi} = 51\) MeV.
- The energy-dependent width of the \(K^*\) is parametrized as \(\Gamma_{K^*} = A p_{CM}^3\) with \(A = 2.05 \times 10^{-6}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), giving \(\Gamma_{K^*} \sim 51\) MeV at the resonance position.
- The \(\pi^0 \Sigma^0\) invariant mass distribution from \(\Lambda(1405)\) decay peaks at 1420 MeV, corresponding to the higher pole position.
- The initial photon energy chosen for the invariant mass distributions is \(E_\gamma = 2500\) MeV, while the threshold for \(K^* \Lambda(1405)\) production is 2350 MeV.
- The two poles of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) from prior work are cited at \(z_1 = 1390 - 66i\) MeV and \(z_2 = 1426 - 16i\) MeV (Jido 2003).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, which has been shown by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), and Lutz (2001) to be a dynamically generated quasibound state of coupled meson-baryon channels. The existence of two poles in the \(\Lambda(1405)\) region was first found in the cloudy bag model by Fink (1990) and later detailed in chiral dynamics by Jido (2003), who found poles at 1390 - 66i MeV (coupling to \(\pi\Sigma\)) and 1426 - 16i MeV (coupling to \(\bar K N\)). The present reaction complements the \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma\) reaction studied by Hyodo (2003), which favours the first pole, and the \(K^- p \to \gamma \pi \Sigma\) reaction studied by Nacher (1999), which gave a similar mass distribution to the present one. Experimental confirmation of the two-pole structure would provide new clues to non-perturbative QCD dynamics.
