# Strangeness equilibration in heavy ion collisions

> Using a relativistic transport model for heavy ion collisions at energies that are below the threshold for kaon and antikaon production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, we study how their abundances approach the canonical equilibrium during the collisions. We find that kaons are far from chemical equilibrium at the initial and high density stage, and they approach equilibrium only during the expansion stage of the collisions when their production rate is small and becomes comparable to their annihilation rate. In contrast, antikaons approach chemical equilibrium much earlier but eventually fall out of equilibrium again as a result of their large annihilation cross sections in nuclear matter.

# Strangeness equilibration in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Kaons approach chemical equilibrium only during the expansion stage of heavy ion collisions at subthreshold energies, when their production rate becomes small and comparable to their annihilation rate; antikaons approach chemical equilibrium much earlier but eventually fall out of equilibrium again due to their large annihilation cross sections in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model (RVUU) based on the nonlinear Walecka model, including both scalar and vector mean-field potentials for nucleons and kaons, to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1A GeV and impact parameter b=0 fm. They improved the perturbative treatment of strange particle production by including kaon annihilation by hyperons via detailed balance relations, which had been neglected in previous transport model studies of subthreshold kaon production.

## Key numbers

- Kaon yield reduction from including kaon annihilation by hyperon: about 10% in Ni+Ni collisions at 1A GeV and b=0 fm
- Kaon production and annihilation rates become comparable at about 17 fm/c
- Baryon density at which kaons reach chemical equilibrium: about 1.2 ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Kaon chemical equilibration time at t=12 fm/c: 60 fm/c, derived from N_K = 4.2 × 10⁻³ and dN_L/dt = 7.0 × 10⁻⁵
- Thermal average of kaon annihilation cross section σ_{KY→πN}: about 0.25 fm² at temperature T=75 MeV, changing by less than 20% for 50 < T < 100 MeV
- Effective volume in which kaon annihilation occurs: about 15 fm³

## Context

This work extends kinetic theory predictions by Rate (year not specified) that canonical ensemble equilibration times for rare particles carrying U(1) charge are much shorter than grand canonical expectations, and applies them to a full transport model. It contradicts the suggestion by Brown (year not specified) that kaon equilibration time could be shortened by kaon mass reduction in dense matter via vector decoupling, noting that such vector decoupling is inconsistent with observed kaon flow at GSI and AGS energies. The study enables more accurate extraction of chemical freeze-out temperatures from thermal model analyses by showing that the final kaon abundance does not provide sufficient information on the dynamics of kaon production.
