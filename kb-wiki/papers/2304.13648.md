# Determination of meson fragmentation functions in the Field-Feynman model

> We study the fragmentation functions of both pions and kaons in the Field-Feynman recursive model with the extended SU(2) flavor symmetry relations of fragmentation functions and fitting parameters. Parametrizations are determined from a leading-order (LO) analysis of HERMES experimental multiplicity data of meson production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and uncertainties are estimated with the Hessian method. We compare our results with the experimental data and the analysis results of other parametrizations. The SU(2) flavor symmetry breaking effect of meson fragmentation functions of $ud$ quarks is also discussed, and we show that the fragmentation functions of kaons have a bigger SU(2) flavor symmetry breaking effect of $ud$ quarks than these of pions.

## Key claim

The authors determine pion and kaon fragmentation functions using the Field-Feynman recursive model with extended SU(2) flavor symmetry breaking, finding that kaon fragmentation functions exhibit a larger SU(2) flavor symmetry breaking effect of ud quarks than pion fragmentation functions, based on a leading-order analysis of HERMES semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering multiplicity data.

## Method

The authors parametrize meson fragmentation functions using the Field-Feynman recursive model with 8 free parameters per meson (pion or kaon), extending prior work by allowing d_u and d_d parameters to differ (breaking SU(2) flavor symmetry). They perform a leading-order chi-squared fit to 80 data points from HERMES multiplicity measurements on proton and deuteron targets, using MSTW08 LO parton distribution functions, and estimate uncertainties via the Hessian method with a tolerance parameter Delta-chi-squared = 9.3028 for 68% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- Total chi-squared per data point: 1.387 for 80 data points across 8 processes (pion and kaon production on proton and deuteron targets)
- For pion: d_u^pi = 1.724 +/- 0.523, d_d^pi = 2.205 +/- 0.479, d_s^pi = 1.777 +/- 0.586
- For kaon: d_u^K = 1.709 +/- 0.307, d_d^K = 2.126 +/- 0.116, d_s^K = 2.757 +/- 0.281
- For pion: beta_uu^pi = 0.487 +/- 0.022, beta_ud^pi = 0.470 +/- 0.014, beta_du^pi = 0.491 +/- 0.011, beta_dd^pi = 0.404 +/- 0.041, beta_su^pi = 0.430 +/- 0.018
- For kaon: beta_uu^K = 0.102 +/- 0.056, beta_us^K = 0.153 +/- 0.131, beta_du^K = 0.471 +/- 0.036, beta_su^K = 0.863 +/- 0.016, beta_ss^K = 0.050 +/- 0.013
- Mean Q^2 of HERMES data: 2.5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Field-Feynman model analysis of Hua and Ma (2003), which previously determined pion fragmentation functions from KLC parametrizations and could only predict kaon fragmentation functions due to lack of kaon data. The authors now use the final HERMES SIDIS data (2012) to independently determine both pion and kaon fragmentation functions, finding that SU(2) flavor symmetry breaking is significant for kaons but not for pions. The results enable comparison with DSS (de Florian, Sassot, Stratmann, 2007-2017) and HKNS (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai, Sudoh, 2007) parametrizations, and the authors note that including gluon contributions (absent in the Field-Feynman model) may be necessary for kaons, suggesting future analysis of neutral kaon data from COMPASS could resolve this.
