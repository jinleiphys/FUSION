# Kaon properties in (proto)neutron stars

> The modification on kaon and antikaon properties of in the interior of (proto-)neutron stars is investigated using a chiral SU(3) model. The parameters of the model are fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties, baryon octet vacuum masses, hyperon optical potentials and low energy a kaon-nucleon scattering lengths. We study the kaon/antikaon medium modification and explore the possibility of antikaon condensation in (proto-)neutron star matter at zero as well as finite temperature/entropy and neutrino content. The effect of hyperons on kaon and antikaon optical potentials is also investigated at different stages of the neutron star evolution.

## Key claim

Using a chiral SU(3) model, the authors find that antikaon condensation in (proto-)neutron stars is possible only in cold neutron star matter after neutrino emission has cooled the star, and even then it occurs only at the very center without causing considerable changes to macroscopic star properties. The abstract's mention of "exploring the possibility" glosses over the paper's main conclusion that condensation is ruled out for proto-neutron stars and is marginal for cold stars.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral SU(3) model in the mean-field approximation to describe hadronic matter inside (proto-)neutron stars, with parameters fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties, baryon octet vacuum masses, hyperon optical potentials, and low-energy kaon-nucleon scattering lengths. They compute kaon and antikaon dispersion relations and optical potentials in charge-neutral, isospin-asymmetric nucleonic and hyperonic matter, including the effects of finite temperature (via fixed entropy per baryon S/B=2) and neutrino trapping (via fixed lepton fraction Y_Le=0.4). The threshold for K- condensation is determined by the condition that the in-medium K- energy at zero momentum equals the electron chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Onset of K- condensation in cold (T=0) beta-equilibrated nucleonic matter occurs at a density of about 5.3 rho_0 (where rho_0 is nuclear saturation density).
- When hyperons are included in cold beta-equilibrated matter, the K- condensation threshold shifts to about 6.1 rho_0.
- With neutrino trapping (Y_Le=0.4) at zero temperature, K- condensation in nucleonic matter occurs at about 9.3 rho_0; with hyperons included, no condensation is seen up to 10 rho_0.
- For finite entropy per baryon S/B=2 in beta equilibrium, K- condensation occurs at about 6 rho_0 for nucleonic matter and about 7.4 rho_0 for hyperonic matter.
- With both S/B=2 and Y_Le=0.4, K- condensation in nucleonic matter occurs at about 10 rho_0; with hyperons, no condensation is seen up to 10 rho_0.
- The empirical KN scattering lengths used are a_KN(I=0) approx -0.09 fm and a_KN(I=1) approx -0.31 fm, giving an isospin-averaged value of about -0.255 fm.
- The parameters d_1 and d_2 are calculated as 2.5/m_K and 0.72/m_K respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; various authors on kaon condensation) by using a chiral SU(3) model that consistently includes hyperons, finite temperature, and neutrino trapping effects. The results contradict earlier calculations (Banik and Bandyopadhyay, 2008) that found a temperature of around 30 MeV sufficient to melt antikaon condensation in nucleonic matter, whereas the present model finds condensation persists at higher densities even with finite entropy. The findings enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling evolution, showing that antikaon condensation is relevant only for cold neutron stars and does not significantly affect macroscopic properties like mass and radius.
