# Special point "trains" in the M-R diagram of hybrid stars

> We present a systematic investigation of the possible locations for the special point (SP), a unique feature of hybrid neutron stars in the mass-radius diagram. The study is performed within the two-phase approach where the high-density (quark matter) phase is described by the covariant nonlocal Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model equation of state (EOS) which is shown to be equivalent to a constant-sound-speed (CSS) EOS. For the nuclear matter phase around saturation density different relativistic density functional EOSs are used: DD2p00, its excluded-volume modification DD2p40 and the hypernuclear EOS DD2Y-T. In the present contribution we apply the Maxwell construction scheme for the deconfinement transition and demonstrate that a simultaneous variation of the vector and diquark coupling constants results in the occurrence of SP "trains" which are invariant against changing the nuclear matter EOS. We propose that the SP train corresponding to a variation of the diquark coupling at constant vector coupling is special since it serves as a lower bound for the line of maximum masses and accessible radii of massive hybrid stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that varying the vector and diquark coupling constants in a quark matter equation of state (EOS) produces series of "special points" (SPs) in the mass-radius diagram of hybrid neutron stars, which line up in "trains" (SPTs) that are invariant under changes of the hadronic nuclear matter EOS. The authors propose that the SPT corresponding to variation of the diquark coupling at constant vector coupling serves as a lower bound for the line of maximum masses and accessible radii of massive hybrid stars.

## Method

The study uses a two-phase approach with a Maxwell construction for the deconfinement transition between a nuclear matter phase (described by relativistic density functional EOSs DD2p00, DD2p40, or the hypernuclear DD2Y-T) and a quark matter phase (described by the covariant nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model EOS, shown to be equivalent to a constant-sound-speed (CSS) EOS). The dimensionless vector meson coupling η_V = G_V/G_S and diquark coupling η_D = G_D/G_S are varied simultaneously in discrete steps, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius curves for each hybrid star sequence.

## Key numbers

* The focal point "X" where all SPTs meet has coordinates (M_X, R_X) = (1.8663 M_⊙, 11.112 km).
* The slope a_ξ of SPTs follows a_ξ = tan φ(ξ) = α - β ξ with α = 0.47074 and β = 0.7252.
* For the nlNJL model with hadronic phase DD2p40, the characteristic onset mass for deconfinement is M_onset^* = 0.75 M_⊙.
* For the confining relativistic density functional approach with DD2Y-T, the characteristic onset mass is M_onset^* = 1.4 M_⊙.
* The maximum mass follows M_max = M_SP + δ |M_onset^* - M_onset|^κ with κ = 2.

## Context

This work extends the concept of a single special point in hybrid star mass-radius diagrams, first identified by Yudin (2014) for a simple bag model with varying bag constant, to a systematic study of "trains" of special points arising from simultaneous variation of two Lagrangian parameters (vector and diquark couplings). It builds on earlier Bayesian analyses by Ayriyan (2021) and Shahrbaf (2021) that first observed these SPTs, and on studies by Cierniak (2020, 2021) that explored SP locations for CSS EOSs. The invariance of SPTs under changes of the hadronic EOS is demonstrated, contrasting with results from Sen (2022) where a density-dependent bag model showed SP non-invariance. The findings provide a phenomenological tool for interpreting mass-radius measurements from NICER (Miller 2021, Riley 2021) and GW170817 (Abbott 2018) in terms of quark matter Lagrangian parameters, and enable future Bayesian analyses that inherently include the QCD phase transition.
