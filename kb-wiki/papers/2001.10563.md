# Hyperon-nucleon three-body forces and strangeness in neutron stars

> Three-body forces acting on a $Λ$ hyperon in a nuclear medium are investigated, with special focus on the so-called hyperon puzzle in neutron stars. The hyperon-nucleon two-body interaction deduced from SU(3) chiral effective field theory is employed at next-to-leading order. Hyperon-nucleon three-body forces are approximated using saturation by decuplet baryons and are transcribed to density-dependent effective two-body interactions. These together are taken as input in a Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation with explicit treatment of the $ΛN\leftrightarrowΣN$ and $ΛNN\leftrightarrowΣNN$ coupled channels. Single-particle potentials of a $Λ$ hyperon in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter are calculated. With parameters of the $ΛNN$ three-body force constrained by hypernuclear phenomenology, extrapolations to high baryon density are performed. By comparison of the $Λ$ and neutron chemical potentials at densities characteristic of the core of neutron stars it is found that the combined repulsive effects of two- and three-body correlations makes the appearance of $Λ$ hyperons in neutron stars energetically unfavourable, thus offering a possible solution to a longstanding question.

## Key claim

The combined repulsive effects of hyperon-nucleon two-body and three-body correlations, with the three-body force parameters constrained by hypernuclear phenomenology, can make the appearance of Λ hyperons in neutron stars energetically unfavourable at high baryon densities, potentially resolving the hyperon puzzle. The authors find that a necessary condition for non-occurrence of Λ hyperons in neutron matter at three times nuclear saturation density is that the Λ single-particle potential exceeds 65 MeV, with a stricter condition of 80 MeV for suppression upon extrapolation to higher densities.

## Method

The authors employed SU(3) chiral effective field theory hyperon-nucleon two-body interactions at next-to-leading order (NLO13 and NLO19 versions) and approximated hyperon-nucleon three-body forces using decuplet baryon saturation, transcribing them into density-dependent effective two-body interactions. These were used as input in a Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation with explicit treatment of the ΛN↔ΣN and ΛNN↔ΣNN coupled channels, solving self-consistently for the single-particle potentials of a Λ hyperon in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter. The two free parameters (H₁, H₂) of the three-body contact terms were constrained by the hypernuclear phenomenology requirement that UΛ(ρ ≈ ρ₀) = -30 MeV.

## Key numbers

- UΛ(ρ ≈ ρ₀) = -30 MeV: the Λ single-particle potential depth at nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), constrained by hypernuclear phenomenology and used to fix three-body force parameters.
- ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³: the equilibrium density of normal nuclear matter.
- UΛ(3ρ₀) > 65 MeV: the minimal condition for non-occurrence of Λ hyperons in neutron matter at three times saturation density, derived from requiring μΛ > μn.
- UΛ(3ρ₀) > 80 MeV: the stricter condition for maximally repulsive three-body forces that likely prevents Λ appearance upon extrapolation to higher densities.
- Δ = 270 MeV: the average decuplet-octet baryon mass difference used in the three-body force expressions.
- C ≈ 0.95: the coupling strength of the octet-decuplet transition vertex, determined from the Δ(1232) decay width Γ(Δ→πN) ≈ 115 MeV and the large-Nc relation C = 3gA/4 with gA = 1.26.
- f⁻² = 4.8 fm²: the inverse squared pseudoscalar meson decay constant determining the interaction strength in the one-meson exchange three-body force.
- Cutoff λ = 500 MeV (default) and λ' = 700 MeV: momentum cutoffs used for the hyperon-nucleon potentials and in the Brueckner calculation, respectively.
- Calculations performed up to ρ ≈ 3.5 ρ₀, beyond which numerical instabilities limit the computations.

## Context

This work extends previous Brueckner calculations of hyperon-nuclear potentials using NLO13 interactions reported by Petschauer (2016), Haidenbauer (2017), and Kohno (2018), with the novel inclusion of ΛNN↔ΣNN coupled channels treated explicitly in the Bethe-Goldstone equation. The study addresses the hyperon puzzle identified by Djapo (2010) and Hell (2014), where naive introduction of Λ hyperons with only two-body interactions softens the equation of state and violates the two-solar-mass neutron star constraint from Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013), and Fonseca (2016). The results support the phenomenological analysis by Bombaci and Samart (2015) indicating that Λ-nuclear interactions must become repulsive below three times ρ₀, and enable future extrapolations to neutron star core densities to determine whether hyperons are excluded entirely from neutron star matter.
