# Partial correlation analysis method in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We argue that statistical data analysis of two-particle longitudinal correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions may be efficiently carried out with the technique of partial covariance. In this method, the spurious event-by-event fluctuations due to imprecise centrality determination are eliminated via projecting out the component of the covariance influenced by the centrality fluctuations. We bring up the relationship of the partial covariance to the conditional covariance. Importantly, in the superposition approach, where hadrons are produced independently from a collection of sources, the framework allows us to impose centrality constraints on the number of sources rather than hadrons, that way unfolding of the trivial fluctuations from statistical hadronization and focusing better on the initial-state physics. We show, using simulated data from hydrodynamics followed with statistical hadronization, that the technique is practical and very simple to use, giving insight into the correlations generated in the initial stage. We also discuss the issues related to separation of the short- and long-range components of the correlation functions, and show that in our example the short-range component from the resonance decays is largely reduced by considering pions of the same sign. We demonstrate the method explicitly on the cases where centrality is determined with a single central control bin, or with two peripheral control bins.

## Key claim

The paper argues that partial covariance analysis can effectively remove spurious centrality-fluctuation effects from two-particle longitudinal correlation measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but only if the centrality constraint is imposed at the level of initial sources rather than at the level of detected hadrons, a distinction the abstract does not emphasize.

## Method

The authors apply partial covariance formulas to simulated Pb+Pb collision events at 2.76 TeV generated with event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics followed by statistical hadronization via THERMINATOR. They compute the two-particle correlation function for hadron multiplicities in forward and backward pseudorapidity bins, using a central pseudorapidity bin (or two peripheral bins) as the control variable, and remove autocorrelations according to the superposition model to impose constraints at the source level rather than the hadron level.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic push in the longitudinal direction is estimated as η ≃ k η∥ with k = 1.2 for the applied model.
- The freeze-out temperature is taken as Tf = 150 MeV.
- The simulated data cover |η1,2| ≤ 5.1 divided into 51 bins of width 0.2, with the central reference bin covering |ηC| ≤ 0.5 (five physical bins).
- The beam rapidity is yb ≃ 8 for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.
- In the sample, autocorrelations increase the variance by approximately 100%, making the distinction between source-level and hadron-level constraints very important.

## Context

This work extends the conditional correlation method used by the STAR Collaboration (Abelev, 2009) for forward-backward multiplicity correlations in Au+Au and p+p collisions at 200 GeV. The partial covariance technique is related to the strongly-intensive measures approach of Gazdzicki (1992) and Gorenstein (2011), and to Principal Components Analysis as used by Bhalerao (2014). The method enables experimental access to initial-state correlations by unfolding trivial statistical hadronization fluctuations, and is demonstrated using the Bzdak-Teaney (2012) wounded quark model as the initial-state benchmark.
