# Nucleon form factors and moments of generalized parton distributions using $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions

> We present results on the axial and the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon, as well as, on the first moments of the nucleon generalized parton distributions using maximally twisted mass fermions. We analyze two N_f=2+1+1 ensembles having pion masses of 210 MeV and 354 MeV at two values of the lattice spacing. The lattice scale is determined using the nucleon mass computed on a total of 18 N_f=2+1+1 ensembles generated at three values of the lattice spacing, $a$. The renormalization constants are evaluated non-perturbatively with a perturbative subtraction of ${\cal O}(a^2)$-terms. The moments of the generalized parton distributions are given in the $\bar{\rm MS}$ scheme at a scale of $ μ=2$ GeV. We compare with recent results obtained using different discretization schemes. The implications on the spin content of the nucleon are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD results for nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors, as well as the first moments of generalized parton distributions, using \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermions at two lattice spacings and pion masses of 213 MeV and 373 MeV, with the caveat that disconnected contributions are assumed to be small and are neglected in the isoscalar combinations needed for the spin decomposition.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions using maximally twisted mass fermions with the Iwasaki improved gauge action, employing Gaussian smeared quark fields and sequential inversions through the sink. The generalized form factors are extracted from plateau values of optimized ratios of correlation functions, and the renormalization constants are determined non-perturbatively in the RI\(^\prime\)-MOM scheme with perturbative subtraction of \(\mathcal{O}(a^2)\) terms, then converted to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV. The lattice spacing is determined from the nucleon mass using 17 ensembles at three \(\beta\) values, with chiral extrapolation performed using \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings determined from nucleon mass: \(a_{\beta=1.90} = 0.0934(13)(35)\) fm, \(a_{\beta=1.95} = 0.0820(10)(36)\) fm, \(a_{\beta=2.10} = 0.0644(7)(25)\) fm
- Pion masses of the two ensembles analyzed: \(a m_\pi = 0.15518(21)(33)\) at \(\beta=1.95\) (corresponding to 213 MeV) and \(a m_\pi = 0.06975(20)\) at \(\beta=2.10\) (corresponding to 373 MeV)
- Renormalization constants at \(\beta=1.95\): \(Z_V = 0.625(2)\), \(Z_A = 0.757(3)\), \(Z_{DV}^{\mu\mu} = 1.019(4)\), \(Z_{DV}^{\mu\neq\nu} = 1.053(11)\), \(Z_{DA}^{\mu\mu} = 1.086(3)\)
- Renormalization constants at \(\beta=2.10\): \(Z_V = 0.664(1)\), \(Z_A = 0.771(2)\), \(Z_{DV}^{\mu\mu} = 1.048(5)\), \(Z_{DV}^{\mu\neq\nu} = 1.105(4)\), \(Z_{DA}^{\mu\mu} = 1.112(5)\)
- Sink-source separation: 12 lattice units at \(\beta=1.95\) and 18 lattice units at \(\beta=2.10\), corresponding to approximately 1 fm

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure by the same collaboration (Alexandrou et al., 2011) and others (Syritsyn et al., 2009; Yamazaki et al., 2009; Bali et al., 2012; Green et al., 2012) by using \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermions that include dynamical strange and charm quarks, enabling comparison with results obtained using different discretization schemes. The results enable determination of the quark contribution to the nucleon spin via Ji's sum rule and provide benchmark lattice calculations for the axial charge, electromagnetic form factors, and moments of generalized parton distributions that can be compared with experimental measurements and used to constrain parton distribution functions.
