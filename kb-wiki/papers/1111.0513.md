# Scattering lengths of strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions

> We reconsider a method based on dispersion theory, that allows one to extract the scattering length of any two-baryon system from corresponding final-state interactions in production reactions. The application of the method to baryon-baryon systems with strangeness S=-2 and S=-3 systems is discussed. Theoretical uncertainties due to the presence of inelastic channels with near-by thresholds are examined for the specific situation of the reaction K^- d -> K^0 Lambda Lambda and the coupling of Lambda Lambda to the Xi N channel. The possibility to disentangle spin-triplet and spin-singlet scattering lengths by means of various polarization measurements is demonstrated for several production reactions in K^- d and gamma d scattering. Employing the method to available data on the Lambda Lambda invariant mass from the reaction ^{12}C(K^-,K^+ Lambda Lambda X), a 1S0 scattering length of a(Lambda Lambda)=-1.2\pm 0.6 fm is deduced.

# Scattering lengths of strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a dispersion-theory method for extracting scattering lengths from final-state interactions can be applied to the $\Lambda\Lambda$ system, yielding a $^1S_0$ scattering length of $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -1.2 \pm 0.6$ fm from existing data, but this result carries a theoretical uncertainty of 0.3–0.4 fm due to the nearby $\Xi N$ inelastic threshold at about 25 MeV, and the method assumes no bound state exists in the system.

## Method

The authors apply a dispersion-relation formalism that exploits scale separation between a short-ranged production operator and long-ranged final-state interactions, requiring large momentum transfer in the production reaction. For the $\Lambda\Lambda$ system, they use an inverted dispersion integral (Eq. 4 in the paper) that relates the differential cross section as a function of the $\Lambda\Lambda$ invariant mass to the scattering length, with a cutoff $\epsilon_{max} = 60$ MeV chosen to minimize higher-energy contributions. They re-estimate theoretical uncertainties from inelastic channels (specifically the $\Xi N$ channel coupling) using three variants of a chiral effective field theory ($\chi$EFT) hyperon-hyperon interaction, and then apply the method to the $\Lambda\Lambda$ invariant mass spectrum from the $^{12}C(K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda X)$ reaction measured by the KEK-PS E522 Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -1.2 \pm 0.6$ fm: the $^1S_0$ scattering length extracted from the $^{12}C(K^-,K^+\Lambda\Lambda X)$ data of Yoon et al. (2007).
- $\epsilon_{max} = 60$ MeV: the chosen upper limit of integration for the dispersion integral, corresponding to the maximum kinetic energy in the $\Lambda\Lambda$ system.
- 25.8 MeV: the location of the $\Xi N$ inelastic threshold above the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold (using isospin-averaged masses), compared to 76.96 MeV for the $\Sigma N$ threshold in the $\Lambda p$ case.
- Theoretical error estimates for three $\chi$EFT interaction variants: $\delta a^{in}_1 + \delta a^{m_{max}} = -0.19$ fm (for $a=-1.36$ fm), $-0.11$ fm (for $a=-1.50$ fm), and $-0.22$ fm (for $a=-1.70$ fm); with $|\delta a^{in}_2| < 0.28$, $0.30$, and $0.14$ fm respectively, times ${\rm Max}|A_2/A|$.
- ${\rm Max}|f_{12}p_2| < 0.3$: the estimated upper bound for the ratio of production amplitudes from unitarity considerations.
- Full theoretical error estimate: $\delta a^{th} < 0.3$–$0.4$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theory method previously developed by Gasparyan, Haidenbauer, and Hanhart (2003, 2005, 2007) for hyperon-nucleon systems to the strangeness $S=-2$ baryon-baryon sector, specifically the $\Lambda\Lambda$ system. The study is motivated by the Nagara event (Takahashi et al., 2001) which indicated a moderately attractive $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction, contradicting earlier expectations of a deeply bound $H$-dibaryon predicted by Jaffe (1977). The method provides a more reliable alternative to the Migdal-Watson approach used in earlier analyses of the same data by Yoon et al. (2008, 2010), which gave $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.10^{+0.37}_{-1.56} \pm 0.28$ fm but suffers from systematic deviations of up to 0.7 fm for small scattering lengths. The results enable future dedicated experiments at J-PARC (e.g., $K^- d \to K \Lambda\Lambda$) and JLab (e.g., $\gamma d \to K^+K^0\Lambda\Lambda$) to determine $\Lambda\Lambda$ scattering lengths with higher precision, and complement lattice QCD studies by the NPLQCD and HAL QCD collaborations that suggest the $H$-dibaryon may be bound by 0–7 MeV or unbound at the physical pion mass.
