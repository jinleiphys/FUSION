# Radiative pion photoproduction in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of radiative pion photoproduction in the framework of covariant chiral perturbation theory with explicit $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom. The analysis is performed employing the small scale expansion scheme adjusted for the $Δ$ region. Depending on the channel, we include contributions up to next-to-next-to-leading order. We fit the available experimental data for the reaction $ γp\toγpπ^0 $ and extract the value of the $Δ^+$ magnetic moment. Errors from the truncation of the small scale expansion are estimated using the Bayesian approach. We compare our results both with the previous studies within the $δ$-expansion scheme and with the $Δ$-less theory. We also give predictions for radiative charged-pion photoproduction.

## Key claim

The authors extract the Delta+ magnetic moment from radiative neutral-pion photoproduction data using covariant chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, employing the small-scale expansion scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass difference is treated as order O(M_pi), and find that the extracted value depends on the theoretical framework used, with previous delta-expansion results lying outside the range of theoretical predictions.

## Method

The calculation was performed in covariant chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, using the small-scale expansion (SSE) scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass difference is treated as order O(M_pi). The extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme was adopted for meson-baryon loops. Contributions were included up to next-to-next-to-leading order, with the Delta magnetic moment as the only free parameter, while other low-energy constants were taken from analyses of other reactions. Theoretical uncertainties from truncation of the small-scale expansion were estimated using a Bayesian approach.

## Key numbers

- The Particle Data Group value of the Delta+ magnetic moment is (2.7 +1.0 -1.3 (stat.) +/- 1.5 (syst.) +/- 3 (theor.)) mu_N, where mu_N = e/(2 m_N) is the nucleon magneton
- Theoretical predictions for the Delta+ magnetic moment span the range 1.7-3.5 mu_N from various approaches (quark models, Dyson-Schwinger equations, QCD sum rules, etc.)
- Lattice QCD calculations report Delta+ magnetic moment values in the range 1.0-2.4 mu_N
- A previous analysis using the delta-expansion scheme with newer high-statistics MAMI data yielded Delta+ magnetic moment = (3.77 +0.14 -0.15 (stat.) +/- 0.65 (syst.)) mu_N
- The lower limit for integration over outgoing photon energy was set to 30 MeV, matching the experimental methodology of Schumann (2010) and Kotulla (2002)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of radiative pion photoproduction in the Delta-resonance region, specifically the covariant chiral perturbation theory calculations within the delta-expansion scheme by Pascalutsa (2004, 2007) and the phenomenological model analyses of the TAPS/A2 experiment at MAMI by Kotulla (2002) and Schumann (2010). The authors compare their results with both the delta-expansion scheme and Delta-less chiral perturbation theory, and provide predictions for radiative charged-pion photoproduction, which gives access to the Delta0 magnetic moment. The use of the small-scale expansion scheme with Bayesian uncertainty estimation enables a more reliable extraction of the Delta+ magnetic moment compared to previous phenomenological approaches that could not provide robust theoretical uncertainty estimates.
