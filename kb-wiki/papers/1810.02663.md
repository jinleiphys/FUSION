# Advantages of the multinucleon transfer reactions based on 238U target for producing neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126

> The mechanism of multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126 is investigated within two different theoretical frameworks: dinuclear system (DNS) model and isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The effects of mass asymmetry relaxation, N=Z equilibration, and shell closures on production cross sections of neutron-rich heavy nuclei are investigated. For the first time, the advantages for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126 is found in MNT reactions based on 238U target. We propose the reactions with 238U target for producing unknown neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126 in the future.

# Advantages of the multinucleon transfer reactions based on 238U target for producing neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126

## Key claim

Multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions using a 238U target show advantages for producing unknown neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126, based on three conjectures: mass asymmetry relaxation promotes nucleon transfer from 238U to lighter partners, the N = 126 neutron closed shell attracts neutron flow from 238U, and the large N/Z ratio of 238U enhances the neutron-richness of projectile-like products. The claim is qualified by the authors' statement that these advantages are found "for the first time" and that the reactions are "proposed" for future production, indicating the results are theoretical predictions rather than experimental confirmations.

## Method

The authors employed two theoretical frameworks: the dinuclear system (DNS) model combined with the GEMINI statistical code for de-excitation, and the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. They calculated production cross sections for eight reactions at incident energies 1.15 times the Coulomb barrier: 176Yb + 238U, 186W + 238U, 192Os + 238U, 198Pt + 238U, and four corresponding reactions with lighter targets (176Yb + 170Er, 186W + 160Gd, 192Os + 154Sm, 198Pt + 136Xe). The DNS model included deformation degrees of freedom and temperature-dependent shell corrections, solving a master equation for the distribution probability of fragment proton number, neutron number, and quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies for the eight reactions: 176Yb + 238U at E_c.m. = 621 MeV; 186W + 238U at 660 MeV; 192Os + 238U at 684 MeV; 198Pt + 238U at 711 MeV; 176Yb + 170Er at 470 MeV; 186W + 160Gd at 475 MeV; 192Os + 154Sm at 478 MeV; 198Pt + 136Xe at 458 MeV
- For producing 202Os, 201Re, 200W, and 199Ta (all with N = 126), production cross sections in reactions with 238U target are almost 2 to 4 orders of magnitude larger than in reactions with lighter targets
- The average N/Z ratio of projectile-like fragments in 186W + 238U increases quickly to about 1.54, then gently to 1.55, close to the compound system N/Z ratio; 238U has N/Z = 1.587
- The characteristic relaxation time of radial energy tau_R = 2 x 10^-22 s; the angular momentum relaxation time tau_J = 12 x 10^-22 s
- In the IQMD model, relaxation processes initiate at contact configurations: 90 fm/c for 186W + 238U and 100 fm/c for 186W + 160Gd after simulation start

## Context

This work extends theoretical investigations of MNT reactions for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126, following prior studies such as Corradi (2001), Zagrebaev (2005), and Watanabe (2001) who proposed reactions like 136Xe + 208Pb and 136Xe + 198Pt. The authors build on earlier findings by Zhu (2004) and Adamian (2002) that projectiles with large neutron excess show advantages for production cross sections, and on experimental work by Mayer (2001) using 238U projectiles with lighter targets. The study enables future experimental searches for unknown neutron-rich isotopes around N = 126 using direct-kinematics MNT reactions with 238U targets, which the authors argue are easier to perform than inverse kinematics approaches.
