# Study of ground state properties of carbon isotopes with deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> Ground state properties of carbon isotopes, including root-mean-square radii, neutron separation energies, single particle spectra, and shapes are systematically studied with the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum. The calculations with the effective interaction PK1 reproduce the available data reasonably well. The shell evolution in this isotopic chain is investigated by examining the single particle spectra. The inversion of neutron orbitals $ν2s_{1/2}$ and $ν1d_{5/2}$ compared with the order of neutron orbitals in stable nuclei is revealed in $^{15-20,22}\mathrm{C}$ when these nuclei are constrained to spherical shape. Neutron halos in $^{15,19,22}$C are studied in detail and their halo structures are mainly caused by the inversion of ($ν2s_{1/2},ν1d_{5/2}$) and deformation effects. In particular, $^{15}$C and $^{22}$C are deformed halo nuclei with shape decoupling effects in ground states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inversion of neutron orbitals ν2s₁/₂ and ν1d₅/₂ (compared with the order in stable nuclei) combined with deformation effects produces neutron halos in ¹⁵,¹⁹,²²C, but the halo in ²²C is "shrunk" relative to early experimental claims, and ¹⁵C and ²²C are deformed halo nuclei with shape decoupling between core and halo in their ground states.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of ground state properties for carbon isotopes ¹²–²²C using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc theory), solving the RHB equation in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with axial deformation and spatial reflection symmetry. The effective interaction PK1 was used in the particle-hole channel with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force, and for odd-A nuclei the blocking effect was included to obtain ground states. Constraint calculations with the augmented Lagrangian method were performed to examine single-particle level evolution with quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of ¹⁵C: 1.1895 ± 0.0008 MeV (experimental, AME2016)
- One-neutron separation energy of ¹⁹C: 0.5633 ± 0.0915 MeV (experimental, AME2016)
- Two-neutron separation energy of ²²C: 35 ± 20 keV (AME2016); calculated as 0.40 MeV with PK1 and 1.93 MeV with NL3
- Matter radius of ²²C: 3.44 ± 0.08 fm (Togano 2016), 3.38 ± 0.10 fm (Nagahisa 2018); calculated as 3.25 fm with PK1 and 3.22 fm with NL3
- Matter radius of ¹⁵C: experimental 2.54 ± 0.04 fm (Kanungo 2016); calculated as 2.64 fm (PK1) and 2.65 fm (NL3)
- Matter radius of ¹⁹C: experimental 3.16 ± 0.07 fm (Kanungo 2016); calculated as 3.02 fm with both PK1 and NL3
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ for ¹²C: experimental 0.583 ± 0.015; calculated as −0.3223 (PK1, oblate) and 0.0000 (NL3, spherical)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ for ²²C: calculated as −0.2649 (PK1) and −0.2311 (NL3)
- ²¹C is unbound with PK1 (Sₙ = −28 keV) but bound with NL3 (Sₙ = 0.22 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations (Poeschl 1997) that found no halos in carbon isotopes, and spherical relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Lu 2013) that found halos in ¹⁷,¹⁹C but not in ²²C. It builds on the authors' prior study of ²²C (Sun 2018) using the same DRHBc theory, now providing a systematic survey across the full carbon isotopic chain. The results enable further investigation of shape decoupling in deformed halo nuclei and the interplay between shell evolution, deformation, and halo formation in neutron-rich light nuclei, particularly regarding the N=16 shell closure question raised by experiments (Ozawa 2000, Otsuka 2001) and shell model studies (Coraggio 2010).
