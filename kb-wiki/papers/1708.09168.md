# Basic parameters of the helium accreting X-ray bursting neutron star in 4U 1820-30

> The ultracompact low-mass X-ray binary 4U 1820-30 situated in the globular cluster NGC 6624 has an orbital period of only $\approx$11.4 min which likely implies a white dwarf companion. The observed X-ray bursts demonstrate a photospheric radius expansion phase and therefore are believed to reach the Eddington luminosity allowing us to estimate the mass and the radius of the neutron star (NS) in this binary. Here we re-analyse all Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer observations of the system and confirm that almost all the bursts took place during the hard persistent state of the system. This allows us to use the recently developed direct cooling tail method to estimate the NS mass and radius. However, because of the very short, about a second, duration of the cooling tail phases that can be described by the theoretical atmosphere models, the obtained constraints on the NS radius are not very strict. Assuming a pure helium NS atmosphere we found that the NS radius is in the range 10-12 km, if the NS mass is below 1.7 $M_\odot$, and in a wider range of 8-12 km for a higher 1.7-2.0 $M_\odot$ NS mass. The method also constrains the distance to the system to be 6.5$\pm$0.5 kpc, which is consistent with the distance to the cluster. For the solar composition atmosphere, the NS parameters are in strong contradiction with the generally accepted range of possible NS masses and radii.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the direct cooling tail method can be applied to the neutron star in 4U 1820-30, but the obtained constraints on the neutron star radius are not very strict because the cooling tail phases that can be described by theoretical atmosphere models last only about one second.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed all Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer (RXTE) observations of 4U 1820-30, identifying 16 short photospheric radius expansion (PRE) X-ray bursts that occurred predominantly during the hard persistent state. They applied the direct cooling tail method, which compares the observed blackbody normalization K versus flux F_BB relation from burst spectra to theoretical model atmosphere curves w - w f_c^4 L/L_Edd computed for different surface gravities and chemical compositions, using chi-squared minimization to find the best-fitting neutron star mass and radius.

## Key numbers

- Orbital period of the binary system: approximately 11.4 minutes
- Neutron star radius range assuming pure helium atmosphere: 10-12 km for neutron star mass below 1.7 solar masses; 8-12 km for neutron star mass in the range 1.7-2.0 solar masses
- Distance to the system constrained by the method: 6.5 +/- 0.5 kpc
- Burst fluences for the eight selected bursts: range from (3.25 +/- 0.17) x 10^-7 erg cm^-2 to (4.19 +/- 0.36) x 10^-7 erg cm^-2
- Ratio of maximum blackbody normalization to touchdown normalization (K_max/K_td) for selected bursts: range from 1.38 +/- 0.14 to 1.73 +/- 0.18
- Persistent flux before bursts in the hard state: approximately 0.07 L_Edd
- Recurrence times for two burst pairs: 4.16 hours and 1.89 hours, corresponding to persistent fluxes of 3.5 x 10^-9 erg s^-1 cm^-2 and 5.8 x 10^-9 erg s^-1 cm^-2 respectively
- Alpha values (ratio of energy release between bursts to burst fluence): 125 +/- 10 and 110 +/- 10

## Context

This work extends the direct cooling tail method developed by Suleimanov et al. (2011) and modified by Suleimanov et al. (2017) to the specific case of 4U 1820-30, which has a helium-rich white dwarf companion as established by Rappoport et al. (1987). The results are consistent with previous work by Guver et al. (2010) and GZM13 showing that bursts occur in the hard spectral state, and the pure helium atmosphere solution agrees with the chemical composition inferred from thermonuclear flash models by Cumming (2003). The study enables validation of the cooling tail method for systems with known companion composition and provides constraints on the neutron star equation of state, though the short cooling tail duration limits the precision of radius measurements.
