# The Sensitivity of Core-Collapse Supernovae to Nuclear Electron Capture

> A weak-rate library aimed at investigating the sensitivity of astrophysical environments to variations of electron-capture rates on medium-heavy nuclei has been developed. With this library, the sensitivity of the core-collapse and early post-bounce phases of core-collapse supernovae to nuclear electron-capture is examined by systematically and statistically varying electron-capture rates of individual nuclei. The rates are adjusted by factors consistent with uncertainties indicated by comparing theoretical rates to those deduced from charge-exchange and $β$-decay measurements. To ensure a model independent assessment, sensitivity studies across a comprehensive set of progenitors and equations of state are performed. In our systematic study, we find a +16/-4 % range in the mass of the inner-core at the time of shock formation and a $\pm$20% range of peak νe-luminosity during the deleptonization burst. These ranges are each five times as large as those seen from a separate progenitor study in which we evaluated the sensitivity of these parameters to 32 presupernova stellar models. It is also found that the simulations are more sensitive to a reduction in the electron-capture rates than an enhancement, and in particular to the reduction in the rates for neutron-rich nuclei near the N = 50 closed neutron-shell. As measurements for medium-heavy (A > 65) and neutron-rich nuclei are sparse, and because accurate theoretical models which account for nuclear structure considerations on the individual nucleus level are not readily available, rates for these nuclei may be overestimated. If more accurate estimates confirm this, results from this study indicate that significant changes to the core-collapse trajectory can be expected. For this reason, experimental and theoretical efforts should focus in this region of the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The core-collapse supernova simulations show a +16/-4% range in inner-core mass at shock formation and a ±20% range in peak electron-neutrino luminosity during the deleptonization burst when electron-capture rates are varied within uncertainties indicated by comparisons between theoretical rates and charge-exchange measurements; these variations are five times larger than those seen from varying presupernova stellar models, and the simulations are more sensitive to reductions in electron-capture rates than enhancements, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei near the N=50 closed neutron shell.

## Method

The authors developed a modular weak-rate library incorporating 445 electron-capture rates for 304 unique nuclei from multiple tabulations (FFN, Oda, LMP, LMSH) and an approximate parameterized rate for nuclei not in the tables, then implemented this library into the spherically-symmetric general-relativistic neutrino-transport and hydrodynamics code GR1D. They performed approximately 150 collapse simulations systematically and statistically varying electron-capture rates of individual nuclei by factors consistent with uncertainties from comparisons of theoretical rates to charge-exchange and beta-decay measurements, across four progenitors (s12, s15WW95, s20, s40) and three equations of state (SFHo, DD2, TMA).

## Key numbers

- Inner-core mass at shock formation varies by +16/-4% across the systematic rate variations
- Peak electron-neutrino luminosity during the deleptonization burst varies by ±20%
- These ranges are each five times as large as those from a separate progenitor study evaluating sensitivity to 32 presupernova stellar models
- QRPA calculations systematically overestimate electron-capture rates by approximately 100-3000% compared to charge-exchange measurements, depending on density and temperature
- Shell-model estimates produce rates similar to measured values, with deviations of approximately 1-50%
- The range of initial central electron fraction across progenitors goes from approximately 0.422 for s15WW95 to 0.447 for s40WH07, a range of about 6%
- Trapped lepton fraction ranges from approximately 0.288 to 0.297 across progenitors
- Charge-exchange measurements at intermediate energies (greater than approximately 100 MeV/u) are accurate at the approximately 10% level

## Context

This work extends the pioneering weak-rate tabulations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1982) and the shell-model based rate implementations of Hix (2003) by systematically quantifying sensitivity rather than comparing single rate sets. The authors build on comparisons by Cole (2012), Scott (2014), and Noji (2014) showing that QRPA calculations overestimate rates while shell-model calculations agree with charge-exchange measurements, but note that shell-model calculations are computationally challenging for nuclei beyond the pf-shell. The results motivate experimental and theoretical focus on medium-heavy (A > 65) and neutron-rich nuclei near N=50, where rates may be overestimated and measurements are sparse, enabling targeted measurements and improved theoretical models for these species.
