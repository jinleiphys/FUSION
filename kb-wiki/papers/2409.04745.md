# Leptodermic corrections to the TOV equations and nuclear astrophysics within the effective surface approximation

> The macroscopic model for a neutron star (NS) as a liquid drop at the equilibrium is used to extend the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations taking into account the gradient terms responsible for the system surface. The parameters of the Schwarzschild metric in the spherical case are found with these surface corrections to the known leading (zero) order of the leptodermic approximation $a/R<<1$, where $a$ is the NS effective-surface (ES) thickness, and $R$ is the effective NS radius. The energy density $\mathcal{E}$ is considered in a general form including the functions of the particle number density and of its gradient terms. The macroscopic gravitational component $Φ(ρ)$ of the energy density is taken into account in the simplest form as expansion in powers of $ρ-\overlineρ $, where $\overlineρ$ is the saturation density, up to second order, in terms of its contributions to the separation particle energy and incompressibility. Density distributions $ρ$ across the NS ES in the normal direction to the ES, which are derived in the simple analytical form at the same leading approximation, was used for the derivation of the modified TOV (MTOV) equations by accounting for their NS surface corrections. The MTOV equations are analytically solved at first order and the results are compared with the standard TOV approach of the zero order.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations by including gradient (surface) terms of the energy density within the leptodermic approximation where the effective-surface thickness is much smaller than the neutron star radius ($a/R \ll 1$), and analytically solves the resulting modified TOV (MTOV) equations at first order in this small parameter.

## Method

The authors start from the Einstein-Gilbert equations of general relativity for a spherically symmetric system and the Schwarzschild metric, then incorporate an energy density functional that includes both a volume term $\mathcal{A}(\rho)$ and a gradient term $\mathcal{C}(\nabla\rho)^2$. The macroscopic gravitational field $\Phi(\rho)$ is expanded to second order in $\rho-\overline{\rho}$ around the saturation density $\overline{\rho}$. Using the effective-surface approach (ESA) with the leptodermic expansion parameter $a/R \ll 1$, they derive density distributions across the neutron star surface and obtain modified TOV equations that account for surface corrections. The MTOV equations are solved analytically at first order and compared with the standard zero-order TOV solutions.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron star radius $R = 10$ km and crust diffuseness $a = 1$ km used as example parameters for density distributions (Fig. 1)
- Schwarzschild radius parameter $R_{\rm S} \approx 15.6$ km for a neutron star mass $M = 1.4 M_\odot$ with effective radius $R = 10$ km
- Gravitational radius $r_{\rm g} = 2GM/c^2 \approx 4.1$ km for the same parameters ($R = 10$ km, $M = 1.4 M_\odot$)
- Physical mass range $M/M_\odot \approx 1.4$ to $2.0$ considered for comparison with experimental neutron star data
- Nuclear matter incompressibility modulus $K \sim 200$ MeV cited as example for the non-gravitational part

## Context

This work extends the effective-surface approach (ESA) previously developed for nuclear physics by Magner et al. (2009), Bondorf and Mishustin (2013, 2015), and applied to neutron stars by Magner and Maydanyuk (2024). It builds on the classic TOV derivations by Tolman (1939) and Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939), and the liquid-drop model for neutron stars by Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (1971). The modified TOV equations enable analytical treatment of surface capillary pressure effects in neutron stars, analogous to van der Waals surface effects in molecular systems, and allow comparison with experimental neutron star mass-radius data from recent observations.
