# Variational Methods for Nuclear Systems with Dynamical Mesons

> We derive a model Hamiltonian whose ground state expectation value of any two-body operator coincides with that obtained with the Jastrow correlated wave function of the many-body Fermi system. Using this Hamiltonian we show that the variational principle can be extended to treat systems with dynamical mesons, even if in this case the concept of wave function looses its meaning.

## Key claim

The authors show that the variational principle for many-body Fermi systems can be extended to systems with dynamical mesons by translating the variational problem from a trial wave function to a trial Hamiltonian, even though the wave function concept loses meaning in such systems; the equivalence holds provided the correct link between the correlation function and the model Hamiltonian's interaction term is established.

## Method

The authors derive a model Hamiltonian \(H_0^{(J)}\) whose ground state expectation value of any two-body operator coincides with that obtained from a Jastrow correlated wave function, using the Feynman path integral formalism and the inequality \( \langle e^{-A} \rangle \geq e^{-\langle A \rangle} \). They then expand the variational energy diagrammatically in Goldstone diagrams and demonstrate topological equivalence to the Fermi Hyper Netted Chain (FHNC) diagrams, establishing the relation \( h = e^{-\lambda} - 1 \) between the FHNC correlation function \( h = f^2 - 1 \) and the model Hamiltonian's "potential" \(\lambda\). Finally, they integrate out the pion field from a Lagrangian with nucleon-pion coupling to obtain an effective nucleon-only action with a fully dynamical pion propagator, to which the variational method is applied.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed Green's function is given by \( \mathcal{G}_0(k,k_0) = \frac{\theta(k-k_F)}{k_0 + i\eta} + \frac{\theta(k_F - k)}{k_0 - \Delta - i\eta} \), where \(\Delta\) is a parameter that cancels out in the variational energy at each order.
- The pion propagator in the effective action is \( D_0(x-y) = \int \frac{d^4q}{(2\pi)^4} e^{iq\cdot(x-y)} \frac{1}{q_0^2 - \mathbf{q}^2 - m_\pi^2} \), with \(m_\pi\) the pion mass.
- The Pauli correlation line of FHNC diagrams is \( l(k_F x) = \frac{\nu}{\rho} \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} \theta(k_F - p) e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}} \), where \(\nu\) is the spin-isospin multiplicity and \(\rho\) the density.

## Context

This work extends the FHNC approach (Fantoni and Rosati, 1975; Fantoni and Pandharipande, 1983) and Correlated Basis Function perturbation theory (Clark and Westhaus, 1968; Fantoni and Pandharipande, 1984), which were previously constrained to non-relativistic potential theories where wave functions exist. The method enables variational treatment of dynamical meson exchange in nuclear matter, which is not reducible to a potential theory, and could also be applied to solid state systems such as the Ising and Hubbard models. The approach allows for energy-dependent correlation functions and opens the possibility of including state-dependent correlations and time-dependent correlations in future work.
