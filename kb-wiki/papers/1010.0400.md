# Evidence for Hydrodynamic Evolution in Proton-Proton Scattering at LHC Energies

> In $pp$ scattering at LHC energies, large numbers of elementary scatterings will contribute significantly, and the corresponding high multiplicity events will be of particular interest. Elementary scatterings are parton ladders, identified with color flux-tubes. In high multiplicity events, many of these flux tubes are produced in the same space region, creating high energy densities. We argue that there are good reasons to employ the successful procedure used for heavy ion collisions: matter is assumed to thermalizes quickly, such that the energy from the flux-tubes can be taken as initial condition for a hydrodynamic expansion. This scenario gets spectacular support from very recent results on Bose-Einstein correlations in $pp$ scattering at 900 GeV at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies reach energy densities comparable to those in gold-gold collisions at RHIC, justifying the application of a hydrodynamic expansion model (previously used for heavy ions) to these small systems. The claim is supported by Bose-Einstein correlation data from ALICE at 900 GeV, which the hydrodynamic scenario reproduces while a baseline model without hydro and without cascade does not.

## Method

The authors employ the EPOS 2.05 model, where elementary scatterings are treated as parton ladders (color flux-tubes). In high-multiplicity events, many flux-tubes overlap in space, creating high energy densities that serve as initial conditions for ideal hydrodynamic evolution. The system expands hydrodynamically until a hadronization temperature of 166 MeV, after which the hadronic cascade model UrQMD handles final-state interactions. The authors compare three scenarios: full calculation (hydro + cascade), no cascade (hydro only), and base (no hydro, no cascade), and compute two-pion correlation functions to compare with ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density of about 50 GeV/fm\(^3\) for a high multiplicity \(pp\) collision at 900 GeV with \(dn/d\eta = 12.9\), comparable to central gold-gold collisions at 200 GeV
- Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c for the hydrodynamic start
- Hadronization temperature of 166 MeV used to stop the hydrodynamic evolution
- Transverse flow velocity reaches typically around 70% of the speed of light
- For the base scenario (no hydro, no cascade), the extracted exponential radius \(R\) from the correlation function ranges from 0.64 fm (KT1, \(k_T = 100-250\) MeV) to 1.63 fm (KT5, \(k_T = 700-1000\) MeV)
- For the full scenario (with hydro and cascade), the extracted radius is about 1.80 fm (mixed-event normalization) or 2.10 fm (bare normalization) and is nearly \(k_T\)-independent across all three \(k_T\) bins

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously developed for AuAu collisions at RHIC (Werner et al., 2008) to proton-proton scattering at LHC energies, challenging the conventional view that \(pp\) systems are too small for collective behavior. The results contradict the simple string-decay picture (base scenario) and support the interpretation that the \(k_T\)-independence of the extracted radii in \(pp\) is a flow signal, analogous to the \(k_T\)-dependent radii seen in AuAu but with a competing effect from the \(p_t\)-dependent source size in the smaller system. The paper enables further study of collectivity in small systems and motivates similar analyses at higher LHC energies and with heavier identified particles.
