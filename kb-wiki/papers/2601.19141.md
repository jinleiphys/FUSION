# Origin of the nucleon gravitational form factor $B_N(t)$: Exposition in light-front holographic QCD

> Recent lattice QCD simulations and phenomenological models indicate that the nucleon's gravitational form factor $B_N(t)$ remains remarkably small at finite momentum transfer $t$. While $B_N(0) = 0$ is a known consequence of the equivalence principle, the physical origin of its suppression at finite $t$ has not been fully elucidated. In this work, we demonstrate that the smallness of $B_N(t)$ arises from a fundamental cancellation within the nucleon's wave functions. Using light-front holographic QCD, we show that $B_N(t)$ is governed by an antisymmetric factor in the longitudinal dynamics that leads to the exact vanishing of the form factor in the symmetric limit and significant suppression for realistic nucleon structures. Our results suggest that the smallness of $B_N(t)$ is a signature of the nucleon's dominant S-wave character, providing a formal justification for its frequent omission in practical applications like near-threshold $J/ψ$ production.

## Key claim

The smallness of the nucleon gravitational form factor $B_N(t)$ at finite momentum transfer arises from an antisymmetric factor in the longitudinal dynamics of the light-front wave functions, which causes exact vanishing in the symmetric limit and significant suppression for realistic nucleon structures; this suppression is a signature of the nucleon's dominant S-wave character rather than a general property of field theories.

## Method

The authors employ light-front holographic QCD (LFHQCD) to compute the gravitational form factor $B_N(t)$ from overlap integrals of light-front wave functions for a quark-diquark model of the nucleon. They generalize the standard holographic wave functions by incorporating a longitudinal wave function $X(x)$ that depends on the longitudinal momentum fraction, and show that $B_N(t)$ is governed by an antisymmetric factor $X_+(x)X_-(x) - X_+(1-x)X_-(1-x)$ in the holographic current. The analysis compares the suppression for S-wave, P-wave, and D-wave components of the longitudinal wave function using an invariant mass ansatz with quark masses $m_q = 46$ MeV and diquark masses $m_D = 140$ MeV (LFHQCD values) and $m_q = 300$ MeV, $m_D = 600$ MeV (constituent quark model values).

## Key numbers

- The confining strength in LFHQCD is $\kappa = 0.54$ GeV.
- For the LFHQCD longitudinal wave function, the quark mass is $m_q = 46$ MeV and the diquark mass is $m_D = 140$ MeV.
- For the constituent quark model comparison, the quark mass is $m_q = 300$ MeV and the diquark mass is $m_D = 600$ MeV.
- The first Mellin moments of the GPD $E$ at forward limit give $\kappa_u \approx +1.67$ and $\kappa_d \approx -2.03$ from constituent quark model estimates.
- A simple non-relativistic quark model estimate yields $B_N(0) \approx 0.44$ from flavor contributions.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the nucleon gravitational form factor $B_N(t)$ beyond the known consequence of the equivalence principle that $B_N(0)=0$ (Teryaev 1999, Brodsky 2000). It addresses the persistent smallness of $B_N(t)$ at finite $t$ observed in lattice QCD simulations (Deka 2013, Hackett 2023) and dispersion relation analyses (Cao 2024, Broniowski 2025, Cao 2025), which contrasts with the non-vanishing values predicted by some models. The LFHQCD framework bridges the flavor cancellation explanation from generalized parton distributions (Teryaev 1998) and the helicity-conservation mechanism in holographic QCD (Abidin 2009), providing a robust mechanism that enables future studies of excited baryonic states like $N^*$ resonances and interpretation of near-threshold $J/\psi$ production data from electron-ion colliders and Jefferson Lab.
