# Nonequilibrium distribution functions of nucleons in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The collision smearing of the nucleon momenta about their initial values during relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is investigated. To a certain degree, our model belongs to the transport type, and we investigate the evolution of the nucleon system created at a nucleus-nucleus collision. However, we parameterize this development by the number of collisions of every particle during evolution rather than by the time variable. It is assumed that the group of nucleons which leave the system after the same number of collisions can be joined in a particular statistical ensemble. The nucleon nonequilibrium distribution functions, which depend on a certain number of collisions of a nucleon before freeze-out, are derived.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the nonequilibrium distribution of nucleons in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions can be parameterized by the number of collisions each nucleon experiences before freeze-out, rather than by time, and that nucleons with the same number of collisions form a distinct statistical sub-ensemble. The model is explicitly a complement to transport simulations like UrQMD, not a replacement, and its validity is demonstrated by fitting UrQMD "data" rather than direct experimental spectra.

## Method

The authors develop a multiscattering-statistical model in which each nucleon from one nucleus undergoes a sequence of independent random momentum transfers as it passes through the other nucleus. The total momentum transfer after M collisions is treated as a Gaussian random variable via the central limit theorem, yielding a single-particle distribution function that combines a Boltzmann factor with a Gaussian smearing term. The model is applied to a two-stream system (nucleons with positive and negative longitudinal momenta), and the parameters (mean longitudinal momentum transfer, slope parameter, longitudinal and transverse variances) are extracted by fitting the longitudinal momentum distributions from UrQMD simulations for Au+Au collisions at 10.8 and 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- The maximal number of collisions for nucleons at AGS and SPS energies is determined to be M_max = 43.
- The most populated nucleon sub-ensembles correspond to collision numbers in the range M = 14 to 17.
- The model is fitted to UrQMD data for Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 10.8 AGeV and 158 AGeV.
- Experimental transverse nucleon distributions from central Au+Au reactions at p_lab = 11.6 AGeV/c (E-802 collaboration) are described using the model.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Lurcat and Mazur (1964) and Hakim (2011) by incorporating dynamical information from multiple scattering, which is usually lost in purely statistical descriptions. It directly uses UrQMD simulations (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999) as a source of "data" to determine the populations and parameters of each collision-number sub-ensemble. The model enables a transparent, parameterized description of the nonequilibrium evolution of net nucleons, providing a bridge between transport simulations and thermodynamic models for heavy-ion collisions.
