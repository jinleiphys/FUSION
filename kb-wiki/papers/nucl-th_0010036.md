# Observing compact quark matter droplets in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Compactness is introduced as a new method to search for the onset of the quark matter transition in relativistic heavy ion collisions. That transition supposedly leads to stronger compression and higher compactness of the source in coordinate space. That effect could be observed via pion interferometry. We propose to measure the compactness of the source in the appropriate principal axis frame of the compactness tensor in coordinate space.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "compactness" as a new observable for detecting the onset of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition in relativistic heavy ion collisions, based on the expectation that a first-order phase transition leads to stronger compression and a more compact source in coordinate space that can be measured via pion interferometry. The authors hedge that this effect depends on the transition actually occurring at the chosen bombarding energy, impact parameter, and system size, and that the compression factor is affected by incompressibility evaluated on the Rankine-Hugoniot-Taub adiabat, not along a path of fixed specific entropy.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at 8 AGeV and impact parameter b = 3 fm, using two different equations of state: a relativistic mean field (RMF) hadron fluid without phase transition, and the same RMF supplemented by a Bag Model equation of state (bag constant B^{1/4}=235 MeV) with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma constructed via Gibbs' phase equilibrium condition. They defined a configuration space sphericity tensor (compactness tensor) as the second moment of the net baryon current with a density cut to discard spectator matter, then diagonalized it to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, defining compactness as the ratio of in-plane radii sqrt(f2/f1).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy per baryon W0 = 923 MeV and ground state baryon density ρ0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- For ρ = 0, the critical temperature for phase coexistence is TC ≈ 170 MeV
- At T = 0, phase coexistence sets in at ρ ≈ 4.6 ρ0
- The bag constant used is B^{1/4} = 235 MeV
- The simulation energy is Elab^{kin} = 8 AGeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 3 fm
- The compactness tensor is nearly a factor of two flatter in the model with phase transition compared to the model without

## Context

This work extends prior studies of signatures for the quark matter transition in relativistic heavy ion collisions (QM99 and references therein) and builds on the Rankine-Hugoniot-Taub shock adiabat solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics (Baumgardt 1975, Rischke 1995). The proposed compactness observable complements momentum-space flow angles and configuration space tilt angles (Brachmann 2000), and the experimental measurement is enabled by a novel interferometry analysis developed by Lisa et al. (2000). The approach is analogous to the higher compactness of compact stars with quark matter cores compared to pure neutron stars (Glendenning 1997).
