# Realistic Interactions and Dilepton Production off pp-Collisions

## Key claim

The authors present a model for dilepton production in proton-proton collisions using a realistic T-matrix that includes Delta-isobar degrees of freedom and fits NN-scattering data up to 2 GeV; while the overall agreement with earlier calculations using less sophisticated interactions is good, significant differences appear in the nucleonic contribution, which is larger than found in models that only include on-shell NN scattering information.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross-section for dilepton pair production in proton-proton collisions at 1 GeV laboratory energy using a T-matrix based on the Paris potential with additional Delta degrees of freedom, which describes nucleon-nucleon data up to 2 GeV. They include nucleon single-scattering and rescattering diagrams as well as Delta single-scattering and rescatter diagrams, using N-Delta-gamma coupling constants from Jones and Scadron (1973) and from vector dominance estimates. To address the non-gauge invariance of the nucleonic current, the authors express the time-like component M0 in terms of the spatial components Mi following the method of Naus et al. (1990) and Caballero et al. (1993).

## Key numbers

- At low invariant mass M, the nucleonic cross-section from the T-matrix approach is around a factor 2 larger than the result of Schafer et al. (1994)
- Using a pseudo-vector pion coupling instead of pseudo-scalar reduces the Schafer result by a factor 10
- The weak coupling result (G1 = 2.0 GeV^-1, G2 = 0.0 GeV^-2) is about 25 percent smaller than the strong coupling result (G1 = 2.68 GeV^-1, G2 = -1.84 GeV^-2)
- The Jones and Scadron (1973) coupling constants used are G1 = 2.68 GeV^-1 and G2 = -1.84 GeV^-2
- The vector dominance estimate gives G1 = 2.0 GeV^-1 and G2 = 0.0 GeV^-2
- The Schafer et al. (1994) values are G1 = 2.3 GeV^-1 and G2 = 0.0 GeV^-2

## Context

This work extends the study of real-photon bremsstrahlung by de Jong et al. (to be published) to the case of virtual photon production (dilepton pairs), motivated by experiments such as CERES (1995) that show increased dilepton production around the rho meson mass in heavy ion collisions. The authors compare their T-matrix approach with the One Boson Exchange model of Schafer et al. (1994) and the Soft Photon Approximation of Korchin and Scholten (1995) and Zhang et al. (1995), finding that the T-matrix provides a more realistic description of off-shell behavior. The work enables more reliable extraction of in-medium modifications of the rho meson from heavy ion collision data, though the authors note that further investigation of form factors and more refined observables like dilepton anisotropies is needed.
