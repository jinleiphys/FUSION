# Disassembling the Nuclear Matrix Elements of the Neutrinoless double beta Decay

> In this article we analyze the nuclear matrix elements (NME) of the neutrinoless double beta decays of the nuclei 48-Ca, 76-Ge, 82-Se, 124-Sn, 130-Te and 136-Xe in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model (ISM). We study the relative value of the different contributions to them, such as higher order terms in the nuclear current, finite nuclear size effects and short range correlations, as well as their evolution with the maximum seniority permitted in the wave functions. We discuss also the build-up of the NME's as a function of the distance between the decaying neutrons. We calculate the decays to final 0+ first excited states and find that these decays are at least 25 times more supressed with respect to the ground state to ground state transition.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the relative contributions of higher-order current terms (HOC), finite nuclear size (FNS), and short-range correlations (SRC) to neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements are similar between Interacting Shell Model and QRPA calculations, but the absolute NME values differ because QRPA effectively truncates seniority at low values, missing high-seniority components that reduce the NME.

## Method
The authors performed Interacting Shell Model calculations for seven candidate nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹²⁸Te, ¹³⁰Te, ¹³⁶Xe) using full diagonalizations within different valence spaces and effective interactions (KB3, GCN28.50, GCN50.82). They systematically decomposed the NME into Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and tensor contributions, and studied the effects of higher-order nucleon current terms, finite nuclear size via dipole form factors, and short-range correlations using both Jastrow and UCOM prescriptions. The radial buildup of the NME was analyzed via the operator C(r), and decays to first excited 0⁺ states were computed.

## Key numbers
- For ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se, the full ISM NME including all corrections is 2.30 (Jastrow SRC) or 2.81 (UCOM SRC)
- Higher-order current terms reduce the bare NME by 19% before FNS and 14% after FNS for ⁷⁶Ge
- Jastrow SRC reduce the NME by 22% for ⁷⁶Ge; UCOM SRC reduce it by only 5%
- The ratio χ^F = (g_V/g_A)² M^F/M^GT for ⁷⁶Ge is 0.12 in full ISM, compared to 0.23 at seniority s_m=4 and 0.27–0.32 in QRPA
- The tensor ratio χ^T = M^T/M^GT for ⁷⁶Ge is 0.4% in full ISM
- For decays to 0₁⁺ excited states, the half-life suppression factor relative to ground-state decay ranges from 12 (⁷⁶Ge) to 85 (⁴⁸Ca) in the phase-space factor ratio G₀₁(gs→gs)/G₀₁(gs→0₁⁺)
- The NME for ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti ground state is 0.61 (Jastrow SRC); for the excited 0₁⁺ state it is 0.68
- The radial function C(r) peaks near r = 1 fm for all studied transitions

## Context
This work extends previous ISM calculations by Retamosa (1995) and Caurier (1996) that included only the dominant aa and vv current terms, incorporating the higher-order current contributions first noted by Simkovic (1999) in QRPA. The results are compared with QRPA calculations from the Jyväskylä group (Kortelainen 2007) and the Tübingen group (Simkovic 1999, Rodin 2007). The radial dependence analysis confirms the QRPA findings of Simkovic (2007) that the NME is dominated by nucleon pairs at distances around 1 fm. The study of decays to excited 0⁺ states extends QRPA work by Suhonen (2000–2003) and Simkovic (2001) to the ISM framework, finding similar suppression factors. The seniority truncation analysis explains the systematic difference between ISM and QRPA NME values, and the UCOM versus Jastrow SRC comparison quantifies an important source of theoretical uncertainty (5–20% range).
