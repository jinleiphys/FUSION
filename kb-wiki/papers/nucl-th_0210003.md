# Mixed-Mode Calculations in Nuclear Physics

> The one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box problem is used to introduce the concept of a mixed-mode shell-model scheme. The method combines low-lying ``pure mode'' states of a system to achieve a better description in situations where complete calculations cannot be done and the dynamics is driven by a combination of modes. The scheme is tested for real nuclei by combining traditional spherical states, which yield a diagonal representation of the single-particle interaction, with collective SU(3) configurations that track deformation. An application to the ds-shell $^{24}$Mg nucleus, using the realistic two-body interaction of Wildenthal, is explored to test the validity of the concept. The results shown that the mixed-mode scheme reproduces the correct binding energy of $^{24}$Mg (within 2% of the exact result) as well as low-energy configurations that have greater than 90% overlap with exact solutions in a space that spans less than 10% of the full space. In the pf-shell, the Kuo-Brown-3 interaction is used to illustrate coherent structures of the low-lying states of $^{48}$Cr. Alternative basis sets are suggested for future mixed-mode shell-model studies.

# Mixed-Mode Calculations in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
The mixed-mode (oblique-basis) shell-model scheme, which combines low-lying pure-mode states from two different basis sets, can reproduce the binding energy of a nucleus within 2% of the exact result and achieve greater than 90% wavefunction overlap with exact solutions while using less than 10% of the full model space, but only when the dynamics is driven by a combination of competing single-particle and collective modes.

## Method
The authors first introduce the concept using a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box, where the critical energy E_c = ω²L²/2 separates the harmonic oscillator and particle-in-a-box regimes. They then apply the mixed-mode scheme to real nuclei by combining traditional spherical shell-model states (which diagonalize the single-particle interaction) with collective SU(3) configurations (which diagonalize the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction). For ²⁴Mg in the ds-shell, they use the Wildenthal realistic two-body interaction; for ⁴⁸Cr in the pf-shell, they use the Kuo-Brown-3 (KB3) interaction.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁴Mg: the mixed-mode scheme reproduces the correct binding energy within 2% of the exact full-space result.
- For ²⁴Mg: low-energy configurations have greater than 90% overlap with exact full-space solutions.
- The mixed-mode calculation for ²⁴Mg uses a space that spans less than 10% of the full model space.
- In contrast, a conventional m-scheme spherical shell-model calculation for comparable results requires about 60% of the full space.
- The numerical study of the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator in a box included up to 50 harmonic oscillator states below the critical energy E_c.

## Context
This work extends the spherical shell model (Whitehead, 1970s) and the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) by providing a method to combine both basis sets when neither single-particle nor collective behavior dominates. The approach is tested on ²⁴Mg using the Wildenthal interaction and on pf-shell nuclei ⁴⁴–⁴⁸Ti and ⁴⁸Cr using the KB3 interaction (Kuo and Brown, 1960s). The results enable future multi-mode calculations that could incorporate pairing interaction eigenvectors within the Sp(4) algebraic approach (Dukelsky et al., 2000s; Sviratcheva, 2000s) and Hamiltonian-driven basis sets, potentially mimicking the Interacting Boson Model (Iachello, 1987) and broken-pair theory for systems far from any exactly solvable limit.
