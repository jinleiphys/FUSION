# Hidden Local Symmetry at Loop -- A New Perspective of Composite Gauge Boson and Chiral Phase Transition

> We develop an effective field theory of QCD and QCD-like theories, based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS) model. We formulate the chiral perturbation theory with loops of rho as well as pi and further include quadratic divergences which are crucial to the chiral phase transition. Detailed calculations of the one-loop renormalization-group equation of the parameters of the HLS model are given, with the bare parameters (defined at cutoff Lambda) determined by the matching (``Wilsonian matching'') with the underlying QCD at Lambda through the operator-product expansion of current correlators. The Wilsonian matching predicts low energy phenomenology for the three-flavored QCD in remarkable agreement with the experiments. When the chiral symmetry restoration takes place in the underlying QCD, the HLS model uniquely leads to the Vector Manifestation (VM) as a new pattern of Wigner realization of chiral symmetry, with the rho becoming degenerate with the massless pi as the chiral partner. The VM is in fact realized in the large Nf QCD when Nf to Nf^{crit}-0, with Nf^{crit} = 5 Nc/3 being in rough agreement with the lattice simulation for Nc=3. The VM can be realized also in hot and/or dense QCD.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory of QCD based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) that includes loop corrections from both rho and pi mesons, and shows that when chiral symmetry is restored in the underlying QCD, the HLS model uniquely leads to the Vector Manifestation (VM) as a new pattern of Wigner realization where the rho becomes degenerate with the massless pi as the chiral partner, with the condition that this occurs only when the bare parameters satisfy specific matching conditions with the underlying QCD.

## Method

The authors formulate chiral perturbation theory with HLS including loops of rho mesons as gauge bosons and pi mesons as Nambu-Goldstone bosons, performing one-loop renormalization-group equation calculations of the HLS model parameters. They determine the bare parameters at the cutoff Lambda through "Wilsonian matching" with the underlying QCD using operator-product expansion of current correlators, and include quadratic divergences which are crucial for describing the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers

- For three-flavored QCD with N_c=N_f=3, the cutoff Lambda is chosen as approximately 1.1 GeV
- The physical pion decay constant in the chiral limit is F_pi(0) = 86.4 +/- 9.7 MeV
- The critical number of flavors for chiral restoration is N_f^{crit} ~ 5 N_c/3, which for N_c=3 gives N_f^{crit} ~ 5, in rough agreement with lattice simulations giving 6 < N_f^{crit} < 7
- The bare F_pi^2 at the cutoff is given by F_pi^2(Lambda) ~ 2(1+delta_A) (N_c/3) (Lambda/(4 pi))^2, where delta_A ~ 0.5 for N_f=3
- The KSRF (I) relation g_rho = 2 g_{rho pi pi} F_pi^2 holds as a low energy theorem independent of Lagrangian parameters

## Context

This work extends the tree-level HLS phenomenology reviewed by Bando, Kugo and Yamawaki (1988) to include loop effects, building on Georgi's (1990) demonstration that HLS enables systematic loop expansion with vector mesons. The Wilsonian matching approach provides the HLS theory with explicit N_c-dependence from the underlying QCD through operator-product expansion, enabling predictions for the chiral phase transition in large N_f QCD that agree with lattice simulations by Iwasaki et al. (1992-1998). The VM pattern of chiral restoration, where the rho meson mass vanishes near the critical temperature, contrasts with the conventional linear sigma model picture where the scalar meson mass vanishes, and supports the Brown-Rho (1991) scaling picture.
