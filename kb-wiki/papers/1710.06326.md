# Revised neutrino-gallium cross section and prospects of BEST in resolving the Gallium anomaly

> O(1) eV sterile neutrino can be responsible for a number of anomalous results of neutrino oscillation experiments. This hypothesis may be tested at short base-line neutrino oscillation experiments, several of which are either ongoing or under construction. Here we concentrate on the so-called Gallium anomaly, found by SAGE and GALLEX experiments, and its foreseeable future tests with BEST experiment at Baksan Neutrino Observatory. We start with a revision of the neutrino-gallium cross section, that is performed by utilizing the recent measurements of the nuclear final state spectra. We accordingly correct the parameters of Gallium anomaly and refine the BEST prospects in testing it and searching for sterile neutrinos. We further evolve the previously proposed idea to investigate the anomaly with Zn-65 artificial neutrino source as a next option available at BEST, and estimate its sensitivity to the sterile neutrino model parameters following the Bayesian approach. We show that after the two stages of operation BEST will make 5$σ$-discovery of the sterile neutrinos, if they are behind the Gallium anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper revises the neutrino-gallium cross section using recent nuclear data and finds that the central values and uncertainties of the cross sections have not changed much compared to previous estimates, meaning the Gallium anomaly remains intact and cannot be explained by cross-section uncertainties. The authors show that after two stages of operation (with both Cr-51 and Zn-65 sources), BEST will make a 5-sigma discovery of sterile neutrinos if they are behind the Gallium anomaly, but this claim depends on the anomaly being real and the combined analysis of both runs.

## Method

The authors recalculated the neutrino capture cross section on Ga-71 for the reaction nu_e + Ga-71 -> e- + Ge-71 using the computer program "speccros" by John Bahcall, adapted to incorporate recent measurements of nuclear final state spectra from Frekers (2013) and Frekers (2011). They used a new threshold energy Q = 233.5 +/- 1.2 keV for the gallium-to-germanium ground state transition and updated Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements to excited states, then computed cross sections for Cr-51, Ar-37, and Zn-65 artificial neutrino sources. The revised cross sections were used to refine the parameters of the Gallium anomaly from SAGE and GALLEX experiments and to estimate BEST sensitivity to sterile neutrino oscillations using a Bayesian approach for both Cr-51 and Zn-65 sources.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy for gallium transition to germanium ground state: Q = 233.5 +/- 1.2 keV
- log ft_{1/2}(Ge-71) = 4.353 +/- 0.005
- Ground state Gamow-Teller matrix element: B(GT)_{g.s.} = 0.086 +/- 0.001
- Scale factor sigma_0 = (8.6 +/- 0.1) x 10^{-46} cm^2
- Total cross section for Cr-51 source: sigma(Cr-51) = (59.10 +/- 1.14) x 10^{-46} cm^2
- Total cross section for Ar-37 source: sigma(Ar-37) = (71.38 +/- 1.46) x 10^{-46} cm^2
- Total cross section for Zn-65 source: sigma(Zn-65) = (87.76 +/- 2.03) x 10^{-46} cm^2
- Best fit oscillation parameters from combined SAGE+GALLEX: Delta m^2 = 2.5 eV^2 and sin^2(2 theta) = 0.3
- Combined anomaly ratio R (observed-to-expected) from all gallium experiments: R = 0.84^{+0.05}_{-0.05}
- BEST experimental error with Cr-51 source: 4.9% per zone and 4.2% for total target (improved from 5.5% and 4.8%)
- For Zn-65 source with 1 MCi activity, expected signal ratios at best fit: R = (0.827, 0.781) for the two vessels

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Gallium anomaly found by SAGE (Abdurashitov 1998, 2005) and GALLEX (Kaether 2010) by revising the neutrino-gallium cross section using new nuclear data from Frekers (2013) and Frekers (2011). It builds on previous sterile neutrino parameter estimates by Giunti (2012) and Barinov (2016), and refines the prospects for the BEST experiment at Baksan Neutrino Observatory originally proposed by Gavrin (2010, 2015). The paper enables improved sensitivity to sterile neutrino models by proposing a two-stage approach using both Cr-51 and Zn-65 sources, which provides better coverage of the sterile neutrino parameter space than using identical sources.
