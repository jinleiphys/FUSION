# Signatures of the $Δ$ isobar in spin observables of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ electrodisintegration

> The electrodisintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ is considered focusing on the effects of the $Δ$ isobar excitation which is treated dynamically on the same footing as nucleons. In the region beyond the quasi-elastic peak the predicted transverse response functions $R_{T}$ and $R_{T'}$ are visibly affected. This leads to sizable $Δ$ isobar effects for inclusive and exclusive electron polarization asymmetries in particular kinematic regions. A measurement performed in the proposed regime could provide judgment for models of nuclear forces and currents.

## Key claim

The Δ isobar produces sizable effects in inclusive and exclusive electron polarization asymmetries of ³He electrodisintegration, but only in specific kinematic regions beyond the quasi-elastic peak, namely at moderate momentum transfers around Q = 400–450 MeV, energy transfers around 150–200 MeV, and moderate to large electron scattering angles, where the cross section is low but still potentially measurable.

## Method

The author solves Faddeev-type equations in momentum-space partial-wave representation with channel coupling, treating the Δ isobar as an active degree of freedom on the same footing as nucleons in both the hadronic interactions and electromagnetic currents. The CD Bonn + Δ potential provides the two-baryon interaction, while the electromagnetic current includes one-baryon and meson-exchange two-baryon contributions with π, ρ, and ω mesons. A simple prescription based on relativistic and nonrelativistic scaling variables is applied to correct the position of the quasi-elastic peak when comparing with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Transverse response function R_T at Q = 450 MeV shows visible Δ isobar effects beyond the quasi-elastic peak, supported by experimental data from Marchand (1985) and Dow (1988)
- At Q = 550 MeV, the quasi-elastic peak is displaced by about 13 MeV in Q_0 without the relativistic kinematics correction
- The inclusive electron polarization asymmetry at 350 MeV electron energy and θ_e = 90 degrees changes from 7% to 17% (absolute change of 10%) when including the Δ isobar at energy transfers beyond the quasi-elastic peak
- At θ_e = 150 degrees, the asymmetry changes from -5% to 6% with Δ isobar inclusion
- The differential cross section in the region of interest (Q_0 ≥ 130 MeV) ranges from roughly 1500 to 60 pb/(MeV·sr)
- The magnetic dipole form factor g_ΔN^M1(0) = 4.59 in units of nuclear magneton, with overall uncertainty not exceeding 5%
- Calculations are limited to total relative three-nucleon energy below 175 MeV, well below the theoretical Δ isobar production threshold of 293 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Deltuva 2004) that found only small Δ isobar effects in ³He electrodisintegration around the quasi-elastic peak, by identifying specific kinematic regimes where the effects become sizable. It contrasts with approaches using the static approximation for the Δ isobar (Carlson 1998, Marcucci 2005, Yuan 2011) and with purely nucleonic chiral effective field theory calculations. The results enable experimental tests of Δ isobar dynamics in nuclear forces and currents, particularly through measurements of electron polarization asymmetries in ³He(e,e') and ³He(e,e'd) reactions at facilities with few-hundred-MeV electron beams.
