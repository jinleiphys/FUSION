# Heavy-flavored tetraquark states with the $QQ\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ configuration

> In the framework of the color-magnetic interaction, we systematically investigate the mass spectrum of the tetraquark states composed of four heavy quarks with the $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$ configuration in this work. We also show their strong decay patterns. Stable or narrow states in the $bb\bar{b}\bar{c}$ and $bc\bar{b}\bar{c}$ systems are found to be possible. We hope the studies shall be helpful to the experimental search for heavy-full exotic tetraquark states.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate the mass spectrum of tetraquark states composed of four heavy quarks (QQQQ configuration) using the color-magnetic interaction model, and find that stable or narrow states may exist specifically in the bbbarc and bcbar{b}c systems, but not in all heavy tetraquark configurations.

## Method

The authors adopt the color-magnetic interaction (CMI) Hamiltonian derived from one-gluon-exchange, constructing color-spin wave functions in a diquark-antidiquark picture with explicit consideration of the Pauli principle for identical quarks. They calculate the CMI matrix elements for six independent flavor configurations (bbbar{b}b, ccbar{c}c, bbbar{c}c, bbbar{b}c, ccbar{c}b, bcbar{b}c) and estimate masses using two approaches: one with effective quark masses (m_b=5052.9 MeV, m_c=1724.8 MeV) and another using meson-meson thresholds as reference points.

## Key numbers

- C_{bbar{b}} = 2.9 MeV extracted from the mass splitting m_Upsilon - m_eta_b = 61 MeV
- C_{cbar{c}} = 5.3 MeV extracted from the mass splitting m_J/psi - m_eta_c = 114 MeV
- C_{bbar{c}} = 3.3 MeV estimated from m_B_c* - m_B_c = 70 MeV calculated with a quark model
- For the bbbar{b}b system with J^{PC}=2^{++}: mass estimated at 20243 MeV (with effective quark masses) or 18921 MeV (from UpsilonUpsilon threshold)
- For the bbbar{b}b system with J^{PC}=0^{++}: two states at 20275 MeV and 20155 MeV (effective quark masses), or 18954 MeV and 18834 MeV (from UpsilonUpsilon threshold)
- For the ccbar{c}c system with J^{PC}=2^{++}: mass estimated at 6956 MeV (effective quark masses) or 6194 MeV (from psi psi threshold)
- For the ccbar{c}c system with J^{PC}=0^{++}: two states at 7016 MeV and 6797 MeV (effective quark masses), or 6254 MeV and 6035 MeV (from psi psi threshold)
- The mass splitting between the two scalar tetraquarks is around 120 MeV for the bottom system and 220 MeV for the charmed system
- Uncertainty caused by the change of C_{QQ} is less than 20 MeV in the bbbar{b}b case and less than 37 MeV in the ccbar{c}c case

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tetraquark states using the chromomagnetic interaction approach, following the framework of Hogaasen (2004) and Buccella (2006) for multiquark systems. It builds upon the pioneering investigation of the ccbar{c}c system by Chao (1980) and the work of Silvestre-Brac (1992) on chromomagnetic interactions for Q^2bar{Q}^2 systems. The results are consistent with those of Karliner (2016), Hughes (2017), and Richard (2017) in finding that all bbbar{b}b and ccbar{c}c tetraquark masses lie above the lowest meson-meson threshold, suggesting these states are not stable. The study enables future experimental searches for heavy exotic tetraquark states, particularly in the bbbar{b}c and bcbar{b}c systems where stable or narrow states are predicted.
