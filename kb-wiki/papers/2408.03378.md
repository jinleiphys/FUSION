# Euclidean Effective Theory for Partons in the Spirit of Steven Weinberg

> The standard formulation of parton physics involves light-cone correlations of quark and gluon fields in a hadron, which leads to a widespread impression that it can only be studied through real-time Hamiltonian dynamics or light-front quantization, which are challenged by non-perturbative computations with a pertinent regulator for light-cone/rapidity divergences (or zero modes). As such, standard lattice QCD studies have been limited to indirect parton observables such as first few moments and short-distance correlations, which do not provide the $x$-distributions without solving the model-dependent inverse problem. Here I describe an alternative formulation of partons in terms of equal-time (or Euclidean) correlators, which allows to compute precision-controlled $x$-distribution through lattice QCD simulations. This approach is in accord with Weinberg's pioneering idea of effective field theory as well as Wilson's renormalization group, in which the large hadron momentum serves as a natural cut-off for light-cone/rapidity divergences and can ultimately be eliminated through a method like the ``perfect action'' program in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that parton physics can be reformulated using equal-time (Euclidean) correlators rather than light-cone correlators, enabling precision-controlled calculations of parton distribution functions (PDFs) through lattice QCD simulations, with the caveat that this approach works only when parton momenta are at perturbative scales (xP >> Λ_QCD) and breaks down at small x ~ Λ_QCD/P.

## Method

The author derives an effective field theory called large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) by factorizing deep-inelastic scattering in the Bjorken frame, where the hadron moves with large velocity v and the virtual photon momentum has only a z-component. The quark fields are split into collinear components satisfying the equation of motion slashed{v} ψ_v = 0, which allows elimination of the k^0 component and reduction to equal-time correlation functions along the z-direction. The resulting momentum distributions are then matched to light-cone PDFs through a perturbative Wilson coefficient C, with power corrections organized by twists of local operators.

## Key numbers

- The hadron momentum in the Bjorken frame is P^μ = Mγ v^μ with γ = sqrt(1 + Q^2/(4x_B M^2)), where in the Bjorken limit γ ~ Q → ∞ and v → 1
- The virtual photon momentum is q^μ = (0,0,0,-Q) with Q being the photon virtuality
- Power corrections to twist-2 parton distributions scale as (Λ_QCD/P^z)^2 for twist-4 contributions
- The expansion breaks down at small x ~ Λ_QCD/P, where P is the hadron momentum

## Context

This work extends the author's own LaMET framework (Ji 2014) and contrasts with the standard light-cone formulation of parton physics, which requires light-front quantization or soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) as developed by Bauer et al. (2000-2001). The approach is analogous to Symanzik's improved/perfect action program in lattice QCD (Symanzik 1983), where the large hadron momentum serves as a natural cutoff for light-cone/rapidity divergences that can be systematically eliminated. This enables direct lattice QCD computation of x-distributions without solving the model-dependent inverse problem that plagues approaches using only the first few moments or short-distance correlations.
