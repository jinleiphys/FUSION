# Gravitational wave asteroseismology of neutron stars with unified EOS: on the role of high-order nuclear empirical parameters

> We analyze the sensitivity of non-radial fluid oscillation modes and tidal deformations in neutron stars to high-order nuclear empirical parameters (NEP). In particular, we study the impact of the curvature and skewness of the symmetry energy $K_{\rm sym}$, $Q_{\rm sym}$, and the skewness of the binding energy in symmetric nuclear matter $Q_{\rm sat}$. As we are interested in the possibility of gravitational wave detection by future interferometers, we consider that the tidal interaction is the driving force for the quadrupolar non-radial fluid oscillations. We have also studied the correlations between those quantities, which will be useful to understand the strong physics of gravitational wave phenomena. Our main results show that $K_{\rm sym}$ impacts the frequencies of the fundamental mode mainly for low-mass neutron stars. The NEP $Q_{\rm sym}$ and $Q_{\rm sat}$ affect the fundamental modes of intermediate and heavy neutron stars, respectively. In the case of the first pressure mode, $K_{\rm sym}$ shows a small effect, while $Q_{\rm sat}$ shows a considerable decrease in this oscillation mode independent of the neutron star mass. Similarly, for tidal deformability, the NEP $Q_{\rm sat}$ and $Q_{\rm sym}$ show a bigger impact than $K_{\rm sym}$. Given the impact of the NEP on gravitational wave phenomena and the currently large uncertainties of these parameters, the prospect of higher sensitivity in future gravitational wave detectors promise a possible new tool to constrain high-order NEP.

## Key claim

The paper shows that high-order nuclear empirical parameters (NEP), specifically the curvature of the symmetry energy \(K_{\rm sym}\), the skewness of the symmetry energy \(Q_{\rm sym}\), and the skewness of the binding energy in symmetric nuclear matter \(Q_{\rm sat}\), each affect neutron-star oscillation modes and tidal deformability in distinct mass ranges, but the results are qualitative because each parameter was varied independently while keeping all other NEP fixed to the reference H2\(_{MM}\) equation of state.

## Method

The authors constructed twelve unified (crust-core) equations of state using a nuclear meta-model (MM) fitted to the chiral Hamiltonian H2 from Drischler et al. (2016), with the crust modeled via a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM). They independently varied \(K_{\rm sym}\) (five values from -200 to -1 MeV), \(Q_{\rm sym}\) (three values from 100 to 1000 MeV), and \(Q_{\rm sat}\) (four values from -400 to 400 MeV) around the reference H2\(_{MM}\) values, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and the linearized perturbation equations for quadrupolar (\(l=2\)) non-radial fluid oscillations and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- The reference H2\(_{MM}\) EoS has \(K_{\rm sym} = -144\) MeV, \(Q_{\rm sat} = -220\) MeV, and \(Q_{\rm sym} = 700\) MeV.
- The \(f\)-mode frequency is greater than 1.8 kHz for neutron stars with masses above 1.4 M\(_{\odot}\), with a maximum frequency of approximately 2.3 kHz.
- The \(p\)-mode frequency is above approximately 7.0 kHz for stars with masses greater than 1.4 M\(_{\odot}\).
- Varying \(K_{\rm sym}\) within the range \(-200\) to \(-1\) MeV (current uncertainty approximately \(\pm 100\) MeV) shows a small impact on tidal deformability; the model with \(K_{\rm sym} = -1\) MeV lies on the 50% confidence contour of GW170817.
- For \(Q_{\rm sym}\), the value \(Q_{\rm sym} = 1000\) MeV places the tidal deformability slightly inside both the 50% and 90% confidence contours of GW170817.
- For \(Q_{\rm sat}\), models with \(Q_{\rm sat} \geq 0\) MeV lie inside both GW170817 contour lines, while negative values produce tidal deformability below the 50% line.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Sukrit et al. (2021) and Pradhan et al. (2023), which investigated the role of lower-order nuclear parameters (such as effective mass and symmetry energy) on neutron-star \(f\)-modes, by focusing on the previously unconstrained high-order NEP \(K_{\rm sym}\), \(Q_{\rm sym}\), and \(Q_{\rm sat}\). The results show that future third-generation gravitational-wave detectors (e.g., Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) could provide a new tool to constrain these high-order NEP, which are currently poorly determined by nuclear experiments and ab-initio theory.
