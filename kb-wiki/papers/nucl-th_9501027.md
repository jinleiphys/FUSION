# An $U_{qp}(u_2)$ Rotor Model for Rotational Bands of Superdeformed Nuclei

> A nonrigid rotor model is developed from the two-parameter quantum algebra $U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)$. [This model presents the $U_{qp}({\rm u}_2)$ symmetry and shall be referred to as the qp-rotor model.] A rotational energy formula as well as a qp-deformation of E2 reduced transition probabilities are derived. The qp-rotor model is applied (through fitting procedures) to twenty rotational bands of superdeformed nuclei in the $A \sim 130$, 150 and 190 mass regions. Systematic comparisons between the qp-rotor model and the q-rotor model of Raychev, Roussev and Smirnov, on one hand, and a basic three-parameter model, on the other hand, are performed on energy spectra, on dynamical moments of inertia and on B(E2) values. The physical signification of the deformation parameters q and p is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a nonrigid rotor model based on the two-parameter quantum algebra \(U_{qp}(\mathrm{u}_2)\) and applies it to twenty superdeformed rotational bands, claiming that the additional parameter \(p\) (beyond the single parameter \(q\) used in earlier \(q\)-rotor models) yields systematically better fits to experimental gamma-ray energies, particularly when the distortion or stretching phenomenon is pronounced. The hedge is that the quality of the fit depends on whether the parameters \(q\) and \(p\) are taken as complex conjugates (parametrization (a)) or as real numbers (parametrization (b)), with the best results for the \(A \sim 190\) mass region using parametrization (a).

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian proportional to the invariant \(C_2(U_{qp}(\mathrm{u}_2))\) of the two-parameter quantum algebra, yielding the energy formula \(E(I)_{qp} = (1/2\mathcal{I}) [[I]]_{qp} [[I+1]]_{qp} + E_0\), where \([[X]]_{qp} = (q^X - p^X)/(q-p)\). They also derive a \(qp\)-deformed expression for E2 reduced transition probabilities \(B(\mathrm{E2}; I+2 \to I)_{qp}\) by replacing ordinary boson operators with \(qp\)-deformed bosons in the van der Waerden realization of the tensor operator. The model is fitted to twenty superdeformed bands in the \(A \sim 130\), 150, and 190 mass regions by minimizing a chi-squared statistic on gamma-ray transition energies, and the results are compared with the one-parameter \(q\)-rotor model of Raychev, Roussev and Smirnov and with a three-parameter Bohr-Mottelson-type expansion.

## Key numbers

* For the \(qp\)-rotor model, chi-squared values range from 0.6 to 4.2 in the \(A \sim 190\) mass region (except \(^{194}\mathrm{Hg(a)}\) with \(\chi = 9.1\)), compared to 0.9 to 29.4 for the \(q\)-rotor model in the same region.
* For the \(A \sim 130\) and 150 mass regions, chi-squared values for the \(qp\)-rotor model range from 1.9 to 20.3, while those for the \(q\)-rotor model range from 8.8 to 87.9.
* The experimental gamma-ray energies for the \(A \sim 190\) region range from 200 to 900 keV for angular momenta from 8 to 50; for the \(A \sim 130\) and 150 regions, they range from 600 to 1700 keV for angular momenta from 14 to 64.
* The experimental errors \(\Delta E_\gamma(I)\) are 0.5 keV for most bands, except for \(^{192}\mathrm{Hg}\), \(^{194}\mathrm{Hg(a)}\), and \(^{194}\mathrm{Hg(b)}\) where they are as low as 0.1 keV.

## Context

This work extends the one-parameter \(q\)-rotor model of Raychev, Roussev and Smirnov (1990) and the related work of Bonatsos et al. (1991) by introducing a second deformation parameter \(p\), which allows the model to interpolate between complex-conjugate and real parameter regimes. The \(qp\)-rotor model is shown to outperform both the \(q\)-rotor model and a three-parameter Bohr-Mottelson expansion (with terms \(A'I(I+1) + B'[I(I+1)]^2 + C'[I(I+1)]^3\)) in describing the distortion or stretching of superdeformed rotational bands. The results enable future systematic studies of two-parameter quantum algebra symmetries in nuclear spectroscopy, particularly for bands where the dynamical moment of inertia varies strongly with spin.
