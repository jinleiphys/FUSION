# Nuclear Ground-State Masses and Deformations

> We tabulate the atomic mass excesses and nuclear ground-state deformations of 8979 nuclei ranging from $^{16}$O to $A=339$. The calculations are based on the finite-range droplet macroscopic model and the folded-Yukawa single-particle microscopic model. Relative to our 1981 mass table the current results are obtained with an improved macroscopic model, an improved pairing model with a new form for the effective-interaction pairing gap, and minimization of the ground-state energy with respect to additional shape degrees of freedom. The values of only 9 constants are determined directly from a least-squares adjustment to the ground-state masses of 1654 nuclei ranging from $^{16}$O to $^{263}$106 and to 28 fission-barrier heights. The error of the mass model is 0.669~MeV for the entire region of nuclei considered, but is only 0.448~MeV for the region above $N=65$.

## Key claim

The authors present a new global nuclear mass table based on the finite-range droplet macroscopic model and folded-Yukawa single-particle microscopic model, achieving a model error of 0.669 MeV for all 8979 nuclei considered, but only 0.448 MeV for the region above N=65, with the caveat that these errors are determined using a maximum-likelihood statistical method that subtracts experimental uncertainties rather than using a simple root-mean-square deviation.

## Method

The macroscopic-microscopic method combines the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) for the macroscopic energy with the folded-Yukawa single-particle potential for microscopic shell-plus-pairing corrections, using the Lipkin-Nogami pairing model with an improved effective-interaction pairing gap. The ground-state energy is minimized with respect to epsilon_2, epsilon_4, epsilon_3, and epsilon_6 shape degrees of freedom, and an eighth-order Strutinsky shell correction is applied. Nine model constants were determined from a least-squares adjustment to 1654 experimental ground-state masses and 28 fission-barrier heights.

## Key numbers

- Mass table covers 8979 nuclei from O-16 to A=339
- Model error (sigma_th) of 0.669 MeV for the entire region of nuclei considered
- Model error of 0.448 MeV for the region above N=65
- 9 constants determined from least-squares adjustment to 1654 ground-state masses (ranging from O-16 to 263106) and 28 fission-barrier heights
- Previous 1981 calculation included only 4023 nuclei

## Context

This work extends the authors' 1981 mass calculation (Moller and Nix, 1981) and their 1988 interim progress reports by resolving issues related to pairing calculations, higher-multipole distortions, and shell-correction details. The FRDM improves upon the earlier finite-range liquid-drop model (FRLDM) by incorporating nuclear compressibility effects and an empirical exponential term for light nuclei, building on the droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki (1974). The new mass table enables predictions for nuclei between the proton and neutron drip lines and superheavy nuclei up to A=339, with additional nuclear-structure properties to be published in a forthcoming article on nuclear astrophysics.
