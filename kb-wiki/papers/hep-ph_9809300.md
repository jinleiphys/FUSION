# Charmonium Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions by Prompt Gluons

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions, also the bremsstrahlung of gluons in the fragmentation regions of the nuclei suppresses the produced charmonium states. In the energy range of the SPS, the radiation of semi-hard gluons occurs in the Bethe-Heitler regime and the density of gluons and therefore the suppression goes like $(AB)^{1\over3}$, where $A$ and $B$ are the nucleon numbers of the projectile and target nuclei. In contrast, the suppression via collisions with nucleons is proportional to $(A^{1\over3} + B^{1\over3})$. Parameter free perturbative QCD calculations are in a good agreement with the data on $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ suppression in heavy ion collisions at SPS CERN. At higher energies (RHIC, LHC) the number of gluons which are able to break-up the charmonium substantially decreases and the additional suppression is expected to vanish.

# Charmonium Suppression in Heavy Ion Collisions by Prompt Gluons

## Key claim

The anomalous suppression of J/Ψ and Ψ' in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies can be explained by charmonium break-up from gluon bremsstrahlung produced in multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors claim that this additional suppression scales as (AB)^(1/3) at SPS energies but is expected to vanish at higher energies (RHIC, LHC) due to the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect.

## Method

The authors calculate charmonium suppression in pA and AB collisions using a parameter-free perturbative QCD framework that combines two mechanisms: (i) final-state absorption by nucleons, with cross sections derived from photoproduction data and modified by formation time effects, and (ii) absorption by gluons radiated in the Bethe-Heitler regime from "wounded" nucleons in multiple NN collisions. The suppression factor is computed via an integral over nuclear densities and impact parameters, with the mean number of radiated gluons ⟨n_g⟩ determined by integrating the gluon radiation spectrum subject to a formation length constraint.

## Key numbers

- Mean number of radiated gluons per NN collision at SPS (√s = 20 GeV): ⟨n_g⟩ = 0.69 for ω_min = 0.5 GeV, and ⟨n_g⟩ = 0.25 for ω_min = 1.0 GeV
- Mean number of radiated gluons at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): ⟨n_g⟩ = 6.9 × 10^(-3)
- Mean number of radiated gluons at LHC (√s = 1200 GeV): ⟨n_g⟩ = 1.2 × 10^(-3)
- Inelastic J/Ψ-nucleon cross section from photoproduction: σ_in(J/Ψ N) ≈ 4 mb
- Inelastic J/Ψ-gluon cross section: σ_in(J/Ψ g) ≈ 3 mb (derived as 3/4 of σ_in(J/Ψ N))
- Effective absorption cross sections in Pb-Pb at E_lab = 158 GeV/A with x_F = 0.15: σ_eff^A(J/Ψ N) = 3.9 mb, σ_eff^B(J/Ψ N) = 3.3 mb, σ_eff^A(Ψ' N) = 8.6 mb, σ_eff^B(Ψ' N) = 4.8 mb
- Formation lengths in Pb-Pb: l_f^A = 2 fm (projectile rest frame), l_f^B = 4 fm (target rest frame)
- Ratio of mean square radii Ψ' to J/Ψ: 7/3 in oscillator model, approximately 4 from realistic wave functions

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach of Huefner and Kopeliovich (1997) for charmonium suppression in nuclear collisions by adding gluon bremsstrahlung as an additional suppression mechanism. It directly addresses the "anomalous" J/Ψ suppression observed by the NA38 and NA50 collaborations at SPS CERN, which had been interpreted by some authors as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation following the original proposal by Matsui and Satz. The paper provides an alternative explanation that does not require QGP, using only perturbative QCD with no adjustable parameters. The prediction that prompt gluon suppression vanishes at RHIC and LHC energies enables future experimental tests to distinguish between this mechanism and comover or QGP-based explanations, which are expected to increase with energy.
