# Infrared regularization for spin-1 fields

> We extend the method of infrared regularization to spin-1 fields. As applications, we discuss the chiral extrapolation of the rho meson mass from lattice QCD data and the pion-rho sigma term.

## Key claim

The authors extend the method of infrared regularization, previously developed for baryon chiral perturbation theory, to spin-1 fields such as vector mesons, enabling a systematic power-counting scheme for loop diagrams where the heavy particle appears only inside the loop (not on external lines), which was not covered by the original Becher and Leutwyler (1999) formulation.

## Method

The authors use dimensional regularization and a Feynman parameter representation to separate one-loop integrals containing one heavy (vector meson) and one light (Goldstone boson) propagator into an infrared-singular part (obeying chiral power counting) and a regular part (analytic in the small parameters). The regular part is dropped, as it can be absorbed into renormalization of the low-energy constants of the effective Lagrangian. The method is then applied to the Goldstone boson self-energy graph where the vector meson appears only inside the loop, the triangle graph, and the vector meson self-energy with a pure Goldstone boson loop.

## Key numbers

* The vector meson mass in the chiral limit is denoted \(M_V\), with the physical rho meson mass cited as approximately 770 MeV.
* The coupling constants for vector meson interactions are \(F_V\) and \(G_V\); from measured decay rates for \(\rho^0 \rightarrow e^+e^-\) and \(\rho \rightarrow 2\pi\), one obtains \(F_V = 154\) MeV and \(G_V = 69\) MeV (though the latter requires correction because the pions from rho decay are not soft).
* The parameter \(a = M_\phi^2 / M_V^2\) and \(b = q^2 / M_V^2\) are defined for the scalar loop integral \(I_{V\phi}(q)\), where \(M_\phi\) is the Goldstone boson mass.

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), originally developed for baryon chiral perturbation theory, to the case of spin-1 fields. It addresses the problem that loop diagrams with heavy vector mesons violate the standard chiral power counting, similar to the problem first analyzed by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) for nucleons. The method enables systematic chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data for the rho meson mass and calculations of the pion-rho sigma term, complementing earlier approaches such as the heavy baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and the subtraction scheme of Tang (1996).
