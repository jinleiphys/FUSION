# Analysis of three-nucleon forces effects in the $A=3$ system

> Using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions in the description of the $A=3,4$ nuclear systems the $χ^2$ per datum results to be much bigger than one. In particular it is not possible to reproduce the three- and four-nucleon binding energies and the $n-d$ scattering length simultaneously. This is one manifestation of the necessity of including a three-nucleon force in the nuclear Hamiltonian. In this paper we perform an analysis of some, widely used, three-nucleon force models. We analyze their capability to describe the aforementioned quantities and, to improve their description, we propose modifications in the parametrization of the models. The effects of these new parametrization are studied in some polarization observables at low energies.

## Key claim

Modern nucleon-nucleon interactions alone cannot simultaneously reproduce the three- and four-nucleon binding energies and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length; even after including widely used three-nucleon force models (Tucson-Melbourne, Brazil, Urbana IX, and N2LOL), the simultaneous description of all three observables remains incomplete, motivating modifications to the profile functions and parametrizations of these forces.

## Method

The author used the Hyperspherical Harmonics method to solve the three- and four-nucleon bound and scattering states, employing the AV18 and N3LO-Idaho two-nucleon potentials combined with the Urbana IX (URIX), Tucson-Melbourne (TM'), and N2LOL three-nucleon force models. New one-parameter profile functions \(Z_0(r)\), \(y(r)\), and \(T(r)\) were constructed to match the short-range behavior of the N2LOL model's cutoff functions, and the strengths of the \(b\)-, \(d\)-, and \(E\)-terms in the URIX model were refitted to reproduce the triton binding energy and the doublet scattering length. The resulting predictions for polarization observables in \(p-d\) scattering at \(E_{lab}=3\) MeV were then compared to the original URIX model and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy \(B(^3\text{H})\): AV18 alone gives 7.624 MeV, AV18+TM' gives 8.440 MeV, AV18+URIX gives 8.479 MeV, N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL gives 8.474 MeV; experimental value is 8.482 MeV.
- \(^4\)He binding energy \(B(^4\text{He})\): AV18 alone gives 24.22 MeV, AV18+TM' gives 28.31 MeV, AV18+URIX gives 28.48 MeV, N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL gives 28.37 MeV; experimental value is 28.30 MeV.
- Doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length \(^2a_{nd}\): AV18 alone gives 1.258 fm, AV18+TM' gives 0.623 fm, AV18+URIX gives 0.578 fm, N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL gives 0.675 fm; experimental value is 0.645\(\pm\)0.003\(\pm\)0.007 fm.
- With the new parametrization of the URIX model (using \(A^{PW}_{2\pi} = -0.1200\) MeV, \(D^{PW}_{2\pi} = 0.50\), \(A_R = 0.0155\) MeV), the triton binding energy is reproduced and \(^2a_{nd} = 0.645\) fm, matching the experimental value.
- The momentum cutoff in the N2LOL model is fixed at \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV; in the TM' model typical values are around \(\Lambda = 5\,m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the comparative study of three-nucleon force models by Kievsky (2010) and the review of bound and zero-energy states for \(A=3,4\) by the same group (Report). It addresses the long-standing Phillips line correlation between the triton binding energy and the doublet scattering length, showing that the original Urbana IX model (Pieper, 2001) fails to reproduce both simultaneously. The proposed modifications to the profile functions enable the AV18+URIX model to correctly describe \(B(^3\text{H})\) and \(^2a_{nd}\), but the minimal improvement in vector analyzing powers \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) suggests that the spin-isospin structure of the URIX model remains incomplete, pointing toward the need for further refinements in the operatorial structure of three-nucleon forces.
