# An ab initio theory of double odd-even mass differences in nuclei

> Two aspects of the problem of evaluating double odd-even mass differences D_2 in semi-magic nuclei are studied related to existence of two components with different properties, a superfluid nuclear subsystem and a non-superfluid one. For the superfluid subsystem, the difference D_2 is approximately equal to 2Δ, the gap Δbeing the solution of the gap equation. For the non-superfluid subsystem, D_2 is found by solving the equation for two-particle Green function for normal systems. Both equations under consideration contain the same effective pairing interaction. For the latter, the semi-microscopic model is used in which the main term calculated from the first principles is supplemented with a small phenomenological addendum containing one phenomenological parameter supposed to be universal for all medium and heavy atomic nuclei.

# An *ab initio* theory of double odd-even mass differences in nuclei

## Key claim
The double odd-even mass difference D₂ in semi-magic nuclei can be described by a single semi-microscopic model with one universal phenomenological parameter γ=0.06, but only if the superfluid and non-superfluid subsystems are treated separately; the agreement for the non-superfluid case (rms deviation 0.36 MeV) is worse than for the superfluid case (rms deviation ~0.13-0.14 MeV for the gap), and phonon corrections are less regular in the non-superfluid subsystem.

## Method
For the superfluid subsystem, the BCS gap equation was solved in a model subspace using an effective pairing interaction obtained from the Argonne v₁₈ potential via a two-step method with the Local Potential Approximation (LPA), supplemented by a phenomenological term γ C₀ ρ(r)/ρ̄(0). For the non-superfluid subsystem, the two-particle Green function equation was solved in the same model space with the same effective interaction, identifying D₂ with the first poles of the interaction amplitude Γ. The calculations used the self-consistent λ-basis of the Fayans Energy Density Functional (DF3 and DF3-a).

## Key numbers
- For the superfluid subsystem (pairing gap Δ): overall rms deviation √( (Δ_th - Δ_exp)² ) ≈ 0.13 MeV for DF3 functional and ≈ 0.14 MeV for DF3-a functional, based on 34 nuclei from Sn, Pb, and N=82 chains.
- For the non-superfluid subsystem (D₂ values): total rms deviation δM_tot(γ=0) = 0.66 MeV and δM_tot(γ=0.06) = 0.43 MeV across all nuclei; restricting to nuclei with chemical potential μ < -4 MeV gives δM_tot(γ=0) = 0.64 MeV and δM_tot(γ=0.06) = 0.36 MeV.
- For lead isotopes (proton D₂): δM_2p(γ=0) = 0.64 MeV and δM_2p(γ=0.06) = 0.42 MeV (N_p=26).
- For tin isotopes (proton D₂): δM_2p(γ=0) = 0.68 MeV and δM_2p(γ=0.06) = 0.45 MeV (N_p=21).
- For N=82 isotones (neutron D₂): δM_2n(γ=0) = 0.66 MeV and δM_2n(γ=0.06) = 0.42 MeV (N_n=19).
- For the double-magic nucleus ²⁰⁸Pb: D₂p⁺(γ=0) = -2.35 MeV; phonon correction δ_ph D₂p = -0.03 MeV (negligible).
- For the semi-magic nucleus ²⁰⁴Pb: D₂p⁺ phonon correction δ_ph D₂p⁺ = 0.72 MeV for the 1h₉/₂ state, comparable to the effect of γ=0.06.
- The BCS gap for Sn isotopes with γ=0 is approximately 30% larger than experimental values.

## Context
This work extends the semi-microscopic pairing model developed by the Moscow-Catania group (Pankratov 2007, Baldo 2008, Saperstein 2009) from the pairing gap to double odd-even mass differences in non-superfluid subsystems. It builds on earlier *ab initio* BCS calculations by the Milan group (using Argonne v₁₄ potential with Saxon-Woods basis) and by Duguet et al. (using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with Sly4 force), which found that effective mass corrections and many-body corrections (phonon exchange) partially compensate. The present work uses the same effective interaction for both subsystems, enabling a unified description. The results enable systematic inclusion of phonon corrections in future work, with the parameter γ to be readjusted, and highlight the need for improved treatment near the drip line where the mean-field concept breaks down.
