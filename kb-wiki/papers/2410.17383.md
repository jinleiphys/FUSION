# A Machine Learning Approach to Trapped Many-Fermion Systems

> We apply a variational Ansatz based on neural networks to the problem of spin-$1/2$ fermions in a harmonic trap interacting through a short distance potential. We showed that standard machine learning techniques lead to a quick convergence to the ground state, especially in weakly coupled cases. Higher couplings can be handled efficiently by increasing the strength of interactions during "training".

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a neural-network-based variational ansatz can efficiently converge to the ground state of trapped spin-1/2 fermions with contact interactions, with the hedge that the method works especially well for weakly coupled cases and that higher couplings require a transfer learning strategy where interaction strength is gradually increased during training.

## Method
The authors use a variational Monte Carlo (VQMC) approach where the trial wave function is constructed as a product of two Slater determinants (one for each spin species) multiplied by a Gaussian factor, with each determinant entry parameterized by a separate feed-forward neural network. The neural network parameters are optimized by minimizing the energy expectation value via gradient descent using the Adam optimizer, with Monte Carlo sampling performed via the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm. Transfer learning is employed by training first at zero coupling and then incrementally increasing the coupling strength in steps of δg = 0.5, using the converged parameters from one coupling as the initial condition for the next.

## Key numbers
- For the two-fermion system (N↑ = N↓ = 1), a neural network with α = 100 nodes per hidden layer (β ≈ 2×10^4 parameters) achieved sub-percent accuracy in ground state energy across coupling strengths from g = -1 to g = 2, compared to the exact Busch solution.
- For the N↑ = N↓ = 5 system, neural networks with α = 150 (β ≈ 2×10^5 parameters) obtained ground state energies within sub-percent of the energies computed with α = 200.
- Transfer learning from g = 0 to g = 2.5 for the N↑ = N↓ = 2 system converged within approximately 15 gradient descent steps, compared to approximately 30 steps for random parameter initialization.
- Ground state energies were computed for impurity systems with N↑ from 1 to 10 at coupling strengths g = 0.2 and g = 2.0, with results lying below the modified McGuire expression and approaching it as N↑ → ∞.

## Context
This work extends the application of neural-network variational methods from condensed matter and quantum chemistry (where long-range Coulomb forces are relevant, as in references PhysRevResearch.2.033429 and doi:10.1080/23746149.2020.1797528) to nuclear and atomic trap systems with short-range forces, building on prior work by Gnech (2023), Kim (2023), Fore (2022), and others. The results for the impurity system are benchmarked against diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Astrakharchik and Brouzos (2013) and the modified McGuire expression. The method enables efficient computation of ground states for larger fermion systems and opens the door to three-dimensional calculations and systems with more particles, with the future goal of extracting simpler, more interpretable ansatze from the trained neural networks.
