# Conserved energy-momentum tensor for real-time lattice simulations

> We derive an expression for the energy-momentum tensor in the discrete lattice formulation of pure glue QCD. The resulting expression satisfies the continuity equation for energy conservation up to numerical errors with a symmetric procedure for the time discretization. In the case of the momentum conservation equation, we obtain an expression that is of higher accuracy in lattice spacing ($\mathcal{O}(a^2)$) than the naive discretization where fields in the continuum expressions are replaced by discretized counterparts. The improvements are verified by performing numerical tests on the derived expressions using classical real-time lattice gauge theory simulations. We demonstrate substantial reductions in relative error of one to several orders of magnitude compared to a naive discretization for both energy and momentum conservation equations. We expect our formulation to have applications in the area of pre-equilibrium dynamics in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, in particular for the extraction of transport coefficients such as shear viscosity.

## Key claim
The paper derives an improved lattice energy-momentum tensor for pure glue QCD that satisfies the energy conservation continuity equation exactly (up to numerical errors) and satisfies the momentum conservation equation to higher accuracy in lattice spacing (O(a²)) than the naive discretization, but the momentum conservation derivation requires approximating plaquettes by the identity matrix, introducing a relative error of O(a²).

## Method
The authors start from the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for classical SU(Nc) Yang-Mills theory in temporal axial gauge and construct the energy density T⁰⁰ by spatially averaging electric and magnetic fields to be centered at lattice sites while preserving total energy. They then derive T⁰ⁱ (the Poynting vector) by requiring that the discrete continuity equation for energy conservation holds exactly, and obtain Tⁱʲ from the momentum conservation continuity equation using the equations of motion, with a time-symmetrization procedure to reduce finite timestep errors.

## Key numbers
- The improved T⁰ⁱ satisfies the energy conservation continuity equation exactly (up to numerical errors from time discretization)
- The improved Tⁱʲ satisfies the momentum conservation continuity equation to O(a²) accuracy in lattice spacing, compared to the naive discretization
- Numerical tests demonstrate reductions in relative error of one to several orders of magnitude compared to a naive discretization for both energy and momentum conservation equations
- The derivation uses a three-dimensional lattice with spacing aμ in spatial direction μ and product a_s³ = a_x a_y a_z

## Context
This work extends the classical-statistical lattice gauge theory approach used for Glasma simulations in heavy-ion collisions, as studied by Lappi (2006), Schenke (2012), Berges (2013), and Boguslavski (2020). It differs from the lattice QCD approach of Caracciolo (1989, 1991) and Bochicchio (1985), which constructs the EMT to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities at the quantum level after renormalization. The improved EMT enables more accurate extraction of transport coefficients such as shear viscosity from real-time lattice simulations of pre-equilibrium dynamics.
