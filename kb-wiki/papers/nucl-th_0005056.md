# Systematic analysis of ΔI = 4 bifurcation in A~150 superdeformed nuclei: Active orbitals

> A simple criterion for the ΔI = 4 bifurcation is applied to thirty superdeformed bands in the A~150 mass region. The analysis allows to differentiate between active and inactive for staggering single-particle states. The consideration is based on additivity of nonaxial hexadecapole moment, which plays a key role in the phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper applies a previously proposed necessary condition for the existence of Delta I = 4 bifurcation (staggering) in superdeformed bands to thirty bands in the A ~ 150 mass region, finding that the condition (based on the sign of a parameter c derived from the nonaxial hexadecapole moment) is not violated in any band with known staggering significance, but the authors caution that the condition is necessary but insufficient and that the criterion becomes unreliable at low rotational frequencies.

## Method

The authors calculate the expectation values q_44 of the nonaxial hexadecapole operator for single-particle states using the modified oscillator (MO) potential with the code GAMPN, employing cranking wave functions in the stretched rotating basis. They identify active (nonperturbative) and inactive (perturbative) orbitals for staggering based on the dependence of q_44 on rotational frequency, then compute the total nonaxial hexadecapole moment Q_44 for each superdeformed band using additivity of contributions from individual active orbitals. The sign of the parameter c, which determines whether the necessary condition for staggering is satisfied, is compared with the experimental staggering significance Y from the literature.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Pavlichenkov (1999) which proposed that Delta I = 4 bifurcation arises from coupling of rotation with single-particle motion via nonaxial hexadecapole interactions, and applies it to a much larger set of thirty superdeformed bands compared to the eight bands analyzed previously. The analysis enables identification of specific active single-particle orbitals (those with Omega = 3/2 and 5/2, particularly high-j intruder states) that control the staggering phenomenon, and explains why identical bands can have different staggering properties, as observed by Haslip (1999). The results also connect to the additivity of multipole moments for superdeformed bands established by Satula (1996) and Karlgren (1999).
