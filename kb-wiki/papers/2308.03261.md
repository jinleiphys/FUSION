# Bottom-charmed baryons in a nonrelativistic quark model

> In this work, we study the low-lying mass spectra for bottom-charmed baryons in a nonrelativistic quark model by solving the three-body Schrödinger equation. The lowest $Ξ_{bc}$, $Ξ_{bc}^\prime$, $Ω_{bc}$, and $Ω_{bc}^\prime$ states are predicted to be about 6979, 6953, 7109, and 7092 MeV, respectively. Also, the strong decays for the low-lying excited states are investigated. Our results indicate that some of $λ-$mode $P-$wave bottom-charmed baryons are relatively narrow, which can be searched for in future experiments. For the low-lying $ρ-$mode and $ρ-λ$ hybrid states, their strong decays are highly suppressed and they can survive as extremely narrow states. Moreover, the mass spectra and strong decays for bottom-charmed baryons preserve the heavy quark symmetry well. We hope our calculations can provide helpful information for further experimental and theoretical researches.

## Key claim

The authors predict the masses of the lowest-lying bottom-charmed baryon ground states using a nonrelativistic quark model, but these predictions depend on adjusting an overall zero-point-energy parameter by fixing the mass of the known doubly charmed baryon Xi_cc(3621), and the results are presented as approximate values ("about") rather than precise measurements.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation for a system of one bottom quark, one charm quark, and one light quark (up, down, or strange) using a nonrelativistic quark model with a Hamiltonian containing linear confinement, Coulomb, and spin-dependent potentials. The orbital wave functions were expanded in Gaussian basis functions and the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle was applied to obtain mass eigenvalues and eigenfunctions. Strong decay widths for pseudoscalar meson emission were then calculated using the axial-vector coupling between the meson and the light quark, with the decay amplitude evaluated in the helicity basis using effective harmonic oscillator parameters derived from the computed wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The lowest Xi_bc (1S) state is predicted at about 6979 MeV
- The lowest Xi_bc' (1S) state is predicted at about 6953 MeV
- The lowest Omega_bc (1S) state is predicted at about 7109 MeV
- The lowest Omega_bc' (1S) state is predicted at about 7092 MeV
- The Xi_bc'* (1S) state with J^P = 3/2^+ is predicted at 6997 MeV
- The Omega_bc'* (1S) state with J^P = 3/2^+ is predicted at 7125 MeV
- The rho-mode P-wave Xi_bc states range from 7157 to 7193 MeV
- The rho-mode P-wave Omega_bc states range from 7297 to 7323 MeV
- The lambda-mode P-wave Xi_bc states are at 7391 and 7403 MeV
- The lambda-mode P-wave Omega_bc states are at 7451 and 7458 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model approach previously applied to heavy baryons with two identical quarks by Yoshida (2015) to the case of three non-identical quarks (bottom, charm, and light). The predictions for ground state masses are consistent with earlier potential model calculations by Kiselev (2001) and Ebert (2002), while the systematic study of strong decays for excited states goes beyond previous work by Eakins (2012). The results provide specific mass and width predictions that can guide experimental searches at LHCb and other facilities for the as-yet-unobserved bottom-charmed baryons.
