# Probing Pair Correlations in QCD Matter with Photon Spectra

> Correlations in the phase-space distribution of partons play an important role in the initial stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the matter is dense and far from equilibrium. Photons produced in the hot medium, which predominantly originate from two-parton initial states, are sensitive to two-particle correlations in the phase-space distribution. In this work, we study how pair correlations in non-equilibrium QCD matter affect in-medium photon production. We decompose the two-particle distribution as $\mathcal F_{ab}=f_a f_b+g_{ab}$, where $g_{ab}$ is the pair correlation. Focusing on the $2\to2$ quark--antiquark annihilation and Compton channels, we compute the leading-logarithmic photon spectrum by expanding the single-particle distribution and pair correlation in a spectral basis, thereby accommodating a broad class of two-particle distributions. For a rotationally invariant medium, we find that relative-angle modes of the pair correlation generate sign-changing modifications to the photon spectrum, with magnitudes that can be comparable to the factorized contribution. Thus, photon spectra, although single-particle observables, can measure the momentum correlations of the emitting medium and therefore probe the early-time hydrodynamization.

## Key claim

The paper shows that genuine two-particle correlations in non-equilibrium QCD matter can produce sign-changing modifications to the photon spectrum, with magnitudes comparable to the factorized (uncorrelated) contribution, but only for a rotationally invariant medium and at leading-logarithmic order in the \(2\to2\) quark–antiquark annihilation and Compton channels, not the complete leading-order photon emission rate.

## Method

The authors decompose the two-particle phase-space distribution as \(\mathcal F_{ab}=f_a f_b+g_{ab}\), where \(g_{ab}\) is the connected pair correlation, and expand both the single-particle distribution and the pair correlation in a spectral basis (generalized Laguerre polynomials for radial momenta and Legendre polynomials for the relative angle). They then compute the leading-logarithmic photon spectrum from the \(2\to2\) quark–antiquark annihilation and Compton channels by evaluating basis-response functions that convert the expansion coefficients into the photon production rate, neglecting genuine three-particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- The flavor-summed initial-state averaged squared matrix element for the annihilation channel is \(\overline{|\mathcal{M}_{\rm ann}|^2} = \frac{N_c^2-1}{N_c^2} e^2 g_s^2 \left( \sum_{f=u,d,s} Q_f^2 \right) \left( \frac{u}{t} + \frac{t}{u} \right)\), with \(\sum_{f=u,d,s} Q_f^2 = 2/3\) for \(N_c=3\).
- The kernel for the annihilation channel is \(\mathcal K_{\rm ann} = 4(N_c^2-1) e^2 g_s^2 \left( \sum_{f=u,d,s} Q_f^2 \right) \left( \frac{u}{t} + \frac{t}{u} \right)\).
- The kernel for the quark Compton channel is \(\mathcal K_{\rm Comp}^{q} = 4(N_c^2-1) e^2 g_s^2 \left( \sum_{f=u,d,s} Q_f^2 \right) \left( -\frac{s}{u} - \frac{u}{s} \right)\).
- The color factor for the annihilation process is \((N_c^2-1)/N_c^2 = 8/9\) for QCD with \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends the spectral BBGKY framework of Lu (2025) to photon production, providing a systematic method to incorporate pair correlations beyond the factorized rates commonly used in photon phenomenology. It addresses the direct-photon puzzle, the difficulty of simultaneously describing the direct-photon yield and elliptic flow, by suggesting that early-time pair correlations may modify the emission rate. The results enable future studies that incorporate these correlation effects into space-time evolution models to test whether they can resolve the tension between yield and flow measurements.
