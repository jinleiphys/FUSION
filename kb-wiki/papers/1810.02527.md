# Cross sections of $α$-induced reactions slightly below doubly-magic $^{40}$Ca from the statistical model

> New experimental data for the $^{38}$Ar($α$,n)$^{41}$Ca and $^{38}$Ar($α$,p)$^{41}$K reactions were used to find a best-fit set of parameters for statistical model calculations. The very good agreement between the experimental data and the best-fit calculation confirms the applicability of the statistical model for nuclei in the vicinity of the doubly-magic $^{40}$Ca despite of their relatively low level densities. The present study investigates the sensitivities and finds that the $α$-nucleus and the nucleon-nucleus potentials are the most important ingredients for the calculation of ($α$,n) and ($α$,p) reactions in the statistical model. Furthermore, the width fluctuation correction plays an essential role in the peculiar case of $^{38}$Ar. The best-fit parameters from $^{38}$Ar are applied to the mirror nucleus $^{38}$Ca and the neighboring $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ar nuclei. For $^{38}$Ca this results in an astrophysical reaction rate of the $^{38}$Ca($α$,p)$^{41}$Sc reaction which has a flatter temperature dependence compared to all previous calculations. For $^{40}$Ar a better reproduction of $^{40}$Ar($α$,p)$^{43}$K data from literature is obtained. The disagreement between calculation and an early experimental data point for the $^{36}$Ar($α$,p)$^{39}$K reaction persists.

## Key claim

The statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) can successfully describe alpha-induced reaction cross sections on 38Ar near doubly-magic 40Ca, provided that width fluctuation corrections are included (which are usually negligible for such reactions) and that the alpha-nucleus optical model potential by McFadden and Satchler (1966) is used, despite the relatively low level densities in this mass region.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared search using the TALYS 1.80 code to find the best combination of input parameters (alpha-nucleus optical model potential, nucleon-nucleus optical model potential, level density, gamma-ray strength function, and width fluctuation correction method) that reproduces new experimental cross section data for the 38Ar(alpha,n)41Ca and 38Ar(alpha,p)41K reactions. The best-fit parameters were then applied to predict cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates for the mirror nucleus 38Ca and the neighboring isotopes 36Ar and 40Ar.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit calculation reproduces the 38Ar(alpha,n)41Ca and 38Ar(alpha,p)41K data with a chi-squared per data point of about 4.8 and an average deviation of about 14%.
- The Q-values for the 38Ar(alpha,n)41Ca and 38Ar(alpha,p)41K reactions are Q_n = -5.22 MeV and Q_p = -4.02 MeV, respectively.
- For 38Ca, the Q-values are Q_n = -12.01 MeV and Q_p = +1.72 MeV; the 38Ca(alpha,p)41Sc reaction rate at T9 = 1.0 is 3.87 x 10^{-10} cm^3 s^{-1} mole^{-1}, with a Gamow window energy of 2191 keV.
- The astrophysical reaction rate for the 38Ca(alpha,p)41Sc reaction has a flatter temperature dependence compared to all previous calculations.
- For 40Ar, a better reproduction of 40Ar(alpha,p)43K data from literature is obtained, while disagreement persists for the 36Ar(alpha,p)39K reaction.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of alpha-induced reactions in the A approximately 20-50 mass range by Mohr (2015), which found that the simple McFadden and Satchler (1966) alpha-nucleus optical model potential works well for most nuclei in this range. The present paper addresses the special case of nuclei near doubly-magic 40Ca where both the (alpha,n) and (alpha,p) channels have negative Q-values, making width fluctuation corrections essential. The results enable improved predictions for the 38Ca(alpha,p)41Sc reaction rate relevant to the alpha p-process in X-ray bursts, superseding earlier estimates from studies such as those by Kahl (2018), Long (2017, 2018), and Schmidt (2017).
