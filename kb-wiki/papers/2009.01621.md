# Local well-posedness in Sobolev spaces for first-order barotropic causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics

> We study the theory of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics introduced in arXiv:1109.0985 and arXiv:1907.12695, which provided a causal and stable first-order theory of relativistic fluids with viscosity in the case of barotropic fluids. The local well-posedness of its equations of motion has been previously established in Gevrey spaces. Here, we improve this result by proving local well-posedness in Sobolev spaces.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the equations of motion for a first-order causal and stable relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (for barotropic fluids) are locally well-posed in Sobolev spaces, improving upon a previous result that only established well-posedness in the more restrictive Gevrey spaces. This improvement holds only for the case of Minkowski background, not when the equations are coupled to Einstein's equations.

## Method

The authors decompose the energy-momentum conservation equations into components parallel and orthogonal to the fluid four-velocity, then construct new variables from combinations of the energy density, velocity, and their derivatives. They diagonalize the principal symbol of the resulting system using pseudodifferential operators, obtain energy estimates for a linearized version, and set up a convergent iteration scheme; to connect this to the original equations, they derive an auxiliary system solved in Gevrey functions and use a density argument to obtain Sobolev-space solutions.

## Key numbers

- The Sobolev regularity requirement is \(r > 9/2\) for the initial data, with \(\varepsilon_{(0)} \in H^r(\mathbb{T}^3,\mathbb{R})\), \(\varepsilon_{(1)} \in H^{r-1}(\mathbb{T}^3,\mathbb{R})\), \(u_{(0)} \in H^r(\mathbb{T}^3,\mathbb{R}^3)\), and \(u_{(1)} \in H^{r-1}(\mathbb{T}^3,\mathbb{R}^3)\).
- The solution exists on a time interval \([0,T]\) with regularity \(\varepsilon \in C^0([0,T],H^r) \cap C^1([0,T],H^{r-1}) \cap C^2([0,T],H^{r-2})\) and similarly for the velocity field \(u\).
- The transport coefficients must satisfy \(\uplambda,\upchi_1,\upeta > 0\), \(P > 0\), \(c_s^2 := P^\prime > 0\), and a set of inequalities including \(\uplambda \ge \upeta\) and \(3\upchi_4 > 4\upeta\).

## Context

This work extends the local well-posedness result from Gevrey spaces (established in Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha (2019) for the same barotropic first-order theory) to Sobolev spaces, which are more standard for PDE analysis and numerical approximation. The theory itself was introduced simultaneously in Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha (2019) and Kovtun (2019) using effective field theory arguments, providing a causal and stable alternative to the Muller-Israel-Stewart theory; the present result enables further mathematical study of this first-order approach, though coupling to Einstein's equations remains an open problem.
