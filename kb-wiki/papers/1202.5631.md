# Periodic-orbit approach to the nuclear shell structures with power-law potential models: Bridge orbits and prolate-oblate asymmetry

> Deformed shell structures in nuclear mean-field potentials are systematically investigated as functions of deformation and surface diffuseness. As the mean-field model to investigate nuclear shell structures in a wide range of mass numbers, we propose the radial power-law potential model, V \propto r^α, which enables a simple semiclassical analysis by the use of its scaling property. We find that remarkable shell structures emerge at certain combinations of deformation and diffuseness parameters, and they are closely related to the periodic-orbit bifurcations. In particular, significant roles of the "bridge orbit bifurcations" for normal and superdeformed shell structures are pointed out. It is shown that the prolate-oblate asymmetry in deformed shell structures is clearly understood from the contribution of the bridge orbit to the semiclassical level density. The roles of bridge orbit bifurcations in the emergence of superdeformed shell structures are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the prolate-oblate asymmetry in deformed nuclear shell structures, which underlies the observed prolate-shape dominance in medium-mass to heavy nuclei, can be clearly understood from the contribution of "bridge orbit bifurcations" to the semiclassical level density, rather than solely from the asymmetric level fanning unique to sharp-surface potentials. The claim is hedged by noting that spin-orbit coupling also plays an important role and will be discussed in a forthcoming paper.

## Method

The author systematically investigates deformed shell structures as functions of deformation and surface diffuseness using a radial power-law potential model, \(V \propto r^\alpha\), which interpolates between the harmonic oscillator (\(\alpha=2\)) and infinite square-well (cavity, \(\alpha=\infty\)) limits. Quantum single-particle spectra are computed for spheroidal deformations, and the fluctuating part of the level density is analyzed via Fourier transformation to extract the contributions of classical periodic orbits. The stability of periodic orbits is studied through the monodromy matrix, and bifurcation points (where \(\Tr\BM=2\)) are identified as the loci of enhanced shell effects, with special attention to "bridge orbit bifurcations" that connect equatorial and three-dimensional orbits.

## Key numbers

- The radial parameter \(\alpha\) for nuclei with mass number \(A=20\) is 2.80; for \(A=100\), \(\alpha=5.23\); for \(A=200\), \(\alpha=6.75\) (Table I, using \(R_A=1.3A^{1/3}\) fm, \(W=50\) MeV, \(a=0.7\) fm).
- The relation between \(\alpha\) and nuclear radius/diffuseness is \(\alpha \sim -0.62 + 0.68\,R_A/a\) (Eq. 13).
- For the spherical power-law potential, the scaled period of the circular orbit is \(\tau_{\rm C}=2\pi\sqrt{\alpha}\left(\frac{2}{2+\alpha}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{\alpha}}\).
- Bifurcation values of \(\alpha\) for period-\(m\)upling from the circular orbit are given by \(\alpha = n^2/m^2 - 2\); for \(m=1\) these include \(\alpha=2,7,14,\dots\).
- The average level density in the Thomas-Fermi approximation is \(\bar{g}(\cE)=\frac{2\sqrt{2}}{\pi} \rB\left(1+\frac{3}{\alpha},\frac32\right)\cE^2\), independent of deformation under volume conservation.
- The smoothing width used for coarse-grained quantum level density is \(\varGamma=0.3\) (in scaled energy units).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical periodic-orbit approach of Strutinsky et al. (StrMag) and Frisk (Frisk) for the cavity model to a power-law potential that bridges the harmonic oscillator and cavity limits, enabling a systematic study of shell structure from light to heavy nuclei. It directly addresses the prolate-shape dominance problem discussed by Hamamoto and Mottelson (HamMot) and by Tajima et al. (Tajima96, Tajima2001), who found that prolate-shape dominance in microscopic calculations requires strong correlation between \(l^2\) and \(ls\) terms. The paper enables future inclusion of spin-orbit coupling (promised in a forthcoming paper) and provides a framework for understanding superdeformed shell structures at 2:1 axis ratio via bridge orbit bifurcations, complementing earlier work on equatorial orbit bifurcations in the cavity model (ASM, Magner2).
