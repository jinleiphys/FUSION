# Neutron Star Equation of State with Nucleon Short-Range Correlations: A Concise Review and Open Issues

> Nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs) and the associated high-momentum tail (HMT) in its momentum distribution $n(k)$ represent a universal feature of strongly interacting Fermi systems. In nuclear matter, SRCs arise primarily from the spin-isospin dependence of the tensor and short-range components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, leading to a substantial depletion of its Fermi sea and a characteristic $k^{-4}$ tail populated predominantly by isosinglet neutron-proton pairs. These microscopic structures modify both the kinetic and interaction contributions to the Equation of State (EOS) of dense matter and thereby influence a broad range of neutron-star (NS) properties. This short review provides a streamlined overview of how SRC-induced changes in $n(k)$ reshape the kinetic EOS, including its symmetry energy part and how these effects propagate into macroscopic NS observables, including mass-radius relations, tidal deformabilities, direct Urca thresholds and core-crust transition. We summarize key existing results, highlight current observational constraints relevant for testing SRC-HMT effects, and outline open questions for future theoretical, experimental, and multimessenger studies of dense nucleonic matter.

## Key claim
Nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs) and the associated high-momentum tail (HMT) in the nucleon momentum distribution are not subleading corrections but structural components of a realistic equation of state (EOS) for neutron-star matter, though the review acknowledges that quantitative predictions depend on the specific parametrization of SRC parameters and that many open questions remain.

## Method
The authors review how SRC-induced modifications to the single-nucleon momentum distribution n(k), parametrized as a depleted Fermi sea plus a universal k^{-4} tail, alter the kinetic contribution to the EOS of asymmetric nuclear matter, including the symmetry energy. They then summarize how these changes propagate to neutron-star observables such as mass-radius relations, tidal deformabilities, direct Urca thresholds, and core-crust transitions, drawing on results from Gogny-type momentum-dependent potentials and relativistic mean-field models.

## Key numbers
- The high-momentum tail fraction in symmetric nuclear matter is x_{SNM}^{HMT} ≈ 28% ± 4%, while in pure neutron matter it is x_{PNM}^{HMT} ≈ 1.5% ± 0.5%.
- The kinetic EOS of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is E_0^{kin}(ρ_0) ≈ 39.77 ± 8.13 MeV.
- The kinetic symmetry energy at saturation density is E_sym^{kin}(ρ_0) ≈ -14.28 ± 11.59 MeV (non-relativistic) or ≈ -12.01 ± 8.23 MeV (with relativistic corrections).
- The isospin-quartic kinetic term at saturation density is E_sym,4^{kin}(ρ_0) ≈ 7.18 ± 2.52 MeV.
- The extrapolated a_2 factor for infinite nuclear matter is a_2(∞) ≈ 7 ± 1.
- In 12C, about 20% of nucleons participate in SRCs, with 90% ± 10% of SRC pairs being neutron-proton pairs.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on SRC effects in nuclear matter pioneered by Hen (2014), the universal contact formalism of Tan (2008), and the parametrization of Cai (2015). It synthesizes results from microscopic many-body calculations (SCGF, BHF, FHNC, QMC) and phenomenological models, connecting them to multimessenger neutron-star constraints from GW170817, NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, and other pulsar mass measurements. The review enables future studies to incorporate SRC-HMT effects into EOS models for interpreting gravitational-wave events and X-ray observations of neutron stars.
