# The stability of the spectator, Dirac, and Salpeter equations for mesons

> Mesons are made of quark-antiquark pairs held together by the strong force. The one channel spectator, Dirac, and Salpeter equations can each be used to model this pairing. We look at cases where the relativistic kernel of these equations corresponds to a time-like vector exchange, a scalar exchange, or a linear combination of the two. Since the model used in this paper describes mesons which cannot decay physically, the equations must describe stable states. We find that this requirement is not always satisfied, and give a complete discussion of the conditions under which the various equations give unphysical, unstable solutions.

## Key claim

The one-channel spectator, Dirac, and Salpeter equations for mesons with confining potentials of scalar and time-like vector exchange do not always produce stable, physical solutions; the stability depends on the specific combination of Lorentz structure and equation type, and the authors provide a complete classification of when unphysical instabilities arise.

## Method

The authors study the stability of the Dirac, Salpeter, and one-channel spectator (1CS) equations for quark-antiquark bound states using a linear confining potential with scalar and time-like vector exchange kernels, both separately and in linear combinations. They analyze the equations analytically using a peaking approximation and solve them numerically with spline functions in momentum space, applying four stability criteria: real eigenvalues, numerical stability under changes in spline rank, separation of unstable negative-energy states from positive-energy states, and correct coordinate-space structure of wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For the Dirac equation with a pure scalar confining potential (y=0), the positive energy expectation value is ⟨U⟩₊ = m + σr and the negative energy expectation value is ⟨U⟩₋ = -m - σr, producing confinement for both positive and negative energy states.
- For the Dirac equation with a pure vector confining potential (y=1), the positive energy expectation value is ⟨U⟩₊ = m + σr and the negative energy expectation value is ⟨U⟩₋ = -m + σr, leaving negative energy states unconfined and producing the Klein paradox instability.
- The subtraction term in the relativistic potential includes a finite part C(p²) = (2σ/πm₁){m₁/E_{p₁} + m₁³/(p E_{p₁}²) log((E_{p₁}+p)/m₁)} that vanishes in the m₁→∞ limit.

## Context

This work extends and reconciles contradictory prior findings on the stability of covariant confinement models: Parramore and Piekarewicz found the Salpeter equation stable when vector strength exceeded scalar strength, while Munz and collaborators found the Salpeter equation stable for pure scalar confinement with sufficiently large quark mass. The paper also addresses the Klein paradox in the Dirac equation (Bjorken and Drell) and the conflicting claims about whether the confining potential in QCD is scalar (J. F. Lagae) or vector (D. Gromes). The results enable systematic identification of which relativistic equations and kernel choices produce phenomenologically acceptable meson models.
