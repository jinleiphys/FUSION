# Isobaric incompressibility of the isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> The isospin dependence of the saturation properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, particularly the incompressibility $K_\infty (X) = K_\infty + K_τX^2 + O(X^4)$ at saturation density is systematically studied using density dependent M3Y interaction. The $K_τ$ characterizes the isospin dependence of the incompressibility at saturation density $ρ_0$. The approximate expression $K_{asy} \approx K_{sym}-6L$ is often used for $K_τ$ where $L$ and $K_{sym}$ represent, respectively, the slope and curvature parameters of the symmetry energy at $ρ_0$. It can be expressed accurately as $K_τ=K_{sym}-6L-\frac{Q_0}{K_\infty}L$ where $Q_0$ is the third-order derivative parameter of symmetric nuclear matter at $ρ_0$. The results of this addendum to Phys. Rev. C 80, 011305(R) (2009) indicate that the $Q_0$ contribution to $K_τ$ is not insignificant.

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent part of the nuclear incompressibility, \(K_\tau\), is accurately given by \(K_\tau = K_{sym} - 6L - \frac{Q_0}{K_\infty}L\) rather than the commonly used approximation \(K_{asy} \approx K_{sym} - 6L\), and the contribution from the third-order derivative parameter \(Q_0\) is not insignificant, leading to differences of about ten percent or more.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter within a Fermi gas model. The saturation density and energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter were determined by minimizing the energy per nucleon, and the third-order density derivative parameter \(Q_0\) was computed analytically from the energy density functional. The exact \(K_\tau\) was then compared with the approximate \(K_{asy}\) and with predictions from relativistic mean field (RMF) models including FSUGold, NL3, and Hybrid.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.1533\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Saturation energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter: \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26 \pm 0.52\ \text{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K_\infty = 274.7 \pm 7.4\ \text{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 30.71 \pm 0.26\ \text{MeV}\)
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: \(L = 45.11 \pm 0.02\ \text{MeV}\)
- Curvature parameter of symmetry energy: \(K_{sym} = -183.7 \pm 3.6\ \text{MeV}\)
- Approximate isospin-dependent incompressibility: \(K_{asy} = -454.4 \pm 3.5\ \text{MeV}\)
- Third-order derivative parameter: \(Q_0 = -276.5 \pm 10.5\ \text{MeV}\)
- Exact isospin-dependent incompressibility: \(K_\tau = -408.97 \pm 3.01\ \text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation using the DDM3Y interaction (Basu, Roy Chowdhury, and Samanta, 2008; Chowdhury, Basu, and Samanta, 2009) by including the \(Q_0\) correction to the isospin-dependent incompressibility. The results are compared with RMF models such as FSUGold, NL3, and Hybrid (Piekarewicz, 2009) and with Skyrme interactions. The derived \(K_\tau\) value of \(-408.97\ \text{MeV}\) is in reasonably close agreement with the experimental constraint \(K_\tau = -550 \pm 100\ \text{MeV}\) from giant monopole resonance data on Sn isotopes (Li et al., 2007; Garg et al., 2007) and in excellent agreement with values extracted from reproducing GMR energies of \({}^{208}\text{Pb}\), Sn isotopes, and \({}^{90}\text{Zr}\) (Sharma, 2009). The work enables more accurate modeling of neutron star properties and heavy-ion reaction dynamics by refining the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.
