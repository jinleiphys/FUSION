# Jet broadening in deeply inelastic scattering

> In deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering (DIS), the average jet transverse momentum is broadened because of the multiple scattering in the nuclear medium. The size of jet broadening is proportional to the multi-parton correlation functions inside nuclei. We show that at leading order, jet broadening in DIS and nulcear enhancement in di-jet momentum imbalance and Drell-Yan $< q_T^2 > $ share the same four-parton correlation functions. We argue that jet broadening in DIS provide an indenpendent measurement of the four-parton correlation functions and a test of QCD treatment of multiple scattering.

## Key claim

Jet broadening in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering (DIS) is directly proportional to the same four-parton (twist-four) correlation functions that govern nuclear enhancement in di-jet momentum imbalance and Drell-Yan transverse momentum, but only at leading order; the paper argues that this provides an independent measurement of these correlation functions and a test of QCD treatment of multiple scattering, though higher-order corrections may modify the leading-order predictions.

## Method

The author derived the nuclear enhancement of the Drell-Yan average transverse momentum squared using the same generalized factorization theorem and collinear expansion method previously used by Luo, Qiu and Sterman (LQS) for di-jet momentum imbalance. The same technique was then applied to derive the jet broadening in DIS, calculating the double-scattering contribution to the hadronic tensor from Feynman diagrams with two gluon exchanges and extracting the leading contribution proportional to the four-parton correlation function \(T_{q/A}(x_B)\). The resulting expressions for both observables were compared using the LQS model \(T_{f/A}(x)=\lambda^2 A^{4/3} \phi_{f/N}(x)\) to make numerical predictions.

## Key numbers

- \(\lambda^2_{\text{di-jet}} \approx 0.05\sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\): the size of the four-parton correlation functions estimated by LQS from Fermilab E683 di-jet momentum imbalance data.
- \(\lambda^2_{\text{DY}} \approx 0.01\) GeV\(^2\): the size of the four-parton correlation functions estimated from Fermilab E772 and CERN NA10 data on nuclear enhancement of Drell-Yan \(\langle q_T^2 \rangle\), which is at least a factor of five smaller than the di-jet value.
- \(\Delta \langle l_T^2 \rangle \approx (0.66 \sim 1.31) \, \alpha_s \, A^{1/3}\): predicted jet broadening in DIS using the di-jet value of \(\lambda^2\).
- \(\Delta \langle l_T^2 \rangle \approx 0.13 \, \alpha_s \, A^{1/3}\): predicted jet broadening in DIS using the Drell-Yan value of \(\lambda^2\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized factorization theorem of Qiu and Sterman (1991) for multiple scattering in QCD, and the calculation of di-jet momentum imbalance by Luo, Qiu and Sterman (LQS) in photon-nucleus collisions. It addresses a discrepancy between the size of four-parton correlation functions extracted from di-jet data (LQS, using Fermilab E683) and from Drell-Yan data (using Fermilab E772 and CERN NA10), which differ by a factor of five. The paper enables experimental tests of QCD multiple-scattering dynamics by predicting jet broadening in DIS as an independent observable, using existing Fermilab E665 data and future HERA nuclear beam data to distinguish between the two extracted values.
