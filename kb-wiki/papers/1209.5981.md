# Deformation effects on the Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the double-beta decay partners 76Ge and 76Se

> A theoretical approach based on a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation built on a Skyrme selfconsistent mean field is used to describe the recent measurements of the Gamow-Teller GT- strength distribution extracted from the charge-exchange reaction 76Ge(3He,t)76As with high energy resolution. The same analysis is made to describe the Gamow-Teller GT+ strength distribution measured in the 76Se(d,2He)76As reaction. Combining these two branches, the nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino double-beta decay process is evaluated and compared to experiment. The role of the nuclear deformation on those processes is emphasized and analyzed.

## Key claim

The deformed QRPA approach reproduces the gross features of the Gamow-Teller strength distributions in both single-beta branches and the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element, but it fails to account for a detailed description of the high resolution data at low excitation energy, with the agreement being optimum only for the most reasonable choices of deformations and strengths of the residual interaction.

## Method

The author performed deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme SLy4 effective interaction assuming axial deformation and time reversal symmetry, including BCS pairing correlations with fixed pairing gap parameters from experimental binding energies. A deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with separable spin-isospin residual interactions in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels was then solved on the deformed ground state basis for 76Ge and 76Se to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions and compute the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Experimental half-life for 2nu-beta-beta decay in 76Ge: T_{1/2}^{2nu-beta-beta} = (1.5 +/- 0.1) x 10^21 yr (recommended value from Barabash (2010))
- Experimental nuclear matrix element M_{GT}^{2nu-beta-beta} = 0.129 MeV^{-1} using bare g_A = 1.269, and 0.216 MeV^{-1} using quenched g_A = 1
- Phase-space factor G^{2nu-beta-beta}(76Ge) = 1.49 x 10^{-20} yr^{-1} MeV^2
- QRPA calculation with beta=0.15 for 76Ge gives accumulated B(GT^-) strength: 4.0 (0-5 MeV), 5.5 (0-7 MeV), 2.2 (7-10 MeV), 10.6 (10-20 MeV), 18.3 (0-20 MeV)
- QRPA calculation with beta=0.2 for 76Se gives accumulated B(GT^+) strength: 0.15 (0-2 MeV), 0.87 (0-5 MeV), 0.53 (5-20 MeV), 1.4 (0-20 MeV)
- Experimental charge root mean square radii: 76Ge = 4.080/4.127 fm, 76Se = 4.088/4.162 fm; calculated with SLy4: 76Ge = 4.104 fm, 76Se = 4.151 fm
- Calculated quadrupole deformations: 76Ge beta = 0.14, 76Se beta = 0.17 (SLy4); experimental ranges: 76Ge beta = 0.10/0.26, 76Se beta = 0.16/0.31

## Context

This work extends previous deformed QRPA studies by Sarriguren and collaborators to describe the new high resolution charge-exchange data from Thies (2012) for 76Ge(3He,t)76As and Grewe (2008) for 76Se(d,2He)76As, which revealed unusually strong fragmentation of GT strength and lack of correlation between the two branches. The results confirm earlier findings by Simkovic (2004) and Alvarez (2004) that nuclear matrix elements for the 2nu-beta-beta process are suppressed relative to the spherical case with a reduction factor scaling roughly with the deformation difference between parent and daughter, and show that the combination of deformations beta(76Ge)=0.15 and beta(76Se)=0.2 produces optimal results reproducing the experimental half-life.
