# Spin-orbit splitting of 9 Lambda Be excited states studied with the SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions

> The previous Faddeev calculation of the two-alpha plus Lambda system for 9 Lambda Be is extended to incorporate the spin-orbit components of the SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions. We employ the Born kernel of the quark-model Lambda N LS interaction, and generate the spin-orbit component of the Lambda alpha potential by the alpha-cluster folding. The Faddeev calculation in the jj-coupling scheme implies that the direct use of the quark-model Born kernel for the Lambda N LS component is not good enough to reproduce the small experimental value Delta E^exp_{ls}=43 +- 5 keV for the 5/2^+ - 3/2^+ splitting. This procedure predicts three to five times larger values in the model FSS and fss2. The spin-orbit contribution from the effective meson-exchange potentials in fss2 is argued to be unfavorable to the small ls splitting, through the analysis of the Scheerbaum factors for the single-particle spin-orbit potentials calculated in the G-matrix formalism.

## Key claim

The direct use of the quark-model Born kernel for the Lambda-N LS interaction in Faddeev calculations of 9LambdaBe predicts spin-orbit splittings three to five times larger than the experimental value of 43 +/- 5 keV, indicating that the simple Born approximation is insufficient and that P-wave Lambda N - Sigma N coupling effects must be incorporated.

## Method

The authors extended previous Faddeev calculations of the two-alpha plus Lambda system for 9LambdaBe by incorporating spin-orbit components from SU_6 quark-model baryon-baryon interactions (models FSS and fss2). They generated the Lambda-alpha spin-orbit potential by folding the Born kernel of the quark-model Lambda N LS interaction with the alpha-cluster wavefunction, then solved the Faddeev equations in the jj-coupling scheme using central plus spin-orbit Lambda-alpha interactions. The Scheerbaum factors for single-particle spin-orbit potentials were also calculated using G-matrix formalism in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Experimental spin-orbit splitting of 5/2+ and 3/2+ excited states in 9LambdaBe: Delta E_ls = 43 +/- 5 keV
- FSS model prediction for Delta E_ls: 137 keV (using SB force with u=0.82)
- fss2 model prediction for Delta E_ls: 198 keV (using SB force with u=0.82)
- FSS ground-state energy: -6.623 MeV (1/2+ state)
- fss2 ground-state energy: -6.623 MeV (1/2+ state)
- Scheerbaum factor S_Lambda for FSS in G-matrix calculation: -1.93 MeV*fm^5 (full calculation with k_F=1.07 fm^-1)
- Scheerbaum factor S_Lambda for fss2 in G-matrix calculation: -11.26 MeV*fm^5 (full calculation with k_F=1.07 fm^-1)
- Born approximation S_Lambda for FSS: -7.8 MeV*fm^5
- When EMEP contribution switched off in fss2: Delta E_ls = 86 keV

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of the alpha-alpha-Lambda system by Fujiwara et al. (2004) that used only central forces, and directly tests the quark-model spin-orbit interactions against the small experimental splitting measured by Hyperball gamma-ray spectroscopy (Akikawa et al., 2002; Tamura et al., 2003). The results contradict the simpler approach of Hiyama et al. (2000), who adjusted LS potential strengths to match experiment, by showing that the quark-model Born kernel alone gives splittings 3-5 times too large. The analysis demonstrates that P-wave Lambda N - Sigma N coupling, which significantly reduces the Scheerbaum factor in G-matrix calculations for model FSS, must be incorporated into cluster-model calculations to reproduce the observed small spin-orbit splitting.
