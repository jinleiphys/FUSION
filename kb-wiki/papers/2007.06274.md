# Total and differential cross sections of the $\boldsymbol{dp\to {}^3}\textrm{He}\,\boldsymbolη$ reaction at excess energies between 1 and 15 MeV

> New high precision total and differential cross sections are reported for the $dp\to {}^3\textrm{He}\,η$ reaction close to threshold. The measurements were performed using the magnetic spectrometer ANKE, which is an internal fixed target facility at the COSY cooler synchrotron. The data were taken for deuteron beam momenta between $3.14641~\textrm{GeV}/c$ and $3.20416~\textrm{GeV}/c$, which corresponds to the range in excess energy $Q$ for this reaction between $1.14~\textrm{MeV}$ and $15.01~\textrm{MeV}$. The normalization was established through the measurement in parallel of deuteron-proton elastic scattering and this was checked through the study of the $dp\to {}^3\textrm{He}\,π^0$ reaction. The previously indicated possible change of sign of the slope of the differential cross sections near the production threshold, which could be explained by a rapid variation of the $s$- and $p$-wave interference term, is not confirmed by the new data. The energy dependence of the total cross section and the $90^{\circ}$ slope parameter are well explained by describing the final state interaction in terms of a complex Jost function and the results are significant in the discussion of $η$-mesic nuclei. In combination with recently published WASA-at-COSY data [P. Adlarson $et\, al.$, Phys. Lett. B 782, 297 (2018)], a smooth variation of the slope parameter is achieved up to an excess energy of $80.9~\textrm{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The previously indicated possible change of sign of the slope of the differential cross sections near the production threshold, which could be explained by a rapid variation of the s- and p-wave interference term, is not confirmed by the new high-precision data.

## Method

The dp → ³He η reaction was measured using the magnetic spectrometer ANKE, an internal fixed target facility at the COSY cooler synchrotron, with deuteron beam momenta between 3.14641 GeV/c and 3.20416 GeV/c. The normalization was established through simultaneous measurement of deuteron-proton elastic scattering and checked via the dp → ³He π⁰ reaction. The ³He ejectiles were detected in the forward detector system, and η production was inferred from the missing mass; background was subtracted using sub-threshold data at Q = -5.15 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section reaches a plateau at about σ = 400 nb for excess energies above about 1 MeV.
* Data were taken for excess energies Q between 1.14 MeV and 15.01 MeV.
* Deuteron beam momenta ranged from 3.14641 GeV/c to 3.20416 GeV/c.
* The luminosity was determined with a systematic precision of ΔL_sys ≈ 6% and statistical precision ΔL_stat ≈ 1%, an improvement by at least a factor of two compared to previous ANKE measurements.
* The dp elastic scattering differential cross sections used for normalization are on the order of 10⁴ μb/(GeV/c)² over the ANKE acceptance range between 0.08 (GeV/c)² ≤ |t| < 0.26 (GeV/c)².
* The geometrical acceptance factor is 90% or higher for most energies, except for the 15.01 MeV data where acceptance factors lie between 50% and 80%.
* The excess energy uncertainty is ΔQ_stat = 0.01 MeV (statistical) and ΔQ_sys = 0.03 MeV (systematic) for each flattop.

## Context

This work extends the measurements of the dp → ³He η reaction near threshold first studied at Saclay (Berger, 1988) and later refined at ANKE (Mersmann, 2007) and COSY-11 (Smyrski, 2007). The new high-precision data do not confirm the possible sign change of the slope parameter near threshold that had been suggested by earlier experiments. In combination with WASA-at-COSY data (Adlarson, 2018), a smooth variation of the slope parameter is achieved up to an excess energy of 80.9 MeV. The results are significant for the discussion of η-mesic nuclei, as the real and imaginary parts of the pole position in the complex Q plane are of great importance for understanding whether a (quasi)-bound state of the η-³He system exists near the production threshold.
