# Heavy-Quark Symmetry Partners of the Pc(4450) Pentaquark

> The spectrum of heavy-hadron molecules is constrained by heavy-quark symmetry in its different manifestations. Heavy-quark spin symmetry for instance connects the properties of the ground and excited states of heavy hadrons, while heavy-antiquark-diquark symmetry connects the properties of heavy antimesons ($\bar{D}$, $\bar{D}^*$) and doubly heavy baryons ($Ξ_{cc}$, $Ξ_{cc}^*$). A prediction of these symmetries is that if the $P_c(4450)$ is indeed a $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ bound state, then there should be a series of $\bar{D}^* Σ_c^*$, $Ξ_{cc} Σ_c$, $Ξ_{cc}^* Σ_c$, $Ξ_{cc} Σ_c^*$ and $Ξ_{cc}^* Σ_c^*$ partners. The concrete application of heavy-quark spin symmetry indicates that, if the $P_c(4450)$ is a $\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* Σ_c$ molecule, the existence of a $\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* Σ_c^*$ partner with similar binding energy --- which we call $P_c(4515)$, given its expected mass --- is likely. Conversely, the application of heavy-antiquark-diquark symmetry indicates that the $0^+$ $Ξ_{cc} Σ_c$, $1^+$ $Ξ_{cc} Σ_c^*$, $2^+$ $Ξ_{cc}^* Σ_c$ and $3^+$ $Ξ_{cc}^* Σ_c^*$ molecules are likely to bind too, with binding energies in the $20-30\,{\rm MeV}$ range.

## Key claim

If the $P_c(4450)$ is a $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ hadronic molecule, heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) predicts a likely $\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ partner with similar binding energy (called $P_c(4515)$), and heavy-antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS) predicts four triply heavy baryon-baryon molecules ($\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c$, $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c^*$, $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c$, $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c^*$) with binding energies in the $20-30\,{\rm MeV}$ range, though these predictions depend on the unknown relative sizes of the two contact couplings $C_a$ and $C_b$ and on the approximate nature of the symmetries.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order contact-range effective field theory for the $\bar{H}_c S_c$ system (heavy antimeson and singly heavy baryon) using non-relativistic superfields for the heavy hadrons, with two unknown couplings $C_a$ and $C_b$. They fix the coupling combination $C_a + \frac{2}{3}C_b$ by requiring that the $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ channel reproduces the $P_c(4450)$ binding energy of $12\,{\rm MeV}$, then apply HQSS to predict the $\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c^*$ partner and HADS (via superfield substitutions) to predict binding energies for the $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c$, $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c^*$, $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c$, and $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c^*$ molecules by solving a non-relativistic bound-state equation with a Gaussian regulator and cutoff variation $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0\,{\rm GeV}$.

## Key numbers

- $P_c(4450)$ binding energy: $B = 12 \pm 3\,{\rm MeV}$ (assumed for the $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^{-}$ $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ molecule)
- Predicted $P_c(4515)$ mass: $4515\,{\rm MeV}$ (from the name, expected binding similar to $P_c(4450)$)
- Predicted binding energies for $0^+$ $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c$ and $2^+$ $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c$ molecules: $B(0^+) \simeq B(2^+) \simeq 19^{+15}_{-13}\,{\rm MeV}$ (for cutoff $\Lambda = 0.5\,{\rm GeV}$) and $29^{+33}_{-24}\,{\rm MeV}$ (for $\Lambda = 1.0\,{\rm GeV}$)
- Predicted binding energy range for $1^+$ $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c^*$ and $3^+$ $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c^*$ molecules: $20-30\,{\rm MeV}$ (cutoff variation only)
- Masses used: $m(D^*) = 2009\,{\rm MeV}$, $m(\Sigma_c) = 2454\,{\rm MeV}$, $m(\Sigma_c^*) = 2518\,{\rm MeV}$, $m(\Xi_{cc}) = 3621\,{\rm MeV}$, $m(\Xi_{cc}^*) = 3727\,{\rm MeV}$
- HADS uncertainty estimated at about $30\%$; HQSS uncertainty in charm sector about $15\%$ ($\Lambda_{\rm QCD} / m_Q$ with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 200-300\,{\rm MeV}$)

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $P_c(4450)$ pentaquark discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2015), which has been modeled as a $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ bound state by Karliner (2015), Roca (2015), Xiao (2015), Burns (2015), and Geng (2017). It applies heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and heavy-antiquark-diquark symmetry (HADS) to predict new molecular partners, following the approach of effective field theories for $H_c \bar{H}_c$ molecules (Valderrama 2012) and $S_c \bar{S}_c$ molecules (Lu 2017). The predictions enable experimental searches for the $P_c(4515)$ pentaquark and triply charmed baryon-baryon molecules, though the authors note that lattice QCD may be a more expedient way to determine the existence of the $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c$, $\Xi_{cc} \Sigma_c^*$, $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c$, and $\Xi_{cc}^* \Sigma_c^*$ molecules due to the difficulty of triple charm production.
