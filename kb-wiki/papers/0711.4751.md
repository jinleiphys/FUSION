# Gamow-Teller strength distributions for double-beta-decaying nuclei within continuum-QRPA

> A version of the pn-continuum-QRPA is outlined and applied to describe the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $ββ$-decaying open-shell nuclei. The calculation results obtained for the pairs of nuclei $^{116}$Cd-Sn and $^{130}$Te-Xe are compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper outlines an isospin-selfconsistent version of the proton-neutron continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-cQRPA) and applies it to describe Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for double-beta-decaying nuclei, but the authors note that only general features of low-energy strength distributions can be described within the approach.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed pn-cQRPA approach by using a modern version of the phenomenological isoscalar mean field (including the spin-orbit term) deduced from isospin-selfconsistent analysis of experimental single-quasiparticle spectra in double-closed-shell nuclei. The pairing problem is solved on a basis of bound plus quasibound single-particle states using the BCS model, while the single-particle continuum is taken into account in the particle-hole channel by transforming the free two-quasiparticle propagator using the Green function of the radial Schrodinger equation. The residual interaction uses zero-range Landau-Migdal forces with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller giant resonance energies and the 2nu beta-beta-decay amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For the pair 116Cd-116Sn: the Landau-Migdal strength parameter g' = 0.77 was used to reproduce the experimental GTR energy; the particle-particle strength ratio g_pp = 1.0 was taken in the calculation
- For the pair 130Te-130Xe: g' = 0.88 was found by fitting the experimental GTR energy in 130I; the amplitude M^(2nu)_GT = 0.03 MeV^(-1) (experimental value from Bar06) was reproduced at g_pp = 0.99
- For 116Sn to 116In: only one 1+ state with B^(+)(GT) = 0.47 was found in the calculation within the interval E_x < 5 MeV, corresponding to the 1g^p_9/2 to 1g^n_7/2 transition
- For 116In: within E_x <= 3 MeV, the calculated GT^(-) strength distribution shows one 1+ state with B^(-)(GT) = 1.05, corresponding to the back-spin-flip transition 1g^n_7/2 to 1g^p_9/2
- The mean field parameters used: U_0 = 51.62 MeV (116Cd-Sn) and 51.74 MeV (130Te-Xe); U_SO = 34.08 MeV.fm^2 (116Cd-Sn) and 34.025 MeV.fm^2 (130Te-Xe); diffuseness a = 0.618 fm (116Cd-Sn) and 0.628 fm (130Te-Xe)

## Context

This work extends the pn-cQRPA approach of Rodin (2003) and Rodin (2007) by using a modern mean field parameterization from Igashov (2006), and applies it to the double-beta-decaying pairs 116Cd-Sn and 130Te-Xe. The approach addresses the marked sensitivity of the 2nu beta-beta-decay amplitude to the particle-particle interaction strength ratio g_pp, which was analyzed by Rodin (2005) in terms of spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry violation. The results enable comparison with experimental GT strength data from charge-exchange reactions such as (3He,t) and (d,2He), and provide a framework for evaluating beta-beta-decay amplitudes with improved reliability.
