# A New State of Baryonium

> The recent discovery of a narrow resonance in the decay $J/ψ\to γp \bar{p}$ is described as a zero baryon number, ``deuteron-like singlet ${}^1S_0 $'' state. The difference in binding energy of the deuteron (-2.225 MeV) and of the new state (-17.5 MeV) can be accounted for in a simple potential model with a $λ\cdot λ$ confining interaction.

## Key claim

The narrow resonance recently observed by BES in the decay \(J/\psi \to \gamma p \bar{p}\) can be described as a zero baryon number, deuteron-like singlet \({}^1S_0\) baryonium state, with the difference in binding energy between the deuteron (-2.225 MeV) and this new state (-17.5 MeV) accounted for by a simple potential model using a \(\lambda \cdot \lambda\) confining interaction. The authors note that this interpretation is conditional on the resonance being an S-wave state, as the BES data can also be fit with a P-wave Breit-Wigner function.

## Method

The authors construct a simple square-well potential model for the nucleon-antinucleon system, analogous to the phenomenological model used for the deuteron. They solve the bound-state equation \(\alpha \cot(\alpha a) = -\beta\) for a square well of radius \(a \approx 2\) fm, using the known deuteron binding energy to calibrate the potential depth for the \(NN\) system, then adjusting the depth to reproduce the new \(p\bar{p}\) state's binding energy. The factor by which the potential depth increases (from 36.5 MeV for the deuteron to 64 MeV for the new state) is compared to the factor of two expected from the color \(\lambda \cdot \lambda\) interaction when going from \(qq\) to \(q\bar{q}\).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.225 MeV (triplet \({}^3S_1\) neutron-proton bound state).
- New \(p\bar{p}\) state binding energy: -17.5 MeV (from the BES S-wave fit).
- Square-well potential depth for deuteron: \(V = 36.5\) MeV (for well radius \(a \approx 2\) fm).
- Square-well potential depth for the new \(p\bar{p}\) state: \(V = 64\) MeV, a factor of 1.76 deeper than the deuteron potential.
- BES resonance mass (S-wave fit): \(M = 1859 {}^{+3}_{-10} (\text{stat}) {}^{+5}_{-25} (\text{sys})\) MeV/\(c^2\), with total width \(\Gamma < 30\) MeV/\(c^2\) at 90% confidence level.
- Virtual \({}^1S_0\) neutron-proton state energy: 0.0382 MeV above zero.
- Predicted \(\Lambda \bar{\Lambda}\) \(0^{-+}\) state binding energy: 31 MeV, at a mass of 2200 MeV/\(c^2\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing idea of baryonium states (nucleon-antinucleon bound systems) dating back to the 1960s and the MIT bag model of Jaffe (1977), and connects to the recent BES observation of a narrow enhancement in \(J/\psi \to \gamma p \bar{p}\) and similar enhancements seen by Belle in \(B^+ \to K^+ p \bar{p}\) and \(\bar{B}^0 \to D^0 p \bar{p}\). The authors also note a previously reported narrow S-wave triplet \(p\bar{p}\) resonance at 1870 MeV/\(c^2\) with \(J^{PC} = 1^{--}\) from Antonelli et al. (1998). The model suggests that further baryonium resonances should exist, such as a \(\Lambda \bar{\Lambda}\) state, and that these states could enable new studies of exotic mesons and baryons in B decays, as also noted by Rosner (2003).
