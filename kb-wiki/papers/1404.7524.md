# Comprehensive Amplitude Analysis of $γγ\rightarrow π^+π^-, π^0π^0$ and $\overline{K} K$ below 1.5 GeV

> In this paper we perform an amplitude analysis of essentially all published pion and kaon pair production data from two photon collisions below 1.5 GeV. This includes all the high statistics results from Belle, as well as older data from Mark II at SLAC, CELLO at DESY, Crystal Ball at SLAC. The purpose of this analysis is to provide as close to a model-independent determination of the $γγ$ to meson pair amplitudes as possible. Having data with limited angular coverage, typically $|\cos θ| < 0.6-0.8$, and no polarization information for reactions in which spin is an essential complication, the determination of the underlying amplitudes might appear an intractable problem. However, imposing the basic constraints required by analyticity, unitarity, and crossing-symmetry makes up for the experimentally missing information. Final state interactions among the meson pairs are critical to this analysis. To fix these, we include the latest $ππ\toππ$, ${\overline K}K$ scattering amplitudes given by dispersive analyses, supplemented in the ${\overline K}K$ threshold region by the recent precision Dalitz plot analysis from BaBar. With these hadronic amplitudes built into unitarity, we can constrain the overall description of $γγ\toππ$ and $\overline{K}K$ datasets, both integrated and differential cross-sections, including the high statistics charged and neutral pion, as well as $K_sK_s$ data from Belle. Since this analysis invokes coupled hadronic channels, having data on both $γγ\toππ$ and $\overline{K}K$ reduces the solution space to essentially a single form. We present the partial wave amplitudes, show how well they fit all the available data, and give the two photon couplings of scalar and tensor resonances that appear. These partial waves are important inputs into forthcoming dispersive calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering.

## Key claim
By imposing constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry, the authors achieve a near-model-independent determination of the two-photon to meson-pair amplitudes below 1.5 GeV, despite limited angular coverage (typically |cos θ| < 0.6–0.8) and no polarization information. The analysis reduces the solution space to essentially a single form by invoking coupled hadronic channels and including data on both γγ → ππ and γγ → K̅K.

## Method
The authors performed a coupled-channel amplitude analysis of all published pion and kaon pair production data from two-photon collisions below 1.5 GeV, including high-statistics Belle data and older data from Mark II, CELLO, and Crystal Ball. They incorporated the latest ππ → ππ and K̅K scattering amplitudes from dispersive analyses (the Madrid-Krakow collaboration's Constrained Fit to Data IV parametrization), supplemented near the K̅K threshold by the BaBar Dalitz plot analysis. Using a K-matrix representation with coupled-channel unitarity saturated by ππ and K̅K channels, they fitted the γγ → ππ and γγ → K̅K amplitudes to determine partial wave amplitudes for S and D waves with isospin 0 and 2.

## Key numbers
- The Belle charged pion measurement binned the π⁺π⁻ invariant mass in 5 MeV steps and in angular intervals of 0.05 in cos θ.
- The sum of π⁺π⁻ and π⁰π⁰ integrated cross-sections (Σ(Z=0.6)) shows a peak around 1250 MeV associated with the f₂(1270) resonance and a smaller peak just below 1 GeV associated with the f₀(980).
- Belle's simple fit gave a two-photon width for the f₀(980) of 205 +95/-83 (stat) +147/-117 (syst) eV.
- A previous amplitude analysis by one of the authors and Belle colleagues found a two-photon width for the f₀(980) between 100 eV and 540 eV.
- The analysis covers energies from ππ threshold up to 1.5 GeV, with hadronic inputs used up to 1.42 GeV for the I=J=0 channel.

## Context
This work extends previous amplitude analyses by Pennington and Belle colleagues (2008) that used only charged pion data from Belle, now incorporating the published neutral pion results and high-statistics K_sK_s data from Belle. The analysis provides partial wave amplitudes that serve as key input for forthcoming dispersive calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering, which is important for reducing uncertainties in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. The results enable determination of two-photon couplings for scalar and tensor resonances (σ/f₀(500), f₀(980), f₀(1370), f₂(1270)) that can be compared with model predictions for different compositions of these states.
