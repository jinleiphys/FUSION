# Topology, magnetic field, and strongly interacting matter

> Gauge theories with compact symmetry groups possess topologically non-trivial configurations of gauge field. This has dramatic implications for the vacuum structure of Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD) and for the behavior of QCD plasma, as well as for condensed matter systems with chiral quasiparticles. I review the current status of the problem with an emphasis on the interplay of chirality with a background magnetic field, and on the observable manifestations of topology in heavy ion collisions, Dirac semimetals, neutron stars, and in the Early Universe.

## Key claim

This review argues that topologically nontrivial configurations of gauge fields in QCD, when combined with a background magnetic field, produce observable phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME), but the quantitative predictions remain uncertain because the Chern-Simons diffusion rate at strong coupling relevant for heavy ion collisions cannot yet be reliably computed from first principles.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to topological transitions in QCD matter, including weak-coupling semiclassical sphaleron calculations, holographic AdS/CFT computations for strongly coupled plasmas, and lattice QCD simulations of topological susceptibility and the QCD phase diagram in magnetic fields. It then surveys experimental signatures of these topological effects in heavy ion collisions (charge-dependent azimuthal correlations), condensed matter systems (magneto-transport in Dirac semimetals), neutron stars, and the Early Universe.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field in heavy ion collisions is estimated as \(eB \sim \mathcal{O}(10\ m_\pi^2)\) (Kharzeev 2007).
* The weak-coupling extrapolation for the Chern-Simons diffusion rate gives \(\Gamma \simeq 30\ \alpha_s^4\ T^4\) (Moore 2010).
* The holographic result for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at strong coupling is \(\Gamma = (g^2 N)^2 / (256\ \pi^3)\ T^4\) (Son 2002).
* In improved holographic QCD at \(\alpha_s = 0.5\), the Chern-Simons diffusion rate at the transition temperature is \(\Gamma(T_c) \geq 1.64\ T_c^4\) (Gursoy 2012).
* The chiral condensate in a weak magnetic field increases as \(\Sigma(B) = \Sigma(0)\ [1 + (eB\ \ln 2)/(4 \pi F_\pi)^2 + \mathcal{O}((eB)^2/F_\pi^4)]\) (Shushpanov 1997).
* In a strong magnetic field limit, the Chern-Simons diffusion rate becomes \(\Gamma(B,T) = (g^2 N)^2 / (384 \sqrt{3} \pi^5)\ B\ T^2\) for \(B \gg T^2\) (Basar 2012).
* The CME current is given by \(\vec{J} = (e^2 / 2 \pi^2)\ \mu_5\ \vec{B}\) (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008).
* The charge asymmetry parameter in heavy ion collisions is estimated as \(a \sim 1\%\) (Kharzeev 2004).
* STAR Collaboration measured charge asymmetry fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (Abelev 2009); ALICE Collaboration reported measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (Belmont 2014).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on topological effects in QCD initiated by instanton solutions (Belavin 1975) and the theta-vacuum structure (Callan 1976, 't Hooft 1976), and connects to recent lattice studies of topological susceptibility (Bonati 2013) and the QCD phase diagram in magnetic fields (Bali 2011). It enables further experimental searches for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and in condensed matter experiments on Dirac semimetals such as ZrTe\(_5\) (Li 2014), as well as theoretical work on anomalous hydrodynamics and chiral magnetic waves.
