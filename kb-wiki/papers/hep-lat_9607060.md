# Dirac operator as a random matrix and the quenched limit of QCD with chemical potential

> The behavior of quenched QCD at nonzero chemical potential $μ$ has been a long-standing puzzle. An explicit solution is found using the random matrix approach to chiral symmetry breaking. At nonzero $μ$ the quenched QCD is not a simple $n\to0$ limit of a theory with $n$ quarks: a naive `replica trick' fails. A limit that leads to the quenched QCD is that of a theory with $2n$ quarks: $n$ quarks with original action and $n$ quarks with conjugate action.

# Dirac operator as a random matrix and the quenched limit of QCD with chemical potential

## Key claim

The quenched limit $n \to 0$ of QCD with $n$ quarks is not smooth at nonzero chemical potential $\mu$, and therefore the quenched theory is not a good approximation to full QCD at finite $\mu$; instead, the quenched QCD corresponds to the $n \to 0$ limit of a theory with $2n$ quarks, $n$ quarks with the original action and $n$ quarks with the conjugate action.

## Method

The study uses a random matrix model where the Dirac operator is represented in chiral block-diagonal form with a Gaussian-distributed complex random matrix $X$ and a chemical potential term $\mu\gamma_0$. The quenched free energy $V = -\langle \ln\det(z-D) \rangle$ is computed via the replica trick, first for $n$ fermion species and then taking $n \to 0$, and separately for a theory with $n$ quarks and $n$ conjugate quarks. The eigenvalue density $\rho(x,y)$ in the complex plane is obtained from the analytic properties of the quark condensate $G(z) \equiv \langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle$ using the relation $\rho = (1/\pi) \Re (\partial G / \partial z^*)$.

## Key numbers

- For $\mu^2 \leq 1/8$, the cut of $G(z)$ lies on the imaginary axis; for $\mu^2 > 1/8$, the branch points bifurcate and cuts go into the complex plane.
- Chiral symmetry is restored above $\mu_c^2 = 0.278\ldots$, where the cut no longer passes through $z = 0$.
- The eigenvalue density inside the quenched blob is given by $\rho = \frac{1}{4\pi} \left( \frac{x^2 + \mu^2}{(\mu^2 - x^2)^2} - 1 \right)$.
- Numerical results are shown for random $100 \times 100$ matrices at $\mu^2 = 0.06$ and $\mu^2 = 0.40$.

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle from the mid-1980s: Monte Carlo studies of quenched QCD at finite $\mu$ (Barbour 1986) gave $\mu_c \approx m_\pi/2$, an unphysical result since the pion carries no baryon number. The random matrix approach extends the work of Shuryak and Verbaarschot (1993) on small eigenvalues of the Dirac operator. The result shows that the quenched theory is instead a smooth limit of a theory with conjugate quarks, as suggested by Gocksch (1988), and explains the formation of a baryonic condensate and a "baryonic pion" bound state. This demonstrates that the phase of the fermion determinant is crucial for simulating full QCD at finite $\mu$.
