# Particle production in antiproton induced nuclear reactions

> The quantum molecular dynamics model has been improved to investigate the reaction dynamics induced by antiprotons. The reaction channels of elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange and inelastic collisions have been included in the model. Dynamics on particle production, in particular pions, kaons, antikaons and hyperons, is investigated in collisions of $\overline{p}$ on $^{12}$C, $^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ca, $^{112}$Sn, $^{181}$Ta, $^{197}$Au and $^{238}$U from a low to high incident momentum. The rapidity and momentum distributions of $π^{+}$ and protons from the LEAR measurements can be well reproduced. The impacts of system size and incident momentum on particle emissions are investigated from the inclusive spectra, transverse momentum and rapidity distributions. It is found that the annihilations of $\overline{p}$ on nucleons are of importance on the particle production. Hyperons are mainly produced via meson induced reactions on nucleons and strangeness exchange collisions when the incident momentum is below the threshold value of annihilation reaction. A higher nuclear temperature is obtained from the kaon emission, but it has a lower value for hyperon production.

## Key claim

The improved LQMD transport model reproduces LEAR measurements of pion and proton rapidity and momentum distributions in antiproton-nucleus collisions, but the extracted nuclear temperatures from kaon and hyperon spectra remain below the conditions needed for quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) model was extended to include antiproton-induced reaction channels: elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange, and inelastic collisions. The model incorporates mean-field potentials for baryons, antibaryons (via G-parity transformation with a scaling factor ξ=0.25), hyperons (via light-quark counting rule), and kaons/antikaons (via chiral Lagrangian dispersion relations), and simulates collisions of antiprotons on targets from 12C to 238U across incident momenta from 50 MeV/c to 4 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Antinucleon optical potential at zero momentum and normal nuclear density ρ0=0.16 fm⁻³: V_N̄ = -164 MeV (with scaling factor ξ=0.25)
- Optical potentials at saturation density: -32 MeV for Λ(Σ), -16 MeV for Ξ, -164 MeV for N̄, -436 MeV for Λ̄, -218 MeV for Ξ̄
- Kaon-nucleon potential strength: +27.8 MeV (repulsive) at saturation density for isospin symmetric matter
- Antikaon-nucleon potential strength: -100.3 MeV (attractive) at saturation density for isospin symmetric matter
- Temperature parameters for π⁰ from Maxwell-Boltzmann fits: 103±4 MeV (¹²C), 106±3 MeV (⁴⁰Ca), 110±2 MeV (¹⁸¹Ta) at 4 GeV/c
- Temperature parameters for K⁰: 150±7 MeV (¹²C), 144±4 MeV (⁴⁰Ca), 136±3 MeV (¹⁸¹Ta) at 4 GeV/c
- Temperature parameters for neutral hyperons: 101±16 MeV (¹²C), 97±15 MeV (⁴⁰Ca), 94±8 MeV (¹⁸¹Ta) at 4 GeV/c
- Threshold momentum for N̄N → Λ̄Λ annihilation reaction: 1.439 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the intranuclear cascade (INC) model studies of Cu89 and the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model calculations of Ga12 and La12 for antiproton-nucleus reactions. The improved LQMD model enables systematic investigation of strangeness production and hypernucleus formation, with ongoing work combining the transport model with a statistical model to predict hypernuclei yields for future PANDA experiments at FAIR.
