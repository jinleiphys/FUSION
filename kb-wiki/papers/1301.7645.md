# Effective gluon potential and Yang-Mills thermodynamics

> We derive the Polyakov-loop thermodynamic potential in the perturbative approach to pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory. The potential expressed in terms of the Polyakov loop in the fundamental representation corresponds to that of the strong-coupling expansion, of which the relevant coefficients of the gluon energy distribution are specified by characters of the SU(3) group. At high temperature, the potential exhibits the correct asymptotic behavior, whereas at low temperature, it disfavors gluons as appropriate dynamical degrees of freedom. To quantify the Yang-Mills thermodynamics in confined phase, we introduce a hybrid approach which matches the effective gluon potential to that of glueballs, constrained by the QCD trace anomaly in terms of dilaton fields.

# Effective gluon potential and Yang-Mills thermodynamics

## Key claim
The authors derive a Polyakov-loop thermodynamic potential for pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory that, while reproducing the correct high-temperature ideal gas limit, yields unphysical negative entropy and energy densities in the confined phase, indicating that gluons are physically forbidden as degrees of freedom below the critical temperature, not merely statistically suppressed.

## Method
The authors employ the background field method to evaluate the Yang-Mills partition function, decomposing the gluon field into background and quantum components. They perform the color trace over the adjoint Polyakov loop matrix and express the resulting gluon thermodynamic potential in terms of characters of the SU(3) group, specifically the Polyakov loop Φ and its conjugate Φ̄ in the fundamental representation. To address the failure of the gluon-only potential in the confined phase, they introduce a hybrid approach that matches the effective gluon potential above T_c to a dilaton-field description of glueballs below T_c, constrained by the QCD trace anomaly.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature for the first-order phase transition is set to T_c = 270 MeV.
- For the hybrid model, the glueball mass is taken as M_glueball = 1.7 GeV and the nucleon mass as M_nucleon = 0.94 GeV, yielding constituent masses M_g = 0.85 GeV for gluons and M_q = 0.31 GeV for quarks.

## Context
This work extends the Polyakov-loop effective model approach of Pisarski (2000) and the background field method of Gava, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981), and connects to the strong-coupling character expansion of the effective action by SlQCD (2009). The derived potential reproduces the form used in the PNJL model of Fukushima (2004) and Ratti, Thaler, and Weise (2006) as a limiting case. The hybrid approach enables a more realistic description of Yang-Mills thermodynamics across the phase transition, with the potential for extension to full QCD with dynamical quarks, guided by lattice results.
