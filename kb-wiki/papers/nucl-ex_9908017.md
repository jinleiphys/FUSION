# Determination of the E2/M1 Ratio in the γN \to Δ(1232) Transition from a Simultaneous Measurement of p(\vecγ,p)π^0 and p(\vecγ,π^+)n

> Tagged linearly polarized photons have been used at the Mainz Microtron MAMI for simultaneous measurements of the p(\vecγ,p)π^0 and p(\vecγ,π^+)n reaction channels to study the γN \to Δ(1232) transition. The energy dependence of the magnetic dipole M_{1+}^{3/2} and electric quadrupole E_{1+}^{3/2} amplitudes have been extracted from these data in the photon energy range from 270 to 420 MeV. The E2/M1 ratio for the γN \to Δ(1232) transition has been determined to be - (2.5+-0.1_{stat}+-0.2_{sys}) % at the resonance position delta_{33}=90^0.

## Key claim

The E2/M1 ratio for the γN → Δ(1232) transition is determined to be −(2.5 ± 0.1_stat ± 0.2_sys)% at the resonance position (δ33 = 90°), extracted from a simultaneous measurement of both p(γ,p)π⁰ and p(γ,π⁺)n reaction channels using tagged linearly polarized photons at MAMI, with the result being largely independent of absolute normalization and systematic errors because it is derived from the ratio of fitted angular distribution coefficients.

## Method

Tagged linearly polarized photons from coherent bremsstrahlung in a diamond crystal were used at the Mainz Microtron MAMI to measure differential cross sections and photon asymmetries for both p(γ,p)π⁰ and p(γ,π⁺)n simultaneously using the large-acceptance detector DAPHNE. The energy dependence of the magnetic dipole M_{1+}^{3/2} and electric quadrupole E_{1+}^{3/2} amplitudes was extracted via three analyses: a fit to the pπ⁰ channel alone using the ratio of coefficients C_∥/A_∥ from the parallel-component angular distribution; an energy-independent simultaneous multipole analysis of both channels to obtain isospin separation; and an energy-dependent fixed-t dispersion relation analysis including additional observables.

## Key numbers

- E2/M1 ratio = −(2.5 ± 0.1_stat ± 0.2_sys)% at the resonance position δ33 = 90°
- Photon energy range covered: 270 to 420 MeV
- 16 energy bins between Eγ = 270 MeV and 420 MeV
- Angular range for nπ⁺ channel: 45° to 135°; for pπ⁰ channel: from 95°–125° at 270 MeV to 65°–125° at 420 MeV
- Photon tagging resolution: about 2 MeV over the range 50 to 800 MeV
- Tagging efficiency: about 55% for incoherent bremsstrahlung
- Absolute precision of photon polarization and flux: better than ±2%
- Target density accuracy: ±0.5%
- Systematic scale uncertainties: ∼3% for MAMI results, ∼1% for LEGS results
- Energy-dependent discrepancy of up to 15% between MAMI and LEGS absolute differential cross sections

## Context

This work extends previous measurements from the Mainz group (Beck 1997) which reported REM = −(2.5 ± 0.2_stat ± 0.2_sys)% and the LEGS group (Blanpied 1997) which reported REM = −(3.0 ± 0.3_stat+sys ± 0.2_mod)%, addressing the unsatisfactory variation in theoretical analyses from RPI (Davidson 1997, REM = −(3.2 ± 0.25)%), VPI (Workman 1997, REM = −(1.5 ± 0.5)%), and Mainz (Hanstein 1996, REM = −(2.5 ± 0.1)%). The simultaneous measurement of both pion channels with the same setup minimizes systematic errors and enables a consistent isospin separation of the s- and p-wave amplitudes, providing a benchmark for constituent-quark models predicting −2% < REM < 0, Skyrme models predicting −6% < REM < −2.5%, chiral bag models giving −2% to −3%, and lattice QCD results of REM = (+3 ± 9)% (Leinweber 1992).
