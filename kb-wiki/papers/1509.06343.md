# Neutrons on a surface of liquid helium

> We investigate the possibility of ultracold neutron (UCN) storage in quantum states defined by the combined potentials of the Earth's gravity and the neutron optical repulsion by a horizontal surface of liquid helium. We analyse the stability of the lowest quantum state, which is most susceptible to perturbations due to surface excitations, against scattering by helium atoms in the vapor and by excitations of the liquid, comprised of ripplons, phonons and surfons. This is an unusual scattering problem since the kinetic energy of the neutron parallel to the surface may be much greater than the binding energies perpendicular. The total scattering time constant of these UCNs at 0.7 K is found to exceed one hour, and rapidly increasing with decreasing temperature. Such low scattering rates should enable high-precision measurements of the scheme of discrete energy levels, thus providing improved access to short-range gravity. The system might also be useful for neutron beta-decay experiments. We also sketch new experimental concepts for level population and trapping of UCNs above a flat horizontal mirror.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the possibility of storing ultracold neutrons (UCNs) in quantum states above a horizontal surface of liquid helium, finding that the total scattering time constant at 0.7 K exceeds one hour, but this result depends on the assumption that the kinetic energy of the neutron parallel to the surface may be much greater than the binding energies perpendicular, making this an unusual scattering problem.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional Schrodinger equation for a neutron in the combined potential of Earth's gravity and the neutron optical repulsion by liquid helium, calculating the discrete energy levels and wave functions for the lowest quantum states. They then computed scattering rates from three mechanisms: collisions with helium atoms in the vapor above the surface, scattering by ripplons (surface capillary waves), and scattering by surfons (surface excitations), using Fermi's golden rule with appropriate matrix elements for each process.

## Key numbers

- Neutron optical potential of liquid helium: V0 = 18.5 neV (from bound coherent scattering length a_He = 3.26 x 10^-13 cm and helium density n_He = 21.8 nm^-3)
- Characteristic length scale of neutron wave function: z0 = 5.87 micrometers
- Neutron penetration depth into liquid helium: kappa_0^-1 = 33 nm
- Energy level spacing scale: mgz0 = 0.6 peV
- First four energy level parameters for finite V0: alpha_1^He = 2.332, alpha_2^He = 4.082, alpha_3^He = 5.515, alpha_4^He = 6.781
- Scattering rate from helium vapor at T = 1 K: w_vap = 0.007 s^-1 (mean scattering time 138 s)
- Scattering rate from helium vapor at T = 0.7 K: w_vap = 1.6 x 10^-4 s^-1
- Crossover ripplon frequency between adiabatic and diabatic regimes: omega_qc = 915 s^-1, corresponding to wave number q_c = 70 cm^-1

## Context

This work extends the concept of gravitational quantum states of neutrons, first proposed by Luschikov and Frank (1978) and demonstrated experimentally by Nesvizhevsky et al. (2002, 2005) and Westphal et al. (2007) using polished quartz mirrors. The use of a liquid helium surface as a mirror offers advantages over solid mirrors (which are expensive, limited to tens of centimeters, and require active leveling) and could enable high-precision measurements of discrete energy levels for tests of short-range gravity, chameleon fields, and axion-like particles. The system is analogous to the well-studied two-dimensional electron gas on liquid helium surfaces, suggesting possibilities for neutron-based studies of quantum matter and neutron beta-decay experiments.
