# Chiral and trace anomalies in Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> Inspired by recent work by Tarasov and Venugopalan, we calculate the one-loop quark box diagrams relevant to polarized and unpolarized Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) by introducing off-forward momentum $l^μ$ as an infrared regulator. In the polarized case, we rederive the pole $1/l^2$ related to the axial (chiral) anomaly. In addition, we obtain the usual logarithmic term and the DIS coefficient function. We interpret the result in terms of the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $\tilde{H}$ and $\tilde{E}$ and discuss the possible violation of QCD factorization for the Compton scattering amplitude. Remarkably, we also find poles in the unpolarized case which are remnants of the trace anomaly. We argue that these poles are cancelled by the would-be massless glueball poles in the GPDs $H$ and $E$ as well as in their moments, the nucleon gravitational form factors $A,B$ and $D$. This mechanism sheds light on the connection between the gravitational form factors and the gluon condensate operator $F^{μν}F_{μν}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the one-loop quark box diagram for Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) contains a pole \(1/l^2\) not only in the polarized (chiral anomaly) sector, but also in the unpolarized sector, where it is a remnant of the QCD trace anomaly; the authors argue that these poles must be canceled by would-be massless glueball poles in the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) \(H\) and \(E\) and in the nucleon gravitational form factors \(A, B, D\), but they note that a rigorous field-theoretic justification of this cancellation mechanism is still needed.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop quark box diagrams for the Compton scattering amplitude in off-forward kinematics, using the off-forward momentum \(l^\mu\) as an infrared regulator instead of dimensional regularization. They evaluated the diagrams analytically with the Mathematica package Package-X, keeping the full dependence on the skewness parameter \(\xi\) in the symmetric (unpolarized) part, and extracted the pole terms proportional to \(1/l^2\) as well as the logarithmic and finite coefficient functions. The results were interpreted in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) \(\tilde{H}, \tilde{E}\) for the polarized case and \(H, E\) for the unpolarized case, and the pole terms were related to the matrix elements of the twist-four operators \(F\tilde{F}\) and \(F^2\).

## Key numbers

- The polarized splitting function obtained is \(\Delta P_{qg}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R(2\hat{x}-1)\) with \(T_R = 1/2\), and the coefficient function is \(\delta C_g^{\rm off}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R(2\hat{x}-1)\left(\ln \frac{1}{\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})}-1\right)\).
- The anomaly coefficient in the polarized sector is \(\delta C_g^{\rm anom}(\hat{x}) = 4T_R(1-\hat{x})\).
- The unpolarized splitting function is \(P_{qg}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R((1-\hat{x})^2+\hat{x}^2)\), and the coefficient functions are \(C_{1g}^{\rm off}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R ((1-\hat{x})^2+\hat{x}^2) \left(\ln \frac{1}{\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})}-1\right)\) and \(C_{2g}^{\rm off}(\hat{x}) = 2T_R((1-\hat{x})^2+\hat{x}^2) \left(\ln \frac{1}{\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})}-1\right) + 8T_R\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})\).
- The trace-anomaly-related kernel functions are \(K(\hat{x},\hat{\xi}) = 2T_R\frac{\hat{x}(1-\hat{x})}{1-\hat{\xi}^2}\) and \(L(\hat{x},\hat{\xi}) = 2T_R\frac{\hat{x}(\hat{\xi}-\hat{x})}{1-\hat{\xi}^2}\).
- The QED analog for the energy-momentum tensor matrix element between on-shell photon states is \(\langle p_2|\Theta_{\rm QED}^{\mu\nu}|p_1\rangle = -\frac{ e^2}{24\pi^2 l^2} \left(p^\mu p^\nu + \frac{l^\mu l^\nu-l^2 g^{\mu\nu}}{4}\right)\langle p_2|F^{\alpha\beta}F_{\alpha\beta}|p_1\rangle +\ldots\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Jaffe and Manohar (1989) and the recent worldline-formalism calculations by Tarasov and Venugopalan (2020, 2021) on anomaly poles in polarized DIS, providing a standard perturbative cross-check and generalizing the analysis to the unpolarized sector. It connects the trace-anomaly pole in the symmetric Compton amplitude to the gluon condensate operator \(F^{\mu\nu}F_{\mu\nu}\) and to the nucleon gravitational form factors \(A, B, D\), building on known results in QED by Giannotti, Armillis, and Donoghue (2008, 2009, 2015). The findings challenge the standard QCD factorization for DVCS in the \(\tilde{E}\) and \(E\) GPD sectors and motivate further work on the nonperturbative cancellation of massless poles, potentially involving the \(\eta'\) meson and the mechanism of mass generation in QCD.
