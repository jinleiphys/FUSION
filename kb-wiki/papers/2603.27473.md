# Isolation of photon-nuclear interaction backgrounds in the search for the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions originates from a chirality imbalance among quarks within metastable QCD vacuum domains and may be linked to $CP$ violation, which is believed to play a crucial role in the matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe. Over the past two decades, extensive experimental efforts at RHIC and the LHC have been devoted to the search for evidence of the CME. Recent advances have greatly improved our understanding of background contributions that can mimic CME-like signals. In particular, analyses utilizing techniques designed to suppress flow-related backgrounds indicate that the CME signal at RHIC, if present, is small. To further investigate potential background sources, particularly those associated with strong electromagnetic fields, we estimate the contribution from coherent photon-nuclear interactions. These interactions are driven by intense electromagnetic fields produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, with cross sections that scale with the field strength. Notably, the polarization of the incident photons is aligned with the electric field, which is oriented along the impact parameter direction and perpendicular to the magnetic field. Consequently, such processes can generate charge-dependent correlations that mimic key features of the CME signal, yet originate from different physics mechanisms and are distinct from flow-induced backgrounds. In this study, we quantitatively assess the influence of these coherent photon-nuclear interactions on the precision measurement of the CME, aiming to improve the separation of the genuine CME signal from these background contributions.

## Key claim

The coherent photon-nuclear production of \(\rho^0\) mesons in semi-central heavy-ion collisions generates a charge-dependent background to the chiral magnetic effect observable \(\Delta\gamma\) that is negative in sign and distinct from flow-induced backgrounds; this background causes the current experimental measurement of the possible CME signal fraction \(f_{\rm CME}\) to be underestimated by about 0.2%.

## Method

The authors calculate the total cross section for coherent \(\rho^0\) photoproduction in hadronic (semi-central) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV by integrating the photon flux (using a realistic Woods-Saxon form factor and accounting for collision geometry) with the photon-nuclear cross section derived from the vector meson dominance model and Glauber formalism. They then estimate the contribution to the three-point correlator \(\Delta\gamma\) by combining the yield of coherent \(\rho^0\) mesons at mid-rapidity, the angular distribution of their \(\pi^+\pi^-\) decay products (which are aligned with the impact parameter direction), and a multiplicity dilution factor.

## Key numbers

* Coherent \(\rho^0\) yield in mid-rapidity (\(|y|<1\)): \(N_{\rm coherent~\rho^0} = 0.03\) for 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV.
* Average correlation between decay pions and the impact parameter direction: \(\langle\cos(2\phi-2\psi)\rangle = 0.38\) for 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV.
* Estimated contribution to the three-point correlator: \(\langle \Delta\gamma_{\rm bkgd}^{\rm coherent~\rho^0} \rangle = -0.33 \times 10^{-6}\).
* This contribution corresponds to approximately \(-0.2\%\) of the inclusive \(\Delta\gamma = 1.89 \times 10^{-4}\) and of the reported CME fraction \(f_{\rm CME} = 14.7 \pm 5\%\).
* The transverse momentum of coherently produced \(\rho^0\) mesons is limited to \(p_T \le \hbar/R_A \approx 100\) MeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to isolate the chiral magnetic effect signal in heavy-ion collisions, following recent analyses at RHIC that have suppressed flow-related backgrounds and reported a small residual CME signal at the 5-10% level with 2-3\(\sigma\) significance (STAR 2021, STAR 2020). The authors identify coherent photon-nuclear interactions as a background source directly correlated with the electromagnetic field, distinct from the flow-induced backgrounds described by Voloshin (2004) and Wang (2009, 2016). The results suggest that applying a lower cut on pair transverse momentum (e.g., pair \(p_T > 100\) MeV/\(c\)) can mitigate this background, and the work also provides a qualitative explanation for the observed \(\Delta\gamma\) difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions in the RHIC isobar program (STAR 2023).
