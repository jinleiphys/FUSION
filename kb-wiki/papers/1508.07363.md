# Electromagnetic structure of light nuclei

> The present understanding of nuclear electromagnetic properties including electromagnetic moments, form factors and transitions in nuclei with A $\le$ 10 is reviewed. Emphasis is on calculations based on nuclear Hamiltonians that include two- and three-nucleon realistic potentials, along with one- and two-body electromagnetic currents derived from a chiral effective field theory with pions and nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current understanding of nuclear electromagnetic properties in light nuclei (A ≤ 10), claiming that ab initio calculations including two- and three-nucleon realistic potentials along with one- and two-body electromagnetic currents from chiral effective field theory can successfully explain experimental data, but with the important qualifier that the theoretical description of the first diffraction region in trinucleon magnetic form factors remains incomplete and that M1 transition widths in ⁸Be are systematically underpredicted.

## Method

The author reviews calculations using nuclear Hamiltonians that combine realistic two-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18) with three-nucleon potentials (Urbana IX or Illinois-7), as well as chiral effective field theory potentials at N3LO (NN) and N2LO (3N). Electromagnetic charge and current operators are derived from chiral effective field theory with pions and nucleons up to N3LO, including one- and two-body contributions, and are evaluated using Green's Function Monte Carlo methods for A=6-10 nuclei and other ab initio techniques for A=2-3 systems.

## Key numbers

- Two-body current corrections to magnetic moments can be as large as 40%, as seen in the case of ⁹C
- Two-body current corrections to M1 transitions in ⁸Be are at the 20%-30% level
- Deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors agree with experimental data up to q ≈ 3 fm⁻¹ and q ≈ 6 fm⁻¹ respectively
- Trinucleon magnetic form factors show excellent agreement between theory and experiment for q ≤ 2 fm⁻¹
- The deuteron magnetic form factor agrees with experimental data for q ≈ 3 fm⁻¹

## Context

This review extends the line of work on meson-exchange currents dating back to Riska and Brown (1972), who first identified the missing 10% correction to the impulse approximation in radiative neutron-proton capture. It builds on chiral effective field theory formulations of electromagnetic currents by Park, Min, and Rho (1996) and more recent developments by the JLab-Pisa group (Pastore et al., 2008-2012) and the Bochum-Bonn group (Kölling et al., 2009-2012). The work enables future consistent Quantum Monte Carlo calculations using chiral potentials and currents, as well as studies of weak processes including neutrino-nucleus interactions and axial two-body currents.
