# Anharmonic double-phonon excitations in the interacting boson model

> Double-$γ$ vibrations in deformed nuclei are analyzed in the context of the interacting boson model. A simple extension of the original version of the model towards higher-order interactions is required to explain the observed anharmonicities of nuclear vibrations. The influence of three- and four-body interactions on the moments of inertia of ground- and $γ$-bands, and on the relative position of single-$γ$ and double-$γ$ bands is studied in detail. As an example of a realistic calculation, spectra and transitions of the highly $γ$-anharmonic nuclei $^{164}$Dy, $^{166}$Er, and $^{168}$Er are interpreted in this approach.

# Anharmonic double-phonon excitations in the interacting boson model

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) with only up to two-body interactions cannot produce the large anharmonicities observed in double-γ vibrations of deformed nuclei; only by adding three- or four-body boson interactions can the observed anharmonicity ratios (ranging from 2.5 to 2.8 for the double-γ over single-γ energy ratio) be reproduced, though three-body terms alone fail to describe the moments of inertia correctly.

## Method

The authors extended the standard IBM-1 Hamiltonian (containing one- and two-body terms) by adding three-body d-boson interactions and, separately, four-body interactions expressed either as a quadrupole expansion or as an expansion in pseudo-Casimir operators of SU(3). They computed energy ratios R₀^γ and R₄^γ (defined to remove rotational contributions) for the K^π=0⁺ and K^π=4⁺ double-γ band heads relative to the single-γ excitation energy, and compared these with experimental data for ¹⁶⁴Dy, ¹⁶⁶Er, and ¹⁶⁸Er. They also calculated moments of inertia and E2 transition probabilities using the Consistent-Q Formalism.

## Key numbers

- Experimental energy ratios for ¹⁶⁶Er: R₀^γ = 2.76 and R₄^γ = 2.50
- For ¹⁶⁶Er with a three-body (l=4) Hamiltonian: calculated R₀^γ = 2.82 and R₄^γ = 2.45, with double-γ band heads at 1926 keV (K=0) and 1972 keV (K=4)
- For ¹⁶⁴Dy: experimental R₄^γ = 2.84; calculated R₀^γ = 3.31 and R₄^γ = 2.81
- For ¹⁶⁸Er: experimental R₄^γ = 2.50; calculated R₀^γ = 2.93 and R₄^γ = 2.46
- For ¹⁶⁶Er with the pseudo-Casimir Hamiltonian: calculated R₀^γ = 3.08 and R₄^γ = 2.43
- Effective charges used: e_eff² = (1.66)² W.u. for ¹⁶⁴Dy, (1.83)² W.u. for ¹⁶⁶Er, and (1.67)² W.u. for ¹⁶⁸Er
- Boson numbers: N=16 for ¹⁶⁴Dy and ¹⁶⁸Er, N=15 for ¹⁶⁶Er

## Context

This work extends earlier IBM studies (Garcia-Ramos 1998) that showed the standard IBM with two-body interactions is essentially harmonic for γ vibrations. It addresses the controversy over double-γ band identification raised by Burke (1994) and others, and provides an alternative to models such as the quasi-phonon model (Soloviev 1994, 1995), the geometrical model (Bohr 1982), the multi-phonon model (Jammari 1988), the self-consistent collective-coordinate method (Matsuo 1986, 1987), and the sdg-IBM (Yoshinaga 1986, 1988). The results enable systematic IBM calculations of anharmonic multi-phonon bands in deformed nuclei and suggest that pseudo-Casimir expansions are the most appropriate framework for describing anharmonic vibrations.
