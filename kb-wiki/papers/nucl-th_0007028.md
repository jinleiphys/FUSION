# Anharmonic double-phonon excitations in the interacting boson model

> Double-$╬│$ vibrations in deformed nuclei are analyzed in the context of the interacting boson model. A simple extension of the original version of the model towards higher-order interactions is required to explain the observed anharmonicities of nuclear vibrations. The influence of three- and four-body interactions on the moments of inertia of ground- and $╬│$-bands, and on the relative position of single-$╬│$ and double-$╬│$ bands is studied in detail. As an example of a realistic calculation, spectra and transitions of the highly $╬│$-anharmonic nuclei $^{164}$Dy, $^{166}$Er, and $^{168}$Er are interpreted in this approach.

# Anharmonic double-phonon excitations in the interacting boson model

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) with only up to two-body interactions cannot produce the large anharmonicities observed in double-╬│ vibrations of deformed nuclei; only by adding three- or four-body boson interactions can the observed anharmonicity ratios (ranging from 2.5 to 2.8 for the double-╬│ over single-╬│ energy ratio) be reproduced, though three-body terms alone fail to describe the moments of inertia correctly.

## Method

The authors extended the standard IBM-1 Hamiltonian (containing one- and two-body terms) by adding three-body d-boson interactions and, separately, four-body interactions expressed either as a quadrupole expansion or as an expansion in pseudo-Casimir operators of SU(3). They computed energy ratios RРѓђ^╬│ and RРѓё^╬│ (defined to remove rotational contributions) for the K^¤ђ=0РЂ║ and K^¤ђ=4РЂ║ double-╬│ band heads relative to the single-╬│ excitation energy, and compared these with experimental data for ┬╣РЂХРЂ┤Dy, ┬╣РЂХРЂХEr, and ┬╣РЂХРЂИEr. They also calculated moments of inertia and E2 transition probabilities using the Consistent-Q Formalism.

## Context

This work extends earlier IBM studies (Garcia-Ramos 1998) that showed the standard IBM with two-body interactions is essentially harmonic for ╬│ vibrations. It addresses the controversy over double-╬│ band identification raised by Burke (1994) and others, and provides an alternative to models such as the quasi-phonon model (Soloviev 1994, 1995), the geometrical model (Bohr 1982), the multi-phonon model (Jammari 1988), the self-consistent collective-coordinate method (Matsuo 1986, 1987), and the sdg-IBM (Yoshinaga 1986, 1988). The results enable systematic IBM calculations of anharmonic multi-phonon bands in deformed nuclei and suggest that pseudo-Casimir expansions are the most appropriate framework for describing anharmonic vibrations.
