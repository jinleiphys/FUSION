# Collisions in Chiral Kinetic Theory

> Using a covariant formalism, we construct a chiral kinetic theory Lorentz invariant to order $\mathcal O(\hbar)$ which includes collisions. We find a new contribution to the particle number current due to the side jumps required by the conservation of angular momentum during collisions. We also find a conserved symmetric stress-energy tensor as well as the $H$-function obeying Boltzmann's $H$-theorem. We demonstrate their use by finding a general equilibrium solution and the values of the anomalous transport coefficients characterizing chiral vortical effect.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a chiral kinetic theory that is Lorentz invariant to order O(hbar) and includes collisions, by introducing a new contribution to the particle number current from "side jumps" required by angular momentum conservation during collisions. The authors note that the correct form of the collision term is presented as an educated guess or conjecture, and that deriving it from underlying field theory is beyond the present scope.

## Method

The authors develop a covariant formalism for massless spinning particles, using a frame-dependent spin tensor and a "no-jump frame" (the center-of-mass frame of each collision) to define particle positions. They construct a Lorentz-covariant phase-space current that includes a magnetization term from intrinsic rotation and a new "jump current" term from the spacelike motion of particles during collisions, then derive the corresponding collision kernel using the distribution function in the no-jump frame. They verify Lorentz covariance of the current and collision term to linear order in hbar, and prove the existence of a conserved symmetric stress-energy tensor and an H-function obeying Boltzmann's H-theorem.

## Key numbers

- The chiral vortical effect transport coefficient for the number current is xi = mu^2/(4 pi^2) + T^2/12, where mu is the chemical potential and T is the temperature.
- The chiral vortical effect transport coefficient for the stress-energy tensor is xi_T = mu^3/(6 pi^2) + (mu T^2)/6, which is twice the result found in PhysRevD.20.1807 due to spin coupling to vorticity.
- The entropy current transport coefficient is xi_H = (mu T)/6.
- The helicity lambda is of order O(hbar), and the side jump displacement Delta^mu is proportional to lambda.

## Context

This work extends the collisionless chiral kinetic theory developed by Son and Yamamoto (2012) and the Lorentz-invariant formulation with side jumps by Chen, Son, Stephanov, and Yee (2014) to include collisions. It provides the missing collision term for chiral kinetic theory and enables the calculation of anomalous transport coefficients for the chiral vortical effect from kinetic theory, which were previously derived from hydrodynamics by Son and Surowka (2009) and from gauge/gravity duality by Erdmenger et al. (2008) and Banerjee et al. (2008). The results agree with the general form required by the second law of thermodynamics found by Neiman and Oz (2010).
