# Soft rescattering in the timelike proton form factor within a spacetime scheme

> The annihilation of a lepton pair into a proton-antiproton pair, and the reverse process, allow for a measurement of the timelike proton form factors. This work aims at studying the corrections due to direct soft interactions between the proton and the antiproton before their annihilation or after their creation. The analysis is carried on in the spacetime formalism used by us in a series of recent works on timelike FFs. In particular the effect of $\bar{p}p$ annihilation into many-meson states is considered. We expect it to determine similar real and imaginary parts for the form factor near the $\bar{p}p$ channel threshold. We also discuss the possibility that the timelike FF differ by a phase in the hadron pair production and annihilation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that soft rescattering between the proton and antiproton (defined as "avoidable" interactions involving near-on-shell hadrons, as opposed to hard processes or unavoidable formation dynamics) can produce comparable real and imaginary contributions to the timelike proton form factor near the \(\bar{p}p\) threshold, and that the timelike form factor may differ by a phase between the hadron pair production and annihilation channels.

## Method

The authors analyze the timelike proton form factor using a four-dimensional spacetime Fourier transform formalism previously developed in their series of works, where the form factor \(F(q)\) is expressed as the Fourier transform of a spacetime distribution \(F(x)\). They separate the full amplitude into a "bare formation" term and soft rescattering corrections, where rescattering is restricted to interactions between color-singlet hadrons with soft virtuality, modeled via an optical potential approach. The analysis focuses specifically on the charge-associated form factor \(F(q)\) and considers elastic rescattering, charge exchange (e.g., \(\bar{n}n \rightarrow \bar{p}p\) via pion exchange), and annihilation into multi-meson states as the relevant soft rescattering mechanisms.

## Key numbers

* The effective timelike form factor \(|F_p|^2\) is defined as \(|F_p|^2 = \frac{3\beta q^2 \sigma}{2\pi\alpha^2 (2+1/\tau)}\), where \(\beta = \sqrt{1-1/\tau}\), \(\tau = q^2/(4M^2)\), and \(M\) is the proton mass.
* The oscillatory modulation term in the form factor data is parameterized as \(F_{osc}(p) = A e^{-Bp} \cos(Cp + D)\), where the magnitude \(A \simeq 0.1\) and the phase \(D \approx 0\) for fits to BABAR data up to \(q^2 \approx 36\) GeV\(^2\).
* The oscillations around the leading trend are approximately 10% modulations of the form factor magnitude for \(q < 4\) GeV.
* The leading power-law fall of the form factor follows approximately \(1/q^4\) behavior, as in the quark counting rule \(|F_{scaling}(q^2)| = \mathcal{A}/(q^2)^2 \log^2(q^2/\Lambda^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Bianconi 2015, Bianconi 2016) on oscillatory modulations in the timelike proton form factor, where an optical potential model was used to explain the 10% oscillations above threshold. It builds on the spacetime formalism developed in Bianconi (2016) for interpreting timelike form factors as time distributions of photon-quark-antiquark creation vertices. The paper contrasts with approaches based on quark counting rules (Matveev 1973, Brodsky 1973) and perturbative QCD, and engages with explanations of threshold enhancements in neutral baryon pair production proposed by Haidenbauer (2014) (using nucleon-antinucleon strong-force potentials) and Baldini (2007) (focusing on local electric interactions between quarks and antiquarks). The work enables future studies of the imaginary part of the timelike form factor and the phase difference between production and annihilation channels, which could be tested by future experiments at facilities like PANDA.
