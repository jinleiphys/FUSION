# Model Analysis of the $ep \to ep'Ď€^+Ď€^-$ Electroproduction Reaction on the Proton

> Recent CLAS data on the $pĎ€^+Ď€^-$ electroproduction off protons at 1.3$<$W$<$1.57 GeV and 0.25$<$$Q^{2}$$<$0.6 GeV$^{2}$ have been analyzed using a meson-baryon phenomenological model. By fitting nine 1-fold differential cross section data for each $W$ and $Q^{2}$ bin, the charged double pion electroproduction mechanisms are identified from their manifestations in the observables. We have extracted the cross sections from amplitudes of each of the considered isobar channels as well as from their coherent sum. We also obtained non-resonant partial wave amplitudes of all contributing isobar channels which could be useful for advancing a complete coupled-channel analysis of all meson electroproduction data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the JM06 phenomenological model can describe all nine 1-fold differential cross sections of the $p\pi^+\pi^-$ electroproduction reaction on the proton using CLAS data, but only after replacing contact interactions in the direct $2\pi$ production mechanisms with additional exchange processes; the model is sensitive primarily to the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ nucleon resonances in the kinematic region of $W < 1.6$ GeV and $0.25 < Q^2 < 0.6$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The authors analyzed CLAS data on $p\pi^+\pi^-$ electroproduction at $1.3 < W < 1.57$ GeV and $0.25 < Q^2 < 0.6$ GeV$^2$ using a meson-baryon phenomenological model (JM06) based on tree-diagrams of effective Lagrangians. They fitted nine 1-fold differential cross sections (three invariant mass distributions and six angular distributions) for each $W$ and $Q^2$ bin, separating contributions from $\pi\Delta$ isobar channels (with resonant, Born, and contact terms) and direct $2\pi$ production mechanisms parameterized by particle-exchange amplitudes with exponential momentum-transfer dependence.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic range: $1.3 < W < 1.57$ GeV and $0.25 < Q^2 < 0.6$ GeV$^2$ for the CLAS data analyzed
- Binning: $\Delta W = 25$ MeV and $\Delta Q^2 = 0.05$ GeV$^2$ for the new CLAS data
- $\chi^2/(\text{data points})$ values: $2.71-2.80$ for $Q^2$ interval $0.25-0.4$ GeV$^2$, $1.77-1.87$ for $0.4-0.5$ GeV$^2$, and $1.39-1.65$ for $0.5-0.6$ GeV$^2$
- $\chi^2/\text{d.p.}$ values per $Q^2$ bin center: $2.74$ at $0.275$ GeV$^2$, $2.01$ at $0.325$ GeV$^2$, $1.90$ at $0.375$ GeV$^2$, $1.60$ at $0.425$ GeV$^2$, $1.37$ at $0.475$ GeV$^2$, $1.63$ at $0.525$ GeV$^2$, and $1.25$ at $0.575$ GeV$^2$
- Slope parameter for direct $2\pi$ production: $b = 4.0$ GeV$^{-2}$ determined from the fit
- Isobar channels account for $70$ to $90\%$ of the fully integrated cross sections in the nucleon resonance region

## Context

This work extends the JM05 model (Mokeev et al., 2006; Aznauryan et al., 2005) to describe new CLAS data (Fedotov et al., 2007) that provide much finer binning and higher precision than previous measurements (Ripani et al., 2003). The analysis enables extraction of electrocouplings for the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances and provides non-resonant partial wave amplitudes that can advance complete coupled-channel analyses of all meson electroproduction data, building on earlier efforts by the MAID group (Drechsel et al., 2008) and GWU group (Arndt et al., 2008) for single-pion channels.
