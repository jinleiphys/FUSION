# Internal structures of the baryons $Ξ(2030)$ and $Ξ(2120)$

> Currently, there is much controversy surrounding the interpretation of the $Ξ(2030)$ and $Ξ(2012)$ as traditional hadrons containing double strange quarks. In particular, the ratios of the partial decay widths into $Λ\bar{K}$ and $Σ\bar{K}$ for $Ξ(2030)$ cannot obtain a suitable explanation under the $qss$ three quark structure~\cite{Xiao:2013xi}. Thus, we suggest the $Ξ(2030)$ and $Ξ(2012)$ to be $VB(=\bar{K}^{*}Σ/ρΞ/\bar{K}^{*}Λ/ϕΞ/ωΞ)$ molecular states. In this work, we perform a systematical investigation of possible molecular states from the $VB(=\bar{K}^{*}Σ/ρΞ/\bar{K}^{*}Λ/ϕΞ/ωΞ)$ interaction. The interaction of the system considered is described by the $t$-channel vector ($ρ,ω,ϕ,\bar{K}^{*}$) and pseudoscalar ($π,η,\bar{K}$) mesons exchanges. By solving the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with the obtained one-boson-exchange potentials, the $VB(=\bar{K}^{*}Σ/ρΞ/\bar{K}^{*}Λ/ϕΞ/ωΞ)$ bound states with different quantum numbers are searched. The calculation suggests that $Ξ(2030)$ can be assigned as a $P$-wave $\bar{K}^{*}Σ/ρΞ/\bar{K}^{*}Λ/ϕΞ/ωΞ$ molecular state with spin parity $J^P=5/2^{+}$. The calculation also predict the existence of four $\bar{K}^{*}Σ/ρΞ/\bar{K}^{*}Λ/ϕΞ/ωΞ$ bound states with $J^P=1/2^{\pm}$ and $J^P=3/2^{\pm}$. The $Ξ(2012)$ may be a candidate for one of these four bound states. If $Ξ(2012)$ is an $S$-wave molecular state with $J^P=1/2^{-}$ or $J^P=3/2^{-}$, we suggest determining its spin and parity by studying its decay width, owing to the difference in their molecular components.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the baryons Ξ(2030) and Ξ(2120) are not conventional three-quark (qss) states but rather vector meson-baryon (VB) molecular states, specifically assigning Ξ(2030) as a P-wave bound state with spin-parity J^P = 5/2^+, while noting that the interpretation of Ξ(2120) remains uncertain and it may be one of four predicted S-wave bound states with J^P = 1/2^± or 3/2^±.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to describe the interactions between vector mesons (K̄*, ρ, φ, ω) and baryons (Σ, Ξ, Λ) in five coupled channels: K̄*Σ, ρΞ, K̄*Λ, φΞ, and ωΞ. The t-channel exchange potentials were derived from SU(3) chiral Lagrangians including vector (ρ, ω, φ, K̄*) and pseudoscalar (π, η, K̄) meson exchanges, with form factors applied at each vertex. Bound states were searched for by solving the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with the resulting coordinate-space potentials for various quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The Ξ(2030) has a mass of 2023 ± 5 MeV and a width of 20^(+15)_(-5) MeV, as listed by the Particle Data Group.
- The coupling constant g = 4.64 was fixed from the experimental strong decay width Γ_(K*+) = 50.3 ± 0.8 MeV.
- The constants D = 2.4 and F = 0.82 were used for the tensor coupling of vector mesons to baryons, reproducing baryon magnetic moments.
- The pseudoscalar coupling constants were F₁ = 0.51 and D₁ = 0.75, with the pion decay constant f = 93 MeV.
- The cutoff parameter Λ = m_i + α Λ_QCD was used with Λ_QCD = 220 MeV, where α is a free parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous molecular interpretations of Ξ baryons, particularly addressing the failure of the conventional three-quark (qss) model to explain the decay ratios of Ξ(2030) into ΛK̄ and ΣK̄ as shown by Xiao (2013). The study builds on earlier chiral unitary approach calculations by Oset (2010) and Gamermann (2011) that suggested poles around 2100 MeV from meson-baryon interactions, and on the work of Khemchandani (2016) which identified a J^P = 3/2⁻ state near 2046 MeV as a meson-baryon molecule. The results predict four additional molecular bound states with J^P = 1/2^± and 3/2^±, providing candidates for future experimental searches and suggesting that measuring the decay width of Ξ(2120) could determine its spin and parity.
