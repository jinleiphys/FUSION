# Lattice determination of $I= 0$ and 2 $ππ$ scattering phase shifts with a physical pion mass

> Phase shifts for $s$-wave $ππ$ scattering in both the $I=0$ and $I=2$ channels are determined from a lattice QCD calculation performed on 741 gauge configurations obeying G-parity boundary conditions with a physical pion mass and lattice size of $32^3\times 64$. These results support our recent study of direct CP violation in $K\toππ$ decay \cite{Abbott:2020hxn}, improving our earlier 2015 calculation \cite{Bai:2015nea}. The phase shifts are determined for both stationary and moving $ππ$ systems, at three ($I=0$) and four ($I=2$) different total momenta. We implement several $ππ$ interpolating operators including a scalar bilinear "$σ$" operator and paired single-pion bilinear operators with the constituent pions carrying various relative momenta. Several techniques, including correlated fitting and a bootstrap determination of p-values have been used to refine the results and a comparison with the generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) method is given. A detailed systematic error analysis is performed which allows phase shift results to be presented at a fixed energy.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first lattice QCD calculation of both I=0 and I=2 s-wave ππ scattering phase shifts over a range of two-pion energies with a physical pion mass, finding that the I=0 phase shift at 471 MeV is 32.3(1.0)(1.4) degrees, which is in much better agreement with the dispersive prediction of 35.9 degrees than the authors' earlier 2015 result of 23.8(4.9)(1.2) degrees, after correcting for the difference between the lattice pion mass of 142.3 MeV and the physical neutral pion mass of 135 MeV.

## Method

The calculation uses 741 gauge configurations on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with 2+1 flavors of Mobius domain wall fermions and G-parity boundary conditions in three spatial directions, with an inverse lattice spacing of 1.3784(68) GeV. The authors compute two-point correlation functions using multiple ππ interpolating operators including a scalar bilinear sigma operator and paired single-pion bilinear operators with various relative momenta, then extract phase shifts via the Luscher finite-volume technique for both stationary and moving ππ systems at three (I=0) and four (I=2) different total momenta, employing correlated fitting and bootstrap determination of p-values.

## Key numbers

- Lattice pion mass mπ(lat) = 142.3 MeV; physical pion mass mπ(phy) = 135 MeV
- Lattice size: 32^3 x 64, corresponding to a spatial volume of (4.6 fm)^3
- Inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1.3784(68) GeV
- Number of gauge configurations: 741
- I=0 s-wave phase shift at center-of-mass energy 471 MeV: δ0 = 32.3(1.0)(1.4) degrees
- I=0 s-wave phase shift from the 2015 calculation at Eππ = 498(11) MeV: δ0 = 23.8(4.9)(1.2) degrees
- Dispersive prediction for δ0 at 471 MeV: 35.9 degrees (evaluated from Colangelo (2001) using Mπ = 139.6 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' 2015 calculation (Bai 2015) of I=0 ππ scattering at a single energy near the kaon mass, which showed a significant discrepancy with dispersive predictions from Colangelo (2001). The improved agreement with dispersive results was achieved by adding multiple interpolating operators (including the sigma operator) and increasing statistics, revealing a previously unrecognized nearby excited state. The results directly support the companion calculation of direct CP violation in K→ππ decay (Abbott 2020) by providing the Lellouch-Luscher factor and finite-volume state energies needed for that analysis, and complement existing lattice calculations at heavier pion masses (Feng 2009, Beane 2011, Dudek 2012, Kurth 2014, Bulava 2016, Briceno 2016, Briceno 2017, Fu 2017, Culver 2019) by removing the need for chiral extrapolation.
