# Effective Operator Treatment of the Anharmonic Oscillator

> We analyse the one dimensional quartic oscillator using the effective operator methodology of Lee and Suzuki. We reproduce known results for low lying energy eigenvalues.

# Effective Operator Treatment of the Anharmonic Oscillator

## Key claim
The Lee-Suzuki effective operator method, when applied to the one-dimensional quartic oscillator with a one-dimensional model space, reproduces known low-lying energy eigenvalues and yields an infinite system of equations equivalent to the Hill determinant solution, demonstrating that the similarity transformation generator S is proportional to the expansion coefficients of the exact wavefunction in the unperturbed harmonic oscillator basis.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective Hamiltonian using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation for the one-dimensional quartic oscillator H = p²/(2m) + mω²x²/2 + λx⁴, with a one-dimensional model space containing only the harmonic oscillator ground state. They implemented an iterative solution for the generator S of the similarity transformation, truncating the Q-space to the first ten even-parity harmonic oscillator eigenstates, and computed ground state energies after seven iterations. They also derived a system of coupled equations for the matrix elements S_i = ⟨i|S|0⟩ and showed these are proportional to the wavefunction expansion coefficients α_i that satisfy the Hill determinant equations.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy for λ = 0.1: 1.06529 (after 6 iterations, with ℏ = 1, ω = 2, m = 0.5)
- Ground state energy for λ = 0.2: 1.11831 (after 6 iterations, same parameters)
- Ground state energy for λ = 0.3: 1.16407 (after 6 iterations, same parameters)
- Ground state energy for λ = 1.0: 1.39238 (after 6 iterations, same parameters)
- Ground state energy for λ = 2.0: 1.60757 (after 6 iterations, same parameters)
- Ground state energy for λ = 3.0: 1.76962 (after 6 iterations, same parameters)
- Truncating the Q-space to include only states up to i_max/2 = 10 (20 even-parity states) yields ground state energies agreeing with the iterative procedure to at least five significant figures for all λ values tested
- Varying the parameter Ω between -2 and +2 changes the obtained eigenvalues only at the fifth significant figure or beyond

## Context
This work extends the Lee-Suzuki effective operator methodology (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) from nuclear many-body applications to the well-studied non-perturbative problem of the one-dimensional quartic oscillator, whose perturbation expansion is known to diverge regardless of coupling strength (Bender and Wu, 1969). The authors demonstrate that the truncation of the Q-space, a key simplification in nuclear many-body calculations, is justified by showing that the S_i coefficients fall off rapidly for large i, making low-energy eigenvalues only weakly dependent on high-energy unperturbed states. This enables future applications of the effective operator method to other non-perturbative systems and provides a framework for understanding convergence properties with increasing cluster size and model space size in nuclear structure calculations.
