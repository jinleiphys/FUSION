# Femtoscopy between $π$, $K$ and $p$ in different heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 GeV

> Momentum correlation functions between $π$, $K$ and $p$ are calculated for several heavy-ion collision systems, namely $_{5}^{10}\textrm{B}+_{5}^{10}\textrm{B}$, $_{8}^{16}\textrm{O}+_{8}^{16}\textrm{O}$, $_{20}^{40}\textrm{Ca}+_{20}^{40}\textrm{Ca}$ and $_{79}^{197}\textrm{Au}+_{79}^{197}\textrm{Au}$ in central collisions as well as $_{79}^{197}\textrm{Au}+_{79}^{197}\textrm{Au}$ collision in different centralities at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 GeV within the framework of A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model complemented by the Lednick$\acute{y}$ and Lyuboshitz analytical method. The results present the centrality and system-size dependence of the momentum correlation functions among pairs of $π$, $K$ and $p$, from which the emission source-size can be deduced. It is found that the deduced source sizes increase with the decreasing of centrality for Au + Au system or with the increasing of system-size in central collisions with different nuclear size. In addition, through the momentum correlation functions of nonidentical particle pairs gated on velocity, the average emission sequence of non-identical particles can be indicated. The results illustrate that in the small relative momentum region, protons are emitted in average earlier than $π^+$ and $K^+$, and $K^+$ are emitted averagely earlier than $π^+$. Furthermore, it seems that larger interval of the average emission order among them is exhibited for smaller collision systems. The present study sheds light on the dynamics of light particle emission at RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that momentum correlation functions of π, K, and p pairs, calculated using the AMPT model with the Lednický and Lyuboshitz analytical method, can reveal the emission sequence of these particles in heavy-ion collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, with the hedge that the quantitative reproduction of experimental data is not the main concern and that the maximum rescattering time of 100 fm/c is a "basically safe choice" for the calculations.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with melting initial conditions to generate phase-space distributions of particles in B+B, O+O, Ca+Ca, and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV. They then applied the Lednický and Lyuboshitz analytical formalism to compute two-particle momentum correlation functions, incorporating quantum statistics and final-state interactions including Coulomb and strong interactions. The correlation functions were analyzed with gates on centrality, system size, total pair momentum, and particle velocity to extract source sizes and emission chronology.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radii used: 387 fm for pion-pion, 248.52 fm for pion-kaon, 222.56 fm for pion-proton, and 83.59 fm for kaon-proton pairs
- Maximum rescattering time of 400 fm/c was used for meson-meson pairs when comparing with STAR data, and 700 fm/c for proton-meson pairs
- Maximum rescattering time of 100 fm/c was fixed for the systematic calculations
- Five centrality classes were analyzed for Au+Au: 0-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, and 60-80%
- Four collision systems were studied in central collisions: 10B+10B, 16O+16O, 40Ca+40Ca, and 197Au+197Au

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of identical particles (pion-pion, kaon-kaon) to nonidentical particle pairs (pion-kaon, pion-proton, kaon-proton) at RHIC energy, building on prior work by the STAR collaboration (Zbroszczyk 2019, Siejka 2019) and theoretical frameworks by Lednický and Lyuboshitz (2006, 2008, 2009). The study enables systematic comparison of emission source sizes and emission chronology across different collision systems and centralities, complementing earlier blast wave model analyses by Liu et al. that suggested mass-dependent kinetic freeze-out scenarios. The results also connect to investigations of small-system physics at high multiplicity events in p+p, p+Pb, and d+Au collisions compared to Au+Au collisions (Nagle 2018).
