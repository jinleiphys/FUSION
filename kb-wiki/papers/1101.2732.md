# Three-Body Model Calculation of Spin Distribution in Two-Nucleon Transfer Reaction

> The differential cross sections of two-nucleon transfer reactions 238U(18O,16O)240U around 10 MeV per nucleon are calculated by one-step Born-approximation with a 16O+2n+238U three-body model. The three-body wave function in the initial channel is obtained with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method, and that in the final channel is evaluated with adiabatic approximation. The resulting cross sections have a peak around the grazing angle, and the spin distribution, i.e., the cross section at the peak as a function of the transferred spin, is investigated. The shape of the spin distribution is found not sensitive to the incident energies, optical potentials, and treatment of the breakup channels both in the initial and final states, while it depends on the excitation energy of the residual nucleus 240U. The peak of the spin distribution moves to the large-spin direction as the excitation energy increases. To fulfill the condition that the peak position should not exceeds 10 hbar, which is necessary for the surrogate ratio method to work, it is concluded that the excitation energy of 240U must be less than 10 MeV.

## Key claim

The spin distribution of the residual nucleus $^{240}$U populated in the $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{240}$U two-nucleon transfer reaction has a peak that shifts to larger spin values as the excitation energy of $^{240}$U increases; for the surrogate ratio method (SRM) to work, the excitation energy of $^{240}$U must be less than 10 MeV so that the peak position does not exceed 10 $\hbar$.

## Method

The differential cross sections of the $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{240}$U reaction at 180 MeV (10 MeV per nucleon) were calculated using a one-step Born approximation with a $^{16}$O+$^{2}n$+$^{238}$U three-body model. The three-body wave function in the initial channel was obtained with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), and that in the final channel was evaluated with the adiabatic approximation. Two neutrons were treated as a bound di-neutron particle, and the sequential two-nucleon transfer process was neglected.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross sections of $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{240}$U at 180 MeV show a bell-shaped angular distribution with a peak around $\theta \sim 35^\circ$ in the center-of-mass frame.
- The spin distribution of the cross section at the peak has a maximum at $J = 5$ (in units of $\hbar$) for the ground state of $^{240}$U (binding energy $\epsilon_{\rm B} = 10.74$ MeV).
- The total breakup cross section of $^{18}$O is very small, approximately 2.8 mb.
- For an excitation energy $E_{\rm ex} = 10.0$ MeV of $^{240}$U (corresponding to $\epsilon_{\rm B} = 0.74$ MeV), the peak of the spin distribution is located at $J \sim 9$.
- The spin distributions for $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{240}$U and $^{236}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{238}$U at 180 MeV show good agreement with each other.

## Context

This work extends the surrogate ratio method (SRM) proposed by Chiba and Iwamoto (2010), which requires that the spin distribution of the compound nucleus populated in a surrogate reaction have a peak below $J = 10$ for the method to be valid. The calculation provides the first quantitative evaluation of the spin distribution of $^{240}$U from a three-body reaction model, in contrast to previous studies by Esch (2006), Forsberg (2007), and Escher (2010) where spin-parity distributions were assumed rather arbitrarily. The results enable the selection of suitable surrogate reaction pairs for the SRM and establish that the $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,$^{16}$O)$^{240}$U reaction at around 10 MeV per nucleon is a promising candidate, with the excitation energy of the residual nucleus being the key parameter controlling the spin distribution.
