# Evolution of the $γ$-ray strength function in neodymium isotopes

> The experimental gamma-ray strength functions (gamma-SFs) of 142,144-151Nd have been studied for gamma-ray energies up to the neutron separation energy. The results represent a unique set of gamma-SFs for an isotopic chain with increasing nuclear deformation. The data reveal how the low-energy enhancement, the scissors mode and the pygmy dipole resonance evolve with nuclear deformation and mass number. The data indicate that the mechanisms behind the low-energy enhancement and the scissors mode are decoupled from each other.

## Key claim

The gamma-ray strength functions (gamma-SFs) of nine neodymium isotopes (142,144-151Nd) reveal that the low-energy enhancement (LEE) and the scissors mode (SM) evolve differently with nuclear deformation, indicating that the mechanisms behind these two structures are decoupled from each other, contrary to earlier speculation that they might be connected.

## Method

The Oslo method was applied to particle-gamma coincidence data from (p,p') and (d,p) reactions on stable neodymium targets at the Oslo Cyclotron Laboratory, using proton beams of 16.0 MeV and deuteron beams of 13.5 MeV. The gamma-ray detector arrays CACTUS (NaI(Tl) scintillators) and OSCAR (LaBr3(Ce) scintillators) were used. The spin distribution populated in the reactions was corrected using a reduction factor eta determined from sidefeeding into the rotational ground-state band of 150Nd and the recently developed shape method of Wiedeking (2021).

## Key numbers

*   The level-density reduction factor eta for the (p,p') reaction on neodymium isotopes was determined to be 0.22(2) at the neutron separation energy, based on sidefeeding analysis of 150Nd.
*   The quadrupole deformation beta_2 values for the even-mass neodymium isotopes range from 0.092(2) for 142Nd to 0.283(2) for 150Nd, taken from the compilation of Pritychenko (2016).
*   The neutron separation energies S_n for the studied isotopes range from 5.039 MeV (149Nd) to 9.828 MeV (142Nd).
*   The average neutron s-wave resonance spacing D_0 values used for normalization range from 3.0(10) eV for 150Nd to 450(50) eV for 145Nd.
*   The total level density at the neutron separation energy rho(S_n) ranges from 0.32(5) x 10^6 MeV^-1 for 144Nd to 4.8(18) x 10^6 MeV^-1 for 150Nd.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of gamma-ray strength functions along an isotopic chain from near-spherical (142Nd) to well-deformed (151Nd) nuclei, building on the Oslo method framework established by Guttormsen (1987, 1996) and Schiller (2000). The results provide a unique dataset that enables testing of theoretical models for the low-energy enhancement, scissors mode, and pygmy dipole resonance, and their dependence on deformation and mass number, complementing the nuclear level density study of the same isotopes by Guttormsen (2021).
