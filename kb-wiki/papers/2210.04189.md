# Revealing the short-range structure of the "mirror nuclei" $^3$H and $^3$He

> When protons and neutrons (nucleons) are bound into atomic nuclei, they are close enough together to feel significant attraction, or repulsion, from the strong, short-distance part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. These strong interactions lead to hard collisions between nucleons, generating pairs of highly-energetic nucleons referred to as short-range correlations (SRCs). SRCs are an important but relatively poorly understood part of nuclear structure and mapping out the strength and isospin structure (neutron-proton vs proton-proton pairs) of these virtual excitations is thus critical input for modeling a range of nuclear, particle, and astrophysics measurements. Hitherto measurements used two-nucleon knockout or ``triple-coincidence'' reactions to measure the relative contribution of np- and pp-SRCs by knocking out a proton from the SRC and detecting its partner nucleon (proton or neutron). These measurementsshow that SRCs are almost exclusively np pairs, but had limited statistics and required large model-dependent final-state interaction (FSI) corrections. We report on the first measurement using inclusive scattering from the mirror nuclei $^3$H and $^3$He to extract the np/pp ratio of SRCs in the A=3 system. We obtain a measure of the np/pp SRC ratio that is an order of magnitude more precise than previous experiments, and find a dramatic deviation from the near-total np dominance observed in heavy nuclei. This result implies an unexpected structure in the high-momentum wavefunction for $^3$He and $^3$H. Understanding these results will improve our understanding of the short-range part of the N-N interaction.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement using inclusive electron scattering from the mirror nuclei $^3$H and $^3$He to extract the np/pp short-range correlation (SRC) ratio in the A=3 system, finding a dramatic deviation from the near-total np dominance observed in heavier nuclei, with the np-SRC enhancement factor being significantly smaller than in heavier systems.

## Method

The experiment used inclusive A(e,e$^\prime$) scattering at Jefferson Lab Hall A with beam energies of 4.332 GeV at 17 degrees (Q$^2$ $\ge$ 1.4 GeV$^2$) and data from a second run at 20.88 degrees (Q$^2$ $\approx$ 1.9 GeV$^2$), covering the quasielastic scattering region at x $\ge$ 1.4 to isolate 2N-SRC contributions. Cross section ratios of $^3$H and $^3$He to $^2$H were measured, and the direct $^3$H/$^3$He ratio was used to extract the pp/np SRC ratio via an equation relating the cross section ratio to the elastic electron-proton to electron-neutron cross section ratio and the relative number of pp and np SRC pairs. The analysis avoided the large final-state interaction corrections required in previous two-nucleon knockout measurements by using inclusive scattering and comparing mirror nuclei with nearly identical structure but different proton-neutron asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* The $a_2$ value (relative SRC contribution compared to deuteron) for $^3$H is $1.784\pm0.016$ and for $^3$He is $2.088\pm0.026$, combining data at Q$^2$ = 1.4 and 1.9 GeV$^2$.
* The direct $^3$H/$^3$He cross section ratio in the SRC-dominated region ($1.4 < x < 1.7$) is $0.854\pm0.010$.
* The extracted pp/np SRC ratio is $R_{pp/np} = 0.230\pm0.023$, corresponding to an np/pp SRC ratio of $R_{np/pp} = 4.34^{+0.49}_{-0.40}$.
* The np-SRC enhancement factor (ratio of observed np/pp ratio to the pair-counting prediction) is $2.17^{+0.25}_{-0.20}$ for A=3.
* The average pp-SRC contribution from previous measurements on heavier nuclei is only $(2.9\pm0.5)\%$ that of np-SRCs.

## Context

This work extends previous inclusive SRC measurements on heavier nuclei (Fomin 2012, Schmookler 2019) and two-nucleon knockout measurements (Subedi 2008, Korover 2014, CLAS 2018) that found near-total np-SRC dominance, but with limited precision and large model-dependent final-state interaction corrections. The new result on A=3 shows a significantly smaller np enhancement than seen in heavier nuclei, contradicting the expectation from isospin symmetry and the tensor-force-driven np dominance observed in medium and heavy nuclei. This measurement enables improved constraints on the relative contributions of the short-range tensor interaction and the very short-range repulsive central core of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and impacts the interpretation of $^3$He as an effective polarized neutron target and extractions of the neutron structure function (MARATHON 2021).
