# Collective flows of clusters and pions in heavy-ion collisions at GeV energies

> Within the framework of the quantum molecular dynamics transport model, the collective flows of clusters and pions in heavy-ion collisions have been systematically investigated. The clusters are recognized by the Wigner phase-space density approach at the stage of freeze out in nuclear collisions, i.e., deuteron, triton, $^{3}$He and $α$. The directed and elliptic flows of protons and deuterons in the reaction of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at incident energy 1.23\emph{A} GeV are nicely consistent with the recent HADES data. The higher order collective flows, i.e., triangular and quadrangle flows, manifest the opposite trends with the less amplitude in comparison with the rapidity distributions of directed and elliptic flows. The flow structure of $^{3}$He and $α$ is very similar to the proton spectra. The influence of the pion potential on the pion production is systematically investigated and compared with the FOPI data via the transverse momentum, longitudinal rapidity and collective flows in collisions of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au. It is manifested that the pion yields are slightly suppressed in the domain of mid-rapidity and high momentum. The antiflow phenomena is reduced by implementing the pion potential and more consistent with the FOPI data in collisions of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at the incident energy 1.5\emph{A} GeV.

## Key claim

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, using the Wigner phase-space density approach for cluster recognition and including a pion potential, reproduces the directed and elliptic flows of protons and deuterons in 197Au+197Au collisions at 1.23A GeV consistently with HADES data, but the higher-order triangular and quadrangular flows show opposite trends and smaller amplitudes than the data, and the model's agreement with FOPI pion data requires the inclusion of an attractive pion potential to reduce the antiflow phenomenon.

## Method

The authors used the LQMD transport model to simulate 197Au+197Au collisions at 1.23A GeV and 1.5A GeV, with nucleon dynamics governed by Hamilton's equations under a Skyrme-type momentum-dependent potential. Clusters (deuteron, triton, 3He, alpha) were identified at freeze-out using the Wigner phase-space density method and a coalescence model with parameters P0 = 200 MeV/c and R0 = 3 fm, with secondary de-excitation handled by the GEMINI++ code. Pion production was modeled via resonance decays (Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535)) with a pion optical potential based on the Delta-hole model, and collective flow coefficients v1, v2, v3, v4 were extracted from Fourier decomposition of azimuthal distributions.

## Key numbers

- The compression modulus of isospin symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is K = 230 MeV, with saturation density rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is 31.5 MeV, with slope parameters L = 42, 82, and 139 MeV for soft, linear, and hard symmetry energy, respectively.
- The nucleon effective mass ratio m*/m = 0.75 in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density.
- The coalescence parameters used are P0 = 200 MeV/c and R0 = 3 fm.
- The root-mean-square radii for cluster Wigner densities are 1.61 fm for triton and 1.74 fm for 3He.
- The pion potential isospin coefficient C_pi = 36 MeV, with gamma_pi = 2.
- The pion-nucleon-Delta coupling constant f_Delta ~ 2, cutoff factor b ~ 7 m_pi, and Migdal parameter g' ~ 0.6.
- The minimum of the pion optical potential occurs near momentum p = 290 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of collective flows in heavy-ion collisions at GeV energies, particularly the HADES measurements of proton and deuteron flows (Ad20, Ad22) and FOPI measurements of pion flows (Re07). The authors compare their LQMD results with recent IQMD calculations (Fa23) that also investigated HADES data, and note that the Wigner phase-space density method underestimates alpha yields compared to FOPI data (Re10). The study enables further constraints on the nuclear equation of state at high baryon density and the high-density symmetry energy, which has applications to neutron star properties and binary neutron star mergers (La23).
