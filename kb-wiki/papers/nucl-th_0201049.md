# Ground State Properties of Many-Body Systems in the Two-Body Random Ensemble and Random Matrix Theory

> We explore generic ground-state and low-energy statistical properties of many-body bosonic and fermionic one- and two-body random ensembles (TBRE) in the dense limit, and contrast them with Random Matrix Theory (RMT). Weak differences in distribution tails can be attributed to the regularity or chaoticity of the corresponding Hamiltonians rather than the particle statistics. We finally show the universality of the distribution of the angular momentum gap between the lowest energy levels in consecutive J-sectors for the four models considered.

## Key claim

The ground-state energy distribution of many-body bosonic and fermionic systems in the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) is largely universal and similar to Random Matrix Theory (RMT) predictions, but the tails of these distributions depend on whether the underlying Hamiltonian is regular or chaotic rather than on particle statistics.

## Method

The authors computed the lowest eigenvalue distribution for four TBRE models: two bosonic models (U(4) vibron model and U(6) interacting boson model) and two fermionic models (single j-shell model and a model for randomly interacting electrons). They rescaled the distributions by recentering and normalizing by the standard deviation, then compared them to analytical GOE distributions for dimensions N=2 and N=infinity. They also computed the distribution of the spin gap, defined as the energy difference between the lowest levels in consecutive angular momentum sectors.

## Key numbers

- For the GOE, the average ground state energy follows ⟨E₀⟩ = 2.01(2)√N + 1.58(2)N^{-1/6}, fitting numerical results for N ∈ [2,1000]
- The U(6) IBM was studied with N_p=16 bosons, typical for a heavy collective nucleus
- The single j-shell model used N_p=6 particles in the j=15/2 shell
- The IEM was studied with up to N_p=7 fermions on K/2=7 orbitals
- For the IEM, chaotic behavior occurs when U/Δ ≫ 1/(K N_p²), with data shown for U/Δ=1 in the chaotic regime
- The IEM at half-filling (K=14, N_p=7) shows ground-state distribution closer to N=2 GOE despite having Wigner-Dyson spectral statistics

## Context

This work extends analytical results for the TBRE beyond the dilute limit, which was previously the only analytically tractable regime (Kota, 1980). The authors contrast their findings with RMT predictions from Wigner's Gaussian orthogonal ensemble and the Tracy-Widom distribution (Tracy and Widom, 1996). The universality of the spin gap distribution is attributed to the bosonic nature of the corresponding excitation in all four models, and this distribution cannot be addressed by RMT because it involves correlations between Hilbert subspaces with different quantum numbers. The results enable further study of generic low-energy properties of complex many-body systems such as molecules, atomic nuclei, and quantum dots.
