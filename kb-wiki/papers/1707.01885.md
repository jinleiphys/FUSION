# Regimes of the Pomeron and its Intrinsic Entropy

> We suggest that the perturbative and non-perturbative descriptions of the Pomeron can be viewed as complementary descriptions of different phases in the Pomeron phase diagram, with a phase boundary where the proper description of the produced systems are "string balls". Their intrinsic entropy is calculated and turned out to be the same, as the recently reported perturbative entanglement entropy. The distribution of large multiplicities stemming from the string balls is also wide, with its moments close to those reported for hadrons in $pp$ collisions at the LHC. At low-x, the quantum string is so entangled that sufficiently weak string self-attraction can cause it to turn to a string ball dual to a black hole. We suggest that low-x saturation occurs when the density of wee-strings reaches the Bekenstein bound, with a proton size that freezes with increasing rapidity. Some of these observations maybe checked at the future eIC.

## Key claim

The perturbative (BFKL) and non-perturbative (stringy BKYZ) descriptions of the Pomeron are complementary phases in a Pomeron phase diagram, with a phase boundary where the produced systems are "string balls" whose intrinsic entropy matches the perturbative entanglement entropy reported by Kharzeev and Levin (2017), provided the Pomeron intercept is changed appropriately.

## Method

The authors calculate the intrinsic entropy of the stringy BKYZ Pomeron by treating the exchanged string world-sheet ("tube") as a thermal system with an effective temperature derived from the Unruh temperature associated with the accelerated string endpoints. They derive the thermal free energy from the transverse partition function of the tube, then obtain the entropy via the thermodynamic relation S = β² ∂/∂β (-ln K_T / β). For inelastic collisions, they argue the entropy is half that of the elastic amplitude, giving S_inelastic = χ Δ(λ). They also propose a density of "string bits" (wee-strings) using a holographic extension with an AdS wall, satisfying a diffusion equation analogous to the BFKL cascade.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic Hagedorn temperature for the Pomeron is T_H ~ 160 MeV, lower than the Yang-Mills Hagedorn temperature of ~270 MeV.
- The effective temperature of the string world-sheet is given by 1/T = β = 2πb/χ, where b is the impact parameter and χ = ln(s/s₀) is the relative rapidity.
- The Pomeron intercept Δ = D_⟂/12 - D_⟂ Σ_{n=1}^∞ n/(e^{2χn} - 1) → D_⟂/12 at large χ, with D_⟂ = 2 or 3 depending on the setting.
- The total number of string bits grows as N = e^{Δ(λ)χ}.
- The experimental moments of the hadron multiplicity distribution at LHC are C₂_exp = 2.0 ± 0.05, C₃_exp = 5.9 ± 0.6, C₄_exp = 21 ± 2, C₅_exp = 90 ± 19, compared to theoretical predictions C₂ ≈ 1.83, C₃ ≈ 5.0, C₄ ≈ 18.2, C₅ ≈ 83.
- At LHC energies, the transition region in impact parameter is between b = 0.5 and 1 fm, compared to the typical impact parameter √(σ_tot/π) ≈ 1.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Shuryak and Zahed, 2013) which identified three regimes of the Pomeron in elastic pp collisions (cold string, near-critical string, and over-excited string) and showed they are visible in LHC data. It connects the stringy BKYZ Pomeron (Basar, Kharzeev, Yee, and Zahed, 2012) with the perturbative BFKL Pomeron (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, and Lipatov, 1977) by showing they produce the same intrinsic entropy, and relates the string ball concept (Kalaydzhyan, 2014; Qian, 2015) to the Hagedorn regime. The work enables future checks at the electron-ion collider (eIC) and suggests that low-x saturation occurs when the density of wee-strings reaches the Bekenstein bound, with a proton size that freezes with increasing rapidity.
