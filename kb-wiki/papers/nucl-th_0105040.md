# Flow analysis from multiparticle azimuthal correlations

> We present a new method for analyzing directed and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions. Unlike standard methods, it separates the contribution of flow to azimuthal correlations from contributions due to other effects. The separation relies on a cumulant expansion of multiparticle azimuthal correlations, and includes corrections for detector inefficiencies. This new method allows the measurement of the flow of identified particles in narrow phase-space regions, and can be used in every regime, from intermediate to ultrarelativistic energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for measuring directed and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions that, unlike standard methods, systematically separates the contribution of flow to azimuthal correlations from nonflow correlations (such as resonance decays or momentum conservation) using a cumulant expansion of multiparticle azimuthal correlations, and it works even when nonflow correlations are of the same magnitude as flow correlations.

## Method

The method constructs a generating function of multiparticle azimuthal correlations for each event, averages it over events, and then defines cumulants via a transformation of this averaged generating function. The cumulants of order 2k (involving 2k particles) are extracted numerically from the generating function using interpolation formulas, and these cumulants are related to the flow coefficients v_n through equations that eliminate contributions from lower-order nonflow correlations. The analysis includes corrections for detector inefficiencies and partial azimuthal coverage, and can be applied to both integrated flow (averaged over a large phase-space region) and differential flow (for identified particles in narrow phase-space windows).

## Key numbers

- The condition for flow to be measurable using cumulants of order 2k is v_n ≫ 1/M, where M is the event multiplicity (this is the necessary condition for any flow measurement).
- The systematic error from nonflow correlations on the estimate v_n{2k} scales as δv_n{2k} ∼ (M v_n)^{1-2k}, becoming smaller as k increases.
- The statistical error on v_n{2k} scales as 1/√(M^{2k} N_evts), where N_evts is the number of events.
- The optimal cumulant order is given by 2k_opt ≈ 2 + (ln N_evts)/(ln M); in most practical cases, the fourth-order cumulant (2k=4) is preferred.
- When the dimensionless parameter χ ≡ v_n √M is much larger than 1, the statistical error becomes δ(v_n{2k})_stat = 1/√(2M N_evts), independent of the cumulant order.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cumulant-based method (Borghini 2001) by introducing a new generating function that eliminates interference from higher harmonics (e.g., v_{2n}, v_{3n}) that could hinder measurements of directed flow when elliptic flow is large, as expected at RHIC energies (Snellings 2000). The method supersedes the standard two-particle correlation approach of Danielewicz and Odyniec (1985) and the subevent method of Poskanzer (1998), which cannot systematically separate flow from nonflow correlations; it also improves on earlier multiparticle methods (Jiang 1992) that applied only to integrated flow. The technique enables reliable differential flow measurements of identified particles (kaons, Λ hyperons, antiprotons) in narrow phase-space regions, even at SPS energies where nonflow correlations are as large as flow correlations (Dinh 2000, Borghini 2000).
