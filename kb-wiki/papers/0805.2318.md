# Liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter from realistic many-body approaches

> The existence of a liquid-gas phase transition for hot nuclear systems at subsaturation densities is a well established prediction of finite temperature nuclear many-body theory. In this paper, we discuss for the first time the properties of such phase transition for homogeneous nuclear matter within the Self-Consistent Green's Functions approach. We find a substantial decrease of the critical temperature with respect to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. Even within the same approximation, the use of two different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions gives rise to large differences in the properties of the critical point.

# Liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter from realistic many-body approaches

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter depends strongly on both the many-body approximation and the nucleon-nucleon interaction used; the Self-Consistent Green's Functions (SCGF) approach yields substantially lower critical temperatures than the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation, and within the same approximation, the Argonne V18 and CDBONN interactions produce critical temperatures differing by up to 6.5 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature SCGF ladder approximation self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter, using the Luttinger-Ward formalism to compute the free energy from the Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sum-rule for the internal energy and the dynamical quasi-particle entropy. They compared these results with the finite-temperature BHF approximation, which omits hole-hole propagation and uses a mean-field entropy, for two realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: the Argonne V18 and the CDBONN potentials. The liquid-gas coexistence curves were obtained via the Maxwell construction, and the spinodal region was determined from the mechanical stability condition.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for CDBONN in BHF: T_c = 23.3 MeV
- Critical temperature for CDBONN in SCGF: T_c = 18.5 MeV
- Critical temperature for Argonne V18 in BHF: T_c = 18.1 MeV
- Critical temperature for Argonne V18 in SCGF: T_c = 11.6 MeV
- Flashing temperatures for SCGF are about 3 MeV lower than for BHF with the same potential
- Flashing temperatures for Argonne V18 are about 4-5 MeV lower than for CDBONN within the same approximation
- The SCGF approach yields thermodynamic consistency with less than 1 MeV error in the chemical potential over a wide density range, while BHF violations can exceed 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature nuclear matter studies by applying the fully self-consistent SCGF approach to the liquid-gas phase transition for the first time, whereas earlier realistic calculations used the BHF approximation (Baldo 1999, 2004), the Bloch-de Dominicis formalism (Baldo 2004), or variational methods with frozen correlations (Friedman and Pandharipande 1981; Kanzawa 2007). The SCGF results for CDBONN (T_c = 18.5 MeV) agree closely with the Bloch-de Dominicis result of 18 MeV for the Bonn B potential (Baldo 2004), but the Argonne V18 SCGF result (T_c = 11.6 MeV) is substantially lower than most previous estimates, which range from 16-22 MeV. This work enables future studies to incorporate three-body forces and to assess whether experimental constraints from heavy-ion collisions can discriminate between different many-body approaches and interactions.
