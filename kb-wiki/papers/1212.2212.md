# Features and flaws of a contact interaction treatment of the kaon

> Elastic and semileptonic transition form factors for the kaon and pion are calculated using the leading-order in a global-symmetry-preserving truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations and a momentum-independent form for the associated kernels in the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The computed form factors are compared both with those obtained using the same truncation but an interaction that preserves the one-loop renormalisation-group behaviour of QCD and with data. The comparisons show that: in connection with observables revealed by probes with |Q^2|<~ M^2, where M~0.4GeV is an infrared value of the dressed-quark mass, results obtained using a symmetry-preserving regularisation of the contact-interaction are not realistically distinguishable from those produced by more sophisticated kernels; and available data on kaon form factors do not extend into the domain whereupon one could distinguish between the interactions. The situation is different if one includes the domain Q^2>M^2. Thereupon, a fully consistent treatment of the contact interaction produces form factors that are typically harder than those obtained with QCD renormalisation-group-improved kernels. Amongst other things also described are a Ward identity for the inhomogeneous scalar vertex, similarity between the charge distribution of a dressed-u-quark in the K^+ and that of the dressed-u-quark in the pi^+, and reflections upon the point whereat one might begin to see perturbative behaviour in the pion form factor. Interpolations of the form factors are provided, which should assist in working to chart the interaction between light-quarks by explicating the impact on hadron properties of differing assumptions about the behaviour of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for momentum transfers \(|Q^2| \lesssim M^2\) (where \(M \approx 0.4\) GeV is an infrared dressed-quark mass), a symmetry-preserving contact interaction yields kaon and pion form factors that are not realistically distinguishable from those produced by more sophisticated QCD renormalisation-group-improved kernels, but that the two approaches diverge markedly for \(Q^2 > M^2\), where the contact interaction produces harder form factors.

## Method

The authors computed elastic and semileptonic transition form factors for the kaon and pion using the leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, with a momentum-independent (contact) interaction kernel in both the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations. They compared these results with those obtained using the same truncation but with a momentum-dependent interaction that preserves the one-loop renormalisation-group behaviour of QCD, and with experimental data, providing interpolating functions for the computed form factors.

## Key numbers

- \(M \approx 0.4\) GeV: the infrared value of the dressed-quark mass that defines the domain \(|Q^2| \lesssim M^2\) where contact-interaction and QCD-improved results are indistinguishable.
- Computed charged-kaon charge radius: \(r_{K^+} = 2.00\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\) (computed \(m_\rho\)).
- Computed pion charge radius: \(r_{\pi^+} = 2.12\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Computed neutral-kaon radius magnitude: \(|r_{K^0}| = 0.78\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Computed \(K_{\ell 3}\) transition radius: \(r_{K\pi} = 1.79\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Empirical charged-kaon radius: \(r_{K^+} = 2.20 \pm 0.12\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Empirical pion radius: \(r_{\pi^+} = 2.64 \pm 0.03\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Empirical \(K_{\ell 3}\) transition radius: \(r_{K\pi} = 2.14 \pm 0.07\) in units of \(1/m_\rho\).
- Interpolation for charged-kaon elastic form factor on \(x = Q^2/m_\rho^2 \in (-1, 10]\): \(F_K(x) = (1 + 1.112 x + 0.228 x^2) / (1 + 1.778 x + 0.780 x^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of \(u,d\)-quark systems (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2010, 2011, Wilson 2011) and a recent meson and baryon spectrum calculation (Chen 2012) to include kaon and pion elastic and transition form factors. It enables future computation of baryon form factors containing strange quarks, since diquark correlations (mathematically similar to mesons) are required inputs for the Faddeev equation description of baryons. The paper also provides a detailed Ward identity analysis for the inhomogeneous scalar vertex and demonstrates that existing kaon form factor data do not extend to momentum transfers large enough to distinguish between different interaction models.
