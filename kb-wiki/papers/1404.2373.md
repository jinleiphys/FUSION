# Ground-state properties of neutron-rich Mg isotopes

> We analyze recently-measured total reaction cross sections for 24-38Mg isotopes incident on 12C targets at 240 MeV/nucleon by using the folding model and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The folding model well reproduces the measured reaction cross sections, when the projectile densities are evaluated by the deformed Woods-Saxon (def-WS) model with AMD deformation. Matter radii of 24-38Mg are then deduced from the measured reaction cross sections by fine-tuning the parameters of the def-WS model. The deduced matter radii are largely enhanced by nuclear deformation. Fully-microscopic AMD calculations with no free parameter well reproduce the deduced matter radii for 24-36Mg, but still considerably underestimate them for 37,38Mg. The large matter radii suggest that 37,38Mg are candidates for deformed halo nucleus. AMD also reproduces other existing measured ground-state properties (spin-parity, total binding energy, and one-neutron separation energy) of Mg isotopes. Neutron-number (N) dependence of deformation parameter is predicted by AMD. Large deformation is seen from 31Mg with N = 19 to a drip-line nucleus 40Mg with N = 28, indicating that both the N = 20 and 28 magicities disappear. N dependence of neutron skin thickness is also predicted by AMD.

## Key claim

The folding model with deformed Woods-Saxon densities using AMD deformation parameters reproduces measured total reaction cross sections for 24-38Mg on 12C at 240 MeV/nucleon, but fully-microscopic AMD calculations still considerably underestimate the deduced matter radii for 37,38Mg, suggesting these isotopes are candidates for deformed halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyzed total reaction cross sections for 24-38Mg isotopes incident on 12C targets at 240 MeV/nucleon using the folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Projectile densities were evaluated using the deformed Woods-Saxon model with deformation parameters determined from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations using the Gogny-D1S interaction. Matter radii were deduced from measured reaction cross sections by fine-tuning either the potential depth or deformation parameter in the def-WS model, using the linear relation between rms matter radius and reaction cross section.

## Key numbers

- Matter radius of 24Mg: 3.03 +/- 0.08 fm
- Matter radius of 37Mg: 3.62 +/- 0.03 fm (present analysis) or 3.65 +0.09/-0.05 fm (previous analysis)
- Matter radius of 38Mg: 3.60 +/- 0.04 fm
- AMD deformation parameter beta_2 = 0.433 for 24Mg and beta_2 = 0.362 for 37Mg
- Normalization factor F = 0.982 applied to folding model to reproduce mean experimental data for 12C
- One-neutron separation energy for 37Mg: 0.16 +/- 0.68 MeV (empirical value from Audi 2012)

## Context

This work extends the folding model analysis previously applied to Ne isotopes by Minomo (2011) and Takechi (2012) to the Mg isotopic chain, where Takechi had suggested 37Mg is a halo nucleus. The results confirm that both N=20 and N=28 magicities disappear in the Mg region, with large deformation seen from 31Mg (N=19) to 40Mg (N=28). The AMD predictions for deformation parameters and neutron skin thickness provide a framework for future experimental tests, particularly for the halo candidates 37Mg and 38Mg.
