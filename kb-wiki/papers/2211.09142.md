# Maximum entropy freezeout of hydrodynamic fluctuations

> We propose a general approach to freezing out fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using the principle of maximum entropy. We find the results naturally expressed as a direct relationship between the irreducible relative correlators quantifying the deviations of hydrodynamic as well as hadron gas fluctuations from the ideal hadron gas baseline. The method also allows us to determine heretofore unknown parameters crucial for the freezeout of fluctuations near the QCD critical point in terms of the QCD equation of state.

# Maximum entropy freezeout of hydrodynamic fluctuations

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the principle of maximum entropy can provide a general method for freezing out hydrodynamic fluctuations into hadron gas fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, with the results expressed as a direct relationship between irreducible relative correlators. The method claims to determine previously unknown parameters for freezeout of fluctuations near the QCD critical point in terms of the QCD equation of state, but the authors note this is limited to the linearized limit where correlations relative to the ideal hadron gas are sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors formulate a variational problem where the entropy of fluctuations (an object mathematically similar to the n-PI action in quantum field theory) is maximized subject to constraints that connect hydrodynamic correlators H_n to hadron gas correlators G_n via the relation H^{ab...} = integral over phase space of G_{AB...} P_A^a P_B^b ... . They solve this constrained optimization using Lagrange multipliers, obtaining equations for G_2, G_3, and G_4 that can be solved iteratively, and then linearize these equations to obtain a compact relationship between irreducible relative correlators (IRCs) on the hydrodynamic and hadron gas sides.

## Key numbers

- For the critical mode coupling to particles at zero momentum, the estimated values are: hat g_{p,0} approximately -3.1, hat g_{pi,0} approximately 0.18, hat g_{anti-p,0} approximately 5.5, using parameters mu_c = 350 MeV, T_c = 143.2 MeV, xi_0 = 1 fm from Refs. Parotto (2018) and Karthein (2021)
- The parameter Z is expressed as Z = limit of (c_p T)/(n^2 (T xi)^(2-eta)) as T,mu approach T_c,mu_c, with explicit formula involving M_0, h_0, w, alpha_1, alpha_2, and xi_0
- The hadron gas contribution to fluctuations of specific entropy m is given by (bar H^{-1})_{mm} = n^2 / bar c_p

## Context

This work extends the maximum entropy freezeout approach previously applied to mean hydrodynamic variables by Everett (2021) to the problem of freezing out fluctuations, which had been addressed only partially for specific cases such as charge diffusion by Ling (2013) and De (2020). The method generalizes the procedure of subtracting ideal gas contributions before applying freezeout smearing, which was done for two-point correlators by Ling (2013) and De (2020), to higher-order non-Gaussian correlators. The results enable implementation of fluctuation freezeout in Hydro+ calculations (Stephanov 2017, Rajagopal 2019, Du 2020) and in full hydrodynamic fluctuation equations (An 2019, An 2020), and can be applied to Beam Energy Scan data from STAR.
