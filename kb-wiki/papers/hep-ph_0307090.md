# Geometric Scaling in Inclusive Charm Production

> We show that the cross section for inclusive charm production exhibits geometric scaling in a large range of photon virtualities. In the HERA kinematic domain the saturation momentum $Q_{sat}^2(x)$ stays below the hard scale $μ_c^2=4m_c^2$, implying charm production probing mostly the color transparency regime and unitarization effects being almost negligible. We derive our results considering two saturation models which are able to describe the DESY ep collider HERA data for the proton structure function at small values of the Bjorken variable $x$. A striking feature is the scaling on $τ=Q_2^2/Q_{sat}^2(x)$ above saturation limit, corroborating recent theoretical studies.

# Geometric Scaling in Inclusive Charm Production

## Key claim
The cross section for inclusive charm production in deep inelastic scattering at HERA exhibits geometric scaling as a function of the single variable τ = Q²/Q²_sat(x) over a large range of photon virtualities, even though the saturation scale Q²_sat(x) remains below the hard scale μ²_c = 4m²_c, meaning charm production probes mostly the color transparency regime where unitarization effects are almost negligible.

## Method
The authors analyze HERA data on inclusive charm production using the color dipole formalism, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair that scatters off the proton. They apply two saturation models: the phenomenological Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff (GBW) model, where the dipole cross section depends on Q_sat r_perp, and the symmetric saturation model of Munier, which describes the proton as a collection of independent dipoles with sizes distributed around 1/Q_sat. For the symmetric model, they derive an analytical expression for the charm production cross section as a function of τ, with parameters fixed from fits to the proton structure function F₂ data.

## Key numbers
- The saturation momentum Q²_sat at HERA is approximately 1 GeV², staying below the hard scale μ²_c = 4m²_c ≈ 10 GeV² (for charm mass m_c = 1.5 GeV).
- The HERA data analyzed cover the range 1.5 ≤ Q² ≤ 130 GeV².
- The transition point in τ behavior occurs at τ ≈ 10, corresponding to μ²_c/Q²_sat.
- The cross section at large τ (Q² ≫ μ²_c) scales approximately as 1/τ, specifically as σ_T^c ∼ (σ₀ Q²_sat(x)/Q²) (1 + ln(Q²/μ²_c)).
- At small τ (Q² ≪ μ²_c), the cross section scales as σ_T^c ∼ σ₀ Q²_sat(x)/μ²_c, giving a smooth, τ-independent behavior.
- The symmetric saturation model prediction uses a flavor-blind coupling with normalization factor N_{c\bar{c}} = (2/5)N, where 2/5 corresponds to the charm charge fraction e²_c/(∑ e²_{u,d,s} + e²_c).

## Context
This work extends the concept of geometric scaling, previously established for the total proton structure function F₂ at small x (x ≤ 0.01, Q² ≤ 400 GeV²) by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2001), to the specific channel of inclusive charm production. It corroborates theoretical studies by Iancu, Itakura, and Munier (2002) showing that geometric scaling predicted at low momenta is preserved by BFKL evolution up to relatively large virtualities, and by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kwiecinski (2001) showing DGLAP evolution approximately preserves scaling at very small x. The results enable further investigations of geometric scaling in nuclear heavy quark photoproduction, as the authors note similar behavior is expected in the nuclear case.
