# Understanding the structure of nucleon excitations from their wavefunctions

> Relativistic wavefunctions of nucleon excitations are scrutinised to understand their node structure and the underlying role of local interpolating fields in generating the nucleon spectrum. In addressing quark model perspectives, approximately 4000 propagators are employed on the heaviest PACS-CS ensemble at $m_π\simeq$ 702 MeV. We examine the ground and four lowest-lying excited states at zero momentum for both positive- and negative-parity spectra, where the proton's d-quark wavefunction is calculated about the two u quarks at the origin. This is achieved using two local interpolating fields that each carry the quantum numbers of the nucleon but with differing spin-flavour structures, one of which vanishes in the nonrelativistic limit. We find that two distinct types of wavefunction nodes are manifest: "superposition nodes" formed through a linear combination of interpolating fields, and novel "built-in nodes" that are fundamentally built in to the s-wave Dirac components of an individual interpolating field. These are investigated qualitatively through visualisations in the form of both volume and surface renderings, and quantitatively by the calculation of radial wavefunctions. Combined, these findings build a comprehensive picture of the single-particle nucleon spectrum and how its properties derive from fundamental lattice operators.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two distinct types of wavefunction nodes in nucleon excitations: "superposition nodes" arising from linear combinations of interpolating fields, and novel "built-in nodes" that are fundamentally part of the s-wave Dirac components of an individual interpolating field, with the latter representing a new mechanism not previously described in lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure.

## Method

The authors perform a correlation-matrix variational analysis using a 10x10 matrix of two local nucleon interpolating fields (scalar-diquark χ₁ and pseudoscalar-diquark χ₂) at five levels of gauge-invariant Gaussian smearing on 399 PACS-CS gauge configurations with lattice volume 32³ × 64 at m_π ≃ 702 MeV. The d-quark wavefunction is calculated about two u quarks fixed at the origin in Landau gauge, with the wavefunction extracted as a 4×4 Dirac matrix from the overlap of a shifted annihilation operator with energy eigenstates obtained via the variational method.

## Context

This work extends the earlier wavefunction analysis of Roberts (2013) which examined only the scalar-diquark interpolator at four smearing levels, finding a typical node structure of zero nodes in the ground state and one node per excitation. By including the pseudoscalar-diquark interpolator χ₂ (which vanishes in the nonrelativistic limit) and a larger variational basis, the authors reveal that negative-parity states exist in χ₁/χ₂ pairs with the χ₂-dominated state sitting slightly lower, while positive-parity excitations also occur in pairs but with the χ₁-dominated state lower. The findings enable future studies of nonzero momentum wavefunctions and comparisons with other gauges such as Coulomb gauge and maximal centre gauge.
