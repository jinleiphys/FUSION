# Isospin Recoupling and Bose-Einstein Pion Correlations in Nucleon-Antinucleon Annihilations

> We study the effect of isospin projections on a two source picture of coherent pions coming from nucleon-antinucleon annihilation. We show that important Hanbury-Brown Twiss correlations among like charge pion pairs compared with unlike arise from these isospin projections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that careful isospin projections in a two-source coherent pion model for nucleon-antinucleon annihilation produce strong Hanbury-Brown Twiss (H-BT) correlations among like-charge pion pairs compared with unlike-charge pairs, without requiring thermal or random phase assumptions. The authors previously reported this result using a simpler isospin averaging treatment, and this note provides the detailed calculation substantiating that the central results do not change under more rigorous isospin projection.

## Method

The authors construct a coherent state for pion radiation from two spatially separated sources, each emitting pions with fixed isospin \(I_1\) and \(I_2\) coupled to a total isospin \(I\) and z-component \(M\). They compute the one-pion and two-pion spectra using isospin projection formalism with coherent states, then evaluate the integrals in the large-\(N_0\) (mean pion number) limit, which reduces the problem to angular integrals over isospin directions. They restrict all isospins to be less than or equal to 1 and calculate the coefficients \(C\) and \(D\) in the two-pion correlation function \(W_2 = C + D \cos[\Delta E(t_1-t_2) - \vec{Q}\cdot(\vec{x}_1-\vec{x}_2)]\) for various isospin configurations, including diagonal (\(I=I'\)) and off-diagonal terms relevant for the \(\overline{p}p\) initial state.

## Key numbers

* For the diagonal case \(I=I'=0\) with \(I_1=I_2=0\), the one-pion spectrum gives \(W_1(+)=W_1(-)=W_1(0)=2/3\;|f(k)|^2\).
* For the diagonal case \(I=I'=0\) with \(I_1=I_2=0\), the ratio \(H\) in the like-to-unlike ratio \(W_2(+,+)/W_2(+,-) = 1 + H \cos[\Delta E(t_1-t_2)-\vec{Q}\cdot(\vec{x}_1-\vec{x}_2)]\) is \(H=1/3\).
* For the diagonal case \(I=I'=1\) with \(I_1=I_2=0\), the ratio \(H\) is \(H=1/5\).
* For the diagonal case \(I=I'=1\) with \(I_1=1, I_2=0\), the ratio \(H\) is \(H=1/3\).
* For the diagonal case \(I=I'=1\) with \(I_1=I_2=1\), the ratio \(H\) is \(H=1/3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Amado et al., 1994) which introduced the two-source coherent pion picture for nucleon-antinucleon annihilation and treated isospin via a simpler averaging procedure. The present paper provides the rigorous isospin projection calculation that confirms the earlier results, showing that the Hanbury-Brown Twiss correlations among like-charge pions survive under proper isospin recoupling. The formalism uses coherent states and isospin projection techniques previously developed by the same group (Amado et al., 1993; Amado et al., 1994). The results enable further studies of pion correlations in \(\overline{p}p\) annihilation, particularly for understanding how initial state distortions that make the relative phase between isospin 0 and 1 amplitudes complex could affect the correlation patterns.
