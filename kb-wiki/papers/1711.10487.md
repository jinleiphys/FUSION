# Anomaly matching for phase diagram of massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD

> We elucidate that the phase diagram of massless $N$-flavor QCD under $\mathbb{Z}_N$ flavor-twisted boundary condition (massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD) is constrained by an 't Hooft anomaly involving two-form gauge fields. As a consequence, massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD turns out to realize persistent order at any temperatures and quark chemical potentials, namely, the symmetric and gapped phase is strictly forbidden. This is the first result on the finite-$(T,μ)$ phase diagram in QCD-type theories based on anomaly matching related to center and discrete axial symmetries.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of massless $N$-flavor QCD under a $\mathbb{Z}_N$ flavor-twisted boundary condition (massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD) is constrained by a mixed 't Hooft anomaly involving two-form gauge fields, which forbids a symmetric and gapped phase at any temperature and quark chemical potential. This is the first result applying anomaly matching related to center and discrete axial symmetries to a finite-temperature and finite-chemical-potential phase diagram in QCD-type theories.

## Method

The authors compute the mixed 't Hooft anomaly of four-dimensional massless $N$-flavor QCD by introducing background gauge fields for the vector-like symmetry $SU(N)_\mathrm{f} \times U(1)_\mathrm{q} / ((\mathbb{Z}_N)_{\mathrm{cq}} \times (\mathbb{Z}_N)_{\mathrm{fq}})$ and for the discrete axial symmetry $(\mathbb{Z}_{2N})_{\mathrm{axial}}$, using the Fujikawa method to evaluate the change of the fermion measure under a $(\mathbb{Z}_{2N})_{\mathrm{axial}}$ transformation. They then compactify the theory on a circle of size $L$ with a $\mathbb{Z}_N$ flavor-twisted boundary condition (including a quark chemical potential $\mu$) to obtain massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD, and derive the resulting three-dimensional anomaly by dimensionally reducing the four-dimensional anomaly expression.

## Key numbers

* The mixed 't Hooft anomaly for four-dimensional massless $N$-flavor QCD is $\mathcal{A} = -\frac{N}{2\pi} \int B_\mathrm{c} \wedge B_\mathrm{f}$, where $B_\mathrm{c}$ and $B_\mathrm{f}$ are $\mathbb{Z}_N$ two-form gauge fields.
* The anomaly for massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD is $\mathcal{A} = -\frac{N}{2\pi} \int B_\mathrm{c}^{(1)} \wedge B_\mathrm{f}^{(2)} \in \frac{2\pi}{N} \mathbb{Z}$, where $B_\mathrm{c}^{(1)}$ is a one-form gauge field for the $(\mathbb{Z}_N)_{\mathrm{shift,cq}}$ symmetry and $B_\mathrm{f}^{(2)}$ is a two-form gauge field.
* The gluon contribution to the one-loop effective potential of the color Polyakov loop at high temperature is $V_{\mathrm{gl}}(\Phi) = -\frac{2}{\pi^2 L^4} \sum_{n\ge 1} \frac{1}{n^4} \left( |\mathrm{tr}_\mathrm{c}(\Phi^n)|^2 - 1 \right)$, of order $O(N^2)$.
* The quark contribution to the one-loop effective potential at $\mu=0$ is $V_{\mathrm{qk}}(\Phi) = \frac{2 N^{-3}}{\pi^2 L^4} \sum_{n\ge 1} \frac{1}{n^4} \left( e^{i n N \phi} \mathrm{tr}_\mathrm{c}(\Phi^{N n}) + \mathrm{h.c.} \right)$, of order $O(N^{-2})$.
* For $N\ge 3$, the anomaly matching implies the inequality $T_{\mathrm{chiral}}(\mu) \ge T_{\mathrm{deconf}}(\mu)$ at any chemical potential $\mu$, where $T_{\mathrm{chiral}}$ is the chiral restoration temperature and $T_{\mathrm{deconf}}$ is the deconfinement temperature.

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft anomaly matching technique, previously applied to condensed matter systems (Haldane conjecture, deconfined quantum criticality) and to lower-dimensional QFTs, to the finite-temperature and finite-density phase diagram of QCD. It builds on the concept of $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD introduced by Kouno (2012), Sakai (2012), and others, and uses the dimensional reduction technique from Tanizaki (2017) to preserve the anomaly upon compactification. The result rules out the symmetric gapped phase that appeared in effective model studies by Kouno (2015), and provides a rigorous constraint that can be tested by lattice QCD simulations (Iritani, 2015) and guides future work on cold dense QCD, including the possibility of chiral symmetry breaking at all densities in the zero-temperature limit.
