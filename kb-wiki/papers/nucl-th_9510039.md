# Antiprotonic studies of nuclear neutron haloes

> Nuclear capture of antiprotons from atomic states is studied. Partial widths for single nucleon capture events leading to cold residual nuclei are calculated. Recent CERN experiments that compare the neutron and proton captures are analysed. Nuclear density distributions at extreme nuclear surface are calculated and tested against the experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that antiproton absorption occurs approximately 3.0 fm beyond the nuclear half-density radius, significantly further out than earlier hadronic atom experiments, and that the measured (N-1)/(Z-1) ratios can be interpreted in terms of neutron haloes or neutron skins at the extreme nuclear surface, provided the uncertainties in the N̄N interaction range and final-state meson interactions are properly quantified.

## Method

The authors calculated partial absorption widths for antiproton capture on individual nucleons leading to cold residual nuclei (excited below the neutron emission threshold) using an optical potential model for the antiproton-nucleus interaction combined with eikonal descriptions of the final-state pions. Nuclear density distributions were computed using three approaches: an asymptotic density model following the Bethe-Siemens method, self-consistent Hartree-Fock theory with Skyrme-type interactions, and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov methods. The calculated absorption densities were weighted by a "missing probability" P_miss that accounts for the probability that annihilation mesons do not excite the residual nucleus, and a "deep hole" factor P_dh that limits the sum over initial nucleon states to those leaving excitation energies below the neutron separation threshold plus 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton absorption occurs around 3.0 fm beyond the nuclear half-density radius, compared to roughly 2.0 fm for earlier hadronic atom experiments.
- The phenomenological N̄N scattering length t_N̄N extracted from antiprotonic atom data is approximately -1.5 - i2.5 fm, yielding an imaginary optical potential of about 200 MeV at the nuclear centre.
- The average pion multiplicity from N̄N annihilation is 4-5 mesons, with multiplicities ranging from 2 to 8.
- For a typical N̄N absorption range r_0 = 1 fm, the finite-range correction factor f_X takes values of 0.5-0.6, nearly constant in the surface region.
- The missing probability P_miss(R) changes by less than 10 percent when two-nucleon pion absorption is included.
- The absorption density for cold (A-1) residual nuclei is shifted to the periphery by almost 1 fm compared to the full absorption density.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic atoms by Bugrov (1973), Davies (1967), and others that used X-ray cascade measurements and meson detection to probe nuclear surfaces at roughly 2.0 fm beyond the half-density radius. The paper provides the theoretical framework for interpreting the CERN antiproton experiments of Lubinski (1994), which discovered large neutron excess at the nuclear surface of several heavy nuclei, complementing similar findings from subcoulomb neutron pickup reactions by Korner (1971). The results enable quantitative extraction of neutron halo and neutron skin properties from antiprotonic atom data, though the authors note that the method is limited by uncertainties in the N̄N interaction range and the semi-quantitative nature of the missing probability calculations.
