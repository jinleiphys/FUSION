# Asymptotics of the nucleus ground-state and single-hole nature of the bound states of the nuclei

> We consider nuclei composed of nucleons which interact via two-body potentials decreasing exponentially at infinity. Protons and neutrons are not distinguished in order to simplify notations. The basic result is the rigorous mathematical proof that the bound eigenstates of the nuclei belong to the subspace $P \mathscr{H}$ spanned by the states of a single hole in the ground state $ψ_{0}$ of the parent nucleus with an extra nucleon, as in the uncorrelated models. This follows from the exponential decay of $ψ_{0}$ when a nucleon is very far apart from the residual nucleus. We prove that the real difference from the uncorrelated models is that $P \mathscr{H}$ has an infinite dimension and contains generalized single hole states, distinguishable from the usual ones by the fact that one cannot assign a wave function to the hole. The bound eigenstates of the nuclei are just states of this kind. Some physical consequences are discussed, in particular the unexpected fact that the dynamical part of the single-hole Hamiltonian, although nonnull, does not affect the single-hole overlaps with the bound eigenstates. The decay of $ψ_{0}$ provides the asymptotic behaviours of many single-hole quantities, in particular the nuclear density matrix. Thus a by product of this paper is the rigorous proof of the method developed by Van Neck, Waroquier and Heyde to calculate overlaps and separation energies.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous mathematical proof that bound eigenstates of an A-nucleon system belong to the subspace spanned by single-hole states in the ground state of the (A+1)-nucleon parent nucleus, even for correlated systems with exponentially decaying two-body potentials, but the subspace has infinite dimension and contains generalized single-hole states that cannot be assigned a wave function, unlike in uncorrelated models.

## Method

The author considers nuclei of A+1 ≥ 3 identical nucleons interacting via two-body potentials that satisfy L^p integrability and exponential decay at infinity, using Jacobi coordinates to remove center-of-mass motion and treating the orbital part separately from spin. The proof relies on the O'Connor-Simon upper bound for the ground state wave function and an integral expression for the overlap functions (φ_λ, ψ_0,r) derived from the eigenvalue equation, from which strong limits in the radial direction are established showing that the bound eigenstates φ_n belong to the subspace Pℋ spanned by vectors a_rψ_0.

## Key numbers

- The exponential decay rate of the overlap functions is given by k_n = (2μ_0(ε_n - E_0))^(1/2), where μ_0 = (A/(A+1))m is the reduced mass.
- The strong limit result s-lim_{r→∞} r e^{k_n r} ξ_{n,r} = d_n(𝐫̂) φ_n holds for ε_n < min{ε_C, ε_M}, where ε_C is the continuum threshold and ε_M is an energy depending on A and the potential's decay rate R.
- The two-body potential satisfies |V(s)| ≤ V_0 e^{-s/R} for all s ≥ R_0 > 0, with no restriction on the rate R.
- The occupation numbers n_ν of the one-body density matrix belong to the interval [0,1] and satisfy Σ_ν n_ν = A+1.

## Context

This work extends the rigorous mathematical treatment of ground-state asymptotics initiated by Lieb and Simon (1977) for compact-support potentials to a wider class of exponentially decaying potentials relevant to nuclear physics, including the Yukawa potential. The paper provides the first rigorous justification of the method developed by Van Neck, Waroquier and Heyde (V.W.H.) for calculating overlaps and separation energies from the one-body density matrix, and establishes that the dynamical part of the single-hole Hamiltonian, although nonnull in correlated systems, does not affect the single-hole overlaps with bound eigenstates, a sharp contrast with Feshbach's theory for particles.
