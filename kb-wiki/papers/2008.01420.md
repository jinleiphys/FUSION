# Continuum and three-nucleon force in Borromean system: The 17Ne case

> Starting from chiral two-nucleon (2NF) and chiral three-nucleon (3NF) potentials, we present a detailed study of 17Ne, a Borromean system, with the Gamow shell model which can capture continuum effects. More precisely, we take advantage of the normal-ordering approach to include the 3NF and the Berggren representation to treat bound, resonant and continuum states on equal footing in a complex-momentum plane. In our framework, 3NF is essential to reproduce the Borromean structure of 17Ne, while the continuum is more crucial for the halo property of the nucleus. The two-proton halo structure is demonstrated by calculating the valence proton density and correlation density. The astrophysically interesting $3/2^-$ excited state has its energy above the threshold of the proton emission, and therefore the two-proton decay should be expected from the state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both three-nucleon force (3NF) and continuum coupling are necessary to describe the Borromean and halo properties of 17Ne, but with a crucial division of labor: 3NF is essential for reproducing the Borromean binding pattern (making 16F unbound while 17Ne remains bound), while the continuum is more critical for producing the extended two-proton halo density distribution.

## Method

The authors performed Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations using chiral N3LO two-nucleon force and chiral N2LO three-nucleon force, with the 3NF included via normal-ordering approximation using a 14O core reference state. The Berggren basis was employed to treat bound, resonant, and continuum single-particle states on equal footing in the complex-momentum plane, with the effective interaction in the model space constructed using the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method with second-order Q-box folded diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 17Ne relative to 15O: -0.26 MeV (2NF+3NF), compared to experimental -0.95 MeV and 2NF-only value of -0.73 MeV
- Ground-state energy of 16F relative to 15O: +0.08 MeV (2NF+3NF, unbound), compared to experimental +0.54 MeV and 2NF-only value of -0.14 MeV (bound)
- Proton single-particle energies in 15F: 1s1/2 at 0.456 - 0.002i MeV, 0d5/2 at 1.957 - 0.034i MeV
- 54% s-wave component (π1s1/2^2 ⊗ ν0p1/2^1) in the 17Ne ground state configuration
- Cutoff Λ = 2.6 fm^-1 used for Vlow-k softening of the two-nucleon force
- Maximum shell number N_shell = 23 with l ≤ 4 in the harmonic oscillator basis, with 40 discretized scattering states in Berggren continuum contours

## Context

This work extends previous Gamow shell model studies of neutron-rich nuclei with 3NF (Zhuo 2020) to the proton-rich Borromean system 17Ne, which has been studied experimentally (Tanaka 2010, Sharov 2017, Charity 2018) and theoretically with three-body models (Casal 2016, Parfenova 2018). The results confirm the two-proton halo structure suggested by Zhukov (1995) and demonstrate that the 3/2- excited state lies above the two-proton emission threshold, which is relevant for the astrophysical rp-process bypass of the 15O waiting point in the hot CNO cycle. The work enables future ab initio studies of weakly-bound proton-rich nuclei where both continuum effects and three-nucleon forces are essential.
