# Chiral Symmetry Realization for Even- and Odd-parity Baryon Resonances

> Baryon resonances with even and odd parity are collectively investigated from the viewpoint of chiral symmetry(ChS). We propose a quartet scheme where Delta's and N^*'s with even and odd parity form a chiral multiplet. This scheme gives parameter-free constraints on the baryon masses in the quartet, which are consistent with observed masses with spin 1/2, 3/2, 5/2. The scheme also gives selection rules in the one-pion decay: The absence of the parity non-changing decay N(1720) -> pi Delta(1232) is a typical example which should be confirmed experimentally to unravel the role of ChS in baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "quartet scheme" in which baryon resonances with even and odd parity and different isospin (Delta and N*) form a chiral multiplet under the linear representation of chiral symmetry, yielding parameter-free mass relations that are consistent with observed masses. The authors hedge that this scheme applies only when the "mirror assignment" for axial charges is adopted, not the "naive assignment," and that the explicit breaking of chiral symmetry due to quark masses is neglected.

## Method

The authors construct a linear sigma model for baryon resonances with spin J = 1/2, 3/2, and 5/2, assigning the fields to the (1, 1/2) ⊕ (1/2, 1) representation of the chiral SU(2)L × SU(2)R group. They use the "mirror assignment" where two independent Rarita-Schwinger fields ψ1 and ψ2 have opposite axial charges, and derive the mass terms and Yukawa couplings after spontaneous symmetry breaking via the sigma condensate. The resulting mass formulas yield three parameter-free constraints on the masses of the four baryons in each quartet.

## Key numbers

- For J = 3/2, the mass relation (mΔ− − mΔ+)/2 = mN*+ − mN*− is satisfied by observed masses: mΔ+(1232) = 1232 MeV, mΔ−(1700) ≈ 1700 MeV, mN*+(1720) = 1720 MeV, mN*−(1520) = 1520 MeV.
- For J = 1/2 case 1 (N(1535) as partner), the relation (mΔ− − mΔ+)/2 = mN*+ − mN*− gives mΔ+(1600) ≈ 1600 MeV, mΔ−(1700) ≈ 1700 MeV, mN*+(1440) = 1440 MeV, mN*−(1535) = 1535 MeV.
- For J = 1/2 case 2 (N(1650) as partner), the same relation gives mΔ+(1600) ≈ 1600 MeV, mΔ−(1700) ≈ 1700 MeV, mN*+(1440) = 1440 MeV, mN*−(1650) = 1650 MeV.
- For J = 5/2, the relation gives mΔ+(1905) ≈ 1905 MeV, mΔ−(1930) ≈ 1930 MeV, mN*+(1680) = 1680 MeV, mN*−(1675) = 1675 MeV.
- The chiral-invariant mass parameter m0 is found to be approximately 1500 MeV for the (1, 1/2) ⊕ (1/2, 1) multiplet, compared to m0 = 270 MeV for the (1/2, 0) ⊕ (0, 1/2) doublet from DeTar and Kunihiro (1990).
- The pion decay constant is taken as σ0 = fπ = 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the "mirror assignment" idea of DeTar and Kunihiro (1990), who introduced a chiral doublet for the nucleon and its odd-parity partner, to a quartet scheme that includes both Delta and N* resonances with arbitrary spin. It contrasts with the "naive assignment" (Jido, Nagahiro, and Hosaka, 1998) which would give a mass relation mΔ± = 2 mN*∓ that contradicts observed spectra. The scheme enables future experimental tests, particularly the confirmation of the suppressed decay N(1720) → π Δ(1232) in the J = 3/2 sector, and motivates lattice QCD studies of baryonic excitations in the chiral restored phase.
