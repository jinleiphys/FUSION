# Perturbative structure of two- and four-point functions of color charge in a non-Gaussian small-$x$ action

> We compute the perturbative expansion of the two- and four-point functions of color charges in the Color Glass Condensate framework considering the quartic correction to the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model of Gaussian color charge fluctuations. Expressions for these correlators in the perturbative expansion for small and large non-Gaussian color charge fluctuations are derived for arbitrary orders in perturbation theory. We explicitly show that the perturbative series does not converge at higher orders as expected. We apply the Borel-Padé resummation method to our problem to construct a convergent series. It is shown that the fully non-perturbative solution can be described by the Borel-Padé approximants constructed from the first few terms of the perturbative series for small non-Gaussian fluctuations.

## Key claim

The perturbative expansion of color-charge correlators in the Color Glass Condensate with a quartic non-Gaussian correction to the McLerran-Venugopalan model is divergent at higher orders, but the fully non-perturbative solution can be accurately reconstructed using Borel-Padé approximants constructed from just the first few terms of the perturbative series, provided the non-Gaussian fluctuations are small.

## Method

The authors compute the two- and four-point functions of color charges in SU(N_c) gauge theory on a square lattice with lattice spacing a, using a weight function that includes both a quadratic term (with coupling μ²) and a quartic term (with coupling κ). They perform perturbative expansions in 1/κ for small non-Gaussian fluctuations and in Z (a renormalization factor) for large non-Gaussian fluctuations, evaluating Feynman diagrams up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They then apply Borel-Padé resummation to the divergent perturbative series and compare the resummed results to the exact non-perturbative solution obtained by direct numerical integration.

## Key numbers

* The two-point function at leading order in 1/κ: ⟨ρᵃ_x ρᵇ_y⟩ = (μ²/a²) δᵃᵇ δ_xy [1 − 4(N_c²+1)(μ⁴/κa²)], with the renormalized color charge defined as μ̄² = μ²[1 − 4(N_c²+1)(μ⁴/κa²)].
* The two-point function at NLO in 1/κ: μ̄² = μ²[1 − 4(N_c²+1)(μ⁴/κa²) + 32(N_c²+1)(N_c²+2)(μ⁸/κ²a⁴)].
* The connected part of the four-point function at LO in 1/κ: A_con = −8(μ̄⁸/κa⁶)(δᵃᵇδᶜᵈ + δᵃᶜδᵇᵈ + δᵃᵈδᵇᶜ)δ_xyδ_xuδ_xv.
* The connected part of the four-point function at NLO in 1/κ: A_con = (μ̄⁴/a⁴)[−8(μ̄⁴/κa²) + 32(μ̄⁸/κ²a⁴)(N_c²+7)].
* The coefficients for the two-point function expansion in x = μ⁴/κa²: c = 4(N_c²+1), a₁ = −8(N_c²+2).
* The coefficients for the four-point function expansion in y = μ̄⁴/κa²: c₁ = −8, c₂ = 32(N_c²+7).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of Dumitru et al. (2011) for non-Gaussian color charge fluctuations in the Color Glass Condensate framework to higher orders in perturbation theory. It also builds on the non-perturbative analysis of Giannini and Nara (2020) for arbitrarily large non-Gaussian fluctuations. The results enable systematic inclusion of non-Gaussian corrections to the JIMWLK evolution equation for the four-point function of color charges, which was not considered in Dumitru et al. (2010), and may help understand whether the ridge effect observed in small collision systems (pp and pA) arises from initial-state dynamics rather than collective effects.
