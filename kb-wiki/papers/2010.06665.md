# Mass structure of hadrons and light-front sum rules in t' Hooft model

> We study the mass/energy structure of the bound state of hadrons in two-dimensional quantum chromodynamics in the large number of color limit (t' Hooft model). We analyze separately the contributions from the traceless and trace part of the energy-momentum tensor, and show that the masses are related to the matrix elements of the scalar charge and Coulomb energy. We derive the light-front sum rules for the scalar charge and Coulomb energy, expressed in terms of the light-front wave functions, and find that they are regular at $x=0$ without the delta function contribution. We also consider the result for the massless Goldstone boson, as well as the structure of the gravitational form factors of the bound meson states.

## Key claim
The authors show that in the two-dimensional t'Hooft model, the chiral-odd quark parton distribution function does not develop a delta-function contribution at x=0, contrary to earlier suggestions; instead, the rapid vanishing of the wavefunction at the edge enforces the finite sum rule, with the vacuum physics encoded in how the distribution vanishes near x=0 rather than in a singular term.

## Method
The authors analyze the mass/energy structure of meson bound states in two-dimensional QCD at large N_c using both equal-time quantization in the A^z=0 gauge and light-front quantization in the A^+=0 gauge. They decompose the symmetric energy-momentum tensor into traceless and trace parts, compute the matrix elements of the scalar charge (m ψ̅ψ) and Coulomb energy (E^aE^a) in terms of the light-front wave functions φ_n(x) that solve the t'Hooft equation, and derive the corresponding light-front sum rules for the chiral-odd quark distribution Q_n(x) and twist-four gluon distribution F_n(x).

## Key numbers
- The string tension is σ = π m_0^2/2, where m_0^2 = g^2 N_c/(2π) = λ/(2π)
- The vacuum chiral condensate is ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ = -N_c m_0/√12
- The zeroth moment of the chiral-odd quark distribution is Q_{n,0} = (2m/M_n) ∫_0^1 dx |φ_n(x)|^2/x = C_n^2 π m_0/(M_n √3)
- The scalar condensate in an n-bound state is ⟨P,n|ψ̅ψ|P,n⟩ = C_n^2 (2π m_0/√3) = -4π C_n^2 ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩/N_c
- For the would-be Goldstone mode (n=0), C_0 = 1 and the pseudoscalar decay constant is f_0 = √(N_c/2π)
- The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner-like relation is f_0^2 M_0^2 = -2 m ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩

## Context
This work extends the light-front mass sum rule approach of Ji (1994, 1995) and the recent analysis of twist-four gluon distributions by Ji (2020) to the exactly solvable t'Hooft model in two dimensions. It addresses the question of zero-mode delta-function contributions raised by Efremov (2002) and discussed by Aslan (2018) and Bhattacharya (2020). The results provide a controlled laboratory for understanding the partonic decomposition of hadron mass, with implications for interpreting measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider. The analysis also connects to earlier work on the Bars-Green equation by Bars (1977) and the t'Hooft equation by t'Hooft (1974).
