# Detailed Study of the Eikonal Reaction Theory for the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei

> Background: One-neutron removal reactions are used to study the single-particle structure of unstable nuclei, and in particular the exotic halo nuclei. The Eikonal Reaction Theory (ERT) has been developed by Yahiro, Ogata and Minomo in Prog. Theor. Phys. 126, 167 (2011) to include dynamical effects, which are missing in the usual eikonal description of these reactions. Encouraging results have been obtained for total breakup cross sections in comparison to more elaborate reaction models. Purpose: We extend these comparisons to more differential breakup cross sections expressed as functions of the relative energy or parallel momentum between the core and halo neutron. Method: ERT predictions of these cross sections are compared to state-of-the-art calculations. Results: The hypotheses upon which the ERT is based are confirmed and their range of validity is made clearer. The actual ordering of the evolution operators {affects ERT differential} cross sections and a specific choice leads to excellent agreement with the reference calculation. Dynamical effects in the treatment of the neutron-target interaction can be significant in the parallel-momentum observable. Conclusions: The role of the different interactions in the dynamics of breakup reactions of one-neutron halo nuclei are better understood and improvements to the ERT are suggested.

# Detailed Study of the Eikonal Reaction Theory for the breakup of one-neutron halo nuclei

## Key claim

The Eikonal Reaction Theory (ERT) reproduces total diffractive-breakup cross sections within about 5% of full dynamical calculations, but the original implementation produces unphysical oscillations in energy distributions and fails to reproduce the asymmetry of parallel-momentum distributions on light targets; these issues can be corrected only if the neutron-target interaction is included in the dynamical evolution rather than treated adiabatically.

## Method

The authors implement the ERT within the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) and compare its predictions to full DEA calculations for the diffractive breakup of 11Be on 12C at 67 MeV/nucleon and on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon. They test three variants of the ERT: the original formulation treating the neutron-target interaction adiabatically after dynamical treatment of the core-target interaction; a variant placing the neutron-target eikonal S-matrix at the distance of closest approach (Z=0); and a variant treating the nuclear part of the core-target interaction adiabatically while keeping the neutron-target interaction dynamical.

## Key numbers

- Total diffractive-breakup cross section for 11Be on 12C at 67 MeV/nucleon: DEA = 144 mb, ERT = 138 mb (about 5% lower)
- Total diffractive-breakup cross section for 11Be on 208Pb at 69 MeV/nucleon: DEA = 1890 mb, ERT = 1925 mb (about 2% higher)
- ERT with adiabatic treatment of nuclear core-target interaction (ERT(c)) gives 146 mb on 12C and 1895 mb on 208Pb, nearly identical to DEA
- 11Be ground state one-neutron separation energy: 504 keV
- 11Be 1/2- excited state binding energy: 184 keV
- 5/2+ resonance in 10Be-n continuum at 1.274 MeV
- Energy integration for total cross sections performed up to E = 33 MeV in the 10Be-n continuum

## Context

This work extends the ERT developed by Yahiro, Ogata and Minomo (2011) from total cross sections to differential observables (energy and parallel-momentum distributions), which had not been previously tested. The ERT was originally designed to include dynamical effects missing in the usual eikonal approximation used for knockout reactions, which predicts symmetric parallel-momentum distributions contrary to experimental data from Aumann et al. (2000), Sauvan et al. (2001), and others. The study enables better understanding of which interactions require dynamical treatment in breakup reactions of one-neutron halo nuclei and suggests improvements for analyzing knockout data.
