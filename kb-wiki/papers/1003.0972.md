# Analyses of decay constants and light-cone distribution amplitudes for s-wave heavy meson

> In this paper, a study of light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) for $s$-wave heavy meson are presented in both general and heavy quark frameworks. Within the light-front approach, the leading twist light-cone distribution amplitudes, $ϕ_M(u)$, and their relevant decay constants of heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons, $f_M$, have simple relations. These relations can be further simplified when the heavy quark limit is taken into consideration. After fixing the parameters that appear in both Gaussian and power-law wave functions, the corresponding decay constants are calculated and compared with those of other theoretical approaches. The curves and the first six $ξ$-moments of $ϕ_M(u)$ are plotted and estimated. A conclusion is drawn from these results: Even though the values of the decay constants of the distinct mesons are almost equal, the curves of their LCDAs may have quite large differences, and vice versa. Additionally, in the heavy quark limit, the leading twist LCDAs, $Φ_{Qq}(ω)$ and $Φ_{Qq}(ω)$, are compared with the $B$-meson LCDAs, $ψ_+(ω)$, suggested by the other theoretical groups.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the decay constants of different heavy mesons (pseudoscalar and vector) are nearly equal in value, their light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) can differ substantially, and vice versa; this conclusion is drawn from calculations within the light-front quark model using both Gaussian and power-law wave functions, with the heavy quark limit providing further simplifications.

## Method

The author derives the leading twist light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) for s-wave heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c and their vector counterparts) within both a general framework and the heavy quark effective theory (HQET) framework. Using the light-front quark model (LFQM), the decay constants and LCDAs are extracted from matrix elements of nonlocal operators, with the momentum-space wave functions parameterized by Gaussian and power-law forms. The first six ξ-moments of the LCDAs are calculated, and in the heavy quark limit the leading twist LCDAs Φ_Qq(ω) are compared with the B-meson LCDAs ψ_+(ω) introduced by Grozin and Neubert (1997).

## Key numbers

- Decay constants are calculated for D, D_s, B, B_s, and B_c mesons (both pseudoscalar and vector), with values compared to those from lattice simulations, the relativistic quark model, and the field correlator method; specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text.
- The first six ξ-moments ⟨ξ^n⟩ of the leading twist LCDAs φ_M(u) are estimated for each meson; explicit moment values are not provided in the truncated text.
- The normalization condition ∫₀¹ du φ(u) = 1 is used for all leading twist LCDAs.
- In the heavy quark limit, the relation ⟨ω⟩_+ = (4/3)Λ̄ is obtained, where Λ̄ = M - m_Q is the residual center mass of a heavy-light meson.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy meson decay constants from lattice simulations (Becirevic, 2001), the relativistic quark model (Choi, 2007; Ebert, 2003), and the field correlator method (2005), while also building on the LCDA formalism for B-mesons developed by Grozin and Neubert (1997) and subsequent HQET studies. The results enable more precise calculations of exclusive B-physics processes relevant to BaBar and Belle experiments, particularly for hard exclusive processes involving heavy meson production or annihilation.
