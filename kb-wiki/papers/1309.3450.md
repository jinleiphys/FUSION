# Non-linear evolution of unintegrated gluon density at large values of coupling constant

> We propose an evolution equation for unintegrated gluon densities that is valid for large values of the QCD coupling constant $\barα _s$. Our approach is based on the linear resummation model introduced by Staśto. We generalize the model including a non-linear term in the diffusive regime. The validity of the diffusive evolution at strong coupling is supported by the AdS/CFT consideration, as well as perturbative arguments. We solve the evolution equation numerically and extract the saturation scale, which we compare with the weak coupling counterpart.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the linear BFKL equation enhanced with kinematical corrections and DGLAP effects is insufficient to prevent a diverging gluon density even at large values of the QCD coupling constant, and that a non-linear term must be included to achieve stabilization.

## Method

The authors start from the linear BFKL equation in diffusion approximation and incorporate higher-order corrections via kinematical constraints and DGLAP anomalous dimension resummation following the model of Stasto (2007). They solve the resulting eigenvalue equation numerically to obtain the unintegrated gluon density for various coupling strengths. They then generalize the model by adding a non-linear term in the diffusive regime, motivated by the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation and supported by AdS/CFT considerations, and extract the saturation scale from the numerical solution.

## Key numbers

* The effective eigenvalue at infinite strong coupling is fitted as \(\chi_{eff\,\infty}(\omega,1/2+i\nu) = 1.02795 - 2.04635 \nu^2\), giving \(\lambda_{st} = 1.02\) and \(\lambda_{st}^{\prime} = 4.08\) for the diffusion equation.
* The AdS/CFT result for the Pomeron intercept in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM is \(j_0 = 2 - 2/\sqrt{\lambda} + O(1/\lambda)\) and the diffusion constant is \(\mathcal{D} = 1/(2\sqrt{\lambda}) + O(1/\lambda)\).
* The gluon density is evaluated at \(x = 10^{-6}\) and \(x = 10^{-4}\) for coupling values \(\bar{\alpha}_s = 0.2, 0.5, 1, 10, 10^4\), showing increasing density with coupling.

## Context

This work extends the linear resummation model of Stasto (2007) to include non-linear saturation effects at strong coupling, motivated by the observation that the linear BFKL equation with higher-order corrections still produces a growing gluon density at large coupling. The diffusive evolution at strong coupling is independently supported by AdS/CFT results for the Pomeron in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory, as derived by Maldacena (1998), Witten (1998), and Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998). The non-linear extension enables the extraction of a saturation scale at strong coupling, which can be compared with weak coupling results from approaches such as those of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1984) and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation.
