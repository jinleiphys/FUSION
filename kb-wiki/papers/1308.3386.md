# Finite volume effects in low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering

> We present a lattice calculation of neutron-deuteron scattering at very low energies and investigate in detail the impact of the topological finite-volume corrections. Our calculations are carried out in the framework of pionless effective field theory at leading order in the low-energy expansion. Using lattice sizes and a lattice spacing comparable to those employed in nuclear lattice simulations, we find that the topological volume corrections must be taken into account in order to obtain correct results for the neutron-proton S-wave scattering lengths.

# Finite volume effects in low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering

## Key claim

The topological finite-volume corrections to the binding energy of composite particles must be taken into account in lattice calculations of neutron-deuteron scattering to obtain correct results for the S-wave scattering lengths, even when using lattice sizes and spacings comparable to those employed in modern nuclear lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice calculations of neutron-deuteron scattering within pionless effective field theory at leading order, using a discretized Hamiltonian with O(a^2)-improved Laplacian on spatial lattices. They determined the low-energy constants C_0 and C_{I^2} by fitting to the deuteron binding energy (2.22 MeV) and the ^1S_0 neutron-proton scattering length (-23.7 fm), and fixed the three-nucleon contact interaction D by reproducing the triton binding energy (8.48 MeV). The neutron-deuteron scattering lengths in both spin-quartet and spin-doublet channels were extracted from the lattice energy spectrum using the Luscher formula, with and without applying the topological volume corrections derived from the twisted boundary conditions for moving bound states.

## Key numbers

- Spin-quartet scattering length without topological corrections: (a_3^q)^{nc} = 4.53 ± 0.16 fm after continuum extrapolation
- Spin-quartet scattering length with topological corrections: (a_3^q)^c = 5.11 ± 0.02 fm after continuum extrapolation
- Spin-doublet scattering length without topological corrections: (a_3^d)^{nc} = -0.77 ± 0.50 fm after continuum extrapolation
- Spin-doublet scattering length with topological corrections: (a_3^d)^c = 0.66 ± 0.08 fm after continuum extrapolation
- Reference continuum values from Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation: a_3^q ≈ 5.1 fm and a_3^d ≈ 0.5 fm
- Lattice spacings used: a_latt = 1.4 to 2.6 fm
- Lattice sizes: N = 6 to 17 sites
- Two-body input: deuteron binding energy B_d = 2.22 MeV, triton binding energy B_t = 8.48 MeV, ^1S_0 scattering length a_2^s = -23.7 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of topological volume corrections, previously investigated by Bour et al. (2011) for universal three-body systems of two-component fermions, to the realistic case of neutron-deuteron scattering in nuclear physics. The authors compare their lattice results to continuum solutions of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation (Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian, 1957) using the same input parameters. The findings demonstrate that these corrections are essential for precision lattice calculations of few-nucleon scattering, enabling reliable extraction of scattering parameters from lattice simulations that use the same lattice spacings as current chiral EFT nuclear lattice simulations of light nuclei, dilute neutron matter, and the Hoyle state.
