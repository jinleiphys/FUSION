# Supernarrow Dibaryons Production in pd Interactions

> An analysis of experimental data, obtained at the Proton Linear Accelerator of INR, is carried out with the aim of searching for supernarrow dibaryons in the pd->p+pX_1 and pd->p+dX_2 reactions. Dibaryons with masses 1904\pm 2, 1926\pm 2, and 1942\pm 2 MeV have been observed in invariant mass M_{pX_1} spectra. In missing mass M_{X_1} spectra, the peaks at M_{X_1}=966\pm 2, 986\pm 2, and 1003\pm 2 MeV have been found. The analysis of the data obtained leads to the conclusion that the observed dibaryons are supernarrow dibaryons, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. A possible interpretation of exited nucleon states with small masses is suggested.

# Supernarrow Dibaryons Production in pd Interactions

## Key claim
Three narrow resonances observed in the invariant mass spectra of the pd → p + pX₁ reaction at 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV are interpreted as supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs) whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle, rather than ordinary NN-coupled dibaryons. The identification relies on the observed angular distributions of decay protons being confined to a narrow cone (consistent with SND decay via γNN) and on missing mass peaks at 966, 986, and 1003 MeV that differ significantly from the neutron mass, indicating X₁ = γ + n rather than a single neutron.

## Method
The experiment used a 305 MeV proton beam from the Linear Accelerator of INR incident on CD₂ and ¹²C targets, with the two-arm spectrometer TAMS detecting scattered protons at a fixed left-arm angle of 70° and a second charged particle (proton or deuteron) in the right arm at three angles (34°, 36°, and 38°). Invariant mass spectra M_{pX₁} and missing mass spectra M_{X₁} were constructed for the pd → p + pX₁ reaction, and the angular dependence of the observed peak yields was compared to calculated ratios for isovector (T=1) versus isoscalar (T=0) SND hypotheses.

## Key numbers
- Dibaryon masses observed in invariant mass M_{pX₁} spectra: 1904 ± 2 MeV, 1926 ± 2 MeV, and 1942 ± 2 MeV
- Missing mass M_{X₁} peaks: 966 ± 2 MeV, 986 ± 2 MeV, and 1003 ± 2 MeV (compared to neutron mass 939.6 MeV)
- Statistical significance of the three resonances: 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations for the 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV peaks, respectively (using nine points per peak)
- Experimental resolution: approximately 4 MeV (widths of the resonances equal to the experimental resolution)
- Calculated contribution ratios for the 1904 MeV SND at θ_R = 34°, 36°, 38°: 1:0.92:0.42 (T=1) and 1:0.95:0.67 (T=0)
- Calculated contribution ratios for the 1926 MeV SND: 1:0.85:0.34 (T=1) and 1:0.71:0.46 (T=0)
- Calculated contribution ratios for the 1942 MeV SND: 1:0.6:0.2 (T=1) and 1:0.78:0.55 (T=0)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous searches for supernarrow dibaryons in pd interactions (Fil'kov et al., 2000; 2001) by using shifted detection angles (34°, 36°, 38° instead of 35°, 37°, 39°) to obtain additional angular distribution information. The observed missing mass peak at 1003 ± 2 MeV corresponds to a state previously reported by Tatischeff et al. (1998) and attributed to an excited nucleon state N*; the present authors instead interpret it as the γ + n decay product of a 1942 MeV SND, which would not contribute to Compton scattering on the nucleon (as argued by L'vov, 1999). The results enable future searches for isovector SNDs via the pd → n + X reaction to determine isotopic spin more precisely, and suggest that the states at 1004 and 1044 MeV reported by Tatischeff et al. (1998) may be γ + n signals from SNDs rather than genuine excited nucleon states.
