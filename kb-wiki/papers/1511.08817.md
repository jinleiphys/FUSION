# Spin Decomposition of Electron in QED

> We perform a systematic study on the spin decomposition of an electron in QED at one-loop order. It is found that the electron orbital angular momentum defined in Jaffe-Manohar and Ji spin sum rules agrees with each other, and the so-called potential angular momentum vanishes at this order. The calculations are performed in both dimensional regularization and Pauli-Villars regularization for the ultraviolet divergences, and they lead to consistent results. We further investigate the calculations in terms of light-front wave functions, and find a missing contribution from the instantaneous interaction in light-front quantization. This clarifies the confusing issues raised recently in the literature on the spin decomposition of an electron, and will help to consolidate the spin physics program for nucleons in QCD.

## Key claim

The electron orbital angular momentum defined in the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji spin sum rules agrees at one-loop order in QED, and the potential angular momentum vanishes at this order, contrary to recent claims of a finite difference; this agreement holds only when the instantaneous interaction in light-front quantization is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors computed the electron spin, orbital angular momentum (OAM), and gravitational form factors A_e(0) and B_e(0) at one-loop order in QED using both dimensional regularization (with the HVBM scheme for gamma_5) and Pauli-Villars regularization. They performed calculations via Feynman diagrams in the light-cone gauge and via light-front wave functions, explicitly evaluating the potential angular momentum and surface terms to check consistency between the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji spin sum rules.

## Key numbers

- Electron spin at one-loop: ΔΣ_e/2 = α/(2π) × 1/2 (dimensional regularization) or ΔΣ_e = 1 − α/(2π) × 1 (Pauli-Villars)
- B_e(0) = α/(2π) × 2/3 (dimensional regularization) or B_e(0) = α/(4π) × 4/3 (Pauli-Villars)
- A_e(0) = 1 − α/(2π) [4/3 (N_ε + ln(μ²/m²)) + 17/9] (dimensional regularization) or A_e(0) = 1 − α/(4π) [8/3 ln(Λ²/m²) + 44/9] (Pauli-Villars)
- Kinetic OAM L_e = α/(2π) [−2/3 (N_ε + ln(μ²/m²)) − 10/9] (dimensional regularization) or L_e = −α/(4π) [4/3 ln(Λ²/m²) + 7/9] (Pauli-Villars)
- Canonical OAM L_e = α/(2π) [−2/3 (N_ε + ln(μ²/m²)) − 10/9] (dimensional regularization) or L_e = −α/(4π) [4/3 ln(Λ²/m²) − 2/9] (Pauli-Villars)
- Potential angular momentum l_pot = 0 at one-loop
- Missing instantaneous interaction contribution to A_e(0): δA_e(0) = α/(2π)

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between Burkardt et al. (2008) and Liu and Ma (2014), who found a finite difference between canonical and kinetic OAM for an electron in QED at one-loop order. The authors show that the earlier calculations missed a contribution from the instantaneous interaction in light-front quantization, which when included makes the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji sum rules consistent. This clarifies the spin decomposition for electrons in QED and consolidates the theoretical foundation for the spin physics program for nucleons in QCD, where analogous sum rules apply.
