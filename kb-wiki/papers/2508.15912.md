# $f$-mode Oscillations for Hyperons and H-dibaryons in Neutron Stars

> The fundamental ($f$-mode) oscillations of neutron stars are studied within the quark meson coupling model, a relativistic Hartree-Fock theory of dense nuclear matter, which takes into account the self-consistent modification of the valence quark structure of the bound baryons in the associated strong Lorentz scalar mean fields. For the first time, hyperons and H-dibaryons are included, along with the effects of potential additional short-range repulsion within this scheme, and their influence on $f$-modes is investigated. Universal relations are studied within the relativistic Cowling approximation and compared against those in the existing literature for potential applications in gravitational wave asteroseismology.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first study of neutron star $f$-mode oscillations within the quark meson coupling (QMC) model that includes hyperons and H-dibaryons, finding that the $f$-mode frequencies are sensitive to the equation of state and the presence of exotic matter, but the universal relations between $f$-mode frequency and macroscopic properties remain approximately EoS-independent even with these exotic components.

## Method

The authors computed neutron star equations of state using the QMC model in the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, including nucleons, hyperons, and H-dibaryons with self-consistent modification of valence quark structure in scalar mean fields. They solved the TOV equations to obtain mass-radius relations, then calculated $f$-mode oscillation frequencies for $l=2$ within the relativistic Cowling approximation by solving the perturbed fluid equations for six QMC parameter sets, each with and without H-dibaryons, using a nominal H-dibaryon mass of 2258 MeV.

## Key numbers

- $f$-mode frequencies for low mass neutron stars (around $1.0 M_\odot$) lie between 1.97 and 2.13 kHz across all QMC parametrizations
- $f$-mode frequencies for high mass neutron stars (near maximum mass) vary between 2.40 and 2.66 kHz
- The H-dibaryon rest mass used is $M_H = 2258$ MeV, derived as $2M_\Lambda + 26$ MeV, with additional calculations at $M_H = 2247$ MeV and $M_H = 2269$ MeV
- The QMC model parameters reproduce saturation density $n_0 = 0.14-0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, binding energy $E/A = -15.8 \pm 1.2$ MeV, and symmetry energy $S_0 = 30 \pm 2$ MeV
- Incompressibility values range from $K_\infty = 225$ MeV (QMC-C) to $K_\infty = 293$ MeV (Overlap-A)
- The overlap high-density repulsion parameters are $E_0 = 5500$ MeV and $b = 0.5$ fm; the EVE hardcore radius is $r = 0.45$ fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $f$-mode oscillations in neutron stars with exotic matter (Pradhan 2021, Pradhan 2022) by applying the QMC model, which has successfully predicted heavy neutron stars with hyperons before observational confirmation (Stone 2007) and accurately described over 800 even-even nuclei (Martinez 2018, Martinez 2020). The inclusion of H-dibaryons follows recent work showing compatibility with neutron star constraints (Leong 2025), and the universal relations are compared against those established by Andersson and Kokkotas (1996, 1998) and Benhar (2004) for potential application in gravitational wave asteroseismology with next-generation detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
