# On the elliptical flow in asymmetric collisions and nuclear equation of state

> We here present the results of elliptical flow for the collision of different asymmetric nuclei (10Ne20 +13 Al27, 18Ar40 +21 Sc45, 30Zn64 +28 Ni58, 36Kr86 +41 Nb93) by using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model. General features of elliptical flow are investigated with the help of theoretical simulations. The simulations are performed at different beam energies between 40 and 105 MeV/nucleon. A significant change can be seen from in-plane to out-of-plane elliptical flow of different fragments with incident energy. A comparison with experimental data is also made. Further, we predict, for the first time that, elliptical flow for different kind of fragments follow power law dependence ? C(Atot)? for asymmetric systems.

## Key claim
The paper claims, for the first time, that elliptical flow for different kinds of fragments in asymmetric nuclear collisions follows a power law dependence on the total mass of the system, of the form α C (A_tot)^τ. This claim is conditional on the specific asymmetric systems and the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model framework used, and the power law is presented as a fit to the simulated data rather than a derived theoretical relation.

## Method
The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate four asymmetric nuclear reactions: 10Ne20 + 13Al27, 18Ar40 + 21Sc45, 30Zn64 + 28Ni58, and 36Kr86 + 41Nb93. Simulations were performed at beam energies between 45 and 105 MeV/nucleon over the full collision geometry from central to peripheral impact parameters. The elliptical flow (V2) was calculated for free nucleons, light mass fragments (LMFs, A=2-4), and intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, A=5 to A_tot/6) using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method for clusterization at a final state time of 200 fm/c. The study examined the dependence of V2 on transverse momentum, impact parameter, system size, and incident energy, and compared results with experimental data from the 4π Array.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies used: between 40 and 105 MeV/nucleon (simulations performed at 45, 65, 75, 85, 95, and 105 MeV/nucleon).
- For the reaction Ar+Sc at 95 MeV/nucleon and scaled impact parameter b̂ = 0.5, the peak V2 values for free nucleons were 0.49, 0.38, 0.30, and 0.25 for the four systems (Kr+Nb, Zn+Ni, Ar+Sc, Ne+Al respectively), with corresponding V2 ratios of 1.96, 1.52, 1.21, and 1.0.
- The compressibility values used: K = 380 MeV for hard and HMD equations of state; K = 200 MeV for soft and SMD equations of state.
- The Yukawa potential parameter t3 = -6.66 MeV with range m = 1.5 fm.
- The momentum-dependent interaction parameters: t4 = 1.57 MeV and t5 = 5 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻².

## Context
This work extends previous studies of elliptical flow, which had focused primarily on symmetric systems like Au+Au (studied by the Plastic Ball group at Bevalac and the MINIBALL/ALADIN collaboration), to asymmetric systems where projectile and target masses differ. The study builds on the theoretical framework of Stocker et al. (1982) who first predicted midrapidity emission perpendicular to the reaction plane, and the experimental observation of in-plane to out-of-plane transition by the NAUTILUS collaboration at GANIL in 1994 using Zn+Ni reactions. The comparison with experimental data from the 4π Array (Magestro) for the Ar+Sc system enables testing of different equations of state (hard, soft, SMD, HMD), with the hard equation of state providing the best agreement. The predicted power law dependence of elliptical flow on total system mass for asymmetric systems is presented as a new result that enables systematic predictions for other asymmetric collision systems.
