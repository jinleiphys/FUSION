# On the thermalization of quarkonia at the LHC

> We argue that the relative yields of $Υ$ states observed at the LHC can be understood as bottomonium states coming to early thermal equilibrium and then freezing out. The bottomonium freezeout temperature is approximately 250 MeV. We examine its systematics as a function of centrality. We remark on the interesting differences seen by the CMS and ALICE experiments in the charmonium sector.

## Key claim

The relative yields of $\Upsilon$ states observed at the LHC can be interpreted as bottomonium states coming to early thermal equilibrium and then freezing out, with the caveat that the freezeout temperature is approximately 250 MeV and the analysis relies on the assumption that the system thermalizes before freezeout, which may not hold for all kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors use a thermal model where the equilibrium density of heavy quarkonia depends on mass and freezeout temperature, with a single free parameter (the freezeout temperature $T_f$) extracted from the ratio of $\Upsilon(2S)$ to $\Upsilon(1S)$ yields in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon. They correct for feed-down from excited states decaying to lower-lying quarkonium states before detection, and then use the extracted $T_f$ to predict yields of other bottomonium states. The centrality dependence is examined using double ratios reported by CMS.

## Key numbers

- Freezeout temperature for bottomonium: $T_f = 252^{+40}_{-39}$ MeV, extracted from the ratio $Y[\Upsilon(2S)] = N_{\text{PbPb}}[\Upsilon(2S)]/N_{\text{PbPb}}[\Upsilon(1S)] = 0.12 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.02$ in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon.
- The ratio $Y[\Upsilon(3S)] = N_{\text{PbPb}}[\Upsilon(3S)]/N_{\text{PbPb}}[\Upsilon(1S)] = 0.02 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.02$ gives a compatible but less precise freezeout temperature of $228^{+57}_{-228}$ MeV.
- For charmonium at central rapidity ($|y|\le1.6$) with $p_T$ between 6.5 and 30 GeV, $Y[\psi(2S)] = 0.024\pm0.008$ yields $T_f = 149\pm14$ MeV; at larger rapidity ($1.5\le|y|\le2.4$) with $p_T$ between 3 and 30 GeV, $Y[\psi(2S)] = 0.105\pm0.02$ yields $T_f = 255\pm25$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermal interpretation of quarkonium suppression first proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986) and later developed in statistical hadronization models, but differs by allowing the freezeout temperature of heavy quarkonia to be determined by data rather than forcing it to equal the light-hadron freezeout temperature. The analysis contradicts comover interaction models (Capella et al., 2000) as an explanation for LHC data, since the fireball and spectator fragments are separated by $\Delta y \simeq 6$ at central rapidity. The results enable future extraction of charmonium freezeout temperatures from ALICE data with no lower $p_T$ cutoff, and motivate lattice QCD calculations of quarkonium spectral functions at finite temperature.
