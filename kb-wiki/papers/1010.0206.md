# Baryons in and beyond the quark-diquark model

> We examine the nucleon's electromagnetic form factors in a Poincare-covariant Faddeev framework. The three-quark core contributions to the form factors are obtained by employing a quark-diquark approximation. We implement the self-consistent solution for the quark-photon vertex from its inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. We find that the resulting transverse parts which add to the Ball-Chiu vertex have no significant impact on nucleon magnetic moments. The current-quark mass evolution of the form factors agrees with results from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The transverse parts of the quark-photon vertex, obtained from the self-consistent solution of its inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, have no significant impact on nucleon magnetic moments and change the nucleon charge radii by less than 5 percent compared to a simpler Ball-Chiu plus rho-meson ansatz, but only within the quark-diquark approximation of the Faddeev framework which excludes pion-cloud corrections.

## Method

The authors solved the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex in a rainbow-ladder truncation, using the same effective interaction that appears in the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and the diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations. They then inserted this vertex into the electromagnetic current of the nucleon within a Poincare-covariant quark-diquark Faddeev framework, which includes quark and diquark impulse-approximation diagrams, couplings to the exchanged quark, and seagull terms. The resulting nucleon form factors were computed at the physical pion mass and as functions of the current-quark mass, and compared to results obtained with a simpler Ball-Chiu vertex augmented by an effective rho-meson contribution.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment with Ball-Chiu plus rho ansatz: 2.56(6); with full vertex: 2.52(12); experimental: 2.79
- Neutron magnetic moment with Ball-Chiu plus rho ansatz: -1.59(3); with full vertex: -1.59(7); experimental: -1.91
- Proton squared charge radius R_E^p (in (GeV fm)^2) with Ball-Chiu plus rho: 0.55(2); with full vertex: 0.58(1); experimental: 0.69(2)
- Neutron squared charge radius R_E^n (in (GeV fm)^2) with Ball-Chiu plus rho: 0.00(1); with full vertex: 0.01(1); experimental: -0.10
- Proton squared magnetic radius R_M^p (in (GeV fm)^2) with Ball-Chiu plus rho: 0.46(1); with full vertex: 0.50(1); experimental: 0.64(5)
- Neutron squared magnetic radius R_M^n (in (GeV fm)^2) with Ball-Chiu plus rho: 0.45(1); with full vertex: 0.49(1); experimental: 0.67(2)
- The quark-diquark approximation introduces only a 5 percent change in the nucleon mass (from Ref. Eichmann (2009))
- The form factor evolution is essentially identical for both vertex choices up to Q^2 of 3 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the covariant Faddeev approach to nucleon electromagnetic form factors previously developed by Eichmann (2009) and Eichmann (2007), where the quark-diquark approximation was shown to reproduce the nucleon mass to within 5 percent of the full three-quark calculation. The study improves upon earlier form factor calculations by Eichmann (2008, 2009) that used a phenomenological rho-meson ansatz for the transverse part of the quark-photon vertex, by instead solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation self-consistently. The results agree well with lattice QCD data from Alexandrou (2006, 2008), Syritsyn (2009), Yamazaki (2009), Bratt (2010), and Lin (2010) at larger pion masses where meson-cloud effects are suppressed, confirming that the calculated quantities represent the quark core of the nucleon. The next step is to include chiral (pion-cloud) corrections and to go beyond the quark-diquark approximation for the form factors themselves.
