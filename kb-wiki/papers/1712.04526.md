# The role of strangeness and isospin in low density expansions of hadronic matter

> We compare relativistic mean field models with their low density expansion counterparts used to mimic non-relativistic models by consistently expanding the baryonic scalar density in powers of the baryonic number density up to ${\cal O}(13/3)$, which goes two orders beyond the order considered in previous works. We show that, due to the non-trivial density dependence of the Dirac mass, the convergence of the expansion is very slow, and the validity of the non-relativistic approximation {\bf is} questionable even at subsaturation densities. In order to analyze the roles played by strangeness and isospin we consider $n-Λ$ and $n-p$ matter separately. Our results indicate that these degrees of freedom play quite different roles in the expansion mechanism and $n-Λ$ matter can be better described by low density expansions than $n-p$ matter in general.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the low-density expansion of relativistic mean-field models converges very slowly even at subsaturation densities, making the non-relativistic approximation questionable; however, the convergence is better for neutron-Lambda matter than for neutron-proton matter, indicating that strangeness and isospin play qualitatively different roles in the expansion mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic low-density expansion of the baryonic scalar density in powers of the baryonic number density up to order ρ^(13/3), which is two orders beyond previous work. They applied this expansion to both n-Λ matter (using a linear RMF model with σ, ω, σ*, and φ mesons) and n-p matter (using a non-linear RMF model with σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons), comparing the expanded results to the full relativistic mean-field calculations for constant couplings and density-dependent couplings (DDHδ parametrization).

## Key numbers

- The expansion was carried out up to O(ρ^(13/3)), going two orders beyond the order considered in previous works
- For n-Λ matter with Λ fraction Y_Λ=0.5, the effective masses show good agreement with RMF up to ρ ≈ 0.25 fm^(-3) when terms up to O(ρ^(10/3)) are included
- The meson masses used in the linear parametrization are: m_σ=550 MeV, m_ω=783 MeV, m_σ*=980 MeV, and m_φ=1020 MeV
- The couplings for the linear model are: g_σn=√91.64 and g_ωn=√136.2, with hyperon coupling ratios χ_σΛ=0.5, χ_ωΛ=0.522, χ_σ*Λ=0.5, and χ_φΛ=0.612

## Context

This work extends the low-density expansion technique originally developed by Serot (1997) and later applied to asymmetric nuclear matter by Margueron (2007) and Providencia (2007). It addresses the systematic differences between relativistic and non-relativistic functionals that persist even at subsaturation densities, which affect predictions for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition and neutron star crust-core transitions. The study of n-Λ matter with this expansion technique had not been carried out before, despite its importance for understanding strangeness-driven phase transitions observed in Skyrme-based non-relativistic functionals (Fran02) but not in relativistic mean-field models (Oertel 2015, James 2017).
