# Merger of a Neutron Star with a Black Hole: one-family vs. two-families scenario

> A kilonova signal is generally expected after a Black Hole - Neutron Star merger. The strength of the signal is related to the equation of state of neutron star matter and it increases with the stiffness of the latter. The recent results obtained by NICER from the analyses of PSR J0740+6620 suggest a rather stiff equation of state and the expected kilonova signal is therefore strong, at least if the mass of the Black Hole does not exceed $\sim 10 \mathrm{\,M}_\odot$ and if the adimensional spin parameter is not too small and the orbit is prograde. We compare the predictions obtained by considering equations of state of neutron star matter satisfying the most recent observations and assuming that only one family of compact stars exists with the results predicted in the two-families scenario. In the latter a soft hadronic equation of state produces very compact stellar objects while a rather stiff quark matter equation of state produces massive strange quark stars, satisfying NICER results. The expected kilonova signal in the two-families scenario is very weak: in particular the Hadronic Star - Black Hole merger produces a much weaker signal than in the one-family scenario because the hadronic equation of state is very soft. Moreover, according to the only existing simulation, the Strange Quark Star - Black Hole merger does not produce a kilonova signal because the amount of mass ejected is negligible. These predictions will be easily tested with the new generation of detectors if Black Holes with an adimensional spin parameter $χ_\mathrm{BH}\gtrsim 0.2$ or a mass $\mathrm M_\mathrm{BH} \lesssim 4 \mathrm M_\odot$ can be present in the merger.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strength of a kilonova signal from a Black Hole - Neutron Star merger can discriminate between the standard one-family scenario (where all compact stars are neutron stars with a stiff equation of state) and the two-families scenario (where hadronic stars and strange quark stars coexist), provided the black hole has an adimensional spin parameter greater than about 0.2 or a mass less than about 4 solar masses.

## Method

The authors used semi-analytical models from Barbieri (2020), Foucart (2018), and Kawaguchi (2016) to estimate the dynamical ejecta mass and disk mass from BH-NS mergers as functions of the neutron star mass, compactness, tidal deformability, and the black hole mass and parallel spin component. They compared predictions for three nucleonic equations of state (MPA1, DD2, AP3) representing the one-family scenario against the SFHO+HD equation of state representing the hadronic branch of the two-families scenario. A toy-model was employed to generate synthetic posteriors mimicking the correlations between masses and effective spin parameter observed in LIGO-Virgo analyses, and the probability of observing a kilonova signal with LSST at 200 Mpc was computed for each equation of state.

## Key numbers

* For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with MPA1 (stiff EoS), a disk forms for black hole spin parameter greater than about 0.3 when the black hole mass is 4 solar masses; for SFHO+HD (soft hadronic EoS), no disk forms for spin parameters less than about 0.65 at the same black hole mass.
* The tidal deformability for MPA1 at 1.4 solar masses is about 462; for SFHO+HD it is about 151.
* For a 5 solar mass black hole with spin parameter 0.4, the dynamical ejecta mass for a 1.2-1.3 solar mass neutron star is much smaller for SFHO+HD than for the nucleonic EoSs, and becomes zero at 1.4 solar masses.
* The probability of observing a kilonova signal with LSST after 1 day at 200 Mpc for a 1.3 solar mass neutron star and 5 solar mass black hole with effective spin 0.1 ranges from 0.00 (SFHO+HD) to 0.26 (MPA1) to 0.48 (DD2); for effective spin 0.5, the range is 0.00 (SFHO+HD) to 0.18 (MPA1) to 0.52 (DD2).
* For a 1.3 solar mass neutron star and 5 solar mass black hole with effective spin 0.5, the probability ranges from 0.82 (SFHO+HD) to 1.00 (MPA1, DD2, AP3).
* The g-band limiting magnitude for LSST was set at 24.7 with effective wavelength 4830 Angstroms, following Chase (2021).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Barbieri (2020) on kilonova signals from BH-NS mergers by explicitly comparing the one-family and two-families scenarios for compact stars. The two-families scenario was outlined by Drago (2014) and posits that hadronic stars and strange quark stars coexist, with the hadronic equation of state being very soft due to hyperons and delta resonances. The paper builds on NICER results for PSR J0740+6620 from Riley (2021) and Miller (2021) which suggest a stiff equation of state for the one-family case. The results enable future tests of the two-families scenario using next-generation detectors, particularly if black holes with spin parameter greater than about 0.2 or mass less than about 4 solar masses are present in mergers. The analysis also relates to the recent LIGO-Virgo detections GW200105 and GW200115 reported by LIGO Scientific (2021), which found small ejected masses consistent with the absence of electromagnetic counterparts.
