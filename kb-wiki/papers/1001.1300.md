# Antikaon interactions with nucleons and nuclei

> This report summarizes our understanding of antikaon-nucleon interactions and reviews the present theoretical situation in the quest for quasibound antikaon-nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The antikaon-nucleon interaction is strongly attractive at low energies, driven primarily by the Tomozawa-Weinberg term, but quantitative predictions for far-subthreshold properties remain unavoidably vague and will presumably remain so until fully conclusive experimental data can be used to set more stringent constraints.

## Method

The paper reviews chiral SU(3) effective field theory combined with non-perturbative coupled-channels methods to describe the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, treating the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a quasibound state emerging from coupling between $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi\Sigma$ channels. For the $K^-pp$ system, both Faddeev three-body calculations with separable interactions and variational calculations using phenomenological or chiral SU(3) based $\bar{K}N$ effective interactions are compared. Heavier antikaon-nuclear systems are studied using Klein-Gordon equations with complex potentials or relativistic mean field theory.

## Key numbers

- The $K^-p$ scattering length from DEAR experiment: $a = -0.47\,(\pm 0.10) + i\,0.30\,(\pm 0.17)$ fm
- The $K^-p$ scattering length from KEK experiment: $a = -0.78\,(\pm 0.18) + i\,0.49\,(\pm 0.37)$ fm
- The $\bar{K}N$ I=0 quasibound state pole position from chiral SU(3) dynamics: $\sqrt{s} \simeq 1420$ MeV with width about 40 MeV
- The I=0 $\pi\Sigma$ resonance from chiral SU(3) dynamics: around $\sqrt{s} \simeq 1390$ MeV with width 100-150 MeV
- $K^-pp$ binding energies from various calculations range between about 20 and 80 MeV, with widths between 40 and 110 MeV
- Non-mesonic width from $\bar{K}NN \rightarrow YN$ absorption: $\delta\Gamma \simeq 22$ MeV at normal nuclear matter density, with simpler estimate $\delta\Gamma \sim 10$ MeV for $K^-pp$
- Charged kaon mass: $m_K \simeq 494$ MeV
- Pseudoscalar decay constant: $f \simeq 0.1$ GeV
- $K^-p$ threshold energy: $\sqrt{s_{thr}} = 1432$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the long history of antikaon-nuclear studies dating back to Dalitz (1967), who first suggested the $\Lambda(1405)$ is a $\bar{K}N$ quasibound state, and early work on $K^-$ condensation in neutron star matter. The chiral SU(3) coupled-channels approach builds on the framework of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998). The paper contrasts the chiral SU(3) dynamics approach with the Akaishi-Yamazaki potential (2002), finding the chiral-based effective interaction is far less attractive in the deep subthreshold region. The review enables future experimental constraints from SIDDHARTA at LNF and photoproduction experiments at JLab to refine subthreshold extrapolations, while noting that kaon condensation in dense matter appears unlikely based on work by Gazda, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2008).
