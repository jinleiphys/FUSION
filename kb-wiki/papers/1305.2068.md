# Neutron knockout in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions

> The ongoing and future searches for diffuse supernova neutrinos and sterile neutrinos carried out with large water-Cherenkov detectors require a precise determination of the backgrounds, especially those involving gamma rays. Of great importance, in this context, is the process of neutron knockout through neutral-current scattering of atmospheric neutrinos on oxygen. Nuclear reinteractions of the produced neutron may in fact lead to the production of gamma rays of energies high enough to mimic the processes of interest. In this Letter, we focus on the kinematical range suitable for simulations of atmospheric-neutrino interactions and provide the neutron-knockout cross sections computed using the formalism based on realistic nuclear spectral function. The role of the strange-quark contribution to the neutral-current axial form factor is also analyzed. Based on the available experimental information, we give an estimate of the associated uncertainty.

# Neutron knockout in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions

## Key claim

The formalism based on realistic nuclear spectral functions can provide accurate calculations of neutron-knockout cross sections from neutral-current quasielastic scattering of atmospheric neutrinos on oxygen, with the strangeness-related uncertainty estimated to be smaller than reported in prior work. The cross sections are computed for the kinematical range suitable for simulations of atmospheric-neutrino interactions, covering energies up to 10 GeV.

## Method

The authors employ the impulse approximation (IA) framework, in which nuclear interactions are treated as the incoherent sum of interactions between the beam particles and individual nucleons, focusing on quasielastic (QE) scattering. The nuclear structure information is encoded in a realistic spectral function of oxygen obtained in the local-density approximation (LDA), which combines shell structure deduced from experimental (e,e'p) data with a correlation component calculated from uniform nuclear matter results. The elementary neutral-current QE cross sections for scattering on off-shell nucleons are computed using state-of-the-art parametrizations of electromagnetic form factors and an axial mass of M_A = 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The strange axial coupling constant is taken as g^s_A = -0.08 ± 0.05, estimated by adding in quadrature the COMPASS measurement error and the uncertainty from possible SU(3)_f breaking effects.
- In the interval 0.2 ≤ E_ν ≤ 10 GeV, the neutron-knockout cross section for neutrinos changes by less than 5.71% (when g^s_A = -0.13) and less than 5.90% (when g^s_A = -0.03) relative to the central value.
- In the same energy interval, the neutron-knockout cross section for antineutrinos changes by less than 7.62% (when g^s_A = -0.13) and less than 8.08% (when g^s_A = -0.03).
- At probe energy 0.6 GeV, using M_A = 1.03 GeV instead of 1.2 GeV would decrease total NC QE cross sections by 13.6% for neutrinos and 16.3% for antineutrinos, translating into a 12.1% and 12.6% change of neutron-knockout cross sections respectively.
- The COMPASS Collaboration extracted Δs = -0.08 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.02 (syst) from inclusive polarized deep-inelastic scattering on a ⁶LiD target.

## Context

This work extends the spectral-function-based approach previously applied to carbon targets (Ankowski and Benhar, 2012) to oxygen, which is the relevant nucleus for water-Cherenkov detectors like Super-Kamiokande. The calculations are directly applicable to the ongoing searches for diffuse supernova neutrinos (Super-Kamiokande Collaboration, 2012) and for sterile neutrinos in the T2K experiment. The results enable improved background estimates for these searches, particularly for events involving neutron production followed by gamma-ray emission that can mimic the inverse beta decay signal used for diffuse supernova neutrino detection.
