# SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry and nuclear shapes

> We consider several variants of SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry in relation to quadrupole shapes in nuclei. Explicit construction of Hamiltonians with such property is presented in the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM), including higher order terms, and in its proton-neutron extension (IBM-2). The cases considered include a single prolate-deformed shape with solvable ground and $γ$ or $β$ bands, coexisting prolate-oblate shapes with solvable ground bands, and aligned axially-deformed proton-neutron shapes with solvable symmetric ground and $γ$ bands and mixed-symmetry scissors and $γ$ bands.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) can be realized in the interacting boson model (IBM) and its proton-neutron extension (IBM-2) such that only a subset of nuclear states (e.g., ground, gamma, or beta bands) retain exact SU(3) symmetry and analytic solvability, while other states are mixed, a condition the abstract describes as "partial" rather than complete symmetry.

## Method

The author constructs Hamiltonians as sums of SU(3)-tensor products that annihilate specific SU(3) irreducible representations (irreps), ensuring those states remain zero-energy eigenstates with good SU(3) quantum numbers. For a single prolate shape, the tensors are boson-pair operators (e.g., \(P_0\), \(P_{2m}\)) and three-boson operators (e.g., \(W_{\ell m}\), \(\Lambda\)) that annihilate the \((2N,0)\) ground-band irrep and, depending on the choice, also annihilate either the \(\gamma^k\) bands or the \(\beta\) band. For shape coexistence, the method is extended to require simultaneous annihilation of states in two different irreps (e.g., \((2N,0)\) for prolate and \((0,2N)\) for oblate) from incompatible symmetry chains.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{168}\)Er: SU(3)-PDS Hamiltonian parameters \(h_2 = 4\) keV, \(C = 13\) keV, \(h_3 = 4\) keV, with boson number \(N = 16\); the beta band contains 13% admixtures into the dominant \((2N-4,2)\) irrep.
- For \(^{156}\)Gd: SU(3)-PDS Hamiltonian parameters \(h_1 = 0\), \(h_2 = 7.6\) keV, \(C = 12.0\) keV, \(h_6 = -0.23\) keV, \(h_7 = 1.54\) keV, with \(N = 12\); the gamma band contains 15% SU(3) admixtures into the dominant \((2N-4,2)\) irrep.
- For \(^{156}\)Gd: E2 operator parameters \(e_{\rm b} = 0.166\) \(eb\) and \(\chi = -0.168\); measured \(B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1) = 0.933\) (uncertainty 25) and PDS reproduces this value exactly; measured \(B(E2; 0^+_\beta \to 2^+_1)\) ranges from 0.021 to 0.055, with PDS predicting 0.034.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry concept introduced by Leviatan (2011) and the algorithm for constructing PDS Hamiltonians developed by Leviatan (1992) and further elaborated by Ramos, Leviatan, and Van Isacker (2009). It builds on Elliott's SU(3) model (1958) and the interacting boson model of Iachello and Arima. The results enable parameter-free predictions for E2 transition ratios in deformed nuclei like \(^{168}\)Er and \(^{156}\)Gd, and provide a framework for describing shape coexistence with multiple partial symmetries, as seen in nuclei near shell closures (Heyde, 2011).
