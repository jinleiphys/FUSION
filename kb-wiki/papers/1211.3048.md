# Exclusive diffractive processes in electron-ion collisions

> We present a new technique to calculate the cross-section for diffractive vector meson production and DVCS in electron-ion collisions based on the dipole model. The measurement of these processes can provide valuable information on non-linear QCD phenomena, such as gluon saturation, and is the the only known way to gain insight into the spatial distribution of gluons in nuclei. We present predictions of differential cross-section distribution $dσ/dQ^2$ and $dσ/dt$ for $J/ψ$ and $ϕ$ meson production for diffractive processes of heavy nuclei and demonstrate the feasibility of extracting the gluon source distribution of heavy nuclei, F(b), from coherent diffraction. We briefly introduce a new event generator based on our method that can be used for studying exclusive diffractive processes at a future electron-ion collider.

## Key claim

The authors present a new technique for calculating both coherent and incoherent diffractive cross-sections in electron-ion collisions using the bSat dipole model, without introducing approximations beyond those already inherent in the model, and demonstrate that the gluon spatial distribution of heavy nuclei can be extracted from coherent diffraction data at a future electron-ion collider.

## Method

The authors extend the bSat (impact-parameter dependent saturation) dipole model from electron-proton to electron-nucleus collisions by treating the nucleus as a collection of nucleons distributed according to a Woods-Saxon transverse density profile, using the independent scattering approximation where the nuclear scattering amplitude is constructed as a product over individual nucleon amplitudes. The total, coherent, and incoherent diffractive cross-sections for exclusive vector meson production (J/ψ and φ) and DVCS are computed by averaging over nucleon configurations, with the first moment of the amplitude calculated analytically and the second moment obtained through Monte Carlo sampling over 500 nucleon configurations. A non-saturated version of the model (bNonSat) is constructed by linearizing the dipole cross-section to study saturation effects.

## Key numbers

- For the bSat model parameters fitted to HERA data: B_G = 4 GeV^{-2}, μ_0^2 = 1.17 GeV^2, λ_g = 0.02, A_g = 2.55; quark masses: m_u = m_d = m_s = 0.14 GeV, m_c = 1.4 GeV
- For the bNonSat model parameters: B_G = 4 GeV^{-2}, μ_0^2 = 0.8 GeV^2, λ_g = -0.13, A_g = 3.5; quark masses: m_u = m_d = m_s = 0.15 GeV, m_c = 1.4 GeV
- Approximately 500 nucleon configurations are needed for convergence of the total cross-section for -t < 0.3 GeV^2
- The skewedness and real-part corrections together amount to approximately 60% of the cross-section, with the skewedness correction contributing about 45%
- Predictions are shown for EIC beam energies of 20 GeV for the electron and 100 GeV/u for the ion beam, with an integrated luminosity of 10 fb^{-1}
- A cut-off in dipole radius of r < 3 fm for protons and r < 3R_0 for nuclei is introduced, where R_0 is the nuclear radius from the Woods-Saxon parametrization
- The cross-sections are scaled by a factor A^{4/3}, where A is the atomic number (approximately 200 for gold)

## Context

This work extends the bSat dipole model, previously applied successfully to HERA electron-proton data by Kowalski, Teaney, and Motyka (2006), to electron-nucleus collisions for the first time with full treatment of both coherent and incoherent cross-sections at all momentum transfers t. Previous attempts to describe exclusive eA collisions (Kowalski 2003, Caldwell 2010, Lappi 2010) failed to describe the disappearance of the incoherent cross-section as t approaches zero and were poorly suited for Monte Carlo implementation. The results enable direct measurements of the spatial gluon distribution in nuclei at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Large Hadron-electron Collider (LHeC), which would provide the initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions and test gluon saturation predictions.
