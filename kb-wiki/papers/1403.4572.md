# On some properties of Deuteron and Antideuteron production in high energy lead-lead collisions at 158A GeV

> We would attempt here to understand some properties of the transverse momentum ($p_T$) and rapidity ($y$) spectra on production of deuteron ($d$) and antideuteron($\overline{d}$) in lead-lead $(Pb+Pb)$ collisions at 158A GeV recently reported by NA49 collaboration. Starting from some basic properties of $p+p$ reactions for production of secondary proton-antiprotons the cases of production of the composite set of particles, like $d$ and $\overline{d}$, would be analysed. Some ratio-behaviours would also be dealt with in the light of the same approaches. It is found that the combination of the models put into use here capture modestly well the trends of the data on some important observables. Some limitations of the approach would also be pointed out in the end.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new combination of models (NCM), specifically Hagedorn's Model for nucleon-nucleon collisions combined with a polynomial parametrization of the nuclear mass-number dependence (De-Bhattacharyya Parametrization, DBP), can capture the trends of deuteron and antideuteron transverse momentum and rapidity spectra in lead-lead collisions at 158A GeV with "modest" or "fair" agreement, but the authors explicitly note that the antideuteron data are "too sparse" and suffer from "high degree of measuremental uncertainties with large error bars," and that the approach is "purely phenomenological" with free parameters that lack physical identification.

## Method

The authors start from Hagedorn's Model for particle production in proton-proton collisions, using a power-law form for the inclusive cross-section as a function of transverse momentum. They then apply Peitzmann's Approach to scale these results to nucleus-nucleus collisions by introducing a polynomial exponent \(f(p_T) = (1 + \alpha p_T - \beta p_T^2)\) on the product of the nuclear mass numbers (A.B), which they call the De-Bhattacharyya Parametrization (DBP). For rapidity spectra, they use a three-parameter Fermi-type function for proton-proton collisions and scale it to nucleus-nucleus collisions using a similar polynomial exponent \(f(y) = \alpha' + \beta' y + \gamma' y^2\) on (A.B), setting \(\beta' = 0\) for symmetric collisions.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of rapidity-density of deuteron to antideuteron for Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV (\(\sqrt{s} \approx 17.3\) GeV) is predicted to be \(\sim 3.2 \times 10^{-3}\).
* For proton-proton collisions at ISR energies (\(\sqrt{s} \sim 20\) GeV to 62.4 GeV), the parameter \(\Delta\) in the rapidity distribution is \(\sim 0.30\) for deuterons/antideuterons, \(\sim 0.35\) for protons/antiprotons, and \(\sim 0.55\) for pions and kaons.
* The energy-dependence of the rapidity parameter \(y_0\) is empirically given by \(y_0 = k \ln \sqrt{s_{NN}} + 0.8\), where \(k\) varies slowly with center-of-mass energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of the De-Bhattacharyya Parametrization to particle-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions (De and Bhattacharyya, various years) and is directly motivated by the first measurements of deuteron and antideuteron production at various centralities in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV by the NA49 collaboration (Anticic et al.). The approach contrasts with the standard coalescence model, and the authors note that their model-based values of the coalescence parameter are somewhat lower than those from pure coalescence, suggesting either the models need refinement or the coalescence picture requires modification. The work enables future comparisons with cosmic antideuteron flux data from experiments such as p-GAPS (Mognet et al., Fuke et al.), expected by 2017/2018.
