# Fluctuations, correlations and some other recent results from lattice QCD

> I summarize recent results from lattice QCD on a few selected topics which are of interest to the heavy-ion physics community. Special emphasis is placed upon observables related to fluctuations of conserved charges and their connection to event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD results for the QCD transition temperature, the curvature of the chiral transition line, and generalized susceptibilities of conserved charges are now precise enough to allow direct comparison with heavy-ion collision experiments, but the comparisons rely on the assumption that the freeze-out conditions can be modeled by the hadron resonance gas model and that the lattice calculations use improved actions (such as HISQ) to control discretization errors.

## Method

The paper summarizes lattice QCD calculations using staggered fermion discretizations (p4fat3, Asqtad, HISQ, and stout actions) on finite-temperature lattices. The transition temperature is extracted from chiral observables via O(N) scaling analysis and continuum extrapolation. The curvature of the chiral transition line at small baryon chemical potential is determined by studying the universal scaling properties of the "thermal" susceptibility of the chiral order parameter. Generalized baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness susceptibilities are computed via Taylor expansion of the QCD partition function around zero chemical potential, and ratios of these susceptibilities are compared to experimentally measured moments of net-proton fluctuations from the STAR experiment.

## Key numbers

* Transition temperature at physical quark masses: T_c = 157 ± 4 ± 3 ± 1 MeV (HotQCD preliminary, continuum extrapolated)
* Previously published transition temperature range: T_c = 147-157 MeV (Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, using observables sensitive to light quarks)
* Curvature of the chiral transition line: κ_B = 0.0066(9) (BNL-Bielefeld-GSI collaboration, using p4fat3 staggered fermions)
* The phase boundary for physical quark masses agrees with the freeze-out curve up to μ_B ~ 150 MeV, then slowly deviates
* O(N) scaling of the order parameter holds for light quark masses m_l ≲ m_s/20, corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass m_π ≃ 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of the QCD transition by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al.) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Fodor et al., Borsanyi et al.), and the scaling analysis of the chiral transition by the BNL-Bielefeld collaboration (Ejiri et al.). The curvature determination by the BNL-Bielefeld-GSI collaboration (Kaczmarek et al.) enables comparison of the phase boundary with the chemical freeze-out curve from Cleymans et al. The comparison of lattice QCD susceptibilities with STAR experimental data on net-proton fluctuations (STAR collaboration) represents a first direct link between lattice QCD and heavy-ion collision measurements. Future work requires using improved actions like HISQ for continuum-extrapolated higher-order susceptibilities, especially for electric charge fluctuations.
