# Role of neutron transfer in the reaction mechanism of $^9$Be+$^{169}$Tm, $^{181}$Ta, $^{187}$Re and $^{197}$Au systems

> The contribution of one neutron stripping cross section to the total reaction cross section has been studied for $^9$Be projectile incident on $^{169}$Tm, $^{181}$Ta, $^{187}$Re and $^{197}$Au targets around Coulomb barrier energy. The measured one neutron stripping cross sections for these systems have been compared with the coupled channel calculations. The recently developed global set of optical model potential parameters for $^9$Be projectile has been used in the present calculations. The cumulative of measured complete fusion (CF), incomplete fusion (ICF), one neutron stripping and calculated non-capture breakup (NCBU) cross sections is found to explain almost the reaction cross sections for all the targets. A very small contribution from target inelastic states and elastic breakup may contribute to the remaining part. The percentage fraction of cross section for CF, ICF, one neutron stripping, and NCBU over reaction cross section show the dominance of neutron transfer and NCBU at below barrier energies while CF and ICF processes have the major contribution at above barrier energies.

## Key claim

The cumulative cross sections of measured complete fusion (CF), incomplete fusion (ICF), one neutron stripping, and calculated non-capture breakup (NCBU) account for almost all of the reaction cross section for the four systems studied, with a small remaining fraction (about 10%) possibly attributable to target inelastic excitations and elastic breakup that were not estimated.

## Method

Coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations using the FRESCO code were performed to estimate one neutron stripping cross sections, employing a recently developed global optical model potential for the ⁹Be projectile. Continuum Discretised Coupled Channel (CDCC) and combined CDCC+CRC calculations were carried out to estimate non-capture breakup (NCBU) and neutron transfer cross sections simultaneously, treating ⁹Be as a ⁸Be+n cluster structure. The measured CF and ICF cross sections from prior work were combined with the calculated transfer and NCBU cross sections and compared to the reaction cross sections derived from the global optical potential.

## Key numbers

- One neutron stripping cross sections were measured for ⁹Be+¹⁶⁹Tm, ¹⁸¹Ta, ¹⁸⁷Re, and ¹⁹⁷Au systems over a wide energy range around the Coulomb barrier.
- The ICF contribution ranges between 5% and 20% for all four target systems across the energies studied.
- The cumulative fraction of CF, ICF, one neutron transfer, and NCBU leaves a remaining difference of about 10% relative to the total reaction cross section, attributed to unestimated target inelastic excitations.
- The ⁹Be projectile has a neutron separation energy Sₙ = 1.67 MeV, which is the smallest among the projectiles compared (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ¹⁰B), and yields the highest neutron transfer cross section.
- The crossover energy where CF and ICF begin to dominate over neutron transfer and NCBU occurs at approximately the Coulomb barrier energy V_b for all target systems.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of ⁹Be-induced reactions on heavy targets, building on prior measurements of one neutron stripping cross sections by Fang (2016) and Kaushik (2021), and CF/ICF measurements by Fang (2015), Zhang (2014), and Kaushik (2020). The results contrast with those for ⁶Li and ⁷Li projectiles, where ICF and CF dominate even at below-barrier energies, highlighting the unique role of the ⁸Be+n cluster structure in ⁹Be. The findings enable future investigations of neutron transfer systematics with projectiles having intermediate neutron separation energies, such as ¹³C (Sₙ = 4.95 MeV), and with unstable nuclei having even lower Sₙ values.
