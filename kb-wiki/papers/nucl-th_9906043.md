# Magnetic Monopole Decay and Its Consequences

> We return to further examination of the need for the existence of magnetic monopoles based on the decades old derivation [Sherman Frankel, Amer. Jour. of Physics, 44, 7, 683-6 1976], of both the monopole-charge force, vec(F) = eg(vec(r) * vec(v)/c)/r^3, as well as the Dirac angular momentum, vec(l_f) = eg hat(r), from P and T conservation alone, without any recourse whatsoever to Maxwell's Equations. The eg product also appeared in the charge conserving equation, deg/dt = 0 = edg/dt + gde/dt, and this paper examines the second possible solution, dg/dt = - de/dt, namely monopole decay, accounting for the non-observance of stable monopoles. It treats astrophysics and neutrino physics consequences and suggestions for experimental searches.

# Magnetic Monopole Decay and Its Consequences

## Key claim

The paper argues that the non-observance of stable magnetic monopoles can be explained by monopole decay, which is a valid solution to the generalized charge conservation equation d(ge)/dt = 0 = g de/dt + e dg/dt, where the alternative solution dg/dt = -de/dt allows monopoles to decay into ordinary charge. This derivation follows from parity and time reversal invariance alone, without recourse to Maxwell's Equations, but the author acknowledges this is a classical theory and that incorporating monopole decay into a full quantum-mechanical theory cannot be accomplished by merely inserting monopoles into classical Maxwell Equations.

## Method

The author extends a previous derivation (Frankel, American Journal of Physics, 1976) that obtained the monopole-charge force F = eg(r × v/c)/r^3 and the Dirac field angular momentum l_f = eg r̂ from P and T conservation and charge conservation alone. The paper examines the second solution to the charge conservation equation d(ge)/dt = 0, namely dg/dt = -de/dt (monopole decay), and explores its consequences for astrophysics, neutrino physics, and experimental searches at accelerators.

## Key numbers

- The monopole fine structure constant g^2/ħc is at least (137)^2 times larger than the electric fine structure constant e^2/ħc
- The field angular momentum quantization condition is |l_f| = eg = nħ, where n is a ratio of integers
- A fourth neutrino from monopole decay would need a mass greater than 45 GeV if the leptom were coupled to the Z boson in the same way as ordinary leptons

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier derivation (Frankel, 1976) of monopole-charge interactions from P and T invariance, and references Dirac's original argument that a single monopole would result in quantized electric charge, as well as H. A. Wilson's classical derivation and Goldhaber's impulse approximation treatment. The paper proposes that monopole decay could account for the failure to observe stable monopoles despite over half a century of searches, and suggests experimental searches for monopole photon bursts, monopole neutrinos, monopole production at Fermilab, and possible spatial asymmetry in the universe using existing detectors.
