# Quantum Algebraic Symmetries in Nuclear and Molecular Physics

> Various applications of quantum algebraic techniques in nuclear structure physics and molecular physics are briefly reviewed. Contains 81 references.

## Key claim

This paper reviews how quantum algebraic techniques (deformed Lie algebras, or quantum groups) can be applied to nuclear structure and molecular physics, but it is a survey of existing work rather than a presentation of new results; the authors state that the purpose is "to provide the reader with references for further reading."

## Method

The authors compile and summarize published applications of quantum algebras (primarily su$_q$(2) and its generalizations) to rotational and vibrational spectra of nuclei and molecules, pairing correlations, deformed versions of nuclear models (such as the Interacting Boson Model and the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model), and the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator. They also review molecular applications including diatomic rotational and vibrational spectra, the vibron model, and quasi-molecular resonances, citing 81 references.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter $\tau$ (with $q = e^{i\tau}$) in the su$_q$(2) rotator model is found to correspond to the softness parameter of the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model.
- In diatomic molecules, $\tau$ is of the order of 0.01.
- The su$_q$(2) model predicts an increase of B(E2) values with angular momentum, while the rigid rotator model predicts saturation.
- For vibrational and transitional nuclear spectra, $J(J+1)$ is replaced by $J(J+c)$, where $c$ is an additional parameter.
- The deformation parameter $\tau$ in pairing correlations is inversely proportional to the size of the shell.
- The o$_q$(3) limit of a toy IBM model has been used for the description of $^{16}$O + $\alpha$ cluster states in $^{20}$Ne, with positive results.
- Quasi-molecular resonances in the systems $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O have been described in terms of a $q$-deformed oscillator plus a rigid rotator.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on quantum algebras in physics, which began with the introduction of the $q$-deformed harmonic oscillator for boson realizations of su$_q$(2) and initial applications to spin chains and squeezed states. It connects the su$_q$(2) rotator model to the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), and it relates the deformation parameter to nuclear deformation $\beta$ and valence nucleon pairs. The review enables further exploration of $q$-deformed versions of nuclear models (such as the Moszkowski model and the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model) and molecular models (such as the vibron model and coupled deformed oscillators for polyatomic molecules), and it points to open questions such as the full su$_q$(3) decomposition for the IBM and the use of complex $q$ values.
