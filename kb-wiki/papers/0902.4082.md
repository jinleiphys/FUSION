# Lepton FCNC in Type III Seesaw Model

> In Type III seesaw model, there are tree level flavor changing neutral currents (FCNC) in the lepton sector, due to mixing of charged particles in the leptonic triplet introduced to realize seesaw mechanism, with the usual charged leptons. In this work we study these FCNC effects in a systematic way using available experimental data. Several FCNC processes have been studied before. The new processes considered in this work include: lepton flavor violating processes $τ\to P l$, $τ\to V l$, $V \to l \bar l'$, $P \to l \bar l'$, $M\to M' l \bar l'$ and muonium-antimuonium oscillation. Results obtained are compared with previous results from $l_i \to l_j l_k \bar l_l$, $l_i \to l_j γ$, $Z \to l \bar l'$ and $μ- e$ conversion. Our results show that the most stringent constraint on the $e$-to-$τ$ FCNC effect comes from $τ\to π^0 e$ decay. $τ\to ρ^0 μ$ and $τ\to π^0 μ$ give very stringent constraints on the $μ$-to-$τ$ FCNC effect, comparable with that obtained from $τ\to μ\bar μμ$ studied previously. The constraint on the $e$-to-$μ$ FCNC effect from processes considered in this work is much weaker than that obtained from processes studies previously, in particular that from $μ- e$ conversion in atomic nuclei. We find that in the canonical seesaw models the FCNC parameters, due to tiny neutrino masses, are all predicted to be much smaller than the constraints obtained here, making such models irrelevant. However, we also find that in certain special circumstances the tiny neutrino masses do not directly constrain the FCNC parameters. In these situations, the constraints from the FCNC studies can still play important roles.

# Lepton FCNC in Type III Seesaw Model

## Key claim

In the Type III seesaw model, tree-level flavor changing neutral currents (FCNC) in the lepton sector arise from mixing between the charged leptons and the charged components of the fermion triplets; the authors systematically constrain the FCNC parameter matrix epsilon using available experimental data, finding that the most stringent constraint on the e-to-tau FCNC comes from tau to pi0 e decay, while the constraint on e-to-mu FCNC from the new processes considered is much weaker than that from mu-e conversion studied previously. The authors note that in canonical seesaw models the FCNC parameters are predicted to be much smaller than these constraints, but in special circumstances where tiny neutrino masses do not directly constrain the FCNC parameters, these constraints can still play important roles.

## Method

The authors computed decay rates for lepton flavor violating processes mediated by tree-level Z boson exchange, using the effective FCNC coupling parameter epsilon_{ll'} that arises from the mixing between the leptonic triplet and standard model charged leptons. They derived analytic expressions for tau to pseudoscalar meson l (P = pi0, eta, eta') and tau to vector meson l (V = rho0, omega, phi) decays, for vector meson to l lbar' and pseudoscalar meson to l lbar' decays, for semileptonic three-body meson decays (B to K, K*, pi and K to pi with l lbar'), and for muonium-antimuonium oscillation, then compared the resulting constraints on |epsilon_{ll'}| with previous results from li to lj lk lbar l, li to lj gamma, Z to l lbar', and mu-e conversion.

## Key numbers

- tau- to pi0 e- branching ratio < 8.0 x 10^{-8} gives |epsilon_{e tau}| < 4.2 x 10^{-4}
- tau- to pi0 mu- branching ratio < 1.1 x 10^{-7} gives |epsilon_{mu tau}| < 7.0 x 10^{-4}
- tau- to rho0 mu- branching ratio < 6.8 x 10^{-8} gives |epsilon_{mu tau}| < 6.8 x 10^{-4}
- mu-e conversion rate < 4.3 x 10^{-12} gives |epsilon_{e mu}| < 1.7 x 10^{-7}
- mu- to e+ e- e- branching ratio < 1 x 10^{-12} gives |epsilon_{e mu}| < 1.1 x 10^{-6}
- tau- to mu+ mu- mu- branching ratio < 3.2 x 10^{-8} gives |epsilon_{mu tau}| < 4.9 x 10^{-4}
- tau- to e+ e- e- branching ratio < 3.6 x 10^{-8} gives |epsilon_{e tau}| < 5.1 x 10^{-4}
- Z to e+/- mu-/+ branching ratio < 1.7 x 10^{-6} gives |epsilon_{e mu}| < 1.8 x 10^{-3}
- B+ to K* (892)+ e+/- mu-/+ branching ratio < 1.4 x 10^{-7} gives |epsilon_{e mu}| < 1.7 x 10^{-2}
- K+ to pi+ e+ mu- branching ratio < 1.3 x 10^{-11} gives |epsilon_{e mu}| < 0.44
- Upsilon (3S) to e+/- tau-/+ branching ratio < 5 x 10^{-6} gives |epsilon_{e tau}| < 0.39
- Muonium-antimuonium oscillation probability P < 8.3 x 10^{-11} (90% C.L.) in 0.1 T magnetic field

## Context

This work extends previous studies of lepton flavor violating processes in the Type III seesaw model, which had examined li to lj lk lbar l, li to lj gamma, Z to l lbar', and mu-e conversion (He et al., earlier works). The new processes considered include tau to P l, tau to V l, V to l lbar', P to l lbar', M to M' l lbar', and muonium-antimuonium oscillation. The results enable future experimental searches to directly constrain the FCNC parameter epsilon in the Type III seesaw framework, particularly for the e-tau and mu-tau sectors where tau decays to pseudoscalar and vector mesons provide the most stringent bounds.
