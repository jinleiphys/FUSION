# Assisted tunneling of a wave packet between square barriers

> The assisted tunneling of a wave packet between square one dimensional barriers is treated analytically. The tunneling rate is calculated exactly for a potential mimicking a constant electric field with arbitrary time dependence. The pole spectrum of the barriers determines the decay time. The tunneling probability is enhanced by the perturbation. The results are exemplified for a simplified model of nuclear $\ds α$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an external time-dependent perturbation can enhance the tunneling probability of a wave packet between square barriers, but this enhancement is significant only when the perturbation frequency is sufficiently high to keep the argument of the oscillatory harmonic functions in the amplitudes of order one; otherwise, the pole contributions that dominate the decay are suppressed.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for a one-dimensional system with two square barriers and a spatially linear, time-dependent perturbation potential that mimics a constant electric field. They apply a unitary transformation to remove the explicit space dependence of the perturbation, then expand the transformed wave function in the complete set of even and odd stationary states of the unperturbed system. The resulting coupled equations for the expansion amplitudes are solved exactly for an initial Gaussian wave packet, and the wave function is evaluated by contour integration in the complex momentum plane, where the poles of the normalization factors govern the exponential decay.

## Key numbers

* The square barrier parameters used for a typical alpha decay model are: barrier height gamma = 22 MeV (0.11 fm^-1), inner barrier edge x_0 = 12 fm, outer barrier edge d = 22 fm, and alpha particle mass m = 3727 MeV (18.88 fm^-1).
* The initial Gaussian wave packet width is Delta = 4 fm.
* For the harmonic perturbation mimicking an electric field, the parameters used are field strength E = 0.1 Volt/m and frequency omega = 10 pi x 10^17 Hz.
* The unperturbed decay time for the square barrier case is orders of magnitude larger than the delta barrier case, which has a decay time of around 10^-16 seconds.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analytical treatment of assisted tunneling between delta function barriers (Kalbermann, earlier work) to the more realistic case of square barriers, which better represent the potentials involved in nuclear alpha decay. The method builds on the standard quantum tunneling paradigm established by Gurney, Condon, and Gamow for alpha decay lifetimes. The results enable future work on three-dimensional generalizations, applications to nuclear technologies and solid-state devices, and the inclusion of electron screening effects for experimentally meaningful predictions.
