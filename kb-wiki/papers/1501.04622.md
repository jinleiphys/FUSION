# Color confinement and screening in the $\mathbf╬Ė$ -vacuum

> QCD perturbation theory ignores the compact nature of $SU(3)$ gauge group that gives rise to the periodic $╬Ė$-vacuum of the theory. We propose to modify the gluon propagator to reconcile perturbation theory with the anomalous Ward identities for the topological current in the $╬Ė$-vacuum. As a result, the gluon couples to the Veneziano ghost describing the tunneling transitions between different Chern-Simons sectors of the vacuum; we call the emerging gluon dressed by ghost loops a "glost". We evaluate the glost propagator and find that it has the form $G(p) = (p^2 + Žć_{top}/p^2)^{-1}$ where $Žć_{top}$ is the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility related to the $╬Ę'$ mass by Witten-Veneziano relation; this propagator describes confinement of gluons at distances $\sim Žć_{top}^{-1/4} \simeq 1$ fm. The same functional form of the propagator was originally proposed by Gribov as a solution to the gauge copies problem that plagues perturbation theory. The resulting running coupling coincides with the perturbative one at $p^2 \gg \sqrt{Žć_{top}}$, but in the infrared region either freezes (in pure Yang-Mills theory) or vanishes (in full QCD with light quarks), in accord with experimental evidence. Our scenario makes explicit the connection between confinement and topology of the QCD vacuum; we discuss the implications for spin physics, high energy scattering, and the physics of quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that modifying the gluon propagator to account for the periodic ╬Ė-vacuum structure of QCD, which is ignored in standard perturbation theory, yields a "glost" propagator that describes gluon confinement and resolves the Gribov copy problem, provided the topological susceptibility Žć_top is non-zero.

## Method

The authors introduce a coupling between the gluon and the Veneziano ghost, which saturates the anomalous Ward identity for the topological current in the ╬Ė-vacuum. They evaluate the resulting gluon self-energy from ghost loops and solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation to obtain the dressed gluon (glost) propagator. The running coupling is then computed in Coulomb gauge using this modified propagator, with the topological susceptibility Žć_top = ╬╝^4 serving as the dimensionful parameter.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov (1978) approach to gauge copies by identifying the Gribov parameter with the topological susceptibility Žć_top, linking confinement directly to the topology of the QCD vacuum. It builds on the Veneziano (1979) ghost mechanism for solving the U_A(1) problem and the Witten-Veneziano relation connecting the ╬Ę' mass to topological susceptibility. The resulting framework enables practical computation of power-suppressed corrections to QCD amplitudes and has implications for spin physics, high-energy scattering (including the Froissart bound), and the behavior of the quark-gluon plasma near the deconfinement transition.
