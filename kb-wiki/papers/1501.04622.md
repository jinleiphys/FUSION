# Color confinement and screening in the $\mathbfθ$ -vacuum

> QCD perturbation theory ignores the compact nature of $SU(3)$ gauge group that gives rise to the periodic $θ$-vacuum of the theory. We propose to modify the gluon propagator to reconcile perturbation theory with the anomalous Ward identities for the topological current in the $θ$-vacuum. As a result, the gluon couples to the Veneziano ghost describing the tunneling transitions between different Chern-Simons sectors of the vacuum; we call the emerging gluon dressed by ghost loops a "glost". We evaluate the glost propagator and find that it has the form $G(p) = (p^2 + χ_{top}/p^2)^{-1}$ where $χ_{top}$ is the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility related to the $η'$ mass by Witten-Veneziano relation; this propagator describes confinement of gluons at distances $\sim χ_{top}^{-1/4} \simeq 1$ fm. The same functional form of the propagator was originally proposed by Gribov as a solution to the gauge copies problem that plagues perturbation theory. The resulting running coupling coincides with the perturbative one at $p^2 \gg \sqrt{χ_{top}}$, but in the infrared region either freezes (in pure Yang-Mills theory) or vanishes (in full QCD with light quarks), in accord with experimental evidence. Our scenario makes explicit the connection between confinement and topology of the QCD vacuum; we discuss the implications for spin physics, high energy scattering, and the physics of quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that modifying the gluon propagator to account for the periodic θ-vacuum structure of QCD, which is ignored in standard perturbation theory, yields a "glost" propagator that describes gluon confinement and resolves the Gribov copy problem, provided the topological susceptibility χ_top is non-zero.

## Method

The authors introduce a coupling between the gluon and the Veneziano ghost, which saturates the anomalous Ward identity for the topological current in the θ-vacuum. They evaluate the resulting gluon self-energy from ghost loops and solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation to obtain the dressed gluon (glost) propagator. The running coupling is then computed in Coulomb gauge using this modified propagator, with the topological susceptibility χ_top = μ^4 serving as the dimensionful parameter.

## Key numbers

* The glost propagator has the form G(p) = (p^2 + χ_top/p^2)^{-1}, where χ_top is the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility.
* The confinement distance is approximately χ_top^{-1/4} ≃ 1 fm.
* The topological susceptibility parameter μ is taken as μ = 0.18 GeV, determined from the η' meson mass via the Witten-Veneziano relation.
* For QCD with N_c = N_f = 3, the integrated coupling α_0 = (1/μ_I) ∫^{μ_I} dk α_s(k) ≈ 0.59 for μ_I = 2 GeV, compared to the experimental value of approximately 0.5.
* The running coupling coincides with perturbative QCD at p^2 ≫ √χ_top, but in the infrared either freezes (pure Yang-Mills) or vanishes (QCD with light quarks).

## Context

This work extends the Gribov (1978) approach to gauge copies by identifying the Gribov parameter with the topological susceptibility χ_top, linking confinement directly to the topology of the QCD vacuum. It builds on the Veneziano (1979) ghost mechanism for solving the U_A(1) problem and the Witten-Veneziano relation connecting the η' mass to topological susceptibility. The resulting framework enables practical computation of power-suppressed corrections to QCD amplitudes and has implications for spin physics, high-energy scattering (including the Froissart bound), and the behavior of the quark-gluon plasma near the deconfinement transition.
