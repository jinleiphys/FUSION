# Pure Collective Precession Motion of High-Spin Torus Isomer

> We investigate the precession motion of the exotic torus configuration in high-spin excited states of $^{40}$Ca. For this aim, we use the three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method. Although the high-spin torus isomer is a unique quantum object characterized by the alignment of angular momenta of independent single-particle motions, we find that the obtained moment of inertia for rotations about an axis perpendicular to the symmetry axis is close to the rigid-body value. We also analyze the microscopic structure of the precession motion using the random-phase approximation (RPA) method for high-spin states. In the RPA calculation, the precession motion of the torus isomer is generated by coherent superposition of many one-particle-one-hole excitations across the sloping Fermi surface that strongly violates the time-reversal symmetry. By comparing results of the TDHF and the RPA calculations, we find that the precession motion obtained by the TDHF calculation is a pure collective motion well decoupled from other collective modes.

# Pure Collective Precession Motion of High-Spin Torus Isomer

## Key claim

The high-spin torus isomer in ⁴⁰Ca, despite being a unique quantum object built from aligned single-particle angular momenta with strong time-reversal symmetry violation, performs collective precession about a perpendicular axis with a moment of inertia close to the classical rigid-body value, and this precession mode is well decoupled from other collective modes.

## Method

The authors used the three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method with the Sky3D code and SLy6 interaction to simulate the precession motion of the torus isomer in ⁴⁰Ca with total angular momentum K=60 ℏ. They triggered the precession by applying an impulsive external potential at t=0 to give the system total angular momentum I=61 ℏ, then tracked the time evolution of the density distribution for 3000 fm/c (about 7.5 periods). Separately, they performed random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations using a radial displaced harmonic-oscillator (RDHO) model to analyze the microscopic particle-hole structure of the precession mode.

## Key numbers

- The torus isomer in ⁴⁰Ca has total angular momentum K=60 ℏ, generated by alignment of orbital angular momenta Λ=+4, +5, and +6 ℏ for spin-up and spin-down neutrons and protons
- The density distribution is fitted by ρ(r)=ρ₀e^{-(r-R₀)²/σ²} with ρ₀=0.12 fm⁻³, R₀=6.06 fm, and σ=1.64 fm
- The rigid-body moment of inertia calculated from the density distribution is 𝒯_⟂^{rid}=21.1 ℏ²/MeV
- The average precession period from TDHF is T_prec=403.0 fm/c, giving ω_prec=3.08 MeV/ℏ
- The TDHF moment of inertia is 𝒯_⟂^{TDHF}=19.8 ℏ²/MeV, close to the rigid-body value
- The RPA calculation with the RDHO model yields ω_RPA=3.02 MeV/ℏ and 𝒯_⟂^{RPA}=19.6 ℏ²/MeV; replacing K with I=K+1 gives ω_RPA=3.07 MeV/ℏ
- The adiabatic cranking moment of inertia is 𝒯_⟂^{crank}=20.0 ℏ²/MeV
- The tilting angle fluctuates between 10° and 11° during the precession

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding of a stable torus configuration in high-spin excited states of ⁴⁰Ca (Ichikawa 2012) by demonstrating its dynamical precession properties. The result that the moment of inertia is close to the rigid-body value contrasts with measured moments of inertia for precession motions of high-K prolate isomers, which are significantly smaller than rigid-body values (delaroche 2004, shimizu 2005), and with expectations for high-K oblate isomers where moments of inertia are thought to be much reduced due to oblate shell structure (andersson 1981). The work enables future experimental searches for precession modes of high-K torus and oblate isomers, and demonstrates that periodic TDHF solutions for rotational modes can be obtained and connected to quantum excitation energies via RPA.
