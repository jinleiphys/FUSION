# Energy Dependence of the NN t-matrix in the Optical Potential for Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering

> The influence of the energy dependence of the free NN t-matrix on the optical potential of nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering is investigated within the context of a full-folding model based on the impulse approximation. The treatment of the pole structure of the NN t-matrix, which has to be taken into account when integrating to negative energies is described in detail. We calculate proton-nucleus elastic scattering observables for $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb between 65 and 200 MeV laboratory energy and study the effect of the energy dependence of the NN t-matrix. We compare this result with experiment and with calculations where the center-of-mass energy of the NN t-matrix is fixed at half the projectile energy. It is found that around 200 MeV the fixed energy approximation is a very good representation of the full calculation, however deviations occur when going to lower energies (65 MeV).

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the free NN t-matrix in the full-folding optical potential for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering has a negligible effect on calculated observables at 200 MeV projectile energy, but deviations from the fixed-energy approximation become sizable at 65 MeV, especially due to the deuteron pickup and di-proton virtual state channels.

## Method

The authors computed the first-order full-folding optical potential within the impulse approximation of the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory, using a Dirac-Hartree nuclear density matrix and the charge-dependent Bonn NN potential. They performed the three-dimensional integration coupling the integration variable to the energy of propagation, explicitly treating the pole structure of the NN t-matrix (deuteron bound state at -2.225 MeV and virtual state in the 1S0 channel at -66 keV) by factorizing the pole term and using Cauchy principal value integration. Elastic scattering observables (differential cross section, analyzing power, spin rotation function) were calculated for proton scattering on 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb at laboratory energies from 65 to 200 MeV and compared to calculations where the NN t-matrix energy was fixed at half the projectile energy.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron bound state pole at -2.225 MeV in the 3S1-3D1 channel; virtual state in the 1S0 channel at -66 keV (pole on second sheet of complex energy plane, manifesting as a peak about 100 keV wide)
- For 40Ca at 200 MeV, the (p,d) reaction differential cross section at small angles (10-20 degrees) is about 1 mb/sr; at 65 MeV it is about 10 mb/sr
- For the energy integration at higher projectile energies, integrating out to -100 MeV c.m. energy in the NN t-matrix was sufficient; at 65 MeV, integration out to -400 MeV c.m. energy was needed
- The averaging procedure for the virtual state used 40 energy points over a momentum interval of 0.5 MeV; the numerical error from using an alternative averaging method was 1% for the worst case tested (ENA = 50 MeV for proton scattering from 208Pb)
- For 40Ca at 65 MeV, the full calculation including energy dependence showed visible differences from the fixed-energy calculation, with the differential cross section from the full calculation being higher and slightly closer to experiment

## Context

This work extends the full-folding model calculations of Arellano, Brieva, and Love (1994, 1995) by providing a more complete treatment of the NN t-matrix energy dependence and pole structure, finding smaller differences between full and fixed-energy calculations than those earlier studies suggested. The results confirm that the impulse approximation is insufficient at energies around 100 MeV and below, as previously noted by Chinn, Elster, Thaler, and Weppner (1995). The calculations enable future work requiring a complete Faddeev calculation of the correction term coupling the struck target nucleon to the residual nucleus, which was not possible at the time of publication.
