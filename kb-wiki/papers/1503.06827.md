# Revisiting Scalar Glueballs

> It is commonly believed that the lowest-lying scalar glueball lies somewhere in the isosinglet scalar mesons $f_0(1370), f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$ denoted generically by $f_{0}$. In this work we consider lattice calculations and experimental data to infer the glue and $q\bar q$ components of $f_0$. These include the calculations of the scalar glueball masses in quenched and unquenched lattice QCD, measurements of the radiative decays $J/ψ\toγf_{0}$, the ratio of $f_{0}$ decays to $ππ$ and $K\bar K$, the ratio of $J/ψ$ decays to $f_0(1710)ω$ and $f_0(1710)ϕ$, the $f_0$ contributions to $B_s\to J/ψπ^+π^-$, and the near mass degeneracy of $a_0(1450)$ and $K_0^*(1430)$. All analyses suggest the prominent glueball nature of $f_0(1710)$ and the flavor octet structure of $f_0(1500)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scalar meson \(f_0(1710)\) is predominantly a scalar glueball, while \(f_0(1500)\) is primarily a flavor octet \(q\bar q\) state, contradicting earlier models that favored \(f_0(1500)\) as the dominant glueball. This conclusion is hedged by the recognition that chiral suppression in scalar glueball decays makes the ratio of \(\pi\pi\) to \(K\bar K\) decays naturally small, so the observed small ratio for \(f_0(1710)\) does not rule out a large glueball component.

## Method

The authors construct a mixing model for the three isosinglet scalar mesons \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\), and \(f_0(1710)\) as admixtures of \(n\bar n = (u\bar u + d\bar d)/\sqrt{2}\), \(s\bar s\), and a pure glueball state \(|G\rangle\). They incorporate as inputs the near degeneracy of \(a_0(1450)\) and \(K_0^*(1430)\) (indicating approximate SU(3) symmetry in the scalar quarkonia sector) and the quenched lattice QCD result for the scalar glueball mass near 1710 MeV. The mixing matrix is then fitted to experimental data including radiative \(J/\psi\) decay branching fractions, the ratios of \(f_0\) decays to \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar K\), and the scalar meson contributions to \(B_s \to J/\psi \pi^+ \pi^-\).

## Key numbers

- Scalar glueball mass from quenched lattice QCD (Chen et al., 2006): \(1710 \pm 50 \pm 80\) MeV.
- Scalar glueball mass from unquenched lattice QCD (Gregory et al., 2012): \(1795 \pm 60\) MeV.
- Ratio \(R(f_0(1710)) \equiv \Gamma(f_0(1710) \to \pi\pi) / \Gamma(f_0(1710) \to K\bar K) = 0.31 \pm 0.05\) from CLEO data (Dobbs et al., 2015).
- Ratio \(R(f_0(1500)) \equiv \Gamma(f_0(1500) \to \pi\pi) / \Gamma(f_0(1500) \to K\bar K) = 4.1 \pm 0.5\) from PDG.
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710))\): \((3.27 \pm 1.88) \times 10^{-3}\) from \(f_0(1710) \to \pi\pi\) and \((2.80 \pm 0.96) \times 10^{-3}\) from \(f_0(1710) \to K\bar K\).
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1500))\): \((3.13 \pm 0.73) \times 10^{-4}\) from \(f_0(1500) \to \pi\pi\).
- Lattice QCD result for \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi \to \gamma G)\) (pure glueball): \((3.8 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-3}\).
- In Model II (this work), the mixing matrix yields glueball content in \(f_0(1710)\) of \(0.934 \pm 0.004\) (coefficient \(\gamma_3\)), with \(M_G = 1674 \pm 14\) MeV, \(M_N = 1474\) MeV, and \(M_S = 1496 \pm 14\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier mixing model of Cheng, Chua, and Liu (CCL) and contrasts with Model I (Amsler, Close, and Kirk; Close and Zhao; He et al.; Yuan et al.), where \(f_0(1500)\) is primarily a glueball and \(f_0(1710)\) is mainly \(s\bar s\). The new analysis incorporates updated lattice glueball masses (Chen et al., 2006; Gregory et al., 2012), recent CLEO data on radiative \(J/\psi\) decays (Dobbs et al., 2015), and the near mass degeneracy of \(a_0(1450)\) and \(K_0^*(1430)\) from lattice QCD (Mathur et al., 2006). It enables future lattice calculations to directly compute the overlap of \(f_0(1710)\) with glue and \(q\bar q\) operators in full QCD, and motivates further experimental study of chiral suppression in scalar glueball decays.
