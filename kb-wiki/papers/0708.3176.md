# Integrating out the Dirac sea: Effective field theory approach to exactly solvable four-fermion models

> We use 1+1 dimensional large N Gross-Neveu models as a laboratory to derive microscopically effective Lagrangians for positive energy fermions only. When applied to baryons, the Euler-Lagrange equation for these effective theories assumes the form of a non-linear Dirac equation. Its solution reproduces the full semi-classical results including the Dirac sea to any desired accuracy. Dynamical effects from the Dirac sea are encoded in higher order derivative terms and multi-fermion interactions with perturbatively calculable, finite coefficients. Characteristic differences between models with discrete and continuous chiral symmetry are observed and clarified.

## Key claim

The authors derive an effective Lagrangian for positive-energy fermions only by systematically "integrating out" the Dirac sea in 1+1 dimensional large N Gross-Neveu models, showing that the expansion works quantitatively for small filling fraction and/or large bare fermion mass, but the effective action is only "almost local" and requires resummation of infinite series of diagrams to be meaningful.

## Method

The authors work in the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation and decompose all fermion loop diagrams into contributions from negative-energy (Dirac sea) and positive-energy (valence) states. They systematically compute cactus-type diagrams up to five loops, resumming scalar vacuum polarization bubbles into a momentum-dependent effective coupling using the gap equation, and then construct an effective Lagrangian whose Euler-Lagrange equation yields a non-linear Dirac equation for the valence fermions. The coefficients of the effective Lagrangian are determined by evaluating standard one-loop Feynman diagrams in the vacuum, without reference to finite density or baryons.

## Key numbers

- The leading order effective coupling constant is \(g_{\rm eff}^2 = \pi/N\) for the massless GN\(_2\) model, derived from the resummation of scalar vacuum polarization bubbles.
- The baryon mass expansion for the massless GN\(_2\) model to O(\(\nu^6\)) is \(M_B = nm \left(1 - \frac{\pi^2\nu^2}{24} + \frac{\pi^4\nu^4}{1920} - \frac{\pi^6\nu^6}{322560}\right)\), matching the expansion of the exact result \(M_B = nm \sin\theta/\theta\) with \(\theta = \pi\nu/2\).
- The NNLO effective action yields the baryon binding energy with an error of less than 1.5% even for full occupation (\(\nu=1\)) and arbitrary \(\gamma\); in the chiral limit the error is below 0.3% for all \(\nu\).
- For the NJL\(_2\) model, the pseudoscalar effective coupling after resummation is \(G_{\rm eff}^2(k) = -(4m^2/k^2)(\pi/N)\), exhibiting the pole of the massless pion.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical baryon solutions of Dashen, Hasslacher and Neveu (1975) and Shei (1976) for the Gross-Neveu and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models, respectively, by providing a systematic effective field theory framework that accounts for Dirac sea effects. The authors clarify why earlier "no-sea" calculations (which neglected the Dirac sea) worked well for the GN\(_2\) model but failed for the NJL\(_2\) model, as the latter requires resummation of pseudoscalar bubble sums that produce a massless pion pole. The technique is not restricted to 1+1 dimensions and may be applicable to higher-dimensional mean field theories such as the (\(\sigma,\omega\)) model, where Dirac sea effects have historically been neglected.
