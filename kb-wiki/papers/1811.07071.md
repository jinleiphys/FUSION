# Sound velocity and tidal deformability in compact stars

> The sound velocity $v_s$ and dimensionless tidal deformability $Λ$ are analyzed using the pseudo-conformal model we developed before. In contrast to the conclusion obtained in the previous works in the literature, our model with the upper bound of the sound velocity $v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}$, the so-called conformal sound velocity, set in at a { density relevant to compact stars} $\gsim 2 n_0$ where $n_0$ is the normal nuclear matter density, can accommodate {\it all} presently established nuclear matter and compact-star properties including the maximum star-mass constraint $ \simeq 2.3 M_\odot$. This observation is associated with a possible emergence of pseudoconformal structure in compact star matter---in which the trace of energy-momentum tensor is a nearly density-independent nonzero constant---brought in by a topology change at $2.0 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 \lesssim 4.0$ commensurate with a possible change of degrees of freedom from hadrons.

# Sound velocity and tidal deformability in compact stars

## Key claim

Contrary to the "no-go" argument in the literature that neutron stars with mass ~2.0 solar masses are inconsistent with the conformal sound velocity bound v_s ≤ 1/√3, the authors' pseudoconformal model (PCM) with v_s = 1/√3 setting in at densities ≥ 2 n_0 can accommodate all presently established nuclear matter and compact-star properties including a maximum star mass of ~2.3 solar masses, provided the topology change density n_{1/2} lies in the range 2.0 n_0 < n_{1/2} < 4.0 n_0.

## Method

The authors apply their previously developed pseudoconformal model (PCM) based on a scale-chiral effective theory (bsHLS) that incorporates hidden local symmetry for vector mesons and a dilaton for scale symmetry, with density-dependent parameters matched to QCD condensates. The model features a topology change from skyrmion matter to half-skyrmion matter at a density n_{1/2} (between 2 n_0 and 4 n_0), above which parity doubling in the nucleon structure emerges and the trace of the energy-momentum tensor becomes a nearly density-independent nonzero constant, forcing the sound velocity to v_s = 1/√3. The equation of state in Region II (n ≥ n_{1/2}) is given by the analytic form E/A = -m_N + X^α x^{1/3} + Y^α x^{-1} where x = n/n_0, with parameters matched to the V_{lowk} renormalization group results at n_{1/2}.

## Key numbers

- Maximum star mass constraint: ~2.3 M_⊙ (solar masses)
- Topology change density range: 2.0 ≤ n_{1/2}/n_0 ≤ 4.0, with upper bound n_{1/2} < 4.0 n_0 required to avoid v_s > 1 (causality violation)
- For a 1.4 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 2.0 n_0: central density = 2.02 n_0, tidal deformability Λ = 785, radius R = 13.0 km
- For a 1.4 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 3.0 n_0: central density = 2.30 n_0, Λ = 652, R = 12.8 km
- For a 1.4 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 4.0 n_0: central density = 2.30 n_0, Λ = 652, R = 12.8 km
- For a 2.0 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 2.0 n_0: central density = 4.50 n_0, Λ = 37, R = 11.5/12.2 km
- For a 2.0 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 3.0 n_0: central density = 3.60 n_0, Λ = 55, R = 12.6 km
- For a 2.0 M_⊙ star with n_{1/2} = 4.0 n_0: central density = 3.21 n_0, Λ = 55, R = 12.7 km
- Maximum star mass achieved: 2.23 M_⊙
- The chiral invariant nucleon mass m_0 is in the range (0.6-0.9) m_N where m_N is the free-space nucleon mass

## Context

This work directly contradicts the "no-go" argument of Bedaque and Steiner (2014) and subsequent supporting analyses by Tews et al., Moustakidis et al., and Alsing et al. (2017), which claimed that the conformal sound velocity bound v_s ≤ 1/√3 is incompatible with observed ~2.0 M_⊙ neutron stars. The authors' PCM provides a counter-example to the claim by Tews et al. that the conformal speed of sound could only be approached at asymptotic densities > 50 n_0. The model also challenges the observation by Tews et al. that a sign change in the trace of the energy-momentum tensor from negative to positive at high density is related to v_s^2 > 1/3. The PCM enables future tests through LIGO/Virgo observations: if Λ_{1.4} is tightened to a value much smaller than 650, the PCM would be in tension. The topology change density range 2.0 n_0 < n_{1/2} < 4.0 n_0 is consistent with the Quarkyonic matter picture proposed by Jeong, McLerran and Sen (2019), where both pictures agree on deconfined quasiparticles at (2-4) n_0 and sound velocity approaching v_s^2 → 1/3 at n > 2.0 n_0.
