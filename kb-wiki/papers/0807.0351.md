# Short Range Correlations in Medium- and High-Energy Scattering off Nuclei

> 8 pages, 7 figures, invited talk at the 6th International Conference on Hadronic Physics, ICTP, Trieste, 12-16 May, 2008. To appear in AIP Proceedings Series

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) significantly affect medium- and high-energy scattering off nuclei, but their unambiguous identification requires careful treatment of competing final-state interaction (FSI) effects, which can mask or mimic SRC signals.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) for few-body systems (³He, ⁴He) and a cluster expansion approach with correlated wave functions for complex nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁶Fe). For inclusive and exclusive electron scattering, they compared factorized and non-factorized treatments of FSI, and for high-energy hadron scattering they included both SRC and Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections to the Glauber multiple scattering expansion.

## Key numbers

- In the ³He(e,e'p) three-body breakup channel, differences between factorized and non-factorized approximations amount to at most 10-15%.
- For ⁴He(e,e'p) at Q²=1.78 (GeV/c)² and x~1.8, finite formation time (FFT) effects are too small to be detected, but become observable in the region 5 ≤ Q² ≤ 10 (GeV/c)² at x=1.4.
- The percentage probability of finding a pp pair in the correlation region (k_rel between 1.5 and 3.0 fm⁻¹) ranges from 2.9% in ⁴He to 24.0% in ⁴⁰Ca, while pn pair probabilities range from 97.1% in ⁴He to 76.0% in ⁴⁰Ca.
- In medium-weight nuclei, P_pp ≈ 10% and P_pn ≈ 90% in the correlation region, consistent with findings from ¹²C(e,e'pn) and ¹²C(e,e'pp) reactions.

## Context

This work extends the Perugia group's systematic program (Alvioli et al., 2005-2008) of incorporating realistic SRC into nuclear structure and reaction calculations, building on earlier demonstrations of mean-field inadequacy for high-momentum wave functions (Ciofi degli Atti and Simula, 1996). The results confirm and extend the Variational Monte Carlo findings of Schiavilla et al. (2007) on tensor-force-driven pn dominance to complex nuclei up to A=40, and enable quantitative predictions for JLab experiments including ¹²C(e,e'p) and ⁵⁶Fe(e,e') measurements.
