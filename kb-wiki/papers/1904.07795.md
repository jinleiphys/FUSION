# QED corrections to the big-bang nucleosynthesis reaction rates

> We compute radiative corrections to nuclear reaction rates that determine the outcome of the Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN). Any nuclear reaction producing a photon with an energy above $2m_e$ must be supplemented by the corresponding reaction where the final state photon is replaced by an electron-positron pair. We find that pair production brings a typical $0.2 \%$ enhancement to photon emission rates, resulting in a similar size corrections to elemental abundances. The exception is $^4{\rm He}$ abundance, which is insensitive to the small changes in the nuclear reaction rates. We also investigate the effect of vacuum polarisation on the Coulomb barrier, which brings a small extra correction when reaction rates are extrapolated from the measured energies to the BBN Gamow peak energies.

# QED corrections to the big-bang nucleosynthesis reaction rates

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to BBN nuclear reaction rates, specifically the replacement of final-state photons by electron-positron pairs and vacuum polarization effects on the Coulomb barrier, produce typical enhancements of about 0.2% to photon emission rates, but the magnitude of these corrections depends on the specific reaction and its Q-value, with the largest correction (0.416%) occurring for the ³H+p → ⁴He+γ reaction at T₉=0.8.

## Method

The authors computed the ratio of pair-production to photon-production cross sections for five BBN reactions (n+p→D+γ, p+D→³He+γ, ³H+⁴He→⁷Li+γ, ³He+⁴He→⁷Be+γ, and ³H+p→⁴He+γ) using an electric and magnetic dipole expansion of the nuclear transition current, valid when photon wavelengths are much larger than nuclear size. For vacuum polarization corrections to the p+D→³He+γ reaction, they evaluated the modification of the Gamow penetration factor using the Uehling-Serber potential, including the centrifugal barrier for ℓ=1 waves which account for approximately 85% of the reaction at BBN energies.

## Key numbers

- Pair production correction to thermally averaged rates at T₉=0.8: 0.220% for p+D→³He+γ (Q=5.493 MeV), 0.106% for ³H+⁴He→⁷Li+γ (Q=2.468 MeV), 0.057% for ³He+⁴He→⁷Be+γ (Q=1.587 MeV), and 0.416% for ³H+p→⁴He+γ (Q=19.81 MeV)
- The analytic approximation for the electric dipole ratio: r_ℰ ≈ (2α_FS/3π)[ln(2E_a/m_e) - 5/3] when E_a ≫ m_e
- The analytic approximation for the magnetic dipole ratio: r_ℬ ≈ (2α_FS/3π)[ln(2E_a/m_e) - 13/6 + 9m_e²/(2E_a²)] when E_a ≫ m_e
- The fitting formula for the n+p→D+γ reaction correction over 0.05 < T₉ < 5 uses coefficients a₀=3.6367×10⁻⁴, a₁=9.0830×10⁻⁵, a₂=4.1675×10⁻⁵, a₃=-1.1675×10⁻⁵, a₄=1.0263×10⁻⁶
- The energy of the unstable spin-zero state of deuterium is E_(s=0) ≈ 0.067 MeV

## Context

This work extends the treatment of radiative corrections to BBN reaction rates, which had previously been ignored in BBN reaction network compilations, while radiative corrections to weak interaction rates had been addressed by Dicus (1982), Lopez (1997), Lopez and Turner (1998), Brown and Sawyer, and Serpico (2004). The pair-production calculation follows the approach of Kroll (1955) and Landsberg (1985), while the vacuum polarization treatment follows Bahcall (1994) who applied these corrections to solar nuclear reactions. The results enable more precise BBN predictions for elemental abundances, particularly for deuterium where current measurements have reached 1.2% precision (Cooke 2018), and for ⁴He where errors of 1.6% have been claimed (Aver 2015).
