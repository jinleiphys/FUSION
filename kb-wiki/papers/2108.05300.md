# Shear viscosity at finite baryon densities

> We use the excluded volume Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with the most up-to-date hadron list to calculate $ηT/w$ at low temperatures and at finite baryon densities $ρ_B$. This $ηT/w$ is then matched to a QCD-based shear viscosity calculation of the QGP for different profiles of $ηT/w$ across {T,$μ_{B}$} including cross-over and critical point transitions. When compared to ideal hydrodynamic trajectories across {T,$μ_{B}$}, we find that the $ηT/w(T,μ_B)$ profiles would require initial conditions at much larger baryon density to reach the same freeze-out point.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a framework for a temperature- and baryon-density-dependent shear viscosity to enthalpy density ratio, $\eta T/w(T,\mu_B)$, that can be used in hydrodynamic simulations; the specific shape and location of the QGP-to-HRG transition line in the phase diagram strongly determines the $\mu_B$ dependence of $\eta T/w$, and using these profiles in viscous hydrodynamics shifts freeze-out trajectories toward lower $\mu_B$ compared to ideal hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors used the excluded-volume Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with the 2016 Particle Data Group hadron list to compute $\eta T/w$ in the hadronic phase at finite baryon density. They matched this to a parametrized QCD-based description of $\eta T/w$ in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase across four different transition profiles: two smooth crossovers and two with a critical point (at $(T,\mu_B)=(143,350)$ MeV and $(89,724)$ MeV). The resulting $\eta T/w(T,\mu_B)$ was then used as input in relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Au-Au collisions at 19.6 GeV, following the hydrodynamic background from Shen (2018), to study the effect on $T-\mu_B$ trajectories.

## Key numbers

- The critical point scenarios are placed at $(T,\mu_B) = (143, 350)$ MeV (matching the BEST collaboration equation of state) and $(T,\mu_B) = (89, 724)$ MeV (matching a holographic prediction).
- The HRG-phase $\eta T/w$ is normalized to $\eta/s = 0.08$ at $\mu_B = 0$.
- Hydrodynamic trajectories are computed starting at $T = 500$ MeV for various initial $s/\rho_B$ values.
- At forward rapidity in the 19.6 GeV Au-Au collision, the average $\mu_B$ values probed are approximately 200-300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior HRG-model calculations of shear viscosity (Noronha-Hostler, 2008; Gorenstein, 2007; Rischke, 1991) and transport-model approaches (Rose, 2017) by incorporating finite baryon density and matching to QGP-phase descriptions. It provides a framework that can be used as input for relativistic viscous hydrodynamic codes simulating heavy-ion collisions at low RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, where finite baryon chemical potential is important. The results enable future studies that require a full description of $\eta T/w$ across the $(T,\mu_B,\mu_S,\mu_Q)$ space to better model low-energy collisions.
