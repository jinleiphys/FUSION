# Simultaneous $γ$-ray and electron spectroscopy of $^{182,184,186}$Hg isotopes

> Background: The mercury isotopes around $N=104$ are a well-known example of nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence. Mixing of configurations can be studied by measuring the monopole strength $ρ^2(E0)$, however, currently the experimental information is scarce and lacks precision, especially for the $I^π\rightarrow I^π$ ($I \neq 0$) transitions. Purpose: The goals of this study were to increase the precision of the known branching ratios and internal conversion coefficients, to increase the amount of available information regarding excited states in $^{182,184,186}$Hg and to interpret the results in the framework of shape coexistence using different models. Method: The low-energy structures in $^{182,184,186}$Hg were populated in the $β$ decay of $^{182,184,186}$Tl, produced at ISOLDE and purified by laser ionization and mass separation. The $γ$-ray and internal conversion electron events were detected by five germanium clover detectors and a segmented silicon detector, respectively, and correlated in time to build decay schemes. Results: In total, 193, 178 and 156 transitions, including 144, 140 and 108 observed for the first time in a $β$-decay experiment, were assigned to $^{182,184,186}$Hg, respectively. Internal conversion coefficients were determined for 23 transitions, out of which 12 had an $E0$ component. Extracted branching ratios allowed the sign of the interference term in $^{182}$Hg as well as $ρ^2(E0;0^+_2\rightarrow 0^+_1)$ and $B(E2;0^+_2\rightarrow 2^+_1)$ in $^{184}$Hg to be determined. By means of electron-electron coincidences, the $0^+_3$ state was identified in $^{184}$Hg. The experimental results were qualitatively reproduced by five theoretical approaches, the IBM with configuration mixing with two different parametrizations, the General Bohr Hamiltonian, the BMF model and the SCCM model. However, a quantitative description is lacking.

## Key claim

The study confirms shape coexistence in neutron-deficient mercury isotopes and provides new spectroscopic data, but the abstract's claim of "qualitative reproduction" by five theoretical models is hedged by the explicit statement that "a quantitative description is lacking."

## Method

Low-energy structures in 182,184,186Hg were populated via beta decay of 182,184,186Tl produced at ISOLDE, purified by laser ionization and mass separation. Gamma rays and internal conversion electrons were detected simultaneously using five germanium clover detectors and a segmented silicon detector (SPEDE spectrometer), with events correlated in time to build decay schemes. Internal conversion coefficients were extracted from gamma-electron and electron-electron coincidence spectra, and branching ratios were used to determine monopole strengths and E2 matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- 193, 178, and 156 transitions assigned to 182Hg, 184Hg, and 186Hg respectively, with 144, 140, and 108 observed for the first time in a beta-decay experiment
- Internal conversion coefficients determined for 23 transitions, of which 12 had an E0 component
- In 182Hg: ratio B(E2;16)/B(E2;351) = 2.9(4) for transitions from the 2+1 state, yielding absolute matrix element |⟨0+2||E2||2+1⟩| = 2.2(3) eb
- In 182Hg: total ICC for the 2+2 → 2+1 transition at 196.5 keV measured as 7.6(7), in agreement with previous value of 7.2(13)
- In 182Hg: K-ICC of 0.030(8) for the 586-keV transition (6+2 → 6+1) suggests mixed E2/M1 character
- In 184Hg: total ICC for the 2+2 → 2+1 transition at 168.0 keV measured as 12.8(24), with K-ICC of 9.4(24)
- In 184Hg: K-ICC of 0.066(6) for the 616.6-keV transition (2+3 → 2+1) indicates E0+M1+E2 character
- In 184Hg: lower limit K-ICC > 1.256 for the 748.5-keV transition (0+3 → 0+2) confirms E0 character

## Context

This work extends the beta-decay studies of Rapisarda (2017) on mercury isotopes, improving precision on branching ratios and internal conversion coefficients that previously had uncertainties as large as 30%. The results provide essential spectroscopic input for interpreting Coulomb excitation measurements such as those by Wrzosek-Lipska (2019) and Bree (2014), and enable future Coulex studies to better constrain mixing matrix elements between coexisting shapes. The identification of the 0+3 state in 184Hg via electron-electron coincidences and the determination of the interference term sign in 182Hg provide new constraints for theoretical models including the Interacting Boson Model with configuration mixing, the General Bohr Hamiltonian, the Beyond Mean-Field model, and the symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing model.
