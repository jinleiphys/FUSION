# Optimization of relativistic mean field model for finite nuclei to neutron star matter

> We have optimized the parameters of extended relativistic mean-field model using a selected set of global observables which includes binding energies and charge radii for nuclei along several isotopic and isotonic chains and the iso-scalar giant monopole resonance energies for the $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb nuclei. The model parameters are further constrained by the available informations on the energy per neutron for the dilute neutron matter and bounds on the equations of state of the symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at supra-nuclear densities. Two new parameter sets BSP and IUFSU* are obtained, later one being the variant of recently proposed IUFSU parameter set. The BSP parametrization uses the contributions from the quartic order cross-coupling between $ω$ and $σ$ mesons to model the high density behaviour of the equation of state instead of the $ω$ meson self-coupling as in the case of IUFSU* or IUFSU. Our parameter sets yield appreciable improvements in the binding energy systematics and the equation of state for the dilute neutron matter. The importance of the quartic order $ω-σ$ cross coupling term of the extended RMF model, as often ignored, is realized.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the quartic-order omega-sigma cross-coupling term in the extended relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, which is often ignored, significantly improves the high-density behavior of the equation of state for beta-equilibrated neutron-rich matter, while the IUFSU* and IUFSU parameter sets (which use omega meson self-coupling instead) show larger deviations from observational constraints at very high energy densities.

## Method

The authors optimized the parameters of an extended RMF model by fitting to a large set of experimental data: binding energies for 62 nuclei and charge radii for 50 nuclei along isotopic and isotonic chains, plus isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energies for 90Zr and 208Pb. The fit was further constrained by the energy per neutron for dilute neutron matter from microscopic calculations, and by observational bounds on the equations of state (EOS) for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and beta-equilibrated neutron-rich matter at supra-nuclear densities. Two new parameter sets were generated: BSP (which uses quartic-order omega-sigma cross-coupling) and IUFSU* (a variant of IUFSU using omega meson self-coupling), with parameters searched via simulated annealing.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy rms errors for 513 even-even nuclei: BSP (2.7 MeV), IUFSU* (2.6 MeV), IUFSU (5.7 MeV)
- Charge radii rms error for fitted nuclei: approximately 0.02 fm for all three parameter sets
- Symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density: BSP (30.0 MeV), IUFSU* (30.9 MeV), IUFSU (31.8 MeV)
- Slope of symmetry energy L at saturation density: BSP (53.9 MeV), IUFSU* (53.9 MeV), IUFSU (49.2 MeV)
- Neutron-skin thickness for 208Pb: BSP (0.153 fm), IUFSU* (0.164 fm), IUFSU (0.161 fm)
- ISGMR constraint energies used in fit: 17.81 MeV for 90Zr, 14.18 MeV for 208Pb (with theoretical error 0.2 MeV)
- EOS data points used: 6 for SNM and 6 for PNM over density range 2.0-4.5 rho_0; 32 for beta-equilibrated matter over energy density 150-1600 MeV/fm^3; 8 for dilute neutron matter over neutron density 0.1-0.3 rho_0
- Theoretical error on pressure for beta-equilibrated matter: 25% of required value
- Theoretical error on energy per neutron for dilute matter: 0.05 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier extended RMF parameterizations such as G1, G2, TM1*, and FSU (Todd-Rutel 2005, Piekarewicz 2005), which had issues with symmetry energy density dependence or neutron star maximum mass. It directly addresses limitations of the IUFSU parameter set (Fattoyev 2010), which gave poor binding energy systematics and high energy per neutron for dilute neutron matter. The new BSP and IUFSU* parameter sets enable improved modeling of finite nuclei properties alongside neutron star observables, and demonstrate that the quartic-order omega-sigma cross-coupling term is important for realistic high-density EOS behavior, a term often omitted in previous parametrizations.
