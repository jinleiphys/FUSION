# Ratios of collective flow observables in high-energy isobar collisions are insensitive to final state interactions

> The ratios of bulk observables, such as harmonic flow $v_2$ and $v_3$, between high-energy $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions were recently argued to be a clean probe of the nuclear structure differences between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr. Using a transport model simulation of isobar collisions, we quantify this claim from the dependence of the ratios $v_{2,\mathrm{Ru}}/v_{2,\mathrm{Zr}}$ and $v_{3,\mathrm{Ru}}/v_{3,\mathrm{Zr}}$ on various final state effects, such as the shear viscosity, hadronization and hadronic cascade. Although the $v_2$ and $v_3$ change by more than 50% when varying the final state effects, the ratios are unchanged. In addition, these ratios are independent of the transverse momentum $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ and hadron species, despite of up to a factor of two change in $v_n$. The ratio of mean transverse momentum $\left\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\right\rangle$ is found to be controlled by the nuclear skin and nuclear radius, but is only slightly impacted by the final state effects. Therefore, these isobar ratios serve as a clean probe of the initial condition of the quark-gluon plasma, which in turn is controlled by the collective structure of the colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The ratios of harmonic flow \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) between \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr collisions are nearly unchanged even when the individual \(v_n\) values vary by more than 50% due to changes in shear viscosity, hadronization, and hadronic cascade; this insensitivity holds across transverse momentum and hadron species, supporting the claim that these isobar ratios are a clean probe of initial-state nuclear structure differences rather than final-state medium effects.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT transport model (v2.26t5, string-melting mode) to simulate \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV. They varied the partonic cross-section (1.5, 3.0, 6.0, and 10.0 mb) to change the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) (estimated from a pocket formula assuming \(T=0.38\) GeV) and also reduced the hadronic cascade time from 30 fm/\(c\) to 15 fm/\(c\). Flow harmonics \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) were calculated via the two-particle correlation method for charged hadrons with \(|\eta|<2\) and \(0.2<p_{\mathrm{T}}<2\) GeV/\(c\), and isobar ratios were computed at matching charged-particle multiplicity \(\nch\).

## Key numbers

- \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) change by up to 30% and 50%, respectively, when varying the partonic cross-section from 1.5 mb to 10 mb, but the ratios \(v_{2,\mathrm{Ru}}/v_{2,\mathrm{Zr}}\) and \(v_{3,\mathrm{Ru}}/v_{3,\mathrm{Zr}}\) remain almost unchanged.
- The average charged-particle multiplicity \(\langle \nch \rangle\) in 0–5% central collisions decreases by about 10% from \(\sigma=1.5\) mb to 10 mb (e.g., Ru+Ru: 281.60 for 1.5 mb, 258.43 for 10 mb; data: 289.32).
- The ratio of mean transverse momentum \(R_{\langle p_{\mathrm{T}} \rangle}\) shows a maximum enhancement of 0.15% around \(\nch=80\) (approximately 35% centrality) due to nuclear structure differences, but this magnitude is about a factor of three smaller than the STAR preliminary measurement.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used: \(^{96}\)Ru (\(R_0=5.09\) fm, \(a_0=0.46\) fm, \(\beta_2=0.162\), \(\beta_3=0\)); \(^{96}\)Zr (\(R_0=5.02\) fm, \(a_0=0.52\) fm, \(\beta_2=0.06\), \(\beta_3=0.20\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Jia (2021) and Nijs (2021) that used isobar collisions to extract nuclear structure parameters (quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\), octupole deformation \(\beta_3\), neutron skin) from STAR data. It directly tests the assumption that final-state interactions do not distort the isobar ratios, which was previously only partially addressed. The results enable future precision extractions of nuclear structure from isobar data by confirming that the ratios of bulk observables (especially \(v_2\) and \(v_3\)) are robust against variations in medium properties, hadronization, and hadronic transport, thus providing a clean link between the initial condition of the quark-gluon plasma and the collective structure of the colliding nuclei.
