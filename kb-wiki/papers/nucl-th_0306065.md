# Disappearance of Transverse Flow in Central Collisions for Heavier Nuclei

> For the first time, mass dependence of balance energy only for heavier systems has been studied. Our results are in excellent agreement with the data which allow us to predict the balance energy of U+U, for the first time, around 37-39 MeV/nucleon. Also our results indicate a hard equation of state along with nucleon-nucleon cross-section around 40 mb.

# Disappearance of Transverse Flow in Central Collisions for Heavier Nuclei

## Key claim

For the first time, the mass dependence of balance energy is studied exclusively for heavy systems (combined mass A ≥ 175), yielding a power-law scaling approximately proportional to 1/√A rather than the A⁻¹/³ dependence previously reported for lighter and medium nuclei. The authors predict the balance energy for ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U central collisions to be 37–39 MeV/nucleon, but note that this prediction depends on using a hard equation of state and a nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 40 mb.

## Method

The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model simulations of central heavy-ion collisions for systems with combined mass A ≥ 175: ⁸⁶Kr + ⁹³Nb, ⁹³Nb + ⁹³Nb, ¹²⁹Xe + ¹¹⁸Sn, ¹³⁹La + ¹³⁹La, ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au, and ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U. Incident energies were varied from 30 to 80 MeV/nucleon in steps of 10 MeV, and the energy of vanishing flow (balance energy) was extracted by straight-line interpolation between calculated values of the average directed transverse momentum ⟨p_x^dir⟩. The simulations used a hard equation of state and an isotropic, energy-independent nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 40 mb.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy for ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U central collisions predicted to be 37–39 MeV/nucleon (power-law fits give 37–38 MeV/nucleon; QMD simulation gives ~39 MeV/nucleon).
- Power-law exponent τ from fit to experimental data: τ = −0.52451 ± 0.06261.
- Power-law exponent τ from fit to theoretical values (excluding ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U): τ = −0.53261 ± 0.16373.
- Power-law exponent τ from fit to theoretical values (including ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U): τ = −0.50872 ± 0.10883.
- Power-law exponent τ for mean-field contribution to transverse momentum at balance energy: τ = −0.70931 ± 0.24234.
- ⁹³Nb + ⁹³Nb balance energy lies between 55–60 MeV/nucleon.
- ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U in-plane flow is already positive around 40 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of balance energy that focused on lighter and medium-mass systems (A ≤ 200), such as those by Zhou et al. and the experimental compilations cited in the paper, which found a mass dependence closer to A⁻¹/³. The authors show that for heavier nuclei (A ≥ 175), Coulomb repulsion becomes an important additional factor, steepening the power-law slope to approximately 1/√A. The results enable prediction of the balance energy for ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U, a system not previously studied, and provide a benchmark for future experimental measurements and for testing the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections in heavy systems.
