# Model-independent WIMP Scattering Responses and Event Rates: A Mathematica Package for Experimental Analysis

> The community's reliance on simplified descriptions of WIMP-nucleus interactions reflects the absence of analysis tools that integrate general theories of dark matter with standard treatments of nuclear response functions. To bridge this gap, we have constructed a public-domain Mathematica package for WIMP analyses based on our effective theory formulation. Script inputs are 1) the coefficients of the effective theory, through which one can characterize the low-energy consequences of arbitrary ultraviolet theories of WIMP interactions; and 2) one-body density matrices for commonly used targets, the most compact description of the relevant nuclear physics. The generality of the effective theory expansion guarantees that the script will remain relevant as new ultraviolet theories are explored; the use of density matrices to factor the nuclear physics from the particle physics will allow nuclear structure theorists to update the script as new calculations become available, independent of specific particle-physics contexts. The Mathematica package outputs the resulting response functions (and associated form factors) and also the differential event rate, once a galactic WIMP velocity profile is specified, and thus in its present form provides a complete framework for experimental analysis. The Mathematica script requires no a priori knowledge of the details of the non-relativistic effective field theory or nuclear physics, though the core concepts are reviewed here and in arXiv:1203.3542.

## Key claim

The paper presents a public-domain Mathematica package that implements a model-independent effective field theory (EFT) formulation of WIMP-nucleus elastic scattering, showing that the standard spin-independent/spin-dependent treatment omits four of the six allowed nuclear response functions and can mischaracterize the magnitude, multipolarity, and target-mass dependence of scattering for many candidate interactions, particularly those with velocity-dependent couplings.

## Method

The authors constructed a Galilean-invariant non-relativistic EFT of WIMP-nucleon interactions, generating up to 15 independent operators (with 28 isoscalar and isovector coupling parameters) built from the momentum transfer \(\vec{q}\), the relative velocity \(\vec{v}^\perp\), and the WIMP and nucleon spins \(\vec{S}_\chi\) and \(\vec{S}_N\). They embedded this interaction in the nucleus via a multipole decomposition of the plane wave \(e^{-i\vec{q}\cdot\vec{x}(i)}\) combined with the nucleon operators, factorizing the cross section into WIMP response functions \(R_k\) (depending on the EFT coefficients and kinematics) and nuclear response functions \(W_k^{\tau\tau'}\) (depending on one-body density matrices and the nuclear size parameter \(y = (qb/2)^2\)). The Mathematica package takes as inputs the EFT coefficients (in units of the weak scale \(m_v = 246.2\ \mathrm{GeV}\)) and pre-computed one-body density matrices for common target nuclei, and outputs response functions, form factors, and differential event rates given a galactic WIMP velocity profile.

## Key numbers

* The momentum transfer in direct detection is typically a few hundred MeV or less, while the WIMP mass scale is \(\gtrsim 100\ \mathrm{GeV}\); the ratio \(\vec{q}/m_N\) is associated with the typical velocity of bound nucleons, \(\sim 1/10\).
* The WIMP velocity in the laboratory frame is \(v/c \sim 10^{-3}\), while the internal nucleon velocity scale is \(\sim 10^{-1}\), leading to a parametric enhancement of composite operator contributions by a factor \(\sim 100\) relative to point-nucleus treatments.
* The weak interaction scale is defined as \(m_v = (2G_F)^{-1/2} = 246.2\ \mathrm{GeV}\), where \(G_F \sim 1.166 \times 10^{-5}\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\); all EFT coefficients \(c_i\) are input in units of \(1/m_v^2\).
* The total cross section for derivative couplings is enhanced by a factor \(\sim 10 (\mu_T/m_N)^2\) relative to point-nucleus treatments, where \(\mu_T\) is the WIMP-target reduced mass.
* The EFT has 28 parameters total (14 space/spin operators each with distinct proton and neutron couplings), or 20 parameters if operators not associated with spin-0 or spin-1 mediators are excluded.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic EFT formulation of WIMP-nucleon interactions developed by Fitzpatrick et al. (2013) and Fan et al. (2010), embedding it in nuclei to produce the most general elastic nuclear response. It contradicts the conventional spin-independent/spin-dependent analysis (which treats the nucleus as a point particle with form factors) by showing that six nuclear response functions arise from basic symmetry considerations, not two, and that the standard treatment misses operators such as orbital angular momentum \(\vec{l}(i)\) and spin-orbit \(\vec{\sigma}(i)\cdot\vec{l}(i)\) that are familiar from semi-leptonic electroweak interactions. The package enables experimentalists to compare sensitivities across different targets, particle theorists to constrain ultraviolet theories from low-energy data, and nuclear structure theorists to update nuclear physics calculations independently of particle-physics contexts.
