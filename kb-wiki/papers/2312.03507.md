# Gravitational wave double copy of radiation from gluon shockwave collisions

> In a recent paper, we showed that the gravitational wave spectrum from trans-Planckian shockwave scattering in Einstein gravity is determined by the gravitational Lipatov vertex expressed as the bilinear double copy $Γ^{μν} = \frac12 C^μC^ν- \frac12 N^μN^ν$ where $C^μ$ is the QCD Lipatov vertex and $N^μ$ is the QED soft photon factor. We show here that this result can be directly obtained by careful application of the classical color-kinematic duality to the spectrum obtained in gluon shockwave collisions.

# Gravitational wave double copy of radiation from gluon shockwave collisions

## Key claim
The gravitational wave spectrum from trans-Planckian shockwave scattering in Einstein gravity, previously shown to be determined by the gravitational Lipatov vertex expressed as a bilinear double copy of the QCD Lipatov vertex and the QED soft photon factor, can be directly recovered by applying the classical color-kinematic duality to the gluon radiation spectrum from gluon shockwave collisions, but only if the ultrarelativistic limit is taken *after* applying the double copy prescription, not before, because the eikonal approximation does not commute with the double copy procedure.

## Method
The authors start from the classical Yang-Mills radiation field solution for the collision of two colored scalar point particles, obtained by perturbatively solving the coupled Yang-Mills and Wong equations as derived by Goldberger and Ridgway (2016). They apply the classical Bern-Carrasco-Johansson (BCJ) color-kinematic replacement rules, mapping color charges to momenta, structure constants to the gravitational three-vertex, and the gauge coupling to the gravitational coupling, to this general-kinematics expression, and then take the ultrarelativistic (Regge) limit to obtain the gravitational wave amplitude. The resulting expression is shown to contain the gravitational Lipatov vertex after gauge-fixing and contraction with the graviton polarization tensor.

## Key numbers
- The gravitational Lipatov vertex is expressed as $\Gamma^{\mu\nu} = \frac12 C^\mu C^\nu - \frac12 N^\mu N^\nu$, where $C^\mu$ is the QCD Lipatov vertex and $N^\mu$ is the QED soft photon factor.
- The QCD Lipatov vertex is given by $C^\mu(\mathbf{q}_1, \mathbf{q}_2) \simeq -\mathbf{q}_1^\mu + \mathbf{q}_2^\mu + p_1^\mu\left(\frac{p_2 \cdot k}{p_1 \cdot p_2} - \frac{\mathbf{q}_1^2}{p_1 \cdot k}\right) - p_2^\mu\left(\frac{p_1 \cdot k}{p_1 \cdot p_2} - \frac{\mathbf{q}_2^2}{p_2 \cdot k}\right)$, where $k$ is the four-momentum of the produced gluon.
- The soft photon factor is $N^\mu(\mathbf{q}_1,\mathbf{q}_2) = \sqrt{\mathbf{q}_1^2 \mathbf{q}_2^2} \left(\frac{p_1^\mu}{p_1\cdot k} - \frac{p_2^\mu}{p_2\cdot k}\right)$.
- The squared center-of-mass energy $s = 2p_1\cdot p_2$ appears in the gravitational amplitude prefactor as $\kappa^3 s / k^2$.
- The lightcone gauge form of the gravitational Lipatov vertex is $(\mathbf{q}_2^i - k^i \mathbf{q}_2^2/\mathbf{k}^2)(\mathbf{q}_2^j - k^j \mathbf{q}_2^2/\mathbf{k}^2) - (k^i k^j/\mathbf{k}^4) \mathbf{q}_1^2 \mathbf{q}_2^2$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous result (Raj and Venugopalan, 2023) that the gravitational wave spectrum from trans-Planckian shockwave scattering in Einstein gravity is determined by the gravitational Lipatov vertex, which was originally computed by Lipatov (1982, 1988) in the context of $2\rightarrow N$ graviton scattering in Regge asymptotics. The present paper shows that this result follows directly from the classical double copy framework of Goldberger and Ridgway (2016), which relates Yang-Mills theory to dilaton gravity (with the dilaton decoupling in the ultrarelativistic limit). This enables a systematic mapping between QCD amplitudes in the BFKL framework and their gravitational counterparts, and opens the door to applying the classical double copy to derive gravitational shockwave propagators from the Color Glass Condensate effective theory of high-energy QCD, as part of a proposed CGC-Black Hole correspondence (Dvali, 2021).
