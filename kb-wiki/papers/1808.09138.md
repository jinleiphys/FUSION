# Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock in nuclear matter without the average momentum approximation

> Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory allows to derive the $G$-matrix as an effective interaction between nucleons in the nuclear medium. It depends on the center of mass momentum $\bm{P}$ of the two particles and on the two relative momenta $\bm{q}$ and $\bm{q'}$ before and after the scattering process. In the evaluation of the total energy per particle in nuclear matter usually the angle averaged center of mass momentum approximation has been used. We derive in detail the exact expressions of the angular integrations of the momentum $\bm{P}$ within relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory, especially for the case of asymmetric nuclear matter. In order to assess the reliability of the conventional average momentum approximation for the binding energy, the saturation properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter are systematically investigated based on the realistic Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. It is found that the exact treatment of the center of mass momentum leads to non-negligible contributions to the higher order physical quantities. The correlation between the symmetry energy $E_{\mathrm{sym}}$, the slope parameter $L$, and the curvature $K_{\mathrm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy are investigated. The results of our RBHF calculations for the bulk parameters characterizing the equation of state are compared with recent constraints extracted from giant monopole resonance and isospin diffusion experiments.

## Key claim
The exact treatment of the center-of-mass momentum in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory produces small but non-negligible differences in the binding energy per nucleon compared to the conventional average momentum approximation, and these differences become significant for higher-order bulk parameters such as the skewness parameter \(Q_0\), the slope of the incompressibility \(M_0\), and the curvature of the symmetry energy \(K_{\mathrm{sym}}\).

## Method
The authors derived exact analytic expressions for the angular integrations of the center-of-mass momentum \(\bm{P}\) in the Thompson equation for the \(G\)-matrix within RBHF theory, avoiding the angle-averaged approximation used in prior work. They solved the in-medium Thompson equation self-consistently using the realistic Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials (A, B, and C) for both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, employing the continuous choice for the starting energy and the angle-averaged Pauli operator. The total energy per particle and symmetry energy were then computed, and the resulting equation of state was parameterized to extract bulk parameters such as incompressibility, skewness, symmetry energy slope, and curvature.

## Key numbers
* Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.180\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for Bonn A with exact treatment, compared to \(0.182\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) with the average approximation.
* Binding energy per nucleon \(E/A = -15.38\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), versus \(-15.04\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = -0.34\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\mathrm{sym}} = 33.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), compared to \(32.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = 1.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Incompressibility \(K_{\infty} = 286\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), versus \(289\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = -3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Skewness parameter \(Q_0 = 731\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), versus \(650\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = 81\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Slope parameter of symmetry energy \(L = 75.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), versus \(74.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = 1.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Curvature of symmetry energy \(K_{\mathrm{sym}} = -57.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for Bonn A (exact), versus \(-53.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (average); the difference \(\Delta = -3.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework by removing the average center-of-mass momentum approximation that had been standard in earlier RBHF calculations (e.g., Alonso 2003, Sammarruca 2012, Sammarruca 2014) and in non-relativistic BHF work (Brueckner 1968). The exact treatment enables more reliable predictions for higher-order equation-of-state parameters, which are essential for interpreting experiments such as giant monopole resonance and isospin diffusion. The results also allow a quantitative assessment of the approximation \(K_{\tau} \approx K_{\mathrm{sym}} - 6L\) commonly used in the literature (e.g., Chen 2005, Li 2008, Centelles 2009), showing that the \(Q_0\) contribution to \(K_{\tau}\) cannot be neglected.
