# Beyond-mean-field study of the hyperon impurity effect in hypernuclei with shape coexistence

> [Background] The hyperon impurity effect in nuclei has been extensively studied in different mean-field models. Recently, there is a controversy about whether the $Λ$ hyperon is more tightly bound in the normal deformed (ND) states than that in the superdeformed (SD) states.[Purpose] This article is aimed to provide a beyond-mean-field study of the low-lying states of hypernuclei with shape coexistence and to shed some light on the controversy.[Method] The models of relativistic mean-field and beyond based on a relativistic point-coupling energy functional are adopted to study the low-lying states of both $^{37}_Λ$Ar and $^{36}$Ar. The wavefunctions of low-lying states are constructed as a superposition of a set of relativistic mean-field states with different values of quadrupole deformation parameter. The projections onto both particle number and angular momentum are considered.[Results] The $Λ$ binding energies in both ND and SD states of $^{37}_Λ$Ar are studied in the case of the $Λ$ hyperon occupying $s, p$, or $d$ state in the spherical limit, respectively. For comparison, four sets of nucleon-hyperon point-coupling interactions are used respectively. Moreover, the spectra of low-lying states in $^{36}$Ar and $^{37}_{Λ_s}$Ar are calculated based on the same nuclear energy density functional. The results indicate that the SD states exist in $^{37}_Λ$Ar for all the four effective interactions. Furthermore, the $Λ_s$ reduces the quadrupole collectivity of ND states to a greater extent than that of SD states. For $^{37}_Λ$Ar, the beyond-mean-field decreases the $Λ_s$ binding energy of the SD state by 0.17 MeV, but it almost has no effect on that of the ND state. ## Key claim

The paper provides a beyond-mean-field study of the low-lying states of hypernuclei with shape coexistence, finding that the conclusion about whether a Lambda hyperon is more tightly bound in superdeformed (SD) states than in normal deformed (ND) states depends on the hyperon orbital and the effective nucleon-hyperon interaction; for the Lambda_s hyperon in Ar-37, the beyond-mean-field calculation indicates it is less bound in the SD state than in the ND state, contrary to the mean-field result.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field model with point-coupling (RMF-PC) nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon effective interactions, combined with beyond-mean-field calculations using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with particle-number and angular-momentum projection (PNAMP+GCM). Wavefunctions for low-lying states were constructed as superpositions of relativistic mean-field states with different quadrupole deformation parameters, and the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation was solved to obtain energies. Four different sets of nucleon-hyperon point-coupling interactions (PCY-S1, PCY-S2, PCY-S3, PCY-S4) were used for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field excitation energy of the SD state in Ar-36 is 9.8 MeV relative to the ND state.
- For Ar-37 with the Lambda_s hyperon using PC-F1 and PCY-S1 interactions, the beyond-mean-field calculation decreases the Lambda_s binding energy of the SD state by 0.17 MeV, while it almost has no effect on that of the ND state.
- The Lambda_s separation energy in the ND state of Ar-37 is 18.614 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1) and in the SD state is 19.212 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1) at the mean-field level.
- The Lambda_p separation energy in the ND state of Ar-37 is 10.157 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1) and in the SD state is 12.129 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1).
- The Lambda_d separation energy in the ND state of Ar-37 is 1.440 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1) and in the SD state is 5.424 MeV (PC-F1, PCY-S1).
- The overlap I_ovlp between Lambda hyperon and core nucleons for Lambda_s in the ND state is 0.1323 fm^-3 and in the SD state is 0.1338 fm^-3 (PC-F1, PCY-S1).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on hyperon impurity effects in nuclei with shape coexistence, addressing a controversy between different theoretical approaches. The relativistic mean-field with meson-exchange (RMF-ME) model by Lu (2014) predicted larger Lambda binding in SD states, while the HyperAMD model by Isaka (2015) and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach by Zhou (2016) predicted smaller Lambda separation energy in SD states for Ar-37. The present study uses a beyond-mean-field framework to provide more reliable predictions and shows that the conclusion depends on the hyperon orbital and interaction, with the beyond-mean-field correction reversing the mean-field result for the Lambda_s case. This enables future studies to incorporate beyond-mean-field effects when analyzing hyperon binding in shape-coexisting nuclei.
