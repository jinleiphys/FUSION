# Poles of light resonances from unitarized Chiral perturbation Theory and their large Nc behavior

> We have recently completed the one loop calculation of meson-meson scattering within Chiral Perturbation Theory. Once unitarized, these amplitudes provide simultaneously a remarkable description of the resonance region up to 1.2 GeV as well as the low energy region, respecting the chiral symmetry expansion. The position of the poles in these amplitudes is related to the mass and width of the associated resonances that are generated without being explicitly included in the Lagrangian. The spectroscopic nature of these states can then be deduced by studying the behavior of these poles, through the Nc dependence of the Chiral Perturbation Theory parameters, which can be obtained from QCD in the large Nc limit.

## Key claim
Unitarized one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes can simultaneously describe low-energy meson-meson scattering and the resonance region up to 1.2 GeV, generating resonances without including them explicitly in the Lagrangian; the spectroscopic nature of these states can then be deduced from the large Nc behavior of the poles, but the results for the a0(980) are strongly dependent on how the amplitudes are parametrized (using only fπ versus using fπ, fK, and fη).

## Method
The author unitarized the complete one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory meson-meson scattering amplitudes using the coupled-channel Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), which enforces exact perturbative unitarity. The amplitudes were renormalized in the standard MS-1 scheme, allowing direct comparison with standard chiral parameters Li. Pole positions in the second Riemann sheet were extracted for each resonance, and their large Nc behavior was studied by imposing the QCD-derived scaling of the chiral parameters at μ = 770 MeV.

## Key numbers
- ρ(770) pole: 754 ± 18 − i(74 ± 10) MeV (IAM II fit)
- K*(892) pole: 889 ± 13 − i(24 ± 4) MeV (IAM II fit)
- σ (f0(600)) pole: 440 ± 8 − i(212 ± 15) MeV (IAM II fit)
- κ pole: 753 ± 52 − i(235 ± 33) MeV (IAM II fit)
- f0(980) pole: 973 − i(11) MeV, with asymmetric errors +39/−127 in real part and +189/−11 in imaginary part (IAM II fit)
- a0(980) pole: 1117 − i(12) MeV, with asymmetric errors +24/−320 in real part and +43/−12 in imaginary part (IAM II fit); no pole appears when using only fπ
- Scattering length a00 = 0.231 +0.003/−0.006 (IAM fit I), compared with experimental value 0.26 ± 0.05
- Scattering length a11 = 0.0377 ± 0.0007 (IAM fit I), compared with experimental value 0.038 ± 0.002

## Context
This work extends the earlier approximated coupled-channel IAM calculation by Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1997), which used only leading-order ChPT with a cutoff regulator, by incorporating the full one-loop ChPT amplitudes in dimensional regularization and enabling direct comparison with standard chiral parameters. The large Nc analysis follows the framework of Jaffe (1977) for multiquark states and the large Nc scaling of chiral parameters from ChPT. The results enable future studies of the f0(980) and a0(980) poles near the KKbar threshold and their possible mixing with heavier multiplets, as well as direct analysis of the large Nc behavior of phase shifts.
