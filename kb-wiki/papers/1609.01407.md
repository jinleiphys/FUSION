# 3$α$-cluster structure and monopole transition in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$C

> 3$α$-cluster structures and monopole transitions of $0^+$ states in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$C were investigated with $3α$- and $^{10}$Be+$α$-cluster models. A gas-like $3α$ state and a bending-chain $3α$ state were obtained in the $0^+_2$ and $0^+_3$ states of $^{12}$C, respectively. In $^{14}$C, a linear-chain 3$α$ structure is found in the $0^+_4$ state near the $^{10}$Be+$α$ threshold, but a cluster gas-like state does not appear because valence neutrons attract $α$ clusters and suppress spatial development of 3$α$ clustering. It was found that the linear-chain state in $^{14}$C is stabilized against the bending and $α$ escaping modes by valence neutrons. The monopole transition strengths in $^{12}$C are enhanced by $3α$-cluster developing, whereas, those in $^{14}$C are not enhanced so much because of the tight binding of $α$ clusters by valence neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in $^{14}$C, a linear-chain $3\alpha$ structure appears in the $0^+_4$ state near the $^{10}$Be+$\alpha$ threshold, but a cluster gas-like state does not appear because valence neutrons attract $\alpha$ clusters and suppress spatial development of $3\alpha$ clustering; the monopole transition strengths in $^{14}$C are not enhanced as much as in $^{12}$C because of the tight binding of $\alpha$ clusters by valence neutrons.

## Method

The authors performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations using $3\alpha$-cluster wave functions for $^{12}$C and $^{10}$Be+$\alpha$-cluster wave functions for $^{14}$C, based on the Brink-Bloch $\alpha$-cluster model. For $^{14}$C, two valence neutrons were described with Gaussian wave packets, and the generator coordinates included the $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance ($d_{2\alpha}$), the distance ($D_\alpha$) and angle ($\theta_\alpha$) of the third $\alpha$ cluster relative to the core, and the angle ($\phi$) of the neutron pair around the $2\alpha$ core. The authors analyzed the effects of $\alpha_3$-cluster rotation, valence neutron configurations (vertical vs. planar), and $\alpha$-$\alpha$ core modes by performing truncated GCM calculations with restricted model spaces.

## Key numbers

* $^{12}$C: The fixed-$\theta$ GCM $0^+_2$ has 73% overlap with the Full-GCM $0^+_2$ and 23% overlap with the Full-GCM $0^+_3$, indicating splitting of the gas-like state component by $\alpha_3$ rotation.
* $^{14}$C: The fixed-$\theta$ GCM $0^+_2$ has 90% overlap with the Full-GCM $0^+_2$ and gains about 1 MeV energy by $\alpha_3$ rotation.
* $^{14}$C: The fixed-$\theta$ GCM $0^+_3$ component is fragmented by rotation into the Full-GCM $0^+_3$ and $0^+_4$ states with fractions of 60% and 30%, respectively.
* $^{14}$C: The linear-chain state at $^{14}$C($0^+_4$) of the Full-GCM has 84% overlap with the V-GCM $0^+_3$ state having the bending-chain $3\alpha$ structure.
* $^{14}$C: The approximated ground state wave function has 93% overlap with the ground state of the Full-GCM.
* $^{14}$C: The fixed-$\theta$ V-GCM $0^+_2$ component is fragmented by rotation into the V-GCM $0^+_2$ and $0^+_3$ states with fractions of 64% and 25%.
* $^{12}$C: The $0^+_1$ state of the fixed-$\theta$ GCM has 100% overlap with that of the Full-GCM, indicating no $\alpha_3$ rotation contribution to the ground state.
* $^{14}$C: The $0^+_1$ state of the fixed-$\theta$ V-GCM has 100% overlap with that of the V-GCM.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $3\alpha$ cluster structures in $^{12}$C, including the gas-like $3\alpha$ state (Uegaki, 1970s; THSR model; Funaki, 2000s) and the bending-chain $3\alpha$ state predicted by AMD and FMD calculations (Neff, 2004; Kanada-En'yo, 2007; Suhara, 2010). For $^{14}$C, it builds on the $\beta$-$\gamma$ constraint AMD calculation by Suhara (2014) that predicted a linear-chain $3\alpha$ structure, and on the simplified cluster model by Itagaki (2001). The analysis enables future experimental identification of $0^+$ states in $^{14}$C via monopole transition measurements and clarifies the role of valence neutrons in stabilizing linear-chain configurations versus suppressing gas-like clustering.
