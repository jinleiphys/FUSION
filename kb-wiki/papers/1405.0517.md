# Constraints on light neutrino parameters derived from the study of neutrinoless double beta decay

> The study of the neutrinoless double beta ($0 ββ$) decay mode can provide us with important information on the neutrino properties, particularly on the electron neutrino absolute mass. In this work we revise the present constraints on the neutrino mass parameters derived from the $0 ββ$ decay analysis of the experimentally interesting nuclei. We use the latest results for the phase space factors (PSFs) and nuclear matrix elements (NMEs), as well as for the experimental lifetimes limits. For the PSFs we use values computed with an improved method reported very recently. For the NMEs we use values chosen from literature on a case-by-case basis, taking advantage of the consensus reached by the community on several nuclear ingredients used in their calculation. Thus, we try to restrict the range of spread of the NME values calculated with different methods and, hence, to reduce the uncertainty in deriving limits for the Majorana neutrino mass parameter. Our results may be useful to have an up-date image on the present neutrino mass sensitivities associated with $0 ββ$ measurements for different isotopes and to better estimate the range of values of the neutrino masses that can be explored in the future double beta decay (DBD) experiments.

# Constraints on light neutrino parameters derived from the study of neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim

The authors derive updated constraints on the light Majorana neutrino mass parameter from neutrinoless double beta decay analysis, claiming that by applying a community consensus on nuclear ingredients (higher-order currents, finite nucleon size, short-range correlations, and unquenched axial coupling) they can restrict the spread of nuclear matrix element values to about a factor of two, thereby reducing the uncertainty in the derived neutrino mass limits. The strongest constraints come from 136Xe and 76Ge, which are already probing the quasi-degenerate neutrino mass hierarchy around 0.5 eV.

## Method

The authors compute the Majorana neutrino mass parameter using the standard lifetime formula for 0νββ decay mediated by light Majorana neutrino exchange, combining experimental half-life limits, phase space factors (PSFs) recalculated with improved numerical precision using exact Dirac electron wave functions including finite nuclear size and screening effects with a realistic Coulomb potential, and nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) selected from literature on a case-by-case basis. For NMEs, they apply a consensus set of nuclear ingredients: inclusion of higher-order currents, finite nucleon size effects, softer short-range correlation parametrizations (UCOM or CD-Bonn), and an unquenched axial coupling constant gA = 1.25, then take the range of values from different nuclear structure methods (Shell Model, QRPA, IBM-2, GCM, PHFB) that satisfy these criteria.

## Key numbers

- 136Xe: experimental half-life limit > 1.6 × 10^25 yr (EXO), PSF = 1.45 × 10^-14 yr^-1, NME range 2.19–4.20, yielding ⟨mν⟩ < [0.25–0.48] eV
- 76Ge: experimental half-life limit > 2.1 × 10^25 yr (GERDA), PSF = 2.37 × 10^-15 yr^-1, NME range 2.81–6.16, yielding ⟨mν⟩ < [0.37–0.82] eV
- 48Ca: experimental half-life limit > 5.8 × 10^22 yr, PSF = 2.46 × 10^-14 yr^-1, NME range 0.81–0.90, yielding ⟨mν⟩ < [15.0–16.7] eV
- 100Mo: experimental half-life limit > 1.1 × 10^24 yr, PSF = 1.57 × 10^-14 yr^-1, NME range 3.93–6.07, yielding ⟨mν⟩ < [0.64–0.99] eV
- 130Te: experimental half-life limit > 2.8 × 10^24 yr, PSF = 1.41 × 10^-14 yr^-1, NME range 2.65–5.13, yielding ⟨mν⟩ < [0.50–0.97] eV
- New PSF values differ from older calculations (Doi 1983, Doi 1993, Suhonen 1998) by up to 28%
- The quasi-degenerate neutrino mass hierarchy is around 0.5 eV

## Context

This work updates previous constraints on neutrino mass parameters from 0νββ decay by incorporating the most recent experimental limits from GERDA (76Ge) and EXO (136Xe), together with improved PSF calculations that use exact Dirac wave functions rather than approximate electron wave functions. The authors build on the community consensus on nuclear ingredients discussed in reviews by Avignone et al. (2008) and Vergados et al. (2012), and they note that when QRPA calculations adjust single-particle energies to reproduce experimental occupancy numbers from Schiffer et al. (2009), the QRPA NMEs become much closer to Shell Model results. The results enable better estimation of the neutrino mass ranges that future experiments (e.g., next-generation ton-scale detectors) can explore, particularly for distinguishing the quasi-degenerate from the inverted hierarchy scenarios.
