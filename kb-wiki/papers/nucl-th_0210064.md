# Deformed two center shell model

> A highly specialized two-center shell model has been developed accounting for the splitting of a deformed parent nucleus into two ellipsoidaly deformed fragments. The potential is based on deformed oscillator wells in direct correspondance with the shape change of the nuclear system. For the first time a potential responsible for the necking part between the fragments is introduced on potential theory basis. As a direct consequence, spin-orbit {\bf ls} and {\bf l$^2$} operators are calculated as shape dependent. Level scheme evolution along the fission path for pairs of ellipsoidaly deformed fragments is calculated. The Strutinsky method yields the shell corrections for different mass asymmetries from the superheavy nucleus $^{306}$122 and $^{252}$Cf all along the splitting process.

## Key claim

A deformed two-center shell model (DTCSM) is developed that, for the first time, introduces a potential for the necking region between two ellipsoidally deformed fission fragments, based on a rejective oscillator force; the spin-orbit and squared angular momentum operators are consequently shape-dependent, and the model is applied to calculate level schemes and shell corrections for the superheavy nucleus 306122 and 252Cf along the fission path.

## Method

The potential consists of two deformed Nilsson-type oscillator wells for axially symmetric ellipsoidal fragments, plus a newly constructed neck potential derived from a rejective oscillator force centered on a sphere tangent to both ellipsoids. The Hilbert space is divided by matching potential ellipsoids (MPEs) that ensure continuous transition between the fragment potentials and the neck potential. Single-particle levels are obtained by diagonalizing the total Hamiltonian (including shape-dependent spin-orbit and l² terms) in a basis of oscillator functions, and shell corrections are computed using the Strutinsky method.

## Key numbers

- The surface potential value V₀ is approximately 54.5 MeV, derived from ℏωᵢ = 41A⁻¹/³ and Rᵢ = r₀A¹/³ with r₀ ≈ 1.16 fm.
- The neck potential V_g1 reaches a maximum of 2V₀ at the center of the neck sphere and decreases to V₀ at the neck surface at distance R₃.
- Calculations are presented for the superheavy nucleus ³⁰⁶122 and for ²⁵²Cf, for different mass asymmetries and neck parameter values.
- The model uses five independent parameters: two fragment shape asymmetries χ₁ = b₁/a₁ and χ₂ = b₂/a₂, mass asymmetry A₁/A₂, neck radius R₃, and distance between centers R.

## Context

This work extends the asymmetric two-center shell model developed by Maruhn, Greiner and the Frankfurt school (1970s), which considered only spherical fragments, to include deformed (ellipsoidal) fragments and a microscopic neck potential. It also builds on earlier two-center oscillator treatments by Merzbacher (1961) for symmetric spherical wells. The model enables microscopic studies of fission, fusion, and cluster decay for deformed nuclei, and is particularly relevant for analyzing shell effects in superheavy element synthesis and decay, as previously emphasized by Poenaru and collaborators.
