# Islands of shape coexistence for Z=38-84 in a non-relativistic mean-field approach using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> Based on the microscopic mechanism of the particle-hole (p-h) excitations in the proton and neutron single-particle energy levels relative to the Fermi energy, a search for islands of shape coexistence (SC) is performed over a wide range of even-even nuclei from Z=38 to 84 using non-relativistic self-consistent mean-field with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory using the Skyrme-SKI3 functional. The results of the present study show that neutron-induced islands of SC, corresponding to proton p-h excitations, are found around the magic numbers Z=82 and Z=50, centered at the relevant neutron midshells of N=104 and N=66 respectively, while proton-induced islands of SC, corresponding to neutron p-h excitations, are found around the neutron numbers N=90 and N=60, centered at the relevant proton midshells Z=66 and Z=38 respectively. In addition, islands of SC due to both neutron and proton particle-hole excitations are found around N=40, Z=40. The results of the present study are compared with the results of covariant density functional theory using the DDME2 functional, using the same p-h mechanism. The islands of SC that appeared in the CDFT work with the DDME2 functional are corroborated by the present study with the Skyrme-SKI3 functional, thus confirming the robustness of the particle-hole excitations mechanism in searching for islands of SC. In addition, the current study revealed new regions of SC, adjacent to the earlier islands and expanding their shores.

# Islands of shape coexistence for Z=38-84 in a non-relativistic mean-field approach using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

## Key claim

The particle-hole excitation mechanism for shape coexistence, previously tested in covariant density functional theory with the DDME2 functional, is confirmed to be robust using a non-relativistic Skyrme-SKI3 functional, with the present study revealing new regions of shape coexistence adjacent to earlier islands and expanding their shores. The results are conditional on the specific choice of the Skyrme-SKI3 functional and the pairing strength of -300.00 MeV fm^3, which were selected to maximize similarity to the earlier covariant density functional theory results.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic self-consistent mean-field calculations using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the Skyrme-SKI3 functional, implemented in the HFBTHO code. They tracked single-particle energy levels of protons and neutrons relative to the Fermi energy across series of isotopes and isotones, identifying regions where orbitals change from particle-like to hole-like status, which indicates shape coexistence. The calculations used an axial transformed harmonic oscillator basis with N_max=20, a mixed volume-surface pairing force with strength V_0^(n,p)=-300.0 MeV fm^3, and a cut-off quasi-particle energy of 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Shape coexistence from neutron-induced proton particle-hole excitations found around Z=82 centered at N=104, and around Z=50 centered at N=66
- Shape coexistence from proton-induced neutron particle-hole excitations found around N=90 centered at Z=66, and around N=60 centered at Z=38
- Shape coexistence from both neutron and proton particle-hole excitations found around N=40, Z=40
- For Z=78-82 region: proton particle-hole excitations seen in Po isotopes for N=98-138, Pb isotopes for N=96-120, Hg isotopes for N=94-114, and Pt isotopes for N=88-120
- For Z=50-52 region: 2p-2h proton excitations seen in Te isotopes for N=64-70
- For Z=38-40 region: 6p-6h proton excitations seen in Zr (Z=40) isotopes for N=36-42; 4p-4h proton excitations seen in Sr (Z=38) isotopes at N=46
- For N=90-96 region: neutron particle-hole excitations found in N=90 isotones at Z=58-68, N=92 isotones at Z=58-68, and N=94 isotones at Z=58-66
- For N=60 region: 4p-4h neutron excitations found at Z=36-44
- For N=58 region: 4p-4h neutron excitations found at Z=36-42
- For N=40 region: 6p-6h neutron excitations found at Z=36-42
- For N=38 region: 4p-4h neutron excitations found at Z=32-42

## Context

This work extends the dual-shell mechanism predictions for shape coexistence (Martinou 2021, Bonatsos 2023) and tests them against non-relativistic mean-field calculations, complementing earlier covariant density functional theory studies using the DDME2 functional (Bonatsos 2022a, Bonatsos 2022b). The results corroborate the islands of shape coexistence found in the relativistic approach while revealing additional regions, particularly for neutron-rich Po isotopes with N=114-138, Pb and Pt isotopes with N=114-120, and Hg isotopes with N=114, as well as neutron-deficient Pt isotopes with N=88-94 and Hg isotopes with N=94. The findings enable further experimental searches at rare isotope facilities and provide a benchmark for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field descriptions of shape coexistence across the nuclear chart.
