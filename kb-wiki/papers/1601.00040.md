# Nonlinear hydrodynamic response confronts LHC data

> Higher order harmonic flow $v_n$ (with $n\ge4$) in heavy-ion collisions can be measured either with respect to their own plane, or with respect to a plane constructed using lower-order harmonics. By assuming that higher flow harmonics are the superposition of medium nonlinear and linear responses to initial anisotropies, we propose a set of nonlinear response coefficients $χ_n$'s, which are independent of initial state by construction. In experiments, $χ_n$'s can be extracted as the ratio between higher order harmonic flow measured in the plane constructed by $v_2$ and $v_3$, and moments of lower order harmonic flow. Simulations with single-shot hydrodynamics and AMPT model lead to results of these nonlinear response coefficients in good agreement with the experimental data at the LHC energy. Predictions for $v_7$ and $v_8$ measured with respect to plane of lower order harmonics are given accordingly.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that higher-order harmonic flow coefficients ($v_n$ with $n\ge4$) can be decomposed into linear and nonlinear hydrodynamic responses to initial-state anisotropies, and that the resulting nonlinear response coefficients $\chi_n$ are independent of the initial state by construction, making them sensitive primarily to medium properties rather than initial conditions.

## Method

The authors decompose higher-order harmonic flow $V_n$ into a linear component proportional to the corresponding initial eccentricity and nonlinear components proportional to products of lower-order harmonics (e.g., $V_4 = V_4^L + \chi_4 V_2^2$). They extract the nonlinear response coefficients $\chi_n$ from experimental data using ratios of higher-order harmonic flow measured in the event-plane of lower-order harmonics to moments of lower-order flow distributions. The coefficients are then compared with results from single-shot hydrodynamic simulations (with both ideal and viscous hydrodynamics at $\eta/s=1/4\pi$) and from AMPT model simulations with parton elastic cross-section $\sigma=1.5$ mb.

## Key numbers

* $\chi_4$, $\chi_5$, $\chi_{62}$, $\chi_{63}$, and $\chi_7$ extracted from PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC, shown as functions of centrality percentile
* Ideal hydrodynamics predicts quantitative relations: $\chi_4\sim\chi_{63}\sim\frac{1}{2}\chi_5$ and $\chi_{62}\sim\frac{1}{3}\chi_7$, consistent with experimental data
* Predictions for $v_7$ and $v_8$ measured in the event-plane constructed by $v_2$ and $v_3$ are provided from hydro simulations with $\eta/s=1/4\pi$ (viscous) and $\eta/s=0$ (ideal) as bounding bands

## Context

This work extends the study of nonlinear hydrodynamic response in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier formulations by Gardim (2011) and Teaney (2012) who showed that nonlinear hydrodynamic response to initial eccentricities produces significant contributions to higher-order flow harmonics. The paper provides a systematic framework for extracting initial-state-independent nonlinear response coefficients from experimental data, enabling direct comparison between hydrodynamic model predictions and LHC measurements from the CMS and ATLAS collaborations. The results enable future measurements of $v_7$ and $v_8$ with respect to lower-order event-planes, which can further constrain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio $\eta/s$ of the quark-gluon plasma.
