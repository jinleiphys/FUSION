# Efficient production of $^{229m}$Th via nuclear excitation by electron capture

> The nuclear isomeric state $^{229m}$Th with an exceptionally low excitation energy makes the $^{229}$Th isotope a crucial candidate for nuclear clocks and many other applications. Efficient and controllable production of $^{229m}$Th is essential and still remains a challenge. Here we report a novel approach for efficient production of $^{229m}$Th by the excitation of $^{229}$Th to the above-lying excited state at $29.19$ keV energy via the process of nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC). We show theoretically that the production rate of $^{229m}$Th per nucleus with accessible conditions can be six orders of magnitude larger than the value experimentally demonstrated using $29$-keV synchrotron radiation for this indirect excitation. With the efficient production of $^{229m}$Th, our results identify scenarios, as well as the characteristic NEEC signature with which NEEC events could be unambiguously identified, for a clear experimental identification of the long-sought NEEC phenomenon.

## Key claim

The authors theoretically demonstrate that the production rate of $^{229m}$Th per nucleus via nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) can be six orders of magnitude larger than the value experimentally demonstrated using 29-keV synchrotron radiation for indirect excitation, but only under accessible conditions in electron beam ion traps (EBITs) and storage rings, and only for specific charge states and NEEC channels.

## Method

The authors calculated NEEC cross sections and resonance strengths using the multiconfigurational-Dirac-Fock method (GRASP2018) for bound electronic wave functions and numerically solving the Dirac equation for continuum electron wave functions, adopting nuclear reduced transition probabilities from theoretical calculations by Minkov (2017, 2019, 2021) and Kirschbaum (2022). They then computed NEEC rates by convolving the cross section with electron flux distributions for EBIT and storage ring parameters, considering the indirect excitation pathway: NEEC populates the second-excited state of $^{229}$Th at 29.19 keV, which then decays to the isomeric state $^{229m}$Th with a branching ratio.

## Key numbers

- The NEEC resonance strength $S_{\rm{neec}}$ reaches a maximum of 0.51 b eV for initial ion charge state $q=90$.
- For EBITs, the NEEC rate reaches $10^{-5}$ s$^{-1}$ per ion for the channel capturing into the $3s$ orbital of initial charge state $q=80$ (resonance electron energy 17.46 keV, $S_{\rm{neec}} = 1.0 \times 10^{-2}$ b eV) and $0.56 \times 10^{-5}$ s$^{-1}$ per ion for capturing into the $3p_{1/2}$ orbital of initial charge state $q=78$ (resonance electron energy 18.22 keV, $S_{\rm{neec}} = 5.6 \times 10^{-3}$ b eV).
- The $^{229m}$Th production rate via NEEC in an EBIT reaches the order of $10^{-1}$ isomer per second, assuming $10^5$ Th ions with 10% Th$^{80+}$ or 20% Th$^{78+}$.
- For storage rings, the NEEC rate is $\sim 10^{-5}$ s$^{-1}$ per ion for the channel of NEEC into $2p_{3/2}$ for initial ion charge state $q=86$ (resonance electron energy 2.907 keV, $S_{\rm{neec}} = 0.12$ b eV, width $\sim 0.1$ meV), while the radiative recombination rate is $\sim 10^{-3}$ s$^{-1}$ per ion.
- A 100-hour scan of the electron energy with a range of 10 eV in the precision of 1 meV would be sufficient to observe a narrow peak in the number of recombined ions above the radiative recombination background with the event number of a few hundreds.
- The characteristic signature includes an x-ray photon at 3.7 keV from the atomic transition, followed by a $\gamma$ photon at 29.1 keV with a time delay of about 30 ns from the radiative decay of the nuclear excited state.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of NEEC in $^{229}$Th, previously investigated for direct excitation to $^{229m}$Th in laser-cluster interaction plasmas by Qi (2023), and proposes a new indirect excitation pathway via the 29.19 keV second-excited state. It addresses the challenge of controllable isomer production, contrasting with statistical methods like $\alpha$-decay of $^{233}$U (Wense 2016, Seiferle 2019) and $\beta$-decay of $^{229}$Ac (Kraemer 2023), and improves upon the synchrotron radiation approach of Masuda (2019) by six orders of magnitude in production rate. The work also identifies a characteristic NEEC signature (recombined ion plus two-photon coincidence) that could resolve the ongoing debate about NEEC observation following the controversial $^{93m}$Mo isomer depletion claim by Chiara (2018) and subsequent contradictions by Wu (2019) and Polasik (2021).
