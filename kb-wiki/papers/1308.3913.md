# Discussion for the Solutions of Dyson-Schwinger Equations at m>0 in QED3

> In the case of nonzero fermion mass, within a range of Ansatze for the full fermion-boson vertex, we show that Dyson-Schwinger equation for the fermion propagator in QED3 has two qualitatively distinct dynamical chiral symmetry breaking solutions. As the fermion mass increases and reaches to a critical value mc, one solution disappears, and the dependence of mc on the number of fermion flavors is also given.

# Discussion for the Solutions of Dyson-Schwinger Equations at m>0 in QED3

## Key claim

For nonzero fermion mass in QED3, the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the fermion propagator possesses two distinct dynamical chiral symmetry breaking solutions (a "+" solution and a "-" solution) rather than only one, but the second solution disappears when the fermion mass exceeds a critical value m_c that depends on the number of fermion flavors. This contradicts the conventional expectation that only the Nambu-Goldstone solution exists at finite mass.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the fermion propagator and photon vacuum polarization in QED3 in the Landau gauge, using three different Ansätze for the full fermion-photon vertex: the bare vertex γ_ν, the form (1/2)[E(m,p²)+E(m,k²)]γ_ν, and the form E(m,p²)E(m,k²)γ_ν. Starting from different initial conditions (E=1, F=1, Π=1 versus F=-1, E=1, Π=1), they iterated the coupled integral equations until convergence to obtain two distinct stable solutions, then varied the fermion mass m as a continuous parameter to determine the critical mass where the second solution vanishes.

## Key numbers

- At N=1 and m=10⁻³, the "+" solution and "-" solution are both stable and distinct, with the fermion self-energy F(m,p²) positive in the "+" solution and negative in the "-" solution in the infrared region.
- For the "+" solution as m increases, the infrared values E(0) and F(0) increase while Π(0) decreases; for the "-" solution, the infrared values show a different trend as m increases.
- The critical mass m_c decreases as the number of fermion flavors N increases, and m_c vanishes at a critical N_c that corresponds to the critical number of fermion flavors for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of multi-solutions of the quark gap equation, previously studied in QCD by Zong et al. (2005), Chang et al. (2007), and Williams, Fischer, and Pennington (2007), to the simpler Abelian toy model QED3. The authors build on the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework for QED3 developed by Maris (1996) and use vertex Ansätze inspired by the Ball-Chiu vertex. The finding of a second solution at nonzero mass that disappears above a critical mass enables further study of the interplay between explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and raises questions about whether analogous solutions exist in QCD.
