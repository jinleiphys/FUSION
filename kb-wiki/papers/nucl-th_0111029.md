# Note on the Deformed Boson Scheme in Time-Dependent Variational Method

> The Holstein-Primakoff representation for the su(2)-algebra is derived in the deformed boson scheme. The following two points are discussed : (i) connection between a simple Hamiltonian and the Hamiltonian obeying the su(2)-algebra such as Lipkin model and (ii) derivation of the Hamiltonian for describing the damped and amplified motion for the su(2)-boson model.

# Note on the Deformed Boson Scheme in Time-Dependent Variational Method

## Key claim

The Holstein-Primakoff representation for the su(2)-algebra can be derived within the deformed boson scheme for a specific choice of the deformation function $\tilde{f}(n) = (1 - n/n_0)^{-1/2}$, but only when a projection operator $\hat{P}_{n_0}$ is introduced to handle the singularity at $n = n_0$, a case explicitly excluded in the authors' prior work (Part I).

## Method

The authors define a deformed boson annihilation operator $\hat{\gamma} = \hat{P}_{n_0} \tilde{f}(\hat{N})^{-1} \hat{c}$ and its Hermitian conjugate, where $\hat{c}$ is a standard boson operator, $\hat{N} = \hat{c}^\dagger \hat{c}$, and $\hat{P}_{n_0}$ projects onto the subspace spanned by $\{|n\rangle; n=0,1,\dots,n_0\}$. For the specific choice $\tilde{f}(n) = (1 - n/n_0)^{-1/2}$, these deformed operators become proportional to the Holstein-Primakoff representation of su(2) spin operators with magnitude $n_0/2$. The same formalism is applied to a simple boson Hamiltonian to obtain an su(2)-algebra Hamiltonian analogous to the Lipkin model, and extended to a two-boson system describing damped and amplified motion.

## Key numbers

- The deformation function $\tilde{f}(n) = (1 - n/n_0)^{-1/2}$ diverges at $n = n_0$, where $n_0$ is a positive integer.
- The resulting su(2) spin magnitude is $n_0/2$.
- The commutation relation for the deformed bosons becomes $[\hat{\gamma}, \hat{\gamma}^\dagger] = \hat{P}_{n_0} (1 - 2\hat{N}/n_0) \hat{P}_{n_0}$.
- For the su(1,1) case, the deformation function is $\tilde{f}(n) = (1 + n/n_0)^{-1/2}$ with $n_0$ positive (not necessarily integer), yielding spin magnitude $n_0/2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior deformed boson scheme (Kuriyama et al., Part I, 2001) by treating the previously excluded case where the deformation function diverges at a finite boson number $n_0$. The construction reproduces the Holstein-Primakoff representation for su(2) and su(1,1) algebras, with the projection operator formalism following earlier work by Marshalek (1975). The method enables mapping of simple boson Hamiltonians onto su(2)-algebra Hamiltonians such as the Lipkin model (Lipkin et al., 1965), and provides a conservative framework for describing damped and amplified motion in two-boson systems previously studied by Vitiello et al. (1986) and the present authors (Tsue et al., 1995).
