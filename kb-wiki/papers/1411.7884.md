# Constraints on the chiral unitary $\bar KN$ amplitude from $πΣK^+$ photoproduction data

> A chiral unitary approach for antikaon-nucleon scattering in on-shell factorization is studied. We find multiple sets of parameters for which the model describes all existing hadronic data similarly well. We confirm the two-pole structure of the $Λ(1405)$. The narrow $Λ(1405)$ pole appears at comparable positions in the complex energy plane, whereas the location of the broad pole suffers from a large uncertainty. In the second step, we use a simple model for photoproduction of $K^+πΣ$ off the proton and confront it with the experimental data from the CLAS collaboration. It is found that only a few of the hadronic solutions allow for a consistent description of the CLAS data within the assumed reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach for antikaon-nucleon scattering at next-to-leading order yields multiple parameter sets that describe all existing hadronic data equally well, but only a few of these solutions (specifically solutions #2, #4, and #5 out of eight) allow for a consistent description of the CLAS photoproduction data within the assumed simple reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral unitary amplitude for antikaon-nucleon scattering using a next-to-leading order chiral potential in on-shell factorization, solving a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation. They fit the 14 low-energy constants and 6 regularization scales to hadronic cross sections (K^-p reactions at laboratory momentum below 300 MeV), threshold decay ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width. In the second step, they built a simple semi-phenomenological photoproduction model where the reaction mechanism for gamma p -> K^+ M_i B_i is described by energy-dependent constants, and the final-state interaction MB -> pi Sigma is taken from the hadronic amplitude, then fit to the CLAS invariant mass distributions for all three pi Sigma charge channels at nine total energies from 2.0 to 2.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The narrow Lambda(1405) pole for solution #2 is located at 1434^{+2}_{-2} - i 10^{+2}_{-1} MeV, and for solution #4 at 1429^{+8}_{-7} - i 12^{+2}_{-3} MeV.
* The broad Lambda(1405) pole for solution #2 is located at 1330^{+4}_{-5} - i 56^{+17}_{-11} MeV, and for solution #4 at 1325^{+15}_{-15} - i 90^{+12}_{-18} MeV.
* The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the hadronic data ranges from 0.90 (solution #8) to 1.35 (solution #1) across the eight solutions.
* The chi-squared per data point for the CLAS photoproduction data ranges from 1.77 (solution #4) to 6.11 (solution #6); the next best solution after #2, #4, and #5 has a 40% larger total chi-squared than the best one.
* The SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen energy shift and width are Delta E - i Gamma/2 = (283 +/- 42) - i (271 +/- 55) eV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Mai (2012) and Borasoy (2006) for antikaon-nucleon scattering by incorporating the CLAS photoproduction data from Moriya (2013) as a new constraint. It confirms the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) found by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003), and shows that the broad pole ambiguity can be reduced by combining hadronic data with photoproduction data, similar to the analyses of Roca (2013) and Nakamura (2013). The results enable future construction of a fully gauge-invariant two-meson photoproduction amplitude along the lines of Borasoy (2007) and Mai (2012).
