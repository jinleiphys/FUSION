# Universal symmetry energy contribution to the neutron star equation of state

> We discuss the observation that under neutron star conditions of charge neutrality and $β-$equilibrium the contribution from the symmetry energy to the equation of state (EoS) follows a universal behaviour. We call this behaviour the conjecture of a Universal Symmetry Energy Contribution (USEC). We find that an USEC holds provided the density dependence of the symmetry energy $E_s(n)$ follows a behaviour that limits the proton fraction $x(n)$ to values below the threshold for the direct Urca (DU) cooling process. The absence of DU cooling in typical mass neutron stars appears to be supported by the phenomenology of neutron star cooling data and allows to constrain the behaviour of $E_s(n)$ at high densities. Two classes of symmetry energy functions are investigated more in detail to elucidate the USEC. We derive an analytic formula for the USEC to the neutron star EoS based on the result for the symmetry energy extracted from isobaric analog states of nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the contribution of the nuclear symmetry energy to the neutron star equation of state follows a universal behavior (termed USEC), but only under the condition that the density dependence of the symmetry energy limits the proton fraction below the threshold for the direct Urca cooling process, which appears to be supported by neutron star cooling data.

## Method
The authors solve the equations for charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium in neutron star matter to obtain proton and muon fractions as functions of baryon density for two classes of symmetry energy models: a power-law (MDI-type) ansatz and a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD2-type) parametrization. They then compute neutron star mass-radius relations from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and compare central densities to direct Urca threshold densities, deriving an analytic formula for the universal symmetry energy contribution based on the Danielewicz-Lee constraint from isobaric analog states.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at reference density n* = 0.105 fm^-3 is E_s* = 25.7 MeV from the Danielewicz-Lee analysis (2013)
- The proton fraction at this reference density is x* = 0.0363
- The universal symmetry energy contribution at n* is S* = 22.04 MeV
- For the asymptotic limit n → ∞, the universal proton fraction is x = 1/8 and muon fraction is x_mu = 1/16, giving S(n) = 21.05 MeV (n/n*)^(1/3)
- The direct Urca threshold without muons is x_DU = 1/9 = 11.1%
- For the MDI-type model with gamma = 2/3, the DU threshold density is n_DU = 0.485 fm^-3, which is not exceeded by central densities of 2.0 M_sun stars (n_c = 0.458 fm^-3)
- For the DD2++ model, the DU threshold density is n_DU = 0.354 fm^-3, exceeded for stars with masses 1.60 M_sun and above

## Context
This work extends the earlier USEC conjecture by Klahn (2006) and builds on the isobaric analog state constraints from Danielewicz and Lee (2013). The paper contrasts two symmetry energy classes: the MDI-type power-law ansatz from Tsang (2003) and the DD2-type density functional approach from Typel (2014). The results enable constraining the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy using neutron star cooling phenomenology, and the analytic formula derived can be used to incorporate the universal symmetry energy contribution into neutron star equation of state calculations without detailed knowledge of the symmetry energy at supersaturation densities.
