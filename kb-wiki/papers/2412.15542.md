# Correlations of net baryon number and electric charge in nuclear matter

> We investigate the correlations between net baryon number and electric charge up to sixth order related to the interactions of nuclear matter at low temperature, and explore their relationship with the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT) within the framework of the nonlinear Walecka model. The calculation shows that strong correlations between the baryon number and electric charge exist in the vicinity of LGPT, and the higher order correlations are more sensitive than the lower order ones near the phase transition. However, in the high-temperature region away from the LGPT the rescaled lower order correlations are relatively larger than most of the higher order ones. Besides, some of the fifth- and sixth-order correlations possibly change the sign from negative to positive along the chemical freeze-out line with the decrease of temperature. In combination with the future experimental projects at lower collision energies, the derived results can be referred to study the phase structure of strongly interacting matter and analyze the related experimental signals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlations between net baryon number and electric charge up to sixth order, calculated within the nonlinear Walecka model, become stronger near the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT), with higher-order correlations more sensitive than lower-order ones near the critical region; however, the abstract's claim that some fifth- and sixth-order correlations "possibly change sign" is hedged by the fact that this behavior occurs only along the extrapolated chemical freeze-out line when approaching the LGPT critical region from the high-temperature side.

## Method

The authors used the nonlinear Walecka model in the mean-field approximation to compute generalized susceptibilities (off-diagonal correlations) of net baryon number and electric charge up to sixth order in nuclear matter. They imposed an isospin asymmetry constraint of ρ_Q/ρ_B = 0.4 to simulate conditions relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments, and evaluated the correlations as functions of baryon chemical potential at fixed temperatures of T = 25, 50, and 75 MeV. The results were then projected onto a parameterized chemical freeze-out line from Cleymans (2006) to connect with experimental observables.

## Key numbers

- The critical end point (CEP) of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is located at T = 13 MeV and μ_B = 919 MeV.
- The model parameters are fitted to nuclear saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, with compression modulus K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy a_sym = 31.3 MeV, effective nucleon mass m*_N = 0.75 m_N, and binding energy B/A = -16.0 MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out line is parameterized as T(μ_B) = 0.166 GeV - 0.139 GeV^{-1} μ_B^2 - 0.053 GeV^{-3} μ_B^4.
- The value of |μ_Q| is smaller than 40 MeV in the low-density region where ρ_B < 0.1 ρ_0.
- At chemical freeze-out, the fourth-order correlations satisfy χ_{13}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q < χ_{22}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q < χ_{31}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q for each temperature.
- At chemical freeze-out, the fifth-order correlations satisfy |χ_{14}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{23}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{32}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{41}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q|.
- At chemical freeze-out, the sixth-order correlations satisfy |χ_{15}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{24}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{33}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{42}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q| < |χ_{51}^{BQ}/χ_2^Q|.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of net baryon number fluctuations (kurtosis, skewness, hyperskewness, hyperkurtosis) in the nonlinear Walecka model by Shao (2020), Xu (2023), and Yang (2024) to include off-diagonal correlations between baryon number and electric charge. It complements lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas calculations of charge correlations at high temperature (Bellwied 2015, Ding 2015, Fodor 2018) by focusing on the low-temperature nuclear matter regime. The results are intended to guide analysis of upcoming experimental data from the RHIC BES program at collision energies below 7.7 GeV, the HIAF facility, and the HADES collaboration at GSI, which will probe the LGPT region through higher-order net proton and net charge fluctuations.
