# Surface Incompressibility from Semiclassical Relativistic Mean Field Calculations

> By using the scaling method and the Thomas-Fermi and Extended Thomas-Fermi approaches to Relativistic Mean Field Theory the surface contribution to the leptodermous expansion of the finite nuclei incompressibility has been self-consistently computed. The validity of the simplest expansion, which contains volume, volume-symmetry, surface and Coulomb terms, is examined by comparing it with self-consistent results of the finite nuclei incompressibility for some currently used non-linear sigma-omega parameter sets. A numerical estimate of higher-order contributions to the leptodermous expansion, namely the curvature and surface-symmetry terms, is made.

## Key claim

The surface incompressibility coefficient \(K_{sf}\) in the leptodermous expansion of finite-nucleus incompressibility can be self-consistently computed within Relativistic Mean Field Theory using semiclassical Thomas-Fermi and Extended Thomas-Fermi approaches, but the precise value is model-dependent and the simplest expansion (containing only volume, volume-symmetry, surface, and Coulomb terms) fails to reproduce self-consistent results for light and very asymmetric nuclei unless curvature and surface-symmetry terms are added.

## Method

The authors applied the scaling method to symmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter within the Relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) and Relativistic Extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximations to the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory. They computed the second derivative of the surface tension with respect to the central density at saturation, \(\ddot{\sigma}(\rho_0)\), and from it the surface incompressibility coefficient \(K_{sf}\) using the Blaizot formula. For finite nuclei, they compared self-consistent RETF calculations of the incompressibility \(K_A\) with the leptodermous expansion, and numerically estimated curvature and surface-symmetry coefficients by fitting results for large uncharged systems (A from 250 to 300000).

## Key numbers

- For the NL1 parameter set (\(K_{\infty}=211.1\) MeV): \(K_{sf} = -170.6\) MeV (RTF) and \(-225.4\) MeV (RETF); the ratio \(-K_{sf}/K_{\infty}\) is 0.81 (RTF) and 1.07 (RETF).
- For the NL3 parameter set (\(K_{\infty}=271.5\) MeV): \(K_{sf} = -310.4\) MeV (RTF) and \(-313.7\) MeV (RETF); the ratio \(-K_{sf}/K_{\infty}\) is 1.14 (RTF) and 1.16 (RETF).
- For the NL-SH parameter set (\(K_{\infty}=355.0\) MeV): \(K_{sf} = -469.8\) MeV (RTF) and \(-429.6\) MeV (RETF); the ratio \(-K_{sf}/K_{\infty}\) is 1.32 (RTF) and 1.21 (RETF).
- For the HS linear set (\(K_{\infty}=546.8\) MeV): \(K_{sf} = -996.7\) MeV (RTF) and \(-804.2\) MeV (RETF); the ratio \(-K_{sf}/K_{\infty}\) is 1.82 (RTF) and 1.47 (RETF).
- Linear fits of \(-K_{sf}\) versus \(K_{\infty}\) for all parameter sets give: \(-K_{sf} = 1.47 K_{\infty} - 84\) (RETF) and \(-K_{sf} = 2.19 K_{\infty} - 295\) (RTF).
- Estimated curvature coefficients from fits for uncharged symmetric nuclei: \(-317.2\) MeV (NL1), \(-229.8\) MeV (NL3), \(-185.6\) MeV (NL-SH).
- Estimated surface-symmetry coefficients from fits for uncharged nuclei with \(I=0.1\): 1951.4 MeV (NL1), 1754.0 MeV (NL3), 1716.5 MeV (NL-SH).
- The extended leptodermous expansion including curvature and surface-symmetry terms reproduces self-consistent \(K_A\) with average errors smaller than 1.2% (NL1), 0.9% (NL3), and 0.3% (NL-SH).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic scaling-method calculations of the surface incompressibility coefficient (Blaizot 1980; Nayak, Pearson, Farine, Gleissl, and Brack 1990) to the relativistic mean-field framework, where only approximate estimates existed previously (Stocker and Sharma 1991; Sharma, Nagarajan, and Ring 1994). It provides self-consistent values of \(K_{sf}\) for several linear and non-linear \(\sigma-\omega\) parameter sets and demonstrates that the simplest leptodermous expansion is insufficient for light and neutron-rich nuclei, motivating the inclusion of curvature and surface-symmetry terms. The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear matter incompressibility from giant monopole resonance data in finite nuclei and can be compared with future Hartree-level RMF calculations.
