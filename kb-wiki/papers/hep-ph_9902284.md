# Rare Pionium Decays and Pion Polarizability

> We calculate the decay of pionium atoms into two photons. The pion polarizability gives rise to a 10% correction to the corresponding decay width for pointlike pions. This opens the possibility to obtain the difference between the electric and magnetic polarizability of the charged pion from a future measurement of the branching fraction of pionium into two photons. For such an experiment the ππ-scattering lengths would have to be known to better than 5% precision. We also comment on the contribution of the axial anomaly to the decay of pionium into γπ^0.

# Rare Pionium Decays and Pion Polarizability

## Key claim
The branching ratio of pionium decay into two photons receives a correction of order 10% from the charged pion polarizabilities, enabling a clean extraction of the difference between the electric and magnetic polarizability of the charged pion, provided the ππ-scattering lengths are known to better than 5% precision.

## Method
The authors calculate the decay width for pionium → γγ using the Compton scattering amplitude for charged pions, crossing symmetry, and the nonrelativistic bound-state wave function of pionium. The amplitude is expanded at threshold to include the pion polarizability contribution (α_π − β_π), and the branching ratio relative to the dominant π⁰π⁰ decay channel is derived, eliminating dependence on the pionium wave function.

## Key numbers
- The decay width for pionium → γγ is Γ = (m_π α⁵ / 4) × [1 + (m_π³/α)(α_π − β_π)]², where α is the fine structure constant and m_π is the charged pion mass.
- Using the Serpukhov experimental value α_π = −β_π = 6.8 ± 1.4 ± 1.2 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), the width becomes Γ = 0.723 meV × [1 + 0.132 + 0.004], giving a 13.2% polarizability correction.
- The branching ratio for pionium → γγ relative to the total width is approximately 0.28% times the polarizability correction factor squared.
- The current experimental value for the ππ-scattering length difference is (a₀⁰ − a₀²) = (0.288 ± 0.051) m_π⁻¹ from Dum83.
- Chiral Perturbation Theory predicts α_π = −β_π = 2.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at one loop (Hol90), and α_π = 2.4 ± 0.5, β_π = −2.1 ± 0.5 at two loops (Bue96).

## Context
This work extends the study of pionium decays beyond the dominant π⁰π⁰ channel, which is the focus of the DIRAC experiment at CERN (Sch98). Prior measurements of pion polarizabilities from radiative pion scattering (Primakoff effect, Ant83) and radiative pion photoproduction (Aib86) disagree with Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions (Hol90, Bue96). The proposed pionium decay method offers a threshold determination of α_π − β_π without the extrapolation uncertainties that plague other approaches. The authors also note that the decay pionium → γπ⁰ is suppressed at threshold and governed by the axial anomaly (WZW71, Hol96), but is not competitive for polarizability extraction.
