# Search for the possible S=+1 Pentaquark states in Quenched Lattice QCD

> We study spin $\frac12$ hadronic states in quenched lattice QCD to search for a possible $S=+1$ pentaquark resonance. Simulations are carried out on $8^3\times 24$, $10^3\times 24$, $12^3\times 24$ and $16^3\times 24$ lattices at $β$=5.7 at the quenched level with the standard plaquette gauge action and the Wilson quark action. We adopt a Dirichlet boundary condition in the time direction for the quark to circumvent the possible contaminations due to the (anti)periodic boundary condition for the quark field, which are peculiar to the pentaquark. By diagonalizing the $2\times 2$ correlation matrices constructed from two independent operators with the quantum numbers $(I,J)=(0,\frac12)$, we successfully obtain the energies of the lowest state and the 2nd-lowest state in this channel. The analysis of the volume dependence of the energies and spectral weight factors indicates that a resonance state is likely to exist slightly above the NK threshold in $(I,J^P)=(0,\frac12^-)$ channel.

## Key claim

The study finds that a resonance state is likely to exist slightly above the NK threshold in the $(I,J^P)=(0,\frac12^-)$ channel, but this conclusion is conditional on quenched lattice QCD with heavy quarks on coarse lattices, and the authors explicitly assume that the qualitative spectral content would not change drastically in the continuum and chiral limits.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on $8^3\times 24$, $10^3\times 24$, $12^3\times 24$, and $16^3\times 24$ lattices at $\beta=5.7$ using the standard plaquette gauge action and Wilson quark action. They constructed $2\times 2$ correlation matrices from two independent interpolating operators with quantum numbers $(I,J)=(0,\frac12)$, applied a variational method to extract the lowest and second-lowest energy states, and analyzed the volume dependence of both energies and spectral weight factors to distinguish a possible resonance from NK scattering states.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing $a = 0.17$ fm from the Sommer scale, giving physical lattice sizes of $1.4^3\times 4.0$ fm$^4$, $1.7^3\times 4.0$ fm$^4$, $2.0^3\times 4.0$ fm$^4$, and $2.7^3\times 4.0$ fm$^4$
- Hopping parameters $(\kappa_{u,d},\kappa_{s})$ = $(0.1600,0.1650)$, $(0.1625,0.1650)$, $(0.1650,0.1650)$, $(0.1600,0.1600)$, and $(0.1650,0.1600)$, corresponding to current quark masses $(m_{u,d},m_{s})\sim(240,100)$, $(170,100)$, $(100,100)$, $(240,240)$, and $(100,240)$ MeV
- 2900 gauge configurations for $8^3\times 24$ and $10^3\times 24$ lattices, 1950 for $12^3\times 24$, and 950 for $16^3\times 24$
- Energy difference $\delta E = E_{NK} - (E_N + E_K) = -0.0128(38)$ for $(\kappa_{u,d},\kappa_s)=(0.1650,0.1650)$ on $12^3\times 24$ lattice
- The energy difference between the lowest and next-lowest NK scattering states ranges from 180 MeV to 860 MeV for spatial extents $1.4$ fm $\leq L \leq 3.5$ fm
- The empirical NK scattering length in the $I=0$ channel is $a_0^{KN}(I=0) = -0.0075$ fm

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, which had produced contradictory conclusions: some groups (Csikor et al. 2003, Sasaki 2003, Takahashi et al. 2004) found negative parity, while Chiu and Hsieh (2004) reported a positive parity state, and others (Mathur et al. 2004, Ishii et al. 2004) suggested the absence of $\Theta^+$. The present study improves on prior work by using a Dirichlet boundary condition to avoid contaminations from hadronic states propagating across the temporal boundary, which the authors argue can mimic a fake plateau in the correlator. The results enable future unquenched calculations with lighter quarks and larger volumes to confirm the existence and properties of the candidate resonance state.
