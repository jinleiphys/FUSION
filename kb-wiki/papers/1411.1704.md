# Theoretical Constraints and Systematic Effects in the Determination of the Proton Form Factors

> We calculate the two-photon exchange corrections to electron-proton scattering with nucleon and $Δ$ intermediate states. The results show a dependence on the elastic nucleon and nucleon-$Δ$-transition form factors used as input which leads to significant changes compared to previous calculations. We discuss the relevance of these corrections and apply them to the most recent and precise data set and world data from electron-proton scattering. Using this, we show how the form factor extraction from these data is influenced by the subsequent inclusion of physical constraints. The determination of the proton charge radius from scattering data is shown to be dominated by the enforcement of a realistic spectral function. Additionally, the third Zemach moment from the resulting form factors is calculated. The obtained radius and Zemach moment are shown to be consistent with Lamb shift measurements in muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange corrections to electron-proton scattering show a significant dependence on the elastic nucleon and nucleon-Delta-transition form factors used as input, leading to changes compared to previous calculations; the determination of the proton charge radius from scattering data is dominated by the enforcement of a realistic spectral function, and the obtained radius and third Zemach moment are consistent with Lamb shift measurements in muonic hydrogen.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon exchange corrections including nucleon and Delta intermediate states using realistic form factors at the photon-baryon vertices, employing both a parametrization of the gamma-N-Delta vertex with dipole behavior and a direct matching to helicity amplitudes from electroproduction data. They applied these corrections to the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) data and world electron-proton scattering data, then performed fits to extract nucleon form factors using parametrizations constrained by analyticity and unitarity via dispersion relations, including the two-pion continuum and vector meson pole contributions.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction factor delta_{2gamma,Delta} at Q^2 = 3 GeV^2 shows coefficients C_M ranging from 2.55 x 10^{-4} at epsilon = 0.1 to 0.89 x 10^{-4} at epsilon = 0.9, with the NFF parametrization from Lorenz (2012)
- The dipole behavior of the gamma-N-Delta vertex assumes a cutoff Lambda = 0.84 GeV
- The photocouplings used for the gamma-N-Delta vertex are g_1 = 6.59, g_2 = 9.08, g_3 = 7.12
- The isovector two-pion continuum threshold is t_0 = 4 M_pi^2 and the isoscalar threshold is 9 M_pi^2
- The isoscalar vector meson residua satisfy 0.5 GeV^2 < |a_1^omega| < 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous two-photon exchange calculations by Blunden et al. (2005) and Kondratyuk et al. (2005) by including realistic momentum-dependent form factors at the vertices, and improves upon earlier physically constrained fits by Belushkin et al. (2006) and Lorenz et al. (2012) by applying the TPE corrections to the most precise MAMI data. The results enable a consistent extraction of the proton charge radius and third Zemach moment that can be compared with muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements, and provide input for future work on nucleon form factors in the timelike region via dispersion relations.
