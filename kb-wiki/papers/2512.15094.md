# Crossover or First-Order: Impacts of Regularization

> The equation of state of hot, dense nuclear matter plays a fundamental role in many areas. However, owing to the nonperturbative nature of strong interactions, a reliable treatment is still under debate. We use a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector\,$\otimes$\,vector contact interaction to study related issues at nonzero temperature or quark chemical potential, and carefully compare a noncovariant and a covariant regularization scheme. The numerical results show that the character of the phase transition depends sensitively on the regularization scheme and parameter choices.

## Key claim

The character of the chiral phase transition in hot, dense nuclear matter, whether it is a crossover or first-order, depends sensitively on the choice of regularization scheme (noncovariant three-momentum cutoff versus covariant proper-time regularization) and on the inclusion of the vector mean field \(R_C\), not merely on the interaction kernel itself.

## Method

The authors solved the rainbow-truncated quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using a symmetry-preserving vector\(\otimes\)vector contact interaction (SCI) at nonzero temperature or quark chemical potential. They compared two regularization schemes: a noncovariant three-momentum (3M) cutoff and a covariant proper-time (PT) regularization, solving the gap equations for the scalar functions \(R_B\) (effective quark mass) and \(R_C\) (vector self-energy) at zero chemical potential with varying temperature, and at zero temperature with varying chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* At \(T=0\) GeV and beyond the chiral limit, the 3M regularization scheme yields a first-order phase transition with Nambu and Wigner solutions coexisting in the range \(\mu \approx 421.5\) MeV to \(\mu \approx 422.0\) MeV.
* The PT regularization scheme at \(T=0\) GeV produces a crossover transition, qualitatively similar to the temperature-driven transition, with \(R_C\) becoming nonzero and increasing gradually with \(\mu\).
* At \(\mu=0\) GeV, both regularization schemes give very similar results, with a second-order phase transition in the chiral limit (\(m=0\) GeV) and a crossover for finite current quark mass (\(m=0.005\) GeV for 3M, \(m=0.007\) GeV for PT).
* The 3M cutoff parameter is \(\Lambda_{3M}=0.650\) GeV; the PT ultraviolet cutoff is \(\Lambda_{uv}=0.905\) GeV and infrared cutoff \(\Lambda_{ir}=0.24\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD phase diagram using effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov-loop extended version (PNJL), which are known to be sensitive to regularization prescriptions (Inagaki, 2015; Kohyama, 2016). The authors show that the SCI, while formally similar to the NJL model, carries different color factors and that the choice between a hard 3M cutoff and a soft PT regularization can determine whether the low-temperature transition is first-order or a crossover. This result highlights the need for careful regularization in nonrenormalizable models and enables future work to assess which scheme better captures the true QCD phase structure, particularly for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collisions.
