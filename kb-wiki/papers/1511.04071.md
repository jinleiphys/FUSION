# Diffusion of dark matter in a hot and dense nuclear environment

> We calculate the mean free path in a hot and dense nuclear environment for a fermionic dark matter particle candidate in the $\sim$GeV mass range interacting with nucleons via scalar and vector effective couplings. We focus on the effects of density and temperature in the nuclear medium in order to evaluate the importance of the final state blocking in the scattering process. We discuss qualitatively possible implications for opacities in stellar nuclear scenarios, where dark matter may be gravitationally accreted.

## Key claim

The mean free path of a fermionic dark matter particle in a hot and dense nuclear environment is calculated, showing that in-medium effects (Fermi blocking, finite temperature, and effective nucleon masses) significantly modify the scattering cross-section compared to the simplified estimate λχ ≃ 1/σχN n, and that the diffusive approximation is well grounded for dark matter in neutron star interiors, provided the dark matter-nucleon cross-section exceeds the geometrical cross-section σ0 ≃ 10⁻⁴⁵ cm².

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory Lagrangian with scalar and vector contact interactions between a Dirac fermion dark matter particle and nucleons (protons and neutrons). They compute the doubly differential and integrated dark matter-nucleon scattering cross-section per unit volume in a relativistic framework, incorporating Fermi-Dirac distributions for the nucleon sector (including Fermi blocking factors), finite temperature effects via detailed balance factors, and effective nucleon masses and chemical potentials from the Walecka model at baryonic densities n = (0.5–3)n₀ and temperatures T ≲ 50 MeV. The mean free path is obtained as the inverse of the integrated cross-section per unit volume, and monopolar form factors with a cutoff Λ = 1.5 GeV are used to model nucleon structure.

## Key numbers

* Dark matter mean free path λχ ≪ R (star radius ~12 km) for mχ = 0.5–5 GeV at n = n₀ and T = 0, confirming diffusive behavior.
* For a simplified estimate with σχN ∼ 10⁻⁴¹ cm², λχ ≃ 1/σχN n ∼ 5.9 m (dot-dashed line in Figure 6).
* At T = 0 and n = n₀, the differential cross-section per unit volume shows a triangular shape with maximum energy transfer ω − mχ ≈ 130 MeV for mχ = 0.5 GeV.
* Effective nucleon mass ratios m*N/mN ≃ 0.85, 0.7, and 0.4 for densities n = 0.5n₀, n₀, and 2n₀, respectively.
* At n = 2n₀, the averaged scalar and vector cross-sections per particle are ⟨σs,χN⟩ ∼ 10⁻⁴⁷ cm² and ⟨σv,χN⟩ ∼ 10⁻⁴³ cm² for mχ ∼ GeV.
* The center-of-mass energy for the boosted scattering scenario is √s ≲ 6 GeV for mχ ≲ 5 GeV.
* The Lorentz factor at the neutron star surface is γ ≈ 1.26 (β ≈ 0.6) for MNS = 1.5 M⊙ and RNS = 12 km.

## Context

This work extends the study of dark matter interactions in dense nuclear matter beyond the simplified mean free path estimate, incorporating in-medium corrections previously applied to neutrino scattering (Reddy et al., 1998; Horowitz et al., 1999). It is motivated by the possibility of dark matter gravitational accretion onto neutron stars (Kouvaris, 2008; Zurek, 2014; Bramante, 2015) and the need to compare bounds from collider searches (ATLAS, CMS) and direct detection experiments (CDMS, SuperCDMS) in the low-mass region mχ ≲ 5 GeV. The results enable future calculations of dark matter energy transport in proto-neutron stars and assessments of dark matter opacity in stellar nuclear environments.
