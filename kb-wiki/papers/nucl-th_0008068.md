# External Coulomb and angular momentum influence on isotope composition of nuclear fragments

> The Markov chain statistical multifragmentation model predicts inhomogeneous distributions of fragments and their isospin in the freeze-out volume caused by an angular momentum and external long-range Coulomb field. These effects can take place in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies and lead to neutron-rich isotopes produced in the midrapidity kinematical region of the reactions.

## Key claim

The Markov chain statistical multifragmentation model predicts that in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions, the combined effects of angular momentum and the external Coulomb field of the target nucleus produce an inhomogeneous spatial distribution of fragment isospin in the freeze-out volume, leading to neutron-rich isotopes preferentially emitted into the midrapidity region. This effect is presented as a statistical alternative to dynamical "neck" emission explanations for symmetry violation in fragment production.

## Method

The author used the Markov chain version of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), which generates partitions via the Metropolis algorithm and places primary hot fragments directly into the freeze-out volume to calculate their Coulomb interaction and moment of inertia. Calculations were performed for a Au projectile source (A_s=197, Z_s=79) at excitation energy 3 MeV/nucleon and freeze-out density ρ_s=ρ_0/6, placed at a fixed distance of 20 fm from a target Au nucleus, corresponding to a peripheral collision at 35 A·MeV. The model included angular momentum conservation and accounted for secondary deexcitation of hot fragments via evaporation, fission, or Fermi break-up.

## Key numbers

- Au source with A_s=197, Z_s=79 at excitation energy E_s*=3 MeV/nucleon and freeze-out density ρ_s=ρ_0/6 (ρ_0 is normal nuclear density)
- Angular momentum of 150 ħ applied to the source increases the neutron-to-proton (N/Z) ratio of intermediate mass fragments (IMF, charges Z=3-20)
- Target-projectile distance fixed at 20 fm, corresponding to a break-up time of approximately 100 fm/c after a peripheral collision at 35 A·MeV
- Characteristic break-up time for highly excited projectile-like nuclei is around 100 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) developed by Botvina and collaborators (1995) and the Markov chain implementation (Botvina, 1999) to include the influence of both angular momentum and external Coulomb fields on fragment isospin. It builds on earlier findings (Botvina, 1999) that Coulomb interaction between target and projectile-like sources leads to predominant midrapidity emission of intermediate mass fragments. The results contradict the interpretation that spatial asymmetry of fragment emission is necessarily a sign of dynamical "neck" emission, instead proposing a statistical explanation where the symmetry of phase space is deformed by the interaction of the two sources. This work enables experimental tests of whether neutron-rich isotopes observed in the midrapidity region of peripheral collisions can be explained by statistical processes rather than non-equilibrium dynamics.
