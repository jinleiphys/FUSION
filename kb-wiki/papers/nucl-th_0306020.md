# Deuteron Anapole Moment with Heavy Mesons

> Parity-nonconserving two-body currents due to vector meson exchange are considered with the aim to determine the related contributions to the anapole moment. A particular attention is given to the requirement of current conservation which is essential for a reliable estimate of this quantity. An application is made for the deuteron case.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the contributions of vector-meson-exchange parity-nonconserving two-body currents to the deuteron anapole moment, with particular attention to satisfying current conservation; the authors note that these heavy-meson contributions could contribute as much as, if not more than, the previously studied pion-exchange component.

## Method

The authors construct parity-nonconserving two-body electromagnetic currents from vector meson exchange using an S-matrix approach based on the DDH (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein) potential model, which includes π, ρ, and ω mesons. The currents are derived at leading order in 1/M_N and verified to satisfy current conservation with the DDH potential. The deuteron wave function is obtained using the Argonne v_18 potential, including parity-admixed components in the ³P̃₁ and ¹P̃₁ channels induced by the PNC interaction, and the anapole moment matrix elements are evaluated from both one-body and two-body currents.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constants used are g_πNN = 13.45, g_ρNN = 2.79, g_ωNN = 8.37
- The DDH best values (in units of 10⁻⁷) are: h_π¹ = 4.6, h_ρ⁰ = -11.4, h_ρ¹ = -0.2, h_ρ² = -9.5, h_ω⁰ = -1.9, h_ω¹ = -1.1
- The spin current contribution to the anapole moment (in fm²) is: -0.547 h_π¹ e I + (-3.7 h_ρ¹ + 10.8 h_ω¹ + 7.3 h_ρ⁰ + 3.7 h_ω⁰) × 10⁻³ e I
- The convection current contribution is: 0.039 h_π¹ e I + (2.7 h_ρ¹ - 7.6 h_ω¹) × 10⁻⁴ e I
- The pion exchange current contribution (pair + mesonic) is: (-0.027 + 0.028) h_π¹ e I = 0.001 h_π¹ e I

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the deuteron anapole moment that considered only pion-exchange components of the PNC nucleon-nucleon force, including calculations using effective field theory (Savage and Springer, 1998, 2001), zero-range strong forces (Khriplovich and Korkin, 2000), and realistic NN strong forces (Hyun et al., 2003). The paper addresses the requirement of gauge invariance and consistency with the DDH potential model, building on earlier work by Hiyama et al. (2002). The results enable future comparisons between pion-exchange and vector-meson-exchange contributions to the deuteron anapole moment, though the authors note that experimental measurement is not feasible in the near future.
