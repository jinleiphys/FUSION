# Pairing in spherical nuclei: quasi-particle random phase approximation calculations with the Gogny interaction

> We investigate the effects of the pairing in spherical nuclei. We use the same finite-range interaction of Gogny type in the three steps of our approach, Hartree-Fock, Bardeen, Cooper and Schrieffer, and quasi-particle random phase approximation calculations. We study electric and magnetic dipole, quadrupole and octuple excitations in oxygen and calcium isotopes and also in isotopes with 20 neutrons. We investigate the pairing effects on single particle energies and occupation probabilities, on the excitation energies, $B$-values and collectivity of low-lying states including the isoscalar electric dipole and the magnetic dipole excitations, and also the giant resonances. The inclusion of the pairing increases the values of the excitation energies in all the cases we have studied. In general, the effects of the pairing are too small to remarkably improve the agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of pairing in QRPA calculations for spherical nuclei increases excitation energies in all studied cases, but the effects are generally too small to significantly improve agreement with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations to generate single-particle wave functions and energies, then solved Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) equations to obtain occupation probabilities and quasiparticle energies, and finally constructed QRPA matrices to describe excited states. The same finite-range Gogny D1M interaction was used consistently across all three steps (HF, BCS, and QRPA), with the pairing effects isolated by switching off the G-terms in the QRPA equations (QRPA(F) calculations) and by comparing to standard RPA calculations without pairing.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy differences between QRPA and RPA calculations: about 2 MeV for 1- states, 3 MeV for 2+ states, and 1.5 MeV for 3- states
- Average energy difference (QRPA minus RPA) for 2+ states: 0.85 MeV with standard deviation 0.53 MeV
- Average energy difference (QRPA minus RPA) for 1- states: 0.31 MeV with standard deviation 0.35 MeV
- Average energy difference (QRPA minus RPA) for 3- states: 0.14 MeV with standard deviation 0.19 MeV
- Average energy difference (QRPA minus QRPA(F)) for 2+ states: -0.22 MeV with standard deviation 0.47 MeV
- BCS configuration space included all single-particle states with energy up to 10 MeV, providing ground-state energy stability within the keV range
- Pearson correlation coefficient between Delta v and Delta epsilon across all nuclei: smaller than 0.04

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HF+BCS studies (Anguiano 2014, 2015, 2016a) by adding QRPA calculations with the same Gogny interaction, following the approach originally outlined by Decharge and Gogny (1980). The calculations belong to the category of QRPA approaches that use the same interaction for mean-field, pairing, and residual particle-hole matrix elements, similar to the work of Giai (2003) and Margueron (2011). The results enable systematic comparison of pairing effects across multiple multipolarities and isotopic chains, and show that while pairing systematically increases excitation energies, the magnitude is insufficient to resolve discrepancies with experiment, suggesting that other physics (such as beyond-QRPA correlations or improved interactions) may be needed.
