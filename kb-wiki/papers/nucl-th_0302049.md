# Parity-Violating Electron Scattering: How Strange a Future

> I discuss several physics issues that can be addressed through the present and future program of parity-violating electron scattering measurements. In particular, I focus on strange quark form factors, hadronic effects in electroweak radiative corrections, and physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

Parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) provides a theoretically clean probe of strange quark contributions to nucleon form factors, but the interpretation of results for the strangeness magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) from measurements at a single kinematic point is insufficient without knowledge of the \(q^2\)-dependence, because higher-order chiral contributions to the magnetic radius are potentially large and governed by an unknown low-energy constant.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for extracting strange quark form factors from PVES measurements, focusing on chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) expansions for the strangeness magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) and radii \(\langle r^2_s\rangle_{M,E}\), lattice QCD computations using noise methods for disconnected diagrams, and dispersion relation analyses of the spectral function for the strangeness vector current. The author compares results from the Kentucky-Adelaide lattice QCD group and low-mass dispersion relation calculations for the low-energy constants \(b_s\), \(b_s^r\), and \(c_s^r\) that govern the strangeness form factors.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion for the strangeness magnetic radius gives \(\langle r^2_s\rangle_M = -[0.04 + 0.3 b_s^r] \; \text{fm}^2\), where the loop contribution is \(-0.04\;\text{fm}^2\) and the unknown low-energy constant \(b_s^r\) is expected to be of order unity.
- The chiral expansion for the strangeness electric radius gives \(\langle r^2_s\rangle_E \approx [-0.15 + 0.17 c_s^r] \; \text{fm}^2\).
- The low-mass dispersion relation prediction for the strangeness magnetic moment is \(\mu_s \approx -0.3\).
- The Kentucky-Adelaide lattice QCD/dipole fit gives \(b_s + 0.6 b_8 = -0.6 \pm 0.1\), \(b_s^r = 0.23 \pm 0.13\), and \(c_s^r = -0.06 \pm 0.4\).
- The low-mass dispersion relation gives \(b_s + 0.6 b_8 = -0.6\), \(b_s^r = -1.1\), and \(c_s^r = 3.4\).
- The SAMPLE experiment measured \(\GMS\) at \(Q^2 = 0.1 \; (\text{GeV}/c)^2\).

## Context

This work reviews the status of the PVES program following the first results from the SAMPLE Collaboration at MIT-Bates (2001) and anticipates future measurements from the G0, HAPPEx, A4, and Q-Weak experiments. The paper extends earlier theoretical work by Musolf et al. (1994) on electroweak radiative corrections and by Hammer et al. (2002) on chiral perturbation theory for strange form factors, and it highlights the disagreement between the Kentucky-Adelaide lattice QCD results (Liu et al., 2002) and the Lewis et al. lattice calculation that found no significant signal for strangeness vector current matrix elements. The resolution of these theoretical issues and completion of the full PVES experimental program are needed to determine whether the strange sea is "hidden" through cancellations as suggested by dispersion relation analyses.
