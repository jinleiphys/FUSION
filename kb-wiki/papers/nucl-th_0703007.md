# Exact boson mapping of the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian

> An exact boson mapping of the reduced BCS (equal strength) pairing Hamiltonian is considered. In the mapping, fermion pair operators are mapped exactly to the corresponding bosons. The image of the mapping results in a Bose-Hubbard model with level dependent hopping. Though the resultant Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian is non-Hermitian, all eigenvalues are real when Uk/t< 1, where k is the total number of bosons. When U/t=1, a part of spectrum of the Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian corresponds exactly to the whole spectrum of the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian can be mapped exactly onto a Bose-Hubbard model with level-dependent hopping, but the resulting boson Hamiltonian is non-Hermitian; the mapping is exact only when the boson Hamiltonian is projected onto a physical subspace, and the full spectrum of the BCS Hamiltonian corresponds to only a subset of the Bose-Hubbard eigenvalues (specifically when \(U/t=1\), a part of the Bose-Hubbard spectrum matches the whole BCS spectrum).

## Method

The authors map fermion pair operators \(S_j^+\), \(S_j^-\), and \(\hat{k}_j\) directly onto boson creation and annihilation operators \(b_j^\dagger\), \(b_j\), and number operator \(n_j = b_j^\dagger b_j\), respectively, for each level \(j\). They then construct a boson Hamiltonian whose eigenstates are required to take the same Richardson-Gaudin Bethe ansatz form as the original fermion wavefunctions. The resulting boson Hamiltonian is a Bose-Hubbard model with site-dependent hopping, and the physical subspace is isolated via a projection operator built from the Bethe ansatz eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The condition for all eigenvalues of the Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian to be real is \(Uk/t < 1\), where \(k\) is the total number of bosons and \(U\) and \(t\) are the on-site repulsion and hopping parameters.
- When \(U/t = 1\), a part of the Bose-Hubbard spectrum (consisting of \(p!/((p-k)!k!)\) eigenvalues) corresponds exactly to the whole spectrum of the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian.
- The total number of eigenvalues of the Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian for \(p\) levels and \(k\) bosons is \((k + p - 1)!/(k!(p - 1)!)\).
- For a test case with \(p=3\), \(k=2\), \(t=1\), \(\epsilon_1=1.1\), \(\epsilon_2=2.2\), \(\epsilon_3=3.3\), and \(U=1/3\), the six eigenvalues are 1.768, 4.475, 6.763, 7.622, 9.744, and 12.428 (dimension 6).
- For the same parameters with \(k=3\), the ten eigenvalues are 3.930, 6.097, 8.237, 8.577, 10.704, 12.108, 13.302, 13.718, 16.142, and 19.184 (dimension 10).

## Context

This work extends the exact solvability of the reduced BCS pairing model, originally solved by Richardson (1963) and later via the Gaudin algebraic Bethe ansatz, by providing a direct boson mapping that yields a Bose-Hubbard model. It contrasts with earlier boson expansion methods (e.g., the Dyson mapping and iterative boson expansion approaches) by being exact rather than approximate, though it shares their non-Hermiticity and need for projection onto a physical subspace. The result enables a direct link between mean-field-plus-pairing models and the interacting boson model of nuclei (Iachello and Arima, 1987), with angular momentum projection to be carried out in future work.
