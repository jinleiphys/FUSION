# Finite Nuclei in the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) Model

> We report the first use of the effective QMC energy density functional (EDF), derived from a quark model of hadron structure, to study a broad range of ground state properties of even-even nuclei across the periodic table in the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock+BCS framework. The novelty of the QMC model is that the nuclear medium effects are treated through modification of the internal structure of the nucleon. The density dependence is microscopically derived and the spin-orbit term arises naturally. The QMC EDF depends on a single set of four adjustable parameters having clear physical basis. When applied to diverse ground state data the QMC EDF already produces, in its present simple form, overall agreement with experiment of a quality comparable to a representative Skyrme EDF. There exist however multiple Skyrme paramater sets, frequently tailored to describe selected nuclear phenomena. The QMC EDF set of fewer parameters, as derived in this work, is not open to such variation, chosen set being applied, without adjustment, to both the properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter.

# Finite Nuclei in the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) Model

## Key claim
The QMC energy density functional (EDF), derived from a quark model of hadron structure and containing only four adjustable parameters, predicts ground state properties of even-even nuclei across the periodic table with overall agreement comparable to a representative Skyrme EDF, but the model's incompressibility K0 = 340 ± 3 MeV is considerably higher than the 220–240 MeV frequently adopted in non-relativistic calculations, though recent analysis suggests limits of 250 < K0 < 315 MeV.

## Method
The QMC EDF was implemented in a non-relativistic Hartree-Fock+BCS framework using the skyax code, which allows for axially symmetric and reflection-asymmetric shapes. The four adjustable parameters (meson-nucleon couplings Gσ, Gω, Gρ and the σ meson mass mσ) were first constrained by properties of symmetric infinite nuclear matter at saturation, then further refined by fitting to selected ground state properties of specific nuclei including binding energies, charge radii, surface thickness, pairing gaps, and single-particle energies. The MIT bag radius was fixed at 1 fm and meson masses and magnetic moments were taken at their physical values.

## Key numbers
- Best fit parameters: Gσ = 11.85 ± 0.02 fm², Gω = 8.27 ± 0.02 fm², Gρ = 7.68 ± 0.03 fm², mσ = 722 ± 1 MeV
- Nuclear matter saturation properties from fit: E0 = -16.0 ± 0.2 MeV, ρ0 = 0.153 ± 0.003 fm⁻³, S0 = 30 MeV (fixed), L = 23 ± 4 MeV, K0 = 340 ± 3 MeV
- RMS deviation for binding energies: 0.36% for QMC EDF compared to 0.24% for Skyrme SV-min EDF
- Absolute RMS deviation for 15 super-heavy nuclei: 1.97 MeV for QMC versus 6.17 MeV for SV-min EDF
- Experimental 2⁺₁ state energies in ²⁴⁸–²⁵⁶Fm, ²⁵⁴No and ²⁵⁶Rf lie in range 44–48 keV, with R = E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) between 3.24–3.52
- Experimental β₂ values for neighbouring U-Pu-Cm-Cf region: 0.27–0.30

## Context
This work extends the QMC model, originally developed by Guichon (1987) and collaborators, from applications to nuclear matter to finite nuclei across the periodic table. It contrasts with the Skyrme EDF approach pioneered by Vautherin and Brink (1971), which typically requires ~10–23 coupling constants and has produced many parameter sets often tailored to specific phenomena. The QMC EDF introduces fundamentally new physics by modeling nuclear medium effects through modification of the internal structure of the nucleon, with the density dependence microscopically derived and the spin-orbit term arising naturally. This work enables future inclusion of explicit pion exchange to reduce the incompressibility, and application to nuclei near the limits of stability and potential islands of stability at very large mass number.
