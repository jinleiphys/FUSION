# Coexistence of phases in asymmetric nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields

> The equation of state of nuclear matter is strongly affected by the presence of a magnetic field. Here we study the equilibrium configuration of asymmetric nuclear matter for a wide range of densities, isospin composition, temperatures and magnetic fields. Special attention is paid to the low density and low temperature domain, where a thermodynamical instability exists. Neglecting fluctuations of the Coulomb force, a coexistence of phases is found under such conditions, even for extreme magnetic intensities. We describe the nuclear interaction by using the non--relativistic Skyrme potential model within a Hartree--Fock approach. We found that the coexistence of phases modifies the equilibrium configuration, masking most of the manifestations of the spin polarized matter. However, the compressibility and the magnetic susceptibility show clear signals of this fact. Thermal effects are significative for both quantities, mainly out of the coexistence region.

## Key claim

The coexistence of phases (liquid-gas phase transition) in asymmetric nuclear matter persists even under extreme magnetic fields up to 10^19 G, but this phase coexistence masks most manifestations of spin-polarized matter; only the compressibility and magnetic susceptibility show clear signals of the phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the non-relativistic Skyrme potential model (SLy4 parametrization) within a Hartree-Fock approximation to describe nuclear matter under a uniform external magnetic field. They applied the Gibbs construction for phase coexistence, neglecting Coulomb force fluctuations, to study equilibrium configurations across a range of densities, isospin asymmetries, temperatures (T < 10 MeV), and magnetic fields (up to B = 10^19 G). The spin polarization, isothermal compressibility, and magnetic susceptibility were computed analytically.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of the SLy4 parametrization: n0 = 0.159 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: EB = -15.97 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: K0 = 229.9 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: ES = 32 MeV
- In-medium nucleon mass at saturation: m*/m = 0.694
- Magnetic fields studied: B = 10^18 G and B = 10^19 G (extreme value)
- Temperature range: T < 10 MeV, with specific results shown for T = 1, 5, and 10 MeV
- Densities studied: up to twice the saturation density (n/n0 up to ~2)
- Isospin asymmetries studied: w = 0, 0.4, and 0.8
- For B = 10^18 G and T = 10 MeV, the coexistence region disappears for asymmetries above a typical value w0 < 1
- For B = 10^19 G and w = 0.8, no coexistence exists at T = 10 MeV
- For B = 10^19 G, the proton component is completely polarized for all densities and isospin asymmetries studied
- For B = 10^19 G, w = 0, T = 1 MeV, a local maximum in compressibility appears at n/n0 ≈ 1.9

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter under magnetic fields using various models, including covariant field theoretical models (Yue 2006, Sinha 2010, Rabhi 2008, Ryu 2010, Dong 2013, Diener 2008) and non-relativistic models (Garcia 2012, Isayev 2006, Aguirre 2011). It specifically builds on the liquid-gas phase transition studies in nuclear matter (Schramm 2003, Weise 2005) and recent investigations of neutron matter under magnetic fields (Aguirre 2013). The results enable more realistic equations of state for neutron star structure calculations (Lattimer 2001) and for understanding heavy-ion collision dynamics where strong magnetic fields are produced (Kharzeev 2008, Mo 2013, Skokov 2009).
