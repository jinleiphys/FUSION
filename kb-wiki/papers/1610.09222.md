# Inconsistency of the interactions between pseudoscalar, spinor and Rarita-Schwinger fields

> We perform the Dirac quantization of RS fields interacting with a spinor and the first derivative of a pseudoscalar field. We achieve the calculations for two forms of this interaction: first we review the conventional coupling of lowest derivative order, reproducing the well known inconsistencies in its anticommutator algebra. Then, we perform the analysis on the next order term popularly known as spin 3/2 gauge invariant interaction, which is claimed to be free of these inconsistencies. Nevertheless we find that the direct application of the Dirac formalism leads to inconsistencies in complete analogy to the previous case. This is of high relevance in the particle phenomenology field, where these interactions are used to interpret experimental data involving Delta(1232) resonances.

## Key claim

The authors show that the so-called "spin 3/2 gauge invariant" interaction for the Rarita-Schwinger field, which was claimed to be free of consistency problems, in fact suffers from the same positivity and sign-definiteness issues as the conventional lowest-order coupling when analyzed via Dirac quantization, contradicting the conjecture of consistency made in prior work.

## Method

The authors perform Dirac quantization of the Rarita-Schwinger field interacting with a spinor and the first derivative of a pseudoscalar field, using the Dirac bracket formalism generalized to include fermions via Grassmann variables. They analyze two interaction Lagrangians: the conventional lowest-derivative coupling (NEK) and the next-order derivative coupling (P), computing the secondary constraints and their brackets to determine whether the anticommutator algebra remains positive-definite. The analysis is carried out on a classical scalar field background to test the signature of the Hilbert space.

## Key numbers

- For the NEK coupling, the anticommutator of spinor fields is \( [\psi(x), \psi^\dagger(y)]_+ = \hbar \frac{ \delta^3(x-y)}{1-\frac{2g^2}{3m^2}(\nabla\phi)^2} \), which can become negative for sufficiently large gradients.
- For the P coupling, the bracket \( \{ \theta_0(x),\theta_{0^\dagger}(y) \}_{II} = \frac{3im^2}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{2g^2}{3}({\bf \nabla}\phi)^2 \right) \delta^3(x-y) \) can vanish for certain gradient values.
- For the P coupling, the norm of a Rarita-Schwinger state at rest in a constant scalar gradient \( f(x)=Ax_1 \) is proportional to \( 2 \left( 1 + \frac{\frac{1}{2}g^2 A^2}{1-\frac{2}{3}g^2 A^2} \right) \chi^\dagger \chi \), which becomes negative for large enough \( A \).

## Context

This work extends the foundational inconsistency results of Johnson and Sudarshan (1961) and Hagen (1971) for the conventional NEK coupling to the newer derivative coupling proposed by Pascalutsa (1999) and later reformulated by Lenske (2005). The authors show that the gauge invariance argument used to claim consistency of the P coupling is trivial, as the gauge transformation does not transform the scalar and spinor fields. This result is relevant for hadron phenomenology involving Delta(1232) resonances, where both interactions are used to interpret experimental data, and implies there is no basis to prefer the P coupling over the NEK coupling on consistency grounds.
