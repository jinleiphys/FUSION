# Nonperturbative Flow Equations at Finite Temperature

> Nonperturbative Flow Equations within an effective constituent quark model for two quark flavors with Heat-Kernel methods are studied.

# Nonperturbative Flow Equations at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The paper derives nonperturbative renormalization group flow equations for the linear sigma model using a novel heat-kernel infrared cutoff that allows analytical calculation of threshold functions, but the resulting chiral symmetry breaking scale of approximately 800 MeV is somewhat smaller than the experimental hint from deep inelastic scattering around 1 GeV, which the authors note could be improved by including a running Yukawa coupling and wave function renormalizations.

## Method

The authors truncate the effective mesonic potential of the linear sigma model to include only quartic and quadratic couplings, then apply a heat-kernel infrared cutoff prescription to derive evolution equations for two massless quark flavors. They solve these flow equations numerically at zero temperature to obtain reasonable starting values for the coupling constants, then extend the evolution to finite temperature where the ratio of temperature over the infrared scale parameter becomes the relevant variable. The decoupling of massive modes is handled analytically through the threshold functions that arise in the flow equations.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \(k_{\chi SB} \approx 800\) MeV, where the beta-function of the mesonic self-interaction \(\lambda_k\) is continuous and the mass parameter \(m_k\) tends to zero
- Critical temperature for the second order chiral phase transition in the chiral limit: \(T_c \approx 130\) MeV
- Critical exponent for the order parameter: \(\beta = 0.40\), in good agreement with Monte Carlo calculations
- High resolution scale associated with perturbative QCD: \(\Lambda \geq 1.5\) GeV
- Scale where chiral symmetry breaking processes become important: \(\Lambda_{\chi SB} \approx 1.0\) GeV
- Vacuum expectation value at the end of evolution: \(\lim_{k \to 0} \phi_k = f_\pi\)

## Context

This work extends the exact renormalization group approach previously applied to calculate sigma-model parameters (J. W. and collaborators, prior work) by introducing a novel heat-kernel cutoff that enables analytical treatment of threshold functions. The method allows treatment of critical fluctuations near the second order chiral phase transition at finite temperature for two massless quark flavors, where mean field methods or effective mass sub-summations are insufficient. The approach enables analytical insight into the decoupling of massive modes and connects to experimental observations of chiral symmetry restoration around \(Q^2 \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\) in deep inelastic scattering structure functions.
