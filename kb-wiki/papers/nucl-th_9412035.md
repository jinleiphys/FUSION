# Production of Strange Clusters and Strange Matter in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at the AGS

> Production probabilities for strange clusters and strange matter in Au+Au collisions at AGS energy are obtained in the thermal fireball model. The only parameters of the model, the baryon chemical potential and temperature, were determined from a description of the rather complete set of hadron yields from Si+nucleus collisions at the AGS. For the production of light nuclear fragments and strange clusters the results are similar to recent coalescence model calculations. Strange matter production with baryon number larger than 10 is predicted to be much smaller than any current experimental sensitivities.

## Key claim

The thermal fireball model, assuming full chemical equilibrium, predicts that strange matter clusters with baryon number larger than 10 are produced in central Au+Au collisions at AGS energy at rates many orders of magnitude below any current experimental sensitivity, but this prediction carries substantial uncertainty from the unknown strangelet mass.

## Method

The authors used a thermal fireball model with baryon chemical potential μb = 0.54 GeV and temperature range T = 0.12–0.14 GeV, previously fitted to hadron yields from Si+nucleus collisions at 14.5 GeV/c per nucleon. They computed particle number densities from momentum integrals with finite excluded volume corrections, fixing the strangeness chemical potential μs by strangeness conservation. For strange matter clusters, they applied the Berger and Jaffe mass formula with Madsen's curvature and surface corrections to determine the most stable charge and strangeness for each baryon number, then calculated production probabilities per central Au+Au collision using a freeze-out volume normalized to a total pion multiplicity of 450.

## Key numbers

- Baryon chemical potential μb = 0.54 GeV and temperature range T = 0.12–0.14 GeV, determined from Si+nucleus data
- Strangeness chemical potential μs = 0.108 GeV at T = 0.12 GeV and μs = 0.135 GeV at T = 0.14 GeV
- Freeze-out volume: 2600 fm³ at T = 0.14 GeV and 5000 fm³ at T = 0.12 GeV, normalized to total pion multiplicity of 450 in central Au+Au
- Production probability for the strangelet ¹⁰₁St⁻⁸ (baryon number 10): 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁴ per central Au+Au at T = 0.12 GeV and 7.3 × 10⁻¹³ at T = 0.14 GeV
- Production probability for the strangelet ¹²₁St⁻⁹ (baryon number 12): 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁷ at T = 0.12 GeV and 1.7 × 10⁻¹⁵ at T = 0.14 GeV
- Production probability for ordinary ¹²C at T = 0.14 GeV: 5.4 × 10⁻¹¹ per central Au+Au
- Penalty factor for each increase in baryon number by one: approximately 50 at T = 0.14 GeV and 70 at T = 0.12 GeV
- Even the most optimistic strangelet mass scenario (bulk binding 0.88 GeV) raises the ¹²₁St⁻⁹ probability only to 2.7 × 10⁻¹³

## Context

This work extends the thermal equilibration model of Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, Wessels, and Xu (1994) from Si+nucleus to Au+Au collisions at AGS energy, and finds results for light nuclear fragments and strange clusters similar to the coalescence model calculations of Baltz, Dover, Kahana, Pang, Schlagel, and Schnedermann (1994). The predictions for strange matter production are many orders of magnitude smaller than the quark-matter production scenario of Crawford, Desai, and Shaw (1992), and the authors suggest that strangeness distillation models such as Greiner, Koch, and Stöcker (1987, 1988) should be tested against existing strange hadron data before being invoked to enhance strangelet yields.
