# Charmed-strange Meson Spectrum: Old and New Problems

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently reported the observation for the first time of a spin-$3$ resonance in the heavy quark sector. They have shown that the $\bar{D}^{0}K^{-}$ structure seen in the $B_{s}^{0}\to \bar{D}^{0}K^{-}π^{+}$ reaction and with invariant mass $2.86\,{\rm GeV}$ is an admixture of a spin-$1$ and a spin-$3$ resonances. Motivated by the good agreement between our theoretical predictions some time ago and the properties extracted from the experiment of the $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2860)$ and $D_{s3}^{\ast}(2860)$ states, we perform an extension of the study of the strong decay properties of the $D_{sJ}^{\ast}(2860)$ and present the same analysis for the $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$ and $D_{sJ}(3040)$ mesons. This provides a unified and simultaneous description of the three higher excited charmed-strange resonances observed until now. For completeness, we present theoretical results for masses and strong decays of the low-lying charmed-strange mesons and those experimental missing states which belong to the spin-multiplets of the discovered $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$, $D_{sJ}^{\ast}(2860)$ and $D_{sJ}(3040)$ resonances. The theoretical framework used is a constituent quark model which successfully describes hadron phenomenology from light to heavy quark sectors.

## Key claim

The LHCb Collaboration's observation of a spin-3 resonance in the heavy quark sector confirms the authors' earlier theoretical predictions that the 2.86 GeV structure in the $\bar{D}^{0}K^{-}$ invariant mass is an admixture of a spin-1 and a spin-3 resonance, motivating an extended unified study of strong decay properties for the three higher excited charmed-strange resonances.

## Method

The authors employed a constituent quark model (CQM) with a screened linear confinement potential and one-gluon exchange (OGE) including one-loop corrections, solving the Schrodinger equation via the Gaussian Expansion Method to obtain meson masses and wave functions. Strong decay widths were calculated using the $^{3}P_{0}$ decay model with a scale-dependent strength parameter $\gamma(\mu)$ determined from a global fit to meson decay widths across light to heavy quark sectors. Partial decay widths into all open-flavor channels were computed for the $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$, $D_{sJ}^{\ast}(2860)$, and $D_{sJ}(3040)$ resonances, as well as for low-lying states and experimentally missing spin-multiplet partners.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2860)$: $(2859 \pm 12 \pm 6 \pm 23)$ MeV; width: $(159 \pm 23 \pm 27 \pm 72)$ MeV (LHCb experimental values)
- Mass of $D_{s3}^{\ast}(2860)$: $(2860.5 \pm 2.6 \pm 2.5 \pm 6.0)$ MeV; width: $(53 \pm 7 \pm 4 \pm 6)$ MeV (LHCb experimental values)
- Predicted total decay width for $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2860)$ as $3\,1^{-}$ state: $153.20$ MeV, compared to experimental $159 \pm 23 \pm 27 \pm 72$ MeV
- Predicted total decay width for $D_{s3}^{\ast}(2860)$ as $1\,3^{-}$ state: $85.12$ MeV, compared to experimental $53 \pm 7 \pm 4 \pm 6$ MeV
- Predicted total decay width for $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$ as $2\,1^{-}$ state: $170.75$ MeV, compared to experimental $125 \pm 30$ MeV
- Predicted mass of $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$ as $2^{3}S_{1}$ state: $2.79$ GeV
- Predicted total decay width for $D_{s2}^{\ast}(2573)$: $18.67$ MeV, compared to LHCb value $16.9 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.4$ MeV
- Predicted total decay width for $D_{s1}(2536)$: $0.99$ MeV, compared to experimental $0.92 \pm 0.05$ MeV
- Branching ratio $D^{\ast}K/DK$ for $D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$: $2.6$, compared to BaBar measurement $0.91 \pm 0.13 \pm 0.12$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Segovia 2012) which predicted the $1^{3}D_{1}$ and $1^{3}D_{3}$ resonances at 2.86 GeV with widths 153 MeV and 85 MeV respectively, now confirmed by LHCb (Aaij 2014). It provides a unified description of the three higher excited charmed-strange resonances ($D_{s1}^{\ast}(2700)$, $D_{sJ}^{\ast}(2860)$, $D_{sJ}(3040)$) within the same constituent quark model framework (Vijande 2004) that successfully describes hadron phenomenology from light to heavy quark sectors. The results enable experimentalists to search for missing spin-multiplet states and clarify the nature of states like the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$, which may require non-$q\bar{q}$ contributions as previously studied by the authors (Segovia 2009).
