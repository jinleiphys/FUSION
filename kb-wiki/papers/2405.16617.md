# Four-nucleon clustering near the QCD critical point: theory versus experiment

> This letter relates theoretical predictions made previously with recently announced RHIC BES-II experimental data on multi-baryon fluctuations. We conclude that new statistically significant effect is observed, and its features are in good agreement with predictions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a statistically significant suppression of kurtosis (C4/C2 ratio) observed in RHIC BES-II data is in good agreement with the author's prior theoretical predictions, but with the crucial hedge that the observed effect is a *suppression* rather than the enhancement anticipated in earlier works, and that this sign reversal arises from repulsive many-body forces near the QCD critical point.

## Method

The author used classical molecular dynamics, a semiclassical "flucton" method, and direct Path Integral Monte Carlo simulations with standard nuclear forces and versions modified near the QCD critical point, focusing on four-nucleon systems. The temperature dependence of quadratic, cubic, and quartic couplings was derived from lattice results for critical fluctuations and from analytic results for the effective potential from the Heidelberg group's Wetterich functional equation. These predictions were then compared to new STAR BES-II data on event-by-event fluctuations of net-proton multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- The dip in kurtosis deviation is located near collision energy of approximately 20 GeV per nucleon.
- The suppression is predicted to occur in a narrow vicinity of the critical point, at reduced temperature |t| < 1/10, where t = (T - Tc)/Tc.
- The four-nucleon preclustering probability scales as exp(-ΔF/T), where ΔF changes sign and becomes strongly repulsive as t approaches zero.
- The four-nucleon clusters decay into approximately 50 bound states of 4He with small binding energies.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior sequence of papers (Shuryak 2018, Shuryak 2019, DeMartini 2020) that predicted four-nucleon clustering effects near the QCD critical point, and it directly compares those predictions to new STAR BES-II data presented at the CPOD 2024 conference. The paper contradicts earlier expectations of enhanced fluctuations near the critical point (Stephanov 1998) by showing that repulsive many-body forces from cubic and quartic couplings dominate near the critical point, producing suppression rather than enhancement. This enables future work on fitting the Ising model parameters to the kurtosis data, measuring the three components of the tritium production ratio separately, and searching for low-binding excited states of 4He via t+p or d+d resonances.
