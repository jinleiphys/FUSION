# Chiral Magnetic Spiral

> We argue that the presence of a very strong magnetic field in the chirally broken phase induces inhomogeneous expectation values, of a spiral nature along the magnetic field axis, for the currents of charge and chirality, when there is finite baryon density or an imbalance between left and right chiralities. This "chiral magnetic spiral" is a gapless excitation transporting the currents of (i) charge (at finite chirality), and (ii) chirality (at finite baryon density) along the direction of the magnetic field. In both cases it also induces in the transverse directions oscillating currents of charge and chirality. In heavy ion collisions, the chiral magnetic spiral possibly provides contributions both to the out-of-plane and the in-plane dynamical charge fluctuations recently observed at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the chirally broken phase of QCD under a strong magnetic field, the presence of finite baryon density or chiral imbalance induces spatially inhomogeneous, spiral-shaped expectation values for the transverse components of both the charge and axial currents, not just the longitudinal currents previously associated with the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). This "chiral magnetic spiral" is a gapless excitation, and its existence depends on the dimensional reduction to 1+1 dimensions caused by the strong magnetic field and the resulting "chiral spiral" phase known from lower-dimensional models.

## Method

The authors use a dimensional reduction approach, projecting the 3+1 dimensional Dirac theory onto the lowest Landau level (LLL) in a strong, uniform magnetic field. They analyze the fermion dispersion relations in the presence of a chiral chemical potential (mu_5) or a regular chemical potential (mu), and compute the expectation values of the charge and axial current components by pairing particles and holes at the Fermi surface. The resulting spatial modulations are derived from the phase shifts induced by the chemical potentials in the 1+1 dimensional effective theory, drawing an analogy to the "chiral spiral" condensate found in the chiral Gross-Neveu model.

## Key numbers

*   The strong magnetic field strength is estimated as \( eB \sim m_{\pi}^2 \), based on prior work by Kharzeev (2007) and Skokov (2009).
*   The longitudinal charge current for nonzero chiral chemical potential is \(\langle J^3\rangle = \frac{eB}{2\pi}\frac{e \mu_5}{\pi}\), and the axial charge density is \(\langle J_5^0\rangle = \frac{eB}{2\pi}\frac{e \mu_5}{\pi}\), consistent with prior CME calculations.
*   For nonzero baryon chemical potential, the longitudinal axial current is \(\langle J_5^3\rangle = \frac{eB}{2\pi}\frac{e \mu}{\pi}\), as found by Son (2004) and Gorbar (2009).
*   The transverse components of the charge and axial currents oscillate with a wavenumber of \(2\mu_5\) (for nonzero chiral chemical potential) or \(2\mu\) (for nonzero baryon chemical potential), as shown in equations (currentsmu5) and (currentsmu).

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), previously described by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and Fukushima (2008), and the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) described by Son (2004) and Metlitski (2005), by predicting new oscillatory transverse currents in the chirally broken phase. It builds on the concept of the "chiral spiral" phase found in 1+1 dimensional models like the chiral Gross-Neveu model (Schon, 2000; Basar, 2009) and the 't Hooft model (Bringoltz, 2009), and applies it to 3+1 dimensions via magnetic catalysis. The results suggest that in heavy-ion collisions, the chiral magnetic spiral could contribute to both out-of-plane and in-plane charge fluctuations, potentially explaining recent observations by the STAR collaboration at RHIC.
