# The entropy of a correlated system of nucleons

> Realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction induce correlations to the nuclear many-body system which lead to a fragmentation of the single-particle strength over a wide range of energies and momenta. We address the question of how this fragmentation affects the thermodynamical properties of nuclear matter. In particular, we show that the entropy can be computed with the help of a spectral function which can be evaluated in terms of the self-energy obtained in the Self-Consistent Green's Function approach. Results for the density and temperature dependences of the entropy per particle for symmetric nuclear matter are presented and compared to the results of lowest order finite temperature Brueckner--Hartree--Fock calculations. The effects of correlations on the calculated entropy are small, if the appropriate quasi-particle approximation is used. The results demonstrate the thermodynamical consistency of the self-consistent T-matrix approximation for the evaluation of the Green's functions.

## Key claim

The entropy of correlated symmetric nuclear matter can be accurately computed using only the dynamical quasi-particle (DQ) entropy term \(S^{DQ}\), which is derived from the single-particle spectral function, provided that the contribution from higher-order correlations \(S'\) is negligible; the paper demonstrates that this approximation yields thermodynamically consistent results for the density and temperature range explored, as long as short-range correlations are considered.

## Method

The authors solve the Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) approach within the ladder (T-matrix) approximation at finite temperature, using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The single-particle spectral function is obtained from the self-energy via Dyson's equation, and the entropy is computed from the Luttinger-Ward formalism, retaining only the dynamical quasi-particle term \(S^{DQ}\) which includes finite-width effects through the function \(\Xi(k,\omega)\). Results are compared to those from a finite-temperature Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) approach, where the entropy is taken in a mean-field quasi-particle form.

## Key numbers

* Entropy per particle for symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (\(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)) and \(T = 10\ \text{MeV}\) is approximately \(S/A \approx 1.5\ k_B\) from the SCGF calculation, while the BHF quasi-particle approximation gives a similar value, showing small correlation effects.
* At a density of \(\rho = 0.32\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and \(T = 20\ \text{MeV}\), the SCGF entropy per particle is about \(S/A \approx 2.0\ k_B\), with the BHF result differing by less than 5%.
* The quasi-particle approximation to the SCGF spectral function yields an entropy that agrees with the full SCGF result to within 0.1 \(k_B\) per particle across the studied density and temperature range (up to \(T = 20\ \text{MeV}\) and densities up to \(0.48\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature SCGF approach for symmetric nuclear matter, previously developed by Frick et al. (2005), to compute thermodynamic properties via the Luttinger-Ward formalism. It contrasts with the finite-temperature BHF approach of Lejeune et al. (1986) and Bombaci et al. (1994), which neglects hole-hole propagation and uses a mean-field entropy expression. The results enable reliable entropy calculations for astrophysical applications, such as core-collapse supernova simulations where entropy per baryon is a key dynamical variable, and they validate the use of the simpler quasi-particle approximation for the entropy in correlated nuclear matter.
