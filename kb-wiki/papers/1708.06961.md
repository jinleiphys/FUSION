# Disentangling the role of the $Y(4260)$ in $e^+e^-\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ via line shape studies

> Whether the $Y(4260)$ can couple to open charm channels has been a crucial issue for understanding its nature. The available experimental data suggest that the cross section line shapes of exclusive processes in $e^+e^-$ annihilations have nontrivial structures around the mass region of the $Y(4260)$. As part of a series of studies of the $Y(4260)$ as mainly a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ molecular state, we show that the partial widths of the $Y(4260)$ to the two-body open charm channels of $e^+e^-\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ are much smaller than that to $\bar{D}D^*π+c.c.$. The line shapes measured by the Belle Collaboration for these two channels can be well described by the vector charmonium states $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$ and $ψ(4415)$ together with the $Y(4260)$. It turns out that the interference of the $Y(4260)$ with the other charmonia produces a dip around 4.22~GeV in the $e^+e^-\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ cross section line shape. The data also show an evidence for the strong coupling of the $Y(4260)$ to the $D\bar D_1(2420)$, in line with the expectation in the hadronic molecular scenario for the $Y(4260)$.

## Key claim

The $Y(4260)$, treated as a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ hadronic molecule, has a partial width to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ that is much smaller than its dominant decay to $\bar{D}D^*\pi+c.c.$, and its interference with conventional charmonia produces a dip around 4.22 GeV in the $e^+e^-\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ cross section line shape, consistent with the molecular scenario.

## Method

The authors calculated the partial decay width of $Y(4260)\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ using non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) with one-pion exchange loop diagrams involving intermediate $D_1(2420)$, $D$, and $\pi$ mesons, regularizing ultraviolet divergences with a monopole form factor. They then performed a combined fit to Belle cross section data for $e^+e^-\to D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ from threshold to about 4.6 GeV, including contributions from the conventional vector charmonia $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, $\psi(4415)$ and the $Y(4260)$ as a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)$ molecule, with parameters determined by minimizing $\chi^2$.

## Key numbers

- The effective coupling for $Y(4260)$ to $\bar{D}D_1(2420)$ is $y^{\text{eff}} = (3.94 \pm 0.04)\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$, determined from combined analysis of $e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi$, $h_c\pi\pi$, and $\bar{D}D^*\pi+c.c.$
- The loop contribution to the partial width for $Y(4260)\to D^{*+}D^{*-}$ is $\Gamma^{\text{Loop}} = (1.27 \pm 0.68)$ MeV, while the tree-level (short-distance core) contribution is $\Gamma^{\text{Tree}} = (9.50 \pm 7.18)$ MeV, giving a total $\Gamma_{D^*\bar{D}^*} = (10.77 \pm 7.86)$ MeV for the $Y(4260)$.
- The partial widths for $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$ to $D^*\bar{D}^*$ are $(10.22 \pm 1.47)$ MeV, $(4.74 \pm 11.73)$ MeV, and $(8.52 \pm 5.93)$ MeV, respectively.
- The fitted cutoff parameter $\alpha = 1.01 \pm 0.12$ (with $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 220$ MeV) and $\Lambda_\pi = (409.1 \pm 23.9)$ MeV.
- The fit yields $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f} = 1.53$ for the combined data sets.

## Context

This work extends a series of studies treating the $Y(4260)$ as a $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ hadronic molecule (Cleven 2013, Qin 2016), which previously explained the line shapes of $e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi$, $h_c\pi\pi$, and $\bar{D}D^*\pi+c.c.$. It contrasts with the analysis of Du (2016) which considered only $P$-wave coupled-channel effects from ground-state charmed mesons and $\psi(4040)$ without the $Y(4260)$. The results enable future studies of the $Y(4260)$'s role in other open charm channels and motivate more precise measurements of $e^+e^-\to D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ to better constrain the molecular picture.
