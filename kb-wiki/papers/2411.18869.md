# Quantum computing of chirality imbalance in SU(2) gauge theory

> We implement a variational quantum algorithm to investigate the chiral condensate in a 1+1 dimensional SU(2) non-Abelian gauge theory. The algorithm is evaluated using a proposed Monte Carlo sampling method, which allows the extension to large qubit systems. The obtained results through quantum simulations on classical and actual quantum hardware are in good agreement with exact diagonalization of the lattice Hamiltonian, revealing the phenomena of chiral symmetry breaking and restoration as functions of both temperature and chemical potential. Our findings underscore the potential of near-term quantum computing for exploring QCD systems at finite temperature and density in non-Abelian gauge theories.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a variational quantum algorithm with Monte Carlo sampling can compute the chiral condensate in a 1+1 dimensional SU(2) non-Abelian gauge theory at finite temperature and chemical potential, with results matching exact diagonalization; however, the chiral condensate only vanishes at infinite temperature, meaning no genuine phase transition occurs in this small system.

## Method

The authors map the 1+1D SU(2) lattice Hamiltonian with staggered fermions to a purely fermionic form by eliminating gauge fields via a local gauge transformation and Gauss's law, then encode the system into 8 or 12 qubits using the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They prepare the Gibbs state by minimizing the free energy with a Quantum Alternating Operator Ansatz (QAOA) parameterized unitary, where the optimization is performed once and reused across temperatures, and they compute thermal averages of the chiral condensate using Monte Carlo sampling of eigenstates. The algorithm is executed on classical simulators using the QuSpin package and on IBM's ibm_sherbrooke quantum hardware with 5000 shots per measurement.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate at T→0 converges to a fixed value of -4 for all m/g values (system of 8 qubits with two spatial points, maximum expected value).
- For the 8-qubit system, M=20 randomly selected states per optimization step suffice to represent the Gibbs state density matrix (compared to the full 256 eigenstates).
- Monte Carlo sampling uses 1000 sampled states for the 8-qubit system and 1000 or 2000 states for the 12-qubit system.
- Results are shown for m/g values in {5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30} and chemical potentials μ/g in {4, 6, 8, 10, 20, 30, 40} at m/g=5.
- Quantum hardware measurements use 5000 shots per measurement on the ibm_sherbrooke device.

## Context

This work extends variational quantum simulations of chiral dynamics from the 1+1D Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Czajka, 2021), the SYK model (Araz, 2024), and the Schwinger model (Ikeda, 2024) to a non-Abelian SU(2) gauge theory, which shares features with QCD such as spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and confinement. The approach enables studies at finite chemical potential where classical lattice Monte Carlo methods suffer from the sign problem, and the Monte Carlo sampling strategy is designed to scale to larger qubit systems where enumerating all eigenstates becomes intractable.
