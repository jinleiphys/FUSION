# Impact of the pre-equilibrium phase for the determination of nuclear geometry in high-energy isobar collisions

> Ultrarelativistic isobar collisions have been proposed as a useful tool to investigate nuclear structure. These systems are not created in equilibrium, rather undergo a pre-thermalization stage. In this stage, some of the initial structure information may be lost and additional effects introduced. The objective of this paper is to study this possibility in the extreme case of a "free-streaming" pre-equilibrium stage. We do this by computing estimators for ratios of various measured (or measurable) quantities (elliptic and triangular flows, mean transverse momentum and associated cumulants, correlators between elliptic or triangular flows and mean transverse momentum, symmetric cumulant and two-plane correlator) and study their sensitivity to the duration of the free-streaming stage. We find that the correlators between elliptic or triangular flows and mean transverse momentum, the so-called $ρ_2$ and $ρ_3$, are indeed sensitive to the duration of the free-streaming stage and that the normalized symmetric cumulant, $\varepsilon NSC(2,3)$ might also depend on this duration.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the pre-equilibrium free-streaming stage can significantly affect certain observables used to infer nuclear geometry in isobar collisions, specifically the Pearson correlators ρ₂ and ρ₃ between anisotropic flow and mean transverse momentum, and possibly the normalized symmetric cumulant εNSC(2,3); however, ratios of eccentricities themselves (ε₂, ε₃, ε₄) and the root-mean-square radius show minimal sensitivity to the free-streaming duration, with changes of at most 1% for central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTo model to generate initial conditions for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, sampling six distinct nuclear configurations with systematically varied parameters (R₀, a, β₂, β₃, γ) to interpolate between typical Ru and Zr shapes. They applied a free-streaming pre-equilibrium stage with three different durations (τFS = 0.4 fm/c, τFS = 1.0 fm/c, and a centrality-dependent case varying between approximately 1.35 and 1.49 fm/c) and computed estimators for spatial eccentricities (ε₂, ε₃, ε₄), root-mean-square radius, the normalized symmetric cumulant εNSC(2,3), and the Pearson correlators ρ₂ and ρ₃, comparing ratios between different nuclear configurations to isolate the effect of free-streaming.

## Key numbers

- For ε₂ ratios between configurations 1-5 and configuration 6, free-streaming changes the ratios by at most 1%, with less effect in central collisions
- For ε₃ ratios, free-streaming produces a splitting of almost 1% between different τFS values for configurations 1 and 2 in central collisions
- The radius varies at most 2% across configurations, with the effect being smaller for central collisions
- For ρ₂, the splitting between triaxial (configuration 1) and spheroidal (configuration 2) cases increases from approximately 0.3 at initial condition level to approximately 0.5 when considering the centrality-dependent free-streaming case
- The free-streaming time values used were: τFS = 0.4 fm/c, τFS = 1.0 fm/c, and a centrality-dependent case with τR = 1.46 fm/c, ε̄R = 4.0 GeV/fm², α = 0.031, giving τFS between approximately 1.35 and 1.49 fm/c
- For ε₄, the diffuseness parameter dependence is 3% for central collisions when going from configuration 4 to configuration 5

## Context

This work extends the study of isobar collisions as a tool for nuclear structure investigation, following experimental measurements by the STAR Collaboration (2021) showing that Ru+Ru collisions produce up to 10% more particles, 3% larger elliptic flow, and 7% smaller triangular flow compared to Zr+Zr collisions. The paper systematically tests the assumption, common in previous studies such as Jia et al. (2021) and Zhang et al. (2021), that ratios of observables in isobar collisions are insensitive to the pre-equilibrium stage; it finds that while eccentricity ratios are robust, the ρ₂ and ρ₃ correlators identified by Bally et al. (2021) and the ATLAS Collaboration (2022) as sensitive to triaxiality are indeed affected by free-streaming duration, which must be accounted for in precise nuclear geometry determinations.
