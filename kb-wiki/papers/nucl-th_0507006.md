# Spectroscopy of Medium to Heavy Single $Λ$-Hypernuclei

> We develop a method for calculating the doublet splittings of select ground-state single $Λ$-hypernuclei. This hypernuclear spectroscopy is conducted by supplementing the self-consistent single-particle equations with an effective interaction, which follows directly from the underlying lagrangian,to simulate the residual particle-hole interaction. Our previous investigation, performed using only the leading-order contributions to the particle-hole interaction, was inadequate. In the present work, this method is improved upon by increasing the level of truncation in the residual interaction to include gradient coupling to the neutral vector meson, and thereby incorporating the tensor force into the calculation (which is known to play a crucial role in these systems). As a result, we obtain a realistic description of the effect of the tensor coupling on the doublet orderings and splittings.

## Key claim

By including gradient couplings to the neutral vector meson in the residual particle-hole interaction, the author obtains a realistic description of the tensor coupling effect on doublet orderings and splittings in medium to heavy single Λ-hypernuclei, whereas the previous leading-order calculation (McIntire 2004) was inadequate.

## Method

The author supplements self-consistent Kohn-Sham single-particle equations with a Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) analysis of particle-hole states, using an effective ΛN interaction derived from meson-exchange potentials. The interaction includes scalar and neutral vector meson exchange, with higher-order gradient couplings (tensor couplings) added to incorporate the tensor force. Particle-hole matrix elements are computed from Dirac wave functions obtained by solving the radial Kohn-Sham equations, and the doublet splitting is taken as the difference between the two available J states.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state splitting for ¹⁶ΛO: 22 keV (with proper level ordering, matching the experimental value of +26 keV from Tamura (2005) and Ukai (2005))
- Ground-state splitting for ¹²ΛB: -2011 keV (experimental upper bound ≤ 140 keV from Juric (1973))
- Ground-state splitting for ³²ΛS: -2260 keV (experimental upper bound ≤ 1000 keV from Bertini (1979))
- Ground-state splitting for ²⁰⁸ΛPb: -104 keV (experimental upper bound ≤ 2000 keV from Hasegawa (1996))
- Ground-state splitting for ⁴⁰ΛCa: +107 keV (experimental upper bound ≤ 2200 keV from Pile (1991))
- Ground-state splitting for ²⁸ΛSi: -1011 keV (experimental upper bound ≤ 400 keV from Hasegawa (1996))

## Context

This work extends the author's previous leading-order calculation (McIntire 2004) by including tensor couplings that were known to be crucial from shell model calculations by Millener (1985, 2005). The method is applied to medium-to-heavy hypernuclei where experimental constraints come primarily from (π⁺,K⁺) reactions (Pile 1991, Bertini 1979, Hasegawa 1996), while recent γ-ray and (e,e'K⁺) experiments (Tamura 2005, Ukai 2005, Urciuoli 2001, Miyoshi 2003) have measured lighter systems. The approach enables future calculations of excited states and multiplets for this class of nuclei, though the author notes that some calculated splittings (e.g., ³²ΛS) remain well outside experimental error bars, suggesting short-distance physics corrections are needed.
