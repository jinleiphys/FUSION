# Efimov Physics in Cold Atoms

> Atoms with a large scattering length have universal low-energy properties that do not depend on the details of their structure or their interactions at short distances. In the 2-atom sector, the universal properties are familiar and depend only on the scattering length. In the 3-atom sector for identical bosons, the universal properties include the existence of a sequence of shallow triatomic molecules called Efimov trimers and log-periodic dependence of scattering observables on the energy and the scattering length. In this review, we summarize the universal results that are currently known. We also summarize the experimental information that is currently available with an emphasis on 3-atom loss processes.

## Key claim

The paper reviews universal low-energy properties of atoms with large scattering length, emphasizing that in the three-body sector for identical bosons, the existence of a sequence of shallow triatomic molecules called Efimov trimers and the log-periodic dependence of scattering observables on energy and scattering length are robust predictions that do not depend on the details of short-range interactions, but the experimental confirmation of Efimov states has remained challenging and only indirect evidence existed as of 2006.

## Method

The authors present a theoretical review using the scaling limit (zero-range limit) where the S-wave phase shift takes the simple form \(k \cot \delta_0(k) = -1/a\) and all higher partial wave phase shifts vanish, treating deviations from this limit as perturbations. They formulate the three-body problem using hyperspherical coordinates and Faddeev equations, employing an adiabatic hyperspherical approximation to derive channel potentials for the hyperradial variable. The universal results are derived by analyzing the discrete scaling symmetry that emerges in the three-body system when the scattering length is large compared to the natural low-energy length scale.

## Key numbers

* For identical bosons in the resonant limit \(a \to \pm \infty\), the asymptotic ratio of binding energies of successive Efimov states is \(E_T^{(n+1)}/E_T^{(n)} \to 1/515.03\) as \(n \to +\infty\)
* For \(^4\)He atoms, the van der Waals length is \(\ell_{\rm vdW} \approx 10.2 \, a_0\) (where \(a_0 = 0.529177\) Å is the Bohr radius), and the scattering length from potential models is \(a = 189 \, a_0\) with effective range \(r_s = 14 \, a_0\)
* The \(^4\)He dimer binding energy is \(E_2 = 1.31\) mK, much smaller than the natural low-energy scale \(\hbar^2/m \ell_{\rm vdW}^2 \approx 420\) mK
* For polarized \(^3\)H atoms, the spin-triplet scattering length is \(a_t = -82.1 \, a_0\) with van der Waals length \(\ell_{\rm vdW} = 13.7 \, a_0\), and there is a single three-body bound state with binding energy about 4.59 mK
* For \(^{23}\)Na atoms in the \(|1,+1\rangle\) hyperfine state, Feshbach resonances occur near 853 G and 907 G; the \(|1,-1\rangle\) state has a resonance near 1195 G
* For \(^{85}\)Rb atoms in the \(|2,-2\rangle\) hyperfine state, a Feshbach resonance occurs near 155 G

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Efimov (1971, 1979) on universal three-body properties for identical bosons with large scattering length, and summarizes results from the authors' more comprehensive review (Braaten and Hammer, 2006). The paper connects Efimov's theoretical predictions to the emerging field of ultracold atom physics, where Feshbach resonances (first predicted for Cs atoms by Tiesinga, Verhaar, and Stoof in 1993) allow experimental control of scattering lengths. The review enables future experimental searches for Efimov states in ultracold atomic gases, building on the first indirect evidence reported by Kraemer et al. (2006) from enhanced three-body recombination losses in ultracold \(^{133}\)Cs atoms.
