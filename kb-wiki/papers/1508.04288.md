# Vector meson spectral function and dilepton rate in an effective mean field model

> We have studied the vector meson spectral function (VMSF) in a hot and dense medium within an effective QCD model namely the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and its Polyakov Loop extended version (PNJL) with and without the effect of isoscalar vector interaction (IVI). The effect of the IVI has been taken into account using the ring approximation. We obtained the dilepton production rate (DPR) using the VMSF and observed that at moderate temperature it is enhanced in the PNJL model as compared to the NJL and Born rate due to the suppression of color degrees of freedom.

# Vector meson spectral function and dilepton rate in an effective mean field model

## Key claim

The vector meson spectral function and dilepton production rate are enhanced in the PNJL model compared to the NJL model and the Born rate at moderate temperature, but this enhancement is conditional on the suppression of color degrees of freedom by the Polyakov Loop fields; without the isoscalar vector interaction, the NJL spectral functions become quantitatively equal to those of free field theory.

## Method

The authors computed the vector meson spectral function within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov Loop extended version (PNJL), both with and without the isoscalar vector interaction (IVI). The effect of the IVI was included using the ring approximation, and the differential dilepton production rate was obtained from the spectral function via the standard relation involving the fine structure constant and the thermal Bose factor.

## Key numbers

- At T = 200 MeV with zero external momentum and zero chemical potential, the PNJL spectral function has a larger threshold than the NJL one due to larger quark mass; at T = 300 MeV the thresholds become almost equal.
- For T = 300 MeV, quark chemical potential μ = 100 MeV, and external momentum q = 200 MeV, the spectral strength increases with the ratio G_V/G_S (isoscalar vector to scalar coupling strength) for both NJL and PNJL, with PNJL showing greater strength for a given G_V/G_S.
- The dilepton rate at T = 240 MeV with q = 0 and μ = 0 is compared with quenched lattice QCD results from Ding (2010).

## Context

This work extends studies of vector meson spectral functions and dilepton rates in effective QCD models, building on the NJL model framework reviewed by Klevansky (1992) and Buballa (2003). The results are compared with quenched lattice QCD data from Ding (2010) and with perturbative QCD calculations by Braaten (1990), Greiner (2010), and Mustafa (1999). The enhancement of the dilepton rate in the PNJL model suggests that nontrivial color correlations exist in the deconfined phase, and the authors note that their results for non-zero IVI could be tested in future lattice QCD calculations with dynamical fermions.
