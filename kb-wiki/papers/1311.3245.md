# True Muonium $(μ^+ μ^-)$ on the Light Front

> Applying Discretized Light Cone Quantization, we perform the first calculation of the spectrum of true muonium, the $μ^+ μ^-$ atom, as modified by the inclusion of an $|e\bar e >$ Fock component. The shift in the mass eigenvalue is found to be largest for triplet states. If $m_e$ is taken to be a substantial fraction of $m_μ$, the integrated probability of the electronic component of the $1 \, {}^3 /! S_1$ state is found to be as large as $O(10^{-2})$. Initial studies of the Lamb shift for the atom are performed. Directions for making the simulations fully realistic are discussed.

## Key claim

The first calculation of the true muonium ($\mu^+ \mu^-$) spectrum using Discretized Light Cone Quantization (DLCQ) with an additional $|e\bar e\rangle$ Fock component shows that the mass eigenvalue shift is largest for triplet states, but only when the electron mass $m_e$ is taken to be a substantial fraction of the muon mass $m_\mu$ (rather than the physical ratio $m_e/m_\mu \simeq 4.8\cdot 10^{-3}$) and with the unphysically large coupling $\alpha = 0.3$.

## Method

The authors applied Discretized Light Cone Quantization (DLCQ) to solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation for true muonium, truncating the Fock space to include $|\mu\bar\mu\rangle$, $|\mu\bar\mu\gamma\rangle$, $|e\bar e\rangle$, $|e\bar e\gamma\rangle$, and $|\gamma\rangle$ states. They used the method of iterated resolvents to incorporate the effect of higher Fock states as effective interactions, and employed a counterterm method to regularize the Coulomb singularity, with separate counterterms for each flavor sector. The momentum-space integrals were discretized using Clenshaw-Curtis quadrature for the off-shell momentum variable $\mu$ and Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature for the angular variable $\theta$.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state radius of true muonium is 512 fm (stated in the introduction as a Bohr atom property).
- The physical electron-to-muon mass ratio is $m_e/m_\mu \simeq 4.8\cdot 10^{-3}$.
- The coupling constant used in the simulations is $\alpha = 0.3$ (unphysically large, to enhance effects).
- When $m_e$ is taken as a substantial fraction of $m_\mu$, the integrated probability of the electronic component of the $1 \, {}^3 \! S_1$ state is found to be as large as $O(10^{-2})$.
- The leading-order hyperfine splitting for true muonium is $\Delta E = \frac{7}{12} m_\mu \alpha^4$.
- The vacuum polarization energy shift for $n \, {}^3 \! S_1$ states is given by $\Delta E_{\rm VP}(n^3S_1)=\frac{m_\mu\alpha^5}{4\pi n^3}\left[\frac{1}{3}\ln\left(\frac{4m_\mu^2}{m_e^2}\right)-\frac{5}{9}-\frac{i\pi}{3}+O\left(\frac{m_e^2}{4m_\mu^2},\alpha\right)\right]$.
- The cutoff $\Lambda_\mu$ was varied over the range $1 \le \Lambda_\mu \le 65$ (in units of muon Bohr momentum $\alpha m_\mu/2$).
- The numerical calculations used $N=25$ sampling points (where $N_\mu=N_\theta=N$).

## Context

This work extends the DLCQ approach previously applied to positronium by Trittmann and Pauli (1997) to the true muonium system, incorporating a second fermion flavor (electrons) into the Fock space. The inclusion of $|e\bar e\rangle$ states is motivated by the fact that vacuum polarization due to electrons in the single-photon annihilation channel (VP-e-A) was shown by Jentschura et al. (1997) to be the second-largest correction to the hyperfine splitting in true muonium in instant-form perturbation theory. The results enable future fully realistic simulations that would require decreasing $m_e$ and $\alpha$ to physical values, including hadronic polarization corrections, and addressing renormalization evolution between the widely separated muon and electron mass scales.
