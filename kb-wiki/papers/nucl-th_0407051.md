# The Nuclear Reactions in Standard BBN

> Nowadays, the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) anisotropies studies accurately determine the baryon fraction omega_b, showing an overall and striking agreement with previous determinations of omega_b obtained from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN). However, a deeper comparison of BBN predictions with the determinations of the primordial light nuclides abundances shows slight tensions, motivating an effort to further improve the accuracy of theoretical predictions, as well as to better evaluate systematics in both observations and nuclear reactions measurements. We present some results of an important step towards an increasing precision of BBN predictions, namely an updated and critical review of the nuclear network, and a new protocol to perform the nuclear data regression.

## Key claim

The paper presents an updated and critical review of the nuclear reaction network for Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) and a new protocol for nuclear data regression that avoids the systematic bias introduced by previous methods, where the fitted functions typically passed below the majority of the data.

## Method

The authors developed a chi-squared minimization method that treats statistical and normalization errors separately, allowing different renormalization factors for each experimental dataset while disfavoring renormalizations greater than the estimated normalization errors. The reaction rates were obtained by numerical integration of astrophysical S-factors convolved with the Boltzmann/Gamow kernel, with uncertainties propagated through the covariance matrix of fitted parameters. The final nuclide abundance uncertainties were derived using a generalization of linear propagation methods.

## Key numbers

- The analysis was performed for the typical baryon fraction value omega_b = 0.023
- The first stage of Li-7 production occurs around T approximately 70 keV
- The authors state that determining the H-2(d,n)He-3, H-2(d,p)H-3, and He-4(He-3,gamma)Be-7 reactions at the 1% accuracy level over the energy interval up to approximately 2 MeV could significantly improve predictions for both H-2 and Li-7
- The He-4 error budget is dominated by the uncertainty on the neutron lifetime

## Context

This work extends the seminal BBN network review by Smith (1992) and addresses systematic biases in the regression method used by Cyburt (2004). The new compilation and regression protocol enable more reliable BBN predictions and can be applied to non-standard BBN scenarios. The paper also identifies that sub-leading reactions such as Li-7 + p -> Be-8* -> gamma + 2 He-4, often neglected in BBN studies, are truly marginal even with new measurements.
