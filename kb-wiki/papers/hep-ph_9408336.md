# SU(2)-Flavor-Symmetry Breaking in Nuclear Antiquark Distributions

> SU(2)-flavor-symmetry breaking in antiquark distributions of the nucleon was suggested by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC) in deep inelastic muon scattering. As an independent test, Drell-Yan data for the tungsten target have been used for examining the asymmetry. We investigate whether there exists significant modification of the $\bar u -\bar d$ distribution in nuclei in a parton recombination model. It should be noted that a finite $\bar u-\bar d$ distribution is theoretically possible in nuclei even if the sea is $SU(2)_f$ symmetric in the nucleon. In neutron-excess nuclei such as the tungsten, there exist more $d$-valence quarks than $u$-valence quarks, so that more $\bar d$-quarks are lost than $\bar u$-quarks are due to parton recombinations in the small $x$ region. Our results suggest that the nuclear modification in the tungsten is a 2--10 % effect on the $\bar u-\bar d$ distribution suggested by the NMC data. Nuclear effects on the flavor asymmetric distribution could be an interesting topic for future theoretical and experimental investigations.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in neutron-excess nuclei such as tungsten can produce a finite \(\bar u - \bar d\) asymmetry even if the antiquark sea is SU(2)-flavor symmetric in the free nucleon, but this nuclear modification is only a 2–10% effect on the asymmetry suggested by the NMC data.

## Method

The author applies a parton recombination model to compute the modification of the \(\bar u - \bar d\) distribution in a tungsten nucleus. The model accounts for parton interactions between different nucleons in the small-\(x\) region, where the longitudinal size of a parton exceeds the average nucleon separation. The calculation uses MRS-D0 parton distributions at \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and evaluates the recombination contributions from \(\bar q G \to \bar q\) and \(\bar q q \to G\) processes, with the neutron-excess parameter \(\varepsilon = (N-Z)/(N+Z)\) controlling the magnitude of the asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-excess parameter for tungsten \(_{74}^{184}W_{110}\): \(\varepsilon = 0.196\).
- The NMC Gottfried sum rule integral: \(\int_{0.004}^{0.8} \frac{dx}{x} [F_2^{\mu p}(x) - F_2^{\mu n}(x)] = 0.221 \pm 0.008 \pm 0.019\) at \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\), yielding \(S_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026\).
- The nuclear modification of the \(\bar u - \bar d\) distribution in tungsten is a 2–10% effect relative to the asymmetry suggested by the MRS-D0 distribution.
- Maximum value of the nuclear modification: \(x[\bar u(x) - \bar d(x)]_A \approx +0.0001\) or \(+0.00025\) per nucleon, depending on the input sea symmetry assumption.

## Context

This work extends the study of SU(2)-flavor symmetry breaking in antiquark distributions, first suggested by the NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule (NMC, 1994). It addresses the possibility that Drell-Yan data on tungsten targets, previously used to test the asymmetry, could be affected by nuclear modifications. The parton recombination model employed was developed by Kumano (1993) to explain nuclear shadowing in \(F_2^A(x,Q^2)\). The results show that nuclear effects are small but non-negligible, and they motivate future Drell-Yan experiments on proton and deuteron targets at Fermilab, as well as the recent CERN-NA51 result indicating \(\bar u/\bar d = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05\) at \(x=0.18\) and \(Q^2=20\ \text{GeV}^2\).
