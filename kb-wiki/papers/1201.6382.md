# KNO scaling of fluctuations in pp and pA, and eccentricities in heavy-ion collisions

> Multiplicity fluctuations at midrapidity in p+p collisions at high energies are described by a negative binomial distribution and exhibit approximate Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling. We find that these KNO fluctuations are important also for reproducing the multiplicity distribution in d+Au collisions observed at RHIC, adding to the Glauber fluctuations of the number of binary collisions or participants. We predict that the multiplicity distribution in p+Pb collisions at the LHC also deviates little from the KNO scaling function. Finally, we analyze various moments of the eccentricity of the particle production zone in A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC and find that particle production fluctuations increase fluctuation dominated moments such as the triangularity epsilon_3 substantially.

## Key claim

The paper finds that intrinsic particle production fluctuations (described by a negative binomial distribution) are essential for reproducing the multiplicity distribution in d+Au collisions at RHIC, and that these fluctuations dominate over Glauber geometry fluctuations for p+Pb collisions at the LHC, resulting in a multiplicity distribution close to the KNO scaling function measured in p+p collisions.

## Method

The authors use a kT-factorization approach within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework to compute gluon production in high-energy collisions, solving the non-linear Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with running-coupling kernel. For heavy-ion collisions, they include fluctuations of the locations of valence charge sources in the transverse plane (geometry fluctuations) and then add intrinsic particle production fluctuations by treating the actual multiplicity in each transverse cell as a negative binomial distribution (NBD) random variable. They compute eccentricity moments of the initial gluon density distribution in A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* In p+p collisions at 2.36 TeV, the ratio of the NBD fluctuation parameter to the mean multiplicity is k/bar n = 0.16.
* The NBD fluctuation parameter for p+p collisions is constant: k_pp = (1/pi) * Delta^2 r_perp * Delta_eta * Lambda^2_QCD, with Lambda_QCD = 0.24 GeV, Delta_eta = 1, and Delta^2 r_perp the area of a transverse coordinate cell.
* The hard valence charges are smeared over an energy-independent area sigma_0 = 4.2 fm^2, corresponding to sigma_NN at 200 GeV.
* For p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 4400 GeV, the predicted average charged particle multiplicity is <N_ch> = 16.
* Adding particle production fluctuations with k proportional to min(T_A, T_B) increases higher-order eccentricities (epsilon_3, epsilon_4, epsilon_5) by as much as 50% compared to geometry fluctuations alone.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based approach of Gelis (2009) and the dipole evolution model of Avsar (2010) by explicitly connecting NBD multiplicity fluctuations in p+p collisions to KNO scaling and applying them to pA and AA collisions. The authors find that geometry fluctuations alone (as in standard Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations) are insufficient to reproduce the d+Au multiplicity distribution from STAR (2008), and that particle production fluctuations substantially increase fluctuation-dominated eccentricity moments such as epsilon_3, consistent with predictions from the DIPSY Monte-Carlo. This enables future studies of how these initial-state fluctuations affect higher-order flow coefficients in viscous hydrodynamics and the centrality dependence of jet quenching.
