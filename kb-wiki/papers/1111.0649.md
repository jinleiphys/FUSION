# D vs d: CP Violation in Beta Decay and Electric Dipole Moments

> The T-odd correlation coefficient D in nuclear beta decay probes CP violation in many theories beyond the Standard Model. We provide an analysis for how large D can be in light of constraints from electric dipole moment (EDM) searches. We argue that the neutron EDM d_n currently provides the strongest constraint on D, which is 10 - 10^3 times stronger than current direct limits on D (depending on the model). In particular, contributions to D in leptoquark models (previously regarded as "EDM safe") are more constrained than previously thought. Bounds on D can be weakened only by fine-tuned cancellations or if theoretical uncertainties are larger than estimated in d_n. We also study implications for D from mercury and deuteron EDMs.

## Key claim

The T-odd correlation coefficient D in nuclear beta decay is constrained by electric dipole moment (EDM) searches much more strongly than by direct measurements, with the neutron EDM providing a bound 10 to 10^3 times stronger than current direct limits on D, depending on the model; however, these bounds can be weakened only by fine-tuned cancellations or if theoretical uncertainties in the neutron EDM are larger than estimated.

## Method

The authors computed the relationship between the CP-violating D correlation coefficient in nuclear beta decay and the CP-odd four-quark operator O_LR that contributes to neutron, mercury, and deuteron electric dipole moments, using chiral effective theory and quark model estimates for hadronic matrix elements. They analyzed constraints on D from EDM bounds in several beyond-Standard-Model scenarios: left-right symmetric models, the R-parity violating MSSM with baryon number violation, models with exotic fermions, and leptoquark models, calculating the one-loop radiative corrections that generate EDMs from the same CP-violating phases entering D.

## Key numbers

- Neutron D correlation measurement: D_n = (-1.0 ± 2.1) × 10^{-4} (emiT collaboration, 2011)
- 19Ne D correlation measurement: D_Ne = (1 ± 6) × 10^{-4} (average of previous measurements)
- Neutron EDM bound: |d_n| < 2.9 × 10^{-26} e cm (90% CL, Baker 2006)
- Mercury EDM bound: |d_Hg| < 3.1 × 10^{-29} e cm (95% CL, Griffith 2009)
- Model-independent bound from neutron EDM: |D_t/κ| < 3 × 10^{-7}
- Neutron EDM from O_LR: d_n = -1 × 10^{-19} k_LR e cm, with O(1) uncertainty
- Mercury EDM from O_LR: |d_Hg| = 7 × 10^{-24} |k_LR| e cm, with order-of-magnitude uncertainty
- Deuteron EDM from O_LR: |d_D| ≈ 4.5 × 10^{-20} |k_LR| e cm, with 20-30% uncertainty
- Scalar LQ mass limit from CMS: m_R1 > 340 GeV (β_e > 0.5)
- Scalar LQ mass limit from ATLAS: m_R1 > 319 GeV (β_e > 0.5)
- Vector LQ mass limit from D0: m_V1 > 302 GeV (β_e > 0.1) or > 144 GeV (β_e < 0.1)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Herczeg (2001, 2005) on CP violation in beta decay and EDM constraints, incorporating recent improved computations of the neutron EDM by An (2009) and mercury EDM by Ban (2010) that revealed large nuclear uncertainties weakening the mercury constraint. The authors show that leptoquark models, previously regarded as "EDM safe" by Herczeg (2001, 2005), are actually more constrained than thought because radiative corrections generate the four-quark operator O_LR without suppression by light quark masses. This analysis enables future beta decay experiments targeting D_n ~ 10^{-7} to be interpreted in light of EDM constraints, and provides the first computation of D_t in the R-parity violating MSSM with baryon-number violation.
