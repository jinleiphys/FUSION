# Relativistic transport theory for $N$, $Δ$ and $N^{*}$(1440) system

> A self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation for the $N^{*}$(1440) resonance is developed based on an effective Lagrangian of baryons interacting through mesons. The equation is consistent with that of nucleon's and delta's which we derived before. Thus, we obtain a set of coupled equations for the $N$, $Δ$ and $N^{*}$(1440) distribution functions. All the $N^{*}$(1440)-relevant in-medium two-body scattering cross sections within the $N$, $Δ$ and $N^{*}$(1440) system are derived from the same effective Lagrangian in addition to the mean field and presented analytically. Medium effects on the cross sections are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation for the N*(1440) resonance, coupled with existing equations for nucleons and deltas, but the numerical results for in-medium cross sections are sensitive to the unknown N*N* coupling strengths and should be viewed with caution because screening and anti-screening effects of the medium on the interaction were not included.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian describing baryons (N, Delta, N*(1440)) interacting through sigma, omega and pi mesons. Using the closed time-path Green's function technique with semi-classical, quasi-particle and Born approximations, they derive the RBUU equation for the N*(1440) distribution function and obtain analytical expressions for all N*(1440)-relevant in-medium two-body scattering cross sections within the N, Delta and N*(1440) system. The centroid N*(1440) mass is introduced via a Breit-Wigner distribution with momentum-dependent decay width to account for the broad resonance width, and phenomenological form factors are applied at vertices.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1440) resonance centroid mass M0 = 1440 MeV with vacuum decay width Gamma0 = 200 MeV
- The ratio g_pi_NN* / g_pi_NN = 0.351, determined from the pion decay width of the N*(1440) resonance
- Cut-off masses for form factors: Lambda_sigma = 1200 MeV, Lambda_omega = 808 MeV, Lambda_pi = 500 MeV for NN-meson vertices; Lambda*_sigma = 480 MeV and Lambda*_omega = 323 MeV for NN*-meson vertices (40% of the NN values)
- The free pp -> pp*(1440) cross section calculated with the 40% form factor scaling agrees with experimental data; using Lambda*_A = Lambda_A gives cross sections three times larger than empirical values at higher energy
- Three coupling strength scenarios tested: alpha(N*)=beta(N*)=1; alpha(N*)=beta(N*)=1.5; and alpha(N*)=1, beta(N*)=1.5, where alpha = g_omega_N*N*/g_omega_NN and beta = g_sigma_N*N*/g_sigma_NN

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RBUU transport models for nucleons (Mao et al., 1994, 1997) and deltas (Mao et al., 1996) to include the N*(1440) resonance, motivated by experimental indications that at incident energies from SIS (about 1 AGeV) to AGS (about 15 AGeV) a gradual transition to resonance matter occurs, with more than 30% of nucleons excited to resonance states at 2 GeV/nucleon (Averbeck et al., 1994). The calculated in-medium cross sections differ significantly from the Cugnon parameterization (Cugnon, 1981) commonly used for N*N elastic scattering in transport models, and the results are compared with the one-boson-exchange model of Huber and Aichelin (1994). The approach enables self-consistent inclusion of N*(1440) dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collision simulations, though further investigation of screening and anti-screening effects is needed.
