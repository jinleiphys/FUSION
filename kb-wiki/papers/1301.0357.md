# Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory and the Real-Time Dynamics of Fermi Superfluids

> I describe the Time-Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation, which is an adiabatic extension of the Density Functional Theory to superfluid Fermi systems and their real-time dynamics. This new theoretical framework has been applied to describe a number of phenomena in cold atomic gases and nuclear collective motion: excitation of the Higgs modes in strongly interacting Fermi superfluids, generation of quantized vortices, crossing and reconnection of vortices, excitation of the superflow at velocities above the critical velocity, excitation of quantum shock waves and domain walls in the collisions of superfluid atomic clouds, excitation of collective states in nuclei.

## Key claim

The Time-Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation (TDSLDA) provides a first-principles theoretical framework for the real-time dynamics of superfluid Fermi systems, including the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) and nuclear systems, but is valid only at zero temperature and when collisions leading to entropy production and thermalization can be ignored, similar in spirit to Landau's Fermi liquid theory for high-frequency zero-sound propagation.

## Method

The authors formulate TDSLDA as an adiabatic extension of Density Functional Theory to superfluid Fermi systems, deriving equations of motion from an action integral that yields time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for quasiparticle wave functions. The energy density functional for the UFG is constructed using dimensional arguments and symmetry requirements (translational and rotational invariance, parity, local gauge and local Galilean invariance, renormalizability), with dimensionless constants α, β, γ, and λ determined by fitting to accurate quantum Monte Carlo calculations for uniform systems and inhomogeneous systems in external traps. The resulting equations are solved numerically on modern supercomputers for various dynamical scenarios including vortex nucleation, Higgs mode excitation, and supercritical flow.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter ξ = 0.372(5) from quantum Monte Carlo calculations and ξ = 0.376(4) from experimental measurements, characterizing the ground state energy of a homogeneous UFG as ξ × 3ε_F N/5
- Pairing gap in UFG: Δ ≈ 0.5 ε_F, one of the largest relative pairing gaps in any fermion system
- Gradient correction constant λ ≈ -0.1 (negative) for the UFG energy density functional
- Effective mass parameter α ≈ 1.1, close to the bare mass value
- Landau critical velocity at unitarity: v_c ≈ 0.37 v_F, where v_F = ħk_F/m is the Fermi velocity
- Sound velocity in UFG: c = √ξ ħk_F/√3 m
- Higgs mode frequency predicted in weak coupling: ħω = 2Δ_0, but in UFG the nonlinear Higgs-like modes have frequencies ħΩ_H < 2Δ_0

## Context

This work extends the Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) formulated in Refs. [by, ab02, bfm] to time-dependent phenomena (TDSLDA), building on the original DFT framework of Hohenberg, Kohn, and Sham and the time-dependent DFT of Runge and Gross. The approach contrasts with the two-fluid hydrodynamics of Landau, Tisza, and Khalatnikov, which cannot describe vortex generation or Cooper pair breaking, and with the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for Bose superfluids, which would fail to capture the nonlinear Higgs mode dynamics. The framework enables first-principles studies of vortex nucleation and dynamics, quantum shock waves, domain walls, and collective mode excitation in both cold atomic gases and nuclear systems, with the Higgs mode dissipation mechanism remaining an open question.
