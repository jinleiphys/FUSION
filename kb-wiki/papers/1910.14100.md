# Invariant-mass spectroscopy of $^{14}$O excited states

> Excited states in $^{14}$O have been investigated both experimentally and theoretically. Experimentally, these states were produced via neutron-knockout reactions with a fast $^{15}$O beam and the invariant-mass technique was employed to isolate the 1$p$ and 2$p$ decay channels and determine their branching ratios. The spectrum of excited states was also calculated with the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum that treats bound and scattering states in a unified model. By comparing energies, widths and decay branching patterns, spin and parity assignments for all experimentally observed levels below 8 MeV are made. This includes the location of the second 2$^{+}$ state that we find is in near degeneracy with the third 0$^{+}$ state. An interesting case of sequential 2$p$ decay through a pair of degenerate $^{13}$N excited states with opposite parities was found where the interference between the two sequential decay pathways produces an unusual relative-angle distribution between the emitted protons.

## Key claim

By combining experimental invariant-mass spectroscopy with Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) calculations, the authors assign spin and parity for all experimentally observed levels in 14O below 8 MeV, including the identification of a near-degenerate second 2+ state and third 0+ state; however, the assignment of the third 0+ state relies on the peak near the known 2+2 state being a doublet rather than a single state, as the width of the peak fitted as a singlet (128 keV) is almost twice the previously reported width of the 2+2 state (76 keV).

## Method

Excited states in 14O were produced via neutron-knockout reactions using a fast 15O beam (E/A=52.1 MeV) and a mixed 17Ne beam (E/A=62.9 MeV) impinging on a 1-mm-thick 9Be target at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The invariant-mass technique was applied to detected charged particles in the High Resolution Array (HiRA) to isolate the 1p and 2p decay channels, and Monte Carlo simulations incorporating detector geometry and energy resolution were used to extract intrinsic widths and cross sections. The experimental results were compared to SMEC calculations using the modified YSOX interaction and a Wigner-Bartlett continuum-coupling interaction with strength V0 = -350 MeV·fm3 to assign spin and parity.

## Key numbers

- The 2+1 state at 6.585(1) MeV has an intrinsic width less than 25 keV (3σ upper limit), consistent with the more restrictive limit of less than 5 keV from Negret (2005).
- The 2+2 state at 7.768 MeV has a width of 76 keV (fixed to the evaluated value from Solove (1991)), while the newly identified candidate 0+3 state at 7.669(53) MeV has a width less than 128 keV.
- The 2+3 state at 9.755(10) MeV has a width of 229(51) keV and a cross section of 4.2 mb for production from the 15O beam.
- The 8.787(13) MeV state (tentatively assigned as 1-2) has a width of 182(32) keV and decays 31% via p1 and 69% via p2+p3 branches.
- The systematic uncertainty in excitation energies is estimated at ±10 keV based on calibration checks showing invariant-mass peaks within 10 keV of tabulated values.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 14O excited states by Teranishi (2007) and Wang (2008), and the most recent evaluation by Solove (1991), by providing new spin-parity assignments and decay branching ratios from combined experimental and SMEC theoretical analysis. The identification of a near-degenerate 2+2 and 0+3 state, and the observation of sequential 2p decay through a pair of degenerate 13N excited states with opposite parities producing interference effects in proton angular distributions, demonstrates the importance of continuum coupling in open-channel nuclear systems as emphasized in prior SMEC work by Okolowicz (2016, 2018). The results enable future studies of threshold effects and mirror symmetry breaking in light proton-rich nuclei.
