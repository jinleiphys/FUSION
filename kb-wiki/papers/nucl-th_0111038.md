# Microscopic Models for the Particle-Vibration Coupling in Exotic Nuclei

> Recent results obtained, often in fruitful collaboration with Japanese colleagues in the study of the interplay between single-particle and collective degrees of freedom are reviewed.

## Key claim
The paper reviews how the coupling between single-particle motion and collective surface vibrations (particle-vibration coupling) renormalizes nuclear properties in exotic nuclei, but emphasizes that the effects can be qualitatively different from stable nuclei due to reduced collectivity, asymmetric phase-space, and the blocking of cancellation mechanisms.

## Method
The author reviews extensions of the microscopic model developed by the Milano-Orsay collaboration, which uses an effective energy-dependent complex Hamiltonian to treat the coupling of quasiparticle states to QRPA collective vibrations (low-lying quadrupole and octupole states) via the \(W^{\downarrow}(E)\) term. For pairing correlations, the work compares results from Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA with finite-range Gogny D1S force HFB+QRPA calculations, and discusses solving the Dyson (Nambu-Gor'kov) equation to treat self-energy and induced pairing interaction on equal footing.

## Key numbers
- In \(^{18}\)O and \(^{20}\)O, coupling to doorway states increases low-lying dipole strength below 15 MeV by up to 35% compared to QRPA values.
- In \(^{24}\)O, the particle-vibration coupling produces a very large energy gap (larger than 6 MeV) for the new magic number \(N=16\).
- For Sn isotopes, the experimental pairing gap of about 1.4 MeV is reproduced solving the full Dyson equation with a pairing strength \(G\) of about 0.17 MeV, compared to 0.23 MeV without particle-vibration coupling.
- In oxygen isotopes \(^{18,20,22}\)O, experimental pairing gaps \(\Delta(N,Z)\) are of the order of 2 MeV, reproduced by Gogny D1S calculations.
- For Sn isotopes A=102 to A=130, the calculated first \(2^+\) energy is about 2 MeV (experimental about 1 MeV), and the centroid energy of dipole strength over 18 MeV is about 3 MeV higher than experimental values.

## Context
This review extends decades of work on particle-vibration coupling in stable nuclei (Bohr and Mottelson 1975; Mahaux et al. 1985; Bortignon et al. 1998) to exotic nuclei, building on pioneering studies from Orsay (Vinh Mau 1995) and Milano (Colò et al. 1998; Barranco et al. 1999). The results enable improved modeling of low-lying dipole strength relevant for astrophysical r-process rates, and provide a framework for understanding new magic numbers (e.g., \(N=16\) in \(^{24}\)O) and pairing correlations in neutron stars.
