# Maximal CP and Bounds on the Neutron Electric Dipole Moment from P and CP Breaking

> We find in theories with spontaneous P and CP violation that symmetries needed to set the tree level strong CP phase to zero can also set all non-zero tree level CP violating phases to the maximal value π/ 2 in the symmetry basis simultaneously explaining the smallness of \barθ and the largeness of the CKM CP violating phase. In these models we find the one loop lower bound \barθ > 10^{-11} relevant for early discovery of neutron edm d_n > 10^{-27} ecm. The lower bound relaxes to \barθ > 10^{-13} or d_n > 10^{-29} ecm for the case where the CP phases are non-maximal. Interestingly the spontaneous CP phase appears in the quark sector, not the Higgs sector, and is enabled by a heavy left-right symmetric vectorlike quark family with mass M. These results do not vanish in the decoupling limit of M_{H_2^+} > M \rightarrow \infty (where M_{H_2^+} is the mass of heavy Higgs at the parity breaking scale) and the age-old expectation that laws of nature (or its Lagrangian) are parity and matter-antimatter symmetric may be testable by the above predictions and EDM experiments, even if new physics occurs only at see-saw, GUT or Planck scales. There is also a region in parameter space with M_{H_2^+} < M where the above bounds are dampened by the factor (M_{H_2^+}/M)^2. By using flavour symmetries and texture arguments we also make predictions for the CKM phase that arises from the maximal phase on diagonalization to the physical basis. There are no axions predicted in this model.

## Key claim
In theories with spontaneous P and CP violation, symmetries that set the tree-level strong CP phase to zero also force all non-zero tree-level CP-violating phases to the maximal value π/2 in the symmetry basis, simultaneously explaining the smallness of θ̄ and the largeness of the CKM CP phase; the lower bound on θ̄ is then ≥ 10⁻¹¹ (or dₙ ≥ 10⁻²⁷ ecm) for the maximal case, relaxing to θ̄ ≥ 10⁻¹³ (dₙ ≥ 10⁻²⁹ ecm) if the CP phases are non-maximal.

## Method
The author extends a left-right symmetric model with P and CP symmetries, adding a second Higgs bidoublet and a vectorlike quark family (one SU(2)L doublet and one SU(2)R doublet) to allow spontaneous CP violation. A Z₂ symmetry (with φₐ odd and φₛ even) eliminates dangerous imaginary quartic couplings, ensuring all Higgs potential parameters are real; real vacuum expectation values then satisfy the minimization conditions, producing a maximal CP phase π/2 in the quark mass matrix in the symmetry basis. One-loop radiative corrections to θ̄ are computed using the resulting mass matrices.

## Key numbers
- Lower bound on the strong CP phase: θ̄ ≥ 10⁻¹¹ for maximal CP phase (π/2) in the symmetry basis.
- Corresponding neutron EDM lower bound: dₙ ≥ 10⁻²⁷ ecm for the maximal CP case.
- Lower bound on θ̄ relaxes to θ̄ ≥ 10⁻¹³ for non-maximal CP phases.
- Corresponding neutron EDM lower bound: dₙ ≥ 10⁻²⁹ ecm for the non-maximal case.
- Experimental bound on neutron EDM: dₙ ≤ 1.9 × 10⁻²⁶ ecm at 90% C.L. (from PhysRevLett.97.131801).
- Relation used: dₙ ∼ 2 × 10⁻¹⁶ θ̄ ecm (from Pospelov (2005)).
- Suppression factor for the case M_{H₂⁺} < M: (M_{H₂⁺}/M)².

## Context
This work extends the author’s earlier left-right symmetric model (Kuchimanchi (2010)) that solved the strong CP problem using P and CP without a Peccei-Quinn symmetry, by now allowing spontaneous CP violation and showing the CP phase becomes maximal. It contrasts with the Peccei-Quinn mechanism (Peccei and Quinn (1977)) which predicts dₙ ∼ 10⁻³² ecm, too small for current experiments, and with supersymmetric models where dₙ ∼ 10⁻²⁵ (TeV/M_{SUSY})² ecm diminishes rapidly at high scales. The results enable tests of P and CP symmetry at ongoing and planned neutron EDM experiments (e.g., Burghoff (2011), Baker (2010), Beck (2011), Martin (2011), Serebrov (2009)) even if new physics exists only at GUT or Planck scales.
