# Model independent Breit-Wigner parameters of nucleon resonances S11(1535), S11(1650) and P11(1710)

> Estimates of Breit-Wigner parameters of nucleon resonances were obtained by phenomenological analysis of eta meson photoproduction on protons performed completely by statistical procedures without appealing to theoretical models

# Model independent Breit-Wigner parameters of nucleon resonances S11(1535), S11(1650) and P11(1710)

## Key claim

The Breit-Wigner parameters (mass, full width, and helicity amplitude) for the nucleon resonances S11(1535), S11(1650), and P11(1710) can be extracted from eta meson photoproduction data using purely statistical procedures without appealing to any theoretical model, though the results depend on the chosen energy interval and the statistical quality of the fit degrades when extending to the full energy range (chi-squared per degree of freedom rises from 1.29 to 3.97).

## Method

The authors performed a multipole decomposition of differential cross sections for the reaction gamma p -> eta p, expanding the angular distributions in Legendre polynomials to obtain the coefficient a0(W) (the sum of squares of multipole amplitudes) as a function of center-of-mass energy W. They then fitted the energy dependence of a0(W) using Breit-Wigner formulae for one, two, and three resonances, testing the statistical quality of each model via the chi-squared per degree of freedom criterion across different energy intervals from 1490 to 1724 MeV. The primary data came from the GRAAL collaboration, with additional GRAAL data included for the widest energy interval.

## Key numbers

- S11(1535): mass W_R = 1538.62 ± 0.69 MeV, full width Gamma_R = 163.0 ± 4.09 MeV, helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = (99.23 ± 1.01) × 10^{-3} / sqrt(GeV) (for the energy interval 1490–1603 MeV)
- S11(1535): mass W_R = 1539.47 ± 4.15 MeV, full width Gamma_R = 174.79 ± 19.49 MeV, helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = (112.02 ± 18.23) × 10^{-3} / sqrt(GeV) (for the full energy interval 1490–1724 MeV)
- S11(1650): mass W_R = 1636.58 ± 1.4 MeV, full width Gamma_R = 78.24 ± 11.2 MeV, helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = (36.2 ± 5.8) × 10^{-3} / sqrt(GeV) (for the energy interval 1490–1676 MeV)
- P11(1710): mass W_R = 1708.75 ± 1.71 MeV, full width Gamma_R = 45.47 ± 15.73 MeV, helicity amplitude A_{1/2} = (59.28 ± 19.03) × 10^{-3} / sqrt(GeV) (for the energy interval 1490–1716 MeV)
- The adopted branching fractions (fixed in the analysis) were: beta_{eta N} = 0.55, beta_{pi N} = 0.35 for S11(1535); beta_{eta N} = 0.08, beta_{pi N} = 0.77 for S11(1650); beta_{eta N} = 0.06, beta_{pi N} = 0.15 for P11(1710)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the three-resonance model: 1.36 (1490–1716 MeV) and 3.97 (1490–1724 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the energy-independent partial-wave analysis previously performed by the same authors (Balandina, Leikin, Yudin, references bal1–bal3) by providing a model-independent determination of resonance parameters through purely statistical fitting. The results contrast with earlier model-dependent extractions using isobar models (Chiang et al., 2002, 2003), dispersion relations (Aznauryan, 2003), and quark models (Saghai, 2001), which the authors note yield contradictory values. The model-independent parameters are intended to help clarify the nature of these nucleon resonances, as discussed by Krusche (2005).
