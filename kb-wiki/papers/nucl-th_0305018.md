# Alternative Size and Lifetime Measurements for High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Two-Particle correlations based on the interference of identical particles has provided the chief means for determining the shape and lifetime of sources in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Here, Strong and Coulomb induced correlations are shown to provide equivalent information.

## Key claim

Strong and Coulomb-induced correlations between non-identical particles can provide equivalent information about source size and lifetime in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as the standard identical-particle interferometry, offering a truly independent measurement strategy.

## Method

The authors compute two-particle correlation functions by convolving a Gaussian source function with squared wave functions that include Coulomb and strong interactions, using Monte Carlo integration. For Coulomb interactions, they compare quantum solutions of the Schrödinger equation with a classical approximation; for strong interactions, they incorporate experimentally tabulated phase shifts into partial-wave expansions of the wave function, handling spin and resonant channels such as the Δ⁺⁺ resonance in pπ⁺ correlations.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian source parameters used for pK⁺ correlations: R_out = 8 fm, R_⊥ = 4 fm (with R_long = R_side = 4 fm)
- For pπ⁺ correlations with strong interaction dominance: Q_inv = 450 MeV/c near the Δ⁺⁺ resonance, with source parameters R_out = R_side = 4 fm, R_long = 8 fm
- Coulomb correlation strength is of order 1% at the relevant Q_inv values
- Useful analysis range for pK⁺ Coulomb correlations is restricted to Q_inv < 150 MeV/c to avoid competing correlations at the 1% level
- The classical approximation for Coulomb correlations becomes reasonable for large Q_inv, where the suppression ratio at cosθ=0 approaches (R_out/R_side)²

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strong and Coulomb correlations for angle-averaged source functions (Brown and Danielewicz, E895 imaging, Verde imaging) to shape and lifetime determination, which had previously been confined to identical-particle interferometry. The authors note that two-pion analyses at RHIC (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS) show equal outwards and sidewards sizes suggesting sudden emission, contradicting dynamical models with phase transitions (Teaney, Bass and Dumitru). The proposed method enables independent verification of source lifetimes using non-identical particle pairs, avoiding issues with two-track resolution and providing complementary information to identical-particle HBT analyses.
