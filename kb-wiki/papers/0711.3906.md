# Low energy properties of non-perturbative quantum systems: a space reduction approach

> We propose and test a renormalization procedure which acts in Hilbert space. We test its efficiency on strongly correlated quantum spin systems by working out and analyzing the low-energy spectral properties of frustrated quantum spin systems in different parts of the phase diagram and in the neighbourhood of quantum critical points.

# Low energy properties of non-perturbative quantum systems: a space reduction approach

## Key claim

The authors propose and test a Hilbert-space renormalization procedure that reduces the dimension of the Hilbert space step by step while renormalizing the coupling strength to preserve the ground-state energy, but the method's accuracy degrades when basis states with sizable amplitudes in the ground-state wavefunction are eliminated, and the procedure becomes unstable at quantum phase transition points where the coupling strength is predicted to remain constant.

## Method

The algorithm starts with a Hamiltonian H = H0 + g H1 in a Hilbert space of dimension N, computes the ground-state eigenvalue and eigenvector via the Lanczos technique, then projects the system into a subspace of dimension N-1 by eliminating the basis state with the smallest ground-state amplitude. The coupling strength g is renormalized at each step by solving a nonlinear equation that enforces the ground-state energy to remain unchanged. The procedure is iterated until the spectrum becomes unstable, and the flow of the coupling strength is monitored to detect fixed points corresponding to quantum phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- For a frustrated two-leg spin-1/2 ladder with L=9 sites per leg (N=48620 basis states, Mtot=0), Jt=15, Jl=5, Jc=3: the ground-state energy error p(1) reaches 0.5% when the reduced space dimension n lies between 500 and 80; p(2) and p(3) are about 0.05% at n ≈ 500.
- For the same ladder with Jt=2.5, Jl=5, Jc=3: p(1) reaches 0.5% when n lies between 1000 and 100; p(2) and p(3) are about 2.5% at n ≈ 500.
- For L=6 sites per leg (N=924 basis states, Mtot=0) at a first-order phase transition point with Jt=15, Jl=Jc≈12.21: the ground-state energy e1=-11.25 and first-excited energy e2=-11.25 (level crossing), with e3≈-10.7; the coupling strength stays constant down to n<100.
- For the same system near the transition with Jt=15, Jl≈12.21, Jc≈11: e1≈-11.38, e2≈-10.67, e3≈-10.4; the renormalized coupling stays stable down to n<250.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed Hilbert-space reduction method (Khalil and Richert, 2005) and tests it on frustrated quantum spin ladders, which are strongly correlated systems inaccessible to perturbation theory. The approach differs from the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (White, 1992; Schollwöck, 2005) which is lattice-specific, and shows connections to a density-matrix-based space reduction method for phonon problems (Weiße and Fehske, 1998). The results demonstrate that the procedure can substantially reduce Hilbert space dimensions while preserving low-energy spectral information, and that the coupling flow correctly identifies quantum phase transition points as fixed points, enabling future application to other microscopic quantum systems including finite-temperature studies.
