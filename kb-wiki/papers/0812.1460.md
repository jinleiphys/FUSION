# Strangeness and charm at FAIR

> We study the properties of strange and charm mesons in hot and dense matter within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach for the experimental conditions of density and temperature expected for the CBM experiment at FAIR/GSI. The in-medium solution at finite temperature accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on all the baryons involved, and meson self-energies. In the strange sector, the $\bar K$ spectral function spreads over a wide range of energies, reflecting the melting of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the contribution of $(Λ,Σ,Σ^*)N^{-1}$ components at finite temperature. In the case of charm mesons, the dynamically-generated $Λ_c(2593)$ and $Σ_c(2880)$ resonances remain close to their free-space position while acquiring a remarkable width. As a result, the $D$ meson spectral density shows a single pronounced peak for energies close to the $D$ meson free-space mass that broadens with increasing matter density with an extended tail particularly towards lower energies. We also discuss the implications for the $D_{s0}(2317)$, $D_0(2400)$ and the predicted X(3700) resonances at FAIR energies.

# Strangeness and charm at FAIR

## Key claim

The in-medium spectral functions of strange and charm mesons are computed self-consistently at finite temperature and density, but the dynamically-generated charm resonances $\Lambda_c(2593)$ and $\Sigma_c(2880)$ remain close to their free-space positions while acquiring a remarkable width, unlike the $\Lambda(1405)$ which melts in the strange sector.

## Method

A self-consistent coupled-channel approach was used, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell amplitudes. For the strange sector, a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels incorporated $s$-wave (from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term of the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian) and $p$-wave (via hyperon-hole excitations) kaon-nucleon interactions. For the charm sector, a broken SU(4) $s$-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term was used, with a cutoff regularization scheme fixed by reproducing the $\Lambda_c(2593)$ resonance. The in-medium solution at finite temperature accounted for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on baryons via a temperature-dependent $\sigma-\omega$ model, and meson self-energies.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: $\rho_0 = 0.17 \, \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Attraction of the $\bar K$ potential at normal nuclear matter density: approximately -50 MeV, obtained by unitarized chiral theories in coupled channels
- The $D_{s0}(2317)$ resonance acquires a width of the order of 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- The $X(3700)$ resonance acquires a width of the order of 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange mesons in dense matter, such as the analysis of kaonic atoms by Friedman (2007) and heavy-ion collision studies by Fuchs (2005), as well as earlier self-consistent coupled-channel calculations by Ramos (1999) and Tolos (2006, 2008). The inclusion of charm mesons addresses the future CBM experiment at FAIR/GSI, where the modification of $D$ and $\bar D$ meson properties in hot dense baryonic matter may help explain $J/\Psi$ suppression. The results enable experimental investigation of in-medium widths of charm and hidden charm scalar resonances through transparency ratios at FAIR.
