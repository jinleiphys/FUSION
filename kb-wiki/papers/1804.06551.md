# Coulomb Energy of alpha-Aggregates on a Soap Bubble Shape

> We study the property of alpha-aggregates on a soap bubble shape within a microscopic framework, which takes full account of the Pauli principle. Our special attention is payed to the Coulomb energy for such an exotic shapes of nuclei, and we discuss the advantage of alpha-clusters with geometric configurations compared with the uniform density distributions in reducing the repulsive effect. We consider four kinds of configurations of alpha clusters on a soap bubble, which are dual polyhedra composed of a dodecahedron and an icosahedron, octacontahedron and two types of truncated icosahedrons, that is, two kinds of Archimedean solids. The latter two are an icosidodecahedron and a fullerene shape. When putting each alpha-cluster on the vertex of polyhedra, four $α$-cluster aggregates correspond to the following four nuclei; Gd (64 protons), Po (84 protons), Nd (60 protons) and a nucleus with 120 protons, respectively.

# Coulomb Energy of alpha-Aggregates on a Soap Bubble Shape

## Key claim

The Coulomb repulsion in heavy nuclei can be significantly reduced if protons are arranged as alpha-clusters in geometric configurations on a thin spherical shell (soap bubble shape) rather than as a uniform density distribution, but the Pauli principle only drastically affects the Coulomb energy at small sphere radii; at plausible nuclear radii the point-charge approximation (without Pauli principle) works surprisingly well.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic alpha-cluster model using Brink-Bloch wave functions with full antisymmetrization, placing alpha-clusters on the vertices of four polyhedral configurations (30, 32, 42, and 60 alpha-clusters) on a sphere of variable radius rho. They extracted only the Coulomb energy from the total binding energy and compared the full microscopic result (including Pauli exchange terms) with the direct term (Pauli switched off), the point-charge approximation, and two uniform distribution models (on the surface and inside the sphere).

## Key numbers

- For 30 alpha-clusters (Nd, Z=60) at nearest neighbor distance 3.3 fm (rho=5.34 fm): E_c/n = 6.76 MeV, compared to uniform surface value 8.50 MeV and uniform volume value 10.11 MeV.
- For 32 alpha-clusters (Gd, Z=64) at nearest neighbor distance 3.3 fm (rho=5.15 fm): E_c/n = 7.44 MeV, compared to uniform surface 9.35 MeV and uniform volume 11.14 MeV.
- For 42 alpha-clusters (Po, Z=84) at nearest neighbor distance 3.3 fm (rho=6.04 fm): E_c/n = 8.56 MeV, compared to uniform surface 10.42 MeV and uniform volume 12.42 MeV.
- For 60 alpha-clusters (Z=120) at nearest neighbor distance 3.3 fm (rho=8.18 fm): E_c/n = 9.43 MeV, compared to uniform surface 10.87 MeV and uniform volume 13.08 MeV.
- Point-charge geometric constants C_s(n): 0.4153 (n=30), 0.4156 (n=32), 0.4252 (n=42), 0.4384 (n=60), approaching 1/2 as n approaches infinity.
- Pauli effect index p(rho) at plausible radii ranges from 0.578 to 1.000 across the four configurations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Tohsaki and Itagaki, 2018, Phys. Rev. C 97, 011301R) which found energy minima for hollow alpha-cluster configurations in heavy nuclei. It builds on the long-standing concept of bubble nuclei (Wilson, 1948; Bulgac, 2000) and torus nuclei (Wong, 1973; Ichikawa et al., 2012), as well as experimental evidence for a central density depression in 208Pb (Heisenberg et al., 1969). The results enable future studies using the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Roepke (THSR) wave function to describe gas-like alpha distributions on the surface or inside the sphere, and motivate investigation of excess neutron distributions and effective inter-nucleon forces for heavy cluster models.
