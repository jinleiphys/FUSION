# Cold dilute nuclear matter with $α$-particle condensation in a generalized nonlinear relativistic mean-field model

> We explore the thermodynamic properties of homogeneous cold (zero-temperature) nuclear matter including nucleons and $α$-particle condensation at low densities by using a generalized nonlinear relativistic mean-field (gNL-RMF) model. In the gNL-RMF model, the $α$-particle is included as explicit degree of freedom and treated as point-like particle with its interaction described by meson exchanges and the in-medium effects on the $α$ binding energy is described by density- and temperature-dependent energy shift with the parameters obtained by fitting the experimental Mott density. We find that below the dropping density $n_{\rm{drop}}$ ($\sim 3\times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$), the zero-temperature symmetric nuclear matter is in the state of pure Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of $α$ particles while the neutron-rich nuclear matter is composed of $α$-BEC and neutrons. Above the $n_{\rm{drop}}$, the fraction of $α$-BEC decreases with density and vanishes at the transition density $n_t$ ($\sim 8\times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$). Above the $n_t$, the nuclear matter becomes pure nucleonic matter. Our results indicate that the empirical parabolic law for the isospin asymmetry dependence of nuclear matter equation of state is heavily violated by the $α$-particle condensation in the zero-temperature dilute nuclear matter, making the conventional definition of the symmetry energy meaningless. We investigate the symmetry energy defined under parabolic approximation for the zero-temperature dilute nuclear matter with $α$-particle condensation, and find it is significantly enhanced compared to the case without clusters and becomes saturated at about $7$ MeV at very low densities ($\lesssim 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$). The critical temperature for $α$-condensation in homogeneous dilute nuclear matter is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in zero-temperature dilute nuclear matter, α-particles form a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) below a dropping density of about 3×10⁻³ fm⁻³, and this condensation heavily violates the empirical parabolic law for the isospin asymmetry dependence of the equation of state, making the conventional definition of symmetry energy meaningless. The authors qualify that the symmetry energy defined under the parabolic approximation becomes saturated at about 7 MeV at very low densities (≲10⁻³ fm⁻³), but this definition is only an approximation because the parabolic expansion does not converge.

## Method

The authors used a generalized nonlinear relativistic mean-field (gNL-RMF) model with the FSUGold parameter set, treating α-particles as point-like bosons with meson-exchange interactions and including in-medium effects on the α binding energy via a density- and temperature-dependent energy shift fitted to experimental Mott densities. At zero temperature, they solved the coupled equations of motion for the meson fields and the densities of nucleons and α-particles in the BEC state, minimizing the free energy per baryon to determine the composition and thermodynamic properties of homogeneous cold nuclear matter for various isospin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Dropping density n_drop ≈ 3×10⁻³ fm⁻³ for symmetric nuclear matter (δ=0), below which the system is pure α-BEC; for δ=0.3, n_drop ≈ 4×10⁻³ fm⁻³; for δ=0.6, n_drop ≈ 5×10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- Transition density n_t ≈ 8×10⁻³ fm⁻³, above which α-particles vanish and the system becomes pure nucleonic matter.
- The internal energy per baryon of symmetric nuclear matter at zero density approaches the negative binding energy per baryon of the α-particle in vacuum, approximately -7.1 MeV.
- The symmetry energy defined under the parabolic approximation saturates at about 7 MeV at very low densities (≲10⁻³ fm⁻³).

## Context

This work extends the gNL-RMF model previously developed by Zhang and Chen (2017) for finite-temperature nuclear matter including light clusters, now applied to zero-temperature conditions where α-condensation becomes relevant. It contradicts the assumption that the empirical parabolic law holds for dilute nuclear matter, as shown earlier by Typel et al. (2010) and others for finite-temperature systems, and demonstrates that α-BEC formation drastically enhances the symmetry energy compared to pure nucleonic matter from the NL-RMF model. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star crusts and core-collapse supernova matter, where low-density clustering and condensation effects are important, and provide constraints for comparison with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions such as those by Wada and Kowalski (2010).
