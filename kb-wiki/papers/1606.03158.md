# Quark-Meson Coupling model based upon the Nambu-Jona Lasinio model

> The NJL model for the octet baryons, using proper time regularization to simulate some of the features of confinement, is solved self-consistently in nuclear matter. This provides an alternative framework to the MIT bag model which has been used in the quark-meson coupling model. After fitting the parameters of the model to the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter the model is used to explore the equation of state of pure neutron matter as well as nuclear matter at densities relevant to heavy ion collisions. With a view to future studies of high mass neutron stars, the binding of hyperons is also explored.

## Key claim

The NJL model with proper time regularization can serve as an alternative to the MIT bag model for describing baryon structure within the quark-meson coupling framework, yielding a slightly softer equation of state for nuclear matter than the bag model parametrizations used in earlier work by the same authors.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the octet baryons in the quark-diquark picture using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with proper time regularization, where an infra-red cut-off simulates quark confinement. The baryon masses were computed as functions of the mean scalar field in nuclear matter and parametrized using a quadratic form, then used in a Hartree-Fock calculation of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter including Dirac and Pauli terms for vector mesons. The model parameters were fitted to reproduce the pion decay constant, pion mass, nucleon axial charge, and saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Infra-red cut-off: Λ_IR = 240 MeV; light constituent quark mass: M_ℓ = 400 MeV
- Nucleon mass in vacuum: M_N = 0.94 GeV; Σ mass: M_Σ = 1.23222 GeV; Ξ mass: M_Ξ = 1.32 GeV; Λ mass: M_Λ = 1.118 GeV
- σ-N coupling in the NJL model: g_σN^NJL = 12.9852; scalar polarisability: d = 1.39786 GeV^{-1}
- Incompressibility at saturation for the standard scenario: K_0 = 263 MeV; slope of symmetry energy: L_0 = 81 MeV
- Λ optical potential in the standard scenario: U_Λ = -5 MeV; Σ^- optical potential: U_Σ^- = 27 MeV; Ξ^- optical potential: U_Ξ^- = -5 MeV
- In the Hartree scenario: K_0 = 261 MeV; U_Λ = -55 MeV; U_Σ^- = -17 MeV; U_Ξ^- = -26 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model by replacing the MIT bag model description of hadron structure with the NJL model, following the approach of Bentz and Thomas (2001) who first hadronised the NJL model for this purpose. The results complement earlier work by the same authors (Whittenbury et al., 2014; Whittenbury et al., 2015) that used bag model parametrisations including one gluon exchange, and the new NJL-based equation of state is softer than those previous results, which may lead to lower maximum neutron star masses unless a transition to quark matter occurs.
