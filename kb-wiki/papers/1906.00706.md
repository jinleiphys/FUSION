# Shell model results for $^{47-58}$Ca isotopes in the $fp$, $fpg_{9/2}$ and $fpg_{9/2}d_{5/2}$ model spaces

> We have reported shell-model results for $^{47-58}$Ca isotopes in the $fp$, $fpg_{9/2}$ and $fpg_{9/2}d_{5/2}$ model spaces using realistic $NN$ interaction. We have also performed a systematic shell-model study using interactions derived from in-medium similarity-renormalization group (IM-SRG) targeted for a particular nucleus with chiral $NN$ and $3N$ forces. The results obtained are in a reasonable agreement with the available experimental data in $fp$ model space with $NN$ interaction. It is shown that the $g_{9/2}$ and $d_{5/2}$ orbitals play an important role for heavier neutron-rich $^{54-58}$Ca isotopes, while it is marginal for $^{47-52}$Ca. We have also examined spectroscopic factor strengths using $NN$ and $NN+3N$ interactions for recently available experimental data.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using realistic NN interactions in the fp, fpg9/2, and fpg9/2d5/2 model spaces achieve reasonable agreement with experimental data for 47-52Ca, but the g9/2 and d5/2 orbitals become important only for the heavier neutron-rich 54-58Ca isotopes, while their role is marginal for 47-52Ca.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations using the KSHELL code with the GXPF1Br+VMU interaction in three model spaces (fp, fpg9/2, and fpg9/2d5/2) without truncation, and also used interactions derived from the in-medium similarity-renormalization group (IM-SRG) with chiral NN and 3N forces. For the fpg9/2 model space, they also tested a modified single-particle energy for the g9/2 orbital, increasing it by 2 MeV. The maximum dimension diagonalized was 2.7 x 10^8 for the ground state of 58Ca.

## Key numbers

- The first excited 2+ state in 54Ca is predicted at 2.689 MeV in the fpg9/2d5/2 space and at 2.569 MeV in the fpg9/2 space, compared to the experimental value of 2.043 MeV
- The IM-SRG calculation for 54Ca predicts the first excited 2+ state at 3.352 MeV, much higher than the experimental energy
- For 48Ca, the B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) value is 10.35 e^2fm^4 (fp), 10.50 e^2fm^4 (fpg9/2), and 10.60 e^2fm^4 (fpg9/2d5/2), compared to the experimental value of 19 +/- 6.4 e^2fm^4
- For 56Ca, the fpg9/2d5/2 calculation predicts a 2+ state at about 2.5 MeV excitation energy
- For 58Ca, the 2+ state is predicted around 3 MeV excitation energy
- The spectroscopic factor C^2S for the 48Ca -> 47Ca transition to the lowest 7/2- state is 7.5 (IM-SRG) and ~7.7 (GXPF1Br+VMU), compared to the experimental value of 6.4(+0.8/-0.9)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich Ca isotopes by Holt et al. (2012) using many-body perturbation theory with NN+3N forces in the pfg9/2 model space, which produced compressed energy spectra. The present calculations improve upon those results by obtaining reasonable energy spectra for natural parity states up to 54Ca and correctly predicting both N=32 and N=34 subshell closures, whereas the earlier MBPT calculations gave 0+ as the first excited state in 48Ca and 54Ca contrary to experiment. The results enable future experimental studies of 55-58Ca, for which only ground state spins and parities are currently known, and provide theoretical predictions for spectroscopic factors in 52Ca -> 51Ca and 54Ca -> 53Ca transitions that can be tested by upcoming experiments.
