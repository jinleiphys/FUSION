# Monopole Excitation to Cluster States

> We discuss strength of monopole excitation of the ground state to cluster states in light nuclei. We clarify that the monopole excitation to cluster states is in general strong as to be comparable with the single particle strength and shares an appreciable portion of the sum rule value in spite of large difference of the structure between the cluster state and the shell-model-like ground state. We argue that the essential reasons of the large strength are twofold. One is the fact that the clustering degree of freedom is possessed even by simple shell model wave functions. The detailed feature of this fact is described by the so-called Bayman-Bohr theorem which tells us that SU(3) shell model wave function is equivalent to cluster model wave function. The other is the ground state correlation induced by the activation of the cluster degrees of freedom described by the Bayman-Bohr theorem. We demonstrate, by deriving analytical expressions of monopole matrix elements, that the order of magnitude of the monopole strength is governed by the first reason, while the second reason plays a sufficient role in reproducing the data up to the factor of magnitude of the monopole strength. Our explanation is made by analysing three examples which are the monopole excitations to the $0^+_2$ and $0^+_3$ states in $^{16}$O and the one to the $0^+_2$ state in $^{12}$C. The present results imply that the measurement of strong monopole transitions or excitations is in general very useful for the study of cluster states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large monopole excitation strength from the ground state to cluster states in light nuclei arises from two reasons: first, the clustering degree of freedom is already present in simple shell model wave functions via the Bayman-Bohr theorem, which governs the order of magnitude of the strength; second, ground state correlations induced by activation of those cluster degrees of freedom play a sufficient role in reproducing the data up to the factor of magnitude of the monopole strength.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for monopole matrix elements between the ground state and cluster states in $^{16}$O and $^{12}$C using the Bayman-Bohr theorem, which establishes equivalence between SU(3) shell model wave functions and cluster model wave functions. For $^{16}$O, they used microscopic $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster wave functions to analyze transitions to the $0^+_2$ and $0^+_3$ states. For $^{12}$C, they used the THSR wave function to analyze the transition to the Hoyle state ($0^+_2$).

## Key numbers

- $^{16}$O: monopole matrix element $M({\rm E0})$ between ground state and $0^+_2$ state at $E_x = 6.05$ MeV is $3.55 \pm 0.21$ fm$^2$
- $^{16}$O: $M({\rm E0})$ between ground state and $0^+_3$ state at $E_x = 12.05$ MeV is $4.03 \pm 0.09$ fm$^2$
- $^{12}$C: $M({\rm E0})$ between ground state and $0^+_2$ state (Hoyle state) at $E_x = 7.66$ MeV is $5.4 \pm 0.2$ fm$^2$
- Single particle strength estimate for $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei is $(3/5)R^2 \sim 5.4$ fm$^2$ (with $R \sim 3.0$ fm)
- Energy weighted strengths as fraction of sum rule: in $^{16}$O about 3% for $0^+_2$ and 8% for $0^+_3$; in $^{12}$C about 16% for $0^+_2$
- Analytical expression for $^{16}$O $0^+_2$ transition: $M({\rm E0},0_2^+ - 0_1^+) = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\tau_{0,4}/\tau_{0,6}} \, \eta_6 \, \langle R_{40}(r,\nu_N) | r^2 | R_{60}(r,\nu_N) \rangle$, where $\langle R_{40}(r,\nu_N) | r^2 | R_{60}(r,\nu_N) \rangle = \sqrt{21/8} \, (1/\nu_N)$
- Analytical expression for $^{16}$O $0^+_3$ transition: $M({\rm E0},0_3^+ - 0_1^+) = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\tau_{2,4}/\tau_{2,6}} \, \zeta_6 \, \langle R_{42}(r,\nu_N) | r^2 | R_{62}(r,\nu_N) \rangle$, where $\langle R_{42}(r,\nu_N) | r^2 | R_{62}(r,\nu_N) \rangle = (3/2)(1/\nu_N)$
- Total monopole strength from doubly closed shell wave function in $^{16}$O: $\sqrt{69/32} \, (1/\nu_N) = 1.47/\nu_N$
- Monopole strength of the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ cluster wave function absorbing total monopole strength: $\sqrt{45/32} \, (1/\nu_N) = 1.19/\nu_N$, about 80% of total

## Context

This work extends the understanding of monopole transitions in light nuclei by providing a theoretical explanation for why cluster states exhibit large monopole strengths comparable to single particle strength, despite having very different structure from the shell-model-like ground state. The analysis builds on the Bayman-Bohr theorem (Bayman and Bohr, 1960s) and the SU(3) shell model of Elliott (1958), and it uses cluster model calculations such as the $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ OCM calculation by Suzuki and the $3\alpha$ RGM calculation by Kamimura and Uegaki. The results enable the use of strong monopole transitions as a general experimental tool for identifying cluster states, as already practiced by Kawabata and collaborators in studies of $^{11}$B.
