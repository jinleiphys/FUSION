# Kinetic, time irreversible evolution of the unstable $π^{\pm}$ - meson

> In Liouville formalism the expression for density matrix, determining the time evolution of unstable $π^{\pm}$ - meson in the framework of unified formulation of quantum and kinetic dynamics is defined. The eigenvalues problem is investigated in the framework of Prigogine's principles of description of nonequilibrium processes at microscopic level. The problem was solved on the basis of complex spectral representation. It was shown that the approach leads to Pauli master equation for the weakly interacting system.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Brussels-Austin group's complex spectral representation approach, which introduces time ordering through infinitesimal imaginary terms in denominators, can be applied to the relativistic quantum field theory description of charged pion decay to yield the standard decay rate and a strictly exponential time evolution, provided one works outside the Hilbert space with Gamow vectors.

## Method

The author formulates the weak interaction Hamiltonian for the decay \(\pi^{\pm} \rightarrow l^{\pm} + \nu_l (\tilde{\nu}_l)\) and solves the complex eigenvalue problem for the Hamiltonian using perturbation theory with "delayed analytic continuation" to avoid Poincare divergences. The Liouville formalism is then employed, where the density matrix evolution is decomposed into "subdynamics" components using spectral projectors constructed from the complex eigenstates of the Liouvillian, leading to a kinetic equation for the diagonal "privileged" component.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for weak interaction is \(G \approx 10^{-5}/m_p^{2}\), where \(m_p\) is the proton mass.
- The pion decay constant is \(f_{\pi} = 0.91 m_{\pi}\), where \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass.
- For a pion at rest, the decay width is \(\gamma_{\mathbf{p}_{\pi}=0} = \frac{1}{2}\Gamma = \frac{1}{2}\frac{G^{2}f_{\pi}^{2}}{8\pi}m_{\pi}m_{\mu}^{2}\left(1-\frac{m_{\mu}^{2}}{m_{\pi}^{2}}\right)^{2}\), yielding a lifetime \(\tau_0 = 1/\Gamma \approx 2.6 \times 10^{-8}\) s.
- For a moving pion, the lifetime increases with momentum: at \(|\mathbf{p}|_{\pi} = 0.5\) GeV, \(\tau \approx 9.8 \times 10^{-8}\) s; at \(|\mathbf{p}|_{\pi} = 1.5\) GeV, \(\tau \approx 2.8 \times 10^{-7}\) s; at \(|\mathbf{p}|_{\pi} = 3\) GeV, \(\tau \approx 5.7 \times 10^{-7}\) s.

## Context

This work extends the Brussels-Austin group's formalism (Prigogine, Petrosky, and co-workers) for treating irreversible dynamics at the microscopic level from the simple Friedrichs model to a realistic relativistic quantum field theory model of charged pion decay. The approach yields the standard decay rate found in textbooks (e.g., the work cited as "wb") and demonstrates agreement with Einstein time dilation for moving pions, while also providing a derivation of the Pauli master equation for the weakly interacting system. This enables a unified framework connecting quantum dynamics with kinetic, time-irreversible evolution for unstable particles in nuclear and particle physics.
