# Neutrino-nucleus scattering reexamined: Quasielastic scattering and pion production entanglement and implications for neutrino energy reconstruction

> We apply the GiBUU model to questions relevant for current and future neutrino long-baseline experiments, we address in particular the relevance of charged-current reactions for neutrino disappearance experiments. A correct identification of charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) events - which is the signal channel in oscillation experiments - is relevant for the neutrino energy reconstruction and thus for the oscillation result. We show that about 20% of the quasielastic cross section is misidentified in present-day experiments and has to be corrected for by means of event generators. Furthermore, we show that also a significant part of 1pi+ (> 40%) events is misidentified as CCQE mainly caused by the pion absorption in the nucleus. We also discuss the dependence of both of these numbers on experimental detection thresholds. We further investigate the influence of final-state interactions on the neutrino energy reconstruction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that about 20% of events identified as charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) in present-day neutrino oscillation experiments are actually misidentified non-CCQE events (mainly from pion production followed by pion absorption), and that over 40% of true CC1π⁺ events are misidentified as CCQE due to pion absorption in the nucleus, both fractions depending on experimental detection thresholds.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model, which combines an initial neutrino-nucleon interaction within a local Thomas-Fermi approximation (including Pauli blocking and mean-field potentials) with a coupled-channel semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) for particles propagating through the nucleus. They simulated charged-current νμ scattering on carbon at neutrino energies between 0.5 and 1.5 GeV, classifying events according to the detection criteria of generic Cherenkov detectors (e.g., MiniBooNE) and tracking detectors (e.g., SciBooNE, K2K SciFi), and then reconstructed the neutrino energy from the muon kinematics using the standard quasifree two-body formula.

## Key numbers

- About 20% of the CCQE-like cross section in a Cherenkov detector consists of fake (non-CCQE) events, mainly from initial Δ excitation followed by pion absorption or pion-less decay (Figure 2, top panel).
- Less than 60% of true CC1π⁺ pions leave the nucleus and can be detected even at zero pion kinetic energy threshold; this fraction decreases rapidly with increasing threshold (Figure 5, top panel).
- For true CCQE events at Eνreal = 1.0 GeV, the reconstructed energy has an expected value of 1.03 GeV with a standard deviation of 0.15 GeV (15%); for CCQE-like events in a Cherenkov detector, the expected value is 0.96 GeV with a standard deviation of 0.22 GeV (23%) (Table I).
- For CC1π⁺ events after FSI at Eνreal = 1.0 GeV, the reconstructed energy has an expected value of 0.95 GeV with a standard deviation of 0.19 GeV (20%) (Table II).
- The CCQE-like cross section in a tracking detector underestimates the true CCQE cross section by about 20% even at zero proton threshold, due to charge-exchange and multi-proton knockout (Figure 2, bottom panel; Figure 3, top panel).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier GiBUU studies of neutrino-induced pion production (Leitner et al., 2008) and addresses the entanglement of CCQE and CC1π⁺ channels that affects neutrino energy reconstruction in long-baseline experiments such as MiniBooNE and K2K. The results contradict simpler treatments that consider only CCQE in the initial state and neglect Δ excitation and FSI, such as those by Blondel et al. (2004) and Butkevich (2008), which found smaller discrepancies. The paper enables improved event generator corrections for oscillation experiments and highlights the need for state-of-the-art descriptions of πNΔ dynamics in nuclei.
