# Topological production of charmonia with event-shape engineering in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV using PYTHIA8

> The production of heavy quarks (charm and beauty) in high-energy hadronic and nuclear collisions provides an excellent testing ground for the theory of strong interactions and validates models based on quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In this work, prompt and nonprompt production of $\rm{J/}ψ$ in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV are studied as a function of transverse spherocity using PYTHIA8. $\rm{J/}ψ$ is reconstructed via its electromagnetic decay to dielectrons and dimuons, in mid- and forward-rapidity, respectively. Transverse spherocity, an event shape observable, is used to distinguish hard QCD events from the softer, isotropic ones. In PYTHIA8, the production of $\rm{J/}ψ$ can be influenced by the average number of multiple parton interactions ($\langle N_{\rm mpi} \rangle$), owing to the underlying events (UE), which have a dominant contribution to particle production at lower transverse momentum. Since transverse spherocity is correlated to $\langle N_{\rm mpi} \rangle$, this can serve as an experimentally accessible tool for event selection to study the underlying QCD processes influencing the prompt and nonprompt $\rm{J/}ψ$ production. This study reveals the correlation between heavy-flavor production dynamics and topological event selection in $pp$ collisions using PYTHIA8, whose relevance awaits experimental validation.

# Topological production of charmonia with event-shape engineering in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV using PYTHIA8

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that transverse spherocity ($S_0$) can serve as an experimentally accessible event-shape classifier to separate prompt and non-prompt J/$\psi$ production mechanisms in $pp$ collisions, but the authors explicitly state that the relevance of these PYTHIA8-based findings "awaits experimental validation."

## Method

The authors generated 10 billion minimum-bias $pp$ collision events at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV using PYTHIA8 with the 4C tune, enabling all charmonia and bottomonia production processes. J/$\psi$ mesons were reconstructed via the dielectron decay channel at midrapidity ($|y| < 0.9$) and the dimuon decay channel at forward rapidity ($2.5 < y < 4$). Events were classified into jetty (lowest 20% of transverse spherocity values) and isotropic (highest 20% of transverse spherocity values) categories, with spherocity calculated from charged tracks within $|\eta| < 0.8$ and $p_{\rm T} > 0.15$ GeV/$c$.

## Key numbers

- The yield of non-prompt J/$\psi$ is approximately ten times smaller than that of prompt J/$\psi$ in the low $p_{\rm T}$ regime for both rapidity regions.
- The partonic modification factor $Q_{\rm pp}$ for non-prompt J/$\psi$ at midrapidity attains its peak (for isotropic events) or valley (for jetty events) at approximately 5 GeV/$c$, compared to a later peak for prompt J/$\psi$.
- At forward rapidity, $Q_{\rm pp}$ from isotropic and jetty events has its first crossing at around 3-4 GeV/$c$ for both prompt and non-prompt J/$\psi$, with a second crossing at approximately 10 GeV/$c$ for non-prompt J/$\psi$.
- The non-prompt J/$\psi$ fraction $f_{\rm B}$ at midrapidity shows a crossing between jetty and isotropic events at $p_{\rm T} \approx 6$ GeV/$c$, while at forward rapidity this crossing occurs at $p_{\rm T} \approx 8$ GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the ALICE Collaboration (ALICE 2012, 2020, 2021, 2025) on multiplicity-dependent J/$\psi$ production, which observed a steeper-than-linear increase of self-normalized J/$\psi$ yield with charged-particle density. The authors address autocorrelation biases inherent in multiplicity-based event classification, which were discussed by Weber (2018) and ALICE (2025), by using transverse spherocity as an alternative classifier that correlates with the average number of multiple parton interactions. The study builds on prior PYTHIA8 investigations by Deb (2018) showing that inclusive J/$\psi$ yield increases linearly with $N_{\rm mpi}$, and by Weber (2018) demonstrating that color reconnection enhances prompt J/$\psi$ production in high-multiplicity events. The results enable future experimental comparisons with LHC data from ALICE and LHCb to validate the topological selection approach for separating prompt and non-prompt charmonium production.
