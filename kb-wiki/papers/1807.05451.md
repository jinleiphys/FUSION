# Hydrodynamic Fluctuations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a novel approach to the treatment of thermal fluctuations in the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic simulation MUSIC. The phenomenological impact of thermal fluctuations on hadronic observables is investigated using the IP-Glasma + hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade hybrid approach. The anisotropic flow observed in heavy-ion collision experiments is mostly attributed to the hydrodynamic response to the event-by-event collision geometry and to the sub-nucleon quantum fluctuations. However, hydrodynamic fluctuations are present during the dynamical evolution of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) and are quantified by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. They can leave their imprint on final-state observables. By analyzing the thermal noise mode-by-mode, we provide a consistent scheme of treating these fluctuations as the source terms for hydrodynamic fields. These source terms are then evolved together with hydrodynamic equations of motion. Such a treatment captures the non-perturbative nature of the evolution for these thermal fluctuations.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations during the hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma can leave an imprint on final-state hadronic observables, but for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV the effect on lower-order anisotropic flow harmonics (v2, v3, v4) is not statistically significant; the main impact appears in higher harmonics (v5, v6) and in event-plane correlators.

## Method

The authors implemented stochastic source terms for thermal fluctuations in the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC, treating the noise term S^μν with its own evolution equation coupled to the shear relaxation time τ_π. The random noise ξ^μν was sampled from a Gaussian distribution with variance given by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, then Fourier-transformed, filtered by removing all wavenumber modes above a local cutoff p_cut = 0.6/τ_π, and inverse-transformed back to coordinate space before being evolved with the hydrodynamic equations. The full hybrid framework used IP-Glasma initial conditions, hydrodynamics, Cooper-Frye particlization at 145 MeV, and the UrQMD hadronic cascade.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio was fixed at 0.095 for the study.
- The cutoff wavenumber for noise filtering was set to p_cut = 0.6/τ_π, where τ_π is the shear-relaxation time.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, thermal fluctuations produced a statistically significant increase in v5 and v6, while v2, v3, and v4 showed no statistically significant effect.
- The event-plane correlator cos[4(Ψ2 − Ψ4)] showed decorrelation due to thermal fluctuations for charged hadrons with 0.5 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 2.0 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of thermal fluctuations in simplified Bjorken flow (Kapusta, 2011) and Gubser flow (Yan, 2015), as well as more realistic simulations that treated fluctuations as perturbations (Young, 2014) or used smearing of noise terms (Sakai, 2017). The mode-by-mode analysis provides a consistent scheme for noise evolution that captures non-perturbative effects, and the authors note this technique could be used to study critical fluctuations relevant to the QCD critical point search. The framework enables future studies of small collision systems where thermal fluctuations are expected to be more dominant, and of rapidity-dependent two-particle correlations using non-boost-invariant initial conditions.
