# On lower bound of relaxation time for massless fluid in presence of magnetic field

> Due to lowest possible quantum fluctuation, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio never reach to zero, rather has some lower bound (0.08 approx). Here, we have attempted to link our calculation, based on relaxation time approximation for massless relativistic fluid, with that value and interestingly found a lower and also an upper limit of relaxation time.

# On lower bound of relaxation time for massless fluid in presence of magnetic field

## Key claim
The authors derive analytic expressions for the relaxation time of a massless relativistic fluid in a magnetic field at which the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio reaches the KSS bound of 1/(4π). The relaxation time has both a lower and an upper bound within a temperature-magnetic field domain where the KSS bound can be achieved, while outside this domain the bound cannot be reached.

## Method
The authors start from the standard kinetic theory expression for shear viscosity in the relaxation time approximation, then introduce an effective relaxation time that combines the collision time τ_c and the magnetic time scale τ_B = ω/(eB). They compute the shear viscosity component η₂ and entropy density s for massless bosonic and fermionic fluids, set η₂/s = 1/(4π), and solve the resulting quadratic equation for τ_c as a function of temperature T and magnetic field B.

## Key numbers
- The KSS bound for η/s is 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08.
- For a massless fluid without magnetic field, η/s = τ_c T/5, giving τ_c⁰ = 5/(4π T) at the KSS bound.
- For bosons in a magnetic field, the condition for reaching the KSS bound requires T ≥ [(ζ(3)/ζ(4)) × (5 eB)/(6π)]^(1/2).
- For fermions in a magnetic field, the condition requires T ≥ [(2ζ(3))/(7ζ(4)) × (5 eB)/(6π)]^(1/2).
- For Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics, the condition is T ≥ (5 eB/(6π))^(1/2).

## Context
This work extends the KSS bound analysis (Kovtun, Son, Starinets, 2005) to massless relativistic fluids in the presence of a magnetic field, building on prior calculations of shear viscosity in magnetic fields by the same group (Dey et al., 2019). The analytic expressions for τ_c(T,B) provide a framework for identifying temperature-magnetic field regimes where a fluid can approach perfect fluidity, and define both lower and upper bounds on the relaxation time within the allowed domain.
