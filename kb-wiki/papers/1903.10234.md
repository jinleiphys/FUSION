# Excited-state quantum phase transitions in systems with two degrees of freedom: III. Interacting boson systems

> The series of articles [Ann. Phys. 345, 73 (2014) and 356, 57 (2015)] devoted to excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) in systems with $f=2$ degrees of freedom is continued by studying the interacting boson model of nuclear collective dynamics as an example of a truly many-body system. The intrinsic Hamiltonian formalism with angular momentum fixed to $L=0$ is used to produce a generic first-order ground-state quantum phase transition with an adjustable energy barrier between the competing equilibrium configurations. The associated ESQPTs are shown to result from various classical stationary points of the model Hamiltonian, whose analysis is more complex than in previous cases because of (i) a non-trivial decomposition to kinetic and potential energy terms and (ii) the boundedness of the associated classical phase space. Finite-size effects resulting from a partial separability of both degrees of freedom are analyzed. The features studied here are inherent in a great majority of interacting boson systems.

## Key claim

The paper extends the analysis of excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) to the interacting boson model (IBM) as a genuine many-body system with two degrees of freedom, showing that the associated singularities arise from classical stationary points whose analysis is more complex than in previous cases due to coordinate-dependent kinetic terms and a bounded classical phase space.

## Method

The authors employ the intrinsic Hamiltonian formalism with angular momentum fixed to L=0 to describe a first-order ground-state quantum phase transition between spherical and axially deformed configurations in the IBM. They construct the classical limit of the bosonic Hamiltonian via a Holstein-Primakoff transformation and coherent-state methods, then analyze stationary points of the resulting quartic Hamiltonian in the compact phase-space domain. The smoothed level density and level flow are computed semiclassically to identify ESQPT signatures, and finite-size effects from partial separability of the two degrees of freedom are examined.

## Key numbers

- The classical phase space is the interior of a 2f-dimensional sphere with radius R0 = sqrt(2), where f = n is the number of active degrees of freedom.
- The condition for the phase-space domain is R^2 = sum_{k=1}^n (q_k^2 + p_k^2) ≤ 2.
- For systems with f=2, non-degenerate stationary points cause singularities in the first derivative of the smoothed level density, with behavior proportional to either a step function Θ(E - E_c^λ) for r = 0,2,4 or a logarithmic divergence ln|E - E_c^λ| for r = 1,3, where r is the index of the stationary point.
- The boundary-induced singularity exponent I = (2f-1)/K + 1/M - 1, where for the most generic case K=2 and M=1/2, a divergent derivative of order f+1 in the smoothed level density is expected.

## Context

This work continues the series initiated in Ann. Phys. 345, 73 (2014) (part I) and Ann. Phys. 356, 57 (2015) (part II), which analyzed ESQPTs in systems with two degrees of freedom but with simpler kinetic terms and unbounded phase spaces. The present study extends that framework to the IBM, where earlier ESQPT work by Cejnar (2006), Heinze (2006), and Macek (2006) focused on the U(5)-O(6) transition with second-order ground-state QPT, while Zhang (2016, 2017) studied the U(5)-SU(3) transition with first-order QPT using a consistent-Q Hamiltonian. The current approach uses the intrinsic formalism of Kirson (1985) and Leviatan (1987) to provide an adjustable energy barrier, enabling a more generic analysis of ESQPT effects in first-order QPTs. The findings are relevant to the majority of interacting boson systems and enable future study of ESQPTs in other many-body models with compact phase spaces and non-separable kinetic terms.
