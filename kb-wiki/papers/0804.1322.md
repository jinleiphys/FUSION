# Current conservation, screening and the magnetic moment of the $Δ$ resonance. -- 1. Formulation without quark degrees of freedom

> The pion-nucleon bremsstrahlung $π+N\Longrightarrowγ'+π'+N'$ is studied in a new form of current conservation. According to this condition, the internal and external particle radiation parts of the $πN$ radiation amplitude have opposite signs, i.e., they contain terms which must cancel each other. Therefore, one has a screening of the internal and external particle radiation in the $πN$ bremsstrahlung. In particular, it is shown that the double $Δ$ exchange diagram with the $Δ-γ' Δ'$ vertex cancel against the appropriate longitudinal part of the external particle radiation diagrams. Consequently, a model independent relation between the magnetic dipole moments of the $Δ^+$ and $Δ^{++}$ resonances and the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton $μ_p$ is obtained, where $μ_Δ$ is expressed by $μ_p$ as $μ_{Δ^+}={{M_Δ}\over {m_p}} μ_p$ and $μ_{Δ^{++}}={3\over 2}μ_{Δ^+}$ in agreement with the values extracted from the fit for the experimental cross section of the $π^+ p\toγ'π^+ p$ reaction.

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent relation between the magnetic dipole moments of the Δ⁺ and Δ⁺⁺ resonances and the anomalous magnetic moment of the proton, based on a new form of current conservation for the πN bremsstrahlung amplitude. This relation holds because the double Δ exchange diagram with the Δ-γ'Δ' vertex cancels against the appropriate longitudinal part of the external particle radiation diagrams, a screening effect not accounted for in previous low-energy photon theorem approaches.

## Method

The authors derived a modified Ward-Takahashi identity for the on-shell πN bremsstrahlung amplitude, decomposing the total amplitude into external particle radiation diagrams (Fig. 1) and internal particle radiation diagrams (Fig. 2A). They then performed a series of decompositions, over nucleon and antinucleon exchange parts, longitudinal and transverse parts, and partial wave decomposition, to isolate the spin-3/2, isospin-3/2 Δ resonance contributions. The double Δ exchange amplitude with an on-mass-shell Δ-γΔ vertex (Fig. 2B) was constructed unambiguously using a 3D time-ordered field theoretical approach without effective Lagrangians, avoiding off-mass-shell Δ ambiguities.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic dipole moment of Δ⁺ is given by μ_Δ⁺ = (M_Δ / m_p) μ_p, where M_Δ is the effective complex mass of the Δ resonance and m_p is the proton mass.
* The magnetic dipole moment of Δ⁺⁺ is given by μ_Δ⁺⁺ = (3/2) μ_Δ⁺.
* Previous model-dependent extractions of μ_Δ⁺⁺ from experimental data range from 4.7–6.9 μ_B (low-energy photon theorem, Nefkens) to 5.6–7.5 μ_B (potential models, Wittman) and 4.5 ± 0.95 μ_B (Boss).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Low (1958) and subsequent low-energy photon theorem formulations (Mink, Ding, Lin) for πN bremsstrahlung, but goes beyond the infrared limit k' → 0 to derive exact current conservation valid for any final photon energy. The key advance is the identification of the screening mechanism where the internal Δ radiation amplitude cancels against the longitudinal part of the external radiation amplitude, a relation not recognized in earlier treatments by Ding and others that used Brodsky-Brown identities in tree approximation. This enables a model-independent determination of Δ magnetic moments from the known proton anomalous magnetic moment, and the formalism can be applied to other reactions with conserved currents such as pion photoproduction, Compton scattering, and processes with external vector mesons.
