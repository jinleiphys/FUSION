# A stable and causal model of magnetohydrodynamics

> We formulate the theory of first-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics in an arbitrary hydrodynamic frame under the assumption of parity-invariance and discrete charge symmetry. We study the mode spectrum of Alfvén and magnetosonic waves as well as the spectrum of gapped excitations and derive constraints on the transport coefficients such that generic equilibrium states with constant magnetic fields are stable and causal under linearised perturbations. We solve these constraints for a specific equation of state and show that there exists a large family of hydrodynamic frames that renders the linear fluctuations stable and causal. This theory does not require introducing new dynamical degrees of freedom and therefore is a promising and simpler alternative to Müller-Israel-Stewart-type theories. Together with a detailed analysis of transport, entropy production and Kubo formulae, the theory presented here is well suited for studying dissipative effects in various contexts ranging from heavy-ion collisions to astrophysics.

## Key claim
The authors formulate a first-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) theory in an arbitrary hydrodynamic frame that can be made stable and causal under linear perturbations without introducing new dynamical degrees of freedom, unlike Müller-Israel-Stewart-type theories. The claim is conditional on parity-invariance, discrete charge symmetry, and a specific choice of hydrodynamic frame that satisfies derived constraints on transport coefficients.

## Method
The authors recast MHD using a two-form current formalism that treats the Bianchi identity as a conservation law, avoiding explicit electric fields. They parametrize the first-order dissipative corrections to the stress tensor and two-form current in terms of 28 transport coefficients, then study linear perturbations around equilibrium states with constant magnetic fields to derive constraints from stability and causality for Alfvén and magnetosonic wave channels.

## Key numbers
- 28 transport coefficients are required at first order in a general hydrodynamic frame under parity-invariance and discrete charge symmetry
- 14 genuine frame-invariant transport coefficients exist after accounting for frame transformation freedom
- 7 independent transport coefficients remain in the Landau frame under parity-invariance and discrete charge symmetry
- 8 transport coefficients are constrained by entropy production inequalities (e.g., ζ⊥, ζ∥, r⊥, η⊥, η∥, r∥ ≥ 0)
- Onsager relations require ζ× = ζ×' from Kubo formulae

## Context
This work extends the BDNK approach (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, Kovtun 2019-2020) for stable and causal first-order relativistic hydrodynamics to the magnetohydrodynamic case, which is complicated by anisotropy from magnetic fields and three types of wave excitations. It builds on the two-form formulation of MHD developed by Armas et al. (2018) and provides an alternative to Müller-Israel-Stewart theories that avoids introducing new dynamical degrees of freedom, with potential applications to heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical plasmas.
