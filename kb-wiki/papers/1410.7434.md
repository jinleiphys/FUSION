# Alpha clusters and collective flow in ultra-relativistic carbon - heavy nucleus collisions

> We investigate ultrarelativistic collisions of the 12C nucleus with heavy targets and show that the harmonic flow measures based on ratios of cumulant moments are particularly suited to study the intrinsic deformation of the 12C wave function. That way one can probe the expected alpha clusterization in the ground state, which leads to large initial triangularity in the shape of the fireball in the transverse plane. We show that the clusterization effect results in very characteristic behavior of the ratios of the cumulant moments as functions of the number of participant nucleons, both for the elliptic and triangular deformations. Thus the experimental event-by-event studies of harmonic flow in ultrarelativistic light-heavy collisions may offer a new window to look at the ground-state structure of light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ratios of four-particle to two-particle cumulant moments of harmonic flow in ultra-relativistic carbon-heavy nucleus collisions can serve as a clean probe of alpha clusterization in the ground state of 12C, provided the analysis is restricted to high-multiplicity events (centrality below 10%) where the geometric signal dominates over random fluctuations.

## Method

The authors generate Monte Carlo configurations of 12C with three alpha clusters arranged in an equilateral triangle, with parameters fitted to reproduce the one-body radial density from Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC) or Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations. These configurations are used in Glauber-model simulations of 12C collisions with 197Au or 208Pb at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, with the fireball formed from wounded nucleons and binary collisions. The initial-state eccentricities are computed event-by-event, and the ratios of four-particle to two-particle cumulant moments of eccentricity are evaluated as proxies for the corresponding flow ratios, relying on the approximate linear relation between flow coefficients and initial eccentricities.

## Key numbers

- Edge length of the equilateral triangle of alpha clusters: l = 3.05 fm (BEC case) or 2.84 fm (VMC case)
- RMS radius of each alpha cluster: r_alpha = 0.96 fm (BEC case) or 1.15 fm (VMC case)
- Short-distance NN repulsion cutoff: 0.9 fm
- Smearing width for sources in the transverse plane: sigma = 0.4 fm
- Inelastic NN cross sections used: 32 mb at SPS (sqrt(s_NN)=17 GeV), 42 mb at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), 73 mb at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5200 GeV)
- For centralities 10%, 1%, and 0.1% in C-Au collisions at RHIC, the average impact parameters are 2.4 fm, 1.5 fm, and 1.2 fm, respectively
- The hydrodynamic response coefficient kappa_3 increases by 50% from N_w=30 to N_w=80

## Context

This work extends the method proposed in Broniowski (2013) for probing alpha clusterization via harmonic flow, providing a more robust observable (ratios of cumulant moments) that eliminates sensitivity to the unknown hydrodynamic response coefficients. The approach contrasts with earlier studies of flow in small systems such as d-Au and p-Pb collisions (Bozek 2011, Adare 2013), and enables experimental tests of the cluster structure of light nuclei using standard flow analysis techniques at RHIC and LHC. The key result is that for 12C on heavy targets, the ratio epsilon_3{4}/epsilon_3{2} increases with multiplicity for clustered configurations while decreasing for uniform distributions, offering a clear experimental signature.
