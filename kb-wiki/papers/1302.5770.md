# Asymmetry in the neutrino and anti-neutrino reactions in a nuclear medium

> We study the effect of the density-dependent axial and vector form factors on the electro-neutrino ($ν_e$) and anti-neutrino $({\bar ν}_e)$ reactions for a nucleon in nuclear matter or in $^{12}$C. The nucleon form factors in free space are presumed to be modified for a bound nucleon in a nuclear medium. We adopt the density-dependent form factors calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, and apply them to the $ν_e$ and ${\bar ν}_e$ induced reactions with the initial energy $E = $ 8 $\sim$ 80 MeV. We find that the total $ν_e$ cross sections on $^{12}$C as well as a nucleon in nuclear matter are reduced by about 5% at the nuclear saturation density, $ρ_0$. This reduction is caused by the modification of the nucleon structure in matter. Although the density effect for both cases is relatively small, it is comparable with the effect of Coulomb distortion on the outgoing lepton in the $ν$-reaction. In contrast, the density effect on the ${\bar ν}_e$ reaction reduces the cross section significantly in both nuclear matter and $^{12}$C cases, and the amount maximally becomes of about 35% around $ρ_0$. Such large asymmetry in the $ν_e$ and ${\bar ν}_e$ cross sections, which seems to be nearly independent of the target, is originated from the difference in the helicities of ${\bar ν}_e$ and $ν_e$. It is expected that the asymmetry influences the r-process and also the neutrino-process nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The density-dependent modification of nucleon weak form factors in nuclear matter, calculated via the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, produces a large asymmetry between neutrino and anti-neutrino cross sections: the anti-neutrino cross section is reduced by up to about 35% at saturation density, while the neutrino cross section is reduced by only about 5% at most, with the asymmetry originating from the helicity difference between the two particles.

## Method

The authors adopted density-dependent axial and vector form factors for a bound nucleon, calculated self-consistently using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which accounts for the modification of nucleon internal structure in nuclear matter via the scalar mean field. They applied these in-medium form factors to compute charged-current cross sections for electron-neutrino and electron-anti-neutrino reactions on a free nucleon in nuclear matter and on \(^{12}\)C, using the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) for the nuclear structure of \(^{12}\)C. The cross sections were evaluated for initial neutrino energies from 8 to 80 MeV, and the density dependence was studied by varying the nuclear density from zero up to 2.5 times the saturation density \(\rho_0\).

## Key numbers

- Total \(\nu_e\) cross sections on a nucleon in nuclear matter and on \(^{12}\)C are reduced by about 5% at the nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0\).
- Total \(\bar{\nu}_e\) cross sections on a nucleon in nuclear matter and on \(^{12}\)C are reduced by up to about 35% at \(\rho_0\).
- For the \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \rightarrow e^+ + n\) reaction in matter, the total cross section decreases by about 15% per each increase of density by \(0.5 \rho_0\).
- For the \(\nu_e + n \rightarrow e^- + p\) reaction, the variation of the cross section below \(E_\nu \sim 30\) MeV is less than 2% per each increase of density by \(0.5 \rho_0\); at \(E_\nu \sim 80\) MeV, the cross section decreases by about 5% at \(\rho_0\).
- The in-medium axial coupling constant \(g_A(\rho, Q^2)\) is reduced by 11% at \(\rho_0\) in the region of small momentum transfer.
- The vector form factor \(F_2^{V, CC}\) is enhanced by about 11% at \(\rho_0\) at small \(Q^2\).
- For the \(^{12}\)C\((\nu_e, e^-)^{12}\)N\(_{g.s. (1^+)}\) reaction, the cross section is reduced by about 5% around \(\rho_0\), comparable to the Coulomb distortion effect of about 5–6%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of density-dependent weak form factors in nuclear matter by Kim et al. (2004), which used a simple relativistic Fermi gas model for \(^{12}\)C\((\nu_\mu, \mu^-)\)X reactions, by employing the more realistic QRPA framework for \(^{12}\)C and by examining both \(\nu_e\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e\) reactions. The results provide a low-energy extension (8–80 MeV) of the cross-section ratio calculations by Kuzmin et al. (2006), which assumed energies above 100 MeV. The predicted asymmetry between neutrino and anti-neutrino cross sections in dense matter is expected to influence r-process and neutrino-process nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae, particularly affecting anti-neutrino propagation inside proto-neutron stars.
