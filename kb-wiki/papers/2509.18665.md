# Listening to the long ringdown: A novel way to pinpoint the EOS in neutron-star cores

> Gravitational waves (GWs) from binary neutron star (BNS) merger remnants complement constraints from the inspiral phase, mass-radius measurements, and microscopic theory by providing information about the neutron-star equation of state (EOS) at extreme densities. We perform general-relativistic simulations of BNS mergers using EOS models that span the uncertain high-density regime. We find a robust correlation between the ratio of energy and angular momentum lost during the late-time post-merger GW signal - the long ringdown - and the EOS at the highest densities in neutron star cores. Applying this correlation to post-merger GW signals reduces EOS uncertainty at several times saturation density, where no direct constraints currently exist.

## Key claim

The paper claims a robust correlation between the slope \(d\hat{E}_{\rm GW}/d\hat{J}_{\rm GW}\) extracted from the late-time post-merger gravitational-wave signal (the "long ringdown") and the equation of state at the highest densities in neutron star cores, specifically the central pressure and number density of maximally massive stars. This correlation is presented as a way to reduce EOS uncertainty at several times saturation density, but the results are based on simulations using a selected set of "golden" EOSs and assume future third-generation detector sensitivity.

## Method

The authors performed general-relativistic simulations of binary neutron star mergers using the FUKA and FIL codes, with fixed chirp mass \(\mathcal{M}_{\rm chirp} = 1.18\,M_\odot\) and mass ratios \(q = 0.7, 0.85, 1\). They constructed a set of six "golden" EOSs via principal component analysis of a Gaussian Process ensemble in the 4D parameter space \((M_\text{TOV},\,C_\text{TOV}, \, \ln p_\text{TOV}, \, R_{1.4})\), selecting EOSs near the extrema of the \(68\%\) credible region. From the simulated GW strain components, they computed the slope \(dE_{\rm GW}/dJ_{\rm GW}\) and instantaneous frequency \(f_{\rm GW}\) during the long ringdown phase (1–15 ms post-merger), then fitted bilinear correlations between this slope and the TOV central pressure and number density.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_{\rm sat} = 0.16\) baryons/fm\(^3\)
- Fixed chirp mass for simulations: \(\mathcal{M}_{\rm chirp} = 1.18\,M_\odot\)
- Mass ratios used: \(q = 0.7, 0.85, 1\)
- Long ringdown time window: between 1 and 15 ms post-merger
- Assumed measurement uncertainty for mock joint measurements of slope and \(f_2\): \(\pm 4\%\)
- The \(68\%\) and \(95\%\) credible intervals are reported for the bilinear fitting model correlating \(d\hat{E}_{\rm GW}/d\hat{J}_{\rm GW}\) with \(p_\mathrm{TOV}\) and \(n_\mathrm{TOV}\)

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain the neutron-star EOS using gravitational-wave signals from binary neutron star mergers, building on the tidal deformability measurements from GW170817 (Radice 2020) and spectral feature correlations (Huez 2025). The authors specifically reference their own earlier proposal (Ecker 2024) for the long ringdown analysis. The results enable future third-generation observatories (Evans 2021, Abac 2025) to directly constrain the EOS at the highest core densities, where no direct constraints currently exist, by combining the long ringdown slope with the post-merger peak frequency \(f_2\) in a Bayesian framework.
