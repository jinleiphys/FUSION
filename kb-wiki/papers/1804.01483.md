# First direct lattice-QCD calculation of the $x$-dependence of the pion parton distribution function

> We present the first direct lattice-QCD calculation of the Bjorken-$x$ dependence of the valence quark distribution of the pion. Using large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), we calculate the boosted pion state with long Wilson link operators. After implementing the one-loop matching and meson mass corrections, our result at $m_π\approx 310$ MeV is in agreement with those extracted from experimental data as well as from Dyson-Schwinger equation in small $x$ region, but a sizeable discrepancy in the large $x$ region. This discrepancy provides a nice opportunity to systematically study and disentangle the artifacts in the LaMET approach, which will eventually help to discern various existing analyses in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors present the first direct lattice-QCD calculation of the Bjorken-x dependence of the pion valence quark distribution, finding agreement with experimental data and Dyson-Schwinger equation results at small x but a sizeable discrepancy at large x, which they attribute to artifacts in the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach that require further systematic study.

## Method

The calculation uses large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) to compute the boosted pion state with long Wilson link operators on a lattice ensemble with Nf=2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by the MILC Collaboration. The authors perform nonperturbative renormalization in the regularization-independent momentum-subtraction (RI/MOM) scheme, apply one-loop matching and meson mass corrections, and use a derivative method to improve the Fourier transform from coordinate space to momentum space.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi ≈ 310 MeV, lattice spacing a = 0.12 fm, box size L ≈ 3 fm
* Pion momenta used: P_z = {0.86, 1.32, 1.74} GeV, corresponding to n = {2, 3, 4} × 2π/L
* RI/MOM renormalization scale: μ_R = 3.7 GeV, with p_z^R = 6 × 2π/L (varied between 4 and 8 for systematic error)
* Wilson line renormalization counterterm: δm = 253(3) MeV at a = 0.12 fm
* Final result presented at scale μ = 4 GeV, compared with DSE result at 5.2 GeV and ASV fit at 4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach, previously applied to nucleon PDFs (Lin 2017, Chen 2018) and meson distribution amplitudes (Zhang 2017, Chen 2017), to the first direct lattice calculation of the pion valence quark distribution. The result enables systematic study of LaMET artifacts and can help discern between conflicting theoretical predictions for the large-x behavior of the pion PDF, which range from (1-x)^2 behavior from perturbative QCD and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Hecht 2000, Nguyen 2011, Chen 2016) to linear (1-x) dependence from constituent quark models (Frederico 1994, Szczepaniak 1993) and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Shigetani 1993, Davidson 1994).
