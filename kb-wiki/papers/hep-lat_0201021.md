# Moments of Nucleon Light Cone Quark Distributions Calculated in Full Lattice QCD

> Moments of the quark density, helicity, and transversity distributions are calculated in unquenched lattice QCD. Calculations of proton matrix elements of operators corresponding to these moments through the operator product expansion have been performed on $16^3 \times 32$ lattices for Wilson fermions at $β= 5.6$ using configurations from the SESAM collaboration and at $β= 5.5$ using configurations from SCRI. One-loop perturbative renormalization corrections are included. At quark masses accessible in present calculations, there is no statistically significant difference between quenched and full QCD results, indicating that the contributions of quark-antiquark excitations from the Dirac Sea are small. Close agreement between calculations with cooled configurations containing essentially only instantons and the full gluon configurations indicates that quark zero modes associated with instantons play a dominant role. Naive linear extrapolation of the full QCD calculation to the physical pion mass yields results inconsistent with experiment. Extrapolation to the chiral limit including the physics of the pion cloud can resolve this discrepancy and the requirements for a definitive chiral extrapolation are described.

## Key claim

At the quark masses accessible in these lattice calculations, there is no statistically significant difference between quenched and full QCD results for moments of nucleon light cone quark distributions, indicating that the contributions of quark-antiquark excitations from the Dirac Sea are small; however, naive linear extrapolation of the full QCD calculation to the physical pion mass yields results inconsistent with experiment, and extrapolation including the physics of the pion cloud may resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The collaboration calculated proton matrix elements of twist-2 operators corresponding to moments of the quark density, helicity, and transversity distributions using the operator product expansion on \(16^3 \times 32\) lattices with Wilson fermions. They used unquenched configurations from the SESAM collaboration at \(\beta = 5.6\) and from SCRI at \(\beta = 5.5\), included one-loop perturbative renormalization corrections to convert results to the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme at \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), and evaluated only connected diagrams using sequential propagators with fixed source-sink separation and smeared Wuppertal sources.

## Key numbers

- The calculations were performed on \(16^3 \times 32\) lattices at \(\beta = 5.6\) (SESAM configurations) and \(\beta = 5.5\) (SCRI configurations).
- Results are presented at the scale \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme, corresponding to \(\mu^2 = 1/a^2 \sim 4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The overlap of a point source with the nucleon ground state is of order \(10^{-4}\).
- For smeared sources, a window of \(\delta t = 3\) was used for the plateau analysis; for unsmeared sources, \(\delta t = 5\) was used.

## Context

This work extends prior quenched lattice QCD calculations of moments of structure functions by the QCDSF collaboration (1990s) and others, which showed significant discrepancies with experiment (e.g., the axial charge 10-20% low and the first moment of the spin-averaged structure function ~50% high). The present study demonstrates that quenching is not the primary source of these discrepancies, contrary to earlier conjectures, and shows that instanton-associated quark zero modes play a dominant role. The results enable future work on chiral extrapolations incorporating pion cloud physics, which the authors argue can resolve the remaining discrepancies with experimental data from CTEQ, GRV, MRS, GRSV, and GS global fits.
