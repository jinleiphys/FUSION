# Relativistic hydrodynamical model in the presence of long-range correlations

> Effects of dynamical long-range correlations over a fluid cell size scale on a relativistic fluid are discussed. It is shown that such correlations among the fluid elements introduced into hydrodynamical model induce some weak dissipation and viscosity into the fluid. The influence of the long-range correlations on the entropy current is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "thought experiment" in which long-range correlations (LRC) among fluid elements can be switched on and off for a perfect relativistic fluid, and shows that such correlations induce weak dissipation and viscosity, with the resulting dissipative fluid dynamics being fully determined by the deviation of the flow velocity from an equilibrium reference fluid field (RFF).

## Method

The author introduces a reference fluid field (RFF) defined as the solution of ideal hydrodynamics without LRC, then assumes that switching on LRC produces a dissipative fluid whose energy-momentum tensor can be mapped onto the equilibrium tensor via a transformation involving the flow velocity difference delta u^mu. By imposing that the projected energy-momentum tensor components (hat epsilon, hat P, hat W^mu, hat pi^mu nu) equal their equilibrium counterparts, the energy flow vector W^mu and shear viscous tensor pi^mu nu are expressed entirely in terms of delta u^mu and the known RFF, reducing the dissipative problem to solving for the three spatial components of delta u^mu.

## Key numbers

* The relaxation time tau_Pi is estimated from a 1+1 dimensional Bjorken scaling solution as tau_Pi = (3/4) tau, where tau is proper time, based on the enthalpy evolution h_eq(tau) ~ (tau_0/tau)^(4/3).
* The bulk pressure Pi is related to the equilibrium enthalpy h_eq and the scalar gamma (defined from delta u^mu) by Pi = -(1/3) gamma (2 - gamma) h_eq(T, mu).
* The model yields tensor relations including pi^mu nu pi_mu nu = 6 Pi^2 and W^mu W_mu = -3 Pi (1 - gamma)^2 h_eq.

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive/dissipative correspondence developed by Osada (2008) to a relativistic hydrodynamic framework, providing a specific mechanism by which long-range correlations (observed experimentally as the "soft ridge" phenomenon at RHIC by Adams (2006) and Daugherity (2008)) can generate dissipative effects without introducing ad hoc viscosity parameters. The model enables future studies of pre-thermalization and memory effects in heavy-ion collisions, where the relaxation time tau_Pi depends only on the equilibrium enthalpy evolution rather than on the deviation from equilibrium itself.
