# The Shear Viscosity to Entropy Ratio: A Status Report

> This review highlights some of the lessons that the holographic gauge/gravity duality has taught us regarding the behavior of the shear viscosity to entropy density in strongly coupled field theories. The viscosity to entropy ratio has been shown to take on a very simple universal value in all gauge theories with an Einstein gravity dual. Here we describe the origin of this universal ratio, and focus on how it is modified by generic higher derivative corrections corresponding to curvature corrections on the gravity side of the duality. In particular, certain curvature corrections are known to push the viscosity to entropy ratio below its universal value. This disproves a longstanding conjecture that such a universal value represents a strict lower bound for any fluid in nature. We discuss the main developments that have led to insight into the violation of this bound, and consider whether the consistency of the theory is responsible for setting a fundamental lower bound on the viscosity to entropy ratio.

# The Shear Viscosity to Entropy Ratio: A Status Report

## Key claim

The universal value eta/s = 1/4pi, which was conjectured to be a strict lower bound for any fluid in nature (the KSS bound), is violated by certain higher curvature corrections to Einstein gravity that correspond to finite N (1/N) effects in the dual gauge theory. The bound is satisfied in the limit of infinite number of colors, N approaching infinity, and the question of whether a new fundamental lower bound exists remains open.

## Method

The review examines the shear viscosity to entropy ratio in strongly coupled field theories using the holographic gauge/gravity duality, focusing on how higher derivative curvature corrections modify the universal Einstein-gravity result. The shear viscosity is computed via the Kubo formula relating it to the retarded Green's function of the shear component of the stress-energy tensor, which on the gravity side is obtained by adding small shear metric fluctuations to the appropriate black brane metric. The analysis covers both top-down string theory constructions (Type IIB supergravity on AdS_5 x S^5 and its orbifolds) and phenomenological bottom-up approaches with generic curvature-squared corrections, relating the geometric couplings to the central charges of the dual conformal field theory through the holographic Weyl anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The universal value in all gauge theories with Einstein gravity duals is eta/s = 1/(4pi) (with hbar and k_B set to 1)
- For N=4 SYM at finite 't Hooft coupling, the leading correction is eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 + 15 zeta(3) lambda^{-3/2}], where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling
- For generic curvature-squared corrections in five dimensions, eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 - 8 alpha_3/L^2], where alpha_3 is the coupling of the R_{mu nu rho lambda}^2 term
- In the Kats-Petrov construction (N=2 Sp(N) gauge theory), eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 - 1/(2N)] + O(1/N^2), with central charges a = (1/24)(12N^2 + 12N - 1) and c = (1/24)(12N^2 + 18N - 2)
- For the Gauss-Bonnet gravity model, eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 - 4 lambda_GB], violating the bound for positive lambda_GB
- For N=2 gauged supergravity with R-charge, eta/s = (1/4pi)[1 - (c-a)/a (1+Q)], where Q is the R-charge, with the allowed range 0 <= Q <= 2 giving (1/4pi)(1 - 3(c-a)/a) <= eta/s <= (1/4pi)(1 - (c-a)/a)
- The RHIC estimate yields eta/s <= 2.5/(4pi) for the quark gluon plasma

## Context

This review synthesizes developments following the discovery by Policastro, Son and Starinets (2001) that eta/s = 1/4pi for N=4 SYM plasma, and the subsequent KSS conjecture by Kovtun, Son and Starinets (2003) that this represents a universal lower bound. The review describes how curvature-squared corrections in the gravitational dual, first shown to violate the bound by Kats and Petrov (2007) in a consistent N=2 superconformal gauge theory, correspond to finite N effects parametrized by the difference of central charges c-a. The work enables further investigation into whether microscopic consistency conditions (causality, unitarity, positivity of energy) impose a new fundamental lower bound on eta/s, and extends the analysis to include finite chemical potential effects studied by Myers, Paulos and Sinha (2009) and Cremonini (2009).
