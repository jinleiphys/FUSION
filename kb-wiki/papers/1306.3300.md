# Strange quark matter in strong magnetic fields within a confining model

> We construct an equation of state of strange quark matter in strong magnetic field within a confining model. The confinement is modeled by means of the Richardson potential for quark-quark interaction modified suitably to account for strong magnetic field. We compare our results for the equation of state and magnetization of matter to those derived within the MIT bag model. The differences between these models arise mainly due to the momentum dependence of the strong interaction between quarks in the Richardson model. Specifically, we find that the magnetization of strange quark matter in this model has much more pronounced de Haas-van Alfven oscillations than in the MIT bag model, which is the consequence of the (static) gluon-exchange structure of the confining potential.

## Key claim

The Richardson potential model predicts that the magnetization of strange quark matter in strong magnetic fields exhibits much more pronounced de Haas-van Alphen oscillations than the MIT bag model, due to the momentum dependence of the confining interaction; however, this effect becomes significant only for fields above approximately \(10^{18}\) G, and the absolute magnetization is larger by about a factor of 2 in the Richardson model at sufficiently large fields.

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state for charge-neutral strange quark matter (u, d, s quarks) in a strong magnetic field using the Richardson potential for quark-quark interaction, which incorporates both asymptotic freedom and confinement. The quark masses are made density-dependent via a sech parametrization that interpolates between constituent masses at low baryon density and current masses at high density. The single-particle energies and phase space are modified by Landau quantization in the magnetic field, and the thermodynamic pressure and magnetization are computed self-consistently under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, then compared to results from the MIT bag model.

## Key numbers

- The Richardson potential parameters used: \(\Lambda = 100\) MeV, \(\alpha_0 = 0.2\), \(M_q = 310\) MeV, \(M_u = 4\) MeV, \(M_d = 7\) MeV, \(M_s = 150\) MeV, \(\nu = 0.333\).
- The magnetic field values considered range up to \(B = 3 \times 10^{19}\) G, with the effect of the field becoming significant only above \(B \sim 10^{18}\) G.
- At \(B = 3 \times 10^{19}\) G and \(n_b = 6 n_0\), \(T = 20\) MeV, the magnetization in the Richardson model is larger by about a factor of 2 compared to the MIT bag model.
- The maximum mass of strange stars in the Richardson model is about 20% smaller than in the MIT bag model with \(B_{\rm MIT} \sim 60-70\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).
- The thermodynamic pressure is shown at fixed baryon density \(n_b = 6 n_0\) for temperatures up to about 60 MeV and for magnetic fields \(B=0\) and \(B=3\times 10^{19}\) G.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strange quark matter in strong magnetic fields that used the MIT bag model (e.g., Huang et al., 2010) or noninteracting quark matter, by incorporating a confining interaction with momentum dependence. The Richardson potential model was originally introduced by Dey et al. (1998) for zero-field quark matter and is here adapted to include Landau quantization. The results enable future calculations of mass-radius relations for strongly magnetized strange stars, which require solving Einstein's equations in axial symmetry due to the pressure anisotropy induced by the magnetic field.
