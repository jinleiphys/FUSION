# Relativistic Effects in 3-Nucleon Forces for Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei

> In order to simulate the relativistic effects of the Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock approach for finite nuclei the part of the Urbana 3 nucleon (3N) force is considered, which represents the enhancement of the small components of the Dirac spinors for the nucleons in the nuclear medium. This 3N force is included in a Brueckner Hartree Fock calculation with rearrangement terms using a realistic model for the NN interaction. The strength of the 3N force is adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter and then used in corresponding studies of the closed shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca. Special attention is paid to a consistent treatment of the spectrum of particle states in the NN propagator of the Bethe-Goldstone equation.

## Key claim

The Urbana 3N force's repulsive term (parameterized by $U$), which simulates the enhancement of the small components of Dirac spinors in the nuclear medium, can be adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter using only a single parameter, and the same force then yields a fair description of bulk properties for finite nuclei, though the agreement is not as precise as for infinite matter.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and the closed-shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca using the CD-Bonn realistic NN interaction, with the relativistic part of the Urbana 3N force (the $V_{ijk}^R$ term, setting the $2\pi$ exchange parameter $A=0$) included as a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction. The strength parameter $U$ of this 3N force was adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter, and the same value was then used in finite-nucleus calculations. Special attention was paid to the consistent treatment of the particle-state spectrum in the Bethe-Goldstone equation and to the inclusion of rearrangement terms (starting-energy and Pauli rearrangement) in the single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation point with CD-Bonn alone (BHF, no angle-average): binding energy per nucleon of approximately -24 MeV at a Fermi momentum $k_F$ around 1.8 fm$^{-1}$ (saturation density about 2.3 times the empirical value).
- Nuclear matter saturation point with CD-Bonn alone (BHF with angle-average approximation): binding energy per nucleon of approximately -20 MeV at about twice the empirical saturation density.
- Nuclear matter saturation point with CD-Bonn plus 3N force (parameter $U$ adjusted): reproduced the empirical saturation point (approximately -16 MeV binding energy at $k_F \approx 1.36$ fm$^{-1}$).
- For $^{16}$O with CD-Bonn alone (BHF): energy per nucleon = -6.08 MeV, charge radius $R_c$ = 2.35 fm (experimental: -7.98 MeV, 2.74 fm).
- For $^{16}$O with CD-Bonn plus 3N force (RBHF): energy per nucleon = -5.22 MeV, charge radius $R_c$ = 2.66 fm.
- For $^{16}$O with CD-Bonn plus 3N force in global density approximation (RBHF): energy per nucleon = -4.81 MeV, charge radius $R_c$ = 2.72 fm.
- Occupation probabilities $P$ for $^{16}$O with 3N force (RBHF) range from 0.792 to 0.918 for the various occupied orbitals.

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach by simulating its relativistic effects through a three-nucleon force, avoiding the full solution of Dirac-Brueckner equations for finite nuclei, which Van Giai et al. (2010) identified as a major open problem. The approach contrasts with the more popular method of constructing relativistic density functionals from DBHF results for infinite matter, as done by Fuchs et al. (1995) and others. The authors note that previous attempts using isobar-mediated 3N forces (delta excitations) did not resolve the Coester band problem, and they address the criticism by Hebeler and Schwenk (2010) and Carbone et al. (2014) regarding the equivalence of 3N force and density-dependent NN interaction treatments, showing that consistency is achieved when rearrangement terms are included. The results enable future work on finite nuclei using BHF-type calculations with 3N forces, though the authors note that the treatment of the particle-state spectrum in the Bethe-Goldstone equation requires special attention for finite systems.
