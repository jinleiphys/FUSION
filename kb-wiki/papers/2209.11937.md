# Determination of Lattice QCD Equation of state at a finite chemical potential

> Exponential resummation to all orders in $μ_B$ is a promising scheme which can capture contributions to all orders in $μ_B$ by considering only the first few Taylor coefficients in the Taylor expansion of thermodynamic observables in lattice QCD. This approach however, gets affected by biased estimates of $n$-point correlation functions $D_n$ whose effects can significantly hinder probe of finite density QCD. We present an unbiased exponential resummation formalism, which can reproduce the Taylor series up to the desired order in $μ_B$, besides retaining the original form of the all-order resummation estimate of QCD partition function.

## Key claim

The paper presents an unbiased exponential resummation formalism that eliminates stochastic bias in the estimation of n-point correlation functions D_n, while reproducing the Taylor series up to a desired order in the baryon chemical potential μ_B and retaining the all-order resummation structure of the QCD partition function.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks action and a Symanzik-improved gauge action on 100,000 gauge configurations. They implement the unbiased exponential resummation in both the chemical potential (μ_B) basis and the cumulant (X) basis, using N=2,4 and M=4, and compare results against the old biased resummation formula and standard Taylor series expansions.

## Key numbers

- Results obtained using 100,000 gauge configurations at temperatures of 135 MeV, 157 MeV, and 176 MeV
- The new formalism with only 500 random sources for all D_n provides improved results compared to the old resummation formula using 2000 random sources for D_1 alone
- Stochastic bias is most dominant at 135 MeV and progressively reduces at higher temperatures of 157 MeV and 176 MeV
- The formalism eliminates bias up to O(μ_B^2) and agrees with fourth order Taylor results for excess pressure and number density

## Context

This work extends the exponential resummation approach previously developed by Mitra, Hegde, and Schmidt (2022) which eliminated bias order-by-order but lost the all-order resummation structure. The new formalism addresses the stochastic bias problem that affected earlier exponential resummation methods (Mitra 2022), which hindered the probe of finite density QCD. This enables more reliable determination of the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potential and better understanding of the QCD phase diagram, building on prior work by Halasz (1998), Karsch (2001), and Fukushima and Hatsuda (2011).
