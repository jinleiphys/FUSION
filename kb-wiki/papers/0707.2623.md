# Spectroscopy of Mesons with Heavy Quarks

> I will give a concise overview of mesons with heavy quarks including p-wave charmed mesons and charmonium (or charmonium-like) states such as X(3872), Y(4260), X(3940), Y(3940), Z(3930) etc. The effect from the nearby S-wave open channels on the quark model spectrum is emphasized.

## Key claim

This overview argues that while the low-mass charmed-strange mesons \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}^*(2460)\) are probably conventional \(c\bar s\) states, the higher charmonium-like XYZ states remain controversial; the author emphasizes that the effect of nearby S-wave open channels on the quark model spectrum may resolve several puzzles, but many assignments are still tentative.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from Belle, BaBar, CLEO, CDF, and D0 collaborations on P-wave charmed mesons and charmonium-like states, comparing measured masses, widths, and decay ratios to quark model predictions, lattice QCD results, and theoretical models (tetraquark, molecular, hybrid, and coupled-channel). The author evaluates each state against the naive quark model expectations and discusses the role of S-wave coupled-channel effects and nearby thresholds in modifying the spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\): 2317 MeV; mass of \(D_{s1}^*(2460)\): 2459 MeV; both lie roughly 160 MeV below quark model predictions.
- Mass of \(D_0^*\): two measurements, 2308 ± 17 ± 32 MeV (FOCUS) and 2407 ± 21 ± 35 MeV (Belle), with widths 276 ± 21 ± 63 MeV and 240 ± 55 ± 59 MeV respectively.
- Mass of \(D_1^*\) (the \(1^+\) state in the \((0^+,1^+)\) doublet): 2427 ± 26 ± 25 MeV with width 384\(^{+107}_{-75}\) ± 74 MeV (Belle).
- Ratio \(\Gamma(D_{sJ}(2460) \to D_s\gamma) / \Gamma(D_{sJ}(2460) \to D_s^*\pi^0)\): Belle 0.55 ± 0.13 ± 0.08, BaBar 0.375 ± 0.054 ± 0.057, CLEO <0.49; light-cone QCD sum rule prediction 0.56.
- X(3872) mass: 3871.2 ± 0.54 MeV; width less than 2.3 MeV.
- Ratio \(B(X(3872)\to \gamma J/\psi) / B(X(3872)\to \pi^+\pi^- J/\psi)\): Belle 0.14 ± 0.05, BaBar ≈0.25; molecular model prediction 0.007.
- Ratio \(B(X(3872)\to D^0\bar D^0\pi^0) / B(X(3872)\to \pi^+\pi^- J/\psi)\): Belle 9.4\(^{+3.6}_{-4.3}\); molecular model prediction 0.054.
- Y(4260) mass central values: BaBar 4259 MeV, CLEO-c 4260 MeV, CLEO III 4283 MeV, Belle 4295 MeV; widths: BaBar 88 MeV, CLEO III 70 MeV, Belle 133 MeV.
- Leptonic width of Y(4260): \(\Gamma(Y\to e^+e^-) < 240\) eV; hidden charm decay width \(\Gamma(Y\to J/\psi \pi\pi) > 1.8\) MeV.
- Z(3930) total width around 20 MeV; quark model predictions for \(\chi_{c2}^\prime\) mass range from 3972 to 4030 MeV (40–100 MeV higher).
- X(3940) width less than 52 MeV; lies about 100 MeV below quark model prediction for \(\eta_c^{\prime\prime}\).

## Context

This review extends the discussion of heavy meson spectroscopy following the discovery of the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}^*(2460)\) by BaBar and CLEO, and the subsequent XYZ states found by Belle and BaBar. It contrasts the charmed-strange meson puzzles with the charmonium-like states, referencing prior work such as the tetraquark scheme of Polosa (2005), the molecular model of Swanson (2004), and the hybrid charmonium interpretation for Y(4260) proposed by several groups in 2005. The paper enables future searches at BESIII, J-PARC, and B factories by clarifying which states are well-understood and which remain controversial, particularly noting that the \(1^{--}\) spectrum may be overpopulated if the PDG classification is correct.
