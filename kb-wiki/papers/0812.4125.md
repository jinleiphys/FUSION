# Solution of the RHIC HBT puzzle with Gaussian initial conditions

> It is argued that the consistent description of the transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and the HBT radii in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions studied at RHIC may be obtained within the hydrodynamic model if one uses the Gaussian profile for the initial energy density in the transverse plane. Moreover, we show that the results obtained in the scenario with an early start of hydrodynamics (at the proper time tau0 = 0.25 fm) are practically equivalent to the results obtained in the model where the hydrodynamics is preceded by the free-streaming stage of partons (in the proper time interval 0.25 fm < tau < 1 fm) which suddenly equilibrate and with the help of the Landau matching conditions are transformed into the hydrodynamic regime (at the proper time tau0 = 1 fm).

# Solution of the RHIC HBT puzzle with Gaussian initial conditions

## Key claim
The paper argues that a consistent simultaneous description of transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii at RHIC can be obtained within a hydrodynamic model if Gaussian profiles for the initial energy density in the transverse plane are used. The hedge is that this consistency is achieved only when the Gaussian profiles are combined with a semi-hard equation of state, a complete set of hadronic resonances, and a single freeze-out assumption; additionally, results with an early hydrodynamic start at tau0 = 0.25 fm are practically equivalent to a scenario where free-streaming precedes hydrodynamics with sudden equilibration at tau0 = 1 fm.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC using Gaussian initial energy-density profiles (fitted to Monte Carlo Glauber model distributions) with the same spatial variances as the standard optical Glauber profiles. They used a semi-hard equation of state interpolating between a hadron-gas model and lattice QCD data, included feeding from all known hadronic resonances, and modeled hadron emission at freeze-out with the THERMINATOR Monte Carlo code implementing two-particle correlation functions with Coulomb corrections. They also tested a scenario where a free-streaming stage of partons (0.25 fm < tau < 1 fm) was followed by sudden equilibration via Landau matching conditions before the hydrodynamic phase.

## Key numbers
- The ratio R_out/R_side for pions at average transverse momentum k_T ~ 0.5 GeV was reduced from typical hydrodynamic values around 1.5 to 1.20-1.25 using the semi-hard equation of state and standard Glauber initial conditions.
- The hydrodynamic evolution started at proper time tau0 = 0.25 fm in the main scenario; the free-streaming plus sudden equilibration scenario used tau0 = 1 fm for the start of hydrodynamics.
- The Gaussian profiles were defined with spatial widths a and b such that <x^2> = a^2 and <y^2> = b^2, matching the second moments of the optical Glauber model distributions.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Chojnacki (2007) and Broniowski (2008) by showing that Gaussian initial conditions (rather than standard optical Glauber profiles) further improve the description of HBT radii, particularly the R_out/R_side ratio. The paper contradicts the notion that the RHIC HBT puzzle requires exotic physics, instead showing that a proper combination of initial profile shape, equation of state, and resonance feeding can resolve it. The results enable future studies of delayed thermalization scenarios, as the free-streaming plus sudden equilibration scenario produces equivalent results to early-start hydrodynamics, and complement the viscous hydrodynamic approach of Pratt (2008) which achieves similar HBT radii through very early start (tau0 = 0.1 fm) and viscosity.
