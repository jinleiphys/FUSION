# Exploring Skewed Parton Distributions with Two-body Models on the Light Front I: bimodality

> We explore skewed parton distributions for simple, model wave-functions in a truncated two-body Fock space. Consideration of non-Gaussian wave functions is our main emphasis, from which we observe the distributions are bimodal (there can be two distinct peaks) in the Fock-space diagonal overlap region (x > xi). We demonstrate that this behavior arises from convoluting two light-front wave functions to form the skewed parton distribution. Furthermore, the factorization Ansaetze, which have been used often, are shown not to hold. At high t, we can write the skewed parton distribution in terms of quark distribution amplitudes and perturbative wave functions thereby providing new tests.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that skewed parton distributions (SPDs) computed from simple two-body light-front wave functions exhibit bimodality (two distinct peaks) in the Fock-space diagonal overlap region (x > ξ) when non-Gaussian, power-law wave functions are used, but this bimodality is absent for Gaussian wave functions. The authors show that the commonly used factorization Ansätze for SPDs do not hold in general.

## Method

The authors model a scalar "meson" composed of two scalar "quarks" using light-front wave functions in a truncated two-body Fock space, restricting to the kinematical regime x > ξ. They compute the SPD as a convolution of two light-front wave functions (Eq. 4.2 in the paper) and evaluate it for three model wave functions: a Gaussian (G), a Hulthén (H), and a Wick-Cutkosky (WC) wave function in the weak-binding limit. The analysis focuses on the x-dependence of the SPD at fixed momentum transfer t and skewness ξ, with particular attention to the behavior at high momentum transfer where asymptotic approximations apply.

## Key numbers

- For the Wick-Cutkosky model at -t/m² = 0.1 and ξ = 0.07, the SPD shows bimodality with peaks displaced from x = 1/2
- At high momentum transfer -t/m² = 100 and ξ = 0.1, the Wick-Cutkosky SPD peaks are located at x_p = 0.45 and x̄_p = 0.55, matching the analytic prediction x_p(ξ) = (1-ξ)/2 and x̄_p(ξ) = (1+ξ)/2
- For the Wick-Cutkosky model at -t/m² = 100, the SPD increases by a factor of approximately 10 near maximal skewness
- For the Hulthén model at |t| = 20 GeV², the SPD rises by approximately a factor of 5 as ξ approaches maximal skewness (ξ_m ≈ 0.77)
- The Hulthén model at |t| = 1 GeV² and ξ = 0.15 shows visible bimodality
- The ratio of transverse momentum flow to mass δ(ξ) = (1 - x̄)|Δ⊥|/M drops by approximately a factor of 10 near maximal skewness (shown for t/M = -20 and x = 0.9)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of SPDs using simple models by Radyushkin (1997), Ji (1997), Diehl (1999), and Choi (2001), and specifically builds on the light-front Fock-space expansion formalism of Diehl (2001). The bimodality was previously encountered in the chiral quark-soliton model of the nucleon by Petrov (1998), but the authors provide a general explanation in terms of light-front wave functions. The paper enables new tests of perturbative QCD treatments of SPDs at high momentum transfer, as Eq. (4.2) expresses the SPD in terms of quark distribution amplitudes and perturbative wave functions. The authors note that the asymptotic behavior of their model wave functions (ψ ∼ 1/t²) differs from perturbative QCD wave functions (ψ_pQCD ∼ 1/t) due to scalar versus vector exchange dynamics.
