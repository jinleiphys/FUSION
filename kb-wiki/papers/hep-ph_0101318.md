# Mesons on a transverse lattice

> The meson eigenstates of the light-cone Hamiltonian in a coarse transverse lattice gauge theory are investigated. Building upon previous work in pure gauge theory, the Hamiltonian and its Fock space are expanded in powers of dynamical fields. In the leading approximation, the couplings appearing in the Hamiltonian are renormalised by demanding restoration of space-time symmetries broken by the cut-off. Additional requirements from chiral symmetry are discussed and difficulties in imposing them from first principles in the leading approximation are noted. A phenomenological calculation is then performed, in which chiral symmetry in spontaneously broken form is modelled by imposing the physical pion-rho mass splitting as a constraint. The light-cone wavefunctions of the resulting Hamiltonian are used to compute decay constants, form factors and quark momentum and spin distributions for the pion and rho mesons. Extensions beyond leading order, and the implications for first principles calculations, are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a coarse transverse lattice gauge theory, when expanded to leading order in dynamical fields and renormalized by demanding restoration of broken space-time symmetries, can produce a phenomenologically reasonable description of light mesons, but chiral symmetry cannot be imposed from first principles at this order and must instead be modeled by enforcing the physical pion-rho mass splitting as an external constraint.

## Method

The author constructs a light-cone Hamiltonian for transverse lattice QCD with quarks, expanding both the Hamiltonian and Fock space in powers of dynamical link fields and quark fields, then truncating to the leading (cubic and quartic) interactions. The Hamiltonian couplings are renormalized by searching coupling space for regions where Poincaré symmetries broken by the transverse lattice cut-off are being restored, while chiral symmetry is imposed phenomenologically by constraining the pion-rho mass splitting to its physical value. The resulting light-cone wavefunctions are used to compute decay constants, form factors, and quark momentum and spin distributions for pion and rho mesons.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless renormalized couplings appearing in the Hamiltonian equations are defined as \(m_b = \mu_b/\bar{G}\), \(m_f = \mu_f/\bar{G}\), \(k_a = \kappa_a\sqrt{N}/\bar{G}\), and \(k_s = \kappa_s\sqrt{N}/\bar{G}\), where \(\bar{G} = G\sqrt{(N^2-1)/N}\).
- The DLCQ discretization uses a compactification circumference \(\mathcal{L} = 2\pi K/P^+\) where \(K\) is a positive integer, with (anti)-periodic boundary conditions for \(M\) (\(u\)).
- The one-link truncation of Fock space includes states with either a quark-antiquark pair at the same transverse lattice site or separated by one link, with momentum fractions \(x_1, x_2, x_3\) satisfying \(x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 1\).
- The instantaneous gluon exchange kernel \(K\) contains principal-value integrals with denominators \((y-x_1)^2\) and similar structures.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous pure gauge theory studies (Dalley and van de Sande, 1998-2000) following the original color-dielectric approach of Bardeen et al. (1983-1984). The transverse lattice method was first proposed for QCD with quarks in reference (Mat 2000). The leading approximation yields a useful quantitative starting point for computing meson observables, but the difficulties in imposing chiral symmetry from first principles at this order motivate future work including higher-order terms and renormalization of operators beyond the Hamiltonian.
