# Physics-guided residual correction of $α$-decay half-lives based on the effective liquid drop model

> To improve the prediction accuracy of $α$-decay half-lives in heavy and superheavy nuclei, a physics-guided residual-correction framework combining the effective liquid drop model (ELDM) with machine-learning methods is proposed. The ELDM is first used as the macroscopic baseline for describing the barrier-penetration process, and XGBoost and TabPFN models are then employed to learn the residual deviations between ELDM predictions and experimental data. To incorporate microscopic nuclear-structure information, several physically motivated descriptors are constructed, including deformation-related quantities, Geiger--Nuttall-related features, and minimum orbital angular momentum. The results show that machine-learning residual correction significantly improves the predictive performance of the ELDM baseline. Among all models, TabPFN-term3 achieves the best accuracy, reducing the RMSE and MAE to 0.348 and 0.248, corresponding to improvements of 38.60\% and 40.46\%, respectively. Residual-distribution and feature-ablation analyses further indicate that the corrected predictions are closer to experimental values and that physically motivated descriptors play an important role in learning nonlinear residual structures. Overall, the proposed ELDM-based residual-correction framework can effectively compensate for missing microscopic nuclear-structure effects while preserving physical interpretability, providing a feasible strategy for high-precision $α$-decay half-life prediction.

# Physics-guided residual correction of α-decay half-lives based on the effective liquid drop model

## Key claim

The proposed physics-guided residual-correction framework, which combines the effective liquid drop model (ELDM) with XGBoost or TabPFN machine-learning models, improves α-decay half-life prediction accuracy while preserving physical interpretability, but only for the specific dataset of experimentally known heavy and superheavy nuclei from NUBASE2020 and AME2020, and only when physically motivated descriptors (deformation, Geiger–Nuttall features, minimum orbital angular momentum) are included.

## Method

The ELDM was first used as a macroscopic baseline to calculate α-decay half-lives via a one-dimensional barrier-penetration problem along a collective coordinate, using the WKB approximation with an effective assault frequency of 1.0×10²² s⁻¹. The residual deviations between ELDM predictions and experimental half-lives were then learned by XGBoost (gradient-boosted decision trees) and TabPFN (a probabilistic foundation model for small tabular datasets) using 10-fold cross-validation, with four different feature sets incorporating proton number Z, neutron number N, decay energy Qα, deformation-related descriptors (x_def = √(κ₂β₂) with κ₂ = +2 for prolate, -1 for oblate, 0 for spherical), Z/√Qα terms, and minimum orbital angular momentum l_min.

## Key numbers

- TabPFN-term3 achieved the best accuracy, reducing RMSE to 0.348 and MAE to 0.248, corresponding to improvements of 38.60% and 40.46% respectively over the ELDM baseline.
- The ELDM baseline used a pre-exponential factor λ₀ = 1.0×10²² s⁻¹ for the decay constant calculation.
- XGBoost hyperparameters included n_estimators = 300, max_depth = 4, learning_rate = 0.05, subsample = 0.85, colsample_bytree = 0.85, reg_alpha = 0.0, reg_lambda = 1.0, with random_state = 42 and n_splits = 10.
- TabPFN calculations used random seed = 50 with 10-fold cross-validation.
- The deformation descriptor x_def = √(κ₂β₂) used κ₂ = +2 for prolate, κ₂ = -1 for oblate, and κ₂ = 0 for nearly spherical nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the effective liquid drop model approach of Goncalves (1993), Tavares (1998), and Zhang (2006) by adding machine-learning residual correction, addressing limitations where macroscopic liquid-drop-type models cannot fully capture microscopic nuclear-structure effects such as deformation, shell effects, pairing correlations, and angular-momentum hindrance. It contrasts with purely data-driven machine-learning studies on α decay (Gao 2023, Qu 2025, He 2023) that lack physical interpretability, and it enables high-precision half-life predictions for superheavy nuclei relevant to exploring the "island of stability," while preserving the physical constraints of the underlying barrier-penetration model.
