# Population of isomers in decay of the giant dipole resonance

> The value of an isomeric ratio (IR) in N=81 isotones ($^{137}$Ba, $^{139}$Ce, $^{141}$Nd and $^{143}$Sm) is studied by means of the ($γ, n)$ reaction. This quantity measures a probability to populate the isomeric state in respect to the ground state population. In ($γ, n)$ reactions, the giant dipole resonance (GDR) is excited and after its decay by a neutron emission, the nucleus has an excitation energy of a few MeV. The forthcoming $γ$ decay by direct or cascade transitions deexcites the nucleus into an isomeric or ground state. It has been observed experimentally that the IR for $^{137}$Ba and $ ^{139}$Ce equals about 0.13 while in two heavier isotones it is even less than half the size. To explain this effect, the structure of the excited states in the energy region up to 6.5 MeV has been calculated within the Quasiparticle Phonon Model. Many states are found connected to the ground and isomeric states by $E1$, $E2$ and $M1$ transitions. The single-particle component of the wave function is responsible for the large values of the transitions. The calculated value of the isomeric ratio is in very good agreement with the experimental data for all isotones. A slightly different value of maximum energy with which the nuclei rest after neutron decay of the GDR is responsible for the reported effect of the A-dependence of the IR.

## Key claim

The isomeric ratio (IR) in N=81 isotones produced via the (γ,n) reaction depends not only on statistical properties of the intermediate states but also on the detailed single-particle structure of the excited states, with the maximum excitation energy of the residual nucleus after neutron decay being the key factor that explains the observed A-dependence of the IR.

## Method

The isomeric ratio was measured for 137Ba, 139Ce, 141Nd, and 143Sm using bremsstrahlung radiation with an endpoint energy of 25 MeV from the MT-25 microtron, irradiating enriched oxide targets and measuring residual activity with a Ge(Li) detector. The structure of excited states up to 6.5 MeV was calculated within the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM), including quasiparticle and quasiparticle⊗phonon configurations, and the partial γ-widths for E1, E2, and M1 transitions to the isomeric and ground states were computed. The IR was then obtained from the ratio of cross sections for populating the isomeric versus ground state, incorporating neutron transmission coefficients and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- IR for 137Ba at Eγmax = 25 MeV: 0.12
- IR for 139Ce at Eγmax = 25 MeV: 0.14
- IR for 141Nd at Eγmax = 25 MeV: 0.06
- IR for 143Sm at Eγmax = 25 MeV: 0.046
- Isomeric state energy: 661 keV in 137Ba; approximately 755 keV in 139Ce, 141Nd, and 143Sm
- Maximum excitation energy of residual nuclei after (γ,n) reaction: 5.4 MeV for 137Ba, 5.2 MeV for 139Ce, 4.72 MeV for 141Nd, and 4.72 MeV for 143Sm
- Single-particle strength concentration in 139Ce: 4.7% at 4.18 MeV and 5.2% at 6.15 MeV for the 9/2+ component
- Neutron kinetic energy used in calculations: mean value of 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isomer photoexcitation using bremsstrahlung at lower endpoint energies (4-4.5 MeV) by Ponomarev (1990), von Neumann-Cosel, Carroll, and Huber, which found a linear dependence of isomer yield on endpoint energy with breaks attributed to specific doorway states. The present paper applies the QPM, previously successful at lower energies, to the higher-energy regime of the GDR decay (10-25 MeV), where statistical approaches such as those of Arifov, Vandenbosch, and Ignatyuk had been used. The results enable a microscopic understanding of isomer population in photonuclear reactions and demonstrate that single-particle structure remains important even at intermediate excitation energies of 5-7 MeV.
