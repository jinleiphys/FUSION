# Probing Transverse Momentum Broadening in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the dijet azimuthal de-correlation in relativistic heavy ion collisions as an important probe of the transverse momentum broadening effects of a high energy jet traversing the quark-gluon plasma. We take into account both the soft gluon radiation in vacuum associated with the Sudakov logarithms and the jet P_T-broadening effects in the QCD medium. We find that the Sudakov effects are dominant at the LHC, while the medium effects can play an important role at RHIC energies. This explains why the LHC experiments have not yet observed sizable P_T-broadening effects in the measurement of dijet azimuthal correlations in heavy ion collisions. Future investigations at RHIC will provide a unique opportunity to study the P_T-broadening effects and help to pin down the underlying mechanism for jet energy loss in a hot and dense medium.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed absence of transverse momentum broadening in dijet azimuthal correlations at the LHC is not evidence against medium effects, but rather because Sudakov vacuum radiation effects dominate at LHC energies; the medium-induced broadening should become observable at RHIC energies where Sudakov effects are weaker.

## Method

The authors combine the BDMPS framework for medium-induced transverse momentum broadening with a Sudakov resummation formalism for vacuum gluon radiation in dijet production. They incorporate both effects into a unified transverse-momentum-dependent factorization formula, where the medium broadening enters as an additional Gaussian term \(Q_s^2 b_\perp^2/4\) in the Sudakov form factor. They then compute the \(b_\perp\)-space distribution \(W(b_\perp)\) and the resulting azimuthal angular correlation \(\Delta\phi\) for dijets at LHC and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient parameter: \(Q_s^2 = \hat{q} L \simeq 6\ \text{GeV}^2\) at RHIC and \(\hat{q} L \simeq 10\ \text{GeV}^2\) at the LHC for quark jets with medium length \(L = 5\ \text{fm}\).
- For gluon jets, \(\tilde{Q}_s^2\) is \(\frac{2N_c^2}{N_c^2 - 1}\) times the quark-jet value due to Casimir factors.
- At the LHC (\(\sqrt{S}=2.76\ \text{TeV}\)), calculations for leading jet \(P_\perp = 120\ \text{GeV}\) and \(50\ \text{GeV}\) show negligible sensitivity to \(Q_s^2\) values of 0, 8, and \(20\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- At RHIC (\(\sqrt{S}=200\ \text{GeV}\)), for leading jet \(P_\perp = 35\ \text{GeV}\), the medium broadening significantly affects the \(b_\perp \times W(b_\perp)\) distribution in the region \(b \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the Sudakov resummation formalism for dijet production in \(pp\) collisions developed by Mueller, Wu, Xiao, and Yuan (2013) and Sun, Yuan, and Yuan (2014) to heavy ion collisions. It explains the null results from ATLAS and CMS on dijet azimuthal correlations in PbPb collisions at the LHC, and predicts observable broadening at RHIC, motivating future measurements by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations. The paper is complementary to Monte Carlo event generator approaches such as JEWEL (Zapp, 2008; Zapp, 2012) and to earlier energy-loss calculations for the \(A_J\) asymmetry by Qin et al. (2010), Young et al. (2011), and He et al. (2011).
