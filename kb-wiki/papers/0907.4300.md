# Elliptic Flow in Collisions of Deformed Nuclei

> Initial eccentricity of the interacting volume of QCD matter created in collisions of prolate and oblate nuclei is studied using optical Glauber simulation. Collisions of prolate 165-Ho + 165-Ho nuclei and Au+Au nuclei are investigated (assuming oblate ground-state deformation for 197-Au nucleus). Qualitative predictions are obtained for RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that ground-state deformation of nuclei used in heavy-ion collisions generates additional initial eccentricity (and consequently elliptic flow \(v_2\)) fluctuations on top of those existing in collisions of spherical nuclei, and that the behavior of elliptic flow in the most central collisions differs between oblate and prolate nuclei.

## Method

The author used an optical Glauber model simulation to calculate the number of participating nucleons (\(N_{\text{part}}\)), number of nucleon-nucleon collisions (\(N_{\text{coll}}\)), and initial eccentricity (\(\varepsilon\)) for collisions of prolate \(^{165}\)Ho and oblate \(^{197}\)Au nuclei at fixed impact parameters \(b = 0, 1, 2, \ldots 14\) fm, varying the orientation angles (\(\theta_1, \phi_1, \theta_2, \phi_2\)) of the nuclear deformation axes in 15-degree steps. Each collision event was weighted by spherical angle factors \(\sin(\theta_1)\sin(\theta_2)\) and impact parameter factor \(2\pi b\) to account for unpolarized experimental conditions. Charged-particle multiplicity \(dN_{\text{ch}}/d\eta\) was calculated using a two-component model with parameters \(n_{pp}=2.25\) and \(x=0.11\) from Kharzeev (2000) to define collision centrality.

## Key numbers

- Prolate \(^{165}\)Ho nucleus has ground-state deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.3\) (from Moller, 1995).
- Oblate \(^{197}\)Au nucleus is assumed to have deformation \(\beta_2 = -0.131\) (from Moller, 1995), with quadrupole moment \(Q \approx 0.55 \times 10^{-24}\) cm\(^2\) (from Pyykko, 1992).
- Impact parameters were scanned from \(b = 0\) fm to \(b = 14\) fm in 1 fm steps for the main simulations, and from \(b = 0.0\) fm to \(b = 3.2\) fm in 0.2 fm steps for central collisions.
- For the two-component multiplicity model, \(n_{pp} = 2.25\) and \(x = 0.11\) (from Kharzeev, 2000), giving approximately 40% of secondary particles originating from \(N_{\text{coll}}\).
- For prolate nuclei at \(b = 0\) fm, the highest multiplicity occurs when spins are parallel to the beam axis and to each other, producing significantly more nucleon-nucleon collisions than when spins are orthogonal to the beam axis.
- For oblate nuclei at \(b = 0\) fm, the highest \(N_{\text{coll}}\) occurs when spins are parallel to each other and orthogonal to the beam axis, yielding non-zero eccentricity proportional to \(|\beta_2|\).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow from spherical nuclei (e.g., Pb) to deformed nuclei, building on the optical Glauber model of Filip (2007) and the Monte Carlo Glauber model of Miller (2007). The author compares predictions with PHOBOS collaboration data (2007) on relative elliptic flow fluctuations \(\sigma(v_2)/\langle v_2 \rangle\) in Au+Au collisions, finding that the oblate deformation of \(^{197}\)Au (\(|\beta_2| \approx 0.13\)) can almost fully account for the observed fluctuations in the most central collisions. The results suggest that precise elliptic flow measurements at RHIC could determine ground-state nuclear deformations, including for zero-spin nuclei like \(^{28}\)Si and \(^{180}\)W, and that Monte Carlo Glauber simulations should re-investigate the consequences of \(^{197}\)Au deformation.
