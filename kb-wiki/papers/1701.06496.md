# Harmonic decomposition of three-particle azimuthal correlations at RHIC

> We present measurements of three-particle correlations for various harmonics in Au+Au collisions at energies ranging from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ to 200 GeV using the STAR detector. The quantity $\langle\cos(mϕ_1+nϕ_2-(m+n)ϕ_3)\rangle$ is evaluated as a function of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$, collision centrality, transverse momentum, $p_T$, pseudo-rapidity difference, $Δη$, and harmonics ($m$ and $n$). These data provide detailed information on global event properties like the three-dimensional structure of the initial overlap region, the expansion dynamics of the matter produced in the collisions, and the transport properties of the medium. A strong dependence on $Δη$ is observed for most harmonic combinations consistent with breaking of longitudinal boost invariance. Data reveal changes with energy in the two-particle correlation functions relative to the second-harmonic event-plane and provide ways to constrain models of heavy-ion collisions over a wide range of collision energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents measurements of three-particle azimuthal correlations \(\langle\cos(m\phi_1+n\phi_2-(m+n)\phi_3)\rangle\) in Au+Au collisions, showing that the strong \(\Delta\eta\) dependence observed for most harmonic combinations is consistent with breaking of longitudinal boost invariance, but the authors note that no specific model has been shown to describe the data and that non-flow scenarios appear difficult to construct to account for the long-range variation.

## Method

The STAR detector was used to measure charged particles with \(p_T>0.2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta|<1\) from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7\) to 200 GeV. The three-particle correlation quantity \(C_{m,n,m+n}=\langle\cos(m\phi_1+n\phi_2-(m+n)\phi_3)\rangle\) was evaluated as a function of collision centrality, transverse momentum, pseudo-rapidity difference \(\Delta\eta\), and harmonic combinations \((m,n)\). Corrections were applied for \(p_T\)-dependent efficiency, detector acceptance, and track-merging effects, with Q-vector algebra used for computational efficiency in large datasets.

## Key numbers

- \(\langle\cos(\phi_1+\phi_2-2\phi_3)\rangle\) (C\(_{1,1,2}\)) at 200 GeV: negative for all centralities except most peripheral (70-80%), where it is slightly positive but consistent with zero; scaled by \(N_{\rm part}^2\) shows strong \(\Delta\eta\) dependence, changing from negative at small \(|\eta_1-\eta_2|\) to near zero or positive near \(|\eta_1-\eta_2|=1.5\) in central collisions.
- \(\langle\cos(\phi_1+2\phi_2-3\phi_3)\rangle\) (C\(_{1,2,3}\)) at 200 GeV: positive in mid-central collisions, negative in central collisions, consistent with zero in peripheral collisions; exhibits strong dependence on \(|\eta_1-\eta_3|\) but weak dependence on \(|\eta_1-\eta_2|\).
- \(\langle\cos(2\phi_1+2\phi_2-4\phi_3)\rangle\) (C\(_{2,2,4}\)) at 200 GeV: approximately 5 times larger in magnitude than the next largest correlator C\(_{2,3,5}\); shows approximately 20% variation with \(|\eta_1-\eta_3|\) in mid-central collisions.
- \(\langle\cos(2\phi_1+3\phi_2-5\phi_3)\rangle\) (C\(_{2,3,5}\)) at 200 GeV: exhibits strong short-range correlation at \(|\eta_2-\eta_3|<0.4\) in central and mid-central collisions, consistent with HBT and Coulomb correlations.
- C\(_{1,2,3}\) at 200 GeV is mostly positive, but at 62.4 GeV it is consistent with zero or slightly negative, and at lower energies (19.6, 14.5, 11.5, 7.7 GeV) it becomes more negative.
- C\(_{1,1,2}\) at 7.7 GeV is smaller in magnitude than at higher energies, becoming consistent with zero.

## Context

This work extends two-particle azimuthal correlation measurements (e.g., \(v_n^2\{2\}=\langle\cos n(\Delta\phi)\rangle\) studied as a function of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\), centrality, and \(\Delta\eta\) in the RHIC beam energy scan) to three-particle mixed harmonic correlations, as originally proposed by Teaney and Yan (2010) to probe correlations between the first, second, and third harmonics of the spatial density distribution. The data provide constraints for hydrodynamic models that include bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, and their temperature dependence (e.g., Ryu et al., 2015), and enable studies of the three-dimensional structure of the initial overlap region and the breaking of boost invariance assumed in models like Bjorken (1982). The measurements also inform interpretations of the chiral magnetic effect, previously studied through charge-dependent measurements of \(\langle\cos(\phi_1+\phi_2-2\phi_3)\rangle\) by the STAR Collaboration (2009).
