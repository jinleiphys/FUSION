# Remarks about spin measurements in $\bar{\rm p}{\rm p} \to \overlineΛΛ$

> We comment on the proposed measurement of the spin-depolarisation parameter $D_{nn}$ in the strangeness-exchange reaction $\bar{\rm p}{\rm p} \rightarrow \overlineΛΛ$. It is shown that the existing data on the correlation coefficients $C_{ij}$ limit the range allowed for $D_{nn}$.

## Key claim

Existing data on the spin-correlation coefficient $C_{zz}$ in $\bar{\rm p}{\rm p} \to \overline{\Lambda}\Lambda$ already constrain the allowed range of the depolarisation parameter $D_{nn}$ via the inequality $C_{zz}^2 + D_{nn}^2 \le 1$, meaning that a large measured $|C_{zz}|$ forces $D_{nn}$ to be small, contrary to earlier claims that $D_{nn}$ alone could cleanly discriminate between meson-exchange and quark models.

## Method

The author derives algebraic relations among the spin observables of the reaction $\bar{\rm p}{\rm p} \to \overline{\Lambda}\Lambda$ expressed in terms of six helicity amplitudes $a,b,c,d,e,g$. Using the published expressions for the correlation coefficients $C_{ij}$ and the depolarisation parameter $D_{nn}$, the author proves the inequality $C_{zz}^2 + D_{nn}^2 \le 1$ and analogous constraints involving $C_{xx}$, $C_{xz}$, and $K_{nn}$. These constraints are then compared with existing PS185 data on $C_{zz}$ at two beam momenta (1546 and 1695 MeV/$c$) to evaluate the realistic range for $D_{nn}$.

## Key numbers

- The inequality $C_{zz}^2 + D_{nn}^2 \le 1$ is derived from the amplitude expressions, showing that $D_{nn} = -1$ would require $a = b = e = 0$, which would force $C_{zz} = 0$.
- Existing PS185 data (Barnes 1991) show $C_{zz}$ close to $-1$ at some angles and energies, but recent data (Roehrich 1995) indicate values of $|C_{zz}|$ smaller than the published values, especially above 1.9 GeV/$c$.
- The spin-singlet fraction is given by $F_0 = \frac{1}{4}(1 + C_{xx} - C_{yy} + C_{zz})$, and would be $F_0 = 1/4$ in absence of spin-dependent interaction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Holinde et al. (1992), who argued that $D_{nn}$ could discriminate between meson-exchange models (predicting $D_{nn}$ near $-1$) and constituent-quark models (predicting positive or slightly negative values). It also comments on the model of Alberg et al. (1995), who proposed that $D_{nn} = -1$ could be reached in an idealised version of their polarised-sea picture. The paper shows that at energies and angles where $|C_{zz}|$ is large, $D_{nn}$ and $K_{nn}$ are necessarily small and cannot provide further tests of models, suggesting that the relative merits of longitudinal versus transverse target polarisation should be reconsidered for the proposed PS185 extension with a polarised target (Bassalleck 1995).
