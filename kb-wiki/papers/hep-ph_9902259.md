# Exclusive Vector Meson Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Exclusive vector meson production reactions such as $Au + Au \to Au + Au + V$, where $V=ρ, ω, ϕ$ or $J/ψ$ can proceed through photon-Pomeron and photon-meson interactions. Photons from the electromagnetic field of one nucleus interact coherently with the other nucleus. Photonuclear cross sections are scaled from $γp$ data, and convoluted with the photon spectrum to find the exclusive rates. The cross sections at the RHIC and LHC heavy ion colliders are huge, 10% of the total hadronic cross section at RHIC, and 50% at LHC. These accelerators may be useful as vector meson factories. With iodine beams at RHIC, 640 $ρ$ are produced each second (10^{10}/year); with calcium at the LHC the rate is 240 kHz. The $ϕ$ rates are 39 Hz at RHIC and 15 kHz at LHC, while the $J/ψ$ rate is 0.3 Hz at RHIC and 780 Hz at the LHC. Because of the coherent couplings, the reactions kinematics are similar to coherent two-photon interactions; we discuss the interplay between the two reactions.

# Exclusive Vector Meson Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Exclusive vector meson production (ρ, ω, φ, J/ψ) via photon-Pomeron and photon-meson interactions in heavy ion collisions will have cross sections reaching 10% of the total hadronic cross section at RHIC and 50% at LHC, making these accelerators potentially useful as vector meson factories; however, the J/ψ calculations must be treated with caution because the HERA data used for input only extend to Wγp ≈ 180 GeV, and extrapolations to LHC energies are required, with the J/ψ cross section eventually needing to flatten out to avoid dominating the total photonuclear cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate exclusive vector meson production rates by scaling photonuclear cross sections from γp data using a Glauber model, then convoluting these cross sections with the Weizsäcker-Williams photon flux from the emitting nucleus. The photon flux is integrated over impact parameter, modulated by the probability of no hadronic interactions calculated from a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution and nucleon-nucleon cross sections of 52 mb at 100 GeV rising to 88 mb at 2.9 TeV. The γA → VA cross sections are found via the optical theorem and vector dominance, using a hard sphere plus Yukawa potential form factor for the nuclear Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive vector meson cross sections are 10% of the total hadronic cross section at RHIC and 50% at LHC
- With iodine beams at RHIC: 640 ρ produced per second (10^10/year); with calcium at LHC: 240 kHz ρ rate
- φ rates: 39 Hz at RHIC, 15 kHz at LHC
- J/ψ rates: 0.3 Hz at RHIC, 780 Hz at LHC
- Total cross sections for gold at RHIC: ρ = 590 mb, ω = 59 mb, φ = 39 mb, J/ψ = 0.29 mb
- Total cross sections for lead at LHC: ρ = 5200 mb, ω = 490 mb, φ = 460 mb, J/ψ = 32 mb
- At W = 10 GeV, total Vp cross sections: ρ = 24 mb, ω = 26 mb, φ = 12 mb, J/ψ = 1.0 mb
- Perpendicular momentum cutoff from nuclear form factors: roughly √2 ħ/RA ≈ 45 MeV/c for gold
- ρ0ρ0 pair production cross section: 720 μb at RHIC (gold), 8800 μb at LHC (lead)

## Context

This work extends earlier photonuclear studies from the 1960s and 1970s that used real photon beams and disk nuclear models, providing updated calculations for the RHIC and LHC colliders using Glauber theory and modern HERA γp data. The authors compare with earlier calculations by Baur, Hencken and Trautman (1998) who found σ(Au+p → Au+p+V) = 3.5 mb using Weizsäcker-Williams flux for gold on a free proton target, and note that their own earlier black-disk scaling model (Nystrand and Klein, 1998) predicted much lower cross sections. The results enable experimental searches for exclusive vector meson production at RHIC and LHC, including multiple vector meson production from single ion pairs, and raise the possibility of interference between photonuclear and two-photon production channels.
