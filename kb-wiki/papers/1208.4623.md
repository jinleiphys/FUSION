# Exclusive production of $π^+π^-$ and $π^0π^0$ pairs in photon-photon and in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> The $γγ\toππ$ reactions are discussed. To describe those processes, we include dipion continuum, resonances, high-energy pion-pion rescatterings, $ρ$ meson exchange and pQCD Brodsky-Lepage mechanisms. The cross section for the production of pion pairs in photon-photon collisions in peripheral heavy ion collisions is calculated with the help of Equivalent Photon Approximaption (EPA) in the impact parameter space. We show predictions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.5$ TeV which could be measured e.g. by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC.

# Exclusive production of π⁺π⁻ and π⁰π⁰ pairs in photon-photon and in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors claim that the cross sections for exclusive pion pair production in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at LHC energies are "fairly large" and potentially measurable, but explicitly state that "whether they can be measured requires further studies." The abstract's implication of measurability is thus qualified by the need for additional experimental feasibility work.

## Method

The authors calculate the elementary γγ→ππ cross section from threshold (W=2mπ) up to about W=6 GeV by including dipion continuum, scalar (f0(600), f0(980)), tensor (f2(1270), f′2(1525), f2(1560)), and spin-4 (f4(2050)) resonances via relativistic Breit-Wigner distributions with spin-dependent Blatt-Weisskopf form factors, plus Born pion exchange for charged pions, high-energy pion-pion rescattering, t/u-channel ρ± meson exchange, and the pQCD Brodsky-Lepage mechanism. They then convolve this elementary cross section with photon fluxes obtained from the Equivalent Photon Approximation in impact parameter space, imposing an absorption cutoff at impact parameter b > R1+R2 ≈ 14 fm, to obtain nuclear cross sections for PbPb→PbππPb at √sNN=3.5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for PbPb→PbPbπ⁺π⁻: 112 mb (no transverse momentum cut)
- Total cross section for PbPb→PbPbπ⁺π⁻ with pt>0.5 GeV cut: 14 mb
- Total cross section for PbPb→PbPbπ⁰π⁰: 10 mb (no transverse momentum cut)
- Total cross section for PbPb→PbPbπ⁰π⁰ with pt>0.5 GeV cut: 5 mb
- Absorption impact parameter cutoff: b > R1+R2 ≈ 14 fm
- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: √sNN=3.5 TeV (LHC lead-lead collisions)
- Elementary cross section calculated from threshold (W=2mπ) up to W≈6 GeV
- Experimental angular cuts used for comparison: |cosθ|<0.6 for π⁺π⁻, |cosθ|<0.8 for π⁰π⁰

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous b-space EPA calculations for ρ⁰ρ⁰ pairs (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2012), μ⁺μ⁻ (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2012), QQbar (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2012), DDbar (Luszczak and Szczurek, 2012), and high-mass dipions (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2012) to the full low-to-intermediate energy range of pion pair production. The calculation enables experimental comparisons for the ALICE collaboration at the LHC and tests of the interplay between resonant, non-resonant, and perturbative QCD mechanisms in γγ→ππ, which no previous work had simultaneously described for both charged and neutral pions at low and intermediate energies.
