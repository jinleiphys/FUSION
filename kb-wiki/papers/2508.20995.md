# Bottomonium transport in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> Quarkonium production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions remains a key probe of the quark-gluon plasma formed in these reactions, but the development of a fully integrated nonperturbative approach remains a challenge. Toward this end, we set up a semiclassical transport approach that combines nonperturbative reaction rates rooted in lattice-constrained $T$-matrix interactions with a viscous hydrodynamic medium evolution. Bottomonium suppression is computed along trajectories in the hydrodynamic evolution while regeneration is evaluated via a rate equation extended to a medium with spatial gradients. The much larger reaction rates compared to previous calculations markedly enhance both dissociation and regeneration processes. This, in particular, requires a reliable assessment of bottomonium equilibrium limits and of the non-thermal distributions of the bottom quarks transported through the expanding medium. Within current uncertainties our approach can describe the centrality dependence of bottomonium yields measured in Pb-Pb ($\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$=5.02\,TeV) collisions at the LHC, while discrepancies are found at large transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The authors present a semiclassical transport approach for bottomonium in heavy-ion collisions that combines nonperturbative reaction rates (rooted in lattice-constrained T-matrix interactions) with a viscous hydrodynamic medium evolution, finding that the much larger reaction rates compared to previous calculations markedly enhance both dissociation and regeneration processes. Within current uncertainties the approach can describe the centrality dependence of bottomonium yields measured in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, while discrepancies are found at large transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors set up a semiclassical transport approach where bottomonium suppression is computed along trajectories in a 3+1-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamic evolution, using momentum- and temperature-dependent dissociation rates from a nonperturbative T-matrix framework constrained by lattice-QCD data. Regeneration is evaluated via a rate equation extended to a medium with spatial gradients, using a thermodynamically-motivated approach with an equilibrium limit computed from the fireball volume and bottom-quark fugacity, including corrections for incomplete b-quark thermalization and escape effects of bottom quarks from the fireball.

## Key numbers

- Reaction rates are based on nonperturbative T-matrix interactions constrained by lattice-QCD data in both heavy- and light-parton sectors (Tang 2023)
- Bottom quark relaxation time: tau_b approximately 7.5 fm/c, calculated from the same interactions as the Y reaction rates
- Final temperature for integration: T_f = 170 MeV
- Formation time variation: coefficient C between 1 and 2 causes approximately 10% difference in final results for 3S suppression in peripheral collisions
- Average b-quark recoil velocity: <v_b> = 0.65 c, estimated from B-meson pT spectra
- Initial strong-interaction range: r_0 = 1 fm
- Thermalization onset time: tau_0 = 0.25 fm
- Average regeneration temperatures for Upsilon states: T = 280, 180, 170 MeV for Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) respectively
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

## Context

This work extends previous fireball calculations by Du (2017) that used perturbative coupling to a quasiparticle medium, replacing them with nonperturbative reaction rates from Wu (2025) and Tang (2023) that are constrained by lattice-QCD data and selfconsistently coupled to a strongly interacting medium. The approach establishes consistency with nonperturbative interactions required to describe individual heavy-quark diffusion in the strongly coupled QGP, as studied by the ALICE collaboration (2021) and lattice-QCD calculations by Altenkort (2023). The framework enables future calculations of bottomonium elliptic flow and more detailed comparisons with experimental data from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE at the LHC.
