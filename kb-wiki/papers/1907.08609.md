# Effective Lagrangian for Nambu-Goldstone modes in nonequilibrium open systems

> We develop the effective field theory of diffusive Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes associated with spontaneous internal symmetry breaking taking place in nonequilibrium open systems. The effective Lagrangian describing semi-classical dynamics of the NG modes is derived and matching conditions for low-energy coefficients are also investigated. Due to new terms peculiar to open systems, the associated NG modes show diffusive gapless behaviors in contrast to the propagating NG mode in closed systems. We demonstrate two typical situations relevant to the condensed matter physics and high-energy physics, where diffusive type-A or type-B NG modes appear.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes arising from spontaneous symmetry breaking in nonequilibrium open systems, where conservation laws are violated due to coupling with an environment. The key hedge is that the construction is restricted to the semi-classical level (including terms up to O(pi_A^2)) and assumes the broken symmetry generators are irreducible under the unbroken symmetry transformation H.

## Method

The authors generalize the Callan-Coleman-Wess-Zumino (CCWZ) coset construction to the Schwinger-Keldysh (real-time) formalism, introducing doubled NG fields (pi_R, pi_A) that parametrize the average and difference parts of the doubled degrees of freedom. They construct the general G_A-invariant effective Lagrangian using Maurer-Cartan 1-forms and the adjoint field pi_A, respecting the basic constraints of the Schwinger-Keldysh EFT (vanishing condition, complex conjugation condition, and positivity of the imaginary part). The quadratic part of the effective Lagrangian yields inverse retarded/advanced Green's functions, from which dispersion relations are derived by solving det(G_R)^{-1}(omega, k) = 0.

## Key numbers

- For the driven-dissipative BEC (type-A NG mode): dispersion relation omega = -i (g / (m gamma_0)) k^2 + O(k^4), where g is the coupling constant, m the mass, and gamma_0 the dissipation parameter
- For the dissipative SU(2) x U(1) model (type-B NG mode): gapless mode dispersion omega = (plus or minus m - i gamma) / (m^2 + gamma^2) k^2 + O(k^4), and gapped partner omega = f^2 (plus or minus m - i gamma) + (plus or minus m + i gamma) / (m^2 + gamma^2) k^2 + O(k^4), where m is the mass parameter, gamma the dissipation coefficient, and f the decay constant
- The condition from probability conservation requires A >= 0, and when charge diffuses into the environment, gamma > 0

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach for NG modes from closed systems (Leutwyler 1993, Watanabe 2012, 2014) and nonequilibrium closed systems (Crossley 2015, Glorioso 2017) to open systems where conservation laws are violated. It generalizes the CCWZ coset construction (Coleman 1969, Callan 1969) to the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism for open systems, building on earlier work on dissipative hydrodynamics and open system EFT (Minami 2018, Sieberer 2015, Hongo 2018, Hayata 2018). The formalism enables future studies of low-energy theorems, loop corrections, the Mermin-Wagner theorem in open systems, and applications to cold atomic, condensed matter, high-energy, and active matter systems.
