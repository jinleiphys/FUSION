# From AdS/CFT correspondence to hydrodynamics

> We compute the correlation functions of R-charge currents and components of the stress-energy tensor in the strongly coupled large-N finite-temperature N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, following a recently formulated Minkowskian AdS/CFT prescription. We observe that in the long-distance, low-frequency limit, such correlators have the form dictated by hydrodynamics. We deduce from the calculations the R-charge diffusion constant and the shear viscosity. The value for the latter is in agreement with an earlier calculation based on the Kubo formula and absorption by black branes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the long-distance, low-frequency limit of retarded thermal correlation functions in strongly coupled large-\(N\) finite-temperature \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, computed via a Minkowskian AdS/CFT prescription, reproduces the universal forms dictated by hydrodynamics, with the caveat that the analysis is restricted to diffusive modes (R-charge diffusion and shear mode) and does not treat sound waves.

## Method

The authors compute two-point retarded Green's functions of R-charge currents and stress-energy tensor components in the strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory at finite temperature using a recently formulated Minkowskian AdS/CFT prescription. This involves solving linearized Maxwell equations in the five-dimensional near-horizon non-extremal three-brane background for the R-charge sector, and linearized Einstein equations for metric perturbations in the shear channel, imposing incoming-wave boundary conditions at the black brane horizon. The resulting correlators are expanded in the low-frequency, long-wavelength limit to extract their hydrodynamic form and the associated transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

* R-charge diffusion constant: \(D = 1/(2\pi T)\), independent of the 't Hooft coupling in the strong coupling limit \(g^2_{YM}N \rightarrow \infty\).
* Shear viscosity from Kubo formula: \(\eta = \frac{\pi}{8} N^2 T^3\), agreeing with an earlier calculation by the same authors based on absorption by black branes.
* Shear diffusion constant: \(\mathcal{D} = 1/(4\pi T)\), which is twice smaller than the R-charge diffusion constant.
* Entropy density: \(s = \frac{\pi^2}{2} N^2 T^3\), which is 3/4 of the zero-coupling value from Gubser, Klebanov, and Peet (1996).
* The diffusion pole position including the first correction: \(i\omega = D q^2 \left( 1 + \frac{q^2 \ln{2}}{4 \pi^2 T^2}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature AdS/CFT correspondence beyond Euclidean correlators, which decay exponentially at large separations, to Minkowskian correlators that exhibit universal long-time non-exponential tails characteristic of hydrodynamics. The results confirm the consistency of the gravity dual description with the hydrodynamic framework of Kadanoff and Martin (1963) and Landau and Lifshitz, and provide a nontrivial check of the Minkowskian AdS/CFT prescription formulated by the same authors in a companion paper. The computed shear viscosity agrees with the earlier absorption calculation by the same authors, and the R-charge diffusion constant represents a new prediction for strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma that can be compared with weak-coupling results from Jeon (1995), Jeon and Yaffe (1996), and others. This work enables future treatment of sound wave propagation and higher-order corrections to hydrodynamic transport in strongly coupled gauge theories.
