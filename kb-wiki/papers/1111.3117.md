# Quadrupole moments of spherical semi-magic nuclei within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems

> The quadrupole moments of odd neighbors of semi-magic lead and tin isotopes and $N=50,N=82$ isotones are calculated within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. Two sets of parameters, DF3 and DF3-a, fixed previously are used. They differ by the spin-orbit and effective tensor force parameters, the latter being significantly bigger in the DF3-a functional. Results for the two functionals turned out to be rather different. The functional DF3-a leads to quadrupole moments in reasonable agreement with the experimental ones for most nuclei examined.

## Key claim

The self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Fayans Energy Density Functional can reproduce experimental quadrupole moments of odd neighbors of semi-magic nuclei, but only when the DF3-a functional (with stronger effective tensor force) is used; the original DF3 functional systematically overestimates quadrupole moments due to an overly collective \(2^+_1\) state.

## Method

The authors solve the TFFS equations for the static effective field \(V(\omega=0)\) in the external quadrupole field \(V_0 = \sqrt{16\pi/5}\, r^2 Y_{20}\), using the Fayans Energy Density Functional with two parameter sets (DF3 and DF3-a) that differ in spin-orbit and effective tensor force parameters. For each odd nucleus, the quadrupole moment is computed as \(Q_\lambda = (u^2_\lambda - v^2_\lambda) V_\lambda\), where the Bogolyubov factor \((u^2_\lambda - v^2_\lambda)\) is obtained from the BCS scheme with blocking, and the matrix element \(V_\lambda\) is taken as the half-sum of values in the two neighboring even-even nuclei. Surface pairing is used throughout.

## Key numbers

- For the tin isotope chain (11 nuclei), the rms deviation between theory and experiment is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.167\) e b for DF3 and 0.164 e b for DF3-a.
- For the lead isotope chain, the rms deviation is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.386\) e b for DF3 and 0.277 e b for DF3-a.
- For the \(N=50\) isotone chain, the rms deviation is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.116\) e b for DF3 and 0.041 e b for DF3-a.
- For the \(N=82\) isotone chain, the rms deviation is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.119\) e b for DF3 and 0.093 e b for DF3-a.
- For all 42 odd-neutron nuclei combined, the rms deviation is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.240\) e b for DF3 and 0.189 e b for DF3-a.
- For the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{118}\)Sn, DF3 gives \(\omega_2 = 0.913\) MeV and \(B(E2) = 0.272\) e b, while DF3-a gives \(\omega_2 = 1.217\) MeV and \(B(E2) = 0.172\) e b.
- For the odd-proton neighbors of tin isotopes, the rms deviation is \(\sqrt{\overline{(\delta Q)^2_{\rm rms}}} = 0.688\) e b for DF3 and 0.249 e b for DF3-a.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously applied to magnetic moments (Tolokonnikov et al., 2011) and to \(2^+_1\) excitations in even-even tin and lead nuclei (Tolokonnikov et al., 2011) to the calculation of static quadrupole moments. It contrasts with phenomenological shell-model calculations for medium-mass nuclei (Brown et al., 1983) and for copper isotopes (Flanagan et al., 2009) by providing a fully self-consistent microscopic framework for heavy semi-magic nuclei. The results enable systematic predictions for quadrupole moments of radioactive nuclei far from stability, and demonstrate that the spin-orbit and effective tensor force parameters of the energy density functional can be constrained by quadrupole moment data, which are more sensitive to these parameters than masses or radii.
