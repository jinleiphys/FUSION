# Contribution of $SU(3)$ quadratic Casimir squared to rotational bands in the interacting boson model

> Rotational bands are commonly used in the analysis of the spectra of atomic nuclei. The early version of the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello has been foundational to the description of rotations in nuclei. The model is based on a unitary spectrum generating algebra $U(6)$ and an orthogonal (angular momentum) symmetry algebra $SO(3)$. A solvable limit of the model contains $SU(3)$ in its dynamical symmetry chain. The corresponding Hamiltonian is written as a linear combination of linear and quadratic Casimir invariants of all the algebras in the chain. Prompted by these facts, a Hamiltonian containing the $SU(3)$ quadratic Casimir squared is proposed to evaluate its effects on rotational bands. The additional term yields three undetermined parameters into the theory, which need be obtained from experiment. The lack of data does not allow one to perform a detailed numerical analysis, but a rather restricted one in terms of a one-parameter fit. Nevertheless, these additional terms provide a good description of rotational bands of two nuclei of interest, $^{156}\mathrm{Ga}$ and $^{234}\mathrm{U}$.

## Key claim

Adding a term proportional to the square of the $SU(3)$ quadratic Casimir operator to the standard interacting boson model Hamiltonian can improve the description of rotational band energy spacings, but the lack of experimental data restricts the analysis to a one-parameter fit rather than a full determination of the four parameters introduced by the new term.

## Method

The authors constructed a four-body $SU(3)$-invariant Hamiltonian term $H^{(4)}$ from the tensor product of two $SU(3)$ adjoint representations, using projection operators to decompose the product into irreducible components transforming as the 1, 8, and 27 representations of $SU(3)$. They simplified these components to linear combinations of the quadratic Casimir $C^{(2)}_{SU(3)}$ and its square, then performed one-parameter fits to the experimental energies of the first excited states of rotational bands in $^{156}$Gd and $^{234}$U, treating the bands as degenerate in the approximation.

## Key numbers

- For $^{156}$Gd (boson number $N=12$), the best one-parameter fit used $\alpha_1^2 = 0.0593964$ keV (with $c_5 = \alpha_8 = \alpha_{27} = 0$), corresponding to the $H_1$ term proportional to $[C^{(2)}_{SU(3)}]^2$.
- For $^{234}$U (boson number $N=12$), the best one-parameter fit used $\alpha_8^2 = 0.1500500$ keV (with $c_5 = \alpha_1 = \alpha_{27} = 0$), corresponding to the $H_8$ term.
- The eigenvalues of $H_1^{(4)}$ for an irrep $(\lambda,\mu)$ are given by $\frac{1}{192} [\lambda^2 + \mu^2 + \lambda\mu + 3(\lambda+\mu)]^2$.

## Context

This work extends the standard interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello (1975), which uses up to two-body terms in the $U(6) \supset SU(3) \supset SO(3)$ dynamical symmetry chain, by introducing a four-body $SU(3)$-invariant term. The approach parallels earlier studies by Stefanescu, Heyde, and Casten that showed how cubic IBM terms can describe triaxiality. The results enable future fits to more complete experimental data sets when they become available, potentially allowing a two-parameter fit using the effective parameters that multiply $C^{(2)}_{SU(3)}$ and $[C^{(2)}_{SU(3)}]^2$.
