# Spectral function at high missing energies and momenta

> The nuclear spectral function at high missing energies and momenta has been determined from a self-consistent calculation of the Green's function in nuclear matter using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results are compared with recent experimental data derived from ($e,e'p$) reactions on $^{12}C$. A rather good agreement is obtained if the Green's functions are calculated in a non-perturbative way.

# Spectral function at high missing energies and momenta

## Key claim
The nuclear spectral function at high missing energies and momenta, derived from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations in nuclear matter using the CD-Bonn interaction, agrees well with experimental ($e,e'p$) data on $^{12}C$ only when the Green's functions are calculated non-perturbatively and include multi-hole-multi-particle configurations beyond the two-hole-one-particle level.

## Method
The spectral function was computed from the retarded nuclear self-energy using three approximation schemes: a perturbative approach (second-order in the Brueckner G-matrix), a quasiparticle ladder approximation (all particle-particle hole-hole ladder diagrams with on-shell self-consistency), and the full self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach (ladder self-energy solved with complete spectral functions). The calculations were performed for nuclear matter at half the empirical saturation density, using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, and compared to experimental data from ($e,e'p$) reactions on $^{12}C$ at momenta above the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear matter density used was one half of the empirical saturation density, chosen to match the mean particle density of $^{12}C$.
- The Fermi momentum was $k_F = 209$ MeV/c.
- The correlated strength from the SCGF theory, integrated over the experimental region (missing energy above 40 MeV and momenta above the Fermi level), was 0.61, compared to the experimental value of $0.61 \pm 0.06$ (including a 4% rescattering contribution).
- The SCGF calculation was performed at a temperature of $T = 2$ MeV, the quasiparticle approximation at $T = 5$ MeV, and the perturbative approach at $T = 0$ MeV.
- The chemical potential in the quasiparticle and SCGF calculations was between $-20$ and $-30$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of short-range correlations in nuclear matter using self-consistent Green's functions (Müther, 2000; Dickhoff, 1998) and compares directly to experimental ($e,e'p$) data on $^{12}C$ from Rohe (2004). The SCGF approach improves upon the correlated basis function (CBF) theory (Rohe, 2004) by providing better agreement at high missing energies. The results demonstrate that short-range correlations are insensitive to the bulk structure of the nuclear system, enabling future studies of finite nuclei using similar methods, while long-range correlations at low missing energies require dedicated finite-system calculations.
