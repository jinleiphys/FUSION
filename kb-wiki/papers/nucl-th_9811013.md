# Electromagnetic form factors of baryons

## Key claim
Nucleon elastic and transition form factors can be simultaneously described by a collective model of baryons, provided that meson cloud couplings (via vector meson dominance) and relativistic corrections are included; the effect of relativistic corrections is small and shows up mainly at small values of $Q^2$.

## Method
The authors model baryons as an oblate top with vibrational and rotational excitations, and compute electromagnetic form factors by folding charge and magnetization distributions over the baryon volume in the Breit frame. Meson cloud effects are incorporated by coupling the Sachs form factors to isoscalar ($\omega$, $\phi$) and isovector ($\rho$) vector mesons, with the $\rho$ width treated via the prescription of IJL and perturbative QCD constraints imposed by scaling $Q^2$ with the strong coupling constant. A simultaneous fit is performed to all four nucleon elastic form factors and the nucleon charge radii, and transition form factors are compared to helicity amplitude data for the N(1520)$D_{13}$ and N(1535)$S_{11}$ resonances.

## Key numbers
- The dipole form factor used for normalization is $F_D = 1/(1 + Q^2/0.71)^2$, where $Q^2$ is in (GeV/c)$^2$.
- Ratio of neutron to proton helicity amplitudes for N(1535)$S_{11}$: experimental value $-0.84 \pm 0.15$ (Nimai1), collective model prediction $-0.81$, and with relativistic corrections $-0.90$.
- Ratio of $A^p_{3/2}$ to $A^p_{1/2}$ for N(1520)$D_{13}$: experimental value $-2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.4$ (Nimai2), collective model prediction $-2.53$, and with relativistic corrections $-2.66$.

## Context
This work extends the collective model of baryons introduced by Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (BIL) to a simultaneous analysis of elastic and transition form factors, incorporating new data presented at the Baryons '98 conference. The meson cloud treatment follows the vector meson dominance approach of IJL and the perturbative QCD scaling of GK. The results enable further tests of the collective model against upcoming electroproduction data for baryon resonances, and provide a framework for comparing nonrelativistic and relativistically corrected descriptions of baryon structure.
