# Effects of Magnetic Fields in Hot White Dwarfs

> In this work, we study the effects of temperature on magnetic white dwarfs. We model their interior as a nuclei lattice surrounded by a relativistic free Fermi gas of electrons, accounting for effects from temperature, Landau levels and anomalous magnetic moment. We find that, at low densities (corresponding to the outer regions of star), both temperature and magnetic field effects play an important role in the calculation of microscopic thermodynamical quantities. To study macroscopic stellar structures within a general-relativistic approach, we solve numerically the coupled Einstein's-Maxwell's equations for fixed entropy per particle configurations and discuss how temperature affects stellar magnetic field profiles, masses and radii.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for white dwarfs with central magnetic fields up to ~10^13 G (below the critical field of 4.4x10^13 G), temperature effects significantly modify the equation of state at low densities and alter stellar mass-radius relations, but magnetic field effects at this strength are not large enough to change macroscopic stellar masses and radii.

## Method
The authors modeled white dwarf interiors as a lattice of Carbon nuclei surrounded by a relativistic free Fermi gas of electrons, including finite temperature, Landau level quantization, and anomalous magnetic moment effects. They solved the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations numerically for axisymmetric configurations with fixed entropy per particle, using a current function j^phi = f_0 (epsilon + P) to generate poloidal magnetic fields, and expanded the fields in Green's functions iteratively.

## Key numbers
- Maximum white dwarf mass increases from 1.397 M_sun to 1.419 M_sun due to thermal effects (higher entropy per particle), with radii ~1000 km
- Magnetic field effects cause a 1% increase in equatorial direction and 1.8% increase in poloidal direction at energy density epsilon = 0.1 MeV/fm^3 for the S/A = 0.2 case (T ~ 10^10 K)
- Typical white dwarf effective temperatures range from 3,000 to 140,000 K, core temperatures from 10^6 to 10^7 K
- Atmospheric magnetic fields range from 10^3 to 10^9 G; central magnetic fields studied up to ~10^13 G
- Up to 20% of white dwarfs have surface magnetic fields of B = 10^7 G or larger
- Central mass density of white dwarfs varies from 10^6 to 10^8 g/cm^3
- The critical field where electron cyclotron energy equals rest mass is B_crit = 4.4 x 10^13 G

## Context
This work extends the relativistic formalism of Strickland (2012) from nucleons in neutron stars to electrons in white dwarfs, and builds on previous studies of magnetized compact objects by Franzon (2016) and Bonazzola (1993). It is the first simultaneous treatment of temperature and magnetic field effects in the white dwarf equation of state. The results enable future studies of pycnonuclear fusion reactions and crystalline structure in magnetic white dwarfs, and confirm that Newtonian approaches (as used by Chatterjee 2016) overestimate magnetic field effects on stellar masses.
