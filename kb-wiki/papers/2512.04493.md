# Nucleon to Roper transition amplitudes and electromagnetic form factors

> The second excitation of the nucleon, the Roper, has properties differentiated from other low-lying nucleon resonances. Their properties challenge our understanding of the structure of the baryons in terms of the degrees of freedom from QCD. In the present work we discuss the properties of the Roper resonance and the nucleon to Roper electromagnetic transition, based on the quark degrees of freedom, that are expected to dominate for large square momentum transfer $Q^2$. We also discuss the analytic structure of the transition amplitudes in the low-$Q^2$ region, and how the contributions of baryon-meson states can help to describe the low and intermediate $Q^2$ data, and the nature of the Roper.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Roper resonance N(1440) can be interpreted as the first radial excitation of the nucleon, with valence quark degrees of freedom dominating at large momentum transfers (above Q^2 = 2 GeV^2), but acknowledges that the model calculations fail to describe data in the low Q^2 region (0-1.5 GeV^2), suggesting a hybrid structure where baryon-meson contributions become important at low Q^2.

## Method

The author uses the covariant spectator quark model, where baryons are treated as three-quark systems with wave functions derived from SU(6) symmetry and radial wave functions determined phenomenologically from nucleon elastic form factor data. The Roper wave function is obtained by imposing orthogonality with the nucleon wave function, requiring no additional free parameters. The electromagnetic transition current is calculated using relativistic impulse approximation, and results are compared with JLab/CLAS data for helicity amplitudes and transition form factors. Additionally, light-front holographic calculations from AdS/QCD are presented for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance is identified as N(1440) with J^P = 1/2^+, mass M_R = 1.440 GeV
- JLab/CLAS data cover the range Q^2 = 0.3-4.6 GeV^2 for helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}
- Model calculations agree well with data above Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, supporting the radial excitation interpretation
- The pseudothreshold occurs at Q^2 = -(M_R - M)^2, where M is the nucleon mass
- The fine-structure constant alpha is approximately 1/137
- For the second radial excitation (tentatively N(1880)), amplitudes converge with Roper and nucleon amplitudes above Q^2 = 4 GeV^2
- The nucleon equivalent S_{1/2} amplitude has a zero near Q^2 = 7.0 GeV^2
- Nucleon elastic form factors G_E and G_M fall as 1/Q^4 at large Q^2
- Holographic model predictions are expected to be accurate above Q^2 = 2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to nucleon elastic form factors (Ramalho, earlier works) and the gamma* N to Delta(1232) transition to the Roper resonance. It contradicts models based solely on baryon-meson structure, which cannot reproduce the observed falloff of amplitudes at large Q^2. The results enable future measurements at JLab-12 GeV to test predictions for extremely large Q^2 values, and support the interpretation that the Roper has a hybrid structure with quark dominance at high Q^2 and meson cloud contributions at low Q^2, consistent with dynamical coupled-channel models like the Sato-Lee/EBAC model and ANL-Osaka model.
