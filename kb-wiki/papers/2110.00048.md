# Collinear factorization of diphoton photoproduction at next to leading order

> We calculate in the framework of collinear factorization the amplitude for the photoproduction of a near forward large mass diphoton at leading twist and next to leading order (NLO) in $α_s$. We demonstrate the validity of factorization at this order, which was never achieved for such a reaction where the coefficient function describes a $2 \to 3$ hard process. While the Born order amplitude was purely imaginary and only probed the $x=\pm ξ$ cross-over line of generalized parton distributions (GPD) domain, the NLO result contains both a real and an imaginary part and probes the whole domain of definition of quark GPDs. The phenomenology of our results for medium (JLab) and higher energy (EIC) experiments will be developed in a future study.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that collinear factorization holds at next-to-leading order (NLO) in $\alpha_s$ for a $2 \to 3$ hard process, specifically the photoproduction of a near-forward large-mass diphoton; the NLO result probes the entire domain of quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs), unlike the Born-order amplitude which only probed the $x = \pm \xi$ crossover line.

## Method

The authors calculated the NLO amplitude for the reaction $\gamma + N \to \gamma + \gamma + N'$ in the collinear factorization framework at leading twist, using dimensional regularization to handle divergences. They computed contributions from self-energy insertions, vertex corrections, box diagrams, and pentagonal diagrams, summing over all permutations of the three photons. The divergent parts of the NLO amplitude were shown to be absorbable into the GPDs via the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, thereby proving factorization at this order.

## Key numbers

- The Born-order amplitude coefficient function $C^q_0(x,\xi)$ is proportional to $\mathrm{Im}(1/(x+\xi-i0))$, yielding a purely imaginary amplitude.
- The NLO coefficient function $C^q_{coll}$ contains a factor $\alpha_S C_F / (4\pi)$ multiplying terms with $\log((x+\xi)/(2\xi))$ and a constant $-3$.
- The divergent part of the pentagonal diagram contributes a factor $(2/\varepsilon) \cdot (\alpha_S C_F/(4\pi))$ times terms involving $(x-\xi)/\xi$ and $(x+\xi)/\xi$ logarithms.
- The total divergent part of the amplitude for a given photon permutation is $-(2/\varepsilon) \cdot (\alpha_S C_F/(4\pi)) \cdot Tr[i\mathcal{M}^0 \slashed{p}]$ multiplied by $(3 + \frac{x+\xi}{\xi}\log(-(x-\xi)/(2\xi)) - \frac{x-\xi}{\xi}\log((x+\xi)/(2\xi)))$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order studies of diphoton photoproduction (Pedrak 2017, Pedrak 2020) and provides the first NLO factorization proof for a $2 \to 3$ hard process, which had previously only been assumed at leading order for reactions studied by Ivanov (2002), Kumano (2009), and Boussarie (2016). The result is structurally analogous to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) as studied by Mueller (1998) and Moutarde (2013), but with the coefficient function describing a $2 \to 3$ process rather than $2 \to 2$. The phenomenological consequences for experiments at JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will be developed in a future publication.
