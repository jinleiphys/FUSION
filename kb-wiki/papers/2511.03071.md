# Constraining capture cross sections using proton inelastic scattering as a surrogate reaction

> The surrogate reaction method is an alternative to direct measurements of compound nuclear reaction cross sections. We introduce theory tools for extracting capture cross sections from experiments that use proton inelastic scattering as a surrogate reaction mechanism. This makes it possible to constrain compound nucleus decay models which are typically the largest source of uncertainty in capture cross section calculations. This letter describes the theory developments that were used to simultaneously infer $^{89}$Y$(p,γ)$ and $^{89}$Zr$(n,γ)$ cross sections from $^{90}$Zr$(p,p'γ)$ surrogate measurements.

## Key claim

The authors introduce new theory tools, including two-step reaction contributions, Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) parameter inference, and corrections for Porter-Thomas fluctuations, to extract capture cross sections from proton inelastic scattering surrogate experiments, and demonstrate that these tools can simultaneously constrain the previously unknown \(^{89}\)Zr\((n,\gamma)\) cross section and the known \(^{89}\)Y\((p,\gamma)\) cross section from existing \(^{90}\)Zr\((p,p'\gamma)\) data, with the caveat that the results depend on the assumed low-energy behavior of the M1 strength function.

## Method

The spin-parity population \(F_{pp'}^{\rm{CN}}\) of \(^{90}\)Zr following proton inelastic scattering was calculated using microscopic QRPA transition densities with the D1M-Gogny interaction, folded with the JLM-B nucleon-nucleon interaction, and implemented in coupled-channels calculations with FRESCOX under the distorted Born wave approximation; for the first time, two-step \((p,d)(d,p')\) contributions were included via two-step DWBA. The decay of the populated \(^{90}\)Zr compound nucleus was modeled with a modified version of the reaction code YAHFC, using the Kopecky-Uhl E1 strength function and a composite Gilbert-Cameron level density model, with 14 parameters fitted to the experimental \(P_{p'\gamma}\) coincidence probabilities via affine-invariant MCMC sampling (200 walkers, 5000 iterations) using the COMMCAS code. The fit accounted for the impact of partial-width fluctuations (Porter-Thomas) on the \(\gamma\)-emission probability due to the low density of states available in the particle emission channels near the neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of \(^{90}\)Zr: \(S_n = 11.97\) MeV; proton separation energy: \(S_p = 8.35\) MeV.
- Proton beam energy for the surrogate experiment: 28.56 MeV at the K150 Cyclotron facility at Texas A&M University.
- The spin-parity population was calculated for excitation energies \(E_{\rm{ex}} = 0.1\) to 20 MeV, with the result at \(E_{\rm{ex}} = 12\) MeV shown in Fig. 1; the maximum total angular momentum considered was \(J_{\rm{max}} = 10\).
- The giant dipole resonance peak was found at \(16.9 \pm 0.1\) MeV; the pygmy dipole resonance centroid at \(9.2 \pm 0.49\) MeV; the spin-flip M1 resonance centroid at \(8.6 \pm 0.57\) MeV.
- Average \(s\)-wave radiative width: \(\Gamma_\gamma = 195 \pm 50\) meV; average \(s\)-wave neutron resonance spacing: \(D_0 = 38 \pm 10\) eV.
- The TENDL-astro-2023 evaluation for the \(^{89}\)Zr\((n,\gamma)\) cross section is a factor of 2.7 lower than the authors' result.
- The uncertainty in the proton-capture cross section from optical potential variations is about 10% above 4 MeV proton incident energy and 5% below 4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the surrogate reaction method, previously demonstrated for \((p,d)\) and \((d,p)\) reactions (Escher 2018, Andrew 2019), to proton inelastic scattering as a surrogate mechanism, building on earlier \(^{208}\)Pb\((p,p'\gamma)\) measurements at the GSI/FAIR storage ring that extracted neutron capture cross sections for \(^{207}\)Pb. The new theory tools enable application to nuclei near shell closures and away from stability, where low level densities make Porter-Thomas corrections essential, and allow simultaneous extraction of both \((p,\gamma)\) and \((n,\gamma)\) cross sections from a single surrogate measurement. The results for \(^{89}\)Zr\((n,\gamma)\) are compared to the nuclear data evaluations JENDL-5.0, JEFF-3.3, and TENDL-astro-2023, and the extracted GSF is compared to experimental data from Netterdon (2015), Fedorets (2013), and others.
