# Dispersion-theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors: Inclusion of time-like data

> We update a recent dispersion--theoretical fit to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors by including the existing data in the time--like region. We show that while the time--like data for the proton can be described consistently with the existing world space--like data, this is not the case for the neutron. Another measurement of the process $e^+ e^- \to \bar n n$ is called for. We furthermore sharpen the previous estimate of the separation between the perturbative and the non--perturbative regime, which is characterized by a scale parameter $Λ^2 \simeq 10\,$GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a dispersion-theoretical fit with three isoscalar and three isovector poles can describe the time-like proton form factor data consistently with the existing space-like data, but this is not possible for the neutron; the authors call for another measurement of the process \(e^+ e^- \to \bar n n\) before increasing the number of meson poles.

## Method

The authors perform a dispersion-theoretical analysis of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, separating the spectral functions into a hadronic (vector meson pole) component and a quark (pQCD) component characterized by a scale parameter \(\Lambda^2\). They fit the magnetic form factors in the time-like region using three isoscalar and three isovector poles, keeping the number of poles fixed to avoid washing out predictive power, and compare the results to fits using only space-like data.

## Key numbers

- The scale parameter separating perturbative and non-perturbative regimes is \(\Lambda^2 = 9.73\) GeV\(^2\) for the space-like fit (fit 1) and \(\Lambda^2 = 12.0\) GeV\(^2\) when including time-like data (fit 2).
- The \(\chi^2\)/datum for the space-like fit (fit 1) is 1.06; for the fit including time-like data (fit 2) it increases to 1.47.
- The isoscalar pole masses are fixed at \(M_\omega = 0.782\) GeV, \(M_\phi = 1.019\) GeV, and \(M_{S'} = 1.60\) GeV; the isovector pole masses are \(M_{\rho'} = 1.68\) GeV, \(M_{\rho''} = 1.45\) GeV, and \(M_{\rho'''} = 1.69\) GeV in fit 1, while in fit 2 the \(\rho'\) mass becomes \(M_{\rho'} = 1.40\) GeV.
- The QCD parameters are \(\gamma = 2.148\) and \(Q_0^2 = 0.35\) GeV\(^2\).
- Removing the FENICE neutron time-like data from the fit improves the \(\chi^2\)/datum to 1.37.
- The allowed range for \(\Lambda^2\) to keep \(\chi^2\)/datum below 1.72 is from 9.3 to 16.3 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical analysis of Hammer, Meißner, and Drechsel (1995) (Ref. [MMD]) by including time-like data that were previously omitted, and improves upon the earlier analysis of Höhler et al. (1976). The results enable sharper constraints on the separation between perturbative and non-perturbative QCD regimes, but also highlight the need for new experimental data on the neutron time-like form factor to resolve inconsistencies with the FENICE experiment.
