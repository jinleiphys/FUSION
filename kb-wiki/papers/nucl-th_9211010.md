# Physics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Central nuclear collisions at energies far above 1 GeV/nucleon may provide for conditions, where the transition from highly excited hadronic matter into quark matter or quark-gluon plasma can be probed. Here I review our current understanding of the physical properties of a quark-gluon plasma and review ideas about the nature of, and signals for, the deconfinement transition. I also give a detailed presentation of recent progress in the treatment of the formation of a thermalized state at the parton level.

## Key claim

Central nuclear collisions at energies far above 1 GeV/nucleon may provide conditions where the transition from highly excited hadronic matter into quark-gluon plasma can be probed, but many aspects of this transition, including its order, the precise value of the critical energy density, and how fast thermal equilibrium is established, remain matters of debate.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to the quark-gluon plasma equation of state, comparing hadronic models (Hagedorn resonance gas, sigma-condensate models, dispersion relations, QCD sum rules) with quark-gluon descriptions based on perturbative QCD and the MIT bag model. It also surveys results from lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(3) gauge theory and from studies of bubble nucleation dynamics and phase coexistence.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature for a hadronic resonance gas is approximately \(T_c \sim 200\) MeV, derived from the exponential mass spectrum \(\rho(m) \simeq m^a \exp(m/m_0)\) with \(m_0 \approx 200\) MeV.
- The QCD scale parameter is \(\Lambda \simeq 150\) MeV.
- The vacuum quark condensate is \(\langle 0|\bar{q}q|0\rangle = -(225 \pm 25\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- In the MIT bag model with \(B \simeq (200\ \text{MeV})^4\), the critical temperature for phase coexistence between a pion gas and a free quark-gluon gas is \(T_c \simeq 0.75 B^{1/4} \simeq 150\) MeV, with a latent heat \(\Delta\varepsilon_c \simeq 4B \simeq 0.8\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The surface tension of the interface between hadronic and quark-gluon phases is \(\sigma \simeq 20\)–\(50\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
- In baryon-rich hadronic matter at \(T = 160\) MeV and baryon density \(\rho = 4\rho_0\), the mass shift of kaons is of order 100 MeV, positive for \(K^+\) and negative for \(K^-\).
- The ratio \(K^+/K^-\) in dense baryon-rich matter at \(T \simeq 150\) MeV is limited to about 5, matching the ratio observed in heavy ion collisions at the AGS.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the QCD phase transition initiated by Cabibbo and Parisi (1975), who speculated that the Hagedorn temperature signals a transition to deconfined quark-gluon plasma, and by Collins and Perry, Baym and Chin, McLerran et al., and Shuryak, who pioneered the quark-gluon approach. It also discusses medium modifications of hadron structure via sigma-condensate models, dispersion relations, and QCD sum rules, and connects these to astrophysical implications for neutron stars and the early universe. The paper enables further experimental searches for quark-gluon plasma signals at the Brookhaven AGS and CERN SPS, and motivates studies of strangelet production and the dynamics of phase coexistence.
