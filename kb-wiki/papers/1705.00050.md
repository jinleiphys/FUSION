# Multistage Monte-Carlo simulation of jet modification in a static medium

> The modification of hard jets in an extended static medium held at a fixed temperature is studied using three different Monte-Carlo event generators (LBT, MATTER, MARTINI). Each event generator contains a different set of assumptions regarding the energy and virtuality of the partons within a jet versus the energy scale of the medium, and hence, applies to a different epoch in the space-time history of the jet evolution. For the first time, modeling is developed where a jet may sequentially transition from one generator to the next, on a parton-by-parton level, providing a detailed simulation of the space-time evolution of medium modified jets over a much broader dynamic range than has been attempted previously in a single calculation. Comparisons are carried out for different observables sensitive to jet quenching, including the parton fragmentation function and the azimuthal distribution of jet energy around the jet axis. The effect of varying the boundary between different generators is studied and a theoretically motivated criterion for the location of this boundary is proposed. The importance of such an approach with coupled generators to the modeling of jet quenching is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multistage Monte-Carlo simulation coupling three different jet quenching formalisms (MATTER, MARTINI, LBT) on a parton-by-parton basis can describe jet modification in a static medium, but the results depend significantly on the choice of the separation scale \(Q_0\) between the high-virtuality (MATTER) and low-virtuality (MARTINI or LBT) regimes, with a dynamically determined \(Q_0 = \sqrt{2E\hat{q}}\) proposed as a theoretically motivated criterion.

## Method

The authors simulated a single hard quark propagating through a static "brick" medium of fixed temperature 250 MeV and variable length (4 or 8 fm). Three Monte-Carlo event generators were used: MATTER for high-virtuality partons (\(Q^2 \gg \sqrt{\hat{q}E}\)) using medium-modified DGLAP evolution, and either MARTINI (based on BDMPS/AMY rate equations) or LBT (based on higher-twist formalism with Boltzmann transport) for low-virtuality partons. Partons were transitioned from MATTER to MARTINI/LBT when their virtuality dropped below a separation scale \(Q_0\), which was either fixed (1, 2, or 3 GeV) or dynamically determined from the parton energy and medium transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\). The final parton fragmentation functions and azimuthal energy distributions were compared across different coupling schemes and brick sizes.

## Key numbers

- Medium temperature: fixed at 250 MeV throughout the static brick.
- Brick lengths studied: 4 fm and 8 fm.
- Fixed separation scales \(Q_0\) tested: 1, 2, and 3 GeV.
- Initial quark energies: 50 GeV and 200 GeV.
- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) used in MARTINI and LBT portions; running \(\alpha_s\) used in MATTER.
- Minimum \(Q_0\) set to 1 GeV for partons outside the medium or when the dynamical formula yields a value below 1 GeV.
- Dynamical separation scale formula: \(Q_0^2 = \sqrt{2E\hat{q}}\), with \(\hat{q} = C_R \alpha_s \mu_D^2 T \log(6ET/\mu_D^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier brick-medium comparisons by the TECHQM and JET collaborations (Armesto et al., 2011), which studied event-averaged energy loss of leading partons using separate formalisms. The JETSCAPE Collaboration here provides the first parton-by-parton coupling of multiple generators (MATTER, MARTINI, LBT) to simulate the full space-time evolution of jet showers across a broader dynamic range than any single formalism previously allowed. The results serve as benchmarks for future JETSCAPE event generators and enable systematic studies of how different energy loss mechanisms dominate at different stages of jet evolution, with the ultimate goal of modeling jet modification in realistic heavy-ion collisions with dynamically evolving media.
