# Chiral symmetry amplitudes in the s-wave isoscalar and isovector channels and the $σ, f_0(980), a_0 (980)$ scalar mesons

> We use a nonpertubative approach which combines coupled channel Lippmann Schwinger equations with meson-meson potentials provided by the lowest order chiral Lagrangian. By means of one parameter, a cut off in the momentum of the loop integrals, which results of the order of 1 GeV, we obtain singularities in the S-wave amplitudes corresponding to the $σ$, f_0 and a_0 resonances. The $ππ\to ππ, ππ\to K \bar{K}$ phase shifts and inelasticities in the T = 0 scalar channel are well reproduced as well as the $π^0 η$ and $K \bar{K}$ mass distributions in the T = 1 channel. Furthermore, the total and partial decay widths of the f_0 and a_0 resonances are properly reproduced including also the decay into the $γγ$ channel. The results seem to indicate that chiral symmetry constraints at low energy and unitarity in coupled channels is the basic information contained in the meson-meson interaction below $\sqrt{s} \simeq 1.2 GeV$.

## Key claim

Using only a single parameter (a momentum cutoff of order 1 GeV) in a nonperturbative coupled-channel approach, the authors generate the σ, f₀(980), and a₀(980) resonances as poles in the S-wave scattering amplitudes, suggesting that chiral symmetry constraints at low energy combined with unitarity in coupled channels contain the essential information for meson-meson interactions below √s ≈ 1.2 GeV.

## Method

The authors start from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for pseudoscalar mesons and use the tree-level amplitudes as potentials in coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations with relativistic meson propagators. The channels are K̅K and ππ for isoscalar (T=0) and K̅K and πη for isovector (T=1) S-wave scattering. The loop integrals are regularized with a single momentum cutoff q_max, which is the only free parameter, and the on-shell factorization of the amplitudes reduces the integral equations to algebraic ones.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum q_max = 1.1 GeV (with corresponding Λ = (q_max² + m_K²)^(1/2) = 1.2 GeV) gives best agreement with data
- f₀(980) pole position: P⁰_f₀ = (981.4 + i 22.4) MeV, corresponding to mass M_f₀ = 981.4 MeV and width Γ_f₀ = 44.8 MeV
- σ pole from Δ_π zero: P⁰_σ = (468.5 + i 193.6) MeV; from Δ_c zero: P⁰_σ = (469.5 + i 178.6) MeV, giving Γ_σ ≈ 400 MeV
- a₀(980) pole position: P⁰_a₀ = (973.2 + i 85.0) MeV, corresponding to width Γ_a₀ = 170 MeV
- Peak positions in |t₁₂|²: √s ≈ 987 MeV for f₀ and √s ≈ 982 MeV for a₀

## Context

This work extends the successful application of chiral Lagrangians combined with unitarization from the meson-baryon sector (as done in refs. 34,35) to the meson-meson scalar sector. It contrasts with approaches requiring many parameters (around 25 in refs. 4-7,21 or 5-6 in refs. 8,15) by using only one parameter, and differs from standard chiral perturbation theory which is limited to energies below 600-700 MeV. The results enable further study of the nature of scalar mesons (q̅q states, multiquark states, or K̅K molecules) and provide a framework for investigating glueball candidates.
