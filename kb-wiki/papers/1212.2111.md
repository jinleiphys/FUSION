# Quark-diquark model for p(\bar p)-p elastic scattering at high energies

> A model for elastic scattering of protons at high energies based on the quark-diquark representation of the proton is discussed. The predictions of the model are compared with experimental data for the differential elastic cross-sections from available databases

# Quark-diquark model for p(p̅)-p elastic scattering at high energies

## Key claim
The quark-diquark model, originally proposed by Tsarev (1979), provides a reasonable description of the differential elastic cross-section for both proton-proton and antiproton-proton scattering across a wide energy range (√s ≥ 546 GeV), but the model overestimates the value of the dip at −t ≈ 1.3 GeV², albeit less severely than the simplified version of Bialas and Bzdak (2007) which omitted the real part of the scattering amplitude.

## Method
The authors implement the quark-diquark model in which the proton is treated as a bound state of a quark and a diquark, and the elastic scattering amplitude is constructed from one- and two-pomeron exchanges between quark-quark, diquark-diquark, and quark-diquark pairs. The amplitude includes a nonzero real part derived from the standard Pomeron parametrization, with free parameters (quark-quark cross-section and proton radius) fitted to experimental data. The model predictions are compared with published differential elastic cross-section data for antiproton-proton scattering at √s = 546 GeV and 1960 GeV, and for proton-proton scattering at √s = 7 TeV from the TOTEM Collaboration.

## Key numbers
- The model is compared with antiproton-proton elastic scattering data at √s = 546 GeV and √s = 1960 GeV, and with proton-proton data at √s = 7 TeV.
- The Pomeron trajectory slope is set to α̃ = 0.15 GeV⁻².
- The quark radius is r₁ = 0.173 r and the diquark radius is r₂ = 0.316 r, where r is the proton radius (fitted to data).
- The quark mass is m₁ = m/3 and the diquark mass is m₂ = 2m/3, where m is the proton mass.
- The reference energy scale is s₀ = 1 GeV².
- The model overestimates the dip value at −t ≈ 1.3 GeV², though less than the model of Bialas and Bzdak (2007) which had no real part of the scattering amplitude.

## Context
This work extends the quark-diquark model of Tsarev (1979) to describe elastic scattering at LHC energies, following the earlier application by Bialas and Bzdak (2007) which omitted the real part of the amplitude and consequently overestimated the dip value more severely. The model was previously applied by Niccolai and collaborators (2012) to describe the TOTEM data at √s = 7 TeV at the same level of accuracy. The results enable further refinement of the model through more sophisticated form factors and additional sources for the real part of the scattering amplitude, as well as broader comparison with experimental data.
