# On the phase structure and thermodynamics of QCD

> We discuss the phase structure and thermodynamics of QCD by means of dynamical chiral effective models. Quark and meson fluctuations are included via the functional renormalization group. We study the influence of confinement in addition to the impact of fluctuations by comparing the results of the chiral models to their Polyakov-loop extended versions. Furthermore, we discuss the mass sensitivity of the phase structure and thermodynamics and find interesting modifications close to the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when both quark and meson fluctuations are included via the functional renormalization group, and when the matter back-coupling to the gauge sector is properly accounted for, the chiral and deconfinement transitions remain close together throughout the phase diagram, eliminating the previously observed splitting that had supported speculation about a quarkyonic matter region.

## Method

The authors employ the Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model as a low-energy effective truncation of two-flavor QCD, solving the Wetterich functional renormalization group flow equation for the free energy. They incorporate the matter back-coupling to the gauge sector by making the Polyakov-loop potential parameter T0 dependent on flavor number, temperature, and chemical potential via a running coupling analysis. The initial conditions at the UV scale Lambda = 950 MeV are fixed to reproduce vacuum observables (f_pi = 93 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, m_q = 297 MeV, m_sigma = 540 MeV), and the explicit chiral symmetry breaking parameter c is varied to study mass sensitivity.

## Key numbers

* Physical pion mass point: chiral crossover at T approximately 160 MeV at low chemical potential, with the critical endpoint located at (mu_CEP, T_CEP) approximately (290, 20) MeV in the PQM model
* Chiral limit: critical temperature decreases by approximately 20 MeV compared to physical masses; the PQM model shows only one critical point surviving on the inner branch at (mu_CP, T_CP) = (255, 27) MeV
* For the QM model in the chiral limit, two critical points appear: one on the outer transition branch at low T and a further CP at higher temperature
* The chiral transition line defined by sigma(T_chi, mu_chi)/sigma(0,0) = 1/2 hits the mu axis at mu approximately 322 MeV for the QM model at physical masses
* The parameter hat_gamma = 0.85 is chosen for all main-text figures, as the lowest value hat_gamma = 0.5 leads to T0(mu) tending to zero already at mu approximately 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous functional renormalization group studies of the PQM model by Herbst (2010) and Skokov (2010) with a more refined inclusion of matter back-coupling and systematic study of mass sensitivity. The results contradict earlier mean-field calculations by Schaefer (2007) that found a splitting of chiral and deconfinement transitions at finite chemical potential, and instead agree with recent nonperturbative Dyson-Schwinger studies by Fischer (2011, 2012) for two- and (2+1)-flavors. The findings enable improved comparisons with lattice QCD at small chemical potential and provide constraints on the location of the critical endpoint, excluding its existence at low mu/T ratios.
