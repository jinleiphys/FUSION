# Saturation physics and angular correlations at RHIC and LHC

> We investigate the angular correlation between pions and photons produced in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC and proton-lead collisions at LHC using the Color Glass Condensate formalism and make predictions for the dependence of the production cross section on the angle between the pion and photon at different rapidities and transverse momenta. Measuring this dependence would shed further light on the role of high gluon density and saturation dynamics at RHIC and LHC.

# Saturation physics and angular correlations at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The angular correlation between pions and photons produced in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC and proton-lead collisions at LHC, computed using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, is predicted to show a decorrelation when the two particles are separated by large rapidity; however, the quantitative results depend on the dipole scattering probability \(N_F\) which must be solved numerically, and the formalism does not include gluon-initiated contributions from the projectile that fragment into photons, which may be numerically important at mid-rapidity.

## Method

The cross section for inclusive photon-plus-parton production in quark-target scattering is derived within the CGC formalism, expressed in terms of the quark-antiquark dipole scattering probability \(N_F\). This parton-level cross section is then convoluted with CTEQ6 quark distribution functions for the projectile proton (or deuteron, using isospin symmetry) and KKP pion fragmentation functions to obtain the hadron-photon production cross section in deuteron-gold (or proton-lead) collisions. The angular correlation function \(C(\theta)\) is defined as the ratio of the differential cross section at a given angle \(\theta\) between the pion and photon to its integral over all angles, and is evaluated numerically for various rapidity and transverse momentum configurations.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC with both pion and photon at mid-rapidity, the correlation function at \(\theta \rightarrow 180\) degrees increases by almost an order of magnitude as the transverse momenta of the pion and photon increase from 2 GeV to 10 GeV.
- For proton-lead collisions at LHC with \(\sqrt{s} = 8800\) GeV, the transverse momentum dependence of the correlation function is much weaker in the back-to-back configuration compared to RHIC.
- An isolation cut parameter \(\epsilon = 0.1\) is applied for the photon at RHIC in the configuration with \(y_h = 0\) and \(y_\gamma = 4\), which severely diminishes the cross section unless the rapidity separation is large and negative or the transverse momenta differ by orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism from single inclusive hadron production and two-hadron correlations (which involve complicated higher-point functions of Wilson lines) to the simpler case of photon-pion correlations, where only the dipole scattering probability \(N_F\) is needed. Prior work on two-hadron correlations (Jalilian-Marian, 2008) required either solving JIMWLK equations for higher-point functions or making a mean-field Gaussian approximation, which may neglect important correlations. The photon-pion correlation provides a cleaner probe of saturation dynamics because it avoids hadronization uncertainties, and experimental measurement at RHIC and LHC could help discriminate between CGC-based predictions and more traditional approaches (such as those that also fit the forward rapidity suppression data).
