# Femtoscopic correlation function for the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state

> We have conducted a study of the femtoscopic correlation functions for the $D^0D^{*+}$ and $D^+D^{*0}$ channels that build the $T_{cc}$ state. We develop a formalism that allows us to factorize the scattering amplitudes outside the integrals in the formulas, and the integrals involve the range of the strong interaction explicitly. For a source of size of 1 fm, we find values for the correlation functions of the $D^0 D^{*+}$ and $D^+D^{*0}$ channels at the origin around 30 and 2.5, respectively, and we see these observables converging to unity already for relative momenta of the order of 200 MeV. We conduct tests to see the relevance of the different contributions to the correlation function and find that it mostly provides information on the scattering length, since the presence of the source function in the correlation function introduces an effective cut in the loop integrals that makes them quite insensitive to the range of the interaction.

# Femtoscopic correlation function for the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state

## Key claim
The femtoscopic correlation functions for the $D^0D^{*+}$ and $D^+D^{*0}$ channels that build the $T_{cc}$ state are shown to be primarily sensitive to the scattering length and largely insensitive to the range of the strong interaction, because the source function introduces an effective cutoff in the loop integrals that suppresses sensitivity to the momentum cutoff $q_\text{max}$.

## Method
The authors develop a momentum-space formalism for the femtoscopic correlation function that factorizes the scattering amplitudes outside the integrals, using a separable s-wave potential regularized by a cutoff $q_\text{max}$. The correlation function is computed via the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian source function, incorporating coupled-channel effects from the $D^0D^{*+}$ and $D^+D^{*0}$ channels, where the $T$-matrix elements are taken from the hidden gauge approach of Feijoo et al. (2021). Tests are performed by replacing the full $T$-matrix with approximations using only the scattering length, or the scattering length plus effective range, and by varying the cutoff parameter $q_\text{max}$ in the loop integrals.

## Key numbers
- For a source size of $R = 1$ fm, the correlation function at the origin for the $D^0D^{*+}$ channel is approximately 30, and for the $D^+D^{*0}$ channel it is approximately 2.5.
- The correlation functions converge to unity for relative momenta of the order of 200 MeV.
- The scattering lengths from the model are $a_{D^0D^{*+}} = (7.78 - 1.82i)$ fm and $a_{D^+D^{*0}} = (2.07 - 1.21i)$ fm.
- The effective ranges are $r_{0,D^0D^{*+}} = -3.49$ fm and $r_{0,D^+D^{*0}} = (-0.06 - 2.27i)$ fm.
- The experimental scattering lengths from LHCb are $a^\text{exp}_{D^0D^{*+}} = (7.16 \pm 0.51 - 1.85 \pm 0.28i)$ fm and $a^\text{exp}_{D^+D^{*0}} = (1.76 - 1.82i)$ fm.
- The cutoff parameter used in the model is $q_\text{max} = 420$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the femtoscopic correlation function formalism to the charm sector, specifically for the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state discovered by LHCb (2022). It contrasts with the coordinate-space approach of Kamiya et al. (2022), which uses one-pion exchange potentials, by employing a momentum-space formalism with vector-meson exchange from the hidden gauge approach. The results enable future experimental extraction of the $D^0D^{*+}$ and $D^+D^{*0}$ scattering lengths from correlation function measurements in heavy-ion collisions, complementing the information obtained from the $T_{cc}$ mass and width.
