# Low-lying level structures in $^{162}$Lu

> The well-tested empirical two quasiparticle rotor model calculations are used to characterize low-lying isomers in the odd-odd nucleus $^{162}$Lu. Physically admissible 2qp bandheads are obtained to construct the low-energy level structure of the nucleus, assign spin-parity (J$^π$), orbital configuration, and level energies (E$_x$), for the gs and the two reported isomers. The spin-parity and orbital configuration of the 1.5 min isomer are confirmed to be J$^π$= 4$^-$\{$π$5/2$^+$[402$\uparrow$] $\otimes$ $ν$3/2$^-$[521$\uparrow$]\}. Its level energy has been estimated to be E$_x$ $\thickapprox$ 62 keV. The 1.9 min isomer is characterized for the first time with J$^π$= 6$^+$\{$π$9/2$^-$[514$\uparrow$] $\otimes$ $ν$3/2$^-$[521$\uparrow$]\} and energy E$_x$ $\thickapprox$ 157 keV. Based on these assignments, individual $β$-decay branches of gs and the two isomers to the daughter levels in $^{162}$Yb are proposed. The study is extended to neighboruing isotopes $^{164,166,168}$Lu. A short-lived isomer, tentatively proposed in a previous study of $^{164}$Lu, is identified as J$^π$= 6$^+$\{$π$7/2$^+$[404$\downarrow$] $\otimes$ $ν$5/2$^+$[402$\uparrow$]\} with energy E$_x$ $\thickapprox$ 52 keV. The energy of the J$^π$=3$^+$ isomer in $^{168}$Lu, currently listed with large uncertainty, is deduced as E$_x$ $\thickapprox$ 100 keV significantly improving its value.

## Key claim
The empirical two-quasiparticle rotor model (TQRM) calculations confirm the spin-parity and orbital configuration of the 1.5 min isomer in 162Lu and characterize the 1.9 min isomer for the first time, but the authors note that the model-calculated level energies serve only as "location guides" with a general uncertainty of about 30 keV, and the 1.9 min isomer assignment requires further experimental validation.

## Method
The authors applied the empirical three-step TQRM using experimental single-particle (1qp) proton and neutron orbital energies from neighboring odd-mass Lu isotopes and N=91 isotones as inputs. They constructed physically admissible two-quasiparticle (2qp) bandheads by coupling these 1qp orbitals, calculated bandhead energies using the empirical formula including rotational energy and residual neutron-proton interaction terms, and employed beta-decay selection rules (Alaga rules, allowed unhindered, spin-flip, and first forbidden fast transitions) to propose favored beta-decay branches from the ground state and isomers to daughter levels in 162Yb.

## Key numbers
- 162Lu ground state: Jπ = 1− {π1/2+[411↓] ⊗ ν3/2−[521↑]}, half-life t1/2 ≈ 1.37 min (adopted from ENSDF)
- 162Lu 1.5 min isomer: Jπ = 4− {π5/2+[402↑] ⊗ ν3/2−[521↑]}, level energy Ex ≈ 62 keV
- 162Lu 1.9 min isomer: Jπ = 6+ {π9/2−[514↑] ⊗ ν3/2−[521↑]}, level energy Ex ≈ 157 keV (abstract) or ≈ 160 keV (text)
- 164Lu short-lived isomer: Jπ = 4− {π5/2+[402↑] ⊗ ν3/2−[521↑]}, level energy Ex ≈ 52 keV
- 168Lu Jπ = 3+ isomer: level energy deduced as Ex ≈ 100 keV, significantly improving the previously listed value with large uncertainty
- 162Lu 196 keV level (from Hild et al.): assigned Jπ = 1+ {π1/2+[411↓] ⊗ ν3/2+[651↑]} at Ex ≈ 199 keV from TQRM

## Context
This work extends the empirical TQRM framework previously applied by the same group to odd-odd nuclei in both rare-earth and actinide regions (e.g., 154Pm, 156Pm, 184Ta, 186Ta, 240Np, 244Np, 252Md) to characterize low-lying isomers in 162Lu and neighboring isotopes 164,166,168Lu. The study addresses ambiguities in the adopted level schemes from earlier decay spectroscopy by Schrewe et al., Hild et al., and Behrens, and provides assignments that can serve as location guides for future experimental studies, particularly for the 1.9 min isomer in 162Lu and the tentative isomer in 164Lu reported by Hunter et al.
