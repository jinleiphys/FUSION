# An extrapolation method for shell model calculations

> We propose a new shell model method, combining the Lanczos digonalization and extrapolation method. This method can give accurate shell model energy from a series of shell model calculations with various truncation spaces, in a well-controlled manner. Its feasibility is demonstrated by taking the fp shell calculations.

# An extrapolation method for shell model calculations

## Key claim

The paper proposes that accurate shell model energies can be obtained by combining Lanczos diagonalization with an extrapolation method based on the linear relationship between the energy difference from the true eigenvalue and the energy variance, provided the approximate wavefunction is a good approximation of the true eigenstate. The method is demonstrated for fp-shell nuclei, but the authors note that extrapolated energies may become lower than the exact energy because the process does not necessarily guarantee the variational principle.

## Method

The method computes shell model energies in a series of truncated spaces defined by the maximum number of nucleons (t) allowed to be excited across the shell gap between the f7/2 orbit and the f5/2, p3/2, p1/2 orbits. For each truncated space, the energy eigenvalue and the energy variance (defined as (⟨H^2⟩ - ⟨H⟩^2)/⟨H⟩^2) are calculated using the Lanczos method. The ground state energy is then obtained by linear extrapolation of the energy versus energy variance plot to zero variance, using results from multiple truncation levels. The same procedure is applied to expectation values of other operators, and for transition matrix elements between different states, an average energy variance is used.

## Key numbers

- For 48Cr with the KB3 interaction: extrapolated ground state energy from t=1,2,3 results is -33.148 ± 0.15 MeV; from t=3,4,5 results is -33.047 ± 0.02 MeV; from t=4,5,6 results is -32.975 ± 0.02 MeV; exact ground state energy is -32.954 MeV
- For 48Cr: extrapolated quadrupole moment from t=1,2,3 results is -24.6 ± 4.1 e fm^2; from t=3,4,5 results is -29.0 ± 0.3 e fm^2; exact value is -29.1 e fm^2
- For 48Cr: extrapolated B(E2:2^+_1 → 0^+_1) from t=2,3,4 results is 220 ± 7 e^2 fm^4; exact value is 220 e^2 fm^4
- For 54Fe with the FPD6 interaction: extrapolated ground state energy from t=3,4,5 is -174.49 ± 0.02 MeV; t=7 truncated shell model energy is -174.217 MeV
- For 54Fe: extrapolated total GT+ strength from t=3,4,5 is 4.98 ± 0.06; t=7 calculation gives 5.03
- For 56Ni: extrapolated ground state energy from t=4~6 is -203.26 ± 0.06 MeV; QMCD result is -203.152 MeV; t=8 truncated space energy is -203.178 MeV
- For 56Ni: extrapolated 2^+_1 energy from t=4~6 is -200.46 ± 0.05 MeV; t=8 truncated shell model result is -200.075 MeV
- For 56Ni: extrapolated 2^+_2 energy from t=3~6 is -198.35 ± 0.35 MeV; extrapolated quadrupole moment is -28.2 ± 8.5 e fm^2; extrapolated occupation number is 11.5 ± 0.6

## Context

This work extends the extrapolation method previously used in the Path-integral renormalization group (PIRG) method for condensed matter problems (Imada and Kashima, 2000) to nuclear shell model calculations. The method is demonstrated on nuclei such as 48Cr, 54Fe, and 56Ni, which had been studied by other approaches including the Quantum Monte Carlo diagonalization (QMCD) method (Honma, Mizusaki, and Otsuka, 1995; Otsuka, Honma, and Mizusaki, 1998), the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method (Alhassid, Dean, Koonin, Lang, and Ormand, 1994), and statistical truncation methods (Horoi, Brown, and Zelevinsky, 1994). The approach enables accurate energy estimates from smaller truncated shell model spaces and can be applied to excited states and transition strengths, potentially improving the efficiency of QMCD calculations and other nuclear structure methods.
