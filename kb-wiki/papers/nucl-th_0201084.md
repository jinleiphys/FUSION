# Nucleon mass and pion loops

> Poincare' covariant Faddeev equations for the nucleon and Delta are solved to illustrate that an internally consistent description in terms of confined-quark and nonpointlike confined-diquark-correlations can be obtained. pi N-loop induced self-energy corrections to the nucleon's mass are analysed and shown to be independent of whether a pseudoscalar or pseudovector coupling is used. Phenomenological constraints suggest that this self-energy correction reduces the nucleon's mass by up to several hundred MeV. That effect does not qualitatively alter the picture, suggested by the Faddeev equation, that baryons are quark-diquark composites. However, neglecting the pi-loops leads to a quantitative overestimate of the nucleon's axial-vector diquark component.

## Key claim

Pion-loop self-energy corrections to the nucleon mass are shown to be independent of whether pseudoscalar or pseudovector coupling is used, and phenomenological constraints suggest these corrections reduce the nucleon mass by up to several hundred MeV; however, this does not qualitatively alter the quark-diquark composite picture of baryons, though neglecting the pion loops leads to a quantitative overestimate of the nucleon's axial-vector diquark component.

## Method

The authors solved Poincaré covariant Faddeev equations for the nucleon and Delta using confined-quark and nonpointlike confined-diquark correlations, retaining scalar and pseudovector diquark channels. They then analyzed pion-nucleon loop-induced self-energy corrections to the nucleon mass within a model field theory framework, comparing both linear and nonlinear realizations of chiral symmetry to determine the coupling-scheme dependence of the mass shift.

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation approach to baryons developed in earlier studies by Oettel (2000) and others, which achieved 2% rms accuracy for octet and decuplet baryon masses using only quark and diquark degrees of freedom. The paper addresses a potential flaw raised by Cloudy Bag Model calculations (Thomas, 1980s) and perturbative studies by Ishii (1990s) that suggested large pion self-energy corrections of several hundred MeV could undermine the Faddeev approach. The results show that while pion loops do reduce the nucleon mass significantly, the quark-diquark picture remains qualitatively intact, enabling future work to incorporate chiral corrections systematically into covariant Faddeev calculations of baryon properties.
