# Three-Nucleon Correlations in Light Nuclei Yields Ratios from AMPT Model for QCD Critical Point Investigation

> This research use the AMPT model in Au+Au collisions to study the influence of the three nucleons correlation $C_{n^2p}$ on the light nuclei yield ratios. It is found that neglecting $C_{n^2p}$ leads to an overestimated relative neutron density fluctuation extraction. Including $C_{n^2p}$ will enhances the agreement with experimental results with higher yield ratios, yet it does not change the energy dependence of the yield ratio. Since there is no first-order phase transition or critical physics in the AMPT model, our work fails to reproduce the experimental energy-dependent peak around $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = $20-30 GeV. Our work might offer a baseline for investigating critical physics phenomena using the light nuclei production as a probe.

## Key claim

Including the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) in the AMPT model enhances the agreement with experimental light nuclei yield ratios, but it does not change the energy dependence of the yield ratio; because the AMPT model contains no first-order phase transition or critical physics, it fails to reproduce the experimental energy-dependent peak around \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 20\text{--}30\) GeV.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model for Au+Au collisions, extracting event-by-event multiplicities and fluctuations of protons and neutrons across varying rapidity intervals and collision centralities. They calculated the relative neutron density fluctuation \(\Delta\rho_n\), the two-nucleon correlation \(C_{np}\), and the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) from the model, then computed the light nuclei yield ratio \(N_p N_t / N_d^2\) using coalescence-model formulas that incorporate these correlations. Centrality was defined by charged-particle multiplicity \(N_{\text{ch}}\) for pseudorapidity \(|\eta| \leq 0.5\).

## Key numbers

* For 0–10% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the relative neutron density fluctuation \(\sigma_n/\langle n \rangle\) and the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) both decrease with increasing rapidity coverage; \(C_{np}\) is nearly zero.
* For 60–80% peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 39\) GeV, the converged value of \(\sigma_p/\langle \rho_p \rangle\) at larger rapidity coverage is approximately 0.7, compared to roughly 0.14 for central collisions at the same energy.
* For 60–80% peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 39\) GeV, \(C_{np}\) is about 0.45–0.5 and independent of rapidity coverage.
* For a given centrality center value, the light nuclei yield ratios increase by approximately 2% to 4% with increasing centrality bin width.
* At 0–10% central collisions, the yield ratios are consistent with the coalescence model baseline \(1/2\sqrt{3}\).
* The discrepancy induced by omitting \(C_{n^2p}\) is unaffected by collision energy except at 7.7 GeV, where the influence of \(C_{n^2p}\) becomes more important.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMPT study (Yu et al., 2023) that examined only the two-body neutron-proton correlation \(C_{np}\) and found it had little effect on the yield ratio for central collisions. The present paper adds the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\), which directly affects triton yields and overall yield ratios. The results provide a more accurate baseline for experimental searches for the QCD critical point at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan program, complementing measurements from the STAR collaboration (2020, 2022) and NA49 (2002, 2006). The failure to reproduce the non-monotonic peak around 20–30 GeV confirms that critical physics absent from AMPT is needed to explain that feature.
