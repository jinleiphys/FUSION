# Solving the Dirac equation with nonlocal potential by Imaginary Time Step method

> The Imaginary Time Step (ITS) method is applied to solve the Dirac equation with the nonlocal potential in coordinate space by the ITS evolution for the corresponding Schrödinger-like equation for the upper component. It is demonstrated that the ITS evolution can be equivalently performed for the Schrödinger-like equation with or without localization. The latter algorithm is recommended in the application for the reason of simplicity and efficiency. The feasibility and reliability of this algorithm are also illustrated by taking the nucleus $^{16}$O as an example, where the same results as the shooting method for the Dirac equation with localized effective potentials are obtained.

## Key claim

The Imaginary Time Step (ITS) method can solve the Dirac equation with nonlocal potential by evolving the corresponding Schrödinger-like equation for the upper component, and the evolution can be performed equivalently with or without localization of the nonlocal terms; the nonlocalized algorithm is recommended for simplicity and efficiency.

## Method

The Dirac equation with nonlocal potential was reduced to a set of coupled integro-differential equations for the upper and lower components \(F_a(r)\) and \(G_a(r)\). The ITS evolution was applied to the Schrödinger-like equation for the upper component, using either a localized form (where the nonlocal terms are absorbed into effective potentials) or a nonlocalized form that directly includes the integrals \(X_a(r)\) and \(Y_a(r)\). The evolution started from initial wave functions (spherical Bessel functions for the upper component, zero for the lower), and the wave functions were orthogonalized by Gram-Schmidt at each iteration until convergence.

## Key numbers

- The calculation used a spherical box of radius \(R = 20\) fm with mesh size \(dr = 0.1\) fm and a time step \(\Delta t = 10^{-26}\) s.
- For the test case of \(^{16}\text{O}\), the nonlocal potential parameters were \(V_0 = 10\) MeV, \(r_0 = 1.04\) fm, \(\alpha = 0.65\) fm, and \(\gamma = 1.0\) fm.
- The ITS evolution for the \(\nu 1s_{1/2}\) state converged to the same result as the shooting method; convergence was illustrated at \(2 \times 10^3\) and \(4 \times 10^3\) iterations.

## Context

This work extends the ITS method, previously applied to the Dirac equation with local potentials (ITSDirac, 2009), to the nonlocal potentials arising in relativistic Hartree-Fock theory (e.g., DDRHF theory by Long et al., 2006). The approach enables solving the Dirac equation in coordinate space for systems where Fock terms introduce nonlocality, which cannot be handled by the conventional shooting method directly. This development is a step toward applying the ITS method in relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for deformed exotic nuclei, where coordinate-space methods are needed but coupled-channel approaches (Price, 1987) are difficult.
