# On extracting information about hadron-nuclear interaction from hadronic atom level shifts

> It is argued, that adjusting strong potentials directly to observed hadronic atom level shifts may lead to significantly different scattering lengths, than those, predicted by the Deser formula. On the example of the 1s level shift of kaonic hydrogen it is demonstrated, that the usually adopted Deser values deduced from the two recent measurements in KEK and by the DEAR Collaboration $a_D({\rm KEK}) = 0.78 - 0.49i$ fm and $a_D({\rm DEAR}) = 0.47 - 0.3i$ fm should be replaced by $a_s({\rm KEK})\simeq 0.85 - 0.62i$ fm and $a_s({\rm DEAR})\simeq 0.49 - 0.35i$ fm, correspondingly.

## Key claim

The widely used Deser formula, which relates hadronic atom level shifts directly to scattering lengths, introduces errors of order 10-15% in the extracted scattering lengths; for kaonic hydrogen, the corrected scattering lengths differ significantly from the Deser values, and modern computational capabilities make direct potential fitting to level shifts preferable to applying corrections to the Deser formula.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrödinger equation for the kaonic hydrogen 1s level, including both the Coulomb potential and a strong nuclear potential, by numerical integration in the internal region and matching logarithmic derivatives at the boundary. They derived strong potentials from three conditions: exact reproduction of the measured level shift, reproduction of the Deser scattering length, and reproduction of the Coulomb-modified scattering length, using four potential shapes (exponential, Gaussian, square well, and Yamaguchi separable) at different ranges. They also derived a new approximate relation (their equation d) connecting the level shift directly to the strong potential via a Wronskian and an integral over the nuclear region.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen reduced mass: μ = 323.478 MeV/c²
- Bohr radius for kaonic hydrogen: r₀ = 83.594 fm
- Atomic energy unit: ε₀ = 17.226 keV
- KEK experimental 1s level shift: ΔE(KEK) = -323 + 203.5i eV
- Deser scattering length from KEK data: a_D(KEK) = 0.78 - 0.49i fm
- Corrected strong scattering length for KEK data: a_s(KEK) ≈ 0.85 - 0.62i fm
- Deser scattering length from DEAR data: a_D(DEAR) = 0.47 - 0.30i fm
- Corrected strong scattering length for DEAR data: a_s(DEAR) ≈ 0.49 - 0.35i fm

## Context

This work extends the long line of research on the Deser formula (Deser, 1954) and its corrections, including the Coulomb-modified scattering length approach (Lambert, 1969) and studies of the strong potential with nearly bound states (Truhlik, 1970; Popov, 1971). The paper demonstrates that direct numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation for hadronic atoms is now computationally straightforward and yields more accurate scattering lengths than the traditional Deser formula approach. This enables more reliable extraction of the K⁻N interaction parameters from kaonic hydrogen data, which is relevant for understanding possible bound antikaon-nuclear states.
