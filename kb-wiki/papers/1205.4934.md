# QGP flow fluctuations and the characteristics of higher moments

> The dynamical development of expanding Quark-gluon Plasma (QGP) flow is studied in a 3+1D fluid dynamical model with a globally symmetric, initial condition. We minimize fluctuations arising from complex dynamical processes at finite impact parameters and from fluctuating random initial conditions to have a conservative fluid dynamical background estimate for the statistical distributions of the thermodynamical parameters. We also avoid a phase transition in the equation of state, and we let the matter supercool during the expansion. Then central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV are studied in an almost perfect fluid dynamical model, with azimuthally symmetric initial state generated in a dynamical flux-tube model. The general development of thermodynamical extensives are also shown for lower energies. We observe considerable deviations from a thermal equilibrium source as a consequence of the fluid dynamical expansion arising from a least fluctuating initial state.

## Key claim

Even when all random initial-state fluctuations, azimuthal asymmetries, and phase-transition effects are eliminated to obtain a "least fluctuating" fluid dynamical background, the spatial expansion of the QGP alone produces non-Gaussian higher moments (skewness and kurtosis) that change sign with time and beam energy, mimicking the signals often attributed to critical fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D relativistic fluid dynamical simulations using the Particle-in-Cell (PIC) method with an MIT Bag model equation of state (bag constant B = 0.397 GeV/fm³) that contains no phase transition, allowing supercooled QGP expansion. They studied central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (and Au+Au at lower energies) with an azimuthally symmetric initial state generated by a dynamical flux-tube model, then computed the cell-by-cell baryon-charge-weighted spatial distributions of specific energy densities and baryon densities, extracting their variance, skewness, and kurtosis as functions of time.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant fixed to B = 0.397 GeV/fm³ for the MIT Bag model equation of state.
- Central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV; the average specific energy in the calculational frame is constant at <ε^CF> = 1/2 √s_NN = 1.38 TeV.
- At √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and t = 8 fm/c, half of the total available energy is converted into kinetic energy; at √s_NN = 39 GeV, only 20% is converted.
- The kurtosis of the baryon density (both CF and LR) is negative at early times and turns positive around t = 13 fm/c.
- The skewness of the invariant scalar specific energy density ε^LR changes sign from negative to positive at approximately t = 7 fm/c, coinciding with the estimated freeze-out time.
- The skewness of the CF specific energy density ε^CF and the LR specific energy density ε^LR cross at approximately t = 8 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., the v₁ to v₈ harmonics measured at RHIC and LHC) by isolating the pure fluid-dynamical contribution to higher statistical moments, in contrast to studies that focus on critical fluctuations from a phase transition (Stephano et al., Bass et al., Gavai, Csernai). The results provide a baseline for hybrid models (e.g., PIC hydro matched to the PACIAE cascade of Sa et al.) and for experimental mixed-event methods (Daimei et al., CPOD) to separate flow-driven fluctuations from phase-transition signals.
