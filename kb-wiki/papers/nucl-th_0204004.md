# Can we observe the $σ$ meson in peripheral heavy ion collisions?

> We discuss the possible production of the $σ$ meson and detection of its two photon decay mode in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Assuming the mass and total decay width values reported recently by the E791 Collaboration, we verify that the $σ$ meson can be observed in the subprocess $γγ\to γγ$ if its partial decay width into photons is of the order of a few keV.

# Can we observe the σ meson in peripheral heavy ion collisions?

## Key claim

The σ meson can be observed in peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC through the subprocess γγ → γγ, provided its partial decay width into photons is of the order of a few keV. The observation is conditional on using the mass and total decay width values reported by the E791 Collaboration, and on the ability to separate the resonant signal from the continuum box diagram background and other competing processes.

## Method

The authors computed the γγ → γγ subprocess for Au ions at RHIC with √s = 200 GeV/nucleon, using the equivalent-photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) approximation in impact parameter space with the photon distribution of Cahn and Jackson. They calculated the resonant σ contribution using both a Breit-Wigner profile (with E791 mass and width values) and an effective Lagrangian model, and compared it to the continuum fermion box diagram background. An angular cut of |cos θ| < 0.5 was applied to eliminate double bremsstrahlung background, and the significance for σ discovery was evaluated as a function of the two-photon partial decay width.

## Key numbers

- σ meson mass: 478 (+24/-23 ± 17) MeV, total decay width: 324 (+42/-40 ± 21) MeV, from the E791 Collaboration
- Assumed partial decay width into two photons: Γγγ = 4.7 keV, as determined by Pennington and Boglione
- RHIC luminosity: 2.0 × 10^26 cm^-2 s^-1
- For Γγγ > 4.7 keV, the significance exceeds the 2σ (95% confidence level) limit
- For a 5σ discovery criterion, Γγγ > 7.5 keV is required
- Integrating in a bin of energy 700 ± 100 MeV (free of strong resonances), 1532 events/year are expected assuming 100% efficiency
- The γ-Pomeron → ω rate is 10 Hz at RHIC, three orders of magnitude higher than for a similar mass meson of spin 0 or 2

## Context

This work extends the discussion of two-photon physics in peripheral heavy ion collisions, following earlier proposals by Baur and Bertulani (1989) and the calculations of the γγ → γγ continuum by Karplus and Neuman (1951) and De Tollis (1964). The paper builds on the E791 Collaboration's evidence for the σ meson from D+ → π- π+ π+ decays (2001) and addresses the long-standing controversy over the σ meson's existence, which had been debated for roughly forty years. The proposed measurement would provide a cleaner signal than the usual two-pion decay channel because the initial and final states are not strongly interacting. The work enables future experimental searches at RHIC, though the authors emphasize that a full simulation of all background processes (including γ-Pomeron production of vector mesons, double-Pomeron exchange, and misidentified neutral pions) is necessary before a definitive observation can be claimed.
