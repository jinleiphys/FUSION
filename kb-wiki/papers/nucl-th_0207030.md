# Three-Body approach to the K^- d Scattering Length in Particle Basis

> We report on the first calculation of the scattering length A_{K^-d} based on a relativistic three-body approach where the two-body input amplitudes coupled to the Kbar N channels have been obtained with the chiral SU(3) constraint, but with isospin symmetry breaking effects taken into account. Results are compared with a recent calculation applying a similar set of two-body amplitudes,based on the fixed center approximation, considered as a good approximation for a loosely bound target, and for which we find significant deviations from the exact three-body results. Effects of the hyperon-nucleon interaction, and deuteron $D$-wave component are also evaluated.

## Key claim

The first calculation of the $K^-d$ scattering length using a relativistic three-body approach with chiral SU(3)-constrained two-body input and isospin symmetry breaking effects yields $A_{K^-d} \approx (-1.8 + i1.5)\ \hbox{fm}$, but the fixed center approximation (FCA) is found to be significantly less reliable than previously claimed, especially regarding charge exchange contributions.

## Method

A relativistic Faddeev three-body calculation was performed for $K^-d$ scattering at threshold, using two-body $\Kbar N$ amplitudes derived from chiral SU(3) Lagrangians with isospin symmetry breaking (particle basis). Two sets of separable potentials (OS1 and OS2) were constructed with form factors and fitted to low-energy $K^-p$ data, and the three-body equations were solved exactly without the fixed center approximation. The dependence on deuteron models (including $D$-wave components) and the effects of $\pi N$ and $YN$ interactions were also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- $A_{K^-d} \approx (-1.8 + i1.5)\ \hbox{fm}$ from the full Faddeev calculation, with results from three models agreeing within $\pm 20\%$
- Faddeev total $A_{K^-d}$ for OS1: $(-1.99 + i1.65)\ \hbox{fm}$; for OS2: $(-1.68 + i1.66)\ \hbox{fm}$; for Oset-Ramos input: $(-1.92 + i1.58)\ \hbox{fm}$
- FCA total $A_{K^-d}$ for OS1: $(-2.15 + i1.92)\ \hbox{fm}$; for OS2: $(-1.73 + i1.76)\ \hbox{fm}$; for Oset-Ramos: $(-1.99 + i1.95)\ \hbox{fm}$
- Charge exchange contribution in Faddeev is much smaller than in FCA: e.g., for OS1, charge exchange adds $(-0.29 + i0.34)\ \hbox{fm}$ in Faddeev versus $(-0.83 + i0.82)\ \hbox{fm}$ in FCA
- $a_{K^-p}$ from KEK experiment: $(-0.78 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.03) + i(-0.49 \pm 0.25 \pm 0.12)\ \hbox{fm}$
- OS1 $a_{K^-p}$: $(-1.04 + i0.83)\ \hbox{fm}$; OS2 $a_{K^-p}$: $(-0.71 + i0.92)\ \hbox{fm}$
- Switching from isospin basis to particle basis changes imaginary part dramatically: e.g., OS1 in isospin basis gives $(-1.76 + i2.91)\ \hbox{fm}$ versus $(-1.99 + i1.65)\ \hbox{fm}$ in particle basis
- Using a pure $^3S_1$ deuteron model changes the imaginary part by about $20\%$ compared to a realistic model with $D$-wave component

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body calculations of $A_{K^-d}$ by Hetherington and Schick (1965), Toker et al. (1982), and Torres et al. (1986), which used ad-hoc separable potentials and gave values around $(-1.5 + i1.0)\ \hbox{fm}$. It directly contradicts the claim by Deloff (2000) that the fixed center approximation is reliable for this system, showing instead that FCA overestimates charge exchange contributions by a factor of 2-3. The results enable extraction of the $K^-n$ scattering length from future DEAR experiment measurements at DA$\Phi$NE, and provide input for determining kaon-nucleon sigma terms related to chiral symmetry breaking.
