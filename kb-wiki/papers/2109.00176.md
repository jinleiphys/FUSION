# Effect of tensor force on lowering of 5/2$^{-}$ level in heavier Cu isotopes

> The inversion of 3/2$^{-}$ and 5/2$^{-}$ levels in heavier Cu isotopes is one of the most visible example of shell-evolution, caused by the strong monopole attraction between the nucleons occupying the orbitals $π0f_{5/2}$ and $ν0g_{9/2}$. The tensor part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is expected to be the driving force behind this monopole migration. In shell model framework, usually spin-tensor decomposition is used to get the information of individual force components to the shell evolution, however, in the present scenario, this method can not apply on \textit{pfg} model space due to the missing spin-orbit partners $ 0f_{7/2}$ and $ 0g_{7/2}$. Therefore, we have analytically obtained the tensor force two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) for this model space using Yukawa potential, and subtract it from effective interaction jj44b \cite{}. The interaction without tensor part, named as jj44a, have been used for calculation of Ni, Zn, Ge and Cu isotopes with various physics viewpoints. In most of the cases, the theoretical results are in good agreement with the experiment only when tensor force is included to the interaction jj44a.

## Key claim

The tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is the driving force behind the monopole migration that causes the inversion of the 3/2⁻ and 5/2⁻ levels in heavier Cu isotopes, but the spin-tensor decomposition method cannot be applied to the *pfg* model space because the spin-orbit partners 0f₇/₂ and 0g₇/₂ are missing, so the authors analytically calculated tensor force two-body matrix elements using a Yukawa potential and subtracted them from the jj44b effective interaction to isolate the tensor force effect.

## Method

The authors analytically calculated tensor force two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) for the *pfg* model space (orbitals 1p₃/₂, 0f₅/₂, 1p₁/₂, and 0g₉/₂ on top of a ⁵⁶Ni core) using a Yukawa potential with a Compton scattering length of 1.41 fm and a strength parameter V₀ obtained by fitting the tensor force monopole matrix elements of the USDB interaction. They subtracted these calculated tensor TBMEs from the jj44b effective interaction to produce a new interaction without tensor force, named jj44a. They then performed shell-model calculations using NUSHELLX@MSU with both jj44a and jj44b to compute effective single-particle energies, excitation spectra, electromagnetic moments, transition probabilities, occupation numbers, and spectroscopic factors for Cu, Ni, Zn, and Ge isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit partners gap π(1p₁/₂−1p₃/₂) decreases by approximately 1.0 MeV with the filling of the ν0g₉/₂ orbital.
- For ⁵⁹Cu, the interaction without tensor force predicts 1/2₁⁻ and 5/2₁⁻ states very close to each other, whereas these states are separated by 0.423 MeV in the experiment.
- The 5/2₁⁻ level is observed almost constant between 0.91 MeV and 1.11 MeV for ⁵⁹⁻⁶⁷Cu, and is 1.21 MeV, 0.53 MeV, and 0.17 MeV for ⁶⁹Cu, ⁷¹Cu, and ⁷³Cu, respectively.
- The experimentally measured 1/2₁⁻ state comes down from approximately 1.10 MeV for ⁶⁷,⁶⁹Cu to 61 keV for ⁷⁵Cu with the filling of the ν0g₉/₂ orbital.
- The 7/2₁⁻ excitation energy drops from 1.711 MeV in ⁶⁹Cu to 0.981 MeV in ⁷¹Cu.
- The B(E2; 5/2⁻ → 3/2⁻) value is less than 5 W.u. for ⁶⁹⁻⁷³Cu, indicating single-particle nature of the 5/2⁻ level.
- The B(E2; 1/2⁻ → 3/2⁻) transition is greater than 5 W.u., indicating collectivity in the 1/2⁻ state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Jha and Raina, recent work) where they used analytical tensor force expressions to improve disparities in *p*-shell and *pf*-shell effective interactions (CK(8-16) and GX1B1, respectively). The study builds on the established understanding that the inversion of 3/2⁻ and 5/2⁻ levels in heavier Cu isotopes is caused by strong monopole attraction between nucleons in π0f₅/₂ and ν0g₉/₂ orbitals, as reported by Otsuka (2005), Franchoo (2001), and others. The work enables systematic investigation of tensor force effects in the *pfg* model space where standard spin-tensor decomposition (Kirson, 1973; Klingenback, 1979; Kenji, 2005) cannot be applied due to missing spin-orbit partners, and provides a method to isolate tensor contributions in shell-model calculations for nuclei around ⁷⁸Ni.
