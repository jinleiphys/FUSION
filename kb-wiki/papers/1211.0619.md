# Fermion bag solutions to some sign problems in four-fermion field theories

> Lattice four-fermion models containing $N$ flavors of staggered fermions, that are invariant under $Z_2$ and U(1) chiral symmetries, are known to suffer from sign problems when formulated using the auxiliary field approach. Although these problems have been ignored in previous studies, they can be severe. In this talk, we show that the sign problems disappear when the models are formulated in the fermion bag approach, allowing us to solve them rigorously for the first time.

## Key claim

The sign problems that plague lattice four-fermion models with Z₂ and U(1) chiral symmetries when formulated using the auxiliary field approach disappear when the same models are reformulated in the fermion bag approach, allowing rigorous Monte Carlo simulations for the first time; the proof relies on the fact that certain correlation functions of free staggered fermions are positive semi-definite.

## Method

The author first reviews the auxiliary field formulation of lattice Gross-Neveu (GN) models with N flavors of staggered fermions in three dimensions, showing that the Z₂ model has a sign problem for odd N and the U(1) model has a sign problem for all N. The severity of the sign problem is demonstrated by generating Gaussian random auxiliary field configurations and computing the determinant distribution, which shows nearly identical positive and negative weight distributions on 6³ and 12³ lattices. The fermion bag approach is then introduced: the auxiliary fields are integrated out first to obtain an equivalent four-fermion action, each four-fermion coupling is expanded as a bond variable, and the partition function becomes a sum over bond configurations weighted by products of correlation functions C_i that are proven to be non-negative. For the U(1) model, the critical exponents are extracted from finite-size scaling of the chiral condensate susceptibility, chiral winding number susceptibility, and fermion two-point correlator ratio on lattices from size 16 to 40.

## Key numbers

- For the Z₂ model on a 6³ lattice, one million configurations were generated; on a 12³ lattice, 5000 configurations were generated to study the sign problem.
- For the U(1) GN model, the critical coupling U_c = 0.0909(1), the correlation length exponent ν = 0.88(1), the anomalous dimension η = 0.63(1), and the fermion anomalous dimension η_ψ = 0.37(1), with χ²/d.o.f. = 0.89.
- For the Thirring model (U(1) model without body-diagonal coupling), ν = 0.85(1), η = 0.65(1), η_ψ = 0.37(1), with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.3.
- A combined fit of both models gives ν = 0.85(1), η = 0.64(1), η_ψ = 0.37(1), with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.19.

## Context

This work extends the fermion bag approach developed by Chandrasekharan (2009-2012) to solve sign problems that were previously ignored or circumvented in lattice GN model simulations. Earlier studies by Hands (1995, 2001) and Christofi (2006) avoided the sign problem by studying only even N in the Z₂ case or by introducing conjugate fermions that change the flavor symmetry from U(N) to U(N)×U(N). The author shows that the U(1) GN model and the Thirring model belong to the same universality class, contradicting earlier claims of different critical exponents from conventional methods, and enables future studies of the Z₂ GN model and more complex models with dynamical bosons and fermions.
