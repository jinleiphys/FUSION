# Continuum study of deconfinement at finite temperature

> Deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are explored in a confining, renormalisable, Dyson-Schwinger equation model of two-flavour QCD. An order parameter for deconfinement is introduced and used to establish that, in the chiral limit, deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are coincident at $T_c\approx 150\,$MeV. The transitions are second order and each has the same critical exponent: $β\approx 0.3$. The deconfinement transition is found to exhibit sensitivity to the current-quark mass. $f_π$ and $m_π$ change by no more than 10\% for $T<0.7\,T_c$, however, as $T\to T_c$, thermal fluctuations cause the pion bound state contribution to the four-point quark-antiquark correlation function to disappear.

## Key claim

In a confining, renormalisable Dyson-Schwinger equation model of two-flavour QCD, deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration are coincident at T_c ≈ 150 MeV in the chiral limit, with both transitions being second order and sharing the same critical exponent β ≈ 0.3; however, the deconfinement transition exhibits sensitivity to the current-quark mass, with the u/d-quark deconfined at T ∼ 180 MeV for physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature Dyson-Schwinger equation for the renormalised dressed-quark propagator numerically, using a model dressed-gluon propagator with an integrable infrared singularity that generates confinement and no Lehmann representation. They introduced an order parameter for deconfinement, ν₀, defined as the inverse of the position of the first zero in the scalar part of the quark Schwinger function, and used the chiral-limit value B₀(p=0, ω₀) as the order parameter for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The rainbow approximation for the quark-gluon vertex and the Abelian approximation for the Ward identities were employed, with all model parameters fixed at T=0 from pion observables.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for both deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration in the chiral limit: T_c = 150 MeV
- Critical exponent for the chiral order parameter: β_χ = 0.33
- Critical exponent for the deconfinement order parameter: β_ν₀ = 0.30
- For the massive u/d-quark, deconfinement temperature: T_c^ν_m = 180 MeV (with β_ν_m = 0.71 for T > T_c^χ)
- Pion decay constant and pion mass change by no more than 10% for T < 0.7 T_c (T_c = 150 MeV)
- At T ≈ 0.9 T_c^χ, the π → μ ν_μ decay width would be reduced by 20%
- Model parameters: m_t = 0.69 GeV, m_R(μ) = 1.1 MeV, renormalisation point μ = 9.47 GeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature Dyson-Schwinger equation model of Frank and Roberts (1996) to finite temperature, providing a continuum framework complementary to lattice-QCD simulations at finite temperature, such as those by Karsch (1995). The results enable further studies of meson properties near the deconfinement transition and have implications for astrophysical and cosmological applications, as well as for heavy-ion collision phenomenology. The disappearance of the pion bound state contribution to the four-point quark-antiquark correlation function as T approaches T_c raises open questions about the behaviour of hadronic observables in the transition region.
