# Collectivity in small systems - Initial state vs. final state effects

> Observations of long rang azimuthal correlations in small collision systems (p+p/A) have triggered an enormous excitement in the heavy-ion community. However, it is presently unclear to what extent the experimentally observed correlations should be attributed to initial state momentum correlations and/or the final state response to the initial state geometry. We discuss how a consistent theoretical description of the non-equilibrium dynamics is important to address both effects within a unified framework and present first results from weakly coupled non-equilibrium simulations in \cite{Greif:2017bnr} to quantify the relative importance of initial state and final state effects based on theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The relative importance of initial state momentum correlations versus final state geometric response in small collision systems depends strongly on event multiplicity and transverse momentum; a consistent non-equilibrium description shows that both effects can be quantitatively important for two-particle correlations in p+Pb collisions, with initial state correlations contributing on the order of 25% to the pT-integrated v2 in high-multiplicity events and 50% in low-multiplicity events.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations of p+Pb collisions by matching a classical-statistical lattice description of particle production and early time dynamics (IP-Glasma model, τ < 0.2 fm/c) to an effective kinetic description of the subsequent non-equilibrium evolution using the parton cascade BAMPS with pQCD matrix elements for 2↔2 and 2↔3 interactions. They extracted the phase space distribution of gluons containing both initial state momentum correlations and initial state geometry, then simulated final state re-scattering dynamics to track the time evolution of azimuthal correlations.

## Key numbers

- Low multiplicity p+Pb events: final state re-scattering affects correlations only at very low momenta pT ≲ 2 GeV; initial state momentum correlations at higher momenta pT ≳ 2 GeV persist throughout the evolution.
- High multiplicity p+Pb events (〈dNg/dy〉/〈dNg/dy〉 > 2.5): most initial state correlations get destroyed on a time scale ∼1 fm/c while new final state induced correlations build up simultaneously in response to system geometry.
- Low multiplicity events (0.5 < 〈dNg/dy〉/〈dNg/dy〉 < 1): initial state correlations contribute ∼50% to the pT-integrated v2.
- High multiplicity events: initial state correlations contribute ∼25% to the pT-integrated v2.
- Even in high multiplicity events, an average number of ≈1 large angle scattering is sufficient to build up a geometric response.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate between initial state explanations (e.g., Dusling 2012, Dumitru 2014, Schenke 2016) and final state hydrodynamic explanations (e.g., Bozek 2011, Werner 2013, Mantysaari 2017) for long-range azimuthal correlations in small systems. The authors argue that both approaches fall short in consistently describing dynamics across a wide range of multiplicities, and their non-equilibrium framework (Greif 2017) provides a unified description that interpolates between the initial-state-dominated and final-state-dominated regimes. This enables future studies of whether specific observables (such as higher order cumulants of v2 at low momenta or survival of back-to-back correlations at higher momenta) can indicate which mechanism dominates.
