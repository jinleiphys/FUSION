# $D Ξ$ and $D^* Ξ$ Molecular States from One Boson Exchange

> We explore the existence of $D Ξ$ and $D^* Ξ$ molecular states within the one boson exchange model. We regularize the potential derived in this model with a form factor and a cut-off of the order of $1\,{\rm GeV}$. To determine the cut-off, we use the condition that the $X(3872)$ is reproduced as a pole in the $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ $D^*\bar{D}$ amplitude. From this we find that the $J^P= {\frac{1}{2}}^{-}$ $D^*\,Ξ$ system is on the verge of binding and has an unnaturally large scattering length. For the $J^P= {\frac{1}{2}}^{-}$ $D\,Ξ$ and the $J^P= {\frac{3}{2}}^{-}$ $D^*\,Ξ$ systems the attraction is not enough to form a bound state. From heavy quark symmetry and the quark model we can extend the previous model to the $P Ξ_{QQ}$ and $P^* Ξ_{QQ}$ systems, with $P = D, \bar{B}$, $P^* = D^*, \bar{B}^*$ and $Ξ_{QQ} = Ξ_{cc}, Ξ_{bb}$. In this case we predict a series of triply heavy pentaquark-like molecules.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^- D^* \Xi$ system is on the verge of binding with an unnaturally large scattering length, but only when the cut-off of the one boson exchange potential is fixed by requiring that the $X(3872)$ is reproduced as a pole in the $J^{PC} = 1^{++} D^* \bar{D}$ amplitude; the $D \Xi$ and $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^- D^* \Xi$ systems do not bind under the same condition.

## Method

The authors construct a one boson exchange (OBE) potential for the $D \Xi$ and $D^* \Xi$ systems, including exchange of $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons, with couplings determined from heavy quark symmetry, the quark model, and SU(3) flavour relations. They regularize the potential with a monopolar form factor and a cut-off $\Lambda$ of order $1\,{\rm GeV}$, fixing $\Lambda$ by the condition that the $X(3872)$ appears as a bound state pole in the $D^* \bar{D}$ system. They then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the $D \Xi$ and $D^* \Xi$ systems and extend the model via heavy quark symmetry and the quark model to predict bound states in the $P \Xi_{QQ}$ and $P^* \Xi_{QQ}$ systems with $P = D, \bar{B}$ and $\Xi_{QQ} = \Xi_{cc}, \Xi_{bb}$.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off $\Lambda$ is of the order of $1\,{\rm GeV}$.
- The $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^- D^* \Xi$ system has an unnaturally large scattering length (explicit numerical value not given in the text).
- The $D \Xi$ threshold is in the range $3179-3191\,{\rm MeV}$.
- The $\Xi_{cc}$ mass is taken as $3621\,{\rm MeV}$ (from LHCb).
- The $\Xi_{bb}$ mass is taken as $10127\,{\rm MeV}$ (from lattice QCD).
- The axial coupling $g_1$ for the $D$/$D^*$ mesons is $0.60$, compatible with $g_1 = 0.59 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.07$ from $D^* \to D \pi$ decay.
- The $\sigma$ coupling $g_{\sigma 1} = g_{\sigma 2} = 3.4$, derived from $g_{\sigma NN} \simeq 10.2$ divided by 3.
- The $\rho$ coupling $g_{\rho 1} = 2.6$ (from lattice QCD) and $g_{\rho 2} = 2.9$ (from Sakurai universality).
- The magnetic couplings are $\kappa_{\rho 1} = \kappa_{\omega 1} = 4.5$ for the $D$/$D^*$ and $\kappa_{\rho 2} = \kappa_{\omega 2} = -1.5$ for the $\Xi$.

## Context

This work extends the one boson exchange model, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Machleidt 1989) and to hadronic molecules such as the $X(3872)$ and $Z_b$ states (Liu 2008, Sun 2011, Chen 2015), to the $D \Xi$ and $D^* \Xi$ systems. It is motivated by the LHCb observation of five narrow $\Omega_c$ states (Aaij 2017) and a hint of a structure near $3188\,{\rm MeV}$ close to the $D \Xi$ threshold, which Wang (2017) studied with the Bethe-Salpeter formalism and Debastiani and Liang (2017) with a chiral unitary model. The present work predicts a series of triply heavy pentaquark-like molecules in the $P \Xi_{QQ}$ and $P^* \Xi_{QQ}$ systems, which could guide future experimental searches for states containing two or three charm or bottom quarks.
