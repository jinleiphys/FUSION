# Linear response theory and neutrino mean free path using Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functionals

> The Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functionals have been successful to describe properties of both finite nuclei and infinite homogeneous nuclear matter. In their latest version, these functionals have been equipped with two extra density-dependent terms in order to reproduce simultaneously ground state properties of nuclei and infinite nuclear matter properties while avoiding at the same time the arising of ferromagnetic instabilities. In the present article, we extend our previous results of the linear response theory to include such extra terms at both zero and finite temperature in pure neutron matter. The resulting formalism is then applied to derive the neutrino mean free path. The predictions from the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functionals are compared with ab-initio methods.

## Key claim

The Brussels-Montreal Skyrme functionals BSk19-21, which include two extra density-dependent terms to avoid ferromagnetic instabilities, predict neutrino mean free paths in pure neutron matter that are compatible with ab-initio Correlated Basis Function (CBF) calculations at saturation density, but only when the functionals also satisfy the Landau stability condition \(G_0^n > -1\).

## Method

The authors extended their previous linear response (Random Phase Approximation) formalism for Skyrme functionals to include the extra density-dependent terms present in the BSk17-21 functionals, at both zero and finite temperature in pure neutron matter. They calculated the RPA response functions and strength functions for spin channels \(S=0\) and \(S=1\), then used these to compute the neutrino mean free path via the neutral-current scattering cross section formula, comparing results with CBF calculations from Benhar and Lovato (2013, 2014).

## Key numbers

* The BSk functionals reproduce experimentally known nuclear masses with a root-mean-square deviation of \(\sigma = 0.58\) MeV.
* For BSk21, the neutron effective mass \(m^*_n/m\) reaches 1.1 at \(\rho_n \approx 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) and decreases to 0.9 at \(\rho_n \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The functional BSk17 violates the Landau inequality \(G_0^n > -1\) at \(\rho_n \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), leading to a ferromagnetic instability; SLy5 violates it at \(\rho_n \approx 0.6\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* At \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and zero temperature, the neutrino mean free path from BSk19-21 functionals is compatible with CBF results; BSk17 gives a much smaller mean free path near \(E_\nu \approx 40\) MeV due to finite-size instabilities.
* At temperature \(T = 2\) MeV, the CBF results remain in reasonable agreement with BSk19-21 predictions.

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism of Pastore et al. (2012) for the TIJ Skyrme family to the Brussels-Montreal BSk functionals, which were designed to simultaneously fit finite nuclei properties and infinite nuclear matter constraints while avoiding ferromagnetic instabilities found in earlier Skyrme functionals (Vidaurre 1984, Margueron 2002). The results enable more reliable neutrino transport calculations for supernova and neutron star modeling, and the comparison with CBF calculations (Benhar 2013, Lovato 2014) provides a benchmark for validating Skyrme functionals against ab-initio methods.
