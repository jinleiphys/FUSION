# Radiative corrections in neutrino-deuterium disintegration

> The radiative corrections of order $α$ for the charged and neutral current neutrino-deuterium disintegration for energies relevant to the SNO experiment are evaluated. Particular attention is paid to the issue of the bremsstrahlung detection threshold. It is shown that the radiative corrections to the total cross section for the charged current reaction are independent of that threshold, as they must be for consistency, and amount to a slowly decreasing function of the neutrino energy $E_ν$, varying from $\sim$ 4% at low energies to $\sim$ 3% at the end of the $^8$B spectrum. The differential cross section corrections, on the other hand, do depend on the bremsstrahlung detection threshold. Various choices of the threshold are discussed. It is shown that for a realistic choice of the threshold, and for the actual electron energy threshold of the SNO detector, the deduced $^8$B $ν_e$ flux should be decreased by $\sim$ 2%. The radiative corrections to the neutral current reaction are also evaluated.

## Key claim

The radiative corrections of order α to the total charged-current neutrino-deuterium cross section are independent of the bremsstrahlung detection threshold, as required for consistency, and vary from ~4% at low neutrino energies to ~3% at the end of the ⁸B spectrum; however, the differential cross section corrections do depend on the bremsstrahlung threshold, and for a realistic threshold and the SNO detector's electron energy threshold, the deduced ⁸B νₑ flux should be decreased by ~2%.

## Method

The authors evaluated order-α radiative corrections to both charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino-deuteron disintegration, using effective field theory to handle hadronic contributions and a conventional infrared regulator (photon mass λ) split into bremsstrahlung contributions below and above a threshold energy E_γ^(min). They computed virtual photon and Z-boson exchange graphs, bremsstrahlung graphs, and accounted for the momentum dependence of the Gamow-Teller ³S₁→¹S₀ matrix element and the proper ordering of limits involving E_γ^(min) and the infrared regulator. Three bremsstrahlung threshold scenarios were considered: no detection (E_γ^(min)→∞), always detected (E_γ^(min)→0), and a realistic case with E_γ^(min)=1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The radiative correction to the total CC cross section varies from ~4% at low neutrino energies to ~3% at the end of the ⁸B spectrum.
- For a realistic bremsstrahlung threshold and the SNO detector's electron energy threshold, the deduced ⁸B νₑ flux should be decreased by ~2%.
- The NC radiative correction amounts to ~1.5% of the tree-level NC cross section.
- The hadronic scale uncertainty in the box diagram logarithm 3Q̄ ln(M_Z/M_had) = 2.39_{-0.21}^{+0.67} corresponds to a spread of 0.2% in cross section predictions.
- The width of the |I(p²)|² function is determined by ~(ħc)²/(a_pp² M_p) ~ 0.7 MeV, where a_pp = -7.82 fm.

## Context

This work resolves a controversy raised by Beacom and Parke (2001) regarding inconsistencies in the radiative correction treatment of Towner (1999), who had neglected the strong momentum dependence of the Gamow-Teller matrix element and used improper ordering of limits. The authors extend the formalism of Sirlin (1978) for beta-decay radiative corrections to the neutrino-deuteron system, using effective field theory to quantify hadronic uncertainties. The results enable more precise extraction of the ⁸B solar neutrino flux from SNO data and provide a framework for future high-precision determinations of Gamow-Teller transitions.
