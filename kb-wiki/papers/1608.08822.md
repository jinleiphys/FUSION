# K-Long Facility for JLab and its Scientific Potential

> Our main interest in creating a secondary high-quality KL-beam is to investigate hyperon spectroscopy through both formation and production processes. We propose to study two-body reactions induced by the KL-beam on the proton target. The experiment should measure both differential cross sections and self-analyzed polarizations of the produced $Λ$-, $Σ$-, and $Ξ$-hyperons using the GlueX detector at the Jefferson Lab Hall D. New data will greatly constrain partial-wave analysis and reduce model-dependent uncertainties in the extraction of strange resonance properties, providing a new benchmark for comparisons with QCD-inspired models and LQCD calculations. The measurements will span c.m. $\cosθ$ from -0.95 to 0.95 in c.m. range above W = 1490MeV and up to 4000 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a secondary high-quality $K_L$ beam at Jefferson Lab Hall D, using the GlueX detector, can significantly improve knowledge of hyperon spectroscopy by measuring differential cross sections and self-analyzed polarizations of $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ hyperons, but the actual scope is limited to two-body reactions on a proton target, with the caveat that the current database for $K_Lp$ scattering contains very few polarization data.

## Method

The authors propose to use the existing GlueX detector in Hall D at Jefferson Lab, with a 12 GeV electron beam from CEBAF producing bremsstrahlung photons that hit a beryllium target to generate $K_L$ mesons. The $K_L$ beam will be directed onto a liquid hydrogen cryotarget, and the experiment will measure differential cross sections and recoil polarizations for two-body reactions $K_Lp \rightarrow \pi Y$ and $K_Lp \rightarrow KY$ in the center-of-mass energy range W = 1490–4000 MeV. The $K_L$ momentum will be determined using time-of-flight technique with a 60 ns time structure, and the data will be used in a coupled-channel partial-wave analysis combined with available and future J-PARC $K^-p$ measurements.

## Key numbers

* Estimated $K_L$ beam flux on the GlueX LH$_2$ target: about $10^5$ $K_L$/s, compared to about $10^3$ $K_L$/s used at NINA and SLAC
* Center-of-mass energy range: W = 1490–4000 MeV
* Center-of-mass $\cos\theta$ coverage: from -0.95 to 0.95
* Mean lifetime of $K_L$: 51.16 ns ($c\tau = 15.3$ m), compared to $K^-$ mean lifetime of 12.38 ns ($c\tau = 3.7$ m)
* Number of experimentally identified resonances in PDG2014 summary tables: 14 $\Lambda^\ast$, 12 $\Sigma^\ast$, 7 $\Xi^\ast$, and 2 $\Omega^\ast$
* Number of predicted states needed to complete SU(3)$_F$ multiplets: no less than 17 $\Lambda^\ast$, 41 $\Sigma^\ast$, 41 $\Xi^\ast$, and 24 $\Omega^\ast$
* Constituent quark models predict no less than 64 $N^\ast$ and 22 $\Delta^\ast$ states with mass less than 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hyperon spectroscopy program initiated at the KL2016 workshop at Jefferson Lab in February 2016, following the LoI-12-15-001 proposal. The proposed measurements address the "missing states" problem identified by Koniuk (1980) and aim to improve upon the limited data from earlier kaon-beam experiments at NINA (Albrow 1970) and SLAC (Brandenburg 1973). The new data will enable coupled-channel partial-wave analyses similar to those performed by the SAID group for $\pi N$ scattering (Arndt 2006), and will provide benchmarks for comparisons with QCD-inspired models and lattice QCD calculations, potentially resolving ambiguities in the extraction of strange resonance properties.
