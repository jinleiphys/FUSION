# Electromagnetic Form Factors and the Localization of Quark Orbital Angular Momentum in the Proton

> A new picture is given of generalized parton distributions probed in experiments in which the probe scale $Q^{2}$ and the momentum transfer $\DD^{2}$ are well separated. Application of this picture to the $Q^{2}$ dependence of the form factors $F_{1}, F_{2}$ shows that gauge invariant quark orbital angular momentum can be measured and indeed {\it localized} in the transverse profile of the proton. A previous prediction that $\sqrt{Q^{2}}F_{2}(Q^{2})/F_{1}(Q^{2}) \sim const. $ is generalized to GPD language. This prediction appears to have been confirmed by recent CEBAF data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that gauge-invariant quark orbital angular momentum can be measured and spatially localized in the transverse profile of the proton using generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and that a previous prediction that \(\sqrt{Q^{2}}F_{2}(Q^{2})/F_{1}(Q^{2}) \sim const.\) appears to have been confirmed by recent CEBAF data, though the authors note this is a generalization to GPD language and the data extend only to \(Q^{2}=6\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\).

## Method

The authors develop a physical picture of GPDs by analyzing the dependence of quark-proton scattering amplitudes on the transverse momentum transfer \(\DD_{T}\), which is conjugate to the impact parameter \(\bar b_{T}\) of the struck quark pair relative to the proton center-of-mass. They apply this to the Pauli form factor \(F_{2}\) in a Lorentz frame where the photon momentum has only transverse components, using an ansatz for the vertex function that includes an operator \(\omega\) which selects orbital SO(2) harmonics and probes \(\bar b_{T} \times \vec p \cdot \vec s_{T}\). The asymptotic scaling rule \(\bar b_{T} \sim Q_{T}/Q^{2}\) is then used to derive the ratio \(F_{2}/F_{1}\).

## Key numbers

- The CEBAF data extend to \(Q^{2}=6\ \mathrm{GeV}^{2}\) and appear to support the prediction that \(\sqrt{Q^{2}}F_{2}(Q^{2})/F_{1}(Q^{2}) \sim const.\)
- The asymptotic scaling rule derived gives \(F_{2}/F_{1} \sim (Q_{T}\cdot s_{T})/Q^{2}\), which yields \(F_{2}/F_{1} \sim 1/\sqrt{Q^{2}}\) after direct calculation.
- The normalization of \(QF_{2}/F_{1}\) indicates that the relative amplitudes for one and zero units of orbital angular momentum are comparable.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier introduction of GPDs and their relation to quark orbital angular momentum (Jain and Ralston, 1993), correcting a math error from that paper. It contradicts the conventional distribution amplitude formalism of Brodsky and Lepage (1989), which excludes orbital angular momentum and predicts \(F_{2}=0\). The approach enables future deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) experiments where \(Q^{2} \gg \DD_{T}\) to examine the target on a resolution small compared to the target size, revealing that the transversely polarized proton is flattened by the correlation of transverse spin with the scattering plane.
