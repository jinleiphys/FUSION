# Thermal Radiation from Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC

> The direct photon spectrum measured by the PHENIX collaboration in Au + Au collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV has been analyzed. It has been shown that the data can be reproduced reasonably well by assuming a deconfined state of thermalized quarks and gluons. The effects of the equation of state on the value of the initial temperature have been studied. The modifications of hadronic properties at non-zero temperature have been taken in to account.

## Key claim

The direct photon spectrum from PHENIX Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV can be reproduced by assuming a deconfined state of thermalized quarks and gluons, but the inferred initial temperature depends on the equation of state used: T_i ~ 400 MeV with a bag-model EOS versus T_i ~ 300 MeV with a lattice QCD EOS.

## Method

The author used (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry to model the space-time evolution of the system. Photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma were taken from complete order-alpha_s calculations using hard thermal loop resummation (Arnold et al.), while hadronic photon production included all possible reactions and decays involving pi, rho, omega, eta, and a1 mesons with in-medium mass modifications following the Brown-Rho scaling scenario. The initial thermalization time was set to tau_i = 0.2 fm/c and the freeze-out temperature to T_f = 120 MeV, fixed from pion and kaon p_T spectra.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_i = 400 MeV with bag-model equation of state (EOS type I) and tau_i = 0.2 fm/c reproduces the PHENIX direct photon data
- Initial temperature T_i ~ 300 MeV (with larger tau_i ~ 0.5 fm/c) reproduces the data when using lattice QCD equation of state (EOS type II)
- Transition temperature T_c = 190 MeV used in the analysis
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 120 MeV fixed from pion and kaon p_T spectra
- Total charged particle multiplicity dN/dy ~ 1100 used to constrain initial conditions
- Photons from N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory are enhanced by about 20% compared to thermal QCD in the p_T region ~ 2 GeV
- Initial thermalization time tau_i = 0.2 fm/c used for the bag-model EOS case

## Context

This work extends the analysis of direct photon data from the PHENIX collaboration at RHIC, following earlier analyses of WA98 data at SPS energies (Alam et al.) which gave T_i ~ 200 MeV. The results show sensitivity of the inferred initial temperature to the equation of state and to in-medium modifications of hadronic properties, highlighting the need to understand hadron properties in hot and dense environments. The comparison with photon production from N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory provides an estimate of the upper limit for photon production from a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, relevant since the QGP formed at RHIC may be strongly coupled.
