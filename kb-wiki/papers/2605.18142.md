# Systematic study of one-point kinetic energy density functionals for atomic nuclei

> To explore the applicability of orbital-free density functional theory (OF-DFT) in nuclear physics, we perform a systematic benchmark of 36 one-point kinetic energy density functionals, which are originally developed for electron systems in condensed matter physics. It is found that the direct use of the original parameters for electron systems leads to inconsistent performance, with certain functionals exhibiting physically unacceptable asymptotic behaviors. However, through parameter re-optimization targeting nuclear densities, different mathematical forms of generalized gradient approximation (GGA) functionals converge to a consistent root-mean-square error of approximately 13 MeV. From a physical perspective, this consistent behavior signifies that the optimized semi-local GGAs have successfully captured the macroscopic, liquid-drop-like background of the nucleus, while the residual deviations appear as periodic oscillations at the magic numbers that could reflect the quantum shell effects.

## Key claim

After parameter re-optimization targeting nuclear densities, 36 one-point kinetic energy density functionals originally developed for electron systems converge to a consistent root-mean-square error of approximately 13 MeV, but the residual deviations appear as periodic oscillations at magic numbers that likely reflect quantum shell effects which semi-local approximations cannot capture.

## Method

The authors performed non-self-consistent evaluations of 36 one-point kinetic energy density functionals (KEDFs) on Hartree-Fock densities from a simplified Skyrme interaction (SkP parameters) for 22 spherical, spin-saturated, isospin-symmetric even-even nuclei with Z=N=8-50, neglecting Coulomb, spin-orbit, and pairing correlations. They then re-optimized the parameters of selected functionals (LKT, VT84F, PBE2, E00, TFλW) by minimizing the mean squared error between KEDF and HF reference kinetic energies on a training set of 11 nuclei, using early stopping monitored on a validation set.

## Key numbers

- RMSE of original TF5W functional: 14.186 MeV across 22 nuclei
- RMSE of original E00 functional: 18.967 MeV across 22 nuclei
- RMSE of original LKT functional: 123.993 MeV across 22 nuclei
- RMSE of original VT84F functional: 150.478 MeV across 22 nuclei
- RMSE of original PBE2 functional: 151.551 MeV across 22 nuclei
- After re-optimization, all five functionals converged to RMSE values between 13.166 MeV (LKT*) and 13.629 MeV (E00*) across 22 nuclei
- The optimized TFλW functional used λ = 0.21025 (original TF5W used λ = 0.2)
- The liquid drop model re-fitted to HF binding energies gave RMSE of 13.895 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of orbital-free density functional theory (OF-DFT) from condensed matter physics to nuclear physics, systematically testing the transferability of 36 KEDFs developed for electron systems (Thomas-Fermi, von Weizsäcker, GEA, GGA, and meta-GGA forms) to finite nuclei. The study builds on prior work by Colò (2023) exploring OF-DFT for nuclei, and complements recent efforts using nonlocal KEDFs (Wu 2026) and machine learning approaches (Wu 2022, Hizawa 2023, Chen 2024) to capture shell effects. The finding that optimized semi-local GGAs converge to a ~13 MeV accuracy floor while missing shell effects establishes a benchmark for future nonlocal or data-driven KEDF development in nuclear physics.
