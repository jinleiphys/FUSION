# On p_T-broadening of high energy partons associated with the LPM effect in a finite-volume QCD medium

> We study the contributions from radiation to $p_{\perp}$-broadening of a high energy parton traversing a QCD medium with a finite length $L$. The interaction between the parton and the medium is described by decorrelated static multiple scattering. Amplitudes of medium-induced gluon emission and parton self-energy diagrams are evaluated in the soft gluon limit in the BDMPS formalism. We find both the double-logarithmic correction from incoherent scattering, which is parametrically the same as that in single scattering, and the logarithmic correction from the LPM effect. Therefore, we expect a parametrically large correction from radiation to the medium-induced $p_\perp$-broadening in perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the radiative correction to $p_\perp$-broadening of a high-energy parton in a finite-length QCD medium, finding that the correction is parametrically large due to both a double-logarithmic term from incoherent scattering and a logarithmic term from the LPM effect, but the numerical coefficient $C_{rad}$ in the parametric form $\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle_{rad} \simeq \alpha_s N_c C_{rad} Q_s^2$ had not been evaluated in perturbative QCD prior to this work.

## Method

The calculation is performed in the BDMPS formalism, where the interaction between the high-energy parton and the medium is described by decorrelated static multiple scattering. The authors evaluate amplitudes for medium-induced gluon emission and parton self-energy diagrams in the soft gluon limit, deriving the $p_{R\perp}$-differentiated gluon spectrum and the corrections from medium-induced self-energy diagrams. The evolution of the gluon emission amplitude $\vec{f}$ is obtained by solving a Schrödinger-like equation for a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator in impact parameter space.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order $p_\perp$-broadening from multiple scattering is $\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle_{m0} = \hat{q} L$, where $L$ is the medium length and $\hat{q}$ is the transport coefficient.
- The radiative energy loss is related to $p_\perp$-broadening as $\Delta E \propto \alpha_s N_c \langle p_\perp^2 \rangle_{m0} L = \alpha_s N_c \hat{q} L^2$.
- RHIC data on $J/\Psi$ suppression indicate $\hat{q} < 1~\text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}$ at thermalization time in most central Au+Au collisions, while fits to high-$p_\perp$ hadron spectra give time-averaged $\hat{q} > 5~\text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}$ (Eskola 2004, Wu 2010).
- The parametric form of the radiative contribution is $\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle_{rad} \simeq \alpha_s N_c C_{rad} Q_s^2$ with $Q_s^2 \equiv \hat{q} L$, and in strongly coupled SYM plasma the same form holds with $\alpha_s N_c$ replaced by $\sqrt{\lambda}/(2\pi)$ (Dominguez 2008).
- In single scattering, the contribution from an uncorrelated gluon emission is $\omega \frac{d}{d\omega} \langle p_{R\perp}^2 \rangle \simeq 2 \alpha_s N_c \hat{q}_R L \log \omega$, showing double-logarithmic enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS formalism (Baier 1996, 1997, 1998) to calculate the $p_\perp$-broadening from radiation, which had not been evaluated in perturbative QCD despite the parametric form being known. The results address a paradox where leading-order perturbative QCD results for $p_\perp$-broadening and radiative energy loss give contradictory values of $\hat{q}$ when compared to RHIC data (Eskola 2004, Baier 2006, Wu 2010). The calculation enables a quantitative comparison between perturbative QCD and strongly coupled SYM plasma results (Dominguez 2008), helping to determine whether the parametric dominance of multiple scattering over radiation in perturbative QCD holds numerically.
