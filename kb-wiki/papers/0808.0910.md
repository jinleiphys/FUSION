# Toward the AdS/CFT Gravity Dual for High Energy Collisions. 3.Gravitationally Collapsing Shell and Quasiequilibrium

> The equilibration of matter and onset of hydrodynamics can be understood in the AdS/CFT context as a gravitational collapse process, in which "collision debris" create a horizon. In this paper we consider the simplest geometry possible, a flat shell (or membrane) falling in the holographic direction toward the horizon. The metric is a combination of two well known solutions: thermal AdS above the shell and pure AdS below, while motion of the shell is given by the Israel junction condition. Furthermore, when the shell motion can be considered slow, we were able to solve for two-point functions of all boundary stress tensor and found that an observer on the boundary sees a very peculiar $quasiequilibrium$: while the average stress tensor $<T_{μν>}$ contains the equilibrium plasma energy and pressure at all times, the spectral densities of the correlators(related with occupation probabilities of the modes) reveal additional oscillating terms absent in equilibrium. This is explained by the "echo" phenomenon, a partial return of the field coherence at certain "echo" times.

## Key claim

The paper shows that during gravitational collapse of a flat shell in AdS, a boundary observer measuring only the average stress tensor would conclude the matter is fully equilibrated at all times, but a two-point observer measuring stress tensor correlation functions would see deviations from thermal equilibrium in the form of additional oscillating terms in the spectral densities, explained by an "echo" phenomenon of partial return of field coherence.

## Method

The authors construct a metric combining thermal AdS-black hole outside a falling flat shell and pure AdS inside, with the shell's motion governed by the Israel junction condition. They solve for two-point functions of the boundary stress tensor by propagating graviton perturbations across the shell, using the junction condition itself to derive matching conditions for the metric perturbations at the shell's position. In the quasistatic limit where the Fourier mode frequency is much larger than the shell's falling velocity, they solve the wave equations numerically and using WKB approximation for three channels (scalar, shear, and sound) to extract retarded correlators and spectral densities.

## Key numbers

- The shell trajectory shows three stages: initial acceleration (z = z_0 + #t^2), intermediate near-constant velocity fall, and final near-horizon freezing with exponentially small deviation (z = z_h - e^{-#t})
- The initial position z_0 is identified with the inverse saturation scale 1/Q_s, where Q_s is about 1.5 GeV at RHIC
- The horizon radius z_h = 1/pi T, with initial temperatures at RHIC believed to be about 0.35 GeV, giving pi T approximately 1.1 GeV
- The thermalization time suggested by RHIC elliptic flow data is of order 0.5 fm/c
- The parameter constraint is kappa_5^2 p < 6, with the specific value kappa_5^2 p = 1 used in numerical calculations
- Spectral densities are plotted at q = 0, q = 1.5, and q = 4.5 for five values of u_m (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9) representing different stages of thermalization

## Context

This work continues the authors' previous papers Lin and Shuryak (2006) and Lin and Shuryak (2007), which studied falling strings between departing charges without a horizon, extending to heavy ion collisions and equilibration with horizon creation. It builds on early collapsing shell work by Shellon, Danielsson, and Giddings, who used bulk scalar fields as probes, but improves upon those by using graviton perturbations instead and deriving boundary conditions at the shell from the junction condition itself. The results enable understanding of how matter equilibrates rapidly in RHIC collisions, showing that quasiequilibrium with oscillatory deviations from thermal spectral densities precedes full thermalization, with the oscillations damping in amplitude and growing in frequency as the shell approaches the horizon.
