# Magnetic properties of the hadron resonance gas with physical magnetic moments

> We study magnetic properties of the Hadron Resonance Gas in the presence of a strong ($0 \le B \le 0.15~{\rm GeV}^2$) uniform magnetic field, using physical values of the magnetic moments of hadrons, i.e., including their anomalous parts. The values of these moments are taken from experiment, or when unavailable, from theoretical estimates. We evaluate the conserved charge susceptibilities, finding the expected sizable effects of the anomalous magnetic moments, in particular of the octet baryons, such as the proton and neutron, where they are exceptionally large. We also study in detail the large effects of the magnetic moments of the $Δ(1232)$ states, for which various theoretical estimates and experimental values differ significantly. We compare our model results with the lattice QCD data and find reasonable agreement within the model uncertainty.

## Key claim

The inclusion of physical (anomalous) magnetic moments of hadrons, particularly the octet baryons and the Δ(1232) states, produces sizable enhancements in conserved charge susceptibilities compared to the previously used g=2Q prescription, and yields reasonable agreement with lattice QCD data within model uncertainty for magnetic fields up to B ≤ 0.15 GeV².

## Method

The authors compute diagonal and non-diagonal conserved charge susceptibilities (χ_BB, χ_QQ, χ_SS, χ_BS, χ_BQ, χ_QS) within the Hadron Resonance Gas model using the QMHRG2020 hadron list, incorporating physical magnetic moments from experiment or theoretical estimates for low-mass hadrons. For spin-1/2 states they use the exact relativistic energy spectrum from Tsai and Yildiz (1971), while for higher-spin states they adopt approximate formulas that treat the anomalous part as a perturbation to the g=2Q spectrum. The susceptibilities are evaluated as functions of temperature (T = 100-165 MeV) and magnetic field (B = 0-0.15 GeV²) and compared to lattice QCD data from Bollweg (2021) and Ding (2025).

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field range studied: 0 ≤ B ≤ 0.15 GeV²
- For the proton, the g-factor is g_p ≈ 5.586 (compared to g=2Q=2), yielding an enhancement factor R ≈ 14 in χ_BB relative to the g=2Q prescription
- For the neutron, g_n ≈ -3.831 (neglected entirely in g=2Q prescription), contributing about 40% of the total nucleon contribution to χ_BB
- At T = 145 MeV and B = 0.15 GeV², nucleons and Δ(1232) states together contribute approximately 50% of the total χ_BB
- At T = 100 MeV, nucleons alone contribute approximately 60% of the total χ_BB
- For χ_QQ at T = 145 MeV, pions contribute about 35% and Δ states about 18% of the total
- The upper applicability limit from the condition μ_p B ≲ m_Δ - m_p gives B ≲ 0.2 GeV²
- At B = 0, the HRG calculation overestimates lattice χ_QQ results by about 8%

## Context

This work extends the HRG studies of Endrodi (2013), Bali (2014, 2020), and the lattice QCD comparisons of Ding (2023, 2025), which all neglected anomalous magnetic moments by using g=2Q for spin-1/2 hadrons. It builds on earlier work by Bhattacharyya, Ghosh, Ray, and Samanta (2015) who investigated anomalous magnetic moments in HRG prior to lattice data availability. The results enable improved modeling of hot hadronic matter under strong magnetic fields relevant to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and neutron star physics, while identifying the Δ(1232) magnetic moments as the dominant source of model uncertainty.
