# Dynamic scaling and quenching for heavy quark in the linear expanding medium

## Key claim
Scaling is a good approximation for the energy loss of heavy quarks propagating through linearly expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP), and the effective quenching parameter of the equivalent static medium does not depend on the heavy quark mass or the dead cone angle. The claim holds for both Salgado-Wiedemann scaling (total parton energy loss) and Caucal-Iancu-Soyez scaling (jet energy loss), but the agreement is less good for very small emitted gluon energies that do not contribute significantly to parton energy loss.

## Method
The authors calculate the heavy quark energy loss and gluon radiation spectrum using both the BDMPSZ (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff-Zakharov) approach and the ASW (Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann) approach, which includes the kinematic constraint k_t < omega. They consider a Bjorken linear expansion (h=1) of the QGP, using the harmonic approximation and the Gyulassy-Wang model, and compare the results for expanding media to those for an equivalent static medium with a quenching coefficient q_hat_stat derived from the time-dependent q_hat(t). The analysis includes both bulk and boundary contributions to the energy loss, and the authors compute quenching weights for bottom quarks (mass 5 GeV) and massless quarks at various parton transverse momenta.

## Key numbers
- For linear expansion, the effective static quenching coefficient is q_hat_stat = 2 q_hat_0 t_0 / L in the limit L >> t_0, where q_hat_0 = 3-6 GeV^3 and t_0 = 0.1-0.2 fm (0.5-1 GeV^-1).
- Quenching weights for bottom quarks (m_b = 5 GeV) in expanding media using ASW: 0.84 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.79 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.79 at p_T = 25 GeV; for massless quarks: 0.89 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.80 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.73 at p_T = 25 GeV.
- Quenching weights for bottom quarks in equivalent static media using ASW: 0.83 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.78 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.80 at p_T = 25 GeV; for massless quarks: 0.87 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.77 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.70 at p_T = 25 GeV.
- Quenching weights for bottom quarks in expanding media using BDMPSZ: 0.80 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.69 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.64 at p_T = 25 GeV; for massless quarks: 0.78 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.65 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.50 at p_T = 25 GeV.
- Quenching weights for bottom quarks in equivalent static media using BDMPSZ: 0.79 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.71 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.69 at p_T = 25 GeV; for massless quarks: 0.79 at p_T = 100 GeV, 0.62 at p_T = 50 GeV, 0.46 at p_T = 25 GeV.
- The characteristic frequency omega_br for heavy flavour quark jets (alpha_s = 0.24, R = 0.4, L = 4 fm, t_0 = 0.2 fm, q_hat_0 = 3 GeV^3, q_hat_stat = 0.6 GeV^3) in BDMPSZ: 3.75 GeV for theta_0 = 0, 3.75 GeV for theta_0 = 0.05, 3.5 GeV for theta_0 = 0.1, 2.5 GeV for theta_0 = 0.2.

## Context
This work extends the scaling ideas of Salgado and Wiedemann (2003-2004) and Caucal, Iancu, and Soyez (2019-2020), which were previously developed only for massless partons, to the case of heavy quarks. The authors show that the scaling property and the mass-independence of the effective quenching parameter hold for both the total heavy quark energy loss and the heavy quark jet energy loss in linearly expanding media. This enables the use of simpler static-medium calculations to describe heavy quark energy loss in expanding QGP, and provides a framework for estimating the dead cone angle dependence of jet energy loss, though the authors note that a full quantitative solution would require solving cascade equations for the massive case, which is more complex than the massless case treated by Turbide et al. (2006) and Fister et al. (2015).
