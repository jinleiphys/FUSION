# System size dependence of intermediate mass fragments in heavy-ion collisions

> We simulate the central reactions of $^{20}$Ne+$^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ar+$^{45}$Sc, $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni, $^{86}$Kr+$^{93}$Nb, $^{129}$Xe+$^{118}$Sn, $^{86}$Kr+$^{197}$Au and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at different incident energies for different equations of state (EOS), binary cross sections and different widths of Gaussians. A rise and fall behaviour of the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) is observed. The system size dependence of peak center-of-mass energy E$_{c.m.} ^{max}$ and peak IMF multiplicity $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$ is also studied, where it is observed that E$_{c.m.}^{max}$ follows a linear behaviour and $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$ shows a power law dependence. A comparison between two clusterization methods, the minimum spanning tree and the minimum spanning tree method with binding energy check (MSTB) is also made. We find that MSTB method reduces the $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$ especially in heavy systems. The power law dependence is also observed for fragments of different sizes at E$_{c.m.} ^{max}$ and power law parameter $τ$ is found to be close to unity in all cases except A$^{max}$.

## Key claim
The peak center-of-mass energy for maximum intermediate mass fragment (IMF) production scales linearly with system mass, while the peak IMF multiplicity follows a power law with exponent close to unity, but these scaling relations depend on the clusterization method used, with the MSTB method reducing the peak multiplicity especially for heavy systems.

## Method
Central collisions of seven symmetric and asymmetric systems (from 20Ne+20Ne to 197Au+197Au) were simulated using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model at various incident energies. The simulations employed different equations of state (hard, soft, with and without momentum-dependent interactions), different nucleon-nucleon cross sections (energy-dependent Cugnon and constant 55 mb), and two Gaussian widths (L=1.08 and L=2.16 fm²). Fragment formation was analyzed using both the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method and the Minimum Spanning Tree with binding energy check (MSTB) method, with quadratic fits to the multiplicity versus energy data used to extract peak values.

## Key numbers
- For 86Kr+93Nb at 75 AMeV, changing the Gaussian width from L to L^broad reduced IMF multiplicity by approximately 30%.
- The peak center-of-mass energy E_cm^max showed a linear dependence on composite system mass A, proportional to mA, across all model ingredients.
- The peak IMF multiplicity ⟨N_IMF⟩^max followed a power law ∝ A^τ with τ close to unity (τ∼1.0) for all fragment types except the largest fragment A^max.
- The power law parameter τ was found to be close to unity for free nucleons, light charged particles (LCPs, 2≤A≤4), medium mass fragments (MMFs, 5≤A≤9), heavy mass fragments (HMFs, 10≤A≤44), and IMFs (5≤A≤44), but showed no clear system size dependence for A^max.
- For the MSTB method with Gaussian width L^broad, the ⟨N_IMF⟩ was nearly zero in the incident energy range studied.

## Context
This work extends the experimental findings of Sisan et al. (2001) and the theoretical QMD calculations of Vermani and Puri (2009) by systematically testing the sensitivity of system-size scaling to model ingredients such as equation of state, nucleon-nucleon cross section, and Gaussian width. The study also compares MST and MSTB clusterization methods, showing that the MSTB method reduces peak IMF multiplicities in heavy systems by excluding loosely bound fragments. The results enable future work to constrain model parameters by comparing with experimental data from Tsang et al. (1993), Sisan et al. (2001), and Peaslee et al. (1994), and suggest that the power law system mass dependence predicted by Dhawan and Puri (2006) at the energy of vanishing flow also holds at the energy of maximum IMF production.
