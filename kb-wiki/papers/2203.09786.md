# Spin Effects In Heavy Ion Collisions at High Energies

> In non-central high energy heavy ion collisions, the colliding system possesses a huge orbital angular momentum along the normal direction of the reaction plane. Due to the spin orbit interaction in the system, such a huge orbital angular momentum leads to the spin polarization of quarks and anti-quarks in the quark matter system produced in the collision. Such an effect, known as the global polarization effect, was predicted many years ago and has been confirmed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC. The discovery of the global polarization effect opens a new avenue in heavy ion physics in general and in studying the properties of quark-gluon plasma in particular. This talk will briefly review the original ideas and calculations that lead to the prediction and summarize progresses and problems in related aspects.

# Spin Effects In Heavy Ion Collisions at High Energies

## Key claim

The global polarization effect (GPE) of quarks and anti-quarks in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in non-central heavy ion collisions arises from spin-orbit coupling transferring the system's huge orbital angular momentum into spin polarization, and this effect has been confirmed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC. However, the talk acknowledges that while the overall magnitude of hyperon polarization is consistent with qualitative theoretical estimates, several fine-structure measurements (vector meson spin alignment, local polarization azimuthal dependence, and low-energy behavior) show significant deviations from theoretical expectations, indicating that the underlying mechanisms are not yet fully understood.

## Method

The talk reviews the original theoretical approach of Liang (2005) and Gao (2007, 2020) that calculated quark polarization from parton scattering at fixed impact parameter, using both a screened static potential model and hard thermal loop resumed gluon propagators for quark-quark scattering in a thermal medium. The global orbital angular momentum of the colliding system was estimated using the Landau fireball model and the Bjorken scaling scenario, with numerical input from the HIJING Monte-Carlo model and the BGK model. Predictions for observable hadron polarization were derived for both quark combination and quark fragmentation mechanisms, and compared with experimental data from STAR, ALICE, and HADES collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The global orbital angular momentum in non-central heavy ion collisions is of the order of 10^5 (in units of hbar) at most impact parameters for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.
- In Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the average local relative orbital angular momentum l_0 for two neighboring partons separated by Delta x=1 fm is approximately -1.7 (in units of hbar).
- The longitudinal momentum gradient dp_0/dx is approximately 0.34 GeV/fm for Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.
- The average rapidity shear from HIJING in the central rapidity region gives partial derivative of longitudinal momentum with respect to transverse position of approximately 0.003 GeV/fm.
- The STAR collaboration measured global Lambda hyperon polarization averaged over beam energies as (1.08 +/- 0.15 +/- 0.11)% and for anti-Lambda as (1.38 +/- 0.30 +/- 0.13)%.
- In the static potential model, the quark polarization P_q is given by -pi * mu_D * |p| / (2E(E+m_q)), where mu_D is the Debye screening mass.

## Context

This talk reviews the theoretical prediction of global polarization by Liang (2005) and its experimental confirmation by the STAR collaboration (2017), which opened the field of spin physics in heavy ion collisions. The work extends earlier studies of spin effects in high energy physics and builds on the understanding that the reaction plane in heavy ion collisions can be determined experimentally via elliptic flow v_2 and directed flow v_1 measurements (Ollitrault, 1992). The discovery enables new investigations into spin-vortical coupling in strongly interacting systems (Betz, 2007; Becattini, 2007, 2013, 2016), and has motivated hydrodynamic model calculations and Monte-Carlo event generator simulations of QGP vorticity (Deng, 2016; Pang, 2016). Current challenges include explaining the unexpectedly large vector meson spin alignment observed by STAR and ALICE, resolving the sign problem in local polarization measurements (STAR, 2019), and understanding the energy dependence of GPE at low energies where HADES has reported preliminary results.
