# Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha clusters and new soft mode in 12C--52Fe 4N nuclei in field theoretical superfluid cluster model

> Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha clusters in light and medium-heavy nuclei is studied in the frame of the field theoretical superfluid cluster model. The order parameter of the phase transition from the Wigner phase to the Nambu-Goldstone phase is a superfluid amplitude, square of the moduli of which is the superfluid density distribution. The zero mode operators due to the spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase in the finite number of alpha clusters are rigorously treated. The theory is systematically applied to N alpha nuclei from12C-52Fe at various condensation rates. In 12C it is found that the energy levels of the gas-like well-developed alpha cluster states above the Hoyle state are reproduced well in agreement with experiment for realistic condensation rates of alpha clusters. The electric E2 and E0 transitions are calculated and found to be sensitive to the condensation rates. The profound raison d'etre of the alpha cluster gas-like states above the Hoyle state, whose structure has been interpreted geometrically in the nuclear models without the order parameter such as the cluster models or ab initio calculations, is revealed. It is found that in addition to the Bogoliubov-de Gennes vibrational mode states collective states of the zero mode operators appear systematically at low excitation energies from the N alpha threshold energy. These collective states, new-type soft modes in nuclei due to the Bose-Einstein condensation of the alpha clusters, emerge systematically in light and medium-heavy mass regions and are also located at high excitation energies from the ground state in contrast to the traditional concept of soft mode in the low excitation energy region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Hoyle state and its excited states in 12C can be understood as a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of alpha clusters with a realistic condensation probability around 70%, not requiring 100% condensation, and that the order parameter formalism reveals new collective zero-mode states that are systematically missed by traditional cluster models lacking an order parameter.

## Method

The authors apply a field-theoretical superfluid cluster model (interacting zero mode formulation) to N-alpha nuclei from 12C to 52Fe, where the order parameter is the superfluid amplitude from spontaneous symmetry breaking of the global phase. They solve the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the condensate wavefunction using an external harmonic trapping potential and the Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha interaction, then treat the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) vibrational modes and the zero mode operators (Nambu-Goldstone operators) separately, with the zero mode states solved from a nonlinear Hamiltonian that avoids the phase diffusion problem in finite systems.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle state in 12C is at 7.654 MeV excitation energy, with a condensation probability of about 70% (N0 = 0.7N for N=3 alpha clusters) based on prior work by Matsumura and Suzuki (2004) and Yamada and Schuck (2005)
- The study covers N-alpha nuclei from N=3 to N=13, specifically 12C, 16O, 20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, 48Cr, and 52Fe
- The direct three-alpha decay from the Hoyle state is reported as less than 0.043% (Aquila 2017) and 0.047% (Smith 2017) from recent precise experiments

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical superfluid cluster model previously applied to 12C by Nakamura (2016) under 100% condensation assumption, now using realistic 70% condensation rates. It contradicts the interpretation that the small direct three-alpha decay branching ratio makes the BEC interpretation less likely, arguing that BEC can be realized with smaller but significant condensation probability. The approach enables systematic study of BEC in medium-heavy nuclei up to 52Fe, revealing new zero-mode collective states that appear as soft modes at high excitation energies, in contrast to traditional soft modes like quadrupole collective motion at low energies.
