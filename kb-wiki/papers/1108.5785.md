# Non-perturbative Analysis of the Influence of the Proton Magnetization and Charge Densities on the Hyperfine Splitting of Muonic Hydrogen

> We investigate the influence of the spatial extent of the proton magnetization and charge densities on the 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen. The use of a non-perturbative relativistic Dirac approach leads to corrections of 15% to values obtained from the perturbative treatment encapsulated by the Zemach radius, which surpass the next-leading order contribution in the perturbation series by an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative relativistic Dirac treatment of the proton's spatial charge and magnetization distributions yields a Zemach correction to the 2S hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen that differs by 10–15% from the perturbative value used in the analysis of Pohl (2010), and this difference is an order of magnitude larger than the next-leading order perturbative contribution.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Dirac equation for the 2S hyperfine states (F=0 and F=1) of muonic hydrogen, including the finite-size Coulomb potential, finite-size vacuum polarization potential, and the Zemach spin-spin interaction potential. The proton magnetization density was modeled as a normalized exponential distribution parameterized by the magnetic radius, and calculations were performed for two values of the proton charge radius (0.84184 fm and 0.8768 fm) and for magnetic radii extracted from Mainz and Jefferson Laboratory data. The computed hyperfine splittings were fit to a polynomial function of the mean-square magnetic radius.

## Key numbers

- The non-perturbative Zemach correction to the 2S hyperfine splitting is -0.1306 meV for r_p^C = 0.84184 fm and -0.1292 meV for r_p^C = 0.8768 fm.
- The perturbative Zemach correction used by Pohl (2010), from Martynenko (2004), is -0.1518 meV, which is 10–15% larger in magnitude than the non-perturbative result.
- The next-leading order perturbative contribution (order alpha^6) is -0.0017 meV, an order of magnitude smaller than the 0.02 meV difference between the perturbative and non-perturbative Zemach corrections.
- The difference in hyperfine splitting between using the Mainz magnetic radius (0.777 ± 0.029 fm) and the Jefferson Laboratory magnetic radius (0.850 ± 0.030 fm) is only 0.35 μeV for r_p^C = 0.84184 fm and 0.23 μeV for r_p^C = 0.8768 fm.
- The final non-perturbative 2S hyperfine splitting values are 22.6384 meV (r_p^C = 0.84184 fm) and 22.6347 meV (r_p^C = 0.8768 fm).
- Using the corrected Zemach contribution, the extracted proton charge radius is 0.84182(67) fm.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of proton structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen, which was previously calculated by Martynenko (2004) and used in the analysis of the proton radius measurement by Pohl (2010). The non-perturbative Dirac approach reveals that the perturbative Zemach radius treatment does not capture the full effect of the proton's spatial distributions, with the correction being an order of magnitude larger than the next perturbative term. The results enable more precise extraction of the proton charge radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and motivate re-evaluation of other perturbative corrections in the analysis.
