# Dynamic Universality Class of Model H with Frustrated Diffusion: $ε$-Expansion

> We study a variation of the dynamic universality class of model H in a spatial dimension of $d=4-ε$, by frustrating charge diffusion and momentum density fluctuations along $d_T=1$ or $d_T=2$ dimensions, while keeping the same dynamics of model H in the other $d_L=d-d_T$ dimensions. The case of $d_T=2$ describes the QCD critical point in a background magnetic field. We find that these models belong to a different dynamical universality class due to extended conservation laws compared to the model H, although the static universality class remains the same as the 3-dimensional Ising model. We compute the dynamic critical exponents of these models in first order of $ε$-expansion to find that $x_λ\approx 0.847\,ε$, $x_{\barη}\approx 0.153\,ε$, and $z=4-x_λ\approx 3.15$ when $ε=1$ and $d_T=2$. For $d_T=1$ the results are numerically similar to the model H values: $z\approx 3.08$.

## Key claim

The paper studies a variation of the dynamic universality class of model H where charge diffusion and momentum density fluctuations are frustrated along \(d_T=1\) or \(d_T=2\) spatial dimensions, while retaining model H dynamics in the remaining \(d_L = d - d_T\) dimensions. The author finds that these models belong to a different dynamical universality class from the original model H due to extended conservation laws, even though the static universality class remains the same as the 3-dimensional Ising model.

## Method

The author performs a Wilsonian renormalization group analysis in \(d = 4 - \epsilon\) spatial dimensions using the \(\epsilon\)-expansion at first order. The model modifies the standard model H by suppressing charge conductivity and momentum density fluctuations along \(d_T\) dimensions, while keeping them active in the remaining \(d_L\) dimensions. The dynamic critical exponents are computed by integrating over momentum shells near the UV cutoff \(\Lambda\) and analyzing the renormalization group flow equations for the transport coefficients \(\lambda_\parallel\), \(\bar\eta_\perp\), and \(\bar\eta_\parallel\), along with the non-linear coupling \(g\).

## Key numbers

* For \(d_T = 2\) and \(\epsilon = 1\): \(x_\lambda \approx 0.847\,\epsilon\) (so \(x_\lambda \approx 0.847\)), \(x_{\bar\eta} \approx 0.153\,\epsilon\) (so \(x_{\bar\eta} \approx 0.153\)), and the dynamic critical exponent \(z = 4 - x_\lambda \approx 3.15\).
* For \(d_T = 1\) and \(\epsilon = 1\): the dynamic critical exponent \(z \approx 3.08\), which is numerically similar to the standard model H values.
* The static universality class remains the 3-dimensional Ising model, with the anomalous dimension of \(\psi\) given by \(\eta/2 \sim \epsilon^2\).

## Context

This work extends the classic model H analysis of Siggia, Halperin, and Hohenberg (1976) and Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) by introducing frustrated diffusion along a subset of spatial dimensions. The \(d_T = 2\) case is motivated by the description of the QCD critical point in a strong background magnetic field, following the work of Li and Yee (2017). The results enable future studies of dynamic critical phenomena in systems with reduced dimensionality for charge transport, such as layered materials or systems in strong magnetic fields, and the author notes that the Feynman diagram method of Wilson (1971) can in principle be extended to higher orders in \(\epsilon\).
