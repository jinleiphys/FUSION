# Dense nucleonic matter and the renormalization group

> Fluctuations are included in a chiral nucleon-meson model within the framework of the functional renormalization group. The model, with parameters fitted to reproduce the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, is used to study the phase diagram of QCD. We find good agreement with results from chiral effective field theory. Moreover, the results show a separation of the chemical freeze-out line and chiral symmetry restoration at large baryon chemical potentials.

## Key claim
Fluctuations included via the functional renormalization group shift the nuclear liquid-gas transition line away from the mean-field result, bringing it into very good agreement with chiral effective field theory calculations; the chiral order parameter remains above 0.65 for temperatures up to 100 MeV and baryon chemical potentials below about 1 GeV, indicating that chiral symmetry restoration is well separated from the liquid-gas transition and no chiral first-order transition appears in the region of applicability.

## Method
The authors start from a chiral nucleon-meson model with nucleons, pions, a scalar sigma field, and an isoscalar vector omega field, with parameters fitted to reproduce nuclear saturation density, binding energy, surface tension, and compression modulus. They go beyond mean-field by applying the functional renormalization group (FRG) in the local-potential approximation, using Wetterich's flow equation with a Litim regulator, to self-consistently include thermal mesonic and nucleonic fluctuations around the liquid-gas phase transition. The flow of the effective potential and the background vector field is solved numerically on a discretized grid for temperatures and baryon chemical potentials around the transition.

## Key numbers
- The critical chemical potential at the T=0 intercept of the liquid-gas phase transition line is mu_c = 923 MeV, given by the difference of the nucleon mass and the binding energy per nucleon.
- The temperature of the critical point in the FRG treatment of the nucleon-meson model is T_c = 18.3 MeV, compared to 15.1 MeV in chiral effective field theory (Fiorilla 2012).
- The chiral order parameter sigma/f_pi exceeds 0.65 for temperatures up to 100 MeV and baryon chemical potentials smaller than about 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends mean-field studies of the chiral nucleon-meson model (Berges 2003, Floerchinger 2012) by including fluctuations via the functional renormalization group, which previously found no relationship between chiral restoration and chemical freeze-out points at mean-field level. The results are compared with chiral effective field theory calculations (Fiorilla 2012, Holt 2013) that include one- and two-pion exchange, three-body forces, and Delta-isobar excitations, showing good agreement for the liquid-gas transition. The separation of the chemical freeze-out line from chiral symmetry restoration at large baryon chemical potentials provides constraints for modeling the QCD phase diagram and for interpreting heavy-ion collision data.
