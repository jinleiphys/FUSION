# Vacuum Polarization and Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking: Phase Diagram of QED with Four-Fermion Contact Interaction

> We study chiral symmetry breaking for fundamental charged fermions coupled electromagnetically to photons with the inclusion of four-fermion contact self-interaction term. We employ multiplicatively renormalizable models for the photon dressing function and the electron-photon vertex which minimally ensures mass anomalous dimension = 1. Vacuum polarization screens the interaction strength. Consequently, the pattern of dynamical mass generation for fermions is characterized by a critical number of massless fermion flavors above which chiral symmetry is restored. This effect is in diametrical opposition to the existence of criticality for the minimum interaction strength necessary to break chiral symmetry dynamically. The presence of virtual fermions dictates the nature of phase transition. Miransky scaling laws for the electromagnetic interaction strength and the four-fermion coupling, observed for quenched QED, are replaced by a mean-field power law behavior corresponding to a second order phase transition. These results are derived analytically by employing the bifurcation analysis, and are later confirmed numerically by solving the original non-linearized gap equation. A three dimensional critical surface is drawn to clearly depict the interplay of the relative strengths of interactions and number of flavors to separate the two phases. We also compute the beta-function and observe that it has ultraviolet fixed point. The power law part of the momentum dependence, describing the mass function, reproduces the quenched limit trivially. We also comment on the continuum limit and the triviality of QED.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in unquenched QED with a four-fermion contact interaction, the presence of virtual fermion loops (vacuum polarization) changes the nature of the chiral phase transition from the exponential Miransky scaling found in quenched QED to a mean-field square-root power law, and that a critical number of fermion flavors \(N_f^c\) exists above which chiral symmetry is restored, with the caveat that these results rely on multiplicatively renormalizable models for the photon dressing function and the electron-photon vertex that minimally ensure a mass anomalous dimension \(\gamma_m = 1\).

## Method
The authors employ Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs) for the fermion propagator in Landau gauge, using multiplicatively renormalizable power-law models for the photon dressing function \(G(q^2) = (q^2/\Lambda^2)^s\) and a vertex ansatz that ensures \(\gamma_m = 1\) in the quenched limit. They perform a bifurcation analysis of the linearized gap equation near criticality, converting it into a Bessel differential equation, and then solve the original non-linearized gap equation numerically to confirm the analytic results for the dynamically generated fermion mass as a function of the electromagnetic coupling \(\alpha\), the four-fermion coupling \(\lambda\), and the number of fermion flavors \(N_f\).

## Key numbers
* The critical electromagnetic coupling in quenched QED is \(\alpha_c \sim 1\) (from prior work, cited as Maskawa and Nakajima and the Kiev group).
* The mass anomalous dimension is \(\gamma_m = 1\) in the quenched limit, and with unquenching the power-law part of the mass function implies \(\gamma_m = 1 + s\), where \(s = \alpha N_f / (3\pi)\).
* The dynamically generated mass scales as \(m/\Lambda = A_1 (\alpha - \alpha_c)^{1/2}\) for fixed \(N_f\) and \(\lambda\).
* The dynamically generated mass scales as \(m/\Lambda = A_2 (\lambda - \lambda_c)^{1/2}\) for fixed \(N_f\) and \(\alpha\).
* The dynamically generated mass scales as \(m/\Lambda = A_3 (N_f^c - N_f)^{1/2}\) for fixed \(\alpha\) and \(\lambda\).
* The beta-functions are \(\beta_\alpha = -2(\alpha - \alpha_c)\) and \(\beta_\lambda = -2(\lambda - \lambda_c)\), showing that \(\alpha_c\) and \(\lambda_c\) are ultraviolet fixed points.
* Numerical results are shown for \(\alpha = 2.5\) and \(N_f = 2\) when varying \(\lambda\), for \(\alpha = 2.5\) and \(\lambda = 0.3\) when varying \(N_f\), and for \(N_f = 2\) and \(\lambda = 0.6\) when varying \(\alpha\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of unquenched QED without four-fermion interactions (Calcaneo-Roldan et al., 2011) by including the four-fermion contact term, and it generalizes the quenched QED results with four-fermion interactions (Gorbar, 1991) to the unquenched case. The paper shows that the square-root scaling laws for the dynamically generated mass as a function of \(\alpha\), \(\lambda\), and \(N_f\) are robust to the inclusion of the four-fermion term, and it provides a three-dimensional critical surface in the phase space of \((\alpha, \lambda, N_f)\) that separates the chirally symmetric and broken phases. The results enable future studies of the continuum limit and triviality of QED, and they suggest that the renormalized coupling at zero momentum \(\alpha_R(0)\) goes to zero on the phase boundary, consistent with lattice computations (Rakow, 1991).
