# Speed of sound and polytropic index in QCD matter

> We investigate the speed of sound and polytropic index of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) matter in the full phase diagram based on a 3-flavor Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (pNJL) model. The speed of sound and polytropic index in isothermal and adiabatic cases all have a dip structure at the low chemical potential side of the chiral phase transition boundary, and these quantities reach their global minimum values at the critical endpoint (CEP) but are not completely zero, where the values in adiabatic are lightly greater than those in isothermal. Different from the speed of sound, the polytropic index also exists a peak around the chiral phase transition boundary. Along the hypothetical chemical freeze-out lines, the speed of sound rapidly decreases near the CEP, followed by a small spinodal behavior, while the polytropic index, especially in isothermal, exhibits a more pronounced and nearly closed to zero dip structure as it approaches the CEP.

## Key claim

Using a 3-flavor Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, the speed of sound and polytropic index reach their global minimum values at the critical endpoint (CEP) but are not completely zero; the adiabatic values are slightly greater than the isothermal values, and the polytropic index additionally exhibits a peak structure around the chiral phase transition boundary that the speed of sound does not show.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential density of the 3-flavor PNJL model at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, solving the gap equations for the quark condensates and Polyakov loop fields in the mean-field approximation. From the resulting pressure, energy density, baryon number density, and entropy density, they derive the squared speed of sound in both isothermal (\(c_T^2\)) and adiabatic (\(c_{s/\rho_B}^2\)) cases using Jacobian determinant methods, and then obtain the corresponding polytropic indices \(\gamma_T\) and \(\gamma_{s/\rho_B}\) via \(\gamma = (\varepsilon/P) c_s^2\). The behavior of these quantities is mapped across the \(\mu_B\)-\(T\) plane, including along hypothetical chemical freeze-out lines constructed by rescaling the chiral phase transition boundary.

## Key numbers

- The CEP is located at \(\mu_B = 950\) MeV and \(T = 98\) MeV in this PNJL model.
- At the CEP, the isothermal squared speed of sound is \(c_T^2 = 0.002\) and the adiabatic squared speed of sound is \(c_{s/\rho_B}^2 = 0.01\).
- The squared speed of sound in the chiral breaking region is mostly less than 0.1.
- At high temperature and baryon chemical potential, both \(c_T^2\) and \(c_{s/\rho_B}^2\) approach the conformal limit \(c_s^2 = 1/3\).
- The polytropic index in the chiral breaking region is almost less than 1.5, and approaches \(\gamma = 1\) in the conformal limit at high temperature and baryon chemical potential.
- The polytropic index criteria for separating quark matter from hadronic matter in neutron star cores are cited as \(\gamma < 1.75\) (Ann20) or \(\gamma \leq 1.6\) and \(c_s^2 \leq 0.7\) (Han23), and a newer conformality criterion \(d_c < 0.2\) (Ann23).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the speed of sound in QCD matter using effective models such as the NJL and quark-meson models (He22, He23, Mot20), which also found a global minimum at the CEP that does not vanish in mean-field approximation. The polytropic index, previously applied primarily to neutron star studies as a criterion for distinguishing hadronic from quark matter (Ann20, Liu237, Liu238, Han23), is here investigated across the full QCD phase diagram for the first time. The results suggest that the polytropic index, especially in the isothermal case, may provide a more sensitive probe of the CEP in heavy-ion collision experiments than the speed of sound alone, and could connect to ongoing Beam Energy Scan measurements at RHIC and to neutron star and gravitational wave studies.
