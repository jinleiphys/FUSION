# Hadronic production of eta--mesons: recent results and open questions

> 12 pages. Talk presented at the Eta'05 Workshop on production and decay of eta and eta' mesons 15th--18th September 2005, Jagellonian University, Cracow, Poland

## Key claim

The nature of the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance, whether it is a compact quark state or a dynamically generated molecular state, remains unresolved, and a systematic study of $\eta$ production in various hadronic environments is needed to distinguish between these possibilities, but theory is currently far from being equally accurate as the available high-precision data.

## Method

The author reviews recent experimental data on $\eta$-meson production from hadrons, including $\pi N \to \eta N$, $\gamma N \to \eta N$, $NN \to NN\eta$, $pn \to d\eta$, $pd \to \eta {^3}$He, and $\gamma {^3}$He $\to \eta {^3}$He. For the few-nucleon systems, a final-state interaction enhancement factor in the scattering-length approximation (Watson-Migdal formula) is applied to extract the $\eta$-nucleus scattering length from production data. The analysis of the $\gamma {^3}$He $\to \pi^0 p X$ reaction below the $\eta {^3}$He threshold uses the same FSI formula with interference terms to distinguish between a bound-state and a virtual-state scenario.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta N$ scattering length extracted from a compilation of non-diagonal channels is $a_{\eta N} = (0.2-1.1, 0.26-0.35)$ fm, where the first range is the real part and the second the imaginary part.
- At $E_{tot} = 1540$ MeV, the inelastic cross section for $\pi N$ scattering in the $S_{11}$ partial wave derived from the inelasticity parameter is $\sigma_{in}^{\pi N} = 3.5$ mb, which is significantly larger than the measured peak value of the $\pi N \to \eta N$ cross section.
- The $\eta {^3}$He scattering length extracted from a fit to $pd \to \eta {^3}$He world data is $a = (4.3 \pm 0.3) + i(0.25 \pm 0.25)$ fm.
- For the $\gamma {^3}$He $\to \pi^0 p X$ data, a fit with $a = (0, 3.5)$ fm gave a $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom of 1, while fits with $a = (\pm 4, 1)$ fm gave $\chi^2$ worse than 3, favoring a virtual state interpretation over a bound state.

## Context

This review extends the line of work investigating the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance, whose nature is debated between quark-model interpretations (Isgur 1978; Glozman and Riska 1996) and dynamically generated state descriptions (Kaiser, Weise, and Oset 1995). The analysis of $\eta$ production in few-nucleon systems builds on earlier proposals by Weinberg (1965) for distinguishing molecular from compact quark states, and on the Haider and Liu (1986) criterion for $\eta$-nucleus bound states. The results enable future double-polarized measurements to isolate $NN$ $P$-wave contributions in $pp \to pp\eta$ and a new high-statistics experiment to determine whether the $\eta {^3}$He system forms a bound or virtual state.
