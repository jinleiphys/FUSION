# Factorization of event-plane correlations over transverse momentum in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a multi-phase transport model

> Momentum-space azimuthal harmonic event planes (EP) are constructed from final-state midrapidity particles binned in transverse momentum (pT ) in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions in a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. The EP correlations between pT bins, corrected by EP resolutions, are smaller than unity. This indicates that the EP's decorrelate over pT in AMPT, qualitatively consistent with data and hydrodynamic calculations. It is further found that the EP correlations approximately factorize into single pT-bin EP correlations to a common plane. This common plane appears to be the momentum-space EP integrated over all pT, not the configuration space participant plane (PP).

## Key claim

The paper shows that event-plane (EP) correlations between different transverse momentum bins in AMPT model simulations are smaller than unity, indicating EP decorrelation over pT, and that these correlations approximately factorize into products of single pT-bin correlations to a common plane; this common plane is the momentum-space EP integrated over all pT, not the configuration-space participant plane (PP).

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting (3 mb parton cross section) to simulate 20-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They constructed second- and third-order harmonic event planes from final-state charged particles within |eta| < 1.0 in ten equal-width pT bins from 0 to 2 GeV/c, and calculated the resolution-corrected EP correlations between different pT bins. They tested factorization by fitting all correlation combinations with a product of single-bin parameters and compared those parameters to correlations with the integrated EP and with the initial-state participant plane.

## Key numbers

* EP resolution for n=2 in the 0.2-0.4 GeV/c pT bin: 0.557 +/- 0.005
* EP resolution for n=2 in the 0.4-0.6 GeV/c pT bin: 0.719 +/- 0.003
* EP resolution for n=2 in the 1.8-2.0 GeV/c pT bin: 0.33 +/- 0.01
* EP resolution for n=3 in the 0.2-0.4 GeV/c pT bin: 0.17 +/- 0.02
* EP resolution for n=3 in the 0.4-0.6 GeV/c pT bin: 0.339 +/- 0.008
* EP resolution for n=3 in the 1.8-2.0 GeV/c pT bin: 0.23 +/- 0.01
* Resolution-corrected EP correlation C_2 between pT bins 0.4-0.6 and 0.6-0.8 GeV/c: 0.88 +/- 0.04
* Resolution-corrected EP correlation C_2 between pT bins 0.4-0.6 and 1.8-2.0 GeV/c: 0.86 +/- 0.02
* Resolution-corrected EP correlation C_3 between pT bins 0.4-0.6 and 0.6-0.8 GeV/c: 0.97 +/- 0.01
* Resolution-corrected EP correlation C_3 between pT bins 0.4-0.6 and 1.8-2.0 GeV/c: 0.86 +/- 0.02
* Fit chi-squared per degree of freedom for C_2 factorization: 30.5/35
* Fit chi-squared per degree of freedom for C_3 factorization: 45.5/35

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic calculations by Gardim et al. (2012) and viscous hydrodynamic studies that predicted pT-dependent harmonic plane decorrelation, as well as experimental observations by CMS at the LHC. The AMPT model results confirm that EP decorrelation over pT occurs in a transport model, not just in hydrodynamics, and the factorization property suggests that the global harmonic plane is the momentum-space EP rather than the participant plane, which has implications for how anisotropic flow measurements should be interpreted and for understanding the nature of flow fluctuations.
