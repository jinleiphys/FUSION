# Compositeness of hadron resonances in finite volume

> We develop a theoretical framework to quantify the structure of unstable hadron resonances. With the help of the corresponding system in a finite volume, we define the compositeness of resonance states which can be interpreted as a probability. This framework is used to study the structure of the scalar mesons f_0(980) and a_0(980). In both mesons, the Kbar K component dominates about a half of the wave function. The method is also applied to the Lambda(1405) resonance. We argue that a single energy level in finite volume represents the two eigenstates in infinite volume. The KbarK N component of Lambda(1405), including contributions from both eigenstates, is found to be 58%, and the rest is composed of the pi Sigma and other channels.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new definition of the compositeness (probability of molecular component) for unstable hadron resonances by using finite-volume eigenstates, which yields a real and probabilistic value unlike the complex compositeness obtained from infinite-volume resonance poles; the method is argued to be well-defined only for sufficiently narrow resonances where the structure is meaningful.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory Hamiltonian for s-wave scattering of a two-body system coupled to a discrete state, defined in a cubic box of size L with periodic boundary conditions. They derive closed-form expressions for the compositeness and elementariness of each finite-volume eigenstate, which satisfy a probability sum rule. To define the compositeness of a resonance, they identify the first excited finite-volume state whose eigenenergy lies within the half-maximum region of the infinite-volume scattering amplitude peak, average its compositeness over a range of box sizes L where this condition holds, and take that average as the resonance compositeness.

## Key numbers

* For the scalar mesons f0(980) and a0(980), the Kbar K component dominates about a half of the wave function (no explicit numerical value given for the fraction).
* For the Lambda(1405) resonance, the Kbar N component including contributions from both eigenstates is found to be 58%, with the rest composed of pi Sigma and other channels.
* Three single-channel models are constructed with resonance poles at: Model I: 2600 - 3i MeV (narrow width, Gamma ~ 1 MeV); Model II: 2600 - 29i MeV (medium width, Gamma ~ 10 MeV); Model III: 2600 - 165i MeV (broad width, Gamma ~ 100 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadron compositeness (Weinberg 1965; Hyodo 2013; Kamiya 2015, 2016) that showed the compositeness of unstable resonances is generally complex and lacks probabilistic interpretation. By using finite-volume techniques developed by Luscher (1985, 1986, 1990) and applied to lattice QCD by Hall (2013) and Molina (2015), the authors provide a framework that yields a real, probability-interpretable compositeness. The method enables quantitative characterization of hadron structure for resonances like f0(980), a0(980), and Lambda(1405), and can be compared with future lattice QCD calculations.
