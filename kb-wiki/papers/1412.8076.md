# The same key to different doors - temperature puzzles

> The notion of temperature in many body elementary particle processes is in a common use for decades. Thermal models have become simple and universal effective tools to describe particle production -- not only in high energy heavy ion collisions but also in high energy elementary particle collisions. We perform a critical analysis of the temperature concepts in such processes. Although the temperature concept is a very useful tool, nevertheless it should be used with the care, taking into account that usually it is just model dependent fitted parameter.

# The same key to different doors - temperature puzzles

## Key claim

Temperature in high-energy hadronic and elementary particle collisions is not a self-consistent physical quantity but rather a model-dependent fitted parameter; although thermal models yield similar temperatures when applied to similar data, there is no expectation of constructing a universal hadronic thermometer that would give a "real" temperature of the hadronic medium.

## Method

The paper presents a critical analysis of the temperature concept in many-body elementary particle processes, tracing its historical development from Koppe (1948) and Fermi (1950) through Hagedorn's statistical bootstrap model and limiting temperature. It derives the standard equations of state for an ideal hadron gas in thermal and chemical equilibrium, including expressions for energy density, baryon number density, strangeness density, electric charge density, and entropy density, and discusses how temperature emerges from maximization of relevant entropy under constraints on macro-variables.

## Key numbers

- The paper derives the standard ideal hadron gas equations where energy density epsilon is given by an integral over momentum p with the factor (2s_i+1) for spin degeneracy, and the denominator contains exp{(E_i - mu_i)/T} + g_i, where g_i is a statistical factor.
- Entropy density s is given by an integral over momentum p with the factor (2s_i+1) and the term (E_i - mu_i) exp{(E_i - mu_i)/T} divided by (exp{(E_i - mu_i)/T} + g_i)^2.
- The chemical potential for each species i is expressed as mu_i = B_i mu_B + S_i mu_S + Q_i mu_Q, where B_i, S_i, Q_i are baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, and mu_B, mu_S, mu_Q are overall chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the critical discussion of temperature in hadronic physics that began with Wigner's (1959) lecture on the uncanny usefulness of mathematical concepts, and builds on the statistical model framework of Fermi (1950) and the systematic statistical bootstrap approach of Hagedorn (1965, 1970). The paper enables a more careful interpretation of fitted temperature parameters in thermal models of heavy-ion collisions and elementary particle collisions, emphasizing that different choices of relevant macro-variables lead to different temperatures within the same statistical physics scheme.
