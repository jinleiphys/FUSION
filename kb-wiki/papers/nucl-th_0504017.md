# Evolution of spectral properties along the O(6)-U(5) transition in the interacting boson model. II. Classical trajectories

> This article continues our previous study of level dynamics in the [O(6)-U(5)]$\supset$O(5) transition of the interacting boson model [nucl-th/0504016] using the semiclassical theory of spectral fluctuations. We find classical monodromy, related to a singular bundle of orbits with infinite period at energy E=0, and bifurcations of numerous periodic orbits for E>0. The spectrum of allowed ratios of periods associated with beta- and gamma-vibrations exhibits an abrupt change around zero energy. These findings explain anomalous bunching of quantum states in the E$\approx$0 region, which is responsible for the redistribution of levels between O(6) and U(5) multiplets.

## Key claim

Classical monodromy, related to a singular bundle of orbits with infinite period at energy E=0, and bifurcations of numerous periodic orbits for E>0 explain the anomalous bunching of quantum states in the E≈0 region, which is responsible for the redistribution of levels between O(6) and U(5) multiplets in the interacting boson model.

## Method

The authors constructed the classical limit of the [O(6)-U(5)]⊃O(5) transitional Hamiltonian in the interacting boson model (IBM) using Glauber coherent states, reducing the system to two-dimensional integrable dynamics for zero angular momentum. They numerically generated about 50000 classical trajectories for η=0.6 in the energy range E∈[-0.1,+0.3] and classified them by the ratio R=Tγ/Tβ of periods associated with γ- and β-vibrations, identifying periodic orbits and their bifurcations through analysis of radial widths and frequency of occurrence.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state shape-phase transition occurs at ηc=4/5.
- The energy range accessible for the classical Hamiltonian is E∈[Emin,Emax]⊂[-1,+1], with Emax=η and Emin = -(5η-4)²/[16(1-η)] for η<4/5, and Emin=0 for η≥4/5.
- For E>0, the lower bound of allowed frequency ratios is constant at Rmin=2; at E=0, the upper bound passes through Rmax=4.
- The 3/1 periodic orbits show a sharp peak in occurrence at E=0.02.
- The central disc radius for E<0 converges to zero as βmin ∝ √(-E).

## Context

This work continues the authors' previous study of level dynamics in the [O(6)-U(5)]⊃O(5) transition (Part I, nucl-th/0504016) and extends semiclassical analyses of IBM by Hatch and Levit (1985) and Alhassid and Whelan (1990s). The findings connect the level bunching pattern in IBM spectra to classical monodromy and orbit bifurcations, analogous to shell effects in nuclei, quantum dots, and metallic clusters studied by Mottelson (1990s) and others. This enables a semiclassical understanding of the redistribution of levels between O(6) and U(5) multiplets and provides a framework for analyzing spectral fluctuations in integrable nuclear models.
