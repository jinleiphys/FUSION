# The nuclear symmetry energy, the inner crust, and global neutron star modeling

> The structure and composition of the inner crust of neutron stars, as well as global stellar properties such as radius and moment of inertia, have been shown to correlate with parameters characterizing the symmetry energy of nuclear matter such as its magnitude J and density dependence L at saturation density. It is thus mutually beneficial to nuclear physicists and astrophysicists to examine the combined effects of such correlations on potential neutron star observables in the light of recent experimental and theoretical constraints on J, L, and relationships between them. We review some basic correlations between these nuclear and astrophysical observables, and illustrate the impact of recent progress in constraining the J-L parameter space on the composition of the inner crust, crust-core transition density and pressure, and extent of the hypothesized pasta region. We use a simple compressible liquid drop model in conjunction with a simple model of nuclear matter which allows for independent, smooth, variation of the J and L. We extend the model into the core using the same nuclear matter model to explore the effects on global crust and core properties, and on potential observables such as crust oscillation frequencies and mechanically supported crust deformation. Throughout we illustrate the importance of the relationship between J and L implicit in a particular model of nuclear matter to the predictions of neutron star properties.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that correlations between the symmetry energy magnitude \(J\) and its slope \(L\) at saturation density, when constrained by recent experimental and theoretical bounds, significantly narrow the possible composition and thickness of the inner crust and mantle, but the exact predictions depend on which specific \(J\)-\(L\) correlation (e.g., fixed \(J\), fixed low-density PNM, or fixed \(S(0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3})\)) is adopted.

## Method
The authors use a compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) for the inner crust, combined with a modified Skyrme-like (MSL) nuclear matter model that allows independent, smooth variation of \(J\) and \(L\) while keeping symmetric nuclear matter properties fixed. They generate sequences of crust and core equations of state by varying \(L\) under different constraints on \(J\) (fixed \(J\), fixed low-density pure neutron matter EOS, or fixed symmetry energy at \(n=0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)), then compute crust-core transition densities and pressures, pasta-phase boundaries, and global neutron star properties such as radius and moment of inertia.

## Key numbers
- Crust-core transition density \(n_{\rm cc}\) correlates inversely with \(L\): \(n_{\rm cc} = a_{\rm n} - b_{\rm n} L + c_{\rm n} L^2\), with \(a_{\rm n}\sim0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(b_{\rm n}\sim10^{-3}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), \(c_{\rm n}\sim4\cdot10^{-6}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\).
- Inner crust-mantle (pasta) transition density \(n_{\rm p}\) is roughly constant with respect to symmetry energy parameters, typically \(n_{\rm p} \sim 0.04 - 0.06\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- For a \(1.4 M_{\odot}\) star, radius scales as \(R \approx 0.5 C(M, n_0) L^{0.25}\) with \(C(M,n_0) \approx 9.3\), giving \(R \sim L^{0.25}\).
- The correlation between \(J\) and \(L\) from fitting the MSL model to low-density PNM is \(J = 20.53 + 0.207L\) (in MeV).
- The correlation from fixing \(S(0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}) = 26\ \mathrm{MeV}\) gives \(J = 29.0 + 0.1L\) (in MeV).
- Experimental constraints give a conservative range \(25 < L < 115\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(25 < J < 35\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends correlations between symmetry energy parameters and neutron star properties established by Lattimer (2001), Steiner (2005), and Ducoin (2011), and uses recent experimental constraints from isospin diffusion (Tsang 2009), neutron skins (Centelles 2009), and theoretical PNM calculations from chiral EFT (Hebeler 2010) and quantum Monte Carlo (Gandolfi 2011). It enables more precise modeling of neutron star observables such as crust oscillation frequencies, glitch properties, and gravitational wave signatures by showing how the \(J\)-\(L\) correlation narrows the range of possible crust thickness and composition.
