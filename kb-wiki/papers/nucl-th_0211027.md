# Baryon Resonance Phenomenology

> The Japan Hadron Facility will provide an unprecedented opportunity for the study of baryon resonance properties. This talk will focus on the chiral nonanalytic behaviour of magnetic moments exclusive to baryons with open decay channels. To illustrate the novel features associated with an open decay channel, we consider the ``Access'' quark model, where an analytic continuation of chiral perturbation theory is employed to connect results obtained using the constituent quark model in the limit of SU(3)-flavour symmetry to empirical determinations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that incorporating the next-to-leading nonanalytic (NLNA) structure of chiral perturbation theory into the extrapolation function is vital for decuplet baryon magnetic moments, because the NLNA terms contain information about the branch point at \(m_{\pi} = M_\Delta - M_N\) associated with the \(\Delta \to N \pi\) decay channel; this effect is negligible for octet baryons but plays a significant role in decuplet-baryon magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors construct an extrapolation function for \(\Delta\)-baryon magnetic moments that preserves both the leading nonanalytic (LNA) and next-to-leading nonanalytic (NLNA) behavior of chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) while incorporating the Dirac-moment mass dependence in the heavy quark mass regime. They fit this function to two data points from the constituent quark model (CQM) near the SU(3)-flavour limit, using established input parameters (\(M_s = 565\) MeV and \(c\,m_s^{\rm phys} = 144\) MeV) from a previous octet baryon analysis, and connect constituent quark masses to pion masses via the relation \(M = M_\chi + c\,m_q\).

## Key numbers

* \(\Delta^{++}\) magnetic moment prediction: \(+5.39\ \mu_N\) (AccessQM), compared to experimental results of \(4.52 \pm 0.50 \pm 0.45\ \mu_N\) and \(6.14 \pm 0.51\ \mu_N\).
* \(\Delta^{+}\) magnetic moment prediction: \(+2.58\ \mu_N\) (AccessQM), compared to the proton magnetic moment of \(2.77\ \mu_N\) from a previous extrapolation.
* \(\Delta^{-}\) magnetic moment prediction: \(-2.99\ \mu_N\) (AccessQM).
* Octet-decuplet mass splitting used: \(\delta_N = M_{10} - M_{8} = 1377 - 1151 = +226\) MeV.
* Coupling constants used: \(\mathcal{H} = -2.2\) (decuplet-decuplet), \(\mathcal{C} = -1.2\) (decuplet-octet transition).
* Pion decay constant: \(f_{\pi} = 93\) MeV; kaon decay constant: \(f_K = 112\) MeV.
* Strange-to-light current quark mass ratio: \(\chi_{sq} = m_s^{\rm phys} / m_q^{\rm phys} = 24.4 \pm 1.5\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic continuation method for octet baryon magnetic moments (Cloet, Leinweber, Thomas, 2002; Leinweber, Lu, Thomas, 1999; Hackett-Jones, Leinweber, Thomas, 2000) by incorporating NLNA terms, which are vital for decuplet baryons but contribute little to octet baryons. The results contradict earlier lattice QCD predictions based on linear extrapolations (Leinweber, Draper, Woloshyn, 1995) that reported the \(\Delta^{+}\) moment greater than the proton moment; the present work predicts the opposite. The techniques enable future application to decuplet hyperon resonances where the kaon cloud becomes important, and the authors look forward to new results from the Japan Hadron Facility.
