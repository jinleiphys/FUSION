# PDFSense: Mapping the PDF sensitivity of future facilities (HL-LHC, LHeC, and EIC)

> Particle and nuclear physics are moving toward a new generation of experiments to stress-test the Standard Model (SM), search for novel degrees of freedom, and comprehensively map the internal structure of hadrons. Due to the complex nature of QCD and wide array of past, present, and possible future experiments, measurements taken at these next-generation facilities will inhabit an expansive space of high-energy data. Maximizing the impact of each future collider program will depend on identifying its place within this sprawling landscape. As an initial exploration, we use the recently-developed PDFSense framework to assess the PDF sensitivity of two future high-energy facilities --- the high-luminosity upgrade to the LHC (HL-LHC) and the Large Hadron-electron Collider (LHeC) proposal --- as well as the electron-ion collider (EIC) proposed to map the few-GeV quark-hadron transition region. We report that each of these experimental facilities occupies a unique place in the kinematical parameter space with specialized pulls on particular collinear quantities. As such, there is a clear complementarity among these programs, with an opportunity for each to mutually reinforce and inform the others.

# PDFSense: Mapping the PDF sensitivity of future facilities (HL-LHC, LHeC, and EIC)

## Key claim
The HL-LHC, LHeC, and EIC each occupy unique and complementary regions of kinematic parameter space with specialized pulls on particular collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs), such that there is a clear opportunity for each program to mutually reinforce and inform the others. This complementarity is demonstrated using the PDFSense framework applied to pseudodata, but the authors note that the LHeC projections assume only 100 fb⁻¹ of integrated luminosity, which may be a lower bound if LHeC errors are statistics-limited.

## Method
The authors used the PDFSense framework, developed in Wang (2018), to compute the kinematic sensitivity of pseudodata representative of each future facility to unpolarized proton PDFs. For the HL-LHC, they used NNLO theory predictions for pseudodata at 3 ab⁻¹ and √s = 14 TeV; for the LHeC, they implemented 100 fb⁻¹ of unpolarized electron/positron scattering pseudodata at √s = 1.3 TeV; and for the EIC, they used pseudodata for a generic √s = 100 GeV DIS collider. Sensitivity maps were computed using PDF4LHC15 NNLO PDFs and CT14HERA2 NNLO PDFs as baselines.

## Key numbers
- HL-LHC will operate at √s = 14 TeV with integrated luminosity up to 3 ab⁻¹, probing parton momentum fractions 5×10⁻⁵ < x < 1 at large factorization scales μ.
- LHeC would scatter 60 GeV e± off LHC protons, reaching √s = 1.3 TeV and accessing x ≳ 5×10⁻⁶.
- Aggregated sensitivity to the gluon PDF: S_g^HL-LHC = 245 versus S_g^LHeC = 151, indicating HL-LHC advantage for gluon constraints.
- Aggregated sensitivity to the d-quark PDF: S_d^LHeC = 214 versus S_d^HL-LHC = 171, indicating LHeC advantage for d-quark constraints.
- The total Higgs production cross section at 14 TeV is σ_H(14 TeV) = 62.1 pb, of which 88 ± 4% comes from gluon fusion.
- EIC pseudodata sensitivity to the d-quark PDF generally surpasses that of fixed-target experiments, with an upper scale choice of |S_f| = 1.2 for highlighted points in the sensitivity maps.

## Context
This work extends the PDFSense framework of Wang (2018) to compare the PDF sensitivity of three proposed future facilities, building on earlier studies of HL-LHC and LHeC physics potential from Apollinari (2017), Abelleira Fernandez (2012), and Khalek (2018). The findings of complementarity between HL-LHC and LHeC are broadly consistent with results reported by Abdul Khalek (2019). The analysis also connects to lattice QCD calculations of integrated PDF moments and quasi-PDFs, as discussed in Hobbs (2019), suggesting a future synergy between EIC phenomenology and lattice studies. This work enables quantitative comparisons of the unique contributions each facility would make to global QCD analyses and to precision Standard Model measurements at the energy frontier.
