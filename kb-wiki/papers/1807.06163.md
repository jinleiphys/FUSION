# Charge and spin asymmetries in elastic lepton-nucleon scattering

> Elastic lepton scattering off of a nucleon has proved to be an efficient tool to study the structure of the hadron. Modern cross section and asymmetry measurements at Jefferson Lab require effects beyond the leading order Born approximation to be taken into account. Availability of unpolarized beams of both electrons and positrons in respective experiments would enable to reduce systematic uncertainties due to higher-order charge-odd contributions. In addition, information on an unpolarized electron-to-positron cross section ratio could serve as a test for theoretical models that provide predictions for charge-dependent radiative corrections to elastic lepton-nucleon scattering. Availability of polarized beams of leptons would allow for even more comprehensive study of higher-order effects as some of them are dominant in polarized lepton-nucleon scattering asymmetries. We present a brief overview of effects due to the lepton's charge and target's polarization on elastic lepton-nucleon scattering measurements.

## Key claim

Modern precision measurements at Jefferson Lab require radiative corrections beyond the leading-order Born approximation, and the availability of both electron and positron beams would reduce systematic uncertainties from charge-odd contributions; however, the paper presents a brief overview rather than a complete calculation, and notes that no complete calculation of two-photon exchange valid at all kinematics currently exists.

## Method

The authors updated the Monte Carlo generator Elradgen 2.0 to include lepton mass effects in elastic l±p scattering, calculating model-independent charge-odd contributions to the charge asymmetry δch using the Tsai approach. They also estimated helicity-flip meson exchange contributions for Q2 ≲ 0.5 GeV2 using the vector meson dominance model, performing the calculation partly analytically and partly numerically with LoopTools software. For transverse single-spin asymmetries, they separated the hadronic tensor into elastic and inelastic pieces and compared their elastic contribution estimates to recent Jefferson Lab neutron-normal SSA measurements.

## Key numbers

- The helicity-flip σ meson exchange contribution to the charge-dependent correction was computed to be at most ∼ 0.1% for muons, about three orders of magnitude larger than for electrons.
- Neutron-normal single-spin asymmetries measured at Jefferson Lab: Ayn = -3.32 ± 0.4 ± 0.72% at ε1 = 1.245 GeV and Q2 = 0.127 GeV2.
- Neutron-normal single-spin asymmetries: Ayn = -1.78 ± 0.2 ± 0.66% at ε1 = 2.425 GeV and Q2 = 0.460 GeV2.
- Neutron-normal single-spin asymmetries: Ayn = -1.38 ± 0.14 ± 0.24% at ε1 = 3.605 GeV and Q2 = 0.967 GeV2.
- The beam momentum for the MUSE kinematics shown in the soft-photon asymmetry calculation is |k1| = 115 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for radiative corrections in elastic lepton-nucleon scattering beyond the ultra-relativistic approximation used in the standard prescriptions of Tsai (1961) and Maximon and Tjon (2000). The updated Monte Carlo generator is essential for the future MUSE experiment (Gilman 2013), which will simultaneously measure elastic e±p and μ±p scattering. The paper also addresses the long-standing discrepancy in the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio (Blunden 2003, Chen 2004, Afanasev 2005) by enabling better extraction of two-photon exchange corrections, and provides context for the first non-zero target-normal SSA measurement on a polarized 3He target (Zhang 2015), for which no theoretical model currently exists at GeV beam energies and nearly forward scattering angles.
