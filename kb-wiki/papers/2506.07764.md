# Level order of quark systems: The puzzle of the Roper resonance, and related questions

> The problem of ordering of radial vs.\ orbital excitations is reviewed. It is shown that the current quark models cannot explain the location of the Roper resonance which is slightly lower than the lowest negative-parity excitations. We also study some related spectral problems, such as the dependence of the energies on the quark masses, and the possibility of bound states in simple chromelectric models.

# Level order of quark systems: The puzzle of the Roper resonance, and related questions

## Key claim

Current quark models cannot explain the location of the Roper resonance, which sits slightly lower than the lowest negative-parity excitations; the paper provides an almost rigorous proof that in any local, symmetric potential growing faster than a Coulombic interaction, the first radial excitation must lie *above* the orbital one, contradicting the observed ordering.

## Method

The paper reviews the harmonic oscillator (HO) model of baryons developed by Dalitz and extended by Isgur and Karl, then analyzes the three-quark Hamiltonian using the hyperspherical formalism. By expanding the wavefunction and potential in multipoles on the hypersphere, the author derives separate hyperradial equations for the ground state, the first radial excitation (Roper), and the first orbital excitations, all sharing the same hyperradial potential. Theorems on level ordering derived for mesons are then applied to these equations with effective angular momenta ℓ=3/2 and ℓ=5/2, establishing the ordering condition. The paper also examines spectral problems including dependence on quark masses, number of constituents, and spread of couplings in multiquark systems.

## Key numbers

- Roper resonance mass: 1440 MeV, with a width of about 180 MeV
- Lowest negative-parity excitations: 1520 MeV (1/2⁻) and 1535 MeV (3/2⁻)
- In the strange sector: φ(s\bar s) < Ω(sss), with φ ~ 1.02 GeV and Ω ~ 1.67 GeV
- The inequality 3 φ(s\bar s) < 2 Ω(sss) is well satisfied with these values
- For the tetraquark with equal masses: 2 (q\bar q) ≤ (qq\bar q\bar q), showing no binding in the chromoelectric model
- For unequal masses: (QQ\bar q\bar q) ≤ 2 (Q\bar q) can be inverted

## Context

This work reviews and extends the long-standing puzzle of the Roper resonance first identified by Roper (1964), which has been studied extensively in quark models by Isgur and Karl (1978), Gromes (1979), and Bowler and Tynemouth (1983). The paper builds on rigorous ordering theorems for mesons by Grosse and Martin (1984) and extends them to baryons following Hogaasen and Richard (1982). The analysis of baryon mass dependence on quark masses extends work by Nussinov (1983) and Lieb (1985), while the multiquark stability analysis connects to the discovery of the T_cc⁺ state at LHCb (2021). The paper enables future work incorporating chiral dynamics and coupling to decay channels, as lattice calculations by Mathur et al. (2003) show the Roper degeneracy appears only at very small pion mass.
