# Skyrme Strings

> We construct nontopological string solutions with U(1) Noether charge in the Skyrme model with a pion mass term, and examine their stability by taking linear perturbations. The solution exhibits a critical angular velocity beyond which the configuration energetically prefers to decay by emitting pions. This critical point is observed as a cusp in the relation between energy and charge. We find that the maximum length for the string to be stable is comparable to the size of one skyrmion. Beyond the length, it is unstable to decay. This instability raises the possiblity of dynamical realization of Skyrme strings from monopole strings inside a domain wall.

## Key claim

Nontopological string solutions with U(1) Noether charge in the Skyrme model with a pion mass term are stable only when their length is comparable to the size of one skyrmion; beyond that length they are unstable to decay, and there exists a critical angular velocity beyond which the configuration energetically prefers to decay by emitting pions.

## Method

The authors construct string solutions by imposing a cylindrical ansatz with steady U(1) rotation in internal space on the Skyrme Lagrangian with a pion mass term. They solve the resulting field equation numerically subject to boundary conditions f(0)=π and f(∞)=0, then examine linear stability by introducing infinitesimal fluctuations in the internal space and analyzing the Sturm-Liouville equation for the perturbation mode with m=n=0.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular velocity for stability is ω̂₊ = m̂π, where m̂π = mπ/(eFπ); for ω̂ > ω̂₊ the solution becomes oscillatory in the radial direction and the string tension diverges.
- The pion mass parameter used in numerical solutions is m̂π² = 0.1, corresponding to mπ = 137 MeV, e = 5.45, and Fπ = 93 MeV.
- The mass of one skyrmion is Ms/(eFπ) = 1.97, which is O(1) in the same units as the maximum stable string length L ∼ O(1).
- The approximate asymptotic formula for string tension is εω ∼ const + (m̂π/2)Q̂, holding within a few percent error in numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the nontopological string solutions previously obtained in the Skyrme model by Jackson (1988) to include steady U(1) rotation in internal space, analogous to Q-ball constructions. The results contradict the possibility of long-lived Skyrme strings, as the maximum stable length is comparable to a single skyrmion size. The instability raises the possibility of dynamical realization of Skyrme strings from monopole strings inside a domain wall, following the construction of Eto et al. (2005) where skyrmions are obtained from instanton holonomy.
