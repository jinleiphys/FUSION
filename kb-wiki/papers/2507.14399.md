# Sources of Radial Flow Fluctuations in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The differential radial flow fluctuation $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ has emerged as a new probe of the quark-gluon plasma. However, its characteristic rise-and-fall pattern with $p_{\mathrm{T}}$, resembling anisotropic flow, remains unexplained. I introduce a momentum rescaling framework that factorizes $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ into kinematic and dynamical components: $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})/v_0 = -[d\ln\langle n(p_{\mathrm{T}})\rangle/d\ln p_{\mathrm{T}} + 1] \times g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$. The first factor, determined by spectral shape, generates the rise-and-fall pattern as the spectra transition from exponential to power-law behavior. The dynamical component $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ isolates $p_{\mathrm{T}}$-dependent dynamics: $<1$ signals suppressed fluctuations, $>1$ indicates enhancement. Analysis of LHC data reveals $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ deviates from unity by 20-40% in central collisions. Predictions for RHIC show that spectral shape alone generates the rise-and-fall baseline pattern with substantial energy dependence. This framework enables tighter medium property constraints by separating kinematic from dynamical effects, with broad applications to anisotropic flow and higher-order radial flow fluctuations.

## Key claim
The characteristic rise-and-fall pattern of differential radial flow fluctuation $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ is primarily generated by the spectral shape of particle production, not by dynamical medium effects; the dynamical component $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ extracted from ATLAS data deviates from unity by 20–40% in central collisions, indicating that genuine physics beyond global momentum rescaling is present but subdominant.

## Method
The author introduces a momentum rescaling framework where event-by-event spectral fluctuations are modeled as a $p_{\mathrm{T}}$-dependent rescaling of the momentum axis: $\Delta_{p_{\mathrm{T}}}(p_{\mathrm{T}})/p_{\mathrm{T}} = g(p_{\mathrm{T}}) \delta[p_{\mathrm{T}}]/\langle[p_{\mathrm{T}}]\rangle$. This factorizes $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})/v_0$ into a kinematic component determined by the spectral slope $-d\ln\langle n(p_{\mathrm{T}})\rangle/d\ln p_{\mathrm{T}} - 1$ and a dynamical component $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$. The framework is applied to published charged hadron spectra from ALICE and ATLAS at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV, and the dynamical component $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ is extracted from ATLAS $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ data after acceptance and offset corrections.

## Key numbers
- $g(p_{\mathrm{T}})$ deviates from unity by 20–40% in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV
- The acceptance factor $C_A$ for ATLAS reference range $A=[0.5,2]$ GeV varies from 0.228 (peripheral) to 0.269 (central) at LHC
- For an exponential spectrum, $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})/v_0 = 2(p_{\mathrm{T}}/\langle[p_{\mathrm{T}}]\rangle - 1)$
- For a power-law spectrum at high $p_{\mathrm{T}}$, $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})/v_0$ asymptotes to $m-1$, where $m$ is the spectral index (approximately 5 at LHC, 7 at RHIC)
- The reference range $0.5 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2$ GeV captures approximately 40% of total momentum fluctuations at LHC but 55% at RHIC
- For $v_0=0.1$ (peripheral/small systems), deviations from the linear approximation reach 40% at high $p_{\mathrm{T}}$

## Context
This work extends the study of radial flow fluctuations initiated by Mazeliauskas and Teaney (2015) and Schenke et al. (2020), who introduced the differential observable $v_0(p_{\mathrm{T}})$. It directly addresses the unexplained rise-and-fall pattern observed in ATLAS (2025) and ALICE (2025) measurements. The framework provides a baseline for interpreting deviations from global momentum rescaling, which have been tentatively attributed to bulk viscosity effects or subleading radial flow modes by Parida et al. (2024). The predictions for RHIC energy establish that spectral shape differences alone generate substantial energy-dependent variations, enabling future beam-energy-scan measurements to isolate genuine dynamical effects.
