# An equation-of-state-meter of QCD transition from deep learning

> Supervised learning with a deep convolutional neural network is used to identify the QCD equation of state (EoS) employed in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions from the simulated final-state particle spectra $ρ(p_T,Φ)$. High-level correlations of $ρ(p_T,Φ)$ learned by the neural network act as an effective "EoS-meter" in detecting the nature of the QCD transition. The EoS-meter is model independent and insensitive to other simulation inputs, especially the initial conditions. Thus it provides a powerful direct-connection of heavy-ion collision observables with the bulk properties of QCD.

## Key claim

Supervised learning with a deep convolutional neural network can identify whether a crossover or first-order QCD equation of state was used in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, based solely on the final-state particle spectra ρ(p_T, Φ) from single events. The method achieves this identification with high accuracy while being largely insensitive to variations in initial conditions, shear viscosity, equilibration time, and freeze-out temperature, though the abstract's claim of "model independence" is qualified by the fact that the training and testing data are both generated from hydrodynamic simulations, not from actual experimental data.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 22,000 event-by-event ρ(p_T, Φ) distributions of charged pions at mid-rapidity using the CLVisc relativistic hydrodynamic code with fluctuating AMPT initial conditions, employing two different equations of state: a lattice-QCD parametrization (EOSL) for crossover and a Maxwell construction (EOSQ) for first-order phase transition. A deep convolutional neural network with two convolutional layers (16 filters of size 8×8, then 32 filters of size 7×7×16), batch normalization, dropout, and PReLU activation, followed by a fully connected layer with 128 neurons and a softmax output layer, was trained on these labeled spectra. The trained network was then tested on independently generated spectra from two different hydrodynamic packages (iEBE-VISHNU with MC-Glauber initial conditions and CLVisc with IP-Glasma-like initial conditions) with varied shear viscosity, freeze-out temperatures, and collision energies.

## Key numbers

* Testing accuracy on CLVisc-generated data with AMPT initial conditions (same generator as training): 99.88 ± 0.04%
* Testing accuracy on iEBE-VISHNU data with MC-Glauber initial conditions (different hydrodynamic solver and initial conditions): 93.46 ± 1.35%
* Testing accuracy on CLVisc data with IP-Glasma-like initial conditions: 95.12 ± 3.08%
* Training dataset contained 7435 Au-Au events at √s_NN = 200 GeV and 4967 Pb-Pb events at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for EOSL, plus 5328 Au-Au and 2828 Pb-Pb events for EOSQ, all with η/s = 0
* Additional training data: 500 events each for EOSL and EOSQ at both collision energies with η/s = 0.08
* Testing dataset GROUP 1: 7343 events from iEBE-VISHNU with MC-Glauber initial conditions, with η/s ranging from 0 to 0.16 and freeze-out temperature varied between 115 and 142 MeV
* Testing dataset GROUP 2: 8917 events from CLVisc with IP-Glasma-like initial conditions, with η/s = 0 and freeze-out temperature 137 MeV
* Mean prediction accuracy decreased from 97.1% (η/s = 0.0) to 96.6% (η/s = 0.08) and 87% (η/s = 0.16) in 10-fold cross validation for testing GROUP 1

## Context

This work extends the application of deep learning to heavy-ion collision phenomenology, following earlier applications in particle physics by Baldi et al. (2014) and Barnard et al. (2016), and in nuclear physics by Utama et al. (2016). It directly addresses the long-standing challenge of extracting the QCD equation of state from experimental data, which previously relied on comparisons of event-averaged observables like anisotropic flow (Luzum, 2008) or Bayesian global fitting (Pratt, 2015; Bernhard, 2016). The method demonstrates that high-level correlations in raw particle spectra contain discriminative information about the QCD transition that is robust against uncertainties in initial conditions and other model parameters, potentially enabling direct experimental determination of the QCD equation of state and the search for the critical endpoint.
