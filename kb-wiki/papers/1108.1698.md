# Electromagnetic Polarizabilities: Lattice QCD in Background Fields

> Chiral perturbation theory makes definitive predictions for the extrinsic behavior of hadrons in external electric and magnetic fields. Near the chiral limit, the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of pions, kaons, and nucleons are determined in terms of a few well-known parameters. In this limit, hadrons become quantum mechanically diffuse as polarizabilities scale with the inverse square-root of the quark mass. In some cases, however, such predictions from chiral perturbation theory have not compared well with experimental data. Ultimately we must turn to first principles numerical simulations of QCD to determine properties of hadrons, and confront the predictions of chiral perturbation theory. To address the electromagnetic polarizabilities, we utilize the background field technique. Restricting our attention to calculations in background electric fields, we demonstrate new techniques to determine electric polarizabilities and baryon magnetic moments for both charged and neutral states. As we can study the quark mass dependence of observables with lattice QCD, the lattice will provide a crucial test of our understanding of low-energy QCD, which will be timely in light of ongoing experiments, such as at COMPASS and HIγS.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD with background fields can determine electric polarizabilities and baryon magnetic moments for both charged and neutral hadrons, but the method requires careful treatment of charged particles and spin-1/2 hadrons where magnetic moment interactions complicate the extraction of polarizabilities.

## Method

The authors couple quarks to external electromagnetic fields by multiplying color gauge links with classical U(1) links, then compute two-point correlation functions of hadron interpolating fields in the presence of a uniform electric field. For neutral spinless hadrons, the ground-state energy shift at second order in the field directly gives the electric polarizability. For charged hadrons, the correlation function is matched to the predicted form from Schwinger's proper-time trick for a charged particle in a harmonic oscillator potential. For spin-1/2 hadrons, boost projection is used to separate the magnetic moment contribution from the electric polarizability signal by tracing correlation functions with boost generator matrices.

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability scales as \(\alpha_E(h) = N(h) \, \alpha_{fs} \left( \frac{4}{3} \pi \, [\text{fm}^3] \right)\), where \(\alpha_{fs}\) is the fine-structure constant and \(N(h)\) is a hadron-dependent pure number
- In chiral perturbation theory, polarizabilities scale as \(\alpha_E^\chi(h) = N^\chi(h) \, \alpha_{fs} / (f_\pi [m_q \langle \overline{\psi} \psi \rangle]^{1/2})\), where \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant, \(\langle \overline{\psi} \psi \rangle\) is the chiral condensate, and \(m_q\) is the quark mass
- The pion polarizability prediction from chiral perturbation theory differs from the experimental determination by a factor of two, citing Ahrens (2004)
- Finite volume effects from virtual charged pions wrapping around the lattice scale as \(e^{-n m_\pi L} \cos(n \Phi)\), where \(\Phi = -\mathcal{E} L x_4\) is the holonomy of the gauge field

## Context

This work extends the background field method for lattice QCD, previously applied to neutral hadrons by Bernard (1982) and Martinelli (1982), to charged hadrons and spin-1/2 baryons. The authors' techniques for charged particles build on their earlier work Detmold (2006) using single-particle effective actions, and the boost-projection method for nucleons was developed in Detmold (2010). The approach enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of polarizabilities to test chiral perturbation theory predictions, which have shown discrepancies with experiment for the pion polarizability (Ahrens, 2004), and will be timely for ongoing experiments at COMPASS and HIgammaS.
