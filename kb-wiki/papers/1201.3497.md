# Recent development of CDCC

> This paper shows a brief review on CDCC and the microscopic reaction theory as a fundamental theory of CDCC. The Kerman-McManus-Thaler theory for nucleon-nucleus scattering is extended to nucleus-nucleus scattering. New development of four-body CDCC is presented. An accurate method of treating inclusive reactions is presented as an extension of CDCC and the Glauber model.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method and its extensions, particularly the microscopic reaction theory based on the Kerman-McManus-Thaler formalism and the eikonal reaction theory, provide accurate frameworks for analyzing reactions involving unstable nuclei, but the applicability of the Glauber model is limited by the adiabatic approximation causing divergence when Coulomb interaction is included, and the double-folding model is reliable only when projectile breakup is weak.

## Method

The authors extend the Kerman-McManus-Thaler multiple scattering theory from nucleon-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus scattering, replacing the bare nucleon-nucleon potential with the Brueckner g-matrix (Melbourne interaction) which has milder r-dependence. They apply the double-folding model with projectile densities from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and deformed Woods-Saxon (DWS) models to calculate reaction cross sections for Ne isotopes scattering on 12C and 28Si targets. For four-body CDCC, they discretize three-body continuum states of the projectile using Gaussian basis functions and treat the continuum with the complex-scaling method. The eikonal reaction theory combines CDCC with the Glauber model by separating the S-matrix into neutron and core parts.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections for Ne isotopes on 12C at 240 MeV/nucleon: AMD calculations reproduce experimental data for A=28-32 except 31Ne, where the AMD result underestimates the data; the tail correction from AMD+RGM is 35 mb (2.5% of σ_R)
- The deformation effect increases σ_R by at most 5% compared to spherical Gogny-HF calculations
- Breakup correction for 31Ne is 10 mb (0.7% of σ_R)
- Dynamical deformation effect on σ_R for 30Ne+12C at 240 MeV/nucleon is less than 0.1% (1469 mb single-channel vs 1468 mb coupled-channel)
- Reorientation effect for 31Ne(3/2−)+12C at 240 MeV/nucleon is 0.2% (1512 mb with non-spherical potential vs 1515 mb without)
- Integrated inelastic cross section to first 2+ state of 30Ne is 2.9 mb (0.2% of σ_R)
- For 31Ne+208Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon, the error from replacing the neutron-nucleus interaction operator is 0.2% for σ_R, 1.9% for breakup cross section σ_bu, and 4.1% for stripping cross section σ_str

## Context

This review extends the Kerman-McManus-Thaler theory (KMT, 1959) for nucleon-nucleus scattering to nucleus-nucleus scattering, building on the multiple scattering theory of Watson (1953). It addresses the limitation of the Glauber model (Glauber, 1959) that the adiabatic approximation makes removal cross sections diverge when Coulomb interaction is included, as noted by Hussein (1990s) and Hencken (1990s). The work enables analysis of halo nuclei in the "Island-of-inversion" region, particularly 31Ne, which Takechi (2012) found to have large interaction cross sections and Nakamura (2009) identified as the heaviest experimentally suggested halo nucleus. The four-body CDCC with complex-scaling method provides a tool for studying two-neutron halo nuclei like 6He, where previous four-body DWBA calculations (4BDW) showed similar overestimations for 12C targets and underestimations for 208Pb targets due to missing inelastic breakup effects.
