# Strangeness and Pion Production as Signals of QCD Phase Transition

> A systematic analysis of data on strangeness and pion production in nucleon-nucleon and central nucleus-nucleus collisions is presented. It is shown that at all collision energies the pion/baryon and strangeness/pion ratios indicate saturation with the size of the colliding nuclei. The energy dependence of the saturation level suggests that the transition to the Quark Gluon Plasma occurs between 15 AGeV/c (BNL AGS) and 160 AGeV/c (CERN SPS) collision energies. The experimental results interpreted in the framework of a statistical approach show that the effective number of degrees of freedom increases in the course of the phase transition and that the plasma created at CERN SPS energies may have a temperature of about 280 MeV (energy density 10 GeV/fm-3). The presence of the phase transition can lead to the non-monotonic collision energy dependence of the strangeness/pion ratio. After an initial increase the ratio should drop to the characteristic value for the QGP. Above the transition region the ratio is expected to be collision energy independent. Experimental studies of central Pb+Pb collisions in the energy range 20--160 AGeV/c are urgently needed in order to localize the threshold energy, and study the properties of the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The transition to Quark Gluon Plasma occurs between 15 AGeV/c (BNL AGS) and 160 AGeV/c (CERN SPS) collision energies, based on saturation of pion/baryon and strangeness/pion ratios with nuclear size; the presence of the phase transition can lead to a non-monotonic collision energy dependence of the strangeness/pion ratio, with an initial increase followed by a drop to the characteristic value for QGP.

## Method

The author analyzed experimental data on pion and strangeness production from about 100 different experiments for nucleon-nucleon and central nucleus-nucleus collisions. The normalized pion multiplicity (pions per participant nucleon) and the strangeness/pion ratio (defined as the sum of Lambda hyperons and kaons plus antikaons divided by pion multiplicity) were studied as functions of the number of participant nucleons at fixed collision energies, and then as functions of the Fermi energy variable across collision energies from Dubna (4.5 AGeV/c) through AGS (15 AGeV/c) to SPS (160 AGeV/c). The results were interpreted within a generalized Landau model assuming formation of an equilibrated QGP in the early stage volume.

## Key numbers

- The transition to QGP occurs between 15 AGeV/c (BNL AGS) and 160 AGeV/c (CERN SPS) collision energies.
- The plasma created at CERN SPS energies may have a temperature of about 280 MeV and an energy density of about 10 GeV/fm^3.
- The effective number of degrees of freedom increases by a factor of about 3 (1.33^4) when crossing the transition collision energy, estimated as an upper limit.
- The inelastic energy at SPS was measured to be (67 +/- 7)% of the available energy, the same for S+S and Pb+Pb collisions.
- The strange quark mass was taken to be 175 +/- 25 MeV, and the average mass of light quarks was taken to be 7.2 MeV.
- The conversion factor between calculated entropy and experimental entropy was taken to be 4 (entropy per pion at T about 150 MeV).
- The conversion factor between total strangeness and strangeness measured by Lambda and K+antikaon yields was taken to be 1.36.
- The effective nuclear radius parameter r0 was taken as 1.3 fm for the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach of Fermi (1950) and Landau (1953) to heavy-ion collisions, using the generalized Landau model developed by Gazdzicki and collaborators. The analysis of strangeness and entropy production as signals of QGP formation builds on earlier suggestions by Koch (1986) and Van Hove (1982). The results indicate that the threshold energy for QGP creation lies between AGS and SPS energies, and the paper calls for experimental studies of central Pb+Pb collisions in the energy range 20-160 AGeV to localize the threshold energy more precisely and study the properties of the QCD phase transition.
