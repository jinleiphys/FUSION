# Generalized form factors and spin structures of the kaon

> We investigate the spin structure of the kaon, based on the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum. We first revisit the electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon, improving the results for the kaon. We evaluate the generalized tensor form factors of the kaon in order to determine the probability density of transversely polarized quarks inside the kaon. We consider the effects of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking, so that the probability density of the up and strange quarks are examined in detail. It is found that the strange quark behaves differently inside the kaon in comparison with the up quark.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the spin structure of the kaon, specifically the probability density of transversely polarized quarks inside it, can be described within the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum, but only if the constituent-quark mass at zero virtuality is treated as a free parameter (model II) rather than strictly following the model's self-consistent equation (model I), because the original model underestimates the kaon electromagnetic form factor and decay constant.

## Method
The authors computed the generalized vector and tensor form factors (A_{10}, B_{10}, B_{20}) for the positively charged kaon (K^+) using the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum, with explicit flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking (current-quark masses m_u = m_d = 5 MeV, m_s = 180 MeV). They used two parameter sets: model I with the self-consistently determined constituent-quark mass M_0 ≈ 350 MeV, and model II with M_0 fitted to 407 MeV to reproduce the experimental kaon charge radius. The form factors were parametrized with monopole and p-pole forms, Fourier-transformed to impact-parameter space, and combined to obtain the probability density of transversely polarized quarks.

## Key numbers
- Kaon charge radius: model I gives (0.639 fm)^2, model II gives (0.560 fm)^2, compared to experimental (0.560 ± 0.031 fm)^2.
- Kaon decay constant: model I gives F_K = 100.17 MeV, model II gives F_K = 118.64 MeV, compared to empirical 113 MeV.
- Pion charge radius: model I gives (0.673 fm)^2, model II gives (0.672 fm)^2, compared to experimental (0.672 ± 0.008 fm)^2.
- Pole masses for model II: (M_{A^{K,u}_{10}}, M_{B^{K,u}_{10}}, M_{B^{K,u}_{20}}) = (0.894, 0.898, 0.918) GeV; (M_{A^{K,s}_{10}}, M_{B^{K,s}_{10}}, M_{B^{K,s}_{20}}) = (1.081, 1.272, 1.520) GeV.
- Average shift ⟨b_y^{K,q}⟩ for model II: 0.133 fm for up quark, 0.100 fm for strange quark.
- Tensor charges at μ = 2 GeV (model II): B^{K,u}_{10}(0) = 0.611, B^{K,s}_{10}(0) = -0.421; B^{K,u}_{20}(0) = 0.090, B^{K,s}_{20}(0) = -0.076.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pion tensor form factors and spin structure by the same authors (Nam 2010) and by Broniowski et al. (2010) within chiral quark models, motivated by lattice QCD results from the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaborations (Brommel 2007) on pion transversity. It also improves upon the authors' earlier kaon electromagnetic form factor calculation (Nam 2007) by introducing a phenomenological fit (model II) to compensate for the absence of meson-loop corrections. The results provide predictions for the kaon's spin structure that can be compared with future lattice QCD simulations at a scale of μ = 2 GeV.
