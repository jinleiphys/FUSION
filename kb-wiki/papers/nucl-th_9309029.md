# NN Correlations and Relativistic Hartree Fock in Finite Nuclei

> Two different approximation schemes for the self-consistent solution of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation for finite nuclei are discussed using realistic One-Boson-Exchange potentials. In a first scheme, the effects of correlations are deduced from a study of nuclear matter and parameterized in terms of an effective $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ exchange. Employing this effective interaction relativistic Hartree-Fock equations are solved for finite nuclei \osi , \caf\ and \cafe . In the second approach the effect of correlations are treated in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation directly for the finite nuclei, but the modifications of the Dirac spinors in the medium are derived from nuclear matter assuming a local-density approximation. Both approaches yield rather similar results for binding energies and radii in fair agreement with experimental data. The importance of the density dependent correlation effects is demonstrated and different ingredients to the spin-orbit splitting in the shell-model of the nucleus are discussed.

# NN Correlations and Relativistic Hartree-Fock in Finite Nuclei

## Key claim

Two different approximation schemes for solving the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation for finite nuclei yield rather similar results for binding energies and radii, suggesting that a complete self-consistent DBHF calculation would also produce comparable outcomes; however, the effective coupling constants must be treated as density-dependent to avoid overestimating the change of Dirac spinors at high densities.

## Method

The authors compare two approaches using realistic One-Boson-Exchange potentials (versions A and C of the Bonn potential). In the first approach (effective meson exchange), correlation effects from nuclear matter DBHF calculations are parameterized as density-dependent effective coupling constants for sigma, omega, and pi mesons, which are then used in relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations for finite nuclei. In the second approach (local density approximation), the Dirac spinor modifications are taken from nuclear matter DBHF calculations at the average density of each single-particle orbit, while the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation is performed directly for the finite nucleus using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion.

## Key numbers

- Effective coupling constants G_sigma and G_omega decrease with increasing density; the square of the coupling constants is quenched by a factor of 2 when nuclear density increases from 0.2 rho_0 to 1.4 rho_0 (rho_0 denoting empirical saturation density)
- Hartree calculations using coupling constants appropriate for small densities overestimate binding energy by about 8 MeV per nucleon compared to self-consistent DBHF results
- Calculations performed for O-16, Ca-40, and Ca-48 using OBE potentials A and C
- Meson masses used: m_pi = 138 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV; pion coupling constant fixed to f^2_pi/4pi = 0.08

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach from nuclear matter (Brockmann and Machleidt 1984; Horowitz and Serot 1984) to finite nuclei, building on earlier attempts by Brockmann and Toki (restricted to Hartree approximation) and the authors' own preliminary Hartree-Fock results. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that non-relativistic BHF calculations fall on the Coester band (Day 1981; Coester 1970), where either binding energy or radius disagrees with experiment. The two approximation schemes enable systematic comparison of how correlation and relativistic effects can be incorporated into finite-nucleus calculations without performing the full, computationally intensive DBHF self-consistency problem.
