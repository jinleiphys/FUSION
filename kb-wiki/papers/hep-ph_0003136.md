# Nuclear Shadowing and Coherence Length for Longitudinal and Transverse Photons

> Motivated by the recent results for DIS off nuclei from the HERMES experiment we have performed a systematic study of shadowing for transverse and longitudinal photons. We found that the coherence length which controls the onset of nuclear shadowing at small x_{Bj} is much longer for longitudinal than transverse photons, and is much shorter for shadowing of gluons. The light-cone Green function approach we apply properly treats shadowing in the transition region x_{Bj}>0.01. It also incorporates the nonperturbative effects and is legitimate at small Q^2. We calculate nuclear shadowing and compare with data from the HERMES and NMC experiments. Although we expect different nuclear shadowing for longitudinal and transverse photons, numerically it cannot explain the strong effect observed by the HERMES collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors find that the coherence length controlling nuclear shadowing is substantially longer for longitudinal photons than for transverse photons, and much shorter for gluon shadowing; however, despite expecting different shadowing for longitudinal and transverse photons, the calculated effect cannot numerically explain the strong shadowing observed by the HERMES collaboration.

## Method

The authors apply a light-cone Green function formalism that treats the propagation of interacting quark-antiquark pairs in vacuum, incorporating nonperturbative effects via a harmonic oscillator potential. They calculate the mean coherence length for transverse and longitudinal photons by averaging the factor P = l_c / l_c^{max} weighted by the dipole cross section squared, using both perturbative wave functions and nonperturbative wave functions derived from the Green function. For gluon shadowing, they extend the formalism to the q-qbar-gluon Fock component, treating the system as a color octet-octet dipole at high Q^2.

## Key numbers

- At x_Bj = 0.01, the factor ⟨P^L⟩ for longitudinal photons is substantially larger than ⟨P^T⟩ for transverse photons, indicating a longer coherence length for longitudinal photons (Fig. l-Q2, dotted/dashed/solid curves)
- The coherence length for gluons ⟨P^G⟩ is much shorter than both ⟨P^T⟩ and ⟨P^L⟩ for q-qbar fluctuations at Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 (Fig. l-Q2)
- The effective quark mass used is m_q = 0.2 GeV, corresponding to the confinement radius
- The dipole cross section parameterization uses r_0(s) = 0.88 (s/s_0)^{-0.14} fm with s_0 = 1000 GeV^2, and σ_{tot}^{πp}(s) = 23.6 (s/s_0)^{0.08} mb
- The calculated transverse cross section for nitrogen is shadowed by about 20%, much less than the HERMES data require
- For the gluon coherence length calculation, the lower cutoff on α_G is α_G^{min} = 2b_0^2/(0.2s - Q^2) with b_0 ≈ 0.65 GeV, and the upper cutoff is α_G^{max} = 2b_0^2/Q^2

## Context

This work is motivated by HERMES data showing unexpectedly strong nuclear shadowing at small x_Bj ≈ 0.02 and Q^2 < 1 GeV^2, which previous approaches could not explain. The authors extend the perturbative QCD method of Kopeliovich, Schafer, and Tarasov (1999) to low Q^2 by introducing nonperturbative inter-quark interactions, and compare with data from both HERMES and NMC experiments. The results enable future calculations of nuclear shadowing in the transition region x_Bj > 0.01 where neither the vector dominance model nor perturbative QCD alone is reliable, and predict that gluon shadowing sets in at smaller x_Bj than quark shadowing.
