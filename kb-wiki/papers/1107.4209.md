# Collective excitations in the neutron star inner crust

> We study the spectrum of collective excitations in the inhomogeneous phases in the neutron star inner crust within a superfluid hydrodynamics approach. Our aim is to describe the whole range of wavelengths, from the long-wavelength limit which can be described by macroscopic approaches and which is crucial for the low-energy part of the spectrum, to wavelengths of the order of the dimensions of the Wigner-Seitz cells, corresponding to the modes usually described in microscopic calculations. As an application, we will discuss the contribution of these collective modes to the specific heat of the "lasagna" phase in comparison with other known contributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that collective excitations in the inhomogeneous "lasagna" phase of the neutron star inner crust can be described across all wavelengths using a superfluid hydrodynamics approach, but the authors acknowledge that for their concrete example the coherence length is of the same order as the layer sizes, meaning the approach touches its limit of validity.

## Method

The authors linearize superfluid hydrodynamic equations (continuity and Euler equations) around stationary equilibrium for a two-phase system consisting of a neutron gas and a dense phase containing neutrons and protons. They solve the resulting wave equations with boundary conditions at the interfaces between slabs, using the Floquet-Bloch theorem to account for periodicity, and obtain the dispersion relation for collective modes in a one-dimensional slab structure (lasagna phase) with equilibrium properties taken from Avancini et al. (2009).

## Key numbers

- Baryon number density range for lasagna phase: 0.077 fm$^{-3}$ $\lesssim n_B \lesssim$ 0.084 fm$^{-3}$ (from Avancini et al. 2009)
- Example density studied: $n_B = 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$
- Slab thicknesses: slab 1 (neutron gas) $L_1 = 9.40$ fm, slab 2 (dense phase) $L_2 = 7.38$ fm, total period $L = 16.78$ fm
- Neutron densities: $n_{n1} = 0.0701$ fm$^{-3}$ (slab 1), $n_{n2} = 0.0885$ fm$^{-3}$ (slab 2)
- Proton density in slab 2: $n_{p2} = 0.0041$ fm$^{-3}$; slab 1 has zero protons
- Sound velocities in slab 1: $u_1 = 0.0641\,c$; in slab 2: $u^+ = 0.0354\,c$, $u^- = 0.1369\,c$
- Acoustic branch sound speed for propagation perpendicular to slabs ($\theta = 0$): $u_s = 0.072\,c$
- Acoustic branch sound speed for propagation parallel to slabs ($\theta = \pi/2$): $u_s = 0.085\,c$
- Second acoustic branch "two-dimensional sound velocity" $u_s^\prime$ varies between $0.04\,c$ and $0.046\,c$

## Context

This work extends the long-wavelength superfluid hydrodynamic treatments of Pethick (2010) and Cirigliano (2011) to shorter wavelengths where the inhomogeneous structure of the pasta phases becomes important, bridging the gap between macroscopic approaches and microscopic Wigner-Seitz calculations (e.g., Sandulescu 2004, Khan 2005, Fortin 2009). The results enable calculation of the specific heat contribution from collective modes in the lasagna phase, which had not been considered before, and can be compared with contributions from electrons, lattice vibrations, and collective excitations of nuclei discussed by Gnedin (2001).
