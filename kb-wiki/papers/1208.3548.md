# The hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment and renormalization group for EFT

> We give a short overview of the theory of the muon anomalous magnetic moment with emphasis on the hadronic light-by-light and the pion loop contribution. We explain the difference between the hidden local symmetry and full VMD pion loop and discuss leading logarithms in the anomalous sector of 2-flavour chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the hadronic light-by-light (HLBL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is approximately (10 ± 3) × 10⁻¹⁰, but notes that the exact central value and error are somewhat subjective, as different models (e.g., BPP vs. PdRV) give different central values and error treatments.

## Method
The authors review the standard model prediction for a_μ, focusing on the HLBL contribution, which is evaluated using hadronic models including pseudo-scalar exchange, axial-vector exchange, scalar exchange, quark-loop, and charged pion-loop contributions. They compare two independent calculations: one using the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model (BPP) and another using purely hadronic exchanges with a quark-loop and vector meson dominance (VMD) suppression (HKS/PdRV). They also discuss the application of renormalization group methods to compute leading logarithms in the anomalous sector of two-flavour chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for processes like π⁰ → γ*γ* and γ3π.

## Key numbers
- The experimental value of a_μ is 11 659 208.9 × 10⁻¹⁰ with an error of 6.3 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The standard model prediction is 11 659 180.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ with an error of 4.9 × 10⁻¹⁰, giving a difference of 28.7 × 10⁻¹⁰ with an error of 8.1 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The HLBL contribution is (10.5 ± 2.6) × 10⁻¹⁰ in the PdRV model and (8.3 ± 3.2) × 10⁻¹⁰ in the BPP model.
- The pseudo-scalar exchange contribution is (8.5 ± 1.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ (BPP) and (11.4 ± 1.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ (PdRV).
- The charged pion-loop contribution is −1.9 × 10⁻¹⁰ in the VMD approach (BPP) and −0.45 × 10⁻¹⁰ in the hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach (HKS).
- The bare pion loop gives a contribution of −4.1 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- The leading logarithms for the π⁰ → γ*γ* form-factor at six loops give a numerical series: 1 + 0 − 0.000372 + 0.000088 + 0.000036 + 0.000009 + 0.0000002 + ...
- The leading logarithms for the γ3π vertex give F₀^{3π LL} = (9.8 − 0.3 + 0.04 + 0.02 + 0.006 + 0.001 + ...) GeV⁻³.

## Context
This paper extends the ongoing effort to resolve the discrepancy between the experimental value of a_μ from BNL E821 and the standard model prediction, which has motivated many beyond-standard-model (BSM) theories. It compares the BPP (Bijnens, Pallante, Prades) and PdRV (Prades, de Rafael, Vainshtein) calculations of the HLBL contribution, highlighting differences in the treatment of the pion loop (HLS vs. full VMD) and the short-distance constraint from the operator product expansion (OPE) introduced by Melnikov and Vainshtein (2004). The renormalization group analysis for ChPT, building on work by Buchler, Colangelo, and others, enables calculation of leading logarithms to high loop orders, which is important for improving the theoretical prediction of the HLBL contribution and related processes like π⁰ → γ*γ* and γ3π.
