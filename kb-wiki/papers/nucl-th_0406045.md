# The Single Particle Sum Rules in the Deep-Inelastic Region

> We have modelled parton distribution in nuclei using a suitably modified nuclear Fermi motion. The modifications concern the nucleon rest energy which changes the Björken x in nuclear medium. We also introduce final state interactions between the scattered nucleon and the rest of the nucleus. The energy-momentum sum rule is saturated. Good agreement with experimental data of the EMC effect for $x> 0.15$ and nuclear lepton pair production data has been obtained.

# The Single Particle Sum Rules in the Deep-Inelastic Region

## Key claim

The EMC effect for nuclear deep inelastic scattering in the region $0.15 < x < 1$ can be explained by introducing an $x$-dependent effective nucleon rest energy (mass) that interpolates between a reduced value $M_B \approx M_N - (20\div30)$ MeV at large $x > 0.6$ and the free nucleon mass $M_N$ at small $x < 0.25$, thereby saturating the momentum sum rule with only about 1% of nucleon momenta carried by additional sea partons.

## Method

The authors model nuclear parton distributions using a convolution of the nuclear distribution function from the Relativistic Mean Field model with a modified free nucleon structure function. For $x > 0.6$, where the longitudinal scale $z = 1/(M_N x)$ is smaller than the nucleon hard core radius (0.35 fm), they treat nucleons as noninteracting with a modified rest energy $M_B$ derived from the nuclear binding energy and Fermi energy. For $x < 0.6$, they introduce a linear probability function $f(x)$ that interpolates between scattering on a single nucleon and scattering on a correlated nucleon pair, with the effective nucleon mass $M_x$ resetting toward the free value $M_N$ due to final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon binding energy: $\epsilon \simeq -8$ MeV (for $A>50$ nuclei)
- Average Fermi energy: $E_{Fermi} \simeq 0.6 \cdot \left(\vec{p}_F^2/(M_N+\epsilon)\right)$
- Modified nucleon rest energy at large $x$: $M_B \cong M_N + \epsilon - E_{Fermi} \approx M_N - (20\div30)$ MeV
- Boundary between regimes: $x_B = 0.6$, corresponding to longitudinal scale $z = 1/(M_N x) < r_C = 0.35$ fm
- Nucleon hard core radius: $r_C \simeq 0.35$ fm; nucleon average radius: $r_N \simeq 0.8$ fm
- Average internucleon distance in independent pair approximation: $r_h \simeq 1.7$ fm
- For $^{56}Fe$: $\bar{n}_A = 0.12$ fm$^{-3}$, $\epsilon = -8.8$ MeV
- Momentum sum rule violation reduced to 1% with $x$-dependent mass (from 3% with constant mass $M_B$)
- Sea quark momentum increase required: 1% (compared to 3% for constant mass model)
- Calculations performed at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ and $Q^2 = 30$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the approach of Rożynek and Wilk (2003) by introducing an $x$-dependent nucleon rest energy to account for final state interactions in nuclear deep inelastic scattering, improving upon earlier models that used only constant mass modifications. The model addresses the failure of simple convolution approaches (which required large mesonic contributions) to simultaneously describe both the EMC effect data from SLAC and nuclear lepton pair production data from Alde (1990). The results can be compared with the chiral soliton model and direct quark-meson coupling mechanism of Miller and Smith (1995), but here the medium modifications are expressed entirely through global nuclear parameters (Fermi energy and mass defect) without additional free parameters. The approach enables a consistent description of nuclear structure functions across the full range $x > 0.15$ while satisfying the momentum sum rule within the conventional picture of interacting nucleons.
