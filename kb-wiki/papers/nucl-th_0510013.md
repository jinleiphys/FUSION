# Generalized dynamical equation and low energy nucleon dynamics

> Low energy nucleon dynamics is investigated by using the generalized dynamical equation derived in [J. Phys. A v.32, 5657 (1999)]. This equation extends quantum dynamics to describe the time evolution in the case of nonlocal-in-time interactions. We show that the use of the generalized dynamical equation, which allows one to take into account that effective actions in quantum field theory, arising after integrating out certain degrees of freedom, are generally nonlocal, provides a new way to formulate the effective theory of nuclear forces. In this way we construct the most general possible operator describing the short-range part of the NN interaction in the 1S0 channel. In particular, this operator can be incorporated into the chiral potential model. This is shown to provide an extension of the standard chiral approach. Being equivalent to the chiral potential model for some values of the constants characterizing the short-range of the NN interaction this approach allows the solutions that cannot be reproduced by any potential model. In this way we find that for certain values of the constants inconsistent with potentials models the effect of the short-range forces on the half off-shell 2N T-matrix with high off-shell momenta grows in magnitude rapidly as the kinetic energy of outgoing protons decreases at laboratory energies below 15 MeV. We show that this effect may be the origin of the existing discrepancy between the theory and experiment in describing the proton-proton bremsstrahlung.

## Key claim

The generalized dynamical equation (GDE), which extends quantum dynamics to nonlocal-in-time interactions, provides a new way to formulate the effective theory of nuclear forces that is equivalent to the chiral potential model for some parameter values but also allows solutions that cannot be reproduced by any potential model; for certain constants inconsistent with potential models, the effect of short-range forces on the half off-shell two-nucleon T-matrix with high off-shell momenta grows rapidly as the kinetic energy of outgoing protons decreases below 15 MeV, which may explain the existing discrepancy between theory and experiment in proton-proton bremsstrahlung.

## Method

The authors use the generalized dynamical equation derived from the principle of superposition of probability amplitudes and unitarity, which reduces to the Schrodinger equation for instantaneous interactions but permits non-Hamiltonian dynamics for nonlocal-in-time interactions. They apply this equation to the two-nucleon system in the 1S0 channel, constructing the T-matrix by expanding it in powers of Q/Lambda where Q is a low energy scale and Lambda is the breakdown scale, without resorting to regularization or renormalization procedures. The approach is then extended to incorporate the short-range part of the NN interaction into the chiral potential model framework.

## Key numbers

- The effect of short-range forces on the half off-shell 2N T-matrix with high off-shell momenta grows in magnitude rapidly as the kinetic energy of outgoing protons decreases at laboratory energies below 15 MeV
- The parameter alpha characterizing the form factor behavior at large momentum (phi(p) ~ |p|^(-alpha)) determines whether dynamics is Hamiltonian (alpha > 1/2) or non-Hamiltonian (alpha < 1/2)
- The leading order solution for the T-matrix involves the function M_0(z) = (m/(4*pi))*sqrt(-z*m), where m is the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg program (Weinberg, 1997) for deriving NN potentials from chiral perturbation theory, which produces singular potentials requiring regularization and renormalization. The authors argue that the divergence problems in the standard chiral approach may arise from forcing non-Hamiltonian dynamics into the Schrodinger equation framework. The approach enables a new formulation of effective field theory for nuclear forces that avoids UV divergences and can potentially resolve the longstanding discrepancy between potential model predictions and experimental data for proton-proton bremsstrahlung, as noted by M-S and C-T.
