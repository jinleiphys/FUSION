# (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics CLVisc at finite net baryon density: identified particle spectra, anisotropic flows and flow fluctuations across BES energies

> To study the bulk properties of the quark-gluon-plasma (QGP) produced at the beam energy scan (BES) energies at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), we extend the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics CLVisc to include net baryon number conservation and Israel-Stewart-like equation for baryon diffusion with the NEOS-BQS equation of state, fluctuating initial conditions from Monte-Carlo Glauber model, and the afterburner SMASH. This integrated framework is shown to provide a good description of identified particle spectra, mean transverse momenta and anisotropic flows for different centralities and over a wide range of collision energies (7.7-62.4 GeV). It is found that the mean momenta of identified particles and anisotropic flows increases mildly with the collision energy due to larger radial flow. We further compute the multiple-particle cumulant ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ of elliptic flow across BES energies, and find that the relative fluctuations of elliptic flow are insensitive to the collision energy, consistent with the preliminary STAR data. Our model provides a benchmark for understanding the RHIC-BES data and studying the critical properties and phase structure of hot and dense QCD matter.

## Key claim

The extended CLVisc framework provides a good description of identified particle spectra, mean transverse momenta, and anisotropic flows across BES energies (7.7-62.4 GeV), but the abstract's claim of "good description" is qualified by the fact that the model underestimates experimental data at the lowest energy (7.7 GeV) and for most central collisions, and the authors note that bulk viscosity is neglected and the pre-equilibrium stage is not included.

## Method

The authors extended the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics code CLVisc to include net baryon number conservation and an Israel-Stewart-like equation for baryon diffusion, using the NEOS-BQS equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential. Fluctuating initial conditions were generated from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with longitudinal envelope functions for entropy and baryon density, and the afterburner SMASH was used for hadronic transport after particlization via the Cooper-Frye formula. The Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm with second-order Runge-Kutta time integration was used to solve the hydrodynamic equations, with specific shear viscosity set to C_eta_v = 0.08 and baryon diffusion coefficient C_B = 0.4 as constants for all collision energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time tau_0 ranges from 3.6 fm at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to 1.0 fm at sqrt(s_NN) = 62.4 GeV
- Specific shear viscosity C_eta_v = 0.08 and baryon diffusion coefficient C_B = 0.4, set as constants for all collision energies
- Freeze-out energy density e_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Transverse Gaussian smearing width sigma_r = 0.5 fm for initial condition nucleon positions
- 500 event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations performed per centrality class at each collision energy
- 2000 SMASH afterburner events generated per hydrodynamic event for final analysis
- Hydrodynamic grid size N_x x N_y x N_eta = 201 x 201 x 201; running a single event at 19.6 GeV takes ~1800 seconds on a GeForce GTX 3080 GPU
- Scale factor K ranges from 7.67 at 7.7 GeV to 10.8 at 62.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the CLVisc hydrodynamics framework (Pang 2018) to finite net baryon density, building on prior studies that included baryon diffusion (Denicol 2018, Wu 2021) and the NEOS-BQS equation of state (Monnai 2019, Monnai 2021). The model provides a baseline for understanding RHIC-BES data and enables future studies of critical properties of QCD matter, testing equations of state with critical points (Parotto 2018), and investigating jet quenching and polarization phenomena at finite baryon density.
