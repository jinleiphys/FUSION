# The two pion decay of the Roper resonance

> We evaluate the two pion decay of the Roper resonance in a model where explicit re-scattering of the two final pions is accounted for by the use of unitarized chiral perturbation theory. Our model does not include an explicit $ε$ or $σ$ scalar-isoscalar meson decay mode, instead it generates it dynamically by means of the pion re-scattering. The two ways, explicit or dynamically generated, of introducing this decay channel have very different amplitudes. Nevertheless, through interference with the other terms of the model we are able to reproduce the same phenomenology as models with explicit consideration of the $ε$ meson.

## Key claim
The authors show that the two-pion decay of the Roper resonance can be described without an explicit scalar-isoscalar meson (σ or ε) decay mode, by instead generating that mode dynamically through pion re-scattering. However, this agreement with the phenomenology of Manley (1984, 1992) is achieved only at the Roper mass; the model underpredicts the low-invariant-mass region by a factor of about 0.42 relative to the phenomenological coupling constant.

## Method
The authors construct a model for the Roper → N π π decay using two types of contributions: "open diagrams" where the Roper decays into a baryon (Δ, nucleon, or Roper itself) and a pion, followed by baryon decay into N π, and "closed diagrams" where the two final pions undergo final-state interaction via a unitarized chiral perturbation theory ππ t-matrix in the L=0, I=0 channel. The coupling constant f_{RΔπ} is fixed by fitting the total Roper decay width into two pions (153 MeV) as obtained from Manley's approach, using a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 0.7 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Total Roper decay width into two pions: Γ_{R→Nππ} = 153 MeV (used as fit target, from Manley's approach)
- Open-diagrams contribution to width: 55.3 MeV (with Δ alone: 54.7 MeV; without Δ: 0.6 MeV)
- Closed-diagrams contribution to width: 68.6 MeV (with Δ alone: 31.8 MeV; without Δ: 8.2 MeV)
- f_{RΔπ} = 1.1 (for Λ = 0.7 GeV), compared to 1.56 in Manley's approach
- For Λ = 0.9 GeV: f_{RΔπ} = 0.83; for Λ = 1.1 GeV: f_{RΔπ} = 0.62
- Effective coupling constant at low invariant mass (M = 1218 MeV): g_{eff.}^{Our-model} = (6.5 + i 2.0) × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}
- Ratio of effective coupling to phenomenological value: |g_{eff.}^{Our-model}| / g_{RNππ} = 0.42

## Context
This work extends the approach of Manley (1984, 1992), who fitted Roper decay data using an explicit ε meson (σ meson, f₀(400-900)) plus Δπ channels. The authors instead use the unitarized chiral perturbation theory framework of Oller (1997) and Oller, Oset, and Pelaez, where the σ meson is dynamically generated from ππ rescattering. The model successfully reproduces Manley's invariant mass distributions at the Roper mass, but fails at low invariant masses, suggesting missing contributions. This enables future work on a microscopic description of the NN → NN* transition and complements quark-model studies by Julia-Diaz.
