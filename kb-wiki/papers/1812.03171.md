# The criticality of multiplicative processes

> Keeping in view applications to numerical simulations of the evolution of a nuclear reactor core around criticality, we use a general mathematical framework for describing the evolutions of multiplicative processes (processes involving particle creation) both in particle generations and in time. This framework allows us to obtain, within a same formalism, two corresponding estimates of the multiplication factor which describes the growth of particle numbers at large times. We obtain the relative positions of both estimates with respect to each other and to criticality. These relations may show particularly useful when simulating in a realistic way the monitoring of nuclear cores in subcritical states, such as is the case for Accelerator Driven Systems (ADS). More generally, this study applies to various multiplicative processes which can be found in nature.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the two distinct estimates of the multiplication factor, one derived from time evolution and one from generation evolution, are equivalent near criticality, with the generation-based eigenvalue always lying farther from unity than the time-based eigenvalue when the system is not exactly critical.

## Method

The authors model neutron multiplication in a nuclear reactor core as a Markovian multiplicative stochastic process on a discretized phase space of cells, using a Master equation approach. They define a time-evolution matrix **S** from the probabilities of absorption, diffusion, and fission per time step, and a generation transfer matrix **T** from a partial resummation over all time steps of the free propagation kernel. The relation **S = 1 − (1 − T)(1 − R)** (where **R** is the substochastic matrix for propagation without fission) is derived and used to compare the Perron-Frobenius eigenvalues λ_S and λ_T.

## Key numbers

- For subcritical systems (λ_T < 1), the inequality λ_T ≤ λ_S < 1 holds; for supercritical systems (λ_S > 1), the inequality 1 < λ_S ≤ λ_T holds.
- At exact criticality, λ_T = 1 if and only if λ_S = 1.
- Near criticality, the eigenvalues are related by λ_T(ε) − 1 = r (λ_S(ε) − 1), where the proportionality coefficient r > 1 depends only on the matrix **R** and the eigenvectors of **S** and **T** at criticality.
- The time step Δt is constrained by 0 ≤ Δt ≤ 1 / max_β (Ȧ_β + Σ_γ Ḋ_γβ + Σ_{ {q_γ} } Ḟ({q_γ}|β) ), where the rates are defined per unit time.

## Context

This work extends the formal treatment of multiplicative processes used in Monte Carlo simulations of nuclear cores, building on the Master equation approach of Keizer (1987), Kubo (1965), and Gaveau (1999). It directly addresses the ambiguity noted in experimental ADS studies (KUCA experiments, 2010–2015) between kinetic parameters obtained from generation-based codes and time-dependent measurements. The derived relation between λ_S and λ_T enables consistent comparison of multiplication factors from different simulation approaches and is particularly relevant for monitoring subcritical cores in Accelerator Driven Systems.
