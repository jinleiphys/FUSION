# Pairing-induced speedup of nuclear spontaneous fission

> Collective inertia is strongly influenced at the level crossing at which quantum system changes diabatically its microscopic configuration. Pairing correlations tend to make the large-amplitude nuclear collective motion more adiabatic by reducing the effect of those configuration changes. Competition between pairing and level crossing is thus expected to have a profound impact on spontaneous fission lifetimes. To elucidate the role of nucleonic pairing on spontaneous fission, we study the dynamic fission trajectories of $^{264}$Fm and $^{240}$Pu using the state-of-the-art self-consistent framework. We employ the superfluid nuclear density functional theory with the Skyrme energy density functional SkM$^*$ and a density-dependent pairing interaction. Along with shape variables, proton and neutron pairing correlations are taken as collective coordinates. The collective inertia tensor is calculated within the nonperturbative cranking approximation. The fission paths are obtained by using the least action principle in a four-dimensional collective space of shape and pairing coordinates. Pairing correlations are enhanced along the minimum-action fission path. For the symmetric fission of $^{264}$Fm, where the effect of triaxiality on the fission barrier is large, the geometry of fission pathway in the space of shape degrees of freedom is weakly impacted by pairing. This is not the case for $^{240}$Pu where pairing fluctuations restore the axial symmetry of the dynamic fission trajectory. The minimum-action fission path is strongly impacted by nucleonic pairing. In some cases, the dynamical coupling between shape and pairing degrees of freedom can lead to a dramatic departure from the static picture. Consequently, in the dynamical description of nuclear fission, particle-particle correlations should be considered on the same footing as those associated with shape degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations, when treated as dynamical collective coordinates alongside shape degrees of freedom, can significantly reduce the spontaneous fission half-life by lowering the collective action, but the effect is conditional on the interplay between pairing and triaxiality: for nuclei where triaxiality strongly lowers the fission barrier (like $^{264}$Fm), pairing has a weak impact on the shape trajectory, whereas for nuclei with a smaller triaxiality effect (like $^{240}$Pu), pairing fluctuations can fully restore axial symmetry along the fission path.

## Method

The authors employed superfluid nuclear density functional theory with the Skyrme energy density functional SkM$^*$ and a density-dependent mixed pairing force. They performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations in a four-dimensional collective space consisting of two quadrupole shape coordinates ($Q_{20}$ for elongation and $Q_{22}$ for triaxiality) and two pairing coordinates (isoscalar and isovector pairing fluctuations controlled by Lagrange multipliers $\lambda_{2\tau}$). The collective inertia tensor was computed using the nonperturbative cranking approximation, and minimum-action fission paths were obtained via the least action principle using both dynamical programming and Ritz methods.

## Key numbers

- For $^{264}$Fm, the effect of triaxiality on the fission barrier is approximately 4 MeV; for $^{240}$Pu, it is approximately 2 MeV.
- The calculated spontaneous fission half-life of $^{264}$Fm in the three-dimensional (shape + pairing) variant is decreased by as much as three decades compared to the two-dimensional calculation without pairing fluctuations.
- The collective ground state energy $E_0$ was taken as 1 MeV for consistency with prior work.
- The number of assaults on the fission barrier per unit time was taken as $n = 10^{20.38} \, \text{s}^{-1}$.
- The mesh used for the three-dimensional calculation in $^{264}$Fm was $90 \times 61 \times 31$ points, with ranges $21 \le x_1 \le 110$, $-10 \le x_3 \le 50$, and $-15 \le x_4 \le 15$.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent least-action approach to spontaneous fission by adding pairing correlations as dynamical collective coordinates, building on the earlier framework of Sadhukhan et al. (2013) which studied fission pathways using shape degrees of freedom alone. The results confirm earlier macroscopic-microscopic studies by Staszczak et al. (1985, 1989), Lojewski et al. (1999), and Mirea et al. (2010) that pairing fluctuations can reduce the collective action, but go further by showing that pairing dynamics can restore symmetries broken in static mean-field calculations, particularly axial symmetry in $^{240}$Pu. This work enables future systematic comparisons with experimental spontaneous fission lifetimes by incorporating full Adiabatic Time Dependent HFB treatment of collective inertia, reflection-asymmetric coordinates, and energy density functionals optimized for fission.
