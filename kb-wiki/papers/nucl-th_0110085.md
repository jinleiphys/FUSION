# Duality in strong interactions

> By introducing Q^2-dependence in resonance-reggeon "soft" dual models with nonlinear trajectories, they are extended to "hard" processes, sharing the property of parton-hadron duality. The resulting object is a two-component complex function of three variables - s,t and Q^2, reducing to classical dual models on the mass shell and to deep inelastic structure functions, or Compton scattering amplitudes - forward and non-forward - otherwise. As a consequence, the small- and large-x behavior of the structure functions is also related by duality. This duality relation holds for each o components (diffractive and non-diffractive for the amplitude or singlet and non-singlet for the structure) separately.

## Key claim

By introducing a specific \(Q^2\)-dependence into the Regge residues of a dual amplitude with Mandelstam analyticity (DAMA), the authors extend soft hadronic dual models to hard processes, establishing that the small-\(x\) and large-\(x\) behavior of structure functions are related by duality, and that this duality holds separately for the diffractive and non-diffractive components of the amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component complex scattering amplitude \(A(s,t,Q^2)\) using DAMA, which incorporates nonlinear complex Regge trajectories with a finite number of resonances. The \(Q^2\)-dependence is introduced by matching the Regge asymptotic behavior of the amplitude to standard parameterizations of deep inelastic structure functions, specifically by making the DAMA parameter \(g\) a function of \(Q^2\) via a transcendental equation. The resulting amplitude is then used to extrapolate from the high-energy, low-\(x\) region down to the resonance region, where its pole expansion is evaluated for a single \(\Delta^+(1236)\) resonance and a smooth background modeled by an exotic trajectory.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta\) resonance trajectory is parameterized as \(\alpha_{\Delta}(s)=0.1+0.84s+0.1331(\sqrt{s_0}-\sqrt{s_0-s})\) with \(s_0=(m_\pi^2+m_N^2)\).
- The exotic (background) trajectory is \(\alpha_E(s)=-0.25+0.25(\sqrt{1.21}-\sqrt{1.21-s})\) with \(s_E=1.1^2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For the \(\Delta\) resonance component, \(Q_{lim}^2=62\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(Q_0^2=0.01\ \text{GeV}^2\); for the background component, \(Q_{lim}^2=120\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(Q_0^2=2.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The \(f\) Regge trajectory intercept is \(\alpha_f(0)=0.9\); the Pomeron intercept is \(\alpha_P(0)=1+0.077\cdot\left(1+\frac{2Q^2}{Q^2+1.117}\right)\).
- The normalization coefficient is \(c=0.03\).
- Cross sections are shown for \(Q^2=1\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2=6\ \text{GeV}^2\) in the resonance region \(\sqrt{s}=1.1-1.5\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the classical resonance-Regge duality of Veneziano (1968) and the Bloom-Gilman hadron-parton duality (1970) by constructing an explicit dual model that unifies both concepts. It builds on earlier attempts to combine resonance behavior with Bjorken scaling by authors such as Domokos and Gunion, and on recent experimental measurements of nucleon structure functions at JLab (Niculescu, 2000). The resulting amplitude enables a unified description of deep inelastic scattering from the resonance region to the scaling region, and can be used to study the interrelation between small-\(x\) and large-\(x\) behavior of structure functions, as well as to test the validity of parton-hadron duality with a finite number of resonances.
