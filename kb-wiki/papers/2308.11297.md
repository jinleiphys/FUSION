# Stellar weak-interaction rates for $rp$-process waiting-point nuclei from projected shell model

> We propose a projected shell model (PSM) for description of stellar weak-interaction rates between even-even and odd-odd nuclei with extended configuration space where up to six-quasiparticle (qp) configurations are included, and the stellar weak-interaction rates for eight $rp$-process waiting-point (WP) nuclei, $^{64}$Ge, $^{68}$Se, $^{72}$Kr, $^{76}$Sr, $^{80}$Zr, $^{84}$Mo, $^{88}$Ru and $^{92}$Pd, are calculated and analyzed for the first time within the model. Higher-order qp configurations are found to affect the underlying Gamow-Teller strength distributions and the corresponding stellar weak-interaction rates. Under $rp$-process environments with high temperatures and densities, on one hand, thermal population of excited states of parent nuclei tends to decrease the stellar $β^+$ decay rates. On the other hand, the possibility of electron capture (EC) tends to provide increasing contribution to the rates with temperature and density. The effective half-lives of WP nuclei under the $rp$-process peak condition are predicted to be reduced as compared with the terrestrial case, especially for $^{64}$Ge and $^{68}$Se.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) with up to six-quasiparticle configurations predicts that the effective half-lives of eight rp-process waiting-point nuclei under rp-process peak conditions are reduced compared to terrestrial half-lives, especially for ⁶⁴Ge and ⁶⁸Se, but this result depends on the inclusion of higher-order quasiparticle configurations which significantly affect the Gamow-Teller strength distributions.

## Method

The authors extended the projected shell model to even-even and odd-odd nuclei by including up to six-quasiparticle configurations in the basis space, using three major harmonic-oscillator shells (N=2,3,4) for the model space. They calculated Gamow-Teller transition strengths from both ground states and first excited 2⁺ states of the parent nuclei, then used these strengths with Boltzmann-weighted thermal occupation factors and phase-space integrals for electron capture and β⁺ decay to compute stellar weak-interaction rates for eight rp-process waiting-point nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The eight rp-process waiting-point nuclei studied are: ⁶⁴Ge, ⁶⁸Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁶Sr, ⁸⁰Zr, ⁸⁴Mo, ⁸⁸Ru, and ⁹²Pd
- ⁶⁴Ge has the longest terrestrial β⁺ half-life of 63.7 s among the waiting-point nuclei
- The rp-process peak conditions are temperature T = 1–3 GK and density ρ = 10⁶–10⁷ g/cm³
- A quenching factor f_quench = 0.75 is applied to the axial-vector coupling constant ratio (g_A/g_V)_bare = -1.2599(25)
- The constant K = 6146 s is used for the weak rate formula from superallowed Fermi transitions
- ⁷⁶Sr and ⁸⁰Zr have first excited 2⁺ states with excitation energy E_i ≲ 300 keV, which can be thermally populated at rp-process peak temperatures

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach previously developed for odd-mass nuclei (Wang 2018, 2020, 2021) to even-mass nuclei for the first time, enabling calculation of stellar weak-interaction rates for rp-process waiting-point nuclei. The results contrast with the phenomenological hypothesis of Lau (2018, 2020) which assumed transitions from first excited states are 10 times faster than ground-state rates, and with EXVAM calculations by Petrovici (2015, 2019) which predicted similar Gamow-Teller strength distributions from ground and first excited states. The calculated rates can be used in X-ray burst models to determine nucleosynthesis paths, isotopic abundances, and light-curve timescales.
