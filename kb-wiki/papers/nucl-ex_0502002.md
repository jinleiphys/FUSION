# Effect of the break-up on the fusion and elastic scattering of weakly bound projectiles on $^{64}$Zn

> We study the behavior of the fusion, break-up, reaction and elastic scattering of different projectiles on $^{64}$Zn, at near and above barrier energies. We present fusion and elastic scattering data with the tightly bound $^{16}$O and the stable weakly bound $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li and $^{9}$Be projectiles. The data were analyzed by coupled channel calculations. The total fusion cross sections for these systems are not affected by the break-up process at energies above the barrier. The elastic (non-capture) break-up cross section is important at energies close and above the Coulomb barrier and increases the reaction cross sections. In addition we also show that the break-up process at near and sub-barrier energies is responsible for the vanishing of the usual threshold anomaly of the optical potential and give rise to a new type of anomaly.

## Key claim

The total fusion cross sections for the $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, and $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn systems are not affected by the break-up process at energies above the barrier, but the elastic (non-capture) break-up cross section is significant at near-barrier energies and increases the reaction cross sections; additionally, the break-up process at near and sub-barrier energies causes the usual threshold anomaly of the optical potential to vanish and gives rise to a new type of anomaly (the Break-up Threshold Anomaly, BTA).

## Method

The authors measured elastic scattering angular distributions for $^{16}$O, $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, and $^{9}$Be projectiles on a $^{64}$Zn target using surface barrier detectors at the Pelletron Laboratory of the Universidade de São Paulo. Total fusion cross sections for $^{6}$Li and $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn were measured using the time-of-flight method at the TANDAR Laboratory in Buenos Aires. The data were analyzed with coupled channel calculations using the CCFULL code and with the São Paulo Optical Potential (SPOP) to extract the energy dependence of the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear potential.

## Key numbers

- Total fusion cross sections for $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn were measured at beam energies of 16, 18, 20, 22, and 24 MeV.
- Total fusion cross sections for $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn were measured at beam energies of 20, 22, and 24 MeV.
- Elastic scattering angular distributions for $^{6}$Li + $^{64}$Zn were measured at 17, 20, and 22 MeV, and for $^{7}$Li + $^{64}$Zn at 20 and 22 MeV, over an angular range of $10^\circ \leq \theta_{\text{Lab}} \leq 150^\circ$.
- The $^{64}$Zn target thickness was 60 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ for elastic scattering measurements and 50 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ for fusion measurements.
- The uncertainties in the differential elastic scattering cross section data vary from 1% to 8%.
- For the $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn system, the incomplete fusion cross section ($\sigma_{\text{ICF}}$) was estimated to be less than 10% of the total fusion cross section ($\sigma_{\text{TF}}$).
- For the $^{16}$O + $^{64}$Zn system, reaction cross sections were derived in the energy range 40-68.5 MeV.
- The SPOP analysis for $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn showed that the normalization factor for the real potential ($N_R$) drops significantly below the barrier (from about 0.9 to lower values), while the normalization factor for the imaginary potential ($N_i$) increases from about 0.78 at energies above the barrier to higher values at sub-barrier energies.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fusion and break-up processes for weakly bound projectiles on medium-mass targets, particularly the $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn system previously reported by the same group (Gomes et al., earlier works). It provides a systematic comparison of tightly bound ($^{16}$O) and weakly bound ($^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{9}$Be) projectiles on the same target, complementing earlier studies on heavier targets such as $^{9}$Be + $^{209}$Bi (Signorini et al.) and $^{9}$Be + $^{208}$Pb. The identification of the Break-up Threshold Anomaly (BTA) in the energy dependence of the optical potential for $^{9}$Be + $^{64}$Zn contrasts with the usual threshold anomaly observed for tightly bound systems like $^{16}$O + $^{64}$Zn, and provides a framework for understanding similar effects in reactions with radioactive weakly bound projectiles.
