# Effective restoration of dipole sum rules within the renormalized random-phase approximation

> The dipole excitations for calcium and zirconium isotopes are studied within the fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean field incorporated with the renormalized random-phase approximation (RRPA) using the Skyrme interaction SLy5. The RRPA takes into account the effect of ground-state correlations beyond RPA owing to the Pauli principle between the particle-hole pairs that form the RPA excitations as well as the correlations due to the particle-particle and hole-hole transitions, whose effects are treated here in an effective way. By comparing the RPA results with the RRPA ones, which are obtained for isoscalar (IS) and isovector (IV) dipole excitations in $^{48, 52, 58}$Ca and $^{90, 96, 110}$Zr, it is shown that ground-state correlations beyond the RPA reduce the IS transition strengths. They also shift up the energy of the lowest IV dipole state and slightly push down the peak energy of the IV giant dipole resonance. As the result, the energy-weighted sums of strengths of both IS and IV modes decrease, causing the violation of the corresponding energy-weight sum rules (EWSR). It is shown that this sum rule violation can be eliminated by taking into account the contribution of the particle-particle and hole-hole excitations together with the particle-hole ones in a simple and perturbative way. Consequently, the ratio of the energy-weighted sum of strengths of the pygmy dipole resonance to that of the giant dipole resonance increases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the violation of energy-weighted sum rules (EWSRs) for isoscalar and isovector dipole excitations, which occurs in the standard renormalized random-phase approximation (phRRPA) due to ground-state correlations, can be effectively eliminated by perturbatively including the contributions from particle-particle and hole-hole transitions alongside the usual particle-hole ones, without requiring a full diagonalization of the enlarged RRPA matrix.

## Method

The authors modified a self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (RPA) code using the Skyrme interaction SLy5 to incorporate ground-state correlations via the renormalized RPA (phRRPA) method, which renormalizes the residual interaction by a factor \(\sqrt{D_{ph}}\) derived from particle and hole occupation numbers. To restore the violated sum rules, they added contributions from particle-particle and hole-hole excitations in a perturbative way, approximating the energies of these new phonon states as close to the corresponding particle-particle or hole-hole poles with a small adjustable energy shift \(\delta E\), and computing the associated amplitudes using a separable-interaction formalism. The method was applied to isoscalar and isovector dipole excitations in \(^{48,52,58}\)Ca and \(^{90,96,110}\)Zr.

## Key numbers

- The energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for isovector dipole excitations is related to the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule: TRK = \( \frac{9}{4\pi} \frac{\hbar^2}{2m^{*}} \frac{NZ}{A} \), with the isovector EWSR given as \( m_1^{IV} = (1+\kappa) \times \text{TRK} \), where \(\kappa\) is an enhancement factor from velocity-dependent interactions.
- The isoscalar dipole EWSR is given by \( m_1^{IS} = \frac{\hbar^2}{2m^{*}} \frac{A}{4\pi} (33\langle r^4 \rangle - 25\langle r^2 \rangle^2) \).
- The phRRPA iteration convergence criterion was set to \( |E_{Ji}(n) - E_{Ji}(n-1)| < 10^{-6} \) MeV.
- The escape width used to smooth strength functions was set to \(\Gamma^{\uparrow} \equiv 2\varepsilon\), with \(\varepsilon\) chosen such that the width is around a few hundred keV, compared to the total GDR width of around 4–5 MeV in medium and heavy nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the renormalized RPA (phRRPA) method, previously applied to low-lying \(3^{-}_1\) states in \(^{146}\)Gd and \(^{208}\)Pb by Catara, Dang and Sambataro (1993), to dipole excitations in calcium and zirconium isotopes. It addresses the known problem of sum rule violation in the phRRPA, first pointed out by Hirsch et al., by adopting a perturbative inclusion of particle-particle and hole-hole transitions, similar in spirit to the finite-temperature RPA studies by Sommer (1981) and Vautherin (1980), which showed that including such terms restores sum rules at finite temperature. The approach enables practical calculations of pygmy dipole resonance properties while preserving the EWSR, complementing more computationally intensive methods like second RPA or quasiparticle-phonon model.
