# IP-Glasma Phenomenology Beyond 2D

> We present a novel formulation of the IP-Glasma initial state model in 3+1D in which the 2+1D boost invariant IP-Glasma is generalized through JIMWLK rapidity evolution of the pre-collision Wilson lines \cite{Schenke:2016ksl}. In order to consistently accommodate a non-boost-invariant system, the initial conditions are generalized to 3+1D. By breaking boost invariance, the 3+1D model no longer trivially satisfies Gauss' law at the initial time, and we now enforce it locally. We compare the time evolution of the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields as well as the transverse and longitudinal pressures in the 3+1D case with the boost invariant result.

## Key claim
The authors generalize the 2+1D boost-invariant IP-Glasma initial state model to 3+1D by introducing non-zero rapidity gradients and a local solution of Gauss' law, but note that the JIMWLK-evolved effective source terms are not yet included in Gauss' law enforcement, leaving this for future work.

## Method
The 2+1D IP-Glasma initial conditions are extended to 3+1D by adding a non-zero $\eta$-component to the pre-collision gauge fields, derived from the same pure-gauge Wilson line formalism. The initial longitudinal electric field is unchanged from the 2+1D case, while the transverse electric fields are determined by solving Gauss' law as a covariant Poisson equation via the Jacobi method, using an ansatz $E^i=[D^i,\phi]$. Rapidity dependence is introduced through JIMWLK renormalization group evolution of the pre-collision Wilson lines, implemented via a Langevin step formulation.

## Key numbers
- The initial transverse chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields contribute to the energy density with a factor of $1/\tau^2$, causing them to dominate at early times in 3+1D.
- By typical hydrodynamic initialization times of $\tau=0.2-0.6$ fm, the 3+1D field components all have similar contributions to the energy density, matching the 2+1D behavior.
- In the $\tau \to 0^+$ limit, the longitudinal pressure to energy ratio $P_L/\epsilon$ approaches 1 in 3+1D versus -1 in 2+1D, while the transverse pressure ratio $P_\perp/\epsilon$ approaches 0 in 3+1D versus 1 in 2+1D.
- The pressure isotropy condition ($\epsilon/3$) is reached at the intersection of longitudinal and transverse pressures, but the system then approaches the 2+1D asymptotic behavior for large $\tau$ as longitudinal pressure free-streams toward zero.

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma framework of Schenke (2012) from 2+1D to 3+1D, building on the JIMWLK rapidity evolution approach first applied to IP-Glasma by Schenke (2016). It differs from prior 3+1D CYM studies by Schenke (2016), Gelis (2013), Romatschke (2006), Gelfand (2016), Kapusta (2016), and Shen (2017) which used 2+1D boost-invariant initial conditions either directly or as a background for perturbations; here the initial conditions themselves are generalized to consistently accommodate non-boost-invariant systems. The work enables future studies of longitudinal dynamics in heavy-ion collisions and provides a framework for including JIMWLK-evolved source terms in Gauss' law.
