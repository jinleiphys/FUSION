# Extended Skyrme interactions for transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions

> Based on an extended Skyrme interaction that includes the terms in relative momenta up to sixth order, corresponding to the so-called Skyrme pseudopotential up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N3LO), we derive the expressions of Hamiltonian density and single nucleon potential under general non-equilibrium conditions which can be applied in transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei. While the conventional Skyrme interactions, which include the terms in relative momenta up to second order, predict an incorrect behavior as a function of energy for nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter, the present extended N3LO Skyrme interaction can give a nice description for the empirical nucleon optical potential. We also construct three interaction sets with different high-density behaviors of the symmetry energy, by fitting both the empirical nucleon optical potential up to energy of $1$ GeV and the empirical properties of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. These extended N3LO Skyrme interactions will be useful in transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate and high energies, and they can also be useful in nuclear structure studies within the mean-field model.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that an extended Skyrme interaction including terms up to sixth order in relative momenta (N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential) can reproduce the empirical nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV, overcoming the failure of conventional Skyrme interactions (which only include terms up to second order) that predict an incorrect energy dependence above about 300 MeV/nucleon.

## Method
The authors derived analytic expressions for the Hamiltonian density and isospin- and momentum-dependent single nucleon potential under general non-equilibrium conditions using the Hartree-Fock approximation with the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential. They then constructed three new parameter sets (SP6s, SP6m, SP6h) by fitting to the empirical nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV and to empirical properties of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, with additional constraints on the symmetry energy at high density (0.5 fm⁻³) to produce soft, moderate, and hard high-density behaviors.

## Key numbers
- The empirical nucleon optical potential is fitted up to nucleon kinetic energy of 1 GeV (corresponding to nucleon momentum up to 1.5 GeV/c).
- Three interaction sets are constructed with symmetry energy constraints at ρ_h = 0.5 fm⁻³: E_sym(ρ_h) = 0.0 ± 1.0 MeV (SP6s), 40.0 ± 1.0 MeV (SP6m), and 80.0 ± 1.0 MeV (SP6h).
- The error bars used in fitting are 3.16 MeV for U_0(p,ρ_0) and 5 MeV for U_sym,1(p,ρ).
- The conventional Skyrme interactions fail above about 300 MeV/nucleon incident energy.

## Context
This work extends the conventional Skyrme interactions (e.g., Vautherin and Brink (1972), Chabanat et al. (1997, 1998)) which are limited to second-order momentum dependence and cannot describe heavy-ion collisions above about 300 MeV/nucleon. The N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential was previously developed by Carlsson et al. (2008) and Raimondi et al. (2011) for nuclear structure studies. The new interactions enable consistent treatment of both finite nuclei properties and heavy-ion collisions up to 1 GeV/nucleon incident energy within the same effective interaction framework, and allow transport model simulations to probe nuclear matter up to about 3ρ₀.
