# Skewness of nuclear matter and three-particle correlations

> We present a study of the skewness of nuclear matter, which is proportional to the third derivative of the energy per nucleon with respect to the baryon density at the saturation point, in the framework of the Landau-Migdal theory. We derive an exact relation between the skewness, the nucleon effective mass, and two-particle and three-particle interaction parameters. We also present qualitative estimates, which indicate that three-particle correlations play an important role for the skewness.

## Key claim

The skewness of nuclear matter, defined as the third derivative of the energy per nucleon with respect to baryon density at saturation, is exactly related to the nucleon effective mass, its momentum slope, and two-particle and three-particle Landau-Migdal interaction parameters; qualitative estimates indicate that three-particle correlations, beyond what can be captured by effective density-dependent two-particle interactions, are likely necessary to reproduce the empirical range of skewness values.

## Method

The authors used the Landau-Migdal theory of nuclear matter, extending Landau's Fermi liquid formula for the variation of the energy density to include third-order terms in the quasiparticle occupation number deviations. They derived an exact expression for the third derivative of the energy density by taking density derivatives of the quasiparticle energy and the two-particle forward scattering amplitude, using Galilei invariance to eliminate the derivative of the two-particle amplitude in favor of the effective mass and three-particle amplitudes. The three-particle forward scattering amplitude was split into two-particle correlation (2pc) and three-particle correlation (3pc) contributions, and the 2pc piece was estimated using a contact interaction approximation for the in-medium t-matrix.

## Key numbers

* The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is taken as rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}.
* The empirical range for the incompressibility K is 0.20 GeV < K < 0.30 GeV.
* The empirical range for the skewness J is -0.50 GeV < J < -0.01 GeV.
* The Fermi gas value for the skewness is J_free = -10 K_free, approximately -2.2 GeV, which is much larger in magnitude than the empirical range.
* The empirical range for the effective mass ratio M*/M at saturation is 0.7 < M*/M < 1.0.
* The inequality derived from the lower limits of K and J requires (M/M*)(H_0 - H_1) > 1.24, where H_0 and H_1 are dimensionless three-body Landau-Migdal parameters.
* The estimated contribution from the two-particle correlation piece alone gives (M/M*) H_0^{(2pc)} between 0.75 and 1.23, based on Landau-Migdal parameters from Skyrme and chiral effective field theory calculations.

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Migdal theory, previously applied to the incompressibility and two-particle interactions (Migdal 1967, Migdal 1990, Kamerdzhiev 2003), to directly relate the skewness to three-particle interaction parameters, rather than relying on effective density-dependent two-particle interactions as in Skyrme-type models (Chen 2009). Empirical constraints on K and J were taken from analyses of heavy ion collision flow data and neutron star observations (Steiner 2010, Cai 2014). The results motivate more quantitative studies of three-particle correlations using, for example, the Faddeev method in the framework of effective field theories, building on the long history of three-particle correlation studies in nuclear matter (Bethe 1965, Day 1981, Fritsch 2004).
