# Neutron capture cross sections of radioactive nuclei

> Alternative methods to calculate neutron capture cross sections on radioactive nuclei are reported using the theory of Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) developed by Hussein and McVoy [1]. The statistical coupled-channels theory proposed in Ref. [2] is further extended in the realm of random matrices. The case of reactions with the projectile and the target being two-cluster nuclei is also analyzed and applications are made for scattering from a deuteron target [3]. An extension of the theory to a three-cluster projectile incident on a two-cluster target is also discussed. The theoretical developments described here should open new possibilities to obtain information on the neutron capture cross sections of radioactive nuclei using indirect methods.

## Key claim

The paper presents alternative methods to calculate neutron capture cross sections on radioactive nuclei using the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory, but the authors hedge that the theoretical developments "should open new possibilities" rather than claiming definitive results; the method requires that the neutron capture cross section be inferred from the difference between the total reaction cross section and other direct reaction contributions, such as inelastic excitation.

## Method

The authors apply the INEB theory developed by Hussein and McVoy (1985) to reactions of the type a + A → p + (n + A), where the differential cross section for the proton is proportional to the total reaction cross section of the neutron with the target, including medium corrections. They extend the statistical coupled-channels theory proposed in Ref. [BDH14] using random matrix methods to treat the large number of weakly-coupled channels, and they analyze the case of two-cluster projectiles on two-cluster targets, specifically considering deuteron targets and three-cluster projectiles on two-cluster targets.

## Key numbers

- The thermal neutron capture cross section of n+¹³⁵Xe is 2.65 × 10⁶ barns, the largest known cross section for neutron induced reactions.
- For the (d,p) reaction on ¹³⁵Xe at effective deuteron energies of 30 MeV in inverse kinematics, the calculated neutron absorption cross section from the s-state of the deuteron is 7.02 × 10² mb and from the d-state is 2.85 × 10² mb.
- For ¹⁵⁷Gd, the thermal neutron capture cross section is 2.54 × 10⁵ barns, and the calculated (d,p) cross section at 30 MeV is 7.31 × 10¹ mb (s-state) and 17.3 mb (d-state).
- For ⁸⁸Zr, the thermal neutron absorption cross section is 8.61 × 10⁵ barns, and the calculated (d,p) cross section at 30 MeV is 1.54 × 10² mb (s-state) and 0.37 × 10² mb (d-state).
- The inclusive proton spectrum from reactions with one-neutron halo nuclei is anticipated to show a low energy peak at 2.22 MeV and a weaker peak at approximately 5.00 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the INEB theory originally developed by Hussein and McVoy (1985) and Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985) to radioactive nuclei and multi-cluster reactions, building on the statistical coupled-channels approach of Bertulani, Descouvemont, and Hussein (2014). The method provides an alternative to the surrogate method (Escher 2012), the Trojan horse method (Baur 1986), and the ANC method (Mukhamedzhanov 2001) for extracting neutron capture cross sections on unstable nuclei, and it enables indirect measurements using radioactive beams at facilities such as those studying ¹³⁵Xe as a benchmark for neutron poison reactions.
