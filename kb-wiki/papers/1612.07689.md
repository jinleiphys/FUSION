# Electric Properties of One-Neutron Halo Nuclei in Halo EFT

> We exploit the separation of scales in weakly-bound nuclei to compute E2 transitions and electric form factors in a Halo EFT framework. The relevant degrees of freedom are the core and the halo neutron. The EFT expansion is carried out in powers of $R_{core}/R_{halo}$, where $R_{core}$ and $R_{halo}$ denote the length scales of the core and halo, respectively. We include the strong $s$-wave and $d$-wave interactions by introducing dimer fields. The dimer propagators are regulated by employing the power divergence subtraction scheme and matched to the effective range expansion in the respective channel.Electromagnetic interactions are included via minimal substitution in the Lagrangian. We demonstrate that, depending on the observable and respective partial wave, additional local gauge-invariant operators contribute in LO, NLO and higher orders.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that electric properties of one-neutron halo nuclei can be computed in Halo EFT with a controlled expansion in powers of \(R_{core}/R_{halo}\), but for \(^{15}\)C this ratio is only approximately 0.3–0.45, so effects beyond leading order are not negligible and additional local gauge-invariant operators contribute at LO, NLO, and higher orders depending on the observable and partial wave.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic Halo EFT Lagrangian with a \(^{14}\)C core field and a neutron field, introducing auxiliary dimer fields for the \(s\)-wave (\(1/2^+\)) and \(d\)-wave (\(5/2^+\)) bound states of \(^{15}\)C. The dimer propagators are regulated using the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme and matched to the effective range expansion (ERE) in each channel, requiring three ERE parameters (\(a_2, r_2, \mathcal{P}_2\)) at LO for the \(d\)-wave due to renormalization of divergences. Electromagnetic interactions are included via minimal substitution, and the irreducible vertices for electric form factors and E2 transitions are computed up to NLO in the Breit frame and Coulomb gauge, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of the \(1/2^+\) state of \(^{15}\)C: 1218 keV; neutron separation energy of the \(5/2^+\) state: 478 keV.
- First excitation of \(^{14}\)C: 6.1 MeV above the \(0^+\) ground state.
- Expansion parameter \(R_{core}/R_{halo} \approx 0.3\)–\(0.45\) for \(^{15}\)C.
- Experimental E2 transition strength: B(E2) = 0.97(2) \(e^2\) fm\(^4\) (or 0.44(1) Weisskopf units).
- Extracted value of \(1/(r_2 + \mathcal{P}_2 \gamma_2^2) = -323(7)\) fm\(^3\).
- Predicted hexadecupole moment: \(\mu_H^{(d)} = 0.030\) \(e\) fm\(^4\).
- Predicted quadrupole radius: \(\langle r_Q^2 \rangle^{(d)} = -0.578\) fm\(^4\).
- Predicted hexadecupole radius: \(\langle r_H^2 \rangle^{(d)} = 0.004\) fm\(^6\).

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT approach of Hammer and collaborators (2011) for electric properties of \(^{11}\)Be to the \(^{15}\)C system, which features a \(d\)-wave excited state rather than a \(p\)-wave state. It builds on prior calculations of electromagnetic form factors for \(^{15}\)C by Fernando and collaborators (2015) and radiative neutron capture on \(^{14}\)C by Rupak and collaborators (2012). The results enable predictions for the hexadecupole moment and quadrupole radius of the \(d\)-wave state, and the authors plan to investigate the correlations found in Halo EFT using ab initio calculations.
