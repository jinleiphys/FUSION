# Ab initio Leading Order Effective Potentials for Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Scattering

> Background: Calculating microscopic effective interactions (optical potentials) for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering has already in the past led to a large body of work. For first-order calculations a nucleon-nucleon (\textit{NN}) interaction and a one-body density of the nucleus were taken as input to rigorous calculations of microscopic full-folding calculations. Purpose: Based on the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series we employ a chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) nucleon-nucleon interaction on the same footing in the structure as well as in the reaction calculation to obtain an in leading-order consistent effective potential for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering, which includes the spin of the struck target nucleon. Methods: The first order effective folding potential is computed by first deriving a nonlocal scalar density as well as a spin-projected momentum distribution. Those are then integrated with the off-shell Wolfenstein amplitudes $A$, $C$, and $M$. The resulting nonlocal potential serves as input to a momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation, whose solutions are summed to obtain the nucleon-nucleus scattering observables. Results: We calculate elastic scattering observables for $^4$He, $^6$He, $^8$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O in the energy regime between 100 and 200 MeV projectile kinetic energy, and compare to available data. We also explore the extension down to about 70 MeV, and study the effect of ignoring the spin of the struck nucleon in the nucleus. Conclusions: In our calculations we contrast elastic scattering off closed-shell and open-shell nuclei. We find that for closed-shell nuclei the approximation of ignoring the spin of the struck target nucleon is excellent. We only see effects of the spin of the struck target nucleon when considering $^6$He and $^8$He, which are nuclei with a $N/Z$ ratio larger than 1.

## Key claim

For elastic scattering off closed-shell nuclei, the traditional approximation of ignoring the spin of the struck target nucleon is excellent; effects of that spin only appear for nuclei with a neutron-to-proton ratio larger than 1, such as \(^6\)He and \(^8\)He, and even there the effect is small and noticeable only at higher momentum transfers.

## Method

The first-order effective folding potential was computed by deriving a nonlocal scalar density and a spin-projected momentum distribution from no-core shell-model (NCSM) wavefunctions, then integrating those with the off-shell Wolfenstein amplitudes \(A\), \(C\), and \(M\) obtained from the chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) nucleon-nucleon interaction. The resulting nonlocal potential was used as input to a momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation, whose solutions were summed to obtain nucleon-nucleus scattering observables for \(^4\)He, \(^6\)He, \(^8\)He, \(^{12}\)C, and \(^{16}\)O.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering observables were calculated for projectile kinetic energies between 100 and 200 MeV, with extension down to about 70 MeV.
- For \(^4\)He, NCSM calculations used \(N_{\rm max}=18\) and \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV; the predicted charge radius was about 1.66 fm compared to the experimental value of 1.68 fm (Angeli 2013).
- For \(^{16}\)O, \(N_{\rm max}=10\) and \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV were used; the predicted charge radius was about 2.39 fm versus the experimental value of 2.70 fm (Angeli 2013).
- For \(^6\)He, the predicted charge radius was 1.95 fm compared to the experimental value of 2.07 fm (Angeli 2013); for \(^8\)He, 1.90 fm compared to 1.92 fm (Angeli 2013).
- For \(^{12}\)C, the predicted charge radius was 2.35 fm compared to the experimental value of 2.47 fm (Angeli 2013), using \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV.
- The NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interaction was fitted for NN laboratory energies up to 125 MeV, with \(\chi^2/{\rm datum} \approx 2\) for np scattering between 125 and 183 MeV and \(\approx 24\) for pp scattering (Ekstrom 2013).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order multiple-scattering approach of Burrows (2018) and Gennari (2017) by including the spin of the struck target nucleon on the same footing as the structure calculation, using a chiral NNLO interaction consistently in both parts. Prior calculations (e.g., Elster 1996, Chinn 1994) ignored this spin contribution, an approximation now shown to be valid for closed-shell nuclei. The results enable future studies of spin-dependent effects in elastic scattering from neutron-rich nuclei and provide a framework for incorporating three-nucleon forces consistently.
