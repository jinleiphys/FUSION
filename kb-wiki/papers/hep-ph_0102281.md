# The Spin Structure of the Nucleon: Theoretical Overview

> I review what is known about the quark and gluon spin distributions in the nucleon. I discuss in some detail (a) the existence of sum rules for angular momentum; (b) the interpretation and possible measurement of the nucleon's transversity distributions; and (c) the uses of spin-dependent fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The nucleon spin can be decomposed into quark and gluon spin and orbital angular momentum contributions, but the "classic" sum rule that expresses each term as an integral over a measurable parton x-distribution exists only for the light-front plus-component of the angular momentum tensor, and the orbital terms in that sum rule are not experimentally accessible.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical status of polarized parton distribution functions in QCD, analyzing the operator structure of the energy-momentum and angular momentum tensors in both time-component and light-front plus-component formulations. He examines the gauge invariance, interaction dependence, and measurability of each term in the angular momentum sum rule, and discusses the transversity distribution and spin-dependent fragmentation functions as probes of chiral symmetry breaking and novel parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken sum rule theoretical evaluation gives 0.181 ± 0.003 at Q² = 5 GeV², while the experimental value extracted by SMC is 0.174 with statistical error ±0.05, systematic errors +0.011/-0.009, and theoretical errors +0.021/-0.006 at the same Q².
- The integrated quark spin accounts for only about 30% of the nucleon's spin, as discovered by the European Muon Collaboration in 1987.
- The Bjorken sum rule includes QCD corrections through order αs³/π³ with coefficients -43/12 for αs²/π² and -20.215 for αs³/π³.

## Context

This theoretical overview was presented at the 2000 EPIC workshop and summarizes the state of QCD spin physics following the EMC discovery (Ashman 1988) that quarks carry only about 30% of the proton's spin. The paper extends the analysis of angular momentum sum rules from Jaffe and Manohar (1990) and Ji (1997), and discusses transversity first introduced by Ralston and Soper (1979) for Drell-Yan processes. The work enables future measurements of the polarized gluon distribution Δg via direct channels at RHIC and through evolution, and motivates measurements of transversity using chiral-odd fragmentation functions as filters, following the Hermes asymmetry reported by Airapetian (2000).
