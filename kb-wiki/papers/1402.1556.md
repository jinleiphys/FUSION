# Nonlocal energy density functionals for low-energy nuclear structure

> We introduce a finite-range pseudopotential built as an expansion in derivatives up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) and we calculate the corresponding nonlocal energy density functional (EDF). The coupling constants of the nonlocal EDF, for both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, are expressed through the parameters of the pseudopotential. All central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms of the pseudopotential are derived both in the spherical-tensor and Cartesian representation. At next-to-leading order (NLO), we also derive relations between the nonlocal EDF expressed in the spherical-tensor and Cartesian formalism. Finally, a simplified version of the finite-range pseudopotential is considered, which generates the EDF identical to that generated by a local potential.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a finite-range pseudopotential expanded in derivatives up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and derives the corresponding nonlocal energy density functional (EDF), with the coupling constants expressed through the pseudopotential parameters for both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. The authors note that at next-to-leading order (NLO) they derive relations between the nonlocal EDF expressed in spherical-tensor and Cartesian formalisms, and they also present a simplified version that generates an EDF identical to that from a local potential.

## Method

The authors constructed a finite-range pseudopotential as an expansion in derivatives up to N3LO (sixth order) and derived the corresponding nonlocal energy density functional. All central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms were derived in both spherical-tensor and Cartesian representations. The coupling constants of the nonlocal EDF for finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter were expressed through the parameters of the pseudopotential, and explicit expressions were provided for the coupling constants at zero, second, fourth, and sixth orders.

## Key numbers

- 444 fourth-order isoscalar and isovector coupling constants of the nonlocal EDF are expressed by 30 fourth-order finite-range pseudopotential parameters.
- The pseudopotential expansion includes terms up to N3LO (sixth order in derivatives), with explicit expressions given for zero, second, fourth, and sixth orders.
- At NLO (second order), relations between the nonlocal EDF in spherical-tensor and Cartesian formalisms are derived.

## Context

This work extends the development of energy density functionals for nuclear structure calculations by introducing a finite-range pseudopotential approach up to N3LO, going beyond the local Skyrme-type functionals commonly used in the field. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of nonlocal effects in nuclear EDFs, which can improve descriptions of nuclear properties such as pairing and surface effects. The explicit relations between coupling constants and pseudopotential parameters provide a framework for future fitting to nuclear data and for connecting with ab initio calculations, building on prior work by the authors and others on Skyrme functionals and finite-range interactions.
