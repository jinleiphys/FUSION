# Quadrupole and octupole collectivity and cluster structures in neon isotopes

> The lowest positive- and negative-parity bands of $^{20}$Ne and neutron-rich even-even Ne isotopes are investigated using a theoretical framework based on energy density functionals. Starting from a self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation of axially-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric deformation energy surfaces, the collective symmetry-conserving states are built using projection techniques and the generator coordinate method. Overall a good agreement with the experimental excitation energies and transition rates is obtained. In particular, the model provides an accurate description of the excitation spectra and transition probabilities in $^{20}$Ne. The contribution of cluster configurations to the low-energy states is discussed, as well as the transitional character of the ground state. The analysis is extended to $^{22}$Ne and the shape-coexisting isotope $^{24}$Ne, and to the drip-line nuclei $^{32}$Ne and $^{34}$Ne. The role of valence neutrons in the formation of molecular-type bonds between clusters is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a beyond-mean-field framework based on a universal relativistic energy density functional (DD-PC1), without any parameter adjusted specifically to the nuclei under study, provides a good quantitative description of low-energy spectroscopic properties and cluster structures across the neon isotopic chain from $^{20}$Ne to the drip-line nucleus $^{34}$Ne, but the model fails to reproduce the experimentally observed large ground-state quadrupole deformation in $^{30}$Ne because the functional predicts a persistent $N=20$ neutron shell closure.

## Method

Starting from self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations of axially-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric deformation energy surfaces in the $\beta_2-\beta_3$ plane, the authors performed angular momentum and parity projection on the mean-field states, followed by generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing to build collective symmetry-conserving states. The relativistic functional DD-PC1 was used for the particle-hole channel, with a separable pairing force adjusted to reproduce the Gogny D1S pairing properties. The Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov equations were solved by expanding nucleon spinors in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with $N_{sh}=10$ major shells for large components and $N_{sh}=11$ for small components.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state binding energy of $^{20}$Ne: $-162.49$ MeV (with cut-off parameter $\zeta = 5 \times 10^{-3}$)
* Ground-state binding energy of $^{34}$Ne: $-224.43$ MeV (with cut-off parameter $\zeta = 5 \times 10^{-3}$)
* $^{24}$Ne exhibits nearly-degenerate oblate and prolate minima on the mean-field level, separated in energy by only $240$ keV
* $^{34}$Ne is predicted to be the last stable neon isotope; $^{36}$Ne has negative two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n} = -1.16$ MeV and negative four-neutron separation energy $S_{4n} = -0.58$ MeV
* Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the $2_1^+$ state in $^{20}$Ne: $-16.61$ e fm$^2$ (experimental value: $-23 \pm 3$ e fm$^2$)
* Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the $2_1^+$ state in $^{22}$Ne: $-15.01$ e fm$^2$ (experimental value: $-19 \pm 4$ e fm$^2$)
* The RHB model predicts prolate equilibrium minima with $\beta_2 \approx 0.5$ for $^{20}$Ne and $^{22}$Ne
* For $^{32}$Ne and $^{34}$Ne, the RHB model predicts prolate minima with $\beta_2 = 0.33$ and $\beta_2 = 0.44$, respectively
* The average $\langle |\beta_3| \rangle$ values in the correlated ground states vary between $0.30$ and $0.35$ across the isotopic chain, with octupole collectivity most pronounced in $^{20}$Ne

## Context

This work extends the energy density functional (EDF) framework for studying cluster structures beyond the mean-field level, building on previous studies of clustering in light nuclei using EDFs by Ebran (2017) and the anti-stretching mechanism in $^{20}$Ne by Zhou (2016). The results are compared with the angular momentum-projected GCM study based on the Gogny D1S effective interaction by Rodriguez (2003), which predicted $^{34}$Ne to be unstable against two-neutron emission, contrary to the present finding. The approach enables systematic studies of cluster configurations and shape coexistence across isotopic chains using a universal functional, though the failure to reproduce the $^{30}$Ne ground-state deformation highlights the need for functionals that correctly describe the breakdown of the $N=20$ shell closure, as experimentally established by Sorlin (2008).
