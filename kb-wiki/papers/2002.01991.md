# Evolution of the N=20 and 28 Shell Gaps and 2-particle-2-hole states in the FSU Interaction

> The FSU $spsdfp$ cross-shell interaction for the shell model was successfully fitted to a wide range of mostly intruder negative parity states of the $sd$ shell nuclei. This paper reports the application of the FSU interaction to systematically trace out the relative positions of the effective single-particle energies of the $0f_{7/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$ orbitals, the evolution from normally ordered low-lying states to the "Island of Inversion" (IoI), and the behavior of a wide range of excited states with a $0f_{7/2}$ proton and neutron coupled to maximum spin of $7 \hbar$. Above a proton number of about 13 the $0f_{7/2}$ orbital lies below that of $1p_{3/2}$, which is considered normal ordering, but systematically at $Z = 10$ to $12$ the orbitals cross. The calculations reproduce well the 2p2h - 0p0h inversion in the configurations of nuclei inside the IoI, they reproduce the absolute binding energies and the transition to normal ordering as the proton number approaches that of the neutrons. The important role of $1p_{3/2}$ neutron pairs in the IoI is also demonstrated. The calculations account well for the energies of the fully aligned states with 0, 1, or 2 individual $sd$ nucleon aligned in spin with the aligned $π0f_{7/2}$ - $ν0f_{7/2}$ pair and reproduce well their systematic variation with $A$ and number of aligned $sd$ nucleons. The results presented in this paper give hope for the predictive power of the FSU interaction for more exotic nuclei to be explored in near future.

## Key claim

The FSU interaction reproduces the inversion of 2p2h and 0p0h configurations in the Island of Inversion (IoI) and the transition to normal ordering as proton number increases, but the crossing of the neutron $0f_{7/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$ effective single-particle energies occurs between Z=10 and Z=12, while the crossing of the lowest $7/2^-$ and $3/2^-$ states occurs between Z=12 and Z=14, showing the relative orbital positions are more nuanced than previously expected.

## Method

The authors fitted 70 parameters (single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements) to 270 experimentally observed states in nuclei from $^{13}$C through $^{51}$Ti and $^{49}$V, using the WBP interaction as a starting point and the USDB interaction for the $sd$ shell. The fit was performed within an $N_{\rm max}=1$ harmonic oscillator basis truncation (including $0\hbar\omega$ and $1\hbar\omega$ excitations), and the resulting root-mean-square deviation from experiment was 190 keV. Effective single-particle energies were computed using a spectroscopic-factor-weighted sum over the 30 lowest states, and 2p2h states were calculated separately without mixing with the fitted 0p0h states.

## Key numbers

- Overall rms deviation from experiment for the fit: 190 keV
- For $^{31}$Na ($N=20$), the lowest 0p0h state ($5/2^+$) lies approximately 2.5 MeV higher in excitation energy than the 2p2h ground state
- For $N=20$ isotones, the rms deviation between experimental binding energies and FSU predictions (using 2p2h for Z<13 and 0p0h for higher Z) is 276 keV
- The FSU interaction predicts the $0^+_2$ state in $^{34}$Si at 2432 keV (experimental value 2719 keV)
- The FSU interaction predicts the $0^+_2$ states in $^{36}$S and $^{38}$Ar at 3373 keV and 3140 keV respectively (experimental values 3346 keV and 3376 keV)
- For $^{30}$Ne and $^{32}$Mg, the calculated B(E2) transition strengths from the lowest $2^+$ to ground state exceed 400 e$^2$fm$^4$
- The FSU interaction predicts $^{29}$F as an IoI nucleus with a 2p2h ground state 2201 keV more bound than the 0p0h configuration

## Context

This work extends the WBP interaction (Warburton and Brown, 1992) and its modified versions WBP-A, WBP-B, and WBP-M, which were each successful only for limited regions of the $sd$-$fp$ cross-shell space. The FSU interaction improves upon the WBMB interaction (Warburton, Becker, Millener, and Brown, 1986) by fitting a much broader dataset and using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian (Honma et al., 2004) for the $fp$ shell. The results enable systematic predictions for exotic nuclei near the neutron drip line around N=20 and N=28, and provide a tool for interpreting future experiments on high-spin states and intruder configurations in neutron-rich nuclei.
