# Sum Rules from an Extra Dimension

> Using the gravity side of the AdS/CFT correspondence, we investigate the analytic properties of thermal retarded Green's functions for scalars, conserved currents, the stress tensor, and massless fermions. We provide some results concerning their large and small frequency behavior and their pole structure. From these results, it is straightforward to prove the validity of various sum rules on the field theory side of the duality. We introduce a novel contraction mapping we use to study the large frequency behavior of the Green's functions.

## Key claim

Using the gravity side of the AdS/CFT correspondence, the authors prove that thermal retarded Green's functions for scalars, conserved currents, the stress tensor, and massless fermions satisfy sum rules, provided certain technical assumptions hold (such as positivity of a potential term Y and the absence of instabilities that would place quasinormal modes in the upper half of the complex frequency plane).

## Method

The authors study a class of asymptotically anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetimes with a black hole horizon, dual to thermal field theories in d dimensions. For bosonic operators (scalars, currents, stress tensor), they analyze a single second-order ordinary differential equation (Eq. 2) that governs the bulk fluctuations, using ingoing boundary conditions at the horizon. They introduce a novel contraction mapping (Eq. contractmap) to bound the large-frequency behavior of the Green's functions, and they use a Wronskian argument to prove the absence of poles on the real frequency axis (except possibly at zero) and to establish positivity of the spectral function. For massless fermions, they perform a separate but analogous analysis using a contraction-like map.

## Key numbers

- For the Wronskian at the horizon: \( r^n F (A' \bar A - \bar A' A) = -i \omega r_h^n \) (Eq. horizonW), where \( r_h \) is the horizon radius and \( n \) depends on the operator type.
- The large-\( r \) expansion of the metric function \( F \) is given by \( F \sim r^2 \left( 1 - p r^{-d} + o(r^{-d}) \right) \) (Eq. fexpansion), where \( p \) is related to the pressure of the field theory.
- For the spectral density of bosonic operators: \( \text{Im} \, G_R(\omega) \sim \frac{\omega}{2} \lim_{r\to \infty} \frac{r_h^n}{|A|^2} \) (Eq. bosonicOne), showing positivity for \( \omega > 0 \).

## Context

This work extends prior holographic sum rule studies, such as those by Romatschke and Son (2009) for the stress tensor of \( \mathcal{N}=4 \) Super Yang-Mills theory, by Hartnoll et al. (2008) for a holographic superconductor, and by Baier et al. (2009) for R-charge correlators. The authors provide a more rigorous and general gravitational derivation of these sum rules, showing how causality and stability on the gravity side enforce the sum rules on the field theory side. The results enable a systematic understanding of sum rules for thermal Green's functions in strongly coupled field theories via holography, and they suggest future applications to systems with nonzero charge density or superfluid phases.
