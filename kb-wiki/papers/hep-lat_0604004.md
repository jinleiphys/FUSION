# Deconfinement in dense 2-color QCD

> We study SU(2) lattice gauge theory with two flavors of Wilson fermion at non-zero chemical potential mu and low temperature on a 8^3x16 system. We identify three regimes along the mu-axis. For mu<~m_pi/2 the system remains in the vacuum phase, and all physical observables considered remain essentially unchanged. The intermediate regime is characterised by a non-zero diquark condensate and an associated increase in the baryon density, consistent with what is expected for Bose-Einstein condensation of tightly bound diquarks. We also observe screening of the static quark potential here. In the high-density deconfined regime we find a non-zero Polyakov loop and a strong modification of the gluon propagator, including significant screening in the magnetic sector in the static limit, which must have a non-perturbative origin. The behaviour of thermodynamic observables and the superfluid order parameter are consistent with a Fermi surface disrupted by a BCS diquark condensate. The energy per baryon as a function of mu exhibits a minimum in the deconfined regime, implying that macroscopic objects such as stars formed in this theory are largely composed of quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper identifies three distinct regimes along the chemical potential axis in dense 2-color QCD, but the abstract's claim of a "deconfined regime" is hedged: the Polyakov loop becomes non-zero and the gluon propagator shows strong modification only for chemical potentials above approximately 0.7–0.8 in lattice units, and the authors note that the screening in the magnetic sector "must have a non-perturbative origin," meaning the deconfinement signal is not simply perturbative.

## Method

The authors performed lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) with two flavors of Wilson fermion on an 8³×16 lattice at β=1.7 and κ=0.178, using a hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with a diquark source term J to lift low-lying eigenmodes. They computed the quark number density, quark energy density, diquark condensate, Polyakov loop, and gluon energy density as functions of chemical potential μ, with diquark source strengths j=0.02, 0.04, and 0.06, and extrapolated to j→0 where possible. The lattice spacing was determined from the static quark potential fit to the Cornell potential, yielding a=0.223(4) fm from the string tension σa²=0.218(8).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of 2-color QCD using staggered fermions (Hands 2000, Hands 2001, Kogut 2002) and Wilson fermions (Muroya 2002) by providing a systematic investigation of the phase structure along the chemical potential axis. The use of Wilson fermions avoids the theoretical issues with staggered fermions, such as the different Goldstone spectrum and the proximity to the Banks–Zaks threshold. The results enable future studies of the deconfinement mechanism in dense matter, particularly the non-perturbative screening in the magnetic gluon sector, and provide a benchmark for comparing with effective field theory predictions from chiral perturbation theory (Kogut 2000) and the Stefan–Boltzmann free quark gas.
