# The Quenching of the Axial Coupling in Nuclear and Neutron-Star Matter

> Using a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian, we calculate the density and isospin dependences of the in-medium axial coupling, $g_A^*$, in spatially uniform matter present in core collapse supernovae and neutron stars. The quenching of $g_A^*$ with density in matter with different proton fractions is found to be similar. However, our results suggest that the quenching of the nucleon's $g_A^*$ in matter with hyperons is likely to be significantly greater than in matter with nucleons only.

# The Quenching of the Axial Coupling in Nuclear and Neutron-Star Matter

## Key claim

Using a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian at the mean-field level, the authors calculate that the in-medium axial coupling \(g_A^*\) is quenched similarly in matter with different proton fractions (isospin symmetric, pure neutron, and beta-stable neutron-star matter), but suggest that quenching is likely to be significantly greater in matter containing hyperons than in matter with nucleons only. The result is a prediction within the confines of the adopted chiral model, not a direct measurement.

## Method

The authors employ a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian with nucleons, scalar (\(\sigma\)), pseudoscalar (\(\pi\)), vector (\(\omega\), \(\rho\)), and axial-vector (\(a_1\)) mesons at the mean-field level, solving the equations of motion for the non-zero expectation values of \(\sigma\), \(\omega_0\), and \(\rho_0^3\) in spatially uniform matter. The in-medium axial coupling \(g_A^*\) is derived from the axial current via Eq. (6), with density dependence entering solely through the scalar mean field \(\bar\sigma\), which depends on the proton fraction. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce vacuum \(g_A = 1.26\), nuclear matter saturation at \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(E/A = -16\) MeV, and the empirical symmetry energy of about 30 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum axial coupling: \(|g_A/g_V| = 1.2601 \pm 0.0025\) from neutron beta decay.
- Nuclear matter saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Energy per baryon at saturation: \(E/A = -16\) MeV.
- Effective mass at saturation: \(M^* = 0.68 M\).
- Compression modulus: \(K \cong 320\) MeV.
- Symmetry energy: \(\cong 30\) MeV.
- Quenching of \(g_A^*\): 12% drop at \(n_0\) and 19% drop at \(4n_0\) relative to vacuum.
- Maximum neutron-star mass from the model's equation of state: \(M_{\text{max}}/M_\odot = 1.6\) for beta-stable matter, with central density \(n_c/n_0 \cong 7.4\) and radius \(R = 10.24\) km.
- For pure neutron matter: \(M_{\text{max}}/M_\odot = 2.2\), \(n_c/n_0 \cong 5.3\), \(R = 12.74\) km.
- Parametrization of \(g_A^*\): \(g_A^* \simeq g_A \left(1 - \frac{n_B}{4.15 (n_0 + n_B)}\right)\), accurate to within 1% for all \(n_B \le 4.5 n_0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral approaches to the quenching of \(g_A\) in isospin symmetric matter (Rho, references therein; chiral approaches in Ref. [chiral]) to isospin asymmetric and neutron-star matter, which had been largely ignored in the literature. The results are directly relevant to weak interaction rates in core collapse supernovae and neutron star cooling, where processes such as electron capture, bremsstrahlung, and modified Urca reactions depend on \(g_A^{*2}\). The authors note that the quenching mechanism (scalar field and \(\pi\)-\(a_1\) mixing) is virtually independent of isospin content, suggesting that laboratory studies of nearly symmetric nuclei can guide astrophysical modeling, but that extension to hyperonic matter (as in KPE) would likely yield stronger quenching.
