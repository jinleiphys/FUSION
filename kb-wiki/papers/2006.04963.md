# Fast neutrino cooling of nuclear pasta in neutron stars: molecular dynamics simulations

> The direct URCA process of rapid neutrino emission can occur in nonuniform nuclear pasta phases that are expected in the inner crust of neutron stars. Here, the periodic potential for a nucleon in nuclear pasta allows momentum conservation to be satisfied for direct URCA reactions. We improve on earlier work by modeling a rich variety of pasta phases (gnocchi, waffle, lasagna, and anti-spaghetti) with large-scale molecular dynamics simulations. We find that the neutrino luminosity due to direct URCA reactions in nuclear pasta can be 3 to 4 orders of magnitude larger than that from the modified URCA process in the NS core. Thus neutrino radiation from pasta could dominate radiation from the core and this could significantly impact the cooling of neutron stars

## Key claim

The direct URCA process of rapid neutrino emission can occur in nonuniform nuclear pasta phases in the inner crust of neutron stars, because the periodic potential for nucleons in pasta allows momentum conservation to be satisfied. The neutrino luminosity from direct URCA in pasta can be 3 to 4 orders of magnitude larger than that from the modified URCA process in the neutron star core, potentially dominating the core's neutrino radiation and significantly impacting neutron star cooling.

## Method

The authors used large-scale semi-classical molecular dynamics simulations (IUMD code) to model four nuclear pasta phases: gnocchi (spheres), lasagna (planar slabs), waffle (perforated slabs), and anti-spaghetti (uniform matter with cylindrical holes). They calculated the Fourier-transformed nucleon potential from 100 trajectory configurations of each pasta phase, then used perturbation theory to compute the reduction factor R(n) that determines the direct URCA neutrino emissivity in the pasta layer relative to uniform matter.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino emissivity of direct URCA in pasta is given by Q ≈ 4×10²⁷ (n_e/n_0)^(1/3) T_9⁶ erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹, where T_9 = T/10⁹ K
- The proton fraction in the pasta layer at beta equilibrium is Y_P ≈ 0.03, based on Oyamatsu (1993)
- Simulation box sizes ranged from 83.4 fm to 150.8 fm, with grid point spacing d ≈ 2 fm
- MD simulations used timesteps of 2 fm/c, with 12 total simulations: 2 gnocchi, 4 lasagna, 4 waffle, and 2 anti-spaghetti configurations
- Nucleon densities studied ranged from n = 0.015 fm⁻³ (gnocchi) to n = 0.0882 fm⁻³ (anti-spaghetti)
- Temperatures in simulations ranged from 0.8 MeV to 1.6 MeV
- Total nucleon numbers in simulations were 51,200, 102,400, or 204,800

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Gusakov et al. (2004), who used a liquid-drop model and found direct URCA emissivity in pasta only 2 orders of magnitude above modified URCA. The present molecular dynamics approach allows study of more complex pasta shapes and long-range disorder not possible with unit-cell approximations. The results suggest that neutrino cooling from pasta could dominate over core cooling in lower-mass neutron stars where direct URCA is forbidden in the core, and could produce significant bulk viscosity affecting r-mode damping in neutron stars.
