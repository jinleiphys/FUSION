# Quadrupole-octupole coupling and the onset of octupole collectivity

> Octupole deformation and collective excitations are studied within the interacting boson model. By using the results of the self-consistent mean-field calculations with a universal energy density functional, the Hamiltonian of the interacting $s$, $d$, and $f$ boson system is completely determined. A global systematic study confirms that significant octupole effects are present in actinide, lanthanide, and rare-earth nuclei corresponding to particular nucleon numbers for which octupole correlations are empirically suggested to be enhanced.

# Quadrupole-octupole coupling and the onset of octupole collectivity

## Key claim

Octupole deformation and collective excitations are confirmed in actinide, lanthanide, and rare-earth nuclei at specific nucleon numbers (34, 56, 88, 134) using a fully microscopic mapping from self-consistent mean-field calculations to the interacting boson model, but the agreement is conditional on the underlying energy density functional not being specifically tuned to produce octupole deformations, and some higher-order effects (shape coexistence, triaxiality) must still be incorporated for precise descriptions of certain regions.

## Method

The author performs self-consistent mean-field calculations using constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) with the DD-PC1 energy density functional or constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) with the Gogny-D1M interaction to obtain potential energy surfaces (PES) in the quadrupole ($\beta_2$) and octupole ($\beta_3$) deformation planes. These PES are then mapped onto the equivalent coherent-state expectation value of an $sdf$-interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian, which is subsequently diagonalized to compute excitation spectra and transition probabilities. For nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence, the $sdf$-IBM is extended to include configuration mixing of normal (0p-0h), two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h), and four-particle-four-hole (4p-4h) intruder states.

## Key numbers

- $^{226}$Th: global minimum at ($\beta_2,\beta_3$) $\approx$ (0.2, 0.15) from constrained RHB with DD-PC1 EDF; the ground-state and negative-parity bands form an alternating-parity band starting near spin $I^\pi \approx 7^-$
- U isotopes ($^{222-232}$U): most pronounced octupole minimum at $\beta_3 \approx 0.2$ for $^{226}$U or $^{228}$U; octupole deformation disappears from $^{232}$U onward
- U isotopic chain: negative-parity yrast levels show parabolic dependence on neutron number $N$ with minima around $N=134$ and a second parabolic behavior around $N=146$
- $B(E3;3^-_1 \to 0^+_1)$ values in actinides show inverse parabolic behavior with maximal values at $N \approx 134$
- Lanthanide/rare-earth region ($N \approx 88$, $Z=54-64$): octupole deformed ground state with $\beta_3 \approx 0.1$ from Gogny-HFB SCMF calculations
- Neutron-deficient $N \approx Z \approx 56$ region: octupole mean-field minimum suggested for $^{112}$Ba, $^{114}$Ba, and $^{114}$Ce
- $^{76}$Kr: three minima in PES (spherical global minimum, oblate local at $\beta_2 \approx 0.25$, prolate local at $\beta_2 \approx 0.45$); configuration mixing lowers excited $0^+$ levels dramatically
- Neutron-rich $N\approx 56$ region: lowest negative-parity states at excitation energies $\approx 1.5 - 2.0$ MeV from $sdf$-IBM, but no octupole-deformed ground state produced by SCMF

## Context

This work extends the SCMF-to-IBM mapping technique previously developed by Nomura (2008) and applied to octupole degrees of freedom by Nomura (2013, 2014) to a global systematic study across multiple mass regions. The results confirm empirical expectations for enhanced octupole collectivity at nucleon numbers 34, 56, 88, and 134, consistent with the review by Butler (2016). The framework enables future studies of more challenging cases involving shape coexistence, as demonstrated for $^{72}$Ge, $^{74}$Se, and $^{74,76}$Kr by Nomura (2022), and points to the need for incorporating additional degrees of freedom (triaxiality, tetrahedral, hexadecapole deformations) for precise descriptions of proton-rich $N \approx Z \approx 34$ and neutron-rich $N \approx 56$ nuclei.
