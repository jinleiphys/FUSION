# Evaluating transport coefficients in real time thermal field theory

> Transport coefficients in a hadronic gas have been calculated earlier in the imaginary time formulation of thermal field theory. The steps involved are to relate the defining retarded correlation function to the corresponding time-ordered one and to evaluate the latter in the conventional perturbation expansion. Here we carry out both the steps in the real time formulation.

# Evaluating transport coefficients in real time thermal field theory

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that transport coefficients in a hadronic gas can be calculated entirely within the real time formulation of thermal field theory, avoiding the analytic continuation to imaginary time required by the imaginary time formulation. The method is illustrated for shear viscosity, but the authors claim it applies to any linear response retarded two-point function.

## Method
The authors derive Källén-Lehmann spectral representations for both time-ordered and retarded bosonic two-point functions in the real time formulation, obtaining a relation between them via a change in the $i\eta$ prescription. They then compute the time-ordered correlation function of the energy-momentum tensor to leading order using skeleton expansion with complete propagators, and analytically continue it to the retarded function. The shear viscosity is extracted from the derivative of the retarded function at zero frequency.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity formula: $\eta = \frac{\beta}{10\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dl \, l^6 \frac{n(\omega)\{1+n(\omega)\}}{\omega^2 \Gamma}$, where $\omega = \sqrt{l^2 + m^2}$, $\beta$ is inverse temperature, and $\Gamma(l)$ is the pion width
- The spectral function for the complete propagator with finite width: $\rho(k_0, \mathbf{l}) = \frac{1}{i} \left[ \frac{1}{(k_0 - i\Gamma)^2 - \omega^2} - \frac{1}{(k_0 + i\Gamma)^2 - \omega^2} \right]$
- The integral over $k_0$ of the function $F(k_0, \mathbf{l})$ yields $-\frac{1}{8\pi} \frac{\beta}{\omega^2 \Gamma} n(\omega)\{1+n(\omega)\}$, retaining only the leading singular term for small $\Gamma$

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of transport coefficients in hadronic gases using the imaginary time formulation by Hosoya (1984), Lang (1993), and Jeon (1995). The authors provide a direct real-time alternative that avoids the intermediate step of analytic continuation to imaginary time. The method enables calculation of any linear response retarded two-point function in thermal field theory, and the authors note that quadratic response formulae have been derived in the real time formulation by Carrington. The numerical evaluation of shear viscosity using the derived formula relies on pion widths from chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler and by Goity.
