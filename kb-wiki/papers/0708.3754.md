# Convergence in the no-core shell model with low-momentum two-nucleon interactions

> The convergence of no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using renormalization group evolved low-momentum two-nucleon interactions is studied for light nuclei up to Li-7. Because no additional transformation was used in applying the NCSM framework, the energy calculations satisfy the variational principle for a given Hamiltonian. Dramatic improvements in convergence are found as the cutoffs are lowered. The renormalization group equations are truncated at two-body interactions, so the evolution is only approximately unitary and converged energies for A > 2 vary with the cutoff. This approximation is systematic, however, and for useful cutoff ranges the energy variation is comparable to natural-size truncation errors inherent from the initial chiral effective field theory potential.

## Key claim
The convergence of no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations is dramatically improved when using renormalization group evolved low-momentum two-nucleon interactions, but because the RG equations are truncated at two-body interactions, the evolution is only approximately unitary for A > 2 and converged energies vary with the cutoff; this variation is systematic and comparable to natural-size truncation errors from the initial chiral effective field theory potential.

## Method
The authors performed NCSM calculations for ground states and low-lying natural-parity excited states of light nuclei (³H, ⁴He, ⁶He, ⁶Li, and ⁷Li) using similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved potentials and both sharp and smooth cutoff versions of low-momentum interactions (Vlowk) derived from an N³LO chiral effective field theory two-nucleon potential. No additional transformation of the Hamiltonian was applied in the NCSM framework, so the energy calculations satisfy the variational principle for a given Hamiltonian. The SRG flow equations were evaluated for NN interactions in a partial-wave momentum basis using H0 = T, and matrix elements of the momentum-space potential were evaluated in a harmonic-oscillator basis for input to the Many-Fermion Dynamics (MFD) code.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴He at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, Nmax = 2 is within 1 MeV of the converged binding energy and Nmax = 4 is within 0.3 MeV; for the initial N³LO potential, Nmax = 2 is at most bound by 1 MeV and Nmax = 4 by only 8 MeV.
- For ⁴He at λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹, Nmax = 2 is still about 14.5 MeV short of the converged binding energy and Nmax = 4 is about 7.3 MeV short.
- For the deuteron at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, the energy is converged at the 10 keV level by Nmax = 12.
- For ³H at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹, the SRG calculation is 0.5 MeV from converged at Nmax = 6 and only 25 keV away at Nmax = 12; at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, discrepancies are 90 and 4 keV; at λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹, discrepancies are 1.9 MeV and 200 keV.
- For ⁴He at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹, discrepancies are 30 keV at Nmax = 6 and 1 keV at Nmax = 10; at λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹, discrepancies are about 2.6 MeV at Nmax = 6 and 0.4 MeV at Nmax = 10.
- Ground-state energy of ³H at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ is -8.53 MeV; at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ is -8.48 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of ⁴He at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ is -28.90 MeV; at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ is -28.86 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of ⁶Li at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ is -33.1(1) MeV; at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ is -33.7 MeV.
- Ground-state energy of ⁷Li at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ is -41.2(5) MeV; at λ = 1.5 fm⁻¹ is -41.7(4) MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous NCSM investigations that used Lee-Suzuki transformations to improve convergence (Nogga 2005, Navratil 2007), which lose the variational property when making a cluster approximation, by instead using interactions evolved in free space that retain the variational principle. The convergence patterns are compared to those found for the UCOM framework (Roth 2005), where the SRG at λ = 3.0 fm⁻¹ is roughly equivalent to UCOM in terms of convergence. The results set the baseline for upcoming calculations that will include three-nucleon (3N) interactions, and the running of energies with cutoff provides a way to assess the expected net contributions from many-body forces in more complete future calculations.
