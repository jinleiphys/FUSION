# Exact solution for the non-equilibrium attractor in number-conserving relaxation time approximation

> We extend previous studies of the conformal 0+1d kinetic non-equilibrium attractor in relaxation time approximation by enforcing number conservation through the introduction of a dynamical fugacity (chemical potential). We derive two coupled integral equations for the effective temperature and fugacity which are then solved numerically to obtain the exact solution. The resulting solutions exhibit convergence to a unique non-equilibrium attractor when the scaled moments of the distribution function are plotted as a function of the rescaled time w = tau/tau_eq. This occurs even though the system is out of chemical equilibrium at late times. In addition, compared to the case where number conservation was not imposed, we find that the moments converge to their respective attractors more quickly, particularly for moments with m=0. Finally, we compare the resulting attractor moments with predictions from different hydrodynamic frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous studies of the non-equilibrium attractor in conformal 0+1d kinetic theory by enforcing number conservation via a dynamical fugacity, and finds that the system still converges to a unique attractor even though it remains out of chemical equilibrium at late times (i.e., the fugacity does not approach unity).

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation (RTA) for massless particles under Bjorken flow, imposing both energy and number conservation through Landau matching conditions that determine the effective temperature T and fugacity Γ. They derive two coupled integral equations for T(τ) and Γ(τ), which are solved numerically on a logarithmically-spaced grid using a CUDA-based GPU code with 4096 temporal lattice points and iterated to sixteen-digit convergence. From these solutions, general moments of the distribution function are computed and compared to predictions from Navier-Stokes, second-order viscous hydrodynamics (DNMR), and anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro).

## Key numbers

- The maximum pseudo-thermalization time with strong convergence criteria (δ_c = 10⁻⁶) is w̄_c^max ≈ 14 when number conservation is enforced, compared to w̄_c^max ≈ 28 without number conservation (Strickland 2018).
- For weak convergence criteria (δ_c = 0.01), moments with m ≠ 0 have pseudo-thermalization times in the range w̄_c ∼ 1–3, corresponding to τ_c ∼ 0.5–3 fm/c for a RHIC energy heavy-ion collision with initial central temperature 500 MeV at τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c.
- The attractor solution was obtained using initial conditions τ₀ = 10⁻³ fm/c, T₀ = 1 GeV, α₀ = 2.5 × 10⁻², and Γ₀ = 1.
- Specific solutions used τ₀ = 10⁻¹ fm/c, T₀ = 1 GeV, α₀ ∈ 0.1…1.5, and Γ₀ = 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of the non-equilibrium attractor in RTA kinetic theory by Strickland (2018) to include number conservation, which was previously neglected. The authors find that imposing number conservation causes moments to converge to their attractors more quickly, particularly for moments with m=0. The results enable quantitative comparison of attractor moments with hydrodynamic frameworks; anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) provides the best approximation to the exact attractor for all moments considered, consistent with findings in the non-number-conserving case (Strickland 2018). The work also demonstrates that the attractor concept extends beyond "hydrodynamization" to what the authors term "pseudo-thermalization," since higher moments converge to a non-hydrodynamic attractor state that remains far from equilibrium.
