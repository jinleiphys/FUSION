# Shell-model study of boron, carbon, nitrogen and oxygen isotopes based on monopole-based-universal interaction

> We study boron, carbon, nitrogen and oxygen isotopes with a newly constructed shell-model Hamiltonian developed from monopole-based-universal interaction ($V_{MU}$). The present Hamiltonian can reproduce well the ground-state energies, energy levels, electric quadrupole properties and spin properties of these nuclei in full psd model space including $(0-3)\hbarω$ excitations. Especially, it correctly describes the drip lines of carbon and oxygen isotopes and the spins of the ground states of $^{10}$B and $^{18}$N while some former interactions such as WBP and WBT fail. We point out that the inclusion of $2\hbarω$ excitations is important in reproducing some of these properties. In the present $(0+2)\hbarω$ calculations small but constant E2 effective charges appear to work quite well. As the inclusion of the $2\hbarω$ model space makes rather minor change, this seems to be related to the smallness of $^{4}$He core. Similarly, the spin g factors are very close to free values. The applicability of tensor and spin-orbit forces in free space, which are taken in the present Hamiltonian, is examined in shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The newly constructed shell-model Hamiltonian based on the monopole-based-universal interaction (V_MU) can reproduce ground-state energies, energy levels, electric quadrupole properties, and spin properties of boron, carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen isotopes in the full psd model space including (0-3)ħω excitations, but the central force in the ⟨psd|V|psd⟩ and ⟨pp|V|sdsd⟩ matrix elements must be reduced by factors of 0.85 and 0.55 from the original V_MU, respectively, to achieve this agreement.

## Method

The authors constructed a shell-model Hamiltonian by taking ⟨pp|V|pp⟩ matrix elements from the SFO interaction (with a 0.5 MeV reduction in one monopole term), ⟨sdsd|V|sdsd⟩ from SDPF-M, and ⟨psd|V|psd⟩ and ⟨pp|V|sdsd⟩ from V_MU plus the M3Y spin-orbit force and bare π+ρ tensor force, with the central part of V_MU scaled by 0.85 and 0.55 in those two cross-shell parts respectively. Calculations were performed in a full psd model space with ⁴He as the core, including (0+2)ħω excitations, with center-of-mass correction applied using the Gloeckner and Lawson method with β=10. Coulomb corrections were applied using energy differences of mirror nuclei near N=Z.

## Key numbers

- The central force in ⟨psd|V|psd⟩ matrix elements is reduced to 0.85 of the original V_MU central force; the central force in ⟨pp|V|sdsd⟩ is reduced to 0.55 of the original V_MU central force.
- The sd-shell single-particle energies (with ⁴He core) are ε_d5/2 = 8.01 MeV, ε_d3/2 = 10.11 MeV, and ε_s1/2 = 2.11 MeV; the p-shell single-particle energies are ε_p3/2 = 1.05 MeV and ε_p1/2 = 5.30 MeV.
- ²⁶O is about 1.2 MeV unbound in the present result (compared to 1.0 MeV bound in WBT).
- ²²C is 0.1 MeV bound in the present result (0.2 MeV unbound in WBT, 0.6 MeV unbound in WBP).
- ¹⁶B is 153 keV unbound in the present Hamiltonian (65 keV unbound in WBT, 144 keV bound in WBP).
- ¹⁹B is unbound by 160 keV in the present Hamiltonian (381 keV in WBT, 538 keV in WBP).
- The Z- and N-independent effective charges obtained are e_p = 1.26, e_n = 0.21 for the present Hamiltonian and e_p = 1.27, e_n = 0.23 for WBP; in 0ħω model space the present Hamiltonian gives e_p = 1.25 and e_n = 0.25.
- The spin g-factor quenching factors used are g_s^(eff)/g_s = 0.95 for the present 2ħω calculation, 0.92 for the present 0ħω calculation, and 0.90 for WBP, with δg^(l)_π,ν = ±0.1 μ_N.

## Context

This work extends the V_MU approach (Otsuka 2010) from describing shell evolution to full shell-model calculations in the psd region, improving upon earlier interactions such as WBP and WBT (Warburton 1992) which fail to reproduce the drip lines of carbon and oxygen isotopes and the ground-state spins of ¹⁰B and ¹⁸N. The inclusion of 2ħω excitations is shown to partially account for mass-dependent effects that WBP and WBT treat through an explicit mass-dependent term (18/A)^0.3, and the results enable systematic studies of neutron-rich light nuclei, particularly for understanding how cross-shell matrix elements affect drip-line predictions and spectroscopic properties.
