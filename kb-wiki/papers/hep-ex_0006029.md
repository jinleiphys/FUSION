# New Evidence for Supernarrow Dibaryons Production in pd Interactions

> The analysis of new experimental data, obtained at the Proton Linear Accelerator of INR, with the aim to search for supernarrow dibaryons in the $pd\to ppX_1$ and $pd\to pdX_2$ reactions is presented. Narrow peaks with an experimental width of 5 MeV at masses of 1904$\pm 2$, 1926$\pm 2$, and 1942$\pm 2$ MeV have been observed in missing mass $M_{pX_1}$ spectra. In the missing mass $M_{X_1}$ spectra, the peaks at $M_{X_1}=966\pm 2$, 986$\pm 2$, and 1003$\pm 2$ MeV have been found. The analysis of the data obtained leads to the conclusion that the observed peaks in $M_{pX_1}$ spectra are most likely supernarrow dibaryons, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. An alternative interpretation of the spectra by assuming a decay of the supernarrow dibaryons in "exotic baryon states" with masses $M_{X_1}$ is discussed.

# New Evidence for Supernarrow Dibaryons Production in pd Interactions

## Key claim
The observed narrow peaks in missing mass spectra at 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV are most likely supernarrow dibaryons whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle, but an alternative interpretation involving decay into "exotic baryon states" with masses around 966, 986, and 1003 MeV is also discussed and cannot be ruled out.

## Method
The experiment used a 305 MeV proton beam from the INR Linear Accelerator incident on CD2 and 12C targets, with the TAMS two-arm spectrometer detecting scattered protons in the left arm at 70 degrees and coincident protons or deuterons in the right arm at 34, 36, and 38 degrees. Missing mass spectra for the reactions pd → ppX1 and pd → pdX2 were constructed from four-fold coincidence events with time-of-flight and energy identification, applying software cuts to suppress background from pd → ppn and carbon-induced reactions. The observed peaks were compared to predictions from a one-meson exchange model for supernarrow dibaryon production with isospin T=0 or T=1.

## Key numbers
- Three peaks observed in missing mass M_{pX1} spectra at 1904 ± 2 MeV, 1926 ± 2 MeV, and 1942 ± 2 MeV, each with experimental width of 5 MeV
- Statistical significances of 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations for the 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV resonances, respectively
- Peaks observed in missing mass M_{X1} spectra at 966 ± 2 MeV, 986 ± 2 MeV, and 1003 ± 2 MeV
- Energy resolution of 4 MeV (left arm) and 7 MeV (right arm); time-of-flight resolution better than 0.5 ns
- Overall mass resolution of the spectrometer was approximately 5 MeV; angular resolution approximately 1 degree
- For the isovector SND D(T=1,J^P=1^{±}) with mass 1904 MeV, predicted angular contributions at 34, 36, and 38 degrees relate as 1:0.92:0.42; for isoscalar D(0,0^{±}) the ratio is 1:0.95:0.67

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous searches for supernarrow dibaryons in pd interactions (Fil'kov et al., 1999; 2000) where peaks at 1905 and 1924 MeV were observed with 4.8 and 4.9 standard deviations. The new experiment confirms those states and adds a third at 1942 MeV. The supernarrow dibaryon concept was proposed by Mulders et al. (1980) in the MIT bag model and by Fil'kov et al. (1995) as states whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli principle, with predicted widths below 1 keV. The results are compared to earlier searches for dibaryons with exotic quantum numbers in pp → ppγγ at JINR (Khruschev et al., 1995) and to upper limits from Uppsala bremsstrahlung data (Calen et al., 1998). The observed M_{X1} peak at 1003 MeV corresponds to a state previously attributed to an exotic baryon N* below πN threshold (Tatischeff et al., 1998), but the authors suggest this could instead be a decay product of a supernarrow dibaryon rather than a genuine resonance. Future experiments on SND photoproduction in γd → π^{±}D with polarized photons could separate the T=1, J^P=1^+, S=1 and T=1, J^P=1^-, S=0 states (Aleksandrov et al., 1999).
