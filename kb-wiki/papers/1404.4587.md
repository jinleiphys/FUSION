# Energy-independent PWA of the reaction $γp\to K^+Λ$

> Using all recent data on the differential cross sections and spin observables for the reaction $γp \to K^+ Λ$, an energy-independent partial-wave analysis is performed. The analysis requires multipoles up to $L = 2$; there is no evidence that the fit requires multipoles with $L=3$. At present the available data allow us to extract the dominant multipoles only. These are compatible with the multipoles obtained in the energy-dependent fit. This result supports the reliability of the Bonn-Gatchina energy-dependent results.

## Key claim
The energy-independent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of the reaction γp → K⁺Λ requires multipoles only up to L = 2, with no evidence for L = 3 contributions, but the available data allow extraction of only the dominant multipoles (E₀₊, E₁₊, M₁₊, M₁₋) without further constraints; these are compatible with the Bonn-Gatchina energy-dependent results, supporting their reliability.

## Method
The authors performed an energy-independent (single-energy) truncated partial-wave analysis of γp → K⁺Λ data in eleven independent energy bins from threshold up to √s = 1918 MeV. They expanded the photoproduction amplitude into electric (E_L±) and magnetic (M_L±) multipoles up to L = 2, fitting differential cross sections and eight polarization observables (Σ, P, T, O_x', O_z', C_x, C_z) from CLAS, GRAAL, and MAMI experiments. The analysis was conducted first with only L = 0,1 multipoles, then with L = 0,1,2 multipoles both freely and with penalty functions guiding the fit toward the energy-dependent BnGa2013 solution.

## Key numbers
- The analysis used eleven energy bins in √s: 1642–1653, 1672–1683, 1697–1708, 1727–1738, 1752–1758, 1777–1788, 1807–1818, 1832–1843, 1857–1868, 1882–1893, and 1900–1918 MeV.
- The mean χ² per data point improved from 1.8 (L = 0,1 only) to 0.8 (L = 0,1,2 free fit), with 165 fit parameters (8 amplitude moduli and 7 phases at 11 energies).
- For the GRAAL beam asymmetry Σ, χ² dropped from 6.77 (L = 0,1) to 0.57 (L = 0,1,2 free fit).
- The L = 0,1 multipoles (E₀₊, E₁₊, M₁₊, M₁₋) were found to be 5 to 10 times larger than L = 2 multipoles in the energy-dependent PWA up to 2000 MeV.
- The energy-dependent BnGa2013 solution had a mean χ² per data point of 1.57; the constrained energy-independent fit with penalty function gave mean χ² = 0.85.

## Context
This work extends the Bonn-Gatchina (BnGa) group's coupled-channel energy-dependent PWA studies (Anisovich et al., 2011; Anisovich et al., 2012) by performing an energy-independent analysis of the γp → K⁺Λ reaction as a crucial cross-check. The reaction is sensitive to N* resonances in the 1.6–2.4 GeV mass range and benefits from isospin conservation excluding Δ excitations. The results enable independent verification of resonance claims from complex coupled-channel analyses and demonstrate that energy-independent PWA of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction can determine dominant multipoles without additional constraints, paving the way for searches for missing nucleon resonances.
