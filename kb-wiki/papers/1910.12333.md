# Tetraneutron resonance in the presence of a dineutron

> Background: Several previous studies provided contradicting results for the four-neutron system, some claiming the existence of a 0+ near-threshold resonance, others denying presence of any observable resonant states. Purpose: Since most of the studies employed enhanced two-neutron interactions to follow the evolution of an artificially bound state into a continuum one, we examine several enhancement schemes that produce a bound dineutron as well. Methods: We study the four-neutron system by solving exact four-particle equations. By varying the interaction enhancement factor we calculate two-dineutron scattering phase shifts and cross sections. Results: When the same enhancement factor is used in all partial waves, a bound tetraneutron emerges together with a strongly bound dineutron. Furthermore, such a 0+ tetraneutron evolves not into a resonance but into a virtual state. Weak enhancement of S waves together with strongly enhanced higher waves is needed for the emergence of the resonant state. Anyhow the resonant behavior disappears well before reaching the physical interaction strength. Conclusions: The interaction enhancement scheme using the same factor for all waves, employed in several previous works, is misleading for the search of 0+ resonance as only a virtual state can emerge. Evolution of a bound tetraneutron into a resonance via an intermediate virtual state is possible with strong enhancement of higher two-neutron waves.

## Key claim

When the two-neutron interaction is enhanced by the same factor in all partial waves (as done in several previous studies), the resulting bound tetraneutron evolves not into a resonance but into a virtual state as the enhancement is reduced toward physical strength, making that enhancement scheme misleading for resonance searches. A resonance can appear only if higher partial waves are enhanced much more strongly than S-waves, but even then the resonant behavior disappears well before reaching the physical interaction strength.

## Method

The authors solved exact four-particle Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in coordinate space and equivalent Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations in momentum space for the four-neutron system. They varied enhancement factors applied to the neutron-neutron interaction (using a next-to-leading order chiral potential) and calculated two-dineutron scattering phase shifts and cross sections as functions of the center-of-mass kinetic energy. They also studied a two-body two-Gaussian potential to illustrate S-matrix pole trajectories in a simpler system.

## Key numbers

* A bound dineutron emerges in the 1S0 partial wave at enhancement factor f0 ≈ 1.1.
* With equal enhancement in all waves (f0 = fL = f), a bound tetraneutron appears at f ≈ 2.4 for local realistic potentials (Nijmegen II, Argonne V18), where the dineutron energy is below −20 MeV.
* For the NLO chiral potential with equal enhancement, the tetraneutron becomes bound at f = 2.665, where the dineutron energy Ed = −12.01 MeV and the tetraneutron energy E4n = −24.02 MeV.
* With S-wave enhancement fixed at f0 = 1.3 (giving Ed = −0.316 MeV), a bound tetraneutron requires higher-wave enhancement fL = 3.898.
* At fL = 3.8975 (with f0 = 1.3), the two-dineutron scattering length add ≈ −29 a0 and effective range rdd ≈ −9 a0, where a0 = 12.72 fm is the two-neutron scattering length at f0 = 1.3.
* At fL = 3.897, add ≈ −2 a0 and rdd ≈ −14 a0.
* In the two-body model, a virtual state appears at k = −0.0046i fm−1 (f0 = 1.004325) and a resonance at k = 0.0357 − 0.0078i fm−1 (f0 = 1.0042), with effective range r0 ≈ −125 fm.

## Context

This work resolves contradictions between studies claiming a 0+ tetraneutron resonance near threshold (e.g., using harmonic oscillator representation of the continuum or quantum Monte Carlo with extrapolation) and rigorous calculations finding no observable resonance (e.g., Faddeev-Yakubovsky solutions by Lazauskas (2005) and Deltuva (2018)). The authors show that the common practice of enhancing the neutron-neutron interaction equally in all waves, used by Shirokov (2016), Gandolfi (2017), and Li (2017), produces a virtual state rather than a resonance, explaining the discrepancies. The work enables future studies to correctly interpret bound-state-to-continuum evolution in few-neutron systems and clarifies that a large negative effective range is required for the bound-state-to-resonance pathway.
