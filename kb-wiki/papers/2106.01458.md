# Efimov resonances above four-boson threshold

> Four-boson Efimov physics is well known in the negative energy regime but far less above the four-body breakup threshold. The part of this region with negative two-boson scattering length is studied solving rigorous four-particle scattering equations for transition operators in the momentum space. Moving away from the unitary limit the Efimov tetramers evolve from unstable bound states into resonances. Their energies and widths are studied as functions of the two-boson scattering length; a universal behavior is established and given in a dimensionless representation. The Efimov tetramers have finite width in the whole regime; they broaden rapidly in the resonance regime but remain narrower than the associated trimer. The resonant behavior is most clearly seen in the four-particle recombination rate.

# Efimov resonances above four-boson threshold

## Key claim

The Efimov tetramers evolve from unstable bound states into resonances above the four-body breakup threshold when moving away from the unitary limit, but they have finite width in the entire regime, not just in the resonance region, and remain narrower than the associated trimer even as they broaden rapidly.

## Method

The author solved rigorous four-particle Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) scattering equations for transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using a rank-one separable s-wave two-boson potential with a momentum cutoff parameter. The resonance parameters (energy and width) were extracted from the energy dependence of physical transition operators in the complex-energy plane, employing the complex-energy method with special integration weights to handle singularities from Efimov trimer bound state poles and the free resolvent.

## Key numbers

- The universal Efimov ratio for the trimer threshold crossings is $a_{n+1}^- / a_n^- \approx 22.694$.
- The tetramer threshold crossing ratios are $a_{n,1}^- / a_n^- = 0.4254(2)$ for the deep tetramer and $a_{n,2}^- / a_n^- = 0.9125(2)$ for the shallow tetramer.
- For $n=2$ calculations, the ratio $|r_s/a|$ quantifying finite-range corrections is of order $0.001$, while for $n=1$ it is of order $0.02$.
- The range of $|a \Lambda|$ values in the $n=2$ calculations is $1020 < |a \Lambda| < 3776$.
- The results are limited to $|a_{n,k}^-|/a > -1.6$, beyond which the resonant behavior becomes physically unobservable.
- For the $(n,k)=(1,1)$ resonance at $a = 0.4\, a_1^- = 0.9062 \, a_{1,1}^-$, the dimensionless energy is $\varepsilon_{1,1} = 0.433$ and the width ratio is $\gamma_{1,1} / \varepsilon_{1,1} = 0.286$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Efimov tetramers in the negative energy regime below the four-body breakup threshold (Deltuva 2012) and the study of Efimov resonances in the three-boson system (Deltuva 2020). It also builds on techniques developed for four-nucleon reactions above breakup threshold and the search for the four-neutron resonance (Deltuva 2018). The results enable quantitative predictions for four-particle recombination rates and boson-trimer scattering observables in the resonance regime, and establish a universal dimensionless representation for the tetramer energies and widths that can be tested in cold-atom experiments.
