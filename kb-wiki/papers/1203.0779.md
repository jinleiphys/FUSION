# Alpha-like quartet condensation and isovector pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei

> We propose a simple quartet condensation model (QCM) which describes with very high accuracy the isovector pairing correlations in self-conjugate nuclei. The quartets have an alpha-like structure and are formed by collective isovector pairs. The accuracy of the QCM is tested for N=Z nuclei for which exact shell model diagonalizations can be performed. The calculations are done with two isovector pairing forces, one extracted from standard shell model interactions and the other of seniority type, acting, respectively, upon spherical and axially-deformed single-particle states. It is shown that for all calculated nuclei the QCM gives very accurate values for the pairing correlations energies, with errors which do not exceed 1%. These results show clearly that the correlations induced by the isovector pairing in self-conjugate nuclei are of quartet type and also indicate that QCM is the proper tool to calculate the isovector proton-neutron correlations in mean field pairing models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a quartet condensation model (QCM) that describes isovector pairing correlations in self-conjugate (N=Z) nuclei with errors below 1% relative to exact shell model diagonalizations, but only for the isovector (T=1, J=0) part of the interaction; the model does not address the competing quadrupole or isoscalar degrees of freedom that are also important in these nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a collective quartet operator from isovector Cooper pairs (nn, pp, and np) coupled to total isospin T=0 and angular momentum J=0, then build a condensate wavefunction by applying this operator repeatedly. The mixing amplitudes for the collective pairs are found by minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation using recurrence relations for matrix elements between states with arbitrary numbers of neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and proton-neutron pairs. The model is tested against exact shell model results for three sets of N=Z nuclei (in the sd-shell, pf-shell, and 50-82 major shells) using two types of isovector pairing forces: one extracted from standard shell model interactions (USDB, KB3G, BonnA) with spherical single-particle states, and a seniority-type force (g=-24/A) with axially-deformed single-particle states from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SLy4) calculations.

## Key numbers

- For spherical single-particle states with standard shell model interactions, QCM correlation energies have errors relative to exact shell model: 0.033% for 20Ne, 0.166% for 24Mg, 0.374% for 28Si, 0.309% for 32S, 0.151% for 44Ti, 0.250% for 48Cr, 0.539% for 52Fe, 0.052% for 104Te, 0.829% for 108Xe, and 1.002% for 112Ba.
- For axially-deformed single-particle states with seniority-type pairing (g=-24/A), QCM errors relative to exact shell model: 0.168% for 20Ne, 0.415% for 24Mg, 0.279% for 28Si, 0.117% for 32S, 0.159% for 44Ti, 0.494% for 48Cr, 0.495% for 52Fe, 0.184% for 104Te, 0.374% for 108Xe, and 0.592% for 112Ba.
- For 52Fe with constant-strength pairing (g=-24/A) and spherical states, QCM gives correlation energy 8.25 MeV versus exact value 8.29 MeV, while the particle-number-and-isospin projected BCS (PBCS(N,T)) gives only 7.63 MeV.
- The overlap between exact shell model wavefunctions with isovector pairing only and with the full two-body interaction is 0.614 for 48Cr, indicating strong competition from other degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends earlier quartet condensation ideas by Flowers et al. and Eichler et al. by formulating the model in terms of collective Cooper pairs rather than single-particle-level quartets, enabling particle-number-conserving calculations for larger systems. The QCM is shown to be significantly more accurate than the particle-number-projected BCS (PBCS) approximations and the particle-number-and-isospin projected BCS (PBCS(N,T)) approach of Chen et al., indicating that quartet-type correlations beyond BCS are essential. The model enables future studies of isovector pairing in nuclei with unequal numbers of neutrons and protons, and provides a practical tool for mean-field calculations where exact shell model diagonalizations are impossible.
