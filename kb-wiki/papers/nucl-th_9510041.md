# Nuclear Gluon Shadowing via Continuum Lepton Pairs in $p+A$ at $\sqrt s=200$ AGeV

> The dilepton spectra from open charm decay in $p+A$ reactions at $\sqrt s=200$ AGeV are proposed to constrain gluon shadowing in nuclei. We show that the required measurements are feasible at RHIC.

## Key claim

The dilepton spectra from open charm decay in $p+A$ reactions at $\sqrt s=200$ AGeV can constrain gluon shadowing in nuclei, provided that the assumption of hard fragmentation of charm quarks (delta function fragmentation) holds at RHIC energies, which must be checked experimentally via single inclusive measurements of $D$ production.

## Method

The authors computed the initial charm pair distribution from $p+A$ nuclear collisions using perturbative QCD with gluon fusion ($gg\rightarrow c\bar{c}$) as the dominant production mechanism, incorporating nuclear parton density functions with impact-parameter-dependent shadowing. They used the MRSA parton structure functions with $m_c=1.4$ GeV, $K=3$, and $Q^2 = \hat s/2$ for gluon fusion, then hadronized charm quarks via delta function fragmentation and decayed the $D\bar{D}$ pairs using JETSET7.3 Monte Carlo to obtain dilepton spectra. The ratio of dilepton $dN/dMdy$ spectra in shadowed $p+Au$ over unshadowed $p+Au$ was compared to input gluon shadowing functions from HIJING and Eskola.

## Key numbers

- Charm pair cross section in $pp$ collisions at RHIC: $340 \mu b$ (with $m_c=1.4$ GeV, $K=3$)
- Signal-to-background ratio for $ee$ channel: very large (dielectron signal easiest to extract)
- Signal-to-background ratio for $e\mu$ channel: about 2.5
- Signal-to-background ratio for $\mu\mu$ channel: about 1/4
- Typical increment $\delta p_\perp^2(A)$ due to multiple collisions: limited to $\sim 0.34$ GeV$^2$ for heaviest nuclei
- Momentum spread parameter $\Delta^2 \sim 0.17$ GeV$^2$ for multiple scattering distribution
- Relative change in pair spectrum from Cronin effect and energy loss: $F^{\prime}(p_\perp)/F(p_\perp) \simeq 1 - 0.1(e^{-y} - 1)$ for $m_\perp \sim 3$ GeV, $\delta E=10$ GeV
- Lepton pair mass range studied: $1 \le M \le 4$ GeV
- Kinematical cuts: $E_e > 1$ GeV, $E_\mu > 2$ GeV, relative azimuthal angle $\phi _{\ell^+\ell^-} > 90^\circ$

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to use open charm background as a probe of evolving gluon density (pcm, MW) and addresses the need for systematic measurement of gluon shadowing, which perturbative QCD analysis (mullerq, eskolaq) predicts at $x<0.01$ due to gluon recombination. The approach contrasts with $J/\psi$ production, where QCD scaling was found violated in the energy range $20 < \sqrt{s} < 40$ AGeV (moss), precluding determination of $R_A$. The proposed measurement at RHIC with the PHENIX detector could provide unique information on low $x_A$ nuclear gluon structure, which is of central importance for understanding mini-jet production and total entropy production in $A+A$ collisions (hijingprl).
