# Probing low-mass vector bosons with parity nonconservation and nuclear anapole moment measurements in atoms and molecules

> In the presence of P-violating interactions, the exchange of vector bosons between electrons and nucleons induces parity-nonconserving (PNC) effects in atoms and molecules, while the exchange of vector bosons between nucleons induces anapole moments of nuclei. We perform calculations of such vector-mediated PNC effects in Cs, Ba$^+$, Yb, Tl, Fr and Ra$^+$ using the same relativistic many-body approaches as in earlier calculations of standard-model PNC effects, but with the long-range operator of the weak interaction. We calculate nuclear anapole moments due to vector boson exchange using a simple nuclear model. From measured and predicted (within the standard model) values for the PNC amplitudes in Cs, Yb and Tl, as well as the nuclear anapole moment of $^{133}$Cs, we constrain the P-violating vector-pseudovector nucleon-electron and nucleon-proton interactions mediated by a generic vector boson of arbitrary mass. Our limits improve on existing bounds from other experiments by many orders of magnitude over a very large range of vector-boson masses.

## Key claim

Atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) experiments can constrain the P-violating vector-pseudovector nucleon-electron and nucleon-proton interactions mediated by a generic vector boson of arbitrary mass, improving on existing bounds from other experiments by many orders of magnitude over a very large range of vector-boson masses, provided the boson mass is not so low that the interaction range exceeds atomic scales.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic many-body calculations of atomic PNC amplitudes for Cs, Ba⁺, Yb, Tl, Fr, and Ra⁺ using the same approaches as in earlier standard-model PNC calculations (relativistic Hartree-Fock-Dirac with random-phase approximation, correlation potential method, and CI+MBPT), but replacing the short-range weak interaction operator with the long-range Yukawa potential from vector boson exchange. They also calculated nuclear anapole moments due to vector boson exchange using a simple nuclear shell model with a constant core density and nonrelativistic nucleons, considering the two limiting cases of very heavy and very light vector bosons.

## Key numbers

- For Cs (6s-7s transition), the calculated PNC amplitude for an infinitely massive vector boson is \(8.9 \times 10^{-12}\) in units of \(i \times\) atomic unit (a.u.), decreasing to \(1.1 \times 10^{-21}\) for a 10 eV boson.
- For Yb (6s² ¹S₀ - 6s5d ³D₁ transition), the calculated PNC amplitude for an infinitely massive vector boson is \(1.1 \times 10^{-9}\) a.u., decreasing to \(8.9 \times 10^{-20}\) for a 10 eV boson.
- The derived limit on \(|g_e^A g_N^V|/m_{Z'}^2\) from Cs is \(3.9 \times 10^{-8}\) GeV⁻² for high-mass bosons, and the limit on \(|g_e^A g_N^V|\) from Cs is \(3.1 \times 10^{-14}\) for low-mass bosons.
- The derived limit on \(|g_p^A g_N^V|/m_{Z'}^2\) from the ¹³³Cs anapole moment is \(2.3 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV⁻² for \(m_{Z'} \gg 1/r_0\), and the limit on \(|g_p^A g_N^V|\) is \(6.0 \times 10^{-8}\) for \(m_{Z'} \ll 1/R\).
- The experimentally measured nuclear anapole moment parameter \(\kappa_a\) for ¹³³Cs is \(0.364(62)\), while the single-particle shell-model prediction is \(0.27(8)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard-model atomic PNC calculations of Dzuba, Flambaum, and collaborators (2001, 2002, 2011) to the case of a generic vector boson mediator, and complements earlier searches for new vector bosons with masses above 100 keV by Bouchiat (1983), Marciano (1990, 2012), and others. The derived limits improve on existing constraints from torsion-pendulum experiments (Adelberger, 2006, 2008) and magnetometry experiments (Romalis, 2009) by many orders of magnitude for boson masses above approximately \(10^{-16}\) eV and \(10^{-5}\) eV, respectively. Ongoing and future PNC experiments with atoms (Williams, 2013; Leefer, 2014; Antypas, 2017; Tandecki, 2014; Portela, 2013) and molecules (Cahn, 2014; Isaev, 2010) may further improve sensitivity, particularly for low-mass bosons where high-angular-momentum states in atoms like dysprosium are not suppressed by the centrifugal barrier.
