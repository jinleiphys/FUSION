# Meson widths from string worldsheet instantons

> We show that open strings living on a D-brane which lies outside an AdS black hole can tunnel into the black hole through worldsheet instantons. These instantons have a simple interpretation in terms of thermal quarks in the dual Yang-Mills (YM) theory. As an application we calculate the width of a meson in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma which is described holographically as a massless mode on a D7 brane in AdS_5 \times S_5. While the width of the meson is zero to all orders in the 1/\sqrtλ expansion with λthe 't Hooft coupling, it receives non-perturbative contributions in 1/\sqrtλ from worldsheet instantons. We find that the width increases quadratically with momentum at large momentum and comment on potential phenomenological implications of this enhancement for heavy ion collisions. We also comment on how this non-perturbative effect has important consequences for the phase structure of the YM theory obtained in the classical gravity limit.

## Key claim

Meson widths in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma are zero to all orders in the perturbative \(1/\sqrt{\lambda}\) expansion (where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling), but receive non-perturbative contributions from string worldsheet instantons that are exponentially suppressed in \(\sqrt{\lambda}\). The width increases quadratically with momentum at large momentum, which the authors suggest could have phenomenological implications for heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors consider a holographic dual of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory with added fundamental quarks, described by a D7-brane in an \(AdS_5 \times S_5\) black hole geometry. They compute the Euclidean worldsheet path integral for open strings on the D7-brane, evaluating it in the saddle-point approximation for topological sectors with nontrivial winding number around the Euclidean time circle. The \(m=\pm 1\) instanton sectors correspond to worldsheets connecting the tip of the D7-brane to the black hole horizon, and their contribution to the D7-brane effective action is computed to quadratic order in the meson fluctuation field \(\chi^\phi\), yielding a nonlocal correction to the meson equation of motion from which the width is extracted via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass at temperature \(T\) is \(m_q^{(T)} = \sqrt{\lambda} T (\Lambda_m - 1)/2\), where \(\Lambda_m = r_m/r_0\) is a dimensionless number of order \(\lambda^0\).
- The meson masses are of order \(M = 4\sqrt{2}\pi m_q/\sqrt{\lambda}\), parametrically smaller than \(m_q\) in \(\sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The dissociation temperature is \(T_{\rm diss} = 0.122 M\).
- The instanton action for the \(m=\pm 1\) sectors is \(I_\pm = \beta m_q^{(T)}\).
- The width formula for the \(n\)-th meson excitation is \(\Gamma_n^{(\pm1)} = (32\pi^3\sqrt{\lambda}/N_c m_q^2) |\psi_n(\theta=0)|^2 n_\pm\), where \(n_\pm\) are thermal quark/antiquark densities proportional to \(N_c\).
- For large momentum \(k\) and \(T \ll M\), the width ratio scales as \(\Gamma_n(k)/\Gamma_n(0) \approx [2(4\pi)^4/((n+2)(n+3/2))] (T^4 k^2/M^6)\).
- The width turns up quadratically around \(k/M = 0.52 (T_{\rm diss}/T)^2\), roughly the momentum where the meson approaches its limiting velocity.
- For a \(J/\psi\) with \(M \approx 3\) GeV and \(T_{\rm diss} \approx 370\) MeV, at RHIC temperature \(T=250\) MeV the width increases by a factor of 2 at \(k=6\) GeV and by a factor of 10 at \(k=13\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of mesons in strongly coupled plasmas beyond the classical gravity approximation, where mesons are stable for \(T < T_{\rm diss}\) and completely disappear for \(T > T_{\rm diss}\) (Kruczenski 2003, Hoyos 2006, Mateos 2007). The authors show that worldsheet instantons generate nonzero meson widths and quark densities that are invisible in the perturbative \(1/\sqrt{\lambda}\) expansion, smoothing the phase transition in chemical potential found by Mateos (2007) and Karch (2007) at \(\lambda = \infty\). The momentum dependence of the width is consistent with the velocity-enhanced screening from the heavy quark potential calculation of Liu (2006), and the results enable quantitative predictions for quarkonium suppression at large transverse momentum in heavy ion collisions.
