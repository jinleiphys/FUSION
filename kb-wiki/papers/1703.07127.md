# Two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski Space: the Nakanishi Way

> The possibility of solving the Bethe-Salpeter Equation in Minkowski space, even for fermionic systems, is becoming actual, through the applications of well-known tools: i) the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and ii) the light-front projection onto the null-plane. The theoretical background and some preliminary calculations are illustrated, in order to show the potentiality and the wide range of application of the method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Bethe-Salpeter Equation for two-fermion systems can be solved directly in Minkowski space by combining the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with light-front projection, provided that the singular integrals arising from fermionic propagators are treated carefully using techniques from infinite-momentum-frame field theory.

## Method

The authors decompose the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for a \(0^+\) two-fermion system into four scalar functions multiplied by orthogonal Dirac structures, each expressed via a Nakanishi integral representation. They apply light-front projection (integration over \(k^-\)) to both sides of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation, transforming it into a coupled system of integral equations for the Nakanishi weight functions. The singular contributions in the kernel matrix, arising from the numerator structure of fermionic propagators, are handled analytically using the identity \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty dx / [\beta x - y \mp i\epsilon]^2 = \pm 2\pi i \delta(\beta) / [-y \mp i\epsilon]\) and its derivative, following the work of Yan and collaborators. The resulting generalized eigenvalue problem is discretized by expanding the weight functions on an orthonormal basis of Laguerre polynomials in \(\gamma\) and Gegenbauer polynomials in \(z\).

## Key numbers

* For a scalar-exchange kernel with boson mass \(\mu/m = 0.15\) and vertex form factor \(\Lambda = 2\), the coupling \(g^2\) for binding energy \(B/m = 0.01\) is 7.844 (full calculation including singular contributions), compared to 7.879 without singular terms and 7.813 from the smoothing-function approach of Carbonell and Karmanov (2010).
* For \(B/m = 0.50\) with the same parameters, the full calculation gives \(g^2 = 88.964\), while the non-singular calculation gives 121.342 and the smoothing-function result is 86.95.
* The singular integrals encountered have the form \(\mathcal{C}_j = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} (dk^-/2\pi) (k^-)^j \mathcal{S}(k^-,v,z,z',\gamma,\gamma')\) with \(j = 1,2,3\), where the worst-case asymptotic behavior is \(\mathcal{S} \sim 1/[k^-]^2\) for \(k^- \to \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi-based approach previously applied to two-scalar systems by Kusaka (1997), Carbonell and Karmanov (2006), and Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2014) to the more challenging case of fermionic constituents. It resolves the light-front singularities that had been treated pragmatically with smoothing functions by Carbonell and Karmanov (2010), establishing a rigorous treatment based on the infinite-momentum-frame studies of Yan (1973). The method enables calculation of light-front momentum distributions for two-fermion bound states directly in Minkowski space, and the authors report favorable comparison with Euclidean-space results from Dorkin (2014). The technique is being extended to boson-fermion and vector-vector systems.
