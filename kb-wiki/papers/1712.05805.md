# Multi moment cancellation of participant fluctuations

> We summarize the new method for the correction of participant fluctuations in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. It allows to estimate a fluctuation baseline in comparison to a useful signal. In particular cases of a weak signal compared to baseline, it allows to cancel the baseline contribution from participants.

# Multi moment cancellation of participant fluctuations

## Key claim
The multi moment cancellation of participant fluctuations (MMCP) method can remove the baseline contribution from participant fluctuations in a combination of several high fluctuation moments of a given particle type, but only under the condition that the parameters characterizing participant fluctuations (α, |β|, |γ|) are much smaller than 1, and either δ or ε can be neglected.

## Method
The authors derive analytic relations between measured fluctuation moments (scaled variance ω, normalized skewness Sσ, normalized kurtosis κσ²) and the corresponding source fluctuations, assuming an independent participant model where participants are identical and independent. They introduce parameters α, β, γ that quantify the strength of participant fluctuations relative to source fluctuations, and parameters δ, ε that characterize the relative strength of high and low order fluctuations from one source. Under the conditions α, |β|, |γ| ≪ 1 and δ ≪ ε, they obtain a simple analytic solution that expresses source fluctuations solely in terms of measured quantities. The method is tested using the EPOS 1.99 model applied to net-electric charge in ⁴⁰₁₈Ar+⁴⁵₂₁Sc reactions.

## Key numbers
- The test uses ⁴⁰₁₈Ar+⁴⁵₂₁Sc reactions at p_lab = 150 GeV/c with y^CMS > 0
- Centrality bins range from 0-20% down to 0-0.2% (13 bin widths: 20%, 17.5%, 15%, 12.5%, 10%, 7.5%, 5%, 2.5%, 1.5%, 1%, 0.75%, 0.5%, 0.2%)
- For a hadron gas away from critical point: 0 ≤ δ = ε = 1/ω_A² = tanh²(μ_B/T) ≤ 1
- Near critical point: α ∼ ⟨n_A⟩² ω_P/ξ² → 0, |β| ∼ ⟨n_A⟩² |S_P σ_P|/ξ² → 0, |γ| ∼ ⟨n_A⟩⁴ |κ_P σ_P²|/ξ⁴ → 0, while δ ∼ ⟨n_A⟩ ξ^0.5 → ∞ and ε ∼ ⟨n_A⟩² ξ → ∞
- For the test system: S_A σ_A ≃ 0.1 ≪ Sσ ≃ 3⟨n_A⟩ ω_P > 0, and S_A σ_A ≃ 0, κ_A σ_A² ≃ 0
- The fourth order fluctuations of a source, κ_A σ_A², change non-monotonously for bin width smaller than 5% in the range from -1 to +1

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Begun 2017) on participant fluctuations and proposes an alternative to existing correction methods. The paper contrasts with the Centrality Bin Width Correction procedure (Luo 2011) and the use of strongly intensive quantities (Gazdzicki 1992, Gorenstein 2011), noting that the former still leaves residual participant fluctuations and the latter requires the strong assumption that two different particle species share the same volume fluctuations. The MMCP method enables extraction of source fluctuations from measured moments of a single particle species, which is particularly relevant for experimental searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as NA61/SHINE and STAR.
