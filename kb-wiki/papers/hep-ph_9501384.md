# Chiral Dynamics in Nucleons and Nuclei

> 154 pp, plain TeX, uses epsf macros, figures appended in figrev.uu (as compressed uuencoded tar files), complete PS file available from meissner@pythia.itkp.uni-bonn.de

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral perturbation theory (CHPT), the effective field theory of QCD based on spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, can be systematically extended to processes involving one or more nucleons, provided one uses a heavy-baryon formalism to handle the non-vanishing nucleon mass in the chiral limit. The authors state that to rigorously test the chiral structure of QCD in nucleon and nuclear studies, calculations to order O(E⁴/Λ⁴) are mandatory in many cases.

## Method

The authors construct the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons using the external field technique, then apply the heavy-baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) to eliminate the troublesome baryon mass term and establish a consistent power counting scheme. They review calculations of pion-nucleon scattering, the reaction πN → ππN, nucleon electromagnetic form factors, Compton scattering, axial properties, threshold pion photo- and electroproduction, and two-pion production, all within the CHPT framework to next-to-leading order. For systems with two or more nucleons, they apply the chiral power counting only to irreducible diagrams and solve a Schrödinger or Lippmann-Schwinger equation to generate the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

* Running quark masses at 1 GeV: m_u ≈ 5 MeV, m_d ≈ 9 MeV, m_s ≈ 175 MeV, with uncertainties m_d = 5 ± 2 MeV, m_d = 9 ± 3 MeV, m_s = 175 ± 55 MeV.
* Quark mass ratios relative to the ρ-meson mass: m_u/M_ρ ≈ 0.006, m_d/M_ρ ≈ 0.012, m_s/M_ρ ≈ 0.23.
* The leading-order elastic scattering amplitude for π⁺π⁰ → π⁺π⁰ (massless pions) is T = t / F_π², where t is the invariant four-momentum transfer squared.
* The vector-vector correlator from τ decay peaks around s ≈ 0.5 GeV² ≈ M_ρ², while the axial-axial correlator peaks around s ≈ 1.5 GeV² ≈ M_A₁².

## Context

This review extends the systematic CHPT framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) for the meson sector to include baryons, building on the foundational work of Gasser, Sainio, and Švarc (1988) and the heavy-baryon method of Jenkins and Manohar (1991). It covers applications to the two-flavor sector with one nucleon line, as well as the extension to two or more nucleons following Weinberg (1990). The work enables systematic low-energy calculations of nucleon structure, pion-nucleon interactions, and nuclear forces from QCD, and identifies the need for future lattice gauge theory calculations to supply numerical values for the low-energy constants.
