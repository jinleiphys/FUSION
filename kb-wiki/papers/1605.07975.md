# Exotic Discoveries in Familiar Places: Theory of the Onia and Exotics

> This brief overview presents an introduction to the heavy-quark exotic hadron candidates, $X$, $Y$, $Z$, $P_c$, believed to be tetraquark and pentaquark states. After an abridged account of their discovery, we outline the various theoretical pictures employed to describe their structure, with special focus on the new dynamical diquark picture.

## Key claim

This overview argues that the heavy-quark exotic hadron candidates (X, Y, Z, Pc) are now experimentally established beyond reasonable doubt, but that no single theoretical picture (molecule, hybrid, diquark, hadrocharmonium, or kinematic effect) universally explains all observed states; the new "dynamical diquark" picture is presented as a promising alternative that accommodates features such as narrow widths and preferential decay modes.

## Method

The paper reviews the experimental discovery history of the X(3872), Y(4260), Zc(3900), Z(4430), and Pc(4380)/Pc(4450) states, summarizing the evidence for their exotic nature (quantum numbers, decay modes, phase-shift analysis). It then surveys the major theoretical approaches, hadronic molecules, hybrids, hadrocharmonium, static diquark models, and kinematic cusp/rescattering effects, and introduces the dynamical diquark picture, in which a diquark-antidiquark pair is created with large relative momentum, separates until brought to rest by the confining flux tube, and then hadronizes via exponentially suppressed tails of meson wave functions.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass: 3871.69 ± 0.17 MeV; mass difference to D0 D*0 threshold: −0.11 ± 0.21 MeV; width < 1.2 MeV
- Z(4430) confirmed at 13.9σ significance by LHCb (2014), with J^P = 1^+
- Pc(4380) observed at 9σ and Pc(4450) at 12σ by LHCb (2015), decaying to J/ψ p
- As of BEAUTY 2016: 29 exotic candidates observed (24 in c-cbar sector, 4 in b-bbar sector, 1 in b-s-u-d sector); 12 confirmed by a second experiment
- Z(4430) decays to ψ' (2S) over J/ψ (1S) by at least a factor of 10
- Dynamical diquark model: calculated diquark-antidiquark separation r_Z = 1.16 fm for Z(4430); charge radii from Cornell potential: ⟨r_J/ψ⟩ = 0.39 fm, ⟨r_ψ'⟩ = 0.80 fm
- Lowest baryon-antibaryon threshold (Λc Λc-bar) at 4573 MeV; lowest exotic candidate above it is X(4630)

## Context

This review extends the line of work following the 2003 discovery of X(3872) by Belle (Choi 2003) and the subsequent identification of charged charmoniumlike states such as Zc(3900) by BESIII and Belle (2013) and Z(4430) by Belle (2008) and LHCb (2014). It contrasts the dynamical diquark picture (Brodsky, Lebed 2014) with earlier static approaches including the Cornell potential (Eichten 1978, 1979), hadronic molecule models (Voloshin 1976, De Rujula 1976), hybrid models (Giles 1976), hadrocharmonium (Voloshin 2007), and diquark models (Maiani 2004, 2014). The paper enables future work by identifying open questions: whether different exotics require different structural explanations, and whether the dynamical diquark picture can be tested against upcoming data from ATLAS, BESIII, CMS, LHCb, and Belle II.
