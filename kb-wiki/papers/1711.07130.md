# Metric anisotropies and emergent anisotropic hydrodynamics

> Expansion of a locally equilibrated fluid is considered in an anisotropic space-time given by Bianchi type I metric. Starting from isotropic equilibrium phase-space distribution function in the local rest frame, we obtain expressions for components of the energy-momentum tensor and conserved current, such as number density, energy density and pressure components. In the case of an axis-symmetric Bianchi type I metric, we show that they are identical to that obtained within the setup of anisotropic hydrodynamics. We further consider the case when Bianchi type I metric is a vacuum solution of Einstein equation: the Kasner metric. For axis-symmetric Kasner metric, we discuss the implications of our results in the context of anisotropic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the expressions for energy-momentum tensor components and conserved current obtained from free-streaming in an axis-symmetric Bianchi type I metric are identical to those obtained within the framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics, but only under the assumption of a collision-less (free-streaming) evolution rather than an interacting medium.

## Method

The authors start from an isotropic equilibrium phase-space distribution function in the local rest frame at an initial time \(t_0\) and evolve it using the collision-less Boltzmann equation in a Bianchi type I metric. They compute the components of the energy-momentum tensor and number density by integrating over momentum space with the appropriate red-shift factor \(z\) that accounts for the anisotropic expansion. They then specialize to the axis-symmetric case and further to the Kasner metric, which is a vacuum solution of Einstein's equations, to obtain closed-form expressions for the thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers

* For the axis-symmetric Kasner metric, two possible parameter sets exist: Case I \((a,b,c) = (0,0,1)\) and Case II \((a,b,c) = (2/3,2/3,-1/3)\).
* For Case I (longitudinal expansion), the variable \(\xi = t^2/t_0^2\); in the limit \(1/\xi \rightarrow 0\), the asymptotic pressures satisfy \({\cal P}_{\parallel}= 0\) and \({\cal P}_{\bot}= \varepsilon/2\).
* For Case II (transverse expansion), the variable \(\xi = t_0^2/t^2\); in the limit \(\xi \rightarrow 0\), the asymptotic pressures satisfy \({\cal P}_{\parallel}= \varepsilon\) and \({\cal P}_{\bot}= 0\).
* The number density for any Kasner-type expansion evolves as \(n = n_0 t_0 / t\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of anisotropic hydrodynamics by providing a geometric interpretation based on Bianchi type I metrics, rather than the usual kinetic-theory approach of Romatschke and Strickland (2003). The Kasner metric analysis connects to earlier studies of free-streaming in Bjorken expansion by Kapusta (1992), Martinez (2008), and Florkowski (2008). The framework may enable applications to cosmological models where the universe is described by a perturbed Friedmann-Robertson-Walker metric, which is a special case of Bianchi type I.
