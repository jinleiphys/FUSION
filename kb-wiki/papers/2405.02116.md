# Intriguing aspects of light baryon resonances

> We discuss that some light baryon resonances exhibit properties which cannot be described when attributing a three-valence quark structure to them. Besides pointing out the hadron resonances which clearly require description beyond the quark model, we focus on the third $s_{11},~ N^*$ state and its decay to final states consisting of the lightest hyperon resonances which have a partial width comparable to that for the decay to $πN$. Such properties of the mentioned nucleon resonance get manifested in the cross sections and other observables related to processes producing the lightest hyperon resonances. We show that all these findings arise from the strong association of the baryon resonances to the dynamics among the ground-state hadrons.

# Intriguing aspects of light baryon resonances

## Key claim

The paper argues that several light baryon resonances, particularly the third S11 nucleon resonance near 1.9 GeV (N*(1895)), cannot be adequately described by a three-valence quark structure and instead arise from meson-baryon molecular dynamics. The key hedge is that this interpretation depends on solving coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations with regularization parameters fixed by chi-squared fits to experimental data, and that the existence of the proposed Sigma(1400) state, which is central to the argument, is not yet well-established and requires better quality data for confirmation.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel approach using s-wave interactions, considering channels composed of pseudoscalar mesons and octet baryons as well as vector mesons and octet baryons. The tree-level interaction kernels are derived from chiral Lagrangians for pseudoscalar-baryon interactions and hidden local symmetry Lagrangians for vector-baryon interactions, with coupling constants D=2.4, F=0.82, D'=0.8, and F'=0.46 fixed to reproduce experimental magnetic couplings and the axial coupling constant g_A=1.26. The regularization parameters are determined by chi-squared fits to experimental data on kaon-nucleon total cross sections, pi-N amplitudes, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data.

## Key numbers

- The third S11 nucleon resonance is found near 1.9 GeV, identified as N*(1895), with the traditional quark model predicting such a state around 2 GeV
- The Xi(1690) has a total width of Gamma = 20 +/- 15 MeV, with decay to pi Xi suppressed despite large available phase space
- The axial coupling constant of the nucleon is reproduced as F' + D' = g_A = 1.26
- The total cross sections for gamma + p -> K+ + Sigma(1400) are predicted to be about an order of magnitude smaller than those for gamma + p -> K+ + Lambda(1405)
- The fits to K- p -> K- p and K- p -> anti-K0 n processes are performed near threshold regions about 30-50 MeV above respective thresholds

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of the mass inversion puzzle between Lambda(1405) and N*(1535), originally noted by Dalitz (1959) and Glozman (1995), and builds on the meson-baryon molecular interpretation developed by Kaiser (1995, 1996), Oset (1997), and Inoue (2001). The authors specifically extend their own prior work on coupled-channel interactions in meson-baryon systems (Khemchandani 2012, 2013, 2016, 2018) to show that the N*(1895) resonance couples strongly to kaon-light hyperon channels, enabling predictions for photoproduction of Lambda(1405) and the proposed Sigma(1400) that can be tested at JLAB and LEPS. The results also connect to the HADES puzzle of hyperon abundance in Ar+KCl collisions (HADES 2009, 2015), where the existence of narrow resonances like Xi(1690) can affect the yield of stable hadrons reaching detectors.
