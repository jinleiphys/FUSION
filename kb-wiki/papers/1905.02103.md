# Magnetized baryons and the QCD phase diagram: NJL model meets the lattice

> We determine the baryon spectrum of 1 + 1 + 1-flavor QCD in the presence of strong background magnetic fields using lattice simulations at physical quark masses for the first time. Our results show a splitting within multiplets according to the electric charge of the baryons and reveal, in particular, a reduction of the nucleon masses for strong magnetic fields. This first-principles input is used to define constituent quark masses and is employed to set the free parameters of the Polyakov loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in a magnetic field-dependent manner. The so constructed model is shown to exhibit inverse magnetic catalysis at high temperatures and a reduction of the transition temperature as the magnetic field grows - in line with non-perturbative lattice results. This is contrary to the naive variant of this model, which gives incorrect results for this fundamental phase diagram. Our findings demonstrate that the magnetic field dependence of the PNJL model can be reconciled with the lattice findings in a systematic way, employing solely zero-temperature first-principles input.

# Magnetized baryons and the QCD phase diagram: NJL model meets the lattice

## Key claim

The authors determine the baryon spectrum of 1+1+1-flavor QCD in strong background magnetic fields using lattice simulations at physical quark masses for the first time, and use these zero-temperature first-principles results to fix the magnetic field-dependent coupling of the Polyakov loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The resulting "lattice-improved" PNJL model reproduces inverse magnetic catalysis and a reduction of the transition temperature with increasing magnetic field, contrary to the naive variant of the model which gives incorrect results for this phase diagram.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three flavors of stout-improved staggered quarks at physical quark masses, using tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action on lattices with varying spacings to enable continuum extrapolation. Baryon masses for the octet (proton, neutron, Sigma baryons) were extracted from exponential fits to baryon correlators at zero temperature, with the magnetic field dependence extrapolated to the continuum using a Taylor expansion ansatz. The resulting baryon masses were used to define constituent quark masses via a simple non-relativistic quark model, which then fixed the magnetic field-dependent four-fermion coupling G(B) in the PNJL model; the model was solved in mean-field approximation to obtain the thermodynamic potential, quark condensate, and Polyakov loop as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass set to 138 MeV and pion decay constant to 93 MeV for fixing NJL parameters at B=0
- Bare current quark mass m0 = 3.50(5) MeV and cutoff Lambda = 675(10) MeV from fitting to lattice baryon masses
- Four-fermion coupling G decreases from 12.8(9) GeV^{-2} at eB=0 to 6.7(7) GeV^{-2} at eB=0.6 GeV^2
- Average constituent quark mass squared M^2 decreases from 0.097(6) GeV^2 at eB=0 to 0.079(15) GeV^2 at eB=0.6 GeV^2
- Pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing magnetic field in the lattice-improved PNJL model is Tc(B=0) = 204(3) MeV
- Continuum extrapolation of baryon masses performed for magnetic fields up to eB approximately 0.7 GeV^2
- Goodness of fit for constituent quark mass determination yields chi^2 < 1 for all magnetic fields

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of magnetized hadron properties, which had used heavier-than-physical quarks (Martinelli 1982, Chang 2015, Parreno 2016) or focused on mesons (Bali 2011, Hidaka 2012, Bali 2017), by providing the first continuum-extrapolated baryon spectrum at physical quark masses in strong magnetic fields. The results contradict earlier PNJL model studies (Gatto 2010) that found magnetic catalysis at all temperatures and an increase of transition temperature with B, and instead reconcile the model with lattice findings (Bali 2011, Endrodi 2015) by using zero-temperature first-principles input. The approach enables systematic improvement of low-energy effective models for regions where lattice simulations are hindered, such as finite baryon chemical potential, and provides input for magnetized compact star phenomenology and heavy-ion collision physics.
