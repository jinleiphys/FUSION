# Improved di-neutron cluster model for 6He scattering

> The structure of the three-body Borromean nucleus 6He is approximated by a two-body di-neutron cluster model. The binding energy of the 2n-αsystem is determined to obtain a correct description of the 2n-αcoordinate, as given by a realistic three-body model calculation. The model is applied to describe the break-up effects in elastic scattering of 6He on several targets, for which experimental data exist. We show that an adequate description of the di-neutron-core degree of freedom permits a fairly accurate description of the elastic scattering of 6He on different targets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a simple two-body di-neutron cluster model can describe elastic scattering of the Borromean nucleus 6He on various targets, provided the 2n-alpha binding energy is adjusted to 1.6 MeV (rather than the two-neutron separation energy of 0.975 MeV) to reproduce the density distribution and transition strengths from a realistic three-body calculation.

## Method

The authors approximate the three-body 6He (n+n+alpha) system as a two-body di-neutron cluster, treating only the (nn)-alpha relative coordinate while ignoring the n-n degree of freedom. They generate bound and continuum states using a Woods-Saxon 2n-alpha potential with fixed geometry (radius 1.90 fm, diffuseness 0.25 fm) and adjust the potential depth to obtain a binding energy of 1.6 MeV, chosen to match the asymptotic slope of the three-body wavefunction. These states are then used in standard Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations to describe elastic scattering of 6He on 64Zn, 208Pb, 209Bi, and 197Au targets.

## Key numbers

- The three-body calculation gives a 6He binding energy of epsilon_b = 0.955 MeV and an rms point nucleon matter radius of 2.557 fm (assuming alpha rms matter radius 1.47 fm).
- The conventional di-neutron model using the two-neutron separation energy (S_2n = 0.975 MeV) yields a 2n-alpha rms of 4.36 fm, while the modified model with epsilon_b = 1.6 MeV gives 3.4 fm, compared to the three-body result of 3.25 fm.
- The 2n-alpha potential depth for s-waves is V_0 = 96.06 MeV with the modified binding energy (1.6 MeV), versus 93.51 MeV with the conventional binding energy (0.975 MeV); for l=2 waves the depths are 92.7 MeV and 91.25 MeV respectively.
- The 2+ resonance is placed at 0.825 MeV above the breakup threshold.
- CDCC calculations include continuum up to a maximum excitation energy of 7 MeV, with N=7 energy bins and s, p, d waves for the 2n-alpha relative motion.

## Context

This work extends the di-neutron cluster model approach previously applied to 6He scattering (Rusek 2001, 2003, 2005; Keeley 2003) by correcting the overestimation of dipole couplings that plagued earlier calculations. The improved model reproduces elastic scattering data for 6He on 64Zn (Di Pietro 2004), 209Bi (Aguilera 2000), 208Pb (Kakuee 2003), and 197Au (Raabe 2001) that full three-body CDCC calculations (Matsumoto 2004, 2006) had successfully described but with much greater complexity. The method could be extended to other Borromean systems such as 11Li or 14Be.
