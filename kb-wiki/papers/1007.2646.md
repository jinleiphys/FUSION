# Alternate proof of the Rowe-Rosensteel proposition and seniority conservation

> For a system with three identical nucleons in a single-$j$ shell, the states can be written as the angular momentum coupling of a nucleon pair and the odd nucleon. The overlaps between these non-orthonormal states form a matrix which coincides with the one derived by Rowe and Rosensteel [Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 87}, 172501 (2001)]. The propositions they state are related to the eigenvalue problems of the matrix and dimensions of the associated subspaces. In this work, the propositions will be proven from the symmetric properties of the $6j$ symbols. Algebraic expressions for the dimension of the states, eigenenergies as well as conditions for conservation of seniority can be derived from the matrix.

## Key claim

The paper proves the Rowe-Rosensteel proposition (that the eigenvalues of the matrix \(M^{\Omega}_{\gamma J}\) must equal \(-1\) or \(2\)) using symmetric properties of \(6j\) symbols rather than quasispin tensor decomposition, and extends the proof to a more general form where the matrix \(M\) has eigenvalues \(1\) or \(-1/2\) for any total spin \(I\), not only the special case \(I=j\) considered by Rowe and Rosensteel.

## Method

The authors construct the overlap matrix \(B^j_I(J_{\alpha}J_{\alpha}')\) for three identical nucleons in a single-\(j\) shell, where states are formed by coupling a nucleon pair of even spin \(J_{\alpha}\) with the odd nucleon to total spin \(I\). Using orthogonality relations of \(6j\) symbols (Eqs. (10.13) and (10.14) of Talmi (1993)), they derive the matrix equation \(2M^2 - M - \mathcal{I} = 0\) for the matrix \(M^j_I(J_{\alpha}J_{\alpha}')\), which directly gives eigenvalues \(1\) and \(-1/2\). Algebraic expressions for the dimensions of orthonormal states and for seniority conservation conditions are then derived from the trace of \(M\) and from symbolic calculations with Racah's analytic formula for the \(6j\) symbol.

## Key numbers

* The eigenvalues of the matrix \(M\) are \(1\) or \(-1/2\) (derived from Eq. (m): \(2M^2-M-\mathcal{I}=0\)).
* The eigenvalues of the overlap matrix \(B\) are \(3\) or \(0\) (from \(B = \mathcal{I} + 2M\) and the eigenvalues of \(M\)).
* For \(I \leq j\), the dimension of orthonormal states is \(D_I(j) = \left[\frac{I+2}{3}\right] = \left[\frac{2I+3}{6}\right]\) (Eq. (d1)).
* For \(I \geq j\), the dimension is \(D_I(j) = \left[\frac{3j+3-I}{6}\right] - \delta_{\text{mod}(3j-3-I,6),1}\).
* The number of linear constraints for seniority conservation is \(n = D_j(j)-1 = [(j-1)/3] = [(2j-3)/6]\).
* For \(j=15/2\), two explicit seniority conservation conditions are given: \(1330V_2-2835V_4-1807V_6+612V_8+3150V_{10}+3175V_{12}-3625V_{14} = 0\) and \(77805V_2-169470V_4-85527V_6-4743V_8+222768V_{10}+168025V_{12}-208858V_{14} = 0\).
* The energy of the unique seniority \(\nu=1\), \(I=j\) state is \(E = \frac{2j-1}{2j+1}V_0 + \sum_{J>0}\frac{4(2J+1)}{(2j+1)(2j-1)}V_J\).

## Context

This work extends the Rowe-Rosensteel proposition (Rowe and Rosensteel, 2001) by providing an alternate proof based on angular momentum coupling and \(6j\) symbol symmetries, rather than the original quasispin tensor decomposition. It also provides algebraic expressions for state dimensions that were empirically obtained by Zhao et al. (2003a) and analytically proved by Talmi (1993). The results enable exact analytic expressions for eigenenergies and wave functions of three-nucleon systems in a single-\(j\) shell, and clarify the conditions under which seniority is conserved, which is relevant for understanding nuclear structure in medium-heavy and heavy semi-magic nuclei.
