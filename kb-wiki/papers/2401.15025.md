# A New Determination of the (Z,A) Dependence of Coherent Muon-to-Electron Conversion

> Should muon-to-electron conversion in the field of a nucleus be found in the current generation of experiments, the measurement of the atomic number dependence of the process will become an important experimental goal. We present a new treatment of the (Z,A) dependence of coherent muon-to-electron conversion in 236 isotopes. Our approach differs from previous treatments in several ways. Firstly, we include the effect of permanent quadrupole deformation on the charged lepton flavor violating matrix elements, using the method of Barrett moments. This method also enables the addition of muonic X-ray nuclear size and shape determinations of the charge distribution to the electron scattering results used previously. Secondly, we employ a Hartree-Bogoliubov model to calculate neutron-related matrix elements for even-even nuclei. This takes into account the quadrupole deformation of the neutron distributions and the fact that neutrons are, in general, in different shell model orbits than protons. The calculated conversion rates differ from previous calculations, particularly in the region of large permanent quadrupole deformation. Finally, we propose an alternative normalization of the muon-to-electron conversion rated, which related more closely to what a given experiment acturally measures, and better separate lepton physics from nuclear physics effects.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new determination of the (Z,A) dependence of coherent muon-to-electron conversion that differs from previous treatments by including the effect of permanent quadrupole deformation on the charged lepton flavor violating matrix elements using Barrett moments, and by employing a deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model for neutron-related matrix elements; the calculated conversion rates differ from previous calculations particularly in the region of large permanent quadrupole deformation.

## Method

The authors combined electron scattering and muonic X-ray measurements of nuclear charge distributions using the method of Barrett moments to include 236 isotopes, incorporating the effect of permanent Y20 quadrupole deformations on the effective nuclear skin thickness. They employed the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to calculate separate neutron and proton distributions for even-even nuclei, accounting for quadrupole deformation of neutron distributions and the fact that neutrons occupy different shell model orbits than protons. The Dirac equation was solved for a spherically-symmetric 2-parameter Fermi distribution, with deformed nuclei converted to an equivalent spherical distribution by adjusting the skin thickness parameter to maintain a constant Barrett moment.

## Key numbers

- The most stringent experimental upper limit on muon-to-electron conversion is Rμe ≤ 7 × 10−13 at 90% C.L. from SINDRUM-II using a gold target
- Mu2e aims to reach a single event sensitivity of ~3 × 10−17 for the conversion rate relative to muon capture
- COMET has a planned sensitivity of 2 × 10−15 for Phase I
- The study includes 236 isotopes with natural abundance above 1%
- For neodymium isotopes, the quadrupole deformation β ranges from 0.104±0.02 (142Nd) to 0.279±0.005 (150Nd) from B(E2) measurements
- The Barrett moment for 142Nd is 15.99±0.03 from muonic X-ray data and 15.98±0.03 from electron scattering data
- The relative uncertainty on the Barrett moment across all available elements never exceeded 1%
- The relative uncertainty on the half-density radius c does not exceed 2% across all isotopes
- The relative uncertainty on β can be up to 20%

## Context

This work extends the treatments of Kitano et al. (2002) and Cirigliano et al. (2009), which included 55 isotopes using mainly electron scattering data and assumed identical neutron and proton distributions scaled by N/Z. The new approach enables inclusion of many strongly-deformed nuclei above Z=60 where muonic X-ray measurements provide precise quadrupole deformation data, and improves upon the neutron distribution treatment using the DRHBc model of Zhang et al. (2022). The results enable future experiments like Mu2e-II and conversion searches at the Advanced Muon Facility to better separate lepton physics from nuclear physics effects when measuring muon-to-electron conversion in different target materials.
