# Nucleon Spin-Polarisabilities from Polarisation Observables in Low-Energy Deuteron Compton Scattering

> We investigate the dependence of polarisation observables in elastic deuteron Compton scattering below the pion production threshold on the spin-independent and spin-dependent iso-scalar dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon. The calculation uses Chiral Effective Field Theory with dynamical Delta(1232) degrees of freedom in the Small Scale Expansion at next-to-leading order. Resummation of the NN intermediate rescattering states and including the Delta induces sizeable effects. The analysis considers cross-sections and the analysing power of linearly polarised photons on an unpolarised target, and cross-section differences and asymmetries of linearly and circularly polarised beams on a vector-polarised deuteron. An intuitive argument helps one to identify kinematics in which one or several polarisabilities do not contribute. Some double-polarised observables are only sensitive to linear combinations of two of the spin-polarisabilities, simplifying a multipole-analysis of the data. Spin-polarisabilities can be extracted at photon energies \gtrsim 100 MeV, after measurements at lower energies of \lesssim 70 MeV provide high-accuracy determinations of the spin-independent ones. An interactive Mathematica 7.0 notebook of our findings is available from hgrie@gwu.edu.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin-polarisabilities of the nucleon can be extracted from polarisation observables in elastic deuteron Compton scattering at photon energies above approximately 100 MeV, but only after measurements at lower energies (below about 70 MeV) provide high-accuracy determinations of the spin-independent polarisabilities; the analysis uses Chiral Effective Field Theory with dynamical Delta(1232) degrees of freedom at next-to-leading order, and the authors caution that their study emphasises relative sensitivity rather than credible absolute predictions.

## Method

The calculation uses Chiral Effective Field Theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom in the Small Scale Expansion at next-to-leading order (order epsilon^3), with the interaction kernel convoluted with phenomenological deuteron wavefunctions (Argonne V18 potential for NN-rescattering, NNLO ChiEFT deuteron wavefunction at cutoff 650 MeV). The analysis considers cross-sections and the analysing power of linearly polarised photons on an unpolarised target, plus cross-section differences and asymmetries of linearly and circularly polarised beams on a vector-polarised deuteron, with the two short-distance contributions to spin-independent polarisabilities fitted to 28 data points of elastic deuteron Compton scattering below 100 MeV using the iso-scalar Baldin sum rule as constraint.

## Key numbers

- Static iso-scalar dipole polarisabilities from the fit: alpha_E1 = 11.3 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3), beta_M1 = 3.2 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3), with uncertainties of +/-0.7_stat +/-0.6_Baldin +/-1_theory
- Iso-scalar spin-polarisabilities (parameter-free predictions, in units of 10^{-4} fm^4): gamma_E1E1 = -5.5, gamma_M1M1 = 3.1, gamma_M1E2 = 1.0, gamma_E1M2 = 1.0
- Baldin sum rule constraint: alpha_E1 + beta_M1 = 14.5 +/- 0.6 (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Neutron forward spin-polarisability gamma_pi^(n) = 58.6 +/- 4.0_model - [42.7 to 46.7]_pi0-pole = [15.9 to 11.9] +/- 4.0_model (in units of 10^{-4} fm^4)
- Analysis limited to photon energies omega less than or approximately 120 MeV to avoid threshold effects
- Rescattering contributions become negligible in unpolarised Compton scattering for omega greater than approximately 60 MeV
- Thomson limit fulfilled at the 0.6% level numerically

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron Compton scattering in Chiral Effective Field Theory by Hildebrandt et al. (2005) and Chen, Ji and Li (2004) to include both the Delta(1232) as a dynamical degree of freedom and a wider energy range from zero to the pion-production threshold. The analysis supports experimental planning at facilities like HIGS, MAMI and MAXlab, and provides a road-map for extracting the six iso-scalar nucleon polarisabilities from polarised deuteron Compton scattering experiments, complementing earlier work on unpolarised deuteron Compton scattering by Beane et al. (1999, 2002, 2004) and Hildebrandt et al. (2005).
