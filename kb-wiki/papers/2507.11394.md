# Bayesian Model Selection and Uncertainty Propagation for Beam Energy Scan Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We apply the Bayesian model selection method (based on the Bayes factor) to optimize $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$-dependence in the phenomenological parameters of the (3+1)-dimensional hybrid framework for describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider. The effects of various experimental measurements on the posterior distribution are investigated. We also make model predictions for longitudinal flow decorrelation, rapidity-dependent anisotropic flow and identified particle $v_0(p_\mathrm{T})$ in Au+Au collisions, as well as anisotropic flow coefficients in small systems. Systematic uncertainties in the model predictions are estimated using the variance of the simulation results with a few parameter sets sampled from the posterior distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bayesian model selection, using the Bayes factor, can quantitatively determine whether introducing collision-energy-dependent parameters into a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid model is justified by experimental data, rather than simply adding complexity. The authors find that only the initial energy smearing parameters (hotspot sizes) show moderate evidence for energy dependence, while most other parameters do not benefit from such an extension.

## Method

The authors performed separate Bayesian analyses for Au+Au collisions at three beam energies (7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV) using the iEBE-MUSIC framework with 3D-Glauber initial conditions, MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic transport. They trained PCSK Gaussian Process emulators as surrogate models for the 20-dimensional parameter space and computed Bayes factors using the pocoMC package to compare model variants where selected parameters were allowed to depend on collision energy. The analysis included 858 experimental data points from STAR and PHOBOS measurements.

## Key numbers

- 20 model parameters were varied in the baseline analysis, with prior ranges listed in Table 1 (e.g., specific shear viscosity parameters eta_0, eta_2, eta_4 each with prior [0.001, 0.3]; switching energy density e_sw with prior [0.15, 0.5] GeV/fm^3)
- 858 total experimental data points were included in the inference across all three collision energies (7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV)
- Bayes factor for sigma_x(sqrt_sNN) model: ln(B_A/B) = -2.00 +/- 0.10, indicating moderate evidence favoring energy dependence
- Bayes factor for combined sigma_x(sqrt_sNN) and sigma_eta(sqrt_sNN) model: ln(B_A/B) = -3.01 +/- 0.08, the strongest preference among tested models
- Bayes factor for constant shear viscosity (eta_0 = eta_2 = eta_4) vs. mu_B-dependent shear viscosity: ln(B_A/B) = 1.91 +/- 0.07, favoring the mu_B-dependent parameterization
- Bayes factor for zeta_max(sqrt_sNN) model: ln(B_A/B) = 0.04 +/- 0.06, showing no significant improvement

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inference studies by Shen (2023) and Jahan (2024) that extracted mu_B-dependent QGP viscosity and initial-state stopping power from RHIC Beam Energy Scan data. The authors build on the Bayesian model selection framework used by JETSCAPE (2020) and Paquet (2023) to systematically test whether introducing collision energy dependence in model parameters is warranted. The results enable more reliable model predictions for longitudinal flow decorrelation, rapidity-dependent anisotropic flow, and identified particle v_0(p_T) in Au+Au collisions, as well as anisotropic flow in small systems, with properly quantified systematic uncertainties.
