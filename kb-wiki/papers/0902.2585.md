# Nuclei and chiral dynamics

> Nuclear theory has entered an exciting era. This is due to advances on many fronts, including the development of effective field theory and the renormalization group for nuclear forces, advances in ab-initio methods for nuclear structure, an effort to develop a universal density functional based on microscopic interactions, and the application of large-scale computing resources. I discuss their impact, recent highlights and the frontiers in understanding and predicting nuclei and the structure of strongly-interacting matter based on chiral interactions.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (EFT) combined with renormalization group (RG) evolution to low-momentum interactions provides a systematic and unified framework for describing nuclei and nuclear matter, but the consistent inclusion of three-nucleon (3N) interactions remains a common frontier, and theoretical uncertainties from cutoff variation and from poorly constrained couplings (especially \(c_3\) and \(c_4\)) must be quantified.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of chiral EFT nuclear forces at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO), their RG evolution to low-momentum interactions \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\), and the fitting of leading 3N interaction couplings to the \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He binding energies or radius. These interactions are then applied in ab-initio many-body methods, coupled-cluster (CC) theory, hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) expansion, and no-core shell model (NCSM), to compute ground-state energies, phase shifts, and nuclear matter properties, with cutoff variation used to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale of chiral EFT is roughly \(\Lambda_b \sim m_\rho\) (not given numerically, but stated as \(\Lambda_b \sim m_\rho\)).
- RG-evolved low-momentum interactions use a smooth regulator with cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (shown in Fig. 2 caption).
- Ground-state energies of \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) are computed with CC theory using a chiral N\(^3\)LO NN potential, with near-converged results obtained in gigantic spaces (15 major shells on a single processor).
- For helium isotopes, ground-state energies are computed with low-momentum NN interactions for cutoffs \(\Lambda = 1.8\), \(2.0\), and \(2.4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (Fig. 4 caption).
- The Tjon-line correlation between \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He binding energies is explained by cutoff dependence from low-momentum NN interactions (Fig. 6).
- Nuclear matter saturation is predicted within theoretical uncertainties when 3N interactions are constrained by the \(^4\)He radius (Fig. 8).
- Neutron matter energy per particle versus density is shown with uncertainties dominated by \(c_i\) values (mainly \(c_3\)) rather than cutoff variation (Fig. 9).
- The spin relaxation rate \(C_\sigma\) in neutron matter is computed from chiral EFT interactions to N\(^3\)LO over a wide density range, showing that two-pion-exchange and shorter-range noncentral forces at NLO reduce the rates significantly (Fig. 12).

## Context

This work extends the program of chiral EFT for nuclear forces (Weinberg 1990, van Kolck 1994, Epelbaum et al. 2005, Entem and Machleidt 2003) by combining it with RG evolution to low-momentum interactions (Bogner et al. 2003, 2007) and applying it to ab-initio calculations of light and medium-mass nuclei, nuclear matter, and neutrino interactions. It enables systematic uncertainty quantification for nuclear structure and astrophysics, and motivates the derivation of a universal nuclear energy density functional (SciDAC UNEDF project). The results contradict the one-pion-exchange approximation for neutrino bremsstrahlung rates (Hannestad and Raffelt 1998) by showing that higher-order chiral contributions significantly reduce the rates.
