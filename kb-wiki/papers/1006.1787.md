# Simultaneous analysis of elastic scattering and transfer/breakup channels for the 6He+208Pb reaction at energies near the Coulomb barrier

> The elastic and alpha-production channels for the 6He+208Pb reaction are investigated at energies around the Coulomb barrier (E_{lab}=14, 16, 18, 22, and 27 MeV). The effect of the two-neutron transfer channels on the elastic scattering has been studied within the Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) method. We find that the explicit inclusion of these channels allows a simultaneous description of the elastic data and the inclusive alpha cross sections at backward angles. Three-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations are found to reproduce the elastic data, but not the transfer/breakup data. The trivially-equivalent local polarization potential (TELP) derived from the CRC and CDCC calculations are found to explain the features found in previous phenomenological optical model calculations for this system.

# Simultaneous analysis of elastic scattering and transfer/breakup channels for the 6He+208Pb reaction at energies near the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
The explicit inclusion of two-neutron transfer channels within the Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) method allows a simultaneous description of both the elastic scattering data and the inclusive alpha cross sections at backward angles, whereas three-body Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations reproduce the elastic data but fail to describe the transfer/breakup data.

## Method
The authors performed Coupled-Reaction-Channels (CRC) calculations for the 6He+208Pb reaction at five laboratory energies (14, 16, 18, 22, and 27 MeV), using a di-neutron model for the 6He projectile and including two-neutron transfer couplings to bound and unbound states of 210Pb. The bare interaction was taken as the Sao Paulo potential (SPP) with a short-range imaginary Woods-Saxon potential to simulate fusion, and the coupled equations were solved iteratively using the code FRESCO with partial waves up to l_f=0-8 for the 2n-208Pb relative motion. For comparison, three-body CDCC calculations were performed using the same di-neutron model with continuum states discretized via the binning method for relative angular momenta l_i=0,1,2.

## Key numbers
- Laboratory energies studied: Elab = 14, 16, 18, 22, and 27 MeV
- For the di-neutron model: Woods-Saxon potential with radius R=1.9 fm and diffuseness a0=0.39 fm; effective two-neutron separation energy S2n=1.6 MeV
- For the 2+ resonance in 6He: excitation energy Ex=1.8 MeV above the ground state
- Maximum excitation energy for CDCC continuum: ranging from 5 MeV (at Elab=14 MeV) to 8 MeV (at Elab=27 MeV)
- For CRC calculations: 2n-208Pb continuum discretized using 1 MeV bins up to a maximum relative energy of 5 MeV; 5 bound states evenly spaced in steps of 2 MeV below threshold
- Partial waves required for convergence: l_f=0-8 for CRC; l_i=0,1,2 for CDCC
- Bare interaction imaginary Woods-Saxon parameters: W=50 MeV, R0=1 fm, a0=0.1 fm

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological optical model analyses by Kak (2003, 2006) and Sanchez (2008), which required very diffuse imaginary potentials (diffuseness approximately 2 fm) to reproduce elastic data for the 6He+208Pb system, a phenomenon termed "long range absorption." The CRC calculations confirm that two-neutron transfer channels, previously treated only in DWBA by Escrig (2007), can explain both the elastic scattering suppression and the large alpha particle yield without phenomenological fitting. The results enable future studies of weakly bound projectiles where transfer and breakup channels compete, and suggest that CDCC and CRC methods populate largely the same final states despite different basis representations.
