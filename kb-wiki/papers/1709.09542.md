# Lepton Number Violation, Lepton Flavour Violation and Baryogenesis in Left-Right Symmetric Model

> We did a model independent phenomenological study of baryogenesis via leptogenesis, neutrinoless double beta decay (NDBD) and charged lepton flavour violation (CLFV) in a generic left-right symmetric model (LRSM) where neutrino mass originates from the type I + type II seesaw mechanism. We studied the new physics contributions to NDBD coming from the left-right gauge boson mixing and the heavy neutrino contribution within the framework of LRSM. We have considered the mass of the RH gauge boson to be specifically 5 TeV, 10 TeV and 18 TeV and studied the effects of the new physics contributions on the effective mass and baryogenesis and compared with the current experimental limit. We tried to correlate the cosmological BAU from resonant leptogenesis with the low energy observables, notably, NDBD and LFV with a view to finding a common parameter space where they coexists.

# Lepton Number Violation, Lepton Flavour Violation and Baryogenesis in Left-Right Symmetric Model

## Key claim

The authors perform a model-independent phenomenological study showing that resonant leptogenesis, neutrinoless double beta decay (NDBD), and charged lepton flavour violation (CLFV) can coexist in a common parameter space within a generic left-right symmetric model (LRSM) with type I + type II seesaw, but only when the right-handed gauge boson mass is specifically chosen at 5 TeV, 10 TeV, or 18 TeV and the light-heavy neutrino mixing is not required to be large (of order 10^{-6}) to obtain sizeable baryon asymmetry and NDBD effective mass.

## Method

The authors construct the neutrino mass matrix in LRSM using a combination of type I and type II seesaw mechanisms, with a tribimaximal mixing pattern and equal relative strength (X = 0.5) between the two seesaw terms. They compute the CP-violating asymmetries from the decay of two quasi-degenerate heavy right-handed Majorana neutrinos (resonant leptogenesis) and calculate the baryon asymmetry using efficiency factors for washout effects. For NDBD, they consider only the momentum-dependent lambda and eta contributions (light-heavy mixing and left-right gauge boson mixing) and the purely right-handed neutrino contribution, while for LFV they compute branching ratios for mu -> 3e and mu -> e gamma mediated by heavy neutrinos and doubly charged triplet Higgs bosons.

## Key numbers

- Right-handed gauge boson masses considered: 5 TeV, 10 TeV, and 18 TeV
- Left-handed gauge boson mass: M_WL = 80 GeV
- Light-heavy neutrino mixing parameter zeta approximately 10^{-6}
- Baryon asymmetry of the universe reference value: eta_B = (6.5^{+0.4}_{-0.3}) x 10^{-10}
- Current experimental upper limit on NDBD half-life from KamLAND-Zen: T_{1/2}^{0nu} > 1.07 x 10^{26} yr at 90% CL, corresponding to effective Majorana mass range (0.061-0.165) eV
- Current experimental upper limit on BR(mu -> 3e): < 1.0 x 10^{-12} at 90% CL (SINDRUM)
- Current experimental upper limit on BR(mu -> e gamma): < 4.2 x 10^{-13} at 90% CL (MEG)
- Dimensionless parameter gamma fine-tuned to approximately 10^{-10}
- Higgs bidoublet VEV v = 174 GeV
- Planck mass: 1.2 x 10^{19} GeV
- Effective degrees of freedom g_* approximately 107

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the lower bound of M_WR for successful leptogenesis, where earlier work by the authors of reference (18 TeV bound) found an absolute lower bound of 18 TeV for maximal CP asymmetry epsilon = 1, while subsequent work (3 TeV bound and 10 TeV bound) showed that with specific symmetry textures of the Dirac and Majorana mass matrices and larger Yukawa couplings, the bound could be lowered to 3 TeV or 10 TeV. The present study differs by using a general (non-specific) structure for the Dirac mass matrix obtained from the type I seesaw formula, and by correlating baryogenesis simultaneously with NDBD and LFV observables to find a common parameter space, rather than treating these phenomena separately as in prior works. The results enable future experimental searches at colliders and in NDBD experiments to test whether leptogenesis, NDBD, and LFV can all be realized within the same TeV-scale LRSM parameter space.
