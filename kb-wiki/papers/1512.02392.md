# QMC approach based on the Bogoliubov independent quark model of the nucleon

> The quark-meson coupling model due to Guichon is formulated on the basis of the independent quark model of the nucleon proposed by Bogoliubov and is applied to the phenomenological descriptions of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. For symmetric matter, the model predicts, at saturation density, the incompressibility $K=335.17$ MeV, the quark effective mass $m_q^*=238.5$ MeV, and the effective nucleon mass $M^*= 0.76 M,$ where $M$ is the nucleon mass in vacuum. Neutron star massesabove two solar masses are obtained.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, originally formulated with the MIT bag model by Guichon, is re-derived using the Bogoliubov independent quark model with a linearly rising confining potential; the model predicts nuclear matter saturation properties and neutron star masses above two solar masses, but the incompressibility comes out "a bit too high" compared to some empirical estimates.

## Method

The authors formulate the QMC model by replacing the MIT bag description of the nucleon with the Bogoliubov independent quark model, where quarks are confined by a Dirac scalar linear potential with string tension κ. The quark mass in the presence of an external scalar σ field is obtained by solving a Klein-Gordon-type equation (simplified model) and a full Dirac equation (full model) using variational ansätze with Gaussian trial wavefunctions. The energy density of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter is computed in the mean-field approximation, with the σ and ω meson fields determined by minimization, and the parameters are fitted to reproduce the saturation density (0.15 fm⁻³) and binding energy (−15.7 MeV) of symmetric nuclear matter, as well as the vacuum nucleon mass (939 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility at saturation: K = 335.17 MeV (full model, eq. (m²-alpha))
- Quark effective mass at saturation: m_q* = 238.5 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M* = 0.76 M (where M = 939 MeV is the free nucleon mass)
- String tension: κ = 37106 MeV² (full model) vs. κ = 32656 MeV² (simplified model)
- Maximum gravitational neutron star mass: M_max = 2.40 M⊙ (full model), 2.22 M⊙ (simplified exact), 2.21 M⊙ (simplified variational)
- Neutron star radius at maximum mass: R = 12.65 km (full model)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32 MeV (used to fix ρ-quark coupling)

## Context

This work extends the QMC approach of Guichon (1988) by replacing the MIT bag model with the Bogoliubov independent quark model (Bogoliubov, 1968), which uses a linearly rising confining potential motivated by gauge theories. The results are compared to the original QMC model based on the MIT bag, which gives an incompressibility of 291 MeV. The predicted incompressibility of 335.17 MeV is higher than the empirical value of 288 ± 20 MeV from Satpathy and Nayak and the value of 230 ± 40 MeV from Vidana constrained by giant monopole resonance data. The model successfully produces neutron star masses above 2 M⊙, consistent with the pulsar PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013). Future work will consider a consistent equation of state for the hadron-quark phase transition using the Bogoliubov model for the quark phase.
