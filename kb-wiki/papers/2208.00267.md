# Sub-barrier d+$^{208}$Pb scattering and sensitivity to nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We employ the non-perturbative time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach to solve for the sub-Coulomb barrier scattering of the deuteron projectile on the $^{208}$Pb target. Specifically, we treat the target as a source of a strong external Coulomb field that induces higher-order effects in electric-dipole excitations of the deuteron projectile including the population of states not accessible through direct electric-dipole transitions from the ground state of the deuteron. We calculate the electric-dipole polarizability of the deuteron and elastic scattering observables for comparison with experimental data. With no adjustable parameters, the tBF approach provides good agreement with experimentally available differential cross-section ratios. The dependence of these measured quantities on nucleon-nucleon interactions is investigated. We also investigate the detailed scattering dynamics and identify characteristics of coherent and incoherent processes.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach, with no adjustable parameters, provides good agreement with experimentally available differential cross-section ratios for sub-Coulomb barrier deuteron scattering on 208Pb, but the calculated electric-dipole polarizability of the deuteron shows an approximately 8% spread depending on the choice of nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, with all results remaining consistent with experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the deuteron projectile moving on a classical Rutherford trajectory modified by a polarization potential, using a basis of deuteron eigenstates obtained from diagonalizing the NN Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis. They considered only electric-dipole (E1) transitions among states with total spin S=1 and total angular momentum J≤2, employing five different NN interactions: JISP16, Daejeon16, and three chiral effective field theory interactions from LENPIC at N2LO, N3LO, and N4LO orders. The time evolution was computed numerically using a non-perturbative multistep differencing scheme up to second order (MSD2), and elastic scattering probabilities were obtained by summing over the three orientations of the deuteron ground state after evolution.

## Key numbers

- Sommerfeld parameter minimum value: 6.93, corresponding to the maximum incident deuteron energy of 7 MeV
- E1 polarizability of deuteron with LENPIC-N2LO: 0.6292(1) fm³
- E1 polarizability of deuteron with LENPIC-N3LO: 0.6352(1) fm³
- E1 polarizability of deuteron with LENPIC-N4LO: 0.6349(1) fm³
- E1 polarizability of deuteron with JISP16: 0.6164(1) fm³
- E1 polarizability of deuteron with Daejeon16: 0.6658(1) fm³
- Spread of E1 polarizability across the five interactions: about 8%
- Experimental E1 polarizability from Rodning (1982): 0.70(5) fm³
- Experimental E1 polarizability from Friar (1983): 0.61(4) fm³
- Basis truncation parameters used for converged results: Nmax=200, ω=20 MeV, Ecut=14 MeV, providing 165 states of the np system
- Convergence of observables to order 10⁻⁴ under the chosen truncation parameters

## Context

This work extends the tBF approach previously developed by Du (2018) and applied to deuteron scattering on 208Pb by Yin (2019), now systematically testing sensitivity to five different NN interactions including chiral EFT interactions from the LENPIC collaboration (Epelbaum 2014, Binder 2016, Binder 2018). The results are compared with experimental data from Rodning (1982) and Friar (1983), and with prior theoretical calculations using Reid soft core, Argonne V14, Argonne V18, Bonn (CS), and Paris interactions reported by Friar (1997). The approach enables future applications to reactions involving unstable rare isotopes at radioactive ion beam facilities, and the authors note the method is well-suited for quantum computing applications in nuclear scattering dynamics.
