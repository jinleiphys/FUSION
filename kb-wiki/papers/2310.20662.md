# Harmonics of Lepton-Jet Correlations in inclusive and diffractive scatterings

> Based on our previous work, we study the harmonic coefficient of both inclusive and diffractive azimuthal angle dependent lepton-jet correlations in Hadron-Electron Ring Accelerator and the future electron-ion collider. Numerical calculations for inclusive and diffractive harmonics and the ratio of harmonics in $e+\text{Au}$~and $e+p$ indicate their strong discriminating power for non-saturation model and saturation model. Additionally, we demonstrate that the t-dependent diffractive harmonics can serve as novel observables for nuclear density profile.

## Key claim

The harmonic coefficients of lepton-jet correlations in both inclusive and diffractive deep inelastic scattering can discriminate between saturation and non-saturation models, with the suppression of harmonics being more pronounced for gold targets than for proton targets because the saturation scale grows with nuclear size; the t-dependent diffractive harmonics are additionally sensitive to the nuclear density profile.

## Method

The authors calculate the azimuthal-angle-dependent lepton-jet cross section in the correlation limit (back-to-back configuration) using small-x factorization with the Color Glass Condensate effective theory for the saturation model, and collinear TMD factorization with CT18A (proton) and EPPS21 (gold) PDFs for the non-saturation model. They include multiple soft gluon resummation via Sudakov factors, QED soft-photon corrections, and evaluate the dipole scattering matrix using both the GBW parameterization and the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with modified MV initial conditions. For diffractive production, they extend the formalism to include t-dependent diffractive harmonics.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum for proton: \(Q_{s,p}^2(x) = (x_0/x)^{0.28}~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(x_0 = 3\times 10^{-4}\) (GBW model)
- Saturation momentum for gold nucleus: \(Q_{s,A}^2 \approx 5 Q_{s,p}^2\)
- rcBK initial condition parameters: \(\gamma = 1.118\), \(\Lambda = 0.241\) GeV, \(Q_{s0,p}^2 = 0.16\) GeV\(^2\) at \(x_0 = 0.01\)
- EIC kinematics used: \(\sqrt{s_{eN}} = 89\) GeV, \(y_\ell = 2.41\), \(0.008 \leq x \leq 0.01\), \(4~\text{GeV} \leq P_\perp \leq 4.4~\text{GeV}\), \(5.6~\text{GeV} \leq Q \leq 5.9~\text{GeV}\)
- Jet cone size: \(R = 0.4\)
- Saddle point values for EIC kinematics: \(b^{\text{sp}}_{\perp 0} \approx 1.5~\text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(b^{\text{sp}}_{\perp n} \approx 2.5~\text{GeV}^{-1}\) for \(n = 1, 2, 3\)
- Non-perturbative Sudakov \(b_\text{max} = 1.5~\text{GeV}^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Tong, 2022) on lepton-jet harmonics as a probe of gluon saturation in DIS, and builds on the formalism for azimuthal anisotropy from soft gluon radiation developed by Hatta et al. (2021) and Hatta et al. (2020). It also follows recent work on diffractive parton distributions by Iancu et al. (2021) and Hatta et al. (2022). The results enable experimental searches for saturation at the future Electron-Ion Collider and at HERA, where the first lepton-jet correlation measurement was published by the H1 collaboration (2021).
