# Early signal of emerging nuclear collectivity in neutron-rich $^{129}$Sb

> Radioactive $^{129}$Sb, which can be treated as a proton plus semi-magic $^{128}$Sn core within the particle-core coupling scheme, was studied by Coulomb excitation. Reduced electric quadrupole transition probabilities, $B(E2)$, for the $2^+$ $\times$ $πg_{7/2}$ multiplet members and candidate $πd_{5/2}$ state were measured. The results indicate that the total electric quadrupole strength of $^{129}$Sb is a factor of 1.39(11) larger than the $^{128}$Sn core, which is in stark contrast to the expectations of the empirically successful particle-core coupling scheme. Shell-model calculations performed with two different sets of nucleon-nucleon interactions suggest that this enhanced collectivity is due to constructive quadrupole coherence in the wavefunctions stemming from the proton-neutron residual interactions, where adding one nucleon to a core near a double-shell closure can have a pronounced effect. The enhanced electric quadrupole strength is an early signal of the emerging nuclear collectivity that becomes dominant away from the shell closure.

## Key claim

The total electric quadrupole strength of $^{129}$Sb is a factor of 1.39(11) larger than that of its $^{128}$Sn core, contradicting the particle-core coupling sum rule which predicts equal strengths; this enhancement is interpreted as an early signal of emerging nuclear collectivity, with the caveat that shell-model calculations under-predict the observed strength but qualitatively reproduce the enhancement through constructive proton-neutron quadrupole coherence.

## Method

A radioactive ion beam of $^{129}$Sb at 400 MeV was Coulomb excited on a $^{50}$Ti target at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility. Recoiling target nuclei were detected in a 2$\pi$ CsI array (BareBall) and coincident $\gamma$ rays were measured in a Compton-suppressed HPGe Clover array (CLARION). Reduced electric quadrupole transition probabilities $B(E2)$ were extracted using the semi-classical Coulomb-excitation code GOSIA, with absolute normalization relative to the known $B(E2)$ of $^{48}$Ti, and compared to shell-model calculations using two different nucleon-nucleon interactions (SM1 with NuShellX and SM2 with Antoine).

## Key numbers

- Total $B(E2\uparrow)$ sum for $^{129}$Sb is 29.1(25) W.u., giving a ratio of 1.39(11) relative to the $^{128}$Sn core $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ value
- Individual $B(E2\uparrow)$ values for the $2^+ \otimes \pi g_{7/2}$ multiplet members: $\nicefrac{3}{2}^+$ state at 913 keV has 6.4(7) W.u.; $\nicefrac{11}{2}^+$ state at 1128 keV has 11.3(7) W.u.; $\nicefrac{9}{2}^+$ state at 1161 keV has 9.2(8) W.u.; $\nicefrac{5}{2}^+$ ground state at 645 keV has 1.52(25) W.u.; $\nicefrac{5}{2}_2^+$ state at 1252 keV has 0.75(9) W.u.
- The $^{128}$Sn core $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ is taken from prior work (JMA) and the $^{48}$Ti reference $B(E2; 0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)$ is 0.0662(29) e$^2$b$^2$
- Beam composition: 41(2)% $^{129}$Sb, 6.2(7)% $^{129}$Sn, 52(1)% $^{129}$Te; of the $^{129}$Sb component, 63(2)% was in the ground state and 37(2)% in the $\nicefrac{19}{2}^-$ isomeric state

## Context

This work extends the particle-core coupling framework of de-Shalit (1950s) and Bohr and Mottelson (1950s-1970s), which had been empirically validated by Tuttle et al. (1976) for $^{113,115}$In, by demonstrating a clear breakdown of the $E2$ sum rule near the $^{132}$Sn double-shell closure. The results enable quantitative study of how proton-neutron residual interactions drive the emergence of collectivity, complementing recent work by Peters et al. on $^{132}$Xe, and provide a benchmark for shell-model calculations in the $^{132}$Sn region.
