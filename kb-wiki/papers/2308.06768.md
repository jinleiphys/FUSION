# $X(3960)$, $X_0(4140)$, and other compact $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ states

> We study the spectrum and rearrangement decays of S-wave $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark states in a simplified quark model. The masses and widths are estimated by assuming that the $X(4140)$ is the lower $1^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark. Comparing our results with experimental measurements, we find that the recently observed $X(3960)$ by LHCb can be assigned as the lowest $0^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark state and the $X_0(4140)$ could be the second lowest $0^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark. Predictions of ratios between partial widths for the involved tetraquarks are given. We call for searches for more $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$, $0^{++}$, and $2^{++}$.

## Key claim

The authors assign the recently observed $X(3960)$ as the lowest $0^{++}$ compact $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark state and the $X_0(4140)$ as the second lowest $0^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark, but only under the condition that the $X(4140)$ (with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$) is taken as the reference state to fix the model parameters; using the $X(4274)$ as the reference gives a worse description.

## Method

The authors use a color-magnetic interaction (CMI) model to compute the mass spectrum of S-wave $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark states, with coupling constants extracted from conventional hadron masses. They then calculate rearrangement decay widths using a simplified constant Hamiltonian $H_{decay}={\cal C}$, where the single parameter ${\cal C}$ is fixed by assuming the $X_1(4140)$ is the lower $1^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark and using its measured width from LHCb (2016). The decay amplitudes are obtained by recoupling the initial tetraquark wave functions into final meson-meson states in color and spin space.

## Key numbers

- The lowest $0^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark has mass 3938.2 MeV and total width 31.8 MeV, making it a candidate for the $X(3960)$ (measured mass $3956\pm5\pm10$ MeV, width $43\pm13\pm8$ MeV).
- The second lowest $0^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark has mass 4078.5 MeV and width 60.2 MeV, compared to the $X_0(4140)$ (measured mass $4133\pm6\pm6$ MeV, width $67\pm17\pm7$ MeV).
- For the lowest $0^{++}$ state assigned as $X(3960)$, the predicted decay ratio is $\Gamma(\eta_c\eta)/\Gamma(D_s^+D_s^-)\simeq 7.8$.
- For the two $2^{++}$ $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks (masses 4316.9 and 4294.6 MeV), the predicted width ratios are $\Gamma(J/\psi\phi)/\Gamma(D_s^{*+}D_s^{*-})\simeq 2.3$ (higher state) and $\simeq 0.4$ (lower state).
- The coupling parameter ${\cal C}=72822$ MeV is extracted from the $X_1(4140)$ width of 83 MeV (LHCb 2016).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CMI model study of $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark spectra (Wu et al., 2016) by adding decay calculations using the same simplified scheme previously applied to pentaquarks (Cheng et al., 2019; Li et al., 2023) and tetraquarks with different flavors (Cheng et al., 2020). It competes with molecular interpretations of $X(3960)$ (Bayar et al., 2022; Ji et al., 2022) and with QCD sum rule calculations (Agaev et al., 2022) that also assign $X(3960)$ as a scalar tetraquark. The results enable experimental searches for additional $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ states with $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$, $0^{++}$, and $2^{++}$ in channels such as $\eta_c\eta$, $D_s^+D_s^-$, $J/\psi\phi$, and $D_s^{*+}D_s^{*-}$.
