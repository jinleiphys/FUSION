# Determination of nuclear matter radii by means of microscopic optical potentials: the case of $^{78}$Kr

> In this work we use microscopic Nucleon-Nucleus Optical Potentials (OP) to analyze elastic scattering data for the differential cross section of the $^{78}$Kr (p,p) $^{78}$Kr reaction, with the goal of extracting the matter radius and estimating the neutron skin, quantities that are both needed to determine the slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy. Our analysis is performed with the factorized version of the microscopic OP obtained in a previous series of papers within the Watson multiple scattering theory at the first order of the spectator expansion, which is based on the underlying nucleon-nucleon dynamics and is free from phenomenological inputs. Differently from our previous applications, the proton and neutron densities are described with a two-parameter Fermi (2pF) distribution, which makes the extraction of the matter radius easier and allows us to make a meaningful comparison with the original analysis, that was performed with the Glauber model. With standard minimization techniques we performed data analysis and extracted the matter radius and the neutron skin. Our analysis produces a matter radius of $R_m^{\rm (rms)} = 4.12$ fm, in good agreement with previous matter radii extracted from $^{76}$Kr and $^{80}$Kr, and a neutron skin of $ΔR_{np} \simeq - 0.1$ fm, compatible with a previous analysis. Our factorized microscopic OP, supplied with 2pF densities, is a valuable tool to perform the analysis of the experimental differential cross section and extract information such as matter radius and neutron skin. Without any free parameters it provides a reasonably good description of the experimental differential cross section for scattering angles up to $\approx$ 40 degrees. Compared to the Glauber model our OP can be applied to a wider range of scattering angles and allows one to probe the nuclear systems in a more internal region.

## Key claim
The factorized microscopic optical potential, when supplied with two-parameter Fermi (2pF) densities, can extract the matter radius and neutron skin of $^{78}$Kr from elastic scattering data with only one free parameter (the neutron half-height radius), and provides a good description of the differential cross section for scattering angles up to approximately 40 degrees without any phenomenological inputs.

## Method
The authors used the factorized version of a microscopic nucleon-nucleus optical potential derived from Watson multiple scattering theory at first order of the spectator expansion, with chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions (NNLO$_{\mathrm{opt}}$ and N$^4$LO) as the only input. They described the proton and neutron densities of $^{78}$Kr with a two-parameter Fermi distribution, fixed the proton half-height radius to reproduce the known proton rms radius of 4.13 fm, and performed a $\chi^2$ minimization with the neutron half-height radius and an absolute normalization as free parameters to fit the relative differential cross section data for $^{78}$Kr(p,p)$^{78}$Kr at 152.7 MeV/u.

## Key numbers
- Matter radius: $R_m^{\rm (rms)} = 4.12$ fm, extracted from the fit to elastic scattering data for $^{78}$Kr(p,p)$^{78}$Kr at 152.7 MeV/u.
- Neutron skin: $\Delta R_{np} \simeq -0.1$ fm, obtained as the difference between neutron and proton rms radii.
- Neutron half-height radius from fit: $R_n^{\rm (2pF)} = 4.62$ fm, with corresponding neutron rms radius $R_n^{\rm (rms)} = 4.12$ fm.
- Proton half-height radius fixed to $R_p^{\rm (2pF)} = 4.63$ fm, giving proton rms radius $R_p^{\rm (rms)} = 4.13$ fm.
- Diffuseness parameters fixed to $a_n = a_p = 0.55$ fm.
- Fit quality: $\chi^2/{\rm datum} \leq 3$ for the best fit using the NNLO$_{\mathrm{opt}}$ potential.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous series of microscopic optical potential calculations (Vorabbi 2015, 2017, 2018) by using a simplified factorized version with 2pF densities to make the tool accessible for experimental data analysis, bridging the gap between theoretical and experimental approaches. It directly compares with the Glauber model analysis of the same $^{78}$Kr data (PhysRevC.108.014614), showing that the microscopic optical potential can be applied to a wider range of scattering angles and probe more internal regions of the nucleus. The extracted matter radius agrees with previous values for $^{76}$Kr and $^{80}$Kr (PhysRevC.77.034315), and the neutron skin result is compatible with the earlier Glauber analysis, enabling future systematic extraction of matter radii and neutron skins needed to determine the slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy.
