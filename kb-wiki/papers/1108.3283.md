# Phenomenological and microscopic cluster models I. The geometric mapping

> The geometrical mapping of algebraic nuclear cluster models is investigated within the coherent state formalism. Two models are considered: the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) and the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM), which is a special limit of the SACM. The SACM strictly observes the Pauli exclusion principle while the PACM does not. The discussion of the SACM is adapted to the coherent state formalism by introducing the new SO(3) dynamical symmetry limit and third-order interaction terms in the Hamiltonian. The potential energy surface is constructed in both models and it is found that the effects of the Pauli principle can be simulated by higher-order interaction terms in the PACM. The present study is also meant to serve as a starting point for investigating phase transitions in the two algebraic cluster models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Pauli exclusion principle, strictly enforced in the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM), can be simulated in the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM) by introducing higher-order interaction terms, but only when the minimal number of relative oscillation quanta \(n_0\) is set to zero in the PACM as a special limit of the SACM.

## Method

The authors applied the coherent state formalism to construct potential energy surfaces for both the SACM and the PACM, introducing a new \(SO(3)\) dynamical symmetry limit and third-order interaction terms in the SACM Hamiltonian to adapt it to this formalism. They derived the geometric mapping by evaluating the expectation value of the algebraic Hamiltonian with respect to a coherent state, expressed in terms of the inter-cluster distance variable \(\alpha\), and compared the resulting potentials between the two models. The analysis included a renormalization of the variable \(\alpha\) to remove unphysical dependence on the total boson number \(N\), which serves as a cut-off rather than a particle number.

## Key numbers

* The minimal number of relative oscillation quanta in the SACM, \(n_0\), is set to zero in the PACM limit, defining the key difference between the two models.
* The expectation value of the one-body interaction \(a_1 \boldsymbol{n}_\pi\) in the PACM is given by \(a_1 N \frac{\alpha^2}{(1+\alpha^2)}\), which scales with the total boson number \(N\).
* After renormalization with \(\alpha^2 \sim \delta^2 / N\), the expectation value of \(\boldsymbol{n}_\pi\) becomes \(\frac{\delta^2}{(1+\frac{1}{N}\delta^2)}\), removing the global dependence on \(N\).
* The total number of relative oscillation quanta in the coherent state is redefined as \((N+n_0)\), where \(n_0\) is the minimal number of quanta required by the Wildermuth condition.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of phase transitions in algebraic models, such as the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) (Gilmore, 1970s; Kirson, 1970s) and the vibron model (Roosmalen et al., 1980s), by applying the coherent state method to cluster models that include the Pauli principle. It builds on the SACM formalism (Sacm1, Sacm2) and earlier geometric mapping of the SACM (Geom, 2000s), and it enables future investigation of phase transitions between the \(SU(3)\) and \(SO(4)\) dynamical symmetries in nuclear cluster systems, as well as the role of the Pauli principle in determining the order of such transitions.
