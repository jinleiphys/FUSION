# The multiple-charm hierarchy in the statistical hadronization model

> In relativistic nuclear collisions the production of hadrons with light (u,d,s) quarks is quantitatively described in the framework of the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM). Charm quarks are dominantly produced in initial hard collisions but interact strongly in the hot fireball and thermalize. Therefore charmed hadrons can be incorporated into the SHM by treating charm quarks as 'impurities' with thermal distributions, while the total charm content of the fireball is fixed by the measured open charm cross section. We call this model SHMc and demonstrate that with SHMc the measured multiplicities of single charm hadrons in lead-lead collisions at LHC energies can be well described with the same thermal parameters as for (u,d,s) hadrons. Furthermore, transverse momentum distributions are computed in a blast-wave model, which includes the resonance decay kinematics. SHMc is extended to lighter collision systems down to oxygen-oxygen and includes doubly- and triply-charmed hadrons. We show predictions for production probabilities of such states exhibiting a characteristic and quite spectacular enhancement hierarchy.

### Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the Statistical Hadronization Model extended to charm quarks (SHMc) can describe measured single-charm hadron multiplicities in lead-lead collisions at LHC energies using the same thermal parameters as for light-flavor hadrons, and predicts a strong enhancement hierarchy for doubly- and triply-charmed hadrons. The key condition is that charm quarks must be fully deconfined and thermalized in the quark-gluon plasma prior to hadronization for this enhancement pattern to emerge.

### Method
The authors extend the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) to include charm quarks as thermalized "impurities" whose total yield is fixed by the measured open charm cross section, not by the fireball temperature. They solve the charm balance equation to determine the charm fugacity factor \(g_c\), then compute rapidity densities of charmed hadrons using a grand-canonical partition function with canonical corrections for smaller collision systems. Transverse momentum distributions are calculated using a blast-wave model that includes resonance decay kinematics, and a core-corona picture is applied to describe centrality dependence.

### Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: \(T_{cf} = 156.5\) MeV, used for all charm hadron calculations.
- Fireball volume for central 0-10% Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV: \(V = 4997 \pm 455\) fm\(^3\).
- Charm fugacity factor for central Pb-Pb collisions: \(g_c = 29.6 \pm 5.2\).
- Rapidity density of charm quark pairs in central Pb-Pb collisions: \(\ud N_{c\bar{c}}/\ud y = 12.95 \pm 2.27\).
- Enhancement factor for doubly-charmed hadrons relative to purely thermal: approximately 900; for triply-charmed hadrons: approximately \(2.6 \times 10^4\).
- Canonical correction factors for central Pb-Pb: 0.98 for single-charm, 0.92 for double-charm, and 0.84 for triple-charm hadrons.

### Context
This work extends the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) for light-flavor hadrons (Andronic 2017) to the charm sector, building on earlier developments by Braun-Munzinger (2000) and Andronic (2003, 2006). It provides the first comprehensive SHMc predictions for multi-charm hadrons (\(\Xi_{cc}\), \(\Omega_{ccc}\)) in heavy-ion collisions, enabled by recent ALICE and STAR data on open charm hadrons. The predictions can be tested in LHC Run3 and Run4, and a future ALICE3 experiment could access the full charmed hadron spectrum, providing a direct test of charm quark deconfinement in the quark-gluon plasma.
