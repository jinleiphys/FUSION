# Deciphering the charged heavy quarkoniumlike states in chiral effective field theory

> We generalize the framework of chiral effective field theory to study the interactions of the isovector $D^\ast\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$ and $B^\ast\bar{B}^{(\ast)}$ systems up to the next-to-leading order, in which the long-, mid-, and short-range force contributions as well as the $S$-$D$ wave mixing are incorporated. Based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, we fit the invariant mass distributions of the elastic channels measured by the BESIII and Belle Collaborations. Our results indicate that the four charged charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike states $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$ and $Z_b(10610)$, $Z_b(10650)$ can be well identified as the $D\bar{D}^{\ast},D^\ast\bar{D}^{\ast}$ and $B\bar{B}^{\ast},B^\ast\bar{B}^{\ast}$ molecular resonances. The bound state explanations are vetoed in our framework. Our study favors the $Z_c$ and $Z_b$ states are the twin partners under the heavy quark symmetry.

# Deciphering the charged heavy quarkoniumlike states in chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The four charged charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike states Zc(3900), Zc(4020), Zb(10610), and Zb(10650) can be identified as molecular resonances (not bound states) generated from D*D̅(*), D*D̅*, BB̅*, and B*B̅* interactions respectively, when the chiral effective field theory potentials are extended to next-to-leading order. The bound state explanations are vetoed in this framework because the leading-order-only fits produce cutoff parameters exceeding the chiral effective field theory validity scale of approximately 0.77 GeV.

## Method

The authors generalized chiral effective field theory to study isovector D*D̅(*) and B*B̅(*) systems up to next-to-leading order, incorporating long-range one-pion-exchange, mid-range two-pion-exchange, short-range contact interactions, and S-D wave mixing. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to fit the invariant mass distributions of elastic channels measured by BESIII and Belle Collaborations, with four free parameters (three partial-wave low-energy constants and a cutoff) fitted to the experimental lineshapes.

## Key numbers

- Zc(3900) pole mass: 3881.3 +3.0/-3.0 MeV, width: 12.4 +5.0/-5.0 MeV (experimental: mass 3881.7 +2.3/-2.3 MeV, width 26.6 +3.0/-3.0 MeV from Ablikim (2015))
- Zc(4020) pole mass: 4026.5 +4.5/-4.5 MeV, width: 10.1 +7.2/-7.2 MeV (experimental: mass 4025.5 +3.7/-5.6 MeV, width 26.0 +6.0/-6.0 MeV from Ablikim (2015))
- Zb(10610) pole mass: 10607.9 +2.2/-2.2 MeV, width: 10.9 +3.0/-3.0 MeV (experimental: mass 10607.2 +2.0/-2.0 MeV, width 18.4 +2.4/-2.4 MeV from Belle (2011))
- Zb(10650) pole mass: 10652.8 +2.7/-2.7 MeV, width: 10.9 +3.4/-3.4 MeV (experimental: mass 10652.2 +1.5/-1.5 MeV, width 11.5 +2.2/-2.2 MeV from Belle (2011))
- Thresholds: D*D̅* at 3875.8 MeV, D*D̅* at 4017.1 MeV, BB̅* at 10604.4 MeV, B*B̅* at 10649.4 MeV
- Axial coupling g = 0.57 for charmed mesons (from D*+ to D0π+ decay), g = 0.52 for bottom mesons (from lattice QCD)
- Pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV
- Cutoff values: 0.33 GeV (Zc), 0.34 GeV (Zc'), 0.51 GeV (Zb), 0.51 GeV (Zb')

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory, previously successful for nucleon-nucleon interactions (Weinberg 1990, 1991; Epelbaum 2008; Machleidt 2011), to heavy meson systems for the first time at next-to-leading order. It contradicts bound-state interpretations of the Zc and Zb states, favoring instead a molecular resonance picture where the peaks arise from poles in the second Riemann sheet of the T-matrix. The framework enables systematic study of other near-threshold exotic states (such as the Pc pentaquarks from LHCb and the recently reported X0,1 states) to determine whether they share the same origin as dynamically generated resonances from meson-meson interactions.
