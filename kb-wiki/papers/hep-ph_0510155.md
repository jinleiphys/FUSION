# A sign change of the moment at $n=0$ of the polarized structure function $g_1^{p}$

> The sum rule for the structure function $g_1^{p}$ which is related to the cross section of the photo-production is used to show that a sign change of the integral corresponding to the appropriately defined moment at $n=0$ where the integral is cut at the point $x=x_c(Q^2)$ occurs at very small $Q^2$ near $Q^2\sim 0.09$(GeV/c)$^2$ and $x_c\sim 0.024$. This fact shows that the origin of the sign difference between the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule and the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule lies in the rapid change of the elastic contribution at low $Q^2$ which is compensated by the inelastic contribution to satisfy the sum rule at $n=0$. Hence it occurs at very small $Q^2$.

# A sign change of the moment at n=0 of the polarized structure function g1^p

## Key claim

The appropriately defined moment at n=0 of the polarized structure function g1^p, defined as the integral from x_c to 1 of (dx/x) g1^p(x,Q^2), becomes zero near Q^2 ~ 0.09 (GeV/c)^2 with x_c ~ 0.024, but this zero point shifts to Q^2 ~ 0.16 (GeV/c)^2 with x_c = 0.042 when using an alternative parameterization from Simula et al. (2002). The sign change occurs because the rapid variation of the elastic (Born) contribution at low Q^2 is compensated by the inelastic contribution to satisfy the Q^2-independent sum rule at n=0.

## Method

The author derives sum rules for the polarized structure functions g1 and g2 from the Deser-Gilbert-Sudarshan representation of current anticommutators on the null-plane, obtaining a Q^2-independent sum rule for the integral of g1/x. To handle possible divergences from small-x behavior, the sum rule is regularized by analytical continuation from non-forward kinematics, introducing a cutoff parameter x_c(Q^2) = Q^2/(2 nu_c^Q) with nu_c corresponding to E_c = 2 GeV. The regularized sum rule relates the integral of g1^p from x_c to 1 to the Born term difference B(Q^2) and the low-energy photo-production cross section difference (sigma_{3/2} - sigma_{1/2}) integrated up to E_c, plus a correction term K(E_c,Q^2) that accounts for high-energy cancellations.

## Key numbers

- The sign change of the moment occurs near Q^2 ~ 0.09 (GeV/c)^2 with x_c ~ 0.024.
- Using the parameterization of Simula et al. (2002), the zero point shifts to Q^2 ~ 0.16 (GeV/c)^2 with x_c = 0.042.
- The integral of the photo-production cross section difference from threshold to 2 GeV gives (m_N/(8 pi^2 alpha_em)) integral_{E_0}^{2} dE (sigma_{3/2} - sigma_{1/2}) ~ 0.45.
- The correction term K(2,0.05) ~ -0.014 and K(2,0.1) ~ -0.027 for Q^2 = 0.05 and 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 respectively.
- The Born term B(Q^2) is computed using the standard dipole fit with G_E^p = 1/(1 + Q^2/0.71)^2, G_M^p = mu_p G_E^p, where mu_p = 2.793, and the Dirac form factors satisfy F_1^p(0) = 1, F_2^p(0) = 1.79.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Koretune (1993) which derived sum rules for g1 and g2 from null-plane current anticommutation relations, and applies it to understand the sign difference between the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974) and the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule (Drell and Hearn, 1966; Gerasimov, 1965). The result shows that the sign change occurs at very low Q^2 because the Born term varies rapidly below Q^2 < 0.5 (GeV/c)^2, with the inelastic contribution compensating to maintain the Q^2 independence of the n=0 moment. The regularized sum rule enables future investigation of the Q^2 evolution of resonance structure in the low and intermediate energy region without contamination from high-energy behavior, and can be extended to larger Q^2 where resonance contributions transition to continuum contributions.
