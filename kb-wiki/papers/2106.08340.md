# Exact equilibrium distributions in statistical quantum field theory with rotation and acceleration: Dirac field

> We derive the general exact forms of the Wigner function, of mean values of conserved currents, of the spin density matrix, of the spin polarization vector and of the distribution function of massless particles for the free Dirac field at global thermodynamic equilibrium with rotation and acceleration, extending our previous results obtained for the scalar field. The solutions are obtained by means of an iterative method and analytic continuation, which leads to formal series in thermal vorticity. In order to obtain finite values, we extend to the fermionic case the method of analytic distillation introduced for bosonic series. The obtained mean values of the stress-energy tensor, vector and axial currents for the massless Dirac field are in agreement with known analytic results in the special cases of pure acceleration and pure rotation. By using this approach, we obtain new expressions of the currents for the more general case of combined rotation and acceleration and, in the pure acceleration case, we demonstrate that they must vanish at the Unruh temperature.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact closed-form expressions for the Wigner function, conserved currents, spin density matrix, and distribution function of the free Dirac field at global thermodynamic equilibrium with rotation and acceleration, but these results are obtained through an iterative method and analytic continuation that yields formal series in thermal vorticity, requiring a mathematical prescription ("analytic distillation") to remove unphysical non-analytic terms and obtain finite physical values.

## Method

The authors factorize the density operator into a product of an exponential of the four-momentum and a unitary Lorentz transformation by setting the thermal vorticity to imaginary values, then use Poincaré group representation theory to compute expectation values of creation and annihilation operators iteratively, obtaining a series of delta functions. They then extend the analytic distillation method (previously introduced for bosonic series) to the fermionic case to remove non-analytic terms and analytically continue back to real thermal vorticity, obtaining finite physical expectation values for the Wigner function and derived quantities.

## Key numbers

- The density operator is parameterized by a constant four-vector \(b_\mu\) and an anti-symmetric constant tensor \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\) (thermal vorticity), with local temperature \(T = 1/\sqrt{\beta^2}\) where \(\beta_\mu = b_\mu + \varpi_{\mu\nu} x^\nu\).
- For pure acceleration, the mean values of vector and axial currents for the massless Dirac field are shown to vanish at the Unruh temperature.
- The series expansion for the number operator expectation value reproduces the Fermi-Dirac distribution \(\langle \wad{p}{s} \wa{p'}{t}\rangle = 2\varepsilon \, \delta^3(\bm{p}-\bm{p}')\delta_{ts} \, 1/(e^{b\cdot p}+1)\) in the limit of vanishing thermal vorticity.
- The spinors \(U(p)\) and \(V(p)\) satisfy normalization relations \(\bar U(p) U(p) = 2m I\) and \(\bar V(p) V(p) = -2m I\) for both massive and massless cases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for the scalar field (Palermo, Buzzegoli, Becattini, 2021) to the Dirac field of spin 1/2 particles, providing exact equilibrium distributions that go beyond the leading perturbative order in thermal vorticity. The results are relevant for relativistic heavy ion collision phenomenology (Becattini, 2020) and chiral kinetic theory (Son and Stephanov, 2012; Hidaka, 2016; Gao, 2017), and the derived exact Wigner function represents a new non-trivial solution of the Wigner equation beyond the homogeneous equilibrium case.
