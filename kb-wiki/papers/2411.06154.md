# Boost-invariant spin hydrodynamics with spin feedback effects

> A recently formulated extension of perfect spin hydrodynamics, which includes second-order corrections in the spin polarization tensor to the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current, is studied in the case of a one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion. The presence of second-order corrections introduces feedback from spin dynamics on the hydrodynamic background, constraining possible spin polarization configurations. However, as long as the magnitude of the spin polarization tensor remains small (below unity in natural units), the permitted spin dynamics differs very little from that found in the case without the second-order corrections.

## Key claim

The inclusion of second-order corrections in the spin polarization tensor to the energy-momentum tensor and baryon current introduces feedback from spin dynamics on the hydrodynamic background, which constrains possible spin polarization configurations; however, as long as the magnitude of the spin polarization tensor remains below unity in natural units, the permitted spin dynamics differs very little from the case without second-order corrections.

## Method

The authors studied a recently formulated extension of perfect spin hydrodynamics, which includes second-order corrections in the spin polarization tensor, in the case of a one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion. They derived the conservation laws for baryon number, energy-momentum, and the spin part of angular momentum, obtaining an overdetermined system of eleven equations for eight unknown functions of proper time. To obtain a well-defined system, they imposed additional symmetry constraints leading to two special cases: a longitudinal configuration and a transverse configuration, and solved the resulting ordinary differential equations numerically.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time was set to tau_0 = 1 fm and final time to tau_f = 10 fm.
- Particle mass was taken as that of the Lambda hyperon, m = 1.116 GeV.
- Initial temperature was T_0 = 155 MeV and initial baryon chemical potential was mu_0 = 800 MeV.
- Initial values for the coefficients C were chosen as 0.25, 0.50, and 0.75 (dimensionless in natural units).
- For the longitudinal configuration with initial values C^0_{kz} = C^0_{omega z} = 0.75, differences from the no-feedback case were about 15%.
- For initial values exceeding unity, the obtained results started to diverge with time, indicating breakdown of the expansion.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of perfect spin hydrodynamics presented in Florkowski (2019), which ignored second-order corrections to the baryon current and energy-momentum tensor. The formalism builds on the framework of perfect spin hydrodynamics reviewed in Florkowski (2018) and its extension to incorporate dissipative phenomena developed in Florkowski (2024) and Drogosz (2024). The results enable future studies of dissipative effects in boost-invariant geometry, particularly the role of asymmetric components of the energy-momentum tensor responsible for spin-orbit interactions.
