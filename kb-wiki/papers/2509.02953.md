# Observation of renormalization group invariance in symmetry-restored nuclear lattice effective field theory

> Renormalization group (RG) invariance implies that the predictions of effective field theory are independent of the momentum cutoffs introduced during regularization. Here we report the first systematic verification of RG invariance for realistic nuclear few-body systems within nuclear lattice effective field theory. To restore broken continuum rotational and Galilean symmetries, we employ Galilean-invariance-restoration counterterms and use a soft momentum regulator. We calibrate the two- and three-body next-to-next-to leading order (N$^2$LO) chiral forces using $A\leq 3$ observables and perform precision quantum Monte Carlo calculations to compute the $^4$He binding energy. The predicted energy remains constant across cutoffs from $250$~MeV to $400$~MeV and agrees well with the experimental value, with discrepancies of order 100 keV. Our results demonstrate the capability of extracting accurate, cutoff-independent predictions within lattice-regulated \textit{ab initio} nuclear theory.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first systematic verification of renormalization group (RG) invariance for realistic nuclear few-body systems within nuclear lattice effective field theory, but only after introducing Galilean-invariance-restoration counterterms and using a soft momentum regulator; without these symmetry-restoration steps, the predicted binding energies would show cutoff dependence.

## Method

The authors constructed a next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) chiral interaction on a cubic lattice with a soft single-particle momentum regulator, and added Galilean-invariance-restoration (GIR) counterterms proportional to the pair total momentum squared. They calibrated the two-body low-energy constants to reproduce empirical neutron-proton phase shifts in the center-of-mass frame, and the three-body contact term to the triton binding energy, then performed Lanczos diagonalization for A=3 systems and perturbative quantum Monte Carlo (ptQMC) calculations for the ^4He binding energy across cutoff values from 250 MeV to 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ^4He binding energy remains constant across contact-term cutoffs from 250 MeV to 400 MeV, with discrepancies of order 100 keV relative to the experimental value.
- The lattice spacing was fixed at a = 0.987 fm, corresponding to an anisotropic momentum cutoff of approximately 628 MeV.
- The one-pion-exchange and Coulomb cutoffs were fixed at Lambda_pi = Lambda_cou = 300 MeV throughout.
- Neutron-proton phase shifts were fitted up to relative momenta P_rel <= 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NLEFT studies of the Tjon line by Klein (2018), which used first-order perturbation theory and did not fully account for symmetry-breaking effects, preventing definitive establishment of RG invariance. The present study uses the ptQMC method of Lu (2022) to achieve higher precision and explicitly restores Galilean invariance via counterterms following the approach of Li (2019) and Elhatisari (2024). The results enable future systematic investigations of cutoff dependence in heavier nuclei and provide a pathway to connect lattice formulations with continuum calculations, potentially resolving discrepancies such as the factor-of-two difference in the ^4He monopole transition form factor between traditional ab initio methods and experiment.
