# Unphysical Discontinuities, Intruder States and Regularization in $GW$ Methods

> By recasting the non-linear frequency-dependent $GW$ quasiparticle equation into a linear eigenvalue problem, we explain the appearance of multiple solutions and unphysical discontinuities in various physical quantities computed within the $GW$ approximation. Considering the $GW$ self-energy as an effective Hamiltonian, it is shown that these issues are key signatures of strong correlation in the $(N\pm1)$-electron states and can be directly related to the intruder state problem. A simple and efficient regularization procedure inspired by the similarity renormalization group is proposed to avoid such issues and speed up convergence of partially self-consistent $GW$ calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unphysical discontinuities in $GW$ quasiparticle energies arise from intruder states, external (N±1)-electron determinants that become nearly degenerate with the reference determinant, and that these discontinuities are a signature of strong correlation in the (N±1)-electron states, not merely a numerical artifact. The authors propose a regularization procedure based on the similarity renormalization group (SRG) that removes these discontinuities while preserving accuracy, with the caveat that the optimal regularization parameter κ is system-dependent.

## Method

The authors recast the nonlinear frequency-dependent $GW$ quasiparticle equation into an equivalent linear eigenvalue problem via an upfolding process, where the 2h1p and 2p1h sectors are upfolded from the 1h and 1p sectors. They then introduce a regularized $GW$ self-energy using an energy-dependent regularizer derived from second-order perturbative analysis of the SRG equations, $f_κ(Δ) = (1 - e^{-2Δ^2/κ^2})/Δ$, and solve the regularized quasiparticle equation for each orbital. The method is tested on the H₂ molecule in the 6-31G basis set and the F₂ molecule in the cc-pVDZ basis set at the G₀W₀@HF and evGW@HF levels, with comparison to CCSD and CC3 reference data.

## Key numbers

- For H₂ at the G₀W₀@HF/6-31G level, discontinuities appear around R_HH = 1.2 Å for the LUMO+1 orbital and around R_HH = 0.5 Å for the LUMO+2 orbital.
- With the SRG-based regularizer at κ = 1.0 hartree, the regularization induces an error below 10 meV for the HOMO and LUMO orbitals of H₂ in regions without intruder states.
- For the simple energy-independent regularizer, a value of η = 0.1 hartree is appropriate around R_HH = 1.1 Å but insufficient around R_HH = 0.5 Å for H₂.
- For the SRG regularizer on H₂, κ = 0.1 hartree is too small (irregularities remain), κ = 10 hartree is too large (large difference in quasiparticle energies), and κ = 1.0 hartree provides a good compromise.
- For F₂ with the cc-pVDZ basis set, the BSE@G₀W₀@HF potential energy surface is "bumpy" around equilibrium without regularization; with κ = 1 hartree the curve is smoothed, and with κ = 10 hartree the BSE@G₀W₀@HF and BSE@evGW@HF curves are superposed and of similar quality to CCSD.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies (Loos 2018, Véril 2018) that first identified unphysical discontinuities in $GW$ energy surfaces for model and real molecular systems. It provides a physical explanation linking these discontinuities to the intruder state problem, which is well-known in multiconfigurational perturbation theory. The proposed SRG-based regularization is inspired by the work of Evangelista (2014) on regularized electronic structure theories and is related to regularizers used by Head-Gordon and coworkers in orbital-optimized MP2 theory (Lee 2018, Shee 2021). The method enables smooth potential energy surfaces for self-consistent $GW$ calculations, which were previously difficult to converge, and broadens the applicability of Green's function methods to molecular systems where strong correlation in (N±1)-electron states occurs.
