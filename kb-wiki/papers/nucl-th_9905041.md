# On the q-deformation of the NJL model

> Using a q-deformed fermionic algebra we perform explicitly a deformation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Hamiltonian. In the Bogoliubov-Valatin approach we obtain the deformed version of the functional for the total energy, which is minimized to obtain the corresponding gap equation. The breaking of chiral symmetry and its restoration in the limit $q \to 0$ are then discussed.

## Key claim

The authors perform a q-deformation of the NJL Hamiltonian itself (rather than simply deforming the quark condensates in the gap equation, as in their prior work Timoteo and Lima (1999)), and find that the resulting gap equation has the same functional form as the non-deformed case when expressed in q-deformed variables, but that the deformation enhances the dynamical mass and condensate for q > 1 and restores chiral symmetry in the limit q → 0.

## Method

The authors apply a q-deformed fermionic algebra (based on Ubriaco (1996)) that modifies only the negative-helicity quark and anti-quark creation and annihilation operators, writing the deformed quark fields in terms of these operators. They then compute the vacuum expectation value of the q-deformed NJL Hamiltonian in a Bogoliubov-Valatin variational BCS-like vacuum, obtaining a deformed functional for the total energy which is minimized to yield the deformed gap equation. The resulting equations are expressed in terms of new q-dependent variables (P_q, G') that reduce to the standard variables when q → 1.

## Key numbers

- For a coupling constant G/G_c = 0.75 in the non-deformed case, the pion decay constant is F_π = 88 MeV; setting the deformation parameter to q = 1.2 shifts this to F_π = 92 MeV, close to the experimental value f_π = 93 MeV.
- The dynamical mass is fixed at 300 MeV for the study of the q-dependence of the variational angle and generated mass.
- The number of terms in the Hamiltonian matrix elements increases from 4096 (without deformation) to 20736 (with deformation), with 16640 new terms and 8320 terms of first order in Q.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Timoteo and Lima, 1999) where the q-deformation was applied only to the quark condensates in the gap equation, by instead deforming the NJL Hamiltonian at a more fundamental level. The results show a stronger enhancement of the condensate and dynamical mass for q > 1 compared to that earlier approach, and the chiral symmetry restoration as q → 0 is noted as being different from the finite-temperature restoration studied by Asakawa and Yazaki (1989) and Vogl and Weise (1991). The authors indicate that a combined study of temperature and q-deformation effects on chiral symmetry restoration is in progress.
