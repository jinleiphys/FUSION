# Dispersion relations of mesons in symmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate dispersion relations and propagators for the $σ$, $ω$, $π$, $ρ$, $δ$, $η$ and $a_1$ mesons in relativistic, dense, hot, symmetric nuclear matter. In addition to the usual mixing of the $σ-ω$ system, we obtain mixing of the $δ$ with the longitudinal $ρ$ mode and of the tranverse $ρ$ with the transverse $a_1$ mode. Finally, the component of the $a_1$ polarization along the transferred momentum modifies the in-medium pion propagator in a way similar to the Migdal contact interaction, but with the opposite sign. The spurious pion condensate as well as the additional contribution from the $a_1$ meson are removed by a contact term. We compare two ways of implementing contact term subtraction.

## Key claim

The paper calculates dispersion relations and propagators for seven mesons (σ, ω, π, ρ, δ, η, a₁) in relativistic symmetric nuclear matter, finding that the a₁ meson modifies the in-medium pion propagator in a way formally similar to the Migdal contact interaction but with the opposite sign, so that the a₁ would enhance the unobserved pion zero sound mode rather than suppress it.

## Method

The dispersion relations were derived using a linear response analysis in the Wigner operator formalism around the homogeneous Hartree ground state, which corresponds to the random phase approximation (RPA). The calculation included nucleon-nucleon loops for all seven mesons of the Bonn model, with the meson fields treated as perturbations around the relativistic Hartree equilibrium. The resulting dispersion matrix was factorized into three decoupled blocks: σ-ω, η, and ρ-δ-a₁-π, and the propagators were obtained by inverting this matrix.

## Key numbers

- The a₁ meson mass is taken as m_a = 1260 MeV
- The δ meson coupling strength at saturation density from de Jong and Lenske is g_δ²(n_sat)/(4π) = 4.61
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' extracted from Gamow-Teller resonance data is g' = 0.6
- The delta subtraction procedure for the pion alone amounts to taking g' = 0.333
- Phenomenology favors g' values in the range 0.5 - 0.9

## Context

This work extends previous RPA calculations of meson dispersion relations in nuclear matter (DA85, MGP01) by including the a₁ meson and its mixing with ρ and π mesons, as well as the δ meson mixing with the longitudinal ρ mode. The paper compares two approaches for implementing short-range corrections: the propagator replacement Ansatz of Horowitz et al. (HP94, KPH95) versus adding contact terms to the Lagrangian as done by Schäfer et al. (SDEM94). The results can be applied to calculations of screened nucleon-nucleon potentials, dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, and neutrino-nucleon scattering in proto-neutron star matter.
