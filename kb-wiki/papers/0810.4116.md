# Angular Dependence of Jet Quenching Indicates Its Strong Enhancement Near the QCD Phase Transition

> We study dependence of jet quenching on matter density, using "tomography" of the fireball provided by RHIC data on azimuthal anisotropy $v_2$ of high $p_t$ hadron yield at different centralities. Slicing the fireball into shells with constant (entropy) density, we derive a "layer-wise geometrical limit" $v_2^{max}$ which is indeed above the data $v_2<v_2^{max}$. Interestingly, the limit is reached only if quenching is dominated by shells with the entropy density exactly in the near-$T_c$ region. We show two models that simultaneously describe the high $p_t$ $v_2$ and $R_{AA}$ data and conclude that such a description can be achieved only if the jet quenching is few times stronger in the near-$T_c$ region relative to QGP at $T>T_c$. One possible reason for that may be recent indications that the near-$T_c$ region is a magnetic plasma of relatively light color-magnetic monopoles.

# Angular Dependence of Jet Quenching Indicates Its Strong Enhancement Near the QCD Phase Transition

## Key claim

The observed azimuthal anisotropy v2 of high-pT hadrons at RHIC can only be simultaneously described with the nuclear modification factor RAA if jet quenching is about 2-5 times stronger in the near-Tc region (entropy density s = 4-8 fm^-3) than in the higher-temperature QGP phase, contrary to earlier assumptions that quenching is proportional to matter density.

## Method

The authors slice the expanding fireball from AuAu collisions into 24 entropy-density shells and calculate, via a Glauber simulation with 1-D Bjorken dilution, the v2 and RAA that would result if each single shell were the sole source of quenching. They then construct two phenomenological models (Model A with a step function and Model B with a Gaussian peak) for the quenching strength kappa(s) as a function of entropy density, fit kappa_R globally from RAA(Npart) data, and determine the relative quenching strength between the near-Tc region and the QGP by fitting v2(Npart) data from PHENIX and STAR for pT > 6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- RAA(pT > 5 GeV) parameterized as [1 - 8.3e-3 * Npart^0.58]^(n-2) with n ≈ 8.10
- The entropy shells producing maximal v2 correspond to the interval s = 4-8 fm^-3 for all centralities
- Model A best fit: kappa_R = 0.00435 fm, relative quenching strength lambda = 0.4 (near-Tc region 2.5 times stronger than QGP)
- Model B best fit: kappa_R = 0.00745 fm, relative quenching strength xi = 0.2 (near-Tc region 5 times stronger than QGP)
- For most central AuAu collisions, RAA at large pT > 6 GeV is about 0.2

## Context

This work extends earlier geometric limit approaches by Shuryak (2001) and Drees et al. (2003), which assumed quenching proportional to matter density and failed to reproduce the measured high-pT v2. The authors show that the data satisfy a new "layer-wise geometric limit" and that successful simultaneous description of v2 and RAA requires quenching to peak near Tc, consistent with other known peaks in trace anomaly, specific heat, and shear viscosity near Tc from lattice results (Cheng et al., 2007). The proposed microscopic explanation invokes the magnetic plasma of light color-magnetic monopoles near Tc (Liao and Shuryak, 2006). The models predict a rapid drop of v2 for very peripheral collisions (Npart << 100) and provide a testable prediction for CuCu collisions at 200 GeV.
