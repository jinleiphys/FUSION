# Jet quenching in the strongly-interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We propose a hybrid model for medium-induced parton energy loss, in which the hard scales in the process are treated perturbatively, while the soft scales which involve strong coupling dynamics are modeled by AdS/CFT calculations. After fitting a single parameter on R_AA for central Au+Au collisions, we are able to predict different observables like R_AA and I_AA as a function of centrality and reaction plane. We obtain a consistent picture of how jet quenching is modified if the quark-gluon plasma is strongly interacting, and we provide quantitative predictions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid model for medium-induced parton energy loss that treats hard scales perturbatively while modeling soft-scale strong-coupling dynamics via AdS/CFT calculations; after fitting a single parameter on central Au+Au collision data, the model predicts that the stronger path-length dependence of energy loss in a strongly-coupled plasma (L^3 versus L^2) produces a larger spread between in-plane and out-of-plane suppression than weak-coupling calculations, in agreement with preliminary PHENIX data.

## Method

The authors replace the weak-coupling expressions for the saturation momentum Q_s^2 and the maximum radiated gluon energy ω_m in the BDMPS-Z radiative energy loss formalism with strong-coupling expressions derived from AdS/CFT: Q_s^2 = T^4 L^2 and ω_m = T^4 L^3 (instead of Q_s^2 = q̂ L and ω_m = q̂ L^2 at weak coupling). They then integrate these quantities along parton trajectories through a 3-d hydrodynamical medium model, using line integrals Q_s^2(r₀,φ) = K ∫ dξ ξ T^4(ξ) and ω_m(r₀,φ) = K ∫ dξ ξ^2 T^4(ξ), and compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA and dihadron suppression factor I_AA via the quenching weight formalism and a Monte Carlo simulation for back-to-back correlations.

## Key numbers

- The fitted normalization parameter K is approximately 6 in the strong-coupling case (Eqs. 13) and approximately 27 in the weak-coupling case (Eqs. 11) for 200 AGeV Au+Au collisions.
- For central collisions (b = 2.4 fm), both the weak-coupling and strong-coupling calculations give a good and virtually identical description of the PHENIX R_AA data as a function of P_T.
- For peripheral collisions (b = 7.5 fm), the strong-coupling scenario predicts less suppression for in-plane emissions and a larger spread between in-plane and out-of-plane R_AA values compared to the weak-coupling scenario.
- The strong-coupling approach predicts more suppression of I_AA for central collisions but less suppression for peripheral collisions than the weak-coupling approach, with a larger spread between in-plane and out-of-plane values.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z radiative energy loss formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, Zakharov, 1990s) by incorporating strong-coupling dynamics from AdS/CFT, following the idea of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) to use a strong-coupling transport coefficient but correcting their assumption of a constant q̂. The hybrid model enables quantitative predictions for R_AA and I_AA as functions of centrality and reaction plane that can be tested against PHENIX and STAR data, and it suggests that the stronger L^3 path-length dependence at strong coupling may help resolve the discrepancy between pQCD calculations and heavy-flavor R_AA measurements.
