# Strangeness production and hypernuclear formation in proton and antiproton induced reactions

> Strange particles and hyperfragments in collisions of antiprotons and protons on nuclei have been investigated systematically within a microscopic transport model. The hyperons are produced from the annihilation in antibaryon-baryon collisions and strangeness exchange process in antiproton induced reactions. A coalescence approach is used for constructing the primary hyperfragments in phase space and the statistical model is modified for describing the decay of hyperfragments via evaporating hyperon, neutron, charged particles etc, in which the shell effect, binding energy and root-mean-square radii are taken into account. It is found that the influence of the hyperon-nucleon interaction on the free $Λ$ and $Ξ^{-}$ production is negligible. However, the large hyperfragment yields are obvious with the attractive potential. The production of double strangeness hyperfragments are reduced below 1$μb$ in comparison to the yields of $Λ$-hyperfragments with the cross sections of 0.05-0.1 mb in the antiproton induced reactions on $^{63}$Cu at the incident momenta of 1-5 GeV/c. The light hyperfragments are formed in the dynamical fragmentation process. The energy dependence of hyperfragment formation is weak once the incident energy above the threshold energy for the hyperon production.

## Key claim

The influence of the hyperon-nucleon optical potential on the production of free Λ and Ξ⁻ hyperons is negligible, but the attractive potential significantly enhances the yields of hyperfragments (bound hypernuclei) by reducing the relative momentum between the hyperon and the nuclear cluster, enabling capture.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate proton- and antiproton-induced reactions on various nuclear targets. Hyperons were produced via antibaryon-baryon annihilation, strangeness exchange reactions, and meson-baryon collisions. Primary hyperfragments were constructed using a coalescence approach in phase space (with coalescence parameters P₀ = 200 MeV/c and R₀ = 3 fm), and the de-excitation of excited fragments was described by a modified statistical model (GEMINI code) incorporating shell effects, binding energy, and root-mean-square radii.

## Key numbers

- Λ-hyperfragment production cross sections in antiproton-induced reactions on ⁶³Cu at incident momenta of 1–5 GeV/c: 0.05–0.1 mb.
- Double-strangeness hyperfragment production cross sections in the same system: below 1 μb.
- Maximal yields of double-strangeness hypernuclei in antiproton-induced reactions on ⁶³Cu at momenta of 2–5 GeV/c: 0.5–1 μb.
- Λ-hyperfragment yields in antiproton-induced reactions exceed those in proton-induced reactions by over two orders of magnitude (proton-induced yields above 1 μb).
- Threshold momentum for direct Λ production via annihilation (p̄N → Λ̄Λ): 1.44 GeV/c.
- Threshold momentum for direct Ξ production via annihilation (p̄N → Ξ̄Ξ): 2.62 GeV/c.
- Optical potential depth for Λ at saturation density: −32 MeV.
- Optical potential depth for Ξ at saturation density: −16 MeV.
- Optical potential depth for Σ at saturation density: +12 MeV (weakly repulsive).
- Antiproton optical potential depth at normal nuclear density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³): −164 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior transport-model studies of hypernucleus formation in heavy-ion collisions and hadron-induced reactions, specifically the GiBUU model (Gaitanos et al., 2009, 2012) and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) (Botvina et al., 2007, 2019). The LQMD results are compared with GiBUU+SMM calculations for p̄ + ⁶⁴Cu at 5 GeV/c and with experimental data from the LEAR facility at CERN (Jahn et al., 1993). The calculations enable predictions for hypernuclear production at upcoming facilities: PANDA at FAIR (antiproton beams) and HIAF in China (high-energy proton beams).
