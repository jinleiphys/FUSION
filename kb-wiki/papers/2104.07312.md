# Jülich-Bonn-Washington Model for Pion Electroproduction Multipoles

> Pion electroproduction off the proton is analyzed in a new framework based on a general parametrization of transition amplitudes, including constraints from gauge invariance and threshold behavior. Data with energies $1.13~{\rm GeV}<W<1.6~{\rm GeV}$ and $Q^2$ below $6~{\rm GeV}^2$ are included. The model is an extension of the latest Jülich-Bonn solution incorporating constraints from pion-induced and photoproduction data. Performing large scale fits ($\sim10^5$ data) we find a set of solutions with $χ^2_{\rm dof}=1.69-1.81$ which allows us to assess the systematic uncertainty of the approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new framework for analyzing pion electroproduction multipoles that extends the Jülich-Bonn (JüBo) photoproduction solution to finite photon virtuality \(Q^2\), but the analysis is limited to single-pion final states and does not yet include a fully coupled-channel treatment of \(\eta\) and kaon electroproduction data; the quoted \(\chi^2_{\rm dof}\) range of 1.69–1.81 reflects systematic uncertainty across a set of solutions rather than a single best fit.

## Method

The authors construct a general parametrization of electroproduction transition amplitudes in the CGLN and helicity-amplitude formalisms, enforcing gauge invariance via the continuity equation and Siegert’s condition at the pseudo-threshold \(q=0\). They extend the JüBo2017 hadronic and photoproduction solution to \(Q^2>0\) by fitting longitudinal and transverse multipoles to world data on pion electroproduction off the proton, covering invariant masses \(1.13\ \text{GeV} < W < 1.6\ \text{GeV}\) and photon virtualities up to \(Q^2=6\ \text{GeV}^2\). Large-scale fits are performed on approximately \(10^5\) data points, producing a family of solutions whose spread quantifies systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* The fit quality across the set of solutions is \(\chi^2_{\rm dof} = 1.69\)–\(1.81\) for approximately \(10^5\) data points.
* The analysis covers the energy range \(1.13\ \text{GeV} < W < 1.6\ \text{GeV}\).
* Data with photon virtuality \(Q^2\) below \(6\ \text{GeV}^2\) are included; the upper limit is varied between \(4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(6\ \text{GeV}^2\) to assess stability.
* The pseudo-threshold condition is applied at \(Q^2_{\rm PT-} = -(W-m)^2\), where \(m\) is the nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn coupled-channel approach (JüBo2017, Rönchen et al., 2018) from photoproduction to electroproduction, providing the first unified analysis of pion electroproduction multipoles that respects gauge invariance and Siegert’s condition. It contrasts with single-channel analyses such as MAID (Drechsel et al., 2007) and the Jefferson Lab group (Aznauryan et al., 2009), and with the dynamical coupled-channel electroproduction work of the ANL-Osaka group (Kamano et al., 2018). The framework enables future inclusion of \(\eta\) and kaon electroproduction data and a fully coupled-channel fit of photo- and electroproduction, which will be necessary to extract resonance couplings as a function of \(Q^2\) and to connect with lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory.
