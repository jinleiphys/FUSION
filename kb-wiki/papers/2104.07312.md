# JĂĽlich-Bonn-Washington Model for Pion Electroproduction Multipoles

> Pion electroproduction off the proton is analyzed in a new framework based on a general parametrization of transition amplitudes, including constraints from gauge invariance and threshold behavior. Data with energies $1.13~{\rm GeV}<W<1.6~{\rm GeV}$ and $Q^2$ below $6~{\rm GeV}^2$ are included. The model is an extension of the latest JĂĽlich-Bonn solution incorporating constraints from pion-induced and photoproduction data. Performing large scale fits ($\sim10^5$ data) we find a set of solutions with $Ď‡^2_{\rm dof}=1.69-1.81$ which allows us to assess the systematic uncertainty of the approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new framework for analyzing pion electroproduction multipoles that extends the JĂĽlich-Bonn (JĂĽBo) photoproduction solution to finite photon virtuality \(Q^2\), but the analysis is limited to single-pion final states and does not yet include a fully coupled-channel treatment of \(\eta\) and kaon electroproduction data; the quoted \(\chi^2_{\rm dof}\) range of 1.69â€“1.81 reflects systematic uncertainty across a set of solutions rather than a single best fit.

## Method

The authors construct a general parametrization of electroproduction transition amplitudes in the CGLN and helicity-amplitude formalisms, enforcing gauge invariance via the continuity equation and Siegertâ€™s condition at the pseudo-threshold \(q=0\). They extend the JĂĽBo2017 hadronic and photoproduction solution to \(Q^2>0\) by fitting longitudinal and transverse multipoles to world data on pion electroproduction off the proton, covering invariant masses \(1.13\ \text{GeV} < W < 1.6\ \text{GeV}\) and photon virtualities up to \(Q^2=6\ \text{GeV}^2\). Large-scale fits are performed on approximately \(10^5\) data points, producing a family of solutions whose spread quantifies systematic uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the JĂĽlich-Bonn coupled-channel approach (JĂĽBo2017, RĂ¶nchen et al., 2018) from photoproduction to electroproduction, providing the first unified analysis of pion electroproduction multipoles that respects gauge invariance and Siegertâ€™s condition. It contrasts with single-channel analyses such as MAID (Drechsel et al., 2007) and the Jefferson Lab group (Aznauryan et al., 2009), and with the dynamical coupled-channel electroproduction work of the ANL-Osaka group (Kamano et al., 2018). The framework enables future inclusion of \(\eta\) and kaon electroproduction data and a fully coupled-channel fit of photo- and electroproduction, which will be necessary to extract resonance couplings as a function of \(Q^2\) and to connect with lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory.
