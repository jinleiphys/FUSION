# Phenomenology of double deeply virtual Compton scattering in the era of new experiments

> We revisit the phenomenology of the deep exclusive electroproduction of a lepton pair, i.e. double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS), in view of new experiments planned in the near future. The importance of DDVCS in the reconstruction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in their full kinematic domain is emphasized. Using Kleiss-Stirling spinor techniques, we provide the leading order complex amplitudes for both DDVCS and Bethe-Heithler sub-processes. Such a formulation turns out to be convenient for practical implementation in the PARTONS framework and EpIC Monte Carlo generator that we use in simulation studies.

# Phenomenology of double deeply virtual Compton scattering in the era of new experiments

## Key claim

Double deeply virtual Compton scattering (DDVCS) can become a main source of information on generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the unexplored kinematic domain where the skewness parameter xi is not equal to the absolute value of the generalized Bjorken variable rho, provided that future experiments achieve sufficient luminosity and effective muon reconstruction; the study does not include any detector effects and should be considered a rough estimate.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-order complex amplitudes for DDVCS and Bethe-Heitler sub-processes using Kleiss-Stirling spinor techniques, which allow expression of the amplitudes as easy-to-implement complex quantities. The amplitudes were implemented in the PARTONS framework and the EpIC Monte Carlo generator for simulation studies. The authors validated their results against the DVCS limit (Q'^2 approaching 0) and the TCS limit (Q^2 approaching 0) using independent codes available in PARTONS.

## Key numbers

- Integrated DDVCS cross-section for JLab12 (E_e = 10.6 GeV, fixed target): 0.14 pb, requiring 70 fb^-1 integrated luminosity for 10000 events
- Integrated DDVCS cross-section for JLab20+ (E_e = 22 GeV, fixed target): 0.46 pb, requiring 22 fb^-1 integrated luminosity for 10000 events
- Integrated DDVCS cross-section for EIC (5 GeV x 41 GeV beam energies): 3.9 pb, requiring 2.6 fb^-1 integrated luminosity for 10000 events
- Integrated DDVCS cross-section for EIC (10 GeV x 100 GeV beam energies): 4.7 pb, requiring 2.1 fb^-1 integrated luminosity for 10000 events
- Kinematic ranges used in Monte Carlo generation: Q^2 in (0.15, 5) GeV^2, Q'^2 in (2.25, 9) GeV^2, |t| in (0.1, 0.8) GeV^2 for JLab experiments and (0.05, 1) GeV^2 for EIC experiments
- Fraction of pure DDVCS sub-process events after y-cuts: 1 for JLab12 and JLab20+ (y_min = 0.1), 0.73 for EIC 5x41 GeV (y_min = 0.05), 0.32 for EIC 10x100 GeV (y_min = 0.05)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenology of DDVCS, which was previously described in terms of seven kinematic variables by Belitsky (2002) and Guidal (2003), and provides the first Monte Carlo feasibility studies for upcoming experiments at JLab12, JLab20+, and the Electron-Ion Collider. The authors note that their analysis should be complemented with NLO expressions of DDVCS Compton form factors from Pire (2011), which include gluon GPD contributions that have proven phenomenologically important in DVCS and TCS studies by Moutarde (2013). The work enables future experimental programs to extract GPDs in the full kinematic domain where xi differs from |rho|, which is inaccessible through DVCS or TCS alone.
