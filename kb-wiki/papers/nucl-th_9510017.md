# Searching for three-nucleon resonances

> We search for three-neutron resonances which were predicted from pion double charge exchange experiments on He-3. All partial waves up to J=5/2 are nonresonant except the J=3/2^+ one, where we find a state at E=14 MeV energy with 13 MeV width. The parameters of the mirror state in the three-proton system are E=15 MeV and Gamma=14 MeV. The possible existence of an excited state in the triton, which was predicted from a H(He-6,alpha) experiment, is also discussed.

# Searching for three-nucleon resonances

## Key claim

The authors find a single three-neutron resonance in the J=3/2+ partial wave at E=14 MeV with 13 MeV width, but note that using realistic interactions with a full model space (including l≥3) may change these parameters, so the aim is to show existence rather than precise values. The results regarding the narrow excited state of the triton are inconclusive, but the authors question whether it originates from the (nn)p channel as previously claimed.

## Method

The authors use the complex scaling method (CSM) to search for three-neutron resonances, solving the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation by expanding the wave function in products of Gaussian functions with different widths. They employ the Minnesota effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with a tensor force, including all n+n partial waves up to l=2, and check that l=3 contributions are insignificant. For the triton excited state, they use analytic continuation of the S-matrix in a two-body d+n model and approximately include the n+n+p channel via discretized continuum states.

## Key numbers

- Three-neutron J=3/2+ resonance: E=14 MeV energy, Γ=13 MeV width
- Mirror three-proton J=3/2+ resonance: E=15 MeV energy, Γ=14 MeV width
- All partial waves up to J=5/2 are nonresonant except J=3/2+
- In the d+n model for the triton excited state, a 1/2+ pole is found at E=1.4 MeV d+n center-of-mass energy with 9 MeV width
- The previously reported triton excited state from Aleksandrov (1994) had E*=7.0±0.3 MeV excitation energy and Γ=0.6±0.3 MeV width

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing question of whether three-neutron resonant states exist, following experimental indications from pion double charge exchange experiments by Sperinde (1970) and later by Stetz (1986). The authors extend earlier theoretical work by Glöckle (1978), who ruled out a low-energy 1/2+ three-neutron resonance using pure 1S0 N-N interaction, by systematically searching all partial waves up to J=5/2 with a more complete interaction. The results enable future calculations with realistic interactions and full model spaces to refine the resonance parameters, and challenge the interpretation by Aleksandrov (1994) and Barabanov (1995) that a narrow triton excited state originates from the (nn)p channel.
