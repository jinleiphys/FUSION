# Light kaonic atoms: from "corrected" to "summed up" Deser formula

> Accuracy of "corrected Deser" and "summed up Deser" formulas was checked for the $K^- p$ and $K^- d$ systems. It was found that the last one is much more accurate and should be used for connection the $1s$ level shift to the corresponding scattering length.

## Key claim
The "summed up Deser" formula is much more accurate than the "corrected Deser" formula for connecting the 1s level shift of kaonic atoms to the corresponding scattering length, with accuracy better than 2% for the two-body K- p system and 6% for the three-body K- d system, though the paper notes that previous checks for the three-body case were unclear due to approximations in the methods used.

## Method
The author used three different antikaon-nucleon potentials (two phenomenological and one chiral) that reproduce SIDDHARTA experimental data for kaonic hydrogen. For the two-body K- p system, the 1s level shift and width were calculated directly by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with strong plus Coulomb potentials, and these exact results were compared with values from the "corrected Deser" and "summed up Deser" formulas. For the three-body K- d system, the exact results came from Faddeev-type three-body equations with directly included strong plus Coulomb interactions, and the K- d scattering lengths were evaluated using dynamically exact Faddeev-type AGS equations with strong potentials only.

## Key numbers
- K- p scattering lengths for three potentials reproducing SIDDHARTA data: a = -0.76 + i 0.89 fm (V_1,SIDD), -0.74 + i 0.90 fm (V_2,SIDD), -0.77 + i 0.84 fm (V_Chiral)
- For K- p system, accuracy of "summed up Deser" formula: 0.8-1.6% for the shift and 0.2-1.1% for the width
- For K- d system, accuracy of "summed up Deser" formula: 0.1-3.3% for the shift and 0.8-5.3% for the width
- K- d scattering lengths: a = -1.49 + i 1.24 fm (V_1,SIDD), -1.51 + i 1.25 fm (V_2,SIDD), -1.59 + i 1.32 fm (V_Chiral)
- Exact 1s level shift for K- p with V_1,SIDD: -313 eV shift, 597 eV width
- Exact 1s level shift for K- d with V_1,SIDD: -767 eV shift, 928 eV width

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Shevchenko and collaborators who performed dynamically exact Faddeev-type calculations of the K- NN system, and directly addresses the accuracy of approximate formulas first suggested by Deser for pion-nucleon systems and later corrected for heavier mesons by the authors of the "corrected Deser" formula. The paper enables experimentalists at SIDDHARTA-2 and J-PARC E57 to reliably extract scattering lengths from measured 1s level shifts in kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium, using the more accurate "summed up Deser" formula rather than the commonly used "corrected Deser" formula.
