# Tri-meson bound state $BBB^{\ast}$ via delocalized $π$~Bond

> During the last decades, numerous exotic states which cannot be explained by the conventional quark model have been observed in experiment. Some of them can be understood as two-body hadronic molecules, such as the famous $X(3872)$, analogous to deuteron in nuclear physics. Along the same line, the existence of the triton leaves an open question whether there is a bound state formed by three hadrons. Since, for a given potential, a system with large reduced masses is more easier to form a bound state, we study the $BBB^{\ast}$ system with the one-pion exchange potential as an exploratory step by solving the three-body Schrödinger Equation. We predict that a tri-meson molecular state for the $BBB^{\ast}$ system is probably existent as long as the molecular states of its two-body subsystem $BB^*$ exist.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a tri-meson molecular bound state of the $BBB^{\ast}$ system is probably existent, but only under the condition that the molecular states of its two-body subsystem $BB^*$ exist. The prediction is exploratory and depends on the one-pion exchange potential and the value of the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for the $BBB^{\ast}$ system using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, where the system is divided into three two-body subsystems. The one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) is used as the interaction, with a monopole form factor to regularize short-distance behavior, and the coupled-channel effect between $BB^{\ast}$ and $B^{\ast}B^{\ast}$ is included. The wave functions are expanded in Laguerre polynomials, and the binding energy is obtained as a function of the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- The $BB^*$ subsystem with isospin $I=1$ and quantum number $J^P=1^+$ has a binding energy of 5.08 MeV for a cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 1440$ MeV.
- The isospin singlet and triplet $BB^*$ systems have the same binding energy of 5.08 MeV when the cutoff parameter is $\Lambda = 1107.7$ MeV.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_{\pi} = 132$ MeV, the pionic coupling constant is $g = 0.57$, and the pion mass is $m_{\pi} = 139$ MeV.
- The masses of the bottom mesons are $M_B = 5279$ MeV and $M_{B^*} = 5325$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of three-hadron molecular states, analogous to the triton in nuclear physics, following the observation of two-body hadronic molecules like $X(3872)$. It contrasts with the study by Wilbring (2017) which ruled out universal bound states of three bottomed mesons from the Efimov effect using only contact interactions, suggesting that including the long-range OPEP may change the outcome. The paper also builds on the authors' previous work on double heavy tri-meson systems such as $DD^{\ast}K$ and $BB^{\ast}\bar{K}$ (Ma, 2017) using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation.
