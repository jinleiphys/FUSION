# Binding-energy independence of reduced single particle strengths derived from $(p,d)$ reactions

> An overall reduction factor (ORF) is introduced for studying the quenching of single particle strengths through nucleon transfer reactions. The ORF includes contributions of all the probed bound states of the residual nucleus in a transfer reaction and permits a proper comparison with results of inclusive knockout reactions. A systematic analysis is made with 103 sets of angular distribution data of $(p,d)$ reactions on 21 even-even targets with atomic mass numbers from 8 to 56 using the consistent three-body model reaction methodology proposed in [J. Lee, J.A. Tostevin, B.A. Brown, et al., Phys. Rev. C 73, 044608 (2006)]. The extracted ORFs are found to be nearly independent on the nuclear isospin asymmetry, which is different from the systematics of inclusive knockout reactions but is consistent with the recent measurement of $(d,t)$, $(d,3He)$, $(p,2p)$, and $(p,pn)$ reactions on nitrogen and oxygen isotopes and \textit{ab initio} calculations.

# Binding-energy independence of reduced single particle strengths derived from $(p,d)$ reactions

## Key claim
The overall reduction factors (ORFs) extracted from 103 sets of $(p,d)$ reaction data on 21 even-even targets show nearly no dependence on isospin asymmetry over a wide range of $\Delta S$ values, contradicting the strong linear trend seen in inclusive knockout reactions but consistent with recent transfer and knockout measurements on light isotopes and *ab initio* calculations. The authors argue that previous discrepancies between transfer and knockout results arose partly from different definitions of reduction factors (state-by-state versus inclusive), and that defining an ORF that sums over all probed bound states permits a proper comparison.

## Method
The authors analyzed 103 sets of angular distribution data from $(p,d)$ reactions on 21 even-even targets with mass numbers from 8 to 56, using the consistent three-body model reaction methodology (TBMRM) of Lee, Tostevin, Brown et al. (2006). This methodology employs the Johnson-Soper adiabatic approximation, constrains single-particle potential parameters using Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme SkX interaction, and calculates nucleon-target optical potentials by folding the effective JLM nucleon-nucleon interaction with Hartree-Fock density distributions. For each reaction, experimental spectroscopic factors were extracted for 2-4 bound states of the residual nucleus, and an overall reduction factor was computed as the ratio of summed experimental to summed theoretical differential cross sections at the peaks of angular distributions.

## Key numbers
- 103 sets of angular distribution data analyzed from $(p,d)$ reactions on 21 even-even targets
- Target mass numbers range from 8 to 56 (nuclei include \nuc{8}{He}, \nuc{12,14}{C}, \nuc{14,16,18}{O}, \nuc{22}{Ne}, \nuc{26}{Mg}, \nuc{28,30}{Si}, \nuc{34}{S}, \nuc{34,36,38}{Ar}, \nuc{40,42,44,48}{Ca}, \nuc{46}{Ar}, \nuc{46}{Ti}, and \nuc{56}{Ni})
- $\Delta S$ values range from -22.3 MeV to 18.6 MeV for these reactions
- For the \nuc{14}{C}$(p,d)$\nuc{13}{C} reaction at 35 MeV, the extracted ORF is $R_s^\textrm{tr}=0.565\pm 0.048$
- Linear fit with free slope yields $a = -3.37\times10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $b = 0.567$ with $\chi^2 = 3.4$; fixed slope from knockout systematics ($a = -1.46\times10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-1}$) gives $\chi^2 = 5.9$
- Bootstrap analysis (1000 resamplings) gives average slope $\langle a \rangle = 8.55\times10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-1}$ with standard deviation $\sigma_a = 2.68\times10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$
- Global uncertainty of 20% adopted for extracted spectroscopic factors, following Tsang et al.

## Context
This work extends systematic studies of single-particle strength quenching from transfer reactions, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between reduction factors from knockout reactions (which show strong isospin asymmetry dependence, as in Gade (2008) and Tostevin (2014)) and those from transfer reactions (which show weaker dependence, as in Kay (2013) and Flavigny (2013)). The authors introduce an overall reduction factor that sums over all probed bound states, analogous to the inclusive treatment in knockout reactions, enabling direct comparison. The finding that ORFs are nearly independent of isospin asymmetry is consistent with recent measurements of $(d,t)$, $(d,\nuc{3}{He})$, $(p,2p)$, and $(p,pn)$ reactions on nitrogen and oxygen isotopes by Flavigny (2013), Atar (2018), Kawase (2018), and Moro (2018), as well as *ab initio* calculations by Barbieri (2009) and Cipollone (2015). The result suggests that the systematic discrepancy between transfer and knockout reactions is not due to the exclusive versus inclusive treatment of experimental data, leaving the origin of the difference as an open question.
