# Impact of non-zero strangeness on thermodynamics of finite volume quark matter

> This paper investigates the impact of strangeness chemical potential and finite volume on QCD critical end point by employing a (2+1) flavored Polyakov quark meson model. Within the mean-field approximation, the model has been extended to study the effect of vector interactions on the thermodynamics of isospin-asymmetric quark matter. Susceptibilities of conserved charges like the quark and strangeness number are analyzed using Taylor's series expansion. The chiral phase transition boundary in the QCD phase diagram is found to be shifted towards higher values of the quark chemical potential ($μ_q$) and lower temperature (T) for decreasing system size. On the other hand, there is an opposite change to lower quark chemical potential and higher temperature for decreasing strangeness chemical potential.

# Impact of non-zero strangeness on thermodynamics of finite volume quark matter

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition boundary in the QCD phase diagram shifts toward higher quark chemical potential and lower temperature when system size decreases, but shifts toward lower quark chemical potential and higher temperature when strangeness chemical potential decreases (becomes more negative), based on mean-field calculations in the (2+1) flavor Polyakov quark meson model with vector interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a (2+1) flavor Polyakov quark meson (PQM) model within the mean-field approximation, extended to include vector meson interactions and isospin asymmetry. The total thermodynamic potential was minimized with respect to scalar fields (sigma_u, sigma_d, sigma_s), vector fields (omega, rho, phi), and Polyakov loop variables (Phi, anti-Phi) to obtain the phase structure. Susceptibilities of conserved charges (quark number and strangeness number) were computed using Taylor series expansion at zero chemical potential, and the chiral phase transition boundaries were determined from the peaks of chiral condensate derivatives.

## Key numbers

* Critical point for infinite volume and zero strangeness chemical potential: (mu_q^CP, T_CP) = (306 MeV, 55 MeV)
* For R = 5 fm at mu_S = 0: critical temperature drops by 16.37% (to 46 MeV) and quark chemical potential increases by 4.91% (to 321 MeV)
* For R = 3 fm at mu_S = 0: critical temperature drops by 19.57% (to 37 MeV) and quark chemical potential increases by 9.66% (to 352 MeV)
* For mu_S = -100 MeV at infinite volume: critical temperature rises by 50.9% (to 83 MeV) and mu_q^CP falls by 12.42% (to 268 MeV)
* For mu_S = -160 MeV at infinite volume: critical temperature increases further by 4.81% (to 87 MeV) and mu_q^CP decreases by 11.2% (to 238 MeV)
* The critical temperature at zero quark chemical potential appears around 155 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results

## Context

This work extends previous finite-volume studies using the PQM model (Bhattacharyya 2017, Magdy 2019) and the Polyakov loop modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Tripolt 2014, Bhattacharyya 2013) by simultaneously incorporating strangeness chemical potential, finite volume, and vector interactions. The inclusion of the fermion vacuum fluctuation term significantly impacts the critical point position, as noted by Chatterjee (2012) and Kovacs (2023). The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter thermodynamics in heavy-ion collisions where both finite system size and non-zero strangeness are present, and provide predictions for the QCD critical point location that can be tested against future experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
