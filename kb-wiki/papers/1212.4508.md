# The NRQED lagrangian at order 1/M^4

> The parity and time-reversal invariant effective lagrangian for a heavy fermion interacting with an abelian gauge field, i.e., NRQED, is constructed through order $1/M^4$. The implementation of Lorentz invariance in the effective theory becomes nontrivial at this order, and a complete solution for Wilson coefficient constraints is obtained. Matching conditions in the one-fermion sector are presented in terms of form factors and two-photon matrix elements of the nucleon. The extension of NRQED to describe interactions of the heavy fermion with a light fermion is introduced. Sample applications are discussed; these include the computation of nuclear structure effects in atomic bound states, the model-independent analysis of radiative corrections to low-energy lepton-nucleon scattering, and the study of static electromagnetic properties of nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the NRQED Lagrangian through order \(1/M^4\) for a heavy fermion interacting with an Abelian gauge field, finding that a complete solution for Wilson coefficient constraints from Lorentz invariance is possible at this order, but that a simple "reparametrization invariance" ansatz fails and must be replaced by a more general variational or invariant operator construction.

## Method

The authors build a complete basis of rotationally invariant, Hermitian operators in the one-fermion sector through order \(1/M^4\), then enforce Lorentz invariance via two equivalent methods: a variational calculation using infinitesimal boost transformations on the heavy fermion field, and an explicitly invariant operator construction using a matrix-valued field \(\Gamma(v,iD)\). Matching to the full theory is performed in the one-fermion sector by relating Wilson coefficients to nucleon form factors and to the low-energy expansion of virtual Compton scattering amplitudes, using the basis of Drechsel et al. (1997) for the non-Born contributions. The four-fermion sector is constructed for a heavy fermion and a light fermion, with Lorentz invariance constraints derived for arbitrary mass ratio.

## Key numbers

- The Lagrangian through order \(1/M^4\) contains 12 coefficients in the one-fermion sector (including \(c_{X1}\) through \(c_{X12}\)) plus 21 four-fermion operators (\(d_1\) through \(d_{21}\)).
- Lorentz invariance fixes 5 of the 12 one-fermion coefficients at \(1/M^4\) via relations: \(32 c_{X1} = 5Z/4 - c_F + c_D\), \(32 c_{X2} = -Z/2 + 2c_F\), \(32 c_{X4} = -Z^2 - 4c_F(Z-c_F) + 4Zc_D - 2c_{A2}\), \(32 c_{X5} = -Z + 4c_F\), \(32 c_{X6} = 4(Z-c_F) + c_D - 4c_{W1}\).
- One-photon matching yields \(c_F = \bar{F}_1 + \bar{F}_2 \equiv Z + a_N + \mathcal{O}(\alpha)\), \(c_D = \bar{F}_1 + 2\bar{F}_2 + 8\bar{F}_1^\prime \equiv Z + \frac{4}{3} M^2 (r_E^N)^2 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha)\).
- Two-photon matching gives \(c_{A1} = \bar{F}_1^2 + 4\bar{f}_{1,0} \equiv Z^2 + (4M^3/\alpha)\beta_M + \mathcal{O}(\alpha)\), \(c_{A2} = 4\bar{F}_1\bar{F}_2 + 2\bar{F}_2^2 + 16\bar{F}_1\bar{F}_1^\prime + 32\bar{f}_{2,0} \equiv 2a_N^2 + (8Z/3)M^2(r_E^N)^2 - (8M^3/\alpha)(\alpha_E + \beta_M) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha)\).
- Six new phenomenological parameters (\(c_{X7}\) through \(c_{X12}\)) are required at \(1/M^4\) from two-photon matching, expressed in terms of form factor derivatives and Compton scattering amplitudes \(f_{i,0}\) and \(f_{i,1}\).
- Lorentz invariance reduces the 21 four-fermion operators to 12 independent ones through \(1/M^4\), with constraints such as \(rd_4 + d_5 = d_2/4\), \(d_5 = r^2 d_6\), and \(rd_{14} + d_{18} = d_1/4\), where \(r = M_\chi/M\).

## Context

This work extends the NRQED construction of Caswell and Lepage (1986) and Manohar (1997) from order \(1/M^3\) to order \(1/M^4\), correcting the reparametrization invariance ansatz of Luke and Manohar (1992) which breaks down at this order as shown by Heinonen et al. (2012). The results enable model-independent computation of nuclear structure effects in atomic bound states (relevant to the proton radius puzzle of Pohl et al., 2010), radiative corrections to low-energy lepton-nucleon scattering, and analysis of nucleon static electromagnetic properties including polarizabilities and generalized moments as studied by Lvov (1993) and Drechsel et al. (1998).
