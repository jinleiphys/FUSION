# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of symmetric nuclear matter

> We present an accurate numerical study of the equation of state of nuclear matter based on realistic nucleon--nucleon interactions by means of Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations. The AFDMC method samples the spin and isospin degrees of freedom allowing for quantum simulations of large nucleonic systems and can provide quantitative understanding of problems in nuclear structure and astrophysics.

## Key claim

Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter using a realistic two-body interaction (AV6') produce an equation of state that differs significantly from Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) results at high densities, and do not show any lowering of energies at saturation density compared to those methods, suggesting inadequacies in commonly used three-nucleon interaction models.

## Method

The authors performed AFDMC simulations of symmetric nuclear matter with 28, 76, and 108 nucleons in periodic boxes, using the Argonne v8' potential truncated to the first six operators (AV6'), which includes central spin-isospin dependent and tensor components but omits spin-orbit terms. The AFDMC method uses Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations to linearize quadratic spin-isospin operators, replacing the sum over spin-isospin states with sampling of rotations of four-component spinors, and employs the fixed-phase approximation to handle the fermion sign problem.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (ρ/ρ₀ = 1.0), the AFDMC energy per particle for 28 nucleons is -11.5(1) MeV
- The minimum energy per particle from the fitted equation of state is E₀/A = -14.04(4) MeV at a saturation density of ρ/ρ₀ = 1.83(1)
- The compressibility at saturation is approximately 190 MeV
- At ρ/ρ₀ = 0.5, the AFDMC energy per particle is -7.64(3) MeV for 28 nucleons, -7.7(1) MeV for 76 nucleons, and -7.45(2) MeV for 108 nucleons
- At ρ/ρ₀ = 3.0, the AFDMC energy per particle is -10.6(1) MeV for 28 nucleons, -10.7(6) MeV for 76 nucleons, and -10.8(1) MeV for 108 nucleons
- The fitted parameters are α = 3.09(6) MeV and β = -0.44(8) MeV

## Context

This work extends AFDMC, previously applied to pure neutron matter (Fantoni 2001, Sarsa 2003) and neutron drops (Gandolfi 2006, Pederiva 2004), to symmetric nuclear matter with both protons and neutrons. The results are compared with FHNC/SOC and BHF calculations by Bombaci (2005) using the same potential, showing that FHNC overbinds at high density and BHF underbinds, with the discrepancies attributed to neglected elementary diagrams in FHNC and insufficient hole-line contributions in BHF. The method enables future calculations with spin-orbit terms, three-body forces, and asymmetric nuclear matter for neutron star and supernova applications.
