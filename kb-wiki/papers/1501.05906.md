# The role of correlation entropy in nuclear fusion in liquid lithium, indium and mercury

> Nuclear fusion cross-sections considerably higher than corresponding theoretical predictions are observed in low-energy experiments with metal matrix targets and accelerated deuteron beams. The cross-section increment is significantly higher for liquid than for solid targets. We propose that the same two-body correlation entropy used in evaluating the metal melting entropy explains the large liquid-solid difference of the effective screening potential that parameterizes the cross-section increment. This approach is applied to the specific case of the $^6$Li(d,$α$)$^4$He reaction, whose measured screening potential liquid-solid difference is $(235 \pm 63)$ eV. Cross sections in the two metals with the highest two-body correlation entropy (In and Hg) have not yet been measured: increments of the cross sections in liquid relative to the ones in solid metals are estimated with the same procedure.

## Key claim

The difference in effective screening potential between liquid and solid lithium for the \(^6\)Li(d,α)\(^4\)He reaction can be explained by the two-body correlation entropy of the liquid metal, specifically the energy contributed by a "cage" of correlated atoms surrounding the colliding particles, rather than by standard atomic electron screening or Debye-Hückel models alone.

## Method

The authors model the liquid metal as a collection of correlated atoms forming a cage around the incoming deuteron, with the cage's internal energy contribution derived from the two-body correlation entropy \(S_2\) (in units of \(k_B\)) as calculated by Wallace (1991). They equate the energy gained by the deuteron in the liquid phase to \(\Delta U = N_{\text{cage}} \, k_B \, T_m \, S_2\), where \(N_{\text{cage}}\) is the number of atoms in the cage and \(T_m\) is the melting temperature (or experimental temperature \(T\)). Using the known \(S_2\) for lithium and the measured liquid-solid screening potential difference, they solve for \(N_{\text{cage}}\) and the cage volume, then extrapolate to indium and mercury using their respective \(S_2\) values and density parameters.

## Key numbers

- Measured liquid-solid screening potential difference for \(^6\)Li(d,α)\(^4\)He: \((235 \pm 63)\) eV (Fang et al.), with a reference value of \((200 \pm 70)\) eV used in the paper.
- Two-body correlation entropy values: \(S_2(\text{Li}) = -2.48\), \(S_2(\text{In}) = -3.39\), \(S_2(\text{Hg}) = -3.97\) (all in units of \(k_B\)).
- For lithium at \(T = 520\) K, the estimated number of atoms in the cage is \(N_{\text{cage}} \approx 1800\) for \(\Delta E = 200\) eV.
- Cage volume for lithium: \(V_{\text{cage}} \approx 2.0 \times 10^{-19}\) cm\(^3\) for \(\Delta E = 200\) eV at \(T = 520\) K.
- Dimensionless correlation length for lithium: \(x \approx 20\) (cage correlation length about twenty times the atomic diameter).
- Density parameters: \(\rho\sigma^3(\text{Li}) = 1.25\), \(\rho\sigma^3(\text{In}) = 1.37\), \(\rho\sigma^3(\text{Hg}) = 1.12\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous explanations for enhanced fusion in solid metal matrices (Coraddu et al., 2009), which invoked modified Debye-Hückel screening, stopping power corrections, and quantum energy-momentum uncertainty (Galitskii-Yakimets effect). It specifically addresses the liquid-solid difference observed by Kasagi's group (Fang et al., Yonemura et al.) and applies the two-body correlation entropy formalism of Wallace (1991, 1994) to fusion reactions. The predictions for indium and mercury are intended to guide future experiments, such as those recently performed by the Kasagi group (Kasagi, 2014) on liquid indium.
