# Bethe-Salpeter equation at leading order in Coulomb gauge

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation and leptonic decay constants for pseudoscalar and vector quark-antiquark mesons with arbitrary quark masses are studied in Coulomb gauge, under a leading order truncation. As input, we use a pure linear rising potential, supplemented by a contact term arising from the conservation of total color charge. It is shown how the equations can be written in terms of manifestly finite functions, despite the infrared singular interaction. The resulting equations are solved numerically. Both the pattern of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the leading order heavy quark limit are visible.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector mesons in Coulomb gauge can be rendered manifestly finite despite the infrared singular interaction (a pure linear rising potential supplemented by a contact term from color charge conservation), provided the equations are rewritten in terms of the finite quark mass function rather than the individually divergent dressing functions A and B.

## Method

The authors truncate the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations at leading order in Coulomb gauge, keeping only the instantaneous temporal gluon exchange interaction (a linear rising potential corresponding to a \(1/q^4\) infrared singularity plus a contact term). They decompose the quark propagator and meson Bethe-Salpeter vertex into Dirac components, perform the energy integrals analytically exploiting the instantaneous nature of the interaction, and solve the resulting coupled integral equations numerically for the mass function and meson bound-state amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The input string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma}\) is taken in the range 440–762 MeV, corresponding to the physical Wilson string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma_W}\) up to \(\sqrt{3\sigma_W}\) based on lattice results.
* The chiral condensate is defined as \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -2N_c \int d\vec{k} \, M_k / \omega_k\) (Eq. 13), with \(N_c=3\).
* The pion decay constant convention used gives \(f_\pi = 92.2\) MeV, noted as a factor \(\sqrt{2}\) smaller than the modern value \(f_\pi = 130.4\) MeV.
* The Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation is given as \(M_{PS}^2 f_{PS}^2 = -(m^+ + m^-) \langle \bar{q}q \rangle\) in the chiral limit (Eq. 40).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order Coulomb gauge truncation scheme of Watson (2011) from the quark gap equation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons, building on the earlier Adler-Davis gap equation (1984) and the heavy-quark limit studies of Popovici (2010, 2011). It demonstrates that both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the heavy-quark limit can be accommodated within a single truncation, enabling future calculations of the full meson spectrum and decay constants in Coulomb gauge, though the authors note that quantitative phenomenology would require a more sophisticated truncation beyond leading order, such as that of Pak (2011).
