# $N\bar N$ Scattering at NLO Order in An Effective Theory

> We have proposed to use an effective theory to describe interactions of an $N\bar N$-system. The effective theory can be constructed in analogy to the existing effective theory for an $NN$-system. In this work we study the next-to-leading order correction to $N\bar N$ scattering near the threshold in the effective theory. We find that the experimental data can be well described with the effective theory.

# N\bar N Scattering at NLO Order in An Effective Theory

## Key claim

The authors claim that an effective field theory (EFT) for the nucleon-antinucleon (N\bar N) system, constructed in analogy to the existing EFT for the NN system, can describe experimental data for p\bar p elastic scattering near threshold at next-to-leading order (NLO), but with the caveat that the momentum expansion may converge slowly because contributions from pion exchanges become dominant around p ~ 200 MeV, limiting the applicable energy region to below E ~ 50 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a nonrelativistic effective field theory containing nucleon, antinucleon, and pion fields, with contact interactions and pion-exchange terms fixed by chiral symmetry. They compute the N\bar N scattering amplitude at next-to-leading order (order p^1) by evaluating one-loop diagrams in dimensional regularization with the minimal subtraction scheme, and they fit the resulting amplitude to experimental data for p\bar p elastic scattering. The fit is performed both with and without an additional resummation of rescattering effects in S-wave channels.

## Key numbers

- The NLO fit to p\bar p elastic scattering data yields chi/d.o.f. ≈ 1.7, compared to chi/d.o.f. ≈ 2.6 at leading order.
- Determined coupling constants (in units of GeV^{-2}) from the NLO fit without rescattering: c_0 = (180 ± 4) + i(20 ± 3), c_1 = -211 + i87, d_0 = -(60 ± 2) + i(61 ± 4), d_1 = (37 ± 3) + i(56 ± 4).
- Scattering lengths (in fm) from the NLO fit without rescattering: a_{[^1S_0,0]} = -(5.88 ± 0.22) - i(1.12 ± 0.17), a_{[^1S_0,1]} = 0.67 - i0.09, a_{[^3S_1,0]} = 4.24 - i0.06, a_{[^3S_1,1]} = -(0.25 ± 0.06) - i(1.08 ± 0.08).
- The effective theory describes data below E ~ 50 MeV, corresponding to p ~ 200 MeV.
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_pi ≈ 93 MeV, and the axial coupling as g_A ≈ 1.25.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier tree-level effective theory for N\bar N scattering (Chen and Ma, 2010) to next-to-leading order, building on the effective field theory framework for NN systems developed by Weinberg (1990), van Kolck (1994), and Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996). The approach contrasts with traditional potential models (Nijmegen, Paris, Julich groups) that include meson exchanges and phenomenological imaginary parts for annihilation. The results enable future consistent treatment of the threshold enhancement observed in J/psi radiative decay (BES Collaboration), B decays (Belle Collaboration), and e+e- annihilation (BaBar Collaboration), and can be tested against spin-dependent N\bar N scattering at the planned PAX experiment.
