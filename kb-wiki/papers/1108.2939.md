# QCD matter in extreme environments

> We review various theoretical approaches to the states of QCD matter out of quarks and gluons in extreme environments such as the high-temperature states at zero and finite baryon density and the dimensionally reduced state under an intense magnetic field. The topics at high temperature include the Polyakov loop and the 't Hooft loop in the perturbative regime, the Polyakov loop behaviour and the phase transition in some of non-perturbative methods; the strong-coupling expansion, the large-Nc limit and the holographic QCD models. These analyses are extended to hot and dense matter with a finite baryon chemical potential. We point out that the difficulty in the finite-density problem has similarity to that under a strong magnetic field. We make a brief summary of results related to the topological contents probed by the magnetic field and the Chiral Magnetic Effect. We also address the close connection to the (1+1) dimensional system.

## Key claim
This review argues that the deconfinement phase transition and the dynamics of the Polyakov loop can be understood through a combination of perturbative and non-perturbative analytical approaches, but the perturbative regime alone cannot capture confinement physics near and below the critical temperature. The author emphasizes that the difficulty in finite-density QCD has a structural similarity to the problem of QCD under a strong magnetic field.

## Method
The paper surveys perturbative calculations of the Polyakov loop effective potential (Weiss potential) up to two-loop order in SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, then examines non-perturbative methods including the strong-coupling expansion on the lattice, the large-Nc limit, and holographic QCD models. For the magnetic field case, the author discusses the Chiral Magnetic Effect and the connection to (1+1) dimensional systems, using the 't Hooft loop as a disorder parameter and the dimensional reduction framework of Electrostatic QCD (EQCD) and Magnetostatic QCD (MQCD).

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 nucleon/fm³
- Pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD: Tc ≈ (150–160) MeV
- Critical energy density: εc ≈ (0.8–1.0) GeV/fm³
- Energy density at normal nuclear density: m_N ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 GeV/fm³ (with nucleon mass m_N = 0.94 GeV)
- Initial energy density at SPS (√s_NN = 17.2 GeV): ε₀ = (1.2–2.6) GeV/fm³ (at τ₀ = 0.8 fm)
- Initial energy density at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV): ε₀ = (12–20) GeV/fm³ (at τ₀ = 0.6 fm)
- Strong coupling constant at Z⁰ boson mass: α_s(M_Z²) = 0.1184(7)
- String tension coefficient from spatial Wilson loop in pure gluonic theory: c ≈ 0.553(1)
- Fitting parameters for Polyakov loop potential: a(T)/T⁴ = 3.51 − 2.47 t⁻¹ + 15.2 t⁻² and b(T)/T⁴ = −1.75 t⁻³ with t = T/T_c

## Context
This review extends the author's previous pedagogical reviews (Fukushima 2008, 2010) which covered chiral-symmetry breaking but not deconfinement physics, making this work complementary by focusing on the Polyakov loop dynamics and the 't Hooft loop. The paper connects perturbative results (Weiss 1980, 1981; Belyaev 1991; Korthals-Altes 1993) with non-perturbative strong-coupling expansions (Polyakov 1978; Gross 1983) and lattice data (Borsanyi 2010; Bazavov 2010; Gupta 2007), and it enables future work on the QCD phase diagram, particularly the search for the QCD critical point through beam-energy scan programs at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and JPARC.
