# Particle-Hole Optical Model: Fantasy or Reality?

> An attempt to formulate the optical model of particle-hole-type excitations (including giant resonances) is undertaken. The model is based on the Bethe--Goldstone equation for the particle-hole Green function. This equation involves a specific energy-dependent particle-hole interaction that is due to virtual excitation of many-quasiparticle configurations and responsible for the spreading effect. After energy averaging, this interaction involves an imaginary part. The analogy between the single-quasiparticle and particle-hole optical models is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper attempts to formulate a particle-hole optical model for describing the spreading effect on particle-hole strength functions at arbitrary (but high enough) excitation energies, analogous to the single-quasiparticle optical model. The model is presented as a "semimicroscopic" approach that relies on the statistical assumption that different doorway states decay independently into many-quasiparticle configurations after energy averaging.

## Method

The author starts from the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the nonlocal particle-hole Green function and introduces a specific energy-dependent particle-hole interaction (polarization operator) that accounts for virtual excitation of many-quasiparticle configurations. After energy averaging over an interval larger than the density of many-quasiparticle states, this interaction acquires an imaginary part and is parameterized similarly to the single-quasiparticle optical-model self-energy. The resulting energy-averaged particle-hole Green function satisfies equations analogous to the Dyson equation for the single-quasiparticle case, and the strength function is obtained as a superposition of doorway-state resonances with a complex energy shift.

## Key numbers

- The Landau-Migdal force strength parameter C = 300 MeV fm^3 is used in the parametrization of the local polarization operator.
- The observable total width of the charge-exchange giant monopole resonance in ^208Bi is approximately 15 MeV, used to calibrate the imaginary parts of both the single-quasiparticle optical potential and the particle-hole polarization operator.
- The isobaric analog resonance Fermi strength S_A is approximately (N - Z), which for ^208Pb parent nucleus would be 44.

## Context

This work extends the semimicroscopic approach previously developed by the same group (Urin and collaborators, various references) for describing giant resonance spreading effects, which had been limited to the "pole" approximation valid only near the giant resonance energy. The particle-hole optical model aims to describe the low- and high-energy tails of giant resonances, which are needed for phenomena such as the asymmetry of (gamma,n) reaction cross sections at the isovector giant quadrupole resonance energy and the isospin-selfconsistent description of isobaric analog resonance damping. The model is analogous to the dispersive single-quasiparticle optical model of Mahaux and collaborators (1980s), which is widely used for describing single-particle excitations at relatively high energies.
