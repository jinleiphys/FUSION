# Scalar mesons nonet in a scaled effective Lagrangian

> A scaled SU(3) Nambu - Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian is used to compute the mass of the nine scalar mesons in the vacuum and the mass of the gluball. It is shown that a suitable choice of the vacuum gluon condensate allows to reproduce the experimental masses of the scalar mesons except for the {$K^*_{0}$}(1430). This choice corresponds to a weak coupling between the gluon and quark condensates, giving a {$f_{0}$}(1500) or a {$f_{J}$}(1710) which is nearly a pure glueball.

## Key claim

Using a scaled SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian with a suitable choice of the vacuum gluon condensate (around 350 MeV), the experimental masses of the scalar meson nonet can be reproduced except for the K*_0(1430), and this choice yields a weak coupling between gluon and quark condensates such that the f_0(1500) or f_J(1710) is nearly a pure glueball.

## Method

The authors bosonize a scaled SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective action that includes a dilaton field chi representing the gluon condensate, then expand the action to second order in meson and dilaton fluctuations. They compute the masses of the scalar nonet by finding the zeroes of the inverse propagators for the u-ubar, s-sbar, and mixed channels, diagonalizing a 3x3 matrix that mixes the sigma_0, sigma_8, and chi fields. The constituent up quark mass is fixed to reproduce the a_0(980) mass, while the vacuum gluon condensate chi_0 is varied as a free parameter, with calculations performed for glueball candidates at 1500 MeV and 1710 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Constituent up quark mass M_u^0 = 489 MeV, chosen to reproduce the a_0(980) mass at 980 MeV
- For chi_0 = 350 MeV with glueball at 1500 MeV: m_Phi_I [f_0(980)] = 973 MeV, m_Phi_II [f_0(1370)] = 1367 MeV, m_Phi_III = 1482 MeV (pure glueball mass before mixing)
- For chi_0 = 350 MeV with glueball at 1710 MeV: m_Phi_I [f_0(980)] = 976 MeV, m_Phi_II [f_0(1370)] = 1377 MeV, m_Phi_III = 1706 MeV
- The K*_0(1430) mass is calculated as 1183 MeV for all chi_0 values, below the experimental 1430 MeV
- Quark condensates: <u-bar u>^(1/3) = -208 MeV, <s-bar s>^(1/3) = -207 MeV
- For chi_0 = 350 MeV and glueball at 1500 MeV, the mass increase of the glueball from q-qbar mixing is 18 MeV (Delta_III = 1.2 x 10^-2)
- For chi_0 = 350 MeV and glueball at 1710 MeV, the mass increase from mixing is 4 MeV (Delta_III = 2.3 x 10^-3)

## Context

This work extends the scaled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model previously developed in SU(2) by Ripka and Jaminon and in SU(3) by Jaminon and Van den Bossche to compute scalar meson masses including mixing with the glueball. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of scalar meson spectroscopy, where experimental identification of the nonet and glueball candidates remains confused, with competing scenarios from Montanet, Palano, Tornqvist, and Lindenbaum and Longacre. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD predictions from Amsler, Close, and Sexton et al., showing that the f_0(1500) or f_J(1710) can be identified as a nearly pure glueball only when the coupling between quark and gluon sectors is weak.
