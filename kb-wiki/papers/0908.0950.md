# Spin response of a normal Fermi liquid with noncentral interactions

> We consider the spin response of a normal Fermi liquid with noncentral interactions under conditions intermediate between the collisionless and hydrodynamic regimes. This problem is of importance for calculations of neutrino properties in dense matter. By expressing the deviation of the quasiparticle distribution function from equilibrium in terms of eigenfunctions of the transport equation under the combined influence of collisions and an external field, we derive a closed expression for the spin-density--spin-density response function and compare its predictions with that of a relaxation time approximation. Our results indicate that the relaxation time approximation is reliable for neutrino properties under astrophysically relevant conditions.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact closed expression for the spin-density–spin-density response function of a normal Fermi liquid with noncentral interactions under conditions intermediate between the collisionless and hydrodynamic regimes, and finds that the relaxation time approximation (with a single relaxation time chosen to reproduce the correct collisionless behavior) differs from the exact result by less than 10% for astrophysically relevant conditions.

## Method

The authors start from the linearized quasiparticle kinetic (Boltzmann) transport equation for a spatially uniform, time-dependent magnetic field, including the collision integral for two-quasiparticle scattering. They express the deviation of the quasiparticle distribution from equilibrium in terms of eigenfunctions of the homogeneous transport equation (the Brooker–Sykes eigenfunctions), obtained by Fourier transforming the energy variable and solving the resulting Schrödinger-like equation. Using these eigenfunctions, they derive a closed sum expression for the spin-density–spin-density response function \(X_\sigma\) (in the absence of mean-field effects) valid for arbitrary \(\omega\tau_\sigma\), and compare it with the single-relaxation-time approximation \(X_\sigma^{\rm relax} = N(0)/(1 - i\omega\tau_\sigma)\).

## Key numbers

* The spin relaxation time \(\tau_\sigma\) is related to the quasiparticle relaxation time \(\tau_0\) by \(\tau_\sigma = 3\tau_0 / [2\pi^2 (1-\lambda_\sigma)]\) (Eq. 15); for \(\lambda_\sigma=0\), this gives \(\tau_\sigma \approx 0.15\,\tau_0\).
* For \(\lambda_\sigma=0\), the ratio of the spin relaxation time in the hydrodynamic limit to that in the collisionless limit is \(\tau_\sigma^{\rm hydro}/\tau_\sigma = \pi^2/9 \approx 1.10\) (from Eq. 18 and surrounding text).
* For \(\lambda_\sigma=-1\) (maximal lack of spin conservation), \(\tau_\sigma^{\rm hydro}/\tau_\sigma \approx 1.17\); for \(\lambda_\sigma \to 1\), the ratio approaches 1.
* In the high-frequency limit \(\omega\tau_\sigma \gg 1\) for \(\lambda_\sigma=0\), the ratio of the real parts of the response functions is \({\rm Re} X_\sigma^{\rm relax} / {\rm Re} X_\sigma \to 5/6\).
* The differences between the exact and relaxation-time-approximation response functions are generally less than 10% for conditions relevant to neutrino interactions in stellar collapse, supernovae, and neutron stars.

## Context

This work extends exact solution methods for the transport equation in normal Fermi liquids, previously applied to transport coefficients in the hydrodynamic limit (Jensen, Smith, and Wilkins, 1968; Brooker and Sykes, 1968) and to relaxational modes without an external field (Brooker and Sykes, 1972), to the case of the spin response with an external field and noncentral interactions. It provides a benchmark for the single-relaxation-time approximation used in neutrino-process calculations (Lykasov, 2008) and shows that approximation is reliable to within 10% for astrophysical conditions. The results enable more accurate calculations of neutrino scattering, production, and absorption rates in dense matter (supernovae, neutron stars) and can be generalized to other spherical harmonic components and multicomponent systems.
