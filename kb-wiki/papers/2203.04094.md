# Status of deep subbarrier $\mathbf{{}^{12}{\rm C}+{}^{12}{\rm C}}$ fusion and advancing the Trojan horse method

> In this paper, I will update the current status of the carbon-carbon fusion research taking into account that after the latest analysis [Beck {\it et al.} Eur. Phys. J. A {\bf 56}, 97 (2020), Letter to the Editor] new important experimental and theoretical results had been published and will discuss how to advance new THM measurements to extract the low-energy astrophysical $S$-factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the sharp rise in the low-energy astrophysical S-factor for 12C+12C fusion originally reported by Tumino et al. (Nature, 2018) using the Trojan Horse Method (THM) was an artifact of neglecting Coulomb-nuclear distortions in the plane-wave approximation; including these distortions reduces the extracted S-factor by up to three orders of magnitude, and the renormalized THM results show qualitative agreement with the first fully microscopic AMD calculations by Taniguchi and Kimura (2021).

## Method

The author reviews the current experimental and theoretical status of deep subbarrier 12C+12C fusion, then derives the formal R-matrix formalism for multi-level, two-channel THM reactions, including the full distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) amplitude with Coulomb-nuclear distorted waves, the off-shell factor, and the Coulomb factor for narrow resonances. The kinematics and triple differential cross-section equations are presented for two candidate THM reactions: 12C(14N,d)24Mg* and 12C(13C,n)24Mg*, with the goal of guiding future THM measurements that properly account for Coulomb interactions in the initial, intermediate, and final states.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier for head-on 12C+12C collision (using carbon radius 1.4×12^(1/3) fm) is ≈ 6.6 MeV, making astrophysically relevant energies deep sub-Coulomb.
- The Gamow window for stars of mass (8-10) solar masses is E ≈ 1.2-1.8 MeV, corresponding to temperature interval (0.35-0.64)×10^9 K; for ~25 solar masses it is E ≈ 1.8-2.6 MeV, corresponding to (0.64-1.15)×10^9 K.
- The lowest measured resonance energy from direct measurements is E ≈ 2.14 MeV (Spillane et al., 2007); direct measurements have not reached below 2.1 MeV as of 2020 (STELLA collaboration and Notre Dame-Mexico experiments).
- The original THM data (Tumino et al.) reached E = 0.8 MeV and showed resonances at 0.88, 0.98, and 1.5 MeV; including Coulomb-nuclear distortions reduces the low-energy astrophysical factor by up to a factor of 10^3.
- The AMD calculation (Taniguchi and Kimura, 2021) shows strong resonances at 0.94 MeV and 1.5 MeV, and a resonance at 2.2 MeV.
- At the normalization energy of 2.67 MeV for THM data, the relative d-24Mg kinetic energy is 0.92 MeV with a Coulomb barrier of about 3 MeV; the Coulomb parameter in the entry channel (14N+12C) is 4.4.

## Context

This paper updates the status of 12C+12C fusion research following the analysis by Beck et al. (2020, European Physical Journal A), incorporating new direct measurements from the STELLA collaboration and Notre Dame-Mexico group (2020), and the first fully microscopic AMD calculation by Taniguchi and Kimura (2021). The work extends earlier theoretical efforts including coupled-channel calculations by Esbensen et al., TDHF methods by Bonasera et al., and density-constrained approaches by Godbey et al., none of which reproduced the low-energy resonance structure because they lacked cluster degrees of freedom. The paper provides the formal framework for advancing new THM measurements that properly include Coulomb-nuclear distortions, which could finally resolve the long-standing debate over the low-energy S-factor and its resonance structure for carbon-carbon fusion.
