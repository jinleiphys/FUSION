# Novel Phenomena in Particle Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The novel phenomena observed in particle angular correlations are reviewed. They include the double-peak away-side azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the long-range pseudorapidity near-side (ridge) correlations in heavy-ion as well as in proton-induced collisions. The collision system and energy dependence of these phenomena are examined, wherever possible and most abundantly for the ridge correlations. Their possible theoretical interpretations and what might be learned about the properties of the collision systems from theoretical comparisons are discussed. Prospective future measurements and theoretical undertakings are outlined that might help further the understanding of the physics mechanisms underlying these phenomena.

## Key claim

The double-peak away-side azimuthal correlations and the long-range near-side ridge correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may be largely explained by higher-order hydrodynamic flow harmonics, particularly triangular flow \(v_3\), rather than by jet-medium interactions such as Mach-cone shock waves or deflected jets; however, the quantitative question of whether the measured correlations are entirely due to hydrodynamic flow remains difficult because flow measurements are contaminated by non-hydrodynamical sources.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of two-particle angular correlations in azimuthal angle \(\Delta\phi\) and pseudorapidity difference \(\Delta\eta\) from RHIC and LHC experiments, using trigger and associated particle pairs. The event-wise flow background from elliptic flow \(v_2\) is subtracted using the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) procedure, and the residual correlation structures are examined for their dependence on collision system, centrality, and particle transverse momentum. The review also discusses the extraction of higher-order flow harmonics \(v_n\) via event-plane and two-particle cumulant methods, including the use of pseudorapidity gaps to reduce nonflow correlations.

## Key numbers

- The initial temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is inferred from photon measurements to be in excess of 220 MeV (Adare 2008).
- The away-side double-peak structure is observed for trigger \(p_T\) range \(2.5 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 4.0\) GeV/\(c\) and associated \(p_T\) range \(1.0 < p_T^{\rm asso} < 2.5\) GeV/\(c\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (PHENIX, Adler 2005).
- The near-side ridge extends to at least 4 units in \(\Delta\eta\) for trigger \(p_T > 2.5\) GeV/\(c\) in 0-30% central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (PHOBOS, Alver 2010).
- The factorization of two-particle correlation Fourier coefficients holds to a good approximation at low-intermediate \(p_T\) below 4 GeV/\(c\) for all harmonics except \(v_1\) (LHC experiments).
- The \(v_3\) measured with respect to the third-order event plane is compared to hydrodynamic calculations, and correlations between \(\psi_3\) event planes on opposite sides of the collision region are measured (PHENIX, Adare 2011).

## Context

This review extends the understanding of particle correlations beyond the initial focus on jet-quenching and elliptic flow, which were established by experiments at RHIC (Adams 2005, Adler 2005) and later at the LHC. The work connects the observed ridge and double-peak structures to the realization that event-by-event fluctuations in initial nucleon distributions generate odd harmonic flow, particularly triangular flow \(v_3\), as proposed by Mishra (2007) and Alver (2010). The review enables future measurements that can disentangle hydrodynamic flow from nonflow correlations, such as jet contributions, and outlines the need for improved theoretical calculations of initial-state fluctuations and their evolution in the quark-gluon plasma.
