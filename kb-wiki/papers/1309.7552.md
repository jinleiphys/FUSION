# Pairing Effects in Nuclear Fusion Reaction

> We simulate a heavy-ion collision using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) treating pairing correlation in the three-dimensional coordinate space. We apply the Cb-TDHFB to 22O+22O collision with a contact-type pairing energy functional, and compare results of Cb-TDHFB and TDHF to investigate the effects of pairing correlations in nuclear fusion. Our results seem to indicate that pairing effects do not increase the fusion cross section in this system.

## Key claim

The paper's simulations indicate that pairing correlations do not increase the fusion cross section in the 22O+22O system; in fact, the pairing correlation appears to hinder fusion, contrary to the expectation that attractive pairing might enhance it.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional simulations of 22O+22O collisions using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) with a contact-type pairing energy functional and the Skyrme SkM* effective interaction. They compared these results with standard time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) simulations at the same incident energy and impact parameters, and also tested a weaker pairing strength to examine the dependence of the fusion cross section on pairing.

## Key numbers

- Incident center-of-mass energy: E_cm = 10 MeV, which is above the Coulomb barrier for the 22O+22O system.
- Impact parameters tested: b = 2.7 to 3.1 fm.
- Average neutron gap energy for 22O: 2.06 MeV, obtained from experimental binding energies using the three-point formula.
- Simulation box dimensions: 32 fm × 20 fm × 40 fm, with a mesh spacing of Δx = Δy = Δz = 1.0 fm.
- Number of canonical basis states treated: 48 for the total system.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier development of the Cb-TDHFB method (Ebata and Nakatsukasa, 2010), which had previously been validated for linear response calculations against the quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA). It is the first application of TDHFB with a modern effective interaction in three-dimensional space to heavy-ion collisions, overcoming numerical difficulties that had prevented such studies (Bulow and Lacroix, 2010). The results suggest that pairing correlations can suppress fusion in symmetric oxygen isotopes, and the authors indicate that further study is needed for heavier systems and asymmetric projectile-target combinations where nucleon transfer might alter the effect.
