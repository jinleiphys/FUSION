# Ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations for neutron-rich P, Cl, and K isotopes

> Neutron-rich P, Cl, and K isotopes, particularly those with neutron numbers around $N=28$, have attracted extensive experimental and theoretical interest. We utilize the \textit{ab initio} valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group approach, based on chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, to investigate the exotic properties of these isotopes. Systematic calculations of the low-lying spectra are performed. A key finding is the level inversion between $3/2_1^+$ and $1/2_1^+$ states in odd-$A$ isotopes, attributed to the inversion of $π0d_{3/2}$ and $π1s_{1/2}$ single-particle states.\textit{Ab initio} calculations, which incorporate the three-nucleon forces, correlate closely with existing experimental data. Further calculations of effective proton single-particle energies provide deeper insights into the shell evolution for $Z=14$ and $16$ sub-shells. Our results indicate that the three-body force plays important roles in the shell evolution for $Z=14$ and $16$ sub-shells with neutron numbers ranging from 20 to 28. Additionally, systematic \textit{ab initio} calculations are conducted for the low-lying spectra of odd-odd nuclei. The results align with experimental data and provide new insights for future research into these isotopes, up to and beyond the drip line.

## Key claim

The *ab initio* valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations, using two sets of chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon forces, reproduce the level inversion between the \(3/2_1^+\) and \(1/2_1^+\) states in neutron-rich odd-\(A\) P, Cl, and K isotopes around \(N=28\), but the agreement depends on the specific interaction: for example, the EM1.8/2.0 interaction predicts a \(3/2^+\) ground state for \(^{43}\)P contrary to the experimental suggestion of \(1/2^+\), while the \(NN+3N\)(lnl) interaction does not show a crossover in effective single-particle energies yet still predicts the experimental inversion in \(^{47}\)K and \(^{41}\)Cl.

## Method

The intrinsic Hamiltonian, including nucleon-nucleon (\(NN\)) and three-nucleon (\(3N\)) chiral forces, was normal-ordered and truncated at the two-body level. The VS-IMSRG approach, using the Magnus formulation, decoupled the Hamiltonian from the full Hilbert space to a valence space consisting of the \(sd\) shell for protons and the \(pf\) shell for neutrons, with the \(^{28}\)O core. Two sets of chiral interactions were employed: EM1.8/2.0 (SRG-evolved \(NN\) at \(\lambda = 1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) plus nonrenormalized \(3N\) with cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\)) and \(NN+3N\)(lnl) (bare N\(^3\)LO \(NN\) with SRG scale \(\lambda = 2.6\) fm\(^{-1}\) plus local/nonlocal \(3N\) regulators). Final diagonalization of the valence-space Hamiltonians was performed with the KSHELL shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator frequency was fixed to \(\hbar \omega = 16\) MeV, with basis truncations \(e_{\rm max} = 14\) and \(E_{3\rm max} = 14\).
- For \(^{47}\)K, the energy gap between the \(\pi 0d_{3/2}\) and \(\pi 1s_{1/2}\) orbits at \(N=28\) when tensor forces are included is only 0.228 MeV, indicating near degeneracy.
- The omission of tensor forces increases the shell gap between the \(0d_{5/2}\) and \(1s_{1/2}\) orbits to 2 MeV in K isotopes.
- In \(^{47}\)K, the average occupancy of the \(\pi 0d_{3/2}\) orbit increases to 3.777 (with \(NN+3N\)) compared to 3.118 (with only \(NN\)), while the \(\pi 1s_{1/2}\) occupancy decreases to 1.250 (with \(NN+3N\)) from 1.890 (with only \(NN\)).
- The ground state of \(^{49}\)K is predicted to be \(1/2^+\) by both interactions, consistent with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends *ab initio* VS-IMSRG calculations, previously successful for \(^{40}\)Mg and \(^{42}\)Si (Yuan 2024), to neutron-rich P, Cl, and K isotopes, addressing the monopole-driven shell evolution around \(N=28\) that has been studied experimentally (Touchard 1982, Sun 2020) and with phenomenological shell models such as SDPF-U (PhysRevC.79.014310) and Gorkov self-consistent Green's function theory (PhysRevC.104.044331). The results enable future experimental validation of predicted low-lying spectra for odd-odd nuclei up to and beyond the drip line, and clarify the roles of three-nucleon forces and tensor forces in the disappearance of the \(Z=16\) sub-shell at \(N=28\).
