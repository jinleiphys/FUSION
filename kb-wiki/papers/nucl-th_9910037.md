# A Practical Method for Relativistic 3N-Scattering Calculations with Realistic Potentials

> Using a scale transformation in momentum space a phase equivalent relativistic potential is generated from the nonrelativistic potential. By that transformation a practical method for the relativistic 3N scattering with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials is introduced. The formalism can be applied to any realistic nonrelativistic potential. We also discuss the locations of the moving logarithmic singularities of the free relativistic three-body Green's function. It enters in the relativistic 3N Faddeev equations, which have been formulated long time ago for a 3-boson bound state and which we propose to also use for 3N scattering. Finally we compare relativistic deuteron wave functions to nonrelativistic ones.

## Key claim
A scale transformation in momentum space can generate a phase-equivalent relativistic nucleon-nucleon potential from any nonrelativistic realistic potential, enabling practical relativistic three-nucleon (3N) scattering calculations via Faddeev equations. The method is proposed for 3N scattering, though the relativistic Faddeev equations were previously formulated only for a three-boson bound state.

## Method
The author applies a scale transformation in momentum space to convert a nonrelativistic realistic nucleon-nucleon potential into a phase-equivalent relativistic potential. The resulting relativistic potential is then inserted into the relativistic 3N Faddeev equations, which were originally formulated for three-boson bound states. The locations of moving logarithmic singularities in the free relativistic three-body Green's function are analyzed, as these enter the relativistic 3N Faddeev equations. Relativistic deuteron wave functions are computed and compared to their nonrelativistic counterparts.

## Key numbers
- Relativistic deuteron wave functions (S-wave and D-wave components) are compared to nonrelativistic ones over a radial range from 0 to 5 fm, as shown in the figures.
- The momentum-space plots cover relative momenta p_ij and p_jk from 0 to 2.5 fm^{-1}.
- The relativistic and nonrelativistic wave functions are shown for the deuteron S-wave and D-wave components, with the relativistic wave functions labeled as "Relativistic" and nonrelativistic as "Nonrelativistic" in the figures.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Faddeev approach, which had been formulated for three-boson bound states, to the case of 3N scattering with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The scale transformation method allows any existing nonrelativistic realistic potential to be used in relativistic calculations without refitting. This enables future relativistic 3N scattering calculations using established potentials, and the comparison of relativistic versus nonrelativistic deuteron wave functions provides insight into relativistic effects in the two-body subsystem.
