# Nucleon Resonances with Hidden Charm in $γp $ reactions

> The excitations of nucleon resonances with hidden charm, $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$, in the $γp$ reactions are investigated by using the predictions from the available meson-baryons (MB) coupled-channel models. For the $γ\,p\rightarrow J/ψ\, p$ process, we first calculate the Pomeron-exchange amplitudes with the parameters determined from fitting the available total cross section data up to invariant mass $W=300$ GeV. We then add the resonant amplitudes to examine the effects of $ N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ excitations on the cross sections of $γ\,p\rightarrow J/ψ\, p$ in the near threshold energy region. The $N^*_{c\bar{c}}\to MB$ transition matrix elements are determined from the partial decay widths predicted by the meson-baryons coupled-channel models. The $γp \to N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ transition amplitudes are calculated from the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model. The total $γp\rightarrow J/ψ\, p$ amplitudes then depend on an off-shell form factor, parameterized as $F_V(q^2)=Λ^4/(Λ^4 + (q^2 - m^2_V)^2)$ with $Λ=550$ MeV. We then demonstrate that the $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ can be most easily identified in the differential cross sections at large angles of outgoing $J/ψ$ where the contribution from Pomeron-exchange becomes negligible. With the same VMD model and the same coupled-channel models of $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$, we also calculate the resonant amplitudes for the $γ\,p\rightarrow Vp\rightarrow N^*_{c\bar{c}} \rightarrow \bar{D}^0Λ_c^+ (\bar{D}^{*0}Λ_c^+)$ processes. By adding the non-resonant amplitudes due to the exchange of $\bar{D}^{*0}$ ($\bar{D}^{0})$, we then predict the cross sections of $γ\,p\rightarrow \bar{D}^0Λ_c^+(\bar{D}^{*0}Λ^+_c)$ for additional experimental tests of the available meson-baryon coupled-channel models of $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether nucleon resonances with hidden charm ($N^*_{c\bar{c}}$), predicted by meson-baryon coupled-channel models, can be observed in $\gamma p$ reactions, but it does not revise these models to fit the LHCb pentaquark data; instead, it uses the existing model predictions for masses, spins, parities, and partial decay widths to calculate photo-production cross sections, finding that the resonances are most easily identified in differential cross sections at large $J/\psi$ angles where the Pomeron-exchange background is negligible.

## Method

The authors calculated the $\gamma p \rightarrow J/\psi p$ amplitude as a sum of a Pomeron-exchange background, using the Donnachie and Landshoff model with parameters fit to total cross section data up to $W=300$ GeV, and resonant amplitudes from $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ excitations. The $\gamma p \rightarrow N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ transition was modeled via the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model with an off-shell form factor $F_V(q^2)=\Lambda^4/(\Lambda^4 + (q^2 - m^2_V)^2)$ using $\Lambda = 550$ MeV, and the $N^*_{c\bar{c}} \rightarrow MB$ transition matrix elements were taken from the partial decay widths of available coupled-channel models. For the $\gamma p \rightarrow \bar{D}^0\Lambda_c^+$ and $\bar{D}^{*0}\Lambda_c^+$ processes, resonant amplitudes were calculated with the same VMD and coupled-channel models, and non-resonant amplitudes from $\bar{D}^{*0}$ or $\bar{D}^{0}$ exchange were added.

## Key numbers

*   The off-shell form factor parameter is $\Lambda = 0.55$ GeV (550 MeV).
*   The Pomeron-exchange model parameters include $\beta_{u/d}=2.07$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\beta_c = 0.84$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\alpha_0=1.25$, and $\alpha'_P = 0.25$ GeV$^{-2}$.
*   The vector meson decay constants used in VMD are $f_\rho = 5.33$, $f_\omega = 15.2$, $f_\phi = 13.4$, and $f_{J/\psi} = 11.2$.
*   The predicted masses of $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ states from various models range from 4262 MeV to 4481 MeV, with total widths from 1.8 MeV to 144.3 MeV.
*   For the $\gamma p \rightarrow J/\psi p$ process, the Pomeron-exchange model was fit to data up to an invariant mass $W=300$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ states (Wu 2010, 2012) and their photo-production (Huang 2013) by using a more complete set of coupled-channel models and a refined treatment of the VMD off-shell form factor. It directly addresses the upcoming JLab experiments (E12-16-007 and GlueX) by providing predictions for $\gamma p \rightarrow J/\psi p$ and $\gamma p \rightarrow \bar{D}^0\Lambda_c^+$ cross sections, enabling experimental tests of the meson-baryon molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquark states (Aaij 2015, 2019). The approach contrasts with two-gluon and three-gluon exchange models (Brodsky 2000) by using a Pomeron-exchange background that better describes high-energy data.
