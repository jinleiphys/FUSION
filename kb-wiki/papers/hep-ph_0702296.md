# QCD phase diagram and charge fluctuations

> We discuss the phase structure and fluctuations of conserved charges in two flavor QCD. The importance of the density fluctuations to probe the existence of the critical end point is summarized. The role of these fluctuations to identify the first order phase transition in the presence of spinodal phase separation is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that charge density fluctuations can serve as probes of the QCD phase diagram, but emphasizes that divergent fluctuations are not unique to a critical end point (CEP) or tricritical point (TCP); they also appear at a first-order phase transition when spinodal instabilities are present, meaning that observing such fluctuations alone is a necessary but not sufficient condition for identifying a CEP.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation to study the chiral phase transition in two-flavor QCD, computing the dynamical quark mass as an order parameter and the associated susceptibilities (quark number susceptibility χ_q and isovector susceptibility χ_I) as functions of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ_q. They compare these model results with lattice QCD (LGT) calculations for N_f=2 with m_q ≈ 80 MeV, and also extend the analysis to include spinodal instabilities by examining the pressure derivative and the resulting behavior of χ_q across the first-order transition region.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point (TCP) disappears from the NJL phase diagram when the isovector-vector coupling G_V exceeds 0.6 G_S (where G_S is the scalar coupling strength).
- At the TCP, the quark number susceptibility χ_q diverges with a correlation length critical exponent γ_q = 1 for paths approaching asymptotically tangential to the phase boundary, and γ_q = 1/2 for other paths (in mean-field approximation).
- Along the second-order critical line at μ_q=0, the critical properties of χ_q are governed by the specific heat critical exponent α ≈ -0.2 (consistent with O(4) universality class).
- Lattice results for N_f=2 with m_q ≈ 80 MeV show χ_q at μ_q=0 exhibits a cusp-like structure that increases with μ_q, while χ_I shows no peak structure and depends only weakly on μ_q.
- At fixed T=50 MeV in the NJL model, χ_q diverges and changes sign when crossing the isothermal spinodal lines of the first-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective model calculations (e.g., Stephanov (1998), Hatta and Ikeda (2003)) and lattice QCD studies (LGT1, LGT2, LGT3) of the QCD phase diagram, specifically addressing the role of charge fluctuations as signals for the critical end point. The paper shows that the non-monotonic behavior of χ_q along the phase boundary, previously proposed as a CEP signature, is modified when spinodal instabilities are considered, and that divergent fluctuations also occur at first-order transitions. This enables future experimental searches in heavy-ion collisions to distinguish between equilibrium and non-equilibrium scenarios by looking for divergent charge fluctuations as probes of both the CEP and first-order phase boundaries.
