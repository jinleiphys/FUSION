# The $Σ$ and $Ξ$ electromagnetic form factors in the extended vector meson dominance model

> We propose a phenomenological extended vector meson dominance model for the baryon electromagnetic structure, and it is found that the current experimental data on the $Σ$ and $Ξ$ electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region can be well described. Meanwhile, we can also reproduce the ratios of the total cross sections of reactions $e^+e^- \to Σ^+\barΣ^-$, $Σ^0\barΣ^0$, and $Σ^-\barΣ^+$, which are $9.7 \pm 1.3 : 3.3 \pm 0.7 :1$ at center-of-mass energies from $2.3864$ to $3.02$ GeV. We also analytically continue the expression of the form factors to space-like region and estimate the charge radii of the $Σ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons. The result for the $Σ^-$ is in agreement with the experimental date.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extended vector meson dominance model that can simultaneously describe the electromagnetic form factors of all three \(\Sigma\) hyperon charge states (\(\Sigma^+\), \(\Sigma^0\), \(\Sigma^-\)) using a single common value of the intrinsic form factor parameter \(\gamma\), provided that the isospin combinations from isovector and isoscalar mesons are properly accounted for; for the \(\Xi\) hyperons, additional excited vector meson states are required to reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological extended vector meson dominance model for the Dirac and Pauli form factors of \(\Sigma\) and \(\Xi\) hyperons, incorporating contributions from the isovector \(\rho^0\) meson and isoscalar \(\omega\) and \(\phi\) mesons with their isospin combinations. For the \(\Xi\) hyperons, two additional excited vector meson states (\(V_1\) and \(V_2\)) are included. The model parameters are determined by \(\chi^2\) fits to experimental data on the effective form factors \(G_{\rm eff}\) in the time-like region from BESIII, Belle, and BABAR collaborations, and the resulting expressions are analytically continued to the space-like region to compute charge radii.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of total cross sections for \(e^+e^- \to \Sigma^+\bar{\Sigma}^-\), \(\Sigma^0\bar{\Sigma}^0\), and \(\Sigma^-\bar{\Sigma}^+\) is \(9.7 \pm 1.3 : 3.3 \pm 0.7 : 1\) at center-of-mass energies from 2.3864 to 3.02 GeV.
* Fitted model parameters for \(\Sigma\) hyperons: \(\gamma = 0.527 \pm 0.024\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\beta_\rho = 1.63 \pm 0.07\), \(\beta_{\omega\phi} = -0.08 \pm 0.06\), \(\alpha_{\omega\phi} = -3.18 \pm 0.77\), with \(\chi^2/{\rm dof} = 1.69\) for 33 data points.
* Fitted mass and width of the excited vector meson \(V_1\): \(M_{V_1} = 2.742\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{V_1} = 71\) MeV.
* Fitted mass and width of the excited vector meson \(V_2\): \(M_{V_2} = 2.993\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{V_2} = 88\) MeV (taken from BESIII (2020)).
* Calculated mean squared charge radii: \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Sigma^+} = 0.78\) fm\(^2\), \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Sigma^0} = 0.12\) fm\(^2\), \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Sigma^-} = 0.65\) fm\(^2\), \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Xi^-} = 0.43\) fm\(^2\), \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Xi^0} = -0.07\) fm\(^2\).
* The calculated \(\langle r_{ch}^2 \rangle_{\Sigma^-} = 0.65\) fm\(^2\) agrees with the experimental value \(0.61 \pm 0.12 \pm 0.09\) fm\(^2\) from SELEX (2001).

## Context

This work extends previous vector meson dominance studies of hyperon electromagnetic form factors, particularly the work on \(\Lambda\) hyperons by Yang (2019) and Li (2021), and on \(\Sigma\) hyperons by Li (2020) and Dai (2021). Unlike those earlier studies which used different values of the parameter \(\gamma\) for different \(\Sigma\) charge states, the present model uses a single common \(\gamma\) by incorporating isospin combinations of the vector meson contributions. The model successfully reproduces the BESIII data on the ratios of \(\Sigma\) production cross sections that had disagreed with earlier theoretical predictions by Anselmino (1992) and Kubis (2000). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted charge radii and the properties of the excited vector meson states \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) at facilities such as BESIII.
