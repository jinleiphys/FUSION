# Elliptic flow fluctuations in central collisions of spherical and deformed nuclei

> Elliptic flow ($v_2$) fluctuations in central heavy-ion collisions are direct probes of the fluctuating geometry of the quark-gluon plasma, and, as such, are strongly sensitive to any deviation from spherical symmetry in the shape of the colliding nuclei. We investigate the consequences of nuclear deformation for $v_2$ fluctuations, and we assess whether current models of medium geometry are able to predict and capture such effects. Assuming linear hydrodynamic response between $v_2$ and the eccentricity of the medium, $\varepsilon_2$, we perform accurate comparisons between model calculations of $\varepsilon_2$ fluctuations and STAR data on cumulants of elliptic flow, in central Au+Au and U+U collisions. From these comparisons, we evince that the most distinct signatures of nuclear deformation appear in the non-Gaussianities of $v_2$ fluctuation, and we show, in particular, that the non-Gaussian $v_2$ fluctuations currently observed in central Au+Au collisions are incompatible with model calculations that implement a quadrupole coefficient of order 12\% in the $^{197}$Au nuclei. Finally, we make robust predictions for the behavior of higher-order cumulants of $v_2$ in collisions of non-spherical nuclei.

## Key claim

The non-Gaussian fluctuations of elliptic flow ($v_2$) in central heavy-ion collisions are the most distinct signatures of nuclear deformation, and the non-Gaussian $v_2$ fluctuations observed in central Au+Au collisions are incompatible with model calculations that implement a quadrupole coefficient of order 12% in the $^{197}$Au nuclei, assuming linear hydrodynamic response between $v_2$ and the eccentricity of the medium.

## Method

The author used the TRENTo model of initial conditions with two parametrizations (geometric mean with p=0 and arithmetic mean with p=1) to simulate Au+Au and U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, implementing nuclear shapes via a Fermi distribution with quadrupole ($\beta_2$) and hexadecapole ($\beta_4$) deformation parameters. Eccentricity fluctuations $\varepsilon_2$ were computed event-by-event and rescaled by a constant hydrodynamic response factor $\kappa$ to compare with STAR data on cumulants $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$ in central collisions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{238}$U nuclei: $R=6.80$ fm, $a=0.60$ fm, $\beta_2=0.236$, $\beta_4=0.098$
- For $^{197}$Au nuclei: $R=6.40$ fm, $a=0.53$ fm; spherical case $\beta_2=\beta_4=0$; deformed case $\beta_2=-0.125$, $\beta_4=-0.017$
- TRENTo fluctuation parameter $k=0.5$ for both parametrizations at RHIC (compared to $k=2.0$ at LHC)
- Hydrodynamic response factor $\kappa=0.165$ for TRENTo p=0 model; $\kappa=0.27$ for TRENTo p=1 model
- The knee of multiplicity distribution: $dN_{ch}/d\eta|_{\rm knee}=663$ in Au+Au collisions, $dN_{ch}/d\eta|_{\rm knee}=821$ in U+U collisions
- The change of sign of $v_2\{4\}^4$ in Au+Au collisions occurs around 2.5% centrality in the p=0 model with spherical $^{197}$Au nuclei

## Context

This work extends studies of elliptic flow fluctuations in deformed nuclei (Goldschmidt 2015, Giacalone 2017) by focusing on non-Gaussian signatures rather than just the root-mean-square elliptic flow. It fills a gap in the literature where previous theoretical studies of the prolate $^{238}$U shape (Kolb 2000, Heinz 2004, Kuhlman 2005, Schenke 2014, Moreland 2014) did not assess non-Gaussian fluctuations. The results enable future Bayesian analyses to constrain nuclear deformation parameters from flow fluctuation data and predict that higher-order cumulants of $v_2$ will show distinct behavior in collisions of non-spherical nuclei.
