# Separabelized Skyrme Interactions and Quasiparticle RPA

> A finite rank separable approximation for the quasiparticle RPA with Skyrme interactions is applied to study the low lying quadrupole and octupole states in some S isotopes and giant resonances in some spherical nuclei. It is shown that characteristics calculated within the suggested approach are in a good agreement with available experimental data.

# Separabelized Skyrme Interactions and Quasiparticle RPA

## Key claim
The finite-rank separable approximation for quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) with Skyrme interactions reproduces low-lying quadrupole and octupole states and giant resonances in good agreement with available experimental data, but the authors note that some overestimate of energies in ³⁴S and ³⁸S indicates room for two-phonon effects not yet included.

## Method
The authors start from the Skyrme interaction SIII and compute the single-particle spectrum via Hartree-Fock (HF) with the continuum discretized on a harmonic oscillator basis. The particle-hole residual interaction is approximated by its Landau-Migdal form keeping only l=0 terms, then expressed as a sum of N separable terms using a Gauss integration formula with N=45 abscissas. The QRPA equations are solved in the finite-rank separable form, which reduces the matrix inversion to dimension 4N×4N regardless of configuration space size, and pairing is treated via BCS with a cutoff at 5 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the calculated energy centroid of the giant dipole resonance is 12.7 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 13.4 MeV.
- For the 3⁻₁ state in ²⁰⁸Pb, the calculated excitation energy is 2.66 MeV and EWSR = 21%, compared to experimental values of 2.62 MeV and 20.4%.
- For the low-energy octupole resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb below 7.5 MeV, the calculated centroid energy is 5.96 MeV with EWSR = 12%, versus experimental values of 5.4 MeV and 15.2%.
- For the high-energy octupole resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb, the calculated centroid energy is 20.9 MeV with EWSR = 61%, versus experimental values of 20.5 ± 1 MeV and EWSR = 75 ± 15%.
- The energy centroids for the giant dipole resonance are 19.9 MeV in ³⁶Ar, 15.8 MeV in ¹¹²Sn, and 12.7 MeV in ²⁰⁸Pb.
- Increasing N from 45 to 60 in ²⁰⁸Pb changes energies and transition probabilities by not more than 1%.
- For octupole excitations in ²⁰⁶Pb, the finite-rank approximation requires inverting a 2N=90 matrix instead of diagonalizing a 1376×1376 matrix.

## Context
This work extends the finite-rank separable approximation for particle-hole interactions of Skyrme type, previously developed by the same authors (Severyukhin et al., 1998), to include pairing and apply it to QRPA calculations. The approach is contrasted with the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) of Soloviev (1989), but unlike QPM all parameters are derived from Skyrme forces rather than being phenomenological. The results for S isotopes are compared to QRPA calculations with full Skyrme forces by Khan et al. (2001). The method enables future inclusion of two-phonon configurations while keeping matrix dimensions manageable (not exceeding 40 for giant resonance calculations in heavy nuclei), which would otherwise require diagonalizing matrices of order thousands.
