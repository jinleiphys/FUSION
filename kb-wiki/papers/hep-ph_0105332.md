# Ising-like dynamical signatures and the end-point of the QCD transition line

> An increase in the size of coherent domains in the one component $Φ^4$ field theory under the influence of a uniformly changing external magnetic field near the critical end-point $T_Φ=T_c, h_Φ=0$ was proposed recently as an estimate also for the variation of the chiral correlation length of QCD near its respective hypothetical end point in the $T_{QCD}-μ_{QCD}$ plane. The present detailed numerical investigation of the effective model suggests that passing by the critical QCD end point with realistic rate of temperature change will trigger large amplitude oscillations in the temporal variation of the chiral correlation length. A simple mechanism for producing this phenomenon is suggested.

## Key claim

The paper claims that passing by the hypothetical QCD critical end-point with a realistic rate of temperature change will trigger large amplitude oscillations in the temporal variation of the chiral correlation length, rather than the smooth increase predicted by earlier first-order relaxational dynamics. This result is conditional on the validity of mapping QCD dynamics onto a one-component classical three-dimensional Φ⁴ field theory in the Ising universality class.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the classical equations of motion of a three-dimensional one-component Φ⁴ field theory on lattices of size N=16-128, with a uniformly changing external magnetic field h(t) representing the passage through the QCD critical end-point. They measured the order parameter and the correlation length using multiple algorithms, including a spectral method based on the two-point function and a resummed method using field fluctuations. The system was initialized in thermal equilibrium at h₀=-0.2 and evolved with constant dh/dt, covering the same parameter range as Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the Φ⁴ model was determined as T_Φ,c=1.5 for L=32 and T_Φ,c=1.57 for L=64 at h=0.
- The maximal correlation lengths reached were in the range (2.5-3.5)ξ₀, where ξ₀ is the initial correlation length.
- The shift in the location of the first maximum of the correlation length scales as h(ξ_max,a)=c a^(-0.5±0.01).
- The maximal correlation length itself scales as ξ_max(a)=c' a^(0.211±0.01), in agreement with the dynamical scaling prediction of 0.222 from Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000) using ν=0.630, β=0.326, δ=4.8, z=2+(6ln(4/3)-1)η, η=0.0335.
- The error of order parameter trajectories from different thermal initial conditions was ≈0.007 for h<0 and ≈0.02 for h>0, about 100 times smaller than the oscillation amplitude.
- The relaxation rate Γ was found in the range 0.005-0.025, independent of r_i and a.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000), who proposed a first-order relaxational dynamics for the inverse correlation length when passing through the QCD critical end-point, predicting a factor of 2-3 increase in correlation length. The present numerical investigation of the full Φ⁴ dynamics reveals that the first-order approximation is insufficient: the system exhibits oscillatory behavior in the correlation length after passing the critical point, with the correlation length steeply dropping to a minimum following its maximum. This suggests that experimental observation of the critical end-point via increased coherence in pion radiation might be missed if freeze-out occurs slightly beyond the maximum of the correlation length. The work also relates to the classification of critical dynamics by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977), noting that a more complete theory including conserved baryon number density would fall into class C and is left for future study.
