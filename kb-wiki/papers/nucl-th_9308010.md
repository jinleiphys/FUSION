# Pionic Bound States in Momentum Space

> Bound states of the pion--nucleus system are investigated in momentum space using a microscopic optical potential with inherent energy dependences and nonlocalities arising from elementary potential models. The wave equation and computational techniques are tested, shallow levels are calculated, and a comparison is made with experiment. Deep levels are found to be deeper and broader than those in other investigations.

# Pionic Bound States in Momentum Space

## Key claim

Deeply bound pionic states in heavy nuclei such as lead are found to be approximately 7% more bound and 20-300% wider than previously reported by other investigations, but remain quasistable (nonoverlapping) and therefore experimentally observable. The model dependence arises from the inclusion of energy dependences in the optical potential, complex energy extrapolations, and the treatment of the finite-range annihilation term.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Schrodinger equation in momentum space for the pion-nucleus system, using a microscopic optical potential (LPOTT) with nonlocality, off-shell unitarity, and energy dependence derived from a separable pi-nucleon T matrix. Bound state energies and widths were determined by searching for poles of the combined Coulomb-plus-nuclear T matrix in complex energy space using the Lande subtraction technique, with the annihilation strengths B0 and C0 fit to experimental 1s and 2p levels in O-16 and Ca-40.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-208 1s state: binding energy approximately 7 MeV, width approximately 0.5 MeV (in contrast to approximately 20 MeV widths expected from lowest-order perturbation theory)
- Relativistic effects: 2% for Ca-40 1s, 70% for Pb-208 1s, and 48% for Pb-208 2s
- Fitted annihilation parameters: B0 = (-0.074 + 0.067 i) m_pi^{-4}, C0 = (0.051 + 0.069 i) m_pi^{-6}
- For Pb-208: calculated binding energies approximately 7% larger than Toki et al. (1989), Nieves et al. (1993), and Konijn et al.; s-state widths approximately 20% larger than Toki et al. and approximately 200% larger than Nieves et al.; p and d state widths approximately 50% larger than Toki et al. and approximately 200-300% larger than Nieves et al.
- Vacuum polarization increases binding by 0.5% and width by 2%
- For pure nuclear bound states: optical potential strength must be increased 8- to 15-fold before nuclear states appear, with Pb binding energies ranging from -(7 + 0.4 i) MeV to -(22 + 13 i) MeV

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space approach of Kwon and Tabakin (1989) and Cieply et al. (1990) to deeply bound pionic states, and contrasts with coordinate-space calculations by Friedman and Scoff, Hirenzaki et al., and Toki and Yamazaki (1988, 1989) who used the phenomenological Ericson-Ericson optical potential in the approximate Klein-Gordon equation. The results enable more precise predictions for experimental searches using (n,p), (n,d), (pi-,gamma), and (pi-,p) reactions, and reveal that the effective local potential for 1s pions in lead has an inner attractive part despite overall repulsion, a finding that could be tested by further experimental and theoretical work.
