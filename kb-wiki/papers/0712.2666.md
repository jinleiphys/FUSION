# A covariant view on the nucleons' quark core

> Established results for the quark propagator in Landau gauge QCD, together with a detailed comparison to lattice data, are used to formulate a Poincare covariant Faddeev approach to the nucleon. The resultant three-quark amplitudes describe the quark core of the nucleon. The nucleon's mass and its electromagnetic form factors are calculated as functions of the current quark mass. The corresponding results together with charge radii and magnetic moments are discussed in connection with the contributions from various ingredients in a consistent calculation of nucleon properties, as well as the role of the pion cloud in such an approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev approach, using quark propagators that reproduce lattice QCD data and rainbow-ladder truncation for the kernel, can describe the quark core of the nucleon; however, the calculated nucleon properties (mass, electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, magnetic moments) are discussed in connection with the role of the pion cloud, which is not included in the calculation and is expected to provide additional contributions.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in Landau gauge using a parameterized coupling that reproduces lattice results for the quark propagator. They then solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in rainbow-ladder truncation to obtain meson and diquark amplitudes on their mass shells, using a Chebyshev decomposition for the angular dependence. The nucleon is described via a quark-diquark Faddeev equation, where the quark-diquark amplitudes are decomposed into scalar and axial-vector components, and the diquark propagator is constructed from the quark loop with an ansatz for its off-shell behavior constrained by the pole and residue conditions.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass is calculated as a function of the current quark mass, with the physical point corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 140 MeV.
* The pion decay constant is calculated via Eq. (bse:pdc) and used as a benchmark; the paper reports that the rainbow-ladder BSE reproduces meson masses within an error of less than 20% (citing Maris 1997, Maris 1999, Maris 2002).
* The diquark masses are obtained from the BSE: scalar diquark mass and axial-vector diquark mass are determined at the respective mass poles \(P^2 = -M^2\).
* The nucleon's electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, and magnetic moments are calculated as functions of the current quark mass; specific numerical values for these observables are not provided in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the covariant Faddeev approach of Oettel (2002) by using quark propagators that are directly compared to lattice QCD data, rather than relying on simpler parameterizations. The calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors within this framework enables a systematic study of the quark core contributions, with the explicit goal of separating them from pion cloud effects that must be added later. The authors note that the rainbow-ladder truncation reproduces meson masses to within 20% accuracy (Maris 1997, Maris 1999, Maris 2002) and that the same truncation is applied to the diquark and nucleon sectors.
