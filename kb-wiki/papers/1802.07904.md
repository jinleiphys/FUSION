# Bulk viscosity of a hot QCD/QGP medium in strong magnetic field within relaxation-time approximation

> The bulk viscosity of hot QCD medium has been obtained in the presence of strong magnetic field. The present investigation involves the estimation of the quark damping rate and subsequently the thermal relaxation time for quarks in the presence of magnetic field while realizing the hot QCD medium as an effective Grand-canonical ensemble of effective gluons and quarks-antiquarks. The dominant process in the strong field limit is $1\rightarrow 2$ ($g\rightarrow q \bar{q}$) which contributes to the bulk viscosity in a most significant way. Further, setting up the linearized transport equation in the framework of an effective kinetic theory with hot QCD medium effects and employing the relaxation time approximation, the bulk viscosity has been estimated in lowest Landau level (LLL) and beyond. The temperature dependence of the ratio of the bulk viscosity to entropy density indicates towards its rising behavior near the transition temperature.

# Bulk viscosity of a hot QCD/QGP medium in strong magnetic field within relaxation-time approximation

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of a hot QCD medium in a strong magnetic field receives significant contributions from $1\rightarrow 2$ (gluon to quark-antiquark) processes, which dominate over $2\rightarrow 2$ scattering because their rate is proportional to $\alpha_s$ rather than $\alpha_s^2$, but this result is obtained within the relaxation-time approximation and the effective kinetic theory framework, and the temperature dependence of the ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density shows a rising behavior near the transition temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) to encode hot QCD medium effects, then computed the quark damping rate and thermal relaxation time for the dominant $1\rightarrow 2$ process ($g\rightarrow q\bar{q}$) in a strong magnetic field background. They set up a linearized relativistic transport equation within the relaxation-time approximation and solved for the bulk viscosity in the lowest Landau level (LLL) and with higher Landau level (HLL) corrections up to $l=3$, using the Debye mass and effective running coupling constant derived from the quasiparticle distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass ratio $m_D^2/\alpha_s$ was computed at $|eB| = 0.3$ GeV$^2$ for temperatures up to $l=3$ Landau level, with HLL contributions beyond $l=3$ found negligible in the chosen temperature range.
- The effective coupling ratio $\alpha^l_{eff}/\alpha_s$ was evaluated for $|eB| = 0.3$ GeV$^2$ and $|eB| = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, showing that the ratio is nearly identical for both field strengths, indicating weak dependence on magnetic field magnitude.
- The ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density $\zeta_{eff}/s$ was computed as a function of $T/T_c$ for $|eB| = 0.3$ GeV$^2$ in LLL approximation, showing higher values near the transition temperature and approaching zero at very high temperature.
- HLL corrections to bulk viscosity were shown up to $l=3$ Landau level at $|eB| = 0.3$ GeV$^2$, with corrections beyond $l=3$ negligible in the chosen temperature range.

## Context

This work extends the estimation of transport coefficients in hot QCD medium under strong magnetic fields, building on prior calculations of longitudinal electrical conductivity by Kurian and Chandra (2017) using the same EQPM framework, and on the work of Hattori et al. (2016, 2017) who computed transport coefficients including bulk viscosity in strong magnetic fields using kinetic theory. The results are compared with lattice QCD data from Meyer (2007) and sum rule analysis by Karsch (2007) for bulk viscosity in the absence of magnetic field, showing that the magnetic field enhances $\zeta/s$. The analysis enables further calculations of shear viscosity and charge diffusion coefficients in strong magnetic field backgrounds within the same effective kinetic theory approach.
