# The origin of the nucleon mass

> It is often claimed that 98% of the nucleon mass is generated by quantum chromodynamics. The decomposition of the nucleon mass based on the trace of the energy-momentum tensor suggests that gluons play by far a dominant role. About 25 years ago, Ji proposed another decomposition based on the energy component of the energy-momentum tensor, leading to a quite different picture. Recently, we critically revisited these decompositions and argued that both overlooked pressure effects. In particular we showed that Ji's decomposition, although mathematically correct, makes little sense from a physical point of view. We identify the proper mass decomposition along with a balance equation for the pressure forces.

# The origin of the nucleon mass

## Key claim

The paper argues that both the standard trace decomposition and Ji's decomposition of the nucleon mass are mathematically correct but physically misleading because they overlook pressure effects. The author identifies that the proper mass decomposition must separate internal energy from pressure-volume work, and that the nucleon mass is actually shared roughly equally between quarks and gluons, contrary to the common claim that gluons dominate.

## Method

The author critically reexamines the two standard decompositions of the nucleon mass based on the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT). The trace decomposition uses the forward matrix element of the trace anomaly to relate the nucleon mass to gluon and quark contributions. Ji's decomposition separates the EMT into traceless and trace parts, then further into quark, gluon, quark mass, and trace anomaly contributions, evaluated in the nucleon rest frame. The author proposes a new decomposition based on the gravitational form factors A_i(0) and Cbar_i(0) for quarks and gluons, interpreting the averaged EMT components as partial internal energy density and isotropic pressure in a perfect-fluid analogy.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs mechanism generates current quark masses accounting for only about 2% of the nucleon mass, with the remaining 98% coming from relativistic kinetic energy and strong interactions.
- The trace decomposition gives 2M^2 = <P|(beta(g)/2g) G^2|P> + <P|(1+gamma_m) psibar m psi|P>, where the second term is known to give a rather small contribution.
- In the new decomposition, the partial internal energies satisfy U_i = [A_i(0) + Cbar_i(0)] M and the pressure-volume works satisfy W_i = -Cbar_i(0) M, with sum rules M = sum_i U_i and 0 = sum_i W_i.
- The nucleon mass is more or less equally shared between quarks and gluons: U_q ≈ U_g (Lorce 2017, Lorce 2018).

## Context

This work extends and critically revises the trace decomposition approach of Shifman (1978), Roberts (2016), and Krein (2017), which suggested gluons dominate the nucleon mass, and Ji's decomposition (Ji 1994, Ji 1995) which gave a different picture. The author shows that both previous approaches overlooked pressure effects, and that the gluon dominance in the trace decomposition actually indicates large negative gluon pressure balanced by repulsive quark pressure. The new decomposition enables a physically consistent separation of internal energy and pressure forces, with the stability condition expressed as a balance equation for the total pressure forces between quarks and gluons.
