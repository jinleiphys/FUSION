# The Photon Content of the Neutron

> In this work, we complete our CT18qed study with the neutron's photon parton distribution function (PDF), which is essential for the nucleus scattering phenomenology. Two methods, CT18lux and CT18qed, based on the LUXqed formalism and the DGLAP evolution, respectively, to determine the neutron's photon PDF have been presented. Various low-$Q^2$ non-perturbative variations have been carefully examined, which are treated as additional uncertainties on top of those induced by quark and gluon PDFs. The impacts of the momentum sum rule as well as isospin symmetry violation have been explored, and turn out to be negligible. A detailed comparison with other neutron's photon PDF sets has been performed, which shows a great improvement in the precision and a reasonable uncertainty estimation in our results. Finally, two phenomenological implications are demonstrated with photon-initiated processes: neutrino-nucleus $W$-boson production, which is important for the near-future TeV--PeV neutrino observations, and the axion-like particle production at a high-energy muon beam-dump experiment.

## Key claim

This work completes the CT18qed study by providing the neutron's photon parton distribution function (PDF) using both the LUXqed formalism (CT18lux) and DGLAP evolution (CT18qed), with careful treatment of low-\(Q^2\) non-perturbative variations as additional uncertainties; the authors find that momentum sum rule and isospin symmetry violation effects are negligible, contrary to earlier assumptions in CT14qed where the neutron's elastic photon was taken as zero.

## Method

The neutron's photon PDF was determined using two complementary approaches: the CT18lux method applies the LUXqed formalism integrating over the complete \((x,Q^2)\) plane using elastic form factors from Ye et al. (2017) and inelastic structure functions from HERMES GD11-P/D fits, CLAS resonance data, and pQCD calculations; the CT18qed method initializes the photon PDF at a low scale using the LUXqed result and then evolves it to higher scales via DGLAP evolution with proper treatment of the momentum sum rule and isospin symmetry. Various low-\(Q^2\) non-perturbative variations (higher-twist corrections, target-mass corrections, different resonance fits, and alternative low-\(x\) extrapolations) were examined and treated as additional uncertainties on top of those from quark and gluon PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Neutron elastic photon PDF at \(\mu = 1.3\) GeV is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the proton's at small \(x\), scaling as the magnetic moment ratio \(\mu_n/\mu_p \approx -1.913/2.793\) at large \(x\) (\(x \gtrsim 0.7\))
- Neutron elastic photon from Galster parameterization is about 3% larger than with Ye et al. (2017) form factors at low \(x\), mainly due to larger low-scale \(G_M\) when \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.6\) GeV\(^2\)
- MSHT20qed elastic photon is about 30% of the Ye et al. (2017) result at \(\mu = 1.3\) GeV, due to a much lower integration limit
- The matching scale \(Q^2_{\rm PDF}\) varies between 5 and 9 GeV\(^2\), with the difference quantifying the corresponding uncertainty
- The resonance region is defined as \(W^2 < W_{\rm lo}^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\), with the low-\(Q^2\) continuum region for \(W^2 > W_{\rm hi}^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 < Q_{\rm lo}^2\)
- The higher-twist correction parameter for \(F_L\) is \(A_{\rm HT} = 5.5 \pm 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) from the HERA fit

## Context

This work extends the CT18qed proton study (Xie et al., 2021) to include neutron PDFs, building on the LUXqed formalism (Manohar et al., 2016, 2017) and the earlier MRST2004qed (Martin et al., 2004) and CT14qed (Schmidt et al., 2015) neutron PDF determinations. It improves upon the MMHT2015qed (Harland-Lang et al., 2019) and MSHT20qed (Cridge et al., 2021) neutron PDFs by using more precise elastic form factors from Ye et al. (2017) and a more comprehensive treatment of low-\(Q^2\) uncertainties. The results enable more precise predictions for photon-initiated processes in neutrino-nucleus \(W\)-boson production relevant for TeV-PeV neutrino observations and axion-like particle production at high-energy muon beam-dump experiments.
