# Charged-Particle Thermonuclear Reaction Rates: III. Nuclear Physics Input

> The nuclear physics input used to compute the Monte Carlo reaction rates and probability density functions that are tabulated in the second paper of this series (Paper II) is presented. Specifically, we publish the input files to the Monte Carlo reaction rate code RatesMC, which is based on the formalism presented in the first paper of this series (Paper I). This data base contains overwhelmingly experimental nuclear physics information. The survey of literature for this review was concluded in November 2009.

## Key claim

This paper presents the nuclear physics input data used to compute Monte Carlo reaction rates and probability density functions, with the caveat that the uncertainties provided are statistical in nature and do not account for unknown systematic errors in the nuclear input data.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental nuclear physics information from refereed journals (and occasionally conference proceedings and Ph.D. theses) for charged-particle thermonuclear reactions, using weighted averages for multiply-measured quantities and normalizing resonance strengths to a set of carefully measured "standard" resonance strengths where possible. They assigned Gaussian probability density functions to measured resonance energies and lognormal distributions to measured resonance strengths or partial widths, with the input files formatted for the Monte Carlo code RatesMC which implements the formalism from Paper I of the series.

## Key numbers

- For direct capture S-factors, an uncertainty (square root of the variance) of 40% is adopted, based on a systematic comparison of experimental spectroscopic factors from direct capture and transfer reaction studies
- For particle partial widths calculated from measured spectroscopic factors, uncertainties of 40% are assumed; for gamma-ray partial widths, uncertainties of 50% are assumed
- For the Porter-Thomas distribution of dimensionless reduced widths, mean values of ⟨θ²_p⟩ = 0.0045 for protons (or neutrons) and ⟨θ²_α⟩ = 0.010 for alpha-particles are used
- For the ¹⁴C(p,γ)¹⁵N reaction, resonance strengths are listed for five low-energy resonances: 2.9×10⁻⁴ eV (at 242.3 keV), 3.7×10⁻² eV (at 325.9 keV), 3.1×10⁻³ eV (at 242.3 keV), 0.84 eV (at 494.5 keV), and 0.27 eV (at 596.6 keV)
- For the ¹⁴C(α,γ)¹⁸O reaction, the nonresonant S-factor parameters are S(0) = 3.98×10³ keVb, S'(0) = 1.6 b, and S''(0) = 1.64×10⁻³ b/keV with a fractional uncertainty of 1.53

## Context

This work extends the previous charged-particle reaction rate evaluations of Angulo et al. (1999) and Iliadis et al. (2001), incorporating the rapid progress in nuclear astrophysics over the intervening years. The paper provides the reproducible input data for the Monte Carlo reaction rates tabulated in Paper II of the series, enabling future measurements to be motivated by identifying where precise resonance energy measurements are needed, since these uncertainties often produce long tails in reaction rate probability density functions.
