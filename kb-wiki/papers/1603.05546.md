# General considerations on the nature of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ from their pole positions

> The nature of the bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ is studied by calculating the $B^{(*)}\overline B^{*}$ compositeness ($X$) in those resonances. We first consider uncoupled isovector $S$-wave scattering of $B^{(*)}\overline B^{*}$ within the framework of effective-range expansion (ERE). Expressions for the scattering length ($a$) and effective range ($r$) are derived exclusively in terms of the masses and widths of the two $Z_b$ states. We then develop compositeness within ERE for the resonance case and deduce the expression $X=1/\sqrt{2r/a-1}$, which is then applied to the systems of interest. Finally, the actual compositeness parameters are calculated in terms of resonance pole positions and their experimental branching ratios into $B^{(*)}\overline{B}^*$ by using the method of Ref.[1]. We find the values $X=0.66\pm 0.11$ and $0.51\pm 0.10$ for the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$, respectively. We also compare the ERE with Breit-Wigner and Flatté parameterizations to discuss the applicability of the last two ones for near-threshold resonances with explicit examples.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ resonances are dominated by $B^{(*)}\overline{B}^*$ molecular components, with compositeness values of $0.66\pm 0.11$ and $0.51\pm 0.10$ respectively, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the effective-range expansion (ERE) is applicable up to the resonance pole positions despite the nominal three-momentum at the pole being around $m_\pi$, which is beyond the strict radius of convergence of the ERE.

## Method

The authors performed an uncoupled isovector $S$-wave effective-range expansion (ERE) analysis of $B^{(*)}\overline{B}^*$ scattering, fixing the scattering length ($a$) and effective range ($r$) by reproducing the resonance pole positions from the experimental masses and widths of the two $Z_b$ states. They then derived a compositeness formula $X=1/\sqrt{2r/a-1}$ for resonances within the ERE framework and applied it to the $Z_b$ systems. Finally, they used the method of Oller (2015) to calculate compositeness parameters incorporating the experimental branching ratios of the $Z_b$ states into $B^{(*)}\overline{B}^*$ channels as measured by Belle.

## Key numbers

* $Z_b(10610)$: mass $M = 10607.2 \pm 2.0$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 18.4 \pm 2.4$ MeV
* $Z_b(10650)$: mass $M = 10652.2 \pm 1.5$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 11.5 \pm 2.2$ MeV
* $Z_b(10610)$: scattering length $a = -1.03 \pm 0.17$ fm, effective range $r = -1.49 \pm 0.20$ fm
* $Z_b(10650)$: scattering length $a = -1.18 \pm 0.26$ fm, effective range $r = -2.03 \pm 0.38$ fm
* $Z_b(10610)$: compositeness $X = 0.66 \pm 0.11$ (from branching ratio method) and $X = 0.75 \pm 0.15$ (from ERE method)
* $Z_b(10650)$: compositeness $X = 0.51 \pm 0.10$ (from branching ratio method) and $X = 0.67 \pm 0.16$ (from ERE method)
* Experimental branching ratio $Br(Z_b(10610)^+ \to B\overline{B}^*) = (86.0 \pm 3.6)\%$
* Experimental branching ratio $Br(Z_b(10650)^+ \to B^*\overline{B}^*) = (73.4 \pm 3.6)\%$

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $Z_b$ states proposed by Voloshin (2011), Cleven et al. (2011), and others, by providing a model-independent compositeness analysis based solely on scattering theory rather than specific dynamical assumptions. The results are compatible with the effective field theory analysis of Chen et al. (2015), who performed a combined data analysis of $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays and obtained similar compositeness values. The paper enables future studies to distinguish between molecular, tetraquark, and hybrid interpretations of other near-threshold $XYZ$ resonances by applying the same ERE-based compositeness formalism.
