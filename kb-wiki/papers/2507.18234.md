# New superconvergence relations for spin and tensor structure functions of $γγ$ fusion

> The Burkhardt--Cottingham sum rule is an exact superconvergence relation for a spin-structure function, derived from general principles of light absorption and scattering, and valid at any momentum transfer $Q^2$. I illustrate how a class of such relations emerges from the Siegert point, an unphysical kinematical point where both the probe and the target are at rest. From light-by-light scattering, new sum rules for $γ^\ast γ^\ast$ fusion are emerging, valid for arbitrary photon virtualities. Regarding the convergence of these relations, there is a simple argument for the suppression of longitudinal photon polarizations at high energy. Among its consequences is the prediction of $σ_L/ σ_T \to 0$ at high energy, for the ratio of unpolarized nucleon photoabsorption cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper derives a broad class of superconvergence relations for spin and tensor structure functions of photon fusion, including the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule, from the equivalence of transverse and longitudinal photon polarizations at the Siegert point (where the photon is at rest), with the hedge that the convergence of these relations relies on the high-energy suppression of longitudinal photon polarizations due to electromagnetic gauge invariance.

## Method

The author uses dispersion relations for forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitudes on a spin-1/2 target, imposing the condition that transverse and longitudinal amplitudes are equal at the Siegert point (nu^2 = -Q^2). For light-by-light scattering, the method is extended to doubly-virtual gamma*gamma* fusion, where the Siegert point is defined as nu^2_S = Q_1^2 Q_2^2, and the reduction of the amplitude to only three Lorentz structures at that point yields five inter-relations among the eight amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule: integral from 0 to 1 of dx g_2(x, Q^2) = 0, valid at any momentum transfer Q^2
- For the unpolarized case, the sum rule for the subtraction function T_1(0, Q^2) = (2/pi) * integral from nu_el to infinity of d nu * [nu^2/(nu^2+Q^2)] * [(Q^2/nu^2) sigma_T - sigma_L](nu, Q^2)
- The high-energy limit of longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio: sigma_L/sigma_T -> 0 as nu -> infinity for the nucleon
- In leading-order QED, the polarized cross sections satisfy: sigma_LT(nu, Q^2) -> -(Q/nu) sigma_TT(nu, Q^2) as nu -> infinity
- For real photons (one virtuality -> 0), the sum rules become: integral from 0 to 1 of dx g_1^gamma(x, Q^2) = 0 and integral from 0 to 1 of dx g_2^gamma(x, Q^2) = 0

## Context

This work extends the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule (Burkhardt and Cottingham, 1970) by providing a new derivation from the Siegert point (Siegert, 1937) and generalizing it to doubly-virtual gamma*gamma* fusion. The new sum rules for gamma*gamma* fusion at arbitrary virtualities, Eqs. (newSR1) and (newSR2), are the central result and can be used to constrain doubly-virtual meson transition form factors, similar to the single-virtual case studied by Dai et al. (2017) and Danilkin et al. (2016). The approach also reproduces the known sum rule for the photon spin-structure function g_1^gamma (Bass, 1998) and opens the possibility of extending to spin-1 targets (deuteron) where only the Close-Kumano sum rule (Close and Kumano, 1987) is known, and to higher spins such as spin-3/2 for the Delta(1232) isobar.
