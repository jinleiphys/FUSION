# Evidence for the fourth P11 resonance predicted by the constituent quark model

> It is pointed out that the third of five low-lying P11 states predicted by a constituent quark model can be identified with the third of four states in a solution from a three-channel analysis by the Zagreb group. This is one of the so-called ``missing'' resonances, predicted at 1880 MeV. The fit of the Zagreb group to the pi N -> eta N data is the crucial element in finding this fourth resonance in the P11 partial wave.

## Key claim

The third of five low-lying P11 states predicted by a constituent quark model at 1880 MeV can be identified with the third of four states found in a three-channel partial wave analysis by the Zagreb group, but only when the fit includes pi N -> eta N data; this identification is conditional on the four-resonance solution being favored over the three-resonance solution based on comparison with the pi^2 N branching ratio from Manley and Saleski (1992).

## Method

The Zagreb group performed a multi-channel, multi-resonance unitary partial wave analysis of pi N scattering using a three-channel model (pi N, eta N, pi^2 N) with an improved S11 amplitude to constrain fits to pi N -> eta N cross sections near threshold. Two solutions were found: one with three P11 resonances and one with four P11 resonances. The authors compared these solutions to predictions from a constituent quark model using the 3P0 decay model, which predicts five P11 states below 2000 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The third quark model P11 state is predicted at 1880 MeV with a total width of 155 MeV, branching ratios x_pi = 5%, x_eta = 18%, and x_pi^2 = 76%
- The four-resonance Zagreb solution includes a P11 state at 1740(11) MeV with width 140(25) MeV, branching ratios x_pi = 28(34)%, x_eta = 12(9)%, and x_pi^2 = 60(35)%
- The quark model predicts four P11 states below 2000 MeV at 1540, 1770, 1880, and 1975 MeV, with the fifth at 2065 MeV
- The fourth quark model state at 1975 MeV has a predicted width of only 45 MeV and branching ratios x_pi = 8%, x_eta = 0%, and x_pi^2 = 92%
- The PDG lists P11 states at 1440(135) MeV and 1710(120) MeV, with no third or fourth P11 state

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1994) and Copley, Isgur, and Karl (1979) by identifying a "missing" resonance in the P11 partial wave that had not been seen in previous partial wave analyses. The result contradicts the three-resonance solution found in earlier Zagreb analyses and the PDG listing, which lacks this state. The identification enables future experimental searches for the fourth predicted P11 state at 1975 MeV, which would require explicit treatment of individual inelastic channels like pi Delta, and helps distinguish between symmetric quark models and diquark models, which also predict a missing P11 state near this mass.
