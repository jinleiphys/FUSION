# Testing the validity of the surface approximation for reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei with a fully quantum-mechanical model

> We examine the validity of surface approximation for breakup reactions using a fully quantum-mechanical model proposed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (IAV). Analogous to the semi-classical picture, we introduce radial cut-offs to scattering waves in the IAV framework, which we refer to as IAV-cut. Systematic calculations are conducted for nonelastic breakup reactions induced by $^6$Li and deuterons at various incident energies. A comparison between the results obtained from IAV and IAV-cut is performed. The excellent agreement observed between IAV and IAV-cut in $^{6}$Li induced reactions, regardless of incident energy and target nuclei, signifies their insensitivity to the inner part of the scattering wave function, thus providing validation for the semi-classical picture. However, for deuteron induced breakup reactions, the IAV-cut results exhibit a suppression in the cross section, suggesting a strong dependence on the interior wave functions. This suppression is further enhanced as the incident energy increases.

## Key claim

The surface approximation (relying only on the asymptotic part of scattering wave functions) is valid for nonelastic breakup reactions induced by $^6$Li across all tested energies and targets, but fails for deuteron-induced reactions where the cross section is strongly suppressed when the inner part of the wave function is removed, with the suppression increasing at higher incident energies.

## Method

The authors introduced radial cut-offs to the incoming and outgoing distorted waves in the fully quantum-mechanical Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) framework, setting the radial wave functions to zero below a chosen cut-off radius $R_{\mathrm{cut}}$ (typically comparable to the optical potential radius $R_0 = r_0 \times A_T^{1/3}$). They then solved the inhomogeneous differential equation for the $x$-channel wave function using the cut-off scattering waves and computed the nonelastic breakup cross section from the product of the wave function modulus squared and the imaginary part of the participant-target optical potential. Systematic calculations were performed for $^6$Li-induced reactions on $^{208}$Pb and deuteron-induced reactions on $^{208}$Pb at various incident energies.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb($^6$Li, $\alpha X$) at $E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 100$ MeV: the optical potential radius parameter is $R_0 = 9.08$ fm; cut-off radii of 4 fm, 6 fm, and 10 fm produced NEB cross sections that agreed with the full IAV calculation within typical experimental uncertainty, with the 4 fm and 6 fm cases nearly indistinguishable.
- For $^{208}$Pb($d, p X$) at $E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 70$ MeV: the IAV-cut results showed suppression of the NEB cross section compared to the full IAV calculation, with the suppression increasing for larger cut-off radii (4 fm, 6 fm, and 8 fm tested).
- For $^{28}$Si($d, p X$) at $E_d = 30$ MeV with relative outgoing energy $E_p = 10$ MeV: convergence of the Gaussian quadrature method required more than 100 quadrature points, while Simpson's rule required a minimum of 1000 grids for the same convergence.

## Context

This work extends the fully quantum-mechanical IAV model developed by Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985) and later implemented numerically by Potel et al. (2015) and Carlson et al. (2016). It directly tests the semi-classical surface approximation underlying the Hussein-McVoy (1985) model and the transfer-to-continuum model, which assume that nonelastic breakup cross sections depend only on asymptotic properties of scattering waves. The results validate this approximation for $^6$Li-induced reactions but challenge its applicability for deuteron-induced reactions, suggesting that future semi-classical treatments of deuteron breakup may need to account for interior contributions. The IAV-cut method provides a systematic way to test surface approximations without introducing other semi-classical assumptions.
