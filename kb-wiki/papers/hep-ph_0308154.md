# Strangeness Excitation Function in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study as function of energy strangeness created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We consider statistical hadronization with chemical freeze-out in both equilibrium and nonequilibrium. We obtain strangeness per baryon and per entropy in the energy range $8.75\lessim\sqrt{s_{NN}}\lessim 200 A$ GeV. A baryon density independent evaluation of the kaon to pion ratio is presented.

## Key claim

The strangeness per baryon yield in heavy ion collisions is a smooth, continuous function of collision energy from SPS to RHIC, but the strangeness per entropy shows an unexpected 50% increase at RHIC compared to SPS, suggesting a possible new physics threshold that requires data between the two energy ranges to confirm.

## Method

The authors performed statistical hadronization fits to experimental particle yields from SPS (NA49 at 40, 80, and 158 A GeV Pb-Pb) and RHIC (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR at 130 and 200 A GeV Au-Au), allowing for chemical equilibrium, semi-equilibrium (strange quark nonequilibrium), and full nonequilibrium (both light and strange quark phase space occupancies). They extracted freeze-out parameters (temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_b, strangeness chemical potential μ_S, and quark occupancy factors γ_q and γ_s) and used these to compute global fireball properties including strangeness per baryon (s/b), strangeness per entropy (s/S), and the baryon-density-independent kaon-to-pion ratio K/π = √(K⁺K⁻)/(π⁺π⁻).

## Key numbers

- At RHIC-200 (√s_NN = 200 A GeV), each interacting baryon pair produces about 20 strange quark-antiquark pairs.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC-200 is T = 143 ± 7 MeV (nonequilibrium fit) or T = 160 ± 8 MeV (semi-equilibrium fit), with baryochemical potential μ_b = 21.5 ± 1 MeV (nonequilibrium) or μ_b = 24.5 ± 1 MeV (semi-equilibrium).
- At RHIC-130, the nonequilibrium fit gives T = 144 ± 3 MeV, μ_b = 29.2 ± 1.5 MeV, γ_q = 1.6 ± 0.2, and γ_s/γ_q = 1.3 ± 0.1 with χ²/dof = 15.8/24.
- The fraction of energy per baryon pair initially thermalized is 50 ± 5%, independent of collision energy in the range 9 < √s_NN < 200 GeV.
- The K/π ratio at RHIC shows a clear enhancement compared to both the AGS/SPS trend and the K⁺/π⁺ ratio measured in elementary pp collisions.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach of Rafelski and Letessier (2000) and Rafelski (2003) to study strangeness production across a wide energy range, comparing chemical equilibrium and nonequilibrium scenarios. The results quantitatively agree with predictions made assuming a quark-gluon plasma state and gluon fusion strangeness formation, as shown in figure 38 of Acta Physica Polonica (1996). The paper identifies two energy domains for future threshold investigations: the 35+35 GeV RHIC range and the 20 A GeV low SPS energy range where the K/π ratio saturates, and calls for filling the energy gap between SPS and RHIC with new data.
