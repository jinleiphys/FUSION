# Universality of the critical point mapping between Ising model and QCD at small quark mass

> The universality of the QCD equation of state near the critical point is expressed by mapping pressure as a function of temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ$ in QCD to Gibbs free energy as a function of reduced temperature $r$ and magnetic field $h$ in the Ising model. The mapping parameters are, in general, not universal, i.e., determined by details of the microscopic dynamics, rather than by symmetries and long-distance dynamics. In this paper we point out that in the limit of small quark masses, when the critical point is close to the tricritical point, the mapping parameters show universal dependence on the quark mass $m_q$. In particular, the angle between the $r=0$ and $h=0$ lines in the $(μ,T)$ plane vanishes as $m_q^{2/5}$. We discuss possible phenomenological consequences of these findings.

## Key claim

The mapping parameters that connect the QCD equation of state near the critical point to the Ising model Gibbs free energy become universal in the limit of small quark masses, because the critical point lies close to the tricritical point. Specifically, the angle between the \(r=0\) and \(h=0\) lines in the \((\mu,T)\) plane vanishes as \(m_q^{2/5}\), a power that is independent of microscopic details.

## Method

The authors use a Ginzburg-Landau (mean-field) expansion of the thermodynamic potential near the tricritical point, including terms up to \(\Phi^6\) to describe the change from second-order to first-order chiral restoration. They expand the potential around the critical point solution for a given quark mass \(m_q\), eliminate the redundant \(\phi^3\) term by a field redefinition, and read off the mapping parameters \(h\) and \(r\) from the resulting \(\phi^4\) theory. The slopes of the \(h=0\) and \(r=0\) lines are then expressed in terms of derivatives of the coefficients \(a\) and \(b\) of the \(\Phi^2\) and \(\Phi^4\) terms with respect to temperature and chemical potential. The analysis is extended beyond mean-field using epsilon expansion to two-loop order, and illustrated with a random matrix model (Halasz et al., 1998).

## Key numbers

* The difference between the slopes of the \(r=0\) and \(h=0\) lines scales as \(m_q^{2/5}\) (Eq. 52), with the explicit prefactor \(\frac{20}{7 a_T^2} \frac{\partial(a,b)}{\partial(\mu,T)} \left(\frac{3}{8c}\right)^{2/5}\).
* The critical order parameter scales as \(\Phi_c = \left( \frac{3 m_q}{8c} \right)^{1/5}\) (Eq. 42).
* The critical values of the Ginzburg-Landau coefficients scale as \(a_c = 5c \Phi_c^4 \sim m_q^{4/5}\) and \(b_c = -\frac{10c}{3} \Phi_c^2 \sim m_q^{2/5}\) (Eq. 42).
* The mapping parameters \(\rho\) and \(w\) scale as \(\rho \sim m_q^{3/10}\) and \(w \sim m_q^{-1/2}\) (Eq. 56).

## Context

This work extends the revised scaling approach of Rehr and Mermin (1973) and the QCD critical point mapping framework of Parotto et al. (2018) by showing that the non-universal mapping parameters become constrained when the critical point is near the tricritical point, as argued earlier by Stephanov (1998). The result that the slopes of the \(h=0\) and \(r=0\) lines converge as \(m_q^{2/5}\) provides a testable prediction for lattice QCD and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and can be used to narrow the parameter space for hydrodynamic simulations of the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC.
