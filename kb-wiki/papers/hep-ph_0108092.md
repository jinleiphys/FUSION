# Coherent gluon production in very high energy heavy ion collisions

> The early stages of a relativistic heavy-ion collision are examined in the framework of an effective classical SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the transverse plane. We compute the initial energy and number distributions, per unit rapidity, at mid-rapidity, of gluons produced in high energy heavy ion collisions. We discuss the phenomenological implications of our results in light of the recent RHIC data.

## Key claim

The paper provides non-perturbative numerical results for gluon production in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, showing that the energy and number distributions of produced gluons can be expressed as functions of the saturation scale Λ_s, but the comparison to RHIC data requires phenomenological factors (κ_inel for inelastic gluon production and κ_work for hydrodynamic work) that introduce significant uncertainty in extracting Λ_s from experiment.

## Method

The authors solve classical SU(3) Yang-Mills equations on a lattice with initial conditions given by the color glass condensate (CGC) gauge fields of two colliding nuclei. They compute the transverse energy density and gluon transverse momentum distributions in Coulomb gauge, then extrapolate to the continuum limit using linear fits for two values of the dimensionless product Λ_s R (25 and 83.7). The gluon momentum distribution is fitted with a piecewise function: a Bose-Einstein form at low k_T and a perturbative log-corrected power law at high k_T.

## Key numbers

- f_E(25) = 0.537 and f_E(83.7) = 0.497 for the energy density function in the expression dE_T/dη/(πR²) = (1/g²) f_E(Λ_s R) Λ_s³
- Formation time parameter γ = 0.362 ± 0.023 for Λ_s R = 83.7, giving τ_D ≈ 0.3 fm for RHIC (Λ_s = 2 GeV) and ≈ 0.13 fm for LHC (Λ_s = 4 GeV)
- Initial energy density ε = (0.17/g²) Λ_s⁴ at formation time τ_D = 1/γ̄/Λ_s with γ̄ = 0.34
- Gluon number per unit rapidity: f_N(83.7) = 0.3 in the expression dN/dη/(πR²) = (1/g²) f_N(Λ_s R) Λ_s²
- Fit parameters for gluon momentum distribution: a₁ = 0.0295, m = 0.067 Λ_s, T_eff = 0.93 Λ_s, a₂ = 0.0343
- For RHIC at √s_NN = 130 GeV: dN^h/dη ≈ 1000 for central collisions; experimental ratio R^h = dE_T^h/dη / dN^h/dη = 0.5 GeV
- Under no-hydrodynamic-work assumption (κ_work = 1): Λ_s = 1.02 GeV, κ_inel = 3.4
- Under hydrodynamic-work assumption (κ_work ≈ 2): Λ_s = 1.28 GeV, κ_inel = 2.13

## Context

This work extends previous SU(2) numerical simulations by Krasnitz and Venugopalan (2000, 2001) to the physically relevant SU(3) gauge group, enabling direct comparison to RHIC data from the PHOBOS, PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS collaborations. The classical Yang-Mills approach builds on the color glass condensate effective field theory developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the renormalization group methods of Jalilian-Marian, Kovchegov, and Weigert (1997-1999). The results enable extraction of the saturation scale Λ_s from experimental data and provide initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution models of the quark-gluon plasma.
