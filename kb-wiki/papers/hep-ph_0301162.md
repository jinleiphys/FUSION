# Anisotropic Color Superconductivity

> We discuss the possibility that in finite density QCD an anisotropic phase is realized. This case might arise for quarks with different chemical potential and/or different masses. In this phase crystalline structures may be formed. We consider this possibility and we describe, in the context of an effective lagrangian, the corresponding phonons as the Nambu-Goldstone bosons associated to the breaking of the space symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an anisotropic color superconducting phase (the LOFF phase) may be realized in finite-density QCD when quarks have different chemical potentials or different masses, but only within a narrow window of the chemical potential difference, specifically for \(\mu > M_s^2/(2\Delta)\) in the massive-flavor case or for \(\delta\mu\) between approximately \(0.707\Delta_{BCS}\) and \(0.754\Delta_{BCS}\) in the two-flavor case.

## Method

The author reviews the LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) mechanism applied to two-flavor QCD, where quarks with different chemical potentials pair with nonzero total momentum, breaking translational and rotational invariance. A Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the grand potential up to sixth order in the gap is used to evaluate the free energy of various crystalline structures, and the phonon effective Lagrangians are derived by introducing scalar fields as phases of the condensate and expanding to second order in derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The condition for condensation in the one-massive, one-massless flavor case is \(\mu > M_s^2/(2\Delta)\).
- The common Fermi momentum for paired fermions is \(p^F_{\text{comm}} = \mu - M_s^2/(4\mu)\).
- The difference in grand potential between unpaired and paired states is \(\Omega_{\text{unpair.}} - \Omega_{\text{pair.}} \approx (1/16\pi^2)(M_s^4 - 4\Delta^2\mu^2)\).
- For the two-flavor LOFF phase, the BCS-to-normal first-order transition occurs at \(\delta\mu = \Delta_{BCS}/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707\Delta_{BCS}\).
- The second-order LOFF-to-normal transition occurs at \(\delta\mu_2 \approx 0.754\,\Delta_{BCS}\).
- The optimal pair momentum satisfies \(q v_F \approx 1.2\,\delta\mu\).
- Near \(\delta\mu_2\), the LOFF gap squared scales as \(\Delta^2 \approx 1.76\,\delta\mu_2(\delta\mu_2 - \delta\mu)\).
- The LOFF grand potential relative to normal is \(\Omega_{\text{LOFF}} - \Omega_{\text{normal}} \approx -0.44\,\rho(\delta\mu - \delta\mu_2)^2\).
- For the single plane-wave phonon: \(v_\perp^2 = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 - (\delta\mu/|\vec q|)^2\right)\), \(v_\parallel^2 = (\delta\mu/|\vec q|)^2\).
- For the face-centered cube phonon: \(a = 1/12\), \(b = 0\), \(c = \frac{1}{12}\left(3(\delta\mu/|\vec q|)^2 - 1\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the LOFF mechanism, originally developed for ferromagnetic alloys with paramagnetic impurities by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrel (1964), to two-flavor QCD as first calculated by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000). It also builds on the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase for three-flavor QCD (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1998; Schafer, Wilczek 1998) and considers the intermediate-density regime where the strange quark mass becomes relevant. The results enable future study of crystalline color superconducting phases in neutron star interiors, where a LOFF shell could pin CFL-core vortices and explain pulsar glitches, reinforcing the possibility of strange quark stars.
