# Transverse spin sum rule of the proton

> The transversely-polarized state of a proton with arbitrary momentum is not an eigenstate of transverse angular momentum operator. The latter does not commute with the QCD Hamiltonian. However, the expectation value of the transverse angular momentum in the state is well-defined and grows proportionally to the energy of the particle. The transverse spin content of the proton is analyzed in terms of the QCD angular momentum structure. In particular, we reconfirm that the generalized parton distributions $H+E$ provide transverse orbital angular momentum densities of quarks and gluons in the infinite momentum frame.

## Key claim

The expectation value of the transverse angular momentum operator in a transversely-polarized proton state is well-defined and grows proportionally to the particle's energy, leading to a transverse spin sum rule where the quark and gluon contributions are frame-independent and given by (A+B)/2, contrary to earlier controversial results in the literature that included spurious center-of-mass contributions.

## Method

The authors analyze the transverse angular momentum of a proton using the QCD angular momentum operator expressed through the energy-momentum tensor, separating intrinsic spin contributions from center-of-mass motion contributions by discarding terms proportional to the momentum operator. They evaluate the matrix elements of the quark and gluon angular momentum densities in a transversely-polarized proton state using the form factors A, B, C, and \(\bar C\) from the energy-momentum tensor parametrization, and derive a partonic sum rule in the infinite momentum frame using generalized parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic transverse angular momentum expectation value in a moving proton is \(\langle J^x \rangle = \gamma (\hbar/2)\), growing proportionally to the Lorentz boost factor \(\gamma\)
- The quark transverse spin contribution \(\Delta \Sigma_\perp = \gamma^{-2} \Delta \Sigma\) is suppressed by \(1/\gamma^2\) as the proton momentum increases, where \(\Delta \Sigma\) is the singlet axial charge
- The quark and gluon contributions to the transverse spin sum rule are \(J^{q,g}_\perp = (A_{q,g}+B_{q,g})/2\), independent of the proton momentum
- The transverse spin sum rule is \(J^q_\perp + J^g_\perp = \hbar/2\)

## Context

This work extends the helicity sum rule framework of Ji (1996) to the transverse spin case, resolving controversies in prior work by Bakker (2004), Leader (2011), and others who obtained incorrect results by including center-of-mass motion contributions. The analysis enables future experimental measurements of the twist-three contributions to the transverse angular momentum densities and provides a partonic interpretation where generalized parton distributions \(H+E\) give the transverse orbital angular momentum densities of quarks and gluons in the infinite momentum frame.
