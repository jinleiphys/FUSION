# Algebraic nonlinear collective motion

> Finite-dimensional Lie algebras of vector fields determine geometrical collective models in quantum and classical physics. Every set of vector fields on Euclidean space that generates the Lie algebra sl(3, R) and contains the angular momentum algebra so(3) is determined. The subset of divergence-free sl(3, R) vector fields is proven to be indexed by a real number $Λ$. The $Λ=0$ solution is the linear representation that corresponds to the Riemann ellipsoidal model. The nonlinear group action on Euclidean space transforms a certain family of deformed droplets among themselves. For positive $Λ$, the droplets have a neck that becomes more pronounced as $Λ$ increases; for negative $Λ$, the droplets contain a spherical bubble of radius $|Λ|^{1/3}$. The nonlinear vector field algebra is extended to the nonlinear general collective motion algebra gcm(3) which includes the inertia tensor. The quantum algebraic models of nonlinear nuclear collective motion are given by irreducible unitary representations of the nonlinear gcm(3) Lie algebra. These representations model fissioning isotopes ($Λ>0$) and bubble and two-fluid nuclei ($Λ<0$).

# Algebraic Nonlinear Collective Motion

## Key claim

The paper proves that all divergence-free sl(3,R) vector fields on Euclidean space that contain the angular momentum algebra so(3) are indexed by a single real number Λ, with the linear representation (Riemann ellipsoidal model) corresponding to Λ=0; the nonlinear vector fields model fissioning isotopes when Λ>0 and bubble or two-fluid nuclei when Λ<0, but the nonlinear representation for Λ>0 may only form one-parameter semigroups rather than full groups because integral curves can run into an excluded sphere.

## Method

The authors classify all smooth vector fields on R³ that satisfy the sl(3,R) commutation relations and contain the standard so(3) angular momentum subalgebra, using spherical coordinates and imposing the highest weight condition [L₊, T₊₂]=0 and the commutation relation [T₊₂, T₋₂]=-4L₀. They then restrict to divergence-free (incompressible) vector fields, obtaining A(r)=r+Λ/r², and show that the nonlinear vector fields are obtained by applying a nonlinear coordinate transformation (r' = ∛(r³+Λ), θ'=θ, φ'=φ) to the linear representation, mapping between charts on the manifold.

## Key numbers

- The divergence-free sl(3,R) vector fields are indexed by a real number Λ, where A(r)=r+Λ/r² and Λ has dimensions of volume.
- For Λ>0, the droplets develop a neck that becomes more pronounced as Λ increases; the chart excludes a sphere of radius R=∛Λ.
- For Λ<0, the droplets contain a spherical bubble of radius |Λ|^(1/3); the nonlinear representation is defined on the region exterior to the sphere of radius ∛|Λ|.
- The Λ=0 solution recovers the linear representation corresponding to the Riemann ellipsoidal model.

## Context

This work extends the linear geometrical collective models based on GL(3,R) and SL(3,R) (Bohr-Mottelson model, Riemann ellipsoidal model of Chandrasekhar (1969)) to include nonlinear velocity fields, which are necessary for describing fissioning isotopes with a neck or exotic bubble shapes. The paper determines all possible nonlinear sl(3,R) vector fields preserving so(3) and constructs the nonlinear gcm(3) algebra, enabling quantum algebraic models of nonlinear nuclear collective motion via irreducible unitary representations of the GCM(3) Lie group.
