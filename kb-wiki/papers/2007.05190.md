# $QQ\bar{s}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks in the chiral quark model

> The low-lying $S$-wave $QQ\bar{s}\bar{s}$ ($Q=c, b$) tetraquark states with $IJ^P=00^+$, $01^+$ and $02^+$ are systematically investigated in the framework of complex scaling range of chiral quark model. Every structure including meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type configurations, and all possible color channels in four-body sector are considered by means of a commonly extended variational approach, Gaussian expansion method. Several narrow and wide resonance states are obtained for $cc\bar{s}\bar{s}$ and $bb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks with $IJ^P=00^+$ and $02^+$. Meanwhile, narrow resonances for $cb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks are also found in $IJ^P=00^+$, $01^+$ and $02^+$ states. These results confirm the possibility of finding hadronic molecules with masses $\sim\,0.6\,\text{GeV}$ above the noninteracting hadron-hadron thresholds.

## Key claim
The chiral quark model with complex scaling finds several narrow and wide resonance states for $cc\bar{s}\bar{s}$ and $bb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks with $IJ^P=00^+$ and $02^+$, and narrow resonances for $cb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ tetraquarks in $IJ^P=00^+$, $01^+$ and $02^+$ states, confirming the possibility of hadronic molecules with masses approximately 0.6 GeV above noninteracting hadron-hadron thresholds.

## Method
The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation for four-body systems using the chiral quark model, which includes color-confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange potentials. They employed the Gaussian expansion method within a Rayleigh-Ritz variational approach, considering meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type configurations with all possible color channels for $QQ\bar{s}\bar{s}$ ($Q=c,b$) tetraquarks in the $S$-wave, isoscalar sector with $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters: strange quark mass $m_s = 555$ MeV, charm quark mass $m_c = 1752$ MeV, bottom quark mass $m_b = 5100$ MeV
- Theoretical mass of $D_s^+$ (1S): 1989 MeV (experimental: 1969 MeV); $D_s^{*+}$ (1S): 2116 MeV (experimental: 2112 MeV)
- Theoretical mass of $\bar{B}_s^0$ (1S): 5355 MeV (experimental: 5367 MeV); $\bar{B}_s^*$ (1S): 5400 MeV (experimental: 5415 MeV)
- Theoretical mean-square radius of $D_s^+$ (1S): 0.47 fm; $D_s^{*+}$ (1S): 0.55 fm
- Theoretical mean-square radius of $\bar{B}_s^0$ (1S): 0.47 fm; $\bar{B}_s^*$ (1S): 0.50 fm
- Confinement potential parameters: $a_c = 430$ MeV, $\mu_c = 0.70$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Delta = 181.10$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies on double-heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ ($q=u,d$) reported in Yang, Ping, and Segovia (2020) and on double-charm pentaquarks in Yang and Ping (2020), using the same chiral quark model that successfully described the $P_c^+$ pentaquark states in Yang, Ping, and Segovia (2015). The results enable future experimental searches for these tetraquark states at facilities such as LHCb, and provide predictions for the $QQ\bar{s}\bar{s}$ sector that complement existing lattice QCD computations and QCD sum rule studies of double-heavy tetraquarks.
