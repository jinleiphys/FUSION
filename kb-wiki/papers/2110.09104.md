# The cross-section measurement for the $^3{\textrm H}(e,e'K^+)nnΛ$ reaction

> The small binding energy of the hypertrition leads to predictions of non-existence of bound hypernuclei for isotriplet three-body systems such as $nnΛ$. However, invariant mass spectroscopy at GSI has reported events that may be interpreted as the bound $nnΛ$ state. The $nnΛ$ state was sought by missing-mass spectroscopy via the $(e,e'K^+)$ reaction at Jefferson Lab's experimental Hall A. The present experiment has higher sensitivity to the $nnΛ$-state investigation in terms of better precision by a factor of about three. The analysis shown in this article focuses on the derivation of the reaction cross-section for the $^3{\rm{H}}(γ^{*},K^+)\textrm{X}$ reaction. Events that were detected in an acceptance, where a Monte Carlo simulation could reproduce the data well ($|δp/p| < 4\%$), were analyzed to minimize the systematic uncertainty. No significant structures were observed with the acceptance cuts, and the upper limits of the production cross-section of the $nnΛ$ state were obtained to be $21$ and $31~\rm{nb/sr}$ at the $90\%$ confidence level when theoretical predictions of $(-B_Λ, Γ) = (0.25,0.8)$ and $(0.55, 4.7)$ MeV, respectively, were assumed. The cross-section result provides valuable information for examining the existence of $nnΛ$.

## Key claim

The experiment searched for the bound $nn\Lambda$ state via missing-mass spectroscopy of the $^3{\rm H}(e,e'K^+)X$ reaction and found no significant structures, setting upper limits on the production cross-section under specific assumptions about the binding energy and width of the state, rather than claiming detection or non-existence outright.

## Method

The experiment used 4.32 GeV/c electron beams from CEBAF at Jefferson Lab impinged on a tritium gas target, with scattered electrons detected in HRS-L and produced $K^+$ mesons detected in HRS-R, both at 13.2 degrees scattering angle. Missing-mass reconstruction was performed using the measured momenta and angles of $e'$ and $K^+$, with event selection restricted to an acceptance region where Monte Carlo simulation could reproduce data well ($|\delta p/p| < 4\%$) to minimize systematic uncertainty. The analysis derived the reaction cross-section for the $^3{\rm H}(\gamma^{*},K^+)X$ reaction and set upper limits on $nn\Lambda$ production.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit of $nn\Lambda$ production cross-section: 21 nb/sr at 90% confidence level, assuming $(-B_\Lambda, \Gamma) = (0.25, 0.8)$ MeV
- Upper limit of $nn\Lambda$ production cross-section: 31 nb/sr at 90% confidence level, assuming $(-B_\Lambda, \Gamma) = (0.55, 4.7)$ MeV
- Beam energy: 4.32 GeV/c with energy uncertainty $\Delta E/E \lesssim 1\times10^{-4}$ in FWHM
- Missing-mass resolution after calibration: $\sigma = 1.3\pm0.1$ MeV/$c^2$ from Gaussian fit over $|{\rm M}_X - {\rm M}_{\Lambda}|<2$ MeV/$c^2$
- Total beam charge on tritium target: 16.9 C; on hydrogen target: 4.7 C
- Tritium target areal density: $84.8\pm0.8$ mg/cm$^2$; hydrogen target areal density: $70.8\pm0.4$ mg/cm$^2$

## Context

This work extends the search for the $nn\Lambda$ state following invariant mass spectroscopy at GSI by the HypHI Collaboration (Rappold 2013), which reported events possibly interpreted as a bound $nn\Lambda$ state with binding energy $-B_\Lambda=0.5\pm1.1\pm2.2$ MeV and width $\Gamma=5.4\pm1.4$ MeV. The present experiment provides higher sensitivity by a factor of about three in precision compared to that previous work. The results inform theoretical calculations that have been unable to reproduce such a bound state using methods like Faddeev calculations (Miyagawa 1995, Afnan 2015, Filikhin 2016, Gal 2014, Kamada 2016, Garcilazo 2014) and variational methods (Hiyama 2014), while some predictions suggest a resonant state may exist with specific interaction strengths.
