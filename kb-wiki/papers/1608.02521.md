# Correlations in Double Parton Distributions: Perturbative and Non-Perturbative effects

> The correct description of Double Parton Scattering (DPS), which represents a background in several channels for the search of new Physics at the LHC, requires the knowledge of double parton distribution functions (dPDFs). These quantities represent also a novel tool for the study of the three-dimensional nucleon structure, complementary to the possibilities offered by electromagnetic probes. In this paper we analyze dPDFs using Poincaré covariant predictions obtained by using a Light-Front constituent quark model proposed in a recent paper, and QCD evolution. We study to what extent factorized expressions for dPDFs, which neglect, at least in part, two-parton correlations, can be used. We show that they fail in reproducing the calculated dPDFs, in particular in the valence region. Actually measurable processes at existing facilities occur at low longitudinal momenta of the interacting partons; to have contact with these processes we have analyzed correlations between pairs of partons of different kind, finding that, in some cases, they are strongly suppressed at low longitudinal momenta, while for other distributions they can be sizeable. For example, the effect of gluon-gluon correlations can be as large as 20 $\%$. We have shown that these behaviors can be understood in terms of a delicate interference of non-perturbative correlations, generated by the dynamics of the model, and perturbative ones, generated by the model independent evolution procedure. Our analysis shows that at LHC kinematics two-parton correlations can be relevant in DPS, and therefore we address the possibility to study them experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper shows that factorized expressions for double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), which neglect two-parton correlations, fail to reproduce the calculated dPDFs especially in the valence region; however, at low longitudinal momenta relevant for LHC kinematics, some correlations (e.g., gluon-gluon) can be as large as 20%, while others are strongly suppressed, and this behavior arises from a delicate interference between non-perturbative correlations from the model dynamics and perturbative correlations from QCD evolution.

## Method

The authors calculate dPDFs using a Poincaré covariant Light-Front constituent quark model with an Hypercentral potential, restricted to the valence Fock component at a low hadronic scale (μ₀² ≈ 0.1 GeV²). They then apply QCD evolution to higher scales (up to 10⁴ GeV²) and compare the full dPDFs with factorized approximations, including the Diehl-Kasemets-Keane (DKK) Gaussian ansatz and a GPD-based factorization involving H and E generalized parton distributions. Sea quarks and gluons are introduced at the low scale via a perturbative splitting mechanism to enable studies of correlations between different parton species.

## Key numbers

- The effect of gluon-gluon correlations can be as large as 20%.
- The hadronic scale of the model is μ₀² ≈ 0.1 GeV².
- The starting scale for the DKK factorization comparison is Q₀² = 2 GeV², with evolution studied up to Q² = 10⁴ GeV².
- The dPDF normalization satisfies ∫ dx₁ dx₂ u_Vu_V(x₁,x₂,k⊥=0,μ₀²) = 2.
- The GPD H^{u_V} normalization satisfies ∫ dx H^{u_V}(x,ξ=0,-k⊥²) = 2.

## Context

This work extends the Light-Front constituent quark model approach to dPDFs previously developed by the same group (Rinaldi et al., 2014) and compares with the factorization scheme proposed by Diehl, Kasemets, and Keane (2014) and the GPD-based approximation of Diehl (2011). The analysis enables assessment of whether two-parton correlations survive QCD evolution to LHC energies and addresses the possibility of experimentally studying these correlations in DPS processes at the LHC.
