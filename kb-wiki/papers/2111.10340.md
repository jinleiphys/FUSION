# Chromoelectric and chromomagnetic correlators at high temperature from gradient flow

> The heavy quark diffusion coefficient is encoded in the spectral functions of the chromoelectric and the chromomagnetic correlators that are calculable on the lattice. We study the chromoelectric and the chromomagnetic correlator in the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory using Symanzik flow at two temperatures $1.5T_c$ and $10000 T_c$, with $T_c$ being the phase transition temperature. To control the lattice discretization errors and perform the continuum limit we use several temporal lattice extents $N_t=16,20,24$ and 28. We observe that the flow time dependence of the chromomagnetic correlator is quite different from chromoelectric correlator most likely due to the anomalous dimension of the former as has been pointed out recently in the literature.

## Key claim

The gradient flow method can be used to non-perturbatively renormalize and reduce noise in lattice calculations of both the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic correlators in quenched SU(3) gauge theory, but the zero flow time limit can only be taken for the chromoelectric correlator; the chromomagnetic correlator shows a qualitatively different flow time dependence that prevents this extrapolation, likely due to its anomalous dimension as pointed out by Laine (2021).

## Method

The authors performed lattice calculations of the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic correlators in quenched SU(3) gauge theory at two temperatures, using the Symanzik gradient flow for noise reduction and non-perturbative renormalization. They generated ensembles with the Wilson gauge action using heatbath and overrelaxation algorithms, with temporal lattice extents Nt = 16, 20, 24, and 28 to control discretization errors, and performed continuum extrapolations by interpolating data in τT with cubic splines and linearly extrapolating over 1/Nt^2 using the Nt = 20, 24, 28 lattices. The zero flow time limit for the chromoelectric correlator was obtained by linear extrapolation in τF T^2 over a restricted flow time range where the perturbative flowed correlator deviates less than 1% from the unflowed correlator.

## Key numbers

- Two temperatures studied: 1.5 Tc and 10000 Tc, with Tc the critical temperature of the SU(3) gauge theory.
- Lattice parameters: spatial extents Ns = 48 or 56, temporal extents Nt = 16, 20, 24, 28; β values range from 6.872 to 7.321 for T = 1.5 Tc and from 14.443 to 14.925 for T = 10000 Tc.
- Number of configurations: 990 to 4200 per ensemble for T = 1.5 Tc; 990 to 1950 per ensemble for T = 10000 Tc.
- Flow time range used for zero flow time extrapolation: a ≤ √(8τF) ≤ (τ - a)/3, where a is the lattice spacing and τ the temporal separation.
- The chromoelectric correlator results at T = 1.5 Tc agree with previous gradient flow results from Altenkort (2020) and multilevel results from Brambilla (2020).
- The chromomagnetic correlator is approximately flow time independent for τT ≥ 0.25 at T = 1.5 Tc and for τT ≥ 0.3 at T = 10000 Tc.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of the chromoelectric correlator for heavy quark diffusion by Meyer (2010), Francis (2011, 2015), Banerjee (2011), and Brambilla (2020), which used multilevel algorithms for noise reduction, by instead applying the gradient flow method as first demonstrated for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient at 1.5 Tc by Altenkort (2020). The study also addresses the chromomagnetic correlator, which encodes 1/M corrections to heavy quark diffusion as formulated by Bouttefeux (2020), and confronts the issue of its anomalous dimension raised by Laine (2021). The results enable future extraction of the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ from the spectral functions of these correlators, with the chromoelectric correlator contributing at leading order and the chromomagnetic correlator at first order in T/M.
