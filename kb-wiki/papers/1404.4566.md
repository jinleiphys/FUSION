# Di-jet asymmetry and wave turbulence

> We describe a new physical picture for the fragmentation of an energetic jet propagating through a dense QCD medium, which emerges from perturbative QCD and has the potential to explain the di-jet asymmetry observed in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The central ingredient in this picture is the phenomenon of wave turbulence, which provides a very efficient mechanism for the transport of energy towards the medium, via many soft particles which propagate at large angles with respect to the jet axis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the di-jet asymmetry observed in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC can be explained by wave turbulence, a mechanism within perturbative QCD that efficiently transports jet energy to large angles via multiple, quasi-democratic branchings of soft gluons, but this explanation depends on the condition that the soft gluon energies fall below a threshold (ω ≲ ᾱ²ωc) where multiple branchings become certain and deterministic.

## Method

The author reformulates the in-medium jet evolution as a classical stochastic branching process, using the BDMPSZ medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum as the branching rate. This allows treatment of successive gluon splittings as independent events due to the loss of color coherence from medium rescattering. The energy distribution of the gluon cascade is then studied analytically via a rate equation in 1+1 dimensions (energy fraction x and evolution time τ), which reveals a fixed-point spectrum D(x) ∝ 1/√x characteristic of wave turbulence.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy for the BDMPSZ mechanism is ωc = q̂L²/2 ≈ 40 GeV, using q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm and L = 4 fm.
- The critical emission angle is θc ≈ 0.05 for the same parameters.
- The total energy loss by the leading particle is ΔEtot ∼ ᾱωc ∼ ᾱq̂L².
- The energy transferred to the medium via the turbulent cascade is ΔEth ≈ υ ᾱ² ωc, with υ ≈ 4.96, yielding ΔEth ≈ 20 GeV for ωc = 40 GeV.
- The flow energy fraction after evolution time τ is ℰflow(τ) = 1 − e^{−πτ²}.
- For a jet with E = 100 GeV ≈ 2ωc, the evolution time is τ ≈ ᾱ ≈ 0.3.
- The non-perturbative energy scale below which the turbulent cascade develops is ω ∼ ᾱ²ωc ≈ 4 GeV.
- The lower limit on the emission angle for gluons at this scale is θ ≈ 0.5; for typical thermal gluons with ω ∼ 1–2 GeV, the angle is θ ∼ O(1), consistent with CMS data showing most missing energy at θ ≳ 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPSZ mechanism for medium-induced gluon radiation (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) by incorporating multiple gluon branchings, which were previously neglected in studies focusing on hard emissions at small angles. The author builds on recent reformulations of in-medium jet evolution as a classical stochastic process (Blaizot 2012) and analytic studies of energy flow (Blaizot 2013). The wave turbulence picture provides a natural explanation for the di-jet asymmetry observed by CMS (Chatrchyan 2011) and ATLAS (Aad 2012), where most missing energy is deposited at very large angles (θ > 0.8), and enables future Monte Carlo simulations of full 3+1 dimensional jet evolution in medium.
