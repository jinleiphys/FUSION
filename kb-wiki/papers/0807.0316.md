# Baryon Physics in Holographic QCD

> In a simple holographic model for QCD in which the Chern-Simons term is incorporated to take into account the QCD chiral anomaly, we show that baryons arise as stable solitons which are the 5D analogs of 4D skyrmions. Contrary to 4D skyrmions and previously considered holographic scenarios, these solitons have sizes larger than the inverse cut-off of the model, and therefore they are predictable within our effective field theory approach. We perform a numerical determination of several static properties of the nucleons and find a satisfactory agreement with data. We also calculate the amplitudes of ``anomalous'' processes induced by the Chern-Simons term in the meson sector, such as omega -> pi gamma and omega -> 3pi. A combined fit to baryonic and mesonic observables leads to an agreement with experiments within 16%.

## Key claim

In a simple holographic model for QCD that incorporates the Chern-Simons term to account for the QCD chiral anomaly, baryons arise as stable 5D solitons (analogs of 4D skyrmions) whose sizes are larger than the inverse cut-off of the model, making them predictable within the effective field theory approach, contrary to 4D skyrmions and previously considered holographic scenarios where the soliton size is of order the cut-off.

## Method

The authors construct a 5D U(2)_L × U(2)_R gauge theory in an AdS_5 background with a Chern-Simons term fixed by the QCD anomaly coefficient N_c=3. They find static soliton solutions with unit topological charge (baryon number) by solving the coupled equations of motion numerically using the COMSOL 3.4 package, reducing the problem to five real functions of two variables (r and z) via cylindrical symmetry. The soliton is then quantized by introducing time-dependent collective coordinates associated with SU(2)_V rotations, following the standard rigid-rotor quantization procedure used for 4D skyrmions.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M_N = 1140 MeV (experimental: 940 MeV)
- Root-mean-square isoscalar electric charge radius sqrt(⟨r²_{E,S}⟩) = 0.94 fm (experimental: 0.79 fm)
- Axial coupling g_A = 1.0 (experimental: 1.25)
- Isovector magnetic moment difference μ_p − μ_n = 2μ_V = 3.9 (experimental: 4.7)
- Combined fit to baryonic and mesonic observables agrees with experiments within 16%
- Input parameters: F_π = 87 MeV, m_ρ = 775 MeV, F_ω/F_ρ = 0.88

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to baryons beyond earlier studies such as the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Hata 2007, Hong 2006), where baryon sizes were found to be of order the string scale and thus incalculable. It also improves upon truncated resonance models (Nawa 2006) and the authors' own previous work (Pomarol 2007) which lacked the Chern-Simons term and did not provide a good description of baryons. The approach enables systematic calculation of baryon static properties and anomalous meson processes (ω → πγ, ω → 3π) within a consistent effective field theory framework, with the expansion parameter 1/(ρΛ_5) ensuring calculability.
