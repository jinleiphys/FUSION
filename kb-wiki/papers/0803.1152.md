# Non-factorized calculation of the process 3He(e,e'p)2H at medium energies

> The exclusive process 3He(e,e'p)2H has been analyzed by a non-factorized and parameter-free approach based upon realistic few-body wave functions corresponding to the $AV18$ interaction and treating the rescattering of the struck nucleon within a generalized eikonal approximation. The results of our calculations, compared with recent JLab experimental data, show that the left-right asymmetry exhibits a clear dependence upon the final state interaction demonstrating the breaking down of the factorization approximation at "negative" and large (> 300 MeV/c) values of the missing momentum.

## Key claim

The factorization approximation (FA) breaks down for the exclusive process 3He(e,e'p)2H at "negative" missing momenta (φ=0) with magnitude greater than approximately 300 MeV/c, as demonstrated by a non-factorized, parameter-free calculation using realistic wave functions and a generalized eikonal approximation for final state interactions (FSI).

## Method

The authors performed a non-factorized calculation of the 3He(e,e'p)2H cross section in momentum space, using realistic three-body wave functions from a variational solution of the Schrödinger equation with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction. Final state interactions of the struck nucleon with the spectator pair were treated within a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), which includes single and double rescattering contributions and accounts for a parallel momentum shift of nuclear origin. The nuclear current operator was taken in the "CC1" form of de Forest, and the elastic NN amplitude was fixed using experimental data for the total cross section, slope parameter, and ratio of real to imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

- Factorization approximation breaks down at missing momentum magnitudes |p_m| ≳ 300 MeV/c for φ=0 (negative missing momentum).
- PWIA and FSI results practically coincide up to |p_m| ∼ 400 MeV/c, and both agree fairly well with experimental data in that region.
- At |p_m| ≳ 400 MeV/c, PWIA appreciably underestimates experimental data, while including FSI strongly reduces the disagreement.
- Quantitative disagreement persists around |p_m| ≃ 0.6–0.65 GeV/c at φ=0.
- At p_m ≤ 250 MeV/c, the PWIA result for the left-right asymmetry A_TL is in reasonable agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same authors (Ciofi degli Atti and Kaptari, 2005) that only considered positive missing momentum (φ=π) and used the factorization approximation. It contrasts with the factorized approach, which fails at large negative missing momenta, and complements non-factorized calculations by Laget (2005) and Schiavilla (2005) that used configuration-space methods with different non-relativistic reductions of the current operator. The results enable future investigations into the origin of remaining discrepancies at high missing momentum, which are not resolved by including meson exchange currents as considered in the JLab experimental papers and by Schiavilla (2005).
