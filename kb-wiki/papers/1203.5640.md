# The Thermal Model at the Large Hadron Collider

> A discussion is presented of results with identified particles at the Large Hadron Collider. Possible deviations from the standard statistical distributions are investigated by considering in detail results obtained using the Tsallis distribution. Matter-antimatter production is discussed within the framework of chemical equilibrium in \pp and heavy ion collisions.

# The Thermal Model at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim
The thermal model provides a consistent description of identified particle production in both heavy ion and proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, but the Tsallis distribution (with parameter q between 1.1 and 1.2) better describes transverse momentum spectra than the standard Boltzmann distribution, and the baryon chemical potential in pp collisions is systematically lower than in heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The author fits transverse momentum distributions of identified hadrons measured by the ALICE and CMS collaborations using a thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution (Eq. 1, labeled Tsallis-B) and compares it to the form used by the experimental collaborations (Eq. 2). The chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential are extracted from particle yields using the THERMUS model, and the resulting parameters are used to predict yields of light nuclei, antinuclei, hypernuclei, and antihypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- Hagedorn temperature from hadronic resonance counting: T_H = 174 ± 11 MeV
- Observed chemical freeze-out temperature at LHC heavy ion collisions: T ≈ 0.160 GeV
- Tsallis q parameter for pions: q = 1.154 ± 0.036 (π⁺) and q = 1.146 ± 0.036 (π⁻) from ALICE pp data at √s = 2760 AGeV
- Tsallis temperature for pions: T = 0.0682 ± 0.0026 GeV (π⁺) and T = 0.0704 ± 0.0027 GeV (π⁻)
- Tsallis q parameter for protons: q = 1.107 ± 0.147 (p) and q = 1.106 ± 0.158 (p̄)
- Tsallis q parameter for kaons: q = 1.158 ± 0.142 (K⁺) and q = 1.157 ± 0.139 (K⁻)
- Tsallis q parameter for Λ baryons: q = 1.114 ± 0.047
- All fitted q values lie consistently between 1.1 and 1.2 across hadron species

## Context
This work extends the thermal model approach previously applied at lower energies by Hagedorn (1965) and by Becattini (2000s) and Stachel (2000s) to LHC energies. The Tsallis distribution treatment follows work by Tsallis (1988, 1998) and the thermodynamically consistent formulation by Cleymans and Worku (2012). The antimatter production analysis builds on measurements by NA49 (Alt, 2005; Alt, 2007) and STAR (Abelev, 2006) and uses the THERMUS model parameterizations from Wheaton and Cleymans (2009). The results enable predictions for antihypernuclei yields at LHC energies and provide insight into the mechanism of the unexpectedly large antihypertriton production observed by STAR (Abelev, 2010) at RHIC.
