# Speed of sound exceeding the conformal bound in dense QCD-like theories

> We investigated the phase structure and the equation of state (EoS) for dense two-color QCD at low temperatures using the lattice Monte Carlo simulations. A rich phase structure below the pseudo-critical temperature $T_c$ as a function of quark chemical potential has been revealed. In a high-density regime, we can see a superfluid phase, where the diquark condensate takes a non-zero expectation value. We have newly found that the speed of sound exceeds the conformal bound, which is the value of the relativistic free theory. This talk is based on Refs.~\cite{Iida:2022hyy, Iida:2024irv, Itou:2025vcy}.

## Key claim

The authors find that the speed of sound in dense two-color QCD exceeds the conformal bound of \(c_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) at low temperatures, based on lattice Monte Carlo simulations that are free from the sign problem. This result is presented as a controlled first-principles indication that dense QCD-like theories can realize a sound velocity larger than the conformal value, but the authors caution that implications for neutron star physics must be considered with appropriate caution.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors at low temperatures (40 MeV and 80 MeV) and finite quark chemical potential, using a diquark source term to access the superfluid phase. They computed the equation of state (pressure and energy density) from the lattice data and evaluated the speed of sound numerically as \(c_s^2/c^2 = \Delta p / \Delta e\) using a finite-difference method. The results were compared to predictions from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) in the BEC phase and to weak-coupling expectations in the BCS phase.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared \(c_s^2/c^2\) exceeds the conformal bound of \(1/3\) in the high-density regime of two-color QCD at \(T = 40\) MeV and \(T = 80\) MeV.
- The pion decay constant \(F\) was extracted from fits to the equation of state: \(F = 51.1(5)\) MeV from fits of \(p/\mu_c^4\) and \(F = 56.7(7)\) MeV from fits of \(e/\mu_c^4\).
- The superfluid onset occurs at \(\mu_c \approx m_{\rm PS}/2\), where \(m_{\rm PS}\) is the pseudo-scalar meson mass, and the superfluid phase persists up to temperatures as high as approximately 100 MeV.
- The earlier result for \(F\) from an independent simulation using rooted staggered fermions was \(F = 60.8(1.6)\) MeV (Astrakhantsev 2020).

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of two-color QCD at finite density, building on prior simulations by the same group (Iida 2022, Iida 2024, Itou 2025) and independent lattice groups. The finding that the speed of sound exceeds the conformal bound contradicts the behavior observed in lattice QCD at zero chemical potential over roughly forty years, where \(c_s^2/c^2 \leq 1/3\) was consistently satisfied. Similar results have recently been reported in lattice studies of three-color QCD with isospin chemical potential (Brandt 2022, Abbott 2024) and another two-color QCD study (Hands 2024). The results enable future work combining lattice constraints with effective model building (Fujimoto 2024, Fukushima 2024, Pasqualotto 2025, Lopes 2025) to explore the intermediate-density regime of real QCD and its implications for neutron star physics.
