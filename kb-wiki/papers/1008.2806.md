# Hard Photodisintegration of 3He

> Large angle photodisintegration of two nucleons from the 3He nucleus is studied within the framework of the hard rescattering model (HRM). In the HRM the incoming photon is absorbed by one nucleon's valence quark that then undergoes a hard rescattering reaction with a valence quark from the second nucleon producing two nucleons emerging at large transverse momentum . Parameter free cross sections for pp and pn break up channels are calculated through the input of experimental cross sections on pp and pn elastic scattering. The calculated cross section for pp breakup and its predicted energy dependency are in good agreement with recent experimental data. Predictions on spectator momentum distributions and helicity transfer are also presented.

## Key claim

The hard rescattering model (HRM) provides parameter-free cross section calculations for both pp and pn breakup channels in 3He photodisintegration, with the pp breakup cross section showing good agreement with recent experimental data, but only under specific assumptions about the helicity amplitudes of the corresponding NN elastic scattering reactions.

## Method

The HRM amplitude is constructed from a Feynman diagram where the incoming photon is absorbed by a valence quark in one nucleon, which then undergoes a hard rescattering with a valence quark from the second nucleon, with both quarks recombining with spectator systems to form two high-transverse-momentum nucleons. The scattering amplitude is reduced to a convolution of the 3He nuclear wave function and the scattering amplitude of a kinematically corresponding NN elastic scattering reaction, with the cross section computed using experimental cross sections for pp and pn elastic scattering as input. For pp breakup, the calculation additionally assumes that S-state dominance and Pauli principle effects suppress the S12 spectral function and enforce φ4 ≈ −φ3, leading to a predicted suppression of the pp breakup cross section relative to pn breakup.

## Key numbers

- The HRM predicts the energy dependence dσ/dt ∼ s⁻¹¹ for the breakup cross section at large s and -t, consistent with the constituent counting rule.
- For pp breakup, the cross section is proportional to β²/(1+2C²) times the pp elastic scattering cross section, where C² = (φ3²/φ1²) ≈ (φ4²/φ1²) and β = (|φ3| - |φ4|)/|φ1|.
- For pn breakup, the charge factor QF = 1/3; for pp breakup, QF = 5/3.
- The helicity transfer asymmetry Cz' is predicted to be approximately 0 for pp breakup and approximately 2/3 for pn breakup.

## Context

This work extends the hard rescattering model, previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration by Frankfurt (1999), to the breakup of NN pairs in 3He, as proposed by Brodsky (2003). The HRM predictions are compared with competing approaches including the reduced nuclear amplitude (RNA) approach by Brodsky (1983) and the quark gluon string (QGS) model by Grishina (2001), which both require normalization to data, unlike the parameter-free HRM calculation. Recent experimental data by Pomerantz (2009) shows better agreement with HRM calculations than with the other models, and the predicted spectator momentum distributions and helicity transfer asymmetries are expected to be tested in future experiments.
