# Baryons and Pentaquarks in terms of Mesons

> We investigate light meson bound state contributions to six quark Green's functions and establish that the latter has poles corresponding to baryon bound states. We estimate light baryon masses including the Roper resonance. Constituent quark model and the Parton model are natural consequences of this quantum field theoretic picture. We elaborate this analysis to pentaquarks and heptaquarks. An almost model independent prediction for the mass of $θ$ pentaquark is $1.57Gev$.

# Baryons and Pentaquarks in terms of Mesons

## Key claim
The six-quark Green's function in QCD has poles corresponding to baryon bound states, arising from meson exchange contributions; the mass prediction for the $\theta$ pentaquark is 1.57 GeV, but this result depends on the assumption that the intermediate mesons are stable and that the baryon mass formula $M_B = \sum (M_i + \delta_i)$ holds with the meson size parameter $\delta_i$ inferred from the pion.

## Method
The authors analyze the six-quark Green's function $\langle qqq \bar q \bar q \bar q \rangle$ in QCD by considering contributions from quark-antiquark scattering via color-singlet meson poles (pion, $\rho$, $\omega$, $K$). They use spectral representations for the Bethe-Salpeter wavefunctions of mesons and apply Hadamard/Landau conditions and Cutkosky cuts to extract the singularity structure of a three-meson-exchange diagram, yielding a baryon pole. The baryon mass is obtained by minimizing the total energy expression derived from the on-shell conditions imposed by the cuts.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass taken as $M_\pi = 139$ MeV
- Pion size parameter $\delta_\pi = 174$ MeV (corresponding to 1 fm)
- Lightest baryon (proton) mass: $M_B = 3(M_\pi + \delta_\pi) = 3(139 + 174) = 939$ MeV (implicitly from the formula)
- Roper resonance estimate: $M_B = 2(M_\pi + \delta_\pi) + (M_\rho + \delta_\rho) \approx 1474$ MeV, with $\delta_\rho \approx \delta_\pi/2$ and $\Gamma_{\text{Roper}} > \Gamma_\rho \approx 150$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ baryon mass estimate: $M_\Lambda = 1.2$ GeV, using two pions and a $K^0$ meson with $\delta_K \approx \delta_\pi/2$
- $\theta$ pentaquark mass: $M_\theta = 3(M_\pi + \delta_\pi) + M_K + M_\pi = M_P + M_K + M_\pi = 1571$ MeV
- $ud ud \bar u$ pentaquark mass: $M_P + 2M_\pi = 1217$ MeV
- Heptaquark mass ($ud us \bar d s \bar u$): $M_P + 2M_K + 2M_\pi = 2313$ MeV
- Threshold mass bound: $\tilde m \leq 591$ MeV, with a model estimate of $\tilde m \approx 550$ MeV for $u,d,s$ quarks from $\sigma$QCD

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model and parton model by deriving them from a quantum field theoretic picture based on meson exchange in QCD Green's functions. The approach contrasts with traditional Hamiltonian eigenvalue methods by using singularity analysis of Green's functions. The pentaquark prediction follows the experimental discovery discussed by the Pentaquark Collaboration (2003) and theoretical work by Jaffe (2003). The method is inapplicable to heavy quark systems where binding energies are small compared to threshold masses, and exact chiral symmetry yields a marginally bound baryon. The analysis enables predictions for multi-quark states (pentaquarks, heptaquarks) and suggests that color non-singlet systems like diquarks have only branch cut singularities, not poles.
