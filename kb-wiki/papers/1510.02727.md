# Inclusive deuteron-induced reactions and final neutron states

> We present in this paper a formalism for deuteron-induced inclusive reactions. We disentangle direct elastic breakup contributions from other processes (which we generically call non-elastic breakup) implying a capture of the neutron both above and below the neutron emission threshold. The reaction is described as a two step process, namely the breakup of the deuteron followed by the propagation of the neutron-target system driven by an optical potential. The final state interaction between the neutron and the target can eventually form an excited compound nucleus. Within this context, the direct neutron transfer to a sharp bound state is a limiting case of the present formalism.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for deuteron-induced inclusive reactions that treats direct elastic breakup separately from non-elastic breakup, where the neutron is captured either above or below the neutron emission threshold. The formalism claims that direct neutron transfer to a sharp bound state emerges as a limiting case when the imaginary part of the neutron-target optical potential is small, but this approximation requires the resonance width to be much smaller than the spacing between resonances and fails when a resonance lies within one width of the neutron emission threshold.

## Method

The reaction is described as a two-step process: breakup of the deuteron followed by propagation of the neutron-target system driven by an optical potential. The authors derive the non-elastic breakup cross section in the prior representation using a three-body Hamiltonian, expressing it in terms of a neutron wavefunction obtained from an optical-model Green's function and a source term that involves the proton and deuteron distorted waves. The neutron wavefunction is computed for arbitrary positive and negative energies by imposing appropriate boundary conditions on the Green's function, and the final cross section is obtained by partial-wave expansion and numerical evaluation of two-dimensional integrals.

## Context

This work extends earlier formalisms from the late 1970s through early 1990s by Kerman (1979), Udagawa (1980), Austern (1981), Li (1984), Ichimura (1985, 1990), and Hussein (1985, 1989), which left an unresolved controversy regarding different approaches. The paper provides a unified framework that connects inclusive deuteron reactions to direct neutron transfer in DWBA, enabling the surrogate reaction method for studying neutron-induced reactions (n,γ) and (n,f) on radioactive isotopes in inverse kinematics, as pursued by Escher (2010, 2012), Scielzo (2010), Hughes (2012), Ross (2012), and Carlson (2014).
