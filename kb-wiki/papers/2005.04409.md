# Evolution of clustering structure through the momentum distributions in $^{8-10}$Be isotopes

> We investigate the evolution of clustering structure through the momentum distributions in the $^{8-10}$Be isotopes. The nucleon dynamics within the inter-cluster antisymmetrization are discussed via the momentum distribution of a Brink type $α$-$α$ wave function. For the state with a small $α$-$α$ distance, we observe a significant depression with a dip structure at zero-momentum and an enhanced tail at relatively higher momentum region. In addition, we find the "cluster structure" in the intrinsic frame of momentum space, which is complementary to its significant $α$-cluster dissolution in the coordinate space because of the strong antisymmetrization. For the physical $^{8-10}$Be isotopes, the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-R{ö}pke (THSR) wave functions are adopted. The evolution from the dilute clustering state to the compact one is demonstrated by a successive depression at the zero-momentum of nucleon distribution for the two $α$-clusters within $^{8-10}$Be isotopes. For the compact $^{10}$Be nucleus, the momentum distribution of all nucleons shows significant depression at zero-momentum with a dip structure, which is found to be contributed by both the inter-cluster antisymmetrization and the $p$-orbit occupation of the valence neutrons. This study proposes a new window for the investigations of the $α$-clustering effects via the low-momentum components of nuclei, which is expected to be extended to the heavier nuclear clustering states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the evolution of alpha-clustering structure in Be isotopes can be probed through nucleon momentum distributions, specifically showing that a dip structure at zero-momentum and an enhanced tail at higher momentum indicate strong inter-cluster antisymmetrization, but this signature is only clearly visible for the total nucleon distribution in compact nuclei like $^{10}$Be, while for the two-alpha subsystem the depression is present but does not produce a dip.

## Method

The authors used Brink-type alpha-alpha wave functions as a toy model to study the effect of inter-cluster distance on momentum distributions, then adopted Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke (THSR) wave functions for the physical $^{8-10}$Be isotopes, which describe nonlocalized cluster motion. The nucleon momentum distributions were calculated analytically using formulations developed for Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) wave functions, with proper subtraction of center-of-mass motion, and the THSR wave functions were variationally optimized for each isotope.

## Key numbers

- The width parameter for the single-nucleon Gaussian wave packets is $\nu = 0.27$ fm$^{-2}$, chosen to reproduce the binding energy of the alpha cluster.
- The Fermi momentum is given as $k_F = 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$, below which the clustering correlation is expected to dominate over tensor and short-range correlations.
- For the Brink model, alpha-alpha distances of $D_z = \{4, 3, 2, 1\}$ fm were studied, corresponding to evolution from weak to strong overlap between the two alpha clusters.
- The optimized THSR deformation parameters for $^8$Be are $\beta_{\alpha,xy} = 1.0$ fm and $\beta_{\alpha,z} = 11.0$ fm; for $^9$Be: $\beta_{\alpha,xy} = 0.1$ fm, $\beta_{\alpha,z} = 4.2$ fm, $\beta_{n,xy} = 2.5$ fm, $\beta_{n,z} = 2.8$ fm; for $^{10}$Be: $\beta_{\alpha,xy} = 0.1$ fm, $\beta_{\alpha,z} = 2.5$ fm, $\beta_{n,xy} = 1.9$ fm, $\beta_{n,z} = 2.9$ fm.
- The valence neutrons in $^{10}$Be contribute mostly around $k \approx 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to a mean location of the valence neutron at about $D = 1$ fm from the center of mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of clustering in Be isotopes using THSR wave functions (Lyu 2015, Lyu 2016) and the analytical formulation for momentum distributions (Lyu 2019b). It contrasts with earlier work on $^9$Be momentum distributions using AMD wave functions (Hirai 2011) where center-of-mass motion was not properly treated. The study proposes that low-momentum components of nuclei, accessible via electron scattering reactions (Ciofi degli Atti 1996), can serve as a new probe of alpha-clustering effects, and suggests this approach could be extended to heavier nuclear clustering states.
