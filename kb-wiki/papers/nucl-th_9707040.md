> Efforts to observe color transparency in the reactions: (e,e'p), (p,pp) and coherent nuclear diffractive dissociation of pions into two jets are reviewed.

# Color Transparency - Color Coherent Effects in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim

Color transparency, the vanishing of initial and final state interactions predicted by QCD for high momentum transfer quasielastic nuclear reactions, has been observed in (p,pp) experiments but not in (e,e'p) experiments, with the discrepancy attributed to the rapid expansion of point-like configurations at the relatively low momenta of ejected protons in the electron scattering measurements.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental searches for color transparency in three reaction channels: (e,e'p) quasielastic knockout, (p,pp) quasielastic scattering, and coherent nuclear diffractive dissociation of pions into two jets. For the (p,pp) reaction, calculations using a model that includes both point-like configurations and blob-like configurations of average size are compared to experimental data, with the ratio of measured to Born approximation cross sections serving as the transparency observable.

## Key numbers

- (p,pp) experiment at BNL (Carroll et al., 1988): proton momenta p_L = 6, 10, and 12 GeV/c, with Q^2 = 4.8, 8.6, and 10.4 GeV^2/c^2; expansion times roughly 3.4, 5.7, and 6.8 fm
- NE18 (e,e'p) experiment (Makins et al., 1994): Q^2 = 1, 3, 5, and 7 GeV^2/c^2; outgoing proton momenta 1.1, 2.3, 3.5, and 4.6 GeV/c; expansion times about 0.65, 1.3, 2.0, and 2.6 fm
- New EVA (p,pp) experiment at BNL: ratio of measured to Born approximation cross sections rises by about 30% between p_L = 6 and 7.5 GeV/c at quasielastic kinematics with alpha = 1
- For coherent pion diffraction into two jets: k_perp greater than about 1 GeV/c and less than about 3 to 5 GeV/c; expected cross section proportional to A^2 for forward amplitude, or A^(4/3) for integrated t

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman (1994) on color transparency and point-like configurations in QCD. The (p,pp) results of Carroll et al. (1988) showed evidence for color transparency, while the NE18 (e,e'p) experiment of Makins et al. (1994) did not, with models by Farrar, Liu, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1988) and Jennings and Miller (1990) explaining the discrepancy through expansion effects. The work enables future experiments at Jefferson Lab and higher-energy measurements to observe enhanced cross sections at Q^2 around 10 GeV^2/c^2, as well as new approaches such as chiral transparency in (e,e'Delta^{++}) reactions and coherent pion diffraction into two jets at Fermilab experiment E791.
