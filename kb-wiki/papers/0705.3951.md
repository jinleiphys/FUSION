# A non factorized calculation of the process ^3He(e,e'p)^2H at medium energies

> The exclusive process ^3He(e,e^\prime p)^2H has been analyzed using realistic few-body wave functions corresponding to the AV18 interaction and treating the final state interaction (FSI) within the Eikonal Approximation to describe the multiple rescattering of the struck nucleon with the nucleons of the spectator two-nucleon system. Calculations have been performed in momentum space so that the nucleon electromagnetic current could be left in the fully covariant form avoiding by this way non relativistic reductions and the factorization approximation. The results of calculations, which are compared with recent JLab experimental data, show that the left-right asymmetry exhibit a clear dependence upon the multiple scattering in the final state and demonstrate the breaking down of the factorization approximation at ϕ=0 i.e. for "negative" and large \geq 300MeV/c values of the missing momentum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the factorization approximation breaks down for the process ^3He(e,e'p)^2H at missing momenta greater than or equal to 300 MeV/c for the left side (azimuthal angle phi=0), and that the left-right asymmetry is governed entirely by final state interaction (FSI) effects, though quantitative disagreements with experimental data remain in some regions, particularly around |p_m| approximately 0.6-0.65 GeV/c at phi=0.

## Method

The authors performed a non-factorized calculation of the exclusive process ^3He(e,e'p)^2H in momentum space using realistic few-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction, treating final state interaction within the Eikonal Approximation (a generalized Glauber approach) to describe multiple rescattering of the struck nucleon with the spectator two-nucleon system. The nucleon electromagnetic current was left in fully covariant form (using the CC1 prescription) without non-relativistic reductions, and the calculations included PWIA, single rescattering, and double rescattering contributions to the FSI.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer |q| = 1.5 GeV/c, energy transfer nu = 0.84 GeV, Q^2 = 1.55 (GeV/c)^2, and Bjorken x approximately 1 for the experimental kinematics
- Factorization approximation breaks down at negative missing momentum values p_m greater than or equal to 300 MeV/c
- PWIA and FSI results are almost the same up to |p_m| approximately 400 MeV/c, but PWIA underestimates experimental data at larger values
- The left-right asymmetry A_TL is rather small (approximately 0-20%) for values of p_m up to approximately 650 MeV/c
- The ratio R of reduced cross sections deviates from unity at p_m approximately 0.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same authors (NashPR, NashPRL) that used the factorization approximation and only covered the right side (phi=pi) of missing momentum up to 1.1 GeV/c. It contrasts with the non-factorized calculation by Schiavilla (2005) that used a non-relativistic form for the nucleon electromagnetic current and included meson exchange currents. The results enable future investigations of the process ^4He(e,e'p)^3H within a non-factorized approach, and suggest that the treatment of FSI at high Q^2 is under control, though mechanisms such as meson exchange currents and Delta-isobar configurations may be needed to resolve remaining disagreements with data.
