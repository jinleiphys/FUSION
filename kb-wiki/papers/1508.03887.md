# Heavy flavor in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study charm production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The initial charm quarks are produced by the PYTHIA event generator tuned to fit the transverse momentum spectrum and rapidity distribution of charm quarks from Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations. The produced charm quarks scatter in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with the off-shell partons whose masses and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM), which reproduces the lattice QCD equation-of-state in thermal equilibrium. The relevant cross sections are calculated in a consistent way by employing the effective propagators and couplings from the DQPM. Close to the critical energy density of the phase transition, the charm quarks are hadronized into $D$ mesons through coalescence and/or fragmentation. The hadronized $D$ mesons then interact with the various hadrons in the hadronic phase with cross sections calculated in an effective lagrangian approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $D^0$ mesons from PHSD are compared with the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ =200 GeV and to the ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ =2.76 TeV. We find that in the PHSD the energy loss of $D$ mesons at high $p_T$ can be dominantly attributed to partonic scattering while the actual shape of $R_{AA}$ versus $p_T$ reflects the heavy-quark hadronization scenario, i.e. coalescence versus fragmentation. Also the hadronic rescattering is important for the $R_{AA}$ at low $p_T$ and enhances the $D$-meson elliptic flow $v_2$.

## Key claim
The PHSD transport approach reproduces the measured nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D^0\) mesons at both RHIC and LHC energies without including radiative energy loss, attributing the dominant suppression at high \(p_T\) to collisional scattering in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and the shape of \(R_{AA}\) to the hadronization mechanism (coalescence versus fragmentation). The authors hedge that radiative energy loss may become important at very high \(p_T\) (above 6 GeV/c at RHIC and 15 GeV/c at LHC), which is not yet accounted for in the present calculations.

## Method
The authors embed charm quarks, initially produced by the PYTHIA event generator tuned to FONLL calculations, into the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The charm quarks scatter elastically in the QGP with off-shell partons whose masses and widths are given by the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM), with cross sections computed consistently from DQPM propagators and couplings. Near the critical energy density (0.5 GeV/fm\(^3\)), charm quarks are hadronized into \(D\) mesons via coalescence or fragmentation, and the resulting \(D\) mesons interact with hadrons using cross sections from an effective Lagrangian with heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D^0\) mesons are compared to STAR data for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The coalescence probability for \(D\)-meson formation is evaluated for two assumed \(D\)-meson radii: 0.5 fm and 0.9 fm.
- For LHC energies, the \(v_2\) of \(D^0+D^+ +D^{*+}\) mesons in 20% central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV reaches up to about 10%.
- The charm quark mass is taken as 1.5 GeV in the scattering calculations.
- The hadronization threshold in PHSD is set at a local energy density below 0.5 GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends the PHSD transport approach, previously applied to light-hadron production from SPS to LHC energies, to heavy-flavor dynamics. It contrasts with earlier pQCD-based models that use massless light quarks and fixed or running coupling, instead employing the DQPM which reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics. The results enable a consistent microscopic description of charm quark energy loss, hadronization, and hadronic rescattering, and provide a baseline for future inclusion of radiative energy loss at higher \(p_T\). The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) extracted from the cross sections shows a minimum near \(T_c\), consistent with lattice QCD data above \(T_c\) and hadronic many-body calculations below \(T_c\) (as in Hamza et al., 2014).
