# Chiral $2π$-exchange NN-potentials: Two-loop contributions

> We calculate in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory the local NN-potentials generated by the two-pion exchange diagrams at two-loop order. We give explicit expressions for the mass-spectra (or imaginary parts) of the corresponding isoscalar and isovector central, spin-spin and tensor NN-amplitudes. We find from two-loop two-pion exchange a sizeable isoscalar central repulsion which amounts to $62.3 $MeV at $r=1.0 $fm. There is a similarly strong isovector central attraction which however originates mainly from the third order low energy constants $\bar d_j$ entering the chiral $πN$-scattering amplitude. We also evaluate the one-loop $2π$-exchange diagram with two second order chiral $ππNN$-vertices proportional to the low energy constants $c_{1,2,3,4}$ as well as the first relativistic 1/M-correction to the $2π$-exchange diagrams with one such vertex. The diagrammatic results presented here are relevant components of the chiral NN-potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim
Two-loop chiral two-pion exchange generates a sizeable isoscalar central repulsion of 62.3 MeV at 1.0 fm internucleon distance, but this result depends on the inclusion of previously omitted two-loop diagrams beyond the correlated 2π-exchange considered in earlier work; the similarly strong isovector central attraction originates mainly from third-order low-energy constants \(\bar d_j\) rather than from the two-loop dynamics itself.

## Method
The author calculates local NN-potentials in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory by evaluating the imaginary parts (mass spectra) of the isoscalar and isovector central, spin-spin, and tensor NN-amplitudes from two-loop two-pion exchange diagrams, using the Cutkosky cutting rule and integrating over the Lorentz-invariant 2π-phase space. The coordinate-space potentials are then obtained via continuous superpositions of Yukawa functions. Additionally, the one-loop 2π-exchange diagram with two second-order chiral ππNN-vertices (proportional to \(c_{1,2,3,4}\)) and the first relativistic \(1/M\)-corrections to diagrams with one such vertex are evaluated.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar central potential \(\widetilde V_C(r) = 62.28\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm (repulsive).
- Isovector central potential \(\widetilde W_C(r) = -96.81\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm (attractive), but dominated by \(\bar d_j\) constants; omitting them reduces the magnitude by more than one order of magnitude.
- Isoscalar spin-spin potential \(\widetilde V_S(r) = 32.96\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm.
- Isoscalar tensor potential \(\widetilde V_T(r) = -27.49\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm.
- Isovector spin-spin potential \(\widetilde W_S(r) = -6.93\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm.
- Isovector tensor potential \(\widetilde W_T(r) = 6.40\) MeV at \(r = 1.0\) fm.
- Input parameters: \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV (average pion mass), \(g_A = 1.3\) (giving \(g_{\pi N} = 13.2\)).
- Third-order low-energy constants used: \(\bar d_1+\bar d_2 = 3.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\bar d_3 = -3.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\bar d_5 = 0.1\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\bar d_{14}-\bar d_{15} = -5.7\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context
This work extends the chiral NN-potential to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO) by computing two-loop 2π-exchange contributions, building on the one-loop 2π-exchange results of Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2000) and the correlated 2π-exchange of the same group. The two-loop isoscalar central repulsion is nearly a factor 100 larger than the correlated 2π-exchange result from those earlier works. The results are intended for implementation in full N\(^3\)LO calculations of the two-nucleon system and in empirical analyses of low-energy elastic NN-scattering, complementing the author's previous work on three-pion exchange at two-loop order.
