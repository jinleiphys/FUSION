# Hydrodynamical evolution near the QCD critical end point

> Hydrodynamical calculations have been successful in describing global observables in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, which aim to observe the production of the quark-gluon plasma. On the other hand, recently, a lot of evidence that there exists a critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram has been accumulating. Nevertheless, so far, no equation of state with the CEP has been employed in hydrodynamical calculations. In this paper, we construct the equation of state with the CEP on the basis of the universality hypothesis and show that the CEP acts as an attractor of isentropic trajectories. We also consider the time evolution in the case with the CEP and discuss how the CEP affects the final state observables, such as the correlation length, fluctuation, chemical freezeout, kinetic freezeout, and so on. Finally, we argue that the anomalously low kinetic freezeout temperature at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider suggests the possibility of the existence of the CEP.

## Key claim

The QCD critical end point (CEP) acts as an attractor of isentropic trajectories in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane, meaning that fine-tuning of collision energy is not required for the system to pass near the CEP, provided the critical region is sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state containing the QCD critical end point by mapping the three-dimensional Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram via the universality hypothesis, using the parametric representation of the Ising equation of state with critical exponents beta=0.326, delta=4.80, and alpha=0.11. They matched the singular entropy density near the CEP with non-singular hadron phase entropy density from the excluded volume approximation and QGP phase entropy density from the Bag model, then solved the hydrodynamical evolution using Bjorken's scaling solution with initial temperature 200 MeV and proper time 1 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- CEP located at (T, mu_B) = (154.7 MeV, 367.8 MeV) in the primary calculation, and alternatively at (143.7 MeV, 652.0 MeV)
- Critical exponents used: beta = 0.326, delta = 4.80, alpha = 0.11, nu = 0.63 (three-dimensional Ising model)
- Bag constant B = (220 MeV)^4 with N_f = 2 flavors
- Hadron radius fixed at 0.7 fm in the excluded volume approximation
- Initial temperature set to 200 MeV and initial proper time to 1 fm/c
- Dynamic critical exponent z = 3 (Model H universality class)
- Non-universal parameter A = 1 in the rate equation for correlation length evolution

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998) that isentropic trajectories focus on the CEP from the first-order side, by demonstrating numerically that the focusing occurs on both the first-order and crossover sides of the CEP. The authors contrast their equation of state with the Bag plus excluded volume model (used in most hydrodynamical calculations, e.g., Kolb and Heinz 2003), which has no CEP and always exhibits first-order phase transitions with no focusing effect. This enables future hydrodynamical simulations to incorporate critical phenomena and predict experimental signatures such as anomalous kinetic freezeout temperatures at RHIC.
