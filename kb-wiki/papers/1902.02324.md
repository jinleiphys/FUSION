# Complex-energy analysis of proton-proton fusion

> An analysis of the astrophysical $S$ factor of the proton-proton weak capture ($\mathrm{p}+\mathrm{p}\rightarrow {}^2\mathrm{H}+\mathrm{e}^++ν_{\mathrm{e}}$) is performed on a large energy range covering solar-core and early Universe temperatures. The measurement of $S$ being physically unachievable, its value relies on the theoretical calculation of the matrix element $Λ$. Surprisingly, $Λ$ reaches a maximum near $0.13~\mathrm{MeV}$ that has been unexplained until now. A model-independent parametrization of $Λ$ valid up to about $5~\mathrm{MeV}$ is established on the basis of recent effective-range functions. It provides an insight into the relationship between the maximum of $Λ$ and the proton-proton resonance pole at $(-140-467\,\mathrm{i})~\mathrm{keV}$ from analytic continuation. In addition, this parametrization leads to an accurate evaluation of the derivatives of $Λ$, and hence of $S$, in the limit of zero energy.

## Key claim

The maximum of the weak capture matrix element Λ near 0.13 MeV, which had been unexplained, is shown to originate from the analytic structure of the effective-range function |Δ⁺₀|⁻¹, specifically from the proximity of the proton-proton ¹S₀ sub-threshold resonance pole at (−140±467 i) keV, rather than from any nuclear-structure effect.

## Method

The authors derive a model-independent parametrization of the dimensionless matrix element Λ(E) for the p+p→²H+e⁺+νₑ reaction, valid up to about 5 MeV, by separating the far-field (Coulomb) part of the nucleon-nucleon wave functions from the short-range part C(E). They express Λ in terms of the effective-range function ϰ₀, the modified Coulomb functions Φ and Ψ, and a linearized function L(E) fitted to numerical wave functions from three potential models (Av18, Reid93, NijmII). The analytic continuation of Λ to complex energies is then performed using this parametrization to reveal the role of the ¹S₀ resonance pole.

## Key numbers

* The matrix element Λ²(0) = 7.034(33) at zero energy, consistent with the recommended value from Adelberger et al. (2011).
* The corresponding astrophysical S-factor S(0) = 3.95(3)×10⁻²³ MeV fm², after including 1.62% radiative corrections.
* The maximum of Λ occurs at E ≈ 130 keV, with an inflection point at 16.2 keV.
* The linearized function L(E) = 8.42(1) + 0.55(1) ε, fitted on the interval [300, 600] keV, where ε = E/B is the dimensionless energy.
* The proton-proton ¹S₀ resonance pole lies at (−140±467 i) keV in the complex energy plane.
* The short-range correction at zero energy is C(0) = 0.260(1) from potential models, compared to the Bahcall and May (1969) estimate C(0) ≈ 0.262(1).
* The deuteron asymptotic normalization coefficient A = 0.8850(5) fm⁻¹/² from three potential models.

## Context

This work extends the effective-range analysis of the proton-proton weak capture originally developed by Bethe and Critchfield (1938), Salpeter (1952), and Bahcall and May (1969), and later refined in potential-model calculations (e.g., Wiringa 1995, Stoks 1994) and chiral effective field theory (e.g., Marcucci 2013, Acharya 2016). The model-independent parametrization provides a more accurate determination of the derivatives S′(0) and S″(0) than the polynomial extrapolations used in previous work, and explains the previously unaccounted maximum of Λ at 130 keV as a consequence of the nearby ¹S₀ resonance pole. This enables improved predictions of the pp‑chain reaction rate for solar and Big Bang nucleosynthesis models.
