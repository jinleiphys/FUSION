# The chiral anomaly, Berry's phase and chiral kinetic theory, from world-lines in quantum field theory

> We outline a novel chiral kinetic theory framework for systematic computations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The real part of the fermion determinant in the QCD effective action is expressed as a supersymmetric world-line action of spinning, colored, Grassmanian point particles in background gauge fields, with equations of motion that are covariant generalizations of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi and Wong equations. Berry's phase is obtained in a consistent non-relativistic adiabatic limit. The chiral anomaly, in contrast, arises from the phase of the fermion determinant; its topological properties are therefore distinct from those of the Berry phase. We show that the imaginary contribution to the fermion determinant too can be expressed as a point particle world-line path integral and derive the corresponding anomalous axial vector current. Our results can be used to derive a covariant relativistic chiral kinetic theory including the effects of topological fluctuations that has overlap with classical-statistical simulations of the CME at early times and anomalous hydrodynamics at late times.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a first-principles world-line derivation of relativistic chiral kinetic theory from QCD, showing that Berry's phase arises only in a non-relativistic adiabatic limit of the real part of the fermion determinant and is topologically distinct from the chiral anomaly, which instead originates from the imaginary (phase) part of the fermion determinant.

## Method

The authors express the real part of the QCD fermion determinant as a supersymmetric world-line path integral of spinning, colored Grassmannian point particles in background gauge fields, with equations of motion that are covariant generalizations of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi and Wong equations. They then adapt a trick due to D'Hoker and Gagné to express the imaginary part (the relative phase) of the fermion determinant as a similar point-particle path integral, using a parameter that regulates chiral symmetry breaking. The non-relativistic adiabatic limit of the real-part Lagrangian is taken to recover Berry's phase, while the imaginary part is shown to yield the standard chiral anomaly relation.

## Context

This work extends the world-line formalism of Strassler (1992) and D'Hoker and Gagné (1995) to provide a first-principles derivation of chiral kinetic theory, addressing the conflation of Berry's phase with the chiral anomaly noted by Fujikawa (2005). The results can be matched to classical-statistical simulations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect at early times (Mace 2016, Mueller 2016) and to anomalous hydrodynamics at late times (Hongo 2013, Hirono 2014), and will be used to derive an anomalous Bodeker kinetic theory in a follow-up work.
