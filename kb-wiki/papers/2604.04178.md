# Disentangling Flow Contributions from the Chiral Magnetic Effect in U+U Collisions with Forward-Backward Multiplicity Asymmetry

> The observation of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions remains challenging because of large flow-induced backgrounds and experimental constraints. We demonstrate that the forward-backward multiplicity asymmetry (FBMA) provides a robust and experimentally accessible control parameter to separate the flow background from CME signal in the collisions of deformed nuclei, such as prolate uranium where FBMA is naturally enhanced and correlated with the initial-state geometry. Monte Carlo Glauber simulations indicate that varying FBMA within a fixed centrality class modulates ellipticity largely independently of the magnetic-field correlator, establishing FBMA as a practical tool for disentangling CME signals from flow driven background.

# Disentangling Flow Contributions from the Chiral Magnetic Effect in U+U Collisions with Forward-Backward Multiplicity Asymmetry

## Key claim

The forward-backward multiplicity asymmetry (FBMA) provides a robust and experimentally accessible control parameter to separate flow background from Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) signal in collisions of deformed nuclei, but this separation works significantly better in U+U collisions than in Au+Au collisions because the prolate deformation of uranium naturally enhances FBMA and its correlation with initial-state geometry.

## Method

The authors employed the shadowed Monte Carlo Glauber model (shMCGM) to simulate 500 million events each for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV. They computed the FBMA from charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions, the participant eccentricity epsilon_2 as a proxy for elliptic flow v_2, and the magnetic field correlator gamma_B using Lienard-Wiechert potentials as a proxy for the CME signal. The correlation between FBMA and these quantities was studied within fixed centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- Uranium Woods-Saxon parameters: R=6.81 fm, a=0.55 fm, beta_2=0.28, beta_4=0.093; gold parameters: R=6.37 fm, a=0.535 fm, beta_2=-0.13, beta_4=0
- Shadowing parameter lambda=0.11, as required to explain ultra-central U+U data
- Pseudorapidity acceptance for FBMA: eta_max=1.0, consistent with STAR experimental acceptance
- Tilt parameter eta_T=1.40 for shMCGM, recalibrated from MCGM value of 3.33
- Hardness parameter x_hard=0.29 for shMCGM, compared to 0.14 for MCGM
- Negative binomial parameters for shMCGM: mean n_bar=2.2, shape k=1.3
- In U+U collisions, varying FBMA within a fixed centrality bin produces multiple values of gamma_B at a single value of epsilon_2, enabling disentanglement; this effect is absent in Au+Au where gamma_B values from different FBMA bins largely coincide with the centrality-averaged baseline

## Context

This work extends the strategy proposed by Chatterjee (2014) for using spectator neutron asymmetry to select Body-Tip configurations in U+U collisions, but replaces that observable with FBMA which relies exclusively on charged particles detected with higher efficiency. The approach complements event-shape engineering techniques (Schukraft, 2012) and addresses the fundamental challenge identified by Voloshin (2010) that varying centrality to suppress flow backgrounds also suppresses the magnetic field strength. The use of the shadowed Monte Carlo Glauber model follows Chatterjee (2015) and improves upon the standard two-component Glauber model by better describing ultra-central U+U data, including the absence of the predicted "knee" in the ellipticity-multiplicity correlation observed by STAR. The results enable future experimental analyses at RHIC and LHC to test whether measured charge-dependent correlations are driven solely by flow or contain a genuine CME contribution, by checking whether gamma_ab-v_2 correlations at fixed centrality remain unchanged when FBMA is varied.
