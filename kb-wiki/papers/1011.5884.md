# Chiral dynamics predictions for eta' -> eta pi pi

> The hadronic decays eta' -> eta pi pi are studied in the frameworks of large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory, at lowest and next-to-leading orders, and Resonance Chiral Theory in the leading 1/Nc approximation. Higher order effects such as pi-pi final state interactions are taken into account through a detailed unitarization procedure. The inclusion of finite-width effects in the case of RChT is also discussed. The Dalitz plot distribution and the differential branching ratio are computed in both approaches. The predicted Dalitz plot parameters obtained from the different treatments are compared with the most recent measured values. We find that the eta' -> eta pi pi branching ratios are easily understood, while the Dalitz plot parameters require the inclusion of pi-pi loops in order to achieve a reasonable agreement. Our final predictions agree with the experimental measurements. We hope our results to be of relevance for present and future experimental analyses of these decays.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the branching ratios for η′ → ηππ decays are easily understood within large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory and Resonance Chiral Theory, but the Dalitz plot parameters require the explicit inclusion of ππ loop effects and unitarization of final state interactions to achieve reasonable agreement with experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors studied η′ → ηππ decays using two chiral frameworks: large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory at lowest and next-to-leading orders, and Resonance Chiral Theory in the leading 1/Nc approximation. They computed the Dalitz plot distribution and differential branching ratio in both approaches, incorporating ππ final state interactions through a unitarization procedure and including finite-width effects for the Resonance Chiral Theory case.

## Key numbers

- The lowest-order large-Nc ChPT amplitude yields a branching ratio of only 3% of the measured value, using θ_P = (−13.3 ± 0.5)° and f_π = 92.2 MeV
- The next-to-leading order predicted branching ratios for different LEC sets are: 14% (set1), 9% (set2), and 64% (set3)
- The ratio of charged to neutral decay widths is Γ(η′ → ηπ⁺π⁻)/Γ(η′ → ηπ⁰π⁰) = 1.99 ± 0.08
- The experimental branching ratios used are B(η′ → ηπ⁺π⁻) = 0.432 ± 0.007 and B(η′ → ηπ⁰π⁰) = 0.217 ± 0.008 from the Review of Particle Properties
- The Dalitz parameter a/d = κ₂₁/(2κ₄₀) = 3.4 is predicted to be independent of chiral couplings, depending only on pseudoscalar masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies of η′ → ηππ decays within effective chiral Lagrangian approaches, including the work of Fariborz (1999) combining scalar mesons into a nonet and Borasoy and Nissler (2005) using U(3) chiral effective field theory with a relativistic coupled-channels approach. The results are intended to be of relevance for present and future experimental analyses at KLOE-2, Crystal Ball, Crystal Barrel, WASA, and BES-III, where large samples of η′ decays are expected.
