# Investigation of diffractive photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ in hadronic collisions

> In this work we study the diffractive photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ in proton-proton, proton - nucleus and nucleus - nucleus collisions at LHC energies using the color dipole formalism and different models for the forward dipole - target scattering amplitude. Our goal is to estimate the theoretical uncertainty present in the current predictions in the literature. Our results are compared with the experimental data and predictions for higher energies are presented.

## Key claim

The color dipole formalism can describe the diffractive photoproduction of J/Ψ in hadronic collisions at LHC energies, but the predictions are strongly dependent on the model used for the forward dipole-target scattering amplitude; the theoretical uncertainty among different models is large enough that only the bCGC NEW model simultaneously describes both ALICE and LHCb data.

## Method

The authors computed the cross sections for diffractive J/Ψ photoproduction in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions using the color dipole formalism, with a unified treatment of the photon spectrum and J/Ψ wave function (Gauss-LC model). They compared four models for the forward dipole-proton scattering amplitude: the GBW model, the bCGC model with two parameter sets (bCGC and bCGC NEW), and the running-coupling BK (rcBK) solution; for nuclear targets, the dipole-nucleus amplitude was obtained via the Glauber-Gribov formalism using the nuclear profile function.

## Key numbers

* For pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the total cross section predictions are: GBW = 74.0 nb, bCGC = 49.0 nb, bCGC NEW = 59.0 nb.
* For pp collisions at √s = 14 TeV, the total cross section predictions are: GBW = 113.0 nb, bCGC = 71.2 nb, bCGC NEW = 93.7 nb.
* For pPb collisions at √s = 5 TeV, the total cross section predictions are: GBW = 51.3 μb, bCGC = 41.0 μb, bCGC NEW = 42.8 μb.
* For PbPb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, the total cross section predictions are: GBW = 18.2 mb, bCGC = 13.6 mb, bCGC NEW = 11.0 mb.
* For PbPb collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV, the total cross section predictions are: GBW = 33.8 mb, bCGC = 24.4 mb, bCGC NEW = 20.3 mb.
* At central rapidity in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the predictions differ by approximately 30%; in the LHCb rapidity range the difference is approximately 50%.
* For γPb collisions, the rcBK and bCGC predictions differ by a factor of 1.8 at larger energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier color dipole studies of vector meson photoproduction in hadronic collisions (Vicmag et al., 2007; Motyka and Watt, 2008; Lappi, 2013; Griep, 2013) by comparing multiple dipole models under a unified treatment of the photon spectrum and meson wavefunction. It contrasts with collinear factorization approaches (Guzey, 2013; Martin, 2013) that are strongly dependent on gluon distribution parameterizations and nuclear shadowing. The results enable future measurements at higher LHC energies and at proposed electron-ion colliders to discriminate between QCD dynamics models and constrain the magnitude of nonlinear (saturation) effects.
