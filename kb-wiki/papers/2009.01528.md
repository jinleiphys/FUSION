# Brownian motion of neutrinos in the Sun [On the distribution of collisions of a neutrino with nucleons of the Sun]

> When solving the solar neutrino problem on the basis of the hypothesis of the existence of a new interaction between electron neutrinos and nucleons, carried by a massless pseudoscalar boson, it becomes necessary to find the consequences of the short-term Brownian motion of neutrinos in the Sun. Earlier, we transmitted these consequences by the effective number of collisions of a neutrino with nucleons of the Sun, assuming the equality of fluxes near the Earth of left- and right-handed solar neutrinos. In this paper, we analyze the transfer of the mentioned consequences by a test geometric distribution of collisions with one free parameter. This distribution provides good agreement between theoretical and experimental results for all five observed processes with solar neutrinos, while it gives the ratio of fluxes near the Earth of left- and right-handed neutrinos equal to 0.516:0.484. With such a ratio, the acceptability of the method of the effective number of collisions of a neutrino with nucleons is again confirmed.

# Brownian motion of neutrinos in the Sun [On the distribution of collisions of a neutrino with nucleons of the Sun]

## Key claim

The geometric distribution of neutrino-nucleon collisions in the Sun, with a single free parameter, provides good agreement between theoretical and experimental results for all five observed solar neutrino processes, while yielding a left-handed to right-handed neutrino flux ratio near Earth of 0.516:0.484, which introduces a small correction to the earlier assumption of approximately equal fluxes.

## Method

The author models the short-term Brownian motion of electron neutrinos in the Sun, caused by a new interaction mediated by a massless pseudoscalar boson with Yukawa coupling to neutrinos and nucleons, using a geometric distribution P(n) = p(1-p)^n for the number of collisions n a neutrino undergoes before leaving the Sun. The free parameter p is determined by fitting theoretical rates to experimental results for all five observed solar neutrino processes (chlorine, gallium, elastic scattering, charged-current deuteron, and neutral-current deuteron reactions), with the neutrino energy after each collision taken as one of the two boundary values from the allowed interval.

## Key numbers

- Free parameter of the geometric distribution: p = 0.062
- Probability that a solar neutrino near Earth is left-handed: W_left = 0.516 (corresponding to flux ratio 0.516:0.484 left-handed to right-handed)
- Average number of collisions per neutrino: n-bar = 16.1
- Total rate for chlorine-37 transitions: 2.77 SNU (geometric distribution, p=0.062), compared to experimental value 2.56 ± 0.16 ± 0.16 SNU
- Total rate for gallium-71 transitions: 64.2 SNU (geometric distribution, p=0.062), compared to experimental values 62.9^{+6.0}_{-5.9} SNU and 65.4^{+3.1}_{-3.0}{}^{+2.6}_{-2.8} SNU
- Effective flux from elastic scattering (SK III, E_c=5.0 MeV): 2.26 × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1} (geometric distribution), compared to experimental 2.32 ± 0.04 ± 0.05 × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1}
- Effective flux from charged-current deuteron process (SNO I, E_c=5.5 MeV): 1.88 × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1} (geometric distribution), compared to experimental 1.76^{+0.06}_{-0.05}{}^{+0.09}_{-0.09} × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1}
- Total neutral-current flux from deuteron process: 4.92 × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1} (geometric distribution), compared to SNO I value 5.09^{+0.44}_{-0.43}{}^{+0.46}_{-0.43} × 10^6 cm^{-2}s^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier alternative solution to the solar neutrino problem (Slad, 2020), which proposed a new fundamental interaction mediated by a massless pseudoscalar boson rather than neutrino oscillations. It replaces the earlier effective-number-of-collisions method (previously using n_0 = 11 or 12) with a geometric distribution, finding consistent results. The paper explicitly argues that the MSW mechanism (Mikheev and Smirnov, 1986; Wolfenstein, 1978) with parameters from SNO and Super-Kamiokande collaborations contradicts three out of five observed solar neutrino processes, and that no prior publication has presented summarized theoretical results for all five processes compared with experiment. This work enables a complete formulation of the alternative solution to the solar neutrino problem.
