# Short-Range Correlations in He-4 Liquid and Small He-4 Droplets described by the Unitary Correlation Operator Method

> The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) is employed to treat short-range correlations in both, homogeneous liquid and small droplets of bosonic He-4 atoms. The dominating short-range correlations in these systems are described by an unitary transformation in the two-body relative coordinate, applied either to the many-body state or to the Hamiltonian and other operators. It is shown that the two-body correlated interaction can describe the binding energy of clusters of up to 6 atoms very well, the numerical effort consisting only in calculating one two-body matrix element with Gaussian single-particle states. The increasing density of bigger droplets requires the inclusion of correlation effects beyond the two-body order, which are successfully implemented by a density-dependent two-body correlator. With only one adjusted parameter the binding energies and radii of larger droplets and the equation of state of the homogeneous He-4 liquid can be described quantitatively in a physically intuitive and numerically simple way.

# Short-Range Correlations in He-4 Liquid and Small He-4 Droplets described by the Unitary Correlation Operator Method

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) can describe short-range correlations in both homogeneous liquid and small droplets of bosonic He-4 atoms using only one adjusted parameter, but the two-body approximation is only sufficient for clusters of up to 6 atoms; larger droplets and the homogeneous liquid require a density-dependent two-body correlator to account for three-body and higher-order correlation effects.

## Method

The authors apply a unitary transformation in the two-body relative coordinate to treat short-range correlations, either acting on the many-body state or on the Hamiltonian and other operators. For small clusters up to 6 atoms, they use a two-body correlated interaction requiring only one two-body matrix element with Gaussian single-particle states. For larger droplets and the homogeneous liquid, they implement a density-dependent two-body correlator to incorporate correlation effects beyond two-body order, using a cluster expansion to estimate three-body contributions.

## Key numbers

- Lennard-Jones potential parameters: epsilon = 10.22 K, sigma = 2.556 Angstrom
- s-wave scattering length for two He-4 atoms via Lennard-Jones potential: a = -171.3 Angstrom
- Matching radius for hybrid uncorrelated wave function: chi = 7.83 Angstrom
- Core radius of repulsive core: approximately 2.5 Angstrom
- Smallness parameter threshold for two-body approximation validity: kappa = 0.3 (relative three-body contribution exceeds 10% above this value)

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Correlation Operator Method, previously applied to nuclear many-body problems (UCOM98), to bosonic He-4 systems. The authors compare their approach to Monte-Carlo-type calculations (KaLe81, ScSc65) and note that their method achieves excellent agreement with greatly reduced numerical effort, requiring only a few one-dimensional integrals. The approach enables quantitative description of binding energies and radii of He-4 droplets and the equation of state of homogeneous He-4 liquid using a physically intuitive framework, with the density-dependent correlator successfully capturing the effects that would otherwise require explicit three-body calculations.
