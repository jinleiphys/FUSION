# Neutrino Oscillations and Entanglement

> An improved treatment of neutrino oscillations follows when neutrino mass eigenstates and their associated recoiling particle states are entangled. When the neutrino and its recoil partner are treated as a single entangled quantum state the conservation of energy and momentum in neutrino oscillations can be assured, even in a plane-wave treatment. The oscillation wavelength between the neutrino and its associated recoil partner emerges as the fundamental periodicity in this analysis. However, the experimentally determined oscillation wavelength for neutrinos detected at some distance from a known source region is still in all cases given by the standard expression in current use.

## Key claim

When neutrino mass eigenstates and their associated recoiling particle are treated as a single entangled quantum state, conservation of energy and momentum in neutrino oscillations can be assured even in a plane-wave treatment; however, the experimentally determined oscillation wavelength for neutrinos detected at some distance from a known source region is still in all cases given by the standard expression in current use.

## Method

The paper analyzes two-body decays at rest (e.g., \(^{7}\)Be electron capture to \(^{7}\)Li and a neutrino) by writing the final state as an entangled product wavefunction of the neutrino and recoil particle. Lorentz transformations are applied to boost the system from the parent rest frame to the laboratory frame, and the phase differences between mass-eigenstate components are extracted from the exponential factors. The oscillation wavelength in the neutrino-recoil separation coordinate is derived and then related to the standard oscillation wavelength in the neutrino-origin coordinate by expressing the recoil position through kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For the decay \(^{7}\)Be + e\(^{-}\) \(\rightarrow\) \(^{7}\)Li + \(\nu_e\), the neutrino and recoil energies in the parent rest frame are \(E_{i(Ri)} = (M^2 - (+) m^2 + (-) m_i^2)/(2M)\), where \(M\) is the parent mass, \(m\) the recoil mass, and \(m_i\) the neutrino eigenmass.
- The oscillation wavelength in the neutrino-recoil separation coordinate in the laboratory frame is approximately \(1/\lambda_{ij}' \simeq (\Delta m^2_{ij}/(4\pi \hbar c)) \cdot (1/(\gamma M)) \cdot ((M^2+m^2)/(M^2-m^2))\), dropping terms of order \(m_i^4\).
- In terms of the neutrino beam energy \(E_0'\) (the energy massless neutrinos would have), this becomes \(1/\lambda_{ij}' \simeq (\Delta m^2_{ij}/(4\pi \hbar c)) \cdot ((1+\beta)/E_0') \cdot ((M^2+m^2)/(2M^2))\).
- The standard oscillation wavelength is \(1/\lambda_{ij}' = \Delta m^2_{ij}/(4\pi \hbar c E_0')\).
- In the parent rest frame, the relation between neutrino and recoil separation is \(-X+x \simeq x \cdot (2M^2/(M^2+m^2))\), neglecting neutrino masses.

## Context

This work extends the entangled-state treatment of neutrino oscillations previously considered by Cohen (2008), Dolgov (1997), Burkhardt (2003, 1998), Lowe (1996), and others, who generally obtained the standard oscillation formulae. The paper resolves a difficulty noted by Akhmedov and Smirnov (2009) regarding the inability to assign both well-defined energy and momentum to a propagating neutrino superposition, and shows that the entangled-state approach yields a group velocity equal to the classical particle velocity. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of why the standard oscillation wavelength remains valid despite the different fundamental periodicity in the neutrino-recoil system, and it clarifies that recoil oscillations would only appear under impractical conditions where the decay point is unknown.
