# Collective modes of p-wave superfluid Fermi gases in BEC phase

> The low-energy modes of a superfluid atomic Fermi gas at zero temperature are investigated. The Bose-Einstein-condensate (BEC) side of the superfluid phase is studied in detail. The atoms are assumed to be in only one internal state, so that for a sufficiently diluted gas the pairing of fermions can be considered effective in the l=1 channel only. In agreement with previous works on p-wave superfluidity in Fermi systems, it is found that the $p_x+ip_y$ phase represents the lowest energy state in both the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) and BEC sides. Our calculations show that at low energy three branches of collective modes can emerge, with different species of dispersion relations: a phonon-like mode, a single-particle-like mode and a gapped mode. A comparison with the Bogoliubov excitations of the corresponding spinor Bose condensate is made. They reproduce the dispersion relations of the excitation modes of the p-wave superfluid Fermi gas to a high accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the BEC phase of a p-wave superfluid Fermi gas, three branches of collective modes emerge (phonon-like, single-particle-like, and gapped), and that these dispersion relations are reproduced to high accuracy by the Bogoliubov excitations of a corresponding spinor Bose condensate. The hedge is that this analysis is performed only in the BEC phase, because in the BCS side the collective modes are expected to be strongly damped due to the vanishing threshold for pair-breaking.

## Method

The authors use a fermion-only model with a schematic p-wave interaction (V₁(k,k') = -λ k k' for k,k' < k_c) and apply a saddle-point approximation to the effective action obtained via a Stratonovich-Hubbard transformation. They solve the gap equation and density equation at zero temperature for the p_x+ip_y phase, then expand the effective action to second order in fluctuations of the pairing field to derive the propagator and determine collective mode dispersion relations from the poles of the propagator. The results are compared with the Bogoliubov excitations of a spinor Bose condensate described by time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii equations.

- For scaled cut-off k_c/k_F = 10 and 1/(k_F³v₁) = 10: scaled energy gap Δ̃₀ = 0.821, chemical potential μ = -4.28 ε_F, energy per particle E/N = -4.34 ε_F.
- For k_c/k_F = 20: dimensionless boson coupling constants Γ₀ = 0.135 and Γ₁ = -0.045.
- For k_c/k_F = 10: dimensionless boson coupling constants Γ₀ = 0.349 and Γ₁ = -0.116.
- Phase velocity of the phonon-like mode in units of Fermi velocity: 0.150 for k_c/k_F = 20 and 0.241 for k_c/k_F = 10.
- The difference between phase velocities for two orthogonal propagation directions is less than 1% on average.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of p-wave superfluidity in Fermi systems, particularly the work of Anderson and Morel (1961) on liquid ³He and the study of collective modes in the p_x phase by Iskin and collaborators (2006). The authors go beyond prior work by considering the p_x+ip_y phase (the true ground state) and by including amplitude fluctuations of the pairing field. The results enable a direct mapping between a p-wave superfluid Fermi gas in the BEC phase and a spinor Bose condensate, which may help experimental identification of the BEC regime in p-wave superfluid Fermi gases via observation of the excitation spectrum.
