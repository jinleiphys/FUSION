# Photoproduction of neutral pion pairs in the Coulomb field of the nucleus

> The total cross section for Coulomb photoproduction of neutral pion pairs in the reaction gamma A --> pi0 pi0 A is estimated within the effective chiral lagrangian approach. The amplitude of gamma gamma* --> pi0 pi0 with one off-shell photon is calculated at O(p^6) in the momentum expansion; in addition, nuclear absorption is taken into account. Besides its experimental feasibility, the results of the calculation demonstrate that the reaction gamma A --> pi0 pi0 A is a powerful source of information on the process gamma gamma --> pi0 pi0 close to threshold.

## Key claim

The reaction gamma A -> pi0 pi0 A can serve as a powerful source of information on the elementary process gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0 near threshold, provided that nuclear absorption and the off-shellness of the virtual photon are taken into account, and that the background from double pion photoproduction on nucleons is suppressed by using an isoscalar target.

## Method

The amplitude for gamma gamma* -> pi0 pi0 with one off-shell photon was calculated at O(p^6) in chiral perturbation theory, including tree-level, one-loop, and factorizable two-loop diagrams. The nuclear Coulomb photoproduction cross section was computed using Monte Carlo techniques, incorporating a nuclear form factor that treats the nucleus as a completely absorbing sphere and including off-shell corrections for the virtual photon. The background from the reaction gamma N -> pi0 pi0 N via baryon resonance exchange was estimated and shown to be strongly suppressed for isoscalar targets.

## Key numbers

- For carbon (Z=6) with incident photon energy epsilon <= 4 GeV and momentum transfer |q2| <= 200 MeV, the total cross section for gamma A -> pi0 pi0 A is approximately 40 pb.
- For silicon (Z=14) under the same conditions, the total cross section is approximately 120 pb.
- The effective mass of the pi pi system varies in the range 4 m_pi^2 <= m_pipi^2 <= 2 epsilon q_max for momentum transfer cutoff q_max << epsilon.
- The correction term Delta F_A to the nuclear form factor does not exceed 10% of the total.
- The coupling constant difference (tilde_g^N_gamma - tilde_g^N_sigma) is 0.157 for the proton and -0.136 for the neutron.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Bel'kov et al. (1995), which used a one-loop amplitude at O(p^4) and did not include nuclear absorption or off-shell corrections. The present calculation improves upon that by including O(p^6) contributions from chiral perturbation theory, off-shell corrections for the Coulomb virtual photon, and nuclear structure and absorption effects. The results enable experimental investigation of the gamma gamma -> pi0 pi0 process near threshold using existing photon beams, complementing measurements from the Crystal Ball Collaboration and the physics programme at DA Phi NE.
