# Two-Photon-Exchange Correction to Parity-Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> Higher-order QED effects play an important role in precision measurements of nucleon elastic form factors in electron scattering. Here we introduce a two-photon exchange QED correction to the parity-violating polarization asymmetry of elastic electron-proton scattering. We calculate this correction in the parton model using the formalism of generalized parton distributions, and demonstrate that it can reach several per cent in certain kinematics, becoming comparable in size with existing experimental measurements of strange-quark effects in the proton neutral weak current.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange correction to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering can reach several per cent in certain kinematics, becoming comparable in size with existing experimental measurements of strange-quark effects in the proton neutral weak current. The correction is both Q-squared and epsilon dependent, increasing toward backward electron scattering angles (small epsilon) and reaching above one per cent.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon exchange correction to the parity-violating polarization asymmetry using a parton model formalism with generalized parton distributions (GPDs). They separated the photon phase space in the 4-dimensional loop integral into soft and hard regions using a gauge-invariant procedure, treating the soft part via a low-energy theorem and the hard part using GPDs to describe quark emission and reabsorption by the nucleon. The calculation was restricted to momentum transfers Q-squared greater than M-squared (proton mass squared) and not extended to small epsilon values due to model limitations.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction increases the magnitude of A_PV by above 1 per cent at certain kinematics (backward angles, small epsilon)
- At Q-squared = 5 GeV-squared, the correction as a function of epsilon reaches above one per cent
- The correction was calculated for Q-squared values of 2 GeV-squared, 5 GeV-squared, and 9 GeV-squared
- A 1 per cent increase in the magnitude of A_PV results in about the same percentage decrease in the magnitude of the extracted G_Mp^Z, making the extracted strange G_M^s more positive by about 1 per cent of the value of G_Mp^Z
- At backward angles, the correction changes the extracted Delta s by about 10 per cent the value of G_A(Q-squared)
- The Born asymmetry formula uses G_F Q-squared / (4 pi alpha sqrt(2)) as the prefactor

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-photon exchange corrections that were previously studied for the Rosenbluth/polarization controversy in proton form factor measurements (Chen 2004, Blunden 2003). The authors note that while the possibility of such radiative corrections was mentioned in the literature (Musolf 1999), no general or model-based analysis had been done previously. The results call for an update of model corrections to the proton neutral weak current from the gamma-Z box and an extension of model calculations to lower momentum transfer (Q-squared less than 1 GeV-squared), and suggest that electron versus positron scattering comparisons on spin-zero targets like helium-4 could help constrain these corrections experimentally.
