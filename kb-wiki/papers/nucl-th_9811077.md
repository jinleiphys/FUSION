# Pairing in low-density Fermi gases

> We consider pairing in a dilute system of Fermions with a short-range interaction. While the theory is ill-defined for a contact interaction, the BCS equations can be solved in the leading order of low-energy effective field theory. The integrals are evaluated with the dimensional regularization technique, giving analytic formulas relating the pairing gap, the density, and the energy density to the two-particle scattering length.

# Pairing in low-density Fermi gases

## Key claim

The BCS gap equation for a dilute Fermi gas with a short-range interaction can be solved analytically using dimensional regularization within low-energy effective field theory, yielding finite results that relate the pairing gap, density, and energy density to the two-particle scattering length, but only in the leading-order low-density limit where the contact interaction is the dominant term, and the theory is ill-defined for a pure contact interaction without regularization.

## Method

The authors start from a BCS Hamiltonian with a contact interaction and derive the gap equation, density equation, and energy density equation. They subtract the divergent integral defining the scattering length from the divergent gap integral to obtain a convergent master equation, then evaluate the remaining convergent integral using dimensional regularization and Legendre functions. The same technique is extended to include the effective range correction in an appendix, where a quadratic momentum-dependent gap ansatz is used and the coupling constants are renormalized by matching to the low-energy scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For large values of \(k_F a\), the pairing gap approaches \(\Delta \approx 1.16\lambda\) (where \(\lambda\) is the chemical potential).
- For small \(k_F a\), the gap is exponentially small: \(\Delta = (8/e^2)\lambda \exp(-\pi/(2 k_F |a|))\).
- For neutron matter, the scattering length is \(a = -18.8\) fm and the effective range is \(r_0 = 2.75\) fm.
- At \(|k_F a| \approx 1\) (density \(N/V \approx 5 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\)), pairing lowers the energy by about 3% compared to noninteracting neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the low-density expansion framework of Abrikosov, Gorkov, and Dzyaloshinski (1963), who noted the pairing singularity was logarithmic and unimportant for integrated quantities, by providing a quantitative analytic treatment of pairing within effective field theory. The approach complements numerical potential-model studies of pairing in nuclear and neutron matter by Wambach (1989), Baldo (1990), Chen (1993), Takatsuka (1993), Khodel (1996), Carlson (1997), and Elgaroy (1998). The results enable model-independent calculations of pairing effects in dilute fermionic systems such as neutron matter and gaseous \(^3\)He, and provide a baseline for including higher-order corrections from effective range and pions.
