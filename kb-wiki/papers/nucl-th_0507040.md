# Evolution of Observables in a Numerical Kinetic Model

> The numerical solutions of the nonrelativistic and relativistic Boltzmann equations have been studied at various initial conditions. Particularly, the known analytical solution of the nonrelativistic Boltzmann equation at spherically symmetric Gaussian initial conditions has been recovered and the corresponding conservation of particle spectra and correlation functions has been confirmed. Similar conservation properties have been found also for a more general class of solutions describing the nonrelativistic and relativistic systems at anisotropic initial conditions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a specific class of initial conditions (spherically symmetric Gaussian) the nonrelativistic Boltzmann equation yields time-independent particle spectra and correlation functions, and that similar approximate conservation properties hold for more general anisotropic and relativistic initial conditions, though the conservation is exact only for the spherically symmetric Gaussian case.

## Method

The authors implemented a Monte Carlo cascade algorithm (UKM-R) to solve the nonrelativistic and relativistic Boltzmann equations numerically, considering only elastic binary collisions of identical spin-0 bosons with constant cross sections. They initialized the system with various spatial and momentum distributions (spherically symmetric Gaussian, uniformly populated parallelepiped, nonthermal momentum cube) and evolved the system in time, tracking particle coordinates and momenta at different evolution times to compute spectra and two-particle correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_0 = 0.130\) GeV and initial Gaussian radius \(R_0 = 7\) fm for the spherically symmetric Gaussian test case
- Particle mass \(m = 0.938\) GeV/c\(^2\) for nonrelativistic simulations, \(m = 0.140\) GeV/c\(^2\) for relativistic simulations
- Elastic cross sections used: \(\sigma_{el} = 40\) mb and \(\sigma_{el} = 400\) mb
- For the anisotropic parallelepiped initial condition (|x| ≤ 14 fm, |y|,|z| ≤ 7 fm), the interferometry volume changed from \(177 \pm 6\) fm\(^3\) initially to \(182 \pm 6\) fm\(^3\) finally
- For the nonthermal momentum cube initial condition with r.m.s. \(\sigma = 201.6\) MeV/c, the final Gaussian fit of the \(p_x\) distribution gave \(\sigma = 202.4 \pm 0.2\) MeV/c and the transverse interferometry radius was \(R_t = 7.14 \pm 0.11\) fm

## Context

This work extends the analytical solution of the nonrelativistic Boltzmann equation for spherically symmetric Gaussian initial conditions found by Sinyukov (2000) and CCL98, confirming that the exact conservation of spectra and correlation functions holds numerically in a cascade model. The study also connects to the approximate conservation of interferometry volume observed in chemically frozen hydrodynamic evolution by Akksin (2004), and addresses the "interferometry puzzle" of approximately energy-independent correlation radii in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable testing of numerical transport models and suggest conditions under which hydrodynamic and kinetic approaches can yield equivalent observables.
