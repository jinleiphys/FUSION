# Role of Four-Quark Condensates in QCD Sum Rules

> The QCD sum rule approach to the in-medium behavior of hadrons is discussed for omega meson, nucleon and D meson. Emphasis is devoted to the impact of four-quark condensates and to order parameters of spontaneous symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper argues that four-quark condensates, rather than the chiral condensate, dominate the density dependence of hadron properties in QCD sum rules for light-quark hadrons such as the omega meson and nucleon, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific hadron channel and the Borel transformed sum rule framework where the chiral condensate is numerically suppressed by the small quark mass factor.

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion (OPE) to current-current correlators for the omega meson, nucleon, and D meson, then perform Borel transformations to obtain QCD sum rules that relate hadronic spectral functions to QCD condensates. For the omega meson and nucleon, they parameterize the unknown four-quark condensates in terms of vacuum chiral condensate squared and density-dependent corrections using dimensionless parameters kappa, and solve the sum rules to extract in-medium mass parameters and self-energies. For the D meson, they discuss the need for condensate renormalization to handle mass logarithms and mixing between condensate types.

## Key numbers

- For the omega meson, the density dependence parameter for the four-quark condensate combination is at least kappa_omega^med = 4 to counteract the Landau damping term that would otherwise push up the weighted strength.
- For the nucleon, specific values used for the density dependence parameters are kappa_s^med = 1.2, kappa_q^med = -0.4, and tilde_kappa_v^med = 0.1, entering three independent four-quark condensate combinations.
- The vacuum four-quark condensate parameter for the nucleon is kappa_q^vac = 0.8.
- The chiral condensate enters the D meson sum rule in the combination m_c * <q_bar q>, where the charm quark mass m_c acts as a magnifier of the chiral condensate.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) to finite baryon density, building on earlier in-medium studies by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and the work of Leupold (2005) on four-quark condensates. The paper specifically addresses the finding from a 2005 Physical Review Letter that omega meson in-medium modifications depend crucially on four-quark condensate combinations, and extends this analysis to the nucleon channel using the formalism of Furnstahl (1993) and to the D meson following Hayashigaki (1996) and Morath (1998). The results enable systematic investigation of multiple hadron species to disentangle the many independent four-quark condensate combinations, and suggest that chiral symmetry restoration signals may be better sought in four-quark condensates rather than the chiral condensate alone.
