# Decay theory of double giant resonances

> The decay theory of double giant resonances incorporating fluctuation contributions of the Brink-Axel type is developed. The gamma and neutron emission decay of Double Giant Dipole Resonances (DGDR) in 208Pb is discussed in connection with a recent measurement.

## Key claim

The paper develops a decay theory for double giant resonances that includes fluctuation contributions of the Brink-Axel type, arguing that the previously missing fluctuation component is essential because integrated cross sections from decay data deviate appreciably from harmonic coupled-channel calculations. The authors conclude that in 208Pb, the 2-gamma emission cross section should approximately agree with its harmonic estimate, while the neutron emission cross section reflects the summed double and hot giant dipole excitation cross section.

## Method

The authors extend the Direct plus Fluctuation (DF) model to describe the decay of double giant dipole resonances (DGDR), splitting the transition amplitude into an averaged part (from coupled-channels calculations) and a fluctuation part arising from coupling of the one-phonon state to complex background states. They perform statistical decay calculations using the code STAPRE for 208Pb, modeling the single GDR as a 1-particle-1-hole configuration at 13.4 MeV, the DGDR as a 2-particle-2-hole configuration at 26.8 MeV, and the hot dipole resonance as a 3-particle-3-hole configuration at the same excitation energy. Neutron transmission coefficients use the Becchetti-Greenless optical potential, and E1 gamma emission uses the Brink-Axel approximation with the 208Pb GDR parameters.

## Key numbers

- Level density parameter for 208Pb: a = 7.32 MeV^-1; for 207Pb: a = 8.48 MeV^-1
- Single GDR in 208Pb located at E_d = 13.4 MeV with width Gamma_d = 4 MeV
- Branching ratio for single-step statistical gamma decay of single GDR to ground state: B_{d->gamma}^{comp} = 2.7 x 10^-5
- Total branching ratio for gamma emission from single GDR: B_{d->gamma} = 1.9 x 10^-2
- Branching ratio for occupation of 208Pb ground state after statistical cascade from DGDR and hot dipole: B_{d^2->gamma-cascade}^{comp} approx B_{d^*->gamma-cascade}^{comp} approx 2.6 x 10^-9
- Branching ratio for double gamma emission from DGDR and hot dipole: B_{d^2->2gamma}^{comp} approx B_{d^*->2gamma}^{comp} approx (B_{d->gamma}^{comp})^2 approx 1.0 x 10^-9
- Branching ratio for direct neutron emission from single GDR: B_{1p-1h}^{d->n} approx 2.6 x 10^-2
- Branching ratio for direct neutron emission from DGDR: B_{d^2->n}^{dir} approx 6.0 x 10^-2
- Experimental ratio sigma_{2gamma} / sigma_{2gamma}^{harm} = 1.25(40)
- Experimental ratio sigma_n / sigma_n^{harm} = 1.06(35)
- Authors' estimate for sigma_n / sigma_n^{harm} = (sigma_dir + sigma_fl)/sigma_dir approx 1.33

## Context

This work extends the Direct plus Fluctuation model of Carlson, Canto, Cruz-Barrios, Hussein, and de Toledo Piza (1997) to the decay of double giant resonances, addressing discrepancies between harmonic coupled-channel calculations and experimental data from Boretzky et al. (1996). The theory enables distinction between double and hot giant dipole resonances through their different gamma and neutron decay signatures, showing that 2-gamma emission in 208Pb comes predominantly from the double dipole resonance while neutron emission comes about equally from both. The results are consistent with the experimental measurements of Boretzky et al. (1996) and provide a framework for analyzing future experiments on multiphonon giant resonances.
