# Negative Off-Diagonal Conductivities in a Weakly Coupled Quark Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the conductivity matrix of a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma at the leading-log order. By setting all quark chemical potentials to be identical, the diagonal conductivities become degenerate and positive, while the off-diagonal ones become degenerate but negative (or zero when the chemical potential vanishes). This means a potential gradient of a certain fermion flavor can drive backward currents of other flavors. A simple explanation is provided for this seemingly counter intuitive phenomenon. It is speculated that this phenomenon is generic and most easily measured in cold atom experiments.

## Key claim

The conductivity matrix of a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma at leading-log order is symmetric and positive definite, but when all quark chemical potentials are set equal, the off-diagonal conductivities become negative at finite chemical potential, meaning a potential gradient of one fermion flavor can drive backward currents of other flavors.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma with \(N_f\) massless quark flavors at finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potentials \(\mu_a\), using two-particle scattering amplitudes regularized by hard thermal loop propagators. They computed the conductivity matrix \(\lambda_{ab}\) at leading-log order in the strong coupling constant \(g\), working in the Landau frame and restricting to the case where all quark chemical potentials are identical (\(\mu_a = \mu\)).

## Key numbers

- The normalized diagonal conductivity \(\tilde{\lambda}_{qq} \equiv \lambda_{qq} \beta^2 g^4 \ln g^{-1}\) is positive for all \(\mu/T\) and \(N_f\) values shown.
- The normalized off-diagonal conductivity \(\tilde{\lambda}_{qq'}\) is negative for finite \(\mu/T\) and zero when \(\mu = 0\).
- The flavor-singlet eigenvalue \(\tilde{\lambda}_+ \equiv \tilde{\lambda}_{qq} + (N_f - 1)\tilde{\lambda}_{qq'}\) is positive for all cases shown.
- The flavor-non-singlet eigenvalue \(\tilde{\lambda}_- \equiv \tilde{\lambda}_{qq} - \tilde{\lambda}_{qq'}\) is positive for all cases shown.
- Results are presented for \(N_f \leq 16\) such that the system remains asymptotically free.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD approach previously applied to shear and bulk viscosities at finite chemical potential by Chen et al. (2012) to compute the conductivity matrix at leading-log order. The negative off-diagonal conductivities are a novel prediction that the authors speculate could be most easily measured in cold atom experiments, and they provide a simple physical explanation for this counter-intuitive phenomenon.
