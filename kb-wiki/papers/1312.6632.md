# Neutrino emissivity from Goldstone boson decay in magnetized neutron matter

> Neutron matter at densities somewhat above nuclear densities is believed to be superfluid due to the condensation of neutron pairs in the 3 P2 channel. This condensate breaks rotational symmetry spontaneously and leads to the existence of Goldstone bosons (angulons). We show that the coupling to magnetic fields mediated by the magnetic moment of the neutron makes angulons massive and capable of decaying into a neutrino-antineutrino pair. We compute the rate for this process and argue they become competitive with other cooling processes for temperatures around 10^7 K as long as the interior magnetic field of the star is in the B=10^15 G range or above.

## Key claim

The authors show that in magnetized neutron matter, the coupling of Goldstone bosons (angulons) to magnetic fields via the neutron magnetic moment gives the angulons a mass and allows them to decay into neutrino-antineutrino pairs; this process becomes competitive with other cooling mechanisms for interior magnetic fields of approximately 10^15 G or above and temperatures around 10^7 K.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory for the 3P2 superfluid phase of neutron matter, including the coupling of angulons to magnetic fields and to the electroweak Z0 boson. They diagonalize the quadratic action to obtain the dispersion relations of the two angulon modes, finding that one mode acquires a mass gap proportional to eB/M. They then compute the tree-level decay rate of the massive angulon into a neutrino-antineutrino pair via Z0 exchange, and integrate over phase space to obtain the neutrino emissivity as a function of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The emissivity is given by Q_ang = G_F^2 M k_F T^7 h(x) where x = (e g_N B)/(2 a M T), with a ≈ 4.81 and g_N = -1.913 (neutron anomalous magnetic moment in nuclear magnetons).
* The function h(x) is well approximated by h(x) ≈ 0.000042 x^7 e^{-x}.
* The angulon emissivity peaks at x ≈ 7, corresponding to T ≈ (eB)/(2aM) × 7 ≈ 3 × 10^7 K × B_15 where B_15 = B/(10^15 G).
* The volume fraction of the star with sizable magnetic fields is approximately 0.04 B_15 (0.1 n_0)/n_p, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is nuclear saturation density and n_p is the proton density.
* The condensate parameter r changes slightly to r = -1/2 + (0.017 B_15)^2 due to the magnetic field, a correction negligible for B ≲ 10^17 G.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of angulon properties in the 3P2 superfluid phase by Bedaque (2003) and the effective field theory development by Bedaque (2012), where angulon-angulon bremsstrahlung was found to be proportional to T^9 and small. The angulon decay process complements the well-studied Cooper pair breaking and formation (PBF) process (Flowers, 1976; Leinson, 2011), which is only effective near the critical temperature. The PBF emissivity is much larger than the angulon emissivity at temperatures near the gap Δ_0, but the angulon process dominates at lower temperatures around T ∼ eB/M. The mechanism requires strong interior magnetic fields (∼10^15 G) such as those found in magnetars, and a proper assessment of its impact on neutron star cooling requires realistic cooling simulations.
