# Probing Hadronic Structure with The Decay $Δ\rightarrow Nl^+l^-$

> We compute the branching ratio for $Δ\rightarrow Ne^+e^-$ and $Δ\rightarrow Nμ^+μ^-$ in chiral perturbation theory and find that both decays should be observable at CEBAF. With sufficiently low thresholds on the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass a branching ratio of $\sim 10^{-5}$ may be observed for $Δ\rightarrow Ne^+e^-$. For the $Δ\rightarrow Nμ^+μ^-$ decay mode we predict a branching ratio of $3\times 10^{-7}$. The dependence of the M1 and E2 amplitudes on the momentum transfer will provide a useful test of chiral perturbation theory which predicts $\sim 20\%$ variation over the allowed kinematic range.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory predicts that the decays Δ → N e⁺ e⁻ and Δ → N μ⁺ μ⁻ should be observable at CEBAF, but the branching ratio for the electron mode depends critically on achieving a sufficiently low threshold on the e⁺ e⁻ invariant mass to suppress soft QED backgrounds.

## Method

The authors compute the decay rates for Δ → N l⁺ l⁻ using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) at one-loop order, extending their previous calculations of the M1 and E2 amplitudes for the real-photon decay Δ → N γ. They retain the s-dependence (lepton-pair invariant mass) of the kaon and pion loop graphs to obtain the off-shell behavior of the radiative vertex, then integrate the differential decay rate over the allowed kinematic range from 4 m_l² to Δ m².

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for Δ → N e⁺ e⁻: ∼ 10⁻⁵ (with sufficiently low threshold on e⁺ e⁻ invariant mass)
- Branching ratio for Δ → N μ⁺ μ⁻: 3 × 10⁻⁷ (at minimum invariant mass 4 m_μ²)
- Branching ratio for Δ → N e⁺ e⁻ at minimum invariant mass 4 m_e²: approximately 5 × 10⁻⁵
- Chiral perturbation theory predicts ∼ 20% variation in the M1 amplitude over the entire allowed kinematic range of s
- Systematic uncertainty from one-loop truncation: ∼ 30%
- Additional uncertainty from coupling constants: approximately 20%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HBχPT calculations of strong and electromagnetic decays of the baryon decuplet (Butler, Savage, Springer 1992) and the E2/M1 mixing ratio for Δ → N γ (Butler, Savage, Springer 1993). It also complements lattice QCD studies of octet-to-decuplet electromagnetic transitions (Leinweber, Draper, Woloshyn 1992). The predicted s-dependence of the M1 amplitude provides a test of chiral perturbation theory, and the same analysis could be applied to Σ* → Σ l⁺ l⁻ and Ξ* → Ξ l⁺ l⁻ decays, though CEBAF is unlikely to produce enough strange resonances for such measurements.
