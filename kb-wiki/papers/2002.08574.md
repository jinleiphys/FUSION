# Elliptic flow splitting between protons and antiprotons from hadronic potentials

> The difference in elliptic flow $v_{2}$ between protons and antiprotons, produced in $^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}$ collisions at center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5-12~\text{GeV}$, is studied within a modified version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model. Two different model scenarios are compared: the cascade mode and the mean field mode which includes potential interactions for both formed and pre-formed hadrons. The model results for the elliptic flow of protons and the relative $v_{2}$ difference between protons and antiprotons obtained from the mean field mode agree with the available experimental data, while the $v_{2}$ difference is near zero for the cascade mode. Our results show that the elliptic flow splitting, observed for particles and antiparticles, can be explained by the inclusion of proper hadronic interactions. In addition, the difference in $v_{2}$ between protons and antiprotons depends on the centrality and the rapidity window. With smaller centrality and/or rapidity acceptance, the observed elliptic flow splitting is more sensitive to the beam energy, indicating a strong net baryon density dependence of the effect. We propose to confirm this splitting at the upcoming experiments from Beam Energy Scan (BES) Phase-\Rmnum{2} at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) at Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR), High Intensity heavy ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) and Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA).

# Elliptic flow splitting between protons and antiprotons from hadronic potentials

## Key claim

The elliptic flow splitting between protons and antiprotons in Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies 5–12 GeV can be explained entirely by hadronic interactions (mean-field potentials for both formed and pre-formed hadrons) within the UrQMD model, without requiring partonic degrees of freedom. The splitting is strongly dependent on net baryon density, as evidenced by its sensitivity to centrality and rapidity windows, and disappears in pure cascade simulations where no potentials are present.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model in two modes: a cascade mode (UrQMD/C, version 2.3) with only binary scattering, and a mean-field mode (UrQMD/M) that includes density- and momentum-dependent potentials for both formed hadrons and pre-formed hadrons (string fragments). More than 10 million events were simulated for each energy and each mode for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5–12 GeV. The elliptic flow v2 was computed from the Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal particle distribution relative to the reaction plane, with results compared to experimental data from E895, NA49, E877, CERES, and STAR.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 5–12 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Nuclear incompressibility used in the potential: K = 314 MeV, with parameters alpha = -268 MeV, beta = 345 MeV, gamma = 1.167
- Normal nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Width parameter of Gaussian wave packets: L = 2 fm^2
- For 10–40% central collisions with |y| < 0.1 and p_t < 2 GeV/c, the UrQMD/M mode reproduces the experimental v2 of protons and the relative v2 difference between protons and antiprotons, while the UrQMD/C mode gives near-zero v2 difference
- For 0–80% central collisions with |eta| < 1, the relative v2 difference [v2(p) - v2(anti-p)]/v2(p) from UrQMD/M quantitatively describes STAR data, while UrQMD/C gives essentially zero difference
- At energies below sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the relative v2 difference appears to saturate and does not strongly depend on beam energy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptic flow splitting observed by the STAR Collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan Phase-I (BES-I) at RHIC, where particle-antiparticle v2 splitting increases with decreasing beam energy (STAR, Phys. Rev. C 93, 014907, 2016). It provides an alternative to partonic explanations (e.g., Xu et al., Phys. Rev. C 94, 054909, 2016, which used mean-field potentials in both partonic and hadronic phases) by showing that purely hadronic potentials can reproduce the effect. The results also build on earlier UrQMD studies of elliptic flow at SPS energies (Li et al., Phys. Rev. C 74, 064908, 2006) and pre-formed hadron potential effects (Li et al., Phys. Lett. B 659, 525, 2008). The authors propose that future measurements at BES Phase-II at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7–19.6 GeV), CBM at FAIR (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7–4.9 GeV), and NICA (sqrt(s_NN) = 4–11 GeV) can further test the net baryon density dependence of the splitting.
