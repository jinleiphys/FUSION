# Nuclear transparency and shadowing of the two-step process in the $A(e,e'ŽĆ^+)$ reaction

> We investigate nuclear transparency induced by pion electroproduction on nuclei, $A(e,e'ŽĆ^+)$, with our interest in the role of the two-step process in the Glauber multiple scattering theory. Based on the framework in which the quantum diffusion model is incorporated into the Glauber theory, the experimental data in the range of photon virtuality $0.5< Q^2< 10$ GeV$^2/c^2$ are analyzed, including the recent JLab data at the 6 GeV electron beam together with the proposal for the 12 GeV upgrade. Application of the quantum diffusion model during the formation length of the quark/antiquark $(q\bar{q})$ structure of a pion reproduces the increase in the nuclear transparency up to high $Q^2$, showing evidence of color transparency. In contrast, through the coherent length of the $╬│^*$ fluctuation into $Žü^0$, the two-step process with the $ŽüN$ scattering cross section chosen to be $Žā_{ŽüN}=Žā_{ŽĆN}$ contributes to a reduction of the nuclear transparency in the whole range of $Q^2$. Combined with the one-step process (direct photon coupling) usually adopted in the original formulation of the Glauber theory, this reaction mechanism provides another source of the $Q^2$ dependence of the transparency at low virtualities through the shadowing in the initial state interaction. By the further absorption due to the shadowing, a better agreement is obtained with the pion transparency measured in the $A(e,e'ŽĆ^+)$ reaction for $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au nuclei. A discussion of the $Q^2$ dependence of the parameter $╬▒$ for the transparency $T_A=A^{╬▒-1}$ is given.

# Nuclear transparency and shadowing of the two-step process in the $A(e,e'\pi^+)$ reaction

## Key claim

The increase in nuclear transparency with photon virtuality $Q^2$ (evidence of color transparency) is reproduced by the quantum diffusion model, but this overestimates the JLab data for heavier nuclei; introducing a two-step process where the virtual photon fluctuates into a $\rho^0$ meson provides additional absorption via shadowing in the initial state interaction, yielding better agreement with experimental data without refitting the quantum diffusion model parameters.

## Method

The authors incorporate the quantum diffusion model (QDM) into the Glauber multiple scattering theory for pion electroproduction on nuclei, replacing the free-space $\pi N$ cross section with an effective cross section that depends on the path length of the $q\bar{q}$ pair during its formation length. They additionally include a two-step process in which the virtual photon fluctuates into a $\rho^0$ meson before interacting with the nucleon, with the $\rho N$ scattering cross section set equal to $\sigma_{\pi N}$, and the shadowing effect depends on the coherence length $l_c = 2\nu/(Q^2+m_\rho^2)$. The nuclear transparency $T_A = \sigma_A/(A\sigma_N)$ is calculated using the Wood-Saxon nuclear density distribution for four target nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Photon virtuality range analyzed: $0.5 < Q^2 < 10$ GeV$^2/c^2$, covering JLab 6 GeV data ($1.1 \leq Q^2 \leq 4.69$ GeV$^2/c^2$) and predictions for 12 GeV upgrade ($Q^2$ up to 9.5 GeV$^2/c^2$)
- Coherence length $l_c$ ranges from 0.35 fm (at $Q^2=4.69$ GeV$^2/c^2$) to 0.9 fm (at $Q^2=0.55$ GeV$^2/c^2$)
- Formation length $l_h$ ranges from 1.46 fm (at $Q^2=0.55$ GeV$^2/c^2$) to 5.31 fm (at $Q^2=9.5$ GeV$^2/c^2$)
- $\pi N$ total cross section values used: 30 mb (low $Q^2$ region), 28 mb (intermediate $Q^2$), 25 mb (high $Q^2$ region)
- Parameter $\alpha$ from $T_A = A^{\alpha-1}$ extracted from JLab data: $0.78 \leq \alpha(Q^2) \leq 0.83$, showing $Q^2$ dependence
- Nuclear targets studied: $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au
- QDM parameters: $n=2$ valence quarks, $\langle k_t^2 \rangle^{1/2} \simeq 0.35$ GeV$/c$, $\Delta M^2 = 0.7$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends previous Glauber theory calculations for $A(e,e'\pi^+)$ reactions by Larson et al. and Cosyn et al., who incorporated the quantum diffusion model but overestimated JLab data from Clasie and Qian for heavier nuclei. The authors introduce the two-step process (virtual photon fluctuation into $\rho^0$) originally formulated by Yennie (1970) and Bauer (1978), which had been neglected in prior studies of this reaction because the coherence length ($0.35 \leq l_c \leq 0.8$ fm) is smaller than in $A(e,e'\rho^0)$ reactions. The approach differs from Das (who included short-range correlations) by focusing on shadowing rather than refitting QDM parameters. The results enable predictions for the JLab 12 GeV upgrade experiments proposed by Dutta et al. and provide theoretical guidance for analyzing electron-nuclear reactions.
