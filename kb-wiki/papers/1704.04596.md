# Spontaneous Spin Polarization due to Tensor Selfenergies in Quark Matter

> We study the magnetic properties of quark matter in the NJL model with the tensor interaction. The spin-polarized phase given by the tensor interaction remains even when the quark mass is zero, while the phase given by the axial vector interaction disappears. There are two kinds of spin-polarized phases: one appears in the chiral-broken phase, and the other appears in the chiral-restored phase where the quark mass is zero. The latter phase can appear independently of the strength of the tensor interaction.

## Key claim

The tensor-type spin polarization (T-type SP) in quark matter can persist even when the quark mass is zero (chiral-restored phase), whereas the axial-vector-type spin polarization (AV-type SP) disappears in that limit; the chiral-restored spin-polarized phase (SP-II) can appear independently of the strength of the tensor interaction, though its critical density depends on the coupling.

## Method

The authors studied spontaneous spin polarization in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with an additional tensor interaction channel, using the mean-field approximation at zero temperature for flavor-symmetric two-flavor quark matter. They solved the gap equations for the dynamical quark mass and the tensor mean-field self-consistently, using the proper time regularization for the vacuum part of the scalar density while omitting the vacuum contribution to the tensor density due to regularization ambiguities. Two parameter sets (PM1 and PM2) from prior work (NT05) were employed, and the vector interaction was tuned to make the chiral transition second order.

## Key numbers

* Critical density for SP-I (chiral-broken spin-polarized phase) appears at \(\rho_B \approx 1.9 \rho_0\) when \(-G_T M_N^2 = 13.8\) (\(-G_T/G_s = 0.949\)) with PM1, and at \(\rho_B \approx 0.96 \rho_0\) when \(-G_T M_N^2 = 21.0\) (\(-G_T/G_s = 0.819\)) with PM2.
* SP-II (chiral-restored spin-polarized phase) critical density becomes lower than the chiral phase transition density when \(-G_T M_N^2 > 17.7\) (\(G_T/G_s < -1.21\)) with PM1 and when \(-G_T M_N^2 > 27.6\) (\(G_T/G_s < -1.07\)) with PM2.
* The tensor density in the massless limit is \(\rho_T(D) = - (N_d / 12\pi) e_F^3 \neq 0\), where \(N_d = 6\).
* Normal nuclear density used for normalization: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin polarization in quark matter using axial-vector interactions (Tatsumi 2000, NT05, MNT) by focusing on the tensor interaction channel, which had been less explored because it does not arise from Fierz transformation of the one-gluon-exchange interaction. The authors show that the tensor channel produces a spin-polarized phase that can exist in the chiral-restored phase, unlike the axial-vector case studied by Maedan (2007) and others, and that it can contribute more strongly to magnetization. This enables future studies of the stability of the coexisting SP-I and SP-II phases and their relation to magnetic field generation in compact stars, though the present model cannot determine which phase is energetically favored due to the omission of the vacuum tensor contribution.
