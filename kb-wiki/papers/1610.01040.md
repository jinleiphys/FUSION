# The N/D method with non-perturbative left-hand-cut discontinuity and the $^1S_0$ $NN$ partial wave

> In this letter we deduce an integral equation that allows to calculate the exact left-hand-cut discontinuity for an uncoupled $S$-wave partial-wave amplitude in potential scattering for a given finite-range potential. The results obtained from the $N/D$ method for the partial-wave amplitude are rigorous, since now the discontinuities along the left-hand cut and right-hand cut are exactly known. This solves the open question with respect to the $N/D$ method and the effect on the final result of the non-perturbative iterative diagrams in the evaluation of $Δ(A)$. A big advantage of the method is that short-range physics (corresponding to integrated out degrees of freedom within low-energy Effective Field Theory) does not contribute to $Δ(A)$ and it manifests through the extra subtractions that are implemented within the method. We show the equivalence of the $N/D$ method and the Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation for a nonsingular $^1S_0$ $NN$ potential (Yukawa potential). The equivalence between the $N/D$ method with one extra subtraction and the LS equation renormalized with one counter term or with subtractive renormalization also holds for the singular attractive higher-order ChPT potentials. The $N/D$ method also allows to evaluate partial-wave amplitudes with a higher number of extra subtractions, that we fix in terms of shape parameters within the effective range expansion. The result at NNLO shows that the $^1S_0$ phase shifts might be accurately described once the electromagnetic and charge symmetry breaking terms are included. Our present results can be extended to higher partial waves as well as to coupled channel scattering.

## Key claim

The authors derive an integral equation that yields the exact left-hand-cut discontinuity for an uncoupled S-wave partial-wave amplitude in potential scattering, thereby solving the long-standing question of how non-perturbative iterative diagrams affect the N/D method's input. The method is shown to be equivalent to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for regular potentials and, with one extra subtraction, also equivalent to the renormalized Lippmann-Schwinger equation for singular attractive higher-order Chiral Perturbation Theory potentials.

## Method

The authors use the N/D method, which factorizes the partial-wave T-matrix as T(A)=N(A)/D(A) such that N(A) contains only the left-hand cut and D(A) only the right-hand cut. They derive an integral equation for the left-hand-cut discontinuity Δ(A) by summing iterated one-pion-exchange contributions to all orders, obtaining a closed-form integral equation for the singlet 1S0 partial wave. The resulting Δ(A) is then used as input in N/D integral equations with varying numbers of subtractions, where subtraction constants are fixed by matching to the effective range expansion parameters (scattering length a, effective range r, and shape parameter v2).

## Key numbers

- For the unphysical value g_A=6.80, the non-perturbative Δ(A) from the integral equation yields effective range parameters a_s=-23.75 fm, r=8.90 fm, v2=18.7 fm^3 for the N/D_11 case, matching the Lippmann-Schwinger result
- The 1S0 antibound state energy from the N/D method with one subtraction is -0.070 MeV at LO, -0.067 MeV at NLO and NNLO, and -0.066 MeV with more than one subtraction in all cases
- For the physical LO ChPT potential, the N/D method uses a=-23.75 fm, r=2.66 fm, and v2=-0.63 fm^3 to fix subtraction constants
- At NNLO, the low-energy constants used are c1=-0.74 GeV^{-1}, c3=-3.61 GeV^{-1}, and c4=2.44 GeV^{-1} from πN Roy-Steiner equations

## Context

This work extends the N/D method approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering previously pursued by Oller et al. (2016, 2015, 2014) using perturbative left-hand-cut discontinuities from Chiral Perturbation Theory up to NNLO. It resolves the open question regarding the effect of non-perturbative iterative diagrams on the discontinuity Δ(A), which had been approximated perturbatively in earlier N/D calculations. The method enables regularization-independent calculations for singular attractive potentials, going beyond the limitations of momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation solutions with counter terms (as studied by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2015)), and can be extended to higher partial waves and coupled-channel scattering.
