# Thermoelectric Conduction in General Relativity: A Causal, Stable, and Well Posed Theory

> We present a covariantly stable first-order framework for describing charge and heat transport in isotropic rigid media embedded in curved spacetime. Working in the Lorenz gauge, we show that the associated initial value problem is both causal and locally well-posed in the fully nonlinear regime. We then apply such framework to explore a range of gravitothermoelectric effects in metals undergoing relativistic acceleration. These include (1) the separation of charge through acceleration, (2) the non-uniformity of Joule heating across accelerating circuits due to time dilation, and (3) the effect of redshift on magnetic diffusion. As an astrophysical application, we derive a relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation governing the charge distribution inside a compact object, also accounting for Seebeck charge displacements driven by cooling.

## Key claim
The paper presents a first-order relativistic thermoelectric framework that is causal, stable, and locally well-posed in the fully nonlinear regime, provided the Lorenz gauge is adopted and the transport coefficients satisfy the condition σ₁κ₃ − σ₃κ₁ ≠ 0 everywhere. The abstract's claim of "covariantly stable" is qualified by the requirement that the theory be causal and that stability in the rest frame implies covariant stability.

## Method
The author constructs a first-order constitutive theory for charge and heat transport in Born-rigid media by starting from the most general isotropic linear response to gradients of the redshifted chemical potential and temperature, then using field redefinitions of T and μ to eliminate four of the eight transport coefficients (σ₂, σ₄, κ₂, κ₄). The resulting system, combined with Maxwell's equations in the Lorenz gauge, is shown to reduce to a set of wave equations ∇_μ∇^μΨ = ℱ(Ψ,∇_αΨ,x^α) for Ψ = {A^ν, μ, T}, which guarantees well-posedness via standard theorems. The entropy current is constructed to first order, and Onsager's principle is applied to relate the cross-coefficients.

## Key numbers
- The condition for well-posedness is σ₁κ₃ − σ₃κ₁ ≠ 0 everywhere.
- The second law requires σ₁ > 0 and κ₃ − μσ₃ − Tσ₃²/σ₁ > 0, the latter being interpretable as positive heat conductivity.
- In the magnetic diffusion equation for a Rindler rocket, the stationary solutions take the form ℬ^y = c₁ + c₂/z, with the true equilibrium corresponding to uniform redshifted field gzℬ^y = const.
- For the charged star application, the compactness is set to C = 0.33 and the Debye-like length parameter b = 0.1 R_⋆.

## Context
This work extends the Benfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) strategy (Bemfica 2019, Kovtun 2019, Bemfica 2020) to thermoelectricity, providing the first causal and well-posed relativistic generalization of the standard solid-state thermoelectric theory found in Landau and Lifshitz (Vol. 8) and Sommerfeld. It generalizes earlier gravitoelectric studies by Tolman and Stewart (1916), Anandan (1984), and the Schiff-Barnhill effect (1966), and enables rigorous modeling of charge distributions in compact objects via a relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation, superseding ad hoc charge profiles used by Ray (2003, 2004), Negreiros (2009), and others.
