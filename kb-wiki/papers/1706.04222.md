# Finite temperature pairing re-entrance in drip-line $^{48}$Ni nucleus

> Finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using Skyrme interactions and Relativistic Hartree-Fock effective Lagrangians, predicts $^{48}$Ni as being a possible candidate for the finite temperature pairing re-entrance phenomenon. For this proton-drip-line nucleus, proton resonant states are expected to contribute substantially to pairing correlations and the two predicted critical temperatures are $T_{c1}\sim 0.08-0.2$~MeV and $T_{c2}\sim 0.7-0.9$~MeV. It is also shown that pairing re-entrance modifies the proton single particle energies around the Fermi level, as well as occupation numbers, and quasi-particle levels. The understanding of pairing re-entrance in $^{48}$Ni presently challenge our understanding of exotic matter under extreme conditions.

## Key claim

Finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) theory, using both Skyrme interactions and Relativistic Hartree-Fock effective Lagrangians, predicts that the proton-drip-line nucleus $^{48}$Ni is a candidate for the finite temperature pairing re-entrance phenomenon, but only if the continuum resonant states are located at sufficiently low energy (below about 4 MeV) to be populated by thermal excitation while being sufficiently high (above about 2 MeV) such that the ground state is unpaired.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in coordinate space using the Numerov method with Dirichlet asymptotic boundary conditions, with a box size of 30 fm and a step of 0.2 fm. They employed several Skyrme interactions (SLY4, SLY5, SIII, SKMS, SKI1-5, SGII, RATP) with a mixed surface/volume pairing interaction, and two Relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangians (PKA1, PKO3) with the Gogny D1S pairing force. The pairing strength for each Skyrme interaction was adjusted to reproduce experimental two-proton separation energies and proton pairing gaps for N=20 isotones from the AME2012 mass table.

## Key numbers

- Two predicted critical temperatures for $^{48}$Ni: $T_{c1} \sim 0.08-0.2$ MeV (low-temperature boundary) and $T_{c2} \sim 0.7-0.9$ MeV (high-temperature boundary) for the pairing re-entrance domain.
- For Skyrme forces SLY5, SLY4, and SIII, the critical temperatures are $T_{c1} \sim 0.1-0.2$ MeV and $T_{c2} \sim 0.7-0.8$ MeV.
- For relativistic Lagrangians PKA1 and PKO3, the re-entrance domain extends from 0.08-0.1 MeV up to 0.7-0.9 MeV.
- The proton single-particle gap at zero temperature is 3.12 MeV for PKO3 and 3.46 MeV for PKA1.
- The lower limit for the half-life of $^{48}$Ni from experiment is about 0.5 ms.
- The saturation density used in the pairing force is $\rho_{sat}=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of finite-temperature pairing re-entrance, previously found in the neutron channel of extremely neutron-rich $^{176-180}$Sn nuclei by Margueron et al. (2012), to the proton channel of the drip-line nucleus $^{48}$Ni. The phenomenon was first predicted for hot rotating nuclei by Kammuri (1964) and Morreto (1972), and later named "pairing re-entrance" by Balian, Flocard, and Veneroni (1999). The present study systematically searched over many semi-magic and doubly magic nuclei using multiple interactions and found that $^{48}$Ni is the only currently synthesised nucleus where the phenomenon may occur in the thermal equilibrium state. The results challenge both nuclear theory and future experimental facilities such as FAIR and FRIB to investigate exotic matter under extreme conditions.
