# Equilibrium and Non-Equilibrium Hard Thermal Loop Resummation in the Real Time Formalism

> We investigate the use of the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation technique in non-equilibrium field theory. We use the Keldysh representation of the real time formalism (RTF). We derive the HTL photon self energy and the resummed photon propagator. We show that no pinch singularities appear in the non-equilibrium HTL effective propagator. We discuss a possible regularization mechanism for these singularities at higher orders. As an example of the application of the HTL resummation method within the RTF we discuss the damping rate of a hard electron.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation technique can be extended to non-equilibrium field theory using the Keldysh representation of the real time formalism, provided one assumes quasistationary distribution functions and that the time scale of the microscopic process is much smaller than the relaxation time of the background. The authors show that no pinch singularities appear in the non-equilibrium HTL effective propagator, contrary to what might be expected from naive inspection.

## Method

The authors use the Keldysh (closed time path) representation of the real time formalism, which expresses propagators and self energies in terms of retarded, advanced, and symmetric components. They compute the HTL photon self energy in QED for both equilibrium and non-equilibrium cases, then construct the resummed photon propagator via the Dyson-Schwinger equation. As an application, they calculate the damping rate of a hard electron in a QED plasma, both in equilibrium and in a chemically non-equilibrated plasma described by fugacity factors.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium thermal photon mass: \( m_\gamma = eT/3 \)
- The non-equilibrium thermal photon mass squared: \( \tilde m_\gamma^2 = \frac{4e^2}{3\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dk\, k\, f_F(k) \)
- The equilibrium longitudinal HTL photon self energy: \( \Pi_R^L(P) = -3 m_\gamma^2 \left(1 - \frac{p_0}{2p} \ln \frac{p_0+p+i\epsilon}{p_0-p+i\epsilon}\right) \)
- The equilibrium symmetric HTL photon self energy (longitudinal): \( \Pi_F^L(P) = -6\pi i\, m_\gamma^2 \frac{T}{p} \theta(p^2-p_0^2) \)
- The equilibrium symmetric HTL photon self energy (transverse): \( \Pi_F^T(P) = -3\pi i\, m_\gamma^2 \frac{T}{p} \left(1 - \frac{p_0^2}{p^2}\right) \theta(p^2-p_0^2) \)
- The equilibrium electron damping rate: \( \Gamma_{eq}(p) \simeq \frac{e^2T}{2\pi} [1-n_F(p)] \ln \frac{const}{e} \)
- The non-equilibrium electron damping rate: \( \Gamma_{neq}(p) \simeq \frac{e^2A}{4\pi} [1-f_F(p)] \ln \frac{const}{e} \), where \( A = \frac{\int_0^\infty dk\, k^2\, f_F(k)[1-f_F(k)]}{\int_0^\infty dk\, k\, f_F(k)} \)
- For a chemically non-equilibrated QED plasma with fugacity \( \lambda_F \): \( A = 2T + 0.192T(1-\lambda_F) \)
- The non-equilibrium rate relative to equilibrium: \( \Gamma_{neq}(p) = \frac{1-\lambda_F n_F(p)}{1-n_F(p)} [1+0.096(1-\lambda_F)] \Gamma_{eq}(p) \)

## Context

This work extends the Braaten-Pisarski HTL resummation method (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990), originally formulated in the imaginary time formalism for equilibrium systems, to non-equilibrium situations using the Keldysh representation. It builds on prior work by Altherr (1994) on pinch singularities in scalar field theory and by Baier et al. (1997) on photon production in chemical non-equilibrium. The formalism enables consistent calculation of observables such as damping rates, energy loss, and particle production rates in a non-equilibrium parton gas, which is relevant for describing the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions where chemical equilibration is slow compared to thermalization.
