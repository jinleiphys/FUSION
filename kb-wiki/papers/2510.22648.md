# Nucleon-nucleon scattering up to next-to-leading order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory: low phases and the deuteron

> Recently the nucleon-nucleon interaction derived using time-ordered perturbation theory in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory was shown to yield promising results for peripheral neutron-proton scattering. In this work we study low partial waves at next-to-leading order by treating the potential non-perturbatively in the scattering equation. Reasonable description of the phase shifts in the $S$ and $P$ waves as well as the deuteron properties is observed, which can be regarded as a feasibility study for the application of our formalism to the few- and many-body calculations.

# Nucleon-nucleon scattering up to next-to-leading order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory: low phases and the deuteron

## Key claim
The manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory framework, using time-ordered perturbation theory, yields a reasonable description of S- and P-wave neutron-proton phase shifts and deuteron properties at next-to-leading order when the potential is treated non-perturbatively in the scattering equation. However, the authors explicitly characterize this as a "feasibility study" and note that relativistic effects and finite-cutoff artifacts cannot yet be quantitatively disentangled.

## Method
The nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-leading order (NLO) is constructed from covariant chiral Lagrangians using time-ordered perturbation theory, including one-pion exchange and two-pion exchange contributions. The full NLO potential is iterated non-perturbatively in the Kadyshevsky scattering equation, with a Gaussian momentum-space regulator (exponent n=2) applied to both contact terms and pion-exchange potentials. Low-energy constants for S- and P-waves are determined by fitting to Nijmegen partial wave analysis phase shifts at laboratory energies up to 50 MeV, with cutoff variation from 400 to 650 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: M_π± = 139.570 MeV, M_π0 = 134.977 MeV, average M_π = 138.0 MeV
- Nucleon mass: m_N = 938.918 MeV; pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV; axial coupling: g_A = 1.29 at NLO
- 1S0 scattering length at NLO: a = -23.179 to -23.287 fm (Nijmegen PWA: -23.739 fm)
- 1S0 effective range at NLO: r = 2.548 to 2.574 fm (Nijmegen PWA: 2.680 fm)
- 3S1 scattering length at NLO: a = 5.444 to 5.451 fm (Nijmegen PWA: 5.420 fm)
- 3S1 effective range at NLO: r = 1.697 to 1.708 fm (Nijmegen PWA: 1.753 fm)
- Deuteron binding energy at NLO: E_d = -2.1716 to -2.1277 MeV (experiment: -2.2254575 MeV), deviating by approximately 2.5%
- Deuteron quadrupole moment at NLO: Q_d = 0.2731 to 0.2738 fm^2 (experiment: 0.2859 fm^2)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio at NLO: η_d = 0.0254 to 0.0255 (experiment: 0.0256)
- Deuteron D-state probability at NLO: P_D = 2.6003% to 3.5318%
- Cutoff range varied: Λ = 400 to 650 MeV

## Context
This work extends the manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to peripheral partial waves (Ren, 2022) into the non-perturbative regime of low partial waves. It builds on the modified Weinberg approach proposed by Epelbaum (2012) using the Kadyshevsky equation, and the LO study by Baru (2019) which established a renormalizable framework. The results are compared to the non-relativistic (heavy-baryon) chiral potentials developed up to fifth order by Entem (2017) and Reinert (2017). This feasibility study enables future applications to few- and many-body calculations, though the authors note that the energy dependence of the potential must first be removed for compatibility with established ab initio methods.
