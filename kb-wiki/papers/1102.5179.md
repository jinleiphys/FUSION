# Probing collectivity in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision by leptons and photons

> It has been shown that the evolution of collectivity in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision is manifested in the variation of various HBT radii with invariant mass ($M$) extracted from the correlation functions of two lepton pairs. The value of the radial velocity ($v_r$) can be estimated from the ratio of the $p_T$ distributions of single photons to lepton pairs for various $M$ windows. It has been argued that the variation of radial flow with appropriate kinematic variables can be used as an indicator of a phase transition from initially produced partons to hadrons. We also consider the elliptic flow ($v_2^{HF}$) of the matter as probed by the single electron spectra originating from the semileptonic decays of heavy mesons. The measured values of $v_2^{HF}$ and the nuclear suppression factor ($R_{\mathrm AA}$) at RHIC energy have been reproduced simultaneously by including both the collisional and radiative processes within the scope of perturbative quantum chromodynamics. The $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and $v_2^{HF}$ have been predicted for LHC energy.

## Key claim

The evolution of collectivity in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions can be probed through the non-monotonic variation of HBT radii and radial velocity with dilepton invariant mass, where the dip in $R_{\mathrm side}$ at $\langle M\rangle\sim m_\rho$ is caused by large radial flow in the hadronic phase and vanishes if radial velocity is set to zero.

## Method

The author computed Bose-Einstein correlation functions for two virtual photons (dilepton pairs) using a relativistic hydrodynamic model with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariance, evaluating the source function for lepton pairs from QGP (thermal quark annihilation) and hadronic phase (decays of thermal light vector mesons $\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$). For heavy flavor studies, the Fokker-Planck equation was solved with drag and diffusion coefficients including both collisional and radiative processes (with dead cone and LPM effects), using temperature-dependent strong coupling and Debye mass, with initial heavy quark distributions from NLO MNR code and Peterson fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature $T_i = 290$ MeV and thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.6$ fm/c for RHIC conditions
- Chemical freeze-out temperature $T_{ch} = 170$ MeV and kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_f = 120$ MeV, fixed by particle ratios and hadron $p_T$ spectra slopes
- For RHIC heavy flavor analysis: initial temperature $T_i = 400$ MeV and thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.2$ fm/c
- For LHC predictions: initial temperature $T_i = 700$ MeV and thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.08$ fm/c (for $v_2^{HF}$) or 0.1 fm/c (for $R_{\mathrm AA}$)
- $R_{\mathrm AA} < 1$ for $p_T \geq 2$ GeV at RHIC, indicating substantial heavy quark interaction with the plasma
- $v_2^{HF}$ at LHC is predicted to be similar to the value at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the use of electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) for studying collectivity in heavy ion collisions, building on prior HBT interferometry methods reviewed in the literature (HBT review) and earlier work on dilepton interferometry (Mam). The heavy flavor analysis addresses discrepancies between experimental data and theoretical descriptions that have motivated various approaches including non-perturbative contributions from quasi-hadronic bound states (HvH), 3-body scattering effects (Ko), heavy meson dissociation (Adil), running coupling constants (Gossiaux), and path length fluctuations (Wicks). The results enable using the non-monotonic variation of radial velocity with invariant mass as a signal for the parton-to-hadron phase transition, and provide predictions for LHC measurements of $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and $v_2^{HF}$ that can test the sensitivity to the equation of state.
