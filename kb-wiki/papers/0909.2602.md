# Nuclear effects in electron reactions and their impact on neutrino processes

> We suggest that superscaling in electroweak interactions with nuclei, namely the observation that the reduced electron-nucleus cross sections are to a large degree independent of the momentum transfer and of the nuclear species, can be used as a tool to obtain precise predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections in both charged and neutral current-induced processes.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that superscaling in electron-nucleus scattering, where reduced cross sections are largely independent of momentum transfer and nuclear species, can be used to obtain precise predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but this approach has limitations related to the quality of superscaling and cannot be applied at very low momentum transfer where nuclear collective effects become important.

## Method

The authors extracted two phenomenological superscaling functions from inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data, one for the quasielastic (QE) region and one for the Delta resonance region, by dividing experimental cross sections by single-nucleon cross sections and multiplying by the Fermi momentum. They then used these functions to predict charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections by multiplying the superscaling functions by the appropriate single-nucleon neutrino cross sections, an approach called the SuperScaling Approximation (SuSA). The results were compared with predictions from other models including the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG), Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA), Relativistic Mean Field (RMF), and a semi-relativistic shell model.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological QE and Delta superscaling functions were extracted from world data on inclusive electron scattering, with typical deviations of 10% or less for data sets covering the Delta region
- For charged-current neutrino scattering on 12C at 1 GeV/c neutrino momentum and 90 degree muon scattering angle, the QE and Delta peaks have roughly the same height
- The SuSA and RMF predictions for the fully integrated charged-current cross section on 12C are about 15% lower than the RFG prediction at neutrino energies above 1.2 GeV
- For neutral-current neutrino scattering on 12C at 1 GeV, the SuSA cross sections are lower by about 25% at the peak compared to RFG results
- Meson-exchange currents yield a depletion of the quasielastic peak of about 10-15%, depending on kinematics

## Context

This work extends the superscaling concept first proposed by West (1975) and developed by others (1988, 1990) for inclusive electron-nucleus scattering, and applies it to neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE and K2K/T2K. The SuSA approach minimizes model dependence by using experimental electron scattering data to extract nuclear dynamics, but it neglects meson-exchange currents and two-body correlation currents that break scaling, and fully relativistic treatments of these effects remain an open challenge. The work enables more reliable predictions for neutrino cross sections in both charged and neutral current processes, which are essential for interpreting neutrino oscillation measurements.
