# Excitation Functions of Tsallis-like Parameters in High-Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> The transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and protons produced at mid-rapidity in central nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions at high energies are analyzed by considering particles to be created from two participant partons which are assumed to be contributors from the collision system. Each participant (contributor) parton is assumed to contribute to the transverse momentum by a Tsallis-like function. The contributions of the two participant partons are regarded as the two components of transverse momentum of the identified particle. The experimental data measured in high-energy AA collisions by international collaborations are studied. The excitation functions of kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity are extracted. The two parameters increase quickly from $\approx3$ to $\approx10$ GeV (exactly from 2.7 to 7.7 GeV) and then slowly at above 10 GeV with the increase of collision energy. In particular, there is a plateau from near 10 GeV to 200 GeV in the excitation function of kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity extracted from transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and protons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions show a rapid increase from approximately 3 to 10 GeV (specifically from 2.7 to 7.7 GeV) in center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair, followed by a slower increase above 10 GeV, with a plateau in the kinetic freeze-out temperature from near 10 GeV to 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors analyzed transverse momentum and transverse mass spectra of charged pions, kaons, and protons produced at mid-rapidity in central Au-Au, Pb-Pb, and Xe-Xe collisions across a center-of-mass energy range from 2.7 GeV to 5.44 TeV. They assumed each particle is created from two participant partons, where each parton contributes to the transverse momentum according to a revised Tsallis-like function, and the two contributions are treated as perpendicular components of the final particle's transverse momentum. The experimental data from the E866, E895, E802, NA49, STAR, and ALICE Collaborations were fitted using this two-component model, and the kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity were extracted via an alternative method relating effective temperature to rest mass and mean transverse momentum to mean energy.

## Key numbers

* Kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity increase quickly from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7$ GeV to 7.7 GeV, then slowly above 10 GeV.
* A plateau in the kinetic freeze-out temperature excitation function exists from near 10 GeV to 200 GeV.
* Effective temperature values for pions range from $130 \pm 4$ MeV (at 2.7 GeV, Au-Au) to $238 \pm 12$ MeV (at 5.44 TeV, Xe-Xe) for the $\mu_i=0$ case.
* Effective temperature values for protons range from $183 \pm 4$ MeV (at 2.7 GeV, Au-Au) to $327 \pm 14$ MeV (at 5.44 TeV, Xe-Xe) for the $\mu_i=0$ case.
* Entropy index $q$ values for pions range from $1.062 \pm 0.003$ (at 2.7 GeV) to $1.140 \pm 0.002$ (at 5.44 TeV) for the $\mu_i=0$ case.
* At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV (Pb-Pb), a two-component fit was used with $k = 0.999 \pm 0.001$ for the first component contribution fraction.

## Context

This work extends the multisource thermal model approach previously applied by the same group (Li and Liu, recent work) to proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions using blast-wave models with Boltzmann-Gibbs or Tsallis statistics. The extracted excitation functions of kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity provide systematic trends that may help map the QCD phase diagram, complementing studies that typically use chemical freeze-out temperature (as in work by various authors cited in the paper). The results enable future comparisons with other models and may indicate changes in the equation of state related to the deconfinement phase transition from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma.
