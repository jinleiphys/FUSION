# A unified quark-nuclear matter equation of state from the cluster virial expansion within the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach

> We consider a cluster expansion for strongly correlated quark matter where the clusters are baryons with spectral properties that are described within the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach by a medium dependent phase shift. We employ a simple ansatz for the phase shift which fulfils the Levinson theorem by describing an on-shell bound state with an effective mass and models the continuum by an anti-bound state located at the mass of the three-quark threshold. The quark and baryon interactions are accounted for by the coupling to scalar and vector meson mean fields modelled by density functionals. At increasing density and temperature, due to the different medium-dependence of quark and baryon masses, the Mott dissociation of baryons occurs and the nuclear cluster contributions to the thermodynamics vanish. It is demonstrated on this simple example that this unified approach to quark-nuclear matter is capable of describing crossover as well as first order phase transition behaviour in the phase diagram with a critical endpoint. Changing the meson mean field, the case of a "crossover all over" in the phase diagram is also obtained.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a unified quark-nuclear matter equation of state can be obtained from a cluster virial expansion using a simplified, generic ansatz for baryon phase shifts, but the parameter choices are purely academic and a thorough parameter scan remains to be performed.

## Method
The authors employ a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach with a medium-dependent phase shift ansatz for baryons that features a step-up at the effective baryon mass (bound state) and a step-down at the three-quark continuum threshold (anti-bound state), satisfying Levinson's theorem by construction. Quark and baryon interactions are accounted for by coupling to scalar and vector meson mean fields modelled by density functionals: the string-flip model for quarks and the DD2 model for nucleons. The thermodynamic potential is obtained from a cluster virial expansion, and phase transitions are constructed using Maxwell constructions.

## Key numbers
- Pseudocritical temperature of the hadron-to-quark matter crossover at vanishing baryochemical potential from lattice QCD: \(T_\mathrm c(\mu=0)=156.5\pm 1.5\) MeV (Bazavov 2018)
- Critical endpoint of the quark-hadron transition: \(T_\mathrm C = 43\) MeV and \(\mu_\mathrm C = 909\) MeV
- Vacuum string tension parameter: \(\sqrt{D_0} = 290\) MeV
- Volume fraction parameter for critical endpoint scenario: \(\alpha = 0.15\) fm\(^6\)
- Linear vector coupling parameter: \(a = 12\) MeV fm\(^3\)
- At \(T=0\), the homogeneous hadron liquid exists for \(0.15\) fm\(^{-3} < n_\mathrm B < 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The binodal region for hadron liquid and quark-gluon plasma coexistence spans \(0.16\) fm\(^{-3} < n_\mathrm B < 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The spinodal region of mechanical instability lies at \(0.19\) fm\(^{-3} < n_\mathrm B < 0.33\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Homogeneous multi-component plasma with quarks and bound hadrons exists for \(0.4\) fm\(^{-3} < n_\mathrm B < 0.99\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Pure quark plasma appears for \(n_\mathrm B > 0.99\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Crossover-all-over scenario obtained by changing volume fraction parameter to \(\alpha = 0.10\) fm\(^6\)

## Context
This work extends the cluster virial expansion and generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Ropke 1982, Ropke 1983, Schmidt 1990) to quark matter systems, building on the formalism developed in Blaschke (2013). It contrasts with two-phase approaches (Cleymans 1985, Satz 2012) that always yield first-order phase transitions, and with van-der-Waals models (Vovchenko 2017) that do not produce a transition to quark matter. The approach enables future inclusion of a larger set of particles (diquarks, mesons, baryons) and generalization to isospin-asymmetric matter for astrophysical applications.
