# Entropy for Colored Quark States at Finite Temperature

> The quantum entropy at finite temperatures is analyzed by using models for colored quarks making up the physical states of the hadrons. We explicitly work out some special models for the structure of the states of SU(2) and SU(3) relating to the effects of the temperature on the quantum entropy. We show that the entropy of the singlet states monotonically decreases meaning that the mixing of these states continually diminishes with the temperature. It has been found that the structure of the octet states is more complex so that it can be best characterized by two parts. One part is very similar to that of the singlet states. The other one reflects the existence of strong correlations between two of the three color states. Furthermore, we work out the entropy for the {\it classical} Ising and the {\it quantum} XY spin chains. In Ising model the quantum (ground state) entropy does not directly enter into the canonical partition function. It also does not depend on the number of spatial dimensions, but only on the number of quantum states making up the ground state. Whereas, the XY spin chain has a finite entropy at vanishing temperature. The results from the spin models qualitatively analogous to our models for the states of SU(2) and SU(3).

## Key claim

The quantum entropy of colored quark states at finite temperature is analyzed using simple thermodynamical models for SU(2) and SU(3) color structures, showing that the entropy of singlet states monotonically decreases with temperature while octet states exhibit a more complex behavior with residual correlations between two of the three color states even at high temperature.

## Method

The authors construct finite-temperature reduced density matrices for single quarks in SU(2) and SU(3) color representations by introducing Boltzmann-weighted thermal factors into the ground-state density matrices. For SU(2), the thermal density matrix uses the Pauli matrix σ^z with a Boltzmann factor e^(-ε(p)/T), while for SU(3) the authors use the three complex roots of -1 as weighting factors to maintain the trace condition. The quantum entropy is then computed from the eigenvalues of these temperature-dependent density matrices using the von Neumann entropy formula S = -Tr(ρ ln ρ). Additionally, the entropy of classical Ising and quantum XY spin chains is calculated for comparison with the color models.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) single quark ground state entropy: S_q,2 = ln 2 (dimensionless)
- For SU(3) single quark singlet state entropy: S_q,3 = ln 3 (dimensionless)
- For SU(3) octet state from the eighth Gell-Mann matrix: S_o,8 = ln 3 - (1/3) ln 2 (dimensionless)
- For SU(3) at high temperature limit, the asymptotic entropy value is 0.8516 (dimensionless)
- For the 1D Ising model at zero temperature with J=0, single spin entropy: S = ln 2 (dimensionless)
- For the 1D Ising model at high temperature, entropy per spin approaches: S/N → ln 2 (dimensionless)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of ground state entropy for colored quark states in SU(3)_c (Miller, 2003) to finite temperatures, and connects to lattice QCD results for the entropy difference in quark-antiquark singlet states reported by Felix and collaborators (2003), which gave a value of 2 ln 3 at vanishing temperature. The paper also draws on von Neumann's foundational work on quantum entropy (von Neumann, 1930s) and Schrodinger's discussion of ground state degeneracy and entropy in multi-particle systems (Schrodinger, lecture series). The spin model comparisons with the Ising and XY models provide analogies for understanding color correlations, and the results may inform future studies of confinement, heavy quark potentials in lattice QCD, and the modification of bag constants in hadron models.
