# Multi-chiral facets in symmetry restored states: Five chiral doublets candidates in even-even nucleus $^{136}$Nd

> A triaxial projected shell model including configurations with more than four quasiparticles in the configuration space is developed, and applied to investigate the recently reported five chiral doublets candidates in a single even-even nucleus $^{136}$Nd. The energy spectra and transition probability ratios $B(M1)/B(E2)$ are reproduced satisfactorily. The configuration mixing along the rotational bands is studied by analyzing the intrinsic composition of the eigenfunctions. The chiral geometry of these nearly degenerate bands is examined by the \textit{K plot} and the \textit{azimuthal plot}, and the evolution from the chiral vibration to the static chirality with spin is clearly demonstrated for four pairs of partner bands. From the features in the \textit{azimuthal plot}, it is difficult to interpret the other candidate as chiral partners.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (PSM) including up to 6-quasiparticle configurations reproduces the energy spectra and B(M1)/B(E2) ratios for four of the five reported chiral doublet candidates in $^{136}$Nd, but from the features in the azimuthal plot it is difficult to interpret bands D3 and D3-C as chiral partners, despite their near-degeneracy.

## Method

A triaxial projected shell model was developed with a configuration space including up to 6-quasiparticle states built on a Nilsson mean field with pairing plus quadrupole interactions. Angular momentum projection was performed using three-dimensional projection operators, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the projected basis. The resulting eigenfunctions were analyzed using K plots (probability distributions of angular momentum components along the principal axes) and azimuthal plots (probability distributions for the orientation of the total angular momentum in the intrinsic frame) to examine the chiral geometry of the five candidate doublet bands.

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach to chiral doublet bands previously applied by Bhat et al. (2012) and Chen et al. (2017, 2018) by including configurations with more than four quasiparticles, enabling the study of the five chiral doublet candidates recently observed in $^{136}$Nd by Petrache et al. (2018). The results confirm chiral vibration-to-static chirality evolution for four band pairs, while the D3/D3-C pair shows strong configuration mixing that prevents a clear chiral interpretation, motivating future three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory studies as suggested by Zhao et al. (2017).
