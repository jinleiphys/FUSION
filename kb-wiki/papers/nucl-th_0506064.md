# The importance of strange mesons in neutron star properties

> In order to obtain the properties of compact stellar objects, appropriate equations of state have to be used. In the literature, strange meson fields, namely the scalar meson field $σ^*(975)$ and the vector meson field $ϕ(1020)$, had to be considered in order to reproduce the observed strongly attractive $ΛΛ$ interaction. The introduction of these strange mesons makes the equations of state harder (EOS) due to the repulsive effect of the $ϕ(1020)$ meson. In this work the inclusion of these mesons in the equation of state and their influence on the properties of the neutron stars are investigated.

## Key claim

The inclusion of strange meson fields (σ*(975) and φ(1020)) in the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) makes the equation of state harder at high energy densities due to the repulsive effect of the φ(1020) meson, but the resulting changes to bulk neutron star properties (maximum mass, radius, central energy density) are not very relevant compared to the effects of hyperon inclusion alone.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) at zero temperature with the lowest baryon octet (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) in β equilibrium with electrons and muons, and compared results with and without the strange meson sector (σ* and φ). They solved the mean-field equations for the meson fields, computed the equation of state (energy density and pressure), and then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star profiles.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass without strange mesons: 1.93 M_sun; with strange mesons: 1.92 M_sun
- Radius at maximum mass without strange mesons: 11.30 km; with strange mesons: 10.91 km
- Central energy density at maximum mass without strange mesons: 6.39 fm^-4; with strange mesons: 7.01 fm^-4
- Maximum radius (both cases): 13.33 km, with mass 1.39 M_sun and central energy density 1.75 fm^-4 (without) and 1.78 fm^-4 (with)
- Strange meson effects become significant at baryon densities above 5 ρ_0, where ρ_0 is the saturation density of nuclear matter
- Coupling constants used: g_σ = 8.910, g_ω = 10.626, g_ρ = 8.208; g_σ*Λ = g_σ*Σ = 5.11, g_σ*Ξ = 9.38; g_φΛ = g_φΣ = 4.31, g_φΞ = 8.62; non-linear parameters k = -6.426 × 10^-4, λ = 5.530

## Context

This work extends the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) by adding the strange meson sector (σ* and φ) as introduced by earlier studies (Schaffner et al., 1994; Greiner, 2002) to reproduce the strongly attractive ΛΛ interaction. The results show that while strange mesons harden the equation of state at high densities, their effect on neutron star bulk properties is small compared to the softening caused by hyperon inclusion. The authors note that at densities around four times saturation density, a phase transition to deconfined quarks or kaon condensates would likely be more important than the strange meson effects, and they indicate that investigations of protoneutron stars at temperatures of 30-40 MeV with trapped neutrinos are ongoing.
