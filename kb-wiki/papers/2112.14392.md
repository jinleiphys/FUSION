# Quantum kinetic theory for spin transport of quarks with background chromo-electromagnetic fields

> We derive the quantum kinetic equations for massive and massless quarks coupled with the background chromo-electromagnetic fields from the Wigner-function approach with the $\hbar$ expansion and effective power-counting scheme. For each case, one obtains coupled color-singlet and color-octet kinetic equations, which also involve the scalar and axial-vector components for the charge and spin transport. These kinetic equations delineate entangled evolution of the corresponding distribution functions decomposed in color space. At weak coupling, we derive the close form of the color-singlet kinetic equations for spin transport, which incorporates the diffusion term and the source term that triggers dynamical spin polarization led by correlation functions of color fields. Also, the non-dynamical source term is found in the axial Wigner function. The induced spin polarization and axial charge currents by these source terms are discussed under physical assumptions for color-field correlators in near-equilibrium quark gluon plasmas. In the constant-field limit, we further obtain non-vanishing axial Ward identities, from which we extract the pseudo-scalar condensate for massive quarks at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper derives quantum kinetic equations for spin transport of quarks in background chromo-electromagnetic fields, showing that color-field correlators generate source terms that can induce dynamical spin polarization, but this result relies on the assumption that collision terms are suppressed by sufficiently strong background color fields and that other sources of spin polarization (such as thermal vorticity) are negligible.

## Method

The author starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equation for quarks in QCD with background color fields, applies a Wigner-function approach with an \(\hbar\) expansion and effective power-counting scheme, and decomposes the Wigner operators into Clifford-algebra components in spinor space and into color-singlet and color-octet components in color space. The master equations are then solved perturbatively to obtain the scalar kinetic equation (SKE) and axial-vector kinetic equation (AKE), with the color-octet distributions treated as subleading at weak coupling to derive closed-form color-singlet kinetic equations for spin transport.

## Key numbers

- The collision term is estimated to be of \(\mathcal{O}(g^4 \ln g)\) at weak coupling, while the background color-field effects are of \(\mathcal{O}(g^2)\), justifying the collisionless approximation.
- The power counting assigns \(\mathcal{V}^{\mu} \sim \mathcal{O}(\hbar^0)\) and \(\mathcal{A}^{\mu} \sim \mathcal{O}(\hbar)\) for the vector and axial-vector components of the Wigner operator.
- The derivation assumes \(m \gg \mathcal{O}(\partial)\) for massive quarks, where \(m\) is the quark mass, as a technical condition for the rest-frame choice \(n^{\mu}=p^{\mu}/m\).

## Context

This work extends the axial kinetic theory (AKT) previously developed for QED by Hattori et al. (2019) and Yang et al. (2020) to QCD with color degrees of freedom, building on the Wigner-function approach of Elze et al. (1986). It generalizes the chiral kinetic theory (CKT) for massless fermions to massive quarks with color fields, and it enables future studies of dynamical spin polarization in quark-gluon plasmas where chromo-electromagnetic fields from Weibel-type instabilities may dominate over collisional effects, as proposed by Asakawa et al. (2006).
