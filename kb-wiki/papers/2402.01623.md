# Solving coupled Non-linear Schrödinger Equations via Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution

> Coupled non-linear Schrödinger equations are crucial in describing dynamics of many particle systems. We present a quantum imaginary time evolution (ITE) algorithm as a solution to such equations in the case of nuclear Hartree-Fock equations. Under a simplified Skyrme interaction model, we calculate the ground state energy of an oxygen-16 nucleus and demonstrate that the result is in agreement with the classical ITE algorithm.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum imaginary time evolution (ITE) algorithm that solves coupled non-linear Schrodinger equations for the nuclear Hartree-Fock problem, demonstrating agreement with classical ITE for oxygen-16 under a simplified Skyrme interaction, but only for small basis sizes (up to N=2 qubits); for larger bases the quantum algorithm suffers from measurement sampling errors that prevent convergence.

## Method

The authors encode the radial wave functions of the oxygen-16 nucleus in a 3D isotropic oscillator basis, storing the expansion coefficients in N-qubit target states. They implement the non-unitary ITE operator using a duality computer approach with 2N ancillary qubits, decomposing the operator into Pauli matrices and applying it iteratively for 400 steps with an imaginary time step of 0.005 MeV^-1, using the QASM backend from QISKIT with 10000 shots per measurement.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy of oxygen-16 for N=1 (2 expansion states): classical -86.31 MeV, quantum with 10000 shots -86.29 MeV, with optimized oscillator length 1/b = 2.0402 fm
* Ground state energy for N=2 (4 expansion states): classical -104.32 MeV, quantum with 10000 shots -103.85 MeV, with optimized oscillator length 1/b = 2.7040 fm
* Ground state energy for N=3 (8 expansion states): classical -110.57 MeV, quantum with 10000 shots -106.19 MeV, quantum with 100000 shots -109.47 MeV, with optimized oscillator length 1/b = 3.7060 fm
* Ground state energy for N=4 (16 expansion states): classical -112.65 MeV, quantum with 10000 shots failed (11.91 MeV), with optimized oscillator length 1/b = 5.1508 fm
* Skyrme force parameters used: t0 = -1132.4 MeV fm^3 and t3 = 23610.4 MeV fm^6

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum ITE study on helium-4 (Li, Al-Khalili, Stevenson 2024) to the more complex case of coupled non-linear Schrodinger equations for oxygen-16. The algorithm faces two main bottlenecks: an exponentially decreasing success probability (scaling as O(2^{-2N})) that causes measurement failures for larger bases, and an exponentially growing state preparation circuit size. The authors propose mitigation strategies including optimized imaginary time step sizes and basis truncation of the Pauli operator expansion, which could reduce the ancillary state preparation scaling from exponential to polynomial O(N^{w_t}).
