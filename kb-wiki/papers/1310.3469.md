# Net-baryon number fluctuations with the hadron resonance gas model using Tsallis distribution

> We explore a hadron resonance gas model using Tsallis non-extensive distribution to study the energy dependence of the product of the moments, $Sσ$ and $κσ^2$ of net-proton multiplicity distributions of published STAR data in \auau collisions at relativistic heavy-ion collider (RHIC) energies. While excellent agreements are found between model predictions and measurements of $Sσ$ and $κσ^2$ of most peripheral collisions and $Sσ$ of most central collisions, the $κσ^2$ for most central collisions deviates significantly from the predictions particularly at \sqsn = 19.6 GeV and $27$ GeV. This could be an indication of the presence of dynamical fluctuations, which are not contained in the HRG-Tsallis model.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas model using a Tsallis non-extensive distribution (HRG-Tsallis) can explain the energy dependence of the product of moments \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for net-proton multiplicity distributions in most peripheral Au+Au collisions and for \(S\sigma\) in most central collisions, but it fails to explain \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for most central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV and 27 GeV, which may indicate dynamical fluctuations not contained in the model.

## Method

The authors used a hadron resonance gas model with Tsallis non-extensive Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions, incorporating a temperature-dependent non-extensive parameter \(q\) that approaches unity at high temperatures. They calculated generalized susceptibilities \(\chi^3/\chi^2\) (equivalent to \(S\sigma\)) and \(\chi^4/\chi^2\) (equivalent to \(\kappa\sigma^2\)) for net-baryon distributions within STAR kinematic acceptance, using freeze-out temperatures and chemical potentials parametrized from experimental data for both central (0-5%) and peripheral (70-80%) Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

* For most peripheral (70-80%) collisions, HRG-Tsallis with an average \(q \sim 1.01\) explains both \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for all collision energies, where HRG model (\(q=1\)) gives \(\kappa\sigma^2\) close to unity while data are about 15% below HRG values.
* For most central (0-5%) collisions, \(S\sigma\) data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 39\), 62.4, and 200 GeV are described by HRG-Tsallis with \(\alpha = 0.01\) (related to \(q\)), while \(\kappa\sigma^2\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) and 27 GeV requires a larger \(\alpha \sim 0.35\) to match data.
* For central collisions, \(q\) values obtained from agreement with \(S\sigma\) data range smoothly from 1.0 to 1.015 from higher to lower energies; \(q\) values from agreement with \(\kappa\sigma^2\) data show a sudden increase from 1.0 to 1.06 for energies below 39 GeV.
* The reference temperature \(T_0\) is fixed at 0.167 GeV such that \(q=1\) for \(T_f \geq T_0\).

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model framework used by Karsch (2010) and Cleymans (2005) for central collisions, and uses freeze-out parameters extracted from STAR preliminary data by Das (2012) for peripheral collisions. It builds on prior studies showing that Tsallis distributions describe particle production in heavy-ion collisions (Tang 2008, Cleymans 2011) and that the non-extensive parameter \(q\) can be interpreted as arising from temperature fluctuations (Wilk 1999). The results provide a baseline for identifying dynamical fluctuations possibly associated with a QCD phase transition or critical point, particularly at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) and 27 GeV where the model fails to simultaneously describe \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for central collisions.
