# Hyperon Electromagnetic Properties in Two-Flavor Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The pion mass dependence of hyperon electromagnetic properties is determined using two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Specifically we compute chiral corrections to the charge radii, magnetic moments, and magnetic radii of the spin one-half hyperons, as well as the charge radii, magnetic moments, magnetic radii, electric quadrupole moments, and quadrupole radii of the spin three-half hyperons. Results for the nucleon and delta are also included. Efficacy of the two-flavor theory is investigated by analyzing the role played by virtual kaons. For the electromagnetic properties of spin one-half hyperons, kaon loop contributions are shown to be well described by terms analytic in the pion mass squared. Similarly kaon contributions to the magnetic moments of spin three-half hyperons are well described in the two-flavor theory. The remaining electromagnetic properties of spin three-half resonances can be described in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, however, this description fails just beyond the physical pion mass. For the case of experimentally known hyperon magnetic moments and charge radii, we demonstrate that chiral corrections are under reasonable control, in contrast to the behavior of these observables in the three-flavor chiral expansion. The formulae we derive are ideal for performing the pion mass extrapolation of lattice QCD data obtained at the physical strange quark mass.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that two-flavor (SU(2)) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory provides a better-controlled chiral expansion for hyperon electromagnetic properties than the three-flavor (SU(3)) theory, but this description fails for spin three-half hyperon electromagnetic radii and quadrupole moments just beyond the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop chiral corrections to the electromagnetic form factors of spin one-half and spin three-half hyperons using two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. They evaluate the pion mass dependence of charge radii, magnetic moments, magnetic radii, electric quadrupole moments, and quadrupole radii for the Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Sigma*, and Xi* hyperons, and investigate the efficacy of the two-flavor theory by analyzing the role played by virtual kaon loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- The mass splitting between Sigma and Lambda in the chiral limit is Delta_{LS} = 77 MeV, treated as a small parameter ~ epsilon in the power counting
- The mass splitting between Sigma* and Lambda in the chiral limit is Delta_{LS*} = 270 MeV
- The mass splitting between Xi* and Xi in the chiral limit is Delta_{XX*} = 215 MeV
- The pion decay constant used is f = 130 MeV
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi = 2 sqrt(2) pi f
- In SU(3) HBChPT, the relative size of NLO loop contributions compared to experiment for the Sigma+ magnetic moment is 120%, for the Lambda is 220%, for the Xi0 is 210%, and for the Xi- is 176%
- In SU(2) HBChPT, the relative size of NLO loop contributions compared to experiment for the Sigma+ magnetic moment is 17%, for the Lambda is 0%, for the Xi0 is less than 1%, and for the Xi- is less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory approach previously applied to hyperon masses (Tiburzi 2008), isovector axial charges (Jiang 2009), and pion-hyperon scattering lengths (Mai 2009) to the electromagnetic properties of hyperons. It contrasts with the three-flavor SU(3) HBChPT approach of Jenkins (1992), Bernard (1992), Meissner (1997), and Kubis (1999), where chiral corrections to individual baryon magnetic moments do not appear under perturbative control. The derived formulae are intended for performing the pion mass extrapolation of lattice QCD data obtained at the physical strange quark mass.
