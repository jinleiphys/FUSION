# Taming instability of magnetic field in chiral medium

> Magnetic field is unstable in a medium with time-independent chiral conductivity. Owing to the chiral anomaly, the electromagnetic field and the medium exchange helicity which results in time-evolution of the chiral conductivity. Using the fastest growing momentum and helicity state of the vector potential as an ansatz, the time-evolution of the chiral conductivity and magnetic field is solved analytically. The solution for the hot and cold equations of state shows that the magnetic field does not develop an instability due to he helicity conservation. Moreover, it develops a peak only if a significant part of the \emph{initial} helicity is stored in the medium. The initial helicity determines the height and position of the peak.

## Key claim

Magnetic field instability in a chiral medium is tamed not by energy conservation but by helicity conservation via the chiral anomaly; the magnetic field develops a peak only if a significant part of the initial helicity is stored in the medium rather than in the field, and the field eventually vanishes at late times.

## Method

The vector potential is expanded into Chandrasekhar-Kendall helicity eigenstates (circularly polarized plane waves) and approximated by the single fastest-growing mode, whose momentum \(k_0 = \sigma_\chi \lambda / 2\) is time-dependent because the chiral conductivity \(\sigma_\chi\) evolves. The Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations are solved analytically in the adiabatic approximation together with the chiral anomaly equation and two equations of state (hot linear, cold cubic) that relate chiral charge density to axial chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- In a hot medium, the magnetic field has a maximum only if the initial chiral conductivity \(\sigma_0' > 1/2\) (i.e., more than 50% of initial helicity is in the medium); the maximum occurs at \(\sigma_\chi'(\tau_*) = 1/2\).
- In a cold medium, the magnetic field has a maximum only if \(\sigma_0' > 1/2^{2/3} \approx 0.62\) (more than about 62% of initial helicity in the medium); the maximum occurs at \(\sigma_\chi'(t_*) = 1/2^{2/3}\).
- For an insulating hot medium (\(\sigma=0\)), the magnetic field maximum value is proportional to \(1/[2\sqrt{\sigma_0'(1-\sigma_0')}]\) and occurs at \(\tau_* = \ln[\sigma_0'/(1-\sigma_0')]\).
- In a cold insulating medium, the chiral conductivity vanishes at a finite time \(\tau_m\) given by Eq. (n17); for a conducting cold medium with \(\sigma \gg \sigma_\chi\), \(\tau_m\) is proportional to the electrical conductivity \(\sigma'\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of magnetic field instability in chiral media, previously studied by Joyce and Shaposhnikov (1997), Boyarsky et al. (2011), Hirono et al. (2015), and Manuel and Torres-Rincon (2015), who assumed constant chiral conductivity. It directly addresses the argument by Kaplan et al. (2016) that total energy and helicity conservation curtail the instability. The paper provides analytical solutions for the time evolution of chiral conductivity and magnetic field under two equations of state, enabling quantitative predictions for magnetic field evolution in quark-gluon plasma (hot medium) and cold dense matter. The results are qualitatively similar to the numerical stages II and III of Hirono et al. (2015), but differ from the monochromatic approximation of Manuel and Torres-Rincon (2015).
