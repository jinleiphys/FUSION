# A novel Dual Chiral Density Wave in nuclear matter based on a parity doublet structure

> We study the Dual Chiral Density Wave (DCDW) in nuclear matter using a hadronic model with the parity doublet structure. We first extend the ordinary DCDW ansatz so as to incorporate the effect of an explicit chiral symmetry breaking. Then via numerically evaluating and minimizing the effective potential, we determine the phase structure. We find, in addition to the ordinary DCDW phase where the space average of the chiral condensate vanishes, a new DCDW phase (sDCDW) with a nonvanishing space average depending on the value of the chiral invariant mass parameter.

## Key claim

Using a hadronic model with parity doublet structure, the authors find that, depending on the value of the chiral invariant mass parameter \(m_0\), a new type of Dual Chiral Density Wave phase (sDCDW) appears in addition to the ordinary DCDW phase; the sDCDW phase is characterized by a nonvanishing space average of the chiral condensate, which the abstract notes depends on the chiral invariant mass parameter.

## Method

The authors adopt a parity doublet hadron model including \(N(939)\) and its chiral partner \(N^\ast(1535)\), with \(\omega\) mesons included via hidden local symmetry and a six-point scalar interaction. They extend the ordinary DCDW ansatz to include an explicit chiral symmetry breaking term \(\delta\sigma\), then numerically evaluate and minimize the effective potential obtained from the Bogoliubov-de Gennes Hamiltonian for nucleons in a periodic potential. The phase structure is determined by solving stationary conditions for the wave number \(f\), the shift \(\delta\sigma\), the amplitude \(\sigma_0\), and the \(\omega\) meson field \(\omega_0\) as functions of baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) and chiral invariant mass \(m_0\).

## Key numbers

- For \(m_0 = 800\) MeV, the DCDW onset density is \(\rho_B^c \sim 4.7 \rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) is the normal nuclear saturation density.
- For \(m_0 = 800\) MeV, the stable DCDW phase appears at \(\rho_B \sim 4.8 \rho_0\).
- For \(m_0 = 700\) MeV, the sDCDW phase exists for chemical potentials \(900 \lesssim \mu_B \lesssim 1020\) MeV, with wave number \(f \sim 50\)–\(100\) MeV.
- For \(m_0 = 700\) MeV, the ordinary DCDW phase appears for \(\mu_B \gtrsim 1070\) MeV, with wave number \(f \gtrsim 220\) MeV.
- In the sDCDW phase for \(m_0 = 700\) MeV, the space-averaged chiral condensate \(M_1\) is \(M_1 \sim 50\)–\(70\) MeV, while in the ordinary DCDW phase it is less than 10 MeV.
- For \(m_0 = 800\) MeV, the wave number \(f\) in the DCDW phase has values of \(200\)–\(300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of inhomogeneous chiral phases in nuclear matter using a parity doublet hadron model, following the approach of Heinz (2013) who first showed DCDW phases in such a model. The present paper improves upon that work by including the effect of explicit chiral symmetry breaking via an extended ansatz, and finds a new sDCDW phase that appears for chiral invariant masses \(m_0 \lesssim 780\) MeV. The results enable future investigations of elementary excitations in the sDCDW phase, including possible phonon modes, and extensions to include external magnetic fields, as previously studied in quark matter by Nishiyama (2015) and others.
