# Hyperon-nucleon scattering and hyperon masses in the nuclear medium

> We analyze low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering using an effective field theory in next-to-leading order. By fitting experimental cross sections for laboratory hyperon momenta below 200 MeV/c and using information from the hypertriton we determine twelve contact-interaction coefficients. Based on these we discuss the low-density expansion of hyperon mass shifts in the nuclear medium.

# Hyperon-nucleon scattering and hyperon masses in the nuclear medium

## Key claim

The authors determine twelve contact-interaction coefficients for low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering by fitting experimental cross sections and hypertriton information, but the scarce data and large error bars mean the resulting hyperon mass shifts in nuclear medium are somewhat uncertain, with multiple fits of similar quality possible.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory with Kaplan-Savage-Wise resummation at next-to-leading order, representing the two-baryon interaction through SU(3)-symmetric contact terms (six at leading order, six at next-to-leading order) plus two symmetry-breaking parameters from one-pion exchange. They fit 28 data points for total cross sections (laboratory momenta below 200 MeV/c) and the capture ratio at rest, imposing constraints from hypertriton binding to fix the Lambda-nucleon scattering lengths, then compute hyperon mass shifts in nuclear medium via a low-density expansion.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory hyperon momenta below 200 MeV/c (center-of-mass momenta less than 100 MeV/c) define the data range
- Capture ratio at rest: r_R = 0.468 ± 0.010 (experimental value)
- Best fit (parameter set A) achieves chi-squared = 13.5 for 16 degrees of freedom
- Lambda-nucleon scattering lengths from fit: a_LambdaN^(0) = -2.50 fm (singlet), a_LambdaN^(1) = -1.78 fm (triplet)
- Lambda-nucleon effective ranges: r_LambdaN^(0) = 1.61 fm, r_LambdaN^(1) = 1.42 fm
- Sigma+ proton scattering lengths: a_Sigma+p^(0) = 0.55 fm, a_Sigma+p^(1) = 0.94 fm
- Sigma+ proton effective ranges: r_Sigma+p^(0) = 0.36 fm, r_Sigma+p^(1) = 0.35 fm
- Subtraction point mu = 0.15 GeV, varied in range 0.1 GeV to 0.3 GeV
- Maximum density for validity: 0.4 rho_0 for isospin-symmetric matter, 0.2 rho_0 for neutron matter (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3)
- Correction factor for Sigma- p to Lambda n kinematic suppression: r_F ≈ 0.16
- Ratio of D-wave to S-wave contributions for Sigma- p to Lambda n: r_ds ≈ 0.57

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) from the nucleon-nucleon system to hyperon-nucleon scattering, and builds on the chiral perturbation theory analysis of hyperon mass shifts by Savage and Wise (1996), who could not obtain numerical results due to unknown parameters. The results are compared to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using one-boson exchange potentials from Schulze et al. (1998) and Maessen, Rijken, and de Swart (1989); the authors find similar Lambda mass shifts at low densities but different Sigma mass shifts. The work enables future studies of hyperon binding in hypernuclei and neutron star equations of state, though the authors note that Brueckner-Hartree-Fock computations provide more reliable results at larger densities despite ambiguities from different potentials.
