# A Relativistic Separable Potential to Describe Pairing in Nuclear Matter

> Using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation to study nuclear pairing, we have found the short-range correlations of the Dirac $^1$S$_0$ pairing fields to be essentially identical to those of the two-nucleon virtual state at all values of the baryon density. We make use of this fact to develop a relativistic separable potential that correctly describes the pairing fields.

## Key claim

The short-range momentum dependence of the Dirac \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing fields in nuclear matter is essentially identical to that of the two-nucleon virtual state vertex function at all baryon densities, not merely similar as in nonrelativistic approaches. This quasi-identity allows construction of a rank-one separable potential that accurately reproduces the exact Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov pairing fields.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (DHFB) self-consistency equations for symmetric nuclear matter using a \(\sigma-\omega\) meson-exchange interaction, extracting the three components (\(\Delta_S\), \(\Delta_0\), \(\Delta_T\)) of the Dirac pairing field. They compare these to the vertex functions of the \(^1\)S\(_0\) two-nucleon virtual state obtained from the ladder-approximated Bethe-Salpeter equation, finding the momentum dependence of the components to be proportional at all densities. Using this proportionality, they construct a separable pairing potential whose components are products of the vacuum vertex functions with strength parameters \(\lambda_S\) and \(\lambda_0\) fixed by the physical virtual state energy and vertex function ratios.

## Key numbers

* The momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) in the self-consistency integrals is varied between 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\) and 15.0 fm\(^{-1}\) in the study of pairing field stability.
* For the \(\sigma-\omega\) interaction of Bouyssy et al. (1987), a cutoff of \(\Lambda = 3.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) is used, obtained by adjusting the virtual state energy to its physical value.
* The magnitude of the density-dependent factor \(d(k_F)\) decreases between 5% and 10% as the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) increases from 3.3 fm\(^{-1}\) to 10 fm\(^{-1}\).
* The tensor component \(\Delta_T\) is two to three orders of magnitude smaller than the other components \(\Delta_S\) and \(\Delta_0\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic DHFB pairing calculations of Guimaraes, Carlson, and Frederico (1996) and Carlson, Frederico, and Guimaraes (1997) by exploiting the near-identity between the Dirac pairing field and the virtual state vertex function. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic approach of Khodel, Khodel, and Clark (1996), where the virtual state serves only as a starting point for the gap function, not as an essentially identical form. The separable potential enables future implementation in configuration-space DHFB codes for finite nuclei, providing a more accurate description of short-range pairing correlations than the usual constant gap assumption.
