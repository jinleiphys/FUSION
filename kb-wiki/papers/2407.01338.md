# Effects of saturation and fluctuating hotspots for flow observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the effects of saturation dynamics on midrapidity flow observables by adding fluctuating hotspots into the novel Monte Carlo EKRT (MC-EKRT) event generator for high-energy nuclear collisions. We demonstrate that the intensity of the saturation effects significantly affects the ratio between the flow coefficients $v_3$ and $v_2$ at the LHC. Adding a hotspot substructure to the nucleons enhances the saturation effects and improves the agreement with the measured data. We show that the collision-energy dependence of the flow coefficients obtained using the MC-EKRT initial states with hotspots is improved in comparison with the earlier event-by-event EKRT model. In addition, we present the results for the charged hadron multiplicity distribution in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and show that the minijet-multiplicity originating fluctuations of the saturation scale included in MC-EKRT, as well as the presence of hotspots, are necessary for describing the measured large-multiplicity tail in the distribution.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that adding fluctuating hotspot substructure to nucleons within the MC-EKRT event generator enhances saturation effects and improves agreement with measured flow coefficient ratios at LHC energies, but the intensity of saturation effects significantly affects the ratio between flow coefficients \(v_3\) and \(v_2\), a condition the abstract presents as a demonstrated result rather than a parameter-dependent finding.

## Method
The authors generated event-by-event initial conditions for 2+1 D dissipative fluid dynamics using the MC-EKRT event generator, which samples minijet production from perturbative QCD with a saturation mechanism that filters excessive dijet candidates based on transverse overlap and energy conservation. They introduced Gaussian hotspot substructure to nucleons (three hotspots per nucleon) and used deep convolutional neural networks trained on EbyE-EKRT data to predict flow observables from initial energy density profiles, validating the networks against full hydrodynamic simulations for 10k MC-EKRT events with hotspots.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon width \(\sigma_N = 0.517\) fm for 2.76 TeV and \(\sigma_N = 0.532\) fm for 5.023 TeV collision energies, based on H1 measurements with parameters \(b_0=4.63\), \(\alpha'_P=0.164\), and \(W_0=90\) GeV.
- Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm used for propagating partons to the fluid dynamics initialization surface.
- Transverse smearing width \(\sigma_\perp = 0.4\) fm used in the neural network validation test with \(\kappa_{\mathrm{sat}} = 2.5\) and \(K = 2.2\).
- Hotspot width \(\sigma_h = 0.2\) fm used in the validation test with three hotspots per nucleon.
- Dynamical decoupling conditions set to \(C_{\mathrm{Kn}} = 0.8\) and \(C_R = 0.15\), with forced decoupling when temperature \(T < 150\) MeV.
- Neural network predictions for 10 million events take approximately 20 hours on Nvidia Tesla V100 GPU.

## Context
This work extends the earlier EbyE-EKRT model (Niemi 2015) by introducing minijet multiplicity fluctuations and nucleon substructure, addressing the missing description of charged multiplicity distributions in central collisions. The results enable improved global analyses of heavy-ion observables through Bayesian inference, building on prior work by Hirvonen (2022) and the T\(_\mathrm{R}\)ENTo initial state model (Moreland 2014) which showed slight preference for nucleon substructure but could compensate with other parameters. The neural network approach follows Hirvonen (2023) and allows inclusion of statistics-expensive observables in future global analyses.
