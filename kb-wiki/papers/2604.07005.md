# Measurement of inclusive $J/ψ$ polarization in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV at STAR

> The first measurement of inclusive J/psi polarization at mid-rapidity (|y^{J/psi}| < 0.8) in 200 GeV Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with the STAR experiment at RHIC is presented. J/psi mesons are reconstructed through their di-electron (e+e-) decay channel. The polarization parameters (lambda_theta, lambda_phi) are measured as a function of the J/psi transverse momentum (p_T) and collision centrality in both the helicity and the Collins-Soper frames. These polarization parameters are found to be consistent with zero across the measured J/psi p_T range of 0.2 < p_T < 10 GeV/c and across collision centralities within 0-80 percent in both frames. These results are consistent with corresponding measurements p+p collisions at the same collision energy and with transport-model calculations.

## Key claim

The inclusive J/ψ polarization parameters (λ_θ, λ_φ) in 200 GeV Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions are consistent with zero across the measured p_T range and centrality classes, but the current experimental precision is insufficient to rule out possible modifications to polarization from altered feed-down contributions (e.g., from χ_cJ suppression) that could differ from p+p collisions.

## Method

J/ψ mesons were reconstructed via the di-electron (e⁺e⁻) decay channel using the STAR detector's TPC, TOF, and BEMC subsystems. The polarization parameters λ_θ and λ_φ were extracted by simultaneously fitting the cosθ and φ angular distributions of daughter leptons after correcting for detector acceptance and efficiency via an iterative toy Monte Carlo procedure, in both the helicity and Collins-Soper reference frames.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ p_T range: 0.2 < p_T < 10 GeV/c
- Rapidity range: |y^{J/ψ}| < 0.8
- Centrality range: 0–80%
- Total raw J/ψ yield after mass window correction: 67,682 ± 542 candidates
- Collision systems: approximately 2×10⁹ events each for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Polarization parameters consistent with zero within uncertainties in both helicity and Collins-Soper frames across all measured p_T and centrality bins
- Regenerated J/ψ fraction in the THU model: approximately 50% in central collisions decreasing to less than 5% in peripheral collisions
- Non-prompt J/ψ contribution estimated to be less than 15% depending on J/ψ p_T

## Context

This is the first measurement of J/ψ polarization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, extending prior work that measured J/ψ polarization in p+p collisions at √s = 200 GeV by STAR (Liu 2020). The results are consistent with those p+p measurements and with transport-model calculations from the Tsinghua (THU) model. At LHC energies, ALICE found a 3.3σ difference in λ_θ between Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and p+p collisions at 7 TeV (LHCb 2013), whereas the present RHIC measurement shows no such deviation, likely due to smaller regeneration contributions and negligible non-prompt J/ψ from b-hadron decays at lower collision energies. The results enable future comparisons with more precise measurements in both p+p and heavy-ion collisions to disentangle QGP effects on different J/ψ production channels.
