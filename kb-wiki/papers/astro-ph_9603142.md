# Thermal Evolution of Compact Stars

> A collection of modern, field-theoretical equations of state is applied to the investigation of cooling properties of compact stars. These comprise neutron stars as well as hypothetical strange matter stars, made up of absolutely stable 3-flavor strange quark matter. Various uncertainties in the behavior of matter at supernuclear densities, e.g., hyperonic degrees of freedom, behavior of coupling strengths in matter, pion and meson condensation, superfluidity, transition to quark matter, absolute stability of strange quark matter, and last but not least the many-body technique itself are tested against the body of observed cooling data.

## Key claim

The paper tests a broad collection of modern, field-theoretical equations of state against observed cooling data for both neutron stars and hypothetical strange matter stars, but the conclusions are conditional on numerous model assumptions about superdense matter behavior that remain unresolved, such as hyperonic degrees of freedom, coupling strengths, meson condensation, superfluidity, and the absolute stability of strange quark matter.

## Method

The authors solved the general-relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure combined with the energy balance and thermal transport equations for spherically symmetric, non-rotating compact stars. They applied a collection of equations of state derived from non-relativistic variational methods and relativistic field-theoretical approaches (Hartree, Hartree-Fock, and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximations) to compute the cooling evolution of neutron stars and strange stars, incorporating various neutrino emission processes, superfluidity effects, and crust-photosphere boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for the UV$_{14}$+UVII equation of state: $n_0 = 0.175$ fm$^{-3}$, with binding energy $E/A = -11.5$ MeV
- Saturation density for the HV equation of state: $n_0 = 0.145$ fm$^{-3}$, with binding energy $E/A = -15.98$ MeV
- Compression modulus for the HV equation of state: $K = 285$ MeV
- Maximum gap energy for neutron $^1$S$_0$ superfluidity: $\Delta_\mathrm{sf}^\mathrm{max} = 1.13$ MeV, with critical temperature $T_c^\mathrm{max} = 7.4 \times 10^9$ K
- Maximum gap energy for neutron $^3$P$_2$ superfluidity: $\Delta_\mathrm{sf}^\mathrm{max} = 0.62$ MeV, with critical temperature $T_c^\mathrm{max} = 0.8 \times 10^9$ K
- Maximum gap energy for proton $^1$S$_0$ superfluidity: $\Delta_\mathrm{sf}^\mathrm{max} = 0.25$ MeV, with critical temperature $T_c^\mathrm{max} = 1.6 \times 10^9$ K
- Transition density to quark matter for the G$^\mathrm{K240}_\mathrm{B180}$ equation of state: $1.6\,n_0$, with pure quark-matter phase reached at about $10\,n_0$
- Bag constant for strange star models: $B^{1/4} = 145$ MeV, with strange quark mass $m_s = 150$ MeV, placing the energy per baryon of strange quark matter at 850 MeV
- Density threshold for neutron $^1$S$_0$ superfluidity: from $7 \times 10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$ to $2 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (for the HV equation of state)
- Density threshold for proton $^1$S$_0$ superfluidity: from $2 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$ to $4 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (for the HV equation of state)
- Density threshold for neutron $^3$P$_2$ superfluidity: from $2 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$ to $5 \times 10^{16}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (for the HV equation of state)

## Context

This work extends earlier cooling studies by incorporating a wider range of modern field-theoretical equations of state, including those with hyperonic degrees of freedom, meson condensation, and quark matter transitions, building on the approaches of Wiringa (1988), Glendenning (1989, 1992, 1995), and Huber (1994, 1995). It also tests the strange star hypothesis of Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) against cooling data, enabling future observational discrimination between neutron stars and strange stars using X-ray observations from Einstein, EXOSAT, and ROSAT.
