# Simultaneous Measurement of Half-Life and Spectral Shape of $^{115}$In $β$-decay with an Indium Iodide Cryogenic Calorimeter

> Current bounds on neutrino Majorana mass are affected by significant uncertainties in the nuclear calculations for neutrinoless double-beta decay. A key issue for a data-driven improvement of the nuclear theory is the actual value of the axial coupling constant $g_A$, which can be investigated through forbidden $β$-decays. We present the first measurement of 4$^{th}$-forbidden $β$-decay of $^{115}$In with a cryogenic calorimeter based on Indium Iodide. Exploiting the enhanced spectral shape method for the first time to this isotope, our study accurately determines simultaneously spectral shape, $g_A$, and half-life. The Interacting Shell Model, which best fits our data, indicates a half-life for this decay at $T_{1/2}=(5.26\pm0.06) \times 10^{14}$,yr.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first measurement of the fourth-forbidden beta decay of 115In using a cryogenic calorimeter, simultaneously determining spectral shape, axial coupling constant gA, and half-life. The Interacting Shell Model best fits the data, but the authors note that the simultaneous determination of gA and sNME is limited by the signal-to-background ratio, and the extracted gA values depend on the nuclear model used.

## Method
A 7x7x7 mm³ Indium Iodide (InI) crystal calorimeter with a Neutron Transmutation Doped germanium thermistor was operated at approximately 16 mK at Laboratori Nazionali del Gran Sasso. The enhanced spectral shape method was applied for the first time to 115In, using Bayesian inference with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo to fit the measured energy spectrum against templates from three nuclear models (Interacting Shell Model, Microscopic Quasi-Particle Phonon Model, and Interacting Boson-Fermion Model) while varying gA and the small relativistic nuclear matrix element (sNME). Two fitting approaches were used: a "best fit" letting both gA and sNME vary freely, and a "matched half-life fit" fixing sNME to match the known half-life.

## Key numbers
- Half-life from best fit with ISM model: T1/2 = (5.26 ± 0.06) × 10^14 yr
- Half-life from best fit with MQPM model: T1/2 = (5.26 ± 0.07) × 10^14 yr
- Half-life from best fit with IBFM-2 model: T1/2 = (5.25 ± 0.07) × 10^14 yr
- gA from best fit with ISM model: 0.964 (+0.010, -0.006)
- gA from best fit with MQPM model: 1.104 (+0.019, -0.017)
- gA from best fit with IBFM-2 model: 1.172 (+0.022, -0.017)
- sNME from best fit with ISM model (positive solution): 1.75 (+0.13, -0.08)
- Crystal mass: (1.91 ± 0.01) g
- Energy resolution: 3.9 keV FWHM at 238.6 keV
- Detection threshold: 3.4 keV
- Measurement time: 128.8 h
- Q-value of 115In beta decay: 497.489(10) keV
- Natural isotopic abundance of 115In: (95.719 ± 0.052)%
- Analysis cut efficiency: (52.2 ± 0.3)%

## Context
This work extends the enhanced spectral shape method previously applied to other forbidden beta decays such as 113Cd (Kostensalo 2021) and 99Tc (Paulsen 2023) to the 115In isotope, using a cryogenic calorimeter approach from the ACCESS project. The results provide data-driven constraints on the axial coupling constant gA, which is crucial for reducing uncertainties in neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element calculations that currently limit the conversion of half-life limits into effective Majorana mass bounds. The study enables future systematic investigations of sNME preference schemes across different nuclear models and isotopes, potentially improving the reliability of nuclear theory for double-beta decay experiments.
