# The shape of gold

> Having a detailed theoretical knowledge of the low-energy structure of the heavy odd-mass nucleus $^{197}$Au is of prime interest as the structure of this isotope represents an important input to theoretical simulations of collider experiments involving gold ions performed worldwide at relativistic energies. In the present article, therefore, we report on new results on the structure of $^{197}$Au obtained from state-of-the-art multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) calculations. Our MR-EDF calculations were realized using the Skyrme-type pseudo-potential SLyMR1, and include beyond mean-field correlations through the mixing, in the spirit of the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), of particle-number and angular-momentum projected triaxially deformed Bogoliubov quasi-particle states. Comparison with experimental data shows that the model gives a reasonable description of $^{197}$Au with in particular a good agreement for most of the spectroscopic properties of the $3/2_1^+$ ground state. From the collective wave function of the correlated state, we compute an average deformation $\barβ(3/2_1^+)=0.13$ and $\barγ(3/2_1^+)=40^\circ$ for the ground state. We use this result to construct an intrinsic shape of $^{197}$Au representing a microscopically-motivated input for precision simulations of the associated collider processes. We discuss, in particular, how the triaxiality of this nucleus is expected to impact $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collision experiments at ultrarelativistic energy.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the ground state of $^{197}$Au has an average triaxial deformation of $\bar{\beta}(3/2_1^+)=0.13$ and $\bar{\gamma}(3/2_1^+)=40^\circ$, but this result is qualified by the caveat that the squared collective wave function cannot be strictly interpreted as a probability distribution due to the non-orthogonality of the reference states.

## Method
The authors performed multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) calculations using the Skyrme-type pseudo-potential SLyMR1, incorporating beyond mean-field correlations through the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected triaxially deformed Bogoliubov quasi-particle states. They generated one-quasi-particle reference states on a three-dimensional Cartesian Lagrange mesh with 32 points in each direction, exploring triaxial deformations with a spacing of $\Delta q_1 = \Delta q_2 = 375$ fm$^2$. The final configuration mixing included 24 positive-parity and 19 negative-parity one-quasi-particle states, with cutoffs applied to remove poorly represented components.

## Key numbers
- Average ground state deformation: $\bar{\beta}(3/2_1^+)=0.13$ with standard deviation $\Delta \beta (3/2_1^+)=0.03$, and $\bar{\gamma}(3/2_1^+)=40^\circ$ with standard deviation $\Delta \gamma (3/2_1^+)=15^\circ$
- Ground state binding energy: theoretical -1556.044 MeV compared to experimental -1559.384 MeV
- Ground state root-mean-square charge radius: theoretical 5.389 fm compared to experimental 5.4371(38) fm
- Ground state spectroscopic quadrupole moment: theoretical +0.65 $e$b compared to experimental +0.547(16) $e$b
- Ground state magnetic dipole moment: theoretical -0.38 $\mu_N$ compared to experimental +0.1452(2) $\mu_N$
- The $11/2_1^-$ state excitation energy: theoretical 792 keV compared to experimental 409 keV
- Energy centroid of the $1/2^+, 3/2^+, 5/2^+, 7/2^+$ quartet: theoretical 630.7 keV compared to experimental 364.2 keV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous MR-EDF study of xenon isotopes (Bally 2022) to the odd-mass nucleus $^{197}$Au, which is the primary species used at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The results provide a microscopically-motivated intrinsic shape for gold ions that can serve as input for precision simulations of $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collision experiments at ultrarelativistic energy. The calculations are compared with the core-excitation model of De-Shalit (1961) and with axially-symmetric mean-field calculations using the Gogny D1S functional reported in the AMEDEE database (Hilaire 2007), as well as with five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian calculations of neighboring even-even nuclei (Delaroche 2010).
