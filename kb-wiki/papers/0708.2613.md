# Pion freeze-out through HBT correlation in HICs from AGS/FAIR to RHIC energies

> In this talk we present the results of two-pion HBT correlation at freeze-out in heavy ion collisions (HICs) from AGS to RHIC energies. The UrQMD hadron-string transport model as well as the CRAB analyzing program are adopted. Based on the cascade mode, in general, the calculations are satisfying and well in line with the experimental data although discrepancies are not negligible. Such as: I), the HBT time-related puzzle exists at all energies. II), at low AGS energies, the calculated volume as well as the mean free path of pion source at freeze-out are lower than data. It implies that a better description of interactions of particles at early stage of HICs is required.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD hadron-string transport model in cascade mode, the calculated HBT radii, freeze-out volume, and mean free path of pions generally agree with experimental data from AGS to RHIC energies, but discrepancies remain: the HBT time-related puzzle (excess of the duration-time quantity sqrt(R_O^2 - R_S^2) over data) persists at all energies, and at low AGS energies the calculated freeze-out volume and mean free path are lower than data, indicating that a better description of early-stage particle interactions is required.

## Method

The authors employed the UrQMD v2.2 hadron-string transport model in cascade mode to simulate heavy ion collisions, then used the CRAB analyzing program to compute two-pion HBT correlation functions. The correlation functions were fitted with a Gaussian form in the LCMS system to extract the Pratt radii R_L, R_O, R_S and the cross term R_OL. Calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at AGS beam energies 2, 4, 6, 8, and 11.6 A GeV; Pb+Pb and Pb+Au at SPS beam energies 20, 30, 40, 80, and 160 A GeV; and Au+Au at RHIC energies sqrt(s_NN) = 30, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV. Additional calculations with a mass-dependent resonance lifetime treatment and with a soft momentum-dependent equation of state (SM-EoS) for formed baryons were also performed.

## Key numbers

- At AGS energies, the calculated R_L and R_S values are visibly smaller than data when using the default cascade mode; at E_b = 2 A GeV, the measured freeze-out volume V_f is about 2-3 times larger than the calculated value.
- At SPS energies, the calculated R_L and R_S values are up to 25% lower than data at small k_T; the calculated R_O values are larger than both NA49 and CERES data, especially at large k_T.
- At RHIC energies, the calculated R_O values are about 25% too large compared to experimental data.
- The calculated sqrt(R_O^2 - R_S^2) values are about 3.5-5 fm while measured values are 1.5-4 fm across all energies.
- The mean free path lambda_f of pions at freeze-out increases gradually from about 0.5 fm to about 1 fm from AGS to RHIC energies; both theory and experiment give values between 0.5-1 fm, with a nearly constant value of about 0.7 fm.
- When the SM-EoS potential is applied for formed baryons, the R_O/R_S ratio at AGS energies becomes smaller than the cascade result and reproduces the data; at SPS and RHIC the ratio increases monotonically with beam energy and deviates from data again.

## Context

This work extends prior UrQMD-based HBT studies by Li and collaborators (2006, 2007) and provides a comprehensive excitation function of HBT parameters from AGS to RHIC energies. The results are compared with experimental data from E895 (Lisa, 2000), E802 (Ahle, 2002), NA49 (Kniege, 2004, 2006), CERES (Adamova, 2002), PHOBOS (Back, 2004), STAR (Adler, 2001; Adams, 2004), and PHENIX (Adcox, 2002; Adler, 2004). The HBT time-related puzzle, where the calculated duration-time quantity exceeds data, is addressed by testing a momentum-dependent mean field (SM-EoS) for formed baryons, following the approach of Isse (2005) with RQMD/S and JAM. The work enables further investigation of the role of interactions of unformed particles from string fragmentation at SPS and RHIC energies.
