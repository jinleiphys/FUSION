# Chaotic Dynamics of SU(2) Gauge Fields in the Presence of Static Charges

> We have found in numerical simulations that the chaoticity of the classical hamiltonian lattice SU(2) gauge theory is reduced in the presence of static charges at the same total energy. The transition from strongly to weakly chaotic behavior is rather sudden at a critical charge strength.

## Key claim

The chaoticity of classical Hamiltonian lattice SU(2) gauge theory is reduced in the presence of static charges at the same total energy, with a sudden transition from strongly to weakly chaotic behavior at a critical charge strength; the reduction occurs only for strong charges (order of magnitude 10 in scaled units), while weak static charges leave the chaotic dynamics essentially unchanged.

## Method

The author performed numerical simulations of classical SU(2) gauge fields on a cubic lattice using a mixed explicit-implicit algorithm that exactly conserves Gauss' law. A diagonal flux line (Wilson line) was initialized across the lattice to represent static color charges at its endpoints, with the charge strength controlled by a parameter q. The leading Lyapunov exponent was measured as the logarithmic time-derivative of the gauge-invariant distance between two initially adjacent field configurations, fitted over linear growth epochs.

## Key numbers

- The total scaled energy per link was set to g²aE = 2.00 for the N=6 cubic lattice simulations.
- The leading Lyapunov exponent was measured for charge strengths q = 0.0, 8.70, 9.50, and 12.10, with corresponding reduced random magnetic backgrounds covering 100%, 35%, 15%, and 45% of the SU(2) group angle interval [0,2π].
- The time step used was a = 0.005, equal to the lattice spacing.
- The allowed phase space dimension for the N=6 system is 3239.

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of chaotic behavior in classical SU(2) gauge fields (IJMPC5) that demonstrated linear scaling of the maximal Lyapunov exponent with energy, and contrasts with Abelian systems that do not show such scaling. The results are relevant to understanding thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions and the early universe, where chromoelectric strings (color ropes) between static charges may coexist with random chromomagnetic fields; the finding that weak charges do not suppress chaos suggests that soft collective chaotic dynamics can still contribute to thermalization toward a quark-gluon plasma.
