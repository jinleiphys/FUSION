# Spin polarization induced by magnetic field and the relativistic Barnett effect

> First, I study the analogy between the magnetization of a material and the spin polarization of particles in a fluid. Using the relativistic version of the Barnett effect, i.e. the magnetization of a material induced by mechanical rotation, the spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity is obtained within a purely classical model, where spin is treated as an intrinsic magnetic moment and rotation is included as a non-inertial effect. I argue that since spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity can be obtained in a classical theory, it can not be dominated by quantum anomalies. Second, the spin polarization induced by magnetic field is obtained for a fluid at local thermal equilibrium using statistical quantum field theory. The obtained formula is valid beyond the weak field approximation and when contributions from the non-homogeneity of the magnetic field are small. The exact form of spin polarization is studied for a free Dirac field at global equilibrium, and, like magnetic susceptibility, it oscillates according to the de Haas - van Alphen effect. Finally, I briefly review how magnetic field contributes to the difference between the spin polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ observed in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity can be obtained from a purely classical model treating spin as an intrinsic magnetic moment and rotation as a non-inertial effect, which implies that the phenomenon cannot be dominated by quantum anomalies despite claims connecting it to the gravitational anomaly.

## Method

The author derives spin polarization in a relativistic fluid using the Zubarev non-equilibrium statistical operator formalism for local thermal equilibrium, expanding thermodynamic and gauge fields around a point to obtain an approximate statistical operator. The Wigner function for a free Dirac field in an external magnetic field is then evaluated using exact solutions of the Dirac equation, and the spin polarization vector is obtained from the scalar and axial parts of the Wigner function's particle branch.

## Key numbers

- The classical magnetization formula is \( M = n \mu L(\mu B/T) \) where \( L(x) = \coth(x) - 1/x \) is the Langevin function
- For spin \( S=1/2 \) in the non-relativistic limit, the Curie law gives \( M = n\beta \mu_0^2 B \)
- The relativistic Barnett effect yields an effective magnetic field \( B^\mu_{\rm Eff} = (\varepsilon_p / q) \omega^\mu \), which in the non-relativistic limit reduces to \( B_{\rm Eff} = (m/q)\omega = (1/2\mu_0)\omega \)

## Context

This work extends the analogy between magnetization and spin polarization in relativistic fluids, building on the Barnett effect (Barnett 1915, 1935) and the relativistic formulation of thermal vorticity-induced polarization (Becattini 2013, 2016). It provides a full quantum field theory derivation of magnetic field-induced spin polarization using the Zubarev formalism, going beyond previous kinetic theory approaches (Gao 2012, Yi 2021) and weak-field approximations. The results enable more accurate predictions for the difference between \(\Lambda\) and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic field contributions are opposite in sign for particles and antiparticles, and can help disentangle magnetic field effects from vorticity-induced polarization in experimental data from STAR and ALICE.
