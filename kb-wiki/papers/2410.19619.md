# Formation, dissociation and regeneration of charmonia within microscopic Langevin simulations

> We present a microscopic dynamical model to study the formation, dissociation, and recombination processes of charmonium states in a heat bath at constant temperature and volume. Within this classical approach, heavy quarks are described as Brownian particles in a background medium of light constituents and can therefore be modeled by a Fokker-Planck equation with constant transport coefficients, which is then implemented through relativistic Langevin simulations. The heavy quarks interact classically via a Coulomb-like screened potential to form a bound state if the relative energy of the pair becomes negative. Dissociation of bound states is possible as a result of screening effects on the potential as well as through scatterings with plasma particles. We demonstrate the full equilibration of the system and show that the resulting equilibrium charmonium yields are in accordance with the Statistical Hadronization Model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a classical Langevin simulation of charm quarks interacting via a screened Coulomb potential can reproduce equilibrium charmonium yields consistent with the Statistical Hadronization Model, but only after tuning the strong coupling constant to \(\alpha_s = 0.7\) (rather than the typical value of 0.5) and using a charm quark mass of 1.8 GeV.

## Method

Heavy quarks are treated as Brownian particles in a thermal bath at constant temperature and volume, with their motion described by relativistic Langevin equations derived from a Fokker-Planck equation. Charm and anticharm quarks interact via a complex screened Coulomb potential from Blaizot et al., where the real part provides attraction for bound state formation and the imaginary part encodes dissociation through scatterings with medium particles. Bound states are identified classically when the relative energy of a charm-anticharm pair becomes negative, and the system is evolved in box simulations with periodic boundary conditions until equilibration.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to \(M_c = 1.8\ \text{GeV}\) to yield a physical \(J/\psi\) mass of \(3.1\ \text{GeV}\)
- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.7\) used (with \(\alpha_s(T_c) \simeq 0.5\) being the typical value)
- Debye screening mass cutoff \(\Lambda = 4\ \text{GeV}\) applied to regularize the potential singularity
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 160\ \text{MeV}\) used in the running coupling parametrization
- Box simulations performed with volume \(V = (8\ \text{fm})^3\) and \(N_{\text{event}} = 4 \times 10^4\) events
- Equilibration time of \(t = 400\ \text{fm}\) used for the long-time limit comparison
- Strongest bound state appears at approximately \(1\ \text{GeV}\) of binding energy at \(T = 160\ \text{MeV}\)
- Spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) from the model is smaller than values from the Texas A and M model (Huggins 2012) and the Catania quasi-particle model (Scardina 2017), but consistent with recent lattice QCD data (Altenkort 2024)

## Context

This work extends the classical Langevin approach originally proposed by Shuryak and Young (2008, 2009) and Blaizot et al. for studying charmonium dynamics in a quark-gluon plasma. The model provides a microscopic dynamical description that complements the Statistical Hadronization Model of Andronic et al. (2003, 2017, 2021), which treats charmonium production as an instantaneous statistical process at freeze-out without dynamical evolution. The approach enables future studies of charmonium formation, dissociation, and regeneration in expanding fireball geometries relevant to heavy-ion collisions, going beyond the constant-temperature box simulations presented here.
