# Neutral pion mass in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks at arbitrary magnetic field

> We calculate the neutral pion mass in the presence of an external magnetic field of arbitrary strength in the framework of the linear sigma model coupled to quarks at zero temperature. We find nonmonotonic behavior of the pion mass as a function of magnetic field. We are also able to reproduce existing results for the weak-field approximation.

# Neutral pion mass in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks at arbitrary magnetic field

## Key claim
The neutral pion mass in the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (LSMq) shows nonmonotonic behavior as a function of external magnetic field strength: it decreases for weak fields (qualitatively agreeing with lattice QCD and earlier weak-field approximations) but begins to increase for large magnetic fields, which the authors attribute to the LSMq model becoming unreliable in the high-energy regime.

## Method
The authors compute the one-loop neutral pion self-energy in the presence of a constant external magnetic field at zero temperature, including contributions from quark-antiquark loops and charged pion tadpole diagrams. The calculation uses Schwinger's proper-time representation for charged particle propagators in a magnetic field, with the vacuum part subtracted for renormalization. The magnetic-field-dependent pion mass is obtained by solving the dispersion relation numerically, incorporating effective one-loop corrections to the fermion mass, the boson self-coupling, and the vacuum expectation value of the sigma field.

## Key numbers
- The vacuum pion mass is set to \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV.
- The sigma meson mass is taken as \(m_\sigma = 0.45\) GeV for the main results.
- The coupling constants used are \(\lambda = 0.86\) and \(g = 1.11\), taken from Ayala (2015).
- The weak-field approximation for the pion mass is valid for \(eB \lesssim m_\pi^2\) (approximately \(eB \lesssim 0.02\) GeV²).
- The corrected weak-field expression from Ayala (2018) deviates from the exact solution for \(|eB| \gtrsim 0.85\, m_\pi^2\).
- The quark-loop contribution in the weak-field limit includes terms of order \((q_f B)^2\) and \((q_f B)^4\); the charged pion tadpole contribution in weak field is expanded as \(\Pi_{\pi_\pm}^w(B) = \frac{\lambda}{64\pi^2}\left[-\frac{(eB)^2}{6m_\pi^2} + \frac{7(eB)^4}{180m_\pi^6} - \frac{31(eB)^6}{360m_\pi^{10}} + \ldots\right]\).

## Context
This work extends the weak-field calculation of the neutral pion mass by Ayala (2018) to arbitrary magnetic field strengths within the LSMq framework. The results qualitatively agree with lattice QCD studies by Bali (2017) for low magnetic fields, where the neutral pion mass decreases with increasing field. The calculation enables future extensions to finite temperature (relevant for heavy-ion collision physics) and to finite baryon density (relevant for astrophysical objects such as compact stars and magnetars). The authors note that the linear sigma model is an effective model with a scalar degree of freedom (sigma meson) that remains under consideration after the identification of \(f_0(500)\) with \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\), and suggest that the nonlinear sigma model might be an interesting alternative for studying light mesons in magnetic fields.
