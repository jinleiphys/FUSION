# Scalar resonance effects on the B_s - \bar B_s mixing angle

> The $B_s^0 \to J/ψϕ$ and $B_s^0 \to J/ψf_0(980)$ decays are analyzed within generalized QCD factorization including all leading-order corrections in $\alphas$. We point out that the ratio of our calculated widths, $Γ(B_s^0\to J/ψf_0(980), f_0(980)\to π^+π^-)/ Γ(B_s^0 \to J/ψϕ,ϕ\to K^+K^-)$, strongly indicates that $S$-wave effects in the $f_0(980)$'s daughter pions or kaons cannot be ignored in the extraction of the $B_s-\bar{B}_s$ mixing angle, $-2β_s$, from the $B_s^0 \to ϕJ/ψ$ decay amplitudes.

# Scalar resonance effects on the B_s - \bar B_s mixing angle

## Key claim

The ratio of the calculated widths for B_s^0 → J/ψ f_0(980) (with f_0(980) → π⁺π⁻) to B_s^0 → J/ψ φ (with φ → K⁺K⁻) strongly indicates that S-wave effects from the f_0(980)'s daughter pions or kaons cannot be ignored when extracting the B_s–\bar B_s mixing angle −2β_s from B_s^0 → φ J/ψ decay amplitudes, even though the Standard Model prediction for this mixing phase is small and the QCDF approach for these decays is not self-evident due to the heavy-light final state.

## Method

The authors analyze B_s^0 → J/ψ φ and B_s^0 → J/ψ f_0(980) decays within generalized QCD factorization (QCDF) including all leading-order corrections in α_s, using the helicity formalism for the vector-vector (φ J/ψ) final state and a scalar-vector treatment for the f_0(980) J/ψ final state. They compute the ratio R_{f_0/φ} of the two decay widths, employing a B_s → f_0 transition form factor from a previous model (El-Bennich, 2008) and constraining the analysis with available observables (polarizations and branching ratio) for B_s^0 → J/ψ φ, while allowing for phenomenological amplitudes ζ^h and ζ to account for possible suppressed contributions or new physics.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R_{f_0/φ} = Γ(B_s^0 → J/ψ f_0(980), f_0(980) → π⁺π⁻) / Γ(B_s^0 → J/ψ φ, φ → K⁺K⁻) is estimated to be of order 20%–30% by Stone and Zhang (2008), and the authors' QCDF calculation yields a value of O(1) with corrections of O(Λ_QCD/m_b)² and O(Λ_QCD/m_b)⁴.
- The B_s^0 lifetime used is τ_{B_s^0} = (1.470 ± 0.026) ps (Amsler, 2008).
- The strong coupling constants are α_s(m_b) = 0.224 and α_s(m_b/2) = 0.286 (Amsler, 2008).
- The B_s^0 decay constant f_{B_s^0} is varied among 230, 260, and 290 MeV, with corresponding phenomenological parameters ρ_C ranging from 4.52 ± 2.24 to 7.33 ± 1.63 and ρ_H from 1.90 ± 0.20 to 1.54 ± 0.15.
- The first Gegenbauer moment for the f_0(980) is B_1(m_b/2) = −0.54 (Cheng, 2005).
- The B_s^0 → φ transition form factors A_0, A_1, and V are evaluated at q² = m_{J/ψ}², and the B_s^0 → f_0 form factor F_1^{B_s^0→f_0}(m_{J/ψ}²) is taken from El-Bennich (2008).

## Context

This work extends the QCDF formalism (Beneke, 2000; Beneke, 2001; Beneke, 2003; Beneke, 2006) to B_s decays into a heavy-light final state, where the emitted meson is heavy (J/ψ) and the spectator s-quark is absorbed by the light meson (φ or f_0(980)). It directly addresses the extraction of the B_s–\bar B_s mixing phase −2β_s, which recent CDF (Aaltonen, 2007) and D∅ (Abazov, 2008) measurements found to be larger than Standard Model predictions. The paper provides a first robust QCDF calculation of the ratio R_{f_0/φ}, improving upon the qualitative estimate of Stone and Zhang (2008) and the S-wave effect study of Xie (2009), and enables more reliable extraction of β_s by quantifying the S-wave contamination from the f_0(980) resonance in the φ J/ψ channel.
