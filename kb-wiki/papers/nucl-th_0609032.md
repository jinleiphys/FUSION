# Fusion Hindrance and the Role of Shell Effects in the Superheavy Mass Region

> We present the first attempt of the systematical investigation about the effects of shell correction energy for dynamical processes, which include fusion, fusion-fission and quasi-fission processes. In the superheavy mass region, for the fusion process, the shell correction energy plays a very important role and enhances the fusion probability, when the colliding partner has strong shell structure. By analyzing the trajectory in the three-dimensional coordinate space with a Langevin equation, we reveal the mechanism of the enhancement of the fusion probability caused by shell effects.

## Key claim

Shell correction energy enhances the fusion probability in superheavy element synthesis, but only when the colliding partner has strong shell structure; this enhancement operates through two mechanisms: the formation of "cold fusion valleys" in the potential energy surface and the appearance of a temporary pocket at the turning point that traps the trajectory and blocks it from moving to the fission region.

## Method

The authors employed a three-dimensional Langevin equation with collective coordinates (distance between potential centers, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) to simulate fusion, fusion-fission, and quasi-fission trajectories. The potential energy surface was calculated using a two-center shell model, and the dynamics were compared for cases with and without shell correction energy at zero nuclear temperature. Trajectories were analyzed for different injection points corresponding to different projectile-target combinations, specifically at mass asymmetry values of 0.46 and 0.24.

## Key numbers

- For the system 284114, the fusion probability at excitation energy E* = 20 MeV is larger for the injection point with initial mass asymmetry alpha = 0.46 (reaction 76Ge + 208Pb) than for alpha = 0.24 (reaction 108Ru + 176Yb), because the shell correction energy makes the potential energy at the contact point lower for the former.
- The cold fusion valleys in the potential energy surface for 292114 disappear when the deformation parameter delta changes to about 0.4.
- For the system 256No (Z=102), a temporary pocket appears at the turning point (z approximately 0.3, delta approximately 0.5) when shell correction energy is included, which traps the trajectory and enhances fusion probability.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of fusion hindrance in the superheavy mass region (Aritomo and Ohta, 2006) by now focusing on fusion enhancement from shell effects. It builds on the concept of "cold fusion valleys" identified by Gupta and Greiner (1999, 2000) and connects to experimental cold fusion reactions used to synthesize superheavy nuclei by Hofmann and Munzenberg (2000) and Morita et al. (2004). The study also relates to work by Satou et al. (2002) and Ikezoe et al. (2003) on how nuclear structure of projectile-target combinations correlates to touching probability. The results enable future studies incorporating temperature dependence of the shell correction energy during the dynamical process.
