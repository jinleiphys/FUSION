# Jet energy loss and fragmentation in heavy ion collisions

> Recent LHC results indicate a suppression of jet fragmentation functions in Pb-Pb collisions at intermediate values of $ξ=\ln(1/z)$. This seems to contradict the picture of energy loss based on the induced QCD radiation that is expected to lead to the enhancement of in-medium fragmentation functions. We use an effective 1+1 dimensional quasi-Abelian model to describe the dynamical modification of jet fragmentation in the medium. We find that this approach describes the data, and argue that there is no contradiction between the LHC results and the picture of QCD radiation induced by the in-medium scattering of the jet. The physics that underlies the suppression of the in-medium fragmentation function at intermediate values of $ξ=\ln(1/z)$ is the partial screening of the color charge of the jet by the comoving medium-induced gluon.

## Key claim

The suppression of the in-medium jet fragmentation function at intermediate values of ξ = ln(1/z) observed by CMS is not contradictory to the expected enhancement from induced QCD bremsstrahlung; the suppression arises from the partial screening of the jet's color charge by a comoving medium-induced gluon, as described by an effective 1+1 dimensional quasi-Abelian model.

## Method

The authors use a bosonized form of massless QED in 1+1 dimensions (the Schwinger model) as an effective theory of jet fragmentation, extended to N_c copies of the U(1) gauge group to account for color rotation of the quark in the medium. They construct external currents representing the quark jet undergoing multiple scatterings in the medium and emitting a perturbative gluon, then compute the momentum distribution of produced particles from the Fourier transform of the scalar field source. The ratio of in-medium to vacuum fragmentation functions is evaluated for a jet energy of 120 GeV, with the medium length set to 5 fm and the first scattering at t1 ≈ 1 fm, for different mean free paths.

## Key numbers

- Jet energy p_jet = 120 GeV used for the fragmentation function ratio calculation.
- Medium length L = 5 fm; first scattering time t1 ≈ 1 fm (assumed thermalization time).
- Mean free paths λ = 0.57, 0.4, and 0.2 fm used for the three curves in the fragmentation ratio plot.
- Scalar field mass m = 0.6 GeV used in the model.
- The final-state gluon is emitted at a rapidity interval Δη ~ 1/α_s ≈ 2 away from the leading parton.
- For the D_AA calculation, jet energy range 20 < p_jet < 40 GeV is used with λ = 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quasi-Abelian model (Loshaj and Kharzeev, 2011) by including perturbative medium-induced gluon radiation and non-zero momentum transfer from the jet to the medium, addressing a deficiency where energy loss was previously due only to enhanced soft hadron production from color rotation. The results reconcile the CMS (2012) observation of suppressed fragmentation at intermediate ξ with the conventional pQCD picture of induced bremsstrahlung (Baier et al., 1996; Gyulassy et al., 2000) and the BDMPS approach, and a similar effect from coherent parton branching was recently considered by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2012). The model enables future development of a hybrid approach combining DGLAP perturbative evolution, induced gluon radiation, and non-perturbative dynamical fragmentation.
