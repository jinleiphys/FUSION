# Parton distribution functions from Lattice QCD

> We present recent results on the first moments of parton distributions using gauge configurations generated with two degenerate flavors of light twisted mass quarks with pion mass fixed approximately to its physical value. We also present a first study of the vector parton distribution function using a twisted mass ensemble at pion mass of 373 MeV.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations at near-physical pion mass, including for the first time disconnected quark contributions at the physical point, bring the computed intrinsic spin of the nucleon into agreement with experiment, but the results for the nucleon momentum fraction and spin are preliminary and require larger statistics at larger sink-source separations to fully control excited-state contamination.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements using gauge configurations generated with two degenerate flavors of twisted mass quarks (Nf=2) and, for the gluon moment, also Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass ensembles. Three-point functions are formed by inserting local operators at a time slice between source and sink, and the desired matrix elements are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point functions using the summed-ratio method to suppress excited-state contributions. Renormalization is performed non-perturbatively with perturbative subtraction of O(a^2) lattice artifacts, and disconnected contributions are computed using GPU-accelerated methods with O(150,000) statistics.

## Key numbers

- For the Nf=2 twisted mass plus clover ensemble at a=0.093 fm and m_pi=132 MeV, the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g = 0.282(39) in the MSbar scheme at μ=2 GeV.
- At the physical point, preliminary values for the total quark spin are J^{u+d}=0.296(17) and the orbital angular momentum L^{u+d}=0.067(28).
- At the physical point, the intrinsic quark spin is 1/2 ΔΣ^{u+d}=0.229(20) and including strange quarks gives 1/2 ΔΣ^{u+d+s}=0.211(21).
- For the Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass ensemble at m_pi=373 MeV, exact deflation of the lowest ~1800 eigen-modes yields a speedup factor of 20 compared to the conjugate gradient method after ~1000 right-hand sides.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure by the ETM Collaboration (Abdel-Rehim 2015, Abdel-Rehim 2014) to near-physical pion mass, including for the first time disconnected contributions at the physical point. It builds on prior results from LHPC (Green 2012) and QCDSF/UKQCD (Bali 2014, Pleiter 2011) for the nucleon momentum fraction, and enables future determination of the individual quark angular momentum contributions to the nucleon spin, as well as direct computation of parton distribution functions via the quasi-distribution approach of Ji (2013).
