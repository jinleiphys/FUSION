# Effective theory for deformed nuclei

> Techniques from effective field theory are applied to nuclear rotation. This approach exploits the spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry and the separation of scale between low-energy Nambu-Goldstone rotational modes and high-energy vibrational and nucleonic degrees of freedom. A power counting is established and the Hamiltonian is constructed at next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

This paper proposes that the low-energy rotational excitations of deformed nuclei can be described model-independently using an effective theory based on the spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry from SO(3) to SO(2), treating even-even, odd-mass, and odd-odd nuclei on an equal footing. The approach is an effective theory (not a full field theory) because the Nambu-Goldstone modes depend only on time, not space, due to the finite size of the nucleus.

## Method

The author constructs the low-energy Lagrangian by parameterizing the coset SO(3)/SO(2) using two Euler angles as Nambu-Goldstone modes, computing the angular velocities E_x, E_y, and E_z from the time derivative of the coset element. A power counting is established based on the separation between the low-energy rotational scale (tens of keV) and the high-energy vibrational scale (about 1 MeV), and the Hamiltonian is derived at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) via Legendre transformation and quantization in curvilinear coordinates. For odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, a Wess-Zumino term proportional to E_z is added to account for nonzero ground-state spin and time-reversal symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- For even-even rare-earth nuclei, the rotational J^pi = 2+ state lies at about 80 keV, while the vibrational I_0 = 2 band head is at about 1 MeV, giving a separation of scales xi/Omega approximately 10^-1.
- The leading-order Hamiltonian yields eigenvalues E_LO(q,l) = [l(l+1) - q^2] / (2C_0), where q = I_0 is the ground-state spin and C_0 is a low-energy constant determined by fit to data.
- The NLO Hamiltonian adds a term proportional to (E_x^2 + E_y^2)^2 with low-energy constant C_2, where C_2/C_0 ~ Omega^-2, making the NLO correction suppressed by (xi/Omega)^2 relative to LO.
- The NLO spectrum is a polynomial of degree two in l(l+1), with the low-energy constant C_2 determined by fitting the spacing between the l = I_0 + 4 and l = I_0 rotational states.

## Context

This work extends the Bohr-Mottelson model (Bohr and Mottelson) and the general collective model (Gneuss, Hess, Greiner) by providing a systematic power counting and a model-independent framework based on effective field theory techniques, in contrast to algebraic models like the interacting boson model (Arima) which are difficult to extend systematically. The approach enables the computation of rotational spectra with controlled error estimates and can be extended to include coupling to vibrational and nucleonic degrees of freedom, building on the nonlinear realization methods of Weinberg, Coleman-Wess-Zumino, and Callan-Coleman-Wess-Zumino, as well as Leutwyler's application to non-relativistic systems.
