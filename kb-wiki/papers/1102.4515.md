# Chirally motivated K^- nuclear potentials

> In-medium subthreshold KbarN scattering amplitudes calculated within a chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled-channel model are used self consistently to confront K^- atom data across the periodic table. Substantially deeper K^- nuclear potentials are obtained compared to the shallow potentials derived in some approaches from threshold amplitudes, with Re V_{chiral} = -(85+/-5) MeV at nuclear matter density. When KbarNN contributions are incorporated phenomenologically, a very deep K^- nuclear potential results, Re V_{chiral+phen.} = -(180+/-5) MeV, in agreement with density dependent potentials obtained in purely phenomenological fits to the data. Self consistent dynamical calculations of K^- nuclear quasibound states are reported and discussed.

# Chirally motivated K^- nuclear potentials

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that kaonic atom data probe the subthreshold regime of the in-medium K^-N scattering amplitude, and that chirally motivated models produce K^- nuclear potentials with depths of approximately 85 MeV at nuclear matter density when using only single-nucleon mechanisms, but require additional phenomenological multi-nucleon terms to reach depths of approximately 180 MeV that match purely phenomenological fits.

## Method

The authors constructed in-medium K^-N scattering amplitudes using the chirally motivated coupled-channel separable-potential model CS30, incorporating Pauli blocking and self-consistent meson and baryon self energies. These subthreshold amplitudes were used to solve the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently for kaonic atoms across the periodic table, with the energy shift given by sqrt(s) ≈ E_th - B_N - ξ_N B_K - 15.1(ρ/ρ_0)^(2/3) + ξ_K Re V_K^-(ρ). For K^- nuclear quasibound states, the same self-consistent scheme was applied within a dynamical relativistic mean-field framework that allowed nuclear density polarization.

## Key numbers

- Re V_chiral = -(85 ± 5) MeV at nuclear matter density ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^(-3), derived from the CS30 model without adjustable parameters for kaonic atoms
- Re V_chiral+phen. = -(180 ± 5) MeV at nuclear matter density, obtained after adding phenomenological ρ^2 terms representing K^-NN contributions
- Im V_chiral = -(20 ± 10) MeV and Im V_chiral+phen. = -(70 ± 20) MeV at nuclear matter density for the CS30 model
- Binding energies B_K for 1s K^- nuclear quasibound states: 54.8 MeV (^12C), 54.9 MeV (^16O), 68.2 MeV (^40Ca), 77.3 MeV (^90Zr), 82.2 MeV (^208Pb)
- Widths Γ_K for 1s K^- nuclear quasibound states: 11.7 MeV (^12C), 11.4 MeV (^16O), 9.8 MeV (^40Ca), 9.4 MeV (^90Zr), 8.6 MeV (^208Pb)
- The range parameter α = 639 MeV for the separable-potential form factor in the CS30 model
- The Fermi motion contribution to the energy shift: 15.1(ρ/ρ_0)^(2/3) MeV

## Context

This work extends the chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled-channel approach of Cieplý and Smejkal (2010) to in-medium applications, and provides a microscopic link between the shallow K^- nuclear potentials of Ramos and Oset (2000) and Baca, García-Recio, and Nieves (2000) derived from threshold amplitudes, and the deep phenomenological potentials obtained from global fits to kaonic atom data by Friedman, Gal, and collaborators (1993, 2006). The self-consistent dynamical calculations of K^- nuclear quasibound states enable comparison with the static calculations of Weise and Härtle (2008), and the results inform experimental searches for deeply bound K^-pp configurations reported by the FINUDA and DISTO collaborations.
