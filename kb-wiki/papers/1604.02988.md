# $N^*$ Resonances in Lattice QCD from (mostly) Low to (sometimes) High Virtualities

> I present a survey of calculations of the excited $N^*$ spectrum in lattice QCD. I then describe recent advances aimed at extracting the momentum-dependent phase shifts from lattice calculations, notably in the meson sector, and the potential for their application to baryons. I conclude with a discussion of calculations of the electromagnetic transition form factors to excited nucleons, including calculations at high $Q^2$.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the excited nucleon (N*) spectrum are now able to access states up to spin 7/2 and reveal a counting of states richer than the quark model, but the low-lying Roper resonance ordering remains controversial and most calculations lack the multi-hadron operators needed for a complete description of resonances.

## Method

The author surveys lattice QCD calculations of the excited N* spectrum using the variational method with a basis of interpolating operators constructed from up to two covariant derivatives, enabling orbital angular momentum up to L=2 and states up to spin 7/2. The formalism for extracting momentum-dependent phase shifts from finite-volume energies via the Lüscher method is described for the meson sector, with discussion of its potential extension to baryons. Electromagnetic transition form factors are computed from three-point correlation functions, treating both incoming and outgoing particles as single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of 396 MeV used in the spin-identified excited nucleon and Delta spectrum calculations on a 16^3 x 64 anisotropic clover lattice (Edwards 2011)
- The spectrum is shown in units of the Omega mass, with the strange quark mass at its physical value
- The χQCD group's calculation using overlap fermions on a domain-wall fermion ensemble is the exception that shows a low-lying Roper resonance below the lowest negative-parity state (Liu 2014)
- The magnetic dipole form factor G_M1(Q^2) and the ratio R_EM = -G_E2(Q^2)/G_M1(Q^2) are shown for the N to Delta transition (Alexandrou 2010)

## Context

This survey extends the benchmark lattice QCD calculations of the low-lying hadron spectrum by the BMW collaboration (Durr 2008) and their recent inclusion of QED for the proton-neutron mass difference (Borsanyi 2014). The work builds on the variational method developments by Basak (2005) and Edwards (2011, 2012) for constructing baryon operators with definite J^P, and on the Lüscher method (Luscher 1986, 1990) for relating finite-volume energies to infinite-volume phase shifts. The calculations enable future confrontation between lattice QCD and experimental N* measurements, particularly as the full Lüscher formalism is extended from the meson sector (Dudek 2012) to baryon resonances.
