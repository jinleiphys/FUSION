# Comment on "A Quantum-Mechanical Equivalent-Photon Spectrum for Heavy Ion Physics"

> We critically discuss the recently developed quantum-mechanical equivalent photon spectrum by Benesh et al. We point out that the key point, the strong absorption in heavy ion collisions, is not treated adequately. Conclusions drawn from such a spectrum are invalid. Equivalent photon spectra appropriate for heavy ions, have been given before in quantal as well as semiclassical versions and were found to be very satisfactory.

# Comment on "A Quantum-Mechanical Equivalent-Photon Spectrum for Heavy Ion Physics"

## Key claim

The quantum-mechanical equivalent photon spectrum proposed by Benesh, Hayes, and Friar (1996) is invalid for heavy ion collisions because it treats strong absorption inadequately through a plane-wave Born approximation with a phenomenological momentum cutoff, rather than using a proper coordinate-space treatment with Glauber wave functions. The authors argue that previous quantal and semiclassical equivalent photon spectra (Bertulani and Baur, 1988; Bertulani and Nathan, 1993) remain satisfactory, especially for mildly relativistic collisions with gamma less than 2-3.

## Method

The authors derive a quantum-mechanical equivalent photon spectrum using Glauber wave functions for the projectile in the initial and final states, with a black-disk model where the Glauber phase vanishes for impact parameters b less than the strong absorption radius R and includes the Coulomb phase for b greater than R. They contrast this with the approach of Benesh et al. (1996), which uses a Born-approximation T-matrix and introduces a phenomenological cutoff q_max approximately 1/R on transverse momentum transfer, arguing that such momentum-space cutoffs lack a one-to-one correspondence with impact-parameter-space cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- The significant deviations claimed by Benesh et al. (1996) occur for mildly relativistic collisions with gamma less than 2-3, which the authors argue is surprising given the validity of semiclassical and eikonal methods in this regime.
- The total (angle-integrated) cross-section is the same for semiclassical and quantum treatments in the sharp-cutoff model, as shown in Bertulani and Baur (1988) and Bertulani and Nathan (1993).

## Context

This comment directly criticizes the equivalent photon spectrum of Benesh, Hayes, and Friar (1996), arguing it contradicts well-established results from Winther and Alder (1979), Bertulani and Baur (1988), and Bertulani and Nathan (1993). The authors also defend the multiphonon excitation mechanisms used in semiclassical analyses by Norbury and Baur (1993) and Llope and Braun-Munzinger (1993) against criticism from Benesh et al., noting that multiphonon effects are a natural consequence of QED observed in relativistic electromagnetic excitation by Emling (1994) and Boretzky et al. (1996). The comment points to further developments including mutual excitation spectra by Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1996) and deformed projectile treatments by Bertulani (1993).
