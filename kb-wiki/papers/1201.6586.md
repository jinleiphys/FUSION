# Universality in Four-Boson Systems

> We report recent advances on the study of universal weakly bound four-boson states from the solutions of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with zero-range two-body interactions. In particular, we present the correlation between the energies of successive tetramers between two neighbor Efimov trimers and compare it to recent finite range potential model calculations. We provide further results on the large momentum structure of the tetramer wave function, where the four-body scale, introduced in the regularization procedure of the bound state equations in momentum space, is clearly manifested. The results we are presenting confirm a previous conjecture on a four-body scaling behavior, which is independent of the three-body one. We show that the correlation between the positions of two successive resonant four-boson recombination peaks are consistent with recent data, as well as with recent calculations close to the unitary limit. Systematic deviations suggest the relevance of range corrections.

## Key claim

The paper confirms a previous conjecture that four-boson systems exhibit a universal four-body scaling behavior that is independent of the three-body scaling, but notes that systematic deviations from the predicted scaling curves suggest that finite-range corrections are relevant and should be considered in future work.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous set of coupled Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) integral equations for four identical bosons using a renormalized zero-range two-body interaction. The regularization procedure introduced two independent subtraction scales (μ₃ and μ₄) in the free four-body Green's functions, where μ₃ fixes the three-body observables and μ₄ controls the four-body scale. The coupled equations for the reduced FY amplitudes (K-type for trimer-plus-atom configurations and H-type for dimer-plus-dimer configurations) were solved in momentum space to obtain tetramer bound state energies and wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The N+1 tetramer state emerges from the 3+1 threshold at a universal ratio E₄^(N)/E₃ = 4.6 for E₂=0, independent of N.
- For tetramers between two successive Efimov trimers, the scaling plot should be used above √(E₃/E₄^(N)) > 1/22.7, allowing up to three tetramers between two Efimov trimers.
- Calculations were performed for scale ratio μ₄/μ₃ = 200, where two four-body excited states exist alongside the ground state.
- The three-body regularization parameter μ₃ was set 200 times smaller than the four-body parameter μ₄ in the structure calculations.

## Context

This work extends the Efimov effect from three-boson systems to four-boson systems, building on prior calculations by Frederico et al. (2011) and Hadizadeh et al. (2011, 2012) that first reported the four-body scaling limit cycle. The results are compared with finite-range potential model calculations by Stecher et al., Deltuva (2012), and others, as well as experimental data from Pollack et al., Ferlaino et al., and Berninger et al. (2011). The paper enables future experimental searches for independent four-body scaling in cold atom systems near Feshbach resonances, and identifies the need to incorporate range corrections into universal scaling functions.
