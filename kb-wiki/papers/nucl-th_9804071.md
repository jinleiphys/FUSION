# A consistent explanation of the Roper phenomenology

> We study the electromagnetic transitions of the Roper N(1440) resonance. Our results, when combined with the previously obtained for the mass and the pionic strong decay widths of the Roper, show that within a non-relativistic constituent quark model scheme, a comprehensible understanding of the Roper phenomenology can be achieved. They also seem to support the view of the Roper as a radial excitation of the nucleon, though more experimental data are needed to reach a definitive conclusion.

## Key claim

Within a non-relativistic constituent quark model combined with an extended vector meson dominance (EVMD) approach that includes a direct quark-core coupling, the Roper N(1440) resonance can be understood as a radial excitation of the nucleon, though the authors note that more experimental data are needed to reach a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors calculate electromagnetic transition amplitudes for the Roper resonance using an extended vector meson dominance (EVMD) model, where the photon couples to the baryon both through intermediate vector mesons (rho, omega) via a $^3P_0$ pair-creation mechanism and through a direct coupling to the quark core using a modified elementary emission model with a $(p/E)$ expansion. Two different potential models (labeled VI and VII) are used to generate the baryon wave functions, with VII incorporating a phenomenological three-quark interaction that accurately reproduces the Roper mass and pionic decay widths from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The proton magnetic moment is fixed to $\mu_p = 2.79$ and the proton square mean radius to $\langle r_p^2 \rangle = 0.54$ fm$^2$ to determine the model parameters.
- For potential model VI, the fitted parameters are $c = 0.49$ and $\gamma/f_\rho = -0.38$; for model VII, $c = 0.17$ and $\gamma/f_\rho = -0.71$.
- The predicted neutron magnetic moment is $\mu_n = -1.82$ for model VI and $\mu_n = -1.84$ for model VII.
- The predicted neutron square mean radius is $\langle r_n^2 \rangle = -0.003$ fm$^2$ for model VI and $\langle r_n^2 \rangle = -0.002$ fm$^2$ for model VII.
- The Roper transverse amplitude changes sign at $Q^2 \approx 0.5$ to $1$ GeV$^2$, a feature suggested by present data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful application of the same non-relativistic constituent quark model to the Roper mass and pionic strong decay widths (Cano 1996, Cano 1997), now adding electromagnetic transitions. It contrasts with alternative descriptions of the Roper such as the breathing bag model (Brown 1983, Guichon 1985) and hybrid state descriptions (Barnes 1983, Close 1988, Carlson 1991, Li 1992) that confer an exotic nature to the Roper. The results enable further tests on other resonances, particularly the Delta(1600) as the Roper of the Delta, where the stronger quark model dependence may provide a more stringent test of the radial excitation interpretation.
