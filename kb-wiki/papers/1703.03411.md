# A Complete Basis of Helicity Operators for Subleading Factorization

> Factorization theorems underly our ability to make predictions for many processes involving the strong interaction. Although typically formulated at leading power, the study of factorization at subleading power is of interest both for improving the precision of calculations, as well as for understanding the all orders structure of QCD. We use the SCET helicity operator formalism to construct a complete power suppressed basis of hard scattering operators for $e^+e^-\to$ dijets, $e^- p\to e^-$ jet, and constrained Drell-Yan, including the first two subleading orders in the amplitude level power expansion. We analyze the form of the hard, jet, and soft function contributions to the power suppressed cross section for $e^+e^-\to$ dijet event shapes, and give results for the lowest order matching to the contributing operators. These results will be useful for studies of power corrections both in fixed order and resummed perturbation theory.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a complete basis of power-suppressed hard scattering operators for processes with two back-to-back collinear sectors (e+e- to dijets, e- p to e- jet, and constrained Drell-Yan) at the first two subleading orders in the amplitude-level power expansion, using the SCET helicity operator formalism to simplify the construction that is otherwise cumbersome with traditional methods.

## Method
The authors use the SCET helicity operator formalism, building on the leading-power basis of Moult (2015), to construct operator building blocks of definite helicity that are scalars under Lorentz transformations, eliminating the need for complicated Dirac and Lorentz index contractions. They enumerate all possible operators consistent with symmetry constraints including reparametrization invariance (RPI) and angular momentum conservation, and perform tree-level matching from QCD to determine the Wilson coefficients for the subleading operators in the e+e- to dijet case.

## Key numbers
- The power counting parameter lambda is defined such that collinear momentum components scale as (n·p, nbar·p, p_perp) ~ nbar·p (lambda^2, 1, lambda), with lambda << 1.
- The subleading Lagrangian is known to O(lambda^2) from Bauer (2003), and the paper constructs operators at O(lambda^2) in the amplitude-level power expansion.
- The leading power factorization formula for thrust involves terms of O(tau^{-1}) including delta(tau), with power corrections starting at O(tau^0) and O(Lambda_QCD/(Q tau)).
- The cross section expansion is written as dsigma/dtau = dsigma^(0)/dtau + dsigma^(1)/dtau + dsigma^(2)/dtau + dsigma^(3)/dtau + O(tau), where dsigma^(n)/dtau ~ tau^{-1+n/2}.

## Context
This work extends the leading-power SCET factorization framework for dijet observables, such as the thrust factorization theorem of Korchemsky (1999), Fleming (2007), and Schwartz (2007), to subleading power. It builds on the helicity operator approach of Moult (2015) and the subleading operator analysis of Kolodrubetz (2016). The results enable systematic studies of power corrections in both fixed-order and resummed perturbation theory for e+e- event shapes, deep inelastic scattering, and Drell-Yan processes, and provide a foundation for deriving subleading factorization theorems analogous to those already known for B decays from Lee (2004).
