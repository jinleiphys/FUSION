# 3D structure of hadrons by generalized distribution amplitudes and gravitational form factors

> Generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) are one type of three-dimensional structure functions, and they are related to the generalized distribution functions (GPDs) by the $s$-$t$ crossing of the Mandelstam variables. The GDA studies provide information on three-dimensional tomography of hadrons. The GDAs can be investigated by the two-photon process $γ^* γ\to h\bar h$, and the GPDs are studied by the deeply virtual Compton scattering $γ^* h \to γh$. The GDA studies had been pure theoretical topics, although the GPDs have been experimentally investigated, because there was no available experimental measurement. Recently, the Belle collaboration reported their measurements on the $γ^* γ\to π^0 π^0$ differential cross section, so that it became possible to find the GDAs from their measurements. Here, we report our analysis of the Belle data for determining the pion GDAs. From the GDAs, the timelike gravitational form factors $Θ_1 (s)$ and $Θ_2 (s)$ can be calculated, which are mechanical (pressure, shear force) and mass (energy) form factors, respectively. They are converted to the spacelike form factors by using the dispersion relation, and then gravitational radii are evaluated for the pion. The mass and mechanical radii are obtained from $Θ_2$ and $Θ_1$ as $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mass}}} =0.56 \sim 0.69$ fm and $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mech}}} =1.45 \sim 1.56$ fm, whereas the experimental charge radius is $\sqrt {\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{charge}}} =0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm for the charged pion. Future developments are expected in this new field to explore gravitational physics in the quark and gluon level.

## Key claim

The authors report the first extraction of pion generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) and gravitational form factors from actual experimental data (Belle measurements of the two-photon process gamma* gamma -> pi0 pi0), but the results are obtained at leading order in the QCD coupling constant and leading twist, and the extracted gravitational radii are presented as ranges (0.56-0.69 fm for mass radius, 1.45-1.56 fm for mechanical radius) rather than single values.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared analysis of Belle data on the differential cross section for gamma* gamma -> pi0 pi0 to determine the quark GDAs for the pion, using a parametrization that includes S-wave and D-wave contributions with continuum and resonance terms (f0 and f2 resonances). From the second moments of the determined GDAs, they calculated the timelike gravitational form factors Theta_1(s) and Theta_2(s), which were then converted to spacelike form factors using dispersion relations, and finally the gravitational radii were obtained from the Fourier transforms of these form factors.

## Key numbers

- Mass radius of the pion: sqrt(<r^2>_mass) = 0.56 to 0.69 fm
- Mechanical radius of the pion: sqrt(<r^2>_mech) = 1.45 to 1.56 fm
- Experimental charge radius of the charged pion: sqrt(<r^2>_charge) = 0.672 +/- 0.008 fm
- The f2(1270) resonance appears as a peak in the D-wave contribution to the timelike gravitational form factor Theta_2(s)
- The f0(980) resonance was not included in the analysis because its q-qbar decay constant would produce cross sections much larger than the Belle data at W approximately 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of GDAs, which had been purely theoretical since their introduction, by using the first experimental measurements from the Belle collaboration (Masuda et al., 2015) on gamma* gamma -> pi0 pi0. The GDAs are related to GPDs by s-t crossing of Mandelstam variables, and this analysis enables the extraction of gravitational form factors and radii from experimental data for the first time. The results open the possibility of studying gravitational physics at the quark and gluon level, with future improvements expected from the upgraded KEK-B factory and the International Linear Collider project.
