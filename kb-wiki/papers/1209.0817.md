# The non-linear Glasma

> We study the evolution of quantum fluctuations in the Glasma created immediately after the collision of heavy nuclei. It is shown how the presence of instabilities leads to an enhancement of non-linear interactions among initially small fluctuations. The non-linear dynamics leads to an enhanced growth of fluctuations in a large momentum region exceeding by far the originally unstable band. We investigate the dependence on the coupling constant at weak coupling using classical statistical lattice simulations for SU(2) gauge theory and show how these non-linearities can be analytically understood within the framework of two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action techniques. The dependence on the coupling constant is only logarithmic in accordance with analytic expectations. Concerning the isotropization of bulk quantities, our results indicate that the system exhibits an order-one anisotropy on parametrically large time scales. Despite this fact, we find that gauge invariant pressure correlation functions seem to exhibit a power law behavior characteristic for wave turbulence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Glasma created after heavy-ion collisions, non-linear interactions among initially small fluctuations lead to "secondary" instabilities with enhanced growth rates over a large momentum region, but the system nevertheless retains an order-one anisotropy on parametrically large time scales, despite gauge-invariant pressure correlation functions exhibiting power-law behavior characteristic of wave turbulence.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations for SU(2) gauge theory in the weak-coupling regime, solving the evolution equations for the Glasma background fields and the statistical two-point correlation functions. They analyzed the dynamics using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action framework to identify the leading self-energy corrections, specifically the one-loop diagram containing two three-gluon vertices, which becomes relevant when statistical fluctuations grow to order 1/g. The evolution was studied in co-moving (τ, η) coordinates with the Fock-Schwinger gauge condition (Aτ = 0), using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the nuclear color charge densities.

- The exponential growth of statistical fluctuations follows the form F(τ, τ') ∝ exp[Γ(ν)(√(g²μτ) + √(g²μτ'))] for characteristic modes, where ν is the Fourier coefficient with respect to relative rapidity.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of plasma instabilities in the Glasma (GlasmaRV, GlasmaGelis, GlasmaFukushima) and builds on earlier observations of secondary instabilities in non-expanding gauge theories (Berges, Scheffler, and Sexty, 2008; Berges, Scheffler, and Serreau, 2009) and cosmological models (Berges and Serreau, 2003). The paper provides an analytic understanding of non-linear amplification within the 2PI effective action framework (Berges, 2004) and enables future studies of thermalization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions, where the classical-statistical approximation can be employed to treat fluctuations on equal footing with background fields beyond the linear instability regime.
