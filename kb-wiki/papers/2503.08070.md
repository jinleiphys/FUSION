# Nuclear Structure Properties and Stellar Weak Rates for 76Se: Unblocking of the Gamow Teller Strength

> At finite temperatures ($\geq 10^7$K), $^{76}$Se is abundant in the core of massive stars and electron capture on $^{76}$Se has a consequential role to play in the dynamics of core collapse. The present work may be classified into two main categories. In the first phase, we study the nuclear structure properties of $^{76}$Se using the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1). The IBM-1 investigations include the energy levels, $B(E2)$ values, and the prediction of the geometry. We performed the extended consistent-Q formalism (ECQF) calculation and later the triaxial formalism calculation (constructed by adding the cubic term to the ECQF). The geometry of $^{76}$Se can be envisioned within the formalism of the potential energy surface based on the classical limit of the IBM-1 model. In the second phase, we reconfirm the unblocking of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in $^{76}$Se (a test case for nuclei having $N > 40$ and $Z < 40$). Using the deformed pn-QRPA model, we calculate GT transitions, stellar electron capture cross section (within the limit of low momentum transfer), and stellar weak rates for $^{76}$Se. The distinguishing feature of our calculation is a state-by-state evaluation of stellar weak rates in a fully microscopic fashion. Results are compared with experimental data and previous calculations. The calculated GT distribution fulfills the Ikeda sum rule. Rates for $β$-delayed neutrons and emission probabilities are also calculated. Our study suggests that at high stellar temperatures and low densities, the $β^+$-decay on $^{76}$Se should not be neglected and needs to be taken into consideration along with electron capture rates for simulation of presupernova evolution of massive stars.

## Key claim

The paper reconfirms that Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in ⁷⁶Se is unblocked under terrestrial conditions due to nuclear correlation effects, and further demonstrates that at high stellar temperatures and low densities, β⁺-decay on ⁷⁶Se should not be neglected alongside electron capture rates for presupernova evolution simulations. The unblocking is conditional on both nuclear correlation effects and finite temperature, with the latter producing significant thermal unblocking of the GT⁺ channel.

## Method

The authors performed two phases of calculation: first, nuclear structure properties of ⁷⁶Se were studied using the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) with both the extended consistent-Q formalism (ECQF) and a triaxial formalism that adds a cubic term to the ECQF Hamiltonian. Second, using the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a separable interaction in a multi-shell deformed single-particle space, they calculated GT transitions, stellar electron capture cross sections (within the low momentum transfer limit), and stellar weak rates for ⁷⁶Se, performing a state-by-state evaluation of stellar weak rates in a fully microscopic fashion without assuming the Brink-Axel hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- Energy ratio in the ground state band: R₄/₂ = 2.38, located between the U(5) ratio of 2.00 and the O(6) ratio of 2.50
- The re-normalized Ikeda sum rule value is 7.26, and the calculated (quenched) difference in strength values was 7.14, corresponding to 98.34% fulfillment of the Ikeda sum rule
- The GT strength was quenched by a factor of f_q² = (0.55)²
- The ratio of axial to vector coupling constant (g_A/g_V) was taken as -1.2694
- Pairing gaps used: Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/√A MeV (giving 1.38 MeV for A=76)
- The deformation parameter β₂ used in pn-QRPA was 0.309, extracted from E2 transition data
- The boson effective charge used for B(E2) calculations was e_b = 0.097 eb
- As stellar temperature increases from 0.5 MeV to 1.0 MeV, the electron capture cross section increases by roughly two orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Nabi et al. (2016, 2017) that applied both IBM-1 and pn-QRPA calculations to waiting point nuclei along the N=Z chain, but unlike those exotic short-lived nuclei, ⁷⁶Se is stable with ample experimental data. The unblocking of GT strength in ⁷⁶Se was experimentally verified independently by (d,²He) charge-exchange reaction at KVI Groningen (Grewe et al., 2008) and by (n,p) reaction at TRIUMF (Helmer et al., 1997), and the present work reconfirms this unblocking theoretically. The results enable improved simulation of presupernova evolution of massive stars by providing state-by-state weak rates that account for both electron capture and β⁺-decay on ⁷⁶Se at finite temperatures.
