# Final state interaction effects in electrodisintegration of the deuteron within the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The electrodisintegration of the deuteron is considered within a relativistic model of nucleon-nucleon interaction based on the Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable interaction kernel. The exclusive cross section is calculated within the impulse approximation under various kinematic conditions. Final state interactions between the outgoing nucleons are taken into account. The comparison of nonrelativistic and relativistic calculations is presented. Partial-wave states of the neutron-proton pair with total angular momentum $J=0,1$ are considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final state interaction (FSI) effects in deuteron electrodisintegration become significant at outgoing neutron momenta above 150 MeV/c, even at low momentum transfer, and that these effects always decrease the cross section; however, the relativistic and nonrelativistic FSI contributions are approximately the same in magnitude, contrary to the expectation that relativistic effects would become important at higher energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the exclusive three-differential cross section for the $d(e,e^\prime p)n$ reaction within the relativistic impulse approximation using the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) approach with a separable interaction kernel. The outgoing nucleon pair is described by a BS amplitude that includes both a plane-wave term (PWA) and a term incorporating FSI via the $T$ matrix obtained from the BS equation. The calculation uses the rank-six separable potential MY6 for the bound deuteron and scattered $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ states, and multirank separable potentials for uncoupled partial-wave states with $J=0,1$ ($^1S_0$, $^1P_1$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$); only positive-energy partial-wave states are considered.

## Key numbers

* Under kinematic set I (electron beam energy 0.500 GeV, $Q^2=0.192$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $\sqrt{s}=1.929$ GeV, $\sqrt{s}-2m=0.051$ GeV), relativistic PWA, relativistic FSI, and nonrelativistic calculations all practically coincide and agree with experimental data.
* Under kinematic set II (electron beam energy 0.500 GeV, $Q^2=0.101$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $\sqrt{s}=1.993$ GeV, $\sqrt{s}-2m=0.115$ GeV), FSI effects decrease the cross section noticeably, with a slight difference between nonrelativistic and relativistic results.
* Under kinematic set III (electron beam energy 0.560 GeV, $Q^2=0.038$ (GeV/c)$^2$, $\sqrt{s}=2.057$ GeV, $\sqrt{s}-2m=0.176$ GeV), PWA and FSI calculations differ significantly, and relativistic effects become significant with increasing outgoing neutron momentum $|\pp_n|$.
* FSI effects are significant at outgoing neutron momenta $|\pp_n|>150$ MeV/c even at low momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends previous nonrelativistic calculations of deuteron electrodisintegration (Shebeko, using the Paris potential) and earlier relativistic plane-wave approximations (Bondarenko et al., 2005, 2010) by incorporating FSI within the Bethe-Salpeter approach using a separable kernel. The authors note that earlier relativistic separable kernel models (Graz II, Rupp 1989) could not calculate FSI due to nonintegrable singularities, a problem solved by the new separable kernels (Bondarenko et al., 2008, 2010). The results enable future inclusion of negative-energy partial-wave states and two-body currents, and the approach can be extended to higher energies where inelastic processes (meson production) become relevant, as the separable potentials have been modified to account for such processes (Bondarenko et al., 2011).
