# The decays $Œõ_{b,c}\to N^*\, l\,ŒΩ$ in QCD

> We present an exploratory study of the $Œõ_{c,b}\to N^*$-form factors and the semileptonic decay width within the framework of light-cone sum rules. We use two different methods and two different interpolating currents for the $Œõ_{c,b}$. As interpolating currents we choose an axial-vector like and a pseudoscalar like current. Our results show that the procedure of eliminating negative parity partners is not well suited for the case at hand and that the second approach using structures with highest powers of $p_+$ with an axial-vector like interpolating current gives the most reliable results. Our predictions are based on the models obtained in \cite{Anikin:2015ita,Braun:2014wpa}. The largest uncertainty comes from the uncertainty of the twist 4 parameters $Œ∑_{10},\,Œ∑_{11}$ and we take the spread between the two models in \cite{Anikin:2015ita} as a measure for this. We get \begin{eqnarray} Œì(Œõ_b\to N^*(1535) l ŒΩ)&=&\left(0.0058^{+0.0010}_{-0.0009}\right)\cdot\left(\dfrac{V_{ub}}{3.5\cdot10^{-3}}\right)^2,\quad\mbox{LCSR(1)} \nonumber Œì(Œõ_b\to N^*(1535) l ŒΩ)&=&\left(0.00070^{+0.00012}_{-0.00011}\right)\cdot\left(\dfrac{V_{ub}}{3.5\cdot10^{-3}}\right)^2,\quad\mbox{LCSR(2)} \nonumber Œì(Œõ_c\to N^*(1535) l ŒΩ)&=&\left(0.0064^{+0.0012}_{-0.0011}\right)\cdot \left(\dfrac{V_{cd}}{0.225}\right)^2,\quad\mbox{LCSR(1)}\nonumber Œì(Œõ_c\to N^*(1535) l ŒΩ)&=&\left(0.00077^{+0.00016}_{-0.00014}\right)\cdot \left(\dfrac{V_{cd}}{0.225}\right)^2,\quad\mbox{LCSR(2)}\nonumber \end{eqnarray} as predictions for the respective decay widths, where LCSR(1) and LCSR(2) refer to the two different models of the distribution amplitudes of the $N^*$. It is seen that even a rough measurement of these decays will greatly help to discriminate different models.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exploratory study of the form factors and semileptonic decay widths for the processes Lambda_b,c -> N*(1535) l nu using light-cone sum rules, finding that the procedure of eliminating negative parity partners of the Lambda_b,c is not well suited for this case and that the second approach using structures with highest powers of p_+ with an axial-vector like interpolating current gives the most reliable results, with the largest uncertainty coming from the twist 4 parameters eta_10 and eta_11.

## Method

The authors used light-cone sum rules (LCSR) to calculate the Lambda_b,c -> N* form factors, employing two different methods: one following Khodjamirian (2011) that eliminates negative parity partners of the Lambda_b,c by taking linear combinations of different Lorentz-structures, and another that extracts form factors by choosing Lorentz-structures with the highest possible powers of p_+. They used two different interpolating currents for the Lambda_b,c (axial-vector like and pseudoscalar like) and based their predictions on two models of the N* distribution amplitudes from Anikin (2015) and Braun (2014), taking the spread between these models as a measure of the uncertainty from twist 4 parameters.

## Key numbers

- Decay width for Lambda_b -> N*(1535) l nu: (0.0058^{+0.0010}_{-0.0009}) * (V_ub / 3.5e-3)^2 for LCSR(1) model
- Decay width for Lambda_b -> N*(1535) l nu: (0.00070^{+0.00012}_{-0.00011}) * (V_ub / 3.5e-3)^2 for LCSR(2) model
- Decay width for Lambda_c -> N*(1535) l nu: (0.0064^{+0.0012}_{-0.0011}) * (V_cd / 0.225)^2 for LCSR(1) model
- Decay width for Lambda_c -> N*(1535) l nu: (0.00077^{+0.00016}_{-0.00014}) * (V_cd / 0.225)^2 for LCSR(2) model
- Borel parameter ranges used: M_b^2 = 15-25 GeV^2, s_0^b = 36-40 GeV^2 for Lambda_b; M_c^2 = 5-10 GeV^2, s_0^c = 6-10 GeV^2 for Lambda_c
- Masses used: m_Lambda_c = 2.286 GeV, m_Lambda_c* = 2.595 GeV, m_Lambda_b = 5.620 GeV, m_Lambda_b* = 5.85 GeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Khodjamirian (2011) for Lambda_b -> N form factors to the case of the N*(1535) resonance, complementing lattice studies by Braun (2014) and electromagnetic form factor analyses by Anikin (2015). The results show that these decays are highly sensitive to twist 4 distribution amplitudes of the N*, meaning that even rough measurements at LHCb or the planned PANDA experiment could discriminate between different models of the N* structure, providing an alternative approach to studying nucleon resonances in terms of QCD degrees of freedom.
