# Bulk properties of rotating nuclei and the validity of the liquid drop model at finite angular momenta

> Out of self-consistent semi-classical calculations performed within the so-called Extended Thomas-Fermi approach for 212 nuclei at all even angular momentum values I ranging between 0 and 80 \hbar and using the Skyrme SkM* effective force, the I-dependence of associated liquid drop model parameters has been studied. The latter have been obtained trough separate fits of the calculated values of the strong interaction as well as direct and exchange Coulomb energies. The theoretical data basis so obtained, has allowed to make a rough quantitative assessment of the variation with I of the usual volume and surface energy parameters up to spin of \sim 30-40 \hbar. As a result of the combined variation of the surface and Coulomb energies, it has been shown that this I-dependence results in a significant enhancement of the fission stability of very heavy nuclei, balancing thus partially the well-known instability due to centrifugal forces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the liquid drop model can be applied to rotating nuclei, but only if the volume and surface energy parameters are allowed to vary with angular momentum I (not with angular velocity omega), and this variation is significant only up to spins of approximately 30-40 hbar, beyond which the fit quality degrades substantially.

## Method

Self-consistent semi-classical calculations using the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach with the Skyrme SkM* effective force were performed for 212 even-even nuclei at all even angular momentum values I from 0 to 80 hbar, constraining all solutions to spherical shapes. The nuclear energy (strong interaction part minus Coulomb energy) and the direct and exchange Coulomb energies were fitted separately to liquid drop model parameterizations for each angular momentum value, using a weighted least-squares minimization with a Gauss-Newton iteration method.

## Key numbers

- 212 even-even nuclei were calculated, covering mass numbers from A=34 up to the drip lines, including five long isotopic series (Ca, Ge, Sn, Sm, Pb)
- Angular momenta I from 0 to 80 hbar in steps of 2 hbar were considered
- For I=0: volume energy parameter a_v = -15.453 +/- 0.007 MeV, surface energy parameter a_s = 16.961 +/- 0.037 MeV
- For I=20 hbar: a_v = -15.444 +/- 0.007 MeV, a_s = 16.908 +/- 0.036 MeV
- For I=40 hbar: a_v = -15.436 +/- 0.007 MeV, a_s = 16.848 +/- 0.037 MeV
- For I=60 hbar: a_v = -15.472 +/- 0.013 MeV, a_s = 17.016 +/- 0.069 MeV
- The semi-classical moment of inertia deviates from the rigid body value by a correction of order A^{-2/3}, with orbital contribution coefficient eta_l approximately -1.94 and spin contribution coefficient eta_s approximately 2.63 for the SkM* force
- For a spherically constrained Pb-208 nucleus, the energy gain from allowing anisotropic density deformation was small (approximately 110 keV) and constant up to hbar*omega = 1.25 MeV (I approximately 130 hbar)

## Context

This work extends the preliminary semi-classical calculations of Bencheikh et al. (1994) which used only 33 nuclei with insufficient accuracy, by providing a much larger data basis of 212 nuclei with high numerical precision. The results contradict the common assumption in earlier liquid drop model approaches (Cohen et al. 1982) that all I-dependence is contained in the rigid rotation kinetic energy, showing instead that the volume and surface energy parameters themselves vary with angular momentum. This enables improved macroscopic descriptions of fission stability at high spin, particularly for very heavy nuclei where the I-dependence of surface and Coulomb energies partially counteracts the centrifugal destabilization.
