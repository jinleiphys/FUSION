# Pions in isospin asymmetric matter and nuclear Drell-Yan scattering

> Using a self-consistent delta-hole model the pion propagation in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is studied. In neutron-rich matter, corresponding to heavy nuclei, a significant difference in positive and negative pion light-cone distributions is obtained leading to a nuclear enhancement of up antiquark distribution compared to the down antiquark one. This means that the sea-quark asymmetry in the free nucleon cannot be extracted model independently from an experiment on a neutron-rich nucleus.

## Key claim

Using a self-consistent delta-hole model, the authors show that in neutron-rich nuclear matter the nuclear medium itself generates a significant difference between up and down antiquark distributions, meaning that the sea-quark asymmetry of the free nucleon cannot be extracted model-independently from Drell-Yan experiments on heavy, neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors extend a self-consistent delta-hole model, previously applied to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, to arbitrary proton and neutron densities. They compute the in-medium pion self-energy, including particle-hole and delta-hole contributions with short-range baryon repulsion via Migdal's \(g'\) parameters, and solve the Schwinger-Dyson equations self-consistently for the delta-isobar and pion spectral functions. The resulting pion light-cone momentum distributions for each charge state are then used in a convolution formula to obtain the medium-modified antiquark distributions per nucleon, which enter the proton-nucleus Drell-Yan cross-section ratio.

## Key numbers

- Asymmetry parameter for tungsten: \(\beta = 0.196\).
- Fermi momenta used: \(p_{Fp} = 238\;\text{MeV}\) and \(p_{Fn} = 272\;\text{MeV}\).
- Energy shifts: \(c_p = 40\;\text{MeV}\), \(c_n = 42\;\text{MeV}\); common effective mass \(M_{*n} = M_{*p} = 0.85\;\text{GeV}\), giving an asymmetry energy of 28 MeV.
- Dipole form-factor cut-off: \(\Lambda = 1\;\text{GeV}\) (variation between 0.9 and 1.1 GeV does not change the medium effect appreciably).
- The quantity \((\delta \bar{u}_{\pi/A} - \delta \bar{d}_{\pi/A})/\beta\) is comparable in magnitude to the free-proton difference \(\bar{d}_p - \bar{u}_p\) measured by Hawker (1998).
- The nuclear effect contributes about 50% of the \(\bar{u} - \bar{d}\) difference in a nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent delta-hole model of Korpa (1995) from isospin-symmetric to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, and builds on the pion-cloud model of Dieperink (1997) for nuclear Drell-Yan scattering. It addresses the discrepancy between earlier predictions of a nuclear enhancement of the pion field and the experimental Drell-Yan data of Alde (1990), which showed little enhancement. The results imply that the assumption of negligible nuclear effects in extracting the free-nucleon antiquark asymmetry, as made by Szczurek (1996), is not justified, and that a simultaneous experiment on a neutron-rich and an \(N \approx Z\) nucleus with 5% accuracy could isolate the sea up-down asymmetry.
