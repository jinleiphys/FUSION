# Continuum Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Green's function method for neutron-rich Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn isotopes

> The possible exotic nuclear properties in the neutron-rich Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes are explored with the continuum Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory formulated with the Green's function method. The available experimental two-neutron separation energies $S_{\rm 2n}$ and one-neutron separation energies $S_{\rm n}$ are well reproduced. Much shorter drip lines predicted by $S_{\rm n}$ are obtained compared with those by $S_{\rm 2n}$. The systematic studies of the neutron pairing energies $-E_{\rm pair}$ shown that values of the odd-$A$ nuclei are much smaller in comparison with those of the neighboring even-even nuclei due to the absent contribution of pairing energy by the unpaired odd neutron. By investigating the single-particle structures, the rms radii and the density distributions, the possible halo structures in the neutron-rich Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes are predicted, in which the sharp increases of rms radii with significant deviations from the traditional $r\varpropto A^{1/3}$ rule and very diffuse spatial distributions in densities are observed. By analyzing the contributions of different partial waves to the total neutron density $ρ_{lj}(r)/ρ(r)$, the orbitals locating around the Fermi surface especially those with low angular momenta are found the main reason causing the extended nuclear density and large rms radii. Finally, the numbers of the neutrons $N_λ$~($N_0$) occupied above the Fermi surface $λ_n$~(in the continuum) are discussed, the behaviors of which are basically consistent with those of pairing energy, supporting the key role of the pairing correlations in the halo phenomena.

## Key claim

The continuum Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Green's function method reproduces experimental separation energies for neutron-rich Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes, but the predicted neutron drip lines are much shorter when determined by one-neutron separation energies ($S_{\rm n}$) compared to two-neutron separation energies ($S_{\rm 2n}$), and possible halo structures are predicted only in Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes, not in Zr isotopes.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in coordinate space using the Green's function method, which imposes correct asymptotic boundary conditions for bound states, weakly-bound states, and continuum states. The SLy4 parameter set was used for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) for the pairing channel, with the blocking effect included for odd-A nuclei. The calculations were performed with a box size of 20 fm, mesh size of 0.1 fm, maximal angular momentum j_max=25/2, and quasiparticle energy cutoff E_cut=60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy $S_{\rm 2n}$ drops from 13.25 MeV at $^{132}$Sn to 4.94 MeV at $^{134}$Sn across the N=82 magic number
- Neutron drip line nuclei predicted by $S_{\rm 2n}$: $^{67}$Ca, $^{89}$Ni, $^{123}$Zr, and $^{177}$Sn
- Neutron drip line nuclei predicted by $S_{\rm n}$: $^{60}$Ca, $^{86}$Ni, $^{122}$Zr, and $^{148}$Sn
- Pairing interaction strength $V_0 = -458.4$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$, with density $\rho_0 = 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$, $\eta = 0.71$, $\alpha = 0.59$
- Traditional liquid-drop model coefficients for rms radii: $r \approx 0.991A^{1/3}$ for Ca, $0.984A^{1/3}$ for Ni, $0.957A^{1/3}$ for Zr, and $0.961A^{1/3}$ for Sn, determined from stable nuclei below shell closures N=28, 50, 50, and 80 respectively

## Context

This work extends the continuum Skyrme-HFB theory with Green's function method previously developed by Zhang et al. (2011) and the blocking-effect extension by Sun et al. (2019) to a systematic survey of four isotopic chains. It contrasts with earlier predictions of giant halos in Zr isotopes using the RCHB theory with NLSH parameter set by Meng et al. (1998) and continuum Skyrme-HFB with SKI4 by Grasso et al. (2006) and Zhang et al. (2012), finding instead that Zr isotopes are unlikely to exhibit halos due to the N=82 shell closure near the continuum threshold. The results enable further studies of Borromean systems, where even-even nuclei beyond the $S_{\rm n}$ drip line remain bound while the odd-neutron systems are unbound.
