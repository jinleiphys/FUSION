# The three-dimensional Ising model: A paradigm of liquid-vapor coexistence in nuclear multifragmentation

> Clusters in the three-dimensional Ising model rigorously obey reducibility and thermal scaling up to the critical temperature. The barriers extracted from Arrhenius plots depend on the cluster size as $B \propto A^σ$ where $σ$ is a critical exponent relating the cluster size to the cluster surface. All the Arrhenius plots collapse into a single Fisher-like scaling function indicating liquid-vapor-like phase coexistence and the univariant equilibrium between percolating clusters and finite clusters. The compelling similarity with nuclear multifragmentation is discussed.

# The three-dimensional Ising model: A paradigm of liquid-vapor coexistence in nuclear multifragmentation

## Key claim

Clusters in the three-dimensional Ising model rigorously obey reducibility and thermal scaling up to the critical temperature, and the extracted barriers follow $B \propto A^\sigma$ with $\sigma$ close to 2/3; however, the value of $\sigma$ determined in this work ($0.7230 \pm 0.0008$) is larger than the usual value of $0.64$ from other studies of three-dimensional Ising systems, possibly due to finite-size effects of the $50^3$ lattice.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations using the Swendsen-Wang algorithm were performed on a simple cubic lattice of $50^3$ spins with periodic boundary conditions. Clusters were identified using the Coniglio-Klein prescription, and equilibrium cluster distributions were determined as a function of temperature. The barriers for each cluster size were extracted from linear fits to Arrhenius plots of the cluster abundances below the critical temperature, and the scaling behavior was tested against Fisher's droplet model.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature determined from power-law fit: $T_c = (4.515 \pm 0.010) J/k_B$, consistent with the infinite-system value of $4.513 J/k_B$
- Barrier fit: $B = (12.77 \pm 0.04) A^{(0.7230 \pm 0.0008)}$, where $\sigma = 0.7230 \pm 0.0008$
- Power-law fit at critical temperature: $n_A(T_c) = (30084 \pm 7) A^{(-2.39671 \pm 0.00017)}$, with $\tau = 2.39671 \pm 0.00017$ (compared to expected infinite-system value $\tau^\infty = 2.21$)
- $q_0$ from fit: $q_0^{fit} = 30084$, compared to predicted $q_0^{\infty} = 27566$
- Arrhenius plot linearity extends over more than four or five orders of magnitude for temperatures $0 < T < T_c$
- Scaling collapse extends over six orders of magnitude for the broadest range of cluster sizes

## Context

This work extends the study of clusterization in the three-dimensional Ising model, which has received relatively little attention compared to its continuous phase transition, and demonstrates that the model captures both reducibility and thermal scaling observed experimentally in nuclear multifragmentation. The results show that the Ising model clusters obey Fisher's droplet model scaling (Fisher 1967, Fisher 1969), indicating liquid-vapor phase coexistence with $\Delta \mu = 0$, and the compelling similarity with nuclear multifragmentation data (Elliott 2000) suggests that nuclear multifragmentation should be interpreted as clusterization of a nuclear vapor in equilibrium with its liquid. This approach contrasts with percolation models (Stauffer 1979, Bauer 1985), which do not lend themselves to non-trivial thermal studies.
