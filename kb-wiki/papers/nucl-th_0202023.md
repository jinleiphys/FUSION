# Study $ω$ and $ϕ$ photoproduction in the nucleon isotopic channels

> We present results for the photoproduction of $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson in the nucleon isotopic channles. A recently developed quark model with an effective Lagrangian is employed to account for the non-diffractive {\it s}- and {\it u}-channel processes; the diffractive feature arising from the {\it natural} parity exchange is accounted for by the {\it t}-channel pomeron exchange, while the {\it unnatural} parity exchange is accounted for by the {\it t}-channel pion exchange. In the $ω$ production, the isotopic effects could provide more information concerning the search of "missing resonances", while in the $ϕ$ production, the isotopic effects could highlight non-diffractive resonance excitation mechanisms at large angles.

## Key claim
The quark-model effective Lagrangian approach can describe both diffractive and non-diffractive mechanisms in ω and φ photoproduction off protons and neutrons, but the isotopic effects are primarily visible in the non-diffractive s- and u-channel contributions at large angles, while forward-angle observables are dominated by t-channel pomeron and pion exchange and show almost no isotopic dependence.

## Method
The authors employed a quark model with an effective Lagrangian for the vector-meson–quark coupling to compute s- and u-channel resonance excitations in the non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry. Diffractive natural-parity exchange was described by t-channel pomeron exchange, and unnatural-parity exchange by t-channel pion exchange; the model was applied to both proton and neutron targets for ω and φ photoproduction, with parameters fitted to existing data from SAPHIR, CLAS, and older measurements.

## Key numbers
- For ω photoproduction: coupling parameters a = −2.72 and b′ ≡ b − a = −3.42 were determined from an overall fit to SAPHIR data.
- For φ photoproduction: coupling parameters a = 0.241 ± 0.105 and b′ ≡ b − a = 0.458 ± 0.091 were determined from fits to data at Eγ = 2.0 GeV (Besch et al., 1974) and CLAS data at 3.6 GeV.
- The φ meson photoproduction threshold is Eγ ≈ 1.57 GeV.
- The pomeron–nucleon coupling uses β0 = 1.27 GeV⁻¹; the pomeron trajectory parameters are α′ = 0.25 GeV⁻² and μ0 = 1.2 GeV for the cut-off energy.
- Pion–nucleon coupling: g²πNN/4π = 14; ωπγ coupling: g²ωπγ = 3.315; φπγ coupling: g²φπγ = 0.143; the form factor uses απ = 300 MeV.
- Differential cross sections for ω photoproduction are shown at Eγ = 1.225, 1.450, 1.675, and 1.915 GeV.
- Parity asymmetry data for γp → ωp exist at Eγ = 2.8, 4.7, and 9.3 GeV (Ballam et al., 1973).

## Context
This work extends the quark-model approach previously applied to ρ meson photoproduction (Zhao, 1998; Zhao, 2001) and to φ photoproduction (Zhao, 1999; Zhao, 2001) by systematically comparing proton and neutron targets. The isotopic channels are motivated by the search for "missing resonances" predicted by the NRCQM (Koniuk and Isgur, 1980) that may couple strongly to vector meson channels, and by the possibility of detecting strangeness components in the nucleon (Henley et al., 1992; Titov et al., 1997). The results enable future experimental studies with neutron targets at facilities such as JLab (CLAS), ELSA (SAPHIR), MAMI, ESRF (GRAAL), and SPring-8 to disentangle reaction mechanisms and test model-independent features.
