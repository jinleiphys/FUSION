# Remarks on Delta Radiative and Dalitz Decays

> Phenomenological expressions are derived for rates of the $Δ(1232)$ radiative and Dalitz decays, $Δ(1232)$ $\to Nγ$ and $Δ(1232)$ $\to Ne^{+}e^{-}$. Earlier calculations of these decays are commented.

# Remarks on Delta Radiative and Dalitz Decays

## Key claim
The authors derive phenomenological expressions for the Δ(1232) → Nγ and Δ(1232) → Ne⁺e⁻ decay rates using helicity amplitudes, and they claim that earlier calculations by Xiong (1990), Wolf (1990), Butler (1993), Titov (1995), Ernst (1998), and Aliev (1999) are not equivalent to each other nor to the present result, with discrepancies likely attributable to errors in those prior works.

## Method
The authors use the helicity amplitude formalism of Jones and Scadron (1973) to compute the Δ → Nγ* decay width as a function of the virtual photon mass M, expressed in terms of magnetic (G_M), electric (G_E), and Coulomb (G_C) transition form factors. They then apply the factorization prescription to convert the virtual photon decay width into the dilepton decay rate Δ → Ne⁺e⁻ by multiplying with the virtual photon decay width into e⁺e⁻ and integrating over the dilepton invariant mass M.

## Key numbers
- The Δ → Nγ* decay width formula (Equation 2) contains the factor α/16 × (m_Δ + m_N)²/(m_Δ³ m_N²) × ((m_Δ + m_N)² − M²)^{1/2} × ((m_Δ − m_N)² − M²)^{3/2} × (G_M² + 3G_E² + (M²/(2m_Δ²))G_C²), where α is the fine-structure constant, m_Δ and m_N are the Δ and nucleon masses, and M is the virtual photon invariant mass.
- The virtual photon decay width into e⁺e⁻ is given by M Γ(γ* → e⁺e⁻) = (α/3)(M² + 2m_e²)√(1 − 4m_e²/M²), where m_e is the electron mass.
- In the heavy-baryon limit (m_Δ − m_N ≪ m_N), the form factors satisfy G_M = (2/3)m²F₁, G_E = 0, and G_C = (2/3)m²(F₁ + F₂), and the M1 mode is dominant.

## Context
This work extends the line of calculations for Δ(1232) electromagnetic decays, which are important for understanding dilepton yields in nucleon-nucleon and heavy-ion collisions. The authors explicitly compare their results with prior calculations by Xiong (1990), Wolf (1990), Butler (1993), Titov (1995), Ernst (1998), and Aliev (1999), finding that none of these earlier results are equivalent to each other or to the present derivation. The authors agree with the expression of Devenish (1976) for the radiative decay of a spin-J_R baryon resonance. This work enables a consistent treatment of Δ decays in transport models for heavy-ion collisions, resolving ambiguities in the kinematic factors that appeared in previous formulations.
