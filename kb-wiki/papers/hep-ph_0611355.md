# Energy gain of heavy quarks by fluctuations in the QGP

> The collisional energy gain of a heavy quark due to chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in a quark-gluon plasma is investigated. The field fluctuations lead to an energy gain of the quark for all temperatures and velocities. The net effect is a reduction of the collisional energy loss by 15-40% for parameters relevant at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in a quark-gluon plasma cause a heavy quark to gain energy for all temperatures and velocities, reducing the net collisional energy loss by 15-40% for parameters relevant at RHIC energies. This reduction is most significant at low momenta, with the fluctuation gain diverging linearly as the quark velocity approaches zero.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical approach within linear response theory, treating the QGP as an equilibrium, weakly-coupled plasma in the Abelian approximation. They start from the Fokker-Planck equation and derive the energy loss from the statistical average of the work done by the total chromo-electric field (induced plus fluctuating) on a heavy quark moving along a straight-line trajectory. The power spectrum of the field fluctuations is obtained from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem using the high-temperature gauge-invariant dielectric functions, and the resulting expression for the fluctuation-induced energy gain is evaluated numerically for charm and bottom quarks.

## Key numbers

* For a charm quark with momenta 4-20 GeV, the fluctuation effect reduces the collisional energy loss by 17-39% at T=250 MeV with alpha_s=0.3 and N_f=2.
* For a bottom quark with momenta 4-20 GeV, the fluctuation effect reduces the collisional energy loss by 12-31% at T=250 MeV with alpha_s=0.3 and N_f=2.
* The Debye mass squared is m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + N_f/6), with N_f=2 and T=250 MeV used in the numerical calculations.
* Charm and bottom quark masses are taken as 1.25 GeV and 4.2 GeV, respectively.
* The upper integration limit k_max is given by min{E, 2q(E+p)/sqrt(m^2+2q(E+p))} where q ~ T is the typical momentum of thermal partons.

## Context

This work extends earlier semiclassical calculations of collisional energy loss in the QGP by Thoma (1990) and Mrowczynski (1991), which neglected microscopic field fluctuations. The fluctuation-induced energy gain is analogous to effects previously studied in non-relativistic classical plasmas by Hubbard, Sitenko, and others. The reduction of collisional energy loss by fluctuations worsens the agreement with non-photonic single electron data from RHIC, but the authors note that energy gain at low momenta may help explain the large elliptic flow coefficient v_2 observed in semi-central Au-Au collisions, as suggested by Rapp.
