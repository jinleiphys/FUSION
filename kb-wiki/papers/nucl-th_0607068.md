# Scalar Lambda N and Lambda Lambda interaction in a chiral unitary approach

> We study the central part of Lambda N and Lambda Lambda potential by considering the correlated and uncorrelated two-meson exchange besides the omega exchange contribution. The correlated two-meson is evaluated in a chiral unitary approach. We find that a short range repulsion is generated by the correlated two-meson potential which also produces an attraction in the intermediate distance region. The uncorrelated two-meson exchange produces a sizeable attraction in all cases which is counterbalanced by omega exchange contribution.

## Key claim

The scalar isoscalar Lambda N and Lambda Lambda potentials are constructed from correlated and uncorrelated two-meson exchange plus omega exchange, where the correlated part is evaluated using a chiral unitary approach; the abstract's claim of "short range repulsion" and "intermediate distance attraction" from the correlated two-meson potential is qualified by the finding that this contribution is actually much smaller than the uncorrelated two-meson and omega exchange contributions, which dominate the total potential.

## Method

The correlated two-meson exchange (pion-pion and kaon-antikaon) was evaluated using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions, with the meson-meson amplitudes unitarized via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation in coupled channels using a cutoff regularization (q_max = 1.0 GeV). The triangle loop integrals coupling the two mesons to the baryons were regularized with a static form factor (cutoff Lambda = 1.0 GeV), and contributions from intermediate decuplet baryons (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*) were included. Uncorrelated two-meson exchange (direct and crossed box diagrams) and omega exchange (with g_omega NN = 13 and Lambda_omega = 1.4 GeV) were also computed, and the method was extended to the Lambda Lambda interaction by replacing nucleon triangle contributions with Lambda ones.

## Key numbers

* The correlated two-meson contribution to the Lambda N potential has a peak around q = 400 MeV, similar in position and magnitude to the NN case found in Oset and Toki (2000) and Palomar et al. (2005).
* The omega exchange coupling is taken as g_omega NN = 13, with g_omega Lambda Lambda = (2/3) g_omega NN from ideal mixing, and the form factor cutoff is Lambda_omega = 1.4 GeV.
* The cutoff for the meson-meson loop function is q_max = 1.0 GeV, which reproduces the pi pi phase shift up to 1.2 GeV.
* The cutoff for the triangle loop form factor is Lambda = 1.0 GeV.
* The pi N Delta coupling uses f*_pi N Delta = 2.12 f_pi NN to reproduce the Delta(1232) width.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the NN scalar isoscalar interaction by Oset and Toki (2000) and Palomar et al. (2005) to the strange sector, specifically the Lambda N and Lambda Lambda interactions. It contrasts with phenomenological one-boson-exchange models such as those of the Nijmegen group (Nagels et al., Rijken et al.) and the Juelich model (Holinde et al., Haidenbauer et al.), which treat correlated two-pion exchange via dispersion relations or phenomenological scalar mesons; the present approach instead uses explicit chiral unitary dynamics for the meson-meson interaction. The results enable future studies of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions based on a more fundamental treatment of the scalar-isoscalar channel, and can be compared with empirical YN potentials from scattering data.
