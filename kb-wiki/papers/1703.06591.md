# Solution of the eta-4He problem with quasi-particle formalism

> The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations for the five-body eta-4N problem are solved for the case of the driving eta N and NN potentials limited to s-waves. The quasi-particle (Schmidt) method is employed to convert the equations into the effective two-body form. Numerical results are presented for the eta-4He scattering length.

## Key claim

The $\eta^4$He scattering length is predicted to be smaller than the $\eta^3$He scattering length, contrary to the expectation that adding a nucleon would increase attraction; this is attributed to the stronger binding of $^4$He pushing the effective in-medium $\eta N$ interaction further into the subthreshold region where the $\eta N$ amplitude decreases, and the resulting attraction is too weak to support an $\eta^4$He bound state with the $\eta N$ parameters used.

## Method

The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations for the five-body $\eta-4N$ system were solved using the quasi-particle (Schmidt) method, with driving $\eta N$ and $NN$ potentials limited to $s$-waves. The $NN$ interaction was approximated by rank-one separable potentials (Yamaguchi or Gaussian form factors), and the $\eta N$ interaction was reduced to excitation of the $N(1535)1/2^-$ resonance via a coupled-channel formalism including pions. The energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method was used to generate separable expansions of the subamplitudes, reducing the five-body equations to coupled quasi-two-body equations of Lippmann-Schwinger form.

## Key numbers

- $\eta^4$He scattering length with Gauss $NN$ potential and Set I $\eta N-\pi N$ parameters: $a_{\eta^4\mathrm{He}} = 4.8 + 1.2\,i$ fm
- $\eta^4$He scattering length with Gauss $NN$ potential and Set II $\eta N-\pi N$ parameters: $a_{\eta^4\mathrm{He}} = 1.0 + 0.3\,i$ fm
- $\eta^4$He scattering length with Yamaguchi $NN$ potential and Set I $\eta N-\pi N$ parameters: $a_{\eta^4\mathrm{He}} = 2.2 + 0.3\,i$ fm
- $\eta^4$He scattering length with Yamaguchi $NN$ potential and Set II $\eta N-\pi N$ parameters: $a_{\eta^4\mathrm{He}} = 0.5 + 0.1\,i$ fm
- For comparison, $\eta^3$He scattering length with Gauss $NN$ and Set I: $a_{\eta^3\mathrm{He}} = 6.7 + 4.0\,i$ fm
- For comparison, $\eta^3$He scattering length with Gauss $NN$ and Set II: $a_{\eta^3\mathrm{He}} = 1.3 + 0.7\,i$ fm
- Convergence of the separable expansion was achieved with $N_1=N_2=6$, $N_3=N_4=8$ terms, giving $a_{\eta^4\mathrm{He}} = 4.79 + 1.22\,i$ fm (Gauss, Set I)
- Singlet $np$ scattering length: $a_0 = 23.690$ fm; triplet $np$ scattering length: $a_1 = -5.378$ fm; cut-off momentum $\beta = 1.4488$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work presents the first rigorous few-body calculation of the $\eta-4N$ system, extending the quasi-particle formalism previously applied to $\eta NN$ (Shev, Pena, FiAr2N) and $\eta-3N$ (FiAr3N) scattering. The results contradict the expectation from experimental data (Willis) that an $\eta^4$He bound state is highly probable; instead, the predicted scattering lengths are smaller than those for $\eta^3$He, consistent with the less steep rise of the $dd\to\eta^4$He cross section near threshold observed by Adlarson et al. The calculation enables future work incorporating nucleon spin and more sophisticated $NN$ potentials, and suggests that the absence of $\eta^4$He bound state signals in reactions such as $dd\to^3$He $n\pi^0$ (Krzemien, Adlarson) may be explained by subthreshold suppression of the $\eta N$ interaction.
