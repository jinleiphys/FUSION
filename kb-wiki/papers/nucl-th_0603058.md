# Scalar-Meson - Baryon Coupling Constants in QCD Sum Rules

> The external-field QCD Sum Rules method is used to evaluate the coupling constants of the light-isoscalar scalar meson (``σ'' or ε) to the Λ, Σ, and Ξbaryons. It is shown that these coupling constants as calculated from QCD Sum Rules are consistent with SU(3)-flavor relations, which leads to a determination of the F/(F+D) ratio of the scalar octet assuming ideal mixing: we find α_s \equiv F/(F+D)=0.55. The coupling constants with SU(3) breaking effects are also discussed.

## Key claim

The coupling constants of the light-isoscalar scalar meson to the Λ, Σ, and Ξ baryons, as calculated from QCD Sum Rules, are consistent with SU(3)-flavor relations, yielding an F/(F+D) ratio of α_s = 0.55 for the scalar octet under the assumption of ideal mixing; the paper also discusses SU(3) breaking effects but does not provide final numerical values for those broken-symmetry couplings.

## Method

The external-field QCD Sum Rules method was used, starting with a correlation function of baryon interpolating fields in a constant external isoscalar-scalar σ field. The operator product expansion (OPE) was computed in the Euclidean region using quark propagators that include the external-field response, and the resulting sum rules at the Dirac structure \(\hat{q}\) were matched to a hadronic side saturated by the lowest-lying baryon states, including single-pole and continuum-threshold-response contributions. Sum rules were derived for the ΛΛσ, ΞΞσ, and ΣΣσ coupling constants, and the analysis was first performed in the SU(3) flavor symmetric limit.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{NN\sigma}/g_q^\sigma = 3.9 \pm 1.0\) (nucleon, SU(3) symmetric limit, from QCDSR)
- \(g_{\Lambda\Lambda\sigma}/g_q^\sigma = 1.9 \pm 0.5\) (Λ baryon, SU(3) symmetric limit, from QCDSR)
- \(g_{\Xi\Xi\sigma}/g_q^\sigma = 0.4 \pm 0.1\) (Ξ baryon, SU(3) symmetric limit, from QCDSR)
- \(g_{\Sigma\Sigma\sigma}/g_q^\sigma = 3.8 \pm 1.0\) (Σ baryon, SU(3) symmetric limit, from QCDSR)
- Adopting \(g_q^\sigma = 3.7\) from the sigma model gives \(g_{NN\sigma} = 14.4 \pm 3.7\), \(g_{\Lambda\Lambda\sigma} = 7.0 \pm 1.9\), \(g_{\Xi\Xi\sigma} = 1.5 \pm 0.4\), \(g_{\Sigma\Sigma\sigma} = 14.1 \pm 3.7\) (all at t=0)
- \(F/(F+D)\) ratio \(\alpha_s = 0.55\) (determined from the SU(3) fit assuming ideal mixing for the q\(\bar{q}\) picture)
- Octet coupling \(g/g_q^\sigma = g_{NNa_0}/g_q^\sigma = 3.3\) and singlet coupling \(g_1/g_q^\sigma = 3.2\) (from the SU(3) fit)

## Context

This work extends the external-field QCD Sum Rules method, previously applied to the NNσ coupling constant by Erkol et al. (2005), to the hyperon sectors (Λ, Σ, Ξ). It provides a model-independent determination of scalar-meson–baryon couplings that are crucial for one-boson-exchange (OBE) models of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, such as the Nijmegen soft-core (NSC) potentials of Maessen et al. (1989) and Rijken et al. (1998). The results enable a more reliable estimate of the ΛΛ interaction strength, which is key to understanding the binding energy of double-Λ hypernuclei, and they test the SU(3) flavor structure of the scalar nonet, distinguishing between the q\(\bar{q}\) and q²\(\bar{q}\)² pictures for the scalar mesons.
