# Nucleon momentum distribution extracted from the experimental scaling function

> The connection between the scaling function, directly extracted from the analysis of electron scattering data, and the nuclear spectral function or nuclear momentum density is investigated at depth. The dependence of the scaling function on the two independent variables in the scattering process, the transfer momentum ($q$) and the scaling variable ($y$), is taken into account, and the analysis is extended to both, positive and negative $y$-values, i.e., below and above the center of the quasielastic peak, respectively. Analytical expressions for the derivatives of the scaling function, evaluated at the finite limits of integration dealing with the kinematically allowed region, are connected with the spectral function. Here, contributions corresponding to zero and finite excitation energies are included. The scaling function is described by the Gumbel density distribution, whereas short-range correlations are incorporated in the spectral function by using some simple models. Also different parametrizations for the nucleon momentum distribution, that are compatible with the general properties of the scaling function, have been considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nucleon momentum distribution can be extracted from the experimental scaling function by solving the integro-differential equations that connect the spectral function to the scaling function, provided that the scaling function's dependence on both momentum transfer \(q\) and the scaling variable \(y\) is fully accounted for and that the analysis is extended to both negative and positive \(y\)-values (below and above the quasielastic peak).

## Method

The authors start from the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) expression for the inclusive quasielastic \((e,e')\) cross section, which factorizes into a single-nucleon cross section and the scaling function \(F(q,\omega)\). The scaling function is expressed as an integral of the nuclear spectral function \(S(p,\mathcal{E})\) over the kinematically allowed region in missing momentum \(p\) and excitation energy \(\mathcal{E}\). The spectral function is split into a term for zero excitation energy \(n_0(p)\delta(\mathcal{E})\) and a term for finite excitation energy \(S_1(p,\mathcal{E})\). Using Leibniz's rule, the derivatives of the scaling function with respect to \(y\) and \(q\) are evaluated analytically, and under the assumption of first-kind scaling (independence of \(F\) on \(q\) at large \(q\)), explicit expressions for the momentum distribution \(n_0(k)\) are derived for both negative and positive \(y\) regions. The scaling function is modeled using the Gumbel density distribution, and short-range correlations are incorporated into the spectral function via simple models.

## Key numbers

* The analysis assumes first-kind scaling is valid for momentum transfers \(q > 500\) MeV (stated as "\(q>500\)~MeV" in the text).
* For the example of \(^{12}\)C at \(q = 3\) GeV and \(y = -1.25\) GeV, the allowed momentum range is \(1.25\) GeV \(\leq p \leq 3.94\) GeV; at \(y = 1.25\) GeV, the range is \(0\) GeV \(\leq p \leq 5.76\) GeV.
* The ratio \(Y(q,y) = |y|\) occurs at \(y_0 \sim -2.7482\) GeV for \(^{12}\)C at \(q = 3\) GeV; for \(y < y_0\) the region is not allowed because \(Y(q,y) < |y|\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (PhysRevC.53.1689, PhysRevC.41.R2474, and related papers), who extracted the nucleon momentum distribution from \(y\)-scaling analysis but restricted to negative \(y\) values and required an asymptotic scaling function and a binding correction term \(dB/dy\). The present paper provides a more complete formalism that includes both negative and positive \(y\) regions, uses exact expressions for the derivatives of the scaling function, and explicitly accounts for short-range correlations via the finite-excitation-energy part of the spectral function \(S_1(p,\mathcal{E})\). The derived connection between the derivative of the superscaling function and \(S_1(p,\mathcal{E})\) enables a direct extraction of the momentum distribution from experimental scaling data without relying on the asymptotic limit or the binding correction approximation used in prior work.
