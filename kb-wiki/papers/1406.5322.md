# Transport coefficients of heavy quarks around $T_c$ at finite quark chemical potential

> The interactions of heavy quarks with the partonic environment at finite temperature $T$ and finite quark chemical potential $μ_q$ are investigated in terms of transport coefficients within the Dynamical Quasi-Particle model (DQPM) designed to reproduce the lattice-QCD results (including the partonic equation of state) in thermodynamic equilibrium. These results are confronted with those of nuclear many-body calculations close to the critical temperature $T_c$. The hadronic and partonic spatial diffusion coefficients join smoothly and show a pronounced minimum around $T_c$, at $μ_q=0$ as well as at finite $μ_q$. Close and above $T_c$ its absolute value matches the lQCD calculations for $μ_q=0$. The smooth transition of the heavy quark transport coefficients from the hadronic to the partonic medium corresponds to a cross over in line with lattice calculations, and differs substantially from perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations which show a large discontinuity at $T_c$. This indicates that in the vicinity of $T_c$ dynamically dressed massive partons and not massless pQCD partons are the effective degrees-of-freedom in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (DQPM) predicts that the spatial diffusion coefficient of heavy quarks joins smoothly between hadronic and partonic matter around the critical temperature \(T_c\), both at zero and finite quark chemical potential \(\mu_q\), in contrast to perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations which show a large discontinuity at \(T_c\). This smooth behavior is interpreted as evidence that dynamically dressed massive partons, rather than massless pQCD partons, are the effective degrees of freedom near \(T_c\).

## Method

The authors compute heavy-quark transport coefficients (spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) and energy loss \(dE/dx\)) using the DQPM, which models QGP degrees of freedom as massive quasi-particles with broad spectral functions fitted to lattice QCD results for the equation of state at \(\mu_q=0\). The model is extended to finite \(\mu_q\) by introducing an effective temperature \(T^{*2} = T^2 + \mu_q^2/\pi^2\) and a \(\mu_q\)-dependent critical temperature \(T_c(\mu_q)\). Transport coefficients are calculated from \(2 \to 2\) scattering matrix elements using on-shell partons (with finite-width effects estimated at 10-20% from prior work), and the spatial diffusion coefficient is obtained from the zero-momentum limit of the drag coefficient via \(D_s = \lim_{p_Q\to 0} T/(M_Q \eta_D)\).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero chemical potential is \(T_c(\mu_q=0) = 158\) MeV.
- The \(\mu_q\)-dependent critical temperature is parameterized as \(T_c(\mu_q) = T_c(\mu_q=0) \sqrt{1 - d^2 \mu_q^2}\) with \(d^2 = 8.79\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The deviation from the Einstein relation for the DpQCD model at small momenta is of order 10-15%.
- For finite \(\mu_q\), pQCD calculations of the spatial diffusion coefficient near \(T_c\) are a factor of 3 higher than DpQCD results.
- The energy loss of a charm quark with incoming momentum 10 GeV/c is shown as a function of temperature and \(\mu_q\) (specific numerical values for \(dE/dx\) are not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the DQPM approach, previously used to compute shear viscosity and electric conductivity (Marty 2013, Cassing 2013), to heavy-quark transport at finite chemical potential. It confronts DQPM results with nuclear many-body calculations below \(T_c\) (Torres-Rincon 2013) and with pQCD calculations (Moore and Teaney 2004) and lattice QCD results (Banerjee 2011) above \(T_c\). The smooth joining of hadronic and partonic diffusion coefficients supports a crossover transition and enables more realistic modeling of heavy-quark energy loss in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as at future FAIR energies where finite baryon density is significant.
