# Anisotropic shear viscosity of a strongly coupled non-Abelian plasma from magnetic branes

> Recent estimates for the electromagnetic fields produced in the early stages of non-central ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions indicate the presence of magnetic fields $B\sim \mathcal{O}(0.1-15\,m_π^2)$, where $m_π$ is the pion mass. It is then of special interest to study the effects of strong (Abelian) magnetic fields on the transport coefficients of strongly coupled non-Abelian plasmas, such as the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy ion collisions. In this work we study the anisotropy in the shear viscosity induced by an external magnetic field in a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ SYM plasma. Due to the spatial anisotropy created by the magnetic field, the most general viscosity tensor of a magnetized plasma has 5 shear viscosity coefficients and 2 bulk viscosities. We use the holographic correspondence to evaluate two of the shear viscosities, $η_{\perp} \equiv η_{xyxy}$ (perpendicular to the magnetic field) and $η_{\parallel} \equiv η_{xzxz}=η_{yzyz}$ (parallel to the field). When $B\neq 0$ the shear viscosity perpendicular to the field saturates the viscosity bound $η_{\perp}/s = 1/(4π)$ while in the direction parallel to the field the bound is violated since $η_{\parallel}/s < 1/(4π)$. However, the violation of the bound in the case of strongly coupled SYM is minimal even for the largest value of $B$ that can be reached in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity parallel to an external magnetic field in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma violates the universal viscosity bound \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), but the violation is minimal for the magnetic field strengths achievable in heavy ion collisions (\(B \sim \mathcal{O}(0.1-15\,m_\pi^2)\)), while the shear viscosity perpendicular to the field always saturates the bound.

## Method

The authors used the gauge/gravity duality (holographic correspondence) to model a strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma subjected to a constant, homogeneous external magnetic field. They solved the Einstein-Maxwell equations numerically to obtain the magnetic brane background geometry, which interpolates between an asymptotically AdS\(_5\) spacetime in the UV and a BTZ\(\times T^2\) geometry in the IR. Using the membrane paradigm, they computed the shear viscosity coefficients \(\eta_{\perp} \equiv \eta_{xyxy}\) (perpendicular to the field) and \(\eta_{\parallel} \equiv \eta_{xzxz} = \eta_{yzyz}\) (parallel to the field) from metric fluctuations about this background.

## Key numbers

* \(\eta_{\perp}/s = 1/(4\pi)\) exactly, independent of the magnetic field strength.
* \(\eta_{\parallel}/s < 1/(4\pi)\) for any \(B > 0\); the ratio \((\eta/s)_{\parallel}/(\eta/s)_{\perp} = w/v\), where \(w\) and \(v\) are numerically determined rescaling parameters.
* In the strong-field limit \(\mathcal{B} \gg T^2\), \(\eta_{\parallel}/s \sim \pi T^2/\mathcal{B}\).
* For typical early-stage heavy ion collision temperatures \(T \sim 2 m_\pi\) and \(\mathcal{B} \sim 40 m_\pi^2\), \(4\pi\eta_{\parallel}/s \sim 0.9\).
* The rescaled magnetic field parameter \(b\) has an upper bound \(b_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{3}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic shear viscosity in strongly coupled plasmas, previously investigated for anisotropic deformations due to a spatial-dependent axion profile by Mateos (2011) and computed by Rebhan (2011), to the case of an external magnetic field, which is more directly relevant to heavy ion phenomenology. The results are qualitatively similar to those found in the axion-driven anisotropy case. The findings enable future studies of magnetic field effects on QGP hydrodynamics, including possible instabilities and the interplay between confinement scales and magnetic fields in non-conformal holographic models.
