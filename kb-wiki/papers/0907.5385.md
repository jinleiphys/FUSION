# The phi meson at high temperatures and densities

> The spectral density of the phi meson in a hot bath of nucleons and pions is calculated by relating the vector meson self-energy to the forward scattering amplitude, which is constrained by experimental data. Dispersion techniques are used to verify the relationship between real and the imaginary parts of the in-medium self energy. The position of the spectral peak of the phi meson is found to be shifted from its vacuum position by only a small amount, but its width is considerably increased.

## Key claim

The spectral peak of the phi meson in a hot bath of nucleons and pions is shifted from its vacuum position by only a small amount (within approximately -2.5 MeV to 15 MeV at zero momentum), but its width is considerably increased, with the change in width being more density-driven than temperature-driven.

## Method

The in-medium self-energy of the phi meson was calculated by relating it to the forward scattering amplitude (FSA) for phi interactions with pions and nucleons, constrained by experimental data. The FSA was constructed using a two-component approach: a low-energy part from s-channel resonances (including both above-threshold and subthreshold resonances, with widths for phi-nucleon channels estimated via the Lipkin scaling ratio R_phi/omega and vector meson dominance) and a high-energy part from a Regge parametrization with Pomeron and P' terms. Dispersion techniques were used to verify the consistency between the real and imaginary parts of the FSA, and the matched FSA was then used to compute the in-medium self-energy, spectral density, width, and mass shift of the phi meson at finite temperature and nucleon density.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant for the phi to rho-pi interaction was determined to be g^2 = 1.766 × 10^{-4} MeV from the experimentally measured branching ratio, with a mixing angle theta_V = 40.1 degrees.
- The Lipkin scaling ratio R_phi/omega = 13.4 × 10^{-3} (averaged from Sibirtsev et al.) was used to relate phi-nucleon resonance widths to omega-nucleon widths.
- At T = 150 MeV and zero nucleon density, the in-medium width generated by collisions with pions is about 40 MeV.
- At T = 150 MeV and nucleon density n_N = n_0/2, the phi meson width is 68 MeV; at n_N = n_0, the width is 98 MeV; at n_N = 2n_0, the width is 159 MeV.
- At zero nucleon density and T = 170 MeV, the full width at half maximum is about 58 MeV; at T = 200 MeV, the width is around 100 MeV.
- The mass shift at p = 0 GeV ranges from approximately -2.5 MeV to 15 MeV across the densities and temperatures studied; at p = 1.5 GeV, n_N = 2n_0, and T = 150 MeV, the mass shift reaches 38 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium vector meson modifications, which had concentrated on the lower-mass rho and omega mesons (e.g., Eletsky et al., Martell and Ellis), to the phi meson using a similar FSA-based approach constrained by experimental data. The results enable future comparisons with experimental measurements of phi meson properties in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, such as those from the KEK-PS E325 collaboration which claimed evidence of in-medium modification, and provide input for dilepton production rate calculations via vector meson dominance. The findings agree with the width calculations of Holt and Haglin at T = 170 MeV but disagree at T = 200 MeV, and are consistent with the density-driven broadening conclusions of Rapp and van Hees.
