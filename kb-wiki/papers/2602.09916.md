# Liquid-gas phase transition of nuclear matter

> A survey is presented summarizing the empirical evidence for and interpretations of a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter. Earlier developments and the present state of knowledge about the extraction of the critical point for such a transition, primarily from the systematics of multifragmention data, are outlined. By analogy with a Van der Waals equation of state, the empirically deduced critical temperature and pressure permit to draw a schematic picture of the underlying nuclear potential. More detailed approaches to the liquid-gas transition using self-consistent nuclear Hartree-Fock and variational calculations are described. Critical exponents are reported. Then chiral effective field theory, as the low-energy realization of QCD, is discussed in the context of nuclear thermodynamics. Its implications for the liquid-gas transition in symmetric nuclear matter as well as in neutron-rich matter are reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper surveys empirical evidence and theoretical interpretations for a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, concluding that the empirically deduced critical parameters are consistent with a Van der Waals type equation of state, but only within their ranges of uncertainty and after correcting for finite-size and Coulomb effects in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors review multifragmentation data from intermediate-energy nuclear collisions, where excited nuclei decay into nucleons and clusters, and analyze caloric curves (temperature vs. excitation energy) to identify a liquid-gas coexistence region. They extract the critical point of symmetric nuclear matter by combining data sets from compound nuclear reactions and multifragmentation processes, applying a liquid-drop ansatz to separate bulk volume and surface parts and correcting for angular momentum. The empirical critical parameters are then compared with a Van der Waals equation of state, and the second virial coefficient at the critical temperature is used to constrain a schematic two-body potential combining two-pion exchange attraction with short-range repulsion. The paper also reviews thermal Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme effective interactions and variational many-body calculations as theoretical approaches to the liquid-gas transition.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of symmetric nuclear matter: \(T_c = 17.9 \pm 0.4\) MeV (Elliot et al., 2013)
- Critical pressure: \(P_c = 0.31 \pm 0.07\) MeV/fm\(^3\) (Elliot et al., 2013)
- Critical density: \(n_c = 0.06 \pm 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Elliot et al., 2013); updated uncertainty \(n_c = 0.06 \pm 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Moretto et al., 2017)
- Van der Waals excluded volume parameter from empirical critical data: \(b = 7.2 \pm 1.5\) fm\(^3\), implying \(n_c \simeq 0.05 \pm 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Second virial coefficient at the critical temperature: \(B(T_c) = -17.1 \pm 3.6\) fm\(^3\)
- Saturation density of nuclear matter at \(T=0\): \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Two-pion exchange coupling strength: \(W_S \simeq 26.6\) MeV, derived from \(g_A = 1.276\), \(f_\pi = 92.2\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.6\) MeV, and \(\Delta = 291\) MeV
- Regularization scale for the schematic potential: \(d = 1.31\) fm to reproduce the median \(B(T_c)\)
- Range of critical temperatures from Skyrme Hartree-Fock models: \(T_c \simeq 14 - 20\) MeV

## Context

This survey extends earlier work on nuclear multifragmentation and the liquid-gas phase transition, building on foundational studies by Boal (1986), Csernai et al. (1986), and Durand et al. (2001). It synthesizes empirical critical point extraction methods from Elliot et al. (2013) and connects them to theoretical frameworks including thermal Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces (Sauer et al., 1976; Jaqaman et al., 1983) and chiral effective field theory (Wellenhofer et al., 2014, 2015; Holt et al., 2016; Drischler et al., 2021). The schematic potential derived from the second virial coefficient shows features similar to the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) central NN potentials from lattice QCD (Aoki et al., 2023). The work enables future studies of the nuclear matter phase diagram, particularly for neutron-rich matter relevant to supernova dynamics and neutron star structure.
