# The Effects of Multi-$Λ$ Hyperons on Collective Modes in Nuclei

> The dynamical influence of $Λ$ hyperons on the excited-state properties of closed-shell multi-$Λ$ Ca, Ni, Sn and Pb hypernuclei is investigated using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock + Random Phase Approximation in coordinate space. The strength distributions for the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole modes are calculated, revealing a systematic upward energy shift with increasing $Λ$ hyperon number $-S$. The scaling behavior of the computed centroid energies $\sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}$ with respect to both the mass and hyperon number is determined. The nuclear incompressibility modulus $K_A$ is found to increase monotonically with $-S$. The largest value is found in the $^{258}_{50Λ}$Pb hypernucleus, reaching $K_A = 322 $ MeV. Calculations in uniform hypernuclear matter confirm that this stiffening is a bulk effect driven by both the $NΛ$ and $ΛΛ$ interactions. Analysis of the transition densities for states with maximal collective coherence indicates that the dynamical effect of $Λ$ hyperons is predominantly in phase with the protons, especially in the case of the isovector E1 modes.

## Key claim

The addition of multiple Λ hyperons systematically stiffens the nuclear equation of state, causing a monotonic increase in the nuclear incompressibility modulus K_A and an upward shift of all collective mode energies; however, the effect is linear with strangeness number and becomes significant only when the number of hyperons exceeds about ten, with the stiffening factor γ of about 1% per unit of strangeness.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF-RPA) calculations in coordinate space for closed-shell multi-Λ hypernuclei of Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. They used the Skyrme-type SGII interaction for the nucleon-nucleon channel, the NSC97f interaction for the ΛN channel, and the EmpC force for the ΛΛ channel, solving the RPA eigenvalue problem with a 60 MeV energy cutoff and a 1 MeV Lorentzian smearing width.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear incompressibility modulus K_A reaches 322 MeV in the 258_50ΛPb hypernucleus, the largest value found in the study.
- For ordinary nuclei (S=0), K_A values cluster between 121-126 MeV: 121.49 MeV for 48Ca, 124 MeV for 56Ni, 126 MeV for 132Sn, and 122 MeV for 208Pb.
- At strangeness -S=8, K_A rises to approximately 154-158 MeV for lighter nuclei (Ca, Ni) and 150-153 MeV for heavier nuclei (Sn, Pb).
- At -S=20, K_A values approach 185-195 MeV across all isotopes.
- The isoscalar monopole centroid energies follow the scaling relation √(m_1/m_{-1}) = 75.37 A^{-1/3} (1-0.01 S).
- The isoscalar quadrupole centroid energies follow √(m_1/m_{-1}) = 55.12 A^{-1/3} (1-0.01 S).
- The isovector dipole centroid energies follow √(m_1/m_{-1}) = (36.35 A^{-1/3} + 17.19 A^{-1/6}) (1-0.008 S).
- All three scaling functions reproduce centroid energies with a relative error of less than 2% across the mass table.
- In 208Pb, the dominant dipole peak shifts from 14 MeV at -S=0 to roughly 16 MeV at -S=50.
- In 48Ca, the isoscalar quadrupole high-energy peak shifts from approximately 17.5 MeV at S=0 to nearly 19.5 MeV at -S=20.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that considered only one or two Λ hyperons (Lv 2018; Minato 2013; Minato 2012) to systematically investigate multi-Λ hypernuclei, marking the first mode-resolved analysis of collective excitations with large strangeness content. Unlike prior work using Skyrme interactions in the ΛN channel, this study employs the NSC97f interaction derived from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, which reproduces experimental binding energies of single-Λ hypernuclei across a wide mass range (Cugnon 2000; Vidana 2001). The results enable future experimental searches for multi-Λ hypernuclei, which are becoming accessible through machine-learning-based emulsion analysis (Nakazawa 2020; He 2025) and collider experiments (ALICE 2025), and provide constraints for the hyperon puzzle in neutron star equations of state.
