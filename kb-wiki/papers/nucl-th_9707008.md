# Coherent Photons and Pomerons in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Ultrarelativistic heavy ion beams carry large electromagnetic and strong absorptive fields, allowing exploration of a variety of physics. Two-photon, photon-Pomeron, and double Pomeron interactions can probe a huge variety of couplings and final states. RHIC will be the first heavy ion accelerator energetic enough to produce hadronic final states via coherent couplings. Virtual photons from the nuclear EM fields can interact in two-photon interactions, which can be exploited to study many particle spectroscopy and QCD topics. Because the photon flux scales as $Z^2$, Two-photon luminosities are large up to an energy of about γ\hbar c/R~ 3 GeV/c. Photon-Pomeron interactions are sensitive to how different vector mesons, including the $J/ψ$, interact with nuclear matter. $PP$ collisions rates are sensitive to the range of the Pomeron. Signals can be separated from backgrounds by using cuts on final state isolation (rapidity gaps) and $p_\perp$. We present Monte Carlo studies of different backgrounds, showing that representative signals can be extracted with good rates and signal to noise ratios.

## Key claim

At RHIC, coherent two-photon, photon-Pomeron, and double Pomeron interactions will produce hadronic final states with good rates and signal-to-noise ratios, provided that experimental cuts on final state isolation (rapidity gaps) and transverse momentum are applied to separate signals from backgrounds; the usable two-photon luminosity is reduced by about 50% due to the requirement of avoiding hadronic collisions.

## Method

The authors calculated two-photon luminosities by convoluting the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon fields of two nuclei and integrating over impact parameters greater than twice the nuclear radius. They performed Monte Carlo simulations of coherent signals and backgrounds (grazing nuclear collisions and beam-gas events, simulated with FRITIOF and Venus) for gold-on-gold collisions at RHIC, applying cuts on charged and neutral multiplicity, event topology, transverse momentum (p_perp < 100 MeV), and invariant mass. Three representative analyses were studied: f2(1270) → π+π-, threshold ρ0ρ0 → π+π-π+π-, and ηc → K*0K-π+.

## Key numbers

- Maximum photon energy for gold beams at RHIC: γℏc/R ≈ 3 GeV/c; maximum two-photon energy is about 6 GeV.
- For gold, the maximum Q² of the virtual photons is about (30 MeV/c)², limiting photon perpendicular momentum to p_perp < ℏc/R.
- f2(1270) → π+π-: 85% efficiency, 920,000 detected events/year, with FRITIOF background of 53,000 and Venus background of 100,000.
- ρ0ρ0 → π+π-π+π- (invariant mass 1.5–1.6 GeV/c²): 38% efficiency, 16,000 detected events/year, with FRITIOF background of 3,500 and Venus background of 1,400.
- ηc → K*0K-π+ (with particle identification): 57% efficiency, 70 detected events/year, with FRITIOF background of 8 and Venus background of 20.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of two-photon luminosities at heavy ion colliders by Cahn and Jackson (1990), Baur and Ferreira Filho (1990), and Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1995) to include detailed Monte Carlo studies of backgrounds and experimental feasibility at RHIC. It also builds on photon-Pomeron studies at HERA and earlier fixed-target vector meson production measurements by NMC (Ashman et al., 1988) and Fermilab E-665 (Baskin, 1997). The results enable experimental searches for glueball candidates via non-observation in two-photon collisions, studies of vector meson interactions with nuclear matter (including the J/ψ), and measurements of the Pomeron range via double Pomeron cross sections.
