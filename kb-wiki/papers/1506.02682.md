# Two photon exchange amplitude with πN intermediate states: spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 channels

> We calculate two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes for the elastic electron-proton scattering, and take into account intermediate hadronic states containing πN system with total angular momentum 1/2 or 3/2, which includes 8 different channels. This is the improvement of our previous calculation, where only the πN states with quantum numbers of Δ(1232) resonance were included. The results show good consistency with recent experimental data. At high Q^2, newly-calculated contributions affect the correction to the measured proton form factor ratio μG_E/G_M. The total correction becomes somewhat smaller compared to our previous work, but is still significant and grows approximately linearly with Q^2. Comparing contributions of different channels, we found that larger contributions come from the channels with quantum numbers of lightest resonances.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic electron-proton scattering, computed with eight πN intermediate-state channels (spin-1/2 and spin-3/2), improves agreement with experimental data compared to earlier calculations that included only the P33 (Δ-resonance) channel; at high Q² the total correction to the proton form factor ratio μG_E/G_M becomes somewhat smaller than in the previous work but remains significant and grows approximately linearly with Q².

## Method

The authors extended their previous TPE calculation by including all πN intermediate states with spin-parities J^P = 1/2^+, 1/2^-, 3/2^+, 3/2^- and isospin I = 1/2, 3/2, covering eight channels (S11, S31, P11, P31, P13, P33, D13, D33). They treated each channel as a continuum of resonances with varying mass W, using transition form factors derived from helicity electroproduction amplitudes A_H taken from the MAID model, and integrated over W to obtain the full TPE contribution. The calculation was performed with an updated version of the TPEcalc program, incorporating finite resonance widths, realistic resonance shapes and form factors, and non-resonant background.

## Key numbers

- For the VEPP-3 experiment, the best χ²/d.o.f. achieved with the present work (eight πN channels) is 1.06, compared to 1.68 for the full P33 channel only, 1.86 for narrow Δ, and 2.19 for elastic intermediate state only (first approach, normalization at highest ε).
- For the VEPP-3 experiment with the renormalization approach, χ²/d.o.f. = 2.44 for the present work, versus 3.18 for full P33, 3.37 for narrow Δ, and 3.87 for elastic only.
- For the CLAS experiment, χ²/d.o.f. = 1.21 for the present work, compared to 1.22 for full P33, 1.29 for narrow Δ, and 1.24 for elastic only.
- The GEp2γ experiment at Q² = 2.5 GeV² shows no evidence for ε variation of the measured form factor ratio R; the authors' calculations do not conflict with these data.
- At high Q² and fixed ε = 0.5, the total TPE correction δR to the measured proton form factor ratio grows approximately linearly with Q².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Borisyuk and Kobushkin, 2013) that included only the P33 (Δ-resonance) πN channel, and improves upon the approach of BMTres (2007) which assumed infinitely narrow resonances and considered only the unpolarized cross-section. The results show better agreement with experimental data from VEPP-3 (2013) and CLAS (2014) than earlier models, and the authors note that contributions from different channels tend to cancel each other, in qualitative agreement with BMTres (2007) and their own earlier work (Borisyuk and Kobushkin, 2013). The work enables future inclusion of higher-spin intermediate states (J ≥ 5/2), which the authors state is currently underway.
