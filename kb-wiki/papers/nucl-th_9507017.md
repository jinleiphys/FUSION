# phi-meson in Nuclear Matter

> $ϕ$-meson mass in nuclear matter ($m_ϕ^*$) is investigated using an effective Lagrangian of $ϕ$ interacting with octet baryons. $m_ϕ^*$ decreases by a few \% in nuclear matter due to the current conservation and effective nucleon/hyperon masses. Its implication to the p-A and A-A collisions are briefly discussed.

# φ-meson in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The φ-meson mass in nuclear matter decreases by 2–3% at densities between normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀) and twice that density (2ρ₀), based on an effective hadronic model with φ-meson coupling to octet baryons. This decrease is a direct consequence of current conservation and the reduction of effective baryon masses in medium, and the result is presented as a qualitative trend rather than a precise prediction, with the nucleon-loop contribution representing an upper bound due to the uncertain φ-nucleon coupling.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian coupling the φ-meson to octet baryons (nucleons, Λ, Σ) with vector couplings, using coupling constants from quark counting rules and hypernuclear level fits. They compute the one-loop φ-meson self-energy in nuclear matter at zero temperature, including both Dirac-sea (baryon-antibaryon) and Fermi-sea (baryon scattering) polarizations, with effective baryon masses parametrized as functions of density using relativistic mean-field theory relations. The effective φ-meson mass at rest is obtained by solving the dispersion relation with the renormalized self-energy, subtracting the vacuum contribution to remove logarithmic divergences.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum φ-meson mass: m_φ = 1019.4 MeV; vacuum width: Γ_φ = 4.4 MeV
- Effective φ-meson mass decreases by 2–3% in the density range ρ₀ < ρ < 2ρ₀ (hyperon-loop only calculation)
- Effective nucleon mass parametrization: M_N*/M_N ≈ 1 – 0.15 ρ/ρ₀
- φ-nucleon coupling upper bound: g_φN/g_ωN < 0.4; the value 0.32 is used for the nucleon-loop calculation (close to the upper bound)
- φ-hyperon couplings: g_φΛ = g_φΣ = 2.3; φ-nucleon coupling: g_φN = 4.2 (upper bound)
- σ-baryon couplings: g_σN = 8.7, g_σΛ = g_σΣ = 5.2; ω-baryon couplings: g_ωN = 10.6, g_ωΛ = g_ωΣ = 6.9

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) and others who studied ρ and ω meson mass shifts in nuclear matter using nucleon-loop calculations, generalizing it to the φ-meson by including hyperon loops. The results are consistent with the earlier finding that current conservation and reduced effective baryon masses lead to a decrease of vector meson masses in medium. The paper enables experimental searches for φ-meson mass shifts in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, referencing planned experiments at KEK (Enyo, 1995) and preliminary AGS data from E859, and discusses possible signals such as a double φ-peak in e⁺e⁻ spectra or changes in the branching ratio Γ(φ→e⁺e⁻)/Γ(φ→K⁺K⁻).
