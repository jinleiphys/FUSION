# Magnetic Field Effect on Charmonium Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> It is important to understand the strong external magnetic field generated at the very beginning of high energy nuclear collisions. We study the effect of the magnetic field on the charmonium yield and anisotropic distribution in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy. The time dependent Schrödinger equation is employed to describe the motion of $c\bar{c}$ pairs. We compare our model prediction of non- collective anisotropic parameter $v_2$ of $J/ψ$s with CMS data at high transverse momentum. This is the first attempt to measure the magnetic field in high energy nuclear collisions.

# Magnetic Field Effect on Charmonium Production in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The initial magnetic field in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy can be probed via the non-collective anisotropic parameter \(v_2\) of high transverse momentum \(J/\psi\) mesons, provided the field lifetime (0.2 fm/c) is shorter than the charmonium formation time (0.5 fm/c) and the Lorentz force dominates over spin-field and harmonic potential terms at high momentum.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a \(c\bar{c}\) pair evolving under a Cornell potential plus spin-spin interaction, with the magnetic field treated as a time-dependent external perturbation lasting 0.2 fm/c. The relative wave function is expanded in charmonium eigenstates (principal quantum number \(n \leq 6\), orbital angular momentum \(l \leq 7\)), and the coupled equations for the probability amplitudes \(C_\Psi(t)\) are evolved from an initial Gaussian wave package whose parameters (\(r_0 = 0.68\) fm, \(\sigma_0 = 0.02\) fm) are fixed by fitting p+p charmonium fractions at LHC. The magnetic field is taken as uniform along the \(y\)-axis within an almond-shaped region defined by impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm, with magnitude \(eB = 25 m_\pi^2\) and lifetime \(t_B = 0.2\) fm/c from MC simulations.

## Key numbers
- Peak magnetic field: \(eB = 25 m_\pi^2\) for semi-central Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (centrality 40%, impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm); at RHIC the field is \(eB \simeq m_\pi^2\).
- Magnetic field lifetime: \(t_B = 0.2\) fm/c; charmonium formation time: \(t_f = 0.5\) fm/c.
- At \(p_T = 10\) GeV/c and azimuthal angle \(\varphi = 0\): direct \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi'\) feed-down each enhanced by 10%; \(\chi_c\) contribution suppressed by 23%.
- Maximum \(J/\psi\) yield enhancement at \(\varphi = 0\) and \(p_T = 10\) GeV/c: 13%.
- \(\psi'\) yield ratio \(N_{\psi'}^{(B)}/N_{\psi'}^{(0)} = 1.29\) (29% enhancement); \(\chi_c\) yield ratio \(N_{\chi_c}^{(B)}/N_{\chi_c}^{(0)} = 0.84\) (16% suppression).
- Non-collective \(J/\psi\) \(v_2\) at \(p_T \geq 8\) GeV/c: as large as 4%, comparable to CMS data.

## Context
This work extends the study of magnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions beyond the chiral magnetic effect and related phenomena (Kharzeev 2008, Gursoy 2014) by proposing a new observable: the anisotropic production of high-\(p_T\) charmonia. It contrasts with hydrodynamic collective flow models (Zhou 2014, Rapp 2010, Gossiaux 2012) that attribute \(J/\psi\) \(v_2\) to medium interactions at low and intermediate \(p_T\). The calculation uses the time-dependent Schrödinger equation approach previously applied to magnetic field effects on quarkonium (Alford 2013, Yang 2014). The result enables experimental determination of the initial magnetic field strength at LHC via measurement of \(J/\psi\) \(v_2\) at high transverse momentum, and predicts a negative \(v_2\) for \(\chi_c\) if the \(J/\psi\) non-collective \(v_2\) is positive.
