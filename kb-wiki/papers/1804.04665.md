# First Subleading Power Resummation for Event Shapes

> We derive and analytically solve renormalization group (RG) equations of gauge invariant non-local Wilson line operators which resum logarithms for event shape observables $τ$ at subleading power in the $τ\ll 1$ expansion. These equations involve a class of universal jet and soft functions arising through operator mixing, which we call $θ$-jet and $θ$-soft functions. An illustrative example involving these operators is introduced which captures the generic features of subleading power resummation, allowing us to derive the structure of the RG to all orders in $α_s$, and provide field theory definitions of all ingredients. As a simple application, we use this to obtain an analytic leading logarithmic result for the subleading power resummed thrust spectrum for $H\to gg$ in pure glue QCD. This resummation determines the nature of the double logarithmic series at subleading power, which we find is still governed by the cusp anomalous dimension. We check our result by performing an analytic calculation up to ${\cal O}(α_s^3)$. Consistency of the subleading power RG relates subleading power anomalous dimensions, constrains the form of the $θ$-soft and $θ$-jet functions, and implies an exponentiation of higher order loop corrections in the subleading power collinear limit. Our results provide a path for carrying out systematic resummation at subleading power for collider observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that subleading power logarithms for event shapes can be resummed to all orders in αs, using thrust in H→gg in pure glue QCD as a concrete example, with the result that the double logarithmic series at subleading power is still governed by the cusp anomalous dimension, but the resummation requires new θ-jet and θ-soft functions arising through operator mixing at the cross-section level.

## Method

The authors derive and analytically solve renormalization group (RG) equations for gauge-invariant non-local Wilson line operators that resum logarithms for event shape observables τ at subleading power in the τ≪1 expansion. They introduce an illustrative example obtained by multiplying the leading power factorization by τ, which captures the generic features of subleading power resummation and yields a 2×2 mixing structure between δ-function and θ-function jet and soft operators. The RG equations are solved including the running coupling, and the result is checked by performing an analytic fixed-order calculation up to O(αs³).

## Key numbers

* The subleading power thrust spectrum for H→gg in pure glue QCD at leading logarithmic accuracy begins at O(αs) with coefficient c_{1,1}^{(2)} = 8 C_A for the log τ term.
* The O(αs²) term in the subleading power thrust spectrum has coefficient c_{2,3}^{(2)} = -32 C_A² for the log³ τ term.
* The O(αs³) term in the subleading power thrust spectrum has coefficient c_{3,5}^{(2)} = 64 C_A³ for the log⁵ τ term.
* The one-loop gluon cusp anomalous dimension is Γ^{g,0}_{cusp} = 4 C_A, which appears in the mixing anomalous dimensions γ^S_{g,δθ} = 4 Γ^g_{cusp}[αs] and γ^J_{g,δθ} = -2 Γ^g_{cusp}[αs].
* The subleading power soft function at O(αs) gives S^{(2)}_{g,δ}(k,μ) = 8 C_A (αs(μ)/4π) θ(k) (1/ε + log(μ²/k²) + O(ε)), from which the mixing anomalous dimension γ^S_{g,δθ} = 16 (αs/4π) C_A = 4 (αs/4π) Γ^{g,0}_{cusp} is extracted.

## Context

This work extends the systematic understanding of subleading power corrections beyond fixed-order calculations for thrust and N-jettiness by Freedman (2013), Moult (2016), and Boughezal (2016), which had computed O(λ²) terms analytically to O(αs² log³). It provides the first all-orders resummation framework for subleading power event shapes, building on the SCET operator bases developed by Feige (2017), Moult (2017), and Chang (2017), and the factorization of radiative contributions from subleading power Lagrangian insertions by Moult (2019). The results enable systematic resummation at subleading power for collider observables, with the structure of the θ-jet and θ-soft functions providing a path toward higher logarithmic accuracy and higher orders in the power expansion.
