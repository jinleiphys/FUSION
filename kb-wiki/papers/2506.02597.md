# Ab initio study of the radii of oxygen isotopes

> We present an {\em ab initio} study of the charge and matter radii of oxygen isotopes from $^{16}$O to $^{20}$O using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with high-fidelity N$^3$LO chiral interactions. To efficiently address the Monte Carlo sign problem encountered in nuclear radius calculations, we introduce the {\em partial pinhole algorithm}, significantly reducing statistical uncertainties and extending the reach to more neutron-rich and proton-rich isotopes. Our computed charge radii for $^{16}$O, $^{17}$O, and $^{18}$O closely match experimental data, and we predict a charge radius of $2.810(32)$ fm for $^{20}$O. The calculated matter radii show excellent agreement with values extracted from low-energy proton and electron elastic scattering data, but are inconsistent with those derived from interaction cross sections and charge-changing cross section measurements. These discrepancies highlight model-dependent ambiguities in the experimental extraction methods of matter radii and underscore the value of precise theoretical benchmarks from NLEFT calculations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with high-fidelity N3LO chiral interactions can accurately reproduce experimental charge radii for 16O, 17O, and 18O, and predict the charge radius of 20O, but only after introducing a new "partial pinhole algorithm" to mitigate the Monte Carlo sign problem that previously prevented precise radius calculations for neutron-rich isotopes.

## Method
The authors perform ab initio calculations of charge and matter radii for oxygen isotopes 16O through 20O using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) on a spatial lattice with spacing 1.32 fm and a cubic box of length 13.2 fm. They introduce the partial pinhole algorithm, which reduces the rank of the many-body density operator from A-body (total nucleon number) to M-body (with M=4 in this work), combined with the wave function matching method and high-fidelity N3LO chiral interactions. The correlation functions Gαβ for proton-proton, proton-neutron, and neutron-neutron pairs are evaluated via Monte Carlo importance sampling, with double-exponential fits used to extrapolate results to infinite projection time.

## Key numbers
- Charge radius of 16O: 2.704(17) fm (NLEFT) versus 2.699(5) fm from electron scattering data
- Charge radius of 17O: 2.709(15) fm (NLEFT) versus 2.693(8) fm from electron scattering data
- Charge radius of 18O: 2.768(17) fm (NLEFT) versus 2.776(2) fm from electron scattering data
- Charge radius of 20O: predicted 2.810(32) fm (NLEFT), no experimental value available
- Matter radius of 16O: 2.576(17) fm (NLEFT) versus 2.60(8) fm from electron and proton scattering
- Matter radius of 20O: 2.863(33) fm (NLEFT) versus 2.90(10) fm from electron and proton scattering, but 2.69(3) fm from interaction cross sections and 2.71(3) fm from charge-changing cross sections
- Spatial lattice spacing: a = 1.32 fm, corresponding to momentum cutoff Λ ≈ 471 MeV
- Temporal lattice spacing: a_t = 0.001 MeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends previous NLEFT studies by Elhatisari et al. (2022) that used the wave function matching method for binding energies but suffered from large statistical uncertainties in charge radii calculations. It addresses the failure of other ab initio approaches, such as those by Cipollone et al. (2014) and Ekstrom et al. (2015), which underestimated the 16O charge radius by 7-15% and could not reproduce the pronounced increase in 18O charge radius. The results enable precise theoretical benchmarks for matter radii that can help resolve discrepancies between experimental extraction methods, particularly the 0.2 fm difference for 20O between electron/proton scattering data and interaction cross section measurements reported by Lapoux et al. (2016) and Ozawa et al. (2000).
