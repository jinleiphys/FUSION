# Finiteness following from underlying theory: a natural strategy

> A tentative proposal is demonstrated that there is a natural strategy to get rid of unphysical (UV) infinities in QFTs if one adopts the modern standard point of view that a fundamental theory that is complete and well-defined in all respects underlies the QFTs. This simple strategy works in principle for any interaction model and space-time dimension. It provides a physical rationality behind the UV divergence and the conventional renormalization programs and improves the latter in several important aspects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that UV infinities in quantum field theories arise from an illegitimate order of operations, specifically, taking the low-energy limit before performing loop-momentum integration, and that by reversing this order (differentiating with respect to external momenta, integrating, then inverting) one can obtain finite results without ad hoc regularization, though ambiguities remain that must be fixed by phenomenology.

## Method

The author argues that a well-defined underlying theory exists at high energies, and that conventional QFTs are its low-energy (LE) limits. The strategy is to first apply a sufficient number of derivatives with respect to external momenta to a divergent Feynman amplitude, so that the resulting integrand becomes convergent; then the LE limit operator can be moved across the loop integration; after performing the convergent integration, the inverse operation (indefinite integration with respect to external momenta) is applied, yielding a finite expression plus an ambiguous polynomial term. The procedure is extended to multi-loop graphs by recursively treating divergent subgraphs, and to nonperturbative contexts such as the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with delta-potentials.

## Key numbers

* For odd space-time dimension d, the integral I(E) for the two-body T-matrix with delta-potential yields: I_{d;odd}(E) = -i (2μ π^{d/2+1})/(Γ(d/2)(2π)^d) (2μE)^{d/2-1} + N^{[(d-1)/2]}(2μE; {c'}), where N is a polynomial of order [(d-1)/2] in 2μE with arbitrary constants {c'}.
* For even space-time dimension d, the integral yields: I_{d;even}(E) = (2μ π^{d/2})/(Γ(d/2)(2π)^d) (2μE)^{d/2-1} ln(2μE/c'_0) + N^{[d/2]}(2μE; {c'}), where c'_0 is an arbitrary constant.

## Context

This work extends the standard viewpoint (Weinberg, 1995) that QFTs are low-energy effective theories of a more fundamental theory, but argues that existing formulations do not fully exploit this perspective to eliminate UV divergences naturally. The proposal aims to provide a physical rationale for renormalization and to improve upon it by avoiding ad hoc regularization; it also connects to the self-adjoint extension problem in quantum mechanics (Jackiw, 1991; Albeverio, 1988) and to the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics (Weinberg, 1990; Beane et al., 1998). The author claims the strategy works for both renormalizable and unrenormalizable theories, and that it reproduces results from cutoff and dimensional regularization as special cases.
