# Isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section and symmetry energy: sensitivity towards collective transverse flow

> We study the sensitivity of isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross section and symmetry energy on the collective transverse in-plane flow. We find that collective transverse flow in Ca+Ca reactions shows great sensitivity to isospin dependence of nn cross section and symmetry energy.

# Isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section and symmetry energy: sensitivity towards collective transverse flow

## Key claim
Collective transverse in-plane flow in Ca+Ca reactions at 100 MeV/nucleon shows significant sensitivity to both the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section and the symmetry energy, but the paper does not quantify the relative magnitude of these two effects beyond stating that the maximum decrease in flow occurs for the most neutron-rich system when both effects are removed simultaneously.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate Ca+Ca and Xe+Xe reactions at an incident energy of 100 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2-0.4. They calculated the time evolution of the directed transverse momentum <p_x^dir> for systems with N/Z ratios of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0, and then systematically turned off the symmetry energy (setting its strength to zero) and made the nucleon-nucleon cross section isospin-independent (setting sigma_np = sigma_nn) to isolate the contribution of each effect.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy: 100 MeV/nucleon for all simulated reactions.
- Impact parameter range: b/b_max = 0.2-0.4.
- Systems simulated: ^40Ca+^40Ca (N/Z = 1.0), ^52Ca+^52Ca (N/Z = 1.6), ^60Ca+^60Ca (N/Z = 2.0); also ^110Xe+^110Xe, ^140Xe+^140Xe, and ^162Xe+^162Xe.
- The directed transverse momentum <p_x^dir> saturates at around 80 fm/c for all Ca+Ca reactions.
- In the isospin-dependent case, the neutron-proton cross section is three times that of the neutron-neutron or proton-proton cross section.

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin effects in collective flow, building on earlier investigations by Li (1996) and Gautam (unreferenced in text) that showed isospin effects arise from competition between nucleon-nucleon collisions, symmetry energy, and Coulomb potential. The results demonstrate that both the symmetry energy and the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section contribute to transverse flow in neutron-rich systems, with the strongest combined effect seen in the most neutron-rich Ca isotope. This enables future work to disentangle the relative contributions of these two isospin-dependent inputs in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.
