# The Shear Viscosity and Thermal Conductivity of Nuclear Pasta

> We calculate the shear viscosity $η$ and thermal conductivity $κ$ of a nuclear pasta phase in neutron star crusts. This involves complex non-spherical shapes. We use semiclassical molecular dynamics simulations involving 40,000 to 100,000 nucleons. The viscosity $η$ can be simply expressed in terms of the height $Z^*$ and width $Δq$ of the peak in the static structure factor $S_p(q)$. We find that $η$ increases somewhat, compared to a lower density phase involving spherical nuclei, because $Z^*$ decreases from form factor and ion screening effects. However, we do not find a dramatic increase in $η$ from non-spherical shapes, as may occur in conventional complex fluids.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the shear viscosity of nuclear pasta increases somewhat compared to a lower-density phase with spherical nuclei, but they do not find a dramatic increase from non-spherical shapes as might occur in conventional complex fluids. This result is conditional on their semiclassical molecular dynamics model at a temperature of 1 MeV and proton fraction of 0.2.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical molecular dynamics simulations with 40,000 to 100,000 nucleons using a two-body interaction that includes short-range nuclear attraction and long-range screened Coulomb repulsion. They calculated the proton static structure factor S_p(q) from the simulation trajectories and used it to compute the Coulomb logarithm for electron-proton scattering, from which the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity were derived assuming these transport properties are dominated by electrons.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity at density 0.01 fm^-3: 5.3 x 10^10 Pa-s (40,000 nucleons, simulation time 1.3 x 10^6 fm/c)
- Shear viscosity at density 0.025 fm^-3: 17 x 10^10 Pa-s (100,000 nucleons, simulation time 52,000 fm/c)
- Shear viscosity at density 0.05 fm^-3: 63 x 10^10 Pa-s (100,000 nucleons, simulation time 28,000 fm/c)
- Thermal conductivity at density 0.01 fm^-3: 2.5 x 10^20 erg/K cm s
- Thermal conductivity at density 0.025 fm^-3: 4.7 x 10^20 erg/K cm s
- Thermal conductivity at density 0.05 fm^-3: 1.1 x 10^21 erg/K cm s
- Peak height Z* in S_p(q): 34.4 at 0.01 fm^-3, 38.6 at 0.025 fm^-3, 20.3 at 0.05 fm^-3
- Peak position q*: 0.28 fm^-1 at 0.01 fm^-3, 0.31 fm^-1 at 0.025 fm^-3, 0.38 fm^-1 at 0.05 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in neutron star crusts by Chugunov and Yakovlev (who assumed spherical nuclei) and by Flowers and Itoh, and Nandkumar and Pethick, who calculated viscosity in the liquid phase. The authors provide the first calculation of shear viscosity for nuclear pasta phases, showing that the viscosity can be simply expressed in terms of the height and width of the peak in the static structure factor. This enables future work to estimate transport properties without detailed knowledge of pasta shapes, and the results are relevant for damping of neutron star oscillations and crust cooling times.
