# The neutron magnetic form factor G_M^n(Q^2) from Quasi-Elastic inclusive scattering data on D and 4He

> We analyze cross sections for Quasi-Elastic inclusive scattering of electrons on nuclei and show that the observed isolated peaks for relatively low $Q^2$ are unique for the lightest targets. Focusing in particular on D and $^4$He, we investigate in two ways to what measure the above peaks can be allocated to nucleon-elastic processes. We first compute approximate upper limits for the nucleon-inelastic background in the Quasi-Elastic region due to inclusive $Δ$ excitation, and find those to be small. Far more precise is a semi-phenomenological approach, where the dominance of nucleon-elastic processes is translated into a set of stringent requirements. We show that those are very well fulfilled for recent D data, and to a somewhat lesser extent for older D and $^4$He data. With knowledge of $G_{E,M}^p$ and information on $G_E^n$, we then extract $G_M^n$ and find agreement with values obtained by alternative methods. We discuss the sensitivity of the extraction method and mention future applications.

# The neutron magnetic form factor $G_M^n(Q^2)$ from Quasi-Elastic inclusive scattering data on D and $^4$He

## Key claim

The authors show that quasi-elastic inclusive electron scattering on deuterium and $^4$He can be used to extract the neutron magnetic form factor $G_M^n$, provided the nucleon-elastic (NE) component dominates the cross section in the quasi-elastic peak region; this dominance is well fulfilled for recent deuterium data and to a somewhat lesser extent for older deuterium and $^4$He data, but the extracted $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ values are sensitive to the precision of the input cross sections, with 5% changes in cross sections potentially producing ten times larger relative changes in $\alpha_n$.

## Method

The authors analyze total inclusive cross sections for quasi-elastic electron scattering on deuterium and $^4$He, separating the nuclear structure functions into nucleon-elastic (NE) and nucleon-inelastic (NI) components using the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory for the point-nucleon spectral function $f^{PN,A}(x,Q^2)$. They first estimate the NI background from inclusive $\Delta$ excitation as an upper limit, then apply a semi-empirical approach requiring that the measured cross sections follow the computed $x$-dependence of $f^{PN,A}$, that extracted $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ be independent of the $x$-values chosen, and that $\alpha_n$ be independent of the target nucleus. With known proton form factors $G_{E,M}^p$ and information on $G_E^n$, they solve for $\alpha_n(Q^2) = G_M^n(Q^2)/\mu_n G_d(Q^2)$ from the NE component of the reduced cross section.

## Key numbers

- For deuterium data at $E=4.045$ GeV, $\theta=15^\circ$ and $23^\circ$, the extracted $\alpha_n$ shows little variation with $x_k$ and produces excellent fits to the data; deviations due to NI appear on the inelastic side of the QEP and grow with $\nu$ and $Q^2$.
- For $^4$He data at $E=2.02$ GeV, $\theta=20^\circ$ (average $Q^2\approx 0.45$ GeV$^2$), the extracted average $\langle \alpha_n\rangle = 1.08 \pm 0.03$ from 9 points, increasing to $1.10 \pm 0.03$ after removing an irregular point near the QEP.
- For $^4$He data at $E=3.6$ GeV, $\theta=16^\circ$, the average $\langle \alpha_n\rangle = 1.05 \pm 0.02$ from 9 points with $0.375$ GeV $\le \nu\le 0.495$ GeV.
- For $^4$He data at $E=3.6$ GeV, $\theta=20^\circ$, the average over 8 points with $\nu < 0.630$ GeV gives $\langle \alpha_n\rangle = 1.06 \pm 0.02$.
- The dipole form factor is $G_d(Q^2)=[1+Q^2/0.71]^{-2}$ with $Q^2$ in GeV$^2$.
- The proton deviation $\alpha_p$ reaches a maximum of approximately 1.07 at $Q^2\approx 2$ GeV$^2$ and crosses 1 at $Q^2\approx 5$ GeV$^2$.
- The ratio $\gamma = \mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ is 1 for $Q^2\lesssim 0.3$ GeV$^2$ and approximately $[1-0.14(Q^2-0.3)]$ for $0.3 \lesssim Q^2\lesssim 5.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The critical $Q^2$ below which NE dominates is estimated as $Q_c^2\approx (2.0-2.5)$ GeV$^2$.
- For the older deuterium data of Lung (1993) at $Q^2=1.75$ and $2.50$ GeV$^2$, the total cross sections and separated transverse components produce consistent $\alpha_n$ values, but the data are given only to two decimals and follow theoretical predictions to about 10% accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the standard method of extracting $G_M^n$ from quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering, previously used by Lung (1993) and others, by applying improved GRS theory calculations of the point-nucleon spectral function $f^{PN,A}$ that include final state interactions and relativistic effects. The authors compare their results with values obtained from alternative methods such as semi-inclusive D$(e,e'N)X$ experiments (Anklin, Kubon) and asymmetry measurements in $\vec{^3}{\rm He}(\vec{e},e')X$ (Gao, Xu). The analysis enables future application to forthcoming high-precision JLab data on $^3$He, deuterium, and $^4$He, which promise to sharpen the extracted $\alpha_n(Q^2)$ values and test whether $\alpha_p$ and $\alpha_n$ continue to behave similarly as functions of $Q^2$.
