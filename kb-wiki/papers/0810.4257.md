# Charmed hadron signals of partonic medium

> We present a short review of our results on the collectivity and the suppression pattern of charmed mesons in heavy-ion collisions based on the microscopic Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach for different scenarios of charm interactions with the surrounding matter - the 'comover' dissociation by mesons with further recreation by D+Dbar channels and 'pre-hadronic' interaction scenarios. While at SPS energies the hadronic 'comover' absorption scenario is found to be compatible with the experimental data, the dynamics of c and cbar quarks at RHIC are dominated by partonic or 'pre-hadronic' interactions in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma stage and cannot be modeled by pure hadronic interactions. We find that the collective flow of charm in the purely hadronic scenario appears compatible with the data at SPS energies but underestimates the data at top RHIC energies. Thus, the large elliptic flow v2 of D mesons and the low R_AA(pT) of J/Psi seen experimentally at RHIC have to be attributed to early interactions of non-hadronic degrees of freedom. Simultaneously, we observe that non-hadronic interactions are mandatory in order to describe the narrowing of the J/Psi rapidity distribution from p+p to central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy. We demonstrate additionally that the strong quenching of high-pT J/Psi's in central Au+Au collisions indicates that a fraction of final J/Psi mesons is created by a coalescence mechanism close to the phase boundary.

## Key claim

The dynamics of charm quarks in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies are dominated by partonic or 'pre-hadronic' interactions in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma stage and cannot be modeled by pure hadronic interactions, whereas at SPS energies the hadronic 'comover' absorption scenario remains compatible with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach to simulate charm production and interactions in heavy-ion collisions. They compared two scenarios: a 'comover' dissociation model where formed hadrons dissociate charmonia via processes like \(c\bar{c}+meson\to D+\bar{D}\) with recreation by inverse channels, and a 'pre-hadronic' interaction scenario where charm quarks interact with pre-hadrons (states still under formation or in high-energy-density cells) before hadronization. The calculations were compared to experimental data from SPS (NA50, NA60) and RHIC (PHENIX, STAR) collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Formation time for newly produced hadrons in HSD: \(\tau_F \approx 0.8\) fm/c in their rest frame
- Energy density threshold for QGP formation: 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) in the local rest frame
- At SPS (158 A\(\cdot\)GeV), the hadronic comover absorption scenario matches \(J/\Psi\) suppression in In+In and Pb+Pb as well as the \(\Psi^\prime\) to \(J/\Psi\) ratio
- For Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, HSD predicts an enhancement of \(D+\bar{D}\) mesons at low momenta with a maximum at \(p_T \approx 1\) GeV/c and relative suppression for \(p_T > 2\) GeV/c
- The purely hadronic scenario underestimates the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D\)-mesons at RHIC; including pre-hadronic interactions increases \(v_2\) but still stays below PHENIX data

## Context

This work extends earlier HSD studies by Bratkovskaya et al. (2003, 2005) and Linnyk et al. (2007) on charm dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. It contradicts the purely hadronic 'comover' absorption model at RHIC energies, which had been successful at SPS energies as shown by Olena et al. (2007). The findings enable further investigation of partonic interactions in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and suggest that coalescence of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs near the phase boundary contributes to final \(J/\Psi\) production at RHIC.
