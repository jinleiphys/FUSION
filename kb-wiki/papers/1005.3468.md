# On the convergence of complex Langevin dynamics: the three-dimensional XY model at finite chemical potential

> The three-dimensional XY model is studied at finite chemical potential using complex Langevin dynamics. The validity of the approach is probed at small chemical potential using imaginary chemical potential and continuity arguments, and at larger chemical potential by comparison with the world line method. While complex Langevin works for larger beta, we find that it fails for smaller beta, in the region of the phase diagram corresponding to the disordered phase. Diagnostic tests are developed to identify symptoms correlated with incorrect convergence. We argue that the erroneous behaviour at smaller beta is not due to the sign problem, but rather resembles dynamics observed in complex Langevin simulations of simple models with complex noise.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics works for the three-dimensional XY model at finite chemical potential in the ordered phase (large beta) but fails in the disordered phase (small beta), and this failure is not due to the sign problem but rather resembles dynamics observed in complex Langevin simulations of simple models with complex noise.

## Method

The authors simulated the three-dimensional XY model on lattices of size 8^3 and 10^3 using complex Langevin dynamics with an adaptive stepsize and real noise. They compared results at small chemical potential using continuity arguments from imaginary chemical potential simulations, and at larger chemical potential by direct comparison with the world line formulation solved via the worm algorithm, which has no sign problem. Diagnostic tests were developed based on the width of the distribution in the imaginary field direction and the distribution of the maximal drift force.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition at zero chemical potential occurs at beta_c = 0.45421, separating the disordered phase (beta < beta_c) from the ordered phase (beta > beta_c).
- At beta = 0.2 and mu^2 = 0.1 on an 8^3 lattice, the average phase factor in the phase quenched theory is approximately 0.60, with Omega Delta f approximately 0.51.
- The strong-coupling expansion gives the action density as -0.0621 + O(10^-4) for beta = 0.2 and -0.145 + O(10^-3) for beta = 0.3, independent of chemical potential.
- The strong-coupling estimate for the critical coupling at nonzero mu is beta_c(mu) = 2e^{-mu}.
- The Roberge-Weiss transition occurs at mu_I = pi/N_tau, corresponding to mu^2 = -0.154 for N_tau = 8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of complex Langevin dynamics at finite chemical potential (Aarts 2008, 2009) and applies it to the three-dimensional XY model, which is closely related to the relativistic Bose gas where complex Langevin was shown to work well at weak coupling. The study enables direct comparison with the world line formulation of Chandrasekharan (2008) and Banerjee (2010), which has no sign problem and can be simulated efficiently with the worm algorithm. The findings contradict the expectation that the sign problem is the primary obstacle, instead identifying the disordered phase as the region where complex Langevin fails, similar to behavior seen in simple models with complex noise.
