# Enhanced binding and cold compression of nuclei due to admixture of antibaryons

> We discuss the possibility of producing a new kind of nuclear system by putting a few antibaryons inside ordinary nuclei. The structure of such systems is calculated within the relativistic mean--field model assuming that the nucleon and antinucleon potentials are related by the G-parity transformation. The presence of antinucleons leads to decreasing vector potential and increasing scalar potential for the nucleons. As a result, a strongly bound system of high density is formed. Due to the significant reduction of the available phase space the annihilation probability might be strongly suppressed in such systems.

# Enhanced binding and cold compression of nuclei due to admixture of antibaryons

## Key claim

The presence of a single antiproton in a light nucleus like ¹⁶O or ⁸Be can produce a strongly bound, high-density nuclear system (termed a Super Bound Nucleus) with binding energy up to ~1159 MeV, provided that the annihilation rate is suppressed by a factor of 20–30 due to reduced phase space for decay products.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for ¹⁶O and ⁸Be nuclei containing one antiproton, using three different parametrizations (NL3, NL-Z2, and TM1). The antiproton potentials were obtained from the nucleon potentials via G-parity transformation, and the coupled equations for nucleons, antinucleons, and meson fields were solved iteratively with axial and reflection symmetry, including BCS pairing for nucleons and a blocking approximation for the antiproton.

## Key numbers

- Maximum nucleon density in ¹⁶O with one antiproton reaches (2–4) ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≃ 0.15 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear density; the largest compression is predicted by the TM1 parametrization.
- Antiproton binding energy in ¹⁶O: ~1130 MeV (TM1), nearly the same for NL3, shallower for NL-Z2.
- Total binding energy of the ¹⁶O+antiproton system: 828 MeV (NL-Z2), 1051 MeV (NL3), 1159 MeV (TM1); compared to 127.8–130.3 MeV for normal ¹⁶O.
- For ⁸Be with one antiproton (NL3): maximum density increases from 0.20 fm⁻³ to 0.61 fm⁻³; binding energy increases from 52.9 MeV to about 700 MeV; deformation β₂ changes from 1.20 to 0.23.
- Available energy Q for annihilation in the lowest antiproton states: 600–680 MeV (TM1), 810–880 MeV (NL3), 990–1050 MeV (NL-Z2).
- Estimated formation probability W for trapping an antiproton in a light nucleus: W ≃ 1.4 × 10⁻³ for a 10 GeV antiproton beam on oxygen.
- Estimated lifetime of the antiproton in the SBN: 15–20 fm/c, corresponding to a width Γ ≃ 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field treatment of nuclei to include explicit real antibaryons, going beyond earlier discussions of negative-energy states in the Dirac sea (Mishustin 1990, 1993) and calculations by Mao et al. (1999) that did not allow the target nucleus to rearrange self-consistently. The predicted compression effect is analogous to that found for K⁻ bound states in ⁸Be (Kishimoto and Behnke). The authors note that the RMF model may become questionable at densities of (2–3) ρ₀, and that an extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Mishustin 1999) gives similar deeply bound states but with binding energies smaller by about a factor of 3. The work enables experimental searches for narrow pion or kaon lines near 1 GeV as signatures of deeply-bound antibaryon states, and suggests that energetic antiproton beams at multi-GeV energies could produce such systems.
