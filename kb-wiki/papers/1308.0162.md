# High-spin structures of 88Kr and 89Rb: Evolution from collective to single-particle behaviors

> The high-spin states of the two neutron-rich nuclei, 88Kr and 89R have been studied from the 18O + 208Pb fusion-fission reaction. Their level schemes were built from triple gamma-ray coincidence data and gamma-gamma angular correlations were analyzed in order to assign spin and parity values to most of the observed states. The two levels schemes evolve from collective structures to single-particle excitations as a function of the excitation energy. Comparison with results of shell-model calculations gives the specific proton and neutron configurations which are involved to generate the angular momentum along the yrast lines.

## Key claim

The high-spin level schemes of 88Kr and 89Rb evolve from collective vibrational structures at low excitation energy to single-particle excitations at higher energy, with specific proton and neutron broken-pair configurations identified via shell-model calculations; the abstract's claim of "evolution from collective to single-particle behaviors" is qualified by the finding that even the first excited states are already mixed, and that the single-particle dominance applies specifically to states above the first few excited levels.

## Method

High-spin states of 88Kr and 89Rb were populated via the 18O + 208Pb fusion-fission reaction at 85 MeV incident energy, using the Euroball array to detect gamma rays. Level schemes were constructed from triple-gamma coincidence data, and gamma-gamma angular correlations measured at three average relative angles (22°, 46°, and 75°) were used to assign spin and parity values. Shell-model calculations using the "glekpn" interaction and the NuShellX@MSU code, with five proton orbits and five neutron orbits (excluding the πf7/2 and νg9/2 subshells which were constrained to be fully occupied), were performed to interpret the configurations.

## Key numbers

- 88Kr: 2+1 state at 775.1 keV; 4+1 state at 1643.5 keV; level scheme extended up to 8 MeV excitation energy
- 88Kr: 460-keV transition identified as ΔI=0 dipole (4+ → 4+), with the 2103.2-keV state assigned Iπ = 4+
- 88Kr: 440-keV and 751-keV transitions show energy broadenings implying half-lives ≤ 1 ps, giving B(M1, 440 keV) ≥ 0.45 μn2 and B(M1, 751 keV) ≥ 0.09 μn2
- 89Rb: ground state 3/2−; 220.5-keV state (5/2−); 585.7-keV state (5/2−); 997.1-keV state (7/2−); 1194.6-keV state (9/2+); 2003.4-keV state (13/2+); 2839.5-keV state (17/2+); 4032.1-keV state (21/2+)
- 89Rb: 1195-keV level is isomeric with T1/2 = 15.2(28) ns (Tornyi 2009) or 8(2) ns (Pasternak 2009); B(M2; 9/2+ → 5/2−) = 3.9(7)×10−2 W.u. or 7(2)×10−2 W.u.
- 89Rb: 974.3-keV transition is M2 (quadrupole) connecting the 9/2+ state to the 5/2− state at 220.5 keV
- 88Kr: 2+1 energies of even-Z N=52 isotones range from 832 keV (Z=38) to 624 keV (Z=32); E(4+1)/E(2+1) ratios are close to 2

## Context

This work extends the high-spin study of neutron-rich N=52 isotones beyond previous measurements on 88Kr from spontaneous fission of 248Cm (Rzaca-Urban 2000) and on 89Rb from heavy-ion reactions (Bucurescu 2007; Tornyi 2009; Pasternak 2009). The results are compared with shell-model calculations using the "glekpn" interaction (M. J. A. de Voigt 1990) and the NuShellX@MSU code (Brown 2007). The identification of specific broken-pair configurations (e.g., (νd5/2)1(νg7/2)1(πg9/2)1 for the 21/2+ state in 89Rb) provides input for testing two-body matrix elements relevant to predicting the behavior of 78Ni and its neighbors. The measured B(M2) values for 89Rb (3.9–7)×10−2 W.u. fall in the same range as those known in 85,87,91Rb (4.4–10)×10−2 W.u., confirming systematic trends for M2 transitions in this region.
