# Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting in deformed odd-odd nuclei within a microscopic approach

> Low-lying bandhead states in axially prolate deformed odd-odd nuclei have long been described essentially within the rotor+two-quasiparticle picture. This approach allows one to explain the appearance of so-called Gallagher-Moszkowski doublets of bandheads with $K = Ω_n \pm Ω_p$, sum and difference of neutron and proton angular momentum projections on the symmetry axis. According to an empirical rule stated by Gallagher and Moszkowski the spin-aligned configuration lies lower in energy than the spin-anti-aligned configuration. A recent study by Robledo, Bernard and Bertsch in Phys. Rev. C 89, 021303(R) (2014) within the Gogny energy-density functional with selfconsistent blocking of the unpaired nucleons showed that calculations fail to reproduce this rule in about half of the cases and points to the density-dependent term of the functional as responsible of this failure. In this paper we aim at pushing further this analysis to exhibit the mechanism underlying the energy splitting in a Gallagher-Moszkowski doublet. We work in the framework of the Skyrme energy-density functional approach, including BCS pairing correlations with selfconsistent blocking. We use the SIII parametrization with time-odd terms and seniority pairing matrix elements extending a previous study of K-isomeric states in even-even nuclei [Phys. Rev. C 105, 044329 (2022)]. We find that the energy splitting results from a competition between the spin-spin, density-dependent and current-current terms of the Skyrme energy-density functional. In doublets where the larger K value is lower in energy the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule is always satisfied by the SIII Skyrme energy-density functional. In doublets, on the contrary, where the smaller K value lies lower, the energy splittings are calculated to be rather small and often a disagreement with the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule occurs.

## Key claim

The energy splitting in Gallagher-Moszkowski doublets results from a competition between the spin-spin, density-dependent, and current-current terms of the Skyrme energy-density functional; the SIII parametrization always satisfies the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule for doublets where the larger K value is lower in energy, but often disagrees with the rule for doublets where the smaller K value lies lower, where calculated energy splittings are rather small.

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent blocking calculations within the Skyrme energy-density functional approach using the SIII parametrization with time-odd terms, combined with BCS pairing correlations using seniority pairing matrix elements. They studied selected strongly deformed odd-odd nuclei in the rare-earth region (around A=156 and A=176) and the actinide region (A=230-250), comparing calculated bandhead excitation energies to experimentally observed Gallagher-Moszkowski doublets. The single-particle states were expanded in a truncated cylindrical harmonic-oscillator basis with parity symmetry, and the minimal scheme of time-odd fields (spin and current vector fields) was retained in the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing strength parameter G_n = 17.1 MeV adjusted to the first 2+ excitation energy in 156Gd, with G_p = 0.9 × G_n
- Calculated excitation energies in 154Eu, 156Eu, 158Tb, 160Tb, 172Tm, 174Lu, 176Lu, 178Lu, 178Ta, 180Ta, 230Pa, 234Pa, 238Np, and 242Am generally agree with experimental data within less than 200 keV for most doublets
- The (0-,1-) doublet in 230Pa shows the largest discrepancy, with overestimation of excitation energies of about 1 MeV
- The (5+,0+) doublet in 160Tb shows a discrepancy of about 600 keV
- The (0-,1-) doublet in 172Tm shows an overestimation of about 450 keV
- In 242Am, the experimental excitation energy of the 0- state is 44.092 keV, used as reference for calculated states
- A large shell gap of about 1 MeV is obtained for N=142, and about 1.5 MeV for N=152 in 250Cf

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Robledo, Bernard, and Bertsch (2014), who studied Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting with the Gogny energy-density functional and found that calculations fail to reproduce the empirical rule in about half of cases due to the density-dependent term. The present study uses the Skyrme SIII parametrization, extending a previous investigation of K-isomeric states in even-even nuclei by Minkov et al. (2022). The results enable a microscopic understanding of the mechanism behind the Gallagher-Moszkowski rule and identify which terms of the energy-density functional drive the energy splitting, potentially guiding improvements to nuclear energy-density functionals for describing odd-odd deformed nuclei.
