# Double heavy tri-hadron bound state via delocalized $π$ bond

> The number of exotic candidates which are beyond the conventional quark model has grown dramatically during the last decades. Some of them could be viewed as analogues of the deuteron. Similarly, the existence of the triton indicates that bound states formed by three hadrons could also exist. To illustrate this possibility, we study the $DD^*K$ and $BB^*\bar{K}$ systems by using the Born-Oppenheimer Approximation. To leading order, only one-pion exchange potentials are considered, which means that the three constitutes share one virtual pion. That is similar to the role of the delocalized $π$ bond for the formation of Benzene in chemistry. After solving the Schrödinger equation, we find two three-body $DD^*K$ and $BB^*\bar{K}$ bound states with masses $4317.92_{-4.32}^{+3.66}~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $11013.65_{-8.84}^{+8.49}~\mathrm{MeV}$, respectively. The masses of their $D\bar{D}^*K$ and $B\bar{B}^*\bar{K}$ analogues are $4317.92_{-6.55}^{+6.13}~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $11013.65_{-9.02}^{+8.68}~\mathrm{MeV}$. From the experimental side, the $D\bar{D}^*K$ bound state could be found by analyzing the current world data of the $B\to J/ψππK$ process by focusing on the $J/ψπK$ channel. Its confirmation could also help to understand the formation of kaonic nuclei in nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The authors predict that three-hadron bound states exist in the $DD^*K$ and $BB^*\bar{K}$ systems, but only when the two-body $D^*K$ or $B^*\bar{K}$ subsystem is an isospin singlet with sufficient attraction; the existence of the three-body state depends on the binding energy of that two-body subsystem exceeding a critical value.

## Method
The authors apply the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to the three-body problem, treating the two heavy mesons ($D$ and $D^*$ or $B$ and $B^*$) as fixed and solving the Schrodinger equation for the light kaon at each separation. Only leading-order one-pion exchange potentials are included, coupling the $DD^*K$, $DDK^*$, and $D^*DK$ channels, with a monopole form factor cutoff $\Lambda_{D^*K}=803.2~\mathrm{MeV}$ tuned to reproduce the $D_{s1}(2460)$ as a $D^*K$ bound state. The effective Born-Oppenheimer potential from the kaon is then used in a second Schrodinger equation for the heavy meson pair.

## Key numbers
- $DD^*K$ bound state mass: $4317.92_{-4.32}^{+3.66}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- $BB^*\bar{K}$ bound state mass: $11013.65_{-8.84}^{+8.49}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- $D\bar{D}^*K$ analogue mass: $4317.92_{-6.55}^{+6.13}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- $B\bar{B}^*\bar{K}$ analogue mass: $11013.65_{-9.02}^{+8.68}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- Binding energy of $DD^*K$ relative to threshold: $8.29^{+4.32}_{-3.66}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- Binding energy of $BB^*\bar{K}$ relative to threshold: $41.76^{+8.84}_{-8.49}~\mathrm{MeV}$
- Root-mean-square radius between $K$ and $D^{(*)}$: $1.14~\mathrm{fm}$
- Root-mean-square radius between $DD^*$: $1.65~\mathrm{fm}$
- Root-mean-square radius between kaon and bottom mesons: $0.96~\mathrm{fm}$
- Root-mean-square radius between $BB^*$: $0.65~\mathrm{fm}$

## Context
This work extends the hadronic molecule concept beyond two-body systems, analogous to how the triton extends the deuteron in nuclear physics. It builds on studies of kaonic nuclei such as $\bar{K}NN$ by the E15 Collaboration (Sada 2016, Hashimoto 2014) and on chiral dynamical analyses of $D^*K$ and $B^*\bar{K}$ interactions (Guo 2006). The prediction enables experimental searches in existing $B\to J/\psi\pi\pi K$ data by focusing on the $J/\psi\pi K$ channel, and could inform the understanding of kaonic nuclei formation.
