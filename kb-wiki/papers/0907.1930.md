# Chirally symmetric effective field theory for nuclei

> The Lorentz-invariant nuclear lagrangian of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (FST) is discussed. The FST lagrangian is derived in terms of an effective field theory and exhibits a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$. The relevant degrees of freedom are nucleons, pions and the low-lying non-Goldstone bosons: isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) mesons, and isovector vector ($ρ$) mesons. The terms in the lagrangian are organized by applying Georgi's naive dimensional analysis and naturalness condition. As a consequence all coupling constants in theory are dimensionless and of order unity.

# Chirally symmetric effective field theory for nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents the FST (Furnstahl, Serot, Tang) effective chiral Lagrangian as a Lorentz-invariant effective field theory for nuclei that incorporates nonlinear chiral symmetry, but the construction is limited to quartic order (index \(\tilde{\Delta} \leq 4\)) and requires thirteen low-energy constants that must be determined from experimental data, with the mean-field (Dirac-Hartree) approximation neglecting all quantum fluctuations of the meson fields.

## Method
The FST Lagrangian is constructed by expanding in powers of hadronic fields (nucleons, pions, \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons) and their derivatives, organized using Georgi's naive dimensional analysis (NDA) with a naturalness condition that all dimensionless coupling constants are of order unity. The chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly via the WCCWZ formalism (Weinberg, Callan, Coleman, Wess, Zumino), and the Lagrangian is truncated at quartic order. The Dirac-Hartree (mean-field) approximation is then applied, treating meson fields as classical expectation values and solving coupled one-dimensional radial differential equations for spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Pion-decay constant: \(f_{\pi} \approx 93\) MeV
- Large energy scale: \(\Lambda \sim 4\pi f_{\pi} \sim 1\) GeV
- Nucleon mass used as large scale in power counting: \(M = 939\) MeV
- Axial coupling constant: \(g_{\mathrm{A}} \approx 1.26\)
- \(\omega\) meson mass: \(m_{\mathrm{v}} = 782\) MeV
- \(\rho\) meson mass: \(m_{\rho} = 770\) MeV
- Anomalous magnetic moments: \(\lambda_{\mathrm{p}} = 1.793\) (proton), \(\lambda_{\mathrm{n}} = -1.913\) (neutron)
- Vector-meson dominance coupling: \(g_{\gamma} = 5.01\)
- Center-of-mass correction (empirical estimate by Reinhard): \(E_{\mathrm{CM}} \approx 17.2 A^{-0.2}\) MeV
- Older harmonic oscillator shell model estimate: \(E_{\mathrm{CM}} \approx \frac{3}{4} 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV

## Context
This work extends the Walecka quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) framework (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986) by incorporating chiral symmetry through the nonlinear realization of Weinberg (1968) and Callan, Coleman, Wess, and Zumino (1969), and by applying Georgi's naive dimensional analysis (Georgi, 1984, 1993) to organize the Lagrangian expansion. The FST approach enables a systematic connection between QCD and nuclear structure calculations at the mean-field level, with the Dirac-Hartree approximation providing a framework for computing binding energies and charge radii of spherical nuclei, while leaving open the challenge of determining the thirteen low-energy constants from experimental data and extending the approach beyond mean-field to include quantum fluctuations.
