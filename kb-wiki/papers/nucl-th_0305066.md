# Relativistic formulation of Glauber theory for A(e,e'p) reactions

> At sufficiently large proton energies, Glauber multiple-scattering theory offers good opportunities for describing the final state interactions in electro-induced proton emission off nuclear targets. A fully unfactorized relativistic formulation of Glauber multiple-scattering theory is presented. The effect of truncating the Glauber multiple-scattering series is discussed. Relativistic effects in the description of the final-state interactions are found not to exceed the few percent level. Also the frequently adopted approximation of replacing the wave functions for the individual scattering nucleons by some average density, is observed to have a minor impact on the results. Predictions for the separated $^4$He$(e,e'p)$ response functions are given in quasi-elastic kinematics and a domain corresponding with $1 \leq Q^2 \leq 2$ (GeV)$^2$.

## Key claim

At sufficiently high proton energies (lab momenta above roughly 1 GeV), Glauber multiple-scattering theory provides a viable alternative to optical-potential-based distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) models for describing final-state interactions in A(e,e'p) reactions, with relativistic effects in the final-state interactions found not to exceed the few percent level.

## Method

The authors developed a fully unfactorized relativistic formulation of Glauber multiple-scattering theory for A(e,e'p) reactions, starting from the Dirac equation in the eikonal approximation and deriving a Dirac-Glauber transition amplitude that incorporates the multiple-scattering operator as a product of profile functions for proton-nucleon scattering. The formalism uses mean-field Slater-determinant wave functions from the sigma-omega model for the initial and final nuclear states, with the Glauber operator acting on the struck proton's wave function to account for successive scatterings off frozen spectator nucleons. The authors evaluated the Dirac-Glauber phase for several nuclei and compared results from truncating the multiple-scattering series at various orders against the full operator.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic effects in the description of final-state interactions were found not to exceed the few percent level (no specific percentage given beyond "few percent").
- Predictions for separated 4He(e,e'p) response functions are given in quasi-elastic kinematics for the domain 1 ≤ Q² ≤ 2 (GeV)².
- The eikonal approximation was shown to successfully reproduce exact Dirac partial-wave results for elastic p-40Ca scattering at T_p ≈ 500 MeV.
- Bianconi and Radici showed that for ejectile momenta exceeding 1 GeV, the eikonal approximation almost reproduced 12C(e,e'p) differential cross sections obtained from partial-wave expansion.

## Context

This work extends non-relativistic Glauber models for A(e,e'p) reactions, such as those by Benhar (2000) for 4He and by Kohama (2000), Frankfurt (1995), and Nikolaev (1995) for heavier nuclei, into a fully relativistic unfactorized framework. It also builds upon relativistic DWIA (RDWIA) models developed by Picklesimer (1985), Johansson (1996), Udias (1993), and Meucci (2000), which use optical potentials derived from elastic p+A scattering data but become impractical at higher proton energies. The relativistic Glauber formulation enables calculations of A(e,e'p) observables at proton momenta above 1 GeV where optical potential approaches are less natural, and provides a framework for investigating color transparency phenomena and medium modifications of nucleons.
