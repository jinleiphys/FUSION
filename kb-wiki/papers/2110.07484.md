# Effective range expansion for narrow near-threshold resonances

> We discuss some general features of the effective range expansion, the content of its parameters with respect to the nature of the pertinent near-threshold states and the necessary modifications in the presence of coupled channels, isospin violations and unstable constituents. As illustrative examples, we analyse the properties of the $χ_{c1}(3872)$ and $T_{cc}^+$ states supporting the claim that these exotic states have a predominantly molecular nature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to recent claims, the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$ and $T_{cc}^+$ states are predominantly molecular in nature, provided one properly accounts for coupled-channel effects, isospin violations, and finite-range corrections when interpreting the effective range expansion parameters through the Weinberg compositeness criterion.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the scattering length and effective range from a coupled-channel Flatte parameterization, explicitly separating contributions from the elastic channel, the higher-threshold charged channel, and inelastic channels. They then develop a procedure to subtract isospin-violating and coupled-channel effects to obtain single-channel ERE parameters suitable for the Weinberg criterion, and introduce a generalized compositeness measure $\bar X$ that avoids singularities present in the original Weinberg formula.

## Key numbers

- For the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$: using $g_1^2 \ge 0.108$ and $E_f = -7.2$ MeV from LHCb data, the corrected effective range is $r \gtrsim -3$ fm, yielding compositeness $\bar X \gtrsim 0.9$
- For the $T_{cc}^+$: the scattering length from LHCb is $a = [-(7.16 \pm 0.51) + i(1.85 \pm 0.28)]$ fm, with an upper bound on the effective range $|r| < 11.9$ fm at 90% CL, yielding compositeness $\bar X \gtrsim 0.6$
- The finite-range correction to the effective range from short-range physics is estimated as $r_{\rm finite} \simeq 1$ fm for both states
- The isospin-violating correction to the effective range from the charged channel is $-1.57$ fm for the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$ and $-3.78$ fm for the $T_{cc}^+$
- The convergence range of the ERE is limited to $k \le 7$ MeV for the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$ and $k \le 9$ MeV for the $T_{cc}^+$ due to the finite width of the $D^*$ mesons

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg compositeness analysis (Weinberg, 1965) originally developed for the deuteron and later generalized to unstable states by Baru et al. (2003). It directly addresses and contradicts the conclusion of Esposito et al. (2021) that the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$ is predominantly a compact state, showing that the large negative effective range they cited arises from coupled-channel and isospin-violating effects that must be subtracted before applying the Weinberg criterion. The analysis enables future experimental studies to extract the nature of near-threshold exotic states by properly accounting for coupled-channel dynamics, isospin breaking, and finite-range corrections in the effective range expansion.
