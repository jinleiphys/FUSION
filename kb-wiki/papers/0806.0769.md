# Probing gluon shadowing with forward photons at RHIC

> There is a major need to better constrain nuclear parton densities in order to provide reliable perturbative QCD predictions at the LHC as well as to probe possible non-linear evolution at small values of x. In these proceedings, we discuss how the production of prompt photons at large rapidity in p-p and d-Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) is sensitive to the nuclear modifications of gluon distributions at x~0.001 and at rather low scales, Q^2~10 GeV^2. The nuclear production ratio, R_dAu=sigma(d+A->gamma+X)/(2A sigma(p+p->gamma X), is computed for isolated prompt photons at NLO using the nDSg nuclear parton densities, in order to assess the visibility of the signal. We also emphasise that the expected counting rates in a year of running at RHIC are large, indicating that R_dAu could be measured with a high statistical accuracy.

# Probing gluon shadowing with forward photons at RHIC

## Key claim

The nuclear production ratio R_dAu for isolated prompt photons at forward rapidity (y=3) in d-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV is sensitive to gluon shadowing at x ~ 0.001 and Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2, but the observed suppression is significantly affected by isospin effects because the deuteron projectile is poorer in u quarks than the proton, requiring an isospin correction factor of approximately 5/6 to relate the measured ratio to the nuclear gluon modification ratio R_G.

## Method

The nuclear production ratio R_dAu = sigma(d+A -> gamma+X) / (2A * sigma(p+p -> gamma+X)) was computed at next-to-leading order (NLO) for isolated prompt photons using the nDSg nuclear parton densities, with the calculation performed at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and forward rapidity y=3. The authors derived an approximate factorization showing that at forward rapidity the nuclear production ratio is proportional to the gluon nuclear modification ratio R_G(x) evaluated at x = x_T * e^{-y}, and then corrected for isospin differences between the deuteron and proton projectiles.

## Key numbers

- The predicted nuclear production ratio R_dAu for isolated photons at y=3 and sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using nDSg NLO parton densities is approximately 0.65.
- The gluon nuclear modification ratio R_G at the same kinematics is approximately 0.8, giving an isospin correction factor of roughly 5/6 (0.65/0.8).
- The isospin-corrected R_G agrees with the predicted R_dAu within about 10%.
- Expected integrated luminosities are 0.02 pb^{-1} at RHIC I and 0.45 pb^{-1} at RHIC II for d-Au collisions.
- Photon yields are large up to rather high p_T in the rapidity range y=2.5-3.5 at both RHIC I and RHIC II.

## Context

This work extends the study of Arleo (2007) on using prompt photon production to probe nuclear gluon distributions, addressing the poor precision (approximately 20%) on nuclear gluon densities at x > 10^{-2} from prior determinations (Armesto, 2006). The approach is complementary to non-linear QCD evolution studies at small x (Gelis, 2007). The results indicate that forward photon measurements at RHIC by PHENIX and STAR can provide tight constraints on G^A/G^p at small x, which is needed for reliable perturbative QCD predictions at the LHC.
