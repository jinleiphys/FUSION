# Pseudorapidity and initial energy densities in p+p and heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A known exact and accelerating solution of relativistic hydrodynamics for perfect fluids is utilized to describe pseudorapidity densities of $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV Pb$+$Pb and $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV $p$$+$$p$ collisions at LHC. We evaluate a conjectured initial energy densities $ε_{\rm corr}$ in these collisions, and compare them to Bjorken's initial energy density estimates, and to results for Pb$+$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV and $p$$+$$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 7$ and 8 TeV.

# Pseudorapidity and initial energy densities in p+p and heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The accelerating hydrodynamic solution with a super-hard equation of state (κ=1) can describe pseudorapidity densities in both Pb+Pb and p+p collisions at LHC, and the conjectured initial energy density correction ε_corr that accounts for longitudinal acceleration yields values 15-25% larger than the standard Bjorken estimate, but this correction is based on results derived exactly only for κ=1 and remains a conjecture for realistic equations of state with c_s² ≈ 0.1.

## Method
The authors fit a 1+1 dimensional exact accelerating solution of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics (the Csörgő-Nagy-Csanád family) to measured charged-particle pseudorapidity density distributions, embedding it into 1+3 dimensions via the Buda-Lund hydro model for transverse momentum dependence. The longitudinal acceleration parameter λ is extracted from fits to ALICE Pb+Pb data at 5.02 TeV and CMS p+p data at 13 TeV, and used to compute both the Bjorken energy density ε_Bj and the conjectured corrected energy density ε_corr for a squared speed of sound c_s² = 0.1 and freeze-out to thermalization time ratio τ_f/τ_0 = 6±2.

## Key numbers
- Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (0-5% centrality): dn/dη|_η=η0 = 1929±46, λ = 1.046±0.013, ε_Bj = 14.85±0.53 GeV/fm³, ε_corr = 17.40±0.61 GeV/fm³
- Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (0-5% centrality): dn/dη|_η=η0 = 1615±39, λ = 1.050±0.005, ε_Bj = 12.50±0.44 GeV/fm³, ε_corr = 15.07±0.81 GeV/fm³
- p+p at √s = 13 TeV: dn/dη|_η=η0 = 6.50±0.02, λ = 1.065±0.013, ε_Bj = 0.56±0.02 GeV/fm³, ε_corr = 0.69±0.02 GeV/fm³
- p+p at √s = 7 TeV: dn/dη|_η=η0 = 5.78±0.01, λ = 1.073±0.001, ε_Bj = 0.51±0.01 GeV/fm³, ε_corr = 0.64±0.01 GeV/fm³
- p+p at √s = 8 TeV: dn/dη|_η=η0 = 5.36±0.02, λ = 1.067±0.001, ε_Bj = 0.52±0.01 GeV/fm³, ε_corr = 0.64±0.01 GeV/fm³

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analyses of pseudorapidity distributions using accelerating hydrodynamics (Csanád et al. 2016, Jiang et al. 2017) from RHIC and lower LHC energies to the highest available LHC collision energies. The corrected energy density estimate ε_corr is compared to the standard Bjorken estimate (Bjorken 1983) and accounts for longitudinal acceleration effects that the Bjorken formula neglects. The results are consistent with longitudinal expansion dynamics of hydrodynamic origin in both proton-proton and heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that ongoing work (Csörgő et al. 2018, Kasza et al. 2018) aims to derive exact results for realistic values of the speed of sound rather than the κ=1 super-hard equation of state used here.
