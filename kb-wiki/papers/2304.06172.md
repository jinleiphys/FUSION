# Four-Body Systems at Large Cutoffs in Effective Field Theory

> Four-body systems are studied using an effective field theory with two- and three-body contact interactions. A method to systematically address deep trimers (three-body bound states that are more tightly bound than four-body bound/resonant states) in four-body calculations is developed using a diagrammatic approach. Previous calculations were limited by the existence of deep trimers, which this work overcomes. For cold $^4$He atoms, binding energies of 526.1(5)~mK and 128.517(1)~mK are obtained at leading order for the tetramer ground and excited states, respectively, where errors come from the truncation of three-body partial waves. Tetramer binding energies and decay widths are also computed approaching the unitary limit. In the unitary limit, there are two tetramers associated with each trimer of binding energy $B_3^{(0)}$. The binding energy and decay width for the associated tetramer ground state are $E_4^{(0)} = 4.60(1)B_3^{(0)}$ and $Γ_4^{(0)}/2 = 0.0160(1)B_3^{(0)}$, respectively, and for the associated tetramer excited state, $E_4^{(1)} = 1.0022(3)B_3^{(0)}$ and $Γ_4^{(1)}/2 = 2.57(2)\times 10^{-4}B_3^{(0)}$, respectively. This calculation is a gateway to higher-order and/or more-body calculations in nuclear and atomic systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to include or subtract deep trimers (three-body bound states more tightly bound than the tetramer) in four-body calculations using effective field theory, overcoming a previous limitation that restricted calculations to cutoffs below a threshold where deep trimers appear. The author demonstrates convergence of tetramer binding energies at cutoffs above this threshold, which is necessary for higher-order calculations where residual cutoff dependence would otherwise dominate the theoretical error.

## Method

The author derives a four-boson integral equation from Feynman diagrams with two- and three-body contact interactions, then rewrites it in terms of three-boson amplitudes using a diagrammatic approach. The three-boson amplitude is separated into a part without three-body forces and a part containing the trimer propagator, allowing the trimer poles to be included or subtracted via the Cauchy principal value prescription. The integral equation is projected onto a partial-wave basis, and the tetramer binding energies are obtained by solving for the dimer-dimer amplitude using Eq. (gamma2_main) after discretizing the momenta with a sharp cutoff.

## Key numbers

- For cold $^4$He atoms at leading order: tetramer ground state binding energy = 526.1(5) mK, tetramer excited state binding energy = 128.517(1) mK, where errors come from truncation of three-body partial waves
- In the unitary limit, for each trimer of binding energy $B_3^{(0)}$: tetramer ground state binding energy $E_4^{(0)} = 4.60(1)B_3^{(0)}$ and decay width $\Gamma_4^{(0)}/2 = 0.0160(1)B_3^{(0)}$
- In the unitary limit: tetramer excited state binding energy $E_4^{(1)} = 1.0022(3)B_3^{(0)}$ and decay width $\Gamma_4^{(1)}/2 = 2.57(2)\times 10^{-4}B_3^{(0)}$

## Context

This work extends the four-boson calculations of Platter et al. (2004), who solved the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation but were limited to cutoffs below a threshold $\Lambda_t$ where deep trimers appear. The method enables calculations at arbitrarily large cutoffs, which is necessary for next-to-leading order calculations where the residual cutoff dependence from low-cutoff calculations (approximately 5% for the tetramer ground state and 2% for the excited state in cold $^4$He atoms) would otherwise dominate the EFT error. This approach is a first step toward higher-order and/or more-body calculations in nuclear and atomic systems, including four-nucleon systems where a similar situation occurs for the alpha-particle binding energy.
