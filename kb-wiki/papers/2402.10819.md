# In-medium static inter-quark potential on high resolution quenched lattices

> We re-investigate the interactions between static color sources in a finite temperature gluonic medium using both high resolution isotropic and anisotropic quenched lattice QCD ensembles. The underlying ill-posed inverse problem, related to the extraction of spectral functions, is attacked with a range of different methods, including Bayesian inference, Padé interpolation and model fits. Among the latter we include a tail amended Gaussian ansatz and a HTL-inspired fit ansatz. We reconfirm the presence of a dominant low-lying spectral feature that supports the existence of a potential picture for the in-medium evolution of the static charges at late real times. Using the raw unmodified lattice data, all applicable methods show clear signs of screening of the real-part of the potential. After applying a subtraction procedure featured in a previous study we find however that screening disappears from the extracted potential. Paths towards the resolution of this puzzle are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper re-investigates the static inter-quark potential in quenched lattice QCD at finite temperature and finds that while all methods show screening of the real-part of the potential when using raw unmodified lattice data, applying a subtraction procedure from a previous study makes screening disappear from the extracted potential, creating a puzzle that the authors discuss but do not resolve.

## Method

The authors extracted spectral functions from Euclidean Wilson loop and Coulomb-gauge Wilson line correlators computed on high-resolution isotropic and anisotropic quenched lattice QCD ensembles. They applied multiple methods to solve the ill-posed inverse problem of spectral reconstruction: Bayesian inference (BR method), Padé interpolation, a tail-amended Gaussian fit ansatz, and a hard-thermal-loop (HTL) inspired fit ansatz. The methods were benchmarked against semi-analytically computed HTL spectral functions at T=2.33Tc using mock data salted with Gaussian noise.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the first-order phase transition in SU(3) gauge theory is Tc=271 MeV
- The HTL mock data used for benchmarking was computed at T=2.33Tc, corresponding to approximately 631 MeV
- The spatial distances investigated in the HTL mock data ranged from r=0.065 fm to r=0.46 fm
- The heavy quark masses referenced are mc≈1.2 GeV and mb≈4.6 GeV
- The maximum temperature estimated in LHC heavy-ion collisions is Tmax_LHC≈0.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior quenched lattice QCD studies by Rothkopf (2011), Burnier (2016), and Bala (2019) that concluded a time-independent complex potential exists and shows screening of the real part above Tc. It directly addresses a discrepancy with more recent full QCD studies by Bala (2021) using HISQ actions, which found indications that the real-part of the potential remains unchanged with temperature. The paper also discusses that staggered actions like HISQ can exhibit an artificial "stripe phase" (S4) at strong coupling, as reported by Kotov (2021) and Cheng (2011), where chiral symmetry is restored while color charges remain confined, which may help explain the unexpected confining potential results on HISQ lattices at temperatures where a chirally restored QGP is expected.
