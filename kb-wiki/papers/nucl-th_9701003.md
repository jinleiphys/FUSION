# Comment on `Measurement of the $π^+ \vec p$ analyzing power at 68.3 MeV'

> We comment on a recent paper by Weiser et al. [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 54}, 1930 (1996)]. The authors have performed a single-energy analysis of $π^+ p$ scattering data at 68.3 MeV, finding a value for the $S_{31}$ phase shift about 1^o smaller than found in the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH) partial-wave analysis. The authors use this result to argue for a dispersion relation analysis using recently measured data, so that their effect on the $πNN$ coupling constant (f^2) and $Σ$ amplitude can be determined. We note that these tasks were accomplished prior to the submission of the above paper. We clarify the effect of this new analyzing power data on f^2 and the $Σ$ amplitude.

# Comment on "Measurement of the π⁺ p̅ analyzing power at 68.3 MeV"

## Key claim

The new single-energy analysis of π⁺p scattering data at 68.3 MeV by Weiser et al. (1996) finds an S₃₁ phase shift about 1° smaller than the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH) partial-wave analysis, but this discrepancy does not require a new dispersion relation analysis because the effect of the new analyzing power data on the πNN coupling constant f² and the Σ amplitude had already been determined prior to the submission of the Weiser et al. paper.

## Method

The authors compare the S₃₁, P₃₁, and P₃₃ phase shifts and χ² values from multiple partial-wave analyses (KH80, KA84, SM90, SM95, and a single-energy solution C6) fitted to the 68.3 MeV analyzing power data of Weiser et al. (1996). They examine the renormalization factors required for two data sets (Set I with 3 points and Set II with 4 points) and evaluate the effect of including systematic errors on the χ² values. The authors also reference the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme (GMO) sum rule relating S-wave scattering lengths to f² and an integral over total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- S₃₁ phase shift from the single-energy analysis C6: -6.08°, compared to -6.96° from KH80 (a difference of about 0.88°)
- χ² per datum for KH80 against the 7 analyzing power data: 21.3/7 when systematic error is neglected; drops by more than a factor of 5 when the ±5% systematic error is included
- χ² per datum for the single-energy solution C6: 4.3/7, with renormalization factors of 0.96 (Set I) and 0.99 (Set II)
- Σ amplitude estimate from Arndt et al.: approximately 68 MeV; Sainio (1995) gave a range of Σ = 60 ± 10 MeV
- f² value from solution SM95: f² = 0.076 ± 0.001, lying within the range f² = 0.076 ± 0.003 quoted by Weiser et al. (1996)

## Context

This comment responds to Weiser et al. (1996), who performed a single-energy analysis of π⁺p scattering at 68.3 MeV and argued for a new dispersion relation analysis to determine the πNN coupling constant and Σ amplitude. The authors note that these tasks were already accomplished by Arndt et al. (1995) using solution SM95, which included the preliminary data of Weiser et al. and applied dispersion relation constraints. The work extends the KH80 analysis of Höhler (1983) and the SM90 energy-dependent analysis of Arndt et al. (1991), and it clarifies that the new analyzing power data do not require a fundamentally new determination of f² or the Σ amplitude.
