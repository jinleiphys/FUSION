# The nature of the Lambda(1405)

> We present here some results supporting the nature of the $螞(1405)$ resonance as dynamically generated from the meson baryon interaction in coupled channels and resulting from the superposition of two close-by poles. We find support for this picture in the $K^- p \to 蟺^0 蟺^0 危^0 $ reaction, which shows a different shape than the one obtained from the $蟺^- p \to K^0 蟺危$ reaction. We also report on recent calculations of the radiative decay of the two $螞(1405)$ states and on reactions to obtain information on these decay modes. ## Key claim

The Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single state but results from the superposition of two close-by poles, one around 1420 MeV (narrow, coupling mostly to Kbar N) and one around 1395 MeV (wide, coupling mostly to pi Sigma), and different reactions produce different observed shapes depending on which pole is more strongly excited.

## Method

The authors use chiral unitary theory to describe the meson-baryon interaction in coupled channels, generating the Lambda(1405) dynamically. They calculate cross sections and invariant mass distributions for several reactions: K- p -> pi0 pi0 Sigma0, K- p -> gamma pi Sigma, radiative decays of the Lambda(1405) to gamma Lambda and gamma Sigma0, and pp -> p K+ Lambda(1405). The calculations are compared with experimental data from the Crystal Ball collaboration (Prakhov, 2004) and the ANKE/COSY collaboration (Zychor, 2007).

## Key numbers

- The two Lambda(1405) poles are located around 1420 MeV (narrow) and around 1395 MeV (wide); the narrow pole has a width of about 30 MeV, narrower than the nominal width of around 50 MeV.
- Radiative decay widths predicted by the chiral unitary approach (UchiPT) for the low-energy pole and high-energy pole respectively: gamma Lambda channel: 16.1 keV and 64.8 keV; gamma Sigma0 channel: 73.5 keV and 33.5 keV.
- The K- p -> gamma pi Sigma reaction shows a peak in the calculated cross section around 1420 MeV with a width of about 30 MeV.
- The pp -> p K+ Lambda(1405) reaction data from ANKE/COSY are compared with theoretical calculations; the theoretical cross section for pi0 Sigma0 production is multiplied by a factor of three to compare with experimental numbers.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the Lambda(1405) resonance, which was first shown to correspond to two poles by Jido (2003). The authors build on prior studies of the K- p -> pi0 pi0 Sigma0 reaction by Magas (2005) and the radiative decay calculations by Geng (2007). The results contradict single-pole models such as the nonrelativistic quark model (Darewych, 1983; Kaxiras, 1985), the Bonn constituent quark model (VanCauteren, 2005), and the MIT bag model (Kaxiras, 1985), which predict different radiative decay widths. The work enables experimental tests of the two-pole structure through reactions that preferentially excite one pole over the other, and provides predictions for future experiments at facilities like COSY.
