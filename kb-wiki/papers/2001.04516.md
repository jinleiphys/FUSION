# Sub-femtometer scale color charge correlations in the proton

> Color charge correlations in the proton at moderately small $x\sim 0.1$ are extracted from its light-cone wave function. The charge fluctuations are far from Gaussian and they exhibit interesting dependence on impact parameter and on the relative transverse momentum (or distance) of the gluon probes. We provide initial conditions for small-$x$ Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution of the dipole scattering amplitude with impact parameter and $\hat r \cdot \hat b$ dependence, and with non-zero $C$-odd component due to three-gluon exchange. Lastly, we compute the (forward) Weizsaecker-Williams gluon distributions, including the distribution of linearly polarized gluons, up to fourth order in $A^+$. The correction due to the quartic correlator provides a transverse momentum scale, $q > 0.5$ GeV, for nearly maximal polarization.

## Key claim

Color charge fluctuations in the proton at moderately small x ~ 0.1, extracted from its light-cone wave function, are far from Gaussian and exhibit dependence on impact parameter and relative transverse momentum of gluon probes, providing initial conditions for small-x Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution that include non-zero C-odd components from three-gluon exchange.

## Method

The authors computed two-point, three-point, and four-point color charge density correlators in the proton by evaluating expectation values of color charge density operators acting on the three-quark Fock state light-front wave function, using the Brodsky-Schlumpf harmonic oscillator model wave function with parameters m = 0.26 GeV and beta = 0.55 GeV. They then related these correlators to the dipole scattering amplitude via two-gluon and three-gluon exchange diagrams, and to the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distributions at fourth order in A+.

## Key numbers

- The two-gluon exchange amplitude T_gg is approximately 0.1 at impact parameter b = 1 GeV^-1 and dipole size r = 2 GeV^-1, corresponding to a saturation momentum Q_s(b) approximately 0.3 GeV at b = 1 GeV^-1 and x ~ 0.1
- The C-odd three-gluon exchange amplitude T_ggg is maximal at impact parameters b ~ 0.5 - 1.2 GeV^-1 and is smaller than T_gg by at least one order of magnitude for b less than or equal to 3 GeV^-1 and r less than or equal to 4 GeV^-1
- For transverse momentum q greater than or equal to 0.5 GeV, the higher twist correction to the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distributions is very small and the polarization is nearly maximal
- The quadratic color charge correlator G_2 integrated over the 2d impact parameter plane at vanishing relative momentum equals zero: integral d^2b G_2(q_12 = 0, b) = 0
- The model wave function parameters are m = 0.26 GeV and beta = 0.55 GeV, with fixed alpha_s = 0.35

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model of Gaussian color charge fluctuations (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) to the proton at moderate x, showing that unlike the MV model for large nuclei, proton color charge fluctuations are non-Gaussian. The results provide realistic initial conditions for Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution that improve upon simplified ad-hoc initial conditions used in fits to HERA data (Albacete et al., 2009; Ducloue et al., 2019). The computed Weizsacker-Williams gluon distributions, including linearly polarized gluons, enable predictions for processes at a future Electron-Ion Collider such as dijet azimuthal asymmetries and exclusive charmonium production.
