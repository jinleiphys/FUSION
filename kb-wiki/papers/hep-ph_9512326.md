# Strangeness in the Nucleon: The Strange Vector Form Factors

> We discuss two descriptions of the nucleon's strange vector form factors in the framework of vector meson dominance. The first, an updated and extended version of Jaffe's dispersion analysis, approximates the spectral functions of the form factors as a sum of vector meson poles, whereas the second combines vector meson dominance in the $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson sector with an intrinsic strangeness distribution from a kaon cloud. (Plenary talk given at the International School for Nuclear Physics, 17th Course: Quarks and Hadrons in Nuclei, Erice, Sicily, Sept 19-27, 1995.)

# Strangeness in the Nucleon: The Strange Vector Form Factors

## Key claim
The strange vector form factors of the nucleon can be estimated using vector meson dominance, but the minimal 3-pole dispersion analysis likely overestimates the strangeness radius and magnetic moment by up to a factor of three, depending on the asymptotic behavior enforced by QCD counting rules. The sign of the strangeness radius may be positive in the pole approximation but becomes negative when explicit kaon cloud contributions are included via generalized vector meson dominance.

## Method
Two approaches based on vector meson dominance are presented. The first is a dispersion analysis that approximates the spectral functions of the strange form factors as a sum of vector meson poles (3-pole, 4-pole, and 6-pole ansatze), using input from fits to isoscalar electromagnetic form factor data and imposing asymptotic constraints from QCD dimensional counting rules. The second approach uses current field identities to couple the strangeness current to the omega and phi meson fields, combined with an intrinsic strangeness distribution from kaon-loop (K-Lambda) diagrams with vertex form factors from the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers
- Updated 3-pole dispersion analysis gives: strangeness radius r_s^2 = 0.22 fm^2, Sachs strangeness radius (r_s^2)_Sachs = 0.20 fm^2, strange magnetic moment mu_s = -0.26 (in nuclear magnetons)
- Jaffe's original 3-pole analysis gave: r_s^2 = (0.16 +/- 0.06) fm^2, (r_s^2)_Sachs = (0.14 +/- 0.07) fm^2, mu_s = -(0.31 +/- 0.09)
- 4-pole ansatz with extrinsic QCD asymptotics and m_4 = 1.9 GeV gives: r_s^2 = 0.15 fm^2, (r_s^2)_Sachs = 0.14 fm^2, mu_s = -0.18
- 6-pole ansatz with intrinsic asymptotics gives: r_s^2 = 0.089 fm^2, (r_s^2)_Sachs = 0.081 fm^2, mu_s = -0.086
- Generalized VMD with kaon cloud gives: r_s^2 = -(0.0243 to 0.0245) fm^2, (r_s^2)_Sachs = -(0.040 to 0.045) fm^2, mu_s = -(0.24 to 0.32)
- The omega-phi mixing angle is epsilon = 0.053 radians
- The third pole mass in the updated fit is m_3 = 1.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends the dispersion analysis of Jaffe (1989) by using updated fits to isoscalar electromagnetic form factor data from Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1995), and by implementing QCD asymptotic constraints through 4-pole and 6-pole ansatze. The generalized VMD approach builds on the kaon loop model of Musolf and Burkardt (1993) and the current field identity formalism of Kroll, Lee, and Zumino (1967). The results provide predictions for upcoming parity-violating electron scattering experiments at CEBAF (including the G0 experiment), MAMI, and the SAMPLE experiment at Bates, which will measure the strange form factors at low momentum transfers and distinguish between the competing theoretical scenarios.
