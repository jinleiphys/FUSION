# Weak decay constant of pseudscalar meson in a QCD-inspired model

> We show that a linear scaling between the weak decay constants of pseudoscalar and the vector mesons masses is supported by the available experimental data. The decay constant scale as $f_m/f_{pi}=M_V/M_ρ$ (f_m is decay constant and M_V vector meson ground state mass). This simple form is justified within a renormalized light-front QCD-inpired model for quark-antiquark bound states.

# Weak decay constant of pseudoscalar mesons in a QCD-inspired model

## Key claim

The paper claims a linear scaling relation between pseudoscalar meson weak decay constants and vector meson ground-state masses, given by \( f_m/f_\pi = M_V/M_\rho \), but this result is derived using only the asymptotic form of the meson wave function, and the authors note that the overestimation of heavier meson decay constants indicates a more refined wave function may be needed.

## Method

The authors solve a renormalized light-front QCD-inspired mass operator equation for quark-antiquark bound states, using the asymptotic (dominant) form of the meson wave function. Constituent quark masses are extracted from vector meson masses (rho, K*, D*, B*), and the pion decay constant \( f_\pi = 92.4 \) MeV is used as input to regulate the logarithmic divergence in the transverse momentum integration. The decay constants for K+, D+, and D_s+ are then computed from the relation \( f_m \propto m_1 + m_2 \), which equals the vector meson mass in the model.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses extracted from vector meson masses: \( m_u = 384 \) MeV (from \( M_\rho = 768 \) MeV), \( m_s = 508 \) MeV (from \( M_{K^*} = 892 \) MeV), \( m_c = 1623 \) MeV (from \( M_{D^*} = 2007 \) MeV), \( m_b = 4941 \) MeV (from \( M_{B^*} = 5325 \) MeV)
- Input pion decay constant: \( f_\pi = 92.4 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.25 \) MeV
- Model prediction for \( f_K \): 107 MeV (experimental: \( 113.0 \pm 1.0 \pm 0.31 \) MeV)
- Model prediction for \( f_D \): 241 MeV (experimental: \( 212^{+127+6}_{-106-28} \) MeV)
- Model prediction for \( f_{D_s} \): 253 MeV (experimental: \( 201 \pm 13 \pm 28 \) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the renormalized light-front QCD-inspired model developed by the same group (Frederico et al., 2002) for meson binding energies to the calculation of weak decay constants. The linear scaling relation contrasts with the heavy-quark limit prediction from Dyson-Schwinger equations (Maris and Roberts, 1997) that decay constants should scale as \( 1/\sqrt{M_m} \). The results provide a simple phenomenological guide for lattice QCD studies of heavy-light meson decay constants, though the authors note that a more sophisticated wave function including confinement (Frederico et al., 2002) is needed to improve the overestimated values for heavier mesons.
