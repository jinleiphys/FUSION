# Global nuclear structure effects of tensor interaction

> A direct fit of the isoscalar spin-orbit (SO) and both isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants to the f5/2-f7/2 SO splittings in 40Ca, 56Ni, and 48Ca nuclei requires a drastic reduction of the isoscalar SO strength and strong attractive tensor coupling constants. The aim of this work is to address further consequences of these strong attractive tensor and weak SO fields on binding energies, nuclear deformability, and high-spin states. In particular, we show that contribution to the nuclear binding energy due to the tensor field shows generic magic structure with tensorial magic numbers at N(Z)=14, 32, 56, or 90 corresponding to the maximum spin-asymmetries in 1d5/2, 1f7/2-2p3/2, 1g9/2-2d5/2 and 1h11/2-2f7/2 single-particle configurations and that these numbers are smeared out by pairing correlations and deformation effects. We also examine the consequences of strong attractive tensor fields and weak SO interaction on nuclear stability at the drip lines, in particular close to the tensorial doubly magic nuclei and discuss the possibility of an entirely new tensor-force driven deformation effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a direct fit of the isoscalar spin-orbit and both isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants to the f5/2-f7/2 spin-orbit splittings in 40Ca, 56Ni, and 48Ca requires a drastic reduction of the isoscalar spin-orbit strength and strong attractive tensor coupling constants, but the authors show that with modest readjustments of other coupling constants the functional can still reproduce binding energies of spherical doubly magic nuclei reasonably well.

## Method

The authors performed spherical and deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations using Skyrme-energy-density-functional parameterizations (SkP, SLy4, SkO) modified by a three-step fitting procedure: first adjusting the isoscalar spin-orbit coupling constant in spin-saturated 40Ca, then the isoscalar tensor coupling constant in spin-unsaturated isoscalar 56Ni, and finally the isovector tensor coupling constant in spin-unsaturated isovector 48Ca. They used the HFBRAD code for spherical calculations and the HFODD code for deformed calculations, employing a volume-delta interaction in the particle-particle channel and testing both gauge-invariant and gauge-symmetry-violating treatments of the time-odd tensor coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar spin-orbit coupling constant from the fit is approximately C0^(nabla J) ≈ -60 ± 10 MeV fm^5
- The isoscalar tensor coupling constant from the fit is approximately C0^J ≈ -40 ± 10 MeV fm^5
- The reduction of the spin-orbit strength varies from 20% for large effective mass forces to 35% for low effective mass (m* ≈ 0.7) forces
- The SkO_T' functional reproduces binding energies with standard deviation σ(SkO_T') ≈ 1.663 MeV, compared to σ(SLy4) ≈ 1.879 MeV
- After further readjustment of central fields, the SkO_T'' functional achieves σ(SkO_T'') ≈ 1.475 MeV
- The tensorial magic numbers are identified at N(Z) = 14, 32, 56, and 90

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fitting strategy (Zalewski 2008) that demonstrated the need for strong tensor and weak spin-orbit fields based on single-particle energy fits, and addresses the conflict with large-scale binding energy fits such as those by Lesinski (2007) that favored weak tensor and stronger spin-orbit terms. The paper shows that the tensor contribution to binding energy exhibits a generic magic structure with tensorial magic numbers shifted relative to classic magic numbers, and that these effects may be observable in neutron-rich regions near Z≈14, N≈32; Z≈32, N≈56; and Z≈56, N≈90, potentially enabling studies of new tensor-force-driven deformation effects and nuclear stability at the drip lines.
