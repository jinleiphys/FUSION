# Holographic two dimensional QCD and Chern-Simons term

> We present a holographic realization of large Nc massless QCD in two dimensions using a D2/D8 brane construction. The flavor axial anomaly is dual to a three dimensional Chern-Simons term which turns out to be of leading order, and it affects the meson spectrum and holographic renormalization in crucial ways. The massless flavor bosons that exist in the spectrum are found to decouple from the heavier mesons, in agreement with the general lore of non-Abelian bosonization. We also show that an external dynamical photon acquires a mass through the three dimensional Chern-Simons term as expected from the Schwinger mechanism. Massless two dimensional QCD at large Nc exhibits anti-vector-meson dominance due to the axial anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper presents a holographic realization of large Nc massless QCD in two dimensions using a D2/D8 brane construction, where the flavor axial anomaly is dual to a three-dimensional Chern-Simons term that is of leading order (not subleading as in higher-dimensional holographic QCD), and this Chern-Simons term fundamentally alters the meson spectrum and holographic renormalization.

## Method

The authors construct a top-down holographic model using Nc D2 branes compactified on a circle with anti-periodic boundary conditions for fermions to realize two-dimensional Yang-Mills theory at low energy, then introduce probe D8 and anti-D8 branes to incorporate fundamental massless fermions. They work in the axial gauge A_z=0, decompose the gauge field into chiral components using projection operators P^(±)_μν, and analyze the equations of motion for the bulk gauge field to determine the near-boundary asymptotics and the discrete meson spectrum. The holographic renormalization is carried out by introducing boundary counterterms to cancel divergences arising from the Maxwell-Chern-Simons action.

## Key numbers

- The meson mass spectrum is computed as m²/M_KK² = 5.515 (+), 9.623 (-), 14.687 (+), 20.705 (-), ... where (±) denotes even or odd under w→-w, with the lowest mode being a vector and the next a pseudovector
- The Chern-Simons coefficient in the action is N_c/(8π), appearing at leading order with no relative suppression by λ = g²_YM N_c or N_c
- The boundary counterterm coefficient C₁ = -1 is fixed by the e^(2w) divergence, independent of the metric function g(w)
- The D2 brane compactification scale M_KK sets the scale for the two-dimensional QCD coupling via g²_YM = g_s M_KK/(2π l_s)

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) to two dimensions, correcting earlier attempts by Gao (2006) and Rodriguez (2005) that overlooked the crucial role of the three-dimensional Chern-Simons term in the massless quark case. The results agree qualitatively with the non-Abelian bosonization picture of Witten (1983), where massless flavor bosons decouple from heavier mesons, and the analysis enables future study of the Schwinger mechanism for photon mass generation and the role of temperature and density on lower-dimensional hadronic spectra at large Nc.
