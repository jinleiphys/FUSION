# Three-Body Bound State Calculations by Using Three-Dimensional Low Momentum Interaction $V_{low-k}$

> Three-dimensional (3D) Faddeev integral equations are solved for three-body (3B) bound state problem without using the partial wave (PW) form of low momentum two-body (2B) interaction $V_{low-k}$ which is constructed from spin independent Malfliet-Tjon V (MT-V) potential. The dependence of 3B binding energy on the cutoff momentum of $V_{low-k}$ is investigated for a wide range of $Λ$ from $1.0$ to $7.0\, fm^{-1}$. The properties of Faddeev components and 3B wave function are displayed and the effect of number of grid points for momentum and angle variables on the accuracy and the stability of numerical results is studied by calculation of the expectation value of total Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The three-body binding energy calculated from the three-dimensional low-momentum interaction \(V_{low-k}\) is strongly cutoff-dependent for small cutoff values (below about \(2.0\,fm^{-1}\)), but becomes nearly cutoff-independent for \(\Lambda\) above \(5.0\,fm^{-1}\), converging to the bare potential result of \(-7.74\) MeV; the approximately \(1.6\) MeV variation across the full cutoff range provides an estimate of the omitted three-body force contribution.

## Method

The author solved the three-dimensional Faddeev integral equations for the three-boson bound state using a spin-independent low-momentum interaction \(V_{low-k}\) constructed from the Malfliet-Tjon V (MT-V) potential via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, without employing a partial-wave expansion. The integral equations were discretized using Gauss-Legendre quadrature with up to 40 mesh points for momentum and angle variables, and solved by a Lanczos-type iterative technique with cubic-Hermitian spline interpolation. The expectation value of the total Hamiltonian was computed to verify the numerical accuracy of the binding energy results.

## Key numbers

* Three-body binding energy for bare MT-V potential: \(-7.738\) MeV.
* Three-body binding energy for \(V_{low-k}\) at \(\Lambda = 1.0\,fm^{-1}\): \(-6.146\) MeV.
* Three-body binding energy for \(V_{low-k}\) at \(\Lambda = 2.0\,fm^{-1}\): \(-7.788\) MeV.
* Three-body binding energy for \(V_{low-k}\) at \(\Lambda = 7.0\,fm^{-1}\): \(-7.738\) MeV.
* Variation of three-body binding energy across \(\Lambda\) from \(1.0\) to \(7.0\,fm^{-1}\): approximately \(1.6\) MeV.
* For \(\Lambda = 2.1\,fm^{-1}\) with 40 mesh points: expectation value of kinetic energy \(\langle H_0 \rangle = 24.788\) MeV, potential energy \(\langle V \rangle = -32.589\) MeV, total Hamiltonian \(\langle H \rangle = -7.801\) MeV, compared to eigenvalue \(E_3 = -7.792\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional formulation of the low-momentum interaction \(V_{low-k}\) (previously developed for two-body systems by Bayegan et al. (2009) and Bayegan et al. (2010)) to three-body bound state calculations, avoiding the angular momentum algebra required in partial-wave-based approaches. The cutoff dependence of the three-body binding energy is consistent with prior partial-wave calculations by Fujii et al. (2004) and Nogga et al. (2005). The results demonstrate that the three-dimensional \(V_{low-k}\) is suitable for few-body calculations and suggest that incorporating three-body forces in this framework will be less cumbersome than in partial-wave representations.
