# Dominance of Electric Fields in the Charge Splitting of Elliptic Flow

> In this study, we investigate the impact of electromagnetic fields, highlighting the dominant effect of electric fields on the splitting of elliptic flow, \( Δv_2 \) with transverse momentum ($p_T$). The velocity and temperature profiles of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is described through thermal model calculations. The electromagnetic field evolution is however determined from the solutions of Maxwell's equations, assuming constant electric and chiral conductivities. We find that the slower decay of the electric fields compared to the magnetic fields makes its impact on the splitting of the elliptic flow more dominant. We further estimated that the maximum value of \( |\langle eF \rangle| \), evaluated by averaging the field values over all spatial points on the hypersurface and across all field components, is approximately \( (0.010003 \pm 0.000195) \, m_π^2 \) for \( \sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 7.7 \, \text{GeV} \), which could describe the splitting of elliptic flow data within the current experimental uncertainty reasonably well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that electric fields, rather than magnetic fields, dominate the charge-dependent splitting of elliptic flow (\(\Delta v_2\)) in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, because electric fields decay more slowly than magnetic fields in the conducting quark-gluon plasma medium. This conclusion is conditional on assuming constant electric and chiral conductivities and a perturbative treatment where the drift velocity from electromagnetic fields is much smaller than the fluid velocity.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma fluid velocity and temperature using a blastwave parametrization with boost invariance, fitting parameters to reproduce STAR data for pion elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 27\) GeV in Au+Au collisions. Electromagnetic fields are computed from Maxwell's equations with constant electrical conductivity \(\sigma = 0.023\) fm\(^{-1}\) and chiral conductivity \(\sigma_{\chi} = 0.004\) fm\(^{-1}\), using Monte Carlo sampling of proton positions in the colliding nuclei via a Woods-Saxon distribution. The drift velocity from electromagnetic fields is calculated in the fluid rest frame, added perturbatively to the fluid velocity using relativistic velocity addition, and the Cooper-Frye formalism is then used to compute the elliptic flow splitting \(\Delta v_2 = v_2^{\pi^-} - v_2^{\pi^+}\) as a function of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of \(|\langle eF \rangle|\), averaged over all spatial points on the hypersurface and across all field components, is approximately \((0.010003 \pm 0.000195) \, m_{\pi}^2\) for \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 7.7\) GeV.
- The electrical conductivity used is \(\sigma = 0.023\) fm\(^{-1}\) and the chiral conductivity is \(\sigma_{\chi} = 0.004\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The freeze-out temperature extracted from fitting STAR data is \(T = 0.0785\) GeV, with freeze-out radius \(R_f = 9.0\) fm, maximum fluid four-velocity \(u_0 = 0.65\), proper time \(\tau = 4\) fm, and spatial eccentricity \(c_2 = 0.04\).
- The drift velocity magnitude relative to fluid velocity is of order \(\sim 0.001\) or less across the hypersurface.
- The effects of transverse magnetic fields on \(\Delta v_2\) are of order \(10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Gursoy (2014) and others, who developed the drift velocity approach for incorporating field effects into flow calculations. The study focuses on the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC and the upcoming Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) at GSI, where lower collision energies allow electromagnetic fields to persist longer. The results enable quantitative comparison with STAR experimental data on charge-dependent elliptic flow and provide a framework for disentangling electromagnetic field effects from other phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect.
