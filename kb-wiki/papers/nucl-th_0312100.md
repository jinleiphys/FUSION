# Quark Coalescence for Charmed Mesons in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We investigate effects of charm-quark interactions in a Quark-Gluon Plasma on the production of $D$ and $J/ψ$ mesons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Employing a previously constructed coalescence model that successfully reproduces the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra and elliptic flow ($v_2(p_T)$) of light hadrons at RHIC from underlying light-quark distributions at the phase transition temperature $T_c$, $D$-meson and $J/ψ$ $p_T$ spectra are evaluated. For the charm-quark distributions, we consider two limiting scenarios: (i) {\em no} rescattering, corresponding to perturbative QCD spectra and (ii) {\em complete} thermalization including transverse expansion. With the $D$-meson spectra acquiring a minimal $v_2$ inherited from their light-quark content, the corresponding semileptonic decay spectra of single electrons are found to practically preserve the $v_2$ of the parent particles, exhibiting marked differences between the pQCD and thermal scenarios for $p_T\ge 1$ GeV. Likewise, the $p_T$-spectra and yields of $J/ψ$'s differ appreciably in the two scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the degree of charm-quark thermalization in the Quark-Gluon Plasma can be inferred from the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of charmed mesons, but the results show that while J/ψ spectra and yields differ appreciably between the two limiting scenarios (no rescattering vs. complete thermalization), D-meson spectra alone cannot clearly distinguish them within current experimental accuracy.

## Method

The authors employed a previously constructed coalescence model in the Wigner formalism, using light-quark distributions at the phase transition temperature Tc that had successfully reproduced light-hadron spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC. For charm quarks, they considered two limiting scenarios: (i) perturbative QCD spectra from PYTHIA representing no rescattering, and (ii) thermal spectra at T=170 MeV with radial flow (βmax=0.5 or 0.65). The coalescence probability was evaluated using Gaussian Wigner functions for the meson wave functions, with J/ψ, χc, and ψ′ radii taken from Cornell-type potential models.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark mass: mc = 1.4 GeV
- Total number of c-cbar pairs: Nc-cbar = 2.5 per unit rapidity in central Au+Au at √s=200 AGeV
- Fireball parameters at hadronization: τ = 4.3 fm, Tc = 170 MeV, Vc = 900 fm³, R = 8.1 fm
- J/ψ rms radius: 0.47 fm; χc: 0.74 fm; ψ′: 0.96 fm
- Thermal J/ψ yield: 2.6×10⁻³; PYTHIA J/ψ yield: 0.8×10⁻³ (factor of ~3 difference)
- J/ψ slope parameters: Tslope = 720 MeV (pQCD), 350 MeV (thermal with βmax=0.5), 550 MeV (thermal with βmax=0.65)
- Average ⟨pT⟩ for J/ψ: 1.95 GeV (pQCD), 1.29-1.60 GeV (thermal)
- Ds/D ratios: 29% (thermal c-quarks), 25% (pQCD c-quarks)
- D-meson elliptic flow differs by about a factor of 2 between scenarios for pT ≥ 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model previously applied to light hadrons by Greco, Ko, and Rapp (2003) to the charm sector, addressing the open question of whether charm quarks thermalize in the QGP. It contrasts with statistical model approaches (e.g., Thews et al., 2003) that assume complete charm thermalization, finding that J/ψ yields from pQCD charm distributions are about a factor of 3 smaller than thermal predictions, differing from the ~50% deviation reported by Thews et al. (2003). The study enables future experimental discrimination of charm-quark rescattering scenarios through combined measurements of J/ψ spectra and D-meson elliptic flow, particularly via semileptonic decay electrons which preserve the parent v2.
