# Two pion electroproduction

> We have extended a model for the $γN\to ππN$ reaction to virtual photons and selected the diagrams which have a $Δ$ in the final state. The agreement found with the $γ_v p\toΔ^0π^+$ and $γ_v p\toΔ^{++}π^-$ reactions is good. The sensitivity of the results to $NΔ$ transition form factors is also studied. The present reaction, selecting a particular final state, is an extra test for models of the $γ_{v} N\to ππN$ amplitude.

# Two pion electroproduction

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously developed model for the real-photon reaction γN → ππN to virtual photons, selecting only those diagrams that produce a Δ resonance in the final state. The model achieves good agreement with measured cross sections for γ_v p → Δ⁰π⁺ and γ_v p → Δ⁺⁺π⁻, but the results depend on the choice of form factors and the gauge-invariance prescription used.

## Method

The model takes the diagrammatic approach of the earlier real-photon work (Oset et al., 1999) and selects the subset of 20 Feynman diagrams that contain a Δπ final state. Three new ingredients are added for virtual photons: the zeroth component of the photon coupling to resonances (not needed in Coulomb gauge for real photons), the q² dependence of the amplitudes, and explicit terms linked to S₁/₂ helicity amplitudes that vanish for real photons. Gauge invariance is enforced by setting four electromagnetic form factors equal (F₁^p, F₁^Δ, F_γππ, and F_c), though alternative prescriptions such as the Berends approach are also tested.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections for γ_v p → Δ⁺⁺π⁻ and γ_v p → Δ⁰π⁺ are shown as a function of W (γ_v p center-of-mass energy) for different Q² values.
- The cross section increases by about 10% when changing the monopole parameter λ_π² from 0.5 GeV² to 0.6 GeV².
- Calculations are performed at Q² = 0.6 GeV² for the γ_v p → π⁻Δ⁺⁺ channel.
- A test with λ_c² = 0.8 GeV² for the F_c form factor (in the dominant Kroll-Ruderman term) shows sensitivity of the results to this parameter.

## Context

This work extends the real-photon model of Oset et al. (1999) for γp → π⁺π⁻p, which contained 67 Feynman diagrams and reproduced cross sections up to E_γ ≈ 1 GeV, and the reduced 20-diagram version valid up to 800 MeV used in Mainz experiments. The extension to virtual photons complements existing two-pion electroproduction experiments selecting Δ final states (data from references cited as [6] and [11]), and provides a test bed for models of the γ_v N → ππN amplitude. The results enable future comparisons with data from Thomas Jefferson Laboratory experiments on both NΔ and Nππ production, and may help settle differences between theoretical models by exploiting the q² dependence of resonance couplings.
