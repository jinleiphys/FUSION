# Theta+ formation in inclusive gamma D -> pK- X

> We analyze the possibility to produce an intermediate Theta^+ via a KN->Θ+ formation process in gamma D-> pK^- X (X=nK^+,pK^0) reactions at some specific kinematical conditions, in which a pK^- pair is knocked out in the forward direction and its invariant mass is close to the mass of Lambda(1520). The Θ^+ signal may appear in the [γD,pK^-] missing mass distribution. The ratio of the signal (cross section at the Θ^+ peak position) to the smooth background processes varies from 0.7 to 2.5 depending on the spin and parity of Theta+, and it decreases correspondingly if the pK^- invariant mass is outside of the Lambda(1520)-resonance region. We analyze the recent CLAS search for the Theta+ in the gamma D ->pK^-nK^+ reaction and show that the conditions of this experiment greatly reduce the Theta+ formation process making it difficult to extract a Theta+ peak from the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Theta+ pentaquark signal may be observable in the inclusive gamma D -> pK- X reaction through a KN -> Theta+ formation process, provided specific kinematic conditions are met (forward pK- pair with invariant mass near Lambda(1520)), but the CLAS experiment's cuts on neutron momentum, Lambda(1520) mass, and kaon angles greatly suppress this formation process, making signal extraction difficult.

## Method

The authors model the reaction gamma D -> pK- X (where X = nK+ or pK0) using a tree-level diagram approach with charged and neutral kaon exchange, where the elementary gamma N -> Lambda(1520) K amplitude is described by effective Lagrangians at low energies (E_gamma below 2.3 GeV) and Reggeized K* exchange at higher energies. The Theta+ formation proceeds via on-shell KN -> Theta+ fusion, with the deuteron wave function from the Paris potential used to describe the internal nucleon momentum distribution. The loop integrals are evaluated using Cutkovsky cutting rules to extract the pole contributions, and the non-resonant background includes Lambda(1520) excitation, vector meson decay (phi meson), and a constant matrix element parameterization for other sources.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of Theta+ signal (cross section at the Theta+ peak position) to smooth background processes varies from 0.7 to 2.5 depending on the spin and parity of Theta+
- The Theta+ decay width is taken as Gamma_Theta = 1 MeV as an input parameter
- The pK- invariant mass window for the resonance region is M_Y = M_Lambda(1520) +/- 20 MeV
- The total cross section for gamma p -> Lambda(1520) K+ at E_gamma = 1.8-2.3 GeV varies from 0.59 to 1.14 microbarn, compared with 0.27 to 1.08 microbarn for gamma n -> Lambda(1520) K0
- The CLAS experiment had a neutron momentum cut of p_n > 0.2 GeV/c, kaon momentum cuts p_K > 0.25 GeV/c, and angular cuts theta_+ > 9 degrees and theta_- > 15 degrees
- The integral over the Breit-Wigner Lambda(1520) -> pK- decay distribution gives N approximately 0.17 for the 20 MeV window
- The branching ratio for Lambda(1520) -> pK- is B_pK- approximately 0.45/2
- The constant matrix element for the non-resonant background is parameterized as |T_BG_Y| = T_0 = 5.95 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Titov et al., 2005) on associated Lambda(1520) Theta+ photoproduction in gamma D interactions, proposing an inclusive measurement to avoid difficulties in detecting the slowly moving spectator nucleon. The paper directly addresses the null results from the CLAS collaboration (JLab-06, DeVita) which searched for Theta+ in gamma D -> pK- nK+ and gamma p -> anti-K0 n K+ reactions, showing that CLAS experimental conditions suppress the KN -> Theta+ formation process. The analysis enables future experiments, such as those by the LEPS collaboration at SPring-8, to optimize kinematic cuts for Theta+ searches by focusing on forward pK- pairs with invariant mass near Lambda(1520) and using missing mass distributions rather than exclusive final states.
