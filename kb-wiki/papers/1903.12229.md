# QED radiative corrections for Polarized Lepton-Proton Scattering

> We discuss QED radiative corrections at first and second order for the parity violating asymmetry $A_\text{PV}$ in elastic electron proton scattering. A measurement of $A_\text{PV}$ is planned at low energies and with high precision by the forthcoming P2 experiment at the new MESA facility in Mainz. Bremsstrahlung leads to a shift of the momentum transfer $Q^2$ which affects the determination of the weak charge of the proton.

## Key claim

QED radiative corrections at first order shift the momentum transfer \(Q^2\) by approximately 5% for the kinematics of the P2 experiment, and this shift directly affects the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) because \(A_{PV}\) is proportional to \(Q^2\); second-order corrections contribute only at the expected level of an additional factor \(\alpha/\pi\) and are negligibly small.

## Method

The authors calculated the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) for elastic electron-proton scattering including QED corrections at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha^2)\). They separated the cross sections into non-radiative parts (containing loop corrections and soft-photon bremsstrahlung) and hard-photon radiative parts, using a soft-photon approximation with a cut-off parameter \(\Delta\) and performing numerical phase-space integration for hard photons. The average shift of the momentum transfer \(\langle \Delta Q^2 \rangle\) was computed from the differential cross section for one hard photon, and the \(\Delta\)-independence of the total cross section was verified as a consistency check.

## Key numbers

- The average relative shift of the momentum transfer due to hard photon radiation is approximately **5%** for P2 kinematics (beam energy \(E = 155\) MeV, scattering angle range \(25^\circ \leq \theta_l \leq 45^\circ\), minimum scattered electron energy \(E_{\rm min}^\prime = 45\) MeV).
- The P2 experiment plans to measure the weak mixing angle with a precision of **0.15%**.
- The beam energy for the Mainz P2 experiment is **155 MeV**.
- The scattering angle acceptance used for the asymmetry calculation is centered at \(35^\circ\) with a variable half-width \(\Delta\theta_l\).
- The minimum energy cut for the scattered electron used in the cross-section test is **45 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends previous first-order QED calculations for the parity-violating asymmetry by Barkanova (2002) and Aleksejevs (2007) to include second-order corrections, and it is directly motivated by the upcoming P2 experiment at the MESA facility in Mainz. The authors verified their first-order unpolarized cross-section calculation against results from Gramolin (2014) and Vanderhaeghen (2000). The results enable a more precise extraction of the proton's weak charge \(Q_W^p\) from the measured asymmetry, though the authors note that box graph contributions must still be included to reach the required precision.
