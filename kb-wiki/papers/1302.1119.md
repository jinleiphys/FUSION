# Photon Signals from Quarkyonic Matter

> We calculate the Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum emitted from dynamically evolving quarkyonic matter, and compare this spectrum with that of a high chemical potential quark-gluon plasma as well as to a hadron gas. We find that the transverse momentum distribution and the harmonic coefficient is markedly different in the three cases. The transverse momentum distribution of quarkyonic matter can be fit with an exponential, but is markedly steeper than the distribution expected for the quark-gluon plasma or a hadron gas, even at the lower temperatures expected in the critical point region. The quarkyonic elliptic flow coefficient fluctuates randomly from event to event, and within the same event at different transverse momenta. The latter effect, which can be explained by the shape of quark wavefunctions within quarkyonic matter, might be considered as a quarkyonic matter signature, provided initial temperature is low enough that the quarkyonic regime dominates over deconfinement effects, and the reaction-plane flow can be separated from the fluctuating component.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum distribution of photons from quarkyonic matter is markedly steeper than that from a quark-gluon plasma or hadron gas, and the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) fluctuates randomly event-by-event and within the same event at different transverse momenta; however, these signatures are conditional on the initial temperature being low enough that the quarkyonic regime dominates over deconfinement effects and on the ability to separate reaction-plane flow from the fluctuating component.

## Method

The authors calculate the Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum from dynamically evolving quarkyonic matter by convolving a pQCD quark-quark scattering matrix element with a quark wavefunction that is a sum of Gaussian wavepackets centered on baryon positions extracted from UrQMD simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7.7\) GeV. The photon rate is integrated over momentum space with a lower cutoff at \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) to incorporate confinement, and the results are averaged over UrQMD events. For comparison, they also compute the spectrum for a thermalized quark-gluon plasma using boosted-thermal quark distributions and for a purely hadronic UrQMD model with photon production from meson scatterings.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7.7\) GeV (within the FAIR energy range) are simulated.
- The quark wavefunction width in configuration space is \(\sim \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^{-1}\), the confinement scale.
- The baryochemical potential regime considered is \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} < \mu_Q < \sqrt{N_c/N_f}\,\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\), with \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} = 250\) MeV and \(N_c=3\), \(N_f=2,3\).
- The quarkyonic photon \(p_T\) distribution is fit with an exponential that is steeper than thermal curves; reproducing it with a thermal curve requires an unrealistically low mean temperature well below deconfinement (e.g., \(T \sim 30\) MeV for the boosted quarkyonic scenario).
- The quarkyonic \(v_2\) is compatible with zero overall but shows strong random variations event-by-event and across \(p_T\) bins, with the spread approximately constant with centrality.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal of quarkyonic matter (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007) by developing a phenomenological signature based on electromagnetic probes, specifically direct photons. It contrasts the photon spectrum and elliptic flow from quarkyonic matter with those from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma and a hadron gas modeled by UrQMD (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999). The results suggest that a steep photon \(p_T\) spectrum and a fluctuating, zero-average \(v_2\) could serve as experimental signatures for the quarkyonic phase in low-energy heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as FAIR, provided the fluctuating component can be isolated from reaction-plane flow.
