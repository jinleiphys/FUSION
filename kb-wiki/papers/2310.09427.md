# Bounds on the Equation of State from QCD Inequalities and Lattice QCD

> We derive robust bounds on the equation of state (EoS) at finite baryon chemical potential using QCD inequalities and input from recent lattice-QCD calculations of thermodynamic properties of matter at nonzero isospin chemical potential. We use lattice data to deduce an upper bound on the baryon density of the symmetric nuclear matter at a given baryon chemical potential and a lower bound on the pressure as a function of the energy density. We also use constraints from perturbative calculations of the QCD EoS at high density derived in earlier work and causality to delineate robust bounds on the EoS of isospin symmetric matter at densities relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive robust bounds on the equation of state (EoS) of isospin-symmetric baryonic matter at finite baryon chemical potential and low temperature using QCD inequalities that relate the partition functions at nonzero baryon and isospin chemical potentials, combined with recent lattice-QCD data at nonzero isospin chemical potential; the bounds are conditional on the validity of the QCD inequality and the lattice data at nearly vanishing temperature (T ~ 17-23 MeV).

## Method

The authors use the QCD inequality P_B(μ_B) ≤ P_I(μ_I = 2μ_B/N_c) derived by Cohen (2003, 2004), which relates the pressure of baryonic matter at baryon chemical potential μ_B to the pressure of isospin matter at isospin chemical potential μ_I. They input lattice-QCD results for the isospin matter pressure from Abbott et al. (2023) at nearly zero temperature to obtain an upper bound on the pressure of symmetric baryonic matter as a function of μ_B. Using the integral constraint method developed by Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021), they translate this bound into constraints on the baryon density n_B(μ_B) and the pressure as a function of energy density P(ε), employing the empirical nuclear saturation point (μ_sat = 923 MeV, n_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}, P=0) as a low-density reference and perturbative QCD calculations at μ_H = 3000 MeV as a high-density reference.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter is n_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}, with corresponding baryon chemical potential μ_sat = 923 MeV and energy density ε_sat = 150 MeV/fm^3.
- The lattice-QCD data from Abbott et al. (2023) cover isospin chemical potentials corresponding to baryon chemical potentials up to μ_B ≈ 3500 MeV, at temperatures T ~ 23 MeV (ensemble A) and T ~ 17 MeV (ensemble B).
- The high-density perturbative QCD reference point at μ_H = 3000 MeV gives n_H = 43.86^{+1.86}_{-2.47} n_sat and P_H = 4982^{+353}_{-882} MeV/fm^3.
- The bound excludes EoS with constant sound speed v_s^2 ≲ 0.2 for μ_B in the range 1-2 GeV.
- The relation μ_* ≈ (3/2) μ_B holds empirically, meaning the lattice constraint at μ_* ≈ 1500 MeV constrains the baryon density around the saturation point.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cohen (2003, 2004) who derived the QCD inequality relating baryonic and isospin matter pressures, and applies it using recent lattice-QCD data from Abbott et al. (2023) to obtain the first lattice-based bounds on the EoS at nonzero baryon chemical potential and nearly zero temperature. The results are complementary to astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations (e.g., GW170817 tidal deformability bounds from Annala et al. 2017, Landry et al. 2020) and to the thermodynamic consistency bounds from perturbative QCD extrapolations developed by Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021). The bounds are relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and QCD critical point searches, and can be combined with chiral effective field theory constraints at low density (Drischler et al. 2021) and perturbative QCD at high density (Ghiglieri et al. 2020) to narrow the allowed region of the symmetric nuclear matter EoS.
