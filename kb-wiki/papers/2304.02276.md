# Exact spin polarization of massive and massless particles in relativistic fluids at global equilibrium

> We present the exact form of the spin polarization vector and the spin density matrix of massive and massless free particles of any spin and helicity at general global equilibrium in a relativistic fluid with non-vanishing thermal vorticity, thus extending the known expression at the linear order. The exact form is obtained by means of the analytic continuation of the relativistic density operator to imaginary thermal vorticity and the resummation of the obtained series. The phenomenological implications for the polarization of the $Λ$ hyperon in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper derives the exact, non-perturbative form of the spin polarization vector and spin density matrix for both massive and massless free particles of any spin at global equilibrium with non-vanishing thermal vorticity, extending the previously known linear-order expression. The exact result is obtained through analytic continuation of the relativistic density operator to imaginary thermal vorticity and resummation of the resulting series, with the condition that the Lorentz transformation \(\Lambda\) belongs to the little group of the momentum \(p\) (i.e., \(\Lambda p = p\)).

## Method

The authors start from the density operator for global equilibrium containing a constant thermal vorticity tensor \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\) and a constant four-vector \(b^\mu\). They analytically continue the density operator by replacing \(\varpi \mapsto -i\phi\), compute expectation values of creation and annihilation operators as a series over quantum statistics, and then analytically continue back to real thermal vorticity. For massive Dirac fermions, the spin polarization vector is obtained using the covariant Wigner function formalism; for massless particles, a separate formula is derived using a light-like reference vector \(q^\mu\). The traces of gamma matrices with the exponential of the spin generator are evaluated explicitly, and the series are summed to closed-form expressions involving hyperbolic functions.

## Key numbers

* For massive Dirac fermions, the exact spin polarization vector is \(S^\mu(p) = \frac{1}{2} \hat{\theta}^\mu \frac{\sinh(\sqrt{-\theta^2}/2)}{\cosh(\sqrt{-\theta^2}/2) + e^{-b\cdot p + \zeta}}\), where \(\theta^\mu = -\frac{1}{2m}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\varpi_{\nu\rho}p_\sigma\) and \(\hat{\theta}^\mu = \theta^\mu/\sqrt{-\theta^2}\).
* The linear-order approximation (Becattini 2013) is recovered when \(\sqrt{-\theta^2} \ll 1\), giving \(S^\mu(p) \approx -\frac{1}{8m}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\varpi_{\nu\rho}p_\sigma \tanh[(b\cdot p - \zeta)/2]\).
* The trace identities yield \(\tr(\exp[-in\phi:\Sigma/2]) = 4\cos\left(\frac{n}{2}\sqrt{\phi:\phi/2}\right)\) and \(\tr(\gamma^\nu \gamma^\mu \gamma_5 \exp[-in\phi:\Sigma/2]) = 4i\tilde{\phi}^{\mu\nu} \frac{\sin(n\sqrt{\phi:\phi/2}/2)}{\sqrt{\phi:\phi/2}}\), with \(\phi:\phi = -2\xi^2\) and \(\xi^\rho = -\frac{1}{2m}\epsilon^{\rho\mu\nu\sigma}\phi_{\mu\nu}p_\sigma\).

## Context

This work extends the linear-order spin polarization formula derived by Becattini (2013) for spin-1/2 Dirac fermions to all orders in thermal vorticity, and generalizes the result to particles of any spin and to massless particles. The exact expression is obtained using the analytic continuation technique developed by Becattini (2020) and Palermo (2021). The results enable quantitative assessment of higher-order corrections to the spin polarization of \(\Lambda\) hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where thermal vorticity may not be small, and provide a benchmark for comparing with the commonly used linear approximation.
