# Two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials with Roper resonance excitation

> Motivated by the recent paper arXiv:2602.11815 [nucl-th], we calculate in these notes the spectral functions (i.e. imaginary parts) of the NN-potentials as they arise from $2π$-exchange with single and double Roper resonance excitation. In contrast to the full one-loop calculation, the spectral functions of the isoscalar and isovector central and tensor potentials can be given in simple analytical form. The pertinent momentum-space potentials $V_C(q), W_C(q), V_T(q), W_T(q)$ are obtained via subtracted dispersion relations. This representation allows also to include a regulator function, that tames high-momentum components of the chiral $2π$-exchange. The calculation is extended to $2π$-exchange with combined $Δ(1232)$-isobar and Roper resonance excitation.

# Two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials with Roper resonance excitation

## Key claim
The spectral functions (imaginary parts) of the NN-potentials from 2π-exchange with single and double Roper resonance excitation can be given in simple analytical form, in contrast to a full one-loop calculation, and the resulting momentum-space potentials are obtained via subtracted dispersion relations that also allow inclusion of a regulator function to tame high-momentum components.

## Method
The author calculates the imaginary parts of the isoscalar and isovector central and tensor NN-potentials arising from one-loop 2π-exchange diagrams (triangle, planar box, crossed box) with single and double Roper N*(1440) resonance excitation, using the Cutkosky cutting rule in the heavy baryon limit. The spectral functions are then transformed into momentum-space potentials V_C(q), W_C(q), V_T(q), W_T(q) via twice- or once-subtracted dispersion relations. The calculation is extended to include combined Δ(1232)-isobar and Roper resonance excitation in the intermediate states.

## Key numbers
- Roper-nucleon mass difference: ρ ≃ 500 MeV
- Δ(1232)-nucleon mass difference: Δ ≃ 300 MeV
- Average pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92 MeV
- Axial coupling constant: g_A ≃ 1.3
- The spectral function for the triangle diagram with Roper excitation (isovector central potential) is given by: ImW_C(iμ) = (g'_A^2)/(π μ (2f_π)^4) { (2m_π^2/3 − 5μ^2/12 − ρ^2) k + ρ(μ^2/2 − m_π^2 + ρ^2) arctan(k/ρ) }
- For planar and crossed box diagrams with single Roper excitation, the isoscalar central potential in momentum space is: V_C(q) = (6g_A^2 g'_A^2)/(π (4f_π)^4 ρ q) (2m_π^2 + q^2)^2 arctan(q/(2m_π))
- For planar and crossed box diagrams with double Roper excitation, the isovector tensor spectral function is: ImW_T(iμ) = (g'_A^4)/(π μ (4f_π)^4) { (1/ρ)(2m_π^2 + 2ρ^2 − μ^2/2) arctan(k/ρ) − 2k }

## Context
This work extends the program of computing 2π-exchange NN-potentials with intermediate nucleon-resonance excitations, following the recent paper arXiv:2602.11815 [nucl-th] and building on prior calculations for Δ(1232)-isobar excitation by Meins (ref. [meins] in the text). The author notes that when including the Roper resonance, one must also consider explicit contributions from Δ(1232)-isobar excitation because of the lower ΔN-mass splitting, as emphasized by Lisheng (ref. [lisheng] in the text). The results enable improved chiral NN-potentials that incorporate both low-lying nucleon resonances, with the spectral representation allowing straightforward regularization of high-momentum components.
