# Anomalous Behavior of 2+ Excitations around 132Sn

> In certain neutron-rich Te isotopes, a decrease in the energy of the first excited 2+ state is accompanied by a decrease in the E2 strength to that state from the ground state, contradicting simple systematics and general intuition about quadrupole collectivity. We use a separable quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian and the quasiparticle random phase approximation to calculate energies, B(E2,0+ -> 2+) strengths, and g factors for the lowest 2+ states near 132Sn (Z >= 50). We trace the anomalous behavior in the Te isotopes to a reduced neutron pairing above the N = 82 magic gap.

## Key claim

The paper traces the anomalous decrease in both the energy and the E2 transition strength of the first excited 2+ state in neutron-rich Te isotopes (132Te, 134Te, 136Te) to a reduced neutron pairing gap above the N=82 magic gap, rather than to changes in quadrupole collectivity. This behavior contradicts the usual inverse relation between these two observables that holds for most isotopic chains.

## Method

The authors used a separable quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to calculate energies, B(E2,0+ -> 2+) strengths, and g factors for the lowest 2+ states in even-even nuclei with Z between 50 and 58 and N between 80 and 84. Single-particle energies were taken from experimental data around 132Sn, and the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with Skyrme SLy4 forces was used to compute static deformations and pairing gaps as input to the QRPA.

## Key numbers

- In 132Te: E(2+) = 0.974 MeV, B(E2) = 0.172 e^2b^2
- In 136Te: E(2+) = 0.606 MeV, B(E2) = 0.103 e^2b^2
- The lowest neutron quasiparticle energy is 0.792 MeV in 132Te and 0.460 MeV in 136Te
- The renormalization factor for neutron pairing gaps in excited 2+ states was 0.6; for protons it was 0.9
- For magic nuclei with N=82 and/or Z=50, the pairing gap was taken as 0.4 MeV
- The isovector quadrupole force strength used was chi_{T=1}(std) = -92.9 A^{-7/3} MeV fm^{-4}
- Calculated g factors: 0.491 for 132Te, 0.695 for 134Te, -0.174 for 136Te
- Experimental g factors: 0.354(7) for 134Xe, 0.766(45) for 136Xe

## Context

This work extends the study of quadrupole collectivity in neutron-rich nuclei around the doubly magic 132Sn, following experimental measurements by Radford (2002) that revealed the anomalous behavior in Te isotopes. The results contradict the simple systematics of the Grodzins (1962) and Raman (2001) relations, and differ from large-scale shell-model calculations by Covello (2002), Radford (2002), and Otsuka (2002) which predicted an increase in B(E2) from N=82 to N=84 in Te. The paper enables future experimental tests of the predicted g factor reduction in 136Te and the predicted increase in B(E2) at 132Sn relative to its neighbors.
