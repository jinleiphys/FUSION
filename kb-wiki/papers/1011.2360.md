# Exes and why Z? Some charming and beautiful observations

> Threshold enhancements like the X(4660) and depletion effects as the X(4260) are listed as c-cbar resonances in the Particle Data Group tables. We will discuss these observations, and present a list of further c-cbar enhancements, which are more likely to represent true vector charmonium excitations. We will furthermore discuss the importance of the observed Z resonances, viz. Z(4050), Z(4250), and Z(4430), for the family of charm-strange mesons. Another piece of very important information that can be extracted from the present data is the universal, flavor independent frequency of 190 MeV for mesons, due to the quark-antiquark oscillations within the glue environment. Finally, we will show hints from the data at a further flavor-independent quantity, having a value of 76 +-2 MeV, the origin of which is not yet understood. Talk available at http://cft.fis.uc.pt/eef/Frascati2010start.htm

## Key claim

The paper argues that many structures listed as charmonium resonances in the Particle Data Group tables are actually nonresonant threshold enhancements or depletion effects, and that the true vector charmonium excitations are often modest enhancements that must be carefully extracted from data. The authors further propose that the observed Z⁺ resonances (Z(4050), Z(4250), Z(4430)) may be higher excitations of the Dₛ* spectrum rather than exotic states.

## Method

The authors apply the Resonance-Spectrum Expansion (RSE) in its harmonic-oscillator approximation (HORSE), which assumes an equidistant quark-antiquark spectrum with a level spacing of 380 MeV independent of flavor. They compare the HORSE quenched (bare) masses for charmonium, bottomonium, and charm-strange mesons to experimental data, and classify observed structures into four categories: genuine resonances, accidental resonances, threshold enhancements, and depletion effects.

- Bottom quark mass m_b = 4.724 GeV used in the HORSE quenched spectrum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RSE formalism (van Beveren and Rupp, various papers from 1980-2010) which classifies enhancements into four types, contradicting the common assumption that all observed structures are resonances. The paper specifically challenges the charmonium assignments in the Particle Data Group tables and the identification of Υ(10865) and Υ(11019) as Υ(5S) and Υ(6S) by Godfrey and Isgur (1985). It builds on the suggestion by Matsuki, Morii, and Sudoh (2008) that Z⁺ resonances might be Dₛ* excitations, and uses BABAR data on D*K invariant-mass distributions (BABAR Collaboration, 2009) to support this interpretation. The work enables a re-evaluation of the charmonium and bottomonium spectra, and suggests that the Z⁺ resonances may not be exotic states but rather conventional charm-strange mesons.
