# Weak Quasielastic Production of Hyperons and Threshold Production of Two Pions

> We have studied quasielastic charged current hyperon production induced by $\barν_μ$ on free nucleon and the nucleons bound inside the nucleus and the results are presented for several nuclear targets like $^{40}Ar$, $^{56}Fe$ and $^{208}Pb$. The hyperon-nucleon transition form factors are determined from neutrino-nucleon scattering and semileptonic decays of neutron and hyperons using SU(3) symmetry. The nuclear medium effects(NME) due to Fermi motion and final state interaction(FSI) effect due to hyperon-nucleon scattering have been taken into account. Also we have studied two pion production at threshold induced by neutrinos off nucleon targets. The contribution of nucleon, pion, and contact terms are calculated using Lagrangian given by nonlinear $σ$ model. The contribution of the Roper resonance has also been taken into account. The numerical results for the cross sections are presented and compared with the experimental results from ANL and BNL.

## Key claim

The paper presents cross sections for quasielastic charged-current hyperon production by antineutrinos on free nucleons and nucleons bound in nuclear targets, as well as threshold two-pion production by neutrinos on nucleons, but the results depend on the specific choices of SU(3)-symmetric form factors and nuclear medium corrections (Fermi motion and final-state hyperon-nucleon scattering) that may not be universally valid.

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross sections for antineutrino-induced hyperon production (p to Lambda, p to Sigma0, n to Sigma-) using Cabibbo theory with SU(3) symmetry to determine the nucleon-hyperon transition form factors from neutrino-nucleon scattering and semileptonic decays of neutron and hyperons. For nuclear targets (Ar-40, Fe-56, Pb-208), they incorporated nuclear medium effects from Fermi motion and final-state interactions from hyperon-nucleon scattering. Separately, they studied charged-current two-pion production at threshold on nucleon targets using a nonlinear sigma model Lagrangian, including contributions from nucleon, pion, contact terms, and the Roper resonance.

## Key numbers

* Axial dipole mass used for the axial form factor: M_A = 1 GeV, with g_A(0) = 1.267
* Vector dipole mass parameter: M_V = 0.84 GeV
* SU(3) symmetry parameters: D = 0.804, F = 0.463
* Proton anomalous magnetic moment: mu_p = 1.792847; neutron anomalous magnetic moment: mu_n = -1.913043
* Nuclear targets studied: Ar-40, Fe-56, Pb-208
* Results compared with experimental data from ANL and BNL

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of hyperon production using Cabibbo theory with SU(3) symmetry (Singh 2006) and quark models (Wu 2013), and builds on prior studies of kaon production (RafiAlam 2010, Alam 2012) and two-pion production (Hernandez 2007). The results are relevant for interpreting cross section measurements in neutrino oscillation experiments such as MINERvA, MINOS, NOvA, LBNE, T2K, and MiniBooNE, and for testing assumptions about SU(3) symmetry, CVC, and PCAC in the strangeness sector.
