# Solar Fluctuations and the MSW Effect

> This talk summarizes the results of recent calculations of how fluctuations within the solar medium can influence resonant neutrino oscillations within the sun. Although initial calculations pointed to helioseismic waves as possibly producing detectable effects, recent more careful calculations show this not to be true. Those features of fluctuations which maximize their influence on neutrino propagation are identified, and are likely to have implications for supernovae and the early universe.

## Key claim

Although initial calculations suggested that helioseismic waves might produce detectable effects on resonant neutrino oscillations in the sun, more careful calculations show this not to be true; fluctuations can cause appreciable deterioration of the MSW resonance only if their correlation lengths are neither too big nor too small compared to the neutrino oscillation length at the resonance position.

## Method

The author summarizes calculations that first identify which features of density fluctuations maximize their influence on neutrino propagation, then perform detailed numerical simulations of neutrino oscillations in the presence of helioseismic waves. For helioseismic waves, the study explicitly computes seismic wave profiles within the sun to correlate their amplitude at the resonance point with surface observations, then numerically evolves neutrinos through these profiles without using the short-correlation-length (Master equation) approximation.

## Key numbers

- The critical correlation length dividing large and small fluctuations for solar neutrinos is approximately 3,000 km, derived from the condition l_crit = 1/(epsilon G_F <n_e>) with epsilon ~ 10% fluctuation amplitude.
- Helioseismic wave correlation lengths at resonance can be as large as R_sun/10 ~ 70,000 km, which easily exceeds the 3,000 km critical length.
- Overstable g-waves could carry energies up to 10^10 times larger than other modes, but their wavelengths are too large to permit a small-correlation-length calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of fluctuation effects on neutrino propagation (Sawyer, 1990; Burgess and Michaud, 1996; Burgess and Michaud, 1997) by performing more precise calculations that do not rely on the short-correlation-length approximation. The results contradict earlier preliminary calculations that had indicated helioseismic waves might produce detectable deviations from the standard MSW prediction. The findings have implications for neutrino propagation in supernovae and the early universe, where similar fluctuation effects might be relevant.
