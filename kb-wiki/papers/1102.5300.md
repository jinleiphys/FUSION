# Flavour blindness and patterns of flavour symmetry breaking in lattice simulations of up, down and strange quarks

> QCD lattice simulations with 2+1 flavours (when two quark flavours are mass degenerate) typically start at rather large up-down and strange quark masses and extrapolate first the strange quark mass and then the up-down quark mass to its respective physical value. Here we discuss an alternative method of tuning the quark masses, in which the singlet quark mass is kept fixed. Using group theory the possible quark mass polynomials for a Taylor expansion about the flavour symmetric line are found, first for the general 1+1+1 flavour case and then for the 2+1 flavour case. This ensures that the kaon always has mass less than the physical kaon mass. This method of tuning quark masses then enables highly constrained polynomial fits to be used in the extrapolation of hadron masses to their physical values. Numerical results for the 2+1 flavour case confirm the usefulness of this expansion and an extrapolation to the physical pion mass gives hadron mass values to within a few percent of their experimental values. Singlet quantities remain constant which allows the lattice spacing to be determined from hadron masses (without necessarily being at the physical point). Furthermore an extension of this programme to include partially quenched results is given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an alternative method for tuning quark masses in 2+1 flavour lattice QCD simulations by keeping the singlet quark mass (average of up, down, and strange quark masses) fixed along the trajectory from the flavour symmetric point to the physical point, rather than the conventional approach of keeping the strange quark mass fixed. This ensures that the kaon mass is always less than its physical value along the entire trajectory, enabling highly constrained polynomial fits for extrapolating hadron masses.

## Method

The authors use group theory to classify all possible quark mass polynomials up to third order under the SU(3) flavour group and the S3 permutation group, expanding about a flavour symmetric point where all three quark masses are equal. They then apply this expansion to 2+1 flavour lattice QCD simulations using clover (non-chiral) fermions, keeping the average sea quark mass constant while varying the individual quark masses along the constant singlet mass surface. The formalism is extended to include partially quenched results where valence and sea quark masses can differ.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of renormalised strange to light quark masses at the physical point is approximately m_s^R/m_l^R ≈ 25
* Extrapolation to the physical pion mass gives hadron mass values to within a few percent of their experimental values
* The factor α_Z representing the fractional difference between singlet and non-singlet renormalisation constants is of order O(1) and is non-negligible at the coupling used
* On the constant singlet mass surface, at O(m_q^k) there are k+1 independent polynomials needed, compared to (1/2)(k+1)(k+2) polynomials if the constraint is dropped

## Context

This work extends the standard approach to 2+1 flavour lattice QCD simulations where the strange quark mass is first extrapolated and then the up-down quark mass is reduced to its physical value. The constant singlet mass method provides an alternative path that keeps the kaon mass below its physical value throughout, in contrast to the conventional fixed strange mass path. The group theory classification builds on the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relations (Gell-Mann 1962, Okubo 1962) and enables more constrained extrapolations. The approach enables lattice spacing determination from hadron masses without necessarily being at the physical point, and the formalism can be extended to partially quenched simulations.
