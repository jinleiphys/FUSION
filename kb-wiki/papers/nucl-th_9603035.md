# Femtometer Toroidal Structures in Nuclei

> The two-nucleon density distributions in states with isospin $T=0$, spin $S$=1 and projection $M_S$=0 and $\pm$1 are studied in $^2$H, $^{3,4}$He, $^{6,7}$Li and $^{16}$O. The equidensity surfaces for $M_S$=0 distributions are found to be toroidal in shape, while those of $M_S$=$\pm$1 have dumbbell shapes at large density. The dumbbell shapes are generated by rotating tori. The toroidal shapes indicate that the tensor correlations have near maximal strength at $r<2$ fm in all these nuclei. They provide new insights and simple explanations of the structure and electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron, the quasi-deuteron model, and the $dp$, $dd$ and $αd$ $L$=2 ($D$-wave) components in $^3$He, $^4$He and $^6$Li. The toroidal distribution has a maximum-density diameter of $\sim$1 fm and a half-maximum density thickness of $\sim$0.9 fm. Many realistic models of nuclear forces predict these values, which are supported by the observed electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron, and also predicted by classical Skyrme effective Lagrangians, related to QCD in the limit of infinite colors. Due to the rather small size of this structure, it could have a revealing relation to certain aspects of QCD.

# Femtometer Toroidal Structures in Nuclei

## Key claim

The two-nucleon density distributions in the isospin T=0, spin S=1, M_S=0 state exhibit toroidal shapes in all studied nuclei (²H, ³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li, ⁷Li, and ¹⁶O), indicating that tensor correlations have near maximal strength at interparticle distances r < 2 fm, but the authors note that the variational wave functions for ⁶Li, ⁷Li, and ¹⁶O are not as accurate as those for A=3 and 4, and the ¹⁶O wave function was adjusted to reproduce the experimental rms radius.

## Method

The two-nucleon density distributions in T=0, S=1 states with spin projections M_S=0 and ±1 were calculated from variational wave functions using Monte Carlo techniques for A > 2 nuclei, with the Argonne v₁₈ two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. For ¹⁶O, a cluster expansion including up to four-body clusters with Monte Carlo integration was used. The density distributions were expanded in Legendre polynomials up to L=2, and the radial coefficients A(r) were computed directly from the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum one-body density of the torus inside the deuteron: ~0.34 ± 0.02 fm⁻³ (approximately twice nuclear matter density)
- Maximum-density diameter of the toroidal distribution: ~1 fm
- Half-maximum density thickness of the torus: ~0.9 fm
- First minimum of the charge form factor F_{C,1}² in the deuteron occurs at q₁ = 3.6 fm⁻¹, giving effective diameter d₁ = 0.87 fm
- First zero of F_{C,0}(q) at q = 9.2 fm⁻¹ gives effective thickness 0.68 fm
- First minimum of T₂₀ experimentally known at q ~ 3.5 ± 0.5 fm⁻¹
- Minimum of F_M(q) observed at q ~ 7 fm⁻¹, supporting thickness estimate of ~0.85 fm
- Zeros of momentum distributions occur at k ≃ 1.5 fm⁻¹ and 5.2 fm⁻¹ for different spin projections
- ⟨v_π⟩_d = -21.3 MeV for the Argonne v₁₈ model, accounting for most of the deuteron potential energy ⟨v⟩_d = -22.1 MeV
- Average photon absorption cross section for deuteron in E_γ = 80-120 MeV: ~0.072 mb
- Total photon absorption cross section for ³He in same energy range: ~0.13 mb (two-body ~0.03 mb, three-body ~0.10 mb)
- Total photon absorption cross section for ⁴He in same energy range: ~0.3 mb

## Context

This work extends the quasi-deuteron model proposed by Bethe and Levinger (1950), finding that the short-range T,S=0,1 neutron-proton wave function in nuclei is indeed similar to that in the deuteron, with the scale factor R_{Ad} identified as the Levinger-Bethe quasi-deuteron number. The toroidal shapes had been previously predicted by classical Skyrme field theory (related to QCD in the limit of large number of colors) for the deuteron by Kopeliovich, Verbaarschot, and Leese, but the present work shows these structures emerge from conventional nuclear many-body theory with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results enable experimental probes of the toroidal structure through polarized electron-deuteron scattering and (e,e'p) reactions on polarized deuterium, and suggest that the reduction of T,S=1,0 pairs due to tensor correlations contributes to saturation of nuclear binding energies.
