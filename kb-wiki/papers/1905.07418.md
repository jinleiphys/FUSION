# Event shape and Multiplicity dependence of Freeze-out Scenario and System Thermodynamics in Proton+Proton Collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8

> Recent observations of QGP-like conditions in high-multiplicity pp collisions from ALICE experiment at the LHC warrants an introspection whether to use pp collisions as a baseline measurement to characterize heavy-ion collisions for possible formation of a Quark-Gluon Plasma. A double differential study of the particle spectra and thermodynamics of the produced system as a function of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse spherocity in pp collisions would shed light into the underlying event dynamics. Transverse spherocity, one of the event shape observables, allows to separate the events in terms of jetty and isotropic events. We analyse the identified particle transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra as a function of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse spherocity using Tsallis non-extensive statistics and Boltzmann-Gibbs Blastwave (BGBW) model in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV using PYTHIA8 event generator. The extracted parameters such as temperature ($T$), radial flow ($β$) and non-extensive parameter ($q$) are shown as a function of charged-particle multiplicity for different spherocity classes. We observe that the isotropic events approach to thermal equilibrium while the jetty ones remain far from equilibrium. We argue that, while studying the QGP-like conditions in small systems, one should separate the isotropic events from the spherocity-integrated events, as the production dynamics are different.

## Key claim
The paper argues that in high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV, isotropic events (selected by transverse spherocity) approach thermal equilibrium more closely than spherocity-integrated events, while jetty events remain far from equilibrium; therefore, when studying QGP-like conditions in small systems, isotropic events should be separated from the spherocity-integrated sample because their production dynamics differ.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA8 event generator (Monash 2013 tune) with multi-parton interactions and color reconnection to simulate 250 million minimum-bias pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV. They classified events into ten V0 multiplicity classes and three spherocity classes (jetty: S0 < 0.29, isotropic: S0 > 0.64, and spherocity-integrated). The transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, K*0, phi, and Lambda were fitted with a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution (full pT range) and with the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blastwave model (pT <= 2 GeV/c) to extract temperature, radial flow, and the non-extensive parameter q.

## Key numbers
- The spherocity cuts define jetty events as those with 0 <= S0 < 0.29 (lowest 20% of events, ~50M events) and isotropic events as those with 0.64 < S0 <= 1 (highest 20% of events, ~50M events).
- The Tsallis distribution fits the generated data up to pT ~ 10 GeV/c; the BGBW model fits up to pT ~ 2 GeV/c.
- For the highest multiplicity class, the average radial flow velocity from BGBW is ~0.62 for pions and 0.31 for Lambda.
- The non-extensive parameter q for isotropic events is lower than for spherocity-integrated events, indicating higher equilibration; q for jetty events is always higher than for spherocity-integrated events.
- The scaled PYTHIA8 simulated data agree with ALICE experimental data within 10-20% at low pT and are consistent with unity at intermediate and high pT.

## Context
This work extends studies of QGP-like signatures in small systems, following observations by ALICE (2017) of strangeness enhancement and thermal effective temperatures in high-multiplicity pp collisions. It builds on prior event-shape analyses using transverse spherocity by Khuntia et al. (2018) and Ortiz et al. (2013), who showed that color reconnection and multi-parton interactions in PYTHIA8 can produce flow-like features. The results provide a baseline for future experimental data with spherocity selection and help bridge understanding of particle production from hadronic to heavy-ion collisions.
