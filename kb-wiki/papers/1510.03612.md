# Time-dependent density functional studies of nuclear quantum dynamics in large amplitudes

> The time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) provides a unified description of the structure and reaction. The linear approximation leads to the random-phase approximation (RPA) which is capable of describing a variety of collective motion in a harmonic regime. Beyond the linear regime, we present applications of the TDDFT to nuclear fusion and fission reaction. In particular, the extraction of the internuclear potential and the inertial mass parameter is performed using two different methods. A fusion hindrance mechanism for heavy systems is investigated from the microscopic point of view. The canonical collective variables are determined by the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method. Preliminary results of the spontaneous fission path, the potential, and the collective mass parameter are shown for 8Be --> alpha+alpha.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) can describe nuclear fusion and fission beyond the linear (RPA) regime, but the extracted inertial mass parameter shows strong energy dependence and significant oscillations after the two nuclei touch, indicating that the canonical definition of collective variables becomes ambiguous in that region.

## Method

The authors apply two complementary methods to extract the internuclear potential, inertial mass, and friction coefficient from TDDFT simulations. The dissipative-dynamics TDDFT (DD-TDDFT) method maps the real-time TDDFT evolution onto a one-dimensional classical equation of motion by computing the center-of-mass positions and momenta of the left and right fragments. The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method determines canonical collective variables (q,p) by solving constrained Hartree-Fock and RPA-like equations self-consistently, yielding a unique potential and mass parameter without a priori assumptions about the collective coordinate.

## Key numbers

- For the 70Zn + 208Pb system, TDDFT simulations were performed at center-of-mass energies E_cm = 350, 300, and 280 MeV; the extracted nucleus-nucleus potential shows a monotonic increase with no ordinary barrier at all three energies.
- For the 100Mo + 110Pd system, the extra-push energy E_extra = 14.7 MeV, with potential increase ΔV = 6.7 MeV and accumulated dissipation energy E_diss = 6.3 MeV.
- For the 96Zr + 124Sn system, E_extra = 13.9 MeV, ΔV = 8.5 MeV, E_diss = 3.9 MeV.
- For the 96Zr + 136Xe system, E_extra = 16.0 MeV, ΔV = 9.7 MeV, E_diss = 5.1 MeV.
- For the spontaneous fission of 8Be → α+α, the ASCC method yields an inertial mass M(R) ≈ 2m (twice the nucleon mass) at large relative distances, corresponding to the reduced mass of the two-alpha system; the potential minimum occurs around R = 3.5 fm and the Coulomb barrier around R = 6.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the density-constrained TDDFT approach proposed by Cusson (1985) and later used by Umar and Oberacker (2007, 2008), as well as the classical equation-of-motion method developed by Washiyama and collaborators (2008, 2009). The fusion hindrance mechanism for heavy systems (charge product Z_P Z_T > 1600-1800) is investigated microscopically, complementing macroscopic fluctuation-dissipation models using Langevin equations (Zagrebaev and Greiner, 2005; Aritomo, 2012) and recent TDDFT analyses of quasi-fission (Simenel, 2012; Golabek and Simenel, 2012; Umar and Oberacker, 2014). The ASCC method, originally formulated by Matsuo, Nakatsukasa, and Matsuyanagi (2000), provides a self-consistent framework for determining canonical collective variables that is applicable to low-energy fusion and fission, with the advantage over the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula or generator coordinate method of including time-odd mean-field effects necessary for correct translational mass.
