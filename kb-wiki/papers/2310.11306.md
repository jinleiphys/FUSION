# Reactions induced by 30 MeV 3He beam on 9Be: Cluster transfer reactions

> $ $An experiment has been carried out for studying the cluster structure of $^9$Be induced by the $^3$He ions at the energy of 30 MeV. As results of the nuclear reaction $^3$He + $^9$Be the differential cross sections for the exit channels - elastic, inelastic, $α$ + $^8$Be, $^6$He + $^6$Be, $^6$Li + $^6$Li, and $^7$Be + $^5$He - were measured. Elastic and inelastic scattering data are treated within both the optical model and Coupled channels method. A new set of optical potential was taken for the elastic scattering. The deformation parameter $δ_2$ was established for the transition $3/2\rightarrow5/2$. Cluster transfer reactions are analyzed by means of the coupled reaction channels method. The nuclear reactions with the exit channels $^6$He + $^6$Be, $^6$Li + $^6$Li, and $^7$Be + $^5$He are complemented by two-step transfer mechanisms. The contribution of each reaction mechanisms are shown, and compared with the findings of other authors.

## Key claim

The cluster structure of 9Be is probed via 3He-induced reactions at 30 MeV, with the claim that the measured differential cross sections for cluster transfer channels (6He+6Be, 6Li+6Li, 7Be+5He) can be explained by a combination of one-step and two-step transfer mechanisms, but the model fails to describe the last few data points in the 7Be+5He channel, indicating that additional mechanisms may be needed.

## Method

Differential cross sections for elastic, inelastic, and cluster transfer channels (alpha+8Be, 6He+6Be, 6Li+6Li, 7Be+5He) were measured using the 3He+9Be reaction at 30 MeV laboratory energy at the Nuclear Physics Institute in Rez, Czech Republic, with four Si-Si(Li) telescopes providing particle identification via the Delta E-E method. Elastic and inelastic scattering data were analyzed using the optical model and coupled channels (CC) method with the FRESCO code, while cluster transfer reactions were analyzed using the coupled reaction channels (CRC) method incorporating both one-step and two-step transfer mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Deformation length delta_2 = 1.97 fm for the 3/2 to 5/2 transition in 9Be, determined from inelastic scattering data
- Optical potential parameters for the 3He+9Be entrance channel: V0 = 101.9 MeV, r_V = 0.700 fm, a_V = 0.777 fm, W0 = 30.81 MeV, r_W = 0.854 fm, a_W = 0.817 fm
- Modified optical potential (mOP) for CRC calculations: V0 = 103.9 MeV, W0 = 23.81 MeV
- Beam energy: 30 MeV; target thickness: 11 micrometers; average beam current: 10 enA
- Energy resolution: 150-200 keV; absolute error in cross section determination: not more than 15%
- Acceptance angle of each telescope: approximately 0.6 degrees; solid angle: approximately 0.2 msr

## Context

This work extends the earlier experimental study by Lukyanov et al. (2016) on the same 3He+9Be reaction at 30 MeV, which used only pure optical model and DWBA calculations that required more sophisticated theoretical models. The analysis applies the CRC method previously used by Rudchik et al. (1996) for the same reaction at 63 MeV, but at the lower energy of 30 MeV, finding that the direct alpha transfer dominates in the 7Be+5He channel and direct t transfer dominates in the 6Li+6Li channel, in contrast to the 63 MeV study where two-step n-d transfer prevailed. The results enable future experiments at higher energies to confirm the role of 2n cluster transfer at backward angles, as suggested by the calculations.
