# Universal Bound on Dynamical Relaxation Times and Black-Hole Quasinormal Ringing

> From information theory and thermodynamic considerations a universal bound on the relaxation time $τ$ of a perturbed system is inferred, $τ\geq \hbar/πT$, where $T$ is the system's temperature. We prove that black holes comply with the bound; in fact they actually {\it saturate} it. Thus, when judged by their relaxation properties, black holes are the most extreme objects in nature, having the maximum relaxation rate which is allowed by quantum theory.

# Universal Bound on Dynamical Relaxation Times and Black-Hole Quasinormal Ringing

## Key claim
The paper derives a universal bound on relaxation times for perturbed thermodynamic systems, τ ≥ ħ/πT, from quantum information theory and thermodynamic considerations. Black holes are shown to comply with this bound and actually saturate it, meaning they achieve the maximum possible relaxation rate allowed by quantum theory.

## Method
The author uses the maximum information transmission rate from quantum information theory (Eq. 1) to derive an upper bound on entropy change rate, then applies the second law of thermodynamics to obtain the minimal relaxation time τ_min = ħ/πT. For black holes, the relaxation time is identified with the inverse of the imaginary part of the fundamental (least damped) quasinormal mode frequency, ω_I. The bound is tested against numerical and analytical results for Kerr, Kerr-Newman, Reissner-Nordström, D-dimensional Schwarzschild, Schwarzschild-de Sitter, and AdS black hole spacetimes.

## Key numbers
- The universal bound on relaxation time: τ ≥ ħ/πT, where T is the system's temperature
- For a laboratory gas at room temperature with R ~ 1 m: τT ~ 10^11 ħ, many orders of magnitude above the bound
- For D-dimensional Schwarzschild black holes in the large ℓ limit: ħω_I/πT_BH = (2/(D-1))^(1/(D-3)) × 2/√(D-1), which is a maximum of 4/(3√3) < 1 at D=4
- For charged Reissner-Nordström black holes in the eQ/T_BH ≫ 1 limit: ω_I = πT_BH, saturating the bound
- For Kerr black holes: fundamental resonances satisfy ħω_I/πT_BH ≲ 1, with saturation in the extremal limit a/M → 1
- For Kerr-Newman black holes: fundamental resonances satisfy the bound, with saturation in the extremal limit a²+Q² → M²

## Context
This work extends the connection between black-hole physics and thermodynamics established by Hawking (1975) and Bekenstein (1973), who discovered black-hole radiation and entropy. It builds on prior bounds on information storage by Bekenstein (1981), 't Hooft (1993), and others, moving from static bounds to dynamical relaxation times. The paper enables a quantitative formulation of the third law of thermodynamics, suggesting that one cannot reach temperature T in a timescale shorter than ħ/πT, and establishes black holes as the most extreme objects in nature in terms of relaxation properties.
