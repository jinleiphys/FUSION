# Energy versus centrality dependence of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ at RHIC and LHC: a new puzzle?

> The central goal of jet quenching studies in high-energy nuclear collisions is the characterization of those QCD medium properties that are accessible by these probes. Most of the discussion in the last years has been focused on the determination of the jet quenching parameter, $\hat q$. We present here an extraction of this parameter using data of inclusive particle suppression at RHIC and LHC energies for different centralities. Our approach consists of fitting a $K$ factor that quantifies the departure of this parameter from an ideal estimate, $K\equiv \hat q/(2ε^{3/4})$, where $\hat q$ is determined by the local medium quantities as provided by hydrodynamical calculations. We find that this $K$ factor is larger at RHIC than at the LHC, as obtained already in previous analyses, but, surprisingly, it is almost independent of the centrality of the collision. Taken at face value, the $K$ factor would not depend on the local properties of the medium as energy density or temperature, but on global collision quantities such as the center of mass energy. This is a very intriguing, unexpected possibility for which we cannot yet provide a clear interpretation. We also comment on the limitations of the formalism that may affect this conclusion.

## Key claim

The authors extract the jet quenching parameter via a K factor (K ≡ q̂/(2ε³/⁴)) and find that K is larger at RHIC than at the LHC, but surprisingly almost independent of centrality, suggesting the K factor depends on global collision energy rather than local medium properties like temperature or energy density.

## Method

The authors fit a K factor to inclusive particle suppression data (RAA) at RHIC and LHC energies using the quenching weights formalism, where the transport coefficient q̂ is related to local energy density from hydrodynamical models as q̂ = K·2ε³/⁴. They use three different hydrodynamical profiles (Hirano, Glauber, fKLN) and three different assumptions for the time before thermalization (zero, constant, or free-streaming extrapolation) to test robustness of results.

## Key numbers

- K factor at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) is approximately 2–3 times larger than at LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV)
- For RHIC AuAu collisions, K values are fitted to 72 data points from PHENIX on inclusive π⁰ suppression at pT > 5 GeV/c
- For LHC PbPb collisions, K values are fitted to 156 data points from ALICE on inclusive charged particles at pT > 5 GeV/c
- The K factor at LHC from the Glauber model fit is K = 1.133 ± 0.028 for √sNN = 2.76 TeV data
- Differences in K between error treatment methods are ~5% except for most peripheral bins where quadrature addition gives ~30% smaller K values

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of the jet quenching parameter by Armesto (2009) and the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013), finding a larger K at RHIC than LHC as seen earlier by Horowitz (2011), but adds the new observation of centrality independence. The results challenge the naive expectation that K should scale with temperature, since central RHIC and semi-peripheral LHC collisions have overlapping energy densities but yield different K values, motivating further theoretical work on the role of early-time dynamics and global collision properties.
