# Clustering effects in the $^6$Li(p,$^3$He)$^4$He reaction at astrophysical energies

> Background: The understanding of nuclear reactions between light nuclei at energies below the Coulomb barrier is important for several astrophysical processes, but their study poses experimental and theoretical challenges. At sufficiently low energies, the electrons surrounding the interacting ions affect the scattering process. Moreover, the clustered structure of some of these nuclei may play a relevant role on the reaction observables. Purpose: In this article, we focus on a theoretical investigation of the role of clustered configurations of $^6$Li in reactions of astrophysical interest. Methods: The $^6$Li(p,$^3$He)$^4$He reaction cross section is described considering both the direct transfer of a deuteron as a single point-like particle in Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), and the transfer of a neutron and a proton in second-order DWBA. A number of two- and three-cluster structure models for $^6$Li are compared. Results: Within the two-cluster structure model, we explore the impact of the deformed components in the $^6$Li wave-function on the reaction of interest. Within the three-cluster structure model, we gauge the degree of $α$-d clustering and explicitly probe its role on specific features of the reaction cross section. We compare the energy trend of the astrophysical $S$ factor deduced in each case. Conclusions: Clustered $^6$Li configurations lead in general to a significant enhancement of the astrophysical factor in the energy region under study. This effect only originates from clustering, whereas static deformations of the ground-state configuration play a negligible role at very low energies.

## Key claim

Clustered configurations of $^6$Li lead to a significant enhancement of the astrophysical S factor for the $^6$Li(p,$^3$He)$^4$He reaction at sub-Coulomb energies, and this enhancement originates purely from clustering effects, while static deformations of the ground-state configuration play a negligible role at very low energies.

## Method

The reaction cross section was calculated using both first-order Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) treating the transferred deuteron as a single point-like particle, and second-order DWBA treating the transfer of a neutron and a proton individually. Several two-cluster and three-cluster structure models for $^6$Li were compared, with the two-cluster model exploring deformed components in the $^6$Li wave function and the three-cluster model gauging the degree of $\alpha$-d clustering. All numerical calculations were performed using the Fresco code with the Numerov method, truncating the partial-wave expansion at a total angular momentum of $7/2$.

## Key numbers

- The adiabatic-limit screening potential for a neutral hydrogen atom impinging on a neutral Li atom is 182 eV (from Bracci 1990).
- For the $^6$Li + p reaction at center-of-mass energies above 75 keV, the enhancement factor $[f_e(E)-1]$ is approximately 1% or smaller assuming $U = 182$ eV.
- At $E = 10$ keV with $U = 182$ eV, the enhancement factor $[f_e(E)-1]$ is approximately 26%.
- The contact-point radius $R_0 = 1.2$ fm $\times (A_1^{1/3} + A_2^{1/3})$ is about 3.5 fm for the $^6$Li + p system.
- The Coulomb barrier height for $R_n = R_0$ is about 1.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological analyses of the electron screening problem in the $^6$Li(p,$^3$He)$^4$He reaction, where direct measurements (Engstler 1992, Cruz 2005, Cruz 2007) show anomalous enhancement beyond standard screening predictions compared to Trojan Horse Method data (Lamia 2013). The study builds on the suggestion by Spitaleri (2016) that clustering effects might contribute to this discrepancy, and provides the first systematic theoretical comparison of two- and three-cluster structure models for $^6$Li in DWBA transfer calculations. The results enable future investigations that include explicit dynamical effects such as coupling to excited states or competing channels.
