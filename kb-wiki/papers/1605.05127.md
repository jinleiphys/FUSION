# Anisotropy of Low Energy Direct Photons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the behavior of low energy photons radiated by the deceleration processes of two colliding nuclei in relativistic heavy ion collisions using the Wigner function approach for electromagnetic radiation fields. The angular distribution reveals the information of the initial geometric configurations. Such a property is reflected in the anisotropic parameter $v_{2}$, showing an increasing $v_{2}$ as energy decreases, which is a behavior qualitatively different from $v_{2}$ from hadrons produced in the collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-energy direct photons produced by coherent bremsstrahlung from the deceleration of two colliding nuclei exhibit an increasing elliptic flow \(v_{2}\) as transverse momentum decreases, reaching a maximum of \(1/2\) at \(p_{T}=0\), a behavior qualitatively different from hadronic \(v_{2}\). However, this result depends on the idealized assumption of full stopping and instantaneous deceleration (Dirac delta in time), which is more applicable to lower-energy collisions (NICA, FAIR) than to RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors model the two colliding nuclei as point-like particles with an effective charge \(Z_{eff}\) moving along straight-line trajectories that stop instantaneously at \(t=0\). They solve the Maxwell equations using the Lienard-Wiechert potentials for these trajectories, then compute the photon phase-space distribution via the Wigner function of the classical electromagnetic fields. The photon spectrum and angular distribution are derived from the Wigner function in the far-field limit, and the elliptic flow parameter \(v_{2}\) is calculated analytically from the resulting momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- Effective charge \(Z_{eff} \sim 80\) and transverse half-distance \(d \sim 1\) fm used for a near-central Au+Au collision.
- Photon spectrum at mid-rapidity: \(\left. \frac{d^{3}N(p)}{2\pi p_{T}dp_{T}dy}\right|_{y=0} \simeq 0.37 \times 10^{-3} \frac{Z_{eff}^{2}}{p_{T}^{2}} \left(1 - J_{0}(2p_{T}d)\right)\).
- Total photon yield in the most favorable condition is less than 20.
- The \(v_{2}\) formula: \(v_{2}(p_{T}) = \frac{J_{2}(2p_{T}d)}{1 - J_{0}(2p_{T}d)}\), with maximum value \(1/2\) as \(p_{T} \rightarrow 0\), independent of \(d\).
- For the mixed coherent+incoherent model, the characteristic scale \(\Delta p = 0.2\) GeV is used.

## Context

This work extends the classical bremsstrahlung approach to direct photons in heavy ion collisions, previously applied to higher-energy photons (above 1 GeV) by Miklos (2013), by focusing on the coherent low-energy regime where the wavelength is comparable to the nuclear separation. The predicted rising \(v_{2}\) at low \(p_{T}\) contrasts with the behavior expected from hydrodynamic, kinetic, or microscopic collective flow mechanisms (see the review by PHSD and references therein). The authors suggest that this signature could be tested at lower-energy facilities such as NICA and FAIR, where full stopping is more realistic, and note that preliminary PHENIX data for \(p_{T} \lesssim 0.5\) GeV show a hint of such an increase.
