# Effective Lagrangian of unitary Fermi gas from $\varepsilon$ expansion

> Using $\varepsilon$ expansion technique proposed in \cite{Nishida:2006br} we derive an effective Lagrangian (Ginzburg-Landau-like functional) of the degenerate unitary Fermi gas to the next-to-leading (NLO) order in $\varepsilon.$ It is demonstrated that for many realistic situations it is sufficient to retain leading order (LO) terms in the derivative expansion. The functional is used to study vortex structure in the symmetric gas, and interface between normal and superfluid phases in the polarized gas. The resulting surface free energy is about four times larger than the value previously quoted in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective Lagrangian (Ginzburg-Landau-like functional) for the degenerate unitary Fermi gas to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the ε expansion, but the results for d=3 are obtained by simply setting ε=1 at the end of NLO calculations, with more sophisticated extrapolations left to future work. The surface free energy for the normal-superfluid interface is about four times larger than the value previously quoted in the literature.

## Method

The author uses the ε expansion technique proposed by Nishida and Son (2006), working in d=4-ε spatial dimensions and performing perturbation theory in ε about d=4. The effective action is constructed by integrating out the auxiliary di-fermion field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, then performing a derivative expansion to obtain a local effective Lagrangian. The leading order derivative terms are determined by gauging the U(1) particle number symmetry and requiring gauge invariance, avoiding explicit Legendre transformation. The functional is then applied to study vortex structure in the symmetric gas and the normal-superfluid interface in the polarized gas.

## Key numbers

- The NLO homogeneous superfluid order parameter: φ₀ = 2μ₀(1 + ε(3C - 1 + Log 2)), where C ≈ 0.14424 and μ₀ = μ/ε
- Single vortex core radius (d=3): r₀ = 0.25 L N^{-1/3} for a spherical trap; r₀ = 0.28 (V/N)^{1/3} for a cylindrical trap
- Surface free energy per unit area: σ = 0.58 √(μ₀/m) (μ₀ m / 2π)^{d/2} + O(ε²), with μ₀ = μ/ε
- For N=10⁵ particles in a spherical trap, the integrated surface free energy coefficient is 0.004, which is four times the value from de Silva and Mueller (2006)
- The vortex core radius at LO: r₀ = 0.43 (m μ)^{-1/2}; at NLO: r₀ = 0.45 (m μ)^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends the ε expansion technique for unitary Fermi gases developed by Nishida and Son (2006) and Nishida (2006) by constructing a derivative-expanded effective Lagrangian applicable to inhomogeneous configurations. The results for vortex structure are compared to calculations by Bulgac and Yu (2003) using a different method. The surface free energy result is about four times larger than the value quoted by de Silva and Mueller (2006). The effective Lagrangian enables simpler treatment of inhomogeneous phenomena compared to the standard Bogoliubov-de Gennes method, and can be applied to multiple vortex configurations, optical lattice systems, and trapped few-fermion systems where the Thomas-Fermi approximation breaks down.
