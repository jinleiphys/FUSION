# Can a protophobic vector boson explain the ATOMKI anomaly?

> In 2016, the ATOMKI collaboration announced [PRL {\bf 116}, 042501 (2016)] observing an unexpected enhancement of the $e^+-e^-$ pair production signal in one of the $^8$Be nuclear transitions induced by an incident proton beam on a $^7$Li target. Many beyond-standard-model physics explanations have subsequently been proposed. One popular theory is that the anomaly is caused by the creation of a protophobic vector boson ($X$) with a mass around 17 MeV [e.g., PRL\ {\bf 117}, 071803 (2016)] in the nuclear transition. We study this hypothesis by deriving an isospin relation between photon and $X$ couplings to nucleons. This allows us to find simple relations between protophobic $X$-production cross sections and those for measured photon production. The net result is that $X$ production is dominated by direct transitions induced by $E1^X$ and $L1^X$ (transverse and longitudinal electric dipoles) and $C1^X$ (charge dipole) without going through any nuclear resonance (i.e. Bremsstrahlung radiation) with a smooth energy dependence that occurs for all proton beam energies above threshold. This contradicts the experimental observations and invalidates the protophobic vector boson explanation.

## Key claim

The protophobic vector boson (X) explanation of the ATOMKI anomaly is invalidated because X production would be dominated by direct E1^X, L1^X, and C1^X transitions (Bremsstrahlung radiation) with a smooth energy dependence at all proton beam energies above threshold, contradicting the experimental observation that the enhancement is associated only with the higher-energy 1^+ resonance.

## Method

The authors derived an isospin relation between photon and X boson couplings to nucleons, showing that the isovector components of the X-production transition matrix elements are proportional to those for photon production by a factor of -ε_v. They applied this relation to an effective field theory model of the ⁷Li(p,γ)⁸Be reaction that includes both direct E1 capture and resonant M1 transitions through the two 1^+ excited states of ⁸Be, then computed X-production cross sections for various X boson masses around 17 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The X boson mass considered is around 17 MeV, with calculations performed for M_X = 16.5, 17, 17.5, and 18 MeV
- At the MIS resonance energy (ω = 18.15 MeV) with M_X = 17 MeV, the ratio σ_{X,ELC1^X}/σ_{X,M1^X} ≈ 8.6
- The isovector dominance condition requires |ε_s/ε_v| ≳ 12 to break the approximation, but protophobic X requires |ε_s/ε_v| ≲ 3
- The two 1^+ resonances have excitation energies of 0.385 MeV and 0.895 MeV, with photon widths of 15.0 ± 1.8 eV and 1.9 ± 0.4 eV respectively
- The four experimental beam energies measured by ATOMKI are 0.8, 1.04, 1.1, and 1.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ATOMKI anomaly first reported by Krasznahorkay (2016) and the protophobic vector boson hypothesis proposed by Feng (2016). The authors use the effective field theory model developed by Zhang (2017) for photon production in ⁷Li(p,γ)⁸Be to compute X boson production. The results contradict the protophobic vector boson explanation and suggest that alternative beyond-standard-model explanations for the anomaly must be sought.
