# Equation of state for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using Lane potential

> A mean field calculation for obtaining the equation of state (EOS) for symmetric nuclear matter from a density dependent M3Y interaction supplemented by a zero-range potential is described. The energy per nucleon is minimized to obtain the ground state of symmetric nuclear matter. The saturation energy per nucleon used for nuclear matter calculations is determined from the co-efficient of the volume term of Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula which is evaluated by fitting the recent experimental and estimated atomic mass excesses from Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass table by minimizing the mean square deviation. The constants of density dependence of the effective interaction are obtained by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and the saturation density of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. The EOS of symmetric nuclear matter, thus obtained, provide reasonably good estimate of nuclear incompressibility. Once the consants of density dependence are determined, EOS for asymmetric nuclear matter is calculated by adding to the isoscalar part, the isovector component of the M3Y interaction that do not contribute to the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter. These EOS are then used to calculate the pressure, the energy density and the velocity of sound in symmetric as well as isospin asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents a mean-field calculation of the equation of state (EOS) for symmetric and isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using a density-dependent M3Y interaction supplemented by a zero-range potential, where the density dependence parameters are fixed by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and saturation density of cold symmetric nuclear matter, and the isovector component (Lane potential) is then added to treat asymmetry.

## Method

The authors use the M3Y-Reid-Elliott effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for the single-nucleon exchange term, with a density dependence of the form \(g(\rho, \epsilon) = C (1 - \beta \rho^n)\). The parameters \(C\) and \(\beta\) are determined by solving the saturation conditions (minimum energy per nucleon and zero pressure) for cold symmetric nuclear matter, using a saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.1533~\text{fm}^{-3}\) and saturation energy \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26~\text{MeV}\) obtained from a fit to the Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass table. For asymmetric matter, the isovector (Lane) component of the M3Y interaction is added, and the energy per nucleon, pressure, energy density, and sound velocity are computed for various isospin asymmetries \(X\) and densities.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.1533~\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Saturation energy per nucleon: \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26~\text{MeV}\).
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter for \(n=2/3\): \(K_0 = 293.4~\text{MeV}\).
- Incompressibility for asymmetric matter at \(X=0.5\) (with \(n=2/3\)): \(K_0 = 182.0~\text{MeV}\) at saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.1315~\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Sound velocity in symmetric nuclear matter becomes superluminal (\(v_s > c\)) at \(\rho \ge 5.3 \rho_0\) (i.e., \(\rho \ge 0.82~\text{fm}^{-3}\)).
- Sound velocity becomes imaginary for symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho \le 0.1~\text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) folding model calculations, which had been successfully applied to elastic and inelastic scattering of protons (Gupta 2005) and alpha particles, as well as to proton and cluster radioactivity (Basu 2003, Chowdhury 2006). The present EOS yields an incompressibility \(K_0 \approx 293~\text{MeV}\) for symmetric nuclear matter, which is in excellent agreement with the experimental value of \(300 \pm 25~\text{MeV}\) from giant monopole resonance measurements (Sharma 1988) and \(290 \pm 50~\text{MeV}\) from hard photon production in heavy-ion collisions (Schmidt 1996). The results enable calculations of pressure, energy density, and sound velocity for neutron star matter and supernova simulations, and the authors note that the EOS becomes superluminal at high densities, a common limitation of non-relativistic approaches.
