# Experimental and theoretical evidences for an intermediate $σ$-dressed dibaryon in the NN interaction

> Numerous theoretical and experimental arguments are presented in favor of the generation of intermediate $σ$-dressed dibaryon in $NN$ interaction at intermediate and short distances. We argue that this intermediate dibaryon can be responsible for the strong intermediate-range attraction and the short-range repulsion in the $NN$ interaction, and also for the short-range correlations in nuclei. The suggested mechanism for the $σ$-dressing of the dibaryon is identical to that which explains the Roper resonance structure, its dominant decay modes and its extraordinary low mass. A similar transformation mechanism from the glue to the scalar field was discovered in $J/Ψ$ decays. The new experimental data on 2$π$-production in the scalar-isoscalar channel produced in $pn$- and $pd$-collisions and in particular the very recent data on $γγ$ correlations in $p$C and $d$C scattering in the GeV region seems to corroborate the existence of the $σ$-dressed dibaryon in two- and three nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an intermediate sigma-dressed dibaryon (a six-quark bag surrounded by a scalar sigma-meson field) is generated in nucleon-nucleon interactions at distances below about 1 fm, and that this dibaryon can explain both the intermediate-range attraction and short-range repulsion in the NN force, as well as short-range correlations in nuclei. The claim is hedged by noting that the mechanism is presented as a "plausible conjecture" supported by multiple independent experimental findings, rather than a proven fact.

## Method

The authors develop a two-component coupled-channel model where the external NN channel (with conventional one- and two-pion exchange) couples to an inner dibaryon channel consisting of a six-quark core dressed with sigma, pi, and rho meson fields. The coupling between channels is treated both microscopically (using six-quark configurations with mixed symmetry) and semi-microscopically (with parametrized vertex functions). The model is applied to fit NN phase shifts up to 1 GeV laboratory energy and to calculate the dibaryon admixture in the deuteron. The authors also interpret experimental data on two-pion production in pn and pd collisions, gamma-gamma correlations in pC and dC scattering, and the Roper resonance decay in terms of the dibaryon mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Dibaryon mass m_DB = 2.2 to 2.3 GeV (for ^1S_0 and ^3S_1-^3D_1 partial waves) from fitting NN phase shifts up to E_Lab ≈ 1 GeV
- Weight W_D of the dressed dibaryon component in the deuteron ≈ 0.025
- Roper resonance mass m_R ≈ 1370 MeV (anomalously low)
- Branching ratio Γ(R → N+σ)/Γ(R → Δ+π) ≈ 4:1 for Roper resonance decay
- Peak in ππ invariant mass at M_ππ ≈ 320-330 MeV in pn → d + (ππ)_00 reactions at E_p^lab ≈ 1.1-1.2 GeV
- Peak in γγ invariant mass at M_γγ ≈ 355 MeV with width Γ_γγ ≈ 49 MeV in d+C → γ+γ+X at 2.75 GeV/c per nucleon
- RMS radius r_ΔΔ of ΔΔ bound states ≤ 0.9 fm (from other calculations cited)
- Branching ratio Γ_σ(γγ)/Γ_σ(ππ) ≈ 10^{-5} to 10^{-6} for sigma meson decay

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier dibaryon model (Kukulin et al., 2001, 2002, 2004) and contrasts with conventional one-boson-exchange (OBE) models and effective field theory approaches that treat the short-range NN interaction phenomenologically. The paper directly addresses the "ABC puzzle" (Booth, 1960s) of enhanced two-pion production near threshold in p+d and d+d reactions, which conventional ΔΔ models (Bar-Nir, 1973) could not explain. The mechanism is related to partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense hadronic matter (Hatsuda, 1990s) and to the structure of the Roper resonance (Dillig, 2000s). The results enable a QCD-based description of nuclear phenomena and suggest that the intermediate six-quark bag predicted by Brodsky, Jaffe, and Brown (1980s) is realized in the NN system.
