# Introduction to Basis Light-Front Quantization Approach to QCD Bound State Problems

> Basis Light-Front Quantized Field Theory (BLFQ) is an $\textit{ab intio}$ Hamiltonian approach that adopts light-cone gauge, light-front quantization and state-of-the-art many-body methods to solve non-perturbative quantum field theory problems. By a suitable choice of basis, BLFQ retains the underlying symmetries to the extent allowed within light-front coordinates. In this talk, we outline the scheme for applying BLFQ to QCD bound state problems. We adopt a 2D Harmonic Oscillator with 1D plane wave basis that corresponds to the AdS/QCD soft-wall solution. Exact treatment of the symmetries will be discussed.

## Key claim

Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) is an *ab initio* Hamiltonian approach that, by adopting a 2D Harmonic Oscillator with 1D plane wave basis corresponding to the AdS/QCD soft-wall solution, can solve non-perturbative QCD bound state problems, but only within a finite truncated Fock space where the full field theory is restored only in the continuum limit and the complete Fock sectors limit.

## Method

The authors formulate QCD bound state problems using light-front quantization on a plane \(x^+ = 0\), adopting light-cone gauge \(A^+ = 0\) and expanding fields in a basis of 2D Harmonic Oscillator (HO) functions in the transverse direction and plane waves in the longitudinal direction. They truncate the Fock space by imposing a maximum HO quantum number \(\mathcal{N}_{\max}\) and discretizing longitudinal momentum via periodic/anti-periodic boundary conditions in a box of length \(\mathsf{L}\), then diagonalize the light-cone Hamiltonian \(H_{\text{lc}} = P^2\) as a large sparse matrix eigenvalue problem using the Lanczos method. The approach preserves a set of compatible kinematic operators \(\{H_{\text{lc}}, P^+, P_+^{\text{cm}}, J^3, \J^3\}\) even after truncation, enabling exact factorization of center-of-mass motion and identification of states by total spin \(\J\).

## Key numbers

* The transverse HO basis states are associated with eigenvalues \(E_{n,m} = (2n + |m| + 1)\Omega\).
* In a positronium model with coupling \(\alpha = g^2/(4\pi) = 0.2\) and basis energy scale \(b = \sqrt{P^+\Omega} = 0.3 m_e\) (electron mass \(m_e = 0.511\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the fine splitting between the singlet state (\(\J = 0, \mathcal{M}_j = 0\)) and triplet state (\(\J = 1, \mathcal{M}_j = 0\)) is \(2.77 \times 10^{-4} m_e\).
* The non-relativistic quantum mechanics prediction for the ground state fine splitting is \(E_{\text{triplet}} - E_{\text{singlet}} = \frac{1}{3} \alpha^4 m_e \simeq 5.33 \times 10^{-4} m_e\).
* The Hamiltonian matrix sparsity follows a power law: number of nonzero elements \(\sim N^{1.485}\), where \(N\) is the basis space dimensionality.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to QED for calculating the electron anomalous magnetic moment (Honkanen 2011, Zhao 2012) and positronium (Wiecki 2013) to the QCD bound state problem. The basis choice is consistent with the AdS/QCD soft-wall solution (Brodsky 2006, Brodsky 2008) and exploits the similarity between light-front dynamics and non-relativistic dynamics. The approach enables future non-perturbative calculations of meson, baryon, and exotic hadron structure, but requires renormalization to handle ultraviolet, infrared, and spurious small-\(x\) divergences, with schemes developed by Wiecki (2013), Perry (2007), Glazek (1995), and Karmanov (2010).
