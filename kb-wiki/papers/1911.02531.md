# Vacuum effects on the properties of nuclear matter under an external magnetic field

> The effects of the Dirac sea of the nucleons are investigated within a covariant model of the hadronic interaction. We extend the usual Mean Field Approximation and present a procedure to deal with divergences which are proportional to polynomials on the magnetic field intensity. For this purpose a nucleon propagator is used which takes accounts of the full effect of the magnetic field as well as the presence of the anomalous magnetic moments of both protons and neutrons. We examine single particle properties and bulk thermodynamical quantities and conclude that within a reasonable range of densities and magnetic intensities the effects found are moderate.

## Key claim

The Dirac sea vacuum contributions to nuclear matter properties under an external magnetic field are found to be moderate for densities up to three times normal nuclear density and magnetic intensities up to about 10^19 G, but the magnitude of these corrections depends significantly on the regularization procedure used to handle divergences, with the present work obtaining smaller effective mass shifts than a prior study by Haber (2014).

## Method

The author extends the standard Mean Field Approximation (MFA) within the Quantum Hadro-Dynamics (QHD) model using the FSUGold parameter set, incorporating a nucleon propagator that includes the full effect of the external magnetic field and the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of both protons and neutrons. Divergent vacuum contributions to scalar densities and energy densities are regularized by subtracting a Taylor expansion in the magnetic field B up to order 2 (for scalar densities) or order 4 (for energy densities), evaluated at zero baryonic density. The self-consistent effective nucleon mass, pressure, and magnetization are then computed at zero temperature for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- At B = 10^19 G, the difference in effective nucleon mass between calculations with and without vacuum corrections is 2.7 MeV at zero density, 2.2 MeV at n/n0 = 1, and 1.9 MeV at n/n0 = 2, where n0 is normal nuclear density.
- At B = 1.6 x 10^19 G, the difference between calculations with and without AMM grows from 10 to 25 MeV depending on density.
- The vacuum correction to the proton component of the magnetization reaches a value of 10^-5 at B = 10^19 G, representing approximately 10% of the MFA result at density n/n0 = 0.2 and 1% at n/n0 = 1.2.
- For intensities below 5 x 10^18 G, the effective mass results with and without vacuum corrections are very similar.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear matter in strong magnetic fields by including both the Dirac sea vacuum contributions and the anomalous magnetic moments of protons and neutrons in a coherent manner, addressing limitations of earlier approaches by Haber (2014) which neglected AMM, and by Mukherjee (2014) which used a low-field expansion without Landau quantization. The results support the validity of the standard MFA for densities and magnetic intensities relevant to neutron star physics, while providing a regularization procedure that yields finite vacuum corrections to the magnetization and magnetic susceptibility.
