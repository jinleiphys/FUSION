# Massive quark propagator and competition between chiral and diquark condensate

> The Green-function approach has been extended to the moderate baryon density region in the framework of an extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, and the thermodynamic potential with both chiral and diquark condensates has been evaluated by using the massive quark propagator. The phase structure along the chemical potential direction has been investigated and the strong competition between the chiral and diquark condensate has been analyzed by investigating the influence of the diquark condensate on the sharp Fermi surface. The influence of the diquark condensate on the quark properties has been investigated, even though the quarks in the color breaking phase are very different from that in the chiral breaking phase, the difference between quarks in different colors is very small.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Green-function approach to moderate baryon density using an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, showing that the diquark condensate smooths the Fermi surface and thereby induces chiral symmetry restoration at a smaller chemical potential than would occur without diquark condensation, but only for specific ratios of the coupling constants \(G_D/G_S\).

## Method

The authors evaluate the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature using the Nambu-Gorkov massive quark propagator, derived via energy projectors for massive particles, in an extended NJL model that includes both scalar quark-antiquark and scalar diquark interaction channels. They solve the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass \(m\) and the diquark gap \(\Delta\) as functions of chemical potential \(\mu\) for several values of the ratio \(G_D/G_S\), and compute the chiral condensates separately for quarks participating in the diquark condensate (first two colors) and the third color quark that does not.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass in vacuum: \(m(\mu=0) = 330\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), momentum cut-off \(\Lambda_f = 0.637\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and coupling constant \(G_S = 5.32\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
* For \(G_D/G_S = 2/3\), the chiral and color superconductivity phase transitions both occur at \(\mu_{\chi} \simeq \mu_{\Delta} = 340\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and are first order.
* The diquark gap \(\Delta\) is plotted for \(G_D/G_S = 2/3,\ 1,\ 1.2,\ 1.5\) as a function of \(\mu\) and of scaled baryon density \(n_b/n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density).
* The excitation spectrum is shown at \(\mu = 500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\Delta/\mu = 0,\ 0.2,\ 0.5,\ 1\).

## Context

This work extends the Green-function approach of Rischke (2000) and Rapp (2000) from asymptotic densities to the moderate baryon density region, where the quark mass cannot be treated perturbatively as in Schafer (2000). It builds on earlier NJL and instanton model studies by Klevansky (1993), Berges (1999), and Vanderheyden (2000) that found strong competition between chiral and diquark condensates for small \(G_D/G_S\) and a mixed broken phase for larger ratios. The results enable future investigation of the phase diagram at densities relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, and clarify the role of the diquark condensate in smearing the Fermi surface and lowering the critical chemical potential for chiral restoration.
