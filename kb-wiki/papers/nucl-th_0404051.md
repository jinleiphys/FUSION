# Alpha Decay Hindrance Factors: A Probe of Mean Field Wave Functions

> A simple model to calculate alpha-decay Hindrance Factors is presented. Using deformation values obtained from PES calculations as the only input, Hindrance Factors for the alpha-decay of Rn- and Po-isotopes are calculated. It is found that the intrinsic structure around the Fermi surface determined by the deformed mean field plays an important role in determining the hindrance of alpha-decay. The fair agreement between experimental and theoretical Hindrance Factors suggest that the wave function obtained from the energy minima of the PES calculations contains an important part of the correlations that play a role for the alpha-decay. The calculated HF that emerges from these calculations render a different interpretation than the commonly assumed n-particle n-hole picture.

## Key claim
The paper presents a simple model for calculating alpha-decay Hindrance Factors (HF) using only deformation values from Potential Energy Surface (PES) calculations, finding that the mean-field wave function captures essential correlations for alpha decay, but the agreement is qualified as "fair" and the model works best only for cases with well-separated energy minima in the PES.

## Method
The authors calculate Hindrance Factors as the ratio of reduced alpha-decay widths for ground-state-to-ground-state versus ground-state-to-excited-state decays, using the Thouless theorem to connect HFB vacuum states with different deformations. The formation amplitude is expressed as a product of proton and neutron pair-transfer amplitudes, evaluated using the Onishi formula with deformation parameters from Woods-Saxon PES calculations as the sole input. The model is applied to zero-angular-momentum (ΔL=0) transitions in even-even nuclei, specifically for decays of Po and Rn isotopes to Pb and Po daughters.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of alpha-decay Hindrance Factors that used a two-level mixing model based on the spherical shell model (Kh 1987; Dup 1990, 2000) and a microscopic RPA-based model (Del 1996). The authors present a mean-field alternative where deformation is derived microscopically rather than treated as a free parameter. The results challenge the commonly assumed n-particle n-hole (np-nh) picture, showing that both protons and neutrons contribute similarly to the Hindrance Factor, and that the decay from spherical to oblate states is not forbidden as the np-nh model would suggest. The approach enables future work using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to account for wave function mixing at different deformations, as attempted by Dug (2003) for axially deformed states.
