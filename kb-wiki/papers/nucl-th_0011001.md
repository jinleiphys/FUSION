# Isospin effects on rotational flow in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> Within the framework of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, the rotational flow in reactions of $^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Fe and $^{58}$% Ni + $^{58}$Ni at 40 MeV/nucleon for different impact parameters is investigated by analyzing the mid-rapidity azimuthal distribution. The rotational observables are also calculated semiquantitatively. For the first time, it is found that the more neutron-rich system ($^{58}$Fe + $^{58}$Fe) exhibits stronger rotational collective flow. This isospin dependence of rotational collective flow is more appreciable in semi-peripheral collisions and it is shown to mainly result from the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross section rather than the symmetry energy. Meanwhile, it is indicated that the rotational flow depends strongly on the impact parameter.

# Isospin effects on rotational flow in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Using an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, the authors find for the first time that the more neutron-rich system (58Fe + 58Fe) exhibits stronger rotational collective flow than the more symmetric system (58Ni + 58Ni) at 40 MeV/nucleon, but this isospin dependence is shown to result mainly from the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross section rather than from the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed IQMD model simulations of 58Fe + 58Fe and 58Ni + 58Ni collisions at 40 MeV/nucleon for impact parameters from central to peripheral, using a soft equation of state with incompressibility K = 200 MeV and symmetry strength C = 32 MeV. They analyzed the mid-rapidity azimuthal distribution dN/dphi of all nucleons, fitting it with a second-order Legendre polynomial to extract the rotational flow coefficient a2, and also calculated classical rotational observables (angular momentum, moment of inertia, angular velocity, rotational energy) for mid-rapidity nucleons. To isolate the origin of the isospin dependence, they compared results using C = 0 versus C = 32 MeV and using isospin-independent Cugnon cross sections versus isospin-dependent experimental cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Rotational flow coefficient a2 = 0.20151 for 58Fe + 58Fe and a2 = 0.18047 for 58Ni + 58Ni at 40 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 6 fm (mid-rapidity nucleons)
- Angular momentum per nucleon for the whole system changes from 1.388 hbar/nucleon to 1.348 hbar/nucleon for 58Fe + 58Fe, and from 1.388 hbar/nucleon to 1.343 hbar/nucleon for 58Ni + 58Ni over the full reaction time at b = 4 fm
- Simulations used 200 events per impact parameter, with results averaged over the time interval from 200 to 300 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of azimuthal distributions and collective flow in heavy ion collisions, particularly the work of Tsang et al. (1984) on the sensitivity of azimuthal distributions to the nuclear equation of state and Soff et al. (1995) showing that rotational flow can persist even when directed flow has disappeared. The study builds on the authors' own previous development of the IQMD model (Chen et al., 1998) and enables future experimental investigations using radioactive nuclear beams to probe isospin-dependent reaction dynamics, with the rotational flow coefficient a2 suggested as a potential observable sensitive to the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
