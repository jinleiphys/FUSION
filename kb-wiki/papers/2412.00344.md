# Spectra of $Λ$ and $Σ$ Baryons under Screened Potential

> The light, strange baryons have been studied through various approaches and attempted to be looked for rigorously in experiments. The screened potential has been applied to heavy baryon sector as well as meson systems in earlier works. Here, this article attempts to compare the results for linear and screened potential for light strange baryons. Also, the Regge trajectories depict the linear nature.

## Key claim
The paper claims that applying a screened confining potential (rather than the usual linear potential) within the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) yields mass spectra for singly strange Lambda and Sigma baryons that are comparable to experimental data, but with the important qualifier that for higher excited states (1F, 1G) the screened-potential masses are under-predicted by more than 100 MeV relative to experiment and to linear-potential results.

## Method
The authors use a non-relativistic hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) with Jacobi coordinates, where the three-quark interaction depends only on the hyperradius x. The confining potential is taken as the screened form V_conf(x)=a(1-e^{-mu x})/mu with string tension a and screening parameter mu=0.3 (chosen following Chaturvedi for mesons), combined with a color-Coulomb term V_Col(x)=-(2/3)(alpha_s/x) and a spin-dependent potential including spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. Constituent quark masses are m_u=m_d=0.290 GeV and m_s=0.500 GeV. The Schrodinger equation is solved numerically to obtain mass spectra for Lambda and Sigma baryons up to high orbital and radial excitations, and results are compared with experimental PDG values and with earlier linear-potential calculations by the same group.

## Key numbers
- Lambda ground state (1S, J^P=1/2^+): screened mass = 1115 MeV, linear mass = 1115 MeV, experimental = 1115 MeV
- Lambda first excited state (2S, J^P=1/2^+): screened mass = 1553 MeV, linear mass = 1589 MeV, experimental = 1600 MeV
- Lambda (1^2P_3/2, J^P=3/2^-): screened mass = 1549 MeV, linear mass = 1544 MeV, experimental = 1520 MeV
- Lambda (1^2D_5/2, J^P=5/2^+): screened mass = 1755 MeV, linear mass = 1767 MeV, experimental = 1820 MeV
- Lambda (1^2G_9/2, J^P=9/2^+): screened mass = 2179 MeV, linear mass = 2246 MeV, experimental = 2350 MeV
- Sigma ground state (1S, J^P=1/2^+): screened mass = 1193 MeV, linear mass = 1193 MeV, experimental = 1193 MeV
- Sigma (1^2P_3/2, J^P=3/2^-): screened mass = 1669 MeV, linear mass = 1702 MeV, experimental = 1670 MeV
- Sigma (1^2D_5/2, J^P=5/2^+): screened mass = 1952 MeV, linear mass = 2013 MeV, experimental = 1915 MeV
- Constituent quark masses: m_u=m_d=0.290 GeV, m_s=0.500 GeV
- Screening parameter mu = 0.3

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies using linear potential for octet and decuplet baryons (Menapara and Rai, earlier papers) and applies the screened potential previously used for heavy baryons and meson systems (Chaturvedi, earlier work) to light strange baryons. The results are compared with experimental PDG data and with other theoretical models including the relativistic quark model of Faustov (earlier work), the algebraic model of Bijker (earlier work), the work of Melde (earlier work), and the model of Chen (earlier work). The paper enables future experimental searches at facilities like PANDA for higher excited strange baryon states, particularly those with one- or two-star PDG status, by providing mass predictions and spin-parity assignments from Regge trajectories.
