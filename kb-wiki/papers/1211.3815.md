# Critical exponents of the quark-gluon bags model with the critical endpoint

> The critical indices α', β, γ' and δof the Quark Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model that has the critical endpoint are calculated and compared with the exponents of other models. These indices are expressed in terms of the most general parameters of the model. Despite the usual expectations the found critical indices do not depend on the Fisher exponent τand on the parameter \varkappa which relates the mean bag surface to its volume. The scaling relations for the obtained critical exponents are verified and it is demonstrated that for the standard definition of the index α' the Fisher and the Griffiths scaling inequalities are not fulfilled in general case, whereas the Liberman scaling inequality is always obeyed. This is not surprising for the phase diagram with the asymmetric properties of pure phases, but the present model also provides us with the first and explicit example that the specially defined index α'_s does not recover the scaling relations as well. Therefore, here we suggest the physically motivated definition of the index α' = α'_c and demonstrate that such a definition recovers the Fisher scaling inequality, while it is shown that the Griffiths inequality should be generalized for the phase diagram with the asymmetric properties. The critical exponents of several systems that belong to different universality classes are successfully described by the parameters of the present model and hence its equation of state can be used for a variety of practical applications.

## Key claim

The critical exponents of the Quark Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model that has a critical endpoint are expressed in terms of the most general parameters of the model and, contrary to usual expectations, do not depend on the Fisher exponent τ or on the parameter ϰ relating mean bag surface to volume; however, the Fisher and Griffiths scaling inequalities are not fulfilled for the standard definition of the index α′ in the general case, and even the specially defined index α′_s does not recover the scaling relations, requiring a new physically motivated definition α′ = α′_c.

## Method

The authors calculate the critical exponents α′, β, γ′, and δ of the Quark Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model (QGBSTM2) by analyzing the singularities of the isobaric partition function near the critical endpoint. The model uses a mass-volume spectrum with a discrete hadron part and a continuous part parameterized by the Fisher exponent τ and a surface tension coefficient that changes sign at the phase coexistence curve, and the exponents are derived from the temperature and chemical potential dependence of thermodynamic quantities such as specific heat, density discontinuity, isothermal compressibility, and the critical isotherm.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent α′ is given by α′ = 2 − 2 min(1, 1/ξ^T), where ξ^T > 0 parameterizes the shape of the phase coexistence curve near the critical endpoint; α′ > 0 only for ξ^T > 1, otherwise α′ = 0.
- The exponent β is given by β = β^± ≡ ζ^± + ξ^± − 1/ξ^T for ζ^− > ζ^+ = 1, or β = min(β^+, β^−) for ζ^− = ζ^+ = 1, with ζ^± ≥ 1 and ξ^± > 0.
- The exponent γ′ is given by γ′ = 1/ξ^T − β.
- The Fisher exponent τ must satisfy τ > 2 for the first-order deconfinement phase transition to exist, ensuring the integrals I_{τ−1}(0,0) and I_{τ−ϰ}(0,0) remain finite.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical exponents from the earlier Quark Gluon Bags with Surface Tension Model (QGBSTM1) with a tricritical endpoint (Bugaev 2007) and the Fisher-Felderhof one-dimensional model (Fisher 1970), where scaling inequalities were also found to be broken. The results enable the equation of state of the QGBSTM2 to be used for practical applications by fixing its parameters to reproduce the critical exponents of universality classes relevant to QCD, such as the 3-D Ising model for three degenerate quark flavors or the O(4) symmetric 3-dimensional spin model for 2+1 quark flavors.
