# Spin Alignment of Vector Mesons in Non-central A+A Collisions

> We discuss the consequence of global polarization of the produced quarks in non-central heavy-ion collisions on the spin alignment of vector mesons. We show that the alignment is quite different for different hadronization scenarios. These results can be tested directly by measuring the vector mesons' alignment through angular distributions of the decay products with respect to the reaction plane. Such angular distributions will give rise to azimuthal anisotropy $v_2$ of the decay products in the collision frame. Constraints provided by the data on the azimuthal anisotropy of hadron spectra at RHIC points to a quark recombination scenario of hadronization.

# Spin Alignment of Vector Mesons in Non-central A+A Collisions

## Key claim

The spin alignment of vector mesons produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions depends strongly on the hadronization scenario, with quark recombination yielding ρ₀₀ < 1/3 and quark fragmentation yielding ρ₀₀ > 1/3; the existing pion azimuthal anisotropy data from RHIC favor the recombination scenario (ρ₀₀ < 1/3) but do not provide stringent constraints on the exact value.

## Method

The authors calculate the spin density matrix elements ρ₀₀ for vector mesons (ρ and K*) by assuming globally polarized quarks and anti-quarks in the quark-gluon plasma formed in non-central A+A collisions, where the global orbital angular momentum of the partonic system produces quark polarization via spin-orbital coupling in parton scattering. They consider three hadronization scenarios: (1) recombination of two polarized quarks/anti-quarks, (2) recombination of one polarized quark with one unpolarized quark, and (3) fragmentation of a polarized quark, using a direct-product construction of the quark and anti-quark spin density matrices transformed to the coupled basis. The resulting angular distributions of decay products are then related to the azimuthal anisotropy v₂ of final pions through Lorentz transformation and a phenomenological pT-dependent ansatz for ρ₀₀.

## Key numbers

- Global quark polarization from elastic scattering in an effective static potential model: Pq = -(π/4) μ p / [E(E+mq)], where μ is the Debye screening mass, E and p are the quark energy and momentum in the parton scattering center-of-mass frame
- For ρ mesons from recombination of similarly polarized quarks and anti-quarks: ρ₀₀^(ρ(rec)) = (1 - Pq²) / (3 + Pq²)
- For K* mesons from recombination: ρ₀₀^(K*(rec)) = (1 - Pq Ps) / (3 + Pq Ps)
- For ρ mesons from fragmentation of polarized quarks: ρ₀₀^(ρ(frag)) = (1 + β Pq²) / (3 - β Pq²), with β ≈ 0.5 obtained from fitting LEP e⁺e⁻ data
- The pT scale at which quark polarization vanishes is set to a₀ = 0.5 GeV/c in the ansatz ρ₀₀(pT) = ρ₀₀⁰ + (1/3 - ρ₀₀⁰)(2/π) tan⁻¹(pT/a₀)
- The ρ/π multiplicity ratio used is 0.183, measured in peripheral Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV
- The effective temperature for low-pT vector meson mT distribution is T = 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier prediction of global quark polarization in non-central heavy-ion collisions (Liang and Wang, 2004) to the spin alignment of vector mesons, complementing their previous work on global hyperon polarization. The spin alignment measurement does not require knowledge of the reaction plane direction (unlike hyperon polarization), making it experimentally more accessible. The results connect to measurements of vector meson spin density matrices in e⁺e⁻ collisions at LEP (DELPHI, OPAL, and L3 collaborations) and to the extensive program of elliptic flow measurements at RHIC (PHENIX experiment). The work enables direct experimental tests of hadronization mechanisms in different kinematic regions of heavy-ion collisions through angular distribution measurements of vector meson decay products.
