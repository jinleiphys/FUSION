# Anisotropic flow background to jet-correlation relative to reaction plane: A revisit of the mathematical framework

> We derive the flow-background formula for jet-correlation analysis with high pT trigger particles in any azimuth window relative to reaction plane, extending the mathematic framework of previous study in [J. Bielcikova, et al., Phys. Rev. C 69 (2004) 021901(R)].

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized flow-background subtraction formula for jet-correlation analyses that use high-\(p_T\) trigger particles selected in *any* azimuthal window relative to the reaction plane, extending the previous framework of Bielcikova et al. (2004) which was limited to trigger particles summed over all four quadrants.

## Method

The authors start from the standard Fourier expansion of the single-particle azimuthal distribution in terms of harmonic coefficients \(v_k\) and the reaction plane angle \(\psi_{RP}\). They then integrate the trigger-associated particle pair distribution over a finite trigger azimuthal slice of half-width \(c\) centered at an angle \(\phis\) relative to the measured event plane \(\psi_{EP}\), incorporating the event-plane resolution via a smearing function \(\rho(\Delta\psi)\). The resulting expression is normalized by the number of trigger particles in that slice to yield the final correlation function, and an explicit approximation is given keeping terms up to \(v_2v_4\) and \(v_6\).

## Key numbers

* Elliptic flow magnitudes used for illustration: \(\vt{2}=0.10\) and \(v_2=0.05\), with higher harmonics set as \(\vt{4}=(\vt{2})^2\), \(\vt{6}=(\vt{2})^3\), \(v_4=v_2^2\), and \(v_6=v_2^3\).
* Event plane resolutions taken as \(\langle\cos 2\Delta\psi\rangle = \langle\cos 4\Delta\psi\rangle = \langle\cos 6\Delta\psi\rangle = 1\) (perfect reconstruction) for the illustrative calculation.
* Trigger azimuthal slice parameters used in the example: \(\phis=22.5^\circ\) and half-width \(c=7.5^\circ\) (i.e., trigger range \(15^\circ < \phi_t < 30^\circ\)).

## Context

This work extends the mathematical framework of Bielcikova et al. (2004) for flow-background subtraction in jet-correlation analyses. The new formula is needed to test predictions from Chiu and Hwa (2005) that the ridge phenomenon and asymmetric correlation functions arise from interactions between radiated gluons and medium flow, where trigger particles must be separated into different quadrants relative to the reaction plane. The result enables experimental studies of jet-medium interactions in non-central heavy-ion collisions, particularly the asymmetric correlation functions predicted for trigger particles in quadrants I+III versus II+IV.
