# Quarkonia and Deconfined Quark-Gluon Matter in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In this report, we present an experimental overview of quarkonium results obtained in nucleus-nucleus collisions, with a focus on the data collected at the LHC. We discuss the current understanding of charmonium and bottomonium behavior in the deconfined medium produced in such collisions, comparing the various observables now accessible to state-of-the-art theoretical models. We also discuss the open points and how future heavy-ion experiments aim to clarify these aspects.

## Key claim
This experimental overview argues that while quarkonium suppression was originally proposed as a unambiguous signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, the current understanding requires a more nuanced picture where (re)generation mechanisms partially balance suppression, particularly at LHC energies where about 120 charm-anticharm pairs are produced on average in central collisions.

## Method
The authors review experimental data on charmonium (J/ψ, ψ(2S)) and bottomonium (Υ(1S), Υ(2S), Υ(3S)) production in nucleus-nucleus collisions from SPS through RHIC to LHC energies, comparing measurements of the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 against three classes of theoretical models: the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHMc), semiclassical transport models (exemplified by TAMU), and open-quantum systems approaches (Munich-KSU, Nantes). The comparison focuses on Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV at the LHC and Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV at RHIC.

## Key numbers
- Number of charm-anticharm pairs produced on average in a central Pb-Pb collision: about 0.2 at top SPS energy (√s_NN = 17.3 GeV), about 10 at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV), and about 120 at top LHC energy (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV)
- At midrapidity in central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: about 16 charm-anticharm pairs per unit rapidity, of which about 0.7% are measured in J/ψ mesons
- J/ψ mass: 3.097 GeV; ψ(2S) mass: 3.686 GeV; Υ(1S) mass: 9.46 GeV; Υ(2S) mass: 10.02 GeV; Υ(3S) mass: 10.36 GeV
- Binding energy proxy Δm: J/ψ = 0.633 GeV, ψ(2S) = 0.044 GeV, Υ(1S) = 1.1 GeV, Υ(2S) = 0.54 GeV, Υ(3S) = 0.21 GeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from LQCD: T_pc = 156-158 MeV; SHMc uses T_cf ≈ 157 ± 2 MeV
- Fireball volume for most central 10% Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: V = 4997 ± 455 fm^3
- Charm fugacity factor g_c ≈ 31.5 for central Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Inclusive charm production cross section dσ_ccbar/dy: SHMc uses 0.68 ± 0.10 mb, TAMU model uses 0.93 ± 0.12 mb
- TAMU transport model predicts J/ψ from (re)generation reaches about 80% of total yield in central collisions, with initially-produced J/ψ contributing only about 5%
- For mid-central collisions with ⟨N_part⟩ ≈ 100, TAMU predicts J/ψ yield from (re)generation equals the primordial yield
- J/ψ to D^0 yield ratio (in percent) as a function of centrality is described by SHMc
- Reaction rates for J/ψ and Υ(1S) at zero momentum show strong temperature dependence, with rates for excited states larger than for ground states
- For central Pb-Pb (0-10%) at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, temperature decreases from about 600 MeV at τ ≈ 0.5 fm/c to T_pc in about 15 fm/c

## Context
This review extends the experimental program initiated by Matsui and Satz (1986) who proposed quarkonium suppression as a signature of QGP formation, following the SPS measurements by NA38, NA50, and NA60 that established anomalous J/ψ suppression at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV. The paper builds on earlier reviews by Kluberg and Braun-Munzinger (2009) and Mocsy, Sorensen, and Bass (2013) and directly compares data to the comprehensive model inter-comparison of Andronic et al. (2024). The results enable future heavy-ion experiments at higher luminosities and energies to resolve the current mismatch between SHMc and transport model inputs for the inclusive charm production cross section, and to clarify whether the ψ(2S) suppression observed in p-Pb collisions indicates partonic or hadronic final-state effects in high-multiplicity small systems.
