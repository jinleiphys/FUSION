# Jastrow-type calculations of one-nucleon removal reactions on open $s$-$d$ shell nuclei

> Single-particle overlap functions and spectroscopic factors are calculated on the basis of Jastrow-type one-body density matrices of open-shell nuclei constructed by using a factor cluster expansion. The calculations use the relationship between the overlap functions corresponding to bound states of the $(A-1)$-particle system and the one-body density matrix for the ground state of the $A$-particle system. In this work we extend our previous analyses of reactions on closed-shell nuclei by using the resulting overlap functions for the description of the cross sections of $(p,d)$ reactions on the open $s$-$d$ shell nuclei $^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si and $^{32}$S and of $^{32}$S$(e,e^{\prime}p)$ reaction. The relative role of both shell structure and short-range correlations incorporated in the correlation approach on the spectroscopic factors and the reaction cross sections is pointed out.

# Jastrow-type calculations of one-nucleon removal reactions on open $s$-$d$ shell nuclei

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed Jastrow-correlation method for calculating overlap functions and spectroscopic factors from closed-shell nuclei to open $s$-$d$ shell nuclei ($^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si, $^{32}$S), and shows that the resulting overlap functions can reproduce absolute cross sections of $(p,d)$ and $(e,e^{\prime}p)$ reactions without additional normalization, though the authors note this is an initial attempt and the harmonic-oscillator basis used for the one-body density matrices does not have the correct exponential asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors construct correlated one-body density matrices (OBDMs) for open-shell nuclei using a factor cluster expansion of Clark and co-workers with a Jastrow-type correlation function $f(r_{ij})=1-\exp[-\beta ({\bf r}_i-{\bf r}_j)^2]$, starting from harmonic-oscillator single-particle wave functions. From these OBDMs, bound-state overlap functions and spectroscopic factors for neutron and proton quasihole states are extracted using the relationship between the OBDM of the $A$-particle system and the overlap functions of the $(A-1)$-particle system. These overlap functions are then used as bound-state form factors in DWBA calculations of $^{24}$Mg$(p,d)$, $^{28}$Si$(p,d)$, $^{32}$S$(p,d)$ reactions at 185 MeV and $^{32}$S$(e,e^{\prime}p)$ reactions, using the DWUCK4 and DWEEPY codes respectively.

- Spectroscopic factor for the $2s_{1/2}$ proton state in $^{32}$S used in $(e,e^{\prime}p)$ calculations: 0.5648
- Incident proton energy for $(p,d)$ reactions: $E_p$ = 185 MeV
- For comparison, HF calculations from Amos (1978) give $1d_{5/2}$ fractional occupancies of 0.479 for $^{24}$Mg and 0.682 for $^{28}$Si
- The $2s_{1/2}$ proton occupancy of 0.67(9) for $^{32}$S was reported by Wesseling (1997)
- For closed-shell $^{40}$Ca within the same Jastrow approach: spectroscopic factors of 0.892 for the $1d$ state and 0.956 for the $2s$ state (from Stoitsov (1996) using LOA)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $(p,d)$ and $(e,e^{\prime}p)$ reactions on closed-shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca (Di Marzio (1997), Gaidarov (1999), Ivanov (2001)) to open $s$-$d$ shell nuclei, using the Jastrow-type OBDM formalism developed by Moustakidis (2000) and Massen (1999). The approach contrasts with standard DWBA calculations that require phenomenological spectroscopic factors as fitting parameters; here the overlap functions already contain the correlation-induced reduction of spectroscopic strength. The results enable future systematic studies of short-range correlation effects in open-shell nuclei, though the authors note that state-dependent correlations (particularly tensor correlations) and long-range correlations should be included in a consistent way, and that Woods-Saxon single-particle bases would improve the asymptotic behavior compared to the harmonic-oscillator basis used here.
