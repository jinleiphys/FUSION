# Neutron Star Structure and the Neutron Radius of 208Pb

> We study relationships between the neutron-rich skin of a heavy nucleus and the properties of neutron-star crusts. Relativistic effective field theories with a thicker neutron skin in $^{208}$Pb have a larger electron fraction and a lower liquid-to-solid transition density for neutron-rich matter. These properties are determined by the density dependence of the symmetry energy which we vary by adding nonlinear couplings between isoscalar and isovector mesons. An accurate measurement of the neutron radius in $^{208}$Pb---via parity violating electron scattering---may have important implications for the structure of neutron stars.

# Neutron Star Structure and the Neutron Radius of 208Pb

## Key claim

An accurate measurement of the neutron radius in 208Pb via parity-violating electron scattering (the PREX experiment) can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which in turn determines both the neutron-skin thickness and the liquid-to-solid transition density in neutron-star crusts. However, the neutron-star radius is not uniquely constrained by the skin thickness alone because the skin depends only on the equation of state at normal and lower densities, while the stellar radius also depends on the high-density equation of state.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic effective field theories with nonlinear couplings between isoscalar (sigma and omega) and isovector (rho) meson fields, specifically adding Lambda_v (omega-rho) and Lambda_4 (sigma-rho) couplings. They adjusted the NNrho coupling constant to maintain a fixed symmetry energy of 23.50 MeV at an average Fermi momentum of k_F = 1.15 fm^{-1}, then computed the neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb and the transition density for uniform neutron-rich matter using a random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculation of the longitudinal dielectric function.

## Key numbers

- The NL3 parameter set predicts a neutron-skin thickness R_n - R_p = 0.280 fm in 208Pb, with a transition density rho_c = 0.052 fm^{-3}
- Increasing Lambda_v from 0 to 0.025 reduces the skin thickness from 0.280 fm to 0.209 fm, while the transition density rises from 0.052 fm^{-3} to 0.081 fm^{-3}
- The approximate linear relation between transition density and skin thickness is rho_c ≈ 0.16 - 0.39 (R_n - R_p), with rho_c in fm^{-3} and skin thickness in fm
- The nonrelativistic equation of state of Friedman and Pandharipande gives rho_c = 0.096 fm^{-3} and R_n - R_p = 0.16 ± 0.02 fm (Brown 2000)
- For the TM1 interaction (Sugahara and Toki 1994), the authors calculate rho_c ≈ 0.059 fm^{-3} and R_n - R_p = 0.27 fm
- A neutron-skin thickness of 0.24 fm or larger suggests that the electron fraction Y_e becomes large enough to allow direct URCA cooling for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star

## Context

This work extends the study of relationships between finite-nuclei observables and neutron-star properties, building on earlier relativistic mean-field models such as NL3 (Lalazissis, Konig, and Ring 1997) and the nonrelativistic microscopic equation of state of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981). The authors show that the neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb is not tightly constrained by existing nuclear observables such as charge densities and binding energies, but a PREX measurement can provide a unique observational constraint. The results enable future combined measurements of the neutron skin and neutron-star radii to constrain the equation of state at both low and high densities, potentially revealing phase transitions in dense matter.
