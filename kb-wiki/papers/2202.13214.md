# Non-iterative finite amplitude methods for E1 and M1 giant resonances

> The finite amplitude method (FAM) is a very efficient approach for solving the fully self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) equations. We use FAM to rederive the RPA matrices for general Skyrme-like functionals, calculate the electric dipole (E1) and the magnetic dipole (M1) giant resonances, and compare the results with available experimental and evaluated data. For the E1 transitions in heavy nuclei, the calculations reproduce well the resonance energy of the photoabsorption cross sections. In the case of M1 transitions, we show that the residual interaction does not affect the transition strength of double-magic nuclei, which suggests that the spin terms in the Skyrme force currently neglected in the present computation could improve the agreement between FAM and experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors derive explicit, non-iterative RPA matrix expressions for Skyrme functionals using the finite amplitude method, showing that for M1 transitions in double-magic nuclei the residual interaction does not affect the transition strength, which implies that spin terms in the Skyrme force (neglected in this computation) are needed to improve agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors analytically linearize the time-dependent Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian for general Skyrme-like functionals by expanding the single-particle states in a small parameter η, then take the limit η→0 to obtain explicit expressions for the RPA matrices A and B. Instead of solving the FAM equations iteratively, they construct the full RPA matrices from these analytic derivatives and invert them directly to obtain the forward and backward amplitudes X and Y for each frequency ω. The method is applied to calculate E1 and M1 giant resonances in spherical nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (Nakatsukasa 2007, Inakura 2009) by providing an explicit non-iterative matrix formulation for Skyrme functionals, avoiding the iterative solution typically used in FAM-RPA calculations. The approach enables direct comparison with experimental giant resonance data and evaluated nuclear data libraries, and the authors note that the missing spin terms in the Skyrme force could improve M1 predictions. The method is designed to be computationally efficient enough to eventually handle deformed superfluid nuclei via QRPA, building on prior FAM extensions to QRPA (Avogadro 2011, Stoitsov 2011) and to beta-decay calculations (Mustonen 2014).
