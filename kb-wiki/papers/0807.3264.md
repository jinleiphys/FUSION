# Mutual friction in a cold color flavor locked superfluid and r-mode instabilities in compact stars

> Dissipative processes acting in rotating neutron stars are essential in preventing the growth of the r-mode instability. We estimate the damping time of r-modes of an hypothetical compact quark star made up by color flavor locked quark matter at a temperature $T \lesssim 0.01$ MeV. The dissipation that we consider is due to the the mutual friction force between the normal and the superfluid component arising from the elastic scattering of phonons with quantized vortices. This process is the dominant one for temperatures $T \lesssim 0.01$ MeV where the mean free path of phonons due to their self-interactions is larger than the radius of the star and they can be described as an ideal bosonic gas. We find that r-modes oscillations are efficiently damped by this mechanism for pulsars rotating at frequencies of the order of 1 Hz at most. Our analysis rules out the possibility that cold pulsars rotating at higher frequencies are entirely made up by color flavor locked quark matter.

## Key claim

The mutual friction force from elastic scattering of phonons off quantized vortices can efficiently damp r-mode oscillations in cold CFL quark stars, but only for pulsars rotating at frequencies of order 1 Hz at most; this rules out the possibility that cold pulsars rotating at higher frequencies are entirely made of CFL quark matter.

## Method

The authors computed the mutual friction force between the superfluid and normal components in rotating CFL quark matter by analyzing the elastic scattering of superfluid phonons off quantized vortex lines, using gravity analogue models to derive the phonon-vortex cross sections. They then calculated the dissipation time scale for r-mode oscillations from this mutual friction and compared it with the gravitational radiation growth time scale to determine the critical rotation frequency for r-mode stability.

## Key numbers

- For a canonical star with mass \(M=1.4 M_\odot\) and radius \(R=12\) km, the critical rotation frequency is \(\nu_c \simeq 2\times 10^5 (T/\mu)\) Hz; for \(T=0.01\) MeV and \(\mu=400\) MeV, this gives \(\nu_c \simeq 1\) Hz.
- The dissipation time scale for negligible vortex mass (\(x \ll 1\)) is \(1/\tau_{MF} = 2\alpha \Omega \simeq 2 \times 18.1 (T/\mu)^5 \Omega\).
- The phonon mean free path from self-interactions scales as \(\ell \sim \mu^4/T^5\) and exceeds the neutron star radius (about 10 km) for \(T \lesssim 0.01\) MeV.
- The vortex mass estimate gives \(x \lesssim 10^{-17}\) for a superconducting gap \(\Delta \gtrsim 10\) MeV, making the vortex mass effect negligible.

## Context

This work extends the study of r-mode damping in CFL quark matter beyond the phonon self-interaction processes considered by Manuel (2004), which were found to be inefficient at low temperatures. The analysis parallels mutual friction studies in conventional neutron stars by Lindblom (1999) and Andersson (2005), where electron scattering off magnetic fields in neutron vortex cores provides damping. The results rule out cold rapidly rotating pulsars as pure CFL quark stars, suggesting instead that such objects may be in the crystalline color superconducting phase, which has gapless quarks and a large shear modulus as discussed by Casalbuoni (2005) and Mannarelli (2007).
