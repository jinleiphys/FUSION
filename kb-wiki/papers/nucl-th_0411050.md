# Energy Spectra of Anti-nucleons in Finite Nuclei

> The quantum vacuum in a many-body system of finite nuclei has been investigated within the relativistic Hartree approach which describes the bound states of nucleons and anti-nucleons consistently. The contributions of the Dirac sea to the source terms of the meson-field equations are taken into account up to the one-nucleon loop and one-meson loop. The tensor couplings for the $ω$- and $ρ$-meson are included in the model. The overall nucleon spectra of shell-model states are in agreement with the data. The calculated anti-nucleon spectra in the vacuum differ about 20 -- 30 MeV with and without the tensor-coupling effects.

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree approach that includes Dirac-sea contributions (one-nucleon and one-meson loop) and tensor couplings for the omega- and rho-meson can reproduce observed nucleon shell-model spectra, but the calculated anti-nucleon bound-state energies differ by about 20–30 MeV depending on whether tensor couplings are included or not.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for nucleons and anti-nucleons in finite nuclei using a relativistic Hartree approach, expanding the field operator into nucleon and anti-nucleon components. Meson fields (sigma, omega, rho, and photon) are obtained from Laplace equations whose source terms include contributions from both valence nucleons and the Dirac sea, evaluated via a derivative expansion technique. The model parameters are fitted to properties of spherical nuclei, and the single-particle energies of protons, neutrons, anti-protons, and anti-neutrons are computed for 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb using two parameter sets: RHA1 (without tensor couplings) and RHAT (with tensor couplings for the omega- and rho-meson).

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass: m*/M_N ≈ 0.78
- Binding energy per nucleon in 16O: 8.00 MeV (RHA1), 7.94 MeV (RHAT), experimental 7.98 MeV
- RMS charge radius in 16O: 2.66 fm (RHA1), 2.64 fm (RHAT), experimental 2.74 fm
- Anti-proton 1s_{1/2} energy in 16O: 299.42 MeV (RHA1), 328.55 MeV (RHAT)
- Anti-neutron 1s_{1/2} energy in 16O: 293.23 MeV (RHA1), 322.47 MeV (RHAT)
- Anti-proton 1s_{1/2} energy in 40Ca: 339.83 MeV (RHA1), 367.90 MeV (RHAT)
- Anti-proton 1s_{1/2} energy in 208Pb: 354.18 MeV (RHA1), 377.37 MeV (RHAT)
- Anti-neutron 1s_{1/2} energy in 208Pb: 313.18 MeV (RHA1), 334.39 MeV (RHAT)
- Anti-particle energies with RHAT are 20–30 MeV larger than with RHA1 across all nuclei studied

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approach developed by Mao et al. (1999, 2003) to include tensor couplings for the omega- and rho-meson, building on earlier studies of the quantum vacuum in finite nuclei by Auerbach et al. (1986) and Reinhard et al. (1986). The consistent treatment of both nucleon and anti-nucleon bound states allows determination of the individual scalar and vector potentials S and V, which is essential for predicting anti-nucleon potential depths in medium and for studying anti-matter and anti-nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
