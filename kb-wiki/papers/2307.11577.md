# Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction Constrained by Light Hypernuclei

> Ab initio structure calculations for p-shell hypernuclei have recently become accessible through extensions of nuclear many-body methods, such as the no-core shell model, in combination with hyperon-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory. However, the low-energy constants in these hyperon-nucleon interactions are poorly constraint due to the very limited amount of experimental scattering data available. We present a hyperon-nucleon interaction that is additionally constrained by experimental ground-state and spectroscopic data for selected p-shell hypernuclei and, thus, optimized for hypernuclear structure calculations. We show that the previous overestimation of the hyperon separation energies in the p-shell is remedied and discuss the significantly improved description of the $_Λ$He isotopic chain. We further discuss the uncertainty quantification for hypernuclear observables on the many-body level, obtained through a novel machine-learning tool.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that adjusting only two low-energy constants (LECs) of a leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interaction, using experimental ground-state and spectroscopic data for p-shell hypernuclei as additional constraints, remedies the systematic overestimation of hyperon separation energies seen with interactions fitted only to scattering data. However, the optimization does not allow simultaneous reproduction of the hypertriton separation energy and those of heavier p-shell hypernuclei, and the optimized interaction slightly underbinds the hypertriton.

## Method

The authors performed importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) calculations for p-shell hypernuclei and their parent nuclei, using a chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interaction at N3LO with cutoff 500 MeV, combined with a leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interaction with cutoff 700 MeV. They constructed a grid in the plane of the two dominant low-energy constants (C^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} and C^{ΛΛ}_{3S1}), interpolated the observables, and minimized a chi-squared metric against a selected set of experimental hyperon separation energies and excitation energy differences for 3H, 5He, 7Li, and 9Be. Converged observables and many-body uncertainties were obtained using a neural-network-based extrapolation tool trained on purely nucleonic systems.

## Key numbers

- Original LECs: C^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = -0.0304, C^{ΛΛ}_{3S1} = -0.0022; optimized LECs: C^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = -0.0146, C^{ΛΛ}_{3S1} = 0.0004, with chi-squared = 96.
- Hyperon separation energy B_Λ for 5He: original YN interaction gave 4.726(8) MeV, optimized YN gave 3.348(10) MeV, experiment is 3.12(2) MeV.
- Hyperon separation energy B_Λ for 7Li: original YN gave 6.506(44) MeV, optimized YN gave 5.049(39) MeV, experiment is 5.58(3) MeV.
- Hyperon separation energy B_Λ for 3H: original YN gave 0.095(5) MeV, optimized YN gave -0.092(5) MeV (unbound), experiment is 0.41(23) MeV.
- Hyperon separation energy B_Λ for 9Be: original YN gave 7.11(77) MeV, optimized YN gave 5.69(49) MeV, experiment is 6.59(15) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio hypernuclear structure calculations using the no-core shell model with chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions (Wirth 2018, Wirth 2018 light, Wirth 2018 diss), which showed systematic overbinding of the hyperon in p-shell hypernuclei. By including hypernuclear structure data as additional constraints, analogous to recent approaches in nucleonic interactions (Ekstrom 2015, Jiang 2020, Huether 2020), the authors achieve a significantly improved description of the ΛHe isotopic chain. The neural-network extrapolation tool (Knoell 2023 machine, Wolfgruber 2023 prediction) enables robust uncertainty quantification for hypernuclear observables. This work enables future optimization of higher-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions using hypernuclear data and highlights the need for consistent treatment of nucleonic interaction uncertainties.
