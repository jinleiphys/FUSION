# Bremsstrahlung Spectrum in alpha Decay

> Using our previous approach to electromagnetic emission during tunneling, an explicit, essentially classical, formula describing the bremsstrahlung spectrum in alpha decay is derived. The role of tunneling motion in photon emission is discussed. The shape of the spectrum is a universal function of the ratio Eg/Eo , where Eg is the photon energy and Eo is a characteristic energy depending only on the nuclear charge and the energy of the alpha particle.

# Bremsstrahlung Spectrum in alpha Decay

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung spectrum in alpha decay is described by an explicit, essentially classical formula that is a universal function of the ratio E_gamma/E_0, where E_0 depends only on the nuclear charge and the alpha particle energy; taking the tunneling motion into account is crucial for the correct description, contrary to the claim of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998) that the main contribution stems from Coulomb acceleration alone.

## Method

The author applies a quasiclassical approach developed in Dyakonov and Gornyi (1996) for electromagnetic emission during tunneling, using the Fourier transform of the particle acceleration taken along a contour in the complex time plane that includes the underbarrier region. The Coulomb potential is extended down to r=0, and the resulting integral for the spectrum is evaluated analytically in terms of a dimensionless frequency nu = omega Z e^2 / (E_alpha v), yielding explicit asymptotic forms for both low and high frequencies.

## Key numbers

- For Z=50, E_alpha=4 MeV, the quasiclassical parameter k_i r_c = 100
- For the experimental conditions of Kasagi et al. (1997) (Z=82, E_alpha=5.3 MeV), the characteristic energy E_0 is approximately 75 keV
- For nu >> 1, |J(nu)|^2 = 2.22 nu^(2/3) exp(-2 pi nu)
- For nu << 1, |J(nu)|^2 is approximately 1 - pi nu
- For nu >> 1, |J_coll(nu)|^2 = 3 |J(nu)|^2
- The high-energy cutoff from inner barrier details for Po-210 is approximately 860 keV (based on r_c/r_0 approximately 5)

## Context

This work extends the quasiclassical tunneling radiation formalism of Dyakonov and Gornyi (1996) to provide an explicit analytic formula for alpha decay bremsstrahlung, resolving a controversy between the experimental results of D'Arrigo et al. (1994) and Kasagi et al. (1997). The author shows that the numerical calculations of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998), while correct, were misinterpreted: their neglect of the imaginary part of the acceleration matrix element effectively replaced the alpha decay problem with a head-on collision problem, obscuring the essential role of tunneling. The derived universal spectrum enables direct comparison with future experiments and clarifies the energy range where inner barrier details become relevant.
