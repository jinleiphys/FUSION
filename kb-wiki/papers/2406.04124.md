# Light quark mass dependence of nucleon mass to two-loop order

> We investigate the nucleon self energy through the sixth chiral order in the covariant $SU(2)$ chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) in the single baryon sector. The validity of the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme is explicitly verified to two-loop order, manifested by the miraculous cancellation of all nonlocal divergences and power-counting-breaking (PCB) terms that are nonanalytic in pion mass. Using the $σ_{πN}$ term determined from the latest lattice simulation to constrain some unknown higher-order low energy constants (LECs), we predict the nucleon mass in the chiral limit to be $856.6\pm 1.7$ MeV. It is found that the EOMS scheme exhibits quite satisfactory convergence behavior through ${\cal O}(q^6)$ around physical point. We also predict the pion mass dependence of the nucleon mass to the accuracy of ${\cal O}(q^6)$, which is in satisfactory agreement with the recent lattice results over a wide range of pion mass.

## Key claim

The authors compute the nucleon mass to two-loop order in covariant SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, verifying that the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme remains self-consistent at this order through cancellation of all nonlocal divergences and power-counting-breaking terms. The prediction for the nucleon mass in the chiral limit (856.6 ± 1.7 MeV) depends on using the sigma term from lattice simulations to constrain two unknown higher-order low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors calculate the nucleon self-energy through sixth chiral order (O(q^6)) in covariant SU(2) chiral perturbation theory using the EOMS renormalization scheme. They compute one-loop and two-loop Feynman diagrams, employ dimensional regularization with d=4-2ε, use integration-by-part reduction via the FIRE package, and solve 13 two-loop master integrals using the differential equation method with results expressed in terms of Goncharov polylogarithms. The physical nucleon mass is obtained by locating the pole of the nucleon propagator, with the chiral expansion truncated to O(q^6).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit: 856.6 ± 1.7 MeV
- Physical nucleon mass used as input: (m_p + m_n)/2 = 938.9 MeV
- Pion mass in the chiral limit: M = 141 MeV (obtained from M_π = 139.6 MeV with chiral corrections)
- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: F = 86.6 MeV (obtained from F_π = 92.4 MeV with chiral corrections)
- Axial-vector coupling in the chiral limit: g_A = 1.13
- Fitted contact term contributions: ê_1 M^4 = 2.7 ± 0.2 MeV, ĝ_1 M^6 = 9.1 ± 0.1 MeV
- Physical point sigma term from lattice: 46.9 ± 1.7 MeV (Agadjanov 2023)
- Physical point sigma term from Roy-Steiner equations: 59.1 ± 3.5 MeV (Hoferichter 2015)
- Mesonic LECs from CLQCD collaboration: l_3 = (2.2 ± 0.9) × 10^(-3), l_4 = (3.2 ± 0.8) × 10^(-3)

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of the nucleon mass, which had been computed to O(q^4) in the EOMS scheme by Fuchs (2003) and to O(q^6) in the infrared regularization scheme by Schindler (2006, 2007). The authors compare their O(q^6) predictions with lattice QCD results from the χQCD collaboration (Yang 2018) and sigma term data from Agadjanov (2023), finding that the O(q^6) calculation can describe lattice data over a wider range of pion mass (up to 350-380 MeV) than the O(q^4) or O(q^5) predictions. This enables more reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results and provides the most precise determination of the nucleon mass in the chiral limit.
