# Four-fermion operators, $Z$-boson exchange, and $τ$ lepton dipole moments

> Asymmetry measurements in $e^+e^-\toτ^+τ^-$ constitute a promising avenue to obtain competitive constraints on the $τ$ dipole moments, the anomalous magnetic moment $a_τ$ and the electric dipole moment $d_τ$, especially, once a polarized electron beam becomes available, as possible at a future polarization upgrade of the SuperKEKB collider. While the main challenges concern the measurement of these asymmetries and the calculation of radiative corrections at the relevant level of precision, at subleading orders also electroweak effects and the potential impact of four-fermion operators parameterizing other beyond-the-Standard-Model scenarios besides those described by dipole operators need to be taken into consideration. Here, we show that $Z$-boson contributions arise at the level of $\simeq 3\times 10^{-6}$, while we estimate the largest possible effect from four-fermion operators as $\simeq 10^{-5} C \, v^2/Λ^2$. In addition, we observe that four-fermion-operator insertions at the loop level can probe Wilson coefficients that are otherwise not constrained directly, and that the imaginary part generated by insertions of the dipole operator at loop level opens another potential avenue towards a determination of $a_τ$ without the need for a polarized electron beam. Despite the inherent loop suppression, a measurement of the required normal asymmetry $A_N^\pm$ with a precision of $\lesssim 10^{-5}$ would allow one to probe the Schwinger term, which could define an intermediate goal to be realized in the current setting at Belle II.

# Four-fermion operators, $Z$-boson exchange, and $\tau$ lepton dipole moments

## Key claim

The authors show that $Z$-boson exchange contributes to the transverse-longitudinal asymmetry $\Delta A_{TL}^\pm$ at the level of $\simeq 3 \times 10^{-6}$, and that four-fermion operator effects are suppressed to $\simeq 10^{-5} C \, v^2/\Lambda^2$, confirming that dipole operators remain the dominant BSM contribution to $\tau$ dipole moment measurements in $e^+e^- \to \tau^+\tau^-$ at Belle II energies, provided the BSM scale is not near the electroweak scale.

## Method

The authors compute the complete tree-level $Z$-boson contributions to the spin-dependent asymmetries $A_N^\pm$, $A_L^\pm$, $A_T^\pm$, and their $CP$-odd counterparts in $e^+e^- \to \tau^+\tau^-$ at Belle II center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV, using the LEFT basis of four-fermion operators from Jenkins (2017). They evaluate the tree-level and one-loop effects of four-fermion operators on the dipole form factors $F_2$ and $F_3$, including both the real and imaginary parts, and derive bounds on Wilson coefficients assuming a measurement precision of $10^{-6}$.

## Key numbers

* $Z$-boson tree-level contribution to $\Re F_2^\text{eff}$: $\simeq 2.9 \times 10^{-6}$ at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV, a factor of two smaller than the naive power-counting estimate of $6 \times 10^{-6}$
* Largest possible effect from four-fermion operators: $\simeq 10^{-5} C \, v^2/\Lambda^2$ for the $Z$-boson interference term, and $\simeq 0.5 \times 10^{-5} v^4/\Lambda^4 |C|^2$ for quadratic four-fermion contributions
* Loop-level bounds on $4\tau$ scalar operator $C_{\tau\tau\tau\tau}^{S,RR}$ from $\Im F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 343$ GeV; from $\Re F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 645$ GeV
* Loop-level bounds on charm quark tensor operator $C_{c\tau\tau c}^{T,RR}$ from $\Im F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 1181$ GeV; from $\Re F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 975$ GeV
* Loop-level bounds on bottom quark tensor operator $C_{b\tau\tau b}^{T,RR}$ from $\Im F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 1201$ GeV; from $\Re F_2$: $\Lambda/\sqrt{C} > 3292$ GeV
* Required precision on normal asymmetry $A_N^\pm$ to probe the Schwinger term: $\lesssim 10^{-5}$

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Bernabeu (2004, 2006, 2007, 2008) and developed by Crivellin (2021) and Gogniat (2025) for measuring $\tau$ dipole moments via asymmetries in $e^+e^- \to \tau^+\tau^-$ at $B$ factories, specifically targeting a future polarization upgrade of SuperKEKB. The authors quantify for the first time the complete $Z$-boson and four-fermion operator backgrounds that could contaminate the interpretation of asymmetry measurements at the $10^{-6}$ level, complementing the QED one-loop analysis of Gogniat (2025). The loop-level four-fermion analysis provides novel constraints on $4\tau$ operators and semileptonic $\bar{q}q\bar{\tau}\tau$ operators, and the observation that the imaginary part from dipole operator insertions at one loop enables a determination of $a_\tau$ via $A_N^\pm$ without requiring electron polarization, building on the strategy proposed by Hoferichter (2025).
