# Chromomagnetic instability in dense quark matter

> The results for the Debye and Meissner screening masses of the gluons and the photon in the case of neutral and beta-equilibrated dense two-flavor quark matter are presented. In the limits of the normal phase and the ideal two-flavor color superconducting phase, the screening masses coincide with the known results. Most interestingly, we find that the Meissner screening masses squared can be negative, indicating a plasma-type chromomagnetic instability in dense quark matter.

# Chromomagnetic instability in dense quark matter

## Key claim
The Meissner screening masses squared for certain gluon modes can become negative in neutral, beta-equilibrated two-flavor quark matter, indicating a plasma-type chromomagnetic instability. This instability occurs not only in the gapless 2SC phase (where it might be expected from prior arguments) but also in the gapped 2SC phase when the mismatch parameter satisfies 1 < Δ/δμ < √2, where the ground state is a local minimum of the effective potential.

## Method
The authors calculate the Debye and Meissner screening masses of gluons and the photon in neutral, beta-equilibrated dense two-flavor quark matter using the polarization tensor in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism. The screening masses are extracted from the eigenvalues of the polarization tensor in the static limit, parametrized by the dimensionless ratio Δ/δμ, where δμ = μ_e/2 is the Fermi momentum mismatch between paired quarks. Results are obtained for the normal phase (Δ/δμ = 0), the gapless 2SC phase (Δ/δμ < 1), and the gapped 2SC phase (Δ/δμ > 1).

## Key numbers
- The Meissner screening mass squared for gluons A=4,5,6,7 becomes negative when 0 < Δ/δμ < √2, with the explicit expression m_{M,4}^2 = (4α_s μ̄²/3π)[(Δ² − 2δμ²)/(2Δ²) + (δμ√(δμ²−Δ²)/Δ²) θ(δμ−Δ)].
- The Meissner screening mass squared for the medium-modified 8th gluon (A=8̃) is m_{M,8̃}² = [4(3α_s+α)μ̄²/(27π)] [1 − δμ θ(δμ−Δ)/√(δμ²−Δ²)], which is negative in the gapless 2SC phase (0 < Δ/δμ < 1).
- The Debye mass for gluons A=1,2,3 in the gapless phase is m_{D,1}² = (4α_s μ̄² δμ)/(π√(δμ²−Δ²)) θ(δμ−Δ), which diverges as Δ/δμ → 1 from below.
- The Debye mass for the 8th gluon is constant: m_{D,8}² = 4α_s μ̄²/π.
- The mixing angle between the 8th gluon and the vacuum photon is sin φ = √(α/(3α_s+α)), identical in both gapless and gapped phases.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of screening masses in the ideal 2SC phase by Rischke (2000) and in the normal phase by Vijande and Manuel. It builds on the discovery of the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase by Shovkovy and Huang (2003) and Huang and Shovkovy (2003), where charge neutrality forces a mismatch between Fermi momenta. The finding of negative Meissner masses squared suggests that the true ground state may involve gluon condensation, possibly breaking rotational symmetry, similar to mechanisms discussed by Mrowczynski (1993) for anisotropic plasmas in heavy-ion collisions, though the origin here differs as the quark distribution is isotropic. The authors note that this instability is distinct from the Sarma instability and propose future investigation of analogous effects in the gapless CFL phase.
