# Supersymmetric Color Superconductivity

> Recent interest in novel phases in high density QCD motivates the study of high density supersymmetric QCD (SQCD), where powerful exact results for supersymmetric gauge theories can be brought to bear in the strongly coupled regime. We begin by describing how a chemical potential can be incorporated into a supersymmetric theory as a spurion vector superfield. We then study supersymmetric SU(N_c) gauge theories with N_f flavors of quarks in the presence of a baryon chemical potential mu, and describe the global symmetry breaking patterns at low energy. Our analysis requires mu < Lambda and is thus complementary to the variational approach that has been successful for mu << Lambda. We find that for N_f < N_c a modified U(1)_B symmetry is preserved, analogous to the non-supersymmetric 2SC phase, whereas for N_f=N_c there is a critical chemical potential above which the U(1)_B is broken, as it is in the non-supersymmetric CFL phase. We further analyze the cases with N_c+1 <= N_f < 3/2 N_c and find that baryon number is broken dynamically for mu > mu_c. We also give a qualitative description of the phases in the `conformal window', 3/2 N_c < N_f < 3N_c, at finite density.

## Key claim

The paper uses exact results from supersymmetric QCD (SQCD) to study whether baryon number is dynamically broken at finite chemical potential, finding that for \(N_f \le \frac{3}{2}N_c\) the answer matches the non-supersymmetric case, but only under the condition \(\mu < \Lambda\) (chemical potential smaller than the dynamical scale), which is complementary to the usual perturbative BCS regime where \(\mu \gg \Lambda\).

## Method

The authors incorporate a baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) into SQCD as a spurion vector superfield, treating it as a background \(U(1)_B\) gauge field with a vacuum expectation value for its time component. They add stabilizing masses (either supersymmetric or soft SUSY-breaking) to prevent immediate squark Bose-Einstein condensation, then use the known infrared descriptions of SQCD for different flavor numbers \(N_f\) relative to colors \(N_c\), meson/baryon effective theories for \(N_f \le N_c+1\) and dual descriptions for larger \(N_f\), to determine whether the low-energy potential develops an instability that breaks \(U(1)_B\) spontaneously.

## Key numbers

* For \(N_f = N_c\) with a supersymmetric stabilizing mass \(m\), the critical chemical potential is \(\mu_c^2 = m^2 / (c_0 N_c^2)\), where \(c_0\) is an \({\cal O}(1)\) Kähler coefficient, requiring \(\mu_c < m\) for validity.
* For \(N_f = N_c\) with soft SUSY-breaking mass \(\tilde{m}\), the critical chemical potential is \(\mu_c^2 = (1 - |c_2/c_1|) \tilde{m}^2 / N_c^2\), where \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are Kähler coefficients, again of order \(\tilde{m}/N_c\).
* The analysis requires \(\mu < \Lambda\) throughout, where \(\Lambda\) is the dynamical scale of the \(SU(N_c)\) theory, and the one-loop beta function coefficient is \(b_0 = 3N_c - N_f\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity from non-supersymmetric QCD (reviewed by Rajagopal and Wilczek, and Alford, 2001) into the strongly coupled regime of supersymmetric QCD, where exact results from Seiberg and collaborators (reviewed by Intriligator and Seiberg, 1995; and Peskin, 1997) can be applied. The results for \(N_f < N_c\) (unbroken \(U(1)_B\), analogous to the 2SC phase) and \(N_f \ge N_c\) (broken \(U(1)_B\), analogous to the CFL phase) match the non-supersymmetric variational calculations of Schäfer and Wilczek (1999, 2000) for \(N_f \le \frac{3}{2}N_c\), and the formalism enables future study of the conformal window \(\frac{3}{2}N_c < N_f < 3N_c\) at finite density.
