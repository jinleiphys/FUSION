# Flow at the SPS and RHIC as a Quark Gluon Plasma Signature

> Radial and elliptic flow in non-central heavy ion collisions can constrain the effective Equation of State(EoS) of the excited nuclear matter. To this end, a model combining relativistic hydrodynamics and a hadronic transport code(RQMD [17]) is developed. For an EoS with a first order phase transition, the model reproduces both the radial and elliptic flow data at the SPS. With the EoS fixed from SPS data, we quantify predictions at RHIC where the Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) pressure is expected to drive additional radial and elliptic flow. Currently, the strong elliptic flow observed in the first RHIC measurements does not conclusively signal this nascent QGP pressure. Additional measurements are suggested to pin down the EoS.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the increase in elliptic flow from SPS to RHIC energies can be explained by hydrodynamic evolution with an equation of state (EoS) that includes a first-order phase transition, but the strong elliptic flow observed in the first RHIC measurements does not conclusively signal the asymptotic pressure of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) because a very soft EoS can produce the same elliptic flow as one with a well-developed QGP phase.

## Method

The authors developed the Hydro-to-Hadrons (H2H) model, which combines relativistic hydrodynamics for the QGP and mixed phases with the hadronic transport code RQMDv2.4 to model differential freezeout of different hadron species. The hydrodynamic evolution solves the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics in the transverse plane assuming Bjorken scaling, constructs a switching surface at T_switch = 160 MeV, generates hadrons using the Cooper-Frye formula, and then allows the hadrons to rescatter sequentially in RQMD until freezeout. A family of bag-model-type EoSs with adjustable latent heat (LH) was constructed, ranging from a Resonance Gas (RG) EoS with no phase transition to EoSs with latent heats of 0.4, 0.8, and 1.6 GeV/fm^3 (labeled LH4, LH8, LH16) and a limiting case LH∞ mimicking non-equilibrium phenomena.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy densities at ~1 fm/c are approximately 4 GeV/fm^3 at SPS (sqrt(s_NN)=17 GeV) and 7 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV).
- The softest point of the EoS occurs at energy density e_sp ≈ 1.4 GeV/fm^3.
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade is T_switch = 160 MeV; the critical temperature is T_c = 165 MeV.
- For the LH8 EoS at RHIC, the collision lifetime is ≈9 fm/c; for LH∞, the lifetime is ≈13 fm/c.
- The H2H model predicts an increase in elliptic flow v_2 from SPS to RHIC by a factor ≈1.4.
- For pure hydrodynamics with decoupling at T_f = 120 MeV, elliptic flow is a factor of two above the SPS data; with the cascade included, LH8 is only ≈20% above the data.
- At SPS multiplicities, proton slope parameters at b=6 fm are ≈7 MeV smaller than at b=0 fm for all EoSs.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic simulations of non-central heavy ion collisions by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999) which over-predicted elliptic flow by a factor of two because they ignored differential freezeout. The H2H model builds on a similar hybrid approach for central collisions by Bass and Dumitru (2000). The paper challenges the prevailing view from Heiselberg and Levy (1999) and Voloshin and Poskanzer (2000) that the SPS is in the low-density regime and that the increase in v_2 represents a transition to the hydrodynamic regime. The results enable future experimental scans of collision energy from SPS to RHIC to distinguish between EoSs with different latent heats, along with measurements of the transverse momentum dependence of v_2 and the impact parameter dependence of elliptic flow.
