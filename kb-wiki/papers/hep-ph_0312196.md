# Heavy Quark $\bar{q}q$ Matrix Elements in the Nucleon from Perturbative QCD

> The scalar heavy quark content of the nucleon, ${\cal M}_q = \bra{N} m_q \bar q q \ket{N},$ is relevant for computing the interaction of dark matter candidates with ordinary matter, while ${\cal M}_s$ is important for predicting the properties of dense matter. We compute ${\cal M}_q$ in perturbative QCD to ${\cal{O}}{(α^3)}.$ As one goes from ${\cal M}_t$ to ${\cal M}_c$ the leading order contribution decreases as the number of light quarks is dropping, while the radiative corrections grow and are all positive. The leading source of uncertainty in the calculation is due to the poorly known value of ${\cal M}_s.$ A related calculation suggests that a large value for ${\cal M}_s$ may be reasonable.

## Key claim

The scalar heavy quark content of the nucleon, ${\cal M}_q = \langle N | m_q \bar q q | N \rangle$, is computed in perturbative QCD to ${\cal O}(\alpha^3)$, but the leading source of uncertainty in the results is the poorly known value of the strange quark matrix element ${\cal M}_s$, which enters through the nucleon mass shift $\Delta_s M$ and cannot be computed with the same perturbative technique since $m_s \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$.

## Method

The calculation uses the Feynman-Hellman theorem to relate the heavy quark matrix element to the derivative of the nucleon mass with respect to the running quark mass at a reference scale, holding the gauge couplings fixed. This derivative is evaluated by differentiating the integrated renormalization group equations for the QCD coupling and quark masses, using the four-loop beta function, four-loop anomalous mass dimension, and three-loop heavy flavor threshold matching coefficients compiled by Chetyrkin, Kuhn, and Steinhauser (2000). The nucleon mass is expanded in inverse powers of the heavy quark masses and in light quark masses, with the strange quark treated separately via a toy model where it is taken as heavy.

## Key numbers

* ${\cal M}_c = 78.61 (1 - x_{uds})$ MeV, where $x_{uds} = (\Sigma_{\pi N} + \Delta_s M)/M$ and $\Sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 45$ MeV
* ${\cal M}_b = 80.92 (1 - x_{uds})$ MeV
* ${\cal M}_t = 84.89 (1 - x_{uds})$ MeV
* The perturbative series for ${\cal M}_c$ is $(2/27)(1 + 0.108 + 0.025 - 0.002) M (1 - x_{uds})$, using nucleon mass $M = 938.3$ MeV
* The perturbative series for ${\cal M}_b$ is $(2/25)(1 + 0.071 + 0.009 - 0.002) M (1 - x_{uds})$
* The perturbative series for ${\cal M}_t$ is $(2/23)(1 + 0.0402 + 0.0009 - 0.0007) M (1 - x_{uds})$
* Input quark masses: $m_c = 1.2$ GeV, $m_b = 4.2$ GeV, $m_t = 175$ GeV; input coupling $\alpha(M_Z) = 0.117$ with $M_Z = 91.18$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of heavy flavor ${\cal M}_q$ by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1978) to ${\cal O}(\alpha^3)$ using techniques similar to those of Voloshin (1980) and Chivukula (1987). The value of ${\cal M}_s$ is important for kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986) and has been debated in baryon chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1982; Jenkins and Manohar, 1991) and the Skyrme model (Kaplan and Klebanov, 1991). The toy model calculation with a heavy strange quark suggests that a large value of ${\cal M}_s$ for the physical strange quark mass may be reasonable, which would have implications for dense matter properties and the fate of supernova remnants.
