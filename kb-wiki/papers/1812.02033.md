# NLO Productions of $ω$ and $K^0_{\rm S}$ with a Global Extraction of the Jet Transport Parameter in Heavy Ion collisions

> In this work, we pave the way to calculate the productions of $ω$ and $K^0_{\rm S}$ mesons at large $p_T$ in p+p and A+A collisions at the RHIC and the LHC. The $ω$ meson fragmentation functions (FFs) in vacuum at next-to-leading order (NLO) are obtained by evolving NLO DGLAP evolution equations with rescaled $ω$ FFs at initial scale $Q_0^2=1.5$ GeV$^2$ from a broken SU(3) model, and the $K^0_{\rm S}$ FFs in vacuum are taken from AKK08 parametrization directly. Within the framework of the NLO pQCD improved parton model, we make good descriptions of the experimental data on $ω$ and $K^0_{\rm S}$ in p+p both at the RHIC and the LHC. With the higher-twist approach to take into account the jet quenching effect by medium modified FFs, the nuclear modification factors for $ω$ meson and $K^0_{\rm S}$ meson at the RHIC and the LHC are presented with different sets of jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}_0$. Then we make a global extraction of $\hat{q}_0$ at the RHIC and the LHC by confronting our model calculations with all available data on 6 identified mesons: $π^0$, $η$, $ρ^0$, $ϕ$, $ω$, and $K^0_{\rm S}$. The minimum value of the total $χ^2/d.o.f$ for productions of these mesons gives the best value of $\hat{q}_0=0.5\rm GeV^2/fm$ for Au+Au collisions with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV at the RHIC, and $\hat{q}_0=1.2\rm GeV^2/fm$ for Pb+Pb collisions with $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC respectively, with the QGP spacetime evolution given by an event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics model IEBE-VISHNU. With these global extracted values of $\hat{q}_0$, the nuclear modification factors of $π^0$, $η$, $ρ^0$, $ϕ$, $ω$, and $K^0_{\rm S}$ in A+A collisions are presented, and predictions of yield ratios such as $ω/π^0$ and $K^0_{\rm S}/π^0$ at large $p_T$ in heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC and the LHC are provided.

## Key claim

The authors present the first NLO pQCD calculations of large-\(p_T\) \(\omega\) and \(K^0_{\rm S}\) meson production in heavy-ion collisions, and use these together with data on four other mesons to globally extract the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\). The extraction is conditional on the choice of the IEBE-VISHNU event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics model for the QGP spacetime evolution, yielding smaller \(\hat{q}_0\) values than those obtained with the Hirano hydro description used in prior work.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive hadron cross sections in p+p collisions at NLO using the pQCD improved parton model with CT14 PDFs, employing NLO DGLAP-evolved fragmentation functions for \(\omega\) (from a broken SU(3) model at \(Q_0^2=1.5\) GeV\(^2\)) and AKK08 parametrization for \(K^0_{\rm S}\). For A+A collisions, jet quenching is incorporated via the higher-twist approach with medium-modified splitting functions, using the IEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamics model to describe the QGP evolution. A global \(\chi^2\) fit to the nuclear modification factors \(R_{\rm AA}\) of six mesons (\(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), \(\rho^0\), \(\phi\), \(\omega\), \(K^0_{\rm S}\)) is performed to extract the best value of \(\hat{q}_0\) at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

*   Best-fit jet transport parameter for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV (RHIC): \(\hat{q}_0 = 0.5^{+0.15}_{-0.05}~\text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\).
*   Best-fit jet transport parameter for Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV (LHC): \(\hat{q}_0 = 1.2^{+0.25}_{-0.15}~\text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\).
*   Initial scale for \(\omega\) fragmentation functions: \(Q_0^2 = 1.5~\text{GeV}^2\).
*   \(\omega\) meson mass: \(782.65\) MeV.
*   QGP formation time used in IEBE-VISHNU: \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO pQCD studies of \(\eta\), \(\rho^0\), and \(\phi\) meson production in heavy-ion collisions (Dai 2015, Dai 2017) to include \(\omega\) and \(K^0_{\rm S}\) mesons. It provides a global extraction of \(\hat{q}_0\) using six identified mesons, updating the JET Collaboration extraction (Burke 2013) by employing the IEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamics model instead of the Hirano hydro description. The results enable predictions for yield ratios such as \(\omega/\pi^0\) and \(K^0_{\rm S}/\pi^0\) at high \(p_T\) at both RHIC and LHC, and offer a framework for more precise extractions of jet transport coefficients as more identified hadron data become available.
