# Dark matter effects on the properties of neutron stars: optical radii

> We study the effects of dark matter on the properties of neutron stars by employing a DM-admixed model. The Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with realistic three-body forces and a generic bosonic self-interacting dark matter model describe the equations of state for nuclear matter and DM, respectively. We study the complete set of stable dark neutron stars and in particular the observable radii of these objects. A rich variety of stellar configurations is found and discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observable (optical) radius of a neutron star can be significantly altered by admixed dark matter, but that notable effects from a limited DM fraction are only expected for DM particle masses in the range of roughly 100–1000 MeV, of the same order as the nucleon mass; for lower masses, huge radius variations are possible but require very speculative high-DM-fraction configurations.

## Method

The authors employ a two-fluid general-relativistic model solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for a system composed of nuclear matter and bosonic self-interacting dark matter. The nuclear equation of state is derived from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory using the Argonne V18 potential with consistent three-body forces, while the DM equation of state is a one-parameter model of massive self-interacting bosons with particle mass μ and coupling λ = π. They compute stable stellar configurations for DM fractions f from 0 (pure neutron star) to 1 (pure dark star) and four DM particle masses (200, 300, 500, 1000 MeV), mapping the resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Pure neutron star maximum mass for the V18 equation of state: 2.36 solar masses
- Pure neutron star radius at 1.4 solar masses (R₁.₄): 12.28 km (V18 EOS)
- For the μ = 200 MeV model, accretion onto an initial (M_G, M_B) = (1.4, 1.56) solar mass pure NS allows a maximum DM fraction f_max ≈ 0.30, yielding a DNS of M_G ≈ 1.84 solar masses
- For μ > 355 MeV, only DM-core configurations exist with reduced radii down to R₁.₄ ≈ 8.5 km
- For μ < 263 MeV, R₁.₄ can increase beyond the pure NS value of 12.28 km at large DM fractions
- DNSs heavier than the pure NS maximum (2.36 solar masses) can only exist for μ < 275 MeV
- Pure NS tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ = 430 (V18 EOS)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM-admixed neutron stars by focusing specifically on the observable optical radius, a fundamental and directly measurable quantity. It builds on the two-fluid approach used by Kodama (1972), Comer (1999), and Sandin (2009), and employs the same bosonic DM model as Colpi (1986), Chavanis (2012), Maselli (2017), Leung (2022), and Karkevandi (2022). The paper systematically maps the full parameter space of DM fraction and particle mass, revealing that DM-core and DM-halo configurations can produce "twin" stars with identical mass and optical radius but completely different internal structure, and that comparing measured radii with tidal deformabilities could potentially reveal the presence of DM. This enables future observational tests using NICER data and gravitational wave detections to constrain DM properties.
