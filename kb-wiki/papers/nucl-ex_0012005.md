# Splitting of the Dipole and Spin-Dipole Resonances

> Cross sections for the 90,92,94Zr(p,n) reactions were measured at energies of 79.2 and 119.4 MeV. A phenomenological model was developed to describe the variation with bombarding energy of the position of the L=1 peak observed in these and other (p,n) reactions. The model yields the splitting between the giant dipole and giant spin dipole resonances. Values of these splittings are obtained for isotopes of Zr and Sn and for 208Pb.

## Key claim

The observed shift of the L=1 peak in (p,n) reactions toward lower excitation energy with increasing bombarding energy can be explained by the changing relative contributions of the overlapping giant dipole resonance (GDR, S=0) and spin-dipole resonance (SDR, S=1), and this shift can be used to extract the energy splitting between them, though the model only applies to the resonant part of the strength and not the higher-lying continuum.

## Method

Cross sections for the 90,92,94Zr(p,n) reactions were measured at 79.2 and 119.4 MeV using the beam swinger time-of-flight facility at the Indiana University Cyclotron Facility. The L=1 peak positions were extracted by fitting spectra with sums of Gaussian peaks plus a quadratic background. A phenomenological model was developed in which the observed centroid of the L=1 strength is a weighted average of the GDR and SDR positions, with the ratio of S=1 to S=0 cross sections assumed proportional to the ratio of forbidden beta decay strengths and scaling with bombarding energy squared, analogous to the L=0 case described by Taddeucci (1987).

## Key numbers

* For 90Zr, the extracted GDR energy E0 is 16.7 +/- 1.2 MeV and the GDR-SDR splitting Delta is 4.0 +/- 1.3 MeV, measured from the isobaric analog state.
* For 92Zr, E0 is 17.1 +/- 1.6 MeV and Delta is 5.1 +/- 1.7 MeV.
* For 94Zr, E0 is 16.9 +/- 1.9 MeV and Delta is 5.4 +/- 2.1 MeV.
* For 208Pb, E0 is 11.0 +/- 1.5 MeV and Delta is 4.7 +/- 2.0 MeV.
* The L=1 peak shifts to lower excitation energy by about 2 MeV as the bombarding energy changes from 40 to 200 MeV for 90Zr.
* The model parameter alpha, which incorporates the ratio of SDR to GDR transition strengths and distortion factors, is taken as 1.5 +/- 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier (p,n) studies on Zr isotopes at 41, 45, 120, and 200 MeV by Nishihara (1985), Sterrenburg (1980), Bainum (1980), and Horen (1981), and provides new data at 79.2 and 119.4 MeV to fill the intermediate energy range. The results enable improved estimates of the GDR position in the charge exchange channel, which had previously been assumed to be at the L=1 peak position near 50 MeV, and require modification of tensor contributions to isospin splitting estimates from Sterrenburg (1980). The findings support the assumptions of Fuller (1999) regarding the relative positions of the spin-dipole and dipole resonances for supernova neutrino detector response calculations, though the present results only apply to the resonant part of the strength.
