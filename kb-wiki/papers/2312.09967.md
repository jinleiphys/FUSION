# Quark matter and nuclear astrophysics: recent developments

> Does deconfined cold quark matter occur in nature? This is currently one of the fundamental open questions in nuclear astrophysics. In these proceedings, I review the current state-of-the-art techniques to address this question in a model-agnostic manner, by synthesizing inputs from astrophysical observations of neutron stars and their binary mergers, and first-principles calculations within nuclear and particle theory. I highlight recent improvements in perturbative calculations in asymptotically dense cold quark matter, as well as compelling evidence for a conformalizing transition within the cores of massive neutron stars.

# Quark matter and nuclear astrophysics: recent developments

## Key claim
Cold deconfined quark matter likely exists in the cores of the most massive stable neutron stars, based on compelling evidence for a conformalizing transition along the stable neutron star sequence when state-of-the-art perturbative QCD input is used; however, this conclusion depends on the absence of a strong phase transition in the neutron star equation of state.

## Method
The author reviews a model-agnostic framework that synthesizes astrophysical observations of neutron stars and their binary mergers with first-principles calculations from chiral effective field theory at low densities and perturbative QCD at high densities, using Bayesian inference to constrain the neutron star equation of state. Recent improvements include the computation of the soft and mixed sectors of the perturbative QCD equation of state at O(alpha_s^3), and a Bayesian uncertainty estimation of truncation errors using a machine-learning based model of convergent series trained on the available perturbative terms.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density n_sat approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Perturbative QCD results are well converged down to approximately 40 n_sat for the soft sector at O(alpha_s^3)
- Indications that the full O(alpha_s^3) result may remain well converged down to n_B approximately 27 n_sat
- The conformality measure d_c exhibits a clear change of behavior around 3-4 n_sat, below the maximum densities reached in massive neutron stars
- The dividing line between non-conformal and conformal matter is set at d_c = 0.2
- The quantity p/p_free in the cores of the most massive neutron stars takes a value about 2/3 of the value in weakly interacting cold quark matter at high densities

## Context
This proceedings paper reviews work extending the approach of Annala (2019) which first attempted to delineate the microphysical phase of neutron star matter using thermodynamic criteria, and Fujimoto (2022) which suggested analyzing the trace anomaly. The key methodological advance builds on Komoltsev (2021), which showed that perturbative QCD constraints at densities above 40 n_sat can rule out specific extreme equations of state at 2.2-2.5 n_sat. The work enables future analyses incorporating explicit phase transitions in the neutron star equation of state prior, as well as investigations of neutron star cooling, transport properties, and binary merger observables to provide complementary evidence for or against quark matter in neutron star cores.
