# Three-Fluid Simulations of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A relativistic 3-fluid 3D hydrodynamic model has been developed for describing heavy-ion collisions at incident energies between few and $\sim$ 200 A$\cdot$GeV. In addition to two baryon-rich fluids which simulate mutually decelerating counterflows of target and projectile nucleons, the new model incorporates evolution of a third, retarded baryon-free fluid created by this decelerated baryonic matter. Different equations of state, including those with the deconfinement phase transition, are treated. A reasonable agreement with experiment is illustrated by proton rapidity spectra, their dependence on collision centrality and beam energy.

## Key claim
The paper presents a relativistic 3-fluid 3D hydrodynamic model for heavy-ion collisions from few to about 200 A·GeV, but the authors state that despite reasonable reproduction of proton rapidity spectra, they are "unable to favor any of considered EoS" (equations of state), meaning the model cannot distinguish between hadronic, mixed-phase, or deconfinement phase-transition scenarios based on the presented observables.

## Method
The model extends a 2-fluid hydrodynamic approach by adding a third, baryon-free "fireball" fluid that represents newly produced particles. The two baryon-rich fluids simulate the counter-streaming target and projectile nucleons with finite stopping power, while the fireball fluid evolves with a retarded source term governed by a formation time (τ). Friction forces between fluids are estimated from nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon cross sections, and the system is closed with equations of state including a mixed-phase model for deconfinement. The 3D code modifies an existing 2-fluid code, and freeze-out is applied when local energy density drops below 0.15 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range: few to ~200 A·GeV (AGS to SPS energies).
- For Pb+Pb at 158 A·GeV and impact parameter b=2 fm, maximal energy density is reached by ~1.1 fm/c.
- At realistic formation times τ~1 fm/c, the fireball fluid loses only 10% of its available energy at 158 A·GeV and 30% at 10.5 A·GeV.
- Freeze-out threshold: local energy density ε_fr = 0.15 GeV/fm³, corresponding to actual freeze-out fluid element energy density of ~0.12 GeV/fm³.
- Proton rapidity spectra calculated for Au+Au at beam energies 6, 8, and 10.5 A·GeV and for Pb+Pb at 158 A·GeV.

## Context
This work extends the 2-fluid model with pion radiation of Mishustin et al. (1991) and the (2+1)-fluid model of Katayama (1993) and Brachmann et al. (1997), which are limiting cases for τ→∞ and τ=0 respectively. The model incorporates a mixed-phase equation of state from Nikonov et al. (1998) that allows first-order deconfinement for pure gluon matter and crossover for matter with quarks, following lattice QCD data. The authors note that the general agreement for central collisions at SPS energies is much better than in any other 2-fluid hydrodynamic model, primarily due to larger energy-momentum transfer in the friction formulation rather than the third fluid itself. The work enables future analysis of more delicate collective observables such as directed and elliptic flows to discriminate between equations of state.
