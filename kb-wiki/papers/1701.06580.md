# Fast neutrino conversions: Ubiquitous in compact binary merger remnants

> The massive neutron star (NS) or black hole (BH) accretion disk resulting from NS--NS or NS--BH mergers is dense in neutrinos. We present the first study on the role of angular distributions in the neutrino flavor conversion above the remnant disk. In particular, we focus on "fast" pairwise conversions whose rate depends on the local angular intensity of the electron lepton number carried by neutrinos. Because of the emission geometry and the flux density of $\barν_e$ being larger than that of $ν_e$, fast conversions prove to be a generic phenomenon in NS--NS and NS--BH mergers for physically motivated disturbances in the mean field of flavor coherence. Our findings suggest that, differently from the core-collapse supernova case, fast flavor conversions seem to be unavoidable in compact mergers and could have major consequences for the jet dynamics and the synthesis of elements above the remnant disk.

## Key claim

Fast neutrino flavor conversions, driven by the angular distribution of the electron lepton number (ELN), are shown to be a generic and unavoidable phenomenon in neutron star-neutron star and neutron star-black hole merger remnants, in contrast to core-collapse supernovae where such conversions are not guaranteed. This conclusion holds for physically motivated disturbances in the mean field of flavor coherence, but the authors note that the results are based on a simplified two-neutrino-emitting disk model whose parameters are guided by existing hydrodynamical simulations.

## Method

The authors modeled the neutrino emission from merger remnants using a simple two-neutrino-emitting disk model with separate surfaces for νe and ν̄e, with parameters guided by the hydrodynamical simulation of Perego (2014). They applied the dispersion relation (DR) approach developed by Izaguirre (2016) to linearize the equation of motion for neutrino flavor evolution and searched for complex solutions in ω (temporal instabilities) and k (spatial instabilities) that would indicate exponentially growing flavor conversions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of ν̄e to νe decoupling surface radii is Rν̄e/Rνe = 0.75, with heights hνe/Rνe = hν̄e/Rν̄e = 0.25
- The benchmark flux ratio α ≡ Φ⁰ν̄e/Φ⁰νe = 2.4, with fast conversions found for any α ≥ 1.1
- The typical inverse length scale μ₀ ≈ 4.25 cm⁻¹ for Lνe = 10⁵³ erg/s, ⟨Eνe⟩ = 10 MeV, and Rνe = 100 km
- Temporal instability growth rates are typically |Im(ω)| ∼ 0.1 μ₀, corresponding to flavor conversion length scales of centimeters
- For the BH-torus model, the inner edge of the emitting surface is set at R₀ = 0.15 Rνe

## Context

This work extends the study of fast neutrino conversions, previously investigated in core-collapse supernovae by Sawyer (2005, 2008, 2015), Chakraborty (2016), Dasgupta (2016), and Izaguirre (2016), to compact binary merger remnants for the first time. It contrasts with the supernova case where ELN crossings are not guaranteed (Tamborra 2017) and may only occur due to the LESA phenomenon (Izaguirre 2016). The findings suggest that the previously studied matter-neutrino resonance (MNR) scenario in mergers (Malkus 2014, 2015; Wu 2015) needs revision, as fast conversions would occur before neutrinos reach MNR locations. This work enables future numerical simulations of nonlinear flavor evolution in mergers and motivates phenomenological exploration of impacts on jet dynamics and r-process nucleosynthesis.
