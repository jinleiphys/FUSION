# eta in the nuclear medium within a chiral unitary approach

> The self-energy of an eta meson in the nuclear medium is calculated in a chiral unitary approach. A coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved to obtain the effective eta-N interaction in the medium. The base model reproduces well the free space pi-N elastic and inelastic scattering at the eta-N threshold or N^*(1535) region. The Pauli blocking on the nucleons, binding potentials for the baryons and self-energies of the mesons are incorporated, including the eta self-energy in a self-consistent way. Our calculation predicts about -54 -i29 MeV for the optical potential at normal nuclear matter for an eta at threshold but also shows a strong energy dependence of the potential.

# η in the nuclear medium within a chiral unitary approach

## Key claim

The η meson optical potential at normal nuclear matter density is predicted to be −54 − i29 MeV at threshold, but this value shows a strong energy dependence below threshold; the real part is about twice as large as in earlier simplified studies, while the imaginary part decreases rapidly as the η energy decreases.

## Method

A coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with eight channels (including πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, and ππN states) is solved in nuclear matter, using kernels from lowest-order chiral Lagrangians. The in-medium loop functions incorporate Pauli blocking on nucleons, binding potentials for baryons (nucleon −70 ρ/ρ0 MeV, hyperons −30 ρ/ρ0 MeV), and self-energies for mesons (pions with P-wave and S-wave terms including N-h, Δ-h, and N*(1440)-h excitations; kaons with a constant self-energy 0.13 mK² ρ/ρ0). The η self-energy is determined self-consistently by iterating the in-medium amplitude until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Optical potential at normal nuclear matter density for η at threshold: −54 − i29 MeV
- Effective mass shift of the η: mη*/mη ≃ 1 − 0.05 ρ/ρ0 (5% downward shift at normal density)
- Upward shift of the N*(1535) resonance center: about 25 MeV for normal nuclear matter density
- Cutoff for meson-baryon loop regularization: q_cm^max = 1 GeV
- Subtraction constants: a_πN = 34 MeV, a_ηn = 14 MeV, a_KΛ = 39 MeV, a_KΣ = −22 MeV
- Baryon binding potentials: nucleon −70 ρ/ρ0 MeV, hyperons −30 ρ/ρ0 MeV (difference +40 ρ/ρ0 MeV added to hyperon energy)
- Kaon self-energy: Π_K(ρ) = 0.13 mK² ρ/ρ0
- Pion-nucleon coupling: f_πNN/m_π = 1.02
- Pion-nucleon-delta coupling ratio: f_πNΔ/f_πNN = 2.13
- Pion-nucleon-N*(1440) coupling ratio: f_πNN*/f_πNN = 0.477
- Short-range correlation parameter: g' = 0.6
- Monopole form factor cutoff for pion: 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Inoue (2002) for free-space πN and coupled-channel scattering to the nuclear medium, following the self-consistent method used by Ramos (2000) for the K̄-nucleus optical potential. The results differ from earlier calculations by Waas (1996), who obtained U_η(ρ) ≃ (−20 − i22) ρ/ρ0 MeV (real part about half the present value), and from Chiang (1998), who obtained (−34 − i24) MeV at ρ0 assuming no N*(1535) mass shift. The parameterized energy- and density-dependent self-energy enables solving the Klein-Gordon equation for η bound states in nuclei using the local density approximation.
