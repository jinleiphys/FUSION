# Three-dimensional integral Faddeev equations without a certain symmetry

> The approach of direct integration of the three-dimensional Faddeev equations with respect to the breakup T-matrix in momentum space for three bodies of different masses is presented. The Faddeev equations are written out explicitly without the requirement for symmetry or antisymmetry of two-body t matrices, taking into account the difference in the masses of three interacting particles. An algorithm for the algebraic search for non-relativistic wave functions of a system of three bodies of different masses is described. A significant change in the domain of logarithmic singularities of the integral kernels of the Faddeev equations from the choice of masses of interacting particles is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for directly integrating three-dimensional Faddeev equations for the breakup T-matrix in momentum space without requiring symmetry or antisymmetry of two-body t-matrices, thereby explicitly accounting for mass differences between three interacting particles. The approach is demonstrated for systems where particle masses differ by factors up to 100, but the author notes that the method yields physically acceptable results for known three-nucleon systems while also allowing arbitrary mass variation.

## Method

The author formulates the three-dimensional integral Faddeev equations for three bodies of different masses by explicitly writing out the matrix elements of all particle permutation operators in the vector basis, without imposing symmetry or antisymmetry on the two-body t-matrices. The system of coupled integral equations for the breakup T-matrices T₁, T₂, and T₃ is solved by direct numerical integration in momentum space using a coordinate system where the Oz axis is aligned with the incident momentum q₀. For eigenvalue problems, the homogeneous system is approximated on an N×N numerical grid to find the algebraic eigenvector corresponding to the three-body binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The partial ¹S₀-S component of the three-nucleon wave function for ³He was calculated using the separable Bonn parameterization of the fourth rank (Haidenbauer 1986), with mass variations of the spectator nucleon m₁ = 0.5mₚ, mₚ, and 2mₚ.
- The regions of logarithmic singularities were analyzed at scattering energy E = 1 MeV for systems with mass variations of 10 times increase and 100 times decrease in each component (m₁, m₂, m₃) relative to the proton-neutron-proton system.
- The boundaries of the logarithmic singularity domain are given by q²∧ = 2m₂E(m₁+m₃)/[M − (m₁m₂/m₃)(y²₀−1)] and q²∨ = 2m₂m₃E(m₁+m₃)/(m₁m₂ + m₃M), where M = m₁+m₂+m₃.

## Context

This work extends the direct three-dimensional integration approach for Faddeev equations previously applied only to the symmetrized case of nucleon-deuteron scattering (Liu 2005) to systems with arbitrary particle masses. The method enables calculations for exotic few-body systems such as meson-nuclear and hyperon-nuclear systems (Nogga 2002, Nogga 2013, Egorov 2023) without relying on partial wave decomposition, and provides a framework for exploring virtual and resonant three-body states through analytical continuation onto unphysical energy sheets (Orlov 1981, Orlov 1984).
