# Determination of the \bar K^0 d scattering length from the reaction pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+

> The real and imaginary parts of the \bar K^0 d scattering length are extracted from the \bar K^0 d mass spectrum obtained from the reaction pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+ measured recently at the Cooler Synchrotron COSY at Jülich. We extract a new limit on the K^- d scattering length, namely Im a \le 1.3 fm and |Re a| \le 1.3 fm. The limit for the imaginary part of the K^- d scattering length is supported by data on the total K^- d cross sections.

# Determination of the \(\bar K^0 d\) scattering length from the reaction \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\)

## Key claim

The real and imaginary parts of the \(\bar K^0 d\) scattering length are extracted from the \(\bar K^0 d\) mass spectrum of the \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) reaction measured at COSY, yielding new limits on the \(K^- d\) scattering length of \(\text{Im } a \le 1.3\) fm and \(|\text{Re } a| \le 1.3\) fm, but only under the condition that the imaginary part has a lower bound of \(\text{Im } a \ge 1\) fm derived from total \(K^- d\) cross-section data.

## Method

The \(\bar K^0 d\) invariant mass spectrum from the \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) reaction at a beam energy of \(T_p = 2.65\) GeV (excess energy 46 MeV) was analyzed by first isolating the \(s\)-wave \(\bar K^0 d\) contribution from the full partial-wave decomposition of the squared matrix element. The final-state interaction was then included by multiplying the relevant \(s\)-wave terms by the Watson-Migdal enhancement factor \(|1 - i Q a|^{-2}\), where \(a\) is the complex \(\bar K^0 d\) scattering length, and the real and imaginary parts of \(a\) were fitted to the experimental mass spectrum with the constraint \(\text{Im } a \ge 1\) fm from total \(K^- d\) cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- Lower bound on the imaginary part of the \(\bar K^0 d\) scattering length: \(\text{Im } a \ge 1\) fm, deduced from extrapolation of total \(K^- d\) cross-section data below 300 MeV/c.
- Extracted limits on the \(K^- d\) scattering length: \(\text{Im } a \le 1.3\) fm and \(|\text{Re } a| \le 1.3\) fm, from the fit to the \(\bar K^0 d\) mass spectrum with \(\chi^2 = 9.6\).
- The \(\bar K d\) \(s\)-wave contributes 57% to the total cross section of the \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) reaction at \(T_p = 2.65\) GeV.
- Experimental mass resolution of the \(\bar K^0 d\) spectrum: \(\simeq 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the \(pp \to d \bar K^0 K^+\) reaction previously performed by Kleber (2003) and the theoretical framework developed by Grishina (2000, 2001), Meissner (2002), and Hanhart (2003). The extracted limits on the \(K^- d\) scattering length provide constraints for coupled-channel chiral perturbation theory calculations and for the SIDDHARTA experiment at DA\(\Phi\)NE, which aims to measure the \(K^- d\) scattering length directly. The results also enable future extraction of the elementary \(K^- p\) and \(K^- n\) scattering lengths using few-body techniques, and motivate further study of the interplay between \(\bar K K\) and \(\bar K d\) interactions.
