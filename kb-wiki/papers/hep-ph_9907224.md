# Quark Structure of $Λ$ from $Λ$-Polarization in Z Decays

> The flavor and spin structure for the quark distributions of the $Λ$-baryon is studied in a perturbative QCD (pQCD) analysis and in the SU(6) quark-diquark model, and then applied to calculate the $Λ$-polarization of semi-inclusive $Λ$ production in $e^+e^-$-annihilation near the $Z$-pole. It is found that the quark-diquark model gives very good description of the available experimental data. The pQCD model can also give good description of the data by taking into account the suppression of quark helicities compared to the naive SU(6) quark model spin distributions. Further information is required for a clean distinction between different predictions concerning the flavor and spin structure of the $Λ$.

## Key claim

The SU(6) quark-diquark model provides a very good description of the available experimental data on Lambda polarization in Z decays, while the perturbative QCD (pQCD) model can also describe the data only after taking into account the suppression of quark helicities compared to the naive SU(6) quark model spin distributions; however, further information is required for a clean distinction between the two predictions concerning the flavor and spin structure of the Lambda.

## Method

The authors calculated the longitudinal polarization of Lambda hyperons produced in e+e- annihilation at the Z-pole by applying two theoretical frameworks: the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model with Melosh-Wigner rotation factors, and a pQCD analysis using counting-rule power-law behaviors for quark distributions. They employed the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) prescription for the light-cone momentum space wave function with specific diquark masses (m_S(ud)=600 MeV, m_S(qs)=750 MeV, m_V(qs)=950 MeV) and quark masses (m_q=330 MeV for u,d; m_s=480 MeV), and compared their theoretical results to data from the ALEPH, DELPHI, and OPAL collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The initial quark polarizations at the Z-pole, averaged over the production angle theta, are P_q = -0.67 for q = u, c and P_q = -0.94 for q = d, s, b.
- In the SU(6) quark-diquark model, the end-point behaviors at x to 1 predict u(x)/s(x) to 0, Delta s(x)/s(x) to 1, and Delta u(x)/u(x) to 1 for the non-dominant valence u and d quarks.
- In the pQCD analysis, the ratio u(x)/s(x) to 1/2 and Delta q(x)/q(x) to 1 (for q = u, d, s) as x to 1.
- The Burkardt-Jaffe values used for the integrated quark helicities in the pQCD case 2 are Delta s = 0.6 and Delta u = Delta d = -0.2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the flavor and spin structure of the Lambda hyperon from the earlier study by Ma, Schmidt, and Yang (1999) on Lambda structure near x=1, and applies it to the experimentally accessible process of Lambda production in Z decays. The results are compared to previous theoretical calculations by Kotzinian (1998), Boros (1998), and Florian (1998) that used simpler ansatz such as constant coefficients C_q or Monte Carlo event generators. The paper enables future tests of the quark-diquark model versus pQCD predictions for the spin structure of the Lambda, particularly the sign and magnitude of the u and d quark polarizations at large x, which can be distinguished with more precise experimental data.
