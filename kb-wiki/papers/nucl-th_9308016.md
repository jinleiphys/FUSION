# Microscopic Calculation of in-Medium Proton-Proton Cross Sections

> We derive in-medium PROTON-PROTON cross sections in a microscopic model based upon the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and the Dirac-Brueckner approach for nuclear matter. We demonstrate the difference between proton-proton and neutron-proton cross sections and point out the need to distinguish carefully between the two cases. We also find substantial differences between our in-medium cross sections and phenomenological parametrizations that are commonly used in heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim
In-medium proton-proton cross sections, derived microscopically from the Bonn potential and Dirac-Brueckner approach, show a strong density dependence and are always smaller than free-space values; the commonly used Cugnon parametrization, which is density-independent and based on free-space pp data, systematically overestimates in-medium cross sections at finite densities.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic one-boson-exchange Bonn A potential and perform a Dirac-Brueckner calculation for nuclear matter to determine the effective nucleon scalar and vector mean fields. Using these mean fields and a Lorentz-boosted Pauli projector, they solve the in-medium Thompson equation (relativistic Bethe-Goldstone equation) to obtain the G̃-matrix, which is identified with the in-medium K-matrix to compute in-medium pp cross sections. Coulomb effects are omitted, so the results also apply to neutron-neutron cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density of normal nuclear matter is taken as ρ₀ = 0.18 fm⁻³.
- In-medium pp total cross sections are parametrized by σ_pp(E_lab, ρ) = (23.5 + 0.0256(18.2 − E_lab^0.5)^4.0) × (1.0 + 0.1667 E_lab^1.05 ρ³) / (1.0 + 9.704 ρ^1.2), where E_lab is in MeV and ρ in fm⁻³.
- At E_lab = 50 MeV, the in-medium differential cross section decreases with increasing density; at E_lab = 250 MeV, it decreases from ρ=0 to ρ=ρ₀ and then increases at ρ=2ρ₀.
- At ρ=ρ₀ and E_lab=100 MeV, the pp differential cross section is nearly isotropic with symmetry dσ/dΩ(θ)=dσ/dΩ(π−θ), while the np differential cross section is highly anisotropic with a backward peak.
- The Cugnon parametrization, which fits free-space pp data, overestimates in-medium cross sections at finite densities because it is density-independent.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ earlier microscopic calculation of in-medium neutron-proton cross sections (Li and Machleidt, 1993) to the proton-proton case, using the same Dirac-Brueckner approach and Bonn potential. It contradicts the common practice in transport models (e.g., BUU and QMD) of using the density-independent Cugnon parametrization (Cugnon et al., 1981) for both pp and np cross sections, showing that pp and np cross sections differ substantially and that in-medium effects reduce cross sections significantly. The results enable more accurate treatment of two-body collisions in heavy-ion reaction simulations at intermediate energies (up to 300 MeV and densities up to 3ρ₀), and the authors provide a data file for interpolation.
