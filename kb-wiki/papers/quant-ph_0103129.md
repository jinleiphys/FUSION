# Entropy production and wave packet dynamics in the Fock space of closed chaotic many-body systems

> Highly excited many-particle states in quantum systems such as nuclei, atoms, quantum dots, spin systems, quantum computers etc., can be considered as ``chaotic'' superpositions of mean-field basis states (Slater determinants, products of spin or qubit states). This is due to a very high level density of many-body states that are easily mixed by a residual interaction between particles (quasi-particles). For such systems, we have derived simple analytical expressions for the time dependence of energy width of wave packets, as well as for the entropy, number of principal basis components and inverse participation ratio, and tested them in numerical experiments. It is shown that the energy width $Δ(t)$ increases linearly and very quickly saturates. The entropy of a system increases quadratically, $S(t) \sim t^2$ at small times, and after, can grow linearly, $S(t) \sim t$, before the saturation. Correspondingly, the number of principal components determined by the entropy, $N_{pc} \sim exp{(S(t))}$, or by the inverse participation ratio, increases exponentially fast before the saturation. These results are explained in terms of a cascade model which describes the flow of excitation in the Fock space of basis components. Finally, a striking phenomenon of damped oscillations in the Fock space at the transition to an equilibrium is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for the time dependence of entropy, energy width, and inverse participation ratio in closed chaotic many-body systems, showing that the entropy increases quadratically at very small times and then linearly before saturation, but these results are conditional on the system being above the chaos threshold where the residual interaction strength exceeds the mean spacing of directly coupled basis states.

## Method

The authors used the Two-Body Random Interaction (TBRI) model, where all two-body matrix elements are independent random variables, and the Wigner Band Random Matrix (WBRM) model to simulate chaotic many-body systems. They derived analytical expressions for the time evolution of wave packets by solving the Schrodinger equation in the Fock space, introducing a cascade model that describes the flow of probability through classes of basis states directly coupled by the two-body interaction. Numerical experiments were performed for systems of n=6 particles occupying m=12 orbitals (total basis size N=924) and n=7 particles with m=13 orbitals, averaging over multiple random matrix realizations.

## Context

This work extends the study of chaotic eigenstates in many-body systems, previously investigated through the TBRI model by French, Kota, and collaborators (1996-2000) and the Anderson-like transition in Fock space by Altshuler, Gefen, Kamenev, and Levitov (1997). The paper provides analytical predictions for time-dependent quantities that go beyond the stationary eigenstate approach, enabling comparison with classical Lyapunov exponents and supporting the quantum-classical correspondence found in the Gaussian regime by Casati, Chirikov, Guarneri, and Izrailev (1996) and by Cohen, Izrailev, and Kottos (2000). The results are relevant for understanding entropy production in nuclei, atoms, quantum dots, and quantum computers with many interacting qubits.
