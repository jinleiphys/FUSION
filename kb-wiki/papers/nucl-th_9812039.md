# Geometric Parameterization of $J/Ψ$ Absorption in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We calculate the survival probability of $J/Ψ$ particles in various colliding systems using a Glauber model. An analysis of recent data has reported a $J/Ψ$-nucleon breakup cross section of 6.2$\pm$0.7 mb derived from an exponential fit to the ratio of $J/Ψ$ to Drell-Yan yields as a function of a simple, linearly-averaged mean path length through the nuclear medium. Our calculations indicate that, due to the nature of the calculation, this approach yields an apparent breakup cross section which is systematically lower than the actual value.

# Geometric Parameterization of $J/\Psi$ Absorption in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The apparent $J/\Psi$-nucleon breakup cross section of 6.2$\pm$0.7 mb derived from an exponential fit to the ratio of $J/\Psi$ to Drell-Yan yields as a function of a linearly-averaged mean path length $\langle L \rangle$ is systematically lower than the actual breakup cross section, due to the fact that surviving $J/\Psi$ particles preferentially come from near the outer edges of the interaction region and the averaging procedure does not weight production points by their survival probability.

## Method

The authors performed a Glauber model calculation of $J/\Psi$ survival probability in p+A and A+A collisions using Woods-Saxon parameterizations of nucleon distributions within the colliding nuclei, with a nucleon-nucleon cross section of 30 mb. For a given collision geometry, the survival probability was computed by integrating over the path length through nuclear material weighted by local nucleon density and the breakup cross section, then averaging over all possible $J/\Psi$ production points within the interaction region. The resulting survival probabilities were plotted as a function of the linearly-averaged mean path length $\langle L \rangle$ and fit with an exponential to extract an apparent breakup cross section, which was compared to the actual input breakup cross section.

## Key numbers

- The NA50 collaboration reported a $J/\Psi$-nucleon breakup cross section of 6.2$\pm$0.7 mb derived from an exponential fit versus $\langle L \rangle$
- Using an input breakup cross section of 6.2 mb in the Glauber calculation, an exponential fit versus $\langle L \rangle$ returned an apparent breakup cross section of 5.5 mb
- The relationship between actual and apparent breakup cross sections is given by: $\sigma_{actual} = 0.118 + 0.932 \sigma_{apparent} + 0.030 \sigma_{apparent}^{2}$
- For the reported apparent breakup cross section of 6.2$\pm$0.7 mb, the model indicates the actual breakup cross section is 7.1$\pm$0.9 mb
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for Pb were C = 6.624 fm and D = 0.549 fm, with R0 = 1.1 fm
- The mean nuclear density used was $\rho_0$ = 0.1793 nucleons/fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of NA50 collaboration data (NA50, 1997) on $J/\Psi$ suppression in p+A and A+A collisions, which had been interpreted as displaying "anomalous $J/\Psi$ suppression" potentially signaling a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma. The paper demonstrates that the geometric averaging procedure used in previous analyses systematically underestimates the true $J/\Psi$-nucleon breakup cross section, though it notes that this effect does not explain away the observed anomalous suppression in Pb+Pb collisions. The results enable a more accurate determination of the breakup cross section through an iterative calculation that properly weights production points by their survival probability, which is important for distinguishing between contributions from "pre-resonance" color octet and color singlet states to the overall absorption.
