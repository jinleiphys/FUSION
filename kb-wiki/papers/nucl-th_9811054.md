# Kaon condensation at finite temperature

> A new formulation is presented to treat thermal fluctuations around the kaon condensate, based on chiral symmetry. Separating the zero mode from the beginning we perform the imaginary-time path integral to the one loop to get the thermodynamic potential at finite temperature. The role of the Goldstone mode in the kaon condensed phase is elucidated in relation to the equation of state.

# Kaon condensation at finite temperature

## Key claim

The authors present a new chiral-symmetry-based formalism for treating thermal fluctuations around the kaon condensate, claiming that the thermal effects in the condensed phase are remarkably larger than those in the normal phase and result in a significant reduction of pressure. The formalism relies on separating the zero mode from the beginning and using an approximation ($c_3=0$) that the authors argue is justified except at very low temperature, where thermal effects are trivially unimportant.

## Method

The authors start from the Kaplan-Nelson effective chiral Lagrangian and introduce chemical potentials for electromagnetic charge and baryon number conservation. They separate the zero mode by representing the kaon condensed phase as a chiral rotation from the vacuum, then introduce fluctuation fields via successive chiral transformations. The imaginary-time path integral is performed to one-loop order, yielding the thermodynamic potential; nucleons are treated as a nonrelativistic background with kinetic energy and a symmetry energy contribution, while leptons are included as free particles.

## Key numbers

- The kaon mass is given as $m_K^2 \simeq 2m_s\alpha/f^2$ from the symmetry-breaking terms.
- The $KN$ sigma terms are $\Sigma_{Kp} = -m_s(a_1+a_2+2a_3)$ and $\Sigma_{Kn} = -m_s(a_2+2a_3)$, with numerical values $a_1 m_s = -67$ MeV, $a_2 m_s = 134$ MeV, and $a_3 m_s = -222$ MeV used in calculations.
- The nuclear density is $\rho_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, and the empirical symmetry energy is $S_0 \simeq 30$ MeV.
- The zero-point energy contribution to pressure, $P_{ZP}$, is always less than several MeV$\cdot$fm$^{-3}$ over the density-temperature region of interest.
- The approximation $c_3=0$ is checked at $T=50$ MeV and density $u=3.87$ (where $u=\rho_B/\rho_0$), causing an error of less than several MeV$\cdot$fm$^{-3}$ for pressure.
- The temperature range of interest is $T \leq 100$ MeV, with protoneutron-star temperatures of several tens of MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation at zero temperature by Lee, Brown and Bethe (1994), and Thorsson et al. (1994), which had established that kaon condensation softens the equation of state and can produce low maximum neutron-star masses around $1.4 M_\odot$. The authors address the need for finite-temperature equations of state for protoneutron-star simulations, as Baumgarte et al. (1996) had used zero-temperature EOS in their numerical simulations of the delayed collapse scenario. The formalism improves upon the treatment by Thorsson and Ellis (1996), who included only quantum fluctuations and produced a complicated thermodynamic potential, and provides a consistent chiral-symmetry-based framework that enables calculation of thermal effects in the kaon condensed phase for protoneutron-star applications.
