# An updated review of the new hadron states

> The past decades witnessed the golden era of hadron physics. Many excited open heavy flavor mesons and baryons have been observed since 2017. We shall provide an updated review of the recent experimental and theoretical progresses in this active field. Besides the conventional heavy hadrons, we shall also review the recently observed open heavy flavor tetraquark states $X(2900)$ and $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ as well as the hidden heavy flavor multiquark states $X(6900)$, $P_{cs}(4459)^0$, $Z_{cs}(3985)^-$, $Z_{cs}(4000)^+$, and $Z_{cs}(4220)^+$. We will also cover the recent progresses on the glueballs and light hybrid mesons, which are the direct manifestations of the non-Abelian $SU(3)$ gauge interaction of the Quantum Chromodynamics in the low-energy region.

## Key claim

This paper provides an updated review of experimental and theoretical progress in hadron physics since 2017, covering both conventional heavy hadrons and exotic multiquark candidates, glueballs, and hybrid mesons, but explicitly excludes topics such as weak interaction studies, CP violation, heavy-ion collisions, and dark matter, and does not claim to cover all recent works in the field.

## Method

The authors compiled and categorized recent experimental observations and theoretical analyses of open heavy flavor mesons and baryons, open heavy flavor tetraquark states (X(2900) and T_cc^+(3875)), hidden heavy flavor multiquark states (X(6900), P_cs(4459)^0, Z_cs(3985)^-, Z_cs(4000)^+, Z_cs(4220)^+), and glueballs and light hybrid mesons. They organized the review by hadron type, using the heavy quark symmetry framework and color/flavor/spin/orbital degrees of freedom to categorize states, and referenced prior reviews including their own previous papers (Chen 2016) and (Liu 2019).

## Key numbers

- The European Muon collaboration measured the fraction of the proton spin carried by quarks as Delta Sigma(Q^2 = 10.7 GeV^2) = 0.060 +/- 0.047 +/- 0.069.
- The light scalar mesons sigma/f_0(500), kappa/K_0^*(700), a_0(980), and f_0(980) are discussed as candidates for the light tetraquark nonet lying below 1 GeV.
- The paper reviews the states X(2900), T_cc^+(3875), X(6900), P_cs(4459)^0, Z_cs(3985)^-, Z_cs(4000)^+, and Z_cs(4220)^+ as specific multiquark candidates observed since 2017.

## Context

This review extends the authors' previous reviews (Chen 2016) and (Liu 2019) by covering experimental and theoretical progress in the past five years, including newly observed states such as T_cc^+ and X(6900) from LHCb and Z_cs states from BESIII and LHCb. It also updates discussions on glueballs and hybrid mesons, which remain poorly understood despite decades of study, and it enables future work by providing a systematic categorization of heavy baryon multiplets using heavy quark symmetry and the Pauli principle.
