# Anomalous baryon production in heavy ion collisions at LHC energies

> In ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions the baryon-meson ratio is not fully understood in the intermediate-to-high transverse momentum region. Although quark coalescence methods combined with jet fragmentation yield results close to the experimental data, some tendencies are not reproduced and require further investigation. We propose a new channel, namely extra quark-antiquark and diquark-antidiquark pair production from coherent gluon field created in the early stage of heavy ion collisions. This process is described quantitatively by the time-dependent Schwinger-mechanism. The extra diquark-quark coalescence yields are able to explain the measured anomalous baryon production.

## Key claim

The authors propose that extra quark-antiquark and diquark-antidiquark pairs produced from a coherent gluon field via the time-dependent Schwinger mechanism can explain the anomalous baryon-to-meson ratio at intermediate-to-high transverse momentum, but the effect is conditional on the field being rapidly changing (time-dependent) rather than constant, and the resulting modification to the proton-to-pion ratio may remain relatively small.

## Method

The authors combine three hadron production channels: (1) leading-order pQCD jet fragmentation with GLV jet energy loss, (2) thermal quark coalescence from a Bjorken-expanding fireball at T ≈ 180 MeV, and (3) a new channel of quark and diquark pair production from a time-dependent strong color field described by the quantum kinetic (QK) equations. The external electric field is parameterized with a tanh rise and power-law decay, and the resulting quark and diquark momentum spectra are used as input to coalescence formulas for mesons and baryons.

## Key numbers

- The thermal coalescence parameters fitted to unidentified hadron R_AA at LHC are: τ A_T = 4226 fm³ and v_T = 0.7.
- The time-dependent field parameters fitted to LHC data are: E₀ = 2.2 E_cr (where E_cr = m_q²/g with m_q = 300 GeV), δ = 6.0, τ₁ = 0.31/m_q, τ₂ = 0.36/m_q.
- The deconfinement transition temperature used for the thermal source is T ≈ 180 MeV.
- The center-of-mass energies considered are √s = 200 AGeV (RHIC) and √s = 5.5 ATeV (LHC), with Lorentz factors γ ≈ 100 at RHIC and γ ≈ 2750 at LHC.

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence/recombination models (ALCOR, Hwa, Greco, Fries) and string/rope phenomenology (LUND, FRITIOF, Biro-Nielsen) by relaxing the assumption of a constant color field. It builds on the authors' previous quantum kinetic studies of pair production in strong fields (Levai and Skokov, 2010) and addresses the anomalous baryon-to-meson ratio observed at RHIC by PHENIX and STAR, predicting a similar effect at LHC energies where identified hadron data were not yet available at the time of publication.
