# Measurement of the $\mathrm{{}^{12}C}(e,e')$ cross sections at $Q^2=0.8\,\mathrm{GeV}^2/c^2$

> We present the findings of a study based on a new inelastic electron-scattering experiment on the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ nucleus focusing on the kinematic region of $Q^2=0.8\,\mathrm{GeV}^2/{c}^2$. The measured cross section is sensitive to the transverse response function and provides a stringent test of theoretical models, as well as of the theoretical assumptions made in Monte-Carlo event-generator codes developed for the interpretation of neutrino-nucleus experiments, such as DUNE and HyperK. We find that modern generators such as GENIE and GiBUU reproduce our new experimental data within 10$\%$.

## Key claim
The new inclusive electron-scattering cross sections on carbon at Q²=0.8 GeV²/c² are reproduced by modern event generators GENIE and GiBUU within 10%, but the agreement depends on the specific nuclear model employed; the SuSAv2-MEC model shows 7% average agreement while the local Fermi gas model in GENIE deviates by 22%.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) using an 855 MeV electron beam incident on a 43 mg/cm² carbon foil target. The A1 Collaboration spectrometer A, positioned at a fixed angle of 70°, measured scattered electron energies across seven momentum settings between 310 MeV/c and 650 MeV/c, covering the quasi-elastic peak and Δ-resonance region. The cross sections were extracted by dividing count distributions by integrated luminosity and simulated solid angle, with radiative corrections applied using the Mo and Tsai formalism.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: E₀ = 855 MeV, with momentum transfer |q| = 0.84 GeV/c at the quasi-elastic peak
- Seven momentum settings from 310 MeV/c to 650 MeV/c, each collecting 1.8 million events
- Spectrometer angular acceptance: 28 msr; momentum acceptance: 20%
- Beam current: 2-3 μA, determined with accuracy <0.2%
- Relative momentum resolution (FWHM): 2.4×10⁻⁴; angular resolution: 4.7 mrad; vertex resolution: 9.4 mm
- Energy corrections from radiative and multiple scattering effects: less than 4% effect on measured cross section
- Dominant systematic uncertainty from particle misidentification: 1.7%
- Absolute energy calibration uncertainty: 2.7 MeV (less than 1/3 of energy bin size)
- Quasi-elastic calculations agree with each other at the 4% level at the QE peak top
- SuSAv2-MEC model agrees with data on average at 7%, with 9% discrepancy at the QE peak top
- Ankowski's global Fermi gas model deviates by 10% on average; local Fermi gas model agrees at 8%
- GiBUU agrees with data at 9%
- GENIE with local Fermi gas model agrees at 22% on average; GENIE with SuSAv2 improves at the QE peak
- Barreau et al. data at θ=145° show scaling function over 20% higher at the QE peak compared to this work

## Context
This work extends the sparse data set for ¹²C(e,e') at large momentum transfer (Q²=0.8 GeV²/c²) where the transverse response dominates, complementing earlier measurements by Barreau et al. (1983) at 560 MeV and Sealock et al. (1989) at 1299 MeV. The results provide stringent tests for nuclear models used in neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and HyperK, and for event generators GENIE (Andreopoulos et al., 2009) and GiBUU (Buss et al., 2011). The data challenge the SuSAv2-MEC model of Megias et al. (2016) and the Fermi gas models of Ankowski (2021), particularly in the dip and Δ-resonance regions, motivating future ab-initio calculations with meson-exchange currents and further experimental measurements at Q²≈1 GeV²/c².
