# A relativistic parton cascade with radiation

> We consider the evolution of a parton system which is formed at the central rapidity region just after an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision. The evolution of the system, which is composed of gluons, quarks and antiquarks, is described by a relativistic Boltzmann equations with collision terms including radiation and retardation effects. The equations are solved by the test particle method using Monte-Carlo sampling. Our simulations do not show any evidence of kinetic equilibration, unless the cross sections are artificially increased to unrealistically large values.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic parton cascade including radiative processes (gg → ggg) and retardation effects does not lead to kinetic equilibration of the parton system formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, unless the cross sections are artificially increased by a factor of 10–20 above realistic perturbative values.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic Boltzmann equations for a mixture of gluons, u/d/s quarks and antiquarks using the test particle method with Monte-Carlo sampling. The collision terms include elastic 2→2 processes, the inelastic gg → ggg process, and quark-antiquark pair production/annihilation, with cross sections computed at leading order in αs = 0.3. Retardation effects are incorporated by determining collision points via the maximal force principle from retarded field propagation, and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect suppresses gluon radiation at high density. The initial state uses a boost-invariant flat rapidity distribution and the Krasnitz-Nara-Venugopalan (KNV) transverse momentum distribution with saturation scale Λs = 1 GeV (RHIC) or 2 GeV (LHC).

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections used: σgg→gg ≈ 10/GeV², σqq→qq ≈ 0.6/GeV², σgq→gq ≈ 0.7/GeV², for αs = 0.3 and minimum momentum transfer 0.5 GeV.
- Total cross section for gg → ggg: σTgg→ggg ≈ 3.6/GeV², with Debye mass μD ≈ 0.4 GeV and mean free path λf = 1.0/GeV.
- Initial parton number density per rapidity: dN/dy ≈ 1000–1500, with 6000 initial gluons.
- Formation proper time: τ0 = 0.3 fm/c at RHIC, 0.13 fm/c at LHC.
- Kinetic equilibration (ratio Σ|px|/Σ|pz| ≈ 1) is not achieved for K-factor = 2 or 10, but is achieved for the unrealistically large K-factor = 20.
- Secondary parton production is less abundant than the analytical estimate of Baier, Mueller, Schiff and Son (2000).

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model of Geiger and Müller (1992, 1995) by including the inelastic gg → ggg process, which Baier et al. (2000) argued is essential for kinetic equilibration, and by incorporating retardation effects to eliminate superluminal propagation problems noted by Kortemeyer et al. (1995). The simulations contradict the expectation that radiative processes drive rapid equilibration, showing instead that realistic cross sections yield insufficient isotropization. The results motivate future work on self-consistent treatment of time-dependent screening and inclusion of 3→2 processes to ensure detailed balance.
