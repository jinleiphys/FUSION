# Spectral fluctuations in the interacting boson model

> The energy dependence of the spectral fluctuations in the interacting boson model (IBM) and its connections to the mean-field structures have been analyzed through adopting two statistical measures, the nearest neighbor level spacing distribution $P(S)$ measuring the chaoticity (regularity) in energy spectra and the $Δ_3(L)$ statistics of Dyson and Metha measuring the spectral rigidity. Specifically, the statistical results as functions of the energy cutoff have been worked out for different dynamical situations including the U(5)-SU(3) and SU(3)-O(6) transitions as well as those near the AW arc of regularity. It is found that most of the changes in spectral fluctuations are triggered near the stationary points of the classical potential especially for the cases in the deformed region of the IBM phase diagram. The results thus justify the stationary point effects from the point of view of statistics. In addition, the approximate degeneracies in the $2^+$ spectrum on the AW arc is also revealed from the statistical calculations.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of spectral fluctuations in the interacting boson model (IBM) is shown to be triggered near the stationary points of the classical potential, particularly for cases in the deformed region of the IBM phase diagram, thereby justifying stationary point effects from a statistical perspective.

## Method

The authors used the consistent-Q Hamiltonian of the IBM with control parameters η and χ to study spectral fluctuations at six parameter points covering the U(5)-SU(3) transition, the SU(3)-O(6) transition, and the Alhassid-Whelan (AW) arc of regularity. They computed the nearest neighbor level spacing distribution P(S) and the Δ₃(L) statistics of Dyson and Mehta as functions of the excitation energy cutoff for states with L^π = 0^+, 2^+, and 3^+ at boson number N = 200, fitting the results to the Brody distribution parameter ω and a Δ₃ parameterization q to quantify the degree of chaos.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical phase (point A, η=0.38, χ=-√7/2), the entire 0^+ spectrum gives ω ≈ 0.15 and q ≈ 0.38, indicating a regular system.
- At the critical point of the U(5)-SU(3) transition (point B, η=0.47, χ=-√7/2), the entire 0^+ spectrum gives ω ≈ 0.45, and ω reaches a maximum ω_max > 0.84 with q_max > 0.9 around E/N ∼ 0.2.
- For the deformed phase (point C, η=0.75, χ=-√7/2), the ω and q values for 0^+ states show minimal values exactly at the local minimum energy E_lmin, with ω = 0.25 (q = 0.45) for 2^+ levels below E/N = 0.19 but ω = 0.79 (q = 0.92) for those above E/N = 0.19.
- On the AW arc (point D, η=0.88, χ=-1.032), the entire 2^+ spectrum gives ω ∼ 0 and q ∼ 0, and the 0^+ spectrum shows ω ∼ 0.3 when the energy cutoff is taken at the local maximum energy E_lmax.
- The SU(3) limit gives ω ∼ 0.12 (q ∼ 0.40) for the 0^+ spectrum and ω ∼ 0.16 (q ∼ 0.43) for the 3^+ spectrum, while the 2^+ spectrum shows a negative ω value due to degeneracies.

## Context

This work extends the statistical analysis of spectral fluctuations in the IBM previously performed by Alhassid (1990-1993) and Whelan (1993), who established the chaotic map of the IBM triangle and discovered the AW arc of regularity. It also builds on the analysis of Karampagia (2015) that studied energy-dependent statistics for 0^+ states near the AW arc, and on the work of Zhang (2016, 2021) that connected excited state quantum phase transitions to stationary points of the potential. The results enable a statistical criterion for distinguishing different families of excited states based on mean-field stationary points, and confirm that the excited state quantum phase transition in nonintegrable U(5)-SU(3) cases is accompanied by a sudden emergence of spectral chaos, as suggested by Fernandez (2011) for quantum optical models.
