# $η_{c}$ Elastic and Transition Form Factors: Contact Interaction and Algebraic Model

> For the flavor-singlet heavy quark system of charmonia in the pseudoscalar ($η_c(1S)$) channel, we calculate the elastic (EFF) and transition form factors (TFF) ($η_c(1S) \rightarrow γγ^*$) for a wide range of photon momentum transfer squared ($Q^2$). The framework for this analysis is provided by a symmetry-preserving Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) treatment of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction (CI). We also employ an algebraic model (AM), developed earlier to describe the light quark systems. It correctly correlates infrared and ultraviolet dynamics of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The CI results agree with the lattice data for low $Q^2$. For $Q^2 \geqslant Q_0^2$, the results start deviating from the lattice results by more than $20 \%$. $Q_0^2 \thickapprox 2.5 {\rm GeV}^2$ for the EFF and $\thickapprox 25 {\rm GeV}^2$ for the TFF. We also present the results for the EFF, TFF as well as $η_c(1S)$ parton distribution amplitude for the AM. Wherever the comparison is possible, these results are in excellent agreement with the lattice, perturbative QCD, the results obtained through an SDE-BSE study, employing refined truncations, as well as the experimental findings of the BABAR experiment.

## Key claim
The contact interaction (CI) model, extended from light quarks to charmonia, reproduces lattice QCD results for the ηc elastic form factor (EFF) only for low Q²; above Q² ≈ 2.5 GeV² the CI results deviate from lattice data by more than 20%, whereas the algebraic model (AM) agrees with lattice results across the full range shown.

## Method
The authors employed a symmetry-preserving Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) treatment using a vector×vector contact interaction (CI) with rainbow-ladder truncation, regularized via proper-time regularization. They also used an SDE-based algebraic model (AM) that correlates infrared and ultraviolet QCD dynamics, extending it from light quarks to charmonia by fixing the parameter ν = 1 and setting the ηc mass and decay constant to known values. The quark-photon vertex was dressed to satisfy the vector Ward-Takahashi identity, introducing a pole at the J/ψ mass.

## Key numbers
- For the CI, the ηc EFF deviates from lattice QCD by more than 20% for Q² ≥ 2.5 GeV²; the transition form factor (TFF) deviates for Q² ≥ 25 GeV².
- The CI best-fit parameters: m_g = 0.8 GeV, α_IR = 0.93π/20, Λ_IR = 0.24 GeV, Λ_UV = 2.788 GeV, current-quark mass m_c = 0.956 GeV, dynamically generated mass M_c = 1.497 GeV.
- The CI yields ηc mass 2.950 GeV (experiment: 2.983 GeV) and decay constant f_ηc = 0.305 GeV (lattice QCD: 0.395 GeV).
- The AM fixes f_ηc = 0.361 GeV and uses σ = m_π / m_ηc.
- The lattice QCD fit to ηc EFF uses the form F_ηc(Q²) = exp[−Q²/(16β²)(1 + αQ²)] with β = 0.480(3) GeV and α = −0.046(1) GeV⁻² on the range [0, 5.5] GeV².

## Context
This work extends the CI model previously applied to light mesons (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2010, 2011) and to charmonia masses and decay constants (Bedolla 2015) to compute ηc elastic and transition form factors. The AM was originally developed for the pion (Chang 2013, Raya 2015) and is here extended to charmonia. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD (Dudek 2007), perturbative QCD, and BABAR experimental data, and highlight the limitations of the CI at high Q² while showing the AM’s ability to reproduce lattice results across a wide momentum range.
