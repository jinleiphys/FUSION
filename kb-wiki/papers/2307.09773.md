# Quantization of the kinetic energy of a deformed nucleus in curvilinear coordinates

> The quantization of the kinetic energy of a deformed nucleus in curvilinear coordinates in the case of octupole oscillations of its surface firstly has been carried out. The obtained form of the Hamiltonian differs from the previously obtained Hamiltonian for quadrupole oscillations only by factors in front of the derivatives $\partial/\partialγ$ and $\partial/\partialη$. An explicit form of the kinetic energy of the Hamiltonian of even-even nuclei with free and effective triaxiality, as well as for axially symmetric even-even nuclei, is given.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first quantization of the kinetic energy of a deformed nucleus in curvilinear coordinates specifically for octupole (third-order spherical harmonic) surface oscillations, but the resulting Hamiltonian differs from the previously known quadrupole case only by the factors in front of the derivatives with respect to the angular variables \(\gamma\) and \(\eta\), due to the different expressions for the moments of inertia.

## Method

The authors start from the classical kinetic energy for octupole surface oscillations expressed in terms of the collective coordinates \(a_{30}\) and \(a_{32}\) and Euler angles, then apply the Pauli quantization procedure in curvilinear coordinates to obtain the quantum kinetic energy operator. They compute the metric tensor, its determinant, and the inverse metric components for the five-dimensional space, then transform the resulting vibrational operator from the coordinates \((a_{30}, a_{32})\) to the polar coordinates \((\beta_3, \eta)\). Finally, they specialize the general result to three cases: free triaxiality (variable \(\eta\)), effective triaxiality (fixed \(\eta = \eta_{eff}\)), and axial symmetry (\(\eta = 0\)).

## Key numbers

* The determinant of the metric tensor for the octupole case is \(G = 64 B_3^5 \sin^2\theta_2 \, a_{32}^2 \left[(3a_{30}^2 + 4a_{32}^2)^2 - 30 a_{30}^2 a_{32}^2\right]\).
* The moments of inertia for octupole deformation are given as \(J^{(3)}_1 = 4B_3\beta_3^2(\sin^2\eta + \frac{\sqrt{15}}{2}\sin\eta\cos\eta + \frac{3}{2}\cos^2\eta)\), \(J^{(3)}_2 = 4B_3\beta_3^2(\sin^2\eta - \frac{\sqrt{15}}{2}\sin\eta\cos\eta + \frac{3}{2}\cos^2\eta)\), and \(J^{(3)}_3 = 4B_3\beta_3^2\sin^2\eta\).
* The vibrational kinetic energy operator in \((\beta_3, \eta)\) coordinates for free triaxiality is \(\hat{T}_{vib} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_3}\left[\frac{\partial^2}{\partial \beta_3^2} + \frac{4}{\beta_3}\frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_3} + \frac{1}{\beta_3^2}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial \eta^2} + \frac{24\cos^2 2\eta - 6\cos 2\eta}{5+5\cos 2\eta+8\cos^2 2\eta}\frac{\cos\eta}{\beta_3^2\sin\eta}\frac{\partial}{\partial \eta}\right]\).
* For axially symmetric nuclei (\(\eta = 0\)), the kinetic energy reduces to \(\hat{T}_{\beta_3} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_3}\frac{1}{\beta_3^2}\frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_3}\left(\beta_3^2\frac{\partial}{\partial \beta_3}\right)\), with volume element \(d\tau = \beta_3^2 d\beta_3 d\theta_1 \sin\theta_2 d\theta_2 d\theta_3\).

## Context

This work extends the quantization procedure previously applied to quadrupole surface oscillations (Greiner, using the Pauli procedure) to the case of octupole oscillations, which had not been performed before. The resulting Hamiltonian structure is compared directly with the quadrupole case of Greiner, showing that the differences arise solely from the distinct forms of the moments of inertia for quadrupole versus octupole deformations. The results enable future numerical solutions of the Schrodinger equation for octupole collective excitations in even-even nuclei, and provide explicit forms for three common approximations (free triaxiality, effective triaxiality, and axial symmetry) that can be used in nuclear structure models.
