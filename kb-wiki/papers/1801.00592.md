# QCD Parameters Correlations from Heavy Quarkonia

> Correlations between the QCD coupling α_s, the gluon condensate < α_s G^2 >, and the c,b-quark running masses m_c,b in the MS-scheme are explicitly studied (for the first time) from the (axial-)vector and (pseudo)scalar charmonium and bottomium ratios of Laplace sum rules (LSR) evaluated at the μ-subtraction stability point where PT @N2LO, N3LO and < α_s G^2> @NLO corrections are included. Our results clarify the (apparent) discrepancies between different estimates of < α_s G^2> from J/ψsum rule but also shows the sensitivity of the sum rules on the choice of the μ-subtraction scale which does not permit a high-precision estimate of m_c,b. We obtain from the (axial-)vector [resp. (pseudo)scalar] channels <α_s G^2>=(8.5+- 3.0)> [resp. (6.34+-.39)] 10^-2 GeV^4, m_c(m_c)= 1256(30) [resp. 1266(16)] MeV and m_b(m_b)=4192(15) MeV. Combined with our recent determinations from vector channel, one obtains the average: m_c(m_c)= 1263(14) MeV and m_b(m_b) 4184(11) MeV. Adding our value of the gluon condensate with different previous estimates, we obtain the new sum rule average: <α_s G^2>=(6.35+- 0.35) 10^-2 GeV^4. The mass-splittings M_χ_0c(0b)-M_η_c(b) give @N2LO: α_s(M_Z)=0.1183(19)(3) in good agreement with the world average (see more detailed discussions in the section: addendum). .

## Key claim
Correlations between the QCD coupling α_s, the gluon condensate ⟨α_s G^2⟩, and the c,b-quark running masses in the MS-scheme are studied from Laplace sum rules, but the results show that the sensitivity of the sum rules to the choice of the μ-subtraction scale does not permit a high-precision estimate of m_c,b, and the J/ψ sum rule alone cannot strongly constrain both ⟨α_s G^2⟩ and m_c simultaneously.

## Method
The author analyzes (axial-)vector and (pseudo)scalar charmonium and bottomium spectra using optimized ratios of Laplace sum rules (LSR) evaluated at the μ-subtraction stability point. Perturbative QCD corrections at N2LO and N3LO are included, along with non-perturbative gluon condensate contributions at NLO and leading-order D=6-8 dimension condensates. The spectral functions are parametrized using a narrow width approximation for known resonances and a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c, with the ratio of moments R^c_n used to extract hadron masses.

## Key numbers
- ⟨α_s G^2⟩ = (8.5 ± 3.0) × 10^{-2} GeV^4 from the (axial-)vector channel; (6.34 ± 0.39) × 10^{-2} GeV^4 from the (pseudo)scalar channel
- m_c(m_c) = 1256(30) MeV from the (axial-)vector channel; 1266(16) MeV from the (pseudo)scalar channel
- m_b(m_b) = 4192(15) MeV from the (pseudo)scalar channel
- Combined average: m_c(m_c) = 1263(14) MeV and m_b(m_b) = 4184(11) MeV
- New sum rule average for gluon condensate: ⟨α_s G^2⟩ = (6.35 ± 0.35) × 10^{-2} GeV^4
- α_s(M_Z) = 0.1183(19)(3) from mass-splittings M_χ_{0c(0b)} - M_η_{c(b)} at N2LO

## Context
This work extends the SVZ (1979) sum rule program for heavy quarkonia, clarifying apparent discrepancies between different estimates of the gluon condensate from J/ψ sum rules reported by various authors (e.g., Ioffe and Zyablyuk 2007, Narison 2012). The results enable improved determinations of QCD parameters by accounting for correlations between α_s, the gluon condensate, and quark masses, and provide a new average for ⟨α_s G^2⟩ that combines these results with previous estimates. The analysis also uses the ratio of dimension-six gluon condensate over the gluon condensate from Narison (2012) and compares with lattice results from Bali and Pineda (2015) and others.
