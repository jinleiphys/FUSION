# Aspects of approximation in modelling of the symmetry energy

> Quality of approximations is an important issue in modelling nuclear matter. It is shown that the Pad{\' e} approximation provides a useful tool for describing the symmetry energy in highly asymmetric systems. The focus is on the symmetry energy constraints. Some implications of the obtained results for astrophysics are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Padé approximation provides a more reliable tool than the standard Taylor (parabolic) expansion for describing the symmetry energy in highly asymmetric nuclear matter, particularly at densities above saturation where the Taylor series may not converge. The authors hedge that this approach depends on the validity of the Padé convergence conjecture and on the unknown density dependence of the quartic term \(E_{4,sym}(n_b)\).

## Method

The authors apply the \([2,2]\) Padé approximant to the energy density of asymmetric nuclear matter expanded in the isospin asymmetry parameter \(\delta_a\), using the coefficients from the Taylor expansion (the quadratic term \(E_{2,sym}\) and the quartic term \(E_{4,sym}\)). They then derive a modified symmetry energy expression \(E_{sym}^P(n_b,\delta_a) = g(n_b) / (1 - f(n_b)\delta_a^2)\), where \(g = E_{2,sym}\) and \(f = E_{4,sym}/E_{2,sym}\). The calculations are performed within a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with nonlinear scalar-vector and mixed isoscalar-isovector couplings, using the TM1 parameter set with varying values of the coupling \(\Lambda_V\) (0.0, 0.015, 0.03) to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density is \(E_{2,sym}(n_0) = 31.6 \pm 2.66\) MeV, with slope \(L(n_0) = 58.9 \pm 16\) MeV, from combined terrestrial and astrophysical constraints (citing Li 2017).
* Theoretical estimates of the quartic term at saturation density \(E_{4,sym}(n_0)\) range from \(1.5\) MeV (chiral pion-nucleon dynamics, Kaiser 2015) to \(20 \pm 4.6\) MeV (extended nuclear mass formula, Wang 2017), with other models giving \(7.18 \pm 2.52\) MeV (interacting Fermi gas, Cai 2015), \(3.27 \sim 12.7\) MeV (quantum molecular dynamics, Nandi 2016), and \(\leq 2\) MeV (relativistic mean field models, Cai 2012).
* From the ASY-EOS experiment on \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, the power-law coefficient for the potential part of the symmetry energy is \(\gamma = 0.72 \pm 0.19\) (Russotto 2016), compared to an earlier FOPI-LAND result of \(\gamma = 0.9 \pm 0.4\).
* The TM1 parameter set used includes meson masses: \(m_\sigma = 511.198\) MeV, \(m_\omega = 783\) MeV, \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV, and coupling constants \(g_\sigma = 10.029\), \(g_\omega = 12.614\), with \(g_\rho\) varying from 9.2644 (for \(\Lambda_V = 0.0\)) to 11.0964 (for \(\Lambda_V = 0.03\)).

## Context

This work extends the standard parabolic approximation of the symmetry energy (which truncates the Taylor expansion at second order in \(\delta_a\)) by introducing a Padé approximant that incorporates the quartic term \(E_{4,sym}\), motivated by the need to describe highly asymmetric systems such as neutron star interiors. The approach addresses the problem that the Taylor expansion may not converge at high densities, as noted by Gonzales (2017) and others. The paper builds on prior RMF models with mixed isoscalar-isovector couplings (Horowitz 2001, Sharma 2009, Bednarek 2015) and on experimental constraints from heavy ion collisions (FOPI-LAND, ASY-EOS Collaboration 2016). It enables future work to test the role of the quartic term in neutron star matter and to compare Padé-based symmetry energy predictions with transport model analyses of isospin-sensitive observables.
