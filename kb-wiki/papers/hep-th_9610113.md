# Multi-soliton dynamics in the Skyrme model

> We exhibit the dynamical scattering of multi-solitons in the Skyrme model for configurations with charge two, three and four. First, we construct maximally attractive configurations from a simple profile function and the product ansatz. Then using a sophisticated numerical algorithm, initially well-separated skyrmions in approximately symmetric configurations are shown to scatter through the known minimum energy configurations. These scattering events illustrate a number of similarities to BPS monopole configurations of the same charge. A simple modification of the dynamics to a dissipative regime, allows us to compute the minimal energy skyrmions for baryon numbers one to four to within a few percent.

## Key claim

The dynamical scattering of multi-skyrmions with baryon numbers two, three, and four proceeds through the known minimum-energy configurations (torus, tetrahedron, cube) in a manner qualitatively similar to BPS monopole scattering, but the analogy is not exact because the Skyrme model's potential prevents the formation of certain configurations (e.g., the toroidal B=3 state) that occur in the monopole case.

## Method

The authors constructed initial configurations of well-separated skyrmions using the product ansatz with a sine-Gordon kink profile function, choosing relative isospin orientations to maximize attraction. They then evolved these configurations using a numerical algorithm with fourth-order spatial differences on grids of 70^3 to 100^3 points, with spatial step Δx=0.1 and time steps 0.01 ≤ Δt ≤ 0.02, solving the Skyrme model field equations in the massless pion limit. To compute minimum-energy configurations, they added a phenomenological dissipation term (ε=0.5) to the equations of motion and allowed the system to settle to static solutions.

## Key numbers

- Single skyrmion energy: E=1.232 (from numerical solution of the profile function)
- Sine-Gordon kink approximation for the profile function gives a skyrmion with energy less than 1% above the true value
- Initial skyrmion separation parameter: a=1.5 for all scattering simulations
- Initial velocities: v=0.3 for B=2 head-on collision; v=0.1√3 for B=3 triangular configuration; v=0.1 for B=3 collinear configuration; no initial boost for B=4 tetrahedral configuration
- Computed energy per baryon (E/B) for minimum-energy skyrmions in the massless pion limit: B=1: 1.233; B=2: 1.171; B=3: 1.149; B=4: 1.110
- Computed baryon numbers from the simulations: B=1: 0.984; B=2: 1.971; B=3: 2.965; B=4: 3.945
- Instanton-computed energies per baryon (E_I) for comparison: B=1: 1.243; B=2: 1.192; B=3: 1.163; B=4: 1.132
- Dissipation coefficient used: ε=0.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-skyrmion dynamics (using analytic methods by Atiyah and Manton (1993) and numerical simulations by Alford et al. (1992)) to higher baryon numbers B=3 and B=4, and demonstrates that symmetric scattering configurations motivated by BPS monopole dynamics (Hitchin et al. (1995)) produce the known minimum-energy skyrmions with tetrahedral and cubic symmetries found by Battye and Sutcliffe (1996) and Leese and Manton (1994). The results enable future studies of higher-charge configurations (B>4) and saddle point configurations, and provide more accurate energy calculations than the instanton holonomy method of Atiyah and Manton (1993), which overestimates energies by about 1%.
