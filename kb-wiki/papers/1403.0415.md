# Domain wall network as QCD vacuum and the chromomagnetic trap formation under extreme conditions

> The ensemble of Euclidean gluon field configurations represented by the domain wall network is considered. A single domain wall is given by the sine-Gordon kink for the angle between chromomagnetic and chromoelectric components of the gauge field. The domain wall separates the regions with self-dual and anti-self-dual fields. The network of the domain wall defects is introduced as a combination of multiplicative and additive superpositions of kinks. The character of the spectrum and eigenmodes of color-charged fluctuations in the presence of the domain wall network is discussed. The concept of the confinement-deconfinement transition in terms of the ensemble of domain wall networks is outlined. Conditions for the formation of thick domain wall junction during heavy ion collisions are discussed, and the spectrum of color charged quasiparticles inside the trap is evaluated. An important observation is the existence of the critical size $L_c$ of the trap stable against gluon tachyonic modes, which means that deconfinement can occur only in a finite region of space-time in principle. The size $L_c$ is related to the value of gluon condensate $\langle g^2F^2\rangle$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the QCD vacuum can be described as an ensemble of domain wall networks, and that a thick domain wall junction (chromomagnetic trap) of finite size can form during heavy ion collisions, with the critical size \(L_c\) for stability against gluon tachyonic modes being related to the value of the gluon condensate \(\langle g^2F^2\rangle\). The claim is hedged by the condition that deconfinement can occur only in a finite region of space-time in principle, and that the existence of the critical size means the trap is stable only below that size.

## Method

The authors construct domain wall networks in Euclidean \(R^4\) using multiplicative and additive superpositions of sine-Gordon kinks for the angle between chromomagnetic and chromoelectric components of the gauge field, starting from the Abelian (anti-)self-dual homogeneous fields that are global minima of a Ginzburg-Landau type effective Lagrangian. They solve the eigenvalue problem for color-charged scalar, vector, and spinor fluctuations in the background of a single infinitely thin planar domain wall by matching bulk and wall solutions via continuity of the charged current, and then evaluate the spectrum inside a thick cylindrical domain wall junction representing a chromomagnetic trap.

## Key numbers

* The eigenvalues for confined scalar fluctuations in the bulk are \(\lambda_{kl} = 2|\breve n|B(k+l+1)\) with \(k,l=0,1,\dots\), where \(B\) is the field strength parameter and \(\breve n\) is a color matrix.
* The dispersion relation for color-charged quasiparticles on the infinitely thin domain wall is \(p_0^2 = p_2^2 + \mu_k^2\) with masses \(\mu_k^2 = 2\sqrt{2}(2k+1)|\breve n|B\) for \(k=0,1,2,\dots\).
* The critical size \(L_c\) of the chromomagnetic trap stable against gluon tachyonic modes is related to the gluon condensate \(\langle g^2F^2\rangle\), though no explicit numerical value for \(L_c\) is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nedelko and Voronin (2011) by providing explicit analytical construction of domain wall networks and refining the spectrum of color-charged fields in their background. It contrasts with approaches based on localized topological configurations such as instantons, monopoles, and vortices (e.g., Ilgenfritz 2007, Moran 2008, de Forcrand 2008), which have vanishing action-to-volume ratio, whereas the domain wall network has extensive action proportional to the four-volume. The results enable further study of the confinement-deconfinement transition in heavy ion collisions, where strong electromagnetic fields (Skokov 2009, Toneev, Warringa) can trigger the formation of a chromomagnetic trap, and the spectrum of quasiparticles inside the trap can be evaluated for comparison with experimental signatures.
