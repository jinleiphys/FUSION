# The rho meson in a scenario of pure chiral restoration

> Based on QCD sum rules we explore the consequences of a pure chiral restoration scenario for the rho meson, where all chiral symmetry breaking condensates are dropped whereas the chirally symmetric condensates remain at their vacuum values. This pure chiral restoration scenario causes the drop of the rho spectral moment by about 120 MeV. The complementarity of mass shift and broadening is discussed. A simple parametrization of the rho spectral function leads to a width of about 600 MeV if no shift of the peak position is assumed.

## Key claim

Based on QCD sum rules, the authors explore a "pure chiral restoration" scenario where only chiral symmetry breaking condensates are dropped while chirally symmetric condensates remain at their vacuum values; this scenario causes the rho meson spectral moment to drop by about 120 MeV, but the authors stress that this may not reflect all the physics contained in QCD and shows only the minimal impact of chiral restoration.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules with the operator-product expansion (OPE) up to mass dimension 6, classifying all relevant condensates by their behavior under chiral transformations. They fix vacuum condensate values from experimental data and the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, then set the chiral symmetry breaking condensates (the two-quark condensate and the chirally odd part of the four-quark condensate) to zero while keeping chirally symmetric condensates unchanged. They compute the normalized spectral moment of the rho meson and also explore a Breit-Wigner parametrization to study the complementarity of mass shift and broadening.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum rho meson mass is reproduced at 775.5 MeV with continuum threshold s_+ = 1.37 GeV^2 using the chirally symmetric four-quark condensate value (267 MeV)^6
- In the pure chiral restoration scenario, the averaged spectral moment drops to 659.8 MeV with continuum threshold s_+ = 1.03 GeV^2, a drop of about 120 MeV
- The chirally symmetric four-quark condensate extracted from the Breit-Wigner fit is (242 MeV)^6
- The vacuum two-quark condensate is -(240 MeV)^3 from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation with F_pi ≈ 92 MeV, m_q = 6 MeV, and M_pi ≈ 140 MeV
- The gluon condensate is (330 MeV)^4 and the strong coupling α_s = 0.38

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the connection between chiral restoration and in-medium modifications of light vector mesons, challenging the naive chain of arguments from Shifman (1978) and Hatsuda (1991) that linked dropping four-quark condensates directly to mass drops. The authors note that most condensates entering the rho meson sum rule are actually chirally symmetric, and that the factorization of four-quark condensates (point 2 in the traditional argument) is questionable. The pure chiral restoration scenario provides a baseline for minimal changes expected from chiral restoration alone, enabling future work to distinguish effects from chirally symmetric condensate changes versus genuine chiral symmetry breaking effects, as discussed in approaches by Rapp (1999) and Leupold (2008).
