# The three channels of many-body perturbation theory: $GW$, particle-particle, and electron-hole $T$-matrix self-energies

> We derive the explicit expression of the three self-energies that one encounters in many-body perturbation theory: the well-known $GW$ self-energy, as well as the particle-particle and electron-hole $T$-matrix self-energies. Each of these can be easily computed via the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a different random-phase approximation (RPA) linear eigenvalue problem that completely defines their corresponding response function. For illustrative and comparative purposes, we report the principal ionization potentials of a set of small molecules computed at each level of theory. The performance of these schemes on strongly correlated systems (\ce{B2} and \ce{C2}) is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit expressions for the three self-energies of many-body perturbation theory (GW, particle-particle T-matrix, and electron-hole T-matrix) on an equal footing via eigenvalues and eigenvectors of different random-phase approximation (RPA) linear eigenvalue problems, but the electron-hole T-matrix self-energy expression is presented as the central new result, and its renormalization factor is not confined to the 0-to-1 range unlike the other two channels.

## Method

The authors recast Hedin's equations into an alternative form using the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-body correlation function, then derived the GW self-energy by approximating the kernel as the bare Coulomb interaction and the T-matrix self-energies by approximating the two-body correlation function as the non-interacting one while introducing an effective four-point interaction T. The three self-energies were computed via the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of three distinct RPA linear eigenvalue problems: the standard electron-hole RPA for GW, the particle-particle RPA for the pp T-matrix, and an exchange-type electron-hole RPA for the eh T-matrix. Principal ionization potentials were calculated for 20 small molecules (the GW20 set) using the def2-TZVPP basis set with a Hartree-Fock starting point in one-shot schemes (G0W0, G0T0pp, G0T0eh).

## Key numbers

- The GW20 set consists of 20 atoms and molecules from the GW100 test set (vanSetten 2015), with geometries extracted from that reference.
- The basis set used is def2-TZVPP.
- The renormalization factor Z for the eh T-matrix self-energy is not confined between 0 and 1; values can extend below 0 and above 1, unlike in GW and pp T-matrix where 0 ≤ Z ≤ 1.
- The paper reports principal ionization potentials computed at each level of theory for the GW20 set, but no specific numerical IP values are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Romaniello (2012) and references therein, which provided an alternative form of Hedin's equations, by deriving the electron-hole T-matrix self-energy in terms of RPA eigenvalues and eigenvectors for the first time. The particle-particle T-matrix expression follows earlier work by Zhang (2017) and Li (2021, 2023). The paper enables systematic comparison of the three many-body perturbation theory channels on molecular systems, and the eh T-matrix approximation is noted as previously used in solids for electron-magnon scattering (Muller 2019, Myczak 2019, Friedrich 2019, Nabok 2021) but never before applied to quasiparticle energies in realistic molecular systems.
