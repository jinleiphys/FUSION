# On the Possible Violation of Sum Rules for Higher-Twist Parton Distributions

> Precise measurements of polarized electro-production and Drell-Yan in the deep inelastic limit will soon provide first information on the higher twist parton distributions $g_T(x)$ and $h_L(x)$. Sum rules for higher-twist structure functions are only valid provided the corresponding Compton amplitudes satisfy un-subtracted dispersion relations. Subtracted dispersion relations have to be used when the (real part of the) forward scattering amplitudes does not fall off rapidly enough for $ν\rightarrow \infty$ (fixed $Q^2$). Formally, such subtractions lead to $δ$-functions at the origin in the parton distributions, which are not accessible to experiment, and the integral over the data fails to satisfy the sum rule. The $δ$-functions in the parton distributions can be identified with the zero-modes that appear in light-front quantization. An explicit {\it infinite momentum boost} identifies these soft quark modes with low momentum contributions to the self-energy.

## Key claim

Sum rules for higher-twist parton distributions like \(g_T(x)\) and \(h_L(x)\) are only valid if the corresponding Compton amplitudes satisfy un-subtracted dispersion relations; if subtracted dispersion relations are required, the integral over experimental data will fail to satisfy the sum rule due to \(\delta\)-functions at the origin in the parton distributions that are not experimentally accessible.

## Method

The author analyzes the operator product expansion (OPE) and dispersion relations for forward scattering amplitudes in deep inelastic scattering, showing that polynomial subtractions in the dispersion relation correspond to \(\delta\)-functions in the light-cone parton distributions. A perturbative one-loop calculation in QCD is performed for \(h_L(x)\) and \(g_T(x)\) for a quark dressed by a gluon, and toy models including a Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions and a linear sigma model are used to demonstrate the mechanism explicitly.

## Key numbers

- In the perturbative QCD calculation for \(h_L(x)\), a \(\delta(x)\) term appears with coefficient proportional to \(g^2 \frac{m}{4\pi^2} \log(\Lambda^2_\perp / m^2)\), where \(m\) is the current quark mass and \(\Lambda_\perp\) is a transverse cutoff.
- In the Gross-Neveu model, the coefficient of the \(\delta(x')\) term in the even part of \(e_{s/u}(x',Q^2)\) behaves as \(\frac{c}{2M_F} \delta(x') \log(Q^2/M_F^2)\) in the limit \(Q^2 \to \infty\), where \(M_F\) is the physical fermion mass.
- In the linear sigma model calculation in 1+1 dimensions, the parton distribution \(e_{d/u}(x)\) contains a \(\delta(x)\) term with coefficient \(\frac{\gamma^2}{2\pi m}\), where \(\gamma\) is the Yukawa coupling and \(m\) is the fermion mass.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of sum-rule violations for higher-twist parton distributions, building on the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule (Burkhardt and Cottingham, 1970) for \(g_2(x)\) and similar sum rules for \(h_L(x)\) derived by Nagai (1992) and Mulders (1990). The paper shows that such sum rules can fail due to subtraction terms in dispersion relations, which are connected to zero-modes in light-front quantization. This enables experimental tests of the \(h_L\) sum rule using polarized Drell-Yan processes at RHIC or semi-inclusive measurements at HERMES, and it warns that sum-rule violations may be significant for strange and heavier quarks.
