# Neutrino Predictions from Generalized CP Symmetries of Charged Leptons

> We study the implications of generalized CP transformations acting on the mass matrices of charged leptons in a model-independent way. Generalized $e-μ$, $e-τ$ and $μ-τ$ symmetries are considered in detail. In all cases the physical parameters of the lepton mixing matrix, three mixing angles and three CP phases can be expressed in terms of a restricted set of independent "theory" parameters that characterize a given choice of CP transformation. This leads to implications for neutrino oscillations as well as neutrinoless double beta decay experiments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that imposing generalized CP transformations on the charged lepton mass matrices, rather than on the neutrino sector, can predict all lepton mixing parameters (three mixing angles and three CP phases) in terms of a restricted set of "theory parameters" that characterize a given CP transformation, with the hedge that the predictions for neutrino oscillation parameters hold equally for both Majorana and Dirac neutrinos while the Majorana phase predictions and neutrinoless double beta decay implications apply only to the Majorana case.

## Method

The authors work in the basis where the neutrino mass matrix is diagonal and impose generalized CP transformations on the charged lepton sector, considering three specific symmetry types: generalized e-mu, e-tau, and mu-tau reflection symmetries. Using the Takagi decomposition of the CP transformation matrix and a real orthogonal matrix parameterized by three angles, they derive the lepton mixing matrix and extract expressions for the mixing angles, Dirac CP phase, and Majorana CP phases in terms of the CP labels (phases alpha, beta, gamma and angle Theta) and the orthogonal matrix parameters theta_1, theta_2, theta_3.

## Key numbers

* For the generalized e-mu symmetry with alpha=beta=0, using best-fit mixing angles and delta_CP=270 degrees, the CP label Theta is predicted to be 69.0 degrees or 111.0 degrees for both normal ordering (NO) and inverted ordering (IO), with theta_1=41.0 degrees (NO) or 50.5 degrees (IO), theta_2=8.4 or 171.6 degrees, and theta_3=0.0 or 90.0 degrees.
* For the generalized e-mu symmetry, the solar mixing angle theta_12 is constrained by the relation cos(Theta)*cos(2*theta_3)=cos(2*theta_12), and from the 3-sigma range 0.273<sin^2(theta_12)<0.379, the CP label Theta is restricted to 0.242<|cos(Theta)|<=1, excluding Theta=pi/2 at 3-sigma.
* For the generalized e-mu symmetry with Theta=3*pi/8, the predicted values are: theta_12=34.5 degrees, theta_13=8.44 degrees, theta_23=41.0 degrees, delta_CP=278.3 degrees, phi_12=67.8 degrees, phi_13=173.0 degrees (for one solution) and delta_CP=261.7 degrees, phi_12=112.2 degrees, phi_13=7.0 degrees (for another solution).

## Context

This work extends the approach of generalized CP symmetries previously applied to the neutrino sector (e.g., the generalized mu-tau reflection symmetry studied by Chen et al. (2015)) by instead imposing these symmetries on the charged lepton mass matrices in a model-independent way. The paper contrasts with the standard mu-tau reflection symmetry (Harrison, Grimus, and collaborators) which predicts maximal theta_23 and delta_CP, showing that generalized e-mu and e-tau symmetries can accommodate non-maximal mixing angles while still providing testable correlations among mixing parameters. The results enable future neutrino oscillation experiments and neutrinoless double beta decay searches to test specific CP symmetry scenarios through correlations such as the theta_12-delta_CP relation given by sin^2(2*theta_12)*sin^2(delta_CP)=sin^2(Theta).
