# Spontaneous CP violation in quark scattering from QCD Z(3) interfaces

> In this paper, we explore the possibility of spontaneous CP violation in the scattering of quarks and anti-quarks from QCD Z(3) domain walls. The CP violation here arises from the nontrivial profile of the background gauge field $(A_{0})$ between different Z(3) vacua. We calculate the spatial variation of $A_{0}$ across the Z(3) interface from the profile of the Polyakov loop $L(\vec{x})$ for the Z(3) interface and calculate the reflection of quarks and antiquarks using the Dirac equation. This spontaneous CP violation has interesting consequences for the relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, such as baryon enhancement at high $P_{T}$. It also acts as a source of additional J/$ψ$ suppression. We also discuss its implications for the early universe.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quarks and antiquarks have different reflection coefficients when scattering from QCD Z(3) domain walls, leading to spontaneous CP violation that arises from the nontrivial spatial profile of the background gauge field \(A_{0}\) between different Z(3) vacua, though the analysis is performed for pure SU(3) gauge theory without dynamical quarks.

## Method

The authors first obtain the spatial profile of the Polyakov loop \(L(\vec{x})\) across a Z(3) interface by minimizing the energy of an effective potential for the Polyakov loop, using the parametrization proposed by Pisarski (2000). They then invert the relation between the Polyakov loop and the gauge field \(A_{0}\) to extract the spatial variation of \(A_{0}\) across the wall, assuming \(A_{0}\) is diagonal in color space and independent of Euclidean time. Finally, they numerically solve the Dirac equation in Minkowski space by approximating the smooth \(A_{0}\) potential as a sequence of step potentials, following the technique of Kalotas and Lee (1991), to compute reflection and transmission coefficients for quarks and antiquarks.

## Key numbers

* Surface tension of Z(3) walls: \(\sigma = 0.34\), 2.62, and 7 GeV/fm\(^2\) for \(T = 200\), 300, and 400 MeV respectively, using the Pisarski (2000) effective potential.
* For the Fukushima (2003) effective potential, the wall profile and \(A_{0}\) profile were calculated at \(T = 391\) MeV (with \(T_c = 178\) MeV) and found to be very close to those from the Pisarski potential at \(T = 400\) MeV.
* The fitted \(A_{0}\) profile parameters for the \((1,1)\) component: \(p = -378.27\), \(q = 7.95001\), \(r = -49.7141\), \(s = -1692.48\) (units not specified in text).
* Reflection coefficients for quarks and antiquarks were calculated at \(E = 3.0\) GeV for \(u\), \(d\), \(s\), and \(c\) quarks, with masses \(m = 2.5\), 5.0, 100, and 127 MeV respectively; the effect is significantly higher for heavier quarks such as charm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Korthals-Altes (1992, 1994) who first demonstrated spontaneous CP violation from Z(3) walls in Euclidean formalism but did not determine the exact gauge field profile or calculate propagating solutions. It contrasts with earlier work by Layek (2005) that studied quark reflection from Z(3) walls by modeling effective quark mass dependence on the Polyakov loop magnitude, without exploring CP violation. The results enable predictions for baryon enhancement at high transverse momentum and additional J/\(\psi\) suppression in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, as well as implications for baryon density inhomogeneities in the early universe.
