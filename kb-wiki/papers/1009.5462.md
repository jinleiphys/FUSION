# Rapidity distribution dependence of the transition energy in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The transition from in-plane to out-of-plane elliptic flow for light charged particles (LCPs) in semi-central Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies occurs **only when the mid-rapidity region is included** in the rapidity bin; if the mid-rapidity region is excluded, no transition is observed. The transition energy increases linearly with the size of the rapidity bin.

## Method
The authors performed simulations of 1000 events for the reaction ⁷⁹Au¹⁹⁷ + ⁷⁹Au¹⁹⁷ at semi-central geometry using the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with a hard equation of state. The analysis was restricted to light charged particles (LCPs, defined as fragments with mass number 1 ≤ A ≤ 4). The excitation function of elliptic flow was computed for different rapidity bins, and the transition energy (the incident energy at which elliptic flow changes from in-plane to out-of-plane) was extracted and fitted with a straight line.

## Key numbers
- The transition energy increases with rapidity bin size from |Y^red| ≤ 0.1 up to |Y^red| ≤ 1.5.
- No transition is observed for rapidity bins with |Y^red| ≤ 1.1 or larger (i.e., when the mid-rapidity region is excluded).
- The transition energy as a function of rapidity bin size is parameterized by a straight line fit: Y = mX + C, where m is the slope and C is a constant (numerical values of m and C are not provided in the text).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the transverse-momentum dependence of elliptic flow reported by Luka (2005), Andr (2005), and Dani (2000). The authors build on the observation by Dani (2000) that cold spectator matter redirects particles emitted toward the reaction plane, while particles emitted perpendicular to the plane are unhindered. The present study systematically examines how the size of the rapidity bin, and thus the inclusion of spectator versus participant matter, affects the transition energy. The results enable future experimental and theoretical work to refine the relationship between the nuclear equation of state and elliptic flow by accounting for rapidity bin selection.
