# Compact static stars in minimal dilatonic gravity

> In the version \cite{Fiziev14} of this paper we presented for the first time the basic equations and relations for relativistic static spherically symmetric stars (SSSS) in the model of minimal dilatonic gravity (MDG). This model is {\em locally} equivalent to the f(R) theory of gravity and gives an alternative description of the effects of dark matter and dark energy using the Branse-Dicke dilaton $Φ$. To outline the basic properties of the MDG model of SSSS and to compare them with general relativistic results, in the present paper we use the relativistic equation of state (EOS) of neutron matter as an ideal Fermi neutron gas at zero temperature. We overcome the well-known difficulties of the physics of SSSS in the f(R) theories of gravity \cite{Felice10,Berti} applying novel highly nontrivial nonlinear boundary conditions, which depend on the global properties of the solution and on the EOS. We also introduce two pairs of new notions: cosmological-energy-pressure densities and dilaton-energy-pressure densities, as well as two new EOS for them: cosmological EOS (CEOS) and dilaton EOS (DEOS). Special attention is paid to the dilatonic sphere (in brief -- disphere) of SSSS, introduced in this paper for the first time. Using several realistic EOS for neutron star (NS): SLy, BSk19, BSk20 and BSk21, and current observational two-solar-masses-limit, we derive an estimate for scalar-field-mass $m_Φ\sim 10^{-13} eV/c^2 ÷4\times 10^{-11} eV/c^2$. Thus, the present version of the paper reflects some of the recent developments of the topic.

## Key claim
The paper claims that minimal dilatonic gravity (MDG) provides a physically consistent description of relativistic static spherically symmetric stars (SSSS) by applying novel nonlinear boundary conditions that depend on the global solution and the equation of state, overcoming difficulties encountered in f(R) theories. The hedge is that this is demonstrated using the simplest withholding potential and simplest matter EOS (ideal Fermi neutron gas at zero temperature), with the explicit caveat that the numerical calculations use a "maybe non-realistic large value" of the dimensionless dilaton Compton length for graphical clarity.

## Method
The author derives the field equations for SSSS in MDG using a spherically symmetric metric and introduces two new pairs of quantities: cosmological-energy-pressure densities and dilaton-energy-pressure densities, along with corresponding equations of state (CEOS and DEOS). The system is solved numerically with the ideal Fermi neutron gas EOS (Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff model) and with realistic EOS (SLy, BSk19, BSk20, BSk21), applying nonlocal boundary conditions that connect the star center to the cosmological horizon where the dilaton field reaches unity.

## Key numbers
- For the ideal Fermi neutron gas EOS in MDG: maximal star mass m*_max ≈ 0.5073 solar masses, maximal star radius r*_max ≈ 7.092 km (compared to GR: m*_max ≈ 0.7051 solar masses, r*_max ≈ 9.209 km).
- Disphere mass for the near-extremal MDG star: m_disphere ≈ 0.1638 solar masses, approximately 32 percent of the star mass; total object mass m_total ≈ 0.6710 solar masses.
- From realistic EOS and the two-solar-masses limit: scalar-field mass estimate m_Φ ∼ 4×10^{-13} eV/c^2 to 10^{-11} eV/c^2.
- For MEOS BSk19: m_Φ ∼ 10^{-11} eV/c^2 allowed; for SLy: m_Φ ∼ 4×10^{-13} eV/c^2 allowed; for BSk20: m_Φ ∈ (10^{-11}, 4×10^{-11}) eV/c^2 allowed; BSk21 excluded.
- Earlier estimate from Earth-surface experiments: m_Φ ≳ 10^{-3} eV; from inflationary cosmology: m_Φ ∼ 1.5×10^{-5} M_pl ≈ 3.65×10^{22} eV/c^2; Planck mission gives m_Φ ∼ 1.3×10^{-5} M_pl ≈ 3.17×10^{22} eV/c^2.

## Context
This work extends the MDG model proposed by O'Hanlon (1972) and developed by Fiziev (2000-2013), which is locally equivalent to f(R) theories of gravity but avoids difficulties with star stability and singularities reported by Berti (2008), Frolov (2008), and Felice (2010). The paper introduces the concept of a "disphere" (dilatonic sphere) surrounding compact stars, which may enable interpretation of the dilaton field as dark matter and provides a mechanism to reconcile soft equations of state (including those for quark and hybrid stars) with the observed two-solar-mass neutron stars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, as reported by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
