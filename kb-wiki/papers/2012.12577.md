# The Chiral Phase Transitions of Helical Matter

> We study the thermodynamics of helical matter, namely quark matter in which a net helicity, $n_H$, is in equilibrium. Interactions are modeled by the renormalized quark-meson model with two flavors of quarks. Helical density is described within the grand-canonical ensemble formalism via a chemical potential, $μ_H$. We study the transitions from the normal quark matter and hadron gas to the helical matter, drawing the phase diagram at zero temperature. We study the restoration of chiral symmetry at finite temperature and show that the net helical density softens the transition, moving the critical endpoint to lower temperature and higher baryon chemical potential. Finally, we discuss briefly the effect of a rigid rotation on the helical matter, in particular on the fluctuations of $n_H$, and show that these are enhanced by the rotation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a net helical density (quark helicity imbalance) softens the chiral phase transition in quark matter, moving the critical endpoint to lower temperature and higher baryon chemical potential, but this conclusion depends on the inclusion of the renormalized vacuum quark loop term in the thermodynamic potential; without it, the transition remains first-order for all values of the helical chemical potential.

## Method

The authors use the renormalized quark-meson (QM) model with two flavors of quarks at mean-field level, introducing a helical chemical potential μ_H coupled to the net helicity operator in addition to the standard baryon chemical potential μ. They compute the thermodynamic grand potential including a renormalized vacuum quark loop term and a thermal part, then solve for the chiral condensate as a function of μ, μ_H, and temperature T. The phase boundaries are identified from the peak of the chiral susceptibility χ_σ, and the quark number and helical density susceptibilities are computed from second derivatives of the potential.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass in vacuum: M_q = 335 MeV (from g = 3.6, F_π = 93 MeV)
* At T = 0 and μ_H = 0, first-order chiral transition occurs at μ_c ≈ 365 MeV
* At T = 0, the inversion point (smoothest crossover) occurs at μ = μ_H ≈ 257 MeV
* Pion mass: M_π = 138 MeV; sigma mass: M_σ = 700 MeV; explicit symmetry breaking parameter: h = 1.78 × 10^6 MeV^3
* For the model without the vacuum term, the first-order transition at μ_H = 0 occurs at μ_c ≈ 320 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of helical matter first introduced by Chernodub (2020) to finite temperature and to the quark-meson model, confirming the zero-temperature phase diagram of that reference. The results show that helicity fluctuations can be enhanced by rigid rotation, which is relevant for the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions where vorticity is present (Liang 2004, Huang 2011, Becattini 2007). The softening of the chiral transition by helical density suggests that local lumps of helical matter formed by event-by-event fluctuations could shift the location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, impacting experimental searches.
