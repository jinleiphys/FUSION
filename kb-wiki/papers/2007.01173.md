# Pion-nucleon scattering to order $p^4$ in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the $T$-matrices of elastic pion-nucleon ($πN$) scattering up to fourth order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The pertinent low-energy constants are determined by fitting to $πN$ phase shifts below 200 MeV pion laboratory momentum in the physical region. The scattering lengths and scattering volumes are extracted from the chiral amplitudes, and turn out to be in good agreement with those of other approaches and the available experimental values. We also discuss the subthreshold parameters and the related issues. On the basis of the various phase shifts, the threshold parameters and the subthreshold parameters, the convergence of the chiral expansion is analyzed in detail. The calculation provides the possibility to consider explicitly more complex processes involving strangeness.

## Key claim

The paper calculates pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes to fourth order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the convergence of the chiral expansion remains problematic: while threshold parameters converge well, the subthreshold parameters show poor convergence and even opposite signs between third- and fourth-order calculations, indicating that the chiral expansion is not yet fully under control despite the higher-order calculation.

## Method

The T-matrices for elastic pion-nucleon scattering were computed up to fourth order (p^4) in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including tree diagrams from the fourth-order Lagrangian and one-loop diagrams with vertices from lower-order Lagrangians. The 23 unknown low-energy constants were determined by fitting to S- and P-wave phase shifts from Roy-Steiner equations in the range of 10-200 MeV pion laboratory momentum. The scattering lengths, scattering volumes, and subthreshold parameters were then extracted from the chiral amplitudes and compared with other approaches.

## Key numbers

- Fitting range: pion laboratory momentum below 200 MeV for S- and P-wave phase shifts
- χ²/d.o.f. for the fourth-order fit: 0.24 (compared to 1.24 at third order and 21.83 at second order)
- S-wave scattering length a_{0+}^{3/2} = -0.122(4) fm; a_{0+}^{1/2} = 0.240(3) fm
- P-wave scattering volume a_{1+}^{3/2} = 0.603(13) fm³; a_{1+}^{1/2} = -0.102(6) fm³
- P-wave scattering volume a_{1-}^{3/2} = -0.110(9) fm³; a_{1-}^{1/2} = -0.221(12) fm³
- Subthreshold parameter d_{00}^{+} = -0.99(15) m_π^{-1} at fourth order (RS value: -1.36(3) m_π^{-1})
- Subthreshold parameter d_{00}^{-} = 0.63(17) m_π^{-2} at fourth order (RS value: 1.41(1) m_π^{-2})
- Pion-nucleon sigma term used as input: σ_{πN} = 59.1 ± 3.5 MeV
- Input masses: m_π = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, m_η = 547.86 MeV, f_π = 92.07 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of meson-baryon scattering by Huang (2015, 2017, 2020) from third order to fourth order. It builds on the SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of Fettes (1998, 2000) and the Roy-Steiner equation analysis of Hoferichter (2015, 2016). The calculation enables future studies of more complex processes involving strangeness, such as kaon-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering, and provides a framework for including the Δ(1232) resonance to improve convergence.
