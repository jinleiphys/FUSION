# Short-range correlations in nuclei with similarity renormalization group transformations

> $\mathbf{Background:}$ Realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions induce short-range correlations in nuclei. To solve the many-body problem unitary transformations like the similarity renormalization group (SRG) are often used to soften the interactions. $\mathbf{Purpose:}$ Two-body densities can be used to illustrate how the SRG eliminates short-range correlations in the wave function. The short-range information can however be recovered by transforming the density operators. $\mathbf{Method:}$ The many-body problem is solved for $^4$He in the no core shell model (NCSM) with SRG transformed AV8' and chiral N3LO interactions. The NCSM wave functions are used to calculate two-body densities with bare and SRG transformed density operators in two-body approximation. $\mathbf{Results:}$ The two-body momentum distributions for AV8' and N3LO have similar high-momentum components up to relative momenta of about $2.5\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, dominated by tensor correlations, but differ in their behavior at higher relative momenta. The contributions of many-body correlations are small for pairs with vanishing pair momentum but not negligible for the momentum distributions integrated over all pair momenta. Many-body correlations are induced by the strong tensor force and lead to a reshuffling of pairs between different spin-isospin channels. $\mathbf{Conclusions:}$ When using the SRG it is essential to use transformed operators for observables sensitive to short-range physics. Back-to-back pairs with vanishing pair momentum are the best tool to study short-range correlations.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation in two-body approximation largely preserves short-range two-body correlations for pairs with vanishing total momentum, but many-body correlations induced by the tensor force cause significant reshuffling of pairs between spin-isospin channels that is not captured in the two-body approximation.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body problem for ⁴He using the no core shell model (NCSM) with SRG-transformed AV8' and chiral N3LO interactions at various flow parameters α. They calculated two-body densities in coordinate and momentum space using both bare density operators (applied to SRG-evolved wave functions) and SRG-transformed density operators in two-body approximation, comparing the flow-parameter dependence to assess the importance of omitted many-body terms.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ⁴He varies by about 10% across flow parameters, indicating small but non-negligible three- and four-body contributions
- For AV8' interaction, number of pairs in the (S,T) = (1,1) channel decreases from 0.428 (bare) to 0.075 (α = 0.20 fm⁴), while (0,1) pairs increase from 2.572 to 2.925
- For N3LO interaction, (1,1) pairs decrease from 0.290 (bare) to 0.079 (α = 0.20 fm⁴), while (0,1) pairs increase from 2.710 to 2.921
- Two-body momentum distributions for AV8' and N3LO have similar high-momentum components up to relative momenta of about 2.5 fm⁻¹
- SRG flow equations solved on momentum space grid up to kmax = 15 fm⁻¹ for AV8' interaction
- NCSM calculations converged with Nmax = 16 and oscillator parameter ħΩ = 36 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of short-range correlations using the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) (Neff et al., 2011) and SRG transformations (Bogner et al., 2007, 2010) by providing detailed two-body density calculations for ⁴He with both AV8' and chiral N3LO interactions. The finding that back-to-back pairs with vanishing pair momentum are insensitive to many-body correlations supports experimental strategies used in triple-coincidence measurements (Subedi et al., 2008; Korover et al., 2014) and connects to the nuclear contact formalism (Weiss et al., 2015). The results enable systematic studies of how SRG-induced many-body terms affect short-range observables and guide the development of consistent operator evolution for experimental observables.
