# Back-to-back correlations of high p_T hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the suppression factor and the azimuthal correlation function for high $p_T$ hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV by using a dynamical model in which hydrodynamics is combined with explicitly traveling jets. We study the effects of parton energy loss in a hot medium, intrinsic $k_T$ of partons in a nucleus, and $p_{\perp}$ broadening of jets on the back-to-back correlations of high $p_T$ hadrons. Parton energy loss is found to be a dominant effect on the reduction of the away-side peaks in the correlation function.

## Key claim

Parton energy loss is the dominant effect reducing the away-side peaks in the azimuthal correlation function, but it is insufficient to completely vanish them; a combination of parton energy loss, intrinsic transverse momentum of partons, and transverse momentum broadening of jets is needed to simultaneously reproduce both the suppression factor \(R_{AA} \sim 0.25\) and the disappearance of back-to-back correlations.

## Method

The authors use a hydro+jet model that combines a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation of thermalized matter with explicit propagation of high-\(p_T\) partons from perturbative QCD hard scatterings (simulated via PYTHIA 6.2). Parton energy loss in the hot medium is implemented using the first-order opacity expansion formula from the reaction operator approach, with the thermalized parton density obtained dynamically from the hydrodynamic evolution. The azimuthal correlation function \(C_2(\Delta\phi)\) is computed for charged hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, and the effects of intrinsic \(k_T\) and \(p_\perp\) broadening are studied by varying the corresponding parameters.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the maximum initial energy density at initial time \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\) is 39.0 GeV/fm\(^3\) (for impact parameter \(b=2.0\) fm).
- The suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) for neutral pions in Au+Au 10% central collisions is quantitatively reproduced with energy loss parameter \(C=0.27\) in the GLV formula.
- With \(\langle k_T^2\rangle = 1\) GeV\(^2/c^2\) and \(C=0.27\), the away-side peaks in \(C_2(\Delta\phi)\) are reduced but not eliminated; only with \(C=1.0\) (which underestimates \(R_{AA}\)) do the away-side peaks almost disappear.
- For \(\langle k_T^2\rangle = 4\) GeV\(^2/c^2\) (with \(C=0.5\) to fit \(R_{AA}\)), the away-side peak height becomes \(\sim 0.02\), but the near-side peak is also reduced and deviates from data.
- For model 1 of \(p_\perp\) broadening, the event average broadening is \(\langle\langle p^2_{\perp}\rangle\rangle \sim 2.5\) GeV\(^2/c^2\); for model 2, it is \(\langle\langle p^2_{\perp}\rangle\rangle \sim 0.78\) GeV\(^2/c^2\).
- The \(p_\perp\) broadening causes 5-20% reduction of the original height of away-side peaks.

## Context

This work extends the hydro+jet model previously developed by Hirano and Nara to study the simultaneous description of the suppression factor and azimuthal correlations at RHIC. It directly addresses the STAR Collaboration measurement (2002) showing disappearance of away-side peaks in central Au+Au collisions, and the PHENIX data on \(R_{AA}\) for neutral pions. The study contrasts with earlier incoherent energy loss models (which gave \(R_{AA}\) increasing with \(p_T\)) and with pQCD parton model studies by Wang (2003). The results indicate that a combination of multiple mechanisms is required to explain both observables, motivating further systematic studies using \(pp\) and \(d\)+Au data to constrain intrinsic \(k_T\) and \(p_\perp\) broadening.
