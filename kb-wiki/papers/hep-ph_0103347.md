# Reply to "Comment about pion electroproduction and the axial form factors"

## Key claim

The criticisms by Guichon (2000) and by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (2001) do not address the main point of Haberzettl's original argument (2000) that the axial form factor \(G_A\) cannot be measured in pion electroproduction at threshold; the splitting of the axial current into weak and hadronic parts is algebraically valid, and the complete cancellation of the \(G_A\) dependence occurs before the \(q=0\) limit is taken, regardless of whether the splitting introduces artificial \(1/t\) poles.

## Method

The author examines the algebraic splitting of the axial current \(\hat{j}^\mu_A\) into a weak part \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,W}\) (containing \(G_A\)) and a hadronic part \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,H}\) (containing the \(\pi NN\) form factor \(G_t\)), as originally introduced in Haberzettl (2000). He shows that the \(1/t\) singularities in both terms are recast into a well-defined \(0/0\) situation via the Goldberger–Treiman relation, and that the subsequent algebraic steps leading to Eq. (23) of the original paper produce complete cancellation of the \(G_A\) dependence before the threshold limit is taken. He then evaluates the specific counterarguments: Guichon (2000) objects to the splitting as "strange" but does not identify a specific algebraic error, while Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (2001) arbitrarily replace the factor \(\mu^2/t\) by unity, which is unjustified.

## Key numbers

- The splitting of the axial current into \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,W}\) and \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,H}\) contains terms proportional to \(1/t\) (where \(t = (p'-p)^2\)), with the hadronic part \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,H}\) proportional to \(f_\pi (p'-p)^\mu (\mu^2/t) [1/(t-\mu^2)] \gamma_5 G_t \tau\).
- In the chiral limit of vanishing pion mass \(\mu \to 0\), the hadronic part \(\hat{j}^\mu_{A,H}\) vanishes, leaving the \(1/t\) pole of the weak part physically relevant.
- The Goldberger–Treiman relation ensures the \(0/0\) situation in the last term of Eq. (3), where the numerator is \(f_\pi G_t - m G_A\).

## Context

This reply defends the position of Haberzettl (2000) against two critiques: Guichon (2000) and Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (2001), both of which challenged the claim that the axial form factor cannot be extracted from threshold pion electroproduction. The work extends the ongoing debate about the proper treatment of axial current splitting and PCAC constraints in electroproduction reactions. The author maintains that the original conclusions stand, and that the critics have not identified a specific algebraic error in the derivation.
