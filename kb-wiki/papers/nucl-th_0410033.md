# Recent developments in light-front dynamics

> Recent results on relativistic few body systems, obtained in the framework of light-front dynamics, are briefly reviewed. The following subjects are discussed: two scalar bosons with ladder and cross ladder kernel; two fermions with OBE kernel; relativistic scattering (elastic and inelastic); three bosons and fermions with zero-range interaction; many-body contributions.

## Key claim

This review argues that light-front dynamics (LFD) provides an efficient framework for relativistic few-body systems, but the abstract's claim of "good progress" glosses over the fact that the approach still requires careful treatment of higher Fock sectors and that the relativistic and nonrelativistic results differ strongly even at very small binding energies, particularly for heavy exchanged meson masses.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations performed within the explicitly covariant version of light-front dynamics, where the state vector is defined on a light-front plane with a general four-vector omega (with omega^2=0). The method involves solving LFD equations for two-body and three-body systems using various kernels (ladder, cross-ladder, zero-range interaction, one-boson-exchange), and comparing results with nonrelativistic calculations and Bethe-Salpeter equation solutions.

## Key numbers

- For two scalar bosons with heavy exchanged mass mu comparable to constituent mass m, relativistic and nonrelativistic results strongly differ even for very small binding energy (figure 1).
- For two fermions with scalar exchange (Yukawa model), a stable bound state with J=0+ exists without form factor if the coupling constant alpha = g^2/(4pi) does not exceed a critical value alpha_c = 3.72.
- For three bosons with zero-range interaction, the critical two-body mass below which the three-body system does not exist is M_c = 1.43 m (where m is the constituent mass).
- In the Wick-Cutkosky model with massless scalar exchange, for maximal coupling constant alpha = 2pi (corresponding to zero bound state mass M=0), the two-body sector contributes 64% to the norm and the three-body sector contributes 26%, totaling 90%.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on light-front dynamics by Karmanov and collaborators (1995, 1999, 2003, 2004) and compares with Bethe-Salpeter equation approaches (2001). The results enable further calculations of hadron form factors and relativistic deuteron electromagnetic properties, with the deuteron wave function calculations from Karmanov (1995) and form factor predictions from Karmanov (1999) later confirmed by experimental t20 data from Jefferson Lab. The many-body analysis in the Wick-Cutkosky model (2004) provides justification for the few-body truncation approximation, though the author notes that cross-ladder contributions may modify this result.
