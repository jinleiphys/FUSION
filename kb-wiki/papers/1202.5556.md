# The Z^0-tagged jet event asymmetry in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Tagged jet measurements provide a promising experimental channel to quantify the similarities and differences in the mechanisms of jet production in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions. We present the first calculation of the transverse momentum asymmetry of Z^0/gamma^*-tagged jet events in sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV reactions at the LHC. Our results combine the O(G_Fα_s^2) perturbative cross sections with the radiative and collisional processes that modify parton showers in the presence of dense QCD matter. We find that a strong asymmetry is generated in central lead-lead reactions that has little sensitivity to the fluctuations of the underlying soft hadronic background. We present theoretical model predictions for its shape and magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the transverse momentum asymmetry of Z^0/gamma*-tagged jet events in sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV lead-lead collisions at the LHC, finding that a strong asymmetry is generated in central reactions with little sensitivity to fluctuations of the underlying soft hadronic background. The hedge is that these are theoretical model predictions for the shape and magnitude, not experimental measurements, and the results are qualitatively representative of other electroweak boson-tagged jet final states.

## Method

The authors combine O(G_F alpha_s^2) perturbative cross sections from the MCFM Monte Carlo code with medium-induced parton splitting described by the reaction operator formalism and collisional energy losses evaluated in the hard thermal loop approximation. They implement jet reconstruction using a midpoint cone algorithm with separation parameter R, and relate the generation of medium-induced parton showers to experimental observables through a convolution that accounts for the fraction of energy redistributed inside the jet cone. The asymmetry distribution is obtained by changing variables from (p_T Z, p_T Jet) to (A_J, p_T Jet) and integrating over p_T Jet.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV for both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Jet reconstruction parameter R=0.4 and R=0.2 used for asymmetry calculations
- Z^0 mass: M_z=91.2 GeV used for invariant mass constraint around the Z^0 peak
- p_T Z range: (80,100) GeV and p_T Jet > 20 GeV for the asymmetry distribution examples
- Standard deviation of background fluctuations: Delta p_T approximately 11 GeV for jet R=0.4 in central Pb+Pb collisions (from ALICE experiment, Abelev 2012)
- Mean asymmetry <A_J> for p+p with R=0.2: -0.025; with R=0.4: -0.040
- Mean asymmetry for Pb+Pb with radiative loss, R=0.2, g_med=2.0: 0.229 (no fluctuations) and 0.228 (with fluctuations)
- Mean asymmetry for Pb+Pb with radiative+collisional loss, R=0.4, g_med=2.0: 0.211 (no fluctuations) and 0.214 (with fluctuations)
- Collisional energy loss simulation: a 75 GeV gluon propagating through medium can transfer as much as 20 GeV of its energy to the medium
- Effect of background fluctuations on asymmetry: less than 20% in the region where A_J is significant for large radii, and completely negligible for R=0.2

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions beyond di-jet asymmetry measurements reported by ATLAS and CMS (Aad 2010) to the electroweak boson-tagged jet channel. The tagged jet approach was proposed by Srivastava (2002) as a way to constrain the initial energy of the associated jet, and the authors build on their own previous NLO calculations (Neufeld 2010) and the reaction operator formalism (Vitev 2007). The results enable experimental programs at the LHC to use Z^0-tagged jets as a complementary channel to di-jet measurements for characterizing parton shower modification in the quark-gluon plasma, with the advantage of minimal sensitivity to background fluctuations and cold nuclear matter effects.
