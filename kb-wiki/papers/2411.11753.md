# An introduction to relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> Spin polarization and spin transport are common phenomena in many quantum systems. Relativistic spin hydrodynamics provides an effective low-energy framework to describe these processes in quantum many-body systems. The fundamental symmetry underlying relativistic spin hydrodynamics is angular momentum conservation, which naturally leads to inter-conversion between spin and orbital angular momenta. This inter-conversion is a key feature of relativistic spin hydrodynamics, closely related to entropy production and introducing ambiguity in the construction of constitutive relations. In this article, we present a pedagogical introduction to relativistic spin hydrodynamics. We demonstrate how to derive the constitutive relations by applying local thermodynamic laws and explore several distinctive aspects of spin hydrodynamics. These include the pseudo-gauge ambiguity, the behavior of the system in the presence of strong vorticity, and the challenges of modeling the freeze-out of spin in heavy-ion collisions. We also outline some future prospects for spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

Relativistic spin hydrodynamics is not a strict hydrodynamic theory for gapless modes but rather a quasi-hydrodynamic framework, because spin density is generally not conserved due to spin-orbit inter-conversion and is only a quasi-conserved mode when spin relaxation is slow (e.g., for massive fermions where spin-orbit coupling is suppressed by the fermion mass).

## Method

The author constructs relativistic spin hydrodynamics by starting from energy-momentum conservation and angular momentum conservation, introducing spin density as a quasi-hydrodynamic variable with a power counting scheme where spin density is treated as first order in gradients. Constitutive relations for the symmetric and antisymmetric parts of the energy-momentum tensor are derived by imposing the second law of local thermodynamics via a covariant entropy current, leading to expressions involving bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, boost heat conductivity, and rotational viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Hyperon spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions is only about a few percent, motivating the power counting scheme where spin density S^{rs} is treated as O(∂) (first order in gradients).
- The QGP temperature reaches 300-500 MeV at RHIC and LHC, indicating extremely large energy density.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of QGP is the lowest among all known fluids, required to explain observed harmonic flow parameters.

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the standard relativistic hydrodynamics framework (based on energy-momentum and U(1) charge conservation) to include angular momentum conservation, building on the discovery of global spin polarization of Λ hyperons by STAR (2017). The work contrasts with conventional hydrodynamics where the energy-momentum tensor is symmetric; here the antisymmetric part drives spin generation via spin-orbit coupling. The approach enables modeling of spin polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions and connects to related work using hydrostatic partition functions (Gallegos, 2021), non-equilibrium statistical operators (Hu, 2021), and kinetic theories (Florkowski, 2017; Weickgenannt, 2020).
