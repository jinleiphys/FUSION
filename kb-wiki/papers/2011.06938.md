# NNLO HTLpt predictions for the curvature of the QCD phase transition line

> We present predictions for the second- and fourth-order curvature coefficients of the QCD phase transition line using the NNLO HTLpt-resummed thermodynamic potential. We present three cases corresponding to (i) $μ_s = μ_l = μ_B/3$, (ii) $μ_s=0$, $μ_l = μ_B/3,$ and (iii) $S = 0$, $Q/B = 0.4$, $μ_l = μ_B/3$. In all three cases, we find excellent agreement with continuum extrapolated lattice QCD results for $κ_2$, given current statistical uncertainties. We also make HTLpt predictions for $κ_4$ in all three cases, finding again excellent agreement with lattice extractions of this coefficient where available.

## Key claim

NNLO hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) predictions for the second-order curvature coefficient κ₂ agree with continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results within current statistical uncertainties for all three chemical-potential scenarios considered; the fourth-order coefficient κ₄ is predicted to be negative but small in all cases, whereas lattice results are consistent with zero.

## Method

The authors used the analytic NNLO (three-loop) HTLpt-resummed thermodynamic potential from Haque (2014) to compute the pressure of the quark-gluon plasma as a function of temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B. They extracted the phase transition temperature from the point where the resummed pressure vanishes (T^μ_{P=0}), and also from lines of constant energy density and entropy density, fitting the results to the expansion T_c^μ / T_c^0 = 1 − κ₂ (μ_B / T_c^μ)² − κ₄ (μ_B / T_c^μ)⁴. The analysis was performed for three cases: (i) μ_s = μ_l = μ_B/3, (ii) μ_s = 0, μ_l = μ_B/3, and (iii) S = 0, Q/B = 0.4, μ_l = μ_B/3, using renormalization scale variation by a factor of two around central values to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- For case (i) (μ_s = μ_l = μ_B/3): HTLpt κ₂ = 0.0204^{+0.0012}_{-0.0006}; lattice κ₂ = 0.020(4) from Cea (2015).
- For case (ii) (μ_s = 0, μ_l = μ_B/3): HTLpt κ₂ = 0.0136^{+0.0008}_{-0.0004}; lattice κ₂ = 0.0135(20) from Bonati (2015) and 0.0145(25) from Bonati (2018).
- For case (iii) (S = 0, Q/B = 0.4, μ_l = μ_B/3): HTLpt κ₂ = 0.0149^{+0.0005}_{-0.0002}; lattice κ₂ = 0.0153(18) from Borsanyi (2020) and 0.012(4) from Bazavov (2018).
- For case (i): HTLpt κ₄ = −0.000594^{+0.000015}_{-0.000139}; lattice κ₄ = −0.0008(4) from Bonati (2014).
- For case (ii): HTLpt κ₄ = −0.000255^{+0.000004}_{-0.000056}; no lattice value available.
- For case (iii): HTLpt κ₄ = −0.000312; lattice κ₄ = 0.00032(67) from Borsanyi (2020) and 0.000(4) from Bazavov (2018).
- For μ_i = 0 and central renormalization scales, the pressure vanishes at T^μ_{P=0} = 148.4 MeV.
- For case (i) with c=1: κ₆ ≈ −2.65 × 10⁻⁵; for case (ii) with c=1: κ₆ ≈ −5.2 × 10⁻⁵.

## Context

This work extends the successful NNLO HTLpt calculations of the QCD pressure and susceptibilities by Haque (2014) to the curvature of the QCD phase transition line, providing a resummed perturbative QCD alternative to lattice methods that suffer from the sign problem at finite baryon density. The results agree with lattice extractions using both the imaginary chemical potential method (Cea 2015, Bonati 2015, Borsanyi 2020) and Taylor expansion methods (Bonati 2018, Bazavov 2018), and provide predictions for κ₄ and κ₆ that can be tested by future lattice calculations with improved precision.
