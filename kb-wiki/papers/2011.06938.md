# NNLO HTLpt predictions for the curvature of the QCD phase transition line

> We present predictions for the second- and fourth-order curvature coefficients of the QCD phase transition line using the NNLO HTLpt-resummed thermodynamic potential. We present three cases corresponding to (i) $őľ_s = őľ_l = őľ_B/3$, (ii) $őľ_s=0$, $őľ_l = őľ_B/3,$ and (iii) $S = 0$, $Q/B = 0.4$, $őľ_l = őľ_B/3$. In all three cases, we find excellent agreement with continuum extrapolated lattice QCD results for $őļ_2$, given current statistical uncertainties. We also make HTLpt predictions for $őļ_4$ in all three cases, finding again excellent agreement with lattice extractions of this coefficient where available.

## Context

This work extends the successful NNLO HTLpt calculations of the QCD pressure and susceptibilities by Haque (2014) to the curvature of the QCD phase transition line, providing a resummed perturbative QCD alternative to lattice methods that suffer from the sign problem at finite baryon density. The results agree with lattice extractions using both the imaginary chemical potential method (Cea 2015, Bonati 2015, Borsanyi 2020) and Taylor expansion methods (Bonati 2018, Bazavov 2018), and provide predictions for őļ‚āĄ and őļ‚āÜ that can be tested by future lattice calculations with improved precision.
