# Charm Quark Energy Loss In Infinite QCD Matter Using A Parton Cascade Model

> We utilize the Parton Cascade Model to study the evolution of charm quarks propagating through a thermal brick of QCD matter. We determine the energy loss and the transport coefficient '$\hat{q}$' for charm quarks. The calculations are done at a constant temperature of 350 MeV and the results are compared to analytical calculations of heavy quark energy loss in order to validate the applicability of using a Parton Cascade Model for the study of heavy quarks dynamics in hot and dense QCD matter.

# Charm Quark Energy Loss In Infinite QCD Matter Using A Parton Cascade Model

## Key claim
The Parton Cascade Model (PCM) reproduces analytical calculations of charm quark collisional energy loss in a thermal QCD medium at fixed temperature, but shows discrepancies for radiative energy loss beyond 5-6 fm of path length due to the PCM's explicit inclusion of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, which the analytical comparison (in the Bethe-Heitler limit) omits. The validation is performed under controlled conditions (fixed coupling, constant temperature) as a benchmark before applying the model to dynamic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The PCM (VNI/BMS) solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation via Monte Carlo algorithms for a charm quark inserted into a thermal brick of QCD matter with periodic boundary conditions at fixed temperature T = 350 MeV. Elastic 2→2 scattering processes (cg → cg, cq → cq) are included with matrix elements regularized by a Debye screening mass μ_D, and radiative corrections are implemented via time-like parton branching using Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions with a Monte Carlo algorithm for the LPM effect. The charm quark energy loss and transport coefficient q̂ are extracted from the evolution over path lengths up to 10 fm.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of the thermal brick: T = 350 MeV (roughly the average QGP temperature at RHIC energies).
- Charm quark mass: M_c = 1.35 GeV.
- Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.3 (fixed for comparison with analytical calculations).
- Number of flavors: N_f = 4; number of colors: N_c = 3.
- For a 16 GeV charm quark at T = 350 MeV, the transport coefficient q̂ = 1.2 ± 0.2 GeV²/fm.
- For a 50 GeV charm quark at T = 350 MeV, the transport coefficient q̂ = 1.1 ± 0.3 GeV²/fm.
- Collisional energy loss dominates over radiative loss for initial charm energies up to 7-12 GeV; beyond this range radiative loss dominates.
- For a 16 GeV charm after 10 fm of propagation including radiative corrections, the bulk of charm quarks shift to energies below 2.0 GeV.
- The screening mass is defined as μ_D = √((2N_c + N_f)/6) g T, with g = √(4πα_s).

## Context
This work extends the Parton Cascade Model, previously applied to light partons, to heavy quarks for the first time (Younus et al., 2013). The PCM results for collisional energy loss are compared to analytical expressions by Peshier and Peigne, and radiative loss is compared to calculations by Abir et al. (in the Bethe-Heitler limit with dead-cone formalism). The study validates the PCM for heavy-quark dynamics in a controlled thermal medium, enabling future application to non-equilibrium heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, including temperature-dependent studies and full collision simulations. The transport coefficient q̂ results (1.0-1.5 GeV²/fm range) are compared to prior estimates for light quarks (0.5-20 GeV²/fm) and heavy quarks (0.3-0.7 GeV²/fm) from other groups.
