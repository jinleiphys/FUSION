# What Do We Know about Low--Energy $\bar{K}N$ Interactions? Need and Possibilities of New Experiments at DA$Φ$NE

> We present results from a dispersion--relation investigation on a coupled--channel analysis of $\bar{K}N$--initiated processes in the low-- and intermediate--energy region. The analysis demonstrates both the effectiveness of these relations to constrain the parameters of the unmeasurable $πY$ channels and the need for better data in the low--energy region, such as could be provided at a $ϕ$--factory. (pres. by G.V. at the "International Workshop on the Spectoscopy of Hypernuclei", Sendai, Japan, January 8-10, 1998, to be publ. in Genshikaku Kenkyuu).

## Key claim

Dispersion-relation consistency tests reveal that the only existing coupled-channel analysis of low-energy $\bar{K}N$ interactions that includes partial waves up to $J=3/2$ (Kim's analysis from about thirty years ago) badly violates analyticity constraints, particularly in the $P_{11}$ wave of the $\pi Y$ channels, indicating the need for new experiments at a $\phi$-factory like DA$\Phi$NE to provide better data.

## Method

The authors evaluated forward dispersion relations for the $B(\omega)/\omega$ amplitude in elastic $\pi\Lambda$ and $\pi\Sigma$ scattering, and for the $C(\omega)/\omega$ amplitude in the $\pi\Lambda\to\pi\Sigma$ reaction, using Kim's multichannel K-matrix parametrization of $\bar{K}N$ interactions for the low-energy region. They then varied the energy at which the dispersion relations were evaluated to test the stability of the extracted $PBB$ coupling constants, and separately analyzed the contributions from each partial wave to identify the sources of inconsistency.

## Key numbers

- The $P_{11}$ wave contribution accounts for about 28.5% of the value of $G^2_{\pi\Lambda\Sigma}/4\pi$ at the $\bar{K}N$ threshold
- The charge-exchange threshold in $K^-p$ scattering is at a laboratory momentum of 90 MeV/c
- The analysis covers data between threshold and a laboratory momentum of about 500 MeV/c
- The $K\Lambda N$ coupling constant $G_{K\Lambda N}^2/4\pi$ oscillates around a value of 19.5 in the central part of the energy range analyzed
- The dispersion relation for $K\Sigma N$ coupling was evaluated at a fixed $KN$ c.m. energy of 1,468 MeV (corresponding to $\omega_2$ = 548 MeV)
- The parametrization of ref. [4a] requires 36 matrix elements for analysis up to $J=3/2$, with 44 parameters when energy dependence is included

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersion-relation studies of $\pi Y$ couplings by Chang and Meiere (who analyzed only elastic $\pi\Lambda$ and $\pi\Sigma$ scattering at the $\bar{K}N$ threshold) by including for the first time the $\pi\Lambda\to\pi\Sigma$ reaction channel and by testing the stability of results across varying evaluation energies. The analysis demonstrates that Kim's thirty-year-old coupled-channel parametrization, the only one including up to D waves down to the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, is inconsistent with fixed-$t$ analyticity, particularly in the $P_{11}$ wave. The results motivate new experiments at DA$\Phi$NE using detectors like KLOE, DEAR, and FINUDA, which could measure differential cross sections and hyperon polarizations at laboratory momenta from about 90 to 110 MeV/c with statistics orders of magnitude better than previous experiments.
