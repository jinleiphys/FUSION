# Level density within a micro-macroscopic approach

> Statistical level density $ρ(E,A)$ is derived for nucleonic system with a given energy $E$, particle number $A$ and other integrals of motion in the micro-macroscopic approximation beyond the standard saddle-point method of the Fermi gas model. This level density reaches the two limits; the well-known Fermi gas grand-canonical ensemble limit for a large entropy $S$ related to large excitation energies, and the finite micro-canonical limit for a small combinatorical entropy $S$ at low excitation energies. The inverse level density parameter $K$ as function of the particle number $A$ in the semiclassical periodic orbit theory, taking into account the extended Thomas-Fermi and Strutinsky shell corrections, is calculated and compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper derives a statistical level density for nucleonic systems using a micro-macroscopic approximation that goes beyond the standard saddle-point method of the Fermi gas model, claiming that this approach unifies the grand-canonical limit at high excitation energies with the finite micro-canonical limit at low excitation energies without requiring free fitting parameters for matching the two regimes.

## Method

The authors start from the microcanonical expression for the level density and integrate over the Lagrange multiplier associated with particle number using the saddle-point method, then perform a more accurate inverse Laplace transformation over the energy-related Lagrange multiplier beyond the standard saddle-point method. The level density parameter is decomposed into a smooth extended Thomas-Fermi component and an oscillating shell correction component calculated using the semiclassical periodic orbit theory for an infinitely deep spherical square-well potential. Two limiting cases are considered: case (i) where the Jacobian is dominated by the smooth level density contribution, and case (ii) where shell-correction contributions dominate the Jacobian.

## Key numbers

* The inverse level density parameter K = A/a has a mean value of about 8 MeV across the particle number range, as predicted by the extended Thomas-Fermi approach with effective mass effects.
* For typical parameters lambda = 40 MeV, A ~ 200, and relative energy shell correction E_sh ~ 0.2-2.0, the Jacobian ratio xi is estimated to be xi ~ 1-10 for temperature T ~ 1 MeV and xi ~ 0.01-0.1 for T ~ 0.1 MeV.
* The Gaussian averaging width for the oscillating level density is gamma = 0.3, corresponding to a dimensional Gaussian width Gamma ~ 3 MeV for lambda = 40 MeV, r_0 = 1.14 fm, and A = 100-200.
* The particle number period of the major shell structure A_sh is approximately 20-30 for A = 100-200.
* The semiclassical curves for K(A) required a shift of about Delta A = 20 along the particle number axis to match experimental magic numbers.

## Context

This work extends the micro-macroscopic approximation originally suggested by Kolomietz and Magner (1979) for the statistical level density, which was based on the Strutinsky shell correction method within the Landau-Migdal quasiparticle theory. The results are compared with experimental data from von Egidy and Bucurescu (2009) for the inverse level density parameter, and with self-consistent extended Thomas-Fermi calculations using Skyrme forces SKM* and KDE0v1 from references KS18 and KS20. The approach enables analytical connection between the Fermi gas model at high excitation energies and the constant temperature model at low excitation energies without free fitting parameters, and provides a framework for understanding shell effects in nuclear level densities through periodic orbit theory.
