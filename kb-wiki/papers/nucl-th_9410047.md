# Time Reversal Symmetry Breaking Effects in Resonant Nuclear Reactions

> We incorporate time reversal symmetry breaking (TRSB) effects into the theory of compound nuclear reactions. We show that the only meaningful test of TRSB in the overlapping resonances regime is through the study of cross-section correlations. The effect is channel-dependent. In the isolated resonance regime, we employ $K$-matrix theory to show the impact of TRSB using the fact that when only one eigen-channel participates in populating and depopulating the compound resonance.

# Time Reversal Symmetry Breaking Effects in Resonant Nuclear Reactions

## Key claim

Time reversal symmetry breaking (TRSB) in compound nuclear reactions can be meaningfully tested only through cross-section correlation functions in the overlapping resonances regime, not through detailed balance comparisons; the effect is channel-dependent, contrary to previous claims that it is channel-independent when TRSB is treated as purely internal mixing.

## Method

The authors extend the Kawai-Kerman-McVoy (KKM) formalism for compound nuclear reactions to include TRSB by introducing a decomposition of the resonance coupling amplitudes into a time-reversal-even part and a TRSB part, with the S-matrix no longer symmetric. For isolated resonances, they employ K-matrix theory to derive expressions for the TRSB asymmetry, showing that only one eigenchannel participates in populating and depopulating each compound resonance.

## Key numbers

- The cross-section correlation function \(C_{ab}\) is given approximately by \(-4 (t_{aa}/T^\circ_{aa} + t_{bb}/T^\circ_{bb})\), where \(T^\circ_{aa}\) is the optical transmission coefficient without TRSB and \(t_{aa}\) is the difference due to TRSB.
- For isolated resonances, the asymmetry \(P_q = \Delta\sigma_q / (2\sigma_q)\) is given by \(\gamma^W_{qc_1} / \overcirc{\gamma}_{qc_0}\), where \(\gamma^W\) is the imaginary (TRSB) part and \(\overcirc{\gamma}\) is the real (T-even) part of the coupling amplitude.
- The radiative decay width \(\Gamma^\gamma_q\) is the dominant piece of the total width \(\Gamma_q\) for isolated resonances.
- The nucleus \(^{139}\)La is identified as a good candidate for studying TRSB, in the mass region \(A \simeq 180\) and 140 where P-wave strength function minima coincide with S-wave strength function maxima.

## Context

This work extends the KKM formalism (Kawai, Kerman, McVoy, 1973) for compound nuclear reactions to include time reversal symmetry breaking, and contradicts earlier results by Ericson (1966), Blanke et al. (1983), and Boosé, Harney, and Weidenmüller (1986) who claimed channel-independence of TRSB effects by treating the symmetry breaking as purely internal. The analysis is motivated by the successful observation of parity violation in neutron resonances (Frankle et al., 1991) and suggests that similar amplification mechanisms in low-energy neutron reactions may make TRSB visible. The results enable experimental searches for TRSB using cross-section correlations in the overlapping resonance regime and asymmetry measurements in isolated resonances, particularly in nuclei where P-wave and S-wave strength functions exhibit favorable configurations.
