# Deformed natural orbitals for ab initio calculations

> The rapid development of ab initio nuclear structure methods towards doubly open-shell nuclei, heavy nuclei and greater accuracy occurs at the price of evermore increased computational costs, especially RAM and CPU time. While most of the numerical simulations are carried out by expanding relevant operators and wave functions on the spherical harmonic oscillator basis, alternative one-body bases offering advantages in terms of computational efficiency have recently been investigated. In particular, the so-called natural basis used in combination with symmetry-conserving methods applicable to doubly closed-shell nuclei has proven beneficial in this respect. The present work examines the performance of the natural basis in the context of symmetry-breaking many-body calculations enabling the description of superfluid and deformed open-shell nuclei at polynomial cost with system's size. First, it is demonstrated that the advantage observed for closed-shell nuclei carries over to open-shell ones. A detailed investigation of natural-orbital wave functions provides useful insight to support this finding and to explain the superiority of the natural basis over alternative ones. Second, it is shown that the use of natural orbitals combined with importance-truncation techniques leads to an even greater gain in terms of computational costs. The presents results pave the way for the systematic use of natural-orbital bases in future implementations of non-perturbative many-body methods.

## Key claim

The natural (NAT) orbital basis, previously shown to accelerate convergence in symmetry-conserving calculations for doubly closed-shell nuclei, also provides a significant computational advantage when used in symmetry-breaking many-body methods that describe superfluid and deformed open-shell nuclei; the benefit is demonstrated for low-order deformed Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (dBMBPT) and can be further enhanced by combining natural orbitals with importance-truncation techniques.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory at second order (dBMBPT(2)) to compute the one-body density matrix of open-shell nuclei, then diagonalized this density matrix to obtain deformed natural orbitals (NAT[dBMBPT(2)]). The Hamiltonian matrix elements were transformed from the spherical harmonic oscillator (sHO) basis into the NAT basis, and a subsequent dBMBPT(2) or dBMBPT(3) calculation was performed on the truncated NAT basis to evaluate ground-state energies. The convergence of the ground-state energy as a function of the basis truncation parameter (effective e_max) was compared between the sHO and NAT bases for several nuclei, using two chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (EM 1.8/2.0 and NNLO_sat at various resolution scales).

## Key numbers

- For 20Ne with EM 1.8/2.0, the 1% error relative to the e_max=12 reference is reached at e_max=8 in the sHO basis but at e_max=6 in the NAT[dBMBPT(2)] basis, reducing the number of basis states by roughly a factor of two.
- For 70Fe with EM 1.8/2.0, a similar advantage is observed: the NAT basis reduces the neutron-proton asymmetry in convergence and achieves the same accuracy with fewer shells than the sHO basis.
- For 18O with EM 1.8/2.0, the same advantage (1% error at e_max=6 vs. e_max=8) is observed for both dBMBPT(2) and dBMBPT(3) calculations.
- For 20Ne with the NNLO_sat (bare) Hamiltonian, e_max=8 in the NAT basis yields a 1% error, while the sHO basis requires two more major shells (e_max=10) for the same accuracy.
- For 56Fe with NNLO_sat (bare), the NAT basis does not offer a significant gain over the sHO basis when targeting a 1% error.
- The relative error for 56Fe with NNLO_sat (bare) is flatter as a function of oscillator frequency hbar-omega in the NAT basis than in the sHO basis; the benefit is minimal at the optimal frequency hbar-omega = 18 MeV for the sHO basis.

## Context

This work extends the use of natural orbitals, previously shown to accelerate convergence in symmetry-conserving methods for closed-shell nuclei (Hoppe 2021) and in exact diagonalization techniques (Fasano 2022, Tichai 2019), to symmetry-breaking expansion methods applicable to open-shell nuclei. The results enable more efficient ab initio calculations of deformed and superfluid nuclei by reducing the required one-body basis size, and the combination of natural orbitals with importance truncation techniques (Roth 2009, Tichai 2019) promises even greater computational savings for non-perturbative methods such as deformed coupled cluster, in-medium similarity renormalization group, and Dyson self-consistent Green's function approaches.
