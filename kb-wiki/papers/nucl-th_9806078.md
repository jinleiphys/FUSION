# Vector mesons in nuclear medium with small three momentum, a QCD sum rule approach

> Using the QCD Operator Product Expansion, we derive the real part of the transverse and longitudinal vector vector correlation function with the $ρ,ω$ quantum numbers to leading order in density and in ${\bf q}^2$ at $-ω^2\to \infty $. To dimension 6, only twist-2 and 4 operators contribute. These OPE, through the energy dispersion relation, provide model independent constraints for the ${\bf q}$ dependence of the vector meson spectral density in nuclear medium. We further make a QCD sum rule type of analysis to extract the momentum dependence of the vector meson dispersion relation in medium. The contributions from twist-2 operators are added up to infinite order to check the validity of the OPE at the relevant Borel window.

# Vector mesons in nuclear medium with small three momentum, a QCD sum rule approach

## Key claim

Using the QCD Operator Product Expansion (OPE) to leading order in density and in q² at -ω² → ∞, the authors derive model-independent constraints on the three-momentum dependence of the vector meson spectral density in nuclear medium, but only for Borel masses above a minimum value (M²_min ~ 0.8 GeV² for transverse, ~ 2.5 GeV² for longitudinal polarization) where the OPE truncation at dimension 6 is reliable.

## Method

The authors perform an OPE for the transverse and longitudinal vector-vector correlation functions with ρ, ω quantum numbers, expanding to linear order in q² at fixed energy. They compute the Wilson coefficients and nucleon matrix elements for twist-2 and twist-4 operators up to dimension 6, using parton distribution functions from DIS data and previously estimated K-parameters for twist-4 contributions. A Borel transformation is applied to the dispersion relation, and the infinite sum of twist-2 operator contributions is compared to the truncated OPE to validate the Borel window.

## Key numbers

- Minimum Borel mass for reliable OPE: M²_min ~ 0.8 GeV² for transverse polarization, M²_min ~ 2.5 GeV² for longitudinal polarization
- Twist-4 K-parameters used: (K¹, K², K¹_ud, K^g) = (-0.173 GeV², 0.203 GeV², -0.083 GeV², -0.238 GeV²) with β = 0.5
- For the ρ meson mass shift with momentum: m_ρ(ρ_n)/m_ρ(0) = 1 - (0.023 ± 0.007) (q/0.5)² (ρ_n/ρ_0), where q is in GeV and ρ_0 is nuclear matter density
- For the ω meson mass shift with momentum: the coefficient 0.023 changes to 0.016
- Scattering term b_scatt = -1/(4m) ρ_n for longitudinal ρ, ω meson and zero for transverse parts

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to finite three-momentum, previously applied only at q=0 by Hatsuda and Lee (1992), and provides model-independent constraints that can test phenomenological models of vector meson spectral densities in nuclear matter. The authors note that phenomenological models based on p-wave nucleon resonances (e.g., Rapp, Chanfray, Wambach 1997; Klingl, Weise 1998) appear to overestimate the momentum dependence. The results enable future model calculations to be checked against these OPE constraints, and the extracted momentum-dependent mass shifts can be used in dilepton production calculations for heavy-ion collisions.
