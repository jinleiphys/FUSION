# The properties of the D-meson in dense matter

> We study the D-meson spectral density in dense matter within the framework of a coupled-channel self-consistent calculation taking, as bare meson-baryon interaction, a separable potential. Our coupled-channel model generates dynamically the $Λ_c(2593)$ resonance. The medium modifications of the D-meson properties due to Pauli blocking and the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons and pions are also discussed. We found that the coupled-channel effects in the self-consistent process reduce the in-medium effects on the D-meson compared to previous works.

# The properties of the D-meson in dense matter

## Key claim
The self-consistent coupled-channel calculation, including the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions, reduces the in-medium effects on the D-meson compared to previous works that predicted a mass drop of 50-60 MeV at nuclear matter density. The actual attraction found is much smaller, lying between 2.6 and 12.3 MeV depending on the cutoff parameter, with the precise value depending on whether nucleons and pions are also dressed.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled-channel G-matrix equation using a separable potential as the bare meson-baryon interaction, with coupling constants and cutoff parameters fixed to reproduce the position and width of the dynamically generated Λc(2593) resonance. The D-meson single-particle potential is obtained self-consistently in a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, including Pauli blocking and the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions in symmetric nuclear matter. The self-energy is then used to compute the D-meson spectral density at various densities.

## Key numbers
- The Λc(2593) resonance is dynamically generated at a position of 2593.9 ± 2 MeV with a width of Γ = 3.6 (+2.0, -1.3) MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ0, the D-meson potential (attraction) lies between 2.6 and 12.3 MeV for cutoff values Λ = 0.8 to 1.4 GeV when nucleons and pions are dressed.
- The quasiparticle peak in the spectral density at zero momentum shifts slightly to lower energies, with the Λc resonance appearing around 1.63-1.65 GeV.
- Previous QCD sum-rule and quark-meson coupling model calculations predicted a mass drop of 50-60 MeV at nuclear matter density, which is substantially larger than the results obtained here.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations based on QCD sum-rules (Arata, 2003) and the quark-meson coupling model (QMC, 2003) that predicted a 50-60 MeV mass drop for D-mesons in nuclear matter, as well as lattice calculations for heavy-quark potentials (Digal, 2003) and a chiral model (Mishra, 2003). The key advance is the inclusion of coupled-channel effects and the self-consistent dressing of intermediate propagators, which were ignored in previous works. The results are relevant for understanding open-charm enhancement in nucleus-nucleus collisions, J/Ψ suppression, and the possible formation of D-mesic nuclei, and can be tested with the future PANDA experiment at GSI.
