# Large scale shell model calculations along Z=28 and N=50 closures: towards the doubly-magic 78Ni

> We present the state-of-the art shell model calculations in a large model space (pf for protons, fpgd for neutrons), which allow to study simultaneously excitations across the Z=28 and N=50 shell gaps. We explore the region in the vicinity of 78Ni, being a subject of intense experimental investigations. Our calculations account correctly for the known low lying excited states in this region, including those which may correspond to cross-shell excitations. We observe the minimum of the N=50 mass gap at Z=32 consistent with experimental data and its further increase towards Z=28, indicating a robustness of the N=50 gap in 78Ni. The evolution of N=50 gap along the nickel chain is shown to bear similarities with what is know in oxygen and calcium chains, providing a new opportunity for the studies of 3-body monopole effects in medium mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the N=50 shell gap remains robust in 78Ni, contrary to some earlier suggestions of weakening, but the minimum in the mass gap observed at Z=32 is due to correlation effects in semi-magic nuclei rather than a change in the spherical mean-field.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations in a model space comprising pf orbitals for protons and fpgd orbitals for neutrons, using the m-scheme code ANTOINE with up to 12p-12h excitations relative to the π0f7/2 and ν0g9/2 orbitals. The effective interaction was based on two-body matrix elements from Lenzi (2010) with monopole modifications to constrain the proton gap evolution from 68Ni to 78Ni. The calculations were compared to known low-lying states in even-even N=50 nuclei (78Ni to 84Se) and odd N=49 isotones (79Zn to 85Kr) to probe the N=50 gap evolution.

## Key numbers

- The N=50 mass gap calculated from mass differences varies from 5 MeV in 78Ni to 3.9 MeV in 82Ge, then increases again towards 86Kr.
- The first excited 2+ state in 78Ni is predicted at nearly 4 MeV.
- The ground state of 78Ni has 79% closed-shell configuration.
- The proton gap in 78Ni is estimated at 5.25 MeV from calculated mass differences.
- The first excited 5/2+ state in 79Zn is predicted at approximately 1 MeV.
- The Z=28 gap reduction between 68Ni and 78Ni is about 0.7 MeV.
- The largest matrix dimensions treated reach 3×10^9.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model study of the Z=28 gap in copper isotopes (Sieja and Nowacki, 2011) to include the d5/2 neutron orbital and examine the N=50 gap. The results contradict earlier suggestions of N=50 weakening by Krumlinde (1984), Rzaca (2007), and Winger (2010), while supporting the conclusions of Verney (2007), Angelis (2007), and Vande Walle (2009). The evolution of the N=50 gap along the nickel chain shows similarities to the behavior attributed to effective three-body forces in oxygen and calcium chains by Holt and collaborators, and the authors call for analogous ab initio studies to examine the origin of the N=50 gap in 78Ni.
