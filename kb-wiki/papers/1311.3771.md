# Polarization of the nuclear surface in deformed nuclei

> The density profiles of around 750 nuclei are analyzed using the Skyrme energy density functional theory. Among them, more than 350 nuclei are found to be deformed. In addition to rather standard properties of the density, we report a non-trivial behavior of the nuclear diffuseness as the system becomes more and more deformed. Besides the geometric effects expected in rigid body, the diffuseness acquires a rather complex behavior leading to a reduction of the diffuseness along the main axis of deformation simultaneously with an increase of the diffuseness along the other axis. The possible isospin dependence of this polarization is studied. This effect, that is systematically seen in medium- and heavy-nuclei, can affect the nuclear dynamical properties. A quantitative example is given with the fusion barrier in the $^{40}$Ca+ $^{238}$U reaction.

## Key claim
The paper reports a non-trivial polarization of the nuclear surface diffuseness in deformed nuclei, where the diffuseness along the main deformation axis decreases while it increases along the compressed axis. This effect is systematic in medium- and heavy-mass nuclei (A > 120) and is predicted by Skyrme energy density functional theory, but the authors note that the effect is only clearly systematic for masses above A > 120 due to large fluctuations in lighter systems.

## Method
The authors analyzed the ground-state density profiles of 749 even-even nuclei (Z=8 to Z=108) using the EV8 code to solve Hartree-Fock+BCS equations with Skyrme functionals (SkM* and Sly4) in r-space. Each density was fitted to a deformed Thomas-Fermi shape with angle-dependent radius and diffuseness parameters, where the perpendicular diffuseness was expanded in spherical harmonics with coefficients β̃₂ and β̃₄ to capture surface polarization beyond geometric effects. The 42 triaxial nuclei were excluded from the analysis, and only axially deformed nuclei were considered.

## Key numbers
- 749 even-even nuclei were analyzed, of which more than 350 were found to be deformed; with the SkM* functional, 346 were spherical, 59 oblate, 290 prolate, and 54 triaxial.
- The mesh size used was Δx = Δy = Δz = 0.8 fm with total mesh dimensions 2Lx = 2Ly = 2Lz = 28.8 fm.
- The average diffuseness a₀ was approximately 0.55 fm across the nuclear chart.
- For ²³⁸U, the deformation parameters from SkM* were β₂ = 0.244 and β₄ = 0.094.
- The relative polarization (a_L - a_S)/a₀ reached up to about 10% for heavy deformed nuclei.
- For the ⁴⁰Ca + ²³⁸U reaction, three diffuseness values were tested: a = 0.494 fm (minimum), 0.5504 fm (average), and 0.5832 fm (maximum), corresponding to the radial diffuseness variation along the nuclear surface.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nuclear density profiles (Bohr and Mottelson, 1997) by systematically quantifying surface diffuseness polarization in deformed nuclei across the nuclear chart, going beyond the simple geometric corrections assumed in rigid-body models. The authors note that similar surface polarization effects were suspected to affect giant dipole resonance strength (Pena, 2009) and that the diffuseness plays an important role in fusion barrier calculations (Dutra, 2010). The results enable improved modeling of fusion reactions involving heavy deformed nuclei, particularly for super-heavy element synthesis, and suggest that collective motion in deformed nuclei may be affected by this surface polarization.
