# Parity Violating Effects in Elastic Electron Deuteron Scattering

> The general expressions for parity violation observables in elastic scattering of polarized and/or unpolarized electrons from unpolarized deuterons are given and are numerically evaluated for the kinematics of SAMPLE, PVA4 and G0 experiments. The dominant contribution from the interference of $γ$ and Z exchange as well as the smaller contributions from strangeness ($s{\bar{s}}$) components of the nucleon, parity odd admixtures in the deuteron wave function, anapole moments and radiative corrections are included and discussed in the context of parity violating electron scattering experiments of present interest.

## Key claim

The paper provides general expressions for parity violating observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering, including contributions from gamma-Z interference, strangeness components, parity-odd admixtures in the deuteron wave function, anapole moments, and radiative corrections, and evaluates them numerically for the kinematics of SAMPLE, PVA4, and G0 experiments, finding that the parity-odd admixture contributions are small in the kinematic regions of these experiments.

## Method

The authors derived general formulas for parity violating observables (differential cross section, vector recoil polarization, and tensor recoil polarization) in elastic scattering of polarized and unpolarized electrons from unpolarized deuterons, using multipole expansions of the hadronic electromagnetic and weak neutral currents. They calculated the parity-odd admixture in the deuteron wave function in first-order perturbation theory using the DDH parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential, and evaluated the multipole matrix elements using the Bonn OBEPQ model for the deuteron wave function with electromagnetic and weak form factors parameterized in dipole form.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.2246 MeV, used as the unperturbed value in the perturbation calculation of parity-odd wave function components
- Vector dipole mass M_V = 0.84 GeV, taken from experimental data on electron-proton scattering
- Axial dipole mass M_A = 1.026 GeV, used in the dipole parameterization of the axial vector form factor
- Weak coupling constants for the DDH model: f_pi = 12, h_rho^0 = -30, h_rho^1 = -0.5, h_rho^2 = -25, h_omega^0 = -5, h_omega^1 = -3, h_rho'^1 = 0 (all in units of g_pi = 3.8 x 10^-8)
- Weak coupling constants for the DZ model: f_pi = +3, h_rho^0 = -22, h_rho^1 = +1, h_rho^2 = -18, h_omega^0 = -10, h_omega^1 = -6, h_rho'^1 = 0 (all in units of g_pi = 3.8 x 10^-8)
- Weak coupling constants for the FCDH model: f_pi = +7, h_rho^0 = -10, h_rho^1 = -1, h_rho^2 = -18, h_omega^0 = -13, h_omega^1 = -6, h_rho'^1 = 0 (all in units of g_pi = 3.8 x 10^-8)
- Proton magnetic moment mu_p = 1.792847, neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -1.913043

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous general formalism (Ahmad, Singh, and Arenhoevel, 2001) for parity violating observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering by including explicit numerical evaluations for the kinematics of the SAMPLE (Spayde et al., 2000; Ito et al., 2004; Aniol et al., 2006), PVA4 (Baunack et al., 2007), and G0 (Roche et al., 2006) experiments. The calculations incorporate contributions from strangeness form factors, anapole moments, and radiative corrections that were previously studied in quasi-elastic scattering by Schiavilla et al. (2003) and Liu et al. (2003), and enable more precise extraction of the strange form factors G_E^S, G_M^S, and G_A^S from elastic deuteron measurements to complement hydrogen target experiments.
