# Spin-orbit coupling and the up-down differential transverse flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> To explore the strength, isospin dependence, and density dependence of the in-medium spin-orbit coupling that is poorly known but relevant for understanding the structure of rare isotopes, the nucleon spin up-down differential transverse flow is studied systematically by varying the beam energy and impact parameter of Au+Au collisions within a spin-isospin dependent transport model recently developed for investigating spin-related phenomena in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. The optimal reaction conditions for delineating and disentangling effects of different terms in the spin-orbit coupling are discussed.

## Key claim

The nucleon spin up-down differential transverse flow in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the strength, isospin dependence, and density dependence of the in-medium spin-orbit coupling, but disentangling these effects requires systematic variation of beam energy and impact parameter rather than a single reaction condition.

## Method

The authors used the spin-isospin dependent transport model SIBUU12, which incorporates spin degrees of freedom and spin-dependent mean-field potentials into the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck framework. They simulated Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 50 to 300 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters from 4 to 12 fm, generating 200,000 events per condition (and up to 1 million events for high-transverse-momentum analyses). The spin up-down differential transverse flow was computed as a function of rapidity, and its slope at mid-rapidity was extracted using a third-order polynomial fit.

## Key numbers

- The strength of the spin-orbit coupling has been constrained between 80 and 150 MeVfm\(^5\) from nuclear structure studies.
- For Au+Au at b=8 fm and 100 MeV/nucleon, the slope parameter \(F'\) of the spin up-down differential flow is \(4.49 \pm 0.35\) for \(W_0=150\) MeVfm\(^5\) and \(2.60 \pm 0.02\) for \(W_0=80\) MeVfm\(^5\), giving a sensitivity parameter \(\delta = 0.27 \pm 0.04\).
- The largest spin up-down differential transverse flow is observed at a beam energy of about 100 MeV/nucleon for mid-central (b=8 fm) Au+Au collisions.
- For neutrons and protons with isospin-like coupling (a=2, b=1) at 100 MeV/nucleon and b=8 fm, the neutron slope is \(F'_n = 5.62 \pm 0.35\) and proton slope is \(F'_p = 2.55 \pm 0.33\), giving \(\delta' = 0.38 \pm 0.06\).
- High-transverse-momentum cuts used were \(p_T > 0.20\) GeV/c at 50 MeV/nucleon, \(p_T > 0.25\) GeV/c at 150 MeV/nucleon, and \(p_T > 0.32\) GeV/c at 200 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Xu et al., 2013) which first introduced the SIBUU12 model and showed sensitivity of the spin up-down differential flow to spin-orbit coupling parameters. It addresses the difficulty that strength, density dependence, and isospin dependence all affect the flow simultaneously, making single-reaction extraction ambiguous. The results enable future experiments at facilities like RIKEN, GSI, NSCL, and GANIL to probe the in-medium spin-orbit force by measuring spin-polarized fragments, providing complementary information to nuclear structure studies of rare isotopes and superheavy elements.
