# Measurement of the reaction gamma p --> K0 Sigma+ at photon energies up to 2.6 GeV

> The reaction gamma p --> K0 Sigma+ was measured in the photon energy range from threshold up to 2.6 GeV with the SAPHIR detector at the electron stretcher facility, ELSA, in Bonn. Results are presented on the reaction cross section and the polarization of the Sigma+ as a function of the kaon production angle in the centre-of-mass system, cos(Theta_K^{c.m.}), and the photon energy. The cross section is lower and varies less with photon energy and kaon production angle than that of gamma p --> K+ Sigma0. The Sigma+ is polarized predominantly at cos(Theta_K^{c.m.}) \approx 0. The data presented here are more precise than previous ones obtained with SAPHIR and extend the photon energy range to higher values. They are compared to isobar model calculations.

## Key claim

The cross section for gamma p -> K0 Sigma+ is lower and varies less with photon energy and kaon production angle than that of gamma p -> K+ Sigma0, contradicting the expectation from simple isospin Clebsch-Gordan coefficients that would predict a factor of four in favor of K+ Sigma0 if a Delta resonance dominates the peak region.

## Method

The reaction was measured using the SAPHIR magnetic multiparticle spectrometer at the ELSA electron stretcher facility in Bonn, with an energy-tagged photon beam from 0.868 to 2.650 GeV. Events were reconstructed from the charged decay products of K0_S -> pi+ pi- and Sigma+ -> p pi0 or n pi+, using kinematical fits to determine the momenta of unmeasured particles, with background from reactions such as gamma p -> p pi+ pi- pi0 and n pi+ pi+ pi- removed via vertex probability cuts and bin-by-bin subtraction.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section at photon energy 1.050-1.150 GeV: 0.205 +/- 0.040 microbarns
* Total cross section at photon energy 1.350-1.450 GeV (peak region): 0.612 +/- 0.059 microbarns
* Total cross section at photon energy 2.450-2.600 GeV: 0.086 +/- 0.027 microbarns
* Final event sample: 3310 events (2114 with Sigma+ -> p pi0, 1196 with Sigma+ -> n pi+)
* Average detector acceptance: 3.4%, varying between 2% and 8% across the kinematical range
* Sigma+ polarization at cos(Theta_K^{c.m.}) between -0.2 and 0.2 for photon energies below 1.55 GeV: 0.759 +/- 0.126
* Sigma+ polarization at cos(Theta_K^{c.m.}) between -0.2 and 0.2 for photon energies above 1.55 GeV: 0.433 +/- 0.343
* Previous SAPHIR cross sections from Goers (1999) were 30 to 40% higher than the current results

## Context

This work extends the earlier SAPHIR measurement by Goers (1999) with a sixfold increase in trigger statistics (from 30 million to 180 million triggers) and extends the photon energy range to 2.6 GeV, while correcting for previously underestimated background from gamma p -> p pi+ pi- pi0. The data are compared to isobar model calculations by Bennhold and Mart and to the KAON-MAID model, showing that the new results require significant readjustment of coupling constants, particularly suppressing K* exchange contributions, and enable improved fits to both the K+ Sigma0 and K0 Sigma+ channels simultaneously.
