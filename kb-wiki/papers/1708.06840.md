# Towards an understanding of discrete ambiguities in truncated partial wave analyses

> It is well known that the observables in a single-channel scattering problem remain invariant once the amplitude is multiplied by an overall energy- and angle-dependent phase. This invariance is called the continuum ambiguity and acts on the infinite partial wave set. It has also long been known that, in the case of a truncated partial wave set, another invariance exists, originating from the replacement of the roots of partial wave amplitudes with their complex conjugate values. This discrete ambiguity is also known as the Omelaenko-Gersten-type ambiguity. In this paper, we show that for scalar particles, discrete ambiguities are just a subset of continuum ambiguities with a specific phase and thus mix partial waves, as the continuum ambiguity does. We present the main features of both, continuum and discrete ambiguities, and describe a numerical method which establishes the relevant phase connection.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for scalar particles, the discrete Gersten-type ambiguities (root-conjugation ambiguities) in truncated partial wave analyses are a subset of the more general continuum ambiguity transformations, specifically those angle-dependent phase rotations that map a truncated amplitude back into another truncated amplitude of the same order.

## Method

The authors derive a mixing formula (Eq. 13) that expresses how a general angle-dependent phase rotation, expanded in Legendre polynomials, transforms the partial wave amplitudes of the original amplitude into a linear combination of waves. They then construct a functional minimization scheme that searches for phase-rotation functions satisfying two constraints: the rotated amplitude must remain truncated at the same maximal angular momentum L, and the rotation function must have unit modulus at all angles. The minimization is performed using a Legendre-series parametrization of the rotation function, with the claim that the only minima consistent with zero correspond to the discrete Gersten-ambiguities.

## Key numbers

* The number of discrete Gersten-ambiguities for a truncation at angular momentum L is 2^L, corresponding to all combinatoric possibilities of conjugating subsets of the L complex roots of the polynomial amplitude.
* The functional minimization uses a Legendre expansion of the phase-rotation truncated at index L_cut, with the maximal choice K_cut = L_cut for the number of vanishing higher partial wave constraints.
* A discrete grid of N_I equidistant points in cos(theta) over [-1,1] is employed, with spacing Delta x = 2/N_I.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of continuum ambiguities by Bowcock and Burkhardt and the discrete ambiguity formalism of Gersten (Gersten), as well as the work of Omelaenko (Omelaenko) on discrete ambiguities in photoproduction. The paper is complementary to the study by Svarc et al (2017) on related ambiguity issues in partial wave analyses. The results enable a unified understanding of how discrete ambiguities arise from the larger class of continuum ambiguity transformations, and the numerical method provides a way to identify all discrete ambiguities in truncated analyses without relying on root-finding.
