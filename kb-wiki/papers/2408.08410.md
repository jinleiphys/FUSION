# Coulomb Excitation of $^{80}$Sr and the limits of the $N = Z = 40$ island of deformation

> The region of $N\approx Z\approx 40$ has long been associated with strongly deformed nuclear configurations. The presence of this strong deformation was recently confirmed through lifetime measurements in $N\approx Z$ Sr and Zr nuclei. Theoretically, however, these nuclei present a challenge due to the vast valence space required to incorporate all deformation driving interactions. Recent state-of-the-art predictions indicate a near axial prolate deformation for $N=Z$ and $N=Z+2$ nuclei between $N=Z=36$ and $N=Z=40$. In this work we investigate the shores of this island of deformation through a sub-barrier Coulomb excitation study of the $N=Z+4$ nucleus, \textsuperscript{80}Sr. Extracting a spectroscopic quadrupole moment of $Q_s(2^+_1) = 0.45^{+0.83}_{-0.88}$~eb, we find that \textsuperscript{80}Sr is inconsistent with significant axial prolate deformation. This indicates that the predicted region of strong prolate deformation around $N=Z=40$ is tightly constrained to the quartet of nuclei: \textsuperscript{76,78}Sr and \textsuperscript{78,80}Zr.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the first excited state in ⁸⁰Sr, \(Q_s(2^+_1) = 0.45^{+0.83}_{-0.88}\) eb, is inconsistent with significant axial prolate deformation at the level of more than 1.5 sigma, indicating that the predicted island of strong prolate deformation around \(N=Z=40\) is tightly constrained to the quartet of nuclei ⁷⁶,⁷⁸Sr and ⁷⁸,⁸⁰Zr rather than extending to the \(N=Z+4\) nucleus ⁸⁰Sr.

## Method

A sub-barrier Coulomb excitation measurement of radioactive ⁸⁰Sr was performed at the TRIUMF-ISAC facility using a 4.25 MeV/u beam impinged upon a 0.882 mg/cm² ²⁰⁸Pb target. Scattered particles were detected in S3-type annular silicon detectors and prompt gamma rays were detected by the TIGRESS array; yields as a function of scattering angle were analysed using the semi-classical coupled-channels code GOSIA with an external chi-squared minimisation, incorporating the adopted literature B(E2; \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\)) value as a constraint due to large ⁸⁰Se beam contamination.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the first \(2^+\) state in ⁸⁰Sr: \(Q_s(2^+_1) = 0.45^{+0.83}_{-0.88}\) eb.
- Ratio \(Q_s(2^+_1)/|Q^{\text{rot}}_s(2^+_1)| = 0.5(9)\), inconsistent with the prolate limit of \(-1\) at more than 1.5 sigma.
- The \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\) transition in ⁸⁰Sr was observed at 386 keV above a Compton background dominated by the ⁸⁰Se contaminant transition at 666 keV.

## Context

This work extends recent lifetime measurements by Llewellyn (2020) that confirmed strong deformation in ⁸⁰Zr and by Lemasson (2012) for ⁷⁶Sr, and tests state-of-the-art shell-model predictions using the PMMU interaction by Kaneko (2021) which predicted axial prolate deformation for \(N=Z\) and \(N=Z+2\) nuclei between \(N=Z=36\) and \(N=Z=40\). The result suggests that ⁸⁰Sr may lie in a transitional region between prolate and oblate minima, similar to the behaviour seen in ⁸²Zr in HFB+GCM calculations, and is consistent with the triaxiality observed in the isotonic neighbour ⁷⁸Kr by Becker (2006).
