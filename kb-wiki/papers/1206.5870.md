# Spatial structure of Cooper pairs in nuclei

> We discuss the spatial structure of the Cooper pair in dilute neutron matter and neutron-rich nuclei by means of the BCS theory and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogioliubov model, respectively. The neutron pairing in dilute neutron matter is close to the region of the BCS-BEC crossover in a wide density range, giving rise to spatially compact Cooper pair whose size is smaller than the average interaparticle distance. This behavior extends to moderate low density ($\sim 10^{-1}$ of the saturation density) where the Cooper pair size becomes smallerst ($\sim 5$ fm). The Cooper pair in finite nuclei also exhibits the spatial correlation favoring the coupling of neutrons at small relative distances $r \lesim 3$ fm with large probability. Neutron-rich nuclei having small neutron separation energy may provide us opportunity to probe the spatial correlation since the neutron pairing and the spatial correlation persists also in an area of low-density neutron distribution extending from the surface to far outside the nucleus.

# Spatial structure of Cooper pairs in nuclei

## Key claim
The Cooper pair in dilute neutron matter and neutron-rich nuclei can become spatially compact (smaller than the average interparticle distance) under certain density conditions, but this conclusion is drawn from BCS and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations that may overestimate the pairing gap; ab initio Monte Carlo calculations predict a reduction of the gap by less than 50%, keeping the qualitative density dependence intact.

## Method
The author uses BCS theory with the bare nuclear force in the 1S channel to compute the Cooper pair wave function and coherence length in uniform dilute neutron matter as a function of density. For finite neutron-rich nuclei, the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model with a density-dependent contact pairing force (reproducing the scattering length a = -18 fm) is employed to calculate the Cooper pair wave function, pair potential, and pair transition densities. The quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on the same Skyrme-HFB model is used to describe soft dipole and octupole excitation modes.

## Key numbers
- Coherence length in neutron matter at saturation density (rho/rho0 = 1): xi = 46 fm.
- Minimum coherence length in neutron matter: xi = 5-8 fm over density range rho/rho0 = 0.2 to 10^-2.
- Smallest coherence length value: xi = 4.9 fm at rho/rho0 = 1/8.
- Pairing gap maximum in neutron matter: Delta ≈ 3 MeV at kF ≈ 0.8 fm^-1 (rho/rho0 ≈ 0.2).
- In finite nuclei, the Cooper pair wave function shows large probability (∼50%) for neutrons coupled at relative distances r ≤ 3 fm.
- In 142Sn, the pair potential extends to r ≥ 12 fm before diminishing.
- For neutron-rich Sn isotopes beyond N=82 (A>132), the pair transition density extends to r ∼ 12 fm.
- One-neutron separation energy for Sn isotopes with A>132: ∼2-3 MeV; for A ≤ 132: >8 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of Cooper pair spatial structure from traditional metal superconductors (where the coherence length is thousands of times larger than the interparticle distance) to nuclear systems, building on earlier BCS calculations of neutron matter superfluidity by Tamagaki and others. The finding that dilute neutron matter lies in the BCS-BEC crossover region (xi/d ∼ 0.7-1.2, Delta/eF ∼ 0.2-0.4 at rho/rho0 ∼ 10^-4 to 10^-1) connects nuclear pairing to the phenomenon studied in ultracold Fermi atom gases by Leggett, Nozieres, and Randeria. For finite nuclei, the work generalizes earlier core-plus-two-neutron model studies of spatial correlation in halo nuclei (Esbensen, Hansen, Ikeda, Hagino) and shell model approaches for stable nuclei (Bertsch, Ibarra) to a broader range of open-shell nuclei using HFB. The predicted enhancement of two-neutron transfer strength in neutron-rich Sn isotopes beyond N=82 provides a testable probe for future radioactive beam experiments, following the demonstration of pair correlation effects in 11Li transfer reactions by Tanihata and Potel.
