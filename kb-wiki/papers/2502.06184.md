# Octupole correlations in superdeformed bands of $^{56}$Ni

> The projected multi-dimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model was employed to calculate the potential energy surface of the high-spin states in $^{56}\text{Ni}$. It is pointed out for the first time that possible octupole deformations exist for the positive and negative parity superdeformed bands in $^{56}\text{Ni}$, with deformations $β_{30}\sim0.14$ and $β_{30}\sim0.24$, respectively, along with a large prolate deformation of $β_{20}\sim 0.42$. These octupole deformations are induced by the coupling between $2p_{3/2}$ and $1g_{9/2}$ orbits at the deformation $β_{20}\sim 0.4$. The calculated excitation energies of the two rotational bands are consistent with the observed superdeformed bands of $^{56}\text{Ni}$. In addition, two rotational bands are predicted, consisting of one superdeformed band with negative parity and one hyperdeformed bands with positive parity.

## Key claim

For the first time, the authors propose that the two experimentally observed superdeformed bands in $^{56}$Ni are a pair of positive- and negative-parity bands with octupole deformations, specifically $\beta_{30}\sim0.14$ for the positive-parity band (SD1) and $\beta_{30}\sim0.24$ for the negative-parity band (SD2), both at a large prolate deformation of $\beta_{20}\sim0.42$. The claim is hedged by noting that the mean-field potential energy surface shows no $\beta_{30}$ minimum but only softness in that direction, meaning the octupole deformation emerges only after angular momentum and parity projection.

## Method

The authors used the projected multi-dimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (p-MDCRHB) model with the PC-PK1 density functional, solving the RHB equation in an axially-deformed harmonic oscillator basis. They first calculated the two-dimensional potential energy surface in the $\beta_{20}$-$\beta_{30}$ plane at the mean-field level, then performed simultaneous angular momentum and parity projection to obtain projected potential energy surfaces for positive-parity states $0^+$ to $16^+$ and negative-parity states $1^-$ to $17^-$. Particle number conservation was handled approximately via a Lipkin-Nogami-type correction added to the Hamiltonian kernel.

## Key numbers

- The positive-parity superdeformed band SD1 has deformations $\beta_{20}=0.42$ and $\beta_{30}=0.14$.
- The negative-parity superdeformed band SD2 has deformations $\beta_{20}=0.42$ and $\beta_{30}=0.24$.
- The calculated excitation energy of the $0_3^+$ state (the bandhead of SD1) is $5.69$ MeV, close to the experimentally observed third $0^+$ state at $5$ MeV.
- The ground state $0_1^+$ after projection has a slight oblate deformation $\beta_{20}=-0.14$ with octupole deformation $\beta_{30}=0.12$.
- The second minimum $0_2^+$ has deformation $\beta_{20}=0.14$, $\beta_{30}=0.22$ with an excitation energy of $0.17$ MeV.
- The mean-field prolate minimum is at $\beta_{20}=0.40$, consistent with the experimental dynamical quadrupole moment corresponding to $\beta_2=0.37$.
- A predicted superdeformed band SD3 (negative parity) has deformation $\beta_{20}\sim -0.34$, $\beta_{30}\sim 0.16$.
- A predicted hyperdeformed band HD1 (positive parity) has deformation $\beta_{20}\sim -0.6$.

## Context

This work extends the study of octupole correlations from the well-established "islands of octupole deformation" around $A\sim220$ and $A\sim150$ (e.g., $^{224}$Ra, $^{144}$Ba) and the newer region around $A\sim80$ (e.g., $^{78}$Br) down to the $A\sim60$ mass region, specifically $^{56}$Ni. The two superdeformed bands in $^{56}$Ni were first observed experimentally in 1999 by Rudolph et al., and earlier cranked Hartree-Fock calculations had suggested the SD2 band involves a $2p_{3/2}$ to $1g_{9/2}$ excitation, which the present work identifies as the source of octupole correlations. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted SD3 and hyperdeformed bands, and motivate further theoretical work incorporating configuration mixing via multi-reference covariant density-functional theory to better describe higher-spin states.
