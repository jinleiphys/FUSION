# Efimovian states of three charged particles

> When three particles in three dimensions interact with a short-range potential fine-tuned to an infinite scattering length, they form an infinite sequence of loosely bound states obeying discrete scale invariance known as Efimov states. Here we show that analogous states are formed by three charged particles carrying two equal charges and one opposite charge in one, two, and three dimensions without any fine-tuning. Our finding is based on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, where an effective inverse-square attraction is induced as a consequence of the dipole-charge interaction between a hydrogenlike heavy-light atom and a far-separated heavy particle. Because the resulting Efimovian states emerge toward the second or higher dissociation threshold, they are to be realized as quasibound states and may be observed by exciting hydrogen molecular ions and trions in excitonic systems. We also consider the same system but with a logarithmic Coulomb potential relevant to quantum vortices in two-dimensional superfluids, where the Efimovian states are shown to emerge as genuine bound states toward the first dissociation threshold.

# Efimovian states of three charged particles

## Key claim
Three charged particles (two equal charges and one opposite charge) form an infinite sequence of loosely bound states obeying discrete scale invariance in one, two, and three dimensions without any fine-tuning of the interaction strength, unlike conventional Efimov states which require tuning to infinite scattering length. However, for the three-dimensional Coulomb case, these states emerge above the first dissociation threshold and are therefore quasibound states (resonances) rather than true bound states, whereas for the logarithmic Coulomb potential relevant to two-dimensional superfluids, they are genuine bound states below the first dissociation threshold.

## Method
The analysis uses the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for a large mass ratio (two heavy particles much heavier than the light particle), factorizing the three-body wavefunction into a light-particle component and a heavy-particle component. For the three-dimensional Coulomb potential, the light particle forms a hydrogenlike atom with the near heavy particle, and the far heavy particle produces a uniform electric field that induces a linear Stark effect, yielding an effective inverse-square attraction between the heavy particles. For the logarithmic Coulomb potential, the light particle's ground state experiences a quadratic Stark effect, also producing an inverse-square attraction. The resulting radial Schrödinger equation for the heavy particles is solved analytically in terms of modified Bessel functions, giving discrete scaling exponents and binding energies.

## Key numbers
- For the three-dimensional Coulomb potential, the effective inverse-square attraction coefficient is \(3(d+1)\hbar^2/(4mR^2)\), where \(d\) is the spatial dimension.
- For the hydrogen molecular ion (\(d=3\), \(M_1/m = M_2/m = 1836\)), the discrete scaling factor \(e^{-2\pi/s_L}\) ranges from 0.919 at \(L=0\) to 0.544 at \(L=73\).
- The ground-state energy of \(\mathrm{H}_2^+\) is \(E_{\mathrm{H}_2^+} = -0.597\,E_\mathrm{h}\) (where \(E_\mathrm{h} \equiv \hbar^2/(ma_0^2) = 27.21\,\mathrm{eV}\)).
- The Efimovian resonances for \(\mathrm{H}_2^+\) accumulate toward \(E_N = -0.125\,E_\mathrm{h} - (\hbar^2\kappa_L^{*2}/2M)e^{-2\pi N/s_L}\) for each \(L \leq 73\).
- For the logarithmic Coulomb potential, the numerical constants \(C_d\) in the inverse-square attraction term \(\hbar^2 C_d/(mR^2)\) are: \(C_1 = 0.3149\), \(C_2 = 0.6152\), \(C_3 = 0.9117\).
- The unperturbed ground-state energy of a heavy-light atom with logarithmic Coulomb potential is \(\mathcal{E}_{n=1} = KQ^2 \times \{0.6978\ (d=3),\ 0.1799\ (d=2),\ -0.8764\ (d=1)\}\).
- The mean-square radius of the ground-state wavefunction for the logarithmic Coulomb potential is \(\langle r^2 \rangle = b_0^2 \times \{2.399\ (d=3),\ 1.091\ (d=2),\ 0.2858\ (d=1)\}\).
- The scattering length between a hydrogen atom and a proton is \(750\,a_0\) (Carbonell, 2003).

## Context
This work extends the concept of Efimov states (Efimov, 1970; Efimov, 1973), originally discovered for short-range potentials tuned to infinite scattering length, to systems with long-range Coulomb interactions without any fine-tuning. The analysis uses the Born-Oppenheimer approximation following the approach of Fonseca (1979) for conventional Efimov states. The predicted Efimovian states are analogous to four-body Efimov states (Hammer, 2007; Stecher, 2009) and atomic collapse states (Shytov, 2007; Wang, 2013) in that they are quasibound states embedded in the continuum. The results are potentially relevant to hydrogen molecular ions (Roth, 2008), trions in excitonic systems (Kezerashvili, 2019), and quantum vortices in two-dimensional superfluids (Gauthier, 2019; Johnstone, 2019). The hydrogen molecular ion is noted to have an extremely shallow s-wave bound state making the hydrogen-proton scattering length as large as \(750\,a_0\) (Carbonell, 2003), potentially linking Efimov and Efimovian physics in the same system (Macek, 2007).
