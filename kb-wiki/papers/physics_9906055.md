# Exact Dirac equation calculation of ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> The time-dependent Dirac equation can be solved exactly for ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Ionization calculations are carried out in such a framework for a number of representative ion-ion pairs. For each ion-ion pair, the computed cross section consists of two terms, a constant energy independent term and a term whose coefficient is ln(gamma). Scaled values of both terms are found to decrease with increasing Z of the nucleus that is ionized.

# Exact Dirac equation calculation of ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions

## Key claim

The time-dependent Dirac equation can be solved exactly for ionization induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, yielding cross sections that consist of a constant energy-independent term and a term proportional to ln(gamma); however, for heavy systems like Pb+Pb the perturbative energy-dependent term A is about 60% lower than previous calculations by Anholt and Becker (1987), while the non-perturbative constant term B agrees relatively well.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation exactly using a delta-function gauge for the Coulomb potential of an ultrarelativistic heavy ion, obtaining exact transition amplitudes that incorporate all higher-order effects. Ionization probabilities are calculated as a function of impact parameter for symmetric and non-symmetric ion-ion pairs (Pb, Zr, Ca, Ne, H), using both the deficit of bound state probabilities and the sum of continuum state probabilities. The impact parameter integration is performed numerically for small to intermediate b, matched to a Weizsacker-Williams formulation at large b to determine the high-end cutoff and the ln(gamma) dependence.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb, the energy-dependent coefficient A is 8680 barns (from bound state deficit) or 8450 barns (from continuum sum), compared to the Anholt and Becker (1987) value of 13,800 barns.
- For Pb+Pb, the constant term B is 14,190 barns (bound state deficit) or 12,920 barns (continuum sum), compared to the Anholt and Becker (1987) value of 13,000 barns.
- For H+H, A is 10,770 barns (bound state deficit) and B is 71,090 barns (bound state deficit), with Anholt and Becker (1987) values of 10,540 and 70,000 barns respectively.
- For Pb+Pb at CERN SPS (gamma = 160), the calculated cross section is 58,200 barns (bound state deficit) or 55,800 barns (continuum sum), compared to the Anholt and Becker (1987) no-screening value of 83,700 barns and the Krause et al. (1998) measured Pb+Au value of 42,000 barns.
- For Pb+Pb at RHIC (gamma = 23,000), the calculated cross section is 101,400 barns (bound state deficit) or 97,800 barns (continuum sum).

## Context

This work extends the exact semiclassical Dirac equation approach of Baltz (1997), which previously calculated only impact-parameter-dependent probabilities for Pb+Pb, to produce full ionization cross sections for multiple ion-ion pairs. The results contradict the perturbative calculations of Anholt and Becker (1987), which overestimate the energy-dependent term A for heavy systems by up to 60%, likely because they used approximate relativistic bound state wave functions rather than exact Dirac wave functions. The calculations enable comparison with experimental data from Krause et al. (1998) at CERN SPS, where the present Pb+Pb result (privately communicated) was scaled to account for screening in the Au target and found to agree with the measured cross section of 42,000 barns.
