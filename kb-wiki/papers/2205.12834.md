# Critical net-baryon fluctuations in an expanding system

> In this work we study the consequences of a longitudinal Bjorken expansion and a Hubble-like temperature cooling scenario on a 1+1D non-linear model of the diffusive dynamics of fluctuations in the net-baryon density. The equilibrium behavior of the fluctuations is fully encoded in the temperature dependence of the susceptibilities on the crossover side both in the vicinity of the assumed location of the critical point and at vanishing baryo-chemical potential in-line with lattice QCD calculations. We demonstrate the great sensitivity of the fluctuation observables on the dynamics, in particular on the diffusion length and the freeze-out conditions. While the critical signals are visible and the critical region is broadened by the expansion, a too small diffusion length can strongly reduce the amplitude of the signals. We propose to search for significant anti-correlations of baryons at intermediate rapidity experimentally and to map out the rapidity dependence of the fourth-order cumulant, which in the presence of a critical point (and only in its presence) has a pronounced minimum at intermediate rapidities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that critical net-baryon fluctuations in an expanding system are highly sensitive to the diffusion length and freeze-out conditions, and that a too small diffusion length can strongly reduce the amplitude of critical signals even when the critical region is broadened by the expansion.

## Method

The authors solved a 1+1D stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density fluctuations in Milne coordinates, incorporating a Bjorken longitudinal expansion and a Hubble-like temperature cooling scenario. The equilibrium properties were encoded in a free-energy functional whose second and fourth order susceptibilities were parametrized to connect a 3D Ising model mapping of the QCD critical point with lattice QCD results at vanishing baryo-chemical potential. The full non-linear model included a quartic coupling term, and the authors studied the time evolution, diffusion length dependence, and rapidity window dependence of the second and fourth order cumulants.

## Key numbers

* The critical point is placed at T_c = 150 MeV and mu_{B,c} = 390 MeV.
* The initial temperature is T_i = 500 MeV at initial proper-time tau_0 = 1 fm/c.
* The diffusion length is set to D = 1 fm for the main results.
* The second order susceptibility in the QGP phase is chi_2^{QGP} = 0.02 fm^{-2} at T = 280 MeV, and in the hadronic phase chi_2^{H} = 0.01 fm^{-2} at T = 130 MeV.
* The fourth order susceptibility in the QGP phase is chi_4^{QGP} = 0.00135 fm^{-6}, and in the hadronic phase chi_4^{H} = 0.01 fm^{-6}.
* The surface tension parameter is K(tau) = 2 fm^{-4}, which reduces the final second-order cumulant by approximately 20% compared to the lattice QCD input for a rapidity window of Delta y = 1.
* The constant critical net-baryon density is n_c = 1/3 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic fluid dynamics approach of Nahrgang (2018) and Sakaida (2017) by incorporating a full parametrization of susceptibilities that connects the 3D Ising model scaling region with lattice QCD results from Cheng (2008) and Bazavov (2017). It enables future experimental searches for critical fluctuations at RHIC, LHC, GSI, and FAIR by predicting that the fourth-order cumulant has a pronounced minimum at intermediate rapidities only in the presence of a critical point, and by proposing to search for significant anti-correlations of baryons at intermediate rapidity.
