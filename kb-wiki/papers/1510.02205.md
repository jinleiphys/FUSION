# Interaction of the vector-meson octet with the baryon octet in effective field theory

> We analyze the constraint structure of the interaction of vector mesons with baryons using the classical Dirac constraint analysis. We show that the standard interaction in terms of two independent SU(3) structures is consistent at the classical level. We then require the self-consistency condition of the interacting system in terms of perturbative renormalizability to obtain relations for the renormalized coupling constants at the one-loop level. As a result we find a universal interaction with one coupling constant which is the same as in the massive Yang-Mills Lagrangian of the vector-meson sector.

## Key claim

The authors show that the standard SU(3) interaction of the vector-meson octet with the baryon octet, which at the Lagrangian level contains two independent coupling constants (G_F and G_D), is consistent at the classical Dirac-constraint level without any relation between them; however, requiring perturbative renormalizability at one-loop order forces a universal interaction with a single coupling constant equal to the massive Yang-Mills coupling \(g\).

## Method

The authors performed a classical Dirac constraint analysis on the leading-order effective Lagrangian for a massive vector-meson octet interacting with a baryon octet, verifying that the system yields the correct number of physical degrees of freedom without imposing relations among the couplings \(g\), G_F, and G_D. They then computed the divergent parts of the one-loop vector-meson self-energy and the three-vector and four-vector vertex functions using dimensional regularization, and demanded that the counter-term \(\delta g\) simultaneously cancel the divergences in both the three-vector and four-vector vertex functions, which yields consistency conditions that relate the baryon-vector couplings to the vector self-coupling \(g\).

## Key numbers

* The divergent part of the vector-meson self-energy in SU(3) is \(\Pi_{ab}^{\mu \nu\,\text{div}}(p) = -\frac{\lambda}{6\pi^2}(5\text{G}_\text{D}^2+9\text{G}_\text{F}^2)\delta_{ab}(g^{\mu \nu}p^2-p^\mu p^\nu)\), where \(\lambda = \frac{1}{16\pi^2}\left\{\frac{1}{D-4}-\frac{1}{2}\left[\ln(4\pi)+\Gamma'(1)+1\right]\right\}\).
* In the SU(2) case, the non-trivial solution to the consistency conditions is \(g_{VNN}=g\), corresponding to the universality principle.
* The free vector fields require 16 constraints to reduce from 64 total degrees of freedom to 48 physical degrees of freedom; the free Dirac fields require 64 constraints to reduce from 128 to 64; the interacting theory requires 80 constraints to reduce from 192 to 112 physical degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends the Dirac constraint analysis previously applied to the pion-nucleon-Delta system by Wies et al. (2006) and the pure vector-meson sector by Djukanovic et al. (2010) and Neiser (2011), which found that consistency conditions reduce the number of independent couplings. The result that the baryon-vector coupling becomes universal (equal to the Yang-Mills coupling \(g\)) generalizes the SU(2) universality principle derived by Djukanovic et al. (2004) to SU(3). This enables a parameter-free prediction for the leading-order vector-meson–baryon interaction in effective field theories, reducing the phenomenological input needed for calculations of vector-meson interactions with baryons.
