# Elliptic flow and $R_{AA}$ of D mesons at FAIR comparing the UrQMD hybrid model and the coarse-graining approach

> We present a study of the elliptic flow and $R_{AA}$ of D and anti-D mesons in Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies. We propagate the charm quarks and the D mesons following a previously applied Langevin dynamics. The evolution of the background medium is modeled in two different ways: (I) we use the UrQMD hydrodynamics + Boltzmann transport hybrid approach including a phase transition to QGP and (II) with the coarse-graining approach employing also an equation of state with QGP. The latter approach has previously been used to describe di-lepton data at various energies very successfully. This comparison allows us to explore the effects of partial thermalization and viscous effects on the charm propagation. We explore the centrality dependencies of the collisions, the variation of the decoupling temperature and various hadronization parameters. We find that the initial partonic phase is responsible for the creation of most of the D mesons elliptic flow and that the subsequent hadronic interactions seem to play only a minor role. This indicates that D mesons elliptic flow is a smoking gun for a partonic phase at FAIR energies. However, the results suggest that the magnitude and the details of the elliptic flow strongly depend on the dynamics of the medium and on the hadronization procedure, which is related to the medium properties as well. Therefore, even at FAIR energies the charm quark might constitute a very useful tool to probe the Quark-Gluon Plasma and investigate its physics.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of D mesons at FAIR energies is a "smoking gun" for a partonic phase, but the magnitude and details of this flow depend strongly on the dynamics of the medium and on the hadronization procedure, meaning the signal is not a clean, model-independent observable.

## Method

The authors propagated charm quarks and D mesons using a relativistic Langevin dynamics approach, with drag and diffusion coefficients computed from a resonance model for charm quarks and from effective chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetry Lagrangians for D mesons. The background medium evolution was modeled in two ways: (I) the UrQMD hybrid model (hydrodynamics plus Boltzmann transport with a phase transition to QGP) and (II) the UrQMD coarse-graining approach (extracting macroscopic quantities from an ensemble of microscopic UrQMD cascade events). The study explored Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 7 GeV, varying centrality, decoupling temperature, and hadronization parameters.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) approximately 7 GeV for Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies
- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV; D-meson-like resonance masses set to m_D = 2 GeV
- Strong coupling constant chosen as alpha_s = 0.4, giving resonance widths of Gamma_D = 0.75 GeV
- Charm-quark equilibration times: tau_eq^c = 2-10 fm/c from the resonance model
- For the coarse-graining approach: 1.44 x 10^5 events averaged for impact parameter b = 3 fm; 2.64 x 10^5 events for b = 7 fm
- Gaussian width for initial energy density distributions: sigma = 1 fm (default)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy-quark propagation using Langevin dynamics (van Hees 2005, Moore 2004) to FAIR energies, comparing two different bulk evolution models. The coarse-graining approach had previously been used successfully to describe di-lepton data at various energies (Endres 2014, 2015, 2016). The results indicate that the initial partonic phase creates most of the D meson elliptic flow, with hadronic interactions playing only a minor role, suggesting charm quarks can probe the Quark-Gluon Plasma even at FAIR energies. The study enables future improvements by incorporating more sophisticated treatments of the hadronic phase and transport coefficients.
