# BCS-BEC crossover with a finite-range interaction

> We study the crossover from BEC to BCS pairing for dilute systems but with a realistic finite-range interaction. We exhibit the changes in the excitation spectrum that provide a clean qualitative distinction between the two limits. We also study how the dilute system converges to the results from a zero-range pseudo-potential derived by Leggett.

## Key claim

The BCS-BEC crossover point, defined by the appearance of a minimum in the quasiparticle excitation spectrum at finite momentum, always occurs on the side of the Feshbach resonance where a two-body molecular bound state exists (positive scattering length), and it converges to the zero-range, single-parameter potential result of Leggett only in the low-density limit where the condition \(k_F \langle r \rangle < 0.046\) is satisfied.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field BCS gap and number equations at zero temperature for a single-channel model with a finite-range Gaussian interaction potential \(V(r) = V_0 \exp(-b r^2)\). They fix the potential width \(\langle r \rangle\) and vary the potential depth \(V_0\) (and thus the scattering length \(a_0\)) to explore the crossover at constant density, or vary the density at fixed scattering length. The ground state is obtained from the standard BCS variational wave function, and the quasiparticle excitation spectrum \(E_k\) and density of states \(N_k\) and \(N_E\) are computed as functions of momentum and energy.

## Key numbers

- The crossover point for the zero-range Leggett curve occurs at \((a_0 k_F)^{-1} \approx 0.55\) (where \(\mu = 0\)).
- The Leggett curve predicts \(\mu/\varepsilon_F = 0.59\) at the unitarity limit (\(\eta = 0\)).
- Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations lower the upper bound on \(\mu/\varepsilon_F\) at unitarity to \(0.44 \pm 0.01\), a 25% improvement over the mean-field result.
- The universal zero-range equations are valid when \(k_F \langle r \rangle < 0.046\), corresponding to \(a_0/\langle r \rangle \approx 72\).
- Typical experimental parameters correspond to \(k_F \langle r \rangle \simeq 0.04\).
- The numerical results in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3 are computed at fixed density with \(k_F \langle r \rangle \approx 0.37\).

## Context

This work extends the single-parameter, zero-range treatment of the BCS-BEC crossover pioneered by Leggett (1980) to a realistic finite-range Gaussian potential, allowing the authors to quantify when the universal description breaks down. It also contrasts with density-driven crossover studies using Coulomb potentials in the context of excitons (Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, 1985). The paper enables future work to include beyond-mean-field effects (e.g., via two-particle irreducible effective action methods) and to compare the single-channel model with multi-channel models of Feshbach resonances relevant to experiments on ultracold Fermi gases.
