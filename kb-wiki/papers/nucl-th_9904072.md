# Pion cloud and the $Q^2-$dependence of $γ^* N \leftrightarrow Δ$ transition form factors

> Recent experiment indicates that the behavior exhibited by the ratios $E_{1^+}/M_{1^+}$ and $S_{1^+}/M_{1^+}$ of the $γ^* N \leftrightarrow Δ$ transition remain small and negative for $Q^2 \le 4.0 GeV^2$. It implies that the perturbative QCD is still not applicable at these momentum transfers. We show that these data can be explained in a dynamical model for electromagnetic production of pions, together with a simple scaling assumption for the bare $γ^* N Δ$ form factors. Within our model we find that the bare $Δ$ is almost spherical and the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 quardrupole excitations of the physical $Δ$ are nearly saturated by pion cloud contribution in $Q^2 \le 4.0 GeV^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the small and negative values of the ratios \(E_{1^+}/M_{1^+}\) and \(S_{1^+}/M_{1^+}\) for the \(\gamma^* N \leftrightarrow \Delta\) transition up to \(Q^2 \le 4.0\,\text{GeV}^2\) can be explained by a dynamical model for pion electroproduction combined with a simple scaling assumption for the bare \(\gamma^* N \Delta\) form factors, without invoking perturbative QCD. The authors find that the bare \(\Delta\) is almost spherical and that the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 quadrupole excitations of the physical \(\Delta\) are nearly saturated by the pion cloud contribution in this \(Q^2\) range.

## Method

The authors use a dynamical model for pion photo- and electroproduction where the t-matrix is decomposed into a background term (including Born terms and vector meson exchange) and a term for the bare \(\Delta\) excitation, with unitarity enforced by explicitly including the final-state \(\pi N\) interaction via a principal value integral. The \(Q^2\) dependence of the dominant bare magnetic dipole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta\) is parametrized as \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta(Q^2) = \bar{\mathcal{M}}(0) \frac{|\mathbf{k}|}{k_\Delta} (1+\beta Q^2) e^{-\gamma Q^2} G_D(Q^2)\), and the smaller electric and Coulomb amplitudes are assumed to have the same \(Q^2\) dependence. The parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) are determined by fitting to experimental data on the \(M_{1+}^{(3/2)}\) multipole and the \(G_M^*\) form factor.

## Key numbers

- Bare magnetic dipole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta = 158 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) at \(Q^2=0\); dressed value is \(289 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
- Bare electric quadrupole amplitude \(\bar{\mathcal{E}}^\Delta = 0.4 \pm 0.3 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) at \(Q^2=0\); dressed value is \(-7 \pm 0.4 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
- Bare helicity amplitudes at \(Q^2=0\): \(A_{1/2}^\Delta = -80 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}^\Delta = -136 \pm 3 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); dressed values are \(-134 \pm 2\) and \(-256 \pm 2 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\), respectively.
- Bare Sachs magnetic form factor \(G_M^\Delta(0) = 1.65 \pm 0.02\); dressed value is \(3.06 \pm 0.02\).
- Parameters for the \(Q^2\) dependence of \(\bar{\mathcal{M}}^\Delta\): \(\beta = 0.44\,\text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(\gamma = 0.38\,\text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The ratios \(R_{EM}\) and \(R_{SM}\) remain small and negative for \(Q^2 \le 4.0\,\text{GeV}^2\), in agreement with data from Frolov et al. (1999) at \(Q^2 = 2.8\) and \(4.0\,\text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model for pion electroproduction developed by Yang (1985) and Tanabe and Ohta (1985) to explain the \(Q^2\) dependence of the \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition form factors. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the earlier analysis of DESY data by Burkert and Elouadrhiri (1995), which gave small positive ratios at \(Q^2 = 3.2\,\text{GeV}^2\), and the new data from Frolov et al. (1999) showing negative ratios. The results indicate that the bare \(\Delta\) is almost spherical, with the observed quadrupole excitations dominated by pion cloud effects, and that perturbative QCD predictions (Brodsky and Lepage, 1981) do not apply up to \(Q^2 = 4.0\,\text{GeV}^2\). The work enables future searches for the onset of perturbative QCD at higher \(Q^2\).
