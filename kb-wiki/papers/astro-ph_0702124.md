# Neutrino emissivities in 2SC color-superconducting quark matter

> The phase structure and equation of state for two-flavor quark matter under compact star constraints is studied within a nonlocal chiral quark model. Chiral symmetry breaking leads to rather large, density dependent quark masses at the phase transition to quark matter. The influence of diquark pairing gaps and quark masses on density dependent emissivities for the direct URCA is discussed. Since m_u>m_d, the direct URCA process due to quark masses cannot occur. We present cooling curves for model quark stars and discuss their relation to observational data.

# Neutrino emissivities in 2SC color-superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The direct URCA process in two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter cannot occur via the quark mass effect because the self-consistent up quark mass is always larger than the down quark mass under charge conservation, violating momentum conservation; however, emissivities from perturbative one-gluon exchange (OGE) interactions remain comparable to ideal Fermi gas results, provided the quark masses and chemical potentials are taken from a nonlocal chiral quark model rather than from current quark masses.

## Method

The grand canonical potential for 2SC quark matter is computed within a nonlocal chiral quark model of NJL type with a simple cutoff formfactor, using parameters from Buballa (2003): current quark masses 5.5 MeV, coupling constant G_s Lambda^2 = 2.319, cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, and diquark coupling G_D = G_s. The neutrino emissivity from the direct URCA reactions d -> u + e- + antineutrino_e and u + e- -> d + neutrino_e is evaluated using the Iwamoto (1982) formula, modified by a suppression factor eta for the 2/3 of quarks that are paired in the 2SC phase, with the angle theta_ue determined either from perturbative OGE interactions or from finite quark mass effects via the beta-equilibrium condition.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for the chiral phase transition: mu_q^crit = 340 MeV.
- Diquark gap in the 2SC phase: Delta approximately 192 MeV.
- For the mass-effect scenario without flavor mixing: up quark mass m_u = 98 MeV, down quark mass m_d = 45 MeV; with flavor mixing alpha = 0.11: m_u = 62 MeV, m_d = 46 MeV.
- For the ideal Fermi gas comparison: current quark masses m_u = 5 MeV and m_d = 9 MeV.
- Baryon chemical potential fixed to 1.1 GeV for all emissivity calculations.
- Homogeneous quark star model: radius 9 km, mass 1.2 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the original calculation of neutrino emissivities in quark matter by Iwamoto (1982), who found that the direct URCA matrix element vanishes if quark-quark interactions and quark masses are neglected. It contrasts with calculations using perturbative current quark masses (5-9 MeV) by showing that in the non-perturbative density regime near the chiral phase transition, constituent quark masses from NJL-type chiral quark models (Buballa 2003) can be up to two orders of magnitude larger, altering the relative size of mass effects versus OGE contributions. The results enable more realistic cooling simulations of hybrid stars or quark stars, but the author notes that non-perturbative effects such as rho and omega meson exchanges may need to replace perturbative OGE interactions in the density regime close to the phase transition.
