# Adiabatic hydrodynamization in rapidly-expanding quark-gluon plasma

> We propose a new scenario characterizing the transition of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions from a highly non-equilibrium state at early times toward a fluid described by hydrodynamics at late times. We develop an analogy to the evolution of a quantum mechanical system that is governed by the instantaneous ground states. In the simplest case, these slow modes are "pre-hydrodynamic" in the sense that they are initially distinct from, but evolve continuously into, hydrodynamic modes. For a class of collision integrals, the pre-hydrodynamic mode represents the angular distribution (in momentum space) of those gluons that carry most of the energy. We illustrate this scenario using a kinetic description of weakly-coupled Bjorken expanding plasma. Rapid longitudinal expansion drives a reduction in the degrees of freedom at early times. In the relaxation time approximation for the collision integral, we show quantitatively that the full kinetic theory evolution is dominated by the pre-hydrodynamic mode. We elaborate on the criterion for the dominance of pre-hydrodynamic slow modes and speculate that adiabatic hydrodynamization may describe the pre-equilibrium behavior of the QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the transition of quark-gluon plasma from a highly non-equilibrium state to a hydrodynamic fluid can be governed by "pre-hydrodynamic" modes that are distinct from but evolve continuously into hydrodynamic modes, provided that transitions to excited states are suppressed (adiabaticity) rather than requiring those excited states to have decayed.

## Method

The authors study a kinetic description of weakly-coupled Bjorken-expanding plasma using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the collision integral. They expand the momentum-weighted distribution function in Legendre polynomials and map the evolution to a non-Hermitian matrix equation analogous to the time-dependent Schrodinger equation. They identify the instantaneous ground state eigenmode of this matrix as the pre-hydrodynamic mode and compute its eigenvalue, comparing it to the percentage rate of change of the energy density obtained from full numerical solutions of the kinetic equation.

## Key numbers

- The fractional difference between the full kinetic theory result and the pre-hydrodynamic mode contribution to the energy density rate of change reaches a maximum of 0.045 at intermediate values of the parameter lambda = tau/tau_Coll, indicating at least 95% of the contribution comes from the pre-hydrodynamic mode between tau_Att and tau_Hydro.
- For the bottom-up thermalization scenario in weakly-coupled QGP, adiabaticity is expected to apply during the stage tau_Att less than or equal to tau less than or equal to alpha_s^{-5/2} Q_s^{-1} and tau greater than or equal to alpha_s^{-13/5} Q_s^{-1}.
- The minimum gap Delta E_min between the ground state and first excited mode is order one for lambda much less than 1 and becomes linear in lambda for lambda much greater than 1.

## Context

This work extends studies of far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamics by Romatschke (2017), Heller (2016), and others, but differs by proposing that the dominant slow modes before hydrodynamization are pre-hydrodynamic modes qualitatively distinct from hydrodynamic modes, rather than hydrodynamic modes themselves. The authors contrast their scenario with the paradigm that hydrodynamic modes dominate even far from equilibrium, and note that the attractor behavior previously observed in RTA kinetic theory by Heller (2016) and Romatschke (2017) does not by itself indicate whether one mode or many modes dominate the evolution. The work enables future construction of an effective theory of "pre-hydrodynamics" and suggests that adiabatic hydrodynamization may describe pre-equilibrium behavior in heavy-ion collisions more broadly, including beyond Bjorken expansion.
