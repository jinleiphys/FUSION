# Probing Near-Threshold $s$-Wave Components in Heavy Nuclei via Coulomb-Assisted Neutron Transfer

> We propose a method to probe weakly bound s-wave neutron components near the neutron emission threshold in heavy nuclei using Coulomb-assisted neutron transfer reactions. Weakly bound s-wave neutrons have large asymptotic amplitudes, which are difficult to access directly with conventional methods. This work focuses on the $(d,p)$ reaction at low incident energies and backward angles, where the reaction is localized in the nuclear exterior due to the Coulomb barrier. Under these conditions, the transition amplitude becomes sensitive to the asymptotic part of the single-particle wave function. Finite-range DWBA calculations show that the cross section for weakly bound states exhibits a weak dependence on incident energy, while that for strongly bound states decreases rapidly with decreasing energy. Contributions from orbitals with $l \geq 1$ are suppressed by the centrifugal barrier, resulting in selectivity for s-wave components. This method provides a probe of the strength distribution of weakly bound s-wave components near threshold and the asymptotic structure of their wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Coulomb-assisted $(d,p)$ reactions at low incident energies (around 5 MeV) and backward angles (near 180 degrees) can selectively probe weakly bound s-wave neutron components near the neutron emission threshold in heavy nuclei, with the key condition that the reaction must be localized in the nuclear exterior by the Coulomb barrier to become sensitive to the asymptotic part of the neutron wave function.

## Method

Finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations were performed using the FRESCO code for the $(d,p)$ reaction on $^{157}$Gd, leading to states in $^{158}$Gd. The calculations used Woods-Saxon optical potentials for deuterons and protons with parameters from Daehnick (1981) and Bechetti (1969), respectively, with the neutron bound-state wave function generated by a Woods-Saxon potential with geometry parameters $r_0 = 1.25$ fm and $a = 0.65$ fm, where the depth was adjusted to reproduce the binding energy for each excitation energy. The spectroscopic factor was set to unity to examine reaction selectivity independently of nuclear-structure fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- At incident energy $E_d = 5$ MeV, the cross section for weakly bound states exceeds that for strongly bound states and exhibits a pronounced peak at backward angles, particularly near 180 degrees, in contrast to the behavior at $E_d = 40$ MeV where the cross section for weakly bound states is smaller over all angles
- For a strongly bound state with $E_n = 8$ MeV, the cross section at 180 degrees falls rapidly below $E_d \sim 8$ MeV, while the weakly bound s-wave transition remains sizable down to lower energies
- The cross section for the $3d_{5/2}$ orbital is reduced to about 0.15 times that for the $4s_{1/2}$ orbital at $E_d = 5$ MeV, demonstrating selectivity for s-wave components over $l \geq 1$ orbitals

## Context

This work extends the study of halo structures and weakly bound neutron components from light nuclei, where such phenomena are well established (Tanihata 1985), to heavy nuclei in the mass region $A \sim 160$ where the $4s_{1/2}$ orbital is predicted to lie near the neutron separation threshold (Bohr and Mottelson; Campbell 1960). The method addresses a limitation of thermal neutron capture cross section measurements, which indicate near-threshold s-wave strength in $^{155}$Gd and $^{157}$Gd (Mughabghab; Yamaguchi 2024) but do not directly probe the spatial extension of the wave function. The proposed approach enables systematic measurements along isotopic chains to constrain the distribution and asymptotic structure of s-wave strength near threshold, though future experimental applications will need to account for background from Coulomb-induced breakup processes and may require coupled-channel treatments such as CDCC calculations.
