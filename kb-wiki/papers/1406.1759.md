# Relic Neutrino Freeze-out: Dependence on Natural Constants

> Analysis of cosmic microwave background radiation fluctuations favors an effective number of neutrinos, $N_ν>3$. This motivates a reinvestigation of the neutrino freeze-out process. Here we characterize the dependence of $N_ν$ on the Standard Model (SM) parameters that govern neutrino freeze-out. We show that $N_ν$ depends on a combination $η$ of several natural constants characterizing the relative strength of weak interaction processes in the early Universe and on the Weinberg angle $\sin^2θ_W$. We determine numerically the dependence $N_ν(η,\sin^2θ_W)$ and discuss these results. The extensive numerical computations are made possible by two novel numerical procedures: a spectral method Boltzmann equation solver adapted to allow emerging chemical non-equilibrium, and a method to evaluate Boltzmann equation collision integrals that generates a smooth integrand.

## Key claim

The effective number of neutrinos \(N_\nu\) depends only on two dimensionless parameters: the interaction strength \(\eta \equiv M_p m_e^3 G_F^2\) (a combination of the Planck mass, electron mass, and Fermi constant) and the Weinberg angle \(\sin^2\theta_W\); the paper numerically determines \(N_\nu(\eta,\sin^2\theta_W)\) and argues that variation of these natural constants in the early Universe could reduce or remove the tension between the Standard Model prediction \(N_\nu^{\rm th}=3.046\) and the Planck satellite's central values \(N_\nu\in(3.3,3.6)\).

## Method

The authors solve the general relativistic Einstein-Boltzmann equation for neutrino freeze-out using a spectral method that allows for chemical non-equilibrium (fugacity \(\Upsilon\neq1\)) and reheating, unlike prior fixed-basis chemical-equilibrium approaches. They analytically reduce the 8-dimensional collision integrals for \(2\leftrightarrow2\) weak-interaction processes (involving neutrinos and \(e^\pm\)) to iterated integrals with smoother integrands, enabling efficient numerical computation of \(N_\nu\) as a function of \(\eta\) and \(\sin^2\theta_W\).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum present-day interaction strength: \(\eta_0 = 0.04421\) (from \(M_p m_e^3 G_F^2\) evaluated at current constants).
- Standard Model theoretical prediction: \(N_\nu^{\rm th}=3.046\) (from Mangano (2005) using two-body scattering interactions).
- Planck satellite results: \(N_\nu=3.36\pm0.34\) (CMB only), \(N_\nu=3.30\pm0.27\) (CMB+BAO), \(N_\nu=3.62\pm0.25\) (CMB+\(H_0\)) at 68% confidence levels.
- Weinberg angle values: \(\sin^2\theta_W|_{M_Z}=0.23116\pm0.00012\) and \(\sin^2\theta_W|_{M_W}=0.22296\pm0.00028\); the vacuum value \(\sin^2\theta_W=0.223\) corresponds to \(M_W/M_Z=0.8815\).

## Context

This work extends prior Boltzmann-equation computations of neutrino freeze-out (e.g., Mangano (2005), Esposito (2000), Dolgov and Hansen) by introducing a spectral method that handles chemical non-equilibrium and by isolating the dependence of \(N_\nu\) on natural constants into just two parameters \(\eta\) and \(\sin^2\theta_W\). The results enable future CMB experiments to constrain possible time or temperature variation of Standard Model parameters at \(t\approx1\) second, and they connect to theories of modified neutrino interactions (Mangano (2006)), Goldstone boson models (Weinberg (2013)), and quark-gluon plasma phase transition scenarios (Birrell (2014)).
