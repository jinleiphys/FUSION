# Two-neutrino $ββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe to the first excited $0^+$ state in $^{136}$Ba

> We calculate the nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino $ββ$ decay of $^{136}$Xe into the first excited $0^+$ state of $^{136}$Ba. We use different many-body methods: the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) framework, the nuclear shell model, the interacting boson model (IBM-2), and an effective field theory (EFT) for $β$ and $ββ$ decays. While the QRPA suggests a decay rate at the edge of current experimental limits, the shell model points to a half-life about two orders of magnitude longer. The predictions of the IBM-2 and the EFT lie in between, and the latter provides systematic uncertainties at leading order. An analysis of the running sum of the nuclear matrix element indicates that subtle cancellations between the contributions of intermediate states can explain the different theoretical predictions. For the EFT, we also present results for two-neutrino $ββ$ decays to the first excited $0^+$ state in other nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the half-life of the two-neutrino double-beta decay of 136Xe to the first excited 0+ state in 136Ba varies by more than four orders of magnitude depending on the many-body method used, with the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) giving a half-life near current experimental limits while the nuclear shell model predicts a half-life about two orders of magnitude longer; the authors attribute this spread to subtle cancellations between contributions from intermediate states that differ across methods.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear matrix element for the two-neutrino double-beta decay of 136Xe to the first excited 0+ state in 136Ba using four many-body methods: the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with large single-particle bases, the nuclear shell model with the GCN5082 and QX interactions, the interacting boson model (IBM-2) with reassessed single-particle energies, and an effective field theory (EFT) for beta and double-beta decays. All methods were adjusted to reproduce the known ground-state-to-ground-state decay, either via quenching factors (QRPA, shell model, IBM-2) or by fitting low-energy couplings to measured two-neutrino double-beta decay half-lives (EFT). The running sum of the nuclear matrix element as a function of intermediate-state excitation energy was analyzed to understand the origin of the different predictions.

## Key numbers

* QRPA with adjusted basis: half-life range (0.14-2.9) x 10^2 x 10^21 years (relative to ground-state half-life of 2.2 x 10^21 years)
* QRPA with Woods-Saxon basis: half-life range (0.47-13) x 10^2 x 10^21 years
* Nuclear shell model with GCN5082 interaction: half-life range (2.5-2.9) x 10^5 x 10^21 years
* Nuclear shell model with QX interaction: half-life range (5.8-6.6) x 10^5 x 10^21 years
* IBM-2: half-life range (1.5-3.6) x 10^4 x 10^21 years
* EFT: half-life range (0.62-16) x 10^4 x 10^21 years
* Phase space factor disfavors decay to the 0+2 state relative to the ground state by a factor of approximately 4000
* Shell-model quenching factors: q=0.42 for GCN5082, q=0.68 for QX; IBM-2 quenching factor: q=0.31; QRPA quenching range: q=(0.47-1.00)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of two-neutrino double-beta decay to excited states, which had been measured in 100Mo and 150Nd without prior predictions (Barabash 2020, CUPID-Mo 2022), and provides the first shell-model predictions for 136Xe decay to the 0+2 state. The results are compared with earlier QRPA calculations by Pirinen (2015) and IBM-2 calculations by Barea (2015), and with experimental lower limits from KamLAND-Zen (2015) and EXO-200 (2015). The large spread in predictions across methods highlights that experimental searches for this decay can serve as a stringent test of many-body methods used to calculate neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which currently differ by up to a factor of a few (Agostini 2022).
