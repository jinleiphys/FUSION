# Charge Radius of Neutron-deficient $^{54}$Ni and Symmetry Energy Constraints Using the Difference in Mirror Pair Charge Radii

> The nuclear root-mean-square charge radius of $^{54}$Ni was determined with collinear laser spectroscopy to be $R(^{54}$Ni) = 3.737\,(3)~fm. In conjunction with the known radius of the mirror nucleus $^{54}$Fe, the difference of the charge radii was extracted as $ΔR_{\rm ch}$ = 0.049\,(4)~fm. Based on the correlation between $ΔR_{\rm ch}$ and the slope of the symmetry energy at nuclear saturation density ($L$), we deduced $20 \le L \le 70$\,MeV. The present result is consistent with the $L$ from the binary neutron star merger GW170817, favoring a soft neutron matter EOS, and barely consistent with the PREX-2 result within 1$σ$ error bands. Our result indicates the neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca as 0.15\,-\,0.19\,fm.

## Key claim

The difference in charge radii between the mirror pair $^{54}$Ni and $^{54}$Fe, measured as $\Delta R_{\rm ch} = 0.049\,(4)$ fm, constrains the slope of the symmetry energy $L$ to 20–70 MeV, consistent with a soft neutron matter equation of state from GW170817 but only barely consistent with the stiffer PREX-2 result within 1$\sigma$ error bands.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on radioactive $^{54}$Ni produced at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory, thermalized in a gas cell, and bunched in an RFQ ion trap. The beam was neutralized in a charge-exchange cell and the metastable $3d^94s$ $^3\mathrm{D}_3$ state was populated; fluorescence from excitation to the $3d^94p$ $^3\mathrm{P}_2$ state with 352-nm laser light was recorded as a function of a scanning voltage. Isotope shifts relative to stable $^{58}$Ni and $^{60}$Ni were extracted and combined with atomic field-shift and mass-shift factors from a King-fit analysis to obtain the differential mean-square charge radius and the root-mean-square charge radius of $^{54}$Ni.

## Key numbers

- $R(^{54}\mathrm{Ni}) = 3.737\,(3)$ fm (root-mean-square charge radius, from collinear laser spectroscopy)
- $\Delta R_{\rm ch} = 0.049\,(4)$ fm (difference in charge radii between $^{54}$Ni and $^{54}$Fe)
- $L$ constrained to $20 \le L \le 70$ MeV (slope of symmetry energy at nuclear saturation density, deduced from correlation with $\Delta R_{\rm ch}$)
- Neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca predicted as 0.15–0.19 fm (from the data-to-data correlation with $\Delta R_{\rm ch}$)
- $B(E2,\uparrow)_p = 619$ e$^2$ fm$^4$ for $^{54}$Fe (calculated, compared to experimental 640(13) e$^2$ fm$^4$)
- $B(E2,\uparrow)_p = 467$ e$^2$ fm$^4$ for $^{54}$Ni (calculated, compared to experimental upper limit $<800$ e$^2$ fm$^4$)

## Context

This work extends the mirror-charge-radii method previously applied to the $^{36}$Ca–$^{36}$S pair by Brown (2020) to the $A=54$ mirror pair, providing an independent constraint on the symmetry energy slope $L$. The result is consistent with the range $L = 11$–65 MeV deduced from the binary neutron star merger GW170817 by Raithel (2019), favoring a soft neutron matter equation of state, but is barely consistent with the stiffer $L = 106\,(37)$ MeV implied by the PREX-2 measurement of the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb by Adhikari (2021). The prediction for the neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca is timely for the upcoming CREX result, which will test whether the soft or stiff equation of state is realized.
