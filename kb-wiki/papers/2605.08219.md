# Modeling $Λ$ polarization in Au$+$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV using relativistic spin hydrodynamics

> We investigate spin polarization dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using ideal relativistic spin hydrodynamics, employing non-boost-invariant longitudinal solutions as the hydrodynamic background. Operating in the small-polarization regime, where spin evolves perturbatively on top of the bulk expansion, we first analyze a $(1+1)$D setup with transverse homogeneity. In this framework, symmetry-constrained initial conditions for the spin potential lead to non-trivial evolution and generate both local and global $Λ$ hyperon polarization consistent with qualitative experimental trends, though they fail to reproduce observed azimuthal structures. To address this limitation, we extend the framework by incorporating transverse flow and spatial anisotropy at freeze-out, constructing a $novel$ $(1+1+2)$D model that preserves the longitudinal dynamics. We demonstrate that the inclusion of a longitudinal spin acceleration component, coupled with transverse expansion, results in the emergence of a quadrupole pattern in the longitudinal polarization. The resulting momentum-dependent and integrated observables exhibit qualitative and reasonably good quantitative agreement with experimental data for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. Finally, we provide predictions for the in-plane transverse spin polarization, an observable that, to our knowledge, has not yet been experimentally measured.

## Key claim

Using ideal relativistic spin hydrodynamics in the small-polarization regime with a non-boost-invariant longitudinal background, the authors show that a quadrupole pattern in longitudinal Lambda hyperon polarization emerges only when transverse flow and a longitudinal spin acceleration component are included, while a simpler (1+1)D setup with transverse homogeneity captures qualitative global trends but fails to reproduce the observed azimuthal structure.

## Method

The authors first solve the ideal hydrodynamic background using non-boost-invariant longitudinal solutions from Shi, Jeon, and Gale (2022), then evolve the spin degrees of freedom perturbatively on top of this background using the de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW) spin tensor formulation. They analyze a (1+1)D setup with transverse homogeneity and symmetry-constrained initial conditions for the spin potential, then extend to a (1+1+2)D model that incorporates transverse flow and spatial anisotropy at freeze-out while preserving the longitudinal dynamics. The spin polarization of Lambda hyperons is computed via the mean Pauli-Lubanski vector from the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- The analysis is performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The effective mass used in the spin tensor coefficients is m = 0.3 GeV
- The Lambda hyperon mass m_Lambda is used in the polarization calculation, with transverse mass defined as m_T = sqrt(m_Lambda^2 + p_T^2)
- The spin potential components are initialized with symmetry constraints: omega_y and a_y are eta_s-even functions; omega_x, omega_z, a_x, and a_z are eta_s-odd functions

## Context

This work extends earlier spin hydrodynamic studies by Florkowski et al. (2019, 2021) and Singh et al. (2020, 2022) that used boost-invariant or highly symmetric backgrounds, which were found to be too restrictive for realistic polarization patterns. The non-boost-invariant longitudinal solutions developed by Shi, Jeon, and Gale (2022) provide the analytic background. The paper addresses the longstanding discrepancy between early thermal and hydrodynamic calculations (Karpenko 2016, Xie 2017, Wu 2019, Ivanov 2020, Fu 2020) that reproduced a quadrupole structure in longitudinal polarization but with the wrong sign or magnitude compared to STAR data (2019). The results enable predictions for in-plane transverse spin polarization, an observable not yet experimentally measured.
