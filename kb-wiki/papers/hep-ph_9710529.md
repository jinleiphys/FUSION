# On Chemical Equilibrium in Nuclear Collisions

> The data on average hadron multiplicities in central A+A collisions measured at CERN SPS are analysed with the ideal hadron gas model. It is shown that the full chemical equilibrium version of the model fails to describe the experimental results. The agreement of the data with the off-equilibrium version allowing for partial strangeness saturation is significantly better. The freeze-out temperature of about 180 MeV seems to be independent of the system size (from S+S to Pb+Pb) and in agreement with that extracted in e+e-, pp and p{\bar p} collisions. The strangeness suppression is discussed at both hadron and valence quark level. It is found that the hadronic strangeness saturation factor gamma_S increases from about 0.45 for pp interactions to about 0.7 for central A+A collisions with no significant change from S+S to Pb+Pb collisions. The quark strangeness suppression factor lambda_S is found to be about 0.2 for elementary collisions and about 0.4 for heavy ion collisions independently of collision energy and type of colliding system

## Key claim

The full chemical equilibrium version of the ideal hadron gas model fails to describe average hadron multiplicities in central A+A collisions at CERN SPS, but the off-equilibrium version allowing for partial strangeness saturation agrees significantly better with the data. The freeze-out temperature of about 180 MeV appears independent of system size from S+S to Pb+Pb and matches that extracted from e+e-, pp, and p-pbar collisions.

## Method

The authors fit the ideal hadron gas model to full-phase-space average hadron multiplicities from central S+S, S+Ag (200 A GeV/c), and Pb+Pb (158 A GeV/c) collisions measured by the NA35 and NA49 collaborations at CERN SPS. They introduce a strangeness saturation factor gamma_S that multiplies Boltzmann factors by gamma_S^s for hadrons with s valence strange quarks, and determine temperature T, volume V, baryochemical potential mu_B, and gamma_S by chi-squared minimization. The model uses canonical ensemble treatment with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, and includes decays of all hadronic resonances up to 1.7 GeV mass.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of about 180 MeV is independent of system size from S+S to Pb+Pb
- Hadronic strangeness saturation factor gamma_S increases from about 0.45 for pp interactions to about 0.7 for central A+A collisions
- No significant change in gamma_S from S+S to Pb+Pb collisions
- Quark strangeness suppression factor lambda_S is about 0.2 for elementary collisions and about 0.4 for heavy ion collisions, independent of collision energy and colliding system type
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for full equilibrium fits is about 6-8; for off-equilibrium fits it is about 3.5 for S+S and S+Ag, and close to 1 for Pb+Pb
- For S+S collisions, fitted temperature T = 200 +/- 10 MeV, gamma_S = 0.99 +/- 0.11, mu_B/T = 1.35 +/- 0.09 when excluding negatively charged hadrons from the fit
- For S+Ag collisions with Q/B = 0.5, fitted T = 177.5 +/- 7.9 MeV, gamma_S = 0.707 +/- 0.063, mu_B/T = 1.325 +/- 0.081

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis previously applied to e+e-, pp, and p-pbar collisions by Becattini (1996, 1997) to central nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS energies. It contradicts the findings of Braun-Munzinger (1996) who claimed full equilibrium described Pb+Pb data, a discrepancy the authors attribute to that analysis using particle ratios in limited phase space regions requiring additional dynamical input. The results enable systematic comparison of strangeness production across all collision systems and support the interpretation that chemical freeze-out occurs near the hadronization point with a common temperature, while the strangeness enhancement in heavy ion collisions cannot be fully attributed to increased system size at hadron level.
