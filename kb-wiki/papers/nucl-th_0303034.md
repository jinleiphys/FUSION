# Pion damping width from SU(2) x SU(2) NJL model

> Within the framework of the NJL model, we investigate the modification of the pion damping width in a hot pion gas for temperatures ranging from 0 to 180 MeV. The pion is found to broaden noticeably at T > 60 MeV. Near the chiral phase transition T ~ 180 MeV, the pion width is saturated and amounts to 70 MeV. The main contribution to the width comes from pion-pion collisions. Other contributions are found negligibly small.

## Key claim

Within the SU(2) × SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the pion damping width in a hot pion gas broadens noticeably above 60 MeV and saturates near 70 MeV at the chiral phase transition temperature of about 180 MeV, but this result is obtained under the approximation of a constant, energy- and momentum-independent width and a self-consistent iteration procedure that neglects contributions from processes other than pion–pion scattering.

## Method

The authors used the bosonized SU(2) × SU(2) NJL model with an infrared cutoff to compute the pion–pion scattering amplitude, including contact terms and resonant σ-meson exchange in the s, t, and u channels. They then calculated the pion damping width from the Kadanoff–Baym collision integrals, integrating the cross sections over the thermal phase space with Bose occupation numbers and Breit–Wigner spectral functions, and iterated the width equation to self-consistency for temperatures from 0 to 180 MeV at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Pion damping width saturates at 70 MeV near the chiral phase transition temperature of about 180 MeV.
* Pion broadening becomes noticeable at temperatures above 60 MeV.
* At T = 160 MeV, the pion–pion scattering contribution to the total width is about 80 MeV, the maximum value.
* At T = 180 MeV, the πσ → πσ process contributes about 5 MeV, ππ → σσ about 1 MeV, and ππ → σ less than 1 MeV; decays to quarks contribute less than 7 MeV.
* The constituent quark mass in vacuum is m = 242 MeV, with coupling constants gπ = 2.61 and gσ = 2.18; the pion decay constant is fπ = 93 MeV.
* The a1-meson mass is M_{a1} = 1250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies of pion properties at finite temperature (Asakawa 1989, Ebert 1993, Kunihiro 1994, Blaschke et al. 2001) that used the mean-field approximation and did not account for collisions in the pion gas. By including collision integrals via the Kadanoff–Baym formalism, the authors provide a first estimate of the pion damping width from pion–pion scattering, which may affect dilepton production calculations in heavy-ion collisions (Schulze 1996) and can be extended to other mesons such as the σ- and ρ-mesons.
