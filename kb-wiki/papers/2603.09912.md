# Jet energy loss in anisotropic plasmas meets limiting attractors

> We consider the energy loss of a high-energy parton in the early anisotropic plasma in heavy-ion collisions. Working in the harmonic approximation, we compute the change in the mean energy of an emitted gluon in the presence of an anisotropic background, characterized by anisotropic jet quenching parameters $\hat q_{x}\neq \hat q_{y}$. Evaluating the resulting integrals numerically, we compare with isotropic media, and obtain a simple pocket formula to estimate the impact of anisotropy on the mean emitted gluon energy, which is generally small. We then combine our results with the values of the jet quenching parameter extracted from QCD kinetic theory simulations and show that the medium length dependence of this mean energy loss exhibits the characteristics of limiting attractors, which can be obtained by extrapolating to zero and infinite coupling. Our study thus relates energy loss of jet partons to universal dynamics of anisotropic plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the mean energy loss of a high-energy parton due to single gluon emission in an anisotropic plasma is generally smaller than in an isotropic plasma with the same average jet quenching parameter, but the relative difference is small (less than about 6% even for maximal anisotropy). The medium length dependence of this mean energy loss exhibits characteristics of limiting attractors when combined with jet quenching parameters extracted from QCD kinetic theory simulations.

## Method

The authors work in the harmonic approximation for gluon emission, generalizing the isotropic formalism to an anisotropic plasma by promoting the jet quenching parameter to a tensor with diagonal components $\hat q_x \neq \hat q_y$. They evaluate the resulting double integrals numerically for a static anisotropic brick of QGP, using extrapolation techniques for the integration boundaries, and fit the numerical results to obtain a pocket formula for the anisotropy dependence. They then combine this formula with values of the jet quenching parameter extracted from QCD kinetic theory simulations of the early-stage plasma evolution.

## Key numbers

- The relative energy loss difference $\Delta E/E_{\mathrm{iso}}$ is less than 0.2% in magnitude for anisotropy $\Delta\hat{q}/\hat{q} < 0.2$
- For maximum anisotropy $\Delta\hat{q}/\hat{q} = 1$, the relative deviation is approximately -6%
- The fitted coefficients for the pocket formula $E_{\mathrm{aniso}} \approx \chi\alpha_s C_R \omega_c [1 + a(\Delta\hat{q}/\hat{q})^2 + b(\Delta\hat{q}/\hat{q})^4]$ are $a = -0.0237$ and $b = -0.0376$, with coefficient of determination $R^2 = 0.9973$
- For parameters $\Delta\hat{q}/\hat{q} \simeq 0.75$ and $L \simeq 8$ fm, the absolute change in mean energy loss is $\Delta E \simeq -9$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of jet energy loss in isotropic media (Arnold 2008) to anisotropic plasmas, building on recent studies of anisotropic jet quenching parameters extracted from QCD kinetic theory (Boguslavski 2023, Boguslavski 2024). The results connect jet energy loss to the concept of limiting attractors previously introduced for the early-time dynamics of anisotropic plasmas (Boguslavski 2023). The pocket formula enables phenomenological studies of jet quenching during the pre-hydrodynamic stages of heavy-ion collisions, where the plasma is anisotropic and the jet quenching parameter is large compared to later hydrodynamic values.
