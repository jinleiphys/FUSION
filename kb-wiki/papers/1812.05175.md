# Suppression of light nuclei production in collisions of small systems at the Large Hadron Collider

> We show that the recently observed suppression of the yield ratio of deuteron to proton and of helium-3 to proton in p+p collisions compared to those in p+Pb or Pb+Pb collisions by the ALICE Collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) can be explained if light nuclei are produced from the coalescence of nucleons at the kinetic freeze-out of these collisions. This suppression is attributed to the non-negligible sizes of deuteron and helium-3 compared to the size of the nucleon emission source in collisions of small systems, which reduces the overlap of their internal wave functions with those of nucleons. The same model is also used to study the production of triton and hypertriton in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. Compared to helium-3 in events of low charged particle multiplicity, the triton is less suppressed due to its smaller size and the hypertriton is even more suppressed as a result of its much larger size.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of deuteron-to-proton and helium-3-to-proton yield ratios in p+p collisions compared to p+Pb or Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can be explained by a coalescence model that accounts for the finite sizes of these light nuclei relative to the nucleon emission source size, provided the nucleon source radius is determined from the assumption that entropy per baryon is independent of colliding system.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model in full phase space where light nuclei are formed at kinetic freeze-out from a thermalized expanding spherical fireball of nucleons. The formation probability is given by the overlap of the nucleon phase-space distribution functions (taken as Gaussians in coordinate and momentum space) with the Wigner function of the light nucleus, obtained from the Wigner transform of its internal harmonic oscillator wave function. The nucleon emission source radius was determined from the measured proton number and kinetic freeze-out temperature (extracted from a blast-wave fit to particle spectra) under the assumption that the entropy per baryon is independent of colliding system.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron root-mean-square matter radius is r_d = 1.96 fm, giving the suppression factor parameter sigma = sqrt(8/3) * r_d ≈ 3.2 fm.
- The d/p ratio in the limit of large source size (C_1) is about 4.0 × 10^{-3}, determined from central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The helium-3 matter radius is r_He3 = 1.76 fm; the triton matter radius is r_H3 = 1.59 fm.
- The d/p ratio in p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV is suppressed by more than a factor of 2 compared to central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.
- The predicted ^3H/^3He ratio in p+p collisions at dN_ch/deta = 5 is 1.1 from two-body coalescence and 1.3 from three-body coalescence (10%-30% enhancement of triton over helium-3).
- The hypertriton root-mean-square radius is r_LambdaH3 ≈ 4.9 fm; the distance between deuteron and Lambda in hypertriton is r_Lambdad ≈ 10 fm.
- For dN_ch/deta ~ 10, the ^3_LambdaH/Lambda yield ratio is predicted to be two orders of magnitude smaller than in central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach of Sun, Ko, and collaborators (2017, 2018) to explain the ALICE Collaboration measurements of light nuclei suppression in small collision systems. It contrasts with the statistical hadronization model, which requires either a canonical correlation volume spanning three units of rapidity or a higher chemical freeze-out temperature of 170 MeV to reproduce the data. The results are consistent with the schematic coalescence model of Sombun et al. (2018) using UrQMD nucleons. The study enables future tests of triton production in p+p collisions and measurements of the strangeness population factor S_3 in low-multiplicity collisions to probe the internal structure of the hypertriton.
