# Detection of Atmospheric Neutrinos and Relativistic Nuclear Structure Effects

> Neutrino-nucleus cross sections for the detection of atmospheric neutrinos are calculated in relativistic impulse and random phase approximations. Pion production is estimated via inclusive Delta-hole nuclear excitations. The number of pion events can confuse the identification of the neutrino flavor. As a further source of pions we calculate coherent pion production (where the nucleus remains in the ground state). We examine how these nuclear structure effects influence atmospheric neutrino experiments and study possible improvements in the present detector simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear structure effects, specifically relativistic mean-field reductions and RPA correlations, can significantly alter the predicted number of pion events in atmospheric neutrino detectors, potentially affecting the measured neutrino flavor ratio; however, the quantitative impact on the atmospheric neutrino anomaly remains uncertain because the in-medium delta decay width is poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors calculated inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections for oxygen-16 using a relativistic impulse approximation with particle-hole and delta-hole nuclear excitations, including RPA correlations mediated by pi and rho mesons with a Landau-Migdal parameter. They also computed coherent pion production cross sections via delta-hole excitations in a local density approximation, using a relativistic mean-field model (Walecka model) to account for medium modifications of nucleon and delta masses. The cross sections were folded with a simple atmospheric neutrino flux model to estimate event yields per kiloton-year of detector exposure.

## Key numbers

- The effective masses in the mean-field approximation for Fermi momentum k_F = 225 MeV are M*_Delta = 931 MeV and M*_N = 638 MeV, with vector self-energy V = 239 MeV.
- Mean fields reduce the particle-hole and delta-hole responses by 30% at the peaks; RPA effects reduce the particle-hole peak by 50% at high muon energies.
- At low muon energies, mean fields in impulse approximation reduce total particle-hole events from 33 to 28, while RPA yields 22 events (in a 100 MeV bin after one kiloton-year exposure).
- The delta-hole contribution is about 40% of the RPA calculation at its minimum and reaches a ratio of 1.06 at E_k' = 2 GeV.
- Delta-hole response is reduced by 10% to 25% due to mean fields.
- At E = 300 MeV, coherent neutral pion events (neglecting decay width and mean fields) are comparable in size to electron events calculated in RPA; including mean fields and delta decay width reduces them to only 10% of RPA electron events.
- For coherent charged pions at E = 1 GeV, the full calculation amounts to only 23% of quasielastic electron events; at E = 2 GeV, it increases to 30%.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic nuclear structure calculations of Kim, Piekarewicz, and Horowitz (1995) to atmospheric neutrino detection, directly addressing the atmospheric neutrino anomaly observed by IMB (Becker-Szendy et al., 1992) and Kamiokande (Hirata et al., 1992). The authors compare their results to the nonrelativistic model of Fogli and Nardulli (1979) used in the Kamiokande Monte Carlo simulation (Nakahata et al., 1986), finding that mean-field effects are not included in existing simulations and could provide significant systematic errors. The findings enable improved detector simulations that properly treat pion events, which is necessary for interpreting the multi-GeV Kamiokande data and for distinguishing conventional nuclear physics uncertainties from neutrino oscillation signals.
