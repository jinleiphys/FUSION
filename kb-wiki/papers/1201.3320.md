# Azimuthal anisotropies for Au+Au collisions in the parton-hadron transient energy range

> The azimuthal anisotropies of the collective transverse flow of charged hadrons are investigated in a wide range of heavy-ion collision energies within the microscopic parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport approach which incorporates explicit partonic degrees of freedom in terms of strongly interacting quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) in line with an equation of state from lattice QCD as well as the dynamical hadronization and hadronic collision dynamics in the final reaction phase. The experimentally observed increase of the elliptic flow $v_2$ of charged hadrons with collision energy is successfully described in terms of the PHSD approach. The PHSD scaling properties of various collective observables are confronted with experimental data as well as with hydrodynamic predictions. The analysis of higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$ in the azimuthal angular distribution shows a similar tendency of growing deviations between partonic and purely hadronic models with increasing collision energy. This demonstrates that the excitation functions of azimuthal anisotropies reflect the increasing role of quark-gluon degrees of freedom in the early phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Furthermore, the specific variation of the ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ with respect to bombarding energy, centrality and transverse momentum is found to provide valuable information on the underlying dynamics.

# Azimuthal anisotropies for Au+Au collisions in the parton-hadron transient energy range

## Key claim

The experimentally observed increase of elliptic flow $v_2$ with collision energy is successfully described by the PHSD transport approach, but only because PHSD incorporates explicit partonic degrees of freedom with repulsive mean fields and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state; purely hadronic models (HSD, UrQMD) and parton-cascade models without mean fields (AMPT, AMPT-SM) fail to reproduce this energy dependence.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model, which propagates dynamical quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) with spectral functions, temperature-dependent masses and widths fitted to lattice QCD results from Aoki et al. (2010), and includes repulsive scalar and vector mean-field potentials for partons. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is handled by covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion and three-quark coalescence, while the hadronic phase uses the same string-decay and resonance dynamics as the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) model. Calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ to 200 GeV, and results were compared to the purely hadronic HSD model, the UrQMD hadronic transport model, and the AMPT and AMPT-SM parton-cascade models.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature in the PHSD model is $T_c \approx 160$ MeV, corresponding to a critical energy density of $\varepsilon_c \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the difference between STAR and PHENIX $v_2$ results is less than 2%-5% below $p_T \simeq 2.5$ GeV/c; at $p_T \simeq 5.5$ GeV/c the STAR $v_2$ is systematically larger by 20%.
- The triangular flow $v_3$ from the updated AMPT model ($v_3 \approx 0.2$) is consistent with the PHSD result at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, while the old AMPT gave $v_3 \approx 0.4$.
- The PHSD triangular flow at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV is similar in magnitude to that from a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model with specific viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$.
- The ratio $v_4/(v_2)^2$ from PHSD is practically constant across the whole energy range studied, but deviates significantly from the ideal-fluid estimate of 0.5.
- The flow coefficients $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ reach their asymptotic values by 6-8 fm/c after the beginning of the collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV for impact parameter $b = 8$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Konchakovski et al., 2012, "v2short") by analyzing higher-order harmonics $v_3$ and $v_4$ and their scaling properties across the full Beam Energy Scan range from AGS to top RHIC energies. It contradicts the earlier failure of traditional hadronic models (UrQMD, HSD) and parton-cascade models without mean fields (AMPT, AMPT-SM) to reproduce the rise of $v_2$ with collision energy, as documented in the data compilation of Nasim et al. (2010). The results enable systematic extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ from the excitation functions of $v_3$ and $v_4$, and provide a baseline for interpreting the upcoming RHIC Beam Energy Scan data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations.
