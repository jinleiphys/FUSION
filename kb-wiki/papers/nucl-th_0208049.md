# Low Energy Pion--Nucleon Scattering in the Heavy Baryon and Infrared Schemes

> We study pion--nucleon scattering with a chiral lagrangian of pions, nucleons, and $Δ$-isobars to order $Q^3$, where $Q$ is a generic small momentum. We compare the results from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with those from the infrared regularization scheme. While the former provides a reasonable fit to the data out to a pion c.m. kinetic energy of 100 MeV, the latter is only able to fit up to 40 MeV and even then the parameters obtained are unreasonable. Difficulties with the infrared scheme in the $u$-channel are discussed.

# Low Energy Pion–Nucleon Scattering in the Heavy Baryon and Infrared Schemes

## Key claim

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB) provides a reasonable fit to pion–nucleon scattering phase shift data out to a pion center-of-mass kinetic energy of 100 MeV, while the infrared regularization (IR) scheme can only fit data up to 40 MeV and yields unreasonable parameter values, due to difficulties with the IR scheme in the u-channel.

## Method

The authors computed pion–nucleon scattering amplitudes to order Q^3 using a chiral Lagrangian that includes pions, nucleons, and Delta-isobar fields, evaluating the diagrams in both the heavy baryon (HB) and infrared regularization (IR) schemes. They fitted the calculated S- and P-wave phase shifts to the 2001 VPI/GW group data using both an S-matrix method (summing self-energy insertions to all orders) and a K-matrix method (using the free Delta propagator), with ten low-energy constants plus the pi-Delta-Delta coupling as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- HB scheme fits data out to pion c.m. kinetic energy of 100 MeV (slightly below the Delta resonance at 127 MeV); IR scheme fits only up to 40 MeV
- Fixed input parameters: nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, Delta mass M_Delta = 1232 MeV, pion mass m = 139 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, axial coupling g_A = 1.26, pi-N-Delta coupling h_A = 1.46 (from Delta width Gamma_Delta = 120 +/- 5 MeV)
- Unconstrained S-matrix HB fit yields sigma term sigma(0) = 102.1 MeV; constrained fits give 75.0 MeV and 45.0 MeV
- Goldberger-Treiman ratio zeta_piNN from unconstrained S-matrix fit: 1.088; from constrained fits: 1.056 (sigma=75 MeV) and 1.021 (sigma=45 MeV)
- S-wave scattering length b_0 from unconstrained S-matrix fit: 0.0051 m^{-1}; from constrained fits: -0.0061 m^{-1} (sigma=75 MeV) and -0.0180 m^{-1} (sigma=45 MeV)
- P-wave scattering volume a_33 from unconstrained S-matrix fit: 0.2180 m^{-3}; from constrained fits: 0.1984 m^{-3} (sigma=75 MeV) and 0.1787 m^{-3} (sigma=45 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the HB chiral perturbation theory calculation of Ellis and Torikoshi (2000) (referred to as I) to include a comparison with the IR regularization scheme proposed by Becher and Leutwyler, which had previously been applied in the sub-threshold region by Becher and Leutwyler (2001) but found insufficiently accurate for extrapolation. The HB results are compared with earlier HB studies by Fettes and Meissner (2000, 2001) both with and without explicit Delta fields; the present calculation includes polynomial terms and renormalization scale dependence that were handled differently in prior work. The IR scheme's failure in the physical region suggests that the HB approach remains the preferred method for describing pion–nucleon scattering up to the Delta resonance region, while the constrained sigma term values (45-75 MeV) align with recent determinations from SchrÃ¶der et al. and the George Washington University/TRIUMF group.
