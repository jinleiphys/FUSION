# Hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon g-2: a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach

> We determine the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon using the framework of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations of QCD. Our result for the pseudoscalar ($π^0, η, η'$) meson exchange diagram is commensurate with previous calculations. In our calculation of the quark loop contribution we improve previous approaches by implementing constraints due to gauge invariance. As a consequence, our value $a_μ^{\textrm{LBL;quarkloop}} = (136 \pm 59)\times 10^{-11}$ is significantly larger. Taken at face value, this then leads to a revised estimate of the total $a_μ=116\,591\,891.0(105.0)\times 10^{-11}$.

# Hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon g-2: a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach

## Key claim

The hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is calculated using Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, yielding a significantly larger quark-loop contribution than previous approaches when constraints from gauge invariance are properly implemented; however, transverse contributions (presumably dominated by vector meson poles) are only estimated at this stage, and the pseudoscalar meson exchange result is commensurate with earlier calculations.

## Method

The authors separate the hadronic light-by-light four-point function into a dressed quark-loop diagram and pseudoscalar meson exchange contributions (pi0, eta, eta'), using the framework of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations within a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD. The quark propagators are obtained from the Maris-Tandy model, and the quark-photon vertices are treated at three levels of approximation: bare, 1BC, and full Ball-Chiu vertex, with gauge invariance enforced via a derivative technique that renders the loop integrals manifestly convergent. The pseudoscalar exchange contributions are calculated using on-shell Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the meson-photon-photon form factors, extrapolated off-shell via a commonly used ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Quark-loop contribution: a_mu^{LBL;quarkloop} = (136 +/- 59) x 10^{-11}
- Total revised estimate: a_mu = 116,591,891.0 (105.0) x 10^{-11}
- Experimental value quoted: 116,592,089.0 (63.0) x 10^{-11}
- Theoretical value quoted (prior): 116,591,790.0 (64.6) x 10^{-11}
- Hadronic vacuum polarization contribution: [6,903.0 (52.6) - 100.3 (1.1)] x 10^{-11}
- Maris-Tandy model parameters: omega D = (0.72 GeV)^3, Lambda_QCD = 0.234 GeV, m_t = 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the application of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, previously used for hadron physics and QCD properties, to the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2. It contrasts with earlier effective field theory approaches such as those by Knecht (2001), Melnikov (2003), Nyffeler (2009), and Prades (2009), which used vector meson dominance or chiral models and obtained smaller quark-loop contributions. The approach enables a first-principles calculation with systematically improvable errors, complementing lattice QCD methods which have not yet produced reliable estimates for this quantity due to its multi-scale nature.
