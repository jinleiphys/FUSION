# Finite size of hadrons and Bose-Einstein correlations in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV

> Space-time correlations between produced particles, induced by the composite nature of hadrons, imply specific changes in the properties of the correlation functions for identical particles. The expected magnitude of these effects is evaluated using the recently published blast-wave model analysis of the data for $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV.

# Finite size of hadrons and Bose-Einstein correlations in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV

## Key claim
The composite nature of hadrons forces a minimum separation between produced particles, which introduces space-time correlations that cause the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation function to fall below unity at large relative momentum. Using a blast-wave model fit to ALICE data, the authors show that this effect is large enough to be observable in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV, particularly in the long direction and at high transverse momentum in the side and out directions.

## Method
The authors modify the standard two-particle source function by multiplying the product of single-particle Wigner functions by a cut-off factor $D(x_1-x_2)$ that suppresses pairs with small spatial separation, using a Gaussian form $D = \exp(-d^2/\Delta^2)$ with $\Delta = 1$ fm. They evaluate the resulting Bose-Einstein correlation function using the single-particle source from a blast-wave model with parameters previously fitted to ALICE HBT radii data (Bialas et al., 2014), including a shell-like transverse profile $f(r) \sim \exp[-(r-R)^2/\delta^2]$, and compute the correlation function for all three HBT directions (long, side, out) as a function of pair transverse momentum and event multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- The cut-off distance $\Delta$ is taken as 1 fm, within the range from earlier excluded-volume analyses.
- The depth of the minimum below unity in the long direction varies from approximately 0.02 to 0.01 as the HBT radius $R_{\rm long}$ increases from about 0.8 fm to 2 fm.
- In the side direction, the correlation function falls below unity only for pair transverse momentum $k_\perp \geq 300$ MeV; in the out direction, only for $k_\perp \geq 400$ MeV.
- At high $k_\perp$, the minimum below unity in the out direction is about twice as deep as in the long and side directions.
- The intercept parameter $\lambda$ used in the calculation is approximated as $\lambda = 0.59 - 0.26\, k_\perp$ (with $k_\perp$ in GeV), based on ALICE data.
- For heavy ion collisions, the correction scales as $(\Delta/R)^2$, giving a factor of about 1/30 relative to the $pp$ results.

## Context
This work extends the earlier proposal by Bialas and Zalewski (2014) that space-time correlations from finite hadron size can explain why the two-pion correlation function falls below unity, as observed by L3, LEP2, and CMS. The present paper uses the blast-wave model analysis of ALICE HBT radii data (Bialas et al., 2014) to make quantitative predictions for the effect in all three HBT directions. The results enable experimental tests of the excluded-volume mechanism in $pp$ collisions and suggest that the effect should be negligible in heavy-ion collisions due to the larger source size, but may be observable in smaller systems such as $p$-$Pb$ collisions.
