# Centrality dependence of forward-backward multiplicity correlation in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV

> We have studied the centrality dependence of charged particle forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV with a parton and hadron cascade model, PACIAE, based on PYTHIA. The calculated results are compared with the STAR data. The experimentally observed correlation strength characters: (1) the approximately flat pseudorapidity dependence in central collisions and (2) the monotonous decrease with decreasing centrality are well reproduced. However the theoretical results are bigger than the STAR data for the peripheral collisions. A discussion is given for the comparison among the different models and STAR data.

## Key claim

The parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE reproduces the STAR-observed features of forward-backward multiplicity correlation in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV for central and mid-central events, but systematically overestimates the correlation strength for peripheral (30-50% centrality) collisions and fails to reproduce the exponential dependence on pseudorapidity gap observed by STAR in the 40-50% centrality bin.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE model (a parton and hadron cascade based on PYTHIA) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)=200 GeV. The forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength \(b\) was calculated as the covariance of forward and backward charged-particle multiplicities divided by the forward multiplicity variance, for centrality bins defined either by impact parameter or by charged multiplicity in \(|\eta|<1\). Results were compared with STAR data and with predictions from the wounded nucleon model, the Glauber Monte Carlo code with a "toy" wounded-nucleon model, and the Hadron-String Dynamics transport approach.

## Key numbers

- Total charged particle multiplicity in 0-6% most central Au+Au collisions: PACIAE gives 5100 (total), compared to PHOBOS data of 5060 ± 250.
- In 0-10% most central collisions, the PACIAE model reproduces the STAR data for the correlation strength \(b\) as a function of \(\Delta\eta\).
- For 40-50% central collisions, all model results (PACIAE, wounded nucleon, GMC, HSD) are higher than the STAR data and cannot reproduce the exponential function of \(\Delta\eta\) observed by STAR.
- Prediction: in 0-10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)=5500 GeV, the charged particle multiplicity per unit pseudorapidity at mid-rapidity is around 1200, compared to about 600 in Au+Au at 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of forward-backward multiplicity correlations using the PACIAE model (Yan et al., 2009) from central collisions to a full centrality scan, and compares with STAR data (STAR Collaboration, 2009). It also compares the PACIAE results with the wounded nucleon model (Bzdak, 2009), the Glauber Monte Carlo with a "toy" wounded-nucleon model, and the HSD transport approach (Konchakovski et al., 2009). The paper provides a prediction for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, suggesting that the correlation strength will be smaller than at RHIC, analogous to the observed decrease in elliptic flow from RHIC to LHC. The failure of all models to reproduce the exponential \(\Delta\eta\) dependence in peripheral collisions indicates a need for further theoretical development.
