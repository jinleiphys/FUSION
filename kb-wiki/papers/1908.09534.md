# Impact of the Nuclear Equation of State on the Stability of Hybrid Neutron Stars

> We construct a set of equations of state (EoS) of dense and hot matter with a 1st order phase transition from a hadronic system to a deconfined quark matter state. In this two-phase approach, hadrons are described using the relativistic mean field theory with different parametrisations and the deconfined quark phase is modeled using vBag, a bag-type model extended to include vector interactions as well as a simultaneous onset of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement. This feature results in a non-trivial connection between the hadron and quark EoS, modifying the quark phase beyond its onset density. We find that this unique property has an impact on the predicted hybrid (quark core) neutron star mass--radius relations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the vBag quark model's simultaneous onset of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement creates a non-trivial connection between the hadronic and quark equations of state, which modifies the quark phase beyond its onset density and consequently alters the predicted mass-radius relations of hybrid neutron stars. The authors hedge that this effect depends on the specific relativistic mean-field parametrisation chosen for the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors constructed a set of equations of state for dense, hot matter incorporating a first-order phase transition from hadronic matter to deconfined quark matter. The hadronic phase was described using relativistic mean-field theory with multiple parametrisations, while the quark phase was modeled with the vBag approach, a bag-type model that includes vector interactions and couples chiral symmetry restoration to deconfinement. The resulting hybrid equations of state were then used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid neutron stars that use a two-phase approach with a first-order phase transition, specifically building on the vBag quark model developed by Klaehn and Fischer (2015). The paper contrasts with earlier bag-model treatments that do not couple chiral symmetry restoration to deconfinement, showing that this coupling has a significant impact on the predicted mass-radius relations. The results enable more realistic comparisons between theoretical hybrid star models and observational constraints from neutron star mass and radius measurements.
