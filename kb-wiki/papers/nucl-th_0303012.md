# Nonlinear Regge trajectories and glueballs

> We apply a phenomenological approach based on nonlinear Regge trajectories to glueball states. The parameters, i.e., intercept and threshold, or trajectory termination point beyond which no bound states should exist, are determined from pomeron (scattering) data. Systematic errors inherent to the approach are discussed. We then predict masses of glueballs on the tensor trajectory. For comparison, the approach is applied to available quenched lattice data. We find a discrepancy between the lattice based thresholds and the pomeron threshold that we extract from data.

# Nonlinear Regge trajectories and glueballs

## Key claim

The authors apply a nonlinear (square-root) Regge trajectory formalism, previously successful for ordinary quark-antiquark mesons, to glueball states, finding that the pomeron trajectory threshold extracted from scattering data is larger than thresholds inferred from quenched lattice QCD glueball masses, though the discrepancy may be within uncertainties due to systematic errors and the difference between quenched and unquenched QCD.

## Method

The authors fit the square-root form of the DAMA (dual amplitude with Mandelstam analyticity) Regge trajectory, with the universal asymptotic slope fixed to the value obtained from the rho meson trajectory (3.65 GeV^-1), to pomeron scattering data from gamma p -> phi p at ZEUS energies. They then add the lattice QCD mass of the lowest-lying tensor glueball (2^++) as an additional data point to reduce uncertainties, and repeat the fit with the logarithmic limiting form of DAMA trajectories to estimate systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- Universal asymptotic slope: gamma_{1/2} = 3.65 +/- 0.05 GeV^-1 (determined from the rho trajectory)
- Square-root fit to pomeron scattering data alone: intercept alpha(0) = 1.08 +/- 0.01, sqrt(T) = 11.56 +/- 2.08 GeV, chi^2/d.o.f. = 6.07/9
- Square-root fit including tensor glueball mass: intercept alpha(0) = 1.081 +/- 0.007, sqrt(T) = 11.57 +/- 1.1 GeV, chi^2/d.o.f. = 6.07/10
- Logarithmic fit including tensor glueball mass: intercept alpha(0) = 1.079 +/- 0.007, sqrt(T_log) = 7.23 +/- 0.34 GeV, chi^2/d.o.f. = 6.12/10
- Predicted glueball masses from square-root fit: M(2^++) = 2.38 +/- 0.12 GeV, M(4^++) = 4.21 +/- 0.21 GeV, M(6^++) = 5.41 +/- 0.28 GeV
- Combined systematic estimate for pomeron threshold: (9.4 +/- 1.6) GeV
- Lattice QCD input mass for tensor glueball: M(2^++) = 2.40 +/- 0.13 GeV (from Colin et al.)
- Lattice-based thresholds for other glueball trajectories: sqrt(T) = 3.90 +/- 0.91 GeV (0^-+ and 2^-+ trajectory), sqrt(T) = 4.87 +/- 1.25 GeV (1^+- and 3^+- trajectory)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonlinear Regge trajectory analysis of ordinary quark-antiquark mesons (Brisudova et al., 2003) to glueball states, using the same square-root functional form motivated by color screening of the flux tube. The pomeron threshold they extract (approximately 9-12 GeV) is significantly larger than thresholds obtained from quenched lattice QCD glueball spectra (Colin et al.), raising questions about whether this discrepancy reflects the quenched approximation or the choice of trajectory functional form. The results enable predictions for excited tensor glueball masses that could be tested by future lattice calculations or experimental searches, and suggest that glueball trajectories may support much higher angular momentum states (possibly up to J=36) than ordinary mesons, potentially producing narrow high-spin glueballs amenable to experimental identification.
