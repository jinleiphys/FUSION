# Parton physics of the large-$N_c$ mesons

> We initiate the studies on the structural physics of the tower of stable large-$N_c$ mesons through a first computation of the collinear quark-structure of a large-$N_c$ pion using lattice Monte-Carlo methods. We adapt the large-$N_c$ continuum reduction for the determination of meson correlation functions involving the spatially-extended quasi-PDF operators as a perfect strategy to concentrate only on the short perturbative length scales. We find the internal structures of pion in the large-$N_c$ and $N_c=3$ theories to be quite similar. Interestingly, we find hints that even the observed differences could arise to a large extent via the different perturbative QCD evolution in the two theories from similar initial conditions at low factorization scales.

## Key claim

The internal quark structure of the pion in large-\(N_c\) QCD is quite similar to that in \(N_c=3\) QCD, and the observed differences may arise largely from different perturbative QCD evolution in the two theories from similar initial conditions at low factorization scales, rather than from fundamentally different nonperturbative structure.

## Method

The authors used large-\(N_c\) continuum reduction on an \(8^4\) lattice with \(N_c=17\) and coupling \(b=0.355\) to compute the quasi-PDF matrix elements of the pion via 2-point and 3-point correlation functions. They extracted the pseudo-Ioffe-time distribution (pseudo-ITD) from the ratio of quasi-PDF matrix elements at nonzero and zero momentum, then performed leading-twist operator product expansion (OPE) fits to obtain Mellin moments and a phenomenological valence PDF fit.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing in units of string tension: \(\sqrt{\sigma}a = 0.254(2)\)
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.86\sqrt{\sigma}\)
- First few Mellin moments at scale \(\mu = 4.55\sqrt{\sigma}\): \(\langle x\rangle_{u+\bar u} = 0.25(1)\), \(\langle x^3\rangle_{u+\bar u} = 0.10(2)\) (from Im \(\mathcal{M}\)); \(\langle x^2\rangle_{u-\bar u} = 0.13(2)\), \(\langle x^4\rangle_{u-\bar u} = 0.10(2)\) (from Re \(\mathcal{M}\))
- Valence PDF large-\(x\) exponent from Ansatz fit: \(\beta = 0.7(3)\)
- Valence momentum fraction from PDF Ansatz fit: \(\langle x\rangle_{u-\bar u} = 0.23(2)\)
- Chosen GeV-scale conversion: \(\sqrt{\sigma}=0.44\) GeV, giving \([a^{-1}, \mu, m_\pi] = [1.73, 2.00, 0.38]\) GeV

## Context

This work initiates the study of parton structure in large-\(N_c\) mesons, extending prior lattice QCD studies of pion structure in \(SU(3)\) theory (e.g., Sufian (2019), Gao (2020), Izubuchi (2019)) and the large-\(N_c\) Regge model of Ruiz Arriola (2006). It enables future comparative studies of parton distributions across different stable meson species in large-\(N_c\) QCD, and provides a framework where the sea quark contribution from \(g\to q\bar q\) splitting is absent, helping to isolate the role of sea quarks in real-world QCD.
