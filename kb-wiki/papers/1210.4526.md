# The influence of strong magnetic fields on proto-quark stars

> We analyze different stages of magnetized quark star evolution incorporating baryon number conservation and using an anisotropic energy momentum tensor. The first stages of the evolution are simulated through the inclusion of trapped neutrinos and fixed entropy per particle, while in the last stage the star is taken to be deleptonized and cold. We find that, although strong magnetic fields modify quark star masses, the evolution of isolated stars needs to be constrained by fixed baryon number, which necessarily lowers the possible star masses. Moreover, magnetic field effects, measured by the difference between the parallel and perpendicular pressures, are more pronounced in the beginning of the star evolution, when there is a larger number of charged leptons and up quarks. We also show that having a spatially varying magnetic field allows for larger magnetic fields to be supported.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields modify quark star masses, but for isolated stars the requirement of fixed baryon number necessarily lowers the possible star masses, contrary to the trend seen when baryon number is allowed to vary. Magnetic field effects, measured by the difference between parallel and perpendicular pressures, are more pronounced early in the star's evolution when there are more charged leptons and up quarks.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model with a bag constant of (154 MeV)^4 to describe quark matter, incorporating Landau quantization for charged particles in a magnetic field parallel to the z-direction. They computed the equation of state for three evolutionary snapshots: (i) warm with trapped neutrinos (entropy per baryon s/ρ_B=1, lepton fraction Y_l=0.4), (ii) warm without neutrinos (s/ρ_B=2, μ_ν=0), and (iii) cold deleptonized (s/ρ_B=0, μ_ν=0). The anisotropic energy-momentum tensor was used to obtain parallel and perpendicular pressures, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved using the transverse pressure, both with and without enforcing baryon number conservation.

- Bag constant: B = (154 MeV)^4
- Binding energy per baryon without magnetic field: B/A = 925.9 MeV; with B=4.3×10^18 G: B/A = 914.30 MeV
- Maximum central magnetic field before parallel pressure becomes negative: B_c^max = 4.39×10^18 G
- For constant magnetic field at μ_B=1500 MeV, parallel pressure becomes negative at B=3.17×10^18 G (stage iii) and B=3.37×10^18 G (stages i and ii)
- For constant magnetic field at μ_B=1200 MeV, parallel pressure becomes negative at B=1.77×10^18 G (stage iii)
- Maximum masses without baryon number conservation: B=0 case: 1.66 M_⊙ (i), 1.65 M_⊙ (ii), 1.65 M_⊙ (iii); B_c=4.3×10^18 G: 1.84 M_⊙ (i), 1.85 M_⊙ (ii), 1.88 M_⊙ (iii)
- Maximum masses with fixed baryon number: B=0 case: 1.66 M_⊙ (i), 1.58 M_⊙ (ii), 1.55 M_⊙ (iii); B_c=4.3×10^18 G: 1.84 M_⊙ (i), 1.73 M_⊙ (ii), 1.70 M_⊙ (iii)
- Surface magnetic field used in variable field prescription: B_surf = 10^15 G
- Lepton fraction assumed: Y_l = 0.4

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proto-quark and proto-hybrid stars (Steiner 2001, Pons 2001, Gupta 2002, Berdermann 2005, Shen 2005, Nicotra 2006, Burgio 2007, Yasutake 2009, Bombaci 2011, Lugones 2011, Shao 2011, Chen 2012) by incorporating for the first time the combined effects of strong magnetic fields, finite temperature, trapped neutrinos, and baryon number conservation on proto-quark stars. It also builds on earlier work on magnetized proto-neutron stars (Pal 1998, Bednarek 2002) and zero-temperature magnetized quark stars (Chakrabarty 1996, Isayev 2012). The finding that isolated star maximum masses decrease with time when baryon number is fixed agrees with similar hadronic calculations (Dexheimer 2008). The results enable future real-time simulations of proto-quark star evolution that include magnetic field effects on the equation of state, which have not been performed previously.
