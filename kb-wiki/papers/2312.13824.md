# Deuteron production in a combined thermal and coalescence framework for heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime

> A recently formulated thermal model for hadron production in heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime is combined with the idea that some part of protons and neutrons present in the original thermal system forms deuterons via the coalescence mechanism. Using realistic parametrizations of the freeze-out conditions, which reproduce well the spectra of protons and pions, we make predictions for deuteron transverse-momentum and rapidity spectra. The best agreement with the experimentally known deuteron yield is obtained if the freeze-out temperature is relatively high and, accordingly, the system size at freeze-out is rather small. In addition, the standard Gaussian distribution of the relative distance between nucleons is replaced by the distribution resulting from their independent and approximately uniform production inside the initial thermal system.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that deuteron production in few-GeV heavy-ion collisions can be described by combining a thermal freeze-out model with a coalescence mechanism, but only if the freeze-out temperature is relatively high (70.3 MeV) and the system size is correspondingly small (about 6 fm), contrary to the low-temperature scenario (49.6 MeV) that also fits proton and pion spectra.

## Method
The authors take previously developed thermal freeze-out models (spherical and spheroidal expansion) that reproduce proton and pion spectra from HADES Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV, and apply the standard coalescence formula dN_d/d^3p_d = A_FR * F_p(p_d/2) * F_n(p_d/2) to predict deuteron spectra. The formation rate coefficient A_FR is computed using three combinations of pair-distance distributions (Gaussian or sharp-cutoff from uniform production inside a sphere) and deuteron wave functions (Gaussian or Hulthen), with the sharp-cutoff distribution replacing the standard Gaussian assumption to be consistent with the thermal model's assumption of independent particle production inside a sphere of radius R.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature T = 49.6 MeV (low-temperature scenarios A and spherical) versus T = 70.3 MeV (high-temperature scenario B), with corresponding system radii R = 15.7-16.02 fm versus R = 6.06 fm
- Deuteron formation rate A_SH (sharp-cutoff distribution with Hulthen wave function) = 69,661 MeV^3 (spherical), 73,735 MeV^3 (scenario A), and 942,476 MeV^3 (scenario B)
- Measured deuteron yield in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV is 28.7 deuterons per event
- Spherical model predictions for deuteron yield: N_d = 0.31 (Gaussian-Gaussian), 2.66 (sharp-Gaussian), and 2.88 (sharp-Hulthen), all far below the experimental value
- High-temperature scenario B yields the best agreement with the experimentally known deuteron yield

## Context
This work extends the thermal model of Harabasz (2020, 2022) for hadron production in few-GeV heavy-ion collisions by adding a coalescence mechanism for deuteron formation, contrasting with the usual treatment of thermal and coalescence approaches as exclusive alternatives (Mrowczynski, 2020). The analysis uses HADES Collaboration data for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV and considers two possible freeze-out parameter sets from Motornenko (2021). The results favor the high-temperature freeze-out scenario, which suggests that future work should examine the role of Delta resonances in the thermal approach, particularly differences in the Delta density of states (Lo, 2017).
