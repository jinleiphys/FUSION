# On Two Nucleons Near Unitarity with Perturbative Pions

> We explore the impact of perturbative pions on the Unitarity Expansion in the two-nucleon S-waves of Chiral Effective Field Theory at next-to-next-to leading order. Pion exchange explicitly breaks the nontrivial fixed point's universality and invariance under conformal and Wigner-$\SU(4)$ spin-isospin transformations. This Chiral Effective Field Theory applies tothe Unitarity Window (45deg<delta(k)<135deg,$k\approx m_π$) most relevant for low-energy nuclear systems. Its only LO scale is the scattering momentum; NLO adds only scattering length, effective range and non-iterated one-pion exchange (OPE); and N2LO only once-iterated OPE. Agreement in the 1S0 channel is very good. Apparent large discrepancies in the 3S1 channel at k>100MeV are remedied by taking only the central part of the pion's N2LO contribution. In contradistinction to the tensor part, it is invariant under Wigner-SU(4)symmetry and hence identical in 1S0 and 3S1. NLO pions are Wigner-invariant. Both channels describe PWAs and pole parameters well within mutually consistent quantitative theory uncertainty estimates. Pionic effects are small, even for k>mpi. Empirically determined breakdown scales agree well with $Λ_{NN}=\frac{16πf_π^2}{g_A^2M}=300\;$MeV where iterated OPE is not suppressed. We therefore conjecture: The footprint of both scale invariance and Wigner-symmetry in the Unitarity Expansion shows persistence,i.e. both dominate even for k=mpi and are more relevant than chiral symmetry,so that the tensor/Wigner-SU(4) symmetry-breaking part of OPE does not enter before N3LO. We also discuss the potential relevance of entanglement power and possible resolution of a conflict with the strength of the tensor interaction in the large-Nc expansion.

## Key claim

Perturbative pions in the Unitarity Expansion of Chiral Effective Field Theory at next-to-next-to leading order show that both conformal and Wigner SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry persist even for momenta around the pion mass, with the tensor/Wigner-symmetry-breaking part of one-pion exchange not entering before next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, provided one takes only the central part of the pion's next-to-next-to-leading order contribution in the 3S1 channel.

## Method

The authors applied Chiral Effective Field Theory with perturbative pions in the Unitarity Expansion to two-nucleon S-waves at next-to-leading order (adding scattering length, effective range, and non-iterated one-pion exchange) and next-to-next-to-leading order (adding once-iterated one-pion exchange). They compared the resulting phase shifts and pole parameters to empirical partial-wave analyses, treating the central and tensor parts of the pion exchange separately in the 3S1 channel. The only leading-order scale is the scattering momentum, with the expansion performed about the Unitarity fixed point where the phase shift is near 90 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Binding momentum of the deuteron in the 3S1 channel: 45 MeV, set by the inverse of the scattering length a(3S1) approximately 5 fm
- Virtual bound state momentum in the 1S0 channel: -7 MeV from a(1S0) approximately -24 fm
- Pion mass m_pi approximately 140 MeV; inverse pion mass 1/m_pi approximately 1.4 fm
- Empirical breakdown scale Lambda_NN = (16 pi f_pi^2)/(g_A^2 M) approximately 300 MeV, where iterated one-pion exchange is not suppressed
- Unitarity Window defined as phase shifts between 45 degrees and 135 degrees, relevant for momenta k approximately m_pi
- Apparent large discrepancies in the 3S1 channel appear at k greater than 100 MeV before taking only the central part of the pion's next-to-next-to-leading order contribution

## Context

This work extends the Unitarity Expansion approach to nuclear systems (Kievsky 2015, Konig 2016, van Kolck 2019) by incorporating perturbative pions from Chiral Effective Field Theory, resolving an apparent incompatibility between Unitarity and chiral symmetry. It builds on prior partial-wave analyses by the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993) and the Granada group (Navarro Perez 2013, Ruiz Arriola 2019), and enables systematic inclusion of pionic effects in the Unitarity Window while maintaining the dominance of scale invariance and Wigner SU(4) symmetry. The conjecture about persistence of these symmetries opens the path to applying the Unitarity Expansion to heavier nuclear systems and nuclear matter.
