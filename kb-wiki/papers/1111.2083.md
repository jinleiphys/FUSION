# Energy spectrum and effective mass using a non-local 3-body interaction

> We recently proposed a nonlocal form for the 3-body induced interaction that is consistent with the Fock space representation of interaction operators but leads to a fractional power dependence on the density. Here we examine the implications of the nonlocality for the excitation spectrum. In the two-component weakly interacting Fermi gas, we find that it gives an effective mass that is comparable to the one in many-body perturbation theory. Applying the interaction to nuclear matter, it predicts a large enhancement to the effective mass. Since the saturation of nuclear matter is partly due to the induced 3-body interaction, fitted functionals should treat the effective mass as a free parameter, unless the two- and three-body contributions are determined from basic theory.

# Energy spectrum and effective mass using a non-local 3-body interaction

## Key claim
The non-local three-body interaction operator, which was previously shown to reproduce the Lee-Yang energy expansion for the dilute Fermi gas, predicts an effective mass enhancement in nuclear matter that is so large that the effective mass cannot be reliably derived from theory and must instead be treated as a free parameter in fitted energy functionals, unless the two- and three-body contributions are determined from first principles.

## Method
The authors compute the single-particle energy by functionally differentiating the energy density functional (which contains the non-local three-body interaction term) with respect to the density matrix of an added particle. The three-body contribution to the single-particle energy is evaluated analytically as an integral over the product of spherical Bessel functions and the free Fermi gas density matrix. The effective mass is then obtained from the momentum derivative of the single-particle energy at the Fermi surface, and the result is compared to the quasiparticle energy from Galitskii (1958). For the nuclear matter application, the strength parameter C is treated as adjustable, and the relation between the three-body energy and the effective mass is used to evaluate the effect using phenomenological functional energies for 208Pb.

## Key numbers
- The three-body strength parameter is fixed as C = (ħ² a² / m) × [64π (11 − ln2)] / [3 (92 − 27 ln3)] for the dilute Fermi gas.
- The effective mass relation for nuclear matter is m*/m = (1 + d × (E₃/A) / (ħ² k_F² / (2m)))⁻¹, where d ≈ −1.32.
- For 208Pb, the Skyrme Ska functional gives a three-body (t₃) contribution of 6274 MeV and a total energy of −1640 MeV; the Gogny D1S functional gives a three-body contribution of 6530 MeV and a total energy of −1637 MeV.
- Using these three-body energies in the effective mass formula yields a negative effective mass for both functionals, which is unphysical.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous proposal of a non-local three-body interaction (Gezerlis 2010) that reproduces the Lee-Yang (1957) expansion for the dilute Fermi gas to order ρ⁷/³. The single-particle spectrum is compared to the quasiparticle energy from Galitskii (1958) and the textbook treatment by Fetter and Walecka (1971). The nuclear application builds on the Skyrme and Gogny energy-density functional frameworks (Bender 2003, Baldo 2010, Erler 2010) and uses the ev8 code (Bonche 2005) and HFBaxial code (Robledo) for the 208Pb energies. The results imply that fitted nuclear functionals cannot derive the effective mass from the induced three-body interaction alone, and the effective mass must remain a free parameter unless ab initio calculations become available.
