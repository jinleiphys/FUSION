# Nuclear modification and azimuthal anisotropy of D mesons produced in relativistic heavy ion collision

> In this paper we present a phenomenological treatment of charm quark energy loss before fragmenting into D mesons and calculate nuclear modification factor, '$R_{AA}$' and azimuthal anisotropy, '$v_2$' of D mesons for lead on lead collision at LHC energy of $\sqrt{s}$=2.76 A TeV.

# Nuclear modification and azimuthal anisotropy of D mesons produced in relativistic heavy ion collision

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological model combining collisional and radiative energy loss for charm quarks that can explain the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) and azimuthal anisotropy (v_2) of D mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, but only within certain transverse momentum ranges and centrality classes, with the model failing at low p_T (below 4 GeV) where non-perturbative effects become important.

## Method
The author calculates charm quark production at leading order (LO) in pQCD using CTEQ5M parton distribution functions with EKS98 shadowing and a K-factor of 2.5 for NLO approximation, then applies collisional energy loss from 2-to-2 scattering processes (gc→gc and qc→qc) and radiative energy loss with a heuristic implementation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. The total energy loss per unit path length is taken as a simple sum of collisional and radiative contributions, and the modified charm momentum is fragmented into D mesons using the Peterson fragmentation function with parameter ε_p = 0.13. The medium is modeled with Bjorken longitudinal expansion only, with initial temperature T_0 = 450 MeV and freeze-out temperature T_f = 165 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √s = 2.76 A TeV for Pb+Pb at LHC
- Nuclear overlap function values: T_AA(b) = 277 fm⁻² for 0-10% centrality, 260 fm⁻² for 0-20% centrality, and 40 fm⁻² for 30-50% centrality
- Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.3 (constant throughout calculations)
- Peterson fragmentation parameter: ε_p = 0.13
- K-factor for NLO approximation: 2.5
- Initial medium temperature: T_0 = 450 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 165 MeV
- Momentum broadening parameter: O(1 GeV²/fm) for q̂
- Transition between collisional and radiative dominance: p_T ≈ 4.0 GeV, where collisional loss dominates below and radiative loss dominates above this value
- Best agreement with data for R_AA in the range 4.0 GeV < p_T < 10.0 GeV for mid-centrality classes

## Context
This work extends phenomenological studies of heavy quark energy loss in quark-gluon plasma, building on prior approaches such as DGLV, GWP, BDMPS-Z, and ASW formalisms for radiative energy loss (Refs. rad1, rad2, rad3-rad5 in the paper), and the collisional energy loss formalism by Peshier (2006). The author notes similar simultaneous calculations of R_AA and v_2 have been attempted recently by other groups (Refs. raav21, raav22). The paper enables future work incorporating non-eikonal approximations, transverse expansion of the medium, and calculation of transport coefficients such as momentum broadening q̂, drag, and diffusion coefficients for charm quarks.
