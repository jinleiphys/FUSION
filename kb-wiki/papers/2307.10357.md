# The semileptonic decays $η^{(\prime)} \to π^0 \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $η' \to η\ell^+ \ell^-$ in the standard model

> We perform a theoretical analysis of the semileptonic decays $η^{(\prime)} \to π^0 \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $η' \to η\ell^+ \ell^-$, where $\ell = e, μ$, via a charge-conjugation-conserving two-photon mechanism. The underlying form factors are modeled using vector-meson dominance, phenomenological input, and $\mathrm{U}(3)$ flavor symmetry. We consider both a monopole and a dipole model, the latter tailored such that the expected high-energy behavior is ensured. Furthermore, we benchmark the effect of $S$-wave rescattering contributions to the decays. We infer significant effects of the form factors neglected in the literature so far, still finding branching ratios of the various decays well below the current experimental upper limits.

## Key claim

The standard model branching ratios for the semileptonic decays η(′) → π⁰ ℓ⁺ ℓ⁻ and η′ → η ℓ⁺ ℓ⁻ are predicted to be well below current experimental upper limits, with significant effects from the inclusion of realistic photon-virtuality-dependent transition form factors that have been neglected in previous calculations.

## Method

The authors construct the charge-conjugation-conserving two-photon amplitudes for these decays using vector-meson dominance (VMD) with ρ, ω, and φ meson exchange in t- and u-channel diagrams. They model the vector-to-pseudoscalar transition form factors using both a monopole parameterization (with only the lowest-lying vector mesons) and a dipole parameterization (including excited vector mesons ρ′, ω′, φ′ to ensure the expected asymptotic q⁻⁴ behavior), with normalizations fixed by phenomenological decay widths. The loop integrals are evaluated via Passarino–Veltman decomposition, and the effect of S-wave rescattering contributions is benchmarked separately.

## Key numbers

- For η → π⁰ e⁺ e⁻: branching ratio 9.9 × 10⁻⁹ (VMD model from Cheng 1967), 8.4⁺⁴·⁶₋₃·₈ × 10⁻¹⁰ (unitarity bounds, Ng 1992), 9.2(1.5) × 10⁻¹⁰ (quark-box model, Ng 1993)
- For η → π⁰ μ⁺ μ⁻: branching ratio 3.8⁺²·³₋₁·₅ × 10⁻¹⁰ (unitarity bounds, Ng 1992) and 3.3(5) × 10⁻⁹ (quark-box model, Ng 1993)
- Experimental upper limit for η → π⁰ e⁺ e⁻: < 7.5 × 10⁻⁶ at 90% confidence level (WASA-at-COSY 2018)
- Experimental upper limit for η → π⁰ μ⁺ μ⁻: < 5 × 10⁻⁶ at 90% confidence level (Dzhelyadin 1980)
- Experimental upper limit for η′ → π⁰ e⁺ e⁻: < 1.4 × 10⁻³ (CLEO 1999)
- Experimental upper limit for η′ → π⁰ μ⁺ μ⁻: < 6 × 10⁻⁵ (Dzhelyadin 1980)
- Experimental upper limit for η′ → η e⁺ e⁻: < 2.4 × 10⁻³ (CLEO 1999)
- Experimental upper limit for η′ → η μ⁺ μ⁻: < 1.5 × 10⁻⁵ (Dzhelyadin 1980)
- Vector-meson–pseudoscalar–photon coupling magnitudes: |F_ρω(0)| = 0.73(6) GeV⁻¹, |F_ωπ⁰(0)| = 2.33(4) GeV⁻¹, |F_φπ⁰(0)| = 0.1355(26) GeV⁻¹, |F_ρη(0)| = 1.58(6) GeV⁻¹, |F_ωη(0)| = 0.449(20) GeV⁻¹, |F_φη(0)| = 0.691(7) GeV⁻¹, |F_ρη′(0)| = 1.299(23) GeV⁻¹, |F_ωη′(0)| = 0.401(9) GeV⁻¹, |F_φη′(0)| = 0.712(12) GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the VMD-based calculations of Cheng (1967) and the unitarity-bound approach of Ng (1992, 1993) by incorporating realistic photon-virtuality-dependent transition form factors, which were neglected in the most recent theoretical work by Escribano (2020). The inclusion of these form factors allows for the first time a reliable assessment of S-wave rescattering contributions and provides more accurate standard model predictions that can be compared with future experimental searches by the REDTOP collaboration.
