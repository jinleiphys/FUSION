# Centrality dependence of charged-neutral particle fluctuations in 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Results on the study of localized fluctuations in the multiplicity of charged particles and photons produce 158A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions are presented for four different centrality classes. The charged versus neutral particle multiplicity correlations in common phase space regions of varying azimuthal size are analyzed by two different methods. Various types of mixed events are constructed to probe fluctuations arising from different sources. The measured results are compared to those from simulations and from mixed events. The comparison indicates the presence of non-statistical fluctuations in both the charged particle and photon multiplicities in limited azimuthal regions which decrease with decrease in centrality. However, no correlated charged-neutral fluctuations, a possible signature of formation of disoriented chiral condensates, are observed. An upper limit on the production of disoriented chiral condensates has been set.

## Key claim

The paper reports the presence of non-statistical fluctuations in charged particle and photon multiplicities in limited azimuthal regions for 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions, but finds no correlated charged-neutral fluctuations that would indicate disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation, setting an upper limit on DCC production across four centrality classes.

## Method

The WA98 Collaboration analyzed event-by-event correlations between photon-like clusters (from the preshower photon multiplicity detector, PMD) and charged particle multiplicities (from the silicon pad multiplicity detector, SPMD) in the common pseudorapidity range 2.9 < η < 3.75, dividing the full azimuth into 2, 4, 8, and 16 bins. Two analysis techniques were applied: a correlation method using a scaled distance variable S_Z from a fitted correlation axis, and a discrete wavelet transformation (DWT) analysis using the D-4 wavelet basis on the photon fraction f'(φ) at scale j_max=5. Four types of mixed events (M1, M2, M3-γ, M3-ch) were constructed from the data to isolate contributions from photon-only fluctuations, charged-particle-only fluctuations, and correlated fluctuations between them.

## Key numbers

- Centrality bins: top 5% (centrality-1), 5%-10% (centrality-2), 15%-30% (centrality-3), and 45%-55% (centrality-4) of the minimum bias cross section
- Photon counting efficiency: 68% for central events to 73% for peripheral events
- Purity of photon sample: 65% for central events to 54% for peripheral events
- Charged particle detection efficiency in SPMD: better than 99%
- Photon interaction probability in SPMD silicon: about 0.2%
- PMD transverse momentum acceptance extends down to about 30 MeV/c
- SPMD transverse momentum threshold extends down to about 20 MeV/c
- Systematic error in N_γ-like from energy calibration uncertainty: 2.5%
- Systematic error from clustering in PMD: 3% for peripheral events, 7% for central events
- Systematic error in N_ch determination: about 4%
- MIRAC energy resolution: 17.9%/√E for electromagnetic, 46.1%/√E for hadronic (E in GeV)
- 60,000 VENUS 4.12 events with full detector simulation were generated for comparison

## Context

This work extends the earlier WA98 search for localized DCC domains in central Pb+Pb collisions (WA98, 2000) to four centrality classes, motivated by theoretical suggestions to look for DCC formation in intermediate centrality events (NotsoCentral, year not specified). The analysis uses the same detectors and methods as the previous central-event study but introduces four types of mixed events to isolate fluctuation sources. The results contradict expectations for DCC formation but enable future searches with improved sensitivity, particularly at lower transverse momenta where DCC signals are expected to be stronger.
