# Nucleon form factors in a light-front framework: a model with instanton-induced forces

> Nucleon form factors are evaluated in the spacelike region in a light-front framework. Our phenomenological model is based on a relativistic formulation of the Hamiltonian dynamics. The baryon dynamics is solved in the CMS with relativistic kinetic energy, linearly rising confining potential and a residual interaction based on instanton-induced forces, the so-called 't Hooft interaction. The wavefunctions of the model are used to compute the nucleon form factors in the impulse approximation. In spite of taking no phenomenological constituent quark form factor by the one-body electromagnetic current, we obtain a very good description of all electromagnetic form factors for momentum transfers up to -3 GeV^2. The effect of the 't Hooft interaction is carefully examined in the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a light-front relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics model incorporating instanton-induced ('t Hooft) forces can describe all nucleon electromagnetic form factors up to momentum transfers of -3 GeV² without using any phenomenological constituent quark form factor, but the quality of the description varies: the proton electric form factor is described "very well" while the other form factors are only in "reasonable agreement" with experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve the eigenvalue equation for the mass operator in the light-front relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics (RHD) framework, identifying it with a reduced Salpeter equation for three quarks. The baryon dynamics includes relativistic kinetic energy, a linearly rising confining potential with a mixed scalar and timelike vector Dirac structure, and a residual 't Hooft instanton-induced interaction regularized with a Gaussian of finite range λ. The nucleon wavefunctions are expanded in a harmonic-oscillator basis up to six quanta, and the electromagnetic form factors are computed in the impulse approximation using the light-front current formalism of Capstick and Keister (1995), with the single-quark current taken as the bare charge operator without anomalous magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: up to -q² = 3 GeV² (spacelike region)
- Constituent quark masses: m_n = 220 MeV (Model 1) and m_n = 270 MeV (Model 2)
- Confinement slope: b = 688 MeV/fm (Model 1) and b = 526 MeV/fm (Model 2)
- 't Hooft coupling: g = 118 MeV fm³ (both models)
- 't Hooft range: λ = 0.333 fm (both models)
- Oscillator parameter β ≈ 0.5 fm (both models)
- Computed magnetic moments come out about 10% too small for the proton and about 15% too small for the neutron

## Context

This work extends the light-front RHD approach previously applied by Capstick and Keister (1995) and Cardarelli et al. (1995), but replaces the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) residual interaction with instanton-induced forces. The 't Hooft interaction had been successfully applied in nonrelativistic constituent quark models by Blask et al. (1990) and in a covariant Bethe-Salpeter framework for mesons by Münz et al. (1994, 1995), but this paper presents the first relativistic treatment of nucleon form factors including the 't Hooft interaction in the baryon dynamics. The configuration mixing generated by the 't Hooft interaction is shown to be essential for obtaining the correct positive sign of the neutron electric form factor G_E^n, which enables future calculations of other baryon observables such as axial-vector form factors, electroexcitation helicity amplitudes, and strange-sector form factors.
