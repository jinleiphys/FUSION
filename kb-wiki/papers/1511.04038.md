# Separable Potentials for (d,p) Reaction Calculations

> An important ingredient for applications of nuclear physics to e.g. astrophysics or nuclear energy are the cross sections for reactions of neutrons with rare isotopes. Since direct measurements are often not possible, indirect methods like $(d,p)$ reactions must be used instead. Those $(d,p)$ reactions may be viewed as effective three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. An additional challenge posed by $(d,p)$ reactions involving heavier nuclei is the treatment of the Coulomb force. To avoid numerical complications in dealing with the screening of the Coulomb force, recently a new approach using the Coulomb distorted basis in momentum space was suggested. In order to implement this suggestion, one needs to derive a separable representation of neutron- and proton-nucleus optical potentials and compute their matrix elements in this basis.

# Separable Potentials for (d,p) Reaction Calculations

## Key claim
The authors develop separable representations of neutron-nucleus and proton-nucleus optical potentials in a Coulomb-distorted basis, which are necessary inputs for Faddeev-AGS three-body calculations of (d,p) reactions that avoid the numerical complications of Coulomb screening. The approach is demonstrated for light to medium-mass nuclei (12C and 48Ca) but the authors state the expectation that it can be extended to heavier systems without limitation on target charge, though this remains to be proven in actual three-body calculations.

## Method
The authors generalize the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separable representation scheme to complex, non-Hermitian optical potentials and to charged-particle scattering in a Coulomb-distorted basis. They construct separable representations of Woods-Saxon-type phenomenological optical potentials for neutron-nucleus and proton-nucleus systems, compute the half-shell t-matrix elements in the Coulomb basis using regular radial Coulomb scattering wave functions, and extend the formalism to multi-channel coupled-channel optical potentials that include nuclear excitations. The quality of the representations is tested by comparing momentum-space calculations using the separable potentials with original coordinate-space calculations for elastic scattering observables.

## Key numbers
- Elastic scattering of protons from 12C and 48Ca calculated at Elab = 38 MeV; the unpolarized differential cross section divided by the Rutherford cross section is shown as a function of c.m. angle, with the 12C cross section scaled by a factor of 1.5 for display.
- For the multi-channel separable representation of n+12C scattering, EST support points are chosen at 6, 20, and 45 MeV laboratory kinetic energy, and the S-matrix elements are shown for J^pi = 1/2+ up to 50 MeV.
- The coupled-channel optical potential from Olsson (1989) describes n+12C scattering in the energy range 16.5 to 22 MeV, including excitations at 4.44 MeV (I^pi = 2+) and 14.08 MeV (I^pi = 4+).

## Context
This work extends the EST separable representation method (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1973) to complex optical potentials and to the Coulomb-distorted basis, building on prior separable representations for nucleon-nucleon interactions (Haidenbauer, 1982; Haidenbauer, 1986) and for light nuclei such as 6He (Ghovanlou, 1974) and 6Li (Eskandarian, 1992). The approach is motivated by the three-body theory of Mukhamedzhanov (2012) for (d,p) reactions in the Coulomb basis without screening, which requires separable interactions as input. The results enable future Faddeev-AGS calculations for (d,p) reactions on heavier nuclei, including exotic nuclei to be produced at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams, where the screening-based method of Deltuva (2009) becomes inadequate.
