# Computing probabilities of very rare events for Langevin processes: a new method based on importance sampling

> Langevin equations are used to model many processes of physical interest, including low-energy nuclear collisions. In this paper we develop a general method for computing probabilities of very rare events (e.g. small fusion cross-sections) for processes described by Langevin dynamics. As we demonstrate with numerical examples as well as an exactly solvable model, our method can converge to the desired answer at a rate which is orders of magnitude faster than that achieved with direct simulations of the process in question.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general importance-sampling method for computing probabilities of very rare events (e.g., small fusion cross-sections) in Langevin processes, claiming convergence orders of magnitude faster than direct simulation, but only when the additional drift term in the modified Langevin equation is chosen appropriately, and the efficiency gain depends strongly on that choice.

## Method

The authors modify the original Langevin equation by adding an extra drift term Δv designed to increase the probability of the rare event (fusion). They simulate trajectories under this modified equation, then assign each trajectory a weight w = exp(−ΔA), where ΔA is computed from an integral along the trajectory involving the drift terms and noise. The fusion probability is estimated as the biased average of the indicator function Θ (1 for fusion, 0 otherwise) times the weight w, over N trajectories from the modified ensemble. The method is tested on a simplified model of heavy-ion collisions using two collective variables (relative distance and window opening) with a Gaussian-shaped additional force localized near the saddle point.

## Key numbers

- For the test case of two colliding ¹⁰⁰Zr nuclei at 0.2 MeV above the barrier, the fusion probability is on the order of 10⁻³.
- The authors ran 10⁴ independent trajectories under both the original and modified Langevin equations for this test case.
- The reduced mass parameter μ ≈ 0.176 and the constant conservative force X ≈ 0.677 for the ¹⁰⁰Zr+¹⁰⁰Zr collision.
- The additional force in the modified equations has a Gaussian form with width parameter 0.02, centered at (σ,ν) = (0.0, 0.6), with adjustable strength Λ.

## Context

This work extends the importance-sampling technique to Langevin dynamics for nuclear physics applications, specifically addressing the problem of computing very small fusion cross-sections that are impractical to study with direct simulation. The method is demonstrated on a schematic model of heavy-ion collisions introduced by Świątek and previously studied by Aguiar et al. (1990) using Langevin simulations. The approach enables efficient computation of rare-event probabilities in low-energy nuclear collisions and can be generalized to any process described by Langevin dynamics, including those with memory-dependent forces and multi-dimensional phase space.
