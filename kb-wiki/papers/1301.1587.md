# Addendum to: QRPA uncertainties and their correlations in the analysis of neutrinoless double beta decay

> In a previous article [Phys. Rev. D 79, 053001 (2009)] we estimated the correlated uncertainties associated to the nuclear matrix elements (NME) of neutrinoless double beta decay (0 nu beta beta) within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). Such estimates encompass recent independent calculations of NMEs, and can thus still provide a fair representation of the nuclear model uncertainties. In this context, we compare the claim of 0 nu beta beta decay in Ge-76 with recent negative results in Xe-136 and in other nuclei, and we infer the lifetime ranges allowed or excluded at 90% C.L. We also highlight some issues that should be addressed in order to properly compare and combine results coming from different 0 nu beta beta decay candidate nuclei.

## Key claim

The correlated uncertainties of QRPA nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay, estimated in a 2009 paper, still provide a fair representation of the current spread of theoretical NME calculations, and the authors use them to compare the claimed signal in Ge-76 with recent negative results in Xe-136 and other nuclei, inferring lifetime ranges allowed or excluded at 90% C.L.

## Method

The authors adopt the same NME central values, variances, and correlation matrix from their 2009 QRPA study for eight candidate nuclei (Ge-76, Se-82, Zr-96, Mo-100, Cd-116, Te-128, Te-130, Xe-136). They use logarithmic linearization of the half-life equation to propagate the claimed Ge-76 half-life measurement (with its experimental error) through the correlated NME uncertainties, predicting allowed half-life ranges for other nuclei. These predictions are then compared with experimental 90% C.L. lower limits, most notably the combined EXO-200 and KamLAND-Zen limit on Xe-136.

## Key numbers

* Claimed Ge-76 half-life: \( T = 10^{25.355 \pm 0.072} \) years (logarithmic value \(\tau = 25.355 \pm 0.072\)).
* Combined EXO-200 and KamLAND-Zen 90% C.L. lower limit on Xe-136 half-life: \( T^{90} = 3.4 \times 10^{25} \) years.
* To test the Ge-76 claim at 90% C.L., the required half-life ranges are: Ge-76: \( 2.0 \)–\( 2.9 \times 10^{25} \) years; Xe-136: \( 3.4 \)–\( 4.3 \times 10^{25} \) years; Te-130: \( 0.7 \)–\( 1.1 \times 10^{25} \) years.
* Best current 90% C.L. lower limits for other nuclei include: Se-82: \( 3.6 \times 10^{23} \) y; Mo-100: \( 1.1 \times 10^{24} \) y; Te-130: \( 2.8 \times 10^{24} \) y.

## Context

This addendum updates the authors' 2009 QRPA covariance analysis (Faessler et al., Phys. Rev. D 79, 053001) by incorporating new experimental limits from EXO-200 and KamLAND-Zen on Xe-136, which for the first time overlap with the range favored by the Ge-76 claim of Klapdor et al. (2004, 2006). The work demonstrates that NME correlations significantly affect the comparison of results across different nuclei, and it highlights the need for future experiments to publish full likelihood functions for half-lives rather than just bounds at a fixed C.L., to enable proper statistical combination of positive and negative results.
