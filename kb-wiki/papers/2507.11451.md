# Nuclear modification factor within a dynamical approach to the complex entropic index

> This work introduces a novel approach to the nuclear deformation factor $R_{\text{AA}}$, grounded in the dynamical effects of the Quark-Gluon Plasma on parton momentum. The approach uses the Blast-Wave method combined with Tsallis Statistics, within the Cooper-Frye freeze-out framework and, by profiting from appropriate simplifications, it gives analytical expressions that describe the observed $R_{\text{AA}}$ for two sets of independent measurements at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV and $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV. A nonlinear dynamical equation describes the dynamics and leads to log-periodic oscillations. With the analytical solutions for that equation, it is possible to link the dynamical approach with the complex-$q$ formalism, which was proposed to describe the log-oscillations observed in experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the log-periodic oscillations observed in the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\text{AA}}\) can be explained by dynamical effects of the expanding quark-gluon plasma on parton momentum, without invoking a complex-valued entropic index \(q\), provided one adopts a non-relativistic Plastino-Plastino equation and a simplified blast-wave freeze-out geometry that yields unrealistically high fluid velocities.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma expansion using the Blast-Wave model with cylindrical symmetry and a quasi-instantaneous freeze-out surface described by the Cooper-Frye formalism. The in-medium parton dynamics are governed by the non-linear Plastino-Plastino equation, retaining only the drag term (diffusion is found negligible). The initial parton distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions is taken to be the same as in proton-proton collisions, described by a Tsallis \(q\)-exponential with \(q=1.16\); the dynamical effects then modify the parton momentum via Lorentz transformations between the fluid rest frame and the center-of-mass frame combined with an exponential drag factor \(\exp(-A\tau)\). The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\text{AA}}\) is computed analytically by integrating over the freeze-out surface and fitting to experimental data at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV and \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV for several centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The entropic index for the initial parton distribution is fixed at \(q = 1.16\).
- The fractal dimension of the QGP is taken as \(d = 0.69\).
- The proton-proton reference temperature is \(T_{pp} = 0.166\) GeV for data at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV and \(T_{pp} = 0.079\) GeV for data at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV.
- The fitted drag parameter \(D_0 = A_0 \tau\) decreases approximately linearly with centrality; in the most central collisions it corresponds to a freeze-out time \(\tau\) up to about \(7\) fm/c, implying \(A_0 \cong 0.1\) c/fm.
- The radial expansion velocity of the fluid is fitted as \(u = 0.99899\,c\) at 2.76 TeV and \(u = 0.9985\,c\) at 5.02 TeV, nearly independent of centrality.
- The reduced chi-squared values for the fits at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV range from \(\chi^2_{\text{red}} = 0.04\) to \(0.31\) across centrality bins.

## Context

This work extends the line of research connecting Tsallis statistics and complex-\(q\) formalisms (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2014; Rybczynski and Wlodarczyk, 2015) by providing a dynamical interpretation of the log-periodic oscillations in \(R_{\text{AA}}\) without requiring a complex entropic index. It builds on the Plastino-Plastino equation (Plastino and Plastino, 1995) and the fractal interpretation of nonextensivity in QCD (Deppman, 2020). The approach enables a direct analytical link between the drag dynamics in the expanding QGP and the oscillatory structure of the nuclear modification factor, but the authors note that the simplified assumptions (narrow cylindrical freeze-out shell, constant fluid velocity, non-relativistic dynamics) lead to unrealistically high fluid velocities compared to hydrodynamical models (Borsanyi et al., 2010; Bazavov et al., 2014), and that future numerical implementations should relax these idealizations.
