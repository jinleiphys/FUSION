# QGP fireball explosion

> We identify the major physics milestones in the development of strange hadrons as an observable for both the formation of quark-gluon plasma, and of the ensuing explosive disintegration of deconfined matter fireball formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions at 160--20A GeV. We describe the physical properties of QGP phase and show agreement with the expectations based on an analysis of hadron abundances. We than also demonstrate that the m_t shape of hadron spectra is in qualitative agreement with the sudden breakup of a supercooled QGP fireball.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed strange hadron abundances and transverse-mass spectra in central Pb–Pb collisions at 158–200\(A\) GeV are consistent with a sudden, explosive hadronization of a deeply supercooled quark-gluon plasma fireball, rather than with a gradual hadronization or a purely hadronic reaction scenario. The hedge is that the agreement is "qualitative" for the \(m_\perp\) shapes and that the chemical freeze-out analysis requires introducing non-equilibrium quark-pair occupancy parameters to achieve statistical significance.

## Method

The authors perform a combined chemical and thermal freeze-out analysis of hadron abundances and transverse-mass spectra from CERN-SPS experiments (WA97, WA98). They model the QGP equation of state using a two-loop running coupling, a vacuum bag constant \(\mathcal{B}=0.19\) GeV/fm\(^3\), and a partition function with interacting gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks. They then compare the chemical freeze-out conditions (temperature, baryochemical potential, and non-equilibrium occupancy parameters \(\gamma_q\), \(\gamma_s\)) with the thermal freeze-out parameters obtained by fitting the \(m_\perp\) spectra of \(\Xi\), \(\overline\Xi\), and \(\pi^0\) to a sudden freeze-out picture with a common freeze-out temperature and a collective expansion velocity.

## Key numbers

* The equilibrium phase transition temperature at vanishing baryochemical potential is \(T_{\text{pt}} \simeq 172\) MeV; with finite hadron size correction it is \(T_{\text{fp}} \simeq 166\) MeV.
* The supercooled zero-pressure QGP temperature is \(T_{\text{sc}} = 157\) MeV; an expanding fireball can deeply supercool to \(T_{\text{dsc}} \simeq 147\) MeV before mechanical instability occurs.
* The chemical freeze-out analysis yields a freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 143\) MeV and a freeze-out surface velocity \(v_c = 0.54 \pm 0.03\).
* For \(\overline\Xi\) spectra, the best-fit flow velocity is \(v_c = 0.50 \pm 0.03\) (\(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.6\)); for \(\Xi\) it is \(v_c = 0.49 \pm 0.05\) (\(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.1\)).
* For \(\pi^0\) spectra, the fit yields \(v_c = 0.55 \pm 0.02\) and a hard-scattering power-law exponent \(\kappa = 5.6 \pm 1.2\) (\(\chi^2/\text{dof} < 1.4\)).
* The vacuum bag constant is \(\mathcal{B} = 0.19\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Csörgő and Csernai (1994) that a deeply supercooled QGP fireball would undergo sudden hadronization, and it builds on the authors' own previous chemical freeze-out analysis (Letessier et al., 2000) that introduced non-equilibrium quark-pair occupancy parameters. The paper provides a consistent thermal and chemical freeze-out analysis that had not been performed before, and it confirms the earlier finding (NuXu, 1998) that the apparent temperature hierarchy for different-mass particles is due to collective expansion. The results enable future studies to test the explosive hadronization scenario with more precise data and to refine the hard-scattering contribution to pion spectra.
