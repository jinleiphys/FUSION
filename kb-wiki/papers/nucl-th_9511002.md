# The Weak Parity-Violating Pion-Nucleon Coupling (Revised)

> We use QCD sum rules to obtain the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant $f_{πNN}$. We find that $f_{πNN}\approx 2\times 10^{-8}$, about an order of magnitude smaller than the ``best estimates'' based on quark models. This result follows from the cancellation between perturbative and nonperturbative QCD processes not found in quark models, but explicit in the QCD sum rule method. Our result is consistent with the experimental upper limit found from $^{18}$F parity-violating measurements.

# The Weak Parity-Violating Pion-Nucleon Coupling (Revised)

## Key claim

The weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant is approximately $2 \times 10^{-8}$, about an order of magnitude smaller than the "best estimates" from quark models, but this result depends on a cancellation between perturbative and nonperturbative QCD contributions that is sensitive to the value of the vacuum pion susceptibility and the unknown parameter $m_0^\pi$.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules with a two-point function for the nucleon in an external pionic field, keeping terms in the operator product expansion up to dimension 5. They evaluate the correlator for the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon vertex using the standard model weak Hamiltonian, then eliminate the unknown vacuum susceptibility $\chi_\pi$ by taking derivatives with respect to the Borel mass and forming a ratio with the analogous sum rule for the strong coupling constant $g_{\pi NN}$.

## Key numbers

- $f_{\pi NN} \approx 1.9 \times 10^{-8}$ (central value with $m_0^\pi = 0$)
- $f_{\pi NN} \approx (1.9 \text{ to } 2.4) \times 10^{-8}$ for $m_0^\pi$ in the range 0 to 0.8 GeV
- The unit operator term alone gives $f_{\pi NN} \approx 2 \times 10^{-7}$, similar to quark model values
- The constant $c_w = G_F \sin^2\theta_W (17/3 - \gamma)/(24\pi^2) = 5.5 \times 10^{-8} \text{ GeV}^{-2}$
- Using the method of Belyaev and Kogan (1984) gives $\chi_\pi a = -88 \text{ GeV}^2$, leading to $g_{\pi NN} \approx 155$ (versus experimental value 13.5)
- Using the experimental $g_{\pi NN} = 13.5$ gives $\chi_\pi a \approx -1.88 \text{ GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to predict an unknown hadronic property, the weak parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling, which had previously been estimated by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and Dubovik and Zenkin (1986) using quark models. The result agrees with the chiral soliton model of Kaiser and Meissner (1989-1990) but disagrees with Kaplan and Savage (1993) and with a previous QCD sum rule calculation by Khatsimovskii (1985). The small value obtained implies that the neutral current contribution to the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling cannot be separated from the charged current contribution experimentally, and makes observation of the anapole moment unlikely.
