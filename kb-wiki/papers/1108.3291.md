# Phenomenological and microscopic cluster models II. Phase transitions

> Based on the results of a previous paper (Paper I), by performing the geometrical mapping via coherent states, phase transitions are investigated and compared within two algebraic cluster models. The difference between the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) and the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM) is that the former strictly observes the Pauli exclusion principle between the nucleons of the individual clusters, while the latter ignores it. From the technical point of view the SACM is more involved mathematically, while the formalism of the PACM is closer to that of other algebraic models with different physical content. First- and second-order phase transitions are identified in both models, while in the SACM a critical line also appears. Analytical results are complemented with numerical studies on α-cluster states of the neon-20 and magnesium-24 nuclei.

## Key claim

Phase transitions of first and second order are identified in both the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) and the Phenomenological Algebraic Cluster Model (PACM), but the SACM additionally exhibits a critical line beyond which no phase transition occurs; the authors note that the apparently clear phase transition for any finite boson number N is not a real one because N is finite, and only in the limit N approaches infinity will the discontinuities be clear cut.

## Method

The authors performed geometrical mapping via coherent states to obtain potential energy surfaces for both the SACM and PACM, then identified phase transitions by locating parameter-space boundaries where two minima of the potential have equal energy. The order of each phase transition was determined by comparing derivatives of the potential with respect to the model parameters at the spherical and deformed minima, following a general procedure independent of dynamical symmetries. Analytical results were complemented with numerical studies on alpha-cluster states of neon-20 (two spherical clusters) and magnesium-24 (one deformed and one spherical cluster), using a Hamiltonian with parameters fixed except for x and y, which control the transition between dynamical symmetry limits.

## Key numbers

- The SACM phase transition surface in (A,B,C) parameter space shows a critical line at approximately C approximately -15, beyond which no phase transition occurs.
- For the neon-20 system in the SACM, the curve crossing the phase transition surface occurs at approximately x = 0.6, corresponding to a second-order phase transition.
- The PACM phase diagram shows the solid curve of degenerate minima given by A = B^2/4, separating first-order (B greater than 0) and second-order (B less than or equal to 0) phase transition regions.
- For the neon-20 SACM calculation, the harmonic oscillator parameter hbar-omega was set to 13.2, with n_0 = 8 minimal relative oscillation quanta and N = 12 total bosons.
- The SACM parameter values for the oxygen-16 plus alpha system include a = -0.500, gamma = 0.208, b = -0.009, and c = 0.250.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' previous work (Paper I, 2011) on geometrical mapping of the SACM and PACM, where they showed that differences in the mapped geometrical potential are large and that the PACM can reproduce the SACM potential only with complicated higher-order interactions. The phase transition analysis follows the general framework recommended by Greiner (thermodynamics) and others, and the PACM phase diagram is noted to be similar to Fig. 2 of Cejnar (year not specified in text). The work enables future studies of how real nuclei correspond to points in the (x,y) parameter space and whether series of cluster systems can cross the phase transition surface.
