# Calculation of the properties of the rotational bands of $^{155,157}$Gd

> We reexamine the long-standing problem of the microscopic derivation of a particle-core coupling model. We base our research on the Klein-Kerman approach, as amended by Dönau and Frauendorf. We describe the formalism to calculate energy spectra and transition strengths in some detail. We apply our formalism to the rotational nuclei $^{155,157}$Gd, where recent experimental data requires an explanation. We find no clear evidence of a need for Coriolis attenuation.

## Key claim

The authors find no clear evidence that Coriolis attenuation is needed to reproduce the experimental energy spectra and transition strengths of \(^{155,157}\)Gd, provided the particle-core coupling is treated within the Kerman-Klein approach as amended by Dönau and Frauendorf, rather than in a phenomenological particle-rotor model.

## Method

The authors start from a shell-model Hamiltonian with monopole-pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole separable interactions, then derive equations of motion using the Kerman-Klein method. They restrict the collective subspace to the ground-state rotational band of the even-even cores, replace the self-consistent fields by phenomenological inputs (Bohr-Mottelson quadrupole matrix elements and a constant pairing gap), and solve the resulting eigenvalue problem for the odd-mass nuclei \(^{155,157}\)Gd. Spurious states are removed by an adiabatic switching-on of the core Hamiltonian, using the density-matrix projection method of Dönau and Frauendorf.

## Key numbers

* Moments of inertia: \(\mathcal{I}^{155} = 0.0122\) MeV and \(\mathcal{I}^{157} = 0.0124\) MeV, used for the core rotational energies \(\omega_I = I(I+1)/(2\mathcal{I})\).
* Pairing gap: \(\Delta = 127/A\) MeV, giving \(\Delta \approx 0.82\) MeV for \(A \approx 155\).
* Chemical potentials: \(\lambda^{155} = -7.4845\) MeV and \(\lambda^{157} = -7.1488\) MeV, calculated from the ground-state energy difference of the neighboring even-even cores.
* Quadrupole deformations of the cores: \(\beta_{20} = 0.3294\) for \(^{154}\)Gd, 0.3378 for \(^{156}\)Gd, and 0.3484 for \(^{158}\)Gd; for the odd nuclei the averages are \(\beta_{20}^{155} = 0.329\) and \(\beta_{20}^{157} = 0.3431\).
* Best-fit quadrupole–quadrupole strength: \(\kappa = 0.31\) MeV/fm\(^2\), chosen to reproduce the band-head energies of \(^{157}\)Gd.
* With this strength, the \(5/2^{+}[642]\) band head lies about 0.4 MeV below the \(3/2^{+}[402]\) band head, but the \(1/2^{+}[400]\) band head is off by about 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dönau–Frauendorf core-quasiparticle coupling model (Dönau and Frauendorf, 1980s) by incorporating \(\Delta N = \pm 2\) mixing and applying it to the rotational nuclei \(^{155,157}\)Gd, where recent experimental data on transition strengths had not been sufficiently explained. The authors compare their results to less microscopic particle-rotor model calculations (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) that often require Coriolis attenuation of up to 50%; the present approach avoids such ad-hoc reduction. The formalism enables future inclusion of hexadecapole and quadrupole-pairing interactions, and provides a microscopic basis for testing different effective interactions.
