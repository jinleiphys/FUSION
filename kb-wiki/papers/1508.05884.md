# Unitary Limit of Two-Nucleon Interactions in Strong Magnetic Fields

> Two-nucleon systems are shown to exhibit large scattering lengths in strong magnetic fields at unphysical quark masses, and the trends toward the physical values indicate that such features may exist in nature. Lattice QCD calculations of the energies of one and two nucleons systems are performed at pion masses of $m_π\sim 450$ and 806 MeV in uniform, time-independent magnetic fields of strength {\bf B}| \sim 10^{19}$-$10^{20}$ Gauss to determine the response of these hadronic systems to large magnetic fields. Fields of this strength may exist inside magnetars and in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, and the unitary behavior at large scattering lengths may have important consequences for these systems.

## Key claim
Two-nucleon systems in strong magnetic fields at unphysical quark masses exhibit scattering lengths that diverge at particular field strengths, driving the systems toward unitarity; the trends toward physical quark masses suggest such resonant behavior may exist in nature, but the paper explicitly states that calculations at or near physical quark masses and careful continuum and infinite-volume limits are needed to solidify this conclusion.

## Method
Lattice QCD calculations were performed on two ensembles of gauge-field configurations with clover-improved fermion actions and Lüscher-Weisz gauge actions, at pion masses of approximately 450 MeV and 806 MeV. Uniform, time-independent magnetic fields of strength ~10^19–10^20 Gauss were implemented through U_Q(1) gauge fields multiplied onto the QCD gauge fields, and quark propagators were contracted to form nuclear correlation functions. The binding energy shifts of the dineutron, diproton, and the maximal |j_z|=1 spin state of the deuteron were extracted from ratios of correlation functions with and without the magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: m_π ~ 450 MeV and m_π ~ 806 MeV.
- Lattice spacing: a ~ 0.11 fm.
- Volume for m_π ~ 806 MeV ensemble: L^3 × T = 32^3 × 48; for m_π ~ 450 MeV ensemble: L^3 × T = 32^3 × 96.
- Magnetic field strengths quantized as |eB| = 6π|ñ|/(aL)^2, giving fields of O(10^19) Gauss for ñ=1.
- For the dineutron at m_π ~ 806 MeV, the point of minimum binding occurs at ñ ~ 6; at m_π ~ 450 MeV, it occurs at ñ ~ 3.
- A naive extrapolation of the diproton shift linearly in m_π^2 indicates that for a field of |eB| ~ 10^17 Gauss (corresponding to ñ ~ 0.01), the additional attraction may be enough to bind the diproton.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of magnetic moments (Beane et al., 2014) and polarizabilities (Chang et al., 2015) of nucleons and light nuclei up to atomic number A=4, now focusing on two-nucleon interactions in strong magnetic fields. The results suggest that magnetic fields of the strength expected inside magnetars and in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions could unbind the deuteron and potentially bind the diproton, modifying the dynamics of those systems. The observed approach to unitarity in two-nucleon systems parallels behavior routinely exploited in atomic physics (RevModPhys.82.1225) but not previously observed in nuclear physics, and the authors suggest future lattice QCD calculations using the Lüscher method could probe the corresponding modifications to NN scattering phase shifts and mixing angles.
