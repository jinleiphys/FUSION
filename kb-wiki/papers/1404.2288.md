# Cosmic neutrino cascades from secret neutrino interactions

> The first detection of high-energy astrophysical neutrinos by IceCube provides new opportunities for tests of neutrino properties. The long baseline through the Cosmic Neutrino Background (C$ν$B) is particularly useful for directly testing secret neutrino interactions ($ν$SI) that would cause neutrino-neutrino elastic scattering at a larger rate than the usual weak interactions. We show that IceCube can provide competitive sensitivity to $ν$SI compared to other astrophysical and cosmological probes, which are complementary to laboratory tests. We study the spectral distortions caused by $ν$SI with a large s-channel contribution, which can lead to a dip, bump, or cutoff on an initially smooth spectrum. Consequently, $ν$SI may be an exotic solution for features seen in the IceCube energy spectrum. More conservatively, IceCube neutrino data could be used to set model-independent limits on $ν$SI. Our phenomenological estimates provide guidance for more detailed calculations, comparisons to data, and model building.

## Key claim

IceCube's detection of high-energy astrophysical neutrinos can provide competitive sensitivity to secret neutrino interactions (νSI) that cause neutrino-neutrino elastic scattering at rates larger than weak interactions, though the effects depend on the mediator mass relative to the interaction energy and may produce spectral distortions (dip, bump, or cutoff) rather than simple attenuation.

## Method

The authors model the propagation of astrophysical neutrinos through the Cosmic Neutrino Background (CνB) using a Boltzmann-like transport equation that includes both attenuation and regeneration terms from νSI, assuming a Breit-Wigner s-channel cross section for a scalar mediator. They numerically solve this propagation equation in redshift space, adopting a source evolution that follows the star formation rate and a generic emission spectrum with a power-law and exponential cutoff, then compare the resulting spectra to the free-streaming case for various mediator masses and couplings.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Kolb and Turner (1987), who used SN 1987A neutrinos to set constraints on νSI, by incorporating regeneration and cascade effects beyond pure attenuation and by applying the method to the higher-energy, longer-baseline IceCube neutrinos. It also generalizes earlier treatments by Goldberg (2005) and Baker (2006) that considered simplified regeneration. The results provide guidance for more detailed calculations and model building, and show that IceCube sensitivity lies in a parameter window (MeV-range mediator masses) where astrophysical and cosmological constraints from BBN (Ahlgren 2013) and CMB (Cyr-Racine 2013, Archidiacono 2013) are complementary to flavor-dependent laboratory limits from meson and lepton decays (Lessa 2007).
