# In-medium vector meson masses in a Chiral SU(3) model

> A significant drop of the vector meson masses in nuclear matter is observed in a chiral SU(3) model due to the effects of the baryon Dirac sea. This is taken into account through the summation of baryonic tadpole diagrams in the relativistic Hartree approximation. The appreciable decrease of the in-medium vector meson masses is due to the vacuum polarisation effects from the nucleon sector and is not observed in the mean field approximation.

## Key claim

The authors claim a significant drop of vector meson masses in nuclear matter when baryon Dirac sea effects are included via the relativistic Hartree approximation, but this drop is **not observed** in the mean field approximation and arises specifically from vacuum polarisation effects in the nucleon sector.

## Method

The authors use a chiral SU(3) × SU(3) model with nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, including baryon octet, scalar and vector meson multiplets, and a glueball field to account for the QCD trace anomaly. They compute nuclear matter properties first in the mean field approximation, then include quantum corrections by summing baryonic tadpole diagrams in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The in-medium vector meson masses are obtained by solving the dispersion relation \(k_0^2 - m_V^2 + \text{Re}\,\Pi(k_0, \mathbf{k}=0) = 0\), where the self-energy \(\Pi\) includes both a Feynman (vacuum fluctuation) part and a density-dependent part.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density is taken as \(\rho_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(E/A = -16\ \text{MeV}\).
* In the mean field approximation with quartic vector meson coupling \(g_4 = 2.7\), the effective nucleon mass at saturation is \(m_N^*/m_N = 0.64\) and the incompressibility is \(K = 266.1\ \text{MeV}\).
* In the Hartree approximation with \(g_4 = 2.7\), the effective nucleon mass at saturation rises to \(m_N^*/m_N = 0.73\) and the incompressibility becomes \(K = 304\ \text{MeV}\).
* Vacuum vector meson masses are fixed using \(m_V = 687.33\ \text{MeV}\), \(\mu \sigma_0^2 = 0.41\), and \(\lambda_V = -0.041\) to reproduce the physical \(\omega\), \(\rho\), and \(\phi\) masses.
* The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 93.3\ \text{MeV}\) and the kaon decay constant is \(f_K = 122\ \text{MeV}\), giving scalar condensate vacuum expectation values \(\sigma_0 = 93.3\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\zeta_0 = 106.56\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of in-medium vector meson masses in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) by Hatsuda (1992), Hatsuda et al. (1993), and Jean et al. (1994), which found that dropping masses arise from vacuum polarisation in the baryon sector, an effect absent in mean field. It also relates to the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) that vector meson masses scale with the in-medium pion decay constant. The chiral SU(3) model used here was previously developed by Papazoglou et al. (1998, 1999) for describing nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron star properties. The results enable further studies of low-mass dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, where reduced vector meson masses could explain enhanced yields observed by experiments such as HELIOS, CERES, and DLS.
