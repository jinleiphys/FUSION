# Nonlocality of the NN interaction in an effective field theory

> We investigate low energy nucleon dynamics in the effective field theory (EFT) of nuclear forces. In leading order of the two-nucleon EFT we show that nucleon dynamics is governed by the generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator. This equation is shown to open new possibilities for applying the EFT approach to the description of low energy nucleon dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors show that in leading order of the two-nucleon effective field theory, nucleon dynamics is governed by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator, rather than by the Schrödinger equation; this nonlocality is presented as a consequence of renormalization, not as an assumption, and the authors claim this opens new possibilities for parametrizing the NN interaction without renormalization-scheme dependence.

## Method

The authors apply the formalism of generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) developed in earlier work (Gainutdinov, 1999) to the two-nucleon EFT with spinless nucleons, using the leading-order contact interaction C0 from the effective Lagrangian of Ref. [15]. They show that the T matrix for the contact term satisfies the generalized dynamical equation (a differential equation for the T matrix as a function of complex energy z) rather than the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and they demonstrate that this T matrix has the same mathematical structure as an exactly solvable separable model with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator. The model is then fitted to NN scattering data in the 3S1(np), 1S0(np), and 1S0(pp) channels.

## Key numbers

* In the 3S1(np) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, c1 = 133.5 × 10^2, β × ρ = 433.8, d × ρ = 766.2, b2 × ρ^(1-2α) = 4.207 × 10^(-5), where ρ = 1 MeV^(-1).
* In the 1S0(np) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, c1 = 131.8, β × ρ = 356.3, d × ρ = 3.651 × 10^6, b2 × ρ^(1-2α) = 4.202 × 10^(-5).
* In the 1S0(pp) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, c1 = 320.0, β × ρ = 371.7, d × ρ = 6.763 × 10^5, b2 × ρ^(1-2α) = 4.204 × 10^(-5).
* The form factor asymptotic exponent α satisfies -1/2 < α < 1/2 for the nonlocal-in-time case, with the boundary α = 1/2 also treated separately; the leading asymptotic coefficient b1 for α < 1/2 is b1 = -4π cos(απ) m^(α-3/2).

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics formalism of Gainutdinov (1999) and Gainutdinov and Mutygullina (1999) to the effective field theory of nuclear forces, specifically the two-nucleon EFT approach developed by Weinberg (1990-1992) and others. The authors argue that after renormalization, the EFT dynamics cannot be described by a Hamiltonian and the Schrödinger equation, but instead requires the nonlocal-in-time interaction operator of GQD. This enables a parametrization of the NN interaction that is independent of renormalization schemes, in contrast to subtractive EFT approaches that require cutoff regularization and renormalization (as in the work of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, and others). The model is fitted to the Nijmegen phase shift data of Stoks (1993).
