# Directed flow in relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic expansion for symmetric and asymmetric collision systems

> We construct a dynamical model for high-energy heavy-ion collision based on the relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic framework. Using our newly developed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamics code, we investigate magneto-hydrodynamic expansion in symmetric and asymmetric collision systems as a first application to high-energy heavy-ion collisions. As a realistic initial condition for electromagnetic fields, we consider the solutions of the Maxwell equations with the source term of point charged particles moving in the direction of the beam axis, including finite constant electrical conductivity of the medium. We evaluate the directed flow in the symmetric and asymmetric collisions at RHIC energy. We find a significant effect of finite electrical conductivity on the directed flow in the asymmetric collision system. We confirm that a certain amount of energy transfer by dissipation associated with Ohmic conduction occurs in the asymmetric collision system because of asymmetry of the electric field produced by two different colliding nuclei. Because this energy transfer makes the pressure gradient of the medium flatter, the growth of directed flow decreases.

# Directed flow in relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic expansion for symmetric and asymmetric collision systems

## Key claim
The authors find that finite electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma has a significant effect on directed flow in asymmetric (Cu-Au) collisions at RHIC energy, but the effect is conditional on the asymmetry of the electric field produced by two different colliding nuclei. The energy transfer from Ohmic dissipation flattens the pressure gradient, which decreases the growth of directed flow.

## Method
The authors constructed a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic resistive magneto-hydrodynamic (RRMHD) code in Milne coordinates, solving Maxwell equations with finite constant electrical conductivity simultaneously with relativistic hydrodynamic equations. They used the optical Glauber model with tilted sources for the initial energy density distribution and computed initial electromagnetic fields from solutions of Maxwell equations with point charged particle sources moving along the beam axis, integrated over uniform spherical charge distributions of the colliding nuclei. The code was applied to Au-Au and Cu-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with impact parameter 10 fm.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density at (eta_s, x_T) = (0, 0): e_0 = 55 GeV/fm^3
- Initial proper time: tau_0 = 0.4 fm
- Constant electrical conductivity: sigma_0 = 5.8 MeV
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: sigma_NN^inel = 40 mb
- Collision hardness parameter: alpha_H = 0.05
- Tilted source parameter: eta_m = 3.36
- Plateau width: eta_flat = 5.9
- Gauss function width: w_eta = 0.4
- Magnetic field intensity scale at RHIC: |eB| ~ 10^14-10^15 T ~ m_pi^2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au-Au collisions

## Context
This work extends the relativistic ideal magneto-hydrodynamic simulations of Inghirami (2016, 2019) by incorporating finite electrical conductivity, which is necessary to handle the electric field evolution in asymmetric collision systems. The study builds on the proposal by Hirono (2012) for estimating electrical conductivity from charge-dependent directed flow in asymmetric collisions. The RRMHD framework enables future quantitative extraction of electrical conductivity from experimental data at RHIC and LHC, which is relevant for phenomena such as the chiral magnetic effect and vacuum birefringence of photons.
