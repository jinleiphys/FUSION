# Mean-field calculations of exotic nuclei ground states

> We study the predictions of three mean-field theoretical approaches in the description of the ground state properties of some spherical nuclei far from the stability line. We compare binding energies, single particle spectra, density distributions, charge and neutron radii obtained with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations carried out with both zero and finite-range interactions, and with a relativistic Hartree approach which uses a finite-range interaction. The agreement between the results obtained with the three different approaches indicates that these results are more related to the basic hypotheses of the mean-field approach rather than to its implementation in actual calculations.

## Key claim

The three mean-field approaches (non-relativistic Hartree-Fock with zero-range Skyrme and finite-range Gogny interactions, and relativistic Dirac-Hartree with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings) produce very similar results for ground-state properties of spherical exotic nuclei, suggesting that the predictions depend more on the basic mean-field assumptions than on the specific implementation, though the agreement is qualified by the fact that the interactions were fitted to reproduce similar sets of stable nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations using the zero-range Skyrme SLy5 interaction and the finite-range Gogny D1M interaction, alongside relativistic Dirac-Hartree calculations using the density-dependent DDME2 interaction, to compute binding energies, single-particle spectra, density distributions, charge and neutron radii for 16 spherical nuclei across four isotopic chains (oxygen, calcium, nickel, tin). They compared these results with experimental data where available, and also calculated infinite nuclear matter properties and electron scattering cross sections (both elastic and (e,e'p) knockout) to probe differences in the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ for D1M, SLy5, and 0.15 fm⁻³ for DDME2; all within 2% of each other
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.01 MeV (D1M), -15.98 MeV (SLy5), -16.13 MeV (DDME2); all within 0.4% of each other
- Volume compression modulus K_V = 217 MeV (D1M), 228 MeV (SLy5), 278 MeV (DDME2); D1M and SLy5 close to empirical 220 ± 30 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation e_sym(ρ₀) = 29.45 MeV (D1M), 32.66 MeV (SLy5), 33.20 MeV (DDME2); within 6% of each other
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 25.41 MeV (D1M), 48.38 MeV (SLy5), 54.74 MeV (DDME2); large variation compared to empirical 88 ± 25 MeV
- Largest relative difference in binding energies per nucleon compared to experimental data is 0.6%
- For elastic electron scattering on calcium isotopes, cross sections from different models begin to differ at scattering angles above about 60°, corresponding to momentum transfer of 400 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of mean-field calculations for stable nuclei (Vautherin and Brink 1972 for Skyrme; Decharge and Gogny 1980 for Gogny; Serot and Walecka 1986 for relativistic approaches) to neutron-rich isotopes far from stability, comparing three modern parametrizations (SLy5 from the Saclay-Lyon collaboration, D1M from Gorley et al. 2009, and DDME2 from Lalazissis et al. 2005). The study enables future work on whether the observed similarities hold for deformed nuclei or for observables more sensitive to the high-density behavior of the equation of state, such as neutron star properties, where the three models show large differences in the symmetry energy slope parameter L.
