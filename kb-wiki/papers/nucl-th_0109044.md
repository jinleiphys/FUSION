# Isospin Dependence of Mechanical and Chemical Instabilities in Neutron-Rich Matter

> Within nuclear thermodynamics and an isospin-dependent transport model we investigate respective roles of the nuclear mean field and the 2-body stochastic scattering on the evolution of density and isospin fluctuations in either mechanically or chemically unstable regions of neutron-rich matter. It is found that the mean field dominates overwhelmingly the fast growth of both fluctuations, while the 2-body scattering influences significantly the later growth of the isospin fluctuation only. Moreover, both fluctuations grow in mechanically unstable systems, while only the density fluctuation grows significantly in chemically unstable ones. Furthermore, the magnitude of both fluctuations decreases with the increasing isospin asymmetry because of the larger reduction of the attractive isoscalar mean field by the stronger repuslive neutron symmetry potential in the more neutron-rich matter. Finally, several experimental measurements are proposed to test these findings.

# Isospin Dependence of Mechanical and Chemical Instabilities in Neutron-Rich Matter

## Key claim
The nuclear mean field overwhelmingly dominates the fast growth of both density and isospin fluctuations in neutron-rich matter, while 2-body stochastic scattering only significantly influences the later growth of isospin fluctuations. The magnitude of both fluctuations decreases with increasing isospin asymmetry because the repulsive neutron symmetry potential reduces the attractive isoscalar mean field more strongly in more neutron-rich matter.

## Method
The authors used a Skyrme-type phenomenological equation of state in the parabolic approximation to map the boundaries of mechanical (isothermal spinodal) and chemical (diffusive spinodal) instabilities in the density-isospin asymmetry plane at temperatures of 5, 10, and 15 MeV. They then simulated the evolution of unstable nuclear matter using an isospin-dependent transport model (based on the BUU equation) in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, initializing nucleons with a Boltzmann distribution and using 10^4 test particles per nucleon in 1 fm^3 cells. To isolate the roles of mean field versus collisions, they performed simulations both with and without the collision integral.

## Key numbers
- The Skyrme parameters used: a = -123.6 MeV, b = 70.4 MeV, sigma = 2, corresponding to a stiff nuclear EOS for isospin-symmetric matter.
- Symmetry energy parameterization: S_0(rho_0) = 30 MeV with gamma = 0.5 (constant used throughout simulations).
- Initial conditions for unstable systems: T_i = 5 MeV, rho_i = 0.05 fm^{-3}, with isospin asymmetries delta_i = 0.2, 0.6, and 0.9.
- Before about 40 fm/c, essentially no collisions occur due to strong Pauli blocking.
- The neutron-neutron cross section is about 1/3 of the neutron-proton cross section at beam energies below about 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior predictions from microscopic many-body theories (e.g., Lattimer 1978, Barranco 1980, Muller) that isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter can be mechanically or chemically unstable under certain conditions. It provides dynamical information on fluctuation growth rates that was previously rare, and connects to the density dependence of the symmetry energy studied by Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (2000) and others. The findings enable experimental tests via multifragmentation measurements, neutron-neutron and proton-proton correlation functions, and isospin fractionation studies in reactions induced by neutron-rich nuclei, with implications for understanding neutron star structure and radioactive nuclei stability.
