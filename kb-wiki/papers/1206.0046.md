# Ab initio calculations of nuclear widths via an integral relation

> I describe the computation of energy widths of nuclear states using an integral over the interaction region of ab initio variational Monte Carlo wave functions, and I present calculated widths for many states. I begin by presenting relations that connect certain short-range integrals to widths. I then present predicted widths for 5 <= A <= 9 nuclei, and I compare them against measured widths. They match the data more closely and with less ambiguity than estimates based on spectroscopic factors. I consider the consequences of my results for identification of observed states in ^8B, ^9He, and ^9Li. I also examine failures of the method and conclude that they generally involve broad states and variational wave functions that are not strongly peaked in the interaction region. After examining bound-state overlap functions computed from a similar integral relation, I conclude that overlap calculations can diagnose cases in which computed widths should not be trusted.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to compute nuclear resonance widths from *ab initio* variational Monte Carlo wave functions using a short-range integral relation, claiming that the computed widths match experimental data more closely and with less ambiguity than estimates based on spectroscopic factors, but the method is limited to narrow states (Γ ≲ 1 MeV) and fails for broad states or when variational wave functions are not strongly peaked in the interaction region.

## Method

The author derives an integral relation connecting the partial width in a specified breakup channel to a short-range integral over the interaction region of variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions, using the Argonne v18 two-body and Urbana IX three-body interactions. The method projects the many-body wave function onto channel functions defined by daughter nuclei and their relative motion, then computes the asymptotic normalization constant from an integral involving the difference between the full inter-cluster interaction and the point-Coulomb interaction, which vanishes beyond about 7 fm. The partial width is then obtained from the square of this asymptotic normalization constant, with the integral evaluated using only VMC wave functions (not GFMC) for all narrow one-nucleon decays to bound states in nuclei with mass numbers 5 ≤ A ≤ 9.

## Key numbers

- Widths are computed for all narrow (Γ ≲ 1 MeV) one-nucleon decays to bound states in 5 ≤ A ≤ 9 nuclei
- The nuclear interaction region where the integral is evaluated extends to r ≃ 7 fm, beyond which the difference (U_rel - V_C) goes rapidly to zero
- The ground state of ⁹B has a total energy of -56.3 MeV and a width of 0.5 keV (roughly 0.01% of the total energy), which is too narrow for the method to resolve given the typical 1% precision of GFMC energy calculations
- The typical precision claimed for GFMC energy calculations is 1%

## Context

This work extends the integral relation method previously used for alpha decay (Kadmenskii 1973, Bugrov 1989) and proton decay (Åberg 1997, Davids 1998, Esbensen 2001) of heavy nuclei to *ab initio* many-body wave functions for light nuclei, and builds on the author's prior work on asymptotic normalization coefficients (Nollett and Wiringa 2011). The method provides an alternative to computationally expensive scattering calculations such as the Green's function Monte Carlo particle-in-a-box method (Nollett et al. 2007) or the no-core shell model combined with the resonating group method (Navrátil 2010, 2011), and enables width calculations for states too narrow to resolve by direct QMC methods.
