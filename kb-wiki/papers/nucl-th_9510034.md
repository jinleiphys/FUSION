# Character States and Generator Matrix Elements for $Sp(4) \supset SU(2) \times U(1)$

> A new set of polynomial states (to be called character states) are derived for $Sp(4)$ reduced to its $SU(2) \times U(1)$ subgroup, and the relevant generator matrix elements are evaluated. The group--subgroup in question is that of the seniority model of nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new set of polynomial basis states, called "character states," for the group-subgroup chain $Sp(4) \supset SU(2) \times U(1)$ relevant to the nuclear seniority model, and evaluates the corresponding generator matrix elements; the states are claimed to be simpler than earlier polynomial bases, but they are "contaminated by unwanted states belonging to lower representations," which does not affect the computation of generator matrix elements.

## Method

The authors construct character states as polynomial functions of the fundamental representation states of $Sp(4)$, using a character generator expressed as a rational function whose power series expansion yields complete and non-redundant basis states for each irreducible representation $(a,b)$. Incompatible pairs of fundamental states (those that never appear together in a valid state) are identified from the quadratic direct products of the fundamental representations, and substitutions are derived to eliminate them in favor of compatible pairs. Generator matrix elements for the degenerate representations $(a,0)$ and $(0,b)$ are then computed by applying the $Sp(4)$ root generators (expressed as differential operators in the fundamental state variables) to the character states and using recursion relations derived from orthonormality conditions.

## Key numbers

* For the $(a,0)$ representation, the normalization constant for the highest state of an $SU(2)$ irreducible representation is $N_{t\;\;z} = \sqrt{ \frac{(2 t + 1)!}{(t + z)! (t - z)! (a/2 - t)! (a/2 + t + 1)!} }$, where $a/2 \ge t \ge |z|$ and $2t$, $2z$ have the parity of $a$.
* For the $(a,0)$ representation, the reduced matrix element of the $Sp(4)$ generator $G$ connecting states with $t$ and $t+1$ is $\sqrt{ \frac{(t + z + 1)(t + z + 2)(a/2 - t)(a/2 + t + 2)}{2 (t + 1)} }$.
* For the $(a,0)$ representation, the reduced matrix element of $G$ connecting states with the same $t$ is $-\left( a/2 + 1 \right) \sqrt{ \frac{(2 t + 1)(t + z + 1)(t - z)}{2 t (t + 1)} }$.
* For the $(0,b)$ representation, the normalization constant is $N_{t\;\;z} = \sqrt{ \frac{(2t+1)!! (2b+1)!!}{t! ((b-t-z)/2)! ((b-t+z)/2)! (b+t+z+1)!! (b+t-z+1)!!} }$, with branching rule $b - |z| \ge t \ge 0$.
* For the $(0,b)$ representation, the reduced matrix element of $\overline{G}$ connecting states with $t$ and $t+1$ is $-\sqrt{ (t+1) ((b-t+z)/2) (b+t-z+3) }$.
* For the $(0,b)$ representation, the reduced matrix element of $\overline{G}$ connecting states with $t$ and $t-1$ is $-\sqrt{ t ((b-t-z+2)/2) (b+t+z+1) }$.

## Context

This work extends the polynomial basis state approach previously applied to $SU(3)$, $SO(5)$, $SO(7)$, and $G_2$ (de Guise and Sharp 1991; Burdik, Cummins, Gaskell and Sharp 1992; Farell, Lam and Sharp 1993) to the $Sp(4) \supset SU(2) \times U(1)$ chain of the nuclear seniority model, which was originally classified by Flowers (1952). The new character states are presented as simpler alternatives to earlier polynomial bases given by Hecht (1965), Parikh (1965), Ahmed and Sharp (1970), and Smirnov and Tolstoy (1973). The paper treats only the degenerate representations $(a,0)$ and $(0,b)$ where there is no missing label; a forthcoming paper is promised to treat generic representations $(a,b)$ where a missing label appears.
