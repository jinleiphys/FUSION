# Continuity of Quark and Hadron Matter

> We review, clarify, and extend the notion of color-flavor locking. We present evidence that for three degenerate flavors the qualitative features of the color-flavor locked state, reliably predicted for high density, match the expected features of hadronic matter at low density. This provides, in particular, a controlled, weak-coupling realization of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in this (slight) idealization of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the color-flavor locked state of high-density quark matter, reliably predicted by weak-coupling analysis, has qualitative features that match the expected properties of hadronic matter at low density for three degenerate flavors, suggesting these two phases may be continuously connected without a phase transition.

## Method

The authors analyze the symmetry breaking pattern of the color-flavor locked state using a four-fermion interaction derived from one-gluon exchange, calculating the effective potential in the mean-field approximation. They perform a variational analysis to determine the preferred order parameter for three flavors, diagonalizing the fermion mass matrix and minimizing the effective potential numerically to find the groundstate gap structure and residual symmetries.

## Key numbers

- For three flavors, the fermion spectrum has gaps of \(\Delta\) (8-fold degenerate) and \(2\Delta\) (1-fold degenerate), with \(\Delta_0 = 36\) MeV as the reference scale
- The condensation energy per species for three flavors is \(1.27\epsilon_0\), where \(\epsilon_0 = 0.73\) MeV/fm\(^3\)
- For three flavors, there are 8 unbroken color-flavor symmetries (the diagonal \(SU(3)^{c+L+R}\))
- The singlet gap is twice the octet gap for three flavors

## Context

This work extends the color-flavor locking concept introduced by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) for high-density QCD, and builds on the renormalization group analysis by Schaefer and Wilczek (1998) and Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1998). The paper connects to Jaffe's (1977) prediction of the H dibaryon in the MIT bag model, arguing that a Bose condensate of H dibaryons at low density provides the superfluid counterpart to the color-flavor locked state. This continuity hypothesis enables a controlled weak-coupling realization of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking, with implications for neutron star interiors and heavy ion collisions.
