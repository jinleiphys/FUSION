# Cooling curves for neutron stars with hadronic matter and quark matter

> The thermal evolution of isothermal neutron stars is studied with matter both in the hadronic phase as well as in the mixed phase of hadronic matter and strange quark matter. In our models, the dominant early-stage cooling process is neutrino emission via the direct Urca process. As a consequence, the cooling curves fall too fast compared to observations. However, when superfluidity is included, the cooling of the neutron stars is significantly slowed down. Furthermore, we find that the cooling curves are not very sensitive to the precise details of the mixing between the hadronic phase and the quark phase and also of the pairing that leads to superfluidity.

## Key claim

The authors find that cooling curves for neutron stars containing both hadronic matter and a mixed phase of hadronic and strange quark matter fall too fast compared to observations when the direct Urca process dominates, but that including superfluidity slows the cooling significantly; however, the cooling curves are not very sensitive to the precise details of the phase mixing or the pairing that leads to superfluidity.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for nuclear, hadronic (including hyperons), and mixed hadronic-quark phases using a relativistic mean-field model with derivative coupling for baryons and a bag model for quarks, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar structure. They compute thermal evolution by solving the general relativistic heat transport equations for isothermal neutron stars, using neutrino emissivities from direct Urca processes (both baryonic and quark) and including the effects of superfluidity via suppression factors on heat capacity and emissivity.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for the hadronic equation of state: 1.523 solar masses, radius 9.72 km
- Maximum mass for the mixed equation of state: 1.479 solar masses, radius 9.81 km
- Bag constant used for quark matter: 230 MeV per fm^3
- Direct Urca neutrino emissivity scales as T^6 (temperature to the sixth power), while modified Urca scales as T^8
- Typical proto-neutron star temperature: 10^10 K (approximately 1 MeV)
- Typical Fermi temperature of nucleons: about 10^12 K
- Coupling constants used: (g_sigma/m_sigma)^2 = 7.487 fm^2, (g_omega/m_omega)^2 = 2.615 fm^2, (g_rho/m_rho)^2 = 4.774 fm^2

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star cooling by Yakovlev (2001) and others, which focused primarily on nuclear matter, to include a unified treatment from the low-density nuclear phase through the hadronic phase with hyperons to deconfined quark matter, consistent with the QCD phase diagram. The authors note that previous work by Schaab (1996) and Page (2002) considered strange quark stars, but the cooling behavior of neutron stars containing both hyperons and a mixed phase had not been extensively studied. The results enable comparisons with observational data from young neutron stars like those studied by Popov (2003) and Page (2006), and can help constrain equations of state for medium-mass neutron stars (below about 1.4 solar masses).
