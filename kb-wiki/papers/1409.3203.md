# Massless Mode and Positivity Violation in Hot QCD

> We calculate the quark self-energy at one-loop level at high temperature, taking into account contributions from both the (chromo)electric scale $gT$ and the (chromo)magnetic scale $g^2T$. While reproducing standard massive excitations due to the electric scale, we uncover a novel massless excitation ascribable to the magnetic scale. The residue of this massless excitation is nonpositive at all temperatures, which consequently gives rise to positivity violation in the quark spectral functions. This demonstrates the profound impact of confinement effects on thermal quark collective excitations, which manifest genuine long-range correlations in the system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a novel massless fermionic excitation (the "Gribov pole") emerges in the quark spectral function at high temperature when the nonperturbative magnetic scale is incorporated via the Gribov-Zwanziger action, and that this mode's residue is nonpositive at all temperatures, implying positivity violation. The hedge is that this result is demonstrated at one-loop level in the high-temperature limit (where g << 1) using the GZ action as a "simple demonstration" without loss of generality, and the authors note that the refined Gribov-Zwanziger action would be in better agreement with lattice data.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop quark self-energy in Euclidean space at high temperature using the Gribov-Zwanziger gluon propagator, which incorporates the magnetic scale g^2T via the Gribov parameter gamma_G. They perform the Matsubara summation and then apply the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation for soft external momenta, expanding in small P over K. After analytical continuation to Minkowski space, they extract the dispersion relations and residues of the poles in the resummed quark propagator by decomposing it into positive and negative helicity-to-chirality contributions and studying the singularities of the denominator.

## Key numbers

* The quark screening mass including magnetic-scale effects is m_q^2(gamma_G) = g^2 C_F (1/(4 pi^2)) sum_{pm} integral_0^infty dk (k^2 / E_pm^0) tilde_n_pm(k, gamma_G), which reduces to the conventional value m_q^2(0) = C_F g^2 T^2 / 8 when gamma_G = 0.
* The Gribov parameter at one-loop order at asymptotically high temperatures is gamma_G = ((D-1)/D) (N_c / (4 sqrt(2) pi)) g^2 T, providing an IR cutoff at the magnetic scale.
* The massless Gribov mode has a dispersion relation omega = v_s p at small momenta, with v_s approximately 1/sqrt(3) (speed of sound), independent of coupling for g less than or about 2.
* The low-momentum limit of the conventional quark and plasmino dispersion relations is omega_plus/minus(0; gamma_G) = m_q(gamma_G), confirmed numerically.
* The residue of the Gribov pole Z_G(p) is negative (nonpositive) and tends to zero at vanishing momenta, Z_G(0) = 0.

## Context

This work extends the hard-thermal-loop effective theory of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) by incorporating nonperturbative confinement effects from the magnetic scale via the Gribov-Zwanziger action, which was developed by Gribov (1978) and Zwanziger (1989) to address the Gribov ambiguity and color confinement. The results contradict the conventional perturbative expectation that no collective excitations exist in the spacelike regime (omega < p) and that quark spectral functions are positive. The massless mode and positivity violation align with expectations from lattice QCD and functional methods for gluons (reviewed by Maas, 2011) and enable future studies of shear viscosity using positivity-violating spectral functions, as recently attempted by Haas et al. (2013), as well as investigations of Cherenkov radiation and thermodynamic instabilities in the quark-gluon plasma.
