# Antisymmetry in Strangeness -1 and -2 Three-Baryon Systems

> Using the generalized Pauli principle by adding particle labels to the usual space and spin labels a symmetric Hamiltonian and a corresponding antisymmetric wavefunction is constructed for systems of three baryons in the strangeness sectors $S=-1$ and -2. Applications are the $ΞNN-ΛΛN$ and $NNΛ-NNΣ$ systems. Minimal sets of generalized coupled Faddeev equations for breakup and rearrangement operators as well as (possible) bound states are derived which have the ordinary Pauli principle among identical particles built in. The equations found confirm our previous sets of coupled Faddeev equations for those systems whose derivation was carried through for distinguishable particles and not using the generalized Pauli principle.

# Antisymmetry in Strangeness -1 and -2 Three-Baryon Systems

## Key claim

The authors derive minimal sets of coupled Faddeev equations for three-baryon systems with strangeness S=-1 and S=-2 that correctly incorporate the ordinary Pauli principle among identical particles, confirming their previous sets of equations that had been derived assuming distinguishable particles without using the generalized Pauli principle. The derivation is conceptually and algebraically simpler than their earlier second-quantization approach, and now includes the possibility of a Sigma-admixture that was previously neglected.

## Method

The authors construct a symmetric Hamiltonian and corresponding antisymmetric wavefunction by adding particle labels (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) to the usual space and spin labels, generalizing the familiar isospin technique to SU(3) including strangeness. They decompose the total three-baryon wavefunction into Faddeev amplitudes, impose antisymmetry under exchange of identical particles, and project the Faddeev equations onto a complete set of particle states to obtain coupled equations for the independent amplitudes. The resulting equations are cast into matrix form with a potential matrix that decays into uncoupled subsystems, from which t-matrix Lippmann-Schwinger equations are derived.

## Key numbers

- For S=-1 systems, the particle states considered are: |Lambda NN>, |N Lambda N>, |NN Lambda>, |Sigma NN>, |N Sigma N>, |NN Sigma> (6 states)
- For S=-2 systems, the particle states considered are: |Xi NN>, |N Xi N>, |N Lambda Sigma>, |N Lambda Lambda>, |N Sigma Sigma>, |Lambda N Lambda>, |Lambda N Sigma>, |Sigma N Lambda>, |Sigma N Sigma> (9 states, with 6 additional groupings related by antisymmetry)
- The S=-2 system without Sigma admixture yields a 4x4 potential matrix and four coupled Faddeev equations (Eqs. 3.29-3.32)
- The S=-1 system yields four coupled Faddeev equations (Eqs. 3.25-3.27)
- The full S=-2 system including Sigma is described by a set of nine coupled Faddeev equations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation (Gloeckle and Miyagawa, earlier paper) of coupled Faddeev equations for the Xi NN - Lambda Lambda N system, which had used distinguishable particles and neglected Sigma admixture. The formalism provides a rigorous foundation for extracting hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon forces from final-state interactions, as proposed by Afnan and collaborators (1999) for the Xi d -> Lambda Lambda N process as a source of information on the Lambda-Lambda scattering length. The equations enable rigorous calculations for light hypernuclei, building on prior work for the hypertriton (Gloeckle and collaborators) and for Lambda-4He and Lambda-4H (Hiyama and collaborators), and are applicable to photon-induced K+ Y production on the deuteron (Yamamura and collaborators) and electron-induced processes (Reinhold and collaborators, Lee and collaborators).
