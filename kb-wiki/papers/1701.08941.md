# Compositeness Of Quasibound States from Effective Field Theory

> The internal structure of exotic hadron candidates are studied from the viewpoint of the compositeness of near-threshold states. We focus on the Weinberg's weak-binding relation between the experimental observables and the compositeness of the state. First we extend the relation for quasibound states within the framework of the effective field theory. Next, considering the correction term, we develop a systematic method to estimate the uncertainty of compositeness in the weak-binding relation. Finally, applying the extended relation and the error evaluation method, we conclude that the structure of Lambda(1405) is dominated by the Kbar N component.

## Key claim

The paper extends Weinberg's weak-binding relation to unstable quasibound states using non-relativistic effective field theory, and concludes that the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance is dominated by the antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) composite component, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the magnitude of higher-order correction terms being small but not negligible.

## Method

The authors derive an extended weak-binding relation for quasibound states within a non-relativistic effective field theory framework for coupled-channel s-wave scattering. They introduce a method to estimate the uncertainty of the compositeness by treating the higher-order correction terms as a complex quantity that is varied within a bounded region determined by the typical interaction range and the energy difference between coupled channels. The method is applied to five different parameter sets from chiral SU(3) dynamics analyses of the Lambda(1405) resonance, using the eigenenergy and the I=0 Kbar N scattering length from each set.

## Context

This work extends Weinberg's original weak-binding relation (Weinberg, 1965), which was used to show the deuteron is a proton-neutron composite, to unstable quasibound states relevant for exotic hadron candidates. The method enables model-independent determination of compositeness from experimental observables when the state is weakly bound, and provides a systematic error estimation procedure. The application to Lambda(1405) builds on chiral SU(3) dynamics studies by Ikeda (2012), Mai (2012), Guo (2012), and Mai (2014), and the results consistently indicate Kbar N composite dominance across all five parameter sets.
