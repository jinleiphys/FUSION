# Phases of 2d massless QCD with qubit regularization

> We investigate the possibility of reproducing the continuum physics of 2d SU(N) gauge theory coupled to a single flavor of massless Dirac fermions using qubit regularization. The continuum theory is described by N free fermions in the ultraviolet (UV) and a coset Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) model in the infrared (IR). In this work, we explore how well these features can be reproduced using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian with a finite-dimensional link Hilbert space and a generalized Hubbard coupling. Using strong coupling expansions, we show that our model exhibits a gapped dimer phase and another phase described by a spin-chain. Furthermore, for N=2, using tensor network methods, we show that there is a second-order phase transition between these two phases. The critical theory at the transition can be understood as an SU(2)_1 WZW model, using which we determine the phase diagram of our model quantitatively. Using the confinement properties of the model we argue how the UV physics of free fermions could also emerge, but may require further modifications to our model.

## Key claim
The authors show that a qubit-regularized model of 2d massless QCD with a finite-dimensional link Hilbert space can reproduce the SU(2)_1 WZW fixed point in the infrared, but reproducing the free-fermion ultraviolet fixed point may require further modifications to the model beyond the generalized Hubbard coupling studied here.

## Method
The authors construct a Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian with a finite-dimensional link Hilbert space and add a generalized Hubbard coupling, then analyze the model using strong coupling expansions for general N and tensor network (DMRG) methods for N=2. They determine the phase diagram by studying the second-order phase transition between a gapped dimer phase and a spin-chain phase, identifying the critical theory as an SU(2)_1 WZW model through its central charge and scaling properties.

## Key numbers
- Central charge of the free-fermion fixed point: c_free = N (e.g., c_free = 2 for N=2)
- Central charge of the WZW fixed point: c_WZW = 1 (for any N)
- For N=2, the beta functions for the couplings lambda_c and lambda_s are d(lambda)/d(ln mu) = -(1/(2 pi)) lambda^2 for each
- For N>=3, the beta function for the isotropic coupling lambda is d(lambda)/d(ln mu) = -(N-1)/(2 pi) lambda^2
- The structure constant sum for so(2N) is sum_{b,c} (f^{abc})^2 = 2N-2

## Context
This work extends the D-theory approach of Brower, Chandrasekharan, and Wiese (2003) and the qubit regularization perspective of Singh and Chandrasekharan (2019) to 2d SU(N) gauge theory with massless fermions. It contrasts with the traditional truncation approach of Byrnes (2005), Tagliacozzo (2014), and others who treat the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian as a target model and assume truncation errors must be removed. The results enable future studies of whether qubit models with finite-dimensional local Hilbert spaces can reproduce asymptotically free theories, building on evidence from spin models by Bhattacharya (2020) and Maiti (2023).
