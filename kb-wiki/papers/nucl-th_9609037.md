# Contribution of real and virtual Pions to nuclear Photoabsorption at Intermediate Energies

> The universal curve $σ/A$ of nuclear photoabsorption is investigated within a Fermi gas model of nuclear matter. An energy range from pion threshold up to 400 MeV is considered. The interactions between nucleon, pion, $Δ$-isobar and photon are considered in the non-relativistic approximation with corrections of the order $1/M$ taken into account with respect to proton mass. Analytical expressions are obtained, in which the influence of nuclear correlations, two-nucleon contributions and relativistic corrections is studied explicitely. An extension of the model calculation to nucleon knock-out reactions is discussed.

## Key claim

The universal curve σ/A of nuclear photoabsorption can be fully explained by a simple diagrammatic approach using point-like nucleons and Δ-isobars interacting via pion exchange within a Fermi gas model, without needing explicit ρ-meson contributions, provided a low cut-off parameter (Λ = 800 MeV) is used in the vertex form factor.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic Fermi gas model of nuclear matter with static Hamiltonians for πNN, γNΔ, and πNΔ interactions, including first-order relativistic corrections of order 1/M for the one-nucleon process. They derived analytical expressions for the one-nucleon and two-nucleon contributions to the total photoabsorption cross section, separating each into resonant and non-resonant parts, and evaluated the integrals over nucleon momenta using trace methods and the standard lowest-order central correlation function of a Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameter Λ in the dipole form factor was taken to be 800 MeV, which gave the best agreement with experimental data.
- The Fermi momentum p_F corresponds to an average kinetic energy of approximately 37 MeV (p_F^2/2M ≈ 37 MeV).
- The Δ-isobar mass difference Δ = 292 MeV and the Δ-isobar width Γ = 115 MeV were used.
- The coupling constants used were f^2/(4π) = 0.08, f_Δ = 2, and f_{γNΔ} = 0.35.
- The one-nucleon and two-nucleon parts of the cross section are equally important at energies around 250 MeV.
- Nucleon correlations modify the one-nucleon result by about 15 per cent.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Wakamatsu and Matsumoto (1980s) by treating corrections perturbatively rather than introducing a phenomenological potential, and focuses on total photoabsorption rather than differential cross sections. It provides a flexible diagrammatic framework that can be used as a starting point for investigating additional nuclear or nucleonic effects, and the analytical expressions allow extension to nucleon knock-out reactions such as (γ,pn) and (γ,pp) processes.
