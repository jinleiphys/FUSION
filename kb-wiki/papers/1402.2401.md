# Kinetic description of mixtures of anisotropic fluids

> A simple system of coupled kinetic equations for quark and gluon anisotropic systems is solved numerically. The solutions are compared with the predictions of the anisotropic hydrodynamics describing a mixture of anisotropic fluids. We find that the solutions of the kinetic equations can be well reproduced by anisotropic hydrodynamics if the initial distribution are oblate for both quarks and gluons. On the other hand, the solutions of the kinetic equations have a different qualitative behavior from those obtained in anisotropic hydrodynamics if the initial configurations are oblate-prolate or prolate-prolate. This suggests that an extension of the anisotropic hydrodynamics scheme for the mixture of anisotropic fluids is needed, where higher moments of the kinetic equations are used and present simplifications are avoided.

# Kinetic description of mixtures of anisotropic fluids

## Key claim

The solutions of coupled kinetic equations for quark and gluon anisotropic systems can be well reproduced by anisotropic hydrodynamics only when both initial distributions are oblate (positive anisotropy parameters); for oblate-prolate or prolate-prolate initial configurations, the kinetic and hydrodynamic solutions differ qualitatively, indicating that the anisotropic hydrodynamics scheme for mixtures requires extension using higher moments of the kinetic equations.

## Method

The authors solved a system of three coupled boost-invariant kinetic equations of relaxation-time form for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, using the Romatschke-Strickland form for initial distribution functions with separate anisotropy parameters for quarks ($\xi_q$) and gluons ($\xi_g$). The effective temperature was determined by the Landau matching condition, and baryon number conservation was enforced through the chemical potential. The kinetic results for transverse pressure to energy density ratio ($P_T/\varepsilon$) and longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio ($P_L/P_T$) were compared to the predictions of anisotropic hydrodynamics for a quark-gluon mixture as derived in Florkowski (2012).

## Key numbers

- Initial time $\tau_0 = 0.1$ fm/c and constant relaxation time $\tau_{\rm eq} = 0.25$ fm/c used for all calculations
- For the oblate-oblate case ($\xi_g=10$, $\xi_q=1$), good agreement found between kinetic and hydrodynamic results for global quantities
- For the oblate-prolate case ($\xi_g=10$, $\xi_q=-0.5$), qualitative disagreement observed: hydrodynamic solutions show exponential decay of anisotropies while kinetic solutions do not
- For the prolate-prolate case ($\xi_g=-0.25$, $\xi_q=-0.5$), similar qualitative disagreement: hydrodynamic anisotropy parameters decay exponentially while kinetic results show power-law behavior
- Finite initial baryon density $b_0=1$ fm$^{-3}$ produced negligible effects compared to zero baryon density case
- Internal degrees of freedom: $g_q=6$ for quarks (color and isospin), $g_g=8$ for gluons (color), giving ratio $r = g_g/(2g_q) = 2/3$

## Context

This work extends the comparison of anisotropic hydrodynamics with kinetic theory from one-component systems, previously studied by Florkowski (2013), to mixtures of anisotropic fluids as introduced by Florkowski (2012). The results show that the anisotropic hydrodynamics scheme for mixtures, which assumed equal transverse-momentum scales for quarks and gluons and used only zeroth and first moments of the kinetic equations, is insufficient for mixed oblate-prolate or prolate-prolate initial configurations. This motivates developing an extended scheme incorporating higher moments of the kinetic equation and allowing independent evolution of the transverse-momentum scales for different particle species.
