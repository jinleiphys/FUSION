# Neutron-${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ scattering: Towards including realistic interactions

> Low-energy neutron-${}^{19}$C scattering is studied in the three-body $n + n + {}^{18}$C model using a realistic $nn$ potential and a number of shallow and deep $n$-${}^{18}$C potentials, the latter supporting deeply-bound Pauli-forbidden states that are projected out. Exact Faddeev-type three-body scattering equations for transition operators including two- and three-body forces are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. Phase shift, inelasticity parameter, and cross sections are calculated. For the elastic $n$-${}^{19}$C scattering in the $J^Π=0^+$ partial wave the signatures of the Efimov physics, i.e., the pole in the effective-range expansion and the elastic cross section minimum, are confirmed for both shallow and deep models, but with clear quantitative differences between them, indicating the importance of a proper treatment of deeply-bound Pauli-forbidden states. In contrast, the inelasticity parameter is mostly correlated with the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the ${}^{19}$C bound state. Finally, in the regime of very weak ${}^{19}$C binding and near-threshold (bound or virtual) excited ${}^{20}$C state the standard Efimovian behaviour of the $n$-${}^{19}$C scattering length and cross section was confirmed, resolving the discrepancies between earlier studies by other authors [I. Mazumdar, A. R. P. Rau, V. S. Bhasin, Phys. Rev. Lett. 97 (2006) 062503; M. T. Yamashita, T. Frederico, L. Tomio, Phys. Rev. Lett. 99 (2007) 269201].

## Key claim

The signatures of Efimov physics (a pole in the effective-range expansion and a minimum in the elastic cross section) are confirmed for both shallow and deep potential models of neutron-${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ scattering, but with clear quantitative differences between them, indicating the importance of a proper treatment of deeply-bound Pauli-forbidden states.

## Method

Low-energy neutron-${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ scattering was studied in a three-body $n + n + {}^{18}\mathrm{C}$ model using the realistic CD Bonn potential for the $nn$ interaction and a number of shallow and deep $n$-${}^{18}\mathrm{C}$ Woods-Saxon potentials, the latter supporting deeply-bound Pauli-forbidden states that were projected out. Exact Faddeev-type three-body scattering equations for transition operators (the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas version), extended to include three-body forces, were solved in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. Phase shifts, inelasticity parameters, and cross sections were calculated for the $J^\Pi=0^+$ partial wave and compared across models.

## Key numbers

- The ${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ binding energy was taken as $S_n = 0.58(9)$ MeV (the accepted experimental value from Audi et al. 2012).
- The ${}^{20}\mathrm{C}$ two-neutron separation energy was fixed at $S_{2n} = 3.5$ MeV.
- The $n$-${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ singlet scattering length $a$ ranged from $-5.369$ fm (DPc model) to $-9.802$ fm (Y model).
- The pole position $E_0$ in the effective-range expansion ranged from 0.20626 MeV (DPa) to 0.39663 MeV (WS model).
- The $n$-${}^{18}\mathrm{C}$ scattering length $a_{nA}$ ranged from 7.01 fm (DPc) to 9.23 fm (DPa and DPp).
- The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the ${}^{19}\mathrm{C}$ bound state ranged from 0.657 fm$^{-1/2}$ (DPc) to 0.948 fm$^{-1/2}$ (DPa and DPp).
- The bound excited ${}^{20}\mathrm{C}$ Efimov state appears when $S_n$ is reduced to 0.07-0.09 MeV and $S_{2n}$ to 1.4-1.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the ${}^{18}\mathrm{C}+n+n$ system that used simple rank-one separable potentials with Yamaguchi form factors, such as those by Mazumdar, Rau, and Bhasin (2006) and Yamashita, Frederico, and Tomio (2007), which disagreed on whether the excited ${}^{20}\mathrm{C}$ Efimov state becomes a virtual state or a continuum resonance. By using more realistic interactions including a high-precision $nn$ potential and proper treatment of Pauli-forbidden states, the present work resolves this discrepancy in favor of the virtual-state interpretation (supporting Yamashita et al. 2007 and Shalchi et al. 2017) and excludes the near-threshold resonance predicted by Mazumdar et al. (2006). The results enable future studies of $d$-wave corrections to the $s$-wave dominated Efimov physics in this system.
