# Neutron matter from chiral effective field theory interactions

> The neutron-matter equation of state constrains the properties of many physical systems over a wide density range and can be studied systematically using chiral effective field theory (EFT). In chiral EFT, all many-body forces among neutrons are predicted to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). We present details and additional results of the first complete N3LO calculation of the neutron-matter energy, which includes the subleading three-nucleon as well as the leading four-nucleon forces, and provides theoretical uncertainties. In addition, we discuss the impact of our results for astrophysics: for the supernova equation of state, the symmetry energy and its density derivative, and for the structure of neutron stars. Finally, we give a first estimate for the size of the N3LO many-body contributions to the energy of symmetric nuclear matter, which shows that their inclusion will be important in nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of the neutron-matter energy at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in chiral effective field theory, including subleading three-nucleon and leading four-nucleon forces, but the results carry theoretical uncertainties from cutoff variation, the range of low-energy couplings \(c_1\) and \(c_3\), and the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential, with the convergence of the many-body expansion depending on the specific potential and cutoff.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutron-matter energy per particle at the Hartree-Fock level and include contributions beyond Hartree-Fock using many-body perturbation theory up to third order, with nucleon-nucleon (NN), three-nucleon (3N), and four-nucleon (4N) interactions from chiral EFT at N3LO. They use three perturbative N3LO NN potentials (EGM 450/500 MeV, EGM 450/700 MeV, and EM 500 MeV) and vary the 3N/4N cutoff between 2.0 and 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\) and the \(c_i\) couplings over ranges from a high-order analysis by Krebs, Gasparyan, and Epelbaum (2012). The N2LO 3N forces are included as density-dependent two-body interactions in the second-order calculations, while N3LO 3N and 4N forces are evaluated only at Hartree-Fock level.

## Key numbers

*   NN-only energy per particle at saturation density \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\): 14.7 MeV for EGM 450/500, 12.1 MeV for EGM 450/700, and 12.9 MeV for EM 500 MeV N3LO potentials.
*   Inclusion of N2LO 3N forces adds \(7 \pm 1.5\) MeV per particle at saturation density using the larger N3LO \(c_i\) values.
*   Maximal energy difference between second- and third-order contributions at saturation density for NN-only: 0.8 MeV (EGM 450/500), 0.4 MeV (EGM 450/700), 1.1 MeV (EM 500); with N2LO 3N forces included: 0.6 MeV (EGM 450/500), 0.4 MeV (EGM 450/700), 1.7 MeV (EM 500).
*   For the EGM 450/500 potential with N2LO 3N forces at \(\Lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(c_1 = -0.75\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.77\) GeV\(^{-1}\), the kinetic energy is 35.93 MeV, first-order NN energy is -13.51 MeV, first-order 3N energy is 7.95 MeV, and second-order NN-NN contribution is -8.37 MeV at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous neutron-matter calculations using chiral EFT interactions at N2LO (Hebeler et al., 2010) by including the complete N3LO many-body forces, which were derived by Bernard et al. (2008, 2011) and Epelbaum (2007). The results provide theoretical uncertainties for the neutron-matter equation of state that can be applied to constrain the symmetry energy, its density derivative, and neutron-star structure, and give a first estimate that N3LO 3N forces will be important in nuclear structure calculations for symmetric nuclear matter.
