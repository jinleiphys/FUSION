# Distribution of multiple scatterings in proton-nucleus collisions at high energy

> We consider proton-nucleus collisions at high energy in the Color Glass Condensate framework, and extract from the gluon production cross-section the probabilities of having a definite number of multiple scatterings in the nucleus. Various properties of the distribution of the number of multiple scatterings are studied, and we conclude that events in which the momentum of a hard jet is compensated by many much softer particles on the opposite side are very unlikely except for extreme values of the saturation momentum. In the same framework, we also investigate the possibility to estimate the impact parameter of a proton-nucleus collision, from the measure of the multiplicity of its final state.

## Key claim

The paper extracts from the Color Glass Condensate gluon production cross-section the probabilities for a gluon to undergo a definite number of multiple scatterings in the nucleus, and concludes that events where a hard jet's momentum is balanced by many much softer particles on the opposite side are very unlikely except for extreme values of the saturation momentum, the abstract's claim is conditional on the saturation momentum not being extreme.

## Method

The authors start from the CGC formula for single gluon production in proton-nucleus collisions and rewrite the function \(C(k_\perp)\) (the Fourier transform of a Wilson line correlator) in a Glauber-like form that explicitly sums over the number \(n\) of scatterings. They construct a generating function \(F(z,k_\perp|k_\perp^{\rm min})\) for the probabilities \(P_n(k_\perp|k_\perp^{\rm min})\) of having exactly \(n\) scatterings with momentum transfer above a threshold \(k_\perp^{\rm min}\), and evaluate it numerically in the McLerran-Venugopalan model and in an asymptotic Gaussian model with geometrical scaling.

## Key numbers

- For \(Q_s^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) and produced gluon momentum \(k_\perp = 10\) GeV, the probability distribution \(P_n\) is well described by a shifted Poisson distribution \(P_n = \overline{n}^{\,n-1} e^{-\overline{n}}/(n-1)!\) with \(\overline{n} \equiv g^4 N_c \mu_0^2 / (8\pi (k_\perp^{\rm min})^2)\); the numerical fit at \(k_\perp^{\rm min}=0.4\) GeV deviates by about 25% from the predicted \(\overline{n}\) value.
- The average number of recoils above threshold \(N(k_\perp|k_\perp^{\rm min})\) is approximately \(1 + g^4 N_c \mu_0^2 / (8\pi (k_\perp^{\rm min})^2)\) for \(k_\perp^{\rm min} \ll k_\perp\), and is very close to unity for any \(k_\perp^{\rm min}\) satisfying \(Q_s \ll k_\perp^{\rm min} \lesssim k_\perp\).
- The saturation momentum in the MV model is given by \(Q_s^2 = (g^4 C_f / 4\pi) \mu_0^2 \ln(\mu_0^2 / \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2)\).
- In the asymptotic model, the anomalous dimension is \(\gamma \approx 0.64\) and the constant \(c \approx 4.84\).

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework for gluon production in proton-nucleus collisions (Blaizot, Gelis, Venugopalan 2004) by providing a probabilistic interpretation of multiple scatterings via a Glauber decomposition. It contrasts with earlier studies of back-to-back correlations (Baier, Kovner, Wiedemann 2003; Kharzeev, Levin, McLerran 2004) that examined angular correlations between pairs of hard particles; instead, the authors count the number of recoils above a momentum threshold. The results enable quantitative predictions for whether monojet-like events (a single high-\(p_\perp\) jet with many soft balancing particles) can occur in proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and also allow exploring the relationship between final-state multiplicity and impact parameter.
