# Unconventional rotational responses of hadronic superfluids in a neutron star caused by strong entrainment and a $Σ^-$ hyperon gap

> I show that the usual model of the rotational response of a neutron star, which predicts rotation-induced neutronic vortices and no rotation-induced protonic vortices, does not hold (i) beyond a certain threshold of entrainment interaction strength nor (ii) in case of nonzero $Σ^-$ hyperon gap. I demonstrate that in both these cases the rotational response involves creation of phase windings in electrically charged condensate. Lattices of bound states of vortices which are caused these effects can (for a range of parameters) strongly reduce the interaction between rotation-induced vortices with magnetic-field carrying superconducting components.

# Unconventional rotational responses of hadronic superfluids in a neutron star caused by strong entrainment and a Σ⁻ hyperon gap

## Key claim

The standard model predicting that neutron star rotation induces only neutronic vortices and no protonic vortices fails under two conditions: (i) when the entrainment (drag) strength exceeds a threshold of |ρₚₙ|/ρₚₚ > 1/2, and (ii) when a nonzero Σ⁻ hyperon gap is present. In both cases, rotation creates phase windings in the electrically charged condensate, and the resulting composite vortex lattices can strongly reduce the interaction between rotation-induced vortices and magnetic-field-carrying superconducting components.

## Method

The author analyzes the free energy functional of a mixture of superfluid neutrons, superconducting protons, and normal electrons, including the entrainment (drag) term ρₚₙ vₚ·vₙ that couples the superfluid velocities. The thermodynamic stability of different vortex types is evaluated by comparing free energies in a rotating frame, considering both (0,1) vortices (phase winding only in neutrons) and (1,1) composite vortices (phase winding in both neutrons and protons). The analysis is extended to the case of a second charged condensate of Σ⁻ hyperon Cooper pairs, where the free energy functional permits gauge-invariant combinations decoupled from the vector potential.

## Key numbers

- The drag strength can be as high as ρₚₙ ≈ −0.7 ρₚₚ, based on microscopic calculations giving effective proton masses m*ₚ ranging from approximately 0.3 mₚ to 0.9 mₚ.
- The threshold for thermodynamic instability of pure neutronic (0,1) vortices is |ρₚₙ|/ρₚₚ > 1/2.
- The magnetic field penetration length λ is of order 10–100 fm in the standard picture.
- The energy of intersection between a protonic flux tube and a neutronic vortex is estimated as Eⁱ_ᴄₕ ≈ 5 MeV, following Ruderman and Link.
- The density of rotation-induced vortices in a mixture of charged condensates is ν = (mₚ + m_Σ)Ω/(πℏ).

## Context

This work extends the standard neutron star model of Pethick, Ruderman, and Sauls, which assumes rotation induces only neutronic vortices and magnetic field induces only protonic vortices. It directly addresses the inconsistency raised by Link (2003), who argued that strong pinning between protonic and neutronic vortices is incompatible with observed slow pulsar precession. The paper proposes two alternative mechanisms, strong entrainment beyond a threshold and the presence of a Σ⁻ hyperon condensate (following Jones and hyperon gap calculations), that could produce composite vortices and reduce electromagnetic coupling, in contrast to the type-I superconductor scenario proposed by Link, Zhitnitsky, and Alford. The results enable future work on whether the reduced interaction strength can fall below the temperature scale in some regions of a neutron star, potentially leading to decoupling of superfluid and flux-bearing components.
