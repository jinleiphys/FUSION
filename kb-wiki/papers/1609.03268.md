# Beta spectrum of unique first-forbidden decays as a novel test for fundamental symmetries

> Within the Standard Model, the weak interaction of quarks and leptons is characterized by certain symmetry properties, such as maximal breaking of parity and favored helicity. These are related to the $V-A$ structure of the weak interaction. These characteristics were discovered by studying correlations in the directions of the outgoing leptons in nuclear beta decays. These days, correlation measurements in nuclear beta decays are intensively studied to probe for signatures for deviations from these symmetries, which are an indication of Beyond Standard Model physics. We show that the structure of the energy spectrum of emitted electrons in unique first-forbidden $β$-decays is sensitive to the symmetries of the weak interaction, and thus can be used as a novel probe of physics beyond the standard model. Furthermore, the energy spectrum gives constraints both in the case of right and left coupling of the new symmetry currents. We show that a measurement with modest energy resolution of about 20 keV is expected to lead to new constraints on beyond the standard model interactions with tensor symmetry.

## Key claim
The energy spectrum of electrons emitted in unique first-forbidden beta decays (transitions with ΔJ=2 and parity change) is sensitive to beyond-Standard-Model tensor interactions, and a measurement with modest energy resolution of about 20 keV can constrain both left-handed and right-handed neutrino couplings, unlike allowed decays which cannot separately constrain right-handed couplings.

## Method
The authors derive the differential decay rate for unique first-forbidden beta decays including beyond-Standard-Model tensor couplings, then integrate over lepton angles to obtain the energy spectrum. They simulate spectra with 10 million events at an endpoint energy of 3.5 MeV, apply 20 keV energy resolution, and perform Bayesian analysis using the JAGS framework to extract the two uncorrelated BSM parameters (C_T+C_T')/C_A and (C_T-C_T')/C_A. Recoil corrections are estimated using chiral effective field theory to be at the 0.2-0.4% level.

## Key numbers
- Energy resolution of approximately 20 keV is sufficient to constrain beyond-Standard-Model tensor interactions
- Recoil corrections to the leading-order spectrum are naturally of order 0.2-0.4% compared to leading terms
- Radiative and recoil corrections together are expected to allow theoretical prediction accurate to approximately 10^{-4}
- Present limits on deviations from Standard Model predictions in beta decays are of order 0.1-1%, yielding a limit on new physics scale of approximately 1-10 TeV
- The axial constant g_A ≈ 1.27 and nucleon magnetic moment κ_V ≈ 4.70 are used in the one-body current operators
- The Q-value for unique first-forbidden decays is approximately 10 MeV, leading to a factor of 20 suppression of higher-order multipoles
- The beta decay of ⁹⁰Y has an endpoint energy of approximately 2.3 MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of beta-decay correlations for testing fundamental symmetries, which began with the Wu experiment (1957) proving parity violation. Current experiments mostly study allowed beta decays using trapped ions, but these cannot separately constrain right-handed and left-handed couplings due to inconsistencies in energy averaging, as noted by PhysRevC.94.035503. The proposed method enables simultaneous extraction of both C_T+C_T' and C_T-C_T' from a single spectrum measurement without trapping or cooling, and can be applied to the abundant unique first-forbidden decays in nature, such as ¹⁸⁷Re (studied for neutrino mass effects by PhysRevC.83.045502) and ⁹⁰Y.
