# The Born-Oppenheimer approximation in an effective field theory language

> The Born--Oppenheimer approximation is the standard tool for the study of molecular systems. It is founded on the observation that the energy scale of the electron dynamics in a molecule is larger than that of the nuclei. A very similar physical picture can be used to describe QCD states containing heavy quarks as well as light-quarks or gluonic excitations. In this work, we derive the Born--Oppenheimer approximation for QED molecular systems in an effective field theory framework by sequentially integrating out degrees of freedom living at energies above the typical energy scale where the dynamics of the heavy degrees of freedom occurs. In particular, we compute the matching coefficients of the effective field theory for the case of the $H^+_2$ diatomic molecule that are relevant to compute its spectrum up to ${\cal O}(mα^5)$. Ultrasoft photon loops contribute at this order, being ultimately responsible for the molecular Lamb shift. In the effective field theory the scaling of all the operators is homogeneous, which facilitates the determination of all the relevant contributions, an observation that may become useful for high-precision calculations. Using the above case as a guidance, we construct under some conditions an effective field theory for QCD states formed by a color-octet heavy quark-antiquark pair bound with a color-octet light-quark pair or excited gluonic state, highlighting the similarities and differences between the QED and QCD systems. Assuming that the multipole expansion is applicable, we construct the heavy-quark potential up to next-to-leading order in the multipole expansion in terms of nonperturbative matching coefficients to be obtained from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The Born-Oppenheimer approximation emerges as the leading-order approximation in a systematically constructed effective field theory (BOEFT) for diatomic molecules, where the kinetic energy of the relative motion of the nuclei is suppressed by a factor of \(\sqrt{m/M} \sim \alpha^{3/4}\) relative to the ultrasoft scale, justifying the adiabatic separation; the paper explicitly computes matching coefficients for the \(H_2^+\) molecule up to \(\mathcal{O}(m\alpha^5)\), where ultrasoft photon loops generate the molecular Lamb shift.

## Method

The authors start from pNRQED for a system of two heavy nuclei and one electron, then integrate out the ultrasoft scale (the electron binding energy \(\sim m\alpha^2\)) to obtain a Born-Oppenheimer effective field theory (BOEFT) whose degrees of freedom are the nuclei and photons with energies at or below the scale of the nuclear vibrational motion. The matching is performed by expanding the pNRQED field in eigenfunctions of the leading-order electronic Hamiltonian \(h_0(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{z})\), with tree-level matching sufficient up to \(\mathcal{O}(m\alpha^4)\) and one-loop ultrasoft photon contributions computed at \(\mathcal{O}(m\alpha^5)\). The same EFT framework is then extended to QCD systems containing a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a color-octet state bound with light quarks or gluonic excitations, assuming the multipole expansion is applicable and expressing the heavy-quark potential in terms of nonperturbative matching coefficients to be obtained from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The spectrum of the \(H_2^+\) molecule is computed up to \(\mathcal{O}(m\alpha^5)\), where \(m\) is the electron mass and \(\alpha \approx 1/137\) is the fine-structure constant.
- The electron-nucleus relative momentum scales as \(m\alpha\) and the inverse relative distance \(1/|\mathbf{z}|\) scales as \(m\alpha\).
- The kinetic energy of the relative motion of the nuclei scales as \(m\alpha^2 \sqrt{m/M} \sim m\alpha^{11/4}\), where \(M\) is the nuclear mass.
- The ground-state vibrational energy of the nuclei is \(E_0 \sim m\alpha^2 \sqrt{m/M}\).
- The equilibrium separation of the nuclei is \(r_0 \sim 1/(m\alpha)\).
- The nonadiabatic coupling \(C^{\text{nad}}_{\kappa\kappa'}(\mathbf{r})\) has its largest contribution of \(\mathcal{O}(m\alpha^2 (m/M)^{3/4})\) from the radial piece of the second term in Eq. (nonadia).
- The matching coefficient \(c_D = 1 + (\alpha/\pi)(8/3 \log(m/\mu))\) (renormalized in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme), \(c_S = 1 + \alpha/\pi\), and \(d_2 = \alpha/(60\pi)\) appear in the NLO electron-nucleus potential.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to the hydrogen atom in pNRQED (Pineda, 1997; Pineda, 1998) to the diatomic molecule \(H_2^+\), and provides the first full EFT treatment that integrates out not only the hard scale (as in NRQED) but also the soft and ultrasoft scales, yielding homogeneous operator scaling. The QCD analog builds on proposals for applying the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to heavy-quark systems (Brambilla, 2008; Braaten, 2014) and extends the EFT recently constructed for quarkonium hybrids (Berwein, 2015); it enables systematic high-precision calculations of molecular spectra in QED and provides a framework for computing nonperturbative heavy-quark potentials from lattice QCD for exotic hadrons such as tetraquarks and hybrids.
