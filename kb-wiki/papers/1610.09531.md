# Thermal dynamics on the lattice with exponentially improved accuracy

> We present a novel simulation prescription for thermal quantum fields on a lattice that operates directly in imaginary frequency space. By distinguishing initial conditions from quantum dynamics it provides access to correlation functions also outside of the conventional Matsubara frequencies $ω_n=2πn T$. In particular it resolves their frequency dependence between $ω=0$ and $ω_1=2πT$, where the thermal physics $ω\sim T$ of e.g.~transport phenomena is dominantly encoded. Real-time spectral functions are related to these correlators via an integral transform with rational kernel, so their unfolding is exponentially improved compared to Euclidean simulations. We demonstrate this improvement within a $0+1$-dimensional scalar field theory and show that spectral features inaccessible in standard Euclidean simulations are quantitatively captured.

## Key claim

By simulating thermal quantum fields directly in imaginary frequency space rather than on the compact Euclidean time axis, the authors achieve access to correlation functions at arbitrary frequencies between the conventional Matsubara frequencies, which together with the rational Källén-Lehmann kernel yields exponentially improved accuracy in spectral function reconstruction compared to standard Euclidean simulations.

## Method

The authors formulate a lattice simulation that separates the thermal initial conditions (sampled via stochastic quantization on a compact Euclidean time axis) from the quantum dynamics (simulated along a rotated Schwinger-Keldysh contour in imaginary frequency space). The quadratic part of the action is treated in frequency space while interactions are handled via discrete Fourier transforms back to time space, with the thermal initial condition entering only through the interaction term. The approach is tested in a 0+1-dimensional scalar field theory with quartic interaction, using stochastic quantization with Langevin time to generate 10^6 configurations for each parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Temperature set to β=1 and mass m=1 for all simulations
- For the interacting case λ=24, simulations performed at N_τ=16, 32, 64, and 128 points on the compact Euclidean axis
- Imaginary frequency simulations used N_ω=16, 32, 64, 128, 256, and 512 points
- Total of N_conf=10^6 samples collected, yielding relative statistical error ΔG/G ~ 10^{-3}
- Quantum mechanical reference computation used 32 energy eigenfunctions with N_t=3.2×10^4 time steps of Δt=0.05
- Spectral reconstruction discretized μ∈[10^{-3}, 200] with N_μ=4000 points plus N_μ^HP=1000 points in a high-frequency window

## Context

This work addresses two fundamental limitations of conventional lattice simulations (e.g., lattice QCD as reviewed by Smit (2002)): the finite extent of the Euclidean time axis τ∈[0,1/T] which restricts access to discrete Matsubara frequencies ω_n=2πnT, and the exponentially hard ill-posed inverse problem relating Euclidean correlators to spectral functions via the kernel ~exp(-μτ) as described by Jarrell (1996). The rational kernel approach provides an analytic implementation of ideas behind the Backus-Gilbert method (Brandt, 2015) and Sumudu transform (Pederiva, 2014). The method enables improved computations of transport coefficients and heavy quark spectral functions, and the authors state it can be extended to Abelian and non-Abelian gauge theories.
