# Description of nuclear photoexcitation by Lorentzian expressions for electric dipole photon strength function

> The description of photoabsorption cross-sections of cold nuclei by closed-form Lorentzian models of photon strength functions for photoexcitation by electric dipole gamma-rays is considered. Systematics of the GDR parameters are given and input parameters of different analytical models are discussed The experimental data are compared with theoretical calculations for even-even nuclei using criteria of minimum of both least-square value and root-mean-square deviation factor. Simple extensions of the models with energy-dependent widths to high gamma-ray energies $\gtrsim $ 30MeV which hold the energy-weighted sum rule for E1 gamma-transitions in good approximation are proposed and tested.

# Description of nuclear photoexcitation by Lorentzian expressions for electric dipole photon strength function

## Key claim

Closed-form Lorentzian models (SLO, GLO, SMLO, TLO) can describe photoabsorption cross-sections of cold even-even nuclei up to approximately 30 MeV, but the energy-dependent widths in SMLO and GLO models must be saturated above the GDR energy to avoid violating the energy-weighted sum rule; the proposed extended models (SMLOe and GLOe) with constant width above the GDR energy approximately satisfy the sum rule while preserving agreement with data below 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors compared experimental photoabsorption cross-sections from the EXFOR database for 88 even-even isotopes (from ²⁴Mg to ²³⁸U) with theoretical calculations using four Lorentzian-type models: Standard Lorentzian (SLO), Generalized Lorentzian (GLO), Simplified Modified Lorentzian (SMLO), and Triple Lorentzian (TLO). They derived systematics for GDR parameters (energies, widths, and weights) by least-square fitting to recommended experimental GDR parameters from a prior database (Plujko 2018). For the TLO model, three sets of deformation parameters were used: from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (TLO(1)), from constrained HFB with five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (TLO(2)), and from fitted parameters in the literature (TLO(3)). The quality of description was evaluated using both the least-square deviation (χ²) and the root-mean-square deviation factor (f_α). The authors proposed extended SMLO and GLO models (SMLOe and GLOe) where the energy-dependent width is held constant at the GDR resonance value for gamma-ray energies above the GDR energy.

## Key numbers

- 88 even-even isotopes from ²⁴Mg to ²³⁸U were analyzed for photoabsorption cross-section comparison
- Systematics for SLO model GDR energy: e₁ = 130.0 ± 0.9 MeV, e₂ = 9.0 ± 0.2 (dimensionless) in the formula E_r = e₁·(1-I²)^(1/2)·A^(-1/3) / (1+e₂·A^(-1/3))^(1/2)
- Systematics for SMLO model GDR energy: e₁ = 128.0 ± 0.9 MeV, e₂ = 8.5 ± 0.2 (dimensionless) in the same formula
- Width systematics for SMLO: c = 0.42 ± 0.05, d = 0.90 ± 0.04 in Γ_r = c·(E_r)^d (MeV)
- Width systematics for SLO: c = 0.32 ± 0.03, d = 0.98 ± 0.03 in Γ_r = c·(E_r)^d (MeV)
- Sum of weights s^α = 1.2 for SLO and SMLO models
- Sum of weights s^TLO = 1.02 for TLO model
- TLO width formula: Γ_j^TLO = 0.045·(E_r,j^TLO)^(1.6) MeV
- Relative deviation of experimental average GDR energies between SLO and SMLO does not exceed approximately 6%
- Relative deviation of experimental GDR widths between SLO and SMLO can reach approximately 40%
- Energy-dependent relative uncertainties for experimental data were assumed: minimum 10% near GDR peaks, maximum 50% on GDR tails

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of Lorentzian PSF models previously performed by Plujko (2018) for 162 nuclei, where it was shown that SLO model low-energy tails overestimate experimental data without re-adjusting GDR parameters. The present study focuses on even-even nuclei and adds the TLO model (Junker 2008, Goriely 2017) to the comparison. The proposed SMLOe and GLOe models address a known deficiency: energy-dependent widths that increase without bound violate the energy-weighted sum rule, as noted by Goriely (2017). The systematics for GDR energies follow the liquid droplet hydrodynamic model of Myers (1977) and sum rule prescriptions of Lipparini (1988, 1989). The results enable more reliable modeling of E1 photon strength functions for nuclear reaction codes such as TALYS and EMPIRE, particularly for applications in nuclear astrophysics where extrapolation to exotic nuclei is needed.
