# A comparison study of collisions at relativistic energies involving light nuclei

> We present extensive comparisons of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at the center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and $^{208}$Pb+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=68.5$ GeV as well as $^{20}$Ne+$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and $^{208}$Pb+$^{20}$Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=68.5$ GeV based on a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. We recommend measuring the ratio of the elliptic flow to the triangular flow, which shows appreciable sensitivity to the structure of light nuclei as also found in other studies. This is especially so if the observable is measured near the target rapidity in $^{208}$Pb+$^{16}$O or $^{208}$Pb+$^{20}$Ne collisions, as originally found in the present study. Our study serves as a useful reference for understanding the structure effect on observables in collisions involving light nuclei under analysis or on the schedule.

## Key claim

The ratio of elliptic flow to triangular flow measured near the target rapidity in asymmetric heavy-light collisions (e.g., Pb+O or Pb+Ne) shows appreciable sensitivity to the structure of light nuclei, but the detailed alpha-cluster structure cannot be distinguished from a deformed Woods-Saxon shape with the same global shape after the complicated partonic and hadronic dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, Pb+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=68.5 GeV, Ne+Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, and Pb+Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=68.5 GeV. Density distributions for O and Ne were constructed from three scenarios: spherical Woods-Saxon, deformed Woods-Saxon (with beta_2 and beta_3 parameters matched to the intrinsic multipole moments of the cluster configurations), and alpha-cluster configurations (tetrahedron for O, bowling-pin for Ne) from a Bloch-Brink wave function approach. Central collisions (0-10%) were selected by ordering charged-particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=68.5 GeV; Ne+Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb+Ne collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=68.5 GeV
- Deformed Woods-Saxon parameters for O: R_0=1.973 fm, d=0.507 fm, beta_2=0, beta_3=0.223
- Deformed Woods-Saxon parameters for Ne: R_0=2.160 fm, d=0.580 fm, beta_2=0.666, beta_3=0.250
- AMPT parameters: strong coupling constant alpha_s=0.33, screening mass mu=3.2 fm^-1, parton scattering cross section=1.5 mb; Lund fragmentation parameters a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV^-2
- The ratio v_2/v_3 near target rapidities (2<eta<5) in Pb+O collisions shows the spherical case giving the largest value, with deformation or alpha-clustered O leading to smaller values
- The ratio v_2/v_3 is enhanced by deformation or alpha-cluster structure in Ne in both Ne+Ne and Pb+Ne collisions, with stronger effect near target rapidities in Pb+Ne collisions

## Context

This work extends the program of extracting nuclear deformation from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to heavy nuclei such as Ru, Zr, Au, U (Zhang 2021, Giacalone 2021, STAR 2024) and Xe (Bally 2021, ATLAS 2022, ALICE 2024), to light nuclei with possible alpha-cluster configurations. It builds on prior studies of O+O collisions (Liu 2025, Prasad 2024, Hu 2025) and the proposed fixed-target Pb+O and Pb+Ne collisions at LHCb (Mariani 2021, LHCb 2022, Giacalone 2024). The finding that the ratio v_2/v_3 near target rapidity is sensitive to light-nucleus structure enables experimental tests with upcoming LHCb fixed-target data, though the inability to distinguish cluster from deformed Woods-Saxon shapes suggests that global deformation rather than detailed clustering is probed.
