# Coupling of Baryon Resonances to the $N ω$ channel

> We estimate the resonance coupling strength $f_{RNω}$ and $f_{RNρ}$ from a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) analysis. The isoscalar and isovector part of the photon coupling are obtained separately from helicity amplitudes. The reliability of this approach is tested by comparing VMD predictions for $f_{RNρ}$ with values obtained from fitting the hadronic decay widths into $N ρ$. A reasonable agreement is found, but VMD tends to underestimate the coupling constants. In order to confirm consistency with experimental data, we calculate the cross-sections for photon-and pion induced reactions within a {\it Breit-Wigner} model. Finally, we study how the properties of $ω$ mesons in nuclear matter are affected from the excitation of resonance-hole loops. For an $ω$ at rest, we find a broadening of about 40 MeV, while at higher momenta the effect of resonance excitations is reduced.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) can provide reasonable lower-limit predictions for the coupling strengths of baryon resonances to the \(N\omega\) channel, but the approach tends to underestimate the coupling constants compared to hadronic decay data, typically working within a factor of two.

## Method

The isoscalar and isovector parts of the photon coupling to baryon resonances were extracted separately from experimental helicity amplitudes for proton and neutron targets, using linear combinations to isolate the isoscalar component. These were then related via VMD relations to the hadronic coupling constants \(f_{RN\omega}\) and \(f_{RN\rho}\), using the known \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) couplings to the photon (\(g_\rho = 2.5\), \(g_\omega = 8.7\)). The reliability of the VMD approach was tested by comparing VMD predictions for \(f_{RN\rho}\) with values obtained from fitting the measured hadronic decay widths into \(N\rho\), using two different parameterizations of the \(\rho\) meson spectral function. Finally, the extracted \(f_{RN\omega}\) values were used in a Breit-Wigner model to calculate cross-sections for \(\pi^- p \rightarrow \omega n\) and \(\gamma p \rightarrow \omega p\), and to compute the in-medium self energy of the \(\omega\) meson in nuclear matter via resonance-hole loop excitations.

## Key numbers

*   For an \(\omega\) meson at rest in nuclear matter, the in-medium broadening is about 40 MeV.
*   The coupling constant \(g_\rho\) (rho meson to photon) is taken as 2.5, and \(g_\omega\) (omega meson to photon) as 8.7.
*   The \(\rho\) meson pole mass is 0.768 GeV and its decay width on the pole mass is 150 MeV.
*   The vacuum decay width of the \(\omega\) meson is 8.41 MeV.
*   The VMD prediction for the \(D_{13}(1520)\) coupling to \(N\omega\) is about 3, compared to a value of about 6.5 from a coupled-channel analysis by Friman et al. and about 2.6 from a quark model calculation by Brack et al.
*   The \(D_{13}(2080)\) resonance is predicted to have an \(\omega\) decay width of about 70 MeV.
*   The contribution of the \(D_{13}(2080)\) to the \(\pi^- p \rightarrow \omega n\) cross-section is about 0.1 mb, roughly 10% of the total cross-section.
*   An additional in-medium width for the \(\omega\) of about 40 MeV at the \(\omega\) mass is estimated from the modification of the \(\rho\) spectral function in the medium.

## Context

This work extends the application of VMD to the \(N\omega\) channel for baryon resonances, following similar analyses for the \(\rho\) meson by Post et al. (1998) and others. It provides coupling constants for subthreshold resonances, which are compared with results from a coupled-channel analysis by Friman et al. and a quark model calculation by Brack et al. The findings enable calculations of the in-medium \(\omega\) meson spectral function, yielding a broadening consistent with results from Klingl et al., Weid et al., and Effe et al., and suggest that the \(D_{13}(1520)\) and \(S_{11}(1650)\) resonances dominate the in-medium self energy, analogous to the role of the \(D_{13}(1520)\) for the \(\rho\) meson.
