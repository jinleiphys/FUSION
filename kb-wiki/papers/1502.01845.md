# Spectroscopy of doubly-charmed baryons from lattice QCD

> We present the ground and excited state spectra of doubly charmed baryons from lattice QCD with dynamical quark fields. Calculations are performed on anisotropic lattices of size 16^3 X 128, with inverse spacing in temporal direction 1/a_t = 5.67(4) GeV and with a pion mass of about 390 MeV. A large set of baryonic operators that respect the symmetries of the lattice yet which retain a memory of their continuum analogues are used. These operators transform as irreducible representations of SU(3) symmetry for flavor, SU(4) symmetry for Dirac spins of quarks and O(3) for spatial symmetry. The distillation method is utilized to generate baryon correlation functions which are analysed using the variational fitting method to extract excited states. The lattice spectra obtained have baryonic states with well-defined total spins up to 7/2 and the pattern of low lying states does not support the diquark picture for doubly charmed baryons. On the contrary the calculated spectra are remarkably similar to the expectations from models with an SU(6)X O(3) symmetry. Various spin dependent energy splittings between the extracted states are also evaluated.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD spectra of doubly-charmed baryons do not support the diquark picture for these states; instead, the pattern of low-lying states is remarkably similar to expectations from models with an SU(6) × O(3) symmetry, though this conclusion is drawn from calculations at a single lattice spacing and a pion mass of about 390 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on anisotropic 16³ × 128 lattices with 2+1 flavors of dynamical clover fermions, using a tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action and anisotropic Shekholeslami-Wohlert fermion action. A large set of baryon operators transforming under irreducible representations of SU(3) flavor, SU(4) Dirac spin, and O(3) spatial symmetry were constructed in the continuum and subduced to lattice irreps; correlation functions were generated via the distillation method with 64 eigenvectors of the gauge-covariant Laplacian, and the variational fitting method was used to extract excited states up to spin 7/2.

## Key numbers

* Inverse temporal lattice spacing: a_t⁻¹ = 5.67(4) GeV, determined from the Ω-baryon mass.
* Pion mass: approximately 390 MeV, with m_K/m_π = 1.39.
* Lattice volume: 16³ × 128, corresponding to spatial volume V ∼ (1.9 fm)³ with spatial lattice spacing a_s = 0.12 fm.
* Number of gauge-field configurations used: 96.
* Bare charm quark mass parameters: a_t m_ℓ = -0.0840, a_t m_s = -0.0743.
* Ω-baryon mass in lattice units: a_t m_Ω = 0.2951(22).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of doubly-charmed baryons, which had only computed ground-state spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 spectra (e.g., Na (2007), Liu (2009), Lin (2010), Briceno (2012), Alexandrou (2012), Basak (2012), Bali (2012), Namekawa (2013)), by providing the first excited-state spectra up to spin 7/2. The results contradict the diquark picture for doubly-charmed baryons and instead align with SU(6) × O(3) symmetry models; the computed spectra can be compared to potential model predictions and provide first-principles input for ongoing experiments at LHCb and future experiments such as PANDA at FAIR and Super Belle.
