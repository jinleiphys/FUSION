# A three-dimensional momentum-space calculation of three-body bound state in a relativistic Faddeev scheme

> In this paper, we study the relativistic effects in a three-body bound state. For this purpose, the relativistic form of the Faddeev equations is solved in momentum space as a function of the Jacobi momentum vectors without using a partial wave decomposition. The inputs for the three-dimensional Faddeev integral equation are the off-shell boost two-body $t-$matrices, which are calculated directly from the boost two-body interactions by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The matrix elements of the boost interactions are obtained from the nonrelativistic interactions by solving a nonlinear integral equation using an iterative scheme. The relativistic effects on three-body binding energy are calculated for the Malfliet-Tjon potential. Our calculations show that the relativistic effects lead to a roughly 2\% reduction in the three-body binding energy. The contribution of different Faddeev components in the normalization of the relativistic three-body wave function is studied in detail. The accuracy of our numerical solutions is tested by calculation of the expectation value of the three-body mass operator, which shows an excellent agreement with the relativistic energy eigenvalue.

## Key claim

The relativistic Faddeev equations solved in a three-dimensional momentum-space scheme (without partial wave decomposition) show that relativistic effects reduce the three-body binding energy by roughly 2% for the Malfliet-Tjon potential, with the exact reduction depending on the method used to compute the boost two-body t-matrices.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Faddeev integral equation in momentum space using Jacobi momentum vectors without partial wave decomposition, representing the Faddeev component as a function of continuous momentum and angle variables. The boost two-body interactions were obtained from the nonrelativistic Malfliet-Tjon-V potential by solving a nonlinear integral equation via an iterative scheme, and the off-shell boost two-body t-matrices were then calculated by solving the relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The three-dimensional Faddeev integral equation was discretized using Gauss-Legendre quadrature with hyperbolic-linear mapping for Jacobi momenta and linear mapping for angles, and the eigenvalue equation was solved by searching for the energy where the eigenvalue lambda equals 1 within a tolerance of 10^{-6}.

## Key numbers

* Nonrelativistic three-body binding energy for the MT-V potential: -7.7380 MeV
* Full relativistic three-body binding energy for the MT-V potential: -7.5852 MeV, corresponding to a reduction of about 0.15 MeV or 1.97%
* Individual relativistic corrections to the binding energy: Jacobian function N decreases by 0.06 MeV (to -7.6748 MeV), permutation coefficient C increases by 0.05 MeV (to -7.7900 MeV), relativistic free propagator G0 increases by 0.18 MeV (to -7.9183 MeV), and boost two-body t-matrix Tk decreases by 0.28 MeV (to -7.4540 MeV)
* Expectation value of the relativistic three-body mass operator: 3m - 7.5840 MeV, differing from the eigenvalue by 0.0012 MeV
* Nonrelativistic expectation value of the Hamiltonian: -7.7384 MeV, differing from the nonrelativistic eigenvalue by 0.0004 MeV
* Normalization of Faddeev components: diagonal inner products <psi_i|psi_i> = 0.12658 and off-diagonal inner products <psi_i|psi_j> = 0.10338 for i not equal to j

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional momentum-space approach to relativistic three-body bound states, previously applied by Hadizadeh (2014, 2016) and Lin (2007, 2008), by implementing the iterative scheme of Kamada and Glockle (2007) to compute boost interactions directly from nonrelativistic potentials. The 2% reduction found here is smaller than the 3.3% reduction obtained by Hadizadeh (2014) using the first resolvent equation method, and also smaller than the 2.7% reduction reported by Glockle (1986) for an s-wave projection of the same potential, while Kamada (2002) found a range of 2-6% reduction for different interaction models. The approach enables future calculations at intermediate and high energy scales (few GeV) where partial wave convergence becomes difficult.
