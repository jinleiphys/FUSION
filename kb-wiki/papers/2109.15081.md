# Hydrodynamic attractors in heavy ion collisions: a review

> A review of the recent progress of relativistic hydrodynamic attractors is presented, with a focus on applications in heavy ion collisions and the quark gluon plasma. Pedagogical introductions to the effective descriptions relevant for attractors in high energy physics, namely hydrodynamics, holography and kinetic theory, are followed by highlights of some recent advances.

## Key claim

This review argues that hydrodynamic attractors explain why relativistic hydrodynamics works so well in heavy ion collisions even when the quark-gluon plasma is far from equilibrium, but the claim is conditional on the rapid decay of non-hydrodynamic modes which leaves only long-wavelength modes described by hydrodynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews three complementary theoretical frameworks used to study attractors in heavy ion collisions: relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (specifically the Muller-Israel-Stewart and Denicol-Niemi-Molnar-Rischke truncation schemes), holography via the fluid-gravity correspondence, and kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. The review focuses on the simplest case of (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken flow with boost invariance and conformal equation of state, then discusses generalizations to Gubser flow with radial expansion.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio close to the holographic bound of 1/(4π) is cited as evidence for the "most perfect fluid" behavior of the quark-gluon plasma.
* For the Bjorken flow attractor, the dimensionless constants from holography are C_η = 1/(4π) and C_{τ_Π} = (2 - ln 2)/(2π).
* The linearized perturbation to the attractor decays as δf = exp(-3ω/(2C_{τ_Π})) ω^{C_η/C_{τ_Π}} + O(ω^{-1}), showing exponential approach.
* The group velocity in causal MIS hydrodynamics saturates to a maximum value of sqrt(η/(τ_π(ε+P))), which must be less than 1 for causality.
* In the DNMR equation for Bjorken flow, the coefficient β_{ππ} = 38/21, while for MIS it is β_{ππ} = 4/3, and the relaxation times differ by a factor of 6/5.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on hydrodynamic attractors by Heller and Spalinski (2015), who discovered the attractor in Bjorken flow building on earlier Borel-resummed gradient expansions in holography. It connects to the Kurkela-Mazeliauskas-Paquet-Schlichtling-Teaney (KøMPøST) framework which links early-time kinetic theory to late-time hydrodynamics, and to phenomenological applications relating initial state energies to particle multiplicities. The review enables future work on attractors at finite chemical potential and their use in tracing trajectories through the QCD phase diagram.
