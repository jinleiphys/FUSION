# Tracing the emergence of collectivity phenomena in small systems

> We study initial state momentum correlations and event-by-event geometry in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02~\rm TeV$ by following the approach of extending the IP-Glasma model to 3D using JIMWLK rapidity evolution. On examining the non-trivial rapidity dependence of the observables, we find that the geometry is correlated over large rapidity intervals, while the initial state momentum correlations have a relatively short range in rapidity. Based on our results, we discuss implications for the relevance of both effects in explaining the origin of collective phenomena in small systems.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the event geometry (eccentricity) is correlated over large rapidity intervals, while initial state momentum correlations (anisotropy) have a relatively short range in rapidity, suggesting that both effects may contribute differently to the observed collective phenomena in small systems.

## Method
The authors extended the IP-Glasma model to three dimensions using JIMWLK rapidity evolution, generating 4096 events from proton and lead Wilson line configurations evolved to all rapidities of interest. For each event, they ran independent 2+1D classical Yang-Mills simulations at intervals of Δy=0.4 in the range y_obs ∈ [-2.4, +2.4] to obtain the rapidity dependence of observables, including eccentricities ε_n and the gluon momentum anisotropy v_2^g. They computed normalized rapidity correlation functions and estimators for the correlation of mean transverse momentum and elliptic anisotropy.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √s = 5.02 TeV for p+Pb collisions
- 4096 events generated from 32 proton and 8 lead Wilson line configurations collided at 16 different impact parameters
- Two sets of IR regulators used: m = m̃ = 0.2 GeV and m = m̃ = 0.8 GeV
- Rapidity range studied: y_obs ∈ [-2.4, +2.4] with intervals of Δy = 0.4
- Classical Yang-Mills simulations run up to proper time τ = 0.2 fm/c
- For m = m̃ = 0.2 GeV, the geometric estimator ρ̂_est(ε_2²,[s]) shows positive values for most central and most peripheral events
- For the larger m = m̃ = 0.8 GeV, ρ̂_est(ε_2²,[s]) is always negative

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma model to include longitudinal structure via JIMWLK evolution, building on previous studies of collectivity in small systems by Schenke (2021) and Lappi (2015). The results enable future phenomenological applications of the 3D IP-Glasma model to collective flow in both small and large systems, and motivate further theoretical progress toward fully 3D Wilson line configurations with 3+1D Yang-Mills evolution as explored by Schlichting (2020) and Ipp (2021).
