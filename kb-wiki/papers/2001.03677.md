# Nuclear effects in high-energy neutrino interactions

> Neutrino telescopes like IceCube, KM3NeT and Baikal-GVD offer physicists the opportunity to study neutrinos with energies far beyond the reach of terrestrial accelerators. These neutrinos are used to study high-energy neutrino interactions and to probe the Earth through absorption tomography. Current studies of TeV neutrinos use cross sections which are calculated for free nucleons with targets which are assumed to contain equal numbers of protons and neutrons. Here we consider modifications of high-energy neutrino interactions due to two nuclear effects: modifications of the parton densities in the nucleus, referred to here as shadowing, and the effect of non-isoscalar targets, with unequal numbers of neutrons and protons. Both these effects depend on the interaction medium. Because shadowing is larger for heavier nuclei, such as iron, found in the Earth's core, it introduces a zenith-angle dependent change in the absorption cross section. These modifications increase the cross sections by 1-2\% at energies below 100 TeV (antishadowing), and reduce it by 3-4\% at higher energies (shadowing). Nuclear effects also alter the inelasticity distribution of neutrino interactions in water/ice by increasing the number of low inelasticity interactions, with a larger effect for $ν$ than $\barν$. These effects are particularly large in the energy range below a few TeV. These effects could alter the cross sections inferred from events with tracks originating within the active detector volume as well as the ratio $ν/\barν$ inferred from inelasticity measurements. The uncertainties in these nuclear effects are larger than the uncertainties on the free-proton cross sections and will thus limit the systematic precision of future high-precision measurements at neutrino telescopes.

## Key claim

Nuclear modifications to parton densities (shadowing) and non-isoscalar target effects alter neutrino-nucleus cross sections by 1-2% at energies below 100 TeV (antishadowing) and reduce them by 3-4% at higher energies (shadowing), with the uncertainties in these nuclear effects exceeding those on free-proton cross sections and thus limiting systematic precision of future neutrino telescope measurements.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order perturbative QCD calculation of neutrino-nucleus charged-current and neutral-current cross sections, forming ratios to a parallel calculation assuming an unshadowed, isoscalar deuterium target. They incorporated nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) modifications using the EPS09 and EPPS16 global fit sets at next-to-leading order, accounting for both shadowing and non-isoscalar target effects for elements found in the Earth's crust, mantle, and core (A=4 through A=64). The calculation considered different paths through the Earth corresponding to different zenith angles and determined the overall change in cross sections and inelasticity distributions for neutrino interactions in water/ice.

## Key numbers

- Cross section modifications increase by 1-2% at energies below 100 TeV (antishadowing region) and reduce by 3-4% at higher energies (shadowing region)
- The uncertainty on free-proton PDF cross sections was found to be 5% for neutrino energies from 50 GeV to 10^9 GeV (Cooper-Sarkar 2011)
- The EPS09 nPDF sets cover the range 1.3 < Q < 1000 GeV and 10^-6 < x < 1; EPPS16 extends to 1.3 < Q < 10000 GeV and 10^-7 < x < 1
- The CHORUS neutrino data used in EPPS16 covered 4 < Q^2 < 100 GeV^2 and 0.05 < x < 0.7 on a lead target
- The Drell-Yan data from Fermilab E772 and E866 covered 16 < Q^2 < 81 GeV^2 (dimuon mass range 4 < M < 9 GeV) and probed 0.01 < x < 0.2
- For iron, A^(1/3) ≈ 3.8, relevant for the colored glass condensate onset energy scaling

## Context

This work extends the standard next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations of neutrino-nucleon cross sections (Cooper-Sarkar 2011, Connolly 2011) by incorporating nuclear effects that are neglected in current IceCube analyses. The GENIE model (Andreopoulos 2015) includes non-isoscalar targets and some nuclear effects but only at energies up to a few hundred GeV. The paper enables more precise modeling of neutrino absorption tomography of the Earth and improved extraction of the neutrino/antineutrino ratio from inelasticity measurements at IceCube, KM3NeT, and Baikal-GVD, while noting that the uncertainties from nPDFs (EPS09, EPPS16) are larger than those from free-proton PDFs and will limit future high-precision measurements.
