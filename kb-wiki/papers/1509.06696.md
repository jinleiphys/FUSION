# Strangeness production and long-range correlations in pp collisions in string fusion approach

> The effects of string fusion on the correlations in strange particles production in proton-proton collisions at high energy are studied in the framework of a Monte Carlo string-parton model. The model is based on the strings formation in elementary dipole-dipole collisions. The hardness of the elementary interaction is defined by a transverse size of the colliding dipoles. The interaction between strings is realized in the accordance with the string fusion model prescriptions by the introduction of the lattice in the impact parameter plane and taking into account the finite rapidity length of strings. The particles species differentiation is implemented according to Schwinger mechanism. The parameters of the model are fixed with the experimental data on total inelastic cross section and charged multiplicity. In the framework of the model the long-range correlation functions with an accounting of strangeness have been studied. A new intensive event-by-event observable has been proposed, which characterizes the fraction of strange particles in the event. The predictions on the correlations between strangeness, multiplicity and mean transverse momentum are obtained for pp collisions at 7 TeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that string fusion in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV produces non-trivial long-range correlations between strangeness, multiplicity, and mean transverse momentum, but the effect is conditional: the correlations are only non-zero when string fusion is included in the model, and the strangeness fraction behaves non-monotonically with multiplicity, saturating and then decreasing at high multiplicities due to increased non-strange resonance production.

## Method

The authors extended a Monte Carlo string-parton model based on dipole-dipole collisions, incorporating string fusion via a lattice in the impact parameter plane with finite rapidity length. Particle species differentiation was implemented using the Schwinger mechanism, with string tension modified by the number of overlapping strings. The model parameters were fixed using experimental data on total inelastic cross section and charged multiplicity, and long-range correlation functions were computed for pp collisions at 7 TeV using forward and backward rapidity windows.

## Key numbers

- String radius set to \( r_\mathrm{str} = 0.2 \) fm and intrinsic string transverse momentum \( p_0 = 0.2 \) GeV/c, providing a reasonable description of transverse momentum distributions at LHC energies.
- For a cluster of \( k \) overlapping strings, mean multiplicity scales as \( \mu = \sqrt{k} \mu_1 \) and mean transverse momentum as \( p_T = \sqrt[4]{k} {p_T}_1 \).
- Particle masses used in Schwinger mechanism: pions from \( \rho \) decays (0.775 GeV), direct pions (0.135 GeV), kaons (0.494 GeV), protons (0.938 GeV), with weighting factors \( g_\nu \) of 3, 1, 1, 1 respectively.
- Correlation functions were calculated for pp collisions at \( \sqrt{s} = 7 \) TeV in rapidity windows (-2.5, -0.5) and (0.5, 2.5).

## Context

This work extends the string fusion model previously developed by the same group (Kovalenko and Vechernin, 2014) and builds on the formalism of quark-gluon string formation and fusion (SFa, SFb, SFc). It introduces a new intensive observable, the event fraction of strange particles, for forward-backward correlation studies, enabling comparisons with experimental data from the ALICE collaboration at the LHC. The results show that strangeness-strangeness and transverse-momentum-strangeness correlations are zero without string fusion, making them potential indicators of collective effects in pp collisions.
