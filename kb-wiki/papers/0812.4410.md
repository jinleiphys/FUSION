# Quasifission and fusion-fission in massive nuclei reactions. Comparison of reactions leading to the Z=120 element

> The yields of evaporation residues, fusion-fission and quasifission fragments in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{144,154}$Sm and $^{16}$O+$^{186}$W reactions are analyzed in the framework of the combined theoretical method based on the dinuclear system concept and advanced statistical model. The measured yields of evaporation residues for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm reaction can be well reproduced. The measured yields of fission fragments are decomposed into contributions coming from fusion-fission, quasifission, and fast-fission. The decrease in the measured yield of quasifission fragments in $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm at the large collision energies and the lack of quasifission fragments in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm reaction are explained by the overlap in mass-angle distributions of the quasifission and fusion-fission fragments. The investigation of the optimal conditions for the synthesis of the new element $Z$=120 ($A$=302) show that the $^{54}$Cr+$^{248}$Cm reaction is preferable in comparison with the $^{58}$Fe+$^{244}$Pu and $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reactions because the excitation function of the evaporation residues of the former reaction is some orders of magnitude larger than that for the last two reactions.

## Key claim

The measured disappearance of quasifission fragments at high collision energies in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm reaction and the apparent lack of quasifission in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm reaction are explained by the overlap in mass-angle distributions of quasifission and fusion-fission fragments, not by an actual absence of quasifission. The $^{54}$Cr+$^{248}$Cm reaction is predicted to be preferable for synthesizing element $Z=120$ because its evaporation residue excitation function is "some orders of magnitude larger" than those of the $^{58}$Fe+$^{244}$Pu and $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reactions.

## Method

The authors applied a combined theoretical method based on the dinuclear system (DNS) concept and an advanced statistical model to analyze capture, complete fusion, quasifission, fast-fission, and evaporation residue cross sections. They calculated the driving potential, intrinsic fusion barriers, and quasifission barriers for each reaction, and decomposed measured fission fragment yields into contributions from fusion-fission, quasifission, and fast-fission channels. The method was benchmarked against experimental data for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm, $^{48}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm, and $^{16}$O+$^{186}$W reactions, then applied to predict optimal conditions for synthesizing $^{302}$120 via three candidate reactions.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{48}$Ca+$154$Sm reaction, the fission barrier for the $^{202}$Pb compound nucleus is $B_{\rm f}=12.33$ MeV (from the rotating finite range model by Sierk), with an additional microscopic barrier $B_{\rm f}^{(\rm microscopic)}=-\delta W \cong 8.22$ MeV from nuclear shell structure.
- At $E_{\rm c.m.}=154$ MeV ($E^*_{\rm CN}=63$ MeV) for $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm, the experimental quasifission cross section is much lower than the fusion-fission cross section.
- The "asymmetric shoulders" in the mass distribution of fission fragments from $^{48}$Ca+$^{154}$Sm are peaked around masses 65 and 140 at $E^*_{\rm CN}=49$ and 57 MeV.
- For the $^{48}$Ca+$^{144}$Sm reaction, the missing quasifission fragments are predicted in the mass range $48 < A < 60$, outside the experimentally analyzed range of $60 < A < 130$.
- For the $^{16}$O+$^{186}$W reaction, up to $E_{\rm c.m.}=90$ MeV the capture and complete fusion excitation functions are essentially the same, with quasifission more than one order of magnitude lower.
- The cross section for synthesis of element 118 in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Cf reaction was about 0.5 pb (Oganessian, 2007).

## Context

This work extends the DNS-based analysis of fusion hindrance and quasifission previously applied by the same group (Fazio, 2004; Fazio, 2005; Nasirov, 2005) and others (Aritomo, 2004) to reactions forming lead isotopes, and applies it to the superheavy element region. The paper directly addresses the experimental findings of Knyazheva (2007) on the $^{48}$Ca+$^{144,154}$Sm reactions, explaining why quasifission signatures can be masked by overlap with fusion-fission in mass-angle distributions. The predictions for $Z=120$ synthesis build on the successful description of the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Cf reaction that produced element 118 (Oganessian, 2007), and enable experimentalists to prioritize the $^{54}$Cr+$^{248}$Cm reaction over the more mass-symmetric alternatives.
