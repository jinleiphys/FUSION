# Low-lying dipole response in the Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation and its influence on neutron capture cross sections

> We have computed dipole strength distributions for nickel and tin isotopes within the Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking approximation (RQTBA). These calculations provide a good description of data, including the neutron-rich tin isotopes $^{130,132}$Sn. The resulting dipole strengths have been implemented in Hauser-Feshbach calculations of astrophysical neutron capture rates relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis studies. The RQTBA calculations show the presence of enhanced dipole strength at energies around the neutron threshold for neutron rich nuclei. The computed neutron capture rates are sensitive to the fine structure of the low lying dipole strength, which emphasizes the importance of a reliable knowledge of this excitation mode.

# Low-lying dipole response in the Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation and its influence on neutron capture cross sections

## Key claim
The RQTBA calculations show enhanced dipole strength at energies around the neutron threshold for neutron-rich nuclei, and the computed neutron capture rates are sensitive to the fine structure of this low-lying dipole strength; however, the enhanced low-lying dipole strength has only modest influence on the neutron capture rates for most isotopes, with the largest effect (factors 2-5) found for capture on tin isotopes 129,131Sn where the enhanced strength coincides with the neutron threshold.

## Method
The authors computed dipole strength distributions for chains of nickel and tin isotopes using the Relativistic Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (RQTBA), which extends the relativistic QRPA by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling. These microscopic strength functions were then implemented as E1 gamma-strength functions in Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations of neutron capture cross sections and stellar reaction rates at T=10^9 K, using parity-dependent level densities from a combinatorial HFB approach. The RQTBA results were compared with Lorentzian parametrizations (with energy-dependent width) and with QRPA calculations from Goriely and Khan (2002).

## Key numbers
- The RQTBA calculation for 132Sn predicts a strong enhancement of E1 strength around the neutron separation energy of 7.3 MeV
- For 140Sn, the neutron threshold is 3.5 MeV and the RQTBA model predicts strong fragmentation of gamma strength at low energies (E greater than approximately 5 MeV)
- The largest effect on capture rates is for 129,131Sn (capture on targets leading to 130,132Sn), where the RQTBA model gives rates enhanced by factors 2-5 compared to other models
- A 200 keV imaginary part was included in the energy variable of the strength function to account for configurations beyond RQTBA
- The stellar neutron capture rates were calculated at a temperature of T=10^9 K

## Context
This work extends the RQTBA approach previously demonstrated by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2008) to reproduce experimental photoabsorption cross sections for tin isotopes, including data for 130,132Sn from Adrich, Klimkiewicz et al. (2005). It contrasts with conventional Lorentzian parametrizations of dipole strength (Cowan, Thielemann, Truran 1991; McCullagh 1981) and with QRPA calculations by Goriely and Khan (2002). The results enable improved modeling of neutron capture rates for r-process nucleosynthesis studies, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei where experimental data are unavailable, and highlight the need for parity-dependent level densities as calculated by Hilaire and Goriely (2006).
