# Incoherent processes in dileptons production in proton-nucleus scattering at high energies

> $\mathbf{Purpose}$: Incoherent processes in production of lepton pairs in the scattering of protons off nuclei are investigated. $\mathbf{Methods}$: New quantum mechanical model is constructed which uses generalization of the nuclear model of emission of photons in the proton-nucleus reactions in region from low up to high energies with inclusion of formalism of production of lepton pairs. $\mathbf{Results}$: (1) Model with the coherent matrix elements is tested for the scattering of protons on the Be nuclei at energy of proton beam $E_{\rm p}$ of 2.1 GeV. The calculated cross section of production of lepton pairs is in good agreement with experimental data obtained by DLS Collaboration. (2) We analyzed dilepton production for nuclei-targets $\isotope[9]{Be}$, $\isotope[12]{C}$, $\isotope[16]{O}$, $\isotope[24]{Mg}$, $\isotope[44]{Ca}$, $\isotope[197]{Au}$ at $E_{\rm p}=2.1$ GeV. Coherent cross sections of dilepton production are monotonously decreased with increasing of nucleus-target mass. (3) Production of lepton pairs is more intensive at larger $E_{\rm p}$. (4) For $p + \isotope{9}Be$ at $E_{\rm p}=2.1$ GeV we analyzed role of incoherent processes in production of pairs of leptons. Inclusion of incoherent proton-nucleon processes to the model improves agreement with experimental data a little. (5) Adding of the longitudinal amplitude of virtual photon suppresses the cross section of dilepton production a little. (6) The incoherent contribution has a leading role in dilepton production (ratio between incoherent and coherent contributions is 10-100). Model provides the full spectrum for $p + \isotope[93]{Nb}$ at $E_{\rm p}=3.5$ GeV in good agreement with experimental data of HADES collaboration, shows large role of incoherent processes. Conclusion: Results above confirms importance of incoherent processes in study of dilepton production in this reaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incoherent (many-nucleon) processes dominate dilepton production in proton-nucleus scattering at GeV energies, with the ratio of incoherent to coherent contributions reaching 10–100, though the abstract's claim of "good agreement" with experimental data is qualified by the observation that inclusion of incoherent processes improves agreement only "a little" for the Be target at 2.1 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct a quantum mechanical model that generalizes a previously developed nuclear bremsstrahlung formalism for real photon emission to the production of lepton pairs via virtual photons in proton-nucleus scattering. The S-matrix formalism separates the full emission operator into coherent terms (involving the relative motion between the proton and the center-of-mass of the target nucleus) and incoherent terms (involving relative distances between individual nucleons of the target and the scattering proton). The model is tested against experimental data from the DLS Collaboration for p + Be at 2.1 GeV and the HADES Collaboration for p + Nb at 3.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Ratio between incoherent and coherent contributions to dilepton production is 10–100 for p + Be at E_p = 2.1 GeV
* Coherent cross sections of dilepton production monotonically decrease with increasing target mass for nuclei Be-9, C-12, O-16, Mg-24, Ca-44, and Au-197 at E_p = 2.1 GeV
* For p + Be at E_p = 2.1 GeV, the model with coherent matrix elements shows good agreement with DLS Collaboration experimental data
* The model provides the full spectrum for p + Nb-93 at E_p = 3.5 GeV in good agreement with HADES collaboration data

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Maydanyuk and Zhang (2015) and Maydanyuk, Zhang, and Zou (2016) for bremsstrahlung emission in proton-nucleus scattering to the production of dilepton pairs, incorporating the many-nucleon dynamics that were previously shown to dominate real photon emission. The results contradict the implicit assumption in earlier transport model calculations (such as the BUU-type models of Bertsch and Das Gupta (1988) and Wolf (1993)) that coherent processes alone could adequately describe dilepton production. The work enables future experimental tests at low invariant masses (below 250 MeV) and high invariant masses (above 1000 MeV) to further validate the role of incoherent processes.
