# Dispersion relations in quantum hadrodynamics with a finite cutoff

> The dispersion relations in the real and imaginary parts of the meson self-energies are studied to check the consistency of the "renormalization" in cutoff field theory. It is shown that the dispersion relations are preserved by the "renormalization"even if the finite cutoff and regulator are introduced in the calculation by hand.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dispersion relations between the real and imaginary parts of meson self-energies are preserved in cutoff field theory even when a finite cutoff and regulator are introduced by hand, provided one works with sufficiently high derivatives of the divergent quantities rather than the quantities themselves.

## Method

The authors formulate a renormalization method for quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) based on a finite cutoff, following Lepage's proposal to add higher-order "nonrenormalizable" terms to the effective Lagrangian and determine their coefficients phenomenologically to remove cutoff dependence order by order. They then numerically compute the second and third derivatives of the vacuum fluctuation parts of the sigma-meson and omega-meson self-energies in the random phase approximation (RPA) at finite density, using a sharp momentum cutoff, and check whether the dispersion relation (a principal-value integral connecting the real and imaginary parts) holds for these derivatives.

## Key numbers

* The absolute value of the three-dimensional momentum used in the dispersion relation calculation for the density-dependent part is \(q = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The second derivative of the vacuum polarization \(\Pi^{V(2)}\) is shown to have \(\Lambda\)-dependence of order \(1/\Lambda^2\); the third derivative \(\Pi^{V(3)}\) has \(\Lambda\)-dependence of order \(1/\Lambda^4\).
* Numerical results for \(\Pi^{V(2)}\) and \(\Pi^{V(3)}\) are presented with cutoff values \(\Lambda = 2,\ 3,\ 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The paper states that in QHD the cutoff \(\Lambda_0\) may be several GeV, and that errors of order \(1/\Lambda^2\) cannot be ignored when \(\Lambda\) is of order GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kouno et al., 1990s) on nuclear matter properties and vertex corrections in cutoff field theory, where cutoff dependence was eliminated using Lepage's method. It directly addresses the consistency concern that introducing a finite cutoff by hand might break the dispersion relations that hold in the renormalized theory. The results enable future calculations of effective meson masses at finite baryon density using the same renormalization method, which the authors note is in progress.
