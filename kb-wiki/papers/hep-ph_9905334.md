# Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov Sum Rule for the Nucleon in the Large-N_c Limit

## Key claim

The Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov (DHG) sum rule for the nucleon is entirely saturated by the Delta resonance in the limit of a large number of colors (N_c to infinity), with corrections appearing only at relative order 1/N_c^2. The result holds under the condition that alpha_em times N_c is taken to be independent of N_c and much less than 1, ensuring electromagnetism remains perturbative.

## Method

The authors perform large-N_c counting for the electromagnetic couplings of the nucleon using the contracted SU(2n_f) spin-flavor symmetry of large-N_c QCD. They compare the N_c-scaling of the right-hand side of the DHG sum rule (proportional to the anomalous magnetic moment squared) with the scaling of generic contributions to the left-hand side spin-dependent photo-production cross section integral. The Delta resonance contribution is evaluated separately using its M1 transition coupling to the nucleon, and its N_c-scaling is shown to match the leading-order behavior of the sum rule.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar part of the DHG sum rule has a theoretical expectation (right-hand side) of 219 microbarns, while the Delta-resonance contribution to the left-hand side integral is 240 microbarns (from Karliner, 1973 and Workman and Arndt, 1992).
- The isovector magnetic coupling mu_V scales as N_c, while the isoscalar magnetic coupling mu_S scales as 1/N_c.
- The nucleon-Delta mass splitting scales as 1/N_c.
- The Delta contribution to the DHG sum rule is of order alpha_em N_c^2, matching the leading N_c behavior of the right-hand side.
- The isovector component of the DHG sum rule scales as alpha_em (order N_c^0), with consistent N_c counting on both sides.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Broniowski (1994) that the same contracted spin-flavor SU(4) symmetry responsible for large-N_c consistency of the Adler-Weisberger and Cabibbo-Radicati sum rules also governs the DHG sum rule. The result provides a theoretical explanation from QCD fundamentals for the long-known empirical fact that the isoscalar DHG sum rule is dominated by the Delta resonance. The analysis enables a systematic understanding of how the contracted SU(4) symmetry of large-N_c QCD constrains spin-dependent photo-production cross sections and nucleon electromagnetic properties.
