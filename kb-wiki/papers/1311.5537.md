# CKM benchmarks for electron EDM experiments

> All current experiments searching for an electron EDM d_e are performed with atoms and diatomic molecules. Motivated by significant recent progress in searches for an EDM-type signal in diatomic molecules with an uncompensated electron spin, we provide an estimate for the expected signal in the Standard Model due to the CKM phase. We find that the main contribution originates from the effective electron-nucleon operator $\bar{e} iγ_5 e \bar{N}N$, induced by a combination of weak and electromagnetic interactions at $O(G_F^2α^2)$, and not by the CKM-induced electron EDM itself. When the resulting atomic P,T-odd mixing is interpreted as an {\it equivalent} electron EDM, this estimate leads to the benchmark $d_e^{equiv}(CKM) \sim 10^{-38}$ ecm.

## Key claim

The Standard Model CKM phase induces an equivalent electron EDM of approximately \(10^{-38}\) e cm in paramagnetic atoms and molecules, but this arises primarily from the effective electron-nucleon operator \(\bar{e} i\gamma_5 e \bar{N}N\) rather than from the electron EDM itself; the estimate is an order-of-magnitude benchmark, not a precise calculation.

## Method

The authors computed the CKM-induced contribution to the semileptonic \(CP\)-odd operator \(C_{SP}\) (coefficient of \(\bar{N}N \bar{e} i\gamma_5 e\)) by evaluating two-photon exchange diagrams between an electron and a nucleon. They saturated the hadronic intermediate states with the \(\Sigma\) baryon, used experimental data from \(\Sigma \to p\gamma\) decays to fix the \(CP\)-even \(\Sigma N\gamma\) vertex, and combined it with the \(CP\)-odd electromagnetic penguin operator \(\bar{s}_L\gamma_\mu d_L\) to generate the nucleon-spin-independent operator \(\tilde{F}_{\mu\nu}(\bar{e}\gamma_\nu e)\partial_\mu(\bar{N}N)\). A subsequent loop integral converted this operator into the standard \(C_{SP}\) form.

## Key numbers

* Reduced Jarlskog invariant: \(\mathcal{J} \simeq 2.9 \times 10^{-5}\) (dimensionless, from CKM angles and phase).
* Estimated CKM-induced \(C_{SP}\) coefficient: \(C_{SP}(\mathcal{J}) \sim 10^{-19}\) (dimensionless, with \(G_F\) factored out).
* Equivalent electron EDM benchmark: \(d_e^{\text{equiv}}(\text{CKM}) \sim 10^{-38}\) e cm (for paramagnetic atoms/molecules).
* Direct CKM-induced electron EDM proper: \(d_e(\mathcal{J}) \sim 10^{-44}\) e cm (four-loop order, highly suppressed).
* Best current experimental limit on electron EDM (ThO, 2013): \(|d_e| < 8.7 \times 10^{-29}\) e cm.
* \(r\) coefficients for converting \(C_{SP}\) to equivalent EDM: \(r_{\text{Tl}} = 1.2 \times 10^{-20}\) e cm, \(r_{\text{YbF}} = 0.88 \times 10^{-20}\) e cm, \(r_{\text{ThO}} = 1.33 \times 10^{-20}\) e cm.

## Context

This work extends earlier CKM-induced EDM calculations for nucleons and diamagnetic atoms (Khriplovich and Zhitnitsky 1982; Gavela et al. 1982; Donoghue et al. 1987) to paramagnetic systems with uncompensated electron spin, which had not been explored in detail. The result provides a "line in the sand" for electron EDM searches: a detection above \(10^{-38}\) e cm would unambiguously signal new physics beyond the Standard Model. The benchmark is approximately nine orders of magnitude below the 2013 ThO limit (ACME Collaboration 2013), but only five to six orders below current neutron and diamagnetic atom EDM sensitivities, highlighting the relative importance of the \(C_{SP}\) operator over the direct electron EDM in the CKM context.
