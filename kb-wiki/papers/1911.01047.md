# Absorption of $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons in the inclusive photonuclear reaction

> The $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons dominantly decay outside the nucleus because the decay-lengths of them are much larger than the dimension of the nucleus. Therefore, the medium modification of the above mesons is studied through their absorption in the nucleus. CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Laboratory (Jlab) has investigated the absorption of the $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons by measuring the nuclear transparency ratios for them in the $γA \to ω(ϕ) X \to e^+e^-X$ reaction. The nuclear transparency ratios for both $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons in the above reactions have been calculated, and those are compared with the experimental findings, reported from Jlab, to estimate the $ω$ meson nucleon and $ϕ$ meson nucleon cross sections in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency ratios for both ω and φ mesons, calculated using free-space meson-nucleon cross sections, overestimate the CLAS data; however, a drastically enhanced in-medium ω-nucleon cross section (up to 10 times the free value) still cannot reproduce the measured ω meson absorption, whereas an in-medium φ-nucleon cross section of 1.5–10 times the free value does reproduce the φ meson data.

## Method

The cross section of the inclusive photonuclear reaction γA → VX → e⁺e⁻X (V = ω or φ) was calculated using a Glauber-model eikonal description, where the vector meson-nucleus optical potential is built from the elementary vector meson-nucleon total scattering cross section. The nuclear transparency T_A was defined as the ratio of the full cross section (with the optical potential) to the vacuum cross section (with the optical potential set to zero). The calculated transparency ratios normalized to ¹²C (T_A/T_C) were compared with CLAS data to extract in-medium meson-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- ω meson decay length: ~23 fm; φ meson decay length: ~44 fm (both much larger than nuclear dimensions).
- Free-space ω-nucleon total cross section σ_t^{ωN} at momentum 1.0 GeV/c: 79.18 mb; at 4.0 GeV/c: 41.89 mb.
- Free-space φ-nucleon total cross section σ_t^{φN} at momentum 1.0 GeV/c: 13.07 mb; at 4.0 GeV/c: ~10.35 mb.
- Dielectron decay width Γ_{ω→e⁺e⁻}(m_ω) = 0.6 keV; Γ_{φ→e⁺e⁻}(m_φ) = 1.26 keV.
- In-medium φ-nucleon cross section σ_t^{*φN} = 3σ_t^{φN} is consistent with the value reported by Ishikawa et al.: σ_t^{*φN} ~ 35^{+17}_{-11} mb.
- The ω meson transparency ratio T_A/T_C saturates for σ_t^{*ωN} ≥ 5σ_t^{ωN}, and even σ_t^{*ωN} = 10σ_t^{ωN} cannot reproduce the CLAS data.

## Context

This work extends the study of vector meson in-medium modification from heavy-ion collisions to cold nuclear matter, following the CLAS measurement at JLab (Nasseripour et al., 2008; Wood et al., 2010) which measured nuclear transparency for ω and φ mesons. The calculation uses the Glauber model framework of Bauer et al. (1978) and the vector meson dominance model. The failure of a drastically enhanced ω-nucleon cross section to explain the data contrasts with the φ meson case, where in-medium cross sections of 1.5–10 times the free value reproduce the data, consistent with the earlier result of Ishikawa et al. (2005). This enables further theoretical work on the nature of ω meson absorption in nuclei, possibly requiring mechanisms beyond a simple increase in the in-medium cross section.
