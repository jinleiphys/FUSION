# A quasi-particle model with a phenomenological critical point

> A hybrid parameterization of a quasiparticle equation of state is proposed, with a critical point implemented phenomenologically. On the one hand, a quasiparticle model with finite chemical potential is employed for the quark-gluon plasma phase, calibrated to the lattice quantum chromodynamics data. On the other hand, the low-temperature region for the hadronic phase of the matter is described by the hadronic resonance gas model with excluded volume correction. A particular interpolation scheme is adopted so that the phase transition is a smooth crossover for small chemical potential. A phenomenological critical pointed is implemented beyond which the phase transition becomes that of the first order.

# A quasi-particle model with a phenomenological critical point

## Key claim
The authors propose a hybrid equation of state that connects a quasiparticle model for the quark-gluon plasma phase to a hadronic resonance gas model for the hadronic phase, with a phenomenological critical point implemented such that the phase transition is a smooth crossover at small chemical potential and becomes first-order beyond a critical chemical potential. The model is calibrated to lattice QCD data but the interpolation scheme introduces a secondary peak in energy density at finite chemical potential that is likely an artifact of the smoothing parameterization rather than a physical prediction.

## Method
A quasiparticle model with finite chemical potential, using medium-dependent masses for gluons and up, down, and strange quarks, is calibrated to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2012, 2013) for the high-temperature QGP phase. The low-temperature hadronic phase is described by a hadronic resonance gas model with excluded volume correction using a baryon radius of 0.7 fm and zero radius for mesons. The two phases are connected via an interpolation scheme where the Gibbs condition is modified by a smoothing function delta(mu,T) that depends on a critical chemical potential mu_c = 0.3 GeV, with a piecewise temperature-dependent function delta_0(T) that controls the crossover-to-first-order transition.

## Key numbers
- Strange quark current mass: m_s0 = 0.150 GeV; up and down quark masses: m_u0,d0 = m_s0/28.15 ≈ 5.33 MeV
- Critical chemical potential: mu_c = 0.3 GeV
- Excluded volume radius for baryons: r_0 = 0.7 fm; mesons: r_0 = 0
- Smoothing parameter: delta_0 = 5.90 × 10^{-10} GeV^8; smoothing exponent coefficient: c = 10^3
- Overall normalization factor: 1.06, introduced to account for unknown corrections to effective degrees of freedom
- The trace anomaly maximum occurs near T ~ 0.2 GeV; the speed of sound minimum occurs near T ~ 0.15 GeV

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle model approach originally proposed by Peshier et al. (1994) and reformulated by Gorenstein and Yang (1995), and builds on the authors' previous hybrid equation of state work (Ma et al., 2017, 2018). The critical point implementation follows the scheme of Hama et al. (2005). The model is intended for use in hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, and is compared to the parameterized equation of state by Huovinen and Petreczky (2006) and to the bag model. The results show reasonable agreement with lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. (2012, 2013) at zero chemical potential, but the finite chemical potential results show a secondary peak in energy density that requires further investigation.
