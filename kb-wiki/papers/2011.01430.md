# Multi-system Bayesian constraints on the transport coefficients of QCD matter

> We study the properties of the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma with a multistage model of heavy ion collisions that combines the T$_\mathrm{R}$ENTo initial condition ansatz, free-streaming, viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and a relativistic hadronic transport. A model-to-data comparison with Bayesian inference is performed, revisiting assumptions made in previous studies. The role of parameter priors is studied in light of their importance towards the interpretation of results. We emphasize the use of closure tests to perform extensive validation of the analysis workflow before comparison with observations. Our study combines measurements from the Large Hadron Collider and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, achieving a good simultaneous description of a wide range of hadronic observables from both colliders. The selected experimental data provide reasonable constraints on the shear and the bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma at $T\sim$ 150-250 MeV, but their constraining power degrades at higher temperatures $T \gtrsim 250$ MeV. Furthermore, these viscosity constraints are found to depend significantly on how viscous corrections are handled in the transition from hydrodynamics to the hadronic transport. Several other model parameters, including the free-streaming time, show similar model sensitivity while the initial condition parameters associated with the T$_\mathrm{R}$ENTo ansatz are quite robust against variations of the particlization prescription. We also report on the sensitivity of individual observables to the various model parameters. Finally, Bayesian model selection is used to quantitatively compare the agreement with measurements for different sets of model assumptions, including different particlization models and different choices for which parameters are allowed to vary between RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma are reasonably constrained by experimental data in the temperature range T ~ 150-250 MeV, but their constraining power degrades at higher temperatures T > 250 MeV, and the viscosity constraints depend significantly on how viscous corrections are handled in the transition from hydrodynamics to hadronic transport.

## Method

A multistage model of heavy ion collisions was constructed combining the T_R_ENTo initial condition ansatz, free-streaming, viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and the SMASH hadronic transport model. Bayesian inference was performed to compare model calculations with soft hadron observables from both RHIC and the LHC, using closure tests to validate the analysis workflow before comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* Shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma are constrained at temperatures T ~ 150-250 MeV, with constraining power degrading at T > 250 MeV
* The analysis combines measurements from both the Large Hadron Collider and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider
* Woods-Saxon parameters used for Au are radius R = 6.38 fm and surface thickness a = 0.535 fm; for Pb they are R = 6.62 fm and a = 0.546 fm
* The free-streaming time tau_fs is of order 0.1-1 fm/c
* The cross-over transition from hadronic to deconfined QCD matter occurs near a temperature of 150 MeV at vanishing net baryon density

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of soft hadronic observables by Bernhard (2019) and others, using a more flexible parametrization of the temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities and a wider range of viscosity priors. It is the first to quantify uncertainties in viscosity constraints stemming from theoretical uncertainties in modeling viscous corrections to particlization, and uses Bayesian model selection to compare different particlization models and different choices for which parameters vary between RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable improved estimates of transport coefficients through Bayesian model averaging, as explored in the companion Letter by Everett (2020).
