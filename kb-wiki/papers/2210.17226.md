# Higher twists effects in DIS on nuclei at the EIC and LHeC: A phenomenological analysis

> In this paper we investigate the impact of the higher - twist effects, resummed by the non - linear approaches for the QCD dynamics, on the inclusive observables that will be measured in future electron - ion colliders. We assume a phenomenological model for the dipole - nucleus scattering amplitude which takes into account the non - linear corrections and estimate the contribution of the different twists for $F_2^A(x,Q^2)$, $F_L^A(x,Q^2)$ and $\partial F_2^A /\partial ln Q^2$ considering different values of the Bjorken - $x$ variable, photon virtuality $Q^2$ and atomic number $A$. A comparison with the full predictions is performed and the impact of the distinct twists is estimated. Our results indicate that a future analysis of the logarithmic $Q^2$ slope and longitudinal structure function will allow us to probe the presence of the non - linear effects on the QCD dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a future experimental analysis of the logarithmic $Q^{2}$ slope and the longitudinal structure function in electron-nucleus collisions will allow us to probe the presence of non-linear effects in QCD dynamics, but this conclusion is conditional on the validity of the twist expansion for $\xi \equiv Q_{s,A}^{2}/Q^{2} \lesssim 1$ and on the assumption that the nuclear saturation scale is enhanced by a factor $A^{1/3}$ relative to the proton.

## Method

The authors employ the dipole formalism for deep inelastic scattering on nuclei, using a phenomenological model for the dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude based on the Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff (GBW) model, which resums non-linear corrections. They perform a Mellin transform of the structure function expressions and expand in powers of $\xi = Q_{s,A}^{2}/Q^{2}$ to isolate the leading twist ($\tau=2$) and higher twist ($\tau=4,6,8$) contributions to $F_{2}^{A}$, $F_{L}^{A}$, and $\partial F_{2}^{A}/\partial \ln Q^{2}$. The nuclear saturation scale is taken as $Q_{s,A}^{2} = A^{1/3} \times Q_{0}^{2} (x_{0}/x)^{\lambda}$, with parameters fixed by a fit to HERA $ep$ data from GBW, and the impact parameter dependence is factorized using a Wood-Saxon profile.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear saturation scale is parameterized as $Q_{s,A}^{2} = A^{1/3} \times Q_{0}^{2} (x_{0}/x)^{\lambda}$, with $Q_{0}^{2} = 1.0$ GeV$^{2}$, $x_{0} = 3.04 \times 10^{-4}$, and $\lambda = 0.288$, taken from the GBW fit to HERA data.
* For $Q^{2} = 2.5$ GeV$^{2}$ and $x \approx 10^{-5}$, the observables for $A = 208$ (lead) are suppressed by a factor of approximately 2 compared to the predictions for the proton ($A=1$).
* The difference between the twist-2 and full predictions for $F_{L}^{A=1}$ becomes larger than 10% for $x \lesssim 10^{-3}$, and this threshold moves to larger $x$ values when the atomic number is increased.
* For $A = 208$ and $Q^{2} = 5$ GeV$^{2}$, the higher-twist corrections to $F_{L}^{A}$ are sizeable, with the twist-4 term giving a negative correction to the leading-twist contribution.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of higher-twist effects in DIS on nuclei by Gotsman et al. (2000) by providing a systematic study for the kinematic ranges of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and Large Hadron Electron Collider (LHeC), and by estimating for the first time the impact of these corrections on the logarithmic $Q^{2}$ slope of the nuclear structure function. The study is motivated by previous work on the proton case by Bartels et al. (1999, 2000, 2009) and by the observation from Motyka et al. (2017) that higher-twist contributions to the proton structure are comparable to leading-twist contributions at low $Q^{2} \approx 2$ GeV$^{2}$ and $x \approx 10^{-5}$. The results enable future experimental analyses at the EIC and LHeC to discriminate between linear and non-linear QCD dynamics by measuring $F_{L}^{A}$ and $\partial F_{2}^{A}/\partial \ln Q^{2}$ for different nuclei.
