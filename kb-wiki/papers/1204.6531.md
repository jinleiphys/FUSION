# A new formulation to calculate general HFB matrix elements through Pfaffian

> A new formula is presented for the calculation of matrix elements between multi-quasiparticle Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states. The formula is expressed in terms of the Pfaffian, and is derived by using the Fermion coherent states with Grassmann numbers. It turns out that the formula corresponds to an extension of generalized Wick's theorem and simplifies the combinatorial complexity resulting from practical applications of generalized Wick's theorem by unifying the transition density and the transition pairing tensor in the HFB theory. The resultant formula is simpler and more compact than the traditional description of matrix elements of general many-body operators. In addition, through the derivation of our new formula, we found that the Pfaffian version of the Lewis Carroll formula corresponds to the relation conjectrured by Balian and Brezin for the HFB theory in 1969.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new Pfaffian-based formula for calculating matrix elements between multi-quasiparticle Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states that is simpler and more compact than the traditional generalized Wick's theorem approach; the derivation reveals that the Pfaffian version of the Lewis Carroll formula corresponds to the Balian-Brezin conjecture from 1969, which had remained unproven.

## Method

The authors map the HFB matrix elements to Grassmann integrals using Fermion coherent states with Grassmann numbers, then introduce a generating function with Grassmann source variables to evaluate the integrals. By differentiating the generating function and equating two equivalent expressions, they derive a closed-form Pfaffian formula for the matrix elements in terms of the inverse of the skew-symmetric matrix X that appears in the Grassmann integral representation.

## Key numbers

- The matrix X in the Grassmann integral is a 2M x 2M skew-symmetric matrix, where M is the total dimension of the single-particle model space (assumed even).
- For a 2x2 anti-symmetric matrix A with elements (0, a; -a, 0), the Pfaffian is Pf(A) = a.
- The norm-overlap kernel for zero-quasiparticle states is given by Pf(X), removing the sign ambiguity present in the Onishi formula.
- For two-quasiparticle states, the matrix element involves a Pfaffian of a 4x4 sub-matrix of X^{-1}, which expands to three terms matching the generalized Wick's theorem result.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian approach for HFB norm-overlap kernels introduced by Robledo (2009) and the authors' own previous work on one-quasiparticle states (2011) to general multi-quasiparticle states. The formula provides a more compact alternative to the combinatorial expansions required by generalized Wick's theorem as described in Ring and Schuck (1980), and is simpler than the finite-temperature formalism of Perez-Martin and Robledo (2007) and the Pfaffian formulations of Bertsch and Robledo (2011) and Avez and Bender (2012). The derivation resolves the Balian-Brezin conjecture from 1969 by showing its connection to the Pfaffian version of the Lewis Carroll formula.
