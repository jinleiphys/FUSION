# Medium effects on freeze-out of light clusters at NICA energies

> We estimate the chemical freeze-out of light nuclear clusters for NICA energies of above 2 A GeV. On the one hand we use results from the low energy domain of about 35 A MeV, where medium effects have been shown to be important to explain experimental results. On the high energy side of LHC energies the statistical model without medium effects has provided results for the chemical freeze-out. The two approaches extrapolated to NICA energies show a discrepancy that can be attributed to medium effects and that for the deuteron/proton ratio amounts to a factor of about three. These findings underline the importance of a detailed investigation of light cluster production at NICA energies.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out of light nuclear clusters at NICA energies (above 2 A GeV) can be estimated by extrapolating from both low-energy (about 35 A MeV) and high-energy (LHC) domains, but the two approaches show a discrepancy that for the deuteron-to-proton ratio amounts to a factor of about three, which the authors attribute to medium effects that must be investigated further.

## Method

The authors apply a quantum statistical (QS) approach that includes medium effects on nucleons and light clusters due to Pauli blocking and self-energy shifts, using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD2-RMF) model for the single-nucleon quasiparticle shift. They compare the QS model results with two empirical freeze-out parametrizations from Cleymans et al. and Andronic et al., and with low-energy data from Natowitz et al., to predict cluster yields at NICA energies.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron-to-proton ratio discrepancy between the low-energy and high-energy extrapolations at NICA energies is a factor of about three.
- For a laboratory energy of 2 A GeV (center-of-mass energy 2.35 GeV), the Cleymans parametrization gives freeze-out temperature 56.38 MeV and baryon chemical potential 796.8 MeV; the Andronic parametrization gives 52.513 MeV and 779.76 MeV, respectively.
- For 3.85 A GeV (center-of-mass energy 3 GeV), the Cleymans parametrization gives freeze-out temperature 79.956 MeV and baryon chemical potential 719.07 MeV; the Andronic parametrization gives 72.93 MeV and 701.45 MeV.
- For 9.84 A GeV (center-of-mass energy 4.5 GeV), the Cleymans parametrization gives freeze-out temperature 111.8 MeV and baryon chemical potential 586.94 MeV; the Andronic parametrization gives 107.32 MeV and 569.47 MeV.
- A reasonable freeze-out baryon density for NICA energies is about 0.03 fm^{-3}.
- The fit formula d/p = 0.8 [sqrt(s_{NN})/GeV]^{-1.55} + 0.0036 describes the deuteron-to-proton ratio for energies above sqrt(s_{NN}) ~ 4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out concept from low-energy heavy-ion collisions studied by Natowitz et al. and from high-energy LHC collisions studied by Andronic et al. and Cleymans et al., to the intermediate energy region accessible at the NICA facility. The results enable future experimental investigations at NICA to test the role of medium effects in light cluster production, and the authors plan to address this problem within the THESEUS event generator framework of Batyuk et al. (2016) and the three-fluid hydrodynamical model of Ivanov et al. (2017).
