# Weak response of cold symmetric nuclear matter at three-body cluster level

> We studied the Fermi and Gamow-Teller responses of cold symmetric nuclear matter within a unified dynamical model, suitable to account for both short- and long-range correlation effects. The formalism of correlated basis functions has been used to construct two-body effective interactions and one-body effective weak operators. The inclusion of the three-body cluster term allowed for incorporating in the effective interaction a realistic model of three- nucleon forces, namely the UIX potential. Moreover, the sizable unphysical dependence of the effective weak operator is removed once the three-body cluster term is taken into account.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the three-body cluster term in the correlated basis functions (CBF) formalism allows for a microscopic incorporation of the UIX three-nucleon potential into the effective interaction and removes the sizable unphysical dependence of the effective weak operator, which was present in earlier two-body cluster treatments.

## Method

The authors used correlated basis functions theory to construct two-body effective interactions and one-body effective weak operators for cold symmetric nuclear matter. They performed a cluster expansion of the ground-state expectation value of the Hamiltonian, keeping both two-body and three-body cluster contributions, and included the UIX three-nucleon potential at the three-body cluster level. The effective interaction was then used in correlated Fermi gas, correlated Hartree-Fock, and correlated Tamm-Dancoff approximations to compute the Fermi and Gamow-Teller response functions.

## Key numbers

- The effective potentials were calculated for symmetric nuclear matter at a density of $\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$ (shown in Figure 3 for the central and $t\tau$ channels).
- The equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter was computed in the low density regime using both the Argonne $v_{6}^\prime$ potential alone and with the addition of the UIX three-body interaction (shown in Figure 4).
- The Fermi momentum $k_F$ appears in the Slater function $\ell(r_{ij}) = 3[(\sin(k_F r_{ij}) - k_F r_{ij} \cos(k_F r_{ij}))/(k_F r_{ij})^3]$.
- The weak form factors used are $g_V = 1.00$ and $g_A = 1.26$ at zero momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the earlier CBF calculations of Cowell and Pandharipande (2004) and Benhar and Farina (2009), which treated effective interactions only at the two-body cluster level and used a phenomenological density-dependent potential to account for three-nucleon forces. The present approach improves upon those works by explicitly including three-body cluster contributions, enabling a microscopic treatment of the UIX three-body interaction (Pudliner et al., 1995) and removing unphysical dependencies in the effective weak operator. This enables more reliable calculations of neutrino mean free paths in supernovae and neutron star cooling.
