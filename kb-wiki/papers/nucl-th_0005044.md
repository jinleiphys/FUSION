# Clocking hadronization in relativistic heavy ion collisions with balance functions

> A novel state of matter has been hypothesized to exist during the early stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, with normal hadrons not appearing until several fm/c after the start of the reaction. To test this hypothesis, correlations between charges and their associated anticharges are evaluated with the use of balance functions. It is shown that late-stage hadronization is characterized by tightly correlated charge/anticharge pairs when measured as a function of relative rapidity.

## Key claim

Balance functions, which quantify correlations between charges and their associated anticharges as a function of relative rapidity, can distinguish between early-stage quark production (tau < 1 fm/c) and late-stage hadronization (several fm/c after the start of the reaction), provided that rescattering and annihilation effects are accounted for and that comparisons are made to proton-proton collisions at the same center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The authors define a balance function B(p2|p1) as a combination of conditional probabilities that a particle in bin p1 is accompanied by a particle of opposite charge in bin p2, normalized to unity for globally conserved charges. They model a Bjorken boost-invariant expanding source where particles and antiparticles are produced in pairs at the same space-time point following a local thermal distribution, and they incorporate diffusion via a diffusion equation in Bjorken coordinates (tau, eta) with a time-dependent density. They also simulate randomizing collisions and annihilation by modeling pairs produced at initial proper time tau_0 that undergo N_coll collisions before disassociating at final time tau_f, with temperature cooling linearly from 225 MeV to 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Energy densities in Pb collisions at the CERN SPS may surpass 3 GeV/fm^3, with even higher densities expected at RHIC.
* Strange-quark production is expected to take place 5-10 fm/c into the collision when the local temperature has dropped to near 160 MeV.
* For a Bjorken boost-invariant source, the non-relativistic width of the balance function is sigma_y = (2T/m)^(1/2).
* Balance functions are shown for temperatures of 225 MeV and 165 MeV in a Bjorken thermal model.
* The diffusion equation yields a variance sigma_eta^2 = 2 beta ln(tau/tau_0), where beta is a constant related to thermal velocity and cross section.
* In the rescattering model, temperature cools from 225 MeV at tau = 1 fm/c to 120 MeV at tau = 15 fm/c.
* Particles created at tau = 1 fm/c are expected to undergo 10-20 collisions, with the effective number of completely randomizing collisions closer to a half dozen.
* Particles created at tau = 9 fm/c (T = 165 MeV) would undergo an effective 2-3 completely randomizing collisions.
* Approximately 10^5 events are required to determine a balance function with statistical fluctuations at the level of 10^(-2).

## Context

This work extends the use of balance functions from studies of hadronization in jets from proton-antiproton and electron-positron collisions (Drijard et al., 1979; Brandelik et al., 1981; Aihara et al., 1984) to relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the observable can test whether a quark-gluon plasma state existed before hadronization. The proposal contrasts with alternative hadronic explanations for strangeness enhancement such as color ropes (Biro, 1984; Sorge, 1992) or baryon junctions (Vance and Gyulassy, 1999), and builds on discussions of charge fluctuations between hadronic and QGP phases (Jeon and Koch, 2000; Asakawa, Heinz and Muller, 2000). The paper enables experimental searches at RHIC and the SPS using balance functions constructed from electric charge, strangeness, baryon number, or charm to determine the time of quark production and the mobility of quarks in the early stages of the collision.
