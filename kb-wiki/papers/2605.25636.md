# Odderon Form Factors in Reggeized Spin-2 Pomeron and Spin-3 Odderon Exchange in $pp$ and $p\bar p$ Elastic Scattering

> We investigate the form-factor dependence of Reggeized tensor Pomeron and Odderon exchanges in high-energy elastic $pp$ and $p\bar p$ scattering. The spin structure is implemented through explicit covariant spin-2 and spin-3 projectors, kept factorized from the Reggeized scalar kernels, so that vertex effects can be separated from trajectory dynamics. Seven Odderon--proton form-factor parametrizations are tested against a global dataset including TOTEM $pp$ data at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$, $7$, $8$, and $13$~TeV and Tevatron $p\bar p$ data at $\sqrt{s}=1.80$ and $1.96$~TeV. A clear hierarchy is found. Six dipole, polynomial, Gaussian, and hybrid parametrizations give comparable fit qualities, $Ď‡^2_{\rm red}\simeq 1.44$--$1.48$, whereas a one-parameter exponential form, $F_{\mathbb O}(t)=\exp[-B|t|/2]$, yields $Ď‡^2_{\rm red}=0.98$ for 138 degrees of freedom. The fitted couplings and Regge slopes remain comparatively stable across the form-factor choices, indicating that the improvement is driven mainly by the Odderon--proton vertex rather than by large compensating shifts in trajectory parameters. The exponential form admits an impact-parameter interpretation as a Gaussian transverse profile, with an effective radius $\sqrt{\langle b^2\rangle}=\sqrt{2B}\,\hbar c$. The extracted radii are of hadronic size and suggest a peripheral soft Odderon interaction. The shrinking $t$-range over which the single-Regge-exchange description remains accurate at increasing energy indicates the onset of absorptive and unitarity corrections. These results provide a compact phenomenological framework for connecting the $pp/p\bar p$ dip--bump difference with the transverse structure of $C$-odd color-singlet exchange.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Odderon-proton vertex form factor, rather than compensating shifts in Regge trajectory parameters, is the primary driver of fit quality to elastic $pp$ and $p\bar p$ data, and that a simple one-parameter exponential form $F_{\mathbb O}(t)=\exp[-B|t|/2]$ outperforms six more complex parametrizations, achieving $\chi^2_{\rm red}=0.98$ versus $\chi^2_{\rm red}\simeq 1.44$--$1.48$ for the alternatives.

## Method

The authors construct elastic scattering amplitudes for $pp$ and $p\bar p$ using explicit covariant spin-2 and spin-3 projectors for the Pomeron and Odderon, factorized from Reggeized scalar propagators with linear trajectories. Seven Odderon-proton form-factor parametrizations (dipole, quartic dipole, polynomial series, Gaussian, Gaussian-dipole hybrid, Gaussian-polynomial hybrid, and exponential) are tested against a global dataset comprising TOTEM $pp$ data at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$, $7$, $8$, and $13$ TeV and Tevatron $p\bar p$ data at $\sqrt{s}=1.80$ and $1.96$ TeV, with symbolic spin sums performed using Mathematica and FeynCalc.

## Key numbers

- The exponential form factor FF7 yields $\chi^2_{\rm red}=0.98$ for 138 degrees of freedom, while the six other parametrizations give $\chi^2_{\rm red}\simeq 1.44$--$1.48$.
- The Pomeron intercept extracted from the fit is $\alpha_{\mathbb P}(0)\approx 1.30$, lying between the canonical soft-Pomeron value ($\approx 1.08$) and the LO BFKL hard-Pomeron value ($\approx 1.4$).
- The Odderon trajectory slope is fixed at $\alpha'_{\mathbb O}=0.29~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ and the Odderon effective mass at $M_{\mathbb O}=3.201~\mathrm{GeV}$, with the Pomeron slope $\alpha'_{\mathbb P}=0.25~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ and mass $M_{\mathbb P}=1.918~\mathrm{GeV}$.
- The exponential form factor admits an impact-parameter interpretation with effective radius $\sqrt{\langle b^2\rangle}=\sqrt{2B}\,\hbar c$, yielding radii of hadronic size consistent with a peripheral soft Odderon interaction.

## Context

This work extends the tensor Pomeron-Odderon framework of Ewerz, Maniatis, and Nachtmann (2014) and Magallanes et al. (2024) by systematically testing seven Odderon-proton form-factor parametrizations within a coherent spin-2 and spin-3 exchange formalism. The results provide a compact phenomenological framework connecting the $pp/p\bar p$ dip-bump difference observed by TOTEM and D0 to the transverse structure of $C$-odd color-singlet exchange, while the shrinking $t$-range of validity at increasing energy signals the onset of absorptive and unitarity corrections that future work should incorporate.
