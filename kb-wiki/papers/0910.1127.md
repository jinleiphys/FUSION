# Evaluation of coincidence number of pairs and asymmetry parameter in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay

> We develop a theoretical framework for the evaluation of the coincidence number of pairs, $N_{nN}$, and the asymmetry parameter, $a_Λ$, in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay $ΛN\rightarrow nN$. The primary nomesonic weak hyperon-nucleon decay process is described through the independent particle shell model. When the effect of the strong interactions experimented by the two nucleons leaving the residual nucleus is included, we have in addition to the $2p1h$ primary configuration $2p'1h'$, $3p2h$, $4p3h$, $\cdots$, etc secondary ones. We work within the $2p'1h'$ subspace of final states assuming a simple approach for finite nuclei based on the eikonal approximation. An optical potential including the nucleon-nucleus isoscalar and isovector interaction is introduced to take into account the minimum nuclear medium effects and to describe the nucleon-nucleus dispersion process along the outgoing path. We applied the proposed description to the calculation of the observables in $^{5}_ΛHe$ and $^{12}_ΛC$ nonmesonic decays. The calculated results show that our treatment of FSI even considered as restricted to a subspace of states gives a good agreement with experimental data, similar to that ones presented by more elaborated evaluations. This indicates that the developed theoretical scheme seems to be appropriated to include more complex configurations. We also discuss the ground state normalization effects at the moment of including the two-nucleon induced decay ($ΛNN \rightarrow nNN$) contribution.

# Evaluation of coincidence number of pairs and asymmetry parameter in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay

## Key claim

The authors develop a simplified theoretical framework using the eikonal approximation with an isospin-dependent optical potential to include final state interactions (FSI) in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay, and show that even within a restricted $2p'1h'$ subspace of final states, their treatment gives good agreement with experimental data for $N_{nn}/N_{np}$ and $a_\Lambda$ in $^5_\Lambda$He and $^{12}_\Lambda$C, similar to more elaborated evaluations. However, the FSI effects included are described as the "minimum effect of nucleon-nucleus interaction" and cannot reproduce the sign of $a_\Lambda$ for $^5_\Lambda$He, indicating that additional modifications (such as two-pion correlated or $a_1$-meson exchange) are still needed.

## Method

The primary nonmesonic weak decay $\Lambda N \rightarrow nN$ is described using the independent particle shell model with a one-meson-exchange transition potential comprising the complete pseudoscalar and vector meson octets ($\pi$, $\eta$, $K$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$). Final state interactions are incorporated by modifying the outgoing nucleon plane waves within the nonrelativistic eikonal approximation, using an optical potential with isoscalar ($\tilde{V}_0$) and isovector ($\tilde{V}_1$) depths to describe nucleon-nucleus scattering along the outgoing path. The observables $N_{nN}$ (coincidence number of pairs) and $a_\Lambda$ (asymmetry parameter) are evaluated for $^5_\Lambda$He and $^{12}_\Lambda$C decays, working within the $2p'1h'$ subspace of final states.

## Key numbers

- For $^5_\Lambda$He with no FSI: $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.248$, $a_\Lambda = -0.538$
- For $^5_\Lambda$He with FSI ($\tilde{V}_0 = 15$ MeV, $\tilde{V}_1/2A = 13.75$ MeV): $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.365$, $a_\Lambda = -0.477$
- For $^{12}_\Lambda$C with no FSI: $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.212$, $a_\Lambda = -0.530$
- For $^{12}_\Lambda$C with FSI ($\tilde{V}_0 = 20$ MeV, $\tilde{V}_1/2A = 5$ MeV): $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.265$, $a_\Lambda = -0.492$
- Experimental data for $^5_\Lambda$He: $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.45 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.03$ (Outa 2005, Kang 2006); $a_\Lambda = 0.07 \pm 0.08^{+0.08}_{-0.00}$ (Bhang 2005, Maruta 2006) and $0.11 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.04$ (Outa 2005, Maruta 2005)
- Experimental data for $^{12}_\Lambda$C: $N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.40 \pm 0.10$ (Bahn 2004, Kim 2006); $a_\Lambda = -0.20 \pm 0.26 \pm 0.04$ (Outa 2005, Maruta 2005)
- The eikonal approximation uses average momentum $<p> \sim 400$ MeV/$c$ and nuclear radius $R = 1.25 A^{1/3}$ fm
- Short range correlations use $q_c = 3.93$ fm$^{-1}$ in the Jastrow-like correlation function

## Context

This work extends previous shell model calculations by Barbero et al. (2008) and provides a simpler alternative to intranuclear cascade codes (Ramos 1997, Garbarino 2003, 2004, 2005) and microscopic nuclear matter formalisms (Bauer 2007) for including FSI in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay. The approach uses an isospin-dependent optical potential not considered in earlier optical model treatments (Ramos 1992, Conti 2009). The results enable future inclusion of more complex configurations such as $3p2h$ states and two-nucleon induced decay ($\Lambda NN \rightarrow nNN$), as well as incorporation of two-pion correlated or $a_1$-meson exchange to improve the description of the asymmetry parameter.
