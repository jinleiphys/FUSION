# Light Nuclei as Quantized Skyrmions: Energy Spectra and Form Factors

> We review the SU(2) Skyrme model and describe its topological soliton solutions, which are called Skyrmions. Skyrmions provide a model of nuclei in which the conserved topological charge is identified with the baryon number of a nucleus. The semiclassical quantum theory of Skyrmions, in which they are treated as rigid bodies spinning in space and isospace, is described. We derive the energy spectra corresponding to various light nuclei, and predict a few new states. We also calculate the electromagnetic form factors describing the structure of the alpha-particle and lithium-6. Our recent reparametrization of the model gives results that are in reasonable quantitative agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model, when reparametrized by fitting to the lithium-6 ground state rather than to the nucleon and delta masses, yields energy spectra and electromagnetic form factors for light nuclei that are in "reasonable quantitative agreement with experiment," though the energy splittings for lithium-6 are too large by a factor of three to four.

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) Skyrme model with a pion mass term, finding classical Skyrmion solutions for baryon numbers B=4, 6, and 8 via the rational map ansatz. They then apply semiclassical quantization by promoting the rotational and isorotational collective coordinates to quantum variables, treating the Skyrmions as rigid bodies, and imposing Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints to enforce fermionic statistics. Electromagnetic form factors are computed by evaluating matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator between quantized Skyrmion states in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- New Skyrme parameters: e = 3.26, Fπ = 75.2 MeV, mπ = 138 MeV (dimensionless pion mass m = 1.125)
- B=4 (α-particle) ground state mass: 3679 MeV (experimental α-particle mass: 3727 MeV, within 2%)
- B=4 spin 2, isospin 1 excitation energy: 28.7 MeV (experimental average for A=4 isotriplet: 23.7 MeV)
- B=4 predicted spin 4, isospin 0 excitation energy: 39.4 MeV
- B=6 (lithium-6) ground state (spin 1, isospin 0): 5601 MeV
- B=6 spin 3, isospin 0 excitation energy: 10.3 MeV (experimental: 2.2 MeV)
- B=6 spin 0, isospin 1 excitation energy: 12.1 MeV
- B=8 (beryllium-8) spin 2, isospin 0 excitation energy: 2.9 MeV (experimental: 3 MeV)
- B=8 spin 2 isotriplet excitation energy: 13.3 MeV (experimental average: 16.5 MeV)
- B=8 spin 3 isotriplet excitation energy: 16.2 MeV (experimental average: 19.0 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to nuclear physics pioneered by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), who first quantized the B=1 Skyrmion to describe nucleons and deltas. The authors' reparametrization, based on fitting to lithium-6 properties (Manton and Wood, 2006), replaces the traditional parameter set that gave e=4.84 and Fπ=108 MeV. The paper builds on the rational map ansatz of Houghton, Manton, and Sutcliffe (1998) and the Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraint formalism of Krusch (2006). The results enable further predictions of excited states and form factors for light nuclei, including a predicted spin 0 negative parity isotriplet for B=8 that could correspond to new ground states of lithium-8 and boron-8.
