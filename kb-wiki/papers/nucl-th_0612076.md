# Analyzing Correlation Functions with Tesseral and Cartesian Spherical Harmonics

> The dependence of inter-particle correlations on the orientation of particle relative-momentum can yield unique information on the space-time features of emission in reactions with multiparticle final states. In the present paper, the benefits of a representation and analysis of the three-dimensional correlation information in terms of surface spherical harmonics is presented. The harmonics include the standard complex tesseral harmonics and the real cartesian harmonics. Mathematical properties of the lesser-known cartesian harmonics are illuminated. The physical content of different angular harmonic components in a correlation is described. The resolving power of different final-state effects with regarding to determining angular features of emission regions is investigated. The considered final-state effects include identity interference and strong and Coulomb interactions. The correlation analysis in terms of spherical harmonics is illustrated with the cases of gaussian and blast-wave sources for proton-charged meson and baryon-baryon pairs.

## Key claim

The paper argues that analyzing three-dimensional two-particle correlation functions using spherical harmonic expansions, both complex tesseral and real cartesian, provides a systematic way to extract angular information about the emission source shape, and that different final-state effects (identity interference, Coulomb, and strong interactions) all offer significant resolving power for different harmonic orders \(\ell\), contrary to the naive expectation that \(s\)-wave-dominated strong interactions would preclude shape determination.

## Method

The authors derive the relation between the correlation function \(\mathcal{R}(\mathbf{q})\) and the source function \(\mathcal{S}(\mathbf{r})\) via an integral transform with kernel \(\mathcal{K}(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{r}) = |\phi^{(-)}(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{r})|^2 - 1\), where \(\phi^{(-)}\) is the two-particle relative wavefunction. They expand both \(\mathcal{R}\) and \(\mathcal{S}\) in tesseral spherical harmonics \(Y_{\ell m}\) and in cartesian spherical harmonics \(\mathcal{A}_{\vec{\ell}}\), showing that the expansion coefficients are related by one-dimensional integral transforms with partial kernels \(\mathcal{K}_\ell(q,r)\). The structure of \(\mathcal{K}_\ell\) is then analyzed for three classes of final-state interactions: identical spin-0 boson interference, repulsive Coulomb interaction (both classical and quantal), and short-range strong interactions.

## Key numbers

* For identical spin-0 bosons, the kernel is \(\mathcal{K}_\ell(q,r) = (-1)^{\ell/2} j_\ell(2qr)\) for even \(\ell\) and zero for odd \(\ell\), where \(j_\ell\) is the spherical Bessel function.
* For classical repulsive Coulomb interaction, the dimensionless parameter \(x = r_C/r\) with \(r_C = 2\mu Z_1 Z_2 e^2 / q^2\); the high-energy limit \(x \ll 1\) yields \(\mathcal{K}_\ell(q,r) \approx (-1)^{\ell+1} x/2\) for \(qr \gg \max(1,\ell)\).
* For \(pK^+\) interactions, the classical Coulomb radius at \(q = 75\) MeV/c is \(r_C = 0.17\) fm; at \(q = 15\) MeV/c it rises to \(r_C = 4.1\) fm.
* The optimal momentum range for accessing directional characteristics of sources with typical size \(R \sim 5\) fm in the \(pK^+\) system is \(q \sim (30-100)\) MeV/c.
* For classical Coulomb at high \(q\), the correlation coefficients behave as \(\mathcal{R}_{\ell m}(q) \approx (2\sqrt{\pi} (-1)^\ell Z_1 Z_2 e^2 \mu / q^2) \langle (1/r) Y_{\ell m}^*(\Omega) \rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the imaging technique previously applied to angle-averaged correlation functions (Verde 2003, Brown 2000) to full three-dimensional source reconstruction using spherical harmonic decomposition. It parallels the tesseral-harmonic approach for pure interference described by Brown (2005), while introducing cartesian harmonics (Applequist 1989) as a real-valued alternative with direct geometric interpretation. The paper enables systematic extraction of source anisotropies, such as \(\ell=1\) moments revealing species offsets, \(\ell=2\) moments giving ellipsoidal deformation (\(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\)), and \(\ell=3\) moments probing "boomerang" shapes, from correlation data in heavy-ion collisions, addressing the puzzle of the RHIC \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) discrepancy between data and hydrodynamic predictions.
