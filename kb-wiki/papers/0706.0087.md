# Superscaling and Neutral Current Quasielastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model

> The superscaling analysis is extended to include quasielastic (QE) scattering via the weak neutral current of neutrinos and antineutrinos from nuclei. The scaling function obtained within the coherent density fluctuation model (used previously in calculations of QE inclusive electron and charge-changing (CC) neutrino scattering) is applied to neutral current neutrino and antineutrino scattering with energies of 1 GeV from $^{12}$C with a proton and neutron knockout (u-channel inclusive processes). The results are compared with those obtained using the scaling function from the relativistic Fermi gas model and the scaling function as determined from the superscaling analysis (SuSA) of QE electron scattering.

## Key claim

The superscaling analysis, previously applied to electron and charge-changing neutrino scattering, is extended to neutral-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C at 1 GeV, using the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) scaling function. The results show that an asymmetric CDFM scaling function (with parameter $c_1 = 0.60$) yields cross sections close to those from the superscaling analysis (SuSA) of electron scattering, while a symmetric CDFM scaling function ($c_1 = 0.75$) gives results similar to the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model.

## Method

The authors calculate differential cross sections for neutral-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on $^{12}$C at 1 GeV, for both proton and neutron knockout (u-channel inclusive processes). The cross sections are factorized into a single-nucleon cross section and a scaling function; the scaling function is obtained from the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) using a symmetrized Fermi-type density distribution for $^{12}$C with half-radius $R_{1/2}=2.470$ fm and diffuseness $b=0.420$ fm. Two forms of the asymmetric CDFM scaling function are employed: one with a parabolic form and one with an exponential form for positive $\psi^\prime$ values, each tested with parameter values $c_1 = 0.60$ (asymmetric) and $c_1 = 0.75$ (symmetric). Results are compared with those from the RFG model and from the SuSA empirical scaling function.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutrino and antineutrino energy: 1 GeV.
- Target nucleus: $^{12}$C.
- Half-radius parameter for the symmetrized Fermi density: $R_{1/2} = 2.470$ fm; diffuseness parameter: $b = 0.420$ fm; resulting charge rms radius: $2.47$ fm (matching the experimental value).
- The CDFM scaling function is calculated for two values of the asymmetry parameter: $c_1 = 0.60$ (asymmetric) and $c_1 = 0.75$ (symmetric).
- At forward scattering angles ($20^\circ$), the neutrino cross section maximum is roughly an order of magnitude larger than the antineutrino cross section maximum for proton knockout; at $60^\circ$, the ratio is approximately $2:1$.
- Neutron knockout cross sections are 30-50% higher than proton knockout cross sections (except for antineutrinos at forward angles).

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach of Antonov et al. (2006) and the SuSA framework of Barbaro et al. (2004) from electron and charge-changing neutrino scattering to neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering. It builds on the CDFM scaling function previously applied to electron and charge-changing neutrino reactions by Antonov et al. (2006), and follows the u-channel formalism and scaling criteria established by Barbaro et al. (2006) for the RFG model. The results enable predictions for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections at intermediate-to-high energies, with potential applications to neutrino oscillation experiments, and suggest future extension to the $\Delta$-resonance region.
