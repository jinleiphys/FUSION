# Searching for Lepton Flavour (Universality) Violation and Collider Signals from a Singly-Charged Scalar Singlet

> In recent years, evidence for lepton flavour universality violation beyond the Standard Model has been accumulated. In this context, a singly charged $SU(2)_L$ singlet scalar ($ϕ^\pm$) is very interesting, as it can only have flavour off-diagonal couplings to neutrinos and charged leptons, therefore necessarily violating lepton flavour (universality). In fact, it gives a (necessarily constructive) tree-level effect in $\ell\to\ell^\primeνν$ processes, while contributing to charged lepton flavour violating only at the loop-level. Therefore, it can provide a common explanation of the hints for new physics in $τ\toμνν/τ(μ)\to eνν$ and of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly. Such an explanation predicts ${\rm Br }[τ\to eγ]$ to be of the order of a few times $10^{-11}$ while ${ \rm Br}[τ\to eμμ]$ can be of the order of $10^{-9}$ for order one couplings and therefore in the reach of forthcoming experiments. Furthermore, we derive a {novel} coupling-independent lower limit on the scalar mass of $\approx 200\,$GeV by recasting LHC slepton searches. In the scenario preferred by low energy precision data, the lower limit is even strengthened to $\approx300\,$GeV, showing the complementary between LHC searches and flavour observables. Furthermore, we point out that this model can be tested by reinterpreting DM mono-photon searches at future $e^+e^-$ colliders.

## Key claim
A singly charged SU(2)L singlet scalar can simultaneously explain the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly and the hints for lepton flavour universality violation in tau decays, provided that the coupling λ13 is vanishingly small to avoid the μ→eγ bound. The model predicts Br[τ→eγ] of order a few times 10−11 and Br[τ→eμμ] of order 10−9 for order-one couplings, within reach of forthcoming experiments.

## Method
The authors supplement the Standard Model with a singly charged SU(2)L singlet scalar φ± that has antisymmetric Yukawa couplings λij to left-handed lepton doublets. They compute tree-level contributions to ℓ→ℓ′νν decays, loop-level contributions to ℓ→ℓ′γ and ℓ→ℓ′ℓ′′ℓ′′′ decays, and recast ATLAS slepton searches (139 fb−1) to set mass limits via Drell-Yan pair production. A global fit to electroweak observables, CKM elements from superallowed beta decays, kaon decays, and tau decays is performed using HEPfit.

## Key numbers
- Preferred NP effect in μ→eνν from global fit: δ(μ→eνν) = 0.00065(15)
- Coupling-independent lower limit on scalar mass from LHC: mφ ≈ 200 GeV
- Lower limit on scalar mass in the region preferred by low-energy data: mφ ≈ 300 GeV
- Predicted Br[τ→eγ] of order a few × 10−11
- Predicted Br[τ→eμμ] ≈ 10−10 × (mφ/5 TeV)4
- LEP monophoton bound translates to |λ12,13²|/mφ² ≲ 1/(175 GeV)²
- Experimental limits used: Br[μ→eγ] ≤ 4.2×10−13, Br[τ→μγ] ≤ 4.4×10−8, Br[τ→eγ] ≤ 3.3×10−8 (all at 90% C.L.)

## Context
This work extends the study of singly charged scalars previously considered in the Babu-Zee model (Zee 1985, Babu 1988) and later works (Krauss 2002, Nebot 2007, Herrero-Garcia 2014, 2017) for neutrino mass generation, but here focuses on the scalar as a standalone explanation of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly (Belfatto 2019, Grossman 2019, Crivellin 2020) and LFU violation in tau decays (Amhis 2019). The model is one of only four NP candidates that give constructive interference with the SM in ℓ→ℓ′νν, alongside vectorlike leptons (Crivellin 2020), a left-handed vector SU(2)L triplet (Capdevila 2020), and a left-handed Z′ with flavour-violating couplings. The results enable future tests at BELLE II, FCC-ee, HL-LHC, and FCC-hh through complementary flavour and collider searches.
