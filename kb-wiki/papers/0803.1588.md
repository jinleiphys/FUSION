# Pion and muon production in electron-positron photon plasma

> We study production and equilibration of pions and muons in relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma at a temperature $T\ll m_Ој, m_ПҖ$. We argue that the observation of pions and muons can be a diagnostic tool in the study of the initial properties of such a plasma formed by means of strong laser fields. Conversely, properties of muons and pions in thermal environment become accessible to precise experimental study.

## Key claim

The authors argue that pions and muons produced in electron-positron-photon plasma at temperatures much lower than the particle masses (T вүӘ m_Ој, m_ПҖ) can serve as diagnostic tools for studying the initial properties of such plasma formed by strong laser fields, but note that the neutral pion ПҖ^0, while most copiously produced, is the most difficult to detect experimentally because its decay products arrive at detectors nearly simultaneously with the electromagnetic pulse from the decaying plasma fireball.

## Context

This work extends studies of relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma properties reported by Thoma (2008) to include heavy particle production as a diagnostic tool. The authors build on prior work by Tajima and Mourou on laser-produced plasmas and by Kuznetsova, Kodama, and Rafelski (2008) on ПҖ^0 production via two-photon fusion. The results enable experimental study of in-medium pion mass splitting at temperatures T вүі О”m and provide a framework for understanding the early stages of plasma formation in upcoming facilities like the European Light Infrastructure (ELI).
