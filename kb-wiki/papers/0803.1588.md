# Pion and muon production in electron-positron photon plasma

> We study production and equilibration of pions and muons in relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma at a temperature $T\ll m_μ, m_π$. We argue that the observation of pions and muons can be a diagnostic tool in the study of the initial properties of such a plasma formed by means of strong laser fields. Conversely, properties of muons and pions in thermal environment become accessible to precise experimental study.

## Key claim

The authors argue that pions and muons produced in electron-positron-photon plasma at temperatures much lower than the particle masses (T ≪ m_μ, m_π) can serve as diagnostic tools for studying the initial properties of such plasma formed by strong laser fields, but note that the neutral pion π^0, while most copiously produced, is the most difficult to detect experimentally because its decay products arrive at detectors nearly simultaneously with the electromagnetic pulse from the decaying plasma fireball.

## Method

The authors compute production rates and relaxation times for pions (π^0, π^±) and muons (μ^±) in a relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma using detailed balance relations and invariant reaction rate integrals. For π^0 production, they consider two-photon fusion (γγ → π^0) as the dominant process, while for muons they evaluate both photon fusion (γγ → μ^+μ^-) and electron-positron fusion (e^+e^- → μ^+μ^-) using QED matrix elements. For charged pions, they consider π^0π^0 charge exchange scattering as the dominant production mechanism, with cross sections taken from chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion lifetime in vacuum: τ_π^0 = (8.4 ± 0.6) × 10^{-17} s
- Pion mass splitting: Δm = m_π± − m_π^0 = 4.594 MeV, corresponding to Δm/m̄ = 3.34%
- Muon mass: m_μ = 106 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 140 MeV
- At T = 10 MeV, electron mean free path: l_e ≈ (10 MeV/T)^3 × (E/31.1 MeV)^2 × 0.37 nm
- At T = 10 MeV, photon mean free path: l_γ ≈ (10 MeV/T)^2 × (E/27.5 MeV) × 0.28 nm
- A plasma drop of radius 2 nm at T = 10 MeV contains 13 kJ of energy
- Branching ratio for π^0 → e^+e^-: B = (6.2 ± 0.5) × 10^{-8}
- π^0 charge exchange cross section parameter: a_0^(0) − a_0^(2) = 0.27/M_π±

## Context

This work extends studies of relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma properties reported by Thoma (2008) to include heavy particle production as a diagnostic tool. The authors build on prior work by Tajima and Mourou on laser-produced plasmas and by Kuznetsova, Kodama, and Rafelski (2008) on π^0 production via two-photon fusion. The results enable experimental study of in-medium pion mass splitting at temperatures T ≳ Δm and provide a framework for understanding the early stages of plasma formation in upcoming facilities like the European Light Infrastructure (ELI).
