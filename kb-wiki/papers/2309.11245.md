# Speed of Sound and Phase Transitions in Neutron Stars Indicated by the Thick Neutron Skin of $^{208}$Pb

> The speed of sound is a novel probe of equation of state and phase transitions in dense cores of neutron stars. Recently nuclear experiments extracted a surprising thick neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb, causing tensions to reproduce the tidal deformability in gravitational-wave observations. This work finds that exotic structures in the speed of sound with a small softening slope followed by a steep-rising peak are required to reconcile the thick neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb with astronomical observations of neutron stars. Furthermore, the peak of speed of sound is narrowly constrained around two times the nuclear saturation density with the thick neutron skin. Consequently early and strong first-order phase transitions are comparatively more favorable.

## Key claim

The paper finds that reconciling the thick neutron skin of 208Pb (0.283 ± 0.071 fm from PREX-II) with astronomical observations of neutron stars requires exotic structures in the speed of sound: a small softening slope at densities 1.2–1.5 times nuclear saturation density followed by a steep-rising peak narrowly constrained around 2.0 times saturation density, making early and strong first-order phase transitions comparatively more favorable.

## Method

The authors first optimized a set of new Skyrme density functionals by fitting properties of finite nuclei (binding energies and charge radii of 50 nuclei) and infinite nuclear matter around saturation density, including the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and 48Ca with varying fitting weights. They then constructed the equation of state at densities above 1.2 times saturation density using analytical curves for the speed of sound (a monotonically increasing 4th-order polynomial from the starting density to the peak, followed by a decreasing segment matching the conformal limit of 1/√3), and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm from PREX-II experiments, with three optimized functionals yielding 0.22, 0.247, and 0.284 fm respectively
- The peak of the speed of sound squared is narrowly constrained around 2.0 times nuclear saturation density (n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^-3) for the thick neutron skin case
- Tidal deformability constraint used: Λ = 519 at 1.4 solar masses (a more stringent limit than the observational upper bound of 580 from GW170817)
- Maximum mass constraints applied: 2.07 solar masses from PSR J0740+6620 and 2.35 solar masses from pulsar J0952-0607
- Energy densities at different transition densities: 920.2 MeV fm^-3 at 3.0 n_sat, 1215.9 MeV fm^-3 at 3.5 n_sat, 1547.2 MeV fm^-3 at 4.0 n_sat, and 1913.2 MeV fm^-3 at 4.5 n_sat

## Context

This work extends separate studies of the thick neutron skin of 208Pb (from PREX and PREX-II experiments) and of speed of sound structures in neutron stars (from Refs. sound1, sound-2, sound-3) by performing the first combined analysis. It enables future constraints on the equation of state at intermediate densities (a few times saturation density), the possible appearance of hyperons, and the nature of first-order phase transitions to quark matter, while emphasizing that more precise measurements of the 208Pb neutron skin are highly expected due to their significant consequences.
