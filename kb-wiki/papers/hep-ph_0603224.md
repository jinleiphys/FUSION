# Subthreshold rho contribution in J/psi decay to omega pion pion and Kaon anti-Kaon pion

> We carry out a theoretical and Monte Carlo study on the $J/ψ$ decays into $ωππ$ and $K\bar{K}π$ through intermediate subthreshold $ρ$ meson by using SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian approach. It is found that the subthreshold $ρ$ contribution is not negligible and may have significant influence on partial wave analysis of resonances in these channels, especially near the $ωπ$ and $K \bar{K}$ thresholds.

## Key claim

The subthreshold $\rho$ meson exchange contribution to $J/\psi \to \omega\pi\pi$ and $J/\psi \to K\bar{K}\pi$ decays is not negligible and may have significant influence on partial wave analysis of resonances in these channels, especially near the $\omega\pi$ and $K\bar{K}$ thresholds, contrary to the naive assumption that such contributions can be neglected because the $\rho$ mass is more than 150 MeV below these thresholds.

## Method

The authors use SU(3)-symmetric effective Lagrangians for vector-vector-pseudoscalar (VVP) and pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector (PPV) couplings to construct amplitudes for s-channel subthreshold $\rho$ exchange diagrams. They compute the differential decay widths and perform Monte Carlo simulations, employing both Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factors and monopole form factors with varying cut-off parameters, and test both constant and energy-dependent $\rho$ widths that include $\pi\pi$, $\omega\pi$, and $K\bar{K}$ decay channels.

## Key numbers

- The subthreshold $\rho$ contribution to $J/\psi \to \omega\pi\pi$ ranges from 3.1% to 21.0% of the experimental width, depending on form factor and $\rho$ width choice.
- The subthreshold $\rho$ contribution to $J/\psi \to K\bar{K}\pi$ ranges from 0.9% to 4.5% of the experimental width.
- The coupling constant $g_{\rho\pi\pi}$ is determined as 8.18 (for form factor $F_1$ with constant $\Gamma_\rho=0.149$ GeV) and varies between 6.05 and 10.69 across form factor choices.
- The coupling constant $g_{\psi\rho\pi}$ is determined as $8.61 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$ (for $F_1$ with constant $\Gamma_\rho=0.149$ GeV) and varies between $2.31 \times 10^{-3}$ and $8.70 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The coupling constant $g_{\omega\rho\pi}$ is determined as 14.56 GeV$^{-1}$ (for $F_1$ with constant $\Gamma_\rho=0.149$ GeV) and varies between 11.82 and 14.68 GeV$^{-1}$.
- The $\rho$ meson width is taken as a constant 0.149 GeV or as an energy-dependent width including $\pi\pi$, $\omega\pi$, and $K\bar{K}$ channels.

## Context

This work extends the t-channel meson exchange final state interaction mechanism previously applied to near-threshold structures in $J/\psi$ decays (Wu and Zou, 2003) to address two specific puzzles: the "$b_1$ puzzle" where DM2 and BESII collaborations measured a much broader width for $b_1(1235)$ than the PDG value of $(142\pm 9)$ MeV, and a near-$K\bar{K}$-threshold enhancement observed in DM2 data for $J/\psi \to K^+K^-\pi^0$ and $J/\psi \to K_S K^\pm \pi^\mp$. The results suggest that subthreshold $\rho$ exchange should be included as a background in partial wave analyses of these channels, potentially explaining the $b_1$ width discrepancy and the $K\bar{K}$ threshold enhancement without invoking new resonances.
