# Jet quenching in a multi-stage Monte Carlo approach

> We present a jet quenching model within a unified multi-stage framework and demonstrate for the first time a simultaneous description of leading hadrons, inclusive jets, and elliptic flow observables which spans multiple centralities and collision energies. This highlights one of the major successes of the JETSCAPE framework in providing a tool for setting up an effective parton evolution that includes a high-virtuality radiation dominated energy loss phase (MATTER), followed by a low-virtuality scattering dominated (LBT) energy loss phase. Measurements of jet and charged-hadron $R_{AA}$ set strong constraints on the jet quenching model. Jet-medium response is also included through a weakly-coupled transport description.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a multi-stage jet quenching model, combining a high-virtuality radiation-dominated phase (MATTER) with a low-virtuality scattering-dominated phase (LBT) within the JETSCAPE framework, can simultaneously describe leading hadron, inclusive jet, and elliptic flow observables across multiple centralities and collision energies for the first time. The key qualifier is that the switching virtuality parameter Q0 and coupling alpha_s are tuned to fit the most central 5.02A TeV data, and then applied without further adjustment to other centralities and energies.

## Method
The authors used the JETSCAPE framework to simulate PbPb collisions at 5.02A TeV and 2.76A TeV, employing TRENTO+PYTHIA for initial state hard scattering and (2+1)-D VISHNU hydrodynamics for medium evolution. Parton energy loss was modeled in two stages: a high-virtuality, radiation-dominated phase using the MATTER Monte Carlo generator based on the Higher-Twist formalism, followed by a low-virtuality, scattering-dominated phase using the LBT Monte Carlo generator based on the linear Boltzmann equation, with switching performed on a parton-by-parton basis. Jet-medium response was included through a weakly-coupled transport description of recoil partons, and final hadronization used PYTHIA string fragmentation.

## Key numbers
- Jet R_AA and charged-hadron R_AA were computed for most central 0-10% PbPb collisions at 5.02A TeV for switching virtualities Q0 = 1, 2, and 3 GeV, with jet-medium coupling alpha_s = 0.25; the parameter set Q0 = 2 GeV and alpha_s = 0.25 provided the best simultaneous description of both observables.
- In semi-peripheral 30-40% PbPb collisions at 5.02A TeV, the model described jet R_AA, charged-hadron R_AA, and azimuthal anisotropy v2 within 10% of experimental measurements using parameters extracted from central collisions.
- For most central 0-5% PbPb collisions at 2.76A TeV, the model described jet R_AA and charged-hadron R_AA using parameters extracted from 5.02A TeV central collisions without re-tuning.
- In pp collisions at 5.02 TeV, JETSCAPE results for inclusive jet cross-sections and charged-hadron yields were compatible with experimental data within 10%, while PYTHIA showed discrepancies up to 25% for jets.

## Context
This work extends the JETSCAPE framework (Kumar 2019, Putschke 2019) by demonstrating a unified multi-stage approach that simultaneously describes multiple observables across different collision energies and centralities, which had not been achieved previously. The MATTER component is based on the Higher-Twist formalism (Majumder 2009, PhysRevC.88.014909) and the LBT component on the linear Boltzmann equation (PhysRevLett.111.062301, PhysRevC.91.054908). The results enable future Bayesian calibration of the soft sector and studies of intra-jet and heavy-quark observables, as referenced in the paper.
