# Monopole excitation of the Hoyle state and linear-chain state in 12C

> Background: Two new $J^π=0^+$ states are recently observed around a few MeV above the Hoyle state (the second $0^+$ state in 12C). The characters of them are only poorly discussed in theory and are still mysterious. Purpose: I give for the first time a comprehensive understanding of the structures of the $0^+$ states by analyzing their wave functions, and discuss relationship with the Hoyle state, similarities, and differences between the states. Method: I extend a microscopic $α$-cluster model called Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function so as to incorporate $2α+α$ asymmetric configuration explicitly. The so-called $r^2$-constraint method to effectively eliminate spurious continuum components is also used. Results: The $0_3^+$ state is shown to have a very large squared overlap with a single configuration of the extended THSR wave function in an orthogonal space to the Hoyle state as well as to the ground state. The $0_4^+$ state has a maximal squared overlap with a single extended THSR wave function with an extremely prolately-deformed shape. Conclusions: The $0_3^+$ state appears as a family of the Hoyle state to have a higher nodal structure in the internal motions of the $3α$ clusters, due to the orthogonalization to the Hoyle state. The $0_4^+$ state dominantly has a linear-chain structure, where the $3α$ clusters move freely in a non-localized way, like a one-dimensional gas of the $3α$ clusters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $0_3^+$ state in 12C is a "family" of the Hoyle state arising from orthogonalization to it, and the $0_4^+$ state is dominantly a linear-chain structure with non-localized, gaslike 3α clusters; however, these conclusions depend on the specific extended Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function framework and the use of the r²-constraint method to eliminate spurious continuum components.

## Method

The author extended the microscopic α-cluster THSR wave function to allow explicit 2α+α asymmetric configurations, using two separate deformation parameters (β₁, β₂) for the two Jacobi coordinates. The r²-constraint method was applied to remove spurious continuum components by solving an eigenvalue equation for the root-mean-square radius operator and discarding eigenstates with eigenvalues above a cutoff of R_cut = 6.0 fm. The Hamiltonian used the Volkov No. 2 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with slightly modified strength parameters, and squared overlaps between the calculated 0⁺ states and single THSR configurations were computed both with and without projection operators that subtract lower-lying 0⁺ states.

## Key numbers

- The $0_2^+$ (Hoyle) state has a squared overlap of 0.992 with a single THSR configuration in the space orthogonal to the ground state, at deformation parameters (β_x=β_y=5.0 fm, β_z=1.5 fm).
- The $0_3^+$ state has a maximal squared overlap of 0.89 with a single THSR configuration in the space orthogonal to both the ground state and the Hoyle state, at (β_x=β_y=4.1 fm, β_z=1.6 fm).
- The $0_4^+$ state has a maximal squared overlap of 0.47 with a single THSR configuration at extremely prolate deformation (β_x=β_y=0.6 fm, β_z=6.7 fm).
- The calculated energy of the $0_3^+$ state relative to the 3α threshold is 2.6 MeV (experimental: 1.77(9) MeV), and for the $0_4^+$ state it is 3.9 MeV (experimental: 3.29(6) MeV).
- The calculated monopole transition strength M(E0, 0₃⁺ → 0₂⁺) is approximately 35 fm², much larger than the single-particle strength.
- The r.m.s. radius of the $0_3^+$ state is 4.7 fm, the largest among the 0⁺ states.

## Context

This work extends the THSR wave function approach previously applied to the Hoyle state by Funaki and collaborators (Funaki 2003, 2005, 2009) to the newly observed $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states identified experimentally by Itoh et al. (2013). It contrasts with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) and Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) calculations (Chernykh 2007, En'yo 2007), which reproduce the $0_4^+$ state but miss the $0_3^+$ state, and with 3α Orthogonality Condition Model calculations (Kurokawa 2005, Ohtsubo 2013), which cannot obtain the $0_3^+$ wave function. The results enable a unified understanding of the 0⁺ spectrum in 12C, showing that the $0_3^+$ state arises from monopole excitation of the Hoyle state and the $0_4^+$ state represents a one-dimensional α condensate, supporting the linear-chain concept originally proposed by Morinaga (1956).
