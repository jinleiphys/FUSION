# Subtleties of Lorentz Invariance in Relativistic Constituent Quark Models of the Nucleon and the Spin-Dependent Quark Density

> We study the effects of a barely perceivable violation of Lorentz invariance on results computed using a relativistic constituent quark model wave function. The model nucleon wave function of Gross {\it et al.} is constructed such thatthere is no orbital angular momentum and that the spin-dependent density is spherical. This model wave function is claimed to be manifestly covariant, but we show that this is not so. In particular,the seeming covariance of the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current arises from using the Breit frame. Matrix elements have a different appearance in any other frame.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the relativistic constituent quark model wave function of Gross et al. (2006) and Ramalho et al. (2006), which is claimed to be manifestly covariant and to produce a spherical spin-dependent density, is not actually Lorentz invariant. The apparent covariance of electromagnetic current matrix elements arises only because they are evaluated in the Breit frame; in any other frame, the matrix elements have a different form.

## Method

The authors examine two possible interpretations of the nucleon wave function in an arbitrary reference frame. In the first (conventional) interpretation, the boost operator in the wave function is generalized to arbitrary momentum, and the resulting diquark polarization sum is shown to be non-covariant because it contains explicit three-vectors and Kronecker deltas. In the second (unconventional) interpretation, the diquark polarization vectors are redefined to depend on both initial and final nucleon momenta, which yields a covariant electromagnetic form factor but violates the fundamental requirement that a valence quark model's integrand must factorize into a sum of products of functions depending separately on the initial and final nucleon momenta.

## Key numbers

- The diquark polarization sum in the Breit frame, equation (app), takes the form \(D_{\mu\nu} = (P^+_\mu + P^-_\mu)(P^+_\nu + P^-_\nu) \frac{1}{M^2 + P_+ \cdot P_-} - g_{\mu\nu} - \frac{P^+_\mu P^-_\nu}{M^2}\), which has an illusory covariant appearance but is only valid in that specific frame.
- The electromagnetic current matrix element computed using the unconventional polarization vectors yields a term inversely proportional to \(M^2 + P_+ \cdot P_-\), as shown in equation (resp1): \(\frac{4M (P_+^\alpha + P_-^\alpha)}{M^2 + P_+ \cdot P_-} - \gamma^\alpha\), demonstrating violation of the general factorized form required for valence quark models.

## Context

This work extends the critique by Kvinikhidze and Miller (2006) of the Gross et al. (2006) relativistic constituent quark model, which claimed a spherical nucleon shape. The present paper shows that the later model of Ramalho et al. (2006), which also claims a spherical shape and no orbital angular momentum, is not covariant when examined outside the Breit frame. The analysis enables future work to correctly impose Lorentz invariance in relativistic constituent quark models, showing that a pure S-wave model without orbital angular momentum cannot be constructed in a covariant manner consistent with quantum field theory principles.
