# Magnetic Dipole Transitions with the Full Skyrme Interaction

> The purpose of this paper is to investigate the use of the TDHF method with different Skryme force interactions for the prediction of the M1 strength function associated with $^{52}$Cr, in particular due to the tensor part of the Skyrme force.$^{52}$Cr was chosen due to the recent interest experimentally in this nucleus and the clear data avaliable for comparison. The method used involves applying an instantaneous boost to the wavefunction at the beginning of the dynamical calculations in order to initiate a broadband excitation of the nucleus, following which a Fourier analysis is made. The results of the TDHF simulation found broad agreement with experiment for the range of energies which excite an M1 transition for a variety of forces. The tensor terms appear to be important in qualitatively changing the details of the strength distribution in the main part of the strength function between around 8 and 12 MeV.

## Key claim
The TDHF method with Skyrme forces can broadly predict the energy range of M1 transitions in $^{52}$Cr, but the tensor terms in the Skyrme interaction are important for qualitatively changing the strength distribution details between approximately 8 and 12 MeV, rather than being negligible as previously assumed.

## Method
The authors performed static Hartree-Fock calculations using the Sky3D code on a 3D Cartesian grid from -11.5 fm to +11.5 fm in 1 fm steps to obtain ground states of $^{52}$Cr. They then applied an instantaneous boost to the wavefunction using the M1 operator to initiate broadband excitation, followed by time evolution and Fourier analysis to extract the M1 strength function. Five Skyrme parameterizations were compared: SkM* (no tensor terms), SLy5 (fitted without tensor), SLy5t (tensor added without refitting), and T12 and T22 (fitted including tensor terms).

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon for SLy5: -8.427 MeV; for SLy5t: -8.509 MeV; for SkM*: -8.411 MeV; for T12: -8.423 MeV; for T22: -8.426 MeV; experimental: -8.7762 MeV
- Charge radius for SLy5: 3.708 fm; for SLy5t: 3.700 fm; for SkM*: 3.710 fm; for T12: 3.706 fm; for T22: 3.705 fm; experimental: 3.643(3) fm
- Quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ for SLy5: 0.157; for SLy5t: 0.148; for SkM*: 0.160; for T12: 0.156; for T22: 0.156; experimental: 0.211
- Minimum energy of appreciable M1 strength: 3.00 MeV for SLy5, SLy5t, T12, T22; 2.50 MeV for SkM*; experimental minimum: 5.10 MeV
- Maximum energy of appreciable M1 strength: 12.00 MeV for SLy5; 13.00 MeV for SLy5t; 11.50 MeV for SkM*; 12.50 MeV for T12 and T22; experimental maximum: 11.65 MeV

## Context
This work extends the application of TDHF with Skyrme forces, previously used for heavy ion collisions and giant resonances (Stevenson 2004, 2011), to magnetic dipole transitions in $^{52}$Cr. It builds on experimental data from an older electron scattering experiment (referred to as ES) and a newer photon scattering experiment (referred to as PS), and compares with Random Phase Approximation and Shell Model approaches (reviewed in a 2010 review article). The study enables future investigation of $^{50}$Cr and extension to higher multipole transitions, while also highlighting instabilities in the Skyrme spin-orbit force that require further attention.
