# New explicit expressions for Dirac bilinears

> We derive new explicit expressions for the Dirac bilinears based on a generic representation of the massive Dirac spinors with canonical polarization. These bilinears depend on a direction $n$ in Minkowski space which specifies the form of dynamics. We argue that such a dependence is unavoidable in a relativistic theory with spin, since it originates from Wigner rotation effects. Contrary to most of the expressions found in the literature, our ones are valid for all momenta and canonical polarizations of the spinors. As a by-product, we also obtain a generic explicit expression for the covariant spin vector.

## Key claim

The paper derives new explicit expressions for Dirac bilinears that depend on a direction \(n\) in Minkowski space specifying the form of dynamics; this dependence is argued to be unavoidable in relativistic theories with spin because it originates from Wigner rotation effects, contrary to the abstract's implication that the expressions are simply "valid for all momenta and canonical polarizations."

## Method

The author writes the positive-energy Dirac amplitude as a trace of the outer product \(u\overline u'\) using a generic representation of massive Dirac spinors with canonical polarization, expressed in terms of a reference four-vector \(n\). The outer product is constructed from rest-frame spinors and boosted via the standard form \(u(p,\lambda)=\mathcal N_p(\not p+m)\not n\,\chi_\lambda\), then the 16 Dirac bilinears \(\overline u'\Gamma^A u\) with \(\Gamma^A=\{\mathds 1,\gamma_5,\gamma^\mu,\gamma^\mu\gamma_5,i\sigma^{\mu\nu},i\sigma^{\mu\nu}\gamma_5\}\) are computed algebraically. The results are checked against onshell identities derived from the Dirac equation and against known forward limits.

## Key numbers

* Covariant spin vector in instant form: \(S^\mu_\text{IF}(p,\mathbf s)=\left(\frac{\mathbf p\cdot\mathbf s}{m},\mathbf s+\frac{\mathbf p(\mathbf p\cdot\mathbf s)}{m(E_p+m)}\right)\).
* Covariant spin vector in front form: \(S^\mu_\text{LF}(p,\mathbf s)=\left[\frac{p^+}{m}s_z,\frac{\mathbf p_\perp^2-m^2}{2mp^+}s_z+\frac{\mathbf p_\perp\cdot\mathbf s_\perp}{p^+},\mathbf s_\perp+\frac{\mathbf p_\perp}{m}s_z\right]\).
* Normalization factor: \(\mathcal N^2_p=\left[2(p\cdot n)n^0+n^2(m-p^0)\right]^{-1}\).
* Forward limit of \(\overline u'\gamma^\mu\gamma_5 u\): \(2m\,S^\mu\).
* Forward limit of \(\overline u'i\sigma^{\mu\nu}u\): \(2m\,i\epsilon^{\mu\nu pS}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bouchiat and Michel (1958) for the forward outer product and provides a tensorial alternative to the non-covariant expressions of Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and Brodsky et al. (1997) used in hadronic physics. It enables explicit calculation of spatial distributions of quarks and gluons inside the nucleon via Fourier transforms of Dirac bilinears in momentum space, preserving manifest Lorentz covariance while maintaining the canonical polarization basis needed to separate spin and orbital angular momentum. The paper also generalizes the onshell identities of Gordon (1928) and provides a complete set of relations among the bilinears.
