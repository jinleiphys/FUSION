# Isospin symmetry breaking in the mirror pair $^{73}$Sr-$^{73}$Br

> The recent experimental observation of isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in the ground states of the $T=3/2$ mirror pair $^{73}$Sr - $^{73}$Br is theoretically studied using large-scale shell model calculations. The large valence space and the successful PFSDG-U effective interaction used for the nuclear part of the problem capture possible structural changes and provide a robust basis to treat the ISB effects of both electromagnetic and non-electromagnetic origin. The calculated shifts and mirror-energy-differences are consistent with the inversion of the $I^π$= 1/2$^{-}, 5/2^{-}$ states between $^{73}$Sr - $^{73}$Br, and suggest that the role played by the Coulomb interaction is dominant. An isospin breaking contribution of nuclear origin is estimated to be $\approx 25$ keV.

## Key claim

The inversion of the ground-state spins (5/2⁻ in ⁷³Sr vs. 1/2⁻ in ⁷³Br) is primarily driven by Coulomb effects, with the one-body part of the Coulomb interaction (the shift in proton single-particle energies of the p-orbits relative to the f-orbits) being the dominant mechanism; a non-electromagnetic isospin-breaking contribution of nuclear origin is estimated at approximately 25 keV, but this value is not uniquely required if the empirical proton-neutron single-particle energy differences already absorb such effects.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model configuration-interaction calculations were performed in a valence space comprising the 0f₇/₂, 1p₃/₂, 0f₅/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂, and 1d₅/₂ orbits above a ⁵⁶Ni core, using the PFSDG-U effective interaction for the isospin-conserving nuclear part. Two methods were applied to treat isospin symmetry breaking: Method 1 added the full Coulomb interaction (one-body and two-body) to the nuclear Hamiltonian and diagonalized it for each mirror nucleus; Method 2 evaluated separate contributions to the Mirror Energy Differences (MEDs) from the multipole Coulomb term, electromagnetic spin-orbit and orbit-orbit single-particle energy corrections, a radial term parametrized by changes in low-ℓ orbit occupation, and an empirical isovector interaction V_B.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-conserving nuclear-only calculation places the 1/2⁻ state at 21 keV above the 5/2⁻ ground state.
- In Method 1, the one-body Coulomb correction (C1) for the 1/2⁻ state is +25 keV in ⁷³Sr and -27 keV in ⁷³Br, producing a total MED of 116 keV for the 1/2⁻ state.
- In Method 2, the total calculated MED for the 1/2⁻ state is 43 keV, composed of C_M = 11 keV, C_(ℓs+ℓℓ) = 23 keV, C_r = -16 keV, and V_B = 25 keV.
- The 5/2⁻ state in ⁷³Br is experimentally known at 27 keV excitation; the 1/2⁻ state in ⁷³Sr has not been located experimentally, so the MED lower limit is 27 keV.
- The radial term uses α_r = 200 keV for the p₁/₂ orbit, α_r = 100 keV for the p₃/₂ orbit, and α_r = 300 keV for the g₉/₂ and d₅/₂ orbits.

## Context

This work provides a theoretical explanation for the experimental observation by Hoff et al. (2020) of mirror-symmetry violation in the ground states of the ⁷³Sr-⁷³Br pair, which the original experimental paper could not reproduce with its accompanying calculations. The results extend the framework of Mirror Energy Difference studies in the pf-shell (Zuker et al., 2002; Bentley et al., 2007) to the region beyond N=50, and demonstrate that the inversion can be understood without invoking threshold effects or charge-symmetry-breaking forces beyond the standard Coulomb and electromagnetic corrections. The calculations enable future experimental searches for the 1/2⁻ state in ⁷³Sr to test the predicted MED value and refine the radial and isovector parameters.
