# Structure of single $Λ$-hypernuclei with Gogny-type $Λ$-nucleon forces

> We study the structure of single $Λ$-hypernuclei using the Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov method. Finite range Gogny-type forces are used to describe the nucleon-nucleon and $Λ$-nucleon interactions. Three different $Λ$-nucleon Gogny forces are built. The unknown parameters of these forces are obtained by fitting the experimental binding energies of the $1s$ $Λ$ single-particle state in various hypernuclei using the ``Simulated Annealing Method''. These forces are then used to calculate the binding energies of the other ($1p, 1d, 1f, 1g$) $Λ$ single-particle states in the different hypernuclei. The predicted values are found to be in good agreement with the experimental data for the three forces constructed. In addition, we calculate also the root-mean-square radii of ground state $Λ$ orbital, as well as several global properties of the hypernuclei considered such as their ground-state Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov energy, their pairing energy and their quadrupole moment.

## Key claim

The authors construct three finite-range Gogny-type Λ-nucleon forces by fitting only the 1s Λ single-particle state binding energies in eight hypernuclei, and then use these forces to predict the binding energies of higher-lying Λ states (1p, 1d, 1f, 1g) with "reasonable good agreement" to experiment, though a systematic slight underbinding of these higher states is observed across all hypernuclei studied.

## Method

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method is applied to single Λ-hypernuclei, treating the hypernucleus as a product of an HFB wave function for the nuclear core and a single-particle state for the Λ hyperon. The D1S parametrization of the Gogny force is used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, while a simplified central-only Gogny-type force (without density-dependent or spin-orbit terms) is used for the Λ-nucleon interaction. The six unknown parameters of the ΛN force are determined by fitting experimental 1s Λ binding energies in eight hypernuclei (¹⁶O, ²⁸Si, ³²S, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵¹V, ⁸⁹Y, ¹³⁹La, ²⁰⁸Pb) using a χ² minimization with the Simulated Annealing Method, producing three different parameter sets (ΛN₁, ΛN₂, ΛN₃) from different initial guesses.

## Key numbers

- Three ΛN force parameter sets obtained: ΛN₁ (χ² = 35.115), ΛN₂ (χ² = 42.212), ΛN₃ (χ² = 78.938)
- 1s Λ binding energy in ¹⁶_ΛO: 11.90 MeV (ΛN₁), 11.41 MeV (ΛN₂), 13.29 MeV (ΛN₃) vs. experimental 13.0(2) MeV
- 1s Λ binding energy in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: 26.20 MeV (ΛN₁), 26.22 MeV (ΛN₂), 25.12 MeV (ΛN₃) vs. experimental 26.9(8) MeV
- 1g Λ binding energy in ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: 5.63 MeV (ΛN₁), 5.53 MeV (ΛN₂), 5.25 MeV (ΛN₃) vs. experimental 7.2(6) MeV
- 1s Λ binding energy in ³²_ΛS: 20.11 MeV (ΛN₁), 19.62 MeV (ΛN₂), 21.05 MeV (ΛN₃) vs. experimental 17.5(5) MeV (overbinding noted)
- Ground-state HFB energy of ²⁰⁸_ΛPb: -1684.69 MeV (ΛN₁), -1684.76 MeV (ΛN₂), -1682.42 MeV (ΛN₃) vs. ²⁰⁷Pb core: -1629.76 MeV
- Pairing energy modification due to Λ presence: 0-0.4 MeV for most hypernuclei, with exceptions of ³²_ΛS (~1.65 MeV difference from ³¹S) and ⁵¹_ΛV (~2.5 MeV difference from ⁵⁰V)

## Context

This work extends previous hypernuclear studies using Skyrme-type ΛN interactions (Guleria, 2012; various Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations) and relativistic mean field approaches to the Gogny-type finite-range interaction framework, which is well-established for ordinary nuclei through the D1S parametrization (Berger, 1991). The results enable future beyond-mean-field calculations incorporating quadrupole fluctuations, which the authors identify as necessary to resolve the overbinding of ³²_ΛS and the shape ambiguity in ⁵¹_ΛV, and could be extended to include Λ-nucleon pairing effects and three-body ΛNN forces.
