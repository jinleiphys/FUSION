# Resonance dynamics in the coherent $η$ meson production in the $(p,p^\prime)$ reaction on the spin-isospin saturated nucleus

> For the forward going proton and $η$ meson, the coherent $η$ meson production in the $(p,p^\prime)$ reaction on the spin-isospin saturated nucleus occurs only due to the $η$ meson exchange interaction between the beam proton and nucleus. In this process, the nucleon in the nucleus can be excited to resonances $N^*$ and the $η$ meson in the final state can arise due to $N^* \to Nη$. We investigate the dynamics of resonances, including nucleon Born terms, and their interferences in the coherently added cross section of this reaction. We discuss the importance of $N(1520)$ resonance and show the sensitivity of the cross section to the hadron nucleus interaction.

# Resonance dynamics in the coherent η meson production in the (p,p′) reaction on the spin-isospin saturated nucleus

## Key claim

The resonance N(1520), despite having a much smaller decay branching ratio into the Nη channel than N(1535) at its pole mass, can produce a comparable or even dominant contribution to the coherent η meson production cross section in the (p,p′) reaction on a spin-isospin saturated nucleus, particularly at higher beam energies (2.5 GeV) and when using the latest measured decay width of 0.265 MeV. This contradicts the previous assumption that N(1535) dominates such reactions.

## Method

The author calculates the differential cross section for coherent η meson production in the (p,p′) reaction on ⁴He using a distorted wave Glauber model, where the elementary process proceeds via η meson exchange between the beam proton and a nucleon in the nucleus, exciting a resonance N* that subsequently decays to Nη. The T-matrix includes contributions from nucleon Born terms and five resonances (N(1520), N(1535), N(1650), N(1710), N(1720)) with coupling constants extracted from measured decay widths, and the η meson-nucleus optical potential is evaluated from the η meson self-energy in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The decay width Γ_{N(1520)→Nη} at the pole mass (1520 MeV) has two values: an earlier measurement of 0.12 MeV and a more recent value of 0.265 MeV; the corresponding coupling constants are f_{ηNN(1520)} = 6.72 and 9.98, respectively.
- The increase in the coupling constant by a factor of about 1.5 (from 6.72 to 9.98) increases the coherent η meson production cross section due to N(1520) by a factor of about 5.
- At T_p = 1.2 GeV with the earlier decay width (0.12 MeV), the peak cross section due to N(1520) is 24.7% of that due to N(1535); with the latest decay width (0.265 MeV), the N(1520) peak cross section becomes about 18% larger than that of N(1535).
- At T_p = 1.2 GeV, including distortions and the N*-nucleus optical potential reduces the peak cross section by a factor of 9.6 and shifts the peak position by 32 MeV toward lower η meson energy.
- At T_p = 2.5 GeV, even with the earlier decay width (0.12 MeV), the peak cross section due to N(1520) supersedes that due to N(1535), and three distinct peaks appear from N(1520), N(1535), and N(1710).

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Alvaredo and Oset (2003) on coherent η meson production in the (p,p′) reaction, which only considered N(1535) as the dominant resonance. The author shows that N(1520) cannot be neglected, contrary to previous assumptions, because its decay probability into Nη rises sharply with resonance mass (ηN invariant mass) and its coupling constant has been revised upward. The results enable experimental identification of multiple resonance contributions in the η meson energy spectrum, particularly at higher beam energies, and suggest that the coherent η production reaction can serve as a probe for N(1520) dynamics in the nucleus.
