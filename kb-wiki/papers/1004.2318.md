# Solar fusion cross sections II: the pp chain and CNO cycles

> We summarize and critically evaluate the available data on nuclear fusion cross sections important to energy generation in the Sun and other hydrogen-burning stars and to solar neutrino production. Recommended values and uncertainties are provided for key cross sections, and a recommended spectrum is given for 8B solar neutrinos. We also discuss opportunities for further increasing the precision of key rates, including new facilities, new experimental techniques, and improvements in theory. This review, which summarizes the conclusions of a workshop held at the Institute for Nuclear Theory, Seattle, in January 2009, is intended as a 10-year update and supplement to Reviews of Modern Physics 70 (1998) 1265.

# Solar fusion cross sections II: the pp chain and CNO cycles

## Key claim

This review provides updated recommended values and uncertainties for key nuclear fusion cross sections (S-factors) relevant to hydrogen burning in the Sun and other stars, based on a critical evaluation of data available through 2009, but the authors acknowledge that in cases where discrepant data sets exist or extrapolations depend on models, full consensus could not always be reached and consumers should be aware that resolution may require future measurements.

## Method

The authors organized an international workshop at the Institute for Nuclear Theory in January 2009, modeled after the 1997 workshop that produced Solar Fusion I, with working groups assigned to evaluate specific reactions in the pp chain and CNO cycles. Working group leaders identified key papers for review prior to the workshop, and participants critically analyzed both new and older data throughout 2009 to reach consensus on best-value cross sections, S-factors, and uncertainties, including new topics such as the shape of the 8B neutrino spectrum not addressed in the 1998 review.

## Key numbers

- p(p,e+νe)d S(0) = (4.01 ± 0.04) × 10−22 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 0.7%
- d(p,γ)3He S(0) = (2.14 +0.17 −0.16) × 10−4 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 7.1%
- 3He(3He,2p)4He S(0) = (5.21 ± 0.27) × 103 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 4.3%
- 3He(4He,γ)7Be S(0) = 0.56 ± 0.03 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 5.1%
- 3He(p,e+νe)4He S(0) = (8.6 ± 2.6) × 10−20 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 30%
- 7Be(p,γ)8B S(0) = (2.08 ± 0.16) × 10−2 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 7.5%
- 14N(p,γ)15O S(0) = 1.66 ± 0.12 keV-b, with Gamow peak uncertainty ± 7.2%
- SNO NCD-phase 8B neutrino flux measurement: φ(8B) = (5.54 +0.33 −0.31 +0.36 −0.34) × 106 /cm2 /s, implying core temperature sensitivity of ∼ 0.5%
- Solar core temperature: Tc ∼ 15.5 × 106 K, central density ρc ∼ 153 g/cm3

## Context

This review serves as a 10-year update and supplement to Adelberger et al. (1998) (Solar Fusion I), which had become one of the most heavily cited papers in nuclear astrophysics. The new evaluation reflects progress including the LUNA II program at Gran Sasso providing low-energy measurements of 3He(α,γ)7Be and 14N(p,γ)15O, high-precision measurements of 7Be(p,γ)8B, and new theoretical techniques for the p+p and hep neutrino reactions. The updated cross sections enable more precise analyses of solar neutrino experiments designed to constrain neutrino oscillations (including the mixing angle θ12) and allow solar neutrinos to be used as quantitative probes of the solar core temperature and metallicity, particularly relevant after recent 3D photospheric analyses by Asplund reduced metallicity estimates from Z ∼ 0.0169 to ∼ 0.0122, destroying the once excellent agreement between helioseismology and the standard solar model.
