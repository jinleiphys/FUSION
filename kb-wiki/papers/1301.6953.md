# Chiral Perturbation Theory and Mesons

> The talk contains a short introduction to mesonic Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT). In addition four disparate areas where some progress has been made in recent years are discussed. These are the last fit of the order $p^4$ low-energy-constants $L_i^r$ to data, hard pion ChPT, the recent two-loop work on EFT for QCD like theories and the high order leading logarithm calculations in the massive $O(N)$ nonlinear sigma model.

## Key claim

This talk presents an overview of recent progress in mesonic Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT), including a new fit of the order \(p^4\) low-energy-constants \(L_i^r\) that updates the 2001 fit, but the author notes that three-flavour ChPT "sort of" works given that the relations involve very large correlations and experimental or dispersive input errors might be underestimated.

## Method

The author reviews four areas of recent progress: (1) a new NNLO fit of the \(L_i^r\) constants using updated experimental data including NA48/2 form factors, \(F_K/F_\pi\), \(\pi\pi\) and \(\pi K\) scattering, and the scalar radius; (2) hard pion ChPT, which uses current algebra to obtain chiral logarithms for processes with hard pions by expanding in soft momenta over hard masses; (3) two-loop ChPT calculations for QCD-like theories with fermions in complex, real, and pseudo-real representations of the gauge group; (4) high-order leading logarithm calculations in the massive \(O(N)\) nonlinear sigma model using the fact that only one-loop calculations are needed to obtain leading logarithms.

## Key numbers

* Two-flavour constants: \(\bar l_1 = -0.4 \pm 0.6\), \(\bar l_2 = 4.3 \pm 0.1\), \(\bar l_3 = 2.9 \pm 2.4\), \(\bar l_4 = 4.4 \pm 0.2\), \(\bar l_5 = 12.24 \pm 0.21\), \(\bar l_6 = 16.0 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.7\) (from ChPT at order \(p^6\) and Roy equation analysis).
* Three-flavour fit results (fit "All"): \(10^3 L_1^r = 0.88 \pm 0.09\), \(10^3 L_2^r = 0.61 \pm 0.20\), \(10^3 L_3^r = -3.04 \pm 0.43\), \(10^3 L_4^r = 0.75 \pm 0.75\), \(10^3 L_5^r = 0.58 \pm 0.13\), \(10^3 L_6^r = 0.29 \pm 0.85\), \(10^3 L_7^r = -0.11 \pm 0.15\), \(10^3 L_8^r = 0.18 \pm 0.18\) (from the new fit of Bijnens and Jemos, 2012).
* The number of terms in the ChPT Lagrangian: at LO 2 terms for 2 or 3 flavours, at NLO 7+3 for 2 flavours and 10+2 for 3 flavours, at NNLO 52+4 for 2 flavours and 90+4 for 3 flavours.
* The standard renormalization scale for three-flavour ChPT is \(\mu = m_\rho = 0.77\) GeV.

## Context

This talk extends the previous review of mesonic ChPT at two-loop order by Bijnens (2007) and updates the major fit of the \(L_i^r\) constants originally done in 2001 (Amoros, Bijnens, Talavera). The new fit incorporates NA48/2 data on form factors (Batley et al., 2009) and the updated value of \(F_K/F_\pi\). The work on hard pion ChPT generalizes the approach of Flynn and Sachrajda (2008) for \(K_{\ell3}\) to processes like \(B,D\to\pi,K,\eta\) form factors and charmonium decays, though doubts have been raised by Procura and others (2012) about the validity at three-loop order. The two-loop calculations for QCD-like theories enable lattice QCD simulations to extrapolate to the massless case for theories with fermions in different representations, as done by Buchoff and others. The leading logarithm calculations in the \(O(N)\) model provide high-order results that can be compared to the full series, showing that the large-\(N\) limit is not a good approximation for any of the quantities calculated.
