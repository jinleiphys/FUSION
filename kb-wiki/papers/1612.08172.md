# The deuteron wave function and neutron form factors from the elastic electron deuteron scattering

> A study of the deuteron structure in the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics is presented. Point-form (PF) relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) is applied to the elastic eD scattering. The deuteron wave function and neutron form factors are fitted to the electromagnetic deuteron form factors. We also compare results obtained by different realistic deuteron wave functions stemming from Nijmegen-I, Nijmegen-II, JISP16, CD-Bonn, Paris, Argonne, Idaho and Moscow (with forbidden states) potentials. It is shown that the electromagnetic deuteron form factors may be described without exchange currents.

## Key claim
The paper claims that elastic electron-deuteron scattering data can be described without including meson exchange currents, using only the NN channel in point-form relativistic quantum mechanics, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of deuteron wave function and nucleon form factor parameterizations, and the authors note that modest changes in these inputs can simulate the effects of exchange currents.

## Method
The authors apply point-form relativistic quantum mechanics (PF RQM) to elastic eD scattering, using the spectator approximation for the electromagnetic current operator without expanding it in powers of the momentum transfer parameter h. They fit the deuteron wave function (parameterized as a sum of oscillator functions and exponential terms) and nucleon electromagnetic form factors to the deuteron electromagnetic form factors, comparing results from eight different realistic NN potentials (Nijmegen-I, Nijmegen-II, JISP16, CD-Bonn, Paris, Argonne V18, Idaho, and Moscow with forbidden states).

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: relativistic correction factor (1 - ε/4m) where ε = 2.2233 MeV (effective) vs experimental 2.2246 MeV
- Static deuteron magnetic form factor G_M(0) = 1.7148 (experimental); fitted "eD" model gives 1.715/1.700 (relativistic/nonrelativistic)
- Static deuteron quadrupole form factor G_Q(0) = 25.83 (experimental); fitted "eD" model gives 25.83/25.54 (relativistic/nonrelativistic)
- Neutron electric form factor G_En extracted at Q^2 = 0.255 GeV^2 (Q ≈ 2.6 Fm^{-1}) from quasi-free kinematics: G_En = 0.066 ± 0.036 ± 0.009
- The fitted neutron electric form factor may be 2-3 times greater than results extracted with some "Arenhövel's model"
- Good agreement between theory and experiment for Q < 3 Fm^{-1}; discrepancies at larger Q are comparable to differences between potential models

## Context
This work extends the author's previous investigations of NN scattering up to 3 GeV laboratory energy and electromagnetic reactions with two nucleons (bremsstrahlung, photodisintegration, electrodisintegration). It contrasts with approaches that include meson exchange currents (e.g., Arenhövel and Schiavilla (2002)) and with the analysis of Platchkov et al. which showed that extracted neutron electric form factor values are extremely model dependent. The paper enables a re-evaluation of whether exchange currents are necessary, but notes that definitive conclusions require solid neutron form factor data free from model dependence.
