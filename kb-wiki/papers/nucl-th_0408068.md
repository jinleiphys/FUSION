# Extended Goldstone-Boson-Exchange Constituent Quark Model

> We present an extension of the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model including additional interactions beyond the ones used hitherto. For the hyperfine interaction between the constituent quarks we assume pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges and consider all relevant force components produced by these types of exchanges. The resulting model, which corresponds to a relativistic Poincare-invariant Hamiltonian (or equivalently mass operator), provides a unified framework for a covariant description of all light and strange baryons. The ground states and resonances up to an excitation energy of about 2 GeV are reproduced in fair agreement with phenomenology, with the exception of the first excitations above the Lambda and Xi ground states.

## Key claim

The extended Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model, which includes pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges with all relevant force components, reproduces light and strange baryon ground states and resonances up to about 2 GeV in fair agreement with phenomenology, except for the first excitations above the Lambda and Xi ground states.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic Poincare-invariant Hamiltonian (mass operator) for three constituent quarks with a linear confinement potential and a hyperfine interaction composed of pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson-exchange potentials including central, spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit force components. They solved the eigenvalue problem using the stochastic variational method to obtain baryon spectra, and produced two parameterizations: one without and one with spin-orbit forces.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = 340 MeV, m_d = 340 MeV, m_s = 507 MeV
- Pseudoscalar octet coupling constant: g^2_ps,8 / 4pi = 0.67
- Vector octet coupling constant: (g^V_v,8)^2 / 4pi = 0.55
- Scalar coupling constant: g^2_s / 4pi = 0.67
- Linear confinement strength: C = 1.935 fm^-2
- Cut-off parameters: Lambda_pi = 834 MeV, Lambda_rho = 1145 MeV, Lambda_sigma = 1513 MeV
- The nucleon ground state is fixed to 939 MeV
- The Lambda(1405) resonance is missed by far in the model; the Lambda(1520) is predicted too high by about 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pseudoscalar Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model of Glozman et al. (1998), which used only the spin-spin part of pseudoscalar meson exchange, by adding vector and scalar meson exchanges and all force components including tensor and spin-orbit forces. The extended model addresses deficiencies of the earlier version, particularly the failure to reproduce near degeneracies of positive- and negative-parity N resonances around 1700 MeV. The approach is analogous to meson-exchange models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and provides a unified framework for covariant description of all light and strange baryons, enabling future applications to hadronic reactions beyond spectroscopy.
