# The electromagnetic fine-structure constant in primordial nucleosynthesis revisited

> We study the dependence of the primordial nuclear abundances as a function of the electromagnetic fine-structure constant $α$, keeping all other fundamental constants fixed. We update the leading nuclear reaction rates, in particular the electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference pertinent to $β$-decays, and go beyond certain approximations made in the literature. In particular, we include the temperature-dependence of the leading nuclear reactions rates and assess the systematic uncertainties by using four different publicly available codes for Big Bang nucleosynthesis. Disregarding the unsolved so-called lithium-problem, we find that the current values for the observationally based $^{2}$H and $^{4}$He abundances restrict the fractional change in the fine-structure constant to less than $2\%$, which is a tighter bound than found in earlier works on the subject.

## Key claim

The authors find that current observational abundances of deuterium (²H) and helium-4 (⁴He) restrict the fractional change in the fine-structure constant to less than 2%, which is a tighter bound than found in earlier works, but this result explicitly disregards the unsolved lithium problem.

## Method

The authors calculated the dependence of primordial nuclear abundances on variations of the electromagnetic fine-structure constant α by updating the leading nuclear reaction rates, including the electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference from dispersion relations (Cottingham sum rule) and nuclear Coulomb energies from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory. They incorporated the temperature dependence of the leading nuclear reaction rates and assessed systematic uncertainties by using four different publicly available Big Bang nucleosynthesis codes: those of Kawano (1992), Arbey (2011, 2018), Pisanti (2007) and Consiglio (2017), Gariazzo (2021), and Pitrou (2018).

## Key numbers

- The current experimental value of the fine-structure constant is α₀ = 7.2973525693(11)×10⁻³ = 1/137.035999084(2) (from Workman, 2022).
- The electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is Δm_QED = 0.58 ± 0.16 MeV, with the QCD contribution Δm_QCD = -1.87 ∓ 0.16 MeV (from Gasser, 2020).
- The fractional change in the fine-structure constant is restricted to less than 2% based on observational ²H and ⁴He abundances.
- The nominal electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference from earlier work (Gasser, 1974) was 0.7 ± 0.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on variations of the fine-structure constant from Big Bang nucleosynthesis, specifically those by Bergstrom (1999), Nollett (2002), Coc (2006), and Dent (2007). It goes beyond these earlier works by including temperature dependence of reaction rates, using updated determinations of nuclear Coulomb energies from Lattice EFT, and employing four different BBN codes to assess systematic uncertainties. The results provide tighter bounds on α variation than previously found, and this work is part of a larger program mapping out habitable universes where sufficient amounts of life-essential nuclei are produced in the Big Bang and in stars.
