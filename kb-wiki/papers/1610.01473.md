# ${\bar D}D$ meson pair production in antiproton-nucleus collisions

> We study the $\bar D D$ (${\bar D}^0 D^0$ and $D^-D^+$) charm meson pair production in antiproton (${\bar p}$) induced reactions on nuclei at beam energies ranging from threshold to several GeV. Our model is based on an effective Lagrangian approach that has only the baryon-meson degrees of freedom and involves the physical hadron masses. The reaction proceeds via the $t$-channel exchanges of $Λ_c^+$, $Σ_c^+$, and $Σ_c^{++}$ baryons in the initial collision of the antiproton with one of the protons of the target nucleus. The medium effects on the exchanged baryons are included by incorporating in the corresponding propagators, the effective charm baryon masses calculated within a quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The wave functions of the bound proton have been determined within the QMC model as well as in a phenomenological model where they are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. The initial- and final-state distortion effects have been approximated by using an eikonal approximation-based procedure. Detailed numerical results are presented for total and double differential cross sections for the ${\bar D}^0 D^0$ and $D^-D^+$ production reactions on $^{16}$O and $^{90}$Zr targets. It is noticed that at ${\bar p}$ beam momenta of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment, medium effects lead to noticeable enhancements in the charm meson production cross sections.

# $\bar{D}D$ meson pair production in antiproton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
Medium effects, incorporated via effective charm baryon masses from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, lead to noticeable enhancements in the $\bar{D}^0 D^0$ and $D^- D^+$ production cross sections in antiproton-nucleus collisions at beam momenta of interest to the $\bar{P}ANDA$ experiment, although the calculations rely on an eikonal approximation for distortion effects and use a single average value for the imaginary part of the antiproton-nucleus optical potential.

## Method
The reaction mechanism is described by $t$-channel exchanges of $\Lambda_c^+$, $\Sigma_c^+$, and $\Sigma_c^{++}$ baryons in the initial antiproton collision with a bound proton of the target nucleus, using an effective Lagrangian approach with baryon-meson degrees of freedom and physical hadron masses. Medium effects on the exchanged baryons are included by replacing their free masses with effective masses calculated within the QMC-I model, while the bound proton wave functions are obtained both from the QMC model and from solving the Dirac equation with phenomenological scalar and vector potentials. Initial- and final-state distortion effects are approximated via an eikonal procedure using a single average imaginary optical potential of 125 MeV for the antiproton and a total cross section of 10 mb for $\bar{D}N$ and $DN$ interactions.

## Key numbers
- Threshold momentum for $\bar{D}D$ production in $\bar{p}$-induced reactions on a free proton is 6.40 GeV/c.
- Coupling constants used: $g_{N \Lambda_c^+ D} = 13.50$, $g_{N \Sigma_c^+ D} = 2.69$, $g_{N \Sigma_c^{++} D} = 2.69$.
- Free masses: $\Lambda_c^+$ = 2285 MeV, $\Sigma_c^+$ = 2452 MeV; at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$, the effective masses become $\Lambda_c^+$ = 2162.5 MeV and $\Sigma_c^+$ = 2330.2 MeV.
- The in-medium mass shift of charmed baryons produces an attractive scalar potential of about 120 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- The imaginary part of the $\bar{p}$-nucleus optical potential is taken as 125 MeV, corresponding to a $\bar{p}p$ total cross section of about 75 mb at 15 GeV/c.
- Binding energies of the outermost proton orbits: 12.13 MeV for the $0p_{1/2}$ orbit in $^{16}$O, and 8.35 MeV for the $1p_{1/2}$ orbit in $^{90}$Zr.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous effective Lagrangian model for $\bar{p} + p \to \bar{D} + D$ reactions (Shyam 2016) to nuclear targets, incorporating medium modifications of charmed baryon masses via the QMC model. It contrasts with earlier studies of charm production in $\bar{p}$-nucleus reactions, such as the cascade model of Sibirtsev (1999) and the charmonium production calculations of Gerasyuta (2005) and Larionov (2013), by focusing specifically on open charm meson pair production. The results are intended to provide cross section estimates for the $\bar{P}ANDA$ experiment at FAIR, enabling studies of charm hadron properties in nuclear matter and searches for charm CP violation.
