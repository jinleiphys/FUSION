# Parametrization of light nuclei quasiparticle energy shifts and composition of warm and dense nuclear matter

> Correlations and the formation of bound states (nuclei) are essential for the properties of nuclear matter in equilibrium as well as in nonequilibrium. In a quantum statistical approach, quasiparticle energies are obtained for the light elements that reflect the influence of the medium. We present analytical fits for the quasiparticle energy shifts of light nuclei that can be used in various applications. This is a prerequisite for the investigation of warm and dense matter that reproduces the nuclear statistical equilibrium and virial expansions in the low-density limit as well as relativistic mean field and Brueckner Hartree-Fock approaches near saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytical fits for the quasiparticle energy shifts of light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helion, and alpha particle) in warm and dense nuclear matter, with the hedge that these fits are restricted to subsaturation densities (baryon densities n ≤ 0.16 fm⁻³) and moderate temperatures (T ≤ 20 MeV), and that the treatment neglects Coulomb interactions, weak interaction effects, and heavier clusters.

## Method

The author solves an in-medium Schrödinger equation derived from a quantum statistical approach using a cluster mean-field approximation, where the medium effects enter through single-nucleon self-energy shifts and Pauli blocking terms. A variational Jastrow ansatz is used for the few-nucleon wave functions, with a separable Gaussian interaction potential fitted to reproduce free binding energies and root-mean-square radii. The resulting Pauli blocking shifts are parametrized as functions of center-of-mass momentum P, temperature T, total baryon density n, and proton fraction Yp, with separate parameter tables for each light cluster.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is given as n_s ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, and medium effects become relevant above ~10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Free binding energies: deuteron -2.225 MeV, triton -8.482 MeV, helion -7.718 MeV, alpha -28.30 MeV
- Free kinetic energies: deuteron 10.338 MeV, triton 23.735 MeV, helion 23.021 MeV, alpha 51.575 MeV
- Root-mean-square point radii: deuteron 1.96 fm, triton 1.68 fm, helion 1.71 fm, alpha 1.45 fm
- The intended accuracy of the parametrization is better than 1% in the parameter region considered
- An empirical expression for the effective mass is given as m*/m = 1 - 0.17 n/n_sat

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach of Röpke (previous work) and the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula of Röpke, Mornas, and Schmidt (1995) to provide compact analytical fits for light cluster quasiparticle energy shifts, improving upon earlier parametrizations. The parametrization is designed to be used in nuclear statistical equilibrium calculations and reproduces the low-density virial expansion limit as well as relativistic mean-field and Brueckner Hartree-Fock approaches near saturation density, enabling applications to supernova explosions and heavy ion collisions as done by Sumiyoshi and Röpke (2008) and Natowitz (2010).
