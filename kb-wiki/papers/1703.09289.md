# Core-excitation effects in ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}(d,p){}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ transfer reactions: Suppression or enhancement?

> ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}(d,p){}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ transfer reactions are described using momentum-space Faddeev-type equations for transition operators and including the vibrational excitation of the ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}$ core. The available experimental cross section data at 10.5 MeV/nucleon beam energy for the ${}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ ground state $\frac52^+$ and excited state $\frac12^+$ are quite well reproduced by our calculations including the core excitation. Its effect can be roughly simulated reducing the single-particle cross section by the corresponding spectroscopic factor. Consequently, the extraction of the spectroscopic factors taking the ratio of experimental data and single-particle cross section at this energy is a reasonable procedure. However, at higher energies core-excitation effects are much more complicated and have no simple relation to spectroscopic factors. We found that core-excitation effects are qualitatively very different for reactions with the orbital angular momentum transfer $\ell=0$ and $\ell=2$, suppressing the cross sections for the former and enhancing for the latter, and changes the shape of the angular distribution in both cases. Furthermore, the core-excitation effect is a result of a complicated interplay between its contributions of the two- and three-body nature.

## Key claim

The core-excitation effect in ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}(d,p){}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ transfer reactions can be approximated by a simple reduction of the single-particle cross section by the spectroscopic factor at 10.5 MeV/nucleon beam energy, but at higher energies this factorization fails and the effect becomes qualitatively different for $\ell=0$ and $\ell=2$ transfers, suppressing the former and enhancing the latter.

## Method

The authors solved momentum-space Faddeev-type (AGS) equations for transition operators in an extended Hilbert space that includes both the ground state ($0^+$) and first excited state ($2^+$) of the ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}$ core, using a vibrational model for the quadrupole coupling. They employed Koning-Delaroche (KD) and Chapel Hill 89 (CH) global optical potentials extended for quadrupole vibrations, with the neutron-proton interaction described by the CD Bonn potential, and adjusted the nucleon-core bound-state potentials to reproduce the binding energies and shell-model spectroscopic factors of the ${}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ states.

## Key numbers

- At 10.5 MeV/nucleon beam energy ($E_d = 21$ MeV), the core-excitation effect for the ${}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ ground state $\frac52^+$ (SF = 0.34) scales well with the spectroscopic factor, with the factorization conjecture valid at forward angles.
- For the ${}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ excited state $\frac12^+$ (SF = 0.82) at $E_d = 21$ MeV, the core excitation reduces the differential cross section at forward angles by a factor of 0.77, about 6% stronger than predicted by the factorization conjecture.
- At $E_d = 63$ MeV (31.5 MeV/nucleon) for the $\frac12^+$ state, the ratio $R_x = 0.59$ at $\Theta_\mathrm{c.m.} = 0^\circ$, indicating a 70% too small SF if extracted naively.
- At $E_d = 63$ MeV for the $\frac52^+$ state, $R_x = 1.83$ at $\Theta_\mathrm{c.m.} = 0^\circ$, indicating an 80% too large SF if extracted naively.
- The core excitation energy of ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}$ is $\Delta m_A = 1.684$ MeV.
- The binding energies of the ${}^{21}\mathrm{O}$ states are 3.806 MeV ($\frac52^+$) and 2.586 MeV ($\frac12^+$).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous Faddeev-type treatment of core-excitation effects previously applied to ${}^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p){}^{11}\mathrm{Be}$ reactions using the rotational model (Deltuva 2013, 2015, 2016) to the vibrational excitation of ${}^{20}\mathrm{O}$ and to $\ell=2$ transfer, which was not studied before. The calculations reproduce experimental data at 10.5 MeV/nucleon (Fernandez-Garcia 2015) and demonstrate that at higher energies the simple spectroscopic-factor scaling used in DWBA and ADWA approaches (Johnson 1970) fails, with opposite effects for $\ell=0$ and $\ell=2$ transfers. This enables more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors from transfer reactions on exotic nuclei and clarifies the systematic behavior of core-excitation effects across different orbital angular momentum transfers.
