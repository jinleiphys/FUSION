# Magnetic Field Induced Effects in Quark Matter

> Quark--gluon matter produced in relativistic heavy--ion collisions(RHIC and LHC) is subject to a super--strong magnetic field(MF) $\sim 10^{18} - 10^{20} G$. Quark matter(QM) response to MF allows to get a new insight on its properties. We give a cursory glance on MF induced effects.

# Magnetic Field Induced Effects in Quark Matter

## Key claim
Quark-gluon matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is subject to a super-strong magnetic field of order 10^18–10^20 G, but most studies have oversimplified the problem by assuming the field is constant in time and space and by considering infinite systems in thermodynamic equilibrium; the authors provide a cursory overview of effects that may arise when these assumptions are relaxed.

## Method
The paper surveys several magnetic-field-induced phenomena in quark matter, including the decay rate of the magnetic field, phase-space modifications due to Landau level quantization, the QCD phase diagram under magnetic fields, the chiral magnetic effect, electrical conductivity in a magnetic field, and the giant Nernst effect. For each topic, the authors summarize existing theoretical and lattice results, note open questions, and identify where oversimplifications (such as assuming constant fields or equilibrium) may have obscured important physics.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions: 10^18–10^20 G (RHIC and LHC), compared to the Schwinger critical field for electrons of 4.4 × 10^13 G.
- Magnetic field lifetime without conductivity effects: τ ∼ 0.2 fm; with conductivity, decay time τ′ ≃ σ L², estimated as ∼2 fm for σ/T ≃ 0.5, T ≃ 200 MeV, L ≃ 2 fm.
- For a quark with chemical potential μ_F = 3 m_π, q_f B = 5 m_π², and m_f = 0, the maximum Landau level index is n_max = 1.
- Lattice calculations at μ = 0 and T near the phase transition give σ/T ≃ 0.3–0.4, corresponding to a magnetic field decay time of a few fm.
- Magnetic field dependence of electrical conductivity becomes significant only at eB ≥ 5 m_π² in the high-density, low-temperature regime.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of magnetic field effects in quark matter beyond the commonly studied chiral magnetic effect (CME), which has been the subject of intense debate and still lacks sound experimental confirmation. The authors note that lattice calculations of CME have been performed only at low temperature and in thermodynamic equilibrium, and that microscopic derivation of topological effects is lacking. The paper contrasts with prior studies that assumed constant, uniform magnetic fields and infinite systems in equilibrium, and it highlights open questions such as the magnetic field dependence of the deconfinement temperature T_L (where recent lattice calculations indicate a reduction, contradicting earlier results) and the need to investigate the Nernst effect in heavy-ion collisions.
