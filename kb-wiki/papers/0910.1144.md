# Hot hadron-quark mixed phase including hyperons

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition with the finite size effects at finite temperature. For the hadron phase, we adopt a realistic equation of state in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory including hyperons. The properties of the mixed phase are clarified by considering the finite size effects under the Gibbs conditions. We find that the equation of state becomes softer than that at zero-temperature for some density region. We also find that the equation of state gets closer to that given by the Maxwell construction. Moreover, the number of hyperons is suppressed by the presence of quarks. These are characteristic features of the hadron-quark mixed phase, and should be important for many astrophysical phenomena such as mergers of binary neutron stars.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark mixed phase equation of state (EOS) at finite temperature becomes softer than at zero temperature for some density region, and the EOS gets closer to that given by the Maxwell construction, but these effects are conditional on the mechanical instability of the pasta structures induced by thermal effects, which causes the mixed phase to disappear above approximately T = 60 MeV.

## Method

The authors adopt a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach for the hadron phase, using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential, Urbana UIX three-body forces, and Nijmegen NSC89 hyperon-nucleon potentials, with finite-temperature effects included via the Frozen Correlations Approximation. For the quark phase, they use the thermodynamic bag model with bag constant B = 100 MeV/fm^3. The hadron-quark mixed phase is treated under the Gibbs conditions with finite-size effects (surface tension, Coulomb energy, charge screening) using the Wigner-Seitz approximation for pasta structures (droplet, rod, slab), with a surface tension parameter σ = 40 MeV/fm^2.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant B = 100 MeV/fm^3; surface tension σ = 40 MeV/fm^2
- Pasta structures become mechanically unstable and disappear at T ≳ 60 MeV
- At T = 30 MeV, the maximum mass of hybrid stars is M_max = 1.42 M_sun, compared to M_max = 1.43 M_sun at zero temperature
- Hyperons appear at densities ∼ n_0 (nuclear saturation density) at T = 30 MeV, while they appear above 2 n_0 at T = 0
- Droplet radius at n_B = 2 n_0: R = 12.5 fm at T = 0, R = 18.7 fm at T = 30 MeV; cell sizes R_W = 23.7 fm (T=0) and R_W = 28.8 fm (T=30 MeV)
- Pressure of the mixed phase at finite temperature is 10–30% smaller than at zero temperature

## Context

This work extends the previous zero-temperature studies of Maruyama et al. (2007) on hadron-quark mixed phase with finite-size effects to finite temperature, and includes hyperons which were absent in earlier finite-temperature studies by Burgio et al. (2008) and Yasutake et al. (2009). The results are relevant for astrophysical phenomena such as mergers of binary neutron stars and supernovae, and the authors note that the hyperon suppression in the mixed phase may help resolve the maximum-mass problem for neutron stars previously discussed by Schulze et al. (2006) and Baldo et al. (2006).
