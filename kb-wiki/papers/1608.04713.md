# Thermal Effects in Dense Matter Beyond Mean Field Theory

> The formalism of next-to-leading order Fermi Liquid Theory is employed to calculate the thermal properties of symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matter in a relativistic many-body theory beyond the mean field level which includes two-loop effects. For all thermal variables, the semi-analytical next-to-leading order corrections reproduce results of the exact numerical calculations for entropies per baryon up to 2. This corresponds to excellent agreement down to sub-nuclear densities for temperatures up to $20$ MeV. In addition to providing physical insights, a rapid evaluation of the equation of state in the homogeneous phase of hot and dense matter is achieved through the use of the zero-temperature Landau effective mass function and its derivatives.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) Fermi Liquid Theory formalism reproduces exact numerical results for all thermal variables in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter for entropies per baryon up to 2, corresponding to temperatures up to 20 MeV and densities down to approximately 0.1 fm⁻³, but the agreement degrades at 50 MeV due to the onset of semi-degenerate conditions and temperature dependence of the Dirac effective mass not captured by the zero-temperature formalism.

## Method

The authors employed the NLO extension of Fermi Liquid Theory, which uses Sommerfeld expansions truncated at the third term to derive semi-analytical expressions for entropy, thermal energy, thermal pressure, thermal chemical potential, and specific heats in terms of the zero-temperature Landau effective mass function and its derivatives. These expressions were applied to a relativistic field-theoretical model that includes two-loop exchange contributions from σ, ω, ρ, and π mesons beyond the mean-field level, using the zero-temperature single-particle spectra and Dirac effective masses from the model to compute thermal properties. The NLO results were compared against exact numerical calculations from the same model at temperatures of 20 and 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Entropy per baryon S up to 2: the NLO corrections reproduce exact results for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at T = 20 MeV down to n ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³
- Temperature range of validity: T ≤ 20 MeV for densities n ≥ 0.1 fm⁻³
- Density threshold for homogeneous phase: n ≥ 0.1 fm⁻³ (below this, inhomogeneous phases occur requiring separate treatment)
- Reference density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ used for single-particle spectrum calculations
- Meson masses used: M = 939 MeV, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV
- Couplings: g_σ = 8.604, g_ω = 7.522, g_ρ = 7.614, κ₃ = 4.84, κ₄ = -4.47, f_π = 93 MeV, g_A = 1.26

## Context

This work extends the NLO Fermi Liquid Theory formalism previously developed by Constantinou et al. (2015) for non-relativistic potential models and relativistic mean-field models, now applying it to a relativistic model with two-loop exchange effects recently reported by Zhang et al. (2016). The results demonstrate that the NLO formalism provides a rapid and accurate method for computing thermal properties of dense nucleonic matter from zero-temperature single-particle spectra alone, which is relevant for simulations of core-collapse supernovae, neutron star cooling, and binary neutron star mergers. The approach enables checking of time-consuming finite-temperature many-body calculations and provides physical insights through the use of the Landau effective mass function and its derivatives.
