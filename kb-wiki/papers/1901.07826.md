# The critical notes to the solutions of the "puzzle" of hyperfine structure in 82+ and 80+ $^{209}$Bi ions

> Some aspects of description of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect in hyperfine splitting of the H- and Li-like ions of 209Bi are considered by application of the surface and volume models of the nuclear currents. Extension of these models, used in internal conversion theory, to description of the HFS allows one to successfully describe the effect, without resorting to the specific differences. The latters are shown not to be needed at all. Moreover, they turn out to depend on the nuclear model even stronger than the HFS values themselves. Comparison of the calculated HFS values to experiment shows a satisfactory agreement. Both models provide equally good description of the effect. However, they result in different values of the retrieved rms radius of the nuclear magnetization. In this respect, situation resembles the proton radius puzzle. Prospects of future research are discussed.

# The critical notes to the solutions of the "puzzle" of hyperfine structure in 82+ and 80+ $^{209}$Bi ions

## Key claim

The specific difference (SD) method, proposed to cancel the Bohr-Weisskopf effect in hyperfine splitting of H-like and Li-like $^{209}$Bi ions, is not model-independent and actually depends on the nuclear model more strongly than the hyperfine splitting values themselves; both the surface-current (SC) and volume-current (VC) nuclear models can equally well reproduce the experimental hyperfine splitting values by fitting a single radius parameter, but they yield different values for the retrieved rms radius of nuclear magnetization.

## Method

The authors apply the surface-current (SC) and volume-current (VC) nuclear models, originally developed for internal conversion theory, to calculate the Bohr-Weisskopf penetration matrix elements for the 1s and 2s states of H-like and Li-like $^{209}$Bi ions. They fit the SC model radius to minimize the difference in penetration matrix elements between the two models, then compute hyperfine splitting values including QED corrections (-0.0268 eV for 1s, -0.005 eV for 2s) and electron-electron interactions (-0.030 eV for the Li-like 2s state). The specific difference $\Delta'E = W_{2s} - \zeta W_{1s}$ and the parameter $\zeta = t_{2s}^\nu / t_{1s}^\nu$ are evaluated for both models across a range of nuclear radii.

## Key numbers

- Hyperfine splitting for the 1s state: calculated values range from 5.06970 eV to 5.13809 eV (for $\mu$ = 4.1106 nuclear magnetons) depending on model radius, compared to the experimental value of 5.08503(11) eV
- Hyperfine splitting for the 2s state: calculated values range from 0.794952 eV to 0.806363 eV (for $\mu$ = 4.1106 nuclear magnetons), compared to the experimental value of 0.797645(18) eV
- Specific difference $\Delta'E$ values range from -61.11 meV to -61.26 meV across both models, agreeing only to the third decimal place
- The parameter $\zeta$ coincides between SC and VC models only to the fifth decimal (0.16688 to 0.16691)
- QED corrections used: $\Delta E_{QED}^{1s}$ = -0.0268 eV, $\Delta E_{QED}^{2s}$ = -0.005 eV
- Electron-electron interaction correction for Li-like 2s: $\Delta E_{e-e}^{2s}$ = -0.030 eV
- The alternative magnetic moment value $\mu$ = 4.092 nuclear magnetons rescales all HFS values by approximately 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the application of internal conversion theory methods, specifically the surface-current model of Sliv (1960s) and the volume-current model used in muonic conversion studies, to the hyperfine structure problem in $^{209}$Bi ions. It directly contradicts the claim of model independence for the specific difference method proposed by Shabaev (2015) and used by Lochmann (2014) and others, showing that the SD values are more model-dependent than the HFS values themselves. The authors draw an analogy to the proton radius puzzle, where different experimental methods yield different rms radii, and suggest that a two-parameter magnetic moment method developed in Karpeshin and Trzhaskovskaya (2015) can provide model-independent extraction of the second and fourth moments of the magnetization distribution. Future measurement of the $2p_{1/2}$ hyperfine splitting is identified as critical for resolving the remaining ambiguities.
