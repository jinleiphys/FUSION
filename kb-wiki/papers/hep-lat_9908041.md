# The spectrum of the three-dimensional adjoint Higgs model and hot SU(2) gauge theory

> We compute the mass spectrum of the SU(2) adjoint Higgs model in 2+1 dimensions at several points located in the (metastable) confinement region of its phase diagram. We find a dense spectrum consisting of an almost unaltered repetition of the glueball spectrum of the pure gauge theory, and additional bound states of adjoint scalars. For the parameters chosen, the model represents the effective finite temperature theory for pure SU(2) gauge theory in four dimensions, obtained after perturbative dimensional reduction. Comparing with the spectrum of screening masses obtained in recent simulations of four-dimensional pure gauge theory at finite temperature, for the low lying states we find quantitative agreement between the full and the effective theory for temperatures as low as T = 2 Tc. This establishes the model under study as the correct effective theory, and dimensional reduction as a viable tool for the description of thermodynamic properties. We furthermore compare the perturbative contribution O(g.T) with the non-perturbative contributions O(g^2.T) and O(g^3.T) to the Debye mass. The latter turns out to be dominated by the scale g^2.T, whereas higher order contributions are small corrections.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the three-dimensional SU(2) adjoint Higgs model, when simulated in the metastable confinement region of its phase diagram, quantitatively reproduces the screening mass spectrum of the full four-dimensional finite-temperature SU(2) gauge theory for temperatures as low as T = 2 Tc, with the caveat that the effective theory parameters place it in the Higgs phase while the physical connection to four-dimensional physics is only valid in the confinement phase, which is metastable at those parameters.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of the SU(2) adjoint Higgs model in 2+1 dimensions at three temperatures (T = 2 Tc, T = 4 Tc, T = 5 Tc), using blocked field variables and a variational method with matrix correlators to extract the mass spectrum. They constructed gauge-invariant operators with definite quantum numbers J^P_R (angular momentum, parity, and scalar reflection symmetry) from combinations of scalar fields, gauge links, and mixed operators, then diagonalised the correlation matrices to identify ground states and excitations in multiple channels including 0^+_+, 2^+_+, 0^-_-, and 1^+_-.

## Key numbers

* At T = 2 Tc, the lightest 0^+_+ state (the Debye screening mass) has mass M/T approximately 2.5, with the perturbative contribution O(g T) dominated by non-perturbative contributions at scale g^2 T rather than g^3 T.
* The effective theory parameters are given by g_3^2 = 10.7668 / ln(8.3 T/Tc) times T, x = 0.3636 / ln(6.6 T/Tc), and y(x) = 2/(9 pi^2 x) + 1/(4 pi^2) + O(x).
* The critical temperature used for parameter matching is Tc = 1.23(11) Lambda_MSbar from four-dimensional lattice simulations.
* Quantitative agreement between the full four-dimensional theory and the effective theory for low-lying screening masses is found for temperatures as low as T = 2 Tc.

## Context

This work extends the dimensional reduction programme previously applied to the electroweak phase transition (studied by numerical and analytical methods) to the case of pure SU(2) gauge theory at finite temperature relevant for the quark-gluon plasma. It builds on earlier simulations by the same authors and collaborators (Hart, Philipsen, and others) that established the phase diagram of the three-dimensional adjoint Higgs model, and on four-dimensional finite-temperature simulations by Doring and collaborators (1998-1999) that classified screening masses by three-dimensional symmetry groups. The results enable quantitative use of the dimensionally reduced theory for thermodynamic properties of the quark-gluon plasma, and demonstrate that the lightest gauge-invariant state consists of the A0 field (the electric gauge potential), which therefore cannot be integrated out perturbatively.
