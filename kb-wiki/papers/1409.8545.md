# Effective Spectral Function for Quasielastic Scattering on Nuclei from Deuterium to Lead

> Spectral functions do not fully describe quasielastic electron and neutrino scattering from nuclei because they only model the initial state. Final state interactions distort the shape of the differential cross section at the peak and increase the cross section at the tails of the distribution. We show that the kinematic distributions predicted by the $ψ'$ superscaling formalism can be well described with a modified {\it {effective spectral function}} (ESF). By construction, models using ESF in combination with the transverse enhancement contribution correctly predict electron QE scattering data. Our values for the binding energy parameter $Δ$ are smaller than $\overlineε$ extracted within the Fermi gas model from pre 1971 data by Moniz, probably because these early cross sections were not corrected for coulomb effects.

# Effective Spectral Function for Quasielastic Scattering on Nuclei from Deuterium to Lead

## Key claim

The authors show that the kinematic distributions predicted by the ψ' superscaling formalism can be well described with a modified effective spectral function (ESF), but note that their values for the binding energy parameter Δ are smaller than the average removal energy ε̅ extracted within the Fermi gas model from pre-1971 data by Moniz, probably because those early cross sections were not corrected for Coulomb effects.

## Method

The authors parametrize an effective spectral function for nuclei from deuterium to lead by fitting the momentum distribution parameters and the 1p1h/2p2h process fractions so that the predicted normalized quasielastic differential cross section (1/σ)(dσ/dν) reproduces the ψ' superscaling predictions at Q² values of 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, and 0.7 GeV². The ESF model includes a Q²-dependent correction factor V(Q²) = 1 - e^{-xQ²} with x = 12.04 GeV⁻² to account for final state interactions at low Q², and uses separate energy expressions for the 1p1h (one proton, one hole) and 2p2h (two protons, two holes) processes.

## Key numbers

- For carbon-12: binding energy parameter Δ = 12.5 MeV, with f₁ₚ₁ₕ = 0.808 and f₂ₚ₂ₕ = 0.192
- For helium-4: Δ = 14.0 MeV, f₁ₚ₁ₕ = 0.791
- For lead-208: Δ = 18.8 MeV, f₁ₚ₁ₕ = 0.896
- The Q²-dependent correction factor uses x = 12.04 GeV⁻² in V(Q²) = 1 - e^{-xQ²}
- The ψ' superscaling Fermi broadening parameter K_F ranges from 0.100 GeV (deuterium) to 0.245 GeV (A > 60)
- The ψ' superscaling energy shift E_shift ranges from 0.001 GeV (deuterium) to 0.028 GeV (A = 38-56)
- The ESF momentum distribution is defined for k < 0.65 GeV and set to zero above that value

## Context

This work extends the ψ' superscaling formalism developed by Bosted and Mamyan (2012) and a more recent updated fit by Eric (2014), which fully describe the longitudinal response function of quasielastic electron scattering data. The authors show that existing spectral function models implemented in neutrino event generators such as GENIE, NEUT, NuWro, and GiBUU (including the Fermi gas model by Moniz (1971) and the Benhar-Fantoni spectral function) do not reproduce the ψ' superscaling predictions for the normalized quasielastic differential cross section. The ESF approach enables neutrino Monte Carlo event generators to implement a spectral function that correctly predicts electron quasielastic scattering data by construction, with the transverse enhancement contribution included separately.
