# Thermal model predictions of hadron ratios at LHC

## Key claim
The thermal model, using chemical freeze-out parameters T = 161 ± 4 MeV and μ_b = 0.8^{+1.2}_{-0.6} MeV extrapolated from lower-energy data, predicts that antiparticle/particle ratios in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC will be very close to unity, except for the pbar/p ratio which reflects a small but nonzero baryochemical potential.

## Method
The authors applied the thermal (statistical) model of hadron production, previously fitted to hadron yields from central nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC (Andronic 2005), to predict chemical freeze-out conditions at LHC energy. They then computed hadron yield ratios for central Pb+Pb collisions using those extrapolated freeze-out parameters, assuming no contribution from weak decays to the yields of pions, kaons, and protons.

## Context
This work extends the thermal model predictions from SPS and RHIC energies (Andronic 2005) to LHC energy, providing a baseline for the first heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The predictions for resonance yields (Table 2) are made under the assumption that resonance abundances are fixed at chemical freeze-out, but the authors note that RHIC data (Adams 2006) may indicate rescattering and regeneration of resonances after chemical freeze-out, so this hypothesis requires experimental verification at LHC. The results enable direct comparison with LHC data to test whether the thermal model remains valid at the highest collision energies.
