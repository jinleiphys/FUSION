# Erratum: Anisotropy in the EoS of Magnetized Quark Matter [Phys Rev C91, 065205 (2015)]

> In this erratum we present the correct result for the magnetization of the three flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model which was originally obtained by us in Phys Rev C91, 065205 (2015).

## Key claim

The corrected magnetization for the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, obtained by removing the spurious derivative terms of the condensates with respect to the magnetic field, shows much smaller oscillations than originally reported and is now consistent with the expression previously obtained by Avancini et al. (2017).

## Method

The authors recalculated the magnetization of magnetized quark matter within the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by correcting Eq. (3.4) of the original paper, which had mistakenly included derivatives of the condensates with respect to the magnetic field that vanish due to the extremization conditions. The corrected magnetization includes only the kinetic term of the thermodynamical potential, and was used to reobtain both the magnetization and the parallel and perpendicular components of the pressure for beta-equilibrium matter and isochemical potential quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Magnetization is essentially positive for magnetic fields B > 5 × 10^17 G, but may take negative values below that field, never lower than -0.002 fm^-2
- The perpendicular pressure falls on top of the parallel contribution for fields B ≤ 10^18 G
- P_perp differs from P_parallel only above B > 10^18 G
- Anisotropic effects on the pressure become non-negligible for magnetic fields larger than 4 × 10^18 G

## Context

This erratum corrects the original work by Menezes, Pinto, and Providência (2015) on the anisotropy in the equation of state of magnetized quark matter. The corrected results are now very similar to those obtained by Huang et al. (2010) for strange quark matter and by Strickland, Dexheimer, and Menezes (2012) for a gas of protons without anomalous magnetic moment. The new pressure results align with findings by Rabhi, Providência, and Da Providência (2009) and Dexheimer, Menezes, and Strickland (2014), but differ from those of Avancini et al. (2017). The corrected magnetization no longer shows the strong effects at the onset of the strange quark that were previously attributed to the derivative of the condensate.
