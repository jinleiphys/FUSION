# Does the NJL chiral phase transition affect the elliptic flow of a fluid at fixed $η/s$?

> We have derived and solved numerically the Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equations that includes both two-body collisions and the chiral phase transition by mean of NJL-field dynamics. The scope is to understand if the field dynamics supply new genuine effects on the build-up of the elliptic flow $v_2$, a measure of the asymmetry in the momentum space, and in particular if it can affect the relation between $v_2$ and the shear viscosity to entropy ratio $η/s$. Solving the transport equation with a constant cross section for the condition of $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ AGeV it is shown a sizable suppression of $v_2$ due to the attractive nature of the field dynamics that generates the constituent mass. However the key result is that if $η/s$ of the system is kept fixed by an appropriate local renormalization of the cross section the $v_2$ does not depend on the details of the collisional and/or field dynamics and in particular it is not affected significantly by the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the shear viscosity to entropy ratio (η/s) of the system is kept fixed by local renormalization of the cross section, the elliptic flow v₂ does not depend on the details of the collisional or field dynamics and is not significantly affected by the chiral phase transition, despite a sizable suppression of v₂ from the attractive NJL field dynamics when a constant cross section is used.

## Method

The authors derived and solved numerically the Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation for quarks and antiquarks, including both two-body collisions and the chiral phase transition via the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) mean-field dynamics. The transport equation was coupled self-consistently to the non-equilibrium gap equation that determines the local effective mass M(x) from the distribution functions. Simulations were performed for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 AGeV using a three-dimensional lattice with test particle methods, and the cross section was locally renormalized according to a formula derived for massive particles to keep η/s fixed.

## Key numbers

- The NJL mean field causes a decrease of the average elliptic flow ⟨v₂⟩ of about 15% for a constant transport cross section of σtr = 10 mb.
- The presence of the NJL field suppresses v₂(pT) by about 20% at pT > 1 GeV when using a constant cross section.
- The maximum initial temperature used in simulations is T = 340 MeV with initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for Au+Au at b = 7 fm.
- The NJL parametrization used is the Buballa parametrization: Λ = 588 MeV, gΛ² = 2.88, m = 5.6 MeV.
- The results for fixed η/s were checked at impact parameters b = 3, 5, 9 fm and for η/s up to π⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends previous cascade-level transport approaches (Xu 2007, Molnar 2008, Ferini 2008) that missed field interactions responsible for the chiral phase transition, and goes beyond seminal work on NJL transport (Abada 1994, Mishustin 1997) that lacked a collision term and was not applied to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The finding that v₂(pT) is mainly driven by η/s even when chiral dynamics are included validates the use of viscous hydrodynamics and simpler cascade models for determining η/s from elliptic flow data, and opens the possibility of extending the approach to quasi-particle models fitted to lattice QCD results.
