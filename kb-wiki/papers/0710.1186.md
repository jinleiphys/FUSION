# Heavy quark symmetry constraints on semileptonic form factors and decay widths of doubly heavy baryons

> We show how heavy quark symmetry constraints on doubly heavy baryon semileptonic decay widths can be used to test the validity of different quark model calculations. The large discrepancies in the results observed between different quark model approaches can be understood in terms of a severe violation of heavy quark spin symmetry constraints by some of those models.

## Key claim

Heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) constraints on semileptonic decay widths of doubly heavy baryons can be used to test the validity of different quark model calculations, with large discrepancies between models understood as a severe violation of HQSS by some of those models, though the constraints are only approximate for actual heavy quark masses and near the zero recoil point.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic quark model with the AL1 interquark potential to evaluate form factors and decay widths for semileptonic transitions of doubly heavy baryons (bb to bc and bc to cc) driven by the b to c quark-level transition. They compared their results for 1/2 to 1/2, 1/2 to 3/2, and 3/2 to 3/2 transitions against HQSS predictions derived from the superflavor formalism, which requires all hadronic matrix elements to be governed by a single Isgur-Wise function near zero recoil. They also computed decay width ratios and compared them with results from other quark model approaches including Ebert et al. (2004), Guo et al. (1998), and Sanchis-Lozano (1995).

## Context

This work extends the HQSS formalism developed by White (1991) and Flynn et al. (2007) for doubly heavy baryon semileptonic decays, applying it to test quark model calculations. The authors compare their nonrelativistic quark model results (using the AL1 potential from Silvestre-Brac and Semay, 1994) with those from Ebert et al. (2004), Guo et al. (1998), Sanchis-Lozano (1995), and Onishchenko et al. (2000). The results show that the calculation by Guo et al. (1998) severely violates HQSS constraints, while the authors' model and that of Ebert et al. (2004) are compatible with HQSS predictions. This enables future experimental measurements of these decay widths to discriminate between different theoretical approaches and to determine the physical mixing between Xi_bc and Xi'_bc states.
