# Strange quark suppression from a simultaneous Monte Carlo analysis of parton distributions and fragmentation functions

> We perform the first simultaneous extraction of unpolarized parton distributions and fragmentation functions from a Monte Carlo analysis of inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan lepton-pair production, and single-inclusive $e^+ e^-$ annihilation data. We use data resampling techniques to thoroughly explore the Bayesian posterior distribution of the extracted functions, and use $k$-means clustering on the parameter samples to identify the configurations that give the best description across all reactions. Inclusion of the semi-inclusive data reveals a strong suppression of the strange quark distribution at parton momentum fractions $x \gtrsim 0.01$, in contrast with the ATLAS observation of enhanced strangeness in $W^\pm$ and $Z$ production at the LHC. Our study reveals significant correlations between the strange quark density and the strange $\to$ kaon fragmentation function needed to simultaneously describe semi-inclusive $K^\pm$ production data from COMPASS and inclusive $K^\pm$ spectra in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation from ALEPH and SLD, as well as between the strange and light antiquark densities in the proton.

## Key claim

The strange quark parton distribution function (PDF) is strongly suppressed at momentum fractions \(x \gtrsim 0.01\) when semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data are included in a simultaneous fit with parton distributions and fragmentation functions (FFs), in contrast to the enhanced strangeness reported by ATLAS from \(W^\pm\) and \(Z\) production at the LHC. This suppression arises because the SIDIS and single-inclusive \(e^+ e^-\) annihilation (SIA) data force a large strange-to-kaon fragmentation function, which then requires a small strange quark PDF to describe the kaon production cross sections.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, simultaneously extracting unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs) from inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan (DY) lepton-pair production, SIDIS multiplicities, and SIA data. They used a Monte Carlo approach with data resampling to sample the Bayesian posterior distribution, and applied \(k\)-means clustering on parameter samples to identify the best-fitting configurations across all reactions. The analysis included 4,366 data points from BCDMS, SLAC, NMC, HERA, Fermilab E866, COMPASS, ALEPH, SLD, Belle, BaBar, and other experiments.

## Key numbers

* The strange to nonstrange ratio \(R_s = (s+\bar s)/(\bar u + \bar d)\) is found to be approximately \(0.2-0.3\) at \(x \sim 0.02\) and \(Q^2 = 1.9\) GeV\(^2\), in contrast to the ATLAS value of \(R_s \approx 1.13\) at the same kinematics.
* The final fit sample of 953 fits to 4,366 data points yields a mean reduced \(\chi^2 = 1.30\), with individual \(\chi^2\) values of 1.28 for 2,680 DIS points, 1.25 for 992 SIDIS points, 1.67 for 250 DY points, and 1.27 for 444 SIA points.
* For the SIDIS data, the \(\chi^2\) per datum is 1.18 for pions and 1.30 for kaons; for the SIA data, the \(\chi^2\) per datum is 1.07 for \(\pi^\pm\) and 1.48 for \(K^\pm\).
* The best solutions for the SIA data give \(\chi^2_{\rm SLD} = 1.38\) and \(\chi^2_{\rm ALEPH} = 0.74\), while unfavored solutions with larger strange PDF and smaller strange-to-kaon FF give much larger values of 4.10 and 4.62, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the JAM collaboration's previous simultaneous extraction of spin-dependent PDFs and FFs (JAM, 2017) to the unpolarized case, and is the first global QCD fit to combine standard DIS and DY data sets (as used by MMHT14, CJ15, ABMP16, NNPDF3.1, CT14) with SIDIS multiplicities and SIA data. The results contradict the enhanced strangeness reported by ATLAS (ATLAS-epWZ16, 2017) and the CSKK analysis (Cooper-Sarkar and Wichmann, 2018), and instead agree with the lower strange-to-nonstrange ratios extracted from neutrino experiments such as CCFR, NuTeV, CHORUS, and NOMAD. The analysis enables future simultaneous extractions of transverse momentum dependent PDFs and FFs, and will inform precision determinations of the CKM matrix element \(V_{cs}\) and the \(W\)-boson mass.
