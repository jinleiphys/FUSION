# Stability of two-fermion bound states in the explicitly covariant Light-Front Dynamics

> The covariant light-front equations have been solved exactly for a two fermion system with different boson exchange ladder kernels. We present a method to study the cutoff dependence of these equations and to determine whether they need to be regularized or not. Results are presented for scalar and pseudo-scalar exchange. This latter furthermore exhibits some strange particularities which will be discussed.

## Key claim

The covariant light-front equations for a two-fermion system with scalar exchange require regularization only when the coupling constant exceeds a critical value (α_c ≈ 3.72), while for pseudo-scalar exchange the equations are stable without regularization but exhibit a "quasi-degeneracy" where the coupling constant varies by only about 2% across a five-hundred-fold range of binding energies.

## Method

The authors solved the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) equations exactly for a two-fermion system with scalar and pseudo-scalar boson exchange ladder kernels, focusing on states with J^π = 0^+, 1^+. They developed an analytical method to study the cutoff dependence of the integral equations by examining the asymptotic behavior of the kernels in the (k, k') plane, deriving eigenvalue equations for the power-law exponent β of the wave function at large momenta, and determining the critical coupling constant from the condition β = 0.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling constant for scalar exchange (J=0^+ state): α_c = 3.72, independent of the exchanged boson mass μ
- For scalar exchange below α_c (α=3), binding energy is cutoff-independent; above α_c (α=4), binding energy diverges as k_max → ∞
- For scalar exchange, the asymptotic wave function follows a power law ~1/k^(2+β) with β=0.82 for binding energy B=0.05 and α=1.096
- For pseudo-scalar exchange: α=49.5 for B=0.001 and α=48.6 for B=0.5 (only 2% difference across 500-fold binding energy range)
- Analytical model of the pseudo-scalar second channel gives α=22.5327 for B=0.001 and α=22.6040 for B=2.00

## Context

This work extends the covariant light-front dynamics approach of Karmanov (1976) and the standard light-front formalism reviewed by Brodsky, Pauli, and Pinsky (1998) to two-fermion bound states with various boson exchange couplings. The method for determining critical coupling constants via asymptotic analysis of the kernel behavior parallels the non-relativistic treatment of the -α'/r² potential from Landau and Lifshitz, where binding energy becomes cutoff-dependent above a critical strength. The results enable systematic regularization of light-front equations for fermion systems and show that the asymptotic behavior of bound state wave functions can in principle be used to measure the coupling constant.
