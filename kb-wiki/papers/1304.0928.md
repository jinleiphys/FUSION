# Evaluation of the escape widths of the giant dipole resonances in the fermi-liquid theory

> In the theory of the finite fermi-systems [1], it was shown that giant resonances in nuclei can be consider as the zero-sound excitations which exhaust the large part of the energy-weighted sum rules. In the framework of [1] the solutions of the zero-sound dispersion equation in the symmetric nuclear matter, ω_s(k), are considered. The method of calculation of these solutions is based on the analytical structure of the polarization operators Π(ω,k). The solutions of the dispersion equation, which are real at small k, become complex with k increasing when the overlapping of the collective and 1p1h modes starts. The imaginary part of ω_s(k) is the result of the collective zero-sound excitation decay to the real particle-hole pairs and can be compared with the escape width of resonances. We compare the experimental energy and escape width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in the nucleus A with Reω_s(k) and Imω_s(k) taken at a definite wave vector k=k_A.

# Evaluation of the escape widths of the giant dipole resonances in the fermi-liquid theory

## Key claim
The imaginary part of the zero-sound dispersion solution ω_s(k) in symmetric nuclear matter can be compared to the escape width of giant dipole resonances in nuclei, but the approach is acknowledged as too simple for a satisfactory quantitative description and is primarily useful for qualitative trends.

## Method
The zero-sound dispersion equation 1 + 4C₀FΦ = 0 is solved in symmetric nuclear matter using the Landau-Migdal effective quasiparticle-quasihole interaction with constant coupling parameters (F, F', G, G'). The polarization operator Π(ω,k) is constructed from Migdal's function, and the solutions ω_s(k) become complex when the collective mode overlaps with the 1p1h continuum. The wave vector k_A for a given nucleus A is taken from the Steinwedel-Jensen model as k_A = π/(2R) with R = r₀A^{1/3}, and Re ω_s(k_A) and Im ω_s(k_A) are compared to experimental GDR energies and escape widths.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C: experimental E_GDR = 27.2 MeV and escape width Γ↑_GDR ≈ 3 MeV; calculation with p_F = 200 MeV, F = 1.2, m = m₀ gives E_GDR = 30 MeV and Γ↑_GDR = 7.0 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb: experimental E_GDR = 13.7 MeV and escape width Γ↑_GDR ≈ 0.5–2.0 MeV; calculation with p_F = 260 MeV, F = 1.0, m = m₀ gives E_GDR = 13 MeV and Γ↑_GDR = 0.17 MeV.
- Using the linear dispersion relation ω_s(k) = c k with c = 0.39 ± 0.02 (for p_F = 260 MeV, m = m₀, F' = 1.0) and defining k_A = E_GDR(A)/c yields Γ↑_GDR(¹²C) = 3.0 MeV and Γ↑_GDR(²⁰⁸Pb) = 0.36 MeV.
- The escape widths are approximated by the formula Γ↑_GDR(A) = −5.2 A^{−1/6} + 14.2 A^{−1/3} (in MeV).
- The phenomenological GDR energy formula used is E_GDR(A) = 31.2 A^{−1/3} + 20.6 A^{−1/6} MeV.

## Context
This work extends the theory of finite Fermi systems by Migdal (1970s) and the random phase approximation approach to giant resonances, treating them as zero-sound excitations in nuclear matter. It builds on the Steinwedel-Jensen model (1950) and its modification by Bertsch and collaborators for defining the wave vector k_A. The method enables qualitative predictions for how resonance parameters depend on temperature, proton-neutron density differences, effective interaction form, and additional open 1p1h channels, though the definition of k_A for multipolarities beyond dipole remains problematic.
