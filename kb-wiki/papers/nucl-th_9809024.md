# Effects of Isospin Asymmetry and In-Medium Corrections on Balance Energy

> The effects of an isospin asymmetry and in-medium corrections to the nucleon collision cross section on the balance energy are explored. The BUU model for intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions is used with isospin-dependent mean fields to calculate the balance energies of 58Fe + 58Fe and 58Ni + 58Ni for a range of impact parameters. We find that we are able to reproduce the impact parameter dependence of the balance energy, and the sign (but not the magnitude) of the shift in balance balance energy as a function of isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The BUU model with isospin-dependent mean fields and in-medium corrections to the nucleon-nucleon cross section can reproduce the impact parameter dependence of the balance energy and the sign of the shift in balance energy as a function of isospin asymmetry, but it fails to reproduce the magnitude of that shift, underestimating the splitting between the two systems by at least a factor of 2 to 4.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with two different isospin-dependent mean fields: one from Bao-An Li (compressibility 200 MeV, symmetry energy coefficient C=32 MeV) and one from Sobotka (compressibility 380 MeV in symmetric matter). They calculated proton freeze-out phase-space distributions for 58Fe + 58Fe and 58Ni + 58Ni collisions at reduced impact parameters of 0.275, 0.375, 0.475, and 0.575, and beam energies from 50 to 100 MeV/A, applying a density-dependent in-medium correction to the free nucleon-nucleon cross section parameterized as sigma_NN = sigma_free_NN * (1 + alpha * rho/rho_0) with alpha values of -0.2 and -0.3.

## Key numbers

- Balance energies were calculated for 58Fe + 58Fe and 58Ni + 58Ni at beam energies of 50, 60, 70, 80, 90, and 100 MeV/A
- Isospin asymmetry delta = 0.103 for 58Fe and delta = 0.034 for 58Ni
- The ratio of Coulomb forces between Fe (26 protons) and Ni (28 protons) is (26/28)^2 = 0.862
- The mean field of Bao-An Li used compressibility K = 200 MeV with coefficients A = -109 MeV, B = 82 MeV, sigma = 7/6, and C = 32 MeV
- The Sobotka mean field used coefficients a = -3.66 MeV, b = 15.0, and c = 23.4 MeV, yielding compressibility 380 MeV
- In-medium correction parameter alpha = -0.3 was used with the Li mean field; alpha = -0.2 was used with the Sobotka mean field
- The difference in average nucleon-nucleon cross section between the two systems is approximately 0.5% for zeta = 1
- The theoretical splitting between the two systems was at least a factor of 4 smaller than experiment with the Li mean field and alpha = -0.3, and at least a factor of 2 smaller with the Sobotka mean field and alpha = -0.2

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Pak et al. (1997), who first showed that the balance energy differs for symmetric systems of equal mass but different isospin asymmetry. The study builds on previous BUU calculations by Ogilvie et al. (1990), Klakow et al. (1993), and Westfall et al. (1993) that demonstrated the model consistently under-predicts balance energies when using free-space scattering cross sections. The results enable future experimental studies at radioactive beam facilities to explore larger isospin asymmetries, which could help distinguish between different mean field formulations (Li vs. Sobotka) and improve understanding of the isospin dependence of in-medium cross sections.
