# New Analysis of Threshold Photoproduction Data from MAMI

> In this talk I will review the recently published results by the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations at MAMI on neutral pion photoproduction in the near-threshold region. The combined measurement of the differential cross section and the photon beam asymmetry with low statistical errors allowed for a precise determination of the energy dependence of the real parts of the S- and P-wave amplitudes for the first time, providing the most stringent test to date of the predictions of Chiral Perturbation Theory and its energy region of agreement with experiment.

# New Analysis of Threshold Photoproduction Data from MAMI

## Key claim
The combined measurement of differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry for neutral pion photoproduction near threshold allows the first precise determination of the energy dependence of the real parts of S- and P-wave amplitudes, providing the most stringent test of Chiral Perturbation Theory to date, but only up to approximately 170 MeV photon energy, above which the theory fails to reproduce the differential cross section.

## Method
Single-energy multipoles (E0+, E1+, M1+, M1-) were extracted by fitting the real parts as free parameters to differential cross section and photon beam asymmetry data from the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations at MAMI, with data collected every 2.4 MeV starting at 146 MeV photon energy. The imaginary part of E0+ was set to the unitary value, imaginary parts of the P-wave multipoles were set to zero below 185 MeV, and D waves were fixed to Born terms. Energy-dependent multipole parametrizations were then performed using three approaches: an empirical eight-parameter fit, Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBCHPT) with five parameters, and Relativistic Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (RBCHPT) with five parameters.

## Key numbers
- Data collected starting at E_gamma = 146 MeV photon energy in the laboratory frame, approximately every 2.4 MeV
- Empirical fit provides good description of data and multipoles up to E_gamma = 185 MeV
- Both HBCHPT and RBCHPT provide good description of data and multipoles up to E_gamma approximately 170 MeV
- Above 170 MeV, Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations fail to reproduce the differential cross section
- Below 185 MeV, setting imaginary parts of E1+, M1+, and M1- to zero is an excellent approximation because the Delta contribution to the imaginary part of M1+ starts to play a role above that energy

## Context
This work extends the experimental tests of Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions for neutral pion photoproduction, building on earlier measurements by the A2 and CB-TAPS Collaborations reported by Hornidge (2013) and by the same group in 2012. The extracted multipoles are compared to predictions from several approaches designed for the resonance region: Dispersive Effective Field Theory by Gasparyan and Lutz, the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei model, the Bonn-Gatchina model, and the SN11 solution of SAID. The results enable future incorporation of these precise near-threshold data into resonance-region analyses, which should improve understanding of background contributions to resonance amplitudes, and data on F and T asymmetries in the same energy region are currently under analysis.
