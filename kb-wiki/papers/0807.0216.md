# Electro-Weak Interactions in Light Nuclei

> In this thesis, conducted in the field of few--body nuclear physics,I studied low energy inelastic reactions of electro--weak probes on light nuclei. Such interactions hold experimental and theoretical attention in recent years because of the role they play in various fields of physics, stretching from checking the standard model limits; through nuclear structure and dynamics research; up to microscopic interaction in astrophysical phenomena, such as supernova explosion and the nucleosynthesis of the elements. The reactions considered in this work are: (i) Neutrino interaction with A=3 and $^4$He nuclei, in energies typical to core-collapse supernova. For $^4$He both the neutral and charged current reactions were calculated, whereas only neutral current reactions are calculated for A=3 nuclei. (ii) Photoabsorption on $^4$He. This long standing problem has been the attraction of theoretical and experimental efforts in the last three decades. The calculations make use of two novel methods. the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, which is used to transform the scattering problem to a bound state like problem. The resulting equations are solved using the Effective Interaction in the Hyperspherical Harmonics (EIHH) method.

## Key claim

This thesis presents the first *ab-initio* calculations of inelastic neutrino scattering on A=3 and ⁴He nuclei and of ⁴He photoabsorption that include realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon forces (3NF) with full final state interaction, achieving percentage-level numerical accuracy; however, the comparison of the photoabsorption cross-section to experiments is unclear due to experimental discrepancies reaching a factor of 2.

## Method

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method was used to convert the scattering problem into a Schrödinger-like bound state problem, avoiding direct continuum calculations. The resulting equations were solved using the Effective Interaction in the Hyperspherical Harmonics (EIHH) method with the Argonne v18 (AV18) NN potential and Urbana IX (UIX) 3NF. For weak reactions, axial meson exchange currents were calculated using effective field theory (EFT*) calibrated to reproduce the triton half-life, while electromagnetic currents were treated via the Siegert theorem.

## Key numbers

- The 3NF lowers the ⁴He photoabsorption peak by about 6%, much smaller than the 20% effect on the binding energy
- The 3NF effect on photoabsorption grows to about 35% at higher energy transfer
- Meson exchange currents contribute about 20% of the neutrino scattering cross-section on mass-three nuclei at low neutrino temperature, reducing to less than 2% for neutrino temperatures above 5 MeV
- The error due to effective theory cutoff dependence is less than 1% for neutrino temperatures higher than 2 MeV
- The predicted neutrino scattering cross-sections are considered to be of 5% accuracy
- The mean distance between identical nucleons in ⁴He is found to be 6% larger than the mean distance between proton and neutron

## Context

This work extends prior LIT and EIHH calculations, which had been applied to electromagnetic and strong reactions, to weak reactions for the first time. The photoabsorption calculation is the first evaluation of the ⁴He cross-section including realistic NN potential (AV18), 3NF (UIX), and full final state interaction, addressing a long-standing problem that has attracted theoretical and experimental efforts for three decades. The neutrino scattering results provide microscopic input for core-collapse supernova simulations, where inelastic neutrino interactions with ⁴He and trinuclei can affect neutrino signals, shock revival, and chemical composition; the work also builds on the EFT* method previously applied by Park et al. to pp-fusion and hep processes.
