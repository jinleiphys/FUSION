# Jet quenching in hot strongly coupled gauge theories simplified

> Theoretical studies of jet stopping in strongly-coupled QCD-like plasmas have used gauge-gravity duality to find that the maximum stopping distance scales like E^{1/3} for large jet energies E. In recent work studying jets that are created by finite-size sources in the gauge theory, we found an additional scale: the typical (as opposed to maximum) jet stopping distance scales like (EL)^{1/4}, where L is the size of the space-time region where the jet is created. In this paper, we show that the results of our previous, somewhat involved computation in the gravity dual, and the (EL)^{1/4} scale in particular, can be very easily reproduced and understood in terms of the distance that high-energy particles travel in AdS_5-Schwarzschild space before falling into the black brane. We also investigate how stopping distances depend on the conformal dimension of the source operator used to create the jet.

## Key claim

The typical (as opposed to maximum) jet stopping distance scales like \((EL)^{1/4}\) where \(L\) is the size of the space-time region where the jet is created, and this scale can be reproduced and understood from the distance that high-energy particles travel in AdS\(_5\)-Schwarzschild space before falling into the black brane, but only when the geometric optics approximation holds (i.e., for stopping distances much less than \(E^{1/3}\)).

## Method

The authors show that the wave created by a boundary source with approximately well-defined squared 4-momentum \(q^2\) can be approximated as a classical massless particle in AdS\(_5\)-Schwarzschild space. They compute the stopping distance \(\xstop\) by integrating the null geodesic equation from the boundary (\(u=0\)) to the horizon (\(u=1\)), obtaining \(\xstop \simeq (c/2)(E/\del)^{1/4}\) where \(\del = -q^2/(4E)\). They then treat the original finite-size source as a superposition of such narrow-momentum sources and convolve the particle stopping distance with the probability density for the source to produce a jet with a given \(q^2\), reproducing their earlier wave-based result for charge deposition at distances \(\xz \ll E^{1/3}\).

## Key numbers

* The maximum stopping distance scales as \(E^{1/3}\) for large jet energies \(E\).
* The typical stopping distance scales as \((EL)^{1/4}\), where \(L\) is the size of the space-time region where the jet is created.
* For the specific source used, the charge deposition probability for \(\xz \ll E^{1/3}\) is \(\Prob(\xz) \simeq 2 (4 c^4 EL)^2/(2\xz)^9 \, \Psi(-c^4 EL/(2\xz)^4)\), with \(c \equiv \Gamma^2(1/4)/(2\pi)^{1/2}\).
* For \(\xz \gg E^{1/3}\), the distribution falls exponentially: \(\Prob(\xz) \simeq 4 (c_2 L)^2/E \, \Psi(0) \exp(-2 c_1 \xz/E^{1/3})\), with \(c_1 \simeq 0.927\) and \(c_2 \simeq 3.2\).
* The stopping distance for a particle with 4-momentum \(q_\mu\) is \(\xstop \simeq (c/\sqrt{2})(|\q|^2/(-q^2))^{1/4}\), which for the source gives \(\xstop \simeq (c/2)(E/\del)^{1/4}\).
* The geometric optics approximation (necessary for the particle picture) breaks down when \(\xstop \sim E^{1/3}\), corresponding to \(\del \sim E^{-1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Arnold and Vaman, 2011) of jet stopping in strongly-coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma using gauge-gravity duality, where they found the \((EL)^{1/4}\) scale through a more involved computation. It builds on earlier analyses by Gubser et al. (2008) and Chesler et al. (2009) who studied falling string endpoints in AdS-Schwarzschild, but applies the particle trajectory method directly to jet stopping without involving classical strings. The results enable a simpler understanding of the \((EL)^{1/4}\) scale and show how it arises from the typical virtuality \(q^2\) of the source rather than directly from its spatial size \(L\).
