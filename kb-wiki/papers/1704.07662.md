# Nucleon momentum distributions in $^3$He and three-body interactions

> We calculate momentum distributions of neutrons and protons in $^3$He in the framework of a model which includes 3N interactions together with 2N interactions. It is shown that contribution of 3N interactions becomes essential in comparison with contribution coming from 2N interaction for internal momentum in $^3$He $k>$~250~MeV/c. We also compare calculated momentum distribution of protons with so-called empirical momentum distribution of protons extracted from A$(^3\mathrm{He},p)$ breakup cross-sections measured for protons emitted at zero degree. It is concluded that 3N interactions cannot completely explain the disagreement between the available data on the empirical momentum distribution of protons in $^3\mathrm{He}$ and calculations based on 2N interaction, which is observed at high momentum region of the momentum distribution, $k>$~250~MeV/c.

## Key claim

The authors show that three-nucleon (3N) interactions become essential for internal nucleon momenta in $^3$He above about 250 MeV/$c$, but they conclude that 3N interactions cannot completely explain the disagreement between the empirical proton momentum distribution extracted from $A(^3\mathrm{He},p)$ breakup data and calculations based on two-nucleon (2N) interactions alone in the high-momentum region.

## Method

The authors used the operator form of the three-nucleon bound state wave function, expanding scalar functions $\phi_i(p,q,x)$ in Legendre polynomials. They calculated neutron and proton momentum distributions in $^3$He using two potential combinations: the 2N potential AV18 with the 3N potential Urbana-IX (AV18+U9), and the 2N potential CD-Bonn with the 3N potential Tucson-Melbourne (CDBN+TM). The scalar functions were taken from tabulated grids by Fachruddin et al. and interpolated for the calculations. The calculated proton momentum distribution was compared with the empirical momentum distribution extracted from $^{12}$C($^3$He$,p$) breakup cross-section data at a $^3$He momentum of 10.8 GeV/$c$ with protons emitted at $0^\circ$, using light-front dynamics (infinite momentum frame) to relate the theoretical momentum variable to the measured proton momentum.

## Key numbers

- 3N interactions become essential for internal momentum $k > 250$ MeV/$c$ in $^3$He
- At $K \sim 400$ MeV/$c$, the contribution of 3N interactions dominates over that from 2N interactions in the neutron momentum distribution
- The $\phi_3$, $\phi_4$, and $\phi_5$ components contribute only about 0.05% to the normalization of the wave function and were ignored
- For functions $\phi_3$, $\phi_6$, and $\phi_7$, numerical coefficients for the next Legendre term ($P_3(x)$) were approximately $10^{-5}$ to $10^{-6}$ times smaller than the leading coefficients $C_{31}$, $C_{61}$, and $C_{71}$, respectively
- The experimental data were taken at $p_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 10.8$ GeV/$c$
- The AV18+U9 potential strongly overestimates the empirical momentum distribution data at momenta above 600 MeV/$c$
- Non-nucleonic degrees of freedom become important when nucleon constituents are at distances less than 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends calculations by Fachruddin et al. (2000) who provided the scalar functions for the operator form of the 3N wave function, and by Kobushkin and Syamtomov (1995) who calculated momentum distributions without 3N interactions. The authors compare their results with variational calculations by Schiavilla, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1986) using Urbana+U9 and Argonne+U9 interactions. The empirical momentum distribution was extracted from experimental data by Ableev et al. (1988) on $^{12}$C($^3$He$,p$) breakup. The work enables identification of the momentum region ($k > 250$ MeV/$c$) where non-nucleonic degrees of freedom and mechanisms beyond the impulse approximation become important in $^3$He breakup and related nuclear processes.
