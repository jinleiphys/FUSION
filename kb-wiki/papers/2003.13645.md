# Thermal dilepton production in collisional hot QCD medium in the presence of chromo-turbulent fields

> The effects of collisional processes in the hot QCD medium to thermal dilepton production from $q\overline{q}$ annihilation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions have been investigated. The non-equilibrium corrections to the momentum distribution function have been estimated within the framework of ensemble-averaged diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation, encoding the effects of collisional processes and turbulent chromo-fields in the medium. The contributions from the $2\rightarrow2$ elastic scattering processes have been quantified for the thermal dilepton production rate. It is seen that the collisional corrections enhance the equilibrium dilepton spectra at high $p_T$ and suppress at lower $p_T$. A comparative study between collisional and anomalous contributions to the dilepton production rates has also been explored. The collisional contributions are seen to be marginal over that due to collisionless anomalous transport.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how collisional processes (2→2 elastic scattering) in a hot QCD medium modify thermal dilepton production from quark-antiquark annihilation, finding that collisional corrections enhance the equilibrium dilepton spectra at high transverse momentum and suppress it at lower transverse momentum, though these collisional contributions are marginal compared to those from collisionless anomalous transport due to chromo-turbulent fields.

## Method

The authors solved the ensemble-averaged diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation incorporating both a collision kernel for 2→2 elastic scattering processes and a force term representing chromo-turbulent fields, using a variational method to determine the non-equilibrium corrections to the quark and gluon momentum distribution functions. The equilibrium distributions were modeled using the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to incorporate realistic equation-of-state effects from lattice QCD, and the thermal dilepton production rate from q\(\bar{q}\) annihilation was computed within relativistic kinetic theory in the high-temperature limit (M >> T >> m) where Fermi-Dirac distributions were approximated by Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium dilepton production rate is given by \(\frac{dN_0}{d^4x d^4p} = \frac{z_q^2}{2} \frac{M_{eff}^2 g_d^2 \sigma(M_{eff}^2)}{(2\pi)^5} e^{-u \cdot \tilde{p}/T}\), with \(M_{eff}^2 g_d^2 \sigma(M_{eff}^2) = \frac{80\pi}{9}\alpha^2\) for N\(_f\)=2, N\(_c\)=3
- The anomalous contribution coefficient for quarks (without collisions) is \(A_q^a = \frac{20}{9}\frac{C_f N_c T^3}{(N_c^2-1) \hat{q}}\frac{I_5^q}{I_4^q}\), where C\(_f\) = (N\(_c^2\)-1)/(2N\(_c\)) and \(\hat{q}\) is the jet quenching parameter
- The effective coupling is \(\alpha_{eff} = \alpha_s(T) \left(\frac{\frac{2N_c}{\pi^2}I_2^g + \frac{2N_f}{\pi^2}I_2^q}{\frac{N_c}{3} + \frac{N_f}{6}}\right)\), with the 2-loop running coupling \(\alpha_s(T) = \frac{6\pi}{(33-2N_f)\ln(T/\Lambda_T)}\left(1 - \frac{3(153-19N_f)}{(33-2N_f)^2}\frac{\ln(2\ln(T/\Lambda_T))}{\ln(T/\Lambda_T)}\right)\) and \(\Lambda_{MS} = 1.14 T_c\)
- The leading-order correction to single-particle energy from EQPM is less than 10% at T = 2.5 T\(_c\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anomalous transport effects on dilepton production from chromo-Weibel instabilities (Chandra 2016) by adding collisional contributions from 2→2 elastic scattering, following the formalism developed for anomalous and collisional viscosity in hot QCD plasmas (Asakawa 2006, Majumder 2007). The analysis enables more complete modeling of non-equilibrium effects in thermal dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and can be combined with hydrodynamic evolution models incorporating realistic equations of state to compute total dilepton yields.
