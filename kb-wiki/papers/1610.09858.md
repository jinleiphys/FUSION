# Implications of Efimov physics for the description of three and four nucleons in chiral effective field theory

> In chiral effective field theory the leading order (LO) nucleon-nucleon potential includes two contact terms, in the two spin channels $S=0,1$, and the one-pion-exchange potential. When the pion degrees of freedom are integrated out, as in the pionless effective field theory, the LO potential includes two contact terms only. In the three-nucleon system, the pionless theory includes a three-nucleon contact term interaction at LO whereas the chiral effective theory does not. Accordingly arbitrary differences could be observed in the LO description of three- and four-nucleon binding energies. We analyze the two theories at LO and conclude that a three-nucleon contact term is necessary at this order in both theories. In turn this implies that subleading three-nucleon contact terms should be promoted to lower orders. Furthermore this analysis shows that one single low energy constant might be sufficient to explain the large values of the singlet and triplet scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a three-nucleon contact term (the \(c_E\) term) is necessary at leading order (LO) in chiral effective field theory, not just in pionless theory, because the large singlet and triplet scattering lengths place the few-nucleon systems in the regime of Efimov physics where a three-body force is required to stabilize the cutoff dependence; this conclusion is hedged by the observation that the inclusion of the one-pion-exchange potential reduces the spread in the triton energy but not enough to eliminate the need for the three-body term.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for two, three, and four nucleons at LO using a Hamiltonian with a regularized short-range contact interaction (with cutoff \(\Lambda_0 = 1/r_0\)) and the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) regulated by a parameter \(\beta\). They varied \(r_0\) and \(\beta\) to map renormalization-group trajectories of the two-body coupling constants, then computed the triton binding energy \(B(^3\text{H})\) with only two-body forces, and finally included a regularized three-body contact term (strength \(W_0\)) to fix \(B(^3\text{H})\) to its experimental value and predict the \(^4\text{He}\) binding energy and \(nd\) scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length \(a_0 \approx -24\;\text{fm}\) and triplet scattering length \(a_1 \approx 5.4\;\text{fm}\), both large compared to the nuclear range \(r_N \approx 1.4\;\text{fm}\).
- The universal fixed-point coupling constant at the unitary limit is \(C_\infty = 2.379\) (dimensionless), independent of spin channel and regulator \(\beta\).
- Triton binding energy fixed to \(B(^3\text{H}) = -8.48\;\text{MeV}\) when the three-body contact term is included.
- Doublet \(nd\) scattering length \(^2a_{nd} \approx 0.3\div 0.5\;\text{fm}\) (spread across cutoffs), compared to experimental \(0.65 \pm 0.01\;\text{fm}\).
- Quartet \(nd\) scattering length \(^4a_{nd} \approx 6.35\;\text{fm}\) in all cases, in agreement with experiment.
- \(^4\text{He}\) binding energy described within \(10\%\) of the experimental value \(-28.3\;\text{MeV}\) when the three-body term is included; without it, results show an extremely large spread (e.g., lowest value \(\approx -18\;\text{MeV}\) for the shortest range).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Efimov physics and pionless effective field theory (Bedaque et al., 1999) to chiral effective field theory, showing that the same three-body force required at LO in the pionless theory is also needed in ChPT despite the presence of the OPEP tail. The authors argue that the promotion of the \(c_E\) term to LO implies that subleading three-body contact terms (identified at N4LO by Girlanda, 2010) should be promoted to N2LO, increasing the number of low-energy constants from 2 to 12 at that order. This enables a faster convergence of the chiral expansion and may improve the description of spin observables in three-nucleon scattering, where current N3LO calculations (Bochum group) still show problems in \(P\)-wave observables.
