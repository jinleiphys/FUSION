# Shell-model coupled-cluster method for open-shell nuclei

> We present an approach to derive effective shell-model interactions from microscopic nuclear forces. The similarity-transformed coupled-cluster Hamiltonian decouples the single-reference state of a closed-shell nucleus and provides us with a core for the shell model. We use a second similarity transformation to decouple a shell-model space from the excluded space. We show that the three-body terms induced by both similarity transformations are crucial for an accurate computation of ground and excited states. As a proof of principle we use a nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory, employ a $^4$He core, and compute low-lying states of $^{6-8}$He and $^{6-8}$Li in $p$-shell model spaces. Our results agree with benchmarks from full configuration interaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents a shell-model coupled-cluster (SMCC) method that derives effective shell-model interactions from microscopic nuclear forces using two successive similarity transformations, and shows that including three-body terms induced by both transformations is crucial for accurate computation of ground and excited states in open-shell nuclei, with results agreeing with full configuration interaction benchmarks.

## Method

The method starts from a nucleon-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory and uses coupled-cluster theory with singles, doubles, and leading-order triples (CCSDT-1) to similarity-transform the Hamiltonian and decouple a closed-shell core (4He). A second non-Hermitian similarity transformation, inspired by the Magnus formulation of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), decouples a valence space (the p-shell) from the excluded space by solving for the operator S that satisfies Q-exp(-S)-H-bar-exp(S)-P = 0. The authors implement three levels of approximation: SMCC(2b) truncating all intermediates at the normal-ordered two-body level, SMCC(3b-diag) keeping intermediate three-body terms from double commutators, and SMCC(3b) which adds a perturbative treatment of the three-body operator S3 and leading-order three-body valence interactions.

## Key numbers

* CCSD captures approximately 90% of the full correlation energy for closed-shell systems, while CCSDT-1 captures approximately 99%
* The method uses a 4He core and computes low-lying states of 6-8He and 6-8Li in p-shell model spaces
* Results from SMCC(3b) agree with benchmarks from full configuration interaction (no specific numerical deviations provided in the truncated text)

## Context

This work extends the valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) approach of Bogner (2014) and Stroberg (2016, 2017) by implementing the decoupling of core and valence space within the coupled-cluster framework rather than the IMSRG formalism. It also builds on earlier attempts to derive effective interactions from ab-initio methods using Lee-Suzuki-Okamoto techniques with coupled-cluster methods by Jansen (2014, 2016), which were sensitive to details of high-lying excited states. The SMCC method enables systematic inclusion of three-body terms in the effective interaction, which the authors show is necessary for accurate results, and can be consistently applied to observables other than energy such as charge radii.
