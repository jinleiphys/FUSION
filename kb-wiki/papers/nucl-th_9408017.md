# Limits on the Domain of Coupling Constants for Binding N-Body Systems with No Bound Subsystems

> We study the domain of coupling constants for which a 3-body or 4-body system is bound while none of its subsystems is bound. Limits on the size of the domain are derived from a variant of the Hall--Post inequalities which relate $N$-body to $(N-1)$-body energies at given coupling. Possible applications to halo nuclei and hypernuclei are briefly outlined.

## Key claim

The paper derives rigorous lower bounds on the ratio of the critical coupling constants needed to bind an N-body system versus its (N-1)-body subsystems, showing that for three identical bosons the ratio g_3^c / g_2^c cannot be smaller than 2/3 for any short-range potential, and that this bound is not expected to be saturated because the Hall-Post inequalities become equalities only for harmonic oscillators.

## Method

The authors apply a variant of the Hall-Post inequalities, which relate the N-body Hamiltonian to a sum of (N-1)-body Hamiltonians with rescaled masses and couplings, to derive lower bounds on the critical coupling constants for binding. For three identical bosons, they decompose the three-body Hamiltonian into a sum of two-body Hamiltonians with effective mass 3m/2 and use the variational principle to obtain the inequality g_3^c / g_2^c >= 2/3. For the four-boson case, they perform numerical variational calculations using trial wave functions of correlated Gaussian form to obtain the ratio g_4^c / g_2^c. They also extend the method to systems with an infinitely massive source and to asymmetric four-body systems with two different masses.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons interacting via a Yukawa potential, the critical coupling ratio is g_3^c / g_2^c = 0.804, giving a 20% window for halo binding.
- For an exponential potential V(r) = -exp(-r), the ratio is g_3^c / g_2^c = 0.801; for a Gaussian V = -exp(-r^2), the ratio is 0.794.
- The rigorous lower bound derived for any potential is g_3^c / g_2^c >= 2/3 (approximately 0.667).
- For four identical bosons with a Yukawa potential, the numerical result is g_4^c / g_2^c = 0.67, yielding a 13% window for a genuine four-boson halo.
- For two identical particles bound around an infinitely massive source with no mutual interaction (g=0), the window for halo is limited to h > 1/2 + sqrt(3)/4 = 0.93, i.e., at most 7%.

## Context

This work extends the Hall-Post inequalities, previously applied to hadron spectroscopy by Basdevant, Martin, and Richard (1990) and generalized to unequal masses by Basdevant, Martin, Richard, and Wu (1993), to the problem of Borromean binding in nuclear physics. The results provide rigorous limits on the coupling constant domain where halo phenomena such as those seen in 6He or 11Li can occur, and the authors note that the four-body results may be relevant for (n,n,lambda,lambda) hypernuclei with strangeness S=-2, a field of intense theoretical studies reviewed by Gal (1993).
