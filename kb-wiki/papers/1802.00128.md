# Direct photon production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions -- a theory update

> For tomographic studies of relativistic nuclear collisions and of the quark-gluon plasma, photons (real and virtual) are unique. They are the only probes than can be both soft and penetrating. First we report on advances in modelling the hadron dynamics of heavy-ion collisions using a hybrid approach which consists of IP-Glasma, relativistic fluid dynamics, and hadronic cascade components. We briefly discuss the "photon flow puzzle", and then focus on a recent development in the theory of photon emission from a non-equilibrium, strongly interacting medium.

# Direct photon production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions -- a theory update

## Key claim
The "photon flow puzzle" (tension between theoretical calculations and measured photon elliptic flow, especially at RHIC) remains unresolved despite many attempts to improve photon emission rates, and a new discrepancy between STAR and PHENIX direct photon spectra must be resolved before the field can advance. The paper presents a new field-theoretical formalism for photon emission from non-equilibrium media that avoids the equilibrium Kubo-Martin-Schwinger relation, but its phenomenological consequences are left for future work.

## Method
The author reviews a hybrid dynamical model combining IP-Glasma initial conditions, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (with shear and bulk viscosity using the MUSIC code), and hadronic cascade for final-state transport, applied to Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. For photon emission, the paper derives a leading-order real-time finite-temperature field theory formalism for the photon self-energy in a non-equilibrium strongly interacting medium, expressing the emission rate in terms of occupation densities and an integral equation for a four-point function that generalizes the equilibrium LPM resummation.

## Key numbers
- Specific shear viscosity used in the hybrid model: η/s = 0.095, with a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity ζ/s(T), found to be consistent at both LHC energies (2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV)
- The hybrid model reproduces charged hadron integrated flow coefficients v_n (2 ≤ n ≤ 5) for Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV and √s = 5.02 TeV, compared against ALICE data
- Photon emission rate formula (Eq. 1) contains coupling α_EM and involves integration over three-momentum p with occupation densities F(P+K) and [1-F(P)]

## Context
This work extends the hybrid IP-Glasma + hydrodynamics + cascade approach of McDonald et al. (2016) and the viscous photon emission calculations of Paquet et al. (2015), which used kinetic theory formulations. The "photon flow puzzle" was previously identified in comparisons between those calculations and PHENIX and ALICE data. The new non-equilibrium formalism builds on the equilibrium finite-temperature field theory of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) and the real-time formalism of Baier et al. (1997), and enables future calculations of photon emission from anisotropic or pre-equilibrium plasmas without assuming detailed balance. The STAR Collaboration (2016) virtual photon measurements that agree better with theory than PHENIX data are identified as a prerequisite for progress.
