# Pseudorapidity correlations in heavy ion collisions from viscous fluid dynamics

> We demonstrate by explicit calculations in 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic fluid dynamics how two-particle pseudorapidity correlation functions in heavy ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC depend on the number of particle producing sources and the transport properties of the produced medium. In particular, we present results for the Legendre coefficients of the two-particle pseudorapidity correlation function in Pb+Pb collisions at 2760 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV from viscous hydrodynamics with three dimensionally fluctuating initial conditions. Our results suggest that these coefficients provide important constraints on initial state fluctuations and the transport properties of the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The Legendre coefficients of two-particle pseudorapidity correlations in heavy ion collisions can provide constraints on initial state fluctuations and the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, but the authors note that the calculated coefficients for large n,m are much smaller than experimental data, indicating that short-range non-flow correlations have a significant effect that is not captured by the hydrodynamic calculation alone.

## Method

The authors developed a 3D Monte Carlo Glauber model that extends the conventional MC Glauber approach by including fluctuations of valence quarks in rapidity using the Lexus model, producing three-dimensional fluctuating distributions of net baryon number and entropy density. These initial conditions were evolved using the 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic fluid dynamic simulation Music, solving relaxation-type equations for shear and bulk viscous stresses with transport coefficients derived from the Boltzmann equation near the conformal limit. After Cooper-Frye freeze-out at an energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm³ with δf corrections for shear and bulk viscosities and resonance decays, the final hadron spectra were used to compute two-particle pseudorapidity correlation functions and their Legendre coefficients a_{n,m}.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at 2760 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV were simulated
- Quark-quark cross section σ_qq = 3 mb at √s = 200 GeV
- λ = 0.66 (fraction parameter regulating stopping power)
- Hydrodynamic evolution started at τ_0 = 0.38 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12 used for baseline calculations
- Valence quark mass m_q = 0.34 GeV
- Smearing widths: σ_T = 0.4 fm in transverse plane, σ = 0.2 in space-time rapidity
- Freeze-out at energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm³
- p_T integration threshold: p_T > 0.5 GeV
- Pseudorapidity range for analysis: |η_1|,|η_2| < 2.4
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, all √|a_{n,m}| increased by approximately 50% compared to Pb+Pb at 2760 GeV
- For 20-25% central Pb+Pb collisions, calculated a_{n,m} agreed with experimental data for n=m=1 but were smaller for all other n,m
- For 65-70% central Pb+Pb collisions, coefficients from initial distribution already underestimated experimental data

## Context

This work extends the study of Fourier coefficients v_n of azimuthal particle distributions (Gale, 2013) to pseudorapidity correlations, following the ATLAS collaboration's presentation of Legendre expansion results for two-particle pseudorapidity correlations (ATLAS-CONF-2015-020). The authors introduce a simple initial state model that extends the conventional MC Glauber model (Miller, 2007) to include longitudinal fluctuations, building on approaches using UrQMD (Petersen, 2008; Karpenko, 2015), EPOS (Werner, 2010), or AMPT (Pang, 2014) initial conditions. The results enable future comparisons with more sophisticated initial state models such as IP-Glasma (Schenke, 2012; Gale, 2012) and suggest that measuring these coefficients for net-baryon or net-proton distributions could constrain baryon stopping and transport mechanisms. An independent study with similar conclusions appeared simultaneously (Bozek, 2015).
