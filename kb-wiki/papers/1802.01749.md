# Impact of medium modification of the nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors on the neutrino mean free path in dense matter

> Impact of the in-medium modified nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors on the neutrino mean free path in dense matter is studied by considering both the weak and electromagnetic interactions of neutrinos with the constituents of the matter. A relativistic mean field model and the quark-meson coupling model are respectively adopted for the in-medium effective nucleon mass and nucleon form factors. We find that the cross sections of neutrino scattering in cold nuclear medium decreases when the in-medium modification of the nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors are taken into account. This reduction results in the enhancement of the neutrino mean free path, in particular at the baryon density of around a few times of the normal nuclear matter density. The enhancement of the neutrino mean free path is estimated to be about 10--40\% compared with the values obtained without the medium modification of the nucleon form factors, and the enhancement is expected to accelerate the cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of both the nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors reduces the neutrino scattering cross sections in cold nuclear matter, leading to a 10–40% enhancement of the neutrino mean free path at baryon densities around a few times normal nuclear matter density, which is expected to accelerate neutron star cooling.

## Method

The authors adopt a relativistic mean field (RMF) model to describe nuclear matter and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute the in-medium nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors. They calculate the differential and total cross sections for neutrino elastic scattering with protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons in cold, beta-equilibrated matter, then integrate these to obtain the neutrino mean free path as a function of initial neutrino energy at fixed baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of the neutrino mean free path is estimated to be about 10–40% compared with values obtained without medium modification of the nucleon form factors.
- The saturation density of the RMF model is \(\rho_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(E/A = 16.30\ \text{MeV}\).
- The compression modulus is \(K = 219\ \text{MeV}\), consistent with the empirical value \(K = 210 \pm 30\ \text{MeV}\).
- The symmetry energy coefficient is about \(32.5\ \text{MeV}\).
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation is \(M_N^* = 0.78\,M_N\).
- The in-medium modified nucleon form factors were previously found to reduce neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections by about 8% (TKS03) and 12–18% (CCKSKTM13).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino-nucleus scattering that included in-medium modified weak form factors (TKS03; CCKSKTM13) by additionally incorporating the in-medium modification of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The results provide guidance for supernova simulations (MJBHMM15; MMHJ17) and suggest that the enhanced neutrino mean free path could accelerate neutron star cooling, motivating further investigation of in-medium effects on neutrino transport in dense matter.
