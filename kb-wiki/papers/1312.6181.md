# Comment on the Nature of the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ and $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ Mesons

> Two charm-strange mesons, the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$, have recently been observed by several experiments. There has been speculation in the literature that the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is the $2^3S_1(c\bar{s})$ state and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ is the $1^3D_1(c\bar{s})$ state. In this paper we explore this and other explanations in the context of the relativized quark model and the pseudoscalar emission decay model. We conclude that the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is most likely the $1^3D_1 (c\bar{s})$ state and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ is most likely the $1^3D_3 (c\bar{s})$ state with the $1D_2$ resonances also contributing to the observed signals and explaining the observed ratios of branching ratios to $D^*K$ and $DK$ final states. We point out that measuring the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ spin can support or eliminate this explanation and that there are six excited $D_s$ states in this mass region; the $2^3S_1$, $2^1S_0$, $1^3D_1$, $1^3D_3$ and two $1D_2$ states. Observing some of the missing states would help confirm the nature of the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ states.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ is most likely the $1^3D_1(c\bar{s})$ state and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ is most likely the $1^3D_3(c\bar{s})$ state, but they acknowledge that the predicted ratios of partial widths to $D^*K$ and $DK$ final states are in sharp disagreement with the BaBar measurements, and suggest that overlapping $1D_2$ resonances may enhance the $D^*K$ signal.

## Method

The authors used the relativized quark model to predict masses of excited charm-strange mesons and the pseudoscalar emission model to calculate strong decay widths for $2S$ and $1D$ $c\bar{s}$ states. They compared these predictions to the experimentally measured masses, total widths, and branching ratio ratios for the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ and $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ mesons, and also examined the possibility of $2^3S_1$-$1^3D_1$ mixing as a function of mixing angle.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $D_{s1}^*(2710)$: $2709 \pm 4$ MeV (PDG average)
- Total width of $D_{s1}^*(2710)$: $117 \pm 13$ MeV (PDG average)
- Ratio $\Gamma(D_{s1}^* \to D^*K)/\Gamma(D_{s1}^* \to DK) = 0.91 \pm 0.13 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.12 (\text{syst})$
- Mass of $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$: $2863^{+4.0}_{-2.6}$ MeV (PDG average)
- Total width of $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$: $58 \pm 11$ MeV (PDG average)
- Ratio $\Gamma(D_{sJ}^* \to D^*K)/\Gamma(D_{sJ}^* \to DK) = 1.10 \pm 0.15 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.19 (\text{syst})$
- Predicted mass for $2^3S_1(c\bar{s})$: 2732 MeV; predicted total width: 26 MeV; predicted $\Gamma(D^*K)/\Gamma(DK)$ ratio: 0.99
- Predicted mass for $1^3D_1(c\bar{s})$: 2899 MeV; predicted total width (using measured mass 2863 MeV): 145 MeV; predicted $\Gamma(D^*K)/\Gamma(DK)$ ratio: 0.34
- Predicted mass for $1^3D_3(c\bar{s})$: 2917 MeV; predicted total width (using measured mass 2863 MeV): 29 MeV; predicted $\Gamma(D^*K)/\Gamma(DK)$ ratio: 0.42
- Best fit mixing angle for $2^3S_1$-$1^3D_1$ mixing: $\theta \simeq 88^\circ$
- Predicted $1^3D_1$-$1^3D_3$ splitting: 18 MeV; measured splitting: $\sim 154$ MeV
- Predicted $D_s(2^1S_0)$ mass: 2673 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of excited charm-strange mesons by Close and Swanson (2005), Zhang (2006), Zhong (2009), Li (2009), and Yuan (2012), among others, who have variously identified the $D_{s1}^*(2710)$ as the $2^3S_1$ or $1^3D_1$ state and the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ as the $1^3D_1$, $1^3D_3$, or $2^3P_0$ state. The authors use the pseudoscalar emission model as a complementary approach to the $^3P_0$ model used by many previous calculations. The results enable future experimental measurements of the $D_{sJ}^*(2860)$ spin and searches for the four missing excited $D_s$ states (the $2^1S_0$, $2^3S_1$, and two $1D_2$ states) predicted to lie in this mass region, which would help confirm or rule out the proposed assignments.
