# Quantization of T_d- and O_h-symmetric Skyrmions

> The geometrical construction of rational maps using a cubic grid has led to many new Skyrmion solutions, with baryon numbers up to 108. Energy spectra of some of the new Skyrmions are calculated here by semi-classical quantization. Quantization of the B=20 T_d-symmetric Skyrmion, which is one of the newly found Skyrmions, is considered, and this leads to the development of a new approach to solving Finkelstein-Rubinstein (F-R) constraints. Matrix equations are simplified by introducing a Cartesian version of angular momentum basis states, and the computations are easier. The quantum states of all T_d-symmetric Skyrmions, constructed from the cubic grid, are classified into three classes, depending on the contribution of vertex points of the cubic grid to the rational maps. The analysis is extended to the larger symmetry group O_h. Quantum states of O_h-symmetric Skyrmions, constructed from the cubic grid, form a subset of the T_d-symmetric quantum states.

## Key claim
The quantum states of all \(T_d\)-symmetric Skyrmions constructed from the cubic grid are classified into three classes depending on the contribution of vertex points of the cubic grid to the rational maps, and the analysis extends to the larger symmetry group \(O_h\) where the quantum states form a subset of the \(T_d\)-symmetric quantum states. The semi-classical quantization of the \(B=20\) \(T_d\)-symmetric Skyrmion leads to a new approach for solving Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints using a Cartesian version of angular momentum basis states, which simplifies computations compared to the angular momentum basis method that becomes computationally intractable for spin greater than 4.

## Method
The authors perform semi-classical quantization of Skyrmions by treating them as rigid bodies free to rotate in ordinary space and isospace, parametrized by \(SU(2)\) matrices for spatial and isospatial rotations. They calculate the moment of inertia tensors \(U_{ij}\), \(V_{ij}\), and \(W_{ij}\) numerically for the \(B=20\) \(T_d\)-symmetric Skyrmion, which is constructed using a double-layer rational map ansatz derived from the cubic grid method. The Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints are solved using a new Cartesian method that expresses quantum states as \(T\)-invariant polynomials in Cartesian coordinates \((x,y,z)\) for ordinary space and \((X,Y,Z)\) for isospace, rather than using the standard angular momentum basis with Euler angles. The parity operator is constructed from the rational map's behavior under combined inversion in the complex coordinate and isospace coordinate.

## Key numbers
- The \(B=20\) \(T_d\)-symmetric Skyrmion has an inner rational map of degree 4 constructed from all eight vertices of the first layer of the cubic grid, expressed using quartic Klein polynomials \(p_+(\mathrm{z}) = \mathrm{z}^4 + 2\sqrt{3}i \mathrm{z}^2 + 1\) and \(p_-(\mathrm{z}) = \mathrm{z}^4 - 2\sqrt{3}i \mathrm{z}^2 + 1\).
- The outer rational map for the \(B=20\) Skyrmion has degree 16 and is given by \(R_{20,T_d} = \left( \frac{1+c_2}{1+c_1} \right) \frac{p_+}{p_-} \left(\frac{c_1 p_+^3 + p_-^3}{p_+^3 + c_2 p_-^3}\right)\) with constants \(c_1 = -2.873\) and \(c_2 = 0.178\).
- The energy conversion factor used is \(e^3 F_\pi = 4 \times 10^3\) MeV, where one Skyrme unit of moment of inertia converts to \(\frac{1}{e^3 F_\pi}\) in physical units.
- The ratio of excitation energies between the \(4^+\) and \(3^-\) rotational states is \(\frac{5}{3} \sim 1.67\); for \(^{16}\)O the lowest \(3^-\) and \(4^+\) states have energies 6.130 MeV and 10.356 MeV respectively, giving a ratio of 1.69.
- The first three layers of the cubic grid allow maximum numbers of 8, 56, and 152 points, leading to rational maps of maximum degree 4, 28, and 76 respectively when restricting to zeros and poles of multiplicity one.

## Context
This work extends the geometrical construction of rational maps using a cubic grid developed by Feist, Lau, and Manton (2013), which produced many new Skyrmion solutions with baryon numbers up to 108. The quantization method builds on the semi-classical Skyrmion quantization framework of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and Biedenharn and Chen (1989), and applies Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints (1968) to tetrahedrally-symmetric Skyrmions. The results enable identification of potential tetrahedral and cubic nuclei in experimental spectra, following theoretical studies of tetrahedral nuclei by Dudek et al. (2002) and Dudek, Curien, Dobrowolski, and Olbratowski (2006), and support the observation of \(3^-\) and \(4^+\) states in \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca as signatures of tetrahedral symmetry.
