# Hydrodynamization in systems with detailed transverse profiles

> The observation of fluid-like behavior in nucleus-nucleus, proton-nucleus and high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions motivates systematic studies of how different measurements approach their fluid-dynamic limit. We have developed numerical methods to solve the ultra-relativistic Boltzmann equation for systems of arbitrary size and transverse geometry. Here, we apply these techniques for the first time to the study of azimuthal flow coefficients $v_n$ including non-linear mode-mode coupling and to an initial condition with realistic event-by-event fluctuations. We show how both linear and non-linear response coefficients extracted from $v_n$ develop as a function of opacity from free streaming to perfect fluidity. We note in particular that away from the fluid-dynamic limit, the signal strength of linear and non-linear response coefficients does not reduce uniformly, but that their hierarchy and relative size shows characteristic differences.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that away from the fluid-dynamic limit, the signal strength of linear and non-linear response coefficients extracted from azimuthal flow coefficients does not reduce uniformly, but that their hierarchy and relative size shows characteristic differences as a function of opacity.

## Method

The authors solved the ultra-relativistic Boltzmann equation in the isotropization-time approximation for massless, boost-invariant systems with arbitrary transverse geometry. They initialized the kinetic theory with both a simple Gaussian ansatz for studying linear and non-linear response coefficients and with realistic event-by-event fluctuations from the T_RENTo model. The opacity parameter \(\hat\gamma = \gamma R^{3/4} (\varepsilon_0\tau_0)^{1/4}\) was used as the unique model parameter interpolating between free-streaming (\(\hat\gamma\to 0\)) and ideal fluid dynamics (\(\hat\gamma \to \infty\)).

## Key numbers

- Linear response coefficients \(w_{2,2}\) extracted from the Gaussian profile: 0.166, 0.266, 0.327, and 0.372 for opacities \(\hat\gamma = 1, 2, 3, 4\) respectively
- Linear response coefficients \(w_{2,2}^{\rm (TrENTo)}\) extracted from the T_RENTo profile: 0.156, 0.239, 0.288, and 0.319 for the same opacities \(\hat\gamma = 1, 2, 3, 4\)
- The dependence on the radial profile in linear response coefficients ranges from 5% to 15% in the \(\hat\gamma\)-range studied
- The analogous study of \(w_{3,3}\) shows a 2% to 10% difference between Gaussian and T_RENTo profiles in the same \(\hat\gamma\)-range
- Non-linearities generated by the lowest harmonics \(n\leq 3\) account for half of all the non-linearities

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hydrodynamization that were mostly limited to simplified \(1+1\)D systems assuming exact Bjorken boost invariance (Kurkela 2015, Keegan 2015, Heller 2013, Romatschke 2017). The authors note that only linear response coefficients had been studied in full kinetic theory previously (Kurkela 2018, Kurkela 2019), and that a perturbative analysis in the dilute limit (Borghini 2018) had established scaling of response coefficients with the average number of rescatterings. The paper enables future studies of the evolution of event samples and Boltzmann equations with other phenomenologically relevant complications, pushing the study of hydrodynamization from simplified settings to realistic geometries.
