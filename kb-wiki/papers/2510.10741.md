# Cluster and Halo Structures of Light Nuclei within the NUCLEI-PACK Framework

> As part of the ongoing NUCLEI-PACK project, this study presents a semi-classical framework for exploring the microscopic geometry of light and exotic nuclei based on optimized sphere packing of nucleons and clusters. Starting from explicit nucleon coordinates generated by the packing algorithm, the model provides direct access to charge, matter, and core--valence radii, allowing quantitative analysis of clustering and halo formation. The study covers one-nucleon halo nuclei ($^{11}$Be, $^{15}$C, $^{19}$C, and $^{8}$B) and two-nucleon halo systems ($^{6}$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{19}$B, and $^{17}$Ne). For the halo systems, the fitted geometric offset parameter $Δ$ exhibits an inverse correlation with the nucleon separation energy, reflecting the increasing spatial decoupling between the core and valence nucleons in weakly bound configurations. The framework also reproduces characteristic neutron--neutron separations and opening angles in Borromean nuclei and qualitatively captures the geometric arrangement of $α$ clusters ($^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, and $^{12}$C). These results demonstrate that a simple geometric framework can effectively capture the essential features of both halo and cluster structures, providing an intuitive and computationally efficient link between nuclear geometry, binding, and experimentally observed radii.

## Key claim
The NUCLEI-PACK semi-classical sphere-packing framework can reproduce the essential geometric features of both halo and cluster structures in light nuclei, but only after introducing a fitted geometric offset parameter $\Delta$ that acts as an effective amplitude of spatial fluctuation to account for the weak binding of valence nucleons.

## Method
The study uses a semi-classical model where nucleons are treated as hard spheres with distinct radii ($r_p^{\mathrm{Pack}} = 0.89$ fm, $r_n^{\mathrm{Pack}} = 1.06$ fm) and assembled into nuclei using optimized sphere-packing algorithms. For halo nuclei, the system is decomposed into a core and one or two valence clusters, which are placed in surface contact and then shifted outward by a fitted offset parameter $\Delta$ to reproduce experimental radii. The model calculates charge, matter, and core-valence radii from the explicit nucleon coordinates, including corrections for intrinsic nucleon charge radii and Gaussian smearing.

## Key numbers
*   **$^{11}$Be:** With a fitted offset of $\Delta = 1.6$ fm, the model yields a matter radius of 2.86 fm (experimental range: 2.73-2.91 fm) and a core-valence distance of 5.69 fm (experimental range: 5.7-5.77 fm).
*   **$^{15}$C:** A satisfactory description of the matter radius (2.67 fm) is obtained without any offset ($\Delta = 0$), consistent with the experimental range of 2.61-2.78 fm.
*   **$^{19}$C:** A finite offset of $\Delta = 0.6$ fm is required to reproduce the matter radius of 3.01 fm, aligning with experimental measurements of 3.01-3.13 fm.
*   **$^{8}$B:** A larger offset of $\Delta = 1.2$ fm is required to match the observed charge radius of 2.76 fm (experimental: 2.89(9) fm) and matter radius of 2.62 fm (experimental: 2.58(6) fm).
*   **$^{6}$He:** With a fitted offset of $\Delta = 0.6$ fm, the model yields a matter radius of 2.68 fm (experimental: 2.48(3) fm and 2.67 fm), a core-valence distance of 3.90 fm (experimental: 3.36(9) fm and 3.9(2) fm), and a neutron-neutron separation of 3.32 fm (experimental: 4.1(7) fm and 3.75(93) fm).

## Context
This work extends the NUCLEI-PACK framework, previously established by Maridi (2025) for global nuclear radii and binding energies, to exotic light nuclei. It follows a lineage of geometric approaches including Pauling’s close-packed spheron theory and recent packing arguments by Zaccone (2022) and Anzivino (2024). The framework provides a computationally efficient, intuitive geometric link between nuclear structure and observables, serving as a complementary tool to ab initio theories and experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities.
