# Two-neutrino $ββ$ decay to excited states at next-to-leading order

> We study two-neutrino double-beta decay ($2νββ$) into first-excited $0^+_2$ states of nuclei used in $ββ$ decay experiments, including $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe. We calculate the corresponding nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) within the nuclear shell model, using various Hamiltonians that describe well the spectroscopy of the initial and final nuclei. We evaluate the next-to-leading order (NLO) long-range NMEs recently introduced within chiral effective field theory, keeping three terms in the expansion of the energy denominator. In most cases, NLO contributions to the half-life are below 5%, but they can significantly increase due to cancellations in the leading-order Gamow-Teller NME. A detailed analysis in terms of nuclear deformation, including triaxiality, indicates that larger deformation differences between the initial and final states generally lead to smaller NMEs, but the seniority structure of the states also plays a relevant role. The lower range of our predicted half-lives, with uncertainties dominated by the nuclear Hamiltonian used, are slightly longer than the current experimental limit in $^{76}$Ge and consistent with the very recent half-life indication in $^{82}$Se.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral effective field theory contributions to two-neutrino double-beta decay half-lives to excited states are generally below 5%, but can become significantly larger when cancellations occur in the leading-order Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix element, particularly in cases where the leading-order NME is suppressed.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations for two-neutrino double-beta decay to first-excited 0⁺₂ states in ⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹³⁰Te, and ¹³⁶Xe, using multiple Hamiltonians that reproduce the spectroscopy of initial and final nuclei. They evaluated next-to-leading order long-range nuclear matrix elements from chiral effective field theory, keeping three terms in the expansion of the energy denominator (M⁽⁻¹⁾_GT, M⁽⁻³⁾_GT, and M⁽⁻⁵⁾_GT), and computed phase-space factors by solving the Dirac equation numerically with the RADIAL code. The calculations used both bare Gamow-Teller operators (with phenomenological quenching factors derived from ground-state-to-ground-state decays) and effective Gamow-Teller operators renormalized within the shell-model valence space.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷⁶Ge (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂): half-lives range from 1.99 × 10²³ yr (GCN2850, bare GT) to 7.16 × 10²⁵ yr (RG, bare GT); NLO contributions are 1.3–4.1% for bare GT
- For ⁸²Se (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂): half-lives range from 7.44 × 10²² yr (JJ4BB, bare GT) to 1.80 × 10²⁵ yr (JUN45, effective GT); NLO contributions are 0.07–0.5% for bare GT
- For ¹³⁶Xe (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂): half-lives range from 1.70 × 10²⁶ yr (GCN5082, bare GT) to 2.2 × 10²⁸ yr (QX, effective GT); NLO contributions reach -18.9% (GCN5082, bare GT) and -142% (QX, effective GT)
- For ¹³⁰Te (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂): half-lives range from 1.04 × 10²⁶ yr (GCN5082, effective GT) to 5.52 × 10²⁶ yr (QX, effective GT); NLO contributions are 0.7–1.2%
- For ⁴⁸Ca (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂): half-lives range from 6.5 × 10²³ yr (KB3G, effective GT) to 1.66 × 10²⁴ yr (GXPF1A, bare GT); NLO contributions are 1.1–1.9%
- The Q-value for ⁷⁶Ge (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂) is 0.9168 MeV; for ⁸²Se (0⁺_gs → 0⁺₂) it is 1.5102 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous nuclear shell model studies of two-neutrino double-beta decay to excited states in ⁴⁸Ca (PhysRevC.87.014320), ⁷⁶Ge (gerda_collaboration_2_2015), ¹²⁴Sn (horoi_shell_2016), and ¹³⁶Xe (Jokiniemi:2022yfr), and calculations with renormalized operators including two-nucleon currents (Coraggio24). It is the first evaluation of next-to-leading order chiral effective field theory terms for these decays, following the formalism of morabit20242nubetabetaspectrumchiraleffective. The results enable comparison with recent experimental indications for ⁸²Se (Barabash:2025bxa) and existing limits for ⁷⁶Ge (Arnquist24), and suggest that near-term detection may be feasible for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se but unlikely for ¹³⁰Te and ¹³⁶Xe.
