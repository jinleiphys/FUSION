# Probing Saturon-like Limits in QCD Systems

> High-occupancy QCD matter enters a saturated regime when its entropy or occupancy approaches the unitarity bound $\sim 1/α$, the ``saturon" criterion. We test this criterion for protons and nuclei at small $x$ using analytic and numerical solutions of the BK equation. From these solutions we construct the gluon occupancy $N_g(x)$ and a thermodynamic entropy $S(x)$ via an Unruh-like temperature $T = Q_s/(2π)$ and an emergent gluon mass $M_g \sim Q_s$. For protons, both $N_g$ and $S$ rise toward small $x$ yet stay below $1/α_s$ in our baseline setup. For nuclei, by contrast, the nuclear entropy $S_A$ attains the $1/α_s$ benchmark in a small-$x$ window where the proton does not. This singles out nuclei as the natural environment to search for saturon-like behavior and motivates precision small-$x$ measurements and high-occupancy $pA$ and $AA$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether protons and nuclei at small Bjorken-x can reach the "saturon" criterion, where entropy or occupancy approaches the unitarity bound of approximately 1/alpha_s. The authors find that protons stay below this bound in their baseline setup, while nuclear entropy attains the 1/alpha_s benchmark in a small-x window, singling out nuclei as the natural environment to search for saturon-like behavior.

## Method

The authors solved the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation using both analytic solutions obtained via the homogeneous balance method and numerical solutions from the BKsolver algorithm, for fixed and running coupling scenarios. From these solutions they extracted the saturation scale Q_s and constructed the gluon occupancy N_g(x) and a thermodynamic entropy S(x) using an Unruh-like temperature T = Q_s/(2pi) and an emergent gluon mass M_g ~ Q_s, following the thermodynamic entropy definition proposed by Kutak (2011). For nuclear targets, they extended the unintegrated gluon distribution from a single proton to a nucleus using the Glauber-Gribov-Mueller approximation with nuclear saturation scale Q_{s,A}^2 ~ A^{1/3} Q_{s,p}^2.

## Key numbers

- For protons, both N_g and S rise toward small x yet stay below the saturon bound of 1/alpha_s in the baseline setup.
- For nuclei, the nuclear entropy S_A attains the 1/alpha_s benchmark in a small-x window where the proton does not.
- The saturation scale for nuclei scales as Q_{s,A}^2 ~ A^{1/3} Q_{s,p}^2, with nuclear radius R_A = r_0 A^{1/3}.
- The infrared cutoff Lambda_QCD is taken as 0.2 GeV.
- For the fixed coupling analytic solution, parameters used are A_0=33.3, A_1=-58.3, A_2=26.2, and theta=-3.09.
- For the running coupling analytic solution, parameters fitted to structure function data are A_0=6.98, A_1=-8.46, A_2=2.95, c=3.11, and theta=-2.76.

## Context

This work extends the saturon concept introduced by Dvali et al. (2019-2021) to QCD systems, connecting it to the Color Glass Condensate effective theory and the BK equation framework. It builds on the thermodynamic entropy definition for gluonic systems proposed by Kutak (2011) and the Unruh-temperature approach from Kharzeev (2005) and Castorina (2007). The results motivate precision small-x measurements and high-occupancy pA and AA collisions to search for saturon-like behavior, and suggest a potential correspondence between black hole saturation and saturated gluon states in QCD.
