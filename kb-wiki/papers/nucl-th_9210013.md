# Effects of Octupole Vibrations on Quasiparticle Modes of Excitation in Superdeformed $^{193}$Hg

> A particle-vibration coupling calculation based on the RPA and the cranked shell model has been carried out for superdeformed rotational bands in $^{193}$Hg. The result suggests that properties of single-particle motions in superdeformed nuclei may be significantly affected by coupling effects with low-frequency octupole vibrational modes, especially by the lowest $K=2$ octupole mode.

# Effects of Octupole Vibrations on Quasiparticle Modes of Excitation in Superdeformed $^{193}$Hg

## Key claim

The coupling between quasiparticle motions and low-frequency octupole vibrational modes, particularly the lowest $K=2$ octupole mode, significantly affects the properties of single-particle motions in superdeformed $^{193}$Hg. The calculation suggests that including these coupling effects is necessary to reproduce the experimental crossing frequency between bands 1 and 4 and the aligned angular momentum of band 4, but the calculated interaction matrix element between these bands remains too small compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed a particle-vibration coupling calculation combining the random phase approximation (RPA) with the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky-BCS procedure for superdeformed rotational bands in $^{193}$Hg. They solved RPA equations using doubly-stretched octupole operators in a configuration space of 9 major shells, then derived an effective Hamiltonian describing systems of quasiparticles and octupole vibrations, which was diagonalized within the subspace of one-quasiparticle states and one-quasiparticle-plus-one-octupole-vibration states.

## Key numbers

- Energy shifts $\Delta e'_{\rm vib}$ of 50 to 300 keV due to octupole-vibrational coupling effects are seen in the quasiparticle energy diagram.
- The Landau-Zener crossing frequency between band 1 and band 4 is $\omega_{\rm cross} \approx 0.26$ MeV/$\hbar$ with octupole effects included, in agreement with the experimental value $\omega_{\rm rot}^{\rm exp} \approx 0.27$ MeV, compared to $\omega_{\rm cross} \approx 0.17$ MeV/$\hbar$ when octupole-vibrational effects are neglected.
- The calculated alignment for the [761]3/2 quasiparticle state (band 4) is reduced from $i^{\rm cal} \approx 1.8\hbar$ to $i^{\rm cal} \approx 1.2\hbar$ when octupole-vibrational effects are included, in good agreement with the experimental value $i_{\rm band4}^{\rm exp} \approx 1.3\hbar$.
- The interaction matrix element between bands 1 and 4 increases from almost zero to about 5 keV due to octupole-vibrational effects, but this remains too small compared to the experimental value $V_{\rm int}^{\rm exp} \approx 26$ keV.
- Using a larger pairing gap $\Delta = 0.9$ MeV gives $V_{\rm int} \approx 10$ keV, still too small compared to experiment.
- The octupole force strengths used are $\chi_{3K} = 1.08\chi_{3K}^{\rm HO}$, corresponding to the lowest $K=2$ octupole vibration excitation energy $\hbar\omega_{K=2}^{(-)} \approx 0.5$ MeV at $\omega_{\rm rot} = 0.45$ MeV/$\hbar$.
- The pairing gaps adopted are $\Delta_n = \Delta_p = 0.7$ MeV, with the deformation parameter $\delta_{\rm osc} = 0.43$.

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations by Mizutori, Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1990-1991) and Nakatsukasa, Mizutori and Matsuyanagi (1992) that predicted highly collective low-frequency octupole vibrational modes about the superdeformed equilibrium shape. The importance of octupole correlations in superdeformed high-spin states had also been discussed by Dudek, Werner and Szymanski (1990), Aberg (1990), Holler and Aberg (1990), Chasman (1991), Bonche et al. (1991), Li, Dudek and Romain (1991), Nazarewicz and Dobaczewski (1992), Skalski (1992), Piepenbring (1992), Nazmitdinov and Aberg (1992), and Skalski et al. (1992). The calculation is motivated by experimental data from Cullen et al. (1990) on Landau-Zener band-crossing phenomena in $^{193}$Hg. The results enable future work on improving the treatment of pairing correlations, inclusion of quadrupole-pairing, evaluation of $E1$ transition probabilities, and consideration of alternative interpretations of the experimental data as suggested by Semmes, Ragnarsson and Aberg (1992).
