# A shear spectral sum rule in a non-conformal gravity dual

> A sum rule which relates a stress-energy tensor correlator to thermodynamic functions is examined within the context of a simple non-conformal gravity dual. Such a sum rule was previously derived using AdS/CFT for conformal $\mathcal{N} = 4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, but we show that it does not generalize to the non-conformal theory under consideration. We provide a generalized sum rule and numerically verify its validity. A useful byproduct of the calculation is the computation of the spectral density in a strongly coupled non-conformal theory. Qualitative features of the spectral densities and implications for lattice measurements of transport coefficients are discussed.

## Key claim

The sum rule previously derived for conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, which relates an integral over the shear spectral density to the energy density, does not generalize to non-conformal theories; the authors show that the left and right sides of the original sum rule disagree for a non-conformal gravity dual, and they provide a corrected sum rule that includes an additional function \(F(\varepsilon,P,v_s)\) which vanishes only when the conformal conditions \(\varepsilon=3P\) and \(v_s^2=1/3\) are satisfied.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to study a 5D single scalar model with an exponential potential (the Chamblin-Reall model), which provides a non-conformal gravity dual with a constant speed of sound. They numerically solve the linearized Einstein equations for tensor mode perturbations at vanishing spatial momentum to compute the finite-temperature shear spectral density \(\rho^{\rm shear}(w)\), subtract the analytically known zero-temperature spectral density, and numerically integrate the result to evaluate the right-hand side of the sum rule. The left-hand side is computed from the known thermodynamics of the background, and the disagreement between the two sides is used to derive the corrected sum rule, which is then verified numerically.

## Key numbers

* For \(\delta=0.0\) (conformal case), the left-hand side of the original sum rule is \(0.300...\) and the right-hand side is \(0.300...\) (both in units of \(Ts\)), showing agreement.
* For \(\delta=0.1\), the left-hand side is \(0.318...\) and the right-hand side is \(0.326...\) (in units of \(Ts\)), showing disagreement.
* For \(\delta=0.2\), the left-hand side is \(0.333...\) and the right-hand side is \(0.357...\) (in units of \(Ts\)), showing disagreement.
* For \(\delta=0.3\), the left-hand side is \(0.353...\) and the right-hand side is \(0.395...\) (in units of \(Ts\)), showing disagreement exceeding 10\%.
* The shear viscosity takes the universal value \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) in this model, confirmed by the small-\(w\) slope of the spectral density.

## Context

This work extends the sum rule analysis of Romatschke and Son (2009), who derived the original shear spectral sum rule for conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory using AdS/CFT and claimed it should hold for any Einstein gravity dual. The authors show this claim is incorrect by demonstrating substantial disagreement in the Chamblin-Reall model, a simple non-conformal gravity dual previously studied by Chamblin and Reall (1999) and others. The corrected sum rule and the computed spectral densities enable future lattice measurements of transport coefficients, particularly suggesting that the bulk viscosity (related to \(\delta\) in this model) could potentially be extracted from the tensor correlation function rather than only from the bulk channel.
