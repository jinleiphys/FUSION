# Mini-Proceedings, 18th meeting of the Working Group on Radiative Corrections and MC Generators for Low Energies

> The mini-proceedings of the 18$^{\mathrm{th}}$ Meeting of the "Working Group on Radiative Corrections and MonteCarlo Generators for Low Energies" held in Frascati, 19$^{\mathrm{th}}$ - 20$^{\mathrm{st}}$ May, are presented. These meetings, started in 2006, have as aim to bring together experimentalists and theoreticians working in the fields of meson transition form factors, hadronic contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the leptons, and the effective fine structure constant. The development of MonteCarlo generators and Radiative Corrections for precision $e^+e^-$ and $τ$-lepton physics are also covered, with emphasis on meson production. At this workshop, a documentary entitled {\it Bruno Touschek with AdA in Orsay} commemorating the first observation of electron-positron collisions in a laboratory was also presented. With this edition, the working group reaches 10 years of continuous activities.

## Key claim

The 18th meeting of the Radio MonteCarLow Working Group reviewed the status and future prospects of Monte Carlo generators and radiative corrections for low-energy precision physics, with the aim of improving the Standard Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment and the effective fine structure constant through continued collaboration between experimentalists and theoreticians.

## Method

The workshop brought together seven subgroups covering luminosity, R-measurement, initial-state radiation, hadronic vacuum polarization for g-2 and Delta alpha, gamma-gamma physics, final-state radiation models, and tau decays. Participants reviewed the latest achievements in Monte Carlo generator accuracy for e+e- into leptonic, gamma-gamma, hadronic, and tau final states, discussed new results from CMD-3, and examined proposals for evaluating hadronic light-by-light scattering contributions to the muon g-2 using data-driven dispersive approaches.

## Context

This workshop continues the Radio MonteCarLow series started in 2006, which produced a major summary report in 2010 (Actis et al., 2010) and has held 17 previous meetings documented in proceedings from 2013-2015. The meeting extends work on meson transition form factors crucial for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, where a 3-4 sigma discrepancy between the Standard Model prediction and the experimental value persists. The data-driven dispersive approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering proposed by Colangelo et al. (2014) and Pauk and Vanderhaeghen (2014) is being developed to reduce model dependence, with the goal of reaching 10% precision on the pseudoscalar-pole contributions to enable more stringent tests of the Standard Model in light of upcoming g-2 experiments at Fermilab and J-PARC.
