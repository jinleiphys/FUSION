# Hadron resonances generated from the dynamics of the lightest scalar ones

> We have studied the interactions of the scalar resonances f_0(980) and a_0(980) with the vector resonance ϕ(1020) and with the lightest pseudoscalars π, K, ηand η'. We first obtain the interaction kernels without including any new free parameter. Afterwards, the interaction kernels are unitarized and the final S-wave amplitudes result. We find that these interactions are very rich and generate a large amount of pseudoscalar resonances including the K(1460), π(1300), π(1800), η(1475) and X(1835) resonances. The f_0(980)ϕ(1020) self-interactions give rise to the ϕ(2170) resonance. For realistic choices of the parameters we also obtain an isovector companion in the same mass region from the a_0(980) ϕ(1020) interactions.

## Key claim
The interactions of the scalar resonances f0(980) and a0(980) with light pseudoscalars and the vector φ(1020), calculated without new free parameters and unitarized, generate many observed pseudoscalar resonances and the φ(2170); however, the generation of an isovector companion of the φ(2170) depends on the specific choice of the a0(980) pole parameters.

## Method
The authors computed interaction kernels for scattering between scalar resonances (f0(980), a0(980)) and pseudoscalars (π, K, η, η') using a triangular-loop diagram where the scalar decays into a virtual K¯K pair. The kaon-pseudoscalar scattering amplitude in the loop was taken from previously determined meson-meson amplitudes that contain the scalar resonances as dynamically generated poles. The kernels were then unitarized via the N/D method to obtain final S-wave amplitudes. For φ(1020) interactions, the φ→K¯K vertex was obtained from lowest-order chiral Lagrangians coupled to vector mesons.

## Key numbers
- The f0(980) and a0(980) masses are very close to the K¯K threshold (approximately 990 MeV), which enhances the triangular-loop diagram.
- For the φ(2170) (also called Y(2175)), a combined fit to BABAR and Belle data from Shen (2009) gave M_Y = 2117 +0.59/-0.49 MeV and Γ_Y = 164 +69/-80 MeV.
- The X(1835) resonance is generated from the f0η' channel for a subtraction constant a1 ≈ -1.2.
- The η(1475) resonance appears in the f0η amplitude for |a1| ≥ 0.8.
- The f0(980) mass was taken as either 0.98 GeV or 0.99 GeV in fits to the e+e- → φ(1020)f0(980) cross section.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical generation of resonances from chiral symmetry, where the lightest scalar nonet (f0(980), a0(980), κ, σ) was previously generated from pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar interactions by Oller (1998) and others. It challenges the classification of excited pseudoscalars above 1 GeV, particularly the interpretation of η(1405) as a glueball (favored by Chanowitz (2005) and Close (1997)) which conflicts with lattice QCD predictions by Bali (2000) of a pseudoscalar glueball around 2.4 GeV. The work also supports the conclusion from the authors' prior study (fif0, 2009) that φ(2170) has a large φ(1020)f0(980) component, and enables searches for an isovector partner of φ(2170) via a0(980)φ(1020) interactions.
