# Indirect Detection of Composite (Asymmetric) Dark Matter

> Dark Matter can form bound states upon the emission of quanta of energy equal to the binding energy. The rate of this process is large for strongly-interacting Dark Matter, and further enhanced by long distance effects. The resulting monochromatic and diffuse $γ$-rays can be tested in indirect detection experiments. If Dark Matter has electroweak charge, indirect signals include multiple observable photon lines for masses in the TeV range. Else if it couples only via a dark photon portal, diffuse spectra from dwarf galaxies and CMB reionization set powerful limits for masses below a TeV. This mechanism provides a powerful means of probing Asymmetric Dark Matter today.

## Key claim

Dark matter that forms bound states (dark nuclei) through emission of gauge bosons can produce observable gamma-ray signals in indirect detection experiments, even for asymmetric dark matter which has no annihilation signal today. The cross section for this process is calculable semi-analytically in the limit of shallow bound states and can be large enough to be probed by existing experiments, though the actual signal strength depends sensitively on binding energies and long-distance effects.

## Method

The authors compute bound state formation cross sections for dark matter nucleons forming dark deuterium through magnetic dipole transitions, using the radial Schroedinger equation with a spherical well nuclear potential and long-range electroweak or dark photon interactions. They solve the Schroedinger equation numerically to obtain wave-functions and compute cross sections for two scenarios: composite SU(2)-triplet dark matter with electroweak charges emitting SM photons, and composite SM-singlet dark matter with a dark photon portal emitting dark photons that decay to SM particles. Experimental constraints from FERMI, HESS, CMB reionization (PLANCK), and dwarf spheroidal galaxies are recast to bound the bound state formation cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For composite SU(2)-triplet DM: bound state formation cross section peaks near DM masses of 3-4 TeV, where the signal is just within FERMI sensitivity; the electroweak mass splitting between charged and neutral components is Delta = 165 MeV
- For composite SM-singlet DM with dark photon: CMB bound translates to <sigma_{D gamma*} v_rel>_CMB < (8.2 x 10^{-28} cm^3/s) / f_eff x (M^2/E_B^2) x (E_B/GeV), with f_eff approximately 0.5
- For SM-singlet DM, binding energies must satisfy E_B/M less than about 10^{-3} (M/GeV)^{6/5} (0.001/alpha_D)^{2/5} to satisfy CMB bounds
- Collider bounds from dark pion production require M greater than about 1 TeV for the triplet scenario

## Context

This work extends the study of dark matter bound state formation from Redi (2018) to observational implications, building on the analogy with Standard Model deuterium formation (Bethe and Longmire, 1950; Kaplan, 1998). It provides a mechanism to test asymmetric dark matter models (reviewed in the literature) that otherwise lack indirect detection signals. The results enable future searches for multiple monochromatic gamma-ray lines as a smoking gun signature of bound state formation, distinguishable from dark matter annihilation signals, and motivate lattice calculations of binding energies in strongly-coupled SU(N) gauge theories.
