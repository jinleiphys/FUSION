# Bethe-Salpeter Meson Masses Beyond Ladder Approximation

> The effect of quark-gluon vertex dressing on the ground state masses of the u/d-quark pseudoscalar, vector and axialvector mesons is considered with the Dyson-Schwinger equations. This extends the ladder-rainbow Bethe-Salpeter kernel to 2-loops. To render the calculations feasible for this exploratory study, we employ a simple infrared dominant model for the gluon exchange that implements the vertex dressing. The resulting model, involving two distinct representations of the effective gluon exchange kernel, preserves both the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity and charge conjugation symmetry. Numerical results confirm that the pseudoscalar meson retains its Goldstone boson character. The vector meson mass, already at a very acceptable value at ladder level, receives only 30 MeV of attraction from this vertex dressing. For the axial-vector states, which are about 300 MeV too low in ladder approximation, the results are mixed: the $1^{+-}$ state receives 290 MeV of repulsion, but the $1^{++}$ state is lowered further by 30 MeV. The exotic channels $0^{--}$ and $1^{-+}$ are found to have no states below 1.5 GeV in this model.

## Key claim

The paper extends the ladder-rainbow Bethe-Salpeter kernel to 2-loops by including quark-gluon vertex dressing, but only within a simplified hybrid model that uses a delta-function interaction for the dressing part to keep calculations feasible; the results are exploratory and depend on the specific infrared-dominant model employed.

## Method

The authors construct a symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel by adding a 1-loop vertex dressing (using the Munczek-Nemirovsky delta-function model) to an established finite-range rainbow self-energy (using a Gaussian effective gluon interaction). The resulting 2-loop kernel is symmetrized to preserve charge conjugation symmetry while maintaining the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, and the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations are solved numerically for u/d-quark meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Vector meson mass receives only 30 MeV of attraction from vertex dressing beyond the ladder level
- The 1^{+-} axial-vector state receives 290 MeV of repulsion from vertex dressing
- The 1^{++} axial-vector state is lowered further by 30 MeV from vertex dressing
- Exotic channels 0^{--} and 1^{-+} have no bound states below 1.5 GeV in this model
- The Gaussian gluon model parameters are omega = 0.5 GeV and D = 16 GeV^{-2}, with current quark mass m = 5 MeV
- The Munczek-Nemirovsky strength parameter G = 0.5 GeV^2 (from matching integrated strength), with results explored for G = 0-0.5 GeV^2
- At x = 0 in the chiral limit, the quark mass function B decreases by about 20% and A-1 decreases by about 30% with vertex dressing
- The chiral condensate decreases by 2% from rainbow truncation to the G = 0.5 GeV^2 vertex dressing
- The chiral limit pion decay constant f_pi^0 varies by 1% between rainbow and the G = 0.5 GeV^2 vertex dressing

## Context

This work extends the ladder-rainbow truncation studies of Maris and Tandy (1999) and Alkofer (2002) which successfully described pseudoscalar and vector mesons but produced axial-vector masses about 300 MeV too low (0.8-0.9 GeV). The approach builds on the constructive scheme of Bender (1996) for symmetry-preserving kernels beyond ladder level, and uses the Munczek-Nemirovsky delta-function model previously employed by Bender (2002) and Bhagwat (2004). The results enable systematic examination of vertex dressing effects on P-wave meson states, particularly the axial-vector channels, and provide a framework for future investigations with more realistic vertex models.
