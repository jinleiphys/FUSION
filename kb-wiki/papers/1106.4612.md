# Nuclear collision in strong magnetic field

> We studied nucleus-nucleus collision in strong magnetic field based on a transport model. It is found that neutrons and protons can be separated from a nucleus by strong magnetic field and neutron-rich high density nuclear matter and low density proton collectivity matter can be formed during nucleus-nucleus collision. The electric field produced by proton collectivity can accelerate proton and charged meson up to very high energies. Besides the studies of isospin physics such as symmetry energy, these results may help us to understand the acceleration mechanisms of high energy charged particles in the cosmic rays

# Nuclear collision in strong magnetic field

## Key claim
Nucleus-nucleus collisions in a magnetic field of roughly 10^17 Tesla can separate neutrons from protons, forming neutron-rich high-density matter and low-density proton collectivity; the electric field from the proton collectivity can accelerate protons and charged mesons to very high energies, potentially explaining the acceleration of high-energy cosmic-ray particles. The separation only occurs if the magnetic field is strong enough (approximately 10^17 Tesla) for the Lorentz force to overcome the mean-field gradient force.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model coupled with the relativistic Lorentz force equation to simulate nucleus-nucleus collisions in a strong magnetic field. The mean-field potential was computed using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with Skyrme M* force parameters, with isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections and Pauli blocking factors. The time step for updates was set to 0.0025 fm/c to handle the large magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field threshold for neutron-proton separation: approximately 10^17 Tesla (below this, the Lorentz force cannot overcome the mean-field gradient force)
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at normal density: 31.6 MeV
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: 211 MeV
- Neutron-to-proton ratio (n/p) of high-density nuclear matter formed in collision with magnetic field: up to 4 (compared to about 1 in ordinary nuclear reactions)
- Beam energy used in example simulation: 400 MeV/nucleon for ¹²⁴Sn + ¹¹²Sn collisions
- Estimated maximal proton energy from a proton collectivity of 100 fm radius with density equal to ²⁰⁸Pb: 3 GeV (scaling as 0.03 × R/fm GeV)

## Context
This work extends studies of nuclear pasta phases and isospin physics (e.g., symmetry energy) into the regime of strong magnetic fields, which may exist in white dwarfs, neutron stars, and accretion disks around black holes (Shapiro 2006). The separation mechanism and subsequent acceleration by electric fields from proton collectivity offer a new direct acceleration mechanism for cosmic rays, complementing existing theories such as Fermi acceleration (Fermi 1949) and pulsar-based acceleration (Bhattacharjee 2000). The results suggest that asymmetric nuclear matter with very high n/p ratios (up to 4) could be used to study isospin physics, particularly the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, which has been limited by the small asymmetry achievable in terrestrial laboratory collisions.
