# Formation of mesic nuclei by (γ,p) reactions

> We present a theoretical study on formation rates of ηand ωmeson-nucleus systems induced by (γ,p) reactions on nuclear targets at ideal recoilless condition. We find that the smaller distortion effect in the (γ,p) reaction enables us to investigate properties of the mesons created deeply inside nucleus more clearly. We also consider excitation of scalar-isoscalar (σ) mode in nucleus in order to investigate spectral enhancement around two-pion threshold caused by partial restoration of chiral symmetry. We conclude that valuable information of meson-nucleus interactions can be extracted from global structure of the missing mass spectra in the (γ,p) reaction.

## Key claim
The (γ,p) reaction, due to its smaller distortion effects compared to the established (d,³He) reaction, can provide clearer information on meson properties created deeply inside the nucleus, but the actual feasibility depends on the specific meson-nucleus interaction model and the degree of chiral symmetry restoration in the nuclear medium.

## Method
The authors calculated formation rates of η, ω, and scalar-isoscalar (σ) meson-nucleus systems using a Green function method constructed from the Klein-Gordon equation with appropriate optical potentials. For the η meson, they compared two theoretical models: the chiral doublet model (with parameters C=0.0 and C=0.2 representing different strengths of chiral restoration) and the chiral unitary model. For the ω meson, they evaluated spectra using optical potentials from effective models and studied the incident photon energy dependence. For the σ mode, they evaluated an effective spectral function due to the lack of elementary cross section and large imaginary part of the sigma meson self-energy in vacuum.

## Key numbers
- η-nucleus binding energies and widths for ¹¹B with chiral doublet model (C=0.0): 0s state (13.7 MeV binding, 41.5 MeV width); no bound state found for C=0.2
- η-nucleus binding energies and widths for ³⁹K with chiral doublet model (C=0.0): 0s state (30.3 MeV binding, 42.5 MeV width), 0p state (14.6 MeV binding, 50.7 MeV width); no bound state found for C=0.2
- η-nucleus binding energies and widths for ¹²C with chiral unitary approach: 0s state (9.71 MeV binding, 35.0 MeV width)
- η-nucleus binding energies and widths for ⁴⁰Ca with chiral unitary approach: 0s state (17.9 MeV binding, 34.4 MeV width), 0p state (7.0 MeV binding, 38.6 MeV width)
- ηN scattering length from the chiral doublet model with free-space masses: a_η = 0.24 + i0.38 fm
- The ηNN* coupling constant g_η was determined to be approximately 2.0 to reproduce the partial width Γ_{N*→ηN} ≈ 75 MeV
- The parameter C representing chiral restoration strength at saturation density ρ₀ lies empirically from 0.1 to 0.3

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of η-nucleus systems using (d,³He) reactions (Nagahiro et al., 2004-2005) to the (γ,p) reaction, which was originally proposed for hadron-nucleus system formation in earlier references (PLB231, JPhysG, PLB527, PLB502). The study compares two theoretical approaches for the η-nucleus interaction: the chiral doublet model (based on the linear sigma model for nucleons and their chiral partners) and the chiral unitary model (where N* is dynamically generated in coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering). For the ω meson, the work builds on calculations by Weise and collaborators (NPA624, NPA650, PLB502) and considers another theoretical prediction (Lutz, 2001). The σ-mode investigation is inspired by predictions of enhanced spectral function near the 2π threshold as a signal of partial chiral symmetry restoration (PRD57, PRL82, PRD63), where previous attempts with (d,t) or (d,³He) reactions were found difficult due to large distortion effects (NPA710). The results enable future experimental comparisons at photon facilities to distinguish between different chiral symmetry restoration scenarios.
