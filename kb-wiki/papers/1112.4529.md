# Evidence for 6ΛH

> Evidence for the neutron-rich hypernucleus 6ΛH is presented from the FINUDA experiment at DAΦNE, Frascati, studying (π+, π-) pairs in coincidence from the K- +6Li \rightarrow 6 H+π+ production reaction followed by 6ΛH \rightarrow 6He + π- weak decay. The production rate of 6ΛH undergoing this two-body π- decay is determined to be (2.9\pm2.0)\cdot10-6/K-. Its binding energy, evaluated jointly from production and decay, is BΛ(6ΛH) = (4.0\pm1.1) MeV with respect to 5H+Λ. A systematic difference of (0.98 \pm 0.74) MeV between BΛ values derived separately from decay and from production is tentatively assigned to the 6ΛH 0+g.s. \rightarrow 1+ excitation.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence for the neutron-rich hypernucleus 6ΛH based on three candidate events, but the statistical significance is modest (S=3.9 when including both physical and instrumental backgrounds) and the production rate of (2.9±2.0)×10⁻⁶ per stopped K⁻ has a large relative uncertainty.

## Method

The FINUDA experiment at DAΦNE studied K⁻ stopped in ⁶Li targets, requiring coincidence between π⁺ from the production reaction K⁻+⁶Li → 6ΛH+π⁺ and π⁻ from the subsequent weak decay 6ΛH → ⁶He+π⁻. Events were selected by requiring the sum of π⁺ and π⁻ kinetic energies T_sum = 202-204 MeV, with π⁺ momentum 250-255 MeV/c and π⁻ momentum 130-137 MeV/c, corresponding to kinematic expectations for two-body production and decay at rest.

## Key numbers

- Production rate times branching ratio: (2.9±2.0)×10⁻⁶ per stopped K⁻
- Binding energy B_Λ(6ΛH) = (4.0±1.1) MeV with respect to ⁵H+Λ, evaluated jointly from production and decay
- Systematic difference between B_Λ values from decay and from production: (0.98±0.74) MeV, tentatively assigned to 0⁺_g.s. → 1⁺ excitation
- Three candidate events found from approximately 2.7×10⁷ stopped K⁻ on ⁶Li targets
- Total integrated luminosity: 1156 pb⁻¹
- Total background estimate: 0.43±0.28 expected events (physical background 0.16±0.07, instrumental background 0.27±0.27)
- Statistical significance: S=7.1 considering only physical background, S=3.9 considering both backgrounds

## Context

This work extends earlier searches for neutron-rich hypernuclei, following a previous FINUDA analysis (2006) that gave only an upper limit of (2.5±0.4_stat +0.4/-0.1_syst)×10⁻⁵ per stopped K⁻. The measured binding energy agrees with the 1963 prediction of Dalitz and Levi-Setti (4.2 MeV) but is considerably smaller than the 5.8 MeV predicted by Akaishi (1999) from coherent ΛN-ΣN mixing effects. The result enables future searches for 6ΛH and 10ΛLi in (π⁻,K⁺) reactions scheduled at J-PARC, and constrains the role of ΛN-ΣN mixing in neutron-rich strange matter.
