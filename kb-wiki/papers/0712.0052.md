# Phase diagram of nuclear "pasta" and its uncertainties in supernova cores

> We examine the model dependence of the phase diagram of inhomogeneous nulcear matter in supernova cores using the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD). Inhomogeneous matter includes crystallized matter with nonspherical nuclei - "pasta" phases - and the liquid-gas phase separating nuclear matter. Major differences between the phase diagrams of the QMD models can be explained by the energy of pure neutron matter at low densities and the saturation density of asymmetric nuclear matter. We show the density dependence of the symmetry energy is also useful to understand uncertainties of the phase diagram. We point out that, for typical nuclear models, the mass fraction of the pasta phases in the later stage of the collapsing cores is higher than 10-20 %.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of nuclear pasta phases in supernova cores depends significantly on the nuclear model used in quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations, with differences explainable by the energy of pure neutron matter at low densities and the saturation density of asymmetric nuclear matter. For typical nuclear models, the mass fraction of pasta phases in the later stage of collapsing cores is higher than 10-20%.

## Method

The authors performed QMD simulations of nuclear matter with proton fraction x_p=0.3 using 2048 nucleons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, comparing two different nuclear force models (Model 1 by Maruyama et al. and Model 2 by Chikazumi et al.). Equilibrium states at zero temperature were obtained via frictional relaxation cooling, while finite-temperature states were simulated using a Nosé-Hoover thermostat modified for momentum-dependent potentials. Nuclear shapes were identified quantitatively using Minkowski functionals (area-averaged integral mean curvature and Euler characteristic density) and two-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density ρ₀: 0.165 fm⁻³ for Model 1, 0.168 fm⁻³ for Model 2
- Symmetry energy density-dependence parameter L: 93 MeV for Model 1, 80 MeV for Model 2
- Saturation density at x_p=0.3: 0.136 fm⁻³ for Model 1, 0.147 fm⁻³ for Model 2
- Saturation energy per baryon at x_p=0.3: -12.01 MeV for Model 1, -9.86 MeV for Model 2
- Surface tension σ for ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.62 MeV fm⁻² for Model 1, 1.07 MeV fm⁻² for Model 2
- Surface diffuseness parameter b_p for ²⁰⁸Pb: 3.4 fm for Model 1, 3.1 fm for Model 2
- Surface diffuseness parameter b_n for ²⁰⁸Pb: 3.8 fm for Model 1, 3.4 fm for Model 2
- Energy per baryon of pure neutron matter at low densities: Model 1 gives relatively small energy close to SkM, Model 2 gives larger energy below 0.12 fm⁻³
- Transition to uniform matter observed at T=6-7 MeV for slablike nuclei at ρ=0.0660 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous QMD studies by Watanabe et al. (2003, 2004, 2005) that used only one specific nuclear force, now investigating model dependence by comparing two different QMD parameter sets. The results confirm the sequence of nuclear shapes (sphere, rod, slab, rod-like bubbles, spherical bubbles) found in earlier works using liquid-drop models and Thomas-Fermi approximations by Lorenz et al. (1993), Watanabe et al. (2000, 2002), and Oyamatsu (1993), while also revealing intermediate "spongelike" phases. The findings enable more reliable predictions of pasta phase effects on neutrino opacity in supernova cores and neutron star cooling, as the mass fraction of pasta phases (10-20%) could significantly impact neutrino transport and supernova explosion dynamics.
