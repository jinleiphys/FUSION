# Bjorken model with Freeze Out

> The freeze out of the expanding systems, created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, is discussed. We combine Bjorken scenario with earlier developed freeze out equations into a unified model. The important feature of the proposed model is that physical freeze out is completely finished in a finite time, which can be varied from 0 (freeze out hypersurface) to infinity. The dependence of the post freeze out distribution function on this freeze out time will be studied. As an example model is completely solved and analyzed for the gas of pions.

# Bjorken model with Freeze Out

## Key claim
The paper presents a kinetic freeze-out model that combines Bjorken expansion with gradual freeze out completed in a finite layer, bridging the two extreme schemes of zero-thickness hypersurface freeze out and infinite-volume cascade freeze out. The post-freeze-out distribution functions show strong universality across different freeze-out layer thicknesses, with deviations from thermal distributions appearing only at very low momenta.

## Method
The authors introduce a two-component distribution function (interacting and free) for a Bjorken-expanding fireball, where the interacting component evolves according to modified hydrodynamic equations that include both expansion and freeze-out terms. The freeze-out process occurs in a finite layer bounded by two parallel hypersurfaces at proper times τ₁ and τ₁+L, with the interacting component completely disappearing at the outer edge. The model is solved analytically for the interacting component and numerically for the free component, using a Jüttner equilibrium distribution for a massive pion gas with degeneracy g=3.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature T_i(τ₀=0.05 fm) = 820 MeV, freeze-out temperature T_FO = 180 MeV at τ₁ = 5 fm
- Freeze-out time parameter τ_FO = 0.5 fm
- Transverse area A_xy = π R_Au² with R_Au = 7.685 fm (gold nucleus radius)
- Pseudorapidity range η_R = 4.38
- Post-freeze-out distributions in medium momenta region (0.5 GeV < |p| < 2.5 GeV) can be fitted with thermal distribution at T = 160 MeV
- Total entropy increases during phase II until freeze out is finished, with up to 10% entropy production possible during freeze out

## Context
This work extends earlier simplified freeze-out models without expansion (Magas et al. 2005a, 2005b; old single-layer and two-layer FO models) by incorporating Bjorken expansion, making the model more physically realistic for relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The results show that the key features of gradual freeze out, particularly the universality of post-freeze-out distributions and entropy production, are not smeared out by expansion, which has implications for QGP search estimates that assume adiabatic expansion and freeze out, such as strangeness versus entropy production estimates (Gorenstein, year not specified in text). The model enables calculation of two-particle correlations by tracking both momentum and space-time coordinates of freeze-out points.
