# Microscopic description of triaxiality in Ru isotopes with covariant energy density functional theory

> The triaxiality in nuclear low-lying states has attracted great interests for many years. Recently, the reduced transition probabilities for levels near the ground state in $^{110}$Ru have been measured and provided strong evidences for a triaxial shape of this nucleus. The aim of this work is to provide a microscopic study of low-lying states for the Ru isotopes with $A\sim100$ and to examine in detail the role of triaxiality, and the evolution of quadrupole shapes with the isospin and spin degrees of freedom. The low-lying excitation spectra and transition probabilities of even-even Ru isotopes are described at the beyond mean-field level by solving a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with parameters determined by constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations based on the relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1. The calculated energy surfaces, low-energy spectra, intraband and interband transition rates, as well as some characteristic collective observables, such as $E(4_{\rm g.s.}^+)/E(2^+_{\rm g.s.})$, $E(2^+_γ)/E(4^+_{\rm g.s.})$, $B(E2; 2^+_{\rm g.s.}\to 0^+_{\rm g.s.})$, and $γ$ band staggerings are in a good agreement with the available experimental data. The main features of the experimental low-lying excitation spectra and electric transition rates are well reproduced, and thus strongly support the onset of triaxiality in the low-lying excited states of the Ru isotopes around $^{110}$Ru.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory (5DCH-CDFT) with the PC-PK1 functional successfully reproduces the low-lying excitation spectra and electric transition rates of even-even Ru isotopes, thereby supporting the onset of triaxiality in low-lying excited states around $^{110}$Ru, but the agreement is qualified by noting that the theoretical spectra for $^{100,102}$Ru are "stretched" and intraband transitions are "generally larger," indicating an overestimation of collectivity for these lighter isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field calculations with BCS pairing using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 to determine the collective parameters (mass parameters, moments of inertia, and collective potential) for the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian. The moments of inertia were calculated with the Inglis-Belyaev formula and the mass parameters with the cranking approximation. The resulting Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies, collective wave functions, and $E2$ transition probabilities for even-even $^{100-114}$Ru isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The global minimum of $^{100}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.21, \gamma=4^\circ)$ with a triaxial deformation energy $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.004$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{102}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.25, \gamma=19^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.324$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{104}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.27, \gamma=22^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.868$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{106}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.28, \gamma=25^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.676$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{108}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.27, \gamma=32^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.310$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{110}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.26, \gamma=37^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.073$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{112}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.26, \gamma=38^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.055$ MeV.
- The global minimum of $^{114}$Ru is at $(\beta=0.25, \gamma=33^\circ)$ with $\Delta E_{\rm tri} = -0.360$ MeV.
- The deviations of binding energies from experimental data for mean-field calculations are in the range $2.0$ to $3.5$ MeV; after 5DCH calculations, deviations are reduced to within $1.6$ MeV.
- The measured $E(4_{\rm g.s.}^+)/E(2^+_{\rm g.s.})$ values for Ru isotopes vary from approximately $2.3$ to $2.7$.

## Context

This work extends previous 5DCH-CDFT studies on triaxial structures in Mo isotopes and $N=60$ isotones by Xiang et al. (2016) to the Ru isotopic chain, and provides a microscopic description of recent Coulomb excitation data for $^{110}$Ru from Doherty et al. (2017). The results are compared with mapped interacting boson model (IBM) calculations by Nomura et al. (2016) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Abusara et al. (2017), showing that the 5DCH-CDFT approach reproduces experimental data without effective charges, and enables further studies of shape evolution and triaxiality in neutron-rich nuclei.
