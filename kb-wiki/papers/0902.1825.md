# Constraints for weakly interacting light bosons from existence of massive neutron stars

> Theories beyond the standard model include a number of new particles some of which might be light and weakly coupled to ordinary matter. Such particles affect equation of state of nuclear matter and can shift admissible masses of neutron stars to higher values. The internal structure of neutron stars is modified provided the ratio between coupling strength and mass squared of a weakly interacting light boson is above $g^2/μ^2 \sim 25 ~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$. We provide limits on the couplings with the strange sector, which cannot be achieved from laboratory experiments analysis. When the couplings to the first family of quarks is considered the limits imposed by the neutron stars are not more stringent than the existing laboratory ones. The observations on neutron stars give evidence that equation of state of the $β$-equilibrated nuclear matter is stiffer than expected from many-body theory of nuclei and nuclear matter. A weakly interacting light vector boson coupled predominantly to the second family of the quarks can produce the required stiffening.

# Constraints for weakly interacting light bosons from existence of massive neutron stars

## Key claim

Weakly interacting light bosons (WILBs) can modify the equation of state of nuclear matter and shift admissible neutron star masses, but only if the ratio of coupling strength to boson mass squared exceeds approximately $g^2/\mu^2 \sim 25\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$; the paper provides limits on couplings to the strange sector that cannot be obtained from laboratory experiments, while for couplings to the first family of quarks the neutron star constraints are not more stringent than existing laboratory ones.

## Method

The authors compute the coherent contribution of a hypothetical vector or scalar boson to the energy density of nuclear matter by averaging the Yukawa potential over a homogeneous baryon distribution, yielding $E_I = V g^2 \rho^2 / (2\mu^2)$. They compare this contribution to that of standard $\omega$-meson and $\sigma$-meson exchanges in one-boson exchange potential and relativistic mean field models, and derive upper limits on $g^2/\mu^2$ by requiring that WILBs do not qualitatively modify the internal structure of neutron stars. They then compute mass-radius relations for non-rotating neutron stars using the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland and Baym-Bethe-Pethick equations of state at low densities and a $\beta$-equilibrated hyperon liquid model with compression modulus $K=300$ MeV at high densities, adding the WILB contribution for a flavor-singlet vector boson.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $g^2/\mu^2$ for $\omega$-meson exchange in $NN$ interactions is $175\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ (Stocks 1999); relativistic mean field models give $196\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ (Chin 1974); the compression modulus $K=210\div300$ MeV is consistent with $g^2/\mu^2 = 125\div180\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ (Glendenning 1996); stiff RMF models use values up to $300\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ (Tuchin 2005).
- The constraint for vector WILBs is $g^2/\mu^2 \lesssim 200\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$; for scalar WILBs the constraint is $g^2/\mu^2 \lesssim 300\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$.
- Observed massive pulsars include PSR B1516+02B with mass $1.96^{+0.09}_{-0.12}\ \mathrm{M_{\odot}}$ and PSR J1748-2021B with mass $2.74 \pm 0.22\ \mathrm{M_{\odot}}$ (Freire 2007); the X-ray source EXO 0748-676 gives $M \geq 2.10 \pm 0.28\ \mathrm{M_{\odot}}$ and $R \geq 13.8 \pm 1.8$ km (Ozel 2006).
- A flavor-singlet vector WILB with $g^2/\mu^2 = 25\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ (1/8 of the limit) shifts the maximum mass of hyperon matter neutron stars; $g^2/\mu^2 = 200\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ gives a maximum mass slightly above $3.0\ \mathrm{M_{\odot}}$.
- The ratio $g^2/\mu^2 \approx 50\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$ is suggested as a reasonable value to bring soft hyperon matter models into agreement with astrophysical observations.
- The neutron drip density is $\rho_{drip} = 4.3\times 10^{11}\ \mathrm{g/cm}^3$; the nuclear density is $\rho_{nucl} = 2.8\times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{g/cm}^3$.

## Context

This work extends constraints on hypothetical light bosons from laboratory experiments on deviations from Newtonian gravity (Bordag 1994, Lamoreaux 1997, Decca 2005, Mostepanenko 2008), low-energy neutron scattering (Barbieri 1975, Pokotilovski 2006), spectroscopy of antiproton atoms (Pokotilovski 2006), and near-forward $pn$ scattering (Kamiya 2008) into the astrophysical domain of neutron star structure. The paper addresses the long-standing tension between laboratory heavy-ion collision data suggesting a soft nuclear equation of state at 2-3 times saturation density (Fuchs 2001, Hartnack 2006, Fuchs 2007) and astrophysical observations of massive pulsars requiring a stiff equation of state for $\beta$-equilibrated matter. It enables future searches for WILBs through detailed studies of neutron star structure, hadron decays to missing energy channels, and provides unique constraints on couplings to the strange sector that are inaccessible to laboratory experiments.
