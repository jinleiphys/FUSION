# Multiplicity Fluctuations in Hadron-Resonance Gas

> The charged hadron multiplicity fluctuations are considered in the canonical ensemble. The microscopic correlator method is extended to include three conserved charges: baryon number, electric charge and strangeness. The analytical formulae are presented that allow to include resonance decay contributions to correlations and fluctuations. We make the predictions for the scaled variances of negative, positive and all charged hadrons in the most central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions for different collision energies from SIS and AGS to SPS and RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper extends the microscopic correlator method to include three conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) in the canonical ensemble, showing that exact charge conservation suppresses scaled variances of particle number fluctuations compared to the grand canonical ensemble, and this suppression survives in the thermodynamic limit \(V\rightarrow\infty\) even though average quantities become equivalent between ensembles.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions for microscopic correlators in a relativistic quantum gas with exact conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, using a Gaussian approximation for occupation number fluctuations with delta-function constraints for the conserved charges. They then construct a generating function to incorporate resonance decay contributions to correlations and fluctuations within the canonical ensemble, and apply the formalism to a hadron-resonance gas along the chemical freeze-out line using an extended version of the THERMUS thermal model framework.

## Key numbers

- The parameterization \(\mu_B(\sqrt{s_{NN}}) = 1.308\ \text{GeV} / (1 + 0.273 \sqrt{s_{NN}})\) is used to describe the baryon chemical potential as a function of center-of-mass nucleon-nucleon collision energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) in GeV.
- For a neutral Boltzmann gas with \(Q=0\) in the canonical ensemble, the scaled variance \(\omega^{\pm}_{c.e.} = 1/2\) in the large volume limit, compared to \(\omega^{\pm}_{g.c.e.} = 1\) in the grand canonical ensemble.
- The condition \(\langle E \rangle / \langle N \rangle \approx 1\ \text{GeV}\) is used to fix the temperature along the chemical freeze-out line.
- The charge-to-baryon ratio for heavy nuclei is set to \(Q/B \approx 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic correlator method previously applied to one conserved charge (Begun et al., 2004; 2005) to three conserved charges, and generalizes the canonical ensemble treatment of fluctuations from the Boltzmann ideal gas (Begun et al., 2004) to include quantum statistics and resonance decays. The results enable predictions for scaled variances of charged hadrons in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions across energies from SIS to RHIC, and can be compared to experimental fluctuation measurements to test the thermal model description of the chemical freeze-out stage.
