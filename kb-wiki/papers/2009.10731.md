# Rapidly Spinning Compact Stars with Deconfinement Phase Transition

> We study rapidly spinning compact stars with equations of state featuring a first order phase transition between strongly coupled nuclear matter and deconfined quark matter by employing the gauge/gravity duality. We consider a family of models, which allow purely hadronic uniformly rotating stars with masses up to approximately $2.9\, \mathrm{M}_\odot$, and are therefore compatible with the interpretation that the secondary component ($2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09}\, \mathrm{M}_\odot$) in GW190814 is a neutron star. These stars have central densities several times the nuclear saturation density so that strong coupling and non-perturbative effects become crucial. We construct models where the maximal mass of static (rotating) stars $M_{\mathrm{TOV}}$ ($M_{\mathrm{max}}$) is either determined by the secular instability or a phase transition induced collapse. We find largest values for $M_{\mathrm{max}}/M_{\mathrm{TOV}}$ in cases where the phase transition determines $M_{\mathrm{max}}$, which shifts our fit result to $M_{\mathrm{max}}/M_{\mathrm{TOV}} = 1.227^{+0.031}_{-0.016}$, a value slightly above the Breu-Rezzolla bound $1.203^{+0.022}_{-0.022}$ inferred from models without phase transition.

# Rapidly Spinning Compact Stars with Deconfinement Phase Transition

## Key claim

The authors find that holographic equations of state with a first-order deconfinement phase transition can produce rapidly rotating neutron stars with masses up to approximately 2.9 solar masses, compatible with the secondary component of GW190814 being a neutron star, but only if the rotation frequency exceeds about 1 kHz. The ratio of maximum rotating to maximum static mass is shifted upward to 1.227^{+0.031}_{-0.016} when the phase transition determines the maximum mass, slightly above the Breu-Rezzolla bound of 1.203^{+0.022}_{-0.022} for models without a phase transition.

## Method

The authors use the V-QCD holographic model, which describes both nuclear and quark matter phases within a single framework, to construct three variants (soft, intermediate, stiff) of equations of state featuring a first-order deconfinement phase transition. They match these to the APR equation of state at low densities and compute sequences of uniformly rotating stars in general relativity using the RNS code, determining the maximum mass by either the secular instability (turning-point criterion) or phase-transition-induced collapse. They also perform a scan over an ensemble of hybrid equations of state with different nuclear matter models and matching densities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of non-rotating stars: 2.04 solar masses (soft), 2.22 solar masses (intermediate), 2.35 solar masses (stiff)
- Maximum mass of rotating stars: approximately 2.9 solar masses for the stiffest models
- Mass ratio fit: M_max/M_TOV = 1.227^{+0.031}_{-0.016}, compared to the Breu-Rezzolla bound 1.203^{+0.022}_{-0.022}
- Maximum rotation frequencies: 1.45 kHz (soft), 1.54 kHz (intermediate), 1.60 kHz (stiff)
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: 493 (soft), 536 (intermediate), 567 (stiff)
- Deconfinement phase transition densities: 4.89 n_s (soft), 5.43 n_s (intermediate), 5.61 n_s (stiff), where n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Maximum rest mass ratio: M_b,max/M_max = 1.177^{+0.018}_{-0.020}
- Matching density between APR and holographic model: n_tr = 1.6 n_s

## Context

This work extends the analysis of rapidly rotating neutron stars with holographic equations of state from Ecker (2019) and Jokela (2020), and directly addresses the interpretation of the GW190814 secondary component as a rapidly rotating neutron star, following Most (2020). The results contradict the universal mass ratio found by Breu and Rezzolla (2016) for models without phase transitions, showing that first-order deconfinement transitions can increase M_max/M_TOV. The findings enable future studies of differentially rotating stars and dynamical instabilities in the context of phase-transition-induced collapse.
