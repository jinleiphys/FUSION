# Chiral symmetry, di-electrons and charm

> We survey some prospects of identifying further in-medium modifications of hadrons in a strongly interacting medium with respect to ongoing experiment series of the HADES Collaboration and planned experiments of the CBM Collaboration at FAIR. Di-electrons, strange and charm mesons are considered and their potential for signaling imprints of chiral restoration is highlighted.

## Key claim
The paper surveys prospects for identifying in-medium modifications of hadrons as signals of chiral restoration, but emphasizes that for di-electrons in small collision systems (C+C) even "very drastic medium modifications" produce only a tiny imprint because the system is too short-lived, with spectral functions evolving rapidly to vacuum ones.

## Method
The authors use a BUU-type transport model to simulate di-electron spectra in C(2 AGeV)+C and Au(2 AGeV)+Au collisions, comparing vacuum spectral functions of ρ and ω mesons with in-medium versions including collision broadening and mass shifts of -100 MeV and -50 MeV. For strange and charm mesons, they apply QCD sum rules with operator-product expansion and Borel transformed dispersion relations to extract in-medium mass splittings and spectral moments, and use a quasi-particle model adjusted to lattice QCD results to compute gluon condensates at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- In-medium mass shifts of -100 MeV and -50 MeV were assumed for ρ and ω mesons in the transport model, but produced only a tiny imprint in the C(2 AGeV)+C di-electron spectrum.
- For kaon transverse momentum distributions in Ar(1.756 AGeV)+KCl collisions, precision data at p_t ~ 200 MeV/c in various rapidity bins are needed to pin down the actual values of the interaction potentials.
- The critical temperature for the deconfinement transition was fixed to T_c = 190 MeV in the gluon condensate calculations.
- A scaling law was observed for the gluon condensates: X(μ/T_c = T(μ=0)/T_c - 0.045 T/T_c - 0.027 (T/T_c)^2) = const.

## Context
This work extends the line of Brown-Rho scaling predictions linking hadron masses to the chiral condensate, as surveyed by Leupold (2009) and Rapp (2009), and addresses the need for larger collision systems to verify vector meson modifications seen in cold nuclear matter by Wood (2008) and hot nuclear matter by Rapp (2009). The QCD sum rule approach for D mesons builds on prior work by Hilger (2008) and Hilger (2010), and the gluon condensate calculations at high temperature follow Morita (2007) using lattice results from Bazavov (2009). The results enable future experiments by HADES and CBM at FAIR to design observables for chiral restoration, particularly through D meson spectral functions which couple strongly to the chiral condensate.
