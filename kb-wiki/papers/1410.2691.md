# Mean-Field Calculation Based on Proton-Neutron Mixed Energy Density Functionals

> We have performed calculations based on the Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) that includes arbitrary mixing between protons and neutrons. In this framework, single-particle states are generalized as mixtures of proton and neutron components. The model assumes that the Skyrme EDF is invariant under the rotation in isospin space and the Coulomb force is the only source of the isospin symmetry breaking. To control the isospin of the system, we employ the isocranking method, which is analogous to the standard cranking approach used for describing high-spin states. Here, we present results of the isocranking calculations performed for the isobaric analog states in $A = 40$ and $A = 54$ nuclei.

# Mean-Field Calculation Based on Proton-Neutron Mixed Energy Density Functionals

## Key claim
The Skyrme energy density functional extended to include arbitrary proton-neutron mixing in the particle-hole channel, combined with the isocranking method, can quantitatively describe isobaric analog states in both even-even and odd-odd nuclei, provided the Coulomb force is treated as the only source of isospin symmetry breaking and the isocranking parameters are chosen to avoid level crossings.

## Method
The authors extended the Skyrme energy density functional to include proton-neutron mixing by constructing isoscalar and isovector densities from the local density matrix with isospin indices, following the general rule of Perlinska et al. (2004). They applied the isocranking method, adding a term -λ·t to the Hamiltonian to control the isospin direction, and solved the generalized self-consistent Hartree-Fock equations for isobaric analog states in A=40 and A=54 nuclei using the SkM* parameter set. The Coulomb interaction was calculated exactly in both direct and exchange channels, and the isocranking frequency parameters were determined from the difference of neutron and proton Fermi energies in the standard Hartree-Fock solutions for the T_z = ±T isobars.

## Key numbers
- For T ≈ 4 isobaric analog states in A=40: isocranking parameters (λ_off, λ') = (0, 12.5) MeV without Coulomb and (-6.8, 13.6) MeV with Coulomb, determined from Fermi energy differences in ⁴⁰S and ⁴⁰Cr.
- For T ≈ 8 isobaric analog states in A=40: isocranking parameters (λ_off, λ') = (0, 27.4) MeV without Coulomb and (-6.0, 28.6) MeV with Coulomb, determined from Fermi energy differences in ⁴⁰Mg and ⁴⁰Ni.
- For rigorous isospin conservation, ⟨T²⟩ should equal 72 for T ≈ 8 states; the calculated value deviates from this due to spurious isospin mixing within the mean-field approximation.
- Single-particle Routhians for T ≈ 8 states in A=40 show the Fermi surface around -12.5 MeV at θ' = 0°, with 14 neutron and 6 proton orbitals occupied below it.
- For A=54, the T=1 triplet energies (ground states of ⁵⁴Fe and ⁵⁴Ni at |T_z|=1, and the T_z=0 state in ⁵⁴Co) were calculated and compared with experimental data, showing good agreement.

## Context
This work extends the proton-neutron mixing formalism developed by Perlinska et al. (2004) and builds on the codes HFODD (Schunck et al., 2012) and HFBTHO (Stoitsov et al., 2013), which were previously extended to include p-n mixing in the particle-hole channel (Sato et al., 2013; Sheikh et al., 2014). The isocranking method is analogous to the tilted-axis cranking used for high-spin states. The results demonstrate that single-reference energy density functional approaches with p-n mixing can describe isobaric analog states in odd-odd nuclei, which are inaccessible in standard p-n unmixed models (Sato et al., 2010). The next step is to implement isospin projection to remove the spurious isospin mixing inherent to the mean-field approach.
