# Chiral restoration at finite temperature with meson loop corrections

> We investigate chiral-restoration patterns of QCD for N_{c}=3 and N_{f}=2 at finite temperature (T) and zero quark-chemical potential beyond the chiral limit, indicating the explicit chiral-symmetry breaking. To this end, we employ the instanton-vacuum configuration for the flavor SU(2) sector and the Harrington-Shepard caloron for modifying relevant instanton parameters as functions of T. The meson-loop corrections (MLC), which correspond to 1/N_{c} corrections, are also taken into account to reproduce appropriate m_{q} dependences of chiral order parameters. We compute chiral condensate as a function of T and/or m_{q}. From the numerical calculations, we observe that MLC play an important role to have a correct universality-class behavior of chiral-restoration patterns in this framework, depending on m_{q}: Second-order phase transition in the chiral limit, m_{q}=0 and crossover for m_{q}>0. Without MLC, all the restoration patterns are crossover, due to simple saddle-point approximations. It turns out that T^χ_{c}=159 MeV in the chiral limit and T^χ_{c}=(177,186,196) MeV for m_{q}=(5,10,15) MeV, using the phenomenological choices for the instanton parameters at T=0.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including meson-loop corrections (1/N_c corrections) is necessary to obtain the correct universality-class behavior of chiral restoration in the instanton-vacuum framework: a second-order phase transition in the chiral limit (m_q=0) and crossover for m_q>0. Without these corrections, the saddle-point approximation incorrectly predicts crossover for all quark masses.

## Method

The authors employ an effective thermodynamic potential derived from the instanton-vacuum configuration for N_c=3 and N_f=2 QCD, using the Harrington-Shepard caloron to modify the average instanton size and inter-instanton distance as functions of temperature. They compute the chiral condensate by solving self-consistent saddle-point equations that include meson-loop corrections as next-to-leading order 1/N_c contributions, with the pion fluctuation dominating the meson-loop term. The critical temperature for chiral restoration is extracted from the temperature derivative of the chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

- T^χ_c = 159 MeV in the chiral limit (m_q=0), using phenomenological instanton parameters (average instanton size ≈ 1/3 fm, inter-instanton distance ≈ 1 fm at T=0)
- T^χ_c = (177, 186, 196) MeV for m_q = (5, 10, 15) MeV respectively
- Effective quark mass at zero momentum transfer M_0 ≈ 325 MeV without meson-loop corrections in the chiral limit at T=0, decreasing to about 190 MeV with meson-loop corrections (about 45% reduction)
- Meson-loop corrections reduce M_0 at T=0 by 37% to 63% depending on instanton parameter choices, consistent with prior work by Goeke (2007) and Kim (2005)

## Context

This work extends the instanton-vacuum approach of Nam (2008, 2009) to finite temperature beyond the chiral limit, incorporating meson-loop corrections that were previously shown to be essential for reproducing lattice QCD results for the quark mass dependence of the effective quark mass at zero temperature. The results are consistent with the universality class predictions of Pisarski (1983) and with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations beyond mean-field by Muller (2010). The framework enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram including the critical end point, though the authors note that full 1/N_c corrections and more sophisticated caloron solutions (such as the Kraan-Baal-Lee-Lu caloron) would be needed for a complete treatment.
