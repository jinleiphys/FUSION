# Nucleon spin decomposition with one dynamical gluon

> We solve for the light-front wave functions of the nucleon from a light-front quantum chromodynamics (QCD) effective Hamiltonian with three-dimensional confinement. We obtain solutions using constituent three quarks combined with three quarks and one gluon Fock components. The resulting light-front wave functions provide a good quality description of the nucleon's quark distribution functions following QCD scale evolution. We present the effects from incorporating a dynamical gluon on the nucleon's gluon densities, helicity distribution and orbital angular momentum that constitutes the nucleon spin sum rule.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a dynamical gluon Fock component (|qqqg⟩) alongside the valence three-quark sector in a light-front Hamiltonian approach yields nucleon parton distribution functions and spin structure that agree with global fits and experimental data, but the results are obtained at a model scale determined by fitting the proton mass, electromagnetic properties, and flavor form factors, with a 10% uncertainty adopted for the model scale.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front QCD effective Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem using Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) with a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states in the transverse direction and discretized plane waves in the longitudinal direction, truncating the basis with parameters Nmax=9 and K=16.5. The Hamiltonian includes kinetic terms, vertex and instantaneous interactions, and a three-dimensional confining interaction in the leading |qqq⟩ Fock sector, with model parameters fitted to the proton mass, electromagnetic form factors, and flavor form factors. The resulting light-front wave functions are used to compute unpolarized and helicity parton distribution functions (PDFs) at the model scale, which are then evolved to higher momentum scales using NNLO DGLAP equations via the HOPPET code.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: quark masses m_u=0.31 GeV, m_d=0.25 GeV, gluon mass m_g=0.50 GeV, confinement strength κ=0.54 GeV, vertex quark mass m_f=1.80 GeV, coupling g_s=2.40
- Proton charge radius: √⟨r²_E⟩=0.85±0.01 fm (experimental: 0.840^{+0.003}_{-0.002} fm)
- Proton magnetic radius: √⟨r²_M⟩=0.88±0.07 fm (experimental: 0.849^{+0.003}_{-0.003} fm)
- Proton magnetic moment: μ_p=2.443±0.027 (experimental: 2.79)
- Valence quark ratio at x→1: d^v/u^v=0.225±0.025 (MARATHON extrapolation: 0.230±0.057)
- Up quark unpolarized PDF large-x falloff: (1-x)^{3.2±0.1}
- Down quark unpolarized PDF large-x falloff: (1-x)^{3.5±0.1}
- Quark helicity contribution to proton spin: ½ΔΣ=0.359±0.002 (up: ½ΔΣ_u=0.438±0.004, down: ½ΔΣ_d=-0.080±0.002)
- Gluon helicity contribution: ΔG=0.131±0.003
- Quark orbital angular momentum: L_z^u=0.0327±0.0013, L_z^d=-0.0114±0.0004
- Gluon orbital angular momentum: L_z^g=-0.0065±0.0005

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to nucleons using only a valence Fock representation (Xu 2021) by incorporating a dynamical gluon in the |qqqg⟩ Fock sector, enabling direct computation of gluon PDFs and helicity distributions. The results provide predictions for the gluon helicity asymmetry and orbital angular momentum that can be tested at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and the obtained light-front wave functions can be further used to compute generalized parton distributions, transverse momentum distributions, and Wigner distributions.
