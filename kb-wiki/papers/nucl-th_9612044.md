# Enhanced T-odd P-odd Electromagnetic Moments in Reflection Asymmetric Nuclei

> Collective P- and T- odd moments produced by parity and time invariance violating forces in reflection asymmetric nuclei are considered. The enhanced collective Schiff, electric dipole and octupole moments appear due to the mixing of rotational levels of opposite parity. These moments can exceed single-particle moments by more than two orders of magnitude. The enhancement is due to the collective nature of the intrinsic moments and the small energy separation between members of parity doublets. In turn these nuclear moments induce enhanced T- and P- odd effects in atoms and molecules. First a simple estimate is given and then a detailed theoretical treatment of the collective T-, P- odd electric moments in reflection asymmetric, odd-mass nuclei is presented and various corrections evaluated. Calculations are performed for octupole deformed long-lived odd-mass isotopes of Rn, Fr, Ra, Ac and Pa and the corresponding atoms. Experiments with such atoms may improve substantially the limits on time reversal violation.

# Enhanced T-odd P-odd Electromagnetic Moments in Reflection Asymmetric Nuclei

## Key claim

Collective T-odd, P-odd electromagnetic moments (Schiff, electric dipole, and octupole moments) in reflection asymmetric nuclei can exceed single-particle moments by more than two orders of magnitude, but only when a T- and P-violating interaction mixes rotational parity doublets whose members are separated by small energy splittings; the enhancement relies on both the collective nature of the intrinsic moments and the existence of closely spaced parity doublets in octupole-deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors used a two-fluid liquid drop model to calculate intrinsic dipole, Schiff, and octupole electric moments in reflection asymmetric nuclei, incorporating Coulomb redistribution of protons versus neutrons and Strutinsky shell corrections with BCS pairing. They employed the Nilsson-Strutinsky mean field approach combined with the Leander particle-plus-core model to compute wave functions, parity doublet energy splittings, and T-, P-odd interaction matrix elements for odd-mass isotopes of Rn, Fr, Ra, Ac, and Pa. The mixing of rotational levels of opposite parity by the T-, P-odd interaction was then used to obtain the laboratory-frame collective moments.

## Key numbers

- The collective Schiff moment estimate for a nucleus with A=230, Z=88, beta2=0.12, beta3=0.1, and parity splitting |E+ - E-| = 50 keV yields S ~ 700 x 10^-8 eta e fm^3, where eta is the dimensionless T-, P-odd interaction strength; this is about 500 times larger than the single-particle Schiff moment of S ~ 1.5 x 10^-8 eta e fm^3 from Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984).
- The intrinsic Schiff moment for the same parameters (A=230, Z=88, R0=1.2 x A^(1/3) fm, beta2=0.12, beta3=0.1) is S_intr = 10.4 e fm^3.
- The mixing coefficient alpha is estimated as |alpha| ~ 7 x 10^-7 eta for A=230, beta3=0.1, and |E+ - E-| = 50 keV.
- The intrinsic dipole moment from the two-fluid model is given by d_intr = e A Z (e^2/C) (3/40 pi) sum over l>=2 of terms proportional to beta_l beta_{l+1}, with the symmetry-energy coefficient C in the range 20-35 MeV for the nuclei studied.
- The intrinsic octupole moment is Q_3_intr = (3 e Z R0^3)/(2 sqrt(7 pi)) (beta3 + (2/3) sqrt(5/pi) beta2 beta3 + (15/(11 sqrt(pi))) beta3 beta4 + ...).

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak (1996) that rotating octupole-deformed nuclei have enhanced T-, P-odd moments, providing a more detailed theoretical treatment with realistic nuclear models. It builds on the recognition by Leander, Nazarewicz, and Bertsch (1986) that systematically enhanced E1 transitions in some nuclei are a direct consequence of intrinsic reflection asymmetry, and on the formulation of the Schiff moment by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984). The calculations for specific long-lived odd-mass isotopes of Rn, Fr, Ra, Ac, and Pa enable experiments with these atoms that may improve limits on time reversal violation by factors of approximately 1000 compared to existing measurements in Hg and Xe atoms.
