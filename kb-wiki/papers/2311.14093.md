# Color Confinement and Random Matrices -- A random walk down group manifold toward Casimir scaling --

> We explain the microscopic origin of linear confinement potential with the Casimir scaling in generic confining gauge theories. In the low-temperature regime of confining gauge theories such as QCD, Polyakov lines are slowly varying Haar random modulo exponentially small corrections with respect to the inverse temperature, as shown by one of the authors (M.~H.) and Watanabe. With exact Haar randomness, computation of the two-point correlator of Polyakov loops reduces to the problem of random walk on group manifold. Linear confinement potential with approximate Casimir scaling except at short distances follows naturally from slowly varying Haar randomness. With exponentially small corrections to Haar randomness, string breaking and loss of Casimir scaling at long distance follow. Hence we obtain the Casimir scaling which is only approximate and holds only at intermediate distance, which is precisely needed to explain the results of lattice simulations. For $(1+1)$-dimensional theories, there is a simplification that admits the Casimir scaling at short distances as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that linear confinement with approximate Casimir scaling of string tensions arises naturally from the slowly varying Haar randomness of Polyakov lines in the confined phase of generic gauge theories, but this scaling is only approximate and holds only at intermediate distances, with corrections leading to string breaking and loss of Casimir scaling at long distances.

## Method

The authors show that in the low-temperature regime of confining gauge theories, Polyakov lines are slowly varying Haar random modulo exponentially small corrections with respect to inverse temperature. They reduce the computation of the two-point correlator of Polyakov loops to a random walk on the group manifold, where the displacement between Polyakov lines at different spatial points follows a path-ordered exponential of a slowly varying "velocity" field. The authors analyze both a vanilla random walk (uncorrelated steps) and a more realistic model with gradually changing velocity, using numerical experiments for SU(2) and SU(3).

## Key numbers

- For SU(3), the quadratic Casimir invariants are: C_fund = 4/3, C_adj = 3, C_2-sym = 10/3, C_3-sym = 6 for fundamental, adjoint, rank-2 symmetric, and rank-3 symmetric representations respectively.
- For SU(2), the quadratic Casimir for spin-j representation is C_spin-j = j(j+1).
- The string tension for representation r is proportional to C_r, with the Polyakov loop correlator scaling as exp(-beta * C_r * L * sigma_0^2), where sigma_0 is independent of representation.
- In the vanilla random walk model, the step size parameter Delta^2 is identified as beta * s * sigma_0^2, where s is the correlation length scale.
- The correlation length scale ap is estimated as ap ~ 1/(beta * sigma_0^2).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Hanada and Watanabe (2023) showing that Polyakov lines are slowly varying Haar random in confining gauge theories. It provides a microscopic origin for the Casimir scaling phenomenon previously observed in lattice simulations by Ambjorn (1984), Del Debbio (1995), Bali (2000), and Deldar (1999). The approach differs from earlier random walk models by Brzoska (2004), Arcioni (2005), and Buividovich (2006-2007), which were based on phenomenological observations from lattice simulations, by deriving the random walk nature from first principles. The paper enables a unified understanding of confinement across different gauge groups and matter contents, and explains why Casimir scaling fails in abelianized regimes as studied by Poppitz (2017).
