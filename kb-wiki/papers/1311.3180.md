# Recent progresses on the pseudospin symmetry in single particle resonant states

> The pseudospin symmetry (PSS) is a relativistic dynamical symmetry directly connected with the small component of the nucleon Dirac wave function. Much effort has been made to study this symmetry in bound states. Recently, a rigorous justification of the PSS in single particle resonant states was achieved by examining the asymptotic behaviors of the radial Dirac wave functions: The PSS in single particle resonant states in nuclei is conserved exactly when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign. Several issues related to the exact conservation and breaking mechanism of the PSS in single particle resonances were investigated by employing spherical square well potentials in which the PSS breaking part can be well isolated in the Jost function. A threshold effect in the energy splitting and an anomaly in the width splitting of pseudospin partners were found when the depth of the square well potential varies from zero to a finite value.

## Key claim

The pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in single particle resonant states is exactly conserved when the scalar potential \(S(r)\) and vector potential \(V(r)\) satisfy \(\Sigma(r) \equiv S(r)+V(r)=0\) (or more generally when \(d\Sigma(r)/dr=0\)), but in realistic nuclei this limit cannot be reached because no bound states exist under that condition, so the PSS is always broken in practice.

## Method

The authors derived a Schrodinger-like equation for the lower (small) component of the Dirac spinor and examined the Jost function \(\mathcal{J}_{\kappa}^{G}(p)\) for single particle resonant states. They employed spherical square well potentials for \(\Sigma(r)\) and \(\Delta(r)\) to isolate the PSS-breaking term in the Jost function, and traced the zeros of the Jost function on the complex energy plane to study the conservation and breaking of PSS in resonant states as the potential depth \(C\) was varied.

## Key numbers

- For square-well potentials with \(D=650\) MeV and \(R=7\) fm, when \(C=0\) the Jost function zeros for the PSS doublet \(h_{11/2}\) (\(\kappa=-6\)) and \(j_{13/2}\) (\(\kappa'=7\)) are identical, demonstrating exact PSS conservation.
- When \(C=-66\) MeV (with the same \(D=650\) MeV and \(R=7\) fm), the PSS is broken, as the zeros for \(h_{11/2}\) and \(j_{13/2}\) differ.
- For the PSS partners with pseudo orbital angular momentum \(\tilde{l}=2\) (\(p_{3/2}\) and \(f_{5/2}\)), as the potential depth \(C\) varies from zero to a finite value, the energy splitting first increases then decreases to a minimum when one level becomes a bound state, then increases again.
- For the same \(\tilde{l}=2\) partners, the width splitting first decreases from zero to a maximum negative value, then increases through zero to a maximum positive value, then decreases to zero.

## Context

This work extends the study of pseudospin symmetry from bound states (where it was established by Ginocchio (1997) and Meng (1998, 1999)) to single particle resonant states in the continuum. The authors provide a rigorous justification of PSS conservation in resonances, building on earlier numerical investigations by Guo (2005, 2006) and others. The results enable systematic study of PSS breaking mechanisms in resonant states and open questions about the threshold effect in energy splitting and the anomaly in width splitting of pseudospin partners.
