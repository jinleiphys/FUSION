# Electric $1^+$ state below nuclear scissors

> The solution of time dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations by the Wigner function moments method predicts four low-lying $1^+$ states. Three of them are known as various scissors modes. Fourth state is disposed below all scissors modes and has the electrical nature. It is found that it represents one of three branches of $2^+$ state which can exist in spherical nuclei and which is split in deformed nuclei. It is discovered, that the antiferromagnetic properties of nuclei lead to the splitting of $2^+$ states already at the zero deformation.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a fourth low-lying $1^+$ state below all three known scissors modes, which has electric rather than magnetic nature; this state is one of three branches of a $2^+$ state that splits in deformed nuclei, and the splitting of $2^+$ states already occurs at zero deformation due to the antiferromagnetic properties of nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations using the Wigner function moments (WFM) method, integrating over phase space with weights of quadrupole type to obtain dynamical equations for collective variables. The microscopic Hamiltonian includes a harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit potential plus separable quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions. Calculations are performed for the nucleus $^{164}$Dy, and the equations are linearized in the small-amplitude approximation and solved for isoscalar and isovector variables with $\mu=0,1,2$.

## Key numbers

- The lowest $1^+$ state has energy 1.47 MeV (coupled equations) with $B(E2)=25.44$ W.u. and $B(M1)=0.17$ $\mu_N^2$ for $^{164}$Dy.
- In the decoupled case, the same state appears at 1.29 MeV with $B(E2)=43.60$ W.u. and $B(M1)=0.02$ $\mu_N^2$.
- The three scissors modes appear at energies 2.20, 2.87, and 3.59 MeV in the coupled calculation for $^{164}$Dy.
- The isoscalar GQR branch for $\mu=1$ appears at 10.92 MeV with $B(E2)=50.37$ W.u., and the isovector GQR branch at 21.26 MeV with $B(E2)=21.60$ W.u.
- Including antiferromagnetic effects ($L_{10}^-$(eq) = $22i\hbar$) splits the $2^+$ state in the spherical limit into branches at 1.42 MeV ($\mu=0$), 1.35 MeV ($\mu=1$), and 1.14 MeV ($\mu=2$).
- The nuclear level spacing of approximately 0.1 MeV corresponds to an equivalent magnetic field strength of about 1 TT ($10^{16}$ Gauss), comparable to magnetar fields.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous WFM-based studies of nuclear scissors modes (Balbutsev and Molodtsova, 2018, 2022) by analyzing the nature of a previously predicted low-lying electric $1^+$ state. The three scissors modes (isovector spin-scalar, isovector spin-vector, and isoscalar spin-vector) were established in earlier work by the same group. The paper demonstrates that the electric $1^+$ state is a branch of a $2^+$ state that splits due to deformation and nuclear antiferromagnetism, a phenomenon analogous to antiferromagnetic splitting in condensed matter (Gurevich; Akhiezer). The results enable future experimental searches for this electric mode below the scissors region and further theoretical study of the coupling between shape and spin degrees of freedom in deformed nuclei.
