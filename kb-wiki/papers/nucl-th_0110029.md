# Formation of superheavy elements in heavy-ion collisions

> The cold fusion reactions related to 208Pb and 209Bi targets leading to superheavy elements (SHE) with Z=104-112 have been successfully considered in our model recently. Here we briefly discuss this model and extend our consideration to fusion reactions between similar target and projectile. The reactions between weakly deformed target close to Pb and 76Ge, 82Se projectiles are also studied. The available experimental cross-sections are well described. The nucleus-nucleus interaction potential for reactions leading to SHEs are shortly discussed.

# Formation of superheavy elements in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The model describes measured excitation functions for cold fusion reactions producing superheavy elements with Z=104-112, and can be extended to nearly symmetric reactions and reactions with weakly deformed targets, provided that both low-energy surface vibrations and neutron transfer channels are included simultaneously. The model's success depends on two fitting parameters, and the description of nearly symmetric reactions requires an assumed value for the inner barrier (either 6 MeV or 14 MeV) because exact quantum dynamical shape evolution calculations are not yet available.

## Method
The formation of superheavy elements is modeled in three stages: (i) capture of two spherical nuclei with inclusion of low-energy 2+ and 3- surface vibrations and neutron transfer channels; (ii) shape evolution from two touching nuclei to a spherical or near-spherical compound nucleus, described using a shape parametrization with deformation parameters β₂ and β₄; and (iii) survival of the excited compound nucleus during neutron evaporation and gamma-ray emission competing with fission, with a reduction of the fission barrier due to diminishing shell effects at higher excitation energy. For nearly symmetric reactions, the inner barrier B_Sph is treated as a parameter set to either 6 MeV or 14 MeV to account for competition between compound nucleus formation and quasi-fission.

## Key numbers
- Excitation functions for reactions producing elements Z=104-112 are described, including ⁵⁸Fe + ²⁰⁸Pb → ²⁶⁵Hs + n, ⁵⁰Ti + ²⁰⁸Pb → ²⁵⁷Rf + n, and ⁶²,⁶⁴Ni + ²⁰⁷,²⁰⁸,²¹⁰Pb → element 110 + n
- For the reaction ⁵⁰Ti + ²⁰⁸Pb → ²⁵⁷Rf + n, the threshold of the 2n evaporation channel is near 186 MeV
- The inner barrier B_Sph is set to either 6 MeV (near the adiabatic fission barrier) or 14 MeV (simulating stronger quasi-fission competition) for nearly symmetric reactions
- The cross section for the symmetric reaction ¹¹⁰Pd + ¹¹⁰Pd → ²²⁰U at lowest energy is close to 1 nb
- The touching point distance for ⁷⁰Zr + ²⁰⁸Pb is approximately 12 fm, using r₀ = 1.2 fm

## Context
This work extends the author's previous model (Denisov, 2000) that successfully described cold fusion reactions with ²⁰⁸Pb and ²⁰⁹Bi targets leading to elements Z=104-112. The paper extends this approach to nearly symmetric reactions such as ¹³⁰Xe + ¹³⁶Xe → ²⁶⁵Hs + n and reactions with ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se projectiles on targets slightly lighter than lead. The study also compares ion-ion interaction potentials calculated using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with various Skyrme force parameter sets (Sk3, SkM*, SkP, SLy4) against analytical parametrizations such as the Bass model and proximity approximations, highlighting the uncertainty in the potential near the touching point for very heavy nuclei. The work enables experimental tests of predicted excitation functions for symmetric reactions and reactions with Ge and Se projectiles.
