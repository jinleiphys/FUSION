# Excited bottomonia in quark-gluon plasma from lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice QCD study of up to $3S$ and $2P$ bottomonia at non-zero temperatures. Correlation functions of bottomonia were computed using novel bottomonium operators and a variational technique, within the lattice non-relativistic QCD framework. We analyzed the bottomonium correlation functions based on simple physically-motivated spectral functions. We found evidence of sequential in-medium modifications, in accordance with the sizes of the bottomonium states.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD study of in-medium modifications of up to $3S$ and $2P$ bottomonium states, finding evidence for sequential thermal broadening that follows the hierarchy of the states' sizes, but the analysis relies on simple physically-motivated spectral function models rather than direct extraction of spectral features.

## Method

The authors used lattice non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) with tree-level tadpole-improved Lagrangian accurate up to order $v^4$, including $v^6$ spin-dependent terms, on $48^3 \times 12$ lattices with $2+1$-flavor gauge configurations from the HotQCD collaboration. They constructed novel extended meson operators in Coulomb gauge using shape-functions obtained by solving the discretized Schrodinger equation with a Cornell potential, then applied a variational analysis via the generalized eigenvalue problem to obtain optimized operators for 1S, 1P, 2S, 2P, and 3S bottomonium states. The in-medium properties were studied by analyzing continuum-subtracted effective masses and fitting them with Gaussian and three-peak spectral function models.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used: $a = 0.1088$, $0.0951$, $0.0825$, $0.0655$, $0.0493$ fm, corresponding to temperatures $T = 151$, $173$, $199$, $251$, $334$ MeV
- Vacuum mass differences (in MeV) relative to 1S spin-averaged mass: $\Upsilon(3S)$ at $906.0(25.0)(5.2)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$ at $582.7(9.8)(3.4)$, $h_b(1P)$ at $454.5(4.7)(2.6)$, $\chi_{b0}(1P)$ at $421.3(4.7)(2.4)$
- 2S hyperfine splitting: $24.1(1.0)(0.1)$ MeV; 3S hyperfine splitting: $13.4(6.2)(0.1)$ MeV
- Predicted mass of $\eta_b(3S)$: $10341.8 \pm 6.2$ MeV
- For $T \gtrsim 200$ MeV, $\Gamma_{3S} \gtrsim M_{3S} - M_{2S}$ and $\Gamma_{2P} \gtrsim M_{2P} - M_{1P}$, indicating states become indistinguishable

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of in-medium quarkonium that were almost entirely focused on ground states of S- and P-wave bottomonia (Aarts 2010, Aarts 2011, Aarts 2012, Aarts 2013, Aarts 2014, Kim 2014, Kim 2018) and charmonia (Umeda 2002, Datta 2002, Karsch 2002, Datta 2003, Asakawa 2003, Jakovac 2006, Ohno 2011, Ding 2012, Ding 2017). The authors previously explored extended meson operators for 1S and 1P states (Larsen 2019), but here introduce novel operators that enable access to excited states up to 3S and 2P for the first time. The results enable direct comparison with experimental observations of sequential $\Upsilon(nS)$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions (Chatrchyan 2011, Chatrchyan 2012, Khachatryan 2016, Sirunyan 2017, Wang 2019) and suggest that for $T \gtrsim 200$ MeV it will become difficult to individually identify the 2S, 3S, 1P, and 2P states in experimental line shapes.
