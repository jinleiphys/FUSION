# Hadron resonance gas EoS and the fluidity of matter produced in HIC

> We study the equation of state (EoS) of the hot and dense hadron gas by incorporating the excluded volume corrections into the ideal hadron resonance gas model (HRG). The total hadron mass spectrum of the model is the sum of discrete mass spectrum consisting all the experimentally known hadrons and the exponentially rising continuous Hagedorn states. We confront the EoS of the model with lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) results at finite baryon chemical potential. We find that this modified HRG model reproduce the LQCD results up to T = 160 MeV at zero as well as finite baryon chemical potential. We further estimate the shear viscosity coefficient within ambit of this model in the context of heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, even when separately augmented with either excluded volume corrections or a continuous Hagedorn mass spectrum, cannot reproduce lattice QCD results; only when both excluded volume corrections and Hagedorn states are included simultaneously does the model match lattice QCD data up to T = 160 MeV at zero and finite baryon chemical potential.

## Method
The authors construct a modified excluded volume HRG (MEHRG) model whose total hadron mass spectrum is the sum of a discrete part (all experimentally known hadrons up to 2.225 GeV) and a continuous exponential Hagedorn density of states. Repulsive interactions are incorporated via a uniform hard-core excluded volume correction (radius r_h = 0.4 fm) to the ideal gas partition function. The shear viscosity coefficient is then estimated for the multicomponent hadron gas using the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, with an averaged relaxation time computed from constant cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The model reproduces lattice QCD results up to T = 160 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential and at finite baryon chemical potential (μ_B = 300 MeV).
- The discrete hadron mass spectrum includes all hadrons and resonances up to a cut-off of Λ_M = 2.011 GeV for mesons and Λ_B = 2.225 GeV for baryons.
- The Hagedorn mass spectrum is taken as ρ_HS = A exp(m/T_H) with A = 0.4 GeV⁻¹; the Hagedorn temperature is set to T_H = 198 MeV at μ_B = 0 and T_H = 188 MeV at μ_B = 300 MeV.
- The uniform hard-core radius for all hadrons is r_h = 0.4 fm.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s decreases with temperature and approaches the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of 1/(4π) at high temperature.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Vovchenko (2014) from zero baryon chemical potential to finite baryon chemical potential, confirming that both excluded volume corrections and Hagedorn states are necessary to match lattice QCD thermodynamics. The shear viscosity estimates are compared with results from the Chapman-Enskog method (Denicol, 2013), the scaled hadron masses and coupling (SHMC) model (Khvorostukhin, 2010), the excluded volume HRG model (Kadam, 2015), and chiral perturbation theory (Fernandez-Fraile, 2009). The findings enable improved hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions by providing an equation of state and transport coefficients that are consistent with lattice QCD up to the QCD transition temperature.
