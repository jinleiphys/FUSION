# Color Instabilities in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> When the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) - a system of deconfined quarks and gluons - is in a nonequilibrium state, it is usually unstable with respect to color collective modes. The instabilities, which are expected to strongly influence dynamics of the QGP produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, are extensively discussed under the assumption that the plasma is weakly coupled. We begin by presenting the theoretical approaches to study the QGP, which include: field theory methods based on the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism, classical and kinetic theories, and fluid techniques. The dispersion equations, which give the spectrum of plasma collective excitations, are analyzed in detail. Particular attention is paid to a momentum distribution of plasma constituents which is obtained by deforming an isotropic momentum distribution. Mechanisms of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic instabilities are explained in terms of elementary physics. The Nyquist analysis, which allows one to determine the number of solutions of a dispersion equation without explicitly solving it, and stability criteria are also discussed. We then review various numerical approaches - purely classical or quantum - to simulate the temporal evolution of an unstable quark-gluon plasma. The dynamical role of instabilities in the processes of plasma equilibration is analyzed.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches including field theory methods based on the Keldysh-Schwinger formalism, classical and kinetic theories, and fluid techniques. They analyze dispersion equations for plasma collective excitations, focusing on momentum distributions obtained by deforming an isotropic distribution, and explain mechanisms of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic instabilities. They then review numerical approaches, purely classical or quantum, to simulate the temporal evolution of an unstable QGP, including Wong-Yang-Mills simulations on three-dimensional spatial lattices with the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For weak initial fields in an Abelian anisotropic plasma with energy density on the order of \(0.1\, m_\infty^4/g^2\) (simulation parameters: \(L=5\) fm, \(p_h=16\) GeV, \(g^2\,n_g=20\) fm\(^{-3}\), and \(m_\infty=0.1\) GeV), the energy densities related to transverse magnetic and electric fields exponentially grow and then saturate.
- In the non-Abelian SU(2) case with simulation parameters \(L=5\) fm, \(p_{\rm hard}=16\) GeV, \(g^2\,n_g=10/\)fm\(^3\), \(m_\infty=0.1\) GeV, the energy densities of both electric and magnetic fields show rapid growth and then settle to constant values over the time interval \(t\le 30 \,m^{-1}_\infty\).
- The energy density contained in the fields at late times increases by a factor of 1.5 when going from a \(16^3\) to \(32^3\) to \(64^3\) lattice with the same physical size \(L\).
- For the Gaussian distribution example, \(f(\omega=0) < 0\) and there is an unstable mode for sufficiently small wave vectors if \(\sigma_x^2 < \sigma_z^2\).
- A power-law fit \(t^\alpha\) to the late-time evolution of \(\langle p_z^2 \rangle\) yields \(\alpha \approx 1\), corresponding to a random walk in momentum space.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on plasma instabilities in QCD, building on earlier theoretical analyses such as the Nyquist method from Kra73 and the Penrose criterion first applied to the QGP by Mrowczynski (1994). It discusses numerical simulations by groups including Hu (1996), Moore (1997), Bass (1998), Krasnitz (1998-2003), Dumitru (2005-2006), and others, and it enables future studies of QGP equilibration dynamics, particularly the role of instabilities in isotropization and the limitations of classical approaches when the separation of scales between particles and fields breaks down.
