# Effective Field Theory for Short-Range Forces

> The method of effective field theories (EFTs) is developed for the scattering of two particles at wavelengths which are large compared to the range of their interaction. It is shown that the renormalized EFT is equivalent to the effective range expansion, to a Schroedinger equation with a pseudo-potential, and to an energy expansion of a generic boundary condition at the origin. The roles of regulators and potentials are also discussed. These ideas are exemplified in a toy model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) for short-range forces, when properly renormalized, is equivalent to the effective range expansion, a Schrödinger equation with a pseudo-potential, and an energy expansion of a generic boundary condition at the origin; however, this equivalence holds only under specific power-counting schemes that depend on whether the underlying short-range dynamics is natural, fine-tuned to produce a shallow bound state, or fine-tuned to produce a shallow amplitude zero.

## Method

The author constructs a non-relativistic EFT for two identical, stable spinless particles at energies much smaller than their mass, internal excitation energy, and the range of their interaction, using a Lagrangian with contact interactions containing an arbitrary number of derivatives. The two-particle T-matrix is computed as a sum of bubble diagrams, regularized with a cut-off scale, and renormalized by absorbing cut-off-dependent terms into renormalized parameters; three distinct power-counting regimes are analyzed: natural (single scale M), unnatural with a shallow S-wave bound state (two scales M and aleph), and the case of a shallow amplitude zero.

## Key numbers

- For a natural EFT, the loop expansion parameter is m C_0^(R) Q / (4 pi) ~ Q/M, and the derivative expansion parameter is C_2^(R) Q^2 / C_0^(R) ~ (Q/M)^2.
- For an unnatural EFT with a shallow bound state, the S-wave scattering length a_0 = m C_0^(R) / (4 pi) scales as a_0 = O(aleph^{-1}), while the effective range r_0 = O(M^{-1}) from Eq. (seffran).
- The binding momentum kappa for the shallow bound state is given by kappa = 4 pi / (m C_0^(R)) (1 + 2 C_2^(R)/C_0^(R) kappa^2 + O(kappa^3)), with kappa ~ aleph.
- The P-wave scattering volume is a_1 = m C_2'^(R) / (6 pi), scaling with M as in the natural scenario.
- The shape parameter P_0 r_0^3 = (16 pi / (m C_0^(R))) ((C_2^(R)/C_0^(R))^2 - C_4^(R)/C_0^(R) + C_2^(R)/(4 m^2 C_0^(R)) + 3/(32 m^4)), which requires correlations among coefficients to avoid M/aleph enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Ordonez et al. (1994), clarifying the relationship between EFT and older techniques such as the effective range expansion of Bethe (1949), pseudo-potentials of Fermi (1936) and Huang (1957), and boundary conditions of Peierls (1958) and Breit (1957). The power counting for shallow bound states was subsequently rediscovered within specific regularization schemes by Kaplan et al. (1998) and Gegelia (1998), and the equivalence to the effective range expansion was confirmed using renormalization group methods by Birse et al. (1998). The results enable systematic treatment of low-energy nuclear reactions and have been applied to the three-nucleon problem by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998).
