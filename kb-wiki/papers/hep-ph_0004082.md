# Soft pions at high energy and its phenomenological implications

> The soft pion theorem in the inclusive reaction at high energy applied to the current induced reaction is explained briefly. A characteristic aspect of this theorem is the charge asymmetry produced by the pole terms in the soft pion limit. The pion charge asymmetry in the central region in the target-virtual-photon center of the mass (CM) frame of the semi-inclusive electroproduction and the contribution to the Gottfried sum are illustrated as examples.

# Soft pions at high energy and its phenomenological implications

## Key claim
The soft pion theorem, traditionally considered applicable only at low energies, can be applied to inclusive reactions at high energy if Feynman scaling holds, but the soft-pion limit cannot be interchanged with the manipulation to obtain the discontinuity of the reaction; the limit must be taken first in the reaction itself.

## Method
The author applies the soft pion theorem to semi-inclusive electroproduction using the light cone current algebra at a starting scale \(Q_0^2\), taking care of singlet versus non-singlet and charge conjugation even versus odd quantities. The hadronic tensor is obtained by squaring the amplitude in the soft pion limit, which includes pole terms from initial and final nucleons and a term from the current anticommutation relation on the null plane. The charge asymmetry in the central region of the target-virtual-photon CM frame is computed and compared with experimental data from the Harvard-Cornell group, and the soft pion contribution to the Gottfried sum is estimated using valence quark distribution functions at \(Q_0^2 = 4\;\text{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers
- The theoretical value of the charge asymmetry in semi-inclusive electroproduction is roughly \(0.15 \sim 0.18\) with weak \(x_B\) dependence, while the experimental value (Harvard-Cornell group) for transverse momentum \(|\vec{k}^{\bot}|^2 \leq m_\pi^2\) is \(0.28 \pm 0.05\) in the region \(0 < x_F < 0.1\), gradually decreasing above \(x_F = 0.1\).
- About \(40\%\) of the NMC deficit in the Gottfried sum comes from the region where the kaon momentum in the laboratory frame is above \(4\;\text{GeV}\).
- The soft pion contribution to the Gottfried sum is estimated to be about \(-0.04 \sim -0.02\), with the main contribution coming from the small \(x_B\) region.
- The multiplicity of nucleon and antinucleon is parameterized as \(\langle n \rangle = a \log_e s + 1\) with \(a = 0.2\), based on proton and antiproton multiplicity in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation from the DELPHI collaboration.

## Context
This work extends the soft pion theorem for inclusive reactions originally formulated by Sakai and Yamada (1970s) and builds on the author's own fixed-mass sum rule approach and perturbative approach developed over the 1980s and 1990s. The modified Gottfried sum rule derived from the current anticommutation relation on the null plane explains the NMC deficit almost model independently, in contrast to mesonic models based on \(\pi NN\) and \(\pi N\Delta\) processes which account for only about half of the deficit. The results suggest a dynamical mechanism for flavor asymmetry at medium and high energy that has been overlooked, and the combined analysis of sum rules and soft pions provides insight into sea quark distribution functions and the vacuum structure of the hadron.
