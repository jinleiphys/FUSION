# Hadron form factors and large-Nc phenomenology

> The half width rule provides a way to consider 1/Nc corrections to hadronic models containing resonances. Consequences of such ideas for hadron form factors and Regge trajectories are explored, with special emphasis on the possibility to describe the spectrum of light and heavy unflavored vector mesons in a universal way.

## Key claim

The half-width rule (HWR) provides a way to estimate 1/N_c corrections to hadronic models containing resonances by treating resonance masses as random variables with a dispersion given by the width, rather than assuming the narrow-resonance limit; this allows error estimates for form factors and Regge trajectories that are otherwise absent in large-N_c phenomenology.

## Method

The authors apply the half-width rule to meson-dominance parametrizations of hadron form factors and to linear Regge trajectories for light and heavy unflavored vector mesons. For Regge trajectories, they perform chi-squared minimization fits where each resonance is weighted by its half-width squared, using the updated Particle Data Tables listings. They also test a proposed universal radial Regge trajectory formula (M_n - 2m)^2 = a(n+b) for light and heavy vector mesons, fitting with and without the ground state included.

## Key numbers

- Weighted average slope of radial Regge trajectories for light unflavored mesons: mu^2 = 1.35(4) GeV^2
- Weighted average slope of angular-momentum Regge trajectories: beta^2 = 1.16(4) GeV^2
- Joint fit result for light unflavored mesons: M^2_X(n,J) = (-1.25(4) + 1.38(4) n + 1.12(4) J) GeV^2, showing a 4.5 standard deviation difference between radial and angular-momentum slopes
- Radial fit for kaon sector: mu^2_K = 1.22(21) GeV^2 for K and 1.12(21) GeV^2 for K*
- Angular-momentum fit for kaon sector: beta^2_K = 1.36(6) GeV^2 for K and 1.19(7) GeV^2 for K*
- Universal radial trajectory fit (ground state not included): a = 1.03(19) GeV^2, b = 0.93(50) GeV^2, with constituent quark masses m_c = 1.17(12) GeV and m_b = 4.30(10) GeV
- Universal radial trajectory fit (ground state included): a = 0.716(2) GeV^2, b = 0.153(1) GeV^2, yielding a very bad fit

## Context

This work extends the half-width rule approach previously developed by the authors (Masjuan and Ruiz Arriola, 2012; Masjuan, Ruiz Arriola, and Broniowski, 2012) to hadron form factors and Regge trajectories. It contradicts the assumption of universal radial and angular-momentum slopes (M^2 ~ n+J) proposed by Afonin (2006), finding a statistically significant difference of 4.5 standard deviations. The analysis of universal radial trajectories for light and heavy vector mesons tests the proposal of Afonin and Pusenkov (2013), showing that universality only holds when the ground state is excluded from the fit, and that the HWR provides proper error estimates missing in the original work.
