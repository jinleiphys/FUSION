# Properties of Exotic Matter for Heavy Ion Searches

> We examine the properties of both forms of strange matter, small lumps of strange quark matter (strangelets) and of strange hadronic matter (Metastable Exotic Multihypernuclear Objects: MEMOs) and their relevance for present and future heavy ion searches. The strong and weak decays are discussed separately to distinguish between long-lived and short-lived candidates where the former ones are detectable in present heavy ion experiments while the latter ones in future heavy ion experiments, respectively. We find some long-lived strangelet candidates which are highly negatively charged with a mass to charge ratio like a anti deuteron (M/Z=-2) but masses of A=10 to 16. We predict also many short-lived candidates, both in quark and in hadronic form, which can be highly charged. Purely hyperonic nuclei are bound and have a negative charge while carrying a positive baryon number. We demonstrate also that multiply charmed exotics (charmlets) might be bound and can be produced at future heavy ion colliders.

# Properties of Exotic Matter for Heavy Ion Searches

## Key claim
The paper predicts that strangelets surviving strong and weak hadronic decay are highly negatively charged (Z/A approximately -0.5) with mass-to-charge ratios similar to an antideuteron (M/Z approximately -2), contrary to the conventional expectation that strangelets have a small positive charge. This result depends on the combined effect of strong and weak hadronic decay shifting surviving strangelets to very high strangeness fractions, and holds only if strange quark matter is at least metastable (bag constant B^{1/4} less than about 180 MeV).

## Method
The authors calculate strangelet properties using the MIT bag model with shell mode filling for all combinations of up, down, and strange quarks up to baryon number A=30, then test stability against strong decay (emission of baryons and mesons including multiple hadron emission) and weak hadronic decay (single baryon and meson emission with strangeness change of Delta S = +/- 1). For Metastable Exotic Multihypernuclear Objects (MEMOs), they use an extended relativistic mean field model to compute light hyperonic systems up to a closed p-shell and check for metastability against strong decay. Charmlet properties are studied using a modified bag model including colorelectric and colormagnetic interactions for systems up to A=4.

## Key numbers
- Long-lived strangelet candidates found at A=10 with Z=-4, A=12 with Z=-6, and A=16 with Z=-10 for multiple bag parameter choices (B^{1/4}=145-170 MeV, m_s=150-280 MeV)
- The charge fraction of these long-lived strangelets is Z/A approximately -0.5, with mass-to-charge ratio M/Z approximately -2
- No long-lived strangelet candidates found for bag constant B^{1/4}=180 MeV or higher
- Shell effects in strangelets are on the order of 100 MeV per baryon number
- The quark alpha (A=6, Z=0) was found to be unbound by 0.9 GeV when colormagnetic interactions are included
- MEMOs and short-lived strangelets cover charge fractions between Z/A = +/- 0.6
- Charmlets can be bound by 100 to 200 MeV in SU(3) flavor symmetry, with charges ranging from Z=-4 to Z=+4
- Production rate for double charmed charmlets estimated at about 10^{-2} per event at RHIC; triple charmed at 10^{-5} per event
- Charmlet lifetime estimated at tau approximately 10^{-13} s (similar to charmed hadrons)
- About ten charm-anticharm pairs expected in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV at RHIC

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of strange quark matter by Farhi and Jaffe (1984) and of Metastable Exotic Multihypernuclear Objects by Schaffner et al. (1992, 1993, 1994), and contradicts the conventional picture that strangelets have a small positive charge. The finding that surviving strangelets are highly negatively charged has serious implications for heavy ion searches at experiments such as those described by Majka (1995) and the E864 and E886 collaborations, which had focused on candidates with small charge or high mass. The paper enables future searches at RHIC and LHC for both short-lived strange matter (with lifetimes of about 10^{-10} s) and multiply charmed exotics (charmlets) using silicon vertex detectors at STAR and ALICE.
