# Dilepton production from magnetized quark matter with an anomalous magnetic moment of the quarks using a three-flavor PNJL model

> Dilepton production from hot, dense and magnetized quark matter is studied using the three-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in which the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks is also taken into consideration. This is done by first evaluating the thermo-magnetic spectral function of the vector current correlator employing the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism. The constituent quark mass which goes as an input in the expression of the dilepton production rate (DPR), has been calculated using the three-flavor PNJL model employing Pauli-Villiars (PV) regularization. The obtained constituent quark mass being strongly dependent on the temperature, density, magnetic field and AMM of the quarks, captures the effect of `strong' interactions specifically around the (pseudo) chiral and confinement-deconfinement phase transition regions. The analytic structure of the spectral function in the complex energy plane has been analyzed in detail and a non-trivial Landau cut is found in the physical kinematic domains resulting from the scattering of the Landau quantized quark/antiquark with the photon which is purely a finite magnetic field effect. Due to the emergence of the Landau cut along with the usual unitary cut, the DPR is found to be largely enhanced in the low invariant mass region. Owing to the magnetic field and AMM dependence of the thresholds of these cuts, we find that the kinematically forbidden gap between the Unitary and Landau cuts vanishes at sufficiently high temperature, density and magnetic field leading to the generation of a continuous spectrum of dilepton emission over the whole invariant mass region. In order to see the effects of strangeness and confinement-deconfinement, the rates are compared with the three-flavor NJL and the two-flavor NJL and PNJL models.

## Key claim

The dilepton production rate (DPR) from hot, dense, and magnetized quark matter is significantly enhanced in the low invariant mass region due to the emergence of a non-trivial Landau cut alongside the usual unitary cut, and the kinematically forbidden gap between these cuts vanishes at sufficiently high temperature, density, and magnetic field, leading to a continuous spectrum over the whole invariant mass region, but this result depends on including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks and using the three-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with an infinite number of Landau levels.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermo-magnetic spectral function of the vector current correlator using the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism, with the constituent quark mass obtained from the three-flavor PNJL model employing Pauli-Villars regularization. The analytic structure of the spectral function in the complex energy plane was analyzed to identify the thresholds of the unitary and Landau cuts, and the DPR was computed for arbitrary magnetic field strength by summing over an infinite number of quark Landau levels. The results were compared with those from the three-flavor NJL model and the two-flavor NJL and PNJL models to isolate the effects of strangeness and confinement-deconfinement.

## Key numbers

* The DPR is enhanced in the low invariant mass region (below approximately 1 GeV in invariant mass) due to the Landau cut, which is a purely finite magnetic field effect.
* The constituent quark mass depends strongly on temperature, density, magnetic field, and AMM, and is calculated using the PNJL model with Pauli-Villars regularization.
* The kinematic threshold for the unitary cut is at \(q^2 = 4M_f^2\) for each flavor \(f\), where \(M_f\) is the constituent quark mass.
* The kinematic threshold for the Landau cut is at \(|q^0| < |\vec{q}|\) (space-like virtual photon), modified by the magnetic field and AMM.
* The gap between the unitary and Landau cuts vanishes at sufficiently high temperature, density, and magnetic field (no specific numerical thresholds given for temperature, density, or magnetic field strength).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production from magnetized quark matter using the two-flavor NJL model with AMM (Ghosh, 2020) to the three-flavor PNJL model, incorporating strangeness and confinement-deconfinement effects via the Polyakov loop. It also builds on calculations using the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (Ghosh, 2018) and earlier work using the Ritus formalism (Sadooghi, 2016) and real time formalism (Bandyopadhyay, 2016, 2017). The inclusion of an infinite number of Landau levels removes the need for strong or weak magnetic field approximations used in prior literature. This enables more realistic modeling of dilepton emission from quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where transient magnetic fields of order \(10^{18}\) Gauss are generated.
