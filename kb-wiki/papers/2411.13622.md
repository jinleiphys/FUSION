# Probing small neutron skin variations in isotope pairs by hyperon-antihyperon production in antiproton--nucleus interactions

> We propose a new method to study the evolution of the neutron periphery between different isotopes by considering antiproton--nucleus interactions close to the production threshold of $Λ\overline{Λ}$ and $Σ^-\overline{Λ}$ pairs. At low energies, $Λ\overline{Λ}$ pairs are produced in $\overline{\text{p}} +\text{p}$ collisions, while $Σ^-\overline{Λ}$ pairs can only be produced in $\overline{\text{p}} +\text{n}$ interactions. Within a simple geometrical picture we show that the double ratio for the production of $Σ^-\overline{Λ}$ and $Λ\overline{Λ}$ pairs for two different isotopes are related to the variation of the neutron skin thickness between the two nuclei. Performing high statistics calculations with the Gießen Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model for several isotope pairs covering a wide range of elements we verify a strong correlation between the double ratio from the full transport simulations and the schematic model. This correlation enables us to quantify the potential of the proposed method for precise studies of neutron skin variations in isotope chains.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the double ratio of $\Sigma^-\overline{\Lambda}$ to $\Lambda\overline{\Lambda}$ production yields in antiproton-nucleus interactions for two different isotopes is strongly correlated with the variation of neutron skin thickness between those isotopes, provided the antiproton beam energy is close to the production thresholds. The method does not require absolute cross section measurements and cancels many experimental uncertainties, but the correlation relies on the simplifying assumption that the absorption of produced antihyperons is similar for both channels.

## Method

The authors used the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to simulate antiproton-nucleus interactions at an incident momentum of 2.4 GeV/c for several isotope pairs (Ne, Ca, Ni, Xe). They compared the double ratio of $\Sigma^-\overline{\Lambda}$ to $\Lambda\overline{\Lambda}$ pair production yields from these full transport simulations with predictions from a simple geometrical model that relates the double ratio to the integrated neutron skin density. The initial nuclear density distributions in GiBUU were generated using relativistic mean field (RMF) parameter sets (NL1, NL3, NL3*).

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca with the NL1 RMF parameter set, the GiBUU double ratio is $1.799 \pm 0.018$, compared to 1.225 from the simple zero-impact-parameter model and 1.683 from the finite-impact-parameter schematic model.
- For $^{58}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni with the NL3 RMF parameter set, the GiBUU double ratio is $1.402 \pm 0.030$.
- For $^{129}$Xe and $^{136}$Xe with the NL3 RMF parameter set, the GiBUU double ratio is $1.169 \pm 0.025$, while the neutron skin difference between these isotopes is 0.069 fm.
- The interaction cross sections of $\overline{\text{p}}$+p and $\overline{\text{p}}$+n at 2.4 GeV/c are both about 55 mb, giving an interaction length of about 1.14 fm at normal nuclear density of 0.16 fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the use of antiproton probes for neutron skin studies, previously explored by Lenske (2007) using antiprotonic x-rays, by proposing a new observable based on hyperon-antihyperon pair production. The method addresses the tension between the PREX measurement for $^{208}$Pb and the CREX result for $^{48}$Ca, which disagree with model predictions from Horowitz (2014), Tagami (2022), and Hyun (2022). The approach enables future studies at the PANDA experiment to probe small neutron skin variations (as small as 0.01 fm) along isotope chains, which is difficult with parity-violating electron scattering due to limited statistics.
