# Uncertainty quantification for proton-proton fusion in chiral effective field theory

> We compute the $S$-factor of the proton-proton ($pp$) fusion reaction using chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and perform a rigorous uncertainty analysis of the results. We quantify the uncertainties due to (i) the computational method used to compute the $pp$ cross section in momentum space, (ii) the statistical uncertainties in the low-energy coupling constants of $χ$EFT, (iii) the systematic uncertainty due to the $χ$EFT cutoff, and (iv) systematic variations in the database used to calibrate the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We also examine the robustness of the polynomial extrapolation procedure, which is commonly used to extract the threshold $S$-factor and its energy-derivatives. By performing a statistical analysis of the polynomial fit of the energy-dependent $S$-factor at several different energy intervals, we eliminate a systematic uncertainty that can arise from the choice of the fit interval in our calculations. In addition, we explore the statistical correlations between the $S$-factor and few-nucleon observables such as the binding energies and point-proton radii of $^{2,3}$H and $^3$He as well as the $D$-state probability and quadrupole moment of $^2$H, and the $β$-decay of $^{3}$H. We find that, with the state-of-the-art optimization of the nuclear Hamiltonian, the statistical uncertainty in the threshold $S$-factor cannot be reduced beyond 0.7%.

## Key claim

The paper computes the proton-proton fusion S-factor using chiral effective field theory (χEFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and performs a rigorous uncertainty quantification, finding that the statistical uncertainty in the threshold S-factor cannot be reduced below 0.7% even with state-of-the-art optimization of the nuclear Hamiltonian. The abstract's claim of a "rigorous uncertainty analysis" is qualified by the fact that the calculation only includes S-wave initial state interactions and omits higher-order electromagnetic corrections proportional to α², which are estimated separately.

## Method

The authors computed the pp-fusion S-factor using χEFT at NNLO with consistently derived nuclear weak current operators up to O(Q³). They employed a family of 42 different NNLOsim interactions with regulator cutoffs Λ ranging from 450 to 600 MeV and six different truncations of the maximum scattering energy in the NN scattering database, using the Vincent-Phatak method to handle the Coulomb potential in momentum-space calculations. The S-factor was extrapolated to zero energy using a cubic polynomial fit across the energy interval 1-30 keV, and statistical uncertainties were propagated using covariance matrices of the low-energy constants (LECs) with a linear approximation via Jacobian vectors.

## Key numbers

- Threshold S-factor (including higher-order electromagnetic corrections): S_cor(0) = (4.047⁺⁰·⁰²⁴₋₀·⁰³²) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm²
- Threshold S-factor (without higher-order electromagnetic corrections): S(0) = (4.081⁺⁰·⁰²⁴₋₀·⁰³²) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm²
- Statistical uncertainty in S(0) due to LEC uncertainties: 0.009 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² (relative uncertainty ~0.2%)
- Statistical uncertainty in S(0) including g_A error: 0.019 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm²
- Uncertainty due to Vincent-Phatak matching procedure: 0.002 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² (~0.05%)
- Uncertainty due to polynomial fit interval choice: 0.0002 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² (~0.005%)
- Squared radial overlap Λ²(0) = 7.087 (range 7.064-7.101 across all 42 interactions)
- Two-body current contribution δ₂B = 0.43% (range 0.30%-0.52%)
- First logarithmic derivative S'(0)/S(0) = 10.84(2) MeV⁻¹
- Second logarithmic derivative S''(0)/S(0) = 317.8(13) MeV⁻²
- Estimated 0.84% reduction in S(0) from higher-order electromagnetic effects

## Context

This work extends the χEFT calculation of Marcucci et al. (2013), who obtained S = (4.030 ± 0.006) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² with a seven-times-smaller uncertainty based on simple range estimates from two different regulators, by providing a comprehensive uncertainty quantification using 42 systematically optimized NNLO interactions from Carlsson et al. (2015). The result is consistent with but slightly higher than the previously recommended value from Adelberger et al. (2010) of S = (4.01 ± 0.04) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm², and the analysis demonstrates that cutoff variation alone is insufficient for reliable uncertainty estimates, as the choice of scattering data energy range also significantly affects results. The correlation analysis between the S-factor and few-nucleon observables enables future combined fits that could reduce uncertainties in solar neutrino flux predictions.
