# Bulk spectral function sum rule in QCD-like theories with a holographic dual

> We derive the sum rule for the spectral function of the stress-energy tensor in the bulk (uniform dilatation) channel in a general class of strongly coupled field theories. This class includes theories holographically dual to a theory of gravity coupled to a single scalar field, representing the operator of the scale anomaly. In the limit when the operator becomes marginal, the sum rule coincides with that in QCD. Using the holographic model, we verify explicitly the cancellation between large and small frequency contributions to the spectral integral required to satisfy the sum rule in such QCD-like theories.

## Key claim

The paper derives a sum rule for the bulk (uniform dilatation) channel spectral function of the stress-energy tensor in strongly coupled field theories that have a holographic dual, and shows that in the marginal limit where the scalar operator dimension approaches 4, the sum rule coincides with the QCD sum rule but requires a delicate cancellation between high-frequency and intermediate-frequency contributions to the spectral integral.

## Method

The authors consider a general class of four-dimensional field theories with a scalar operator of dimension Δ₊ that breaks scale invariance, holographically dual to five-dimensional gravity coupled to a single scalar field (dilaton) with potential V(φ). They derive the sum rule analytically using operator product expansion and the holographic correspondence, then verify it numerically by solving the linearized Einstein equations for metric perturbations in the bulk channel, computing the spectral function ρ(ω) from the in-falling wave boundary condition at the black hole horizon, and evaluating the frequency integral of δTρ(ω)/ω.

## Key numbers

- The sum rule is given by (3s ∂/∂s − Δ₊)(ε − 3p) = (2/π) ∫₀^∞ δTρ(ω)/ω dω, where s is entropy density, ε is energy density, p is pressure, and Δ₊ is the scaling dimension of the operator.
- In the marginal limit Δ₊ → 4, the left-hand side is of order Δ₋³ while ρ ∼ Δ₋², where Δ₋ = 4 − Δ₊.
- The leading large-ω asymptotic of the spectral function is ρ₀(ω) = (c²/2κ²) [2πΔ₋²/Γ(2−Δ₋)²] (ω/2)^{4−2Δ₋}.
- The zero-temperature subtracted Green's function at infinite frequency is δT G_R(i∞) = Δ₋ (ε − 3p).

## Context

This work extends the shear channel sum rule derived by Romatschke and Son (2009) to the bulk channel, which is sensitive to scale violation rather than coupling strength. It generalizes the holographic analysis of Springer (2010) for the Chamblin-Reall dilaton potential to a broader class of theories where the speed of sound approaches the conformal value 1/√3 from below at high temperature, as shown by Hohler and Stephanov (2009). The results enable verification of sum rule consistency in QCD-like holographic models and demonstrate the cancellation mechanism between high- and low-frequency contributions that must occur in QCD.
