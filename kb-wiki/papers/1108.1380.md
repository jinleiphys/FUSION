# SU(2) Low-Energy Constants from Mixed-Action Lattice QCD

> An analysis of the pion mass and pion decay constant is performed using mixed-action Lattice QCD calculations with domain-wall valence quarks on ensembles of rooted, staggered n_f = 2+1 MILC configurations. Calculations were performed at two lattice spacings of b~0.125 fm and b~0.09 fm, at two strange quark masses, multiple light quark masses, and a number of lattice volumes. The ratios of light quark to strange quark masses are in the range 0.1 <= m_l / m_s <= 0.6, while pion masses are in the range 235 < m_π< 680 MeV. A two-flavor chiral perturbation theory analysis of the Lattice QCD calculations constrains the Gasser-Leutwyler coefficients bar{l}_3 and bar{l}_4 to be bar{l}_3 = 4.04(40)(+73-55) and bar{l}_4 = 4.30(51)(+84-60). All systematic effects in the calculations are explored, including those from the finite lattice space-time volume, the finite lattice spacing, and the finite fifth dimension in the domain-wall quark action. A consistency is demonstrated between a chiral perturbation theory analysis at fixed lattice spacing combined with a leading order continuum extrapolation, and the mixed-action chiral perturbation theory analysis which explicitly includes the leading order discretization effects. Chiral corrections to the pion decay constant are found to give f_π/ f = 1.062(26)(+42-40) where f is the decay constant in the chiral limit. The most recent scale setting by the MILC Collaboration yields a postdiction of f_π= 128.2(3.6)(+4.4-6.0)(+1.2-3.3) MeV at the physical pion mass.

## Key claim

The Gasser-Leutwyler coefficients \(\bar{l}_3\) and \(\bar{l}_4\) are constrained from mixed-action lattice QCD calculations using domain-wall valence quarks on rooted, staggered \(n_f = 2+1\) MILC configurations, with the analysis relying on two-flavor chiral perturbation theory to extrapolate from pion masses in the range \(235 < m_\pi < 680\) MeV down to the physical point, and the quoted uncertainties include statistical, fitting, and systematic errors from finite volume, finite lattice spacing, and the finite fifth dimension.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations at two lattice spacings (\(b \approx 0.125\) fm and \(b \approx 0.09\) fm) with multiple light quark masses and two strange quark masses, using domain-wall valence propagators on rooted, staggered MILC sea-quark ensembles. Pion masses and decay constants were extracted from correlation functions with antiperiodic temporal boundary conditions, and the results were analyzed with both fixed-lattice-spacing chiral perturbation theory combined with a leading-order continuum extrapolation and mixed-action chiral perturbation theory that explicitly includes leading-order discretization effects. Systematic uncertainties from finite volume, finite lattice spacing, and the residual chiral symmetry breaking in the domain-wall action were quantified by varying fit ranges and comparing ensembles with different volumes and fifth-dimension sizes.

## Key numbers

- \(\bar{l}_3 = 4.04(40)(^{+73}_{-55})\) from the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory analysis of the lattice QCD calculations
- \(\bar{l}_4 = 4.30(51)(^{+84}_{-60})\) from the same analysis
- \(f_\pi / f = 1.062(26)(^{+42}_{-40})\), where \(f\) is the pion decay constant in the chiral limit
- Postdiction of \(f_\pi = 128.2(3.6)(^{+4.4}_{-6.0})(^{+1.2}_{-3.3})\) MeV at the physical pion mass, using the MILC Collaboration scale setting with \(r_1 = 0.311(2)(^{+3}_{8})\) fm
- Pion masses in lattice units range from \(b m_\pi = 0.10160(22)(^{+21}_{-24})\) on the finest ensemble to \(b m_\pi = 0.37469(22)(^{+20}_{-22})\) on the coarsest ensemble
- Pion decay constants in lattice units range from \(b f_\pi = 0.0617(9)(^{+10}_{-13})\) to \(b f_\pi = 0.10749(13)(^{+33}_{-33})\)

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of the Gasser-Leutwyler coefficients by multiple lattice collaborations using \(n_f=2\), \(n_f=2+1\), and \(n_f=2+1+1\) calculations (Allton 2008, Noaki 2008, Aoki 2008, Baron 2009, Bazavov 2010), which were compiled into a review by Colangelo (2010). The mixed-action approach with domain-wall valence quarks on staggered sea configurations, initiated by the LHP Collaboration (Renner 2004, Edwards 2005, Walker-Loud 2008), provides an independent cross-check of systematic effects from different lattice discretizations. The results enable more precise predictions for other observables involving pions and kaons through chiral perturbation theory, and the detailed error analysis indicates that calculations at lighter pion masses are the most important systematic to address in future work.
