# Higher Partial Waves in an Effective Field Theory Approach to nd Scattering

> The phase shifts for the higher partial waves (l\ge 1) in the spin quartet and doublet channel of nd scattering at centre-of-mass energies up to 15 MeV are presented at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order in an effective field theory in which pions are integrated out. As available, the results agree with both phase shift analyses and potential model calculations.

## Key claim

The phase shifts for higher partial waves (l >= 1) in nd scattering are computed using a pion-less effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order, with the hedge that the results agree with phase shift analyses and potential model calculations only "as available" -- meaning the agreement is limited to channels and energies for which such comparisons exist, and the theory's breakdown scale is approximately the pion mass (around 100 MeV).

## Method

The authors use an EFT with pions integrated out, where nucleons are the only explicit degrees of freedom and interactions are described by contact terms. The two-nucleon sector is treated non-perturbatively using dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS), and the deuteron and di-baryon propagators are constructed to include effective range corrections. For the three-body nd system, the Faddeev equations are solved in the quartet and doublet channels for partial waves with l >= 1, with all parameters determined from NN scattering data and no three-body forces entering at the calculated orders.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts are presented for center-of-mass energies up to 15 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy is B = 2.225 MeV, corresponding to a binding momentum gamma_d = sqrt(M B) approximately 46 MeV
- The inverse scattering lengths are 1/a_t = -8.3 MeV (singlet channel) and 1/a_d = 36 MeV (triplet channel)
- The expansion parameter is estimated to be of order 1/3, so NLO calculations are expected to be accurate to about 10% and NNLO calculations to about 4%
- The theory's expected breakdown scale is Lambda approximately 100 MeV (the pion mass scale)

## Context

This work extends the pion-less EFT treatment of nd scattering from the S-wave channels (previously studied by Bedaque, Griesshammer, and collaborators in 1999) to higher partial waves. It builds on the two-nucleon EFT framework developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the three-body formalism established for the quartet S-wave by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998). The calculations provide a testing ground for applying EFT to unresolved problems like the Ay puzzle, and enable systematic, model-independent predictions for nd scattering in channels where three-body forces do not enter at the orders considered.
