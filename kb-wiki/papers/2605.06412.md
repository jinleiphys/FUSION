# GTMDs, orbital angular momentum, and pretzelosity

> The leading Generalized Transverse Momentum Dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) are studied in the bag model. The model description is shown to be theoretically consistent. The orbital angular momentum is studied in terms of the GTMD $F_{1,4}^q$ and Ji sum rule. Analytical proofs of the associated sum rules are given. A deeper relationship between orbital angular momentum and the pretzelosity TMD is established in this model.

## Key claim

The bag model provides a theoretically consistent description of the leading GTMDs, and a deeper relationship between orbital angular momentum and the pretzelosity TMD is established within this model framework, though the authors note that some models and QCD do not support this relation.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order GTMDs $F_{1,1}^q$, $F_{1,2}^q$, $F_{1,3}^q$, and $F_{1,4}^q$ in the MIT bag model, working in the large-$N_c$ limit to consistently satisfy the Ji sum rule. They evaluate the quark-quark correlator in the Breit frame using the bag model wave functions for massless quarks, extract the GTMDs by taking traces with Pauli matrices, and verify theoretical consistency through hermiticity properties, forward limits to TMDs, and integration to GPDs. Analytical proofs of the flavor, momentum, and Ji sum rules are provided using the bag model equation of motion and the virial theorem.

## Key numbers

* The bag model ground-state energy parameter is $\omega_0 \approx 2.04$, the lowest positive solution of $\omega = (1-\omega)\tan{\omega}$.
* The nucleon mass is fixed by $M = \frac{4}{3} N_c \epsilon_0$, where $\epsilon_0 = \omega_0/R$ and $N_c = 3$.
* The quark spin contributes $\sum_q 2S^q = 0.65$ (65%) to the nucleon spin, while the canonical orbital angular momentum contributes $\sum_q 2\LJM_z^q = 0.35$ (35%) in the bag model.
* The anomalous gravitomagnetic moment for the proton yields $B^u(0) = \frac{2}{3}$ and $B^d(0) = -\frac{2}{3}$, with the total $B(0) = 0$.

## Context

This work extends previous bag model studies of GTMDs by Courtoy (2016) and GPDs by Ji (1997), and builds on the framework of Meissner (2009) for GTMD definitions and Lorce (2011) for the relation between $F_{1,4}^q$ and canonical orbital angular momentum. The results enable further exploration of the connection between orbital angular momentum and the pretzelosity TMD, which was previously supported in a class of quark models by She (2009) and Avakian (2010) but not in QCD or some other models by Liu (2014) and Chakrabarti (2016).
