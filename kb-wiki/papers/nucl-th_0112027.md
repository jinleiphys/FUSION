# The $Žā$-meson and $ŽĆ$-$ŽĆ$ Correlation in Hot/Dense Medium: soft modes for chiral transition in QCD

> After a brief overview of the realization of chiral symmetry in hot and/or dense medium, the implications of the existence of a light scalar-isoscalar meson (which we call $Žā$ throughout this report) are discussed in the vacuum and in the medium. Special emphasis is put on its relation to the fluctuation of the chiral order parameter $<\bar{q}q>$. In the vacuum, the $Žā$-meson is an elusive resonance corresponding to a pole deep in the second Riemann sheet of the $ŽĆ$-$ŽĆ$ scattering matrix in the $I$=$J$=0 channel. This is because the the amplitude fluctuation of $<\bar{q}q>$ (corresponding to {\rm ur}-$Žā$) and the phase fluctuation of $<\bar{q}q>$ (2 pion states) mix strongly in the vacuum. As the temperature and/or density of the system increase, however, there arises a softening of this complex pole due to the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. Such soft modes play a key role to understand the QCD phase structure. We demonstrate that even a slight softening of the $Žā$ mode could induce strong spectral enhancement and strong $ŽĆ$-$ŽĆ$ correlation near the 2$m_ŽĆ$ threshold in the $I$=$J$=0 channel, which thereby can be a signature of the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. Such spectral enhancement may be seen not only in hot matter created by the relativistic heavy ion collisions but also in cold matter (heavy nuclei) probed by photons and hadrons. Relevance of the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in heavy nuclei with the recent data by the CHAOS and CB collaborations as well as with on-going and future experiments are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a softening of the complex sigma-meson pole in the second Riemann sheet of the pi-pi scattering matrix occurs due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry as temperature or density increases, and that even a slight softening can induce strong spectral enhancement and strong pi-pi correlation near the 2m_pi threshold in the I=J=0 channel, which can serve as a signature of partial chiral restoration.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the O(4) linear sigma model to compute spectral functions for the scalar-isoscalar (sigma) channel at finite temperature and density. They analyze the complex sigma pole in the pi-pi scattering matrix using analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry constraints, and examine the behavior of the chiral condensate at low temperature via chiral perturbation theory and at finite baryon density via the Feynman-Hellman theorem.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and density, building on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model analyses by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1984, 1985) that first identified soft modes near the chiral critical point. The paper connects to lattice QCD simulations by Karsch (2001) and to the maximum entropy method for extracting spectral functions from lattice data developed by Nakahara, Asakawa, and Hatsuda (1999). The results enable experimental searches for partial chiral restoration signatures in relativistic heavy ion collisions and in heavy nuclei probed by photons and hadrons, with relevance to data from the CHAOS and CB collaborations.
