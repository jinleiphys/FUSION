# Chiral symmetry and pi-pi scattering in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> The pi-pi scattering amplitude calculated with a model for the quark-antiquark interaction in the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) is shown to satisfy the Adler zero constraint imposed by chiral symmetry. The CST formalism is established in Minkowski space and our calculations are performed in momentum space. We prove that the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied by our model. Then we show that, similar to what happens within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, application of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity to the CST pi-pi scattering amplitude allows us to sum the intermediate quark-quark interactions to all orders. The Adler self-consistency zero for pi-pi scattering in the chiral limit emerges as the result for this sum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the pi-pi scattering amplitude calculated within the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) satisfies the Adler self-consistency zero in the chiral limit, provided the interaction kernel satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity (AV-WTI). This result holds for a kernel that includes scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor confining interactions, not only the vector and axial-vector structures typically used in the literature.

## Method

The authors construct a CST interaction kernel in Minkowski space that includes a linear confining part (with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor Dirac structures) plus a non-confining vector-axial-vector part. They prove that the AV-WTI is satisfied by imposing the condition lambda_S = lambda_P on the confining kernel weights, which causes the scalar and pseudoscalar contributions to the self-energy to cancel. The pi-pi scattering amplitude is then computed first in the impulse approximation (which fails to give the Adler zero) and then to all orders in intermediate quark-quark interactions by applying the AV-WTI to sum the kernel insertions.

## Key numbers

* The linear confinement potential in momentum space is given by V_A(p,hat k) = -h^2(p^2) h^2(m^2) * (8 pi sigma) / (p - hat k)^4, where sigma is the string tension.
* The condition for the AV-WTI to hold with scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor confinement requires lambda_S = lambda_P, with lambda_T unconstrained by the condition.
* The reduced bare axial vertex in the chiral limit at zero momentum transfer gives lim_{m0->0, p'->p} gamma^A_R(p',p) = 0.
* The dressed axial vertex in the chiral limit is Gamma^A_{R chi}(p,p) = gamma^5 * 2 A_chi(p^2) / h^2(p^2), where A_chi is the scalar part of the self-energy in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the CST framework previously developed for the pion form factor (Biernat et al., 2014) and for the CST-Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson equations (PhysRevD.89.016005) by testing chiral symmetry constraints on pi-pi scattering. It parallels the Bethe-Salpeter formalism where the AV-WTI similarly allows summation of intermediate interactions to all orders (Adler, 1965). The result enables future calculations of pi-pi scattering beyond the chiral limit and provides a Minkowski-space alternative to Euclidean Dyson-Schwinger approaches (Maris, 2003; Fischer, 2006) and lattice QCD simulations (Edwards; Guo).
