# Interplay between non-interfering neutrino exchange mechanisms and nuclear matrix elements in $0νββ$ decay

> We revisit the phenomenology of neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay mediated by non-interfering exchange of light and heavy Majorana neutrinos, in the context of current and prospective ton-scale experimental searches, as well as of recent calculations of nuclear matrix elements (NME) in different nuclear models. We derive joint upper bounds on the light and heavy contributions to $0νββ$ decay, for different sets of NME, through separate and combined data coming from the following experiments (and isotopes): KamLAND-Zen and EXO (Xe), GERDA, and MAJORANA (Ge) and CUORE (Te). We further consider three proposed projects that could provide, within current bounds, possible $0νββ$ decay signals at $>\!3σ$ level with an exposure of 10 ton years: nEXO (Xe), LEGEND (Ge) and CUPID (Mo). Separate and combined (Xe, Ge, Mo) signals are studied for different representative cases and NME sets, and the conditions leading to (non)degenerate light and heavy neutrino mechanisms are discussed. In particular, the role of heavy-to-light NME ratios in different isotopes is highlighted through appropriate graphical representations. By using different sets of "true" and "test" NME as a proxy for nuclear uncertainties, it is shown that the relative contributions of light and heavy neutrino exchange to $0νββ$ signals may be significantly biased in some cases. Implications for theoretical models connecting light and heavy Majorana neutrino masses are also briefly illustrated. These results provide further motivations to improve NME calculations, so as to better exploit the physics potential of future multi-isotope $0νββ$ searches at the ton scale.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the phenomenology of neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by non-interfering exchange of light and heavy Majorana neutrinos, showing that the relative contributions of these two mechanisms to decay signals may be significantly biased in some cases due to nuclear matrix element (NME) uncertainties, even under the simplifying assumption of incoherent (non-interfering) contributions.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical analysis of current experimental data from KamLAND-Zen and EXO (Xe-136), GERDA and MAJORANA (Ge-76), and CUORE (Te-130), using fifteen sets of NME from four nuclear models (shell model, QRPA, EDF, and IBM) for both light and heavy neutrino exchange. They derived joint upper bounds on the light and heavy effective Majorana mass parameters (m_nu and m_N) through separate and combined data, and then analyzed prospective signals at >3 sigma level for three ton-scale projects (nEXO, LEGEND, CUPID) with 10 ton-years exposure, using different "true" and "test" NME sets as a proxy for nuclear uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* Current combined half-life limits at 90% C.L.: T_90(Ge) = 2.629 x 10^26 y (GERDA + MAJORANA), T_90(Xe) = 2.260 x 10^26 y (KamLAND-Zen + EXO), T_90(Te) = 0.216 x 10^26 y (CUORE)
* Phase space factors used (in units of 10^-26 y^-1 (meV)^-2): G_Xe = 14.78 x 10^-6, G_Ge = 2.40 x 10^-6, G_Te = 14.42 x 10^-6, G_Mo = 16.15 x 10^-6
* Heavy-to-light NME ratios across fifteen NME sets range from approximately 29 to 93 for Xe, 29 to 86 for Ge, 30 to 94 for Te, and 21 to 90 for Mo
* For a typical quenching factor q = 0.79, NME ratios are altered by less than 10% (and at most 15% in one case), much less than the overall factor-of-three spread in the ratios

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of multi-isotope neutrinoless double beta decay, including analyses by Faessler (2011), Meroni (2012), and Lisi (2015, 2022), by incorporating the most recent NME calculations and experimental data. The results provide further motivation to improve NME calculations through benchmarking against nuclear data, as advocated in recent roadmaps by Cirigliano (2022), and to pursue multi-isotope ton-scale searches (nEXO, LEGEND, CUPID) to test the underlying decay mechanism(s).
