# Characterizing the quark-hadron mixed phase in compact star cores : sensitivity to nuclear saturation and quark-model parameters at finite-temperature

> A thorough knowledge of the quark-hadron phase transition in hot and dense matter is essential for constraining the equation of state of neutron stars. In this work, we study the thermodynamics of the quark-hadron mixed phase at finite temperature using the Gibbs construction and examine its impact on hybrid star matter. We systematically explore the role of nuclear saturation properties, including the effective nucleon mass, incompressibility, symmetry energy coefficient, and its slope, together with quark matter parameters such as the bag constant and the vector coupling strength. We find that the width of the mixed phase is mainly controlled by the effective mass and symmetry energy, while the roles of incompressibility and symmetry energy slope are comparatively weak, particularly at higher temperatures. Thermal effects substantially modify the phase structure: increasing temperature reduces the mixed-phase width and softens the equation of state in the coexistence region due to Gibbs phase equilibrium constraints. These effects are reflected in the behavior of the speed of sound, the trace anomaly, and its derivative. Variations in the symmetry energy, effective mass, and quark parameters significantly affect the hadron-quark transition, stellar radii, and maximum mass, while finite temperature softens the equation of state and enhances radius jumps in the mixed phase. Strong vector repulsion is essential to reconcile massive pulsar observations with NICER constraints, whereas weaker repulsion favors more compact, low-mass configurations.

## Key claim

The width of the quark-hadron mixed phase in hybrid star cores is primarily controlled by the effective nucleon mass and symmetry energy coefficient, while the roles of incompressibility and symmetry energy slope are comparatively weak, particularly at higher temperatures; this sensitivity is conditional on the specific choice of hadronic and quark matter parameters within the Gibbs construction framework.

## Method

The authors employed the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with the BigApple parameterization for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model with vector interactions for quark matter, constructing the mixed phase via the Gibbs construction with global charge neutrality. They systematically varied four nuclear saturation parameters (effective mass m*/m, incompressibility K_sat, symmetry energy coefficient J, and its slope L) and two quark matter parameters (bag constant B_0^{1/4} and vector coupling strength G_V) at finite temperatures of 10, 30, and 50 MeV. The resulting equations of state were then used to compute hybrid star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of hadron-quark phase transitions in compact stars by Pal and Chaudhuri (2025) and builds on the Gibbs construction framework established by Glendenning (1992) and applied by Orsaria (2013), Wu (2018), and others. The analysis connects microscopic thermodynamic properties of the mixed phase to astrophysical observables, enabling interpretation of NICER mass-radius measurements from pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, as well as gravitational-wave constraints from LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA. The results motivate further studies of finite-temperature effects in proto-neutron stars and binary neutron star merger remnants.
