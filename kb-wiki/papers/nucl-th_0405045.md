# Boson Dominance in nuclei

> We present a new method of bosonization of fermion systems applicable when the partition function is dominated by composite bosons. Restricting the partition function to such states we get an euclidean bosonic action from which we derive the Hamiltonian. Such a procedure respects all the fermion symmetries, in particular fermion number conservation, and provides a boson mapping of all fermion operators.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new bosonization method for fermion systems that is applicable when the partition function is dominated by composite bosons, and it respects all fermion symmetries including fermion number conservation; however, the method relies on a single approximation concerning the identity operator in the space of composites, and the boson space cannot be arbitrarily truncated even if the nuclear potential contains only monopole and quadrupole pairing interactions.

## Method

The method restricts the nuclear partition function to states of nucleon composite bosons using a projection operator constructed from coherent states of composites, then evaluates the restricted partition function to obtain a closed-form Euclidean bosonic effective action. From this action, which contains anomalous time derivative terms and nonpolynomial interactions due to compositeness, the bosonic Hamiltonian is derived by expanding in inverse powers of the index of nilpotency Ω (half the dimension of the nucleon space) and applying a subtraction procedure analogous to a Bogoliubov transformation that preserves nucleon number conservation. The structure matrices of the composites are determined variationally by minimizing the ground state energy.

## Key numbers

- The index of nilpotency Ω is defined as half the dimension of the nucleon space; for a single j-shell, Ω = j + 1/2.
- The normalization condition for boson form factors is tr(B†_J B_K) = 2Ω δ_{J,K}.
- For a pure monopole pairing interaction, the exact spectrum is E_{n,v} = 2n ē − g₀(Ω + 1)(n − v) + g₀(n − v)² + g₀(n − v)v, where n is the total number of pairs and v is half the seniority.
- In the s-boson condensate case, the ground state energy (neglecting normal ordering) is E₀ = 2n ē − n Ω Ĝ + n²(Ĝ − G/Ω), where Ĝ and G are sums over coupling constants.

## Context

This work extends earlier bosonization approaches including the Beliaev-Zelevinsky method (Beliaev and Zelevinsky, 1960), the Marumori-Yamamura-Togunaga mapping (Marumori, Yamamura, and Togunaga, 1964), and the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello mapping (Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello, 1978) that underlies the Interacting Boson Model. Unlike previous methods that require truncation of the nucleon space and suffer from "intruder" states, this approach avoids preliminary truncation and respects nucleon number conservation throughout. The closed-form action opens the way to numerical simulations of fermion systems in terms of bosonic variables, potentially avoiding the sign problem that affects Monte Carlo studies of many-fermion systems.
