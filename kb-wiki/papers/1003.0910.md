# Medium Modifications of the Rho Meson in Nuclear Photoproduction

> We extend our recent study of dilepton invariant-mass spectra from the decays of rho mesons produced by photon reactions off nuclei. We specifically focus on experimental spectra as recently measured by the CLAS Collaboration at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility using carbon and iron nuclei. Building on our earlier work we broaden our description to a larger set of observables in order to identify sensitivities to the medium effects predicted by microscopic calculations of the rho spectral function. We compute mass spectra for several target nuclei and study the spectral shape as a function of the 3-momentum of the outgoing lepton pair. We also compute the so-called nuclear transparency ratio which provides an alternative means (and thus consistency check) of estimating the inelastic rho width in the cold nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous theoretical model for dilepton spectra from rho-meson decays in photonuclear reactions, finding that the CLAS data for carbon and iron targets are consistent with a moderate in-medium broadening of the rho meson (approximately 70 MeV) without significant mass shift, but the spectral shape alone cannot clearly distinguish vacuum from in-medium effects for carbon, while for iron the in-medium propagator gives a better chi-squared (1.08 versus 1.55 per data point).

## Method

The authors combine a meson-exchange model for elementary rho photoproduction on nucleons (including sigma, f2, pion, and Pomeron t-channel exchange plus s-channel baryon resonances) with an in-medium rho propagator that incorporates nucleon-hole and delta-hole excitations and pion-cloud dressing. They improve on their earlier work by folding over realistic nuclear density distributions with exponential decay characteristics for the rho meson, rather than using a single average density, and compute dilepton invariant-mass spectra and nuclear transparency ratios for several target nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Average density probed by rho decays in the CLAS experiment: approximately 0.4 rho_0 for Fe-56 targets
- In-medium rho width at q=2 GeV and density 0.5 rho_0: approximately 40 MeV from the model, compared to an estimated 60 MeV from a schematic calculation assuming a 40 mb rho-nucleon cross section
- Chi-squared per data point for iron target: 1.08 with in-medium propagator versus 1.55 with vacuum propagator
- Low-mass enhancement factors (ratio of full to vacuum calculation at M=0.5 GeV): 1.35 for A=12, 1.49 for A=56, 1.6 for A=120, and 1.73 for A=238
- Peak ratios (vacuum peak height divided by full calculation peak height) for low-momentum cut (below 0.5 GeV): 2.4 for A=12, 2.8 for A=56, 4.2 for A=120, and 4.4 for A=238
- Peak ratios for high-momentum cut (2.0-2.5 GeV): 1.2 for A=12, 1.4 for A=56, 1.5 for A=120, and 1.6 for A=238
- Photon energy bins used in CLAS input spectrum: 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, and 3.5 GeV with relative weights of 13.7%, 23.5%, 19.3%, 20.1%, 12.6%, and 10.9%, respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Riek et al., 2008) of dilepton spectra from rho decays in photonuclear reactions, building on the meson-exchange model of Oh et al. (2003) and the in-medium rho propagator of Rapp et al. (1999) that successfully described low-mass dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN-SPS. The results are compared to CLAS Collaboration data (2007, 2008) from Jefferson Lab and to BUU transport calculations by the Giessen group (Effenberger et al., 1999; Muhlich et al., 2002) using the spectral function of Post et al. (2000), which also found a broadening of approximately 70 MeV without significant mass shift. The paper enables future experimental studies using heavier targets (up to uranium) and momentum-dependent cuts to better discriminate between different theoretical models of the rho spectral function in nuclear matter.
