# Limit on the neutron-antineutron transitions in the alternative models

> We summarize our study of the neutron-antineutron transition in medium and nuclei in the framework of the field-theoretical approach. The models based on the diagram technique for direct reactions, potential description of antineutron-medium interaction are considered as well. The lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time $τ$ is in the range: $10^{16}\; {\rm yr}>τ>1.2\cdot 10^{9}\; {\rm s}$.

# Limit on the neutron-antineutron transitions in the alternative models

## Key claim

The lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time τ is not a single number but lies in a wide range from 1.2×10⁹ s to 10¹⁶ yr, depending on whether the antineutron propagator is dressed or bare, and the paper argues that values far above the conservative lower bound are realistic.

## Method

The author compares three alternative models for neutron-antineutron (n\(\bar{n}\)) transitions in medium and nuclei: a field-theoretical approach (model 2) using the evolution operator on a finite time interval, a diagram technique for direct reactions (model 1) that treats the neutron as a virtual particle from a nuclear breakup vertex, and a potential model using optical potentials for antineutron-nucleus interaction. The field-theoretical approach is applied with both a bare antineutron propagator (leading to infrared singularities handled by finite-time formulation) and a dressed propagator including antineutron self-energy Σ.

## Key numbers

- Free-space n\(\bar{n}\) oscillation time lower limit with dressed propagator: τᵈ = 1.2×10⁹ s (conservative lower bound)
- Free-space n\(\bar{n}\) oscillation time lower limit with bare propagator: τᵇ = 10¹⁶ yr (interpreted as estimation from above)
- The full allowed range for τ is: 10¹⁶ yr > τ > 1.2×10⁹ s
- The conservative lower bound τᵈ = 1.2×10⁹ s exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14 and the potential model lower limit by a factor of 5
- Characteristic time for the dynamical two-step n\(\bar{n}\) transition in nuclei followed by annihilation: τch ∼ 10⁻²⁴ s

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier field-theoretical approach (Nazaruk, 1996-2013) and directly contradicts the diagram-technique model originally published by the same author in 1992 and later repeated by other authors in 2013. The paper argues that the diagram technique for direct reactions (model 1) is unsuitable because it lacks the infrared singularity present in the correct treatment, does not reproduce the vacuum or medium limits, and cannot be derived from a Hamiltonian. The potential model is rejected because its non-unitary S-matrix leads to the annihilation effect acting in the wrong direction (W ∼ 1/Γ instead of W ∼ Γ). The work identifies the antineutron self-energy Σ as the key quantity requiring further study to narrow the wide uncertainty range in τ.
