# Hydrodynamic fluctuations and ultra-central flow puzzle in heavy-ion collisions

> One of the long-standing problems in the field of high-energy heavy-ion collisions is that the dynamical models based on viscous hydrodynamics fail to describe the experimental elliptic flow $v_2$ and the triangular flow $v_3$ simultaneously in ultra-central collisions. The problem, known as the "ultra-central flow puzzle", is specifically that hydrodynamics-based models predict the flow ratio of the two-particle cumulant method $v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\} > 1$ while $v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\} \sim 1$ in the experimental data. In this Letter, we focus on the effects of hydrodynamic fluctuations during the space-time evolution of the QGP fluid on the flow observables in the ultra-central collisions. Using the (3+1)-dimensional integrated dynamical model which includes relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics, we analyze the anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n\{2\}$ in 0-0.2% central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=2.76~\text{TeV}$. We find that the hydrodynamic fluctuations decrease the model overestimate of $v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\}$ from the experimental data by about 19% within the present setup of $η/s = 1/2π$. This means that the hydrodynamic fluctuations qualitatively have an effect to improve the situation for the puzzle, but the effect of the hydrodynamic fluctuations alone is quantitatively insufficient to resolve the puzzle. The decrease of the ratio largely depends on the shear viscosity $η/s$, which calls for future comprehensive analyses with, for example, a realistic temperature-dependent viscosity.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations during the QGP fluid evolution qualitatively improve the disagreement between model predictions and experimental data for the ratio \(v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\}\) in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions, reducing the model overestimate by about 19% for \(\eta/s = 1/2\pi\), but the effect is quantitatively insufficient to fully resolve the ultra-central flow puzzle.

## Method

The authors employed a (3+1)-dimensional integrated dynamical model combining a Monte-Carlo Glauber model for initial entropy deposition, a relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamic model (rfh) with causal second-order fluctuating hydrodynamics including shear viscosity and stochastic noise terms governed by the fluctuation-dissipation relation, and the JAM hadron cascade model for the final hadronic stage. They used importance sampling to generate ultra-central collision events (0-0.2% centrality) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 2.76~\text{TeV}\), comparing results with and without hydrodynamic fluctuations for two values of specific shear viscosity (\(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and \(1/2\pi\)).

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\}\) with \(\eta/s = 1/2\pi\) and \(|\Delta\eta_p|>2\) was reduced from about 1.39 in viscous hydrodynamics to about 1.32 in fluctuating hydrodynamics, a reduction of the deviation from the experimental value (1.02) by about 19%.
- With \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\), the deviation of the ratio was reduced by about 3%, which was not statistically significant.
- The initial proper time was set to \(\tau_0 = 0.6~\text{fm}\) and the switching temperature to \(T_\text{sw} = 155~\text{MeV}\).
- The spatial regularization widths for the noise fields were \(\lambda_\perp = 1.5~\text{fm}\) and \(\lambda_{\eta_s} = 1.5\).
- The cross-section with impact parameter \(b < 5~\text{fm}\) was about 11.6% of the total cross-section in the MC-Glauber model.
- The impact parameter distribution of the 0-0.2% centrality events peaked at about 0.8 fm, with maximum impact parameter less than 2.5 fm.

## Context

This work addresses the "ultra-central flow puzzle," where hydrodynamics-based models (e.g., those used in Bayesian analyses by Bernhard (2016, 2019) and the JETSCAPE collaboration (2020)) predict \(v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\} > 1\) while experimental data from ALICE (2011, 2018), ATLAS (2012, 2019), and CMS (2013) show \(v_2\{2\}/v_3\{2\} \sim 1\). Previous attempts to resolve the puzzle have focused on improved initial conditions (Luzum, 2012; Shen, 2015; Carzon, 2020), transport coefficients (Luzum, 2012; Rose, 2014; Plumari, 2015), and equations of state (Alba, 2017), but none succeeded. This study extends the authors' prior work on hydrodynamic fluctuations (Murase, 2013, 2015, 2016, 2019; Sakai, 2020, 2021) to the ultra-central regime, showing that while fluctuations help, they alone cannot resolve the puzzle, motivating future studies with temperature-dependent viscosity.
