# $ϕ$ Meson As a Probe of QCD Equation of State

> In this work, we extract the QCD Equation of State (EoS) using experimental results of the $ϕ$ meson produced in nuclear collisions at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. The data are confronted to simple thermodynamic expectations and lattice results. The experimental data indicate a first order phase transition, with a mixed phase stretching energy density between $\sim$ 1 and 3.2 GeV/$fm^3$.

## Key claim

The inverse slope parameter of the $\phi$ meson, when plotted against the Bjorken energy density, shows a plateau between approximately 1 and 3.2 GeV/fm$^3$, which the authors interpret as evidence for a first-order phase transition with a mixed phase of quarks, gluons, and hadrons; however, the authors note that this does not exclude a change in the order of the transition at smaller baryochemical potentials.

## Method

The authors extracted the effective temperature ($T_{\textrm{eff}}$) and thermal freeze-out temperature ($T_{\textrm{th}}$) of the $\phi$ meson from its transverse momentum spectra measured in heavy-ion collisions at AGS (4.87 GeV), SPS (17.3 GeV), and RHIC (62.4, 130, and 200 GeV) using the blast-wave method. They then plotted these temperatures as functions of both the center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ and the Bjorken initial energy density $\epsilon_{Bj}$, comparing the observed behavior to simple thermodynamic expectations and lattice QCD predictions.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase stretches between $\epsilon_{Bj} \sim 1$ and 3.2 GeV/fm$^3$, as inferred from the plateau in $T_{\textrm{eff}}^{\phi}$.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is $T_c \sim 170$ MeV, cited from lattice QCD calculations (Karsch).
- The thermal freeze-out temperature of the $\phi$ meson is $T_{th} \sim 170$ MeV at SPS and RHIC energies, with radial flow velocity $v_r \sim 0.5$ at AGS and SPS, rising to $0.6-0.65$ at RHIC.
- The latent heat density yields a bag constant $B^{1/4} \sim 250$ MeV, derived from $\epsilon_Q(T_c) \sim 3.2$ GeV/fm$^3$ and $\epsilon_H(T_c) \sim 1$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- The ratio of energy densities $\epsilon_Q/\epsilon_H \sim 3.2$ is comparable to the ratio of statistical degeneracies $g_Q/g_H$.

## Context

This work extends the Van Hove (1982) suggestion of using the average transverse momentum as a function of multiplicity to identify deconfinement, applying it specifically to the $\phi$ meson for the first time. It builds on hydrodynamic calculations by Bravina et al. and Kodama et al. that reproduced similar plateau behavior for pions, kaons, and protons using a first-order transition. The results enable future mapping of the QCD phase diagram as a function of baryochemical potential and support the need for further data at energy densities below 1 GeV/fm$^3$ and for comparisons with lattice QCD using matched variables.
