# Field and phenomenological description of absorption of particles

> The complicated processes in the medium (decays, reactions and $n\bar{n}$ conversion) involving final state absorption are considered. The calculations in the framework of field and phenomenological approaches are compared. The reasons for disagreement are studied. The field approach can tend to increase of total process probability as well as probability of channel corresponding to absorption, in comparison with phenomenological model.

# Field and phenomenological description of absorption of particles

## Key claim

The field approach to processes involving final-state absorption (such as $n\bar{n}$ conversion in nuclear matter) yields a total process probability that increases with the annihilation width $\Gamma$, whereas the phenomenological optical-potential model yields an inverse $\Gamma$-dependence; the field approach can therefore give a reinforcement of the total process probability compared to the phenomenological model, provided the real potential $V$ is not too large.

## Method

The author compares two theoretical treatments of the two-step process $n \rightarrow \bar{n} \rightarrow M$ (antineutron annihilation mesons) in nuclear matter. In the field approach, the antineutron Green's function is constructed from the Hamiltonian containing the $n\bar{n}$ conversion term and the real potential difference $V = U_{\bar{n}} - U_n$, and the process width $\Gamma_f$ is computed from the amplitude $M = -\epsilon G M_a$. In the phenomenological approach, the $\bar{n}$-medium interaction is described by an optical potential $U_{opt}$ with $ImU_{opt} = -\Gamma/2$, and the total process width $\Gamma_p$ is obtained from the unitarity condition via the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude. The two results are compared analytically.

## Key numbers

- Annihilation width of antineutron: $\Gamma \sim 100$ MeV.
- Free-space $n\bar{n}$ conversion time: $\tau \sim 1/m_H < 10^{-29}$ s (Higgs boson mass $m_H > 10^5$ GeV).
- Antineutron annihilation time: $\tau \sim 1/\Gamma \sim 10^{-24}$ s.
- Ratio of field-approach width to phenomenological width: $\Gamma_f / \Gamma_p = 1 + (\Gamma/2V)^2 > 1$.
- Observation time in proton-decay-type experiment: $T_0 = 1.3$ yr.
- $n\bar{n}$ conversion parameter: $\epsilon = 1/\tau < 1/(10^8$ s).
- Resulting $2ImT_{ii} < 10^{-31}$ for the phenomenological model under realistic parameters.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of $n\bar{n}$ conversion in nuclear matter (Nazaruk, 1994, 1998) and directly challenges the standard optical-potential treatment used by Dover, Gal and Richard (1985) and Sandars (1980). The paper argues that all existing calculations of $n\bar{n}$ conversion in matter are based on the phenomenological model (7),(8), which produces an unphysical inverse $\Gamma$-dependence. The field approach suggests that absorption in the final state does not suppress the process, which could lead to larger predicted probabilities for $n\bar{n}$ conversion and similar reactions in nuclear media. The author indicates that a subsequent paper will study the range of applicability of non-Hermitian models in more detail.
