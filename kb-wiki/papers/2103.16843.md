# Thermodynamic stability, compressibility matrix, and effects of mediated interactions in a strongly-interacting Bose-Fermi mixture

> We theoretically investigate the thermodynamic stability of a normal-state Bose-Fermi mixture, with a tunable Bose-Fermi pairing interaction $-U_{\rm BF}<0$ associated with a hetero-nuclear Feshbach resonance, as well as a weak repulsive Bose-Bose interaction $U_{\rm BB}\ge 0$. Including strong hetero-pairing fluctuations associated with the former interaction within the self-consistent $T$-matrix approximation, as well as the latter within the mean-field level, we calculate the compressibility matrix, to assess the stability of this system against density fluctuations. In the weak- and the intermediate-coupling regime with respect $-U_{\rm BF}$, we show that an effective attractive interaction between bosons mediated by density fluctuations in the Fermi component makes the system unstable below a certain temperature $T_{\rm clp}$ (leading to density collapse). When $U_{\rm BB}=0$, $T_{\rm clp}$ is always higher than the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) temperature $T_{\rm c}$. When $U_{\rm BB}>0$, the density collapse is suppressed, and the BEC transition becomes possible. It is also suppressed by the formation of tightly bound Bose-Fermi molecules when the hetero-pairing interaction $-U_{\rm BF}$ is strong; however, since the system may be viewed as a molecular Fermi gas in this case, the BEC transition does not also occur. Since quantum gases involving Bose atoms are known to be sensitive to inter-particle correlations, our results would be useful for the study of many-body properties of a Bose-Fermi mixture in a stable manner, without facing the unwanted density collapse.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strongly-interacting normal-state Bose-Fermi mixture becomes thermodynamically unstable due to an effective attractive interaction between bosons mediated by density fluctuations in the Fermi component, leading to density collapse below a certain temperature \(T_{\rm clp}\); this collapse always precedes Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) when the direct Bose-Bose repulsion \(U_{\rm BB}=0\), but a finite repulsive \(U_{\rm BB}>0\) can suppress the collapse and allow BEC to occur.

## Method

The authors extended the self-consistent \(T\)-matrix approximation (SCTMA), originally developed for two-component Fermi systems, to a uniform mass-balanced and population-balanced Bose-Fermi mixture with a tunable attractive Bose-Fermi interaction \(-U_{\rm BF}<0\) (associated with a hetero-nuclear Feshbach resonance) and a weak repulsive Bose-Bose interaction \(U_{\rm BB}\ge 0\). They calculated the compressibility matrix \(\hat{\kappa}\) from the SCTMA self-energies and number equations, and assessed thermodynamic stability by requiring \(\hat{\kappa}\) to be positive definite; the BEC transition temperature \(T_{\rm c}\) was determined from the Hugenholtz-Pines theorem.

* The collapse temperature \(T_{\rm clp}\) is always higher than the BEC temperature \(T_{\rm c}\) when \(U_{\rm BB}=0\), as shown in Fig. 4(d) for interaction strengths \((k_{\rm F}a_{\rm BF})^{-1}\) ranging from about \(-1.5\) to \(1.1\).
* The BEC transition temperature \(T_{\rm c}\) vanishes at \((k_{\rm F}a_{\rm BF})^{-1}\simeq 0.9\) (the quantum critical point, QCP), and the collapse temperature \(T_{\rm clp}\) vanishes at \((k_{\rm F}a_{\rm BF})^{-1}\simeq 1.1\).
* In the unitary limit \((k_{\rm F}a_{\rm BF})^{-1}=0\) with \(U_{\rm BB}=0\), the compressibilities \(\kappa_{\alpha\beta}\) diverge at \(T_{\rm clp}\), and the system is stable only above that temperature (Fig. 5(a)).
* For \(U_{\rm BB}>0\) at unitarity, with \(N_{\rm B}^{1/3}a_{\rm BB}=0.05\) and \(0.1\), the density collapse is suppressed and the system remains stable down to \(T_{\rm c}\) (Fig. 5(b),(c)).

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of Bose-Fermi mixture stability (Molmer (1998), Viverit (2000), Miyakawa (2000), Roth (2002), Modugno (2003)) and the beyond-mean-field approach of Shirasaki (2014), which was restricted to weak coupling. It also goes beyond the variational method of Yu (2011), which could not treat the strong-coupling regime where tightly bound Bose-Fermi molecules dominate. The SCTMA approach covers the full range from weak to strong coupling, and the results enable future studies of many-body properties of Bose-Fermi mixtures in a stable manner, avoiding unwanted density collapse.
