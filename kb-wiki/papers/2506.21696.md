# Impact of magnetic field-driven anisotropies on the equation of state probed in neutron star mergers

> Binary neutron star mergers can produce extreme magnetic fields, some of which can lead to strong magnetar-like remnants. While strong magnetic fields have been shown to affect the dynamics of outflows and angular momentum transport in the remnant, they can also crucially alter the properties of nuclear matter probed in the merger. In this work, we provide a first assessment of the latter, determining the strength of the pressure anisotropy caused by Landau level quantization and the anomalous magnetic moment. To this end, we perform the first numerical relativity simulation with a magnetic polarization tensor and a magnetic-field-dependent equation of state using a new algorithm we present here, which also incorporates a mean-field dynamo model to control the magnetic field strength present in the merger remnant. Our results show that -- in the most optimistic case -- corrections to the anisotropy can be in excess of $10\%$, and are potentially largest in the outer layers of the remnant. This work paves the way for a systematic investigation of these effects.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first assessment of how strong magnetic fields (via Landau level quantization and anomalous magnetic moment) can create pressure anisotropies in neutron star merger remnants, finding that in the most optimistic case corrections can exceed 10%, but this result is an upper bound because finite-temperature effects (which would lower the anisotropy) are neglected.

## Method

The authors performed the first numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers that include a magnetic polarization tensor and a magnetic-field-dependent equation of state, using a new algorithm that also incorporates a mean-field dynamo model to control magnetic field amplification. They used two different nuclear matter descriptions (NL3ωρ and CMF models) with magnetic field effects included, combined with crust descriptions, and compared simulations with and without magnetic field feedback on the equation of state. The pressure anisotropy was quantified as the difference between parallel and perpendicular pressure normalized by the parallel pressure.

## Key numbers

- Pressure anisotropy corrections can be in excess of 10% in the most optimistic case, with the largest effects in the outer layers of the remnant
- Magnetic field strengths of 10^16-10^17 G are reached inside the remnant when a mean-field dynamo term is included, with maximum values exceeding 10^17 G
- Without dynamo amplification, simulations saturate at field strengths between 10^15-10^16 G
- Inside the core, pressure anisotropies are of order Π ≃ 10^-3 to 10^-4 P_∥
- In crustal regions of the remnant, anisotropies can reach between Π ≃ (10^-2 to 10^-1) P_∥
- The simulations used equal-mass non-spinning configurations with a total mass of 2.6 M_⊙ and a finest resolution of 250 m

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic field effects in isolated neutron stars (Chatterjee 2014, Franzon 2015, Strickland 2012) and dynamo amplification in mergers (Kiuchi 2015, Kiuchi 2023, Most 2023) by providing the first assessment of how these fields feed back onto the equation of state during merger dynamics. It enables future systematic investigations of how magnetic-field-driven pressure anisotropies may affect jet and wind launching from merger remnants, particularly through Parker instabilities, though a self-consistent treatment of finite-temperature effects remains an open question.
