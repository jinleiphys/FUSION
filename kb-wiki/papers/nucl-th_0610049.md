# Neutron star matter in the quark-meson coupling model in strong magnetic fields

> The effects of strong magnetic fields on neutron star matter are investigated in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The QMC model describes a nuclear many-body system as nonoverlapping MIT bags in which quarks interact through self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons in the mean-field approximation. The results of the QMC model are compared with those obtained in a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. It is found that quantitative differences exist between the QMC and RMF models, while qualitative trends of the magnetic field effects on the equation of state and composition of neutron star matter are very similar.

# Neutron star matter in the quark-meson coupling model in strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models produce very similar qualitative trends for the effects of strong magnetic fields on neutron star matter, but quantitative differences persist between the two approaches, particularly in the effective nucleon mass and stiffness of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors model neutron star matter as a neutral mixture of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in beta equilibrium under a constant external magnetic field along the z-axis, using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. In this approach, nucleons are treated as nonoverlapping MIT bags containing quarks that interact through self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons in the mean-field approximation; the effective nucleon mass is first calculated at quark level from the bag model with an external scalar field, then the full many-body system is solved at hadron level. The coupled equations for meson fields, charge neutrality, and beta equilibrium are solved self-consistently at a given baryon density, with nucleon anomalous magnetic moments included for field strengths above B* = 10^5. Results are compared with the RMF model using the TM1 parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Electron critical field: B_c^e = 4.414 × 10^13 G
- Current quark mass: m_q = 5.5 MeV
- Bag constant: B_bag^(1/4) = 210.854 MeV
- Zero-point parameter Z = 4.00506, determined to reproduce free nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV and bag radius R = 0.6 fm
- Quark-meson coupling constants: g_sigma^q = 5.957, g_omega^q = 2.994, g_rho^q = 4.325
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.7928 mu_N (nuclear magneton)
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_n = -1.9130 mu_N
- Magnetic field effects become significant only for B* = B/B_c^e > 10^5
- Complete spin polarization of neutrons appears at B* ~ 10^5
- Complete spin polarization of protons occurs at B* ~ 10^4 (without anomalous magnetic moments)
- Proton fraction shows significant enhancement for B* > 10^6, with protons becoming dominant

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strong magnetic field effects on neutron star matter, which had mostly employed relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory as in Lattimer (2000) and others. The authors compare their QMC results with the RMF model using the TM1 parameter set from Sugahara and Toki (1994), which includes nonlinear sigma and omega meson terms. The QMC model, originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and extended by Panda et al. (2004, 2005), explicitly includes quark degrees of freedom, making it potentially applicable to studying the hadron-to-quark matter phase transition in strong magnetic fields. The paper finds that while both models show similar qualitative behavior, the QMC model yields a slightly stiffer equation of state and smaller changes in effective nucleon mass compared to the TM1 model.
