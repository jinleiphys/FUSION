# Influence of the Coulomb Interaction on the Chemical Equilibrium of Nuclear Systems at Break-Up

> The importance of a Coulomb correction to the formalism proposed by Albergo et al. for determining the temperatures of nuclear systems at break-up and the ensities of free nucleon gases is discussed. While the proposed correction has no effect on the temperatures extracted based on double isotope ratios, it becomes non-negligible when such temperatures or densities of free nucleon gases are extracted based on multiplicities of heavier fragments of different atomic numbers.

# Influence of the Coulomb Interaction on the Chemical Equilibrium of Nuclear Systems at Break-Up

## Key claim

The Coulomb correction term proposed by the authors restores symmetry to the chemical equilibrium equation for fragments and free nucleons at break-up, but it has no effect on temperatures extracted from double isotope ratios; however, it becomes non-negligible when temperatures are extracted from isotone ratios or when relative densities of free neutron and proton gases are determined from isobaric ratios of heavier fragments.

## Method

The authors derive a Coulomb-corrected chemical equilibrium equation by adding a term \(V_{coul}^{pF}(Z)\) representing the average mutual Coulomb interaction energy of \(Z\) free protons, estimated assuming a uniform spherical distribution of free protons at density \(\rho_{pF}\). They then apply this correction to the Albergo et al. (ACCR) formalism, evaluating its effect on the ratio of free neutron to free proton densities from isobaric ratios and on break-up temperatures from double isotone ratios, using assumed values of \(T = 3.3\) MeV and \(\rho_{pF} = 0.0011\) fm\(^{-3}\) for the system \(^{32}\)S + \(^{109}\)Ag.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb correction factor \(F_{Coul}^{n/p} = 1.1\) for the isobaric ratio \(Y_{3,1}/Y_{3,2}\) (tritium / helium-3) at \(T = 3.3\) MeV and \(\rho_{pF} = 0.0011\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Coulomb correction factor \(F_{Coul}^{n/p} = 1.28\) for the isobaric ratio \(Y_{13,6}/Y_{13,7}\) at the same conditions.
- Coulomb correction factor \(F_{Coul}^{n/p} = 1.59\) for the isobaric ratio \(Y_{34,16}/Y_{34,17}\) at the same conditions.
- Coulomb correction factor for temperature \(F_{Coul}^T = 0.96\) for the double isotone ratio \((Y_{13,7}/Y_{12,6})/(Y_{4,2}/Y_{3,1})\).
- Coulomb correction factor \(F_{Coul}^T = 1.19\) for the isotone ratio \((Y_{13,7}/Y_{12,6})/(Y_{10,5}/Y_{9,4})\).
- Coulomb correction factor \(F_{Coul}^T = 0.31\) for the isotone ratio \((Y_{14,8}/Y_{13,7})/(Y_{3,2}/Y_{2,1})\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Albergo et al. (1985) for determining break-up temperatures and free nucleon densities from fragment yields, which assumed non-interacting free nucleons. The authors note that more complete models such as the Berlin Microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo and the Copenhagen Statistical Multifragmentation model already account for Coulomb effects rigorously, but the correction remains largely transparent to users. The paper enables systematic studies of experimental thermometers based on isotone ratios, where the Coulomb correction can be substantial, and clarifies that the correction always enriches the free neutron gas relative to the free proton gas, a trivial Coulomb effect distinct from isospin fractionation driven by the equation of state.
