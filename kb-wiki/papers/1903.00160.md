# Masses of ground-state mesons and baryons, including those with heavy quarks

> Using a confining, symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction, we compute the spectra of ground-state pseudoscalar and vector $(f\bar g)$ mesons, scalar and axial-vector $(fg)$ diquarks, and $J^P=1/2^+, 3/2^+$ $(fgh)$ baryons, where $f,g,h \in \{u,d,s,c,b\}$. The diquark correlations are essentially dynamical and play a key role in formulating and solving the three-valence-quark baryon problems. The baryon spectrum obtained from this largely-algebraic approach reproduces the 22 known experimental masses with an accuracy of $2.9(2.4)$%. It also possesses the richness of states typical of constituent-quark models, predicting many heavy-quark baryons not yet observed. This study indicates that diquark correlations are an important component of all baryons; and owing to the dynamical character of the diquarks, it is typically the lightest allowed diquark correlation which defines the most important component of a baryon's Faddeev amplitude.

## Key claim

Using a confining, symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector×vector contact interaction, the authors compute ground-state meson, diquark, and baryon spectra across five quark flavours (u,d,s,c,b), reproducing 22 known experimental baryon masses with an accuracy of 2.9(2.4)%, though the approach requires separate ultraviolet cutoffs and coupling strengths for each meson family, meaning the results are predictions only for systems with mass above the kaon mass.

## Method

The authors employ a contact interaction (vector×vector) in rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, regularised with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs (Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV, m_G = 0.5 GeV). Meson Bethe-Salpeter equations are solved for pseudoscalar and vector channels; diquark correlations are obtained from the meson equations by multiplying the kernel by 1/2; and baryon spectra are computed using a quark+diquark Faddeev equation. Four current-quark masses and four parameters (α_IR, three ultraviolet cutoffs) are fitted to the π, K, η_c, and η_b masses and decay constants, with the coupling and cutoff for heavier systems linked via Eq. (alphaLambda).

## Key numbers

- Baryon spectrum reproduces 22 known experimental masses with an accuracy of 2.9(2.4)%
- Pseudoscalar meson masses: π 0.14 GeV, K 0.50 GeV, η_c 2.98 GeV, η_b 9.40 GeV (fitted); D 1.92 GeV, D_s 2.01 GeV, B 5.41 GeV, B_s 5.50 GeV, B_c 6.28 GeV (predicted)
- Vector meson masses: ρ 0.93 GeV, K* 1.03 GeV, φ 1.13 GeV, J/ψ 3.19 GeV, Υ 9.49 GeV, D* 2.14 GeV, D*_s 2.23 GeV, B* 5.46 GeV, B*_s 5.56 GeV, B*_c 6.38 GeV
- Scalar diquark masses: [ud] 0.77 GeV, [us] 0.93 GeV, [uc] 2.15 GeV, [sc] 2.26 GeV, [ub] 5.51 GeV, [sb] 5.60 GeV, [cb] 6.48 GeV
- Axial-vector diquark masses: {uu} 1.06 GeV, {us} 1.16 GeV, {ss} 1.26 GeV, {cc} 3.30 GeV, {bb} 9.68 GeV
- Mean relative difference between computed and experimental/lattice QCD meson masses: 5(5)%
- Mean absolute relative difference for meson leptonic decay constants: 18(9)%

## Context

This work extends the contact-interaction approach previously applied to SU(3) systems (Gutierrez-Guerrero 2010, Roberts 2010, Chen 2012) and to mesons with heavy quarks (Bedolla 2015, Serna 2017) to a unified description of all ground-state mesons and baryons in SU(5) flavour multiplets. It complements systematic rainbow-ladder studies (Qin 2018, Qin 2019) and lattice QCD calculations (Brown 2014, Alexandrou 2017, Bali 2017) by providing a largely algebraic framework that yields predictions for many heavy-quark baryons not yet observed, while demonstrating that the lightest allowed diquark correlation defines the most important component of a baryon's Faddeev amplitude.
