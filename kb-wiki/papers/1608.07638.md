# Reconstructing parton densities at large fractional momenta

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) are nonperturbative objects defined by nonlocal light-cone correlations. They cannot be computed directly from Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Using a standard lattice QCD approach, it is possible to compute moments of PDFs, which are matrix elements of local operators. Recently, an alternative approach has been proposed, based on the introduction of quasi-parton distribution functions (quasi-PDFs), which are matrix elements of equal-time spatial correlations and hence calculable on lattice. Quasi-PDFs approach standard PDFs in the limit of very large longitudinal proton momenta $P^z$. This limit is not attainable in lattice simulations, and quasi-PDFs fail to reproduce PDFs at high fractional longitudinal momenta. In this paper, we propose a method to improve the reconstruction of PDFs by combining information from quasi-PDFs and from the Mellin moments of regular PDFs. We test our method using the diquark spectator model for up and down valence distributions of both unpolarized and helicity PDFs. In the future, the method can be used to produce PDFs entirely based on lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to improve the reconstruction of parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large fractional momenta \(x\) by combining information from quasi-PDFs (calculable on lattice at finite proton momentum \(P^z\)) and from the Mellin moments of the standard PDFs, but the method is tested only within the diquark spectator model rather than on actual lattice data, and its success depends on the choice of matching point \(x_0\) and on the proton momentum \(P^z\) being sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors define a matching point \(x_0\) (chosen as 0.2 or 0.3) that splits the PDF support into a low-\(x\) region where the quasi-PDF is taken as a good approximation and a high-\(x\) region where a parametric form \(\hat{q}(x) = x^{p_1} (1-x)^{p_2} (1 + p_3 x^{1/2} + p_4 x + p_5 x^{3/2})\) is used. The parametric function is constrained to match the quasi-PDF in value and first derivative at \(x_0\), and the remaining free parameters \(p_1, p_2, p_5\) are fixed by minimizing a weighted \(\chi^2\) between the \(n=2,3,4\) truncated Mellin moments of the quasi-PDF and the parametric function relative to the true PDF moments. The method is tested using the diquark spectator model for up and down valence distributions of both unpolarized (\(f_1\)) and helicity (\(g_1\)) PDFs at the model scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

* For the unpolarized up quark at \(x_0 = 0.2\) and \(P^z = 1.47\) GeV, the relative distance \(\overset{\circ}{r}\) of the reconstructed PDF to the true PDF is \(4.0408 \times 10^{-3}\), compared to \(\tilde{r} = 0.059932\) for the quasi-PDF alone.
* For the unpolarized up quark at \(x_0 = 0.2\) and \(P^z = 2.94\) GeV, the relative distance \(\overset{\circ}{r}\) drops to \(9.1975 \times 10^{-4}\), compared to \(\tilde{r} = 9.0825 \times 10^{-3}\).
* For the helicity up quark at \(x_0 = 0.2\) and \(P^z = 2.94\) GeV, the relative distance \(\overset{\circ}{r}\) is \(6.7572 \times 10^{-4}\), compared to \(\tilde{r} = 0.010776\).
* The smallest momentum fraction accessible on current lattices is estimated as \(x_{\mathrm{min}} = 1/32\) for a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice, and the contribution of the region \(0 \leq x \leq x_{\mathrm{min}}\) to the \(n=2,3,4\) truncated Mellin moments is less than 1% of the full moments.
* The matching point range is found to be convenient at \(0.2 \leq x_0 \leq 0.3\); the choice \(x_0 = 0.4\) causes the reconstruction procedure to fail.

## Context

This work extends the Large Momentum Effective field theory (LaMET) approach proposed by Ji (2013) for computing quasi-PDFs on the lattice, and addresses the known failure of quasi-PDFs to reproduce standard PDFs at large \(x\) for moderate \(P^z\), as previously demonstrated in model calculations by Gamberg et al. (2014) using the same diquark spectator framework. The method enables future production of PDFs entirely based on lattice QCD results by combining lattice-calculable quasi-PDFs with lattice-calculable Mellin moments, bypassing the need for extremely large proton momenta that are currently beyond computational reach.
