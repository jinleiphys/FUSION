# Fission-fragment mass distributions from strongly damped shape evolution

> Random walks on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces were recently found to yield fission-fragment mass distributions that are in remarkable agreement with experimental data. Within the framework of the Smoluchowski equation of motion, which is appropriate for highly dissipative evolutions, we discuss the physical justification for that treatment and investigate the sensitivity of the resulting mass yields to a variety of model ingredients, including in particular the dimensionality and discretization of the shape space and the structure of the dissipation tensor. The mass yields are found to be relatively robust, suggesting that the simple random walk presents a useful calculational tool. Quantitatively refined results can be obtained by including physically plausible forms of the dissipation, which amounts to simulating the Brownian shape motion in an anisotropic medium.

## Key claim

Random walks on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces produce fission-fragment mass distributions in remarkable agreement with experimental data, but the authors show that the simple Metropolis walk is justified only if the dissipation tensor is isotropic in the lattice variables; when physically plausible anisotropic dissipation is included, the mass yields remain relatively robust, though quantitative refinement requires simulating Brownian shape motion in an anisotropic medium.

## Method

The authors simulate fission as strongly damped shape evolution using the Smoluchowski equation of motion, treating the nuclear shape as a Brownian particle moving on a five-dimensional potential-energy surface calculated with the macroscopic-microscopic method for 5,254 even-even nuclei. They perform direct Monte Carlo simulations of the stochastic shape evolution, comparing the discrete Metropolis random walk (originally used in Randrup (2011)) with the continuous transport process, and systematically vary the dissipation tensor from isotropic to anisotropic forms derived from the wall formula for one-body dissipation.

## Key numbers

- The potential-energy surfaces are calculated on a five-dimensional lattice of 5,315,625 shapes for 5,254 even-even nuclei with mass numbers 170 ≤ A ≤ 330 (Moller (2009)).
- The critical neck radius at which no further change in mass asymmetry occurs is taken as c₀ = 2.5 fm, with calculated mass yields relatively insensitive to c₀ in the range 2–3 fm.
- The isotropization parameter f is varied from 0.2 (20% isotropic admixture) through 1.0 (equal mixture) to infinity (fully isotropic), showing the sensitivity of charge yields for ²⁴⁰Pu(n,f), ²³⁶U(n,f), ²³⁴U(n,f), and thorium isotopes.
- When the lattice spacing in the quadrupole moment Q₂ direction is decreased by a factor of three, the mass distribution becomes noticeably narrower; compensating by increasing the mobility by a factor of nine restores the original distribution.

## Context

This work extends the random-walk approach introduced by Randrup (2011) by providing the physical justification within the Smoluchowski equation framework and systematically testing sensitivity to model ingredients. It builds on the pioneering fission theory of Meitner and Frisch (1939) and Bohr and Wheeler (1939), the one-body dissipation mechanism of Blocki (1978) and Sierk (1980), and the comprehensive potential-energy surfaces calculated by Moller (2009). The results enable future applications of the method across the nuclear chart, particularly for low-excitation-energy fission where microscopic shell effects are essential, and suggest that further refinement requires accurate treatment of the dissipation tensor structure.
