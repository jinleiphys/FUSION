# Gauge-Invariant Quantities Characterizing Gauge Fields in Chromodynamics

> We calculate Lorentz-invariant and gauge-invariant quantities characterizing the product $\sum_a D_R(T^a) F^a_{μν}$, where $D_R(T^a)$ denotes the matrix for the generator $T^a$ in the representation $R=$ fundamental and adjoint, for color SU(3). We also present analogous results for an SU(2) gauge theory.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general method for constructing gauge-invariant and Lorentz-invariant quantities that characterize the product \(\sum_a D_R(T^a) F^a_{\mu\nu}\) for SU(\(N\)) gauge theories, applicable to arbitrary spacetime-dependent classical gauge fields, not only the constant-field limit previously studied.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the field strength tensor \(F^a_{\mu\nu}\) for each group index \(a\) to obtain Lorentz-invariant eigenvalues \(\lambda_k^a\) (for \(k=1,\dots,4\)). They then construct the matrix \(V_k = \sum_a D_R(T^a) \lambda_k^a\) in the representation \(R\) and compute its eigenvalues \(\Lambda_{k\ell}\) by solving the characteristic polynomial via group traces of powers of \(V_k\). The method is applied explicitly to the fundamental and adjoint representations of SU(3) and to all representations of SU(2).

## Key numbers

- For SU(3) in the fundamental representation, the eigenvalues of \(V_k\) are \(\Lambda_{k\ell} = \sqrt{C_{k1}/3} \, \cos(\theta_k + 2(\ell-1)\pi/3)\) for \(\ell=1,2,3\), where \(C_{k1} = \sum_a (\lambda_k^a)^2\) and \(\cos^2(3\theta_k) = r_k\) with \(r_k = 3C_{k2}/(C_{k1})^3\).
- For SU(3) in the adjoint representation, the eigenvalues of \(V_k\) are \(\Lambda_{k\ell} = [ (C_{k1}/2) \cos(\phi_k + 2(\ell-1)\pi/3) ]^{1/2}\) for \(\ell=1,2,3\), where \(\cos(3\phi_k) = 2r_k - 1\).
- For SU(2), the invariants reduce to \(\Lambda_{k\ell} = I_\ell C_{k1}\) where \(I_\ell = I_3\) and \(C_{k2}\) does not appear because \(d_{abc}=0\) for SU(2).
- In the special case of zero chromomagnetic field (\(\mathbf{B}^a=0\) for all \(a\)), \(C_{21}=C_{22}=0\) while \(C_{11}\) and \(C_{12}\) reduce to the quantities \(C_1 = \sum_a (E^a)^2\) and \(C_2 = (\sum_{a,b,c} d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c)^2\) from earlier work.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of non-abelian gauge fields beyond the special case of constant chromoelectric fields studied in Nayak (2005) and Nayak (2006) for SU(3) pair production. It provides a general set of gauge-invariant quantities that can be used to express nonperturbative particle production rates for arbitrary spacetime-dependent classical gauge fields, generalizing the Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger, 1951) and the Euler-Heisenberg effective action (Heisenberg and Euler, 1936). The results enable future calculations of gluon and quark-antiquark pair production in time-dependent chromoelectric fields and in heavy-ion collision models.
