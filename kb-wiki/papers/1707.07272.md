# Effects of hadronic mean-field potentials on Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> With the parameters fitted by the particle multiplicity, the energy density at chemical freeze-out, and the charged particle elliptic flow, we have studied the effects of the hadronic mean-field potentials on the Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (HBT) correlation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions based on a multiphase transport model. The hadronic mean-field potentials are found to delay the emission time of the system and lead to large HBT radii extracted from the correlation function. Effects on the energy dependence of $R_o^2-R_s^2$ and $R_{o}/ R_{s}$ as well as the eccentricity of the emission source are discussed. The HBT correlations can also be useful in understanding the mean-field potentials of protons, kaons, and antiprotons as well as baryon-antibaryon annihilations.

# Effects of hadronic mean-field potentials on Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The hadronic mean-field potentials delay the kinetic freeze-out of the system and enlarge the HBT radii extracted from correlation functions, but the quantitative agreement with STAR data is only partial: with mean-field potentials, the longitudinal radii $R_l$ become more consistent with experiment while the outward and sideward radii $R_o$ and $R_s$ are overestimated, suggesting that incorporating both mean-field potentials and enhanced transverse flow is needed to reproduce experimental emission source radii.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with hadronic mean-field potentials incorporated via the test-particle method. They calibrated the model parameters (Lund string fragmentation parameters, partonic phase lifetime, and parton scattering cross sections) to reproduce the pseudorapidity distribution of charged particles, the energy density at chemical freeze-out from a hadron resonance gas model, and the charged particle elliptic flow measured by STAR at five RHIC Beam-Energy Scan energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV). Femtoscopic analyses were performed using the CRAB (Correlation After Burner) model for pions and the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model for protons, kaons, and antiprotons, with the freeze-out criterion defined as when the distance between a hadron's current position and its free-streaming position is less than 0.01 fm.

## Key numbers

- Energy densities at chemical freeze-out used for calibration: 0.49 GeV/fm$^3$ at 7.7 GeV, 0.62 GeV/fm$^3$ at 11.5 GeV, 0.68 GeV/fm$^3$ at 19.6 GeV, 0.69 GeV/fm$^3$ at 27 GeV, and 0.69 GeV/fm$^3$ at 39 GeV
- Partonic phase stopping times to reproduce these energy densities: 2.75 fm/c at 7.7 GeV, 2.4 fm/c at 11.5 GeV, 2.15 fm/c at 19.6 GeV, 2.25 fm/c at 27 GeV, and 2.45 fm/c at 39 GeV
- Isotropic parton scattering cross sections used: 3 mb at 7.7 and 11.5 GeV, 6 mb at 19.6 and 27 GeV, 10 mb at 39 GeV
- With mean-field potentials at 7.7 GeV: $R_o = 6.16 \pm 0.04$ fm, $R_s = 5.53 \pm 0.04$ fm, $R_l = 5.51 \pm 0.04$ fm (compared to STAR: $R_o = 5.57 \pm 0.13$ fm, $R_s = 4.93 \pm 0.10$ fm, $R_l = 5.01 \pm 0.11$ fm)
- With mean-field potentials at 39 GeV: $R_o = 6.42 \pm 0.04$ fm, $R_s = 5.53 \pm 0.04$ fm, $R_l = 5.95 \pm 0.04$ fm (compared to STAR: $R_o = 5.86 \pm 0.07$ fm, $R_s = 4.97 \pm 0.05$ fm, $R_l = 6.18 \pm 0.08$ fm)
- The peak in $R_o^2 - R_s^2$ and $R_o/R_s$ around $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 20$ GeV is reproduced only when mean-field potentials are included
- Kaon potential parameters: $a_K = 0.22$ GeV$^2$fm$^3$, $a_{\bar{K}} = 0.45$ GeV$^2$fm$^3$, $b_K = 0.33$ GeVfm$^3$
- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$; baryon and antibaryon densities in the model are mostly lower than this value

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hadronic mean-field effects in transport models, particularly the earlier incorporation of mean-field potentials in the AMPT/ART model by Xu (2012) and the corrections to inelastic channels by Xu (2016). It builds on previous HBT studies using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics model by Li, Feng, and Qin (2008) that first illustrated mean-field effects on HBT radii, and on isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck studies by Chen, Li, and Wu (2003) showing sensitivity of neutron-proton correlations to different mean-field potentials. The results enable future constraints on the mean-field potentials of protons, kaons, and antiprotons in baryon-rich hadronic matter through HBT correlation measurements, and contribute to understanding the QCD phase diagram at RHIC Beam-Energy Scan energies.
