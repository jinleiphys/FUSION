# Nature of the D_0 meson in the D pi scattering with chiral symmetry

> We study the nature of the scalar D_0 meson from the viewpoint of chiral symmetry. With the linear representation of chiral symmetry, we construct the D pi scattering amplitude satisfying the chiral low-energy theorem, in which the D_0 meson appears as an s-wave resonance. We show that the properties of the D_0 meson can be successfully reproduced as the chiral partner of the D meson coupled with the D pi scattering states. At the same time, we find that the spectral function and the pole position of D_0 are not very sensitive to the reduction of the chiral condensate, indicating the importance of the dressing of the bare state by the D pi molecular component.

## Key claim
The physical D_0 meson can be described as a superposition of the chiral partner of the D meson and a D-pi molecular state, but the D-pi molecular component dominates the internal structure because the spectral function and pole position are not very sensitive to a 30% reduction of the chiral condensate.

## Method
The authors constructed a D-pi scattering amplitude using the linear representation of chiral symmetry, incorporating the D_0 meson as an explicit bare state that serves as the chiral partner of the D meson. They performed an s-wave projection of the tree-level interaction and applied the N/D method (unitarization) to resum the amplitude nonperturbatively, ensuring consistency with the Weinberg-Tomozawa low-energy theorem. The free parameters (bare mass M_D0 and subtraction constant a) were fitted to reproduce the PDG pole position of the D_0 resonance.

## Key numbers
- D_0 pole position reproduced at sqrt(s) = 2318 - i135 MeV, in agreement with the PDG value of 2318 +/- 29 - i(134 +/- 20) MeV
- Best-fit bare mass M_D0 = 2024 MeV and subtraction constant a = 2.5 (at subtraction scale mu = 1000 MeV)
- The bare mass must lie in the range 2001 MeV <= M_D0 <= 2045 MeV to reproduce the PDG uncertainty
- A virtual state pole was found at sqrt(s) = 1795 MeV, far below the D-pi threshold of 2005 MeV
- With 30% reduction of the chiral condensate (Phi = 0.7), the pole position changed by only a few tens of MeV
- Input parameters: m_pi = 138 MeV, M_D = 1867 MeV, sigma_bar = 92.4 MeV, m_sigma = 550 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach used for the sigma meson in pi-pi scattering by Basdevant (1969, 1970), Jido (2000), and Hyodo (2010) to the charm sector. It combines two competing pictures of the D_0 meson: the chiral partner of the D meson (Nowak 1992, Bardeen 1993, 2003, Suenaga 2017, 2018) and the dynamically generated hadronic molecule from D-pi interactions (Kolomeitsev 2003, Hofmann 2003, Guo 2006, Gamermann 2006, Altenbuchinger 2013, Du 2017, 2019). The results enable future studies of the D_0 in nuclear medium using techniques from Suenaga (2017, 2018) and extension to three-flavor systems including the D_s0(2317).
