# Description of nuclear octupole and quadrupole deformation close to the axial symmetry and phase transitions in the octupole mode

> The dynamics of nuclear collective motion is investigated in the case of reflection-asymmetric shapes. The model is based on a new parameterization of the octupole and quadrupole degrees of freedom, valid for nuclei close to the axial symmetry. Amplitudes of oscillation in other degrees of freedom different from the axial ones are assumed to be small, but not frozen to zero. The case of nuclei which already possess a permanent quadrupole deformation is discussed in some more detail and a simple solution is obtained at the critical point of the phase transition between harmonic octupole oscillation and a permanent asymmetric shape. The results are compared with experimental data of the Thorium isotopic chain. The isotope Th-226 is found to be close to the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new parameterization of combined quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom valid for nuclei close to axial symmetry, and finds that the isotope Th-226 is close to the critical point of the phase transition between harmonic octupole oscillation and a permanent reflection-asymmetric shape, but only under the assumption that amplitudes of oscillation in non-axial degrees of freedom are small but not frozen to zero.

## Method

The authors develop a geometrical model based on a new parameterization of octupole and quadrupole deformation amplitudes in an intrinsic reference frame defined by the principal axes of the overall tensor of inertia, using the Bohr hydrodynamical model with irrotational flow assumptions. They derive the classical kinetic energy, construct the quantum Hamiltonian via the Pauli prescription, and classify elementary excitations by their K quantum number and parity. The model is then applied to axial octupole vibrations in nuclei with permanent quadrupole deformation, and the results are compared with experimental data from the Thorium isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- Th-226 is identified as being close to the critical point of the phase transition between harmonic octupole oscillation and permanent asymmetric shape.
- The determinant of the inertia matrix G is proportional to beta_2^2 beta_3^2 (beta_2^2 + 2 beta_3^2)^2 (beta_2^2 + 5 beta_3^2)^{-1} (beta_2^2 gamma_2 + sqrt(5) beta_3^2 gamma_3)^2.
- At the limit beta_3 much less than beta_2, the determinant G is proportional to beta_2^8, consistent with the Bohr model for pure quadrupole motion.
- The energy eigenvalues for the harmonic oscillator in the variable u_0 are E_{u_0} = (N_{u_0}+1) hbar omega_{u_0} with N_{u_0} >= |n_{u_0}|, and excitations carry two units of angular momentum along the 3rd intrinsic axis.
- The K quantum number is given by K = n_phi + 2n_chi + 3n_theta + 2n_{u_0}, with elementary excitations corresponding to K^pi = 1^-, 2^-, 3^- and 2^+.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape phase transitions from quadrupole deformation, where symmetries such as X(5) were identified by Iachello (2001) and observed in nuclei like Sm-152 by Casten (2001) and Nd-150 by Kruecken (2002), to the octupole degree of freedom. The new parameterization addresses criticisms by Donner and Greiner (1966) regarding the consistent treatment of tensor orders in combined quadrupole-octupole models, and provides a framework that can describe octupole vibrations of finite amplitude, going beyond the small-amplitude limit treated by Eisenberg and Greiner (1970). The results enable future studies of octupole phase transitions in other nuclear regions and more detailed comparisons with experimental data on reflection-asymmetric nuclei.
