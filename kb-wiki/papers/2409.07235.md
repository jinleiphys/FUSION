# Phenomenological model for the $γ^*γ\to ηπ^+ π^-$ reaction in the $f_1(1285)$ energy region

> Motivated by the ongoing analysis by the BESIII Collaboration on the single-tagged $e^+e^-\to e^+e^- ηπ^+π^-$ reaction, we present a phenomenological study of the diphoton fusion to $ηπ^+ π^-$, focusing on the production mechanism of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance. Contributions from the $f_1(1285)\to a_0(980)^\pm\, π^\mp$ and $f_1(1285)\to σ/f_0(500)\, η$ channels are included without introducing free parameters within an effective Lagrangian approach. Assuming the destructive interference between the amplitudes, we predict the invariant mass distributions, angular distributions, and total cross sections of the $γ^*γ\to ηπ^+π^-$ process, which will be tested by the forthcoming BESIII measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model for the $\gamma^*\gamma \to \eta\pi^+\pi^-$ reaction that includes contributions from both the $f_1(1285)\to a_0(980)^\pm \pi^\mp$ and $f_1(1285)\to \sigma/f_0(500) \eta$ decay channels, assuming destructive interference between these amplitudes, with the explicit condition that the model contains no free parameters beyond those fixed by known decay widths and branching ratios.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian approach for the $s$-channel production of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance in $\gamma^*\gamma \to \eta\pi^+\pi^-$, incorporating two dominant decay mechanisms: $f_1(1285)\to a_0(980)^\pm \pi^\mp$ and $f_1(1285)\to \sigma/f_0(500) \eta$. Coupling constants were determined from experimental decay widths and branching ratios from the PDG and Belle Collaboration, with the $f_1(1285)$ transition form factors parameterized using a dipole form with $\Lambda_{f_1}=1040$ MeV from the L3 Collaboration. The $\sigma/f_0(500)\to \pi^+\pi^-$ decay was described using an Omnès function, and the relative phase between the two channels was fixed by requiring destructive interference, with the coupling $C$ for the $\sigma\eta$ channel determined at $Q^2=0.5$ GeV$^2$ and $s=M_{f_1}^2$.

## Key numbers

- Equivalent two-photon decay width of $f_1(1285)$: $\tilde{\Gamma}_{f_1\to \gamma\gamma} = 3.5 \pm 0.8$ keV, giving $F_{f_1 \gamma^* \gamma^*}^{LT}(0,0) = 0.44 \pm 0.05$
- Dipole mass for $f_1(1285)$ transition form factors: $\Lambda_{f_1}=1040$ MeV
- Coupling constant $g_{f_1a_0\pi} \simeq 5.26 \pm 0.69$, determined from $\Gamma^\mathrm{Exp.}_{f_1\to a_0\pi}\simeq 8.63 \pm 1.00$ MeV
- Coupling constant $g_{a_0\eta \pi} = 6.85 \pm 0.65$ GeV$^{-1}$, using $a_0(980)$ mass $M_{a_0}^\mathrm{Exp.} = 982.3^{+0.6}_{-0.7}^{+3.1}_{-4.7}$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{a_0}^\mathrm{Exp.} = 75.6^{+1.6}_{-1.6}^{+17.4}_{-10.0}$ MeV from Belle (2009)
- Coupling constant $g_{f_1\to \sigma\eta}\simeq 2.62 \pm 0.39$, using branching ratio $\mathcal{B}(f_1(1285)\to \eta \pi \pi$ excluding $a_0\pi$) $= 14 \pm 4\%$
- Absolute value of the $\sigma\eta$ channel coupling: $|C| \simeq 13.88$ GeV$^{-1}$ at $Q^2=0.5$ GeV$^2$ and $s=M_{f_1}^2$
- Energy averaging window for cross sections: $W_\mathrm{min}=1.22$ GeV to $W_\mathrm{max}=1.36$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological studies of $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ (Ren 2022) and $\gamma\gamma^*\to K^\pm \bar{K}^{*\mp}(892)\to K^+K^-\pi^0$ (Ren 2024) using effective Lagrangian approaches, motivated by ongoing BESIII feasibility studies on single-tagged $e^+e^-\to e^+e^-\eta\pi^+\pi^-$ (Becker Master Thesis). The model improves upon the "GGResRC" Monte Carlo generator (Druzhinin 2014) by including interference between helicity amplitudes of different channels, which was neglected in that generator. The predictions are designed to serve as input for Monte Carlo generators for BESIII data analysis and to enable extraction of the $f_1(1285)$ transition form factors, which remain poorly determined despite prior studies using resonance chiral theory (Roig 2019), holographic models (Leutgeb 2019), and vector-meson dominance parameterizations (Zanke 2021, Hoferichter 2023).
