# Four-jet production via double parton scattering in $pA$ collisions at the LHC

> We present predictions for the double parton scattering (DPS) four-jet production cross sections in $pA$ collisions at the LHC. Relying on the experimental capabilities to correlate centrality with impact parameter $B$ of the proton-nucleus collision, we discuss a strategy to extract the double parton scattering contributions in $pA$ collisions, which gives direct access to double parton distribution in the nucleon. We show that the production cross sections via DPS of four jets, out of which two may be light- or heavy-quark jets, are large enough to allow the method to be used already with data accumulated in 2016 $pA$ run.

# Four-jet production via double parton scattering in pA collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The DPS2 contribution to four-jet and two b-jet plus two light-jet production in pA collisions at the LHC can be disentangled from the leading-twist SPS and DPS1 backgrounds using the different dependence on impact parameter B, provided the integrated luminosity reaches 0.1 pb⁻¹ as accumulated in the 2016 pA run.

## Method
The authors calculate DPS and SPS cross sections for four-jet (4j) and two b-jet plus two light-jet (2b2j) final states in pPb collisions at √s_{pN} = 8.16 TeV using leading-order ALPGEN with CTEQ6L1 parton distributions and EPS09 nuclear parton distributions. They exploit the fact that SPS and DPS1 contributions scale linearly with the nuclear thickness function T(B), while DPS2 scales quadratically with T(B), and construct ratios and double ratios of event counts binned in T(B) to isolate the DPS2 signal.

## Key numbers
- For 4j final state with p_T^{j3,j4} > 20 GeV: DPS1 cross section = 26.0 μb, DPS2 cross section = 72.2 μb, SPS cross section = 170.9 μb, total = 269.2 μb; DPS2 fraction f_DPS2 = 0.27
- For 4j final state with p_T^{j3,j4} > 25 GeV: DPS1 = 10.8 μb, DPS2 = 30.2 μb, SPS = 92.9 μb, total = 133.9 μb; f_DPS2 = 0.22
- For 4j final state with p_T^{j3,j4} > 30 GeV: DPS1 = 5.1 μb, DPS2 = 14.3 μb, SPS = 51.4 μb, total = 70.9 μb; f_DPS2 = 0.20
- For 2b2j final state with p_T^{b,j} > 20 GeV: DPS1 = 2.2 μb, DPS2 = 6.2 μb, SPS = 13.0 μb, total = 21.4 μb; f_DPS2 = 0.29
- For 2b2j final state with p_T^{b,j} > 25 GeV: DPS1 = 0.4 μb, DPS2 = 1.2 μb, SPS = 4.7 μb, total = 6.4 μb; f_DPS2 = 0.19
- For 2b2j final state with p_T^{b,j} > 30 GeV: DPS1 = 0.1 μb, DPS2 = 0.3 μb, SPS = 1.9 μb, total = 2.3 μb; f_DPS2 = 0.13
- Average σ_eff used for DPS1 calculations: 19 mb, taken from experimental analyses at √s = 7 TeV
- Integrated luminosity assumed: ∫ L dt = 0.1 pb⁻¹, corresponding to 2016 pA run data
- Centre-of-mass energy: √s_{pN} = 8.16 TeV with beam rapidity boost Δy = 0.465

## Context
This work extends the method proposed by Alvioli et al. (2019) for separating DPS2 from SPS and DPS1 contributions in pA collisions, previously applied to two-dijet and W+jets final states. The DPS2 mechanism, first described by Strikman and collaborators (2001), involves two partons from the incoming proton interacting with partons from two distinct nucleons in the target nucleus and is parametrically enhanced by A^{1/3} over DPS1. The results enable experimental extraction of double parton distributions in the nucleon using existing LHC pA data, complementing studies of double open charm (LHCb, 2012-2014) and same-sign WW production (CMS, 2019) in pp collisions.
