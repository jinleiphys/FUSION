# Production of the pentaquark exotic baryon Xi_5 in bar{K} N scattering : bar{K} N -> K Xi_5 and bar{K} N -> K* Xi_5

> We investigate the production of the newly found pentaquark exotic baryon Xi_5 in the bar{K} N -> K Xi_5 and the bar{K} N -> K* Xi_5 reactions at the tree level. We consider both positive- and negative parities of the Xi_5. The reactions are dominated by the s- and u-channel processes, and the resulting cross sections depend very much on the parity of Xi_5 and on the type of form factor. We have seen that the cross sections for the positive-parity Xi_5 are generally about a hundred times larger than those of the negative-parity one. This large difference in the cross sections will be useful for further study of pentaquark baryons.

## Key claim

The cross sections for producing the positive-parity pentaquark Xi_5 in antikaon-nucleon scattering are about a hundred times larger than those for the negative-parity Xi_5, but this result depends strongly on the choice of phenomenological form factor and on the unknown sign of the K*SigmaXi_5 coupling constant for K* production.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level Born diagrams for the reactions Kbar N -> K Xi_5 and Kbar N -> K* Xi_5, which proceed only through s-channel and u-channel exchange of an intermediate Sigma baryon because no t-channel strangeness-two meson exists. They used effective Lagrangians with coupling constants taken from the Nijmegen potential and from SU(3) flavor symmetry relations, and employed two different phenomenological form factors (a covariant form F1 with cutoff Lambda_1 = 0.85 GeV and a three-dimensional form F2 with cutoff Lambda_2 = 0.5 GeV). For the K* production channel, they considered four possible values of the unknown coupling constant g_{K*SigmaXi_5} derived from different theoretical assumptions.

## Key numbers

- For Kbar N -> K Xi_5 with positive-parity Xi_5: average total cross section sigma ~ 2.6 microbarn with the F1 form factor and sigma ~ 1.5 microbarn with the F2 form factor, over the center-of-mass energy range 2.35 GeV <= E_CM <= 3.35 GeV.
- For Kbar N -> K Xi_5 with negative-parity Xi_5: average total cross section sigma ~ 26 nanobarn with F1 and sigma ~ 12 nanobarn with F2, over the same energy range.
- For Kbar N -> K* Xi_5 with positive-parity Xi_5: average total cross section sigma ~ 1.6 microbarn with F1 and less than or about 2 microbarn with F2, over the energy range 2.75 GeV <= E_CM <= 3.75 GeV.
- For Kbar N -> K* Xi_5 with negative-parity Xi_5: average total cross section sigma ~ 14 nanobarn with F1 and less than or about 20 nanobarn with F2, over the same energy range.
- The Xi_5 mass is taken as 1862 MeV, with isospin I = 3/2, strangeness S = -2, and assumed spin J = 1/2.
- The coupling constant g_{KSigmaXi_5} = 3.77 for positive parity and 0.53 for negative parity, derived from the Theta+ decay width Gamma_{Theta -> KN} = 15 MeV and M_Theta = 1540 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of pentaquark production to the Xi_5 (strangeness -2) channel, following the experimental report of a Xi_5 signal by the NA49 collaboration (Alt, 2003) and earlier theoretical predictions from the chiral soliton model (Diakonov, 1997) and other approaches. The authors use the same effective-Lagrangian framework previously applied to Theta+ production by Oh et al. (2003) and by Nam et al. (2003-2004), and note that Liu et al. (2003) evaluated the gamma N -> K Xi_5 reaction assuming positive parity. The large factor-of-100 cross-section difference between positive and negative parity found here is more pronounced than the factor-of-10 difference typically seen in Theta+ production studies, and could help determine the parity of the Xi_5 if experimental data become available. However, the authors acknowledge that the CLAS collaboration (DeVita, 2005) found no evidence for the Theta+ pentaquark, and the existence of the Xi_5 remains unconfirmed.
