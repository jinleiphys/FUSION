# Inner crust of neutron stars with mass-fitted Skyrme interaction

> The mass, radius and crustal fraction of moment of inertia in neutron stars are calculated using $β$-equilibrated nuclear matter obtained from Skyrme effective interaction. The transition density, pressure and proton fraction at the inner edge separating the liquid core from the solid crust of the neutron stars are determined from the thermodynamic stability conditions using the KDE0v1 set. The neutron star masses obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using neutron star matter obtained from this set is able to describe highly massive compact stars $\sim$2$M_{\odot}$. The crustal fraction of the moment of inertia can be extracted from studying pulsar glitches. This fraction is highly dependent on the core-crust transition pressure and corresponding density. These results for pressure and density at core-crust transition together with the observed minimum crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia provide a limit for the radius of the Vela pulsar:$R\geq$ 3.69+3.44 M/$M_{\odot}$. Present calculations suggest that the crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia can be at most 3.6% due to crustal entrainment caused by Bragg reflection of unbound neutrons by lattice ions.

## Key claim

Using the KDE0v1 Skyrme interaction, the authors find that the crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia of a neutron star can be at most 3.6% due to crustal entrainment, and that this fraction, combined with glitch observations, provides a radius constraint for the Vela pulsar of R >= 3.69 + 3.44 M/M_sun, but only under the condition that the glitches originate in the liquid of the inner crust.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme effective interaction parameter set KDE0v1 to construct the equation of state of beta-equilibrated nuclear matter. The core-crust transition density and pressure were determined from the thermodynamic stability condition (spinodal method) by setting the thermal stability coefficient V_thermal to zero. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved numerically to obtain the mass-radius relation and total moment of inertia for slowly rotating neutron stars, and the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia was computed using an approximate expression depending on the transition pressure and density.

## Key numbers

- Core-crust transition density: rho_t = 0.0904 fm^-3
- Core-crust transition pressure: P_t = 0.5013 MeV fm^-3
- Proton fraction at transition density: Y_p(rho_t) = 0.0415
- Maximum neutron star mass from the KDE0v1 EOS: ~1.98 M_sun, with corresponding radius ~9.85 km
- Crustal fraction of total moment of inertia can be at most 3.6% due to crustal entrainment
- Vela pulsar radius constraint: R >= 3.69 + 3.44 M/M_sun
- For pulsars with masses 1.8 M_sun or less, the crustal fraction of total moment of inertia is greater than 0.014 (i.e., >1.4%)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star crust-core transitions using Skyrme interactions, specifically building on the selection of the KDE0v1 parameter set from the CSkP family as the only one satisfying constraints from maximum mass and central density (Dutra, 2012). The results for the crustal fraction of moment of inertia and the Vela pulsar radius constraint are in agreement with calculations using the Simple Effective Interaction (Routray, 2016) and the Density Dependent M3Y interaction (Agrawal, 2016). The work enables tighter constraints on neutron star radii from pulsar glitch observations, particularly for the Vela pulsar, by linking the observed minimum crustal fraction of moment of inertia (1.4%) to the core-crust transition properties.
