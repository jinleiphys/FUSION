# Modified Zimanyi-Moszkowski Model for Finite Nuclei

> By scaling the mesonic masses in the Walecka model, to the inverse of the hadronic effective mass in medium, we generate the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) models added to a surface term. For infinite nuclear matter, the surface term vanishes and this new model becomes equivalent to the usual ZM model. For finite nuclei calculations, the surface contribution changes the spin-orbit splitting in the right experimental direction. Calculations for some nuclei are presented.

# Modified Zimanyi-Moszkowski Model for Finite Nuclei

## Key claim
By scaling the mesonic masses in the Walecka model to the inverse of the hadronic effective mass in medium, the authors generate Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) models with an additional surface term that vanishes in infinite nuclear matter but improves spin-orbit splitting for finite nuclei calculations, without introducing new free parameters.

## Method
The authors start from the Walecka model Lagrangian and perform a rescaling of the scalar meson mass as m*_σ = m_σ/(1 - g_σ σ/M), and optionally also the vector meson mass as m*_ω = m_ω/(1 - α g_σ σ/M) with α = 0 for MZM and α = 1 for MZM3. This produces a modified Lagrangian where the derivative mesonic term (the surface term) survives in finite nuclei calculations at the mean-field level, unlike in infinite nuclear matter where it vanishes. The resulting equations are solved self-consistently for finite nuclei using the same coupling constants as the original ZM and ZM3 models.

## Key numbers
- For infinite nuclear matter, the compression modulus K = 224 MeV and nucleonic effective mass M* = 797 MeV are the same as the original ZM model.
- For ¹⁶O, the MZM3 model gives a p₃/₂ - p₁/₂ spin-orbit splitting of 4 MeV.
- The surface term contribution is more relevant for ¹⁶O (where most nucleons are at the surface) and decreases for heavier nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context
This work extends the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990) and its variant ZM3 (Delfino et al., 1995), which were known to produce soft equations of state but insufficient spin-orbit splitting in finite nuclei calculations (Sharma et al., 1993; Wald et al., 1993; Koepf et al., 1993; Chiapparini et al., 1995). The modified versions MZM and MZM3 improve spin-orbit splitting without adding free parameters, providing a better starting point than the original ZM models for extensions such as the tensor coupling proposed by Biró and Zimanyi (1997). The mesonic mass scaling introduced here is opposite to the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) obtained from chiral models, where mesonic masses scale as m* rather than 1/m*.
