# A unified approach to electron and neutrino elastic scattering off nuclei with an application to the study of the axial structure

> We show a relationship between elastic electron scattering observables and the elastic neutrino cross section that provides a straightforward determination of the latter from experimental data of the former and relates their uncertainties. An illustration of this procedure is presented using a Hartree-Fock mean field for the nuclear structure of a set of even-even nuclear targets, using the spectra of the neutrinos produced in pion decay at rest. We also analyze the prospects to measure the incoherent axial contribution to the neutrino elastic scattering in odd targets.

# A unified approach to electron and neutrino elastic scattering off nuclei with an application to the study of the axial structure

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the elastic neutrino-nucleus cross section can be directly obtained from parity-violating elastic electron scattering measurements via the relation (dσ/dΩ)_(ν,ν) = A_(e,e)^2 × (dσ/dΩ)_(e,e), valid within the plane wave Born approximation and the extreme relativistic limit for leptons, but only exactly for even-even nuclear targets where coherent scattering dominates; for non-even-even targets the relation is only an approximation.

## Method

The authors use an axially symmetric Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean field with BCS pairing to compute the nuclear ground states of ten even-even nuclei (12C, 20Ne, 28Si, 40Ar, 48Ca, 76Ge, 114Cd, 130Te, 132Xe, 208Pb) in plane wave Born approximation. They calculate parity-conserving elastic electron cross sections, parity-violating asymmetries, and elastic neutrino cross sections at 100 MeV incident lepton energy, then fold the neutrino cross sections with the Michel spectra from pion decay at rest. For odd-mass targets, they estimate the axial contribution using harmonic oscillator wave functions within an extreme shell model.

## Key numbers

- The relationship (dσ/dΩ)_(ν,ν) = A_(e,e)^2 × (dσ/dΩ)_(e,e) holds for even-even targets in PWBA and ERL, with an additional factor α^ν/(a_A^e)^2 that takes the Standard Model value of 1
- For N=Z nuclei at low momentum transfer, the simplified relation gives κ = 6.84×10^(-9) GeV^(-4) as the proportionality constant
- The weak mixing angle sin^2θ_W ≈ 0.23 sets the long-wavelength limit of the WNC Coulomb monopole form factor to A × sin^2θ_W
- The ratio of PC electron, PV electron, and neutrino scattering probabilities follows approximately 1 : 3×10^(-4) q^2 : 3×10^(-10) q^4, with q in GeV
- For odd targets at q→0, the axial-to-total cross section ratio R(0) is largest for 7Li (8.6%), 9Be (5.2%), and 11B (3.5%)
- The PREX experiment aims for 1.2% precision in the PV observable on 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Donnelly and collaborators (1979) on multipole decomposition of electroweak nuclear responses, and provides a practical bridge between parity-violating electron scattering experiments (such as PREX I/II on 208Pb and CREX on 48Ca) and the emerging neutrino elastic scattering program at SNS-ORNL (Scholberg, 2006) and dark matter detectors (Anderson et al., 2011). The relationship enables extraction of neutrino cross sections from existing electron scattering data, and the axial sensitivity analysis identifies 7Li, 9Be, and 11B as promising targets for future measurements of the axial structure of nuclei, complementing neutron density determinations from coherent scattering (Patton et al., 2012).
