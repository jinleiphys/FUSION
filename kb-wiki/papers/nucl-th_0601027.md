# Radiative decay of the Lambda(1520)

> A recently developed non-perturbative chiral approach to dynamically generate the (3/2^-) baryon resonances has been extended to investigate the radiative decays Lambda^*(1520) --> gamma Lambda(1116) and Lambda^*(1520) --> gamma Sigma^0(1193). We show that the Lambda^*(1520) decay into gamma Lambda is an ideal test for the need of extra components of the resonance beyond those provided by the chiral approach since the largest meson-baryon components give no contribution to this decay. The case is different for gamma Sigma decay where the theory agrees with experiment, though the large uncertainties of these data call for more precise measurements.

## Key claim

The Lambda*(1520) radiative decay into gamma Lambda provides an ideal test for the need of extra components of the resonance beyond those provided by the chiral approach, because the largest meson-baryon components give no contribution to this decay; in contrast, for gamma Sigma decay the theory agrees with experiment, though the large uncertainties of these data call for more precise measurements.

## Method

The authors extended a non-perturbative chiral unitary approach that dynamically generates the 3/2- baryon resonances via coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter resummation of meson-baryon interactions. They included both s-wave channels (pi Sigma* and K Xi*) from the decuplet-octet interaction and d-wave channels (anti-K N and pi Sigma) with effective transition potentials, using coupling strengths fixed from previous fits to anti-K N to anti-K N and anti-K N to pi Sigma data. The radiative decay amplitudes were computed from Kroll-Ruderman and meson-pole diagrams coupling the photon to the meson-baryon loops that generate the resonance.

## Key numbers

* The coupling strengths of the dynamically generated Lambda*(1520) to its channels are: g(Lambda* pi Sigma*) = 0.91, g(Lambda* K Xi*) = -0.29, g(Lambda* anti-K N) = -0.54, g(Lambda* pi Sigma) = -0.45 (dimensionless, from residue at the pole).
* The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 93 MeV, with f = 1.15 f_pi for the s-wave interaction kernel.
* The subtraction constant for s-wave loops is a_i = -2 at a renormalization scale of mu = 700 MeV.
* The cut-off for the pi Sigma* loop function is Lambda = 418 MeV, matched to dimensional regularization at sqrt(s) = 1520 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Sarkar (2004) and Sarkar (2005) that dynamically generates the Lambda*(1520) as a meson-baryon quasibound state, now investigating radiative decays to test the resonance's composition. The results contrast with the quark model of Kaxiras (1985), where the gamma Sigma decay is suppressed due to selection rules from strange quark excitation, while in the present hadronic picture that decay appears enhanced. The study enables future experimental measurements with improved precision to distinguish between dynamically generated and genuine three-quark components in the Lambda*(1520) wave function.
