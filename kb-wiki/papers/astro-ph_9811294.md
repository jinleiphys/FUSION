# Effects of Strong and Electromagnetic Correlations on Neutrino Interactions in Dense Matter

> An extensive study of the effects of correlations on both charged and neutral current weak interaction rates in dense matter is performed. Both strong and electromagnetic correlations are considered.The propagation of particle-hole interactions in the medium plays an important role in determining the neutrino mean free paths. The effects due to Pauli-Blocking and density, spin, and isospin correlations in the medium significantly reduce the neutrino cross sections. Due to the lack of experimental information at high density, these correlations are necessarily model dependent. For example, spin correlations in nonrelativistic models are found to lead to larger suppressions of neutrino cross sections compared to those of relativistic models. This is due to the tendency of the nonrelativistic models to develop spin instabilities. Notwithstanding the above caveats, and the differences between nonrelativistic and relativistic approaches such as the spin- and isospin-dependent interactions and the nucleon effective masses, suppressions of order 2--3, relative to the case in which correlations are ignored, are obtained. Neutrino interactions in dense matter are especially important for supernova and early neutron star evolution calculations. The effects of correlations for protoneutron star evolution are calculated. Large effects on the internal thermodynamic properties of protoneutron stars, such as the temperature, are found. These translate into significant early enhancements in the emitted neutrino energies and fluxes, especially after a few seconds. At late times, beyond about 10 seconds, the emitted neutrino fluxes decrease more rapidly compared to simulations without the effects of correlations, due to the more rapid onset of neutrino transparency in the protoneutron star

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that strong and electromagnetic correlations in dense matter suppress neutrino cross sections by factors of 2-3 relative to the uncorrelated case, but the magnitude of this suppression is highly model-dependent due to the lack of experimental constraints on spin-dependent interactions at high density, with nonrelativistic models showing larger suppressions than relativistic models because they tend to develop spin instabilities.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrino mean free paths and cross sections for both neutral current scattering and charged current absorption in dense matter using the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to sum particle-hole ring diagrams to all orders, incorporating both strong and electromagnetic correlations. They employed both nonrelativistic potential models (Skyrme-type and microscopic Brueckner G-matrix approaches) and relativistic field-theoretical models (sigma-omega-rho Walecka-type models) to evaluate the density, spin, and isospin dependent particle-hole interactions. The resulting opacities were then used as inputs to simulate protoneutron star evolution, tracking the time-dependent neutrino luminosities and mean energies.

## Key numbers

- Suppression factors of order 2-3 in neutrino cross sections relative to the case where correlations are ignored, obtained across both nonrelativistic and relativistic approaches
- Neutrino mean free paths enhanced by a factor proportional to (M/M*)^2 due to the dropping in-medium nucleon effective mass, where M* = 0.8M at nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 for the illustrative Skyrme model
- At late times beyond about 10 seconds, emitted neutrino fluxes decrease more rapidly compared to simulations without correlation effects due to more rapid onset of neutrino transparency in the protoneutron star
- For the microscopic model of Backmann et al., large repulsive spin interactions (G0 > 1) enhance the neutrino mean free path by as much as a factor of four relative to the Hartree result
- Skyrme models SLy4 and SLy5 have nuclear compressibility K0 = 230 MeV and were constrained to reproduce the pressure versus density curve for pure neutron matter from microscopic calculations

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Reddy, Prakash, and Lattimer (1998) for calculating neutrino opacities in interacting matter to include in-medium correlations through RPA summation, building on pioneering work by Sawyer (1975) who showed the importance of strong interactions, and Iwamoto and Pethick (1982) who computed neutrino mean free paths in pure neutron matter using Fermi-liquid theory. The results enable more realistic protoneutron star evolution calculations that incorporate the intimate relationship between the equation of state and neutrino opacities, with the simultaneous independent work of Burrows and Sawyer (1998) providing a complementary approach with different microphysical inputs and equation of state models.
