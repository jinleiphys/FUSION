# Two-Fermion Bound and Scattering States in a Finite Volume including QED in P-Wave and Beyond

> Introducing a short range force coupling the spinless fermions to one unit of angular momentum in the framework of pionless EFT, we first report the two-body scattering amplitudes with Coulomb corrections, extended to two fermions of opposite charge in refs. [1,2]. Motivated by the growing interest in lattice approaches, we immerse the system into a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions and display the finite-volume corrections to the energy of the lowest bound and unbound $T_1^{-}$ eigenstates. The latter turn out to consist of power law terms proportional to the fine-structure constant. In the calculations, quadratic and higher order contributions in $α$ are discarded, on the grounds that the gapped nature of the momentum operator in the finite-volume environment allows for a perturbative treatment of the QED interactions. An outlook on the extension of the analysis to D-wave short-range interactions is eventually given.

# Two-Fermion Bound and Scattering States in a Finite Volume including QED in P-Wave and Beyond

## Key claim

The paper derives finite-volume corrections to the energy of the lowest bound and unbound P-wave eigenstates for two spinless fermions interacting via a short-range force and Coulomb interactions, finding that these corrections consist of power-law terms proportional to the fine-structure constant rather than exponential damping factors. The analysis is restricted to linear order in the fine-structure constant, with quadratic and higher-order contributions discarded based on the argument that the gapped nature of the momentum operator in a finite volume permits a perturbative treatment of QED interactions.

## Method

The author employs pionless effective field theory (EFT) with a short-range potential coupling spinless fermions to one unit of angular momentum (P-wave), and incorporates Coulomb interactions via non-relativistic QED (NRQED) retaining only scalar photon exchange. The two-body scattering amplitudes with Coulomb corrections are derived in infinite volume using dimensional regularization with the Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) scheme, then the system is placed in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions to obtain finite-volume quantization conditions. The finite-volume energy eigenvalues for the lowest bound and unbound states transforming under the T1 irreducible representation of the cubic group are computed by expanding the Luescher functions and solving the effective-range expansion iteratively to leading order in the fine-structure constant.

## Key numbers

- The lowest unbound state energy in finite volume is given by E = 4π²/(ML²) plus corrections; the leading finite-volume shift for the lowest unbound P-wave state contains terms proportional to 4πa'C^(1)/(ML³) multiplied by a factor of 6ξ, where ξ = 4π²/(ML²)
- The leading-order finite-volume shift for the lowest bound P-wave state is ΔE_B^(1,T1) = -αI^(0)/(πL), where I^(0) ≈ -8.913632 is the regulated 3D Riemann sum
- The P-wave bound state shift has the same magnitude but opposite sign compared to the S-wave shift: ΔE_B^(1,T1) = -ΔE_B^(0,A1) at leading order
- The condition for perturbative treatment of QED in finite volume requires ML ≪ 1/α, which is satisfied for sufficiently large volumes as in present-day lattice QCD calculations
- The effective range parameter r0^(1) vanishes for pure strong P-wave interactions, while the Coulomb-corrected effective range is r0^(1) = αM[2/(3-d) + 8/3 - 3γE + 2log(μ√π/(αM))] - 3μ

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative application of QED in pionless EFT from S-wave interactions (Kong and Ravndal, 2000) to P-wave interactions, and generalizes the finite-volume analysis of two-body systems with Coulomb corrections (Beane and Savage, 2014) to higher partial waves. The finite-volume corrections for P-wave states show power-law behavior proportional to α/L, in contrast to the exponential damping factors found with short-range forces alone (Luescher, 1986, 1991). The results enable lattice QCD calculations to incorporate QED corrections for P-wave bound and scattering states, which is relevant for studies of systems such as protonium and for lattice calculations at unphysical quark masses where same-charge bound states may appear.
