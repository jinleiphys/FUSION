# Effective Range Approximation in Variable Phase Approach for Triplet $^3S_1^{\{np\}}$ and Singlet $^1S_0^{ \{nn, np, pp\}}$ State

> This work is a short communication where phase function method has been applied to obtain the phase shifts using Effective Range Approximation potential for $^3S_1-np$, $^1S_0-nn$, $^1S_0-np$, and $^1S_0-pp$ states. No free fitting parameters are used in calculations and reasonably good match with the experimental phase shifts is observed for E $\leq$ 20 MeV. Potentials are obtained for n-n, n-p, and p-p scattering that are exponential well-shaped.

# Effective Range Approximation in Variable Phase Approach for Triplet \(^3S_1^{\{np\}}\) and Singlet \(^1S_0^{\{nn, np, pp\}}\) State

## Key claim
The phase function method combined with an effective range approximation potential produces scattering phase shifts for nucleon-nucleon systems that match experimental data reasonably well, but only for laboratory-frame projectile energies up to 20 MeV; beyond this energy, deviations appear for all singlet \(^1S_0\) states due to the absence of a repulsive core in the exponential well-shaped potential.

## Method
The author applied the variable phase approach (also called the phase function method) by solving the first-order Riccati-type differential equation for the S-wave phase shift using a Runge-Kutta 5th order method. The potential used was an exponential well-shaped form derived from the effective range approximation, with parameters determined solely from the scattering length and effective range for each system (nn, np, pp) without any free fitting parameters. The resulting phase shifts were compared to the partial-wave analysis data of Wiringa (1995) and the Granada group (2016).

## Key numbers
- For the triplet \(^3S_1\)-np state, the potential depth is approximately 70 MeV.
- The phase shift calculations show good agreement with experimental data for laboratory-frame projectile energies E \(\leq\) 20 MeV for all four systems studied.
- For the \(^1S_0\)-nn state, Darewych et al. found a potential depth \(V_0 = 40.38\) MeV, while for the \(^1S_0\)-np state they found \(V_0 = 61.99\) MeV, indicating a substantial difference attributed to charge independence violation.
- Konobeevski et al. reported that a 7% change in the nn potential may lead to a 20-30% change in the scattering length calculation.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous applications of the variable phase approach to various scattering systems including n-n, n-p, \(n-\alpha\), n-d, \(\alpha-\alpha\), and the astrophysical \(\alpha-^3He\) reaction. The study directly compares its results to the partial-wave analyses of Wiringa (1995) and the Granada group (2016), who analyzed 6713 published np and pp scattering data below 350 MeV. The paper also engages with the long-standing debate on charge independence and charge symmetry violations in nuclear forces, referencing the work of Darewych et al., Henley et al., and Babenko and Petrov. The approach enables future inverse-potential constructions for low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering using only scattering length and effective range as inputs, without requiring free parameters.
