# Electron in a transverse harmonic potential

> Non-perturbative solutions to the quantum-field theory is a topic of current and broad interest, especially for the heavy ion and laser physics communities, since they investigate particle production in the presence of strong external fields. We have solved a non-perturbative QED + external field problem of an electron in a strong transverse confining potential using Hamiltonian light-front quantum field theory in a basis function representation. The invariant mass spectra and the anomalous magnetic moment of the lowest state for this two-scale system are also evaluated. With this method the perturbative QED results are also reproduced with a good accuracy. We also discuss the extension of the method to other problems as well.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Basis Light Front Quantized (BLFQ) approach can reproduce perturbative QED results (the Schwinger anomalous magnetic moment) to within 1% accuracy in the non-renormalized case when extrapolated to the zero external field limit, but the renormalized results deviate by about 20% from the Schwinger value due to the use of a dynamical electron mass in the intermediate state propagators.

## Method

The authors solved the light-front QED Hamiltonian for an electron confined by a transverse harmonic oscillator potential, using a basis of 2-D harmonic oscillator states for transverse modes and discretized longitudinal momentum modes with periodic/antiperiodic boundary conditions. The Fock space was truncated to include only single electron states and electron-plus-photon states, with the Hamiltonian matrix diagonalized for increasing basis sizes defined by cutoffs K (longitudinal momentum) and N_max (transverse quanta). The anomalous magnetic moment was extracted from the lowest mass eigenstate by evaluating the probability amplitude for spin-flip relative to the single electron Fock-space component.

## Key numbers

- The non-renormalized extrapolation to zero external field yielded a scaled anomalous magnetic moment sqrt(delta mu / g^2) = 0.1121, compared to the Schwinger result of 0.1125 (less than 1% deviation)
- The renormalized continuum extrapolation gave values of 0.1362 (for Omega >= 0.2 MeV fits) and 0.1383 (for Omega >= 0.1 MeV fits), approximately 20% above the Schwinger result
- The Hamiltonian matrix dimensions for N_max = K = 2, 10, 20 were d = 2, 1670, and 26990 respectively
- The electron mass was set to 0.511 MeV and the fine structure constant to alpha = 1/137.036
- The transverse harmonic oscillator energy Omega was varied, with the non-renormalized results requiring the mass eigenvalue to fall within 25% of the free electron mass

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach introduced by Vary (2009) for solving light-front quantum field theories non-perturbatively, applying it specifically to QED with an external confining potential. The renormalized results are consistent with related works by Brodsky (2004) and Chabysheva (2009) that used Pauli-Villars regularization for one-photon truncated light-front Hamiltonians. The method enables future studies of non-perturbative QED relevant to anomalous lepton production at RHIC and super-critical field generation at next-generation laser facilities, and serves as a foundation for extending the approach to light-front QCD in the non-perturbative domain.
