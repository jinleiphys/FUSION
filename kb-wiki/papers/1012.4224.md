# From EMC- and Cronin-effects to signals of quark-gluon plasma

> The EMC- and Cronin-effects are explained by a unitarized evolution equation, where the shadowing and antishadowing corrections are dynamically produced by gluon fusions. For this sake, an alternative form of the GLR-MQ-ZRS equation is derived. The resulting integrated and unintegrated gluon distributions in proton and nuclei are used to analyze the contributions of the initial parton distributions to the nuclear suppression factor in heavy ion collisions. A simulation of the fractional energy loss is extracted from the RHIC and LHC data, where the contributions of the nuclear shadowing and antishadowing effects are considered. We find a rapid crossover from week energy loss to strong energy loss at a universal critical energy of gluon jet $E_c\sim 10 GeV$.

## Key claim

The authors derive an alternative form of the GLR-MQ-ZRS evolution equation that dynamically produces both nuclear shadowing and antishadowing effects from gluon fusion, and they find a rapid crossover from weak to strong energy loss for gluon jets at a universal critical energy of approximately 10 GeV, with the caveat that this result depends on subtracting the initial-state nuclear effects from the measured nuclear suppression factor.

## Method

The authors reformulate the GLR-MQ-ZRS equation at the double-leading-logarithmic-approximation (DLLA) to evolve unintegrated gluon distributions along the Bjorken x direction, starting from a large x0 where nonlinear corrections are negligible. They solve this equation for both proton and nuclear targets, scaling the nonlinear terms by A^(1/3) for nuclei, and use the resulting integrated and unintegrated gluon distributions to compute the nuclear modification factor R_dA in d+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, from which they extract the fractional energy loss by comparing with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Universal critical energy for gluon jet: E_c ~ 10 GeV, marking the crossover from weak to strong energy loss.
- Starting point of evolution: x0 = 0.15, where nonlinear gluon recombination begins.
- Kinematic regions: shadowing and antishadowing coexist for x ≤ 0.15; only antishadowing exists for 0.15 ≤ x ≤ 0.3.
- Correlation length parameter: R = 2.4 GeV^(-1); minimum k_t^2 = 0.01 GeV^2; alpha_s = 0.3; Delta = 0.02.
- Predicted Q^2-slope for calcium structure function ratio: at x ~ 4×10^(-2), b ~ -0.046; at x ~ 10^(-2), b ~ -0.03.
- Antishadowing effect on gluon jet at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and y=0: distributed in range 8 GeV < k_t < 60 GeV, corresponding to x range 0.02 < x < 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the GLR-MQ-ZRS approach of Zhu, Ruan, and Shen (2008-2010) by deriving a new form of the evolution equation that avoids non-perturbative nuclear modifications to input distributions, in contrast to the DGLAP-based EPS09 analysis of Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado (2009) which requires phenomenological initial conditions. The results contradict saturation models (JIMWLK equation) that neglect antishadowing, and the extracted energy loss enables future studies of quark-gluon plasma properties by separating initial-state nuclear effects from final-state medium modifications in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
