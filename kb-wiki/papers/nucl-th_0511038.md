# Charged and neutral pion production in the S-matrix approach

> The S-matrix approach is used to calculate charged as well as neutral pion production reactions from NN scattering, with the same set of underlying processes and interactions. The chiral perturbation theory piN scattering amplitude is used. For the nucleon-nucleon distortions a newly developed realistic potential within the Bonn family of potentials, valid well above the pion production threshold, is considered. In the pi+ production case, the NN potential, the piN relative p-waves and the treatment of the exchanged pion energy build up the observed cross-section strength.

## Key claim

The S-matrix approach, when combined with a nucleon-nucleon potential tuned above the pion production threshold and a sufficient number of three-body channels with piN relative p-waves, overcomes the problem of insufficient production strength in the pi+ charge channel that had been found in prior work (daRocha 1999) using the same chiral perturbation theory piN amplitude.

## Method

The authors apply the S-matrix prescription to derive effective operators for pion re-scattering, direct production, Z-diagrams mediated by scalar and vector exchanges, and explicit Delta-isobar excitation. The chiral perturbation theory piN scattering amplitude is used for the irreducible pion re-scattering term, and the nucleon-nucleon distortions are described by a newly developed realistic potential from the Ohio group that includes two-boson-exchange box contributions with Delta(1232) and N*(1440) intermediate states, valid up to 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The static approximation (Omega=0) overestimates the cross section by a factor of 2 close to threshold for all charge channels.
- The on-shell approximation deviates from the reference result by up to 20% for all charge channels, even close to threshold.
- For pp -> pn pi+, the fixed kinematics approximation underestimates the cross section by a factor of 1.5-2.5 near threshold and by a factor of 5 at higher energies.
- The c3 parameter in the chiral perturbation theory amplitude is redefined from c3 = -5.29 GeV^{-1} to c3' = c3 - c3^Delta, with c3^Delta approximately -2.78 GeV^{-1}.
- For pi0 production, the J=0 channel alone suffices for convergence; for pi- production, J=1 channels are needed; for pi+ production, J=2 channels are needed for convergence.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix approach previously applied to neutral pion production (Malafaia 2004) to charged pion channels, and addresses the discrepancy with experimental data by factors of 2-5 reported in daRocha (1999) for pi+ production. The results are consistent with trends found in the Juelich phenomenological model (Hanhart 1995), but unlike that model no adjustment of short-range mechanisms is made. The calculations should be relevant for high-precision studies such as charge symmetry breaking investigations.
