# Emulating generator coordinate method with extended eigenvector continuation: Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model

> We present a benchmark study of generator coordinate method (GCM) combined with eigenvector continuation (EC) in two different schemes for the low-lying states of Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model, where the interaction strength is treated as a controlling parameter, simulating quantum many-body systems with the phase transition from non-collective to collective states. We demonstrate that the EC$_{\rm kmax}$ scheme accurately reproduces the low-lying states of the LMG model. In this scheme, the EC basis consists of the wave functions of low-lying states up to the $k_{\rm max}$-th state of sampling Hamiltonians. Compared to EC$_1$, which only includes the wave functions of the $k$-th state of sampling Hamiltonians for the $k$-th state of a target Hamiltonian, the EC$_{\rm kmax}$ scheme exhibits significantly improved efficiency and accuracy. This study suggests the potential utilization of the extended EC scheme as an efficient emulator for GCM calculations.

## Key claim

The EC$_{\rm kmax}$ scheme, which includes wave functions of low-lying states up to the $k_{\rm max}$-th state of sampling Hamiltonians, accurately reproduces the low-lying states of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model across a range of interaction strengths, including across the phase transition from non-collective to collective states. The abstract's claim of "significantly improved efficiency and accuracy" is conditional on using a sufficient number of low-lying states ($k_{\rm max}=20$ in the benchmark), and the scheme works even with only a single sampling Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors benchmarked two eigenvector continuation (EC) schemes combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) for the LMG model, where the interaction strength $\chi = V(\Omega-1)/\varepsilon$ serves as a controlling parameter. In the EC$_1$ scheme, the $k$-th state of a target Hamiltonian is expanded using only the $k$-th state wave functions of sampling Hamiltonians; in the EC$_{\rm kmax}$ scheme, the EC basis consists of the wave functions of the lowest $k_{\rm max}$ states from each sampling Hamiltonian. The GCM wave functions were constructed from Hartree-Fock (HF) states with generator coordinates $\alpha$ and $\varphi$, and the EC+GCM results were compared against exact diagonalization solutions for systems with $\Omega=3$, $8$, and $30$ particles.

## Key numbers

- For $\Omega=30$ particles with $\chi=-3$, the EC$_{\rm kmax=20}$+GCM calculation using only one sampling Hamiltonian ($N_t=1$) reproduced the exact energies of the first three states across the range $\chi\in[-4, 4]$, while the EC$_1$+GCM(1) scheme "totally fails" for the same range.
- The overlap between the 1st and 3rd states from EC$_1$+GCM calculations for the target Hamiltonian with $\chi_\odot=-3$ was $\log_{10}(\langle \Psi^1_{\rm EC}|\Psi^3_{\rm EC}\rangle) = -0.2$, indicating non-orthogonality between states of the same parity.
- In the EC$_{\rm kmax}$+ISM study cited, choosing $k_{\rm max}=5$ improved the relative error of the five lowest states of four $sd$-shell nuclei by a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation method, previously applied primarily to ground states in nuclear structure (Ekstrom 2019, Konig 2020, Furnstahl 2020), to excited states within the generator coordinate method framework. It directly compares two EC schemes: the EC$_1$ approach previously used with many-body perturbation theory (Franzke 2022) and the EC$_{\rm kmax}$ approach previously combined with the interacting shell model (Yoshida 2022). The results demonstrate that the EC$_{\rm kmax}$ scheme can serve as an efficient emulator for GCM calculations, potentially enabling uncertainty quantification for GCM-based studies of nuclear low-lying states, nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay (Yao 2022), and medium-mass deformed nuclei (Yao 2020).
