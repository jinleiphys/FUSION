# Ground state properties and bubble structure of the isotopic chains of Z = 125 and 126 using the relativistic mean-field formalism

> The ground state properties of Z = 125 and 126 nuclei are investigated, taking the isotopic series from the proton to neutron drip-lines. This analysis is conducted using the relativistic mean-field approach with NL3 and the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov model with DD-ME2 parameterization. The bulk properties under examination include the binding energy, the neutron separation energies, the differential variation of the separation energy, the quadrupole deformation parameter $β_2$, and the single-particle energy. We observed the stability at N = 172 and 184 over the isotopic chain for both parameter sets. The quadrupole deformation parameter reveals a shape transition from prolate to spherical and back to prolate with mass number. No signature of a super- and/or hyper-deformed structure is found over the isotopic chain. Furthermore, the analysis is extended to examine the bubble structure, revealing a bubble/semi-bubble structure for a few neutron-rich isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for the isotopic chains of Z = 125 and 126, enhanced stability (indicated by drops in separation energy) occurs at neutron numbers N = 172 and 184, and that a bubble or semi-bubble structure appears in a few neutron-rich isotopes. The hedge is that the shell gaps at these neutron numbers are relatively small (approximately 1.5 to 2.0 MeV), and the bubble structure is only predicted for specific isotopes, not the entire chain.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the NL3 parameter set and the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-ME2 parameter set to calculate ground-state properties of isotopes from the proton drip-line to the neutron drip-line for Z = 125 and Z = 126. They computed binding energy, one- and two-neutron separation energies, quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2, single-particle energy levels, and nucleon density profiles. Pairing correlations were treated using the constant gap approximation of Madland and Nix. Results were compared with the macroscopic-microscopic Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and the WS4 global mass formula.

## Key numbers
* Binding energy of spherical $^{309}$125: 2132.575 MeV (RMF), 2134.965 MeV (RHB), compared to 2138.34 MeV (FRDM) and 2138.1880 MeV (WS4).
* Binding energy of $^{323}$125: 2211.4 MeV (RMF) and 2217.5 MeV (RHB), compared to 2228.71 MeV (FRDM) and 2226.9548 MeV (WS4).
* For Z = 125, the drop in one-neutron separation energy S_n at N = 172 is 1.438 MeV (RMF) and 1.204 MeV (RHB); at N = 184 it is 1.92 MeV (RMF) and 1.56 MeV (RHB).
* For Z = 125, the drop in two-neutron separation energy S_{2n} at N = 172 is 1.114 MeV (RMF) and 1.048 MeV (RHB); at N = 184 it is 1.81 MeV (RMF) and 1.664 MeV (RHB).
* For Z = 126, the drop in S_n at N = 172 is 1.297 MeV (RMF) and 1.098 MeV (RHB); at N = 184 it is 1.80 MeV (RMF) and 1.5126 MeV (RHB).
* For Z = 126, the drop in S_{2n} at N = 172 is 0.96 MeV (RMF) and 0.8969 MeV (RHB); at N = 184 it is 1.72 MeV (RMF) and 1.6218 MeV (RHB).
* Depletion factor for $^{297}$125 (N = 172, Z = 125): 30.03% (neutrons) and 34.12% (protons) in RMF (NL3); 33.0% in RHB (DD-ME2).
* Depletion factor for $^{298}$126 (N = 172, Z = 126): 30.26% (neutrons) and 34.29% (protons) in RMF (NL3); 32.6% in RHB (DD-ME2).
* Depletion factor for $^{323}$125 and $^{324}$126: approximately 12.0% for both neutrons and protons in RMF (NL3); 17.2% in RHB (DD-ME2).
* Shell gap at N = 126 and N = 184 is approximately 2.5 MeV; shell gap at Z = 114 is approximately 1.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical investigations of superheavy nuclei stability, particularly following studies of Z = 120 by Bhuyan et al. (2012) and Z = 124 by Mehta et al. (2015), which identified magicity at N = 172, 184, and 198 using the RMF model. It also builds on predictions from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach (Cwiok et al., 1996) and the Finite-Range Droplet Model (Moller et al., 2016) for shell closures beyond Z = 82 and N = 126. The findings of bubble/semi-bubble structure in $^{297}$125 and $^{298}$126 enable future experimental searches for central density depletion in neutron-rich superheavy nuclei, and the identification of N = 172 and 184 as regions of enhanced stability provides targets for synthesis efforts.
