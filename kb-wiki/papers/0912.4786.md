# SU(3) quasidynamical symmetry underlying the Alhassid--Whelan arc of regularity

> The first example of an empirically manifested quasi dynamical symmetry trajectory in the interior of the symmetry triangle of the Interacting Boson Approximation model is identified for large boson numbers. Along this curve, extending from SU(3) to near the critical line of the first order phase transition, spectra exhibit nearly the same degeneracies that characterize the low energy levels of SU(3). This trajectory also lies close to the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity, the unique interior region of regular behavior connecting the SU(3) and U(5) vertices, thus offering a possible symmetry-based interpretation of that narrow zone of regularity amidst regions of more chaotic spectra.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the first example of a quasidynamical symmetry (QDS) trajectory in the interior of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) symmetry triangle, not just on its legs. This trajectory, defined by the degeneracy condition \(E(2_\beta^+)=E(2_\gamma^+)\), extends from the SU(3) vertex to near the first-order phase transition critical line and lies close to the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity, offering a symmetry-based interpretation of that narrow zone of regular behavior.

## Method

The authors used the IBA Hamiltonian \(H(\eta,\chi) = c[\eta \hat n_d + (\eta-1)/N_B \hat Q^\chi \cdot \hat Q^\chi]\) with parameters \(\eta\) (0 to 1) and \(\chi\) (0 to \(-\sqrt{7}/2 \approx -1.32\)). They performed numerical calculations of energy levels for boson numbers \(N_B = 25, 100, 250\) using the code IBAR. To quantify SU(3) degeneracy preservation, they computed two rms deviation measures: \(\sigma_{\beta\gamma}\) comparing energies of \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) bands up to \(L_{max}=10\), and \(\sigma_0\) comparing the lowest nine \(0^+\) bandhead energies to SU(3) predictions, both normalized to \(E(2_1^+)\) or \(E(0_2^+)\).

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) QDS trajectory for \(N_B=250\) ranges from \((\eta, \chi) \sim (0.8, -0.5)\) to \((0, -1.32)\) at the SU(3) vertex.
- For \(N_B=250\) along the trajectory, \(\sigma_0 < 0.1\) almost to \(\eta=0.8\) and \(\sigma_{\beta\gamma} < 0.4\) down to \(\eta \sim 0.7\).
- At the point \(\eta=0.632, \chi=-0.882\) with \(N_B=250\), deviations from SU(3) for nearly all low-lying states are less than 5\%.
- For \(N_B=250\), the lowest 110 out of 5334 \(0^+\) states exhibit SU(3) degeneracies clearly.
- Interband \(B(E2)\) values belonging to different SU(3) irreps remain at least three orders of magnitude lower than intraband \(B(E2)\) values at the degeneracy point.

## Context

This work extends the concept of quasidynamical symmetries, previously studied along the legs of the IBA triangle by Rowe (2004) and others, into the interior of the triangle for the first time. The discovered QDS trajectory closely tracks the Alhassid-Whelan arc of regularity, first identified by Alhassid and Whelan (1992) as a unique interior region of regular behavior amidst chaotic spectra, and the paper suggests this arc reflects an underlying SU(3) QDS. The results enable future studies of how SU(3) degeneracies persist despite strong symmetry-breaking interactions, and open the question of whether other interior QDS trajectories exist, such as near the first-order phase transition line where suspicions were raised by Cejnar and Jolie (2008).
