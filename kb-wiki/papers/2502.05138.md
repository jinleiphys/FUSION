# Collision energy dependence in heavy ion collisions from nonlinear QCD evolution

> We explore the effects of including the energy dependence determined from evolution equations within the color glass condensate framework on observables in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This amounts to integrating the JIMWLK evolution equations into the IP-Glasma model, which is then coupled to viscous relativistic hydrodynamics. This methodology allows for a systematic representation of nuclei at specific Bjorken-$x$ values, which are probed at different center-of-mass energies of the collision and rapidities of final state particles. Comparing to the conventional IP-Glasma model, we find significant effects on multiplicity distributions and particle spectra, especially in smaller collision systems at the highest center of mass energies. Our results highlight the importance of incorporating nonlinear QCD evolution in the description of heavy ion collisions at varying center of mass energies, as the precise extraction of transport coefficients will be affected. This work establishes a robust framework for understanding the quark gluon plasma and nuclear structure at high energy, integrating small-$x$ physics into the initial conditions of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
Incorporating JIMWLK evolution into the IP-Glasma model significantly modifies the spatial gluon distributions of nuclei at small Bjorken-\(x\), leading to flatter centrality-dependent multiplicity distributions and reduced mean transverse momentum compared to the conventional approach where only the saturation scale is adjusted with energy. The effect is most pronounced in smaller collision systems (p-Pb, O-O) at the highest LHC energies, and the authors argue that this energy evolution must be included for accurate extraction of QGP transport coefficients.

## Method
The authors integrated the JIMWLK evolution equations into the IP-Glasma model, initializing the nuclear Wilson lines at \(x=0.01\) and evolving them to the smaller \(x\) values probed at different collision energies and midrapidity. The evolved initial color fields were then used as input for viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with MUSIC, followed by hadronic transport with UrQMD. Two setups were compared: the full JIMWLK-evolved case ("+JIMWLK") and a baseline where only the saturation scale \(Q_s\) depends on \(x\) according to the IPsat parametrization ("\(Q_s(x)\)").

## Key numbers
- The transverse area of the initial energy density profile in p-Pb collisions at LHC energy is approximately **2.7 times** larger with JIMWLK evolution than without it.
- The normalization factor \(K\) for the energy-momentum tensor is **0.115** for the JIMWLK case at both RHIC and LHC energies; for the \(Q_s(x)\) case, \(K_{\rm 200\,GeV} = 0.106\) and \(K_{\rm 5020\,GeV} = K_{\rm 6800\,GeV} = 0.095\).
- In the 60-70% centrality bin, the ratio of Pb-Pb (5.02 TeV) to Au-Au (200 GeV) multiplicity with JIMWLK evolution is approximately **25% larger** than in the \(Q_s(x)\) setup.
- In the 60-70% centrality bin for O-O collisions, the ratio of 6.8 TeV to 200 GeV multiplicity with JIMWLK evolution is approximately **70% larger** than in the \(Q_s(x)\) case.
- Including JIMWLK evolution reduces the mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_T \rangle\) by approximately **20%** in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV compared to the \(Q_s(x)\) setup.

## Context
This work extends the IP-Glasma model (Schenke et al., 2012) by incorporating perturbative JIMWLK evolution (Jalilian-Marian et al., 1997; Iancu et al., 2001) to describe the energy dependence of initial conditions, rather than relying on the phenomenological \(x\)-dependent saturation scale from the IPSat model (Bartels et al., 2002; Kowalski et al., 2003) or the iterative \(Q_s\) determination used in Schenke et al. (2020). It builds on previous studies of JIMWLK evolution in heavy-ion initial states (Schenke et al., 2016; Mantysaari et al., 2023) but provides the first systematic comparison across multiple collision systems (Au-Au, Pb-Pb, p-Pb, p-Au, O-O) and energies. The results enable future global Bayesian analyses to extract QGP transport coefficients (Bernhard et al., 2016; Nijs et al., 2020) with improved initial-state modeling, and establish a direct link between HERA deep inelastic scattering data and heavy-ion observables at RHIC and LHC.
