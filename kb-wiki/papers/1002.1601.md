# Comparative study of three-nucleon force models in $A=3,4$ systems

> Using modern nucleon-nucleon interactions in the description of the $A=3,4$ nuclei, it is not possible to reproduce both the three- and four-nucleon binding energies simultaneously. This is one manifestation of the necessity of including a three-nucleon force in the nuclear Hamiltonian. In this paper we will perform a comparative study of some, widely used, three-nucleon force models. We will analyze their capability to describe the aforementioned binding energies as well as the $n-d$ doublet scattering length. A correct description of these quantities can be considered a stringent requirement for a nuclear Hamiltonian containing two- and three-nucleon interaction terms. As we will show, this requirement is not fulfilled by several of the models available in the literature. To satisfy it, we propose modifications in the parametrization of the three-nucleon forces and we study their effects on few selected $N-d$ low energy scattering observables.

# Comparative study of three-nucleon force models in $A=3,4$ systems

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that widely used three-nucleon force (TNF) models (TM', URIX, and N2LOL), when combined with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential, cannot simultaneously reproduce the triton binding energy, the $^4$He binding energy, and the $n-d$ doublet scattering length without substantial modifications to their parametrizations; the authors propose such modifications and show that only the N2LOL model improves the description of vector polarization observables in $p-d$ scattering at low energy.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method to solve the Schrödinger equation for $A=3,4$ bound and scattering states in configuration space, employing the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential as the reference two-body interaction. They systematically varied the parameters of the TM', URIX, and N2LOL three-nucleon force models, including the cutoff $\Lambda$, the strengths of the $b$-, $d$-, $D$-, and $E$-terms, and the repulsive term $A_R$, to study their effects on the triton binding energy $B(^3\text{H})$, the $^4$He binding energy $B(^4\text{He})$, and the $n-d$ doublet scattering length $^2a_{nd}$, and then computed $p-d$ polarization observables at $E_{lab}=3$ MeV for selected parametrizations.

- $p-d$ scattering energy: $E_{lab}=3$ MeV (context: polarization observables study)

## Context

This work extends the comparative analysis of three-nucleon forces reported in Ref. [report] for $A=3,4$ systems, where combinations of AV18+URIX, AV18+TM', and N3LO-Idaho+N2LOL were shown to fail in simultaneously describing binding energies and the $n-d$ doublet scattering length. The study builds on the Phillips line correlation (Phillips 1968, Bedaque 2000) and prior chiral effective field theory derivations of three-nucleon forces (Epelbaum 2002, N2LO 2008). The new parametrizations enable a systematic comparison of the short-range structure of different TNF models and their effects on low-energy $N-d$ scattering observables, particularly vector analyzing powers, which are sensitive to the repulsive core of the interaction.
