# Baryon number fluctuations in chiral effective models and their phenomenological implications

> We study the critical properties of net-baryon-number fluctuations at the chiral restoration transition in a medium at finite temperature and net baryon density. The chiral dynamics of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) is modeled by the Polykov-loop extended Quark-Meson Lagrangian, that includes the coupling of quarks to vector meson and temporal gauge fields. The Functional Renormalization Group is employed to properly account for the $O(4)$ criticality at the phase boundary. We focus on the properties and systematics of ratios of the net-baryon-number cumulants $χ_B^n$, for ${1\leq n\leq 6}$, near the phase boundary. The results are presented in the context of the recent experimental data of the STAR Collaboration on fluctuations of the net proton number in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We show that the model results for the energy dependence of the cumulant ratios are in good overall agreement with the data, with one exception. At center-of-mass energies below ${19.6\;\mathrm{GeV}}$, we find that the measured fourth-order cumulant deviates considerably from the model results, which incorporate the expected $O(4)$ and $Z(2)$ criticality. We assess the influence of model assumptions and in particular of repulsive vector-interactions, which are used to modify the location of the critical endpoint in the model, on the cumulants ratios. Finally, we discuss a possibility to test to what extent the fluctuations are affected by nonequilibrium dynamics by comparing certain ratios of cumulants.

## Key claim
The PQM model results for the energy dependence of net-baryon-number cumulant ratios agree well with STAR data for ratios involving the mean, variance, and skewness, but the measured fourth-order cumulant ratio deviates considerably from the model results at center-of-mass energies below 19.6 GeV, where the model incorporates the expected O(4) and Z(2) criticality.

## Method
The authors employed the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) to compute net-baryon-number cumulants up to sixth order in the Polyakov-loop extended Quark-Meson (PQM) model, which includes quark couplings to vector meson and temporal gauge fields. The vector meson and Polyakov loop were treated on the mean-field level, while scalar and pseudoscalar meson fluctuations were included via FRG flow equations solved on a grid of the order parameter sigma and also using Chebyshev polynomial expansion for higher precision. Cumulants for nonvanishing vector coupling were obtained from the zero-coupling results using transformation rules derived from the effective chemical potential shift induced by the omega field.

## Key numbers
- The chiral crossover temperature at vanishing net baryon density is T_c = 154(9) MeV, cited from lattice QCD results of Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2011).
- The kurtosis ratio kappa*sigma^2 = chi_B^4/chi_B^2 drops by approximately an order of magnitude at the phase boundary, from about 1 to about 1/9, reflecting the change from baryon (B=1) to quark (B=1/3) degrees of freedom.
- At vanishing chemical potential, the ratio chi_B^3/chi_B^1 equals chi_B^4/chi_B^2 and is consistent with unity at low T and with 2/(3*pi^2) at high T relative to T_c.
- The vector coupling g_omega = 0.7 g_s shifts the CEP to lower T and larger mu_q, reducing the curvature of the phase boundary and suppressing the magnitude of net-baryon-number susceptibilities.

## Context
This work extends previous FRG studies of the PQM model by Friman (2011) that identified negative values of chi_B^6/chi_B^2 as a signature of partial chiral restoration, and it agrees qualitatively with lattice QCD results by Bazavov (2017). The results are compared directly with STAR Collaboration data from the RHIC beam energy scan (BES) covering baryon chemical potentials 20 MeV < mu_B < 500 MeV, as described by Aggarwal (2010). The discrepancy at low energies suggests that nonequilibrium dynamics or other physics beyond the equilibrium critical behavior modeled here may affect the measured kurtosis, and the authors propose comparing certain cumulant ratios to test the role of nonequilibrium effects.
