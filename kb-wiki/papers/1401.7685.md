# Observation of the $β$-delayed $γ$-proton decay of $^{56}$Zn and its impact on the Gamow Teller strength evaluation

> We report the observation of a very exotic decay mode at the proton drip-line, the $β$-delayed $γ$-proton decay, clearly seen in the $β$ decay of the $T_z$ = -2 nucleus $^{56}$Zn. Three $γ$-proton sequences have been observed after the $β$ decay. Here this decay mode, already observed in the $sd$-shell, is seen for the first time in the $fp$-shell. Both $γ$ and proton decays have been taken into account in the estimation of the Fermi (F) and Gamow Teller (GT) strengths. Evidence for fragmentation of the Fermi strength due to strong isospin mixing is found.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first observation of β-delayed γ-proton decay in the fp-shell, specifically in the β decay of ⁵⁶Zn. This decay mode, where a γ ray populates a proton-unbound level that subsequently emits a proton, affects the conventional determination of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength because the intensity of proton peaks must be corrected for indirect feeding from γ de-excitation, contrary to the previous assumption that GT strength near the proton drip-line can be deduced solely from proton intensities.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the LISE3 facility of GANIL using a ⁵⁸Ni primary beam at 74.5 MeV/nucleon fragmented on a natural Ni target. Fragments were implanted into a Double-Sided Silicon Strip Detector (DSSSD) surrounded by four EXOGAM Ge clover detectors for γ detection. Proton and γ decays correlated with ⁵⁶Zn implants were identified, and the β feedings to ⁵⁶Cu levels were estimated from both proton and γ intensities, accounting for indirect feeding from γ de-excitation. The results were compared with the mirror charge-exchange reaction ⁵⁶Fe(³He,t)⁵⁶Co from Fujita (2010).

## Key numbers
- Half-life of ⁵⁶Zn: 32.9(8) ms, in agreement with Dossat (2007).
- Total proton branching ratio B_p = 88.5(26)% for ⁵⁶Zn.
- γ ray observed at 1834.5(10) keV, attributed to the transition from the 3508 keV IAS to the 1691 keV state in ⁵⁶Cu.
- Fermi strength B(F) = 2.7(5) for the ⁵⁶Cu IAS at 3508(140) keV; missing Fermi strength of 1.3(5) is attributed to the fragmented IAS at 3423(140) keV.
- Isospin mixing parameters for ⁵⁶Cu: off-diagonal matrix element ⟨H_c⟩ = 40(23) keV and isospin impurity α² = 33(10)%.
- γ decays represent 56(6)% of total decays from the 3508 keV IAS and 66(22)% from the 1691 keV state.

## Context
This work extends the observation of β-delayed γ-proton decay from the sd-shell, where it was seen in ³²Ar by Bhattacharya (2008) and suggested by Wrede (2009) and Pfutzner (2012), into the fp-shell for the first time. The results are compared with the mirror charge-exchange reaction on ⁵⁶Fe by Fujita (2010) and with earlier β-decay studies of ⁵⁶Zn by Dossat (2007). The finding that γ de-excitation competes with proton emission even well above the proton separation energy implies that future studies of heavier, more exotic systems with T_z ≤ -3/2 at radioactive beam facilities must include γ detectors to correctly determine GT strengths, rather than relying solely on proton measurements.
