# Mass and width of the $d'$ resonance in nuclei

> We calculated the mass and width of the $d'$ resonance inside nuclei within a nucleon-$Δ$ model by including the self-energy of the $Δ$ in the $NΔ$ propagator. We found that in the nuclear medium the width of the $d'$ is increased by one order of magnitude while its mass changes only by a few MeV. This broadening of the width of the $d'$ resonance embedded in nuclei is consistent with the experimental observations so that the $d'$ can be understood as a $NΔ$ resonance. Thus, given the freedom between either isospin 0 or isospin 2 for the $d'$, our results give weigth to the isospin-2 assignment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed broadening of the $d'$ resonance in nuclei can be explained by modeling the $d'$ as a $N\Delta$ resonance of isospin 2, provided the self-energy of the $\Delta$ in the nuclear medium is included in the propagator. The authors hedge that the isospin-2 assignment is "consistent with everything we know" rather than proven, and that the agreement with experiment required readjusting the sigma meson mass from 234 MeV to 231.5 MeV to compensate for a 3-4 MeV mass shift from the medium.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the $N\Delta$ system using interactions from the chiral quark cluster model, including non-local terms. To study medium effects, they replaced the free $\Delta$ mass and width in the $N\Delta$ propagator with effective values incorporating the real and imaginary parts of the $\Delta$ self-energy, taken from calculations by Hjorth-Jensen, Muther, and Polls (1994) for finite nuclei and by Nieves et al. (1993) for infinite nuclear matter. The self-energy was applied at a "typical" nuclear radius of 1.5 fm, and the sigma meson mass was adjusted to reproduce the experimentally observed mass of the $d'$ in the medium.

## Key numbers

* Free $d'$ mass: 2061.1 MeV (with readjusted sigma meson mass of 231.5 MeV).
* Free $d'$ width: 0.47 MeV (with readjusted sigma meson mass), in agreement with the experimentally extracted value of 0.51 MeV from Bilger et al. (1993).
* $d'$ mass in $^{16}$O: 2064.6 MeV; in $^{40}$Ca: 2064.8 MeV; in $^{100}$Sn: 2065.2 MeV; in infinite nuclear matter: 2065.6 MeV (with readjusted sigma meson mass).
* $d'$ width in $^{16}$O: 5.68 MeV; in $^{40}$Ca: 10.30 MeV; in $^{100}$Sn: 15.83 MeV; in infinite nuclear matter: 23.06 MeV (with readjusted sigma meson mass).
* When the calculation for $^{40}$Ca was repeated with $Im \Sigma_\Delta(\omega)$ divided by two (to account for a larger typical radius of 3 fm), the width became 5.43 MeV, in good agreement with the experimental range of 3-7 MeV from Bilger et al. (1993).
* The real part of the $\Delta$ self-energy was taken as -40 MeV, based on a value of -53 MeV for infinite nuclear matter scaled to a density of $\rho/\rho_0 = 0.75$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Valcarce et al., 1995) which found the $J^P=0^-$ channel to be the most attractive in the isospin-2 sector but could not produce a resonance without adjusting the sigma meson mass. It builds on the experimental analysis of Bilger et al. (1993), who extracted the $d'$ properties from double charge exchange reactions, and on theoretical calculations of the $\Delta$ self-energy in nuclei by Brown and Weise (1975), Nieves et al. (1993), and Hjorth-Jensen, Muther, and Polls (1994). The results support the isospin-2 assignment for the $d'$ over the isospin-0 assignment, which was previously ruled out by Garcilazo and Mathelitsch (1986, 1994) because the $0^-$ channel in isospin 0 is repulsive. The paper suggests a new experimental search for the $d'$ using the reaction $\pi^- d \to \pi^+ X$ with $X=\pi^- nn$, requiring a resolution better than 0.5 MeV in the mass region between 2065 MeV and a couple of MeV below.
