# The enhancement of $v_4$ in nuclear collisions at the highest densities signals a first order phase transition

> The beam energy dependence of $v_4$ (the quadrupole moment of the transverse radial flow) is sensitive to the nuclear equation of state (EoS) in mid-central Au + Au collisions at the energy range of $3 < \sqrt{s_{NN}} < 30$ GeV, which is investigated within the hadronic transport model JAM. Different equations of state, namely, a free hadron gas, a first-order phase transition and a crossover are compared. An enhancement of $v_4$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 6$ GeV is predicted for an EoS with a first-order phase transition. This enhanced $v_4$ flow is driven by both the enhancement of $v_2$ as well as the positive contribution to $v_4$ from the squeeze-out of spectator particles which turn into participants due to the admixture of the strong collective flow in the shocked, compressed nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that an enhancement of the fourth harmonic flow coefficient \(v_4\) at a beam energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 6\) GeV in mid-central Au+Au collisions serves as a clean signal for a first-order phase transition in the nuclear equation of state, but only if such a phase transition is present; the enhancement is driven by both the increase in \(v_2\) and the positive contribution from spectator squeeze-out.

## Method

The authors used the hadronic transport model JAM with a modified scattering style method to simulate different equations of state: a free hadron gas (cascade mode), a first-order phase transition (EOS-Q with bag constant \(B^{1/4}=220\) MeV), and a crossover transition (chiral model EoS consistent with lattice QCD). They computed \(v_4\) with respect to the reaction plane for charged hadrons at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta|<1.0\)) in mid-central Au+Au collisions (impact parameter range \(4.6 \leq b \leq 9.4\) fm) over the beam energy range \(3 < \sqrt{s_{NN}} < 30\) GeV, and also performed calculations with and without spectator nucleon interactions to isolate the effects.

## Key numbers

- An enhancement of \(v_4\) by a factor of two relative to the cascade result is observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV for the first-order phase transition EoS.
- A strong bump in \(v_4\) is predicted around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 6\) GeV for the first-order phase transition EoS.
- The ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) stands close to 0.75 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV and flattens to a constant value of 0.75 at beam energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 10\) GeV, with a slight increase around 6 GeV.
- Without spectator matter interactions, the ratio \(v_4/(v_2)^2\) is smaller, around 0.5 at moderate energies, but approaches 0.75 at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 6\) GeV and above.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions of an enhanced elliptic flow \(v_2\) as a signal of a first-order phase transition (Nara 2017, Chen 2017) to the fourth harmonic \(v_4\). It contrasts with standard cascade simulations (free hadron gas) and crossover EoS, which do not produce the same enhancement. The predicted signal can be tested experimentally at future facilities such as RHIC-BESII, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI, enabling the exploration of the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density.
