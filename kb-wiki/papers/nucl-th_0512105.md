# Many-body nuclear Hamiltonian: Ab exitu approach

> Fully-microscopic No-core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations of all stable $s$ and $p$ shell nuclei are used to determine realistic $NN$ interaction JISP16 describing not only the two-nucleon data but the binding energies and spectra of nuclei with $A\leq 16$ as well. The JISP16 interaction, providing rapid convergence of the NCSM calculations, is obtained in an {\em ab exitu} approach by phase-equivalent transformations of the JISP6 $NN$ interaction.

## Key claim

The JISP16 nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, obtained through phase-equivalent transformations of the JISP6 interaction, can simultaneously describe two-nucleon scattering data and the binding energies and spectra of stable nuclei up to A=16 within the No-core Shell Model (NCSM), while providing rapid convergence and reducing the need for explicit three-nucleon forces. However, the authors note this is only an initial demonstration and that a thorough search through the space of possible phase-equivalent transformations should further improve the description.

## Method

The authors start from the realistic charge-independent NN interaction JISP6, which fits NN scattering data with chi-squared per datum of 1.03 for the 1992 np data base, and apply phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) in selected partial waves to modify the interaction. They perform NCSM calculations in small model spaces (up to 10 hbar-omega for Li-6 and up to 4 hbar-omega for O-16) to fit the excitation energies of the lowest Li-6 levels and the binding energies of Li-6 and O-16, then verify the resulting JISP16 interaction against all stable s and p shell nuclei in larger model spaces.

## Key numbers

- JISP6 NN interaction chi-squared per datum: 1.03 for the 1992 np data base (2514 data) and 1.05 for the 1999 np data base (3058 data)
- Deuteron rms radius with JISP16: r_d = 1.9643 fm; quadrupole moment Q = 0.288585 fm^2
- PET angles in coupled sd waves: theta = -11.0 degrees; in other partial waves: +5 degrees (P-3_2), -6 degrees (P-3_1), -10 degrees (P-3_0), +25 degrees (D-3_2), -12 degrees (P-1_1)
- He-4 binding energy with bare JISP16: 28.297 MeV (experimental: 28.296 MeV) in 14 hbar-omega model space at hbar-omega = 10 MeV
- O-16 ground state energy with bare JISP16: 126.2 MeV (experimental: 127.619 MeV) in 6 hbar-omega model space at hbar-omega = 15 MeV
- Li-6 binding energy extrapolation with effective interaction: 31.70(17) MeV (experimental: 31.995 MeV)
- He-6 binding energy extrapolation with effective interaction: 28.89(17) MeV (experimental: 29.269 MeV)
- Li-6 quadrupole moment with JISP16: Q = -0.0646 e.fm^2 (experimental: -0.082(2) e.fm^2)
- Li-6 point-proton rms radius with JISP16: r_p = 2.151 fm (experimental: 2.32(3) fm)

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix inverse scattering approach used to develop the JISP6 interaction (Shirokov et al., 2004) and the NCSM method (Vary et al., 1995) by introducing an "ab exitu" philosophy that directly targets nuclear properties rather than starting from meson-exchange theory. The approach contrasts with the Green's-function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method (Carlson et al., 1990s) that uses explicit three-nucleon forces like the Argonne AV18 plus Illinois interactions, and with NCSM calculations using NN+NNN forces (Navratil and Ormand, 2000; Nogga et al., 2002). The JISP16 interaction enables extending realistic microscopic calculations to heavier nuclei with improved convergence and reduced computational cost, and opens the path to studying the mechanism by which phase-equivalent transformations reduce the role of multi-nucleon interactions.
