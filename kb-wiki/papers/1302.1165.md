# Third minima in thorium and uranium isotopes in a self-consistent theory

> Background: Deep third minima have been predicted in some non-self-consistent models to impact fission pathways of thorium and uranium isotopes. These predictions have guided the interpretation of resonances seen experimentally. On the other hand, self-consistent calculations consistently predict very shallow potential-energy surfaces in the third minimum region. Purpose: We investigate the interpretation of third-minimum configurations in terms of dimolecular states. We study the isentropic potential-energy surfaces of selected even-even thorium and uranium isotopes at several excitation energies. In order to understand the driving effects behind the presence of third minima, we study the interplay between pairing and shell effects. Methods: We use the finite-temperature superfluid nuclear density functional theory. We consider a traditional functional, SkM*, and a recent functional, UNEDF1, optimized for fission studies. Results: We predict very shallow or no third minima in the potential-energy surfaces of 232Th and 232U. In Th and U isotopes with N=136 and 138, the third minima are deeper. We show that the reflection-asymmetric configurations around the third minimum can be associated with dimolecular states involving the spherical doubly magic 132Sn and a lighter deformed Zr or Mo fragment. The potential-energy surfaces for 228,232Th and 232U at several excitation energies are presented. Conclusions: We show that the neutron shell effect that governs the existence of the dimolecular states around the third minimum is consistent with the spherical-to-deformed shape transition in the Zr and Mo isotopes around N=58. We demonstrate that the thermal reduction of pairing and enhancement of shell effects at small excitation energies help to develop deeper third minima. At large excitation energies, shell effects are washed out and third minima disappear altogether.

# Third minima in thorium and uranium isotopes in a self-consistent theory

## Key claim

Self-consistent density functional theory predicts only very shallow or no third minima in the potential-energy surfaces of 232Th and 232U, contradicting earlier macroscopic-microscopic predictions of deep hyperdeformed minima; however, in lighter Th and U isotopes with neutron numbers N=136 and 138, the third minima are better developed, and at moderate excitation energies (around 21 MeV) the third minimum in 232Th actually deepens due to the interplay between thermal quenching of pairing and enhancement of shell effects.

## Method

The authors employed finite-temperature superfluid nuclear density functional theory (FT-DFT) using the symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme DFT code HFODD, solving the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in a Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis. They used two Skyrme energy density functionals: the traditional SkM* functional and the newer UNEDF1 functional optimized for fission studies, with constraints on the quadrupole moment Q20 and octupole moment Q30 to map potential-energy surfaces. Isentropic potential-energy surfaces were computed by mapping excitation energy E* to fixed temperature T via the relation E*(T) = E_gs(T) - E_gs(T=0), and shell correction energies were extracted using the Strutinsky energy theorem.

## Key numbers

- For 232Th with SkM*, a shallow third minimum appears around Q20 = 165 b; with UNEDF1, a local plateau appears at Q20 approximately 200 b but the third barrier is practically nonexistent.
- The third minima are deeper in lighter isotopes: 226,228Th and 228U (N=136, 138) show better-developed minima than 230,232Th and 232,234U (N=140, 142).
- For 232Th, increasing excitation energy from E* = 0 to E* = 48 MeV gradually reduces the second fission barrier while the third barrier changes little, deepening the third-minimum pocket at intermediate energies.
- At E* = 21 MeV for 228Th, a 1 MeV pocket remains at nearly the same collective coordinates as at zero excitation energy.
- The basis size comparison for 232Th with SkM* shows that increasing from 1140 to 1771 harmonic oscillator basis states reduces the absolute potential energy beyond the second barrier by about 0.5 MeV, but the topology of the potential-energy surface is hardly affected.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent density functional theory studies of third minima in actinides, which had consistently predicted shallower minima than earlier macroscopic-microscopic (MM) calculations by Pashkevich (1971), Moller (1972), and others that guided interpretation of experimental fission resonances. The results contradict the deep hyperdeformed minima predicted in MM approaches for 232Th and 232U, but are consistent with other self-consistent calculations using D1S (Berger, 2002), HFB-14 (Goriely, 2007), and relativistic DFT (Rutz, 1995). The study enables future work on fission dynamics using the generator coordinate method generalized to finite temperature, and motivates investigation of whether dimolecular configurations persist from the third minimum to scission, as well as extension to heavier uranium and thorium isotopes following recent experiments on 238U (Csige, 2013).
