# Existence and transitions properties of three-deuteron muonic molecule (3d 2e^- μ^-)

> We calculated the energy and the size of the three-deuteron muonic molecule (3d 2e^- μ^-) = D_3 μ. It turns out that this system possesses two equilibrium positions, one at distances typical for muonic molecules and a second one at the usual molecular size. We show, moreover, that the fusion probability of the three deuterons is considerably enhanced due to the existence of a ^6Li^* threshold resonance. Our estimates indicate that this probability is considerably higher than the decay rate of the competing Auger transition.

## Key claim

The three-deuteron muonic molecule D₃μ possesses two equilibrium positions (one at muonic-molecule distances and one at ordinary molecular size), and its three-deuteron fusion probability is considerably enhanced by the existence of a near-threshold ⁶Li* resonance, making the fusion rate higher than the competing Auger decay rate.

## Method

The authors used a Born-Oppenheimer-type ansatz for the wave function of the D₃μ molecule, with variational parameters optimized to obtain the effective potential energy surface for the three deuterons. The nuclear transition probability was calculated from the overlap integral between the molecular wave function and the asymptotic wave function of the ⁶Li*(3⁻,0) resonance, using analytic approximations for the Coulomb-distorted wave functions. The Auger transition rate was computed by integrating the squared matrix element of the Coulomb interaction over final-state momenta.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of D₃μ: ℰ₃ = −1.74 keV (in the short-range minimum)
- Size of D₃μ: R₀ = 11.0 × 10⁻¹¹ cm
- Effective charge fitting the short-range potential: Z_eff = 2.8
- Parameters of the harmonic approximation to the short-range minimum: a = 0.31, b = 0.01, R̄ = 4.45 (in units e = ℏ = m_μ = 1)
- ⁶Li* resonance energies: (J^π, T) = (4⁻,1) at E + iΓ = (25 + i4) MeV; (J^π, T) = (3⁻,0) at E = 26.6 MeV
- Three-deuteron threshold energy: E^{3d}_th = 25.32 MeV
- Nuclear transition rates w for three values of γ: 5.42 × 10¹⁹ s⁻¹ (γ = 0.857), 3.37 × 10⁴⁴ s⁻¹ (γ = 1.916), 3.03 × 10⁵⁰ s⁻¹ (γ = 2.710)
- Auger transition rate: λ ≈ 10¹⁵ s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the study of muonic molecules from two-body systems (such as ddμ and dtμ, reviewed by Breunlich (1989)) to a three-deuteron system, following earlier investigations by Belyaev et al. (1995, 1993, 1994, 1996) on threshold resonances in muonic molecules. The predicted enhancement of fusion via the ⁶Li* resonance is analogous to the four-order-of-magnitude enhancement seen in dtμ fusion via the ⁵He(3/2⁺) resonance. The results suggest that D₃μ could be produced via the known D₃⁺ ion (which has a formation rate λ ∼ 10⁹ cm⁻³ s⁻¹, as reported by Oka (1992)) followed by muon capture, and that the system could be detected either by observing Auger electrons with kinetic energy around 1 keV or by detecting the products of triple fusion into ⁶Li.
