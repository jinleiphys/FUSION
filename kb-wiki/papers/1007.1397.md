# Influence of vector interaction and Polyakov loop dynamics on inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phases

> We investigate the role of the isoscalar vector interaction and the dynamics of the Polyakov loop on inhomogeneous phases in the phase diagram of the two-flavor Nambu-Jona--Lasinio (NJL) model. Thereby we concentrate on phases with a one-dimensional modulation, explicitly domain-wall solitons and, for comparison, the chiral spiral. While the inclusion of the Polyakov loop merely leads to quantitative changes compared to the original NJL model, the presence of a repulsive vector-channel interaction has significant qualitative effects: Whereas for homogeneous phases the first-order phase transition gets weakened and eventually turns into a second-order transition or a cross-over, the domain of inhomogeneous phases is less affected. In particular the location of the Lifshitz point in terms of temperature and density is not modified. Consequently, the critical point disappears from the phase diagram and only a Lifshitz point (showing a different critical behavior) remains. As a result, susceptibilities remain finite.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a repulsive vector-channel interaction in the NJL model does not shift the Lifshitz point in temperature, only in chemical potential, so the inhomogeneous phase persists and eliminates the critical point that would otherwise appear in a homogeneous-only analysis; the critical point disappears from the phase diagram and only a Lifshitz point (showing different critical behavior) remains.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor NJL model with an isoscalar vector interaction and Polyakov loop dynamics, restricting to one-dimensional modulations of the order parameter (domain-wall soliton lattices and, for comparison, the chiral spiral). They evaluate the mean-field thermodynamic potential using a density of states derived from the 1+1-dimensional Gross-Neveu model, regularized by a Pauli-Villars scheme, and approximate the spatially varying density by its spatial average to map the problem onto the known G_V=0 case at a shifted chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* For the chiral limit, the model parameters are fixed by requiring f_pi = 88 MeV and vacuum constituent mass M_0 = 300 MeV, yielding Lambda = 757.0 MeV and G_S = 6.002 / Lambda^2.
* The vector coupling G_V is varied between zero and G_S; a Fierz transformation from a color-current interaction would give G_V/G_S = 1/2.
* At T=0 and G_V=0, density profiles are shown for mu = 307.5, 309, 325, and 345 MeV, with the density normalized to the restored-phase density n_rest = (2 N_c / (3 pi^2)) mu^3.
* The Lifshitz point temperature is unchanged by G_V, while the critical point from homogeneous-only analysis moves to lower temperatures and eventually disappears from the phase diagram.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phases in the NJL model by Nickel (2009) to include repulsive vector interactions and Polyakov loop dynamics. The vector interaction was previously shown to weaken first-order transitions in homogeneous phases (Fukushima, 2008; Buballa, 1996), but here it is found not to suppress the inhomogeneous phase. The results enable future studies of susceptibilities and fluctuation signals in heavy-ion collisions, since the critical point divergence is removed from the phase diagram.
