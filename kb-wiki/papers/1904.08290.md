# Initial conditions of bulk matter in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> Dynamical models based on relativistic fluid dynamics provide a powerful tool to extract the properties of the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced by ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. The largest source of uncertainty in these model-to-data extractions is the choice of theoretical initial conditions (ICs) used to model the distribution of energy or entropy at the hydrodynamic starting time. Descriptions of the ICs are generally improved through iterative cycles of testing and refinement. Individual models are compared to experimental data; the worst models are discarded and best models retained. Consequently, successful traits (assumptions) are passed on to subsequent generations of the theoretical landscape. This bottom-up approach correspondingly describes a form of theoretical trial and error, where each trial proposes an ab initio solution to the problem at hand. A natural complement to this strategy, is to employ a top-down or data-driven approach which is able to reverse engineer properties of the ICs from the constraints imposed by the experimental data. In this dissertation, I motivate and develop a parametric IC model based on a family of functions known as the generalized means. The ansatz closely mimics the variability of ab initio calculations and serves as a reasonable parametric form for exploring QGP energy and entropy deposition assuming imperfect knowledge of the complex physical processes which lead to its creation. With the parametric model in hand, I explore broad implications of the proposed ansatz using recently adapted Bayesian methods to simultaneously constrain properties of the ICs and QGP medium using experimental data from the Large Hadron Collider. These analyses show that the ICs are highly constrained by available measurements and provide evidence of a unified hydrodynamic description of small and large nuclear collision systems.

## Key claim

The largest source of uncertainty in extracting quark-gluon plasma properties from hydrodynamic simulations is the choice of theoretical initial conditions, and a parametric model based on generalized means can serve as a meta-model that interpolates between ab initio calculations while being constrained by experimental data through Bayesian methods.

## Method

The author develops a parametric initial condition model called "trento" based on generalized means, which parametrizes a semi-exhaustive subspace of physically reasonable initial condition models for energy and entropy deposition in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. This model is embedded in a realistic hydrodynamic simulation and constrained using Bayesian parameter estimation, with the collision dynamics factorized into three modular components: nuclear structure (sampling nucleon positions from two-parameter Fermi distributions), inelastic nuclear cross sections, and local energy/entropy deposition.

## Key numbers

- The QGP specific shear viscosity obtains a minimum value of 0.085 with uncertainties +0.026 and -0.025 at T_c = 154 MeV
- Nuclear overlap times for Pb-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies (200, 2760, and 5020 GeV) are less than or equal to 0.1 fm/c
- The Lorentz contraction factor gamma is greater than or equal to 100 for these beam energies
- For lead-208 nuclei, the neutron skin thickness Delta r_np = 0.15 +/- 0.03 (stat.) +0.01/-0.03 (sys.) fm
- The minimum distance constraint algorithm works up to d_min = 1.7 fm for spherically symmetric nuclei and d_min = 1.5 fm for deformed nuclei
- The Hulthén wave function parameters for the deuteron are alpha = 0.228 fm^-1 and beta = 1.18 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model by Schenke (2012) and EKRT model by Eskola (1999) by providing a flexible parametric framework that can interpolate between different ab initio calculations rather than being rigidly fixed by theoretical assumptions. The Bayesian analysis builds on the model-to-data comparison framework developed by Bernhard (2018) and enables simultaneous constraints on initial conditions and QGP transport coefficients, providing evidence for a unified hydrodynamic description of both small (p-Pb) and large (Pb-Pb) collision systems.
