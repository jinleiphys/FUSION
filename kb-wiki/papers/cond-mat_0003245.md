# Collective and independent-particle motion in two-electron artificial atoms

> Investigations of the exactly solvable excitation spectra of two-electron quantum dots with a parabolic confinement, for different values of the parameter R_W expressing the relative magnitudes of the interelectron repulsion and the zero-point kinetic energy of the confined electrons, reveal for large R_W a remarkably well-developed ro-vibrational spectrum associated with formation of a linear trimeric rigid molecule composed of the two electrons and the infinitely heavy confining dot. This spectrum transforms to one characteristic of a "floppy" molecule for smaller values of R_W. The conditional probability distribution calculated for the exact two-electron wave functions allows for the identification of the ro-vibrational excitations as rotations and stretching/bending vibrations, and provides direct evidence pertaining to the formation of such molecules.

## Key claim

For two-electron quantum dots with parabolic confinement, the excitation spectrum transitions from a well-developed ro-vibrational pattern characteristic of a rigid linear triatomic molecule (for large Wigner parameter R_W) to a "floppy" molecular spectrum and eventually to independent-particle behavior (for small R_W), with the conditional probability distribution providing direct evidence for electron molecule formation.

## Method

The authors solved the exactly solvable Schrodinger equation for two electrons in a two-dimensional parabolic quantum dot, which separates into center-of-mass and relative-motion coordinates. They computed energy eigenvalues and wave functions for three values of the Wigner parameter R_W (200, 20, and 3), which quantifies the ratio of Coulomb repulsion to zero-point kinetic energy. To probe the molecular structure, they calculated conditional probability distributions (CPD) and pair-correlation functions (PCF) from the exact two-electron wave functions, allowing identification of rotational, stretching, and bending vibrational modes.

## Key numbers

- For R_W = 200, the rotational constant C is approximately 0.037 in units of hbar*omega_0/2
- For R_W = 200, the stretching vibration phonon energy hbar*omega_s is approximately 3.50 in units of hbar*omega_0/2
- For R_W = 200, the bending vibration phonon energy hbar*omega_b equals hbar*omega_0 = 2 in units of hbar*omega_0/2
- For R_W = 200, the ground-state electron separation 2*d_0 is 5.2 in units of l_0*sqrt(2)
- For GaAs material parameters (m* = 0.067 m_e, kappa = 12.9) and R_W = 3, hbar*omega_0 = 1.2 meV and l_0*sqrt(2) = 43.54 nm

## Context

This work extends the study of three-body systems from natural helium-like atoms (Kellman and Herrick 1978, Berry 1981) to artificial atoms (quantum dots), showing that electron molecules and collective ro-vibrational spectra are general properties of quantum dots. The results build on earlier symmetry-breaking Hartree-Fock calculations by Yannouleas and Landman (1999) and provide a foundation for experimental observation of electron molecules in quantum dots through controlled pinning of collective rotations, as suggested by Tarucha (1996) and Ashoori (1996).
