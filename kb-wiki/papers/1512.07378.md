# Modification of generalized vector form factors and transverse charge densities of the nucleon in nuclear matter

> We investigate the medium modification of the generalized vector form factors of the nucleon, which include the electromagnetic and energy-momentum tensor form factors, based on an in-medium modified $ŽĆ$-$Žü$-$Žē$ soliton model. We find that the vector form factors of the nucleon in nuclear matter fall off faster than those in free space, which implies that the charge radii of the nucleon become larger in nuclear medium than in free space. We also compute the corresponding transverse charge densities of the nucleon in nuclear matter, which clearly reveal the increasing of the nucleon size in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The generalized vector form factors (electromagnetic and energy-momentum tensor form factors) of the nucleon in nuclear matter fall off faster with momentum transfer than in free space, implying that the nucleon swells in medium; however, the magnitude of the effect depends on whether the \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons are modified equally (Model I) or the \(\omega\) meson is kept unchanged (Model II), with the neutron electric form factor showing particularly strong model dependence.

## Method

The authors employ an in-medium modified chiral soliton model with explicit \(\pi\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) meson degrees of freedom, where the nucleon emerges as a topological soliton. Medium effects are introduced via density-dependent functionals \(\alpha_p\), \(\alpha_s\), and \(\zeta_V\) that modify the pion kinetic term, the pion mass term, and the vector meson kinetic terms, respectively, with parameters fitted to pionic atom data and the nuclear matter saturation point. Two variants are considered: Model I treats \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) mesons symmetrically in medium, while Model II modifies only the \(\rho\) meson, keeping the \(\omega\) meson as in free space. The generalized vector form factors are computed from the soliton profile functions, and transverse charge densities are obtained via two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the Dirac and Pauli form factors.

## Key numbers

* Proton charge radius in free space: \(\langle r_E^2\rangle_p^{1/2} = 0.93\) fm (Model I and II); in medium at normal density \(\rho_0\): 1.17 fm (Model I) and 1.08 fm (Model II).
* Proton magnetic moment in free space: \(\mu_p = 3.37\,\mu_N\) (Model I) and \(3.39\,\mu_N\) (Model II); in medium: \(5.23\,\mu_N\) (Model I) and \(5.41\,\mu_N\) (Model II), an increase of approximately 40%.
* Neutron magnetic moment in free space: \(\mu_n = -2.58\,\mu_N\) (Model I) and \(-2.61\,\mu_N\) (Model II); in medium: \(-4.56\,\mu_N\) (Model I) and \(-4.73\,\mu_N\) (Model II).
* Transverse mean square charge radius of the proton in free space: \(\langle b_{\mathrm{ch}}^2\rangle_p^{1/2} = 0.70\) fm; in medium: 0.90 fm (Model I) and 0.81 fm (Model II), an increase of approximately 20%.
* Transverse mean square magnetization radius of the proton in free space: \(\langle b_{\mathrm{m}}^2\rangle_p^{1/2} = 0.89\) fm; in medium: 1.65 fm (Model I) and 1.67 fm (Model II), nearly doubling.
* Neutron charge radius in free space: \(\langle r_E^2\rangle_n = -0.23\) fm\(^2\); in medium: \(-0.22\) fm\(^2\) (Model I) and \(-0.40\) fm\(^2\) (Model II).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in nuclear matter using chiral soliton models, such as Yakhshiev (2012), and earlier investigations of energy-momentum tensor form factors in medium by Kim (2012) and Jung (2014). The results are relevant for interpreting future deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments on nuclear targets, following the first such measurement by Airapetian (2009). The transverse charge densities provide a clearer picture of nucleon swelling in medium than traditional three-dimensional charge densities, and the two models bracket the uncertainty in how vector meson modifications affect the neutron electric form factor.
