# Baryon Junction Loops in HIJING/BBv2.0 and the Baryon/Meson Anomaly at RHIC

> A new version, v2.0, of the HIJING/BB Monte Carlo nuclear collision event generator is introduced in order to explore further the possible role of baryon junctions loops in the baryon/meson anomaly (2 $< p_{T} <$ 5 GeV/c) observed in 200A GeV Au+Au reactions at RHIC. We show that junction loops with an enhanced intrinsic $k_T\approx 1$ GeV/c transverse momentum kick may provide a partial explanation of the anomaly as well as other important baryon stopping observables.

# Baryon Junction Loops in HIJING/BBv2.0 and the Baryon/Meson Anomaly at RHIC

## Key claim

Junction anti-junction loops with an enhanced intrinsic transverse momentum kick of approximately 1 GeV/c may provide a *partial* explanation of the baryon/meson anomaly at moderate transverse momentum (2 < pT < 5 GeV/c) observed in 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, as well as other baryon stopping observables. The authors explicitly state this is a "partial explanation," acknowledging that other effects such as jet quenching and radial flow also contribute.

## Method

The authors introduce HIJING/BB v2.0, a Monte Carlo event generator that extends HIJING v1.37 by replacing the earlier junction loop algorithm of HIJING/BB v1.10 with a simplified enhancement of the intrinsic diquark transverse momentum kick parameter (PARJ(21) in JETSET7.3) by a factor FpT = 3, from the default 0.36 GeV/c to approximately 1.08 GeV/c, in strings selected to contain junction loops. The probability of a junction loop occurring in a given string is computed from the number of binary collisions experienced by the incident baryon, using a junction loop cross section sigma_JJbar = 17 mb, a single diffractive cross section of approximately 10 mb, and a total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section of approximately 42 mb at RHIC energies. The diquark/quark suppression factor PARJ(1) is reduced from the JETSET default of 0.1 to 0.07 in junction loop events to match observed proton and antiproton yields.

## Key numbers

- The junction loop cross section is taken as sigma_JJbar = 17 mb, with total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_in approximately 42 mb at RHIC energies, giving a junction loop probability in pp collisions of 17/32 approximately 0.5
- The default diquark transverse momentum kick parameter PARJ(21) = 0.36 GeV/c is enhanced by a factor FpT = 3 to approximately 1.08 GeV/c in junction loop events
- The diquark/quark suppression factor PARJ(1) is reduced from 0.1 to 0.07 in junction loop events
- The average rapidity loss for net-baryons predicted by HIJING/BB v2.0 is compared to the BRAHMS experimental value of <delta y> = 2.0 +/- 0.2
- The total energy per net-baryon after collisions predicted by HIJING models is approximately 40 GeV, compared to the experimental value E_tot_exp = 28 +/- 6 GeV from BRAHMS
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons shows suppression by a factor of approximately 5 at the highest pT bins, with a maximum at pT approximately 2 GeV/c
- The energy loss parameter for quark jets is set to dE/dx = 1 GeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the baryon junction loop mechanism originally proposed by Rossi and Veneziano (1977) based on dual regge theory, later applied to nuclear collisions by Kharzeev (1996), and first implemented in a Monte Carlo event generator as HIJING/B by Vance et al. (1998) and HIJING/BB v1.10 by Vance et al. (1999). The previous version HIJING/BB v1.10 failed to reproduce the observed transverse momentum slopes of antibaryons at moderate pT, as shown by Topor Pop et al. (2003). The new version v2.0 addresses this failure by replacing the earlier junction loop algorithm with a simpler enhancement of diquark transverse momentum kicks. The results are compared with RQMD v2.4 by Sorge et al., which includes hadronic rescattering and strong color field effects but fails to reproduce the antiproton yield and rapidity distributions. The work enables further tests of baryon junction dynamics through rapidity correlation measurements in p+p and p(d)+Au collisions at RHIC, and suggests that junction mechanisms may lead to substantial baryon stopping even at LHC energies.
