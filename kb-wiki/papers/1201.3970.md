# Activities of γ-ray emitting isotopes in rainwater from Greater Sudbury, Canada following the Fukushima incident

> We report the activity measured in rainwater samples collected in the Greater Sudbury area of eastern Canada on 3, 16, 20, and 26 April 2011. The samples were gamma-ray counted in a germanium detector and the isotopes 131I and 137Cs, produced by the fission of 235U, and 134Cs, produced by neutron capture on 133Cs, were observed at elevated levels compared to a reference sample of ice-water. These elevated activities are ascribed to the accident at the Fukushima Dai-ichi nuclear reactor complex in Japan that followed the 11 March earthquake and tsunami. The activity levels observed at no time presented health concerns.

## Key claim

Elevated activities of fission products 131I, 134Cs, and 137Cs were detected in rainwater samples from Greater Sudbury, Canada in April 2011, attributed to the Fukushima Dai-ichi accident; however, the activity levels at no time presented health concerns and were only observable due to the extremely sensitive low-background counting apparatus.

## Method

Rainwater samples (approximately 1 L each) were collected on 3, 16, 20, and 26 April 2011 in aluminum trays, filtered through Whatman Grade 1 paper (>11 μm), sealed in Marinelli beakers, and transported to the SNOLAB underground laboratory (2092 m depth). The samples were gamma-ray counted for approximately 1 day each (2 days for the ice reference sample) using a high-purity germanium detector (63 mm length, 67 mm diameter, 47% relative efficiency at 1333 keV, 1.9 keV FWHM resolution) shielded by 2 inches of copper and 8 inches of lead, with nitrogen purge to reduce radon background.

## Key numbers

- 131I specific activity in rain on 3 April: 668 ± 44 mBq/kg (corrected to collection time)
- 131I specific activity in rain on 16 April: 64.0 ± 8.9 mBq/kg
- 131I specific activity in rain on 20 April: 31 ± 11 mBq/kg
- 131I specific activity in rain on 26 April: 2.4 (+4.6, -2.4) mBq/kg
- 137Cs specific activity in rain on 3 April: 11.0 ± 4.1 mBq/kg
- 137Cs specific activity in rain on 16 April: 22.7 ± 4.9 mBq/kg
- 137Cs specific activity in rain on 20 April: 19.1 ± 4.7 mBq/kg
- 134Cs specific activity in rain on 3 April: 8.3 ± 2.4 mBq/kg
- 134Cs specific activity in rain on 16 April: 16.8 ± 2.9 mBq/kg
- 134Cs specific activity in rain on 20 April: 13.1 ± 2.5 mBq/kg
- Weighted average ratio of 134Cs to 137Cs activity: 0.72 ± 0.13
- Best-fit decay constant for 131I activity over time: t₁ = 5.33 ± 0.30 days (observed half-life 3.7 days, compared to 8.0-day nuclear half-life)
- Ice reference sample: 131I at 22.8 ± 3.7 mBq/kg; 137Cs < 0.4 mBq/kg; 134Cs at 0.4 (+0.8, -0.4) mBq/kg

## Context

This work extends measurements of Fukushima-derived radioactivity across North America, consistent with reports from the western United States by Leon (2011), Norman (2011), and MacMullin (2011), and from Asia by Momoshima (2011). The observed 134Cs/137Cs ratio of 0.72 ± 0.13 agrees with the value of approximately 0.7 reported by Leon (2011) and Momoshima (2011). Unlike some other laboratories, the authors did not detect 132Te, attributing this to its 3.2-day half-life, the delay between release and first measurements, and low tellurium volatility. These data may aid understanding of atmospheric transport of fission products from the Fukushima release.
