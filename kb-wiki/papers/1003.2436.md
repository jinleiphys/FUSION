# Electric Charge Separation in Strong Transient Magnetic Fields

> We discuss various mechanisms for the creation of an asymmetric charge fluctuation with respect to the reaction plane among hadrons emitted in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that such mechanisms exist in both, the hadronic gas and the partonic phases of QCD. The mechanisms considered here all require the presence of a strong magnetic field (the ``chiral magnetic effect''), but they do not involve parity or charge-parity violations. We analyze how a transient local electric current fluctuation generated by the chiral magnetic effect can dynamically evolve into an asymmetric charge distribution among the final-state hadrons in momentum space. We estimate the magnitude of the event-by-event fluctuations of the final-state charge asymmetry due to the partonic and hadronic mechanisms.

# Electric Charge Separation in Strong Transient Magnetic Fields

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral magnetic effect, which produces asymmetric charge fluctuations with respect to the reaction plane in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, does not require deconfinement or parity/charge-parity violation; analogous mechanisms exist in both the hadronic gas and partonic phases of QCD, provided a strong magnetic field is present. The authors emphasize that transverse collective flow and hadronic freeze-out properties are essential to convert a local charge asymmetry in position space into the observed momentum-space asymmetry.

## Method

The authors derive the anomalous electromagnetic current from effective Lagrangians containing pseudoscalar operators, using the general form j^μ_an = -(∂_ν P) \tilde{F}^{μν} from D'Hoker and Goldstone (1985). They compute the pseudoscalar coupling coefficient κ(T) for the mixed QED-QCD interaction via quark box diagrams in both the vacuum (η/η' meson dominance) and high-temperature limits, and construct an interpolating form. For the hadronic phase, they evaluate the magnetic π-ρ conversion mechanism using vector meson dominance and the in-medium ρ-meson propagator from Gale and Kapusta (1990). They then analyze five scenarios (Color Glass Condensate, Quark-Gluon Plasma, Glasma, Corona, Hadron Gas) and develop a kinematic framework relating position-space charge separation on the freeze-out surface to the measured momentum-space asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The experimental limit on the QCD θ parameter: θ < 0.7 × 10^{-11} (from neutron electric dipole moment measurements, Baker 2006)
- The η-η' mixing angle: θ ≈ -20°, with f_η ≈ 1.2 f_π ≈ 157 MeV (Feldmann 1999)
- Vacuum pseudoscalar coupling coefficient: κ_0 ≈ 1.46/(π² f_η² m_η'²)
- High-temperature pseudoscalar coupling coefficient (T ≫ m): κ(T) ≈ 217 ζ(5) / [360 (πT)⁴], including three light quark flavors
- Decay widths for vector meson radiative decays: Γ_{ω→π⁰γ} = 0.79 MeV, Γ_{ρ⁰→π⁰γ} = 0.077 MeV (Achasov 2003)
- Effective coupling constant g_{ρπγ} = 0.58 (Zhu 1998)
- Effective coupling constant g_{ρη'γ} ≈ 1.31, from Γ_{η'→ργ} ≈ 60 ± 5 keV (Amsler 2008)
- The magnetic field strength eB can be of order m_π² (Kharzeev 2007)
- The magnetic field falls off approximately as |B(τ)| ∝ τ^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the chiral magnetic effect originally proposed by Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa (2007) and further analyzed by Fukushima, Kharzeev, and Warringa (2008-2009), who argued the effect was specific to the deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase. The authors introduce a hadronic analogue (magnetic π-ρ conversion) based on parton-hadron duality (Dokshitzer 1991) and emphasize that collective flow is necessary to convert position-space charge separation into the momentum-space asymmetry measured by the STAR collaboration (Abelev 2009). The paper enables quantitative estimates of charge asymmetry fluctuations from both partonic and hadronic mechanisms, and identifies five distinct scenarios (CGC, QGP, Glasma, Corona, Hadron Gas) that could contribute to the observed signal.
