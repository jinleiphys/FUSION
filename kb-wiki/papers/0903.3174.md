# Probing two-photon decay widths of mesons at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

> Meson production cross sections in ultra-peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC are revisited. The relevance of meson models and of exotic QCD states is discussed. This study includes states that have not been considered before in the literature.

# Probing two-photon decay widths of mesons at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

## Key claim
Ultra-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC can produce mesons via two-photon fusion with cross sections large enough to yield millions of events per second for the largest cross sections, enabling the study of both ordinary $q\overline{q}$ mesons and exotic QCD states such as glueballs, provided that inelastic nuclear scattering and central collisions are excluded and uncertainties from the hadronic structure of ions are controlled.

## Method
The author uses the equivalent photon approximation to compute meson production cross sections in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions, where the photon flux is derived from the nuclear charge and form factor with a Gaussian approximation, and the impact parameter is constrained to be greater than twice the nuclear radius to avoid hadronic overlap. The two-photon cross section for a given meson is expressed in terms of its spin, mass, and two-photon partial decay width, with the delta function enforcing energy conservation. Tables of predicted cross sections are generated for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC using experimental or theoretical $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}$ widths for both previously studied and new meson states.

## Key numbers
- Lorentz gamma factor $\gamma = 3000$ for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Nuclear radius taken as $R = 1.2 A^{1/3}$ fm, where $A$ is the atomic number.
- Ion luminosities of $10 \times 26$ cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$ for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC lead up to millions of events per second for the largest cross sections (e.g., charmonium states).
- The Schwinger critical field is $E_{Sch} = 1.3 \times 10^{16}$ V/cm, which is exceeded by the electromagnetic fields in these collisions due to the $Z^2$ enhancement.
- The relative two-photon decay widths within a flavor-SU(3) multiplet of $q\overline{q}$ states are given as $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(f) : \Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(a) : \Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(f^{\prime}) = 25 : 9 : 2$.
- Invariant masses up to 10 GeV can be produced via photon-photon fusion at the LHC.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions (e.g., Bertulani and collaborators in the 2000s) by including states not previously considered in the literature, and revisits the formalism originally developed for $e^{+}e^{-}$ colliders (Budnev 1974) and adapted to heavy ions (Baur, Bertulani, and collaborators). The results enable experimental searches at the LHC for exotic QCD states such as glueballs and hybrid mesons, where the absence of two-photon production would signal a glueball candidate, complementing studies from single timelike photons in $e^{+}e^{-}$ colliders and hadronic collisions.
