# Electromagnetic form factors of light vector mesons

> The electromagnetic form factors G_E(q^2), G_M(q^2), and G_Q(q^2), charge radii, magnetic and quadrupole moments, and decay widths of the light vector mesons rho^+, K^{*+} and K^{*0} are calculated in a Lorentz-covariant, Dyson-Schwinger equation based model using algebraic quark propagators that incorporate confinement, asymptotic freedom, and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, and vector meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes closely related to the pseudoscalar amplitudes obtained from phenomenological studies of pi and K mesons. Calculated static properties of vector mesons include the charge radii and magnetic moments: r_{rho+} = 0.61 fm, r_{K*+} = 0.54 fm, and r^2_{K*0} = -0.048 fm^2; mu_{rho+} = 2.69, mu_{K*+} = 2.37, and mu_{K*0} = -0.40. The calculated static limits of the rho-meson form factors are similar to those obtained from light-front quantum mechanical calculations, but begin to differ above q^2 = 1 GeV^2 due to the dynamical evolution of the quark propagators in our approach.

## Key claim

The authors calculate electromagnetic form factors of light vector mesons using a Dyson-Schwinger equation based model with algebraic quark propagators, but the vector-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are a simple extension of pseudoscalar amplitudes and cannot simultaneously reproduce all experimental decay widths (specifically, the model fails to fit the rho to pi pi decay width while also fitting rho to e+ e- and rho to gamma pi decays).

## Method

The authors use model confined-quark propagators parametrized from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies, with parameters for u, d, and s quarks fitted to pion and kaon observables. Vector-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are constructed as a simple generalization of the pseudoscalar amplitudes, with parameters determined by fitting to the decay widths for rho+ to gamma pi+, rho to e+ e-, and K*+ to gamma K+. The electromagnetic form factors are then computed in impulse approximation using the Ball-Chiu vertex that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity, with transverse contributions neglected.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii: r_rho+ = 0.61 fm, r_K*+ = 0.54 fm, and r^2_K*0 = -0.048 fm^2
- Magnetic moments: mu_rho+ = 2.69, mu_K*+ = 2.37, and mu_K*0 = -0.40 (dimensionless values G_M(0) where mu = G_M(0) e_0/(2M))
- Quadrupole moments: Q_rho+ = 0.055 fm^2, Q_K*+ = 0.029 fm^2, Q_K*0 = -0.0049 fm^2
- Calculated decay constants: f_rho = 4.37 (experiment: 5.05 +/- 0.12), g_gamma_rho_pi = 0.62 (experiment: 0.57 +/- 0.03), g_gamma_K*_K = 0.98 (experiment: 0.75 +/- 0.03)
- The calculated static limits of the rho-meson form factors begin to differ from light-front quantum mechanical calculations above q^2 = 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation based modeling from pseudoscalar mesons (pion and kaon) to vector mesons (rho+, K*+, K*0), using model Schwinger functions previously developed by Burden (1997) for studies of pi and K meson decays and form factors. The calculated static properties of the rho meson are similar to those obtained from light-front quantum mechanical calculations by Keister (1993) and de Melo (1997), but the approach provides dynamical evolution of quark propagators that differs at higher momentum transfers. The results enable future studies of diffractive vector-meson electroproduction, rho-omega mixing, and two- and three-body bound states, though the authors note that meson loop contributions (particularly from two-pion decays) have been neglected and require further investigation.
