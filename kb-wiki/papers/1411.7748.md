# Advances in Basis Light-front Quantization

> Basis Light-front Quantization has been developed as a first-principles nonperturbative approach to quantum field theory. In this article we report our recent progress on the applications to the single electron and the positronium system in QED. We focus on the renormalization procedure in this method.

## Key claim

Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) can be applied to QED systems including the single electron and positronium, but the renormalization procedure requires a basis-state dependent approach with an ad-hoc rescaling of amplitudes to compensate for Ward-identity violations caused by Fock-sector truncation; the positronium binding energy results converge only after introducing an explicit longitudinal momentum regulator with a free parameter.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem in a truncated basis using 2D harmonic oscillator states for transverse degrees of freedom and plane waves with periodic boundary conditions for longitudinal momentum. For the single electron, they use a two-Fock-sector basis (|e⟩ and |eγ⟩) and apply sector-dependent mass renormalization plus a rescaling procedure that divides out external leg corrections to restore the Z₁=Z₂ condition. For positronium, they construct "embedded single electron" systems for each basis state in the |eē⟩ sector to extract basis-state-dependent mass counterterms and wavefunction renormalization factors, then apply these to the full positronium system with an averaged rescaling factor for the |eēγ⟩ sector.

## Key numbers

- Physical electron mass set to M_e = 0.511 MeV as the target for iterative mass renormalization
- Positronium calculations performed at coupling α = 1/π and 2D-HO scale parameter b = αM_e = 0.163 MeV
- Transverse basis truncation in the |eē⟩ sector at N_max1 = 2 (both electron and positron transversely unexcited with n=0, m=0)
- Positronium ground state binding energy converges to approximately 0.016 MeV with longitudinal cutoff parameter x_c = 0.052, compared to the nonrelativistic quantum mechanics value of α²m_e/4 ≈ 0.013 MeV
- Longitudinal regulator applied as f_v(x) = 1 - exp(-x²/x_c²) for vertex interactions and f_i(x) = [f_v(x)]² for instantaneous interactions

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach developed by Vary (2009) and Honkanen (2010) from formal development to concrete QED applications, specifically building on the sector-dependent renormalization procedure of Karmanov (2008, 2012). The single electron results for anomalous magnetic moment and generalized parton distributions agree with perturbation theory, while the positronium calculation represents a first attempt at a two-body bound state with explicit photon degrees of freedom. The next step is to compute the full positronium spectrum and observables, with the ultimate goal of applying the method to QCD for mesons and baryons.
