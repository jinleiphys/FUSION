# Chirally motivated $πΣ$-$\bar{K}N$ model in a finite volume

> We generalize the chirally motivated $πΣ- \bar{K}N$ coupled channels model to the cubic finite volume and use it to calculate the stationary energy spectrum that appears in a nice agreement with the spectrum obtained in the lattice QCD simulations by the BaSc collaboration. Several other comparisons with the BaSc results are made, in particular relating their pole positions of the meson-baryon scattering matrix to the two-pole picture of $Λ(1405)$ generated by our chiral model in the infinite volume.

## Key claim

The chirally motivated Prague model, when generalized to a cubic finite volume, produces stationary energy spectra that agree remarkably well with lattice QCD results from the BaSc collaboration, and the two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance in the infinite volume transforms into a virtual state near the piSigma threshold when using the unphysical pion mass employed in the lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors generalized the s-wave meson-baryon loop function of the Prague coupled-channel piSigma - anti-K N model from infinite volume to a cubic finite volume with periodic boundary conditions, replacing the momentum integral with a discrete sum over lattice momenta p_n = 2pi n/L. They used the finite-volume loop function to compute stationary energy spectra and pole positions, comparing results for two parameter sets (P0 and P2) from Bruns (2021) against the BaSc collaboration lattice data for the G_{1u}(0) irreducible representation. The pole trajectories were studied by linearly scaling hadron masses and decay constants from the BaSc values to physical values.

## Key numbers

* The P0 model stationary energies for L=20 GeV^{-1} (approximately 4.051 fm) are 1375 +/- 2 MeV, 1432 +/- 1 MeV, 1494 +/- 6 MeV, and 1556 +/- 12 MeV; the BaSc G_{1u}(0) irrep energies are 1374 +/- 8 MeV, 1447 +/- 9 MeV, 1495 +/- 13 MeV, and 1566 +/- 12 MeV.
* The infinite-volume pole positions from the P0 model are z1 = (1352.7, -42.8) MeV and z2 = (1428.5, -23.7) MeV.
* The BaSc kaon mass is approximately 486 MeV and pion mass approximately 204 MeV; the BaSc hadron masses correspond to an SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking parameter x_{SU3} approximately 0.86.
* The lower pole converges to its infinite-volume position within 0.1 MeV precision for L approximately 90 GeV^{-1}, while the higher pole requires L approximately 190 GeV^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the Prague chirally motivated coupled-channel model of Bruns (2021) to finite volume, enabling direct comparison with lattice QCD results from the BaSc collaboration (2023). The approach follows the same finite-volume methodology as earlier works by Doring (2011), Martinez Torres (2012), Molina (2015), and Severt (2020). The results confirm the two-pole picture of Lambda(1405) and show that the virtual state found by BaSc near the piSigma threshold is an artifact of the unphysical pion mass, consistent with the analysis of Xie (2023). The finite-volume model enables future comparisons with additional lattice irreducible representations and can be used to test other chirally motivated approaches.
