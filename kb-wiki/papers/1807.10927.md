# Generalized linear sigma model with two glueballs

> We present a generalized linear sigma model that includes both scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs in addition to a quark-antiquark as well as a four-quark chiral nonet. Utilizing the axial and trace anomalies of QCD (at the effective mesonic level), we aim to develope the most general structure of the Lagrangian which can be used to study the interaction of quarkonia with glueballs. We then study the effect of scalar glueball on the vacuum of the model by considering a decoupling limit in which the glueball fields are decoupled from quarkonia. This determines the properties of the pure scalar glueball and builds a practical foundation for determining the model parameters when the interactions are turned on.

## Key claim

The paper develops a generalized linear sigma model that includes both scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs alongside quark-antiquark and four-quark chiral nonets, but the actual analysis is limited to a decoupling limit where glueball fields do not interact with quarkonia, serving only as a foundation for future work when interactions are turned on.

## Method

The authors extend their previously developed generalized linear sigma model with two chiral nonets (a quark-antiquark nonet M and a four-quark nonet M') by adding scalar and pseudoscalar glueball fields H and G (with mass dimension 4), which are redefined as h and g with mass dimension 1 via H=h^4 and G=h^3 g. They construct the most general Lagrangian that satisfies chiral symmetry, U(1)_A axial anomaly, and trace anomaly of QCD, then consider a minimal extension where glueballs are present but decoupled from quarkonia, allowing determination of pure scalar glueball properties from vacuum stability conditions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD predictions for the lowest scalar glueball mass: M_{0+}=1.550 GeV (Bali), M_{0+}=1.730 GeV (Morningstar), M_{0+}=1.709 GeV (Chen)
- QCD sum rules predictions for scalar glueballs: M_{0^+}=0.9-1.1 GeV and M'_{0^+}=1.5-1.6 GeV, with possibility of a broad lower state M_{0+}=0.7 GeV (Narison)
- Lattice QCD predictions for the lowest pseudoscalar glueball mass: M_{0^-}=2.330 GeV (Bali), M_{0^-}=2.590 GeV (Morningstar), M_{0^-}=2.557 GeV (Chen)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generalized linear sigma model (Jora et al., 2015-2017) that described 36 low-lying scalar and pseudoscalar states with two- and four-quark components, by now incorporating glueball degrees of freedom. The approach follows the effective Lagrangian framework of Schechter (1980) for incorporating axial and trace anomalies, and aims to eventually describe the mixing of glueballs with quarkonia in states such as f_0(1370), f_0(1500), and f_0(1710), which are considered to have noticeable glue admixtures. The decoupling limit studied here provides the foundation for determining model parameters when glueball-quarkonia interactions are turned on in future work.
