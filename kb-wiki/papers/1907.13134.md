# Coupling Constant Corrections in a Holographic Model of Heavy Ion Collisions with Nonzero Baryon Number Density

> Sufficiently energetic collisions of heavy ions result in the formation of a droplet of a strongly coupled liquid state of QCD matter known as quark-gluon plasma. By using gauge-gravity duality (holography), a model of a rapidly hydrodynamizing and thermalizing process like this can be constructed by colliding sheets of energy density moving at the speed of light and tracking the subsequent evolution. In this work, we consider the dual gravitational description of such collisions in the most general bulk theory with a four-derivative gravitational action containing a dynamical metric and a gauge field in five dimensions. Introducing the bulk gauge field enables the analysis of collisions of sheets which carry nonzero "baryon" number density in addition to energy density. Introducing the four-derivative terms enables consideration of such collisions in a gauge theory with finite gauge coupling, working perturbatively in the inverse coupling. While the dynamics of energy and momentum in the presence of perturbative inverse-coupling corrections has been analyzed previously, here we are able to determine the effect of such finite coupling corrections on the dynamics of the density of a conserved global charge, which we take as a model for the dynamics of nonzero baryon number density. In accordance with expectations, as the coupling is reduced we observe that after the collisions less baryon density ends up stopped at mid-rapidity and more of it ends up moving near the lightcone.

## Key claim

Decreasing the gauge coupling (i.e., increasing the inverse coupling) in a holographic model of heavy ion collisions with nonzero baryon number density reduces the amount of baryon density that is stopped at mid-rapidity after the collision, with more baryon number ending up moving near the lightcone; this effect is demonstrated perturbatively to leading order in the inverse coupling, supporting the hypothesis that the excessive baryon stopping seen in earlier holographic calculations at infinite coupling is an artifact of the coupling being infinitely strong at the earliest moments of the collision.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional gravitational dual action containing a dynamical metric and a gauge field, with the most general four-derivative terms (Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet terms for the metric and a new coupling \(\beta\) between the metric and gauge field), working perturbatively to first order in the inverse-coupling parameters \(\lambda_{\rm GB}\) and \(\beta\). They collide sheets of energy density and baryon number density moving at the speed of light, assuming the baryon density is small (\(\mu^2/T^2 \sim \epsilon^2\) scales with \(\lambda_{\rm GB}\)), and evolve the system numerically to extract the energy-momentum tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) and conserved current \(J^\mu\) to leading order in the combined expansion in small \(\lambda_{\rm GB}\), \(\beta\), and \(\epsilon^2\).

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(\eta/s = (1/(4\pi))(1 - 4\lambda_{\rm GB})\) to leading order in \(\lambda_{\rm GB}\); the authors choose \(\lambda_{\rm GB} = -0.2\) corresponding to an 80% increase in \(\eta/s\) relative to the infinite-coupling value.
* The baryon number density \(\rho\) is computed to order \(\epsilon \lambda_{\rm GB} \sim \epsilon^3\), where \(\epsilon\) is the small parameter controlling the initial baryon density (\(\rho \propto \epsilon\)).
* The beam rapidity at LHC is \(y = 8.5\); the hot baryon-rich matter in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is found at rapidities \(y \sim 6.5\) and \(y \sim -6.5\), corresponding to a loss of about two units of rapidity per participating nucleon.
* The maximum mid-rapidity net baryon density in heavy ion collisions occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7\) GeV; at top RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV) and LHC energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV) the mid-rapidity baryon density is essentially zero.

## Context

This work extends the holographic collision calculations of Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2016), who studied collisions with nonzero baryon number density at infinite 't Hooft coupling and found that baryon density piles up sharply at mid-rapidity, in stark contrast to ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions where baryon number ends up near the lightcone. It also extends the finite-coupling study of Grozdanov et al. (2016), who analyzed coupling-constant corrections in holographic collisions with zero baryon number density, finding that less energy density is stopped at mid-rapidity when the coupling is reduced. The present work combines both directions, showing that finite-coupling corrections reduce baryon stopping and can drive the mid-rapidity baryon density toward zero, thereby enabling a more realistic holographic model of heavy ion collisions with nonzero baryon number.
