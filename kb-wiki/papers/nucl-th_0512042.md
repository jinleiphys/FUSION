# Suppression of Non-photonic Electrons from Enhancement of Charm Baryons in Heavy Ion Collisions

> At intermediate transverse momentum (2 < p_T < 6 GeV/c), baryon production in Au+Au collisions is enhanced compared to p+p collisions. Since charm baryon decays produce electrons less frequently than charm meson decays, the non-photonic electron spectrum is sensitive to the Lambda_c/D ratio. In this report we study the dependence of the non-photonic electron spectrum on the baryon-to-meson ratio for charm hadrons. As an example, we take the Lambda_c/D ratio to have the same form as the Lambda/K^0_S ratio. In this case, even if the total charm quark yield in Au+Au collisions scales with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (N_bin), the electron spectrum at 2 < p_T < 5 GeV/c is suppressed relative to N_bin scaled p+p collisions by as much as 20%.

## Key claim

Even if the total charm quark yield in Au+Au collisions scales with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (N_bin), the non-photonic electron spectrum at 2 < p_T < 5 GeV/c can be suppressed by as much as 20% relative to N_bin-scaled p+p collisions, provided the Lambda_c/D ratio in Au+Au collisions follows the same enhancement pattern as the Lambda/K^0_S ratio.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA to generate decay electron spectra from input charm hadrons, assuming the Lambda_c/D ratio in Au+Au collisions takes the same functional form as the measured Lambda/K^0_S ratio at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They compared the resulting non-photonic electron spectra for two cases: one where the Lambda_c/D ratio follows the Au+Au Lambda/K^0_S shape, and one where it follows the p+p shape. The total charm hadron spectrum was assumed to follow a power-law with mean p_T = 1.3 GeV/c and n=9, with relative D-meson abundances of 18, 7, and 5 for D^0, D^+, and D_s respectively.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio for Lambda_c -> e + anything is 4.5% ± 1.7%, compared to 17.2% ± 1.9% for D^+ -> e + anything and 6.87% ± 0.28% for D^0 -> e + anything.
- At p_T = 3 GeV/c, in nucleon-nucleon collisions one proton is produced for every three pions (1:3), while in Au+Au collisions protons and pions are created in nearly equal proportion (1:1).
- For p_T > 6.5 GeV/c where the Lambda/K^0_S ratio is unknown, the authors take the value Lambda_c/D = 0.33.
- The suppression of non-photonic electrons at 2 < p_T < 5 GeV/c can be as large as 20% when the Lambda_c/D ratio follows the Au+Au Lambda/K^0_S shape.
- For p_T > 2.0 GeV/c, the PHENIX non-photonic electron data are better represented when the total charm hadron R_AA is 35% greater than charged hadron R_AA.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of baryon enhancement observed at RHIC for light flavor hadrons (protons, Lambdas, Xis, Omegas) to the charm sector, addressing a discrepancy between radiative energy loss models (which predicted smaller suppression for heavy flavor quarks due to the dead-cone effect) and experimental measurements showing large suppression of non-photonic electrons. The authors note that no radiative energy loss models predict the observed suppression, and that collisional energy loss scenarios are being revisited (as in the work of various authors). The study enables future direct measurements of heavy-flavor hadron yields at RHIC with detector upgrades to disentangle the effects of charm baryon enhancement from heavy quark energy loss.
