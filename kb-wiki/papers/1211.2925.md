# Nonconformal holographic model for D-meson suppression at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> The drag force of charm quarks propagating through a thermalized system of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) has been considered within the framework of both conformal/non-conformal Anti de Sitter (AdS) correspondence. Newly derived Einstein Fluctuation-Dissipation relation has been used to calculate the heavy flavor diffusion coefficients. Using the drag and diffusion coefficients as inputs Langevin equation has been solved to study the heavy flavor suppression factor. It has been shown that within conformal AdS correspondence the D-meson suppression at LHC energy can be reproduced where as the non-conformal AdS correspondence fail to reproduce the experimental results. It suggests collisional loss alone within non-conformal AdS correspondence can not reproduce the experimental results and inclusion of radiative loss becomes important.

## Key claim
The conformal AdS/CFT holographic model can reproduce the D-meson suppression factor measured by ALICE at LHC energies, but the non-conformal holographic model fails to reproduce the experimental data when only collisional energy loss is considered. The authors suggest that inclusion of radiative loss is necessary for the non-conformal model to match observations.

## Method
The authors computed drag and diffusion coefficients for charm quarks using both conformal and non-conformal AdS/CFT holography, employing a newly derived Einstein Fluctuation-Dissipation relation. These coefficients were used as inputs to a Langevin equation to simulate heavy quark propagation through a thermalized QGP medium undergoing Bjorken expansion. The resulting charm quark momentum distributions were convolved with Peterson fragmentation functions to obtain D-meson spectra, from which the nuclear suppression factor R_AA was calculated and compared to ALICE data at LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- For the conformal model, the ALICE D-meson R_AA data can be reproduced reasonably well for α = 2 (where α = π√λ/(2√3) = 2.1 ± 0.5)
- The non-conformal drag force is reduced by a factor of 2-4 compared to the conformal case, depending on temperature and momentum
- The string length parameter l_s is fixed at l_s ≈ 0.15 l from lattice QCD string tension matching
- The critical temperature used is T_c = 170 MeV
- The white noise approximation in the Langevin equation is valid for charm quark momenta up to p_max ~ 10 GeV at T ~ T_c and p_max ~ 4.5 GeV at T ~ 2T_c for the conformal case
- For bottom quarks, the corresponding bounds are about 100 GeV at T ~ T_c and 50 GeV at T ~ 2T_c
- The measured total hadronic multiplicity used is dN/dy ≈ 2400 for LHC energies

## Context
This work extends prior holographic studies of heavy flavor suppression, where conformal AdS/CFT had successfully reproduced RHIC results (Hirano, 2008) but under-predicted ALICE data (Gyulassy and Horowitz, 2012). The authors test non-conformal AdS/QCD models (Gursoy, 2009) that incorporate QCD-like equation of state and bulk viscosity, finding that collisional loss alone is insufficient. This enables future work incorporating radiative energy loss from non-conformal holography (Gursoy, 2010) and full frequency-dependent correlators for momenta above 10 GeV (Kiritsis, 2011).
