# Twist-2 relations for the twist-3 tensor-polarized distribution function $f_{LT}$ of a spin-1 hadron by the operator-product-expansion method

> In a spin-1 hadron, tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) exist. The twist-2 function is $f_{1LL}$ and a twist-3 one is $f_{LT}$. Because an experiment is under preparation at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab) to measure the cross section of electron-deuteron deep inelastic scattering with the tensor-polarized deuteron target, these PDFs need to be understood theoretically. Especially, measurements will be done in a relatively low-$Q^2$ region at JLab, so that twist-3 contributions could become sizable in the cross section. In a previous work, a twist-2 relation was derived for $f_{LT}$ in terms of $f_{1LL}$ by using a nonlocal operator, and it corresponds to the Wandzura-Wilczek (WW) relation between $g_1$ and $g_2$. In addition, another relation similar to the Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rule was obtained. It is known that a formal way to derive the WW relation and the BC sum rule is to use the operator product expansion (OPE) with local operators. In this work, the WW-like relation and the BC-like sum rule for $f_{LT}$ are derived by using the local OPE method as a reliable independent way to establish these relations.

# Twist-2 relations for the twist-3 tensor-polarized distribution function \(f_{LT}\) of a spin-1 hadron by the operator-product-expansion method

## Key claim

The twist-3 tensor-polarized parton distribution function \(f_{LT}\) of a spin-1 hadron can be expressed in terms of the twist-2 function \(f_{1LL}\) via a Wandzura-Wilczek-like relation, and satisfies a Burkhardt-Cottingham-like sum rule, provided that the derivation uses the local operator product expansion (OPE) method which manifestly respects rotational invariance. The authors confirm that these relations, previously derived using nonlocal operators, hold as reliable twist-2 approximations, with the caveat that the OPE formalism cannot rigorously prove the relations for the lowest moment (\(n=1\)).

## Method

The authors used the operator product expansion (OPE) with local operators to derive moment relations for the tensor-polarized PDFs \(f_{1LL}\) (twist-2) and \(f_{LT}\) (twist-3) of a spin-1 hadron. They expanded the quark field in a Taylor series, expressed the \(n\)-th moment of the nonlocal vector operator matrix element in terms of local operators, and decomposed those operators into symmetric (twist-2) and mixed-symmetric (twist-3) parts. By matching the OPE expressions to the PDF moments, they obtained the WW-like relation \(f_{LT}^{\pm,\text{twist-2}}(x) = \frac{3}{2} \int_x^1 \frac{dy}{y} f_{1LL}^{\pm}(y)\) and the BC-like sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx \, f_{2LT}^{\pm,\text{twist-2}}(x) = 0\).

## Key numbers

- The WW-like relation: \(f_{LT}^{\pm,\text{twist-2}}(x) = \frac{3}{2} \int_x^1 \frac{dy}{y} f_{1LL}^{\pm}(y)\) (Eq. 4.14 in the paper).
- The BC-like sum rule: \(\int_0^1 dx \, f_{2LT}^{\pm,\text{twist-2}}(x) = 0\), where \(f_{2LT}^{\pm,\text{twist-2}}(x) = \frac{2}{3} f_{LT}^{\pm}(x) - f_{1LL}^{\pm}(x)\).
- The moments of the PDFs are given by \(\int_0^1 dx \, x^{n-1} f_{1LL}^{\pm}(x) = \frac{2(n-1)}{3} a_n\) and \(\int_0^1 dx \, x^{n-1} f_{LT}^{\pm}(x) = \frac{n-1}{n} a_n - \frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2n} d_n\), where \(a_n\) and \(d_n\) are constants from the OPE matrix elements.
- The dynamical twist-3 contribution to the moment of \(f_{LT}\) is \(-\frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2n} d_n\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivation of WW-like and BC-like relations for the spin-1 tensor-polarized PDF \(f_{LT}\) by Kumano (2021), which used nonlocal lightcone correlation functions, by providing an independent confirmation via the formal OPE method with local operators. The OPE approach is the standard technique used for the original WW relation between \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) in spin-1/2 nucleons, as summarized by Jaffe (1989, 1996) and Kodaira (1998). The results are directly relevant to the upcoming JLab experiment (JLab B1, Poudel 2025) measuring electron-deuteron deep inelastic scattering with a tensor-polarized deuteron target at low \(Q^2\) (a few GeV\(^2\)), where twist-3 contributions are expected to be sizable. The confirmed relations enable reliable first estimates of \(f_{LT}\) for the deuteron (Kumano 2025) and the \(\rho\) meson (Puhan 2025), and support future measurements in semi-inclusive DIS (Zhao 2025) and Drell-Yan processes (Qiao 2024).
