# Superheavy Magic Nuclei: Ground-State Properties, Bubble Structure and α-Decay Chains

> A systematic investigation of superheavy nuclei in the isotopic chains of proton numbers Z=106, 114, 120, and 126 together with isotonic chains of neutron numbers N=162, 172, and 184 is presented in the theoretical framework of relativistic mean-field density functionals based on density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. Ground-state properties, including binding energy, shape, deformation, density profile, and radius, are estimated to provide compelling evidence of magicity in these even-even nuclei, aligning with the concept of the 'island of stability'. The analysis reveals central depletion in the charge density, indicating a bubble-like structure, primarily attributed to the substantial repulsive Coulomb field and the influence of higher l-states. A thorough examination of potential decay modes, employing various semi-empirical formulas, is presented. The probable alpha-decay chains are evaluated, demonstrating excellent agreement with available experimental data

## Key claim
The paper claims that superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z=106, 114, 120, 126 and neutron numbers N=162, 172, 184 exhibit magic character and bubble-like central depletion, but this magicity is model-dependent and the evidence is based on relativistic mean-field calculations using the DD-ME2 parameter set, not on experimental confirmation of shell closures.

## Method
The authors performed quadrupole-constrained relativistic mean-field calculations using the density-dependent DD-ME2 parameter set within a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework with a separable Gogny pairing force. Ground-state properties including binding energies, deformation parameters, pairing energies, and proton single-particle levels were computed for even-even superheavy nuclei in isotopic chains Z=106, 114, 120, 126 and isotonic chains N=162, 172, 184. Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using five semi-empirical formulas (MMF, MHF, MSF, SemFIS2, and UNIV), and spontaneous fission half-lives were evaluated using the modified Bao formula (MBF-2020).

## Key numbers
- The uncertainty in theoretical Q-values from DD-ME2 compared to 180 experimental data points is ±0.04 MeV
- Uncertainties in predicted logarithmic half-lives: MMF formula ±0.09, MSF formula ±0.18, MHF formula ±0.30, SemFIS2 formula ±0.15, UNIV formula ±0.22 (all in log10 seconds)
- Zero neutron pairing energy was found for nuclei 290106, 298114, 292120, and 304120, indicating strong magicity
- A shell gap of approximately 3 MeV was observed between levels 1/2-[401] (3/2-[112]) and 7/2+[204] (1/2+[311]) for Z=120 in nuclei 292120 and 304120
- The depletion fraction (D.F.) values for 276114 and 292120 are reported, with 292120 showing substantial central depletion attributed to its magic nature

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical investigations of superheavy magic nuclei using relativistic mean-field theory, including studies by Rutz (1997), Zhang (2005), and Patra (2014, 2021) that predicted stability regions at various proton and neutron numbers. The bubble structure analysis builds on earlier work by Li (2016) and Schuetrumpf (2017) on central depletion in superheavy nuclei, and on the authors' own previous studies (Saxena 2018, 2019) predicting bubble-like structures at N=164, 184, and 228. The alpha-decay chain predictions enable experimental planning for future synthesis attempts at facilities such as GSI Darmstadt, RIKEN Japan, and JINR-FLNR Dubna.
