# Axially symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculations for Nuclei Near the Drip-Lines

> Nuclei far from stability are studied by solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations, which describe the self-consistent mean field theory with pairing interaction. Calculations for even-even nuclei are carried out on two-dimensional axially symmetric lattice, in coordinate space. The quasiparticle continuum wavefunctions are considered for energies up to 60 MeV. Nuclei near the drip lines have a strong coupling between weakly bound states and the particle continuum. This method gives a proper description of the ground state properties of such nuclei. High accuracy is achieved by representing the operators and wavefunctions using the technique of basis-splines. The detailed representation of the HFB equations in cylindrical coordinates is discussed. Calculations of observables for nuclei near the neutron drip line are presented to demonstrate the reliability of the method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for axially symmetric even-even nuclei on a two-dimensional lattice in coordinate space, claiming that this approach gives a proper description of ground state properties for nuclei near the drip lines where there is strong coupling between weakly bound states and the particle continuum. The hedge is that the method is demonstrated only for a few nuclear systems to show convergence, and the work focuses primarily on methodology rather than comprehensive results.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations for axially symmetric even-even nuclei by representing the quasiparticle wavefunctions and operators on a two-dimensional cylindrical coordinate grid using basis-spline (B-spline) techniques, with derivative operators constructed via the Galerkin method and local potentials via collocation. The quasiparticle continuum wavefunctions are included for energies up to 60 MeV, and the HFB Hamiltonian is diagonalized separately for fixed isospin projection and angular momentum projection quantum number Omega, with self-consistency achieved through iterative diagonalization (typically 15-20 iterations for convergence to one part in 10^5 for total binding energy). The calculations use the Skyrme SLy4 force for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range delta pairing interaction with a pairing strength of V0 = -170 MeV fm^3.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of 22O studied as function of energy cutoff, with calculations performed using B-Spline order M = 7, N_r = 18 lattice points, angular momentum projection Omega_max = 5/2, and box size R = 10 fm
* Quasiparticle continuum wavefunctions considered for energies up to 60 MeV
* Pairing strength of V0 = -170 MeV fm^3 used for Skyrme SLy4 force with pure delta-pairing, with equivalent single particle energy cutoff E_max = 60 MeV
* Typically 15-20 iterations sufficient for convergence at the level of one part in 10^5 for the total binding energy

## Context

This work extends the HFB approach to axially symmetric deformed nuclei near the drip lines, building on prior spherical HFB calculations by Dobaczewski et al. (1984) and Dobaczewski and Nazarewicz (1996) who emphasized the need for accurate treatment of pairing in exotic nuclei. The method enables calculations for deformed nuclei that are not spherical in nature, addressing computational challenges for nuclei near the neutron drip line where neutron-matter distributions become diffuse and give rise to neutron halos and skins, and it provides a framework for studying ground state observables such as total binding energy, charge radii, densities, separation energies, and pairing gaps.
