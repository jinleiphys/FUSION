# Precision cross-sections for advancing cosmic-ray physics and other applications: a comprehensive programme for the next decade

> Cosmic-ray physics in the GeV-to-TeV energy range has entered a precision era thanks to recent data from space-based experiments. However, the poor knowledge of nuclear reactions, in particular for the production of antimatter and secondary nuclei, limits the information that can be extracted from these data, such as source properties, transport in the Galaxy and indirect searches for particle dark matter. The Cross-Section for Cosmic Rays at CERN workshop series has addressed the challenges encountered in the interpretation of high-precision cosmic-ray data, with the goal of strengthening emergent synergies and taking advantage of the complementarity and know-how in different communities, from theoretical and experimental astroparticle physics to high-energy and nuclear physics. In this paper, we present the outcomes of the third edition of the workshop that took place in 2024. We present the current state of cosmic-ray experiments and their perspectives, and provide a detailed road map to close the most urgent gaps in cross-section data, in order to efficiently progress on many open physics cases, which are motivated in the paper. Finally, with the aim of being as exhaustive as possible, this report touches several other fields -- such as cosmogenic studies, space radiation protection and hadrontherapy -- where overlapping and specific new cross-section measurements, as well as nuclear code improvement and benchmarking efforts, are also needed. We also briefly highlight further synergies between astroparticle and high-energy physics on the question of cross-sections.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the interpretation of high-precision cosmic-ray data in the GeV-to-TeV range is fundamentally limited by poor knowledge of nuclear reaction cross-sections, and provides a roadmap for the next decade to close the most urgent gaps, though the specific improvements required depend on the physics case and the energy range considered.

## Method
The paper synthesizes outcomes from the third Cross-Section for Cosmic Rays at CERN (XSCRC) workshop held in 2024, reviewing the current state and future perspectives of cosmic-ray experiments, and compiling a prioritized list of nuclear reactions and energies needing measurement. It also surveys existing and planned accelerator facilities where these measurements could take place, and identifies synergies between astroparticle physics, high-energy physics, nuclear physics, and transverse fields such as space radiation protection and hadrontherapy.

## Key numbers
- The cosmic-ray spectrum spans roughly 12 orders of magnitude in energy and 33 orders of magnitude in flux, with a power-law index gamma approximately 2.5 to 3.
- The AMS experiment has measured the antiproton flux between 0.5 GeV and 500 GeV, with data precision below 10% in the GeV-to-TeV range for many cosmic-ray species.
- The Galactic halo half-height L is poorly constrained between 2 and 10 kpc, determined from ratios of radioactive to stable secondary species such as 10Be/9Be.
- The NA49 experiment released a preliminary measurement of antineutron production from proton-proton collisions that is about 20% larger than the cross-section for antiproton production.

## Context
This paper extends the work of the XSCRC workshop series (2017, 2019, 2024) which addresses cross-section limitations in cosmic-ray physics. It builds on precision data from experiments such as AMS (AMS:PhysRep2021) and PAMELA (PAMELA:Galper2017), and aims to enable progress on indirect dark matter searches, Galactic transport modeling, and source property determination. The roadmap targets cross-section measurements needed for interpreting secondary nuclei, antimatter, and gamma-ray production, and also addresses needs in cosmogenic studies, space radiation protection, and hadrontherapy, where overlapping requirements exist.
