# Single-particle spectroscopic strength from nucleon transfer reactions with a three-nucleon force contribution

> The direct reaction theory widely used to study single-particle spectroscopic strength in nucleon transfer experiments is based on a Hamiltonian with two-nucleon interactions only. We point out that in reactions with a loosely-bound projectile, where clustering and breakup effects are important, an additional three-body force arises due to three-nucleon ($3N$) interaction between two nucleons belonging to different clusters in the projectile and a target nucleon. We study the effects of this force on nucleon transfer in $(d,p)$ and $(d,n)$ reactions on $^{56}$Ni, $^{48}$Ca, $^{26m}$Al and $^{24}$O targets at deuteron incident energies between 4 and 40 MeV/nucleon. Deuteron breakup is treated exactly within a continuum discretized coupled-channel approach. It was found that an additional three-body force can noticeably alter the angular distributions at forward angles, with consequences for spectroscopic factors' studies. Additional study of transfer to $2p$ continuum in the $^{25}$F$(p,2p)^{24}$O reaction, involving the same overlap function as in the $^{24}$O($d,n)^{25}$F case, revealed that $3N$ force affects the $(d,n)$ and $(p,2p)$ reactions in a similar way, increasing the cross sections and decreasing spectroscopic factors, although its influence at the main peak of $(p,2p)$ is weaker. The angle-integrated cross sections are found to be less sensitive to the 3N force contribution, they increase by less than 20$\%$. Including 3N interactions in nucleon removal reactions makes an essential step towards bringing together nuclear structure theory, where 3N force is routinely used, and nuclear direct reaction theory, based on two-nucleon interactions only.

# Single-particle spectroscopic strength from nucleon transfer reactions with a three-nucleon force contribution

## Key claim

The paper claims that an additional three-body force arising from three-nucleon (3N) interactions between two nucleons in different clusters of a loosely-bound projectile and a target nucleon can noticeably alter angular distributions in (d,p) and (d,n) reactions, with consequences for spectroscopic factor determination. However, the authors hedge that these conclusions were made ignoring other uncertainties associated with reaction parameters and using a fixed set of optical potentials, and that the effects depend on the specific 3N interaction model chosen (AV18+UIX) and the assumption of zero-spin targets.

## Method

The authors added a 3N-induced contribution to the n-p-A three-body Hamiltonian, derived from the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon interaction combined with the AV18 NN potential, and solved the n-p-A scattering problem within the continuum discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) approach using the FRESCO code. They calculated angular distributions and angle-integrated cross sections for (d,p) and (d,n) reactions on 56Ni, 48Ca, 26mAl, and 24O targets at deuteron incident energies between 4 and 40 MeV/nucleon, and additionally studied the 25F(p,2p)24O reaction treated as transfer to the 2p continuum within CDCC.

## Key numbers

- The 3N-induced coupling potential U^(00)_3N for d+56Ni is repulsive with a strength of 5.1 MeV, determined mostly by the UIX contact part; the 2pi-exchange contribution gives a small attractive contribution with depth of about 1.26 MeV
- For 48Ca(d,n)49Sc at Ed = 40 MeV/nucleon, the 3N contribution reduces spectroscopic factors for 49Sc(g.s.) and 49Sc*(3/2-) by 23% and 47%, respectively
- For 56Ni(d,p)57Ni*(0.768) at Ed = 32 MeV/nucleon, the angle-integrated cross section gives S = 0.78(22) without 3N force and S = 0.72(21) with 3N force
- For 26mAl(d,p)27Al at Ed = 4.6 MeV/nucleon, the 3N effects increase the main peak cross section by 12% for l=2 transfer to the ground state and by 8% and 5% for l=0 transfers to excited states at Ex = 0.84 and 6.8 MeV, respectively
- For 24O(d,n)25F at Ed = 25 MeV/nucleon, the 3N effects increase the main peak cross sections by 23% and 35% for 5/2+ and 1/2+ states, respectively
- For 25F(p,2p)24O at the same three-body energy, the 3N effects increase main peak cross sections by 8% and 18% for the same states
- Angle-integrated cross sections increase by less than 20% when adding the 3N contribution across all cases studied
- The 3N-induced difference in spectroscopic factors obtained from complementary (d,n) and (p,2p) reactions could reach 14%

## Context

This work extends the standard direct reaction theory for nucleon transfer, which has been based on Hamiltonians with only two-nucleon interactions (Satchler), by incorporating a three-body force arising from 3N interactions. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of spectroscopic factor quenching discrepancies between transfer and knockout experiments, particularly the asymmetry-dependent quenching seen in knockout experiments (Tosetti 2021) versus the absence of such quenching in transfers (Flaherty 2018, Manning 2021, Aumann 2021). The paper enables future integration of nuclear structure theory, where 3N forces are routinely used in ab-initio calculations (Hergert 2020), with direct reaction theory, and points to the need for consistent NN+3N interaction models and target density information from structure theorists to advance single-particle spectroscopic strength studies.
