# Spin, Twist and Hadron Structure in Deep Inelastic Processes

> These notes provide an introduction to polarization effects in deep inelastic processes in QCD. We emphasize recent work on transverse asymmetries, subdominant effects, and the role of polarization in fragmentation and in purely hadronic processes. After a review of kinematics and some basic tools of short distance analysis, we study the twist, helicity, chirality and transversity dependence of a variety of high energy processes sensitive to the quark and gluon substructure of hadrons.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide an introduction to polarization effects in deep inelastic processes in QCD, with the central claim that perturbative QCD is sufficiently well understood that it can be used as a "sophisticated probe" to measure nucleon expectation values of quark and gluon distributions, rather than focusing on testing QCD itself or explaining the "spin crisis."

## Method

The notes review the kinematics and basic tools of short-distance analysis for deep inelastic scattering (DIS), inclusive e+e- annihilation, and Drell-Yan processes, using the operator product expansion in coordinate space to classify scattering amplitudes by helicity, chirality, twist, and transversity. The analysis separates leptonic and hadronic tensors, parametrizes the hadronic tensor in terms of structure functions (F1, F2, g1, g2), and examines how these functions scale in the Bjorken limit while mostly ignoring QCD radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken scaling variable x is defined as x = Q^2/2nu, with 0 < x <= 1.
- The fine structure constant alpha is approximately 1/137.
- The Callan-Gross relation gives F1 - (1/2x)F2 ~ 1/ln Q^2, indicating quarks have spin 1/2.
- The ratio R(Q^2) for e+e- annihilation to hadrons includes perturbative QCD corrections: R = sum_q 3 e_q^2 {1 + alpha_QCD/pi + 1.409 (alpha_QCD/pi)^2 - 12.805 (alpha_QCD/pi)^3 + ...} in the MSbar scheme with five flavors.
- Effects associated with g2(x,Q^2) are suppressed by a factor sqrt(kappa-1) = 2Mx/sqrt(Q^2) relative to g1, making g2 a "higher twist" effect.
- For Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, structure functions depend only very weakly on Q^2.

## Context

These lecture notes were presented at the International School of Nucleon Structure (Erice, 1995) and extend the standard parton model treatment found in textbooks by Aitken (1989), Close (1979), and Nachtmann (1990) to include transverse polarization asymmetries, subdominant effects, and polarization in fragmentation and hadronic processes. The work is based on collaborations with Xiangdong Ji (referenced as JafJi91, JafJi1952, JafJi2005, JafJi2158, JafJi2365, JafJi2402) and enables future experimental and theoretical analysis of spin effects in hard processes, including the polarized Drell-Yan program at RHIC.
