# Small-x TMD distributions initial condition: Nc-dependence and Gaussian approximations

> We systematically derive expressions for ten small-$x$ Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) distributions in the Gaussian approximation, three in the quark-gluon sector and seven in the gluon-gluon sector; for the general $SU(N_c)$ gauge group. The derived formulae depend on the logarithm of the dipole amplitude. Using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the initial condition, we simulate the dipole amplitude as well as estimate all ten TMD distributions for $N_c \in \{2,3,4,5\}$. We compare these explicit numerical results with the derived expressions and find very good agreement for all studied values of $N_c$. Consequently, we study the scaling with $N_c$ of the TMD distributions. Thanks to that, we are able to derive the large-$N_c$ limit of the Gaussian approximation results and show that they agree with expressions obtained in the mean-field approximation. By comparing with our numerical results, we are able to demonstrate the size, origin, and significance of subleading-$N_c$ corrections. This work sets the stage for a systematic study of subleading corrections induced by the JIMWLK evolution in the rapidity evolution of the TMD distributions. Interestingly, we find an exact sum rule that relates all seven gluon-gluon TMD operators at $N_c = 3$ and arbitrary $x$.

## Key claim

The paper systematically derives Gaussian approximation expressions for ten small-x TMD distributions (three quark-gluon and seven gluon-gluon) for general SU(N_c), and validates these expressions against numerical simulations using the McLerran-Venugopalan model for N_c in {2,3,4,5}, finding very good agreement for all studied values of N_c, but the abstract glosses over that the Gaussian approximation is validated specifically for the MV initial condition and that significant deviations are anticipated at large separations based on prior quadrupole studies.

## Method

The authors first express all ten TMD operators in terms of Wilson lines and their derivatives, then introduce birdtrack representations to relate these operators to irreducible representation projections of the color charge system. They compute expectation values of the operators \(\hat{\Omega}\) within the Gaussian approximation for general SU(N_c), using the McLerran-Venugopalan model to simulate the dipole amplitude and estimate all ten TMD distributions numerically for N_c = 2, 3, 4, 5. The numerical results are compared with the derived Gaussian approximation expressions and with large-N_c mean-field approximations to identify the size and origin of subleading-N_c corrections.

## Key numbers

- The dipole operator \(\hat{S}(\xvec,\yvec) = \frac{1}{N_c} \tr[V_\xvec V^\dagger_\yvec]\) is defined with normalization factor \(1/N_c\)
- The Casimir operator \(C_F = \frac{N_c^2-1}{2N_c}\) appears in the projector for the \(\overline{3}\) irrep in the quark-gluon sector
- The quantity \(\Delta_{\xvec,\yvec;\zvec} = \langle \hat{S}_{\xvec\zvec} \hat{S}_{\zvec\yvec} \rangle - \langle \hat{S}_{\xvec\zvec} \rangle \langle \hat{S}_{\zvec\yvec} \rangle \sim \mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\) is estimated to be \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\) for N_c=3 but observed to be \(\mathcal{O}(1\%)\)
- The paper studies N_c values in the set {2, 3, 4, 5} for all numerical simulations
- An exact sum rule is found that relates all seven gluon-gluon TMD operators at N_c = 3 and arbitrary x

## Context

This work extends the analysis of N_c-dependence from previous studies of the dipole and quadrupole operators in the color glass condensate framework, particularly following the quadrupole analysis of Dumitru (2011) which showed that JIMWLK evolution agrees with the Gaussian approximation at N_c = 3 but anticipated significant deviations at large distances. The paper sets the stage for systematic study of subleading corrections induced by JIMWLK evolution in the rapidity evolution of TMD distributions, building on the observation from Rummukainen (2003) and Kovchegov (2008) that agreement between JIMWLK and BK evolution for dipole distributions is better than the naive 1/N_c^2 counting by an order of magnitude.
