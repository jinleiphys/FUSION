# Limits on temporal variation of fine structure constant, quark masses and strong interaction from atomic clock experiments

> We perform calculations of the dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on quark masses and obtain limits on the variation of the ratio of quark mass and strong interaction scale (m_q/Lambda_{QCD}) from recent atomic clock experiments with hyperfine transitions in H, Rb, Cs, Yb+, Hg+ and optical transition in Hg+. Experiments with Cd+, deuterium/hydrogen, molecule SF_6, Zeeman transitions in He_3/Xe are also discussed.

# Limits on temporal variation of fine structure constant, quark masses and strong interaction from atomic clock experiments

## Key claim
The paper derives limits on the temporal variation of the dimensionless ratio \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) (quark mass relative to the QCD scale) from atomic clock comparisons, but the accuracy for the dependence on \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) is about 30% (limited by the single-particle approximation for nuclear magnetic moments), and results for the strange quark mass \(m_s\) should be treated as order-of-magnitude estimates only.

## Method
The author calculates the dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on quark masses using the Cloudy Bag Model fit to nucleon g-factors as a function of pion mass, incorporating chiral perturbation theory results for pion and kaon loop corrections. These nuclear magnetic moment variations are combined with the known dependence of hyperfine structure constants on the fine structure constant \(\alpha\) and the relativistic Casimir factor, yielding expressions for the time variation of frequency ratios measured in atomic clock experiments with H, Rb, Cs, Yb\(^+\), Hg\(^+\), and optical transitions in Hg\(^+\).

## Key numbers
- Limit on variation of \(X(CsRb) = \alpha^{0.49} [m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.17}\): \((0.2 \pm 7) \times 10^{-16}\)/year from Cs/Rb hyperfine comparison (Marion et al.)
- Upper limit on variation of \(X(CsH) = \alpha^{0.83} [m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.2}\): \(< 5.5 \times 10^{-14}\)/year from Cs/H hyperfine comparison
- Upper limit on variation of \(X(HgH) \approx \alpha^{2.3}[m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.1}\): \(< 8 \times 10^{-14}\)/year from Hg\(^+\)/H hyperfine comparison (Prestage et al.)
- Limit on variation of \(X(YbCs) = \alpha^{0.7}[m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.11}\): \(\approx -1(2) \times 10^{-13}\)/year from Yb\(^+\)/Cs hyperfine comparison (based on Fisk et al.)
- Upper limit on variation of \(X(Opt) = \alpha^{6}[m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.11}[m_e/\Lambda_{QCD}]\): \(< 7 \times 10^{-15}\)/year from Cs hyperfine / Hg\(^+\) optical transition comparison (Bize et al.)
- Dependence of proton g-factor on quark mass: \(\delta g_p/g_p = -0.087 \, \delta m_q/m_q\)
- Dependence of neutron g-factor on quark masses: \(\delta g_n/g_n = -0.107 (\delta m_q/m_q - \delta m_s/m_s)\)

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier estimates (Flambaum and Shuryak) of limits on \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) variation from H, Cs, and Hg\(^+\) measurements, which gave about \(5 \times 10^{-13}\) per year, by incorporating more precise atomic clock data and improved calculations of nuclear magnetic moment dependence on quark masses. The results complement limits from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, quasar absorption spectra, and the Oklo natural nuclear reactor (Flambaum and Shuryak; Dmitriev et al.; Olive et al.), and provide a framework for interpreting ongoing and planned atomic clock experiments with Cd\(^+\), deuterium/hydrogen, SF\(_6\) molecular vibrations, and Zeeman transitions in \(^3\)He/Xe.
