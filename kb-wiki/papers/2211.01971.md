# Detecting nuclear mass distribution in isobar collisions via charmonium

> The collective properties of final state hadrons produced in the high statistics $_{44}^{96}$Ru+$_{44}^{96}$Ru and $_{40}^{96}$Zr+$_{40}^{96}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200~\mathrm{GeV}$ are found to be significantly different. Such differences were argued to be precise probes of the difference in nucleon distribution in the isobar nuclei. We investigate the $J/ψ$ production in the isobar collision via a relativistic transport approach. By comparing the isobar systems according to equal centrality bin and equal multiplicity bin, we find that the yield ratio of $J/ψ$ is sensitive to the differences in both the number of binary collisions and the medium evolution. Besides, the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $J/ψ$ is qualitatively different from the light hadrons, and the ratio between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is sensitive to the medium evolution. The charmonium production provides an independent probe to study the nucleon distribution in the isobar system.

## Key claim

The yield ratio of $J/\psi$ between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is sensitive to differences in both the number of binary collisions and the medium evolution, and the elliptic flow $v_2$ ratio is sensitive to the medium evolution, providing an independent probe of nucleon distribution in isobar nuclei, but the sensitivity depends on whether the comparison is made at equal centrality or equal multiplicity.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport approach combining Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions with event-averaged hydrodynamic evolution (using the MUSIC package with s95p equation of state, constant shear viscosity $\eta/s = 0.08$, and vanishing bulk viscosity) to model the quark-gluon plasma medium created in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200~\mathrm{GeV}$. They solved a transport equation for charmonium states ($J/\psi$, $\chi_c$, $\psi'$) including initial production (with cold nuclear matter effects: Cronin effect and nuclear shadowing via EPS09), Debye screening dissociation, gluo-dissociation, and regeneration from thermalized charm quarks, then applied feed-down decays from $\chi_c$ and $\psi'$ to obtain prompt $J/\psi$ observables.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200~\mathrm{GeV}$ for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at RHIC
- Dissociation temperatures: $T_d = 2.3~T_c$ for $J/\psi$, $1.2~T_c$ for $\chi_c$, and $1.1~T_c$ for $\psi'$, with $T_c = 160~\mathrm{MeV}$
- Nuclear deformation parameters used: $R_0^\mathrm{Ru}=5.09~\mathrm{fm}$, $a_0^\mathrm{Ru}=0.46~\mathrm{fm}$, $\beta_2^\mathrm{Ru}=0.162$, $\beta_3^\mathrm{Ru}=0.0$; $R_0^\mathrm{Zr}=5.02~\mathrm{fm}$, $a_0^\mathrm{Zr}=0.52~\mathrm{fm}$, $\beta_2^\mathrm{Zr}=0.06$, $\beta_3^\mathrm{Zr}=0.2$
- Charm quark production cross section per unit rapidity in p+p: $d\sigma^{c\bar{c}}_{pp}/dy = 0.162~\mathrm{mb}$
- Prompt $J/\psi$ production cross section in p+p: $d\sigma^{J/\psi,\mathrm{prompt}}_{pp}/dy = 716.7~\mathrm{nb}$
- Direct production cross section fractions: $d\sigma^{\{J/\psi,\chi_c,\psi'\}}_{pp}/dy = \{0.6, 0.3, 0.1\} \times d\sigma^{J/\psi,\mathrm{prompt}}_{pp}/dy$
- Cronin parameter: $a_{gN}=0.1~\mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}$
- Expected difference in $v_{2,J/\psi}$ ratio between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr: approximately $5-10\%$ in non-central collisions
- A cusp in $R_{AA}$ is observed at the $5-10\%$ centrality bin or the $\sim 230$ multiplicity bin

## Context

This work extends the study of isobar collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration, 2021) beyond bulk observables like multiplicity and flow, which were found to differ unexpectedly and were previously analyzed using transport models (Zhang, 2021) and hydrodynamics (Nijs, 2021) to extract nuclear deformation parameters. The authors build on charmonium transport theory (Yan, 2006; Liu, 2009; Zhou, 2014; Chen, 2018) and the finding that Ru has a sizable quadrupole deformation while Zr has an octupole deformation (Mach, 1990; Hofer, 1993; Zhang, 2021; Xu, 2021). This work enables using charmonium as an independent probe of nucleon distribution, complementary to light hadron observables, and suggests that at LHC energies where regeneration dominates, the sensitivity to nuclear deformation may be even stronger.
