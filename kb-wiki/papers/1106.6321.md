# The thermal model on the verge of the ultimate test: particle production in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We investigate the production of hadrons in nuclear collisions within the framework of the thermal (or statistical hadronization) model. We discuss both the ligh-quark hadrons as well as charmonium and provide predictions for the LHC energy. Even as its exact magnitude is dependent on the charm production cross section, not yet measured in Pb-Pb collisions, we can confidently predict that at the LHC the nuclear modification factor of charmonium as a function of centrality is larger than that observed at RHIC and compare the experimental results to these predictions.

## Key claim

The thermal (statistical hadronization) model predicts that at LHC energies the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^{J/\psi}\) will be larger than at RHIC, but the exact magnitude depends on the charm production cross section in Pb-Pb collisions, which was not yet measured at the time of writing.

## Method

The authors applied the statistical hadronization model to light-quark hadrons and charmonium, using chemical freeze-out temperature \(T\) and baryochemical potential \(\mu_b\) parametrized as functions of center-of-mass energy from fits to data at AGS, SPS, and RHIC. For charmonium, they assumed all charm quarks are produced in initial hard collisions, with hadronization occurring at the phase boundary using statistical weights and a balance equation that depends on the charm production cross section from pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- The limiting temperature extracted from fits is \(T_{lim}=164\) MeV.
- The baryochemical potential expected at LHC is around 1 MeV.
- The parametrization for temperature is \(T = T_{lim}/[1+\exp(2.60-\ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}}(\mathrm{GeV}))/0.45)]\).
- The parametrization for baryochemical potential is \(\mu_b [\mathrm{MeV}] = 1303/[1+0.286\sqrt{s_{NN}}(\mathrm{GeV})]\).
- For Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, the model predicts \(\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)=0.033\) and \(\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)=0.005\), compared to 0.32 and 0.15 in \(p\bar{p}\) collisions at Tevatron.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model description of hadron production from AGS through RHIC energies (previously fitted by Andronic et al.) to make predictions for LHC energies, particularly for charmonium. The statistical hadronization model for charmonium was originally proposed by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) and later applied to SPS and RHIC data by Andronic et al. (2007, 2009). The predictions enable experimental tests with ALICE and CMS data at LHC, and if confirmed, would consolidate charmonium as a probe of deconfinement and the QCD phase boundary.
