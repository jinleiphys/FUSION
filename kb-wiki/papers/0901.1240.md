# On Th229 and time-dependent fundamental constants

> The electromagnetic transition between the almost degenerate 5/2+ and 3/2+ states in Th229 is deemed to be very sensitive to potential changes in the fine structure constant alpha. State of the art Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations are performed to compute the difference in Coulomb energies of the two states which determines the amplification of variations in alpha into variations of the transition frequency. The kinetic energies are also calculated which reflect a possible variation in the nucleon or quark masses. A generalized Hellmann-Feynman theorem is proved including the use of density-matrix functionals. As the two states differ mainly in the orbit occupied by the last unpaired neutron the Coulomb energy difference results from a change in the nuclear polarization of the proton distribution. This effect turns out to be rather small and to depend on the nuclear model, the amplification varies between about -4 x 10^4 and +4 x 10^4. Therefore much more effort must be put into the improvement of the nuclear models before one can draw conclusions from a measured drift in the transition frequency on a temporal drift of fundamental constants. All calculations published so far do not reach the necessary fidelity. PACS 06.20.Jr,21.60.Jz,27.90.+b

## Key claim

The amplification factor \(A\) for converting a drift in the fine structure constant \(\alpha\) into a drift of the \(^{229}\)Th nuclear transition frequency varies between about \(-4 \times 10^4\) and \(+4 \times 10^4\) depending on the nuclear model used, meaning that current nuclear models do not reach the necessary fidelity to draw reliable conclusions from a measured frequency drift.

## Method

State-of-the-art Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed for the nucleus \(^{229}\)Th to compute the difference in Coulomb energies \(\Delta V_C\) between the \(5/2^+\) ground state and the \(3/2^+\) isomeric state, which differ mainly by the orbit occupied by the last unpaired neutron. A generalized Hellmann-Feynman theorem was proved for density-matrix functionals, allowing the amplification factor \(A = \Delta V_C / \omega\) to be obtained from the calculated Coulomb energy difference and the measured transition frequency \(\omega = 7.6 \pm 0.5\) eV. Kinetic energies of protons and neutrons were also calculated to account for possible variations in nucleon or quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The transition frequency between the \(3/2^+\) isomeric state and the \(5/2^+\) ground state of \(^{229}\)Th is \(\omega = 7.6 \pm 0.5\) eV (Beck et al., 2007).
- The amplification factor \(A\) for variations in the fine structure constant \(\alpha\) ranges from about \(-4 \times 10^4\) to \(+4 \times 10^4\), depending on the nuclear model.
- Typical Coulomb energies for this nucleus are of order \(V_C \approx 10^9\) eV.
- The estimated electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is \(\Delta m^{\rm{(EM)}}_{np} = -0.68\) MeV, yielding \(\alpha (dm_p/d\alpha)/m_p \approx +0.36 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(\alpha (dm_n/d\alpha)/m_n \approx -0.36 \times 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Peik and Tamm (2003) and Flambaum (2006) to use the \(^{229}\)Th nuclear transition as a sensitive probe for temporal variation of fundamental constants. The calculations show that the amplification factor is highly model-dependent and can even change sign, contradicting earlier expectations of a large and robust sensitivity. The results indicate that much more effort must be put into improving nuclear models before experimental limits on a drift in the transition frequency can be interpreted as a constraint on the drift of \(\alpha\) or quark masses.
