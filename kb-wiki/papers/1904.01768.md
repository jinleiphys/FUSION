# On the convergence of chiral expansions for charmed meson masses in the up, down and strange quark masses

> We discuss the convergence properties of chiral expansions for the pseudoscalar and vector charmed meson masses based on the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. Conventional expansion strategies as formulated in terms of bare meson masses are shown to suffer from poor convergence properties. This changes once the expansion is set up in terms of on-shell masses. We find a rapid convergence of the chiral expansion from vanishing quark masses up to physical values of the strange quark mass in this case. Detailed results are presented at the one-loop level for the D-meson and D^*-meson masses. It is emphasized that our results do not depend on the renormalization scale. An approximation hierarchy for the chiral Ward identities of QCD is obtained that keeps the proper form of low-energy branch points and cuts as they are implied by the use of on-shell masses. Given such a scheme we analyzed the charmed meson masses as available on various QCD lattice ensembles. In terms of the determined low-energy constants we consider the coupled-channel interactions of the Goldstone bosons with open-charm mesons. For the isospin violating hadronic decay width of the D_{s0}^*(2317) we predict the range (104-116) keV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that conventional chiral expansions for charmed meson masses, formulated in terms of bare meson masses, suffer from poor convergence properties; however, when the expansion is set up in terms of on-shell masses, rapid convergence is achieved from vanishing quark masses up to physical values of the strange quark mass, with results that do not depend on the renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian for pseudoscalar (J^P = 0^-) and vector (J^P = 1^-) charmed mesons and derived one-loop mass equations involving bubble-loop corrections proportional to the coupling g_P and counter terms c_0 through c_5. They solved the coupled, non-linear mass equations using a renormalization scheme where heavy tadpole terms are dropped and the scalar bubble loop is expressed in a dispersion-integral form that preserves the proper analytic structure, then fitted the resulting low-energy constants to charmed meson masses from five different QCD lattice ensembles (ETMC, PACS-CS, HSC, HPQCD, LHPC) and to s-wave scattering data.

## Key numbers

* The coupling g_P is determined from the hadronic decay width of the D*-meson as |g_P| = 0.57 ± 0.07.
* For the D meson at physical masses, the bubble loop decomposition yields contributions of -50.2 MeV (full), -48.5 MeV (Q^3), -2.8 MeV (Q^4), and 1.1 MeV (Q^5) using M = 1907.4 MeV and Δ = 191.7 MeV.
* For the D_s meson, the bubble loop contributions are -65.6 MeV (full), -88.3 MeV (Q^3), 20.1 MeV (Q^4), and 2.9 MeV (Q^5).
* For the D* meson, the bubble loop contributions are -113.4 MeV (full), -99.5 MeV (Q^3), -17.1 MeV (Q^4), and 3.1 MeV (Q^5).
* For the D*_s meson, the bubble loop contributions are -166.1 MeV (full), -197.5 MeV (Q^3), 26.3 MeV (Q^4), and 6.6 MeV (Q^5).
* The chiral limit mass M for the D meson ranges from 1.8762 GeV (Fit 1) to 1.9382 GeV (Fit 2) across the four fit scenarios.
* The physical quark-mass ratio m_s/m is compatible with the ETMC result m_s/m = 26.66(32).
* The predicted isospin-violating hadronic decay width of the D_{s0}*(2317) is in the range (104-116) keV, using a π^0-η mixing angle ε = 0.0122(18).

## Context

This work extends the on-shell mass renormalization scheme previously applied to light baryon masses by Lutz (2018) to the charmed meson sector, addressing the poor convergence of conventional chiral perturbation theory expansions in the strange quark mass. The analysis uses lattice data from five groups (ETMC, PACS-CS, HSC, HPQCD, LHPC) and builds on the chiral Lagrangian for open-charm mesons developed by Kolomeitsev (2003), Hofmann (2003), and Lutz (2007). The results enable improved predictions for coupled-channel interactions of Goldstone bosons with open-charm mesons, particularly the isospin-violating decay width of the D_{s0}*(2317) resonance, which is within the expected resolution of the PANDA experiment at FAIR.
