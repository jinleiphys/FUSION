# The maximum and minimum mass of protoneutron stars in the Brueckner theory

> We study the structure of protoneutron stars within the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theoretical approach, paying particular attention to how it is joined to a low-density nuclear equation of state (EOS). We find a slight sensitivity of the minimum value of the protoneutron star mass on the low-density equation of state, whereas the maximum mass is hardly affected.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a protoneutron star is hardly affected by the choice of low-density equation of state, while the minimum mass shows a slight sensitivity to that choice, with both results depending on the idealized assumption of constant entropy and a fixed lepton fraction throughout the star.

## Method

The authors used the finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential and phenomenological Urbana three-body forces to compute the equation of state (EOS) of hot nuclear matter. They joined this high-density EOS to three different low-density liquid-drop-type EOSs (Lattimer-Swesty with two compressibilities, and Shen) using a Maxwell construction to ensure continuity of pressure and chemical potentials. Protoneutron star configurations were then obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for fixed entropy per baryon values of S/A = 0, 1, and 2, with and without neutrino trapping (Y_e = 0.4, Y_mu = 0).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for cold untrapped matter: 2.03 solar masses (radius ~9.9 km, central density ~10.5 times saturation density) for all three low-density EOSs (LS, SKa, Shen).
- Maximum mass for neutrino-trapped matter at S/A = 1 or 2: 1.95 solar masses for all three low-density EOSs.
- Minimum mass for neutrino-trapped matter at S/A = 1: ranges from 0.58 to 0.60 solar masses (radii 38-44 km) depending on the low-density EOS.
- Minimum mass for neutrino-trapped matter at S/A = 2: ranges from 0.70 to 0.77 solar masses (radii 42-52 km) depending on the low-density EOS.
- Transition density between low-density and BHF EOS for untrapped T=0 matter: 0.160 fm^-3 (LS), 0.110 fm^-3 (SKa), 0.120 fm^-3 (Shen).
- Critical temperature of the Van der Waals behavior in symmetric matter: approximately 19 MeV at a critical density of about 0.06 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finite-temperature BHF calculations (Nicotra 2006; Burgio and Schulze 2009) by using an exact finite-temperature treatment of the single-particle potential instead of the frozen correlations approximation, and by carefully joining the high-density BHF EOS to different low-density models. The minimum mass values are similar to those found by Gondek (1997) using the LS EOS, but lower than those calculated by Strobel (1999), due to the different treatment of the PNS evolutionary stage. The results enable future consistent dynamical simulations of protoneutron star evolution that would use self-consistent temperature and neutrino-trapping profiles rather than the idealized constant-entropy profiles employed here.
