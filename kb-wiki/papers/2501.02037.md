# Study of medium-mass and heavy hypernuclei produced through spallation and fission reactions in inverse kinematics

> Innovative experiments using the inverse kinematics technique to accelerate light, medium-mass, and heavy nuclei at relativistic energies have become excellent tools to produce and study hypernuclei. In this work, we investigate hypernuclei created in spallation reactions, where multifragmentation, particle evaporation, and fission processes play an important role in the formation of final hypernuclei residues. For the description of spallation reactions, we couple the Liège intranuclear cascade model, extended recently to the strange sector, to a new version of the ablation (ABLA) model that accounts for the evaporation of Λ-particles from hot hyperremnants produced during the intranuclear cascade stage. These state-of-the art models are then used to study the production of hypernuclei close to the drip lines through spallation-evaporation and fission reactions. Moreover, recent results obtained for the study of hypernuclei dynamics, in particular, for the constraint of the viscosity parameter involved in hyperfission reactions are also presented.

# Study of medium-mass and heavy hypernuclei produced through spallation and fission reactions in inverse kinematics

## Key claim

The coupled INCL+ABLA model can describe hypernuclei production in spallation and fission reactions, and the viscosity parameter for hypernuclear fission is constrained to an average value of (28 ± 12) × 10^21 s^-1, which is approximately 6 times larger than that for normal nuclear matter, implying that the conversion of intrinsic energy into collective motion is much slower in the presence of hyperons.

## Method

The authors coupled the Liège intranuclear cascade model (INCL), extended to the strange sector to include kaon and hyperon production, with a new C++ version of the ablation model ABLA that accounts for evaporation of Λ-particles from hot hyperremnants. The INCL model describes the proton-nucleus collision as a sequence of binary collisions, while ABLA models deexcitation through emission of γ-rays, neutrons, Λ-hyperons, light-charged particles, intermediate-mass fragments, and fission decays using the Weisskopf-Ewing formalism and the Bohr-Wheeler transition-state model with Kramers dissipation.

## Key numbers

- Hypernuclear fission cross sections for proton-induced reactions on 238U decrease when increasing the viscosity parameter β, with values tested at 4.5, 20, and 40 × 10^21 s^-1.
- The viscosity coefficient for hypernuclear fission was constrained to an average value of (28 ± 12) × 10^21 s^-1.
- Uncertainties in the hypernuclear fission cross section calculations do not exceed 18% of the total, with 16% attributed to the fission barrier height.
- The constrained dissipation parameter is 6 times larger than that obtained for normal nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the INCL model (Mancusi 2014) and the ABLA model (Rodríguez-Sánchez 2022) to the strangeness sector, building on prior studies of kaon production in proton-induced reactions (Jason 2020) and hypernuclei production via strangeness-exchange reactions (Rodríguez-Sánchez 2018). The results enable the investigation of cold hypernuclei far from stability and provide the first constraint on nuclear dissipation in hypernuclear matter, which has implications for understanding r-mode oscillations in neutron stars and gravitational-wave emission from pulsars (Andersson 1998, Friedman 1998).
