# Chiral Thermodynamics with Charm

> Chiral thermodynamics of charmed mesons is formulated at finite temperature within a $2+1+1$-flavored effective Lagrangian incorporating heavy quark symmetry. The chiral mass splittings are shown to be less sensitive to the light-quark flavors, attributed to the underlying heavy quark symmetry. Consequently, chiral symmetry restoration is more accelerated in the strange charmed-mesons than in the strange light mesons, and this is in striking contrast to the chiral SU(4) result. We also show the correlations between light and heavy flavors around the chiral crossover.

## Key claim

Chiral mass splittings between scalar and pseudo-scalar charmed mesons are essentially insensitive to the light-quark flavor (non-strange vs. strange), with both splitting sizes near the chiral pseudo-critical temperature being approximately equal, in contrast to the chiral SU(4) result where the non-strange splitting is much smaller than the strange one.

## Method

The author formulates a 2+1+1-flavored chiral effective Lagrangian for light and heavy-light mesons that incorporates heavy quark symmetry, with effective interactions depending on temperature extracted from lattice QCD chiral condensates. In-medium masses of charmed mesons (non-strange D and D*; strange D_s and D_s*) and their parity partners (scalar D_0* and axial-vector D_1) are computed as functions of temperature. Flavor correlations between light and heavy-light mesons are studied via susceptibility matrices derived from the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The chiral pseudo-critical temperature is taken as T_pc = 154 MeV.
- The non-strange scalar charmed meson mass drops by approximately 200 MeV as temperature increases toward T_pc.
- The strange scalar charmed meson mass drops by approximately 100 MeV as temperature increases toward T_pc.
- The mass splittings between non-strange and strange charmed states are around 200 MeV above T_pc.
- The chiral mass splittings between scalar and pseudo-scalar states at T_pc are δM_D(T_pc) ∼ δM_D_s(T_pc) ∼ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory for heavy-light mesons formulated in Sasaki (2015) by incorporating heavy quark symmetry, contrasting with the chiral SU(4) approach (Author, year) where charmed mesons are treated on equal footing with light mesons and yield qualitatively different mass splittings. The results enable using strange charmed meson fluctuations as a probe of the chiral crossover dominated by non-strange light quark dynamics, and suggest similar behavior should hold for B and B_s mesons where heavy quark symmetry is more reliable.
