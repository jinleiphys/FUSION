# Shell Model Study of the Double Beta Decays of $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se and $^{136}$Xe

> The lifetimes for the double beta decays of $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se and $^{136}$Xe are calculated using very large shell model spaces. The two neutrino matrix elements obtained are in good agreement with the present experimental data. For $<m_ν><1$ eV we predict the following upper bounds to the half-lives for the neutrinoless mode: $T^{(0ν)}_{1/2}(Ge) > 1.85\,10^{25} yr.$, $T^{(0ν)}_{1/2}(Se) > 2.36\,10^{24} yr.$ and $T^{(0ν)}_{1/2}(Xe) > 1.21\,10^{25} yr$. These results are the first from a new generation of Shell Model calculations reaching O(10$^{8}$) dimensions.

# Shell Model Study of the Double Beta Decays of $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se and $^{136}$Xe

## Key claim
The paper presents the first large-scale shell model calculations of double beta decay matrix elements for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, and $^{136}$Xe, achieving good agreement with experimental two-neutrino decay data. For the neutrinoless mode, the authors predict lower bounds on half-lives under the condition that the effective neutrino mass is less than 1 eV, but note that the $^{76}$Ge result comes from a truncated (t=4) calculation that is expected to be close to convergence.

## Method
The authors solved the nuclear Schrodinger equation in large valence spaces using the Lanczos algorithm, with valence spaces consisting of the $p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, and $g_{9/2}$ orbitals for A=76,82 and the $d_{5/2}$, $s_{1/2}$, $g_{7/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, and $h_{11/2}$ shells for $^{136}$Xe. They used G-matrix interactions from Kuo and from the Bonn potential, with monopole modifications to reproduce the spectroscopy of Ni isotopes, N=50 isotones, and N=82 isotones. The weak interaction was described using the effective Hamiltonian of Doi et al. (1985), and the two-neutrino matrix elements were computed via an algorithm that provides a reliable approximation to the Gamow-Teller strength in the intermediate nucleus.

## Key numbers
- Two-neutrino matrix element for $^{76}$Ge: 0.14 (compared to SMMC extrapolation of 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV$^{-1}$)
- Two-neutrino matrix element for $^{82}$Se: not explicitly given as a single number in the text, but the paper reports agreement with experimental data
- Two-neutrino matrix element for $^{136}$Xe: nearly constant as valence space is enlarged
- Gamow-Teller sum rule strengths: $S^{(Ge)}_{-} = 17.14$, $S^{(Se)}_{-} = 21.66$, $S^{(Xe)}_{-} = 52.30$ (compared to 3(N-Z) values of 36, 42, and 84 respectively)
- For $<m_{\nu}> < 1$ eV, predicted lower bounds on neutrinoless half-lives: $T^{(0\nu)}_{1/2}(Ge) > 1.85 \times 10^{25}$ yr, $T^{(0\nu)}_{1/2}(Se) > 2.36 \times 10^{24}$ yr, $T^{(0\nu)}_{1/2}(Xe) > 1.21 \times 10^{25}$ yr
- Closure matrix elements for $^{76}$Ge: 0.68 (this work) versus 2.56 (Haxton et al. 1984)
- Closure matrix elements for $^{82}$Se: 0.74 (this work) versus 1.876 (Haxton et al. 1984)

## Context
This work extends the shell model approach beyond the only previous large-scale calculation for $^{48}$Ca (Caurier et al. 1990; Retamosa et al. 1995) to heavier nuclei, using model spaces reaching O($10^8$) dimensions. It contradicts the earlier weak-coupling approximation results of Haxton, Stephenson, and Strottman (1984), finding significantly different closure matrix elements, and addresses the sensitivity issues of the QRPA approach (Vogel 1986; Civitarese et al. 1987; Muto 1988) where the particle-particle interaction strength must be treated as a parameter. The results enable improved constraints on neutrino mass and right-handed current parameters from future neutrinoless double beta decay experiments, and identify $^{82}$Se as a particularly promising candidate due to its minimal ratio of two-neutrino to neutrinoless half-lives.
