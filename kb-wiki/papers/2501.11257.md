# In-medium bottomonium properties from lattice NRQCD calculations with extended meson operators

> We calculate the temperature dependence of bottomonium correlators in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with the aim to constrain in-medium properties of bottomonia at high temperature. The lattice calculations are performed using HISQ action with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses twenty times smaller than the strange quark mass at two lattice spacings $a=0.0493$ fm and $0.0602$ fm, and temporal extents $N_τ=16-30$, corresponding to the temperatures $T=133-250$ MeV. We use a tadpole-improved NRQCD action including spin-dependent $v^6$ corrections for the heavy quarks and extended meson operators in order to be sensitive to in-medium properties of the bottomonium states of interest. We find that within estimated errors the bottomonium masses do not change compared to their vacuum values for all temperatures under our consideration; however, we find different nonzero widths for the various bottomonium states.

## Key claim

The paper finds that within estimated errors, bottomonium masses do not change compared to their vacuum values for all temperatures under consideration (133-250 MeV); however, different nonzero thermal widths are observed for the various bottomonium states, with the caveat that these conclusions depend on the specific ansatz used to model the spectral function and are limited to temperatures where the continuum-subtracted effective masses show a plateau.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action with physical strange quark mass and light quark masses twenty times smaller than the strange quark mass, at two lattice spacings (a=0.0493 fm and 0.0602 fm) and temporal extents Nτ=16-30 corresponding to temperatures T=133-250 MeV. They employed a tadpole-improved NRQCD action including spin-dependent v^6 corrections for the bottom quarks and used both Gaussian-smeared and wave-function-optimized extended meson operators to improve overlap with specific bottomonium states. The in-medium properties were extracted by analyzing continuum-subtracted effective masses and fitting the spectral function with cut Lorentzian and smooth-cut Lorentzian ansatze.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range covered: T=133-250 MeV, using lattice spacings a=0.0493 fm and a=0.0602 fm
- Lattice temporal extents: Nτ=16-30, with spatial extent Nσ=64 for all finite-temperature ensembles
- The Υ(1S) state exists as a well-defined state up to approximately 182 MeV with nearly the same mass as in vacuum
- The χb0(1P) state can exist up to T=167 MeV with the vacuum mass
- Light quark masses set to ms/20, corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of about 160 MeV
- The bare bottom quark mass aMb=0.957(9) for β=7.596 (a=0.0493 fm) and aMb=1.22 for β=7.373 (a=0.0602 fm)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice NRQCD studies of in-medium bottomonium by Larsen et al. (2019) and Larsen et al. (2020), which used only a single lattice spacing and temporal extent Nτ=12 and did not explore temperatures below 199 MeV. The present calculations use larger temporal extents (Nτ=16-30) and two lattice spacings per temperature value, enabling better control over discretization effects and access to lower temperatures. The results are consistent with the absence of screening in the real part of the heavy quark potential up to distances of about 1 fm, as reported by Bala et al. (2021) and Bazavov et al. (2023), while supporting the existence of a sizable imaginary part. The findings enable future comparisons with phenomenological estimates of thermal bottomonium widths, which were found to be larger than most previous phenomenological estimates as noted by Andronic et al. (2024).
