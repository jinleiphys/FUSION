# Search for a mixed phase of strongly interacting matter at the JINR Nuclotron

> 10 pages, 8 figures, LaTeX, Contribution to the Proceedings of the 8th International Workshop "Relativistic Nuclear Physics: from hundreds MeV to TeV", JINR, Dubna, May 23 - 28, 2005

## Key claim

The JINR Nuclotron, accelerating heavy ions up to a maximal planned energy of 5 AGeV, can access a region of the QCD phase diagram near the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration boundary, potentially revealing precursory phenomena of a mixed phase even though the system enters the mixed phase only for a short time and no irregular structure like that seen at 30 AGeV is expected.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical trajectory calculations for central Au+Au collisions using relativistic 3-fluid hydrodynamics, with phase boundaries estimated in a two-phase bag model. They compared these trajectories to the QCD phase diagram in temperature-baryon density and temperature-chemical potential planes, and surveyed existing experimental results on elliptic flow, dilepton spectra, pion pair production, and strangeness enhancement to identify observables sensitive to a mixed phase at Nuclotron energies.

## Key numbers

- Maximal planned Nuclotron energy for heavy ions: \(E_{lab}=5\) AGeV.
- The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) for protons changes sign near \(E_{lab}\sim 5\) AGeV, evolving from out-of-plane to in-plane emission; at \(E_{lab}=2\) AGeV the slope is negative, at 6 AGeV positive, and near zero at 4 AGeV.
- The \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio at \(E_{lab}=20\) AGeV is approximately 0.22, contributing to the "horn" structure near \(E_{lab}\sim 30\) AGeV.
- In-medium modification of \(\rho\)-mesons was observed in CERES experiments for central Pb+Au collisions at 40 AGeV, with enhanced low-mass lepton pairs in the range \(0.2 \lesssim M \lesssim 0.7\) GeV.
- The reduced pion multiplicity \(<n_\pi>/A_{part}\) for nucleus-nucleus collisions equals the elementary NN value near the "horn" energy, with this proximity starting at the Nuclotron energy.

## Context

This paper extends the low-energy scanning program at SPS (NA49 Collaboration, 2005) which found a structure in energy dependence of observables at \(E_{lab}\sim 20-30\) AGeV, and positions the Nuclotron as a precursor to the FAIR GSI project (planned for 2013) for studying compressed baryonic matter at \(E_{lab}=10-30\) AGeV. The work enables a pilot experimental program at the Nuclotron to search for signals of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration, including in-medium hadron modification, elliptic flow changes, and strangeness enhancement, before the higher-energy FAIR facility becomes operational.
