# Relation Between Two Approaches of the Colour Hydrodynamics

> It is argued that the short time scale phenomena can be studied within the framework of hydrodynamics in the quark-gluon plasma. There are two different versions of the hydrodynamic-like equations in the literature. In this work we discuss the possible relationship between these versions. In particular we show that if the colour charges associated with the velocity and density matrices in the matrix version of hydrodynamics are same then both the versions of the hydrodynamics become identical.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two existing versions of colour hydrodynamics (CHD) for quark-gluon plasma become identical if the colour charges associated with the density matrix and the velocity matrix in the matrix formulation are set equal at some initial time, under the simplifying assumption of a cold plasma with zero pressure.

## Method

The authors compare the CHD equations of Kajantie et al. (1980s), where all hydrodynamic variables except the colour charge are colour scalars, with the matrix formulation of Manuel and Mrówczyński (2006), where number density, velocity, energy density, and pressure are all \(N \times N\) matrices for \(SU(N)\). For the \(SU(2)\) group in a cold plasma (\(p=0\), \(\epsilon = m n\)), they decompose the matrix variables \(n_\alpha\) and \(u_\alpha^\mu\) into scalar parts and colour-covariant parts, impose conditions that the colour parts \(I_\alpha\) and \(Q_\alpha\) obey the same dynamical equation as the colour charge in the scalar version, and then show that if \(I_\alpha(t_0) = Q_\alpha(t_0)\) at some initial time, the two formulations become equivalent.

## Key numbers

* The paper works with the \(SU(2)\) colour group and a cold plasma assumption (\(p_\alpha = 0\), \(\epsilon = m_\alpha n_\alpha\)); no specific numerical values for energies, densities, or cross-sections are reported.
* The constant \(\mathcal{N}\) under the condition \(I_\alpha(t) = Q_\alpha(t)\) is given as \(\mathcal{N} = 4 (Q_{\alpha a}^2)^{3/2}\), but no numerical value is assigned.
* Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This work extends the earlier chromo-hydrodynamic (CHD) equations derived by Kajantie et al. (1980s) and the matrix formulation derived from kinetic theory by Manuel and Mrówczyński (2006). It clarifies the relationship between these two approaches, which had been used separately to study collective phenomena such as two-stream instability and thermalization in non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma. The result enables researchers to choose between the two formulations based on the physical problem, and it opens the way to incorporate pressure gradients and extend the equivalence to the gluonic sector using the adjoint representation.
