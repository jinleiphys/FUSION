# Evidence of N*(1535) resonance contribution in the pn->dϕreaction

> The N*(1535) resonance contributions to the pn->dϕreaction are evaluated in an effective Lagrangian model. The π-, η-, and ρ-meson exchange are considered. It is shown that the contributions from π- and ρ-meson exchange are dominant, while the contribution from η-meson exchange is negligibly small. Our theoretical results reproduce the experimental data of both total cross section and angular distribution well. This is another evidence that the N*(1535) resonance has large s\bar{s} component leading to a large coupling to Nϕ, which may be the real origin of the OZI rule violation in the \piN and pN reactions.

# Evidence of N*(1535) resonance contribution in the pn → dφ reaction

## Key claim

The N*(1535) resonance contribution is necessary to reproduce the measured total cross section and angular distribution of the pn → dφ reaction near threshold, providing evidence that this resonance has a large s\bar{s} component leading to a large coupling to Nφ. However, the authors admit that contributions from mesonic and nucleonic currents cannot be definitely excluded, as an alternative combination of those currents with the N*(1535) resonance could also yield a good fit to the present data.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian model with π-, η-, and ρ-meson exchange mechanisms, assuming the reaction proceeds through excitation and decay of the sub-φN-threshold N*(1535) resonance with the final nucleons merging to form the deuteron. The model uses coupling constants determined from experimental partial decay widths of N*(1535) and from previous analysis of pp → ppφ and π⁻p → nφ data, with the deuteron described by the Reid soft core wave function and the N*(1535) propagator including an energy-dependent total width.

## Key numbers

- The ratio σ(pn → dφ)/σ(pn → dω) = (4.0 ± 1.9) × 10⁻² ≈ 9 × R_OZI, where R_OZI ≈ 4.2 × 10⁻³ is the expected OZI rule ratio
- The coupling constant for N*(1535)Nφ is g²_φNN*/4π = 0.13
- The coupling constants for N*(1535) decay channels: g²/4π = 0.033 (Nπ), 0.28 (Nη), 0.10 (Nρ)
- The ρ-meson exchange contribution is larger than π-meson exchange by a factor of 2 in the pn → dφ reaction
- The η-meson exchange contribution is about three orders of magnitude smaller than that of ρ-meson exchange
- Cutoff parameters: Λ_π = Λ_η = 1.3 GeV, Λ_ρ = 1.6 GeV, Λ*_π = Λ*_η = Λ*_ρ = 1.3 GeV
- The N*(1535) resonance form factor uses Λ = 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the model of Xie et al. (2008) from pp → ppφ and π⁻p → nφ reactions to the pn → dφ reaction without introducing new parameters. It contrasts with the meson exchange model of Nakayama et al. (2006), which required an OZI-violating φρπ vertex and could not simultaneously reproduce total cross section and angular distribution data from the COSY-ANKE Collaboration (2006). The results also differ from the two-step model of Grishina et al. (2004) and the bremsstrahlung/conversion model of Kaptari et al. (2006). The findings suggest that the N*(1535) resonance with large s\bar{s} component may be the real origin of OZI rule violation in πN and pN reactions, and this can be further investigated in other reactions such as pd → ³Heφ, γp → φp, and πN → φN.
