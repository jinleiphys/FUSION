# Hydrodynamics without a relaxation gap: memory effects, nonlocality, and superdiffusion

> By studying a simple model of relativistic particles propagating through a background medium with an energy-dependent relaxation time that is unbounded from above, we investigate how long-term memory obstructs the emergence of local hydrodynamics in systems with a gapless non-hydrodynamic sector. In the RTA matching frame, we show that the full gradient expansion is generically divergent in most flows, even for Fourier modes, and that any resummation necessarily retains an infinite set of slow non-hydrodynamic degrees of freedom. The divergence of the gradient expansion therefore reflects a more fundamental breakdown of hydrodynamic locality caused by persistent non-hydrodynamic memory. We finally show that sufficiently singular relaxation spectra can invalidate ordinary diffusion itself. In these regimes, the diffusivity diverges, and the late-time dynamics become superdiffusive, governed by intrinsically nonlocal constitutive relations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the relaxation time of non-conserved degrees of freedom is unbounded from above (a gapless non-hydrodynamic sector), the gradient expansion of the constitutive relation is generically divergent for most flows, including Fourier modes, and that any resummation necessarily retains an infinite set of slow non-hydrodynamic degrees of freedom, reflecting a fundamental breakdown of hydrodynamic locality.

## Method

The authors study a linearized kinetic model of massless relativistic particles propagating through a background medium, using the Boltzmann equation in the Relaxation-Time Approximation (RTA) with an energy-dependent relaxation time tau_epsilon. They work in the RTA matching frame, where the fugacity field phi is determined by the matching condition that particle-number current is conserved, and derive the formal gradient expansion for the current J^mu in terms of phi. The convergence of this expansion is analyzed for plane-wave perturbations with a power-law relaxation spectrum tau_epsilon = g * epsilon^gamma, and the spectral properties of the linearized Boltzmann operator are examined in the L^2 Hilbert space of finite-entropy perturbations.

## Key numbers

* For tau_epsilon = g * epsilon^gamma with gamma > 0, the factor Gamma(3 + n*gamma) in the expansion coefficients grows super-exponentially with n, causing the gradient expansion to diverge for all wavevectors.
* For gamma = 0 (gapped case), the series reduces to geometric series with common ratios gik(omega/k + 1) and gik(omega/k - 1); convergence requires |gk| < 1, equivalent to the Knudsen number condition tau/L < 1.
* For gamma < 0, momentum integrals with n*gamma <= -3 are infrared-divergent due to a low-energy singularity, causing the series to diverge.
* The essential spectrum of the linearized Boltzmann operator is given by the continuous band i*omega in the union over all momenta of (1/tau_epsilon + i*k*v^1).

## Context

This work extends recent studies of kinetic theories with energy-dependent relaxation times that emphasized the non-analytic structure of retarded correlation functions (Brants 2024). The analysis is complementary to that work, reformulating the same obstruction as the impossibility of reducing the dynamics to local constitutive relations for conserved variables alone. The paper builds on prior observations about gapless non-hydrodynamic spectra (Gavassino 2024) and divergent gradient expansions in Bjorken-type flows (Heller 2015, 2021), showing that the divergence occurs even for Fourier modes when the relaxation spectrum is gapless. The results enable a deeper understanding of when hydrodynamics can be formulated as a local effective theory and when intrinsically nonlocal constitutive relations are required.
