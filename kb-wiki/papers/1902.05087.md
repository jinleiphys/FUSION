# Diffractive Dijet Production and Wigner Distributions from the Color Glass Condensate

> Experimental processes that are sensitive to parton Wigner distributions provide a powerful tool to advance our understanding of proton structure. In this work, we compute gluon Wigner and Husimi distributions of protons within the Color Glass Condensate framework, which includes a spatially dependent McLerran-Venugopalan initial configuration and the explicit numerical solution of the JIMWLK equations. We determine the leading anisotropy of the Wigner and Husimi distributions as a function of the angle between impact parameter and transverse momentum. We study experimental signatures of these angular correlations at a proposed Electron Ion Collider by computing coherent diffractive dijet production cross sections in e+p collisions within the same framework. Specifically, we predict the elliptic modulation of the cross section as a function of the relative angle between nucleon recoil and dijet transverse momentum for a wide kinematical range. We further predict its dependence on collision energy, which is dominated by the growth of the proton with decreasing $x$.

## Key claim

The paper computes gluon Wigner and Husimi distributions of protons within the Color Glass Condensate framework and predicts elliptic modulations in coherent diffractive dijet production cross sections at a future Electron Ion Collider, with the hedge that these predictions depend on the specific choices of infrared regulators and the initial condition at \(x_\mathbb{P}=0.01\) from the IP-Sat model.

## Method

The authors compute the dipole amplitude \(\mathcal{N}(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{b},x_\mathbb{P})\) from the Color Glass Condensate effective theory by solving classical Yang-Mills equations with a spatially dependent McLerran-Venugopalan initial configuration and then evolving the Wilson lines via the JIMWLK renormalization group equations at leading logarithmic order, implemented as a Langevin equation. They then use this dipole amplitude to compute the gluon Wigner distribution via a Fourier transform of the generalized transverse momentum dependent dipole gluon distribution, and calculate coherent diffractive dijet production cross sections in \(e+p\) collisions for transversely and longitudinally polarized virtual photons in the dipole picture.

## Key numbers

* The elliptic anisotropy coefficient \(v_2\) of the dipole amplitude is computed over 3 units of rapidity evolution (from \(y=0\) to \(y=3\)), showing suppression with increasing energy for various impact parameter values \(|\mathbf{b}|\).
* The initial condition for JIMWLK evolution is set at \(x_\mathbb{P}=0.01\), with fixed \(\alpha_s=0.21\) and infrared regulators \(\tilde{m}=0.4\) GeV and \(m=0.2\) GeV.
* The running coupling constant is evaluated using \(\mu_0=0.28\) GeV and \(\chi=0.2\) in Eq. (18), with \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) implicit.
* The IR cutoff \(\tilde{m}\) is varied in the range \(0.2-0.4\) GeV to regulate non-perturbative Coulomb tails.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hatta (2016) for accessing gluon Wigner distributions via diffractive dijet production, and provides the first full CGC computation including JIMWLK evolution for the energy dependence of these distributions. It enables experimental tests at a future Electron Ion Collider by predicting the elliptic modulation \(v_2\) of the dijet cross section as a function of the relative angle between nucleon recoil and dijet transverse momentum, and its dependence on collision energy, photon virtuality, and dijet kinematics. The IP-Sat model of Kowalski (2003) is used as a baseline comparison, but is shown to lack the intrinsic \(\mathbf{r}\cdot\mathbf{b}\) correlations necessary for such angular modulations.
