# Scaling Functions and Superscaling in Medium and Heavy Nuclei

> The scaling function $f(ψ')$ for medium and heavy nuclei with $Z\neq N$ for which the proton and neutron densities are not similar is constructed within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) as a sum of the proton and neutron scaling functions. The latter are calculated in the cases of $^{62}$Ni, $^{82}$Kr, $^{118}$Sn, and $^{197}$Au nuclei on the basis of the corresponding proton and neutron density distributions which are obtained in deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS method. The results are in a reasonable agreement with the empirical data from the inclusive electron scattering from nuclei showing superscaling for negative values of $ψ'$, including those smaller than -1. This is an improvement over the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model predictions where $f(ψ')$ becomes abruptly zero for $ψ'\leq -1$. It is also an improvement over the CDFM calculations made in the past for nuclei with $Z\neq N$ assuming that the neutron density is equal to the proton one and using only the phenomenological charge density.

## Key claim

The scaling function \(f(\psi')\) for nuclei with unequal proton and neutron numbers (\(Z \neq N\)) must be constructed as a sum of separate proton and neutron scaling functions, rather than assuming equal densities; this approach yields reasonable agreement with experimental superscaling data for negative \(\psi'\) values (including \(\psi' < -1\)) without any adjustable parameters, improving upon earlier CDFM calculations that used only the phenomenological charge density and assumed neutron density equals proton density.

## Method

The authors used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to compute separate proton and neutron scaling functions \(f_p(\psi')\) and \(f_n(\psi')\) from the corresponding proton and neutron density distributions. These densities were obtained from deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS calculations (using the SG2 interaction with 11 major harmonic-oscillator shells) for the nuclei \({}^{62}\)Ni, \({}^{82}\)Kr, \({}^{118}\)Sn, and \({}^{197}\)Au. The total scaling function was then formed as the weighted sum \(f(\psi') = (Z f_p(\psi') + N f_n(\psi'))/A\) and compared to experimental inclusive electron scattering data at momentum transfer \(q = 1000\) MeV/c.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{197}\)Au, the earlier CDFM calculation (Antonov 2004) using only the Fermi-type charge density with diffuseness parameter \(b = 0.449\) fm and radius \(R = 6.419\) fm failed to reproduce the data; the present work using separate proton and neutron densities achieves reasonable agreement.
* The scaling functions were calculated for momentum transfer \(q = 1000\) MeV/c for all four nuclei (\({}^{62}\)Ni, \({}^{82}\)Kr, \({}^{118}\)Sn, \({}^{197}\)Au) and compared to experimental data from Donnelly (1999) covering nuclei from \({}^{4}\)He to \({}^{197}\)Au.
* The Fermi momentum for each nucleus is calculated within CDFM using the expression \(k_F = \frac{4\pi(9\pi)^{1/3}}{3A^{2/3}} \int_0^\infty dR \rho(R) R\) (Eq. 8), with no free fitting parameter.

## Context

This work extends the CDFM approach of Antonov (2004, 2005) which previously assumed neutron density equals proton density and used only phenomenological charge densities, leading to poor results for heavy nuclei like \({}^{197}\)Au. It improves upon the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model (Alberico 1988, Donnelly 1999) which predicts \(f(\psi') = 0\) for \(\psi' \leq -1\), whereas experimental data show superscaling extending to \(\psi' \approx -2\). The results enable future studies of superscaling in asymmetric nuclei using realistic proton and neutron density distributions from either theoretical calculations or experimental measurements.
