# Quenching parameter in a holographic thermal QCD

> We have calculated the quenching parameter, $\hat{q}$ in a model-independent way using the gauge-gravity duality. In earlier calculations, the geometry in the gravity side at finite temperature was usually taken as the pure AdS blackhole metric for which the dual gauge theory becomes conformally invariant unlike QCD. Therefore we use a metric which incorporates the fundamental quarks by embedding the coincident D7 branes in the Klebanov-Tseytlin background and a finite temperature is switched on by inserting a black hole into the background, known as OKS-BH metric. Further inclusion of an additional UV cap to the metric prepares the dual gauge theory to run similar to thermal QCD. Moreover $\hat{q}$ is usually defined in the literature from the Glauber-model perturbative QCD evaluation of the Wilson loop, which has no reasons to hold if the coupling is large and is thus against the main idea of gauge-gravity duality. Thus we use an appropriate definition of $\hat{q}$: $\hat{q} L^- = 1/L^2$, where $L$ is the separation for which the Wilson loop is equal to some specific value. The above two refinements cause $\hat{q}$ to vary with the temperature as $T^4$ always and to depend linearly on the light-cone time $L^-$ with an additional ($1/L^-$) correction term in the short-distance limit whereas in the long-distance limit, it depends only linearly on $L^-$ with no correction term. These observations agree with other holographic calculations directly or indirectly.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) using a holographic metric (OKS-BH with a UV cap) that is dual to a gauge theory with QCD-like running, and they adopt a non-perturbative definition of \(\hat{q}\) based on the saturation scale rather than the standard Glauber-model perturbative QCD definition; the resulting \(\hat{q}\) always scales as \(T^4\) and depends linearly on the light-cone time \(L^-\) with an additional \(1/L^-\) correction in the short-distance limit.

## Method

The authors use the gauge-gravity duality with the OKS-BH metric, which embeds coincident D7 branes in a Klebanov-Tseytlin background with a black hole and an additional UV cap, to model a thermal QCD-like gauge theory. They compute the Nambu-Goto action for a rectangular Wilson loop in light-cone coordinates, renormalize it by subtracting the action of two disconnected world sheets, and extract \(\hat{q}\) from the definition \(\hat{q} L^- = 1/L^2\), where \(L\) is the quark-antiquark separation at which the real part of the Wilson loop equals a constant (chosen as \(C = 1/2\)). The calculation is performed separately in the short-distance (\(z_m \ll z_h\)) and long-distance (\(z_m \gg z_h\)) limits.

## Key numbers

* In the short-distance limit, the quenching parameter is \(\hat{q} = \frac{2L^-}{\pi^2 \alpha'^2 z_h^4}\left[\frac{3}{4} - \frac{L^-}{2\sqrt{2}\pi \alpha' z_h^2} - \frac{A \pi^2 \alpha'^2 z_h^4}{12 L^{-2}}\right]\), where \(z_h\) is the black hole horizon (related to temperature), \(\alpha'\) is the string tension, and \(A\) is a coefficient from the metric corrections.
* In the long-distance limit, the renormalized action is \(S_I = -\frac{L^-}{\sqrt{2}\pi \alpha' z_h^2}\left[2.44 z_h + 0.5 B z_m z_h^2 + i(-0.16 z_h + 0.5(B+2A)z_m z_h^2)\right]\), where \(B\) is another metric coefficient.
* The temperature dependence of \(\hat{q}\) is always \(T^4\) (since \(z_h \propto 1/T\)).
* The extracted values of \(\hat{q}\) from the JET collaboration (cited as Bruke:PRC90'2014) range from 1 to 25 GeV\(^2\)/fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier holographic calculations of \(\hat{q}\) that used the pure AdS black hole metric (e.g., Liu:PRL97'2006), which is dual to a conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory unlike QCD. By using the OKS-BH metric with a UV cap, the authors incorporate fundamental quarks and a QCD-like running coupling, addressing the lack of conformal invariance. The new definition of \(\hat{q}\) via the saturation scale avoids the perturbative Glauber-model assumption, which is invalid at strong coupling. This enables more realistic holographic estimates of jet quenching in strongly coupled QGP, and the results agree qualitatively with other holographic calculations.
