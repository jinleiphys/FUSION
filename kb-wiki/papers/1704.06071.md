# Existence problem of proton semi-bubble structure in the $2_1^+$ state of $^{34}$Si

> The fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) based on Skyrme-type interaction is used to study the existence problem of proton semi-bubble structure in the $2_1^+$ state of $^{34}$Si. The experimental excitation energy and the B(E2) strength of the $2_1^+$ state in $^{34}$Si can be reproduced quite well. The tensor effect is also studied. It is shown that the tensor interaction has a notable impact on the excitation energy of the $2_1^+$ state and a small effect on the B(E2) value. Besides, its effect on the density distributions in the ground and $2_1^+$ state of $^{34}$Si is negligible. Our present results with T36 and T44 show that the $2_1^+$ state of $^{34}$Si is mainly caused by proton transiton from $π1d_{5/2}$ orbit to $π2s_{1/2}$ orbit, and the existence of a proton semi-bubble structure in this state is very unlikely.

## Key claim

The fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) with Skyrme interactions T36 and T44 reproduces the experimental excitation energy and B(E2) strength of the 2_1^+ state in 34Si, and shows that a proton semi-bubble structure in this state is very unlikely because the transition is dominated by a proton from the 1d_5/2 orbit to the 2s_1/2 orbit, which fills the central density.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock equations in coordinate space with a radial mesh up to 20 fm and a step of 0.1 fm, using a cutoff of single-particle energy at 80 MeV in the RPA calculation. They employed the Skyrme-type interactions T36 and T44, manually switching the tensor terms on and off in both HF and RPA to isolate tensor effects. The density distribution of the 2_1^+ state was computed within the quasiboson approximation, and a depletion factor was defined to quantify the bubble structure.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the 2_1^+ state in 34Si: T36(w/o tensor) = 4.142 MeV, T36(w/ tensor) = 3.299 MeV, T44(w/o tensor) = 3.643 MeV, T44(w/ tensor) = 3.429 MeV; experimental value = 3.326 MeV.
- B(E2;0_1^+ -> 2_1^+) strength: T36(w/o tensor) = 109.5 e^2 fm^4, T36(w/ tensor) = 104.0 e^2 fm^4, T44(w/o tensor) = 104.8 e^2 fm^4, T44(w/ tensor) = 99.3 e^2 fm^4; experimental value = 85(33) e^2 fm^4.
- Depletion factor for the ground state: 31.2% (T36) and 26.2% (T44); for the 2_1^+ state: 0.4% (T36) and 0% (T44).
- Amplitude (X^2 - Y^2) of the dominant pi 2s_1/2 x pi 1d_5/2 configuration: 0.965 (T36 w/o), 0.976 (T36 w/), 0.967 (T44 w/o), 0.963 (T44 w/).
- Weight factor for B(E2) of the dominant configuration: 0.746 (T36 w/o), 0.766 (T36 w/), 0.757 (T44 w/o), 0.757 (T44 w/).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical predictions of a proton semi-bubble structure in the ground state of 34Si from mean-field approaches (Grasso 2009, Nakada 2013) and ab initio methods (Duguet 2017), as well as the experimental evidence from one-proton removal reactions (Mutschler 2017). The authors apply HF+RPA, a method not previously used for this problem, and find that the 2_1^+ state does not support a semi-bubble, consistent with earlier generator coordinate method calculations (Yao 2013). The results enable future systematic searches for bubble structures in excited states of other candidate nuclei using more advanced methods such as projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov plus projected quasiparticle RPA.
