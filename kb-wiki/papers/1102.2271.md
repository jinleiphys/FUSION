# Chirality in odd-$A$ Rh isotopes within triaxial particle rotor model

> Adopting the fully quantal triaxial particle rotor model, the candidate chiral doublet bands in odd-$A$ nuclei $^{103}$Rh and $^{105}$Rh with $πg_{9/2}^{-1}\otimesνh^{2}_{11/2}$ configuration are studied. For the doublet bands in both nuclei, agreement is excellent for the observed energies over entire spin range and $B(M1)/B(E2)$ at higher spin range. The evolution of the chiral geometry with angular momentum is discussed in detail by the angular momentum components and their probability distributions. Chirality is found to change from chiral vibration to nearly static chirality at spin $I=37/2$ and back to another type of chiral vibration at higher spin. The influence of the triaxial deformation $γ$ is also studied.

## Key claim

Using a fully quantal triaxial particle rotor model with a 2-particle-1-hole configuration, the candidate chiral doublet bands in odd-A nuclei 103Rh and 105Rh are reproduced with excellent agreement for energies over the entire spin range and for B(M1)/B(E2) ratios at higher spin, but the chirality is found to change from chiral vibration to nearly static chirality at spin I=37/2 and back to another type of chiral vibration at higher spin, rather than being a simple static chirality.

## Method

The triaxial particle rotor model couples a triaxial rotor with one valence proton hole (g9/2) and two valence neutrons (h11/2) in a fully quantal framework. The total Hamiltonian combines a collective rotor Hamiltonian with moments of inertia for irrotational flow and an intrinsic Hamiltonian for valence nucleons using single-particle energies from a triaxial deformed single-j shell Hamiltonian. The total wave function is expanded in a strong coupling basis symmetrized under the D2 point group, and reduced transition probabilities B(M1) and B(E2) are obtained from the wave function.

## Key numbers

- For 103Rh: deformation parameters beta=0.235 and gamma=20.6 degrees from self-consistent triaxial relativistic mean field calculations
- For 105Rh: deformation parameters beta=0.228 and gamma=21.1 degrees
- Single-particle Hamiltonian parameters: Cp=-0.43 MeV and Cn=0.35 MeV for 103Rh; Cp=-0.42 MeV and Cn=0.34 MeV for 105Rh
- Moment of inertia J0=21.0 MeV/hbar^2 adjusted to experimental energy spectra
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0=2.5 eb, gyromagnetic ratios gR=0.44, gp(g9/2)=1.26, gn(h11/2)=-0.21
- For 103Rh: experimental energy spectra reproduced within 150 keV accuracy; least energy separation between doublet bands at I=37/2 for both experiment and theory
- For 105Rh: calculated results agree with data within 100 keV for spin I<41/2; best degeneracy spin is 41/2 for data but 39/2 for PRM calculation
- Energy differences at spin 37/2 for varying gamma: 946 keV at gamma=14 degrees, 693 keV at 16 degrees, 475 keV at 18 degrees, 261 keV at 20 degrees, 175 keV at 22 degrees, 79 keV at 24 degrees, 21 keV at 26 degrees, 30 keV at 28 degrees, 34 keV at 30 degrees

## Context

This work extends the triaxial particle rotor model approach previously applied to odd-odd nuclei like 106Rh (Wang 2008) and to the odd-A nucleus 135Nd (Qi 2009) to the Rh isotope chain, specifically addressing the lack of quantitative comparison for doublet band separations in 103Rh (Timar 2006) and 105Rh (Timar 2004). The study enables detailed investigation of the chiral quartet of neighboring nuclei 102,103,104,105Rh and tests the validity of theoretical approaches for understanding the influence of valence nucleon configurations on nuclear chirality, while also suggesting possible multiple chiral bands (MchiD) in 105Rh as predicted by Meng (2006).
