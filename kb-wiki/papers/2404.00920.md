# Farside-dominant quasinuclear rainbow in refractive $α$+$α$ scattering

> $α$+$α$ scattering has a long history since the first experiment by Rutherford and Chadwick in 1927 and has been studied thoroughly experimentally and theoretically. However, $α$+$α$ scattering has never been paid attention from the viewpoint of refractive scattering. I have successfully analyzed the experimental angular distributions in $α$+$α$ scattering systematically over a wide range of incident energies $E_L$=53.4 - 280 MeV using a phenomenological optical model with a deep real potential. The existence of a farside-dominant quasinuclear rainbow with no well-defined rainbow angle and no supernumerary bow in the lit side followed by the shadow, which is not a genuine rainbow but a refractive scattering from a marginally small droplet at high energies, is found for the first time in $α$+$α$ scattering. The refraction due to the deep potentials with an attractive core at short distances are discussed from the viewpoint of the Luneburg. The deep vs shallow problem of the potential and the nuclear rainbow scattering in inelastic channels are also discussed.

# Farside-dominant quasinuclear rainbow in refractive α+α scattering

## Key claim

The paper claims that α+α scattering at high energies (E_L = 100–280 MeV) exhibits a farside-dominant quasinuclear rainbow, a refractive scattering pattern from a marginally small droplet, which is *not* a genuine nuclear rainbow because it lacks a well-defined rainbow angle and shows no supernumerary Airy structure in the lit side, only a falloff (shadow) region.

## Method

The author systematically analyzed experimental angular distributions in α+α scattering at incident energies E_L = 53.4–280 MeV using a phenomenological optical model with a two-range Woods-Saxon real potential (V_1 for the outer region and V_2 for the inner region) plus an imaginary Woods-Saxon potential. The calculated angular distributions were decomposed into farside and nearside components using the Fuller prescription, and the deflection function Θ(L) was computed from the phase shifts to understand the semiclassical scattering mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range analyzed: E_L = 53.4–280 MeV (center-of-mass energies 26.7–140 MeV)
- Volume integrals per nucleon pair J_V range: 347–494 MeV·fm³ across the eight energies studied
- At 120 MeV, cross sections in the falloff decrease by almost four orders of magnitude
- At 280 MeV, partial waves L ≤ 30 are involved in refractive scattering, but boson symmetrization reduces the number of physically relevant partial waves by half
- Modulus of the S-matrix for L=0 at E_L = 140 MeV (E_cm = 70 MeV): S_L = 0.4, compared with S_L = 0.06–0.08 for α+¹⁶O scattering at similar energies
- Potential parameters at 140 MeV: V_1 = 53.38 MeV, r_1 = 1.628 fm, a_1 = 0.613 fm; V_2 = 43.92 MeV, r_2 = 0.545 fm, a_2 = 0.142 fm; W_0 = 7.48 MeV, r_W = 2.188 fm, a_W = 0.618 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear rainbow scattering, previously observed in systems such as α+¹⁶O (Michel 1983), α+⁴⁰Ca (Delbar 1978), and ¹⁶O+¹⁶O (Stiliaris 1989, Nicoli 1999, Khoa 2000), to the α+α system, which had never been examined from a refractive scattering perspective despite decades of study since Rutherford and Chadwick (1927). The finding that α+α scattering produces a quasinuclear rainbow rather than a genuine nuclear rainbow is attributed to the small size of the α+α system, which acts as a marginally small droplet, and to the bosonic statistics of the two α particles. The paper proposes experimental searches for genuine nuclear rainbows in inelastic α+⁴He(0₂⁺) scattering, where odd-parity partial waves (forbidden in elastic scattering by Bose statistics) could produce Airy structure.
