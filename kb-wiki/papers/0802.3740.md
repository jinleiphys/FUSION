# Structure of A=138 isobars above the 132Sn core and the N-N interaction in the neutron-rich environment

> Large basis untruncated shell model calculations have been done for the A=138 neutron -rich nuclei in the pi(gdsh) X nu(hfpi) valence space above the 132Sn core. Two (1+2) -body nuclear Hamiltonians, viz., realistic CWG and empirical SMPN in this model space have been used. Calculated ground state binding energies, level spectra and other spectroscopic properties have been compared with the available experimental data. Importance of untruncated shell model calculations in this model space has been pointed out. Shell model results for the very neutron rich Sn isotope (138Sn, N/Z=1.76) of astrophysical interest for which no spectroscopic information except beta -decay half life is available, have been presented. Shell structure and evolution of collectivity in the even-even A=138 isobars have been studied as a function of valence neutron and /or proton numbers. Calculations done for the first time, reproduce remarkably well the collective vibrational states in 138Te and 138Xe. Comparison of some of the important two-body matrix elements of the empirical SMPN, CW5082 and the realistic CWG interactions has been done. These matrix elements are important for ground state binding energies and low-lying spectra of nuclei in this region. Consideration of the predictability of the two interactions seems to suggest that, in order to incorporate the special features of the N-N interaction in such exotic n-rich environment above the 132Sn core, the use of local spectroscopic information from the region might be essential.

## Key claim

Large-basis untruncated shell model calculations in the $\pi(gdsh) \oplus \nu(hfpi)$ valence space above $^{132}Sn$ show that the empirical SMPN Hamiltonian reproduces experimental data for A=138 isobars better than the realistic CWG interaction, but the authors suggest that local spectroscopic information from the neutron-rich region may be essential to fully incorporate the special features of the N-N interaction in this exotic environment.

## Method

Untruncated shell model calculations were performed for A=138 neutron-rich nuclei ($^{138}Sn$, $^{138}Te$, $^{138}Xe$, $^{138}Ba$) in the $\pi(1g_{7/2}, 2d_{5/2}, 2d_{3/2}, 3s_{1/2}, 1h_{11/2}) \oplus \nu(1h_{9/2}, 2f_{7/2}, 2f_{5/2}, 3p_{3/2}, 3p_{1/2}, 1i_{13/2})$ valence space above the $^{132}Sn$ core using the OXBASH code. Two (1+2)-body nuclear Hamiltonians were employed: the realistic CWG interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential and the empirical SMPN interaction. Ground state binding energies, excitation spectra, wavefunction compositions, and B(E2) transition probabilities were calculated and compared with available experimental data and with systematics of neighboring nuclei.

## Key numbers

- $^{138}Sn$: SMPN predicts $E(2^+_1) = 0.433$ MeV, $E(4^+_1) = 0.738$ MeV, $E(6^+_1) = 0.890$ MeV; CWG predicts $E(2^+_1) = 0.761$ MeV, with the $2^+_1$ state dominated by 73% $\nu 2f_{7/2}^6$ configuration (SMPN) versus 24% (CWG)
- $^{138}Sn$: B(E2, $0^+_{gs} \rightarrow 2^+_1$) = 344.7 $e^2fm^4$ (1.63 W.u.) with SMPN, 916.8 $e^2fm^4$ (4.33 W.u.) with CWG, using neutron effective charge 0.64$e$
- $^{138}Te$: SMPN binding energy = -37.303 MeV, CWG = -37.692 MeV, experimental = -36.24(21) MeV
- $^{138}Xe$: SMPN binding energy = -49.011 MeV, CWG = -51.906 MeV, experimental = -48.89(4) MeV
- $^{138}Ba$: SMPN binding energy = -55.266 MeV, CWG = -62.096 MeV, experimental = -55.44(1) MeV
- $^{138}Cs$: SMPN correctly predicts $3^-$ ground state with binding energy -50.867 MeV, matching experimental -50.849(17) MeV; CWG predicts $2^-$ ground state at -55.503 MeV
- B(E2, $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_{gs}$) for $^{138}Ba$: SMPN = 530.6 $e^2fm^4$ (12.5 W.u.), CWG = 504.4 $e^2fm^4$ (11.9 W.u.), experimental = 460(18) $e^2fm^4$
- $R_4 = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ for $^{138}Sn$: 1.70 (SMPN), 1.78 (CWG)
- Matrix dimensions for $^{138}Te$ $6^+$ state: 179,054 (largest among A=138 isobars)

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of neutron-rich nuclei above $^{132}Sn$ by Sarkar and Saha Sarkar (2006-2007) using the empirical SMPN interaction, and by Brown et al. (2005) using the realistic CWG interaction. The calculations address the anomalous low B(E2) value in $^{136}Te$ observed by Radford et al. (2002), which Terasaki et al. (2002) attributed to reduced neutron pairing above N=82. The results enable future studies of r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly for the waiting-point nucleus $^{138}Sn$ (N/Z=1.76) for which no spectroscopic information except beta-decay half-life is available, and provide a benchmark for testing realistic interactions against empirical ones in neutron-rich environments.
