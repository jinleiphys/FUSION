# On the pole trajectory of the subthreshold negative parity nucleon with varying pion masses

> We study the pole trajectory of the recently established subthreshold negative parity nucleon pole, namely the $N^*(920)$, with varying pion masses, in the scheme of linear $σ$ model with nucleons using the $N/D$ unitarization method. We find that as the pion mass increases, the pole moves toward the real axis. For larger pion masses, at tree level, the pole falls to a specific point on $u$-channel cut and crosses to the adjacent Riemann sheet defined by the logarithmic $u$ channel cut. At one-loop level, the pole does not meet the $u$-cut up to $m_π=0.36$GeV. We also re-examined the $σ$ pole trajectory and find it in good agreement with Roy equation analysis result.

## Key claim

The subthreshold negative parity nucleon pole \(N^*(920)\) moves toward the real axis as the pion mass increases, but at one-loop level it does not meet the \(u\)-channel cut up to \(m_\pi = 0.36\) GeV, contrary to the tree-level behavior where it falls onto a specific point on the \(u\)-cut and crosses to an adjacent Riemann sheet.

## Method

The authors use the linear \(\sigma\) model with nucleon fields and apply the \(N/D\) unitarization method to study the pole trajectory of the \(N^*(920)\) in the \(S_{11}\) channel of \(\pi N\) scattering. They compute tree-level and one-loop amplitudes, then solve the \(N/D\) integral equation numerically with a cutoff \(\Lambda = (m_N + m_\sigma)^2\) and subtraction point \(s_0 = s_R\). Two parameter choices are considered: fixing \(m_\sigma\) while varying \(\lambda\), and fixing \(\lambda\) while varying \(m_\sigma\), with the \(m_\pi\) dependence of \(f_\pi\) and \(m_N\) constrained by chiral perturbation theory results.

## Key numbers

- The \(N^*(920)\) pole at physical pion mass is found at \((0.94 - 0.14i)\) GeV using the linear \(\sigma\) model with \(m_\sigma = 0.55\) GeV.
- At one-loop level, the pole does not meet the \(u\)-cut up to \(m_\pi = 0.36\) GeV.
- The critical pion mass for the tree-level pole to reach the \(u\)-cut is approximately \(m_\pi = 0.236\) GeV and \(0.253\) GeV for the two parameter choices (fixed \(m_\sigma\) and fixed \(\lambda\), respectively).
- The \(\sigma\) pole trajectory is reproduced with \(m_\sigma = 0.7\) GeV, giving a pole at approximately \((0.47 - i0.16)\) GeV at physical pion mass; the \(\sigma\) becomes a bound state when \(2m_\pi > m_\sigma\), with the critical point at \(m_\pi \simeq 0.32\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the \(N^*(920)\) pole, previously established in \(\pi N\) scattering data analyses by Wang (2017, 2018) and confirmed by Roy-Steiner equation analysis by Cao (2022) and Hoferichter (2023), to the case of varying pion masses. The \(\sigma\) pole trajectory is compared with the model-independent Roy equation analysis by Cao (2023) and the \(O(N)\) linear \(\sigma\) model study by Lyu (2024), finding good qualitative agreement. The results provide predictions for future lattice QCD calculations of the \(N^*(920)\) trajectory and demonstrate the importance of crossed-channel dynamics (the \(u\)-channel nucleon exchange) in determining the pole behavior.
