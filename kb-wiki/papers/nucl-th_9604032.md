# Triplet pairing in beta-stable neutron star matter

> $^{3}P_{2}$ pairing in neutron matter is investigated using the Bonn potential models. We find pairing energy gaps in pure neutron matter comparable to the results of previous investigators when the attractive tensor coupling is included. However, taking into account that in a neutron star we have matter at $β$ equilibrium, we find that the $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ energy gap is reduced considerably.

## Key claim

The $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ neutron energy gap in pure neutron matter is comparable to previous results (maximum ~0.13 MeV), but when the matter is at $\beta$ equilibrium (as in a real neutron star), the gap is reduced considerably, by approximately a factor of two, to a maximum of about 0.05 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equations for neutrons in the $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ state, first in the uncoupled $^{3}P_{2}$ approximation (neglecting tensor coupling) and then in the full coupled $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ channel (including tensor coupling). Single-particle energies were obtained from a self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculation using the Bonn A, B, and C meson-exchange potentials. For $\beta$-stable matter, the proton fraction was determined by imposing charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium among neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons.

## Key numbers

* Maximum $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ energy gap in pure neutron matter: 0.12–0.13 MeV, occurring for Fermi momenta in the range 1.2–2.5 fm$^{-1}$ (Bonn B potential).
* Maximum $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ energy gap in $\beta$-stable neutron star matter: 0.05 MeV, occurring for Fermi momenta in the range 1.32–2.34 fm$^{-1}$ (Bonn A potential).
* Condensation energy for the $m_J=0$ solution at $k_F=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$: $-\Delta E/(\rho_0/8\pi) = 0.139$ MeV (Bonn B).
* Condensation energy for the $|m_J|=2$ solution at $k_F=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$: $-\Delta E/(\rho_0/8\pi) = 0.133$ MeV (Bonn B).
* Effective mass $m^*/m$ in pure neutron matter: 0.65–0.91 (Bonn B).
* Critical density for onset of direct URCA processes in $\beta$-stable matter: 0.62 fm$^{-3}$ (non-relativistic BHF) and 0.50 fm$^{-3}$ (relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $^{1}S_{0}$ pairing (Elgaroy et al., 1996) to the $^{3}P_{2}$ channel, and is the first calculation of the $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ gap in $\beta$-stable matter. The results for pure neutron matter agree with those of Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1993) and Amundsen and Ostgaard (1985), who used OPEG potentials, but are an order of magnitude smaller than those of Baldo et al. (1992), who used the Argonne $V_{14}$ potential without tensor coupling. The reduction of the gap in $\beta$-stable matter is consistent with the phenomenological finding of Schaab et al. (1996) that lowering the $^{3}P_{2}$ gaps by a factor of two improves agreement with neutron star cooling observations. The authors note that medium polarization effects, neglected here, may increase the $^{3}P_{2}$ gap and should be investigated.
