# Treatment of pairing correlations in nuclei close to drip lines

> We discuss the HFB equations in coordinate representation,a suitable method for handling the full effects of the continuous quasiparticle spectrum. We show how the continuum HFB equations can be solved with the correct asymptotic conditions instead of the discretization conditions which are commonly used in the literature. The continuum HFB method is illustrated with a model where the mean field and pairing field have simple forms. The relationship with the continuum Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) approximation is also discussed. Realistic HFB and HF-BCS calculations based on Skyrme interactions are compared for the case of a neutron-rich nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for the continuous quasiparticle spectrum, rather than with the commonly used box discretization conditions, yields different pairing properties for nuclei near drip lines; the box boundary condition calculations generally overestimate pairing correlations compared to the proper continuum treatment.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations in coordinate space with scattering-wave boundary conditions for the continuum spectrum, using a spherical square-well mean field and a constant pairing field for illustration, and then perform self-consistent calculations with the Skyrme SIII interaction for the mean field and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction for the nucleus 84Ni. They compare these "full" continuum HFB results to HFB calculations using box boundary conditions, and also compare HFB results to continuum Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) calculations that include resonant continuum states with and without their widths.

## Key numbers

- For 84Ni, the Fermi level from full continuum HFB is -1.026 MeV, while from box HFB it is -1.205 MeV
- The average pairing gap from full continuum HFB is 1.230 MeV, while from box HFB it is 1.448 MeV
- The pairing energy from full continuum HFB is -18.092 MeV, while from box HFB it is -24.081 MeV
- The total binding energy from full continuum HFB is -651.079 MeV, while from box HFB it is -654.708 MeV
- The s1/2 quasiparticle resonance closest to the Fermi energy has energy 1.241 MeV and width 0.69 MeV
- The d5/2 quasiparticle resonance has energy 1.816 MeV and width 0.076 MeV
- The g9/2 quasiparticle resonance has energy 5.747 MeV and width 0.001 MeV

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space HFB approach of Dobaczewski, Flocard and Treiner (1984) and Dobaczewski et al. (1996) by implementing correct scattering-wave boundary conditions instead of box discretization, following the formal analysis of Bulgac (1999). The results show that box boundary conditions overestimate pairing correlations in neutron-rich nuclei like 84Ni, which has implications for predicting the location of drip lines; the paper also clarifies the relationship between continuum HFB and continuum HF-BCS approaches for treating resonant states.
