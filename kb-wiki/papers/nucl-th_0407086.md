# Staggering behavior of 0^+ state energies in the Sp(4) pairing model

> We explore, within the framework of an algebraic sp(4) shell model, discrete approximations to various derivatives of the energies of the lowest isovector-paired 0^+ states of atomic nuclei in the 40 <A < 100 mass range. The results show that the symplectic model can be used to successfully interpret fine structure effects driven by the proton-neutron (pn) and like-particle isovector pairing interactions as well as interactions with higher J multipolarity. A finite energy difference technique is used to investigate two-proton and two-neutron separation energies, observed irregularities found around the N=Z region, and the like-particle and pn isovector pairing gaps. A prominent staggering behavior is observed between groups of even-even and odd-odd nuclides. An oscillation, in addition to that associated with changes in isospin values, that tracks with alternating seniority quantum numbers related to the isovector pairing interaction is also found.

## Key claim

The Sp(4) symplectic shell model, with fixed parameters from a prior fit to experimental energies, successfully reproduces fine-structure staggering patterns in the lowest isovector-paired 0+ state energies for even-A nuclei in the 40 ≤ A ≤ 100 mass range, including effects beyond what isovector pairing alone can explain, provided the symmetry (T(T+1)) term is included.

## Method

The authors used an algebraic sp(4) shell model with a Hamiltonian containing isovector (T=1) pairing terms for like-particle and proton-neutron pairs, plus a diagonal isoscalar (T=0) symmetry term proportional to T(T+1). They computed discrete approximations to first, second, third, and fourth derivatives of the Coulomb-corrected energies of the lowest isovector-paired 0+ states with respect to the Sp(4) classification variables (total valence nucleon number n, isospin projection i, and proton/neutron pair numbers N+1 and N-1), using fixed Hamiltonian parameters from a prior fit to experimental data for the 1f7/2 shell and the 1f5/2 2p1/2,3/2 1g9/2 major shell.

## Key numbers

- Hamiltonian parameters for the 1f7/2 shell (40Ca core): G = 0.53Ω, F = 0.45Ω, C = 0.47, D = -0.97, E = -1.12(2Ω), ε = 9.36 MeV
- Hamiltonian parameters for the 1f5/2 2p1/2,3/2 1g9/2 major shell (56Ni core): G = 0.35Ω, F = 0.30Ω, C = 0.19, D = -0.80, E = -0.49(2Ω), ε = 9.57 MeV
- Quadratic mean difference in S2p between the Sp(4) model and Ormand (1997) for the first three nuclei in the predicted drip line: 0.32 MeV
- Quadratic mean difference in S2p between the Sp(4) model and Moller et al. (1997) for all ten nuclei in the predicted drip line: 0.78 MeV
- Quadratic mean difference in S2p between the Sp(4) model and Brown et al. (2002) for the first four nuclei in the predicted drip line: 0.43 MeV
- Average δVpn residual proton-neutron interaction: ~0.71 MeV for the 1f7/2 shell and ~0.52 MeV for the major shell above 56Ni

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior Sp(4) model study (Sviratcheva et al., 2003) that reproduced experimental 0+ state energies, by now applying higher-order discrete derivative filters to isolate fine-structure effects. The staggering analysis connects to earlier work on odd-even mass staggering (Bohr and Mottelson, Madland and Nix, 1988; Satula et al., 1998) and to studies of residual proton-neutron interactions (Zhang et al., 1989; Zamfir and Casten, 1991; Brenner et al., 1990). The model predicts a two-proton drip line near 60Ge, 64Se, 68Kr, 72Sr, 76Zr, 78Zr, 82Mo, 86Ru, 90Pd, and 94Cd, in agreement with other theoretical predictions (Ormand, 1997; Moller et al., 1997; Brown et al., 2002), and enables predictions for proton-rich exotic nuclei not yet experimentally accessible.
