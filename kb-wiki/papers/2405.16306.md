# Magnetic field effect on hadron yield ratios and fluctuations in a hadron resonance gas

> This work studies the influence of an external magnetic field on hadron yields and fluctuations in a hadron resonance gas by performing calculations within an updated version of the open-source Thermal-FIST package. The presence of the magnetic field has a sizable influence on certain hadron yield ratios. Most notably, it leads to enhanced $p/π$ and suppressed $n/p$ ratios, which may serve as a magnetometer in heavy-ion collisions. By attributing the centrality dependence of the $p/π$ ratio in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02~TeV measured by the ALICE Collaboration entirely to the magnetic field, its maximal strength at freeze-out is estimated to be $eB \simeq 0.12$~GeV$^2 \simeq 6.3 m_π^2$ in peripheral collisions. The magnetic field also enhances various conserved charge susceptibilities, which is qualitatively consistent with recent lattice QCD data and is driven in the HRG model by the increase of hadron densities. However, the variances of hadrons do not show any enhancement when normalized by the means, therefore, measurements of second-order fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions appear to offer limited additional information about the magnetic field relative to mean multiplicities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the centrality dependence of the $p/\pi$ ratio in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV measured by ALICE could be explained by a strong magnetic field at freeze-out, but the author explicitly notes that baryon annihilation in the hadronic phase is a more likely explanation and that the extracted magnetic field of $eB \simeq 0.12$ GeV$^2$ is "remarkably large for the freeze-out stage."

## Method

The author implemented a magnetized hadron resonance gas model in the open-source Thermal-FIST package (version 1.5), where charged hadrons acquire discrete Landau levels and modified energy levels depending on their charge, spin, and magnetic field strength. Hadron yields and conserved charge susceptibilities were computed at fixed temperature $T = 155$ MeV and vanishing chemical potentials, incorporating resonance decay feeddown with vacuum branching ratios. The magnetic field strength at each centrality was extracted by mapping the relative enhancement of the $p/\pi$ ratio over the most central bin to the HRG model prediction, assuming the most central collisions have zero magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Maximal magnetic field at freeze-out in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV: $eB \simeq 0.12$ GeV$^2 \simeq 6.3 m_\pi^2$ (from abstract); the extracted value from the analysis is $eB \simeq (0.13 \pm 0.01)$ GeV$^2 \simeq (6.6 \pm 0.6) m_\pi^2$ (from full text)
- Temperature used for freeze-out calculations: $T = 155$ MeV
- Upper limit on magnetic field from $\Lambda$ and $\bar{\Lambda}$ polarization measurements: $eB/m_\pi^2 \sim 0.044$ for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Temperature used for lattice QCD comparison of susceptibilities: $T = 145$ MeV
- The $p/\pi$ ratio in $p-p$ collisions is about $1.8\sigma$ above the value in central Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the magnetized HRG model formulated by Endrodi (2013) and implemented in earlier studies of conserved charge susceptibilities by Bhattacharyya (2015) and Ding (2021, 2023). The author compares results to recent lattice QCD data from Ding (2023) on second-order susceptibilities, finding qualitative agreement but quantitative underprediction for $eB \gtrsim 0.08$ GeV$^2$. The analysis enables future experimental tests of magnetic field effects through measurements of the $n/p$ ratio, which the author identifies as the most sensitive and unambiguous probe due to isospin symmetry breaking, and through comparisons of $\Omega^-/\pi$ versus $\Lambda/\pi$ ratios.
