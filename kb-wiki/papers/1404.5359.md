# Energy and Centrality Dependence of Chemical Freeze-out Thermodynamics parameters

> Driven by the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the RHIC, researches and discussions on the QCD phase diagram have flourished recently. In order to provide a reference from microscopic transport models, we performed a systematic analysis, using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model for the particle yields and a statistical model (THERMUS) for the thermal fit, for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$=7.7-200 GeV. It is found that at a fixed collision centrality the chemical freeze-out parameter, temperature $T_{\text{ch}}$, increases with collision energy and somehow saturates at certain values of $T_{\text{ch}}$ in collisions near $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$=10 GeV, indicating the limiting temperature in hadronic interactions; meanwhile the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ decrease with the collision energy. The saturation temperature is also found to be dependent on partonic interaction. At a given collision energy, it is found that both $T_{\text{ch}}$ and $μ_B$ decrease towards more peripheral collisions in the grand canonical approach. The energy and centrality dependence of other chemical freeze-out parameters, strangeness chemical potential $μ_S$, strangeness undersaturation factor $γ_S$, and the volume of the fireball $V$ are also presented in this paper. The chemical potential ratio $μ_s/μ_B$ is also compared with lattice QCD calculation. The AMPT default model gives better descriptions on both the particle yields and the chemical freeze-out parameters than those from the AMPT string-melting model.

## Key claim
The chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{\text{ch}}\) extracted from AMPT+THERMUS modeling of Au+Au collisions shows a saturation near \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}\approx 10\) GeV only when partonic interactions are included (the string-melting model), whereas the default AMPT model (with negligible partonic stage) yields a nearly energy-independent \(T_{\text{ch}}\approx 160\) MeV across the full range 7.7–200 GeV.

## Method
The authors generated ~2 million AMPT events per configuration for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=7.7\)–200 GeV, extracting mid-rapidity yields of \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(p\), \(\bar{p}\), \(\phi\), \(\Lambda\), \(\bar{\Lambda}\), \(\Xi^-\), \(\bar{\Xi}^+\), \(\Omega^-\), and \(\bar{\Omega}^+\). These yields were then fitted with the THERMUS statistical model using both grand-canonical and strangeness-canonical ensembles to extract \(T_{\text{ch}}\), \(\mu_B\), \(\mu_S\), \(\gamma_s\), and fireball volume \(V\). The hadronic cascade termination time was varied from 0.6 to 30 fm/c to study the effect of hadronic rescattering.

## Key numbers
- In the default AMPT model, \(T_{\text{ch}} \approx 160\) MeV for top 5% central collisions, with weak energy dependence.
- In the string-melting AMPT model, \(T_{\text{ch}}\) saturates at 170–190 MeV for top 5% central collisions near \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}\approx 10\) GeV.
- The baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) decreases with increasing collision energy across all models and centralities.
- At a given collision energy, both \(T_{\text{ch}}\) and \(\mu_B\) decrease from central to peripheral collisions.
- The strangeness undersaturation factor \(\gamma_s\) from the default model decreases from central to peripheral collisions; in the string-melting model \(\gamma_s\) is nearly constant across centrality and smaller than in the default model.
- The ratio \(\mu_S/\mu_B\) from all AMPT model variants converges at \(\mu_B \approx 400\) MeV to a value of 0.24–0.26 at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=7.7\) GeV.
- The fireball volume \(V\) increases linearly with the number of participant nucleons \(N_{\text{part}}\) at fixed collision energy.

## Context
This work provides a transport-model reference for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, extending prior statistical-model fits to experimental data (Andronic; Cleymans) by using AMPT-generated particle yields rather than experimental measurements. The results show that partonic interactions (as implemented in the string-melting AMPT model) are essential to reproduce the saturation of \(T_{\text{ch}}\) near 10 GeV that is seen in data, and that the default AMPT model (dominated by hadronic degrees of freedom) fails to capture this feature. The comparison of \(\mu_S/\mu_B\) with lattice QCD calculations (Bazavov) highlights that the string-melting model produces anomalously large ratios due to unrealistic multistrange baryon-to-antibaryon ratios, while the default model gives better overall agreement with both particle yields and freeze-out parameters.
