# Event-plane decorrelation of photons produced in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions

> We study photon production in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions using a multistage model combining IP-Glasma, KøMPøST and relativistic hydrodynamics. We discuss the small momentum anisotropy of these photons, highlighting the role of the photon-hadron event-plane decorrelation. We comment that this singular characteristic of early photons could be used to provide dynamical information on the complex pre-hydrodynamics phase of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Early-stage photons in heavy-ion collisions have a small momentum anisotropy and their event plane is decorrelated from that of soft hadrons, which could provide dynamical information on the complex pre-hydrodynamics phase; however, the authors note that unless a mechanism can produce simultaneously a larger \(v_2\) and a stronger correlation with soft hadrons, these photons are unlikely to provide a solution to the "direct photon puzzle".

## Method

The authors used a multistage model combining IP-Glasma (up to \(\tau=0.1\) fm), KøMPøST (from \(\tau=0.1\) to \(0.8\) fm), and relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (from \(\tau=0.8\) fm onward) to simulate Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV. Photon emission rates in the deconfined phase were calculated in the weakly-coupled limit and extrapolated to \(g_s=2\), with viscous corrections for both shear and bulk viscosity following the approach of Paquet (2015). The photon-hadron event-plane decorrelation was quantified via \(\cos(2(\Psi_2^{\gamma}-\Psi_2^{h}))\) in the \(v_n\{SP\}\) observable.

## Key numbers

- Photons emitted at higher \(p_T\) (where early-stage photons dominate) show \(\cos(2(\Psi_2^{\gamma}-\Psi_2^{h})) \approx 0.25\), indicating significant decorrelation between photon and hadron event planes.
- The IP-Glasma stage evolves the deconfined matter up to \(\tau=0.1\) fm, followed by KøMPøST evolution up to \(\tau=0.8\) fm, after which hydrodynamics takes over.
- The QCD crossover region corresponds to energy densities between 0.234 and 1.77 GeV/fm\(^3\) (temperatures 150--200 MeV for an equilibrium QCD plasma).

## Context

This work extends the multistage modeling approach of Paquet (2015) by explicitly separating photon production from the pre-hydrodynamics phase (IP-Glasma and KøMPøST) and the hydrodynamic phase. It builds on the IP-Glasma framework of Schenke (2012) and the KøMPøST framework of Kurkela (2018) for the pre-equilibrium evolution. The finding that early photons have a decorrelated event plane from soft hadrons provides a potential new probe of the pre-hydrodynamics stage, distinguishing them from the larger number of photons produced later in the collision.
