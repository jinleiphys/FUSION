# Effects of one valence proton on seniority and angular momentum of neutrons in neutron-rich $^{122-131}$Sb$_{51}$ isotopes

> The neutron-rich $^{122-131}$Sb isotopes were produced as fission fragments in the reaction $^{9}$Be($^{238}$U,~f) with 6.2 MeV/u beam energy. An unique setup, consisting of AGATA, VAMOS++ and EXOGAM detectors, was used which enabled the prompt-delayed gamma-ray ($γ$) spectroscopy of fission fragments in the time range of 100 ns - 200 $μ$s. New isomers, prompt and delayed transitions were established in the even-A $^{122-130}$Sb isotopes. In the odd-A $^{123-131}$Sb isotopes, new prompt and delayed $γ$-ray transitions were identified, in addition to the confirmation of the previously known isomers. The half-lives of the isomeric states and the $B(E2)$ transition probabilities of the observed transitions depopulating these isomers were extracted. The experimental data was compared with the theoretical results obtained in the framework of Large-Scale Shell-Model (LSSM) calculations in a restricted model space. Modifications of several components of the shell model interaction were introduced to obtain a consistent agreement with the excitation energies and the $B(E2)$ transition probabilities in neutron-rich Sn and Sb isotopes. The isomeric configurations in Sn and Sb were found to be relatively pure. Further, the calculations revealed that the presence of a single valence proton, mainly in the $g_{7/2}$ orbital in Sb isotopes, leads to significant mixing (due to the $νπ$ interaction) of: (i) the neutron seniorities ($\upsilon_ν$) and (ii) the neutron angular momentum ($I_ν$). The above features have a weak impact on the excitation energies, but have an important impact on the $B(E2)$ transition probabilities. In addition, a constancy of the relative excitation energies irrespective of neutron seniority and neutron number in Sn and Sb was observed.

## Key claim

The presence of a single valence proton in the $g_{7/2}$ orbital in neutron-rich Sb isotopes leads to significant mixing of neutron seniorities and neutron angular momentum through the neutron-proton interaction, which has a weak impact on excitation energies but a strong impact on $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, while the isomeric configurations in both Sn and Sb remain relatively pure.

## Method

Neutron-rich $^{122-131}$Sb isotopes were produced as fission fragments via fusion- and transfer-induced fission using a $^{238}$U beam at 6.2 MeV/u on a $^{9}$Be target at GANIL. Prompt-delayed gamma-ray spectroscopy was performed using the AGATA tracking array, VAMOS++ spectrometer for isotopic identification, and EXOGAM detectors for delayed gamma rays, covering a time range of 100 ns to 200 microseconds. Half-lives of isomeric states and $B(E2)$ transition probabilities were extracted and compared with Large-Scale Shell-Model calculations in a restricted model space with modified interaction components.

## Key numbers

- Half-life of the (19/2$^{-}$) state in $^{123}$Sb: $T_{1/2}$ = 222(23) ns, giving $B(E2; 19/2^{-} \rightarrow 15/2^{-})$ = 6.9(7) e$^{2}$fm$^{4}$
- Half-life of the 23/2$^{+}$ state in $^{123}$Sb: $T_{1/2}$ = 66(4) $\mu$s, giving $B(E2; 23/2^{+} \rightarrow 19/2^{+})$ = 0.15(1) e$^{2}$fm$^{4}$
- New prompt gamma-ray transitions identified in $^{122}$Sb: 445.2, 657.2, and 1102.2 keV
- New prompt gamma-ray transitions identified in $^{123}$Sb: 244.1 and 439.2 keV
- New prompt gamma-ray transitions identified in $^{124}$Sb: 143, 338, 428, and 1012.0 keV
- The 23/2$^{+}$ isomer half-life in $^{123}$Sb (66(4) $\mu$s) agrees with the previously reported value of 65(1) $\mu$s from Wa09
- The (19/2$^{-}$) isomer half-life in $^{123}$Sb (222(23) ns) agrees with the previously reported value of 214(3) ns from Wa09

## Context

This work extends the study of seniority isomers from semi-magic Sn isotopes (Z=50) to Sb isotopes (Z=51), where a single valence proton is added. Previous work by Re16 on $^{124-128}$Sb and $^{122-126}$In suggested significant mixing of seniorities due to a valence proton particle or hole in the $g_{7/2}$/$g_{9/2}$ orbital. The results enable testing of shell-model interactions near the doubly magic $^{132}$Sn, and the observed constancy of relative excitation energies irrespective of neutron seniority and neutron number in Sn and Sb provides new constraints for theoretical models.
