# A Nuclear Structure Model for Double Charge-Exchange Processes

> A new model, based on the BCS approach, is specially designed to describe nuclear phenomena $(A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z\pm 2)$ of double-charge exchange (DCE). After being proposed, and applied in the particle-hole limit, by one of the authors (F. Krmpotić [1]), so far it was never been applied within the BCS mean-field framework, nor has its ability to describe DCE processes been thoroughly explored. It is a natural extension of the pn-QRPA model, developed by Halbleib and Sorensen [2] to describe the single $β$-decays $(A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z\pm 1)$, to the DCE processes. As such, it exhibits several advantages over the pn-QRPA model when is used in the evaluation of the double beta decay (DBD) rates. For instance, i) the extreme sensitivity of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) on the model parametrization does not occur, ii) it allows to study NMEs, not only for the fundamental state in daughter nuclei, as the pn-QRPA model does, but also for all final $0^+$ and $2^+$ states, accounting at the same time their excitation energies and the corresponding DBD Q-values, iii) together with the DBD-NMEs it provides also the energy spectra of Fermi and Gamow-Teller DCE transition strengths, as well as the locations of the corresponding resonances and their sum rules, iv) the latter are relevant for both the DBD and the DCE reactions, since the involved nuclear structure is the same; this correlation does not exist within the pn-QRPA model. As an example, detailed numerical calculations are presented for the $(A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z+ 2)$ process in $^{48}$Ca $\rightarrow ^{48}$Ti and the $(A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z- 2)$ process in $^{96}$Ru $\rightarrow ^{96}$Mo, involving all final $0^+$ states and $2^+$ states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new (pn,2p2n)-QTDA model, based on the BCS approach, as a natural extension of the pn-QRPA model to double charge-exchange (DCE) processes; it claims several advantages over the pn-QRPA, including reduced sensitivity of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) to model parametrization and the ability to compute NMEs for all final \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states, but the model approximates the initial ground state as the BCS vacuum rather than the more accurate correlated ground state used in pn-QRPA.

## Method

The authors construct a Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) model, labeled (pn,2p2n)-QTDA, where the initial, intermediate, and final nuclei are treated as the BCS vacuum, pn-excitations, and 2p2n-excitations, respectively. The intermediate states \(J_\alpha^+\) are obtained by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian with a \(\delta\)-force residual interaction in the pn-channel, while the final states \(\J_f^+\) are built from two-proton-two-neutron quasiparticle states and diagonalized including pp and nn interactions. The model is applied to the \((A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z+2)\) process in \(^{48}\text{Ca}\rightarrow ^{48}\text{Ti}\) and the \((A,Z)\rightarrow (A,Z-2)\) process in \(^{96}\text{Ru}\rightarrow ^{96}\text{Mo}\), using adjusted single-particle energies and coupling constants calibrated to experimental pairing gaps and the energies of the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) and Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR).

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{48}\text{Ca}\rightarrow ^{48}\text{Ti}\), the experimental value of the total \(2\nu\) NME for the ground state is \(|M^{2\nu}(0^+_1)| = (38 \pm 3) \times 10^{-3}\) (in natural units), used as a benchmark.
*   The \(ph\)-channel coupling constants used are \(v^s_{ph}=27\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) and \(v^t_{ph}=64\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) for \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(v^s_{ph}=55\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) and \(v^t_{ph}=92\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) for all nuclei.
*   The effective axial-vector coupling constant is set to \(g_A = 1.0\), instead of the bare value \(g_A = 1.27\).
*   The \(pp\)-channel coupling constants are chosen such that the ratios \(s = v_{pp}^s / \overline{v}^s_{pair}\) and \(t = v_{pp}^t / \overline{v}^s_{pair}\) are approximately 1, based on partial restoration of spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA model originally developed by Halbleib and Sorensen (1967) for single \(\beta\)-decays to double charge-exchange processes, and builds on a particle-hole limit proposed earlier by Krmpotić (2005). Unlike the standard pn-QRPA, which is limited to ground-state transitions and requires a second QRPA for excited states, this model provides NMEs for all final \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states, their excitation energies, and DCE strength distributions relevant for both double beta decay and heavy-ion-induced DCE reactions (e.g., the NUMEN project). The results enable future comparisons with shell-model calculations (e.g., Shimizu, Menéndez, and Yako, 2018) and experimental data for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{96}\)Ru decays.
