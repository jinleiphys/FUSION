# Masses and magnetic moments of ground-state baryons in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We report on some recent developments in our understanding of the light-quark mass dependence and the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking corrections to the magnetic moments of the ground-state baryons in a covariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory, the so-called EOMS formulation. We show that this covariant ChPT exhibits some promising features compared to its heavy-baryon and infrared counterparts.

# Masses and magnetic moments of ground-state baryons in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The apparent failure of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory to describe octet baryon magnetic moments is caused by the power-counting-restoration procedure used in removing power-counting-breaking terms, and the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) covariant formulation improves upon the heavy-baryon and infrared formulations by preserving both relativity and analyticity of loop results.

## Method

The authors computed octet baryon magnetic moments at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the EOMS formulation of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, using tree-level couplings at O(p^2) and loop diagrams at O(p^3) involving pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons, eta). They also performed NLO fits to 2+1-flavor lattice QCD results from the PACS-CS collaboration for octet and decuplet baryon masses, using empirical values for the axial couplings D=0.80, F=0.46, C=1.0, and H=1.13 rather than treating them as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Octet baryon magnetic moments at NLO in EOMS (in nuclear magnetons): proton 2.58, neutron -2.10, Lambda -0.66, Sigma- -1.10, Sigma+ 2.43, Sigma0 0.66, Xi- -0.95, Xi0 -1.27, Lambda-Sigma0 transition 1.58; with chi-squared = 0.18 compared to experimental values
- Heavy-baryon NLO results gave chi-squared = 1.01; infrared NLO results gave chi-squared = 1.86
- Tree-level (O(p^2)) results gave chi-squared = 0.46
- Extrapolated nucleon mass from EOMS NLO fit to PACS-CS lattice data: 893(19)(39) MeV, compared to experimental 940(2) MeV
- Extrapolated Delta mass from EOMS NLO fit: 1222(24)(49) MeV, compared to experimental 1232(2) MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for EOMS NLO fit to PACS-CS lattice data: 2.1; for heavy-baryon NLO fit: 9.2

## Context

This work extends the EOMS formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory, previously applied to other observables by Geng (2008, 2009) and Martin Camalich (2010), to magnetic moments and masses of ground-state baryons. It contradicts earlier findings in heavy-baryon ChPT by Jenkins (1992) and infrared ChPT by Kubis (2000) that SU(3) breaking corrections to the Coleman-Glashow relations were too large, and addresses the puzzle identified by Walker-Loud (2008) and Ishikawa (2009) that NLO heavy-baryon ChPT could not fit lattice QCD baryon masses without allowing axial couplings to nearly vanish. The results enable improved chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data and suggest that covariant formulations should be used for three-flavor baryon ChPT studies.
