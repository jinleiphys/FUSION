# Excited states in the neutron-rich nucleus 25F

> The structure of the nucleus 25F was investigated through in-beam γ-ray spectroscopy of the fragmentation of 26Ne and 27,28Na ion beams. Based on the particle-γ and particle-γγ coincidence data, a level scheme was constructed and compared with shell model and coupled-cluster calculations. Some of the observed states were interpreted as quasi single-particle states built on top of the closed-shell nucleus 24O, while the others were described as states arising from coupling of a single proton to the 2+ core excitation of 24O.

## Key claim

The level scheme of 25F was constructed from in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy, and while the low-lying states are well described by shell-model and coupled-cluster calculations, the higher-lying states assigned to the proton coupled to the 2+ core excitation of 24O show a systematic deviation of about 500 keV in excitation energy compared to the USDA and USDB shell-model predictions, and the coupled-cluster calculations also place these states too high.

## Method

The experiment used a double-step fragmentation reaction: a primary 36S beam at 77.5 MeV/u fragmented on a carbon target to produce secondary beams of 26Ne and 27,28Na, which then fragmented on an active plastic scintillator target to produce 25F. Gamma rays emitted in-flight were detected with an array of 74 BaF2 crystals, and particle-gamma and particle-gamma-gamma coincidence data were used to construct the level scheme. The experimental results were compared to shell-model calculations using the USD, USDA, and USDB interactions in the 1s0d valence space, and to particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (PA-EOM-CC) calculations using chiral interactions at N2LO and N3LO orders.

## Key numbers

- Gamma-ray energies assigned to 25F: 750(30) keV, 1720(15) keV, 3090(75) keV, 3440(50) keV, 3830(60) keV, and 4210(80) keV.
- Excited states established at 1720(15) keV, 3090(75) keV, 3440(21) keV, 3830(60) keV, and 4195(35) keV.
- The neutron separation energy of 25F is 4.36(12) MeV.
- The first excited state of 24O is at 4.7 MeV (2+) and is unbound by about 1 MeV.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter of 24O is beta2 = 0.15.
- The s-wave spectroscopic factor for the ground state of 24O is 1.74 +/- 0.19.
- The coupled-cluster calculations show that the 1p, 2p1h, and 3p2h partial norms for the 5/2+ ground state are 0.63, 0.30, and 0.07 respectively (using the N2LO interaction).

## Context

This work extends earlier preliminary studies of 25F from single-step fragmentation of 36S (Vajta et al., preliminary papers) and a RIKEN experiment, providing the first gamma-gamma coincidence data for this nucleus. The results are interpreted in the context of the doubly-magic nature of 24O, where N=16 is a magic number near the neutron drip line, as established by Ozawa et al. and supported by spectroscopy of 24O (Hoffman et al., Tshoo et al.). The observed states are compared to shell-model calculations and coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen et al., 2012), and the deviations for the higher-lying states suggest that continuum coupling effects, as studied in 24O by Tsukiyama et al. and Volya et al., may be important but could not be fully quantified due to computational limitations. The absence of a bound negative-parity intruder state is consistent with predictions by Frank et al. and Kimura et al.
