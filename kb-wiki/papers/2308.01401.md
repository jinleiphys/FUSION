# Single inclusive particle production in pA collisions at forward rapidities: beyond the hybrid model

> In this contribution we reconsider the calculation at next-to-leading order of forward inclusive single hadron production in $pA$ collisions within the hybrid approach. We conclude that the proper framework to compute this cross section beyond leading order is not collinear factorization as assumed so far, but the TMD factorized framework.

## Key claim

The proper framework for computing forward inclusive single hadron production in pA collisions beyond leading order is not collinear factorization as previously assumed, but transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization for both the projectile parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors revisited the next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of forward inclusive single hadron production in pA collisions within the hybrid approach, working in a frame where the target is highly evolved and following the formulation of Altinoluk (2014). They reorganized the real and virtual contributions into terms yielding TMD PDFs and TMD FFs plus genuine NLO remainders showing no large soft or collinear logarithmic contributions, working at large Nc and assuming translational invariance of the target averages of Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- The factorization scales for TMD PDFs and TMD FFs are chosen as μ²_T = μ²_F = μ² = max{Q_s², (p/ζ)²}, where Q_s is the saturation scale of the target, p is the hadron transverse momentum, and ζ is the momentum fraction in the fragmentation function.
- The soft divergence in gluon emissions is regulated by a cutoff on momentum fraction ξ₀, which depends on the virtuality l of the gluon as ξ₀(l) = l²/(x s₀), where s₀ is the Ioffe cutoff parameter.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach for single inclusive particle production in pA collisions at forward rapidities, which was formulated at LO by Dumitru (2005) and computed at NLO by Chirilli (2011, 2012). It addresses the negativity problem observed in numerical implementations by Stasto (2013, 2014), where the NLO cross section dropped rapidly with increasing transverse momentum and became negative. Previous attempts to solve this issue included Ioffe time restrictions by Altinoluk (2014), kinematic restrictions by Watanabe (2015), and threshold and Sudakov resummations by Liu (2020), Xiao (2018), and Shi (2021). The present work shows that collinear factorization is physically inappropriate beyond LO and that TMD factorization provides the correct resummation framework, enabling proper treatment of large transverse logarithms from perturbative splittings in the projectile wave function.
