# Screening masses of positive- and negative-parity hadron ground-states, including those with strangeness

> Using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction (SCI) at nonzero temperature, we compute the screening masses of flavour-SU(3) ground-state $J^P=0^\pm$, $1^\pm$ mesons, and $J^P=1/2^\pm$, $3/2^\pm$ baryons. We find that all correlation channels allowed at $T=0$ persist when the temperature increases, even above the QCD phase transition. The results for mesons qualitatively agree with those obtained from the contemporary lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics (lQCD) simulations. One of the most remarkable features is that each parity-partner-pair degenerates when $T>T_c$, with $T_c$ being the critical temperature. For each pair, the screening mass of the negative parity meson increases monotonously with temperature. In contrast, the screening mass of the meson with positive parity is almost invariant on the domain $T\lesssim T_c/2$; when $T$ gets close to $T_c$, it decreases but soon increases again and finally degenerates with its parity partner, which signals the restoration of chiral symmetry. We also find that the $T$-dependent behaviours of baryon screening masses are quite similar to those of the mesons. For baryons, the dynamical, nonpointlike diquark correlations play a crucial role in the screening mass evolution. We further calculate the evolution of the fraction of each kind of diquark within baryons respective to temperature. We observe that, at high temperatures, only $J=0$ scalar and pseudoscalar diquark correlations can survive within $J^P=1/2^\pm$ baryons.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) at nonzero temperature, the authors compute screening masses for all flavour-SU(3) ground-state mesons and baryons with both positive and negative parity, finding that parity-partner pairs become degenerate above the critical temperature \(T_c = 0.197\) GeV, signaling chiral symmetry restoration; however, the screening masses never exceed the free theory limit \(2\pi T\) (for mesons) or \(3\pi T\) (for baryons), a limitation attributed to the oversimplified temperature-independent SCI kernel.

## Method

The rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations is employed with a vector × vector contact interaction (SCI) kernel, where the gluon propagator is replaced by a constant \(\delta_{\mu\nu} 4\pi\alpha_{\rm IR}/m_G^2\) with \(m_G = 0.8\) GeV and \(\alpha_{\rm IR} = 0.93\pi\). The dressed-quark propagator at nonzero temperature is obtained by solving the gap equation with a temperature-dependent infrared regulator \(\Lambda_{\rm ir}^m(T)\) that vanishes at the deconfinement temperature \(T_d = T_c = 0.197\) GeV. Meson screening masses are derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation at zero Matsubara frequency, while baryon screening masses are obtained from the quark+diquark Faddeev equation, incorporating scalar, pseudoscalar, axial-vector, and vector diquark correlations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration (u/d quark): \(T_c = 0.197\) GeV; normalised value \(T_c/m_\rho = 0.212\) (with SCI \(\rho\)-meson mass \(m_\rho = 0.93\) GeV), close to lQCD result \(0.200(3)\).
- Chiral-limit critical temperature: \(T_c^0 = 0.215\) GeV; normalised \(T_c^0/m_\rho^0 = 0.234\) (with SCI chiral-limit \(\rho\)-meson mass \(m_\rho^0 = 0.919\) GeV).
- Dressed-quark masses at \(T=0\): \(M_u = 0.37\) GeV, \(M_s = 0.53\) GeV (current masses \(m_u = 0.007\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.17\) GeV).
- Meson screening masses at \(T=0\) (GeV): \(\pi = 0.14\), \(K = 0.50\), \(\rho = 0.93\), \(K^\ast = 1.03\), \(\phi = 1.13\), \(\sigma = 1.22\), \(\kappa = 1.32\), \(a_1 = 1.37\), \(K_1 = 1.48\), \(f_1 = 1.59\).
- Diquark screening masses at \(T=0\) (GeV): \([ud]_{0^+} = 0.78\), \([sd]_{0^+} = 0.93\), \(\{ud\}_{1^+} = 1.06\), \(\{us\}_{1^+} = 1.16\), \(\{ss\}_{1^+} = 1.26\), \([ud]_{0^-} = 1.15\), \([sd]_{0^-} = 1.26\), \([ud]_{1^-} = 1.33\), \([sd]_{1^-} = 1.44\).
- Critical degenerate temperatures (relative difference 0.5%): for \(\sigma\)-\(\pi\) pair \(T_c^{\delta m} = 1.95\,T_c\); for \(\kappa\)-\(K\) pair \(T_c^{\delta m} = 1.82\,T_c\); for transverse \(a_1\)-\(\rho\) pair \(T_c^{\delta m} = 1.51\,T_c\); for transverse \(K_1\)-\(K^\ast\) pair \(T_c^{\delta m} = 1.58\,T_c\); for transverse \(f_1\)-\(\phi\) pair \(T_c^{\delta m} = 2.51\,T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier SCI study of Wang (2013) on light-quark meson and baryon screening masses by including all diquark correlation types (scalar, pseudoscalar, axial-vector, vector) and strange quarks, motivated by recent findings from Chen (2017), Liu (2022), and Eichmann (2016) that additional diquark correlations are necessary. The meson results qualitatively agree with contemporary lQCD simulations by Bazavov (2019), particularly the parity degeneracy above \(T_c\) and the ordering of critical degenerate temperatures. The baryon results enable future studies of how diquark fractions evolve with temperature, with the observation that only \(J=0\) scalar and pseudoscalar diquarks survive within \(J^P=1/2^\pm\) baryons at high temperatures.
