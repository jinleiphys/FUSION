# Dispersive analysis tools for $ππ$ and $πK$ scattering

> In this talk we discuss the relevance of dispersive methods and their different applications to obtain simple but rigorous parameterizations of pion-pion and kaon-pion scattering. We show as an example our latest dispersive analysis of $πK$ scattering, where we provide simple parameterizations of data satisfying Forward Dispersion Relations up to 1.6 GeV.

# Dispersive analysis tools for ππ and πK scattering

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that unconstrained fits to πK scattering data do not satisfy Forward Dispersion Relations, but that constrained fits can be made consistent with these relations up to 1.6 GeV, providing simple parameterizations that are easy to implement for further phenomenological studies.

## Method
The authors performed a Forward Dispersion Relation (FDR) analysis of πK scattering data, first showing that unconstrained fits to data (UFD) violate the dispersion relations, then obtaining constrained fits to data (CFD) that satisfy FDRs up to 1.6 GeV. The analysis uses fixed-t=0 dispersion relations where the left cut is rewritten in terms of the physical region, with the forward amplitude related by the optical theorem to the total cross section. The resulting parameterizations are simple conformal expansions in the elastic region and piecewise functions at higher energies.

## Key numbers
- The FDR analysis of πK scattering is applied up to 1.6 GeV, where the constrained fits become consistent with dispersion relations within errors.
- The matching point for Roy equations in ππ scattering was set at s_m = (0.8 GeV)^2 in earlier work (Ananthanarayan 2000, Colangelo 2001), later extended to the K̅K threshold for the scalar-isoscalar wave (Moussallam 2011).
- The f0(500) pole parameters from dispersive analyses are estimated as M = 449 (+22, -16) MeV and Γ = 550 ± 24 MeV (Pelaez 2015).
- The FDR analysis for πK scattering is performed up to 1.6 GeV, while Roy-Steiner equations for πK are limited to approximately 1.1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the Madrid-Krakow group's dispersive methodology, previously applied to ππ scattering (Pelaez 2004, Kaminski 2006, GarciaMartin 2011), to the πK system. The ππ analysis used three Forward Dispersion Relations, three Roy equations with two subtractions, and three GKPY equations (Roy equations with one subtraction) for S- and P-waves up to 1.1-1.4 GeV, resolving longstanding data conflicts on inelasticity and determining poles of the f0(500), f0(980), and ρ(770) mesons. For πK, the constrained fits enable further studies of πK and ππ → K̅K scattering, and can be used with Padé sequence methods (Masjuan 2014) to determine resonance poles including the controversial K0*(800) or κ-meson, whose RPP status remains "needs confirmation" despite earlier Roy-Steiner solutions (Buettiker 2003, Descotes-Genon 2006).
