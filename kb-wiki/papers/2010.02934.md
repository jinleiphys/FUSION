# The Fragmentation Spectrum from Space-Time Reciprocity

> Analyzing the single inclusive annihilation spectrum of charged hadrons in $e^+e^-$ collisions, I confront the hadronization hypothesis of local parton-hadron duality with a systematic resummation of the dependence on the small energy fraction. This resummation is based on the reciprocity between time-like and space-like splitting processes in $4-2ε$-dimensions, which I extend to resum all the soft terms of the cross-section for inclusive jet production. Under the local-parton-hadron duality hypothesis, the resulting distribution of jets essentially determines the spectrum of hadrons as the jet radius goes to zero. Thus I take the resummed perturbative jet function as the non-perturbative fragmentation function with an effective infra-red coupling. I find excellent agreement with data, and comment on the mixed leading log approximation previously used to justify local parton-hadron duality.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the hadron fragmentation spectrum can be determined entirely from perturbative QCD under the local parton-hadron duality (LPHD) hypothesis, by resumming the semi-inclusive jet function as the jet radius goes to zero and then identifying that resummed jet function with the non-perturbative fragmentation function, provided an effective infrared coupling is used. The hedge is that this identification is a model, not a derivation, and the LPHD hypothesis goes beyond the rigorous statement of factorization.

## Method

The author derives the resummed fragmentation function by solving angular-ordered evolution equations in 4-2ε dimensions, using the reciprocity between time-like and space-like splitting processes. The semi-inclusive jet function is resummed to all orders in the small energy fraction limit, and the resummed coefficient function and fragmentation function are shown to be inverses of each other in Mellin moment space. The resulting perturbative jet function is then taken as the non-perturbative fragmentation function by freezing the running coupling at an infrared scale, and the spectrum is compared to e+e- annihilation data.

## Key numbers

- The fit to charged hadron data uses 3 parameters: the scale at which the coupling is frozen, the scale at which DGLAP evolution is ended, and the basic normalization.
- The model describes data from LEP and SLAC at the Z-pole for energy fractions below approximately 0.5.
- Good description is also obtained for older data at smaller center-of-mass energies, though hadron mass-corrections limit the description of the extremely small energy fraction range.
- The leading-log splitting matrix in the (gluon, quark) basis is given by P^(0)(n) = (α_s C_A)/(π n) × C, where C is a 2×2 matrix with entries C_11=1, C_12=C_F/C_A, C_21=0, C_22=0.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Neill (2020) to resum the semi-inclusive jet function and fragmentation function simultaneously, and confronts the LPHD hypothesis with systematic resummation. It contrasts with the mixed leading log approximation (MLLA) used historically by Dokshitzer, Khoze, and collaborators (1991), which derived the "limiting spectrum" and distorted Gaussian approaches; the author notes that the MLLA anomalous dimension differs from the next-to-leading logarithmic MS-bar anomalous dimension from small-energy-fraction resummed perturbation theory of Vogt (2011) and Kom (2012). The result enables a consistent description of hadron fragmentation data across different center-of-mass energies using only perturbative QCD inputs plus a frozen coupling, and the formalism applies equally to semi-inclusive jet production.
