# Relativistic effects in the search for high density symmetry energy

> Intermediate energy heavy ion collisions open the unique possibility to explore the Equation of State ($EOS$) of nuclear matter far from saturation, in particular the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Within a relativistic transport model it is shown that the isovector-scalar $δ$-meson, which affects the high density behavior of the symmetry energy density, influences the dynamics of heavy ion collisions in terms of isospin collective flows. The effect is largely enhanced by a relativistic mechanism related to the covariant nature of the fields contributing to the isovector channel. Results for reactions induced by $^{132}Sn$ radioactive beams are presented. The elliptic flows of nucleons and light isobars appear to be quite sensitive to microscopic structure of the symmetry term, in particular for particles with large transverse momenta, since they represent an earlier emission from a compressed source. Thus future, more exclusive, experiments with relativistic radioactive beams should be able to set stringent constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy far from ground state nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the scalar-isovector δ-meson (or δ-like field) can be detected in heavy-ion collisions not just through its effect on the symmetry energy density, but primarily through a relativistic mechanism: the Lorentz factor dynamically enhances the vector-isovector (ρ) field relative to the scalar-isovector (δ) field, breaking the equilibrium balance between them and producing large isospin effects in collective flows even when the symmetry energy as a function of density is nearly identical.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport simulations using the Relativistic Landau Vlasov (RLV) method, solving a covariant Boltzmann-type equation with a Monte-Carlo collision term that includes elastic and inelastic processes (Δ and N* resonances, pion channels). They compared three Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) models for the isovector channel: one with only a ρ field (RMF-ρ), one with both ρ and δ fields (RMF-(ρ+δ)), and one with a density-dependent ρ coupling (RMF-Dρ) tuned to match the symmetry energy of the (ρ+δ) case. The simulations were run for 132Sn+132Sn and 132Sn+124Sn reactions at 1.5 AGeV with semicentral impact parameter b=6 fm.

## Key numbers

- The slope of the proton-neutron differential flow dFpn(y)/d(y/yproj) at mid-rapidity is 46.7 MeV/c for the RMF-(ρ+δ) model and 23.4 MeV/c for the RMF-ρ model, a factor of two difference.
- The coupling constants used are fρ=1.1 fm2 for RMF-ρ, fρ=3.3 fm2 and fδ=2.4 fm2 for RMF-(ρ+δ), and fρ(ρ0)=1.1 fm2 with an increasing density dependence for RMF-Dρ.
- The relativistic enhancement of the vector field becomes important around 1 AGeV; up to 500 AMeV there is no valuable difference in differential flow predictions among the models.
- The reaction energy is 1.5 AGeV and the impact parameter is 6 fm for all presented results.

## Context

This work extends non-relativistic studies of isospin-dependent collective flows (Scala 1999, Bao 2000) by introducing a fully relativistic framework. It contradicts the expectation that the symmetry energy alone determines isospin effects, showing that the Lorentz structure of the isovector fields matters independently. The results enable future experiments with radioactive beams (e.g., 132Sn beams) to constrain the high-density symmetry energy and specifically the strength of the δ-like field, which is not accessible from finite nuclei studies (Typel 1999, Furnstahl 2000, Buevenich 2002) or from spinodal instability analyses (Greco 2003).
