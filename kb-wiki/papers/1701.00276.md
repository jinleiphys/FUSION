# Exit-Channel Suppression in Statistical Reaction Theory

> Statistical reaction theories such as Hauser-Feshbach assume that branching ratios follow Bohr's compound nucleus hypothesis by factorizing into independent probabilities for different channels. Corrections to the factorization hypothesis are known in both nuclear theory and quantum transport theory, particularly an enhanced memory of the entrance channel. We apply the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble to study a complementary suppression of exit channel branching ratios. The effect can be large enough to provide information on the number of exit channels. The effect is demonstrated for the branching ratios of neutron-induced reactions on a $^{235}$U target.

# Exit-Channel Suppression in Statistical Reaction Theory

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that branching ratios in statistical nuclear reactions can be significantly suppressed when one exit channel is heavily favored, and that this suppression can provide a lower bound on the number of exit channels even when only averaged cross sections (not fluctuations) are measured. The effect combines Porter-Thomas width fluctuations with a constraint from the Bohr-Wheeler transmission coefficient limit, and is shown to be large enough to exclude single-channel fission models for neutron-induced reactions on 235U.

## Method

The authors apply the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) statistical model, constructing random Hamiltonian matrices and computing the K-matrix and S-matrix via Monte Carlo sampling to calculate reaction cross sections. They parameterize the model using experimental values for the level spacing D, capture width, and neutron strength function for 235U, then compute average cross sections and branching ratios for models with different numbers of fission channels (one versus five), comparing to experimental data at 10 keV neutron energy.

## Key numbers

- Level spacing D = 0.45 ± 0.05 eV for 235U (from RIPL and Mughabghab)
- Capture width Γ_cap = 38 ± 3 meV (from RIPL)
- Capture cross section σ_cap = 1.05 ± 0.07 b at E_n = 10 keV (from ENDF and Corvi)
- Fission cross section σ_F = 2.96 ± 0.06 b at E_n = 10 keV (from ENDF and Corvi)
- Cross-section ratio α^{-1} = σ_F/σ_cap = 2.8 ± 0.2 at 10 keV (from Corvi)
- Neutron strength function S_0 ≈ 1 ± 0.1 × 10^{-4} eV^{-1/2} (from Moldauer and Mughabghab)
- Model A (one fission channel): σ_F = 1.66 ± 0.05 b, σ_cap = 1.39 ± 0.05 b, α^{-1} = 1.20 ± 0.07
- Model B (five fission channels): σ_F = 2.28 ± 0.10 b, σ_cap = 1.00 ± 0.06 b, α^{-1} = 2.3 ± 0.11
- Hauser-Feshbach with width fluctuation correction for model A requires Γ^K_F = 0.46 ± 0.06 eV to fit data; full S-matrix treatment cannot reproduce the experimental ratio with a single fission channel (saturates at α^{-1} ~ 2.0)

## Context

This work extends the known width fluctuation correction (WFC) to statistical reaction theory, which has been studied extensively by Moldauer (1975) and reviewed by Mitchell (2010) and Kawano (2015). The authors show that exit-channel suppression is complementary to the well-known entrance-channel (elastic enhancement) effect, and that it can provide constraints on fission channel counts that are otherwise difficult to determine. The results enable future application to below-barrier fission, where sharp peaks in fission cross sections have been observed by Bjornholm and Lynn (2013) and may correspond to single-channel behavior. The GOE approach connects to similar phenomena in mesoscopic conductors known as weak localization and dephasing, as discussed by Altshuler (2000).
