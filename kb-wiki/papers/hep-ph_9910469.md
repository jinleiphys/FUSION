# Electromagnetic transitions of excited baryons in a deformed oscillator quark model

> We study electromagnetic transitions of excited baryons in a deformed oscillator quark model, where baryon excited states are described as rotational bands of deformed intrinsic states. We describe all necessary tools to compute transition amplitudes in multipole basis, which are then related to the commonly used helicity amplitudes. We pay a special attention on the sign of the amplitudes as well as their absolute values by computing the photon and pion couplings simultaneously. We have found that the effect of deformation on the transition amplitudes is rather weak. The difficulty in reproducing the empirical amplitude of the Roper state is discussed.

## Key claim

The deformed oscillator quark (DOQ) model, which describes baryon excited states as rotational bands of deformed intrinsic states, predicts that the effect of deformation on electromagnetic transition amplitudes is rather weak, and the model has difficulty reproducing the empirical amplitude of the Roper resonance.

## Method

The authors computed electromagnetic transition amplitudes for excited baryons in the deformed oscillator quark model by first calculating matrix elements in a multipole basis, then relating these to the commonly used helicity amplitudes. They paid special attention to the sign of the amplitudes by computing photon and pion couplings simultaneously, since experimental sign determination requires knowledge of both couplings. The wave functions for deformed intrinsic states were constructed from single-particle deformed oscillator wave functions, and angular momentum projection was performed using the projection-after-variation (PAV) method.

## Key numbers

* The oscillator parameter ω = 644 MeV was determined from the average mass splitting between the first excited 1/2+ states (Roper-like) and ground states for flavor SU(3) baryons.
* For the N=1 negative parity band (prolate deformation), the intrinsic energy is 3.780 ω, compared to 4 ω for the spherical quark model, with a deformation ratio of short to long axes 1:2.
* For the N=2 positive parity band (prolate deformation), the intrinsic energy is 4.327 ω, compared to 5 ω for the spherical state, with a deformation ratio of short to long axes 1:3.
* The rotational band head for the N=2 band (l=0) lies at 0.750 ω = 483 MeV above the ground state, while the N=1 band head (l=1) lies at 0.654 ω = 421 MeV above the ground state.
* The moment of inertia for the N=1 band is 1/(2I) = 0.126 ω, and for the N=2 band is 1/(2I) = 0.0721 ω.

## Context

This work extends the deformed oscillator quark model previously developed by the same authors (Hosaka, Takayama, and Toki, 1999) which successfully described approximately 80% of observed baryon states as rotational bands. The model contrasts with the non-relativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl (1978) and the chiral quark model of Glozman (1996), which rely on residual interactions (one-gluon exchange or Nambu-Goldstone boson exchange) for hyperfine splittings. The present study enables future comparison of the DOQ model predictions with experimental helicity amplitudes from facilities such as TJNAL, COSY, CELCIUS, and SPring8.
