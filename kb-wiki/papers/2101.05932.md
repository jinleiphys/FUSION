# The chiral phase transition and equation of state in the chiral imbalance

> The chiral phase transition and equation of state are studied within a new self-consistent mean-field approximation of the two-flavor Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model. In this newly developed model, modifications to the chemical potential $μ$ and chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ is naturally included by adding vector and axial-vector channels from Fierz-transformed Lagrangian to the standard Lagrangian. In proper-time scheme, the chiral phase transition is a crossover in the $T-μ$ plane. But when $μ_5$ is increased, our study shows that there may exist first order phase transition. Furthermore, the chiral imbalance will soften the equation of state of quark matter. The mass-radius relations and tidal deformability of quark stars are calculated. As $μ_5$ increases, the maximum mass and radius decrease. The vector channel and axial-vector channel have opposite influence on the equation of state. However, when EOS is constrained by astronomical observations, the shape of the mass-radius curve can be used to determine whether there is chiral imbalance in the dense object, and thus indirectly proving the CP violation in the dense matter. Our study shows a different influence of the chiral imbalance on the chiral phase transition in contrary to tree-momentum-cutoff scheme.

## Key claim

Within a self-consistent mean-field approximation of the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model using proper-time regularization, increasing the chiral chemical potential μ₅ can induce a first-order chiral phase transition where none exists at μ₅=0 (the chirally balanced system shows only crossover at any temperature and chemical potential in this regularization scheme). The chiral imbalance softens the equation of state of quark matter, and the shape of the mass-radius curve for quark stars may serve as an indirect probe of CP violation in dense matter, provided other parameters such as magnetic field strength and rotation speed are well understood.

## Method

The authors construct a mixed Lagrangian as a linear combination of the standard two-flavor NJL Lagrangian and its Fierz transformation, weighted by a parameter α, which naturally introduces vector and axial-vector interaction channels. Under mean-field approximation, the modified chemical potential μᵣ and modified chiral chemical potential μ₅ᵣ are defined with contributions from the quark number density n and chiral number density n₅, respectively. The chiral condensate, quark number density, and chiral number density are computed using proper-time regularization with a UV cutoff Λ_UV = 955 MeV, with parameters fixed to reproduce the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, m = 3.5 MeV). The equation of state is obtained via integration of the number density, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities of quark stars.

## Context

This work extends studies of the chiral phase transition in the NJL model with chiral chemical potential, previously investigated by Ruggieri (2011) and Lu et al. (2016) using three-momentum cutoff regularization, where inverse chiral catalysis was observed. The authors use a newly developed self-consistent mean-field approach (Wang, Zhao, and Zong, 2019; Zhao et al., 2019) with proper-time regularization, which yields qualitatively different results: chiral catalysis rather than inverse catalysis, and the appearance of a first-order transition only when μ₅ is nonzero. The study enables future work connecting chiral imbalance effects to compact star observables, suggesting that precise radius measurements combined with tidal deformability constraints (e.g., the range 70 < Λ(1.4 M_⊙) < 580 from LIGO/Virgo observations) could indirectly test for CP violation in dense matter.
