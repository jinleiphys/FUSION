# Relativistic Nuclear Matter with Self-Consistent Correlation Energy

> We study relativistic nuclear matter in the $σ- ω$ model including the ring-sum correlation energy. The model parameters are adjusted self-consistently to give the canonical saturation density and binding energy per nucleon with the ring energy included. Two models are considered, mean-field-theory where we neglect vacuum effects, and the relativistic Hartree approximation where such effects are included but in an approximate way. In both cases we find self-consistent solutions and present equations of state. In the mean-field case the ring energy completely dominates the attractive part of the energy density and the elegant saturation mechanism of the standard approach is lost, namely relativistic quenching of the scalar attraction. In the relativistic Hartree approach the vacuum effects are included in an approximate manner using vertex form factors with a cutoff of 1 - 2 GeV, the range expected from QCD. Due to the cutoff, the ring energy for this case is significantlysmaller, and we obtain self-consistent solutions which preserve the basic saturation mechanism of the standard relativistic approach.

# Relativistic Nuclear Matter with Self-Consistent Correlation Energy

## Key claim

Including the ring-sum correlation energy self-consistently in the relativistic sigma-omega model can produce stable nuclear matter solutions, but the saturation mechanism depends critically on whether vacuum effects are included with a vertex cutoff. In the mean-field case the ring energy dominates the attraction and destroys the standard relativistic saturation mechanism, while in the relativistic Hartree approximation with a vertex cutoff of 1-2 GeV the ring energy remains a small correction and the standard saturation mechanism is preserved.

## Method

The authors calculated the ring-sum correlation energy density for nuclear matter in the QHD-I (sigma-omega) model using analytic forms for the scalar-vector polarizations and performing a Wick rotation to Euclidean momentum space. They then included this ring energy in the total energy density and refitted the meson-nucleon coupling constants self-consistently to reproduce the canonical saturation density (k_F = 1.42 fm^-1) and binding energy per nucleon (-15.75 MeV), while maintaining the self-consistency condition M* = M - (g_s^2/m_s^2) rho_s. Two approaches were considered: mean-field theory (MFT) neglecting vacuum effects, and the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) including vacuum effects with vertex form factors using a standard dipole form with cutoffs of 1 GeV and 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Ring energy per nucleon using mean-field parameters (without self-consistent fit): -33 MeV per nucleon, confirming the calculation by Ji (1988)
- Self-consistent MFT solution: ring energy of -58.4 MeV per nucleon at saturation, compressibility 304 MeV, M*/M = 0.791, with coupling constants g_s^2 = 39.6 and g_v^2 = 104.7
- Self-consistent RHA with 1 GeV cutoff: ring energy of -3.4 MeV per nucleon at saturation, compressibility 503 MeV, M*/M = 0.722, with g_s^2 = 55.1 and g_v^2 = 88.5
- Self-consistent RHA with 2 GeV cutoff: ring energy of -12.5 MeV per nucleon at saturation, compressibility 521 MeV, M*/M = 0.724, with g_s^2 = 54.5 and g_v^2 = 94.9
- Standard MFT (without ring energy fit) gives compressibility ~450 MeV and M*/M = 0.54
- For RHA with sigma mass of 600 MeV and 2 GeV cutoff: compressibility increases to ~600 MeV, M*/M drops to ~0.71

## Context

This work extends the QHD-I model of Walecka (1986) by including the ring-sum correlation energy, which had been previously calculated by Ji (1988) as a large -33 MeV per nucleon effect using mean-field parameters. The authors note that lattice QHD calculations by Brockmann and Frank (1992) suggest all quantum corrections beyond MFT change the binding energy by about -85 MeV per nucleon, meaning the ring sum represents roughly 40% of these corrections. The study addresses the convergence problems of the loop expansion identified by Furnstahl, Perry, and Serot (1989), where two-loop solutions differ radically from one-loop results. By introducing vertex form factors with cutoffs on the scale of 1-2 GeV, motivated by the expected onset of subnucleon degrees of freedom from QCD, the authors show that a credible phenomenology can be maintained. The correlation between larger compressibility and smaller M*/M seen here was previously noted by Furnstahl, Price, and Walker (1987) in non-linear QHD models. This work enables future studies of whether finite nuclear properties such as spin-orbit splitting can be consistently described with the ring-sum correlations included.
