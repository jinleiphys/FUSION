# An explanation of the ABC enhancement in the dd -> alpha X reaction at intermediate energies

> The dd -> alpha X reaction is studied in a model where each pair of nucleons in the projectile and target deuterons undergoes pion production through the NN -> d pi reaction. The condition that the two deuterons fuse to form an alpha-particle then leads to peaks at small missing masses, the well-known ABC enhancement, but also a broad structure around the maximum missing mass. With a simplified input amplitude the model gives a quantitative description of both the alpha-particle momentum and angular distributions for a deuteron beam energy of 1250 MeV.

## Key claim

The ABC enhancement in the dd → αX reaction can be explained as a kinematic effect arising from two independent, sequential NN → dπ reactions involving different nucleon pairs from the projectile and target deuterons, rather than from an exotic ππ interaction; the model gives a quantitative description of the α-particle momentum and angular distributions at 1250 MeV after dividing the predicted cross sections by an overall factor of two.

## Method

The authors model double-pion production in dd → απ⁺π⁻ via a Feynman diagram where each of two nucleon pairs (one from each initial deuteron) undergoes the NN → dπ reaction through the dominant ¹D₂p transition amplitude, with the two final deuterons fusing to form an α-particle. The matrix element is evaluated in a linear Fermi-momentum approximation, using S-state Paris wave functions for the deuterons and an α:dd cluster wave function, with the energy denominator reducing to a δ-function that enforces kinematic constraints. The differential cross section is computed by integrating over the ππ rest frame and smearing over the experimental momentum resolution (σ = 10 MeV/c).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energy: 1250 MeV (the only energy for which angular distributions were measured by the Saclay group).
- ABC peak corresponds to missing masses M_X ∼ 300 MeV.
- The model predictions are a factor of two too high compared to data; after dividing by this factor, agreement is "remarkably good."
- At a laboratory angle of 11°, the c.m. angle in the ABC peak is about 45°, and the predictions follow a change of about a factor of 2.5 in c.m. differential cross section.
- The interval of deuteron kinetic energies T_d = 787–1412 MeV maps onto a proton range T_p = 410–720 MeV for the pp → dπ⁺ input.
- The α:dd wave function is normalised to N_α² = 2.3, based on a scale factor of 4.7 between deuteron and α-particle nucleon-nucleon distributions for separations r_np < 2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the kinematic-enhancement approach of Risser and Shuster (1970s) for the np → dX reaction, who used double Δ-resonance excitation via pion exchange but had limited quantitative success, possibly due to neglect of spin. The model explains the ABC effect originally observed by Abashian, Booth, and Crowe (1960) in pd → ³He X and np → dX reactions, where the low missing-mass peak (I=0, two-pion system) appears most clearly in dd collisions because it yields a pure I=0 channel. The results enable future work incorporating a fuller set of pp → dπ⁺ amplitudes to predict deuteron analysing powers measured at Saclay's SPESIII spectrometer, and suggest that exclusive dd → απ⁺π⁻ measurements could test the predicted (3cos²θ_π + 1)² angular distribution in the central bump region.
