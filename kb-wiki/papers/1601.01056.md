# Neutrino-Deuteron Reactions in the $Δ$(1232) Region

> The results from an investigation of the incoherent single pion electroweak production on the deuteron in the $Δ$(1232) region are reported. The importance of including the nucleon-nucleon final state interactions in extracting the neutrino-nucleon cross sections from the data obtained from the experiments on the deuteron target is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The spectator approximation used in previous analyses to extract neutrino-nucleon cross sections from deuteron-target data is not valid for the $\nu + d \to \mu^- + \pi^+ + n + p$ process, because nucleon-nucleon final state interactions (especially $np$ rescattering) significantly alter the cross sections; however, the spectator approximation remains a good approximation for the $\nu + d \to \mu^- + \pi^0 + p + p$ process due to weak $pp$ final state interactions.

## Method

The author constructed a dynamical model for electroweak single-pion production on the nucleon using a Hamiltonian with $\pi N$, $\gamma N$, and $W^\pm N$ interactions, including both non-resonant (tree-diagram) and resonant ($\Delta$ excitation) terms, with parameters fit to data on $\pi N$ scattering, photo- and electroproduction, and neutrino-induced pion production. For the deuteron target, the incoherent pion production amplitudes were calculated as a sum of an impulse term, a nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) final state interaction term, and a $\pi N$ final state interaction term, using the single-nucleon amplitudes and $NN$ scattering amplitudes from realistic potentials. The model was first tested against $\gamma + d \to N + N + \pi$ data, then used to predict $\nu + d \to \mu + \pi + N + N$ cross sections under spectator kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The bare mass of the $\Delta$ resonance used in the model is $m^0_\Delta = 1299$ MeV.
- For the $\nu_\mu + d \to \mu^- + \pi^+ + p + n$ process, differential cross sections $d\sigma / dE_{\mu^-} d\Omega_{\nu_\mu\,\mu^-} d\Omega_{\pi^+} d^3\vec{p}_N$ were calculated as a function of pion scattering angle for outgoing muon energies $E_{\mu^-} = 550$, 600, and 650 MeV.
- The $np$ final state interaction in $\gamma + d \to \pi^0 + n + p$ greatly reduces the total cross section from the impulse-only calculation (dashed curve) to the impulse-plus-$NN$ calculation (dot-dashed curve), while $\pi N$ rescattering gives negligible contributions.
- For $\gamma + d \to \pi^- + p + p$, both $pp$ and $\pi N$ final state interactions are weak, with little change from the impulse-only calculation.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior dynamical model for electroweak pion production on the nucleon (Lee, Refs. sl1, sl2, sl3) to the deuteron target, and tests the spectator approximation used in earlier experimental analyses from Argonne National Laboratory (ANL), Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL), and BEBC-CERN (references cam73, bar79, rad82, kit86, kit90, all80, all90). The results for $\gamma + d$ reactions are consistent with calculations by Darwish, Fix, Lev, Schuck, and Tarasov. The findings indicate that previous extractions of neutrino-neutron cross sections from deuteron data for charged-current $\pi^+$ production need re-analysis, while $\pi^0$ production extractions are reliable. This enables more accurate determination of neutrino-nucleon cross sections for future neutrino oscillation experiments.
