# Resonances and Reactions from Mean-Field Dynamics

> The time-dependent version of nuclear density functional theory, using functionals derived from Skyrme interactions, is able to approximately describe nuclear dynamics. We present time-dependent results of calculations of dipole resonances, concentrating on excitations of valence neutrons against a proton plus neutron core in the neutron-rich doubly-magic $^{132}$Sn nucleus, and results of collision dynamics, highlighting potential routes to ternary fusion, with the example of a collision of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb resulting in a compound nucleus of element 120 stable against immiedate fission

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations can approximately describe nuclear dynamics, but the results for dipole resonances in $^{132}$Sn are fragmented compared to experimental data due to reflecting boundary conditions, and the ternary fusion results for $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb are "indicative only" that the method should not be ruled out, requiring further detailed studies for a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations were solved using Skyrme energy density functionals, with the KDE0v1 functional for dipole response calculations in $^{132}$Sn and the SLy4d parameterisation for ternary fusion simulations. Dipole boosts of different types were applied to the ground state of $^{132}$Sn to excite specific modes: a standard isovector dipole boost, a boost of all neutrons above the N=50 core against the core, and a boost of only the $\nu h_{11/2}$ orbital against all other nucleons. For ternary fusion, two $^{48}$Ca nuclei with initial kinetic energies of 80 MeV each were directed toward a central $^{208}$Pb nucleus from opposite sides in a 24 fm $\times$ 24 fm $\times$ 60 fm box.

## Key numbers

- Dipole response in $^{132}$Sn: experimental E1 strength shows a low-lying peak at 9.8 MeV and a main GDR peak around 17 MeV (Adr05)
- Time step used in TDHF evolution: $\Delta t \simeq 0.2$ fm/c
- Box size for ternary fusion simulation: 24 fm $\times$ 24 fm $\times$ 60 fm
- Initial positions of calcium nuclei: $(0,0,-22\ \mathrm{fm})$ and $(0,0,18\ \mathrm{fm})$
- Initial kinetic energy of each calcium nucleus: 80 MeV, directed toward the lead nucleus
- Contour steps for density plots: 0.02 fm$^{-3}$, with innermost contour at 0.14 fm$^{-3}$
- Snapshot time for dipole dynamics: t=7300 fm/c (24.0 zs)

## Context

This work extends the TDHF approach previously applied to fusion, quasi-fission (Toh02, Sim04, Uma06, Sim07, Sim08, Sim08b, Uma08, Uma10, Uma12, Ste15, Uma15), fission (Neg78, Sim14, God15), and collective excitations (Str79, Uma86, Cho87, Sim03, Ste04, Nak05, Uma05, Alm05, Ste07, Sim09, Fra12, Kla13, Ste14). The dipole response calculations in $^{132}$Sn build on experimental measurements by Adr05 and earlier TDHF and RPA work by Ste07, Par13, and Par14. The ternary fusion proposal suggests a potential route to element 120 using only stable beams and targets, in contrast to the radioactive targets used for the heaviest elements synthesised to date (e.g., $^{249}$Bk target for element 117 by Oga10).
