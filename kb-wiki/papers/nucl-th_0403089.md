# Production of light antinuclei in heavy ion collisions

> The antideuteron and antihelium-3 production rates at high-energy heavy ion collisions are calculated in the framework of fusion mechanism. It is supposed, that anti-p,n,d,^3He participating in the fusion are moving in the mean field of other fireball constituents. It is demonstrated that at high energies, where many pions are present in the fireball, the number of produced anti-d and ^3He is determined by the balance between created and desintegrated (mainly in collisions with pions) anti-d and ^3He. The explicit formulae for coalescence parameters are presented and compared with the data.

## Key claim

The production rates of antideuterons and antihelium-3 in high-energy heavy ion collisions are determined by a balance between fusion creation and desintegration (mainly in collisions with pions) rather than by statistical or thermal equilibrium, with the coalescence parameter acquiring a factor of \((\varepsilon/\Gamma)^{1/2}\sim 1/10\) due to in-medium interactions that previous sudden-approximation approaches neglected.

## Method

The authors construct effective low-energy Lagrangians for the fusion reactions \(\bar{p}+\bar{n}\to\bar{d}\) and \(\bar{p}+\bar{p}+\bar{n}\to{}^{3}\overline{\mathrm{He}}\), determining the effective coupling constants from the Bethe-Peierls scattering theory and from the deuteron/\({}^{3}\overline{\mathrm{He}}\) polarization operator in field theory. They formulate a transport (kinetic) equation for antinucleus production and propagation in the fireball, treating \(\bar{p},\bar{n},\bar{d},{}^{3}\overline{\mathrm{He}}\) as Breit-Wigner resonances moving in the mean field of other fireball constituents, with widths and mass shifts calculated from forward scattering amplitudes on pions and nucleons. The balance condition equates the fusion production rate to the desintegration rate (dominated by \(\pi+\bar{d}\) or \(\pi+{}^{3}\overline{\mathrm{He}}\) collisions), yielding explicit formulae for the coalescence parameters \(B_2\) and \(B_3\).

## Key numbers

* Antideuteron binding energy: \(\varepsilon = 2.225\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Effective coupling constant for \(\bar{p}+\bar{n}\to\bar{d}\): \(g^2 = 128\pi\, m\sqrt{m\varepsilon}\,(1-\alpha r_0)^{-1}\) with \((1-\alpha r_0)^{-1}\approx 1.67\).
* Antinucleon in-medium width: \(\Gamma \approx 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (after Glauber correction) at SPS energies, with \(\Gamma_{\bar{p}}=\Gamma_{\bar{n}}\approx 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\Delta m\approx 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Coalescence parameter \(B_2^{\mathrm{exp}}\) for NA44 (Pb+Pb, \(\sqrt{s}=17.4A\ \mathrm{GeV}\), centrality 0–10%): \((4.4\pm 1.3)\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* Coalescence parameter \(B_2^{\mathrm{exp}}\) for STAR (Au+Au, \(\sqrt{s}=130A\ \mathrm{GeV}\), centrality 0–18%): \((4.5\pm 0.3\pm 1.0)\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* Coalescence parameter \(B_2^{\mathrm{exp}}\) for E864 (Au+Pb, \(\sqrt{s}=4.8A\ \mathrm{GeV}\), centrality 0–10%): \((41\pm 29\pm 23)\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* Theoretical \(B_2^{\mathrm{th}}\) for NA44 with \(n_N^0=0.24\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(4.00\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (fireball volume \(V=6.03\times 10^3\ \mathrm{fm}^3\), \(\Gamma=187\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
* Theoretical \(B_2^{\mathrm{th}}\) for STAR with \(n_N^0=0.24\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(4.22\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (\(V=7.76\times 10^3\ \mathrm{fm}^3\), \(\Gamma=206\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
* Theoretical \(B_2^{\mathrm{th}}\) for E864 with \(n_N^0=0.24\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(13.9\times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (\(V=3.02\times 10^3\ \mathrm{fm}^3\), \(\Gamma=142\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
* Effective coupling constant for \({}^{3}\overline{\mathrm{He}}\): \(G^2 = 36\sqrt{3}\,(4\pi)^3\, m/\Lambda\) with cutoff \(\Lambda=300\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Estimated energy density at the dense gas stage: \(\epsilon \sim 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^3\) to \(0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the fusion (coalescence) mechanism for light nuclei production, previously applied to deuterons and helium in approaches such as the sudden approximation of Butler and Pearson (1963) and the thermodynamic model of Mekjian (1978), by explicitly including the interaction of antinuclei with the fireball environment, particularly pions, which was neglected in prior studies. The authors show that the in-medium width \(\Gamma\) introduces a factor \((\varepsilon/\Gamma)^{1/2}\sim 1/10\) into the coalescence parameter, in contrast to the factor \((\varepsilon\tau)^{1/2}\sim 1\) from the time-dependent perturbation theory of Kapusta (1980). The results enable estimation of the fireball volume and hadron densities at the dense gas stage (just after hadronization) from experimental antinucleus data, and provide a check for models of fireball evolution such as the Fermi–Pomeranchuk model.
