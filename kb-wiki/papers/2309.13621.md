# A New Model for Jet Energy Loss in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a new model for jet quenching from coherent radiation in a brick medium. The jet energy loss is simulated as a perturbative final-state vacuum parton shower followed by a medium-induced shower originating from elastic and radiative collisions with the medium constituents. Coherency is achieved by starting with trial gluons that act as field dressing of the initial jet parton. These are formed according to a Gunion-Bertsch seed. The QCD version of the LPM effect is attained by increasing the phase of the trial gluons through elastic scatterings with the medium. Above a phase threshold, the trial gluons will be realised and can produce coherent radiation themselves. The model has been implemented in a Monte Carlo code and has been validated by successfully reproducing the BDMPS-Z prediction for the energy spectrum. The realistic case with minimal assumptions are also produced and shown. In particular, we show the influence of various parameters on the energy spectrum and transverse momentum distribution, such as the in-medium quark masses, the energy transfer in the recoil process, and the phase accumulation criteria, especially for low and intermediate energy gluons. Future studies will allow for the interface with full simulations of the quark-gluon-plasma with hydrodynamic evolution, such as vHLLE, along with subsequent hadronisation of the jet partons in order to produce realistic distributions that can be directly compared to LHC and RHIC data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new Monte Carlo model (SUBA-Jet) for jet energy loss that achieves coherency by using trial gluons formed from a Gunion-Bertsch seed, with phase accumulation through elastic scatterings; the model successfully reproduces the BDMPS-Z prediction for the energy spectrum only under the specific conditions of a static brick medium with constant temperature and fixed coupling.

## Method

The algorithm operates in two regimes: a high virtuality regime using a DGLAP-based vacuum parton shower with medium-induced virtuality increases parameterized by \(\hat{q}(p,T)\), and a low virtuality regime where on-shell partons undergo elastic scatterings and radiative interactions. In the low virtuality regime, trial gluons are seeded via the Gunion-Bertsch cross-section, then accumulate phase through elastic scatterings; when the phase reaches a critical threshold \(\phi_c\), the trial gluon is realised with probability \(1/N_s\), where \(N_s\) is the number of elastic scatterings during the formation time, thereby reproducing the LPM effect. The model is validated against a static brick medium with constant temperature \(T = 400\) MeV, fixed \(\alpha_s = 0.4\), path length \(L = 4\) fm, and mono-energetic quark guns with \(E_{\text{ini}} = 100\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Static brick medium conditions: temperature \(T = 400\) MeV, fixed strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.4\), path length \(L = 4\) fm, initial quark energy \(E_{\text{ini}} = 100\) GeV.
- Before LPM evolution, SUBA-Jet reproduces the Gunion-Bertsch \(\omega^{-1}\) energy spectrum and \(k_T^{-4}\) transverse momentum spectrum at large \(k_T\).
- After LPM evolution, SUBA-Jet reproduces the BDMPS-Z \(\omega^{-3/2}\) energy spectrum for intermediate energies and the expected \(k_T^{-2}\) behaviour for large \(k_T\).
- The average number of emitted gluons per jet is around 3 to 4.
- In the high virtuality regime, after roughly 1 to 2 fm/\(c\), around 80% of initial tagged quarks have reached the minimum virtuality \(Q_0 \sim \lambda_{\text{QCD}}\).
- The Gunion-Bertsch energy spectrum is given by \(\omega\frac{\mathrm{d}N^{\text{GB}}}{\mathrm{d}\omega} \simeq \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{\pi} \frac{L}{\lambda}\), where \(C_R\) is the colour factor and \(\lambda\) is the Coulomb mean free path.
- The BDMPS-Z energy spectrum for \(\omega \ll \omega_c\) is \(\omega\frac{\mathrm{d}N^{\text{BDMPS-Z}}}{\mathrm{d}\omega} \simeq \frac{2\alpha_s C_R}{\pi} \sqrt{\frac{\omega_c}{2\omega}}\), with \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2}\hat q L^2\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of JEWEL (Zapp 2011) by using a similar method for achieving coherency through phase accumulation and trial gluon realisation, while also incorporating elements from YaJEM (Renk 2008) for medium modifications in the high virtuality regime. The model enables future comparisons to LHC and RHIC data by allowing interface with full hydrodynamic simulations such as vHLLE (Karpenko 2013) and the EPOS4 framework (Werner 2023), which will provide realistic initial states, hydrodynamic evolution, and hadronisation.
