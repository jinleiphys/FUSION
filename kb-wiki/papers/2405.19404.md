# Dihadron correlations in small-$x$ DIS at NLO: transverse momentum dependent fragmentation

> We compute inclusive dihadron cross-section in Deep Inelastic Scattering at next-to-leading order (NLO) and small $x$ in the Color Glass Condensate. We focus on the kinematic limit where the hadrons are produced at forward rapidities (in the direction of the virtual photon) and back-to-back in the transverse plane. Our calculation demonstrates that the coefficient of the Sudakov double logarithm for this process is $-\frac{α_s}{2π}\left[C_F+\frac{N_c}{2}\right]$ instead of $-\frac{α_s N_c}{4π}$ when back-to-back jets are measured in the final state. To preserve the universality of the Sudakov soft factor associated with the Weizsäcker-Williams transverse momentum dependent (TMD) gluon distribution, we promote the collinear fragmentation functions into TMD fragmentation functions. We then perform the resummation of the Sudakov logarithms through Collins-Soper-Sterman evolution of the TMD fragmentation functions and the Weizsäcker-Williams TMD gluon distribution. Finally, analytic expressions are obtained for the NLO coefficient functions in the $\overline{\rm MS}$-scheme. These results pave the way towards numerically calculating dihadron correlations at small $x$ at the future Electron-Ion Collider with full NLO accuracy.

## Key claim
The coefficient of the Sudakov double logarithm for inclusive dihadron production in small-$x$ DIS is $-\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi}\left[C_F+\frac{N_c}{2}\right]$, which differs from the $-\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{4\pi}$ coefficient obtained for back-to-back dijet production, requiring the promotion of collinear fragmentation functions to transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions to preserve universality of the Sudakov soft factor.

## Method
The authors computed the NLO corrections to the inclusive dihadron cross-section in Deep Inelastic Scattering at small $x$ within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, focusing on the back-to-back limit where hadrons are produced at forward rapidities. They isolated the leading Sudakov double and single logarithms from the NLO impact factor, performed the resummation through Collins-Soper-Sterman evolution of TMD fragmentation functions and the Weizsacker-Williams TMD gluon distribution, and obtained analytic expressions for the NLO coefficient functions in the $\overline{\rm MS}$-scheme.

## Key numbers
- The Sudakov double logarithm coefficient for dihadron production is $-\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi}\left[C_F+\frac{N_c}{2}\right]$, compared to $-\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{4\pi}$ for dijet production
- The LO hard factor for longitudinally polarized photons is $16 z_1^3 z_2^3 Q^2 \frac{\Pt^i \Pt^j}{(\Pt^2+z_1z_2 Q^2)^4}$
- The LO hard factor for transversely polarized photons is $z_1z_2(z_1^2+z_2^2) \left[\frac{\delta^{ij}}{(\Pt^2+z_1z_2Q^2)^2}-\frac{4z_1z_2Q^2\Pt^i\Pt^j}{(\Pt^2+z_1 z_2Q^2)^4}\right]$
- The NLO finite piece from combining real and virtual contributions includes terms $\frac{1}{2}\ln^2\left(\frac{z_2}{z_1}\right)-\frac{\pi^2}{6}+\frac{5}{2}$

## Context
This work extends previous LO calculations of dihadron correlations at small $x$ by Stasto (2018) and Benic (2022), which used LO results in the CGC supplemented with a Sudakov soft factor borrowed from TMD collinear factorization. It also builds on NLO calculations for jet-defined final states by Caucal (2023) and Mueller (2013). The results enable future phenomenological studies of back-to-back dihadron correlations at small $x$ with full NLO accuracy at the Electron-Ion Collider, where hadron measurements at low $p_t$ should be more accessible than dijet measurements.
