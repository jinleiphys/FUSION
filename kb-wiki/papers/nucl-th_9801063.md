# Resonance model study of kaon production in baryon baryon reactions for heavy ion collisions

> The energy dependence of the total kaon production cross sections in baryon baryon ($N$ and $Δ$) collisions are studied in the resonance model, which is a relativistic, tree-level treatment. This study is the first attempt to complete a systematic, consistent investigation of the elementary kaon production reactions for both the pion baryon and baryon baryon reactions. Our model suggests that the magnitudes of the isospin-averaged total cross sections for the $N N \to N Y K$ and $ΔN \to N Y K$ ($Y = Λ$ or $Σ$) reactions are almost equal at energies up to about 200 MeV above threshold. However, the magnitudes for the $ΔN$ reactions become about 6 times larger than those for the $N N$ reactions at energies about 1 GeV above threshold. Furthermore, the magnitudes of the isospin-averaged total cross sections for the $N N \to ΔY K$ reactions turn out to be comparable to those for the $N N \to N Y K$ reactions at $N N$ invariant collision energies about 3.1 GeV, and about 5 to 10 times larger at $N N$ invariant collision energies about 3.5 GeV. The microscopic cross sections are parametrized in all isospin channels necessary for the transport model studies of kaon production in heavy ion collisions. These cross sections are then applied in the relativistic transport model to study the sensitivity to the underlying elementary kaon production cross sections.

# Resonance model study of kaon production in baryon baryon reactions for heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The resonance model suggests that the isospin-averaged total cross sections for $NN \to NYK$ and $\Delta N \to NYK$ reactions are almost equal at energies up to about 200 MeV above threshold, but the $\Delta N$ reactions become about 6 times larger than the $NN$ reactions at energies about 1 GeV above threshold. The model provides the first systematic, consistent investigation of elementary kaon production cross sections for both pion baryon and baryon baryon reactions, though it neglects final state interactions and interference terms, so results should not be taken seriously near threshold.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic, tree-level resonance model incorporating baryon resonances with masses up to about 2 GeV (N(1650), N(1710), N(1720), and an effective $\Delta$(1920) resonance) that decay to hyperon and kaon. They calculated total cross sections for $NN \to NYK$, $\Delta N \to NYK$, and $NN \to \Delta YK$ reactions using effective Lagrangians with $\pi$, $\eta$, and $\rho$ meson exchanges, with coupling constants determined from experimental branching ratios and form factors with cut-off parameters fixed from earlier studies of $\pi B \to YK$ and $pp$ reactions. The resulting cross sections were parametrized for all isospin channels and applied in a relativistic transport model for heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-averaged total cross sections for $NN \to NYK$ and $\Delta N \to NYK$ are almost equal at energies up to about 200 MeV above threshold
- $\Delta N$ reaction cross sections become about 6 times larger than $NN$ reaction cross sections at energies about 1 GeV above threshold
- $NN \to \Delta YK$ cross sections are comparable to $NN \to NYK$ at $NN$ invariant collision energies about 3.1 GeV
- $NN \to \Delta YK$ cross sections are about 5 to 10 times larger than $NN \to NYK$ at $NN$ invariant collision energies about 3.5 GeV
- For the $pp \to p\Lambda K^+$ reaction, the best fit to experimental data used $g^2_{\rho NN}/4\pi = 0.74$ and $\Lambda_\rho = 920$ MeV
- The $\pi NN$ cut-off parameter was adopted as $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 1050$ MeV with $g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 14.4$
- The model overestimates $pn \to p\Lambda K^0$ data by roughly a factor of 2, and at most a factor of 7 for one data point

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic resonance model studies of $\pi B \to YK$ reactions (Tsushima et al., 1997) and $pp \to NYK$ reactions (Tsushima et al., 1997) to include all baryon baryon channels involving $\Delta$ resonances. It contradicts earlier assumptions that $\Delta N$ and $\Delta\Delta$ collisions dominate $K^+$ production in heavy ion collisions (as in work by Randrup and Ko, 1980; Hua and Liu, 1990; Hartnack et al., 1994; Balis et al., 1995), and supports findings by Cassing et al. (1997) and Fuchs et al. (1997) that secondary $\pi N \to YK$ reactions give comparable contributions. The parametrized cross sections enable improved transport model studies of kaon production in heavy ion collisions, particularly for probing nuclear matter properties at high density.
