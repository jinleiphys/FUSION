# Proton structure corrections to electronic and muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting

> We present a precise determination of the polarizability and other proton structure dependent contributions to the hydrogen hyperfine splitting, based heavily on the most recent published data on proton spin dependent structure functions from the EG1 experiment at the Jefferson Laboratory. As a result, the total calculated hyperfine splitting now has a standard deviation slightly under 1 part-per-million, and is about 1 standard deviation away from the measured value. We also present results for muonic hydrogen hyperfine splitting, taking care to ensure the compatibility of the recoil and polarizability terms.

## Key claim

The total calculated hyperfine splitting for ordinary hydrogen now has a standard deviation slightly under 1 part-per-million and is about 1 standard deviation away from the measured value, with no evidence of missing physics at this level. The uncertainty in the polarizability corrections has become comparable to the uncertainty in the elastic structure dependent terms, a significant improvement over previous analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a dispersive calculation of the two-photon exchange diagram to determine proton structure dependent corrections to the hyperfine splitting, separating these into Zemach, recoil, and polarizability contributions. For the polarizability corrections, they used the most recent published data on proton spin-dependent structure functions from the EG1 experiment at Jefferson Laboratory, which extends measurements of g1 down to much lower Q2 than previously available, combined with fits from Simula et al. (2001) in the resonance region and from SLAC E155 in the scaling region. For the elastic form factors, they used modern parameterizations from Arrington, Melnitchouck, and Tjon (2007) and others, and they carefully ensured compatibility between recoil and polarizability terms for the muonic hydrogen case where different protocols exist.

## Key numbers

- Experimental hyperfine splitting of hydrogen ground state: 1,420.405,751,766.7 (9) MHz
- Polarizability correction for electronic hydrogen: 1.88 ± 0.64 ppm
- Zemach correction for electronic hydrogen (using AMT form factor): -41.43 ± 0.44 ppm
- Recoil correction for electronic hydrogen (using AMT form factor): 5.85 ± 0.07 ppm
- Total calculated hyperfine splitting correction for electronic hydrogen: 1102.63 ± 0.78 ppm, with a deficit of 0.85 ± 0.78 ppm compared to experiment
- Polarizability correction for muonic hydrogen: 351 ± 114 ppm
- Zemach correction for muonic hydrogen (using AMT form factor): -7703 ppm
- Recoil correction for muonic hydrogen (using AMT form factor): 931 ppm
- Summed structure dependent correction for muonic hydrogen (using AMT form factor): -6421 ppm

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same authors (Nazaryan, 2005) and by Faustov and Martynenko (2002, 2006), using improved data from the EG1 experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (Prok, 2008) that provides g1 measurements down to much lower Q2 than previously available. The new analysis reduces the uncertainty in the polarizability correction to a level comparable to the uncertainty from elastic form factors, enabling a total calculated hyperfine splitting with uncertainty under 1 ppm. For muonic hydrogen, where no measurement currently exists but one may be possible (Antognini, 2005), the authors provide calculated results while carefully addressing the protocol dependence of separating recoil and polarizability corrections for massive leptons.
