# S-wave kaon-nucleon potentials with all-to-all propagators in the HAL QCD method

> In this paper, employing an all-to-all quark propagator technique, we investigate the kaon-nucleon interactions in lattice QCD. We calculate the S-wave kaon-nucleon potentials at the leading order in the derivative expansion in the time-dependent HAL QCD method, using (2+1)-flavor gauge configurations at the lattice spacing $a \approx 0.09$ fm on $32^3 \times 64$ lattices and the pion mass $m_π \approx 570$ MeV. We take the one-end trick for all-to-all propagators, which allows us to put the zero momentum hadron operators at both source and sink and to smear quark operators at the source. We find the stronger repulsive interaction in the $I=1$ channel than in the $I=0$. The phase shifts obtained by solving the Schrödinger equations with the potentials qualitatively reproduce the energy dependence of the experimental phase shifts, and have the similar behavior to the previous results from lattice QCD without all-to-all propagators. Our study demonstrates that the all-to-all quark propagator technique with the one-end trick is useful to study interactions for meson-baryon systems in the HAL QCD method, so that we will apply it to meson-baryon systems which contain quark-antiquark creation/annihilation processes in our future studies.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the all-to-all quark propagator technique with the one-end trick is useful for studying meson-baryon interactions in the time-dependent HAL QCD method, but the resulting phase shifts are less repulsive than experimental results, likely due to the heavier pion mass (570 MeV) used in the calculation rather than the physical pion mass (140 MeV).

## Method

The authors calculated S-wave kaon-nucleon potentials at leading order in the derivative expansion using the time-dependent HAL QCD method on (2+1)-flavor gauge configurations with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.09 fm on 32³ × 64 lattices. They employed the one-end trick for all-to-all propagators, allowing zero-momentum hadron operators at both source and sink with smeared quark operators at the source, and extracted phase shifts by solving the Schrödinger equation with the fitted potentials.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.09 fm on 32³ × 64 lattices
- Pion mass m_π ≈ 570 MeV
- Kaon mass m_K = 713(1) MeV from 2-point correlation functions
- Nucleon mass m_N = 1405(7) MeV from 2-point correlation functions
- The I=0 potential has a shallow attractive pocket with depth of about 10 MeV at middle distances (0.5 < r < 1.5 fm)
- The I=0 phase shift becomes slightly positive in the low energy region (0 < P_lab < 400 MeV) and turns negative in the higher energy region (400 < P_lab < 1000 MeV)
- The I=1 phase shift differs from previous HAL QCD wall-source results by about 3 degrees in the high energy region

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method to meson-baryon systems using all-to-all propagators, building on previous kaon-nucleon studies with wall sources by Ikeda (2011) and finite volume method calculations by Detmold (2013, 2015) and Torok (2009). The results enable future application to meson-baryon systems containing quark-antiquark creation/annihilation processes, particularly the Δ(1232) resonance from pion-nucleon scatterings, and find no evidence for the Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark in the S-wave kaon-nucleon channels at this quark mass.
