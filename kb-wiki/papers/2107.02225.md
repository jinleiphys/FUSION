# Scattering and gluon emission in a color field: a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We develop a numerical method to nonperturbatively study scattering and gluon emission of a quark from a colored target using a light-front Hamiltonian approach. The target is described as a classical color field, as in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. The Fock space of the scattering system is restricted to the $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ sectors, but the time evolution of this truncated system is solved exactly. This method allows us to study the interplay between coherence and multiple scattering in gluon emission. It could be applied both to studying subeikonal effects in high energy scattering and to understanding jet quenching in a hot plasma.

## Key claim

The paper develops a nonperturbative numerical method to study quark scattering and gluon emission from a colored target, solving the time evolution exactly within a truncated Fock space of $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ sectors. The method explicitly includes the phase rotation from the light-front energy difference, which is neglected in the eikonal limit, allowing the study of coherence effects and formation time physics that the abstract's mention of "interplay between coherence and multiple scattering" glosses over.

## Method

The authors use time-dependent basis light-front quantization (tBLFQ) to evolve a quark state through a classical SU(3) color field described by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The Fock space is truncated to the $\ket{q}$ and $\ket{qg}$ sectors, and the time evolution operator is decomposed into small steps in light-front time $x^+$, combining a Runge-Kutta method for gluon emission/absorption with matrix exponentiation for the background field interaction. The basis states are eigenstates of the free kinetic energy Hamiltonian, discretized on a transverse lattice with periodic boundary conditions and a compactified longitudinal direction.

## Key numbers

- Gluon mass $m_g = 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$ is used to regularize infrared divergences in the background field
- Transverse lattice parameters: $L_\perp = 50\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1} (= 9.87\ \mathrm{fm})$ and $N_\perp = 16$, giving lattice spacing $a_\perp = L_\perp/N_\perp$
- The saturation scale $Q_s^2$ is related to the charge density parameter by $Q_s^2 = C_F \frac{(g^2\tilde\mu)^2 L_\eta}{2\pi}$, where $C_F = 4/3$ and $L_\eta$ is the duration of the field
- The total number of basis states is $N_{tot} = (2N_\perp)^2 \times 2 \times 3 + (K-0.5) \times (2N_\perp)^4 \times 4 \times 24$, where $K$ is the total longitudinal momentum quantum number

## Context

This work extends the tBLFQ approach previously applied to quark-nucleus scattering in the leading $\ket{q}$ sector (Li, 2020) by including gluon emission and absorption via the $\ket{qg}$ sector. It provides a nonperturbative alternative to analytical calculations in the eikonal approximation used in Color Glass Condensate studies (Gelis, 2010) and jet quenching phenomenology (Casalderrey-Solana, 2011; Mehtar-Tani, 2011; Armesto, 2012). The method enables future studies of subeikonal effects relevant for the Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi, 2012) and the interplay between coherence time and scattering center timescales in jet quenching (Blaizot, 2012; Mehtar-Tani, 2019; Barata, 2021).
