# Addition theorems for spin spherical harmonics. I Preliminaries

> We develop a systematic approach to deriving addition theorems for, and some other bilocal sums of, spin spherical harmonics. In this first part we establish some necessary technical results. We discuss the factorization of orbital and spin degrees of freedom in certain products of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and obtain general explicit results for the matrix elements in configuration space of tensor products of arbitrary rank of the position and angular-momentum operators. These results are the basis of the addition theorems for spin spherical harmonics obtained in part II.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic algebraic framework for deriving addition theorems for spin spherical harmonics, but this first part only establishes the necessary technical preliminaries (factorization of Clebsch-Gordan products and matrix elements of tensor operators) without presenting any addition theorems themselves; those are deferred to a second paper (Bouzas 2010).

## Method

The author factorizes products of two Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the form ⟨ℓ′ℓ′_z s′s′_z | j j_z⟩ ⟨ℓℓ_z s s_z | j j_z⟩ using 6j-symbols and the Wigner-Eckart theorem, separating orbital and spin degrees of freedom. Explicit expressions for these factorized products are then derived for spin values s′, s = 0, 1/2, 1, 3/2 and for orbital angular momentum changes |Δℓ| = 0, 1, 2, 3. The matrix elements of tensor products of the position versor r̂ and orbital angular momentum L̂ between angular-momentum eigenstates are computed in configuration space using standard irreducible tensor methods.

## Context

This work extends the standard representation theory of the three-dimensional rotation group (Edmonds 1957, Wigner 1959, Biedenharn and Louck 1981, Varshalovich et al. 1988) to provide explicit factorized forms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficient products needed for spin-dependent partial-wave expansions. The results are the basis for addition theorems for spin spherical harmonics of spins up to 3/2 presented in the companion paper (Bouzas 2010), and the factorization formulas for products of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients are independently useful for computing scattering amplitudes and matrix elements involving transitions between states with definite orbital and spin quantum numbers.
