# Gauge/string duality applied to heavy ion collisions: Limitations, insights and prospects

> Over the last decade a fruitful interplay has developed between analyses of strongly coupled non-abelian plasmas via the gauge/string duality and the phenomenology of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions. I review the reasons why the gauge/string duality is not a precision tool for QCD physics at present, with emphasis on conceptual issues. I then argue that, nevertheless, the duality can provide valuable insights at both the quantitive and the qualitative level. I illustrate this with a few examples, and conclude with a brief discussion of future prospects.

## Key claim

The gauge/string duality is not a precision tool for QCD physics at present because the limit in which string theory reduces to classical gravity requires the gauge theory to be strongly coupled at all energy scales, which excludes asymptotically free theories like QCD; however, certain results from the duality may be universal enough to apply to QCD in certain regimes, providing valuable insights at both quantitative and qualitative levels.

## Method

The paper reviews the conceptual limitations of applying gauge/string duality (also known as AdS/CFT or holography) to QCD, focusing on the two conditions required for tractability: large number of colors (N_c → ∞) and large 't Hooft coupling (λ → ∞). It then illustrates how universal features of the duality, particularly the description of deconfined phases by black hole backgrounds in the bulk spacetime, yield insights into quarkonium physics, shear viscosity, and hydrodynamic behavior of strongly coupled plasmas.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is η/s = 1/4π in any theory with a string dual in the limit N_c → ∞, λ → ∞
- Results from RHIC and LHC indicate 1 ≲ 4π η/s ≲ 2.5 for the QGP at temperatures T_c ≲ T ≲ 2T_c
- In a specific model, the dissociation temperature for J/Ψ mesons is T_diss ∼ (1.6-2.1)T_c
- The subluminal limiting velocity for heavy quarkonium mesons can be as low as 0.27 ≲ v_lim near T_diss
- The Cherenkov angle for quarkonium radiation can be as large as θ_c = arccos(v_lim/v_quark) ≲ 74°
- For temperature range T = 200-400 MeV and N_c = 3, the energy loss from Cherenkov radiation is dE/dx ≈ 2-8 GeV/fm
- A numerical simulation of colliding sheets of energy in N=4 SYM theory yields an estimated thermalization time of 0.3 fm when extrapolated to heavy ion collisions

## Context

This review extends the line of work applying gauge/string duality to heavy ion phenomenology that began with the discovery of the duality by Maldacena (1997) and was developed for QGP applications by many authors including Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) who first computed η/s, and Witten (1998) who established the black hole description of deconfined phases. The paper enables future work on far-from-equilibrium dynamics via numerical simulations of classical Einstein equations, as pioneered by Chesler and Yaffe (2011), and motivates lattice QCD studies of modified meson dispersion relations in the plasma.
