# Chiral kaon dynamics in heavy ion collisions

> The influence of the chiral mean field on the collective motion of kaons in relativistic heavy ion reactions at SIS energies is investigated. We consider three types of collective motion, i.e. the transverse flow, the out-of-plane flow (squeeze-out) and the radial flow. The kaon dynamics is thereby described with a relativistic mean field as it originates form chiral lagrangiens. For the $K$ mesons inside the nuclear medium we adopt a covariant quasi-particle picture including scalar and vector fields and compare this to a treatment with a static potential like force. The comparison to the available data ($K^+$) measured by FOPI and KaoS strongly favor the existence of an in-medium potential. However, using full covariant dynamics makes it more difficult to describe the data which might indicate that the mean field level is not sufficient for a reliable description of the kaon dynamics.

## Key claim

The comparison to available data (K⁺) measured by FOPI and KaoS strongly favors the existence of an in-medium potential for kaons; however, using full covariant dynamics (including Lorentz-force terms) makes it more difficult to describe the data, which might indicate that the mean field level is not sufficient for a reliable description of kaon dynamics.

## Method

The authors describe kaon dynamics in relativistic heavy ion reactions at SIS energies using a covariant quasi-particle picture derived from chiral Lagrangians, incorporating scalar and vector mean fields. They compare this fully covariant treatment, which includes a Lorentz-force term from the spatial components of the vector potential, to a simpler static potential approach that neglects such velocity-dependent forces. The collective motion of K⁺ and K⁻ mesons is studied via Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations for three types of flow: transverse flow, out-of-plane flow (squeeze-out), and radial flow.

## Key numbers

- For K⁺ in a central (b=3 fm) Au+Au reaction at 1 A.GeV, a Boltzmann fit to the transverse mass spectrum including a radial flow yields β=0.11 and T=62 MeV.
- The K⁺-nucleon absorption cross section is approximately 10 mb; the K⁻-nucleon absorption cross section is approximately 50 mb.
- For squeeze-out studies of K⁺ in a semi-central (b=6 fm) Au+Au reaction at 1 A.GeV, a transverse momentum cut of P_T > 0.2 GeV/c was applied to compare with KaoS data.
- For squeeze-out studies of K⁻ in a semi-peripheral (b=8 fm) Au+Au reaction at 1.8 A.GeV, a transverse momentum cut of P_T > 0.5 GeV was applied.
- For transverse flow studies of K⁺ in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 A.GeV, impact parameters b ≤ 4 fm and a transverse momentum cut P_T / m_K > 0.5 were used.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of kaon in-medium potentials in heavy ion collisions by Li and Ko (1995), Brown and Rho (1996), and Waas et al. (1996), and addresses collective flow signatures previously examined by Wang et al. (1997, 1998) and Fuchs et al. (1998). The paper shows that while static potential models can describe FOPI and KaoS data for K⁺ squeeze-out and transverse flow, the inclusion of full covariant dynamics with Lorentz forces produces strong cancelation effects that worsen agreement with data, suggesting that higher-order terms beyond mean field from chiral perturbation theory may be necessary. The radial flow analysis provides a complementary signal for in-medium dynamics that is not affected by the Lorentz-force complications.
