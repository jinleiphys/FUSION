# Numerical analysis of a baryon and its dilatation modes in holographic QCD

> We investigate a baryon and its dilatation modes in holographic QCD based on the Sakai-Sugimoto model, which is expressed as a 1+4 dimensional U($N_f$) gauge theory in the flavor space. For spatially rotational symmetric systems, we apply a generalized version of the Witten Ansatz, and reduce 1+4 dimensional holographic QCD into a 1+2 dimensional Abelian Higgs theory in a curved space. In the reduced theory, the holographic baryon is described as a two-dimensional topological object of an Abrikosov vortex. We numerically calculate the baryon solution of holographic QCD using a fine and large lattice with spacing of 0.04 fm and size of 10 fm. Using the relation between the baryon size and the zero-point location of the Higgs field in the description with the Witten Ansatz, we investigate a various-size baryon through this vortex description. As time-dependent size-oscillation modes (dilatation modes) of a baryon, we numerically obtain the lowest excitation energy of 577 MeV and deduce the dilatational excitation of a nucleon to be the Roper resonance N$^*$(1440).

## Key claim

The paper numerically demonstrates that a baryon in holographic QCD (Sakai-Sugimoto model) can be described as an Abrikosov vortex in a reduced 1+2 dimensional Abelian Higgs theory, and that the lowest dilatation (size-oscillation) mode of this baryon has an excitation energy of 577 MeV, which the authors identify with the Roper resonance N*(1440), though this identification relies on the relation between baryon size and the zero-point location of the Higgs field in the Witten Ansatz description.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized Witten Ansatz to the 1+4 dimensional U(N_f) gauge theory of the Sakai-Sugimoto model under spatial rotational symmetry, reducing it to a 1+2 dimensional Abelian Higgs theory in a curved space with background gravity functions h(w) and k(w). They numerically minimize the total energy (SU(2)_f Yang-Mills plus U(1) Chern-Simons contributions) on a fine lattice with spacing 0.04 fm and size 10 fm, starting from a 't Hooft instanton configuration and iteratively updating the Higgs field φ, Abelian gauge field a, and U(1) gauge field Â₀ to obtain the ground-state baryon solution. For the dilatation modes, they use the relation between baryon size and the Higgs zero-point location to study size-oscillation modes as time-dependent perturbations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: 0.04 fm; lattice size: 10 fm (250 × 250 grid in (r,w) coordinates, with r from 0 to 50 M_KK⁻¹ and w from -125 to 125 M_KK⁻¹)
- Model parameters: M_KK ≃ 948 MeV, κ = 7.46 × 10⁻³, chosen to reproduce f_π ≃ 92.4 MeV and m_ρ ≃ 776 MeV
- Lowest dilatation excitation energy: 577 MeV
- Higgs zero-point location for the ground-state baryon: (r,w) ≃ (2.4, 0) in M_KK = 1 units

## Context

This work extends the holographic baryon program within the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005), which previously relied on flat-background approximations (h(w)=k(w)=1) and 't Hooft instanton solutions (HSSY07, HRYY07, HSS08). By using the Witten Ansatz (Witten, 1977) to include the full curved background gravity from the D4 branes, the authors provide a numerically exact treatment of the baryon as an Abrikosov vortex. The dilatation mode analysis connects to earlier Skyrme model studies (HS84, HH84, ZMK84, WE84, LZB84, BN84) that identified the Roper resonance N*(1440) as a breathing mode of the nucleon; the present work offers a holographic QCD derivation of this excitation.
