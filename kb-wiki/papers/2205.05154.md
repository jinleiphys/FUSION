# One-dimensional complex potentials for quarkonia in a quark-gluon plasma

> Master equations of the Lindblad type have recently been derived to describe the dynamics of quarkonium states in the quark-gluon plasma. Because their full resolution in three dimensions is very challenging, the equations are often reduced to one dimension. The main ingredient of these equations is the complex potential that describes the binding of the heavy quark-antiquark pairs and their interactions with the medium. In this work, we propose a one-dimensional complex potential parameterized to reproduce at best two key properties -- the temperature-dependent masses of the eigenstates and their decay widths -- of a three-dimensional lattice QCD inspired potential. Their spectral decompositions are calculated to check their compatibility with the positivity of the master equations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a one-dimensional complex potential for quarkonium dynamics in a quark-gluon plasma, parameterized to reproduce the temperature-dependent masses and decay widths of eigenstates from a three-dimensional lattice QCD inspired potential, but only as a proxy for phenomenology since a precise quantitative comparison to data would require a full three-dimensional description.

## Method

The authors take a three-dimensional complex potential from Burnier, Kaczmarek, Rothkopf, and Lafferty (2015, 2019), which combines a Coulombic part from hard-thermal loop perturbation theory with a string part derived from the vacuum Cornell potential, and fit its real and imaginary parts to reproduce the temperature-dependent masses and decay widths of charmonium and bottomonium eigenstates in one dimension. They then calculate the spectral decomposition of the imaginary part to check compatibility with the positivity of the Lindblad master equations used for quarkonium dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum parameters from the Cornell potential: $\alpha_s = 0.513 \pm 0.0024$, $\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.412 \pm 0.0041$ GeV, $c = -0.161 \pm 0.0025$ GeV
- Bottom quark mass: $m_b = 4.882$ GeV (renormalon subtracted mass)
- Charm quark mass: $m_c = 1.4692$ GeV (fit to reproduce charmonium spectrum)
- Vacuum temperature $T_0 \approx 0.126025$ GeV where the Debye mass vanishes
- String breaking radius $r_{SB} = 1.25$ fm at $T=T_0$, with saturation potential $V_{\rm SB} \approx 0.8352$ GeV
- Vacuum masses from expectation values: $J/\psi$ at 3.0964 GeV, $\psi$(2S) at 3.6642 GeV, $\Upsilon$(1S) at 9.4611 GeV, $\Upsilon$(2S) at 10.023 GeV, $\Upsilon$(3S) at 10.355 GeV
- Vacuum mean radii from expectation values: $J/\psi$ at 0.387 fm, $\psi$(2S) at 0.856 fm, $\Upsilon$(1S) at 0.188 fm, $\Upsilon$(2S) at 0.463 fm, $\Upsilon$(3S) at 0.687 fm

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional lattice QCD inspired complex potential developed by Burnier, Kaczmarek, Rothkopf, and Lafferty (2015, 2019) to one dimension, enabling simplified quantum master equation simulations of quarkonium dynamics in a quark-gluon plasma. Previous one-dimensional potentials used in works by DeBoni (2017), Kajimoto (2017), Miura (2019), and Alund (2020) were disconnected from in-medium quarkonium physics and three-dimensional potentials, limiting their connection to phenomenology. The proposed potential enables approximate studies of quarkonium suppression and decoherence in realistic QGP simulations while full three-dimensional calculations remain computationally challenging.
