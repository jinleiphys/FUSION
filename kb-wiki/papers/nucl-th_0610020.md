# Relations for Four Nucleons in a Single j Shell – Using 9j Symbols to get rid of 9j Symbols

## Key claim

The paper derives simplified algebraic expressions for the number of proton-neutron pairs with even angular momentum J₁₂ in four-nucleon systems (⁴⁴Ti) within a single j shell, but only for states with total isospin T=0 or T=2; no corresponding simplification is obtained for T=1 states.

## Method

The author starts from a general matrix element expression for four nucleons in a single j shell that contains products of unitary 9j symbols. By choosing a schematic interaction where the T=0 interaction strength is set to one and the T=1 interaction is zero, the matrix element simplifies to a form involving only a single 9j symbol. Using isospin algebra and the eigenvalue equation for this Hamiltonian, the number of proton-neutron pairs of given J₁₂ is expressed in terms of probability amplitudes D^I(J_P,J_N). For T=0 and T=2, charge symmetry relations cause terms with odd intermediate angular momentum J_A to cancel, allowing the pair number expression to be reduced to a simple sum over squared amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For A=4 nucleons, the eigenvalues of the isospin operator are 3, 2, and 0 for total isospin T=0, 1, and 2 respectively.
- For T=0 states, the total number of even J₁₂ pairs is 1, and the total number of odd J₁₂ pairs is 3.
- For A=4 in the single j approximation, the pair counts (odd pairs, even pairs) are: T=0 (3, 3), T=1 (2, 4), T=2 (0, 6).
- For T=2 states, there are zero odd J₁₂ pairs.
- In the 1s shell closure (two neutrons and two protons), there are three J₁₂=0 pairs and three J₁₂=1 pairs.

## Context

This work extends previous results by Zamick, Lee, and Mekjian (2006) that considered pair numbers only for systems with total angular momentum I=0, such as ground states of even-even Ti isotopes. The present paper generalizes those relations to states of any total angular momentum I. The simplified expressions for T=0 and T=2 enable easier calculation of proton-neutron pair distributions in ⁴⁴Ti without evaluating full 9j symbols, and the results are consistent with earlier isospin-based formulas for total pair counts (ref. [2] in the paper). The inability to simplify the T=1 case points to a limitation of the approach and an open question for future work.
