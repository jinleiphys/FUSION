# Determination of the neutrino mass by electron capture in 163 Holmium and the role of the three-hole states in 163 Dysprosium

> 163 Holmium to 163 Dysprosium is probably due to the small Q value of about 2.5 keV the best case to determine the neutrino mass by electron capture. The energy of the Q value is distributed between the excitation of Dysprosium (and the neglected small recoil of Holmium) and the relativistic energy of the emitted neutrino including the restmass. The reduction of the upper end of the deexcitation spectrum of Dysprosium below the Q value allows to determine the neutrino mass. The excitation of Dysprosium can be calculated in the sudden approximation of the overlap of the electron wave functions of Holmium minus the captured electron and one-, two-, three- and multiple hole-excitations in Dysprosium. Robertson and the author have calculated the influence of the two-hole states on the Dysprosium deexitation spectrum. Here for the first time the influence of the three-hole states on the deexcitation bolometer spectrum of 163 Dysprosium is presented. The electron wave functions and the overlaps are calculated selfconsitently in a fully relativistic and antisymmetrized Dirac-Hartree-Fock approach in Holmium and in Dysprosium. The electron orbitals in Dysprosium are determined including the one-hole states in the selfconsistent iteration. The influence of the three-hole states on the deexcitation (by X-rays and Auger electrons) spectrum is hardly visible. The three-hole states seem not to be important for the determination of the neutrino mass.

# Determination of the neutrino mass by electron capture in 163 Holmium and the role of the three-hole states in 163 Dysprosium

## Key claim

The three-hole states in 163 Dysprosium after electron capture from 163 Holmium have a negligible effect on the deexcitation bolometer spectrum and are not relevant for determining the electron neutrino mass, even though they can approach the Q value more closely than one- or two-hole states. This conclusion holds under the assumption that the Q value is approximately 2.8 keV and that the theoretical spectrum is calculated using a fully relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock approach with selfconsistent inclusion of one-hole states.

## Method

The authors extended their previous Dirac-Hartree-Fock formulation for two-hole states to include three-hole excitations in 163 Dysprosium, calculating electron wave functions selfconsistently for both Holmium (Z=67) and Dysprosium (Z=66) with the finite nuclear charge distribution described by a Fermi parametrization. The probability for forming three-hole states was derived using Wick's theorem in the sudden approximation, with the Vatai approximation applied for the second and third hole probabilities. The theoretical bolometer spectrum was computed as a sum over 6 one-hole states, 39 two-hole states, and 208 three-hole states, with resonance energies and widths taken from the ECHo collaboration and from tabulated values in the Handbook of Chemistry.

## Key numbers

- Q value for electron capture in 163Ho: Q = 2.80 ± 0.08 keV (ECHo collaboration measurement)
- Recommended Q value from literature: Q = (2.55 ± 0.016) keV
- Six one-hole excitation energies in 163Dy: 3s1/2 at 2040 eV (width 13.7 eV), 3p1/2 at 1836 eV (width 7.2 eV), 4s1/2 at 411 eV (width 5.3 eV), 4p1/2 at 333 eV (width 8 eV), 5s1/2 at 48 eV (width 4.3 eV), 5p1/2 at 30.8 eV (width 3 eV)
- 39 two-hole states with energies ranging from 352.4 eV (4p1/2 + 5p3/2) to 2472.4 eV (3s1/2 + 4s1/2)
- 208 three-hole states included, limited by energy conservation to Q ≥ E_C(1-hole) + E_C(particle-hole_1) + E_C(particle-hole_2)
- Overlap of electron wave functions with same quantum numbers in Ho and Dy: approximately 0.999 per orbital; product over 65 orbitals gives approximately 0.94
- Relative probability of the 4s1/2 one-hole state: 24.4% relative to the 3s1/2 state
- Relative probability of the 4p1/2 one-hole state: 1.22% relative to the 3s1/2 state
- Experimental data binned in 2 eV intervals; detector resolution approximately 10 eV FWHM

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Robertson (2015) and Faessler and Simkovic (2015), who previously included two-hole states in the Dysprosium deexcitation spectrum, by adding three-hole excitations for the first time. The main improvement over Robertson's work is that the electron wave functions are calculated selfconsistently in Holmium and Dysprosium rather than using Xenon (Z=54) wave functions from Carlson and Nestor. The finding that three-hole states are negligible simplifies the analysis for the ECHo collaboration's ongoing measurement of the neutrino mass from electron capture, as it confirms that only one- and two-hole states need to be considered when fitting the four parameters (neutrino mass, energy distance of the leading resonance to Q, width, and strength) to the upper end of the deexcitation spectrum.
