# Radial Oscillations of Hybrid Stars and Neutron Stars including Delta baryons: The Effect of a Slow Quark Phase Transition

> We study radial oscillations of hybrid neutron stars composed of hadronic external layers followed by a quark matter core. We employ a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model including hyperons and $Δ$ baryons to describe hadronic matter, and a density-dependent quark model for quark matter. We obtain the ten lowest eigenfrequencies and the corresponding oscillation functions of N, N+$Δ$, N+H, and N+H+$Δ$ equations-of-state with a phase transition to the quark matter at 1.4 and 1.8 ${M_{\odot}}$, focusing on the effects of a slow phase transition at the hadron-quark interface. We observe that the maximum mass is reached before the fundamental mode's frequency vanishes for slow phase transitions, suggesting that some stellar configurations with higher central densities than the maximum mass remain stable even when they undergo small radial perturbations. Future gravitational wave detectors and multi-messenger astronomy, complemented by robust microscopic models enabling exploration of various neutron star compositions, including hyperon content, are anticipated to impose precise limitations on the equation of state of baryonic matter under high-density conditions.

## Key claim
For hybrid stars with a slow phase transition at the hadron-quark interface, the maximum mass is reached before the fundamental mode's frequency vanishes, indicating that some stellar configurations with central densities exceeding that of the maximum mass configuration remain stable against small radial perturbations.

## Method
The authors employed a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model including nucleons, hyperons, and Delta baryons to describe hadronic matter, and a density-dependent quark mass (DDQM) model for quark matter. They constructed hybrid equations of state using the Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition, then solved the TOV equations for stellar structure and the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue equations for radial oscillations. The ten lowest eigenfrequencies and corresponding oscillation functions were obtained for four hadronic compositions (N, N+Delta, N+H, N+H+Delta) with phase transitions at 1.4 and 1.8 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- The DD-RMF model predicts nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.152 fm^-3, binding energy B/A = 16.4 MeV, compressibility K0 = 252 MeV, symmetry energy S0 = 32.3 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L0 = 51 MeV.
- For the N composition with DDQM parameters (C=0.65, D^1/2=135 MeV), the phase transition occurs at chemical potential mu0 = 1205.91 MeV and pressure P0 = 68.01 MeV fm^-3, with hadronic density n_B^(H) = 0.387 fm^-3 and quark density n_B^(Q) = 0.427 fm^-3.
- For the N+Delta composition with DDQM parameters (C=0.90, D^1/2=125 MeV), the phase transition occurs at mu0 = 1669.20 MeV and P0 = 373.42 MeV fm^-3, with n_B^(H) = 0.793 fm^-3 and n_B^(Q) = 1.141 fm^-3.
- The Delta baryon mass is m_Delta = 1232 MeV, compared to the neutron mass m_n = 939.6 MeV and the Xi hyperon mass m_Xi = 1315 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior study (Rather et al., 2023) on radial oscillations of hadronic matter containing hyperons and Delta baryons by introducing a deconfinement phase transition to quark matter. It builds on the finding by Pereira and Lugones (2017) and Lugones (2021) that the standard stability criterion dM/dn_c > 0 is insufficient for determining radial stability in hybrid stars with slow conversions. The results enable future gravitational wave detectors such as the Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope to constrain the equation of state of dense baryonic matter through multi-messenger observations.
