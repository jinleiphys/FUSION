# Prediction of exotic doubly charmed baryons within chiral effective field theory

> In this work we study the possible QCD exotic states in the doubly charmed baryon sector. Within chiral effective theory, it is predicted that several excited baryons result from the $S$-wave scattering of ground-state doubly charmed baryons ($Ξ_{cc}^{++}, Ξ_{cc}^{+}, Ω_{cc}^{+}$) and light pseudoscalar mesons ($π, K, η$). The excited doubly charmed baryons can be classified by the strangeness ($S$) and isospin ($I$) quantum numbers. Two of the excited states are clearly exotic, in the sense that they cannot be explained by the conventional baryons with three quarks, since their quantum numbers are $(S,I)=(1,0)$ and $(-1,1)$. Similar to the charmed scalar meson $D_{s0}^{*}(2317)$ in $DK$ scattering and the hyperon $Λ(1405)$ in $\bar{K}N$ scattering, one bound state below the $Ξ_{cc} \bar{K}$ threshold is predicted in the $(S,I)=(-1,0)$ channel. In addition, two resonant structures are found in the $Ξ_{cc}π, Ξ_{cc}η$ and $Ω_{cc} K$ coupled-channel scattering with $(S,I)=(0,1/2)$. The corresponding pole positions and coupling strengths of the excited doubly charmed baryons are given. The scattering lengths of the ground-state doubly charmed baryons and light pseudoscalar mesons are also predicted. The current study may provide useful guides for future experimental measurements and lattice simulations.

# Prediction of exotic doubly charmed baryons within chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
Within chiral effective field theory, several excited doubly charmed baryons are predicted to arise from S-wave scattering of ground-state doubly charmed baryons and light pseudoscalar mesons, but the results depend sensitively on the three-momentum cutoff scale Λ, which is estimated at 1.0 ± 0.2 GeV in the absence of experimental or lattice data to constrain it.

## Method
The leading-order chiral Lagrangian for doubly charmed baryons and light pseudoscalar mesons was constructed, including Weinberg-Tomozawa contact interactions and s- and u-channel ground-state baryon exchanges. The S-wave partial-wave amplitudes were unitarized using the algebraic N/D approximation with a three-momentum cutoff regularization, and the resulting scattering amplitudes were analytically continued to the complex energy plane to locate bound-state and resonance poles.

## Key numbers
- The ground-state doubly charmed baryon masses used are: m(Ξcc) = 3621.4 MeV (from LHCb measurement) and m(Ωcc+) = 3700 MeV (from Chen 2017)
- The pion decay constant is taken as Fπ = 92.1 MeV, and the axial-vector coupling constant is |gA| = 0.2
- The three-momentum cutoff Λ is estimated in the range 0.8 to 1.2 GeV, with a central value of 1.0 GeV
- For the (S,I) = (1,0) channel: a bound-state pole appears on the first Riemann sheet at masses of (none found at Λ=0.8 GeV, 4112 MeV at Λ=1.0 GeV, 4096 MeV at Λ=1.2 GeV); a virtual-state pole on the second sheet appears at (4114, 4115, 4113) MeV
- For the (S,I) = (-1,1) channel: a resonance pole on the second sheet with masses (4191, 4134, 4090) MeV and half-widths (89, 83, 74) MeV for Λ = (0.8, 1.0, 1.2) GeV
- For the (S,I) = (-1,0) channel: a bound state on the first sheet at masses (4018, 3957, 3907) MeV, approximately 150 MeV below the Ξcc K̅ threshold
- For the (S,I) = (0,1/2) channel: two resonance poles on the second sheet, a lower pole at masses (3830, 3816, 3800) MeV with half-widths (76, 50, 33) MeV, and a higher pole at masses (4170, 4146, 4116) MeV with half-widths (8, 18, 22) MeV
- Scattering lengths are predicted for multiple channels; for example, the Ξccπ → Ξccπ scattering length in the (S,I) = (0,1/2) channel is 0.55 (+0.16, -0.10) fm

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD exotic states into the doubly charmed baryon sector, following the same theoretical framework that successfully described the charmed scalar meson Ds0*(2317) in DK scattering (Guo 2009, Guo 2015, Du 2017) and the hyperon Λ(1405) in K̅N scattering (Oller 2000, Jido 2003, Ikeda 2012, Guo 2012). The predicted exotic states with quantum numbers (S,I) = (1,0) and (-1,1) cannot be explained as conventional three-quark baryons, providing clear targets for future experimental searches at LHCb and for lattice QCD simulations. The scattering length predictions offer reference values for future lattice calculations.
