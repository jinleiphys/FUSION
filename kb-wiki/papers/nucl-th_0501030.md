# Systematical Approach to the Exact Solution of the Dirac Equation for A Special Form of the Woods-Saxon Potential

> Exact solution of the Dirac equation for a special form of the Woods-Saxon potential is obtained for the s-states. The energy eigenvalues and two-component spinor wave functions are derived by using a systematical method which is called as Nikiforov-Uvarov. It is seen that the energy eigenvalues strongly depend on the potential parameters. In addition, it is also shown that the non-relativistic limit can be reached easily and directly.

## Key claim

The paper claims an exact analytical solution of the Dirac equation for a special form of the Woods-Saxon potential, but only for s-states (ℓ = 0) and only when the even and odd potential components satisfy a specific relation W(r) = (1/ξ)V(r) − κ/r, which is required to obtain a Schrödinger-like equation.

## Method

The authors apply Alhaidari's formalism (Alhaidari 2001) to transform the radial Dirac equation into a Schrödinger-like second-order differential equation for the upper spinor component. They then solve this equation using the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method, which reduces the problem to a hypergeometric-type equation via the coordinate transformation s = −exp[−(r − R₀)/b]. The energy eigenvalues are obtained by matching the constant λ from the NU method with the quantization condition λ = λₙ, and the two-component spinor wave functions are expressed in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Woods-Saxon potential solutions (Ref. ref3, ref4) to the relativistic Dirac framework, following the formalism of Alhaidari (2001) who solved the Dirac equation for shape-invariant potentials. The authors note that Kennedy previously studied the one-dimensional Dirac equation with the Woods-Saxon potential but did not discuss more realistic cases. The results enable analytical treatment of relativistic single-particle motion in nuclei and can be applied to relativistic nuclear scattering problems, though the solution is restricted to s-states.
