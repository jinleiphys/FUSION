# Quantum statistical effects in warm nuclear matter with light and heavy clusters

> We have investigated the compositions of hot and dense nuclear matter with the focus on the quantum-statistical effects for light clusters. Our main observation is that the formation of heavy nuclei in stellar matter leads to the reduction of the number densities of light clusters. As a result, the quantum statistical effects such as Bose-Einstein condensation of deuterons and $α$-particles are suppressed. The deviations in number densities of light clusters between Boltzmann and quantum statistics at sub-nuclear densities and temperatures 1-3 MeV are $\sim$0.2$\%$ at most. The condensation of $α$-particles in iso-symmetric nuclear matter is predicted only under the assumptions that it is composed only of nucleons and light clusters and no heavy nuclei are present. We also found that the Coulomb screening effects hardly affect the critical baryon densities for alpha condensation, although mass and chemical potential of $α$-particles are modified.

# Quantum statistical effects in warm nuclear matter with light and heavy clusters

## Key claim
The formation of heavy nuclei in stellar matter reduces the number densities of light clusters, thereby suppressing quantum statistical effects such as Bose-Einstein condensation of deuterons and alpha-particles. The condensation of alpha-particles in isosymmetric nuclear matter is predicted only under the assumption that the system is composed solely of nucleons and light clusters with no heavy nuclei present.

## Method
The authors generalized a self-consistent statistical model of clustered nuclear matter by replacing the Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics for light clusters (atomic number Z ≤ 5) with Bose-Einstein statistics for even-mass-number clusters and Fermi-Dirac statistics for odd-mass-number clusters. The free energy density was minimized including dripped nucleons described with Skyrme-type interactions, light clusters with quantum statistics, and a full ensemble of heavy nuclei (6 ≤ Z ≤ 1000) treated as Boltzmann gases with excluded volume effects and compressible liquid drop mass free energies. Calculations were performed for sub-nuclear densities at temperatures 1-3 MeV for both isosymmetric (Yp=0.5) and neutron-rich (Yp=0.2) matter.

## Key numbers
- Deviations in number densities of light clusters between Boltzmann and quantum statistics at sub-nuclear densities and temperatures 1-3 MeV are ∼0.2% at most.
- In the restricted ensemble without heavy nuclei, alpha-particles may condense at baryon densities nB ∼ 0.02 fm⁻³ (T=1 MeV) and 0.09 fm⁻³ (T=3 MeV) in isosymmetric matter (Yp=0.5).
- The ground state minimum in the bulk free energy disappears at temperatures around 14 MeV, at which point heavy nuclei disappear from the nuclear ensemble.
- The critical baryon number densities of deuterons are lower than those of alpha-particles for the same temperature.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent statistical approach (Furusawa 2017, 2018) by incorporating quantum statistics for light clusters, whereas earlier studies of Bose-Einstein condensation of light clusters (Misicu 2017, Sedrakian 2017, Wu 2017, Satarov 2017, Satarov 2019, Zhang 2019) did not consider the full nuclear ensemble including both light and heavy nuclei. The results contradict the possibility of alpha condensation in stellar matter suggested by those prior works, demonstrating that the single-nucleus approximation (e.g., using Fe⁵⁶ or an optimized representative nucleus) considerably underestimates the total mass fraction of heavy nuclei and overestimates light cluster abundances. The findings enable more realistic nuclear equation of state modeling for core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star merger simulations, though the authors note that explicit mean-field interactions for light clusters (as in Satarov 2017, 2019) should be incorporated in future work.
