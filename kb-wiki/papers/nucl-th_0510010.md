# Inclusive nucleon emission induced by quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

> A previous model on inclusive charged-current quasi-elastic nuclear reactions is extended to include neutral- and charged-current nucleon emission reactions. The problem of outgoing nucleon propagation is treated by means of a Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous model for inclusive charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus reactions to also cover neutral-current and charged-current nucleon emission, but the outgoing nucleon propagation is treated via a Monte Carlo simulation rather than an analytic optical potential, leading to a redistribution of strength toward lower nucleon energies rather than a simple overall reduction.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive quasi-elastic cross sections for neutrino-nucleus interactions using a local Fermi gas model with the local density approximation, including long-range correlations (RPA) and a correct energy balance. For neutral currents, they incorporate isoscalar pieces in the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction and strange quark form factors via SU(3) symmetry relations. The propagation of ejected nucleons through the nucleus is simulated with a Monte Carlo method that treats nucleon-nucleon collisions using free-space differential cross sections modified for medium polarization and Pauli blocking, with nucleons moving in a semiclassical nucleon-nucleus potential.

## Key numbers

- For charged-current processes on Argon, the model shows a reduction of high-energy proton flux and production of many secondary nucleons (including neutrons) at lower energies; the solid histogram shows results without final-state interactions (FSI) and the dashed one the full model.
- Neutral-current cross sections on Oxygen are shown at neutrino energies of 500 MeV and 150 MeV as a function of nucleon kinetic energy; the solid histogram shows results without FSI and the dashed one the full model.
- The semiclassical approximation for nucleon propagation is stated to be fairly accurate for nucleons with kinetic energies higher than approximately 20 MeV, citing COV94.
- A 30 MeV cut is applied for secondary nucleons in the proton-to-neutron ratio analysis.

## Context

This work extends the charged-current quasi-elastic model of Nieves (2004) to neutral-current reactions, enabling predictions for experiments like K2K that measure outgoing nucleon spectra. The Monte Carlo treatment of final-state interactions produces a different effect than optical potential approaches such as Martinez (2005), which reduce cross sections at all energies rather than shifting strength to lower energies. The ratio of proton to neutron quasi-elastic cross sections is identified as potentially sensitive to the strange quark axial form factor parameter \(g^s_A\), with larger sensitivity for heavier nuclei and higher neutrino energies.
