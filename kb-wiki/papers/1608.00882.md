# Effect of temperature and magnetic field on two-flavor superconducting quark matter

> We investigate the effect of turning on temperature for the charge neutral phase of two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) dense quark matter in presence of constant external magnetic field. Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, by tuning the diquark coupling strength, we study the interdependent evolution of the quark Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer gap and dynamical mass as functions of temperature and magnetic field. We find that magnetic field $B \gtrsim 0.02$ GeV$^2$ ($10^{18}$ G) leads to anomalous temperature behavior of the gap in the gapless 2SC phase (moderately strong coupling), reminiscent of previous results in the literature found in the limit of weak coupling without magnetic field. The 2SC gap in the strong coupling regime is abruptly quenched at ultrahigh magnetic field due to the mismatched Fermi surfaces of up and down quarks imposed by charge neutrality and oscillation of the gap due to Landau level quantization. The dynamical quark mass also displays strong oscillation and magnetic catalysis at high magnetic field, although the latter effect is tempered by nonzero temperature. We discuss the implications for newly born compact stars with superconducting quark cores.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the gapless two-flavor color superconducting (g2SC) phase at moderately strong coupling, a magnetic field \(B \gtrsim 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) (\(10^{18}\) G) leads to anomalous temperature behavior of the superconducting gap, reminiscent of prior weak-coupling results without magnetic field; however, this behavior is conditional on the charge neutrality condition and the specific diquark coupling strength, and the homogeneous gap can be abruptly quenched at ultrahigh magnetic fields or high temperatures.

## Method

The authors employ a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar and diquark coupling channels, imposing electric and color charge neutrality conditions. They derive the thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and magnetic field using the imaginary time path integral formalism, then numerically solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate (dynamical quark mass \(m\)), the diquark gap \(\Delta\), and the chemical potentials \(\mu_e\) and \(\mu_{8c}\) as functions of quark chemical potential \(\mu\), temperature \(T\), and magnetic field \(B\). A smooth Fermi-Dirac-type cutoff is used for regularization.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field \(B \gtrsim 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) (equivalent to \(10^{18}\) G) is identified as the strong-field regime where effects become clearly visible, corresponding to a maximum number of completely occupied Landau levels \(n_{\text{max}} \lesssim 10\).
- For the g2SC phase at \(\rho = 0.75\) and \(\mu = 0.45\) GeV, the critical magnetic field for breakdown of homogeneous pairing at \(T = 0\) is \(\tilde{e}B_c \approx 0.21\) GeV\(^2\); at \(T = 0.05\) GeV, this shifts to \(\tilde{e}B_c \approx 0.17\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the same parameters, the critical temperature \(T_c\) at which the charge neutral gap disappears is approximately \(0.054\) GeV for \(\tilde{e}B \approx 0\), decreasing to \(T_c \approx 0.043\) GeV for \(\tilde{e}B = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- In the strong coupling regime (\(\rho = 1.15\)), the diquark gap vanishes abruptly at \(T \sim 0.04\) GeV for \(\tilde{e}B \sim 0.6\) GeV\(^2\), with the region of vanishing gap widening from \(\tilde{e}B \sim 0.41-0.57\) GeV\(^2\) at \(T=0\) to \(\tilde{e}B \sim 0.38-0.63\) GeV\(^2\) at \(T=0.12\) GeV.
- Magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking is observed at very large magnetic fields \(\tilde{e}B \gtrsim 0.41\) GeV\(^2\), where only the lowest Landau level is occupied.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the 2SC phase at finite temperature and high magnetic field by Fayazbakhsh (2010) and the authors' own zero-temperature study (Mandal 2012), but crucially includes charge neutrality conditions and the competition between chiral and diquark condensates as a function of coupling strength. The results confirm earlier findings by Huang (2002, 2003) for the zero-field and zero-temperature limits, and show that the anomalous temperature behavior of the gap in the g2SC phase, previously noted in weak-coupling without magnetic field, can also occur at moderate coupling with strong magnetic fields. The findings are relevant for understanding the phase structure of quark matter in protoneutron stars and magnetars, and suggest that more complicated order parameters (e.g., the LOFF state) may be more robust alternatives to homogeneous 2SC pairing in newly born compact stars.
