# Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory: Superdeformed Bands in the $A\sim 190$ Region

> Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory is presented as an extension of Relativistic Mean Field theory with pairing correlations to the rotating frame. Pairing correlations are taken into account by a finite range two-body force of Gogny type and approximate particle number projection is performed by Lipkin-Nogami method. This theory is applied to the description of yrast superdeformed rotational bands observed in even-even nuclei of the $A\sim 190$ mass region. Using the well established parameter sets NL1 for the Lagrangian and D1S for the pairing force one obtains a very successful description of data such as kinematic ($J^{(1)}$) and dynamic ($J^{(2)}$) moments of inertia without any adjustment of new parameters. Within the present experimental accuracy the calculated transition quadrupole moments $Q_t$ agree reasonably well with the observed data.

## Key claim
Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, using the NL1 Lagrangian and D1S pairing force without any parameter adjustment, successfully describes superdeformed rotational bands in the A~190 region, but only when approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method is included; without this projection the agreement with experimental moments of inertia is significantly worse.

## Method
The authors solve the CRHB equations in a one-dimensional cranking approximation, using a basis of anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator states in Cartesian coordinates (fermionic shells up to N_F=14, bosonic shells up to N_B=16). Pairing correlations are treated with a finite-range Gogny-type force (parameter set D1S) in the particle-particle channel, and approximate particle number projection is performed before variation using the Lipkin-Nogami method. The calculations use the NL1 parametrization of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian and fully include the spatial components of the vector meson fields (nuclear magnetism) in a self-consistent way.

## Key numbers
- Increasing the fermionic basis from N_F=14 to N_F=17 changes the kinematic moment of inertia J^(1) and transition quadrupole moment Q_t by less than 1%.
- The lowest transitions in the yrast superdeformed bands of 190Hg, 192Hg, and 196Pb have energies of 316.9 keV, 214.4 keV, and 171.5 keV, respectively; the calculations indicate these most likely correspond to spin changes of 14+→12+, 10+→8+, and 8+→6+.
- Relative average quadrupole moments ΔQ_t between yrast superdeformed bands of Pb and Hg isotopes are approximately 1.6 eb for N=110, 1.4 eb for N=112, and 1.06 eb for N=114.
- The lowest transition in the 192Pb superdeformed band has energy 214.8 keV, corresponding to a spin change 10→8.
- A small peak in J^(2) appears at Ω_x ~ 0.45 MeV in 192Pb, and a plateau at Ω_x ~ 0.4 MeV in 196Pb.

## Context
This work extends cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory, previously applied to A~60 and A~150 regions (Afanasjev 1996, others), by adding pairing correlations crucial for the A~190 region. It contrasts with cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky approaches using Woods-Saxon potentials (Wyss 1991, others) and self-consistent cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approaches with Skyrme forces (Skyrme1, Skyrme2) or Gogny forces (GognyFr, GognySp), all of which required additional parameter adjustments or special pairing treatments. The CRHB approach enables systematic study of superdeformed bands in the A~190 region using only the established NL1 and D1S parameter sets, and the authors note that dedicated experiments measuring Q_t as a function of rotational frequency are needed to further test the theory.
