# Tidal Deformation of Neutron Stars from Microscopic Models of Nuclear Dynamics

> The observation of the gravitational wave signal GW170817, consistent with emission from the inspiral of a binary neutron-star system, provided information on the tidal deformation of the participating stars. The available data may be exploited to constrain the equation of state of densenuclear matter, as well as to shed light on the underlying models describing nuclear dynamics at microscopic level. In this paper we compare the experimental results to the predictions of different theoretical models, based on non relativistic nuclear many-body theory, the relativistic field-theoretical formalism, and a more phenomenological approach constrained by observed nuclear properties. While the precision of the available data does not allow to resolve the degeneracy of the models, our analysis shows a distinct sensitivity to the star compactness predicted by the different equations of state, which turns out to be significantly affected by relativistic boost corrections to the nucleon-nucleon potential.

# Tidal Deformation of Neutron Stars from Microscopic Models of Nuclear Dynamics

## Key claim

While the precision of GW170817 data does not allow to resolve the degeneracy between different theoretical models of the neutron star equation of state, the analysis shows a distinct sensitivity to the star compactness predicted by the different equations of state, which is significantly affected by relativistic boost corrections to the nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Method

The authors computed mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for neutron star models using five different equations of state: three derived from non-relativistic Nuclear Many-Body Theory (APR1, APR2, and BL), one from Relativistic Mean Field Theory (GM3), and one from the liquid drop model (LS). For each equation of state, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the differential equation for the tidal Love number, then compared the predicted tidal deformabilities to the 90% confidence level constraints from the GW170817 event, using the low-spin scenario mass estimates from the LIGO-Virgo Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Chirp mass from GW170817: M = 1.188 (+0.004, -0.002) solar masses
- 90% confidence level estimate of tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass star: 70 <= Lambda(1.4 M_sun) <= 580
- 90% confidence level constraint on the combined tidal deformability in the low-spin scenario: Lambda_tilde <= 800
- Radii ranges for the primary star (M1 between 1.36 and 1.58 solar masses): APR1 gives 12.21-12.28 km, APR2 gives 11.46-11.58 km, BL gives 12.38-12.52 km, GM3 gives 12.90-13.24 km, LS gives 12.48-12.82 km
- Radii ranges for the secondary star (M2 between 1.18 and 1.36 solar masses): APR1 gives 12.28-12.30 km, APR2 gives 11.58-11.70 km, BL gives 12.52-12.61 km, GM3 gives 13.24-13.43 km, LS gives 12.82-13.00 km
- Equilibrium density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Maximum density-dependent correction to the APR2 ground-state energy: 4.5 MeV at subnuclear density rho ~ 0.11 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the comparison of microscopic nuclear dynamics models to tidal deformability constraints from GW170817, following the detection reported by the LIGO-Virgo Collaboration (2017). The authors compare predictions from non-relativistic Nuclear Many-Body Theory models (APR1, APR2, and BL from Benhar and Lovato 2017) with the relativistic mean field model GM3 from Glendenning (1985) and Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991), and the liquid drop model LS from Lattimer and Swesty (1991). The analysis enables future gravitational wave observations with improved sensitivity to potentially constrain three-nucleon potential models in the high-density regime and test the limits of the non-relativistic approximation.
