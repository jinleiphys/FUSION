# Dynamics of $K^+$ Production in Heavy Ion Collisions close to Threshold

> In this article the production of $K^+$ at energies close to the threshold is studied in detail. The production mechanisms, the influence of in-medium effects, cross sections, the nuclear equation of state and the dynamics of the nucleons on the kaon dynamics are discussed. A special regard will be taken on the collision of Au+Au at 1.5 GeV, a reaction that has recently been analyzed in detail by experiments performed by the KaoS and FOPI collaborations at the SIS accelerator at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the production of $K^+$ mesons in heavy ion collisions near threshold is sensitive to in-medium effects, particularly the kaon-nucleon optical potential and the nuclear equation of state, but the detailed dynamics depend on the specific parametrization of the optical potential and the treatment of delta resonances, with the full relativistic mean-field potential raising the $K^+$ in-medium mass by about 90 MeV at high density.

## Method

The study uses the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model, a semi-classical event-by-event simulation, to describe Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV incident energy. Kaons are treated as "virtual particles" with a small production probability to overcome statistical limitations, allowing high-statistics calculations of kaon observables including propagation through the nuclear medium, rescattering, and the influence of the kaon-nucleon optical potential derived from relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations. The dynamics of nucleons, deltas, and pions are first validated against experimental data from the KaoS and FOPI collaborations before analyzing kaon production.

## Key numbers

- The maximum central nucleon density in Au+Au at 1.5 AGeV (b=0 fm) reaches about three times ground state density at approximately 8 fm/c.
- The zero-momentum energy (in-medium mass) of the $K^+$ is enhanced by about 90 MeV at high density using the full RMF optical potential, while the $K^-$ zero-momentum energy is reduced by about 200 MeV.
- The average threshold energy for $BB \to NN K^+ K^-$ reactions is reduced by about 100 MeV due to the combined scalar and vector parts of the optical potential.
- For $BB \to NY K^+$ reactions, the full RMF potential yields an average threshold enhancement of about 70 MeV, while using only the scalar part lowers the threshold by about 70 MeV.
- The delta density reaches a maximum of nearly half ground state density at about 9 fm/c, and the pion density reaches a maximum of around one sixth ground state density at about 10 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangeness production in heavy ion collisions (e.g., Aichelin 1991, Cassing 1990) by providing a detailed microscopic analysis of $K^+$ dynamics using the IQMD model, specifically for the Au+Au system at 1.5 AGeV studied by the KaoS and FOPI collaborations at GSI. The results enable comparisons between different optical potential parametrizations (Schaffner-Bielich RMF, Chiral Perturbation Theory, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) and their effects on kaon observables, which can help constrain the nuclear equation of state and in-medium kaon properties through comparison with experimental data.
