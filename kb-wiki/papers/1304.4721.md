# Towards a better knowledge of the nuclear equation of state from the isoscalar breathing mode

> The measurements of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR), also called the breathing mode, are analyzed with respect to their constraints on the quantity $M_c$, e.g. the density dependence of the nuclear incompressibility around the so-called crossing density $ρ_c$=0.1 fm$^{-3}$. The correlation between the centroid of the GMR, $E_\mathrm{GMR}$, and $M_c$ is shown to be more accurate than the one between $E_\mathrm{GMR}$ and the incompressibility modulus at saturation density, $K_\infty$, giving rise to an improved determination on the nuclear equation of state. The relationship between $M_c$ and $K_\infty$ is given as a function of the skewness parameter $Q_\infty$ associated to the density dependence of the equation of state. The large variation of $Q_\infty$ among different energy density functionnals directly impacts the knowledge of $K_\infty$: a better knowledge of $Q_\infty$ is required to deduce more accurately $K_\infty$. Using the Local Density Approximation, a simple and accurate expression relating $E_\mathrm{GMR}$ and the quantity $M_c$ is derived and successfully compared to the fully microscopic predictions.

## Key claim

The correlation between the centroid of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) and the quantity \(M_c\) (the density dependence of the nuclear incompressibility around the crossing density \(\rho_c = 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\)) is more accurate and universal across different energy density functionals than the correlation between the GMR centroid and the incompressibility modulus at saturation density \(K_\infty\); however, the uncertainty on \(K_\infty\) remains large because it depends on the poorly constrained skewness parameter \(Q_\infty\).

## Method

The authors used the Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) approach with Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic energy density functionals to calculate the GMR centroid energy \(E_\mathrm{GMR}\) for \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{120}\)Sn via the sum-rule method (\(E_\mathrm{GMR} = \sqrt{m_1/m_{-1}}\)). They then performed a correlation analysis comparing \(E_\mathrm{GMR}\) with \(K_\infty\) and with \(M_c\), and derived an analytical expression relating \(E_\mathrm{GMR}\) and \(M_c\) using the Local Density Approximation (LDA) with a Fermi density profile.

## Key numbers

* The crossing density is \(\rho_c = 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to \(\rho_c = 0.71\rho_0\) where \(\rho_0\) is the saturation density.
* For Skyrme EDFs, the incompressibility at the crossing density is \(K_c = 34 \pm 4\) MeV.
* From the correlation analysis across various EDFs for \(^{120}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb, the extracted value is \(M_c = 1100 \pm 70\) MeV (6% uncertainty), compared to \(K_\infty = 230 \pm 40\) MeV (17% uncertainty).
* The skewness parameter \(Q_\infty\) varies by more than 100% among models, from \(-596\) MeV (D1S) to \(+478\) MeV (DDME2), and a typical uncertainty of \(\pm 400\) MeV on \(Q_\infty\) produces a variation on \(K_\infty\) of about \(\pm 40\) MeV.
* In the LDA-derived linearized expression, the coefficient \(\alpha\) is 0.12 MeV for \(^{208}\)Pb and 0.16 MeV for \(^{120}\)Sn, with \(\beta\) values of 42 MeV\(^2\) and 75 MeV\(^2\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Khan (2012) which first proposed that the GMR constrains \(M_c\) rather than \(K_\infty\), and addresses the long-standing problem of the 230 \(\pm\) 40 MeV uncertainty on \(K_\infty\) found in earlier studies by Blaizot (1980), Vretenar (2003), and others. The paper demonstrates that the large variation of \(Q_\infty\) among energy density functionals is the primary source of uncertainty in \(K_\infty\), and that better experimental constraints on \(Q_\infty\) are needed. The derived LDA expression enables a simple and accurate connection between GMR measurements and \(M_c\), providing a path toward improved determination of the nuclear equation of state.
