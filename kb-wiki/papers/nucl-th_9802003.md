# Effects of B-8 size on the low-energy Be7(p,gamma)B8 cross section

> We calculate several "size-like" B-8 observables within the microscopic three-cluster model and study their potential constraints on the zero-energy astrophysical S_{17}(0) factor of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction. We find within our three-cluster model that a simultaneous reproduction of the experimental data for the B-8 radius and quadrupole moment and of the B8-Li8 Coulomb displacement energy implies S_{17}(0)=(23-25) eVb.

# Effects of B-8 size on the low-energy Be7(p,gamma)B8 cross section

## Key claim

Within the microscopic three-cluster model, simultaneous reproduction of experimental data for the B-8 radius, B-8 quadrupole moment, and the B8-Li8 Coulomb displacement energy implies S_{17}(0) = (23-25) eV b, but this result depends on the linear relation found in the three-cluster model and may be affected by model spaces beyond He4+He3+p.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic eight-body calculations using a He4+He3+p cluster model, varying the size parameters of the He3 and He4 clusters while keeping other ingredients fixed, for three different nucleon-nucleon interactions (MN, V2, and MHN). They calculated the B-8 point-nucleon radius, B-8 quadrupole moment, the difference r^2(B8)-r^2(Be7), and the Coulomb displacement energy E(Li8)-E(B8), and studied their linear correlations with the zero-energy astrophysical S-factor S_{17}(0) of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction.

## Key numbers

- S_{17}(0) = (23-25) eV b from simultaneous reproduction of all three experimental indicators in the full three-cluster model with the MN interaction
- B-8 point-nucleon radius: experimental value r(B8) = 2.50 +/- 0.04 fm (Al-Khalili and Tostevin 1996); the full model gives S_{17}(0) = 23.2-24.2 eV b from this constraint
- B-8 quadrupole moment: experimental value Q_8 = (6.83 +/- 0.21) e fm^2 (Minamisono 1992); the full model gives S_{17}(0) = 23.7-24.8 eV b from this constraint
- Coulomb displacement energy: phenomenological value Delta = 3.41 MeV after removing the Nolen-Schiffer shift of approximately 130 keV; the full model gives S_{17}(0) = 24.3 eV b from this constraint
- For the restricted (Be7+p-type) model space with the MN interaction: r(B8) gives S_{17}(0) = 22.8-23.6 eV b, Q_8 gives S_{17}(0) = 25.3-27.5 eV b, Delta gives S_{17}(0) = 23.8 eV b
- For the restricted model with the V2 interaction: r(B8) gives S_{17}(0) = 25.7-26.6 eV b, Q_8 gives S_{17}(0) = 24.1-27.0 eV b, Delta gives S_{17}(0) = 26.5 eV b
- For the restricted model with the MHN interaction: r(B8) gives S_{17}(0) = 21.7-22.7 eV b, Q_8 gives S_{17}(0) = 24.3-27.2 eV b, Delta gives S_{17}(0) = 23.0 eV b
- The full model predicts the Be7 quadrupole moment Q_7 in the range -(5.5-6.0) e fm^2, smaller than the earlier value of Q_7 = -6.9 e fm^2 from Csoto et al. (1995)

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Csoto et al. (1995) which showed a linear correlation between S_{17}(0) and the Be7 quadrupole moment, by investigating additional B-8 size-like observables. The result S_{17}(0) = (23-25) eV b is compatible with the value used in most solar models at the time, S_{17}(0) = 22.4 +/- 2.1 eV b (Johnson et al. 1992), but slightly inconsistent with the then-new experimental value S_{17}(0) = 19^{+4}_{-2} eV b (Adelberger et al., to be published). The authors note that enlarging the Be7 model space by adding a He4+d+p configuration increased the Be7 quadrupole moment by about 10 percent, suggesting that calculations in model spaces beyond He4+He3+p are needed to investigate the effects on S_{17}(0).
