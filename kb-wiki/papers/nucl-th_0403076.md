# Collective modes in strange and isospin asymmetric hadronic matter

> We study the propagation of non-strange and strange meson modes in hadronic matter considering both isospin and strangeness mixings induced by quantum fluctuations in the medium. Baryons are described using the Quark Meson Coupling model extended to include interactions of strange quarks. In particular we evaluate the dependence of the meson masses on the baryonic density, the strangeness fraction and the isospin asymmetry of the medium. We have found a considerable admixture of strangeness and isospin in the sigma-mode in the high density regime.

## Key claim

The authors find a considerable admixture of strangeness and isospin in the sigma-mode in the high density regime, but this mixing is only appreciable for the sigma branch at densities above approximately 3.5 times nuclear saturation density, while vector omega and rho modes are affected by isospin but not by strangeness mixing.

## Method

The authors use the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, where baryons are represented as non-overlapping spherical bags containing three valence quarks, extended to include interactions of strange quarks. They evaluate the meson self-energies in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) including all mixing polarizations allowed by the hadronic Lagrangian, and compute the effective meson masses as the zeros of the dielectric function for the scalar (sigma, zeta, delta) and vector (omega, phi, rho) sectors separately at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy Eb = -16 MeV, symmetry energy as = 35 MeV
- Bag constant: B^1/4 = 210.86 MeV, adjusted to give proton bag radius Rp = 0.6 fm in vacuum
- Current quark masses: mu = md = 5 MeV, ms = 150 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_sigma^u,d = 5.99, g_omega^u,d = 3.00, g_delta^u,d = 1.83, g_rho^u,d = 4.50, g_zeta^s = 4.48
- Vacuum meson masses (MeV): m_sigma = 550, m_zeta = 980, m_omega = 783, m_phi = 1020, m_delta = 984, m_rho = 770
- Vacuum baryon masses (MeV): Mp = Mn = 938.92, M_Lambda = 1115.63, M_Xi = 1318.11
- The omega-rho mass difference around baryon density 2 n0 is close to the pion mass
- Mixing angles for sigma-zeta at the sigma pole become appreciable at n greater than or approximately n0
- Mixing angles for omega-phi at the omega pole become significant only at n greater than or approximately 3 n0
- Sigma-delta mixing becomes appreciable only at densities n greater than or approximately 3.5 n0
- Strangeness mixing angles for sigma-zeta at the zeta pole and omega-phi at the phi pole remain below 3.5 degrees for all densities studied

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium meson properties using the QMC model (ST1, 1995) and the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model (CHIN, LIM, PAL, RHOME, RHODE, SIGOME, EPJA) by including both isospin and strangeness mixing effects simultaneously in the RRPA, which was previously lacking. The results enable predictions for dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, particularly regarding the in-medium modification of vector mesons invoked to explain the gross features of dilepton spectra from the CERES collaboration (Li, Ko, Brown, 1995), and suggest that the omega-rho mixing is large enough at almost all densities to have significant consequences for the dilepton spectra.
