# Exclusive production of $ρ^0 ρ^0$ pairs in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We disuss exclusive electromagnetic production of two neutral $ρ^0$~mesons and show the predictions for the $AA \to AA ρ^0 ρ^0$ reactions for gold-gold collisions at the energy of $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV (RHIC) and for lead-lead collisions at the energy of $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.5 TeV (LHC). The elementary cross section is calculated with the help of the vector-dominance-model (VDM)-Regge approach which usually very well describes the experimental data at large $γγ$ energy. The low-energy $γγ\to ρ^0 ρ^0$ cross section is parametrized. The cross section for nuclear process is calculated by means of the equivalent photon approximation (EPA). We compare the results with realistic charge density with the results for monopole form factor.

## Key claim

The authors predict that exclusive electromagnetic production of two neutral ρ⁰ mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is dominated by a low-energy component of the γγ → ρ⁰ρ⁰ cross section, with the high-energy VDM-Regge component contributing only at larger pair invariant masses; the cross section obtained using a monopole form factor is larger than that from a realistic charge form factor, and the difference is bigger at smaller center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The elementary γγ → ρ⁰ρ⁰ cross section was split into a low-energy part, parametrized and fitted to e⁺e⁻ data, and a high-energy part calculated using a vector-dominance-model (VDM) Regge approach. The nuclear cross section for AA → AA ρ⁰ρ⁰ was then computed via the equivalent photon approximation (EPA), with the photon fluxes depending on either a realistic charge form factor (Fourier transform of the charge distribution) or a monopole form factor. The calculations were performed for Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV (RHIC) and Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV (LHC), with a θ function ensuring only peripheral collisions (impact parameter b > R₁ + R₂ ≈ 14 fm).

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV (RHIC), the cross section distribution in photon-photon center-of-mass energy W_{γγ} = M_{ρ⁰ρ⁰} is shown for both low-energy and high-energy components.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV (LHC), the same distribution is presented.
- The peripheral condition requires impact parameter b > R₁ + R₂ ≈ 14 fm.
- The low-energy component is the dominant one in the cross section distributions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of exclusive ρ⁰ production in heavy-ion collisions, which had previously measured only single ρ⁰ production (STAR collaboration). The authors build on their own earlier work on muon pair production using the same EPA approach (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010) and on their VDM-Regge model for hadronic processes (Szczurek, 2005). The results enable experimental searches for exclusive ρ⁰ρ⁰ pair production at RHIC and LHC, and highlight the importance of using realistic charge form factors rather than the simpler monopole approximation, especially at large pair rapidities.
