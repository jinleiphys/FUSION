# Explicit inclusion of spin-orbit contribution in THSR wave function

> The Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Roepke (THSR) wave function has been successfully used for the studies of gas-like nature of alpha clusters in various nuclei including the so-called Hoyle state of 12C and four alpha states of 16O. In standard alpha cluster models, however, each alpha cluster wave function has spin zero because of its spatial symmetry and antisymmetrization effect. Thus the non-central interactions do not contribute, and this situation is the same in the THSR wave function. In this work, the spin-orbit contribution, which is found to be quite important at short alpha-alpha distances, is taken into account in the THSR wave function by combing it with antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM). The application to 12C is presented. The multi-integration in the original THSR wave function is carried out by using Monte Carlo technique, which is called Monte Carlo THSR wave function. For the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the Tohsaki interaction, which contains finite-range three-body terms and simultaneously reproduces the saturation properties of nuclear systems, the alpha-alpha scattering phase shift, and the size and binding energy of 4He, is adopted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the spin-orbit contribution, which is absent in standard THSR wave functions because each alpha cluster has spin zero, can be explicitly included by combining the THSR wave function with the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM). The method is applied to 12C, and the spin-orbit effect is found to be quite important at short alpha-alpha distances, but the authors hedge that the three-body interaction terms do not strongly depend on the AQCM parameter Lambda and can be approximated using the Brink model (Lambda = 0) wave functions.

## Method

The authors combine the Monte Carlo THSR wave function, where the multi-integration over Gaussian center parameters is replaced by a superposition of Slater determinants with randomly generated center parameters distributed as exp[-R_i^2/sigma^2], with the AQCM transformation that adds an imaginary part to each nucleon's Gaussian center parameter proportional to Lambda times the spin direction. For 12C, all three alpha clusters are transformed into quasi clusters with intrinsic spins arranged with threefold symmetry, and the Tohsaki interaction (F1 parameter set) is used for the central part while the G3RS spin-orbit interaction (V_ls = 1800 MeV) is adopted. The wave functions are projected onto good angular momentum and parity, and the three-body interaction terms are estimated using Brink-type (Lambda = 0) Slater determinants with 1000 basis states.

## Key numbers

- The size parameter nu is chosen to be 0.25 fm^{-2}, which reproduces the observed radius of 4He.
- The spin-orbit interaction strength V_ls = 1800 MeV is employed, taken from the range 1600-2000 MeV suggested by 4He+n scattering phase shift data.
- For the Monte Carlo THSR calculation with Lambda = 0, the energy converges well with 1000 basis states (N_max = 1000).
- The three-alpha threshold energy is at -82.50 MeV.
- For sigma = 1.0 fm and Lambda = 0.2, the spin-orbit energy converges to approximately -6.4 MeV; for sigma = 2.0 fm and Lambda = 0.1, it converges to approximately -1.5 MeV.
- The optimal energy minimum appears at sigma = 2.0 fm with Lambda = 0.1, where the spin-orbit interaction lowers the energy by about 1 MeV compared to the Lambda = 0 case.
- At the limit of sigma = 0 fm, the optimal Lambda value is 0.3, giving a spin-orbit contribution larger than -30 MeV and a local minimum.
- Mixing the two configurations (sigma = 2.0 fm, Lambda = 0.1) and (sigma = 0 fm, Lambda = 0.3) with amplitude ratio 2:1 yields an rms matter radius of 2.37 fm, reproducing the experimental value of 2.35(2) fm.
- The rms matter radius for sigma = 5 fm (Lambda = 0) is 4.18 fm, relevant for the Hoyle state.
- The difference in three-body interaction terms between Lambda = 0.0 and Lambda = 0.1 cases is less than 100 keV.
- The elastic form factor shows a sign change around q ~ 1.6 fm^{-1} for the optimal configuration alone, shifting to q ~ 1.8 fm^{-1} after mixing, consistent with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the THSR wave function framework, previously successful for gas-like alpha cluster states such as the Hoyle state of 12C and four-alpha states of 16O (Tohsaki et al., 2001; Funaki et al., 2015), by incorporating spin-orbit effects through AQCM (Itagaki et al., 2016; 2017). The AQCM method had already been shown to describe subclosure configurations of the jj-coupling shell model (e.g., p3/2 for N=6, d5/2 for N=14) where spin-orbit effects are important. The combined Monte Carlo THSR+AQCM approach enables the description of both cluster and shell-model features in 12C, revealing a competition between the shell-like structure at small sigma and the cluster-like structure at larger sigma. This framework can be applied to other nuclei in future work, and the authors aim to derive analytic formulas for the Hamiltonian matrix elements.
