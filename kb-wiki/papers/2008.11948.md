# Neutron-rich calcium isotopes within realistic Gamow shell model calculations with continuum coupling

> Based on the realistic nuclear force of the high-precision CD-Bonn potential, we have performed comprehensive calculations for neutron-rich calcium isotopes using the Gamow shell model (GSM) which includes resonance and continuum. The realistic GSM calculations produce well binding energies, one- and two-neutron separation energies, predicting that $^{57}$Ca is the heaviest bound odd isotope and $^{70}$Ca is the dripline nucleus. Resonant states are predicted, which provides useful information for future experiments on particle emissions in neutron-rich calcium isotopes. Shell evolutions in the calcium chain around neutron numbers \textit{N} = 32, 34 and 40 are understood by calculating effective single-particle energies, the excitation energies of the first $2^+$ states and two-neutron separation energies. The calculations support shell closures at $^{52}$Ca (\textit{N} = 32) and $^{54}$Ca (\textit{N} = 34) but show a weakening of shell closure at $^{60}$Ca (\textit{N} = 40). The possible shell closure at $^{70}$Ca (\textit{N} = 50) is predicted.

## Key claim
The calculations predict that $^{57}$Ca is the heaviest bound odd calcium isotope and $^{70}$Ca is the dripline nucleus, but the authors note that $^{59}$Ca is weakly unbound with a one-neutron separation energy of $-326$ keV in their calculation, contradicting experimental observation of a bound $^{59}$Ca.

## Method
The authors performed Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations using the CD-Bonn realistic nucleon-nucleon potential softened by the $V_{{\rm low}-k}$ method with a cutoff of $\Lambda = 2.6$ fm$^{-1}$. Neutrons were treated in a Berggren complex-momentum basis that includes bound, resonant, and continuum states, with $^{48}$Ca as the core for isotopes up to $^{60}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca as the core for heavier isotopes. The effective Hamiltonian in the model space was constructed via many-body perturbation theory using the nondegenerate $\hat Q$-box folded-diagram method, and the non-Hermitian Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the Lanczos method allowing at most two particles in the continuum.

## Key numbers
- $^{57}$Ca is the heaviest bound odd isotope; $^{59}$Ca has a calculated one-neutron separation energy of $-326$ keV (unbound).
- The two-neutron dripline is predicted at $^{70}$Ca.
- The neutron $1p_{3/2}$ single-particle energy is $-5.26$ MeV (WS with $^{48}$Ca core), compared to the experimental value of $-5.14$ MeV from $^{49}$Ca.
- The neutron $0f_{5/2}$ single-particle energy is $-1.15$ MeV (WS with $^{48}$Ca core), matching the experimental value of $-1.15$ MeV.
- The calculated one-neutron separation energy in $^{55}$Ca is $1.40$ MeV, agreeing with the experimental datum of $1.56(0.16)$ MeV.
- The maximum discrepancy between calculated and experimental ground-state energies is about $2.5$ MeV, occurring in $^{54}$Ca.
- The calculated $2^+_1$ excitation energy for $^{60}$Ca is around $1.6$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous realistic shell model (RSM) calculations for calcium isotopes (PhysRevC.80.044311, PhysRevC.90.024312, PhysRevC.89.024319) by including continuum coupling via the Gamow shell model, which the authors note was missing in those earlier RSM studies. The results support experimental shell closures at $^{52}$Ca ($N=32$) and $^{54}$Ca ($N=34$) observed by Wienholtz (2013) and Steppenbeck (2013), and show a weakening of the $N=40$ shell closure in calcium compared to the isotone $^{68}$Ni where the spherical $N=40$ closure exists (PhysRevLett.88.092501). The predicted dripline at $^{70}$Ca agrees with recent Skyrme-type density functional theory calculations (PhysRevLett.122.062502), and the predicted resonant states provide guidance for future experiments on particle emissions in neutron-rich calcium isotopes.
