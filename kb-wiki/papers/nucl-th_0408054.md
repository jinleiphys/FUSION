# Role of the baryon resonances in the eta and K+ photoproduction processes on the proton

> Very recent eta and K+ photoproduction data on the proton from threshold up to E = 2.6 GeV are interpreted within a chiral constituent quark formalism, which embodies all known nucleon and hyperon resonances. Possible contributions from an additional S11 resonance are presented.

# Role of the baryon resonances in the eta and K+ photoproduction processes on the proton

## Key claim

A chiral constituent quark model embodying all known nucleon and hyperon resonances can reproduce recent eta and K+ photoproduction data from threshold up to 2.6 GeV, but only if an additional third S11 resonance is introduced; the improvement in chi-squared is substantial for the eta channel (more than a factor of 2) and significant for the kaon channel when fitting the JLab and SAPHIR data sets separately.

## Method

The author uses a chiral constituent quark formalism based on the broken SU(6) x O(3) symmetry, with the low-energy QCD Lagrangian as the starting point. The model includes s-channel, u-channel, and seagull terms, while t-channel contributions are excluded via the duality hypothesis; for resonances below 2 GeV, transition amplitudes are translated into standard CGLN amplitudes in the harmonic oscillator basis, while resonances above 2 GeV are treated as degenerate. Adjustable parameters are the eta-N-N or K-Y-N coupling constants and one SU(6) x O(3) symmetry breaking strength coefficient per resonance, except for the S11(1535)/S11(1650) and D13(1520)/D13(1700) pairs where configuration mixing angles are introduced instead.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 650 data points for the gamma p -> eta p reaction were fitted, including differential cross sections and single polarization asymmetries.
- For the eta channel, the model without the extra resonance misses data above E_gamma_lab ~ 1 GeV; adding a third S11 resonance with M = 1.780 GeV and Gamma = 280 MeV improves the reduced chi-squared by more than a factor of 2.
- Configuration mixing angles extracted from the eta channel fit: theta_S = 12 degrees, theta_D = -35 degrees.
- For the gamma p -> K+ Lambda reaction, 1640 data points were fitted: fitting both JLab and SAPHIR data simultaneously gives reduced chi-squared = 3.7; adding a third S11 resonance improves this only slightly to 3.5.
- Fitting JLab data alone (920 points): reduced chi-squared = 3.0 without extra resonance, improving to 1.6 with a third S11 of M = 1.852 GeV and Gamma = 187 MeV.
- Fitting SAPHIR data alone (720 points): reduced chi-squared = 2.1 without extra resonance, improving to 1.4 with a third S11 of M = 1.835 GeV and Gamma = 246 MeV.
- The third S11 resonance mass from the eta channel (1.780 GeV) and from the kaon channel (1.835-1.852 GeV) is consistent with other studies: the Zagreb group reports M = 1.792 +/- 0.023 GeV and Gamma = 360 +/- 49 MeV; the BES Collaboration finds M = 1.800 +/- 0.040 GeV and Gamma = 165(+165/-85) MeV; a coupled-channel analysis by Chen gives M = 1.803 +/- 0.007 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian approaches (ELA) for both eta photoproduction (RPI, Tiator, Az) and kaon photoproduction (Cot, Ben, AS, You, Bel), which were limited in the number of resonances they could include due to the rapid increase of free parameters per resonance. The chiral constituent quark approach allows inclusion of all known nucleon and hyperon resonances (listed from the PDG, with masses up to 2.5 GeV) and is motivated by QCD-inspired formalisms predicting new baryon resonances. The results are consistent with findings from the Zagreb group (coupled-channel pi-N and eta-N analysis), the BES Collaboration (J/psi decay), the hypercentral constituent quark model (Giannini), and a coupled-channel analysis by Chen, all showing evidence for a third S11 resonance around 1.8 GeV. The work enables future studies incorporating coupled-channel effects, which have been reported to induce 20% changes on the total cross section of the direct channel, and extension to polarization observables and the gamma p -> K+ Sigma0 channel.
