# In-medium properties of antikaons at finite temperature: the K^-/K^+ ratio at GSI

> The $K^-/K^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at GSI is studied including the properties of the participating hadrons in hot and dense matter, paying a particular attention to the in-medium properties of antikaons at finite temperature. The determination of the temperature and chemical potential at freeze-out conditions compatible with the ratio $K^-/K^+$ is very delicate, and depends on the approach adopted for the antikaon self-energy. Different approaches for the $K^-$ self-energy have been considered: non-interacting $K^-$, on-shell self-energy and single-particle spectral density. We observe that the use of an energy dependent $\bar{K}$ spectral density lowers considerably the freeze-out temperature with respect to the on-shell approach. We also conclude that the full off-shell approach gives rise to the "broad-band equilibration" advocated by Brown, Rho and Song.

## Key claim

The temperature and chemical potential at freeze-out that are compatible with the measured $K^-/K^+$ ratio depend very strongly on the approach used for the in-medium antikaon self-energy; the full off-shell spectral density approach gives a freeze-out temperature around 35 MeV and a baryonic chemical potential up to 850 MeV, but this temperature is too low to be compatible with measured freeze-out temperatures and the resulting $K^-/K^+$ ratio exceeds the experimental value by a factor of 2.

## Method

The authors used a statistical model with exact strangeness conservation to compute the $K^-/K^+$ ratio from one-particle partition functions for hadrons in hot and dense matter. For antikaons, they compared three treatments: a non-interacting free gas, an on-shell (mean-field) self-energy from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, and a full off-shell approach using the $K^-$ spectral density including both s-wave and p-wave components from the coupling to hyperon-hole ($YN^{-1}$) states. Hyperons ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$) were dressed with mean-field potentials, and the $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance was described by a Breit-Wigner shape.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $K^-/K^+$ ratio used for comparison is 0.031 $\pm$ 0.005, measured in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV (Menzel).
- For the full $K^-$ spectral density approach with $K^+/K^- = 30$, the freeze-out temperature is $T \approx 35$ MeV and the baryonic chemical potential $\mu_B$ reaches up to 850 MeV.
- At a more plausible freeze-out temperature of $T \approx 70$ MeV, the $K^+/K^-$ ratio is around 15 for a large region of baryonic chemical potential, corresponding to a $K^-/K^+$ ratio about a factor of 2 larger than the experimental value.
- The $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance is described with mass $m_{\Sigma^*} = 1385$ MeV and width $\Gamma = 37$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends statistical model calculations of the $K^-/K^+$ ratio by Cleymans (1999) and others, which used free hadron properties, by incorporating in-medium dressing of antikaons at finite temperature. The study tests the "broad-band equilibration" concept introduced by Brown, Rho and Song, which claimed that medium effects could explain the centrality independence of the $K^-/K^+$ ratio observed by the KaoS collaboration at GSI. The authors find that the full spectral density approach produces a broad band of chemical potentials but at temperatures too low to be realistic, and they note that dynamical non-equilibrium effects need to be addressed in future work to clarify how particles get on-shell at freeze-out.
