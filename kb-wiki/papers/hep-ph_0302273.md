# J/psi couplings to charmed resonances and to pi

> We present an evaluation of the strong couplings JD^(*)D^(*) and JD^(*)D^(*)pi by an effective field theory of quarks and mesons. These couplings are necessary to calculate pi+J/psi --> D^(*)+barD^(*) cross sections, an important background to the J/psi suppression signal in the quark-gluon plasma. We write down the general effective lagrangian and compute the relevant couplings in the soft pion limit and beyond.

## Key claim

The authors compute the strong couplings \(JD^{(*)}D^{(*)}\) and \(JD^{(*)}D^{(*)}\pi\) using a Constituent Quark Meson (CQM) model that incorporates Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) symmetries and chiral symmetry, finding that the couplings are significantly different from \(SU_4\) symmetry predictions due to the large charm quark mass. The results are presented with the caveat that the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) ansatz used to relate the Isgur-Wise function to the \(J/\psi\) couplings is reliable only for \(p^2\) up to about 4 GeV\(^2\), and the method becomes unreliable for larger virtualities due to incomplete cancellation between a kinematical zero and a pole.

## Method

The authors employ the Constituent Quark Meson (CQM) model, an effective field theory where light and heavy quarks and mesons interact via quark-loop diagrams. The \(JD^{(*)}D^{(*)}\) couplings are obtained by computing the Isgur-Wise function \(\xi(\omega)\) from a quark loop and then using a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) ansatz to relate it to the \(J/\psi\) coupling. The \(JD^{(*)}D^{(*)}\pi\) couplings are computed by inserting a soft pion on the light quark line in the same loop diagram, again using VMD, and then extended beyond the soft pion limit by computing form factors as functions of the pion momentum \(|\vec q_\pi|\).

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{JDD} = 8.0 \pm 0.5\) (dimensionless), for on-shell \(D\) mesons and \(J/\psi\) virtuality \(p^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\).
*   \(g_{JDD^*} = 4.05 \pm 0.25\) GeV\(^{-1}\), for the same kinematics.
*   \(g_{JD^*D^*} = 8.0 \pm 0.5\) (dimensionless), equal to \(g_{JDD}\) by HQET spin symmetry.
*   \(g_{D^*D\pi}^{spl} = 13 \pm 1\) (dimensionless), in the soft pion limit.
*   \(g_0/m_D = 125 \pm 15\) GeV\(^{-3}\) (soft pion limit), for the \(JDD\pi\) coupling.
*   Form factor mass parameter for \(JD^{(*)}D^{(*)}\pi\) couplings: \(m_k = 0.20 \pm 0.05\) GeV.
*   Form factor mass parameter for \(g_{D^*D\pi}\): \(m_\chi = 0.37\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(J/\psi\) absorption in heavy ion collisions, which is a background to the \(J/\psi\) suppression signal for quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors improve upon earlier estimates that used \(SU_4\) symmetry (which they argue is invalid for charm) or QCD sum rules (which have large theoretical uncertainties). The results are compared with VMD-based calculations by Cina et al. (2001) and Haglin (2000), and with QCD sum rule results by QCDSR (2000). The computed couplings and form factors enable more reliable calculations of the \(\pi + J/\psi \to D^{(*)} + \bar{D}^{(*)}\) cross sections needed for interpreting heavy-ion collision data.
