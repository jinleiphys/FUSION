# Exact Sum Rules at Finite Temperature and Chemical Potential and their Application to QCD

> Within the framework of the operator product expansion (OPE) and the renormalization group equation (RGE), we show that the temperature and chemical potential dependence of the zeroth moment of a spectral function (SF) in an asymptotically free theory is completely determined by the one-loop structure of the theory. This exact result constrains the qualitative shape of SF's, and implies striking phenomenological effects near phase transitions.

## Key claim

The temperature and chemical potential dependence of the zeroth moment of a spectral function in an asymptotically free theory is completely determined by the one-loop structure of the theory, but only when the leading operator in the operator product expansion has a specific anomalous dimension: if the exponent η_n > 0 the zeroth moment is independent of T and μ, while if η_n = 0 it changes proportionally to the change of the leading condensate.

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) and renormalization group equation (RGE) to derive asymptotic expansions of the subtracted retarded correlator ΔK̃(iQ) and of the subtracted spectral function Δρ(u) in the uniform limit (k=0). By matching the leading terms of these expansions through the dispersion relation, they obtain exact sum rules for the zeroth moment of the spectral function. The results are verified in the Gross-Neveu model in the large-N limit.

## Key numbers

- For non-conserved scalar and pseudoscalar currents (γ_J = 1/4π²), the zeroth moment of the spectral function is independent of T and μ: ∫₀^∞ du² Δρ(u) = 0.
- For conserved vector and axial-vector currents (γ_V = γ_A = 0), the sum rule is: ∫₀^∞ du² Δρ(u) = aΔ⟨[mψ̄ψ]⟩ + Δ⟨[α_s G²]⟩/(2π) + 8Δ⟨[θ₀₀]⟩, where a = 6 for vector and a = -10 for axial channels.
- The chiral susceptibility χ(T,μ) = ∫₀^∞ du² ρ(u;T,μ)/u² diverges near the phase boundary for a continuous chiral transition.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) to finite temperature and chemical potential by deriving exact constraints that do not rely on model parameterizations of spectral functions. The results contradict naive assumptions that spectral function parameterizations valid at zero T and μ remain applicable at finite T and μ, and they enable more reliable extraction of dynamical information from lattice simulations and QCD sum rule calculations near phase transitions. The predicted soft modes near the chiral phase transition confirm the qualitative picture originally proposed in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Kunihiro (1989).
