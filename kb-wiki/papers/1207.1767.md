# Two Photon Exchange for Exclusive Pion Electroproduction

> We perform detailed calculations of two-photon-exchange QED corrections to the cross section of pion electroproduction. The results are obtained with and without the soft-photon approximation; analytic expressions for the radiative corrections are derived. The relative importance of the two-photon correction is analyzed for the kinematics of several experiments at Jefferson Lab. A significant, over 20%, effect due to two-photon exchange is predicted for the backward angles of electron scattering at large transferred momenta.

## Key claim

The authors predict a significant, over 20% effect due to two-photon exchange for the cross section of exclusive pion electroproduction, but only for backward angles of electron scattering at large transferred momenta; the magnitude of the correction is model-dependent when the soft-photon approximation is not used.

## Method

The authors calculated two-photon-exchange QED corrections to the cross sections of the processes \(e+p\rightarrow e+n+\pi^{+}\) and \(e+p\rightarrow e+p+\pi^{0}\) using three approaches: two soft-photon approximations (SPT and SPMT) and an exact model-dependent approach (FM) with monopole form-factors in photon-hadron couplings. The calculations were performed both analytically and numerically using FeynArts and FormCalc, with infrared divergences regularized by giving the photon a small rest mass and canceling that mass by adding the soft-photon bremsstrahlung contribution from the electron, proton, and charged pion.

## Key numbers

- A correction over 20% due to two-photon exchange is predicted for backward angles of electron scattering at large transferred momenta (no specific numerical value for the momentum transfer is given in the truncated text).
- The monopole form-factor used in the FM approach has \(\Lambda^{2}=0.83 m_{p}^{2}\).
- The low-energy constants used in the pion photoproduction coupling are \(D=0.40\pm0.03\) and \(F=0.61\pm0.04\), with the pion coupling constant \(f_{\pi}=\sqrt{2}\cdot 92\,\text{MeV}\).
- For \(\pi^{0}\) electroproduction, the maximum soft-photon energy is \(\Delta\varepsilon=\frac{3}{4}m_{\pi}\); for \(\pi^{+}\) electroproduction, \(\Delta\varepsilon=m_{\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of two-photon exchange corrections from elastic electron-proton scattering (Blunden 2005) and elastic electron-pion scattering (Blunden 2010, Dong and Wong 2010, Borisyuk 2010) to the full exclusive pion electroproduction process on a nucleon. It also builds on earlier QED radiative correction calculations for exclusive pion electroproduction by Afanasev (2002), which included vacuum polarization, bremsstrahlung, and vertex corrections but omitted box diagrams with two-photon exchange. The results enable more precise interpretation of pion form factor measurements at Jefferson Lab and other facilities, particularly at backward angles where the two-photon correction is largest.
