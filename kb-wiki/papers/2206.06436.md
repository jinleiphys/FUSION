# Proton and neutron exchange as a prelude to fusion at near-barrier energies

> Systematic examination of fusion for $^{39,41,45,47}$K + $^{28}$Si and $^{36,44}$Ar + $^{28}$Si provides insight into the impact of neutron and proton exchange on fusion for nuclei at and near the N=20 and N=28 shells. Comparison of the reduced excitation functions reveals a marked difference between the behavior of open-shell and closed-shell systems. While coupled channels calculations provide a good description for the closed-shell nuclei they significantly under-predict the fusion cross-section for open-shell nuclei. The observed trends are examined in the context of a potential energy surface, including shell effects, and multi-nucleon exchange with consideration of Pauli-blocking.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at sub-barrier energies, open-shell nuclei (with unfilled neutron shells) exhibit a significantly larger reduced fusion cross-section compared to closed-shell nuclei, and that this enhancement cannot be explained by ground-state density distributions, coupled-channels calculations including low-lying excitations, or simple Q-value considerations for neutron transfer alone; instead, the authors argue that the interplay of both proton and neutron exchange, moderated by Pauli-blocking, provides a qualitative explanation for the observed differences.

## Method

Fusion excitation functions were measured for radioactive beams of \(^{39,41,45,47}\)K and \(^{36,44}\)Ar incident on a \(^{28}\)Si target at energies near and below the Coulomb barrier, using the ReA3 reaccelerator at the NSCL at Michigan State University. Evaporation residues were detected in annular silicon detectors using the energy/time-of-flight technique, and the fusion cross-section was extracted from the residue yield corrected for detection efficiency (78-84%) calculated with a statistical model. The experimental excitation functions were compared to theoretical predictions from the Sao Paulo model using Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov ground-state densities (one-channel) and coupled-channels calculations including the \(2^+\) and \(4^+\) states of \(^{28}\)Si, and the results were interpreted qualitatively using potential energy surfaces for binary fragmentation including shell and proximity corrections.

## Key numbers

- \(^{28}\)Si target thickness: 258 \(\pm\) 10 \(\mu\)g/cm\(^2\); oxygen contamination thickness: 98 \(\pm\) 4 \(\mu\)g/cm\(^2\)
- Beam intensities ranged from 1.0 \(\times\) 10\(^4\) (\(^{44}\)Ar/s) to 4.5 \(\times\) 10\(^4\) (\(^{39}\)K/s)
- Detection efficiency for evaporation residues varied between approximately 78% and 84% over the entire energy range
- Q-values for two-neutron transfer: \(^{39}\)K: -6.068 MeV; \(^{41}\)K: +1.187 MeV; \(^{45}\)K: +2.899 MeV; \(^{47}\)K: +3.843 MeV
- Q-values for one-neutron transfer: \(^{39}\)K: -4.604 MeV; \(^{41}\)K: -1.621 MeV; \(^{45}\)K: -0.432 MeV; \(^{47}\)K: +0.105 MeV
- The strong absorption radius for the potential energy surface calculations was approximately 10 fm for all systems

## Context

This work extends the prior above-barrier study by Singh (2021) which found that open-shell nuclei near the N=20 and N=28 shells have larger fusion cross-sections than closed-shell nuclei after accounting for size and Coulomb effects, now examining the sub-barrier regime for the first time for these K and Ar isotopes. The results contradict the ability of standard coupled-channels calculations (using the Sao Paulo model with DHB densities) to describe open-shell systems, while confirming that such calculations work well for closed-shell nuclei like \(^{39}\)K and \(^{47}\)K. The qualitative interpretation using potential energy surfaces and Pauli-blocking of nucleon exchange builds on the stochastic nucleon exchange framework developed by Schroder (1984) and Randrup (1979) for damped collisions, and the findings motivate development of a more complete theoretical description that properly accounts for multi-nucleon transfer and Pauli-blocking in the initial stages of fusion.
