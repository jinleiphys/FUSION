# Influence of a Critical Point on Hydrodynamic Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Hydrodynamic fluctuations have been studied in a wide variety of physical, chemical, and biological phenomena in the past decade. In high energy heavy ion collisions, there will be intrinsic fluctuations even if the initial conditions are fixed. Fluctuations will be greatly enhanced if the trajectory in the plane of temperature versus chemical potential passes near a critical point. We construct a model for the thermal conductivity which diverges at the critical point with the correct critical exponents, and use it in a simple illustrative model of a heavy ion collision. The proton correlation function is sensitive to the presence of the critical point.

# Influence of a Critical Point on Hydrodynamic Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Hydrodynamic fluctuations in heavy ion collisions will be greatly enhanced if the trajectory in the temperature versus chemical potential plane passes near a critical point, and the proton correlation function is sensitive to the presence of that critical point. However, the authors note that if the initial entropy per baryon is much larger than that at the critical point, the expanding matter will never pass close to it even under ideal adiabatic expansion.

## Method
The authors constructed a model for thermal conductivity that diverges at the critical point with correct critical exponents using mode-coupling theory, combined with prior work on QCD critical fluctuations. They implemented this in a 1+1 dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken model of heavy ion collisions, solving the smooth perfect fluid equations of motion with a parametrized equation of state, and treating fluctuations as perturbations solved via Green functions.

## Key numbers
- The thermal conductivity enhancement is parametrized as Δλ = c_P × (R_D T)/(6π η_shear ξ) × Ω(q_D ξ), where R_D is a universal constant approximately equal to 1.05
- The equation of state used is P(T,μ) = A_4 T^4 + A_2 μ^2 T^2 + A_0 μ^4 - C T^2 - B, with coefficients A_i appropriate to a plasma with 3 colors and 3 flavors of massless quarks
- Three adiabatic trajectories were studied, each passing increasingly closer to the critical point, with trajectory III showing the largest effect on the proton correlation function

## Context
This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations in heavy ion collisions from Kapusta (2012), which focused on shear viscosity at high energies with zero net baryon number, to the regime of high baryon densities relevant for lower energy collisions. It builds on mode-coupling theory successful for atomic and molecular systems near critical points, and on prior QCD fluctuation studies by Kapusta (2010) that considered static systems in contact with a heat reservoir. The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical point at facilities including RHIC low energy runs, FAIR, SHINE, and NICA.
