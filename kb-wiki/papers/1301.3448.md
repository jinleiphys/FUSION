# Matrix elements of four-quark operators and ΔL=2 hyperon decays

> The study of neutrinoless double beta decays of nuclei and hyperons require the calculation of hadronic matrix elements of local four-quark operators that change the total charge by two units ΔQ=2 . Using a low energy effective Lagrangian that induces these transitions, we compute these hadronic matrix elements in the framework of the MIT bag model. As an illustrative example we evaluate the amplitude and transition rate of Σ- -> p e- e-, a decay process that violates lepton number by two units (ΔL=2). The relevant matrix element is evaluated without assuming the usual factorization approximation of the four-quark operators and the results obtained in both approaches are compared.

## Key claim

The MIT bag model calculation of the hadronic matrix elements for the $\Sigma^- \to p e^- e^-$ decay yields a branching ratio upper limit of order $10^{-23}$, which is ten orders of magnitude larger than the estimate from a loop model with virtual Majorana neutrinos and intermediate baryon states (Barbero 2007), but still far below current experimental sensitivity.

## Method

The authors compute hadronic matrix elements of local four-quark operators with $\Delta Q=2$ using the MIT bag model, without invoking the factorization approximation that inserts intermediate baryon states. They evaluate the non-vanishing form factors for the $\Sigma^- \to p$ transition in the non-relativistic limit using spin-flavor wavefunctions of the hyperon states, then contract these with the leptonic tensor to obtain the decay amplitude and branching ratio for $\Sigma^- \to p e^- e^-$.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for $\Sigma^- \to p e^- e^-$: $B^{\rm bag}(\Sigma^- \to p e^- e^-) = (c_2 d_1 / \Lambda_{\beta\beta})^2 \cdot (4.65 \times 10^{-13}\ {\rm MeV}^2)$, yielding $B^{\rm bag} \leq 10^{-23}$ for $c_i, d_i \sim O(1)$ and $\Lambda_{\beta\beta} \geq 100$ GeV.
- Ratio $R_{ee/e\nu}^{\rm bag}(\Sigma^-) \approx (c_2 d_1 / \Lambda_{\beta\beta})^2 \cdot (4.6 \times 10^{-10}\ {\rm MeV}^2)$, which is six orders of magnitude smaller than the dimensional estimate of Li (2007).
- The loop-model result from Barbero (2007) gives $R_{ee/e\nu}^{\rm loop}(\Sigma^-) = 7.2 \times 10^{-18} |\langle m_{ee} \rangle|^2$ MeV$^{-2}$, corresponding to $B^{\rm loop}(\Sigma^- \to p e^- e^-) \leq 7.3 \times 10^{-33}$ for $|\langle m_{ee} \rangle| \leq 1$ eV.
- Effective form factors in the non-relativistic limit: $A = 3.56 \times 10^5$ MeV$^3$ and $B = 0$ for the $\Sigma^- \to p$ transition.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Li (2007) for $\Delta L=2$ hyperon decays by providing a direct bag-model calculation of the four-quark operator matrix elements, avoiding the factorization approximation used in earlier nuclear double-beta decay calculations and in hyperon loop models (Barbero 2002, Barbero 2007). The result enables comparison between different theoretical treatments of hadronic matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta processes, showing that the loop-model approach may underestimate branching fractions by many orders of magnitude. The method can be applied to other hyperon decay channels listed in the paper, such as $\Xi^- \to p e^- e^-$ and $\Omega^- \to \Sigma^+ e^- e^-$.
