# Inverse meson mass ordering in color-flavor-locking phase of high density QCD: erratum

## Key claim
The meson masses in the color-flavor-locking phase of high-density QCD are of order \(m_q \Delta / \mu\) rather than \(m_q\) as previously reported, and a truly inverse mass hierarchy emerges because the constant \(c'\) vanishes to leading order in perturbation theory.

## Method
The authors recalculated the meson masses by correcting a mistake in the earlier computation: the quark mass insertion changes the quark mass shell from \(E = |\mathbf{p}| - \mu\) to \(E = -|\mathbf{p}| - \mu\), making an almost on-shell quark near the Fermi surface become an off-shell particle with virtuality \(2\mu\). The constants \(c\) and \(c'\) were recomputed via matching vacuum energies, evaluating two Feynman diagrams (plus their parity and charge conjugation mirrors) that contribute to the shift of vacuum energy to leading order in the strong coupling \(g\).

## Key numbers
- The constant \(c\) is given by \(c = 3\Delta^2 / (2\pi^2)\), where \(\Delta\) is the value of the superconducting gap at the Fermi surface.
- The constant \(c'\) vanishes to leading order in perturbation theory.
- Parametrically, meson masses scale as \(m_q \Delta / \mu\) instead of \(m_q\) as reported in the earlier paper (Son and Stephanov, 1999).

## Context
This erratum corrects a mistake in the meson mass calculation of Son and Stephanov (1999) for the color-flavor-locking phase of high-density QCD. The correction was motivated by the work of Hong (year not given in text), who noted the change in quark mass shell upon mass insertion. The corrected result confirms the inverse meson mass ordering anticipated by heuristic arguments in the original paper, and the calculation shows that magnetic gluon exchange cancels between the two diagrams, leaving a result proportional to \(\Delta^2\) rather than \(\Delta^2 \log(\mu / \Delta)\).
