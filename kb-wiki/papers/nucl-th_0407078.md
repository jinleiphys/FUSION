# Orthonormalization procedure for chiral effective nuclear field theory

> We show that the Q-box expansion of nuclear many-body physics can be applied in nuclear effective field theory with explicit pions and external sources. We establish the corresponding power counting and give an algorithm for the construction of a hermitean and energy-independent potential for arbitrary scattering processes on nucleons and nuclei to a given order in the chiral expansion. Various examples are discussed in some detail.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Q-box expansion of nuclear many-body physics can be applied to chiral effective field theory with explicit pions and external sources, and that this expansion respects the chiral expansion such that only a finite number of Q-boxes contribute to the effective potential at a given chiral order, provided the model space is chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors derive a hermitean and energy-independent effective potential using the Q-box expansion method of Kuo and collaborators, building on the unitary transformation approach of Okubo and the decoupling equation formalism of Suzuki and Okamoto. They establish a power counting scheme for the Q-box expansion and provide an explicit algorithm for constructing the effective potential to a given chiral order by drawing all covariant diagrams, classifying time-orderings by the number of model-space intermediate cuts, and shifting those cuts to complementary-space cuts to generate irreducible energy denominators.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is given as Lambda_chi approximately 1 GeV.
* The power counting formula for a diagram is nu = 4 - N - 2C + 2L + sum_i V_i Delta_i, where Delta_i = d_i + (1/2)n_i - 2, with N the number of nucleons, C the number of connected pieces, L the number of loops, V_i the number of vertices of type i, d_i the number of derivatives at vertex i, and n_i the number of nucleons at vertex i.
* The function F(m,n) for the hermitean potential expansion takes values: F(0,0)=1/2, F(0,1)=-1/8, F(1,0)=1/8, F(0,2)=1/16, F(1,1)=0, F(2,0)=-1/16.

## Context

This work extends the unitary transformation method of Epelbaum and collaborators (2000) for constructing hermitean energy-independent nucleon-nucleon potentials in chiral effective field theory, which itself built on the earlier work of Okubo (1954) and Fukuda, Sawada and Taketani. The Q-box approach offers an alternative to the unitary transformation method that avoids the need to define different model spaces and construct the operator A for each different process, enabling a unified treatment of nuclear wave functions and irreducible kernels for scattering processes on nucleons and nuclei.
