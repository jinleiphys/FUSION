# Quantum catastrophes from an algebraic perspective

> We study the properties of quantum cusp and butterfly catastrophes from an algebraic viewpoint. The analysis employs an interacting boson model Hamiltonian describing quantum phase transitions between specific quadrupole shapes by interpolating between two incompatible dynamical symmetry limits. The classical properties are determined by using coherent states to construct the complete phase diagrams associated with Landau potentials exhibiting such catastrophes.The quantum properties are determined by analyzing the spectra, transition rates and symmetry character of the eigenstates of critical Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quantum cusp and butterfly catastrophes can be realized algebraically within the interacting boson model (IBM), with the butterfly catastrophe requiring cubic interaction terms beyond the usual one- and two-body Hamiltonian to accommodate first-order quantum phase transitions involving coexistence of spherical and deformed shapes.

## Method

The authors construct IBM Hamiltonians that interpolate between the U(5) and SO(6) dynamical symmetry limits, using coherent states to derive classical energy surfaces that serve as Landau potentials. For the cusp catastrophe, a one- and two-body Hamiltonian with SO(5) symmetry yields a quartic energy surface; for the butterfly catastrophe, a cubic term is added to produce a sextic energy surface. The quantum properties are studied by numerically diagonalizing the critical Hamiltonians and analyzing the resulting spectra, E2 transition rates, and wave function decompositions in both the U(5) and SO(6) bases.

## Key numbers

- The cusp catastrophe critical point occurs at control parameter value a=0, corresponding to ξ=1/4 in the Hamiltonian of Eq. (H_u5o6)
- The butterfly catastrophe critical line is given by D²-4AF=0 with F>0, D<0, and the barrier height between degenerate minima is h = (2/27)(1- r̄)³ where r̄ ranges from 0 to 1
- For the butterfly catastrophe, the deformed minimum position at the critical line is β₊² = (1- r̄)/(1+ r̄) and the barrier position is β₋² = (1- r̄)/(5+ r̄)
- The tricritical point in the butterfly phase diagram occurs at F=D=0 in the general parameters, or at ε̄=3, r̄=1 in the IBM Hamiltonian parameters
- All spectra are calculated with boson number N=25

## Context

This work extends previous catastrophe theory analyses of the IBM that focused primarily on classical phase diagrams for the cusp catastrophe (Castanos 1996, Cejnar and Iachello 2007, Hellemans 2007) and, to a lesser extent, the butterfly catastrophe (Hellemans 2007, Ramos 2016). The algebraic approach complements earlier studies of quantum catastrophes that solved Schrodinger equations with potentials taken from elementary catastrophe theory (Gilmore 1986, Emary 2005, Cejnar 2008). The critical Hamiltonian for the cusp catastrophe reproduces features of the E(5) critical-point symmetry of Iachello (2000), and the results enable systematic study of quantum shape-phase transitions in nuclei where experimental examples of E(5)-like structure have been observed (Casten and Zamfir 2000).
