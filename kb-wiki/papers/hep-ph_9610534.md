# Bound States and Power Counting in Effective Field Theories

> The problem of bound states in effective field theories is studied. A rescaled version of nonrelativistic effective field theory is formulated which makes the velocity power counting of operators manifest. Results obtained using the rescaled theory are compared with known results from NRQCD. The same ideas are then applied to study Yukawa bound states in 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions, and to analyze when the Yukawa potential can be replaced by a delta-function potential. The implications of these results for the study of nucleon-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a rescaled version of nonrelativistic effective field theory makes velocity power counting manifest, but the validity of replacing a Yukawa potential with a delta-function potential in the effective theory depends on whether bound states exist near threshold; when a weakly bound state is present, the effective four-Fermi theory can produce that bound state with perturbative coefficients only in 1+1 dimensions and only when there is exactly one bound state.

## Method

The authors formulate a rescaled nonrelativistic effective field theory (RNRQCD) by rescaling coordinates and fields so that energy and momentum are of order unity, making the velocity (v) power counting of operators manifest from the Lagrangian. They then apply this rescaling to study Yukawa bound states in 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions, comparing the full Yukawa theory to an effective four-Fermi theory where the scalar is integrated out, and analyze the convergence of the ladder graph sum to determine when the effective theory correctly reproduces bound state properties.

## Key numbers

- In 1+1 dimensions, the Yukawa potential is V(x) = -g^2/(2m) exp(-m|x|), and the delta-function approximation gives a bound state energy E = -g^4 M/(4 m^4) with wavefunction parameter kappa = g^2 M / m^2.
- The one-loop box graph in the rescaled Yukawa theory in 1+1 dimensions gives a leading term i g^4 / (2 m^4 v^2 epsilon^{1/2}) where epsilon is the rescaled energy, with a subleading term -i 3 g^4 M / (4 m^5 v).
- The two-loop box graph in 1+1 dimensions gives a leading term i g^6 / (4 m^6 v^3 epsilon) with subleading terms -i 3 g^6 M / (4 m^7 v^2 sqrt(epsilon)) and +i 41 g^6 M^2 / (24 m^8 v).
- The bound state pole from summing the most singular ladder terms in 1+1 dimensions occurs at epsilon = g^4 / (4 m^4 v^2), which rescales to E = -g^4 M / (4 m^4).

## Context

This work extends the power counting analysis of NRQCD from Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995) by providing a manifest velocity-counting formulation through field rescaling. It then applies these techniques to analyze the validity of effective field theory approaches to nucleon-nucleon scattering proposed by Weinberg (1990, 1991), van Kolck (1994), and Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996). The results show that in 3+1 dimensions, the effective four-Fermi theory has a finite radius of convergence only when no bound state exists near threshold, which has implications for whether chiral perturbation theory can systematically describe nucleon-nucleon scattering without large coefficients in higher-dimension operators.
