# Possibility of narrow resonances in nucleon-nucleon channels

> Compound states manifest themselves as bound states, resonances, or primitives, and their character is determined by their interaction with the continuum. If the interaction experiences a perturbation, a compound state can change its manifestation. Phase analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering indicates the existence of primitives in the 3S1, 1S0, and 3P0 channels. Electromagnetic interaction can shift primitives from the unitary cut, turning them into narrow resonances. We evaluate this effect on the 1S0 proton-proton scattering channel within the framework of the Simonov-Dyson model. We show that electromagnetic interaction turns a primitve with a mass of 2000 MeV into a dibaryon resonance of approximately the same mass and a width of 260 keV. Narrow resonances of a similar nature may occur in other nucleon-nucleon channels. Experimental confirmation of the existence of narrow resonances would have important implications for the theory of nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic interaction can shift a "primitive" (a zero of the D-function on the unitary cut) in the 1S0 proton-proton channel into a narrow dibaryon resonance, provided the underlying strong interaction is described by the Simonov-Dyson model rather than by one-boson-exchange (OBE) models or the hybrid Lee model, where primitives remain stable on the cut.

## Method

The author uses the Simonov-Dyson model, an extension of the Dyson model that incorporates both attraction and repulsion, to describe nucleon-nucleon scattering. The D-function of the model is constructed from a sum over 6-quark compound states, a contact four-fermion interaction, and a dispersion integral over the two-nucleon phase space that includes a form factor with the Gamow-Sommerfeld factor for Coulomb interaction. The model parameters (scattering length, effective range, interaction radius, compound-state mass) are fixed by fitting the low-energy proton-proton scattering phase shift and the empirical scattering lengths in different isospin channels, and then the electromagnetic mass shifts of the nucleons and the 6-quark state are introduced to compute the resulting complex pole of the D-function.

## Key numbers

*   A primitive with a mass of 2000 MeV in the 1S0 proton-proton channel becomes a dibaryon resonance with a mass of 2005.5 MeV and a width of 260 keV when electromagnetic interaction is included.
*   The proton kinetic energy in the laboratory frame corresponding to the vanishing phase shift (the primitive location) is 244 MeV.
*   The scattering length in the proton-proton channel is fitted to 6.5 fm, compared to the empirical value of 7.83 fm.
*   The effective range in the 1S0 proton-proton channel with electromagnetic interaction is 0.84 fm.
*   The interaction radius b is 1.831 fm with electromagnetic interaction and 1.829 fm without it.
*   The compound state mass M is 2003.5 MeV with electromagnetic interaction and 1998.6 MeV without it.
*   The electromagnetic mass shift of the d*+ (I3=0) 6-quark state is -6.2 MeV, and for the d*++ (I3=1) state it is +5.0 MeV.
*   The 3P0 channel is predicted to have a primitive with a mass of about 1970 MeV, which would become a resonance with a width of a few hundred keV.

## Context

This work extends the Simonov-Dyson model (Simonov 1981) and the P-matrix formalism (Jaffe and Low 1979) for nucleon-nucleon interaction, where repulsion is generated by "primitives" (6-quark compound states). It contrasts with the standard one-boson-exchange (OBE) models and the hybrid Lee model, where primitives remain on the unitary cut and do not become resonances under perturbations. The predicted narrow dibaryon resonance offers a specific experimental test to discriminate between these competing theoretical frameworks, and its existence would have implications for the equation of state of nuclear matter and neutron star properties (Krivoruchenko 1987).
