# Stochastic potential and quantum decoherence of heavy quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma

> We propose an open quantum systems approach to the physics of heavy quarkonia in a thermal medium, based on stochastic quantum evolution. This description emphasizes the importance of collisions with the environment and focuses on the concept of spatial decoherence of the heavy quarkonium wave function. It is shown how to determine the parameters of the dynamical evolution, i.e. the real potential and the noise strength, from a comparison with quantities to be obtained from lattice QCD. Furthermore the imaginary part of the lattice QCD heavy quark potential is found to be naturally related to the strength of the noise correlations. We discuss the time evolution of $Q\bar{Q}$ analytically in a limiting scenario for the spatial decoherence and provide a qualitative 1-dimensional numerical simulation of the real-time dynamics.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that heavy quarkonium in a quark-gluon plasma should be described as an open quantum system undergoing stochastic quantum evolution, where the imaginary part of the lattice QCD heavy quark potential is reinterpreted as the strength of noise correlations rather than as a deterministic damping term. The authors hedge that their formulation lacks a quantum analog of the drag force and is valid only for time scales shorter than the heavy quark relaxation time.

## Method
The authors formulate a stochastic Schrödinger equation for the heavy quarkonium wave function with a Gaussian white noise term, deriving a master equation for the reduced density matrix in Lindblad form. They relate the real potential and local noise strength to the position and width of the lowest-lying peak in the spectral function of the thermal Wilson loop, obtainable from lattice QCD via Maximum Entropy Method. One-dimensional numerical simulations are performed using a Crank-Nicholson scheme on a 512-point lattice covering x ∈ [-2.56 fm, 2.56 fm] with time step dt = 0.0001 fm, comparing four parameter sets adapted from lattice QCD, perturbative calculations, and the classic Debye screening scenario.

## Key numbers
- Temperature used in simulations: T = 2.33 T_c ~ 0.4 GeV
- Heavy quark mass set to M = 1.18 GeV
- Lattice spacing: dx = 2.56 fm / 256 = 0.01 fm (512 points over 5.12 fm)
- Number of bound states of the vacuum Hamiltonian: N^v_b = 4
- String breaking distance: r_sb = 1.5 fm
- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: α = 0.1, σ = (0.4 GeV)^2
- Thermal wavelength: l_th = 15 GeV^{-1}
- Correlation length for model (C): l_corr = 4 dx
- Time step for simulations: dt = 0.0001 fm

## Context
This work extends the open quantum systems approach to heavy quarkonia in the QGP, building on earlier attempts by Young (2010) and Borghini (2011). It directly connects to lattice QCD studies by Rothkopf (2011) that first confirmed an imaginary part in the heavy quark potential above T_c, and to perturbative calculations by Laine (2006) that derived a complex potential using Hard-Thermal-Loop approximation. The approach contrasts with purely real potential models based on free or internal energies by Kaczmarek (2005) and with spectral function analyses using Maximum Entropy Method by Asakawa (2000, 2003). The framework enables future determination of off-diagonal noise correlations from lattice QCD and incorporation of friction terms for thermalization.
