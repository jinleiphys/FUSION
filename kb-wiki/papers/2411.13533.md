# Finite-size effects on small-$x$ evolution and saturation in proton and nuclear targets

> Within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, we assess the importance of including a finite size for the target on observables sensitive to small-$x$ evolution. To this end, we study the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with impact-parameter dependence in the initial condition. We demonstrate that neglecting the dependence on the impact parameter can result in overestimated saturation effects for protons, while it has little effect for heavy nuclei at the energies available at current experiments. When fixing the nonperturbative parameters to the energy dependence of the exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction cross section with proton targets, predictions for lead targets are not sensitive to the applied running-coupling prescription, the scheme chosen to resum large transverse logarithms in the BK equation, or the infrared regulator in the evolution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neglecting impact-parameter dependence in the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation can overestimate saturation effects for proton targets, but has little effect for heavy nuclei at current experimental energies; however, this conclusion is conditional on fixing nonperturbative parameters to exclusive J/psi photoproduction data on protons, and the predictions for lead targets are insensitive to the choice of running-coupling prescription, resummation scheme, or infrared regulator.

## Method

The authors solve the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation with full impact-parameter dependence, using an impact-parameter-dependent McLerran-Venugopalan (IP-MV) model as the initial condition at rapidity Y0 = log(1/0.01). They compare this to the case where the impact-parameter dependence is integrated out before evolution, and study the effects of four different running-coupling prescriptions (daughter dipole, parent dipole, smallest dipole, and Balitsky prescription) and three different schemes for resumming higher-order corrections (kinematically constrained BK, target BK, and resummed BK with single and double transverse logarithms). The nonperturbative parameters are fixed by fitting to the energy dependence of exclusive J/psi photoproduction cross sections on proton targets, and predictions are then made for lead targets using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density.

## Key numbers

* The proton thickness function uses a Gaussian profile with width B_p = 3 GeV^{-2}, the infrared regulator m = 0.4 GeV, and the constant kappa = 0.66, chosen to match the initial condition fitted to exclusive J/psi production data.
* The infrared regulator in the BK kernel is set to m' = 0.4 GeV as the standard setup, following the value used in Mantysaari (2022).
* The running coupling uses N_f = 3, mu_0 = 0.28 GeV, zeta = 0.2, and Lambda_QCD = 0.025 GeV, chosen to reproduce the energy dependence of exclusive J/psi production data.
* For nuclear targets, the Woods-Saxon parameters are d = 0.54 fm and R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of impact-parameter-dependent small-x evolution in the Color Glass Condensate framework, particularly following the approach of Mantysaari (2022) and Schlichting (2014) who used JIMWLK evolution. It addresses the tension observed in Mantysaari (2022) and Mantysaari (2023) where CGC calculations could not predict as large nuclear suppression as observed at the LHC, by enabling the study of higher-order resummation effects that are not available in the JIMWLK framework. The results enable more reliable predictions for diffractive vector meson production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC and future Electron-Ion Collider.
