# The Universal Equation of State near the Critical Point of QCD

> We study the universal properties of the phase diagram of QCD near the critical point using the exact renormalization group. For two-flavour QCD and zero quark masses we derive the universal equation of state in the vicinity of the tricritical point. For non-zero quark masses we explain how the universal equation of state of the Ising universality class can be used in order to describe the physical behaviour near the line of critical points. The effective exponents that parametrize the growth of physical quantities, such as the correlation length, are given by combinations of the critical exponents of the Ising class that depend on the path along which the critical point is approached. In general the critical region, in which such quantities become large, is smaller than naively expected.

## Key claim

For two-flavour QCD with non-zero quark masses, the universal equation of state near the line of critical points belongs to the Ising universality class, but the critical region in which physical quantities such as the correlation length become large is smaller than naively expected, because the effective exponents that parametrize their growth are combinations of Ising critical exponents that depend on the path along which the critical point is approached.

## Method

The authors used the exact renormalization group in the formalism of the effective average action, integrating out fluctuations above a cutoff scale k to derive the scale-dependent effective potential for a three-dimensional theory of mesonic fields (sigma and pions) with an O(4) symmetric potential. They started from the quark-meson model for two flavours, performed dimensional reduction at non-zero temperature to obtain an effective three-dimensional theory, and solved the flow equation for the potential to determine the equation of state near the tricritical point (zero quark mass) and near the line of critical points (non-zero quark mass).

## Key numbers

- For the tricritical point with zero quark mass, the critical exponents take mean-field values: beta = 0.25, nu = 0.5 for relatively large deviations from the critical mass term.
- For very small deviations from the critical mass term (dms approaching zero), the critical exponents cross over to O(4) universality class values: beta = 0.41, nu = 0.82.
- The tricritical point is located on the (m^2, lambda) plane between the points (0.0213, -0.335) and (0.0217, -0.338) for g = 1, with all quantities in units of the ultraviolet cutoff Lambda.
- The constituent quark mass at zero temperature and chemical potential is set to M_q = 300 MeV, the pion decay constant to f_pi = 87 MeV, and the sigma mass to m_sigma = 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the QCD phase diagram using the exact renormalization group, building on earlier studies of the quark-meson model by Jungnickel and Wetterich (1995) and Berges, Tetradis, and Wetterich (1998, 1997), as well as the demonstration by Tetradis (2003) that the expected phase diagram structure for two-flavour QCD can be reproduced within this model. The results enable quantitative predictions for the size of the critical region in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, and provide a universal equation of state that can be compared with lattice simulations such as those by Fodor and Katz (2001) and de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002).
