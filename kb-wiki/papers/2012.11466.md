# Measurement of the Ar(e,e$^\prime$ p) and Ti(e,e$^\prime$ p) cross sections in Jefferson Lab Hall A

> The E12-14-012 experiment, performed in Jefferson Lab Hall A, has collected exclusive electron-scattering data (e,e$^\prime$p) in parallel kinematics using natural argon and natural titanium targets. Here, we report the first results of the analysis of the data set corresponding to beam energy of 2,222 MeV, electron scattering angle 21.5 deg, and proton emission angle -50 deg. The differential cross sections, measured with $\sim$4% uncertainty, have been studied as a function of missing energy and missing momentum, and compared to the results of Monte Carlo simulations, obtained from a model based on the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first exclusive (e,e'p) cross section measurements on natural argon and natural titanium targets, but the reported differential cross sections are only for a single kinematic setting (beam energy 2.222 GeV, electron scattering angle 21.5 deg, proton emission angle -50 deg) and are compared to Monte Carlo simulations based on the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation rather than providing a direct extraction of the spectral function.

## Method

The experiment used the two high-resolution spectrometers in Jefferson Lab Hall A to detect scattered electrons and knocked-out protons in coincidence from natural argon and natural titanium targets, under parallel kinematics where the momentum transfer and outgoing proton momentum are parallel. The six-fold differential cross sections were extracted from the event yield as a function of missing energy and missing momentum, applying corrections for detector efficiencies, acceptance, radiative effects, and beam heating (for argon). The measured cross sections were compared to Monte Carlo simulations using the SIMC spectrometer package, which employed a test spectral function with shell-model states parameterized from previous data and included Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation treatment of final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2.222 GeV; electron scattering angle: 21.5 deg; proton emission angle: -50 deg
- Measured differential cross sections with approximately 4% uncertainty
- For argon: scattered electron energy 1.777 GeV, Q^2 = 0.549 GeV^2/c^2, missing momentum 50 MeV/c, missing energy 73 MeV
- For titanium: scattered electron energy 1.799 GeV, Q^2 = 0.556 GeV^2/c^2, missing momentum 58 MeV/c, missing energy 51 MeV
- Average beam current: approximately 15 microamps for argon target, 20 microamps for titanium target
- Overall detection efficiency: 39.6% to 48.9% across kinematic regions for argon; 46.8% to 48.1% for titanium
- Coincidence time cut efficiency: 54.8% for argon, 55.5% for titanium

## Context

This work extends the systematic (e,e'p) measurements previously performed at Saclay (fru84) and NIKHEF-K (NIKHEF) to the medium-mass nuclei argon and titanium, which are relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments using liquid-argon time-projection chambers such as SBN and DUNE. The proton spectral function of titanium is intended to serve as a proxy for the neutron spectral function of argon, exploiting the correspondence between titanium's proton number (Z=22) and argon's neutron number (A-Z=22), following the approach suggested by calculations of inclusive (e,e') cross sections using self-consistent Green's function methods (BRS). The results enable future extraction of the argon and titanium spectral functions needed for neutrino-nucleus interaction modeling in experiments like DUNE, where both proton and neutron spectral functions are required for oscillation analyses.
