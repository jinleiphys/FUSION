# Pre-hydrodynamic evolution in large and small systems

> We extend our previous investigation of the effects of pre-hydrodynamic evolution on final-state observables in heavy-ion collisions to smaller systems. We use a state-of-the-art hybrid model for the numerical simulations with optimal parameters obtained from a previous Bayesian study. By studying p-Pb collisions, we find that the effects due to the assumption of a conformal evolution in the pre-hydrodynamical stage are even more important in small systems. We also show that this effect depends on the time duration of the pre-equilibrium stage, which is further enhanced in small systems. Finally, we show that the recent proposal of a free-streaming with subluminal velocity for the pre-equilibrium stage, thus effectively breaking conformal invariance, can alleviate the contamination of final state observables. Our study further reinforces the need for moving beyond conformal approaches in pre-equilibrium dynamics modeling, especially when extracting transport coefficients from hybrid models in the high-precision era of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The assumption of conformal invariance in pre-hydrodynamic evolution models produces an artificially large bulk pressure at the switch to hydrodynamics, and this contamination of final-state observables is even more pronounced in small systems (p-Pb) than in large systems (Pb-Pb). Using a subluminal free-streaming velocity that breaks conformal invariance can alleviate this artifact, though the effect depends on the duration of the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid model with TRENTo initial conditions, a free-streaming pre-equilibrium stage with variable velocity, MUSIC 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamics, Cooper-Frye sampling via the frzout code, and UrQMD hadronic cascade. They simulated minimum-bias p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV for two free-streaming durations (0.37 fm/c and 1.2 fm/c) and two free-streaming velocities (c and 0.85c), with parameters from a previous Bayesian study by Bernhard et al. (2018). They also performed simulations where the initial bulk pressure was set to zero at the hydrodynamics switching time to isolate the artificial pressure effect.

## Key numbers
- Free-streaming durations tested: tau_fs = 0.37 fm/c and tau_fs = 1.2 fm/c
- Free-streaming velocities tested: v_fs = c and v_fs = 0.85 c
- Center-of-mass energy: 5.02 TeV for both p-Pb and Pb-Pb systems
- Hydrodynamics switching temperature: T_switch = 151 MeV
- Minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: (eta/s)_min = 0.11
- Maximum bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio: (zeta/s)_max = 0.032
- The ratio of initial bulk pressure to thermodynamic pressure (Pi/p) reaches values of order unity (approximately 1) for conformal free-streaming in p-Pb events
- For v_fs = 0.85 c, the free-streaming pressure approximates the QCD equation of state for a considerable range of energies
- The effect of free-streaming duration on transverse momentum spectra inverts around p_T range 1.5 - 2.0 GeV

## Context
This work extends the previous investigation by Nunes da Silva et al. (2020) on pre-hydrodynamic effects in heavy-ion collisions to smaller p-Pb systems. It builds on the Bayesian parameter extraction framework of Bernhard et al. (2018) and the subluminal free-streaming proposal by Nijs et al. (2020). The results demonstrate that moving beyond conformal pre-equilibrium models is even more critical for small systems, particularly for reliable extraction of transport coefficients in the high-precision era of heavy-ion collisions.
