# Bayesian constraints on covariant density functional equations of state of compact stars with new NICER mass-radius measurements

> Recent advancements in astrophysical observations of compact stars, particularly the new and updated NICER constraints, have provided mass-radius ($M$-$R$) data for pulsars spanning masses from 1 to $2\,M_{\odot }$. These data offer a unique opportunity to test modern theories of dense matter using multi-messenger constraints. Covariant density functional (CDF) models of nuclear matter, which capture a broad range of nuclear and astrophysical phenomena, provide a robust theoretical framework to interpret these observations. This study applies the Bayesian framework to a class of CDF models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, specifically those based on nucleonic degrees of freedom. By incorporating the latest multi-messenger constraints, we impose tighter limits on the parameter space of these models and assess their consistency with observational data. Our analysis advances previous efforts by refining the density-dependence parameterization and integrating recent $M$-$R$ ellipses. This enables more stringent evaluations of dense matter models in light of new astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

The paper applies Bayesian inference to covariant density functional (CDF) models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, finding that the new NICER mass-radius data for pulsars J0437 and J1231, together with reanalyses of J0030, refine but do not fundamentally alter the posterior distributions already established by the combination of massive pulsar masses, gravitational-wave tidal deformability constraints, and chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) calculations.

## Method

The authors use a Bayesian framework with seven adjustable CDF parameters (three coupling constants at saturation density and four density-dependence parameters) and uniform priors. They construct likelihood functions from Gaussian kernel density estimates of NICER posterior samples for four pulsars (J0740, J0030, J0437, J1231), from high-precision interpolation of gravitational-wave likelihoods for GW170817 and GW190425, and from Gaussian mass measurements for the massive pulsar J0348. Low-density nuclear matter constraints include Gaussian priors on saturation density, Dirac mass, saturation energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and chiEFT results for pure neutron matter at N3LO.

## Key numbers

* Radius of a canonical-mass (1.4 solar mass) star: 12.47 (+0.48, -0.50) km at 95.4% CI for scenario B (softest) and 12.79 (+0.55, -0.56) km for scenario F (stiffest)
* Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass star: 472 (+163, -121) at 95.4% CI for scenario B and 571 (+207, -162) for scenario F
* Maximum mass: 2.20 (+0.23, -0.17) solar masses for scenario B and 2.32 (+0.24, -0.24) solar masses for scenario F at 95.4% CI
* Incompressibility at saturation: K_sat = 231.4 (+34.4, -33.3) MeV for scenario B and 244.8 (+36.4, -34.7) MeV for scenario F at 68.3% CI
* Skewness at saturation: Q_sat = -391.0 (+333.6, -170.2) MeV for scenario B and -164.7 (+548.9, -280.1) MeV for scenario F at 68.3% CI
* Lower limit on trace anomaly for maximum-mass configurations: Delta >= -0.123 (+0.038, -0.027) for scenario B and Delta >= -0.134 (+0.049, -0.023) for scenario F at 95.4% CI
* Symmetry energy slope L_sym narrowly distributed around 47 MeV
* Radius of a 2 solar mass star has lower limit approximately 11 km at 95.4% CI
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass star has upper limit approximately 13.5 km from GW170817 tidal deformability

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of CDF models (Malik 2022, Beznogov 2023, Parmar 2024) by using the full density-dependent coupling forms rather than simplified exponential functions, and by incorporating the newest NICER results for J0437 (Choudhury 2024) and J1231 (Salmi 2024b) along with the reanalysis of J0030 (Vinciguerra 2024). The results enable more precise predictions for the composition and quasiparticle spectra of neutron star interiors, which are essential for studies of transport and neutrino interactions, and they show that the CDF models can produce massive neutron stars without violating causality (sound speed squared remains below 1).
