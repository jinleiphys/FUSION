# Electron-nucleus scattering and the polarization sum rules

> A formal derivation of the polarization correlations between the incident electron and the scattered electron is given for a general class of transition operators. In correspondence to the case of bremsstrahlung emission, three sum rules for the polarization correlations are predicted, which reduce to the known one for potential scattering. Further examples, including the 1_2^+ and 2_1^+ excitations of the 12C nucleus and a 1^- excitation of the 208Pb nucleus, are discussed and the validity of the corresponding sum rules is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper formally derives three seven-term sum rules for polarization correlations in electron-nucleus scattering, which reduce to the known three-term sum rule for potential scattering, but the sum rules are strictly valid only when the excited nucleus can be represented by a superposition of at most two final states (specifically, the two states with \(\pm M_f\) when the initial nuclear spin is zero).

## Method

The author extends the parametrization of the differential cross section for potential scattering to a general class of transition operators, introducing fifteen polarization correlations \(c_{jk}\). Using results from bremsstrahlung emission in coplanar geometry, three seven-term sum rules are derived and then applied to elastic scattering from spin-zero nuclei and to inelastic excitations of \(^{12}\)C and \(^{208}\)Pb within the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), using transition densities from the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) and Fourier-Bessel fits.

## Key numbers

- The \(1_2^+\) state of \(^{12}\)C at 15.11 MeV: the decay width is \(\Gamma = 35.9\) eV; the photon detector resolution is 0.64% (corresponding to a peak FWHM of 97 keV).
- For 27 MeV electrons on \(^{12}\)C exciting the \(1_2^+\) state, the three-term sum rule \(\Sigma_3\) is violated in the forward direction but well satisfied at large angles where the \(M_f=1\) state dominates.
- For 22 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb exciting the \(1^-\) state at 5.512 MeV, both \(\Sigma_3\) and \(\Sigma_7\) are approximately valid for \(\vartheta_f \lesssim 140^\circ\) when summed over all \(M_f\).
- For 30 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb, both sum rules deviate less from unity than at 22 MeV across the whole angular range.

## Context

This work extends the polarization sum rules previously established for potential scattering by Motz et al. and for bremsstrahlung emission by PMS and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2018). It provides a framework for testing theoretical models of nuclear excitation and for determining spin asymmetries that are not directly accessible experimentally. The numerical examples using the QPM transition densities from Ponomarev (2019) and Fourier-Bessel fits from Deutschmann et al. (1983) demonstrate the conditions under which the sum rules hold, enabling future experimental tests at facilities such as CEBAF and MAMI.
