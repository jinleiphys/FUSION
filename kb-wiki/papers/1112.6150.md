# Deuteron-equivalent and phase-equivalent interactions within light nuclei

> Background: Phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) are well-known in quantum scattering and inverse scattering theory. PETs do not affect scattering phase shifts and bound state energies of two-body system but are conventionally supposed to modify two-body bound state observables such as the rms radius and electromagnetic moments. Purpose: In order to preserve all bound state observables, we propose a new particular case of PETs, a deuteron-equivalent transformation (DET-PET), which leaves unchanged not only scattering phase shifts and bound state (deuteron) binding energy but also the bound state wave function. Methods: The construction of DET-PET is discussed; equations defining the simplest DET-PETs are derived. We apply these simplest DET-PETs to the JISP16 $NN$ interaction and use the transformed $NN$ interactions in calculations of $^3$H and $^4$He binding energies in the No-core Full Configuration (NCFC) approach based on extrapolations of the No-core Shell Model (NCSM) basis space results to the infinite basis space. Results: We demonstrate the DET-PET modification of the $np$ scattering wave functions and study the DET-PET manifestation in the binding energies of $^3$H and $^4$He nuclei and their correlation (Tjon line). Conclusions: It is shown that some DET-PETs generate modifications of the central component while the others modify the tensor component of the $NN$ interaction. DET-PETs are able to modify significantly the $np$ scattering wave functions and hence the off-shell properties of the $NN$ interaction. DET-PETs give rise to significant changes in the binding energies of $^3$H (in the range of approximately 1.5 MeV) and $^4$He (in the range of more than 9 MeV) and are able to modify the correlation patterns of binding energies of these nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new type of phase-equivalent transformation (DET-PET) that preserves not only scattering phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy but also the deuteron wave function itself, thereby keeping all two-body bound state observables unchanged while still modifying the off-shell properties of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors construct DET-PET by applying a finite unitary transformation to the Hamiltonian matrix in a basis of oscillator states, where the unitary submatrix only mixes basis vectors that are orthogonal to the deuteron wave function. They apply the simplest rank-2 DET-PETs to the JISP16 NN interaction, using various combinations of oscillator states (e.g., 0s, 2s, 1s, 3s or 0s, 1s, 0d, 1d) with a rotation angle β varied from 0° to 360°. The transformed interactions are then used to calculate ³H and ⁴He binding energies via the No-core Full Configuration (NCFC) approach, which extrapolates No-core Shell Model (NCSM) results from finite basis spaces up to Nmax=16 or 18 to the infinite basis limit.

## Key numbers

* ³H binding energy varies from 7.21 to 8.67 MeV across all DET-PET types studied, compared to the JISP16 value of 8.369(1) MeV and the experimental value of 8.482 MeV.
* ⁴He binding energy varies from 21.25 to 30.41 MeV across all DET-PET types, compared to the JISP16 value of 28.299(1) MeV and the experimental value of 28.296 MeV.
* For the DET-PET type 0s2s1s3s⁺, ³H binding ranges from 7.21 to 8.37 MeV and ⁴He from 21.25 to 28.49 MeV.
* For the DET-PET type 1s0d0s1d⁻, ³H binding ranges from 8.05 to 8.67 MeV and ⁴He from 26.18 to 30.41 MeV.
* The DET-PET rotation angle β was varied in steps of 60° with finer steps near extremal binding energies; the numerical uncertainties in binding energies are less than 10 keV in most cases.

## Context

This work extends the concept of phase-equivalent transformations (PETs), previously used in inverse scattering theory (Newton) and in studies of the Coester line (Coester), by adding the constraint of preserving the deuteron wave function. The JISP16 interaction (Shirokov 2007) was itself obtained via PETs from the ISTP interaction. The DET-PET results are compared to the Tjon line correlation between ³H and ⁴He binding energies observed with various NN and NN+NNN interactions (Nogga, Pieper, and N3LO authors). The method enables systematic exploration of off-shell freedoms in NN interactions while keeping all two-body observables fixed, and could be extended to three-nucleon interactions.
