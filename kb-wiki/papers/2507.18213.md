# Hyperonic degrees of freedom in binary neutron star mergers

> We analyze the influence of hyperons in binary neutron star mergers considering several different equations of state that include hyperons. By running a large set of simulations, we study the impact of the thermally produced hyperons on the gravitational-wave spectral features, the temperature evolution of the remnant, the mass ejecta and the threshold mass for prompt collapse to a black hole. Models with hyperons tend to stand out in the relation between the dominant postmerger gravitational-wave frequency and the tidal deformability of massive stars. Moreover, the averaged temperature of the remnant is reduced for hyperonic models. The mass ejection of the mergers is tentatively enhanced when hyperons are present in comparison to nucleonic EoSs leading to similar stellar properties of cold neutron stars, whereas the threshold mass for prompt black-hole formation is reduced by about 0.05~$M_\odot$ compared to the nucleonic models.

# Hyperonic degrees of freedom in binary neutron star mergers

## Key claim
Hyperons produced thermally in binary neutron star mergers can be distinguished from purely nucleonic matter through their effect on gravitational-wave frequencies, remnant temperature, mass ejection, and threshold mass for black hole collapse, but the identification depends on the density reached in the remnant and requires precise knowledge of cold neutron star parameters to compare against nucleonic expectations.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic smooth particle hydrodynamics simulations of binary neutron star mergers using a large set of equations of state that include hyperons (and in some cases Delta baryons), comparing them against purely nucleonic models. They ran simulations both with full temperature-dependent equation of state tables and with zero-temperature slices supplemented by an approximate thermal treatment using a fixed thermal index of 1.75, which reproduces nucleonic thermal behavior. The simulations covered 1.4-1.4 solar mass binaries as well as asymmetric and heavier mergers, without intrinsic spin, neutrinos, or magnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- The threshold mass for prompt black hole formation is reduced by about 0.05 solar masses for hyperonic models compared to nucleonic models with the same cold neutron star properties.
- The relative frequency shift of the dominant postmerger gravitational-wave peak for hyperonic models ranges from 2% to 4% higher than the nucleonic baseline.
- The ejecta mass was determined 10 ms after merging for 1.4-1.4 solar mass binaries, shown as a function of the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.
- The thermal index was evaluated at a baryonic density corresponding to a charge fraction of 0.1 and a temperature of 25 MeV for different hyperonic and nucleonic models.

## Context
This work extends the studies of Sekiguchi (2011), Radice (2016), Blacker (2023), and Kochankovski (2025) by using a larger sample of hyperonic equations of state in binary neutron star merger simulations. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the tension between equation of state softening from hyperons and observations of neutron stars with masses above 2 solar masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Fonseca 2016, Cromartie 2020, Romani 2022). The results enable future discrimination of hyperonic from nucleonic matter through high-precision gravitational-wave measurements and electromagnetic observations of merger ejecta, though the authors note that inferring hyperon presence requires well-measured threshold masses together with precise knowledge of cold neutron star parameters.
