# Cross sections for nuclide production in 1 GeV proton-irradiated 208-Pb

> 114 cross sections for nuclide production in a 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated thin 208Pb target have been measured by the direct gamma spectrometry method using a high-resolution Ge detector. The gamma spectra were processed by the GENIE-2000 code. The ITEP-developed SIGMA code was used together with the PCNUDAT nuclear decay database to identify the gamma lines and to determine the cross sections. The 27Al(p,x)22Na reaction was used to monitor the proton flux. Results of a feasibility study of the auxiliary 27Al(p,x)24Na and 27Al(p,x)7Be monitor reactions in the 0.07-2.6 GeV proton-energy range are presented as well. Most of the experimental data have been analyzed by the LAHET (with ISABEL and Bertini options), CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, CASCADE/INPE, and YIELDX codes that simulate hadron-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reports 114 measured cross sections for nuclide production in a 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated thin 208Pb target, but the abstract glosses over that many of these yields are subject to systematic uncertainties from decay-chain analysis, and that the reported values include both independent and cumulative yields with varying reliability depending on the half-life relationships of precursor-daughter pairs.

## Method

A monoisotopic 208Pb metal foil (97.2% enrichment, 139.4 mg/cm2 thickness) was irradiated by a 995 MeV external proton beam from the ITEP U-10 synchrotron for one hour with a total fluence of 5e13 p/cm2. Gamma spectra were measured over six months using a high-resolution Ge detector and processed with the GENIE-2000 code; the ITEP-developed SIGMA code combined with the PCNUDAT nuclear decay database identified gamma lines and determined cross sections. The 27Al(p,x)22Na reaction monitored the proton flux, and the experimental data were compared with simulations from seven codes: LAHET (with ISABEL and Bertini options), CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, CASCADE/INPE, and YIELDX.

## Key numbers

- 114 cross sections for nuclide production measured in 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated thin 208Pb target
- 208Pb sample enrichment: 97.2% 208Pb, 1.93% 207Pb, 0.87% 206Pb, less than 0.01% 204Pb
- Sample thickness: 139.4 mg/cm2; monitor Al foil thickness: 139.6 mg/cm2
- Total proton fluence: 5e13 p/cm2
- Neutron-to-proton flux density ratio in the beam estimated at about (0.2-2)%
- Systematic errors from detection efficiency uncertainties do not exceed 5%
- 27Al(p,x)22Na monitor cross section at 1.0 GeV: 15.0 +/- 0.9 mb
- 27Al(p,x)24Na cross section at 1.0 GeV: 12.5 +/- (0.8 / 1.1) mb
- 27Al(p,x)7Be cross section at 1.0 GeV: 7.5 +/- (0.3 / 0.5) mb
- 192Au yield measured as 46.9 +/- 6.6 mb in this work versus 160 +/- 50 mb in prior work by another group, differing by more than a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends prior gamma-spectrometry studies of proton-induced reactions on lead and bismuth targets (referenced as studies by multiple groups in the 1990s) and provides benchmark data for accelerator-driven system (ADS) design, particularly for Pb-Bi reactor technology. The measurements enable testing of hadron-nucleus simulation codes including LAHET, CEM95, CEM2k, INUCL, CASCADE, CASCADE/INPE, and YIELDX, and complement reverse-kinematics experiments at GSI that can determine about a thousand yields from 208Pb + 1H reactions. The paper also presents a feasibility study of auxiliary monitor reactions 27Al(p,x)24Na and 27Al(p,x)7Be across the 0.07-2.6 GeV proton-energy range.
