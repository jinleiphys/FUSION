# Generating new solutions for relativistic transverse flow at the softest point

> Using the method of prolongation we generate new solutions from a simple particular solution for relativistic transverse flow with cylindrical symmetry in 1+3 dimensions. This is an extension of the longitudinal Bjorken flow ansatz and can be applied among others during first order phase transition in an expanding system. The prolongated solution allows for tracing back the flow profile from a transverse flow conjectured at the end of phase transition at CERN SPS heavy ion collisons.

## Key claim

Using the method of prolongation, the author generates new analytic solutions for relativistic transverse flow from the simple particular solution \(v = r/\tau\), valid when the comoving pressure gradient is zero (the "softest point" of the equation of state), and shows that any initial transverse velocity profile can be evolved forward or backward in longitudinal proper time via an implicit relation \(v = v_0(r - v \tau)\).

## Method

The author starts from the relativistic Euler equation for a perfect fluid with cylindrical symmetry in 1+3 dimensions, simplified under the condition of zero comoving pressure gradient to \(\partial_\tau v + v \partial_r v = 0\). The method of prolongation is applied to find all infinitesimal symmetries (Killing vector fields) of this quasilinear partial differential equation, yielding four classes of transformations. By exponentiating these transformations, the simple particular solution \(v = r/\tau\) is generalized to the implicit solution \(v = v_0(r - v \tau)\), where \(v_0(r)\) is the transverse velocity profile at a given initial longitudinal proper time. This implicit solution is then used to trace the flow backwards from a conjectured final-state profile at CERN SPS.

## Key numbers

- The fit by Ster et al. at \(\tau_0 = 6\) fm/c gives \(\langle u_t \rangle = 0.55 \pm 0.06\) and \(R_G = 7.1 \pm 0.2\) fm, leading to a proportionality coefficient \(\alpha = 0.0775 \pm 0.01\) for the reconstructed transverse flow \(\gamma v = \alpha r\).
- For the bag model equation of state, the ratio of Stefan-Boltzmann constants gives \(\sigma_h = 3\) and \(\sigma_q = 37\), yielding \(\nu \approx 0.12\).
- In the backward-evolved scenario, the mixed phase started at \(\tau = 1.14\) fm/c, with the quark matter fraction decreased to \(1/3\) on average by \(\tau = 2.4\) fm/c.
- The radius of the mixed phase expands from \(R_0 = 5.4\) fm to the final reconstructed radius \(R = 7.1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the longitudinal Bjorken flow ansatz (Bjorken, 1983) to include transverse flow with cylindrical symmetry, building on a recent simple analytic solution by Biro (2000). The method of prolongation provides a systematic way to generate new solutions, enabling backward evolution from experimentally motivated flow profiles such as those proposed by Ster, Csorgo, and Lorstad (1999) for CERN SPS heavy ion collisions. This approach allows reconstruction of the early-stage quark-gluon plasma flow and phase conversion dynamics, complementing numerical studies of the softest point signal by Shuryak (1992), Rischke (1995), and Dumitru (1999).
