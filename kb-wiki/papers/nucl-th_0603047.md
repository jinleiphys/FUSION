# The Paschos-Wolfenstein relation in a hadronic picture

> The Paschos-Wolfenstein (PW) relation joins neutral- and charged-current neutrino- and antineutrino-induced cross sections into an expression that depends on the weak mixing angle $\sin^2 θ_W$. Contrary to the traditional approach with partonic degrees of freedom, we adopt a model built on hadronic degrees of freedom to perform a study of the PW relation at intermediate energies (100 MeV to 2 GeV). With upcoming high-statistics scattering experiments such as MINER$ν$A and FINeSSE, a scrutiny of the PW relation is timely. Employing a relativistic Glauber nucleon knockout model for the description of quasielastic neutrino-nucleus reactions, the influence of nuclear effects on the PW relation is investigated. We discuss nuclear-model dependences and show that the PW relation is a robust ratio, mitigating the effect of final-state interactions, for example to the 1% level. The role played by a possible strangeness content of the nucleon is investigated. It appears that the uncertainties arising from the poorly known strangeness parameters and the difficulties in nuclear modelling seriously limit the applicability of the PW relation as an intermediate-energy electroweak precision tool. On the other hand, we show that nuclear effects may be sufficiently well under control to allow the extraction of new information on the axial strangeness parameter. Results are presented for $^{16} {O}$ and $^{56} {Fe}$.

## Key claim
The Paschos-Wolfenstein (PW) relation, traditionally derived in the deep-inelastic scattering regime using partonic degrees of freedom, is shown to be a robust ratio in a hadronic picture at intermediate energies (100 MeV to 2 GeV), mitigating final-state interaction effects to the 1% level for isoscalar nuclei; however, uncertainties from poorly known strangeness parameters and nuclear modeling seriously limit its applicability as an electroweak precision tool for measuring the weak mixing angle, though it may allow extraction of the axial strangeness parameter.

## Method
The authors employed a relativistic Glauber nucleon knockout model (relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation, RMSGA) to describe quasielastic neutrino-nucleus reactions, using hadronic degrees of freedom with weak one-nucleon currents parameterized by vector and axial form factors. Cross sections were computed for neutral-current and charged-current neutrino and antineutrino scattering on 16O and 56Fe targets, with final-state interactions modeled via a Glauber approach and binding effects included through a relativistic mean-field approximation. The PW ratio was evaluated both as a function of outgoing nucleon kinetic energy and as an integrated total cross-section ratio for neutrino energies from 100 MeV to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Final-state interactions (FSI) increase the computed PW ratio by less than 1% for 16O at an incoming neutrino energy of 1 GeV, in the region where RMSGA is valid (T_N down to 200 MeV).
- For total cross sections on 16O, from 500 MeV incoming neutrino energy onwards, the calculated PW values agree with the standard value at the 0.5% level.
- Neutron excess in 56Fe lowers PW values by approximately 10% or more at 1 GeV incoming neutrino energy.
- A ±1% theoretical uncertainty on the PW relation would translate to a nuclear-model error on the weak mixing angle of Δ_nuc(sin²θ_W) = ∓0.0028.
- The weak mixing angle used in calculations is sin²θ_W = 0.2224, with cosθ_c = 0.974.

## Context
This work extends the study of the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation from the deep-inelastic scattering regime (where it was tested by the NuTeV collaboration at Q² = 20 GeV²) to intermediate energies (100 MeV to 2 GeV) using hadronic rather than partonic degrees of freedom. It builds on earlier work by Donnelly and Musolf (who estimated nuclear uncertainties too large for sin²θ_W determination in parity-violating electron scattering) and by Alberico and collaborators (who suggested PW could serve as a lever for determining the strange-quark contribution to nucleon spin, g_A^s). The results are directly relevant for upcoming experiments such as MINERνA (which plans to study nuclear effects with carbon, iron, and lead targets) and FINeSSE (which aims to measure NC/CC ratios to extract information on the strange axial form factor).
