# Comment on "Nucleon spin-averaged forward virtual Compton tensor at large Q^2"

> In recent work, Hill and Paz apply the operator product expansion to forward doubly virtual Compton scattering. The resulting large-$Q^2$ form of the amplitude $W_1(0,Q^2)$ is compatible with the one we obtain by extrapolation of low-$Q^2$ results from a chiral effective field theory, providing support for our approach. That paper also presents a result for the two-photon contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen that has a much larger uncertainty than in previous work. We show that this an overestimate arising from the inclusion of the proton pole term in the subtracted dispersion relation for $W_1$.

# Comment on "Nucleon spin-averaged forward virtual Compton tensor at large Q^2"

## Key claim
The large-uncertainty result for the two-photon contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen presented by Hill and Paz (2017) is an overestimate, arising primarily from their inclusion of the proton pole term in the subtracted dispersion relation for the amplitude W1, rather than from any genuine physical uncertainty.

## Method
The authors compare the operator product expansion (OPE) result for the large-Q^2 behavior of the forward doubly virtual Compton scattering amplitude W1(0,Q^2) from Hill and Paz (2017) with their own extrapolation from chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) at low Q^2. They then analyze the sources of uncertainty in the two-photon contribution to the muonic Lamb shift by examining the treatment of the Born pole term in the dispersion relation, contrasting the approach of Hill and Paz (2017) with the pole-removed dispersion relation used by Pachucki (1999), Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2011), and the authors' own previous work (Birse and McGovern, 2012).

## Key numbers
- The OPE calculation in Hill and Paz (2017) gives Q^2 W1(0,Q^2)/(2M_p^2) ~ 0.27–0.37 for Q greater than or approximately equal to 5 GeV^2.
- The chiEFT extrapolation from Birse and McGovern (2012) gives a central value of 1.3 for the same quantity, with a wide uncertainty band of 0.2–23.
- Reducing the entire contribution from Q^2 > 0.3 GeV^2 by a factor of 4 would alter the Lamb shift by only about 0.3 micro-eV.
- The Hill and Paz (2017) result for the two-photon contribution to the muonic Lamb shift is Delta E(2P-2S) = +30 +/- 13 micro-eV.
- The Birse and McGovern (2012) result is +33 +/- 2 micro-eV, a factor of 6 smaller uncertainty.
- The slope contributions from the low-energy theorem using Hill and Paz input: Born pole term -80.4 +/- 7.9 GeV^-2, Born non-pole term +11.1 +/- 0.2 GeV^-2, magnetic polarisability +8.4 +/- 1.3 GeV^-2.

## Context
This comment extends the debate on the proton radius extraction from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013). The authors' previous work (Birse and McGovern 2012) used chiEFT to determine the low-Q^2 subtraction term and matched it to asymptotic OPE behavior, finding compatibility with the QCD calculations of Hill and Paz (2017). The comment clarifies that the much larger uncertainty in Hill and Paz (2017) arises from their treatment of the Born pole term, which is absent in approaches using pole-removed dispersion relations (Pachucki 1999, Carlson and Vanderhaeghen 2011, Birse and McGovern 2012). This enables future extractions to use the more constrained chiEFT results for the subtraction term, including the order-Q^4 term left undetermined in Hill and Paz (2017).
