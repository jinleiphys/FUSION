# Finite Volume Corrections to the SU(3) Deconfining Temperature due to a Confined Exterior

> Deconfined regions in relativistic heavy ion collisions are limited to small volumes surrounded by a confined exterior. Using the geometry of a double layered torus, we keep an outside temperature slightly lower than the inside temperature, so that both regions are in the SU(3) scaling region. Deconfined volume sizes are chosen to be in a range typical for such volumes created at the BNL RHIC. Even with small temperature differences a dependence of the (pseudo) deconfining temperature on a colder surrounding temperature is clearly visible. For temporal lattice sizes Ntau=4, 6 and 8 we find consistency with SU(3) scaling behavior for the measured transition temperature signals.

# Finite Volume Corrections to the SU(3) Deconfining Temperature due to a Confined Exterior

## Key claim

Even with a large outside temperature of about 160 MeV (close to the infinite-volume deconfining temperature of 174 MeV), finite volume corrections to the SU(3) deconfining temperature due to small volumes surrounded by a confined exterior are clearly visible, and the measured pseudo transition temperatures show consistency with SU(3) scaling behavior for temporal lattice sizes Ntau=4, 6, and 8.

## Method

The authors use Markov chain Monte Carlo simulations of pure SU(3) gauge theory with the Wilson plaquette action on a 4D hypercubic lattice with a double-layered torus (DLT) geometry, where one layer (outside) is kept at a temperature below the deconfining transition (158.15 MeV or 161.80 MeV) and the other layer (inside) is iterated to find the maximum of the Polyakov loop susceptibility as a signal for the deconfining transition. The DLT boundary conditions use different coupling constants in the two layers, with both layers in the SU(3) scaling region, and the physical temperature varies because the lattice spacing depends on the coupling. Simulations used 2^13 sweeps for equilibration and 2^21 sweeps for data production, with lattice sizes ranging from Ns=20 to Ns=64 depending on Ntau.

## Key numbers

- Infinite volume deconfining temperature: Tc = 174 MeV, corresponding to Tc^{-1} = 1.13 fermi
- Outside temperatures used: Tout = 158.15 MeV and Tout = 161.80 MeV
- Lambda lattice scale: Lambda_L = 5.07 MeV, determined independently of Ntau value
- For Ntau=4, beta^g_out = 5.6500 (Tout=158.15 MeV) and beta^g_out = 5.6600 (Tout=161.80 MeV)
- For Ntau=6, beta^g_out = 5.84318 (Tout=158.15 MeV) and beta^g_out = 5.85514 (Tout=161.80 MeV)
- For Ntau=8, beta^g_out = 6.00458 (Tout=158.15 MeV) and beta^g_out = 6.01821 (Tout=161.80 MeV)
- Production on a 4x32^3 DLT used 1,024 processors and took 5.7 hours real time
- Pseudo transition coupling ranges: [5.69,5.725] for Ntau=4, [5.89,5.93] for Ntau=6, [6.06,6.11] for Ntau=8

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Berg and collaborators (2007) that used cold boundary conditions (CBC) from the strong coupling limit (beta^g to 0), which mixed a SU(3) scaling region with extreme strong coupling boundary conditions. The DLT construction, explored in Berg, Wu, and collaborators (2009), allows both layers to be in the SU(3) scaling region, addressing concerns about the earlier approach. The results show that even with a high outside temperature of about 160 MeV, finite volume corrections remain remarkably large, and the authors note that if this increase in transition temperature holds when quarks are included, previous lattice calculations would underestimate transition temperatures at RHIC. The work enables future investigations incorporating cold boundaries via a surrounding region and inclusion of quarks in such calculations.
