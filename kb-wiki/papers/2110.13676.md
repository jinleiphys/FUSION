# The Impact of the New $^{65\!}$As(p,$γ$)$^{66\!}$Se Reaction Rate on the Two-Proton Sequential Capture of $^{64}\!$Ge, Weak GeAs Cycles, and Type-I X-Ray Bursts such as the Clocked Burster GS 1826$-$24

> We re-assess $^{65}$As(p,$γ$)$^{66}$Se reaction rates based on a set of proton thresholds of $^{66}$Se, $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se), estimated from the experimental mirror nuclear masses, theoretical mirror displacement energies, and full $pf$-model space shell-model calculation. The self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory is employed to obtain the mirror displacement energies with much reduced uncertainty, and thus reducing the proton-threshold uncertainty up to 161 keV compared to the AME2020 evaluation. Using the simulation instantiated by the one-dimensional multi-zone hydrodynamic code, KEPLER, that closely reproduces the observed GS 1826$-$24 clocked bursts, the present forward and reverse $^{65}$As(p,$γ$)$^{66}$Se reaction rates based on a selected $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se) = 2.469$\pm$0.054 MeV, and the latest $^{22}$Mg($α$,p)$^{25}$Al, $^{56}$Ni(p,$γ$)$^{57}$Cu(p,$γ$)$^{58}$Zn, $^{55}$Ni(p,$γ$)$^{56}$Cu, and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As reaction rates, we find that though the GeAs cycles is weakly established in the rapid-proton capture process path, the $^{65}$As(p,$γ$)$^{66}$Se reaction still strongly characterizes the burst tail end due to the two-proton sequential capture on $^{64}$Ge, not found by Cyburt et al. (2016) sensitivity study. The $^{65}$As(p,$γ$)$^{66}$Se reaction influences the abundances of nuclei $A$ = 64, 68, 72, 76, and 80 up to a factor of 1.4. The new $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se) and the inclusion of the updated $^{22}$Mg($α$,p)$^{25}$Al reaction rate increases the production of $^{12}$C up to a factor of $4.5$ that is not observable and could be the main fuel for superburst. The waiting point status of and two-proton sequential capture on $^{64}$Ge, weak-cycle feature of GeAs at region heavier than $^{64}$Ge, and impact of other possible $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se) are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the $^{65}$As(p,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se reaction strongly characterizes the burst tail end of type-I X-ray bursts due to two-proton sequential capture on $^{64}$Ge, a result not found by the Cyburt et al. (2016) sensitivity study, though the GeAs cycles are only weakly established in the rapid-proton capture process path.

## Method

The authors re-assessed the $^{65}$As(p,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se forward and reverse reaction rates using proton thresholds of $^{66}$Se, $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se), estimated from experimental mirror nuclear masses, theoretical mirror displacement energies from self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with DD-ME2 effective interaction, and full $pf$-model space shell-model calculations. They then employed the one-dimensional multi-zone hydrodynamic code KEPLER to simulate type-I X-ray bursts that closely reproduce the observed GS 1826$-$24 clocked bursts, incorporating the new $^{65}$As(p,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se rates along with several other updated reaction rates.

## Key numbers

* The selected proton threshold of $^{66}$Se is $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se) = 2.469 $\pm$ 0.054 MeV, obtained from the axial RHB calculation with AME2020 masses.
* The RHB method reduced the proton-threshold uncertainty by up to 161 keV compared to the AME2020 evaluation.
* The $^{65}$As(p,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se reaction influences the abundances of nuclei with mass numbers $A$ = 64, 68, 72, 76, and 80 up to a factor of 1.4.
* The new $S_\mathrm{p}$($^{66}$Se) and the updated $^{22}$Mg($\alpha$,p)$^{25}$Al reaction rate increase the production of $^{12}$C up to a factor of 4.5.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Cyburt et al. (2016) by using a one-dimensional multi-zone hydrodynamic model that closely reproduces observed GS 1826$-$24 clocked bursts, rather than simpler one-zone models. The paper builds on prior mass measurements of $^{65}$As by Tu et al. (2011) and the AME2020 mass evaluation, and incorporates recently updated reaction rates for $^{22}$Mg($\alpha$,p)$^{25}$Al (Hu et al. 2021), $^{55}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{56}$Cu (Valverde et al. 2019), $^{56}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{57}$Cu (Kahl et al. 2019), $^{57}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{58}$Zn (Lam et al. 2021), and $^{64}$Ge(p,$\gamma$)$^{65}$As (Lam et al. 2016). The enhanced $^{12}$C production could help explain the origin of superbursts, which are longer and more energetic than typical type-I X-ray bursts.
