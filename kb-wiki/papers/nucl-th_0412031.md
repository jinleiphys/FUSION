# Large Momenta Fluctuations Of Charm Quarks In The Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We show that large fluctuations of D mesons kinetic energy (or momentum) distributions might be a signature of a phase transition to the quark gluon plasma (QGP). In particular, a jump in the variance of the momenta or kinetic energy, as a function of a control parameter (temperature or Fermi energy at finite baryon densities) might be a signature for a first order phase transition to the QGP. This behaviour is completely consistent with the order parameter defined for a system of interacting quarks at zero temperature and finite baryon densities which shows a jump in correspondance to a first order phase transition to the QGP. The $J/Ψ$ shows exactly the same behavior of the order parameter and of the variance of the D mesons. We discuss implications for relativistic heavy ion collisions within the framework of a transport model and possible hints for experimental data.

## Key claim

Large fluctuations (specifically a jump in the variance) of D meson momenta or kinetic energy as a function of a control parameter (temperature or Fermi energy at finite baryon densities) might be a signature of a first order phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma, with the behavior being completely consistent with the order parameter defined for a system of interacting quarks at zero temperature and finite baryon densities.

## Method

The authors use a Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) approach for a system of u and d quarks with color degrees of freedom, interacting through the Richardson potential with parameters n_f=2 and Lambda=0.25 GeV. After the light quark system reaches equilibrium, a J/Psi (c cbar pair) is inserted and its evolution is followed; the Pauli principle is enforced via a constraint on the average one-body occupation function. Separately, the JPCIAE transport code (which includes Pythia as a generator of elementary collisions) is used to simulate symmetric nucleus-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV from pp to AuAu.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical molecular dynamics approach of Bonasera (2000) and Papa (2001) to include charm quarks as probes of the QGP phase transition, building on the zero-temperature finite-baryon-density model of Terranova, Zhou, and Bonasera (2004). The authors propose that measuring variances of D meson transverse momenta in heavy ion collisions at RHIC could provide a stronger signal for the QGP phase transition than J/Psi suppression alone, since D mesons are more abundantly produced. The transport model calculations using the JPCIAE code (Sa) provide a baseline without phase transition, against which experimental data could reveal anomalous increases in variance indicative of new physics.
