# Bound state solutions of scalar QED_{2+1} for zero photon mass

> The Feynman-Schwinger representation is used to study the behavior of solutions of scalar QED in (2+1) dimensions. The limit of zero photon mass is seen to be smooth. The Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation also exhibits this property. They clearly deviate from the behavior in the nonrelativistic limit. In a variational analysis we show that this difference can be attributed to retardation effects of relativistic origin.

## Key claim

The limit of zero photon mass in scalar QED in (2+1) dimensions is smooth, with no logarithmic confinement found, provided the mass of the charged particles is kept finite; this smoothness is attributed to retardation effects of relativistic origin, and the nonrelativistic limit is not uniform.

## Method

The authors used the Feynman-Schwinger representation (FSR) to compute the gauge-invariant four-point function for two scalar particles minimally coupled to a massive photon field, integrating out the valence field and neglecting phi-loops. They discretized the resulting path integral and performed Metropolis Monte Carlo calculations of the logarithmic derivative of the Green's function to extract the ground-state energy. For comparison, they also solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the ladder approximation by expanding in O(3) spherical harmonics and truncating at l_max=2, and performed a variational analysis of a gφ³_{2+1} theory with a Gaussian trial wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- For coupling e² = 2.5m and photon mass μ = 10⁻³m, the ground-state energy E₀ was obtained with accuracy within 1% from a fit of L(T) to the spectral decomposition form.
- For μ/m = 0.1, the critical coupling constant where the ground-state energy E₀ = 0 was determined to be e²_crit/m = 6.0 ± 0.5.
- In the variational analysis of the gφ³_{2+1} theory, the binding energy E_B in the nonrelativistic regime goes as ~ (1/4π) λ log ζ, where ζ = mλ/μ², and for fixed large m with μ→0, E_B approaches a finite constant ~ λ log(m/λ) independent of μ.

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Schwinger representation to scalar QED in (2+1) dimensions, a theory that is UV finite in the two-body sector, and compares it to the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation. It contradicts the expectation from compactified quenched spinor QED_{2+1} (Fiebig, 1994) that linear confinement occurs for all values of the charge, showing instead that no logarithmic confinement appears for finite charged-particle mass. The results enable future study of the confinement mechanism in abelian theories and demonstrate that the FSR is a viable nonperturbative alternative to lattice gauge calculations for bound-state energies.
