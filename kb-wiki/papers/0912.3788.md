# Pairing in 4-component fermion systems: the bulk limit of SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonians

> Fermion systems with more than two components can exhibit pairing condensates of much more complex structure than the well-known single BCS condensate of spin-up and spin-down fermions. In the framework of the exactly solvable SO(8) Richardson-Gaudin model with SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonians, we show that the BCS approximation remains valid in the thermodynamic limit of large systems for describing the ground state energy and the canonical and quasiparticle excitation gaps. Correlations beyond BCS pairing give rise to a spectrum of collective excitations, but these do not affect the bulk energy and quasiparticle gaps.

# Pairing in 4-component fermion systems: the bulk limit of SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonians

## Key claim

The BCS approximation remains valid in the thermodynamic limit for describing the ground state energy and quasiparticle excitation gaps of SU(4)-symmetric fermion systems with four components, despite the presence of collective excitations beyond BCS pairing that do not affect these bulk properties. The authors demonstrate this using exactly solvable SO(8) Richardson-Gaudin models, showing that higher-order correlations on top of a single pair condensate are not important for bulk ground state characteristics when the Hamiltonian is of the pairing type studied.

## Method

The authors analyze two exactly solvable models: the pure seniority SO(8) Hamiltonian with degenerate orbitals, which provides analytic expressions for energies, and the SO(8) Richardson-Gaudin (RG) model with non-degenerate single-particle levels. For the RG model, they numerically solve the exact many-body equations for systems with particle numbers N ranging from 160 to 1000 at quarter filling (N = Omega) with interaction strength g = 0.15, then extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit using a cubic polynomial expansion in 1/N. They compare these extrapolated exact results to the BCS limit obtained from solving the BCS gap equations for the same Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy per particle in the thermodynamic limit: exact extrapolation gives a = 0.062022149, BCS gives a = 0.062022154 (agreement to seven significant figures)
- Quasiparticle energy Eq in the thermodynamic limit: exact extrapolation gives a = 0.140148, BCS gives a = 0.140151
- Odd-even pairing gap Delta_o-e in the thermodynamic limit: exact extrapolation gives a = 0.0154637, BCS gives a = 0.0154669
- Canonical gap Delta_c in the thermodynamic limit: exact extrapolation gives a = 0.0154672, BCS gives a = 0.0154669
- For the degenerate seniority Hamiltonian with SU(4) symmetry, the ground state energy per particle is E_0/N = -(1/2)g(1 - f + 3/Omega), where f is the fractional filling and g = G*Omega
- The quasiparticle gap in the bulk limit for all models studied is Delta = g/2 + O(1/Omega)
- For the isovector pairing Hamiltonian, the lowest collective excitation (v=0, T=2) has energy E = 3g/Omega, which vanishes in the bulk limit

## Context

This work extends the known exact solutions of pairing Hamiltonians from the well-studied SU(2) (spin-1/2 fermion) case to SU(4)-symmetric systems with four fermion components, relevant to proton-neutron pairing in atomic nuclei and to trapped cold atoms with four hyperfine states. The authors build on the Richardson-Gaudin integrable models developed by Richardson (1963) and Gaudin (1976), and on earlier work by Flowers (1964) and Evans (1981) on the SO(8) seniority Hamiltonian. The results support the use of BCS theory for calculating pairing energies in the bulk limit of multicomponent fermion systems, while also identifying collective excitations (such as isospin rotations in the isovector case and spin-isospin rotations in the SU(4) case) that lie inside the quasiparticle gap and vanish in the thermodynamic limit. The authors suggest that future work could examine how well standard many-body methods like the Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation or Generator Coordinate Method perform for calculating the effects of these collective degrees of freedom starting from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solutions.
