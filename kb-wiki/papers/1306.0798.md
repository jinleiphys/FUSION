# Fermi gases with imaginary mass imbalance and the sign problem in Monte Carlo calculations

> Fermi gases in strongly coupled regimes, such as the unitary limit, are inherently challenging for many-body methods. Although much progress has been made with purely analytic methods, quantitative results require ab initio numerical approaches, such as Monte Carlo (MC) calculations. However, mass-imbalanced and spin-imbalanced gases are not accessible to MC calculations due to the infamous sign problem. It was recently pointed out that the sign problem, for finite spin imbalance, can be circumvented by resorting to imaginary polarizations and analytic continuation. Large parts of the phase diagram spanned by temperature and polarization then become accessible to MC calculations. We propose to apply a similar strategy to the mass-imbalanced case, which opens up the possibility to study the associated phase diagram with MC calculations. In particular, our analysis suggests that a detection of a (tri-)critical point in this phase diagram is possible. We also discuss calculations in the zero-temperature limit with our approach.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the sign problem in Monte Carlo calculations for mass-imbalanced Fermi gases can be circumvented by analytically continuing the theory to imaginary mass imbalances, and that this approach also makes the zero-temperature limit accessible, unlike the analogous spin-imbalanced case.

## Method

The authors perform a mean-field analysis of a unitary Fermi gas in three spatial dimensions with two fermion species of unequal masses, using complex-valued masses such that \(m_{\uparrow}^{\ast}=m_{\downarrow}\). They compute the order-parameter potential for U(1) symmetry breaking in the mean-field approximation, then fit observables computed for imaginary mass imbalances to polynomial ansatze in \(\bar{m}_{\rm I}^2\) and analytically continue these polynomials to real-valued mass imbalances.

## Key numbers

- The Bertsch parameter \(\xi\) at \(T=\bar{m}=0\) in the mean-field approximation is \(\xi = 0.59\dots\) (standard mean-field result).
- The lower bound for the radius of convergence \(r_{\rm min}\) of the expansion in \(\bar{m}\) is given by \(r_{\rm min}(|\Phi|^2) = \sqrt{(\beta^2 |\Phi|^2 +\pi^2)/(\beta^2|\Phi|^2 +\pi^2 + \beta^2\bar{\mu}^2)}\).
- For the analytic continuation of the Bertsch parameter, good agreement with the exact mean-field result is observed for \(N_{\rm max}=5\) and \(\bar{m} \lesssim \bar{m}_{\rm c}\).
- For the analytic continuation of the critical temperature, good agreement with the exact mean-field result is observed for \(N_{\rm max}=5\) and \(\bar{m}\lesssim 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the strategy of Braun (2012) for spin-imbalanced Fermi gases, where imaginary polarizations circumvent the sign problem, to the mass-imbalanced case. The authors note that mass-imbalanced gases have been studied experimentally (miexp) and theoretically (revstoof), but remained inaccessible to ab initio Monte Carlo calculations due to the sign problem, unlike the mass-balanced unitary Fermi gas studied by Chen (2003) and Bulgac (2005). The approach enables future Monte Carlo studies of the mass-imbalanced phase diagram, potentially locating the (tri)critical point and allowing zero-temperature calculations.
