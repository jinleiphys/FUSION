# Low densities in asymmetric nuclear matter

> Asymmetric nuclear matter is investigated in the low density region below the nuclear saturation density. Microscopic calculations based on the Dirac Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials are used to adjust a low density functional. This functional is constructed on a density expansion of the relativistic mean field theory which allows a clear interpretation of the role of the mesons to the equation of state. It is shown that a correction term should be added to the functional in order to take into account the effects beyond the mean field. Two functionals with different corrections are obtained. Those functionals converge to predict a reduction of the spinodal zone in asymmetric nuclear matter by about 15-20% and an isoscalar unstable mode closer to the constant Z/A direction than the functional without correction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a standard relativistic mean field (RMF) functional cannot reproduce the low-density behavior of microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations without adding explicit correction terms beyond the mean field; two different corrected functionals (RMF+C1 and RMF+C2) both predict a 15-20% reduction of the spinodal zone in asymmetric nuclear matter and shift the isoscalar unstable mode closer to the constant Z/A direction compared to the uncorrected functional.

## Method

The authors performed DBHF calculations using the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential for asymmetric nuclear matter at densities between 0.01 and 0.2 fm^-3, then fitted the DBHF results (Dirac masses and energy densities) with a low-density expansion of the RMF Lagrangian including sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons plus a nonlinear sigma coupling. They extracted the residual difference between DBHF and the RMF fit in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter separately, then parameterized this difference with two different functional forms (C1 and C2) to account for correlations beyond mean field.

## Key numbers

- The spinodal zone in asymmetric nuclear matter is reduced by 15-20% when using the corrected functionals compared to the uncorrected RMF functional
- The RMF fit yields saturation density rho_0 = 0.1933 fm^-3, while the corrected functionals give rho_0 = 0.1857 fm^-3 (RMF+C1) and 0.1856 fm^-3 (RMF+C2), close to the DBHF value of 0.1814 fm^-3
- The incompressibility modulus K_0 is 365 MeV for the uncorrected RMF fit, but drops to 251 MeV (RMF+C1) and 242 MeV (RMF+C2), compared to 230 MeV from DBHF
- The symmetry energy a_s^2 (exact second derivative) is 37.7 MeV for RMF, 36.9 MeV for both corrected functionals, and 34.4 MeV from DBHF
- The binding energy B_0 is -16.08 MeV (RMF), -16.27 MeV (RMF+C1), -16.24 MeV (RMF+C2), and -16.15 MeV (DBHF)
- The fit included 23 calculated points between rho_B = 0.02 and 0.13 fm^-3, plus two densities at 0.1658 fm^-3 and 0.197 fm^-3, for asymmetry y = rho_p/rho_B from 0 to 0.5 in steps of 0.05

## Context

This work extends microscopic DBHF calculations (van Dalen 2004) to construct a low-density functional that bridges between ab initio approaches and phenomenological RMF models. It contrasts with standard RMF parametrizations like NL3 (Lalazissis 1997) and DD-TW (Typel 1999), which do not reproduce the non-linear low-density behavior seen in DBHF and BHF calculations (Baldo 2004). The corrected functionals enable improved studies of the liquid-gas phase transition and spinodal instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter, relevant for heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies and for the equation of state of neutron star crusts.
