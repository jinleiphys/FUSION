# Regular and chaotic classical dynamics in the U(5)-SU(3) quantum phase transition of the IBM

> We study the classical dynamics in a generic first-order quantum phase transition between the U(5) and SU(3) limits of the interacting boson model. The dynamics is chaotic, of Hénon-Heiles type, in the spherical phase and is regular, yet sensitive to local degeneracies, in the deformed phase. Both types of dynamics persist in the coexistence region resulting in a divided phase space.

# Regular and chaotic classical dynamics in the U(5)-SU(3) quantum phase transition of the IBM

## Key claim
The classical dynamics in a generic first-order quantum phase transition between the U(5) and SU(3) limits of the interacting boson model is chaotic (Henon-Heiles type) in the spherical phase and robustly regular in the deformed phase, with both types of dynamics persisting and remaining separable throughout the phase coexistence region, provided the barrier height is sufficiently large (V_b = 0.268 compared to V_b = 0.0018 in previous works).

## Method
The authors constructed two Hamiltonians, H1(rho) and H2(xi), that coincide at the critical point (rho_c = 1/sqrt(2), xi_c = 0) and describe the U(5) and SU(3) limits respectively. They derived classical limits via coherent states in the N to infinity limit, obtaining classical potentials V1(rho) and V2(xi) as functions of shape variables (beta, gamma). The classical dynamics was then analyzed by restricting to L=0 vibrations, reducing the system to two dimensions in polar coordinates (beta, gamma) with conjugate momenta, and examining Poincare surfaces of section at prescribed energies for various values of the control parameters.

## Key numbers
- Barrier height: V_b = 0.268 (compared to V_b = 0.0018 in previous works by Whelan (1993) and Macek (2007))
- Spinodal point: rho* = 1/2, where V1(rho) develops an additional local deformed minimum
- Critical point: rho_c = 1/sqrt(2) (approximately 0.707) and xi_c = 0
- Anti-spinodal point: xi** = 1/3, where the spherical minimum disappears in V2(xi)
- Equilibrium deformation in the deformed phase: beta_eq = 2/sqrt(3) (approximately 1.155), gamma_eq = 0
- Normal mode frequency ratio R = epsilon_beta/epsilon_gamma takes rational values at discrete xi values: xi_R = (3R-1)/2, with examples shown for R = 1/2, 2/3, and 1

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quantum phase transitions and chaos in the interacting boson model by Whelan (1993) and Macek (2007), which used simplified Hamiltonians with extremely low barriers and narrow coexistence regions. The authors build on their own recent identification of IBM Hamiltonians without such restrictions (Leviatan, 2006), enabling a comprehensive analysis of generic first-order QPTs between spherical and deformed shapes (Macek and Leviatan, 2011; Leviatan and Macek, 2012; Macek and Leviatan, 2012). The divided phase space structure found here, with regular dynamics in the deformed region separated from chaotic dynamics in the spherical region, manifests in the quantum analysis as regular rotational bands amidst a complicated environment. This work enables further study of how the interplay of order and chaos manifests in finite nuclear systems undergoing shape transitions.
