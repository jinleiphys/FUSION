# Flavor hierarchy of parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma from a Bayesian analysis

> The quenching of light and heavy flavor hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions probes the color and flavor dependences of parton energy loss through a color-deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP), and thus reveals the properties of QCD matter at extremely high density and temperature. By combining a next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculation of parton production, a general ansatz of parton energy loss functions and parton fragmentation functions, we calculate the nuclear modification of various hadron species -- charged hadrons, $D$ mesons and $B$-decayed $J/ψ$ -- over a wide transverse momentum regime. Comparing our calculations to the experimental data using the Bayesian statistical analysis, we perform a first simultaneous extraction of the energy loss functions of gluons ($g$), light quarks ($q$), charm quarks ($c$) and bottom quarks ($b$) inside the QGP. We find that the average parton energy loss at high energies follows the expected hierarchy of $\langle ΔE_g \rangle > \langle ΔE_q \rangle \sim \langle ΔE_c \rangle > \langle ΔE_b \rangle$, while the parton energy loss distribution can further test the QCD calculations of parton interaction with the dense nuclear matter. We also find that the reduction of experimental uncertainties can significantly improve the precision of the extracted parton energy loss functions inside the QGP.

## Key claim

The authors perform a first simultaneous extraction of the energy loss functions for gluons, light quarks, charm quarks, and bottom quarks inside the quark-gluon plasma using Bayesian inference, finding that the average parton energy loss at high energies follows the hierarchy ⟨ΔE_g⟩ > ⟨ΔE_q⟩ ∼ ⟨ΔE_c⟩ > ⟨ΔE_b⟩. This result is conditional on restricting the analysis to high transverse momentum (p_T > 10 GeV) where perturbative calculations are reliable, and on assuming that the parameters γ and α controlling the p_T dependence and distribution shape are the same for all parton flavors.

## Method

The authors combine a next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculation of parton production, a general ansatz for parton energy loss functions parameterized by six parameters (β_g, C_q, C_c, C_b, γ, α), and vacuum fragmentation functions to compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA of charged hadrons, D mesons, and B-decayed J/ψ in 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV. They perform a Bayesian analysis using Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo sampling with a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 600 design points to simultaneously extract the energy loss functions of gluons, light quarks, charm quarks, and bottom quarks from CMS experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The 90% credible region for β_g (gluon energy loss magnitude) extracted from CMS data is (1.646, 2.56) using original error bars, narrowing to (1.96, 2.39) with halved error bars.
- The 90% credible region for C_q (light quark energy loss relative to gluon) is (0.129, 0.65) with original error bars, narrowing to (0.226, 0.454) with halved error bars.
- The 90% credible region for C_c (charm quark energy loss relative to gluon) is (0.3, 0.567) with original error bars, narrowing to (0.344, 0.459) with halved error bars.
- The 90% credible region for C_b (bottom quark energy loss relative to gluon) is (0.065, 0.277) with original error bars, narrowing to (0.124, 0.207) with halved error bars.
- The 90% credible region for γ (p_T dependence exponent) is (0.137, 0.378) with original error bars, narrowing to (0.184, 0.295) with halved error bars.
- The 90% credible region for α (energy loss distribution shape parameter) is (5.287, 9.061) with original error bars, narrowing to (6.266, 8.401) with halved error bars.
- The analysis uses CMS data on R_AA for charged hadrons, D mesons, and B-decayed J/ψ in 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, restricted to p_T > 10 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier Bayesian extractions of parton energy loss functions that were limited to flavor-averaged jet energy loss (He et al., 2018) or to gluons and charm quarks only from J/ψ data (Zhang et al., 2022). It provides the first simultaneous data-driven constraint on energy loss functions for all parton species (gluon, light quarks, charm, and bottom), enabling quantitative tests of perturbative QCD calculations of color, flavor, and mass dependence of parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The extracted energy loss distributions can serve as stringent constraints on theoretical calculations of parton interactions with hot nuclear matter.
