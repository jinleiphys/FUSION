# Early Thermalization at RHIC

> Triple-gluon elastic scatterings are briefly reviewed since the scatterings explain the early thermalization puzzle in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies. A numerical solution of the transport equation with the triple-gluon elastic scatterings demonstrates gluon momentum isotropy achieved at a time of the order of 0.65 fm/c. Triple-gluon scatterings lead to a short thermalization time of gluon matter.

## Key claim

Triple-gluon elastic scatterings can explain the early thermalization puzzle in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, achieving gluon momentum isotropy at a time of order 0.65 fm/c, but only under the condition that the initial gluon number density is high enough (about 38 fm^{-3}) for such three-body processes to dominate over two-gluon scatterings.

## Method

The author solves a Boltzmann-type transport equation that includes both standard 2-to-2 gluon elastic scattering terms and a new 3-to-3 gluon elastic scattering term, using the spin- and color-averaged squared amplitude |M_{3→3}|^2 calculated in perturbative QCD at order α_s^4. The initial gluon distribution for a central Au-Au collision at √s_NN = 200 GeV is taken from HIJING Monte Carlo simulation, with gluons uniformly distributed in a cylinder and the transverse expansion neglected due to the short thermalization time. The transport equation is evolved from the initial time t_ini = 0.2 fm/c until the time t_iso when local momentum isotropy is reached.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon number density of about 38 fm^{-3} for RHIC, estimated from gluon rapidity density dN^g/dy ~ 1000
- Initial gluon number density of about 30 fm^{-3} for RHIC and 140 fm^{-3} for LHC from minijet production estimates
- Nuclear radius R_A = 6.4 fm for gold
- Initial time t_ini = 0.2 fm/c as estimated from HIJING simulation
- Time to reach momentum isotropy t_iso = 0.65 fm/c
- Thermalization time t_iso - t_ini = 0.45 fm/c
- Fitted Jüttner distribution parameters at isotropy: fugacity λ = 0.065 and temperature T = 0.75 GeV
- The fugacity and temperature are higher than 0.05 and 0.55 GeV obtained from free streaming of partons in Levai, Muller, Wang (1995)
- The thermalization time is less than 0.5 fm/c obtained from free streaming of partons in Levai, Muller, Wang (1995)

## Context

This work extends the proposal of triple-gluon elastic scatterings from Xu, Sun, Chen, Zheng (2004) to explain the early thermalization puzzle at RHIC, which was previously addressed by hydrodynamic calculations (Heinz and Kolb 2002; Hirano 2001; Huovinen 2003) that agreed with elliptic flow data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The results enable more realistic modeling of the early-time dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for LHC energies where three-gluon scatterings are anticipated to thoroughly overwhelm two-gluon elastic scattering processes.
