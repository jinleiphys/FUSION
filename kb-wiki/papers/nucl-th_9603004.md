# Thermal hadron production in Si-Au collisions

> The most abundantly produced hadron species in $Si\!-\!Au$ collisions at the BNL-AGS (nucleons, pions, kaons, antikaons and hyperons) are shown to be in accord with emission from a thermal resonance gas source of temperature $T\simeq 110$ MeV and baryochemical potential $μ_B \simeq 540$ MeV, corresponding to about 1/3 standard nuclear density. Our analysis takes the isopin asymmetry of the initial state fully into account.

## Key claim
The most abundantly produced hadron species in Si-Au collisions at the BNL-AGS are consistent with emission from a thermal resonance gas source, but only when the isospin asymmetry of the initial state (more neutrons than protons) is fully accounted for via a charge chemical potential; without this constraint, no consistent freeze-out parameters can be found.

## Method
The authors fit measured hadron production ratios (π⁺/p, K⁺/π⁺, K⁺/K⁻, Λ/p, π⁻/π⁺) to an ideal gas of all hadrons and hadronic resonances (479 entries from the Particle Data Compilation, excluding charm and bottom). The partition function includes baryon, strangeness, and charge chemical potentials; strangeness is fixed to zero overall, and the charge chemical potential is set so that the final-state charge-to-baryon ratio matches the initial-state value of 46 protons out of 108 participant nucleons in central Si-Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature: T = 110 ± 5 MeV
- Freeze-out baryochemical potential: μ_B = 540 ± 20 MeV
- Baryon density at freeze-out: n_B = 0.055 ± 0.025 fm⁻³, about 1/3 of standard nuclear density (0.17 fm⁻³)
- Number of participant nucleons in central Si-Au: N_part ≈ 108 (46 protons, 62 neutrons)
- Thermal ratio Δ/N = 0.25 ± 0.02
- Thermal π/N_part ratio: 133/108 ≈ 1.23, compared to experimental value 1.15 ± 0.12
- For light hadrons with no collective flow, expected mean transverse momentum ⟨|p_T|⟩ ≈ 2T ≈ 210 MeV

## Context
This work extends the thermal model analysis of Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1995) for Si-Au collisions at the AGS, finding a slightly lower freeze-out temperature by using directly measured hadron ratios rather than relying on Δ production reconstruction. The authors emphasize that the approach to thermalization is species-dependent: the K⁺/π⁺ ratio rises from p-p values near 0.05 to above 0.2 in central collisions, while π/N_part decreases from about 1.5 in p-A to the thermal value. The analysis enables future tests of whether Au-Au collisions at the AGS and higher-energy collisions at the CERN-SPS also show thermal hadron production, which would be a decisive step toward demonstrating that strongly interacting matter is produced in nuclear collisions.
