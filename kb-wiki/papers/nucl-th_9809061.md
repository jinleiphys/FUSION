# Hyperfine Anomaly of Be Isotopes and Anomalous Large Anomaly in $^{11}$Be

> A new result of investigations of the hyperfine structure (hfs) anomaly in Be isotopes is presented. The hfs constant for $^{11}$Be is obtained by using the core plus neutron type wave function: $ |2s_{1\over 2}>+|1d_{5\over2}\times 2^+ ; {1/2}^{+}> $. A large hfs anomaly of $^{11}$Be is found, which is mainly due to a large radius of the halo single particle state.

# Hyperfine Anomaly of Be Isotopes and Anomalous Large Anomaly in $^{11}$Be

## Key claim

The hyperfine structure (hfs) anomaly $\epsilon$ for $^{11}$Be is predicted to be an order of magnitude larger than those of $^{7}$Be and $^{9}$Be, primarily because the ground state of $^{11}$Be contains a neutron halo $2s_{1/2}$ orbit with a large root-mean-square radius (up to 6.4 fm), but the exact magnitude depends on the mixing amplitude $\alpha^2$ between the $|2s_{1/2}\rangle$ and $|1d_{5/2}\times 2^+; 1/2^+\rangle$ components, which is constrained by the measured magnetic moment of $-1.682(3)\,\mu_N$.

## Method

The authors calculate the hfs anomaly $\epsilon$ for $^{7}$Be, $^{9}$Be, and $^{11}$Be using two approaches: (1) a core-plus-neutron wave function for $^{11}$Be mixing $\nu 2s_{1/2}$ and $\nu 1d_{5/2}\times 2^+$ configurations, with matrix elements evaluated from Woods-Saxon wave functions and Cohen-Kurath wave functions for the $^{10}$Be core; and (2) a single-particle shell model with $\Delta\ell=0$ core polarization, where the expectation value of the $\Sigma_i^{(1)}$ operator is expressed in terms of the magnetic moment without free parameters. For $^{7}$Be and $^{9}$Be, the Cohen-Kurath wave functions are used directly.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of $^{11}$Be: $\mu(^{11}\text{Be}) = -1.682(3)\,\mu_N$ (experimental value from Asahi et al.)
- Predicted magnetic moment of $^{7}$Be: $\mu^{\text{Pred}}(^{7}\text{Be}) = -1.377\,\mu_N$ (from isospin symmetry with $^{7}$Li)
- Hfs anomaly for $^{11}$Be with $\alpha^2 = 0.50$, $\beta^2 = 0.50$: $\epsilon = -0.0717\%$ (spherical Woods-Saxon radius); with $\alpha^2 = 0.70$: $\epsilon = -0.091\%$; with $\alpha^2 = 0.80$: $\epsilon = -0.101\%$
- Hfs anomaly for $^{11}$Be using deformed Woods-Saxon halo radius (6.37 fm) with $\alpha^2 = 0.5$: $\epsilon = -0.0758\%$; for $\alpha^2 = 0.5$ to $0.7$: $\epsilon = -0.076\%$ to $-0.097\%$
- Hfs anomaly for $^{11}$Be with effective spin g-factor ($g_s^{\text{eff}} = 1.0$ for halo, $0.85$ for normal orbits) and $\alpha^2 = 0.65$: $\epsilon = -0.0852\%$
- Root-mean-square radius of the halo $2s_{1/2}$ orbit: 6.4 fm (deformed Woods-Saxon model, Muta et al.); 6.37 fm used in calculation
- Separation energies for $^{11}$Be neutron orbits: 0.50 MeV ($2s_{1/2}$) and 3.87 MeV ($1d_{5/2}$)
- Mixing amplitude from magnetic moment fit: $\alpha^2 = 0.5$ (without effective g-factor); $\alpha^2 = 61\%$ (with $g_s^{\text{eff}}$); $\alpha^2 \approx 65\%$ (including meson exchange currents)

## Context

This work extends the study of hyperfine anomalies in light nuclei, previously applied to stable isotopes, into the neutron-halo region. The authors build on the Cohen-Kurath (1965) shell-model wave functions for $p$-shell nuclei and the core-polarization formalism of Fujita and Arima (1975) for the $\Sigma_i^{(1)}$ operator. The magnetic moment calculation for $^{11}$Be follows Suzuki et al. (1997), who first proposed the two-component core-plus-neutron wave function. The predicted large hfs anomaly offers a new experimental observable, measurable via ion-trap techniques (Enders et al., Wada et al.), to probe the neutron halo radius in $^{11}$Be and distinguish between competing models of the ground-state structure.
