# Analysis of $ω$ self-energy at finite temperature and density in the real-time formalism

> Using the real time formalism of field theory at finite temperature and density we have evaluated the in-medium $ω$ self-energy from baryon and meson loops. We have analyzed in detail the discontinuities across the branch cuts of the self-energy function and obtained the imaginary part from the non-vanishing contributions in the cut regions. An extensive set of resonances have been considered in the baryon loops. Adding the meson loop contribution we obtain the full modified spectral function of $ω$ in a thermal gas of mesons, baryons and anti-baryons in equilibrium for several values of temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

Using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, the authors evaluate the in-medium omega meson self-energy from baryon and meson loops, obtaining the full modified spectral function in a thermal gas of mesons, baryons, and anti-baryons in equilibrium. The abstract glosses over the condition that the Landau cut contributions from baryon loops dominate the imaginary part below the omega mass, while the unitary cut from the meson loop becomes relevant only at higher invariant masses, and that the real part receives contributions from both cuts regardless of kinematic thresholds.

## Method

The authors compute the 11-component of the omega self-energy matrix in the real-time formalism for one-loop diagrams containing baryon-nucleon pairs (including spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances such as N*(1440), N*(1520), N*(1535), N*(1650), N*(1720), and the nucleon N(940)) and a rho-pion meson loop. The imaginary part is obtained from discontinuities across branch cuts, specifically the Landau cut (arising from scattering processes like omega N to B) and the unitary cut (from decays like omega to rho pi), using fully relativistic baryon propagators that treat baryons and anti-baryons on equal footing. The baryon resonances are treated with a finite width by convoluting the narrow-width self-energy with the resonance spectral function, and the rho-pion loop is folded with the rho spectral function to account for the omega to 3pi decay.

## Key numbers

* The Landau cut threshold for the rho-pion loop lies at approximately 630 MeV, while the unitary cut threshold lies at approximately 910 MeV, with the omega pole located between these two thresholds.
* For the baryon loops, the N*(1535) resonance provides the most significant contribution to the self-energy, primarily due to its strong coupling to the omega N channel.
* The imaginary part of the omega self-energy from baryon loops shows a noticeable contribution below the nominal omega mass (782 MeV), with the Landau cut dominating in this region.
* At the omega pole, the meson loop contributes a negative real part while the baryon loops contribute a positive real part, leading to a slight positive thermal mass shift that increases with baryon chemical potential and temperature.
* The omega spectral function is suppressed by a factor of approximately 10 compared to the rho spectral function in the dilepton emission rate.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the rho meson spectral function at finite temperature and baryon density (Ghosh et al., 2010; Ghosh and Sarkar, 2011) to the omega meson, using the same unified approach based on branch cuts of the self-energy function. It contrasts with earlier finite-temperature calculations that used a virial approach (Eletsky, 2001) or resonance-hole contributions (Rapp, 2001), and with cold nuclear matter studies that reported positive (Lutz, 1998), negative (Weise, 2000), or zero (Muelich, 2002) mass shifts. The results enable quantitative analysis of dilepton spectra from heavy ion collisions, particularly at the FAIR facility, where the omega spectral strength contributes alongside the dominant rho signal.
