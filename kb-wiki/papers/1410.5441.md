# Isospin transfer modes in exotic nuclei

> This work presents an approach to nuclear spin-isospin response, which is capable of describing the overall strength distribution up to high excitation energies, including the fine structure of the low-lying strength, and resolving the long-standing quenching problem. The model is a covariant realization of the nuclear field theory and based on the self-consistent extensions of the covariant energy density functional (CEDF) theory. Results of the recent calculations for spin-isospin response of ordinary and exotic medium-mass nuclei are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The proton-neutron relativistic time blocking approximation (pn-RTBA) can describe the overall Gamow-Teller strength distribution up to high excitation energies, including the fine structure of the low-lying strength, and resolve the long-standing quenching problem, but the approach does not yet include pairing correlations and the observed quenching in 208Pb is only partially reproduced (approximately 0.5 of the observed quenching below 25 MeV), with the remainder possibly coming from finite momentum transfer not included in the calculations.

## Method

The model is a covariant realization of nuclear field theory based on self-consistent extensions of the covariant energy density functional (CEDF). Calculations proceed in three steps: (i) a relativistic mean field solution is obtained with NL3 parametrization, (ii) phonon spectrum and coupling vertices for phonons with Jπ = 2+, 3-, 4+, 5-, 6+ are obtained by self-consistent relativistic RPA, and (iii) the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for the proton-neutron response function using an interaction that includes static ρ-meson and π-meson exchange, a Landau-Migdal contact term with parameter g′ = 0.6, and an energy-dependent particle-vibration coupling amplitude Φ(ω) with the Φ(0) subtraction to remove double counting.

## Key numbers

* In 208Pb, the RRPA calculation produces the major Gamow-Teller peak at 16.5 MeV with a low-energy peak structure around 10 MeV; the RTBA calculation shows that an additional 5% of the Ikeda sum rule goes above the considered energy region, while the total B(GT+) equals 0.34.
* In 208Pb, the ph⊗phonon configuration window of 30 MeV captures almost the whole effect of coupling to these configurations; extending the window to 60 MeV does not influence the results.
* In 28Si, the ph⊗phonon configurations are accommodated within a 90 MeV energy interval, close to the 100 MeV window for 1p1h pn-RRPA configurations; the GT- strength between 0 and 30 MeV amounts to 70% of the total GT- strength.
* In 28Si, the Ikeda sum rule S(GT-) - S(GT+) = 3(N-Z) is fulfilled with approximately 0.2% accuracy with respect to the total GT+ or GT- strength.
* In 208Pb, approximately 8% of the B(GT-) lies at large negative energies because of transitions to the Dirac sea, within the model space of pn-configurations between -1800 MeV and 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic time blocking approximation (RTBA), previously developed for the neutral channel by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2007, 2008) and its non-relativistic version by Litvinova and Tselyaev (2007) and Tselyaev, Grasso, and Speth (2007), to the spin-isospin channel. The approach was first applied to the spin-dipole resonance in 2012 and to the Gamow-Teller resonance in 2014. The results enable direct determination of beta-decay half-lives in exotic nuclei like 78Ni without introducing an artificial quenching factor, and future experimental data from rare isotope beam facilities will provide decisive tests for constraining many-body coupling schemes and nuclear effective interactions.
