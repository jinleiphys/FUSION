# Converging Many-Body Perturbation Theory for Ab Initio Nuclear-Structure: I. Brillouin-Wigner Perturbation Series for Closed-Shell Nuclei

> Convergence aspects of nuclear many-body perturbation theory for ground states of closed-shell nuclei are explored using a Brillouin-Wigner formulation with a new vertex function enabling high-order calculations. A general formalism for Hamiltonian partitioning and a convergence criterion for the perturbation series are proposed. Analytical derivation shows that with optimal partitionings, the convergence criterion for ground states can always be satisfied. This feature attributes to the variational principle and does not depend on the choice of an internucleon interaction or a many-body basis. Numerical calculations of the ground state energies of 4He and 16O with Daejeon16 and a bare N3LO potential in both harmonic-oscillator and Hartree-Fock bases confirm this finding.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a Brillouin-Wigner perturbation series for closed-shell nuclear ground states can always be made convergent by choosing an appropriate Hamiltonian partitioning parameter ξ, a property that follows from the variational principle and holds independently of the internucleon interaction or many-body basis.

## Method
The authors split the intrinsic A-nucleon Hamiltonian into a solvable part H0 (with an auxiliary one-body potential) and a residual interaction H1, then project the Schrödinger equation into a one-dimensional P-space (the unperturbed ground state). They introduce a diagonal partition parameter ξ in the full model space and expand the Q-space resolvent operator into a Brillouin-Wigner perturbation series. A new vertex function (K̂-box) is devised to compute high-order terms iteratively, and the convergence is controlled by the spectral radius ρ(R) of the ratio operator R defined in Q-space.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴He in HO basis (Nmax=2, Daejeon16, ħω=18 MeV): exact ground-state energy E0 = −26.822 MeV; with ξ = −132.927 MeV the series diverges (E0 lies in the divergence interval [−32.072, 54.218] MeV where ρ(R) ≥ 1); with ξ = −110 MeV the series converges to E0 = −26.822 MeV.
- For ⁴He in HO basis (Nmax=2, bare N³LO, ħω=18 MeV, ξ=0 MeV): exact ground-state energy E0 = −0.6527 MeV, lowest singularity of f0(E) at 25.77 MeV.
- For ⁴He (Nmax=8, Daejeon16, ħω=18 MeV): BW series converges to E0 = −28.36002 MeV (HO basis with ξ = −110 MeV) and to E0 = −27.28577 MeV (HF basis with ξ = ⟨Φ0^HF|H1|Φ0^HF⟩ = 74.494 MeV), matching NCSM results.
- For ¹⁶O (Nmax=4, Daejeon16, ħω=18 MeV): BW series converges to E0 = −123.42361 MeV (HO basis with ξ = −700 MeV) and to E0 = −113.31083 MeV (HF basis with ξ = ⟨Φ0^HF|H1|Φ0^HF⟩ = 409.695 MeV), matching NCSM results.
- For ⁴He in HO basis (Nmax=2, Daejeon16, ξ = −132.927 MeV): Padé[3/3] approximant (8th order) reproduces the exact ground-state energy within 1 keV.

## Context
This work extends the study of order-by-order convergence in nuclear many-body perturbation theory, previously explored for Rayleigh-Schrödinger (RS) series by Roth (2010) and Tichai (2016), who found divergence in harmonic-oscillator bases but convergence in Hartree-Fock bases for chiral interactions softened by similarity renormalization group. The authors provide a systematic convergence criterion for Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory and show that convergence can always be achieved by tuning the partitioning parameter ξ, independent of the interaction or basis. This enables ab initio many-body perturbation theory calculations for closed-shell nuclei without requiring specific renormalization procedures, and the authors state that generalization to open-shell nuclei and to RS perturbation theory is under way.
