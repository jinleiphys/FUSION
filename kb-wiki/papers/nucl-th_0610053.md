# Two-pion exchange NN potential from Lorentz-invariant $χ$EFT

> We outline the progress made in the past five years by the São Paulo group in the development of a two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential within a Lorentz-invariant framework of (baryon) chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The Lorentz-invariant (RB) two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential differs significantly from the heavy-baryon (HB) expanded version at distances beyond about 5 fm, especially in total isospin T=0 channels where the ratio can reach 1.7 at 6 fm, but these large differences are largely washed out in phase-shift predictions because the two-pion exchange potential is already very small in that region and dominated by one-pion exchange.

## Method

The authors evaluated two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials using relativistic loop integrals with dimensional regularization in the MS-bar scheme, subtracting iterated one-pion exchange via the Blankenbecler-Sugar prescription. They compared these relativistic (RB) results with their 1/m_N expanded (HB) counterparts, and computed phase shifts for peripheral partial waves using five different sets of πN low-energy constants (LECs) from the literature, with a phenomenological short-distance cutoff of the Argonne V14-V18 type.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of HB over RB two-pion exchange potential for T=1 partial waves (e.g., ³F₂) shows differences of ~20% at r = 10 fm, increasing with distance
- For T=0 partial waves (e.g., ¹F₃), the ratio reaches a factor of 1.7 at r ~ 6 fm
- The cutoff parameter c in the short-distance regulator [1 - exp(-c r²)]⁴ was varied between 1.5 and 2.5 fm⁻², with very small variations in phase shifts for LECs from Entem and Machleidt
- For Nijmegen LECs, agreement with partial wave analysis was possible only with a cutoff as low as 1.0 fm⁻²
- LEC values from the table: c₁ ranges from -1.23 to -0.76 GeV⁻¹, c₂ from 3.20 to 8.43 GeV⁻¹, c₃ from -5.94 to -3.40 GeV⁻¹, c₄ from 3.40 to 3.96 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz-invariant chiral perturbation theory framework for the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential developed by the São Paulo group (Higa, Robilotta, da Rocha 2003-2006), addressing the anomalous threshold problem in the nucleon scalar form factor identified by Becher and Leutwyler. The results show that peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering favors smaller absolute values for the LEC c₃, and that the discrepancy between Nijmegen group LECs and their own partial wave analysis may arise from using O(q³) rather than O(q⁴) potentials or from using the relativistic versus standard Schrödinger equation.
