# Spin squeezed states and wobbling motion in collective Hamiltonian

> A semiclassical approach is proposed to calculate the collective potential and mass parameters to formulate a collective Hamiltonian capable of describing the wobbling motion in both even-even and odd-mass systems. By diagonalizing the resulting collective Hamiltonian (CH), one can obtain the energies and wave functions associated with the wobbling states. Furthermore, a novel technique called spin squeezed state (SSS) maps is introduced based on the derived wave functions. To validate the results obtained from the collective Hamiltonian, a comparative analysis is conducted against predictions from the triaxial rotor model (TRM) and particle triaxial rotor (PTR) model. Notably, the SSS plots determined using the TRM and PTR models exhibit a strong correlation with the probability density distributions of the wave functions obtained from the CH. This correlation highlights the consistency and coherence between the different theoretical approaches when describing the wobbling phenomenon and associated rotational dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semiclassical approach to derive the collective potential and mass parameters for a collective Hamiltonian that describes wobbling motion, where the total angular momentum is treated as a good quantum number (unlike in the Tilted Axis Cranking approximation used in prior work). The approach is validated by showing that the collective Hamiltonian reproduces the exact triaxial rotor model results and provides a "potential + kinetic" energy interpretation of particle-triaxial-rotor calculations.

## Method

The authors derive a collective Hamiltonian in the form of a kinetic term with a phi-dependent inertia parameter B(phi) and a periodic potential V(phi) by rewriting the triaxial rotor Hamiltonian in the J3-phi representation and applying the Pauli prescription for quantization. For the particle-triaxial-rotor system, they use an adiabatic approximation where the particle angular momentum is treated as an operator while the total angular momentum is treated as a classical vector, expanding the adiabatic energy around theta=pi/2 to obtain the collective potential and mass parameter. The collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis of exponential functions, and the resulting wave functions are used to construct spin squeezed state (SSS) probability distributions via density matrices.

## Key numbers

- Moments of inertia for the triaxial rotor test case: J_m = 30, J_s = 10, J_l = 5 hbar^2/MeV for the medium, short, and long axes respectively
- For the triaxial rotor with I=8, there are five eigenstates (n=0, 2, 4, 6, 8); for I=9, there are four eigenstates (n=1, 3, 5, 7)
- The SSS uncertainty is approximately Delta_phi ≈ 3/(2j), which is smaller than the spin coherent state width of 1/sqrt(2j)
- The overlap between adjacent discrete phi-states is approximately 0.64 at the midpoint between phi_n and phi_{n+1}

## Context

This work extends the collective Hamiltonian framework for wobbling motion previously developed by Chen and Frauendorf (2014, 2016) by replacing the Tilted Axis Cranking approximation with a semiclassical derivation that preserves angular momentum as a good quantum number. The spin squeezed state technique introduced here complements the spin coherent state maps used in Chen and Frauendorf (2022) for classifying wobbling motion as longitudinal or transverse. The approach enables future extension of the microscopic TAC mean field approach to include collective rotation modes about all three principal axes of triaxial nuclei.
