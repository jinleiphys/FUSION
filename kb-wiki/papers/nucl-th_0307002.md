# A quark model analysis of the charge symmetry breaking in nuclear force

> In order to investigate the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the short range part of the nuclear force, we calculate the difference of the masses of the neutron and the proton, $Δ{\rm M}$, the difference of the scattering lengths of the p-p and n-n scatterings, $Δa$, and the difference of the analyzing power of the proton and the neutron in the n-p scattering, $ΔA(θ)$, by a quark model. In the present model the sources of CSB are the mass difference of the up and down quarks and the electromagnetic interaction. We investigate how much each of them contributes to $Δ{\rm M}$, $Δa$ and $ΔA(θ)$. It is found that the contribution of CSB of the short range part in the nuclear force is large enough to explain the observed $ΔA(θ)$, while $Δa$ is rather underestimated.

## Key claim

The short-range part of the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the nuclear force, as modeled by quark mass differences and electromagnetic interactions between constituent quarks, is large enough to explain the observed difference in analyzing power between polarized neutrons and protons in n-p scattering, but the model underestimates the difference between p-p and n-n scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors employ a constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian containing confinement, one-gluon-exchange (OGE), electromagnetic (EM), and instanton induced (III) interactions. For the neutron-proton mass difference, they use a semirelativistic kinetic energy; for the scattering observables, they use the non-relativistic quark cluster model (QCM) with the resonating group method (RGM) to obtain scattering wave functions, treating the CSB contributions via distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA).

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton mass difference: ΔM = 1.29 MeV (experimental value used as input to determine the up-down quark mass difference)
- Up-down quark mass difference: Δm = 7.3 MeV (for P_III = 0.4) and Δm = 5.2 MeV (for P_III = 0.5)
- Average nucleon mass: Ṁ = 939 MeV
- Constituent quark mass: m̄ = 313 MeV
- Gaussian size parameter: b = 0.6 fm
- Calculated Δa (difference of p-p and n-n scattering lengths): 0.79 fm (P_III = 0.4) and 0.52 fm (P_III = 0.5), compared to experimental value 1.5 ± 0.5 fm
- Average scattering length: ā = -17.9 fm for both parameter sets, compared to experimental -18.1 ± 0.5 fm
- ΔA(θ) at E_n = 183 MeV: large enough to reproduce experimental data (shown in figures)
- ΔA(θ) at E_n = 477 MeV: too large compared to data

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model studies of CSB by Chemtob and Yang (CY) and Bräuer et al., who calculated Δa and ΔA(θ) using the quark cluster model but obtained too small effects. The authors correct Bräuer et al.'s omission of the symmetric spin-orbit term (qSLS) and their erroneous unit conversion, finding that the symmetric spin-orbit interaction is three times larger than the antisymmetric one and gives significant contributions. The inclusion of the Instanton Induced Interaction (III) reduces the OGE coupling strength α_s from 1.52 to 0.76-0.91, making it more consistent with perturbative QCD. The model enables simultaneous analysis of three CSB observables (ΔM, Δa, ΔA(θ)) from a common quark-level framework, though Δa remains underestimated and the effective range difference Δr has the wrong sign, indicating the need for further investigation of radial dependence.
