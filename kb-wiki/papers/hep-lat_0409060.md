# Exploring Lattice Methods for Cold Fermionic Atoms

> There has been a surge of experimental effort recently in cooling trapped fermionic atoms to quantum degeneracy. By varying an external magnetic field, interactions between atoms can be made arbitrarily strong. When the S wave scattering length becomes comparable to and larger than the interparticle spacing, standard mean field analysis breaks down. In this case the system exhibits a type of universality, and J-W. Chen and D. B. Kaplan recently showed how this system can be studied from first principles using lattice field theory. This poster presents the first results of exploratory simulations. The existence of a continuum limit is checked and the pairing condensate is studied as a function of the external source strength over a range of temperatures. Preliminary results show simulations can locate the critical temperature.

# Exploring Lattice Methods for Cold Fermionic Atoms

## Key claim

The paper presents exploratory lattice simulations showing that the Chen-Kaplan lattice field theory approach can describe dilute Fermi gases with large scattering lengths, and that a continuum limit exists where both the interparticle spacing and scattering length are large compared to the lattice spacing. The results are preliminary and indicate that simulations can locate the critical temperature, but the validity of linear extrapolations of the order parameter to zero source strength is still under study.

## Method

The authors implement a lattice field theory with two species of nonrelativistic fermions and a local four-fermion interaction, using an auxiliary real scalar field to make the action quadratic in fermions. A source term for fermion pairing is introduced to study spontaneous symmetry breaking in finite volume, and the partition function is rewritten in terms of a Majorana fermion to yield a real, nonnegative integrand. Hybrid Monte Carlo simulations are performed on lattices of size \(6^3 \times 12\) and \(8^3 \times 16\), with the fermion mass \(M\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and coupling \(m^2\) as lattice parameters controlling the physical scattering length \(a\), density \(n\), and temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- Number density \(n \ll 1\) in lattice units at \(1/a = 0\) and \(\mu = 0\) on \(6^3 \times 12\) lattices with \(J = 0.05\), establishing that a continuum limit exists.
- Order parameter \(\Sigma\) studied as a function of pairing source \(J\) on \(8^3 \times 16\) lattices with \(\mu = 0.4\) and \(m^2 = 0.1456\); linear extrapolation to \(J = 0\) indicates U(1) restoration close to \(M = 1.4\).
- For values of \(1/a\) larger than those plotted, 6000 HMC trajectories were insufficient to equilibrate the lattice.

## Context

This work extends the first-principles lattice field theory approach proposed by Chen and Kaplan (2003) for studying cold fermionic atoms when the S-wave scattering length is large and mean field analysis breaks down. The simulations check the existence of a continuum limit and begin mapping the critical temperature in the space of lattice parameters \((m^2, M)\), which can later be translated to physical parameters \((1/a, T)\). The breakdown of the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm at strong coupling is noted as a known issue from simulations of the 3D attractive Hubbard model. Future work requires repeating these steps for larger volumes and smaller lattice spacings to obtain precision results for the critical temperature as a function of \(a n^{1/3}\).
