# $g_{\rm A}$-sensitive $β$ spectral shapes in the mass $A=86-99$ region assessed by the nuclear shell model

> Recent years have witnessed an expanding interest in experimental studies of $β$ electrons (electrons emitted in $β^-$ decay transitions) and their energy distributions, the so-called $β$-electron spectra. These experiments are interested mainly in $β$ transitions with electron spectra sensitive to the effective value of the weak axial coupling $g_{\rm A}$. In the present paper we make an extensive search for $g_{\rm A}$ sensitive $β$ spectral shapes in the $A=86-99$ region using the nuclear shell model with the well established Hamiltonians $\textit{glekpn}$ and $\textit{jj45pnb}$, designed to render a good description of the spectroscopic properties of nuclei in this mass region. We have found eight $β^-$ decay transitions with various degrees of $g_{\rm A}$ sensitivity. Moreover, these transitions are also important in pinning down the value of the so-called small relativistic vector nuclear matrix element, sNME. In addition, some of the corresponding mother nuclei are important contributors to the antineutrino flux from nuclear reactors. All this means that the found $β$ transitions are potentially of great interest for future rare-events experiments.

## Key claim
The paper identifies eight beta-decay transitions in the mass A=86-99 region whose spectral shapes show varying degrees of sensitivity to the effective weak axial coupling g_A, but the actual sensitivity is conditional on the choice of nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian (glekpn or jj45pnb) and on which of the two possible fitted values of the small relativistic vector nuclear matrix element (sNME) is adopted.

## Method
The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations using the KSHELL software with the glekpn and jj45pnb Hamiltonians, employing three different single-particle energy sets for glekpn depending on mass region. For each of eight forbidden non-unique beta-decay transitions, they fitted the sNME to reproduce experimental branching ratios for a range of g_A values from 0.8 to 1.2, then computed the corresponding beta spectral shapes S(w_e) and compared them for sensitivity to both g_A and sNME.

## Key numbers
- Eight beta-decay transitions were identified as potentially g_A-sensitive, spanning mother nuclei from 86Br to 99Tc
- The g_A range studied was 0.8-1.2, compared to the free-nucleon value g_A=1.27
- For 86Br(1-) to 86Kr(0+), the Q-value is 7.633(3) MeV with a branching ratio of 15(8)%
- For 99Tc(9/2+) to 99Ru(5/2+), the Q-value is 0.2975(10) MeV with a branching ratio of 99.9984(4)%
- The CVC-predicted sNME values range from -17.1 x 10^-2 (for 99Tc with jj45pnb) to +6.82 x 10^-2 (for 95Sr with glekpn)
- For 97Zr(1/2+) to 97Nb(3/2-), the glekpn Hamiltonian could not reproduce the measured branching ratio for g_A=1.2, so the range was limited to g_A=0.8-1.1

## Context
This work extends the spectrum-shape method (SSM) previously applied to 113Cd and 115In decays by Bod2020, Kos2021, and Led2022, and the enhanced SSM introduced by Kum2020 and Kum2021. The study focuses on the A=86-99 region where many fission products contribute to reactor antineutrino flux anomalies discussed by Hay2019 and Ram2022. The identified transitions provide candidates for future rare-events experiments aiming to determine the effective g_A value, which is crucial for estimating sensitivities of neutrinoless double-beta decay searches as reviewed by Suh2017 and Eji2019.
