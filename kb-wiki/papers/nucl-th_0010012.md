# RPA quasi-elastic responses in infinite and finite nuclear systems

> Quasi-elastic responses in nuclear matter and in $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ca nuclei are calculated in ring approximation to investigate the finite size effects on the electromagnetic quasi-elastic responses. A method to simulate these effects in infinite systems calculations is proposed. The sensitivity of the results to the various terms of the residual interaction is studied. The results of nuclear matter RPA calculations are compared with those obtained in ring approximation to evidence the importance of the exchange terms.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that using a diffused (smooth) Fermi surface in nuclear matter calculations, rather than the standard sharp step function, can reproduce finite-nucleus quasi-elastic responses in ring approximation, provided the isospin channel of the residual interaction is correctly handled; the agreement is excellent for longitudinal responses but shows small discrepancies in transverse responses due to the isospin part of the force being inactive in nuclear matter ring calculations.

## Method

The authors calculated electromagnetic quasi-elastic response functions for nuclear matter and for the finite nuclei 12C and 40Ca using the ring approximation (RPA without exchange terms) and, for nuclear matter, full RPA including exchange diagrams. To simulate finite-size effects in infinite systems, they replaced the sharp Fermi surface step function with a Fermi function n(h) = 1/(1 + exp((h - kF)/a)), adjusting the parameters kF and a to reproduce the finite-nucleus free responses. The residual interactions used were a finite-range polarization potential and a zero-range Migdal interaction, with nuclear matter RPA exchange terms summed via a contact-interaction decomposition method.

## Key numbers

- For 12C, the diffused Fermi surface parameters were kF = 0.85 fm^-1 and a = 0.20 fm^-1; for 40Ca, kF = 1.0 fm^-1 and a = 0.17 fm^-1.
- For the sharp Fermi surface, effective Fermi momenta were kF = 1.09 fm^-1 for 12C and kF = 1.19 fm^-1 for 40Ca.
- Transferred momenta analyzed were q = 400 MeV/c and q = 500 MeV/c.
- Multipole excitations in finite nucleus calculations were summed up to angular momentum J = 12.
- The zero-range Migdal interaction parameters used were f0 = 386, f'0 = 289.5, g0 = 106.2, and g'0 = 135.1, in MeV fm^3 units.

## Context

This work extends the ring approximation calculations of Co' et al. (1988) for finite nuclei and the nuclear matter RPA framework of Bauer et al. (1996), addressing discrepancies between infinite and finite system results. The proposed diffused Fermi surface method is shown to be superior to the local density approximation (LDA), which Amaro et al. (1994) found to poorly reproduce finite-nucleus quasi-elastic responses, and to the effective momentum approximation. The approach enables simpler nuclear matter calculations that can be extended beyond RPA to second RPA (SRPA) or extended RPA (ERPA) without the large computational effort required for finite-nucleus RPA calculations.
