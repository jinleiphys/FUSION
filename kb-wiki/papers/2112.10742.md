# Study of the Isomeric State in $^{16}$N Using the $^{16}$N$^{g,m}$($d$,$^3$He) Reaction

> The isomeric state of $^{16}$N was studied using the $^{16}$N$^{g,m}$($d$,$^3$He)~proton-removal reactions at \mbox{11.8~MeV/$u$} in inverse kinematics. The $^{16}$N beam, of which 24% was in the isomeric state, was produced using the ATLAS in-fight facility and delivered to the HELIOS spectrometer, which was used to analyze the $^{3}$He ions from the ($d$,$^{3}$He) reactions. The simultaneous measurement of reactions on both the ground and isomeric states, reduced the systematic uncertainties from the experiment and in the analysis. A direct and reliable comparison of the relative spectroscopic factors was made based on a Distorted-Wave Born Approximation approach. The experimental results suggest that the isomeric state of $^{16}$N is an excited neutron-halo state. The results can be understood through calculations using a Woods-Saxon potential model, which captures the effects of weak-binding.

## Key claim

The isomeric state of 16N is an excited neutron-halo state, with the experimental ratio of spectroscopic factors between the ground-state and isomeric-state proton-removal reactions being consistent with unity, indicating that the 1s1/2 neutron in the 16N isomer is similar to that in the 15C halo ground state.

## Method

The 16Ng,m(d,3He) proton-removal reactions were performed in inverse kinematics at 11.8 MeV/u using the ATLAS in-flight facility and the HELIOS spectrometer. A 16N beam containing 24% isomeric-state component bombarded a CD2 target, and the 3He ions from the reactions were analyzed with a position-sensitive Si detector array. The simultaneous measurement of reactions on both the ground and isomeric states reduced systematic uncertainties, and relative spectroscopic factors were extracted using a Distorted-Wave Born Approximation approach.

## Key numbers

- The 16N beam contained 24(2)% isomeric-state component at 11.8 MeV/u
- The Q-value for 16Nm→15C(0.00) was -5.985 MeV, and for 16Ng→15C(0.74) was -5.125 MeV
- The Q-value resolution was approximately 500 keV FWHM
- The ratio R of spectroscopic factors (SF)g0p1/2/(SF)m0p1/2 was 0.81±0.19 from angular distribution fitting
- The ratio R determined from integrated yields over the full angular coverage was 0.92±0.15
- The ratio R over the common center-of-mass angle range 15.63° to 17.30° was 0.75±0.18
- The average value of R from all extraction methods was 0.83±0.17

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron-halo states, particularly the well-known s-shell halo ground state of 15C studied by Fang (2004) and the 16N isomeric state previously investigated through asymptotic normalization coefficients by Li (2016), which deduced an rms radius of 4.82±0.42 fm. The simultaneous measurement technique enables a direct comparison of spectroscopic factors between halo and non-halo states while canceling systematic uncertainties from the DWBA calculations, and the results can be understood through Woods-Saxon potential model calculations that capture weak-binding effects.
