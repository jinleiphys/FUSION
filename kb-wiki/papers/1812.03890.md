# Exotic $Ω_c^0$ baryons from meson-baryon scattering

> A meson-baryon interaction in the charm $+1$, strangeness $-2$ and isospin $0$ sector is built from a t-channel vector meson exchange model employing effective Lagrangians. The implementation of coupled-channel unitarization in the s-wave scattering amplitudes gives rise to two structures that have similar masses and widths to those of the $Ω_c(3050)^0$ and $Ω_c(3090)^0$ states recently observed by the LHCb collaboration. A meson-baryon molecular interpretation of these resonances would assign their spin-parity to be $J^P=1/2^-$.

# Exotic Ω_c^0 baryons from meson-baryon scattering

## Key claim
A meson-baryon molecular interpretation can describe the Ω_c(3050)^0 and Ω_c(3090)^0 states observed by LHCb as dynamically generated resonances with spin-parity J^P = 1/2^-, but the model's heavier state (3103.3 MeV) is about 10 MeV above the experimental mass and its width (17 MeV) is roughly twice the experimental value (8.7 MeV), requiring adjustment of subtraction constants to bring the results within 2σ of the data.

## Method
A t-channel vector meson exchange model with effective Lagrangians from the hidden gauge formalism was used to construct the s-wave meson-baryon interaction kernel in the (I,S,C) = (0,-2,1) sector. The on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved in coupled channels with dimensional regularization, using subtraction constants mapped to a cutoff of Λ = 800 MeV, to generate dynamically the scattering amplitudes and extract pole positions, couplings, and compositeness for the resulting resonances.

## Key numbers
- Pole masses from the pseudoscalar-baryon interaction with Λ = 800 MeV: M₁ = 3051.6 MeV, Γ₁ = 0.45 MeV; M₂ = 3103.3 MeV, Γ₂ = 17 MeV
- Experimental LHCb values: Ω_c(3050)^0 mass = 3050.2 ± 0.1 ± 0.1^{+0.3}_{-0.5} MeV, width = 0.8 ± 0.2 ± 0.1 MeV; Ω_c(3090)^0 mass = 3090.2 ± 0.3 ± 0.5^{+0.3}_{-0.5} MeV, width = 8.7 ± 1.0 ± 0.8 MeV
- After tuning subtraction constants (equivalent cutoff range 320-950 MeV): adjusted masses 3050.3 MeV and 3090.8 MeV, widths 0.44 MeV and 12 MeV
- Compositeness of the higher state at 3090.8 MeV in the DΞ channel: 0.91 (real part of -g² dG/dE)
- Vector-baryon interaction generated resonances at 3231 MeV (D*Ξ bound state) and 3419 MeV (K̅*Ξ'_c composite)
- Predicted Ω_b^- resonances from pseudoscalar-baryon interaction: 6418 MeV and 6519 MeV; from vector-baryon interaction: 6560 MeV, 6665 MeV, and 6797 MeV

## Context
This work extends the meson-baryon molecular approach previously applied to the P_c pentaquarks (Hofmann 2005, Jimenez-Tejero 2009, Romanets 2012) and re-examined by the same authors (Montana 2017) in light of the LHCb Ω_c observations (Aaij 2017). The results are similar to those from other groups (Debastiani 2017, Wang 2017, Chen 2017, Nieves 2017) who also find molecular interpretations for these states, and the predictions for bottom-sector analogs (Liang 2017) are consistent with the present work. The findings enable experimental searches for the predicted Ω_b^- resonances in the 6400-6800 MeV region and motivate further study of whether the vector-baryon resonances, which are artificially narrow in this model due to the lack of coupling to lower pseudoscalar-baryon channels, might correspond to states not yet observed.
