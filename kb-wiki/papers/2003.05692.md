# Effects of quark anomalous magnetic moment on the thermodynamical properties and mesonic excitations of magnetized hot and dense matter in PNJL model

> Various thermodynamic quantities and the phase diagram of strongly interacting hot and dense magnetized quark matter are obtained with the $ 2 $-flavour Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop considering finite values of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks. Susceptibilities associated with constituent quark mass and traced Polyakov loop are used to evaluate chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures. It is found that, inclusion of the AMM of the quarks in presence of the background magnetic field results in a substantial decrease in the chiral as well as deconfinement transition temperatures in contrast to an enhancement in the chiral transition temperature in its absence. Using standard techniques of finite temperature field theory, the two point thermo-magnetic mesonic correlation functions in the scalar ($σ$) and neutral pseudoscalar ($π^0$) channels are evaluated to calculate the masses of $σ$ and $ π^0 $ considering the AMM of the quarks.

## Key claim

Including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the PNJL model with a background magnetic field causes a substantial decrease in both the chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures, in contrast to the enhancement of the chiral transition temperature seen when AMM is omitted.

## Method

The authors use the 2-flavour Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop (PNJL) at finite temperature, chemical potential, and constant external magnetic field, incorporating the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks via a term proportional to \(\hat{a} \sigma^{\mu\nu} F_{\mu\nu}\) in the Lagrangian. They compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation, solve the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass and the traced Polyakov loop, and evaluate the two-point thermo-magnetic mesonic correlation functions in the scalar (\(\sigma\)) and neutral pseudoscalar (\(\pi^0\)) channels using standard finite-temperature field theory techniques.

## Key numbers

* Chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures are found to decrease substantially when the quark AMM is included in the presence of a background magnetic field, compared to the case without AMM where the chiral transition temperature is enhanced.
* The Polyakov loop potential parameters used are \(a_0 = 6.75\), \(a_1 = -1.95\), \(a_2 = 2.625\), \(a_3 = -7.44\), \(b_3 = 0.75\), \(b_4 = 7.5\), with \(T_0 = 190\) MeV.
* The energy eigenvalues of quarks in the magnetic field are given by \(\omega_{nfs} = \sqrt{p_z^2 + \left( \sqrt{|q_f B| (2n+1-s) + M^2} - s \kappa_f q_f B \right)^2}\), where \(n\) is the Landau level index and \(s\) is the spin index.

## Context

This work extends previous PNJL model studies of magnetized quark matter (Fukushima, 2008; Gatto, 2011; Ferreira, 2014; Avancini, 2016) by including, for the first time in this framework, the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks. It addresses the discrepancy between lattice QCD results showing inverse magnetic catalysis (Bali, 2012) and earlier NJL/PNJL results showing magnetic catalysis, following earlier attempts using a lattice-inspired \(eB\)-dependent coupling (Farias, 2017) or beyond-mean-field effects (Ayala, 2014). The results enable further study of mesonic properties in magnetized matter and may help reconcile effective model predictions with lattice QCD at finite magnetic field.
