# Angular pattern of minijet transverse energy flow in hadron and nuclear collisions

> The azimuthal asymmetry of minijet system produced at the early stage of nucleon-nucleon and nuclear collisions in a central rapidity window is studied. We show that in pp collisions the minijet transverse energy production in a central rapidity window is essentially unbalanced in azimuth due to asymmetric contributions in which only one minijet hits the acceptance window. We further study the angular pattern of transverse energy flow generated by semihard degrees of freedom at the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions and its dependence on the number of semihard collisions in the models both including and neglecting soft contributions to the inelastic cross section at RHIC and LHC energies as well as on the choice of the infrared cutoff.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in pp collisions, minijet transverse energy production in a central rapidity window is inherently azimuthally unbalanced because only one jet from a hard 2-to-2 scattering often falls into the acceptance window; this fundamental asymmetry persists in nuclear collisions and its magnitude depends on the number of semihard collisions, the inclusion or neglect of soft contributions to the inelastic cross section, and the choice of infrared cutoff.

## Method

The authors compute the one-jet and two-jet contributions to the transverse energy production cross section in pp collisions at leading order in collinearly factorized QCD, using MRSG parton distribution functions and a unit central rapidity window (|y|<0.5). They then embed these elementary cross sections into a geometric model of nucleus-nucleus collisions (PbPb) where the nuclear collision is treated as a Poissonian superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, with the probability of asymmetric (one-jet) versus symmetric (two-jet) events given by the pp results. A Monte Carlo procedure generates ensembles of events to compute the distribution of the azimuthal asymmetry variable δE and the normalized asymmetry r for central PbPb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, under two scenarios: a "mixed" scenario with a constant soft contribution of 32 mb, and a "hard" scenario with no soft contribution.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the one-jet (asymmetric) contribution dominates at transverse energies below about 4.5 GeV, where the two-jet (symmetric) contribution takes over.
- In pp collisions at √s = 5500 GeV, the asymmetric contribution is clearly dominant over the entire minijet transverse energy range.
- For pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the power-law fit parameters for one-jet and two-jet spectra (c (E⊥/1 GeV)^(-α)) are: α1 = 5.09, c1 = 173 mb/GeV; α2 = 4.53, c2 = 77 mb/GeV.
- For pp collisions at √s = 5500 GeV: α1 = 4.25, c1 = 3099 mb/GeV; α2 = 3.93, c2 = 819 mb/GeV.
- For central PbPb collisions at √s = 200 GeV with σsoft = 0: average number of pp collisions N̄ = 75.7, √⟨δE²⟩ = 21 GeV, standard deviation a of normalized asymmetry r = 0.088 (δφ=π), 0.124 (δφ=π/2), 0.178 (δφ=π/4).
- For central PbPb collisions at √s = 200 GeV with σsoft = 32 mb: N̄ = 17.1, √⟨δE²⟩ = 14 GeV, a = 0.177 (δφ=π), 0.259 (δφ=π/2), 0.387 (δφ=π/4).
- For central PbPb collisions at √s = 5500 GeV with σsoft = 0: N̄ = 87.4, √⟨δE²⟩ = 84 GeV, a = 0.094 (δφ=π), 0.133 (δφ=π/2), 0.189 (δφ=π/4).
- For central PbPb collisions at √s = 5500 GeV with σsoft = 32 mb: N̄ = 47.1, √⟨δE²⟩ = 74 GeV, a = 0.127 (δφ=π), 0.180 (δφ=π/2), 0.257 (δφ=π/4).
- The infrared cutoff E0 for pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV is 2.4 GeV (σsoft=0) or 3.5 GeV (σsoft=32 mb); at √s = 5500 GeV it is 6.9 GeV (σsoft=0) or 8.4 GeV (σsoft=32 mb).
- The probability of an asymmetric one-jet pp collision p1 at √s = 200 GeV is 0.54 (σsoft=0) or 0.48 (σsoft=32 mb); at √s = 5500 GeV it is 0.65 (σsoft=0) or 0.64 (σsoft=32 mb).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of event-by-event azimuthal asymmetries in minijet production first presented in G. R. et al. (1998) using HIJING, but here the authors use a purely S-matrix-based, collinearly factorized QCD approach without assumptions about parton coordinates or higher-order structure. The study builds on the geometric model of nuclear collisions from Eskola, Kajantie, and Lindfors (1989) and the minijet formalism of K. et al. (1989) and Eskola et al. (1989). The results enable future next-to-leading order calculations of energy-energy correlations and provide a baseline for understanding whether primordial angular asymmetries survive parton reinteraction and hadronization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
