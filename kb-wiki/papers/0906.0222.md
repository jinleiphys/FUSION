# On the ϕ(1020)f_0(980) S-wave scattering and the Y(2175) resonance

> We have studied the ϕ(1020)f_0(980) S-wave scattering at energies around threshold employing chiral Lagrangians coupled to vector mesons through minimal coupling. The interaction kernel is obtained by considering the f_0(980) as a K\bar{K} bound state. The Y(2175) resonance is generated in this approach by the self-interactions between the ϕ(1020) and the f_0(980) resonances. We are able to describe the e^+e^-\to ϕ(1020)f_0(980) recent scattering data to test experimentally our scattering amplitudes, concluding that the Y(2175) resonance has a large ϕ(1020)f_0(980) meson-meson component.

## Key claim

The Y(2175) resonance is generated dynamically as a \(\phi(1020)f_0(980)\) meson-meson molecule, arising from the self-interactions between the \(\phi(1020)\) and the \(f_0(980)\) resonances, rather than being a conventional quarkonium or tetraquark state. The authors conclude that the Y(2175) has a "large \(\phi(1020)f_0(980)\) meson-meson component," but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the \(f_0(980)\) is a \(K\bar{K}\) bound state and on the specific choice of the coupling parameter \(g^2\) (taken negative to mimic \(\phi K\) scattering dominated by the \(K_1(1400)\) resonance).

## Method

The authors derived the \(\phi(1020)f_0(980)\) S-wave scattering amplitude by first calculating the \(\phi K\bar{K}\) scattering kernel from the lowest-order SU(3) chiral Lagrangian with vector mesons introduced via minimal coupling, treating the \(f_0(980)\) as a \(K\bar{K}\) bound state. The dominant contribution came from triangular kaon-loop diagrams, which were resummed using the \(K\bar{K}\) \(I=0\) S-wave amplitude from unitarized chiral perturbation theory. The \(\phi f_0\) potential was extracted as the residue at the double \(f_0(980)\) pole, and the full scattering amplitude was obtained by unitarizing this potential with a \(\phi f_0\) loop function, with the subtraction constant \(a_1\) fitted to \(e^+e^-\to \phi f_0(980)\) data from BABAR and Belle.

## Key numbers

* The Y(2175) resonance is generated with a mass of 2.18 GeV and width of 48, 72, or 100 MeV for \(\sqrt{-g^2}=5, 6, 7\) respectively, with the peak fixed at 2.18 GeV (the average of BABAR and BES values: \(M_Y=2.180\pm 0.008\) GeV, \(\Gamma_Y=0.060\pm 0.014\) GeV).
* Best-fit parameters for \(M_{f_0}=0.98\) GeV: \(\sqrt{-g^2}=7.33\pm 0.30\), \(a_1=-2.41\pm 0.14\), \(\mathcal{N}=0.79\pm 0.06\), with \(\chi^2/(46-3)=88/43\).
* Best-fit parameters for \(M_{f_0}=0.99\) GeV: \(\sqrt{-g^2}=3.94\pm 0.18\), \(a_1=-2.84\pm 0.18\), \(\mathcal{N}=0.52\pm 0.05\), with \(\chi^2/(46-3)=108/43\).
* The \(f_0(980)\) coupling to \(|K\bar{K}\rangle_0\) is \(\gamma_{K\bar{K}}\simeq 4\) GeV, and the pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi=92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of unitarized chiral perturbation theory, previously applied to the \(f_0(980)\) as a \(K\bar{K}\) bound state by Oller and collaborators (1998), to the vector-meson sector by including the \(\phi(1020)\). It contrasts with alternative explanations for the Y(2175), such as the tetraquark state proposed by Wang (2007) and Hosaka (2008), the hybrid \(s\bar{s}g\) resonance studied by Ding and Yan (2007), and the \(2^3D_1\) \(s\bar{s}\) quarkonium assignment considered by Ding and Yan (2008). The results enable future experimental discrimination between the molecular interpretation and other models, particularly through the predicted decay patterns and the threshold behavior of the \(\phi f_0\) scattering amplitude.
