# Applicability of the Wigner functional approach to evolution of quantum fields

> Evolution of highly excited quantum field is considered in the framework of Keldysh formalism . It is demonstrated that leading order (LO) term of semiclassical approximation appears as well-known Classical Statistical Approximation (CSA). In simple case of spatially homogeneous scalar field analytical expressions for leading and next-to-leading (NLO) order are presented. It is shown that the range of applicability of CSA strongly depends on the properties of the initial state of the system

## Key claim

The range of applicability of the Classical Statistical Approximation (CSA) for highly excited quantum fields depends critically on the properties of the initial state; the next-to-leading order (NLO) correction grows as \(t_1^3\) at late times, but for highly excited initial states (large \(A\) in the Wigner function) the NLO term is suppressed, making the CSA valid at early times.

## Method

The authors use the Keldysh formalism to compute expectation values of observables for a spatially homogeneous scalar \(\varphi^4\) theory. They expand the Keldysh action in powers of the quantum field \(\phi_q\), identifying the leading order (LO) as the Classical Statistical Approximation (CSA) where the observable is averaged over initial conditions weighted by the Wigner functional. The next-to-leading order (NLO) correction is obtained by treating the \(\phi_q^3\) term via functional derivatives with respect to an auxiliary source \(J\), leading to expressions involving variations of the classical solution. Analytical solutions for the classical field are given in terms of Jacobi elliptic functions, and the averaging over initial conditions is performed analytically using a Gaussian Wigner functional and saddle-point approximation.

## Key numbers

- The classical solution period is \(T_{cl} = \frac{4}{g \phi_m} K(1/2)\), where \(K(1/2)\) is the complete elliptic integral of the first kind.
- The NLO correction to the field expectation value scales as \(\frac{g t_1^3}{4 V_3^2 \phi_m^3}\) relative to the LO term (dimensionless form in Eq. (phi_NLO_3)).
- The NLO correction to the pressure scales as \(\frac{g t_1^3}{4 V_3^2 \phi_m^3}\) relative to the LO term (Eq. (p_NLO)).
- The NLO energy correction is exactly zero: \(\varepsilon_{NLO} = 0\).
- The Gaussian Wigner functional parameters are \(\alpha_0\) (width in field) and \(p_0\) (width in momentum), with the initial field centered at \(A\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation (Leonidov and Radovskaya, 2015) showing that the LO of the Keldysh formalism reproduces the Classical Statistical Approximation, which is widely used in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions (initial1, kurkela3) and cosmology (cosm_1, cosm_3). The paper provides the first systematic calculation of NLO corrections to the CSA for a scalar field, demonstrating that the validity of the CSA depends on the initial state's excitation level. This enables future work to assess when the CSA is reliable for realistic far-from-equilibrium systems such as heavy ion collisions and post-inflationary reheating.
