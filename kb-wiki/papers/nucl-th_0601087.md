# A New Boson Realization of Two-Level Pairing Model in Many-Fermion System and its Classical Counterpart

> A new method for describing the two-level pairing model in a many-fermion system is presented. Basic idea comes from the Schwinger boson representation for the su(2) cross su(2)-algebra, in which four kinds of boson operators govern the dynamics. It is well known that in the original fermion space, it is almost impossible to introduce any trial state for the variation except the BCS state in the framework of the mean field approximation. However, in the Schwinger boson representation, various trial states can be defined on the condition that the mean field approximation is useful. In these states, the mixed-mode coherent state is mainly discussed in this paper.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new boson realization of the two-level pairing model using a Schwinger boson representation for the \(su(2)\otimes su(2)\)-algebra, which enables the construction of trial states beyond the BCS state for mean-field approximations. The key qualifier is that this method works specifically within the Schwinger boson representation, where the mixed-mode coherent state is introduced as a wave packet that induces a classical counterpart of the model.

## Method

The authors map the two-level pairing model from the original fermion space into a boson space using four kinds of boson operators \(({\hat a}_\sigma, {\hat a}_\sigma^*)\) and \(({\hat b}_\sigma, {\hat b}_\sigma^*)\) for \(\sigma=\pm\), which realize the \(su(2)\otimes su(2)\)-algebra. They then apply the MYT boson mapping method (Marumori, Yamamura, and Tokunaga, 1965) to reduce the system to a single boson degree of freedom \(({\hat c},{\hat c}^*)\) under fixed constants of motion \((T,M,L)\). Finally, they construct a mixed-mode coherent state as a product of coherent states for the \(su(2)\)- and \(su(1,1)\)-spin systems, which yields a classical counterpart parametrized by action-angle variables \((K,\psi)\).

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian contains single-particle energy difference \(\hbar \epsilon > 0\) and pairing interaction strength \(\hbar^2 G > 0\).
* The eigenvalues of the Casimir operator \({\hat {\mib S}}(\sigma)^2\) for seniority zero are \((\hbar\Omega_{\sigma}/2)(\hbar\Omega_{\sigma}/2+\hbar)\), where \(\Omega_\sigma = (2j_\sigma+1)/2\).
* The quantum numbers \(k\), \(t\), \(m\), and \(l\) satisfy \(k=0,1,2,\dots\), \(m=0,1/2,1,\dots\), \(l=0,1/2,1,\dots\), and for case (i) \(t=1/2,1,3/2,\dots\) with \(k\leq k^0=2{\rm Min}(l,m)\).
* The classical Hamiltonian is expressed as \(H = -\left[\epsilon(L+M-(T-\hbar/2))+4GTM\right] + 2\left[\epsilon-G(L+M-(T-\hbar/2))\right]K + 2GK^2 - 2G\sqrt{K(2T+K)(2M-K)(2L-K)}\cos\psi\).

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger boson representation approach previously developed by the authors with Kuriyama (2004) for \(su(2)\)- and \(su(1,1)\)-algebras, now applied to the two-level pairing model governed by \(su(2)\otimes su(2)\)-algebra. The method contrasts with the standard BCS theory, which in the original fermion space cannot introduce trial states beyond the BCS state for mean-field approximations. The mixed-mode coherent state enables a classical counterpart description and opens the possibility to study phase transitions in finite fermion systems beyond the mean-field approximation, building on the boson expansion methods reviewed by Marshalek (1987).
