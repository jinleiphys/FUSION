# Rescattering effect on the measurement of K* spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions

> Spin alignment of vector mesons in noncentral relativistic heavy ion collisions provides a novel probe of the global quark polarization and hadronization mechanism. In this paper, we discuss the spin alignment of a short-lived vector meson, namely K*, arising from the hadronic rescattering process using the UrQMD model. This spin alignment is not related to global quark polarization but cannot be distinguished from those arising from global quark polarization in experiments. The spin alignment parameter $ρ_{00}$ is found to deviate from 1/3 by up to -0.008 (0.03) with respect to the reaction (production) plane. These deviations are much larger than the expected signal from all the theoretical models implementing the conventional global quark polarization mechanism as well as the current experimental precision, and should be considered seriously when comparing measurements with theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The hadronic rescattering effect on short-lived K* mesons in heavy-ion collisions produces an artificial spin alignment signal that is not related to global quark polarization but cannot be distinguished from it experimentally; the deviations from 1/3 are much larger than expected from conventional global quark polarization mechanisms and should be considered seriously when comparing measurements with theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, exploiting the model's collision history file to track all K*0 -> K pi decays. They categorized K*0 mesons as "reconstructable" if neither decay daughter participated in any elastic or inelastic collision with other hadrons, and "un-reconstructable" if one or both daughters scattered; the spin alignment parameter rho_00 was then extracted from the cos(theta*) angular distribution of reconstructable K*0 mesons with respect to both the reaction plane and the production plane.

## Key numbers

- The deviation of rho_00 from 1/3 for reconstructable K*0 with respect to the reaction plane reaches as large as -0.008 at 0.8 < p_T < 1.2 GeV/c and 0-20% centrality in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The deviation of rho_00 from 1/3 for reconstructable K*0 with respect to the production plane reaches as large as 0.03 at 0.8 < p_T < 1.2 GeV/c and 0-20% centrality.
- For all K*0 (including un-reconstructable ones) in 20-60% centrality, rho_00 = 0.3325 +/- 0.0009 with respect to the reaction plane and 0.3329 +/- 0.0009 with respect to the production plane, both consistent with 1/3.
- In a specific bin (0.8 < p_T < 1.2 GeV/c, 20-60% centrality), reconstructable K*0 rho_00 = 0.3275 +/- 0.0011 with respect to the reaction plane (deviation of -0.0058, >5 sigma) and 0.3506 +/- 0.0011 with respect to the production plane (deviation of +0.017).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of K* spin alignment measurements by ALICE (2019) and STAR (2022), which found puzzling differences between K*0 and phi meson spin alignment that could not be explained by conventional global quark polarization models (Liang and Wang, 2005; Sheng et al., 2020). The rescattering effect studied here provides a possible source for the observed spin alignment signals, particularly for short-lived resonances like K*0 (lifetime ~4 fm/c) that decay before kinetic freeze-out. The results indicate that hadronic rescattering must be accounted for when interpreting spin alignment measurements, and the authors note that a similar study using the AMPT model (Shen, 2021) found different deviations, suggesting sensitivity to the implementation of rescattering processes.
