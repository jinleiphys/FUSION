# Distortion of the HBT image by mean field interaction

> We study effects of a mean-field interaction on the spacetime geometry of the hadron source measured by utilizing the Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) interferometry in the ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show how a modification of a pion amplitude, caused in the freeze-out process, is incorporated into the correlation function of the interferometry within a semiclassical method. Profiles of the distorted images are illustrated. To make a quantitative estimate of the effects, we construct a mean-field-interaction model on the basis of the pion-pion scattering amplitude, and then investigate to what extent the effects of the mean-field interaction acts to efficiently modify the HBT radii.

# Distortion of the HBT image by mean field interaction

## Key claim
Mean-field interactions between emitted pions and the surrounding hadronic medium can distort the HBT correlation function through phase shifts and attenuation, and these effects may partially resolve the "RHIC HBT puzzle" by reducing the discrepancy between hydrodynamical predictions and experimental HBT radii, particularly for the sideward radius at low momentum.

## Method
The author constructs a semiclassical framework incorporating a modified pion amplitude that includes both a real part (phase shift from attractive/repulsive interactions) and an imaginary part (attenuation) of the pion self-energy. The mean-field interaction is modeled using isospin-averaged forward pion-pion scattering amplitudes, with the medium pion distribution evolved via a Boltzmann equation including a source term for pion emission from a thermalized hadron source at freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of the hadron source: T = 130 MeV, pion chemical potential: μ = 30 MeV
- Transverse flow profile: v⊥ = 0.06 r c⁻¹ (proportional to transverse coordinate r)
- Distorted HBT images shown at transverse momentum k⊥ = 100 MeV
- Isotropic Gaussian source profile with variance σ = 5 fm used for illustration of distortion patterns
- The ρ-meson resonance provides strong attraction and attenuation in the momentum regime relevant for pions with average momenta of several hundred MeV

## Context
This work addresses the "RHIC HBT puzzle," where hydrodynamical models that successfully reproduce single-particle spectra and elliptic flow systematically overpredict the HBT radii ratio Rout/Rside compared to experimental data from RHIC (Lisa et al., 2005 review). The author extends prior semiclassical work (Hattori and Matsui, 2009; Hattori, 2010) to a time-dependent framework incorporating realistic pion-pion scattering amplitudes from chiral perturbation theory (Ananthanarayan et al., 2001). Independent investigations of mean-field effects on HBT were also pursued by Cramer et al. (2005) and Pratt (2006). The results are comparable in magnitude to effects obtained with upgraded hydrodynamical modeling (Pratt, 2009), suggesting that a consistent treatment of mean-field interactions throughout the hadron phase could further improve the description.
