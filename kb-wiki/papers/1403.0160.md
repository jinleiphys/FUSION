# Precise comparison of the Gaussian expansion method and the Gamow shell model

> We perform a detailed comparison of results of the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) and the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) supplemented by the complex scaling (CS) method for the same translationally-invariant cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) Hamiltonian. As a benchmark test, we calculate the ground state $0^{+}$ and the first excited state $2^{+}$ of mirror nuclei $^{6}$He and $^{6}$Be in the model space consisting of two valence nucleons in $p$-shell outside of a $^{4}$He core. We find a good overall agreement of results obtained in these two different approaches, also for many-body resonances.

# Precise comparison of the Gaussian expansion method and the Gamow shell model

## Key claim
The Gaussian expansion method supplemented by complex scaling (GEM+CS) and the Gamow shell model (GSM) produce essentially equivalent results for bound states, narrow resonances, and broad resonances in the mirror nuclei ⁶He and ⁶Be when using the same translationally-invariant cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) Hamiltonian, with differences of at most ~10–20 keV that diminish with improved basis convergence.

## Method
The authors solved the ⁴He+2N three-body problem using COSM coordinates with the modified KKNN potential for the core-nucleon interaction and the Minnesota potential for the valence nucleon-nucleon interaction, plus a phenomenological three-body Gaussian term. In the GEM+CS approach, radial basis functions were Gaussian functions with widths in geometric progression, and resonances were treated by rotating coordinates into the complex plane. In the GSM approach, the Berggren ensemble of bound, resonant, and discretized continuum single-particle states was used, with two-body matrix elements computed via harmonic oscillator expansion.

## Key numbers
- Ground state 0⁺ energy of ⁶He at ℓ_max=5: GEM+CS = −0.978 MeV, GSM = −0.977 MeV
- First excited 2⁺ energy of ⁶He at ℓ_max=5: GEM+CS = 0.589 − i0.021 MeV, GSM = 0.577 − i0.024 MeV
- Ground state 0⁺ energy of ⁶Be at ℓ_max=5: GEM+CS = 1.285 − i0.031 MeV, GSM = 1.279 − i0.024 MeV
- First excited 2⁺ energy of ⁶Be at ℓ_max=5: GEM+CS = 2.517 − i0.491 MeV, GSM = 2.495 − i0.505 MeV
- GEM+CS basis: N_max = 22 Gaussian functions per valence nucleon, with b₀ = 0.1 fm and γ = 1.3, giving maximum width b ≈ 25 fm
- GSM continuum discretized with 40 points per partial wave, maximum momentum k_max = 3.5 fm⁻¹
- Difference between GEM+CS and GSM for the 2⁺ state of ⁶He at ℓ_max=1 was ~1 keV at optimal parameters (θ_opt = 13°, b_HO = 2 fm, N = 41 oscillator shells)

## Context
This work provides a direct benchmark between two complementary open quantum system methods for nuclear structure: the GEM+CS approach previously applied to p-shell nuclei (Myo 2010, Myo 2011) and the GSM approach developed for continuum coupling in shell model calculations (Michel 2002). The comparison extends earlier studies of ⁶He and ⁶Be using COSM coordinates (Aoyama 1995, Myo 2001) and validates both methods for describing weakly bound halo states and many-body resonances. The results enable future applications of either method to more exotic nuclei near drip lines, and suggest that the small differences for broad resonances can be reduced by careful optimization of variational parameters in GEM+CS or of the harmonic oscillator expansion in GSM.
