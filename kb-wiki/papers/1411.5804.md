# Nuclear Electric Dipole Moments in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We provide a consistent and complete calculation of the electric dipole moments of the deuteron, helion, and triton in the framework of chiral effective field theory. The CP-conserving and CP-violating interactions are treated on equal footing and we consider CP-violating one-, two-, and three-nucleon operators up to next-to-leading-order in the chiral power counting. In particular, we calculate for the first time EDM contributions induced by the CP-violating three-pion operator. We find that effects of CP-violating nucleon-nucleon contact interactions are larger than those found in previous studies based on phenomenological models for the CP-conserving nucleon-nucleon interactions. Our results which apply to any model of CP violation in the hadronic sector can be used to test various scenarios of CP violation. As examples, we study the implications of our results on the QCD $θ$-term and the minimal left-right symmetric model.

## Key claim

This paper provides a consistent and complete calculation of the electric dipole moments (EDMs) of the deuteron, helion, and triton within chiral effective field theory, treating CP-conserving and CP-violating interactions on equal footing up to next-to-leading order; the results apply to any model of CP violation in the hadronic sector, but the actual numerical predictions depend on seven low-energy constants that must be extracted from future measurements or lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear EDMs by evaluating the CP-violating transition current for each nucleus, including one-, two-, and three-nucleon CP-violating operators up to next-to-leading order in the chiral power counting. CP-conserving wave functions were obtained from the N2LO chiral potential (for five cutoff combinations) and from phenomenological potentials (Av18, CD-Bonn, with their three-nucleon force extensions), solving scattering equations for the deuteron and Faddeev equations for the helion and triton. The EDM contributions from each CP-violating operator were extracted as coefficients multiplying the corresponding low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron EDM: \(d_{^2\mathrm{H}} = d_n + d_p - [(0.183 \pm 0.017)\,g_1 - (0.748 \pm 0.138)\,\Delta]\,e\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- Helion EDM: \(d_{^3\mathrm{He}} = (0.90 \pm 0.01)\,d_n - (0.03 \pm 0.01)\,d_p + \{(0.017 \pm 0.006)\,\Delta - (0.11 \pm 0.01)\,g_0 - (0.14 \pm 0.02)\,g_1 + (0.61 \pm 0.14)\,\Delta + [(0.04 \pm 0.02)C_1 - (0.09 \pm 0.02)C_2]\times\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\}\,e\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- Triton EDM: \(d_{^3\mathrm{H}} = -(0.03 \pm 0.01)\,d_n + (0.92 \pm 0.01)\,d_p + \{(0.017 \pm 0.006)\,\Delta + (0.11 \pm 0.01)\,g_0 - (0.14 \pm 0.02)\,g_1 + (0.60 \pm 0.14)\,\Delta - [(0.04 \pm 0.02)C_1 - (0.09 \pm 0.02)C_2]\times\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\}\,e\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- For the QCD \(\theta\)-term: \(g_0^\theta = (-15.5 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\), \(g_1^\theta = (3.4 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\), \(\Delta^\theta = (-0.37 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\)
- The three-pion \(\Delta\) vertex contribution to the helion and triton EDMs from the three-body potential is approximately \(0.017\,\Delta\,e\,\mathrm{fm}\), while the loop-induced \(g_1\) vertex correction from the same \(\Delta\) vertex is larger by roughly a factor of 50 (about \(0.6\,\Delta\,e\,\mathrm{fm}\))

## Context

This work extends previous EDM calculations for light nuclei (Khriplovich, Pospelov and de Vries, Liu and Timmermans, Stetcu et al., Song et al., Bsaisou et al., de Vries et al.) by providing a fully consistent chiral EFT treatment with well-defined uncertainties and by including for the first time the CP-violating three-pion operator. The results enable systematic tests of CP-violating scenarios such as the QCD \(\theta\)-term and the minimal left-right symmetric model, and can be combined with future lattice QCD calculations of the low-energy constants to disentangle the underlying source of CP violation.
