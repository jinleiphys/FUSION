# New theoretical developments on the early-time dynamics and approach to equilibrium in Heavy-Ion collisions

> We discuss recent theoretical developments in understanding the early pre-equilibrium dynamics and onset of hydrodynamic behavior in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We highlight possible experimental signatures of the pre-equilibrium phase, and present recent progress in developing a consistent theoretical description of collective flow in small systems.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while significant progress has been made in understanding the pre-equilibrium phase of heavy-ion collisions, allowing its inclusion in state-of-the-art simulations, hydrodynamics is not applicable in small collision systems such as p+p or p+Pb because the quark-gluon plasma does not equilibrate to a sufficient degree.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical developments in kinetic equilibration using weak-coupling QCD kinetic theory, strong-coupling AdS/CFT, and the relaxation time approximation (RTA), comparing the evolution of the longitudinal pressure to energy density ratio across these frameworks. The KoMPoST framework is described, which evolves the energy-momentum tensor using non-equilibrium linear response theory and Green's functions calculated in kinetic theory, and has recently been extended to include conserved charges. For small systems, the author discusses systematic studies in kinetic theory within the conformal RTA, where the collective dynamics depends only on the initial spatial geometry and a single dimensionless opacity parameter.

## Key numbers
- The hydrodynamic onset time at LHC energies is estimated as τ_Hydro ≈ 1.1 fm/c (4π η/s / 2)^{3/2} ((sτ)_∞ / 4.1 GeV^2)^{-1/2}, where η/s is the shear-viscosity to entropy density ratio and (sτ)_∞ is the asymptotic entropy per unit rapidity.
- Non-hydrodynamic response dominates below size scales ℓ ≲ 0.16 fm (200 MeV / T) (η/s / 0.16), indicating hydrodynamics is no longer applicable on such small scales.
- For electromagnetic probes, pre-equilibrium photon contributions are sizeable for transverse momenta 3 GeV ≲ p_T ≲ 6 GeV at LHC energies.
- Intermediate mass dileptons show a dominant pre-equilibrium contribution for invariant masses 1.5 GeV ≲ M ≲ 3 GeV.
- In small systems, opacities γ̂ ≳ 3-4 are necessary for hydrodynamics to provide a quantitatively accurate description of collective flow.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for pre-equilibrium dynamics developed by Kurkela (2018) and collaborators, particularly the KoMPoST effective description, and builds on the concept of hydrodynamic attractors introduced by Heller (2015). The paper contrasts the successful application of these methods to large A+A collisions with the inapplicability of hydrodynamics in small systems, as demonstrated by Ambrus (2022) using kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. The findings enable future phenomenological studies of pre-equilibrium signatures via dileptons and photons at LHC energies, and challenge the community to develop non-equilibrium descriptions for small collision systems such as O+O, which are expected to fall into the transition region between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic behavior.
