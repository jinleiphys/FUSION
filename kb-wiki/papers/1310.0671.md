# Self-similarity in jet events following from p-p collisions at LHC

> Using a Tsallis nonextensive approach, we simultaneously analyze recent data obtained by the LHC ATLAS experiment on distributions of transverse momenta of jets, p_T^{jet}, together with distributions of transverse momenta of particles produced within these jets (defined relative to the jet's axis), p_T^{rel}, and their multiplicity distributions, P(N). The respective nonextensivity parameters for distributions of jets, q_{jet}, for distributions of particles in jets, q_{rel} and the global nonextensivity parameter obtained from P(N), q_N, were then compared with nonextensivity parameters q obtained from minimum bias pp collisions at energies corresponding to the energies of these jets. The values of the corresponding nonextensivity parameters were found to be similar, strongly indicating the existence of a common mechanism behind all these processes. We tentatively identify this as a self-similarity property known to be present there and resulting in Tsallis type distributions. If confirmed, this would considerably strengthen the nonextensive Tsallis approach.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the nonextensivity parameters extracted from transverse momentum distributions of jets, particles within jets, and jet multiplicity distributions are similar to each other and to those from minimum bias pp collisions at comparable energies, which they tentatively identify as evidence for a self-similarity property. The hedge is that this is a tentative identification that would "considerably strengthen the nonextensive Tsallis approach" if confirmed, and the authors note large uncertainties in extracting precise fit parameters due to correlations between T and n.

## Method
The authors fitted Tsallis distributions (Eq. 1, with n = 1/(1-q)) to ATLAS data on jet transverse momentum distributions f(p_T^jet), particle transverse momentum distributions within jets f(p_T^rel), and multiplicity distributions P(N) within jets. They extracted the nonextensivity parameters q_jet, q_rel, and q_N from these fits, then compared them with q values from minimum bias pp collisions at similar energies, plotting q as a function of mean multiplicity ⟨N⟩.

## Key numbers
- Jet p_T distribution at √s = 7 TeV fitted with T = 0.45 GeV and n = 7, corresponding to q = 1.14.
- For particles within jets with p_T^jet ≤ 40 GeV (|η| < 1.9, p_T^track > 0.3 GeV), fits used T = 0.18 GeV; n values ranged from -8.5 (q = 0.88) for p_T^jet = 4-6 GeV to 11.5 (q = 1.09) for p_T^jet = 24-40 GeV.
- For particles within jets with p_T^jet > 40 GeV (|η| < 1.2, p_T^track > 0.5 GeV), fits used T = 0.25 GeV; n values ranged from 70 (q = 1.014) for p_T^jet = 25-40 GeV to 7.5 (q = 1.133) for p_T^jet = 400-500 GeV.
- Multiplicity distributions within jets (p_T^jet ≤ 40 GeV) gave q_N - 1 values from -0.11 (⟨N⟩ = 4.41, Var = 2.31) for p_T^jet = 4-6 GeV to 0.097 (⟨N⟩ = 7.80, Var = 18.1) for p_T^jet = 24-40 GeV.
- The inclusive p_T distribution at 7 TeV has q = 1.15, comparable to the jet q = 1.14.

## Context
This work extends the Tsallis nonextensive approach, previously applied to inclusive p_T spectra in pp collisions (reviewed by Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2012), to jet observables from ATLAS (2011, 2012). It builds on earlier analyses of longitudinal momentum distributions in jets by Biro et al. (2012) and on the Hagedorn formula (Hagedorn, 1965) for p_T spectra. The results suggest a common self-similar cascade mechanism for particle production in both jets and minimum bias collisions, connecting to ideas of fireball self-similarity (Hagedorn, 1965) and QCD-based sub-jet cascades. This enables further investigation of whether thermal models (Bugaev, 2013) or multiplicative noise models (Kaniadakis, 2001) provide the underlying physical basis.
