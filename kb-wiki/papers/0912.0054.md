# State of matter for quark stars

> It depends on the state of matter at supra-nuclear density to model pulsar's structure, which is unfortunately not certain due to the difficulties in physics. In cold quark matter at realistic baryon densities of compact stars (with an average value of $\sim 2-3ρ_0$), the interaction between quarks is so strong that they would condensate in position space to form quark-clusters. We argue that quarks in quark stars are grouped in clusters, then we apply two phenomenological models for quark stars, the polytropic model and Lennard-Jones model. Both of the two models have stiffer EoS, and larger maximum mass for quark stars (larger than 2 $M_\odot$). The gravitational energy releases during the AIQ process could explain the observed energy of three supergiant flares from soft gamma-ray repeaters ($\sim 10^{47}$ ergs).

## Key claim

The paper argues that quarks in quark stars are grouped into clusters (rather than forming a Fermi gas), and that two phenomenological models based on this clustering, the polytropic model and the Lennard-Jones model, produce stiffer equations of state and maximum masses larger than 2 solar masses, but the actual state of matter at supra-nuclear density remains uncertain due to fundamental difficulties in physics.

## Method

The author applies two phenomenological models to cold quark matter at baryon densities typical of compact stars (approximately 2-3 times nuclear saturation density). The polytropic model treats quark clusters analogously to nuclei in normal matter, using a polytropic equation of state with non-zero surface density and optionally including QCD vacuum energy. The Lennard-Jones model describes inter-cluster interactions using a Lennard-Jones potential with short-distance repulsion and long-distance attraction, assuming a simple-cubic lattice structure for the clusters. Both models solve the hydrostatic equilibrium equations (including anisotropic pressure in the polytropic case) to compute mass-radius relations and maximum masses.

## Key numbers

- Average baryon density in compact stars is approximately 2-3 times the nuclear saturation density ρ₀.
- For the polytropic model with surface density ρ_s = 1.5 ρ₀, the maximum mass of quark stars decreases as the polytropic index n increases; stable stars have central densities less than approximately 5 ρ₀.
- For the Lennard-Jones model with cluster sizes of N_q = 3 or 18 quarks, and potential depths U_0 = 50 MeV or 100 MeV, the maximum masses exceed 2 M_⊙ (solar masses).
- The gravitational energy released during star quakes (the AIQ process) can reach approximately 10⁴⁷ ergs, matching the observed photon energy of three supergiant flares from soft gamma-ray repeaters.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions that quark matter could be in a solid state (Xu 2003, Horvath 2005, Owen 2005, Mannarelli 2007) and contrasts with the MIT bag model where quarks form a relativistic Fermi gas. The polytropic model is an extension of earlier work by Lai (2008), and the Lennard-Jones model is based on a 2009 paper by the same author. The results enable future observational tests: if massive pulsars with masses above 2 M_⊙ are discovered, they could support the Lennard-Jones quark star model, while the gravitational energy release mechanism provides a possible explanation for soft gamma-ray repeater flares.
