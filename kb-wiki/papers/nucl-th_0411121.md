# Recent results in CBF theory for medium-heavy nuclei

> We extend the correlated basis functions theory (CBF) for nuclei with different number of protons and neutrons and in j-j coupling scheme. By means of the Fermi hypernetted chain integral equations, in conjunction with the single operator chain approximation (FHNC/SOC), we evaluate the ground state and the one-body densities for 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb nuclei. The realistic Argonne V8' two-nucleon potentials has been used. We compare the ground-state properties of these nuclei calculated by using correlation functions with and without tensor components.

## Key claim

The authors extend the correlated basis functions (CBF) theory with Fermi hypernetted chain/single operator chain approximation (FHNC/SOC) to handle nuclei with unequal proton and neutron numbers in a j-j coupling scheme, but caution that the results shown should not be considered fully realistic because no three-body force has been included yet.

## Method

The authors apply the variational principle with a many-body wave function built from a Slater determinant multiplied by a product of two-body correlation operators. They evaluate the ground-state energy and one-body densities for 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb using the FHNC integral equations with the SOC approximation, employing the realistic Argonne V8' two-nucleon potential. Calculations are performed both with correlation functions including tensor components (f6 model) and without them (f4 model), with the two spin-orbit channels of the potential treated by perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca: -11.56 MeV (f4 model) and -6.73 MeV (f6 model), compared to experimental -8.55 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 48Ca: -10.99 MeV (f4 model) and -7.15 MeV (f6 model), compared to experimental -8.66 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 208Pb: -12.97 MeV (f4 model) and -6.87 MeV (f6 model), compared to experimental -7.80 MeV
- Kinetic energy per nucleon for 40Ca: 38.61 MeV (f4) and 38.18 MeV (f6)
- Kinetic energy per nucleon for 48Ca: 36.36 MeV (f4) and 36.21 MeV (f6)
- Kinetic energy per nucleon for 208Pb: 36.93 MeV (f4) and 36.21 MeV (f6)
- Nuclear interaction energy per nucleon for 40Ca: -51.81 MeV (f4) and -47.11 MeV (f6)
- Nuclear interaction energy per nucleon for 48Ca: -49.29 MeV (f4) and -45.29 MeV (f6)
- Nuclear interaction energy per nucleon for 208Pb: -54.17 MeV (f4) and -47.63 MeV (f6)
- Coulomb energy per proton for 40Ca: 3.78 MeV (f4) and 3.92 MeV (f6)
- Coulomb energy per proton for 48Ca: 2.55 MeV (f4) and 2.67 MeV (f6)
- Coulomb energy per proton for 208Pb: 9.58 MeV (f4) and 10.45 MeV (f6)
- Scalar correlation functions heal at 1.4 fm for all three nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous CBF calculations by Co (1992), Co (1994), Arias de Saavedra (1996), Fabrocini (1998), and Fabrocini (2000) that were limited to 16O and 40Ca in ls coupling with identical single-particle wave functions for protons and neutrons. The new formalism enables microscopic calculations for medium-heavy nuclei with neutron excess, including what the authors state is the first microscopic calculation for 208Pb. The next step is to include a three-body interaction, which the authors report is already well advanced.
