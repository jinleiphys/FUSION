# Magnetic flux loop in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We consider the expectation value of a magnetic flux loop in the immediate forward light cone of collisions of heavy nuclei at high energies. Such collisions are characterized by a non-linear scale Q_s where color fields become strong. We find that loops of area greater than ~2/Q_s^2 exhibit area law behavior, which determines the scale of elementary flux excitations ("vortices"). We also estimate the magnetic string tension, sigma_M = 0.12 Q_s^2. By the time t ~ 1/Q_s even small loops satisfy an area law. We describe corrections to the propagator of semi-hard gluons at very early times in the background of fluctuating magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper finds that magnetic flux loops in the immediate forward light cone of high-energy heavy-ion collisions exhibit area law behavior for loops larger than about \(2/Q_s^2\), which determines the scale of elementary flux excitations ("vortices"); this area law is observed at time \(\tau = +0\) only for sufficiently large loops, but by time \(t \sim 1/Q_s\) even small loops satisfy area law scaling.

## Method

The authors compute the expectation value of a spatial chromo-magnetic Wilson loop in the transverse plane using classical Yang-Mills fields obtained from the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, with random color charge densities for projectile and target. They perform numerical lattice simulations for SU(2) Yang-Mills on a \(4096^2\) lattice, solving the classical field equations at proper time \(\tau = +0\) and at later times, and compare the full SU(2) loop to its Z(2) projection. They also derive analytic perturbative expansions for small loops and a combinatorial model for vortex density.

## Key numbers

- Loops of area greater than \(\sim 2/Q_s^2\) exhibit area law behavior at \(\tau = +0\).
- Magnetic string tension: \(\sigma_M = 0.12 \, Q_s^2\) (with uncertainty \(\pm 0.01\) from the fit).
- Vortex radius: \(R_{\rm vtx} \sim 0.8/Q_s\).
- Probability of finding a vortex within area \(1/Q_s^2\): \(p \simeq 1/20\).
- For asymmetric collisions, \(\sigma_M \sim Q_{s1} Q_{s2}\) (proportional to product of single powers of the respective saturation scales).
- At time \(\tau \sim 1/Q_s\), area law behavior is satisfied even for small loops.
- Adjoint magnetic string tension is about two times larger than the fundamental representation value.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework for heavy-ion collisions, specifically the classical Yang-Mills solution of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) and the magnetic field correlations of Fries, Kapusta, and Li (2006) and Lappi and McLerran. It demonstrates that the longitudinal chromo-magnetic fields produced immediately after a collision form Z(N) vortex-like structures with a finite screening length, as previously indicated by the screening scale of \(1/(5 Q_s)\) from earlier work. The results enable studies of semi-hard gluon propagation at very early times, with corrections to the free propagator given by \(\sigma_M m/p_T\), and motivate future investigations of corrections from higher-order terms in the effective action and quantum fluctuations, as well as the transition to thermal QCD behavior at later times.
