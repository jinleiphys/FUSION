# Four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation of polarization transfer coefficient $\bm{K}_y^{y'}$ in the $^2$H$(\vec{\bm{d}}, \vec{\bm{p}})^3$H reaction at very low energies

> The polarization transfer coefficient $K_y^{y'}$ for the $^2$H$(\vec{d}, \vec{p})^3$H reaction at energies $E_{\rm lab.} = 30$, 50, 70, and 90keV is calculated using the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation. We employ a separable type of the Paris potential. The experimental data [Phys. Rev. {\bf C64}, 047601 (2001)] agrees with our solution at the scattering angle $θ_{\rm c.m.}=0^\circ$, and there is no discrepancy pointed out in that paper. We found that the $K_y^{y'}$ is sensitive to the incident deuteron energy at this angle.

## Key claim

The four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation of the polarization transfer coefficient \(K_y^{y'}\) for the \(^2\)H\((\vec{d}, \vec{p})^3\)H reaction at very low energies (30–90 keV) agrees with the experimental data at \(\theta_{\rm c.m.}=0^\circ\), resolving a previously claimed discrepancy; however, the agreement is qualified by the fact that the calculation includes an approximate treatment of the Coulomb force and uses a separable expansion of the Paris potential.

## Method

The four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equation was solved in momentum space using a separable representation of the Paris potential (PEST) for two-body states including \(^1\)S\(_0\), \(^3\)S\(_1\)-\(^3\)D\(_1\), \(^3\)P\(_0\), \(^1\)P\(_1\), \(^3\)P\(_1\), and \(^3\)P\(_2\). The [3+1] and [2+2] subamplitudes were expanded via the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method with a modification to handle repulsive and multi-rank separable potentials. The Coulomb force was approximated by multiplying the Coulomb-free reaction amplitudes by damping factors and phase-shift corrections derived from the ratio of Coulomb to plane waves at short distances.

## Key numbers

- \(K_y^{y'}\) calculated at \(E_{\rm lab.} = 30\), 50, 70, and 90 keV for the \(^2\)H\((\vec{d}, \vec{p})^3\)H reaction.
- Experimental data from Katabuchi et al. (2001) gave \(K_y^{y'} = 0.09 \pm 0.10\) at 90 keV with an acceptance angle of \(\pm 6.5^\circ\) around \(0^\circ\) in the laboratory system.
- The difference between the present results and approximated ones including only \(^1\)S\(_0\) and \(^3\)S\(_1\)-\(^3\)D\(_1\) states in the two-body subsystem is about 2\%.
- The Sommerfeld parameter for the initial channel is \(\eta = 0.1581/\sqrt{E_i}\) and for the final channel \(\eta' = 0.1369/\sqrt{E_f}\), where \(E_i\) and \(E_f\) are center-of-mass energies in keV.

## Context

This work extends previous four-body FY calculations by Uzu (2000–2002) that used the Yamaguchi potential, now employing the more realistic Paris potential (PEST). It directly addresses the experimental measurement by Katabuchi et al. (2001), who found a 1.5 standard deviation discrepancy between their data and calculations using the amplitude parameterization of Lemaître and Schieck (1995). The results show that the energy dependence of \(K_y^{y'}\) at forward angles arises primarily from the Coulomb modification of S- and P-wave mixing, contrary to the assumption in Katabuchi et al. (2001) that energy dependence comes only from the Coulomb penetrability. This enables future measurements at \(\theta_{\rm c.m.} \approx 60^\circ\)–\(90^\circ\) where peaks are predicted, and further investigation of the suppression ratio for polarized fusion reactions.
