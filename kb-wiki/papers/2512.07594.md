# Gamow shell model predictions for six-proton unbound nucleus $^{20}$Si

> Proton-rich nuclei beyond the proton drip line are of great interest in nuclear structure physics, due to exotic phenomena such as proton emissions and the Thomas-Ehrman shift (TES). In this work, we employ the Gamow shell model (GSM) to investigate the structure and decay of $^{20}$Si, a candidate for six-proton (6$p$) emission, which can be produced via two-neutron knockout from the drip line nucleus $^{22}$Si. We predict that its ground state decays via $6p$ emission to the ground state of $^{14}$O, with a decay energy $E_{6p} = 10.125$ MeV and a width of 371~keV. A $2^+$ state is predicted at 1.7 MeV, comparable with that in $^{18}$Mg, indicating the disappearance of the $Z=14$ magic number in $^{20}$Si. Instead, analyses of the many-body configurations and the average occupancies of the mirror states suggest the presence of $dynamic$ TES in low-lying states of $^{19}$Al/$^{19}$C and $^{20}$Si/$^{20}$C. Further evidence is provided by analyzing the contributions of different components of the GSM Hamiltonian. Moreover, this study offers the first theoretical description of $^{20}$Si and guidance for future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the ground state of the six-proton unbound nucleus $^{20}$Si decays via six-proton emission to the ground state of $^{14}$O, but the authors note that the quantitative results may be influenced by the limitation of a valence space restricted to protons alone, which may miss non-negligible configurations requiring explicit proton-neutron degrees of freedom for state-bearing deformation.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM), a multiconfiguration interaction framework using a core plus valence nucleon picture with a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonance, and complex-energy scattering states. The GSM Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the Jacobi-Davidson method with 2p-2h truncation on a natural orbital basis, using a $^{14}$O core for carbon isotones and a $^{14}$C core for carbon isotopes, with $spdf$ partial waves in the valence space. The residual internucleon interaction was based on the two-body part of pionless effective field theory at next-to-leading order, with Coulomb interaction explicitly included for valence protons.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of $^{20}$Si: six-proton decay energy $E_{6p} = 10.152$ MeV and decay width $\Gamma = 371$ keV
- First $2^+$ state in $^{20}$Si predicted at excitation energy 1.7 MeV
- For $^{20}$Si/$^{20}$C mirror pair, the $4^+_1$ excitation energies of $^{20}$Si are lower than those of $^{20}$C by approximately 800 keV
- Ground state energy shifts of approximately 1.4 MeV in $^{20}$Si when varying the leading-order parameter of the EFT interaction by 2%
- For $^{19}$Al/$^{19}$C mirror pair, all three lowest mirror states exhibit $\Delta E$ values exceeding 2 MeV
- For $^{20}$Si/$^{20}$C mirror pair, both $0^+_1$ and $2^+_1$ mirror states exhibit $\Delta E$ values exceeding 6 MeV
- Coulomb contribution to the energy difference in the $2^+_1$ states of $^{20}$Si/$^{20}$C exceeds that in the $0^+_1$ states by approximately 545 keV

## Context

This work extends previous GSM studies of proton-rich nuclei, including the authors' prior work on $^{16}$Ne/$^{16}$C and $^{18}$Mg/$^{18}$C mirror pairs (PhysRevC.111.014302), and builds on experimental observations of multiproton emitters such as the $4p$ emitter $^{18}$Mg (PhysRevLett.127.262502) and the $5p$ emitter $^{9}$N (PhysRevLett.131.172501). The predictions for $^{20}$Si provide the first theoretical description of this nucleus and offer guidance for future experiments, particularly since $^{22}$Si has recently been identified as a bound nucleus fixing the proton dripline location for silicon.
