# Phases and properties of quark matter

> I review recent developments in finite temperature lattice QCD which are useful for the study of heavy-ion collisions. I pay particular attention to studies of the equation of state and the light they throw on conformal symmetry and the large N_c limit, and to the structure of the phase diagram for N_f=2+1.

# Phases and properties of quark matter

## Key claim
The finite-temperature QCD transition is a crossover, but the exact crossover temperature remains in dispute between collaborations; the Budapest-Wuppertal group finds a substantially smaller value than the BBRC collaboration using the same thermometer, though this disagreement does not impact experimental searches.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice QCD computations at finite temperature for the equation of state and phase diagram, focusing on results from the Budapest-Wuppertal (BW) collaboration using staggered quarks with physical quark masses, the BBRC collaboration using P4 quarks with heavier light quarks (pion mass around 220 MeV) and a physical strange quark, and the HotQCD collaboration using both P4 and Asqtad quarks. For the phase diagram, the author applies Gibbs' phase rule and universality arguments to constrain possible topologies of tricritical and tetracritical surfaces in the 4-dimensional phase diagram of QCD as a function of temperature, number of flavours, quark masses, and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers
- Crossover temperature from a 2001 global analysis: T_c approximately 175 MeV with 20 MeV uncertainty from scale setting
- Energy density in the hadron phase at T=140 MeV: E_1 = 70 +/- 12 MeV/fm^3 (BBRC, 1/a=840 MeV) or 21 +/- 24 MeV/fm^3 (BW, 1/a=840 MeV)
- Energy density in the plasma phase at T=210 MeV: E_2 = 3.11 +/- 0.07 GeV/fm^3 (BBRC, 1/a=1260 MeV) or 2.28 +/- 0.08 GeV/fm^3 (BW, 1/a=1260 MeV)
- Continuum limit quenched QCD energy density at T_c=285 MeV: E = 3.4 +/- 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- HotQCD results at a=1/8T reduce the maximum excursion from the conformal line by about 20% compared to P4 quarks at coarser lattice spacing
- BBRC collaboration uses m_pi approximately 220 MeV; BW collaboration uses physical m_pi and m_K

## Context
This review extends the author's earlier work on QCD phase diagrams and lattice thermodynamics, summarizing results from the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration (BW, 2006-2008), BBRC collaboration (2007-2008), and HotQCD collaboration. The paper discusses how the equation of state shows strong violation of conformal symmetry up to T=2T_c, which has implications for hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions that must include bulk viscosity. The phase diagram analysis builds on the Columbia plot and arguments from de Forcrand and Philipsen (2007) about the curvature of Ising critical surfaces, and the author identifies three possible topologies for the tricritical line that must be resolved by further lattice simulations.
