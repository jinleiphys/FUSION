# Role of triaxiality in the ground state shape of neutron rich Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes

> The evolution of the ground-state shape along the triaxial landscape of several isotopes of Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt is analyzed using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation. Two well reputed interactions (Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy4) have been used in the study in order to asses to which extent the results are independent of the details of the effective interaction. A large number of even-even nuclei, with neutron numbers from N=110 up to N=122 has been considered, covering in this way a vast extension of the nuclear landscape where signatures of oblate-prolate shape transitions have already manifested both theoretically and experimentally.

## Key claim

The prolate-to-oblate shape transition in neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes proceeds through triaxial ground states, but the triaxial minima are shallow (typically less than 0.5 MeV deep) and their precise character depends on the effective interaction used, making dynamical treatment of the \(\gamma\) degree of freedom necessary for quantitative conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with constrained quadrupole operators \(Q_{20}\) and \(Q_{22}\) to map potential energy surfaces in the \(Q_0\)-\(\gamma\) plane for even-even isotopes with neutron numbers N=110 to N=122. Two effective interactions were compared: the Gogny D1S force (with D1N for cross-check) and the Skyrme SLy4 functional with a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction. Axial and triaxial single-particle energy diagrams were also computed to identify the shell-structure origins of the shape transitions.

## Key numbers

- The triaxial minimum in \(^{190}\)W lies at \(\gamma \approx 30^\circ\) with a depth of approximately 300 keV for D1S, 100 keV for D1N, and 250 keV for SLy4.
- The prolate-to-oblate transition occurs at N=116 across the isotopic chains; for example, \(^{186}\)Yb (N=116) shows a sharp transition, while Hf, W, and Os isotopes near N=116 exhibit triaxial ground states.
- Ground-state \(\beta\) deformation values from D1S range from 0.28 (Yb, N=110) down to 0.09 (Pt, N=122), with \(\gamma\) values increasing from 0° (prolate) to 60° (oblate) as neutron number increases.
- Triaxial minima with SLy4 are typically around 0.8 MeV deeper than with D1S for nuclei with N > 116, reaching depths of about 1.25 MeV in \(^{192}\)W and \(^{194}\)Os.

## Context

This work extends prior mean-field studies of shape transitions in the A≈190 region, such as those by Bengtsson (1987), Nazarewicz (1990), and Wood (1992), by systematically comparing two widely used effective interactions (Gogny D1S and Skyrme SLy4) across a broad isotopic range. The results confirm the prolate-oblate transition and triaxial tendencies predicted by earlier calculations (Naik 2004, Stevenson 2005, Fossion 2006) but highlight that the shallow triaxial minima require beyond-mean-field dynamical treatments, such as the five-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian approach recently applied by Li (2009) in other regions. The findings enable future studies of collective spectra and shape coexistence in neutron-rich nuclei near N=126 and Z=82.
