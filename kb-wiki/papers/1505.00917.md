# Neutrino-production of a charmed meson and the transverse spin structure of the nucleon

> We calculate the amplitude for exclusive neutrino production of a charmed meson on an unpolarized target, in the colinear QCD approach where generalized parton distributions (GPDs) factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. We demonstrate that the transversity chiral odd GPDs contribute to the transverse cross section if the hard amplitude is calculated up to order mc/Q. We show how to access these GPDs through the azimuthal dependence of the nu N -> mu D N differential cross section.

## Key claim

The transverse amplitude for exclusive neutrino production of a charmed meson on an unpolarized target receives its leading contribution from chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) when the hard amplitude is calculated up to order \(m_c/Q\), and these GPDs can be accessed through the azimuthal dependence of the differential cross section, provided the kinematic factors \(\xi\), \(m_c/Q\), and \(\Delta_T/m_N\) are not too small.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for the exclusive reactions \(\nu_l N \to l^- D^+ N'\) and \(\bar\nu_l N \to l^+ D^- N'\) in the collinear QCD factorization framework, where nucleon GPDs and the \(D\)-meson distribution amplitude factorize from perturbatively calculable coefficient functions. The longitudinal amplitude is computed from chiral-even GPDs at leading twist, while the transverse amplitude is obtained by including the heavy quark mass \(m_c\) in the hard scattering coefficient function, which allows chiral-odd transversity GPDs to contribute. The azimuthal dependence of the cross section is then expressed in terms of longitudinal, transverse, and interference cross sections \(\sigma_{00}\), \(\sigma_{--}\), and \(\sigma_{-0}\), and the observables \(\langle\cos\varphi\rangle\) and \(\langle\sin\varphi\rangle\) are derived.

## Key numbers

* \(f_D = 0.223\) GeV: the decay constant of the \(D\)-meson, used in the definition of its distribution amplitude.
* The factor \(K\) in the approximate expressions for \(\langle\cos\varphi\rangle\) and \(\langle\sin\varphi\rangle\) is estimated as \(K = O(0.3/\sqrt{\epsilon})\) for \(Q\) in the range of 2–3 GeV and \(\Delta_T/m_N \approx 0.5\).
* The transverse cross section \(\sigma_{--}\) is proportional to \(m_c^2/Q^4\), while the longitudinal cross section \(\sigma_{00}\) is proportional to \(1/Q^2\); the interference cross section \(\sigma_{-0}\) is proportional to \(m_c/Q^3\).
* The CKM matrix element \(V_{dc}\) and the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\) appear in the overall factor \(C = \frac{8\pi}{9} \alpha_s V_{dc}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of transversity GPDs, which have proven difficult to access due to their chiral-odd nature, from virtual photon mediated processes (where access is likely out of reach of present accelerators, as noted in prior work such as the review by Barone (2002) and the proposal for pion electroproduction) to neutrino-induced reactions. It builds on the factorization formalism for exclusive meson leptoproduction established by Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1997) and on previous calculations of neutrino production of pseudoscalar mesons by Kopeliovich, Schmidt, and Siddikov (2012). The results suggest that planned high-energy neutrino facilities, such as NOvA, could enable measurements of transversity GPDs without major additional equipment, though a dedicated feasibility study is needed.
