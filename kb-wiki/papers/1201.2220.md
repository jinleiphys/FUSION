# $π^0\toγ^*γ$ transition form factor within Light Front Quark Model

> We study the transition form factor of $π^0\toγ^* γ$ as a function of the momentum transfer $Q^2$ within the light-front quark model (LFQM). We compare our result with the experimental data by BaBar as well as other calculations based on the LFQM in the literature. We show that our predicted form factor fits well with the experimental data, particularly those at the large $Q^2$ region.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their light-front quark model (LFQM) calculation, using a Gaussian-type wave function with a broader pion distribution amplitude than previous LFQM studies, fits the BaBar experimental data for the $\pi^0 \to \gamma^* \gamma$ transition form factor well, particularly at large $Q^2$ up to 40 GeV$^2$, but they note that the single BaBar data point at $Q^2 = 27.31$ GeV$^2$ cannot be explained within their framework.

## Method

The authors compute the $\pi^0 \to \gamma^* \gamma$ transition form factor $F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2)$ in the space-like region using the light-front quark model with a phenomenological Gaussian-type wave function for the pion. The quark masses ($m_u = m_d = 0.24$ GeV) and the pion scale parameter ($\omega_\pi = 0.31$ GeV) are constrained by fitting the pion decay constant $f_{\pi^0} = 130$ MeV and the branching ratio $\mathcal{B}(\pi^0 \to 2\gamma) = (98.832 \pm 0.034)\%$ from the Particle Data Group. The form factor is evaluated by integrating over the longitudinal momentum fraction and transverse momentum, including both valence and non-valence quark contributions, and the result is parametrized with a double-pole form.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: $f_{\pi^0} = 130$ MeV, used to constrain model parameters.
* Branching ratio: $\mathcal{B}(\pi^0 \to 2\gamma) = (98.832 \pm 0.034)\%$, used to constrain model parameters.
* Quark masses: $m_u = m_d = 0.24$ GeV, used in the numerical calculation.
* Pion scale parameter: $\omega_\pi = 0.31$ GeV, used in the Gaussian wave function.
* Form factor at zero momentum transfer: $F_{\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma}(0,0) = 0.274$ GeV$^{-1}$, extracted from the branching ratio.
* Double-pole fit parameters: $\alpha = 0.325$, $\beta = 1.15$, and dimensionless $M = 3.6$, for the form factor $F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2) = F_{\pi\to \gamma\gamma}(0,0) / [M + (\beta Q)^2 - (\alpha Q)^4]$.
* BaBar fit parameters for the data: $Q^2 |F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2)| = A (Q^2 / 10 \text{ GeV}^2)^\beta$ with $A = 0.182 \pm 0.002$ GeV and $\beta = 0.25 \pm 0.02$, for $Q^2$ up to 40 GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous LFQM calculations by Hwang (year not given in text), which only covered $Q^2$ up to 8 GeV$^2$, and addresses the failure of an earlier LFQM study by LF-MF (year not given in text) to explain the BaBar data. The authors compare their results with experimental data from BaBar, CLEO (year not given in text), and CELLO (year not given in text), and note that their broader pion distribution amplitude yields results nearly identical to those from the Regge model and the double logarithmic behavior from chiral anomaly effects. The study enables further theoretical investigation of the large-$Q^2$ behavior of the pion transition form factor, but the authors caution that future experiments may rule out their parameter choices if they confirm the QCD asymptotic limit instead of the BaBar data.
