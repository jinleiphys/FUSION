# Toward an understanding of short distance repulsions among baryons in QCD -- NBS wave functions and operator product expansion --

> We report on our recent attempts to determine the short distance behaviors of general 2-baryon and 3-baryon forces, which are defined from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter(NBS) wave function, by using the operator product expansion and a renormalization group analysis in QCD. We have found that the repulsion at short distance increases as the number of valence quarks increases or when the number of different flavors involved decreases. This global tendency suggests a Pauli suppression principle among quark fields at work.

## Key claim

The short-distance repulsion in baryon-baryon and three-baryon forces, defined from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, increases with the number of valence quarks and decreases with the number of different flavors involved, suggesting a Pauli suppression principle among quark fields; however, the sign of the repulsion in the two-nucleon case depends on the non-perturbative ratio of matrix elements of leading and subleading operators, which is not determined by the operator product expansion alone.

## Method

The authors define baryon-baryon and three-baryon potentials from equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions in QCD, following the same scheme used in lattice QCD simulations. They apply the operator product expansion to the product of two or three baryon operators at short distances, then use a renormalization group analysis with perturbatively computed one-loop anomalous dimensions to determine the leading asymptotic behavior of the potentials. The spectrum of anomalous dimensions is obtained by constructing gauge-invariant local 6-quark and 9-quark operators, imposing gauge constraints, and diagonalizing the one-loop mixing matrices.

## Key numbers

- For NN potentials with two flavors, the subleading anomalous dimensions are gamma^{(0,1)} = 24d and gamma^{(1,0)} = 40d in the spin-isospin channels (S,I) = (0,1) and (1,0) respectively, where d = 1/(96 pi^2)
- For BB potentials with three flavors, the largest anomalous dimension above 2 gamma_{B0} is 42 (in units of 2d) for the flavor singlet channel with Dirac structure 112334
- For 3N potentials with two flavors, the maximum anomalous dimension is gamma_{A0} = 16d, giving beta_A = (16d - 3 gamma_{B0})/(2 beta_0) < 0
- For 3B potentials with three flavors, the largest anomalous dimension is 48 (in units of 2d) for the octet channel with Dirac structure 111223344, giving beta_{3B}^{max} = 2/9
- The 2B contribution to the same 3B configuration has beta_{BB}^{max} = 1/3 for the singlet channel with Dirac structure 112334

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of NN potentials (Aoki 2010), BB potentials (Aoki 2010), and 3N forces (Aoki 2011) to the general three-baryon case, using the same NBS wave function approach that has been employed in lattice QCD simulations by Ishii (2006), Aoki (2008, 2009), Inoue (2010), and Doi (2011). The results provide short-distance boundary conditions for baryonic potentials from lattice QCD and suggest that the universal repulsion in most three-baryon channels may help explain the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars, as argued by Nishizaki (2002).
