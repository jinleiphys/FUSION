# Influence of neutron-skin thickness on $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in Pb+Pb collisions

> Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU11 using as an input nucleon density profiles from Hartree-Fock calculations based on a modified Skyrme-like (MSL) model, we study the influence of the uncertainty of the neutron skin thickness on the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in both central and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 400 MeV/nucleon and 1000 MeV/nucleon. Within the current experimental uncertainty range of neutron skin in $^{208}$Pb, while the neutron skin effect on the \rpi ratio is negligible in central reactions at both energies, it increases gradually with increasing impact parameter and becomes comparable with or even larger than the symmetry energy effect in peripheral collisions especially at 400 MeV/nucleon. Moreover, we found that while the \rpi ratio is larger with a softer \esym in central collisions, above certain impact parameters depending on the size of the neutron skin, a stiffer \esym can lead to a larger \rpi ratio as most of the pions are produced at densities below the saturation density in these peripheral reactions. Thus, a clear impact parameter selection is important to extract reliable information about the \esym at suprasaturation densities (size of neutron skin) from the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in central (peripheral) heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Within the current experimental uncertainty range of neutron skin in 208Pb (0.11±0.06 fm to 0.33+0.16-0.18 fm), the neutron skin effect on the π−/π+ ratio is negligible in central collisions at both 400 and 1000 MeV/nucleon, but becomes comparable with or even larger than the symmetry energy effect in peripheral collisions, especially at 400 MeV/nucleon.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU11 with nucleon density profiles from Hartree-Fock calculations based on a modified Skyrme-like (MSL) model. They simulated 208Pb+208Pb collisions at beam energies of 400 and 1000 MeV/nucleon across impact parameters from central (5 fm) to peripheral (11 fm), using two symmetry energy parametrizations (x=1 for softer, x=0 for stiffer) and two neutron skin thicknesses (0.1 and 0.3 fm). They computed the (π−/π+)like ratio, which accounts for both free pions and Δ resonances, and quantified the relative sensitivity to symmetry energy versus neutron skin using the measures F(L2) and F(S).

## Key numbers

* Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb ranges experimentally from 0.11±0.06 fm (π+-Pb scattering) to 0.33+0.16-0.18 fm (PREX-I experiment).
* The softer symmetry energy (x=1) has slope parameter L1=16.4 MeV and L2=-100.3 MeV; the stiffer (x=0) has L1=62.1 MeV and L2=85.6 MeV.
* The symmetry energy effect measure F(L2) on the midrapidity π−/π+ ratio at b=5 fm and 400 MeV/nucleon is 13.7×10−2 for S=0.10 fm and 12.2×10−2 for S=0.30 fm.
* The neutron skin effect measure F(S) on the midrapidity π−/π+ ratio at b=11 fm and 400 MeV/nucleon is 41.8×10−2 for x=0 and 33.4×10−2 for x=1.
* At 1000 MeV/nucleon and b=11 fm, F(S) is 20.6×10−2 for x=0 and 18.5×10−2 for x=1.
* The symmetry energy effect F(L2) at b=5 fm and 1000 MeV/nucleon is 4.0×10−2 for S=0.10 fm and 3.8×10−2 for S=0.30 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bao (2002) and others that identified the π−/π+ ratio as a probe of the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities, and by Brown (2000), Typel (2001), Horowitz (2001), and Furnstahl (2002) that linked neutron skin thickness to the symmetry energy around 0.1 fm−3. The study addresses the interplay between these two observables, showing that central collisions probe high-density symmetry energy with minimal neutron skin contamination, while peripheral collisions are sensitive to neutron skin thickness. This enables future experiments like CREX and PREX-II at JLab to provide more accurate neutron skin values, and informs experimental design for heavy-ion collision measurements of the symmetry energy.
