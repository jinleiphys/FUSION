# Three-Nucleon Photodisintegration of 3He

> The three-nucleon photodisintegration of 3He has been calculated in the whole phase space using consistent Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound and scattering states. Modern nucleon-nucleon and 3N forces have been applied as well as different approaches to nuclear currents. Phase space regions are localized where 3N force effects are especially large. In addition semi-exclusive cross sections for 3He(gamma,N) have been predicted which carry interesting peak structures. Finally some data for the exclusive 3N breakup process of 3He and its total breakup cross section have been compared to theory.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that three-nucleon force (3NF) effects in the exclusive three-nucleon photodisintegration of 3He can reach up to 85% in certain phase-space regions at a photon energy of 120 MeV, but these effects are strongly dependent on the specific kinematic configuration, the choice of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and 3N force models, and the treatment of meson-exchange currents (MEC). The authors caution that the results are based on present-day 3NF models and that explicit 3N electromagnetic current operators have not yet been included.

## Method

The authors solved rigorous Faddeev equations in momentum space for the three-nucleon bound and scattering states, using a partial wave decomposition. They employed the high-precision NN potentials AV18 and CD Bonn, combined with the Urbana IX and modified Tucson-Melbourne (TM') three-nucleon forces, respectively. Nuclear currents were treated either via explicit pi- and rho-like meson-exchange currents consistent with AV18, or via the Siegert approximation without long-wavelength approximation; the fivefold differential cross section for exclusive breakup and the threefold differential cross section for semi-exclusive processes were then computed across the full phase space for photon laboratory energies of 12, 40, and 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- 3NF effects in the exclusive fivefold differential cross section reach up to 85% at E_gamma = 120 MeV for the AV18+Urbana IX force combination with the Siegert approach, for angular configurations near Theta_1 = Theta_2 approx 40 degrees, Phi_12 approx 20 degrees, and energies E_1 approx 20 MeV, E_2 approx 20 MeV.
- At E_gamma = 12 MeV, 3NF effects are as large as 50% for the same force and current combination.
- At E_gamma = 40 MeV, 3NF effects are significantly smaller than at 12 or 120 MeV, with the authors noting a similar vanishing of effects around 30 MeV observed in their prior work on pd breakup.
- Using only a single-nucleon current operator instead of MEC leads to errors exceeding 100% in most phase-space regions at higher energies, and errors of about 50% even at 12 MeV.
- The difference between the Siegert approach and explicit MEC predictions stays below about 20% in most phase-space regions at 12 MeV, but grows larger at 40 and 120 MeV.
- For the semi-exclusive process 3He(gamma,p) at E_gamma = 120 MeV and Theta_p = 60 degrees, a pp final-state interaction peak appears at E_p = 20 MeV; for 3He(gamma,n) at Theta_n = 0 degrees, a pronounced peak appears around 74 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior Faddeev-based study of two-body photodisintegration of 3He (Skibinski et al., 2002) to the full three-body breakup channel, and complements a benchmark study by Golak et al. (2002) that compared Faddeev and hyperspherical harmonic methods for total photodisintegration cross sections. The calculations use modern NN and 3N forces (AV18, CD Bonn, Urbana IX, TM') and current operators, building on earlier pioneering Faddeev calculations by Gibson and Lehman (1976) that used S-wave separable potentials. The results enable future experiments to target specific kinematic regions where 3NF effects are maximized, and they highlight the need for improved treatment of three-nucleon electromagnetic currents and for precise new data to discriminate between force and current models.
