# SU(3) parity doubling in cold neutron star matter

> We present a phenomenological model to investigate the chiral phase transition characterized by parity doubling in dense, beta equilibrated, cold matter. Our model incorporates effective interactions constrained by SU(3) relations and considers baryonic degrees of freedom. By constraining the model with astrophysical data and nuclear matter properties, we find a first-order phase transition within realistic values of the slope parameter L. The inclusion of the baryon octet and negative parity partners, along with a chiral-invariant mass $m_{0}$, allows for a non-massless chiral symmetric phase. Through exploration of parameter space, we identify parameter sets satisfying mass and radius constraints without requiring a partonic phase. The appearance of the parity partner of the nucleon, the N(1535) resonance, suppresses strangeness, pushing hyperonization to higher densities. We observe a mild first-order phase transition to the chirally restored phase, governed by $m_{0}$. Our calculations of surface tension highlight its strong dependence on $m_{0}$. The existence of mixed phases is ruled out since they become energetically too costly. We compare stars with metastable and stable cores using both branches of the equation of state. Despite limited lifespans due to low surface tension values, phase conversion and star contraction could impact neutron stars with masses around 1.3 solar masses or more. We discuss some applications of this model in its non-zero temperatures generalization and scenarios beyond beta equilibrium that can provide insights into core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron star evolution, and neutron star mergers. Core-collapse supernovae dynamics, influenced by chiral symmetry restoration and exotic hadronic states, affect explosion mechanisms and nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a first-order chiral phase transition characterized by parity doubling can occur in cold neutron star cores for realistic values of the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter L, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the chiral-invariant mass parameter m0 and the effective nucleon mass at saturation M0, with a crossover transition possible only for the highest values of these parameters and becoming incompatible with astrophysical constraints for L below 70 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological SU(3) parity doublet model that includes the full baryon octet and their negative parity partners, with effective interactions constrained by SU(3) relations and a chiral-invariant mass m0. They fix parameters to reproduce vacuum baryon masses, nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy, saturation density, symmetry energy, incompressibility, effective mass), hyperon potential depths, and astrophysical constraints from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. The equation of state is obtained by solving mean-field stationary equations for homogeneous meson condensates under beta equilibrium and local charge neutrality, then used as input for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}; binding energy: EB = 16 MeV; symmetry energy: S = 30 MeV; incompressibility: K = 260 MeV
- Slope parameter L explored in range 65-80 MeV, with L = 70 MeV used for detailed parameter sets
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation M0 varied between 0.55-0.75 times the vacuum nucleon mass (939 MeV)
- Chiral-invariant mass m0 values explored: 410 MeV and 460 MeV in the four sample parameter sets
- Critical chemical potentials for first-order phase transition: 1047.4-1097.1 MeV across the four sample sets
- Strangeness onset chemical potentials: 1402.3-1421.2 MeV across the four sample sets
- Vacuum masses of negative parity partners fixed to: mN,- = 1535 MeV, mΣ,- = 1750 MeV, mΛ,- = 1670 MeV
- Cascade baryon vacuum masses from parameter fixing: mΞ,+ ~ 1330 MeV, mΞ,- ~ 1845 MeV
- Pion decay constant: fπ = 93 MeV; pion mass: mπ = 139 MeV; kaon decay constant: fK ~ 110 MeV
- Vector meson masses: mω = 782 MeV, mρ = 775 MeV, mφ = 1020 MeV

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor parity doublet model studies (Lee 1972, Nemoto 1998, Hatsuda 1989) to a three-flavor SU(3) framework with hyperonization, building upon a prior model by Fraga et al. (2022) that included hyperons but not parity doubling. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD methods, which are hindered by the sign problem at finite chemical potential, and with perturbative QCD, which applies only at densities exceeding those typical of compact stars (Kurkela 2014, Fraga 2013, Gorda 2023). The results enable future studies of non-zero temperature generalizations and scenarios beyond beta equilibrium relevant to core-collapse supernovae, proto-neutron star evolution, and neutron star mergers, where chiral symmetry restoration and exotic hadronic states may affect explosion mechanisms and nucleosynthesis.
