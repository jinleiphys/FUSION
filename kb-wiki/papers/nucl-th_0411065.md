# On the complexion of pseudoscalar mesons

> A strongly momentum-dependent dressed-quark mass function is basic to QCD. It is central to the appearance of a constituent-quark mass-scale and an existential prerequisite for Goldstone modes. Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSEs) studies have long emphasised this importance, and have proved that QCD's Goldstone modes are the only pseudoscalar mesons to possess a nonzero leptonic decay constant in the chiral limit when chiral symmetry is dynamically broken, while the decay constants of their radial excitations vanish. Such features are readily illustrated using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the DSEs. In this connection we find (in GeV): f_{eta_c(1S)}= 0.233, m_{eta_c(2S)}=3.42; and support for interpreting eta(1295), eta(1470) as the first radial excitations of eta(548), eta'(958), respectively, and K(1460) as the first radial excitation of the kaon. Moreover, such radial excitations have electromagnetic diameters greater than 2fm. This exceeds the spatial length of lattices used typically in contemporary lattice-QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, within QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), the leptonic decay constants of radially excited pseudoscalar mesons vanish identically in the chiral limit, a necessary consequence of chiral symmetry and its dynamical breaking, while the ground-state Goldstone modes retain nonzero decay constants.

## Method

The authors employ a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation of the DSEs and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) to compute masses, leptonic decay constants, and pseudoscalar vertex residues for ground-state and first radially excited pseudoscalar mesons. They also incorporate a Witten-Veneziano-like mechanism to account for the non-Abelian anomaly and estimate its effect on the eta-eta-prime complex and its radial excitations. The model has a single parameter, omega, which defines a length scale for the range of strong attraction.

## Key numbers

- f_{eta_c(1S)} = 0.233 GeV (leptonic decay constant of the charmonium ground state)
- m_{eta_c(2S)} = 3.42 GeV (mass of the first radial excitation of eta_c)
- In the chiral limit (m_hat = 0): m_{pi_0} = 0.0, m_{pi_1} = 1.08 GeV, f_{pi_0} = 0.091 GeV, f_{pi_1} = 0.0
- For light quarks (m(zeta_0) = 0.0055 GeV): m_{pi_0} = 0.14 GeV, m_{pi_1} = 1.10 GeV, f_{pi_0} = 0.093 GeV, f_{pi_1} = -0.002 GeV
- For strange quarks (m(zeta_0) = 0.125 GeV): m_{pi_0} = 0.69 GeV, m_{pi_1} = 1.40 GeV, f_{pi_0} = 0.130 GeV, f_{pi_1} = -0.023 GeV
- For charm quarks (m(zeta_0) = 1.390 GeV): m_{pi_0} = 2.98 GeV, m_{pi_1} = 3.42 GeV, f_{pi_0} = 0.233 GeV, f_{pi_1} = -0.109 GeV
- Electromagnetic diameter of radial excitations greater than 2 fm; specifically, d_{pi_1} approx 2.25 fm
- Predicted mass of K(2S) = 1.52 +/- 0.12 GeV, supporting identification of K(1460) as the kaon's first radial excitation
- A 30% increase in the range of strong attraction raises m_{pi_0} by 3% but reduces m_{pi_1} by 14%

## Context

This work extends the long-standing DSE predictions of Maris and Tandy (1999) and the exact results of Krassnigg (2004) regarding the vanishing of decay constants for radial excitations in the chiral limit. It provides a unified framework that simultaneously describes Goldstone boson properties and the radial Regge trajectory of pseudoscalar mesons, which previous Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics approaches (Klink, 1998) could not achieve. The results enable future lattice-QCD studies to target radial excitations, whose diameters exceed the typical lattice spatial lengths of about 2 fm, and provide predictions for charmonium and strangeonium excited states that can be tested against experimental data from CLEO and the Particle Data Group.
