# Neutron disappearance inside the nucleus

> We consider the possibility that a neutron may disappear inside the nucleus, which will demonstrate the existence of baryon violating $ΔB=1$ interactions. It has recently been proposed that such a process may have an effect on the free neutron decay life time. We evaluate the widths for $n\rightarrow χ$ and $n\rightarrow χγ$, with $χ$ being a light dark matter particle emitted by a loosely bound neutron in various light nuclei. We find that, assuming a mass $m_χ$ close to 938 MeV, the obtained width for $n\rightarrow χ$ in $^{11}$Be is much larger than the corresponding beta decay width. This suggests a severe limit on the possible decay channel of $n \rightarrow χγ$ for free neutron.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates whether a neutron can disappear inside a nucleus via baryon-number-violating decay to a dark matter particle χ, and finds that for the halo nucleus ¹¹Be the calculated decay width for n→χ is much larger than the observed beta-decay width, which severely limits the possibility that the free neutron decay channel n→χγ can explain the discrepancy between bottle and beam neutron lifetime measurements.

## Method

The authors compute decay widths for n→χ and n→χγ in light nuclei using a quark-level model where baryon number violation is mediated by a colored scalar field, with the neutron treated as a three-quark bound state. They evaluate nuclear form factors for the loosely bound neutron in ¹¹Be and ¹⁵C using three different nuclear wave function models: harmonic oscillator (HO), no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), and local scale transformation (LST), incorporating spectroscopic factors from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The free neutron lifetime from bottle experiments is (879.6 ± 0.6) s, and from beam experiments is (888 ± 2.0) s, a 4.0σ discrepancy
- The allowed dark matter mass range is 937.900 MeV < mχ < 938.783 MeV, where the lower bound comes from ⁹Be stability (neutron binding energy Bn = 1.67 MeV) and the upper bound from forbidding χ→p+e⁻+ν̄e
- For ¹¹Be (Bn = 0.504 MeV, half-life 13.81 s), the calculated width for n→χ with mχ near 938 MeV is 1.4×10⁻¹⁶ eV using both NCSMC and LST models, compared to the experimental beta-decay width of 3.3×10⁻¹⁷ eV
- For ¹⁵C (Bn = 1.218 MeV, half-life 2.45 s), the LST model gives a width of 1.6×10⁻¹⁶ eV for δE = 0.092 MeV
- The radiative branching ratio for bound neutron decay is Γ(n→χγ)/Γ(n→χ) ≈ 2.4×10⁻⁷ × (mn-B-mχ)²/(1 MeV)²
- The free neutron radiative width is Γ(n→χγ) ≈ 4×10⁻²⁷ (mn-mχ) in eV

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Fornal and Grinstein (2018) that a dark matter particle χ emitted in neutron decay could resolve the bottle-beam neutron lifetime discrepancy, by examining whether such decays can occur in nuclei rather than free neutrons. The authors find that the ¹¹Be decay width excludes mχ ≤ 939.0 MeV, which combined with the ⁹Be stability limit (mχ ≤ 937.9 MeV) from Fornal and Grinstein (2018) and the constraint from χ stability (mχ ≥ 938.78 MeV), leaves no viable mass window for the proposed mechanism. This contradicts the suggestion that n→χγ could account for the 1% branching ratio needed to explain the lifetime discrepancy, and is consistent with astrophysical constraints from neutron stars discussed by Motta et al. (2018), McKeen et al. (2018), and Baym et al. (2018).
