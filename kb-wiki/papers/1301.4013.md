# Confinement and Z_3 symmetry in three-flavor QCD

> We investigate the confinement mechanism in three-flavor QCD with imaginary isospin chemical potentials $(μ_u,μ_d,μ_s)=(iθT,-iθT,0)$, using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, where $T$ is temperature. As for three degenerate flavors, the system has $\mathbb{Z}_{3}$ symmetry at $θ=2π/3$ and hence the Polyakov loop $Φ$ vanishes there for small $T$. As for 2+1 flavors, the symmetry is not preserved for any $θ$, but $Φ$ becomes zero at $θ=θ_{\rm conf} < 2π/3$ for small $T$. The confinement phase defined by $Φ=0$ is realized, even if the system does not have $\mathbb{Z}_{3}$ symmetry exactly. In the $θ$-$T$ plane, there is a critical endpoint of deconfinement transition. The deconfinement crossover at zero chemical potential is a remnant of the first-order deconfinement transition at $θ=θ_{\rm conf}$. The relation between the non-diagonal element $χ_{us}$ of quark number susceptibilities and the deconfinement transition is studied. The present results can be checked by lattice QCD simulations directly, since the simulations are free from the sign problem for any $θ$.

## Key claim

The confinement phase defined by a vanishing Polyakov loop can be realized even when the system does not have exact Z3 symmetry, as demonstrated in 2+1 flavor QCD with imaginary isospin chemical potentials where the Polyakov loop becomes zero at a value theta_conf less than 2pi/3 for small temperature.

## Method

The authors used the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to analyze three-flavor QCD with imaginary isospin chemical potentials (mu_u, mu_d, mu_s) = (i theta T, -i theta T, 0). They calculated the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation and determined the mean fields (chiral condensates and Polyakov loop) from the global minimum of the potential. The analysis was performed for three degenerate flavors (set S with m_s = 5.5 MeV), realistic 2+1 flavors (set R with m_s = 140.7 MeV), and a heavy strange quark case (set H with m_s = 600 MeV).

## Key numbers

- For set R (realistic 2+1 flavors), the pseudocritical temperature of the deconfinement transition at zero chemical potential is T_D = 161 MeV, while the chiral pseudocritical temperature is T_C = 202 MeV.
- At theta = 2pi/3 with set R, the deconfinement transition becomes first order at T_D = 188 MeV.
- For set S (three degenerate flavors) at theta = 2pi/3, the Polyakov loop is zero below T_D = 194 MeV, where Z3 symmetry is exactly preserved.
- The critical endpoint of deconfinement transition in the theta-T plane for set R occurs at theta_CEP = 0.41 pi and T_CEP = 175 MeV.
- For set R at low T, theta_conf is approximately 0.56 pi (0.85 x 2pi/3), while for set H it is pi/2 (0.75 x 2pi/3), and for set S it is exactly 2pi/3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Z3-symmetric gauge theories with flavor-dependent twist boundary conditions by Kouno and Sakai (2012) to the more realistic case of 2+1 flavor QCD where exact Z3 symmetry is broken by the strange quark mass. The results show that the deconfinement crossover at zero chemical potential is a remnant of the first-order deconfinement transition at theta = theta_conf, and that the non-diagonal quark number susceptibility chi_us can serve as an indicator of the deconfinement transition. The calculations can be directly checked by lattice QCD simulations since they are free from the sign problem for any theta.
