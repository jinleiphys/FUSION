# Exotic Shapes in \(^{32}\)S suggested by the Symmetry-Unrestricted Cranked Hartree-Fock Calculations

## Key claim

The paper suggests, based on symmetry-unrestricted cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, that the superdeformed band in \(^{32}\)S may undergo an internal structure change toward hyperdeformation at angular momenta \(I > 20\hbar\), and that a non-axial \(Y_{31}\) deformed band may appear in the yrast line for \(5 \leq I \leq 13\). These are presented as suggestions from the calculation, not as confirmed experimental observations.

## Method

The authors solved the cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equation in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation with mesh size \(h = 1\) fm inside a sphere of radius \(R = 8\) fm, using the Skyrme III interaction. They removed all restrictions on spatial symmetries, allowing simultaneous breaking of both axial and reflection symmetries, and carefully enforced center-of-mass and principal-axis conditions during iteration. Calculations were started from various initial configurations to explore the high-spin structure of \(^{32}\)S.

## Key numbers

- The superdeformed band becomes yrast for \(I \geq 14\hbar\).
- At rotational frequency \(\omega_{rot} \simeq 3\) MeV/\(\hbar\), a jump occurs: angular momentum \(I\) jumps from about \(22\hbar\) to \(26\hbar\), and deformation \(\delta\) increases from about 0.56 to 0.66.
- The excitation energy of the superdeformed state at \(I = 0\) is about 12 MeV; with rotational zero-point energy corrections (-4.3 MeV for SD, -1.1 MeV for ground state), this becomes about 9 MeV.
- The \(Y_{31}\) band appears in the yrast line with \(5 \leq I \leq 13\), with deformation \(\delta \simeq 0.2\) and \(\beta_{31} = 0.1\) to 0.15.
- The dynamical moment of inertia \(J^{(2)}\) of the SD band increases about 30% due to time-odd components of the mean field.

## Context

This work extends earlier cranked Hartree-Fock calculations with the BKN force by Flocard et al. (1984), which had noted a singular behavior in the SD band at high spin but did not explain its microscopic origin. The paper also builds on the long-anticipated expectation of a doubly magic superdeformed band in \(^{32}\)S, first discussed by Sheline (1972) and Leander (1975). The new symmetry-unrestricted code enables exploration of exotic shapes breaking both axial and reflection symmetries, and the results suggest that experimental searches for hyperdeformation in \(^{32}\)S at \(I > 20\hbar\) and for the predicted \(Y_{31}\) band at intermediate spins could be fruitful. The authors note that similar results were obtained with the Skyrme M\(^*\) interaction, and further study of effective interaction dependence is in progress.
