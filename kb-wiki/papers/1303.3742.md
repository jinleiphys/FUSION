# QCD thermodynamics of effective models with an improved Polyakov-loop potential

> We analyse the role of the quark backreaction on the gauge-field dynamics and its impact on the Polyakov-loop potential. Based on our analysis we construct an improved Polyakov-loop potential that can be used in future model studies. In the present work, we employe this improved potential in a study of a 2+1 flavour Polyakov-quark-meson model and show that the temperature dependence of the order parameters and thermodynamics is closer to full QCD. We discuss the results for QCD thermodynamics and outline briefly the dependence of our results on the critical temperature and the parametrisation of the Polyakov-loop potential as well as the mass of the sigma-meson.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that replacing the pure Yang-Mills (YM) Polyakov-loop potential with a quark-improved glue potential, derived from functional renormalisation group (FRG) computations of the quark backreaction on gauge-field dynamics, brings the thermodynamics and order-parameter evolution of a 2+1 flavour Polyakov-quark-meson (PQM) model significantly closer to full QCD results from lattice simulations, although the transition remains slightly steeper due to the mean-field nature of the matter sector.

## Method

The authors used the FRG to compute the fully dynamical glue potential of QCD, including the backreaction of quark degrees of freedom on gluon propagators, and compared it to the pure SU(3) YM glue potential. They derived a linear relation between the reduced temperature scales of the YM and QCD glue potentials, \( t_{\rm YM} \approx 0.57\, t_{\rm glue} \), and applied this relation to convert existing pure YM Polyakov-loop parametrisations (logarithmic, Poly-I, Poly-II) into improved glue potentials. These improved potentials were then used in a 2+1 flavour PQM model at vanishing chemical potential to compute the pressure, trace anomaly, subtracted chiral condensate, and Polyakov loop as functions of temperature, with the critical temperature of the glue potential and the sigma-meson mass treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers

*   The FRG computation yields a critical temperature for the glue potential in two-flavour QCD in the chiral limit of \( T^{\rm glue}_{\rm cr} = 203\,{\rm MeV} \), compared to \( T^{\rm YM}_{\rm cr} = 276\,{\rm MeV} \) for pure YM theory.
*   The reduced temperature scale translation is \( t_{\rm YM} \approx 0.57\, t_{\rm glue} \).
*   Using the logarithmic parametrisation with a glue-potential critical temperature of \( 210\,{\rm MeV} \) and a sigma-meson mass of \( 500\,{\rm MeV} \), the pseudocritical temperature for the crossover transition at \( \mu_f=0 \) is \( T_{\rm c} = 158\,{\rm MeV} \) with the glue potential, compared to \( 168\,{\rm MeV} \) with the YM potential; lattice results from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations give \( 157\,{\rm MeV} \) and \( 159\,{\rm MeV} \) (or \( 163\,{\rm MeV} \)), respectively.
*   The pseudocritical temperature with the glue potential varies from \( 144\,{\rm MeV} \) to \( 173\,{\rm MeV} \) when the sigma-meson mass is varied from \( 400\,{\rm MeV} \) to \( 600\,{\rm MeV} \) (with a glue-potential critical temperature of \( 210\,{\rm MeV} \)).
*   The pseudocritical temperature with the glue potential varies from \( 152\,{\rm MeV} \) to \( 171\,{\rm MeV} \) when the glue-potential critical temperature is varied from \( 180\,{\rm MeV} \) to \( 270\,{\rm MeV} \) (with a sigma-meson mass of \( 500\,{\rm MeV} \)).

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) and PQM model studies of Schaefer (2007, 2009), Skokov (2010), and Herbst (2010, 2013) by replacing the pure YM Polyakov-loop potential with a QCD glue potential that includes quark backreaction, as computed via FRG methods by Braun (2009) and Pawlowski (2010). The improved model yields thermodynamics and pseudocritical temperatures in better agreement with lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2010) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2011, 2012). The results enable future model studies to incorporate a more realistic Polyakov-loop potential, with the remaining discrepancies attributed to the mean-field treatment of the matter sector and the need for a fully dynamical inclusion of quark-meson fluctuations.
