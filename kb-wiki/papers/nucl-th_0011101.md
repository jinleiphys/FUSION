# Ground-state properties and spins of the odd {\boldmath$Z=N+1$} nuclei {\boldmath$^{61}$}Ga{\boldmath$-$}{\boldmath$^{97}$}In}

> Binding energies, quadrupole deformation parameters, spins and parities of the neutron-deficient odd $Z=N+1$ nuclei in the $A\sim 80$ region are calculated in the relativistic mean field approximation. The ground-state and low-lying configurations of the recently observed $^{77}$Y, $^{79}$Zr and $^{83}$Mo nuclei are analyzed. The calculated results are compared with other theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) model, using four different parameter sets (NL1, NL-SH, TM1, NL3), predicts ground-state shapes, spins, and parities for odd \(Z=N+1\) nuclei in the \(A\sim 80\) region, but the predictions are often parameter-dependent and many solutions are nearly degenerate in energy, making the assignment of the true ground state uncertain.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations for the odd-proton, \(T_z=-1/2\) nuclei \(^{61}\)Ga through \(^{97}\)In, using an expansion in a deformed oscillator basis with \(N_{\rm F}=12\) and \(N_{\rm B}=20\) shells. The odd nucleon was treated via the blocking approximation to restore time-reversal symmetry, with a constant BCS pairing gap of \(\triangle_{\rm n}=\triangle_{\rm p} = 0.5\) MeV. For each nucleus, multiple solutions (prolate, oblate, spherical) were obtained by blocking different single-particle states near the Fermi level, and the solution with the largest binding energy was identified as the ground state.

## Key numbers

*   \(^{61}\)Ga: ground-state quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 \sim 0.22\) (prolate) in all four RMF parameter sets; ground-state spin is \({1/2}^{-}\) (NL1) or \({3/2}^{-}\) (NL-SH, TM1, NL3); the last odd proton is bound by \(-1\), \(-0.9\), \(-1.6\) and \(-0.9\) MeV for NL1, NL-SH, TM1, and NL3, respectively.
*   \(^{69}\)Br: ground-state Nilsson orbital is \([404]{9/2}^{+}\) in all four RMF sets; RMF suggests an oblate ground state with \(\beta_2 \approx -0.29\).
*   \(^{73}\)Rb: most bound RMF solution is oblate (\(\beta_2 \sim -0.35\)) with spin \({7/2}^{+}\) in all parameter sets, while microscopic-macroscopic models predict a prolate shape with \(\beta_2 \sim 0.4\).
*   \(^{81}\)Nb: NL1 gives a nearly spherical \({9/2}^{+}\) ground state; NL-SH and NL3 predict a highly prolate \({1/2}^{+}\) ground state; the single-particle energy of the valence proton in the spherical state is positive for NL1 and TM1, indicating instability against proton emission.
*   One-proton separation energy \(S_{\rm p}\): all nuclei have positive \(S_{\rm p}\) except \(^{81}\)Nb; for \(^{89}\)Rh with NL3, \(S_{\rm p}\) is only about 0.5 MeV.
*   Charge radii \(r_{\rm ch}\): changes between different parameter sets are generally less than 0.05 fm; maximum differences between shapes are \(\sim 0.1\) fm for \(^{77}\)Y, \(^{79}\)Zr, and \(^{81}\)Nb.

## Context

This work extends RMF calculations to the neutron-deficient odd \(Z=N+1\) nuclei in the \(A\sim 80\) region, a line of work previously explored by microscopic-macroscopic (MM) models such as those of Moller et al. (MMa) and Janas et al. (MMb). The calculations are compared with experimental data for \(^{77}\)Y, \(^{79}\)Zr, and \(^{83}\)Mo reported by Janas et al. (2000). The results enable future studies using more sophisticated approaches like the relativistic Hartree-plus-Bogoliubov (RHB) method, which treats pairing and continuum coupling more properly, and angular momentum projection to resolve the ambiguities in ground-state assignments caused by nearly degenerate shape-coexisting solutions.
