# Effects of Final-State Interaction and Screening on Strange- and Heavy-Quark Production

> Final-state interaction and screening have a great influence on $q \bar q$ production cross sections, which are important quantities in many problems in quark-gluon plasma physics. They lead to an enhancement of the cross section for a $q \bar q$ color-singlet state and a suppression for a color-octet state. The effects are large near the production threshold. The presence of screening gives rise to resonances for $q\bar q$ production just above the threshold at specific plasma temperatures. These resonances, especially $c\bar c$ and $b \bar b$ resonances, may be utilized to search for the quark-gluon plasma by studying the temperature dependence of heavy-quark pair production just above the threshold.

# Effects of Final-State Interaction and Screening on Strange- and Heavy-Quark Production

## Key claim

Final-state interaction and screening modify $q\bar q$ production cross sections significantly near threshold, with enhancement for color-singlet states and suppression for color-octet states; the presence of screening in a quark-gluon plasma can produce $q\bar q$ resonances at specific plasma temperatures, particularly for $c\bar c$ and $b\bar b$ systems, which may serve as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors compute a non-perturbative corrective $K$-factor for $q\bar q$ production by evaluating the squared wavefunction at contact for a quark and antiquark interacting via a color-Coulomb potential, then generalize to a screened color-Yukawa potential using the phase-amplitude method of Calogero. The $K$-factor is applied to correct lowest-order cross sections for gluon fusion ($gg \rightarrow q\bar q$) and quark-antiquark annihilation ($q\bar q \rightarrow g^* \rightarrow q\bar q$) for strange and charm quarks, with the gluon fusion mode weighted by a color-octet to color-singlet ratio of 5:2.

## Key numbers

- The $K$-factor for the color-Yukawa interaction exhibits prominent peaks at screening length parameters $\eta = 0.835$ and $\eta = 3.23$, corresponding to the lowest two $s$-wave $q\bar q$ bound states emerging into the continuum.
- For a quark-gluon plasma with coupling constant $\alpha_s = 0.3$ and $N_f = 3$, the Debye screening length at a temperature of 200 MeV is $\lambda_D \approx 0.4$ fm; at 400 MeV it is $\lambda_D \approx 0.2$ fm.
- For a color-singlet $c\bar c$ system, the Bohr radius $a_B \approx 0.5$ fm; for a screening length of 0.4 fm, the screening length parameter is $\eta \approx 0.8$, close to the resonance value $\eta = 0.834$.
- For a color-singlet $s\bar s$ system, $a_B \approx 3$ fm; for screening lengths of 0.2-0.4 fm, $\eta \approx 0.04-0.08$, far from the resonance value.
- Using perturbative QCD estimates, the $\eta = 0.835$ resonance corresponds to $T_{c\bar c} \approx 165$ MeV for $c\bar c$ and $T_{b\bar b} \approx 393$ MeV for $b\bar b$.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of final-state interactions in $q\bar q$ production beyond the lowest-order and next-to-leading order perturbative QCD results used by Rafelski (1996) and Gavai (1996), and beyond the simple empirical $K$-factor approach of Field (1989) and others. The authors build on the Gamow factor concept from Gamow (1928) and the Schwinger interpolation technique (1973) to construct a $K$-factor valid for all relative velocities. The results enable a proposed experimental search for quark-gluon plasma via temperature-dependent enhancement of heavy-quark pair production near threshold, complementing the $J/\psi$ suppression probe suggested by Matsui and Satz (1986, 1988). The authors note that lattice gauge theory gives Debye screening lengths about half the perturbative QCD estimates (Ukawa, 1989), introducing uncertainty in the predicted resonance temperatures.
