# Effective Kbar N interaction and role of chiral symmetry

> We study the consequence of chiral SU(3) symmetry in the kaon-nucleon phenomenology, by deriving the effective single-channel KbarK N potential. It turns out that the pi Sigma interaction is strongly attractive and plays an important role for the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. We discuss the implication of effective potential for the few-body kaonic nuclei.

## Key claim

Chiral SU(3) symmetry forces both the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) channels to be attractive, and the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance arises from two poles whose origins lie in these separate single-channel attractions, not from a single resonance; the effective single-channel \(\bar{K}N\) potential derived from this coupled-channel framework is therefore less attractive than purely phenomenological potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the leading-order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon interaction (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel framework for the \(S=-1\), \(I=0\) channels (\(\bar{K}N\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(\eta\Lambda\), \(K\Xi\)). They then construct an effective single-channel \(\bar{K}N\) potential by integrating out the other channels exactly, and convert this into a local, energy-dependent, complex potential in coordinate space for use in nonrelativistic Schrödinger calculations.

## Key numbers

- The two poles of the full coupled-channel amplitude are found at \(z_1 = 1428 - 17i\ \text{MeV}\) and \(z_2 = 1400 - 76i\ \text{MeV}\).
- The single-channel \(\bar{K}N\) interaction alone generates a bound state at \(z_1(\bar{K}N\ \text{only}) = 1427\ \text{MeV}\).
- The single-channel \(\pi\Sigma\) interaction alone generates a broad resonance at \(z_2(\pi\Sigma\ \text{only}) = 1388 - 96i\ \text{MeV}\).
- The effective single-channel \(\bar{K}N\) potential yields a "binding energy" of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) of approximately \(15\ \text{MeV}\) measured from the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold (around \(1435\ \text{MeV}\)), rather than the nominal \(30\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach developed by the same group (Jido et al., 2003) and others (Kaiser et al., 1995; Oset and Ramos, 1998) that successfully reproduces \(\bar{K}N\) scattering data and dynamically generates the \(\Lambda(1405)\). It directly contradicts purely phenomenological potentials (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002) that predict stronger \(\bar{K}N\) attraction and deeper binding in kaonic nuclei. The derived effective potential enables more realistic few-body calculations of the \(K^-pp\) system (Dote et al., 2008), and the authors note that explicit treatment of the \(\pi\Sigma N\) channel (Ikeda and Sato, 2008) will be needed for energies near that threshold.
