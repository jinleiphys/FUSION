# Digital quantum simulation of an extended Agassi model: Using machine learning to disentangle its phase-diagram

> A digital quantum simulation for the extended Agassi model is proposed using a quantum platform with eight trapped ions. The extended Agassi model is an analytically solvable model including both short range pairing and long range monopole-monopole interactions with applications in nuclear physics and in other many-body systems. In addition, it owns a rich phase diagram with different phases and the corresponding phase transition surfaces. The aim of this work is twofold: on one hand, to propose a quantum simulation of the model at the present limits of the trapped ions facilities and, on the other hand, to show how to use a machine learning algorithm on top of the quantum simulation to accurately determine the phase of the system. Concerning the quantum simulation, this proposal is scalable with polynomial resources to larger Agassi systems. Digital quantum simulations of nuclear physics models assisted by machine learning may enable one to outperform the fastest classical computers in determining fundamental aspects of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a digital quantum simulation of the extended Agassi model with eight sites (j=2) can be implemented on a trapped-ion platform using polynomial resources, and that a machine learning algorithm applied to the time-evolved correlation function can accurately determine the quantum phase of the system, even when using a Trotter approximation with a limited number of steps.

## Method

The extended Agassi model Hamiltonian was mapped onto Pauli matrices using the Jordan-Wigner transformation for an eight-site system (j=2). The time evolution operator was approximated via a Trotter expansion with the Hamiltonian terms grouped into eight non-commuting sets, each implementable with trapped-ion systems using single-qubit and Molmer-Sorensen multiqubit gates. The time evolution of the correlation function C_z(1,2) was computed for a mesh of 9261 points in the parameter space (chi, Sigma, Lambda in [0,2] with 21 steps each) for 100 time steps, and this data was used to train a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) and a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for quantum phase classification.

## Key numbers

* The MLP correctly predicted the quantum phase in 96.7% of test points when trained and tested with exact time evolution, and 93.4% when trained and tested with Trotterized time evolution using n_T=6 Trotter steps.
* The fidelity of the Trotter approximation for n_T=5 drops significantly at times t > 1 for the eight-site system, while n_T=15 provides good accuracy up to t=5.
* The mesh of training points covered the parameter space with 21 steps per parameter (chi, Sigma, Lambda) for a total of 9261 points, each with 100 time steps.
* The implementation requires approximately two thousand quantum gates for the j=2 case.

## Context

This work extends the quantum simulation of the original Agassi model with four sites (j=1) previously proposed by Perez-Fernandez (2021) to the extended Agassi model with eight sites, which presents a richer phase diagram with five quantum phases instead of two. The phase diagram was previously characterized through mean-field formalism by Garcia-Ramos (2019). The use of machine learning (MLP and CNN) to identify quantum phases from time-evolved correlation functions provides a method that avoids computing the ground state, and the approach could enable quantum simulations of nuclear physics models that outperform classical computers for determining fundamental aspects of nuclear matter.
