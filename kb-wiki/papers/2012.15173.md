# Chiral separation effect catalyzed by heavy impurities

> We investigate the influence of Kondo effect, namely, the nonperturbative effect induced by heavy impurities, on the chiral separation effect (CSE) in quark matter. We employ a simple effective model incorporating the Kondo condensate made of a light quark and a heavy quark, and compute the response function of axial current to the magnetic field in static limit and dynamical limit. As a result, we find that the Kondo effect catalyzes the CSE in both the limits, and particularly the CSE in dynamical limit can be enhanced by a factor of approximately three. Our findings clearly show that the presence of heavy impurities in quark matter can play an important role in the transport phenomena of light quarks induced by a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Kondo effect, a nonperturbative correlation between light and heavy quarks, catalyzes the chiral separation effect (CSE) in quark matter; in the dynamical limit the axial current response to a magnetic field can be enhanced by a factor of approximately three, but this result depends on treating the Kondo condensate as a free parameter rather than solving a gap equation.

## Method

The authors employed a simple effective model incorporating a Kondo condensate (scalar and vector) formed by a light quark and a heavy quark within heavy quark effective theory (HQET). They computed the retarded response function of the axial current to an external magnetic field using linear response theory in the imaginary-time formalism, evaluating it separately in the static limit (momentum to zero first) and the dynamical limit (frequency to zero first).

## Key numbers

* The CSE in the dynamical limit can be enhanced by a factor of approximately three relative to the case without Kondo condensate (no absolute value given for the baseline).
* The number of colors is set to \(N_c = 3\) throughout the calculation.
* The analysis assumes the magnetic field magnitude satisfies \(\sqrt{eB} \ll \mu\) (magnetic field much smaller than the Fermi energy).
* The electric charge of light and heavy quarks is taken as identical: \(e \equiv e_q = e_Q\), with examples \(e = +\frac{2}{3}\hat{e}\) (up/charm) or \(e = -\frac{1}{3}\hat{e}\) (down/bottom).

## Context

This work extends studies of the chiral separation effect (CSE) originally formulated by Son (2004) and Metlitski (2005) to include nonperturbative QCD corrections via the Kondo effect, which was previously shown to emerge in quark matter by Yasui (2013) and Hattori (2015). Prior perturbative QED corrections to the CSE were found to be small (Gorbar, 2013), whereas this work demonstrates that the Kondo condensate can significantly modify the CSE, particularly in the dynamical limit. The results enable future studies of transport phenomena in dense quark matter (e.g., in neutron stars or low-energy heavy-ion collisions) where heavy impurities coexist with light quarks.
