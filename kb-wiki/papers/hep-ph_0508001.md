# Space-like and time-like pion electromagnetic form factor and Fock state components within the Light-Front dynamics

> The simultaneous investigation of the pion electromagnetic form factor in the space- and time-like regions within a light-front model allows one to address the issue of non-valence components of the pion and photon wave functions. Our relativistic approach is based on a microscopic vector meson dominance (VMD) model for the dressed vertex where a photon decays in a quark-antiquark pair, and on a simple parametrization for the emission or absorption of a pion by a quark. The results show an excellent agreement in the space like region up to -10 $(GeV/c)^2$, while in time-like region the model produces reasonable results up to 10 $(GeV/c)^2$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified light-front model for the pion electromagnetic form factor in both space-like and time-like regions, achieving excellent agreement with experimental data in the space-like region up to -10 (GeV/c)^2 and reasonable results in the time-like region up to 10 (GeV/c)^2, but only under the simplifying assumption of a massless pion (chiral limit) and using a small set of adjustable parameters including a fitted width for radially excited vector mesons.

## Method

The authors start from the Mandelstam formula for the electromagnetic current in impulse approximation and perform a light-front decomposition of the triangle diagram, separating on-shell and instantaneous contributions from the fermion propagators. The dressed photon-quark vertex is modeled through a microscopic vector meson dominance (VMD) approach, where the photon couples to a sum over vector meson states with masses, decay constants, and widths, while the non-valence components of the pion and photon wave functions are described through a contact interaction parametrizing radiative pion emission from a quark. The pion and vector meson vertex functions are obtained from eigenfunctions of a light-front mass operator with a harmonic oscillator confining potential.

## Key numbers

- Space-like form factor agrees with experimental data up to -10 (GeV/c)^2
- Time-like form factor produces reasonable results up to 10 (GeV/c)^2
- Constituent quark mass m is a parameter of the model (value not specified in the truncated text)
- The model uses experimental widths for vector mesons from the Particle Data Group, with a single fitted width for unknown radial excitations
- The pion is treated in the chiral limit (massless pion approximation)

## Context

This work extends previous light-front studies of the pion form factor in the space-like region (Pach02, ba01, pion99) and time-like region (choi01) by providing a unified framework that simultaneously addresses both kinematic regimes. The approach builds on a covariant generalization of vector meson dominance (Connell) and uses the light-front mass operator model of Frederico, Pace, and Zuffi (2002) for meson spectra. The model enables investigation of non-valence Fock state components of both the pion and photon wave functions, and could be extended to study the nucleon electromagnetic form factor and the vector meson Green function.
