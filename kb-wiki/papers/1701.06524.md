# Equations of state for real gases on the nuclear scale

> The formalism to augment the classical models of equation of state for real gases with the quantum statistical effects is presented. It allows an arbitrary excluded volume procedure to model repulsive interactions, and an arbitrary density-dependent mean field to model attractive interactions. Variations on the excluded volume mechanism include van der Waals (VDW) and Carnahan-Starling models, while the mean fields are based on VDW, Redlich-Kwong-Soave, Peng-Robinson, and Clausius equations of state. The VDW parameters of the nucleon-nucleon interaction are fitted in each model to the properties of the ground state of nuclear matter, and the following range of values is obtained: $a = 330 - 430$ MeV fm$^3$ and $b = 2.5 - 4.4$ fm$^3$. In the context of the excluded-volume approach, the fits to the nuclear ground state disfavor the values of the effective hard-core radius of a nucleon significantly smaller than $0.5$ fm, at least for the nuclear matter region of the phase diagram. Modifications to the standard VDW repulsion and attraction terms allow to improve significantly the value of the nuclear incompressibility factor $K_0$, bringing it closer to empirical estimates. The generalization to include the baryon-baryon interactions into the hadron resonance gas model is performed. The behavior of the baryon-related lattice QCD observables at zero chemical potential is shown to be strongly correlated to the nuclear matter properties: an improved description of the nuclear incompressibility also yields an improved description of the lattice data at $μ= 0$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism to incorporate quantum statistical effects into classical real-gas equations of state for nuclear matter, but the specific values of the van der Waals parameters $a$ and $b$ obtained from fits to the nuclear ground state depend on which real-gas model is used, and the standard van der Waals model yields a nuclear incompressibility factor $K_0$ that is significantly higher than empirical estimates.

## Method

The author generalizes the van der Waals equation of state to include arbitrary excluded-volume procedures (van der Waals or Carnahan-Starling) and arbitrary density-dependent mean fields (van der Waals, Redlich-Kwong-Soave, Peng-Robinson, or Clausius), then augments each classical model with quantum Fermi statistics by replacing the classical ideal-gas free energy with the quantum ideal-gas free energy in the canonical ensemble. The two parameters $a$ (attraction) and $b$ (repulsion) are fitted for each of the eight resulting models to reproduce the ground state of symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, normal density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, zero pressure, and binding energy per nucleon $E/A \cong -16$ MeV.

## Key numbers

* The van der Waals parameters fitted to the nuclear ground state across all eight models fall in the range $a = 330 - 430$ MeV fm$^3$ and $b = 2.5 - 4.4$ fm$^3$.
* The standard van der Waals model yields a nuclear incompressibility factor $K_0 \cong 763$ MeV, which is significantly higher than the empirical estimate of $250 - 315$ MeV (Stone, 2014).
* The normal nuclear density used for fitting is $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, with binding energy $E/A \cong -16$ MeV.
* The van der Waals model predicts a critical temperature $T_c \cong 19.7$ MeV and critical density $n_c \cong 0.072$ fm$^{-3}$ for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.
* The packing fraction at normal nuclear density in the van der Waals model is $\eta_0 = b n_0 / 4 \simeq 0.14$.

## Context

This work extends the quantum-statistical van der Waals model for nuclear matter previously developed by Vovchenko (2015) and Redlich (2016) to a family of eight real-gas equations of state, including Carnahan-Starling repulsion and Redlich-Kwong-Soave, Peng-Robinson, and Clausius attraction terms. The improved models bring the nuclear incompressibility factor closer to empirical estimates from Stone (2014) and the Walecka model, and the formalism enables the inclusion of baryon-baryon interactions into the hadron resonance gas model, where the behavior of lattice QCD observables at zero chemical potential is shown to be strongly correlated with nuclear matter properties.
