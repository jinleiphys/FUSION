# Color superconductivity in dense quark matter

> I discuss recent developments in our understanding of the color-superconducting phases of cold, dense quark matter. I describe the phase diagram as a function of density and the strange quark mass, and outline some ideas about possible observational consequences of these exotic phases.

## Key claim

Cold dense quark matter at low temperatures and high densities exhibits color-superconducting phases whose global symmetries depend on the strange quark mass, and for sufficiently small strange quark mass there may be no phase transition between hadronic matter and quark matter, allowing a continuous mapping between the two descriptions.

## Method

The author analyzes the phase diagram of QCD with two massless quarks (up, down) and a strange quark of variable mass, using a mean-field NJL model with a four-fermion interaction carrying the quantum numbers of single-gluon exchange. The analysis employs a condensate ansatz with five independent superconducting gap parameters that interpolates between the two-flavor (2SC) and three-flavor color-flavor locked (CFL) phases, and includes a chiral condensate for the strange quark.

## Key numbers

* Onset chemical potential for nuclear matter: \(\mu_{\rm o} \sim 300\) MeV
* The unlocking transition between CFL and 2SC phases occurs when the mismatch between up and strange Fermi momenta satisfies \(\frac{M_s(\mu)^2}{2\mu} \lesssim \Delta_{us}\), where \(M_s(\mu)\) is the constituent strange quark mass and \(\Delta_{us}\) is the gap for pairing between strange and light quarks

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity by Barrois (1977), Bailin and Love (1984), and Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) to the realistic case of 2+1 quark flavors with a nonzero strange quark mass. It builds on the color-flavor locking mechanism identified by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the conjecture by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) that quark and hadronic matter may be continuously connected. The paper enables future work on the observational signatures of color-superconducting phases in neutron stars and on the mapping between quark and baryon pairing gaps.
