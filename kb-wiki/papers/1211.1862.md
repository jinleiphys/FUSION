# Three methods to detect the predicted $D \bar{D}$ scalar meson X(3700)

> In analogy to the f_0(500), which appears as a $ππ$ resonance in chiral unitary theory, and the f_0(980), which appears as a quasibound $K \bar K$ state, the extension of this approach to the charm sector also predicts a quasibound $D \bar D $ state with mass around 3720 MeV, named as X(3700), for which some experimental support is seen in the $e^+ e^- \to J/ψD \bar D $ reaction close to the $D \bar D $ threshold. In the present work we propose three different experiments to observe it as a clear peak. The first one is the radiative decay of the $ψ(3770)$, $ψ(3770) \to γX(3700) \to γηη'$. The second one proposes the analogous reaction $ψ(4040) \to γX(3700) \to γηη'$ and the third reaction is the $e^+ e^- \to J/ψX(3700) \to J/ψηη'$. Neat peaks are predicted for all the reactions and the calculated rates are found within measurable range in present facilities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes three specific experimental reactions to detect the predicted scalar meson X(3700), a quasibound \(D \bar D\) state, by looking at its decay into the \(\eta \eta'\) channel rather than the previously suggested inclusive photon spectrum, which suffers from large background and Bremsstrahlung contamination.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach extended to SU(4) to compute the couplings of the X(3700) pole at \((3722 - i18)\) MeV to various meson-meson channels. They then evaluate the triangular-loop diagrams for the radiative decays \(\psi(3770) \to \gamma X\) and \(\psi(4040) \to \gamma X\), and for the reaction \(e^+ e^- \to J/\psi X\), using the known couplings of the \(\psi\) states to \(D \bar D\) and the X(3700) couplings to \(\eta \eta'\) to predict invariant mass distributions and decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The X(3700) pole is found at \((3722 - i18)\) MeV, corresponding to a width of 36 MeV.
- The coupling of X(3700) to \(\eta \eta'\) is \(|g_X| = 1720\) MeV, the largest among light-meson channels.
- For \(\psi(3770) \to \gamma X \to \gamma \eta \eta'\), the integrated width around the peak (3600 < \(M_{inv}\) < 3770 MeV) is \(\Gamma = 0.293\) KeV, giving a branching ratio of \(10^{-5}\).
- For \(\psi(4040) \to \gamma X \to \gamma \eta \eta'\), the integrated width (3600 < \(M_{inv}\) < 3800 MeV) is \(\Gamma = 0.496\) KeV, corresponding to a branching ratio of \(6.2 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The coupling of \(\psi(3770)\) to \(D^+ D^-\) is \(g_\psi = 11.7\); the coupling of \(\psi(4040)\) to \(D^+ D^-\) is \(g_{\psi(4040), D^+ D^-} = 2.15\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach that successfully describes the light scalar mesons \(f_0(500)\) (as a \(\pi\pi\) resonance) and \(f_0(980)\) (as a quasibound \(K \bar K\) state) to the charm sector, where a quasibound \(D \bar D\) state was predicted by Danilkin et al. (2011) and Oset et al. (2012). The only prior experimental support came from Belle (2008) in the \(e^+ e^- \to J/\psi D \bar D\) reaction, interpreted as the X(3700) by Danilkin et al. (2012). The present work enables experimental searches at BESIII by proposing cleaner \(\eta \eta'\) final states that avoid the Bremsstrahlung background and threshold ambiguities present in earlier suggestions.
