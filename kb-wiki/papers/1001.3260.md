# Resonances as a possible observable of hot and dense nuclear matter

> One of the most fundamental questions in the field of relativistic heavy ion physics is how to reach and explore densities which are needed to cross the chiral and/or the deconfinement phase transition. In this analysis we investigate the information we can gather by analyzing baryonic and mesonic resonances on the hot and dense phase in such nuclear reactions. The decay products of these resonances carry information on the resonances properties at the space time point of their decay. We especially investigate the percentage of reconstructable resonances as a function of density for heavy ion collisions in the energy range between $E_{lab}$ = 30 AGeV and $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 AGeV, the energy domain between the future FAIR facility and the present RHIC collider.

## Key claim

Resonances decaying in the hadronic channel can probe the high-density phase of heavy ion collisions, but only if they have high transverse momentum; the reconstruction probability for resonances produced at densities above approximately 2 times ground-state density saturates or even increases slightly, contrary to the naive expectation that it should continue to decrease.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (v2.3) non-equilibrium transport model, which solves the covariant Boltzmann equation, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 30 AGeV and 200 AGeV. They tracked each resonance's decay products and counted a resonance as "reconstructable" only if its daughter particles did not rescatter in the further evolution of the system. The baryonic density at the resonance decay point was computed locally in the Eckart frame, and results were presented as a function of density in units of ground-state density (0.16 per fm^3).

## Key numbers

- The fraction of reconstructable baryon resonances (Delta, Sigma*(1385), Lambda*(1520)) and meson resonances (rho, omega, K*0, Phi) shows a clear peak at very low density and a steady decrease toward higher density, but this trend stops at roughly 2 rho_0 (2 times ground-state density).
- The Phi meson has a lifetime of approximately 40 fm/c, which contributes to its higher reconstruction probability compared to shorter-lived resonances.
- The average transverse momentum of reconstructable Delta and rho resonances is about 200 MeV higher than for all resonances of the same type.
- At low transverse momentum (p_T < 1 GeV), the percentage of reconstructable resonances created at density higher than 2 rho_0 is low; this percentage increases for p_T > 2 GeV, with the effect more pronounced at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV than at E_lab = 30 AGeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of resonances in heavy ion collisions using the UrQMD model (Vogel 2007, Bleicher 2002, Bleicher 2003) and addresses the finding by Markert (2007) that fewer resonances are measured at RHIC than predicted by statistical model calculations (Andronic 2002). The authors argue that high-p_T resonances may provide a new observable for the high-density phase at the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR and the low-energy program at RHIC, complementing earlier work by Vogel (2007) which showed that dileptons might not be favorable for probing the high-density phase.
