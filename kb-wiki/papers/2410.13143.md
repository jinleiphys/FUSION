# Azimuthal Correlation Anisotropies in p + p Collisions Simulated by Pythia

> Stimulated by the keen interest of possible collective behavior in high-energy proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, we study two-particle angular correlations in pseudorapidity and azimuthal differences in simulated p + p interactions by the Pythia 8 event generator. Multi-parton interactions and color connection are included in these simulations which have been perceived to produce collectivity in final-state particles. Meanwhile, contributions from genuine few-body nonflow correlations, not of collective flow behavior, are known to be severe in those small-system collisions. We present our Pythia correlation studies in a pedagogical way and report azimuthal harmonic anisotropies analyzed by several methods. We observe anisotropies in those Pythia simulated events qualitatively and semi-quantitatively similar to experimental data. Our findings highlight the delicate nature of azimuthal anisotropies in small-system collisions, and provide a benchmark helping improve data analysis and interpret experimental measurements in small-system collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Pythia 8 simulations of proton-proton collisions produce azimuthal correlation anisotropies qualitatively and semi-quantitatively similar to experimental data, but these arise from multi-parton interactions and color reconnection rather than genuine collective flow, highlighting that the observed anisotropies in small-system collisions may be largely attributable to nonflow correlations rather than quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors used Pythia 8.303 to simulate 3×10^8 diffractive and 3×10^8 non-diffractive p+p events at √s_NN = 200 GeV, including multi-parton interactions and color reconnection. They analyzed two-particle angular correlations in pseudorapidity (Δη) and azimuthal (Δφ) differences for charged hadrons (π±, K±, p, p̅) within |η|<1 and 0.2 < p⊥ < 2 GeV/c, applying mixed-event acceptance corrections and comparing same-sign and opposite-sign charge pairs across five multiplicity bins.

## Key numbers

- The simulation generated 3×10^8 events each for diffractive and non-diffractive interactions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The average multiplicity per non-diffractive event for π+ is 12.140, for π- is 11.422, for π0 is 13.289, for protons is 4.162, and for gluons is 62.537
- The fitted pedestal value for the ratio of small-Δη to large-Δη same-sign correlations in the 4≤Nch≤6 multiplicity bin is 0.96 ± 0.06
- The fitted pedestal values for the ratio in the 1≤Nch≤3 and 15≤Nch≤20 bins are 0.95 ± 0.01 and 0.96 ± 0.07, respectively
- The fitted pedestal values for small and large Δη correlations in the 4≤Nch≤6 bin are 0.0708 ± 0.0007 and 0.0704 ± 0.0001, respectively

## Context

This work extends studies of possible collective behavior in small-system collisions, following experimental observations from CMS (2010), ATLAS (2012), ALICE (2012), and STAR (2015) that suggested flow-like signals in p+p and p+A collisions. The study provides a benchmark using Pythia 8, which includes multi-parton interactions and color reconnection as implemented by Sjostrand (2014) and Bierlich (2018), to help distinguish genuine collective flow from nonflow correlations in small systems. The results enable improved nonflow subtraction techniques and aid interpretation of experimental anisotropy measurements in small-system collisions, building on the nonflow mitigation methods of Nagle (2018) and Feng (2024).
