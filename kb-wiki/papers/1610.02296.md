# Binding Blocks: building the Universe one nucleus at the time

> We present a new teaching and outreach activity based around the construction of a three-dimensional chart of isotopes using LEGO$^{\circledR}$ bricks. The activity, \emph{Binding Blocks}, demonstrates nuclear and astrophysical processes through a seven-meter chart of all nuclear isotopes, built from over 26,000 LEGO$^{\circledR}$ bricks. It integrates A-level and GCSE curricula across areas of nuclear physics, astrophysics, and chemistry, including: nuclear decays (through the colours in the chart); nuclear binding energy (through tower heights); production of chemical elements in the cosmos; fusion processes in stars and fusion energy on Earth; as well as links to medical physics, particularly diagnostics and radiotherapy.

# Binding Blocks: building the Universe one nucleus at the time

## Key claim

The paper presents a teaching and outreach activity where a three-dimensional chart of isotopes is constructed from LEGO bricks, but the chart uses only experimentally observed nuclides (roughly one third of predicted isotopes) and simplifies each nucleus to its dominant decay mode only, ignoring competing decay channels.

## Method

The authors created a physical 3D nuclide chart by laying out a standard 2D chart of proton number versus neutron number on a table and building towers of LEGO bricks for each isotope. The height of each tower represents the mass excess per nucleon, shifted so that iron-56 (the most stable isotope) sits at a single layer, with all values rounded to the nearest whole number of layers where each layer corresponds to 25,000 GJ/kg. The chart uses color-coded bricks to indicate the dominant decay mode for each isotope: black for stable, light blue for beta-minus decay, red for beta-plus decay, dark blue for neutron emission, orange for proton emission, yellow for alpha decay, and green for spontaneous fission.

## Key numbers

- The full chart measures approximately 7 meters in length and 1 meter in width.
- The full chart is built from over 26,000 LEGO bricks.
- Each layer of LEGO bricks corresponds to 25,000 GJ/kg of mass excess per nucleon.
- The smallest set (covering up to neon-20) uses approximately 2,000 LEGO bricks and fits in a school bag.
- The version including the iron region uses approximately 7,000 LEGO bricks.
- The full implementation costs approximately £3,500.
- At the STFC Daresbury Laboratory Open Day (July 2016), over 1,000 of the 7,500 visitors participated in the Binding Blocks exhibition.
- The UK energy consumption was 125 GJ per capita in 2014 (provided for comparison).

## Context

This activity extends the static display concept of Koura (2015) by making the chart interactive and participatory, with the audience actively building the chart during events. The work enables hands-on teaching of nuclear physics concepts including nuclear decays, binding energy, stellar fusion, element production in the cosmos, and medical physics applications such as PET scanning using fluorine-18. The modular design allows adaptation for different audience sizes and budgets, and the authors provide downloadable materials for other groups to replicate the activity.
