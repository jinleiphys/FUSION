# Yang-Mills correlators at finite temperature: A perturbative perspective

> We consider the two-point correlators of Yang-Mills theories at finite temperature in the Landau gauge. We employ a model for the corresponding Yang-Mills correlators based on the inclusion of an effective mass term for gluons. The latter is expected to have its origin in the existence of Gribov copies. One-loop calculations at zero temperature have been shown to agree remarkably well with the corresponding lattice data. We extend on this and perform a one-loop calculation of the Matsubara gluon and ghost two-point correlators at finite temperature. We show that, as in the vacuum, an effective gluon mass accurately captures the dominant infrared physics for the magnetic gluon and ghost propagators. It also reproduces the gross qualitative features of the electric gluon propagator. In particular, we find a slight nonmonotonous behavior of the Debye mass as a function of temperature, however not as pronounced as in existing lattice results. A more quantitative description of the electric sector near the deconfinement phase transition certainly requires another physical ingredient sensitive to the order parameter of the transition.

## Key claim

The authors show that an effective gluon mass, which captures the dominant infrared physics of Yang-Mills correlators in the vacuum, also describes the magnetic gluon and ghost propagators at finite temperature, but fails to quantitatively reproduce the nonmonotonous behavior of the electric gluon propagator near the deconfinement phase transition, indicating that another physical ingredient sensitive to the order parameter of the transition is needed.

## Method

The authors perform a one-loop calculation of the Matsubara gluon and ghost two-point correlators at finite temperature in the Landau gauge using the Curci-Ferrari model, which includes an effective mass term for gluons. They decompose all self-energies into vacuum and thermal contributions, renormalize using standard conditions at zero temperature, and compare their results to lattice data for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. For temperature-dependent fits, they adjust the mass and coupling parameters by fitting the ghost and magnetic gluon propagators to lattice data at each temperature.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit parameters at zero temperature for SU(2) are m = 0.68 GeV and g = 7.5, defined at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV.
- The Debye mass shows a nonmonotonous behavior with a peak at T_peak ≈ 140 MeV, while lattice simulations find the peak near T_c ∼ 300 MeV.
- The ratio M_D^2(0)/M_D^2(T_peak) is given by 1/(1 - 0.0014 g^2 N), which is much smaller than the lattice ratio of up to 4 near T_c.
- The magnetic mass increases monotonously with temperature, with asymptotic behavior M_mag(T) ∼ (g^2 N)/(6π) T for T ≫ m.
- The ghost dressing function at zero momentum becomes singular for temperatures above approximately 200 MeV when using temperature-independent parameters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature calculations (Tissier and Serreau, 2011) which showed that one-loop results in the Curci-Ferrari model agree remarkably well with lattice data for vacuum gluon and ghost correlators. The study is motivated by lattice simulations of finite-temperature Yang-Mills correlators (Cucchieri et al., 2007; Fischer et al., 2010; Aouane et al., 2011) which show qualitative features such as a nonmonotonous Debye mass and temperature-insensitive ghost correlators. The results suggest that while an effective gluon mass captures the dominant infrared behavior away from T_c, a more refined treatment incorporating the Polyakov loop as an order parameter, as done in functional renormalization group studies (Braun et al., 2007; Fister et al., 2013), is needed to quantitatively describe the electric sector near the phase transition.
