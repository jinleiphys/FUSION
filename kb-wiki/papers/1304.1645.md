# Configuration-interaction Monte Carlo method and its application to the trapped unitary Fermi gas

> We develop a quantum Monte Carlo method to estimate the ground-state energy of a fermionic many-particle system in the configuration-interaction shell model approach. The fermionic sign problem is circumvented by using a guiding wave function in Fock space. The method provides an upper bound on the ground-state energy whose tightness depends on the choice of the guiding wave function. We argue that the antisymmetric geminal product class of wave functions is a good choice for guiding wave functions. We demonstrate our method for the trapped two-species fermionic cold atom system in the unitary regime of infinite scattering length using the particle-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave function as the guiding wave function. We estimate the ground-state energy and energy-staggering pairing gap as a function of the number of particles. Our results compare favorably with exact numerical diagonalization results and with previous coordinate-space Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim

The Configuration-Interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) method provides an upper bound on the ground-state energy of a fermionic system, where the tightness of this bound depends on the quality of the chosen guiding wave function; the method is demonstrated for the trapped unitary Fermi gas using a particle-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) guiding wave function, which is a member of the antisymmetric geminal product (AGP) class.

## Method

The method performs a stochastic projection of the ground-state wave function in the discrete Fock space of Slater determinants (configurations) by propagating an initial wave function in imaginary time using a propagator derived from the CI Hamiltonian. The fermionic sign problem is circumvented by introducing a guiding wave function and a modified Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H}_\gamma\) (with \(\gamma \geq 0\)) whose off-diagonal matrix elements are made non-negative, ensuring a sign-free random walk. The ground-state energy of \(\mathcal{H}_\gamma\) is estimated via a mixed estimate using the local energy, and a linear extrapolation from \(\gamma = 0\) and \(\gamma = 1\) to \(\gamma = -1\) yields the best upper bound for the original Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

*   For a model space of \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max} = 3\) oscillator shells and \(N=6\) particles, the CIMC ground-state energy is \(8.639(7) \, \hbar\omega\), compared to the exact value of \(8.601 \, \hbar\omega\) (within about 0.4%).
*   For \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max} = 3\) and \(N=7\) particles, the CIMC ground-state energy is \(11.176(4) \, \hbar\omega\), compared to the exact value of \(11.021 \, \hbar\omega\) (within about 1.4%).
*   For \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max} = 8\) and \(N=2\) particles, the CIMC ground-state energy is \(2.000(6) \, \hbar\omega\), compared to the exact value of \(2.000 \, \hbar\omega\).
*   For \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max} = 9\) and \(N \leq 20\) particles, the CIMC ground-state energies are \(0.5 - 3\%\) higher than those obtained in the coordinate-space fixed-node GFMC calculations of Blume et al. (2007).
*   The largest calculation, with \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max}=9\) and \(N=20\) particles, took about 900 cpu hours.

## Context

This work extends the lattice Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method of ten Haaf et al. (1995) and Sorella (2000) to the configuration-interaction (CI) framework, providing a new approach for fermionic many-body systems where direct diagonalization is prohibitive due to the combinatorial growth of the Hilbert space. The method is demonstrated for the trapped unitary Fermi gas, a system previously studied with coordinate-space GFMC by Blume et al. (2007) and Chang et al. (2007), and with density functional theory by Bulgac (2007). The CIMC method enables calculations in large CI model spaces (e.g., \(\mathcal{N}_s = 440\) single-particle states for \(\mathcal{N}_{\rm max}=9\)) and can be applied to other fermionic systems such as cold atoms in deformed traps and finite nuclei.
