# Searching for Quark-Gluon Plasma(QGP) Bubble effects at RHIC/LHC

> Since the early eighties, we have shared with Leon Van Hove the following view. That if a QGP were produced in high energy heavy ion colliders, that its hadronization products would likely come from small localized in phase space bubbles of plasma. We develop a model based on HIJING, to which we added a ring of adjoining multiple bubbles in the central rapidity region. Our simulations were designed to be tested by the forthcoming RHIC STAR detector data for 65 GeV/n Au colliding with 65 GeV/n Au. We took into account background and resonance effects to allow a direct comparison with the data. Later 100 GeV/n Au colliding with 100 GeV/n Au and LHC data could also test these ideas. We used two charged particle correlation's as a sensitive method to test for bubbles.

## Key claim
If a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, its hadronization products are more likely to come from small, localized phase-space bubbles rather than a large thermalized volume; the authors simulate a ring of multiple adjoining bubbles embedded in a modified HIJING event generator, and argue that two-particle angular correlations can distinguish bubble signals from background jets and resonances, provided the bubble energy per event is not too low.

## Method
The authors modified the HIJING event generator for 65 GeV/n Au+Au collisions by replacing mini-jets with an average of 13 adjoining QGP bubbles arranged in a single ring at mid-rapidity, each bubble having zero net charge and an energy of about 3.25 GeV. They included elliptic flow effects in the soft (beam-jet) component and applied transverse momentum cuts (0.8–2.0 GeV/c) to enhance space–momentum correlation. Two-particle correlations in azimuthal angle (ΔΦ) and pseudorapidity (Δη), as well as event-by-event charge fluctuations, were computed from 100,000 simulated events and compared to modified HIJING without bubbles (using either standard or doubled mini-jets) and to a resonance gas model.

## Key numbers
- Average of 13 bubbles per central event, each with ~3.25 GeV energy, yielding ~1.95 charged particles per bubble within the cuts (|η|<0.75, 0.8<p_t<2.0 GeV/c).
- For the ΔΦ correlation (0–60° range), the χ² between HIJING+bubbles and HIJING+standard jets is 53 for 8 degrees of freedom (≈8σ effect).
- Doubling the number of mini-jets in HIJING reduces the χ² to 6 (no significant difference from bubbles).
- For a tighter p_t cut (1.2–2.0 GeV/c), the χ² between HIJING+bubbles and HIJING+double jets is 10.
- The width of the net-charge distribution per event (mean 4.0 positive charges) is 9.7 particles for bubbles vs. 10.4 for double jets (consistent with random charge fluctuation).
- For bubbles with 3× the energy per bubble (≈9.75 GeV), the ΔΦ correlation χ² vs. modified HIJING is 1840 (>>20σ), and the charge-fluctuation width is 7.6 (27% reduction from random).
- For the resonance gas model, the ΔΦ correlation χ² vs. HIJING+bubbles is 22 (≈2.5σ) for the standard p_t cut, but rises to 206 (>>20σ) for the tighter p_t cut (1.2–2.0 GeV/c).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ earlier single-bubble treatment (Lindenbaum et al., 2000) and the string-droplet picture of Van Hove (1983, 1987) to the more realistic multiple-bubble case. It directly addresses the charge-fluctuation suppression signal proposed by Jeon & Koch (2000) and Asakawa, Heinz & Mueller (2000), arguing that their predicted factor-of-4 reduction is washed out by color-charge fluctuations and kinematic mixing unless bubbles are localized. The simulations are designed for direct comparison with forthcoming STAR detector data at RHIC for 65 GeV/n Au+Au collisions, and the methods are extendable to higher RHIC energies and LHC.
