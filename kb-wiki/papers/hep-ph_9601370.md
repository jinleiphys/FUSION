# The quark condensate in the GMOR relation

> The quark condensate which enters the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation, is investigated in the framework of one-gluon-exchange models. The usual definition of the quark condensate via the trace of the quark propagator produces a logarithmic divergent condensate. In the product of current mass and condensate, this divergence is precisely compensated by the bare current mass. The finite value of the product in fact does not contradict the relation recently obtained by Cahill and Gunner. Therefore the GMOR relation is still satisfied.

# The quark condensate in the GMOR relation

## Key claim

The product of current quark mass and quark condensate, as defined by the trace of the quark propagator, is finite despite the condensate itself being logarithmically divergent; this finite product satisfies the GMOR relation and agrees with the formula derived by Cahill and Gunner (1996) and by Frank and Roberts (1996), contrary to claims that the GMOR relation fails in one-gluon-exchange models.

## Method

The authors analyze the quark condensate defined via the trace of the quark propagator in the framework of one-gluon-exchange models, where the quark self-energy is obtained from a Dyson-Schwinger equation with an effective gluon propagator that matches perturbative QCD at large momenta. They expand the pion's Bethe-Salpeter equation to leading order in the current quark mass and use a derivative expansion to derive an expression for the product of pion mass squared and decay constant. By expanding the Dyson-Schwinger equation to leading order in the renormalized current mass and integrating over momentum, they show that the cutoff-dependent bare current mass cancels the logarithmic divergence of the condensate, yielding a finite result that matches the standard GMOR relation.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous mass dimension is given by \( d_m = 12 / (33 - 2 N_f) \), where \( N_f \) is the number of quark flavors (equation 5).
- The quark condensate in the chiral limit behaves as \( \langle \bar{q}q \rangle = \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{OPE}(\mu) \, [\ln(\Lambda^2 / \mu^2)]^{d_m} + \ldots \), showing logarithmic divergence with the UV cutoff \( \Lambda \) (equation 6).
- The bare current mass scales as \( m(\Lambda) = m_R(\mu^2) / [\ln(\Lambda^2 / \mu^2)]^{d_m} \), ensuring that the product \( m(\Lambda) \langle \bar{q}q \rangle(\Lambda) = m_R(\mu) \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{OPE}(\mu) \) is finite and cutoff-independent (equations 7 and 8).
- The integral for \( m_\pi^2 f_\pi^2 \) converges because the integrand asymptotically decreases like \( 1/k^8 \) (up to logarithmic corrections), allowing the regulator to be safely removed (equation 12).

## Context

This work addresses a controversy raised by Cahill and Gunner (1996) and by Frank and Roberts (1996), who argued that the GMOR relation fails in one-gluon-exchange models because the quark condensate defined via the trace of the propagator is divergent. The authors show that the divergence is precisely compensated by the cutoff dependence of the bare current mass, a mechanism previously noted by Miransky (1993) in the context of QCD. The result reconciles the one-gluon-exchange model approach with the standard GMOR relation and confirms that the condensate defined via the propagator trace is compatible with the operator-product-expansion definition used in QCD sum rules. This enables continued use of one-gluon-exchange models for meson phenomenology and for studying the density dependence of meson observables in compact star matter.
