# Quasi-Classical Approximation of Electromagnetic Radiation by Fermions embedded in Rigidly Rotating Medium in Strong Magnetic Field

> We develop the quasi-classical (WKB) approximation of the synchrotron radiation by a fermion embedded into uniformly rotating system in external magnetic field. We show that it gives an accurate approximation of the exact expression that we recently obtained at a tiny fraction of the numerical cost. Our results can be used to compute the electromagnetic radiation of the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The quasi-classical (WKB) approximation of synchrotron radiation by fermions in a uniformly rotating system with a strong magnetic field yields accurate results compared to the exact expression at a tiny fraction of the numerical cost, but only for "relatively slow" rotation satisfying \(\Omega^2 \ll |qB|\) where the structure of Landau levels is not completely broken by the lifted degeneracy in the magnetic quantum number \(m\).

## Method

The authors developed a WKB approximation for the Laguerre function \(I_{n,n'}(x)\) that solves the differential equation \(\frac{d^2}{dx^2}(x^{1/2}y(x)) - f_{n,n'}(x)x^{1/2}y(x) = 0\) with potential \(f_{n,n'}(x)\) having two turning points \(x_0 = (\sqrt{n}-\sqrt{n'})^2\) and \(x_0' = (\sqrt{n}+\sqrt{n'})^2\). They constructed a piecewise WKB solution valid in five regions separated by the turning points, using Airy functions near the turning points and exponential or oscillatory forms elsewhere, with normalization fixed by matching to the Sokolov-Ternov approximation. They then expanded the matrix elements and phase space in the ultra-relativistic limit (\(E, E', \omega \gg M, p_z\)) and slow rotation (\(|m\Omega|, |m'\Omega| \ll E, E', \omega\)), replaced summations over final quantum numbers \(n'\) and \(a'\) with integrals, and obtained the quasi-classical expression for the total radiation intensity.

## Key numbers

- The quasi-classical approximation requires \(\omega_B \ll E\), where \(\omega_B/E \sim qB/E^2\) for ultra-relativistic fermions in subcritical magnetic fields.
- The turning points of the \(I\)-function potential are \(x_0 = (\sqrt{n}-\sqrt{n'})^2\) and \(x_0' = (\sqrt{n}+\sqrt{n'})^2\) for large \(n\) and \(n'\).
- The derivative of the potential at the left turning point is \(f'_{n,n'}(x_0) \approx -\sqrt{n n'}/x_0^2\).
- The Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition gives \(p = \min\{n, n'\} + 1\).
- The parameters \(\delta_\alpha\) were fixed to \(\delta_1 = \delta_2 = x_0/10\) and \(\delta_3 = \delta_4 = x_0'/10\), satisfying the WKB condition for \(n, n' > 100\).
- Numerical results were computed for \(n = 1000, 4000, 7000, 10000\) with \(\Omega = 10^{-5}\), \(a = 500\), and \(qB = -0.01\) (in units of fermion mass \(M=1\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exact theory of synchrotron radiation by fermions in rotating systems (Buzzegoli et al., 2022-2024) by developing a computationally efficient quasi-classical approximation. The exact calculations were numerically expensive because rotation lifts the degeneracy of the Landau levels, making analytical expressions more complicated than in the non-rotating case studied by Sokolov and Ternov (1986) and Bordovitsyn. The approximation enables computation of electromagnetic radiation from the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which was recently observed to possess high vorticity collinear with the magnetic field (Csernai et al., 2013-2014; Becattini, 2015; Deng et al., 2016-2020; Jiang et al., 2016; Kolomeitsev, 2018; Xia, 2018). The method follows the Sokolov and Ternov approach but requires significant modifications because rotation introduces an additional \(I_{a,a'}(x)\) function in the matrix element that is not restricted to the region near its turning point.
