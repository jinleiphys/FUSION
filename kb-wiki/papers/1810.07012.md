# Effects of $Δ(1905)5/2^+$ on $K^*Σ$ photoproduction

> The two-channel photoproductions of $γp \to K^{*+} Σ^{0}$ and $γp \to K^{*0} Σ^{+}$ are investigated based on an effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level Born approximation. In addition to the $t$-channel $K$, $κ$, $K^*$ exchanges, the $s$-channel nucleon ($N$) and $Δ$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, $Σ^*$ exchanges, and the generalized contact term, we try to take into account the minimum number of baryon resonances in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the experimental data. It is found that by including the $Δ(1905)5/2^+$ resonance with its mass, width, and helicity amplitudes taken from the Review of Particle Physics [Particle Data Group, C. Patrignani {\it et al.}, Chin. Phys. C {\bf 40}, 100001 (2016)], the calculated differential and total cross sections for these two reactions are in good agreement with the experimental data. An analysis of the reaction mechanisms shows that the cross sections of $γp \to K^{*+}Σ^{0}$ are dominated by the $s$-channel $Δ(1905)5/2^+$ exchange at low energies and $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange at high energies, with the $s$-channel $Δ$ exchange providing significant contributions in the near-threshold region. For $γp \to K^{*0}Σ^{+}$, the angular dependences are dominated by the $t$-channel $K$ exchange at forward angles and the $u$-channel $Σ^*$ exchange at backward angles, with the $s$-channel $Δ$ and $Δ(1905)5/2^+$ exchanges making considerable contributions at low energies. Predictions are given for the beam, target, and recoil asymmetries for both reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the CLAS high-statistics differential and total cross-section data for both $\gamma p \to K^{*+} \Sigma^{0}$ and $\gamma p \to K^{*0} \Sigma^{+}$ can be well described by including only the $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ resonance (a four-star resonance from the Review of Particle Physics) in addition to the standard Born terms, contradicting earlier work that required seven nucleon resonances. The authors hedge that this conclusion is based on cross-section data alone and that spin-observable data would be needed to constrain additional resonances.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach at tree-level Born approximation to construct reaction amplitudes for $\gamma p \to K^{*+} \Sigma^{0}$ and $\gamma p \to K^{*0} \Sigma^{+}$. They included $t$-channel $K$, $\kappa$, and $K^*$ exchanges; $s$-channel nucleon ($N$), $\Delta$, and $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ exchanges; $u$-channel $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma^*$ exchanges; and a generalized contact current to maintain gauge invariance. The resonance parameters (mass, width, helicity amplitudes) for $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ were fixed to the averaged values from the Review of Particle Physics, while hadronic couplings and form-factor cutoffs were fitted to the CLAS differential cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$: $M_R \approx 1880$ MeV, width: $\Gamma_R \approx 330$ MeV, helicity amplitudes: $A_{1/2} \approx 0.022$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $A_{3/2} \approx -0.045$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, all taken from the Review of Particle Physics.
- Fitted hadronic coupling constants for $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$: $g^{(1)}_{R \Sigma K*} = 3.70 \pm 0.03$, $g^{(2)}_{R \Sigma K*} = -9.59 \pm 0.32$, $g^{(3)}_{R \Sigma K*} = 29.29 \pm 0.33$.
- Fitted cutoff parameters: $\Lambda_s = 1358 \pm 2$ MeV (common $s$-channel cutoff), $\Lambda_{K^{*}} = 1233 \pm 26$ MeV ($t$-channel $K^*$ cutoff), $\Lambda_{K,\kappa} = 1197 \pm 56$ MeV ($t$-channel $K$ and $\kappa$ cutoff).
- Coupling constant $g_{\Delta \Sigma K^*}^{(1)} = -8.84 \pm 0.06$ for the $\Delta$ exchange in the $s$-channel.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of $K^*\Sigma$ photoproduction beyond the earlier studies by Kim et al. (2013, 2013), which introduced seven resonances ($N(2080)3/2^-$, $N(2090)1/2^-$, $N(2190)7/2^-$, $N(2200)5/2^-$, $\Delta(2150)1/2^-$, $\Delta(2200)7/2^-$, and $\Delta(2390)7/2^+$) but still left room for improvement in describing the CLAS differential cross-section data. The present work shows that a single resonance, $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$, can achieve a better description with fewer adjustable parameters. The results enable future experimental measurements of beam, target, and recoil asymmetries to further constrain the reaction mechanisms and resonance parameters.
