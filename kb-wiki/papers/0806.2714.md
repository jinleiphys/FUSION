# Microscopic approaches for nuclear Many-Body dynamics: applications to nuclear reactions

> These lecture notes are addressed to PhD student and/or researchers who want a general overview of microscopic approaches based on mean-field and applied to nuclear dynamics. Our goal is to provide a good description of low energy heavy-ion collisions. We present both formal aspects and practical applications of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. The TDHF approach gives a mean field dynamics of the system under the assumption that particles evolve independently in their self-consistent average field. As an example, we study the fusion of both spherical and deformed nuclei with TDHF. We also focus on nucleon transfer which may occur between nuclei below the barrier. These studies allow us to specify the range of applications of TDHF in one hand, and, on the other hand, its intrinsic limitations: absence of tunneling below the Coulomb barrier, missing dissipative effects and/or quantum fluctuations. Time-dependent mean-field theories should be improved to properly account for these effects. Several approaches, generically named "beyond TDHF" are presented which account for instance for pairing and/or direct nucleon-nucleon collisions. Finally we discuss recent progresses in exact ab-initio methods based on the stochastic mean-field concept.

## Key claim

These lecture notes argue that time-dependent mean-field theories, particularly TDHF, provide a good description of low-energy heavy-ion reactions but have intrinsic limitations including absence of tunneling below the Coulomb barrier, missing dissipative effects, and missing quantum fluctuations, requiring "beyond TDHF" extensions to properly account for these effects.

## Method

The authors derive TDHF equations by restricting the variational space to Slater determinants (independent particle states) and applying the Ehrenfest theorem to the one-body density matrix evolution. They then apply TDHF to study fusion of both spherical and deformed nuclei, as well as nucleon transfer below the barrier, using Skyrme-type effective interactions. Finally, they present several "beyond TDHF" approaches including TDHF-Bogoliubov for pairing correlations, extended TDHF for nucleon-nucleon collisions, and stochastic mean-field methods for exact ab-initio calculations.

## Key numbers

- The typical velocity of nucleons in nuclei gives (v/c)^2 ~ 1/10, justifying the non-relativistic treatment
- The mean-free path of a nucleon in nuclear matter is stated to be larger than the size of the nucleus itself, justifying the independent particle hypothesis
- The paper discusses N-body systems with N particles, using Slater determinants formed from N occupied single-particle states

## Context

These lecture notes, presented at the Joliot Curie school in September 2007, provide a pedagogical overview of microscopic mean-field approaches for nuclear dynamics. They extend the foundational work of Dirac (1930) on time-dependent Hartree-Fock and Hartree (1928) and Fock (1930) on static mean-field theory. The notes discuss how TDHF can describe reactions between two nuclei and collective motion in a single nucleus, while also addressing the need for extensions to incorporate pairing correlations and direct nucleon-nucleon collisions that are missing in the standard TDHF framework. The work enables further development of transport theories that incorporate effects beyond the mean-field approximation for low-energy heavy-ion reactions.
