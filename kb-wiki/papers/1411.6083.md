# Recognizing Critical Behavior amidst Minijets at the Large Hadron Collider

> The transition from quarks to hadrons in a heavy-ion collision at high energy is usually studied in two different contexts that involve very different transverse scales: local and non-local. Models that are concerned with the $p_T$ spectra and azimuthal anisotropy belong to the former, i.e., hadronization at a local point in $(η,ϕ)$ space, such as the recombination model. The non-local problem has to do with quark-hadron phase transition where collective behavior through near-neighbor interaction can generate patterns of varying sizes in the $(η,ϕ)$ space. The two types of problems are put together in this paper both as brief reviews separately and to discuss how they are related to each other. In particular, we ask how minijets produced at LHC can affect the investigation of multiplicity fluctuations as signals of critical behavior. It is suggested that the existing data from LHC have sufficient multiplicities in small $p_T$ intervals to make feasible the observation of distinctive features of clustering of soft particles, as well as voids, that characterize the critical behavior at phase transition from quarks to hadrons, without any ambiguity posed by the clustering of jet particles.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that existing LHC data on Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV have sufficiently high multiplicities in small transverse momentum intervals to enable observation of clustering and void patterns characteristic of critical behavior at the quark-hadron phase transition, and that minijets do not pose an ambiguity for this search despite their profuse production at LHC energies.

## Method

The paper combines a review of Ginzburg-Landau theory for second-order and first-order phase transitions with Ising model simulations and the recombination model for hadronization. It analyzes multiplicity fluctuations using normalized factorial moments \(F_q\) and their scaling properties (\(F\)-scaling with exponent \(\nu\)), and examines how shower partons from minijets affect these fluctuations in the \((\eta,\phi)\) space at low \(p_T\) (0.2 to 1 GeV/c). The recombination model is used to separate thermal partons (from the equilibrated medium) from shower partons (fragmentation products of hard and semihard partons), with the shower-parton distribution determined by fitting high-\(p_T\) pion spectra.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent \(\nu = 1.304\) from Ginzburg-Landau theory for second-order phase transitions, independent of GL parameters \(a\) and \(b\) and bin size \(\delta\).
- In the Ising model, \(\nu\) varies from 1.6 at low temperature to 1.0 at \(T = T_c\), with average \(\nu = 1.3\) agreeing with the GL result.
- For first-order phase transitions, \(\nu\) decreases from 1.45 at \(g = 0\) (strong first order) asymptotically to 1.30 at \(g \ge 0.6\) (nearly second order).
- At RHIC (Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV), thermal parton parameters are \(C = 23.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(T = 0.317\) GeV from fitting pion spectra for \(p_T < 3\) GeV/c.
- The ratio \({\cal S}^{\rm LHC}(p_1)/{\cal S}^{\rm RHIC}(p_1)\) for Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV increases from \(\sim 7\) at \(p_1 \sim 0.5\) GeV/c to \(\sim 30\) at \(p_1 \sim 3\) GeV/c.
- The ratio \({\cal S}(p_1)/{\cal T}(p_1)\) at LHC increases from \(\sim 1\) at \(p_1 \sim 0.5\) GeV/c to \(\sim 10^2\) at \(p_1 \sim 6\) GeV/c.
- The erraticity index for \(q=4\) is \(\mu_4 \lesssim 2\) for the critical case in a model with contraction and randomization, but twice larger for the no-contraction case.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach to quark-hadron phase transitions proposed by Hwa and Nazirov (1993) and the Ising model simulations by Cao, Hwa, and Ge (1995), and connects them to the recombination model developed by Hwa and Yang (2003, 2004) for describing \(p_T\) spectra at RHIC and LHC. The paper addresses the open question of whether critical behavior can be observed in heavy-ion collisions despite the presence of minijets, which were studied in the context of shower parton dominance at LHC by Zhang and Hwa (2013). It enables future experimental searches for \(F\)-scaling and erraticity in LHC Pb-Pb data at low \(p_T\), using the scaling exponent \(\nu\) as a measurable signature of the phase transition order and temperature.
