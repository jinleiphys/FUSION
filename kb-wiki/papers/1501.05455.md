# Skyrmions with low binding energies

> Nuclear binding energies are investigated in two variants of the Skyrme model: the first replaces the usual Skyrme term with a term that is sixth order in derivatives, and the second includes a potential that is quartic in the pion fields. Solitons in the first model are shown to deviate significantly from ansätze previously assumed in the literature. The binding energies obtained in both models are lower than those obtained from the standard Skyrme model, and those obtained in the second model are close to the experimental values.

## Key claim
The sextic model and the lightly bound model both produce classical binding energies lower than the standard Skyrme model, but only the lightly bound model yields binding energies close to experimental values; the sextic model remains numerically inaccessible in the parameter regime where binding energies would be realistic (epsilon approximately 0.014), and its energy minimizers deviate significantly from the axially symmetric ansatze previously assumed in the literature.

## Method
The authors numerically minimized static energy functionals for baryon numbers B=1 to 8 in two Skyrme model variants. For the sextic model, they used a cubic lattice with 4th-order finite differences and a quasi-Newton algorithm, tracking minima as the parameter epsilon was decreased from 1 to 0.2; for B=1 and B=2, they additionally used a 2D axial simulated-annealing scheme with a logarithmic radial grid to reach lower epsilon. For the lightly bound model, they used the same 3D lattice method on a 301^3 grid with spacing 0.05, tracking minima as the parameter alpha was varied between 0 and 0.95, and computed binding energies and isoscalar charge radii at alpha=0.95.

## Key numbers
* Sextic model: for B=1 at epsilon=0.2, the energy is approximately 19.74 (2π²) plus a small epsilon-dependent term; the upper bound for any B is E ≤ B(19.74 + 72.36 epsilon).
* Sextic model: the virial constraint D(epsilon) remained below 1.5% for epsilon in [0.2,1] but exploded below epsilon=0.2 for B>1; for the axial simulations, |D(epsilon)| remained below 1%.
* Lightly bound model at alpha=0.95: the B=1 energy is 1.1082 × 8π²; the B=2 energy is 2.2119 × 8π², giving a binding energy per nucleon of 1.84 MeV.
* Lightly bound model at alpha=0.95: binding energies per nucleon for B=3 to 8 range from 3.26 MeV (B=3) to 5.95 MeV (B=8a); the isoscalar charge radius for B=1 is 0.8104 fm and for B=8a is 2.6259 fm.
* Lightly bound model: the virial constraint D(alpha) was at worst 0.01% on numerically determined minima; baryon number was accurate to within 0.001% of the integer value.

## Context
This work extends the study of low-binding-energy Skyrme models, following proposals for a near-BPS sextic model by Adam, Sanchez, and Wereszczynski (2010) and a lightly bound model by Harland (2014). The sextic model results contradict earlier phenomenological studies by Adam, Sanchez, and Wereszczynski (2010, 2011) and Bonenfant, Harbour, and Marleau (2012) that assumed axially symmetric BPS skyrmions; the authors show those ansatze are not restricted harmonic and do not match the numerical minima. The lightly bound model enables a particle-like interpretation of skyrmions as subsets of a face-centred cubic lattice, in contrast to the traditional Skyrme model where individual nucleons are not discernible. The results open the possibility of quantizing the lightly bound model to obtain realistic nuclear spectra and binding energies.
