# Effective field theory for deformed odd-mass nuclei

> We develop an effective field theory (EFT) for deformed odd-mass nuclei. These are described as an axially symmetric core to which a nucleon is coupled. In the coordinate system fixed to the core the nucleon is subject to an axially symmetric potential. Power counting is based on the separation of scales between low-lying rotations and higher-lying states of the core. In leading order, core and nucleon are coupled by universal derivative terms. These comprise a covariant derivative and gauge potentials which account for Coriolis forces and relate to Berry-phase phenomena. At leading order, the EFT combines the particle-rotor and Nilsson models. We work out the EFT up to next-to-leading order and illustrate the results in $^{239}$Pu and $^{187}$Os. At leading order, odd-mass nuclei with rotational band heads that are close in energy and differ by one unit of angular momentum are triaxially deformed. For band heads that are well separated in energy, triaxiality becomes a subleading effect. The EFT developed in this paper presents a model-independent approach to the particle-rotor system that is capable of systematic improvement.

# Effective field theory for deformed odd-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for odd-mass deformed nuclei that treats the system as an axially symmetric core coupled to a nucleon, with the key hedge being that the power counting relies on a separation of scales between low-lying rotations (scale ξ) and higher-lying core excitations (scale Λ), and that the EFT does not provide an expansion in powers of Ω/Λ because the nucleon potential is not constructed systematically, instead, single-particle energies are taken from data.

## Method

The authors construct an EFT based on emergent symmetry breaking from SO(3) to axial SO(2), using the coset SO(3)/SO(2) parametrized by angles (θ, φ) to describe the rotor, and a fermion field operator in the body-fixed frame with an axially symmetric potential. Power counting assigns the low-energy scale ξ to the rotor's angular velocity and the scale Ω to single-nucleon band-head spacings, with the breakdown scale Λ set by the first non-rotational excitation of the core. The nucleon-rotor coupling enters via a covariant derivative that introduces gauge potentials accounting for Coriolis forces and Berry-phase phenomena, with the EFT worked out to next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- Rotational constant A ≈ 7 keV for ²³⁸Pu, corresponding to the low-energy scale ξ ≈ 40 keV (the energy of the I=2 state)
- Breakdown scale Λ ≈ 600 keV for ²³⁸Pu, set by the K^π=1⁻ band head
- Single-particle excitation energy Ω ≈ 300 keV for ²³⁹Pu (the 5/2⁺ band head relative to the 1/2⁺ ground state)
- Rotational constant C₀⁻¹ ranges from about 1 MeV for ⁸Be, 0.5 MeV for ²⁴Mg, 0.2 MeV for ³⁴Mg, 30 keV for rare earth nuclei, to 15 keV for actinides
- Breakdown energy Λ ranges from about 17 MeV for ⁸Be, 4 MeV for ²⁴Mg, 1 MeV for rare earth nuclei, to about 0.5 MeV for actinides
- Single-particle level spacing Ω is about 1.7 MeV for ⁹Be, 0.6 MeV for ²⁵Mg, hundreds of keV for rare earths, and tens to hundreds of keV for actinides

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach previously applied to even-even deformed nuclei by Papenbrock (2011, 2014, 2015) and to the particle-rotor model by Chen (2020), but differs by treating the core as axially symmetric rather than triaxially deformed. The EFT unifies the particle-rotor model and the Nilsson model at leading order, and shows that triaxiality in odd-mass nuclei can arise as a leading-order effect when rotational band heads are close in energy and differ by one unit of angular momentum, while becoming subleading when band heads are well separated. The approach enables systematic improvement of the particle-rotor system and reveals connections to Berry-phase phenomena and gauge potentials previously studied in molecular physics by Wilczek (1984) and Jackiw (1986).
