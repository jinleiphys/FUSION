# $B_c$ mesons and their properties on the light front

> We investigate the unequal mass relativistic bound state system, the $B_c$ mesons, in a light-front Hamiltonian formalism. We adopt an effective Hamiltonian based on soft-wall light-front holography, together with a longitudinal confinement that was first introduced for heavy quarkonium. We also include the one gluon-exchange interaction with a running coupling, which produces the spin structure. We present the mass spectrum and light-front wave functions. We also use the light-front wave functions to calculate decay constants, charge and momentum densities, as well as distribution amplitudes. The results are compared with experiments and other theoretical approaches, all of which are in reasonable agreement.

## Key claim

The authors apply a light-front Hamiltonian model, previously tuned to charmonium and bottomonium, to the unequal-mass \(B_c\) system without introducing any additional free parameters, and find reasonable agreement with experiment and other theories, though the model's predictions for the \(B_c\) mass spectrum are systematically lower than lattice QCD and experimental values.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front eigenvalue equation \(H_{\text{eff}}|\psi_h\rangle = M_h^2|\psi_h\rangle\) using an effective Hamiltonian that combines soft-wall light-front holographic confinement, a longitudinal confinement term, and a one-gluon-exchange interaction with a running coupling \(\alpha_s(Q^2) = 1/[\beta_0 \ln(Q^2/\Lambda^2 + \tau)]\). The light-front wave functions are expanded in a basis of 2D harmonic oscillator functions (transverse) and Jacobi polynomials (longitudinal), with basis truncation parameters \(N_{\text{max}} = L_{\text{max}} = 32\). The model parameters (quark masses \(m_c = 1.603\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.902\) GeV, confining strength \(\kappa_{b\bar{c}} = 1.196\) GeV) are taken from prior charmonium and bottomonium calculations without further fitting.

## Key numbers

- Ground state pseudoscalar decay constant: \(f_{B_c} = 523(62)\) MeV (uncertainty from basis truncation sensitivity, \(\Delta f = 2|f(N_{\text{max}}=32) - f(N_{\text{max}}=24)|\))
- Ground state vector decay constant: \(f_{B_c^*} = 474(42)\) MeV
- r.m.s. deviation of calculated masses from two experimental \(B_c\) states: 37 MeV
- Mean spread \(\overline{\delta_j M}\) quantifying rotational symmetry violation for \(B_c\): 6 MeV (smaller than charmonium at 17 MeV and bottomonium at 8 MeV)
- r.m.s. radii of longitudinal momentum density \(\rho_g(\vec{b}_\perp)\): \(J/\Psi\) = 0.84 GeV\(^{-1}\), \(B_c (1^3S_1)\) = 0.58 GeV\(^{-1}\), \(\Upsilon\) = 0.57 GeV\(^{-1}\)
- Basis UV regulator: \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} = b\sqrt{N_{\text{max}}} \simeq 6.77\) GeV; IR regulator: \(\lambda_{\text{IR}} = b/\sqrt{N_{\text{max}}} \simeq 0.21\) GeV
- Running coupling parameters: \(\Lambda = 0.13\) GeV, \(\tau = 12.3\) giving \(\alpha_s(0) = 0.6\)

## Context

This work extends the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, previously applied to charmonium and bottomonium by Li et al. (2016, 2017), to the unequal-mass \(B_c\) system, testing the predictive power of the model since all parameters are fixed from the equal-mass heavy quarkonia. The results enable further studies of \(B_c\) properties such as decay constants, charge densities, and distribution amplitudes, and provide a foundation for extending the relativistic light-front holographic framework to other flavored mesons. The authors compare their results with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Davies et al. 1996, Lattice 2004, 2005), QCD sum rules (Baker 2014, Wang 2013), light-front quark models (PhysRevD.80.054016), and other approaches.
