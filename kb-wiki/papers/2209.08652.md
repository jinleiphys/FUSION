# The causality and stability of relativistic spin-hydrodynamics

> We study the causality and stability of relativistic hydrodynamics with the inclusion of the spin degree of freedom as a hydrodynamic field. We consider two specific models of spin-hydrodynamics for this purpose. A linear mode analysis for static background shows that a first-order dissipative spin-hydrodynamics remains acausal and admits instabilities. Besides, it is found that the inclusion of the spin field in hydrodynamics leads to new kinds of linear modes in the system. These new modes also exhibit instability and acausal behavior. The second model of the spin-hydrodynamics that we have considered here is equivalent to a particular second-order conventional hydrodynamics with no dissipative effects. For a static background, it is found that the linear modes of this model support the sound waves only. However, when the background has constant vorticity, then the model admits instability and acausality in certain situations. It is found that the spin-dynamics have an effect on the hydrodynamic response of the fluid. These findings point toward the need for a causal and stable theory with spin as a hydrodynamic field to describe the spin-polarized fluid.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first-order dissipative spin-hydrodynamics model remains acausal and unstable for a static background, while a second-order non-dissipative spin-hydrodynamics model (equivalent to conventional second-order hydrodynamics) supports only sound waves in a static background but admits instability and acausality when the background has constant vorticity.

## Method

The authors perform a linear mode analysis of two specific spin-hydrodynamics models around a static homogeneous equilibrium background with zero spin-polarization and spin-potential tensors. For the first-order dissipative model, they derive dispersion relations for longitudinal and transverse modes by solving linearized conservation equations for energy-momentum, angular momentum, and charge. For the second-order non-dissipative model (equivalent to conventional second-order hydrodynamics), they analyze linear modes both for a static background and for a background with constant vorticity in the x and y directions.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal sound mode dispersion relation is ω_{2l} = ± i c_s k + [ (ζ + 4η/3)/(2h_0) + λ(1/(ε_T T_0) + c_s^2/h_0) - κ n_0 ε_n/(ε_T T_0 h_0) ] k^2, where the mode can be stable if the bracketed term is non-negative
- A new longitudinal spin mode appears with dispersion ω_{3l} = -8λ/χ_b + (χ_1/(χ_b T_0)) k^2, which can be unstable when 8λ > (χ_1/T_0) k^2
- The transverse shear mode dispersion is ω_{1t} = (γ+η)/h_0 k^2
- A transverse spin mode has dispersion ω_{3t} = 8γ/χ_s + (χ_1/(T_0 χ_s)) k^2
- An unstable mode appears with ω_{4l} = ω_{4t} = -h_0/(κ+λ), independent of wave-vector k

## Context

This work extends the analysis of causality and stability problems known from first-order relativistic Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics (Hiscock 1985) to the recently developed spin-hydrodynamics frameworks. The authors examine the models of Hattori (2019) and Li (2020), where the latter demonstrated equivalence between second-order conventional hydrodynamics and spin-hydrodynamics in the dissipationless limit. The findings point toward the need for a causal and stable theory with spin as a hydrodynamic field to describe spin-polarized fluids produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the STAR collaboration has measured Λ hyperon polarization.
