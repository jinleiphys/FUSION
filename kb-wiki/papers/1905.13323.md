# Ultracentral Collisions of Small and Deformed Systems at RHIC: ${U} {U}$, ${d} {Au}$, ${}^{9}{Be} {Au}$, ${}^{9}{Be} {}^{9}{Be}$, ${}^{3}{He} {}^{3}{He}$, and ${}^{3}{He} {Au}$ Collisions

> In this paper, we study a range of collision systems involving deformed ions and compare the elliptic and triangular flow harmonics produced in a hydrodynamics scenario versus a color glass condensate (CGC) scenario. For the hydrodynamics scenario, we generate initial conditions using TRENTO and work within a linear response approximation to obtain the final flow harmonics. For the CGC scenario, we use the explicit calculation of two-gluon correlations taken in the high-$p_T$ ``(semi)dilute-(semi)dilute'' regime to express the flow harmonics in terms of the density profile of the collision. We consider ultracentral collisions of deformed ions as a testbed for these comparisons because the difference between tip-on-tip and side-on-side collisions modifies the multiplicity dependence in both scenarios, even at zero impact parameter. We find significant qualitative differences in the multiplicity dependence obtained in the initial conditions+hydrodynamics scenario and the CGC scenario, allowing these collisions of deformed ions to be used as a powerful discriminator between models. We also find that sub-nucleonic fluctuations have a systematic effect on the elliptic and triangular flow harmonics which are most discriminating in $0-1\%$ ultracentral symmetric collisions of small deformed ions and in $0-10\%$ $\mathrm{d} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}$ collisions. The collision systems we consider are ${}^{238}\mathrm{U} {}^{238}\mathrm{U}$, $\mathrm{d} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}$, ${}^{9}\mathrm{Be} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}$, ${}^{9}\mathrm{Be} {}^{9}\mathrm{Be}$, ${}^{3}\mathrm{He} {}^{3}\mathrm{He}$, and ${}^{3}\mathrm{He} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}$.

## Key claim

Ultracentral collisions of deformed ions can serve as a powerful discriminator between initial conditions plus hydrodynamics and color glass condensate (CGC) scenarios because the two frameworks predict qualitatively opposite correlations between elliptic flow and multiplicity, even at zero impact parameter.

## Method

The authors generated initial conditions using the TRENTO model for a range of collision systems, then computed elliptic and triangular flow harmonics under two scenarios: (1) a hydrodynamics scenario using linear response approximation from initial eccentricities, and (2) a CGC scenario using explicit calculations of two-gluon correlations in the high-\(p_T\) (semi)dilute-(semi)dilute regime. For the hydrodynamics scenario, the ratio of cumulants in multiplicity sub-bins was obtained from \(\sqrt{\langle \varepsilon_n^2 \rangle_i / \langle \varepsilon_n^2 \rangle_{0-1\%}}\), while for the CGC scenario the ratio was expressed in terms of moments of nuclear profile functions \(\mathcal{I}_\alpha = \int d^2 x_\bot T_A^\alpha(\vec{x}_\bot) T_B^\alpha(\vec{x}_\bot)\).

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{238}\mathrm{U} {}^{238}\mathrm{U}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=193\) GeV, the CGC picture predicts a positive correlation of \(v_2\{2\}\) with multiplicity, while the initial conditions+hydrodynamics picture predicts an anticorrelation; the STAR data from Adamczyk (2015) favor the hydrodynamics picture.
- The nucleon Gaussian width was varied from \(\sigma = 0.3\) to \(0.51\) fm, with a preference for \(\sigma = 0.3\) fm in small systems from Giacalone (2017).
- Sub-nucleonic fluctuations were studied using parameters tuned to pPb collisions at LHC: \(p=0\), \(k=0.19\), \(n_c = 6\), \(w = 0.855\) fm, \(r_{cp} = 0.81\) fm, \(v = 0.43\) fm, \(d_{min} = 0.81\) fm.
- The most discriminating effects from sub-nucleonic fluctuations appear in \(0-1\%\) ultracentral symmetric collisions of small deformed ions and in \(0-10\%\) d \({}^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) collisions.

## Context

This work extends the prediction of opposite multiplicity dependence of elliptic flow in ultracentral \({}^{238}\mathrm{U} {}^{238}\mathrm{U}\) collisions from Kovchegov (2013) to smaller deformed systems including \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be} {}^{9}\mathrm{Be}\), \({}^{3}\mathrm{He} {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\), d \({}^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), and \({}^{3}\mathrm{He} {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}\). Previous hydrodynamic predictions for uranium were made by Moreland (2014), Goldschmidt (2015), and Schenke (2019). The results enable experimental discrimination between initial-state CGC correlations and final-state hydrodynamic flow in small collision systems where both scenarios remain viable, complementing prior studies of nuclear deformation effects in XeXe collisions by Giacalone (2017) and CMS (2018).
