# Isothermal vs. isentropic description of protoneutron stars in the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory

> We study the structure of hadronic protoneutron stars within the finite temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theoretical approach. Assuming beta-equilibrated nuclear matter with nucleons and leptons in the stellar core, with isothermal or isentropic profile, we show that particle populations and equation of state are very similar. As far as the maximum mass is concerned, we find that its value turns out to be almost independent on T, while a slight decrease is observed in the isentropic case, due to the enhanced proton fraction in the high density range.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a hadronic protoneutron star is almost independent of temperature in the isothermal case, but shows a slight decrease in the isentropic case due to an enhanced proton fraction at high density; this result contradicts the earlier findings of Prakash et al., where the critical mass increased with entropy.

## Method

The authors used the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus Urbana three-body forces, employing the Frozen Correlations Approximation. They solved for beta-equilibrated, neutrino-trapped nuclear matter with nucleons and leptons, calculating the equation of state under either isothermal (fixed T) or isentropic (fixed entropy per baryon S/A = 1 or 2) conditions, and then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}, with binding energy E/A ≈ -16 MeV.
- Electron lepton number at onset of trapping: Y_e ≈ 0.4.
- Entropy per baryon values used: S/A = 1 and S/A = 2.
- Temperature range considered: up to 30-40 MeV, with specific calculations shown at T = 20 MeV and T = 40 MeV.
- Cold crust matching performed in the density range 0.001 fm^{-3} < rho < 0.08 fm^{-3} (Negele and Vautherin) and outer crust at rho < 0.001 fm^{-3} (Feynman, Metropolis, and Teller; Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone approach previously applied to nuclear matter and nuclei (Burgio et al. 2000, 2002) to the study of protoneutron stars, comparing isothermal and isentropic descriptions. The results contradict the earlier findings of Prakash et al. (1997), who found that the critical mass increases with entropy using a mostly local interaction; the authors attribute the difference to the temperature-dependent non-local single-particle potentials in their microscopic approach. The finding that protoneutron star maximum masses are slightly smaller than cold neutron star masses excludes a delayed collapse during cooling, and the work enables more realistic modeling of supernova neutrino signatures and the fate of supernova progenitors.
