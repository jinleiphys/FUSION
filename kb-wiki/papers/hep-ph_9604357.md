# Neutrino Oscillations and Energy-Momentum Conservation

> A description of neutrino oscillation phenomena is presented which is based on relativistic quantum mechanics and includes both entangled state and source dependent aspects, unlike both of the conventional approaches which use either equal energies or equal momenta for the different neutrino mass eigenstates. To second order in the neutrino masses, the standard result is recovered thus showing an absence of source dependence to this order. The time dependence of the wavefunction is found to be crucial to recovering the conventional result. An ambiguity appears at fourth order in the neutrino masses which generally leads to source dependence, but the standard formula can be promoted to this order by a plausible convention.

## Key claim

The standard neutrino oscillation formula is recovered to second order in neutrino masses from a fully covariant, entangled-state treatment that respects both energy and momentum conservation, contrary to conventional approaches that arbitrarily assume either equal energies or equal momenta for the different mass eigenstates. An ambiguity appears at fourth order that can introduce source dependence, but the standard formula can be promoted to this order by defining the oscillation phase in terms of the average momentum rather than the average energy of the neutrino mass eigenstates.

## Method

The author starts from a two-body decay source at rest (e.g., pion decay) and writes the entangled state of the neutrino mass eigenstates and the recoiling source final state, with fixed invariant mass of the recoiling system. The time-evolved neutrino wavefunction is then projected onto the initial flavor state to obtain the persistence probability, expanding all kinematic quantities to fourth order in the neutrino masses. To connect the oscillation phase to the experimentally measured distance, the author averages the classical velocities of the two mass eigenstate components to infer a transit time, then substitutes this relation into the probability expression.

## Key numbers

- The standard oscillation formula \(P_{conv.}(x) = 1 - \sin^2(2\theta) \sin^2[\Delta m^2 x / (4k)]\) is recovered to second order in neutrino masses, with \(k \approx E_\nu\).
- The source-dependent correction factor at fourth order is \(1 + (m_1^2 + m_2^2)(M^2 + M_f^2) / [2 (M^2 - M_f^2)^2]\).
- The coefficient of the sum of neutrino squared masses in the source-dependent term varies from 170 GeV\(^{-2}\) for muon-neutrinos from \(\pi \rightarrow \mu\) decay to 0.21 GeV\(^{-2}\) for those from \(K \rightarrow \mu\) two-body decay.
- The average velocity of the two neutrino components to second order is \(v_{av} = 1 - (m_1^2 + m_2^2) M^2 / (M^2 - M_f^2)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on neutrino oscillation derivations raised by Lipkin (1995), who argued oscillations are a momentum/spatial phenomenon, by showing that both energy/time and momentum/spatial dependence are required to recover the standard result. The analysis uses a covariant entangled-state formalism not previously applied to neutrino oscillations, and the results are consistent with the conventional equal-energy or equal-momentum approaches of Pontecorvo (1968) and Bilenky and Pontecorvo (1978) at leading order. The paper enables a precise framework for evaluating possible source-dependent corrections in future high-precision experiments, particularly for two-body decay sources such as pions and kaons.
