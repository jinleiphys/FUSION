# Light $q\bar{q}$ Resonances: Spectrum and Decays

> I give a brief introduction to light $q\bar{q}$ resonances. The properties of $q\bar{q}$ states are discussed in the context of the constituent quark model, including predictions for mass and partial decay widths. Problems and puzzles in the meson spectrum are pointed out with an emphasis on how an 8+ GeV photon beam can address these. I also point out some areas where I believe theoretical study would be useful.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model generally gives a good description of light meson spectroscopy, but there are far too many missing states (especially radial and orbital excitations) and numerous puzzles that may point to physics beyond this simple picture; progress requires both finding the missing states and making detailed measurements, which will take unprecedented statistics to perform the necessary partial wave analysis.

## Method

The paper reviews the constituent quark model description of light q\bar{q} mesons, including the confinement potential (Coulomb-plus-linear form) and spin-dependent interactions (hyperfine and spin-orbit). It surveys the known meson spectrum, identifies missing states and puzzles (such as extra scalar and tensor mesons), and discusses decay models (the ^3P_0 model and flux-tube-breaking model) as tools to probe internal structure. The author also examines how an 8+ GeV photon beam could address these issues through various photoproduction mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The confining potential has the form V(r) = -(4/3)(α_s(r)/r) + br, with b the slope of the linear confining piece.
- The ^3F_4 s\bar{s} state has a total predicted width of 132 MeV from the ^3P_0 model, with dominant decays to [K*(892) K̅*(892)]_{2,D} at 44 MeV and [K K̅]_{0,G} at 29 MeV.
- The ^3F_2 s\bar{s} state has a total predicted width of 391 MeV, with the largest partial width being [K_1(1270) K̅ + c.c.]_{1,P} at 187 MeV.
- The CLEO collaboration placed a limit of Γ_{γγ} B_{KK}(ξ(2220)) ≤ 1.3 eV, giving Γ_{γγ} ≤ 5 eV, much smaller than the typical quark model expectation of ~1 keV.
- The η_2(1D) q\bar{q} state has predicted partial widths of 147 MeV to ρρ, 46 MeV to ωω, and 264 MeV to a_2 π, while the η_2 hybrid has zero branching fraction to ρρ and ωω and 160 MeV to a_2 π.

## Context

This paper extends the constituent quark model work of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and reviews the status of light meson spectroscopy in the mid-1990s. It highlights puzzles such as the η(1440), f_1/E(1440), f_J/θ(1710), and the scalar meson sector (a_0(980), f_0(980), f_0(1500)), which have been discussed in the context of glueball candidates (e.g., lattice gauge theory calculations) and K̅K molecule interpretations (Weinstein and Isgur, 1990). The paper enables future experimental searches for missing q\bar{q} states and exotic candidates using high-statistics photoproduction facilities, and calls for improved theoretical understanding of coupled channel effects and final state interactions.
