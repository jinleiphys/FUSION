# New Geiger-Nuttall law for two-proton radioactivity

> In the present work, combining with the Geiger-Nuttall law, a two-parameter empirical formula is proposed to study the two-proton (2p) radioactivity. Using this formula, the calculated 2p radioactivity half-lives are in good agreement with the experimental data as well as the calculated ones obtained by Goncalves et al: ([Phys. Lett. B 774, 14 (2017)]) using the effective liquid drop model (ELDM), Sreeja et al: ([Eur. Phys. J. A 55, 33 (2019)]) using a four-parameter empirical formula and Cui et al: ([Phys. Rev. C 101: 014301 (2020)]) using a generalized liquid drop model (GLDM). In addition, this two-parameter empirical formula is extended to predict the half-lives of 22 possible 2p radioactivity candidates, whose the 2p radioactivity released energy Q2p>0, obtained from the latest evaluated atomic mass table AME2016. The predicted results have good consistency with ones using other theoretical models such as the ELDM, GLDM and four-parameter empirical formula.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a two-parameter empirical formula based on the Geiger-Nuttall law that can reproduce experimental two-proton (2p) radioactivity half-lives, but only for nuclei where the orbital angular momentum \(l\) taken away by the two emitted protons is zero; for nonzero \(l\) values the formula relies on predicted half-lives from other models rather than experimental data.

## Method

The authors fit a two-parameter formula \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = 2.032\,(Z_d^{0.8} + l^{0.25})\,Q_{2p}^{-1/2} - 26.832\) to a database consisting of experimental half-lives of five true 2p radioactivity nuclei (\({}^{19}\)Mg, \({}^{45}\)Fe, \({}^{48}\)Ni, \({}^{54}\)Zn, \({}^{67}\)Kr) with \(l=0\) and predicted half-lives of seven nuclei with \(l \neq 0\) taken from Goncalves et al. (2017). The exponent \(\beta = 0.25\) on the orbital angular momentum term was chosen by minimizing the standard deviation between the database and calculated half-lives. The formula was then used to predict half-lives for 22 additional 2p radioactivity candidates with \(Q_{2p} > 0\) from the AME2016 mass table.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{48}\)Ni with \(Q_{2p} = 1.290\) MeV, the calculated \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}\) is \(-2.59\) s, giving a logarithmic error \(\log_{10} HF = 0.07\) compared to the experimental value of \(-2.52\) s.
- For \({}^{45}\)Fe with \(Q_{2p} = 1.154\) MeV, the calculated \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}\) is \(-2.79\) s, with \(\log_{10} HF = 0.24\) compared to the experimental value of \(-2.55\) s.
- For \({}^{19}\)Mg with \(Q_{2p} = 0.750\) MeV, the calculated \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}\) is \(-12.03\) s, with \(\log_{10} HF = 0.63\) compared to the experimental value of \(-11.40\) s.
- For \({}^{67}\)Kr with \(Q_{2p} = 1.690\) MeV, the calculated \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}\) is \(-0.58\) s, with \(\log_{10} HF = -1.12\) compared to the experimental value of \(-1.70\) s.
- The fitted parameters are \(a = 2.032\) and \(b = -26.832\) in the formula \(\log_{10} T_{1/2} = a\,(Z_d^{0.8} + l^{0.25})\,Q_{2p}^{-1/2} + b\).

## Context

This work extends the Geiger-Nuttall law, previously applied to alpha decay, cluster radioactivity, and proton radioactivity by the same group (Liu et al., previous work), to two-proton radioactivity. It directly builds on the four-parameter empirical formula of Sreeja et al. (2019) and the effective liquid drop model (ELDM) calculations of Goncalves et al. (2017), as well as the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) of Cui et al. (2020). The predicted half-lives for 22 candidates may guide future experimental searches for new 2p radioactivity emitters.
