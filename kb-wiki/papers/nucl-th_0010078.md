# Field Redefinitions at Finite Density

> The apparent dependence of nuclear matter observables on off-shell properties of the two-nucleon potential is re-examined in the context of the effective field theory (EFT) approach. Finite density (thermodynamic) observables are invariant under field redefinitions, which extends the well-known theorem about the invariance of S-matrix elements. Simple examples demonstrate how field redefinitions can shift contributions between purely off-shell two-body interactions and many-body forces, leaving both scattering and finite-density observables unchanged. If only the transformed two-body potentials are kept, however, the nuclear matter binding curves will depend on the off-shell part (generating ``Coester bands''). The correspondence between field redefinitions and unitary transformations, which have traditionally been used to generate ``phase-equivalent'' nucleon-nucleon potentials, is also demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite-density (thermodynamic) observables are invariant under field redefinitions, extending the well-known equivalence theorem for S-matrix elements. The apparent dependence of nuclear matter binding curves on off-shell properties of the two-nucleon potential arises only when transformed two-body potentials are kept without the corresponding many-body forces that field redefinitions also generate.

## Method

The authors use a functional integral formalism to show how local field redefinitions of nonrelativistic fermion fields change the effective Lagrangian, generate a Jacobian, and produce additional source terms. They extend the standard equivalence theorem proof from S-matrix elements to thermodynamic observables by considering the Euclidean path integral formulation of the partition function and thermodynamic potential. Simple examples with a dilute Fermi gas illustrate how field redefinitions shift contributions between off-shell two-body interactions and many-body forces, leaving both scattering and finite-density observables unchanged when all induced terms are consistently included.

## Key numbers

- The paper is a theoretical analysis with no original numerical results; it discusses the structure of effective field theories and field redefinitions rather than presenting new numerical calculations.

## Context

This work extends the equivalence theorem of Haag (1958) and Coleman (1969) from S-matrix invariance to thermodynamic observables, resolving the long-standing puzzle of "Coester bands" first observed by Coester (1970) and Srivastava (1970), where phase-equivalent nucleon-nucleon potentials gave different nuclear matter binding energies. The analysis connects field redefinitions to unitary transformations used by Ekstein (1960) to generate phase-equivalent potentials, and builds on the path-integral treatment of field redefinitions by Arzt (1995). The results enable consistent EFT calculations of nuclear matter where off-shell ambiguities are eliminated by including all many-body forces required by the power counting, similar to how Bedaque (1998) showed that a three-body counterterm resolves the Phillips line in the triton.
