# Drag suppression in anomalous chiral media

> We study a heavy impurity moving longitudinal with the direction of an external magnetic field in an anomalous chiral medium. Such system would carry a non-dissipative current of chiral magnetic effect associated with the anomaly. We show, by generalizing Landau's criterion for superfluidity, that the "anomalous component" which gives rise to the anomalous transport will {\it not} contribute to the drag experienced by an impurity. We argue on a very general basis that those systems with a strong magnetic field would exhibit an interesting transport phenomenon -- the motion of the heavy impurity is frictionless, in analogy to the case of a superfluid. We demonstrate and confirm our general results with two complementary examples: weakly coupled chiral fermion gases and strongly interacting chiral liquids.

## Key claim

The authors show that in a chiral medium with a strong external magnetic field, the "anomalous component" responsible for the chiral magnetic effect will not contribute to the drag force on a heavy impurity moving parallel to the magnetic field, provided the impurity velocity is below a characteristic value; this frictionless motion is analogous to superfluidity but arises from the chiral dispersion of zero modes rather than from a Bose condensate.

## Method

The authors generalize Landau's criterion for superfluidity to anomalous chiral media, analyzing the kinematic constraint for momentum transfer between a heavy impurity and the medium. They compute the drag force via Fermi's golden rule using the spectral density of density fluctuations, first for a weakly coupled chiral fermion gas where only the lowest Landau level contributes, and then for a strongly interacting chiral fluid using hydrodynamic constitutive relations that incorporate the chiral magnetic effect and chiral magnetic wave propagation.

## Key numbers

* For a weakly coupled chiral fermion gas in a strong magnetic field, the drag force vanishes for impurity velocity \(v < 1\) (the Fermi velocity, set to the speed of light).
* For a strongly interacting chiral fluid, the drag force vanishes for \(v < v_{\chi}\), where \(v_{\chi} = C_A B / \chi\) is the speed of the chiral magnetic wave, with \(C_A = 1/2\pi^2\).
* In the strong magnetic field limit (\(eB \gg \mu^2, T^2\)), \(v_{\chi} \to 1\) and \(\chi \to C_A B\), so the critical velocity approaches unity in both examples.
* The longitudinal diffusion coefficient scales as \(D_L \sim B^{-1}\) and the transverse diffusion coefficient as \(D_T \sim B^{-3}\) in the large magnetic field limit.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of non-dissipative anomalous transport by connecting the chiral magnetic effect to a drag-free condition analogous to Landau's criterion for superfluidity, which was originally developed for Bose liquids. The results complement studies of drag in chiral plasmas with weak magnetic fields by Rajagopal (2015) and Stephanov (2015), where drag force is non-zero, and show that in the strong field limit the critical velocity is determined by the chiral magnetic wave speed identified by Kharzeev (2010). The findings enable further investigation of impurity dynamics in Weyl semimetals and quark-gluon plasma under strong magnetic fields.
