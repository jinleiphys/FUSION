# What can radiative decays of the X(3872) teach us about its nature?

> Starting from the hypothesis that the X(3872) is a $D\bar D^*$ molecule, we discuss the radiative decays of the X(3872) into $γJ/ψ$ and $γψ'$ from an effective field theory point of view. We show that radiative decays are very weakly sensitive to the long-range structure of the X(3872). In particular, contrary to earlier claims, we argue that the experimentally determined ratio of the mentioned branching fractions is not in conflict with a wave function of the X(3872) that is dominated by the $D\bar D^*$ hadronic molecular component.

# What can radiative decays of the X(3872) teach us about its nature?

## Key claim
Contrary to earlier claims (Swanson 2004), the experimentally measured ratio of branching fractions for X(3872) → γψ' over X(3872) → γJ/ψ is not in conflict with a wave function of the X(3872) that is dominated by the D\(\bar D\)* hadronic molecular component, because radiative decays are very weakly sensitive to the long-range structure of the X(3872) and are instead dominated by short-range contributions.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective field theory for the radiative decays X(3872) → γJ/ψ and X(3872) → γψ', assuming the X(3872) is a D\(\bar D\)* hadronic molecule. They computed the loop amplitudes from triangle diagrams involving charmed mesons (D and D*) using a covariant approach with heavy quark spin symmetry, and included a contact counterterm to absorb divergences. The partial widths and their ratio were evaluated as functions of the unknown coupling constants of the X(3872) to charmed mesons and of J/ψ and ψ' to charmed meson pairs, with the renormalisation scale varied to estimate the size of short-range contributions.

## Key numbers
- The ratio R = Γ(X(3872) → γψ') / Γ(X(3872) → γJ/ψ) was measured by LHCb as 2.46 ± 0.64 ± 0.29 (Aaij 2014).
- For a pure molecular X(3872) with contact terms set to zero, the calculated ratio R depends on the renormalisation scale μ: R = 0.39 (g₂'/g₂)² at μ = m_X/2, R = 0.21 (g₂'/g₂)² at μ = m_X, and R = 0.14 (g₂'/g₂)² at μ = 2m_X, where g₂ and g₂' are the couplings of J/ψ and ψ' to charmed mesons.
- The partial width for X(3872) → γJ/ψ at μ = m_X is 23.5 (r_x r_g)² keV, where r_x and r_g are normalised coupling ratios defined relative to reference values |x_nr⁽⁰⁾| = 0.97 GeV^{-1/2} and |g₂⁽⁰⁾| = 2 GeV^{-3/2}.
- The partial width for X(3872) → γψ' at μ = m_X is 4.9 (r_x r_g')² keV.
- A value of g₂'/g₂ ∼ 3 is sufficient to bring R in accordance with the LHCb measurement for a purely molecular X(3872).
- Quark model estimates for a 2³P₁ c\(\bar c\) charmonium give Γ(X → γJ/ψ) ≈ 50 keV and Γ(X → γψ') ≈ 100 keV, with R ≈ 2.

## Context
This work extends the debate on the nature of the X(3872), which was discovered by Belle in 2003 and has been interpreted as a D\(\bar D\)* molecule, a radial excitation of χ_c1(2P) charmonium (Barnes 2005), a tetraquark, or a mixture. The authors directly challenge the claim of Swanson (2004) that radiative decays are sensitive to the X(3872) structure, and qualitatively confirm the findings of Dong (2008, 2009) that an enhanced γψ' decay is compatible with a predominantly molecular nature. The results imply that to determine the molecular component of the X(3872), one must instead measure decays sensitive to long-distance physics, such as X → D\(\bar D\)γ or X → D\(\bar D\)π (Voloshin 2003, Fleming 2007, Hanhart 2007, Guo 2014, Kalashnikova 2009).
