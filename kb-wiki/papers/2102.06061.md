# Trinucleon form factors with relativistic multirankseparable kernels

> This paper studies elastic electron-trinucleon scattering in the relativistic impulse approximation. The amplitudes for a trinucleon have been obtainedby solving the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equations with amultirank separable kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interactions. The staticapproximation and additional relativistic corrections for the trinucleon electromagnetic form factors have been calculated for the momentum transfersquared up to 50 fm^-2

## Key claim

Using multirank separable kernels of nucleon-nucleon interactions (Graz-II and Paris) in the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev formalism produces a diffraction minimum in trinucleon form factors, which was absent with rank-one kernels; however, the relativistic corrections shift the minimum closer to experimental data but still leave a residual discrepancy of 2-23 fm^-2 depending on the observable and kernel.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic generalization of the Faddeev equations (Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations) with multirank separable kernels for the nucleon-nucleon interactions, using the Graz-II potential (with D-state probabilities p_d = 4%, 5%, 6%) and Paris-1/Paris-2 potentials. They calculated the trinucleon electromagnetic form factors in the relativistic impulse approximation, applying the static approximation (SA) and then adding three relativistic corrections: boost correction (BC) from Lorentz transformations of the one-particle propagator, pole contribution (PC) from a simple pole on q_0, and expansion contribution (EC) from the Taylor series expansion of the final trinucleon wave function arguments. The multiple integrals were evaluated using the Vegas Monte-Carlo integrator with a mesh of N_1 = 35 (q_4 points) and N_2 = 25 (q points), achieving a relative accuracy of 0.01.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer squared range: up to 50 fm^-2
- For the Graz-II kernel with p_d = 4%, the SA gives a form factor zero at t = 33 fm^-2 for the ^3He charge form factor; for Paris-1, the SA zero is at t = 16 fm^-2
- For the ^3H charge form factor with Graz-II, the SA gives the first zero at about 40 fm^-2 (p_d = 4%), 34 fm^-2 (p_d = 5%), and 29 fm^-2 (p_d = 6%)
- The difference between RC and experimental zeros for the ^3He charge form factor with Graz-II is about 18-23 fm^-2; for Paris-1(2) kernels, the difference is about 5(6) fm^-2
- For the ^3H magnetic form factor with Graz-II and p_d = 6%, the RC agree with the experimental data zero
- The RC increase the form factor values by an order of magnitude 0.5-1.5 at t = 50 fm^-2 compared to SA for most multirank kernels
- Different nucleon form factor models (DIPOLE, RHOM, VMDM) change the form factor magnitudes by a factor of 1.5-2 at t = 50 fm^-2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations with rank-one Yamaguchi and Tabakin kernels (Bondarenko 2020) which could not reproduce the diffraction minimum in the ^3He charge form factor. It follows the approach of Rupp (1991) who found that multirank separable kernels in the static approximation could produce the first diffraction minimum, though at higher momentum transfer. The results are compared to conventional approaches, chiral effective field theory (ChEFT), covariant spectator theory (CST), and light-front relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics (RHD) as reviewed by Marcucci (2015). The calculations enable future inclusion of electromagnetic interaction currents to restore gauge invariance and improve agreement with experimental data.
