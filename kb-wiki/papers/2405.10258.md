# The 3He(\vec n,p)3H parity-conserving asymmetry

> Recently, the n$^3$He collaboration reported a measurement of the parity-violating (PV) proton directional asymmetry $A_{\mathrm {PV}} = (1.55\pm 0.97~\mathrm {(st\ at)} \pm 0.24~\mathrm {(sys)})\times 10^{-8}$ in the capture reaction of ${}^3$He$(\vec {n},{\mathrm p}){}^3$H at meV incident neutron energies. The result increased the limited inventory of precisely measured and calculable PV observables in few-body systems required to further understand the structure of hadronic weak interaction. In this letter, we report the experimental and theoretical investigation of a parity conserving (PC) asymmetry $A_{\mathrm {PC}}$ in the same reaction (the first ever measured PC observable at meV neutron energies). As a result of S- and P-wave mixing in the reaction, the $A_{\mathrm {PC}}$ is inversely proportional to the neutron wavelength $λ$. The experimental value is $(λ\times A_{\mathrm {PC}})\equivβ= (-1.97 \pm 0.28~\mathrm{(stat)}\pm 0.12~\mathrm{(sys)}) \times 10^{-6}$ Amstrongs. We present results for a theoretical analysis of this reaction by solving the four-body scattering problem within the hyperspherical harmonic method. We find that in the ${}^3$He$(\vec {n},{\mathrm p}){}^3$H reaction, $A_{\mathrm {PC}}$ depends critically on the energy and width of the close $0^-$ resonant state of ${}^4$He, resulting in a large sensitivity to the spin-orbit components of the nucleon-nucleon force and even to the three-nucleon force. The analysis of the accurately measured $A_{\mathrm {PC}}$ and $A_{\mathrm {PV}}$ using the same few-body theoretical models gives essential information needed to interpret the PV asymmetry in the ${}^3$He$(\vec {n}, {\mathrm p}){}^3$H reaction.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of a parity-conserving (PC) asymmetry \(A_{\mathrm{PC}}\) in the \(^3\mathrm{He}(\vec n,p)^3\mathrm{H}\) reaction at meV neutron energies, finding that the product \(\beta = \lambda \times A_{\mathrm{PC}}\) equals \((-1.97 \pm 0.28 \text{(stat)} \pm 0.12 \text{(sys)}) \times 10^{-6}\) Angstroms; the abstract's claim that this is the first ever PC observable at meV neutron energies is qualified by the fact that the asymmetry arises from S- and P-wave mixing and is inversely proportional to neutron wavelength, making the reported quantity \(\beta\) the wavelength-independent constant.

## Method

The experiment used transversely polarized cold neutrons from the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, with the target/detector chamber rotated 90 degrees relative to the parity-violating measurement setup to maximize sensitivity to the PC asymmetry. The asymmetry was extracted from detector yields with opposite neutron spin directions, corrected for frame twist, magnetic field alignment, and the Mott-Schwinger mechanism from elastic neutron-\(^3\)He scattering. The theoretical analysis solved the four-body scattering problem using the hyperspherical harmonic method, computing \(A_{\mathrm{PC}}\) from the interference of S- and P-waves and its critical dependence on the \(0^-\) resonant state of \(^4\)He.

## Key numbers

- \(\beta = \lambda \times A_{\mathrm{PC}} = (-1.97 \pm 0.28 \text{(stat)} \pm 0.12 \text{(sys)}) \times 10^{-6}\) Angstroms, the central result for the wavelength-independent asymmetry constant.
- \(A_{\mathrm{PC}} = (-42.4 \pm 6.0 \text{(stat)} \pm 0.23 \text{(sys)}) \times 10^{-8}\), the measured parity-conserving asymmetry after corrections.
- Weighted mean neutron wavelength \(\bar\lambda = 4.7775 \pm 0.0317\) Angstroms and mean inverse wavelength \(\bar\mu = 0.2152 \pm 0.0015\) Angstrom\(^{-1}\) across 16 wire planes.
- Mott-Schwinger correction \(\overline{\delta A}_{\mathrm{MS}} = (-1.36 \pm 0.07) \times 10^{-8}\) subtracted from the uncorrected asymmetry.
- Ion collection time uncertainty of 0.8 ms dominates the time-of-flight error budget, combined with 0.32 ms DAQ bin width and 0.140 ms emission time FWHM to give total TOF error of \(\pm 0.87\) ms.

## Context

This work extends the n\(^3\)He collaboration's previous measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{\mathrm{PV}} = (1.55 \pm 0.97 \text{(stat)} \pm 0.24 \text{(sys)}) \times 10^{-8}\) (n3he20, 2020) by measuring the PC asymmetry in the same reaction using the same experimental setup with only a 90-degree chamber rotation. The theoretical analysis shows that \(A_{\mathrm{PC}}\) is highly sensitive to the spin-orbit components of the nucleon-nucleon force and three-nucleon forces through its dependence on the poorly known \(0^-\) resonant state of \(^4\)He, enabling future constraints on nuclear interaction models and aiding interpretation of the parity-violating asymmetry. The measurement is the first PC polarization observable for this reaction at meV energies, complementing prior data on the mirror reaction \(^3\mathrm{H}(p,n)^3\mathrm{He}\) at proton energies above 300 keV (Exfor database; Viviani, 2020).
