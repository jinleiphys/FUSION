# Analysis of correlations between dipole transitions $1^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1$ and $3^-_1\rightarrow 2^+_1$ based on the collective model

> The purpose of the work is to evaluate effect of the isovector dipole and quadrupole-octupole modes coupling on the $B(E1;1^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)/B(E1;3^-_1\rightarrow 2^+_1)$ ratio. The Hamiltonian of the phenomenological collective model is used to calculate mixing of the isovector dipole and quadrupole and octupole modes. The effect of the admixture of the giant dipole resonance to the low-lying collective quadrupole and octupole modes is estimated. It is shown that the coupling of the quadrupole and octupole collective modes to giant dipole resonance leads to decrease of the ratio $B(E1;1^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)/B(E1;3^-_1\rightarrow 2^+_1)$ relative to the value 7/3 predicted by the pure collective quadrupole-octupole model.

## Key claim

The coupling of the isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) to low-lying quadrupole and octupole collective modes reduces the ratio \(R = B(E1;1^-_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)/B(E1;3^-_1\rightarrow 2^+_1)\) below the value 7/3 predicted by the pure quadrupole-octupole collective model, with the reduction being larger in nuclei that have higher excitation energies of the \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) states.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological collective-model Hamiltonian that includes harmonic quadrupole and octupole phonons plus an isovector dipole mode representing the giant dipole resonance (GDR). The GDR was treated in the Tamm-Dankoff approximation, and its coupling to the quadrupole and octupole phonons was introduced through a dipole transition operator containing both a particle-hole term and a term proportional to the product of quadrupole and octupole collective variables. Using perturbation theory, the wave functions of the low-lying \(1^-_1\), \(2^+_1\), \(3^-_1\), and ground states were expanded to include small admixtures of the GDR phonon, and an analytic expression for the ratio \(R\) was derived.

## Key numbers

* The pure quadrupole-octupole collective model predicts \(R = 7/3 \approx 2.33\).
* For \(^{142}\)Nd, the experimental ratio is \(R = 0.77 \pm 0.04\); the calculation with \(C'/C = 0.933\) reproduces this value exactly.
* For \(^{124}\)Sn, the experimental ratio is \(R = 1.00 \pm 0.14\); the calculation with \(C'/C = 0.933\) gives \(R = 1.00\).
* For \(^{148}\)Nd, the experimental ratio is \(R = 3.39 \pm 1.57\); the calculation with \(C'/C = 0.933\) gives \(R = 2.15\).
* The GDR energy was parametrized as \(\omega_{GDR} = 94.68\,(A^{-1/3} - A^{-2/3})\) MeV.
* The parameter \(\omega_0\) (unperturbed particle-hole energy) was taken as \(\omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pietralla (2001), who first reported the systematic correlation between the two E1 transitions and derived the 7/3 ratio in a simple bosonic model. The present calculation shows that the previously neglected coupling to the giant dipole resonance can account for the observed deviations from 7/3, especially in spherical nuclei with higher \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) energies. The results enable future studies to incorporate the full distribution of high-lying \(1^-\) states (rather than a single collective GDR state) for more realistic predictions.
