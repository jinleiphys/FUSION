# Medium effects in K^+ nucleus interaction from consistent analysis of integral and differential cross sections

> Self consistency in the analysis of transmission measurements for K^+ on several nuclei in the momentum range of 500-700 MeV/c is achieved with a "t_{eff}(rho)rho" potential and new results are derived for total cross sections. The imaginary part of the t_{eff} amplitude is found to increase linearly with the average nuclear density in excess of a threshold value. This phenomenological density dependence of the K^+ nucleus optical potential also gives rise to good agreement with recent measurements of differential cross sections for elastic scattering of 715 MeV/c K^+ by Li^6 and C.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent analysis of K⁺ nucleus transmission measurements and elastic scattering data requires an empirical rescaling of the imaginary part of the optical potential, which increases linearly with the average nuclear density above a threshold value of 0.088±0.004 fm⁻³, rather than with the local density.

## Method

The authors analyzed transmission measurements for K⁺ on ⁶Li, C, Si, and Ca at beam momenta of 500–700 MeV/c using a Klein-Gordon equation with a `t_eff(ρ)ρ` optical potential. They introduced a rescaling factor 1+β(ρ̄−ρ_c)Θ(ρ̄−ρ_c) for the imaginary part of the potential, where ρ̄ is the average nuclear density defined as (1/A)∫ρ²dr, and fitted the parameters β and ρ_c simultaneously to reaction and total cross sections. The resulting potential was then tested against differential cross sections for elastic scattering of 715 MeV/c K⁺ by ⁶Li and C, and the entire procedure was iterated until full convergence was achieved.

## Key numbers

- Threshold average nuclear density ρ_c = 0.088±0.004 fm⁻³, above which the imaginary potential must be rescaled.
- Rescaling parameter β = 13.0±3.4 fm³, used in the linear rescaling model.
- Average nuclear densities: ⁶Li = 0.049 fm⁻³, C = 0.103 fm⁻³, Si = 0.110 fm⁻³, Ca = 0.107 fm⁻³, with ±1% uncertainty for the last three.
- For Si at 714 MeV/c, the derived reaction cross section was 317.5±3.6 mb and total cross section 396.5±2.3 mb using a tρ potential, while calculated values were 253 mb and 328 mb respectively.
- The imaginary part of the free KN forward scattering amplitude b₀ was found to increase gradually with energy up to 16% above its free-space value.
- The normalization factor for the angular distribution data was 0.92±0.05.
- Self-consistent total cross sections are 3–5% larger than previously published values.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Marlow et al. (1990), Kraus et al. (1992), Sawafta et al. (1993), and Weiss et al. (1994) on K⁺ transmission measurements, and addresses the long-standing inconsistency noted by Chen et al. (1992) that calculated cross sections using tρ potentials were systematically smaller than measured values. The paper also builds on the re-analysis by Friedman, Gal, and Weiss (1996) that first derived reaction cross sections from these experiments. The empirical density dependence found here enables a consistent description of both integral and differential cross sections, and the threshold density ρ_c may signal the opening of new reaction channels beyond the impulse approximation, possibly related to meson exchange currents as estimated by Garcia-Recio et al. (1995).
