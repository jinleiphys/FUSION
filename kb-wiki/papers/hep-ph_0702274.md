# Stau-catalyzed $^6$Li Production in Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis

> If the gravitino mass is in the region from a few GeV to a few 10's GeV, the scalar lepton X such as stau is most likely the next lightest supersymmetry particle. The negatively charged and long-lived X^- may form a Coulomb bound state (A X) with a nucleus A and may affect the big-bang nucleosynthesis through catalyzed fusion process. We calculate a production cross section of Li6 from the catalyzed fusion (He4 X^-) + d \to Li6 + X^- by solving the Schrödinger equation exactly for three-body system of He4, d, and X. We utilize the state-of-the-art coupled-channel method, which is known to be very accurate to describe other three-body systems in nuclear and atomic reactions. The importance of the use of appropriate nuclear potential and the exact treatment of the quantum tunneling in the fusion process are emphasized. We find that the astrophysical S-factor at the Gamow peak corresponding to T=10 keV is 0.038 MeV barn. This leads to the Li6 abundance from the catalyzed process as Li6|_{CBBN}\simeq 4.3\times 10^{-11} (D/2.8\times 10^{-5}) ([n_{X^-}/s]/10^{-16}) in the limit of long lifetime of X. Particle physics implication of this result is also discussed.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for the stau-catalyzed fusion reaction (⁴He X⁻) + d → ⁶Li + X⁻ at the Gamow peak energy of 36.4 keV (corresponding to T = 10 keV) is 0.038 MeV barn, which is about ten times smaller than the earlier estimate of approximately 0.3 MeV barn by Pospelov (2007) that was based on a naive comparison with the standard radiative capture process.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation exactly for the three-body system of ⁴He, deuteron, and X⁻ using a state-of-the-art coupled-channel method previously developed for muon transfer reactions in muon-catalyzed fusion. They employed a ⁴He-d cluster model with a nuclear potential that reproduces the binding energy (-1.474 MeV) and charge form factor of ⁶Li as well as low-energy ⁴He-d scattering phase shifts, and they expanded the three-body wave function using Gaussian basis functions with geometric progression ranges.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical S-factor at the Gamow peak (E = 36.4 keV, T = 10 keV): S = 0.038 MeV barn
- Cross section at E = 36.4 keV: σ = 6.88 × 10⁻⁴ barn
- Binding energy of the (⁴He X⁻)₁ₛ Coulomb bound state: ε = -337.33 keV
- Binding energy of ⁶Li ground state (⁴He-d cluster): ε = -1.474 MeV
- RMS radius of (⁴He X⁻)₁ₛ state: 6.84 fm
- RMS charge radius of ⁶Li: 2.54 fm
- ⁶Li abundance from CBBN in the long-lifetime limit: ⁶Li|CBBN ≃ 4.3 × 10⁻¹¹ (D/2.8 × 10⁻⁵) ([nX⁻/s]/10⁻¹⁶)
- Observational upper bound on ⁶Li abundance: ⁶Li < 6.1 × 10⁻¹¹ (2σ) leads to bound nX⁻/s < 1.4 × 10⁻¹⁶
- Required dilution factor for thermal relic stau: Δ ≃ (300-600) × (mτ̃/100 GeV)

## Context

This work provides a rigorous quantum-mechanical calculation of the stau-catalyzed ⁶Li production that corrects the earlier estimate by Pospelov (2007), which had assumed the catalyzed process could be approximated by replacing the photon field with the Coulomb field of X⁻ in the standard radiative capture ⁴He(d,γ)⁶Li. The authors note that the standard process is dominated by d-wave (E2) or p-wave (E1) transitions, while the catalyzed process is dominated by s-wave (J=0) with zero orbital angular momentum, making the analogy invalid. The result enables precise constraints on the abundance of long-lived negatively charged supersymmetric particles (such as stau) from cosmological ⁶Li observations, and it quantifies the required entropy dilution factor needed to reconcile thermal leptogenesis with the observational upper bound on ⁶Li.
