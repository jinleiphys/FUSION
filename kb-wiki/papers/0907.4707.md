# Elimination of $0^+$ spurious states in the quasiparticle time blocking approximation

> The quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) is considered as a model for the description of excitations in open-shell nuclei. The QTBA is an extension of the quasiparticle random phase approximation that includes quasiparticle-phonon coupling. In the present version of the QTBA, the pairing correlations are included within the framework of the BCS approximation. Thus, in this model, the $0^+$ spurious states appear, which are caused by the breaking of the symmetry related to the particle-number conservation. In this work, the method is described which solves the problem of the $0^+$ spurious states in the QTBA with the help of the projection technique. The method is illustrated by calculations of $0^+$ excitations in $^{120}$Sn nucleus.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) for open-shell nuclei, which extends the QRPA by including quasiparticle-phonon coupling and BCS pairing, produces fragmented $0^+$ spurious states at low energies even when the pp channel is included; these can be eliminated by a projection technique that removes the coupling of complex configurations to the ghost state, provided the pp channel is also included.

## Method

The author formulates the QTBA within the Green function method, solving the Bethe-Salpeter-type equation for the effective response function. The pp-channel contributions are treated in a restricted discrete basis representation, while the ph channel is handled in coordinate space to include the single-particle continuum. To eliminate the $0^+$ spurious state, projection operators $P$ and $P^\dagger$ are constructed from the singular parts of the pp-channel propagator; these operators are used to project the quasiparticle-phonon coupling amplitude $\bar{\Phi}(\omega)$ as $\bar{\Phi}^{\,\text{proj}}(\omega) = P^\dagger \bar{\Phi}(\omega) P$, which removes the coupling of 2q$\otimes$phonon configurations to the ghost state.

## Key numbers

- The smearing parameter $\Delta$ in the strength function calculation is 200 keV.
- The set of phonons included 29 collective modes with spin $L$ in the interval $2 \leq L \leq 9$ and natural parity $\pi = (-1)^L$.
- Calculations were performed for the $^{120}$Sn nucleus using the T5 Skyrme force parametrization.

## Context

This work extends the QTBA formalism (Tselyaev, 2007a,b) by solving the problem of $0^+$ spurious states that arise from particle-number symmetry breaking in the BCS approximation. The method builds on the earlier combined $(r,\lambda)$ representation (Pankratov and Saperstein, 1988) and the subtraction procedure (Tselyaev, 2007a,b). It enables reliable calculations of $0^+$ excitations, such as the isoscalar giant monopole resonance, in open-shell nuclei without contamination from spurious states, and is illustrated for $^{120}$Sn.
