# Cranked shell model and isospin symmetry near N=Z

> A cranked shell model approach for the description of rotational bands in $N\approx Z$ nuclei is formulated. The isovector neutron-proton pairing is taken into account explicitly. The concept of spontaneous breaking and subsequent restoration of the isospin symmetry turns out to be crucial. The general rules to construct the near yrast-spectra for rotating nuclei are presented. For the model case of particles in a j-shell, it is shown that excitation spectra and the alignment processes are well described as compared to the exact shell model calculation. Realistic cranked shell model calculations are able to describe the experimental spectra of $^{72,73}$Kr and $^{74}$Rb isotopes. \

# Cranked shell model and isospin symmetry near N=Z

## Key claim

The paper formulates a cranked shell model (CSM) approach for rotational bands in N≈Z nuclei that includes isovector neutron-proton pairing, but the approach relies on the assumption that the pair-field has isovector character at low rotational frequency, an assumption supported by self-consistent HFB calculations for a single-j shell model and by prior studies, though the authors caution that the CSM holds below a phase transition and only permits a fair description of the lower part of the extended transition region.

## Method

The authors develop a Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov (HFB) formalism with isovector (t=1) pairing, solving the HFB equations self-consistently for neutrons and protons in a single f7/2 shell interacting via a delta-force. They then adopt the CSM approximation, fixing the mean-fields (deformation and pair-gap) to their ω=0 values and constructing quasiparticle routhians as functions of rotational frequency, with isospin restoration approximated by cranking in isospace (adjusting λτ to fix ⟨Tz⟩) and adding a λτ/2 energy correction. The CSM results are compared to exact shell-model diagonalizations for (3+3), (3+4), and (4+4) particles in the f7/2 shell, and the method is applied to realistic calculations for 72,73Kr and 74Rb using a modified harmonic oscillator potential with standard Nilsson parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the (N=Z=4) system in the f7/2 shell, the t=1 pair-field changes to a t=0 pair-field at rotational frequency ω = 0.45G (where G is the energy unit g∫R⁴nl r² dr).
- The deformation parameter κ=1.75 in the f7/2 shell approximately corresponds to β=0.16.
- For realistic calculations on A≈72 nuclei, the monopole pair-fields are set to Δp=Δn=1.1 MeV, corresponding to about 80% of the experimental even-odd mass differences, with deformation ε=0.3, γ=0, and ε4=0.
- The correction λτ/2 added to the ground-state energy is 0.88G for the (N=Z=3) system and 1.01G for the (4+4) system with (Z=3, N=5).
- For the (N=Z=3) odd-odd system at ω=0.3G, the CSM gives Jx=5.7 while the exact shell model gives Jx=4.7 for the aligned odd-spin band.

## Context

This work extends the traditional cranked shell model (CSM), reviewed by de Voigt (1979) and Bengtsson (1979), which assumed no proton-neutron pair correlations, to the N≈Z regime where isovector pn-pairing is expected to be important. The approach builds on earlier HFB studies of pn-pairing reviewed by Goodman (1979) and on prior work by Sandhu (1993), Satula (1997), Engel (1996), and Dean (1998) suggesting the dominance of isovector pairing at low frequency. The paper enables the analysis of rotational bands in N≈Z nuclei such as 72,73Kr and 74Rb, where modern gamma-ray detector arrays and future radioactive beam facilities will provide new data, and it establishes general rules for constructing quasiparticle excitation spectra in the presence of np-pairing that can be applied to larger shells and more realistic calculations.
