# GW170817 and GW190814: tension on the maximum mass

> The detection of the binary events GW170817 and GW190814 has provided invaluable constraints on the maximum mass of nonrotating configurations of neutron stars, $M_{_{\rm TOV}}$. However, the large differences in the neutron-star masses measured in GW170817 and GW190814 has also lead to a significant tension between the predictions for such maximum masses, with GW170817 suggesting that $M_{_{\rm TOV}} \lesssim 2.3\,M_{\odot}$, and GW190814 requiring $M_{_{\rm TOV}} \gtrsim 2.5\,M_{\odot}$ if the secondary was a (non- or slowly rotating) neutron star at merger. Using a genetic algorithm, we sample the multidimensional space of parameters spanned by gravitational-wave and astronomical observations associated with GW170817. Consistent with previous estimates, we find that all of the physical quantities are in agreement with the observations if the maximum mass is in the range $M_{_{\rm TOV}} = 2.210^{+0.116}_{-0.123} \,M_{\odot}$ within a $2\textrm{-}σ$ confidence level. By contrast, maximum masses with $M_{_{\rm TOV}} \gtrsim 2.5\,M_{\odot}$, not only require efficiencies in the gravitational-wave emission that are well above the numerical-relativity estimates, but they also lead to a significant under-production of the ejected mass. Hence, the tension can be released by assuming that the secondary in GW190814 was a black hole at merger, although it could have been a rotating neutron star before.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the maximum mass of nonrotating neutron stars is constrained to \(M_{_{\rm TOV}} = 2.210^{+0.117}_{-0.123}\,M_{\odot}\) at a \(2\textrm{-}\sigma\) confidence level when using a genetic algorithm to sample the multidimensional parameter space of GW170817 observations; values of \(M_{_{\rm TOV}} \gtrsim 2.5\,M_{\odot}\) required by GW190814 if its secondary was a neutron star are inconsistent with GW170817 data because they demand unrealistically high gravitational-wave emission efficiencies and produce too little ejected mass.

## Method

The authors employed a genetic algorithm to sample a ten-parameter space defined by conservation equations for baryonic and gravitational mass, quasi-universal relations for rotating neutron stars, and constraints from gravitational-wave and kilonova observations of GW170817. They used the relation between the critical mass for collapse and the dimensionless angular momentum, the conversion between baryonic and gravitational mass near the maximum-mass limit, and a quasi-universal relation between total gravitational-wave energy loss and the specific angular momentum of the merger remnant. The algorithm enforced observational priors including the total gravitational mass of GW170817 (\(M_g =2.73_{-0.01}^{+0.04}\,M_{\odot}\)), the total ejected mass (\(0.0537 \pm 0.013\,M_{\odot}\)), and the dynamically ejected mass (\(\approx 10^{-3}\,M_{\odot}\)), while varying parameters such as the maximum mass, the fraction of core baryonic mass, and the fraction of disk mass unbound.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of nonrotating neutron stars: \(M_{_{\rm TOV}} = 2.210^{+0.117}_{-0.123}\,M_{\odot}\) at \(2\textrm{-}\sigma\) confidence level, with a lower bound of \(2.087\,M_{\odot}\) and an upper bound of \(2.326\,M_{\odot}\).
* Total gravitational mass of GW170817: \(M_g =2.73_{-0.01}^{+0.04}\,M_{\odot}\).
* Total ejected mass from GW170817: \(0.0537 \pm 0.013\,M_{\odot}\).
* Dynamically ejected mass: \(\approx 10^{-3}\,M_{\odot}\).
* Range of the dimensionless spin of the black hole produced by the merger: \(0.7 \lesssim j_{_{\rm BH}} \lesssim 0.9\).
* Upper limit on total gravitational-wave energy from numerical-relativity simulations: \(E^{\rm tot}_{\rm GW} /M_g \leq 0.045\,M_{\odot} c^2\) with \(20\%\) uncertainty.
* Range of the fraction of disk mass unbound: \(f_{\rm disk} \simeq 0.2 - 0.5\).
* Range of the ratio of baryonic to gravitational mass at the maximum-mass limit: \(\eta \approx 1.171\pm0.014\) at \(2\textrm{-}\sigma\).
* Range of the ratio of critical mass to maximum mass: \(\chi(1) \approx 1.20_{-0.05}^{+0.02}\) for maximally rotating models.

## Context

This work extends the earlier maximum-mass estimate of Rezzolla (2017), which gave \(M_{_{\rm TOV}}=2.16_{-0.16}^{+0.17}\,M_{\odot}\) using a simpler uniform posterior, by employing a genetic algorithm to simultaneously satisfy multiple observational constraints from GW170817. The results are consistent with the conclusions of Shao (2020) and Fan (2020), who also used post-merger gravitational-wave emission to bound the maximum mass. The paper resolves the tension between GW170817 and GW190814 by concluding that the secondary in GW190814 was most likely a black hole at merger, although it could have been a rotating neutron star earlier in the binary evolution; this enables future work to focus on formation scenarios for black hole-neutron star or black hole-black hole mergers rather than requiring an extreme equation of state supporting \(M_{_{\rm TOV}} \gtrsim 2.5\,M_{\odot}\).
