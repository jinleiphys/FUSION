# Studies of parton thermalization at RHIC

> We consider the evolution of a parton system which is formed in the central region just after a relativistic heavy ion collision. The parton consist of mostly gluons, minijets, which are produced by elastic scattering between constituent partons of the colliding nuclei. We assume the system can be described by a semi-classical Boltzmann transport equation, which we solve by means of the test particle Monte-Carlo method including retardation. The partons proliferate via secondary radiative $gg \to ggg$ processes until the thermalization is reached for some assumptions. The extended system is thermalized at about $t=1.6$ fm/$c$ with $T = 570$ MeV and stays in equilibrium for about 2 fm/$c$ with breaking temperature $T = 360$ MeV in the rapidity central region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a system of minijet gluons produced in the central region of a relativistic heavy ion collision at RHIC can reach kinetic thermalization, but only if small-angle elastic scatterings (with a minimum momentum transfer of 0.5 GeV/\(c\)) are included; the system does not thermalize when only hard scatterings are considered or when a correlated phase-space distribution (CMN distribution) is used.

## Method

The authors solve a semi-classical Boltzmann transport equation for gluons, quarks, and antiquarks using a test-particle Monte-Carlo cascade code, including elastic \(2 \to 2\) scatterings and radiative \(gg \to ggg\) processes. Initial minijet distributions are generated from perturbative QCD with a transverse momentum cutoff \(p_0 = 1\) GeV/\(c\) at RHIC energy, assuming either no correlation between momentum and space coordinates or the correlated CMN distribution of Cooper et al. (2002). The evolution is followed in a small test sphere of radius \(R = 1.1\) fm at the center of the collision, and kinetic isotropy is diagnosed by the ratio \(\sum |p_x| / \sum |p_z|\).

## Key numbers

* The system thermalizes at about \(t = 1.6\) fm/\(c\) with temperature \(T = 570\) MeV and stays in equilibrium for about 2 fm/\(c\) with breaking temperature \(T = 360\) MeV in the rapidity central region.
* The total number of minijets produced at RHIC is about 4800; the total number of particles in the system after secondary production reaches about 9700 for the uncorrelated minijet system.
* In the test sphere, the particle density decreases from 32 fm\(^{-3}\) at \(t = 8\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (1.6 fm/\(c\)) to 11 fm\(^{-3}\) at \(t = 17\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (3.4 fm/\(c\)).
* The energy density in the sphere decreases from 54 GeV/fm\(^3\) at \(t = 8\) GeV\(^{-1}\) to 12 GeV/fm\(^3\) at \(t = 17\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The energy per parton in the sphere decreases from 1.7 GeV at \(t = 8\) GeV\(^{-1}\) to 1.1 GeV at \(t = 17\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The minimum momentum transfer for small-angle elastic scattering is set to \(p_{\rm cut} = 0.5\) GeV/\(c\); hard-only simulations use \(p_{\rm cut} = 1\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up thermalization" scenario of Baier et al. (2000) by explicitly simulating the evolution of a minijet system at RHIC energy using a Boltzmann transport approach. It contrasts with the correlated CMN distribution of Cooper, Mottola, and Nayak (2002), which is shown to prevent thermalization due to space-momentum correlations that suppress collisions. The results enable further studies of the early-time dynamics of quark-gluon plasma formation and the role of radiative processes in chemical and thermal equilibration.
