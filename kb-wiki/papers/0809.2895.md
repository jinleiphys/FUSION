# Non-empirical pairing energy density functional. First order in the nuclear plus Coulomb two-body interaction

> We perform systematic calculations of pairing gaps in semi-magic nuclei across the nuclear chart using the Energy Density Functional method and a {\it non-empirical} pairing functional derived, without further approximation, at lowest order in the two-nucleon vacuum interaction, including the Coulomb force. The correlated single-particle motion is accounted for by the SLy4 semi-empirical functional. Rather unexpectedly, both neutron and proton pairing gaps thus generated are systematically close to experimental data. Such a result further suggests that missing effects, i.e. higher partial-waves of the NN interaction, the NNN interaction and the coupling to collective fluctuations, provide an overall contribution that is sub-leading as for generating pairing gaps in nuclei. We find that including the Coulomb interaction is essential as it reduces proton pairing gaps by up to 40%.

## Key claim
Neutron and proton pairing gaps computed at lowest order in the low-momentum nuclear plus Coulomb two-body interaction are systematically close to experimental data for a large set of semi-magic spherical nuclei, a result that was neither expected nor hoped for. The authors suggest that missing effects (higher partial-waves of the NN interaction, the NNN interaction, and coupling to collective fluctuations) provide an overall sub-leading contribution to pairing gaps in nuclei.

## Method
The authors constructed a non-empirical pairing energy density functional at first order in the low-momentum NN interaction V_low_k (derived from the Argonne v18 potential with renormalization cut-off Lambda=2.5 fm^-1), including the Coulomb interaction for proton pairing. They used a rank-three separable representation of the interaction in the ^1S_0 channel and solved Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in a spherical box of 24 fm radius with a mesh step of 0.3 fm, using the semi-empirical SLy4 Skyrme functional for the normal (single-particle) part of the EDF.

## Key numbers
- Including the Coulomb interaction reduces proton pairing gaps by up to 40%.
- The renormalization cut-off for V_low_k was set to Lambda=2.5 fm^-1, with results unchanged over the range Lambda in [1.8, 3.0] fm^-1.
- The momentum cut-off for single-particle wave functions was k_cut = 4.0 fm^-1, allowing description of states up to about 300 MeV and ensuring convergence of pairing gaps to a fraction of a keV.
- The isoscalar effective mass from SLy4 is m*_0 = 0.7 m at nuclear saturation density, consistent with Hartree-Fock results from low-momentum interactions.
- Coulomb interaction reduces proton pairing gaps by 30% to 50% (stated range in text, consistent with the 40% figure in abstract).
- Calculated neutron gaps in heavy nuclei lie between 1 and 1.5 MeV, while proton gaps from charge-symmetric functional stand above 1.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends systematic calculations of pairing gaps from infinite nuclear matter (Dean 2003, Baldo 2007, Hebeler 2007) to finite nuclei across the nuclear chart, using a non-empirical pairing functional. It contrasts with earlier finite-nucleus studies such as Barranco (2004) and Pastore (2008), which found that the direct term of the vacuum interaction only accounted for a fraction of experimental pairing gaps (e.g., two-thirds of the neutron gap in ^120Sn). The results enable future work on deformed nuclei and explicit incorporation of missing contributions (higher partial-waves, NNN interaction, coupling to collective fluctuations) to test whether they alter pairing gaps only mildly while strongly renormalizing the density of states.
