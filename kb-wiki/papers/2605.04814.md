# Charged current neutrino processes in hot nuclear matter with a recent Skyrme parametrization constrained by microscopic calculations

> Neutrino processes are important in the modeling of supernova explosions, proto-neutron star evolution, and binary neutron star mergers. We study neutrino production and absorption in proto-neutron star and supernova matter and direct Urca neutrino emission of neutron star matter in the framework of the random phase approximation (RPA). As interactions, we employ the recent extended Skyrme parametrization Sky3s whose effective masses and spin-dependent terms were adjusted to microscopic calculations, and the SLy4 parametrization that was used in previous calculations of neutrino rates. The rates obtained for Sky3s differ from those for SLy4 by up to one order of magnitude for some processes and energy regions. We also determine the electron, muon, and proton fractions that lead to a stationary composition of matter for a density above the direct Urca threshold, and find that with Sky3s the standard $β$ equilibrium condition is not as badly violated at finite temperature as predicted in the literature. There are also minor differences between the full RPA and the common Landau approximation, but they are probably not significant for astrophysical simulations. We conclude that it would be worthwhile to repeat the calculation of neutrino rates for the use in astrophysical simulations, and the corresponding simulations, with several and better constrained interactions than SLy4, such as Sky3s.

# Charged current neutrino processes in hot nuclear matter with a recent Skyrme parametrization constrained by microscopic calculations

## Key claim

The neutrino production and absorption rates obtained with the new Skyrme parametrization Sky3s differ from those obtained with the widely used SLy4 parametrization by up to one order of magnitude for some processes and energy regions, but the differences between full random phase approximation (RPA) and the common Landau approximation are minor and probably not significant for astrophysical simulations.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current neutrino production and absorption rates in hot, dense nuclear matter using the random phase approximation (RPA) with extended Skyrme effective interactions. They employed the recently developed Sky3s parametrization, whose effective masses and spin-dependent terms were fitted to microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations, and compared results with the SLy4 parametrization used in previous neutrino rate calculations. The response functions were obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter-like equation for the particle-hole interaction derived from the Skyrme energy-density functional, using generalized Lindhard functions at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino rates for Sky3s differ from those for SLy4 by up to **one order of magnitude** for some processes and energy regions
- Nuclear saturation density used as reference: **ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 nucleons/fm³**
- Fermi weak interaction constant: **G_F ≈ 8.975 × 10⁻⁵ MeV fm³**
- Cabibbo angle cosine: **cos θ_c = 0.974**
- Charged current vector coupling constant: **g_V = 1**
- Charged current axial-vector coupling constant: **g_A = 1.23**
- Sky3s and Sky4s differ mostly by their equation of state at densities **ρ ≳ 0.4 fm⁻³**

## Context

This work extends previous charged-current neutrino rate calculations by Oertel (2020) and Pascal (2022), which used the SLy4 parametrization at the Landau approximation level, by implementing the full RPA with the better-constrained Sky3s interaction. The Sky3s parametrization was constructed by Duan (2024, 2025) to address problems with earlier Skyrme functionals, including unrealistic density dependence of effective masses that caused neutron Fermi velocities exceeding the speed of light at relatively low densities. The results suggest that astrophysical simulations of supernovae, proto-neutron star evolution, and binary neutron star mergers should be repeated using several better-constrained interactions such as Sky3s instead of relying solely on SLy4.
