# Kinetic Mixing and Axial Charges in the Parity-Doublet Model

> The standard parity doublet model with its mass-mixing mechanism fails to describe the axial charge $g_A$ of the nucleon. While $g_A = 1$ in the original Gell-Mann--Levy model, which reproduces the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly of QCD, in the presence of a chirally invariant baryon mass the mass mixing leads to $g_A < 1 $ whereas phenomenologically it is about 1.28. We propose to remedy this problem by introducing kinetic-mixing terms corresponding to meson-baryon derivative couplings, similar in spirit to the two-mixing-angle scenario of the $η$-$η'$ mixing. This extended parity doublet model contains five parameters in the effective baryonic Lagrangian. Three of them can be determined by using the empirical results for the axial charge of the nucleon together with the masses of the nucleon and its parity partner, the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. We discuss various options how to determine the remaining parameters, touching upon the mass of both parity partners if the chiral condensate is put to zero; the mass of the nucleon in the chiral limit; and the values of meson-baryon coupling constants related to the decays of the resonance to pion-nucleon and sigma-nucleon.

## Key claim

The standard parity doublet model predicts an axial charge \(g_A < 1\) for the nucleon, contradicting the phenomenological value of about 1.28; the authors propose that introducing kinetic-mixing terms (derivative couplings between mesons and baryons) can remedy this failure, allowing \(g_A > 1\) while also enabling a small axial charge for the \(N^*(1535)\) resonance.

## Method

The authors extend the standard parity doublet model by adding kinetic-mixing terms proportional to parameters \(h_1\) and \(h_2\) (or equivalently \(h\) and \(\Delta h\)) to the baryonic Lagrangian, which correspond to meson-baryon derivative couplings. They diagonalize the resulting Nambu-Gorkov Dirac operator using a momentum-independent but non-unitary field transformation with two mixing angles \(\theta_1\) and \(\theta_2\), relating these angles to the physical baryon masses and the axial charges. The five parameters of the extended model are then constrained using empirical inputs: the nucleon mass, the \(N^*(1535)\) mass, the nucleon axial charge, and the pion-nucleon coupling to the resonance, with the remaining parameter (the chirally invariant mass \(m_0\)) treated as a free parameter to be determined from additional considerations.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass \(m_N = 939\) MeV and \(N^*(1535)\) mass \(m_{N^*} = 1510\) MeV, used as inputs to determine model parameters.
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi \approx 92\) MeV, used to relate Yukawa couplings to masses.
- The difference of the Yukawa couplings in the standard model is fixed as \(g_2 - g_1 = (m_{N^*} - m_N)/f_\pi \approx 6.21\).
- The experimental axial charge of the nucleon is \(g_A \simeq 1.28\) from neutron and nuclear beta decays.
- The chirally invariant mass \(m_0\) is estimated from QCD sum rules to lie between 500 MeV and 550 MeV, and from the nucleon mass in the chiral limit at about 880 MeV (upper bound).

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model originally developed by Detar and Kunihiro (1988) and Jido et al. (2001), which introduced a mass-mixing mechanism between the nucleon and its parity partner \(N^*(1535)\) but failed to reproduce the nucleon axial charge. The kinetic-mixing approach is analogous to the two-mixing-angle scenario used for \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing in the meson sector (Escribano, 2005). The extended model enables future in-medium studies of hadron properties under chiral restoration, and allows for a consistent description of the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly with \(g_A - g_A^* = 1\) while maintaining a sizable chirally invariant baryon mass.
