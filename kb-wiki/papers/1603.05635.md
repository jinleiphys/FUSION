# Single-particle spectral function of the $Λ$ hyperon in finite nuclei

> The spectral function of the $Λ$ hyperon in finite nuclei is calculated from the corresponding $Λ$ self-energy, which is constructed within a perturbative many-body approach using some of the hyperon-nucleon interactions of the Jülich and Nijmegen groups. Binding energies, wave functions and disoccupation numbers of different single-particle states are obtained for various hypernuclei from $^5_Λ$He to $^{209}_{\,\,\,\,\,Λ}$Pb. The agreement between the calculated binding energies and experimental data is qualitatively good. The small spin-orbit splitting of the $p-, d-, f-$ and $g-$wave states is confirmed. The discrete and the continuum contributions of the single-$Λ$ spectral function are computed. Their appearance is qualitatively similar to that of the nucleons. The $Z$-factor, that measures the importance of correlations, is also calculated. Our results show that its value is relatively large, indicating that the $Λ$ hyperon is less correlated than nucleons. This is in agreement with the results obtained by other authors for the correlations of the $Λ$ in infinite nuclear matter. The disoccupation numbers are obtained by integrating the spectral function over the energy. Our results show that the discrete contribution to the disoccupation number decreases when increasing the momentum of the $Λ$. This indicates that, in the production reactions of hypernuclei, the $Λ$ hyperon is mostly formed in a quasi-free state.

## Key claim

The single-Λ spectral function in finite nuclei is calculated for the first time, showing that the Λ hyperon is less correlated than nucleons (as indicated by a relatively large Z-factor), but the paper hedges that this conclusion is based on a perturbative many-body approach using specific hyperon-nucleon interactions from the Jülich and Nijmegen groups, with the calculation done at a fixed nuclear matter density of 0.17 fm⁻³ and a starting energy of Ω = m_N + m_Λ − 80 MeV, where the dependence on these choices is stated to be weak.

## Method

The Λ self-energy in finite nuclei was constructed within the Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) approximation by first solving the coupled-channel Bethe–Goldstone equation in nuclear matter using bare YN interactions, then relating the finite-nucleus G-matrix to the nuclear-matter G-matrix via a second-order expansion. The real and imaginary parts of the self-energy were obtained, and the Schrödinger equation was solved using the real part as an effective single-particle potential to find bound-state energies and wave functions; the discrete and continuum contributions to the single-Λ spectral function were then computed from the Dyson equation and the discontinuity of the propagator.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function formalism previously applied to nucleon correlations in finite nuclei (e.g., Dickhoff and collaborators, various years) and to Λ correlations in infinite nuclear matter by Robertson and Dickhoff (2004). It provides the first calculation of the single-Λ spectral function in finite nuclei, enabling a more realistic description of hypernuclear production reaction cross sections (e.g., (e,e'K⁺) reactions) beyond the mean-field picture, and complements prior hypernuclear structure studies using Woods-Saxon potentials, Skyrme Hartree–Fock, relativistic mean-field theory, and ab initio approaches.
