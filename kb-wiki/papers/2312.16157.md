# Microscopic Optical Potentials from Chiral Forces and Ab Initio Nuclear Densities

> We derived microscopic optical potentials (OPs) for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering within the framework of chiral effective field theories at the first-order term of the spectator expansion of the Watson multiple-scattering theory and adopting the impulse approximation. Our OPs are derived by folding ab initio nuclear densities with a nucleon-nucleon NN t matrix computed with a consistent chiral interaction. The results of our OPs are in good agreement with the experimental data. Recent achievements of our work are reviewed in this contribution.

# Microscopic Optical Potentials from Chiral Forces and Ab Initio Nuclear Densities

## Key claim
Microscopic optical potentials derived from chiral effective field theories at the first-order spectator expansion with the impulse approximation, folded with ab initio nuclear densities, produce elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering results in "reasonably good agreement" with experimental data, with the caveat that the impulse approximation may become questionable at lower energies (e.g., 65 MeV is considered near the limit of validity).

## Method
The optical potential is obtained as a folding integral of a nucleon-nucleon (NN) t matrix, computed from chiral interactions, with one-body nuclear density matrices from ab initio many-body methods. For light nuclei (A ≤ 16), densities come from the no-core shell model (NCSM) using NN and three-nucleon (3N) chiral interactions; for medium-mass nuclei up to A ≈ 140, densities come from self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory with the ADC(n) truncation scheme. The t matrix is evaluated at the first-order term of the spectator expansion of Watson multiple-scattering theory, adopting the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers
- Proton scattering off ¹²C at laboratory energies between 122 and 300 MeV: differential cross section and analyzing power calculated with density-dependent NN interaction where matter density ρ varied between 0.08 fm⁻³ and 0.13 fm⁻³; results with ρ = 0 fm⁻³ (no 3N forces) also shown.
- Elastic proton scattering off ⁴⁰Ca at 65, 80, 135, and 182 MeV laboratory energies: "remarkable agreement" at 65 MeV, with agreement worsening at larger scattering angles.
- Elastic proton scattering off ⁴⁸Ca at 201 MeV and ⁵⁸Ni at 178 MeV: NNLO_sat and N⁴LO chiral interactions in t_NN produce "significant differences" in both shape and size of cross section and analyzing power; NNLO_sat gives "remarkably good description" of analyzing power depth.
- SCGF calculations for Ca and Ni isotopes: scattering observables "well converged" with respect to model space, 3N forces, and many-body truncation already at ADC(2) level.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous series of papers (Vorabbi et al., 2016-2023) that derived microscopic optical potentials from chiral nuclear interactions, moving from factorized approximations with relativistic mean-field densities to fully folded potentials with ab initio densities. The approach contrasts with phenomenological optical potentials (e.g., Koning and Delaroche, 2003) that fit parameters to elastic scattering data. The use of SCGF densities enables application to medium-mass nuclei up to A ≈ 140, which is a significant advance over the NCSM limitation to A ≤ 16, and opens the path toward consistent structure-reaction descriptions for radioactive beam physics at new rare-isotope facilities.
