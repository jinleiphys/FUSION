# Thermalization of anisotropic quark-gluon plasma produced by decays of color flux tubes

> Kinetic equations are used to study thermalization of the anisotropic quark-gluon plasma produced by decays of color flux tubes possibly created at the very early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The decay rates of the initial color tubes are given by the Schwinger formula, while the collision terms are taken in the relaxation-time approximation. By connecting the relaxation time with viscosity we analyze production and thermalization processes in the plasma characterized by different values of the ratio of the shear viscosity to entropy density.

## Key claim

Kinetic simulations show that a quark-gluon plasma produced by decaying color flux tubes approaches a viscous-hydrodynamics regime within 1–2 fm/c only for the lowest (KSS) value of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, \(4\pi\bar{\eta}=1\); for larger values (\(4\pi\bar{\eta}\geq 3\)), collisions are too inefficient to damp plasma oscillations, making the viscous-hydrodynamics description unsatisfactory.

## Method

The authors solve kinetic equations (Boltzmann-Vlasov type) for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, with particle production from the decay of longitudinal chromoelectric fields given by the Schwinger formula and collision terms treated in the relaxation-time approximation. The relaxation time is connected to the shear viscosity via \(\tau_{\rm eq} = 5\bar{\eta}/T\), where \(\bar{\eta}=\eta/\sigma\) is the constant ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density. The initial chromoelectric field is determined by the Gauss law for a color flux tube of transverse area \(\pi r^2 = 1\ {\rm fm}^2\), with the number of color charges at the tube ends set to \(k=5\) or \(k=10\).

## Key numbers

- The initial chromoelectric field is determined using a quark string tension \(\sigma_q = 1\ {\rm GeV/fm}\) and transverse area \(\pi r^2 = 1\ {\rm fm}^2\).
- Three values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio are considered: \(\bar{\eta} = 1/(4\pi),\ 3/(4\pi),\ 10/(4\pi)\).
- For the lowest viscosity case (\(4\pi\bar{\eta}=1\)), the effective viscosity \(\bar{\eta}_{\rm eff}\) agrees with the input \(\bar{\eta}\) after 1–2 fm/c for both \(k=5\) and \(k=10\).
- For larger viscosities (\(4\pi\bar{\eta}=3\) and \(4\pi\bar{\eta}=10\)), the effective viscosity \(\bar{\eta}_{\rm eff}\) differs from the input \(\bar{\eta}\), indicating that viscous-hydrodynamics after 1–2 fm/c is not satisfactory for \(4\pi\bar{\eta}\geq 3\).
- The effective temperature of the produced plasma reaches \(T_{\rm max} \sim 300-500\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(k=5\) and \(k=10\), values expected at RHIC and the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the color-flux-tube model of Bialas (1987) and others by incorporating a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, connecting the relaxation time to viscosity. It contrasts with earlier kinetic simulations that used fixed \(\bar{\eta}\) independently studied by Ruggieri (2013). The results show that for the lowest (KSS) bound of Kovtun (2004), the system thermalizes into a viscous-hydrodynamics regime within 1–2 fm/c, enabling a bridge between microscopic kinetic theory and macroscopic hydrodynamic descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and the LHC.
