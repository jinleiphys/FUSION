# Higher meson resonances in $ρ\to π^0 π^0 γ$ and $ω\to π^0 π^0 γ$

> The role of higher meson resonances with spin 1 and 2 is investigated quantitatively in the decay processes of $ρ\to π^0π^0 γ$ and $ω\to π^0 π^0 γ$. Among the higher resonances, we find that the $f_2(1270)$ tensor meson can give a nontrivial contribution especially to the $ω\to π^0 π^0 γ$ decay process. When the $f_2$ contribution is combined with the processes involving the vector and scalar meson intermediate states, a good agreement with the recent measurements is achieved for both decays. The effect of the $f_2(1270)$ is found to be sizable at the intermediate photon energies and may be verified by precise measurements of the recoil photon spectrum of the $ω\to π^0 π^0 γ$ decay. The dependence of the decay widths on various models for the $ρ$-$ω$ mixing in the literature is also investigated.

## Key claim

The \(f_2(1270)\) tensor meson, previously neglected because its individual contribution is small, can give a sizable effect through strong interference with other decay amplitudes, especially for \(\omega \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\), and may explain the observed decay width without invoking a direct \(\omega\sigma\gamma\) coupling.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach to compute tree-level amplitudes for \(V \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\) (\(V=\rho,\omega\)) including intermediate vector mesons (\(\rho,\omega\)), the \(f_2(1270)\) tensor meson, chiral loops, and a scalar \(\sigma\) meson. The \(f_2 V\gamma\) coupling was fixed via tensor meson dominance and vector meson dominance, with the \(f_2\pi\pi\) coupling determined from the measured \(\Gamma(f_2 \to \pi\pi)\). The \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing was included for the \(\omega\) decay channel, and the \(\sigma\) meson contribution was treated via the linear sigma model following Bramon et al. (2001, 2003).

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for \(\rho \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\): experimental value \((4.1^{+1.0}_{-0.9} \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-5}\) (SND experiment).
- Branching ratio for \(\omega \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\): experimental value \((6.6^{+1.4}_{-0.8} \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-5}\) (SND experiment).
- With the \(f_2\) meson included in model (C) (vector mesons + \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing + chiral loops + \(\sigma\) meson), the predicted branching ratios are \(4.27 \times 10^{-5}\) for \(\rho \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\) and \(6.36 \times 10^{-5}\) for \(\omega \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\) (using \(G_{fVV} = 5.763\)).
- The \(f_2\) meson alone contributes only 70 eV to \(\Gamma(\rho \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma)\) and 10 eV to \(\Gamma(\omega \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma)\), but interference increases its effect to about 5% for the \(\rho\) decay and 20% for the \(\omega\) decay.
- The \(f_2\) meson effect is most visible at intermediate photon energies around \(k \sim 200\) MeV in the recoil photon spectrum.
- The \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing parameter used is \(\mathcal{M}_{\rho\omega}^2 = -(3.8 \pm 0.4) \times 10^3\) MeV\(^2\).
- The \(f_2\pi\pi\) coupling constant is \(G_{f\pi\pi} \approx 5.76\) (from \(G_{f\pi\pi}^2/4\pi \approx 2.64\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(V \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\) decays that considered only vector meson dominance (Bramon et al., 1992; Guetta and Singer, 2001) and chiral loops with scalar mesons (Bramon et al., 2001, 2003; Marco et al., 1999; Palomar et al., 2002). Those approaches could explain the \(\rho\) decay but underestimated the \(\omega\) decay width, leading some authors (Geng et al., 2000; Kim et al., 2003) to introduce a direct \(\omega\sigma\gamma\) coupling. The present paper shows that including the \(f_2(1270)\) tensor meson can resolve the \(\omega\) decay puzzle without such a direct coupling, and it enables future experimental tests via precise measurements of the recoil photon spectrum in \(\omega \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \gamma\).
