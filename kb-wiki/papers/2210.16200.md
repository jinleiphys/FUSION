# Bremsstrahlung as a probe of baryon stopping in heavy-ion collisions

> In collisions between heavy ions at ultra-relativistic energies the participating protons lose energy, which is converted into new particles. As the protons slow down, they emit bremsstrahlung radiation. The yield and angular distribution of the emitted radiation are sensitive probes of how much energy the incoming protons have lost. In this paper, the spectrum of bremsstrahlung radiation is calculated for different stopping scenarios, and the results are compared with the expected yield of photons from hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

Bremsstrahlung photons emitted when protons slow down in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions can serve as a sensitive probe of baryon stopping, but the feasibility of detecting a signal over the hadronic background depends strongly on the stopping scenario, photon energy, and pseudorapidity range.

## Method

The authors calculated the bremsstrahlung photon spectrum using a semi-classical approach based on the Lienard-Wiechert potentials, integrating over the nuclear charge distribution with a Woods-Saxon form factor. They considered three stopping scenarios for central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC: the Angantyr model in PYTHIA 8.3, the hadron transport model SMASH-2.2, and a phenomenological model with no net protons at midrapidity. The bremsstrahlung yields were compared with the hadronic photon background from PYTHIA 8.3 simulations of the 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Energy density of the quark-gluon plasma formed in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC: 12-14 GeV/fm³ at 1 fm/c after collision
- Lattice QCD deconfinement threshold: 0.2-0.5 GeV/fm³
- Nuclear radius parameter for Pb: R_A = 6.62 fm, with diffuseness a = 0.70 fm and central density ρ₀ = 0.161 fm⁻³
- Average number of participating protons in the 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions: ⟨N_p,part⟩ = 151
- Photon energy range for main analysis: 0.1 ≤ ω ≤ 0.5 GeV
- Photon energy range for low-energy analysis: 0.01 ≤ ω ≤ 0.1 GeV
- Pseudorapidity range where significant signal over background is obtained: η ≳ 4-5
- Photon energy threshold for significant signal: ω ≲ 300-500 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Kapusta (1977) and Bjorken (1984) to use bremsstrahlung as a stopping probe, and follows recent studies by Park (2021) and Park (2022) with improved stopping scenarios that account for the baryon-free central region at LHC energies. The paper provides the first detailed estimate of the hadronic photon background from PYTHIA 8.3, enabling realistic assessments of experimental feasibility. The results can inform the design of future forward detectors such as the ALICE FoCal (planned for 2026-2029) and ALICE-3 (beyond LHC Run 4), which could measure bremsstrahlung photons in the pseudorapidity ranges 3.4 ≤ η ≤ 5.8 and 3.0 ≤ η ≤ 5.0 respectively.
