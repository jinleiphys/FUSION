# Phenomenological Three-Cluster Model of 6He

> By using the method of hyperspherical functions within the appropriate for this method $K_{\min}$ approximation, the simple three-cluster model for description of the ground state and the continuous spectrum states of \6He is developed. It is shown that many properties of \6He (its large rms radius and large values of the matrix elements of electromagnetic transitions from the ground state into the continuous spectrum) follow from the fact that the potential energy of \6He system decreases very slowly (as $ρ^{-3}$) and the binding energy is small.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the large root-mean-square radius and large electromagnetic transition matrix elements of ⁶He follow from the potential energy decreasing as ρ⁻³ and the small binding energy, but this result is obtained within the K_min approximation of the hyperspherical functions method, which neglects hypermomentum components with K>2.

## Method

The authors solve a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation for the hyperradial wave function χ(ρ) of a three-cluster (α+n+n) system, using a phenomenological effective potential V(ρ)=V₀/(1+(ρ/a)³) that has the correct asymptotic ρ⁻³ behavior. The hypermomentum is fixed at K=2 for 0⁺ states and K=2 for 2⁺ states, consistent with the K_min approximation that satisfies the Pauli principle. The equation is integrated numerically from ρ=0 to a large cutoff radius ρ_max, with parameters V₀ and a adjusted to reproduce experimental binding energy and rms radius for the ground state, or resonance energy and width for the 2⁺ state.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: E = -0.97 MeV (experimental value used to fit the potential)
- Ground state rms radius: √⟨r²⟩ = 2.57 Fm (experimental value used to fit the potential)
- Potential parameters for 0⁺ ground state: V₀ = -87 MeV, a = 3.073 Fm
- Hyperradius mean value: √⟨ρ²⟩ = 5.59 Fm
- Asymptotic decay constant: κ ≈ 0.22 Fm⁻¹
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient: C ≈ 0.12 Fm⁻¹/² (compared to √(2κ) ≈ 0.66 Fm⁻¹/²)
- Low-energy phase shift coefficient: δ ≈ A k with A = 3.95 Fm
- Approximate ground-state energy from phase shift: ε_approx ≈ 1.3 MeV (cf. ε_exp = 0.97 MeV)
- 2⁺ resonance energy: 0.822 ± 0.025 MeV
- 2⁺ resonance width: 0.113 ± 0.020 MeV
- Potential parameters for 2⁺ resonance: V₀ = -92 MeV, a = 2.834 Fm

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics approach to three-cluster systems, building on earlier studies of the Pauli principle in the harmonic-oscillator basis (Ho-basis) and the K-harmonics method (K-harm). The slow ρ⁻³ asymptotic behavior of the three-body potential was previously noted (Rho-cube). The results enable predictions for electrodisintegration cross-sections of ⁶He and radiative capture of two neutrons by an alpha particle, and the phase shift analysis follows the framework of Landau and Lifshitz (Landau) and L'vov and Korsheninnikov (LK-63). The 2⁺ resonance parameters are compared with experimental data from Ajzenberg-Selove (Ajz).
