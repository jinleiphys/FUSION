# Skyrme pseudo-potential-based EDF parametrisation for spuriousity-free MR-EDF calculations

> First exploratory steps towards a pseudo-potential-based Skyrme energy density functional for spuriousity-free multi-reference calculations are presented. A qualitatively acceptable fit can be accomplished by adding simple three- and four-body contact terms to the standard central plus spin-orbit two-body terms. To achieve quantitative predictive power, higher-order terms, e.g. velocity-dependent three-body terms, will be required.

## Key claim

A pseudo-potential-based Skyrme energy density functional that is free of the spurious contributions plaguing standard multi-reference EDF calculations can be constructed by adding simple three- and four-body contact terms to the standard two-body central plus spin-orbit terms, but achieving quantitative predictive power will require higher-order terms such as velocity-dependent three-body terms.

## Method

The authors constructed a pseudo-Hamiltonian consisting of a two-body Skyrme operator, a three-body contact term with coupling constant u₀, and a four-body contact term with coupling constant v₀, then computed the full energy density functional as the expectation value of this operator, including all exchange and pairing terms. They performed a least-square fit of the coupling constants to nuclear matter properties and a set of nuclear masses and radii, starting from the SIV parametrisation and adjusting parameters to obtain repulsive spin channels and attractive pairing while avoiding finite-size instabilities. The resulting parametrisation, SLyMR0, was tested in spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov single-reference calculations of singly magic even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³ for SLyMR0 (compared to 0.160 fm⁻³ for SLy4)
- Energy per particle E/A = -15.04 MeV for SLyMR0 (compared to -15.97 MeV for SLy4)
- Symmetry energy a_sym = 23.0 MeV for SLyMR0 (compared to 32.0 MeV for SLy4)
- Isoscalar effective mass m*₀/m = 0.47 for SLyMR0 (compared to 0.69 for SLy4)
- Incompressibility K_∞ = 264.2 MeV for SLyMR0 (compared to 229.9 MeV for SLy4)
- Spin-Landau parameter g₀ = 0.88 for SLyMR0 (compared to 1.38 for SLy4)
- Pairing cutoff at 8.5 MeV above and below the Fermi energy used in HFB calculations
- Coupling constants: t₀ = -1210.093228 MeV·fm³, u₀ = 2529.151191 MeV·fm⁶, v₀ = -14750.0 MeV·fm⁹

## Context

This work extends the earlier pseudo-potential-based Skyrme parametrisations SIII, SIV, SV, and SHZ2 from the 1970s (Beiner 1975, Satula 2012), which suffered from spin instabilities or inadequate pairing, by adding three- and four-body contact terms. The resulting SLyMR0 parametrisation enables spuriousity-free multi-reference EDF calculations for benchmarking, though its low symmetry energy (23.0 MeV versus 32.0 MeV for SLy4 from Chabanat 1997) limits mass predictions. The next step, already underway, is to include the most general central three-body contact operator up to second order in gradients to approach the quality of modern general Skyrme EDFs.
