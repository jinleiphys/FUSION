# Consistent parameter fixing in the quark-meson model with vacuum fluctuations

> We revisit the renormalization prescription for the quark-meson model in an extended mean-field approximation, where vacuum quark fluctuations are included. At a given cutoff scale the model parameters are fixed by fitting vacuum quantities, typically including the sigma-meson mass $m_σ$ and the pion decay constant $f_π$. In most publications the latter is identified with the expectation value of the sigma field, while for $m_σ$ the curvature mass is taken. When quark loops are included, this prescription is however inconsistent, and the correct identification involves the renormalized pion decay constant and the sigma pole mass. In the present article we investigate the influence of the parameter-fixing scheme on the phase structure of the model at finite temperature and chemical potential. Despite large differences between the model parameters in the two schemes, we find that in homogeneous matter the effect on the phase diagram is relatively small. For inhomogeneous phases, on the other hand, the choice of the proper renormalization prescription is crucial. In particular, we show that if renormalization effects on the pion decay constant are not considered, the model does not even present a well-defined renormalized limit when the cutoff is sent to infinity.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the commonly used "BC" parameter-fixing scheme (identifying the pion decay constant with the sigma field expectation value and the sigma mass with the curvature mass) is theoretically inconsistent when vacuum quark fluctuations are included, but for homogeneous phases it yields results practically indistinguishable from the correct "RP" scheme (using the renormalized pion decay constant and sigma pole mass) after a moderate adjustment of the input sigma mass; for inhomogeneous phases, however, the choice of scheme is crucial, and the BC scheme does not even have a well-defined renormalized limit when the cutoff is sent to infinity.

## Method

The authors compare two parameter-fixing schemes in the quark-meson model within an extended mean-field approximation that includes vacuum quark fluctuations, regularized via a Pauli-Villars scheme. The BC scheme identifies the pion decay constant with the vacuum expectation value of the sigma field and the sigma mass with the curvature mass, while the RP scheme uses the renormalized pion decay constant and the sigma pole mass. They compute the thermodynamic potential for homogeneous matter and for a one-dimensional chiral density wave (CDW) inhomogeneous phase, and perform a Ginzburg-Landau analysis to study the critical point and Lifshitz point as functions of the cutoff parameter.

## Key numbers

* For numerical examples, the chiral limit value of the pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi = 88\) MeV and the vacuum constituent quark mass as \(M_v = 300\) MeV, with the sigma mass input value \(m_\sigma = 600\) MeV in the parameter comparison figure.
* When \(m_\sigma = 2M_v = 600\) MeV, the ratio \(\lambda/g^4\) becomes identical in both schemes, yielding the same homogeneous phase diagrams despite very different behavior of the individual parameters.
* In the BC scheme with \(m_\sigma = 2M_v\), the inhomogeneous phase disappears already at a Pauli-Villars cutoff of \(\Lambda = 400\) MeV, while in the RP scheme it survives in the renormalized limit.
* The renormalized limit of the Ginzburg-Landau coefficient \(\gamma_{4,b}\) in the BC scheme diverges logarithmically because \(g^2\) is a constant (from \(g^2 = M_v^2/f_\pi^2\)), while in the RP scheme it is UV-finite.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Carignano (2014) on inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in the quark-meson model, where it was found that including Dirac sea reduces the inhomogeneous phase and that the Lifshitz point coincides with the critical point when the sigma mass equals twice the constituent quark mass. The paper corrects the standard parameter-fixing procedure used in earlier extended mean-field studies such as Skokov (2010), Chatterjee (2011), Gupta (2011), Herbst (2013), and Andersen (2012), who employed the curvature mass and bare pion decay constant. The results enable consistent treatment of inhomogeneous phases in renormalizable chiral models and show that the BC scheme, while accidentally adequate for homogeneous matter, is unsuitable for studying spatially modulated phases.
