# Strong CP Violation and large-$N_c$ spin-flavor symmetry

> We revisit the contribution of the QCD $\bar θ$ term to the CP violating pion-nucleon couplings and the nucleon electric dipole moment in a combined large-$N_c$ and chiral perturbation theory framework. In particular, we approach this issue through the emergent spin-flavor symmetry of the baryon sector at large but finite $N_c$. We obtain good agreement with previous analyses for the pion-nucleon couplings and show that the large-$N_c$ framework indicates that tree-level contributions to the electric dipole moment possibly play a dominant role. The spin-flavor symmetry also enables us to provide novel constraints on CP violating pion-$Δ$ couplings as well as the $Δ$ electric dipole moment and $ΔN$ transition moment.

## Key claim

The paper shows that combining large-\(N_c\) spin-flavor symmetry with explicit inclusion of the U(1)\(_A\) anomaly yields CP-violating pion-nucleon couplings consistent with previous SU(2)\(_L \times\) SU(2)\(_R\) chiral perturbation theory results, but with the caveat that the large-\(N_c\) framework indicates tree-level contributions to the nucleon electric dipole moment may dominate over chiral loop contributions, contrary to the usual chiral perturbation theory expectation.

## Method

The author constructs a combined large-\(N_c\) and chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian for two light quark flavors, incorporating the U(1)\(_A\) anomaly explicitly through a term proportional to \(a/N_c\) that gives mass to the isoscalar meson. The baryon sector is treated using the emergent SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry, where baryon matrix elements of QCD operators are expanded in terms of spin-flavor generators \(\hat J^i\), \(\hat I^a\), and \(\hat G^{ia}\). The CP-violating \(\bar\theta\) term is included via the quark mass matrix, and the vacuum alignment is determined by minimizing the effective potential. The resulting Lagrangian is matched onto the standard form for CP-violating pion-nucleon couplings, and the spin-flavor expansion is used to relate nucleon and \(\Delta\) baryon couplings.

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility of pure Yang-Mills: \(\chi_{\rm YM}^{1/4} = 188.8(1.8)\ {\rm MeV}\)
- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F_0 = 86.2(5)\ {\rm MeV}\); at physical pion mass: \(F_\pi = 92.3\ {\rm MeV}\)
- Isoscalar meson mass from Witten-Veneziano formula: \(m_0 = 827(17)\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(N_f = 2\), equivalently \(a/N_c = (585(12)\ {\rm MeV})^2\)
- CP-violating isovector pion-nucleon coupling: \(\bar g_0^{(\pi NN)}/(2F_0) = 0.7(1)\,\tilde\theta = 0.017(3)\,\bar\theta\)
- CP-violating isoscalar pion-nucleon coupling: \(\bar g_1^{(\pi NN)}/(2F_0) = -0.004(1)\,\bar\theta\)
- Ratio \(\bar g_1^{(\pi NN)}/\bar g_0^{(\pi NN)} \sim -0.2\)
- Average quark mass: \(\bar m = 3.42(9)\ {\rm MeV}\); isospin breaking parameter: \(\varepsilon = 0.37(3)\); neutron-proton mass splitting: \(\delta m_N = 2.49(17)\ {\rm MeV}\)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term coefficient: \(c_1 = -1.0(3)\ {\rm GeV}^{-1}\)
- Ratio of CP-violating \(\pi\Delta\Delta\) to \(\pi NN\) couplings in SU(4) limit: \(g_0^{(\pi\Delta\Delta)}/g_0^{(\pi NN)} = -3 + O(1/N_c^2)\); \(g_1^{(\pi\Delta\Delta)}/g_1^{(\pi NN)} = 1 + O(1/N_c^2)\)

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) treatment of the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term from earlier studies by Pich and de Rafael (1991), Borasoy (2000), and Ottnad et al. (2010), which incorporated large-\(N_c\) constraints in the meson sector but not the emergent spin-flavor symmetry in the baryon sector. The results for \(\bar g_0^{(\pi NN)}\) agree with SU(2) chiral perturbation theory calculations by Mereghetti et al. (2010, 2011) and Hockings and van Kolck (2005), while the isoscalar coupling \(\bar g_1^{(\pi NN)}\) is constrained in terms of the pion-nucleon sigma term and the topological susceptibility, extending the lattice QCD spectroscopy approach of de Vries et al. (2015, 2017). The spin-flavor symmetry provides novel constraints on CP-violating pion-\(\Delta\) couplings and \(\Delta\) electric dipole moments, which could serve as benchmarks for future lattice QCD calculations and inform the parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon potential.
