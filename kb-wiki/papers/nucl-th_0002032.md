# In-medium Pion-Pion Interaction and Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> We discuss medium modifications of the unitarized pion-pion interaction in the nuclear medium. We incorporate both the effects of chiral symmetry restoration and the influence of collective nuclear pionic modes, originating from the p-wave coupling of the pion to delta-hole configurations. We show how the resulting strong enhancement of the sigma-meson spectral function is related to large fluctuations of the condensate associated with the partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the strong enhancement of the sigma-meson spectral function near the two-pion threshold in nuclear matter results from combining chiral symmetry restoration effects with p-wave coupling of pions to delta-hole states, and that this enhancement is a precursor of chiral symmetry restoration associated with large fluctuations of the quark condensate. The authors qualify that at normal nuclear-matter density the threshold peak is enhanced by a factor three compared to a pure p-wave calculation, rather than the factor four obtained in a dropping sigma-mass scenario.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute density-dependent values of the pion mass, sigma-meson mass, and pion decay constant, which are then used to construct an in-medium pion-pion potential formally equivalent to the linear sigma model. P-wave coupling of the pion to delta-hole states is incorporated via a standard nuclear-matter approach with short-range screening parameter g'_ΔΔ = 0.5, and the scattering amplitude is unitarized by including a two-pion loop in the sigma propagator. The sigma-meson spectral function is calculated at various densities up to twice normal nuclear-matter density.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum values used: f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 139 MeV, m_σ = 1 GeV
- At normal nuclear-matter density (ρ_0): m_σ = 795 MeV, m_π = 143.1 MeV, f_π = 79 MeV
- At twice normal nuclear-matter density (2 ρ_0): m_σ = 510 MeV, m_π = 161 MeV, f_π = 56 MeV
- At normal nuclear-matter density, the threshold peak enhancement from chiral symmetry restoration on top of p-wave effects is a factor three compared to pure p-wave calculation
- The s-wave optical potential from the NJL model is 4 MeV at ρ_0, with minor influence from a more phenomenological value of 10 MeV
- The four-quark condensate Q_2 increases with density when chiral symmetry restoration is incorporated, with ΔQ_2 = 0.62 at 2 ρ_0 (compared to ΔQ_1 = -0.75 for pure p-wave case)
- The kappa factor Δκ_2 reaches 3.74 at 2 ρ_0 (compared to Δκ_1 = 1.51 for pure p-wave case)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Aouissat et al. (1999) that used a dropping sigma-mass relation from Brown-Rho scaling, finding a factor four enhancement, while the present NJL-based approach yields a factor three enhancement at normal density. The results are relevant to the CHAOS collaboration data on π-2π reactions on various nuclei (Bonutti et al., 1996), though the authors note that Vicentini et al. (1999) questioned whether those reactions occur only at very low density due to pion absorption. The paper enables further study of precritical chiral fluctuations and their connection to four-quark condensates in QCD sum rule analyses.
