# The Off-Shell Nucleon-Nucleon Amplitude: Why it is Unmeasurable in Nucleon-Nucleon Bremsstrahlung

> Nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung has long been considered a way of getting information about the off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitude which would allow one to distinguish among nucleon-nucleon potentials based on their off-shell properties. There have been many calculations and many experiments devoted to this aim. We show here, in contrast to this standard view, that such off-shell amplitudes are not measurable as a matter of principle. This follows formally from the invariance of the S-matrix under transformations of the fields. This result is discussed here and illustrated via two simple models, one applying to spin zero, and one to spin one half, processes. The latter model is very closely related to phenomenological models which have been used to study off-shell effects at electromagnetic vertices.

## Key claim

Contrary to the long-standing view that nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung can measure off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and thereby distinguish among potentials, Fearing shows that such off-shell amplitudes are not measurable as a matter of principle, because field transformations can change the off-shell part of the elastic amplitude arbitrarily without changing the full bremsstrahlung amplitude.

## Method

The argument is made formally via the invariance of the S-matrix under transformations of the fields in the Lagrangian, and is illustrated with two explicit field-theory models. The first model treats spin-zero pion-pion bremsstrahlung using a chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian extended with equation-of-motion terms; the second treats spin-one-half proton-neutron bremsstrahlung using a simplified scalar-boson-exchange strong interaction with a field transformation that generates off-shell contributions at both strong and electromagnetic vertices. In both models, the full bremsstrahlung amplitude is shown to be independent of the parameters that govern the off-shell elastic amplitude, because those parameters cancel exactly against corresponding contact terms.

## Key numbers

- The spin-zero model Lagrangian includes parameters \(\beta_1\) and \(\beta_2\) (denoted \(\bta\) and \(\btb\) in the text) that govern the off-shell elastic amplitude; the full bremsstrahlung amplitude is independent of these parameters.
- The spin-one-half model introduces arbitrary constants \(\tilde{a}\) and \(\tilde{b}\) (denoted \(\atil\) and \(\btil\)) that generate off-shell contributions; the full bremsstrahlung amplitude is independent of these parameters.
- The paper contains no experimental cross-section numbers or precision values; it is a formal proof-of-principle argument.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the program initiated by Ashkin and Marshak (1949), who proposed using nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung to extract off-shell information, and the many subsequent calculations and experiments designed to measure off-shell effects. It extends the general theorem of Haag (1958) on field transformations to the specific case of bremsstrahlung. The result implies that previous potential-model calculations and experiments aimed at distinguishing potentials via off-shell behavior are invalid for that purpose; instead, the paper argues that future work should focus on comprehensive experiments and microscopic calculations of the contact terms, which reflect the internal currents of the strong interaction.
