# Sarma phase in relativistic and non-relativistic systems

> We investigate the stability of the Sarma phase in two-component fermion systems in three spatial dimensions. For this purpose we compare strongly-correlated systems with either relativistic or non-relativistic dispersion relation: relativistic quarks and mesons at finite isospin density and spin-imbalanced ultracold Fermi gases. Using a Functional Renormalization Group approach, we resolve fluctuation effects onto the corresponding phase diagrams beyond the mean-field approximation. We find that fluctuations induce a second order phase transition at zero temperature, and thus a Sarma phase, in the relativistic setup for large isospin chemical potential. This motivates the investigation of the cold atoms setup with comparable mean-field phase structure, where the Sarma phase could then be realized in experiment. However, for the non-relativistic system we find the stability region of the Sarma phase to be smaller than the one predicted from mean-field theory. It is limited to the BEC side of the phase diagram, and the unitary Fermi gas does not support a Sarma phase at zero temperature. Finally, we propose an ultracold quantum gas with four fermion species that has a good chance to realize a zero-temperature Sarma phase.

## Key claim

Fluctuations beyond mean-field theory induce a second-order phase transition at zero temperature and thus a stable Sarma phase in the relativistic quark-meson model for large isospin chemical potential, but in the non-relativistic ultracold Fermi gas the Sarma phase stability region is smaller than mean-field predictions and does not extend to zero temperature at unitarity.

## Method

The authors employed a Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach in the local potential approximation (LPA) to compute the effective potential for both a relativistic quark-meson model (with quarks, sigma, and pions at finite quark and isospin chemical potentials) and a non-relativistic two-channel model of ultracold fermions near a broad Feshbach resonance. The effective potential was discretized on a grid in field space to resolve first-order transitions and competing minima, and the mean-field results were recovered by neglecting bosonic contributions to the flow equations.

## Key numbers

- For the relativistic system at fixed isospin chemical potential μ_I = m_π, the onset of pion condensation occurs at μ_I = m_π/2 at vanishing quark chemical potential.
- For the relativistic system, a first-order transition inside the pion condensation phase appears for μ_I > 0.79 m_π when bosonic fluctuations are included.
- For the non-relativistic unitary Fermi gas at mean-field level: critical temperature at zero imbalance T_c/μ = 0.665; critical imbalance at zero temperature δμ_c/μ = 0.807; critical point at (δμ_CP/μ, T_CP/μ) = (0.704, 0.373).
- For the non-relativistic unitary Fermi gas with FRG including bosonic fluctuations: critical temperature at zero imbalance T_c/μ = 0.40; critical imbalance at zero temperature δμ_c/μ = 0.83.
- The experimental value for the critical imbalance in the unitary Fermi gas is δμ_c/μ = 0.89 (PhysRevA.88.063614).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of Sarma phase stability between relativistic and non-relativistic fermion systems, motivated by the qualitative agreement of their mean-field phase diagrams. The relativistic results build on prior FRG studies of the quark-meson model by Kamikado (2012), while the non-relativistic analysis extends mean-field studies of the imbalanced BCS-BEC crossover by Gubbels (2006) and others. The finding that bosonic fluctuations induce a zero-temperature Sarma phase in the relativistic system but not in the non-relativistic unitary Fermi gas motivates the proposal of a four-fermion-species ultracold gas as a candidate for realizing a zero-temperature Sarma phase experimentally.
