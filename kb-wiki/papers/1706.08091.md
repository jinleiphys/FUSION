# The static hard-loop gluon propagator to all orders in anisotropy

> We calculate the (semi-)static hard-loop self-energy and propagator using the Keldysh formalism in a momentum-space anisotropic quark-gluon plasma. The static retarded, advanced, and Feynman (symmetric) self-energies and propagators are calculated to all orders in the momentum-space anisotropy parameter $ξ$. For the retarded and advanced self-energies/propagators, we present a concise derivation and comparison with previously-obtained results and extend the calculation of the self-energies to next-to-leading order in the gluon energy, $ω$. For the Feynman self-energy/propagator, we present new results which are accurate to all orders in $ξ$. We compare our exact results with prior expressions for the Feynman self-energy/propagator which were obtained using Taylor-expansions around an isotropic state. We show that, unlike the Taylor-expanded results, the all-orders expression for the Feynman propagator is free from infrared singularities. Finally, we discuss the application of our results to the calculation of the imaginary-part of the heavy-quark potential in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the static hard-loop gluon propagator to all orders in the momentum-space anisotropy parameter $\xi$, extending previous work that relied on Taylor expansions around the isotropic state ($\xi=0$). The authors show that the all-orders expression for the Feynman propagator is free from infrared singularities, unlike the Taylor-expanded results.

## Method

The authors use the real-time Keldysh formalism in the high-temperature hard-loop limit to compute the retarded, advanced, and Feynman self-energies and propagators for a momentum-space anisotropic quark-gluon plasma with a Romatschke-Strickland distribution function. The self-energies are decomposed into a four-tensor basis appropriate for systems with one anisotropy direction, and the dressed propagators are obtained by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation. For the retarded and advanced self-energies, the calculation is extended to next-to-leading order in the gluon energy $\omega$, while the Feynman self-energy is computed exactly to all orders in $\xi$.

## Key numbers

- The QED Debye mass is defined as $m_D^2 = e^2\lambda^2/3$, where $\lambda$ is the temperature-like scale.
- The QCD Debye mass is $m_D^2 = (2N_c+N_f)g^2\lambda^2/6$, with $N_c$ the number of colors and $N_f$ the number of quark flavors.
- The static limit of the inverse propagator components yields $\lim_{\omega\rightarrow 0}\Delta_A^{-1} = -({\bf p}^2 + m_\alpha^2)$ and $\lim_{\omega\rightarrow 0}\Delta_G^{-1} = -\frac{\omega^2}{{\bf p}^2}\left[({\bf p}^2+m_\alpha^2+m_\gamma^2)({\bf p}^2+m_\beta^2) - m_\delta^4\right]$, where $m_\alpha^2$, $m_\beta^2$, $m_\gamma^2$, and $m_\delta^4$ are given by explicit expressions involving the anisotropy parameter $\xi$ and momentum components.
- The quantity $m_\delta^4$ is given by $m_\delta^4 = \frac{\pi^2 m_D^4 \xi^2 p_z^2 p_x^2 {\bf{p}}^2}{16({\bf{p}}^2 + \xi p_x ^2)^{3}}$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the heavy-quark potential in a momentum-space anisotropic QGP, where the real part was obtained to all orders in $\xi$ by Dumitru (2007) but the imaginary part relied on a Taylor expansion to linear order in $\xi$ by Burnier (2009). The results lay the groundwork for computing the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential to all orders in $\xi$, which can be combined with anisotropic hydrodynamics backgrounds for phenomenological calculations of heavy quarkonia suppression in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
