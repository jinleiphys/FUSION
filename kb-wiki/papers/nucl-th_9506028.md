# Compton scattering from a pion and off-shell effects

> We discuss the most general form of the electromagnetic vertex of an off-shell pion. The framework of chiral perturbation theory is used to illustrate the representation dependence of the electromagnetic three-point Green's function. For that purpose we discuss the concept of field transformations which, in comparison with the standard Gasser and Leutwyler Lagrangian, generate equivalent Lagrangians containing additional terms at order p^4 proportional to the lowest-order equation of motion. We consider Compton scattering from a pion to show that calculations involving different off-shell effects in the s- and u-channel pole diagrams may nevertheless lead to the same on-shell Compton scattering amplitude. This is a result of the equivalence theorem which states that two equivalent Lagrangians predict the same S-matrix elements even though they may generate a different off-shell behaviour of Green's functions. We conclude that off-shell effects are not only model dependent but also representation dependent.

## Key claim

Off-shell effects in the electromagnetic vertex of a pion are not only model dependent but also representation dependent: different but physically equivalent representations of the same microscopic theory (chiral perturbation theory) generate different off-shell form functions while yielding the same on-shell Compton scattering amplitude, making it impossible to uniquely extract off-shell contributions from on-shell observables.

## Method

The author uses chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at O(p⁴) for mesons to compute the electromagnetic three-point Green's function of an off-shell pion and the Compton scattering amplitude γπ⁺ → γπ⁺. Field transformations are applied to the standard Gasser and Leutwyler Lagrangian to generate equivalent Lagrangians containing additional terms proportional to the lowest-order equation of motion, parameterized by coefficients β₁ and β₂. The irreducible renormalized vertex, propagator, and contact interactions are calculated consistently, and the resulting on-shell Compton amplitude is compared across representations.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant in the chiral limit is F₀ ≈ 93 MeV.
- The off-shell electromagnetic vertex for real photons (q² = 0) at O(p⁴) is Γ_R^μ(p_f,p_i) = (p_f+p_i)^μ [1 + 16β₁(p_f² + p_i² − 2M_π²)/F_π²].
- The renormalized propagator at O(p⁴) is iΔ_R(p) = i / [p² − M_π² + (16β₁/F_π²)(p² − M_π²)² + iε].
- The difference in the pole contribution to the Compton amplitude between β₁ ≠ 0 and β₁ = 0 is ΔM_P = −ie² (64β₁/F_π²) (p_f·ε' p_i·ε + p_f·ε p_i·ε').
- The contact interaction from the same Lagrangian exactly cancels this difference: ΔM_{γγππ} = ie² (64β₁/F_π²) (p_f·ε' p_i·ε + p_f·ε p_i·ε').

## Context

This work extends the analysis of off-shell effects in electromagnetic interactions of pions, building on the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985). It directly addresses the representation dependence of off-shell form functions, contrasting with earlier approaches that treated off-shell effects as model-dependent but representation-independent. The result enables a clearer understanding of the limitations of extracting off-shell information from on-shell scattering data, and it cautions against overinterpreting off-shell vertices in processes such as pion photo- and electroproduction, pion-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and meson exchange currents in electron-nucleus scattering.
