# Remnants of Initial Anisotropic High Energy Density Domains in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Anisotropic high energy density domains may be formed at early stages of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, e.g. due to phase transition dynamics or non-equilibrium phenomena like (mini-)jets. Here we investigate hadronic observables resulting from an initially created anisotropic high energy density domain. Based on our studies using a transport model we find that the initial anisotropies are reflected in the freeze-out multiplicity distribution of both pions and kaons due to secondary hadronic rescattering. The anisotropy appears to be stronger for particles at high transverse momenta. The overall kaon multiplicity increases with large fluctuations of local energy densities, while no change has been found in the pion multiplicity.

## Key claim
Initial spatial anisotropies in high-energy-density domains created at early stages of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can survive hadronic rescattering and produce measurable anisotropies in freeze-out particle multiplicity distributions, though the translation factor from configuration-space asymmetry to momentum-space anisotropy is small (1.5–8 percent depending on transverse momentum window).

## Method
The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) transport model (version 2.3) to simulate head-on Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy (158 GeV/nucleon) with impact parameter b=0. At t=1 fm/c in the center-of-mass frame, a randomly chosen fraction of hadrons (10% or 20%) with rapidity |y|<1.5 was artificially moved into an elliptical cylinder (8 fm major axis × 2 fm minor axis) in the transverse plane to create anisotropic high-energy-density domains beyond the model's intrinsic fluctuations, after which RQMD propagated the hadrons until freeze-out.

## Key numbers
- For case 1 (10% of hadrons moved, ~90 hadrons representing 120 GeV total energy moved per event): 30% increase of total energy within the cylinder, 20–30% increase in total number of binary collisions, ~11K events studied.
- For case 2 (20% of hadrons moved, ~180 hadrons representing 240 GeV total energy moved per event): 60% increase of total energy within the cylinder, 40–50% increase in total number of binary collisions, ~10K events studied.
- Azimuthal anisotropy parameter α for charged pions: 0.35% for case 1 and 0.69% for case 2 (fit to dN/dϕ ∝ 1−α cos(2ϕ)).
- For case 2, pion anisotropy α increased from 0.4% for pions with p⊥<0.5 GeV/c to 2.2% for pions with p⊥>0.8 GeV/c.
- For a 27% asymmetry in the initial configuration distribution (case 2), a 0.7% anisotropy was found in the pion distribution at freeze-out.
- Translation factor from initial configuration-space asymmetry to final momentum-space anisotropy varied between 1.5% and 8% depending on transverse momentum window.
- Pion multiplicity was reduced by 8% and kaon multiplicity increased by over 50% when including rescattering in RQMD at SPS energy (from prior work by one of the authors).

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of inhomogeneous initial conditions in heavy ion collisions, including Gyulassy et al. (1997) on mini-jet production causing azimuthal asymmetry, Mrówczyński (1997) on color filamentation, and Kapusta et al. (1995) on QGP nucleation. It uses the RQMD approach, which had previously been shown to describe strangeness enhancement systematics (energy and mass dependence) observed by experiments such as E802, NA35, and others. The study enables future event-by-event investigations of initial-state fluctuations and their observable consequences, though the authors note the effect is relatively weak and further work is needed on dependence on location, size, and shape of the high-energy-density domain.
