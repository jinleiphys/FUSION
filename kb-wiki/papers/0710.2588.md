# Phase space constraints and statistical jet studies in heavy-ion collisions

> The effect of the correlation induced by global momentum conservation on the two-particle distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions is discussed, with a focus on the generic case of collisions with a non-vanishing impact parameter.

# Phase space constraints and statistical jet studies in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Global momentum conservation induces a non-trivial two-particle correlation in nucleus-nucleus collisions that, even when the number of emitted particles is large, can produce azimuthal structures (such as a dip at Δφ = π for out-of-plane triggers) that mimic signals attributed to parton energy loss or medium-induced jet modifications, and must therefore be subtracted before interpreting such signals as dynamical effects.

## Method
The author derives the two-particle cumulant from transverse momentum conservation to leading order in 1/N using a saddle-point approximation of the generating function of multiparticle distributions, then incorporates anisotropic flow via the coefficient \(\bar v_2 \equiv \langle p_x^2 - p_y^2 \rangle / \langle p_x^2 + p_y^2 \rangle\). The resulting two-particle joint probability distribution is expanded in Fourier harmonics of the pair angle, and the conditional probability for an associated particle given a trigger particle is computed both directly and after a "flow-subtracted" ZYAM-style subtraction.

## Key numbers
- The two-particle cumulant scales like \(1/N^{M-1}\) for M particles in the large-N limit.
- For the numerical illustration: \(p_{T1} = 6\) GeV/\(c\), \(p_{T2} = 4\) GeV/\(c\), \(v_2(p_{T2}) = 0.2\), \(\bar v_2 = 0.1\), \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle = (500\ \text{MeV}/c)^2\), and total multiplicity \(N = 8000\).
- The two-particle cumulant expression (Eq. f_c(2)) contains a term proportional to \(-\frac{2 p_{T1} p_{T2}}{N \langle p_T^2 \rangle (1 - \bar v_2^2)}\) times an angular factor.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier derivation of multiparticle cumulants from momentum conservation (Borghini 2003) to the case of non-zero impact parameter (anisotropic flow). It directly addresses the interpretation of two-particle correlation measurements at RHIC, where structures such as a lower away-side yield for out-of-plane triggers (Adams 2004) and a dip at Δφ = π (Ulery 2005, Adare 2007) have been attributed to path-length-dependent parton energy loss (Wang 2000) or Mach-cone-like medium response. The paper argues that these same qualitative features arise from the combination of momentum conservation and elliptic flow alone, and must be quantitatively accounted for before claiming new dynamical effects.
