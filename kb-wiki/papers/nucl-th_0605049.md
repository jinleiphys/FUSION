# Lorentz boosted nucleon-nucleon potential applied to the 3He(e,e'p)pn and 3He(e,e'n)pp processes

> We formulate an approximate relativistic framework for an analysis of the 3He(e,e'p)pn and 3He(e,e'n)pp reactions with polarized electrons and 3He in the initial state. Restricting the rescattering series to one term linear in the two-nucleon t-matrix we incorporate various relativistic features when calculating a nuclear current matrix element. These relativistic ingredients encompass the relativistic 3He wave function based on the concept of the Lorentz boosted nucleon-nucleon potential together with the boosted 2N t-matrix, relativistic kinematics and relativistic single-nucleon current operator. This allows us to estimate the magnitude of relativistic effects not included in the standard nonrelativistic approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents an approximate relativistic framework for analyzing electron-induced breakup of 3He, but the approach is not a fully consistent relativistic three-nucleon scattering calculation; it restricts the rescattering series to one term linear in the two-nucleon t-matrix and includes only one "spectator" pair of nucleons assumed not to participate in photon absorption.

## Method

The authors formulate a relativistic framework for the 3He(e,e'p)pn and 3He(e,e'n)pp reactions by incorporating a Lorentz boosted nucleon-nucleon potential, a boosted two-nucleon t-matrix, relativistic kinematics, and a relativistic single-nucleon current operator. They solve the relativistic Faddeev equation for the 3He bound state using the boosted potential, then compute nuclear current matrix elements in a plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) plus a single rescattering term (FSI23) where final state interactions are restricted to one nucleon pair. The boosted t-matrix is obtained via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation that is transformed into a form solvable with standard nonrelativistic techniques.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic binding energies for 3He are smaller by 0.3-0.45 MeV compared to nonrelativistic values based on the same two-nucleon potentials
- Differences between nonrelativistic and relativistic single-nucleon momentum distributions appear only for momenta q greater than approximately 3 fm^-1
- Boost effects in the momentum distribution become visible for q greater than approximately 6 fm^-1
- The approximate boosted potential (leading order in q/omega and v/omega expansion) produces results nearly overlapping with the full relativistic calculation

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kamada (2002) which introduced Lorentz boosted NN potentials for the 3N bound state problem, now applying it to electron-induced breakup reactions. The framework is less rigorous than full relativistic 3N scattering calculations (which the authors note do not yet exist), but provides a practical tool for analyzing experiments at high energy and momentum transfers where nonrelativistic treatments fail. The approximation of restricting final state interactions to one spectator pair was used successfully in earlier analyses by Xu (2003) and Carasco (2003). The results enable estimation of relativistic effects not captured by standard nonrelativistic approaches, though the authors note an alternative method by Keister (2006) provides a way to obtain boosted NN t-matrices without approximation.
