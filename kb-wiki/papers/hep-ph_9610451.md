# Instantons in QCD

> We review the theory and phenomenology of instantons in QCD. After a general overview, we provide a pedagogical introduction to semi-classical methods in quantum mechanics and field theory. The main part of the review summarizes our understanding of the instanton liquid in QCD and the role of instantons in generating the spectrum of light hadrons. We also discuss properties of instantons at finite temperature and the chiral phase transition. We give an overview over the importance of instantons in some other models, in particular two dimensional sigma models, electroweak theory and supersymmetric QCD.

## Key claim

Instantons provide a semi-classical mechanism for understanding non-perturbative QCD phenomena, particularly chiral symmetry breaking and hadron structure, but the instanton ensemble cannot be stabilized by purely classical interactions alone, and the separation of perturbative and non-perturbative effects remains incomplete except in special cases like quantum mechanics and supersymmetric field theory.

## Method

The authors review the theory and phenomenology of instantons in QCD by first providing a pedagogical introduction to semi-classical methods in quantum mechanics and field theory, then summarizing the instanton liquid model and its role in generating the spectrum of light hadrons. ## Context

This review extends the early work on instantons by Callan, Dashen, and Gross (1978) and the introductory reviews by Coleman (1977) and Vainshtein and Zakharov (1982), providing an update after more than a decade without a comprehensive review aimed at a general audience. The instanton liquid model was first proposed by Shuryak (1982) with parameters later confirmed by lattice cooling studies from the MIT group (1993-1994). The review builds on the QCD sum rule method of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (1961) to connect instanton physics with hadronic phenomenology. The work enables quantitative calculations of hadronic correlation functions in all meson and baryon channels and provides a framework for understanding the chiral phase transition at finite temperature relevant to heavy ion collision experiments at AGS, CERN SPS, and RHIC.
