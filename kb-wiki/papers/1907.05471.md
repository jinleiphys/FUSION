# Experimental investigation of $α$-condensation in light nuclei

> Method: To examine signatures of this alpha-condensation, a compound nucleus reaction using 160, 280, and 400 MeV 16O beams impinging on a carbon target was used to investigate the 12C(16O,7a) reaction. This permits a search for near-threshold states in the alpha-conjugate nuclei up to 24Mg. Results: Events up to an alpha-particle multiplicity of 7 were measured and the results were compared to both an Extended Hauser-Feshbach calculation and the Fermi break-up model. The measured multiplicity distribution exceeded that predicted from a sequential decay mechanism and had a better agreement with the multi-particle Fermi break-up model. Examination of how these 7 alpha final states could be reconstructed to form 8Be and 12C(0_2+) showed a quantitative difference in which decay modes were dominant compared to the Fermi break-up model. No new states were observed in 16O, 20Ne, and 24Mg due to the effect of the N-alpha penetrability suppressing the total alpha-particle dissociation decay mode. Conclusion: The reaction mechanism for a high energy compound nucleus reaction can only be described by a hybrid of sequential decay and multi-particle breakup. Highly alpha-clustered states were seen which did not originate from simple binary reaction processes. Direct investigations of near-threshold states in N-alpha systems are inherently impeded by the Coulomb barrier prohibiting the observation of states in the N-alpha decay channel. No evidence of a highly clustered 15.1 MeV state in 16O was observed from (28Si*,12C(0_2+))16O(0_6+) when reconstructing the Hoyle state from 3 alpha-particles. Therefore, no experimental signatures for alpha-condensation were observed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that no experimental signatures for alpha-condensation were observed in the 12C(16O,7alpha) reaction at beam energies of 160, 280, and 400 MeV, despite observing highly alpha-clustered states that did not originate from simple binary reaction processes; the authors conclude that direct investigations of near-threshold states in N-alpha systems are inherently impeded by the Coulomb barrier suppressing the total alpha-particle dissociation decay mode.

## Method

A compound nucleus reaction was performed using 16O beams at 160, 280, and 400 MeV impinging on a natural carbon target to investigate the 12C(16O,7alpha) reaction. Reaction products were measured using the combined CHIMERA and FARCOS detector arrays, which provided 28% solid angle coverage with particle identification via DeltaE-E and time-of-flight methods. The measured alpha-particle multiplicity distributions and 7-alpha final-state reconstructions were compared to predictions from an Extended Hauser-Feshbach sequential decay calculation and the Fermi break-up multi-particle model.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies used: 160, 280, and 400 MeV 16O beams with average beam current of 100 pA
- Carbon target thickness: 58 microgram/cm2 for 280 and 400 MeV, 92 microgram/cm2 for 160 MeV
- Combined detector solid angle coverage: 28% of 4pi
- CHIMERA detector covers lab scattering angles from 1 degree to 176 degrees
- FARCOS detector angular resolution: approximately 0.1 degree; energy resolution deltaE/E less than 1% at 5.5 MeV
- CHIMERA energy resolution: approximately 400 keV for a 30 MeV alpha-particle, increasing to approximately 1000 keV for a 60 MeV alpha-particle
- 4He separation from 3He locus achieved with 99.4% purity in CHIMERA particle identification
- EHF calculation predicts cross section for multiplicity 6 events is less than 0.1 mb
- Binary fission modes constitute 9.6%, 0.2%, and 0.02% of phase space for 160, 280, and 400 MeV beam energies respectively
- For a decay to provide 7 alpha-particles, 38.5 MeV is required

## Context

This work extends previous investigations into alpha-clustering and alpha-condensation in light nuclei, particularly following theoretical predictions by Tohsaki et al. (2001) of alpha-condensate states and experimental studies by Kokalova (2010) demonstrating that alpha-gas states exhibit increased preference for emission of light clusters due to Coulomb barrier modification. The experiment searched for near-threshold states in alpha-conjugate nuclei up to 24Mg and attempted to observe a highly clustered 15.1 MeV state in 16O, but found no evidence for alpha-condensation signatures. The results enable future work to focus on alternative experimental approaches that circumvent the Coulomb barrier suppression of N-alpha decay channels.
