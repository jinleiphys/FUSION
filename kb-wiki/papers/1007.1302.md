# Two-Level Lipkin Model in Unconventional Boson Realization

> With the aim of applying to the Lipkin model in the case of open shell system, a possible form of the boson realization for the su(2)-algebra is proposed both in the Schwinger and the Holstein-Primakoff representation. The basic idea is borrowed from the Schwinger boson realization for the su(4)-algebra for many-quark system recently presented by the present authors. As the simplest approximation, a certain approximate form is given and the result is a natural generalization of the RPA result in the case of closed shell system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a boson realization of the su(2)-algebra for the two-level Lipkin model that works for any fermion number (open shell systems), not only the closed shell case (N = 2Ω) where conventional boson realizations apply. The key condition is that extra boson degrees of freedom must be introduced beyond those in the conventional Schwinger and Holstein-Primakoff representations.

## Method

The authors construct an unconventional boson realization by borrowing ideas from a Schwinger boson representation of the su(4)-algebra previously developed for many-quark systems. They introduce four kinds of boson operators (instead of the usual two) to represent the su(2) generators, along with an associated su(1,1)-algebra, and derive expressions for the degeneracy operator Ω̂ and the total fermion number operator N̂ that maintain one-to-one correspondence with the original fermion space. Both Schwinger-type and Holstein-Primakoff-type representations are formulated, with the latter obtained by eliminating the extra boson degrees of freedom through constraints on the boson number operators.

## Key numbers

- The degeneracy of each single-particle level is 2Ω = 2j + 1, where j is half-integer.
- For the closed shell case (n_p = n_h = n_0), the total fermion number is N = 2Ω and the su(2)-spin magnitude is s = Ω - n_0.
- For open shell systems, the total fermion number ranges from 0 ≤ N ≤ 4Ω, with the su(2)-spin magnitude given by s = Ω - (n_p + n_h)/2.
- In the unconventional Schwinger representation, the degeneracy operator is expressed as Ω̂ = (1/2)(n_p + n_h) + (1/2)(â_p^*â_p + â_h^*â_h + b̂_p^*b̂_p + b̂_h^*b̂_h), with y = 1 determined by requiring Ω̂ to be independent of the eigenvalue.

## Context

This work extends the conventional boson expansion theory for the Lipkin model, which previously was limited to closed shell systems (N = 2Ω) as treated in the standard RPA framework. The authors build on their earlier Schwinger boson realization of the su(4)-algebra for many-quark systems (Tsue, Kuriyama, and Kunihiro, 2000s) and on the general su(M+1)⊗su(N,1) boson representation formalism (Tsue, Providência, da Providência, Yamamura, and Kuriyama, 2000s). The result enables investigation of how collective particle-hole pair excitations vary from closed to open shell systems, and provides a natural generalization of the RPA result for the closed shell case.
