# Monotonic properties of the shift and penetration factors

> We study derivatives of the shift and penetration factors of collision theory with respect to energy, angular momentum, and charge. Definitive results for the signs of these derivatives are found for the repulsive Coulomb case. In particular, we find that the derivative of the shift factor with respect to energy is positive for the repulsive Coulomb case, a long anticipated but heretofore unproven result. These results are closely connected to the properties of the sum of squares of the regular and irregular Coulomb functions; we also present investigations of this quantity.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the derivative of the shift factor with respect to energy is always positive for the repulsive Coulomb case at positive energies, a result that had been long anticipated but previously unproven; the proof relies on the complete monotonicity of the sum of squares of the regular and irregular Coulomb functions.

## Method

The authors use the phase-amplitude parametrization of Coulomb functions and a Nicholson-type integral representation for the sum of squares of the regular and irregular Coulomb functions (A² = F² + G²) to establish that A² is a completely monotonic function of the dimensionless radial coordinate ρ for repulsive Coulomb fields. They then derive integral expressions for the energy, angular momentum, and charge derivatives of the shift factor S and penetration factor P, and determine the signs of these derivatives using properties of completely monotonic functions and integration by parts techniques.

## Key numbers

- For the repulsive Coulomb case: 0 < ∂S/∂E < −ρA²(A²)′/(2E), where the upper bound involves the amplitude A and its derivative with respect to ρ
- For the penetration factor: ρ/(2EA²) < ∂P/∂E < ρA²/(2E)
- For angular momentum variation: (2ℓ+1)(A⁴/ρ²)[ρ(A²)′/(2A²) − 1/2] < ∂S/∂ℓ < 0
- For angular momentum variation of penetration factor: −(2ℓ+1)A²/(2ρ) < ∂P/∂ℓ < 0
- For charge parameter variation: (2A⁴/ρ)[ρ(A²)′/(2A²) − 1/4] < ∂S/∂η < 0
- For charge parameter variation of penetration factor: −A² < ∂P/∂η < 0
- For the neutral case with ℓ=0: A²=1, P=ρ, S=0, and ∂S/∂E=0 for positive energy

## Context

This work extends the foundational R-matrix theory of Lane and Thomas (1958) by providing the first general proof that the energy derivative of the shift factor is positive for repulsive Coulomb fields, which had been shown previously only for negative energies, in the JWKB approximation, and in various limits. The results confirm and extend earlier partial results by Prosser (1958) and are important for establishing the uniqueness of alternative R-matrix parametrizations proposed by Brune (2002). The proof technique using complete monotonicity of A² and Nicholson-type integrals generalizes results by Lorch (1963) and Hartman (1961, 1973) on similar properties of Bessel functions.
