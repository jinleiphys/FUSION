# Factorization Theorem Relating Euclidean and Light-Cone Parton Distributions

> In a large-momentum nucleon state, the matrix element of a gauge-invariant Euclidean Wilson line operator accessible from lattice QCD can be related to the standard light-cone parton distribution function through the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) expansion. This relation is given by a factorization theorem with a non-trivial matching coefficient. Using the operator product expansion we prove the large-momentum factorization of the quasi-parton distribution function in LaMET, and show that the more recently discussed Ioffe-time distribution approach also obeys an equivalent factorization theorem. Explicit results for the coefficients are obtained and compared at one-loop. Our proof clearly demonstrates that the matching coefficients in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme depend on the large partonic momentum rather than the nucleon momentum.

## Key claim

Using the operator product expansion (OPE), the authors prove that the large-momentum factorization of the quasi-parton distribution function (quasi-PDF) in large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) and the small-\(z^2\) factorization of the pseudo-PDF are equivalent representations of the same spatial correlator, and that the matching coefficients in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme depend on the large partonic momentum \(|y|P^z\) rather than the nucleon momentum \(P^z\).

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion (OPE) to the gauge-invariant Euclidean Wilson line operator \(\tilde{O}_\Gamma(z)\) in the limit \(z^2 \to 0\), expanding it in terms of local twist-2 operators. They derive factorization formulas for the spatial correlator, quasi-PDF, and pseudo-PDF, showing they are related by Fourier transforms. One-loop matching coefficients are computed in dimensional regularization with the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme for both \(\Gamma = \gamma^0\) and \(\Gamma = \gamma^z\) using on-shell massless quark states.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop matching coefficient for the pseudo-PDF with \(\Gamma = \gamma^0\) is given by \(C_n(\mu^2 z^2) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \biggl[ \Bigl( \frac{3+2n}{2+3n+n^2} + 2H_n\Bigr) \ln\frac{\mu^2z^2 e^{2\gamma_E}}{4} + \frac{5+2n}{2+3n+n^2}+2(1-H_n)H_n - 2 H_n^{(2)} \biggr]\), where \(H_n\) are harmonic numbers.
* For \(\Gamma = \gamma^z\), the one-loop coefficient differs by \(\Delta C_n^{\gamma^z}(\mu^2 z^2) = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \frac{2}{2+3n+n^2}\).
* The one-loop \(\overline{\rm MS}\) coefficient \(C_0(\mu^2 z^2)\) for the spatial correlator at \(\zeta=0\) is \(C_0(\mu^2z^2) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \biggl[ \frac{3}{2} \ln(\mu^2z^2 e^{2\gamma_E}/4) + \frac{5}{2} \biggr]\).
* The on-shell quark self-energy renormalization constant is \(\delta Z_\psi = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{\epsilon_{\rm UV}} - \frac{1}{\epsilon_{\rm IR}}\right)\).

## Context

This work provides a rigorous OPE-based proof of the factorization theorem for quasi-PDFs in LaMET, originally proposed by Ji (2013), and demonstrates its equivalence to the pseudo-PDF approach introduced by Radyushkin (2017). The derivation corrects earlier assumptions that the matching coefficient depends on the nucleon momentum \(P^z\) rather than the partonic momentum \(|y|P^z\), as used in early lattice calculations by Lin (2014) and Alexandrou (2015). The result enables consistent extraction of PDFs from lattice QCD using either the quasi-PDF or pseudo-PDF formalism, with the same requirement of large nucleon momentum.
