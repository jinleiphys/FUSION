# Equation of state of cold and dense QCD matter in resummed perturbation theory

> We discuss the Hard Dense Loop resummation at finite quark mass and evaluate the equation of state (EoS) of cold and dense QCD matter in $β$ equilibrium. The resummation in the quark sector has an effect of lowering the baryon number density and the EoS turns out to have much smaller uncertainty than the perturbative QCD estimate. Our numerical results favor smooth matching between the EoS from the resummed QCD calculation at high density and the extrapolated EoS from the nuclear matter density region. We also point out that the speed of sound in our EoS slightly exceeds the conformal limit.

## Key claim

The Hard Dense Loop resummation at finite quark mass produces an equation of state for cold and dense QCD matter in beta equilibrium that has much smaller scale variation uncertainty than standard perturbative QCD, and this reduction arises not from making the pressure scale-independent but from a cancellation between the pressure and baryon density scale dependences when constructing the pressure as a function of energy density.

## Method

The authors extended the Hard Dense Loop perturbation theory (HDLpt) to include a finite strange quark mass, solving the beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions for u, d, and s quarks with electrons. They computed the quark pressure from the quark loop with self-energy insertions, separating the contribution into a vacuum part, a quasiparticle pole part, and a Landau damping part, using exact integrations without expansion in the screening mass. The gluon sector followed the standard HTLpt prescription, and the running coupling used the two-loop formula with Lambda_MSbar = 378 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass at 2 GeV was taken as M_s(2 GeV) = 100 MeV.
- The gluon screening mass is defined as m_D^2 = (2 alpha_s/pi) sum_f mu_f^2.
- The quark screening mass for flavor f is m_qf^2 = (alpha_s/2pi)(N_c^2-1)/(2N_c) mu_f^2.
- The constant C_g in the gluon pressure is numerically estimated as C_g ≈ 1.17201.
- For eta_f → 1 (massless limit), D_q(eta_f → 1) → 0 and C_q(eta_f → 1) ≈ -0.03653.
- The plot range for the equation of state is truncated around epsilon ≈ 500 MeV/fm^3 due to uncontrolled uncertainty at lower energy density.

## Context

This work extends the HDLpt framework of Baier (1999) and Andersen (1999, 2002) by incorporating the finite strange quark mass, following the approach of Kurkela (2009) who included strange mass effects in pQCD for neutron star phenomenology. The results show dramatically reduced uncertainty compared to the pQCD calculations of Kurkela (2009) and Fraga (2013), enabling smoother matching with nuclear equations of state from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2010) and the APR equation of state (Akmal 1998). The finding that the speed of sound slightly exceeds the conformal limit c_s^2 = 1/3 provides a counterexample to the conjecture that QCD matter always respects this bound, relevant to discussions in Annala (2019) and Bedaque (2014).
