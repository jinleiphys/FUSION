# "QM19 summary talk": Outlook and future of heavy-ion collisions

> A summary of the QM19 conference is given by highlighting a few selected results. These are discussed as examples to illustrate the exciting future of heavy-ion collisions and the need for further instrumentation. (The arXiv version is significantly longer than the printed proceedings, with more figures.)

## Key claim

The QM19 conference summary argues that the heavy-ion physics community should now focus on understanding how a strongly-coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) can exist even in small collision systems down to multiplicities just above minimum-bias pp collisions at LHC energies, rather than debating whether it is possible.

## Method

The author reviewed selected results presented at the QM19 conference across four frontiers: the high-density frontier (beam energy scans for the critical endpoint and first-order phase transition), the high-energy frontier (precision measurements of flow, jet quenching, and heavy flavor at RHIC and LHC), the cold nuclear matter/small-x frontier (gluon saturation and nuclear PDFs), and the ultra-precision near and far future (planned detector upgrades and facilities including EIC, sPHENIX, FoCal, and LHC upgrades).

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD from the WB collaboration disfavors the critical endpoint location for baryon chemical potential below 300 MeV
- C6/C2 of net-proton distributions in central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV are negative (as expected from lattice QCD), while positive at sqrt(s_NN)=54.4 GeV (a 2-sigma effect)
- Freeze-out parameters explored from T≈155 MeV and mu_B≈0 MeV down to about T=80 MeV and mu_B=900 MeV
- Baryons (p, Lambda) exhibit a change of slope in direct flow v1 around sqrt(s_NN)=14.5 GeV
- J/psi RAA in PbPb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV shows a clear minimum at N_part~100, rising to near unity in central collisions
- D meson production measured down to 0.5 GeV/c in PbPb collisions
- Inclusive jet RAA for R=1.0 yields RAA≈0.8 above 300 GeV/c for central PbPb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Prompt D-meson RpPb at forward rapidity (2.5<y<4.0) in pPb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV is RpPb~0.6 at low pT, increasing mildly with pT
- Lambda_c+/D ratio at 2<pT<4 GeV/c in pp collisions at lowest multiplicity is 4 times larger than in e+e- collisions
- Non-zero v2 up to pT~50 GeV/c observed in pPb collisions using template fit method
- FoCal expected accuracy for direct photons: about 20% at 4 GeV/c, improving to about 5% at 10 GeV/c and above
- EIC will cover sqrt(s)=20-140 GeV with luminosity of 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1

## Context

This summary extends the ongoing experimental and theoretical effort to characterize the QCD phase diagram, building on lattice QCD calculations by the WB and HotQCD collaborations, and the beam energy scan programs at RHIC and SPS. The results enable future precision measurements with planned upgrades (ALICE LS2, sPHENIX, CMS and ATLAS LS3 upgrades) and new facilities (EIC, FoCal, NICA, FAIR, J-PARC) to probe gluon saturation, the critical endpoint, and parton energy loss onset in lighter ion collisions such as OO and ArAr as proposed by Citron (2018).
