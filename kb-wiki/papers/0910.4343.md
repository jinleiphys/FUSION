# Mode-coupling and the pygmy dipole resonance in a relativistic two-phonon model

> A two-phonon version of the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA-2) represents a new class of many-body models for nuclear structure calculations based on the covariant energy density functional. As a fully consistent extension of the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA), the two-phonon RQTBA implies a fragmentation of nuclear states over two-quasiparticle and two-phonon configurations. This leads, in particular, to a splitting-out of the lowest 1$^-$ state as a member of the two-phonon $[2^+\otimes3^-]$ quintuplet from the RQRPA pygmy dipole mode, thus establishing a physical mixing between these three modes. The inclusion of the two-phonon configurations in the model space allows to describe the positions and the reduced transition probabilities of the lowest 1$^-$ states in isotopes $^{116,120}$Sn as well as the low-energy fraction of the dipole strength without any adjustment procedures. The model is also applied to the low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich $^{68,70,72}$Ni isotopes. Recent experimental data for $^{68}$Ni are reproduced fairly well.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) is not a pure mode but physically mixes with the two-phonon $[2^+\otimes3^-]$ quintuplet, and that including two-phonon configurations in the model space pushes the major fraction of the RQRPA pygmy mode above the neutron threshold, contrary to what the simpler RQRPA or single-phonon RQTBA would predict.

## Method

The authors extend the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) to include two-phonon configurations (RQTBA-2) by introducing RQRPA correlations into the intermediate two-quasiparticle propagator of the energy-dependent residual interaction. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in two steps: first with the energy-dependent phonon coupling amplitude (including the new two-phonon terms) to obtain a correlated propagator, then with the static residual interaction to obtain the full response function. The model uses the covariant energy density functional with parameter set NL3 and includes pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{116}$Sn, the total $B(E1)\uparrow$ strength below 8 MeV is 0.20 e$^2$ fm$^2$, agreeing with the experimental value of 0.204(25) e$^2$ fm$^2$ from Gov. (1998)
- For $^{120}$Sn, the total $B(E1)\uparrow$ strength below 8.08 MeV is 0.31 e$^2$ fm$^2$, agreeing with the QPM result of 0.289 e$^2$ fm$^2$ from TLS. (2004)
- The lowest 1$^-$ state in $^{116}$Sn is calculated at 2.66 MeV with $B(E1)\uparrow$ = 14.5 $\times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$ fm$^2$ (experiment: 3.33 MeV, 6.55(65) $\times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$ fm$^2$ from Bry. (1999))
- The lowest 1$^-$ state in $^{120}$Sn is calculated at 3.22 MeV with $B(E1)\uparrow$ = 16.9 $\times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$ fm$^2$ (experiment: 3.28 MeV, 7.60(51) $\times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$ fm$^2$ from Bry. (1999))
- The anharmonicity ratio $R_{\omega} = \omega(1^-_1)/(\omega(2^+_1)+\omega(3^-_1))$ is 0.94 for $^{116}$Sn and 0.95 for $^{120}$Sn (experimental: 0.94 and 0.92, respectively)
- For $^{68}$Ni, the calculated dipole strength distribution has its maximum at 10.30 MeV, with total strength below 12 MeV of 2.73 e$^2$ fm$^2$, corresponding to 7.8% of the total integrated photoabsorption cross section and 11% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) developed by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2008, 2009) by adding two-phonon correlations following the non-relativistic QTBA-2 formalism proposed by Tse. (2007). It addresses the long-standing question of whether the pygmy dipole mode is collective, which had been debated between relativistic QRPA calculations (PRN. 2003) producing a highly collective mode and non-relativistic approaches (TSG. 2007, TL. 2008) showing less collectivity. The model enables a consistent description of the lowest 1$^-$ states as members of the $2^+_1\otimes 3^-_1$ quintuplet, as predicted by Lip. (1971) and VK. (1971) and observed in spherical nuclei, and provides predictions for dipole strength in neutron-rich $^{70,72}$Ni isotopes where experimental data are not yet available.
