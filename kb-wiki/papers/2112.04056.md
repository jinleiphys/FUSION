# Symmetry and shape coexistence in 10Be

> Within the low-lying spectrum of 10Be, multiple rotational bands are found, with strikingly different moments of inertia. A proposed interpretation has been that these bands variously represent triaxial rotation and prolate axially-deformed rotation. The bands are well-reproduced in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations. We use the calculated wave functions to elucidate the nuclear shapes underlying these bands, by examining the Elliott SU(3) symmetry content of these wave functions. The ab initio results support an interpretation in which the ground-state band, along with an accompanying K=2 side band, represent a triaxial rotor, arising from an SU(3) irreducible representation in the 0 hbar omega space. Then, the lowest excited K=0 band represents a prolate rotor, arising from an SU(3) irreducible representation in the 2 hbar omega space.

## Key claim

The ab initio NCCI calculations support an interpretation where the ground-state band and its accompanying K=2 side band in 10Be together represent a triaxial rotor arising from the leading SU(3) irreducible representation in the 0 hbar omega space, while the lowest excited K=0 band represents a prolate rotor from the leading irrep in the 2 hbar omega space, with the caveat that these dominant contributions are dressed by higher oscillator excitations accounting for about half of the wave function norm.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for 10Be using the Daejeon16 interaction, then decomposed the resulting wave functions into contributions from different U(3) x SU(2) symmetry subspaces characterized by quantum numbers Nex(lambda,mu)S. They used the "Lanczos trick" to project wave functions onto eigenspaces of a linear combination operator involving the oscillator excitation number Nex, the SU(3) quadratic Casimir operator, and the spin-squared operator, with the calculations carried out using the code MFDn.

## Key numbers

- The excited K=0 band head lies at approximately 6.18 MeV, with a moment of inertia higher by a factor of approximately 3 compared to the ground-state band
- The ratio E(2+_2)/E(2+_1) is approximately 1.8, suggestive of a triaxial rotor with maximal triaxiality (gamma approximately 30 degrees)
- The leading SU(3) irrep in the 0 hbar omega space is (2,2) with Casimir eigenvalue 16, while the leading irrep in the 2 hbar omega space is (8,0) with Casimir eigenvalue 58.67
- The excited K=0 band calculated ab initio extends to 8+, well past the last experimentally observed band member

## Context

This work extends prior ab initio NCCI studies of rotational bands in 10Be (Caprio 2019) by extracting Elliott SU(3) symmetry content from the calculated wave functions. The results corroborate the cluster interpretation from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations (Kanada-En'yo 1997, 1999; Suhara 2010), in which triaxiality arises from combining prolate proton and oblate neutron distributions. The analysis enables microscopic justification of the triaxial rotor interpretation for the ground-state and side bands, and demonstrates how ab initio wave functions can expose the symmetry structure underlying collective states.
