# Hadron formation in electron induced reactions at HERMES energies

> We investigate meson electroproduction off complex nuclei in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment using a semi-classical transport model which is based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation. We discuss coherence length and color transparency effects in exclusive rho^0 production as well as hadron formation and attenuation of charged pions, kaons, protons and anti-protons in deep inelastic lepton scattering off nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a semi-classical transport model with a constant hadron formation time of about 0.5 fm/c in the rest frame and a reduced leading-hadron cross section can describe hadron attenuation in electron-nucleus reactions at HERMES energies, but the authors note that the HERMES data on exclusive rho0 production do not show evidence for color transparency because the photon energy is too low to produce a large enough gamma factor.

## Method

The authors split the lepton-nucleus interaction into two steps: first, the virtual photon-nucleon interaction is simulated by the Monte Carlo generator PYTHIA v6.2; second, the produced particles are propagated through the nucleus using a coupled-channel transport model based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation. Hadrons are assumed non-interacting during their formation time, with leading hadrons having a reduced effective cross section of 0.33 times the full hadronic cross section during that period, while non-leading hadrons from string fragmentation may interact after vector meson decay.

## Key numbers

- Formation time in the hadron rest frame: constant tau_f = 0.5 fm/c used for all hadrons (mesons, baryons, antibaryons)
- Leading hadron cross section during formation time: sigma_lead = 0.33 sigma_h (scaled from full hadronic cross section)
- Lower bound on formation time from data: tau_f > 0.3 fm/c for all hadrons
- Coherence length formula: l_V = 2 nu / (Q^2 + m_V^2), where nu is photon energy, Q^2 its virtuality, m_V the vector meson mass
- Vector meson masses in string fragmentation: 0.77 to 1.02 GeV
- Typical hadronic radius for formation time estimate: 0.5 to 0.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BUU-based approach (Falter 2002, Falter 2003a) for combining quantum mechanical coherence in the entrance channel with coupled-channel final state interactions, going beyond the purely absorptive Glauber theory used in earlier work. The results contradict the interpretation of Kopeliovich (2001) regarding possible onset of color transparency in rho0 production at HERMES. The model provides an alternative to partonic energy-loss explanations by Accardi (2002) and Wang (2003) for hadron attenuation in deep inelastic scattering, and enables future comparisons with HERMES data on krypton targets.
