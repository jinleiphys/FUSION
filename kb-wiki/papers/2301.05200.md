# Charged pion electric polarizability from four-point functions in lattice QCD

> Polarizabilities reveal valuable information on the internal structure of hadrons in terms of charge and current distributions. For neutral hadrons, the standard approach is the background field method. But for a charged hadron, its acceleration under the applied field complicates the isolation of the polarization energy. In this work, we explore an alternative method based on four-point functions in lattice QCD. The approach offers a transparent picture on how polarizabilities arise from photon, quark, and gluon interactions. We carry out a proof-of-concept simulation on the electric polarizability of a charged pion, using quenched Wilson action on a $24^3\times 48$ lattice at $β=6.0$ with pion mass from 1100 to 370 MeV. We show in detail the evaluation and analysis of the four-point correlation functions and report results on charge radius and electric polarizability. Our results from connected diagrams suggest that charged pion $α_E$ is due to a cancellation between elastic and inelastic contributions. It would be interesting to see how the cancellation plays out at smaller pion masses in future simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a proof-of-concept that charged pion electric polarizability can be extracted from four-point correlation functions in lattice QCD, but the results are limited to connected diagrams only and use quenched Wilson fermions at relatively heavy pion masses (1100 to 370 MeV), meaning the approach requires future validation with disconnected contributions and at lighter pion masses.

## Method

The authors compute four-point correlation functions with two electromagnetic current insertions between pion interpolating fields, using the zero-momentum Breit frame kinematics to mimic low-energy Compton scattering. They evaluate connected Wick contraction diagrams (different flavor, same flavor, and same flavor Z-graph) on a quenched $24^3 \times 48$ lattice at $\beta=6.0$ using wall sources and the sequential source technique (SST) to reduce computational cost, employing both local and conserved (point-split) currents.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses studied range from 1100 MeV down to 370 MeV on a $24^3 \times 48$ lattice at $\beta=6.0$
* The fine structure constant $\alpha = 1/137$ appears in the polarizability formula
* The charge factor for diagram a (different flavor) is $q_u q_{\bar{d}} = 2/9$, and for diagram b (same flavor) the total charge factor is $4/9 + 1/9 = 5/9$
* The elastic contribution to the polarizability formula contains the term $\alpha \langle r_E^2 \rangle / (3 m_\pi)$

## Context

This work extends the four-point function formalism previously developed by Wilcox (1996) and Wilcox (2021) for computing polarizabilities, offering an alternative to the background field method used by Detmold (2009) and others for charged hadrons. The approach treats neutral and charged particles on equal footing, avoiding complications from hadron acceleration in external fields, and enables future studies of how the cancellation between elastic and inelastic contributions to $\alpha_E$ behaves at lighter pion masses with full QCD simulations including disconnected diagrams.
