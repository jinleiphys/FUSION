# Medium-modification of photon-tagged jets in $AA$ collisions

> We study nuclear modification of the photon-tagged jets in $AA$ collisions within the jet quenching scheme based on the light-cone path integral approach to the induced gluon emission. The calculations are performed for running coupling. Collisional energy loss is treated as a perturbation to the radiative mechanism. We obtain a reasonable agreement with the recent data from the STAR Collaboration on the mid-rapidity nuclear modification factor $I_{AA}$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV for parametrization of running $α_s$ consistent with that necessary for description of the data on suppression of the high-$p_T$ spectra.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission, combined with running coupling and treating collisional energy loss as a perturbation, yields reasonable agreement with STAR data on the nuclear modification factor \(I_{AA}\) for photon-tagged jets in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, but only for a parametrization of running \(\alpha_s\) consistent with that needed to describe high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression data.

## Method

The authors calculate medium-modified fragmentation functions (FFs) for photon-tagged jets using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach for induced gluon emission, with multiple gluon emission treated in the independent emission approximation. The QGP fireball is modeled as an ideal gas with Bjorken 1+1D expansion, initial temperature fixed from charged particle multiplicity via the two-component Glauber model, and the collisional energy loss is incorporated as a perturbation to the radiative mechanism. The nuclear modification factor \(I_{AA}\) is computed by averaging over impact parameter, jet direction, and production point, using LO pQCD with CTEQ6 parton distribution functions and EKS98 nuclear corrections.

## Key numbers

* Initial QGP temperature \(T_0 \approx 327\) MeV for \(0-12\%\) centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, obtained from the Bjorken relation with \(\tau_0 = 0.5\) fm.
* Quasiparticle masses used: \(m_q = 300\) MeV and \(m_g = 400\) MeV, supported by lattice data analysis.
* Debye mass ratio \(\mu_D/T \approx 3\) at \(T \sim 1.5 T_c\) and \(\mu_D/T \approx 2.4\) at \(T \sim 4 T_c\), from lattice analysis.
* For typical jet energy \(\sim 15\) GeV and path length \(L \sim 5\) fm, renormalization coefficients for in-medium FFs are \(K_{qq} \approx 1.1\), \(K_{gq} \approx 0.8\), and \(K_{gg} \approx 0.7\).
* The smearing correction to \(I_{AA}\) from intrinsic parton transverse momentum is \(\lesssim 8\%\) at \(z \sim 0.8\) for the photon energy interval \(12 < E_T^\gamma < 20\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous jet quenching model (Zakharov, 2008, 2011, 2012, 2013) used to describe the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for high-\(p_T\) hadron spectra at RHIC and LHC, now applied to photon-tagged jet fragmentation functions measured by the STAR Collaboration (2017). The approach contrasts with other jet quenching schemes such as BDMPS, GLV, and AMY by using the LCPI formalism to treat the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect and finite-size effects beyond the oscillator approximation. The results enable further tests of the independent gluon emission approximation at small \(z\) and motivate development of more complete treatments of in-medium parton cascading.
