# Accurate full configuration interaction correlation energy estimates for five- and six-membered rings

> Following our recent work on the benzene molecule [\href{https://doi.org/10.1063/5.0027617}{J.~Chem.~Phys.~\textbf{153}, 176101 (2020)}], itself motivated by the blind challenge of Eriksen \textit{et al.} [\href{https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jpclett.0c02621}{J.~Phys.~Chem.~Lett.~\textbf{11}, 8922 (2020)}] on the same system, we report accurate full configuration interaction (FCI) frozen-core correlation energy estimates for twelve five- and six-membered ring molecules in the standard correlation-consistent double-$ζ$ Dunning basis set (cc-pVDZ). Our FCI correlation energy estimates, with estimated error smaller than 1 millihartree, are based on energetically optimized-orbital selected configuration interaction (SCI) calculations performed with the \textit{Configuration Interaction using a Perturbative Selection made Iteratively} (CIPSI) algorithm. Having at our disposal these accurate reference energies, the respective performance and convergence properties of several popular and widely-used families of single-reference quantum chemistry methods are investigated. In particular, we study the convergence properties of i) the Møller-Plesset perturbation series up to fifth-order (MP2, MP3, MP4, and MP5), ii) the iterative approximate coupled-cluster series CC2, CC3, and CC4, and iii) the coupled-cluster series CCSD, CCSDT, and CCSDTQ. The performance of the ground-state gold standard CCSD(T) as well as the completely renormalized CC model, CR-CC(2,3), are also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper provides accurate full configuration interaction (FCI) frozen-core correlation energy estimates for twelve five- and six-membered ring molecules in the cc-pVDZ basis set, with estimated errors smaller than 1 millihartree, based on extrapolated orbital-optimized selected configuration interaction (SCI) calculations using the CIPSI algorithm. The authors show that the expected theoretical ranking of methods (MP2 < CC2 < MP3 < CCSD < MP4 < CCSD(T) < CR-CC(2,3) < CC3 < CCSDT < MP5 < CC4 < CCSDTQ) is slightly altered for these systems.

## Method

The authors performed energetically-optimized orbital selected configuration interaction (SCI) calculations using the Configuration Interaction using a Perturbative Selection made Iteratively (CIPSI) algorithm, with orbital optimization via a Newton-Raphson method with trust region. They linearly extrapolated the CIPSI energy to zero perturbative correction (EPT = 0) to obtain FCI limit estimates, using weighted five-point linear fits with weights taken as the inverse square of the perturbative corrections. They then benchmarked the convergence properties of Moller-Plesset perturbation series (MP2 through MP5), approximate coupled-cluster series (CC2, CC3, CC4), complete coupled-cluster series (CCSD, CCSDT, CCSDTQ), CCSD(T), and CR-CC(2,3) against these FCI estimates.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on benzene (Loos 2020), which was motivated by the blind challenge of Eriksen et al. (2020) on the same system. The paper provides reference FCI correlation energies for twelve cyclic molecules that enable benchmarking of popular single-reference quantum chemistry methods. The results enable future studies to assess the accuracy of cheaper methods against these high-quality references, and demonstrate that CC3 outperforms CCSD(T) and CR-CC(2,3) as well as its more expensive parent CCSDT for these systems.
