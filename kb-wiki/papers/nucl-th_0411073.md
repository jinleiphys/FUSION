# Nuclear giant resonances

> This talk presents the recent status of theoretical and experimental studies of giant resonances in nuclei with the emphasis on: (1) charge-exchange Gamow-Teller resonance, (2) multiple-phonon resoanances, (3) giant dipole resonances in highly excited nuclei, and (4) pygmy dipole resonances in neutron rich nuclei. In particular, the description of these resonances within the framework of the phonon damping model is discussed in detail.

# Nuclear giant resonances

## Key claim

The phonon damping model (PDM) provides a unified microscopic description of multiple types of giant resonances (GDR, DGDR, GTR, PDR) across stable, hot, and neutron-rich nuclei, resolving several long-standing discrepancies between earlier theoretical predictions and experimental data, but the model's success depends on the specific treatment of coupling between the GDR phonon and noncollective particle-hole excitations, and on the inclusion of thermal pairing and temperature-dependent quasiparticle occupation numbers.

## Method

The phonon damping model (PDM) describes giant resonance propagation via a Green's function formalism where the polarization operator encodes the coupling of the collective phonon to the quasiparticle field. The energy of the damped collective phonon is found as the pole of the Green's function, and the resonance width is calculated as twice the imaginary part of the polarization operator at that pole energy. The model incorporates temperature-dependent quasiparticle occupation numbers (close to Fermi-Dirac form) and, for hot nuclei, includes the thermal pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- GDR width in 120Sn as a function of temperature: PDM results shown for temperatures up to approximately 2 MeV, with and without thermal pairing gap, compared to experimental data from alpha-induced reactions.
- Electromagnetic cross sections for GDR and DGDR in 136Xe and 208Pb: PDM predictions agree with LAND collaboration data, resolving the long-standing underestimation by the non-interacting phonon picture.
- GTR strength function in 90Nb: PDM predictions (two versions, PDM-1 and PDM-2) compared with experimental data from Wakasa and with a microscopic 2p2h calculation.
- Photoabsorption cross sections for 16O, 18O, 40Ca, and 48Ca calculated within PDM, compared with experimental data.
- Energy-weighted sum rule (EWS) of PDR strength for oxygen isotopes: PDM results shown for excitation energy maxima Emax = 16, 16.5, and 17 MeV, in units of Thomas-Reich-Kuhn sum rule (TRK), compared with GSI experimental data at Emax = 15 MeV and with the cluster sum rule (CSR) prediction.

## Context

This invited talk reviews the status of giant resonance studies as of 2004, extending the phonon damping model developed by the author since 1998 (Dang, 1998) and applied in a series of subsequent papers. The PDM resolves the long-standing problem of electromagnetic cross sections for the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) in 136Xe and 208Pb, where the non-interacting phonon picture significantly underestimated the LAND collaboration data. The model also addresses the width and shape of the hot GDR, which had been treated by thermal shape-fluctuation models (TFM; Kuznesov), and predicts pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei, where the damping mechanism is attributed to coupling between the GDR phonon and noncollective particle-hole excitations rather than to collective neutron-excess oscillation. The work enables further studies of the DGDR in hot nuclei and of PDR in neutron-rich systems, with implications for understanding pairing correlations and the separation of weakly bound neutrons.
