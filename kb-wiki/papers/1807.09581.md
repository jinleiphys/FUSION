# Correlating Schiff moments in the light actinides with octupole moments

> We show that the measured intrinsic octupole moments of $^{220}$Rn, $^{224}$Ra, and $^{226}$Ra constrain the intrinsic Schiff moments of $^{225}$Ra$^{221}$Rn, $^{223}$Rn, $^{223}$Fr, $^{225}$Ra, and $^{229}$Pa. The result is a dramatically reduced uncertainty in intrinsic Schiff moments. Direct measurements of octupole moments in odd nuclei will reduce the uncertainty even more. The only significant source of nuclear-physics error in the laboratory Schiff moments will then be the intrinsic matrix elements of the time-reversal non-invariant interaction produced by CP-violating fundamental physics. Those matrix elements are also correlated with octupole moments, but with a larger systematic uncertainty.

## Key claim

Measured intrinsic octupole moments of even-even nuclei (²²⁰Rn, ²²⁴Ra, ²²⁶Ra) can constrain the intrinsic Schiff moments of nearby odd-\(A\) nuclei (²²¹Rn, ²²³Rn, ²²³Fr, ²²⁵Ra, ²²⁹Pa) through a strong linear correlation, dramatically reducing the theoretical uncertainty from nuclear-structure models. The remaining dominant nuclear-physics error in laboratory Schiff moments then comes from the intrinsic matrix elements of the CP-violating interaction, which are also correlated with octupole moments but with larger systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors use Skyrme and Gogny energy-density functionals (with Hartree-Fock, Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov, or BCS treatments) to compute intrinsic octupole moments \(Q^3_0\) and intrinsic Schiff moments \(S_0\) in a deformed, parity-mixed mean-field basis. They project the intrinsic states onto laboratory states with good angular momentum and parity, verify that the rigid-deformation approximation is accurate to about 1.5% for the Schiff operator and better than 0.1% for the octupole operator, and then perform linear regression to correlate the calculated intrinsic moments. Experimental octupole moments of ²²⁴Ra, ²²⁶Ra, and ²²⁰Rn are used to propagate constrained values of \(S_0\) and of the coefficients \(a_i, b_i\) that multiply the CP-violating coupling constants in the Schiff moment expansion.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic Schiff moment of ²²⁵Ra propagated from the experimental octupole moment of ²²⁴Ra: \(S_0 = 26.6(1.9)\; e\,\text{fm}^3\) (theoretical uncertainty 1.6 \(e\,\text{fm}^3\), experimental contribution 1.1 \(e\,\text{fm}^3\)).
- Experimental intrinsic octupole moment of ²²⁴Ra: \(Q^3_0 = 940(30)\; e\,\text{fm}^3\) (using the definition of Bohr and Mottelson; the spectroscopic value 2520±90 \(e\,\text{fm}^3\) corresponds to a different convention).
- Correlation coefficient between the intrinsic Schiff moment of ²²⁵Ra and the octupole moment of ²²⁴Ra, from the UNEDF0 functional covariance analysis: 0.908.
- UNEDF0 functional alone gives \(S_0 = 32.7(1.9)\; e\,\text{fm}^3\) for ²²⁵Ra and \(Q^3_0 = 1.17(10)\times 10^3\; e\,\text{fm}^3\) for ²²⁴Ra.
- Rigid-deformation approximation error for the Schiff operator: about 1.5%; for the octupole operator in ²²⁴Ra: less than 0.1%; truncation error from using 20 oscillator shells: about 0.02%.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei by Dobaczewski and Engel (2005) and Engel (2013), which carried uncertainties significantly larger than 100%. It uses experimental octupole moments measured by Gaffney et al. (2013) for ²²⁴Ra and ²²⁰Rn and by Wollersheim et al. (1993) for ²²⁶Ra to constrain the nuclear-structure part of the Schiff-moment problem. The results enable more reliable interpretation of atomic electric-dipole-moment experiments (e.g., the current best limit from ¹⁹⁹Hg) in terms of fundamental CP-violating parameters, by reducing the dominant nuclear-physics uncertainty to the intrinsic matrix elements of the CP-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction. Direct measurements of octupole moments in odd-\(A\) nuclei would reduce the uncertainty even further.
