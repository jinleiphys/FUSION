# The European Muon Collaboration effect from short-range correlated nucleons in a nucleon swelling model

> The relation between the nuclear EMC effect and the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation is a hot topic in high-energy nuclear physics, ever since a peculiar linear correlation between these two phenomena discovered. In this paper, the contribution to the nuclear EMC effect arising from the short-range correlated nucleons is examined in a nucleon-swelling model. We find that the structure modifications of the N-N SRC nucleons reproduce more or less the measured EMC ratios of light nuclei, while they are not enough to explain the measured EMC ratios of heavy nuclei. We speculate that the hypothesis of causal connection between SRC and the EMC effect is not exact, or the universality of the inner structure of SRC nucleon is violated noticeably from light to heavy nuclei, or there are other origins for the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the nuclear EMC effect arises entirely from short-range correlated (SRC) nucleons, finding that while SRC nucleon modifications can reproduce the measured EMC ratios of light nuclei, they are insufficient to explain the EMC ratios of heavy nuclei, suggesting the causal connection between SRC and the EMC effect may not be exact.

## Method

The authors employ a nucleon swelling model in which the quark confinement space in an SRC pair is assumed to be twice that of a free nucleon, leading to a reduction in the width of valence quark distributions by a factor of (1/2)^(1/3) according to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle. The valence quark distributions at the hadronic scale Q0^2 are parameterized as Beta functions and evolved to higher Q^2 using DGLAP evolution equations. The nuclear structure function is then computed as a weighted sum of modified SRC nucleon structure functions and free nucleon structure functions, using experimentally measured SRC scaling ratios a2 to determine the number of SRC pairs in each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The number of SRC pairs in deuteron (n_SRC^d) is estimated to be between 0.021 and 0.041; n_SRC^d = 0.021 is obtained from a fit to the correlation between nuclear mass and SRC scaling ratio a2, while n_SRC^d = 0.041 is estimated by counting nucleons with momentum above k_F ≈ 275 MeV/c
- The SRC scaling ratio a2 for 12C is 4.64 ± 0.11 (average of CLAS06, CLAS19, and Hall C measurements)
- The SRC scaling ratio a2 for 208Pb is 4.84 ± 0.20 (from CLAS19)
- The initial scale Q0^2 is set at 0.064 GeV^2, and valence quark distributions are evolved to Q^2 = 4 GeV^2
- To explain the EMC effect of heavy nuclei, n_SRC^d would need to be tuned up to 0.08, which is inconsistent with previous estimations
- The experimental extraction of the slope of the universal modification function ranges from about 0.08 to 0.11

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the relationship between the EMC effect and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, following the discovery of a linear correlation between these phenomena by Weinstein (2010) and Hen (2012). The authors test the SRC-driven model previously examined by the CLAS collaboration (2019) and Arrington (2019), who found a universal modification function for SRC nucleons, but their results contradict the conclusions of Wang (2020) who argued that SRC nucleons do not give a dominant part of the observed EMC effect. The analysis enables future work to explore whether the inner structure of mean-field nucleons is also modified, whether SRC universality is violated from light to heavy nuclei, or whether multi-nucleon SRCs (3N and 4N) contribute to the EMC effect.
