# Propagation of a massive spin-3/2 particle

> The propagation of an off-shell spin-3/2 baryon is investigated in a consistent framework using projections corresponding to one irreducible spin-3/2 and two irreducible spin-1/2 representations arising from the usual spinor-vector representation of the spin-3/2 fields. Starting from the most general Lagrangian invariant under point transformations containing first-order derivatives only and imposing the constraint $γ_μψ^μ=0$, we eliminate one of the spin-1/2 contributions. The resulting propagator provides a clean separation of spin-3/2 proper and the one remaining spin-1/2 contribution. In addition to a conventional pole term describing pure spin-3/2, which is identical to the propagator proposed by Williams, this procedure yields a second pole term describing spin-1/2 only, with a pole at twice the mass parameter of the Lagrangian. Its effect on physical observables may manifest itself in a manner which is indistinguishable from a particle in its own right. The second pole term cancels the $1/p^2$ singularity of the Williams propagator and the propagator derived here is well behaved for vanishing $p^2$.

# Propagation of a massive spin-3/2 particle

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by using the "singular" parameter values of point transformations (A = -1/2, a = -1/4) in the most general first-order-derivative Lagrangian for a massive spin-3/2 field, one can eliminate the spin-1/2 contributions that do not satisfy the Rarita-Schwinger constraint γ_μ ψ^μ = 0, but this procedure unavoidably produces a second pole term at twice the mass parameter M that describes a pure spin-1/2 contribution and may be indistinguishable from an independent spin-1/2 particle.

## Method

The author starts from the most general Lagrangian for a spin-3/2 field containing only first-order derivatives, which is invariant under point transformations parameterized by a free parameter A. By choosing the "singular" value A = -1/2 (corresponding to the point transformation parameter a = -1/4), the Lagrangian is restricted to act only on the Rarita-Schwinger (1,1/2) spinor-vector subspace where γ_μ ψ^μ = 0 holds. The propagator is then obtained by inverting the Lagrangian operator on this restricted subspace rather than on the full spinor-vector space, yielding a clean separation of spin-3/2 proper and the remaining spin-1/2 contribution.

## Key numbers

* The spin-3/2 proper pole term has a pole at p^2 = M^2, where M is the mass parameter of the Lagrangian.
* The additional spin-1/2 pole term has a pole at p^2 = (2M)^2, i.e., at twice the mass parameter of the Lagrangian.
* The Rarita-Schwinger propagator tensor Δ^{μν}_{RS} contains terms with 1/(3M^2) and 1/(3M) dependence, specifically: Δ^{μν}_{RS} = g^{μν} - (1/3)γ^μ γ^ν - (2p^μ p^ν)/(3M^2) - (γ^μ p^ν - γ^ν p^μ)/(3M).
* The contact term G_A^{μν} for general A contains a factor 1/(3M^2) × (A+1)/(2A+1)^2, which becomes infinite at A = -1/2.
* The off-shell parameter z = -1/4 is identified as the value that makes the vertex tensor θ^{μν}(-1/2,-1/4) = D^{μν}, the Rarita-Schwinger projector.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of spin-3/2 field descriptions dating back to Rarita and Schwinger (1941) and the subsequent analyses by Moldauer and Case (1956), Fronsdal (1958), and Peccei (1968). It directly addresses the problem of unwanted spin-1/2 components in spin-3/2 propagators, which was recently examined by Pascalutsa (1998) using Dirac constraint analysis. The paper provides a practical propagator that avoids the 1/p^2 singularity of the Williams propagator (1985) while maintaining a consistent separation of spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 contributions, enabling calculations for processes involving the Δ(1232) resonance at facilities like CEBAF at Jefferson Laboratory.
