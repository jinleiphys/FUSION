# Determination of vector meson properties by matching resonance saturation to a constituent quark model

> The properties of mesonic resonances can be calculated in terms of the low-energy coefficients of chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) by extending unitarized chiPT to higher energies. On the other hand these low-energy coefficients can be calculated in two different models, namely (i) by assuming resonance saturation and (ii) within a constituent quark model. By matching the expressions of the two models combined with the results of unitarized chiPT and the Weinberg sum rules the properties of vector and axial-vector mesons can be calculated in the combined large-N_c and chiral limit.

# Determination of vector meson properties by matching resonance saturation to a constituent quark model

## Key claim
In the combined large-N_c and chiral limit, the masses and coupling constants of vector and axial-vector mesons can be determined purely from chiral symmetry breaking parameters, without input from confinement beyond the requirement that color-white resonances form instead of quark-antiquark pairs.

## Method
The low-energy coefficients of chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) were calculated independently from two approaches: (i) a resonance saturation model where vector mesons mediate low-energy interactions, and (ii) a chiral constituent quark model where quarks couple to Goldstone bosons. These expressions were matched to each other and combined with unitarized chiPT via the inverse amplitude method and the Weinberg sum rules to extract vector and axial-vector meson properties.

## Key numbers
- Rho meson mass: M_V = 826 MeV, calculated from M_V^2 = 24 pi^2 F_pi^2 / N_c using F_pi ≈ 93 MeV, compared to the physical rho mass of 770 MeV
- Vector meson coupling constants: F_V^2 = 2 F_pi^2 and G_V^2 = F_pi^2 / 2, yielding the universality relation F_V = 2 G_V
- Rho-pi-pi coupling constant: g = sqrt(3/N_c) * 2 pi, compared to the experimental value of 6.05 extracted from the rho → pi pi decay width
- Axial-vector a1 mass: M_A = sqrt(3/N_c) * 4 pi F_pi ≈ 1169 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1230 ± 40 MeV
- Axial-vector a1 coupling: F_A = F_pi ≈ 93 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 124 ± 27 MeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Pelaez (2001) who used the inverse amplitude method to unitarize chiPT and reproduce meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV, and of Ecker et al. (1989) who developed the resonance saturation formalism for low-energy constants. The matching procedure demonstrates that chiral symmetry breaking quantitatively determines vector and axial-vector meson properties, with confinement entering only qualitatively. The framework is expected to be extendable to finite temperature and density for studying in-medium changes of meson properties.
