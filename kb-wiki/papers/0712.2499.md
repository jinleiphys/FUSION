# Fusion-fission reactions with modified Woods-Saxon potential

> A modified Woods-Saxon potential model is proposed for a unified description of the entrance channel fusion barrier and the fission barrier of fusion-fission reactions based on the Skyrme energy-density functional approach. The fusion excitation functions of 120 reactions have been systematically studied. The fusion (capture) cross sections are well described with the calculated potential and an empirical barrier distribution. Incorporating a statistical model (HIVAP code) for describing the decay of the compound nucleus, the evaporation residue (and fission) cross sections of 51 fusion-fission reactions have been systematically investigated. Optimal values of some key parameters of the HIVAP code are obtained based on the experimental data of these reactions. The experimental data are reasonably well reproduced by the calculated results. The upper and lower confidence limits of the systematic errors of the calculated results are given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified Woods-Saxon potential model that can simultaneously describe both the entrance-channel fusion barrier and the fission barrier for fusion-fission reactions, based on the Skyrme energy-density functional approach; the claim is hedged by the condition that the model is tested only for reactions where quasi-fission is not important (compound-nuclear charge number Z_CN below about 100 and sufficiently asymmetric systems).

## Method

The authors fit five parameters of an analytical Woods-Saxon nuclear potential to reproduce the fusion barrier heights obtained from the Skyrme energy-density functional (SkM* force) with the extended Thomas-Fermi approach for 66,996 reactions with Z1Z2 ≤ 3000. They then use this potential with an empirical barrier distribution (superposition of two Gaussians) to calculate fusion (capture) cross sections via Wong's formula for 120 reactions. For 51 fusion-fission reactions, they incorporate the HIVAP statistical model for compound-nucleus decay, using the same modified Woods-Saxon potential to compute macroscopic fission barriers, and optimize the level-density radius parameter r_a by minimizing the average logarithmic deviation from experimental evaporation residue and fission cross sections.

## Key numbers

- 66,996 reactions with Z1Z2 ≤ 3000 were used to determine the modified Woods-Saxon potential parameters; the optimal values are r_0 = 1.27 fm, c = -1.37 fm, u_0 = -44.16 MeV, κ = -0.40, a = 0.75 fm.
- For 120 fusion reactions, about 70% of systems have average logarithmic deviation χ²_log less than 0.005 for fusion cross sections at energies near and above the barrier.
- The systematic error for fusion (capture) cross sections at energies near and above the barriers is estimated as 18%.
- For 51 fusion-fission reactions with the modified Woods-Saxon fission barriers and r_a = 1.120 fm, the minimal average logarithmic deviation χ²_log is 0.1086; 68.3% of reactions have χ²_log values smaller than 0.0714.
- The upper and lower confidence limits of the systematic errors for the calculated survival probabilities W_sur are 1.85 W_sur and W_sur/1.85, respectively.
- For the reaction ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb forming ²⁵⁶No, the macroscopic fission barrier obtained with the modified Woods-Saxon potential is 1.74 MeV; for ²⁴⁴Pu the barrier is 4.16 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the modified Woods-Saxon potential model first proposed by Liu (2006) for fusion reactions to also describe fission barriers, enabling a unified treatment of the capture and decay stages of fusion-fission reactions. It systematically tests and refines parameters of the HIVAP statistical model (developed by Reisdorf, 1981, 1985, 1992) against 51 fusion-fission reactions, finding that the modified Woods-Saxon potential gives smaller average deviations than the fission barriers of Cohen and Swiatecki (1963), Sierk, and Dahlinger (1982). The approach enables future studies of quasi-fission in superheavy element synthesis by reducing ambiguity in the capture and survival probability components, allowing more reliable extraction of the compound-nucleus formation probability P_CN.
