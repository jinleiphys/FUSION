# Irregularities in nuclear radii at magic numbers

> Influence of magic numbers on nuclear radii is investigated via the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations and available experimental data. With the $\ell s$ potential including additional density-dependence suggested from the chiral effective field theory, kinks are universally predicted at the $jj$-closed magic numbers and anti-kinks (\textit{i.e.} inverted kinks) are newly predicted at the $\ell s$-closed magic numbers, both in the charge radii and in the matter radii along the isotopic and isotonic chains where nuclei stay spherical. These results seem consistent with the kinks of the charge radii observed in Ca, Sn and Pb and the anti-kink in Ca. The kinks and the anti-kinks could be a peculiar indicator for magic numbers, discriminating $jj$-closure and $\ell s$-closure.

## Key claim

Kinks in nuclear charge and matter radii are universally predicted at $jj$-closed magic numbers, and anti-kinks (inverted kinks) are newly predicted at $\ell s$-closed magic numbers, but only when the spin-orbit potential includes an additional density-dependence suggested by chiral effective field theory; these irregularities may discriminate between $jj$-closure and $\ell s$-closure as a peculiar indicator for magic numbers.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for nuclei with magic proton or neutron numbers, using the M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P6a semi-realistic effective interactions, with the latter incorporating a density-dependent term in the spin-orbit potential motivated by three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. Results were compared with calculations using the Gogny-D1S interaction and with relativistic mean-field (RMF) results using the NL3 parameter set, as well as with experimental charge radii from isotope shifts and matter radii from hadronic probes.

## Key numbers

- Kink at $N=126$ in Pb isotopes: M3Y-P6a reproduces the observed kink in differential mean-square charge radii $\Delta\langle r^2\rangle_c$, while D1S yields no apparent kink and M3Y-P6 gives a weaker kink than observed
- Kink at $N=82$ in Sn isotopes: predicted with M3Y-P6a and later observed experimentally at $^{132}$Sn
- Anti-kink at $N=20$ in Ca isotopes: predicted with M3Y-P6a and consistent with recent measurement at $^{40}$Ca
- Anti-kink at $N=40$ in Ni isotopes: predicted with M3Y-P6a at $^{68}$Ni
- Kink at $N=28$ in Ca isotopes: pronounced with M3Y-P6a and observed at $^{48}$Ca
- Anti-kink predicted at $Z=20$ for $N=20$ isotonic chain with M3Y-P6a
- Kink predicted at $Z=28$ for $N=50$ isotonic chain with M3Y-P6a
- Anti-kink predicted at $Z=58$ and weak kink at $Z=64$ for $N=82$ isotonic chain with M3Y-P6a
- Kink predicted at $N=14$ for oxygen isotopes ($Z=8$), enhanced by the density-dependent spin-orbit term

## Context

This work extends the understanding of kinks in charge radii observed at magic numbers in Ca, Sn, and Pb isotopes (Angeli 2013) by providing a theoretical mechanism based on three-nucleon force contributions to the spin-orbit potential, building on earlier suggestions from chiral effective field theory (Kohno 2013). The results are consistent with recent experimental kink discoveries at $^{48}$Ca and $^{132}$Sn, and the Fayans energy density functional (Fayans 1998) also predicts kinks at these nuclei, though the relation to the nucleonic interaction is less clear in that approach. The predictions of anti-kinks at $\ell s$-closed magic numbers offer a new experimental tool to discriminate between $jj$-closure and $\ell s$-closure, and future measurements around $\ell s$-closed magicity could help identify the dominant source of these irregularities.
