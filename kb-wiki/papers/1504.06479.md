# Effect of in-medium parameters of rho meson in its photoproduction reactions on nuclei

> There exist model calculations showing the modification of the hadronic parameters of $ρ$ meson in the nuclear environment. From these parameters, we extract the $ρ$ meson nucleus optical potential and show the medium effect due to this potential on the $ρ$ meson mass distribution spectra in the photonuclear reaction. The calculated results reproduced reasonably the measured $e^+e^-$ invariant mass, i.e., $ρ$ meson mass, distribution spectra in the $( γ, ρ^0 \to e^+e^- )$ reaction on nuclei.

# Effect of in-medium parameters of rho meson in its photoproduction reactions on nuclei

## Key claim
The paper shows that the in-medium modification of the rho meson in photonuclear reactions manifests primarily as a broadening of the mass distribution spectrum without a significant peak shift (within 10 MeV), and that the calculated spectra reproduce the CLAS experimental data reasonably. The hedge is that the results depend on the specific choice of the momentum-dependent function f(k_rho) for the mass-shift, and that the cross sections are about 2.5 to 3.5 times larger than those obtained from a previous potential based on rho-nucleon scattering parameters (Das 2015).

## Method
The author extracts the rho meson-nucleus optical potential from model predictions of the in-medium mass shift and collision broadening, using the QCD sum rule result of Hatsuda and Lee (1993) for the density-dependent mass and the parametrizations of Weidmann et al. (2000) and Leupold et al. (2006) for the in-medium width. A new logarithmic momentum-dependent function f(k_rho) is introduced to satisfy the behavior of the in-medium mass at low momentum. The differential cross section for the rho meson mass distribution in the (gamma, rho^0 -> e+e-) reaction is calculated using an eikonal propagator and a generalized optical potential for photoproduction, with the photon beam energy profile divided into six bins from 1.0 to 3.5 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The peak cross section for free rho meson propagation through Fe-56 is approximately 0.2 microbarn/GeV at a mass of 740 MeV.
- Including the mass-shift potential alone enhances the peak cross section by a factor of about 1.26.
- Including the momentum-dependent mass-shift reduces the peak cross section by a factor of about 1.5 relative to the mass-shift-only case.
- Including both momentum-dependent mass-shift and collision broadening reduces the peak further by a factor of 1.11 at the peak, giving a total reduction factor of 1.3 compared to the non-interacting case.
- The rho meson mass distribution shows no significant peak shift, within 10 MeV, due to the optical potential.
- The cross sections in the present calculation are about 2.5 to 3.5 times larger than those reported in the earlier work by Das (2015) using the t-rho approximation potential.

## Context
This work extends the study of in-medium vector meson modification from ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions (where STAR at RHIC found a mass decrease of about 70 MeV, and NA60 at CERN reported significant broadening without mass shift) to the cleaner environment of photonuclear reactions on normal nuclei. It builds on the QCD sum rule predictions of Hatsuda and Lee (1993) and the vector meson dominance model of Asakawa et al. (1995) for mass reduction, and the collision broadening parametrizations of Weidmann et al. (2000) and Leupold et al. (2006). The calculated results are compared with CLAS data from Jefferson Laboratory (Nasseripour et al., 2007) for C-12 and combined Ti-48/Fe-56 targets. The paper enables future work to determine the rho meson potential unambiguously once absolute normalized data become available, which would then allow extraction of the in-medium parameters.
