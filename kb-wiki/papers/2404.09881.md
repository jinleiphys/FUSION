# Charmonium Transport in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions at the LHC

> We provide an update on our semi-classical transport approach for quarkonium production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, focusing on $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ mesons in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at both forward and mid-rapidity. In particular, we employ the most recent charm-production cross sections reported in pp collisions, which are pivotal for the magnitude of the regeneration contribution, and their modifications due to cold-nuclear-matter (CNM) effects. Multi-differential observables are calculated in terms of nuclear modification factors as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity, including the contributions from bottom-decay feeddown. For our predictions for $ψ(2S)$ production, the mechanism of sequential regeneration relative to the more strongly bound $J/ψ$ meson plays an important role in interpreting recent ALICE data.

## Key claim
The updated semi-classical transport model for charmonium production in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC finds that the magnitude of the regeneration contribution is critically dependent on the most recent charm-production cross sections from pp collisions and their cold-nuclear-matter modifications, with the sequential regeneration of the less-bound ψ(2S) relative to the J/ψ playing an important role in interpreting recent ALICE data.

## Method
The authors employed a semi-classical transport approach to model the production of J/ψ and ψ(2S) mesons in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The calculation incorporated updated charm-production cross sections from pp collisions and their modifications due to cold-nuclear-matter effects, and included contributions from bottom-decay feeddown. Multi-differential observables were computed as nuclear modification factors as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity at both forward and mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers
- The study focuses on J/ψ and ψ(2S) mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair at the LHC.
- Calculations were performed for both forward and mid-rapidity regions.
- The nuclear modification factor (R_AA) was calculated as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity.

## Context
This work provides an update to the authors' previous semi-classical transport approach for quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions. It extends prior studies by incorporating the most recent charm-production cross sections from pp collisions, which are pivotal for the regeneration contribution, and by including predictions for ψ(2S) production to interpret recent data from the ALICE collaboration. The results enable a more precise comparison between theory and experiment for charmonium suppression and regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma.
