# Regularized Green's Function for the Inverse Square Potential

> A Green's function approach is presented for the D-dimensional inverse square potential in quantum mechanics. This approach is implemented by the introduction of hyperspherical coordinates and the use of a real-space regulator in the regularized version of the model. The application of Sturm-Liouville theory yields a closed expression for the radial energy Green's function. Finally, the equivalence with a recent path-integral treatment of the same problem is explicitly shown.

## Key claim
The paper presents a Green's function approach for the D-dimensional inverse square potential that is equivalent to a previous path-integral treatment, but only in the supercritical (strong coupling) regime where regularization is needed; the subcritical regime requires no regularization and follows from a simple shift in the angular momentum quantum number.

## Method
The authors solve the radial Schrodinger equation for the inverse square potential using Sturm-Liouville theory, introducing a real-space regulator at radial position r=a to restore a sensible boundary condition at the origin for the supercritical coupling regime. They construct the radial energy Green's function from homogeneous solutions of Bessel functions, applying boundary conditions at infinity and at the regulator position a. The resulting expressions are then analytically continued from negative to positive energies to obtain the scattering sector.

## Key numbers
- Critical coupling for each angular momentum channel: lambda_l(*) = (l + nu)^2, where nu = (D-2)/2
- Supercritical regime order parameter: Theta_l = sqrt(lambda - lambda_l(*))
- Bound-state energy levels: E_{n_r l} = -(2 e^{-gamma}/a)^2 exp(-2 pi n_r / Theta_l), where n_r is the radial quantum number and gamma is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- The radial Green's function for bound states (Eq. 3.14): G_{l+nu}(r'',r';E|lambda;a) = -(2M/hbar^2) sqrt(r'r'') [K_{i Theta_l}(kappa r_>)/K_{i Theta_l}(kappa a)] x [K_{i Theta_l}(kappa a) I_{i Theta_l}(kappa r_<) - I_{i Theta_l}(kappa a) K_{i Theta_l}(kappa r_<)], with kappa = sqrt(-2ME)/hbar

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous path-integral treatment of the inverse square potential (Camblong and Ordonez, 2001) by providing an equivalent nonperturbative operator approach. The results are applied to the electron-polar molecule interaction (Camblong and Ordonez, 2001), where the inverse square potential arises in the point-dipole approximation and leads to a quantum anomaly with SO(2,1) conformal symmetry breaking. The paper also connects to earlier regularization and renormalization analyses by Gupta and Rajeev (1993) and by Camblong and Ordonez (2000) using the Schrodinger equation.
