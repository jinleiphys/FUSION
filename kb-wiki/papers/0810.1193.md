# (medium-modified) Fragmentation Functions

> In this short review paper, we discuss some of the recent advances in the field of parton fragmentation processes into hadrons as well as their possible modifications in QCD media. Hadron production data in e+e-, deep inelastic scattering and hadronic collisions are presented, together with global analyses of fragmentation functions into light and heavy hadrons and developments on parton fragmentation in perturbative QCD at small momentum fraction. Motivated by the recent RHIC data indicating a significant suppression of large-pT hadron production in heavy-ion collisions, several recent attempts to model medium-modified fragmentation, e.g. by solving "medium" evolution equations or through Monte Carlo studies, have been proposed and are discussed in detail. Finally we mention the possibility to extract medium-modified fragmentation functions using photon-hadron correlations.

# (medium-modified) Fragmentation Functions

## Key claim

This review argues that while fragmentation functions (FFs) cannot be computed from first principles, their scaling violations are under perturbative control and have been successfully tested in e+e- collisions; however, the recent RHIC data showing a factor-of-5 suppression of large-pT hadron production in central Au-Au collisions motivate the development of medium-modified fragmentation models, though the precise mechanism and the extent to which medium-modified evolution equations or Monte Carlo studies capture the full physics remain open questions.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on hadron production in e+e- annihilation (DORIS/PETRA, PEP, TRISTAN, LEP-I/II, SLD, B-factories), deep inelastic scattering (HERA, HERMES, NOMAD), and hadronic collisions (RHIC, Tevatron), then compares global QCD fits of fragmentation functions (HKNS, DSS, AKK08) that use DGLAP evolution equations solved via Mellin transform or direct z-integration techniques. For medium modifications, the review discusses models that solve medium-modified evolution equations and Monte Carlo parton shower approaches for heavy-ion collisions, and proposes photon-hadron correlations as a method to extract medium-modified fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of single inclusive hadron spectra by a factor of 5 in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC relative to scaled p-p scattering (PHENIX, STAR experiments)
- e+e- data cover center-of-mass energies from sqrt(s)=12-36 GeV (DORIS/PETRA), 29 GeV (PEP), 58 GeV (TRISTAN), 91.2 GeV (LEP-I/SLC), and 133-209 GeV (LEP-II)
- DIS data from HERA cover virtuality range 10 less than Q less than 100 GeV; NOMAD covers 1 less than Q less than 10 GeV
- CDF measured single inclusive charged hadron spectra inside jets for dijet masses from 80 to 600 GeV in p-pbar collisions
- DSS fit obtained normalization parameter N=1.10 for D_{d+bar d} relative to D_{u+bar u} from global analysis including HERMES SIDIS data
- AKK08 fit with large-x resummation achieved chi^2/ndf=1.45 for Lambda/bar Lambda production versus chi^2/ndf=1.73 without resummation
- AKK08 fitted hadron masses within 1% of true masses for baryons (p/bar p, Lambda/bar Lambda), 10% larger for pions, 30% smaller for kaons
- DSS reported D_s^{pi+}=0.83 D_{bar u}^{pi+} from their fit, breaking SU(3) symmetry

## Context

This review extends the line of work on parton fragmentation functions from e+e- annihilation (BKK, BFGW, KKP, Kretzer, AKK05) to include recent global fits with error analyses (HKNS 2007, DSS 2007, AKK08) analogous to parton distribution function extractions (Pumplin 2001, Stump 2001, Botje 1999). It also covers medium-modified fragmentation motivated by RHIC jet quenching data (PHENIX 2001, 2003; STAR 2002, 2003), discussing models by Borghini (2005), Armesto (2007), Domdey (2008), and Monte Carlo studies by Zapp (2008), Renk (2008), and Cunqueiro (2008). The review enables future work on extracting medium-modified fragmentation functions using photon-hadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions and applying these to LHC phenomenology.
