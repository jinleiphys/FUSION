# Deuteron tensor polarization component T_20(Q^2) as a crucial test for deuteron wave functions

> The deuteron tensor polarization component T_20(Q^2) is calculated by relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics approach. It is shown that in the range of momentum transfers available in to-day experiments, relativistic effects, meson exchange currents and the choice of nucleon electromagnetic form factors almost do not influence the value of T_20(Q^2). At the same time, this value depends strongly on the actual form of the deuteron wave function, that is on the model of NN-interaction in deuteron. So the existing data for T_20(Q^2) provide a crucial test for deuteron wave functions.

## Key claim

The deuteron tensor polarization component \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) in elastic electron-deuteron scattering is almost independent of relativistic effects, meson exchange currents, and the choice of nucleon electromagnetic form factors up to \(Q^2 \approx 3\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), but depends strongly on the deuteron wave function; therefore existing data provide a crucial test for discriminating between different models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the deuteron.

## Method

The authors calculate \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) using a variant of the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics (IF RHD), which is a Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics. The electromagnetic current matrix element is constructed via a modified impulse approximation that ensures Lorentz covariance and current conservation by using the Wigner-Eckart theorem for the Poincaré group. The deuteron charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors are expressed as integrals over relativistic wave functions and free two-particle form factors, and the nonrelativistic limit is taken for comparison. The calculation is performed for several model deuteron wave functions (Paris potential, Nijmegen I, II, and 93, charge-dependent Bonn, and the dispersion-theoretic MT wave functions) using different parametrizations of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic corrections to \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) are small up to \(Q^2 \simeq 3\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\); at \(Q^2 \ge 3.5\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) the corrections become larger and model-dependent.
- The experimental data for \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) extend up to \(Q^2 \approx 2\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) and can be described in terms of nonrelativistic theory without relativistic effects or meson exchange currents.
- The predictions for \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) from different wave functions coincide only at \(Q^2 \le 0.5\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\); at higher \(Q^2\) the results are strongly model-dependent.
- The MT wave functions (obtained from the potentialless inverse scattering approach) give the best description of the experimental data, compared to the Paris, Nijmegen, and Bonn wave functions.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing nonrelativistic program (Lev73, MoG74) of using deuteron tensor polarization to probe short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction into a fully relativistic framework. It contradicts the expectation that relativistic effects, meson exchange currents, or nucleon form factor uncertainties would mask the dependence on the deuteron wave function, as was the case in some earlier relativistic approaches (VaD95). The paper enables a clean discrimination between deuteron wave functions using existing experimental data (ScB84, Dmi85, GiH90, ThA91, FeB96, BoA99, AbA00), and specifically favors the dispersion-theoretic MT wave functions (MuT81, Tro94) over the widely used Paris (LaL81), Nijmegen (StK94), and Bonn (Mac01) potentials.
