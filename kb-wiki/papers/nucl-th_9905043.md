# The piNN vertex function in a meson-theoretical model

> The piNN vertex function is calculated within a dispersion theoretical approach, including both pi-rho and pi-sigma intermediate states, where the sigma meson is an abbreviation for a correlated pion pair in a relative S-wave with isospin I=0. A strong coupling between the pi-sigma and the pi-rho states is found. This leads to a softening of the piNN form factor. In a monopole parameterization, a cut-off Lambda=800 MeV is obtained as compared to Lambda=1000 MeV using pi-rho intermediate states only.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a derivative coupling for the sigma-pion-pion vertex, rather than a scalar coupling used in earlier work, produces a strong coupling between the pi-sigma and pi-rho intermediate states that softens the piNN form factor. This result revises earlier findings by Durso (1977) and Dillig (1979) that the pi-sigma channel was negligible.

## Method

The authors calculate the piNN vertex function using a dispersion theoretical approach with time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) for the meson-meson scattering kernel. They include both pi-rho and pi-sigma intermediate states, where the sigma meson represents a correlated pion pair in a relative S-wave with isospin I=0, and solve the coupled-channel scattering equations to obtain the T-matrix. The imaginary part of the form factor in the time-like region is computed from unitarity cuts, and a subtracted dispersion relation is used to obtain the form factor for space-like momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

- With only pi-rho intermediate states, the monopole cutoff is Lambda = 1500 MeV
- With only pi-rho intermediate states and a static pion propagator approximation, the cutoff is Lambda = 1000 MeV (consistent with Janssen (1993))
- With both pi-rho and pi-sigma intermediate states coupled, the monopole cutoff is Lambda = 800 MeV
- The uncorrelated pi-rho exchange gives a cutoff of Lambda = 2100 MeV
- The maximum of the spectral function is located at t approximately 75 m_pi^2 (1.2 GeV), coinciding with the mass of the pi' meson

## Context

This work extends the meson-theoretical model of Janssen (1993) which included only correlated pi-rho states and obtained a cutoff of 1000 MeV, still harder than phenomenological form factors used in other processes. The paper shows that the derivative sigma-pi-pi coupling, motivated by chiral invariance and used in the pion-nucleon scattering model of Schutz (1994), produces a strong coupling between pi-sigma and pi-rho channels that earlier calculations using scalar couplings missed. The resulting form factor with Lambda = 800 MeV matches the phenomenological pi' exchange introduced by Holinde and Thomas (1990), allowing the derived vertex to be interpreted in terms of correlated coupled pi-rho and pi-sigma exchange rather than requiring a separate pi' meson.
