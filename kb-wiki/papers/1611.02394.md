# Spin polarization and color superconductivity in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature

> We investigate the possible existence of spin polarization and color superconductivity in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with a tensor-type interaction at finite density and temperature. The thermodynamic potential is calculated by the functional integral method. Numerical results indicate that at low temperature and quark chemical potential the chiral condensed phase exists, and at intermediate chemical potential the color superconducting phase appears. In addition, depending on the magnitude of the tensor coupling, at large chemical potential and low temperature, a color superconducting phase and a spin polarized phase may coexist while at intermediate temperatures only the spin polarized phase occurs.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether spin polarization and color superconductivity can coexist in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with a tensor-type interaction at finite density and temperature, finding that coexistence is possible only for sufficiently large tensor coupling (greater than 1.5 times the scalar coupling) and that the chiral condensed phase and color superconducting phase do not coexist in the chosen parameter set.

## Method

The authors start from an SU(2) flavor, SU(3) color NJL Lagrangian including scalar, tensor-type, and quark pairing (diquark) interaction terms. They introduce auxiliary fields for the chiral condensate M, the spin polarization order parameter F, and the color superconducting gap Δ, then compute the thermodynamic potential via the functional integral method in the one-loop approximation. The Matsubara summation is performed to obtain the finite-temperature thermodynamic potential, which is then minimized numerically for different values of chemical potential μ, temperature T, and tensor coupling strength G_T.

## Key numbers

* Three parameter sets are used: GT0 (G_T = 0), GT2 (G_T = 2 G_S = 11.0 GeV^{-2}), and GT2.6 (G_T = 2.6 G_S = 14.3 GeV^{-2}), all with Λ = 0.631 GeV, G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2}, and G_C = 6.6 GeV^{-2}
* For model GT0 at μ = 0.25 GeV and T = 0.01 GeV, only the chiral condensed phase exists; at μ = 0.35 GeV and T = 0.01 GeV, the color superconducting phase appears while the chiral condensate disappears
* For model GT2 at μ = 0.42 GeV and T = 0.01–0.03 GeV, the color superconducting gap and spin polarized condensate coexist; at μ = 0.42 GeV and T = 0.05 GeV, only the spin polarized phase remains
* For model GT2.6, the color superconducting phase does not appear; the coexistence phase is realized after the chiral condensed phase disappears
* When G_T = 1.5 G_S, neither the spin polarized phase nor the coexisting phase is obtained; G_T must be larger than 1.5 G_S for these phases to appear

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study (Matsuoka 2016) to finite temperature, building on earlier investigations of spin polarization in the NJL model with tensor-type interactions (Bohr 2012, Tsue 2012, Tsue 2013, Tsue 2015). The results contrast with studies using vector-type interactions (Maedan 2007) and one-gluon-exchange interactions (Tatsumi 2000), and the phase diagrams differ from those obtained with different parameter choices where chiral and color superconducting phases may coexist (Blaschke 2003). The findings enable future work incorporating strange quarks, external magnetic fields, charge neutrality, and beta-equilibrium conditions relevant to neutron star physics.
