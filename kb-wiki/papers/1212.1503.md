# Cooper pairing above the critical temperature in a unitary Fermi gas

> We present an ab initio determination of spin responses of the unitary Fermi gas, based on finite temperature quantum Monte Carlo calculations and the Kubo linear-response formalism. We determine the temperature dependence of the spin susceptibility and the spin conductivity. We show that both quantities exhibit suppression above the critical temperature of the superfluid to normal phase transition due to presence of the Cooper pairs. The spin diffusion transport coefficient does not display the existence of a minimum in the vicinity of the critical temperature and it drops to very low values D_s approx 0.8hbar/m in the superfluid phase. All these spin observables show a smooth and monotonic behavior with temperature when crossing the critical temperature T_c, until the Fermi liquid regime is attained at the temperature T*, where the pseudogap regime disappears.

## Key claim

The spin susceptibility and spin conductivity of the unitary Fermi gas exhibit suppression above the critical temperature \(T_c\) due to the survival of Cooper pairs, and these spin observables show a smooth and monotonic behavior with temperature when crossing \(T_c\) until the Fermi liquid regime is attained at temperature \(T^*\), where the pseudogap regime disappears.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature path integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) calculations on lattices of sizes \(8^3\), \(10^3\), and \(12^3\) to simulate the homogeneous unitary Fermi gas. They extracted the static spin susceptibility directly from the imaginary-time spin-spin correlator and obtained the spin conductivity by inverting the spectral relation for the current-current correlator using singular value decomposition and maximum entropy methods, incorporating the Tan contact density as prior information.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition: \(T_c = 0.15(1)\,\varepsilon_F\), where \(\varepsilon_F = p_F^2/2m\) is the free Fermi gas energy.
- Onset temperature of the pseudogap regime (Cooper-pair formation): \(T^* = 0.20-0.25\,\varepsilon_F\).
- Spin diffusion coefficient in the superfluid phase: \(D_s \approx 0.8\,\hbar/m\).
- Spin susceptibility in the normal phase (0.25–0.5 \(\varepsilon_F\)): approximately \(\chi_s \approx 0.45\,\chi_0\), where \(\chi_0 = 3n/2\varepsilon_F\) is the free Fermi gas susceptibility.
- Spin diffusion coefficient in the normal phase (0.25–0.5 \(\varepsilon_F\)): approximately constant \(D_s \approx 1.8\) (in units of \(\hbar/m\)).

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo studies of the unitary Fermi gas (Magierski et al., 2009; Magierski et al., 2011) that identified a pseudogap regime between \(T_c\) and \(T^*\) from the density of states. The spin response results are consistent with the experimental spin susceptibility measurement of Sanner et al. (2011) and disagree with the MIT group's spin transport measurements (Sommer et al., 2011), which the authors argue probe a nonequilibrium state. The findings enable first-principles calculations of neutrino processes in neutron stars, where the neutron spin response is a key input, and provide a more accurate determination of the pseudogap onset temperature than previous density-of-states analyses.
