# $ρ$ - meson spectral function in hot nuclear matter

> We study the $ρ$-meson spectral function in hot nuclear matter by taking into account the isospin-symmetric pion and the nucleon loops within the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model as well as using an effective chiral SU(3) model. The spectral function of the $ρ$ meson is studied in the mean field approximation (MFA) as well as in the relativistic Hartree (RHA) approximation. The inclusion of the nucleon loop considerably changes the $ρ$-meson spectral function. Due to a larger mass drop of $ ρ$ meson in the RHA, it is seen that the spectral function shifts towards the low invariant mass region, whereas in the MFA the spectral function is seen to be slightly shifted towards the high mass region. Moreover, while the spectral function is observed to be sharper with the nucleon-antinucleon polarization in RHA, the spectral function is seen to be broader in the MFA.

## Key claim

The ρ-meson spectral function in hot nuclear matter is calculated by including both pion and nucleon loops within the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model and an effective chiral SU(3) model, with the key qualifier that the nucleon loop contribution significantly alters the spectral function, and the direction of the shift (low or high invariant mass) depends on whether the mean field approximation (MFA) or the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) is used.

## Method

The authors computed the ρ-meson spectral function by evaluating the in-medium self-energy from ρNN and ρππ interactions using finite-temperature field theory, incorporating thermal nucleon and pion loops. The effective nucleon mass and chemical potential were determined self-consistently within both the QHD-I (Walecka) model and a chiral SU(3) model, using either the mean field approximation (MFA) or the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The spectral function was then obtained from the imaginary part of the retarded propagator for a fixed ρ-meson three-momentum of 0.75 GeV at various temperatures and baryon densities.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- For ρ_B = 0 in QHD-I MFA, M_N^* remains almost constant until T = 0.15 GeV, then drops to around 0.82 M_N at T = 0.2 GeV
- For ρ_B = 0 in chiral SU(3) MFA, M_N^* drops to around 0.8 M_N at T = 0.18 GeV
- Coupling constants used: g_ρππ²/4π = 2.91, g_ρNN² = 6.96, κ_ρ = 6.1
- With RHA in both models at ρ_B = 0 and T = 0.15 GeV, the spectral function peak shifts from around 0.77 GeV to around 0.7 GeV (Walecka) and 0.72 GeV (chiral SU(3))
- Vacuum ρ meson mass: m_ρ = 0.77 GeV; vacuum nucleon mass: M_N = 0.938 GeV; vacuum ω meson mass: m_ω = 0.783 GeV; vacuum σ meson mass: m_σ = 0.52 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vector meson in-medium modifications relevant to dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, building on the QHD-I model of Serot and Walecka and the chiral SU(3) model used by Zschocke and collaborators. The results are compared with the calculations of Chen and collaborators for the nucleon loop contribution, and with the work of Zschocke for the density dependence. The study enables improved modeling of low-invariant-mass dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collision experiments at SPS, RHIC, and future CBM facilities at GSI.
