# Energy and centrality dependence of thermodynamical observables from multiplicity in Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions

> Using a statistical model, we analyze published multiplicity distributions for identified hadrons produced in heavy ion collisions in the energy range from 2.7-200 \gev. Our analysis enables the prediction of the multiplicity distributions for multi-strangeness hadrons that have not yet been measured in lower-energy experiments. Furthermore, we obtain freeze-out parameters, including temperature, baryon chemical and strangeness potentials, the strangeness suppression factor, and the system radius, as functions of centrality and collision energy. Additionally, we computed and discussed the Skewness and its behavior at lower collision energies, highlighting the importance of the freeze-out parameters in determining the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter.

# Energy and centrality dependence of thermodynamical observables from multiplicity in Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions

## Key claim
Using the THERMAL-FIST statistical model in the Grand Canonical Ensemble, the authors extract freeze-out parameters (temperature, baryon chemical potential, strangeness potential, strangeness suppression factor, and system radius) from published multiplicity data across 2.7–200 GeV, but the model reproduces STAR data well only above 10 GeV, with discrepancies up to 13% at lower collision energies where data from AGS and NA49 have larger uncertainties and different collision systems (Au+Au vs. Pb+Pb).

## Method
The authors fitted published multiplicity distributions for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and lambdas from AGS (Au+Au at 2.7–4.86 GeV), NA49 (Pb+Pb at 6.3–17.3 GeV), and STAR (Au+Au at 7.7–200 GeV) using the THERMAL-FIST statistical hadron resonance gas model in the Grand Canonical Ensemble. They extracted freeze-out parameters by minimizing the difference between model predictions and experimental multiplicities, then used the fitted parameters to predict multiplicities for unmeasured multi-strange hadrons (Xi, Omega, Sigma, phi) and to parametrize temperature and chemical potentials as functions of collision energy.

## Key numbers
- Temperature reaches approximately 164 MeV (T_data) and 173 MeV (T_mod) at the highest energies, with fit parameters a_T = 0.16446 ± 0.0012 GeV for T_data and a_T = 0.173 ± 0.002 GeV for T_mod.
- Baryon chemical potential parametrization: d = 1.27347 ± 0.0023 GeV, e = 0.28554 ± 0.00012 GeV^{-1} in Eq. (4).
- Strangeness chemical potential parametrization: d = 1.075 ± 0.079, e = 1.366 ± 0.123 in Eq. (4).
- The baryon chemical potential to strangeness chemical potential ratio reaches approximately 5 at energies around 20 GeV.
- The fireball radius from STAR data saturates at approximately 6 fm at higher collision energies.
- The strangeness suppression factor (gamma_s) reaches a maximum around 7.7 GeV, then decreases slightly and saturates.
- Discrepancy between model and data for temperature at lower collision energies is up to 13%.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model analysis of freeze-out parameters performed by Cleymans (2004) for STAR data at 130 GeV and by Becattini (2009) for hadronization temperatures, now covering a wider energy range from 2.7 to 200 GeV using data from AGS, NA49, and STAR. The re-parametrization of temperature as a function of baryon chemical potential improves the description at lower energies relevant for upcoming NICA and FAIR experiments, particularly for the MPD-NICA detector. The predictions for multi-strange hadron multiplicities at unmeasured energies enable future experimental comparisons, and the skewness analysis connects freeze-out parameters to the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter described by the Quantum Van der Waals model of Vovchenko (2015).
