# Strange Form Factors of Baryons

> The strange magnetic form factor of proton is calculated in a model independent way to confirm the recent experimental result of the SAMPLE Collaboration. We consider a set of six inertia parameters to realize the magnetic moments of the baryon octet. We show that the strange form factor of proton is a positive quantity, i.e. +0.37 n.m.. Its positiveness is analyzed in terms of the vacuum fluctuation coupled to the vector current along the strangeness direction.

# Strange Form Factors of Baryons

## Key claim
The strange magnetic form factor of the proton is predicted to be positive, specifically +0.37 n.m., based on a model-independent parameterization of baryon magnetic moments using six inertia parameters. This positive result depends critically on including flavor symmetry breaking terms that describe vacuum fluctuations in the strangeness direction, which most previous models omitted.

## Method
The authors constructed a general SU(3) collective coordinate quantization scheme for chiral soliton models, parameterizing the magnetic moments of the baryon octet in terms of six inertia parameters (M, N, N', P, Q, and mI₂). They performed a least-squares fit of these parameters to the experimental magnetic moments of the baryon octet, treating the flavor symmetry breaking terms as perturbations that induce representation mixing in the baryon wavefunctions. The strange form factor was then extracted from the fitted parameters using the relation F^(s)_(2,N)(0) = -3 μ^(s)_N.

## Key numbers
- Proton strange magnetic form factor: +0.37 n.m. (predicted value)
- Experimental result from SAMPLE Collaboration: 0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19 n.m.
- Inertia parameter combination from fit: N + (1/2)N' = 10.95 (dimensionless)
- Inertia parameter combination from fit: mI₂ N' = 5.06 mI₂ + 2.87
- Inertia parameter from fit: Q = 0.64 mI₂ - 4.39
- χ² of fit to baryon magnetic moments: of order 10⁻³
- Up-quark contribution to proton magnetic moment: 2.45 n.m. (in case of vanishing strange form factor)
- Down-quark contribution to proton magnetic moment: -0.34 n.m. (in case of vanishing strange form factor)

## Context
This work extends the model-independent parameterization approach of Adkins (1990) for the SU(3) Skyrme model to the chiral bag model (CBM), and confirms the earlier positive strange form factor prediction of Hong and Park (HP) based on the SU(3) CBM. The result contradicts most model calculations that yield negative strange form factors, and supports the up-quark dominance conjecture of McKeown (1989). The positive value arises from flavor symmetry breaking terms describing the difference between kaon and pion decay constants (f_K ≠ f_π) and masses (m_K ≠ m_π, m_s ≠ m_u,d), which most previous models neglected. The work enables further investigation of kaon condensation in dense matter and neutron star cooling scenarios.
