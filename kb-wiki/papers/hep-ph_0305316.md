# An assessment of J/Psi formation in the light of initial RHIC data

> Predictions of J/Psi formation at RHIC via "off-diagonal" combinations of charm and anticharm quarks in a region of color deconfinement are confronted with initial data from the PHENIX collaboration. We find that the measured centrality behavior places significant constraints on the various parameters which control model calculations of J/Psi formation. Within present statistical and systematic uncertainties, one can map out a region of parameter space within which the contribution of formation in a deconfined phase is allowed. As these uncertainties decrease and new data from d-Au interactions becomes available, it is expected that definitive tests for the presence of this formation mechanism will be possible. We anticipate that the rapidity and transverse momentum spectra will prove decisive for a final determination.

# An assessment of J/Psi formation in the light of initial RHIC data

## Key claim

The measured centrality dependence of J/Psi production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, when compared with the kinetic formation model, places significant constraints on model parameters but does not yet definitively distinguish between formation in a deconfined phase and pure dissociation scenarios. Within present statistical and systematic uncertainties, one can map out a region of parameter space where formation in a deconfined phase is allowed, but even with reduced uncertainties an acceptable dissociation-only fit might still be found.

## Method

The kinetic formation model solves a Boltzmann equation for J/Psi and charm quark populations, balancing formation via charm-anticharm recombination (with gluon emission) against dissociation via gluon collisions, using a thermal gluon density and an expanding deconfined volume with isentropic expansion. The model calculations are compared with PHENIX data for J/Psi production at central rapidity in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, presented in three centrality bins, by converting measured rapidity densities to total J/Psi yields and scaling initial charm pair numbers according to the nuclear overlap function T_AA(b). Parameter scans are performed over initial temperature T_0, total charm pair number N_ccbar(b=0), and transverse expansion parameter vtr, using charm quark momentum distributions from leading-order pQCD.

## Key numbers

- PHENIX data: J/Psi production at central rapidity in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, presented in three centrality bins, with error bars from a sum of statistical and systematic uncertainties.
- Model calculations use N_ccbar(b=0) values of 5, 10, 15, and 20, with initial temperature T_0 scanned over a range (including 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5 GeV) to find sets compatible with data.
- The initial J/Psi population parameter x is set to zero for the parameter scan; earlier predictions used x = 0.01 and N_ccbar(b=0) = 10.
- The measured J/Psi rapidity density scaled by number of binary collisions shows a decrease with centrality, in contrast to the quadratic rise predicted by earlier formation model calculations.

## Context

This work confronts the kinetic formation model for J/Psi production via charm-anticharm recombination in a deconfined phase (Thews 2001, Thews 2002) with initial PHENIX data from RHIC, extending earlier SPS-era studies of anomalous J/Psi suppression (Matsui 1986). The results are contrasted with the statistical hadronization model (Braun-Munzinger 2000) and with pure dissociation scenarios using gluon breakup (Kharzeev 1994). The analysis enables future definitive tests using rapidity and transverse momentum spectra, as well as d-Au collision data, to distinguish between formation and dissociation mechanisms.
