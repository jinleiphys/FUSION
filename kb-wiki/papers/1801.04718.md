# Nucleon viewed as a Borromean Bound-State

> We explain how the emergent phenomenon of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking ensures that Poincaré covariant analyses of the three valence-quark scattering problem in continuum quantum field theory yield a picture of the nucleon as a Borromean bound-state, in which binding arises primarily through the sum of two separate contributions. One involves aspects of the non-Abelian character of Quantum Chromodynamics that are expressed in the strong running coupling and generate tight, dynamical color-antitriplet quark-quark correlations in the scalar-isoscalar and pseudovector-isotriplet channels. This attraction is magnified by quark exchange associated with diquark breakup and reformation, which is required in order to ensure that each valence-quark participates in all diquark correlations to the complete extent allowed by its quantum numbers. Combining these effects, we arrive at a properly antisymmetrised Faddeev wave function for the nucleon and calculate, e.g. the flavor-separated versions of the Dirac and Pauli form factors and the proton's leading-twist parton distribution amplitude. We conclude that available data and planned experiments are capable of validating the proposed picture.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) ensures that continuum QCD analyses of the three-valence-quark scattering problem yield a picture of the nucleon as a Borromean bound-state, where binding arises primarily from two contributions: tight color-antitriplet diquark correlations (scalar-isoscalar and pseudovector-isotriplet) plus additional attraction from quark exchange associated with diquark breakup and reformation, which ensures each valence quark participates in all diquark correlations to the full extent allowed by its quantum numbers.

## Method

The authors solved a Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon, reducing the three-body problem to a quark-plus-diquark system by exploiting the dominance of two-body interactions over three-body interactions. They computed the Faddeev amplitude using a kernel that incorporates dressed-quark propagators, diquark correlation amplitudes, and diquark propagators, then used this wave function to calculate electromagnetic form factors and the leading-twist parton distribution amplitude (PDA) via perturbation theory integral representations constrained by continuum bound-state equation solutions.

## Key numbers

- Scalar diquark strength in the nucleon is approximately 62% of the normalization, as predicted by realistic computations (Segovia 2014, Segovia 2015, Chen 2017).
- The ratio of proton electric to magnetic form factors, R_EM(Q^2) = mu_p G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2), decreases almost linearly with Q^2 and might become negative for Q^2 greater than approximately 8 GeV^2.
- The computed proton PDA evolved to zeta = 2 GeV peaks at ([x]) = (0.55, 0.23, 0.22), which is broader than the conformal limit PDA phi_N^cl([x]) = 120 x1 x2 x3.
- The Faddeev amplitude S_1(|p|,cos theta) peaks at (|p| approximately M_N/6, cos theta = 1), where p_q is approximately P/2 and p_d is approximately P/2.
- The d-quark contribution to the proton's Dirac form factor F_1^d is predicted to have a zero at x = Q^2/M_N^2 approximately 7.
- Neutron electric form factor data were released up to Q^2 = 3.4 GeV^2 by the Hall A Collaboration at JLab (Riordan 2011).

## Context

This work extends the continuum QCD approach to nucleon structure pioneered by Cahill (1987, 1988) and developed through two decades of two- and three-body bound-state studies (Maris 2002, Bhagwat 2004, Roberts 2011, Segovia 2013-2016, Eichmann 2016, Burkert 2017). The Borromean bound-state picture contradicts naive quark models by showing that no two-quark subsystem can form an asymptotic bound-state, consistent with lattice QCD results (Alexandrou 2006, Babich 2007) showing diquark correlations exist but are confined. The calculations enable experimental tests of the diquark picture through flavor-separated form factors at JLab and through measurements of the proton's parton distribution amplitude in hard exclusive processes.
