# Nuclear response functions for the N-N*(1440) transition

> Parity-conserving and -violating response functions are computed for the inclusive electroexcitation of the N*(1440)(Roper) resonance in nuclear matter modeled as a relativistic Fermi gas. Using various empirical parameterizations and theoretical models of the N-N*(1440) transition form factors, the sensitivity of the response functions to details of the structure of the Roper resonance is investigated. The possibility of disentangling this resonance from the contribution of Delta electroproduction in nuclei is addressed. Finally, the contributions of the Roper resonance to the longitudinal scaling function and to the Coulomb sum rule are also explored.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance (N*(1440)) can be disentangled from the Delta resonance in inclusive electron scattering primarily through the longitudinal response function, where the Delta contribution is suppressed by an order of magnitude, but only at low momentum transfers (around 500 MeV/c) where contributions from heavier resonances are small; the detectability is further limited by the severe kinematic quenching of the longitudinal channel near the light-cone.

## Method

The authors computed parity-conserving and parity-violating nuclear response functions for the N-N*(1440) transition using a relativistic Fermi gas model for nuclear matter, with the Roper resonance treated via a Breit-Wigner spectral function. They employed multiple empirical parameterizations (Gh1, Gh2 from Gerhardt's data) and theoretical models (non-relativistic quark model, hybrid model, light-front relativistic quark model, chiral chromodielectric model, extended vector-meson dominance model) of the N-N*(1440) transition form factors, relating these to helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}. The response functions were then convolved with the Roper spectral function and summed with the Delta contribution to study scaling behavior and the Coulomb sum rule.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used: k_F = 225 MeV/c for carbon-12 (Z=N=6)
- Roper resonance mass: m* = 1440 MeV
- At |q| = 500 MeV/c, the relevant values of the Roper invariant mass W are below 1.44 GeV, making contributions from heavier resonances plausibly small
- The Delta resonance accounts for at most 5% of the Coulomb sum rule; the Roper contribution ranges from zero (Hybrid model) to almost 20% at |q| = 1 GeV/c (non-relativistic quark model)
- The factor in front of A_{1/2}^2(0) in the longitudinal light-cone limit is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the factor in front of S_{1/2}^2(0), due to the smallness of xi_F = 0.03
- Systematic error on Gerhardt's helicity amplitude analysis is estimated at no smaller than ±12 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear response functions in the quasielastic and Delta resonance regions (Amaro 1999, Donnelly 1992) into the second resonance region, specifically targeting the Roper resonance. The authors build on the relativistic Fermi gas formalism developed by Alberico (1988) and use the same approach as Amaro (1999) for the Delta transition. The paper enables future experimental studies at facilities like JLab to extract N-N*(1440) transition information from nuclear data, particularly at low momentum transfers where the longitudinal response shows sensitivity to the S_{1/2} helicity amplitude, and provides a baseline for incorporating heavier resonances and more sophisticated nuclear models.
