# Two-neutron transfer in Sn isotopes beyond the N = 82 shell closure

> We have performed microscopic distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of differential cross sections for the two reactions 136Sn(p,t)134Sn and 134Sn(t,p)136Sn, which are within reach of near-future experiments with radioactive ion beams. We have described the initial and final nuclear states in terms of the shell model, employing a realistic low-momentum two-body effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential that has already proved quite successful in describing the available low-energy energy spectrum of 134Sn. We discuss the main features of the predicted cross sections for the population of the low-lying yrast states in the two nuclei considered.

## Key claim
Microscopic DWBA calculations predict that for the (p,t) reaction on 136Sn, the first excited 0+ state is populated with a cross section four orders of magnitude smaller than the ground state, while for the (t,p) reaction on 134Sn the excited 0+ state cross section is within one order of magnitude of the ground state. This strong asymmetry contradicts the HFB+QRPA result of Pllumbi (2011) for the pick-up reaction, where the ratio was no greater than 7, but agrees with the findings of Shimoyama (2011).

## Method
The authors performed microscopic distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations using the TWOFNR code, with shell-model wave functions for 134Sn and 136Sn obtained from a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the Vlow-k renormalization approach. The valence neutrons occupy the six orbitals of the 82-126 shell (0h9/2, 1f7/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, 0i13/2) above a 132Sn closed core, with single-neutron energies taken from the experimental spectrum of 133Sn. The calculations used a proton-dineutron approximation with optical potentials from global fits, normalized to reproduce the 124Sn(p,t)122Sn ground-state transition data at 25 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The 2+ state in 136Sn was recently identified at 682 keV excitation energy, slightly lower than the 726 keV 2+ state in 134Sn, making it the lowest first excited 2+ level observed in a semi-magic even-even nucleus.
- The largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental excitation energies is about 100 keV for the 6+ state in 134Sn.
- The ratio of reaction amplitudes f(2p3/2,2p3/2;L=0;θ) / f(1f7/2,1f7/2;L=0;θ) is approximately 1.6.
- The 2p3/2 - 1f7/2 single-particle spacing is about 0.9 MeV; the two J=0 diagonal matrix elements of the (1f7/2)2 and (2p3/2)2 configurations are both about 0.7 MeV, and the non-diagonal matrix element between them is about 0.3 MeV.
- The spectroscopic amplitude for (1f7/2)2 transfer in the 136Sn ground state to 134Sn ground state transition is 1.126; for (2p3/2)2 transfer it is 0.336.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic shell-model studies of stable Sn isotopes via (p,t) reactions (Guazzoni 2006, 2012, 2004, 2011, 2008, 1999) to the neutron-rich region beyond N=82, where only limited spectroscopic information exists for 134Sn and 136Sn. The calculations predict cross sections for reactions within reach of second-generation radioactive ion beam facilities, and the results contrast with the HFB+QRPA approach of Pllumbi (2011) while supporting the findings of Shimoyama (2011, 2013). The weak neutron pairing for N>82, previously discussed by Covello (2013) to explain the low 2+ energies, manifests here as weak configuration mixing between (1f7/2)2 and (2p3/2)2 configurations, which the authors suggest could be tested experimentally through transfer reactions.
