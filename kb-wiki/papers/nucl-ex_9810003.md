# Test of Time Reversal Invariance in Polarized Proton-Deuteron Scattering

> A novel test of time-reversal invariance in proton-deuteron scattering is planned as an internal target transmission experiment at the cooler synchrotron COSY. The P-even, T-odd observable is the polarization correlation $A_{y,xz}$ of the total cross section measured using a polarized internal proton beam (polarization $p_y$) and an internal polarized deuterium target (tensor polarization $p_{xz}$). Measuring this observable is a true null test of time reversal invariance and therefore allows to reach a high accuracy. Sufficient luminosity can be obtained using a window-less storage cell placed on the axis of the proton beam. Tensor polarized atoms are produced in an atomic beam source based on Stern-Gerlach separation in permanent sextupole magnets and adiabatic high frequency transitions. The total cross section correlation is measured by monitoring the beam transmission in the COSY storage ring mode of operation. The proton beam momentum will be in the range 2-3 GeV/c. This momentum is ideally suited to test possible short range contributions, i.e. natural parity charged $ρ$-type and unnatural parity $a_1$-type meson exchange contributions. The feasibility of the experiment, systematic errors and the expected accuracy are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel null test of time-reversal invariance (TRI) in proton-deuteron scattering by measuring the P-even, T-odd polarization correlation \(A_{y,xz}\) of the total cross section, with the aim of reaching an experimental accuracy of \(10^{-6}\), a significant improvement over previous TRI tests which were limited to \(10^{-2}\) to \(10^{-3}\) accuracy.

## Method

The experiment is designed as an internal target transmission measurement at the cooler synchrotron COSY, using a polarized proton beam (polarization \(p_y\)) and a tensor-polarized deuterium target (tensor polarization \(p_{xz}\)). The total cross section correlation is extracted by monitoring the exponential decay rate of the stored proton beam as a function of time for four combinations of the signs of \(p_y\) and \(p_{xz}\), using the generalized optical theorem. Tensor-polarized deuterium atoms are produced via Stern-Gerlach separation in permanent sextupole magnets and adiabatic high-frequency transitions, and stored in a windowless storage cell to achieve sufficient luminosity.

## Key numbers

- The proton beam momentum will be in the range 2–3 GeV/c, chosen to test short-range contributions from natural parity charged \(\rho\)-type and unnatural parity \(a_1\)-type meson exchange.
- The target areal density is about \(5 \times 10^{13}\) cm\(^{-2}\), with \(10^{11}\) polarized protons in the ring and a revolution frequency of \(1.5 \times 10^6\) s\(^{-1}\), yielding a luminosity of \(7.5 \times 10^{30}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\).
- A statistical accuracy of about \(10^{-6}\) can be reached in a 10-day run, with each single decay rate measurement taking about 100 s, allowing roughly \(10^4\) single measurements.
- Measuring \(\sigma_{\text{tot}}\) with an accuracy of \(1 \times 10^{-6}\) yields bounds on the P-even/T-odd coupling constants: \(\phi^{\text{T}}_{\rho} = g^{\text{T}}_{\rho\text{NN}}/g_{\rho\text{NN}} \le 1 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(\phi^{\text{T}}_{a_1} = g^{\text{T}}_{a_1\text{NN}}/g_{a_1\text{NN}} \le 2 \times 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier TRI tests such as detailed balance measurements in \(^{24}\)Mg(\(\alpha\),p)\(^{27}\)Al (Blann 1983) which achieved \(\Delta = 5.1 \times 10^{-3}\), and polarized neutron-Holmium forward scattering (Huffman 1996) which yielded \(\alpha_{\text{T}} \le 7 \times 10^{-4}\). The proposed null test improves on these by several orders of magnitude, and is based on the theoretical analysis by Conzett (1993) showing that \(A_{y,xz}\) is a true null test of TRI. The experiment enables tighter constraints on P-even/T-odd \(\rho\)NN and \(a_1\)NN coupling constants, complementing limits from electric dipole moment measurements (Haxby 1994) and charge symmetry breaking tests (Simonius 1997).
