# Pairing effects in nuclear pasta phase within the relativistic Thomas-Fermi formalism

> Pairing effects in non-uniform nuclear matter, surrounded by electrons, are studied in the protoneutron star early stage and in other conditions. The so-called nuclear pasta phases at subsaturation densities are solved in a Wigner-Seitz cell, within the Thomas-Fermi approximation. The solution of this problem is important for the understanding of the physics of a newly born neutron star after a supernova explosion. It is shown that the pasta phase is more stable than uniform nuclear matter on some conditions and the pairing force relevance is studied in the determination of these stable phases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including pairing effects self-consistently in the relativistic Thomas-Fermi formalism for nuclear pasta phases produces only very small changes to the density profiles, transition densities between pasta geometries, and the symmetry energy (less than 0.1 MeV difference), despite pairing gaps reaching values above 3 MeV for neutrons in some regions.

## Method

The authors solved the nuclear pasta phase problem within a Wigner-Seitz cell using the relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation with a nonlinear Walecka model Lagrangian, considering five geometries (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) and minimizing the thermodynamic grand potential. Pairing was included via a BCS-type gap equation with a separable non-local interaction fitted to reproduce the Gogny D1 force gap for symmetric nuclear matter, implemented either self-consistently (SC) by solving field and gap equations simultaneously, or non-self-consistently (NSC) by solving the gap equation after obtaining the fields. Calculations were performed for four parametrizations (NL3, FSUGold, FSU2H, FSU2R) at zero temperature for proton fractions y_p = 0.1 and y_p = 0.3.

## Key numbers

- The density range for pasta phase stability is approximately 0.1 rho_0 to 0.8 rho_0, where rho_0 is the nuclear saturation density (2.3 x 10^14 g/cm^3 or 0.16 fm^-3)
- For FSUGold with y_p = 0.1, transition densities with pairing (SC) are: droplet-rod at 0.036 fm^-3, rod-tube at 0.049 fm^-3, tube-bubble at 0.058 fm^-3, and bubble-homogeneous at 0.083 fm^-3
- The symmetry energy slope L for FSUGold changes from 60.38 MeV (without pairing) to 60.33 MeV (with pairing)
- For FSUGold with y_p = 0.1, the pasta-to-homogeneous transition density is 0.083 fm^-3 (SC), 0.079 fm^-3 (NSC), and 0.083 fm^-3 (no pairing)
- For NL3 with y_p = 0.1, the pasta-to-homogeneous transition density is 0.074 fm^-3 for all three approaches (SC, NSC, no pairing)
- The pairing gap Delta(k_F) for neutrons in the pasta phase can exceed 3 MeV for intermediate densities

## Context

This work extends previous Thomas-Fermi calculations of nuclear pasta by Avancini (2008) and others, which did not include pairing effects, and complements recent extended Thomas-Fermi calculations by Pearson (2020) and Shelley (2021) that used Skyrme-like functionals. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star crust properties such as specific heat, which is shown to be strongly affected by pairing at low temperatures, with implications for thermalization and cooling of neutron stars as studied by Fortin (2010) and Page (2011).
