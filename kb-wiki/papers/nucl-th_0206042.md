# Generic Rotation in a Collective SD Nucleon-Pair Subspace

> Low-lying collective states involving many nucleons interacting by a random ensemble of two-body interactions (TBRE) are investigated in a collective SD-pair subspace, with the collective pairs defined dynamically from the two-nucleon system. It is found that in this truncated pair subspace collective vibrations arise naturally for a general TBRE hamiltonian whereas collective rotations do not. A hamiltonian restricted to include only a few randomly generated separable terms is able to produce collective rotational behavior, as long as it includes a reasonably strong quadrupole-quadrupole component. Similar results arise in the full shell model space. These results suggest that the structure of the hamiltonian is key to producing generic collective rotation.

# Generic Rotation in a Collective SD Nucleon-Pair Subspace

## Key claim

Collective rotations do not arise generically in a collective SD-pair subspace of identical nucleons when a general two-body random ensemble (TBRE) hamiltonian is used, but they do emerge when the hamiltonian is restricted to a few separable terms including a sufficiently strong quadrupole-quadrupole component. The structure of the hamiltonian, rather than the truncation of the space alone, is the key factor for producing generic collective rotation.

## Method

The authors used the nucleon pair approximation (NPA) to study six identical nucleons in a truncated subspace built from collective S (J=0) and D (J=2) pairs, where the pairs were defined dynamically from the two-nucleon system. For each random interaction that produced a 0+ ground state and 2+ first excited state in the two-nucleon system, the same interaction was diagonalized for six identical nucleons, and the ratio R = (E(4_1+) - E(0_1+)) / (E(2_1+) - E(0_1+)) was computed; rotational behavior was identified by a peak near R = 10/3 ≈ 3.33. Calculations were performed for the sd, pf, sdg, pfh, and sdgi shells, using both general TBRE hamiltonians and restricted separable hamiltonians containing monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole terms with random strengths.

## Key numbers

- For a general TBRE hamiltonian in the sd shell within the SD subspace, the distribution of R values extends to R ~ 1.3, with no peak at R ~ 3.33.
- For the sdg shell with a general TBRE hamiltonian, the distribution shifts to R ~ 1.91, but still no sharp peak appears at R ~ 3.33.
- With a restricted hamiltonian (monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole) in the sd shell and enhanced quadrupole-quadrupole strength (factor epsilon), a peak at R ~ 3.1 gradually appears as epsilon increases.
- For six identical nucleons in the sdgi shell with the restricted hamiltonian and epsilon = 1.0, a well-pronounced peak appears at R ~ 3.3, and the ratio (E(6_1+) - E(0_1+)) / (E(2_1+) - E(0_1+)) is close to 7, the rotational limit value.
- In the full shell model space for the sd shell with monopole pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (epsilon = 10), a rotational peak at R ~ 10/3 is obtained.

## Context

This work extends the study of regularities in many-body systems with random interactions, following the discovery by Johnson and collaborators that patterns such as 0+ ground states persist under TBRE hamiltonians. It contrasts with results from Bijker et al. (2000), who found that both vibrational and rotational features arise in the interacting boson model (IBM) with random interactions, whereas here rotations do not appear in the fermionic SD-pair subspace unless the hamiltonian is restricted. The authors suggest that the IBM should be understood as a truncation arising from the dominance of quadrupole correlations rather than a simple pair truncation, and that the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction plays a key role in producing rotational behavior. The results enable further investigation of whether these conclusions hold for systems with both protons and neutrons.
