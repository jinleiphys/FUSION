# The Effect of Nuclear Rotation on the Collective Transport Coefficients

> We have examined the influence of rotation on the potential energy and the transport coefficients of the collective motion (friction and mass coefficients). For axially symmetric deformation of nucleus Th-224 we have found that at excitations corresponding to temperatures T > 1 MeV the shell correction to the liquid drop energy practically does not depend on the angular rotation. The friction and mass coefficients obtained within the linear response theory for the same nucleus at temperatures larger than T=2 MeV are rather stable with respect to rotation provided that the contributions from spurious states arising due to the violation of rotation symmetry are removed. At smaller excitations both friction and mass parameters corresponding to the elongation mode are growing functions of rotational frequency.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for the nucleus Th-224, the friction and mass coefficients for the elongation mode are rather stable with respect to rotation at temperatures above T=2 MeV, provided that the contributions from spurious states arising from the violation of rotation symmetry are removed; at lower excitations (T between 1 and 2 MeV), both coefficients increase with rotational frequency.

## Method

The authors used the linear response theory to compute the collective transport coefficients (friction and mass) for the elongation mode of the nucleus Th-224, described by a two-center shell model with axially symmetric shapes. They calculated the intrinsic response function from the single-particle spectrum of the Routhian (Hamiltonian minus omega_rot times J_x), including collisional damping via a parameterized self-energy. To remove spurious contributions from the violation of rotational symmetry, they introduced a time-dependent rotational frequency and derived a modified response function that enforces conservation of the average angular momentum.

## Key numbers

* For Th-224, the fission barrier disappears completely at angular momentum I approximately equal to 60 hbar.
* The maximal value of hbar*omega_rot is about 0.4 MeV at the saddle and about 0.6 MeV at the minimum for I=60 hbar.
* The shell correction to the free energy fluctuates by less than 1 MeV for variation of I from zero to 60 hbar at T=1 MeV.
* The collective motion at the saddle changes from underdamped to overdamped at T approximately equal to 1 MeV.
* The friction and mass coefficients are stable with respect to rotation for temperatures larger than T=2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pomorski (1993) on the rotational dependence of the friction coefficient by also examining the mass parameter and shell correction, using the linear response theory of Hofmann (1997) and Yamaji (1998). The study focuses on the compound nucleus Th-224, formed in Pb-208 + O-16 reactions, for which experimental data on the temperature dependence of the damping parameter are available from Hofmann (1992). The results enable more accurate dynamical descriptions of fusion-fission reactions, particularly for the formation of superheavy elements at low excitation energies, and the authors plan to extend the method to treat both pairing and rotation simultaneously.
