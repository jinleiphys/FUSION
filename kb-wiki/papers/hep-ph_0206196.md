# The Equation of State for Dense QCD and Quark Stars

> We calculate the equation of state for degenerate quark matter to leading order in hard-dense-loop (HDL) perturbation theory. We solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain the mass-radius relation for dense quark stars. Both the perturbative QCD and the HDL equations of state have a large variation with respect to the renormalization scale for quark chemical potential below 1 GeV which leads to large theoretical uncertainties in the quark star mass-radius relation.

## Key claim

The leading-order hard-dense-loop (HDL) perturbation theory equation of state for dense quark matter, when used to compute quark star mass-radius relations, still exhibits large theoretical uncertainties from renormalization scale dependence for chemical potentials below 1 GeV, and the authors find that the scale dependence is actually larger for HDLpt than for the weak-coupling expansion at all chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors calculated the free energy of degenerate quark matter to leading order in HDL perturbation theory at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, using dimensional regularization and the three-loop running coupling with quark threshold crossings. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations numerically to obtain the mass-radius relation for non-rotating quark stars, ignoring the nuclear phase transition and considering only pure quark stars.

## Key numbers

- The weak-coupling expansion at NLO shows the quark chemical potential at which pressure vanishes varies from 200 MeV to 650 MeV when the renormalization scale is varied by a factor of two around the central value of Λ=2μ.
- Using HDLpt with Λ/μ between 1.0 and 1.6, the maximum quark star radius R_max ranges from 3.4 km to 10.9 km, and the maximum mass M_max ranges from 0.6 M_⊙ to 2.12 M_⊙.
- Using the perturbative QCD equation of state with the same Λ/μ range, R_max ranges from 2.4 km to 5.6 km and M_max ranges from 0.42 M_⊙ to 0.95 M_⊙.
- The three-loop running coupling yields α_s(1 GeV) = 0.4586, using α_s = 0.1181 at Λ = M_Z = 91.1182 GeV with N_f = 5.

## Context

This work extends the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummation techniques developed for finite-temperature QCD by Karsch, Patkos, and Petreczky (1997) and by Andersen, Braaten, and Strickland (1999-2001) to the finite-density case of degenerate quark matter. The authors compare their HDLpt results to the weak-coupling expansion through order α_s^2 by Freedman and McLerran (1977) and Baluni (1978), finding that neither approach provides reliable predictions for the equation of state in the phenomenologically relevant range of μ between 300 MeV and 1 GeV. The results motivate the need for either a next-to-leading order HDLpt calculation or an order α_s^3 weak-coupling calculation, as well as inclusion of color-superconducting gap effects which could modify the equation of state by 1% to 10% based on gaps of 30-100 MeV.
