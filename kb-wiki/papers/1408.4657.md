# The LHC p+Pb run from the nuclear PDF perspective

> The p+Pb and Pb+Pb runs at the LHC have opened a possibility to investigate the validity of collinear factorization in a clearly higher center-of-mass energy scale than earlier in nuclear collisions. Indeed, some processes that have been measured routinely in p+p($\overline {\rm p}$) collisions and utilized for years in free proton PDF fits, can now finally be reached also in the nuclear case. Such new data are expected to provide conclusive answers concerning the universality of the nuclear PDFs. In this talk, I will contrast some of the first p+Pb and Pb+Pb measurements to the predictions based on the nuclear PDFs.

# The LHC p+Pb run from the nuclear PDF perspective

## Key claim

The first p+Pb dijet data from CMS point toward the existence of gluon antishadowing and EMC-effect in nuclear PDFs, but this conclusion depends on using the normalized (shape) distribution rather than the absolute cross section, where large NLO corrections and scale uncertainties (around 20% or more) could hide nuclear modifications.

## Method

The author compared leading-order and next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations for dijet pseudorapidity distributions, inclusive charged-hadron nuclear modification factors, and heavy gauge-boson rapidity distributions against LHC p+Pb and Pb+Pb data from CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS. Predictions using several nuclear PDF parametrizations (EPS09, HKN07, DSSZ) and the free-proton CT10NLO PDF set were contrasted with the measurements to test the validity of collinear factorization and nuclear PDF universality.

## Key numbers

- CMS dijet measurement: leading jet pT > 120 GeV, subleading jet pT > 30 GeV, at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV in p+Pb collisions
- Proton beam energy: Ep = 4 TeV; lead-ion energy: EPb = (82/208) x 4 TeV ≈ 1.58 TeV, giving a center-of-mass midrapidity shift of about half a unit in the laboratory frame
- NLO correction to the absolute dijet spectrum is almost a factor of two, with scale uncertainty around 20% or more
- RHIC record energy in nuclear collisions before LHC: sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV
- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV for Z and W production measurements
- Inclusive charged-hadron production: CMS reported a large enhancement in RpPb at high pT, while ALICE showed no such enhancement (pT reach more restricted)

## Context

This talk extends the pre-LHC nuclear PDF extractions from RHIC inclusive pion production data (pT < 10 GeV) to the much higher energy scales of LHC p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions. The dijet results support the EPS09 parametrization (Eskola 2009) over HKN07 (Hirai 2007) and DSSZ (de Florian 2011), suggesting universality of nuclear PDFs. The inconsistency between CMS and ALICE charged-hadron results highlights the need for cross-experiment verification, while the Z and W production data in Pb+Pb collisions show that collinear factorization works well and upcoming p+Pb data should provide new constraints on nuclear quark PDFs.
