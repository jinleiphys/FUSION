# Screening of a Moving Parton in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The screening potential of a parton moving through a quark-gluon plasma is calculated using the semi-classical transport theory. An anisotropic potential showing a minimum in the direction of the parton velocity is found. As consequences possible new bound states and J/psi dissociation are discussed.

# Screening of a Moving Parton in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Using semi-classical transport theory (Vlasov equation) in the high-temperature limit, the screening potential of a parton moving through a quark-gluon plasma becomes anisotropic and develops a minimum in the direction of motion, which could allow new bound states (e.g., diquarks) and modify J/ψ dissociation, but the authors caution that for realistic coupling α_s = 0.3 the weak-coupling semi-classical approach may not be reliable, and non-abelian effects are beyond the scope of this work.

## Method
The authors solved the linearized Vlasov and Poisson equations for a moving color charge in an ultrarelativistic plasma, using the relativistic longitudinal dielectric function ε_l(ω,k) from semi-classical transport theory (Eq. e3). They numerically evaluated the screening potential φ(r,t;v) (Eq. e4) for two cases: the direction parallel to the parton velocity and the direction perpendicular to it, for velocities v = 0, 0.5, and 0.8, with parameters α_s = 0.3, T = 0.25 GeV, and n_f = 2.

## Key numbers
- For v = 0.8, the minimum in the screening potential parallel to the velocity occurs at about 1.5 fm with a depth of about 8 MeV.
- The Debye mass is given by m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + n_f/6), with T = 0.25 GeV and n_f = 2 used in the numerical calculation.
- The screening potential was computed for r' = r - vt between 0 and 6 fm (parallel case) and for |r - vt| between 0.1 and 1 fm (perpendicular case, at t = 0).
- The strong fine structure constant was set to α_s = g^2/(4π) = 0.3.

## Context
This work extends the dynamic screening calculation of Chu and Matsui (who used the Vlasov equation for a heavy quark-antiquark pair traversing a QGP) by identifying a minimum in the screening potential that Chu and Matsui did not report, likely because they plotted only up to r < 1/m_D ≈ 0.35 fm. The anisotropic screening potential is analogous to that observed in complex (dusty) plasmas, where an ion flow creates an attractive potential between microparticles (Shukla; Tsytovich). The results suggest that moving J/ψ mesons may experience stronger binding than those at rest, and that new colored bound states (e.g., diquarks) might form if thermal fluctuations do not destroy them, a possibility also raised by Shuryak from lattice data. The authors note that quantum effects and collisions (Carrington) as well as non-abelian effects become important at realistic temperatures, but argue that the qualitative feature of a minimum may persist in strongly coupled plasmas.
