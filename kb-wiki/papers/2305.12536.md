# Exploring the $0^-$ bound state with dressed quarks in Minkowski space

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for a pseudoscalar bound-system, with i) a ladder kernel with massive gluons, ii) dynamically-dressed quark mass function and iii) an extended quark-gluon vertex, is solved in Minkowski space by using the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The quark dressing is implemented through a phenomenological ansatz, which was tuned by lattice QCD calculations of the quark running mass. The latter were also used for assigning the range of the gluon mass and the parameter featuring the extended color density. This framework allows to investigate the gluon dynamics that manifest itself in the quark dressing, quark-gluon vertex and the binding, directly in the physical space. We present the first results for low-density pseudoscalar systems in order to elucidate the onset of the interplay between the above mentioned gluonic phenomena, and we discuss both static and dynamical quantities, like valence longitudinal and transverse distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first Minkowski-space solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a pseudoscalar bound system that simultaneously includes a ladder kernel with massive gluons, a dynamically-dressed quark mass function tuned to lattice QCD, and an extended quark-gluon vertex; however, the results are limited to a low-density regime with system masses between three and five times the pion mass, and the quark dressing is implemented through a phenomenological ansatz rather than a self-consistent gap equation.

## Method

The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for a \(0^{-}\) quark-antiquark bound state is solved in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, combined with a light-front projection of the equation to obtain a system of integral equations for the Nakanishi weight functions. The quark propagator is dressed via a phenomenological mass function fitted to lattice QCD calculations of the quark running mass from Oliveira et al. (2018), with the propagator expressed as a sum of three poles in a Kallen-Lehmann representation. The interaction kernel uses a massive gluon propagator in Feynman gauge and a dressed quark-gluon vertex with a simple monopole form factor, and the system is solved as a generalized eigenvalue problem to obtain the coupling constant and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for each bound-state mass.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass function parameters: bare mass \(m_0 = 0.008\) GeV, infrared mass \(\mathcal{M}(0) = 0.344\) GeV, and \(\lambda = 0.9\) GeV, with \(m = 0.648\) GeV.
- Gluon mass \(\mu = 0.469\) GeV and vertex parameter \(\Lambda = 0.1\) GeV, giving a color source size of approximately \(2\) fm.
- Bound-state masses explored range from \(M = 0.653\) GeV to \(M = 0.447\) GeV, corresponding to \(3m_\pi < M < 5m_\pi\).
- For the dressed case at \(M/\mathcal{M}(0) = 1.9\): coupling constant \(\alpha = 0.61\), with valence spin components \(P_{\uparrow\downarrow} = 93\%\) and \(P_{\uparrow\uparrow} = 7\%\).
- For the dressed case at \(M/\mathcal{M}(0) = 1.3\): coupling constant \(\alpha = 1.38\), with valence spin components \(P_{\uparrow\downarrow} = 94\%\) and \(P_{\uparrow\uparrow} = 6\%\).
- In a fixed-mass comparison at the pion mass with quark mass \(0.344\) GeV, the Fock components beyond valence contribute \(51\%\) and the decay constant is \(f_\pi = 89.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter studies by de Paula et al. (2016, 2017) and Ydrefors et al. (2021, 2023) that used fixed quark masses, by incorporating a phenomenologically dressed quark propagator fitted to lattice QCD calculations from Oliveira et al. (2018). The approach enables direct investigation of gluonic phenomena (quark dressing, extended vertex, and binding) in physical Minkowski space without relying on Euclidean methods or the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction. The next step is to extend the framework to the realistic strongly-bound pion case using self-consistent solutions of the quark gap equation directly in Minkowski space, as pursued by Bicudo (2003), Sauli (2006), and others.
