# Nuclear fusion in a dense plasma

> The standard theory of nuclear fusion rates in a strongly interacting plasma can be (correctly) derived only when the energy release, Q, is large compared to other energies in the problem. We exhibit a result for rates that provides a basis for calculating the finite Q corrections. Crude estimates indicate a significant defect in the conventional results for some regions of high density and strong plasma coupling. We also lay some groundwork for a path integral calculation of the new effects.

# Nuclear fusion in a dense plasma

## Key claim

The standard formula for nuclear fusion rates in strongly coupled plasmas, which assumes the rate factorizes into a product of a vacuum cross-section and a plasma correlator, is only valid when the energy release Q is large compared to other energies in the problem. For finite Q, corrections can become significant in high-density, strongly coupled regimes, and the paper provides a framework for calculating these corrections.

## Method

The author derives an exact expression for the fusion rate from a point-coupling Hamiltonian, then expands the rate in powers of t (real time) to isolate the leading Q⁻¹ corrections. The correction terms are evaluated using the "basically classical" approximation, where plasma coordinates are replaced by an effective two-body potential V_eff(r) from Monte Carlo simulations, combined with a WKB-like treatment of quantum tunneling. A separate perturbative calculation in the plasma coupling strength is performed for the resonance production case, treating the plasma-ion coupling as a perturbation to order e³ and e⁴.

## Key numbers

- Fractional correction to the fusion rate: δw/w_s = -∂S₀/∂β · Q⁻¹ - (129/64) · Γ³/(S₀² β Q), where S₀ = [27π² β M (Ze)⁴ / 4ℏ²]¹/³ is the Gamow action and Γ = Ze²β/a is the classical plasma coupling strength.
- For ¹²C + ¹²C at T = 10⁸ K and density ρ = 10¹⁰ g cm⁻³, the fractional correction from the r² term in the effective potential is ≈ 0.4 MeV / Q.
- For ¹²C + ¹²C at T = 10⁸ K, the perturbative e⁴ correction to the resonance production rate reaches 100% for densities greater than about 10⁸ g cm⁻³.
- The Salpeter correction (weak coupling) is recovered as δw = λ₁ w⁰, where λ₁ = e² Z² κ_D with κ_D² = 4π n_I Z² β.

## Context

This work extends the formal treatment of fusion rates in dense plasmas by Brown and Sawyer (2005), who noted the Q-dependence limitation of the standard factorization formula. It contradicts the implicit assumption in the classical Monte Carlo approach of Militzer and Pollack (2000) and Ogata (1997) that the standard formula (Equation 1) is universally valid. The results are inconsequential for solar physics but relevant for denser stellar environments, such as carbon burning at high densities. The paper also shows that the Salpeter (1954) screening correction is correctly obtained from the full formalism, but that additional corrections from coupling of fusion products to the plasma, as included in works by Itoh et al. and Lamb et al., may be incorrect in the strongly coupled regime. The framework enables future path integral calculations of the full rate without the large-Q approximation.
