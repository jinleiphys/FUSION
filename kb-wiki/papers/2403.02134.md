# The utilization of magneto-hydrodynamics framework in expansion of magnetized viscous conformal flow

> The evolution of magnetized quark gluon plasma (QGP) in the framework of magneto-hydrodynamics is the focus of our study. We are investigating the temporal and spatial evolution of QGP using a second order viscous hydrodynamic framework. The fluid is considered to be magnetized and subjected to the influence of a magnetic field that is generated during the early stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions.We assume boost invariance along the beam line, which is represented by the $z$ coordinate, and fluid expansion in the $x$ direction. Additionally, we assume that the magnetic field is perpendicular to the reaction plane, which corresponds to the $y$ direction. The fluid is considered to have infinite electrical conductivity. To analyze this system, we solve the coupled Maxwell and conservation equations. By doing so, we are able to determine the time and space dependence of the energy density, velocity, and magnetic field in the transverse plane of the viscous magnetized hot plasma. Furthermore, we obtain the spectrum of hadrons and compare it with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a magnetized, viscous, conformal quark-gluon plasma expanding under boost-invariant (1+1D) Bjorken flow, viscosity significantly affects the evolution of transverse fluid velocity and local temperature, but has negligible influence on the spatial and temporal evolution of the magnetic field, at least for the specific values of magnetic susceptibility and initial magnetic field chosen.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics (RMHD) equations and the homogeneous Maxwell equation for a conformal, viscous fluid with infinite electrical conductivity and non-zero magnetization, assuming boost invariance along the beam line and expansion only in the transverse x-direction. They use the method of lines (MOL) to numerically integrate the resulting (1+1D) partial differential equations in Milne coordinates, employing Gubser's analytical conformal solution as the initial condition at proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm. The first- and second-order viscous transport coefficients (eta, tau_pi, lambda_1, lambda_2, lambda_3) are taken from two microscopic theories: Gauge/Gravity (N=4 SYM) and perturbative QCD (pQCD).

## Key numbers

*   The initial magnetic field profile is set such that B^2(tau_0,x)/epsilon(tau_0,x) is a constant chosen from the range alpha in [0.1, 0.7].
*   The magnetic susceptibility chi_m is set to 0.05.
*   The initial parameters used are q = 1/6.3 (fm)^{-1}, hat_T_0 = 5.4, f* = 15, and H_0 = 0.35 at proper time tau = 0.6 fm.
*   The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density from Gauge/Gravity is eta/s = 1/(4 pi), and the paper notes that hydrodynamic models with eta/s = 0.2 account for observed elliptic flow from RHIC experiments.
*   The magnetic field generated in relativistic heavy ion collisions is stated to be on the order of eB ~ 10^18 - 10^19 G.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) evolution by incorporating second-order viscous corrections within a magneto-hydrodynamics (MHD) framework, building on prior analytical conformal solutions by Gubser (2010). It contrasts with ideal MHD treatments and aims to quantify the interplay between viscosity and magnetization on flow and pressure anisotropy. The results enable future comparisons with experimental hadron spectra and provide a baseline for more realistic (3+1)D MHD simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
