# Lattice study on QCD-like theory with exact center symmetry

> We investigate QCD-like theory with exact center symmetry, with emphasis on the finite-temperature phase transition concerning center and chiral symmetries. On the lattice, we formulate center symmetric $SU(3)$ gauge theory with three fundamental Wilson quarks by twisting quark boundary conditions in a compact direction ($Z_3$-QCD model). We calculate the expectation value of Polyakov loop and the chiral condensate as a function of temperature on 16^3 x 4 and 20^3 x 4 lattices along the line of constant physics realizing $m_{PS}/m_{V}=0.70$. We find out the first-order center phase transition, where the hysteresis of the magnitude of Polyakov loop exists depending on thermalization processes. We show that chiral condensate decreases around the critical temperature in a similar way to that of the standard three-flavor QCD, as it has the hysteresis in the same range as that of Polyakov loop. We also show that the flavor symmetry breaking due to the twisted boundary condition gets qualitatively manifest in the high-temperature phase. These results are consistent with the predictions based on the chiral effective model in the literature. Our approach could provide novel insights to the nonperturbative connection between the center and chiral properties.

# Lattice study on QCD-like theory with exact center symmetry

## Key claim

The Z3-QCD model, which imposes flavor-dependent twisted boundary conditions on three fundamental quarks to preserve exact Z3 center symmetry, exhibits a first-order center phase transition at finite temperature, and the chiral condensate decreases rapidly around the same critical temperature with hysteresis, suggesting a strong correlation between center and chiral properties, though the simulations are performed at a heavy quark mass (m_PS/m_V = 0.70) rather than in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors formulate SU(3) lattice gauge theory with three fundamental Wilson quarks using the Iwasaki gauge action, imposing flavor-dependent twisted boundary conditions on the temporal link variables (phases 0, 2π/3, 4π/3 for u, d, s flavors respectively) to realize exact Z3 center symmetry. They perform finite-temperature simulations on 16^3 × 4 and 20^3 × 4 lattices along a line of constant physics with m_PS/m_V = 0.70, using the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm to handle the flavor-dependent determinants, and measure the Polyakov loop and flavor-diagonal subtracted chiral condensates as functions of β (the lattice gauge coupling).

## Key numbers

- The ratio m_PS/m_V = 0.70 is fixed along the line of constant physics, with hopping parameters κ tuned for each β value (e.g., κ = 0.2019 at β = 1.30, κ = 0.1539 at β = 2.20).
- For the Z3-QCD model on 16^3 × 4 lattices, the magnitude of the Polyakov loop ⟨|L|⟩ shows a clear jump from zero to nonzero values in the region 1.55 ≤ β ≤ 1.60 for cold-start configurations, while the jump occurs in 1.80 ≤ β ≤ 1.90 for hot-start configurations.
- On 20^3 × 4 lattices, the hot-start jump occurs in 1.90 ≤ β ≤ 1.95, indicating hysteresis in the range 1.55 < β < 1.90 for the Z3-QCD model.
- The Polyakov loop susceptibility χ_⟨|L|⟩ shows peaks around β = 1.60 and β = 1.90 for cold and hot starts respectively on 20^3 × 4 lattices.

## Context

This work extends the Z3-QCD model proposed by Kouno et al. (2012, 2013, 2015) from analytical studies using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to first-principles lattice numerical simulations. The model reconciles exact Z3 center symmetry with dynamical fundamental quarks, unlike quenched QCD (Kogut, 1982) which has no dynamical quarks, or adjoint QCD (Karsch, 1998) which has excessive dynamical degrees of freedom. The results enable future precise determination of the chiral crossover temperature in standard three-flavor QCD via the 2π/3 periodicity in imaginary chemical potential (Roberge and Weiss, 1986; D'Elia and Lombardo, 2002), and provide a nonperturbative testing ground for the connection between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking.
