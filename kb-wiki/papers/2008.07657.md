# Bulk observables at 5.02 TeV using quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We present comparisons between 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) predictions for a large set of bulk observables and experimental data collected in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. We make aHydroQP predictions for identified hadron spectra, identified hadron average transverse momentum, charged particle multiplicity as a function of pseudorapidity, the kaon-to-pion ($K/π$) and proton-to-pion ($p/π$) ratios, and integrated elliptic flow. We compare to data collected by the ALICE collaboration in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. We find that these bulk observables are quite well described by aHydroQP with an assumed initial central temperature of $T_0=630$ MeV at $τ_0 = 0.25$ fm/c and a constant specific shear viscosity of $η/s=0.159$ and a peak specific bulk viscosity of $ζ/s = 0.048$. In particular, we find that the momentum dependence of the kaon-to-pion ($K/π$) and proton-to-pion ($p/π$) ratios reported recently by the ALICE collaboration are extremely well described by aHydroQP in the most central collisions.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model describes a large set of bulk observables in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions quite well, with the momentum dependence of the kaon-to-pion and proton-to-pion ratios being "extremely well described" in the most central (0-5%) collisions, but only for transverse momenta up to about 2.5 GeV for K/pi and 1.5 GeV for p/pi.

## Method

The authors solved the first and second moments of the Boltzmann equation with temperature-dependent quasiparticle masses, using a relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel and an anisotropic distribution function with diagonal momentum-space anisotropy parameters. The system was evolved from an initial proper time of 0.25 fm/c until a freeze-out temperature of 130 MeV, at which point a constant energy-density hypersurface was constructed and hadronization was performed using a generalized Cooper-Frye prescription with a customized version of THERMINATOR 2. Smooth Glauber-type initial conditions were used, with only two free parameters (initial central temperature T0 and specific shear viscosity eta/s) tuned by fitting pion, kaon, and proton spectra in the 0-5% and 30-40% centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: T0 = 630 MeV at tau0 = 0.25 fm/c
- Specific shear viscosity: eta/s = 0.159 (constant)
- Peak specific bulk viscosity: zeta/s = 0.048
- Freeze-out temperature: T_FO = 130 MeV
- Initial temperature at 5.02 TeV is 5% higher than at 2.76 TeV (T0^2.76 TeV = 600 MeV)
- Good agreement for K/pi ratio extends to pT ~ 2.5 GeV in 0-5% central collisions
- Good agreement for p/pi ratio extends to pT ~ 1.5 GeV in 0-5% central collisions
- For peripheral collisions, agreement with spectra is good only for pT less than about 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior aHydroQP comparisons at 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions (Alqahtani 2017) and 200 GeV Au-Au collisions (Almaalol 2018) to the higher collision energy of 5.02 TeV. The results provide a tuned parameter set (T0 = 630 MeV, eta/s = 0.159) that has already been used to study bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow in 5.02 TeV collisions. The authors note that the smooth initial conditions used do not correctly reproduce the charged-particle elliptic flow v2 in the most central collisions, and they plan to incorporate fluctuating initial conditions and more realistic collision kernels in future work.
