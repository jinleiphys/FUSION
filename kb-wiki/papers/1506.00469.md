# Non-extensive distributions for a relativistic Fermi Gas

> Recently the non-extensive approach has been used in a variety of ways to describe dense nuclear matter. They differ in the methods of introducing the appropriate non-extensive single particle distributions inside a relativistic many-body system, in particular when one has to deal both with particles and antiparticles, as in the case of quark matter exemplified in the NJL approach. I present and discuss in detail the physical consequences of the methods used so far, which should be recognized before any physical conclusions can be reached from the results presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that different choices of non-extensive entropy functional (and corresponding constraints) lead to physically distinct fermion and anti-fermion distributions in a relativistic Fermi gas, and that only one of the three choices (the third, with a dual parameter across the Fermi surface) is fully thermodynamically consistent and can satisfy the particle-hole symmetry relation, but it introduces an energy gap at the Fermi surface that may conflict with known nuclear pairing physics.

## Method

The author derives single-particle occupation numbers for fermions and anti-fermions by extremalizing three different forms of non-extensive (Tsallis-type) entropy under constraints for total particle number and total energy, using the Jaynes maximum-entropy prescription. The three entropy choices are: (1) a single constant q for both particles and antiparticles; (2) a constant q for particles and a dual parameter \(\hat{q}=2-q\) for antiparticles; (3) a piecewise definition with q>1 above the Fermi surface and \(\hat{q}=2-q<1\) below it, plus q>1 for antiparticles. The resulting distributions are compared for their cut-off prescriptions, symmetry properties, and physical implications for dense nuclear matter models such as the Walecka model (WM) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model.

## Key numbers

* For the first entropy choice (Eq. s1) with q>1, the cut-off for particle momenta is \(\epsilon < \mu - T/(q-1)\); for q<1, the cut-off for antiparticle momenta is \(\epsilon > -\mu + T/(1-q)\), which prevents antiparticle propagation at small temperatures.
* For the second entropy choice (Eq. s2), the dual parameter \(\hat{q}=2-q\) restores the symmetry relation \(n_q(x)+\bar{n}_{\hat{q}}(-x)=1\).
* For the third entropy choice (Eq. s3), the effective occupation numbers satisfy \(n_q^{q}(x)+n_q^{q}(-x)\simeq 1\) for \(q\approx 1\) when the jump in q is smeared at the Fermi surface.
* The condition for the Tsallis cut-off for q>1 (first choice) is \(\sqrt{p^2+m^2} \leq -T/(q-1) + \mu\); for q<1 (first choice) it is \(\sqrt{p^2+m^2} \geq -T/(q-1) + \mu\).

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive (Tsallis) statistical approach to relativistic Fermi gases, comparing three entropy formulations previously used in dense nuclear matter models: the q-Walecka model (Pereira et al., 2013; SPSA; LPQ; LP) and the q-NJL model (JG). It clarifies that only the third choice (with a dual parameter across the Fermi surface) is fully thermodynamically consistent, as used by AD1, but notes that the resulting energy gap at the Fermi surface conflicts with the known temperature-dependent pairing gap in nuclear physics (kowal). The analysis enables future comparisons with experimental nucleon momentum distributions from short-range correlations (wein) to constrain the non-extensive parameter q.
