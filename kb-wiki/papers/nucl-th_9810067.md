# Non-axial Octupole Deformations of N=Z Nuclei in $A \sim 60-80$ Mass Region

> By performing a fully three dimensional Hartree-Fock calculation with use of the Skyrm forces, we demonstrate possibility of exotic deformations violating both the reflection and the axial symmetries of N=Z nuclei in $A \sim 60-80$ mass region. The \Ytwo tetrahedral shape predicted in excited \Zr arises from a shell gap at $N,Z = 40$ which is enhanced for the tetrahedron deformation. Softness toward the \Ythree triangular deformation of the oblate state in \Se is also predicted.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-axial octupole deformations (violating both reflection and axial symmetries) are possible in N=Z nuclei in the A~60-80 mass region, but the tetrahedral shape in ⁸⁰Zr is a shape-coexisting excited state rather than the ground state, and the triangular deformation in ⁶⁸Se is characterized as a soft vibrational mode rather than a rigid deformation.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme force (primarily SIII parameter set, with SkM* for comparison) on a Cartesian mesh with 1 fm spacing and a spherical box of radius 13 fm, without imposing any symmetry restrictions on the nuclear shape. Pairing correlations were treated with the BCS method using a seniority force, and the imaginary time method was used for iteration. Constrained Hartree-Fock calculations were performed to map potential energy curves as functions of individual octupole deformation components α₃ₘ (m=0,1,2,3) while fixing quadrupole deformations.

## Key numbers

- ⁸⁰Zr: tetrahedral local minimum at excitation energy 0.96 MeV above the prolate ground state, with β₃₂ = 0.23 and β=0 (spherical quadrupole shape)
- ⁸⁰Zr: energy gain from tetrahedral deformation is 0.70 MeV (difference between minimum at α₃₂=0.23 and α₃₂=0)
- ⁶⁸Se: ground state has oblate quadrupole deformation (β=0.28, γ=60°) with triangular Y₃₃ deformation β₃₃=0.14
- ⁶⁸Se: energy gain from Y₃₃ deformation is only 0.15 MeV, indicating softness rather than rigidity
- ⁷⁶Sr: excited state at 2.72 MeV with β₃=0.13 and β₃₂=0.12
- Mesh parameters: radius R=13 fm, mesh width 1 fm
- Pairing force strengths: Gₚ=16.5/(11+Z) MeV, Gₙ=16.5/(11+N) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of non-axial octupole deformations in actinides (Luo and Deng 1994) and superdeformed nuclei (Li, Chasman) to the N=Z A~60-80 region, where previous work by Takami (1998) and Skalski had suggested such possibilities. The tetrahedral shape in ⁸⁰Zr parallels predictions for metal clusters (Hamamoto, Reimann, Kole) where a shell gap at electron number 40 arises from tetrahedral symmetry, and the authors show this gap persists despite the nuclear spin-orbit potential. The results enable experimental searches for negative-parity rotational bands and enhanced E3 transitions in neutron-deficient nuclei near the proton drip line, with candidate states already reported in ⁶⁴Ge (Ennis) and ⁶⁸Se (Skoda).
