# Direct Urca neutrino radiation from superfluid baryonic matter

> Dense matter in compact stars cools efficiently by neutrino emission via the direct Urca processes n -> p + e + nu and p + e -> n + nu. Below the pairing phase transition temperature Tc these processes are suppressed - at asymptotically low temperatures exponentially. We compute the emissivity of the Urca process at one loop for temperatures T < Tc, in the case where the baryons are paired in the 1S0 partial wave. The Urca process is suppressed linearly, rather than exponentially, within the temperature range 0.1 < T/Tc < 1. The charge-current Cooper pair-breaking process contributes up to 50% of the total Urca emissivity in this temperature range.

## Key claim

The direct Urca neutrino emissivity in superfluid baryonic matter is suppressed linearly, rather than exponentially, in temperature within the range 0.1 ≤ T/Tc ≤ 1, and the charge-current Cooper pair-breaking process contributes up to 50% of the total Urca emissivity in this temperature range.

## Method

The author computed the neutrino emissivity of the direct Urca process at one-loop order using real-time Green's functions and the Matsubara formalism for baryons paired in the 1S0 partial wave. The polarization tensor of matter was evaluated for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, separating the contributions from quasiparticle scattering and pair-breaking processes, and the resulting emissivity was integrated over phase space with the approximation that the neutrino momentum is much smaller than the electron Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- The emissivity can be approximated as ε_ν = 90 ε_0 (T/Tc − 0.1) for T/Tc in the range [0.1, 1], where ε_0 is the normal-state emissivity.
- At T/Tc ∼ 0.3, the pair-breaking process contributes about half of the scattering contribution to the total emissivity.
- The model calculation assumed Δ_n(T=0) = Δ_p(T=0) = 0.5 MeV, with a density of 0.24 fm⁻³, proton Fermi momentum p_Fp = 1.07 fm⁻¹, and neutron Fermi momentum p_Fn = 1.8 fm⁻¹.
- The electron chemical potential was μ_e = 212.1 MeV, with neutron and proton effective chemical potentials μ_n* = 96.3 MeV and μ_p* = 34.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of direct Urca suppression by pair correlations beyond the exponential suppression factor used in earlier work by Yakovlev and collaborators, who treated only the scattering contribution. The results are relevant for numerical simulations of compact star cooling, where both the charge-neutral current pair-breaking processes (studied by Flowers, Voskresensky, and Kaminker) and the direct Urca process below Tc have been found important (Schaab, Page, Tsuruta, and others). The paper enables more accurate cooling simulations of neutron stars by providing a linear suppression factor that replaces the commonly assumed exponential factor exp(−Δ/T).
