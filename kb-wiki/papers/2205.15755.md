# Propagation of spin channel waves

> A mutilated model is constructed to approximate the collision term of spin Boltzmann equation that incorporates newly appearing collisional invariants i.e, the total angular momentum. With recourse to degenerate perturbation theory, the dispersion relations of hydrodynamic modes are formulated, among which spin modes are responsible for spin equilibration. We find that the non-locality does not change the sound speed but slows down the propagation of spin channel waves. The damping rates of spin modes are close to those of spinless modes over a reasonable parameter value range. The results reveal that both spin and momentum should be treated simultaneously in a unified transport framework. In the nonrelativistic limit, the short-wavelength behavior for normal modes is also explored and there exists a critical point for every distinct discrete mode over which only quasiparticle modes contribute.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a mutilated collision operator to approximate the spin Boltzmann equation and finds that, while non-locality in collisions does not change the sound speed, it slows down the propagation of spin channel waves; the damping rates of spin modes are close to those of spinless modes over a reasonable parameter value range, indicating that spin and momentum should be treated simultaneously in a unified transport framework.

## Method

The authors constructed a mutilated collision operator for the spin Boltzmann equation by collapsing all positive eigenvalues of the linearized collision operator into a single representative eigenvalue, while preserving the eleven zero modes corresponding to the collision invariants (1, p^mu, and total angular momentum J^{munu}). Using degenerate perturbation theory analogous to quantum mechanics, they treated the spatial gradient term as a perturbation and derived the dispersion relations for all discrete normal modes up to second order in wavenumber. In the nonrelativistic limit, they solved the dispersion relations analytically using the plasma dispersion function Z(bar{c}) and applied the residue theorem to determine the existence of discrete modes.

## Key numbers

- The propagating speed of spin channel waves c_spin is smaller than the sound speed c_s for all values of reduced mass z = beta m; both speeds are monotonically decreasing functions of z.
- The critical wavenumber kappa_o for longitudinal spin mode (6th mode) is 1.772 n sigma gamma; for longitudinal spin mode (11th mode) is 1.762 +/- 0.040 n sigma gamma; for transverse spin modes is 1.754 +/- 0.079 n sigma gamma (with z=10 and maximum discrepancy from z ranging 5 to 20).
- For comparison, the critical wavenumbers for sound modes, shear modes, and heat mode are 1.853, 1.772, and 1.918 n sigma gamma respectively (from deboer).
- The damping coefficients Gamma_7 for the four propagating transverse spin modes are degenerate and depend on the energy-dependence parameter lambda; for lambda=0 (Anderson-Witting RTA) and lambda=0.5 (QCD-like), the damping rates of spin modes are close to those of spinless modes over a wide range of z.

## Context

This work extends the linear mode analysis of spin hydrodynamics and spin kinetic theory, building on the spin Boltzmann equation with non-local collision effects developed by Weickgenannt (2021). It provides a direct comparison with the results of Ambrus (2022) for the case of spin angular momentum conservation (non-locality turned off), and contrasts with the approaches of Hattori (2019) and Hongo (2021) which use first-order hydrodynamics and find no propagating spin degrees of freedom. The results enable a unified transport framework for spin and momentum and identify the critical wavenumber beyond which only quasiparticle modes contribute, analogous to the transition from collision-dominated to Knudsen regimes in ordinary fluids.
