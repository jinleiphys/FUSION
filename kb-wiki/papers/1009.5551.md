# Analytical properties of the $ππ$ scattering amplitude and the light scalar mesons

> The $ππ$ scattering amplitude with regular analytical properties in the $s$ complex plane has been constructed. It describes simultaneously the data on the $ππ$ scattering, $ϕ\toπ^0π^0γ$ decay and $ππ\to K\bar K$ reaction. The chiral shielding of the $σ(600)$ meson and it's mixing with the $f_0(980)$ meson are taken into account also. The data agrees with the four-quark nature of the $σ(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons. The amplitude in the range $-5 m_π^2 < s < 0.64$ GeV$^2$ also agrees with results, obtained on the base of the chiral expansion, dispersion relations and the Roy equations.

## Key claim

The authors construct a ππ scattering amplitude with correct analytical properties in the complex s-plane that simultaneously describes ππ scattering data, φ→π⁰π⁰γ decay, and the ππ→KK̅ reaction, finding a σ meson pole at 617 − i318 MeV rather than the 441 − i272 MeV obtained from Roy equations, which they argue may indicate the importance of higher channels.

## Method

The S-wave ππ scattering amplitude with isospin I=0 is built as a product of a background term and a resonance term, where the background phase is constructed using dispersion integrals with carefully chosen kernel functions to enforce correct analytic properties (two cuts, Adler zero, no poles on the physical sheet, resonance poles on the second sheet). The inverse propagators for the σ(600) and f₀(980) resonances include contributions from π⁺π⁻, π⁰π⁰, K⁺K⁻, K⁰K̅⁰, ηη, ηη′, and η′η′ channels, with the chiral shielding of σ(600) and σ(600)−f₀(980) mixing taken into account.

## Key numbers

- σ meson pole: M_σ = 617 − i318 MeV (from the full amplitude with all channels)
- f₀(980) pole: M_f₀ = 1188 − i780 MeV (from the full amplitude)
- f₀(980) mass: m_f₀ = 986 MeV; width Γ(f₀→ππ, m_f₀) = 85 MeV
- Without f₀ coupling to ηη, ηη′, η′η′ and small σ coupling to these channels: M_σ = 639 − i313 MeV, M_f₀ = 984 − i423 MeV
- Neglecting σ coupling to KK̅ in that reduced fit: M_σ = 562 − i233 MeV
- The amplitude agrees with chiral expansion, dispersion relations, and Roy equations in the range −5 m_π² < s < 0.64 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous description of φ→π⁰π⁰γ decay, ππ scattering, and ππ→KK̅ (Achasov and Kiselev, 2004) by constructing an amplitude with correct analytical properties in the complex plane, enabling comparison with the Roy-equation analysis of Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006) which found a σ pole at 441 − i272 MeV. The authors find a different σ pole position and argue this discrepancy indicates the importance of multi-channel effects beyond the one-channel Roy equation approach; they plan to apply the amplitude to heavy quarkonium decays and pion polarization studies.
