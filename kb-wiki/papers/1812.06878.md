# Finding the Scatterers in Hot Quark Soup

> We present a brief report on a thought experiment in which an incident energetic parton traverses a brick of quark-gluon plasma (QGP), see arXiv:1808.03250 for the full report. We calculate the probability of detecting a parton showing up at a large angle with respect to its initial direction due to scattering with the constituents of QGP, using leading order perturbative QCD. We include all relevant channels, including the Rutherford-like channel as considered in early works, and those that are not Rutherford-like but become important at a large angle. The resulting probability distributions contain information about the short distance structure of QGP. Our results provide key theoretical input toward finding the scatterers within the QGP liquid, which in turn is the necessary first step toward using precise, high-statistics, suitably differential measurements of jet modification in heavy ion collisions to study the evolution of the properties of QGP with changing resolution scale.

## Key claim
The probability distribution for an energetic parton scattering at large angles in a brick of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be calculated using leading-order perturbative QCD, but this calculation is only an input to future phenomenology, not a quantitative prediction; deviations between predictions and data at varying angles will contain information about how QGP resolved at varying scales deviates from a cloud of partons.

## Method
The authors set up a thought experiment in which an incident gluon or quark with energy p_in traverses a static brick of QGP of thickness L at constant temperature T, modeled as a cloud of noninteracting massless quarks and gluons. They performed a standard leading-order perturbative QCD calculation for the phase space probability distribution F(p, theta) of detecting an outgoing parton with energy p at angle theta, including all relevant 2-to-2 binary scattering channels (both Rutherford-like and non-Rutherford-like processes). They then integrated F(p, theta) over p to obtain the angle distribution P(theta) and the number of hard partons N_hard(theta_min) scattered beyond a minimum angle.

## Key numbers
- For an incident gluon with p_in/T = 100, the probability distribution F(p, theta) features a peak at p approximately equal to p_in for theta = 0.1, but decreases monotonically with increasing p for theta = 0.4 and theta = 0.8.
- Using phenomenologically motivated inputs (T = 0.4 GeV, CT = 3 fm, g_s = 1.5), for an incident gluon with p_in = 40 GeV, single scattering starts dominating over multiple scatterings at theta approximately 0.2.
- For an incident gluon with p_in = 40 GeV, the probability of seeing partons with energies greater than 8 GeV at angles theta > 0.5 is 1/100, and at angles theta > 0.8 is 1/1000.

## Context
This work extends early studies of Rutherford-like scattering in QGP (D'Eramo 2012, Kurkela 2014) by including all 2-to-2 scattering channels, which become important at large angles where outgoing quarks from the medium (the "kickee") can be as probable as outgoing gluons of the same species as the incident parton. The results provide theoretical input for future phenomenological simulations that will replace the single incident parton with a full jet shower and the static brick with an expanding cooling droplet of QGP, enabling analysis of jet substructure observables in high-statistics data from sPHENIX at RHIC and higher luminosity running at the LHC.
