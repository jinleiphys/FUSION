# Allowed and unique first-forbidden stellar electron emission rates of neutron-rich copper isotopes

> The allowed charge-changing transitions are the most common weak interaction processes of spin-isospin form that play a crucial role in several nuclear/astrophysical processes. The first-forbidden (FF) transition becomes important, in the circumstances where allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions are unfavored, specifically for neutron-rich nuclei due to phase space considerations. In this paper deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model is applied, for the first time, for the estimation of allowed GT and unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions ($|Δ$J$|$ = 2) of neutron rich copper isotopes in mass range 72 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 82 under stellar conditions. We compared our computed terrestrial $β$-decay half-life values with previous calculations and experimental results. It was concluded that the pn-QRPA calculation is in good accordance with measured data. Our study suggests that the addition of rank (0 and 1) operators in FF transitions can further improve the comparison which remain unattended at this stage. The deformed pn-QRPA model was employed for the estimation of GT and U1F stellar electron emission ($β$$^{-}$-decay) rates over wide range of stellar temperature (0.01 GK -- 30 GK) and density (10 -- 10$^{11}$ g/cm$^{3}$) domains for astrophysical applications. Our study shows that, in high density and low temperature regions, the contribution of U1F rates to total electron emission rates of neutron-rich copper nuclei is negligible.

# Allowed and unique first-forbidden stellar electron emission rates of neutron-rich copper isotopes

## Key claim

The deformed pn-QRPA model, applied for the first time to compute both allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions for neutron-rich copper isotopes (A = 72–82), shows good agreement with measured terrestrial half-lives, but the authors note that inclusion of rank 0 and rank 1 non-unique first-forbidden operators would further improve the comparison and remains unattended. The U1F contribution to total stellar electron emission rates is negligible at high density and low temperature, but becomes significant for heavier isotopes (A ≥ 79), consistent with Borzov (2005).

## Method

The deformed proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model was used with a Nilsson potential oscillator and BCS pairing to compute Gamow-Teller (GT) and unique first-forbidden (U1F, |ΔJ| = 2) transition strengths and stellar electron emission (β⁻-decay) rates. The calculation employed a 7ħω oscillator space, avoided the Brink-Axel hypothesis by performing state-by-state evaluation from parent excited states, and incorporated measured low-lying energy levels from XUNDL where available. Stellar rates were computed over temperature 0.01–30 GK and density 10–10¹¹ g/cm³ using Fermi-Dirac electron distributions and Boltzmann thermal population of parent states, with summation over 150 parent and 150 daughter excited states.

## Key numbers

- Terrestrial half-lives computed for ⁷²Cu through ⁸²Cu; the pn-QRPA (GT + U1F) half-life for ⁷⁹Cu is in better agreement with the measured value from Audi (2012) than the large-scale shell-model calculation of Zhi (2013), which overestimates it.
- Quenching factor of 0.6 was used for both allowed GT and U1F transitions, consistent with Vetterli (1989) and Ronnqvist (1993) for ⁵⁴Fe.
- Interaction constants: χ = 61.20/A (MeV) for both GT and U1F; κ = 4.85/A (MeV) for GT and 10.92/A (MeV fm⁻²) for U1F.
- Stellar electron emission rates computed at densities ρYₑ = 10, 10³, 10⁷, and 10¹¹ g/cm³ and temperatures T₉ = 0.01–30 (where T₉ is temperature in units of 10⁹ K).
- For ⁸⁰–⁸²Cu, U1F contribution to total electron emission rates is substantial; for A ≤ 78 the U1F contribution is minor, consistent with Borzov (2005) who found FF contribution small for A ≤ 78 but substantial for A ≥ 79.
- At high density (ρYₑ = 10¹¹ g/cm³) and low temperature (T₉ < 15), almost all contribution to total electron emission rates comes from GT transitions.

## Context

This work extends the deformed pn-QRPA calculations of Homma (1996) for unique first-forbidden transitions to stellar temperatures and densities, and complements the spherical DF3 + CQRPA calculations of Borzov (2005) by including nuclear deformation, which Hosmer (2010) showed can considerably affect β-decay half-lives. The results provide microscopic stellar electron emission rates for neutron-rich copper isotopes (⁷²Cu–⁸²Cu) that are relevant for presupernova evolution and r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly for the isotopes ⁷²Cu, ⁷⁴Cu, and ⁷⁶Cu which Aufderheide (1994) identified as significantly altering the electron-to-baryon ratio in massive star cores. The authors note that non-unique first-forbidden transitions (rank 0 and rank 1) remain to be calculated, and that high-temperature corrections discussed by Rauscher (2003) were not applied but could further improve the rates.
