# Numerical Approximation to the Thermodynamic Integrals

> We approximate boson thermodynamic integrals as polynomials in two variables chosen to give the correct limiting expansions and to smoothly interpolate into other regimes. With 10 free parameters, an accuracy of better than 0.009\% is achieved for the pressure, internal energy density and the number density. We also revisit the fermion case, originally addressed by Eggleton, Faulkner and Flannery (1973), and substantially improve the accuracy of their fits.

# Numerical Approximation to the Thermodynamic Integrals

## Key claim

The authors present polynomial approximations to boson thermodynamic integrals that achieve better than 0.009% accuracy for pressure, internal energy density, and number density using 10 free parameters, while also improving upon the fermion fits of Eggleton, Faulkner and Flannery (1973) by introducing a single additional free parameter that allows the approximation to merge exactly into the correct asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors approximate the thermodynamic integrals for pressure, internal energy density, and number density of relativistic bosons and fermions using polynomials in two variables chosen to reproduce the correct analytic expansions in the four limiting cases (non-degenerate non-relativistic, non-degenerate extremely relativistic, extremely degenerate non-relativistic, and extremely degenerate extremely relativistic). For bosons, the polynomial uses variables h (a function of the degeneracy parameter psi) and t (temperature over rest mass), while for fermions the variables are f and g. The polynomial coefficients are determined by least-squares fitting to numerically integrated "exact" results over a grid of temperature and chemical potential values, with the parameter a left free to optimize the fit.

## Key numbers

- For bosons with M=3, N=4 (15+1 parameters): maximum modulus deviation in pressure is 4.9e-5 (0.0049%) and RMS deviation is 1.7e-5 (0.0017%)
- For bosons with M=3, N=4 including constraints from limiting cases (9+1 parameters): maximum modulus deviation in pressure is 7.8e-5 (0.0078%) and RMS deviation is 2.4e-5 (0.0024%)
- For fermions with M=N=3 (10+1 parameters, a fitted): maximum modulus deviation in pressure is 7.1e-5 (0.0071%) and RMS deviation is 2.6e-5 (0.0026%)
- For fermions with M=N=2 (6+1 parameters): RMS deviation in pressure is 3.4e-4 (0.034%)
- For bosons with M=2, N=1 (4+1 parameters): maximum modulus deviation in pressure is 2.1e-2 (2.1%)
- For bosons with M=N=2 (6+1 parameters): maximum modulus deviation in pressure is 2.7e-3 (0.27%)

## Context

This work extends the polynomial approximation method of Eggleton, Faulkner and Flannery (1973) for fermion thermodynamic integrals to the boson case, which had not been treated previously. The authors improve upon the fermion parameterization by introducing a free parameter a in the differential equation for the variable f, yielding a factor of 8 improvement in accuracy for the M=N=3 case compared to fixing a=1 as in the original EFF approach. The results enable efficient computation of thermodynamic quantities for both fermions and bosons across the full range of temperature and chemical potential, with applications to astrophysical problems involving particles and antiparticles in thermal equilibrium, though the authors note that the EFF scheme cannot be directly applied to pair-equilibrium systems and must treat particles and antiparticles separately.
