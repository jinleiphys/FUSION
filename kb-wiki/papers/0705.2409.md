# Charge Density of the Neutron

> A model-independent analysis of the infinite-momentum-frame charge density of partons in the transverse plane is presented for the nucleon. We find that the neutron parton charge density is negative at the center, so that the square of the transverse charge radius is positive, in contrast with many expectations. Additionally, the proton's central u quark charge density is larger than that of the d quark by about 70 %. The proton (neutron) charge density has a long range positively (negatively) charged component.

## Key claim

The neutron's infinite-momentum-frame parton charge density is negative at the center, contradicting long-standing expectations from both gluon-exchange and meson-cloud models that the central charge density should be positive. The analysis is model-independent but about 30% of the value of ρ(0) arises from the unmeasured Q² region where 2 < τ < 4.

## Method

The transverse charge density ρ(b) is obtained as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor F₁(Q²), using existing parameterizations of the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M to compute F₁ via the relation F₁ = (G_E + τ G_M)/(1 + τ) with τ = Q²/(4M²). The analysis uses measured form factor data from the parameterizations of Kelly (2004) and Bradford et al. (2006) to evaluate the integral ρ(b) = ∫₀^∞ (dQ Q / 2π) J₀(Qb) [G_E(Q²) + τ G_M(Q²)]/(1 + τ).

## Key numbers

- The neutron's central charge density ρ(0) is negative; the square of the transverse charge radius (obtained from ∫ d²b b² ρ(b)) is +0.012 fm², small and positive.
- The experimental mean-square radius from G_E is (-0.114 ± 0.003) fm², while the Foldy term contributes -0.126 fm².
- The proton's central u quark charge density is larger than that of the d quark by about 70%.
- About 30% of the value of ρ(0) for the neutron arises from the unmeasured region 2 < τ < 4.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Soper (1977) and the recent GPD-based impact parameter space analyses of Burkardt (2000) and Diehl (2002) to a phenomenological determination of transverse charge densities from existing form factor data. The neutron result contradicts the positive central density predicted by the gluon-exchange model of Isgur and Karl and the meson-cloud model of Carlson and Brown. Future experimental measurements of neutron electromagnetic form factors could render the results more precise or modify them considerably, and the findings present a challenge to lattice QCD and phenomenological model builders.
