# On the different role of protons and neutrons in antinucleon annihilations on nuclei

> We compare data of antineutron and antiproton annihilation cross sections on different targets at very low energies. After subtracting Coulomb effects, we observe that the ratio between the antineutron proton and antiproton proton annihilation cross sections is an oscillating function of the energy at momenta smaller 300 MeV/c. This nontrivial behavior is confirmed by the analysis of the relative number of antiproton-neutron and antiproton-proton annihilations in nuclei. We show that a part of the strong shadowing phenomena in antiproton-nucleus annihilations can be explained in terms of this oscillation, while a part requires different explainations.

# On the different role of protons and neutrons in antinucleon annihilations on nuclei

## Key claim

After subtracting Coulomb effects, the ratio between antineutron-proton and antiproton-proton annihilation cross sections is an oscillating function of energy at laboratory momenta below 300 MeV/c, and this nontrivial behavior is confirmed by the relative number of antiproton-neutron and antiproton-proton annihilations in nuclei; however, the authors caution that antineutron flux normalization is delicate and that comparisons between different experiments must be taken with caution.

## Method

The authors compared existing experimental data on antineutron and antiproton total annihilation cross sections on hydrogen and various nuclear targets (from carbon to lead) at low momenta, primarily from the Obelix Collaboration. They subtracted Coulomb enhancement effects using analytical correction factors derived from optical potential calculations, then performed phenomenological analyses using Woods-Saxon optical potentials and Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) calculations to correlate antinucleon-nucleon annihilation with antinucleon-nucleus data.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb enhancement factor for antiproton-proton annihilation is reproduced within 5% in the range 30-400 MeV/c by the function 1 + 0.0003 β⁻².
- For antiproton-proton annihilation, the optical potential fit fixes the P-wave maximum to about 80 mb at 150 MeV/c; for antineutron-proton, the P-wave maximum is 120-140 mb at approximately 250 MeV/c.
- The ratio of P-wave cross sections at peak momentum is TNA/TPA ≈ 1.6, while purely geometrical considerations would suggest a ratio of approximately 0.4 based on impact parameters of 0.8 fm (TNA) and 1.25 fm (TPA).
- At 180 MeV/c laboratory momentum, the carbon and lead antineutron-nucleus total annihilation cross sections divided by A²/³ give about 150 mb, while the corresponding antineutron-proton and Coulomb-subtracted antiproton-proton cross sections are approximately 200 mb.
- For the PWIA fits of the ratio F_A (antiproton-neutron to antiproton-proton annihilations in nuclei), the antineutron-proton annihilation rates were rescaled by energy-independent factors: α₂ = 0.8 for deuteron and α₄ = 0.6 for ⁴He.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of antinucleon-nucleon annihilation data from the Obelix Collaboration (1990s) and compares with earlier antineutron data from Mutchler et al. and antiproton-nucleus data from various experiments. The authors build on previous optical potential treatments of antiproton-nucleus shadowing (Bianconi et al., 1990s) and multiple scattering theory (Wycech et al., 1990s), showing that part of the strong shadowing in antiproton-nucleus annihilations can be explained by the oscillation in the antineutron-proton to antiproton-proton cross section ratio, while another part requires different explanations related to nuclear structure and the uncertainty principle. The results enable future higher-level potential analyses to understand the isospin dependence of antinucleon-nucleon interactions and the role of nuclear surface composition in annihilation processes.
