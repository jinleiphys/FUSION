# Electroweak Radiative Corrections to Parity-Violating Electron-Nucleus Scattering

> Parity-violating electron-scattering provides a largely model independent way of measuring neutron densities in nuclei that has important implications for the structure of nuclei and neutron stars. In this paper we calculate radiative corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry $A_{\rm pv}$ in electron-nucleus scattering including vertex and vacuum polarization contributions. We find large cancellations between the vertex corrections to the vector and axial-vector vertices. As a result the total radiative correction in 2nd Born approximation is dominated by vacuum polarization and is of order -0.5\%. Coulomb distortions have modest effects on radiative corrections for $^{12}$C and $^{48}$Ca nuclei. For $^{208}$Pb Coulomb distortions reduce the total radiative correction to only about 0.1\%. Therefore, these corrections are not important for the interpretation of the PREX and MREX experiments on $^{208}$Pb and for the CREX experiment on $^{48}$Ca. However, radiative corrections must be carefully included for a precision measurement of the weak charge of $^{12}$C.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the total radiative correction to the parity-violating asymmetry in electron-nucleus scattering is much smaller than the approximately 5% claimed by Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2025), because the earlier work neglected the axial-vector vertex correction which largely cancels the vector vertex correction. The total correction is dominated by vacuum polarization and is of order -0.5% in second Born approximation, with Coulomb distortions reducing it further for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors computed both vector and axial-vector radiative corrections to the electron arm for parity-violating electron-nucleus scattering, including vertex plus self-energy diagrams and vacuum polarization diagrams for both the photon and Z⁰ boson. They evaluated the parity-violating asymmetry in second Born approximation and then implemented the corrections in a full eikonal calculation that includes Coulomb distortions, using symmetrized Fermi function parameterizations for nuclear charge and weak densities of ²⁰⁸Pb, ⁴⁸Ca, and ¹²C.

## Key numbers

- Total radiative correction in second Born approximation for ²⁰⁸Pb at Q=0.4 fm⁻¹ and 1 GeV: -0.53%
- Total radiative correction with Coulomb distortions (eikonal) for ²⁰⁸Pb at Q=0.4 fm⁻¹ and 1 GeV: +0.06%
- Total radiative correction in second Born approximation for ⁴⁸Ca at Q=0.8733 fm⁻¹ and 2.18 GeV: -0.66%
- Total radiative correction with Coulomb distortions (eikonal) for ⁴⁸Ca at Q=0.8733 fm⁻¹ and 2.18 GeV: -0.47%
- Individual vector vertex correction v_vs for ²⁰⁸Pb in second Born: +4.19%
- Individual axial-vector vertex correction v_vs^a for ²⁰⁸Pb in second Born: -4.07%
- Individual vacuum polarization correction v_π for ²⁰⁸Pb in second Born: -0.65%
- Coulomb vertex correction for ²⁰⁸Pb increases from 4.19% (second Born) to 4.73% (eikonal)

## Context

This work directly addresses the claim by Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2025) of a large approximately 5% radiative correction to the parity-violating asymmetry, showing that their result was incomplete because they only considered corrections to the vector interaction while neglecting the weak vertex radiative correction. The results confirm that radiative corrections are not important for interpreting the PREX experiment on ²⁰⁸Pb, the CREX experiment on ⁴⁸Ca, or the future MREX experiment on ²⁰⁸Pb, but must be carefully included for the planned precision measurement of the weak charge of ¹²C, which aims for 0.3% precision. The work extends previous calculations by Milstein and Sushkov (2005) on vacuum polarization and provides the first calculations of the axial-vector vertex correction for these experimental kinematics.
