# The effect of early chemical freeze out on radial and elliptic flow from a full 3D hydrodynamic model

> We investigate the effect of early chemical freeze-out on radial and elliptic flow by using a fully three dimensional hydrodynamic model. We find that the time evolution of temperature and the thermal freeze-out temperature dependence of average radial flow are different from the results by using a conventional hydrodynamic model in which chemical equilibrium is always assumed. We also analyse the p_t spectrum and v_2(p_t) at the RHIC energy and consistently reproduce experimental data by choosing the thermal freeze-out temperature T_th = 140 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including an early chemical freeze-out (where hadron abundances are fixed at T_ch = 170 MeV) in a 3D hydrodynamic model significantly reduces radial flow and alters the temperature evolution compared to conventional models that assume full chemical equilibrium throughout. The model with partial chemical equilibrium (PCE) can simultaneously reproduce both the transverse momentum spectrum and elliptic flow v_2(p_t) from RHIC data only when the thermal freeze-out temperature is chosen as T_th = 140 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed three equations of state (EOS) for a 3D hydrodynamic model of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV: a conventional chemical equilibrium model (CE), a model with complete chemical freeze-out (CFO) where all hadron numbers are fixed below T_ch = 170 MeV, and a partial chemical equilibrium model (PCE) where only stable hadrons (pi, K, eta, N, Lambda, Sigma) have fixed abundances while resonances remain in chemical equilibrium with them. The hydrodynamic simulations used initial conditions at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c with maximum energy density 30.8 GeV/fm^3 (temperature 349.5 MeV) tuned to reproduce PHOBOS pseudorapidity distributions for central (0-6%) and semi-central (35-45%) collisions.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at centre of fluid for central collisions: 30.8 GeV/fm^3 (temperature 349.5 MeV) at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Chemical freeze-out temperature assumed: T_ch = 170 MeV
- Critical temperature for QGP-hadron phase transition: T_c = 170 MeV
- Thermal freeze-out temperature that best fits both p_t spectrum and v_2(p_t): T_th = 140 MeV
- Average radial flow at vanishing space-time rapidity: <v_r>|_eta_s=0 ~ 0.35 c
- Best fit to v_2(p_t) data achieved below 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic analyses of RHIC data (Refs. [1-5] in the paper) that assumed full chemical equilibrium, and is consistent with thermal model analyses (reviewed in the paper) suggesting chemical freeze-out occurs before thermal freeze-out. The results contradict the model II of Broniowski (2001) but are consistent with the findings of Burward-Hoy (2001). The paper enables future investigation of the "HBT puzzle" at RHIC (Heinz, 2000; STAR and PHENIX HBT data) by providing a framework where early chemical freeze-out in hydrodynamics could explain the observed HBT radii, and also motivates extending the model to finite baryon chemical potential for analysis of particle ratios and spectra at various collision energies.
