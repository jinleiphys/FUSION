# Applications of Two Body Dirac Equations to Hadron and Positronium Spectroscopy

> We review recent applications of the Two Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics to meson spectroscopy and describe new extensions to three-body problems in their use in the study of baryon spectroscopy. We outline unique aspects of these equations for QED bound states that distinguish them among the various other approaches to the relativistic two body problem. Finally we discuss recent theorectial solutions of new peculiar bound states for positronium arising from the Two Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics, assuming point particles for the electron and the positron.

## Key claim

The Two Body Dirac Equations (TBDE) of constraint dynamics provide a manifestly covariant 3D reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation that, when applied to meson spectroscopy with the Adler-Piran potential, yields good fits to the experimental meson spectrum across multiple flavor families, though some radial and orbital excitations show significant deviations.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger-like equation (SLE) derived from the TBDE for two spin-1/2 particles, using three invariant potentials (scalar S, electromagnetic-like A, and timelike vector V) obtained by dividing the Adler-Piran static quark potential. For mesons, the SLE is solved numerically with four fitted parameters (quark masses, scale Lambda, and a scalar-vector mixing parameter xi). For baryons, the two-body SLE quasipotential is inserted by hand into a three-body Schrödinger-like equation based on Sazdjian's covariant three-body formalism, solved variationally.

## Context

This work extends the TBDE approach previously applied to QED bound states (tested analytically and numerically against known perturbative QED spectrum) to QCD meson spectroscopy using the Adler-Piran static quark potential (Adler and Piran, 1984). The baryon calculations extend Sazdjian's covariant three-body formalism (Sazdjian, 1989) by inserting the two-body SLE quasipotential by hand. The results enable further refinement of the potential division scheme and extension to more exotic hadron states, while the QED tests (including predictions of new peculiar positronium bound states for point particles) provide confidence in the numerical treatment of the SLE for QCD.
