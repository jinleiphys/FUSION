# The Jost function and Siegert pseudostates from R-matrix calculations at complex wavenumbers

> The single-channel Jost function is calculated with the computational R-matrix on a Lagrange-Jacobi mesh, in order to study its behaviour at complex wavenumbers. Three potentials derived from supersymmetric transformations are used to test the accuracy of the method. Each of these potentials, with s-wave or p-wave bound, resonance or virtual states, has a simple analytical expression for the Jost function, which is compared with the calculated Jost function. Siegert states and Siegert pseudostates are determined by finding the zeros of the calculated Jost function. Poles of the exact Jost function are not present in the calculated Jost function due to the truncation of the potential in the R-matrix method. Instead, Siegert pseudostates arise in the vicinity of the missing poles.

## Key claim

The single-channel Jost function can be accurately calculated using the computational R-matrix on a Lagrange-Jacobi mesh at complex wavenumbers, but poles of the exact Jost function are absent in the calculated Jost function due to potential truncation at the channel radius, and are instead replaced by Siegert pseudostates in the vicinity of the missing poles.

## Method

The authors compute the single-channel Jost function from the computational R-matrix by expanding the internal-region wave function on a shifted Lagrange-Jacobi mesh basis, using Gauss quadrature to evaluate matrix elements. Three test potentials derived from supersymmetric transformations (Eckart/Bargmann potential for s-wave bound/virtual states, a resonance potential for s-wave, and a p-wave potential) with known analytical Jost functions are used to validate the method. Siegert states and Siegert pseudostates are determined by finding zeros of the calculated Jost function, and results are compared with the Siegert method (for ℓ=0) and the iterative R-matrix method.

## Key numbers

- Potential 1 (Eckart/Bargmann, ℓ=0): Jost function given by f₀(k) = (k − iκ₁)/(k + iκ₀), with bound/virtual state at k = iκ₁ and Jost-function pole at k = −iκ₀, where κ₀ > 0 and κ₀ > κ₁.
- Potential 2 (ℓ=0 resonance): defined by parameters κ₀, κ₁, α (complex), with ζᵢ = arctanh(κᵢ/α) + arctanh(κᵢ/α*) = arctanh(2α_R κᵢ/(κᵢ² + |α|²)).
- Potential 3 (ℓ=1): defined by parameters κ₀, κ₁, κ₂ with Jost function f₁(k) = (k − iκ₁)(k − iκ₂)/(k + iκ₀)².
- Channel radius a is chosen sufficiently large for potential truncation; specific numerical values of a and basis size N are not given in the truncated text.
- Siegert pseudostates arise at complex wavenumbers with Im(k) < 0, distributed around the location of the missing exact Jost-function pole.

## Context

This work extends the computational R-matrix on a Lagrange mesh, previously used for phase shifts and cross-sections (Baye 1998, 2002, 2015; Descouvemont 2010), to direct calculation of the Jost function at complex wavenumbers. It builds on the Siegert pseudostate formalism of Tolstikhin (1997, 1998) and the Bargmann potential analysis of Baye (2002, 2014). The method enables locating Siegert states and pseudostates for any angular momentum ℓ and Coulomb parameter η, unlike the ℓ=0-only Siegert method of Tolstikhin (1997, 1998), and provides a unified framework for studying the analytic structure of the Jost function and S-matrix in nuclear scattering.
