# Directed flow, a signal for the phase transition in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions?

> The sign change of the slope of the directed flow of baryons has been predicted as a signal for a first order phase transition within fluid dynamical calculations. Recently, the directed flow of identified particles has been measured by the STAR collaboration in the beam energy scan (BES) program. In this article, we examine the collision energy dependence of directed flow $v_1$ in fluid dynamical model descriptions of heavy ion collisions for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-20$ GeV. The first step is to reproduce the existing predictions within pure fluid dynamical calculations. As a second step we investigate the influence of the order of the phase transition on the anisotropic flow within a state-of-the-art hybrid approach that describes other global observables reasonably well. We find that, in the hybrid approach, there seems to be no sensitivity of the directed flow on the equation of state and in particular on the existence of a first order phase transition. In addition, we explore more subtle sensitivities like e.g. the Cooper-Frye transition criterion and discuss how momentum conservation and the definition of the event plane affects the results. At this point, none of our calculations matches qualitatively the behavior of the STAR data, the values of the slopes are always larger than in the data.

## Key claim

In a state-of-the-art hybrid approach that combines hadronic transport with fluid dynamics, the directed flow of baryons shows no sensitivity to the equation of state or the existence of a first order phase transition, contradicting earlier pure fluid dynamical predictions that a sign change of the slope signals a first order transition. None of the calculations in this study matches the qualitative behavior of the STAR data, with the calculated slopes always larger than the experimental values.

## Method

The authors performed ideal 3+1D fluid dynamical simulations using the SHASTA algorithm with two equations of state: a Bag Model EoS with a Maxwell construction for a first order phase transition, and a chiral crossover EoS (χ-over) consistent with lattice QCD at zero baryochemical potential. They then employed the UrQMD hybrid approach, which uses hadronic transport for the initial interpenetration stage and final hadronic rescatterings, with a fluid dynamical phase in between, transitioning on an iso-energy density surface at ε = 4ε₀ ≈ 0.6 GeV/fm³. The directed flow v₁ was extracted using the Cooper-Frye prescription on both isochronous and iso-energy density hypersurfaces, and compared with STAR beam energy scan data for √sNN = 3–20 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The pure fluid dynamical calculation with the Bag Model EoS shows a negative slope of the directed flow around √sNN = 4 GeV for protons, while the crossover EoS always yields a positive slope.
- The hybrid model calculations used events with impact parameter b = 4.6–9.4 fm to approximate the (10–40)% centrality range of the STAR data.
- At √sNN = 11.5 GeV, the hybrid model overestimates the proton directed flow compared to STAR data, while charged pion v₁ agrees better with experiment.
- The proton and antiproton slopes are overestimated for the entire examined collision energy range of √sNN = 3–20 GeV.
- The pion dv₁/dy changes sign at √sNN ≈ 10–15 GeV in the hybrid model, which is not supported by the STAR data.

## Context

This work extends earlier fluid dynamical predictions by Rischke (1995) and Brachmann (1999) that a sign change of the directed flow slope signals a first order phase transition, but contradicts those findings by showing that the effect disappears in a more realistic hybrid approach. The study also compares with standard UrQMD transport results and STAR data from the beam energy scan (Adamczyk 2014). The authors identify that the definition of the event plane and the treatment of spectator-fireball momentum transfer must be addressed before conclusions about the relation between directed flow and the equation of state can be made.
