# Neutral pion photoproduction on deuterium in baryon chiral perturbation theory to order q^4

> Threshold neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron is studied in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory beyond next-to--leading order in the chiral expansion. To fourth order in small momenta, the amplitude is finite and a sum of two- and three-body interactions with no undetermined parameters. With accurate theoretical and experimental input from the single nucleon sector for the proton amplitude, we investigate the sensitivity of the threshold cross section to the elementary $γn \to π^0 n$ amplitude. A precise measurement of the threshold cross section for $γd \to π^0 d$ is called for.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the threshold cross section for neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron to fourth order in baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding that the amplitude is finite and parameter-free at this order, but the result depends sensitively on the elementary neutron amplitude \(E_{0+}^{\pi^0 n}\), which itself is a prediction of the same chiral framework rather than a direct measurement.

## Method

The authors compute the electric dipole amplitude \(E_d\) for \(\gamma d \to \pi^0 d\) at threshold using baryon chiral perturbation theory to order \(q^4\) (counting index \(\nu = 0\)). They separate the amplitude into a single-scattering (impulse approximation) contribution, which uses the \({\cal O}(q^4)\) chiral perturbation theory predictions for the elementary \(\pi^0 p\) and \(\pi^0 n\) S- and P-wave amplitudes, and three-body (meson exchange current) contributions at orders \(q^3\) and \(q^4\). The three-body contributions are evaluated with phenomenological deuteron wave functions from the Argonne V18, Reid soft core, Nijmegen, Paris, super soft core, and Bonn potentials, and the \(q^4\) three-body terms require a Gaussian cutoff \(\Lambda_\pi\) to regularize divergences.

## Key numbers

*   Threshold photon momentum for \(\gamma d \to \pi^0 d\): \(k_{\rm thr} = 130.1\ {\rm MeV}\).
*   Elementary S-wave amplitudes from \({\cal O}(q^4)\) chiral perturbation theory: \(E_{0+}^{\pi^0 p} = -1.16 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\), \(E_{0+}^{\pi^0 n} = +2.13 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).
*   Single-scattering contribution to the deuteron electric dipole amplitude: \(E_d^{ss} = 0.36 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\), with an uncertainty of \(\delta E_d^{ss} = 0.05 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) from the choice of potential.
*   Three-body contribution at order \(q^3\): \(E_d^{tb,3} = (-1.90,\ -1.88,\ -1.85,\ -1.88) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) for the Argonne V18, RSC, SSC, and Bonn potentials, respectively.
*   Three-body contribution at order \(q^4\): \(E_d^{tb,4} = (-0.25,\ -0.23,\ -0.27) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) for the V18, Reid, and SSC potentials (with \(\Lambda_\pi = 1\ {\rm GeV}\)).
*   Total electric dipole amplitude (Argonne V18): \(E_d = (-1.8 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).
*   Empirical value extracted from Saclay data: \(E_d^{\rm exp} = (-1.7 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).
*   Sensitivity relation: \(E_d^{ss} = 0.36 - 0.38 \cdot (2.13 - E_{0+}^{\pi^0 n})\) in units of \(10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier \({\cal O}(q^3)\) calculation by Beane et al. (1995) to order \(q^4\), motivated by the fact that the lower-order single-nucleon amplitudes were too inaccurate. The improved \({\cal O}(q^4)\) chiral perturbation theory calculation of the elementary \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\) and \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\) amplitudes by Bernard et al. (1996) now allows a consistent treatment. The paper calls for a precise new measurement of the \(\gamma d \to \pi^0 d\) threshold cross section at a 100% duty cycle tagged photon facility, similar in accuracy to the MAMI and SAL experiments on the proton, to test the predicted sensitivity to the neutron amplitude and to probe isospin violation from quark mass differences.
