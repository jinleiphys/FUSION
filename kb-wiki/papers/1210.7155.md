# Self-bound quark matter in the NJL model revisited: from schematic droplets to domain-wall solitons

> The existence and the properties of self-bound quark matter in the NJL model at zero temperature are investigated in mean-field approximation, focusing on inhomogeneous structures with one-dimensional spatial modulations. It is found that the most stable homogeneous solutions which have previously been interpreted as schematic quark droplets are unstable against formation of a one-dimensional soliton-antisoliton lattice. The solitons repel each other, so that the minimal energy per quark is realized in the single-soliton limit. The properties of the solitons and their interactions are discussed in detail, and the effect of vector interactions is estimated. The results may be relevant for the dynamics of expanding quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the previously identified self-bound homogeneous quark matter droplets in the NJL model are actually unstable against forming one-dimensional soliton-antisoliton lattices, and the minimal energy per quark is realized in the single-soliton limit rather than at finite density, provided the vacuum constituent quark mass exceeds approximately 315 MeV.

## Method

The authors study the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit at zero temperature using mean-field approximation, allowing the chiral condensate to vary in one spatial dimension. They construct mass functions using Jacobi elliptic functions parameterized by an amplitude Δ and elliptic modulus ν, then minimize the thermodynamic potential to find the ground state. The density profiles and energy per particle are computed for both homogeneous and inhomogeneous solutions, and the single-soliton limit (ν → 1) is analyzed analytically.

## Key numbers

- The second-order phase transition from homogeneous chirally broken phase to inhomogeneous phase occurs at μci = 323.2 MeV for Mvac = 330 MeV
- The first-order phase transition between homogeneous phases would occur at μch = 329.9 MeV for the same parameters
- The single-soliton density profile is given by n_sol(z) = (Nf Nc / 8π) × (Mvac μci²) / cosh²(Mvac z)
- The number of quarks per transverse area in a single soliton is N/A⊥ = (Nf Nc / 4π) × μci²
- The longitudinal rms radius of a soliton is R_sol,∥^rms = π/√12 × 1/Mvac
- For Mvac = 400 MeV, the binding energy per quark for solitons is about 40 MeV, compared to about 30 MeV for homogeneous matter
- The density rise above onset follows n̄(μ) = -c μci³ / ln(μ/μci - 1) with fitted c = 0.97985

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of self-bound quark matter in the NJL model by Buballa (2005), Buballa (1996), and Alford (1997), which interpreted homogeneous solutions as schematic quark droplets. It contradicts the earlier picture by showing that these homogeneous solutions are unstable when one-dimensional spatial modulations are allowed, as previously found in studies of the NJL phase diagram by Nickel (2009) and Carignano (2010). The results enable a more realistic description of quark matter droplets that are finite in one spatial dimension, and may be relevant for understanding the dynamics of expanding quark matter in heavy-ion collisions or neutron star interiors.
