# Hybrid Stars Built with Density Dependent Models

> Using a density dependent quark model and a relativistic model within the mean-field approximation for hadrons with density dependent meson-baryon couplings, we construct, for the first time, models that describe hybrid neutron stars consisting of nucleons and exotic baryons (hyperons and $Δ$-resonances). We do the study using a Maxwell construction. The quark-hadron phase transition in the stellar matter is determined through; the structure, composition, and properties of the hybrid neutron star matter. The macroscopic properties of the star are determined, and the results for these particular models are found to be compatible with recent observational astrophysical data.

## Key claim

The authors construct, for the first time, hybrid neutron star models that include both hyperons and Δ-resonances in the hadronic phase alongside a deconfined quark phase described by a density dependent quark model, using a Maxwell construction for the phase transition. The results are found to be compatible with recent observational astrophysical data only for these particular models and parameter choices.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density dependent meson-baryon couplings (DDME2 parameterization), including nucleons, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and Δ-isobars, with coupling ratios determined from SU(3) symmetry arguments by Lopes (2022). The quark phase is described by the density dependent quark model (DDQM) with parameters C=0.965 and √D=121 MeV taken from Backes (2020), using current quark masses of 5 MeV, 10 MeV, and 80 MeV for u, d, and s quarks respectively. A first-order Maxwell construction is used to determine the hadron-quark phase transition by finding the crossing point where the pressures and chemical potentials of the two phases are equal.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses: 2.22 M⊙ (NQ, nucleons only), 2.19 M⊙ (NHQ, nucleons+hyperons), 2.15 M⊙ (NHΔQ, nucleons+hyperons+Δ-isobars)
- Radii at maximum mass: 13.03 km (NQ), 13.01 km (NHQ), 12.55 km (NHΔQ)
- Critical baryochemical potential for phase transition: 1364 MeV (NQ), 1400 MeV (NHQ), 1413 MeV (NHΔQ)
- Critical pressure at phase transition: 151.94 MeV fm⁻³ (NQ), 181.54 MeV fm⁻³ (NHQ), 185.49 MeV fm⁻³ (NHΔQ)
- Energy density at onset of phase transition (hadronic side): 562.38 MeV fm⁻³ (NQ), 670.51 MeV fm⁻³ (NHQ), 688.25 MeV fm⁻³ (NHΔQ)
- Energy density at completion of phase transition (quark side): 755.13 MeV fm⁻³ (NQ), 840.38 MeV fm⁻³ (NHQ), 898.34 MeV fm⁻³ (NHΔQ)
- Nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.152 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars by combining, for the first time, a density dependent quark model with a density dependent hadronic model that includes both hyperons and Δ-isobars. It builds on the DDME2 parameterization from the work of Typel and Wolter (2005) and the baryon-meson coupling ratios determined by Lopes (2022). The results are compared with constraints from the GW170817 merger event and NICER observations, and the authors note that the hadron-quark phase transition does not resolve the so-called "hyperon puzzle," consistent with findings by Lopes (2020). The study enables future investigations of how different choices of DDQM parameters (C and D) or different hyperon/Δ-baryon potentials would affect hybrid star properties.
