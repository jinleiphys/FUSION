# Ab initio description of $\bar{p}+\rm{^3H}$ and $\bar{p}+\rm{^3He}$ systems in optical models

> In the context of the ongoing PUMA experiment (CERN), which investigates antiproton annihilation on atomic nuclei, we study the energy shifts and widths of Rydberg states in the $\bar{p}+\rm{^3H}$ and $\bar{p}+\rm{^3He}$ systems by performing ab initio calculations. The scattering lengths and scattering volumes are first determined by solving the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space. The level shifts and widths of the corresponding $\bar{p} \, \rm{^3H}$ and $\bar{p} \, \rm{^3He}$ hydrogen-like states are then obtained using the Trueman formula. A pronounced model dependence associated with the nucleon-antinucleon interaction is observed for certain states. Finally, annihilation densities are computed from the four-body wavefunctions. Comparison with the nuclear density distributions indicates that the nucleon-antinucleon annihilation is predominantly peripheral.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first four-body *ab initio* calculations for antiproton scattering on tritium and helium-3, but the level shifts and widths show a pronounced model dependence on the nucleon-antinucleon interaction for P-wave states, while S-wave states are comparatively robust across different interaction models.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for the four-body systems, treating the nucleon-antinucleon annihilation via optical potentials. Scattering lengths and scattering volumes were computed for S-wave and P-wave states using different combinations of nucleon-nucleon potentials (MT-I-III, AV18, I-N3LO) and nucleon-antinucleon potentials (Kohno-Weise and Jülich). The level shifts and widths of hydrogen-like Rydberg states were then extracted using the Trueman formula truncated at second order.

## Key numbers
- For the $\bar{p}+^3$H system with I-N3LO+KW, the S-wave $0^+$ scattering length is $1.44-0.77i$ fm and the $1^+$ is $1.60-0.65i$ fm.
- For the $\bar{p}+^3$He system with I-N3LO+KW, the S-wave $0^+$ scattering length is $1.50-0.62i$ fm and the $1^+$ is $1.44-0.69i$ fm.
- The $1s$ level shift for $\bar{p}\,^3$H with I-N3LO+KW is $2.57-1.15i$ keV for the $0^+$ state and $2.78-0.94i$ keV for the $1^+$ state.
- The $1s$ level shift for $\bar{p}\,^3$He with I-N3LO+KW is $18.6-4.87i$ keV for the $0^+$ state and $18.4-5.68i$ keV for the $1^+$ state.
- For the $\bar{p}+^3$H $2^-$ P-wave state, the scattering volume changes from $1.24-3.36i$ fm$^3$ (KW) to $0.14-3.15i$ fm$^3$ (Jülich), showing strong model dependence.
- The $n=2$ P-wave level shifts for $\bar{p}\,^3$He are in the electronvolt range, approximately an order of magnitude larger than the millielectronvolt shifts for $\bar{p}\,^3$H.

## Context
This work extends previous *ab initio* studies of antiproton-deuteron systems by the same group (Duerinck and Lazauskas, 2021-2025), which also found strong model dependence in P-wave states. The calculations support the PUMA experiment at CERN by providing theoretical predictions for antiproton annihilation on light nuclei. The results confirm that nucleon-antinucleon annihilation is predominantly peripheral, as indicated by the weak dependence on the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction and the comparison of annihilation densities with nuclear density distributions.
