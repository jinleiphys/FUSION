# Analysis of a low-energy correction to the eikonal approximation

> Extensions of the eikonal approximation to low energy (20MeV/nucleon typically) are studied. The relation between the dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA) and the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with the eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) is discussed. When Coulomb interaction is artificially turned off, DEA and E-CDCC are shown to give the same breakup cross section, within 3% error, of $^{15}$C on $^{208}$Pb at 20MeV/nucleon. When the Coulomb interaction is included, the difference is appreciable and none of these models agrees with full CDCC calculations. An empirical correction significantly reduces this difference. In addition, E-CDCC has a convergence problem. By including a quantum-mechanical correction to E-CDCC for lower partial waves between $^{15}$C and $^{208}$Pb, this problem is resolved and the result perfectly reproduces full CDCC calculations at a lower computational cost.

## Key claim

The dynamical eikonal approximation (DEA) and the eikonal continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (E-CDCC) are formally equivalent when the Coulomb interaction is absent and the semi-adiabatic approximation holds within 1.5% error; however, when Coulomb interaction is included at 20 MeV/nucleon, neither model alone agrees with full quantum-mechanical CDCC, but an empirical correction to the impact parameter combined with a hybrid quantum-mechanical correction to E-CDCC for low partial waves reproduces full CDCC results perfectly at lower computational cost.

## Method

The authors compared DEA and E-CDCC calculations for the breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus ¹⁵C on a ²⁰⁸Pb target at 20 MeV/nucleon, first with the Coulomb interaction artificially turned off and then with it included. They expressed the DEA equations in a coupled-channel representation to clarify the formal relation between the two models, and analyzed the contribution of each projectile-target angular momentum L to the total breakup cross section. An empirical correction replacing the transverse impact parameter b by b' = η₀/K₀ + √(η₀²/K₀² + b²) was applied to account for Coulomb deflection, and a hybrid version of E-CDCC was tested in which a full CDCC calculation was performed for low partial waves (L < 500 ħ) while shifted E-CDCC was used for higher L.

## Key numbers

- The semi-adiabatic approximation (Kᵢ' + Kᵢ)/(2K₀) ≈ 1 holds within 1.5% error at 20 MeV/nucleon incident energy.
- Without Coulomb interaction, DEA and E-CDCC agree within 3% error for the energy spectrum of ¹⁵C breakup on ²⁰⁸Pb at 20 MeV/nucleon.
- The ground state of ¹⁵C is at energy ε₀ = -1.218 MeV.
- In the E-CDCC calculation without Coulomb, the model space used ℓ_max = 10, k_max = 1.4 fm⁻¹, Δk = 0.04 fm⁻¹, resulting in N_ch = 2311 coupled channels.
- In the E-CDCC calculation with Coulomb, ℓ_max = 6, k_max = 0.84 fm⁻¹, Δk = 0.04 fm⁻¹, z_max = 1000 fm, b_max = 150 fm, giving N_ch = 589 coupled channels.
- The computational time for each impact parameter b with E-CDCC is about 1/60 of that for each angular momentum L with full CDCC.
- The transition angular momentum L_C = 500 ħ was chosen for the hybrid calculation.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between DEA and CDCC performed by Capel et al. (2012) for ¹⁵C breakup on ²⁰⁸Pb, which showed that DEA fails at 20 MeV/nucleon due to missing Coulomb deflection. The present study clarifies the formal relation between DEA and E-CDCC, identifies a convergence problem in E-CDCC at low energy with strong Coulomb interaction, and demonstrates that the empirical impact-parameter shift suggested by Broglia and Winther (1981) and Bertulani (2003) corrects the Coulomb deflection in both eikonal models. The hybrid E-CDCC approach, combining full CDCC for low partial waves with shifted E-CDCC for high partial waves, enables accurate calculations at significantly reduced computational cost, which can be used to improve the description of projectile structure in breakup reactions across a wide range of incident energies.
