# Sampling General N-Body Interactions with Auxiliary Fields

> We present a general auxiliary field transformation which generates effective interactions containing all possible N-body contact terms. The strength of the induced terms can analytically be described in terms of general coefficients associated with the transformation and thus are controllable. This transformation provides a novel way for sampling 3- and 4-body (and higher) contact interactions non-perturbatively in lattice quantum monte-carlo simulations. We show that our method reproduces the exact solution for a two-site quantum mechanical problem.

## Key claim
The authors present a generalization of the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation that can generate all possible N-body contact interactions using only a single auxiliary field, with the induced interaction strengths analytically controllable through a set of coefficients \(c_j\). The method reproduces exact results for a two-site quantum mechanical test system, but the abstract's claim of "novel way for sampling 3- and 4-body contact interactions" is qualified by the demonstration being limited to a two-site model with up to three fermion species.

## Method
The authors introduce a generalized auxiliary field transformation where the integral over the auxiliary field \(\phi\) uses a probability distribution \(P_N(\phi) \propto \exp(-\phi^{2N})\) instead of the standard Gaussian. The auxiliary field couples to the fermionic density operator \(\hat\rho\) through a polynomial \(\sum_{j=1}^{2N-1} c_j \phi^j \hat\rho\). By expanding both the transformed integral and the desired effective action \(\exp(-\sum_k \lambda^{(k)} \hat\rho^k)\) in powers of \(\hat\rho\) and matching order-by-order using Faà di Bruno's formula, the authors derive analytic expressions for the induced many-body coupling coefficients \(\lambda^{(M)}\) in terms of the \(c_j\) parameters. The method is tested on a two-site model with three fermion species using stochastic lattice projection calculations with transfer matrix formalism.

## Key numbers
- For \(N=1\) (standard Hubbard-Stratonovich): \(\lambda^{(2)} = -\frac{1}{4}c_1^2\), all other \(\lambda^{(M)} = 0\)
- For \(N=\infty\) with \(c_{j>3}=0\): \(\lambda^{(1)} = \frac{1}{3}c_2\), \(\lambda^{(2)} = -\frac{1}{6}c_1^2 - \frac{2}{45}c_2^2 - \frac{1}{5}c_1c_3 - \frac{1}{14}c_3^2\)
- For \(N=\infty\) with \(c_{j>3}=0\): \(\lambda^{(3)} = \frac{2}{45}c_1^2c_2 + \frac{8}{2835}c_2^3 + \frac{8}{105}c_1c_2c_3 + \frac{2}{63}c_2c_3^2\)
- Numerical tests used \(\lambda^{(2)} \in \{\pm 1.74, 0.0\}\) and \(\lambda^{(3)} \in \{\pm 0.245, 0.0\}\) in units of \(\kappa\), with \(2\times 10^8\) measurements per system
- The two-site model Hamiltonian uses a hopping parameter \(\kappa\) that sets the dynamical scale

## Context
This work extends the standard Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation (Stratonovich 1957, Hubbard 1959), which only generates two-body interactions, to include arbitrary N-body contact interactions. The method enables non-perturbative inclusion of three- and four-body forces in lattice quantum Monte Carlo simulations, which is relevant for nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) as developed by Lee (2008), Epelbaum (2009), and Carlson (2014). The transformation could be applied to ultracold gases of polar molecules where three-body interactions can become dominant (Buchler 2007, Bonnes 2010), density functional theory approaches to nuclear physics (Drut 2009), and systems near the Efimov threshold in cold atoms (Braaten 2006, Naidon 2016). A limitation is that some combinations of many-body forces require complex \(c_j\) coefficients, potentially introducing a numerical sign problem as discussed by Chen (2004).
