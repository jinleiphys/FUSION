# Jet evolution in Yang-Mills-Wong simulations

> We present results for collisional energy loss and momentum broadening of high momentum partons in a hot and dense non-Abelian plasma obtained by solving the coupled system of Yang-Mills-Wong equations on a lattice in real time. Including hard elastic collisions among the particles we obtain cutoff independent results for the collisional energy loss dE/dx and the transport coefficient q-hat. The latter is found to receive a sizable contribution from a power-law tail in the transverse momentum distribution of high-momentum partons. We further argue that the effect of instabilities on jet broadening should be accessible by experiment when employing jet cones with elliptical bases or studying correlations within the cone in full jet reconstruction methods.

# Jet evolution in Yang-Mills-Wong simulations

## Key claim
The paper presents cutoff-independent results for collisional energy loss and the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ by combining soft field-mediated interactions with hard binary collisions in a Yang-Mills-Wong simulation, but the numerical simulations are performed on undersaturated gluon plasmas and then extrapolated to physical densities and temperatures.

## Method
The authors solve the classical Boltzmann-Vlasov transport equation for hard gluons with SU(2) color charge, coupled self-consistently to the Yang-Mills equation for soft gluon fields, by replacing the distribution function with test particles obeying Wong's equations. Hard binary collisions are included via a stochastic method using leading-order $gg\rightarrow gg$ tree-level diagrams, with a lower momentum cutoff $k^*\simeq\pi/a$ set by the lattice spacing to avoid double-counting between field-mediated and collision-mediated momentum exchanges.

## Key numbers
- Extrapolated to SU(3) and thermal gluon density at $T\approx 400$ MeV: $\hat{q}\approx 3.6\pm 0.3\,\mathrm{GeV}^{2}\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ and $dE/dx\approx 1.6\pm 0.4$ GeV fm$^{-1}$ for a jet energy $E=19.2\,\mathrm{GeV}$
- For a simulation at $T=4$ GeV, $g=2$, $n=5\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, $E=48\,T$: the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and elastic energy loss $dE/dx$ are shown to be independent of the separation scale $k^*$
- The power-law tail in the $p_\perp^2$-distribution of high-momentum partons behaves approximately as $p_\perp^{-4}$, contributing significantly to $\hat{q}$
- For unstable plasmas: $\langle\Delta\eta\rangle / \langle\Delta\phi\rangle \approx 1.5$ from numerical simulations
- For a thermal system at $T=400$ MeV the energy density is about 17 GeV/fm$^3$, appropriate as an average over the first 1 fm/c of a central Au+Au collision at RHIC energy

## Context
This work extends prior simulations of the Yang-Mills-Wong system (Dumitru 2005, Dumitru 2006, Dumitru 2007, Schenke 2008) by including hard binary collisions to achieve cutoff-independent results for jet energy loss and broadening. The results reproduce perturbative QCD expectations for the dependence on density, temperature, and parton energy. The estimate of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ shows that early-stage elastic energy loss in the classical Yang-Mills field background is significant but cannot fully account for the experimentally observed flat $R_{AA}\approx 0.2$, motivating further study of inelastic processes. The prediction of anisotropic jet broadening from Chromo-Weibel instabilities (Romatschke 2003) suggests experimental tests using elliptical jet cones or intra-cone correlations in full jet reconstruction.
