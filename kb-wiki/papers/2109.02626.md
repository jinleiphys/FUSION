# Deblurring for Nuclei: 3D Characteristics of Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Observables from nuclear and high-energy experiments can be degraded by detector performance and/or methodology in extracting the observables, such as of the final-state characteristics of heavy-ion collisions in relation to a coarsely estimated reaction-plane direction. We propose the use of deblurring methods, such as in optics, to correct for observable degradation. Our main focus is the restoration of triple-differential particle distributions in heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that these could be extracted from collision measurements following the Richardson-Lucy deblurring method from optics. We illustrate basic features of the restoration methodology in a schematic model assuming either ideal or more realistic particle detection. The inferred three-dimensional (3D) distributions for collisions may easier to interpret in terms of collision dynamics and sought properties of bulk matter than the currently employed Fourier coefficients, that combine information from different azimuthal angles relative to the reaction plane.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the Richardson-Lucy deblurring algorithm from optics can restore three-dimensional (3D) particle distributions relative to the true reaction plane in heavy-ion collisions, even when the reaction-plane direction can only be coarsely estimated from measurements. The method is demonstrated in schematic models and simulated heavy-ion collisions, but the paper does not apply it to actual experimental data.

## Method

The authors apply the Richardson-Lucy (RL) iterative deconvolution algorithm to correct for the blurring caused by uncertainty in the estimated reaction-plane direction. They first test the method on a one-dimensional (1D) schematic model where a projectile emits particles from a known distribution but its velocity is unknown, then extend the model to include detector inefficiencies. Finally, they simulate heavy-ion collisions at a few hundred MeV/nucleon with sideward, radial, and elliptic flows, using the RL algorithm to restore triple-differential particle distributions from blurred measurements.

## Key numbers

- In the 1D model without inefficiencies, N=10 particles per event were sampled from a uniform velocity distribution from -1 to +1 (arbitrary units) relative to the projectile, with 10,000 events generated and H=0.1 binning used for distributions.
- In the 1D model with inefficiencies, N=30 particles per event were used, with a minimum of n_min=10 detected particles required for analysis, and the detector efficiency function was E(v^lab) = 1/2 + 1/2 cos(π v^lab / v^lab_max) for |v^lab| < v^lab_max = 2.
- For the RL algorithm, the authors used regularization factor λ=0.01 and accelerating power ν=1.99.
- In the heavy-ion collision simulations, particles with mass A ≤ 4 were considered, with a moderate number of light charged particles registered.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to quantify azimuthal anisotropies in heavy-ion collisions, which began with identifying transverse collective motion (Gyulassy 1982, Danielewicz 1983) and evolved through reaction-plane estimation (Danielewicz 1985, Ollitrault 1992) to Fourier coefficient analysis (Voloshin 1996, Poskanzer 1998, Borghini 2001, Bilandzic 2011). The authors argue that deblurred 3D distributions may be easier to interpret in terms of collision dynamics than the currently employed Fourier coefficients, which combine information from different azimuthal angles relative to the reaction plane. The method could enable direct access to distributions that reveal bulk properties such as equation of state and viscosity.
