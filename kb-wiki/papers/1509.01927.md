# Neutrinoless double electron capture

> Direct determination of the neutrino mass is at the present time one of the most important aims of experimental and theoretical research in nuclear and particle physics. A possible way of detection is through neutrinoless double electron capture, $0νECEC$. This process can only occur when the energy of the initial state matches precisely that of the final state. We present here a calculation of prefactors (PF) and nuclear matrix elements (NME) within the framework of the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) for $^{124}$Xe, $^{152}$Gd, $^{156}$Dy, $^{164}$Er, and $^{180}$W. From PF and NME we calculate the expected half-lives and obtain results that are of the same order as those of $ 0νβ^+β^+$ decay, but considerably longer than those of $0νβ^-β^-$ decay.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless double electron capture (0nuECEC) half-lives are of the same order as those of 0nu beta+beta+ decay but considerably longer than those of 0nu beta-beta- decay; the process is only possible when the initial and final state energies match precisely, and the authors calculate that even in the most optimistic scenario with unquenched g_A=1.269, 0nuECEC is unreachable in the present generation of experiments.

## Method

The authors calculated prefactors (PF) and nuclear matrix elements (NME) for five candidate nuclei (124Xe, 152Gd, 156Dy, 164Er, 180W) within the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2). The prefactors were computed using Dirac central field bound state wave functions with finite nucleon size and electron screening in the Thomas-Fermi approximation, while nuclear matrix elements were obtained from IBM-2 wave functions fitted to experimental energies and electromagnetic transition rates. The half-lives were then derived by combining PF and NME for both light and heavy neutrino exchange mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- For 152Gd -> 152Sm [0+_1] with light neutrino exchange and unquenched g_A=1.269: half-life = 8.03 x 10^27 yr for <m_nu> = 1 eV
- For 180W -> 180Hf [0+_1] with light neutrino exchange and unquenched g_A=1.269: half-life = 3.44 x 10^27 yr for <m_nu> = 1 eV
- For 124Xe -> 124Te* [0+_4] with light neutrino exchange and unquenched g_A=1.269: half-life = 1.52 x 10^30 yr for <m_nu> = 1 eV
- For 156Dy -> 156Gd* [0+_4] with light neutrino exchange and unquenched g_A=1.269: half-life = 2.89 x 10^30 yr for <m_nu> = 1 eV
- For 164Er -> 164Dy [0+_1] with light neutrino exchange and unquenched g_A=1.269: half-life = 1.88 x 10^30 yr for <m_nu> = 1 eV
- Nuclear matrix element M^(0nu) for 152Gd -> 152Sm [0+_1] (light neutrino): 2.445 (dimensionless)
- Nuclear matrix element M^(0nu) for 180W -> 180Hf [0+_1] (light neutrino): 4.672 (dimensionless)
- Prefactor G_0nu^ECEC for 180W -> 180Hf: 46.2 x 10^-19 yr^-1
- Prefactor G_0nu^ECEC for 152Gd -> 152Sm: 1.46 x 10^-19 yr^-1
- Degeneracy parameter Delta for 152Gd -> 152Sm: 0.91 keV
- Degeneracy parameter Delta for 180W -> 180Hf: 11.24 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of calculations of phase-space factors and nuclear matrix elements for 0nu beta-beta- and 0nu beta+beta+ processes (Kotila 2012, Barea 2012, 2013) to the resonant neutrinoless double electron capture process. The resonant condition was first discussed by Winter (1955) and elaborated by Bernabeu (1983), and the present calculations provide the first IBM-2 results for the five remaining experimental candidates after recent mass measurements (Eliseev 2011, Nesterenko 2012, Droese 2012) ruled out many earlier candidates. The results enable comparison with other theoretical approaches such as QRPA (Fang 2012) and EDF (Rodriguez 2012), and establish that 0nuECEC half-lives are too long for current-generation experiments, though the best candidates (152Gd and 180W) may be relevant for future searches.
