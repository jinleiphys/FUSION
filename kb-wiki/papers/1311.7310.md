# Particle Production in the Color Class Condensate: from electron-proton DIS to proton-nucleus collisions

> We study single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions in the CGC framework. The parameters in the calculation are obtained by fitting electron-proton deep inelastic scattering data. The obtained dipole-proton amplitude is generalized to dipole-nucleus scattering without any additional nuclear parameters other than the Woods-Saxon distribution. We show that it is possible to use an initial condition without an anomalous dimension and still obtain a good description of the HERA inclusive cross section and LHC single particle production measurements. We argue that one must consistently use the proton transverse area as measured by a high virtuality probe in DIS also for the single inclusive cross section in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, and obtain a nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ that at midrapidity approaches unity at large momenta and at all energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) calculation using an initial condition without an anomalous dimension (MV^e model) can fit HERA inclusive DIS data as well as the standard MV^γ model, and that consistently using the proton transverse area measured by a high virtuality probe in DIS leads to a nuclear modification factor R_pA that approaches unity at large transverse momenta and at all energies, in contrast to most CGC calculations in the literature.

## Method

The dipole-proton scattering amplitude was obtained by fitting the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation to combined HERA reduced cross section data at x<0.01 and Q^2<50 GeV^2, using three different initial conditions (MV, MV^γ, and MV^e). The dipole-proton amplitude was then generalized to dipole-nucleus scattering using the optical Glauber model with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density, without introducing any additional nuclear parameters. Single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions was computed using k_T-factorized and hybrid formalism formulas, with the proton transverse area σ_0/2 taken from the DIS fits and the total inelastic cross section σ_inel used to normalize the yield.

## Key numbers

- MV^e model fit to HERA data yields χ^2/d.o.f ≈ 1.15, with σ_0/2 = 16.36 mb and initial saturation scale Q_s^2 = 0.238 GeV^2
- MV^γ model fit yields χ^2/d.o.f ≈ 1.17, with σ_0/2 = 16.45 mb and initial saturation scale Q_s^2 = 0.245 GeV^2
- Pure MV model fit yields χ^2/d.o.f ≈ 2.76, with σ_0/2 = 18.81 mb and initial saturation scale Q_s^2 = 0.139 GeV^2
- The ratio (σ_0/2)/σ_inel is approximately 0.2-0.3, giving an energy-dependent normalization factor in the proton-proton yield formula

## Context

This work extends the CGC description of small-x DIS from the AAMQS analysis (Albacete, 2010) to single inclusive hadron production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC. It contrasts with earlier CGC calculations (e.g., Albacete, 2010; Tribedy, 2011; Albacete, 2012) that used different treatments of the proton transverse geometry, leading to varying predictions for the high-p_T behavior of R_pA. The results enable more controlled predictions for future forward R_pA measurements at the LHC and demonstrate that a consistent treatment of the proton area from DIS resolves ambiguities in the nuclear modification factor at high transverse momentum.
