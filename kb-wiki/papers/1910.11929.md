# Hybrid and quark star matter based on a non-perturbative equation of state

> With the recent dawn of the multi-messenger astronomy era a new window has opened to explore the constituents of matter and their interactions under extreme conditions. One of the pending challenges of modern physics is to probe the microscopic equation of state (EoS) of cold and dense matter via macroscopic neutron star observations such as their masses and radii. Still unanswered issues concern the detailed composition of matter in the core of neutron stars at high pressure and the possible presence of e.g. hyperons or quarks. By means of a non-perturbative functional renormalization group approach the influence of quantum and density fluctuations on the quark matter EoS in $β$-equilibrium is investigated within two- and three-flavor quark-meson model truncations and compared to results obtained with common mean-field approximations where important fluctuations are usually ignored. We find that they strongly impact the quark matter EoS.

## Key claim

Using a non-perturbative functional renormalization group (FRG) approach, quantum and density fluctuations strongly impact the quark matter equation of state (EoS) in beta-equilibrium, in contrast to common mean-field approximations where such fluctuations are ignored; however, the results depend on the truncation (two- versus three-flavor quark-meson model) and the specific approximation scheme (standard MFA, renormalized MFA, or FRG).

## Method

The authors constructed quark matter EoS within two- and three-flavor quark-meson models using three different approximation levels: standard mean-field approximation (sMFA), renormalized mean-field approximation (rMFA) including vacuum quark fluctuations, and the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach incorporating both quark and meson fluctuations. The grand potential was computed at zero temperature for symmetric matter and for matter in beta-equilibrium with charge neutrality, with leptons treated as a relativistic non-interacting Fermi gas. The resulting EoS were then used to compute mass-radius relations for non-rotating hybrid and pure quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical crossover temperature at zero chemical potential for two-flavor quark-meson model: approximately 140 MeV in sMFA, about 170 MeV in rMFA (shift of about 30 MeV)
- First-order phase transition at T=0 occurs near quark chemical potential of approximately 300 MeV for all approximations, corresponding to the light quark mass in vacuum
- Strange quark mass in vacuum: approximately 430 MeV, with a smooth chiral phase transition seen in strange condensates near this chemical potential
- Critical endpoint (CEP) for two and three quark flavors in FRG truncation located at very small temperatures, around T ~ 10 MeV
- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 ~ 0.16 fm^{-3}; central densities of maximum mass neutron star configurations can reach about 1 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous quark matter studies using quark-meson models (Zacchi 2019, Zacchi 2015) and NJL-type models (Buballa 2003, Baldo 2002) by incorporating non-perturbative fluctuations via the FRG method, which had been applied to neutron star matter in recent works (Leonhardt 2019, Friman 2019, Drews 2016). The results enable systematic comparison of fluctuation effects on neutron star observables such as mass-radius relations, and can be combined with hadronic EoS to model hybrid stars with quark cores. The approach is complementary to perturbative QCD (Kurkela 2009), Dyson-Schwinger methods (Fischer 2012, Isserstedt 2019), and holographic approaches (Ishii 2019).
