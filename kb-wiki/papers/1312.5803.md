# The near threshold $π^- p \to ηn$ reaction in an effective Lagrangian approach

> The near threshold $π^- p \to ηn$ reaction is studied within an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model. By considering the contributions from $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon pole and $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance, the total and differential cross sections of the $π^- p \to ηn$ reaction near threshold are calculated. Our theoretical results can fairly reproduce the current experimental data. It is also shown that while the center-of-mass energy lies in the range from the reaction threshold up to $1.65$ GeV, $s$-channel $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance plays the dominant role. The effect from nucleon pole is found to be small but the interference terms between the $N^*(1535)$ resonance and the nucleon pole are significant. The contributions from $t$-channel processes are negligible in the present calculation.

## Key claim

The near-threshold π⁻ p → η n reaction is dominated by the s-channel N*(1535) resonance, but the interference terms between the N*(1535) resonance and the nucleon pole are significant; the nucleon pole contribution alone is small. The paper shows that using an energy-dependent total width for the N*(1535) resonance pushes down its Breit-Wigner mass compared to using a constant width of 150 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with the isobar model, computing tree-level Feynman diagrams for s-channel and u-channel nucleon pole and N*(1535) resonance contributions. They used effective Lagrangians for πNN, ηNN, πNN*, and ηNN* vertices, with coupling constants determined from experimental partial decay widths of the N*(1535) resonance. The total and differential cross sections were calculated from the production threshold up to center-of-mass energy W = 1.65 GeV, using both an energy-dependent total width and a constant total width (150 MeV) for the N*(1535) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1535) resonance mass is taken as 1535 MeV with a total decay width of 150 MeV from the PDG.
- The squared coupling constants used are: g²_πNN/4π = 14.4, g²_ηNN/4π = 0.4, g²_πNN*/4π = 0.037, and g²_ηNN*/4π = 0.28.
- The cutoff parameters for the form factors are Λ_N = 0.6 GeV for the nucleon pole and Λ_N* = 2.0 GeV for the N*(1535) resonance.
- The N*(1535) partial decay widths are taken as Γ_{N*→Nπ} = 75 MeV and Γ_{N*→Nη} = 75 MeV.
- The calculation covers center-of-mass energies from the reaction threshold up to W = 1.65 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the π⁻ p → η n reaction using a chiral quark-model approach (He 2009, Zhong 2007) and an updated coupled-channel method (Shklyar 2012), all of which found N*(1535) dominance near threshold. The present calculation improves on prior work by explicitly including both energy-dependent and constant total widths for the N*(1535) resonance, and by demonstrating that the interference between the N*(1535) resonance and the nucleon pole is significant. The results enable more refined model calculations of the N*(1535) mass and structure, particularly regarding the inverse mass problem of low-lying excited nucleon states.
