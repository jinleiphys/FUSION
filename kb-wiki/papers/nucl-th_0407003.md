# Nuclear Structure Calculations and Modern Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> We study ground-state properties of the doubly magic nuclei 4He, 16O, and 40Ca employing the Goldstone expansion and using as input four different high-quality nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. The short-range repulsion of these potentials is renormalized by constructing a smooth low-momentum potential V-low-k. This is used directly in a Hartree-Fock approach and corrections up to third order in the Goldstone expansion are evaluated. Comparison of the results shows that they are only slightly dependent on the choice of the NN potential.

# Nuclear Structure Calculations and Modern Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

## Key claim

Ground-state properties of doubly magic nuclei calculated using four different high-quality nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen II, Argonne V18, CD-Bonn, and N3LO) show only slight dependence on the choice of potential, but this insensitivity arises specifically because the V-low-k renormalization procedure attenuates differences in off-shell behavior, the original potentials differ substantially in their deuteron D-state probabilities and off-shell tensor force strength.

## Method

The authors constructed a smooth low-momentum potential V-low-k (cutoff momentum Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1) from each of the four bare NN potentials using the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki iterative method, preserving on-shell properties up to the cutoff. They then performed Hartree-Fock calculations in a harmonic-oscillator basis (N=5 for 16O and 40Ca, N=6 for 4He) using each V-low-k, followed by evaluation of Goldstone expansion diagrams up to third order in V-low-k to obtain binding energies and rms charge radii. Center-of-mass corrections were applied to both the kinetic energy and mean square radius operators.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 4He: ranges from 6.61 MeV (N3LO) to 6.95 MeV (CD-Bonn), compared to experimental 7.07 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 16O: ranges from 8.11 MeV (N3LO) to 8.30 MeV (CD-Bonn), compared to experimental 7.98 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for 40Ca: ranges from 9.50 MeV (N3LO) to 9.93 MeV (CD-Bonn), compared to experimental 8.55 MeV
- RMS charge radius for 4He: ranges from 1.63 fm (CD-Bonn) to 1.75 fm (N3LO), compared to experimental 1.67 fm
- RMS charge radius for 16O: ranges from 2.49 fm (CD-Bonn) to 2.66 fm (N3LO), compared to experimental 2.73 fm
- RMS charge radius for 40Ca: ranges from 3.10 fm (CD-Bonn) to 3.29 fm (N3LO), compared to experimental 3.485 fm
- Maximum difference between potentials: 0.43 MeV for binding energy per nucleon and 0.19 fm for rms charge radius
- Deuteron D-state probability (P_D) for full potentials: ranges from 4.51% (N3LO) to 5.76% (Argonne V18); after V-low-k renormalization: ranges from 4.04% (CD-Bonn) to 4.37% (Argonne V18)
- 4He binding energy from Goldstone expansion: 27.523 MeV (Nijmegen II), 27.409 MeV (AV18), 27.799 MeV (CD-Bonn), 26.440 MeV (N3LO); exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky results: 24.560 MeV, 24.280 MeV, 26.260 MeV, 25.410 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Coraggio 2003) on the convergence of the Goldstone expansion for doubly magic nuclei, now performing a systematic comparison across four phase-shift equivalent NN potentials. It contrasts with earlier finite-nucleus studies (Jiang 1992, Hjorth-Jensen 1995, Covello 1999) that made only limited comparisons between potentials, and complements nuclear matter studies (Engvik 1997, Baldo 1998, Muther 1999, Frick 2002) that examined potential dependence in infinite systems. The finding that V-low-k renormalization makes the potentials nearly off-shell equivalent, as evidenced by the convergence of P_D values, enables future many-body calculations to use any of these modern potentials with confidence that results will be similar, and suggests that chiral N3LO potentials, which already have a low-momentum character, may be particularly well-suited for microscopic nuclear structure calculations.
