# Multi-$\bar{K}$ hypernuclei

> Relativistic mean field calculations of multi-$\bar{K}$ hypernuclei are performed by adding $K^-$ mesons to particle-stable configurations of nucleons, $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons. For a given hypernuclear core, the calculated $\bar{K}$ separation energy $B_{\bar{K}}$ saturates with the number of $\bar{K}$ mesons for more than roughly 10 mesons, with $B_{\bar{K}}$ bounded from above by 200 MeV. The associated baryonic densities saturate at values 2-3 times nuclear-matter density within a small region where the $\bar{K}$-meson densities peak, similarly to what was found for multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei. The calculations demonstrate that particle-stable multistrange $\{N,Λ,Ξ\}$ configurations are stable against strong-interaction conversions $Λ\to N \bar{K}$ and $Ξ\to N \bar{K} \bar{K}$, confirming and strengthening the conclusion that kaon condensation is unlikely to occur in strong-interaction self-bound strange hadronic matter.

# Multi-$\bar{K}$ hypernuclei

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that in relativistic mean-field calculations of multi-$\bar{K}$ hypernuclei, the $\bar{K}$ separation energy $B_{\bar{K}}$ saturates below 200 MeV even when hyperons ($\Lambda$, $\Xi$) are added to the nuclear core, confirming that kaon condensation is unlikely to occur in strong-interaction self-bound strange hadronic matter. The saturation holds for any boson-field composition containing the dominant vector $\omega$-meson field, which acts repulsively between $\bar{K}$ mesons.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations by solving coupled Dirac equations for baryons (nucleons, $\Lambda$, and $\Xi$ hyperons) and Klein-Gordon equations for $K^-$ mesons, with meson fields ($\sigma$, $\sigma^*$, $\omega$, $\rho$, $\phi$, $A$) generated dynamically. The coupling constants for hyperons were fixed using SU(6) symmetry and fits to measured hypernuclear binding energies, while $\bar{K}$ couplings to vector mesons adopted purely F-type SU(3) values, with scalar couplings $g_{\sigma K}$ fitted to produce single-$K^-$ separation energies of 100–150 MeV in selected nuclei.

## Key numbers
- $B_{\bar{K}}$ saturates at values bounded from above by 200 MeV for more than roughly 10 $\bar{K}$ mesons.
- Baryonic densities saturate at 2–3 times nuclear-matter density ($\rho_0$) within a small region where $\bar{K}$-meson densities peak.
- In $^{208}{\rm Pb}+106\Lambda+8\Xi^0+18\Xi^-+\kappa K^-$ configurations, $B_{K^-}$ reaches a maximum of approximately 110 MeV for $\kappa=12$ and $\eta=20$ $\Lambda$ hyperons.
- For $^{90}{\rm Zr}+40\Lambda+2\Xi^0+2\Xi^-+10K^-$, the central baryon density exceeds the nuclear density in $^{90}$Zr by a factor of roughly 3.
- Single $K^-$ $1s$ separation energies in normal nuclei range from $B_{K^-}=49.0$ MeV in $^{12}$C to 76.6 MeV in $^{208}$Pb (NL-TM RMF model with vector SU(3) couplings).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RMF calculations of multi-$\bar{K}$ nuclei (Gazda et al., 2007, 2008) into the domain of multi-$\bar{K}$ hypernuclei, checking whether joint consideration of $\bar{K}$ mesons together with hyperons could change earlier conclusions against kaon condensation. The results contradict the scenario proposed by Yamazaki et al. (2004) that $\bar{K}$ mesons might provide degrees of freedom for high-density self-bound strange matter, and challenge the liquid-drop calculations of Muto (2008) which claimed kaon-condensed hypernuclei at densities about $9\rho_0$. The work enables further studies of whether $\bar{K}$ mesons can bind in hadronic systems, and confirms that particle-stable multistrange $\{N,\Lambda,\Xi\}$ configurations are stable against strong-interaction conversions $\Lambda \to N \bar{K}$ and $\Xi \to N \bar{K} \bar{K}$.
