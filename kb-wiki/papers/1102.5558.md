# Pion form factors

> We consider the electromagnetic and transition pion form factors. Using dispersion relations we simultaneously describe both the hadronic, time-like region and the asymptotic region of large energy-momentum transfer. For the latter we propose a novel mechanism of Regge fermion exchange. We find that hadronic contributions dominate form factors at all currently available energies.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the asymptotic behavior of pion form factors at large momentum transfer is driven by a reggeized quark exchange mechanism rather than by leading-twist perturbative QCD, and they find that hadronic (resonance) contributions dominate the form factors at all currently accessible energies, with the asymptotic quark-exchange contribution accounting for only about 50% even at the largest measured momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors use dispersion relations to connect the time-like (hadronic) and space-like (asymptotic) regions for both the charged pion electromagnetic form factor \(F_{2\pi}(s)\) and the neutral pion transition form factor \(F_{\pi\gamma}(s)\). For the hadronic region, they include the \(\rho(770)\) and \(\omega(782)\) resonances using a unitarized parametrization with Omnes-Muskelishvilli functions and Breit-Wigner distributions. For the asymptotic region, they introduce a novel mechanism where the intermediate \(q\bar{q}\) state contributes via a Regge-behaved scattering amplitude, with the quark Regge trajectory related to the \(\rho/f_2\) meson trajectory from \(\pi\pi\) scattering.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\)
* \(\omega(782)\) contribution to transition form factor: coupling \(c^{(3\pi)}_{\pi\gamma} = 0.493\), obtained from \(g_{\omega\pi\gamma} = 1.81\) and \(g_\omega = 17.1\)
* \(\rho(770)\) contribution to transition form factor: coupling \(c^{(2\pi)}_{\pi\gamma} = 0.613\), obtained from \(g_{\rho\pi\gamma} = 0.647\) and \(g_\rho = 4.96\)
* Sum of \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) resonance contributions at \(s=0\) agrees with anomaly-driven normalization to within 10-15%
* Quark Regge trajectory: \(\alpha_q(t) \sim 0.75 + 0.45 t\), with intercept \(\alpha_q(0) \sim 3/4\)
* Quark Regge intercept at \(t = -1\ \text{GeV}^2\): \(\alpha_q(-1\ \text{GeV}^2) \approx 0.3-0.4\)
* Asymptotic behavior: \(s F(s) \propto s^{1/4}\) for both form factors
* Available data on electromagnetic form factor ranges up to \(|s| \lesssim 10\ \text{GeV}^2\)
* BaBar data on transition form factor extends to \(-s \approx 40\ \text{GeV}^2\)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between perturbative QCD predictions for pion form factors and experimental data, particularly the BaBar "anomaly" where the transition form factor grows with \(|s|\) rather than approaching a constant. The authors extend the dispersion relation approach used for the electromagnetic form factor (e.g., by Truong (1988) and others) to the transition form factor, and propose a Regge fermion exchange mechanism as an alternative to the leading-twist pQCD framework of Brodsky and Lepage (1980) and Efremov and Radyushkin (1980). The work enables a unified description of both form factors across all energy scales and predicts that the electromagnetic form factor should also rise as \(s^{1/4}\) at large momentum transfers, which can be tested by future experiments.
