# Non-equilibrium components in very low transverse momentum region in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We analyze Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=2.76$ TeV with a novel framework based on the dynamical core--corona picture that describes particle productions from both equilibrium and non-equilibrium components. We remark the possibility of the contribution from non-equilibrium components at very low transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) region and show that such contributions significantly affect $p_{T}$-integrated four-particle cumulants. These results strongly suggest the necessity of non-equilibrium components when one extracts properties of the quark-gluon plasma from experimental data using sophisticated dynamical models based on relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that non-equilibrium ("corona") components from string fragmentation contribute significantly to particle production at very low transverse momentum (p_T < 1 GeV) in Pb+Pb collisions, and that these contributions must be included in dynamical models to correctly extract quark-gluon plasma properties from experimental data, contrary to the common assumption that the very low p_T region is dominated by equilibrium hydrodynamic emission.

## Method

The authors use the DCCI2 framework, which implements a dynamical core-corona picture: initial partons from PYTHIA8 Angantyr are dynamically separated into equilibrated QGP fluids (core) described by (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics and non-equilibrated partons (corona) that undergo Lund string fragmentation. The core fluids are particlized at T_sw = 0.165 GeV using the iS3D Monte-Carlo sampler, and all hadrons are processed through the JAM hadronic transport model for rescatterings and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV are analyzed
- Corona components reach approximately 20% of charged hadron yields below p_T ≈ 1 GeV in minimum bias collisions
- Fraction of corona components for protons and antiprotons reaches approximately 50% at p_T → 0 GeV in 0-5% central events
- The dynamical initialization uses formation time tau_0 = 0.1 fm, switching temperature T_sw = 0.165 GeV, and parameters p_T0Ref (Pb+Pb) = 1.0 GeV, sigma_0 = 0.3 fm^2, sigma_perp = 0.6 fm
- Four-particle cumulant c_2{4} from corona components is zero-consistent within statistical error bars for the entire N_ch range, while core contributions show negative values above N_ch ≈ 10^2

## Context

This work extends the dynamical core-corona initialization framework previously developed by Kanakubo et al. (2021) to address a long-standing discrepancy between hydrodynamic models and experimental data at very low p_T (p_T < 0.5 GeV), which has been reported even in state-of-the-art Bayesian analyses by Nijs et al. (2020) and Devetak et al. (2019). The results suggest that conventional hybrid models (hydrodynamics plus hadronic afterburner) systematically underestimate yields at very low p_T because they omit the corona contribution, and that this omission also biases extraction of QGP transport coefficients through Bayesian parameter estimation. The paper enables more precise comparisons between dynamical models and experimental data by demonstrating that non-equilibrium components must be included when using multi-particle cumulants to study collective flow.
