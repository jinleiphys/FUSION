# Phenomenological Equation of State of Strongly Interacting Matter with First-Order Phase Transitions and Critical Points

> An extension of the relativistic density functional approach to the equation of state for strongly interacting matter is suggested which generalizes a recently developed modified excluded-volume mechanism to the case of temperature and density dependent available-volume fractions. A parametrisation of this dependence is presented for which at low temperatures and suprasaturation densities a first-order phase transition is obtained. It changes for increasing temperatures to a crossover transition via a critical endpoint. This provides a benchmark case for studies of the role of such a point in hydrodynamic simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The approach is thermodynamically consistent and extendable to finite isospin asymmetries that are relevant for simulations of neutron stars, their mergers and core-collapse supernova explosions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological equation of state for strongly interacting matter that produces a first-order phase transition at low temperatures and suprasaturation densities, which changes to a crossover transition via a critical endpoint at higher temperatures, but the authors explicitly state that no attempt was made to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state at vanishing baryon chemical potential, and the high-temperature Stefan-Boltzmann limit requires future modifications.

## Method

The authors extend a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings (the DD2 parametrization from Typel (2009)) by introducing temperature- and density-dependent available-volume fractions that modify the effective degeneracy factors of nucleons and antinucleons. The available-volume fraction is parameterized with an exponential function of baryon density and temperature, controlled by parameters \(s=3\), \(v=2\) fm\(^3\), \(T_0 = 270\) MeV, and a cutoff density \(n_{\rm cut} = n_{\rm sat}\), such that the effective degrees of freedom increase at suprasaturation densities and low temperatures, producing a softening of the equation of state and a first-order phase transition. The phase transition is constructed via Maxwell construction for isospin-symmetric matter (\(Y_q = 0.5\)), identifying coexisting phases where pressure and baryon chemical potential are equal.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint of the pseudo hadron–quark phase transition at temperature \(T_{\rm crit} \approx 155.5\) MeV and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{B,{\rm crit}} = 591.8\) MeV.
- At zero temperature, the coexistence region of the first-order phase transition spans baryon densities from \(0.270\) to \(0.408\) fm\(^{-3}\), well above the nuclear saturation density \(n_{\rm sat} = 0.149065\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The baryon chemical potential at the phase transition decreases from \(979.1\) MeV at zero temperature to \(591.8\) MeV at the critical point.
- The critical density at the endpoint is \(0.201\) fm\(^{-3}\), still above \(n_{\rm sat}\).

## Context

This work extends the modified excluded-volume mechanism introduced by Typel (2016) to include temperature dependence, enabling a unified phenomenological description of a first-order hadron–quark phase transition at low temperatures and a crossover at low baryon densities, with a critical endpoint. The approach provides a benchmark equation of state for hydrodynamic simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and is designed to be extendable to finite isospin asymmetries relevant for neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernova simulations, complementing microscopic models such as those by Dexheimer (2009) and Mukherjee (2016). The model can be adjusted to produce alternative phase diagram topologies, including a second critical endpoint at low temperatures as suggested by Hatsuda (2006).
