# Astrophysical $S$ factor and reaction rate of the direct $^{12}{\rm C}(p, γ)^{13}{\rm N}$ capture process within a potential model approach

> The astrophysical direct nuclear capture reaction $^{12}{\rm C}(p, γ)^{13}{\rm N}$ is studied within the framework of a potential model. Parameters of the nuclear $p-^{12}$C interaction potentials of the Woods-Saxon form are adjusted to reproduce experimental $p-^{12}$C scattering phase shifts, as well as the binding energies and empirical values of the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the $^{13}$N(1/2$^-$) ground state from the literature. The reaction rates are found to be very sensitive to the description of the value of the ANC of the $^{13}$N($1/2^{-}$) ground state and width of the $^{13}$N($1/2^+$) resonance at the $E_x=2.365$ MeV excitation energy. The potential model, which yields the ANC value of 1.63 fm$^{-1/2}$ for the $^{13}$N($1/2^{-}$) ground state and a value $Γ$=39 keV for the $^{13}$N($1/2^+$) resonance width, is able to reproduce the astrophysical $S$ factor in the energy interval up to 2 MeV, the empirical values of the reaction rates in the temperature region up to $T=10^{10}$ K of the LUNA Collaboration and the results of the R-matrix fit. The astrophysical factor $S(0)=1.35$ keV b is found using the asymptotic expansion method of D. Baye. The obtained value is in a good agreement with the Solar Fusion II result. At the same time, the calculated value of 1.44 keV b of the astrophysical $S$ factor at the Solar Gamow energy is consistent with the result of the R-matrix fit of $S(25~\rm{keV})=1.48 \pm 0.09$ keV b by Kettner {\it et al.}, but slightly less than the result of $S(25~\rm{keV})=1.53 \pm 0.06$ keV b the LUNA Collaboration.

## Key claim

The potential model that reproduces an ANC of 1.63 fm$^{-1/2}\) for the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{-}\)) ground state and a resonance width of 39 keV for the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{+}\)) state at \(E_x=2.365\) MeV yields the best description of the new LUNA experimental data for the astrophysical \(S\) factor and reaction rates, though the recommended theoretical width of 39 keV is slightly larger than the experimental widths of 35.2\(\pm\)0.5 keV.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrodinger equation with Woods-Saxon nuclear potentials and a spherical Coulomb potential for the \(p-^{12}\)C system. Potential parameters in the \(^2S_{1/2}\), \(^2P_{1/2}\), \(^2P_{3/2}\), \(^2D_{3/2}\), and \(^2D_{5/2}\) partial waves were adjusted to reproduce experimental elastic scattering phase shifts, the binding energy and ANC of the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{-}\)) ground state, and the properties of the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{+}\)) resonance. Four potential models (V_M1 through V_M4) were constructed, differing in the adopted ANC value (ranging from 1.28 to 1.63 fm\(^{-1/2}\)) and the \(^2S_{1/2}\) resonance width (35 to 39 keV).

## Key numbers

*   ANC of the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{-}\)) ground state: 1.63 fm\(^{-1/2}\) for models V_M1 and V_M4, consistent with the empirical value of 1.63\(\pm\)0.13 fm\(^{-1/2}\) from Artemov (2022).
*   Width of the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{+}\)) resonance at \(E_x=2.365\) MeV: 39 keV for model V_M4, compared to the experimental value of 35.2\(\pm\)0.5 keV from Gyurky (2023).
*   Astrophysical \(S\) factor at zero energy: \(S(0)=1.35\) keV b for model V_M4, found using the asymptotic expansion method of D. Baye.
*   Astrophysical \(S\) factor at the solar Gamow energy: 1.44 keV b for model V_M4, consistent with the R-matrix fit result of \(S(25~\rm{keV})=1.48 \pm 0.09\) keV b by Kettner et al. (2023).
*   Astrophysical \(S\) factor at the solar Gamow energy from the LUNA Collaboration: \(S(25~\rm{keV})=1.53 \pm 0.06\) keV b.

## Context

This work extends previous potential model studies of the \(^{12}\)C(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{13}\)N reaction by Huang (2010) and Irgaziev (2018, 2020), and directly addresses the spread in empirical ANC values extracted from transfer reactions by Fernandes (2000), Artemov (2002), and Li (2010). The results enable more reliable reaction rate calculations for the CNO cycle in AGB and RGB stars, and the authors recommend further experimental and theoretical study to clarify the \(^{13}\)N(1/2\(^{+}\)) resonance width.
