# Generalization of Uncertainty Relation for Quantum and Stochastic Systems

> The generalized uncertainty relation applicable to quantum and stochastic systems is derived within the stochastic variational method. This relation not only reproduces the well-known inequality in quantum mechanics but also is applicable to the Gross-Pitaevskii equation and the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation, showing that the finite minimum uncertainty between the position and the momentum is not an inherent property of quantum mechanics but a common feature of stochastic systems. We further discuss the possible implication of the present study in discussing the application of the hydrodynamic picture to microscopic systems, like relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized uncertainty relation within the stochastic variational method (SVM) that applies not only to quantum mechanics but also to classical stochastic systems such as those described by the Gross-Pitaevskii equation and the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation. The authors argue that a finite minimum uncertainty between position and momentum is not an inherent property of quantum mechanics but a common feature of any stochastic system with a finite noise intensity, though the specific numerical value of the minimum uncertainty depends on the system parameters.

## Method

The authors employ the stochastic variational method (SVM) proposed by Yasue, which generalizes the classical variational principle to non-differentiable trajectories described by forward and backward stochastic differential equations. They introduce a Hamiltonian formulation of SVM with two conjugate momenta (p and \bar{p}) associated with the two possible time derivatives (forward and backward), and define the standard deviation of momentum as the symmetric average of the standard deviations of these two momenta. Using the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality, they derive a lower bound for the product of the standard deviations of position and momentum, expressed in terms of the noise intensity ν and the parameters α₁ and α₂ that characterize the kinetic energy in the stochastic Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- For quantum mechanics, the parameters are set to (α₁, α₂, ν) = (0, 1/2, ħ/(2m)), and the generalized uncertainty relation reduces to the Kennard inequality: Δ_x Δ_p ≥ ħ/2.
- For water at room temperature, the obtained minimum uncertainty is two orders of magnitude larger than that of quantum mechanics, though still sufficiently small compared to the coarse-grained scale of hydrodynamics.
- The determinant of the matrix M in the stochastic Lagrangian is given by det(M) = (κ − μ²)/(4ν²), where μ = α₁(1+2α₂)ν and κ = 2α₂ν².

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method framework previously developed by Yasue (1979) and Nelson (1966) to derive a unified uncertainty relation applicable to both quantum and classical stochastic systems. It builds on the observation that the Schrödinger equation can be derived from SVM with specific parameter choices, and that the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation and Gross-Pitaevskii equation also emerge from the same formalism with different parameters (Koide and Kodama, 2008). The result enables the application of hydrodynamic approaches to microscopic systems such as relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the finite minimum uncertainty from stochasticity may play an important role.
