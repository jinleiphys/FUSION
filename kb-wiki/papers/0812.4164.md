# New Method of Modelling Dissipative Hydrodynamics

> We propose to model the dissipative hydrodynamics used in description of the multiparticle production processes ($d$-hydrodynamics) by a special kind of the perfect nonextensive fluid ($q$-fluid) where $q$ denotes the nonextensivity parameter appearing in the nonextensive Tsallis statistics. The advantage of $q$-hydrodynamics lies in its formal simplicity in comparison to the $d$-hydrodynamics. We argue that parameter $q$ describes summarily (at least to some extent) all dynamical effects behind the viscous behavior of the hadronic fluid.

# New Method of Modelling Dissipative Hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The authors propose that dissipative hydrodynamics (d-hydrodynamics) used in multiparticle production can be modelled by a perfect nonextensive fluid (q-fluid) based on Tsallis statistics, where the nonextensivity parameter q summarily describes all dynamical effects behind viscous behavior. The correspondence is claimed to hold only "at least to some extent," and the mapping requires that temperature and velocity fields satisfy specific relations in the whole space-time region.

## Method
The authors establish a nonextensive/dissipative correspondence (NexDC) by mapping a perfect q-hydrodynamic flow (with q-temperature T_q and q-flow four-vector u_q^μ) onto a dissipative d-hydrodynamic flow. This is done by requiring that the pressure from q-statistics equals the usual pressure, while the q-energy density equals the usual energy density plus three times the bulk pressure Π. The bulk pressure is defined in terms of the q-enthalpy w_q and the four-velocity difference γ between the q>1 and q=1 flows, leading to constitutive equations for the energy flow vector W^μ and shear pressure tensor π^μν.

## Key numbers
- The bulk pressure Π is defined as Π ≡ (1/3) w_q [γ² + 2γ], where γ(x) ≡ δu_q^μ(x) u_μ(x) = -(1/2) δu_{qμ} δu_q^μ and w_q ≡ ε_q + P_q is the q-enthalpy.
- The q-enthalpy relates to the usual enthalpy w as w_q = w/[1+γ]², leading to Π = w Γ where Γ ≡ (1/3) γ(γ+2)/(γ+1)².
- The NexDC yields relations between dissipative tensor components: W^μ W_μ = -3Π w, π^μν W_ν = -2Π W^μ, and π_{μν} π^μν = 6Π².
- A sum rule relates shear and bulk viscosities to entropy density: 1/(ζ/s) + 3/(η/s) = w σ^μ_{full;μ} / Π².

## Context
This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic model that successfully reproduces RHIC experimental data (Hirano 2008) and the concept of strongly interacting quark-gluon fluid (Lee 2005), while addressing hints that hadronic fluid cannot be totally ideal (nonviscous indicators). It builds on the nonextensive/dissipative correspondence proposed in Osada (2008) and the relativistic nonextensive kinetic theory of Lavagno (2002). The approach enables simpler numerical applications of dissipative hydrodynamics compared to existing d-hydrodynamic models (dhydro, Problems), and further investigations of q-hydrodynamics and its implications are under way.
