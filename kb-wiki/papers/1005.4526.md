# Collective flow in (anti)proton-proton collision at Tevatron and LHC

> Collective flow as a consequence of hydrodynamical evolution in heavy ion collisions is intensively studied by theorists and experimentalists to understand the behavior of hot quark matter. Due to their large mass, heavy ions suffer collective effects even at low (SPS) or intermediate energies (RHIC). In case of light systems such as (anti)proton-proton interactions, collective effects was not expected. Within a global model such as EPOS, where light and heavy systems are treated using the same physics, it appears that Tevatron data are better described if a flow is introduced. Then the extrapolation to LHC can easily be done and we can compare to first data from ATLAS experiment.

# Collective flow in (anti)proton-proton collision at Tevatron and LHC

## Key claim
Collective flow effects, previously considered exclusive to heavy ion collisions, are also present in high-multiplicity (anti)proton-proton collisions at Tevatron and LHC energies, provided the energy density is high enough. The claim is conditional on selecting events with sufficiently high charged-particle multiplicity (ten to twenty times the average) to create a small thermalized system.

## Method
The authors used the EPOS model, a quantum mechanical multiple scattering approach based on partons and strings, to simulate (anti)proton-proton collisions both with and without a hydrodynamic evolution stage. They compared the simulated average transverse momentum as a function of charged-particle multiplicity to data from the CDF experiment at Tevatron (1.8 TeV) and the ATLAS experiment at LHC (900 GeV). The hydrodynamic treatment employed a 3+1 dimensional code with core-corona separation, a realistic equation-of-state from lattice QCD, and a hadronic cascade after hadronization.

## Key numbers
- At Tevatron (1.8 TeV), the average transverse momentum as a function of central charged-particle multiplicity shows a clear mass splitting effect (lambdas, kaons, charged particles) that is better reproduced by EPOS with hydro than without hydro.
- At LHC (900 GeV), ATLAS data points for average transverse momentum versus charged-particle multiplicity are compared to EPOS simulations with hydro (full line) and without hydro (dashed line), showing improved agreement when flow is included.
- The pseudorapidity distribution of charged particles with transverse momentum above 500 MeV at 900 GeV shows little difference between simulations with and without hydro, indicating the flow effect primarily influences transverse momentum rather than total multiplicity.

## Context
This work extends the observation of collective flow from heavy ion collisions at RHIC and SPS energies to light systems such as (anti)proton-proton collisions, as previously suggested by Abreu (2007). It builds on the EPOS model framework, which treats light and heavy systems with the same underlying physics, and compares directly to Tevatron data from CDF (Acosta, 2005) and LHC data from ATLAS (Aad, 2010). The results enable future detailed measurements of particle correlations at LHC to test the space-time evolution of energy density in hadronic interactions.
