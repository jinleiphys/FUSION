# Nuclear Scissors Mode with Pairing

> The coupled dynamics of the scissors mode and the isovector giant quadrupole resonance are studied using a generalized Wigner function moments method taking into account pair correlations. Equations of motion for angular momentum, quadrupole moment and other relevant collective variables are derived on the basis of the time dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations. Analytical expressions for energy centroids and transitions probabilities are found for the harmonic oscillator model with the quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction and monopole pairing force. Deformation dependences of energies and $B(M1)$ values are correctly reproduced. The inclusion of pair correlations leads to a drastic improvement in the description of qualitative and quantitative characteristics of the scissors mode.

## Key claim

Pair correlations, treated via a constant monopole pairing gap in the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, are essential to reproduce the experimentally observed quadratic deformation dependence of the scissors mode's $B(M1)$ strength and its nearly constant energy centroid, correcting the linear deformation dependence predicted by earlier models without pairing.

## Method

The authors generalized the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method to include pair correlations by starting from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations. They derived a closed set of six linearized dynamical equations for collective variables (quadrupole moments, momentum quadrupole moments, angular momenta, and their pairing-field counterparts) in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator model with quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction and constant monopole pairing force. The isovector and isoscalar modes were decoupled, and analytical expressions for eigenfrequencies and transition probabilities were obtained by solving the resulting characteristic equation.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the scissors mode with pairing is given by $E^2_{\pm}=4\Delta^2+[\epsilon^2(2-\alpha)+\chi_1 k_4\Delta]\pm\sqrt{[\epsilon^2(2-\alpha)+\chi_1 k_4\Delta]^2-\chi_1\Delta [4k_4\epsilon^2-k_0\hbar^4/m^2]-4\hbar^4\bar\omega^4(1-\alpha)\delta^2}$, where $\epsilon^2=\hbar^2\bar\omega^2(1+\delta/3)$ and $\Delta$ is the pairing gap.
- The energy-weighted sum rule for $B(M1)$ is $E_{sc}B(M1)_{sc}+E_{iv}B(M1)_{iv}=(1-\alpha)(m\bar\omega^2/4\pi)Q_{00}\delta^2\mu_N^2$, which is identical to the result without pairing, indicating no pairing contribution to the sum rule under the constant-gap approximation.
- For a spherical nucleus ($\delta=0$), the mode that would become the scissors mode has an energy estimated at about 4 MeV, consistent with a QRPA result of approximately 4.2 MeV for $^{158}$Sn from Matsuo et al.
- The $B(M1)$ transition probability for the scissors mode is $B(M1)_{\nu}=[(1-\alpha)/(8\pi)]m\bar\omega^2 Q_{00}\delta^2 (E_{\nu}^2-4\Delta^2-2\epsilon^2)/\{E_{\nu}[E^2_{\nu}-4\Delta^2-\epsilon^2(2-\alpha)-\chi_1 k_4\Delta]\}\,\mu_N^2$, which yields a $\delta^2$ dependence.

## Context

This work extends the earlier WFM treatment of the coupled scissors mode and isovector giant quadrupole resonance by Balbutsev and Schuck (2004), which did not include pairing and predicted a linear deformation dependence for both energy and $B(M1)$. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between those theoretical predictions and experimental data from Ziegler et al. (1990), Pitz et al., and Margraf et al., which showed a quadratic $\delta^2$ dependence of $B(M1)_{sc}$ and a weak deformation dependence of $E_{sc}$. The analytical results confirm the phenomenological parametrization of Pietralla et al. (1998) and the analytical $\delta^2$ extraction by Garrido et al. (1999), and enable future applications of the WFM method to more realistic potentials and residual interactions beyond the harmonic oscillator plus monopole pairing model.
