# The Gamow Golden Rule of Multichannel Resonances

> We construct the Gamow Golden Rule of multichannel scattering, and use it to obtain the decay distributions, the partial decay constants, the partial decay widths, and the branching fractions of a resonance that has several decay modes. We exemplify the results using two coupled-channel square well potentials.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exact multichannel generalization of the Gamow Golden Rule, which provides the decay distributions, partial decay constants, partial decay widths, and branching fractions for resonances with multiple decay modes, where the standard Fermi Golden Rule is only an approximation valid for long-lived (sharp) resonances.

## Method

The authors generalize the single-channel Gamow Golden Rule to multichannel scattering by assuming that the multichannel Gamow state satisfies an integral equation analogous to the single-channel case, obtained by omitting the free incoming plane wave from the multichannel Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They then derive expressions for the partial decay distributions in both the momentum basis and the angular momentum basis for spherically symmetric potentials, and specialize to the coupled-channel approximation. The formalism is exemplified using two coupled-channel square well potentials with the same range and equal reduced masses, where the coupled radial Schrodinger equations are solved by diagonalizing the potential matrix.

## Key numbers

* The two-channel square well potentials have the same range \(a\) and equal reduced masses \(\mu_1=\mu_2=\mu\).
* The eigenvalues of the matrix \({\sf K}^2-{\sf V}\) are given by \(q_{\pm}^2 = \frac{1}{2}[(k_1^2-V_{11})+(k_2^2-V_{22}) \pm \sqrt{[(k_1^2-V_{11})-(k_2^2-V_{22})]^2+4V_{12}V_{21}}]\).
* The normalization constant for the eigenvectors is \(N^2 = V_{12}^2 + (k_1^2-V_{11}-q_+^2)^2 = V_{21}^2 + (k_2^2-V_{22}-q_-^2)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-channel Gamow Golden Rule (de la Madrid, 2015-2024) to multichannel resonances, which are the norm in experimental nuclear and particle physics. The formalism is applicable to any spherically symmetric short-range potential and is expected to hold for charged particles by replacing Bessel and Hankel functions with Coulomb functions, following standard scattering theory (Taylor, 1972; Newton, 1982). The two-channel square well example uses parameters from prior literature (Han, 2000; Grozdanov, 2000) and demonstrates how the decay distribution is affected by a resonance near threshold, which is relevant for astrophysics and nuclear physics (RMP, PRC).
