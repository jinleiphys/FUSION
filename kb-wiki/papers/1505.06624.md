# Asymmetry of the parallel momentum distribution of (p,pN) reaction residues

> The parallel momentum distribution (PMD) of the residual nuclei of the 14O(p,pn)13O and 14O(p,2p)13N reactions at 100 and 200 MeV/nucleon in inverse kinematics is investigated with the framework of the distorted wave impulse approximation. The PMD shows an asymmetric shape characterized by a steep fall-off on the high momentum side and a long-ranged tail on the low momentum side. The former is found to be due to the phase volume effect reflecting the energy and momentum conservation, and the latter is to the momentum shift of the outgoing two nucleons inside an attractive potential caused by the residual nucleus. Dependence of these effects on the nucleon separation energy of the projectile and the incident energy is also discussed.

## Key claim

The asymmetry of the parallel momentum distribution (PMD) of residual nuclei from (p,pN) reactions is caused by two distinct mechanisms: a steep fall-off on the high momentum side due to the phase volume effect reflecting energy and momentum conservation, and a long-ranged tail on the low momentum side due to the momentum shift of outgoing nucleons inside the attractive potential of the residual nucleus. The authors qualify that this analysis applies specifically to (p,pN) reactions in inverse kinematics at 100 and 200 MeV/nucleon, where only elastic breakup occurs.

## Method

The authors used the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with the eikonal approximation and forward scattering assumption to calculate the triple differential cross section and parallel momentum distribution for 14O(p,pn)13O and 14O(p,2p)13N reactions at 100 and 200 MeV/nucleon in inverse kinematics. They separated the phase volume effect from the distortion effect by comparing PWIA calculations (with and without phase volume) against full DWIA calculations, and by selectively setting the real or imaginary parts of the distorting potentials to zero for the outgoing nucleons.

## Key numbers

- For 14O(p,pn)13O at 100 MeV/nucleon: peak position P_cen = -92 MeV/c, FWHM Gamma = 266 MeV/c, Gamma_L = 182 MeV/c, Gamma_H = 84 MeV/c, asymmetry A_Gamma = 2.17
- For 14O(p,2p)13N at 100 MeV/nucleon: peak position P_cen = -31 MeV/c, FWHM Gamma = 178 MeV/c, Gamma_L = 104 MeV/c, Gamma_H = 74 MeV/c, asymmetry A_Gamma = 1.41
- The neutron separation energy of 14O is 23.2 MeV and the proton separation energy is 4.63 MeV
- The eikonal DWIA was validated against experimental triple differential cross section data for 12C(p,2p)11B at 392 MeV with energy transfer 142 MeV and momentum transfer 2.59 fm^-1, using spectroscopic factor S = 1.72 from (e,e'p) experiments
- The real part of the distorting potentials (V_1 and V_2) changed the integrated cross section by only about 5%, but the peak height changed by about 60% depending on the fixed energy used for evaluation

## Context

This work extends the understanding of PMD asymmetry beyond the Glauber model, which is restricted to symmetric PMD shapes and is not suitable for (p,pN) reactions due to its adiabatic approximation. The study builds on earlier work by Tosaka et al. (2002) using CDCC for 15C one-neutron removal, and by Flavigny et al. (2012) using the transfer to continuum method for 14O one-neutron removal at 53 MeV/nucleon. The present DWIA approach enables treatment of both neutron and proton knockout, unlike the transfer to continuum method which is restricted to neutron removal. The results enable future application to extract single-particle structure information from asymmetric PMD data in (p,pN) reactions at intermediate energies.
