# Dynamical coupled-channel approaches on a momentum lattice

> Dynamical coupled-channel approaches are a widely used tool in hadronic physics that allow to analyze different reactions and partial waves in a consistent way. In such approaches the basic interactions are derived within an effective Lagrangian framework and the resulting pseudo-potentials are then unitarized in a coupled-channel scattering equation. We propose a scheme that allows for a solution of the arising integral equation in discretized momentum space for periodic as well as twisted boundary conditions. This permits to study finite size effects as they appear in lattice QCD simulations. The new formalism, at this stage with a restriction to S-waves, is applied to coupled-channel models for the sigma(600), f0(980), and a0(980) mesons, and also for the Lambda(1405) baryon. Lattice spectra are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a scheme to solve dynamical coupled-channel scattering equations in discretized momentum space for both periodic and anti-periodic (twisted) boundary conditions, enabling the prediction of finite-volume lattice spectra from hadron-exchange models. The formalism is restricted to S-waves and applied to models that do not contain effective two-meson states from three-body channels.

## Method

The authors discretize the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation by replacing the continuous momentum integral with a sum over discrete lattice momenta according to periodic or anti-periodic boundary conditions on a simple cubic lattice. The resulting integral equation is solved by matrix inversion, with the discrete sampling points and integration weights determined by the multiplicities of lattice momentum neighbors, given by elliptic theta functions. The method is applied to existing hadron-exchange coupled-channel models for meson-meson (ππ/πη and ¯KK) and meson-baryon (πΛ, πΣ, ¯KN) systems, and the energy levels are obtained from the zeros of det(1 − VG).

## Key numbers

*   For the meson-meson model, the genuine f0(1400) resonance has a pole at 1346 ± i 249 MeV, as given in Table V of Janssen (1994).
*   The momentum cutoff for the discretized sums is chosen such that q_{i_max} ≈ 2.5 – 3 GeV, corresponding to distances of about 0.07 – 0.08 fm.
*   In the meson-baryon sector, the channels ¯KΔ, ¯K*N, and ¯K*Δ contribute only effectively below their respective thresholds at E < 1700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986; Luscher, 1990) for extracting phase shifts from lattice QCD to the case of dynamical coupled-channel models with hadron-exchange interactions, as developed by the Julich group (Krehl, 1999; Gasparyan, 2003; Doring, 2009). It enables the combined analysis of experimental data and lattice spectra within a single framework, and is complementary to chiral unitary approaches (Oller, 1997) that have been used for similar finite-volume studies (Bernard, 2010; ourmanu). The method is applied to models for the σ(600), f0(980), a0(980) mesons and the Λ(1405) baryon, and the predicted lattice spectra show avoided level crossings characteristic of resonances and thresholds.
