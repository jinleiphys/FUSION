# Workshop on Physics with Neutral Kaon Beam at JLab (KL2016) Mini-Proceedings

> The KL2016 Workshop is following the Letter of Intent LoI12-15-001 "Physics Opportunities with Secondary KL beam at JLab" submitted to PAC43 with the main focus on the physics of excited hyperons produced by the Kaon beam on unpolarized and polarized targets with GlueX setup in Hall D. Such studies will broaden a physics program of hadron spectroscopy extending it to the strange sector. The Workshop was organized to get a feedback from the community to strengthen physics motivation of the LoI and prepare a full proposal. Further details about the Workshop can be found on the web page of the conference: http://www.jlab.org/conferences/kl2016/index.html .

## Key claim

The workshop concluded that a secondary neutral kaon beam at JLab's Hall D could provide unique data on strange baryons (Sigma*, Lambda*, Xi*, Omega*), but that such a program would realistically require several years of lead time and would only be feasible after the GlueX experiment's primary photon-beam program and the installation of kaon identification detectors like DIRC bars and a RICH.

## Method

The workshop gathered community feedback on the Letter of Intent LoI12-15-001 "Physics Opportunities with Secondary KL beam at JLab" submitted to PAC43, which proposed using the GlueX setup in Hall D with a neutral kaon beam on unpolarized and polarized targets to study excited hyperons. The workshop consisted of invited talks covering the history of neutral kaon photoproduction, the Hall D complex capabilities, and theoretical motivations for strange baryon spectroscopy, with the goal of strengthening the physics case for a full proposal.

## Key numbers

- The designed electron beam energy for Hall D is E_e = 12 GeV, with a beam dump limited to 60 kW (5 microA at 12 GeV)
- The GlueX main spectrometer is based on a 2-T superconducting solenoid, 4 m long with 1.85 m bore diameter
- The spectrometer covers an angular range of 1 degree < theta < 120 degrees, with momentum resolution sigma_p/p ~ 1-3% depending on polar angle
- The electromagnetic calorimeters have energy resolution of about 7% at 1 GeV
- The main spectrometer is designed for photon beam rates below 100 MHz in the coherent peak, with the first stage of GlueX planned to run at a lower rate of 10 MHz
- The coherent bremsstrahlung peak provides up to ~40% average linear polarization at 9 GeV using a 3.4 mm diameter collimator located about 75 m downstream of the radiator

## Context

This workshop follows the Letter of Intent LoI12-15-001 submitted to PAC43 and builds on the GlueX experiment's capability for meson spectroscopy at JLab Hall D. The proposed program extends hadron spectroscopy into the strange sector, complementing the charged strange particle and hypernuclear program at J-PARC. The workshop aims to enable a full proposal for a secondary K_L beam facility that would be unique worldwide for studying strange baryons, particularly Xi* and Omega* resonances, and for providing K-pi, K-eta, and K-eta' final state interaction data essential for understanding chiral dynamics and the connection between constituent quark models and lattice QCD calculations.
