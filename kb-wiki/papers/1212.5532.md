# Nonsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics with realistic NN potentials

> The Schroedinger equation is solved for an A-nucleon system using an expansion of the wave function in nonsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics. Our approach is both an extension and a modification of the formalism developed by Gattobigio et al.. The extension consists in the inclusion of spin and isospin degrees of freedom such that a calculation with more realistic NN potential models becomes possible, whereas the modification allows a much simpler determination of the fermionic ground state. The approach is applied to four- and six-body nuclei (4He, 6Li) with various NN potential models. It is shown that the results for ground-state energy and radius agree well with those from the literature.

## Key claim

The paper extends the nonsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics (NSHH) method to include spin and isospin degrees of freedom, enabling calculations with realistic NN potentials for A=4 and A=6 nuclei, but the method is currently limited to central potentials for A=6 due to the non-parallel nature of the code.

## Method

The authors expand the A-nucleon wave function in a nonsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics basis that includes spin and isospin quantum numbers, using Laguerre polynomials for the hyperradial part. To identify the antisymmetric ground state, they add a term proportional to the Casimir operator of the permutation group to the Hamiltonian, so that the lowest eigenstate of the modified Hamiltonian is manifestly antisymmetric. The effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) formalism is used to accelerate convergence, and the method is applied to 4He and 6Li with various NN potentials including Volkov, MTI/III, Minnesota, AV4', and AV18.

## Key numbers

- For 4He with the Volkov potential, the converged EI-NSHH ground-state energy is -30.417 MeV with RMS radius 1.4900 fm (Kmax=16)
- For 4He with the MTI/III potential, the converged EI-NSHH ground-state energy is -30.693 MeV with RMS radius 1.4220 fm (Kmax=24)
- For 4He with the AV18 realistic potential, the converged EI-NSHH ground-state energy is -24.246 MeV with RMS radius 1.5170 fm (Kmax=22)
- For 6Li with the Volkov potential, the EI-NSHH ground-state energy is -66.577 MeV with RMS radius 1.5993 fm (Kmax=8)
- For 6Li with the MTI/III potential, the EI-NSHH ground-state energy is -36.269 MeV with RMS radius 2.1796 fm (Kmax=12)
- The number of NSHH basis functions (without hyperradial part) for 4He at Kmax=10 is 1008 for central potentials and 4746 for noncentral potentials
- The number of NSHH basis functions for 6Li at Kmax=10 is 514215 for central potentials and 7392960 for noncentral potentials

## Context

This work extends the NSHH formalism developed by Gattobigio et al. (2009, 2011) by adding spin and isospin degrees of freedom, which previously only allowed calculations with central, spin-isospin independent potentials like Volkov. The modification replaces the cumbersome projection operator method for identifying permutation symmetry with a simpler Casimir operator approach analogous to the Lawson method used in shell model calculations. The results for 4He and 6Li agree well with prior calculations using symmetrized HH methods (Barnea et al., 2000), the stochastic variational method (SVM), and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations by Wiringa and Pieper (2002), demonstrating that the NSHH approach can achieve comparable precision while potentially being more advantageous for systems beyond A=4.
