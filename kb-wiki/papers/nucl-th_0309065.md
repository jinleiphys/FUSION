# Precise method for the determination of the neutron electric form factor based on a relativistic analysis of the process $d(e,e'n)p

> We generalize the recoil polarization method for the determination of the proton form factor to the case of the disintegration of vector polarized deuterons by longitudinally polarized electrons, $\vec d(\vec e, e'n)p$. We suggest to measure for this reaction, in the kinematics of quasi-elastic $en$-scattering, the ratio $R_{xz}=A_x/A_z$ of the asymmetries induced by the $x$- and $z$-components of the deuteron vector polarization. In the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation the ratio $R_{xz}$ is sensitive to $G_{En}$ in a wide interval of momentum transfer squared, whereas it depends weakly on the details of the $np$-interaction and on the choice of the deuteron wave function. Moreover, in the range $0.5\le Q^2\le$1.5 GeV$^2$, the ratio $R_{xz}$ shows a smooth dependence on $Q^2$, making the analysis simpler.

## Key claim
The ratio of asymmetries \(R_{xz}=A_x/A_z\) in the reaction \(\vec d(\vec e, e'n)p\) is sensitive to the neutron electric form factor \(G_{En}\) in a wide interval of momentum transfer squared, while depending weakly on the details of the \(np\)-interaction and on the choice of the deuteron wave function, provided the measurement is performed in quasi-elastic \(en\)-scattering kinematics.

## Method
The authors generalize the recoil polarization method, previously applied to proton form factor measurements, to the electrodisintegration of vector-polarized deuterons by longitudinally polarized electrons. They compute the ratio \(R_{xz}=A_x/A_z\) of T-even asymmetries induced by the \(x\)- and \(z\)-components of the deuteron vector polarization within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), using Feynman diagrams that include one-nucleon exchange, deuteron exchange, and a contact diagram to ensure current conservation. Final-state interactions (FSI) are incorporated via a unitarization procedure that multiplies RIA multipole amplitudes by the appropriate \(np\) phase shifts from the analysis of Bystricky et al. (1987), avoiding nonrelativistic NN-potentials.

## Key numbers
- At \(Q^2 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio \(R_{xz}\) shows large sensitivity to \(G_{En}\) when comparing the Galster parametrization scaled by factors 0.5 and 1.5, with only a small effect of FSI and deuteron wave function at \(\vartheta^* \simeq 160^\circ\).
- At \(Q^2 = 1.0\) GeV\(^2\), the effects of FSI and deuteron wave function on \(R_{xz}\) are negligible, making extraction of \(G_{En}\) simpler.
- At \(Q^2 = 1.5\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio \(R_{xz}\) again shows negligible dependence on FSI and deuteron wave function, with smooth \(Q^2\) dependence in the range \(0.5 \le Q^2 \le 1.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The kinematical parameters for the JLab experiment E93-026 include electron beam energies of 2.721 GeV (at \(Q^2=0.5\) GeV\(^2\)) and 4.232 GeV (at \(Q^2=1.0\) and 1.5 GeV\(^2\)), with electron scattering angles of 15.8°, 14.5°, and 19.26°, respectively, and \(\epsilon \simeq 0.96\)–0.92.

## Context
This work extends the recoil polarization method, originally proposed by Rekalo (1968) for elastic \(ep\) scattering and realized by Jones (1999) and Gayou (2002) for the proton, to the deuteron target case for extracting the neutron electric form factor \(G_{En}\). It improves upon earlier approaches by using a relativistic impulse approximation that rigorously accounts for Fermi motion and Wigner rotation, and by treating FSI via \(np\) phase shifts rather than nonrelativistic potentials. The method is designed for experiments at Mainz and JLab (e.g., E93-026) and enables precise \(G_{En}\) extraction in the \(Q^2\) range 0.5–1.5 GeV\(^2\) with reduced model dependence, analogous to the successful proton form factor measurements.
