# The electromagnetic form factors of the proton in the timelike region

> The reactions ppbar -> e+e- and e+e- -> ppbar are analyzed in the near-threshold region. Specific emphasis is put on the role played by the interaction in the initial- or final antinucleon-nucleon state which is taken into account rigorously. For that purpose a recently published NNbar potential derived within chiral effective field theory and fitted to results of a new partial-wave analysis of ppbar scattering data is employed. Our results provide strong support for the conjecture that the pronounced energy dependence of the e+e- <-> ppbar cross section, seen in pertinent experiments, is primarily due to the ppbar interaction. Predictions for the proton electromagnetic form factors G_E and G_M in the timelike region, close to the NNbar threshold, and for spin-dependent observables are presented. The steep rise of the effective form factor for energies close to the ppbar threshold is explained solely in terms of the ppbar interaction. The corresponding experimental information is quantitatively described by our calculation.

## Key claim

The pronounced energy dependence of the $e^+e^- \leftrightarrow p\bar{p}$ cross section near threshold is primarily due to the $p\bar{p}$ interaction in the initial or final state, and the steep rise of the effective proton form factor can be explained solely by this interaction, assuming the bare electromagnetic form factors are constant and equal.

## Method

The authors employed a recently published $\bar{N}N$ potential derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), fitted to a new partial-wave analysis of $\bar{p}p$ scattering data, to rigorously compute the final-state interaction (FSI) in the $^3S_1$–$^3D_1$ partial waves. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the $\bar{N}N$ scattering amplitude and used it to dress the bare $\bar{N}N\gamma$ vertex functions via Eq. (13), assuming the bare electric and magnetic form factors $G_E^0$ and $G_M^0$ are real, constant, and equal, leaving a single free parameter for overall normalization. The dressed vertex functions were then inverted to obtain the timelike electromagnetic form factors $G_E$ and $G_M$, and all observables were computed within the one-photon exchange approximation.

## Key numbers

- The $\chi^2$/dof for the $e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}$ cross section fit up to $Q \approx 60$ MeV is 0.81–1.01 for the NLO chiral EFT interaction, 0.63–0.71 for the NNLO interaction, and 0.64 for the Jülich model A(OBE).
- The $\bar{N}N$ potential reproduces $\bar{p}p$ scattering phase shifts up to laboratory energies of $T_{lab} \approx 200$–250 MeV.
- The study is restricted to excess energies $Q = \sqrt{s} - 2M_p$ up to around 100 MeV, where the EFT interaction is applicable.
- The ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ drops slightly below 1 right above the $p\bar{p}$ threshold and then rises above 1 with increasing energy.
- The phase between $G_E$ and $G_M$ remains between $\pm 20$ degrees over the energy range considered.
- There is a systematic difference between $e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}$ and $p\bar{p} \to e^+e^-$ cross section data by a factor of about 1.47.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Sibirtsev et al., 2005) that used the Watson-Migdal approach and only the $^3S_1$ partial wave, which could only reproduce the rapid rise near threshold with deviations starting around 50 MeV excess energy. The present calculation improves upon that by including the $^3S_1$–$^3D_1$ coupling and a rigorous treatment of FSI, achieving quantitative agreement up to significantly higher energies. The results support the conjecture that the near-threshold enhancement is due to FSI rather than requiring exotic explanations such as $N\bar{N}$ bound states or unobserved meson resonances, analogous to the interpretation of near-threshold enhancements in $J/\psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}$ and other decays. The work enables predictions for spin-dependent observables (analyzing power $A_y$ and spin-correlation parameters $A_{ij}$) that have not yet been measured, and provides a framework for extracting the timelike proton form factors $G_E$ and $G_M$ near threshold.
