# Intruder band mixing in an ab initio description of 12Be

> The spectrum of 12Be exhibits exotic features, e.g., an intruder ground state and shape coexistence, normally associated with the breakdown of a shell closure. While previous phenomenological treatments indicated the ground state has substantial contributions from intruder configurations, it is only with advances in computational abilities and improved interactions that this intruder mixing is observed in ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) predictions. In this work, we extract electromagnetic observables and symmetry decompositions from the NCSM wave functions to demonstrate that the low-lying positive parity spectrum can be explained in terms of mixing of rotational bands with very different intrinsic structure coexisting within the low-lying spectrum. These observed bands exhibit an approximate SU(3) symmetry and are qualitatively consistent with Elliott model predictions.

## Key claim

The low-lying positive parity spectrum of 12Be can be explained by mixing of rotational bands with very different intrinsic structure, but this intruder mixing is only observed in ab initio no-core shell model predictions when combining computational advances with the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, and the extracted bands exhibit an approximate SU(3) symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using the MFDn code with the Daejeon16 internucleon interaction, solving the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation in a basis of antisymmetrized products of harmonic oscillator states up to Nmax=12 with hbar-omega=15 MeV. They extracted electromagnetic observables (radii, quadrupole moments, E2 and E0 transition strengths) and symmetry decompositions from the NCSM wave functions, then applied a two-state mixing analysis assuming vanishing E0 transitions between pure intruder and normal states to extract properties of the unmixed rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angles at Nmax=12: theta_{0+}=26.3 degrees and theta_{2+}=11.2 degrees for the two 0+ and two 2+ states respectively
- Experimental mixing angle deduced from E2 transitions: theta_exp approximately 18 degrees plus or minus 2 degrees
- Approximate experimental proton radius of the 0_2+ state: r_p(0_2+)=2.26 fm, extracted from the measured ground state proton radius and E0 transition using the experimental mixing angle
- Proton and neutron radii of the pure intruder band are more than 0.1 fm larger than the corresponding radii of the pure normal band at Nmax=12
- The slope of the rotational energy fit to pure normal states is more than 1.5 times larger than the slope for the intruder band, corresponding to a moment of inertia 1.5 times smaller for the normal band

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological treatments of 12Be (Barker 1976, Suzuki 1997, Gori 2004) that indicated intruder configurations in the ground state, and earlier ab initio calculations (Dufour 2010, Maris 2015) that were computationally limited to model spaces insufficient to reproduce the intruder nature. The results enable future studies of shape coexistence and band mixing in light nuclei from an ab initio perspective, and demonstrate that Elliott's SU(3) symmetry framework (Elliott 1958) can describe the emergent rotational bands, with the most deformed SU(3) states in the Nex=0 space [0(2,0)0] and Nex=2 space [2(6,2)0] qualitatively matching the observed normal and intruder bands respectively.
