# Some aspects of collisional sources for electroweak baryogenesis

> We consider the dynamics of fermions with a spatially varying mass which couple to bosons through a Yukawa interaction term and perform a consistent weak coupling truncation of the relevant kinetic equations. We then use a gradient expansion and derive the CP-violating source in the collision term for fermions which appears at first order in gradients. The collisional sources together with the semiclassical force constitute the CP-violating sources relevant for baryogenesis at the electroweak scale. We discuss also the absence of sources at first order in gradients in the scalar equation, and the limitations of the relaxation time approximation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a CP-violating source in the collision term for fermions at first order in gradients, but this source appears only in the first velocity moment (Euler fluid equation) and not in the vector current continuity equation; the authors also argue that the relaxation time approximation, when interpreted to contain a CP-violating source, yields an unphysical nonvanishing source in the vector continuity equation that does not vanish in the static limit.

## Method

The authors consider a model of chiral fermions with a spatially varying complex mass, coupled to bosons via a Yukawa interaction, and perform a consistent weak-coupling truncation of the kinetic equations derived from the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action in the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism. They use a gradient expansion to first order in derivatives, compute the one-loop self-energies, and derive the CP-violating source in the fermionic collision term by taking the real part of the spinorial trace and truncating at first order in gradients. The source is then integrated over momenta to obtain the contribution to the first velocity moment equation.

## Key numbers

* The collisional source in the first velocity moment equation is given by \(2\int_{\pm}\frac{d^4k}{(2\pi)^4}\frac{k_z}{\omega_0} {\cal C}_{\psi (0)} = \pm v_w y^2\frac{s|m|^2\theta'}{64\pi^3 T}{\cal I}_f(|m|,m_\phi)\), where the function \({\cal I}_f\) is evaluated numerically.
* For \(|m|\ll T\) the source behaves as \(\propto (|m|/T)^{3/2}\), and for \(|m|\gg T\) it is Boltzmann suppressed.
* The source magnitude drops drastically when approaching the mass threshold \(m_\phi = 2|m|\), as required for scalar particle decay and inverse decay processes.
* The equilibrium distribution function for fermions in the wall frame is \(n_0 = 1/(e^{\hat k_0/T}+1)\) with \(\hat k_0 = \gamma_w (k_0 + v_w k_z)\), where \(v_w\) is the wall velocity and \(\gamma_w = 1/\sqrt{1-v_w^2/c^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic derivation of CP-violating sources for electroweak baryogenesis presented in Paper I (Kainulainen, Prokopec, Schmidt, Weinstock, 2001) by computing the collisional source at first order in gradients, which complements the semiclassical force originally introduced for baryogenesis in two-Higgs doublet models by Joyce, Prokopec, and Turok (1994) and later adapted to the MSSM by Cline, Joyce, and Kainulainen (2000). The authors show that there is no CP-violating source for scalar particles (e.g., stops) at first order in gradients, contradicting the approach of Riotto (1998), and they argue that the relaxation time approximation as used in earlier work (e.g., Carena, Quiros, Riotto, Vilja, Wagner; Carena, Moreno, Quiros, Seco, Wagner) contains no CP-violating source when correctly interpreted. A full quantitative assessment requires a two-loop analysis of self-energies, which is stated to be work in progress.
