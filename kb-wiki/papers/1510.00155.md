# Near-threshold $J/ψ$ photoproduction off nuclei

> We study the $J/ψ$ photoproduction from nuclei near the kinematic threshold within the first collision model, based on the nuclear spectral function, for incoherent primary photon--nucleon charmonium creation processes. The model takes into account the final $J/ψ$ absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion, the formation length of $J/ψ$ mesons as well as the effect of their nuclear mean-field potential on these processes. We calculate the A dependences of the absolute and relative (transparency ratio) charmonium yields as well as its absolute and relative excitation functions within the different scenarios for the $J/ψN$ absorption cross section, for the $J/ψ$ formation length and for $J/ψ$ in-medium modification. We demonstrate that the studied observables, on the one hand, are not practically affected by the charmonium formation length and mass shift effects and, on the other hand, they are appreciably sensitive to the genuine $J/ψN$ absorption cross section at above threshold beam energies, which means that these observables can be useful to help determine the $J/ψN$ absorption cross section from the comparison of the results of our calculations with the future data from the experiments in the Hall C at the upgraded up to 12 GeV CEBAF facility. We also show that the absolute and relative excitation functions for $J/ψ$ subthreshold production in $γA$ reactions reveal some sensitivity to adopted in-medium modification scenarios for $J/ψ$ mesons. Therefore, such observables, measured in the subthreshold energy domain, may be an important tool to get valuable information on the charmonium in-medium properties in cold nuclear matter.

# Near-threshold J/ψ photoproduction off nuclei

## Key claim

The absolute and relative (transparency ratio) J/ψ yields from photon-nucleus reactions at incident photon energies of 9–11 GeV are practically unaffected by J/ψ formation length and mass shift effects, but are appreciably sensitive to the genuine J/ψ–nucleon absorption cross section; this sensitivity enables determination of that cross section from future CEBAF data, provided the measurements use heavy targets such as Pb where the differences between absorption scenarios exceed approximately 10–20%.

## Method

The authors extended a first-collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, previously applied to proton-nucleus reactions (Paryev and Kiselev, 2014), to describe incoherent primary photon–nucleon J/ψ production processes (γN → J/ψN) on nuclei near the kinematic threshold. The model incorporates target nucleon binding and Fermi motion via the nuclear spectral function, final-state J/ψ absorption with a distance-dependent effective cross section that accounts for the J/ψ formation length, and the effect of a nuclear mean-field potential that reduces the J/ψ mass inside the nucleus. Calculations were performed for target nuclei 12C, 27Al, 40Ca, 93Nb, 208Pb, and 238U at photon energies from 5 to 11 GeV, using five scenarios for the J/ψ mass shift at saturation density (V0 = 0, −25, −50, −100, −150 MeV) and three values of the genuine J/ψ–nucleon absorption cross section (3.5, 7, and 14 mb).

## Key numbers

- The free-space J/ψ photoproduction threshold on a nucleon at rest is 8.2 GeV.
- The parametrization of the elementary γN → J/ψN cross section used is σ = 11.1 (1 − x)² nb, where x = (s_thr − m_N²)/(s − m_N²).
- At Eγ = 11 GeV, the coherence length of the virtual c-cbar fluctuation is approximately 0.5 fm, much smaller than the nuclear radius.
- The J/ψ formation length at Eγ = 11 GeV is approximately 0.8 fm, based on an average J/ψ momentum of about 7.7 GeV/c.
- The total J/ψ production cross section at Eγ = 11 GeV for targets heavier than Al ranges from about 20 to 140 nb, depending on the absorption cross section scenario.
- The transparency ratio TA for Pb/C at Eγ = 11 GeV shows differences of approximately 15–20% between different J/ψN absorption cross section options for targets heavier than Nb.
- At subthreshold energies (Eγ ∼ 5–7 GeV), the transparency ratio for Pb/C is enhanced by a factor of about 1.05 when going from V0 = 0 to V0 = −25 MeV, by about 1.1 when going from V0 = −25 to V0 = −50 MeV, and by about 1.3 when going from V0 = −50 to V0 = −150 MeV.
- The total J/ψ production cross sections at subthreshold energies (Eγ ∼ 5–7 GeV) are in the range of 0.1 to 1000 pb.

## Context

This work extends the spectral-function-based first-collision model previously applied to near-threshold J/ψ production in proton-nucleus collisions (Paryev and Kiselev, 2014) to the cleaner electromagnetic probe of photon-nucleus reactions. The study is motivated by planned experiments at the upgraded 12 GeV CEBAF facility (Hall C) and complements existing J/ψ photoproduction data from Cornell (1975) at 9.3–11.8 GeV and SLAC (1975) at 13–21 GeV, as well as the null result from a subthreshold JLab experiment on carbon below 6 GeV. The calculations demonstrate that above-threshold observables can determine the J/ψ–nucleon absorption cross section, while subthreshold excitation functions can probe J/ψ in-medium mass shifts of −50 MeV or more, providing a tool to test theoretical predictions that the mass shift at normal nuclear matter density is either very small or approximately −20 MeV (due to weak coupling of charm quarks to the medium).
