# Nonperturbative Quantum Field Evolution

> We introduce a nonperturbative, first-principles approach to time-dependent problems in quantum field theory. In this approach, the time-evolution of quantum field configurations is calculated in real time and at the amplitude level. This method is particularly suitable for treating systems interacting with a time-dependent background field. As a test problem, we apply this approach to QED and study electron acceleration and the associated photon emission in a time- and space-dependent electromagnetic background field.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a nonperturbative, first-principles approach to time-dependent quantum field theory (tBLFQ) that computes real-time evolution at the amplitude level, but the demonstration is limited to a simplified QED test case where renormalization is neglected and the background field profile does not satisfy Maxwell's equations in vacuum.

## Method

The method extends Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) to time-dependent problems by solving the light-front Schrödinger equation in an interaction picture, where the time-independent QED Hamiltonian serves as the "main" part and the time-dependent external field interaction is treated as the "interacting" part. The eigenstates of the QED Hamiltonian are constructed by diagonalizing in a basis that uses plane waves for longitudinal degrees of freedom and 2D harmonic oscillator eigenstates for transverse degrees of freedom, with truncation to the lowest two Fock sectors (single electron and electron-photon). Time evolution is implemented numerically by decomposing the time-ordered evolution operator into small finite steps in light-front time, applied as sequential matrix-vector multiplications on the initial state.

## Key numbers

* The background electric field profile has peak amplitude \(E^3_0\) with longitudinal frequency \(l_\LCm\) and light-front time frequency \(l_\LCp\), and a finite light-front time duration \(\Delta x^\LCp\) imposed by theta functions.
* The longitudinal direction is compactified to a circle of length \(2L\), with longitudinal momentum discretized as \(P^\LCp=2\pi K/L\) where \(K\) is an integer or half-integer.
* The transverse basis truncation uses \(N_{\max}\) as the cutoff on the sum \(N_\alpha=\sum_i 2n_i+|m_i|+1\) over all Fock particles in a basis state.
* The longitudinal box length is set to \(L=2\pi\) MeV\(^{-1}\) and the 2D-HO scale parameter to \(b=0.511\) MeV, matching the physical electron mass \(m_e\).

## Context

This work extends the stationary BLFQ approach of Vary (2009) and Honkanen (2010) to time-dependent problems, enabling nonperturbative studies of processes such as nonlinear Compton scattering in strong background fields. The method is presented as an alternative to standard perturbative QED extended to strong backgrounds, and to other nonperturbative approaches such as those of Hebenstreit (2011). Future work requires incorporating renormalization (guided by the sector-dependent scheme of Karmanov (2008) and Zhao (2013)) and extending to more realistic background field profiles that satisfy Maxwell's equations.
