# Effects of Nuclear Potential on the Cumulants of Net-Proton and Net-Baryon Multiplicity Distributions in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 5\,\text{GeV}$

> We analyze the rapidity and transverse momentum dependence for the cumulants of the net-proton and net-baryon distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 5\,\text{GeV}$ with a microscopic hadronic transport (JAM) model. To study the effects of mean field potential and softening of equation of state (EoS) on the fluctuations of net-proton (baryon) in heavy-ion collisions, the calculations are performed with two different modes. The softening of EoS is realized in the model by implementing the attractive orbit in the two-body scattering to introduce a reduction pressure of the system. By comparing the results from the two modes with the results from default cascade, we find the mean field potential and softening of EoS have strong impacts on the rapidity distributions ($\text{d}N/\text{d}y$) and the shape of the net-proton (baryon) multiplicity distributions. The net-proton (baryon) cumulants and their ratios calculated from all of the three modes are with similar trends and show significant suppression with respect to unity, which can be explained by the presence of baryon number conservations. It indicates that the effects of mean field potential and softening of EoS might be not the ingredients that are responsible to the observed strong enhancement in the most central Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC.

## Key claim

The mean field potential and softening of the equation of state (EoS) implemented in the JAM hadronic transport model do not produce the strong enhancement of the net-proton cumulant ratio κσ² above unity that was observed by the STAR experiment in central Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV; instead, all three simulation modes (cascade, mean field, and attractive scattering orbit) yield similar suppression trends dominated by baryon number conservation.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transportation Model) hadronic transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 5 GeV in three modes: default cascade, cascade with a Skyrme-type density-dependent and Lorentzian-type momentum-dependent mean field potential, and cascade with an attractive scattering orbit that reduces the system pressure to simulate softening of the equation of state. They computed the cumulants C₁ through C₄ and their ratios (σ²/M, Sσ, κσ²) for net-proton and net-baryon multiplicity distributions as functions of rapidity acceptance Δy and transverse momentum cuts, focusing on the 0-5% most central collisions.

## Key numbers

- The study is performed at √s_NN = 5 GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The mean field potential yields a smaller dN/dy magnitude than cascade around mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5).
- The attractive scattering orbit produces a narrower dN/dy distribution and significant enhancement relative to cascade at mid-rapidity.
- All cumulant ratios show suppression with respect to unity (the Poisson expectation) when rapidity acceptance Δy or pT coverage is enlarged.
- The observed strong enhancement of κσ² above unity in central Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV from STAR cannot be reproduced by the JAM model with these non-critical effects.

## Context

This work extends baseline calculations for net-proton fluctuations using transport models such as UrQMD and AMPT, which also fail to describe the STAR enhancement at 7.7 GeV. The authors compare their dynamic approach with a static Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculation by Kenji Morita (2015) that found κσ² suppression from the vector field and enhancement from liquid-gas phase transition at low temperature (T = 21 MeV) and high baryon chemical potential (μB = 906 MeV). The study provides a non-critical baseline for future critical point searches at the CBM experiment at GSI and J-PARC at JAEA/KEK.
