# Nucleon-Nucleon Effective Field Theory Without Pions

> Nuclear processes involving momenta much below the mass of the pion may be described by an effective field theory in which the pions do not appear as explicit degrees of freedom. The effects of the pion and all other virtual hadrons are reproduced by the coefficients of gauge-invariant local operators involving the nucleon field. Nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shift data constrains many of the coefficients that appear in the effective Lagrangean but at some order in the expansion coefficients enter that must be constrained by other observables. We compute several observables in the two-nucleon sector up to next-to-next-to leading order in the effective field theory without pions, or to the order at which a counterterm involving four-nucleon field operators is encountered. Effective range theory is recovered from the effective field theory up to the order where relativistic corrections enter or where four-nucleon-external current local operators arise. For the deuteron magnetic moment, quadrupole moment and the $np\to dγ$ radiative capture cross section a four-nucleon-one-photon counterterm exists at next-to-leading order. The electric polarizability and electric charge form factor of the deuteron are determined up to next-to-next-to-leading order, which includes the first appearance of relativistic corrections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory without explicit pion degrees of freedom (denoted EFT(π̸)) reproduces effective range theory for nucleon-nucleon interactions up to the order where relativistic corrections or four-nucleon-external-current local operators enter, with the expansion parameter being the ratio of external momentum to the pion mass.

## Method

The authors constructed a pionless effective field theory for two-nucleon systems using gauge-invariant local operators involving only nucleon fields, with coefficients determined by matching to nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shift data from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis. They computed observables in the two-nucleon sector up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the momentum expansion, treating the large scattering lengths as scaling like Q^{-1} and expanding about the deuteron pole rather than zero momentum. The theory was renormalized using power divergence subtraction (PDS) at a scale μ = m_π.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.224575 MeV
- Inverse deuteron pole location: γ_t^{-1} = 4.318946 fm
- Effective range in spin-triplet channel: ρ_d = 1.764 fm
- Shape parameter: w_2 = 0.389 fm^3
- Leading S-wave contact interaction coefficient: C_{0,-1}^{(³S₁)} = -5.586 fm^2 (at μ = m_π)
- Next-to-leading S-wave coefficient: C_{0,0}^{(³S₁)} = 0.559 fm^2
- Next-to-next-to-leading S-wave coefficient: C_{0,1}^{(³S₁)} = -0.055 fm^2
- Leading S-D mixing coefficient: C_0^{(sd)} = -4.57 fm^4 (at μ = m_π)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear physics pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for the two-nucleon system with dynamical pions. The pionless theory provides a systematic framework for very low-momentum processes (|k| ≪ m_π) and recovers effective range theory (Bethe, 1949) while including relativistic corrections and external current interactions. The results enable matching between QCD and low-energy nuclear observables, and provide analytic expressions that any complete theory (including potential models or chiral EFT with pions) must reproduce.
