# Precise determination of the strangeness magnetic moment of the nucleon

> By combining the constraints of charge symmetry with new chiral extrapolation techniques and recent low mass lattice QCD simulations of the individual quark contributions to the magnetic moments of the nucleon octet, we obtain a precise determination of the strange magnetic moment of the proton. The result, namely G_M^s = -0.046 +/- 0.019 mu_N, is consistent with the latest experimental measurements but an order of magnitude more precise. This poses a tremendous challenge for future experiments.

## Key claim
By combining charge symmetry constraints with finite-range-regularized chiral perturbation theory extrapolations of lattice QCD data, the authors obtain a precise value for the strange magnetic moment of the nucleon that is an order of magnitude more precise than existing experimental measurements, but the result depends on an estimate of the ratio of strange to down sea-quark loop contributions.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations of the valence u-quark contributions to the magnetic moments of the proton, neutron, Σ⁺, and Ξ⁰ using the Fat Link Irrelevant Clover (FLIC) fermion action on 400 20³ × 40 mean-field O(a²)-improved Lüscher-Weisz gauge configurations with lattice spacing 0.128 fm. They extrapolated these lattice results to the physical pion mass using finite-range-regularized heavy-baryon quenched chiral perturbation theory (FRR QχPT) with a dipole regulator, then corrected the quenched results to obtain the valence u-quark contributions in full QCD by removing quenched η′ contributions and adjusting π and K loop coefficients. The ratios u^p/u^Σ and u^n/u^Ξ were then combined with experimental octet baryon magnetic moments and an estimate of the strange-to-down sea-quark loop ratio to extract G_M^s via charge-symmetry relations.

## Key numbers
- G_M^s = -0.046 ± 0.019 μ_N (strange magnetic moment of the nucleon, in nuclear magnetons)
- u^p/u^Σ = 1.092 ± 0.030 (ratio of valence u-quark contributions to proton and Σ⁺ magnetic moments)
- u^n/u^Ξ = 1.254 ± 0.124 (ratio of valence u-quark contributions to neutron and Ξ⁰ magnetic moments)
- ^ℓR_d^s = 0.139 ± 0.042 (ratio of strange to down sea-quark loop contributions, estimated with dipole mass parameter varying between 0.6 and 1.0 GeV)
- Lattice spacing: 0.128 fm (determined by Sommer scale r₀ = 0.50 fm)
- Pion masses as low as 0.3–0.4 GeV in the quenched QCD simulations

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of the strange magnetic moment that gave values ranging from -0.28 ± 0.10 (Mathur, 2000) to +0.05 ± 0.06 (Lewis, 2002), and resolves the confusion among quark model predictions by providing a precise theoretical determination. The result is consistent with experimental measurements from JLab (HAPPEX, Aniol, 2000) and MIT-Bates (SAMPLE, Spayde, 2003) but an order of magnitude more precise, setting a challenge for upcoming experiments including A4 at Mainz, G0 at JLab, and HAPPEX2 at JLab. The chiral extrapolation technique using finite-range regularization (Young, 2002; Young, 2004) enables reliable connection of lattice data at unphysical quark masses to the physical regime.
