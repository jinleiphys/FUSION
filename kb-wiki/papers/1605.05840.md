# Double vector meson production in photon - hadron interactions at hadronic colliders

> In this paper we analyse the double vector meson production in photon -- hadron ($γh$) interactions at $pp/pA/AA$ collisions and present predictions for the $ρρ$, $J/ΨJ/Ψ$ and $ρJ/Ψ$ production considering the double scattering mechanism. We estimate the total cross sections and rapidity distributions at LHC energies and compare our results with the predictions for the double vector meson production in $γγ$ interactions at hadronic colliders. We present predictions for the different rapidity ranges probed by the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS and LHCb Collaborations. Our results demonstrate that the $ρρ$ and $J/ΨJ/Ψ$ production in $PbPb$ collisions is dominated by the double scattering mechanism, while the two - photon mechanism dominates in $pp$ collisions. Moreover, our results indicate that the analysis of the $ρJ/Ψ$ production at LHC can be useful to constrain the double scattering mechanism.

## Key claim

The double scattering mechanism (DSM) dominates double vector meson production in PbPb collisions for ρρ and J/ΨJ/Ψ final states, while the two-photon mechanism dominates in pp collisions; the analysis of ρJ/Ψ production at the LHC can be useful to constrain the double scattering mechanism, but the authors note that for pp collisions the pomeron-pomeron interaction may dominate by more than three orders of magnitude over the photon-induced DSM contribution.

## Method

The authors calculate double vector meson production in photon-hadron (γh) interactions at pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions using the color dipole formalism, where the double scattering cross section is expressed as the product of single vector meson photoproduction cross sections integrated over impact parameter. The single vector meson production is described using the bCGC model for the proton and a Glauber-Gribov based model for nuclei, with the Gauss-LC model for vector meson wave functions. The results are compared with predictions for double vector meson production in γγ interactions from the same authors' previous work (Goncalves et al., 2016), using the same dipole formalism and wave function approach.

## Key numbers

- For J/ΨJ/Ψ production in PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV: DSM total cross section = 1054.951 nb; γγ total cross section = 658.589 nb
- For ρρ production in PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV: DSM total cross section = 29.421 mb; γγ total cross section = 1.973 mb
- For ρJ/Ψ production in PbPb at √s = 5.5 TeV: DSM total cross section = 0.35 mb
- For J/ΨJ/Ψ production in pp at √s = 14 TeV: DSM total cross section = 7.256 × 10⁻⁴ pb; γγ total cross section = 0.4793 pb
- For ρρ production in pp at √s = 14 TeV: DSM total cross section = 7.083 pb; γγ total cross section = 237.006 pb
- For J/ΨJ/Ψ production in PbPb at √s = 2.76 TeV in the ATLAS/CMS rapidity range (−2 < y₁,₂ < 2): DSM = 234.94 nb
- For ρρ production in PbPb at √s = 2.76 TeV in the LHCb rapidity range (2 < y₁,₂ < 4.5): DSM = 0.93 mb

## Context

This work extends the analysis of double vector meson production from γγ interactions (originally proposed by Goncalves et al., 2013, 2014, 2015 and revisited by Goncalves et al., 2016) to the double scattering mechanism in γh interactions, following the approach for double ρ production in heavy ion collisions proposed by Klein (2001) and recently discussed by Mariola (2015). The results are compared with the two-photon mechanism predictions from the same authors' previous work (Goncalves et al., 2016). The paper provides the first predictions for double vector meson production via the double scattering mechanism in pp and pA collisions, and presents cross sections for the rapidity ranges probed by the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb Collaborations, enabling future experimental tests of the double scattering mechanism at the LHC.
