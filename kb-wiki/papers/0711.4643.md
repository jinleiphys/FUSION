# The Friedberg-Lee model at finite temperature and density

> The Friedberg-Lee model is studied at finite temperature and density. By using the finite temperature field theory, the effective potential of the Friedberg-Lee model and the bag constant $B(T)$ and $B(T,μ)$ have been calculated at different temperatures and densities. It is shown that there is a critical temperature $T_{C}\simeq 106.6 \mathrm{MeV}$ when $μ=0 \mathrm{MeV}$ and a critical chemical potential $μ\simeq 223.1 \mathrm{MeV}$ for fixing the temperature at $T=50 \mathrm{MeV}$. We also calculate the soliton solutions of the Friedberg-Lee model at finite temperature and density. It turns out that when $T\leq T_{C}$ (or $μ\leq μ_C$), there is a bag constant $B(T)$ (or $B(T,μ)$) and the soliton solutions are stable. However, when $T>T_{C}$ (or $μ>μ_C$) the bag constant $B(T)=0 \mathrm{MeV}$ (or $B(T,μ)=0 \mathrm{MeV}$) and there is no soliton solution anymore, therefore, the confinement of quarks disappears quickly.

## Key claim

The Friedberg-Lee model predicts a first-order deconfinement phase transition at a critical temperature of approximately 106.6 MeV (at zero chemical potential) and a critical chemical potential of approximately 223.1 MeV (at fixed temperature 50 MeV), above which the bag constant vanishes and soliton solutions no longer exist, implying the disappearance of quark confinement.

## Method

The authors used finite-temperature field theory (the imaginary-time formalism of Dolan and Jackiw) to compute the one-loop effective potential of the Friedberg-Lee model at finite temperature and chemical potential, including fermionic and bosonic thermal contributions while ignoring quantum sigma-loop corrections. They then solved the coupled nonlinear differential equations for the sigma field and quark Dirac spinors in the mean-field approximation, replacing the classical potential with the temperature- and density-dependent effective potential, to obtain soliton solutions for a three-quark system at various temperatures and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_C approximately 106.6 MeV at chemical potential mu = 0 MeV
- Critical chemical potential: mu_C approximately 223.1 MeV at fixed temperature T = 50 MeV
- Temperature at which the second minimum of the effective potential disappears: approximately 129 MeV (at mu = 0 MeV)
- Chemical potential at which the second minimum disappears: approximately 354 MeV (at T = 50 MeV)
- Model parameters used: a = 17.70 fm^{-2}, b = -1457.4 fm^{-1}, c = 20000, g = 12.16

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Friedberg-Lee model at finite temperature and density, particularly those by Reinhardt (1985), Gao (1992), Li (1987), and Wang (1989), which focused mainly on the effective potential but seldom considered soliton configurations at high temperature and density. It also builds on the authors' prior work on the improved quark mass density-dependent model (Mao, 2006), which found a similar critical temperature near 110 MeV. The results enable detailed study of the deconfinement phase transition and the behavior of soliton bags as functions of temperature and chemical potential, though the authors note that their predicted first-order phase transition across the full phase diagram differs from lattice gauge theory predictions, which suggest more complex phase structure including a possible critical point.
