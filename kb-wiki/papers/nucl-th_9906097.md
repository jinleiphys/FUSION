# Coherent electromagnetic heavy ion reactions: (1) exact treatment of pair production and ionization; (2) mutual Coulomb dissociation

> Some recent theoretical results on coherent electromagnetic processes in ultrarelativistic heavy ion reactions are surveyed.

# Coherent electromagnetic heavy ion reactions: (1) exact treatment of pair production and ionization; (2) mutual Coulomb dissociation

## Key claim

Exact solutions of the Dirac equation in the delta-function gauge show that bound-electron positron pair production is slightly reduced (2-3%) relative to perturbation theory at RHIC energies, contrary to earlier coupled-channels calculations that had predicted enhancements; continuum pair production cross sections are identical to perturbation theory, but multiple-pair production probabilities are reduced.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation exactly at the interaction point using a delta-function potential in the singular gauge, derived from the ultrarelativistic Coulomb potential by ignoring terms of order (b - rho)/gamma^2. For continuum pair production, the amplitude is evaluated in closed form using Green's function methods in both the singular gauge and the light-cone gauge, with an infrared cutoff of omega^2/gamma^2. Mutual Coulomb dissociation cross sections are calculated using the Weizsacker-Williams formalism with measured photo-dissociation data as input.

## Key numbers

- Bound-electron positron pair production cross section for 100 GeV x 100 GeV Au + Au at RHIC: about 110 barns
- Continuum electron-positron pair production cross section for the same system: 34,000 barns
- Coulomb dissociation cross section for the same system: about 95 barns
- Ionization cross section for a single electron on one ion: about 100,000 barns
- Bound-electron pair production at RHIC energies (gamma = 23,000): 2-3% reduction from perturbation theory, superseding earlier 10% enhancement estimates
- Measured ionization cross section for Pb on Au at CERN SPS: 42,000 barns (Krause et al.)
- Anholt and Becker calculation for Pb + Pb ionization (with screening): about 64,000 barns
- Present calculation for Pb + Pb ionization (without screening): about 58,000 barns
- Total nuclear plus Coulomb correlated dissociation cross section for Au + Au at RHIC: 11.0 barns (compared to 7 barns pure hadronic)
- Forward-backward single neutron coincidence cross section for Au + Au at RHIC: 0.45 barns
- Forward-backward single neutron coincidence cross section for Pb + Pb at LHC: 0.53 barns
- Total nuclear plus Coulomb correlated dissociation for Pb + Pb at LHC: 14.8 barns
- Dissociation of one ion at RHIC: 102 barns; at LHC: 227 barns
- Dissociation of one ion to one neutron at RHIC: 49 barns; at LHC: 106 barns
- One neutron in one detector and any neutral in the other at RHIC: 1.35 barns; at LHC: 1.88 barns

## Context

This work extends the classic Landau and Lifshitz formula for continuum pair production and the earlier perturbative calculations of Anholt and Becker (1985) for ionization, showing that exact treatment yields results consistent with perturbation theory for continuum pairs but with modifications for multiple pairs and bound-electron pairs. The exact delta-function solution supersedes earlier nonperturbative coupled-channels calculations by Rumrich et al. (1991) that had found large enhancements at low gamma, and the improved coupled-channels results at RHIC energies by Bertulani and collaborators. The mutual Coulomb dissociation calculations provide a practical luminosity monitoring scheme for RHIC and LHC, using the Weizsacker-Williams formalism with measured photo-dissociation data, as proposed by Baur and collaborators. The results enable reliable predictions for luminosity monitors at heavy ion colliders and clarify the role of higher-order effects in electromagnetic processes.
