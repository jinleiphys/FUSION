# The QCD equation of state at nonzero densities: lattice result

> In this letter we give the equation of state of QCD at finite temperatures and densities. The recently proposed overlap improving multi-parameter reweighting technique is used to determine observables at nonvanishing chemical potentials. Our results are obtained by studying n_f=2+1 dynamical staggered quarks with semi-realistic masses on N_t=4 lattices.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of the equation of state at nonzero temperature and nonzero chemical potential, using the overlap-improving multi-parameter reweighting technique on \(N_t=4\) lattices with 2+1 dynamical staggered quarks at semi-realistic masses, but the results are raw lattice values that must be multiplied by continuum correction factors (\(c_p=0.518\), \(c_\mu=0.446\)) to be interpreted as continuum estimates.

## Method

The authors generate an ensemble of QCD configurations at zero chemical potential (\(\mu=0\)) and nonzero temperature, then apply Ferrenberg-Swendsen type reweighting factors to obtain observables at nonzero \(\mu\) and lowered temperature, following the best weight lines on the \(\mu\)–\(\beta\) plane to maximize overlap. They use \(N_t=4\) lattices with spatial sizes \(N_s=8,10,12\) and extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit, with 16 different \(\beta\) values covering \(T/T_c=0.8\) to 3, and perform vacuum subtraction using \(T=0\) lattices of size \(24\cdot14^3\). The pressure is obtained by integrating derivatives of \(\log Z\) with respect to \(\beta\), \(m\), and \(\mu\) along chosen integration paths.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses: \(m_{ud} \approx 65\) MeV and \(m_s \approx 135\) MeV, with pion-to-rho mass ratio \(m_\pi/m_\rho \approx 0.66\)
- Lattice spacing at \(T_c\): \(a \approx 0.28\) fm
- Continuum correction factor for pressure at \(\mu=0\): \(c_p = 0.518\)
- Continuum correction factor for \(\Delta p\): \(c_\mu = 0.446\)
- Baryonic chemical potentials studied: \(\mu_B = 100, 210, 330, 410, 530\) MeV
- The maximal \(\mu\) value scales with volume as \(\mu_{\text{max}} \cdot a \sim (N_t \cdot N_s^3)^{-0.25}\)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD equation of state, previously available only at \(\mu=0\) (Gottlieb 1996, Karsch 2000, AliKhan 2001), to nonzero densities using the multi-parameter reweighting technique introduced by Fodor and Katz (2001). The results enable phenomenological applications in heavy ion collisions and neutron star physics, and the phase diagram and critical endpoint location had been given earlier by the same authors (Fodor and Katz 2001, Fodor and Katz 2002). Future work should use smaller lattice spacings and quark masses to approach the continuum limit.
