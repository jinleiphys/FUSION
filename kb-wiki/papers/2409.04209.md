# Deuteron, triton, helium-3 and hypertriton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions via stochastic multi-particle reactions

> The production of light nuclei in heavy -ion collisions is an excellent probe for studying the phase diagram of quantum chromodynamics and for the search of a critical end point. In this work we apply a hybrid approach in which we study the light nuclei production in the afterburner stage of central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=7.7$, 14.5 and 19.6 GeV. In this stage, light nuclei are produced dynamically in $4\leftrightarrow 2$ catalysis reactions. A comparison of the dynamic production and a coalescence approach is presented for transverse momentum spectra of deuterons, tritons, $^3\rm He$ nuclei and hypertritons and ratios of light nuclei yields. A good agreement with the experimentally measured yield of nuclei is found and we proceed to further investigate the production mechanisms of light nuclei by calculating the rates of the important channels for the formation and disintegration. We find that the afterburner stage is essential for the description of light nuclei formation in heavy-ion collisions, as light nuclei undergo a large number of interactions.

## Key claim

The dynamic production of light nuclei (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and hypertritons) via stochastic multi-particle 4↔2 catalysis reactions in the afterburner stage of a hybrid hydrodynamic+transport model yields a good description of experimental transverse momentum spectra and single yield ratios at RHIC Beam Energy Scan II energies, but the double ratio NtNp/Nd² shows no energy dependence in the model because critical fluctuations are not implemented.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid approach combining (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC code) with the SMASH hadronic transport model for the afterburner stage, switching at an energy density of 0.26 GeV/fm³ via Cooper-Frye sampling. Light nuclei were produced dynamically through stochastic multi-particle 4↔2 catalysis reactions (Xnnp↔Xt, Xnpp↔XHe³, XnpΛ↔XH³_L with X=π,N) using cross sections extrapolated from deuteron-catalyst cross sections scaled by A/2 for A=3 nuclei. Results were compared to a coalescence approach using phase-space distance cutoffs of Δp=300 MeV and Δr=3 fm, applied at each particle's last interaction time.

## Key numbers

- Central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7, 14.5, and 19.6 GeV for the 10% most central events
- Switch from hydrodynamics to transport at energy density ε = 0.26 GeV/fm³
- Coalescence cutoffs: Δp = 300 MeV and Δr = 3 fm
- Box validation at T = 150 MeV in (10 fm)³ volume with 60 nucleons and 60 pions initially
- Calculations performed with 10 test particles per real particle

## Context

This work extends the stochastic multi-particle reaction approach for deuterons from Staudenmaier (2021) to include A=3 nuclei (tritons, helium-3, hypertritons) via 4↔2 catalysis reactions. It builds on earlier SMASH afterburner studies of deuteron production at LHC energies by Oliinychenko (2018) and at RHIC BES energies by Oliinychenko (2020). The results are compared to coalescence calculations similar to those by Sombun (2018) and Deng (2020) using UrQMD, and to experimental data from the STAR collaboration. The work enables future studies of critical fluctuations in light nuclei ratios, as the model currently shows no energy dependence in the NtNp/Nd² double ratio.
