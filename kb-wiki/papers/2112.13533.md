# QCD knows new quarks

> We find that a big gap between indicators for the breaking strengths of the global chiral SU(2) and U(1) axial symmetries in QCD of the Standard Model (SM) can be interpreted as a new fine-tuning problem. This may thus imply calling for a class of Beyond the SM, which turns out to favor having a new chiral symmetry and the associated massless new quark insensitive to the chiral SU(2) symmetry for the lightest up and down quarks, so that the fine-tuning is relaxed. Our statistical estimate shows that QCD of the SM is by more than 300 standard deviations off the desired parameter space, which is free from the fine-tuning, and the significance will be greater as the lattice measurements on the QCD hadron observables get more accurate. As one viable candidate, we introduce a dark QCD model with massless new quarks, which can survive current experimental, cosmological, and astrophysical limits, and also leave various phenomenological and cosmological consequences, to be probed in the future. This is a new indication from QCD, which gives a new avenue to deeper understand QCD, and provides a new guideline to consider going beyond the SM.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new fine-tuning problem in QCD: a large gap between the chiral SU(2) and U(1) axial symmetry breaking strengths, quantified by susceptibilities, which is more than 300 standard deviations away from a fine-tuning-free parameter space. The authors propose that this fine-tuning can be relaxed by introducing a new chiral symmetry and associated massless new quarks that are insensitive to the chiral SU(2) symmetry for up and down quarks.

## Method

The authors used the anomalous Ward identities for the chiral SU(3) symmetry to relate the chiral SU(2) susceptibility, the U(1) axial susceptibility, and the topological susceptibility. Since lattice QCD has not measured these three susceptibilities simultaneously, the authors employed a three-flavor Nambu-Jona Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective low-energy QCD description, fitting its five parameters to lattice QCD data on hadronic observables (pion decay constant, pion mass, kaon mass, eta mass, and eta-prime mass) using a least-chi-squared test. The best-fit model was then used to compute the three susceptibilities and the fine-tuning estimator R.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit NJL model predicts the chiral SU(2) susceptibility chi_chiral = (2.2784 +/- 0.0026) x 10^5 MeV^2, the U(1) axial susceptibility chi_axial = (4.8597 +/- 0.0077) x 10^6 MeV^2, and the topological susceptibility term chi_top/m_l^2 = (-1.158 +/- 0.064) x 10^6 MeV^2.
* The topological susceptibility from the best-fit model is chi_top = (0.025 +/- 0.002) /fm^4, which is consistent with lattice QCD results of 0.019(9)/fm^4 (Bonati 2015) and 0.0245(24)_stat(03)_flow(12)_cont/fm^4 (Borsanyi 2016).
* The statistical estimate shows that QCD of the Standard Model is more than 300 standard deviations away from the desired parameter space free from the fine-tuning.

## Context

This work extends the concept of fine-tuning problems in the Standard Model, such as the gauge hierarchy problem and the strong CP problem, to a new domain within QCD itself. The authors argue that the fine-tuning is distinct from the well-understood chiral symmetry explanation for the small pion mass and the isospin symmetry explanation for the proton-neutron mass difference. The paper proposes that a dark QCD model with massless new quarks, protected by a new chiral symmetry, can resolve this fine-tuning while surviving current experimental, cosmological, and astrophysical limits, opening a new avenue for beyond-the-Standard-Model physics.
