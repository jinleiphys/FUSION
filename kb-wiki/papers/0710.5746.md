# Dynamics, Symmetries and Hadron Properties

> We provide a snapshot of Dyson-Schwinger equation applications to the theory and phenomenology of hadrons. Exact results for pseudoscalar mesons are highlighted, with details relating to the U_A(1) problem. Calculated masses of the lightest J=0,1 states are discussed. We recapitulate upon studies of nucleon properties and give a perspective on the contribution of quark orbital angular momentum to the spin of a nucleon at rest.

# Dynamics, Symmetries and Hadron Properties

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) methods can provide a systematic, symmetry-preserving framework for hadron phenomenology, but the rainbow-ladder truncation is quantitatively reliable only for ground-state pseudoscalar and vector mesons; for scalar, axial-vector, and exotic channels, higher-order contributions are essential and the leading-order truncation is not expected to be accurate.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for dressed-quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using a renormalization-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation. For nucleons, a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation is employed, based on the observation that the same interaction which describes mesons also generates color-antitriplet diquark correlations (scalar $0^+$ and axial-vector $1^+$), with binding effected by iterated exchange between bystander and diquark-participant quarks. The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is used to derive exact results for neutral pseudoscalar mesons and the $U_A(1)$ problem.

## Key numbers
- Critical current-quark mass for which the quark mass function ceases to have a chiral expansion: the corresponding pion-like meson mass is $m_{\bar g f}^{0^-} = 0.45$ GeV; chiral perturbation theory is only valid for $(m_{\bar g f}^{0^-})^2 \lesssim 0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- Rainbow-ladder DSE truncation masses for lightest $J=0,1$ states (GeV): $\bar u u$ $0^{-+}$ = 139, $1^{--}$ = 740, $0^{++}$ = 670, $1^{+-}$ = 830, $1^{++}$ = 900, $0^{--}$ = 860, $1^{-+}$ = 1000, $0^{+-}$ = 1040; $\bar s s$ $0^{-+}$ = 695, $1^{--}$ = 1065, $0^{++}$ = 1080, $1^{+-}$ = 1165, $1^{++}$ = 1240, $0^{--}$ = 1170, $1^{-+}$ = 1310, $0^{+-}$ = 1385.
- Pole position for the scalar meson from two-pion intermediate state analysis: $\sqrt{s_\sigma} = (0.578 - i\,0.311)$ GeV.
- Diquark correlation masses: $m_{[ud]_{0^+}} = 0.7 - 0.8$ GeV; $m_{(uu)_{1^+}} = m_{(ud)_{1^+}} = m_{(dd)_{1^+}} = 0.9 - 1.0$ GeV.
- Electromagnetic size of scalar diquark: $r_{[ud]_{0^+}} \approx 0.7$ fm; axial-vector diquark radius: $r_{(ud)_{1^+}} \sim 0.8$ fm.
- Calculated proton form factor ratio $\mu_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)$ passes through zero at $Q^2 \approx 6.5$ GeV$^2$.
- Neutron electric radius relation: $\mu_n G_E^n(Q^2)/G_M^n(Q^2) = - (r_n^2/6) Q^2$ near $Q^2 = 0$.
- Contribution to nucleon normalization from $(L=0) \otimes (L=0)$ terms is only 37% of the total; the largest single entry (0.62) is from ${\cal S}_1 \otimes {\cal S}_1$ (scalar diquark with bystander quark carrying all spin).
- Predicted $\eta$–$\eta^\prime$ mixing angles: $\sim -15^\circ$; $\pi^0$–$\eta$ angles: $\sim 1^\circ$.
- Strong neutron-proton mass difference: $0.75\,(m_d - m_u)$.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the DSE approach to hadron physics developed by Maris, Roberts, and collaborators (Maris 1997, Maris 1999) for mesons, and by Cahill, Oettel, and others (Cahill 1988, Oettel 1998) for nucleons via the Faddeev equation with diquark correlations. The exact treatment of the $U_A(1)$ problem via the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity extends the analysis of Dorokhov (2003). The results enable systematic improvement of truncation schemes beyond rainbow-ladder, particularly for scalar, axial-vector, and exotic meson channels, and provide a framework for calculating nucleon electromagnetic form factors and spin structure that can be compared with lattice QCD and experimental programs at facilities such as JLab.
