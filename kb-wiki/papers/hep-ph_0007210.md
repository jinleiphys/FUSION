# The 't Hooft interaction at finite T and $μ$: Beyond the mean field theory

> We use the N-quantum approach (NQA) to quantum field theory to construct a solution of the two flavor effective instanton induced `t Hooft interaction model valid for any temperature (T) and chemical potential ($μ$) beyond the mean field theory. The model contains only the $\bar{q}q$ channels. In constructing this solution we calculate the masses, widths and coupling constants of bound $σ$ and resonant color $\bar{3}$ scalar diquark states. We find that the chemical potential induced by the interaction cancel exactly for all values of T in contrast to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Our method can also be extended to include the qq channels in the model Lagrangian and we discuss how the NQA can be used to study the properties of bound diquark states at finite T and $μ$.

## Key claim

The authors construct a non-perturbative solution of the two-flavor effective instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction model beyond the mean field approximation for any temperature and chemical potential, but only include the $\bar{q}q$ channels in the model Lagrangian. They find that the chemical potential induced by the interaction cancels exactly for all values of temperature, in contrast to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, but this result is obtained within the one-loop approximation using only the first and second order terms in the Haag expansion.

## Method

The authors apply the N-quantum approach (NQA) to quantum field theory, using a second-order Haag expansion of the quark fields in the operator equations of motion, then introduce temperature and chemical potential via thermal Bogoliubov transformations on the asymptotic fields. They solve the resulting self-consistency equations for the Haag amplitudes in the one-loop approximation to extract the masses, widths, and coupling constants of the $\sigma$ meson and color $\bar{3}$ scalar diquark ($D_A$) states as functions of temperature and Fermi momentum. The calculation uses a non-covariant cutoff regularization with parameters $\Lambda = 0.61$ GeV and $g_0 = 5.01$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass at $(T, \mu) = (0, 0)$: $m = 0.305$ GeV $\approx \frac{1}{3} M_N$ (nucleon mass), obtained with $\Lambda = 0.61$ GeV and $g_0 = 5.01$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Critical Fermi momentum for chiral symmetry restoration: $(P_F)_c \approx 0.3$ GeV (specifically 0.301 GeV) at $T = 0$.
- $\sigma$ mass equals twice the chiral gap ($m_\sigma = 2m$) for all $T$ and $\mu$ in the Nambu-Goldstone phase, with vanishing width.
- $\sigma qq$ coupling constant at $P_F = 0$: $g^2_{\sigma qq}/4\pi \approx 0.342$; maximum value at the critical point: $0.636$.
- $D_A$ (color $\bar{3}$ scalar diquark) states exist only for $P_F > 0.06$ GeV at $T = 0$; no $D_A$ states in free space.
- $D_A$ coupling constant is one order of magnitude smaller than that of the $\sigma$ state.

## Context

This work extends the NQA formalism previously applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Umino and Vento (2000) to the 't Hooft interaction, which incorporates the symmetries of QCD and resolves the $U_A(1)$ problem. The results contrast with the NJL model studied by Asakawa and Yazaki (1989), where the Fock term induces a chemical potential proportional to quark number density, making it impossible to clearly define critical points in the $T-\mu$ plane. The method enables future study of bound diquark states at finite temperature and chemical potential by adding $qq$ channels to the model Lagrangian, and could determine the color superconducting gap.
