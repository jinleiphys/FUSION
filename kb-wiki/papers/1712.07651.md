# The charming beauty of the strong interaction

> Charmed and beauty hadrons in matter are discussed within a unitarized coupled-channel model consistent with heavy-quark spin symmetry. We analyse the formation of D-mesic states as well as the propagation of charmed and beauty hadrons in heavy-ion collisions from LHC to FAIR energies.

# The charming beauty of the strong interaction

## Key claim

Charmed and beauty hadrons in matter can be described within a unitarized coupled-channel model that is consistent with heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS), but the model's predictions for dynamically-generated states depend on the specific attractive representations of the SU(6) x HQSS scheme and require comparison with experimental data for identification.

## Method

The author uses a unitarized coupled-channel approach based on the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction extended to four flavors, incorporating HQSS constraints for heavy quarks. The on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in coupled channels to calculate scattering amplitudes, whose poles correspond to dynamically-generated baryonic resonances. Nuclear medium modifications are included via Pauli blocking and self-consistent inclusion of D and D* self-energies, while transport coefficients for heavy mesons in hot matter are obtained using the Fokker-Planck equation.

## Key numbers

- A pole around 2618.8 MeV is identified with the experimental Lambda_c(2595) resonance, while a second broad Lambda_c state at 2617 MeV shows a two-pole pattern similar to Lambda(1405)
- The spin-3/2 Lambda_c state is assigned to Lambda_c(2625)
- Saturation density is taken as rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Predicted states include: Sigma_c(2823) and Sigma_c(2868) for I=1, J=1/2; Lambda_c(2660) and Lambda_c(2941) for I=0, J=3/2; Sigma_c(2554) and Sigma_c(2902) for I=1, J=3/2
- The experimental Lambda_b(5912) and Lambda_b(5920) states are identified as HQSS partners with J^P = 1/2^- and 3/2^-, respectively
- Predicted Xi_b states include Xi_b(6035.4) and Xi_b*(6043.3) forming a HQSS doublet

## Context

This work extends the successful unitarized coupled-channel approaches from the light-quark sector (such as those describing the Lambda(1405) two-pole structure) to charm and beauty degrees of freedom, incorporating HQSS constraints that earlier models by Tolos (2004, 2005), Lutz (2003, 2005), and Hofmann (2005, 2006) did not fully respect. The results enable predictions for D-meson bound states in nuclei (contrasting with earlier work by Tsushima (1998) which found stronger binding) and provide transport coefficients for heavy hadrons in heavy-ion collisions from LHC to FAIR energies, with implications for understanding charmonium suppression and the QCD phase diagram.
