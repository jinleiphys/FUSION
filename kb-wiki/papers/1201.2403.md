# Intermediate-energy inverse-kinematics one-proton pickup reactions on neutron-deficient $fp$-shell nuclei

> Background: Thick-target-induced nucleon-adding transfer reactions onto energetic rare-isotope beams are an emerging spectroscopic tool. Their sensitivity to single-particle structure complements one-nucleon removal reaction capabilities in the quest to reveal the evolution of nuclear shell structure in very exotic nuclei. Purpose: To add intermediate-energy, carbon-target-induced one-proton pickup reactions to the arsenal of $γ$-ray tagged direct reactions applicable in the regime of low beam intensities and to apply these for the first time to $fp$-shell nuclei. Methods: Inclusive and partial cross sections were measured for the $\nuc{12}{C}(\nuc{48}{Cr},\nuc{49}{Mn}+γ)$X and $\nuc{12}{C}(\nuc{50}{Fe},\nuc{51}{Co}+γ)$X proton pickup reactions at 56.7 and 61.2 MeV/nucleon, respectively, using coincident particle-$γ$ spectroscopy at the NSCL. The results are compared to reaction theory calculations using $fp$-shell-model nuclear structure input. For comparison with our previous work, the same reactions were measured on \nuc{9}{Be} targets. Results: The measured partial cross sections confirm the specific population pattern predicted by theory, with pickup into high-$\ell$ orbitals being strongly favored; driven by linear and angular momentum matching. Conclusion: Carbon target-induced pickup reactions are well-suited, in the regime of modest beam intensity, to study the evolution of nuclear structure, with specific sensitivities that are well described by theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that carbon-target-induced one-proton pickup reactions at intermediate energies (56.7 and 61.2 MeV/nucleon) are a viable spectroscopic tool for fp-shell nuclei, with the measured partial cross sections confirming the predicted strong preference for population of high-angular-momentum (ℓ=3) orbitals over low-ℓ (ℓ=1) orbitals, driven by linear and angular momentum matching conditions.

## Method

Inclusive and partial cross sections were measured for the 12C(48Cr,49Mn+γ)X and 12C(50Fe,51Co+γ)X one-proton pickup reactions at 56.7 and 61.2 MeV/nucleon, respectively, using coincident particle-γ spectroscopy at the NSCL S800 spectrograph with the SeGA germanium detector array. For comparison, the same reactions were also measured on 9Be targets at 50.7 and 54.8 MeV/nucleon. The experimental results were compared to post-form finite-range DWBA reaction calculations using fp-shell-model nuclear structure input from the GXPF1A effective interaction, with spectroscopic factors from shell-model calculations and single-particle cross sections computed using the fresco code.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for 12C(48Cr,49Mn)X: 2.00(13) mb at 56.7 MeV/nucleon
- Inclusive cross section for 12C(50Fe,51Co)X: 0.53(13) mb at 61.2 MeV/nucleon
- Inclusive cross section for 9Be(48Cr,49Mn)X: 1.63(8) mb at 50.7 MeV/nucleon
- Inclusive cross section for 9Be(50Fe,51Co)X: 0.57(8) mb at 54.8 MeV/nucleon
- Partial cross section for population of the 7/2- state in 49Mn from 12C target: 1.60(22) mb
- Partial cross section for population of the 7/2- state in 49Mn from 9Be target: 1.43(15) mb
- Ground-state cross section upper limit for 49Mn from 12C target: ≤0.40(25) mb
- Ground-state cross section upper limit for 49Mn from 9Be target: ≤0.20(17) mb
- Proton separation energy of 51Co: approximately 90 keV from systematics
- Proton separation energy of 49Mn: 2085(25) keV
- Single-particle cross section for 1f7/2 pickup to 49Mn 7/2- state on 12C: 4.141 mb (summed over 11B final states)
- Single-particle cross section for 2p3/2 pickup to 49Mn 3/2- state on 12C: 0.114 mb
- Single-particle cross section for 2p1/2 pickup to 49Mn 1/2- state on 12C: 0.040 mb

## Context

This work extends the fast-beam one-nucleon pickup reaction technique, previously developed for 9Be targets and applied to sd-shell nuclei by Gade et al. (2007), into the fp-shell region and establishes 12C as a viable target for one-proton pickup studies. The measured cross sections confirm the predicted high-ℓ selectivity of the pickup mechanism at intermediate energies, complementing one-nucleon removal reactions (knockout) that probe hole-like configurations. The technique enables spectroscopic studies of particle-like states in exotic nuclei with modest beam intensities (1-10^3 particles/second), and the results for the weakly-bound dripline nucleus 51Co (Sp ≈ 90 keV) demonstrate applicability even for near-dripline systems. Future work can apply this method to study shell evolution and intruder states in more exotic neutron-deficient fp-shell nuclei.
