# Warm Asymmetric Nuclear Matter and Proto-Neutron Star

> Asymmetric nuclear matter equation of state at finite temperature is studied in SU(2) chiral sigma model using mean field approximation. The effect of temperature on effective mass, entropy, and binding energy is discussed. Treating the system as one with two conserved charges the liquid-gas phase transition is investigated. We have also discussed the effect of proton fraction on critical temperature with and without $ρ$-meson contribution. We have extended our work to study the structure of proto-neutron star with neutron free charge-neutral matter in beta-equilibrium. We found that the mass and radius of the star decreases as it cools from the entropy per baryon S = 2 to S = 0 and the maximum temperature of the core of the star is about 62 MeV for S = 2.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the mass and radius of a proto-neutron star decrease as it cools from entropy per baryon S = 2 to S = 0, and that the maximum core temperature is about 62 MeV for S = 2; however, this result is conditional on using the SU(2) chiral sigma model (MCH model) with mean-field approximation and considering beta-equilibrium matter without neutrinos, and the influence of entropy on the star structure is described as "not very sensitive."

## Method

The authors used the SU(2) chiral sigma model (MCH model) in the mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, including the rho-meson field. They solved the field equations self-consistently to obtain effective mass, entropy, binding energy, and pressure as functions of baryon density, temperature, and proton fraction. For the proto-neutron star structure, they imposed beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions (without neutrino trapping) and integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations at fixed entropy per baryon (S = 0, 1, 2) to obtain mass and radius.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: T_c ≈ 17.2 MeV, with critical density n_c ≈ 0.045 fm^{-3}, critical pressure p_c ≈ 0.274 MeV/fm^3, and critical effective mass M*_c ≈ 887 MeV.
- Without rho-meson, critical temperature decreases from T_c ≈ 17.2 MeV (y_p = 0.5) to 11.6 MeV (y_p = 0); with rho-meson, T_c decreases from 17.2 MeV (y_p = 0.5) to 0 MeV (y_p = 0.02).
- Maximum proto-neutron star mass: 2.18 solar masses (S = 0), 2.21 solar masses (S = 1), 2.33 solar masses (S = 2).
- Corresponding radii: 12.14 km (S = 0), 12.23 km (S = 1), 12.45 km (S = 2).
- Central energy densities: 1230 MeV/fm^3 (S = 0), 1190 MeV/fm^3 (S = 1), 1092 MeV/fm^3 (S = 2).
- Central temperatures: 0.0 MeV (S = 0), 27.85 MeV (S = 1), 62.12 MeV (S = 2).
- Saturation density used for parameter fitting: n_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon of -16.3 MeV.
- The model exhibits a phase transition at zero density at T ≈ 235 MeV (compared to T ≈ 185 MeV in the Walecka model).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Jena and Singh, 2003) on finite-temperature symmetric nuclear matter using the MCH model by adding the rho-meson field to study asymmetric nuclear matter and the liquid-gas phase transition. It builds on prior relativistic mean-field calculations of the liquid-gas phase transition by several authors (referenced as Refs. 4-8 in the paper) and on the Walecka model results (which gave T_c ≈ 18.3 MeV for symmetric matter). The proto-neutron star results are compared with those of Prakash et al. (Ref. 2), and the findings enable further studies of proto-neutron star cooling and the role of composition versus temperature in determining neutron star structure.
