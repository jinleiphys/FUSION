# Interplay of Reggeon and photon in pA collisions

> We discuss the effects of the electromagnetic interaction in high-energy proton collisions with nuclei of large Z at strong coupling $λ=g^2N_c$. Using the holographic dual limit of large $N_c>λ\gg 1$, we describe the Reggeon exchange as a twisted surface and show that it gets essentially modified by the electromagnetic interaction.

## Key claim

The authors show that in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions with large nuclear charge Z, the electromagnetic interaction significantly modifies the QCD Reggeon exchange amplitude at strong coupling, contrary to the usual assumption that QED effects are negligible; this modification arises because Reggeons carry electric charge, unlike neutral Pomerons, and the effect is not merely quantum mechanical interference but a genuine alteration of the Reggeon amplitude itself.

## Method

The authors use holographic QCD (AdS/CFT correspondence) in the limit of large N_c and strong 't Hooft coupling λ ≫ 1, modeling the Reggeon exchange as an open string world-sheet on a helicoid surface between spectator trajectories. They compute the semi-classical Nambu-Goto action for the string together with the QED action from Wilson lines of charged particles, treating the large-Z nucleus as a classical source, and solve the resulting variational equations for the exchanged quark and antiquark trajectories to find the saddle-point contribution to the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The string tension parameter α' = 0.036 fm² is used in numerical evaluations.
- For Z = 100, χ = 15, and b/√α' = 80, the transition point between solution regimes occurs at σ_c ≈ 1.73 (in fm units, from the numerical solution).
- The dimensionless combination (2πα' q_u Z e²)/(4π r_0²) ≈ 0.132 for Z = 80, q_u = 1, r_0 = 1 fm, and α_EM = 1/137, which is less than 1.
- The constant C ≈ 0.23 determines the scaling σ_c = C b/χ for large b.
- The numerical integral over the QED contribution yields a value of 2.74 for the chosen parameters.

## Context

This work extends the holographic treatment of Reggeon exchange originally developed by Janik and collaborators (2001) for pp scattering, where QED effects were neglected, to the case of pA collisions with heavy nuclei where the large nuclear charge Z ~ 100 makes electromagnetic corrections important. The authors distinguish their result from the case of Pomeron exchange (studied by Brower et al. (2006), Nishio et al. (2011), Stoffers et al. (2012), and Watanabe et al. (2012)), where neutral Pomerons are unaffected by QED and only interference effects appear. The analysis enables predictions for dedicated pA experiments at LHC and RHIC using heavy nuclei, and suggests that for extremely large Z where the dimensionless QED coupling exceeds 1, the Reggeon amplitude becomes purely imaginary, indicating complete QED domination.
