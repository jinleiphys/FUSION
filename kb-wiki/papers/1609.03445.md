# R-Matrix Calculations for Few-Quark Bound States

> The R--matrix method is implemented to study the heavy charm and bottom diquark, triquark, tetraquark and pentaquarks in configuration space, as the bound states of quark--antiquark, diquark--quark, diquark--antidiquark and diquark--antitriquark systems, respectively. The mass spectrum and the size of these systems are calculated for different partial wave channels. The calculated masses are compared with recent theoretical results obtained by other methods in momentum and configuration spaces and also by available experimental data.

## Key claim

The R-matrix method is implemented as an effective and efficient approach to solve the Schrödinger equation for heavy few-quark bound states in configuration space, with the hedge that the nonrelativistic spin-independent scheme yields masses that agree with experimental data to within a relative difference below 3.4% for tetraquarks, while relativistic and spin contributions are acknowledged to cause systematic differences.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation in configuration space for two-body systems representing diquark (quark-antiquark), triquark (diquark-quark), tetraquark (diquark-antidiquark), and pentaquark (diquark-antitriquark) bound states, using a linear confining plus Coulomb-like one-gluon exchange potential with form factors. The radial wave function is split into an internal region (r < r_c) expanded in Gaussian basis functions and an external region (r > r_c) described by modified spherical Bessel functions, with continuity enforced via the Bloch operator; the resulting energy-dependent eigenvalue equation is solved iteratively until convergence to 10^{-10} relative precision.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium 1s mass: 3.062 GeV (expectation value of quark-antiquark distance 0.349 fm)
- Bottomonium 1s mass: 9.421 GeV (expectation value 0.184 fm)
- Ground state charm tetraquark (cq\bar{c}\bar{q}, S\bar{S}) mass: 3.885 GeV (expectation value 0.35 fm)
- Ground state bottom tetraquark (bq\bar{b}\bar{q}, S\bar{S}) mass: 10.482 GeV (expectation value 0.23 fm)
- Charm baryon Λ_c mass: 2.396 GeV (expectation value 0.49 fm)
- Bottom baryon Λ_b mass: 5.687 GeV (expectation value 0.45 fm)
- Pentaquark (nucleon + B meson, I=0, potential A) mass: 6.076 GeV
- Pentaquark (nucleon + D meson, I=0, potential A) mass: 2.688 GeV
- Charmoniumlike pentaquark P_c^+ s-wave mass (central + spin-spin + spin-orbit): 4151 MeV (expectation value 0.306 fm)

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix method, originally developed for nuclear resonances by Kapur and Peierls and later by Wigner, to the domain of heavy few-quark bound states, complementing prior momentum-space approaches such as the Lippmann-Schwinger equation solutions by Hadizadeh (2015) and relativistic studies by Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin (EFG) (2006-2008). The results enable systematic nonrelativistic predictions for the mass spectrum and spatial size of tetraquarks and pentaquarks, with the authors noting that future inclusion of spin-dependent and relativistic corrections could improve agreement with experimental candidates such as Y(4260) and Y(4660).
