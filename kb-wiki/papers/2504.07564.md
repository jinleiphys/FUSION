# Excitation energies of $2^+_1$ and $4^+_1$ states of neutron deficient U and Pu isotopes

> The microscopic variant of the Grodzins relation and the Quasiparticle Phonon Model are applied to predict the excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ states of neutron deficient U and Pu isotopes. The P-factor systematics is used to determine the quadrupole deformation of nuclei under consideration. The excitation energies of the $4^+_1$ states are predicted based on the simple universal anharmonic vibrator type relation.

## Key claim

The paper predicts excitation energies of the first 2+ and 4+ states in neutron-deficient U and Pu isotopes using a microscopic variant of the Grodzins relation and the Quasiparticle Phonon Model, but the predictions rely on fixing the ratios of inertia coefficients (Bγ/Brot = 4 and Bβ/Brot = 12) from systematics of well-deformed nuclei and on using P-factor systematics to estimate quadrupole deformations where experimental data are absent.

## Method

The authors calculate E(2+1) using two forms of the Grodzins relation: the full microscopic expression (Eq. 13) with inertia coefficients B_rot, B_γ, and B_β computed via the cranking approximation in the Quasiparticle Phonon Model, and a simplified version (Eq. A) with fixed ratios B_γ/B_rot = 4 and B_β/B_rot = 12. Quadrupole deformations β_2 are taken from experiment where available or estimated by matching the P-factor (N_p N_n / (N_p + N_n)) to a nucleus with known β_2. For E(4+1), they apply the universal anharmonic vibrator relation E(4+1) = 2E(2+1) + ε_4 with ε_4 = 80 keV, derived from systematics of nuclei with similar P-factors.

## Key numbers

- For 226U: calculated E(2+1) = 84-87 keV (range from two methods) compared to experimental value 81 keV; calculated E(4+1) = 248-254 keV compared to experimental value 250 keV.
- For 222U: predicted E(2+1) = 231-258 keV and E(4+1) = 542-596 keV (no experimental data).
- For 232Pu: predicted E(2+1) = 45-46 keV and E(4+1) = 170-172 keV (no experimental data).
- For well-studied 238U: calculated E(2+1) = 30-38 keV compared to experimental value 45 keV.
- The anharmonicity parameter ε_4 is taken as 80 keV, based on values around 67 ± 16 keV for the region.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic derivation of the Grodzins relation by Jolos (Ref. Jolos1) and applies the Quasiparticle Phonon Model of Soloviev (Refs. Soloviev1, Soloviev2) to predict properties of neutron-deficient actinides near the N=126-184 shell, where experimental data are scarce. The P-factor systematics of Casten (Ref. Casten1) is used to estimate deformations, and the anharmonic vibrator relation from Casten (Ref. Casten2) is employed for 4+ states. The predictions are intended to guide planned experiments at the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions in Dubna (Refs. Eremin1, Eremin2, Eremin3) and to test the evolution of collectivity at the beginning of the actinide shell.
