# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the Light-Front Dynamics

> The deuteron form factors are calculated in the framework of the relativistic nucleon-meson dynamics, by means of the explicitly covariant light-front approach. The inflluence of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors is discussed. At $Q^2\leq 3$ (GeV/c)$^2$ the prediction for the structure function $A(Q^2)$ and for the tensor polarization observable $t_{20}$ are in agreement with the recent data of CEBAF/TJNAF.

# Deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the Light-Front Dynamics

## Key claim
The deuteron structure function A(Q²) and tensor polarization t₂₀ calculated in the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics agree with CEBAF/TJNAF data up to Q² ≤ 3 (GeV/c)², but only when using the MMD nucleon electromagnetic form factor parametrization; the agreement is conditional on the choice of nucleon form factors and the inclusion of contact (two-body current) contributions.

## Method
The authors calculated deuteron electromagnetic form factors using the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics, with the deuteron wave function obtained perturbatively from the relativistic one-boson-exchange kernel using Bonn model parameters. The electromagnetic vertex included both the impulse approximation (one-body nucleon current) and contact interaction terms (instantaneous NNBγ vertices) that partially incorporate two-body currents, with the physical form factors extracted by separating them from eight nonphysical ω-dependent structures.

## Key numbers
- The calculation covers momentum transfers up to Q² = 6 (GeV/c)², with agreement with data claimed for Q² ≤ 3 (GeV/c)²
- The deuteron wave function includes three dominating spin components (f₁, f₂, f₅) out of six possible; component f₅ dominates over f₁ and f₂ starting from k ≈ 0.5 GeV/c
- The accuracy of the perturbative solution in reproducing low-energy deuteron observables was estimated at the level of 20% in norm
- The contact term contribution to the magnetic form factor G₁ involves 6-dimensional numerical integration (two-loop)

## Context
This work extends the covariant light-front dynamics approach previously developed by the authors (Carbonell and Karmanov, 1997) to deuteron electromagnetic form factors, using the same Bonn potential parameters as the nonrelativistic Bonn model (Machleidt, 1989). It contrasts with earlier calculations using the standard (non-covariant) light-front approach (Graz group, 1984-1993) and the Bethe-Salpeter approach (Zuilhof and Tjon, 1989; Hummel and Tjon, 1989). The results enable comparison with the first precise TJNAF/CEBAF data on deuteron structure functions and tensor polarization, and demonstrate the importance of the contact interaction which incorporates 50% of the pion-exchange pair term contribution to the isovector electrodisintegration amplitude.
