# Nucleon bound state at finite temperature

> We study the nucleon at finite temperature using Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) where it is considered as a bound state of a scalar-diquark and a quark. The interaction between the diquark and the quark is taken as an exchange quark.The constituent quark and diquark substructures are considered momentum and temperature dependent based on the lattice QCD and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB). The kernel interaction modification with temperature is approximated using the mixed representation in the imaginary time formalism and then we adopt an adiabatic (instantaneous) approximation after evaluating the Matsubara frequencies sum. The results of BSE are compared with those for MIT bag model. We argue the bag pressure is a temperature dependent and it decreases smoothly with temperature to be consistent with the results for BSE. Furthermore, the nucleon bound state obtained by BSE ceases to exist just below the critical temperature and this indicates that the nucleon deconfines to its constituent quarks at the critical temperature. The nucleon mass decreases with temperature as $M_N(T)/M_N(0)\approx {\cal R}^δ(T)$ where ${\cal R}(T)=(1-b\frac{T^2}{T^2_c})^{1/2}$ and $δ\approx 1$ and this suggests $B(T)/B(0)={\cal R}^{4δ}(T)$ for MIT bag.

# Nucleon bound state at finite temperature

## Key claim

The nucleon mass decreases with temperature as $M_N(T)/M_N(0) \approx \mathcal{R}^\delta(T)$ with $\delta \approx 1$, where $\mathcal{R}(T) = (1 - b T^2/T_c^2)^{1/2}$, and the nucleon bound state obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation ceases to exist just below the critical temperature, indicating deconfinement; however, this result depends on the adiabatic (instantaneous) approximation adopted after evaluating Matsubara frequency sums, and on the assumption that constituent quark and diquark masses scale with temperature as the linear confinement dissociation.

## Method

The nucleon is treated as a bound state of a scalar diquark and a quark interacting via quark exchange, described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) at finite temperature. The kernel interaction modification with temperature is approximated using the mixed representation in the imaginary time formalism, adopting an adiabatic (instantaneous) approximation by setting the time component to zero after evaluating the Matsubara frequency sums. Constituent quark and diquark masses are taken as momentum and temperature dependent based on lattice QCD and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, with the temperature dependence following the thermal dissociation of the linear confinement. Results are compared with an extended MIT bag model where the bag pressure is taken as temperature dependent.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at zero momentum and zero temperature: 350 MeV; scalar diquark mass at zero momentum and zero temperature: 650 MeV.
- Critical temperature taken as $T_c = 170$ MeV.
- MIT bag model parameters for free nucleon: $B_0^{1/4} = 188.1$ MeV, $Z_0 = 2.03$, reproducing nucleon mass 939 MeV and bag radius $R_0 = 0.60$ fm.
- Quark substructure momentum scale parameter: $\overline{p} = 870$ MeV; exponent $\gamma = 3.04$; current mass $m_0 = 0$ MeV.
- Quark-diquark coupling parameters: $g_{dqq} = 14.15$, $\lambda = 0.18$ fm$^{-2}$.
- Nucleon mass scaling: $M_N(T)/M_N(0) = \mathcal{R}(T)$ with $\mathcal{R}(T) = (1 - b T^2/T_c^2)^{1/2}$ and $\delta = 1$; bag pressure scaling: $B(T)/B(0) = \mathcal{R}^{4\delta}(T)$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of baryons as quark-diquark bound states using the Bethe-Salpeter equation at zero temperature (Keiner 1995, Keiner 1996, Hellstern 1997, Oettel 2000, Alkofer 2004) to finite temperature, and builds on attempts to study mesons at finite temperature via BSE (Blaschke 1998, Blaschke 2000, Roberts 2000). The temperature dependence of constituent masses is based on lattice-QCD-based Schwinger-Dyson equation studies (Iida 2003, Iida 2004) and the thermal dissociation of linear confinement (Pisarski 1982, Kaczmarek 1999). The results enable comparison between BSE and MIT bag model predictions for nucleon deconfinement at the critical temperature, and suggest that the MIT bag model requires a temperature-dependent bag pressure to match BSE results, though the MIT bag model lacks the first-order phase transition signature found in the BSE approach.
