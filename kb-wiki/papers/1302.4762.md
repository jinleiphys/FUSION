# Carbon-19 in Halo EFT: Effective-range parameters from Coulomb-dissociation experiments

> We study the Coulomb dissociation of the 19C nucleus in an effective field theory that uses the 18C core and the neutron as effective degrees of freedom and exploits the separation of scales in this halo system. We extract the effective-range parameters and the separation energy of the halo neutron from the experimental data taken at RIKEN by Nakamura et al. We obtain a value of (575 +/- 55(stat.) +/- 20(EFT)) keV for the one-neutron separation energy of 19C, and (7.75 +/- 0.35(stat.) +/- 0.3(EFT)) fm for the 18C-neutron scattering length. The width of the longitudinal momentum distribution predicted by EFT using this separation energy agrees well with the experimental data taken at NSCL by Bazin et al., reaffirming the dominance of the s-wave configuration of the valence neutron.

## Key claim

The authors extract the effective-range parameters and one-neutron separation energy of 19C from Coulomb-dissociation data using halo effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), finding that the EFT expansion parameter (r0/a ≈ 0.33) is smaller than the nominal core-to-halo size ratio (0.49), which improves the convergence of the calculation.

## Method

The authors applied a halo EFT with 18C core and neutron as degrees of freedom, treating the s-wave neutron-core interaction at N2LO with two free parameters (scattering length a and effective range r0, or equivalently binding momentum γ0 and r0). They fitted these parameters to the angular distribution of the Coulomb dissociation differential cross section from Nakamura et al. (1999) and the relative energy spectrum from Nakamura et al. (2003), using a combined χ2 minimization with data from 67 A MeV 19C on a 208Pb target. The resulting parameters were then used to predict the longitudinal momentum distribution for comparison with data from Bazin et al. (1998) on a 181Ta target at 88 MeV/u.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of 19C: (575 ± 55 (stat.) ± 20 (EFT)) keV, from combined fit to angular distribution and energy spectrum data.
* 18C-neutron scattering length: (7.75 ± 0.35 (stat.) ± 0.3 (EFT)) fm, from the same combined fit.
* 18C-neutron effective range: (2.6 +0.6 -0.9 (stat.) ± 0.1 (EFT)) fm, from the combined fit.
* Best-fit values from angular distribution alone: B = 540 keV, a = 7.5 fm, with 1-sigma intervals (480,630) keV and (6.9,8.0) fm.
* Best-fit values from energy spectrum alone: B = 580 keV, a = 8.1 fm, with 1-sigma intervals (510,650) keV and (7.6,8.4) fm.
* EFT expansion parameter r0/a = 0.33, giving a relative EFT error of (r0/a)3 = 0.036 (3.6%).
* First excitation energy of 18C core: 1620 ± 20 keV, approximately three times the neutron separation energy.

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT framework previously applied to 11Be by Phillips et al. (2010) and Hammer et al. (2011) to the 19C system, and uses the same formalism as Rupak et al. (2011, 2012) for radiative capture reactions. The results confirm the s-wave dominance of the valence neutron configuration in 19C, consistent with the spin-parity assignment of Jπ = 1/2+ from Nakamura et al. (1999), and resolve discrepancies between earlier mass measurements (160 ± 110 keV) and Coulomb dissociation analyses (530 ± 130 keV). The extracted parameters enable predictions for neutron-core scattering and provide input for studies of Efimov states in the carbon isotopic chain, particularly 20C and 22C.
