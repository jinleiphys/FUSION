# Searching for New Physics beyond the Standard Model in Electric Dipole Moment

> This is a theoretical review of exploration of new physics beyond the Standard Model (SM) in electric dipole moment (EDM) in elementary particles, atoms, and molecule. EDM is a very important CP violating phenomenon and sensitive to new physics. Starting with the estimations of EDM of quarks-leptons in the SM, we explore the new signals beyond the SM. However, these works drive us to more wide fronteers where we serach fundamental physics using atoms and molecules and vice versa. Paramagnetic atoms and molecules have great enhancement factor on electron EDM. Diamagnetic atoms and molecules are very sensitive to nuclear P and T odd processes. Thus the EDM becomes the key word not only of New Physics but also of unprecedented fruitful collaboration among particle, atomic. molecular physics. This review intends to help such collaboration over the wide range of physicists.

## Key claim

This theoretical review argues that electric dipole moments (EDMs) are a uniquely sensitive probe for new physics beyond the Standard Model (SM), but emphasizes that the SM predictions for EDMs are far smaller than current experimental upper limits, meaning any observed EDM would unambiguously signal new physics; the review aims to bridge particle, atomic, and molecular physics communities by showing how paramagnetic and diamagnetic systems enhance fundamental EDMs.

## Method

The author systematically reviews the theoretical foundations of EDMs, beginning with the effective dipole operator formalism and the definition of EDM from CP-odd effective actions. The review then calculates EDM predictions within the Standard Model at one-loop, two-loop, three-loop (for quarks), and four-loop (for leptons) levels, showing that all lower-loop contributions vanish due to CKM unitarity and GIM cancellation. Finally, the review surveys predictions from beyond-the-Standard-Model scenarios, focusing on the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) and its constrained version (cMSSM), and discusses how EDMs are enhanced in paramagnetic atoms (for electron EDM) and diamagnetic atoms (for nuclear P- and T-odd processes via the Schiff moment).

## Key numbers

*   Neutron EDM experimental upper limit: \(d_n < 2.9 \times 10^{-26}\) e cm (90% C.L.) from Baker (2006).
*   Mercury-199 EDM experimental upper limit: \(d({}^{199}Hg) < 3.1 \times 10^{-29}\) e cm (95% C.L.) from the Hg collaboration.
*   Electron EDM from thallium atom: \(d(Tl) = (6.9 \pm 7.4) \times 10^{-28}\) e cm from Regan (2002).
*   Electron EDM from YbF molecule: \(d(YbF) = (-2.4 \pm 5.7_{stat} \pm 1.5_{syst}) \times 10^{-28}\) e cm from the YbF collaboration.
*   Muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy: \(\Delta a_\mu[e^+e^-] = 30.3(8.1) \times 10^{-10}\), corresponding to a \(3.7\sigma\) deviation from the SM prediction.
*   Standard Model prediction for d-quark EDM: \(d_d \simeq -10^{-34}\) e cm from three-loop calculations by Czarnecki (1997).
*   Standard Model prediction for neutron EDM: \(d_n \simeq 10^{-32}\) e cm from Khriplovich (1981), with a range \(1.4 \times 10^{-31} \leq |d_n| \leq 9.9 \times 10^{-33}\) e cm from He.
*   Standard Model prediction for electron EDM: \(d_e \simeq 8 \times 10^{-41}\) e cm from four-loop calculations.
*   Jarlskog invariant value: \(J_{CP} \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}\).
*   Observed baryon asymmetry: \(n_B/n_\gamma = 1 \times 10^{-10}\), while the SM prediction from the Jarlskog invariant gives approximately \(10^{-20}\).

## Context

This review extends the earlier review works by Khriplovich, Ginges and Flambaum, Pospelov and Ritz, and Khriplovich and Lamoreaux, but is distinguished by being written from the perspective of a beyond-the-Standard-Model physicist, emphasizing new physics signals. The paper connects the muon g-2 anomaly (a \(3.3\sigma\) to \(3.7\sigma\) discrepancy from the SM, depending on hadronic data used) and the baryon asymmetry problem to the need for new CP-violating sources beyond the Kobayashi-Maskawa mechanism. It enables future collaboration between particle, atomic, and molecular physicists by showing how paramagnetic systems (like YbF molecules) enhance electron EDM sensitivity and how diamagnetic systems (like mercury atoms) probe nuclear CP violation, providing a framework for interpreting upcoming experimental results.
