# Magnetic field of a neutron star with color superconducting quark matter core

> The behaviour of the magnetic field of a neutron star with a superconducting quark matter core is investigated in the framework of the Ginzburg-Landau theory. We take into account the simultaneous coupling of the diquark condensate field to the usual magnetic and to the gluomagnetic gauge fields. We solve the Ginzburg-Landau equations by properly taking into account the boundary conditions, in particular, the gluon confinment condition. We found the distribution of the magnetic field in both the quark and hadronic phases of the neutron star and show that the magnetic field penetrates into the quark core in the form of quark vortices due to the presence of Meissner currents.

# Magnetic field of a neutron star with color superconducting quark matter core

## Key claim

The magnetic field of a neutron star with a color superconducting quark matter core penetrates into the quark core in the form of quark vortices due to Meissner currents, but only when the Ginzburg-Landau equations are solved with proper boundary conditions including gluon confinement; contrary to earlier claims (Alford et al., 2000) that "rotated electromagnetism" allows a static homogeneous magnetic field to penetrate without Meissner effect, the authors argue that such a result arises from incorrect boundary conditions and that the Meissner currents do effectively screen static homogeneous external fields.

## Method

The authors solve the Ginzburg-Landau equations for a diquark condensate coupled simultaneously to the usual magnetic gauge field and the gluomagnetic gauge field (the "rotated electromagnetism" framework), imposing a sharp boundary between the quark core and hadronic shell with the gluon confinement condition (vanishing gluon potential at the boundary). They derive analytic expressions for the magnetic field components in spherical coordinates for both the quark core (radius a) and the hadronic shell (thickness R-a), assuming the star is a type II superconductor and that the penetration depths are much smaller than the core and shell dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angle α between photon and gluon fields is approximately 1/20 for g²/4π ≈ 1, since α ≈ η e/g with η = 1/√3 and e²/4π = 1/137.
- The new charge q of the diquark pair is "by an order of magnitude larger than e/√3".
- The confinement radius (diffusion boundary layer thickness) is approximately 0.2 to 0.4 fm.
- The critical temperature T_c for the diquark condensate is of order 50 MeV.
- The diquark pairing gap is of order 100 MeV.
- The magnetic field in both quark and hadronic phases is constant and parallel to the rotation axis except within a transition layer of order λ_p + λ_q at the phase boundary.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau treatment of color superconducting quark matter by Sedrakian and Blaschke (2001) to the case of inhomogeneous vortex-type external fields, and directly addresses the controversy raised by Alford et al. (2000) who claimed that rotated electromagnetism prevents the Meissner effect. The authors argue that Bailin and Love (1984) incorrectly concluded quark matter is type I superconductor based on perturbative gluon propagators that gave very small pairing gaps, while later non-perturbative approaches (Alford et al., 1998; Rapp et al., 1998) allow large gaps and type II behavior. The results enable future studies of rotating effects on vortices and gluon-photon mixing, as noted in the added reference to Iida (2002).
