# Calculations of the Exclusive Processes 2H(e,e'p)n, 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(pn) within a Generalized Glauber Approach

> The exclusive processes 2H(e,e'p)n, 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(pn), have been analyzed using realistic few-body wave functions and treating the final state interaction (FSI) within a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA), based upon the direct calculation of the Feynman diagrams describing the rescattering of the struck nucleon with the nucleons of the A-1 system. The approach represents an improvement of the conventional Glauber approach (GA), in that it allows one to take into account the effects of the nuclear excitation of the $A-1$ system on the rescattering of the struck nucleon. Using realistic three-body wave functions corresponding to the AV18 interaction, the results of our parameter free calculations are compared with available experimental data. It is found that in some kinematical conditions FSI effects represent small corrections, whereas in other kinematics conditions they are very large and absolutely necessary to provide a satisfactory agreement between theoretical calculations and experimental data. It is shown that in the kinematics of the experimental data which have been considered, covering the region of missing momentum and energy with p_m < 0.6 GeV/c and E_m < 100 MeV in the perpendicular kinematics, the GA and GEA predictions differ only by less than 3-4 %.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA), which accounts for nuclear excitation of the spectator system, improves upon the conventional Glauber approach (GA) for describing final state interactions in exclusive electron-scattering processes off few-body nuclei; however, for the kinematics considered (missing momentum up to 0.6 GeV/c and missing energy up to 100 MeV in perpendicular kinematics), the GA and GEA predictions differ by less than 3-4%, indicating that the simpler GA is often adequate in this regime.

## Method

The authors calculated the exclusive processes 2H(e,e'p)n, 3He(e,e'p)2H, and 3He(e,e'p)(pn) using realistic few-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction. Final state interactions were treated within both the conventional Glauber approach (GA) and a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) based on direct calculation of Feynman diagrams describing rescattering of the struck nucleon with the nucleons of the A-1 system, with the GEA partly removing the frozen approximation by including excitation energy of the A-1 system. The parameter-free calculations were compared with available experimental data from Nikhef, Saclay, and JLab.

## Key numbers

- The GA and GEA predictions differ by less than 3-4% in the kinematics of the experimental data considered, covering missing momentum p_m ≤ 0.6 GeV/c and missing energy E_m ≤ 100 MeV in perpendicular kinematics.
- For 3He, the minimum removal energy E_min for the two-body break-up channel 3He → D + p is approximately 5.49 MeV.
- The calculations cover the region of missing momentum p_m < 0.6 GeV/c and missing energy E_m < 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous diagrammatic treatments of final state interactions in exclusive A(e,e'p)B processes, building on the formalism developed by Gribov, Bertocchi, and collaborators for particle-nucleus scattering, and later generalized by Markowitz, Strikman, and Misak for exclusive electron scattering. The calculations use the realistic three-body wave functions of the Pisa group (based on the AV18 interaction) and compare with experimental data from Nikhef, Ulmer, Bulten, Saclay, and JLab. The approach enables systematic investigation of the limits of validity of the standard Glauber approximation and provides a framework that can be extended to complex nuclei, while also clarifying the kinematic conditions where final state interactions are small corrections versus where they are essential for agreement with data.
