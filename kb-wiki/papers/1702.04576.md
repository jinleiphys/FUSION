# ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction in the NCSM/RGM approach

> Recently, we applied an $ab$ $initio$ method, the no-core shell model combined with the resonating group method, to the transfer reactions with light p-shell nuclei as targets and deuteron as the projectile. In particular, we studied the elastic scattering of deuterium on $^7$Li and the ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction starting from a realistic two-nucleon interaction. In this contribution, we review of our main results on the ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction, and we extend the study of the relevant reaction channels, by showing the dominant resonant phase shifts of the scattering matrix. We assess also the impact of the polarization effects of the deuteron below the breakup on the positive-parity resonant states in the reaction. For this purpose, we perform an analysis of the convergence trend of the phase and eigenphase shifts, with respect to the number of deuteron pseudostates included in the model space.

# ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction in the NCSM/RGM approach

## Key claim

The NCSM/RGM ab initio method, applied with a chiral N$^3$LO two-nucleon interaction evolved via SRG, can describe the ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li transfer reaction and the $^9$Be spectrum above the $d$+$^7$Li threshold, but the position of the first resonant peak in the integrated cross section is overestimated by about 0.33 MeV at the largest computable model space ($N_{\rm max}=8$), and the inclusion of deuteron pseudostates (approximating virtual breakup) significantly shifts resonance positions by up to ~600 keV.

## Method

The no-core shell model combined with the resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) was applied to the coupled ($d$,$^7$Li) + ($p$,$^8$Li) binary-cluster channels, using a chiral N$^3$LO NN potential evolved by SRG with $\Lambda$=2.02 fm$^{-1}$ and a harmonic oscillator basis with $\hbar\Omega$=20 MeV at $N_{\rm max}=6$ and 8 truncations. The deuteron continuum was approximated by including up to 4 positive-energy pseudostates from the NCSM spectrum in the $^{3}S_1$-$^{3}D_1$ channel, and the coupled integral-differential equations were solved on a Lagrange mesh within the microscopic R-matrix method.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Q-value of ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li is -0.192 MeV; the NCSM/RGM calculation gives Q-values of -0.556 MeV ($N_{\rm max}=6$) and -0.465 MeV ($N_{\rm max}=8$).
- The first resonant peak in the integrated cross section appears at deuteron laboratory energy ~0.78 MeV experimentally; the $N_{\rm max}=8$ calculation overestimates this position by about 0.33 MeV.
- The experimental peak cross section has a recommended value of 0.147$\pm$0.011 b at 0.78 MeV deuteron energy.
- For the $J^\pi T = \frac{3}{2}^+ \frac{1}{2}$ eigenphase shift, the relative difference between resonance positions computed with 3 and 4 deuteron pseudostates is within 5%; omitting pseudostates shifts the resonance by 50% relative to the best calculation.
- The $p$-$^8$Li $^6S_{5/2^+}$ phase shift resonance shifts by ~600 keV toward the $p$+$^8$Li threshold when 4 deuteron pseudostates are included compared to using only the deuteron ground state.
- Deuteron ground-state energy computed in NCSM: -1.96 MeV ($N_{\rm max}=8$), -2.12 MeV ($N_{\rm max}=10$), -2.13 MeV ($N_{\rm max}=12$); experimental value is -2.22 MeV.
- $^7$Li ground-state energy at $N_{\rm max}=8$: -38.01 MeV (experiment: -39.25 MeV); $^8$Li ground-state energy at $N_{\rm max}=8$: -39.66 MeV (experiment: -41.28 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM ab initio approach, previously applied to $d$-$\alpha$ elastic scattering (Navratil 2011, Hupin 2015) and to $^3$H($d,n$)$^4$He and $^3$He($d,p$)$^4$He transfer reactions (Quaglioni 2012, Navratil 2012), to deuteron-induced transfer on a p-shell target ($A>4$) for the first time. The ${}^{7}$Li($d$,$p$)${}^{8}$Li excitation function below deuteron breakup is used experimentally as a calibration tool for $^7$Be($p$,$\gamma$)$^8$B radiative capture measurements (Adelberger 1998). The calculation supports a $J^\pi = \frac{5}{2}^+$ spin-parity assignment for the 0.78 MeV resonance, consistent with the analysis of Friedland (1971) and in conflict with the assignment of Decharge (1972). The work enables future inclusion of three-nucleon forces and the $^8$Be($\alpha$-$\alpha$)-$n$ mass partition to improve agreement with the second experimental peak.
