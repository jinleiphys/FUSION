# Effects of incompressibility on the neutron-proton equilibration in $^{70}$Zn + $^{70}$Zn collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon

> Background: The primary goal of studying isospin dynamics via heavy-ion reactions is to explore the isospin dependence of effective interactions within the nuclear equation of state (EOS). Purpose: This work aims to investigate the effects of nuclear incompressibility ($ K_0 $) on neutron-proton equilibration in projectile-like fragments (PLFs). Method: We simulate $^{70}$Zn + $^{70}$Zn collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, coupled with the statistical decay code GEMINI. Results: The IQMD simulations not only reproduce experimental data patterns but also reveal the dynamic mechanisms underlying the binary breakup of PLFs. The rotation of PLFs is influenced by the transformation of angular momentum, which is connected to the isoscalar component of the EOS. This connection explains why shifts in $ K_0 $ affect the description of neutron-proton equilibration as measured by PLF rotation. The simulations demonstrate that a model with a smaller $ K_0 $ paired with a softer symmetry energy, or a larger $ K_0 $ with a slightly stiffer symmetry energy, both offer better indications of neutron-proton equilibration. Conclusion: Considering the uncertainty in $ K_0 $, the slope of the symmetry energy is constrained within the range of $ L = 20 \sim 40 $ MeV, providing valuable insights into the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nuclear incompressibility \( K_0 \) significantly affects the relationship between the alignment angle and rotation time of projectile-like fragments, and therefore must be accounted for when constraining the slope of the symmetry energy from neutron-proton equilibration measurements; a model with smaller \( K_0 \) (200 MeV) paired with a softer symmetry energy, or larger \( K_0 \) (270 MeV) with a slightly stiffer symmetry energy, both provide better descriptions of the equilibration data.

## Method

The authors simulated \(^{70}\)Zn + \(^{70}\)Zn collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a phase-space density constraint method, coupled with the statistical decay code GEMINI for secondary deexcitation. They varied the incompressibility parameter \( K_0 \) between 200 MeV and 270 MeV while adjusting the slope of the symmetry energy \( L \) across values from 20 to 110 MeV, and compared the calculated alignment angles and isospin asymmetries of projectile-like fragment breakup products to experimental data from Jedele et al. (2023).

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility values used were \( K_0 = 200 \) MeV (parameter sets S1-S4) and \( K_0 = 270 \) MeV (parameter sets H1-H3).
- The slope of the symmetry energy was constrained to the range \( L = 20 \sim 40 \) MeV when accounting for the uncertainty in \( K_0 \).
- For the LF, the equilibration rate constant \( c \) for \( K_0 = 270 \) MeV was 0.009 (IQMD only) and 0.005 (IQMD+GEMINI), compared to \( c = 0.014 \) (IQMD only) and 0.009 (IQMD+GEMINI) for \( K_0 = 200 \) MeV.
- For the HF, the \( c \) value for \( K_0 = 270 \) MeV was 0.005 (IQMD only) and for \( K_0 = 200 \) MeV was 0.011 (IQMD only).
- The beam velocity was 0.27 \( c \) and the mid-velocity was 0.135 \( c \).
- The projectile \(^{70}\)Zn has an isospin asymmetry of 0.142.
- The maximum impact parameter was defined as \( b_{max} = 1.2(A_p^{1/3} + A_t^{1/3}) \).

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-proton equilibration in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, previously investigated by Jedele et al. (2023) using the CoMD and AMD models, which found constraints on the slope parameter \( L \) below 51 MeV and 21 MeV respectively. The paper addresses the correlation between incompressibility and symmetry energy parameters identified by Xu et al. (2021) through Bayesian uncertainty quantification, and demonstrates that the uncertainty in \( K_0 \) must be considered when extracting symmetry energy information from isospin equilibration data. The results enable more reliable constraints on the nuclear equation of state by disentangling the effects of the isoscalar and isovector components.
