# Unveiling the structure of pseudoscalar mesons

> A valuable approach to the analysis of hadron physics observables is provided by QCD's equations-of-motion; namely, the Dyson-Schwinger equations. Drawing from a diverse collection of predictions, we revisit: $γγ^* \to$ neutral pseudoscalar transition form factors, their corresponding valence-quark distribution amplitudes and a recent result on the pion distribution functions.

# Unveiling the structure of pseudoscalar mesons

## Key claim
The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to QCD provides a single unifying framework that can simultaneously describe pseudoscalar meson transition form factors, valence-quark distribution amplitudes, and pion distribution functions, with the interplay between dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and the Higgs mechanism determining the shape of these quantities across different meson species.

## Method
The authors compute pseudoscalar meson properties using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations in the rainbow-ladder approximation, self-consistent with the impulse approximation. Valence-quark distribution amplitudes (PDAs) are obtained from Mellin moments of the Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction projected onto the light-front. Transition form factors (TFFs) for $\gamma^*\gamma^* \to M$ are computed via perturbation-theory integral representations of the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude dressing functions. Pion valence-quark distribution functions (PDFs) are reconstructed from their Mellin moments at a hadronic scale $\zeta_H \sim 0.3$ GeV and evolved through DGLAP equations to incorporate gluon and sea content.

## Key numbers
- The renormalization scale for the pion PDF calculation is $\zeta_H \sim 0.3$ GeV, defined such that fully dressed valence-quark quasiparticles are the correct degrees of freedom.
- The experimental scale for the pion PDF comparison is $\zeta = 5.2$ GeV (denoted $\zeta_5$).
- The asymptotic PDA profile is $\phi_{\text{as}}(x) := 6x(1-x)$, to which the $\eta_s$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$ PDAs lie close.
- The $\eta_{c,b}$ TFF results are normalized to $G_M(0) = 1$, with a band surrounding the $\eta_b$ result corresponding to the non-relativistic QCD result of Feng (2015).
- The pion valence-quark PDF at the hadronic scale satisfies $\int_0^1 dx\; q_V(x;\zeta_H) = 1$.

## Context
This work extends the DSE framework previously applied to the pion electromagnetic form factor by Chang (2013) and to individual meson TFFs by Raya (2015, 2016) and Ding (2018). The results for $\eta_c$, $\eta'$, $\eta$, and $\pi^0$ TFFs are compared with experimental data from CELLO, CLEO, Babar, Belle, L3, and PDG. The paper enables future analyses of kaon PDFs and meson generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum distributions (TMDs), which are noted as currently underway.
