# Skyrmions from Instantons inside Domain Walls

> Some years ago, Atiyah and Manton described a method to construct approximate Skyrmion solutions from Yang-Mills instantons. Here we present a dynamical realization of this construction using domain walls in a five-dimensional gauge theory. The non-abelian gauge symmetry is broken in each vacuum but restored in the core of the domain wall, allowing instantons to nestle inside the wall. We show that the worldvolume dynamics of the wall is given by the Skyrme model, including the four-derivative term, and the instantons appear as Skyrmions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a dynamical realization of the Atiyah-Manton construction, showing that Skyrmions in three dimensions can be understood as Yang-Mills instantons living inside a domain wall in a five-dimensional gauge theory, but only when the instanton size is comparable to the domain wall width.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional U(2) gauge theory with an adjoint scalar field φ and four fundamental scalars q_i, with masses chosen so that m_1=m_2=m and m_3=m_4=-m. They find domain wall solutions interpolating between two color-flavor locked vacua, where the U(2) gauge symmetry is broken in each vacuum but restored in the wall core. The collective coordinate g(x^α) on the wall moduli space is promoted to a worldvolume field, and the low-energy effective action is derived by integrating the five-dimensional action over the transverse coordinate, yielding both the two-derivative chiral Lagrangian and the four-derivative Skyrme term.

## Key numbers

* Domain wall tension: T_wall = 4v²m
* Domain wall width in the limit e²v² ≪ m²: L_wall ∼ 2m/e²v²
* Coefficient of the four-derivative term in the same limit: c ≈ (1/60)(m/e⁴v²)
* Bound on Skyrmion mass: M_bound ≈ 4.4π²/e² per baryon number, which is about 1.1 times the single instanton mass M_inst = 4π²/e²
* The true B=1 Skyrmion mass is approximately 1.2 × M_bound, about 30% higher than the instanton mass in Coulomb phase

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Atiyah and Manton (1985) that Yang-Mills instantons provide good approximations to Skyrmions, by embedding the construction in a dynamical field theory. It builds on domain wall studies by Shifman and Yung (2003) and Nitta (2004), and relates to recent efforts to realize Skyrmions as instantons in higher dimensions, including models using orbifold deconstruction by Hill and Son (2004) and the AdS/CFT correspondence by Sugimoto (2004). The approach enables a quantitative understanding of the Atiyah-Manton approximation and opens the possibility of studying Skyrmion properties, including Wess-Zumino terms from Chern-Simons couplings, from the five-dimensional instanton perspective.
