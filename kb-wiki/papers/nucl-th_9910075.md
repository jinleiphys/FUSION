# Nucleon-Nucleon interaction in a chiral constituent quark model

> We study the nucleon-nucleon (NN) problem as a six-quark system in a nonrelativistic chiral constituent quark model where the Hamiltonian contains a linear confinement and a pseudoscalar meson (Goldstone boson) exchange interaction between the quarks. This interaction has a long range Yukawa-type part, depending on the mass of the exchanged meson and a short range part, mainly responsible for the good description of the baryon spectra. We calculate the NN potential in the adiabatic approximation as a function of Z, the separation distance between the centres of the two three-quark clusters. The orbital part of the six-quark states is constructed either from the usual cluster model states or from molecular orbital single particle states. The latter are more realistic, having proper axially and reflectionally symmetries. In both cases the potential presents an important hard core at short distances, explained through the dominance of the [51]{FS} configuration. However in the molecular orbital basis the core is less repulsive, as a consequence of the fact that this basis gives a better upper bound for the energy of the six-quark system. We calculate the potential for the 3S1 and 3S0 channels with two different parametrizations. We find a small (few MeV) attractive pocket for one of these parametrizations. A middle range attraction is simulated by the addition of a sigma-meson exchange interaction between quarks, of a form similar to that of the pseudoscalar meson exchange. The present study is an intermediate, useful step towards dynamical calculations based on the resonating group method.

# Nucleon-Nucleon interaction in a chiral constituent quark model

## Key claim
The nucleon-nucleon potential calculated in the adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer) approximation from a six-quark system with pseudoscalar meson exchange between quarks produces a repulsive hard core at short distances, but only one of the two parametrizations (Model II) yields a small attractive pocket of a few MeV; the other (Model I) requires an additional sigma-meson exchange interaction between quarks to generate any middle-range attraction.

## Method
The authors diagonalized a nonrelativistic chiral constituent quark Hamiltonian containing linear confinement and pseudoscalar meson (Goldstone boson) exchange in a six-quark system, using the adiabatic approximation to compute the NN potential as a function of Z, the separation distance between the centers of two three-quark clusters. The orbital part of the six-quark states was constructed either from standard cluster model single-particle states (right and left Gaussians) or from molecular orbital single-particle states (sigma and pi orbitals) that have proper axial and reflectional symmetries. The potential was calculated for the 3S1 and 3S0 channels using two different parametrizations of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction, denoted Model I and Model II.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass obtained from the 3q variational solution: 970 MeV in Model I, 1311 MeV in Model II
- Relative kinetic energy of two 3q clusters at infinity: K_rel = 0.448 GeV
- Quark mass: m_u,d = 340 MeV
- Pion mass: mu_pi = 139 MeV; eta mass: mu_eta = 547 MeV; eta-prime mass: mu_eta' = 958 MeV
- Model I parameters: V_0 = 0 MeV, C = 0.474 fm^-2, g_8^2/4pi = 0.67, g_0^2/4pi = 1.206, r_0 = 0.43 fm, alpha = 2.91 fm^-1
- Model II parameters: V_0 = -112 MeV, C = 0.77 fm^-2, g_8^2/4pi = 1.24, g_0^2/4pi = 2.765, Lambda_0 = 5.82 fm^-1, kappa = 1.34
- Repulsion reduction when switching from cluster model to molecular orbital basis: about 15% in the 3S1 channel
- Molecular orbital basis has important effect up to about Z = 1.5 fm
- For the sigma-meson exchange added to Model I: mu_sigma = 675 MeV, r'_0 = 0.86 fm, alpha' = 1.47 fm^-1, g_sigma^2/4pi = g_8^2/4pi

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-separation calculation (Bartz and Stancu, 1999) to finite separation distances Z, and builds on the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman, Papp, Plessas, Varga, and Wagenbrunn (1996, 1997) which successfully describes baryon spectra using Goldstone boson exchange instead of one-gluon exchange. The study contrasts with earlier approaches that used one-gluon exchange (Oka and Yazaki, 1984; Shimizu, 1989) and with hybrid models that added scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchange to OGE (Kusainov, Neudatchin, and Obukhovsky, 1991; Zhang, Faessler, Straub, and Glozman, 1994; Fujiwara, Nakamoto, and Suzuki, 1996). The molecular orbital basis, introduced by Stancu and Wilets (1987), is shown to give a less repulsive core than the cluster model basis of Harvey (1981). The work is an intermediate step toward full resonating group method dynamical calculations that would produce phase shifts for comparison with experiment.
