# Oscillation Modes and Gravitational Waves from Strangeon Stars

> The strong interaction at low energy scales determines the equation of state (EOS) of supranuclear matters in neutron stars (NSs). It is conjectured that the bulk dense matter may be composed of strangeons, which are quark clusters with nearly equal numbers of $u$, $d$, and $s$ quarks. To characterize the strong-repulsive interaction at short distance and the nonrelativistic nature of strangeons, a phenomenological Lennard-Jones model with two parameters is used to describe the EOS of strangeon stars (SSs). For the first time, we investigate the oscillation modes of non-rotating SSs and obtain their frequencies for various parameterizations of the EOS. We find that the properties of radial oscillations of SSs are different from those of NSs, especially for stars with relatively low central energy densities. Moreover, we calculate the $f$-mode frequency of nonradial oscillations of SSs within the relativistic Cowling approximation. The frequencies of the $f$-mode of SSs are found to be in the range from $6.7\,$kHz to $ 8.7\,\rm{kHz}$. Finally, we study the universal relations between the $f$-mode frequency and global properties of SSs, such as the compactness and the tidal deformability. The results we obtained are relevant to pulsar timing and gravitational waves, and will help to probe NSs' EOSs and infer nonperturbative behaviours in quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

For the first time, the oscillation modes of non-rotating strangeon stars are investigated, and the authors find that the f-mode frequencies of strangeon stars are in the range from 6.7 kHz to 8.7 kHz within the relativistic Cowling approximation, which is a simplified treatment that neglects metric perturbations and introduces a relative error of approximately 10% to 30% for the f-mode.

## Method

The authors model strangeon matter using a phenomenological Lennard-Jones potential with two parameters (potential depth epsilon and surface baryon number density n_s) to describe the equation of state, assuming 18-quark clusters (quark-alpha). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating spherical stars, then compute radial oscillation frequencies using a self-adjoint differential operator method following Kokkotas (2000). For nonradial oscillations, they adopt the relativistic Cowling approximation, solving the fluid perturbation equations for the l=2 f-mode frequency and studying universal relations with compactness and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- f-mode frequencies of strangeon stars range from 6.7 kHz to 8.7 kHz
- f-mode frequencies at maximal masses of strangeon stars are 6.676 kHz, 6.832 kHz, 7.977 kHz, and 8.684 kHz for different EOS parameterizations
- Maximal compactness for strangeon stars is approximately C_max = 0.27
- Radial f-mode becomes unstable at central densities above 2.83 x 10^15 g cm^-3 for SLy4, 2.70 x 10^15 g cm^-3 for AP4, and 2.05 x 10^15 g cm^-3 for the MIT bag model
- Maximal masses at instability points are 2.04 solar masses for SLy4, 2.21 solar masses for AP4, and 1.96 solar masses for the MIT bag model
- f-mode frequencies at maximal masses of neutron stars and quark stars are 2.907 kHz (SLy4), 2.823 kHz (AP4), 2.562 kHz (SQM3), and 2.597 kHz (MIT bag model with B=60 MeV fm^-3)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strangeon stars by Gao (2021) on global parameters to include oscillation properties, and builds on the radial oscillation formalism of Kokkotas (2000) and nonradial oscillation methods of Sotani (2010). The results enable gravitational wave asteroseismology to distinguish strangeon stars from neutron stars and quark stars, particularly through the much higher f-mode frequencies of strangeon stars, and provide universal relations that can be used with future detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer to probe the equation of state of dense matter.
