# Generalized correlation functions for conductance fluctuations and the mesoscopic spin Hall effect

> We study the spin-Hall conductance fluctuations in ballistic mesoscopic systems. We obtain universal expressions for the spin and charge current fluctuations, cast in terms of current-current autocorrelation functions. We show that the latter are conveniently parametrized as deformed Lorentzian shape lines, functions of an external applied magnetic field and the Fermi energy. We find that the charge current fluctuations show quite unique statistical features at the symplectic-unitary crossover regime. Our findings are based on an evaluation of the generalized transmission coefficients correlation functions within the stub model and are amenable to experimental test.

## Key claim

The paper derives universal expressions for spin and charge current fluctuations in ballistic mesoscopic systems, showing that the charge current correlation functions exhibit a crossover between sub-Lorentzian and super-Lorentzian shapes depending on the magnetic field and the ratio of open modes between transversal and longitudinal terminals, while the spin current fluctuations remain invariant across the symplectic-unitary crossover.

## Method

The authors model a four-terminal chaotic quantum dot with spin-orbit interaction using random matrix theory, specifically the stub model parameterization of the scattering matrix. They employ a diagrammatic technique to compute ensemble averages and covariances of generalized transmission coefficients, then express the spin and charge current fluctuations in terms of these coefficients. The analytical results are validated by numerical simulations using a Hamiltonian approach to the S-matrix with 400 resonant levels and 40 open channels over 100,000 realizations.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square amplitude of universal spin-Hall conductance fluctuations: approximately 0.18 (e/4 pi) for the case n=1 with delta epsilon = delta x = 0
- For pure circular unitary and symplectic ensembles, the Lorentzian shape parameter h_x = 2 (square Lorentzian) and h_epsilon = 1 (Lorentzian)
- Minimum value of h_x in the crossover regime: approximately 0.64 (sub-Lorentzian)
- Maximum value of h_x in the crossover regime: approximately 2.92 (super-Lorentzian)
- Density of maxima for pure ensembles: approximately 0.68 for magnetic field variations and approximately 0.55 for energy variations
- Magnetic field strengths for extrema of density of maxima: x_min approximately 0.20 and x_max approximately 0.47 for n in [0,5]
- Minimum density of maxima for energy variations: approximately 0.52 at x_min approximately 0.26

## Context

This work extends the understanding of mesoscopic fluctuations from two-terminal geometries to four-terminal geometries with spin polarization, building on the random matrix theory approach of Brouwer (1995) and Cremers (2003) for the symplectic-unitary crossover. The results generalize the correlation functions of Ericson fluctuations (Ericson, 1960) known from nuclear scattering to spin-resolved currents in quantum dots, and provide a method to infer spin current magnitudes from charge current density of maxima measurements, which are more experimentally accessible than direct spin current detection.
