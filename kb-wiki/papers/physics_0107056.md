# Variational description of the Helium trimer using correlated hyperspherycal harmonic basis functions

> A variational wave function constructed with correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic functions is used to describe the Helium trimer. This system is known to have a deep bound state. In addition, different potential models predict the existence of a shallow excited state that has been identified as an Efimov state. Using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle the energies and wave functions of both bound states have been obtained by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem. The introduction of a suitable correlation factor reduces considerably the dimension of the basis needed to accurately describe the structure of the system. The most recent helium-helium interactions have been investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a variational wave function constructed with correlated hyperspherical harmonic (CHH) basis functions can provide high-precision upper-bound estimates for both the deep ground state and the shallow excited (Efimov) state of the helium trimer, with the excited state requiring a basis roughly an order of magnitude larger than the ground state for convergence.

## Method

The authors expand the three-body wave function in a basis of hyperspherical harmonic functions multiplied by a Jastrow-type correlation factor (product of one-dimensional correlation functions f(r) for each pair), which accounts for the near-zero probability of finding two atoms at distances smaller than approximately 3 a.u. The Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle is applied, solving a generalized eigenvalue problem for increasing basis sizes until convergence is achieved for both the ground and excited L=0 states. The hyperradial functions are expanded in Laguerre polynomials with an exponential tail, and the nonlinear parameter β is optimized separately for each state (β=0.40 a.u.⁻¹ for ground, β=0.10 a.u.⁻¹ for excited). Five different He-He potentials (HFDB, LM2M2, TTY, SAPT1, SAPT2) are investigated.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy for LM2M2 potential: E₀ = -126.36 mK (converged with k_max=20, m_max=20)
- Excited state binding energy for LM2M2 potential: E₁ = -2.27 mK (converged with k_max=80, m_max=32)
- The excited Efimov state exists only in the interaction strength interval 0.975 ≲ λ ≲ 1.2 for the LM2M2 potential, with maximum binding at λ ≈ 1.05
- Dimer binding energies for the five potentials span a range of 0.51 mK, with SAPT potentials predicting the highest values and LM2M2/TTY the lowest
- Ground state probability of dimer-like structure P_d⁰ ≈ 0.35 for all potentials
- Excited state probability of dimer-like structure P_d¹ ≈ 0.70-0.75 (0.75 for LM2M2 and TTY; lower for SAPT potentials)
- Numerical accuracy of 10⁻³ mK achieved in binding energy calculations
- Grid parameters: h=0.07 a.u., χ=1.008, n_max≈800 covering 0-5000 a.u.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the helium trimer using Faddeev equations, diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) methods, and variational approaches (Esry et al., 1996; Nielsen et al., 1998; Gattobigio et al., 1999), providing variational upper bounds for both bound states across five modern He-He potentials. The CHH basis was previously applied successfully to light nuclei (Kievsky et al., 1997) and is here adapted to atomic clusters. The results enable future extension to larger systems, particularly the tetramer, which the authors state is under study. The asymptotic constants c_ν extracted from the wave functions could potentially be compared with experimental measurements (Ludwig et al., 1998).
