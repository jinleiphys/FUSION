# J/psi Production and Absorption in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> Measured J/Psi production cross sections for 200 and 450 GeV/c protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets are analyzed within a Glauber framework which takes into account energy loss of the beam proton, the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence, and absorption of the J/Psi on nucleons. The best representation is obtained for a coherence time of 0.5 fm/c, previously determined by Drell-Yan production in proton-nucleus collisions, and an absorption cross section of 3.6 mb, which is consistent with the value deduced from photoproduction of the J/Psi on nuclear targets.

## Key claim

The best representation of J/psi production data in proton-nucleus collisions at 200 and 450 GeV/c requires both a coherence time of 0.5 fm/c (previously determined from Drell-Yan production) and a J/psi absorption cross section of 3.6 mb, which resolves the apparent discrepancy with the lower value inferred from photoproduction experiments.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic Glauber framework (LEXUS) that incorporates beam proton energy loss, the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect via a coherence/formation time, and final-state J/psi absorption on nucleons. They parametrized the elementary nucleon-nucleon J/psi production cross section using data from Lourenco (1997) and the x_F dependence from Fermilab experiment E789, then varied the coherence time and absorption cross section to fit NA38 data at 200 and 450 GeV/c on multiple nuclear targets (C, Al, Cu, W, U).

## Key numbers

- Best-fit absorption cross section: sigma_abs = 3.6 mb (assumed energy independent), obtained from combined fit to 200 and 450 GeV/c data
- Best-fit coherence time: tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c, corresponding to n = 3 collisions at 200 GeV/c and n = 5 collisions at 450 GeV/c
- Nucleon-nucleon J/psi production parametrization: B * sigma_NN (x_F > 0) = 37 (1 - m_J/psi / sqrt(s))^12 nb, with x_F dependence dsigma/dx_F proportional to (1 - |x_F|)^5
- Coherence time range consistent with Drell-Yan: 0.3 to 0.5 fm/c, giving 2 < n < 3 at 200 GeV/c and 3 < n < 5 at 450 GeV/c (using total NN cross section 40 mb and nuclear density 0.155 nucleons/fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadronic analysis of Drell-Yan production in 800 GeV/c proton-nucleus collisions (Gale, Jeon, Kapusta, 1998) to J/psi production, and resolves a long-standing puzzle where earlier Glauber analyses without energy loss or coherence effects gave an absorption cross section of 6-7 mb, inconsistent with the value from photoproduction experiments. The results enable more accurate baseline calculations for J/psi suppression in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that a complementary partonic analysis by Gavin and Milana (1996) using parton energy loss of about 1.5 GeV/fm also gave satisfactory results, though the relationship between hadronic and partonic descriptions remains to be clarified.
