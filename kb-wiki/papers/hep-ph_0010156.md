# Neutrino Interactions In Color-Flavor-Locked Dense Matter

> At high density, diquarks could condense in the vacuum with the QCD color spontaneously broken. Based on the observation that the symmetry breaking pattern involved in this phenomenon is essentially the same as that of the Pati-Salam model with broken electroweak--color SU(3) group, we determine the relevant electroweak interactions in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase in high density QCD. We briefly comment on the possible implications on the cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The symmetry breaking pattern of the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase in high-density QCD is essentially identical to that of the minimal Pati-Salam model, allowing the electroweak interactions of CFL matter to be deduced by mapping onto that grand unification scheme; however, the resulting enhancement of neutrino production is suppressed at low temperatures (below ~MeV) and is expected to be effective only during the early proto-neutron star stage when temperatures are around 20-50 MeV.

## Method

The authors map the CFL phase's symmetry breaking pattern onto the Pati-Salam model with spontaneously broken SU(3)_color, SU(2)_L, and U(1)_R gauge groups, using color-flavored Higgs fields analogous to the diquark condensate. They then derive the mixing of gauge bosons (photon, Z, gluons) and compute the resulting weak interaction vertices for neutrino production processes, including gluon-mediated channels and the running of the effective four-Fermi coupling via renormalization group analysis in dense matter.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the gluon mass scale to the W boson mass is estimated as M_V/M_W ~ 10^{-3}, with M_V ~ few 100 MeV and M_W ~ 80 GeV.
- The ratio of strong to electroweak gauge couplings is estimated as g_s/g ~ g_s/g' ~ 10.
- The suppression factor for the gluon fusion process mediated by the Z boson is ~ (M_V/M_W)^2 ~ 10^{-6} compared to conventional neutrino production.
- The gluon fusion into charged lepton pairs is enhanced relative to weak neutral current processes by a factor ~ (M_V/M_W)^{-2} ~ 10^6.
- The effective four-Fermi coupling constant in dense matter runs as G_F^eff = G_F * (mu/Delta)^{16*pi/363}, where mu is the chemical potential and Delta is the superconducting gap.
- The neutrino emissivity from the direct Urca process in CFL quark matter scales as epsilon_direct proportional to alpha_s * rho * Y_e^{1/3} * T^6.
- The neutrino emissivity from the modified Urca process in CFL matter is suppressed by (Delta/mu)^4 compared to normal quark matter.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the CFL phase developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the continuity arguments of Schafer and Wilczek (1999), applying the Pati-Salam grand unification framework (Pati and Salam, 1973) to deduce electroweak couplings in dense matter. The results enable calculations of neutrino cooling rates in neutron stars and proto-neutron stars, building on earlier work by Iwamoto (1980) on Urca processes in quark matter and by Friman and Maxwell (1979) on standard neutron star cooling. The paper identifies open questions about how the enhanced neutrino production correlates with neutrino-medium interactions in the presence of a superconducting gap, which is under investigation.
