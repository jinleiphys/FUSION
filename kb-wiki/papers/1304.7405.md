# Nuclear state densities of odd-mass heavy nuclei in the shell model Monte Carlo approach

> While the shell model Monte Carlo approach has been successful in the microscopic calculation of nuclear state densities, it has been difficult to calculate accurately state densities of odd-even heavy nuclei. This is because the projection on an odd number of particles in the shell model Monte Carlo method leads to a sign problem at low temperatures, making it impractical to extract the ground-state energy in direct Monte Carlo calculations. We show that the ground-state energy can be extracted to a good precision by using level counting data at low excitation energies and the neutron resonance data at the neutron threshold energy. This allows us to extend recent applications of the shell-model Monte Carlo method in even-even rare-earth nuclei to the odd-even isotopic chains of $^{149-155}$Sm and $^{143-149}$Nd. We calculate the state densities of the odd-even samarium and neodymium isotopes and find close agreement with the state densities extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors show that the ground-state energy of odd-mass heavy nuclei can be extracted to good precision by using level counting data at low excitation energies and neutron resonance data at the neutron threshold energy, circumventing the sign problem that otherwise makes direct shell model Monte Carlo calculations impractical for odd-even nuclei.

## Method

The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method was applied to odd-mass samarium and neodymium isotopes using a model space of single-particle orbitals for protons and neutrons in the rare-earth region, with an effective interaction consisting of monopole pairing and multipole-multipole terms. Because the projection on an odd number of particles introduces a sign problem at low temperatures, the ground-state energy was determined by a one-parameter fit of the SMMC thermal excitation energy to an empirical partition function constructed from known low-lying levels and the back-shifted Bethe formula parametrized using neutron resonance data. State densities were then calculated in the saddle-point approximation from the SMMC thermal energy and partition function.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies extracted for eight odd-mass isotopes: $^{143}$Nd at $-191.609 \pm 0.020$ MeV, $^{145}$Nd at $-210.010 \pm 0.028$ MeV, $^{147}$Nd at $-227.870 \pm 0.029$ MeV, $^{149}$Nd at $-245.440 \pm 0.021$ MeV, $^{149}$Sm at $-244.894 \pm 0.026$ MeV, $^{151}$Sm at $-264.968 \pm 0.020$ MeV, $^{153}$Sm at $-284.796 \pm 0.020$ MeV, and $^{155}$Sm at $-304.245 \pm 0.025$ MeV.
- SMMC thermal energy calculations for odd-even nuclei were limited to inverse temperatures $\beta \sim 4-5$ MeV$^{-1}$, compared to $\beta=20$ MeV$^{-1}$ for neighboring even-even nuclei.
- The back-shifted Bethe formula parameters $a$ (MeV$^{-1}$) and $\Delta$ (MeV) for each isotope are tabulated, e.g., $^{143}$Nd: $a=15.951$ MeV$^{-1}$, $\Delta=0.759$ MeV; $^{155}$Sm: $a=17.067$ MeV$^{-1}$, $\Delta=-0.834$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous SMMC state density calculations for even-even rare-earth nuclei (Alhassid et al., 2008; Ozen et al., 2012) to odd-even isotopic chains of samarium and neodymium. The method addresses a limitation of the SMMC approach where projection on an odd number of particles causes a sign problem, building on a Green's function method introduced by Mukherjee and Alhassid (2012) for medium-mass nuclei. The results enable microscopic calculations of state densities for odd-mass heavy nuclei and show close agreement with experimental level counting data and neutron resonance data, supporting the consistency of the SMMC theory with experiment.
