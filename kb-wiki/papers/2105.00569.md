# Constraints on the kinetic mixing parameter $╬Ą^2$ for the light dark photons from dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy range

> The vector $U$-bosons, or so called 'dark photons', are one of the possible candidates for the dark matter mediators. They are supposed to interact with the standard matter via a 'vector portal' due to the $U(1)-U(1)^\prime$ symmetry group mixing which might make them visible in particle and heavy-ion experiments. While there is no confirmed observation of dark photons, the detailed analysis of different experimental data allows to estimate the upper limit for the kinetic mixing parameter $╬Ą^2$ depending on the mass $M_U$ of $U$-bosons which is also unknown. In this study we present theoretical constraints on the upper limit of $╬Ą^2(M_U)$ in the mass range $M_U \le 0.6$ GeV from the comparison of the calculated dilepton spectra with the experimental data from the HADES Collaboration at SIS18 energies where the dark photons are not observed. Our analysis is based on the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach which reproduces well the measured dilepton spectra in $p+p$, $p+A$ and $A+A$ collisions. Additionally to the different dilepton channels originating from interactions and decays of ordinary matter particles (mesons and baryons), we incorporate the decay of hypothetical $U$-bosons to dileptons, $U\to e^+e^-$, where the $U$-bosons themselves are produced by the Dalitz decay of pions $ŽĆ^0\to ╬│U$, $╬Ę$-mesons $╬Ę\to ╬│U$ and Delta resonances $╬ö\to N U$. Our analysis can help to estimate the requested accuracy for future experimental searches of 'light' dark photons by dilepton experiments.

## Key claim

The study provides theoretical upper limits on the kinetic mixing parameter ╬Ą┬▓ for light dark photons (U-bosons) in the mass range M_U Ōēż 0.6 GeV, derived from the condition that the total dilepton yield from standard matter plus dark photon decays must not exceed the standard matter yield by more than a controllable fraction C_U (e.g., 10%) in each mass bin, given that no dark photon signal has been observed in HADES data.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to calculate dilepton spectra from standard matter sources (ŽĆŌü░, ╬Ę, Žē, ╬ö Dalitz decays; direct decays of Žü, Žē, Žå; bremsstrahlung) in p+p, p+Nb, Ar+KCl, and Au+Au collisions at SIS18 energies. They incorporated hypothetical U-boson production via Dalitz decays of ŽĆŌü░ ŌåÆ ╬│U, ╬Ę ŌåÆ ╬│U, and ╬ö ŌåÆ NU, followed by U ŌåÆ eŌü║eŌü╗ decay, and compared the summed spectra to HADES experimental data after applying the HADES acceptance filter and mass/momentum resolution.

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for dark photons by the HADES Collaboration (Agakishiev 2013), which provided upper limits on ╬Ą┬▓ from p+p, p+Nb, and Ar+KCl data, and complements later tighter constraints from A1 at MAMI (Merkel 2014), NA48/2 (Batley 2015), and BaBar (Lees 2014, 2017) that pushed limits to about 10Ōü╗ŌüČ in the 20-500 MeV mass range. The PHSD-based theoretical constraints enable future experimental searches for light dark photons at FAIR and NICA facilities by indicating the required experimental accuracy for detecting a dark photon signal above standard matter backgrounds.
