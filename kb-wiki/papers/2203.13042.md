# Two-neutrino double-$β$ decay in the mapped interacting boson model

> A calculation of two-neutrino double-$β$ ($2νββ$) decay matrix elements within the interacting boson model (IBM) that is based on the nuclear density functional theory is presented. The constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculation using a universal energy density functional (EDF) and a pairing interaction provides potential energy surfaces with triaxial quadrupole degrees of freedom for even-even nuclei corresponding to the initial and final states of the $2νββ$ decays of interest. The SCMF energy surface is then mapped onto the bosonic one, and this procedure determines the IBM Hamiltonian for the even-even nuclei. The same SCMF calculation provides the essential ingredients of the interacting boson fermion-fermion model (IBFFM) for the intermediate odd-odd nuclei and the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators. The EDF-based IBM and IBFFM provide a simultaneous description of excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates for each nucleus, and single-$β$ and $ββ$ decay properties. The calculated $2νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements are compared with experiment and with those from earlier theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements using the interacting boson model (IBM) derived from nuclear density functional theory, without making the closure approximation, and claims that the EDF-based IBM and IBFFM approaches can provide a consistent description of spectroscopic properties and decay properties simultaneously, though the calculated matrix elements show sensitivity to model assumptions and the effective axial vector coupling constant.

## Method

The constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculation using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework with the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force provides potential energy surfaces as functions of triaxial quadrupole deformations for the initial and final even-even nuclei. These SCMF energy surfaces are then mapped onto the bosonic IBM-2 energy surfaces to determine the IBM Hamiltonian parameters without phenomenological adjustment to experimental data. The intermediate odd-odd nuclei are described in the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2), where the quasiparticle energies, occupation probabilities, and boson-fermion interaction strengths are derived from the same SCMF calculation, and the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators are constructed from the microscopic one-particle transfer operators.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutrino double-beta decay half-life is expressed as [τ_{1/2}^{(2ν)}]^{-1} = G_{2ν}|M_{2ν}|^{2}, where G_{2ν} is the phase-space factor in year^{-1}
- The nuclear matrix element M_{2ν} = g_A^2 · m_e c^2 [M^{GT}_{2ν} - (g_V/g_A)^2 M^{F}_{2ν}] with g_V = 1 and g_A = 1.269
- The sums in the Gamow-Teller and Fermi matrix elements are taken over all intermediate states with excitation energies below 10 MeV
- The IBFFM-2 code generates a maximum of N = 200 eigenvalues for a given angular momentum I
- The residual neutron-proton interaction uses r_0 = 1.2A^{1/3} fm for the delta interaction term

## Context

This work extends the SCMF-to-IBM mapping procedure developed by Nomura (2008) and its extensions to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclear systems by Nomura (2016, 2019), which have been applied to shape evolution, octupole deformation, and beta decays in medium-heavy nuclei. The calculation differs from earlier IBM-based double-beta decay studies by Barea (2009, 2013, 2015) and Kotila (2021), which used phenomenological IBM wave functions and the closure approximation, and from the work of Yoshida (2013) which calculated two-neutrino double-beta decays of 128Te and 130Te without closure approximation but with mostly phenomenological ingredients. The approach enables future calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements using the same theoretical framework.
