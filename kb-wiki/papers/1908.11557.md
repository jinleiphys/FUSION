# Theoretical interpretation of the $D^+_s \to π^+ π^0 η$ decay and the nature of $a_0(980)$

> In a recent paper \cite{Ablikim:2019pit}, the BESIII collaboration reported the so-called first observation of pure $W$-annihilation decays $D^+_s \to a^+_0(980) π^0$ and $D_s^+ \to a^0_0(980)π^+$. The measured absolute branching fractions are, however, puzzlingly larger than those of other measured pure $W$-annihilation decays by at least one order of magnitude. In addition, the relative phase between the two decay modes is found to be about 180 degrees. In this letter, we show that all these can be easily understood if the $a_0(980)$ is a dynamically generated state from $\bar{K} K$ and $πη$ interactions in coupled channels. In such a scenario, the $D^+_s$ decay proceeds via internal $W$ emission instead of $W$-annihilation, which has a larger decay rate than $W$-annihilation. The proposed decay mechanism and the molecular nature of the $a_0(980)$ also provide a natural explanation to the measured negative interference between the two decay modes.

# Theoretical interpretation of the $D^+_s \to \pi^+ \pi^0 \eta$ decay and the nature of $a_0(980)$

## Key claim
The puzzlingly large branching fractions and the approximately 180-degree relative phase observed by BESIII for the $D^+_s \to a_0(980)\pi$ decays can be explained without invoking $W$-annihilation if the $a_0(980)$ is a dynamically generated state from $\bar{K}K$ and $\pi\eta$ interactions in coupled channels, allowing the decay to proceed via internal $W$ emission instead.

## Method
The authors constructed a decay amplitude using the chiral unitary approach, where the $a_0(980)$ resonance emerges from the coupled-channel interaction of $K\bar{K}$ and $\pi\eta$ mesons. They modeled the $D^+_s$ decay as internal $W$ emission producing a $K\bar{K}\pi$ intermediate state, with the $K\bar{K}$ pair subsequently interacting via the $T = [1 - VG]^{-1} V$ scattering matrix to produce the $a_0(980)$ resonance. The double differential width was computed and compared to BESIII data after applying the experimental cut $M_{\pi^+\pi^0} > 1.0$ GeV to remove $\rho^+\eta$ contributions.

## Key numbers
- The combined branching ratio for $D^+_s \to a_0(980)\pi$ modes is about $1.5\%$, which is one order of magnitude larger than typical $W$-annihilation decay rates.
- The cutoff used to regularize the loop function $G_{K\bar{K}}$ is $q_{\rm max} = 600$ MeV.
- The parameter $\bar{V}$ was fixed to 17.8 for comparison with experimental event distributions.
- Event distributions were accumulated in bins of 40 MeV, matching the experimental binning.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller (1997), Kaiser (1998), and Nieves (1999), which showed that low-lying scalar mesons like $a_0(980)$ are dynamically generated from meson-meson interactions rather than being $q\bar{q}$ states. It directly addresses the BESIII measurement (Ablikim, 2019) that reported $D^+_s \to a_0(980)\pi$ decays as pure $W$-annihilation, resolving the rate puzzle by showing the process is actually internal $W$ emission. The same production mechanism has been successfully applied to $B^0_s \to J/\psi f_0(980)$ (Liang, 2014; Daub, 2015), $B^0 \to D^0 f_0(500), f_0(980), a_0(980)$ (Liang, 2014), and $D^0 \to K^0 f_0(500), f_0(980), a_0(980)$ (Xie, 2014). The results provide additional support for the molecular nature of $a_0(980)$ and enable future studies of scalar meson production in heavy meson decays.
