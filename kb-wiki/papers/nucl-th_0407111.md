# Self-Consistent Description of Multipole Strength in Exotic Nuclei I: Method

> We use the canonical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov basis to implement a completely self-consistent quasiparticle-random-phase approximation with arbitrary Skyrme energy density functionals and density-dependent pairing functionals. The point of the approach is to accurately describe multipole strength functions in spherical even-even nuclei, including weakly-bound drip-line systems. We describe the method and carefully test its accuracy, particularly in handling spurious modes. To illustrate our approach, we calculate isoscalar and isovector monopole, dipole, and quadrupole strength functions in several Sn isotopes, both in the stable region and at the drip lines.

# Self-Consistent Description of Multipole Strength in Exotic Nuclei I: Method

## Key claim

The authors implement a fully self-consistent quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) in the canonical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) basis using arbitrary Skyrme energy density functionals and density-dependent pairing functionals, but the method is restricted to spherical even-even nuclei and requires a very large single-quasiparticle space (up to 200-300 quasiparticle states per nucleon type) to achieve the claimed accuracy, particularly for handling spurious modes.

## Method

The authors solve the spherical HFB equations in coordinate space within a large spherical box (typically 20 fm radius) using quadruple precision, a discretization length of 0.05 fm, a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 200 MeV, and maximum angular momentum j_max = 15/2 or 21/2. They construct the canonical basis by orthonormalizing the sum of upper and lower quasiparticle wave function components, then diagonalize the density matrix in this basis to obtain canonical states. The QRPA equations are solved in the canonical basis using the same Skyrme functional (SkM*) and volume pairing functional as in the HFB calculation, with two-quasiparticle spaces of order 20,000 x 20,000 matrices for neutron-rich Sn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Discretization length: 0.05 fm
- Quasiparticle energy cutoff: 200 MeV
- Maximum angular momentum: j_max = 15/2 (for N <= 82) or 21/2 (for N > 82)
- Box radius: 20 fm (varied to 25 fm for tests)
- Pairing parameter V_0 = -77.5 MeV fm^3 (volume pairing)
- Neutron pairing gap in ^174Sn: Delta_n = 1.016 MeV
- Spurious 0+ state energy in ^174Sn: 0.171 MeV (with particle-number squared matrix element 0.120)
- Spurious 1- state energies: 0.964 MeV for ^100Sn, 0.713 MeV for ^120Sn, 0.319 MeV for ^174Sn, 0.349 MeV for ^176Sn
- First physical 1- excited state energies: 7.958 MeV for ^100Sn, 7.729 MeV for ^120Sn, 3.485 MeV for ^174Sn, 2.710 MeV for ^176Sn
- Isoscalar 0+ energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in ^174Sn: right-hand side = 35215 e^2 MeV fm^4, QRPA value = 34985 +/- 15 e^2 MeV fm^4 (summed up to E_k = 50 MeV)
- Isoscalar 1- EWSR in ^174Sn: right-hand side = 403310 e^2 MeV fm^6, QRPA value = 400200 e^2 MeV fm^6
- Isoscalar 1- EWSR in ^176Sn: right-hand side = 406576 e^2 MeV fm^6, QRPA value = 407100 e^2 MeV fm^6
- Isoscalar 2+ EWSR: 37222 e^2 MeV fm^4 for ^120Sn (QRPA: 37030), 34971 e^2 MeV fm^4 for ^174Sn (QRPA: 35010)

## Context

This work extends fully self-consistent QRPA calculations previously applied to beta-decay and Gamow-Teller strength (Engel 1999, Bender 2002) to charge-conserving multipole modes near the drip line, addressing the challenge of spurious states in monopole and dipole channels. It contrasts with earlier coordinate-space Green's function approaches (Shlomo 1975, Hamamoto 1997-2002) that often ignored spin-orbit and Coulomb residual interactions, and with matrix-formulation Skyrme-RPA calculations (Warr 1987, Colo 2003) that discretized the continuum in harmonic-oscillator bases. The method enables systematic investigation of low-energy multipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the dramatic increase in isoscalar dipole strength near the neutron drip line, and provides a framework for testing and improving Skyrme energy density functionals against collective excitation data.
