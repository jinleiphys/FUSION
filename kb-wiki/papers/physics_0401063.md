# Inaccurate use of asymptotic formulas

> The asymptotic form of the plane wave decomposition into spherical waves, which is used to express the scattering amplitude in terms of phase shifts, is incorrect. We explain why and show how to circumvent the mathematical inconsistency.

# Inaccurate use of asymptotic formulas

## Key claim
The standard asymptotic form of the plane wave decomposition into spherical waves, as given in textbooks such as Schiff and Landau & Lifshitz, is mathematically meaningless because the resulting series is divergent and cannot even be treated as an asymptotic expansion of the plane wave at large distances. The authors show that the derivation of the scattering amplitude formula in terms of phase shifts can be corrected by using a projection method that avoids the mathematically inconsistent series.

## Method
The authors demonstrate that the asymptotic approximation \( j_l(kr) \approx \sin(kr - \pi l/2)/(kr) \) requires the condition \( kr \gg \frac{1}{2} l(l+1) \), which is violated for sufficiently large \( l \) when summing over all partial waves. They derive the second term of the \( 1/z \) expansion of the spherical Bessel function to establish this condition. They then present an alternative derivation of the scattering amplitude formula that avoids the problematic asymptotic series by projecting the wave function onto individual Legendre polynomials, equating the asymptotic forms of the projections term-by-term rather than summing the divergent series.

## Key numbers
- The condition for the asymptotic approximation \( j_l(kr) \approx \sin(kr - \pi l/2)/(kr) \) to hold is \( kr \gg \frac{1}{2} l(l+1) \).
- The second term of the \( 1/z \) expansion of \( j_l(z) \) is \( \frac{1}{2} l(l+1) \frac{\cos(z - \pi l/2)}{z^2} \), with corrections of order \( \mathcal{O}(1/z^3) \).
- For the special case \( \cos\Theta = 1 \), the real part of the divergent series becomes \( \frac{\sin(kr)}{kr} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (4n + 1) \) and the imaginary part becomes \( -\frac{\cos(kr)}{kr} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (4n + 3) \), both of which diverge for any value of \( r \).

## Context
This work corrects a mathematical inconsistency present in standard quantum mechanics textbooks, including Schiff and Landau & Lifshitz, where the asymptotic plane wave decomposition is used to derive the scattering amplitude formula. The authors show that while the final formula for the scattering amplitude in terms of phase shifts is correct, the standard derivation using the divergent asymptotic series is mathematically invalid. The corrected derivation using projection onto Legendre polynomials provides a rigorous alternative that avoids the inconsistency. This is relevant for any calculation involving sums over partial waves of asymptotic wave functions, such as correlation functions in nuclear physics.
