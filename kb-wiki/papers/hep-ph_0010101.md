# Signatures of Thermal Dilepton Radiation at RHIC

> The properties of thermal dilepton production from heavy-ion collisions in the RHIC energy regime are evaluated for invariant masses ranging from 0.5 to 3 GeV. Using an expanding thermal fireball to model the evolution through both quark-gluon and hadronic phases various features of the spectra are addressed. In the low-mass region, due to an expected large background, the focus is on possible medium modifications of the narrow resonance structures from $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons, whereas in the intermediate-mass region the old idea of identifying QGP radiation is reiterated including effects of chemical under-saturation in the early stages of central Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

Thermal dilepton radiation in the intermediate-mass region (IMR) at RHIC may be competitive with Drell-Yan production, but only if the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is chemically equilibrated; chemical under-saturation in the early stages of central Au+Au collisions could significantly reduce the QGP signal, making its identification more challenging than the abstract suggests.

## Method

The author used an expanding thermal fireball model to simulate the space-time evolution of central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, covering both hadronic and quark-gluon phases. Dilepton production rates were computed from hadronic degrees of freedom using in-medium spectral functions for the ρ, ω, and φ mesons (including selfenergy corrections from meson-baryon interactions, thermal Bose enhancement, and collisional broadening), and from the QGP phase using lowest-order perturbative qq̅ annihilation. The thermal yields were integrated over the fireball evolution and compared to background sources including Drell-Yan pairs, open charm decays, and hadronic cocktail contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the ρ meson at (T, μ_B) = (180, 0) MeV, baryon and antibaryon densities each reach ρ_B = ρ_B̅ ≈ 0.46 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear matter density)
- At T = 180 MeV, the φ meson vacuum width increases by over a factor of 7 to approximately 32 MeV due to hadronic rescattering
- At T = 150–180 MeV, thermal Bose factors enhance dilepton rates by a factor of 3–10 over the free case around invariant masses M ≈ 0.4 GeV
- At T = 180 MeV, the in-medium hadronic rate in the ω resonance region is suppressed by a factor of about 6 relative to the free rate, and agrees with the qq̅ rate within a factor of 2
- At T = 150 MeV, the in-medium suppression factor is about 3, and in-medium hadronic and qq̅ rates differ by a factor of about 4
- The φ meson collisional broadening at T_0 = 150 MeV is Γ_φ^coll(T_0) = 9 MeV, scaling as (T/T_0)³
- For RHIC, the charged particle rapidity density is dN_ch/dy ≈ 800 and net baryon rapidity density is dN_B^net/dy ≈ 20 at midrapidity for √s = 200 AGeV
- The baryon chemical potential at chemical freezeout (T_ch = 180 MeV) is μ_B^ch = 27 MeV
- At chemical freezeout for RHIC, net baryon density is ρ_B,ch^net = 0.14 ρ_0 and total baryon density is ρ_B,ch^tot = 0.54 ρ_0

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed for interpreting dilepton measurements at the CERN-SpS (CERES, HELIOS3, NA38/50 experiments) to the higher-energy RHIC regime, where the QGP phase is expected to occupy larger space-time volumes with higher initial temperatures. The study builds on prior analyses by Rapp and Wambach (2000) for SpS conditions, and addresses the interplay of thermal emission with Drell-Yan and open charm backgrounds as previously evaluated by Gavin et al. (1996), Shuryak (1997), and Lin and Wang (1998). The results enable experimental planning for the PHENIX detector at RHIC, particularly for distinguishing QGP signals from hadronic emission in the intermediate-mass region and for detecting medium modifications of vector mesons in the low-mass region.
