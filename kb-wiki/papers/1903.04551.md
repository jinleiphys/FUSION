# The Dynamical Diquark Model: First Numerical Results

> We produce the first numerical predictions of the dynamical diquark model of multiquark exotic hadrons. Using Born-Oppenheimer potentials calculated numerically on the lattice, we solve coupled and uncoupled systems of Schroedinger equations to obtain mass eigenvalues for multiplets of states that are, at this stage, degenerate in spin and isospin. Assuming reasonable values for these fine-structure splittings, we obtain a series of bands of exotic states with a common parity eigenvalue that agree well with the experimentally observed charmoniumlike states, and we predict a number of other unobserved states. In particular, the most suitable fit to known pentaquark states predicts states below the charmonium-plus-nucleon threshold. Finally, we examine the strictest form of Born-Oppenheimer decay selection rules for exotics and, finding them to fail badly, we propose a resolution by relaxing the constraint that exotics must occur as heavy-quark spin-symmetry eigenstates.

# The Dynamical Diquark Model: First Numerical Results

## Key claim
The dynamical diquark model, using Born-Oppenheimer potentials from lattice QCD as input to Schrödinger equations, produces mass spectra for tetraquark and pentaquark states that are broadly consistent with observed charmoniumlike exotics, but only if one relaxes the constraint that exotics must occur as heavy-quark spin-symmetry eigenstates, since the strictest form of Born-Oppenheimer decay selection rules fails badly.

## Method
The authors solved uncoupled and coupled systems of Schrödinger equations using Born-Oppenheimer potentials calculated numerically on the lattice by the Juge-Kuti-Morningstar (JKM) collaboration and by Capitani et al. (CPRRW), as well as the phenomenological Cornell potential from Barnes, Godfrey, and Swanson (BGS) for the ground-state potential. For tetraquarks, the heavy sources are diquark-antidiquark pairs; for pentaquarks, they are triquark-diquark pairs. The coupled equations for the Π⁺ᵤ and Σ⁻ᵤ potentials were solved using numerical techniques from Johnson (1978) and Hutson (1994), with fine-structure spin-dependent splittings neglected at this stage.

## Key numbers
- Fixing to X(3872) as a Σ⁺_g(1S) state gives a diquark mass m_δ = 1.8747 GeV (JKM potential) or 1.8535 GeV (CPRRW potential)
- Fixing to Z_c(4430) as a Σ⁺_g(2S) state gives m_δ = 1.8946 GeV (JKM) or 1.8745 GeV (CPRRW)
- The Σ⁺_g(1S) mass eigenvalue when fixing to X(3872) is 3.8711 GeV (JKM), 3.8721 GeV (CPRRW), or 3.8718 GeV (BGS)
- The Σ⁺_g(2S) mass eigenvalue when fixing to Z_c(4430) is 4.4782 GeV (JKM), 4.4781 GeV (CPRRW), or 4.4781 GeV (BGS)
- The Π⁺ᵤ(1P) and Σ⁻ᵤ(1P) coupled system gives mass 4.9156 GeV (JKM) or 4.8786 GeV (CPRRW) when fixing to X(3872)
- The Σ⁻ᵤ(1S) state mass is 5.3139 GeV (JKM) or 5.2897 GeV (CPRRW) when fixing to X(3872)
- The classical turning point separation for the δ-δbar pair in the original dynamical diquark picture was calculated to be 1.16 fm

## Context
This work extends the dynamical diquark picture proposed by Brodsky, Lebed, and collaborators (2014, 2015) into a fully predictive numerical model, building on the formalism developed by Lebed (2017). It applies the Born-Oppenheimer approach previously used for hybrid mesons by Berwein et al. (2015) and for multiquark exotics by Braaten et al. (2013-2014), but with the key difference that the light quarks belong to the diquarks (treated as heavy sources) rather than to the light degrees of freedom. The results enable future inclusion of fine-structure spin-dependent splittings and more complete coupled-channel calculations, and they provide a framework for predicting numerous unobserved tetraquark and pentaquark states that can be tested against experimental searches.
