# Parity violation in radiative neutron capture on deuteron

> Parity violating (PV) effects in neutron-deuteron radiative capture are studied using Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) and effective field theory weak potentials. The values of PV effects are calculated using wave functions, obtained by solving three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space for phenomenological strong potentials. The relations between physical observables and low-energy constants are presented, and dependencies of the calculated PV effects on strong and weak potentials are discussed. The presented analysis shows the possible reason for the existing discrepancy in PV nuclear data analysis using the DDH approach and reveals a new opportunity to study short range interactions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that parity-violating (PV) observables in neutron-deuteron radiative capture can be calculated using both DDH and effective field theory (EFT) weak potentials, and that the observed discrepancy in PV nuclear data analysis using the DDH approach may arise from the different operator structures and radial dependencies of the weak potentials, particularly the short-range interactions.

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space for phenomenological strong potentials (AV18, Reid, NijmII, INOY, and AV18+UIX) to obtain wave functions for the initial neutron-deuteron scattering state and final triton bound state. Parity-violating electric dipole (E1) amplitudes were calculated in first-order perturbation theory using three types of weak potentials: the standard DDH meson-exchange potential, a pionless EFT potential, and a pionful EFT potential. The PV observables (circular polarization of photons, asymmetry relative to neutron polarization, and asymmetry relative to deuteron polarization) were then expressed as linear combinations of matrix elements for each weak potential operator.

## Key numbers

- Total neutron-deuteron radiative capture cross section: sigma_tot = 0.49(1) mb, consistent with experimental data
- M1 amplitudes (in fm^(3/2)): M1_(1/2) = +21.87 + 10.76[(B_model/B_exp)^(-2.5) - 1], M1_(3/2) = -12.24 - 11.35[(B_model/B_exp)^(-2.5) - 1]
- DDH potential results for PV observables expressed in terms of meson-nucleon coupling constants: a_n = 0.42 h^1_pi - 0.17 h^0_rho + 0.085 h^1_rho + 0.008 h^2_rho - 0.238 h^0_omega + 0.086 h^1_omega - 0.010 h'^1_rho = 4.11 x 10^(-7)
- DDH potential results: P_gamma = -1.05 h^1_pi + 0.19 h^0_rho - 0.096 h^1_rho - 0.01 (truncated)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of PV effects in elastic neutron-deuteron scattering by Schiavilla (2008) and Song (2010) to the inelastic radiative capture process, which is more sensitive to short-range interactions. The results are compared with earlier calculations by Moskalev (1969), Hadjimichael (1974), McKellar (1974), and Desplanques (1986). The analysis reveals that the DDH approach may have systematic limitations in describing nuclear PV data, and the EFT approach provides a model-independent framework for parameterizing PV effects, though all low-energy constants remain to be determined from experimental data.
