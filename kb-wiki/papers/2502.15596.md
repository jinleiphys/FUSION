# Projective Imaging of High-Energy Nuclei via Coherent Exclusive Vector Meson Production in Electron-Nucleus Collisions

> One of the major goals of modern nuclear experiments is to study the distributions of gluons inside nuclei at high energy. A key measurement is the coherent exclusive vector meson (VM) production in diffractive electron-nucleus collisions, where the gluon spatial distribution inside the nucleus can be obtained through a Fourier transform of the squared nuclear momentum transfer ($|t|$) distribution. This research aims to overcome the two main obstacles of the $|t|$ measurement: limited precision in measuring $|t|$ arising from the momentum resolution of the outgoing electron and the overwhelming incoherent background. We demonstrate that by measuring the projected $|t|$ distribution along the direction perpendicular to the electron scattering plane, the effect of the outgoing electron's momentum resolution can be effectively mitigated, and the diffractive pattern is largely restored. Furthermore, we propose to measure the angular distribution of the VM's decay daughters to statistically remove the incoherent background.

## Key claim

By measuring the projected $|t|$ distribution along the direction perpendicular to the electron scattering plane, the effect of the outgoing electron's momentum resolution can be effectively mitigated and the diffractive pattern largely restored, provided a selective phase-space constraint (wedge cut) is applied; the incoherent background can be statistically removed by exploiting the angular distribution of vector meson decay daughters in collisions of transversely polarized electron beams.

## Method

The authors decompose the squared four-momentum transfer $|t|$ into components along and perpendicular to the electron scattering plane, defining $t_{\hat{n}}$ as the projection along the normal direction $\hat{n}$. They apply a wedge cut ($\theta_{\rm max} = \pi/12$) in the transverse $t$ plane to select events where the $t_y$ component dominates over $t_x$, thereby eliminating the dependence on the outgoing electron's momentum resolution. For the incoherent background, they propose measuring the $\cos(2\phi)$ modulation of the vector meson decay daughters relative to $\hat{n}$, where the fraction of coherent events equals $\langle\cos(2\phi)\rangle/C$ with $C$ being the probability for the incoming electron to flip its spin.

## Key numbers

- The coherent $|t|$ distribution is smeared with a $|t|$-independent resolution of 25 MeV/$c$, inspired by the EIC detector design.
- With a wedge cut of $\theta_{\rm max} = \pi/12$, approximately 83% of coherent VM events are excluded for the 25 MeV/$c$ resolution case.
- The Yukawa potential range parameter used in the form factor is $a = 0.7$ fm.
- The form factor is based on a Woods-Saxon distribution approximated as a hard sphere potential with nuclear radius $R$ and atomic mass number $A$.

## Context

This work addresses two critical challenges identified in EIC detector simulations by the ECCE, ATHENA, and ePIC proto-collaborations (Adkins 2022, ATHENA 2022), which showed that even with the highest achievable detector resolution, the diffractive pattern for coherent vector meson production remains unresolved. The projective technique extends prior approaches to $|t|$ reconstruction (Method L from the EIC Yellow Report) and enables a high-priority physics program highlighted by the National Academies report on the EIC. The technique can be applied to other electron-hadron experiments including JLab (Paolone 2014, 2016), EicC in China (Anderle 2021), and LHeC at CERN (Agostini 2021).
