# Two-neutron halo nuclei in one dimension: dineutron correlation and breakup reaction

> We propose a simple schematic model for two-neutron halo nuclei. In this model, the two valence neutrons move in a one-dimensional mean field, interacting with each other via a density-dependent contact interaction. We first investigate the ground state properties, and demonstrate that the dineutron correlation can be realized with this simple model due to the admixture of even- and odd-parity single-particle states. We then solve the time-dependent two-particle Schrödinger equation under the influence of a time-dependent one-body external field, in order to discuss the effect of dineutron correlation on nuclear breakup processes. The time evolution of two-particle density shows that the dineutron correlation enhances the total breakup probability, especially for the two-neutron breakup process, in which both the valence neutrons are promoted to continuum scattering states. We find that the interaction between the two particles definitely favours a spatial correlation of the two outgoing particles, which are mainly emitted in the same direction.

# Two-neutron halo nuclei in one dimension: dineutron correlation and breakup reaction

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple one-dimensional three-body model for two-neutron halo nuclei and demonstrate that dineutron correlation enhances the total breakup probability, especially for the two-neutron breakup process where both valence neutrons are promoted to continuum scattering states. However, the model uses a bound dineutron (bound by 0.92 MeV) and a loosely bound single-particle state at -0.15 MeV, meaning the system is not Borromean and corresponds to a limit of strong neutron-neutron interaction, unlike actual Borromean nuclei such as 11Li and 6He.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional three-body model where two valence neutrons move in a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and interact via a density-dependent contact (delta) interaction of surface type. They solved the stationary two-particle Schrodinger equation by expanding the ground state wave function in a basis of single-particle states (including continuum states discretized in a box of 90 fm) and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian. For the breakup dynamics, they solved the time-dependent two-particle Schrodinger equation under a time-dependent Gaussian external field, using an implicit method for time propagation and projecting out the ground state component to isolate breakup contributions.

## Key numbers

* The Woods-Saxon potential parameters were R = 1.27 x 9^(1/3) fm, V0 = -50.085 MeV, and a = 0.67 fm, chosen to mimic the 11Li nucleus.
* The last bound single-particle state had energy epsilon_WS = -0.15 MeV.
* The three-body ground state energy was set to E_g.s. = -1 MeV.
* The pairing interaction strength was g = -12.35 MeV x fm for a cutoff energy of 30 MeV.
* The dineutron (two-neutron bound state) was bound by 0.92 MeV.
* The ground state separation energy from the dineutron threshold was less than 100 keV.
* The external field parameters were V_c = 3 MeV, sigma_t = 2.1 hbar/MeV, and sigma_x = 2.2 fm.
* At ct = 600 fm, the norms of the breakup components were: P_bb = 4.58 x 10^(-4), P_bc = 0.116, and P_cc = 0.466 for the correlated case.

## Context

This work extends a one-dimensional model previously developed for one-neutron halo nuclei by Dasso and Vitturi (2009) to the two-neutron case. The one-dimensional approach provides a transparent picture of dineutron correlation that complements three-dimensional studies by Bertsch and Esbensen (1991), Zhukov et al. (1993), and Hagino and Sagawa (2005). The model enables detailed study of the interplay between ground-state dineutron correlation and final-state interactions in nuclear breakup processes, complementing earlier work by Hagino, Sagawa, and collaborators (2009) on Coulomb breakup of 11Li and 6He, and suggesting that nuclear breakup may reveal dineutron correlation more clearly than Coulomb breakup.
