# Ground state baryons in the flux-tube three-body confinement model using Diffusion Monte Carlo

> We make a systematical diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculation for all ground state baryons in two confinement scenarios, the pairwise confinement and the three-body flux-tube confinement. With the baryons as an example, we illustrate a feasible procedure to investigate the few-quark states with possible few-body confinement mechanisms, which can be extended to the multiquark states easily. For each baryon, we extract the mass, mean-square radius, charge radius, and the quark distributions. We use the Jackknife resampling method to estimate the statistical uncertainties of masses to be less than 1 MeV. To determine the baryon charge radii, we include the constituent quark size effect, which is fixed by the experimental and lattice QCD results. Our results show that both two-body and three-body confinement mechanisms can give a good description of the experimental data if the parameters are chosen properly. In the flux-tube confinement, introducing different tension parameters for the baryons and mesons are necessary, specifically, $σ_Y= 0.9204 σ_{Q\bar{Q}}$. The lesson from the calculation of the nucleon mass with the DMC method is that the improper pre-assignment of the channels may prevent us from obtaining the real ground state. With this experience, we obtain the real ground state (the $η_c η_c$ threshold with the di-meson configuration) of the $cc\bar{c}\bar{c}$ system with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ starting from the diquark-antidiquark spin-color channels alone, which is hard to achieve in the variational method and was not obtained in the previous DMC calculations.

## Key claim

Both the pairwise (Δ-type) and three-body flux-tube (Y-type) confinement mechanisms can reproduce experimental baryon masses if parameters are chosen properly, but in the flux-tube scenario a different string tension for baryons versus mesons is required, specifically σ_Y = 0.9204 σ_{Q\bar{Q}}.

## Method

The authors performed diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) simulations for all ground-state baryons using a nonrelativistic quark model Hamiltonian with one-gluon-exchange terms (Coulomb, hyperfine, constant shift) and two different confinement potentials: a pairwise linear Δ-type potential proportional to the sum of interquark distances, and a Y-type three-body flux-tube potential proportional to the minimal Steiner-tree length connecting the three quarks. The trial wave function used a Jastrow-type product of exponentials, and the Jackknife resampling method over uncorrelated blocks of 500 steps was employed to estimate statistical uncertainties. For the Y-type confinement, the string tension was calibrated by fixing the Ω⁻(sss) mass to its experimental value of 1.672 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Statistical uncertainties of all baryon masses are less than 1 MeV; as an example, the Ω⁻(sss) mass uncertainty is 0.3 MeV.
- The Y-type string tension ratio is σ_Y = 0.9204 σ_{Q\bar{Q}}, consistent with the lattice QCD best-fit ratio of 0.9355 from Takahashi (2002).
- The quark model parameters used are from the AL1 model: m_u = m_d = 0.315 GeV, m_s = 0.577 GeV, m_c = 1.836 GeV, m_b = 5.227 GeV, κ = 0.5069, κ′ = 1.8609, λ = 0.1653 GeV², Λ = 0.8321 GeV, A = 1.6553 GeV^{B−1}, B = 0.2204, C = 0.00202 GeV⁴.
- The DMC simulation used 1×10⁴ walkers, evolved for 1×10⁴ steps (single-channel) or 2×10⁴ steps (coupled-channel) with Δt = 0.01 GeV⁻¹ per step, averaging over the last 5000 steps.

## Context

This work extends the diffusion Monte Carlo approach previously applied to fully-heavy tetraquarks by Bai (2016) and Gordillo (2020) to the full baryon octet and decuplet, including light, strange, charm, and bottom quarks. It directly compares the Δ-type confinement used in the nonrelativistic quark model of Silvestre-Brac (1996) with the Y-type flux-tube confinement favored by lattice QCD simulations of Takahashi (2000, 2002). The results demonstrate that DMC can handle the complicated Y-type potential without the basis-expansion difficulties of variational methods, and the authors note that improper pre-assignment of channels in DMC can prevent finding the true ground state, as illustrated by the cc\bar{c}\bar{c} system where the η_cη_c threshold was reached starting from diquark-antidiquark channels alone.
