# Soft-Gluon Production Due to a Gluon Loop in a Constant Chromo-Electric Background Field

> We obtain an exact result for the soft gluon production and its p_T distribution due to a gluon loop in a constant chromo-electric background field E^a with arbitrary color. Unlike Schwinger's result for e^+e^- pair production in QED which depends only on one gauge invariant quantity, the Electric field E, we find that the p_T distribution of the gluons depend on two gauge invariant quantities, E^aE^a and [d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2.

# Soft-Gluon Production Due to a Gluon Loop in a Constant Chromo-Electric Background Field

## Key claim

The authors derive an exact, non-perturbative result for the transverse momentum distribution of soft gluons produced from vacuum polarization by a constant chromo-electric background field in SU(3) QCD, finding that the distribution depends on two gauge-invariant quantities, $E^aE^a$ and $[d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2$, unlike Schwinger's QED result which depends only on the single electric field strength $E$.

## Method

The authors use the background field method in QCD with covariant background gauge fixing in Feynman-'t Hooft gauge, evaluating the one-loop effective action for a gluon loop and a ghost loop in a constant chromo-electric field $E^a$ along the beam direction (z-axis) with gauge $A_0^a = 0$, $A_3^a = -E^a x^0$. They diagonalize the matrix $\Lambda^{ab} = if^{abc}E^c$ in color space, reduce the problem to harmonic oscillators via similarity transformations, and extract the imaginary part of the effective action to obtain the gluon production rate.

## Key numbers

- The gluon production rate per unit time, volume, and transverse momentum is given by $\frac{dN_{gg}}{dt d^3x d^2p_T} = \frac{1}{4\pi^3} \sum_{j=1}^3 |g\lambda_j| \ln[1 + e^{-\pi p_T^2 / |g\lambda_j|}]$, where $\lambda_j$ are the positive square roots of eigenvalues of the matrix $if^{abc}E^c$.
- The eigenvalues $\lambda_j^2$ are expressed in terms of the Casimir invariants $C_1 = E^aE^a$ and $C_2 = [d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2$ via $\lambda_1^2 = \frac{C_1}{2}[1 - \cos\theta]$, $\lambda_2^2 = \frac{C_1}{2}[1 + \cos(\pi/3 - \theta)]$, $\lambda_3^2 = \frac{C_1}{2}[1 + \cos(\pi/3 + \theta)]$, with $\cos^3\theta = -1 + 6C_2/C_1^3$.
- The corresponding QED result for massless fermion pair production is $\frac{dN_{e^+e^-}}{dt d^3x d^2p_T} = -\frac{|eE|}{4\pi^3} \ln[1 - e^{-\pi p_T^2 / |eE|}]$.
- The authors note applicability to RHIC (Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of quark-antiquark pair production in constant chromo-electric fields by Yildiz and Cox (1980) and total gluon production rates for SU(2) by Savvidy (1977) and for SU(3) by Yildiz (1980), providing the previously missing $p_T$ distribution for gluon production. The result enables more detailed modeling of the gluon spectrum from the classical chromo-field created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where understanding soft gluon production is necessary for interpreting quark-gluon plasma experiments. The authors intend to add a chromo-magnetic field in future work.
