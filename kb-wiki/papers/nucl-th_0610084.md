# Phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter

> We study the phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter within the SU(3) quark mean field model and NJL model. The SU(3) quark mean field model is used to give the equation of state for nuclear matter, while the equation of state for color superconducting quark matter is calculated within the NJL model. It is found that at low temperature, the phase transition from nuclear to color superconducting quark matter will take place when the density is of order 2.5$ρ_0$ - 5$ρ_0$. At zero density, the quark phase will appear when the temperature is larger than about 148 MeV. The phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter is always first order, whereas the transition between color superconducting quark matter and normal quark matter is second order.

## Key claim

The phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter is always first order, but this conclusion depends on including the finite volume (excluded volume) effect of nucleons; without this effect, the model predicts no phase transition at all. The second-order transition reported in some earlier work is actually a transition between chiral symmetry breaking quark matter and chiral symmetry restored quark matter, not a transition from hadronic matter to quark matter.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid approach: the SU(3) quark mean field model (with nucleon finite-size effects via excluded volume) provides the equation of state for nuclear matter, while the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a diquark condensate gives the equation of state for color superconducting quark matter. The phase transition is determined by equating pressures and imposing chemical equilibrium conditions (μ_p = 2μ_u + μ_d, μ_n = μ_u + 2μ_d) between the two phases. The NJL model parameters are fixed by the vacuum quark condensate and constituent quark mass, with a cutoff Λ_q = 634.7 MeV and coupling G_1 = 5.6 GeV⁻².

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the phase transition from nuclear matter to color superconducting quark matter occurs at nucleon chemical potential μ_N ≈ 1.5 – 1.7 GeV.
- At zero baryon density, the quark phase appears at temperature T ≈ 148 MeV.
- For nucleon radius R = 0.6 fm, at zero temperature the transition occurs at nuclear density ≈ 0.76 fm⁻³ (≈ 4.8ρ₀) to quark matter density ≈ 1.17 fm⁻³.
- For nucleon radius R = 0.75 fm, at zero temperature the transition occurs at nuclear density ≈ 0.44 fm⁻³ (≈ 2.8ρ₀) to quark matter density ≈ 0.85 fm⁻³.
- The energy gap (diquark condensate) disappears when temperature exceeds about 52 MeV.
- The bag constant is chosen as B^(1/4) = 209 MeV, with B′^(1/4) = 605.7 MeV.
- The phase transition between chiral symmetry breaking and color superconducting quark matter occurs at quark chemical potential μ_q ≈ 342 MeV at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the QCD phase diagram by Berges and Schwarz (2000) and by Schwarz (2001), which mapped transitions between different quark matter phases but did not include a realistic hadronic equation of state. The authors replace the chiral symmetry breaking quark matter phase with a nuclear matter description from the SU(3) quark mean field model developed by Wang et al. (2003, 2004), and incorporate nucleon finite-size effects following the excluded volume method of Panda (1997). The results enable more realistic predictions for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and the authors note that future work should include strange quarks and gauge field fluctuations that would change the second-order superconducting-to-normal transition to first order.
