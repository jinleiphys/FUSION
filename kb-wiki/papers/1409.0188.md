# Effect of the spin-orbit interaction on flows in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> The effect of the spin-orbit coupling in heavy ion collisions is investigated based on an updated version of the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, in which the Skyrme potential energy density functional is employed. And in special, the spin-orbit coupling effects on the directed and elliptic flows of free nucleons emitted from $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions as functions of both the beam energy and the impact parameter are studied. Our results show that the net contribution of the spin-orbit term to flows of nucleons is negligible, whereas a directed flow splitting between spin-up and spin-down nucleons is visible especially at large impact parameters and a peak of the splitting is found at the beam energy around 150 MeV$/$nucleon. We also found that the directed flow splitting between spin-up and spin-down neutrons is comparable with the neutron directed flow difference calculated by a soft and a stiff symmetry energy, indicating that the directed flow of neutrons cannot be used to pin down the stiffness of symmetry energy any more without considering the spin degree of freedom in models in case of spin polarization.

# Effect of the spin-orbit interaction on flows in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

## Key claim

The net contribution of the spin-orbit term to the directed and elliptic flows of all free nucleons is negligible under spin-mixed conditions, but a directed flow splitting between spin-up and spin-down nucleons is visible, especially at large impact parameters, with a peak splitting at beam energy around 150 MeV/nucleon. The directed flow splitting between spin-up and spin-down neutrons is comparable to the neutron directed flow difference calculated with a soft versus a stiff symmetry energy, implying that neutron directed flow cannot be used to determine the stiffness of symmetry energy without accounting for the spin degree of freedom in models when spin polarization is present.

## Method

The spin-orbit potential was incorporated into the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model using the Skyrme potential energy density functional, including both time-even and time-odd spin-orbit terms. Simulations of 197Au+197Au collisions were performed at various beam energies and impact parameters, with more than 500,000 events per case, using the SkP interaction (incompressibility K0=201 MeV, spin-orbit strength W0=0.1 GeV fm^5). Directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2 were extracted from the Fourier expansion of azimuthal distributions of free nucleons, with nucleons classified as spin-up or spin-down based on the sign of their spin component in the y-direction (perpendicular to the reaction plane).

## Key numbers

- Directed flow splitting between spin-up and spin-down protons increases almost linearly with centrality b0^2 at Elab=150 MeV/nucleon; the ratio (κdown-κup)/κall reaches 0.1 for impact parameter b > 4.5 fm and 0.3 for b > 9.5 fm
- The slope difference κdown-κup peaks at beam energy around 150 MeV/nucleon for Au+Au collisions at b=7 fm, in agreement with IBUU simulations where the largest spin up-down differential transverse flow was observed at Elab=100 MeV/nucleon
- For neutron directed flow, the κ value of the soft symmetry energy with spin-down configuration ("S↓") is almost the same as that of the stiff symmetry energy with spin-up configuration ("H↑"), while the κ value of "H↓" is larger than that of "S↑" by more than 33%
- The proton flow is influenced by spin orientation by more than 15% but by the stiffness of symmetry potential energy by less than 7%
- The width parameter L of the nucleon wave packet was set to 2 fm^2 for Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-orbit coupling effects in heavy-ion collisions beyond the initial investigation by Xu et al. (2014) using the IBUU transport model, confirming the spin up-down differential transverse flow with a different transport model (UrQMD). The results build on prior work by Guo et al. (2012) which found that neutron directed flow is sensitive to the density dependence of symmetry energy, but the present study shows that spin polarization effects can obscure this sensitivity. The findings suggest that future measurements of directed flow of protons from heavy-ion collisions at beam energy around 150 MeV/nucleon with large impact parameters could serve as a probe of the in-medium spin-orbit interaction, and that spin degree of freedom must be considered when using neutron flow to constrain symmetry energy.
