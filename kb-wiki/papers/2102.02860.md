# Halo EFT calculation of charge form factor for two-neutron ${}^{6}\textrm{He}$ halo nucleus: two-body resonant P-wave interaction

> We take a new look at $^{6}\textrm{He}$ halo nucleus and set up a halo effective field theory at low energies to calculate the charge form factor of ${}^{6}\textrm{He}$ system with resonant P-wave interaction. P-wave Lagrangian has been introduced and the charge form factor of ${}^{6}\textrm{He}$ halo nucleus has been obtained at Leading-Order. In this study, the mean-square charge radius of ${}^{6}\textrm{He}$ nucleus relative to ${}^{4}\textrm{He}$ core and the root-mean-square (r.m.s) charge radius of ${}^{6}\textrm{He}$ nucleus have been estimated as $\langle r_{E}^{2}\rangle=1.408\; \textrm{fm}^{2}$ and $\langle r_{E}^{2}\rangle_{{}^{6}He}^{\frac{1}{2}}=2.058 \;\textrm{fm}$, respectively. We have compared our results with the other available theoretical and experimental data.

# Halo EFT calculation of charge form factor for two-neutron 6He halo nucleus: two-body resonant P-wave interaction

## Key claim
The authors calculate the charge form factor of 6He at leading order in halo effective field theory using a P-wave resonant interaction for the n-alpha subsystem, obtaining a mean-square charge radius relative to the 4He core of 1.408 fm^2 and a root-mean-square charge radius of 2.058 fm. These results depend on a specific power counting (Bertulani et al.) that includes both scattering length and effective range of the 2P3/2 channel at leading order, and require handling a spurious pole in the n-alpha propagator via contour rotation.

## Method
The authors construct a halo EFT Lagrangian with neutron, alpha core, and auxiliary dimer fields for the nn S-wave and n-alpha P-wave systems, using the power counting of Bertulani et al. (2012) where both scattering length and effective range contribute at leading order for the resonant 2P3/2 channel. They solve the Faddeev equations for the three-body nn-alpha system to obtain the bound state wave function and trimer-dimer-particle three-point function, then compute the charge form factor from diagrams with minimally coupled photons to the alpha core and the P-wave dimer, extracting the charge radius from the Q^2 expansion at Q^2=0.

## Key numbers
- Mean-square charge radius of 6He relative to 4He core: ⟨r_E^2⟩ = 1.408 fm^2
- Root-mean-square charge radius of 6He: ⟨r_E^2⟩_{6He}^{1/2} = 2.058 fm
- Two-neutron separation energy of 6He: B_{2n} = 0.97 MeV (from literature, cited as [h6])
- n-alpha P-wave scattering length: a_1 = -62.95 fm^3 (from literature, cited as [h2])
- n-alpha P-wave effective range: r_1 = -0.88 fm^{-1} (from literature, cited as [h2])
- n-alpha P-wave shape parameter: P_1 = -3.0 fm (from literature, cited as [h2])
- nn S-wave scattering length: a_0 = -23.714 fm (from literature)
- nn S-wave effective range: r_0 = 2.73 fm (from literature)
- Alpha particle excitation energy: E_alpha = 20.21 MeV (from literature)
- Neutron mean-square charge radius (neglected): ⟨r_E^2⟩_n = -0.115 fm^2 (from literature, cited as [h10])

## Context
This work extends halo EFT calculations for two-neutron halo nuclei, previously applied to S-wave systems like 11Li, 14Be, and 22C by Hagen et al. (2013) and Vanasse (2013), to the P-wave resonant n-alpha interaction in 6He. It builds on the power counting for n-alpha scattering developed by Bertulani et al. (2012) and the three-body nn-alpha Faddeev framework used by Ji et al. (2015) for the 6He ground state. The results enable comparison with experimental charge radius measurements (e.g., 2.060(8) fm from [h33] and 2.054±0.014 fm from [h31]) and with six-body calculations such as large scale shell model and distorted wave Born approximation, while providing a model-independent EFT framework that can be systematically improved with higher-order corrections.
