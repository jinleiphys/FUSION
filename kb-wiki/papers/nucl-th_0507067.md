# Anisotropic flow in Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> The anisotropic flow of charged hadrons in asymmetric Cu+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider is studied in a multi-phase transport model. Compared with previous results for symmetric Au+Au collisions, charged hadrons produced around midrapidity in asymmetric collisions are found to have a stronger directed flow $v_{1}$ and their elliptic flow $% v_{2} $ is also more sensitive to the parton scattering cross section. While higher-order flows $v_{3}$ and $v_{4}$ are small at all rapidities, both $% v_{1}$ and $v_{2}$ in these collisions are appreciable and show an asymmetry in forward and backward rapidities.

## Key claim

In asymmetric Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the AMPT model predicts that charged hadrons produced around midrapidity have a stronger directed flow \(v_{1}\) and a more sensitive elliptic flow \(v_{2}\) to the parton scattering cross section than in symmetric Au+Au collisions, with both \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{2}\) showing an asymmetry between forward and backward rapidities.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, a hybrid model that combines initial partonic and final hadronic interactions, to simulate minimum-bias Cu+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They employed both the default version (without string melting) and the version with string melting, where initial excited strings are converted to valence quarks and antiquarks that then interact via Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) with elastic scatterings; hadronization is modeled via a simple coalescence model, followed by hadronic rescattering using a relativistic transport (ART) model. Anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_1\), \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) were computed from the azimuthal distributions of charged hadrons as a function of pseudorapidity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The \(p_T\)-integrated elliptic flow \(v_2\) around mid-\(\eta\) in the string melting scenario with a parton scattering cross section of 10 mb is about 3% in Cu+Au collisions, compared to about 4.5% in Au+Au collisions at the same energy.
- The ratio of \(v_2\) with \(\sigma_p = 10\) mb to that with \(\sigma_p = 3\) mb is about 1.4 in Cu+Au collisions, reduced to about 1.2 in Au+Au collisions.
- The \(p_T\)-integrated higher-order flows \(v_3\) and \(v_4\) are less than 0.3% across the entire pseudorapidity range for all three scenarios considered.
- At large backward pseudorapidity (\(\eta = -4\)), the directed flow \(v_1\) changes from positive to negative values at a balance transverse momentum of about 1.2 GeV/\(c\); at forward pseudorapidity (\(\eta = 4\)), \(v_1\) changes from negative to positive at about 0.8 GeV/\(c\).
- The impact parameter used for time-evolution studies is \(b = 6.74\) fm, corresponding to the same \(b/b_{\mathrm{max}}\) as \(b = 8\) fm in Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior AMPT model studies of anisotropic flow in symmetric Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Chen et al., 2004; v13plb05, 2005) to asymmetric Cu+Au systems, motivated by the idea that varying the atomic number of colliding nuclei probes the influence of initial collision geometry on flow. The results show that the stronger directed flow and enhanced sensitivity of elliptic flow to parton cross sections in asymmetric collisions provide a new test for the dynamics of partonic matter, and the predicted forward-backward asymmetry in \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) could help improve experimental identification of the reaction plane, which has been difficult in symmetric collisions.
