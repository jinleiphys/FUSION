# Probing nuclear bubble configuration by proton induced reaction

> In the framework of the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, nuclear bubble configuration in the hypothetical $^{48}$Si nucleus is studied by proton induced central reaction at an incident beam energy of 0.8 GeV/nucleon. It is found that along the beam direction more energetic protons are emitted with bubble configuration in the target. In the forward angles, compared with the case without bubble configuration, less scattered energetic protons are emitted with bubble configuration in the target. We thus provide a new way to probe the bubble configuration in nuclei.

# Probing nuclear bubble configuration by proton induced reaction

## Key claim

The authors propose that the angular and energy distribution of emitted protons in central p+⁴⁸Si reactions at 0.8 GeV/nucleon can serve as a probe of the hypothetical bubble (central density depletion) configuration in the target nucleus, but the specific scattering angles where the effect is maximal depend on the size of the bubble and the target nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with isospin-dependent nuclear initialization, nucleon-nucleon cross sections, single nucleon potential, and Pauli blockings. They simulated central (impact parameter 0 fm) p+⁴⁸Si reactions at 0.8 GeV/nucleon, comparing two initial density distributions: one from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) with Skyrme M* force parameters (no bubble) and one from the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) with the PKA1 Lagrangian (dual semi-bubble configuration with central depletion for both neutrons and protons). For each case, one million events were accumulated.

## Key numbers

- The effect of the bubble configuration on energetic emitting protons (E_kin ≥ 600 MeV) in the beam direction reaches 30–40%.
- For scattered energetic protons in forward angles, the effects of the bubble configuration reach 16–70% with kinetic energy cuts from E_kin > 600 MeV to E_kin > 850 MeV.
- The effects of the bubble configuration on scattered energetic protons reach maximum around a scattering angle of θ = 22.5°.
- The incident beam energy used in the simulations is 0.8 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of bubble nuclei using transport simulations by Yong (no year given) and builds on the prediction by Long (2019) that ⁴⁸Si is a candidate dual semi-bubble nucleus using the relativistic Hartree-Fock Lagrangian PKA1. The first experimental evidence for central proton density depletion was reported in ³⁴Si by Nature (2016). The authors propose proton-induced reactions as an alternative to electron scattering experiments for probing bubble configurations, such as those planned at ELISe@FAIR or after an upgrade of the SCRIT facility at RIKEN.
