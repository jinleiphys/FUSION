# The strong-coupling limit of a Kondo spin coupled to a mesoscopic quantum dot: effective Hamiltonian in the presence of exchange correlations

> We consider a Kondo spin that is coupled antiferromagnetically to a large chaotic quantum dot. Such a dot is described by the so-called universal Hamiltonian and its electrons are interacting via a ferromagnetic exchange interaction. We derive an effective Hamiltonian in the limit of strong Kondo coupling, where the screened Kondo spin effectively removes one electron from the dot. We find that the exchange coupling constant in this reduced dot (with one less electron) is renormalized and that new interaction terms appear beyond the conventional terms of the strong-coupling limit. The eigenenergies of this effective Hamiltonian are found to be in excellent agreement with exact numerical results of the original model in the limit of strong Kondo coupling.

## Key claim

In the strong-coupling limit of a Kondo spin coupled antiferromagnetically to a large chaotic quantum dot described by the universal Hamiltonian, the effective Hamiltonian for the reduced dot (with one less electron) contains not only a renormalized exchange coupling constant but also new interaction terms beyond the conventional strong-coupling limit, including a Nozieres-like repulsive interaction and additional spin invariants involving the total spin of the reduced dot.

## Method

The authors start from a model of a Kondo spin coupled to a large chaotic quantum dot described by the universal Hamiltonian, which includes a ferromagnetic exchange interaction. They transform the single-particle orbital basis of the large dot into a chain site basis, where the Kondo spin couples only to site 0. Using a projection technique, they derive an effective Hamiltonian for the reduced dot (with N-1 sites and N-1 electrons) in the strong-coupling limit by expanding in the small parameters t0/Jk and Js/Jk up to fourth order, accounting for virtual transitions between the singlet, doublet, and triplet manifolds.

## Key numbers

* The average single-particle level spacing of the large dot is given by δ̅, with N spin-degenerate levels spanning a bandwidth of 2D = (N-1) × δ̅.
* The average local density of states at the tunneling position is ρ̅ ≈ 1/(N δ̅).
* The strong-coupling limit is defined by Jk ≫ t0, corresponding to Jk ρ̅ ≫ 1.
* The Nozieres term in the effective Hamiltonian is δH1,4 = -(16/3 Jk³) |t0|⁴ n̂1(2 - n̂1), which vanishes when site 1 is empty (n1=0) or doubly occupied (n1=2) but is negative for n1=1.
* The exchange coupling constant Js is typically below ∼ δ̅.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous numerical and analytical study (Rotter and Alhassid, 2008) of a Kondo spin coupled to a mesoscopic quantum dot with ferromagnetic exchange correlations, where they found that in the strong-coupling limit the Kondo spin effectively removes one electron from the dot and the reduced dot can be described by a universal Hamiltonian with a renormalized exchange constant. The present paper goes beyond that work by identifying new interaction terms in the effective Hamiltonian beyond the renormalized exchange, following a strategy similar to Nozieres' Fermi-liquid approach for the conventional Kondo problem (Nozieres, 1974). The results enable analytical calculations of the eigenenergies of the coupled system in the strong-coupling limit that agree with exact numerical diagonalization of the original model.
