# Alternate Solution of the $α$-Potential Mystery

> A consistent set of statistical-model input parameters, validated by analysis of various independent data, makes possible the assessment of an $α$-particle optical model potential [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 90}, 044612 (2014)] also for nucleon-induced $α$-emission within $A$$\sim$60 mass-number range. The advantage of recent data for low-lying states feeding is taken as well. Consideration of additional reaction channels leading to increase of the $α$-emission beyond the statistical predictions has concerned the pickup direct interaction and Giant Quadrupole Resonance similar features.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the same alpha-particle optical model potential previously validated for alpha-induced reactions (Avrigeanu 2014) can also describe nucleon-induced alpha-emission in the A~60 mass range, provided that additional reaction channels (pickup direct interaction and giant quadrupole resonance-like contributions) are included beyond the standard statistical model predictions.

## Method
The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations combined with pre-equilibrium emission models using a consistent parameter set validated against independent data. They calculated pickup direct interaction contributions within the distorted wave Born approximation using the code FRESCO, considering one-step reactions through the pickup of 3H and 3He clusters. For certain target nuclei, they added Gaussian-shaped contributions at the giant quadrupole resonance energy to account for excess yield beyond the combined statistical and direct interaction predictions.

## Key numbers
- Pre-formation probability for the Geometry-Dependent Hybrid model: phi = 0.04
- Giant quadrupole resonance energy used: E_GQR = 65*A^(-1/3) MeV, giving 17.092 MeV for 55Fe and 16.89 MeV for 57Fe excited nuclei
- Gaussian widths for like-GQR contributions: 2.35 MeV and 3.54 MeV for 54Fe and 56Fe targets respectively
- Peak cross sections for like-GQR contributions: 8 mb and 10 mb for 54Fe and 56Fe targets respectively
- 19 states up to excitation energy of ~5 MeV were involved for assessment of pickup excitation function in 64Ni(p,alpha)61Co
- 27 excited states up to 2.088 MeV excitation energy were considered in pickup cross-section calculation for 59Co(n,alpha)56Mn
- 36 excited states up to 5.943 MeV for residual nucleus 51Cr, and 18 states up to 5.557 MeV for 53Cr in (n,alpha) reactions on 54,56Fe

## Context
This work extends the validation of the alpha-particle optical model potential from Avrigeanu (2014) to nucleon-induced alpha-emission, addressing the "alpha-potential mystery" previously discussed by Rauscher (2013) concerning the simultaneous description of alpha absorption and emission. The authors build on earlier work by Avrigeanu (1994) which used a different potential for alpha-emission only, and incorporate pickup direct interaction following the spectator model approach of Smits and Siemssen (1976, 1979). The inclusion of giant quadrupole resonance-like decay follows suggestions by Avrigeanu (2006) and Fallot (2007). This work enables improved nuclear data evaluations for fusion technology and nuclear astrophysics applications in the A~60 mass region.
