# Analysis of quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering off $^{16}$O and neutrino energy reconstruction

> The charged-current quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrino on the oxygen target is analyzed for neutrino energy up to 2.5 GeV using the Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA). The inclusive cross sections $d^2σ/dQ^2$, calculated within the RDWIA, are lower than the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) results in the range of the square of four-momentum transfer $Q^2\leq$0.2 (GeV/c)$^2$. We have also studied the nuclear-model dependence of the neutrino energy reconstruction accuracy using the charged-current quasi-elastic events with no detector effects and background. We found that for one-track events the accuracy is nuclear-model dependent for neutrino energy up to 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) predicts lower charged-current quasi-elastic cross sections than the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model (RFGM) for four-momentum transfer squared \(Q^2 \leq 0.2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), and the accuracy of neutrino energy reconstruction from one-track events is nuclear-model dependent for neutrino energies up to 2.5 GeV, even when detector effects and background are excluded.

## Method

The author computed inclusive and exclusive charged-current quasi-elastic cross sections for muon neutrino scattering off \(^{16}\)O using the RDWIA, which incorporates nuclear shell structure and final-state interactions via relativistic optical potentials. The RDWIA results were compared with those from the RFGM and the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA). The nuclear-model dependence of neutrino energy reconstruction was studied by calculating the mean, variance, and bias of the reconstructed energy distribution for both kinematic and calorimetric methods, using the nucleon momentum and energy distributions from each model.

## Key numbers

- The inclusive cross sections \(d^2\sigma/dQ^2\) calculated within the RDWIA are lower than the RFGM results in the range \(Q^2 \leq 0.2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The axial mass used in the dipole parameterization is \(M_A \simeq 1.032\) GeV.
- The axial form factor at zero momentum transfer is \(F_A(0) = 1.267\).
- The Fermi constant is \(G \simeq 1.16639 \times 10^{-11}\) MeV\(^{-2}\) and \(\cos \theta_C \approx 0.9749\).
- For the oxygen target, the mean missing energy used is \(\langle\varepsilon_m\rangle = 27.1\) MeV and the maximum nucleon momentum used is \(|\mathbf{p}_{max}| = 500\) MeV/c.
- The occupancy normalization factors for \(^{16}\)O are \(S(1p_{3/2}) = 0.66\), \(S(1p_{1/2}) = 0.7\), and \(S(1s_{1/2}) = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the RDWIA, previously tested against electron scattering data (Fissum, 2005) and used for neutrino-nucleus cross sections (Butkevich, 2005; Meucci, 2003; Maieron, 2003; Martin, 2005), to the specific case of oxygen targets relevant to accelerator-based neutrino oscillation experiments (K2K, MiniBooNE, T2K, NOvA). The study directly addresses the data/MC disagreement observed by K2K (Gran, 2006) and MiniBooNE (Katori, 2007) at low \(Q^2\), where the RFGM fails, and quantifies the systematic uncertainty in neutrino energy reconstruction that arises from nuclear model assumptions, enabling more accurate extraction of oscillation parameters.
