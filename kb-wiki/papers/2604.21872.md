# Quark and gluon production in the presence of the time-varying chiral magnetic current

> The chiral magnetic effect consists in the induction of the electric current along the direction of the magnetic field. The corresponding transport coefficient $b_0$, known as the chiral magnetic conductivity, is proportional to the chiral imbalance in the medium. In many systems, such as quark-gluon plasma, $b_0$ is time-dependent. This paper studies the effect of the time variation of $b_0$ on the particle spectra and energy loss produced through the chiral Cherenkov and associated processes in Abelian and non-Abelian systems. The rates of all processes are derived in the ultra-relativistic approximation. The results are applied to the relativistic heavy-ion collisions utilizing a specific model describing the relaxation of the initial $P$-odd domain within the quark-gluon plasma. The corresponding energy loss is computed. The results suggest strong polarization of jets in quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that time variation of the chiral magnetic conductivity $b_0(t)$ significantly modifies particle spectra and energy loss from chiral Cherenkov and associated processes, but the results are derived under the ultra-relativistic approximation and the condition $|\dot b_0| \ll k^2$, meaning the time variation must be slow compared to the squared momentum of the emitted gauge boson.

## Method

The author derives photon and gluon wave functions in the presence of a time-dependent chiral magnetic current by adding a Chern-Simons term to the Maxwell and Yang-Mills Lagrangians, solving the resulting wave equation in the ultra-relativistic limit. The scattering matrix elements are computed for all parton splitting processes ($q\to q+g$, $g\to q+\bar q$, $g\to g+g$) using the time-dependent amplitude $\tilde a(q)$ obtained from the Fourier transform of the wave function. These general expressions are then applied to a specific model where $b_0(t)=A+B\tanh(t/\tau)$, describing the decay of an initial $P$-odd domain in quark-gluon plasma, with the integrals evaluated analytically using Gamma functions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral magnetic conductivity model parameters: $A=10$ MeV, $B=-5$ MeV, and $\tau=25$ GeV$^{-1}\approx 5$ fm/c
- Incident quark and gluon energy: $E=20$ GeV
- Quark mass: $m=0.3$ GeV
- Plasma frequency used in dashed curves: $\omega_p=0.3$ GeV
- The condition for validity of the ultra-relativistic approximation: $|\dot b_0| \ll k^2$
- The plasma frequency in quark-gluon plasma at temperature $T$: $\omega_p^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{18}(2N_c+N_f)$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral Cherenkov radiation by Tuchin (2018), Huang (2018), and Hansen (2020-2024) that assumed constant $b_0$, and builds upon the author's recent work on photon production with time-dependent chiral imbalance (Tuchin 2025). The results enable calculation of energy loss and jet polarization in heavy-ion collisions where the chiral magnetic conductivity decays over time due to the relaxation of $P$-odd domains created in the initial stage, as sourced by parallel chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields (Lappi 2017) or sphaleron transitions (Fukushima 2008, Arnold 1996, Bodeker 1998).
