# Lattice determination of the critical point of QCD at finite T and μ

> Based on universal arguments it is believed that there is a critical point (E) in QCD on the temperature (T) versus chemical potential (μ) plane, which is of extreme importance for heavy-ion experiments. Using finite size scaling and a recently proposed lattice method to study QCD at finite μwe determine the location of E in QCD with n_f=2+1 dynamical staggered quarks with semi-realistic masses on $L_t=4$ lattices. Our result is T_E=160 \pm 3.5 MeV and μ_E= 725 \pm 35 MeV. For the critical temperature at μ=0 we obtained T_c=172 \pm 3 MeV.

## Key claim

Using finite size scaling of Lee-Yang zeros on the complex gauge coupling plane, the authors determine the location of the QCD critical endpoint on $L_t=4$ lattices with $n_f=2+1$ dynamical staggered quarks at semi-realistic masses, finding $T_E=160 \pm 3.5$ MeV and $\mu_E=725 \pm 35$ MeV, but note that the light quark masses are approximately four times heavier than in the real world and that larger volumes, larger $L_t$, and smaller masses are needed for the final answer.

## Method

The authors generate an ensemble of QCD configurations at $\mu=0$ and at the transition temperature, then reweight these configurations to finite $\mu$ and lower temperatures using the Ferrenberg-Swendsen method, with the quark determinant evaluated as a function of $\mu$ via a characteristic polynomial of a matrix $P$ obtained after gauge fixing to $A_0=0$. They determine the Lee-Yang zeros of the partition function on the complex $\beta$ plane for spatial volumes $L_s=4,6,8$ on $L_t=4$ lattices, extrapolate the imaginary part of the zeros to infinite volume using a $\beta_0(V)=\beta_0^\infty+\zeta/V$ fit, and identify the endpoint as the chemical potential where the extrapolated imaginary part vanishes, indicating a transition from crossover to first-order behavior.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint temperature: $T_E = 160 \pm 3.5$ MeV
- Critical endpoint quark chemical potential: $\mu_E = 725 \pm 35$ MeV (baryonic chemical potential $\mu_B = 3\mu_E$)
- Critical temperature at $\mu=0$: $T_c = 172 \pm 3$ MeV
- Bare quark masses used: $m_{u,d}=0.025$, $m_s=0.2$ (lattice units)
- Endpoint in lattice units: $\mu_{end}=0.375(20)$
- Pion mass in lattice units at $\beta=5.208$: $m_\pi=0.393(2)$
- Rho mass in lattice units at $\beta=5.208$: $m_\rho=1.22(2)$
- Sommer scale at $\beta=5.208$: $R_0=1.87(3)$ (lattice units)
- String tension at $\beta=5.208$: $\sqrt{\sigma}=0.58(7)$ (lattice units)
- Product $R_0 \cdot m_\pi = 0.73(6)$, indicating light quark masses at least four times larger than physical

## Context

This work provides the first ab initio lattice QCD determination of the critical endpoint location, extending earlier model-based estimates such as those in CritPoint (1998) which suggested $\mu_E \approx 700$ MeV for $m_s=\infty$. The method builds on the reweighting technique introduced by Fodor and Katz (2001) for $n_f=4$ QCD and applies it to the more realistic $n_f=2+1$ case. The result enables experimental searches at heavy-ion colliders, though the authors note that the endpoint would likely move closer to the $\mu=0$ axis with physical quark masses, making it potentially accessible at RHIC or LHC energies.
