# Proton Compton scattering in a unified proton-Delta Model

> We develop a field-theoretic model for the description of proton Compton scattering in which the proton and its excited state, the Δ+ resonance, are described as part of one multiplet with a single Rarita-Schwinger wavefunction. In order to describe the phenomena observed, it is necessary to incorporate both minimal and non-minimal couplings. The minimal coupling reflects the fact that the Δ+ is a charged particle, and in this model the minimal coupling contributes also to the M1 magnetic transition via the γNΔvertex. The non-minimal couplings consist of five electromagnetic form-factors, which are accessed at fixed and vanishing momentum-transfer squared with real photons in Compton scattering experiments, therefore it is possible to extract a rather well-determined set of optimal parameters which reasonably well fit the data in the resonance region 140-450 MeV. The crucial parameter which determines the γNΔtransition amplitude and therefore the height of the resonance peak is equal to 3.66 +- 0.03, in units of μ_N. We find that this parameter also primarily determines the contributions to magnetic polarizability in this model. In the low-energy region up to 140 MeV, we separately fit the electric and magnetic polarizabilities, while keeping the other parameters fixed and obtain values in line with previous approaches. The basic model is then extended by incorporating the sigma-meson channel with the currently favored parameters, and the pion vertex corrections.

## Key claim

The paper develops a field-theoretic model where the proton and Δ⁺ resonance are unified within a single Rarita-Schwinger multiplet, allowing simultaneous calculation of s- and u-channel contributions; the model fits Compton scattering data in the resonance region 140–450 MeV with a well-determined set of parameters, but the low-energy region up to 140 MeV requires separate fitting of electric and magnetic polarizabilities while keeping other parameters fixed.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian using a modified Rarita-Schwinger formalism (Ranada and Sierra, 1980; Kostas, 2010) where the spin-3/2 component is identified as the Δ⁺ and the spin-1/2 component as the proton, with minimal electromagnetic coupling derived from the substitution pμ → pμ + eAμ. Five non-minimal electromagnetic form factors (F1 through F5) are introduced at the γNΔ vertex, plus a sixth higher-order tensor (F6), and the model is extended by including a sigma-meson channel and pion vertex corrections. The Compton scattering cross section is calculated at tree level from s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel pion exchange diagrams, and the parameters are fitted to experimental data in the resonance region.

## Key numbers

- The crucial γNΔ transition parameter G = 3.66 ± 0.03, in units of μN (nuclear magneton), which determines the height of the resonance peak and primarily contributes to magnetic polarizability.
- The model fits data in the resonance region 140–450 MeV.
- The low-energy region is defined as up to 140 MeV.
- The mass splitting between proton and Δ⁺ is less than 300 MeV.
- The Δ⁺ mass is 1232 MeV with J^P = 3/2⁺.
- In the minimally coupled theory, the Δ⁺ g-factor is 2/3, giving μΔ⁺ = e/(2m).
- The proton magnetic moment μp ≈ 2.79 μN is used as a constraint on the form factors.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic approach of Pascalutsa and Scholten (1995) and subsequent developments by Scholten (1996), Lensky (2010), McGovern (2012), and others, which incorporated the Δ⁺ resonance into Compton scattering models. The unified proton-Δ⁺ description using the Ranada and Sierra (1980) and Kostas (2010) propagator avoids introducing a large number of arbitrary parameters and enables simultaneous calculation of proton and Δ⁺ exchange diagrams. The model enables extraction of static electric and magnetic polarizabilities from Compton scattering data and provides predictions for the Δ⁺ magnetic moment, while the basic model can be extended with sigma-meson channel and pion vertex corrections.
