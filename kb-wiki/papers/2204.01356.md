# Dominant Andreev Reflection through Nonlinear Radio-Frequency Transport

> We theoretically propose the laser-induced Andreev reflection between two-component Fermi superfluid and normal states via spatially-uniform Rabi couplings. By analyzing the tunneling current between the superfluid and normal states up to the fourth order in the Rabi couplings, we find that the Andreev current exhibits unconventional non-Ohmic transport at zero temperature. Remarkably, the Andreev current gives the only contribution in the synthetic junction system at zero detunings regardless of the ratio of the chemical potential bias to the superfluid gap, which is in sharp contrast to that in the conventional superconductor-normal metal junction. Our result may also pave a way for understanding the black hole information paradox through the Andreev reflection as a quantum-information mirror.

# Dominant Andreev Reflection through Nonlinear Radio-Frequency Transport

## Key claim
The authors propose that laser-induced Andreev reflection between a two-component Fermi superfluid and a normal state, mediated by spatially-uniform Rabi couplings, becomes the only transport process at zero detunings regardless of the ratio of chemical potential bias to superfluid gap, but only up to fourth order in the Rabi couplings and under the assumption of stable quasiparticles without spectral broadening.

## Method
The authors model a synthetic normal-superfluid interface using two radio-frequency laser fields that couple two hyperfine states in a BCS superfluid to a third hyperfine state in the normal phase, preserving momentum conservation. They apply the Schwinger-Keldysh Green's function formalism to compute the tunneling current perturbatively up to fourth order in the Rabi couplings, evaluating the contributions from single-particle tunneling, nonlinear quasiparticle tunneling, pair tunneling, and Andreev reflection using mean-field Green's functions for the superfluid reservoir.

## Key numbers
- Andreev current formula: I_A = Θ(μ_S) Ω²_↑,3 Ω²_↓,3 (m √(2m μ_S))/(π Δ_S²) × (e^(Δμ/T) - 1)/(e^(Δμ/T) + 1)
- At zero temperature with finite chemical potential bias, I_A ∝ sign(Δμ) and does not depend on the magnitude of Δμ
- For the unitary limit, the authors set μ_S/E_F = 0.38 and Δ_S/E_F = 0.47, with temperature T/T_F = 0.06
- The Andreev current becomes zero at around 1/(a k_F) = 0.5 where μ_S = 0
- The timescale of tunneling processes is estimated as τ ~ 10^(-3) s for Ω/E_F ~ 0.1

## Context
This work extends the concept of Andreev reflection, originally introduced by Andreev (1964) to explain anomalous heat flow at normal-superconductor interfaces, into a synthetic dimension using ultracold Fermi gases. It contrasts with conventional superconductor-normal metal junctions (PhysRevB.66.165413, PhysRevB.25.4515, PhysRevB.54.7366, PhysRevResearch.2.023340) where Andreev current obeys Ohm's law at low bias and is suppressed in the supergap regime. The proposed momentum-conserved Andreev reflection enables experimental simulation of the information-mirror process analogous to black hole evaporation as described by the Hayden-Preskill model (Hayden 2007, Lloyd 2014), and may allow deterministic teleportation of quantum information.
