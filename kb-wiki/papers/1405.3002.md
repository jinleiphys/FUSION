# Many-body Propagator Theory with Three-Body Interactions: a Path to Exotic Open Shell Isotopes

> Ab-initio predictions of nuclei with masses up to A~100 or more is becoming possible thanks to novel advances in computations and in the formalism of many-body physics. Some of the most fundamental issues include how to deal with many-nucleon interactions, how to calculate degenerate--open shell--systems, and pursuing ab-initio approaches to reaction theory. Self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory is a natural approach to address these challenges. Its formalism has recently been extended to three- and many-body interactions and reformulated within the Gorkov framework to reach semi-magic open shell isotopes. These exciting developments, together with the predictive power of chiral nuclear Hamiltonians, are opening the path to understanding large portions of the nuclear chart, especially within the $sd$ and $pf$ shells. The present talk reviews the most recent advances in ab-initio nuclear structure and many-body theory that have been possible through the SCGF approach.

## Key claim

Self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) theory has been extended to include three-body interactions and reformulated within the Gorkov framework, enabling ab-initio calculations of semi-magic open shell isotopes, but the full implementation of the Gorkov formalism to FRPA and ADC(3) type self-energies is still underway.

## Method

The Dyson equation for the single-particle propagator is solved using self-consistent Green's function theory, with the irreducible self-energy constructed from the Hamiltonian including one-, two-, and three-body interactions. For closed-shell nuclei, the self-energy is calculated using either the third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] or the Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) methods, which include all diagrams up to third order and resum many others. For open shell systems, the formalism is extended using Gorkov's method, which breaks particle number symmetry to describe pairing correlations, introducing anomalous propagators and working with the grand canonical Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies of oxygen isotopes are calculated using SRG evolved NN+3N interactions with cutoff λ_SRG = 2 fm⁻¹, based on the full ADC(3) self-energy
- For finite nuclei and SRG cutoffs of λ_SRG = 2.0 fm⁻¹, the expectation value ⟨Ψ^A_0|Ŵ|Ψ^A_0⟩ can be evaluated at first order in dressed propagators to converge the calculated binding energy
- The three-nucleon force contribution is typically about one order of magnitude less than the NN force contribution: ⟨Ŵ⟩ ≈ (1/10)⟨V̂⟩ for nuclear interactions
- FRPA calculations for proton scattering on ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca show the ℓ-averaged volume integral J_w^avg is reasonably close to the DOM fit up to E-E_F < 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SCGF approach to include three-nucleon forces (3NFs), which have been shown to play a major role in determining features of exotic isotopes such as the evolution of magic numbers and drip line positions (Otsuka 2010, Holt 2011, Hagen 2012, Hergert 2013, Cipollone 2013). The Gorkov formulation builds on earlier work by the collaboration (Soma 2011, 2013, 2014) to reach open shell nuclei, complementing similar developments in the IMSRG framework (Hergert 2013). The method enables ab-initio calculations of optical potentials through the equivalence between the many-body self-energy and Feshbach theory of elastic scattering, providing microscopic input for dispersive optical models (DOM) (Charity 2006, Mahzoon 2014).
