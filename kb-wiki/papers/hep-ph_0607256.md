# Stau-catalyzed Nuclear Fusion

> We point out that the stau may play a role of a catalyst for nuclear fusions if the stau is a long-lived particle as in the scenario of gravitino dark matter. In this letter, we consider d d fusion under the influence of stau where the fusion is enhanced because of a short distance between the two deuterons. We find that one chain of the d d fusion may release an energy of O(10) GeV per stau. We discuss problems of making the stau-catalyzed nuclear fusion of practical use with the present technology of producing stau.

# Stau-catalyzed Nuclear Fusion

## Key claim

The authors propose that a long-lived, negatively charged stau (the scalar partner of the tau lepton) can act as a catalyst for deuterium-deuterium (dd) nuclear fusion, releasing approximately O(10) GeV of energy per stau per fusion chain; however, they conclude that with current or near-future technology for stau production, the energy input required to create the stau far exceeds the energy output, making practical energy generation unfeasible unless the stau lifetime exceeds 300 years and a much more efficient production mechanism is developed.

## Method

The authors analyze the formation of a neutral $\stau d$ atomic system (where the stau captures a deuteron) and the subsequent interaction with a free deuteron to form a $\stau d d$ three-body system, using non-relativistic Coulomb bound-state theory. They estimate the fusion reaction rates for the three major dd fusion channels (to $^3$He+n, t+p, and $^4$He+$\gamma$) using the Gamow tunneling factor and spectroscopic S-factors from low-energy nuclear data, and compute the sticking probability of fusion products to the stau via the sudden approximation using overlap integrals of wave functions. The overall energy yield per stau is then estimated from the fusion Q-values divided by the sticking probability, and the production efficiency is evaluated for muon-nucleon scattering and neutrino-beam scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radius of the $\stau d$ atom: 15 fm; binding energy: 50 keV
- Typical size of the $\stau d d$ three-body system: approximately 45 fm; extra binding energy relative to $\stau d$: 2.8 keV
- Fusion reaction rates: $\lambda_{f\text{(i,ii)}} \sim 10^{14}$ sec$^{-1}$ for the $^3$He+n and t+p channels; $\lambda_{f\text{(iii)}} \sim 10^7$ sec$^{-1}$ for the $^4$He+$\gamma$ channel
- Sticking probabilities: $P_s = 2\times 10^{-7}$ for $\stau p$, $2\times 10^{-5}$ for $\stau t$, $4\times 10^{-4}$ for $\stau ^3$He, and $10^{-2}$ for $\stau ^4$He
- Energy release per fusion chain: approximately 20 GeV/$\kappa$, where $\kappa$ is an unknown suppression factor from flux reduction
- Time scale for one $\stau d + d$ interaction cycle: approximately 2 $\mu$s
- Stau lifetime required for net energy gain: longer than 300 years
- Stau production via muon-nucleon scattering: approximately $10^{-7}$ staus per 1000 TeV muon on a 200 m Fe target
- Stau production via 1000 TeV neutrino beam: approximately $3\times 10^{-4}$ staus per neutrino

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon-catalyzed fusion (MCF), where a muon forms molecular states such as $\mu dt$ and enables about $10^2$ cycles (a few GeV energy release) per muon, to the case of a much heavier negatively charged particle (the stau). The authors compare their results to earlier theoretical work on heavy-particle catalysis by Zweig and Okun, and note that their sticking probabilities are about $10^{-2}$ times smaller than those given by Zweig for the $^3$He case. The paper identifies the need for exact three-body calculations of the $\stau d - d$ system with nuclear fusion processes (citing Sawicki for an alternative approach) and suggests that staus could also serve as probes of the deep interior of heavy nuclei through exotic Coulomb bound states.
