# Parameterization dependence of T matrix poles and eigenphases from a fit to piN elastic scattering data

> We compare fits to piN elastic scattering data, based on a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix formalism. Resonances, characterized by T-matrix poles, are compared in fits generated with and without explicit Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix poles. Diagonalization of the S matrix yields the eigenphase representation. While the eigenphases can vary significantly for the different parameterizations, the locations of most T-matrix poles are relatively stable.

## Key claim

The locations of most T-matrix poles are relatively stable when fitting piN elastic scattering data with either the standard Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix parameterization (WI08) or an alternative form with explicit K-matrix poles in each partial wave (XP08), even though the eigenphases can vary significantly between the two parameterizations.

## Method

The authors performed chi-squared fits to piN elastic scattering data using a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix formalism, comparing two parameterizations: the standard GW-SAID form (WI08) with polynomial K-matrix elements and an explicit pole only in the P33 partial wave, and an alternative form (XP08) with one explicit Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix pole in each partial wave. They diagonalized the S-matrix to obtain eigenphases and extracted T-matrix pole positions in the complex energy plane for selected partial waves.

## Key numbers

- S11 partial wave: WI08 poles at (1499,49), (1647,42), and (1666,260) MeV; XP08 poles at (1538,65), (1675,58), and (1690,121) MeV
- S31 partial wave: WI08 pole at (1594,68) MeV; XP08 pole at (1592,66) MeV
- P11 partial wave: both fits share poles at (1358,80) and (1387,80) MeV (second sheet); XP08 adds a broad pole at (1646,290) MeV
- D13 partial wave: WI08 pole at (1515,55) MeV; XP08 poles at (1513,53), (1740,66), and (1716,370) MeV
- F15 partial wave: WI08 poles at (1674,57) and (1779,138) MeV; XP08 poles at (1672,70) and (1734,61) MeV
- F37 partial wave: WI08 pole at (1883,115) MeV; XP08 pole at (1874,119) MeV
- S11 eigenphase crossings at 1535 and 1670 MeV (WI08) versus 1530 and 1700 MeV (XP08)
- S31 K-matrix pole at 1655 MeV in both fits

## Context

This work extends the SAID-GW piN scattering analyses (Arndt 2006) and the SM95 study that searched for weaker resonance structures using a product S-matrix approach. The authors compare with the SM90 fit (Arndt 1990) which found a broad P11 state at (1636,272) MeV, and with the SM95 fit that reported marginal candidates in S11 at (1689,96) MeV and F15 at (1793,94) MeV. The study demonstrates that while partial-wave amplitudes are stable under parameterization changes, the eigenphase representation can reveal different resonance structures that do not significantly alter the piN elastic amplitude shape, enabling future multi-channel analyses with soft constraints from KH80 and CMB analyses.
