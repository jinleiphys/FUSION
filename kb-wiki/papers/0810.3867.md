# Radiative capture of nucleons at astrophysical energies with single-particle states

> Radiative capture of nucleons at energies of astrophysical interest is one of the most important processes for nucleosynthesis. The nucleon capture can occur either by a compound nucleus reaction or by a direct process. The compound reaction cross sections are usually very small, specially for light nuclei. The direct capture proceeds either via the formation of a single-particle resonance, or a non-resonant capture process. In this work we calculate radiative capture cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for nuclei in the mass region A<20 using single-particle states. We carefully discuss the parameter fitting procedure adopted in the simplified two-body treatment of the capture process. Then we produce a detailed list of cases for which the model works well. Useful quantities, such as spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients, are obtained and compared to published data.

## Key claim
The single-particle potential model, using Woods-Saxon potentials with a careful parameter fitting procedure, can reproduce radiative capture cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for many light nuclei (A<20), but the model works well only for a specific list of cases and requires separate adjustment of the continuum potential depth to fit experimental data.

## Method
The authors solved the radial Schrodinger equation for a nucleon-nucleus system using Woods-Saxon central and spin-orbit potentials with a uniform-sphere Coulomb potential. They varied only the depth of the central potential to reproduce bound-state energies, then calculated direct capture cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for electric and magnetic multipole transitions, adding spectroscopic factors as scaling parameters to match experimental data. For resonances not reproducible with the single-particle model, they used a Breit-Wigner shape parametrization.

## Key numbers
- For d(p,gamma)3He: spectroscopic factor SF=0.7 required to fit experimental data; ANC value sqrt(SF b^2)=1.56 fm^{-1/2}
- For 6Li(p,gamma)7Be: spectroscopic factor 0.83 for ground state and 0.84 for first excited state; ANC values 2.01 fm^{-1/2} (ground state) and 1.91 fm^{-1/2} (first excited state)
- For 7Li(p,gamma)8Be: spectroscopic factor 0.15 for the resonance at 0.386 MeV (c.m.) and 0.05 for the resonance at 0.901 keV (c.m.); non-resonant component obtained with SF=1.0
- For 7Be(p,gamma)8B: M1 resonance at E_cm=0.63 MeV
- For 7Li(p,gamma)8Be: first M1 resonance at 0.386 MeV (c.m.) with V_c=-46.35 MeV; second resonance at 0.901 keV (c.m.) with V_c=-44.55 MeV; non-resonant component with V_c=-56.69 MeV

## Context
This work extends the single-particle potential model approach, previously applied to isolated cases, to a systematic study of radiative capture reactions for many light nuclei with A<20. The authors compare their results to experimental data from multiple sources including GLR62, BKS64, WBL67, SCL95 for d(p,gamma)3He; Bruss93, Switkowski79, Arai02 for 6Li(p,gamma)7Be; and Zahnow95 for 7Li(p,gamma)8Be. The study enables extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients for use in other reaction channels, and provides a reference set of potential parameters for future calculations of astrophysical capture reactions.
