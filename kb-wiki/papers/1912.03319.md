# Connection between Elliott SU(3) and spherical shell model bases

> In the Elliott SU(3) symmetry scheme the single particle basis is derived from the isotropic harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian in the Cartesian coordinate system. These states are transformed into the solutions of the same Hamiltonian within the spherical coordinate system. Then the spin-orbit coupling can be added in a straightforward way. The outcome is a transformation between the Elliott single particle basis and the spherical shell model space.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit algebraic transformations between the Elliott SU(3) single-particle basis (Cartesian harmonic oscillator states) and the spherical shell model basis (coupled angular-momentum states) for the \(p\) and \(sd\) harmonic oscillator shells, enabling the spin-orbit interaction to be added straightforwardly after the transformation.

## Method

The authors start from the 3D isotropic harmonic oscillator solutions in Cartesian coordinates \(\ket{n_z, n_x, n_y}\) (the Elliott basis) and in spherical coordinates \(\{k, l, l_0\}\). They write the known unitary transformations between these two sets of states for the \(\mathcal{N}=1\) and \(\mathcal{N}=2\) oscillator shells, then couple spin via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to obtain the standard shell-model orbitals \(\{k, l, j, j_0\}\). Combining the two steps yields explicit linear expansions of each Elliott state (with spin projection) as a sum of spherical shell-model orbitals, and vice versa.

## Key numbers

* For the \(p\) shell (\(\mathcal{N}=1\)): the Elliott state \(\ket{1,0,0}\ket{+}\) expands as \(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} 1p^{1/2}_{1/2} + \sqrt{\frac{2}{3}} 1p^{3/2}_{1/2}\).
* For the \(sd\) shell (\(\mathcal{N}=2\)): the Elliott state \(\ket{2,0,0}\ket{+}\) expands as \(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} 2s^{1/2}_{1/2} - \frac{2}{\sqrt{15}} 1d^{3/2}_{1/2} + \sqrt{\frac{2}{5}} 1d^{5/2}_{1/2}\).
* For the nucleus \({}^{20}_{10}\mathrm{Ne}_{10}\), using the transformation for two valence protons and two valence neutrons in the \(1d^{5/2}_{\pm 1/2}\) orbitals, the average Elliott quantum numbers are \(\langle n_z\rangle = 12/5\), \(\langle n_x\rangle = 4/5\), \(\langle n_y\rangle = 4/5\), yielding \(\langle \lambda \rangle = 16/5\) and \(\langle \mu \rangle = 0\) for the whole nucleus.
* From these average quantum numbers, the collective deformation parameters are \(\beta = 0.211\) and \(\gamma = 10.4^\circ\), compared to a relativistic mean field prediction of \(\beta = 0.186\) from Lalazissis (no year given in text).

## Context

This work provides the explicit unitary mapping between the Elliott SU(3) basis (Elliott 1958) and the spherical shell-model basis, which had been missing in explicit algebraic form for the \(p\) and \(sd\) shells. It extends the proxy-SU(3) approximation (introduced by Bonatsos et al., 2017) by showing how the replacement of intruder orbitals in the 6-14 shell maps onto the \(p\)-shell Elliott states. The transformations enable parameter-independent estimates of collective deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) from shell-model occupation numbers, as demonstrated for \({}^{20}\mathrm{Ne}\), and open the way to similar calculations for higher shells (\(\mathcal{N} \ge 3\)) as work in progress.
