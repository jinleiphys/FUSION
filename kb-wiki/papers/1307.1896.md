# Decuplet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We present an analysis of the lowest-lying decuplet baryon masses in the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell scheme up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. In order to determine the $14$ low-energy constants, we perform a simultaneous fit of the $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, QCDSF-UKQCD, and HSC Collaborations, taking finite-volume corrections into account self-consistently. We show that up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order one can achieve a good description of the lattice QCD and experimental data. Surprisingly, we note that the current lattice decuplet baryon masses can be fitted rather well by the next-to-leading order baryon chiral perturbation theory, which, however, misses the experimental data a little bit. Furthermore, we predict the pion- and strangeness-sigma terms of the decuplet baryons by use of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) with the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme can describe the light-quark mass dependence of decuplet baryon masses from lattice QCD up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO), but notes that next-to-leading order (NLO) BChPT already fits the lattice data rather well while missing the experimental data a little bit.

## Method

The authors calculated decuplet baryon masses in covariant BChPT using the EOMS renormalization scheme up to N$^3$LO, including finite-volume corrections self-consistently. They performed a simultaneous fit of 14 low-energy constants (LECs) to $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, QCDSF-UKQCD, and HSC Collaborations, together with experimental data, using the inverse of the correlation matrix for each lattice ensemble to calculate the $\chi^2$. The pion- and strangeness-sigma terms were then predicted via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key numbers

* The chiral limit decuplet baryon mass $m_D$ is determined from the fit; the octet-decuplet mass splitting $\delta = 0.231$ GeV is taken as the average gap of the physical octet and decuplet masses.
* The $\phi BD$ coupling is fixed to $\mathcal{C} = 0.85$, the SU(3)-average value among decuplet-to-octet pionic decay channels.
* The $\phi DD$ coupling is fixed to $\mathcal{H} = 1.13$, derived from the large $N_c$ relation $H_A = (9/5)g_A$ with $g_A = 1.26$.
* The meson-decay constant is set to $F_{\phi} = 0.0871$ GeV.
* Only lattice QCD data satisfying $M_\pi < 0.5$ GeV and $M_{\phi}L > 3.8$ were selected, yielding eight data sets: three from PACS-CS, two from QCDSF-UKQCD, and three from HSC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of octet baryon masses in EOMS BChPT (Ren et al., 2012, 2013; Geng et al., 2011; Martin Camalich et al., 2010) to the decuplet sector, where only a few $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD studies existed (Walker-Loud, 2008; Ishikawa, 2009; Semke and Lutz, 2011, 2012). It contrasts with heavy-baryon ChPT studies (Walker-Loud, 2008; Ishikawa, 2009) that could not describe the LHPC and PACS-CS decuplet masses at NNLO. The results enable chiral extrapolations of lattice decuplet baryon masses and predictions of sigma terms for future experimental or lattice comparisons.
