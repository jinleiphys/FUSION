# Transition form factors of the N*(1535) as a dynamically generated resonance

> We discuss how electromagnetic properties provide useful tests of the nature of resonances, and we study these properties for the N*(1535) which appears dynamically generated from the strong interaction of mesons and baryons. Within this coupled channel chiral unitary approach, we evaluate the A_1/2 and S_1/2 helicity amplitudes as a function of Q^2 for the electromagnetic N*(1535) to gamma* N transition. Within the same formalism we evaluate the cross section for the reactions gamma N to eta N. We find a fair agreement for the absolute values of the transition amplitudes, as well as for the Q^2 dependence of the amplitudes, within theoretical and experimental uncertainties discussed in the paper. The ratios obtained between the S_1/2 and A_1/2 for the neutron or proton states of the N*(1535) are in qualitative agreement with experiment and there is agreement on the signs. The same occurs for the ratio of cross sections for the eta photoproduction on neutron and proton targets in the vicinity of the N*(1535) energy. The global results support the idea of this resonance as being dynamically generated, hence, largely built up from meson baryon components. However, the details of the model indicate that an admixture with a genuine quark state is also demanded that could help obtain a better agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the electromagnetic transition form factors of the N*(1535) resonance, calculated from a purely meson-baryon dynamically generated picture, are in fair agreement with experimental data, but the details of the model indicate that an admixture with a genuine quark state is also demanded to obtain better agreement, particularly at larger Q^2.

## Method

The authors evaluate the A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} helicity amplitudes as functions of Q^2 for the electromagnetic N*(1535) to gamma* N transition using a coupled-channel chiral unitary approach, where the N*(1535) is dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions. They calculate the transition amplitudes at one-loop level from Feynman diagrams where the photon couples to the meson and baryon components of the resonance, using coupling constants extracted from the pole residues of the scattering amplitude. The same formalism is used to evaluate cross sections for the reactions gamma N to eta N.

## Key numbers

- Pole positions of the N*(1535) resonance: sqrt(s) = 1537 - 37i MeV for the neutral charge state (n*) and sqrt(s) = 1532 - 37i MeV for the +1 charge state (p*)
- Coupling constants for the n* to meson-baryon channels include: g(eta n) = -1.45 + 0.435i, g(K+ Sigma-) = 2.20 - 0.171i, g(K0 Lambda) = 1.39 - 0.0825i
- Coupling constants for the p* to meson-baryon channels include: g(eta p) = -1.47 + 0.432i, g(K0 Sigma+) = 2.21 - 0.183i, g(K+ Lambda) = 1.37 - 0.100i
- The meson decay constant used is f = 93 MeV for all channels
- Axial vector coupling parameters: D = 0.85 +/- 0.06, F = 0.52 +/- 0.04

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for real photon decays of dynamically generated resonances (Misha and Sourav for Lambda(1520), Misha and Solo for Delta(1700), Misha and Geng for the two Lambda(1405) states) to virtual photons, providing Q^2-dependent helicity amplitudes. The results support the idea that the N*(1535) is largely built from meson-baryon components, as previously suggested by Kaiser, Inoue, and others, but the need for different subtraction constants in different channels (unlike the case of the Lambda(1405) resonance studied by Jose and Ulf) indicates a mixture with a genuine quark component, consistent with the CDD pole analysis of Hyodo (2008).
