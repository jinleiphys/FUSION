# Charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering off nuclei with nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations

> In recent years, many studies on neutrino-nucleus scattering have been carried out to investigate nuclear structures and the interactions between neutrinos and nucleons. This paper develops a charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino-nucleus scattering model to explore the nuclear mean-field dynamics and short-range correlation effects. In this model, the nuclear structure effect is depicted using the scaling function, while the neutrinonucleon interaction is represented by the elementary weak cross section. Results indicate that the double-differential cross section of scattered muon is influenced by the energy and momentum of nucleon in nuclei, and the total cross section depends primarily on the incident neutrino energy. Furthermore, incorporating short-range correlations yields the flux-integrated differential cross sections at high-T region producing larger values, a longer tail, and achieving better experimental consistency. It eventually elucidates the physical relationship between the neutrinonucleus scattering cross section and the variation in incident neutrino energy. The studies in this paper furnishes insights for the research of nucleon dynamics and provides detailed examinations of the neutrino-nucleus scattering mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (NN-SRC) into a charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino-nucleus scattering model yields flux-integrated differential cross sections with larger values and a longer tail in the high-muon-kinetic-energy region, achieving better consistency with experimental data from MiniBooNE. The hedge is that the model still neglects certain complex many-body effects such as two-particle-two-hole excitations and meson-exchange currents, which explains minor remaining discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed a CCQE neutrino-nucleus scattering model using the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), where the cross section is expressed as a product of the elementary weak cross section for neutrino scattering off a free nucleon and a scaling function f(psi) that reflects nuclear structure. The scaling function was derived from a neutron spectral function S(p,E) that combines a mean-field component calculated from the axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model using the SLy4 parameter set with a correlation component obtained via the light-front dynamics (LFD) method, where the high-momentum components of the deuteron momentum distribution are empirically rescaled. The model was applied to carbon-12 and compared with experimental data from MiniBooNE and NOMAD experiments.

- The scaling function was calculated at momentum transfer q = 1000 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-nucleus scattering that used the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model and mean-field models based on energy density functionals, which could not well reproduce experimental data. The paper builds on the scaling function approach developed by Amaro (2005) and Ivanov (2014), and incorporates NN-SRC effects using the LFD method previously applied by Wang (2021) and Niu (2022). The results enable more detailed examinations of neutron momentum distributions through CCQE neutrino scattering, which offers advantages over electron scattering for probing neutron properties due to the weak interaction's selectivity for neutrons.
