# Inclusive electron-nucleus cross section within the Self Consistent Green's Function approach

> We compute inclusive electron-nucleus cross sections using ab initio spectral functions of $^4$He and $^{16}$O obtained within the Self Consistent Green's Function approach. The formalism adopted is based on the factorization of the spectral function and the nuclear transition matrix elements. This allows to provide an accurate description of nuclear dynamics and to account for relativistic effects in the interaction vertex. Our calculations use a saturating chiral Hamiltonian in order reproduce the correct nuclear sizes. When final state interactions for the struck particle are accounted for, we find nice agreement between the data and the theory for the inclusive electron-$^{16}$O cross section. The results lay the foundations for future applications of the Self Consistent Green's Function method, in both closed and open shell nuclei, to neutrino data analysis. This work also presents results for the point-proton, charge and single-nucleon momentum distribution of the same two nuclei. The center of mass can affect these quantities for light nuclei and cannot be separated cleanly in most ab initio post-Hartree-Fock methods. In order to address this, we developed a Metropolis Monte Carlo calculation in which the center of mass coordinate can be subtracted exactly from the trial wave function and the expectation values. We gauged this effect for $^4$He by removing the center of mass effect from the Optimal Reference State wave function that is generated during the Self Consistent Green's Function calculations. Our findings clearly indicate that the residual center of mass contribution strongly modifies calculated matter distributions with respect to those obtained in the intrinsic frame. Hence, its subtraction is crucial for a correct description of light nuclei.

# Inclusive electron-nucleus cross section within the Self Consistent Green's Function approach

## Key claim

The Self Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) approach, using the saturating NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ chiral Hamiltonian, yields inclusive electron-nucleus cross sections for $^{16}$O that agree well with experimental data *when final state interactions for the struck particle are accounted for*, but the method's performance for $^4$He is less definitive and requires center-of-mass subtraction to obtain correct density distributions.

## Method

The authors computed ab initio spectral functions of $^4$He and $^{16}$O using the SCGF approach at the ADC(3) truncation level, expanding operators on a spherical harmonic oscillator basis. They employed the factorization of the spectral function and nuclear transition matrix elements within the Impulse Approximation to calculate inclusive electron-nucleus cross sections, and included final state interactions via a convolution scheme using a Dirac phenomenological optical potential. For light nuclei, they developed a Metropolis Monte Carlo calculation to subtract the center-of-mass contribution exactly from the trial wave function, using the Optimal Reference State Slater determinant as input.

## Key numbers

- Point-proton density calculations for $^4$He converge for $\hbar\Omega \approx 20$ MeV and $\nmax \geq 11$, with different parameter choices producing nearly superimposed curves.
- The charge density of $^4$He after center-of-mass subtraction shows a strong enhancement compared to the uncorrected OpRS result, bringing it close to the QMC calculation of Lonardoni (2017).
- The SCGF-ADC(3) charge density for $^{16}$O perfectly reproduces experimental points, confirming the NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ potential fitted to reproduce the experimental radius of $^{16}$O.
- The SCGF-ADC(3) momentum distribution for $^{16}$O lies above the QMC result of Lonardoni (2017) at low momenta, attributed to the NNLO$_{\rm{sat}}$ potential being much softer than the AV18+UIX potential used in QMC.
- The double differential electron-$^{16}$O cross sections show nice agreement between theory and data when FSI corrections are included, for incident electron energies and scattering angles corresponding to momentum transfer $|{\bf q}| \gtrsim 500$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SCGF method, previously applied to nuclear structure and spectral functions (Barbieri 2009, Cipollone 2014), to the calculation of inclusive electron-nucleus cross sections for neutrino physics applications. It validates the SCGF approach against electron scattering data, following the formalism of Benhar (2006) for spectral function factorization, and uses the convolution approach of Ankowski (2014) and Benhar (1991) for final state interactions. The results lay foundations for future applications to neutrino data analysis, particularly for open shell nuclei such as argon relevant to DUNE, and demonstrate that center-of-mass subtraction via Monte Carlo techniques is crucial for correct description of light nuclei like $^4$He.
