# A New Bayesian Framework with Natural Priors to Constrain the Neutron Star Equation of State

> We propose a new Bayesian framework to infer the neutron star equation of state (EOS) from mass and radius observations and neutron matter theory by defining priors that directly parameterize mass-radius space instead of pressure-energy density space. We use direct and accurate inversion approximations to map mass-radius relations to the underlying EOS. We systematically compare its EOS inferences with those inferred from traditional EOS parameterizations, taking care to quantify the systematic prior uncertainties of both. Our results show that prior uncertainties should be included in all Bayesian approaches. The more natural alternative framework provides broader coverage of the physically allowed mass-radius space, especially small radius configurations, and yields enhanced computational efficiency and substantially reduced dependence on prior choices. Our results demonstrate that direct parameterization in observed space offers a robust and efficient alternative to traditional methods.

# A New Bayesian Framework with Natural Priors to Constrain the Neutron Star Equation of State

## Key claim
The authors propose a Parameterized Mass-Radius (PMR) Bayesian framework that directly parameterizes mass-radius space rather than pressure-energy density space, claiming it provides broader coverage of physically allowed configurations (especially small radii) and substantially reduced dependence on prior choices. However, the PMR method under-populates the large radius and high mass regions compared to traditional approaches, and its posterior behavior is broadly similar to traditional methods across the full energy-density range.

## Method
The PMR method constructs a discrete mesh in mass-radius space anchored to a fixed point from N3LO chiral effective field theory calculations, then generates all physically realistic continuous M-R curves by connecting grid points subject to causality and thermodynamic stability constraints. The authors use analytical inversion formulae (Sun 2025) to map M-R curves to pressure-energy density relations, and apply Bayesian inference using NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0437-4715, and PSR J0030+0451. They systematically compare three PMR priors (uniform M-R sampling, uniform R_1.4 weighting, and varying N3LO boundaries) against traditional piecewise-polytropic (PP4) and spectral (Spectral4) parameterizations with uniform, log-uniform, and Gaussian sampling.

## Key numbers
- The N3LO chiral EFT transition density is n_t = 1.5 n_s, where n_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}; at this density the neutron star mass is M_t = 0.45 M_sun, radius R_t = 13.0 km, and pressure P_t = 9 MeV fm^{-3}
- The critical energy density for the minimum-radius EOS is E_c = 0.63 GeV fm^{-3}, set to achieve M_max = 2.0 M_sun
- The PMR mesh uses horizontal grid lines separated by 0.1 M_sun and angled grid lines separated by 0.2 km, with slope dM/dR = 2.2 M_sun km^{-1} for the central N3LO EOS
- For PMR_p (upper N3LO boundary), (M_t, R_t) = (0.52 M_sun, 13.2 km); for PMR_m (lower boundary), (M_t, R_t) = (0.35 M_sun, 13.0 km)
- The fraction of posterior variance from prior choices (sigma_prior/sigma_tot) remains below 50% in PMR, while traditional frameworks show mean values around 70% with typical variations of 50-80%

## Context
This work extends Bayesian inference methods for neutron star equations of state by addressing prior uncertainties that were largely unquantified in previous analyses (Brandes 2023, Rutherford 2024, Kalita 2025, Dong 2025). The authors build on the inversion formulae developed by Sun (2025) and the N3LO chiral EFT calculations of Drischler (2021), while comparing against standard piecewise-polytropic and spectral parameterizations. The study enables more reliable quantification of systematic prior uncertainties in Bayesian EOS inference and suggests that a conservative estimate of sigma_prior^2 = 0.5 sigma_tot^2 may be adopted for traditional frameworks when multiple prior assessments are impractical.
