# The Breathing Modes of the $B=2$ Skyrmion and the Spin-Orbit Interaction

> The coupling of the breathing and rotational modes of the skyrmion-skyrmion system leads to a nucleon-nucleon spin-orbit interaction of short range, as well as to spin-orbit potentials for the transitions $NN \to N(1440)N$, $NN \to NN(1440)$ and $NN \to N(1440)N(1440)$. The longest range behaviour of these spin-orbit potentials is calculated in closed form.

# The Breathing Modes of the $B=2$ Skyrmion and the Spin-Orbit Interaction

## Key claim
The coupling of breathing and rotational modes in the two-skyrmion system generates a nucleon-nucleon spin-orbit interaction whose longest-range component can be calculated in closed form, but the short-range behavior is only tentative due to the limited reliability of the product approximation and the omission of the stabilizing term in the Lagrangian density.

## Method
The authors employ the product ansatz for the $B=2$ skyrmion, introducing independent breathing coordinates $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$ for each skyrmion via a scale transformation of the hedgehog field. They expand the Lagrangian density to quadratic order in time derivatives, project onto nucleon states using spin and isospin Pauli matrices, and derive the spin-orbit potential by expanding around the minimum of the breathing potential at $\lambda_0 = 0.868$. The long-range behavior is estimated by approximating the field of one skyrmion as constant near the other.

## Key numbers
- The minimum of the effective breathing potential for a free nucleon occurs at $\lambda_0 = 0.868$.
- The breathing potential parameters are $V(\lambda_0) = (F_\pi / e_S) \times 39.14$ and $V = (F_\pi / e_S) \times 35.55$, with $F_\pi = 129$ MeV and $e_S = 5.45$.
- Expectation values for the breathing coordinate fluctuation $\xi^2$ are $\langle 0 | \xi^2 | 0 \rangle = 0.145$ for the ground state and $\langle 0 | \xi^2 | 1 \rangle = 0.380$ for the first excited breathing state (the $N(1440)$).
- The breathing modes do not appreciably modify the isospin-dependent spin-orbit interaction beyond 1 fm separation.

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model treatment of the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Nyman and Riska (1990) and Riska and Dannbom (1995), which considered only rotational modes, by incorporating the breathing modes responsible for the Roper resonance $N(1440)$ and $\Delta(1600)$ as studied by Hajduk and collaborators (1984) and Biedenharn, Dothan, and Tatur (1985). The results enable calculation of transition spin-orbit potentials for $NN \to N(1440)N$, $NN \to NN(1440)$, and $NN \to N(1440)N(1440)$ channels, though the short-range predictions remain tentative due to limitations of the product approximation.
