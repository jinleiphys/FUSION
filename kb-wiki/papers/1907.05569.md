# Decoherence of collective motion in warm nuclei

> Collective states in cold nuclei are represented by a wave function that assigns coherent phases to the participating nucleons. The degree of coherence decreases with excitation energy above the yrast line because of coupling to the increasingly dense background of quasiparticle excitations. The consequences of decoherence are discussed, starting with the well studied case of rotational damping. In addition to superdeformed bands, a highly excited oblate band is presented as a new example of screening from rotational damping. Suppression of pair correlation leads to incoherent thermal M1 radiation, which appears as an exponential spike (LEMAR) at zero energy in the $γ$ strength function of spherical nuclei. In deformed nuclei a Scissors Resonance appears and LEMAR changes to damped magnetic rotation, which is interpreted as partial restoration of coherence.

# Decoherence of collective motion in warm nuclei

## Key claim
The degree of coherence in nuclear collective motion decreases with excitation energy above the yrast line due to coupling to an increasingly dense background of quasiparticle excitations; this decoherence manifests as rotational damping, and in spherical nuclei the suppression of pair correlation leads to incoherent thermal M1 radiation appearing as an exponential spike (LEMAR) at zero energy in the gamma strength function, while in deformed nuclei a Scissors Resonance appears and LEMAR changes to damped magnetic rotation interpreted as partial restoration of coherence.

## Method
The authors analyze rotational damping using the Cranked Shell Model with surface delta-interaction and numerical diagonalization to study band mixing and fragmentation of E2 strength. For the low-energy M1 radiation, they perform Shell Model calculations for nuclei such as 94Mo and Fe isotopes, collecting B(M1) values into energy bins of 100 keV width and averaging over initial-state energy ranges. The resulting gamma strength functions are compared with the constant-temperature level density expression and with experimental data from the Oslo group.

## Key numbers
- Coherence angle for superdeformed bands: Δψ = 5°, giving angular momentum coherence length ΔI = π/Δψ = 36
- Coherence angle for well-deformed nuclei: Δψ = 8°, giving ΔI = π/Δψ = 22
- Coherence angle for weakly deformed magnetic bands: Δψ = 25°, giving ΔI = 2π/Δψ = 14
- Typical number of resolved ridges in warm nuclei: N_ridge = 2–3, giving ΔI = 4–6, compared to ΔI = 22 in cold rare earth nuclei
- Exponential fall-off constant for LEMAR spike in 94Mo: T_B = 0.49 MeV (full residual interaction); T_B = 0.24 MeV with residual interaction reduced by factor of 2
- Experimental B(E2,2+1→0+1) for 60Fe: 190 (eb)^2; for 68Fe: 450 (eb)^2
- Calculated B(E2,2+1→0+1) for 60Fe: 368 (eb)^2; for 68Fe: 406 (eb)^2
- Integrated M1 strength below 5 MeV varies by at most 8% from average of 9.80 μN^2 across Fe isotopes
- Sum B(M1,0+1→1+) from ground state to all 1+ states in 68Fe: 1.7 μN^2
- Moment of inertia from fuzzy rotational threads in 68Fe: J ≈ 14 MeV^-1, compared to rigid body value J_rig ≈ 17 MeV^-1

## Context
This work extends the Copenhagen/Milano/Kyoto collaboration studies of rotational damping, recently reviewed by Leoni and Lopez-Martens, and builds on the Oslo group's discovery (Guttormsen 2005) of enhanced gamma strength below 2 MeV. The Shell Model explanation of LEMAR by Schwengner (2013) for 94Mo is extended to Fe isotopes (Schwengner 2017) and to the region around 132Sn relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis. The paper connects to the concept of coherence angle introduced by Frauendorf and to the statistical ensemble approach of Zelevinsky. The results enable improved modeling of (n,gamma) reaction rates for stellar nucleosynthesis and nuclear engineering applications, and challenge the conjecture that LEMAR appears only where magnetic rotation is predicted.
