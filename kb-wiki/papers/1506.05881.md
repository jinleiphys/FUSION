# Shear Viscosity of a Quark Plasma Near the Chiral Phase Transition

> We calculate the shear viscosity $η$ of a quark plasma through the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit, at finite temperature $T$, and baryon number chemical potential $μ_B$. We solve the Boltzmann equation by using in the collision term cross sections that are correct to leading order in the coupling constant. We find the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density above and slightly below the chiral transition temperature to vary from 1.5 to 13 times the conjectured lower bound of $(4π)^{-1}$ depending on the chemical potential to temperature ratio. Ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is found to be a monotonically increasing function of the ratio of chemical potential to temperature for temperatures between 185 and 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a quark plasma near the chiral phase transition varies from 1.5 to 13 times the conjectured lower bound of (4π)⁻¹ depending on the chemical potential to temperature ratio, but this result is obtained by approximating quark interactions with tree-level cross sections correct to leading order in the coupling constant rather than including all diagrams to leading order in the number of colors.

## Method

The author solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit at finite temperature and baryon number chemical potential, using matrix elements for quark-quark and quark-antiquark scattering from the tree-level scalar and pseudoscalar interaction terms in the Lagrangian. The deviation from equilibrium was expanded in a basis of five functions, and the shear viscosity was computed from the source vector and the inverse of the collision matrix in this basis. Quasiparticle masses were taken as zero in the chirally symmetric phase and as the nonperturbative constituent mass from the gap equation in the broken phase.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperatures for chiral symmetry breaking: T_c = 190 MeV at μ/T = 0.0 and T_c = 187 MeV at μ/T = 0.3.
- The dimensionless product η G² T is constant in the chirally symmetric phase for each μ/T ratio when the 3-momentum cutoff is taken to infinity.
- At vanishing chemical potential and 300 MeV temperature, the ratio η/s ≈ 0.13, which is 60% greater than the conjectured lower bound of (4π)⁻¹.
- The ratio η/T³ scales as T⁻⁴ in the high-temperature phase.
- The NJL model parameters are G Λ² = 2.14 and Λ = 653 MeV.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is a monotonically increasing function of μ/T for temperatures between 185 and 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in the NJL model that used the relaxation time approximation, such as those by Zhuang et al., and finds exact agreement with their result in the broken symmetry phase but different scaling in the chirally symmetric phase. The results are compared to leading-order perturbative QCD calculations by Arnold et al., which gave η/s ≈ 2.60 at vanishing chemical potential, nearly 3 times the highest value obtained here; the NJL model reproduces the low viscosity observed at RHIC much better than perturbative QCD. The calculation is limited to μ/T ≤ 1 and temperatures above 185 MeV, and the author notes that including the width of pions from next-to-leading order in N_c diagrams would be a nontrivial extension for future work.
