# Double beta decay to the first $2^+$ state within a boson expansion formalism with a projected spherical single particle basis

> The Gamow-Teller transition operator is written as a polynomial in the dipole proton-neutron and quadrupole charge conserving QRPA boson operators, using the prescription of the boson expansion technique of Belyaev-Zelevinski type. Then, the $2νββ$ process ending on the first $2^+$ state in the daughter nucleus is allowed via one, two and three boson states describing the odd-odd intermediate nucleus. The approach uses a single particle basis which is obtained by projecting out the good angular momentum from an orthogonal set of deformed functions. The basis for mother and daughter nuclei have different deformations. The GT transition amplitude as well as the half lives were calculated for ten transitions. Results are compared with the available data as well as with some predictions obtained with other methods.

## Key claim

The $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay to the first $2^+$ state in the daughter nucleus, which is forbidden at the pnQRPA level, becomes allowed when higher-order boson expansion terms (one-, two-, and three-boson intermediate states) are included in the Gamow-Teller transition operator, using a projected spherical single-particle basis that accounts for different deformations in mother and daughter nuclei.

## Method

The Gamow-Teller transition operator was expanded as a polynomial in dipole proton-neutron and quadrupole charge-conserving QRPA boson operators using the Belyaev-Zelevinski boson expansion technique. A single-particle basis was constructed by projecting good angular momentum from deformed functions obtained from a particle-core Hamiltonian, with mother and daughter nuclei having different deformations. The $2\nu\beta\beta$ process $0^+ \to 2^+$ was calculated via one-, two-, and three-boson states describing the odd-odd intermediate nucleus, and the GT transition amplitude and half-lives were computed for ten double-beta emitters.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Ca: GT transition amplitude $|M^{(0\to 2)}_{GT}| = 0.901 \times 10^{-3} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$, half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 1.72 \times 10^{24} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{100}$Mo: GT transition amplitude $|M^{(0\to 2)}_{GT}| = 0.136 \times 10^{-2} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$, half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 1.21 \times 10^{25} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{128}$Te: GT transition amplitude $|M^{(0\to 2)}_{GT}| = 0.229 \times 10^{-2} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$, half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 4.7 \times 10^{33} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{130}$Te: GT transition amplitude $|M^{(0\to 2)}_{GT}| = 0.620 \times 10^{-3} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$, half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 6.94 \times 10^{26} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{136}$Xe: GT transition amplitude $|M^{(0\to 2)}_{GT}| = 0.249 \times 10^{-2} \text{ MeV}^{-1}$, half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 3.88 \times 10^{26} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{76}$Ge: half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 5.75 \times 10^{28} \text{ yr}$, experimental lower limit $> 1.1 \times 10^{21} \text{ yr}$
- For $^{116}$Cd: half-life $T_{1/2}^{(0\to 2)} = 3.4 \times 10^{26} \text{ yr}$, experimental lower limit $> 2.3 \times 10^{21} \text{ yr}$
- The $Q$ value for $^{128}$Te is $0.836$ in units of $m_e c^2$; for $^{134}$Xe it is $0.460$ in units of $m_e c^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pnQRPA formalism using a projected spherical single-particle basis (Raduta 2004, 2005) for ground-to-ground $2\nu\beta\beta$ transitions to the $0^+ \to 2^+$ decay branch, which was previously treated only with a spherical basis (Raduta 1991). The results are compared with predictions from Suhonen (1997) using a spherical basis and renormalized pnQRPA, and with Hirsch (1995) using a deformed SU(3) basis; the present calculations yield longer half-lives than the spherical-basis results. The approach enables systematic calculation of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay to excited $2^+$ states in deformed nuclei, where the transition is forbidden at the pnQRPA level but allowed through higher-order boson expansion corrections.
