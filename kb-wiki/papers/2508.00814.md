# $ψ(2S)$ production in jets using NRQCD

> Based on recent data from LHCb, we study $ψ(2S)$ production in jets using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) in conjunction with the Fragmenting Jet Function (FJF) and Gluon Fragmentation Improved Pythia (GFIP) formalisms. Similar to previous studies of $J/ψ$ production in jets, our results show that these formalisms offer a much better description of data than the default Pythia+NRQCD prediction. We compare and contrast the predictions from the FJF formalism and the GFIP approach. In addition, our results show that the distribution of $ψ(2S)$ in jets is an excellent discriminator to test different predictions for the $ψ(2S)$ LDMEs from various extractions. We find a large disparity between the predictions from three different collaborations, showing that a more precise extraction of the $ψ(2S)$ LDMEs may be necessary.

# ψ(2S) production in jets using NRQCD

## Key claim

The Fragmenting Jet Function (FJF) and Gluon Fragmentation Improved Pythia (GFIP) formalisms provide a much better description of LHCb data on ψ(2S) production in jets than the default Pythia+NRQCD prediction, but the agreement depends strongly on which set of long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) is used, with the Bodwin et al. set showing the best consistency while the Brambilla et al. and Butenschoen and Kniel (B&K) sets produce significant discrepancies at high z.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical frameworks: the Fragmenting Jet Function (FJF) formalism based on Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) with next-to-leading-log-prime (NLL') precision, and the Gluon Fragmentation Improved Pythia (GFIP) Monte Carlo approach. In both methods, MadGraph generated hard parton distributions from proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, which were then showered to the scale 2m_c and convolved with NRQCD fragmentation functions using four LDME sets (color-singlet ³S₁[¹] and color-octet ³S₁[⁸], ¹S₀[⁸], ³P_J[⁸]) for gluon and charm fragmentation channels, with kinematic cuts matching the LHCb experimental setup including jet pseudorapidity 2.5 < η < 4.0, jet radius R = 0.5, and p_T(jet) > 5 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- LHCb data collected at center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV in 2016, with ψ(2S) reconstructed via decay to J/ψ(→ μ⁺μ⁻)π⁺π⁻ in the forward region
- Jet radius R = 0.5, pseudorapidity range 2.5 < η < 4.0, p_T(jet) > 5 GeV/c
- Muon candidates required to have p_T > 0.5 GeV/c and p(μ) > 6 GeV/c; pion candidates required to have p_T > 0.5 GeV/c and p(π) > 3 GeV/c
- Color-singlet LDME ⟨O(³S₁[¹])⟩ = 0.76 GeV³ (Bodwin et al. and B&K) or 0.71 ± 0.21 GeV³ (Brambilla et al.)
- Color-octet LDMEs have uncertainties on the order of 100-200%, with the ³P_J[⁸] LDME ranging from -0.114 ± 0.121 × 10⁻² GeV³ (Bodwin et al.) to 0.865 ± 0.055 × 10⁻² GeV³ (B&K) and 1.55 ± 4.9 × 10⁻² GeV³ (Brambilla et al.)
- Comparison restricted to intermediate range 0.1 < z < 0.9 to avoid endpoint regions where perturbative corrections may be sizable
- GFIP parton shower cutoff scale set to 1.6 GeV, corresponding to approximately 2m_c

## Context

This work extends the FJF+NRQCD approach previously applied to J/ψ production in jets (Bain et al., 2016; Bain et al., 2017) to the excited ψ(2S) state, motivated by recent LHCb measurements (LHCb, 2024). The default Pythia+NRQCD combination had previously failed to describe both J/ψ (LHCb, 2017) and ψ(2S) (LHCb, 2024) in-jet data. The results demonstrate that ψ(2S) production in jets is an excellent discriminator between different LDME extractions from Bodwin et al. (2015), Butenschoen and Kniel (2022), and Brambilla et al. (2022), revealing large cancellations between ³S₁[⁸] and ³P_J[⁸] channels that are consistent with earlier observations in J/ψ production (Bodwin et al., 2014; Bain et al., 2016). The work underscores the need for more precise extraction of ψ(2S) LDMEs and enables future studies using this observable to constrain quarkonium production mechanisms.
