# Cumulant versus jet-like three-particle correlations

> Two-particle jet-like azimuthal correlations have revealed intriguing modifications to the away-side of high pt trigger particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Three-particle jet-like azimuthal correlation and three-particle azimuthal cumulant have been analyzed in experiments in attempt to distinguish conical emission of jet-correlated particles from other physics mechanisms. We investigate the difference between three-particle jet-like correlation and three-particle cumulant in azimuth. We show, under the circumstance where the away-side two-particle correlation is relatively flat in azimuth and similar in magnitude to the azimuthal average of the two-particle correlation signal, that the three-particle cumulant cannot distinguish conical emission from other physics mechanisms. The three-particle jet-like correlation, on the other hand, retains its discrimination power.

## Key claim

The three-particle azimuthal cumulant method cannot distinguish conical emission (such as Mach-cone shock waves) from other physics mechanisms when the away-side two-particle correlation is relatively flat in azimuth and similar in magnitude to the azimuthal average of the two-particle correlation signal; the three-particle jet-like correlation method retains its discrimination power under those same conditions.

## Method

The authors compared the three-particle jet-like correlation method (extended from two-particle jet-correlation with event-mixing background subtraction) and the three-particle lab-frame cumulant method (based on mathematically defined cumulants of particle densities in the laboratory azimuthal angle). They constructed a simple analytical model with a Gaussian jet-like two-particle correlation function atop a large uniform background, assuming factorization of the three-particle correlation into products of two-particle correlations, and examined two cases: narrow jets (sigma=0.2) and realistic broad jets (sigma=0.4 and 0.7) with a Mach-cone double-hump structure at cone angle theta=1 radian.

## Key numbers

- Background level used in the model: B_true = 150/(2pi) (uniform background density)
- Near-side jet-correlated particle number: N1 = 0.7; away-side jet-correlated particle number: N2 = 1.2
- Cone angle for Mach-cone structure: theta = 1 radian
- Narrow jet widths: sigma1 = 0.2, sigma2 = 0.2 (case A); realistic jet widths: sigma1 = 0.4, sigma2 = 0.7 (case B)
- For case B, the ZYA1 background normalization overestimates the true background by delta B1 = 0.12

## Context

This work extends the discussion of two-particle jet-like azimuthal correlations at RHIC, which had revealed modifications to the away-side of high-pT trigger particles in central heavy-ion collisions (STAR and PHENIX collaborations, 2004-2005). The paper directly compares the three-particle jet-correlation method used by STAR (Star_3part, 2006) with the lab-frame cumulant method proposed by other authors (cumulant, 2006). The results show that the cumulant method can produce complex structures that obscure or eliminate the distinctive Mach-cone signature, while the jet-correlation method preserves the conical emission structure even when the background is overestimated. This enables future experimental analyses to correctly identify conical emission signatures and potentially extract the speed of sound from Mach-cone angles.
