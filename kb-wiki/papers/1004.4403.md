# Analytical relations between nuclear symmetry energy and single-nucleon potentials in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> Using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, we derive general expressions for the quadratic and quartic symmetry energies in terms of single-nucleon potentials in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. These analytical relations are useful for gaining deeper insights into the microscopic origins of the uncertainties in our knowledge on nuclear symmetry energies especially at supra-saturation densities. As examples, the formalism is applied to two model single-nucleon potentials that are widely used in transport model simulations of heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

Using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, the authors derive general analytical expressions for both the quadratic and quartic symmetry energies in terms of single-nucleon potentials in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, showing that the previously known relation for the quadratic term is generally valid and extending it to the quartic term. The formalism is applied to two model single-nucleon potentials (BGBD and MDI) that are widely used in transport model simulations, but the derived relations themselves are model-independent.

## Method

The authors first use the differential form of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, starting from the neutron and proton chemical potentials, to expand both sides of the difference equation in powers of the isospin asymmetry parameter δ and equate coefficients. They then verify the results using the integral form of the HVH theorem, starting from the total energy density expressed in momentum space and performing Taylor-series expansions around the Fermi momentum. The formalism is applied to the Bombaci-Gale-Bertsch-Das Gupta (BGBD) potential and a modified Gogny Momentum-Dependent-Interaction (MDI) potential, evaluating the kinetic and various potential contributions to both the quadratic and quartic symmetry energies.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic symmetry energy at saturation density is known to be around 30 MeV from analyzing nuclear masses within liquid-drop models
- The quartic term has been estimated to be less than 1 MeV at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀
- The kinetic contribution to the quadratic symmetry energy is E_sym,2^kin(ρ) = (ħ²/6m)(3π²/2)^(2/3) ρ^(2/3)
- The kinetic contribution to the quartic symmetry energy is E_sym,4^kin(ρ) = (ħ²/162m)(3π²/2)^(2/3) ρ^(2/3) = (1/81) t(k_F)
- For the BGBD potential with parameters A=-144 MeV, B=203.3 MeV, C=-75 MeV and σ=7/6, the incompressibility K₀=210 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
- The ratio E_sym,4(ρ)/E_sym,2(ρ) can reach up to about ±10% at high densities for both cases of m_n^*>m_p^* and m_n^*<m_p^*
- For the MDI interaction, C_unlike=-103.4 MeV while C_like=-11.7 MeV, and σ=4/3

## Context

This work extends the earlier results of Brueckner, Dabrowski and Haensel (1960s-1970s) who first showed the relation between the quadratic symmetry energy and single-nucleon potentials using K-matrices within Brueckner theory, and the Lane potential formalism (1962). The derived analytical relations enable deeper insights into the microscopic origins of uncertainties in nuclear symmetry energies, especially at supra-saturation densities where model predictions are diverse, and allow evaluation of corrections due to the quartic term to the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, which can significantly modify the proton fraction in neutron stars at β-equilibrium and the core-crust transition density.
