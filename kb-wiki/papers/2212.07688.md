# Pion and photon beam initiated backward charmonium or lepton pair production

> Hard exclusive reactions initiated by pion or photon beams within the near-backward kinematical regime specified by the small Mandelstam variable $-u$ can be studied to access pion-to-nucleon and photon-to-nucleon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). Checking the validity of collinear factorized description of pion and photon induced reactions in terms of TDAs allows to test the universality of TDAs between the space-like and time-like regimes that is the indispensable feature of the QCD collinear factorization approach. In this short review we consider the exclusive pion- and photo-production off nucleon of a highly virtual lepton pair (or heavy quarkonium) in the near-backward region. We first employ a simplistic cross channel nucleon exchange model of pion-to-nucleon TDAs to estimate the magnitude of the corresponding cross sections for the kinematical conditions of J-PARC. We then illustrate the flexibility of our approach by building a two parameter model for the photon-to-nucleon TDAs based on preliminary results for near threshold $J/ψ$ photoproduction at JLab and provide our estimates for near-backward $J/ψ$ photoproduction and Timelike Compton Scattering cross sections for the kinematical conditions of JLab and of future EIC and EicC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hard exclusive reactions in the near-backward regime (small Mandelstam variable $-u$) initiated by pion or photon beams can be described by collinear factorization in terms of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), but the cross section estimates rely on simplistic cross-channel nucleon exchange models for pion-to-nucleon TDAs and a two-parameter model for photon-to-nucleon TDAs, meaning the numerical predictions are model-dependent rather than derived from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The authors employ the collinear factorization framework at leading twist-3 to compute helicity amplitudes for near-backward $\pi N \to N' \gamma^*$, $\pi N \to N' J/\psi$, $\gamma N \to N' \gamma^*$ (backward Timelike Compton Scattering), and $\gamma N \to N' J/\psi$ reactions, expressing them as convolutions of pion-to-nucleon or photon-to-nucleon TDAs with nucleon distribution amplitudes and hard scattering kernels computed from 21 relevant Feynman diagrams. For pion-to-nucleon TDAs they use a cross-channel nucleon exchange model, and for photon-to-nucleon TDAs they build a two-parameter model based on preliminary results for near-threshold $J/\psi$ photoproduction at JLab. Cross sections are then estimated for the kinematical conditions of J-PARC, JLab, and future EIC and EicC facilities.

## Key numbers

- $f_N = 5.0 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$: nucleon light-cone wave function normalization constant
- $f_\pi = 93$ MeV: pion weak decay constant
- $f_\psi = 415.5 \pm 4.9$ MeV: charmonium normalization constant extracted from $J/\psi \to e^+ e^-$ leptonic decay width
- $\alpha_{em} \simeq 1/137$: electromagnetic fine structure constant
- $\alpha_s \simeq 0.3$: strong coupling used in the calculations
- $\bar{M} = 3$ GeV: average mass approximately equal to the charmonium mass $M_\psi \simeq 2 m_c$
- The skewness variable $\xi$ is approximated as $\xi \simeq Q'^2/(2W^2 - Q'^2)$ for lepton pair production and $\xi \simeq \bar{M}^2/(2W^2 - \bar{M}^2)$ for charmonium production

## Context

This work extends the research program on hard exclusive reactions in near-backward kinematics, which is complementary to the well-studied near-forward Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) and Deeply Virtual Meson Production (DVMP) regimes. The paper builds on prior work by Pire et al. (2016) on pion-to-nucleon TDAs and Pire et al. (2022) on photon-to-nucleon TDAs, and is motivated by recent experimental results including the first Timelike Compton Scattering study by the CLAS collaboration (2021) and near-threshold $J/\psi$ photoproduction measurements by GlueX (2019) and Adhikari et al. (2023). The estimates provided enable future experimental searches at J-PARC, JLab, and planned EIC and EicC facilities to test the universality of TDAs between space-like and time-like regimes.
