# Exact (1+1)-dimensional flows of a perfect fluid

> We present a general solution of relativistic (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics for a perfect fluid flowing along the longitudinal direction as a function of time, uniformly in transverse space. The Khalatnikov potential is expressed as a linear combination of two generating functions with polynomial coefficients of 2 variables. The polynomials, whose algebraic equations are solved, define an infinite-dimensional basis of solutions. The kinematics of the (1+1)-dimensional flow are reconstructed from the potential.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general solution of relativistic (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics for a perfect fluid flowing longitudinally, but the solution is restricted to a specific infinite-dimensional basis of regular solutions derived from the Khalatnikov potential, with the claim that a fully general solution remains an open problem.

## Method

The authors combine the Khalatnikov potential method (a hodograph transformation that converts the nonlinear hydrodynamic equations into a linear partial differential equation) with the previously known "harmonic flow" solutions. They solve the linear Khalatnikov equation by proposing an Ansatz for the solution as a linear combination of two generating functions with polynomial coefficients in two variables (a and b, related to temperature and rapidity). The polynomials are determined by solving coupled algebraic equations derived from the Khalatnikov equation, using Laplace transform methods and homogeneity expansions.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound is treated as a constant, denoted by \(c_s\) with \(c_s^2 \equiv 1/g\), where \(g\) is a constant parameter
- The Khalatnikov equation takes the form \(c_s^2 \partial_\theta^2 \chi - (1-c_s^2) \partial_\theta \chi - \partial_y^2 \chi = 0\)
- The harmonic flow potential is proportional to the imaginary error function \(\text{erfi}[z] = \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_0^z e^{t^2} dt\)
- The entropy distribution for harmonic flow is \(\frac{dS}{dy} \propto \frac{a+b}{ab} e^{-(a-b)^2}\), which contains singularities at \(a=0\) or \(b=0\)
- The regular solutions are indexed by an integer \(K \in \mathbb{N}\), with polynomials of maximum degree \(d=4K\) for \(P^{(1)}\) and \(d=4K-1\) for \(P^{(0)}\)
- The first family of solutions (allowed) uses the multipole at \(\lambda=0\) in the Laplace transform, giving polynomial solutions; the second family (forbidden) uses the multipole at \(\lambda=2\) and leads to obstructions in the polynomial equations

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies of Landau (1953) and Bjorken (1983) on relativistic (1+1)-dimensional perfect fluid flow, which previously only provided specific asymptotic or boost-invariant solutions. It builds upon the harmonic flow solutions of Peschanski and Saridakis (2009) that interpolate between Landau and Bjorken flows, and the Khalatnikov potential formalism from Khalatnikov (1954). The results enable the construction of regular entropy distributions without the singularities present in the harmonic flow, and may be useful for applications of the AdS/CFT correspondence to plasma physics, where going beyond the Bjorken flow is an important open question.
