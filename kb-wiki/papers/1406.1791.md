# Baryon resonances in large $N_c$ QCD

> We review the current status and present open challenges of large $N_c$ QCD baryon spectroscopy. After introducing the $1/N_c$ expansion method we first shortly revisit the latest achievements for the ground state properties. Next we discuss the applicability of this method to excited states, presenting two different approaches with their advantages and disadvantages. Selected results for the spectrum and strong and electromagnetic decays are described. We also show further developments supported by the qualitative compatibility between the quark excitation picture and the meson-nucleon scattering picture. We give a quantitative comparison between results obtained from the mass formula of the $1/N_c$ expansion method and quark models and shortly discuss the implications of different large $N_c$ limits.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current status and open challenges of large \(N_c\) QCD baryon spectroscopy, arguing that the \(1/N_c\) expansion method provides a model-independent and systematic tool for baryon spectroscopy that is closer to QCD than quark models, though the method's applicability to excited states remains technically more difficult and requires two distinct procedures.

## Method

The authors review the \(1/N_c\) expansion method for baryons, starting from 't Hooft's perturbative expansion in powers of \(1/N_c\) and Witten's extension to baryons. They describe two approaches for treating mixed symmetric spin-flavor states in excited baryons: a Hartree approximation that splits the system into a ground state core and an excited quark, and an alternative procedure based on the identity of all quarks that uses completely antisymmetric total wave functions with mixed symmetry orbital and spin-flavor parts. The review covers the operator expansion method for ground and excited states, including mass formulas, strong and electromagnetic decays, and comparisons with quark models.

## Key numbers

* Ground state baryon masses in lattice QCD have been reproduced at a "few percent level" (no specific percentage given in text).
* The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) is \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{1/2})\) in the large \(N_c\) counting.
* The axial vector coupling constant \(g_A\) is \(\mathcal{O}(N_c)\) in the large \(N_c\) counting.
* The baryon mass scales as \(M_{\text{baryon}} \sim \mathcal{O}(N_c)\) in the Hartree approximation.
* The characteristic width of an excited baryon is \(N_c^0\) according to Witten, while for some class of states the width goes like \(N_c^{-1}\).
* The mass splitting for ground state baryons starts at order \(1/N_c\), while for mixed symmetric excited states the splitting starts at order \(N_c^0\).
* The analysis of excited states in the nonstrange sector covers a resonance mass region up to about 2.5 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on large \(N_c\) QCD by 't Hooft (1974) and Witten, and builds upon the consistency conditions derived by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1993-1994) for ground state baryons. It addresses the question raised by Pirjol and Yan and later by Cohen and Lebed about whether the \(1/N_c\) expansion method can be applied to excited baryons, given that excited baryons are not generically stable asymptotic states even at large \(N_c\). The review enables future work on classifying highly excited resonances into SU(3) multiplets and predicting mass ranges for missing partners, as well as combining lattice QCD simulations with varying numbers of colors to test the validity of the \(1/N_c\) expansion.
