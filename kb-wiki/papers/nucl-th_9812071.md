# Production and Validation of Isotope Production Cross Section Libraries for Neutrons and Protons to 1.7 GeV

> For validation and development of codes and for modeling isotope production in high power accelerators and APT Materials studies, we have produced experimental, calculated, and evaluated activation libraries for interaction of nucleons with nuclides covering about a third of all natural elements. For targets considered here, our compilation of experimental data is the most complete we are aware of, since it contains all data available on the Web, in journal papers, laboratory reports, theses, and books, as well as all data included in the large compilation by Sobolevsky with co-authors (NUCLEX) published recently by Springer-Verlag in 4 volumes. Our evaluated library was produced using all available experimental cross sections together with calculations by the CEM95, LAHET, and HMS-ALICE codes and with the European Activation File EAF-97 and LANL Update II of the ECNAF Neutron Activation Cross-Section Library.

## Key claim

The authors have produced experimental, calculated, and evaluated activation libraries for nucleon-nucleus interactions covering about one third of all natural elements, but caution that neither measurements nor available codes permit creation of a reliable library covering all needed data at intermediate energies, as predictions by the best models can differ for yields of certain isotopes above 100 MeV by a factor of 100 or more.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental proton-induced isotope production cross sections from all available sources including the Web, journal papers, laboratory reports, theses, books, and the NUCLEX compilation by Sobolevsky and co-authors, storing data in 32 separate files by element. They performed calculations using the CEM95 code for energies above 100 MeV, the LAHET code system (version 2.83), and the HMS-ALICE code for energies below 100 MeV, then produced an evaluated library by manually approximating excitation functions using experimental data as highest priority alongside the most reliable code results in their respective energy regions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental library contains 22,679 data points for 82 targets of 32 elements covering 2,272 proton-induced reactions.
- For comparison, the NUCLEX compilation contains 13,389 data points for the same targets.
- The library includes 95 data points for neutron-induced reactions on Bi and C targets covering 14 reactions.
- For targets like Ca, Fe, Co, Y, Zr, Ba, and Au, the authors have 2-3 times more data points than in NUCLEX.
- For targets such as 12C, 14N, 16O, 20Ne, 32S, 40Ar, 40Ca, and 126Xe, there are no data at all in NUCLEX, while the authors have data for 181 reactions on these targets.
- Most reactions are calculated for energies from 100 MeV to 1.7 GeV, with some calculated from 10 MeV to 5 GeV.
- The evaluated library covers 70 nuclides of 25 elements for medical isotope production studies.

## Context

This work extends the NUCLEX compilation by Sobolevsky and co-authors (1997) by incorporating recent measurements from groups such as Michel et al. (Hannover), Titarenko et al. (ITEP), and others, as well as data from reverse kinematics experiments. The libraries enable validation and development of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM95) and the HMS-ALICE code, and support applications including accelerator transmutation of waste (ATW), accelerator production of tritium (APT), medical radioisotope production, and cosmochemistry studies. The authors plan to extend the libraries and make them available through the Web.
