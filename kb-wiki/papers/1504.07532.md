# Euler-Heisenberg-Weiss action for QCD+QED

> We derive an analytic expression for one-loop effective action of QCD+QED at zero and finite temperatures by using the Schwinger's proper time method. The result is a nonlinear effective action not only for electromagnetic and chromo-electromagnetic fields but also the Polyakov loop, and thus reproduces the Euler-Heisenberg action in QED, QCD, and QED+QCD, and also the Weiss potential for the Polyakov loop at finite temperature. As applications of this "Euler-Heisenberg-Weiss" action in QCD+QED, we investigate quark pair productions induced by QCD+QED fields at zero temperature and the Polyakov loop in the presence of strong electromagnetic fields. Quark one-loop contribution to the effective potential of the Polyakov loop explicitly breaks the center symmetry, and is found to be enhanced by the magnetic field, which is consistent with the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD simulation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a one-loop effective action for QCD+QED that simultaneously depends on electromagnetic fields, chromo-electromagnetic fields, and the Polyakov loop, thereby unifying the Euler-Heisenberg action and the Weiss potential; the quark one-loop contribution to the Polyakov loop potential explicitly breaks center symmetry and is enhanced by a magnetic field, consistent with inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD, but the derivation assumes covariantly constant background fields and the Polyakov gauge.

## Method

The authors use the Schwinger proper time method to compute the one-loop effective action, treating both the chromo-electromagnetic fields and the Polyakov loop as background fields via the background field method. They decompose the gluon field into a covariantly constant background and quantum fluctuations, integrate out quark, gluon, and ghost fluctuations at one-loop order in the Feynman gauge, and evaluate the resulting functional determinants at finite temperature using Matsubara summation and Poisson resummation. The Polyakov loop is incorporated by shifting the Matsubara frequencies in the temporal direction within the Polyakov gauge.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2): the Polyakov loop is given by \(\Phi = \cos(\pi C)\) where \(C = g\bar{\A}_4/(2\pi T)\).
- For SU(3): the Polyakov loop is given by \(\Phi = \frac{1}{3}\{1 + 2\cos(\pi C)\}\).
- The matrix element \(\langle x | e^{-i(-\mathcal{D}_{v_h}^2)s} |x \rangle\) at finite temperature contains a factor \([1 + 2\sum_{n=1}^\infty e^{i\mathfrak{h}(s)n^2/(4T^2)} \cos(g v_h \bar{\A}_4 n / T)]\), where \(\mathfrak{h}(s)\) depends on the Lorentz invariants \(\aaa\) and \(\bbb\) of the chromo-electromagnetic field.
- The Lorentz invariants are defined as \(\aaa = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\sqrt{\mathcal{F}^4 + (\mathcal{F}\cdot\tilde{\mathcal{F}})^2} + \mathcal{F}^2}\) and \(\bbb = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\sqrt{\mathcal{F}^4 + (\mathcal{F}\cdot\tilde{\mathcal{F}})^2} - \mathcal{F}^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Euler-Heisenberg effective action for QED (Heisenberg 1935, Schwinger 1951) and its QCD analog (Savvidy 1977, Nielsen 1978, Leutwyler 1980) to include both QCD and QED fields simultaneously at finite temperature, while also incorporating the Weiss potential for the Polyakov loop (Gross, Pisarski, Yaffe 1980; Weiss 1980). It generalizes the finite-temperature effective action for the Polyakov loop and chromo-electric field computed by Gies (2000). The result enables the study of quark pair production in combined QCD+QED fields and the behavior of the Polyakov loop under strong electromagnetic fields, providing a framework to investigate inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD simulations (Bali 2011, 2012; Bruckmann 2013).
