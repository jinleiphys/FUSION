# Electromagnetic probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The penetrating nature of electromagnetic probes makes them an ideal candidate to study properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). A selection of recent developments in the theory and phenomenology of electromagnetic probes is discussed, with an emphasis given towards how these probes can be used to constrain QGP trapnsport coefficients. A Bayesian treatment of electromagnetic radiation, similar to the one of soft hadronic observables and jets, is suggested as a path towards imposing more stringent constraints on various transport coefficients of the QCD medium.

# Electromagnetic probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Electromagnetic probes can be used to constrain QGP transport coefficients, but a Bayesian treatment of electromagnetic radiation, similar to existing Bayesian analyses of soft hadronic observables and jets, is needed to impose more stringent constraints on various transport coefficients of the QCD medium. The paper suggests this approach rather than demonstrating it, and acknowledges that additional sources of EM production beyond the hydrodynamical one may reduce the sensitivity of EM probes to QGP transport coefficients.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical calculations of the electromagnetic spectral function using both perturbative QCD (at next-to-leading order) and non-perturbative lattice approaches, as well as hadronic calculations using effective Lagrangians with Vector meson Dominance Model and Functional Renormalization Group approaches. Non-equilibrium viscous corrections to photon and dilepton production rates are incorporated via Chapman-Enskog/14-moment approximations to the distribution functions, and the resulting observables (yields and anisotropic flow \(v_2\)) are computed within hydrodynamic simulations coupled to hadronic transport.

## Key numbers
- The pseudo-critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration from lattice QCD is \(T = 156 \pm 1.5\) MeV (Bazavov 2018).
- At high temperatures \(T \gtrsim 0.16\) GeV, partonic interactions in the QGP dominate electromagnetic emissions; at lower temperatures \(T \lesssim 0.16\) GeV, hadronic processes source EM radiation.
- Dileptons from the QGP are preferentially emitted at intermediate invariant masses \(1 \lesssim M \lesssim 2.5\) GeV.
- Direct photons from the QCD medium are produced at \(p_T \lesssim 1\) GeV (hadronic interactions) and \(1 \lesssim p_T \lesssim 4\) GeV (partonic processes).
- Approximately 30% of photons emitted within the intermediate \(p_T\) range \(5 \lesssim p_T \lesssim 8\) GeV stem from jet-medium interactions (Yazdi 2022).
- A Functional Renormalization Group study using mesonic degrees of freedom shows the \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) spectral functions overlap at \(T = 300\) MeV (Tripolt 2017).

## Context
This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of the QGP that includes calculations by Paquet (2015), Vujanovic (2013, 2017, 2019), and the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020). It builds on perturbative QCD calculations by Ghiglieri (2013, 2016), Laine (2013), Ghisoiu (2014), and Jackson (2019), as well as lattice QCD calculations by Brandt (2017, 2019). The paper suggests that a Bayesian analysis combining electromagnetic probes with soft hadronic observables and jet measurements could improve constraints on transport coefficients such as \(\eta/s(T)\) and \(\hat{q}\), and could also test chiral symmetry restoration via Bayesian model selection.
