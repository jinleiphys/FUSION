# Antisymmetry and channel coupling contributions to the absorption for p + alpha/d + 3He

> To understand recently established empirical p + alpha potentials, RGM calculations followed by inversion are made to study contributions of the d + 3He reaction channels and deuteron distortion effects to the p + alpha potential. An equivalent study of the d + 3He potential is also presented. The contributions of exchange non-locality to the absorption are simulated by including an phenomenological imaginary potential in the RGM. These effects alone strongly influence the shape of the imaginary potentials for both p + alpha and d + 3He. The potentials local-equivalent to the fully antisymmetrised-coupled channels calculations have a significant parity-dependence in both real and imaginary components, which for p + alpha is qualitatively similar to that found empirically. The effects on the potentials of the further inclusion of deuteron distortion are also presented. The inclusion of a spin-orbit term in the RGM, adds additional terms to the phase-equivalent potential, most notably the comparatively large imaginary spin-orbit term found empirically.

# Antisymmetry and channel coupling contributions to the absorption for p + alpha/d + 3He

## Key claim

The local-equivalent potentials from fully antisymmetrised coupled-channels RGM calculations show significant parity-dependence in both real and imaginary components, which for p + alpha is qualitatively similar to that found empirically; however, the paper does not attempt to precisely reproduce empirical potentials, but rather to establish general features arising from the non-locality of antisymmetry and channel coupling.

## Method

Resonating Group Model (RGM) calculations were performed for the 5-nucleon system, coupling the p + alpha and d + 3He channels, with deuteron distortion represented by up to four orthogonal pseudo-states derived from an 8-gaussian deuteron wavefunction. The resulting S-matrices were then inverted to local potentials using the Iterative Perturbative (IP) inversion code IMAGO, producing parity-dependent potentials of the form V(r) = V1(r) + (-1)^l V2(r). A phenomenological imaginary gaussian potential (W0 = 2 MeV, R0 = 1 fm, a = 2 fm) was inserted into single-channel RGM calculations to simulate exchange non-locality effects on absorption, and calculations were performed both with and without a nucleon-nucleon spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

- The d + 3He threshold in the RGM calculations is 19.0 MeV, slightly above the experimental value of 18.36 MeV (Selove)
- For p + alpha at 40 MeV laboratory energy, |S(l=2)| > 0.85, considerably greater than empirical values of ~0.65 and ~0.76 for j = 3/2 and j = 5/2 respectively (Plattner, Burzynski)
- The deuteron ground state energy used is -2.202 MeV, with excited pseudo-state energies of -0.036, 0.2008, 0.888, and 3.94 MeV
- The alpha and 3He internal wavefunctions use gaussian widths of 0.606 fm and 0.367 fm respectively, giving energy expectation values of -24.72 MeV and -3.51 MeV
- For d + 3He with channel spin s=1/2 at 21.67 MeV, the imaginary V2 component induced by exchange is about half the magnitude of the V1 component at r = 1 fm
- The direct real spin-orbit component from RGM is ~12 MeV at r = 1 fm for p + alpha, compared to ~1 MeV at r = 1 fm for d + 3He
- For d + 3He s=3/2, the spin-orbit V2 term from inversion is ~ -0.5 MeV at r = 1 fm
- The imaginary spin-orbit potential from full RGM calculations including channel coupling is at least ~1 MeV at maximum depth for p + alpha

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity-dependent N + alpha potentials established by IP inversion from empirical S-matrices (Cooper, prior work) and single-channel RGM calculations (Cooper, various years). It builds on microscopic RGM investigations of the 5-nucleon system by Reichstein, Chwieroth, and Kanada, and addresses the open question of whether fully antisymmetrised coupled-channels calculations can explain the imaginary parity-dependent components found empirically. The study enables comparison between RGM-derived potentials and empirical potentials from Burzynski, and provides d + 3He potentials established by accurate inversion for the first time, which may inform future reaction calculations involving these systems.
