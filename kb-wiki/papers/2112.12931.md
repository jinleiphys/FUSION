# Quark-quark interaction and quark matter in neutron stars

> Hyperon ($Y$) mixing in neutron-star matter brings about a remarkable softening of the equation of state (EoS) and the maximum mass is reduced to a value far less than $2M_{\odot}$. One idea to avoid this "hyperon puzzle in neutron stars" is to assume that the many-body repulsions work universally for every kind of baryons. The other is to take into account the quark deconfinement phase transitions from a hadronic EoS to a sufficiently stiff quark-matter EoS. In the present approach, both effects are handled in a common framework. As well as the hadronic matter, the quark matter with the two-body quark-quark interactions are treated within the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory beyond the mean field frameworks, where interaction parameters are based on the terrestrial data. The derived mass-radius relations of neutron stars show that maximum masses reach over $2M_{\odot}$ even in the cases of including hadron-quark phase transitions, being consistent with the recent observations for maximum masses and radii of neutron stars by the NICER measurements and the other multimessenger data.

## Key claim

The authors show that neutron stars containing both hyperonic hadronic matter and deconfined quark matter can reach maximum masses over 2 solar masses, provided the quark matter equation of state is stiffened by repulsive two-body quark-quark interactions derived from terrestrial data rather than from phenomenological parameters tuned to stiffen the EoS.

## Method

The hadronic matter EoS was derived using the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) theory with the ESC baryon-baryon interaction model including multi-pomeron exchange potentials (MPP) for universal many-body repulsions. The quark matter was treated within the same BBG framework using two-body quark-quark potentials derived from the ESC model via the constituent quark model, including extended meson-exchange, instanton, one-gluon-exchange, and multi-pomeron contributions. Density-dependent effective interactions parameterizing the G-matrix results were constructed, and hadron-quark phase transitions were modeled using a replacement interpolation method with a pressure shift parameter Delta_P = 0.07.

## Key numbers

- Observed neutron star masses used as constraints: J1614-2230 at (1.97 +/- 0.04) solar masses, J0348+0432 at (2.01 +/- 0.04) solar masses, and J0740+6620 at (2.17 +0.11 -0.10) solar masses
- NICER/XMM-Newton radius and mass for PSR J0740+6620: 12.39 +1.30 -0.98 km and 2.072 +0.067 -0.066 solar masses (Riley 2021) or 13.7 +2.6 -1.5 km and 2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses (Miller 2021)
- Radius at 1.4 solar masses from hadronic EoSs: 12.4 km (H1), 13.3 km (H2), and 13.6 km (H3)
- Quark-gluon coupling constant alpha_S fixed at 0.25; gluon mass taken as 420 MeV
- Instanton potential parameters: G_I = 2.5 GeV^{-2} and Lambda_I = 0.55 GeV
- Constituent quark masses at zero density: 305 MeV for u/d quarks and 500 MeV for s quarks
- Critical density parameter rho_c chosen as 6 rho_0 for density-dependent quark masses
- Pressure shift parameter Delta_P = 0.07 used for phase transition interpolation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of hyperonic matter with multi-pomeron exchange potentials (Yamamoto 2014, 2016; Yamamoto 2017) and the ESC baryon-baryon interaction model (Nagels 2016). It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon mixing softens the EoS below 2 solar masses, a problem also tackled by other groups using quark deconfinement transitions (Schaffner-Bielich 1999; Baldo 2006; Lastowiecki 2012; Maslov 2019; Kojo 2015; Baym 2018). Unlike mean-field approaches such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that treat quark phases phenomenologically, this work provides a unified BBG treatment of both hadronic and quark phases with interactions based on terrestrial data. The results enable future comparisons with NICER radius measurements and multimessenger constraints on neutron star structure.
