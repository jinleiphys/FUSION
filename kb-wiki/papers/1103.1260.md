# Highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics with transverse expansion

> A recently formulated framework of highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics (ADHYDRO) is used to describe the evolution of matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. New developments of the model contain: the inclusion of asymmetric transverse expansion (combined with the longitudinal boost-invariant flow) and comparisons of the model results with the RHIC data, which have become possible after coupling of ADHYDRO with THERMINATOR. Various soft-hadronic observables (the transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic flow coefficient v_2, and the HBT radii) are calculated for different initial conditions characterized by the value of the initial pressure asymmetry. We find that as long as the initial energy density profile is unchanged the calculated observables remain practically the same. This result indicates the insensitivity of the analyzed observables to the initial anisotropy of pressure and suggests that the complete thermalization of the system may be delayed to easily acceptable times of about 1 fm/c.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that soft-hadronic observables (transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow coefficient v_2, and HBT radii) calculated with the ADHYDRO framework are practically insensitive to the initial pressure anisotropy, as long as the initial energy density profile is unchanged. This suggests that complete thermalization of the system may be delayed to times of about 1 fm/c, rather than requiring the sub-1 fm/c thermalization times typically assumed in perfect-fluid hydrodynamics.

## Method
The authors extended their previously formulated ADHYDRO framework to include asymmetric transverse expansion combined with longitudinal boost-invariant flow, and coupled it with the THERMINATOR event generator to compute hadronic observables. They solved the energy-momentum conservation equations and entropy production equations for a fluid with distinct transverse and longitudinal pressures, using a generalized equation of state that interpolates between an anisotropic initial state (parameterized by an anisotropy parameter x) and the equilibrium equation of state. The entropy source term was taken as Σ = (1-√x)²/√x · σ/τ_eq with τ_eq = 0.25 fm, and calculations were performed for three different initial anisotropy values (x₀=100, x₀=1, x₀=0.032) while keeping the initial energy density profile fixed.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.25 fm used for all calculations
- Initial energy density at center for most central collisions: εᵢ = 86.76 GeV/fm³ (corresponding to equilibrium temperature 485 MeV)
- Initial entropy density at center: σᵢ = 227.6 fm⁻³ for x₀=1, and σᵢ = 151.7 fm⁻³ for x₀=100 and x₀=0.032
- Equilibration timescale parameter τ_eq = 0.25 fm (corresponding to collision timescale τ_eq^coll ≈ 0.03 fm)
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV used for hypersurface determination
- Centrality class c = 0-5% with impact parameter b = 2.26 fm
- Mixing parameter α = 0.14 for wounded-nucleon and binary collision density combination

## Context
This work extends the ADHYDRO framework previously developed by Florkowski and Ryblewski (2010) to include transverse expansion, and builds upon earlier attempts to describe RHIC data without assuming very fast thermalization, such as the free-streaming-plus-perfect-fluid model of Broniowski et al. (2008) and the transverse-hydrodynamics model of Ryblewski et al. (2010). The results support the idea of universal flow formation in heavy-ion collisions as pointed out by Vredevoogd and Pratt (2008-2009), and enable future studies of directed flow and other observables that may be sensitive to early pressure anisotropy, requiring extension of the ADHYDRO code to 3+1 dimensions.
