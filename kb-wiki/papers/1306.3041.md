# Strangeness magnetic form factor of the proton in the extended chiral quark model

> Background: Unravelling the role played by nonvalence flavors in baryons is crucial in deepening our comprehension of QCD. Strange quark, a component of the higher Fock states in baryons, is an appropriate tool to investigate nonperturbative mechanisms generated by the pure sea quark. Purpose: Study the magnitude and the sign of the strangeness magnetic moment $μ_s$ and the magnetic form factor ($G_M^s$) of the proton. Methods: Within an extended chiral constituent quark model, we investigate contributions from all possible five-quark components to $μ_s$ and $G_M^s (Q^2)$ in the four-vector momentum range $Q^2 \leq 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$. Probability of the strangeness component in the proton wave function is calculated employing the $^3 P_0$ model. Results: Predictions are obtained without any adjustable parameters. Observables $μ_s$ and $G_M^s (Q^2)$ are found to be small and negative, consistent with the lattice-QCD findings as well as with the latest data released by the PVA4 and HAPPEX Collaborations. Conclusions: Due to sizeable cancelations among different configurations contributing to the strangeness magnetic moment of the proton, it is indispensable to (i) take into account all relevant five-quark components and include both diagonal and non-diagonal terms, (ii) handle with care the oscillator harmonic parameter $ω_5$ and the ${s \bar s}$ component probability.

## Key claim

The strangeness magnetic moment of the proton is predicted to be small and negative, but the exact value is highly sensitive to the ratio of harmonic oscillator parameters for the three- and five-quark components (R = ω₅/ω₃), with results differing by more than 20σ between the two parameter sets considered.

## Method

The authors employed an extended chiral constituent quark model (EχCQM) including all possible five-quark Fock components with strangeness in the proton wave function. They calculated both diagonal matrix elements (within five-quark components) and non-diagonal transition matrix elements (between three-quark and five-quark components) for the strangeness magnetic moment and form factor, using the ³P₀ model to determine the probability amplitudes of the strangeness components without adjustable parameters. The calculations were performed for four-vector momentum transfer Q² ≤ 1 (GeV/c)², with input parameters taken consistently from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness magnetic moment of the proton μ_s = −0.149 ± 0.004 μ_N for Set I (ω₃ = 246 MeV, ω₅ ≈ 225 MeV)
- Strangeness magnetic moment of the proton μ_s = −0.067 ± 0.004 μ_N for Set II (ω₃ = 340 MeV, ω₅ = 600 MeV)
- Total probability of strangeness components in the proton P_{s\bar{s}} = 5.7 ± 0.6% (for V = 570 ± 46 MeV)
- Ratio of probabilities for five-quark components with strange versus light quark-antiquark pairs: P_{s\bar{s}}/(P_{u\bar{u}}+P_{d\bar{d}}) ≈ 0.18
- Diagonal contributions sum to μ_s^D = −0.0413 μ_N (identical for both Sets)
- Non-diagonal contributions sum to μ_s^ND = −0.1082 μ_N (Set I) and −0.0258 μ_N (Set II)
- The PVA4 Collaboration result cited: G_M^s(Q²=0.22) = −0.14 ± 0.11 ± 0.11 μ_N at Q² = 0.22 (GeV/c)²
- The HAPPEX Collaboration result cited: G_M^s(Q²=0.624) = −0.070 ± 0.067 μ_N at Q² = 0.624 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zou and Riska (2005), who first proposed explaining the proton strangeness magnetic moment via five-quark Fock components but included only diagonal matrix elements, and of An (2006), who added non-diagonal terms but treated probability amplitudes as free parameters and considered only the lowest strangeness component. The present study improves upon these by calculating all probability amplitudes within the ³P₀ model, including all relevant five-quark configurations with both diagonal and non-diagonal terms, and properly removing center-of-mass motion contributions as emphasized by Kiswandhi (2011). The predictions are consistent with negative-sign results from lattice-QCD approaches (Leinweber, 1999; Leinweber, 2004; Doi, 2009; Babich, 2010) and with the latest data from the PVA4 Collaboration (Baunack, 2009) and HAPPEX Collaboration (Ahmed, 2011), which both found negative values for G_M^s.
