# Neutrino absorption in two-flavor color-superconducting quark matter

> We calculate the absorption mean free paths of electron and muon neutrinos in two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter in the density and temperature range that is relevant to binary neutron star mergers. We model the strong interaction between quarks using a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, performing calculations self-consistently in the mean-field approximation. Since the 2SC gap is large we restrict our analysis to the contribution of unpaired quarks. We find that at low temperatures absorption by a down quark $ν+d \to u+e^-/μ^-$ is kinematically not allowed, so absorption by a strange quark $ν+s \to u+e^-/μ^-$ dominates the mean free path. As temperature or neutrino energy rises, the $d$ quark absorption channel becomes active, and the mean free path shrinks. We find that in equilibrated 2SC matter with an electron lepton fraction $Y_{L_e}=0.1$, the neutrinos form a degenerate gas with a mean free path of meters or less, independent of the temperature.

## Key claim
In equilibrated two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter with an electron lepton fraction \(Y_{L_e}=0.1\), neutrinos form a degenerate gas with a mean free path of meters or less, independent of temperature; this conclusion relies on the restriction to unpaired quarks only, justified by the large 2SC gap (estimated at 100-300 MeV) which suppresses pair-breaking contributions at temperatures below several tens of MeV.

## Method
The authors model strong interactions using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian with scalar, diquark, and vector couplings, solved self-consistently in the mean-field approximation with a sharp three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda' = 602.3\) MeV. They calculate the absorption mean free path for electron and muon neutrinos via the processes \(\nu_l + d_b \to u_b + l^-\) and \(\nu_l + s_b \to u_b + l^-\) (where only blue, unpaired quarks are considered) using Fermi's golden rule with a simplified matrix element, integrating over phase space with Fermi-Dirac distributions and Pauli blocking factors. The quark dispersion relations include a vector-interaction energy shift proportional to baryon density, and chemical potentials are fixed by beta equilibrium, electric charge neutrality, and color neutrality.

## Key numbers
- The 2SC pairing gap \(\Delta_{ud}\) is estimated to be in the 100-300 MeV range (from prior work cited in the paper).
- The NJL model cutoff is \(\Lambda' = 602.3\) MeV; the diquark coupling is fixed at \(G_D = 1.49 G_S\) and the vector coupling at \(G_V = 0.77 G_S\).
- The Cabibbo angle is \(\theta_c = 13.02^\circ\), giving \(\cos(\theta_c)\) and \(\sin(\theta_c)\) for the matrix elements of \(d\)-quark and \(s\)-quark capture, respectively.
- The Fermi constant is \(G_F = 1.16637 \times 10^{-11} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\).
- The strange quark constituent mass is \(m_s = 140.7\) MeV (bare mass); the up and down bare masses are \(m_{u,d} = 5.5\) MeV.
- The momentum deficit for \(d\)-quark capture at zero temperature is given by \(p_F^d - p_F^u - p_F^e \approx -\mu_Q M^2 / (2 \mu_u \mu_d)\), which is always positive in neutral matter (since \(\mu_Q < 0\)).
- The effective threshold density for \(d\)-quark capture is estimated by comparing the momentum deficit to \(\Delta Q_{\text{th}} = T / v_F^{ub} + 3T\), where \(v_F^{ub}\) is the Fermi velocity of the blue up quark (close to 1).
- Strange quarks appear at baryon densities \(n_B \gtrsim 3.2 n_{\text{sat}}\) (where \(n_{\text{sat}} = 0.16 \text{ fm}^{-3}\)).
- The electron fraction \(Y_e\) has a maximum around 0.08 in the 2SC phase at zero temperature.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of neutrino interactions in color-superconducting quark matter, which have focused on neutrino emission and neutron star cooling (Blaschke 1999, Jaikumar 2001, 2002, 2005, Alford 2004, Grigorian 2004, Schmitt 2005, Anglani 2006, Negreiros 2012, Berdermann 2016), bulk viscosity (Alford 2006, Wang 2010, Berdermann 2016, Alford 2024, 2025), and neutrino mean free paths (Carter 2000, Reddy 2002, Kundu 2004). Unlike earlier work using bag models with free or perturbatively interacting quarks (Steiner 2001, Iwamoto 1982, Gupta 1995, Colvero 2014), the present calculation uses a self-consistent NJL model that yields a small momentum deficit for \(d\)-quark capture, keeping that channel kinematically blocked at low temperatures until thermal effects open it. The results enable more realistic neutrino transport simulations in binary neutron star mergers, where the 2SC phase may be present at densities up to about \(4 n_{\text{sat}}\) and temperatures up to 50 MeV.
