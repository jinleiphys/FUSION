# Superscaling of Inclusive Electron Scattering from Nuclei

> We investigate the degree to which the concept of superscaling, initially developed within the framework of the relativistic Fermi gas model, applies to inclusive electron scattering from nuclei. We find that data obtained from the low energy loss side of the quasielastic peak exhibit the superscaling property, i.e., the scaling functions f(ψ') are not only independent of momentum transfer (the usual type of scaling: scaling of the first kind), but coincide for A \geq 4 when plotted versus a dimensionless scaling variable ψ' (scaling of the second kind). We use this behavior to study as yet poorly understood properties of the inclusive response at large electron energy loss.

# Superscaling of Inclusive Electron Scattering from Nuclei

## Key claim

Inclusive electron scattering data from nuclei with A ≥ 4 exhibit superscaling, meaning the scaling functions f(ψ') are simultaneously independent of momentum transfer (scaling of the first kind) and independent of nuclear mass number (scaling of the second kind), but only on the low energy loss side of the quasielastic peak (ψ' < 0), while the property is badly violated for ψ' > 0 where other processes such as meson-exchange currents and Δ-excitation contribute.

## Method

The authors analyzed a large body of existing inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data for nuclei from A = 4 to 208, defining a dimensionless scaling variable ψ' based on the relativistic Fermi gas model with empirically chosen Fermi momenta (200–240 MeV/c depending on nucleus) and an energy shift of 15–25 MeV to account for binding and final-state interactions. The scaling function f(ψ') was constructed by dividing the measured cross section by the elementary electron-nucleon cross section and multiplying by the Fermi momentum k_F, then tested for independence of both momentum transfer and nuclear mass number.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momenta used: 220 MeV/c for carbon, 230 MeV/c for aluminum, 235 MeV/c for iron, 240 MeV/c for gold, and 200 MeV/c for helium-4
- Energy shift values: 15–25 MeV for nuclei from carbon to gold; 15 MeV for helium-4
- For helium-4 at ψ' = 0, the scaling function f(ψ') is about 15% higher than for heavier nuclei
- Data analyzed at fixed kinematics include electron energy 3.6 GeV at 16° scattering angle (q ≈ 1000 MeV/c) and 500 MeV at 60° (q ≈ 460 MeV/c)
- The TJNAF experiment extended measurements to cross sections a hundred times smaller than previously accessible, reaching ψ' values more negative than −2

## Context

This work extends the concept of superscaling introduced by Alberico et al. (1988) within the relativistic Fermi gas model to actual experimental data, building on decades of y-scaling studies reviewed by Day et al. (1990). The authors find that scaling of the second kind (A-independence) works extremely well for ψ' < 0, including at large negative ψ' values corresponding to high nucleon momenta, which they interpret using the local density approximation and the universal tail of nuclear momentum distributions from short-range correlations as studied by Baldo et al. (1990) and Benhar et al. (1994). The results enable systematic study of the poorly understood longitudinal-transverse response differences and the "dip" region between the quasielastic and Δ peaks, and provide a foundation for using superscaling to extract nuclear spectral function information from inclusive data.
