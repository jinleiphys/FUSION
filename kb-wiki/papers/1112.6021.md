# Dihadron momentum imbalance and correlations in d+Au collisions

> We calculate in perturbative QCD the transverse momentum imbalance of dijet and dihadron production in high energy p+A (d+A) collisions. We evaluate the effect of both initial- and final-state multiple scattering, which determines the strength of this transverse momentum imbalance. Combining this new result with the suppression of the cross section in d+Au collisions, which arises from cold nuclear matter energy loss and coherent power corrections, we are able to describe the dihadron correlations measured by both PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC, including mid-mid, mid-forward, and forward-forward rapidity hadron pairs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear-induced increase in transverse momentum imbalance of dihadron pairs, calculated perturbatively from both initial- and final-state multiple scattering, combined with cold nuclear matter energy loss and coherent power corrections, can describe the dihadron azimuthal correlation data from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC, but the agreement requires the parton scattering parameter ξ² to be constrained by deep inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse momentum imbalance increase Δ⟨q²⊥⟩ for dijet and dihadron production in p+A (d+A) collisions using a high-twist perturbative QCD formalism, evaluating contributions from both initial-state and final-state double scattering Feynman diagrams for all partonic channels (qq'→qq', qg→qg, gg→gg, etc.). They then relate this imbalance to the width σF of the away-side peak in dihadron azimuthal correlations and combine it with the nuclear modification factor R^(2)_dA, which includes dynamical shadowing via momentum fraction shifts and initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss through medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic scale of high-twist corrections per nucleon is ξ² = 0.09–0.12 GeV², extracted from deep inelastic scattering data (Qiu 2003)
- The transverse momentum imbalance increase Δ⟨q²⊥⟩ is on the order of 2–3 GeV² for the kinematics studied
- The vacuum fragmentation parameter ⟨|j⊥y|⟩ ≈ 400 MeV is used, independent of center-of-mass energy and trigger momentum
- The p+p baseline transverse momentum imbalance is ⟨q²⊥⟩_pp ≈ 3.3 GeV², extracted from PHENIX p+p data (Rak 2003)
- The nuclear enhancement factor includes A^(1/3) dependence from the nuclear size

## Context

This work extends the high-twist formalism of Qiu (2004) and Kang (2008) for multiple parton scattering in nuclear collisions, and the dynamical shadowing calculations of Qiu and Vitev (2004, 2006) that resum coherent power corrections. The cold nuclear matter energy loss treatment builds upon the medium-induced radiative corrections derived by Vitev (2007) and earlier phenomenology by Vogt (1999), Gavin (1991), and Arleo (2002). The results enable quantitative separation of cold nuclear matter effects from hot and dense medium effects in heavy ion collisions, and provide a framework for interpreting forward rapidity measurements that probe small-x parton distributions in nuclei.
