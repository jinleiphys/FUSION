# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors from lattice QCD using a nearly physical pion mass

> We present lattice QCD calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors using pion masses $m_π$ = 149, 202, and 254 MeV and an action with clover-improved Wilson quarks coupled to smeared gauge fields, as used by the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration. Particular attention is given to removal of the effects of excited state contamination by calculation at three source-sink separations and use of the summation and generalized pencil-of-function methods. The combination of calculation at the nearly physical mass $m_π$ = 149 MeV in a large spatial volume ($m_πL_s$ = 4.2) and removal of excited state effects yields agreement with experiment for the electric and magnetic form factors $G_E(Q^2)$ and $G_M(Q^2)$ up to $Q^2$ = 0.5 GeV$^2$.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors at a nearly physical pion mass of 149 MeV, combined with systematic removal of excited-state contamination using multiple source-sink separations and the summation method, yield agreement with experimental electric and magnetic Sachs form factors up to momentum transfer Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2, though the authors note that confirmation at the physical pion mass is still required for fully-controlled systematic errors.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using clover-improved Wilson quarks coupled to smeared gauge fields (the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal action) at three pion masses: 149, 202, and 254 MeV. They computed nucleon three-point functions at three source-sink separations and applied three different analysis methods (ratio, summation, and generalized pencil-of-function) to extract ground-state matrix elements, using the summation method as their primary analysis. Form factors were obtained by parameterizing the vector current matrix elements in terms of Dirac (F1) and Pauli (F2) form factors and performing linear fits to overdetermined systems of equations.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: m_pi = 149, 202, and 254 MeV
- Spatial volume condition for the near-physical ensemble: m_pi * L_s = 4.2
- Momentum transfer range where agreement with experiment is achieved: up to Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2
- Isovector Dirac radius at m_pi = 149 MeV: (r1^2)^v = 0.498(55) fm^2 (summation method)
- Isovector anomalous magnetic moment at m_pi = 149 MeV: kappa^v = 3.76(38) (summation method)
- Isovector Pauli radius combination at m_pi = 149 MeV: kappa^v(r2^2)^v = 2.68(62) fm^2 (summation method)
- Isoscalar Dirac radius at m_pi = 149 MeV: (r1^2)^s = 0.581(36) fm^2 (summation method)
- Isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment at m_pi = 149 MeV: kappa^s = -0.10(11) (summation method)
- Chiral perturbation theory inputs: pion decay constant F_pi = 86.2 MeV, delta-nucleon mass splitting Delta = 293 MeV, nucleon axial charge g_A = 1.26

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors by Green (2012) and Bratt (2010) by achieving a nearly physical pion mass of 149 MeV and systematically studying excited-state contamination through comparison of ratio, summation, and generalized pencil-of-function methods. The results enable future ab initio calculations of the proton charge radius at very low momentum transfer to help resolve the discrepancy between experimental results from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Antognini 2013) and dispersion-analysis fits (Lorenz 2012), while also demonstrating that disconnected diagram contributions to isoscalar form factors appear small at the current level of precision.
