# $ρ$ mesons in finite magnetic field and finite temperature

> The mass spectra of $ρ$ mesons ($ρ_{Q=\pm 1}^{s_z=0,\pm 1}$ and $ρ_{Q=0}^{s_z=0,\pm 1}$) at finite magnetic field and temperature are studied in frame of the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Fully considering the breaking of translational invariance induced by external magnetic field, the analytical form of $ρ$ meson propagators have been derived in the Ritus scheme and Schwinger scheme, which gives the same algebraic formula. When solving the pole equation of $ρ$ meson propagators, multiple solutions of the meson mass appear due to the dimension reduction of their constituent quarks in magnetic fields. At vanishing temperature, we focus on the $ρ$ meson masses $M_ρ$ corresponding to the lowest value solution of the pole equation. $M_{ρ^{-}_+}$, $M_{ρ^{0}_+}$ and $M_{ρ^{\pm}_0}$ increase with magnetic field. $M_{ρ^{+}_+}$ firstly decreases and then becomes saturated with increasing magnetic field. $M_{ρ^0_0}$ is not sensitive to magnetic field. These results are consistent with the available LQCD simulations. At finite temperature, we discuss the lowest four/five solutions of $ρ$ meson masses $M^{i=0,1,2,3,4}_ρ$. With fixed magnetic field, they decrease with temperature, and approach the mass sum of their constituent quarks at high temperature. The mass solution $M^{i}_ρ$ for different mesons $ρ_+^{0,\pm}$ and $ρ_0^{0,\pm}$ may become degenerate at finite magnetic field and temperature.

## Key claim

The mass spectra of charged and neutral ρ mesons in a magnetic field exhibit multiple solutions due to the dimension reduction of their constituent quarks, and the lowest-mass solutions at zero temperature are consistent with available lattice QCD simulations, but the behavior of the ρ⁺₊ mass at strong magnetic field depends on the regularization scheme used in the NJL model.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with random phase approximation to compute ρ meson propagators and polarization functions in a constant external magnetic field. They derive the analytical form of the propagators using both the Ritus scheme and the Schwinger scheme for quark propagators, proving that both schemes yield the same algebraic formula. The meson masses are obtained by solving the pole equation of the propagator at vanishing momentum, with the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme used to handle divergences.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum parameters: current quark mass m₀ = 6.28 MeV, scalar coupling G_S = 8.27 GeV⁻², vector coupling G_V = 3.87 GeV⁻², Pauli-Villars mass parameter Λ = 891 MeV, fitted to chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = -(230 MeV)³, m_π = 134 MeV, m_ρ = 775 MeV, and f_π = 93 MeV.
- At T=0 and eB up to 50 m_π²: M_{ρ⁻₊} and M_{ρ⁰₊} increase with magnetic field; M_{ρ⁺₊} first decreases then saturates at strong field; M_{ρ⁰₀} is not sensitive to magnetic field, slightly decreasing then slightly increasing.
- For free point particle approximation at lowest Landau level: effective masses are M_{ρ⁻₊} = √(m_ρ² + 3|eB|), M_{ρ⁰₊} = √(m_ρ² + |eB|), M_{ρ⁺₊} = √(m_ρ² - |eB|).
- At finite temperature with eB = 10 m_π², the lowest four/five mass solutions decrease with temperature and approach the mass sum of their constituent quarks at high temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of ρ mesons in magnetic fields (Ghosh 2020, Avancini 2022, Carlomagno 2022, Gomez 2023) by providing a systematic derivation of ρ meson propagators in both Ritus and Schwinger schemes and proving their equivalence. The results at zero temperature are compared with lattice QCD simulations (Luschevskaya 2017, Bali 2018) and show consistency for most channels, though the behavior of M_{ρ⁺₊} at strong magnetic field differs from the magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR) scheme results. The multiple pole solutions observed here are similar to those found for π, K, η, η', and φ mesons in magnetic fields (Sheng 2021, Sheng 2022, Li 2025, Mei 2022, Mao 2017, Mao 2019, Mei 2026).
