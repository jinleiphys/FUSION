# The effect of inclusion of $Δ$ resonances in relativistic mean-field model with scaled hadron masses and coupling constants

> Knowledge of the equation of state of the baryon matter plays a decisive role in the description of neutron stars. With an increase of the baryon density the filling of Fermi seas of hyperons and $Δ$ isobars becomes possible. Their inclusion into standard relativistic mean-field models results in a strong softening of the equation of state and a lowering of the maximum neutron star mass below the measured values. We extend a relativistic mean-field model with scaled hadron masses and coupling constants developed in our previous works and take into account now not only hyperons but also the $Δ$ isobars. We analyze available empirical information to put constraints on coupling constants of $Δ$s to mesonic mean fields. We show that the resulting equation of state satisfies majority of presently known experimental constraints.

# The effect of inclusion of Δ resonances in relativistic mean-field model with scaled hadron masses and coupling constants

## Key claim
The MKVOR* model, which includes both hyperons and Δ isobars with scaled hadron masses and coupling constants, can satisfy the maximum neutron star mass constraint (2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙) and the flow constraint simultaneously, provided the Δ potential at saturation density is chosen in the range −100 MeV < UΔ < −50 MeV. The abstract glosses over the fact that the model requires a sharp decrease in the ηω scaling function at f > 0.95 to prevent the nucleon effective mass from vanishing when Δs are included.

## Method
The authors extend their previous relativistic mean-field (RMF) model MKVOR by adding Δ isobars to the baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons), using scaling functions for hadron masses and meson coupling constants that depend on the scalar field f. The Δ coupling constants with vector mesons are fixed via SU(6) symmetry relations, while the Δ potential at saturation density UΔ is varied between −50 MeV and −100 MeV to explore the range of possible behavior. The equation of state is computed for isospin-symmetrical matter (ISM) and beta-equilibrium matter (BEM), and neutron star masses and radii are obtained by integrating the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy at saturation: ℰ0 = −16 MeV
- Incompressibility: K = 250 MeV
- Symmetry energy: J̃ = 32 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: mN*(n0) = 0.73 mN
- Hyperon potentials at saturation: UΛ = −28 MeV, UΣ = 30 MeV, UΞ = −15 MeV
- Δ appearance threshold in BEM: n = 2.51 n0 for UΔ = −50 MeV; n = 1.74 n0 for UΔ = −100 MeV
- Maximum Δ fraction in MKVOR*Δ model: 0.23 for UΔ = −50 MeV; 0.17 for UΔ = −100 MeV
- Maximum decrease of NS mass at given central density in MKVOR*HΔφ model: less than 0.02 M⊙ for UΔ = −50 MeV; does not exceed 0.2 M⊙ for UΔ = −100 MeV
- Decrease of maximum NS mass: less than 0.05 M⊙ for UΔ = −100 MeV
- Change of NS radius for given mass: not more than 0.5 km

## Context
This work extends the MKVOR model of Maslov (2015) and Maslov (2015), which solved the hyperon puzzle (the softening of the equation of state by hyperons that lowers the maximum neutron star mass below observed values) but did not include Δ isobars. The inclusion of Δs had previously been shown by Drago (2014) to cause a further decrease of the maximum NS mass, creating a "Δ puzzle." The present paper demonstrates that within the scaled-mass RMF framework, the Δ puzzle is resolved alongside the hyperon puzzle, and the constraint on the gravitational-to-baryon mass relation for the pulsar J0737-3039(B) from Podsiadlowski is better satisfied when Δs are included with UΔ = −100 MeV.
