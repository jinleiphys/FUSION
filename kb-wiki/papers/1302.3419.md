# Scattering Theory with Path Integrals

> Starting from well-known expressions for the $T$-matrix and its derivative in standard nonrelativistic potential scattering I rederive recent path-integral formulations due to Efimov and Barbashov et al. Some new relations follow immediately.

## Key claim

Starting from standard nonrelativistic scattering theory expressions for the T-matrix and its derivative, the author rederives recent path-integral formulations due to Efimov and Barbashov et al. without needing the "anti-velocity" degrees of freedom or Faddeev-Popov tricks required in earlier path-integral approaches, though the derivation relies on controlling infinite-time limits by adiabatically switching off the interaction.

## Method

The author begins with the standard T-matrix expressions T_{i→f} = ⟨ψ_f^{(-)}|V|φ_i⟩ and T_{i→f} = ⟨φ_f|V|ψ_i^{(+)}⟩ from textbook scattering theory, inserts the Møller operator representation of the scattering states, and then substitutes the path-integral representation of the time-evolution operator. The large-time limit is evaluated by the stationary phase method, yielding path-integral forms for the T-matrix that emphasize either the final or initial momentum. A symmetric form is obtained by starting from the derivative of the T-matrix with respect to a potential parameter, ∂T_{i→f}/∂λ = ⟨ψ_f^{(-)}|∂V_λ/∂λ|ψ_i^{(+)}⟩, and inserting two time-evolution operators in the interaction picture, which after path-integral representation and composition law manipulation gives a velocity path integral with the particle traveling along the ray formed by initial and final momenta.

## Key numbers

- The stationary phase evaluation gives the stationary point u_stat = k_f/m for the final-state form and u_stat = k_i/m for the initial-state form, where k_i and k_f are the initial and final momenta.
- The symmetric (Barbashov) form involves a time integral from -∞ to +∞ and a coupling-constant integration ∫₀¹ dλ.
- The scattering virial theorem derivative ∂/∂k (k T_{i→f}) involves the combination [2V(x) + x·∇V(x)].
- The impact-parameter-like representation Eq. (PI for T) contains the factor K = k cos(Θ/2) in front of the impact-parameter integral, where Θ is the scattering angle.

## Context

This work extends the path-integral approach to scattering problems pioneered by Campbell, Flessas, and Jannussis (CFJM) and developed further by Rosenfelder (Rose1), Carlson and Rosenfelder (CaRo2), and Efimov (Efim). It provides a derivation from standard scattering theory (Messiah, Rodberg-Thaler, Goldberger-Watson, Taylor, Newton) that connects to the field-theoretic technique of Barbashov et al. (Barb_etal) and yields new representations including a Feynman path-integral form symmetric in initial and final states. The results enable further analytical or numerical studies of scattering using path integrals, though the author notes that finding a practical path-integral expression for the impact-parameter representation of the scattering amplitude remains an open problem.
