# Exploring Nucleon-Nucleon correlations in ($e,e'NN$) reactions

> Correlations in the nuclear wave-function beyond the mean-field or Hartree-Fock approximation are very important to describe basic properties of nuclear structure. Attempts are made to explore details of these correlations in exclusive nucleon knock-out by electron scattering experiments. Basic results of $(e,e'p)$ experiments are reviewed. The role of correlations in $(e,e'NN)$ experiments is discussed. Special attention is paid to a consistent description of the competing effects due to final state interaction, meson exchange current and isobar currents. Results are discussed for systematic studies of these features in nuclear matter as well as for specific examples for the finite nucleus $^{16}$O.

# Exploring Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations in (e,e'NN) Reactions

## Key claim
Exclusive (e,e'NN) reactions are sensitive to nucleon-nucleon correlations beyond the mean-field approximation, but extracting clean correlation signals requires a consistent treatment of competing effects from final state interaction (FSI), meson exchange currents (MEC), and isobar currents (IC), which the abstract notes but does not quantify as potentially dominating the correlation signal in certain kinematics.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical calculations for nuclear matter and the finite nucleus 16O, comparing Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and coupled-cluster (exp(S)) methods to compute correlated two-body wave functions. For nuclear matter, longitudinal structure functions for (e,e'pp) and (e,e'pn) are calculated using the Bonn A potential, with correlations described by the Brueckner G-matrix, FSI by the T-matrix from the same interaction, and MEC/IC contributions from pion exchange. For 16O, cross sections for exclusive (e,e'pn) reactions to specific final states of 14N are computed using correlated two-hole overlap integrals from the coupled-cluster method with the Argonne V14 potential, combined with distorted wave functions from a phenomenological optical potential.

## Key numbers
- Hartree-Fock energy per nucleon for nuclear matter at saturation density: positive values ranging from 4.64 MeV (CDB potential) to 176.20 MeV (Reid potential), demonstrating that mean-field alone cannot bind nuclear matter.
- Correlated (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) energy per nucleon: ranges from -17.11 MeV (CDB) to -12.47 MeV (Reid), in rough agreement with the empirical value of -16 MeV per nucleon.
- Kinetic energy per nucleon T in nuclear matter: ranges from 36.23 MeV (CDB) to 49.04 MeV (Reid), showing that different phase-shift-equivalent interactions predict different correlation strengths.
- For super-parallel kinematics in nuclear matter (T_p1=156 MeV, T_p2=33 MeV, omega=215 MeV), the longitudinal structure function for (e,e'pn) is almost an order of magnitude larger than for (e,e'pp).
- For 16O(e,e'pn)14N at E0=855 MeV, omega=215 MeV, q=316 MeV/c, the total cross section is about an order of magnitude larger than the corresponding (e,e'pp) cross section.
- The 1+_1 ground state of 14N has a two-nucleon separation energy E_2m = 22.96 MeV; the 1+_2 excited state has E_2m = 26.91 MeV.

## Context
This paper extends the discussion of nuclear correlations from (e,e'p) reactions, which the author shows are primarily sensitive to mean-field properties and spectroscopic factors rather than short-range correlations, as demonstrated by the MAMI 16O(e,e'p)15N data compared with calculations by Nili and collaborators. The work builds on prior Brueckner-Hartree-Fock studies of nuclear matter and the coupled-cluster method of Kummel and collaborators, and provides a framework for interpreting upcoming (e,e'NN) experiments at MAMI. The results enable systematic separation of correlation, FSI, MEC, and IC contributions by exploiting different kinematics (super-parallel vs. symmetric) and different final states in the residual nucleus.
