# Towards the quark mass dependence of $T_{cc}^+$ from lattice QCD

> The $DD^*$ scattering phase shifts in the $T_{cc}^+=cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ channel are extracted from lattice QCD for five different charm quark masses and a fixed light-quark mass corresponding to $m_π\simeq 280$~MeV. The phase shifts are analysed employing two approaches: effective range expansion and Lippmann--Schwinger equation derived in the effective field theory. In the latter case, the results imply an attraction at short range parametrised by contact terms and a slight repulsion at long range mediated by one-pion exchange with $m_π>m_{D^*}-m_D$. The poles in the amplitude across the complex energy plane are extracted and their trajectories are discussed as the charm quark mass is varied. Two complex conjugate poles corresponding to a resonance below threshold are found for $m_c$ close to the physical value. They turn into a pair of virtual states at the largest $m_c$ studied. With further increasing $m_c$, one virtual pole representing $T_{cc}^+$ is expected to move towards the two-body threshold and turn into a bound state. The light-quark mass dependence of the $T_{cc}^+$ pole is briefly discussed using the data on $DD^*$ scattering from other lattice collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper extracts $DD^*$ scattering phase shifts in the $T_{cc}^+$ channel from lattice QCD for five different charm quark masses at a fixed light-quark mass ($m_\pi \simeq 280$ MeV), and finds that the pole representing $T_{cc}^+$ transitions from a pair of complex conjugate poles (a resonance below threshold) at near-physical charm mass to a pair of virtual states at the largest charm mass studied, with the expectation that further increasing the charm mass would produce a bound state.

## Method

The $DD^*$ scattering phase shifts were extracted from lattice QCD eigen-energies using Luescher's formalism on two $N_f=2+1$ CLS ensembles with $a=0.08636(98)(40)$ fm and $m_\pi=280(3)$ MeV, for five values of the charm quark hopping parameter $\kappa_c$ corresponding to $D$-meson masses from 1.762 to 2.415 GeV. The phase shifts were analyzed using two approaches: effective range expansion (ERE) truncated to scattering length $a_0$ and effective range $r_0$, and a Lippmann-Schwinger equation derived from effective field theory that incorporates one-pion exchange and short-range contact terms up to ${\cal O}(p^2)$, with the contact coefficients determined by fitting to the lattice data.

## Key numbers

- Five charm quark masses studied, corresponding to $D$-meson masses: 1.762(1) GeV, 1.927(1) GeV, 2.064(2) GeV, 2.191(2) GeV, and 2.415(2) GeV
- $DD^*$ threshold energies $\Eth = m_D + m_{D^*}$ range from 3.660(3) GeV to 4.922(3) GeV across the five sets
- From ERE: scattering lengths $a_0$ range from 1.3($^{+1.0}_{-0.6}$) fm to 3.2($^{~~\infty}_{-2.6}$) fm; effective ranges $r_0$ range from 1.12($^{+0.24}_{-0.29}$) fm to 0.85($^{+0.25}_{-0.27}$) fm
- From EFT: for Set 1 (lightest charm), pole$_1$ at $\Ep-\Eth = -8.3 - 9.1i$ MeV $\pm(0.7+0.8i)$; for Set 5 (heaviest charm), pole$_1$ at $\Ep-\Eth = -1.2$ MeV $\pm 0.3$ (virtual pole)
- Critical spin-averaged mass for bound state from ERE: $\mbar^{\rm crit} = 2.71(^{+34}_{-26})$ GeV; critical reduced mass: $\muD^{\rm crit} = 1.35(^{+17}_{-13})$ GeV
- Left-hand cut branch point positions $\Elhc-\Eth$ range from -8.17(7) MeV to -7.12(4) MeV across the five sets

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice study (Padmanath 2022) from two charm quark masses to five, and improves the analysis by employing an effective field theory approach (following Du 2023) that accounts for one-pion exchange and the associated left-hand cut, rather than relying solely on effective range expansion. The results address the hypothesis that the $T_{cc}^+$ pole approaches a bound state as the heavy-quark mass increases, consistent with theoretical expectations for a deeply bound doubly bottomed tetraquark $T_{bb}$, and may shed light on the nature of the $T_{bc}$ system (Francis 2018, Padmanath 2023, Alexandrou 2023). The light-quark mass dependence is discussed qualitatively by comparing with other lattice data at $m_\pi \simeq 146$ MeV (Lyu 2023) and 348 MeV (Chen 2022).
