# Demonstration of the Universality of Molecular Structures in Prolate Deformed Nuclei

> The relationship between the deformed harmonic oscillator and the formation of molecular cluster structures, whereby valence neutrons are exchanged between cluster cores, is examined. It is found that there is a strong connection between the properties of the valence orbitals associated with deformed structures in the deformed harmonic oscillator and the molecular orbitals created by linear combinations of single-centre orbitals around nuclear clusters. The conclusion is that in addition to the appearance of clustering in the deformed harmonic oscillator that \emph{every} prolate deformed cluster structure should have molecular orbitals built on that structure. This is demonstrated through a series of examples that range from $^{13}$C to $^{57}$Ni.

## Key claim

The paper argues that *every* prolate deformed cluster structure should have molecular orbitals built on that structure, extending the previously established connection between the deformed harmonic oscillator and molecular cluster structures from alpha-like nuclei to the full spectrum of prolate deformed cluster states across the entire mass range of possible cluster structures, demonstrated through examples from $^{13}$C to $^{57}$Ni.

## Method

The authors compared wave-functions from the deformed harmonic oscillator (DHO) with molecular wave-functions constructed explicitly via the Hückel method, using linear combinations of single-centre neutron orbitals around cluster cores. For each nuclear system, they identified the DHO orbitals above the shell gap associated with the cluster core configuration and matched them to multi-centre molecular orbitals by applying rules for summing quantum numbers along the deformation axis while conserving perpendicular quantum numbers. The comparison was performed for symmetric two-centre systems (e.g., $^{33}$S, $^{81}$Zr), three-centre systems (e.g., $^{13}$C, $^{49}$Cr), and asymmetric systems (e.g., $^{21}$Ne), as well as for 4:1 deformations.

## Key numbers

* $^{13}$C: three-centre molecular orbitals constructed from linear combinations of neutron wave-functions at three alpha clusters, with coefficients 1/2, 1/√2, and 1/2 for the three centres in the lowest energy configuration.
* $^{33}$S: six degenerate DHO orbitals above the $^{32}$S core ([0,0,4], [0,1,2], [1,0,2], [0,2,0], [2,0,0], [1,1,0]) matched one-to-one with two-centre molecular orbitals from $^{16}$O clusters, each with coefficient 1/√2.
* $^{81}$Zr: ten degenerate DHO orbitals above the $^{80}$Zr core at 2:1 deformation, with five shown explicitly matching two-centre molecular orbitals from $^{40}$Ca clusters.
* $^{49}$Cr: six possible valence neutron orbitals at 3:1 deformation ([0,0,6], [0,2,0], [2,0,0], [1,1,0], [0,1,3], [1,0,3]), with four shown matching three-centre molecular orbitals from $^{16}$O clusters.
* $^{21}$Ne: three DHO orbitals ([0,0,3], [0,1,1], [1,0,1]) matched to asymmetric two-centre molecular orbitals combining alpha-particle and $^{16}$O cluster centres.

## Context

This work builds on the earlier study by McEwan et al. (2004) which examined molecular states of alpha-like nuclei, and extends the understanding to the full spectrum of prolate deformed cluster states. It connects to the foundational work by von Oertzen (1996, 1997) on molecular orbitals in beryllium isotopes and the extension to three centres and carbon isotopes by Milin and von Oertzen (2002). The results demonstrate that the deformed harmonic oscillator inherently contains molecular symmetries for valence neutrons across all prolate deformed cluster structures, providing a theoretical basis for interpreting experimental evidence from rotational bands and decay properties in systems ranging from $^{9}$Be to $^{96}$Cd.
