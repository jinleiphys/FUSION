# Virtual Compton scattering at low energies with a positron beam

> The Virtual Compton scattering (VCS) process at low energies explores the electromagnetic structure of the proton in terms of generalized polarizabilities (GPs). In the one-photon exchange approximation, VCS can be accessed with exclusive photon production reactions with electron or positron beams. The extraction of the GPs from VCS with electron beam has seen substantial progress over the past two decades. Nonetheless, a consistent picture of the GPs from low to higher scales demands further investigations. Complementary measurements with positron beams offer an unique possibility, and we present an impact study of such experimental program.

## Key claim

Complementary measurements of virtual Compton scattering with positron beams, when combined with existing electron-beam data, can provide access to beam-charge and beam-spin asymmetries that separate the real part of the longitudinal VCS amplitude (sensitive to the electric dipole generalized polarizability) from the imaginary part (which provides a cross-check of the unitary input in the dispersive formalism). The impact study shows that the sensitivity to generalized polarizabilities is sizeable in the Delta(1232) resonance region, particularly for out-of-plane kinematics.

## Method

The authors used a dispersion relation (DR) formalism for the VCS amplitude, decomposing it into 12 Lorentz invariant functions described through unsubtracted dispersion integrals saturated by pion-nucleon intermediate states using MAID pion photo- and electroproduction multipoles. The two non-converging dispersion integrals were treated as finite-energy sum rules with asymptotic contributions parametrized by dipole functions for the electric and magnetic scalar generalized polarizabilities, with mass-scale parameters Lambda_alpha and Lambda_beta varied in the range 0.5-0.9 GeV. The formalism was applied to predict beam-charge and beam-spin asymmetries for the kinematics of the JLab E12-15-001 experiment (electron beam energy 4.55 GeV, 10 cm liquid hydrogen target) at four-momentum transfers Q^2 = 0.43 and 0.65 GeV^2 and center-of-mass energies W = 1.17 and 1.232 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Q^2 values studied: 0.43 GeV^2 and 0.65 GeV^2
- Center-of-mass energies studied: W = 1.17 GeV and W = 1.232 GeV (Delta(1232) resonance position)
- Dipole mass parameters varied: Lambda_alpha = Lambda_beta = 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9 GeV
- Electron beam energy: E_lab = 4.55 GeV
- Relative effect on beam-charge asymmetry of up to +/- 30% by scaling mass parameters by +/- 0.2 units at phi = 90 degrees and scattering angles theta <= 100 degrees
- MAMI data points at Q^2 = 0.33 GeV^2 show puzzling behavior for the electric GP

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation formalism for VCS developed by Pasquini, Drechsel, and Vanderhaeghen (2001-2002) and the experimental program of GP extractions from electron-beam VCS measurements at MAMI (Roche 2000, Doria 2015), JLab (Laveissiere 2004, Fonvieille 2012), and MIT-Bates (Bourgeois 2006, 2011). The study is motivated by the need to resolve inconsistencies in the GP data, particularly the MAMI points at Q^2 = 0.33 GeV^2, and to complement the ongoing JLab VCS experiment E12-15-001 (Sparveris 2016) which covers Q^2 = 0.3-0.77 GeV^2. The proposed positron-beam measurements would enable separation of the electric and magnetic GPs through beam-charge and beam-spin asymmetries, providing an independent constraint on the electric dipole GP alpha_E1(Q^2) and a test of the unitarity input in the dispersive formalism.
