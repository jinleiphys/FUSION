# The role of intermediate $ΔΔ$ states in nucleon-nucleon scattering in the large-$N_c$ and unitary limits, and $ΔΔ$ and $ΩΩ$ scattering

> We explore potential explanations for why using large-$N_c$ ($N_c$ is the number of colors) scaling to determine the relative size of few-nucleon low-energy operators agrees with experiment even when dynamical $Δ$'s are not explicitly included. Given that the large-$N_c$ analysis is predicated on the nucleons and $Δ$'s being degenerate, this is a curious result. We show that for purely $S$-wave interactions the relationships dictated by large-$N_c$ scaling are unaffected whether the $Δ$ is included or not. In the case of higher partial waves that do not mix with $S$-waves, the impact of the $Δ$ is perturbative, which makes the agreement with naive ($Δ$-less) large-$N_c$ ordering unsurprising. For higher partial waves that mix with $S$-waves, the nucleon and $Δ$ would need to decouple to get agreement with naive large-$N_c$ ordering. We find all $NN$, $ΔN$, and $ΔΔ$ low energy coefficients for leading-order baryon-baryon scattering in $Δ$-full pionless effective field theory in terms of the two independent parameters dictated by the SU($2F$) spin-flavor symmetry that arises in the $N_c \rightarrow \infty$ limit. Because of recent lattice QCD results and experimental interest, we extend our analysis to the three-flavor case to study $ΩΩ$ scattering. We show that in the unitary limit (where scattering lengths become infinite) one of the two SU($2F$) parameters is driven to zero, resulting in enhanced symmetries, which agree with those found in spin-1/2 entanglement studies.

# Wiki Page: Role of Intermediate ΔΔ States in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

## Key claim

The large-Nc scaling relationships for nucleon-nucleon (NN) low-energy coefficients hold in a theory without explicit Δ degrees of freedom because for S-wave interactions the relationships are unaffected by Δ inclusion, for higher partial waves that do not mix with S-waves the Δ effect is perturbative, and for higher partial waves that mix with S-waves the nucleon and Δ would need to decouple to maintain agreement with naive large-Nc ordering.

## Method

The authors analyze baryon-baryon scattering using pionless effective field theory (EFT(π/)) with dynamical Δ degrees of freedom, combining large-Nc constraints from the SU(2F) spin-flavor symmetric four-baryon Lagrangian with the formalism for including intermediate ΔΔ states in NN scattering. They derive all NN, ΔN, and ΔΔ low-energy coefficients (LECs) for leading-order baryon-baryon scattering in terms of the two independent parameters (ã and b̃) dictated by SU(2F) symmetry, and extend the analysis to three-flavor case for ΩΩ scattering. The study examines S-wave, higher partial waves that do not mix with S-waves, and higher partial waves that mix with S-waves (such as 3S1-3D1 mixing and parity-violating S-P couplings) separately.

## Key numbers

- The NN LECs in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels are equal in the SU(4) symmetry limit: C_NN^(1S0) = C_NN^(3S1)
- The 18 independent four-baryon dimension-6 operators describing S-wave interactions among nucleons and Δ's in two-flavor QCD reduce to only 2 independent parameters (ã and b̃) in the large-Nc limit
- In the unitary limit (where scattering lengths become infinite), one of the two SU(2F) parameters is driven to zero, resulting in enhanced symmetries
- The deuteron binding energy scales as B ~ Nc^(-5) in the large-Nc limit, corresponding to a scattering length that scales as Nc

## Context

This work extends the large-Nc analysis of Kaplan and Savage (1996) on SU(4) symmetry constraints in NN interactions, and the formalism of Savage (1996) for including intermediate ΔΔ states in NN scattering within pionless EFT. The paper addresses a puzzle raised by Kaplan (1996) regarding why large-Nc scaling predictions agree with experiment even when dynamical Δ's are not explicitly included in the EFT, given that the large-Nc analysis is predicated on nucleons and Δ's being degenerate. The results enable application of large-Nc constraints to systems where experimental data is insufficient to constrain LECs, such as symmetry-violating interactions or coupling to external fields, and provide connections to recent lattice QCD results on ΩΩ scattering and spin-1/2 entanglement studies.
