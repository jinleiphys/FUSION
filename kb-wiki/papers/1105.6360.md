# Anomalies and time reversal invariance in relativistic hydrodynamics: the second order and higher dimensional formulations

> We present two new results on relativistic hydrodynamics with anomalies and external electromagnetic fields, "Chiral MagnetoHydroDynamics" (CMHD). First, we study CMHD in four dimensions at second order in the derivative expansion assuming the conformal/Weyl invariance. We classify all possible independent conformal second order viscous corrections to the energy-momentum tensor and to the U(1) current in the presence of external electric and/or magnetic fields, and identify eighteen terms that originate from the triangle anomaly. We then propose and motivate the following guiding principle to constrain the CMHD: the anomaly--induced terms that are even under the time reversal invariance should not contribute to the local entropy production rate. This allows us to fix thirteen out of the eighteen transport coefficients that enter the second order formulation of CMHD. We also relate one of our second order transport coefficients to the chiral shear waves. Our second subject is hydrodynamics with (N+1)-gon anomaly in an arbitrary 2N dimensions. The effects from the (N+1)-gon anomaly appear in hydrodynamics at (N-1)'th order in the derivative expansion, and we identify precisely N such corrections to the U(1) current. The time reversal constraint is powerful enough to allow us to find the analytic expressions for all transport coefficients. We confirm the validity of our results (and of the proposed guiding principle) by an explicit fluid/gravity computation within the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that anomaly-induced transport coefficients in relativistic hydrodynamics that are even under time reversal invariance should not contribute to the local entropy production rate, which allows fixing thirteen out of eighteen second-order transport coefficients in Chiral MagnetoHydroDynamics (CMHD), a stronger constraint than the standard positivity of entropy production alone would provide.

## Method

The authors classify all independent conformal second-order viscous corrections to the energy-momentum tensor and U(1) current in four-dimensional CMHD with external electromagnetic fields, assuming conformal/Weyl invariance and treating the field strength as first order in derivatives. They identify eighteen terms originating from the triangle anomaly by considering discrete symmetries of charge conjugation and parity, then impose that time-reversal-even anomaly-induced terms do not contribute to entropy production to fix thirteen transport coefficients. For higher-dimensional spacetimes with (N+1)-gon anomaly in arbitrary 2N dimensions, they identify N corrections to the U(1) current appearing at (N-1)'th order in derivatives and determine all transport coefficients analytically using the same time reversal constraint, confirmed by fluid/gravity computations within the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key numbers

- Eighteen independent conformal second-order viscous corrections to the energy-momentum tensor and U(1) current originate from the triangle anomaly in four dimensions
- Thirteen out of eighteen anomaly-induced transport coefficients are fixed by the time reversal invariance constraint
- The (N+1)-gon anomaly in arbitrary 2N dimensions yields precisely N corrections to the U(1) current at (N-1)'th order in the derivative expansion

## Context

This work extends the first-order anomalous hydrodynamics formulation of Son and Surowka (2009) to second order in derivatives, addressing the practical need for second-order formulations since first-order hydrodynamics has known causality and numerical stability problems. The paper also generalizes earlier classifications of second-order conformal viscous terms by Erdmenger et al. (2008) and Banerjee et al. (2008) to include external electromagnetic fields, and explains the chiral shear wave phenomenon previously observed in AdS/CFT computations by Sahoo et al. (2009), Matsuo et al. (2009), and Nakamura et al. (2009) in terms of a second-order anomalous transport coefficient. The results enable future fluid/gravity computations for the many second-order transport coefficients not yet calculated in any framework.
