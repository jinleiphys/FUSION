# Tubular initial conditions and ridge formation

> The 2D azimuth & rapidity structure of the two-particle correlations in relativistic A+A collisions is altered significantly by the presence of sharp inhomogeneities in superdense matter formed in such processes. The causality constraints enforce one to associate the long-range longitudinal correlations observed in a narrow angular interval, the so-called (soft) ridge, with peculiarities of the initial conditions of collision process. This study's objective is to analyze whether multiform initial tubular structures, undergoing the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution and gradual decoupling, can form the soft ridges. Motivated by the flux-tube scenarios, the initial energy density distribution contains the different numbers of high density tube-like boost-invariant inclusions that form a bumpy structure in the transverse plane. The influence of various structures of such initial conditions in the most central A+A events on the collective evolution of matter, resulting spectra, angular particle correlations and v_n-coefficients is studied in the framework of the HydroKinetic Model (HKM).

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether multiform initial tubular structures (high-density, boost-invariant tube-like inclusions in the transverse plane) undergoing hydrodynamic evolution and gradual decoupling can form the soft ridge, finding that most configurations can produce the ridge structure, but the effect depends sensitively on the number, positions, and background profile of the tubes, and not all configurations produce the characteristic narrow near-side correlations.

## Method
The authors use the HydroKinetic Model (HKM), a (2+1)D boost-invariant model that combines ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma phase with a kinetic description for the gradual decoupling of hadrons. Initial energy density distributions are constructed as a smooth background (either Gaussian or Woods-Saxon) plus a variable number (1, 3, 4, or 10) of Gaussian tube-like fluctuations with transverse radius 1 fm, placed at various radial positions. The hydrodynamic evolution starts at proper time τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c with no initial transverse flow, and the system evolves through a lattice QCD-inspired equation of state matched to a hadron-resonance gas, with chemical freeze-out at T = 165 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic start: τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c.
- Tube transverse Gaussian radius: a = 1 fm for all tube configurations.
- Background Gaussian radius: R = 5.4 fm; maximum background energy density ε_b = 90 GeV/fm³ for the Gaussian case without tubes.
- Tube maximum energy densities: ε_i = 270 GeV/fm³ for single-tube configurations; ε_i = 250 GeV/fm³ for 3-tube and 4-tube configurations; ε_i = 4ε_b (100 GeV/fm³) for the 10-tube configuration.
- Woods-Saxon background parameters: ε_b = 90 GeV/fm³, R = 6.37 fm, surface thickness δ = 0.54 fm.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_ch = 165 MeV.
- Integrated spectra are shown for p_T > 0.9 GeV (associated) and p_T > 2.5 GeV (trigger) thresholds.
- The integral of p_T² v₁(dN/p_T dp_T) over p_T from 0 to 3.5 GeV is less than 10% of the differences between its positive and negative regions, with values 0.004–0.029 GeV across configurations.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of ridge formation from fluctuating initial conditions, particularly the flux-tube scenario and the Nexus-inspired approach of Hama et al. (2010) and Andrade et al. (2011), who analyzed single-tube configurations with Cooper-Frye freeze-out. It also builds on the observation by Alver and Roland (2010) that geometrical fluctuations produce odd flow harmonics like triangular flow v₃. The present study uses the HydroKinetic Model (HKM) developed by Sinyukov et al. (2006, 2008) to include continuous particle liberation during decoupling, and it examines multi-tube configurations (up to 10 tubes) on both Gaussian and Woods-Saxon backgrounds, finding that odd numbers of tubes can produce the soft ridge and that the doubly-peaked away-side structure appears when a peripheral tube is present. The results enable future event-by-event analyses with viscous HKM to quantitatively constrain fluctuating initial conditions from experimental ridge data.
