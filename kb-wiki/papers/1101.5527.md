# A chiral quark model for meson electro-production in the S11 partial wave

> We calculate the meson scattering and electro-production amplitudes in the S11 partial wave in a coupled-channel approach that incorporates quasi-bound quark-model states. Using the quark wave functions and the quark-meson interaction from the Cloudy Bag Model, we obtain a good overall agreement with the available experimental results for the partial widths of the N(1535) and the N(1650) resonances as well as for the pion, eta and kaon electro-production amplitudes. Our model is consistent with the N(1535) resonance being dominantly a genuine three-quark state rather than a quasi-bound state of mesons and baryons.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the S11 partial wave resonances N(1535) and N(1650) can be described by a coupled-channel model incorporating genuine three-quark states from the Cloudy Bag Model, with the N(1535) being dominantly a genuine three-quark state rather than a quasi-bound state of mesons and baryons, though the model requires a free mixing angle parameter and bare masses that are not predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel K-matrix approach that incorporates quasi-bound quark-model states from the Cloudy Bag Model, using quark wave functions and quark-meson interactions to calculate meson-baryon and photon-baryon vertices rather than fitting them to data. They solve the coupled system of integral equations for the dressed vertices and non-resonant background using a separable approximation, including channels such as pi N, pi Delta(1232), pi N(1440), rho N, and K Lambda(1116), with the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances represented as superpositions of bare three-quark states with a mixing angle parameter.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius set to R_bag = 0.83 fm, with quark-pion coupling f_pi = 76 MeV
- Bare three-quark state masses: 1720 MeV for the state corresponding to N(1535) and 1815 MeV for N(1650)
- Mixing angle for bare states: theta_s = -34 degrees, reaching -33 degrees after meson loop corrections
- K-matrix poles at 1535 MeV and 1690 MeV for the two resonances
- For N(1535): total width 95 MeV (model) vs 125 MeV (experimental), with pi N branching ratio 0.44 (model) vs 0.35 (experimental) and eta N branching ratio 0.54 (model) vs 0.53 (experimental)
- For N(1650): total width 144 MeV (model) vs 150 MeV (experimental), with pi N branching ratio 0.60 (model) vs 0.60 (experimental)
- Effective momentum cut-off for s-wave mesons: Lambda = 510 MeV/c at R_bag = 0.83 fm
- Using f_eta = 1.2 f_pi yields a resonant peak at around 1510 MeV in the imaginary part of the pi N amplitude

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel formalism (Golli and Sirca, 2008, 2009) from positive-parity resonances (Delta(1232), Delta(1600), N(1440)) to the negative-parity S11 partial wave, incorporating new channels including s- and d-wave pions, eta mesons, rho mesons, and K Lambda. The results are compared to phenomenological analyses from SAID (Arndt, 2006), MAID (2007), and the PDG, and the model supports the interpretation that N(1535) is dominantly a three-quark state rather than a meson-baryon quasi-bound state as proposed in potential model approaches (Siegel, 1995) and chiral perturbation theory (Kaiser, 1997; Oset, 2002). The approach enables future calculations of electroproduction amplitudes for other partial waves and meson channels within the same unified framework.
