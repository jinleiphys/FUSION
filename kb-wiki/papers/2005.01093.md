# Medium modifications of girth distributions for inclusive jets and $Z^0+{\rm jet}$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> In this paper we investigate the medium modifications of girth distributions for inclusive jets and $Z^0$ tagged jets with small radius ($R=0.2$) in Pb+Pb collisions with $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC. The partonic spectrum in the initial hard scattering of elementary collisions are obtained by a event generator POWHEG+PYTHIA, which matches the next-to-leading (NLO) matrix elements with parton showering, and energy loss of faster parton traversing in hot/dense QCD medium is calculated by Monte Carlo simulation within Higher-Twist formalism of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions. We present the model calculations of event normalized girth distributions for inclusive jets in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, which give nice descriptions of ALICE measurements. It is shown that the girth distributions of inclusive jets in Pb+Pb are shifted to lower girth region relative to that in p+p. Thus the nuclear modification factor of girth distributions for inclusive jets is larger than unity at small girth region, while smaller than one at large girth region. This behavior results from more soft fragments inside a jet as well as the fraction alteration of gluon/quark initiated jets in heavy-ion collisions. We further predict the girth distributions for $Z^0$ boson tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, and demonstrate that the medium modification on girth distributions for $Z^0$ tagged jets is less pronounced as compared to that for inclusive jets because the dominant components of $Z^0$ tagged jets are quark-initiated jets.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that medium modifications of girth distributions for inclusive jets in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are driven by two factors: an increase in soft fragments inside jets and an altered fraction of gluon- versus quark-initiated jets, with the latter effect being less pronounced for Z0-tagged jets because they are dominated by quark jets.

## Method
The authors used POWHEG+PYTHIA to generate next-to-leading-order (NLO) matrix elements matched with parton showering for the p+p baseline, then simulated parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma using a Monte Carlo implementation of the Higher-Twist formalism of jet quenching. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm at radius R=0.2 from charged tracks with pT>0.15 GeV, and the QGP medium evolution was provided by the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model with a jet transport parameter q̂0=1.2 GeV2/fm extracted from hadron suppression data.

## Key numbers
- Jet radius R=0.2 used for all jet reconstructions in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at √s=2.76 TeV
- Inclusive jet transverse momentum range: 40 GeV < pT,jet < 60 GeV in central rapidity |ηjet|<0.7
- Quark jet fraction for inclusive jets: 33% in p+p, 45% in Pb+Pb (leading-order estimate)
- Quark jet fraction for Z0 tagged jets: 88% in p+p, 92% in Pb+Pb (leading-order estimate)
- Jet transport parameter q̂0=1.2 GeV2/fm extracted from identified hadron suppression at √s=2.76 TeV
- Z0 boson reconstructed from lepton pairs with mass 70 GeV < Mll < 110 GeV and pTZ>40 GeV
- Z0 tagged jets required pT,jet>30 GeV, with final selection 40 GeV < pT,jet < 60 GeV and |ηjet|<0.7

## Context
This work extends the study of jet substructure modifications in heavy-ion collisions, specifically following the ALICE measurement of normalized girth distributions for inclusive jets with small radius (Acharya 2018). The authors provide the first theoretical predictions for girth distributions of Z0-tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions, building on prior studies of jet quenching using the Higher-Twist formalism (Guo 2000, Zhang 2003, Majumder 2009) and the POWHEG+PYTHIA framework for p+p baselines. The results enable future comparisons with LHC data to test the flavor dependence of jet quenching and the role of quark/gluon jet fractions in medium-modified jet substructure.
