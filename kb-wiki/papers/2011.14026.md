# Form Factors of the Nucleon Axial Current

> A symmetry-preserving Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation treatment of the nucleon is used to deliver parameter-free predictions for the nucleon's axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors, $G_A$ and $G_P$, respectively. The result for $G_A$ can reliably be represented by a dipole form factor characterised by an axial charge $g_A=G_A(0)=1.25(3)$ and a mass-scale $M_A = 1.23(3) m_N$, where $m_N$ is the nucleon mass; and regarding $G_P$, the induced pseudoscalar charge $g_p^\ast = 8.80(23)$, the ratio $g_p^\ast/g_A = 7.04(22)$, and the pion pole dominance Ansatz is found to provide a reliable estimate of the directly computed result. The ratio of flavour-separated quark axial charges is also calculated: $g_A^d/g_A^u=-0.16(2)$. This value expresses a marked suppression of the size of the $d$-quark component relative to that found in nonrelativistic quark models and owes to the presence of strong diquark correlations in the nucleon Faddeev wave function -- both scalar and axial-vector, with the scalar diquark being dominant. The predicted form for $G_A$ should provide a sound foundation for analyses of the neutrino-nucleus and antineutrino-nucleus cross-sections that are relevant to modern accelerator neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper delivers parameter-free predictions for the nucleon's axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors using a symmetry-preserving Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation treatment, with the caveat that the nucleon described is the dressed-quark core (mass 1.18 GeV) rather than the physical nucleon, and all form factors are expressed in terms of the dimensionless variable x = Q²/m_N² to account for missing meson-cloud effects.

## Method

The authors solve the quark+diquark Faddeev equation to obtain the nucleon's mass and Poincaré-covariant bound-state amplitude, using diquark masses m_{[ud]} = 0.80 GeV and m_{[uu]} = m_{[ud]} = m_{[dd]} = 0.89 GeV, with light quarks characterized by a Euclidean constituent mass M_q^E = 0.33 GeV. They then construct the nucleon weak interaction current consistent with the axial-vector Ward-Green-Takahashi identity, evaluating the sum of diagrams including weak-boson couplings to dressed-quarks, diquark transitions, and seagull interactions. The model uncertainty is obtained by independently varying the diquark masses by ±5%, which changes m_N by ±3%.

## Key numbers

- Axial charge g_A = G_A(0) = 1.25(3)
- Dipole mass scale M_A = 1.23(3) m_N, where m_N is the nucleon mass
- Induced pseudoscalar charge g_p* = 8.80(23)
- Ratio g_p*/g_A = 7.04(22)
- Ratio of flavour-separated quark axial charges g_A^d/g_A^u = -0.16(2)
- Nucleon core mass m_N = 1.18 GeV
- Scalar diquark mass m_{[ud]} = 0.80 GeV; axial-vector diquark masses m_{[uu]} = m_{[ud]} = m_{[dd]} = 0.89 GeV
- Euclidean constituent quark mass M_q^E = 0.33 GeV
- Axial radius m_N ⟨r_A²⟩^{1/2} = 3.25(04)
- Scalar-to-axial-vector diquark transition coupling κ_ax^{sa} = 0.75; axial-vector-to-axial-vector diquark coupling κ_ax^{aa} = 0.73

## Context

This work extends the quark+diquark Faddeev equation framework previously applied to nucleon and Δ-baryon electromagnetic form factors (Segovia 2014, Chen 2017, Chen 2018, Chen 2019, Lu 2019, Cui 2020) to the axial current, improving upon the leading-order rainbow-ladder truncation three-body Faddeev equation analysis of Eichmann (2011) which underestimated contributions tied to emergent hadronic mass. The predicted form for G_A is intended to provide a sound foundation for analyses of neutrino-nucleus and antineutrino-nucleus cross-sections relevant to modern accelerator neutrino experiments, where the axial form factor is a necessary element in successful descriptions of νA scattering (Gysbers 2019, Lovato 2020, King 2020).
