# A Non-Equilibrium Approach to Photon Emission from the Late Stages of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Cross sections for photon production in hadronic scattering processes have been calculated according to an effective chiral field theory. For $π+ ρ\to π+ γ$ and $π+ π\to ρ+ γ$ processes, these cross sections have been implemented into a novel hadronic transport approach (SMASH), which is suitable for collisions at low and intermediate energies. The implementation is verified by systematically comparing the thermal photon rate to theoretical expectations. The photon rates we obtain are compared to previous works, where scattering processes mediated by $ω$ mesons are found to contribute significantly to the total photon production. Finally, the impact of considering the finite width of the $ρ$ meson is investigated, and a significant enhancement of photon production in the low-energy region is observed. This work is the first step towards a consistent treatment of photon emission in hybrid hydrodynamics+transport approaches. The quantification of the importance of the hadronic stage for the resolution of the direct photon flow puzzle is a next step and can be applied to identify equilibrium and non-equilibrium effects in the hadronic afterburner.

## Key claim

The authors implement photon production cross sections from an effective chiral field theory into the SMASH hadronic transport model, and they find that including processes mediated by omega mesons contributes significantly to the total photon production. The work is presented as a first step toward a consistent treatment of photon emission in hybrid hydrodynamics+transport approaches, with the hedge that the quantification of the hadronic stage's importance for the direct photon flow puzzle is a next step.

## Method

The authors derived cross sections for the hadronic scattering processes π + ρ → π + γ and π + π → ρ + γ from an effective chiral field theory Lagrangian containing pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons. These cross sections were implemented into the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport approach, which is designed for low- and intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. The implementation was verified by comparing thermal photon rates extracted from infinite-matter SMASH simulations at a temperature of 150 MeV to semi-analytical theoretical expectations. The finite width of the ρ meson (Γ_ρ = 0.149 GeV) was then incorporated by sampling the ρ mass from a Breit-Wigner distribution, with the systematic error from this approximation found to be smaller than 11%.

## Key numbers

* Thermal photon rates from SMASH at T = 150 MeV show perfect agreement with semi-analytical theoretical expectations for all processes (a) through (g), within the error bands of the theoretical expectation.
* The ρ meson width used in the finite-width extension is Γ_ρ = 0.149 GeV, taken from Agashe (2014).
* The systematic error introduced by the finite-width treatment, due to the mismatch between the ρ meson mass in the loops and the in-/outgoing ρ meson masses, is found to be smaller than 11% in the configurations tested.
* For photon energies E ≲ 0.4 GeV, the SMASH photon rates are larger than those from Kapusta (1992) and Nadeau (1992); for higher photon energies, the opposite trend is observed, with the discrepancy becoming more pronounced.
* Including the finite ρ width produces a significant increase in photon production for π + ρ → π + γ processes in the low- and mid-energy regime, while only a minor effect is seen for π + π → ρ + γ processes.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic photon production framework of Kapusta (1992) and Nadeau (1992), which included only π, ρ, and η mesons, by adding ω- and a1-mediated processes from the effective chiral field theory of Turbide (2003). The results confirm earlier findings by Holt (2015) that ω and a1 mediated processes should not be neglected for a realistic description of mesonic photon production. The implementation into SMASH enables future hybrid hydrodynamics+transport calculations using the 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code MUSIC (Schenke 2010, Ryu 2015) and SMASH, which share the same underlying field theory for photon production in the hadronic phase; this will allow benchmarking of non-equilibrium effects in the late stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and assessment of the hadronic stage's contribution to the direct photon flow puzzle.
