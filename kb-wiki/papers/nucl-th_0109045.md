# Effects of negative energy components in two-body deuteron photodisintegratio

> Several observables in two-body deuteron photodisintegration are investigated in the framework of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. Apart from keeping throughout Lorentz covariance, a special attention is paid to inclusion of both the positive energy and negative energy partial-wave components of the deuteron state. Using the Bethe-Salpeter equation for deuteron in the ladder approximation with one-boson exchanges as a driving force, the contribution of the negative energy states is studied for the unpolarized differential cross as well as the linear photon and tensor target asymmetries. These states are found to have an impact on the observables and, thus, should be taken into account in a complete theoretical development of the reaction in the intermediate energy regime.

# Effects of negative energy components in two-body deuteron photodisintegration

## Key claim

Negative energy partial-wave components of the deuteron Bethe-Salpeter vertex function have a measurable impact on the unpolarized differential cross section and tensor target asymmetries in two-body deuteron photodisintegration, but only when the one-boson exchange (OBE) interaction kernel is used; the separable Graz-II kernel omits these states entirely. The effect is most pronounced at higher photon energies (around 500 MeV) where the negative energy triplet P-components dominate over positive energy states at high three-momentum, yet the global angular structure of all observables remains determined by the positive energy components.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the ladder approximation with a one-boson exchange kernel (pi, omega, rho, eta, delta mesons) using axial-vector pion-nucleon coupling, obtaining the deuteron vertex function decomposed into eight partial-wave states (two positive-energy S and D waves, six negative-energy S, D, and P waves). They compute the reaction amplitude for gamma + D -> p + n in the plane-wave one-body approximation using Mandelstam's formalism, keeping the relative energy variable of the deuteron vertex function equal to zero (BS-ZO approximation) while retaining the k0-dependence of the one-particle propagator and the Lorentz boost. The unpolarized differential cross section, linear photon asymmetry, and three tensor target asymmetries (T20, T21, T22) are evaluated at lab photon energies of 50, 150, and 500 MeV and compared with calculations using the separable Graz-II kernel that includes only positive-energy states.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2250 MeV for both OBE and separable models (empirical: 2.224575(9) MeV)
- Asymptotic D/S ratio: 0.02497 for OBE, 0.02691 for separable (empirical: 0.0256(4))
- Quadrupole moment: 0.2678 fm^2 for OBE, 0.2774 fm^2 for separable (empirical: 0.2859(3) fm^2)
- Magnetic moment: 0.8561 e/(2m) for OBE, 0.8512 e/(2m) for separable (empirical: 0.857506(1) e/(2m))
- D-state probability: 5.10% for OBE, 5.0% for separable
- Pseudoprobability of negative-energy triplet P-state (3P1^o): -0.08%
- Pseudoprobability of all negative-energy states (OBE): P_- = -0.0050%, P_even = -0.0920%, P_odd = -0.0230%
- At E_gamma = 50 MeV, the absolute value of the bound nucleon three-momentum |k_l| lies in the range 200-265 MeV
- At E_gamma = 500 MeV, |k_l| lies in the range 450-950 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter formalism previously applied to deuteron electromagnetic form factors by Tjon and collaborators (1980s) and to deuteron photodisintegration by Kazakov and Shulga (earlier papers). It directly compares the OBE kernel approach, which couples all eight partial-wave states, with the separable Graz-II kernel model of the Graz group (1990s) that omits negative-energy components. The results show that while negative-energy states increase the cross section markedly at higher energies, their omission in separable models leads to systematic deficiencies compared to OBE calculations. The paper enables future fully covariant calculations of deuteron photodisintegration that include the full Dirac structure of the two-nucleon bound state, particularly for intermediate energies up to 1 GeV where meson-nucleon degrees of freedom remain appropriate.
