# Theory of doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions. II. Model-independent study of nuclear correlation effects

> The correlation effects in nuclei owing to which the nuclear wave functions are different from the Slater determinants are studied on the basis of the original theory. The calculated numbers of nucleons out of the nuclear Fermi-surface are in reasonable agreement with the finding from the high-momentum components of the nucleon momentum distributions in nuclei. The problems concerning the nuclear binding energy are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the number of nucleons outside the nuclear Fermi surface (due to correlation effects) can be extracted model-independently by comparing observed nucleon density distributions with "correlation-free" densities calculated from doorway states, and that these numbers are in reasonable agreement with results from high-momentum component analyses, though the approach does not identify the nature of the underlying correlations.

## Method

The authors use the doorway-state formalism for one-nucleon transfer reactions to construct correlation-free single-particle densities. By comparing these to experimentally observed density distributions for doubly closed-shell nuclei, they compute the number of redistributed nucleons \(N_{out}\) via the integral of the density difference up to the intersection point of the two distributions. They also calculate a "static" binding energy from the doorway-state occupation numbers and density-dependent many-particle forces, then compare it to the observed binding energy to estimate the proper correlation energy.

## Key numbers

* \(N_{out}\) for \(^{16}\)O: 1.10 (protons), 1.15 (neutrons), total \(A_{out}=2.25\) (14% of mass number).
* \(N_{out}\) for \(^{40}\)Ca: 3.15 (protons), 3.53 (neutrons), total \(A_{out}=6.68\) (16.7% of mass number).
* \(N_{out}\) for \(^{90}\)Zr: 7.34 (protons), 6.46 (neutrons), total \(A_{out}=13.80\) (15.3% of mass number).
* \(N_{out}\) for \(^{208}\)Pb: 13.15 (protons), 13.22 (neutrons), total \(A_{out}=26.37\) (12.7% of mass number).
* The \(A_{out}/A\) ratio from high-momentum component analyses by Frankfurt and Strikman is \((20\pm3)\%\) for heavy nuclei, cited as being in reasonable agreement with the authors' results.
* Static binding energy \({\cal E}_{st}\) for \(^{16}\)O: \(-47.0\) MeV, compared to observed binding energy \({\cal E}_b = -127.6\) MeV.
* Static binding energy \({\cal E}_{st}\) for \(^{40}\)Ca: \(-120.7\) MeV, compared to observed \({\cal E}_b = -342.0\) MeV.
* Static binding energy \({\cal E}_{st}\) for \(^{90}\)Zr: \(-234.2\) MeV, compared to observed \({\cal E}_b = -783.9\) MeV.
* Static binding energy \({\cal E}_{st}\) for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(-233.3\) MeV, compared to observed \({\cal E}_b = -1636.5\) MeV.
* The finite-range correction parameter \(D\) for the three-particle force ranges from \(-12.12\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\) (for \(^{16}\)O) to \(+34.08\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\) (for \(^{208}\)Pb); with \(r_0^2 = m_\omega^{-2} = 0.1\) fm\(^2\), the effect is negligibly small.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous doorway-state formalism (Birbrair and Ryazanov, Ref. [1]) to study nuclear correlation effects in a model-independent way, contrasting with approaches that use effective forces or the Landau-Migdal quasiparticle picture (Migdal, Ref. [9]) where correlations are implicitly included. The calculated numbers of nucleons outside the Fermi surface are compared to results from high-momentum component analyses by Frankfurt and Strikman (Refs. [10,11]), and the reasonable agreement suggests short-range correlations are the main cause of Fermi-surface smearing in doubly closed-shell nuclei. The paper identifies that the static binding energy calculation suffers from large cancellations among contributions from two-, three-, and four-particle forces, and that the convergence of the many-particle force sequence is not seen, motivating future work on a model that includes all higher-order forces, finite range, and a saturation mechanism.
