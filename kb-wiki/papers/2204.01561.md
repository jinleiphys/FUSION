# Exploring jet transport coefficients by elastic scattering in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We study the interaction of leading jet partons in a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) medium based on the effective dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM). The DQPM describes the non-perturbative nature of the sQGP at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ based on a propagator representation of massive off-shell partons (quarks and gluons) whose properties (characterized by spectral functions with $T,μ_B$ dependent masses and widths) are adjusted to reproduce the lQCD EoS for the QGP in thermodynamic equilibrium. We present the results for the jet transport coefficients, i.e. the transverse momentum transfer squared per unit length $\hat{q}$ as well as the energy loss per unit length $ΔE =dE/dx$, in the QGP and investigate their dependence on the temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ as well as on jet properties such as the leading jet parton momentum, mass, flavor, and the choice of the strong coupling constant. In this first study only elastic scattering processes of a leading jet parton with the sQGP partons are explored discarding presently the radiative processes (such as gluon Bremsstrahlung). We present a comparison of our results for the elastic energy loss in the sQGP medium with the pQCD results obtained by the BAMPS and LBT models as well as with other theoretical approaches such as lattice QCD and the LO-HTL and also with estimates of $\hat{q}/T^3$ by the color string percolation model (CSPM) and the JET and JETSCAPE Collaborations based on a comparison of hydrodynamical calculations with experimental heavy-ion data.

## Key claim
This study calculates jet transport coefficients (transverse momentum transfer squared per unit length $\hat{q}$ and energy loss per unit length $dE/dx$) for elastic scattering only, using the non-perturbative Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), and finds that the collisional energy loss can be dominant at low and intermediate jet momenta, contrary to pQCD expectations where radiative processes dominate.

## Method
The authors compute elastic scattering cross sections for leading jet partons (quarks and gluons) interacting with medium partons using the DQPM, which describes the QGP as massive off-shell quasiparticles with temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent masses and widths fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics. Transport coefficients are evaluated in both on-shell and off-shell formulations using kinetic theory, with matrix elements calculated from leading-order Feynman diagrams for all relevant channels (e.g., $qq \to qq$, $qg \to qg$, $gg \to gg$). The coupling constant $\alpha_s$ is taken as either the DQPM running coupling (temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent) or a constant value of 0.3 for comparison.

## Key numbers
- For a quark jet with momentum 10 GeV/c at $\mu_B=0$ and $T=0.2$ GeV, the scaled coefficient $\hat{q}/T^3$ shows a very weak dependence on jet mass $M$ from 0.01 GeV to 1 GeV.
- The off-shell $\hat{q}/T^3$ is smaller than the on-shell result over the entire temperature range for both 10 GeV/c and 100 GeV/c quark jets, with the difference being stronger at low $T$.
- The critical temperature used in the DQPM is $T_c \simeq 0.158$ GeV (at $\mu_B=0$), and the strange quark mass offset is $\Delta M = 30$ MeV relative to light quarks.
- The DQPM running coupling $\alpha_s$ rises in the vicinity of $T_C$ in line with lattice QCD data, while a constant value of $\alpha_s = 0.3$ is used for comparison with pQCD models like BAMPS.

## Context
This work extends previous DQPM studies of heavy quark energy loss (Song 2015, Berrehrah 2016) to light-flavor jet partons, and provides elastic-only transport coefficients for comparison with pQCD-based models such as BAMPS (Uphoff 2014, Senzel 2020) and LBT (He 2015), as well as lattice QCD results (Majumder 2012, Panero 2013, Kumar 2018) and hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations (Djordjevic 2006). The results enable future inclusion of radiative processes within the DQPM framework and provide input for hydrodynamical calculations of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
