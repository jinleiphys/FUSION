# GPDs at non-zero skewness in ADS/QCD model

> We study Generalized Parton Distribution functions (GPDs) usually measured in hard exclusive processes and encoding information on the three dimensional partonic structure of hadrons and their spin decomposition, for non zero skewness within the AdS/QCD formalism. To this aim the canonical scheme to calculate GPDs at zero skewness has been properly generalized. Furthermore, we show that the latter quantities, in this non forward regime, are sensitive to non trivial details of the hadronic light front wave function, such as a kind of parton correlations usually not accessible in studies of form factors and GPDs at zero skewness.

## Key claim
Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) at non-zero skewness can be calculated within the AdS/QCD soft-wall model by extending the canonical zero-skewness formalism, and these non-forward GPDs are sensitive to two-body parton correlations in the proton light-front wave function that are not accessible from form factors or GPDs at zero skewness.

## Method
The author generalizes the AdS/QCD formalism for GPDs at zero skewness by introducing a two-body intrinsic proton wave function \(\phi_\pm(z_1,z_2)\) and transforming the light-cone correlator variables to include the skewness dependence via \(\bar z_i\) and \(\tilde z_i\) defined from the initial and final state momentum fractions. Two different ansatze for the two-body intrinsic function are constructed: a fully uncorrelated factorized form and a correlated numerical solution using Tricomi confluent hypergeometric functions, with the correlated solution constrained to reproduce the known one-body functions \(\phi_\pm(z)\) via integral normalization conditions. The GPDs \(H_v^q\) and \(E_v^q\) are then evaluated for valence up and down quarks at non-zero \(\xi\) and \(t\).

## Key numbers
- The AdS/QCD scale parameter \(\kappa = 0.4066\) GeV, fixed to fit proton and neutron form factor data.
- GPD \(H^u_v(x,\xi,t)\) evaluated at \(t = -0.3\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\xi = 0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2\) and at \(t = -1.0\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\xi = 0, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\).
- GPD \(E^u_v(x,\xi,t)\) evaluated at the same kinematic points as \(H^u_v\).
- The correlated two-body intrinsic function coefficients: \(C_1 = 1.68944\), \(C_2 = 17.977\), \(C_3 = -18.5158\), \(C_4 = 0.082599\), \(C_5 = 2.75434\) for the plus component; \(D_1 = 1.48814\), \(D_2 = 38.6679\), \(D_3 = 0.208628\), \(D_4 = 0.04298\), \(D_5 = 3.73552\) for the minus component.
- The integral normalization accuracy for the correlated two-body function is good for \(z_1 < 10\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends the AdS/QCD GPD formalism previously developed for zero skewness by Brodsky and de Teramond (2008) and applied to form factors and GPDs at \(\xi=0\) in several works (e.g., Rinaldi 2016, Mondal 2016). The results are qualitatively consistent with constituent quark model calculations of GPDs at non-zero skewness (Pasquini 2007, Vento 2010). The approach enables future calculations of double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) within the AdS/QCD framework, which have been studied in light-front approaches (Rinaldi 2014, 2015) and are sensitive to the same type of parton correlations.
