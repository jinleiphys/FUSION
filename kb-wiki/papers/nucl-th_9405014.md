# Hard Constituent Quarks and Electroweak Properties of Pseudoscalar Mesons

> The high momentum components generated in the wave function of pseudoscalar mes* by the one-gluon-exchange interaction are investigated within a relativistic constituent quark model. Adopting the light-cone formalism, the sensitivity of the weak decay constant and the charge form factor to hard constituent quarks is illustrated.

## Key claim

The high momentum components generated in pseudoscalar meson wave functions by the one-gluon-exchange interaction sharply affect the weak decay constant and charge form factor, and agreement with experimental data can be obtained only by introducing an axial-vector coupling constant and a charge form factor for the constituent quark, not by using the bare quark values.

## Method

The authors solved the Godfrey-Isgur (GI) relativized constituent quark model Hamiltonian for pseudoscalar mesons, expanding the radial wave function onto a truncated harmonic oscillator basis (up to 38 excitation quanta). They then computed the weak decay constant and charge form factor within the light-cone formalism, comparing results obtained using the full GI interaction, the confining potential only, and the spin-independent part only. The sensitivity to hard constituent quarks was tested by introducing a monopole charge form factor and an axial-vector coupling constant at the constituent quark level.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius: experimental value is (0.660 ± 0.024 fm)^2; reproduced using a constituent quark charge radius of (0.48 fm)^2 in a monopole form factor
- Constituent quark masses adopted: m_u = m_d = 0.220 GeV, m_s = 0.419 GeV, m_c = 1.628 GeV, m_b = 4.977 GeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A^q(0) = 0.726 chosen to reproduce the central experimental value of f_A^π
- Ratio f_A^{D_s} / f_A^D = 1.17; ratio f_A^{B_s} / f_A^B = 1.20
- Kaon charge radii with flavour-independent constituent quark form factor: <r^2>_{K^+} = 0.41 fm^2, <r^2>_{K^0} = -0.036 fm^2; experimental values: <r^2>_{K^+}^{exp} = 0.34 ± 0.05 fm^2, <r^2>_{K^0}^{exp} = -0.054 ± 0.026 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the light-cone constituent quark model calculations of meson electroweak properties (previously done by the same group and others using gaussian-like wave functions) by adopting the full Godfrey-Isgur (1995) relativized quark model interaction. The paper shows that the one-gluon-exchange term generates high-momentum components absent in earlier confining-potential-only calculations, and that these components require introducing phenomenological form factors at the constituent quark level. The results enable future extension to nucleon electroweak properties and provide predictions testable at CEBAF for charge and transition form factors.
