# High-energy gluon bremsstrahlung in a finite medium: harmonic oscillator versus single scattering approximation

> A particle produced in a hard collision can lose energy through bremsstrahlung. It has long been of interest to calculate the effect on bremsstrahlung if the particle is produced inside a finite-size QCD medium such as a quark-gluon plasma. For the case of very high-energy particles traveling through the background of a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, it is known how to reduce this problem to an equivalent problem in non-relativistic two-dimensional quantum mechanics. Analytic solutions, however, have always resorted to further approximations. One is a harmonic oscillator approximation to the corresponding quantum mechanics problem, which is appropriate for sufficiently thick media. Another is to formally treat the particle as having only a single significant scattering from the plasma (known as the N=1 term of the opacity expansion), which is appropriate for sufficiently thin media. In a broad range of intermediate cases, these two very different approximations give surprisingly similar but slightly differing results if one works to leading logarithmic order in the particle energy, and there has been confusion about the range of validity of each approximation. In this paper, I sort out in detail the parametric range of validity of these two approximations at leading logarithmic order. For simplicity, I study the problem for small alpha_s and large logarithms but alpha_s log << 1.

## Key claim
The harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation for gluon bremsstrahlung in a finite QCD medium is valid over a wider range of medium thicknesses than previously thought, specifically remaining valid when the medium length L is of order the typical formation length L∞. The paper resolves confusion by showing that the HO and single-scattering (N=1) approximations describe different physical regimes of the underlying elastic scattering (typical multiple scattering vs. rare large-angle scattering), and that the total leading-logarithmic result for the average energy loss ΔE is a sum of contributions from both regimes, which mathematically reproduces the N=1 formula.

## Method
The author performs a parametric analysis of the brick problem (a high-energy particle created inside a uniform QCD medium of length L) at leading logarithmic order in the particle energy E, assuming small α_s and α_s log ≪ 1. The analysis compares the harmonic oscillator approximation (valid for thick media where many soft collisions occur) with the N=1 term of the opacity expansion (valid for thin media), by examining the probability distribution of the total transverse momentum Q_⊥ acquired by the particle. The relative importance of typical diffusive scattering (the HO regime) versus rare single large-angle scattering (the N=1 regime) is determined for both the average energy loss ΔE and the bremsstrahlung spectrum.

## Key numbers
- For thick media (L ≫ L∞), the HO approximation gives ΔE_HO ≃ ¼ C_s α q̂_A L², growing linearly with L.
- For thin media (L ≪ L∞), the HO result for ΔE is ΔE_HO ≃ π C_s C_A α³ 𝒩 L² ln( q̂_A L / m_D² ), while the N=1 result is ΔE_{N=1} ≃ π C_s C_A α³ 𝒩 L² ln( E / m_D² L ).
- The total leading-log result for ΔE when L ≪ L∞ is ΔE ≃ π C_s C_A α³ 𝒩 L² [ ln( q̂_A L / m_D² ) + ln( E / q̂_A L² ) ].
- The transition length L_* where the HO and N=1 contributions to ΔE are equal is L_* ∼ ( m_D⁴ / q̂_A E )^{1/6} L∞.
- For the bremsstrahlung spectrum at fixed ω and small x, the HO approximation gives a dependence ∝ L⁴, while the N=1 approximation gives ∝ L².
- The typical squared transverse momentum acquired by a particle is (Q_⊥²)_typ = q̂_R L, with q̂_R ≃ 4π C_R α² 𝒩 ln( C_R α² 𝒩 L / m_D² ).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, and Schiff (BDMS) (1997) on the harmonic oscillator approximation and the opacity expansion of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (GLV) (2000-2001), as well as the earlier work of Wiedemann and Gyulassy (1999). It directly addresses and refines the conclusions of Zakharov (2004) regarding the domain of validity of the HO approximation. The results enable a more precise understanding of which physical scattering processes (multiple soft vs. single hard) dominate energy loss and the bremsstrahlung spectrum for a given medium thickness, clarifying the interplay between the HO and N=1 approximations at leading logarithmic order.
