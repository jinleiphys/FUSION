# $D_{sJ}^+(2632)$: An Excellent Candidate of Tetraquarks

> We analyze various possible interpretations of the narrow state $D_{sJ}(2632)$ which lies 100 MeV above threshold. This interesting state decays mainly into $D_s η$ instead of $D^0 K^+$. If this relative branching ratio is further confirmed by other experimental groups, we point out that the identification of $D_{sJ}(2632)$ either as a $c\bar s$ state or more generally as a ${\bf {\bar 3}}$ state in the $SU(3)_F$ representation is probably problematic. Instead, such an anomalous decay pattern strongly indicates $D_{sJ}(2632)$ is a four quark state in the $SU(3)_F$ ${\bf 15}$ representation with the quark content ${1\over 2\sqrt{2}} (ds\bar{d}+sd\bar{d}+su\bar{u}+us\bar{u}-2ss\bar{s})\bar{c}$. We discuss its partners in the same multiplet, and the similar four-quark states composed of a bottom quark $B_{sJ}^0(5832)$. Experimental searches of other members especially those exotic ones are strongly called for.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the narrow state \(D_{sJ}(2632)\) is very probably a tetraquark (four-quark) state in the \(SU(3)_F\) **15** representation, rather than a conventional \(c\bar s\) meson or a \(\bar 3\) state, **if** the experimentally measured anomalous branching ratio \(\Gamma(D^0 K^+)/\Gamma(D_s \eta) = 0.16 \pm 0.06\) is confirmed by other experimental groups.

## Method

The authors analyze the decay pattern of \(D_{sJ}(2632)\) using an effective Lagrangian approach with SU(3) flavor Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, comparing the predicted relative branching ratio for a conventional \(c\bar s\) state (or any \(\bar 3\) representation) against the experimental value from the SELEX Collaboration. They then construct the flavor wave functions for tetraquark states in the \(\bar 6\) and 15 representations of \(SU(3)_F\), compute the relative decay widths for the candidate isoscalar state \(D_{s,15}^{-\prime}\) in the 15-plet, and show that the predicted ratio \(\Gamma(\bar D^0 K^-)/\Gamma(D_s^- \eta) \approx 0.25\) (for S-wave decay) is consistent with the experimental result.

## Key numbers

- The experimental branching ratio from SELEX: \(\Gamma(D^0 K^+) / \Gamma(D_s \eta) = 0.16 \pm 0.06\).
- For a conventional \(c\bar s\) state, the predicted ratio is \(\Gamma(D^0 K^+) / \Gamma(D_s \eta) = 2.3 \times (1.54)^{2L} \ge 2.3\), which is about 15 times larger than the experimental value.
- For the tetraquark candidate \(D_{s,15}^{-\prime}\) in the 15-plet, the predicted ratio is \(\Gamma(\bar D^0 K^-) / \Gamma(D_s^- \eta) = 0.25 \times (1.54)^{2L}\), yielding 0.25 for S-wave decay (\(L=0\)) and 0.59 for P-wave decay (\(L=1\)).
- The decay width of \(D_{sJ}(2632)\) is less than 17 MeV at 90% confidence level.
- The state lies 274 MeV above the \(D^0 K^+\) threshold and 116 MeV above the \(D_s \eta\) threshold.
- Using constituent quark masses (\(m_u=m_d=310\) MeV, \(m_s=450\) MeV, \(m_c=1430\) MeV), the estimated mass of \(D_{s,15}^{-\prime}\) is 2640 MeV.
- The predicted mass of the bottom tetraquark partner \(B_{sJ}^0(5832)\) is obtained by adding \(m_b - m_c = 3200\) MeV to the \(D_{sJ}(2632)\) mass.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of narrow charm-strange mesons following the discovery of \(D_{sJ}(2317)\) and \(D_{sJ}(2457)\) by BaBar, CLEO, and Belle, which had also been proposed as tetraquarks by Cheng and others. The paper specifically addresses the new state \(D_{sJ}(2632)\) observed by the SELEX Collaboration, and contrasts its interpretation with alternative proposals: Maiani et al. suggested a \(cd\bar d \bar s\) tetraquark with isospin breaking; Chen et al. tried to identify all three narrow charm-strange mesons as four-quark states; Zhao et al. proposed it as a radial excitation of \(D_s^*(2112)\); and Barnes et al. also considered a diquark-antidiquark picture but noted disagreement with the SELEX branching ratio. The present work provides a systematic SU(3) flavor analysis of tetraquark multiplets and predicts a set of exotic partner states (e.g., \(D^+_{15}\), \(D^{--}_{15}\), \(D^0_{\bar s,15}\)) that should be experimentally searchable, as well as a bottom analog \(B_{sJ}^0(5832)\).
