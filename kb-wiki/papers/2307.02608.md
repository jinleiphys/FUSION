# Resonant screening in dense and magnetized QCD matter

> We calculate the Debye screening mass in thermal, dense and magnetized QCD matter in the frame of resummed perturbation theory. In the limit of zero temperature, when the Landau energy level and Fermi surface of quarks match each other $μ_q^2=2n|qB|$, where $q$, $μ_q$ and $B$ are respectively the quark electric charge, chemical potential and external magnetic field, the screening mass diverges and the system is in the state of weakly interacting parton gas, which is very different from the known result of strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma at high temperature. The divergence disappears in thermal medium, but the screening mass oscillates with clear peaks at the matched magnetic field.

## Key claim

The Debye screening mass in dense and magnetized QCD matter diverges at zero temperature when the Landau energy level matches the Fermi surface (μ_q² = 2n|qB|), producing a fully screened, weakly interacting parton gas; this divergence is washed out at finite temperature but replaced by oscillatory peaks.

## Method

The authors calculate the Debye screening mass from the static, long-wavelength limit of the gluon self-energy using resummed perturbation theory, with quark propagators in the Schwinger formalism for a constant external magnetic field. The polarization tensor is evaluated via a quark-loop diagram including Matsubara frequency summation and Landau-level summation, and the magnetic-field-induced shift of the squared Debye mass is separated into two components δm₁² and δm₂². The zero-temperature limit is taken by converting thermal sums into integrals with step functions and delta functions.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and fixed μ_B = 1 GeV, the Debye mass diverges at magnetic field values |eB| = 1/6, 1/12, … GeV² for d and s quarks, and |eB| = 1/12, 1/24, … GeV² for u quarks.
- In the strong-field limit where |q_fB| > μ_q²/2, the squared Debye mass becomes m_D² = (g²/3π²)|eB|, independent of chemical potential.
- In the weak-field limit, the mass shift expands as δm_D² = [14ζ(3)/(9(2π)⁴)] (g²/T²) |eB|² + O(|eB|⁴).
- In the strong-field limit at finite temperature, the total mass shift is δm_D² = (g²/3π²)|eB| - (N_f g²/(2π²))(μ_q² + π²T²/3) θ(μ_q - πT).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the Debye mass in thermal and magnetized QCD matter (Huang 2022) to include finite baryon chemical potential, recovering the weak-field and strong-field limits of earlier studies (Singh 2017, Karmakar 2018, Hasan 2020). The resonant screening phenomenon, divergence when the Fermi surface matches a Landau level, is analogous to resonant transmission in quantum mechanics and may be relevant for dense magnetized matter in compact stars and intermediate-energy nuclear collisions.
