# Neutron Transfer Reactions for Deformed Nuclei Using Sturmian Basis States

> We study the spin-parity distribution P(J$^π$,E) of $^{156}$Gd excited states above the neutron separation energy $S_{n}=8.536$ MeV \cite{156Gd_list_of_levels} that are expected to be populated via the 1-step neutron pickup reaction $^{157}$Gd($^{3}$He,$^{4}$He)$^{156}$Gd. In analogy with the rotor plus particle model \cite{Bohr&Mottelson-II}, we view excited states in $^{156}$Gd as rotational excitations built on intrinsic states consisting of a neutron hole in the $^{157}$Gd core, that is, a neutron removal from a deformed Woods-Saxon type single-particle state \cite{Woods-Saxon:1954} in $^{157}$Gd. The particle-core interaction usually dominated by a Coriolis coupling are accounted via first order perturbation theory \cite{VGGueorguiev:07062002}. The reaction cross section to each excited state in $^{156}$Gd is calculated as coherent contribution using a standard reaction code \cite{CHUCK} based on spherical basis states. The spectroscopic factor associated with each state is the expansion coefficient of the deformed neutron state in a spherical Sturmian basis along with the spherical form factors \cite{VGGueorguiev:07062002}. The total cross section, as a function of the excitation energy, is generated using Lorentzian smearing distribution function. Our calculations show that, within the assumptions and computational modeling, the reaction $^{3}$He+$^{157}$Gd $\rightarrow$ $^{4}$He+$^{156}$Gd$^{\star}$ has a smooth formation probability P(J$^π$,E) within the energy range relevant to the desired reaction $^{155}$Gd+n $\rightarrow$ $^{156}$Gd$^{\star}$. The formation probability P(J$^π$,E) resembles a Gaussian distribution with centroids and widths that differ for positive and negative parity states.

## Key claim
The one-step neutron pickup reaction $^{157}$Gd($^{3}$He,$^{4}$He)$^{156}$Gd produces a smoothly-varying formation probability P(J$^{\pi}$,E) for excited states in $^{156}$Gd above the neutron separation energy, but only within the assumptions of the model (neglected Coriolis band mixing, neglected pairing effects, and a geometric upper limit of angular momentum transfer of 4). The formation probability resembles a Gaussian distribution with different centroids and widths for positive and negative parity states.

## Method
The authors model excited states in $^{156}$Gd as rotational excitations built on intrinsic states consisting of a neutron hole in the $^{157}$Gd core, using a rotor-plus-particle model. Single-particle states in the deformed Woods-Saxon potential are expanded in a spherical Sturmian basis to maintain correct asymptotic tails, and the reaction cross sections are calculated within the zero-range Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) using the code CHUCK3. The total cross section as a function of excitation energy is generated using a Lorentzian smearing distribution function with width $\Gamma = a + bE$.

## Key numbers
- Neutron separation energy for $^{156}$Gd: $S_n = 8.536$ MeV
- Moment of inertia for rotational bands in $^{156}$Gd: $\hbar^2 / 2\mathcal{I} = 13.59$ keV, fixed by fitting the first four excited states of the $\Omega = 0^+$ ground state band
- Coriolis energy shift coefficient: $c = 17.741$, chosen to reproduce the excitation energy of the $3_1^+$ state in $^{156}$Gd
- Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{157}$Gd: $V_0 = -45.1776$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.25$ fm, $a_0 = 0.65$ fm, $V_{ls} = 19.2015$ MeV, $\beta_2 = 0.29$, $\beta_4 = 0$
- Energy of the 47th neutron state in $^{157}$Gd: $\epsilon_{47} = -6.361$ MeV, near the experimental neutron separation energy $S_n = 6.3598$ MeV for $^{157}$Gd
- Zero-range transfer potential strength: $D_0^2 = 18$
- Maximum angular momentum transfer estimated from geometric arguments: 4

## Context
This work extends the Sturmian basis method for transfer reactions to highly excited states in deformed nuclei, building on the approach of Andersen and Back (1970). The reaction $^{157}$Gd($^{3}$He,$^{4}$He)$^{156}$Gd is studied as a surrogate for the neutron capture reaction $^{155}$Gd+n $\rightarrow$ $^{156}$Gd$^{\star}$, following the Surrogate Method framework of Escher et al. (2012). The results are considered a first estimate; the authors note that recent work by Escher et al. (2016) has demonstrated that two-step reaction mechanisms (pickup followed by inelastic excitation and vice versa) can play an important role in transfer reactions populating highly-excited states above a few MeV, and the present work needs to be extended to include those contributions.
