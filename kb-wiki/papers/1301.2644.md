# Commutators of the four-current and sum rules in relativistic nuclear models

> There is a long-standing problem on the linearly energy-weighted sum of the excitation strengths in the relativistic field theory and nuclear models: The sum value should be positively definite, while its naive calculation using the current commutator or the double commutator of the excitation operator with Dirac Hamiltonian yields the value to vanish. This paradoxical contradiction is solved in an analytic way.

# Commutators of the four-current and sum rules in relativistic nuclear models

## Key claim

The long-standing contradiction in relativistic field theory and nuclear models, where the linearly energy-weighted sum of excitation strengths should be positive definite but a naive calculation using the current commutator or double commutator with the Dirac Hamiltonian yields zero, is resolved by showing that the ground-state expectation value of the commutator must be calculated first in a finite momentum space, and only then can the infinite momentum limit be taken; the order of these operations does not commute.

## Method

The authors define the nucleon field with a finite momentum cutoff \(P_\infty\) in the momentum-space sum, keeping the volume finite. They compute the ground-state expectation value of the commutator \([iJ^0(\mathbf{x}), \mathbf{J}(\mathbf{y})]\) in symmetric nuclear matter with Fermi momentum \(k_F\), using the explicit field expansion and the anti-commutation relations modified by the cutoff. After evaluating the expectation value analytically, they take the limit \(P_\infty \rightarrow \infty\) to obtain the Schwinger term, a gradient of a delta function, which yields a positive sum rule value. They then apply this framework to decompose the total sum into contributions from particle-hole excitations, particle-antinucleon excitations, and Dirac-sea vacuum contributions in relativistic nuclear mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger term obtained in the limit \(P_\infty \rightarrow \infty\) is \(\langle 0 | [iJ^0(\mathbf{x}), \mathbf{J}(\mathbf{y})] | 0 \rangle = \frac{2P_\infty^2}{3\pi^2} \nabla_{\mathbf{x}} \delta(\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{y})\), with the divergent coefficient \(\frac{2P_\infty^2}{3\pi^2}\).
- The divergent part of the Dirac-sea contribution \(\snbar(\mathbf{q})\) for \(q \ll P_\infty\) and \(m \ll P_\infty\) is \(\snbar(\mathbf{q}) = \frac{V}{3\pi^2} q^2 P_\infty^2\).
- The particle-hole sum \(\sph(\mathbf{q})\) in the nonrelativistic limit reduces to \(\sph^{\text{NR}} = \frac{\rho}{2m} \int d^3x\, |\nabla f(\mathbf{x})|^2\) with \(\rho = A/V = 2k_F^2/(3\pi^2)\).
- As \(q \rightarrow 0\), \(\sph(\mathbf{q}) \rightarrow \frac{A}{2E_F} q^2\) with \(E_F = \sqrt{k_F^2 + m^2}\); as \(q \rightarrow \infty\), \(\sph(\mathbf{q}) \rightarrow \frac{k_F^3}{3\pi^2} V q \left(1 - \frac{m^2 + 2k_F^2/5}{2q^2}\right)\).
- The no-sea approximation sum \(\snosea\) vanishes for \(P_\infty - q > k_F\), while the total sum \(S = \svac\) from Dirac-sea transitions is infinite and independent of the Fermi momentum or nucleon number \(A\).

## Context

This work resolves a paradox noted over the previous 50 years in relativistic field theory, where Schwinger (1951) introduced a non-local point-split current to generate a gradient-of-delta-function term (the Schwinger term) from Lorentz covariance, and where Gasiorowicz and Geffen (1969) and Weinberg and Gross et al. derived it via vector-meson dominance or SU(3)×SU(3) algebra. In nuclear physics, Price et al. attempted to interpret the vanishing double commutator in the Walecka-Serot model by invoking particle-hole and particle-antinucleon excitations, but the role of the Schwinger term was not clarified. The present paper provides an analytic derivation of the Schwinger term from the local current in finite momentum space, showing that the no-sea approximation (used by Furnstahl and others) can employ the vanishing double commutator only because it neglects the divergent Dirac-sea contribution that restores the positive sum. This enables a consistent treatment of energy-weighted sum rules in relativistic nuclear models and RPA calculations.
