# Pasta phases within the QMC model

> In this work the low density regions of nuclear and neutron star matter are studied. The search for the existence of pasta phases in this region is performed within the context of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which incorporates quark degrees of freedom. Fixed proton fractions are considered, as well as nuclear matter in beta equilibrium at zero temperature. We discuss the recent attempts to better understand the surface energy in the coexistence phases regime and we present results that show the existence of the pasta phases subject to some choices of the surface energy coefficient. We also analyze the influence of the nuclear pasta on some neutron star properties. The equation of state containing the pasta phase will be part of a complete grid for future use in supernova simulations.

## Key claim

The QMC model predicts the existence of pasta phases in low-density nuclear and neutron star matter, but only for specific choices of the surface energy coefficient; with the parameter \(a=0.023\) fm (yielding \(\mathcal{S}=1.123\) MeV.fm\(^{-2}\) for \(Y_p=0.5\)) pasta phases appear, whereas with \(a=0.1\) fm or \(a=0.21\) fm no pasta phases are found for symmetric matter.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes nucleons as MIT bags containing quarks interacting via \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields in the mean-field approximation. They applied the coexisting phases (CP) approximation to construct pasta structures (droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) at zero temperature for fixed proton fractions and for matter in beta equilibrium, adding surface and Coulomb energy terms. The surface tension coefficient was computed using a geometrical approach with an adjustable parameter \(a\), fitted to reproduce the nuclear surface energy.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties of the QMC model: binding energy \(B/A = -15.7\) MeV at saturation density \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), effective nucleon mass \(M^*/M = 0.77\), symmetry energy \(J = 34.5\) MeV, slope of symmetry energy \(L_0 = 90\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 295\) MeV.
- Surface tension coefficient for \(Y_p = 0.5\): \(\mathcal{S} = 1.12\) MeV.fm\(^{-2}\) (with \(a = 0.023\) fm); for \(Y_p = 0.1\): \(\mathcal{S} = 0.21\) MeV.fm\(^{-2}\); for \(Y_p = 0.3\): \(\mathcal{S} = 0.69\) MeV.fm\(^{-2}\); for \(Y_p = 0.4\): \(\mathcal{S} = 0.93\) MeV.fm\(^{-2}\).
- Transition density from pasta to homogeneous phase for beta-equilibrium matter: \(\rho_t = 0.051\) fm\(^{-3}\) (for both \(a = 0.020\) fm and \(a = 0.023\) fm); for \(Y_p = 0.5\): \(\rho_t = 0.097\) fm\(^{-3}\) (\(a = 0.023\) fm); for \(Y_p = 0.3\): \(\rho_t = 0.093\) fm\(^{-3}\); for \(Y_p = 0.1\): \(\rho_t = 0.063\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Maximum neutron star mass: \(M_{\text{max}} = 2.16\) M\(_\odot\) (same with or without pasta phases).
- Radius of a 1.4 M\(_\odot\) neutron star: 13.8 km with pasta phases (EoS1), 14.4 km without pasta phases (EoS2); the difference is 600 m.

## Context

This work extends the study of pasta phases, previously investigated with quantum hadrodynamic models (Avancini 2008, Avancini 2009), to the QMC model which incorporates quark degrees of freedom. The results are consistent with earlier findings that pasta phases appear at low densities and that the transition density decreases with lower proton fraction, and that for beta-equilibrium matter only droplets are present, as predicted by Oyamatsu (2007) for models with symmetry energy slope above 80 MeV. The equation of state including pasta phases is intended for use in future supernova simulations and neutron star cooling calculations.
