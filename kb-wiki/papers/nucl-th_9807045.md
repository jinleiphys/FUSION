# Consequences of coarse grained Vlasov equations

> The Vlasov equation is analyzed for coarse grained distributions resembling a finite width of test-particles as used in numerical implementations. It is shown that this coarse grained distribution obeys a kinetic equation similar to the Vlasov equation, but with additional terms. These terms give rise to entropy production indicating dissipative features due to a nonlinear mode coupling The interchange of coarse graining and dynamical evolution is discussed with the help of an exactly solvable model for the selfconsistent Vlasov equation and practical consequences are worked out. By calculating analytically the stationary solution of a general Vlasov equation we can show that a sum of modified Boltzmann-like distributions is approached dependent on the initial distribution. This behavior is independent of degeneracy and only controlled by the width of test-particles. The condition for approaching a stationary solution is derived and it is found that the coarse graining energy given by the momentum width of test particles should be smaller than a quarter of the kinetic energy. Observable consequences of this coarse graining are: (i) spatial correlations in observables, (ii) too large radii of clusters or nuclei in self-consistent Thomas-Fermi treatments, (iii) a structure term in the response function resembling vertex correction correlations or internal structure effects and (iv) a modified centroid energy and higher damping width of collective modes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Vlasov equation, when solved numerically with test particles of finite width, is actually replaced by a modified kinetic equation with additional dissipative terms that produce entropy; the stationary solution of this coarse-grained equation approaches a sum of modified Boltzmann-like distributions only if the coarse-graining energy (given by the momentum width of test particles) is smaller than one quarter of the kinetic energy.

## Method

The authors convolve the exact Vlasov equation with Gaussian test-particle widths in space and momentum, deriving the explicit coarse-grained kinetic equation (Equation 13). They analyze entropy production via a balance equation and illustrate the effects using two exactly solvable models: free streaming and a selfconsistent harmonic-oscillator plus separable multipole-multipole interaction. The stationary solution of the coarse-grained Vlasov equation is obtained by separation of variables, and stability is examined through linear response analysis.

## Key numbers

- The condition for approaching a stationary solution is that the coarse-graining energy (momentum width of test particles) must be smaller than one quarter of the kinetic energy.
- For a typical nuclear system, the time scale of entropy production is \(\tau_c \approx \pi R / 2 \sqrt{m / U_{\rm ext}(0)}\), giving \(\tau_c \approx 4.8 A^{1/3}\) fm/c; for \(^{12}{\rm O}\) this yields \(\tau_c \approx 12\) fm/c and for \(^{208}{\rm Pb}\) \(\tau_c \approx 28\) fm/c.
- The entropy for the free-streaming model grows as \(S(t) = 3 \log(2\pi e / \sqrt{\lambda} \sigma_p \, t)\) for large times.
- In the selfconsistent harmonic model, the entropy oscillates as \(1 - \cos(2\omega t)\) with frequency set by the external harmonic oscillator frequency \(\omega\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of coarse-graining in kinetic theory dating back to Gibbs and Ehrenfest, and builds on the observation by Jahr and Bonasera (1996) that numerical coarse-graining generates dissipation in Vlasov simulations. The authors provide an explicit analytic form for the coarse-grained kinetic equation (the Husimi representation of the Wigner function) not previously given, and show that the resulting dissipative features (entropy production, modified stationary distributions, altered damping widths) have direct consequences for nuclear simulations: they predict too large radii in Thomas-Fermi treatments, a structure term in the response function resembling vertex corrections, and a modified centroid energy with higher damping width of collective modes. The work enables quantitative criteria for choosing test-particle widths in numerical Vlasov codes to avoid spurious dissipation.
