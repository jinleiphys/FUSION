# Rotating deformed halo nuclei and shape decoupling effects

> We explore the rotational feature of deformed halos by performing the angular momentum projection (AMP) on the ground state wave functions obtained from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory (DRHBc) in continuum. The DRHBc+AMP approach self-consistently describes the coupling between single particle bound states and the continuum not only in the ground state but also in rotational states. The rotational modes of deformed halos in $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg are investigated by studying properties of rotational states such as excitation energy, configuration, and density distribution. Our study demonstrates that the deformed halo structure persists from the ground state in the intrinsic frame to collective states. Especially, the typical behavior of shape decoupling effects in rotating deformed halo nuclei is revealed.

## Key claim
The deformed halo structure and shape decoupling effects (prolate core, oblate halo) predicted in the intrinsic ground state of $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg persist in low-lying rotational states, but the shape decoupling effect is absent in the $0^+$ projected state and only appears in the $2^+$ and $4^+$ states.

## Method
The authors implemented angular momentum projection (AMP) on intrinsic ground state wave functions obtained from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). The projected wave functions were expanded in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis to properly describe the asymptotic behavior of halo nuclei. The approach was applied to study the rotational bands of $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg, calculating excitation energies, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, and density distributions for the projected states.

## Key numbers
- $^{42}$Mg and $^{44}$Mg are predicted to be deformed halo nuclei with quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta_2 = 0.38$ and $0.31$, respectively.
- The calculated excitation energy of the $2^+$ state is $1.36$ MeV for $^{42}$Mg and $1.37$ MeV for $^{44}$Mg.
- The moment of inertia (MoI) for $^{42}$Mg is $2.34$ $\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\ \hbar^2$ and for $^{44}$Mg is $2.13$ $\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\ \hbar^2$, both much smaller than the MoI of $5.19$ $\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\ \hbar^2$ for the non-halo nucleus $^{40}$Mg.
- For the $2^+$ state of $^{42}$Mg, the mass quadrupole moment of the neutron core is $-46.47$ fm$^2$ (prolate) while that of the neutron halo is $2.24$ fm$^2$ (oblate), confirming shape decoupling.

## Context
This work extends the DRHBc theory (Zhou 2010, Li 2012) by adding angular momentum projection, enabling the study of rotational excitations in deformed halo nuclei for the first time. It confirms earlier predictions of shape decoupling in $^{42,44}$Mg (Zhou 2010, Li 2012) and demonstrates that this exotic structure survives in rotating states. The results enable future experimental searches for rotational bands in these neutron-drip-line nuclei to reveal the deformed halo configuration.
