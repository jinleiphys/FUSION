# Magnetic dipole excitations based on the relativistic nuclear energy density functional

> Magnetic dipole (M1) excitations build not only a fundamental mode of nucleonic transitions, but they are also relevant for nuclear astrophysics applications. We have established a theory framework for description of M1 transitions based on the relativistic nuclear energy density functional. For this purpose the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) is established using density dependent point coupling interaction DD-PC1, supplemented with the isovector-pseudovector interaction channel in order to study unnatural parity transitions. The introduced framework has been validated using the M1 sum rule for core-plus-two-nucleon systems, and employed in studies of the spin, orbital, isoscalar and isovector M1 transition strengths, that relate to the electromagnetic probe, in magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, and open shell nuclei $^{42}$Ca and $^{50}$Ti. In these systems, the isovector spin-flip M1 transition is dominant, mainly between one or two spin-orbit partner states. It is shown that pairing correlations have a significant impact on the centroid energy and major peak position of the M1 mode. The M1 excitations could provide an additional constraint to improve nuclear energy density functionals in the future studies.

## Key claim

The authors establish a relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) framework for describing M1 transitions, but the isovector-pseudovector coupling strength parameter must be fitted to experimental data on M1 transitions in selected nuclei (208Pb and 48Ca) rather than being determined from ground-state properties.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-PC1 density-dependent point-coupling interaction for the nuclear ground state, supplemented by the Gogny-D1S force for pairing correlations. They then extend the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) by adding an isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) contact interaction channel to describe unnatural parity Jπ=1+ M1 transitions, with the IV-PV coupling strength αIV-PV = 0.53 MeV fm3 fitted to reproduce the centroid energies in 208Pb and 48Ca.

## Key numbers

- The optimal isovector-pseudovector coupling strength is αIV-PV = 0.53 MeV fm3, yielding an energy gap Δ less than 1 MeV for both 208Pb and 48Ca.
- For 208Pb, the full M1 response shows two dominant peaks at 6.11 MeV and 7.51 MeV, with total integrated strength ΣB(M1) = 41.99 μN2 up to 50 MeV.
- In 208Pb, the isoscalar strength is ΣB(IS)(M1) = 0.43 μN2 and the isovector strength is ΣB(IV)(M1) = 42.33 μN2 up to 50 MeV.
- The non-energy-weighted sum rule test yields m0 = 2.73 μN2 for 18O (compared to analytical SRV of 2.79 μN2) and m0 = 2.91 μN2 for 42Ca (compared to SRV of 2.99 μN2).
- The bare gyromagnetic ratios used are gπℓ = 1, gνℓ = 0, gπs = 5.586, gνs = -3.826, giving gISs = 0.880 and gIVs = 4.706.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic nuclear energy density functional framework, previously successful for electric excitations and ground-state properties (Vretenar 2005, Niksic 2008, Paar 2007), to magnetic dipole transitions. It addresses difficulties noted in Skyrme functional approaches (Nesterenko 2008, Nesterenko 2009, Speth 2020) where modern Skyrme parameterizations successfully reproduce electric but not magnetic transitions. The framework enables future studies of M1 excitations as constraints for improving nuclear energy density functionals and provides a basis for investigating neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant to supernova physics and r-process nucleosynthesis (Langanke 2001, Loens 2012).
