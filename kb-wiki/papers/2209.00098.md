# Qubitization strategies for bosonic field theories

> Quantum simulations of bosonic field theories require a truncation in field space to map the theory onto finite quantum registers. Ideally, the truncated theory preserves the symmetries of the original model and has a critical point in the same universality class. In this paper, we explore two different truncations that preserve the symmetries of the 1+1-dimensional $O(3)$ non-linear $σ$-model - one that truncates the Hilbert space for the unit sphere by setting an angular momentum cutoff and a fuzzy sphere truncation inspired by non-commutative geometry. We compare the spectrum of the truncated theories in a finite box with the full theory. We use open boundary conditions, a novel method that improves on the correlation lengths accessible in our calculations. We provide evidence that the angular-momentum truncation fails to reproduce the $σ$-model and that the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy model agrees with the full theory.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that the angular-momentum truncation with ℓmax=1 fails to reproduce the 1+1-dimensional O(3) non-linear σ-model, while the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy sphere truncation agrees with the full theory up to correlation lengths ξ≈66 lattice units, though this agreement is demonstrated only for a specific truncation level and may not generalize.

## Method

The authors compare two O(3)-symmetric truncations of the 1+1-dimensional O(3) σ-model: an angular-momentum cutoff at ℓmax=1 (yielding a 4-dimensional local Hilbert space) and a fuzzy sphere truncation with j=1/2 (also yielding a 4-dimensional local Hilbert space). They compute low-lying energy spectra using matrix-product states (MPS) with open boundary conditions, which allows access to larger correlation lengths than periodic boundary conditions. The truncated spectra are compared to Monte Carlo simulations of the full σ-model with open boundaries, using the volume dependence of energy levels to probe both infrared and ultraviolet regimes.

## Key numbers

- Correlation length reached: ξ≈66 lattice units for the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy model
- Bond dimension extrapolation: power law Δ(D)=Δ+A/D^B used for energy gap extrapolation, with error defined as half the distance between extrapolated value and largest-D calculation
- System sizes: up to N=800 sites for the anti-ferromagnetic fuzzy model at g²=0.53
- Local Hilbert space dimension: p=4 for both truncations (ℓmax=1 and fuzzy sphere j=1/2)
- Kinetic term eigenvalues: H₀ᶜ = H₀ᶠ = diag(0,1,1,1) for both truncations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Alexandru 2021) by using open boundary conditions to probe deeper into the ultraviolet regime than was possible with periodic boundary conditions. It contrasts with the standard lattice discretization approach that samples field values at finite points (Patrascioiu 1997, Hasenfratz 2000), which breaks O(3) symmetry and whose critical behavior remains debated (Caracciolo 2001, Hasenfratz 2001). The results suggest that the fuzzy sphere truncation (Madore 1991) may provide a viable qubitization strategy for bosonic field theories, while the commonly used angular-momentum truncation (Bruckmann 2019, Liu 2021) fails at the lowest nontrivial cutoff, enabling future work on whether higher ℓmax values or other truncations can succeed.
