# A simple strategy for renormalization: QED at one-loop level

> We demonstrate our simple strategy for renormalization with QED at one-loop level, basing on an elaboration of the effective field theory philosophy. No artificial regularization or deformation of the original theory is introduced here and hence no manipulation of infinities, ambiguities arise instead of infinities. Ward identities first come to reduce the number of ambiguities, the residual ones could in principle be removed by imposing physical boundary conditions. Renormalization group equations arise as "decoupling theorems" in the underlying theory perspective. In addition, a technical theorem concerning routing of external momenta is also presented and illustrated with the self-energy and vertex function as examples.

## Key claim

The authors propose a renormalization strategy for quantum field theories that avoids any artificial regularization or deformation of the original theory, replacing infinities with local ambiguities that are to be fixed by physical boundary conditions; the method is demonstrated for QED at one-loop level, with the caveat that the approach relies on the existence of an underlying well-defined theory whose details are unknown.

## Method

The strategy determines ill-defined loop amplitudes by differentiating them with respect to external momenta enough times to produce convergent Feynman diagrams, then solving the resulting differential equations by integrating back. The general solution for each divergent diagram contains a polynomial in external momenta with ambiguous coefficients (integration constants), which are then constrained by Ward identities and physical boundary conditions. The authors apply this procedure to the electron self-energy, electron-photon vertex, and vacuum polarization at one-loop level in covariant gauge QED.

## Key numbers

- Self-energy superficial divergence degree: ω(Σ^(1)) = 1
- Vertex function superficial divergence degree: ω(Λ^(1)_μ) = 0
- Vacuum polarization superficial divergence degree: ω(Π^(1)_μν) = 2
- Ward identity constraint on agent constants: C_ψ + C_⊥ = (4 + 11ξ)/6, where ξ is the gauge parameter
- In Feynman gauge (ξ=1), the Ward identity requires C_ψ + C_⊥ = 5/2

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory philosophy to renormalization, building on earlier partial applications by the same group (Yang, 1998; Yang, 2002; Yang, 2005). The approach shares consensus with the mathematically motivated Epstein-Glaser (EG) approach (1973) that perturbative quantum field theories contain local ambiguous terms fixable only through boundary conditions. The method enables examination of symmetry issues like anomalies without being tied to specific regularization prescriptions, and the authors claim it can be applied beyond perturbative frameworks to nonperturbative problems.
