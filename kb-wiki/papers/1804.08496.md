# Particle number conserving BCS approach in the relativistic mean field model and its application to $^{32-74}$Ca

> A particle number conserving BCS approach (FBCS) is formulated in the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. It is shown that the so-obtained RMF+FBCS model can describe the weak pairing limit. We calculate the ground-state properties of the calcium isotopes $^{32-74}$Ca and compare the results with those obtained from the usual RMF+BCS model. Although the results are quite similar to each other, we observe an interesting phenomenon, i.e., for $^{54}$Ca, the FBCS approach can enhance the occupation probability of the $2p_{1/2}$ single particle level and slightly increases its radius, compared with the RMF+BCS model. This leads to an unusual scenario that although $^{54}$Ca is more bound with a spherical configuration but the corresponding size is not the most compact one. We anticipate that such a phenomenon might happen for other neutron rich nuclei and should be checked by further more systematic studies.

## Key claim

The RMF+FBCS model can describe the weak pairing limit and yields ground-state properties of calcium isotopes similar to the usual RMF+BCS model, but for $^{54}$Ca it enhances the occupation probability of the $2p_{1/2}$ single-particle level and slightly increases its radius, leading to a scenario where the nucleus is more bound with a spherical configuration yet not the most compact in size.

## Method

The authors formulated a particle-number-conserving BCS approach (FBCS) within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model using the NL3 effective force and a density-independent contact delta interaction for the pairing channel. They solved the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations self-consistently by expanding in a harmonic oscillator basis (12 shells for fermion fields, 20 shells for meson fields). The FBCS equations were derived by applying a number projection operator to the BCS wave function and varying the projected energy, with residuum integrals evaluated numerically using recursion relations.

## Key numbers

- The critical pairing strength below which the RMF+BCS model yields no non-trivial solution is about 250 MeV fm$^{-3}$ for $^{36}$Ca.
- The pairing strength was fixed at $V_0 = 350$ MeV fm$^{-3}$ in the RMF+BCS model and $V_0 = 274$ MeV fm$^{-3}$ in the RMF+FBCS model by fitting to odd-even mass differences of the calcium isotopic chain.
- For $^{54}$Ca, the increase in neutron r.m.s. radius in the RMF+FBCS model compared to RMF+BCS is about 0.02 fm.
- Near the neutron drip line ($N \ge 50$), the r.m.s. radii in the RMF+BCS model are slightly larger than those in the RMF+FBCS model, at the order of 0.05 fm.

## Context

This work extends the FBCS method, originally developed by Dietrich, Mang, and Pradal (1964), to the relativistic mean field model for the first time in a self-consistent manner. It builds on prior studies of pairing correlations in exotic nuclei using approaches such as the Lipkin-Nogami BCS method (PhysRevC.73.034308, PhysRevC.74.064309), the exact approach (Chen 2013), and the Shell-model-like approach (SLAP) in the RMF framework. The results enable future implementation of particle-number-conserving pairing treatments in coordinate-space or Woods-Saxon basis RMF models for more reliable descriptions of drip-line nuclei.
