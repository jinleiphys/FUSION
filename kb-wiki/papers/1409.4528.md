# Microscopic Study of Vorticities in Relativistic Chiral Fermions

> It is usually believed that unlike the external magnetic field which one can set directly, vorticity is a property of the flow of particles, which is indirectly controlled by external fields and initial conditions. Using the curved-space technics it is shown that the influence of the vorticity on the relativistic chiral fermions can indeed be controlled directly.

## Key claim

Using curved-space techniques, the influence of vorticity on relativistic chiral fermions can be controlled directly, contrary to the usual belief that vorticity is only an indirect property of particle flow. The paper demonstrates this by showing that specific choices of background metric components produce nonzero vorticity components, allowing direct control analogous to how external magnetic fields are applied.

## Method

The author formulates massless chiral fermions in a curved spacetime background with a stationary metric, using the ADM decomposition with lapse function and shift vector. The Dirac equation is solved perturbatively to first order in the background metric perturbations, with the shift vector components chosen to produce constant vorticity along the z-axis. The Hamiltonian is squared to identify interaction terms, and the Dirac equation is solved using WKB approximation in a regime where the energy is much larger than the shift vector.

## Key numbers

* The spin connection component $\Sigma^{xy}_{\quad 0} = -2\omega_z$ for a symmetric setup with $-\partial_x\beta^y = \partial_y\beta^x$, where $\omega^z \equiv -\frac{1}{2}\partial_y\beta_x$
* The momenta of spinors are shifted by $\pm\lambda\omega^z$ with $\lambda = \frac{1}{2}$, depending on chirality and vorticity direction
* The vortical dipole moment term in the squared Hamiltonian is $-2\mu\omega_z\sigma^z$, analogous to the magnetic dipole moment $\boldsymbol{\mu}_B\cdot\mathbf{B}$ with $\boldsymbol{\mu}_B = \frac{\boldsymbol{\sigma}}{2}$
* Energy eigenvalues for right-handed spinors: $\varepsilon_R + \mu + \beta^x p_x = \pm|\mathbf{p}| \mp \lambda\frac{p_z}{|\mathbf{p}|}\omega^z$, and for left-handed spinors: $\varepsilon_L + \mu + \beta^x p_x = \pm|\mathbf{p}| \pm \lambda\frac{p_z}{|\mathbf{p}|}\omega^z$

## Context

This work extends the chiral vortical effect (CVE) first realized by Vilenkin (1980) and later connected to the triangle anomaly by Son and Surowka. It provides a microscopic quantum field theory derivation of the CVE from first principles, complementing previous studies using gauge/gravity duality by Erdmenger, group theory by Roy, kinetic theory by Chen (2014), and topological invariants by Volovik. The approach enables future applications to 3D graphene and Weyl semimetals as noted by Turner, and addresses contradictions in the physical picture of anomaly-induced effects discussed by Landsteiner.
