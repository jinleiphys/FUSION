# Probing the QCD phase transition with chiral mixing in dilepton production

> We perform a systematic study of dilepton emission in a hot QCD medium based on three different scenarios of chiral mixing, each of which yields a characteristic structure in the vector spectral function. The in-medium spectral functions are accommodated into the state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations for a relativistic viscous fluid to calculate the dilepton production rate, fully accounting for the space-time evolution of a created fireball in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that the low-temperature theorem of chiral mixing extrapolated toward a chiral crossover, often used in the literature, leads to critical shortcomings: the inadequacy of width broadening, and a substantial overestimate of the dilepton yield maximized around the invariant mass of $M = 1.2$ GeV. The proper prescription offers a milder yet sizable increase in the window of $1.1 < M < 1.4$ GeV as the direct signature of chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the widely-used low-temperature theorem for chiral mixing, when extrapolated toward the chiral crossover temperature, produces a "false" chiral symmetry restoration signature that overestimates the dilepton yield by up to 25% at invariant mass M = 1.2 GeV, whereas the proper prescription with vanishing chiral mixing at the chiral crossover yields a milder but physically correct enhancement in the window 1.1 < M < 1.4 GeV.

## Method

The authors constructed three scenarios for the vector spectral function: without chiral symmetry restoration (CSR), with proper CSR where the rho and a1 masses become degenerate at the chiral crossover, and with "false CSR" based on naive extrapolation of the low-temperature mixing theorem. These spectral functions were embedded into state-of-the-art relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using TRENTo initial conditions and lattice QCD equations of state, with the dilepton production rate integrated over the full space-time evolution of the fireball for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in the 0-5% centrality window.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature T_chi = 154 MeV, taken from lattice QCD determinations by Aoki (2009) and Bazavov (2011)
- The false CSR scenario produces a maximal mixing at T = sqrt(3)*f_pi = 160 MeV, where the mixing parameter epsilon reaches 1/2
- The false CSR scenario overestimates the dilepton yield by 25% at M = 1.2 GeV compared to the proper CSR scenario
- The proper CSR scenario shows enhancement in the invariant mass window 1.1 < M < 1.4 GeV
- The vacuum mass difference between rho and a1 mesons is 0.47 GeV
- The false CSR scenario shows maximal fake contribution at M = 1.24 GeV, corresponding to the vacuum a1 mass
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm
- The hadronic phase contributes 70% of the thermal dileptons
- The integration covers rapidity |eta| < 0.8 and transverse momentum 0.2 < p_T < 5.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral mixing framework developed by Harada (2008) and contrasts the proper CSR scenario with the naive extrapolation of the low-temperature theorem used by NA60 (2018) and Geurts (2022). The results are directly relevant to upcoming dilepton measurements at the CERN SPS (NA60+) and LHC (ALICE), showing that the widely-accepted maximal mixing signature should be interpreted with caution. The study is restricted to hot and dilute medium relevant to LHC physics, where the two classes of chiral mixing (thermal and baryon-induced) can be separated, unlike in cold and dense matter where both effects coexist as discussed by Domokos (2007) and Harada (2009).
