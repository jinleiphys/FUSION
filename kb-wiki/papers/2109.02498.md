# Low-energy monopole strength in spherical and deformed nuclei : cluster and soft modes

> Background : Several recent experiments report significant low-energy isoscalar monopole strength, below the giant resonance, in various nuclei. In light $α$-conjugate nuclei, these low-energy resonances were recently interpreted as cluster vibration modes. However, the nature of these excitations in neutron-rich nuclei remain elusive. Purpose : The present work provides a systematic analysis of the low-energy monopole strength in isotopic chains, from Neon to Germanium, in order to monitor and understand its nature and conditions of emergence. Methods : We perform covariant quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations, formulated within the finite amplitude method (FAM), on top of constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) reference states. Results : Neutron excess leads to the appearance of low-energy excitations according to a systematic pattern reflecting the single-particle features of the underlying RHB reference state. With the onset of deformation, these low-energy resonances get split and give rise to more complex patterns, with possible mixing with the giant resonance. At lower energy, cluster-like excitations found in $N=Z$ systems survive in neutron-rich nuclei, with valence neutrons arranging in molecular-like orbitals. Finally, at very low energy, pair excitations are also found in superfluid nuclei, but remain negligible in most of the cases. Conclusions : The low-energy part of the monopole strength exhibits various modes, from cluster vibrations ($\sim$ 5-10 MeV) to components of the giant resonance downshifted by the onset of deformation, including soft modes ($\sim$ 10-15 MeV) as well as pair excitation ($<$ 5 MeV), with possible mixing, depending on neutron-excess, deformation, and pairing energy.

## Key claim

The low-energy part of the isoscalar monopole strength in nuclei from Neon to Germanium exhibits multiple distinct modes, cluster vibrations, soft modes, giant resonance components downshifted by deformation, and pair excitations, whose emergence and mixing depend on neutron excess, deformation, and pairing correlations, with the caveat that the calculations use a covariant quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a specific energy density functional (DD-PC1) and separable pairing force, so the results are model-dependent.

## Method

The authors performed covariant quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the finite amplitude method (FAM) on top of constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) reference states, employing the DD-PC1 energy density functional with a separable pairing force in momentum space. The calculations were carried out for even-even isotopes from Neon to Germanium, with the RHB equations solved in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis of 13 to 15 shells, and the strength functions were obtained with a Lorentzian smearing width of 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In $^{50}$Ca, a low-energy resonance appears at 12.8 MeV, dominated by the $2p_{3/2} \rightarrow 3p_{3/2}$ transition carrying 40% of the 2qp contribution.
- In $^{62}$Ca, a pairing-driven mode appears at 4.3 MeV, with dominant 2qp contributions from $1g_{9/2} \rightarrow 1g_{9/2}$ (30%), $2d_{5/2} \rightarrow 2d_{5/2}$ (25%), and $2s_{1/2} \rightarrow 2s_{1/2}$ (19%).
- In $^{58}$Ni, a low-energy mode emerges at 18.1 MeV, dominated by the $2p_{3/2} \rightarrow 3p_{3/2}$ transition (42%).
- In $^{80}$Ni, a mode appears at 10.5 MeV, dominated by the $2d_{5/2} \rightarrow 3d_{5/2}$ transition (32%).
- The ratio of soft-mode integrated strength to total monopole strength can reach close to 80% for very neutron-rich Nickel, Iron, or Sulfur nuclei.
- In $^{32}$Mg, the spherical resonance at 15.5 MeV splits into four components at deformation $\beta_2 = 0.1$, located at 14.0, 15.4, 16.5, and 17.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-energy isoscalar monopole strength in light $\alpha$-conjugate nuclei, where cluster vibration modes were identified by Chiba et al. (2015, 2016) and Kanada-En'yo et al. (2016, 2020), and soft modes in neutron-rich spherical systems studied by Paar et al. (2007) and Gamess et al. (2019). The present systematic analysis across isotopic chains from Neon to Germanium, using a single covariant QRPA framework, enables a unified understanding of how neutron excess, deformation, and pairing correlations interplay to produce cluster vibrations (5-10 MeV), soft modes (10-15 MeV), giant resonance components downshifted by deformation, and pair excitations (below 5 MeV).
