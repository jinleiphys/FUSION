# Light-quark mass dependence of the nucleon axial charge and pion-nucleon scattering phenomenology

> The light-quark mass dependence of the nucleon axial isovector charge ($g_A$) has been studied up to next-to-next-to-leading order, $O(p^4)$, in relativistic chiral perturbation theory using extended-on-mass-shell renormalization, without and with explicit $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom. We show that in the $Δ$-less case, at this order, the flat trend of $g_A (M_π)$ exhibited by state-of-the-art lattice QCD (LQCD) results cannot be reproduced using low energy constants (LECs) extracted from pion-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering. A satisfactory description of these LQCD data is only achieved in the theory with $Δ$. From this fit we report $g_A (M_{π\rm{(phys)}}) = 1.260 \pm 0.012$, close to the experimental result, and $d_{16}= -0.88\pm 0.88$ GeV$^{-2}$, in agreement with its empirical value. The large uncertainties are of theoretical origin, reflecting the difference between $O(p^3)$ and $O(p^4)$ that still persists at large $M_π$ in presence of the $Δ$.

## Key claim

The flat trend of the nucleon axial charge as a function of pion mass exhibited by state-of-the-art lattice QCD results cannot be reproduced at next-to-next-to-leading order using low-energy constants extracted from pion-nucleon scattering phenomenology unless explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom are included in the chiral perturbation theory calculation.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the nucleon axial isovector charge up to next-to-next-to-leading order, O(p^4), using extended-on-mass-shell renormalization. They computed the pion-mass dependence both without and with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, incorporating contributions from Feynman diagrams at O(p), O(p^3), and O(p^4) orders, and performed fits to lattice QCD data from CalLat 18, Mainz 19, RQCD 19, and NME 21 collaborations, including theoretical truncation errors estimated from the convergence pattern.

## Key numbers

- g_A at the physical pion mass from the fit with explicit Delta: g_A(M_pi(phys)) = 1.260 +/- 0.012
- d_16 low-energy constant from the fit with explicit Delta: d_16 = -0.88 +/- 0.88 GeV^{-2}
- At O(p^3) without Delta, the chiral-limit axial coupling is g = 1.251 +/- 0.051; at O(p^4) without Delta, g = 1.089 +/- 0.030
- d_16 from phenomenological input at O(p^3) without Delta: -2.2 +/- 1.1 GeV^{-2}; at O(p^4) without Delta: -1.86 +/- 0.80 GeV^{-2}
- Low-energy constants c_1 through c_4 from phenomenological input: c_1 = -0.89 +/- 0.06 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 3.38 +/- 0.15 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.59 +/- 0.09 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.31 +/- 0.13 GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pion-mass dependence of g_A using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Bernard 2006, Procura 2006) and relativistic approaches (Yao 2017, Lutz 2020) by performing a complete O(p^4) calculation with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom using extended-on-mass-shell renormalization. The results demonstrate that the Delta resonance is essential for describing modern lattice QCD data, and the extracted d_16 value, though with large theoretical uncertainties, is consistent with empirical determinations from pion-nucleon scattering analyses such as Siemens (2017). The work enables improved chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results and provides a systematic estimate of truncation uncertainties for future studies of nucleon structure.
