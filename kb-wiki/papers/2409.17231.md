# Small-$x$ gluon GPD constrained from deeply virtual $J/ψ$ production and gluon PDF through universal-moment parameterization

> We phenomenologically constrain the small-$x$ and small-$ξ$ gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) with the deeply virtual $J/ψ$ production (DV$J/ψ$P) in the framework of GPDs through universal moment parameterization (GUMP). We use a hybrid cross-section formula combining collinear factorization to the next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy of the strong coupling $α_s$, with corrections from non-relativistic QCD to account for the power corrections due to the heavy $J/ψ$ mass. We reach reasonable fit to the measured differential cross-sections of DV$J/ψ$P by H1 at Hadron-Electron Ring Accelerator (HERA) as well as forward gluon PDFs from JAM22 global analysis. We find that both NLO and non-relativistic corrections are significant for heavy vector meson productions. Of course, the gluon GPD we obtain still contain considerable freedom in need of inputs from other constraints, particularly in the distribution-amplitude-like region.

## Key claim

The authors phenomenologically constrain the small-$x$ and small-$\xi$ gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) using deeply virtual $J/\psi$ production data from HERA and forward gluon PDFs from JAM22, but they explicitly note that the extracted gluon GPD still contains "considerable freedom" and requires additional constraints, particularly in the distribution-amplitude-like region.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid cross-section formula that combines collinear factorization at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strong coupling $\alpha_s$ with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) corrections to account for power corrections from the heavy $J/\psi$ mass. They implement the GPDs through the Universal Moment Parameterization (GUMP) framework, which uses conformal moment expansions, and perform a fit to the H1 collaboration's differential cross-section measurements of deeply virtual $J/\psi$ production at HERA together with forward gluon PDF constraints from the JAM22 global analysis.

## Key numbers

- The $J/\psi$ decay constant $f_{J/\psi} \approx 0.41$ GeV, derived from the leptonic decay width $\Gamma[J/\psi \rightarrow e^+ e^-] = 5.53 \pm 0.10$ keV
- The ratio of longitudinal to transverse photon cross-section in the NRQCD framework is predicted as $R = Q^2 / M_{J/\psi}^2$
- For the highest $Q^2$ bin in HERA data, the ratio $M_{J/\psi}^2 / \langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1/2$, indicating that meson mass corrections are sizable
- The skewness parameter $\xi$ reaches values as low as $10^{-4}$ in the HERA kinematics

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GUMP framework analysis (Guo 2023) which constrained up and down quark GPDs using DVCS data from JLab and HERA, lattice calculations, and PDFs, but only constrained gluon GPDs in the forward (PDF) limit. The present analysis adds off-forward gluon GPD constraints from deeply virtual $J/\psi$ production, complementing prior studies of exclusive $J/\psi$ production in various frameworks including those by Ryskin (1992), Ivanov (2004), Chen (2019), and Flett (2021). The results enable future global analyses of GPDs that could combine constraints from light and heavy meson productions, and will be relevant for the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) program.
