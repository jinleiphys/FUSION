# Quark-antiquark resonances in the NJL model

> The problem of the $ρ$ meson resonance solution in the extended NJL model is studied. It has been shown previously that the solution for the $ρ$ meson changes smoothly from a bound state to a "virtual state" when the vector channel attraction becomes too weak to support bound state solutions. We have found that together with these solutions a separate resonance branch appears which is not connected smoothly with the bound state solutions. At small values of constituent quark mass, $m\sim 200$ MeV, it corresponds to the physical vector meson resonance solution in the quark-continuum region. The $a_1$ and $σ$ resonances are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $\rho$ meson in the extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model possesses a separate resonance branch in addition to the previously known bound-state and "virtual state" branches; this resonance branch is not smoothly connected to the bound-state branch and corresponds to the physical $\rho$ meson at small constituent quark masses ($m \sim 200$ MeV), contrary to earlier claims that no such resonance solution exists.

## Method

The authors study the propagator poles of $\rho$, $a_1$, and $\sigma$ mesons in the extended NJL model with spin-1 mesons, using a Pauli-Villars regulator with two subtractions. They develop a method to find resonance parameters without analytically continuing into the complex $p^2$ plane, based on a self-consistency condition $\lambda(q^2)=0$ derived from a Taylor expansion of the inverse propagator around a real point $q^2$. This method is compared to the spectral-function (SF) method and to exact solutions of the complex Bethe-Salpeter equation on the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

*   For large constituent quark mass ($m = 390$ MeV): $f_\pi = 93.3$ MeV, $m_\pi = 139$ MeV, $m_\rho = 776$ MeV (bound state), $m_{a_1} = 1042 - i358$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 439 - i0.28$ MeV.
*   For small constituent quark mass ($m = 180$ MeV): $f_\pi = 93.3$ MeV, $m_\pi = 139$ MeV, $m_\rho = 674 - i300$ MeV, $m_{a_1} = 802 - i289$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 378 - i2.6$ MeV.
*   The empirical $\rho$ meson mass is taken as $m_\rho \simeq 770$ MeV and the $a_1$ meson mass as $m_{a_1} \simeq 1100$ MeV.
*   The pion decay constant and pion mass are fixed to $f_\pi \simeq 93$ MeV and $m_\pi \simeq 139$ MeV.
*   For the large quark mass case, the parameters are $G_S = 9.41$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_V = 10.27$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\widehat{m} = 3.9$ MeV, and $\Lambda = 1$ GeV.
*   For the small quark mass case, the parameters are $G_S = 0.86$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_V = 6.65$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\widehat{m} = 1$ MeV, and $\Lambda = 2.8$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of resonance solutions in the NJL model by Takizawa et al. (1991), who found a bound-state to "virtual state" transition for the $\rho$ meson but could not find a resonance solution. The authors show that a separate resonance branch exists for all values of the vector coupling $G_V$, and that for small constituent quark masses ($m \sim 200$ MeV) this branch corresponds to the physical $\rho$ meson. This finding provides a justification for bosonization approaches to the NJL model (e.g., Bijnens et al., 1994) that use small quark masses and introduce an asymptotic $\rho$ meson field, proving for the first time that such a resonance state exists in the model. The method developed here can be used to find narrow resonance solutions without complex-plane analytic continuation.
