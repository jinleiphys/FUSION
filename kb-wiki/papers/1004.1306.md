# Proton-3He elastic scattering at low energies and the "A_y Puzzle"

> The Kohn variational principle and the hyperspherical harmonic technique are applied to study p-3He elastic scattering at low energies. Preliminary results obtained using several interaction models are reported. The calculations are compared to a recent phase shift analysis performed at the Triangle University Nuclear Laboratory and to the available experimental data. Using a three-nucleon interaction derived from chiral perturbation theory at N2LO, we have found a noticeable reduction of the discrepancy observed for the A_y observable.

## Key claim

Using a three-nucleon interaction derived from chiral perturbation theory at N2LO (the I-N3LO/N-N2LO model) produces a noticeable reduction of the discrepancy in the proton analyzing power \(A_y\) for \(p\)-\(^{3}\)He elastic scattering, though a residual underprediction of approximately 15% at the peak remains rather than a full resolution of the puzzle.

## Method

The authors applied the Kohn variational principle with a hyperspherical harmonic (HH) expansion of the four-nucleon scattering wave function, solving for \(p\)-\(^{3}\)He elastic scattering at low energies. They computed phase shifts and observables using four interaction models: the Argonne \(v_{18}\) (AV18) and the chiral I-N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials, each with and without a three-nucleon force (Urbana IX for AV18, and the N2LO chiral 3N force for I-N3LO). The HH basis was expanded in hyperradial functions using Laguerre polynomials, and the S-matrix elements were determined by making the Kohn functional stationary.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts for \(n\)-\(^{3}\)H elastic scattering at \(E_n = 3.5\) MeV using the I-N3LO potential: the HH calculation gave \({}^{1}S_0 = -65.66^\circ\) and the FY calculation gave \(-65.54^\circ\); for \({}^{3}P_1\), HH gave \(40.98^\circ\) and FY gave \(40.94^\circ\).
- Ratio \(A_y^{\text{theor}}/A_y^{\text{expt}}\) at the peak for the I-N3LO/N-N2LO model: 0.87 at \(E_p = 2.25\) MeV, 0.88 at \(E_p = 4\) MeV, and 0.86 at \(E_p = 5.54\) MeV.
- Ratio \(A_y^{\text{theor}}/A_y^{\text{expt}}\) at the peak for the AV18 model: 0.64 at \(E_p = 2.25\) MeV, 0.65 at \(E_p = 4\) MeV, and 0.67 at \(E_p = 5.54\) MeV.
- Ratio \(A_y^{\text{theor}}/A_y^{\text{expt}}\) at the peak for the AV18/UIX model: 0.66 at \(E_p = 2.25\) MeV, 0.70 at \(E_p = 4\) MeV, and 0.71 at \(E_p = 5.54\) MeV.
- Ratio \(A_y^{\text{theor}}/A_y^{\text{expt}}\) at the peak for the I-N3LO model: 0.66 at \(E_p = 2.25\) MeV, 0.70 at \(E_p = 4\) MeV, and 0.71 at \(E_p = 5.54\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior four-nucleon scattering calculations using the hyperspherical harmonic method, which had previously been limited to local potentials like AV18 (Viviani et al., 2001; Lazauskas et al., 2005), and now treats non-local potentials such as the chiral I-N3LO interaction. The results address the "\(A_y\) Puzzle" in \(p\)-\(^{3}\)He scattering, a discrepancy similar to the well-known \(N\)-\(d\) \(A_y\) puzzle reported by Kievsky and collaborators (1996) and others. The findings enable further tests of chiral three-nucleon forces in four-nucleon systems and suggest that the I-N3LO/N-N2LO model reduces the \(A_y\) discrepancy in \(p\)-\(^{3}\)He more effectively than in the \(N\)-\(d\) case, where the same model produces only a small increase in \(A_y\) (as shown for \(p\)-\(d\) at \(E_p = 3\) MeV).
