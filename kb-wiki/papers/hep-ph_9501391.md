# $K \to 2 π$ Decay in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> We study the $K \to 2 π$ decays using the $U_L(3) \times U_R(3)$ version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the effective $ΔS = 1$ nonleptonic weak interaction. The $ΔI = {3/2}$ amplitude is in reasonable agreement with experimental data. On the other hand, the calculated $ΔI = {1/2}$ amplitudes strongly depend on the mass of the low-lying scalar-isoscalar $σ$ meson, and therefore give a strong constraint on the parameters of the model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $\Delta I = 1/2$ enhancement in $K \to 2\pi$ decays can be reproduced in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, but only if the scalar-isoscalar $\sigma$ meson mass is close to the kaon mass; the calculated $\Delta I = 1/2$ amplitudes depend strongly on this mass, which provides a constraint on the model parameters rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors use a $U_L(3) \times U_R(3)$ Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an effective $\Delta S = 1$ nonleptonic weak interaction lagrangian, calculating $K \to 2\pi$ decay amplitudes via Feynman diagrams in the ladder approximation without derivative expansion. Meson properties (masses, decay constants, quark-meson couplings) are obtained from Bethe-Salpeter type equations, and the model parameters (current quark masses, coupling constant $G_S$, cutoff $\Lambda$) are fitted to reproduce $m_\pi$, $m_K$, and $f_\pi$. The calculation includes both leading and next-to-leading terms in the $1/N_C$ expansion.

## Key numbers

- Experimental $K^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0$ amplitude: $1.83 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (pure $\Delta I = 3/2$)
- Experimental $K^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-$ amplitude: $2.76 \times 10^{-7}$ GeV
- Experimental $K^0 \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ amplitude: $2.63 \times 10^{-7}$ GeV
- Fitted NJL parameters: $m_u = m_d = 7.1$ MeV, $m_s = 178.2$ MeV, $\Lambda = 0.844$ GeV, $\Lambda^2 N_c G_S / (4\pi^2) = 0.66$
- Resulting constituent quark masses: $M_u = M_d = 286.2$ MeV, $M_s = 529.3$ MeV
- Calculated $K^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0$ amplitude: $2.52 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (about 38% larger than experiment)
- Calculated $K^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-$ amplitude: $2.65 \times 10^{-7}$ GeV
- Calculated $K^0 \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ amplitude: $2.30 \times 10^{-7}$ GeV
- Predicted $f_K = 101$ MeV (about 11% smaller than observed)
- Ratio $m_s/m_u = 25.1$, consistent with chiral perturbation theory value $25 \pm 2.5$
- With $\sigma \to 2\pi$ width $\Gamma = 500$ MeV, $K^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-$ amplitude reduces to $8.59 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV and $K^0 \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ to $6.52 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier bosonization approach of Morozumi et al. (1990) by performing full Feynman diagram calculations without derivative expansion, including $1/N_C$ corrections, and using current quark masses consistent with observed Nambu-Goldstone boson properties. The results contrast with chiral perturbation theory treatments by Bardeen et al. (1987) and others, showing that the $\Delta I = 1/2$ enhancement arises from a low-lying $\sigma$ meson pole rather than from chiral loop effects. The study enables future simultaneous analyses of $K \to 2\pi$ decays and $\pi\pi$ scattering, incorporating two-pion intermediate states to better understand the role of the $\sigma$ meson.
