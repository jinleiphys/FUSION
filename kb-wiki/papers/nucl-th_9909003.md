# Quantum Groups and Their Applications in Nuclear Physics

> Quantum algebras are a mathematical tool which provides us with a class of symmetries wider than that of Lie algebras, which are contained in the former as a special case. After a self-contained introduction to the necessary mathematical tools (q-numbers, q-analysis, q-oscillators, q-algebras), the SUq(2) rotator model and its extensions, the construction of deformed exactly soluble models (u(3)>so(3) model, Interacting Boson Model, Moszkowski model), the 3-dimensional q-deformed harmonic oscillator and its relation to the nuclear shell model, the use of deformed bosons in the description of pairing correlations, and the symmetries of the anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies, which underly the structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, are discussed in some detail. A brief description of similar applications to the structure of molecules and of atomic clusters, as well as an outlook are also given.

## Key claim

Quantum algebras provide a class of symmetries richer than Lie algebras, which are contained as a special case when the deformation parameter \(q\) is set to unity; the paper reviews whether these deformed symmetries can describe nuclear structure phenomena that lie outside the realm of ordinary Lie symmetries, but the evidence presented is phenomenological and model-dependent rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The paper is a comprehensive review that first introduces the mathematical tools of \(q\)-numbers, \(q\)-analysis, \(q\)-deformed oscillators, and quantum algebras (su\(_q\)(2), su\(_q\)(1,1), and generalized deformed algebras). It then applies these tools to nuclear structure problems: the su\(_q\)(2) rotator model for rotational bands, deformed versions of exactly soluble models (u(3)\(\supset\)so(3) model, Interacting Boson Model, Moszkowski model), the 3-dimensional \(q\)-deformed harmonic oscillator and its relation to the Nilsson modified harmonic oscillator, deformed bosons for pairing correlations, and the symmetries of anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillators with rational frequency ratios underlying superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

* The \(q\)-number definition: \([x] = (q^x - q^{-x})/(q - q^{-1})\); for \(q\) real with \(q = e^\tau\), this becomes \([x] = \sinh(\tau x)/\sinh(\tau)\); for \(q\) a phase factor with \(q = e^{i\tau}\), it becomes \([x] = \sin(\tau x)/\sin(\tau)\) (Section 2).
* The \(q\)-deformed harmonic oscillator energy eigenvalues: for \(q\) real, \(E(n) = (\hbar\omega/2) \sinh(\tau(n+1/2))/\sinh(\tau/2)\); for \(q = e^{i\tau}\), \(E(n) = (\hbar\omega/2) \sin(\tau(n+1/2))/\sin(\tau/2)\); both reduce to \(E(n) = \hbar\omega(n+1/2)\) when \(q \to 1\) (Section 10).
* The \(Q\)-deformed harmonic oscillator energy eigenvalues: \(E(n) = (\hbar\omega/2)([n]_Q + [n+1]_Q)\) where \([n]_Q = (Q^n - 1)/(Q-1)\); the commutation relation is \([b, b^\dagger] = Q^N\) (Section 11).
* For the su\(_q\)(2) rotator model, the paper compares it to the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), but no specific numerical energy values or transition strengths are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on quantum algebras in nuclear physics, following an earlier review cited as RJP109, and covers applications that began with the introduction of the \(q\)-deformed harmonic oscillator by Biedenharn (1989), Macfarlane (1989), and Sun and Fu (1989), with earlier equivalent versions by Arik and Coon (1976) and Kuryshkin (1980). The paper enables further development of deformed collective models for rotational and vibrational nuclear spectra, including superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands, and connects to applications in molecular structure and atomic clusters.
