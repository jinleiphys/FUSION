# Higher-rank discrete symmetries in the IBM. I Octahedral shapes: general Hamiltonian

> In the context of the interacting boson model with $s$, $d$ and $g$ bosons, the conditions for obtaining an intrinsic shape with octahedral symmetry are derived for a general Hamiltonian with up to two-body interactions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the conditions under which a general two-body Hamiltonian in the $sdg$-interacting boson model (IBM) can produce an intrinsic nuclear shape with octahedral symmetry, showing that such an extremum exists for any Hamiltonian except in the pathological case of zero $s$-$g$ mixing, where additional sign conditions on combinations of coefficients must hold.

## Method

The authors construct the most general coherent state for the $sdg$-IBM using five shape variables ($\beta_2$, $\beta_4$, $\gamma_2$, $\gamma_4$, $\delta_4$) and compute the classical limit (energy surface) of the general two-body Hamiltonian as a function of these variables. They then impose the geometric conditions for octahedral symmetry, vanishing quadrupole deformation and specific ratios of hexadecapole deformation parameters, and solve the extremum conditions (first derivatives of the energy surface equal zero) to find constraints on the 22 independent parameters of the classical energy surface.

## Key numbers

- Octahedral symmetry requires $a_{42}=0$ and $a_{44}/a_{40}=\pm\sqrt{5/14}$ for the hexadecapole deformation parameters.
- The non-zero solution for the hexadecapole deformation parameter at an octahedral extremum is $\beta_4^*=\pm\sqrt{(2c'_{00}-c'_{02})/(-c'_{02}+2c'_{04}+38c^{00}_{04})}$, valid when the $s$-$g$ mixing coefficient $c^{00}_{03}=0$.
- The classical energy surface depends on 22 independent parameters (six coefficients $c'_{kl}$ and 16 coefficients $c^{ij}_{kl}$), reduced from the 35 parameters (three single-boson energies plus 32 two-body interactions) of the quantum Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the study of higher-rank discrete symmetries in nuclear shapes, previously investigated in mean-field approaches by Li and Dudek (1994) and Dudek et al. (2002-2006), into the algebraic framework of the interacting boson model. It provides the foundation for identifying octahedral minima in the $sdg$-IBM, enabling future studies that will examine specific symmetry Hamiltonians (Part II of the series) and potentially guide experimental searches for nuclei with octahedral deformation, complementing the tetrahedral symmetry studies in the $sf$-IBM mentioned in the introduction.
