# Drastic change in inelastic scattering depending on the development of dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be

> We investigated the development and breaking of the dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be by analyzing the elastic and inelastic scatterings with a framework combing the microscopic structure and reaction models. For studying the structure, the $^{10}$Be nucleus was constructed under the assumption of a four-body ($α+ α+ n + n$) cluster model. In this work, we focused on the change in the inner structure for the 0$_1^+$, 2$_1^+$, and 2$_2^+$ states when the strength of the spin-orbit interaction is varied. The inner structure, including various physical quantities such as energy, radius, and transition strength, is drastically influenced by the strength of the spin-orbit interaction. In particular, the development and breaking of the dineutron correlation is governed by the spin-orbit strength. The differences in the inner structure can be manifested by applying the obtained wave functions to elastic and inelastic scatterings with a proton target at $E/A =$ 59.4 and 200 MeV. Although the 0$_1^+$ and 2$_1^+$ states are significantly influenced by the spin-orbit strength of the nuclear structure calculation, the elastic and inelastic cross sections are not much affected. On the other hand, the inelastic cross section of the 2$_2^+$ state depends greatly on the spin-orbit strength of the structure calculation. Thus, we discovered a way to measure the degree of the development of dineutron cluster structure based on its sensitivity to the inelastic cross section of the 2$_2^+$ state of $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim

The degree of development or breaking of the dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be can be measured through the sensitivity of the inelastic scattering cross section of the 2$_2^+$ state to the spin-orbit strength used in the structure calculation, even though the elastic and inelastic cross sections of the 0$_1^+$ and 2$_1^+$ states are not much affected by this variation.

## Method

The authors constructed $^{10}$Be wave functions using a four-body ($\alpha + \alpha + n + n$) microscopic cluster model with the stochastic multiconfiguration mixing method, artificially varying the spin-orbit interaction strength $V_{\rm LS}$ from 0 to 4000 MeV. They then computed diagonal and transition densities from these wave functions and performed microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations using the complex $G$-matrix interaction MPa to obtain elastic and inelastic cross sections for proton scattering at $E/A =$ 59.4 and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the 0$_1^+$ state changed drastically as $V_{\rm LS}$ was varied from 0 to 4000 MeV, with larger $V_{\rm LS}$ giving stronger binding.
- The calculated matter radius of $^{10}$Be was very close to the experimental value of 2.39 $\pm$ 0.02 fm (Tanihata 1985) around $V_{\rm LS}$ = 2500 MeV.
- The calculated proton radius was very close to the experimental value of 2.357 $\pm$ 0.018 fm (Nortershauser 2009) around $V_{\rm LS}$ = 2500 MeV.
- The excitation energy of the 2$_2^+$ state rapidly increased with spin-orbit strength, matching experimental data around $V_{\rm LS}$ = 2000 MeV.
- The inelastic cross section of the 2$_2^+$ state at $E/A =$ 200 MeV showed a drastic dependence on the $V_{\rm LS}$ value used in the structure calculation, while elastic and 2$_1^+$ inelastic cross sections remained almost constant.

## Context

This work extends the dineutron correlation studies in $^{10}$Be by Itagaki (2002), who showed that the persistence of the dineutron cluster is sensitive to spin-orbit strength even when neutron binding energy is kept constant. The present study combines microscopic structure and reaction models to predict that the 2$_2^+$ inelastic cross section can serve as an experimental observable for the dineutron correlation degree. This enables future experimental measurements of the 2$_2^+$ state in $^{10}$Be to directly probe the competition between dineutron clustering and spin-orbit-driven molecular orbital structures.
