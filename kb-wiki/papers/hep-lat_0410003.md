# Thermal Phase Transition in the NJL Model of QCD

> We present results from numerical studies of the NJL model with two massless quarks at nonzero temperature. We show that the model undergoes a second order chiral phase transition which belongs to the 3d O(4) spin model universality class.

## Key claim
The NJL model with two massless quarks undergoes a second-order chiral phase transition consistent with the 3d O(4) universality class, but the fermionic substructure of composite mesons may cause a maximal violation of bosonic character near T_c, and the non-trivial scaling region is suppressed by a factor proportional to 1/N_c.

## Method
Lattice simulations of the NJL model were performed using the Hybrid Monte Carlo method with staggered fermions on asymmetric lattices of volume L_s^3 × L_t, with fixed L_t=4 and L_s ranging from 8 to 54. The temperature was varied by changing the lattice spacing a(g), and finite-size scaling (FSS) analysis was applied to the susceptibility χ and Binder cumulant U_L to extract critical exponents and the critical coupling β_c. Simulations in the broken phase on 4 × 54^3 lattices with N_c=8 and N_c=16 were used to study the order parameter Σ as a function of β.

## Key numbers
- Critical coupling β_c = 0.52579(3) from susceptibility fits, and β_c = 0.5261(1) from Binder cumulant fits.
- Correlation length exponent ν = 0.74(3) from susceptibility fits, and ν = 0.69(4) from Binder cumulant fits; these are close to the 3d O(4) values ν = 0.748(9).
- Ratio γ/ν = 1.95(1) from susceptibility fits, close to the 3d O(4) value γ/ν = 1.9746(38).
- Binder cumulant universal value U_* = 0.616(2), close to the 3d O(4) value 0.636(1).
- For N_c=8, the order parameter exponent β_mag = 0.360(15) extracted from data in the range β = 0.520–0.5225, close to the 3d O(4) exponent β_mag = 0.3836(46).

## Context
This work extends the dimensional reduction scenario previously demonstrated for the (2+1)d Z_2-symmetric Gross-Neveu model (Strouthos, 2004) to the NJL model with continuous chiral symmetry, supporting the Pisarski and Wilczek (1990) conjecture that the QCD chiral transition belongs to the 3d O(4) universality class. The results show that for N_c=8 the transition is described by 3d O(4) exponents, while in the infinite N_c limit mean-field exponents dominate (Kocic, 1993), with the non-trivial scaling region suppressed by a factor ∼1/N_c. This enables future lattice QCD studies to check whether the same suppression of the critical region occurs in full QCD.
