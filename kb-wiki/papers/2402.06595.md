# Damping of density oscillations from bulk viscosity in quark matter

> We study the damping of density oscillations in the quark matter phase that might occur in compact stars. To this end we compute the bulk viscosity and the associated damping time in three-flavor quark matter, considering both nonleptonic and semileptonic electroweak processes. We use two different equations of state of quark matter, more precisely, the MIT bag model and perturbative QCD, including the leading-order corrections in the strong coupling constant. We analyze the dependence of our results on the density, temperature and value of strange quark mass in each case. We then find that the maximum of the bulk viscosity is in the range of temperature from 0.01 to 0.1 MeV for frequencies around 1 kHz, while the associated minimal damping times of the density oscillations at those temperatures might be in the range of few to hundreds milliseconds. Our results suggest that bulk viscous damping might be relevant in the postmerger phase after the collision of two neutron stars if deconfined matter is achieved in the process.

## Key claim
The authors find that bulk viscous damping from electroweak processes in three-flavor quark matter could be relevant in the postmerger phase of neutron star mergers if deconfined quark matter is achieved, but only for temperatures in the range of 0.01 to 0.1 MeV and oscillation frequencies around 1 kHz, where the minimal damping times range from a few to hundreds of milliseconds.

## Method
The authors compute the bulk viscosity of three-flavor quark matter in the neutrino-transparent regime by solving linearized conservation equations for strange quark and electron number densities, including both nonleptonic and semileptonic electroweak processes. They use two equations of state: the MIT bag model with massless up and down quarks and a variable strange quark mass (100-300 MeV), and perturbative QCD including leading-order corrections in the strong coupling constant. The damping time of baryon density oscillations is then derived from the bulk viscosity using the energy dissipation rate.

## Key numbers
- Maximum bulk viscosity at n_B,0/n_0 = 3, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 1 kHz: 2.73 × 10^28 gr cm^{-1} s^{-1} at T = 7.9 × 10^{-2} MeV
- Minimum damping time at n_B,0/n_0 = 3, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 1 kHz: 213.24 milliseconds
- Minimum damping time at n_B,0/n_0 = 3, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 10 kHz: 21.32 milliseconds
- Minimum damping time at n_B,0/n_0 = 3, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 0.1 kHz: 2132.42 milliseconds
- At n_B,0/n_0 = 5, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 1 kHz: maximum bulk viscosity 2.75 × 10^28 gr cm^{-1} s^{-1} at T = 6.2 × 10^{-2} MeV, minimum damping time 420.14 milliseconds
- At n_B,0/n_0 = 6, m_s = 100 MeV, and ω/2π = 1 kHz: maximum bulk viscosity 2.76 × 10^28 gr cm^{-1} s^{-1} at T = 5.7 × 10^{-2} MeV, minimum damping time 535.37 milliseconds
- Electron number density at n_B,0/n_0 = 3 and m_s = 100 MeV: 1.25 × 10^{-5} n_0
- Strange quark mass values explored: 100 to 300 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of quark matter bulk viscosity, which were primarily focused on damping of r-modes in isolated compact stars (Sawyer 1989, Madsen 1992, Alford 2006). It is complementary to similar studies for nuclear matter by Alford and Harris (2019) and Alford (2023), where direct and modified URCA processes mediate the bulk viscosity. The results enable inclusion of bulk viscous damping in numerical simulations of viscous relativistic hydrodynamics for neutron star mergers, an effort recently initiated by Chabanov (2023). The authors note that perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state only converge at densities around 40 times nuclear saturation density (Kurkela 2010, Gorda 2023), which are not realistic for astrophysical settings, motivating the use of the MIT bag model as an alternative.
