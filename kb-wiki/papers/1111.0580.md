# Thermodynamics of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories in 2+1 dimensions II - The deconfined phase

> We present a non-perturbative study of the equation of state in the deconfined phase of Yang-Mills theories in D=2+1 dimensions. We introduce a holographic model, based on the improved holographic QCD model, from which we derive a non-trivial relation between the order of the deconfinement phase transition and the behavior of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor as a function of the temperature T. We compare the theoretical predictions of this holographic model with a new set of high-precision numerical results from lattice simulations of SU(N) theories with N=2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 colors. The latter reveal that, similarly to the D=3+1 case, the bulk equilibrium thermodynamic quantities (pressure, trace of the energy-momentum tensor, energy density and entropy density) exhibit nearly perfect proportionality to the number of gluons, and can be successfully compared with the holographic predictions in a broad range of temperatures. Finally, we also show that, again similarly to the D=3+1 case, the trace of the energy-momentum tensor appears to be proportional to T^2 in a wide temperature range, starting from approximately 1.2 T_c, where T_c denotes the critical deconfinement temperature.

## Key claim

The holographic model predicts that, in the deconfined phase of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories in 2+1 dimensions, the normalized interaction measure falls off as a power law in T/T_c, where the power is determined by the parameter α controlling the IR asymptotics of the geometry, and this power is not directly related to the spacetime dimensionality but rather to the nature of the deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model based on improved holographic QCD (IHQCD) in 2+1 dimensions, using a bottom-up approach with a metric and dilaton field. They make an Ansatz for the scale factor b(r) = (ℓ/r) exp[-(rΛ)^α] with α > 1, derive the black-hole geometry and Hawking temperature, and compute the interaction measure from the entropy via the first law of thermodynamics. They compare these holographic predictions with high-precision lattice simulations of SU(N) gauge theories for N = 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 colors, extrapolating to the thermodynamic and continuum limits.

## Key numbers

- The trace of the energy-momentum tensor appears proportional to T^2 in a wide temperature range starting from approximately 1.2 T_c, where T_c denotes the critical deconfinement temperature.
- The holographic model predicts that for α = 3/2, the normalized interaction measure Δ/(N^2 T^3) follows a specific curve that is compared to lattice results (shown in fig. 6 of the paper).
- For large T, the normalized interaction measure behaves as Δ/(N^2 T^D) ∝ (T/T_c)^{-α} for α ≠ D, with the constants c_1 = 4π(4π M_P ℓ/D)^{D-1} and c_2 = (D-1)(D/(4π c_0))^α, where c_0 relates T_c to the scale Λ via T_c = c_0 Λ.

## Context

This work extends the study of SU(N) Yang-Mills thermodynamics from 3+1 dimensions (previously studied by Panero (2009) and others) to the 2+1-dimensional case, finding similar qualitative behavior including the T^2 scaling of the trace anomaly. The holographic model generalizes the IHQCD approach of Gursoy, Kiritsis, and Nitti (2008) to lower dimensions, and the lattice results confirm that bulk thermodynamic quantities exhibit nearly perfect proportionality to the number of gluons, similar to the 3+1-dimensional case. This enables future detailed holographic model-building to fit the full lattice data and provides a test-bed for applying holographic methods to strongly coupled systems in condensed matter physics.
