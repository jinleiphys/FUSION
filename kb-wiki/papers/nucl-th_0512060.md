# Bose-Einstein or HBT correlation signature of a second order QCD phase transition

> For particles emerging from a second order QCD phase transition, we show that a recently introduced shape parameter of the Bose-Einstein correlation function, the Levy index of stability equals to the correlation exponent - one of the critical exponents that characterize the behavior of the matter in the vicinity of the second order phase transition point. Hence the shape of the Bose-Einstein / HBT correlation functions, when measured as a function of bombarding energy and centrality in various heavy ion reactions, can be utilized to locate experimentally the second order phase transition and the critical end point of the first order phase transition line in QCD.

## Key claim

The Lévy index of stability of the Bose-Einstein correlation function equals the correlation exponent of QCD at a second-order phase transition, but only if the particle emission source is Lévy-stable due to critical fluctuations; this equality is derived from universality class arguments rather than from direct QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors connect the Lévy index of stability of Bose-Einstein correlation functions to the correlation exponent of QCD by analyzing the power-law decay of the order-parameter correlation function at the critical end point. They compare this to the power-law decay of spatial correlations from Lévy-stable sources in coordinate space, and invoke the universality class of the 3d Ising model to fix the numerical value of the correlation exponent. The relationship between the Lévy index and the running QCD coupling is also derived for the case of jet fragmentation using the color dipole model and the τ-model for invariant momentum dependence.

## Key numbers

- The critical end point of QCD is located at T_E = 162 ± 2 MeV and μ_E = 360 ± 40 MeV (Fodor 2004, statistical errors only, no continuum extrapolation).
- At vanishing net baryon density, the critical temperature is T_c = 164 ± 2 MeV (statistical only, from the same calculation).
- An improved calculation (Katz 2005) gives T_c = 189 ± 8 MeV at μ_B = 0 (physical quark masses, working toward continuum extrapolation).
- The Lévy index of stability at the second-order QCD phase transition point is α = 0.50 ± 0.05, taken from the correlation exponent of the 3d Ising model (Rieger 1995).
- The fractal dimension of the QCD jet cascade is d_f = 1 + √(3α_s/(2π)).
- The relationship between the running QCD coupling and the Lévy index from Bose-Einstein correlations in jet events is α_s = (π/6) α²_BEC.

## Context

This work extends earlier connections between Bose-Einstein correlations and intermittency (Bialas 1992) and between Lévy distributions and multiparticle production (Brax and Peschanski 1990). It builds on the authors' previous results linking Lévy indices to QCD jet fractals (Csörgő 2004) and on lattice QCD calculations locating the critical end point (Fodor 2004, Katz 2005). The paper proposes that measuring the excitation function of the Lévy index of stability in heavy ion collisions as a function of bombarding energy and centrality can experimentally locate the critical end point of the QCD phase diagram, though the authors note that resonance decays, hydrodynamic expansion, and time evolution between the phase transition and freeze-out may mask the effect.
