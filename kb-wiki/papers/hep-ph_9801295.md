# Flavor structure of the octet magnetic moments

> We use the chiral quark-soliton model to identify all symmetry breaking terms linear in $m_{s}$ and investigate the strange magnetic moment in a ``model-independent'' way. Assuming hedgehog symmetry and employing the collective quantization, we obtain the most general expression for the flavor-singlet and flavor-octet magnetic moments in terms of seven independent parameters. Having fitted these parameters to the experimental magnetic moments of the octet baryons, we show that the strange magnetic moment turns out to be positive. The best fit obtained by minimizing $χ^2$ assuming 15% theoretical accuracy yields: $μ^{({\rm s})}_{N} = (0.41 \pm 0.18) μ_{N}$.

## Key claim

The strange magnetic moment of the nucleon is positive, with the best fit yielding \(\mu^{({\rm s})}_{\rm N} = (0.41 \pm 0.18) \mu_{\rm N}\), but this result depends on assuming 15% theoretical accuracy in the \(\chi^2\) minimization and on the specific treatment of SU(3) symmetry breaking terms linear in the strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors used the chiral quark-soliton model (\(\chi\)QSM) with hedgehog symmetry and collective quantization to derive the most general expression for flavor-singlet and flavor-octet magnetic moments in terms of seven independent parameters (\(v, w, x, y, z, p, q\)). They fitted these parameters to the experimental magnetic moments of the octet baryons, including SU(3) symmetry breaking contributions from the strange quark mass \(m_s\) up to linear order in both the operator and the baryon wave functions, and then extracted the flavor magnetic moments for u, d, and s quarks.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment of the nucleon from the best \(\chi^2\) fit (fit 4) assuming 15% theoretical accuracy: \(\mu^{({\rm s})}_{\rm N} = (0.41 \pm 0.18) \mu_{\rm N}\)
- Self-consistent \(\chi\)QSM calculation gives a much smaller value: \(\mu^{({\rm s})}_{\rm N} = 0.03 \mu_{\rm N}\)
- The "model-independent" fit range for the strange magnetic moment: \(\mu^{({\rm s})}_{\rm N} \simeq (0.25 - 0.37) \mu_{\rm N}\)
- Fitted parameters from fit (4): \(v = -0.266 \pm 0.030\), \(w = 0.064 \pm 0.036\), \(x' = -0.008 \pm 0.017\), \(y = -0.050 \pm 0.042\), \(z = 0.093 \pm 0.190\), \(q' = 0.006 \pm 0.020\), with \(\chi^2 = 0.017\)
- The SAMPLE collaboration measurement of the strange magnetic form factor at \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\): \(G^{({\rm s})}_{\rm M} = (+0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19) \mu_{\rm N}\)

## Context

This work extends the "model-independent" approach of Kim, Praszalowicz, and Goeke (1997) for baryon magnetic moments to include the flavor-singlet part of the magnetic moment operator, which was not calculated in that earlier work. It also improves upon the chiral bag model analysis by Hong, Park, and Min (1998) by including SU(3) symmetry breaking contributions in the singlet magnetic moment and additional symmetry breaking pieces in the triplet and octet parts. The results enable comparison with ongoing and planned experiments at CEBAF, Mainz, and MIT that aim to measure the strange form factors of the nucleon.
