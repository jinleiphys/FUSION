# Hydrodynamics at RHIC and LHC: What have we learned?

> Fluid dynamical description of elementary particle collisions has a long history dating back to the works of Landau and Fermi. Nevertheless, it is during the last 10-15 years when fluid dynamics has become the standard tool to describe the evolution of matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. In this review I briefly describe the hydrodynamical models, what we have learned when analyzing the RHIC and LHC data using these models, and what the latest developments and challenges are.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hydrodynamical models have become the standard tool for describing ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the extraction of precise transport coefficients like the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (η/s) is hampered by significant uncertainties in the initial state of the evolution and the treatment of the hadronic stage, leading to a range rather than a single value.

## Method

The author reviews the application of relativistic fluid dynamics to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) and LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV), covering ideal hydrodynamics, dissipative (Israel-Stewart) hydrodynamics with shear viscosity, and hybrid models that couple hydrodynamics to hadron cascade codes like UrQMD. The review compares model calculations to experimental data on particle spectra, elliptic flow (v2), and Hanbury Brown–Twiss (HBT) radii, focusing on how different choices of initial conditions (Glauber, KLN, EKRT), equations of state (EoS), and freeze-out prescriptions affect the extracted physics.

## Key numbers

*   Elliptic flow v2 of charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV was one of the first RHIC measurements, and early hydrodynamical calculations reproduced the large observed v2, indicating strong rescattering.
*   The preferred η/s ratio extracted from RHIC data using viscous hydrodynamics depends on the initial state model: η/s ≈ 0.08 with Glauber initial conditions and η/s ≈ 0.16 with KLN initial conditions, leading to an estimated limit of 1/(4π) < η/s < 2.5/(4π) for the effective QGP viscosity.
*   At RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV), the v2(pT) of charged hadrons is insensitive to the value of η/s above the phase transition but very sensitive to its value in the hadronic phase; at the full LHC energy (√sNN = 5.5 TeV), viscosity in the plasma phase dominates and dissipation in the hadronic phase has only a minor effect.

## Context

This review extends the long history of fluid dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions dating back to Landau and Fermi, and it synthesizes results from the last 10–15 years where hydrodynamics became standard. It builds on prior work such as the Glauber model (Miller 2007, Bialas 1976), the KLN model (Kharzeev 2000–2002), and the EKRT saturation model (Eskola 1999, Paatelainen 2012–2013), and it addresses the "HBT puzzle" (Pratt 2008–2009) which required early collective expansion, a hard EoS, dissipative effects, and resonance decays to be resolved. The review enables future systematic studies of collisions at different energies using model emulators to map the parameter space and test lattice QCD predictions for the EoS.
