# Structure of 13Be probed via quasi-free scattering

> We present an investigation of the structure of 13Be obtained via a kinematically complete measurement of the (p; pn) reaction in inverse kinematics at 265 MeV/nucleon. The relative energy spectrum of 13Be is compared to Transfer-to-the-Continuum calculations which use as structure inputs the overlaps of the 14Be ground-state wave function, computed in a three-body model, with the unbound states of the 13Be residual nucleus. The key role of neutron p-wave orbital in the interpretation of the low-relative-energy part of the spectrum is discussed.

## Key claim

The low-relative-energy part of the 13Be spectrum is dominated by a neutron p-wave orbital, specifically a 1/2- resonance at about 0.48 MeV, but this interpretation depends on the choice of core-neutron potential parameters and the inclusion of core-excited components in the three-body model.

## Method

The authors performed a kinematically complete measurement of the 14Be(p,pn) reaction in inverse kinematics at 265 MeV/nucleon using the MINOS TPC, WINDS neutron array, SAMURAI spectrometer, and DALI2 gamma array at RIKEN RIBF. The measured 13Be relative energy spectrum was compared to Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) calculations that use structure inputs from a three-body (12Be+n+n) hyperspherical model of the 14Be ground state, with different core-neutron potentials (P1-P5) to test sensitivity to the 13Be resonance properties.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 265 MeV/nucleon for 14Be incident on liquid hydrogen target
- 13Be relative energy spectrum shows a prominent peak at approximately 0.48 MeV and a broader structure peaked at approximately 2.3 MeV extending from about 1 MeV to about 5 MeV
- Gamma transitions observed in 12Be at 2.1(0.1) MeV (2+ state) and 2.7(0.4) MeV (1- state)
- Photopeak efficiency of DALI2 gamma array: 8.9(5)% at 2.1 MeV and 7.0(4)% at 2.7 MeV
- Fragment transmission: 64(1)% (average of 65.0(1)% for 12Be and 62.9(2)% for 14Be)
- Invariant mass resolution follows FWHM = 0.587*sqrt(Er) MeV
- NEBULA neutron detection efficiency: approximately 35% at the beam energy
- For potential P2: 1/2- resonance at 0.46 MeV with width 0.40 MeV; 5/2+ resonance at 1.96 MeV with width 0.27 MeV; s-wave scattering length -9.2 fm
- 14Be two-neutron separation energy fixed to experimental value of 1.27 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 13Be spectroscopy that showed conflicting interpretations, particularly regarding the low-lying peak around 0.5 MeV which was assigned as either s-wave (Aksyutina 2013) or p-wave (Kondo 2010) dominance. The novel approach uses consistent two- and three-body models for 14Be and 13Be within the Transfer-to-the-Continuum framework proposed by Gomez-Ramos (2017), reducing ambiguities in the analysis. The results enable future quantitative studies of dineutron correlations in 14Be and provide constraints for three-body models of Borromean nuclei.
