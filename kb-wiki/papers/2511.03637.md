# Violation of the elliptic flow scaling of $f_0(980)$ in p-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We investigate the production and elliptic flow of the $f_0(980)$ in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV using a hadronic coalescence model with the $K$ and $\bar K$ phase-space distributions provided by the Hydro-Coal-Frag hybrid model. Our results, which agree with the ALICE and CMS measurements, support the $K\bar K$ molecular interpretation of the $f_0(980)$ structure and show, however, a breakdown of the simple number-of-constituent (NC) scaling of its elliptic flow. The latter is in contrast to the deuteron elliptic flow, which exhibits a significantly better NC scaling when the same coalescence width parameter is used.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the elliptic flow ($v_2$) of the $f_0(980)$ meson in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV violates the simple number-of-constituent (NC) scaling, contrary to the deuteron which exhibits significantly better NC scaling with the same coalescence width parameter; this supports the $K\bar{K}$ molecular interpretation of the $f_0(980)$ structure rather than a conventional $q\bar{q}$ configuration.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic coalescence model with a Gaussian Wigner function to compute $f_0(980)$ production via $K+\bar{K}\to f_0(980)$ coalescence, taking the kaon phase-space distributions from the Hydro-Coal-Frag hybrid model which combines hydrodynamics, quark coalescence, and string fragmentation. The same approach was applied to deuteron production via $p+n\to d$ coalescence for comparison. The coalescence calculations used the phase-space distributions at kinetic freeze-out, with the Gaussian width $\sigma_{\rho}$ related to the root-mean-square radius of each composite particle.

## Key numbers

- $f_0(980)$ production and elliptic flow calculated in p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV
- RMS radius of $f_0(980)$ estimated as $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}\sim 1.0\text{–}1.5$ fm based on binding energy $E_B \approx 10\text{–}20$ MeV
- Deuteron RMS radius fixed at $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}=1.96$ fm from experimental data, corresponding to coalescence parameter $\sigma_{\rho}=2.26$ fm
- For comparison, $f_0(980)$ calculations also performed with $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}=1.96$ fm (same as deuteron)
- $f_0(980)$ decay branching ratio to $\pi^+\pi^-$ is $(46 \pm 6)\%$

## Context

This work extends the hadronic coalescence approach, commonly used for light nuclei like the deuteron, to study the exotic hadron $f_0(980)$. It contradicts the conclusion from CMS Collaboration analysis using number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling, which suggested a conventional $q\bar{q}$ structure for the $f_0(980)$. The results instead support the $K\bar{K}$ molecular interpretation from hadron physics studies by Jaffe (1976), Weinstein (1990), Baru (2003), and Guo (2018). The findings enable future discrimination between compact tetraquark states and loosely bound molecular configurations using elliptic flow measurements in heavy-ion collisions.
