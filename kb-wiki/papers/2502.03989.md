# Radii of light nuclei from the Jacobi No-Core Shell Model

> Accurately determining the size of the atomic nucleus with realistic nuclear forces is a long outstanding issue of nuclear physics. The no-core shell model (NCSM), one of the powerful ab initio methods for nuclear structure, can achieve accurate energies of light nuclei. The extraction of converged radii is more difficult. In this work, we present a novel method to effectively extract the radius of light nuclei by restoring the long-range behavior of densities from NCSM calculations. The correct large distance asymptotic of two-body relative densities are deduced based on the NCSM densities in limited basis size. The resulting radii using the corrected densities show a nice convergence. The root-mean-square matter and charge radii of $^{4,6,8}$He and $^{6,7,8}$Li can be accurately obtained based on Jacobi-NCSM calculations with the high-precision chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces combined with this new method. Our method can be straightforwardly extended to other ab initio calculations, potentially providing a better description of nuclear sizes with realistic nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to extract converged nuclear radii from no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations by correcting the long-range tail of two-body relative densities, which otherwise exhibit incorrect Gaussian asymptotic behavior due to the limited harmonic oscillator basis. The method yields converged radii for light nuclei even with modest basis sizes, but the authors note that the charge radii of some nuclei (e.g., $^{6}$He) remain underestimated compared to experiment, suggesting that other effects such as two-nucleon currents may be needed.

## Method

The authors perform Jacobi-NCSM calculations for $^{4,6,8}$He and $^{6,7,8}$Li using the chiral SMS N$^{4}$LO$^{+}$(450)+N$^{2}$LO interaction with similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution at $\lambda=1.88$ fm$^{-1}$. They compute two-body relative densities from the J-NCSM wave functions and replace the Gaussian tail of each density (which decays as $e^{-\beta r^2}$) with an exponential function $\alpha e^{-\kappa r}$ fitted to the intermediate-range region where the density is approximately basis-independent. The corrected densities are then used to compute root-mean-square matter and charge radii via two-body distance operators.

## Key numbers

- Converged matter radius of $^{4}$He: $1.447$ fm with SRG transformation, $1.466$ fm without; experimental value $1.457\pm 0.010$ fm
- SRG transformation effect on $^{4}$He matter radius: approximately $0.02$ fm
- $^{6}$Li matter radii after correction at $N_\text{HO}=12$ for four frequencies: $2.33$ fm to $2.39$ fm; experimental $2.35\pm 0.03$ fm
- $^{6}$He matter radii after correction at $N_\text{HO}=12$: $2.32$ fm to $2.40$ fm; experimental $2.33(4)$ fm
- $^{8}$He matter radii after correction: consistent with experimental $2.49(4)$ fm to $2.53(7)$ fm
- $^{7}$Li matter radii after correction: $2.401$-$2.443$ fm
- $^{8}$Li matter radii after correction: $2.424$-$2.480$ fm
- $^{4}$He matter radius from Faddeev-Yakubovsky with bare interaction: $1.431$ fm

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM studies of light nuclei using chiral interactions (Maris 2020, LENPIC 2022) that achieved accurate binding energies but struggled with radius convergence. It provides an alternative to extrapolation procedures (Furnstahl 2013, Forssen 2017) and artificial neural network approaches (Wolfgruber 2023) for extracting radii. The method can be extended to other ab initio calculations using harmonic oscillator bases and to other long-range observables, potentially addressing the persistent issue of underpredicted nuclear sizes in calculations with realistic forces.
