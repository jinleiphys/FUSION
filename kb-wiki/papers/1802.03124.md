# Nuclear reaction path and requantization of TDDFT

> Using a theory of large amplitude collective motion, the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method, we derive reaction path for the fusion process at sub-barrier energies. The collective Hamiltonian to describe the fusion process is constructed, based on the obtained reaction path and canonical variables. We study the reaction of N=Z stable nuclei, alpha+16O, 16O+16O, and alpha+12C. The results suggest that, after two nuclei touch, the reaction path is significantly deviated from the simple relative motion, which may affect the deep sub-barrier fusion cross section.

# Nuclear reaction path and requantization of TDDFT

## Key claim

The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method can derive a continuous reaction path for sub-barrier fusion that deviates significantly from simple relative motion after the nuclei touch, which may affect the deep sub-barrier fusion cross section; however, for the \(^{12}\text{C}+\alpha\) system the method has not yet succeeded in obtaining a self-consistent path connecting the separated and fused configurations.

## Method

The authors use the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, which expands the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) state around the zero-momentum limit to derive a collective submanifold decoupled from intrinsic degrees of freedom. They solve the ASCC equations using the imaginary-time method for the mean-field states and the finite amplitude method for the collective generators, employing the Bonche-Koonin-Negele (BKN) energy density functional on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 1.1 fm mesh spacing. The collective Hamiltonian is constructed from the potential and inertial mass along the one-dimensional collective path, then mapped to the relative distance coordinate \(r\) between projectile and target.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}\text{O}+\alpha\), the inertial mass exactly matches the reduced mass of \(3.2\,m_N\) at the asymptotic limit \(r\rightarrow\infty\).
- For \(^{16}\text{O}+^{16}\text{O}\), the inertial mass exactly matches the reduced mass of \(8\,m_N\) at \(r\rightarrow\infty\), and reaches about twice the reduced mass near the superdeformed local minimum in \(^{32}\text{S}\).
- For \(^{16}\text{O}+^{16}\text{O}\), the reduction factor in the fusion cross section due to the dynamical inertial mass could be two orders of magnitude at very low energy.
- For \(^{12}\text{C}+\alpha\), the inertial mass is identical to the reduced mass of \(3\,m_N\) in the asymptotic region.
- The model space is \(12\times 12\times 18\) fm\(^3\) for \(^{16}\text{O}+\alpha\) and \(^{12}\text{C}+\alpha\), and \(12\times 12\times 24\) fm\(^3\) for \(^{16}\text{O}+^{16}\text{O}\).

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method developed by Marumori, Maslov, and others (Marumori 1980, Matsuo 2000, Hinohara 2007, 2009, Nakatsukasa 2012) to sub-barrier fusion reactions, building on real-time TDHF simulations (Simenel 2012, Umar 2016, Sekizawa 2017) that cannot capture full quantum effects at low energies. The approach contrasts with constrained Hartree-Fock calculations using fixed operators (e.g., mass quadrupole or octupole constraints), which produce discontinuous potential curves for \(^{12}\text{C}+\alpha\) in the region \(1000 \lesssim Q_{30} \lesssim 1500\) fm\(^3\), whereas the ASCC method yields a smooth potential. The results enable construction of a quantized collective Hamiltonian for sub-barrier fusion, with the inertial mass reproducing the Thouless-Valatin mass at Hartree-Fock minima, and suggest that the hindrance of deep sub-barrier fusion cross sections arises from the deviation of the reaction path from simple relative motion after contact.
