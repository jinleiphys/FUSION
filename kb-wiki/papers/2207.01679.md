# Reevaluation of heavy-fermion-induced electron EDM at three loops

> Motivated by improved limits on electric dipole moments (EDMs), we revisit the three-loop light-by-light mechanism that transmits $CP$ violation from a heavy fermion to a light one. Applying it to the electron EDM induced by EDMs of heavier flavors, we find additional contributions at the order $ m_e/m_μ$ or $m_e/m_τ$ that were missing before and that make the result 1.4 times larger. Consequently we improve bounds on the tau EDM, and update indirect limits on the muon EDM as well as charm and bottom quark color EDMs.

## Key claim

The three-loop light-by-light mechanism that transmits CP violation from a heavy fermion to a light one contains an additional contribution from expansion in the external electron momentum that was missing in the prior calculation by Grozin, Khriplovich, and Rudenko (2008); including both the mass expansion and the momentum expansion enhances the electron EDM result by approximately 40%.

## Method

The authors reevaluated the 24 three-loop QED diagrams that connect a heavy lepton EDM vertex to an electron line via three photon propagators. They expanded the amplitude to linear order in the external photon momentum, keeping both the expansion in the electron-to-heavy-lepton mass ratio (m_e/m_L) and the expansion in the external electron momentum (p/m_L), then used integration-by-parts reduction with FIRE6 and analytical master integrals to obtain the scalar loop integrals. The two structures from the two expansions were projected onto the EDM operator using Dirac matrix completeness relations in arbitrary dimension d, and the resulting finite contributions were summed.

## Key numbers

* The total coefficient relating the induced electron EDM to the heavy lepton EDM is a = 19/4 zeta(3) - 71/24 ≈ 2.751, where zeta(3) ≈ 1.202.
* The previously known contribution from mass expansion alone gives a^(1) = 15/4 zeta(3) - 31/12 ≈ 1.924; the newly included momentum expansion gives a^(2) = zeta(3) - 3/8 ≈ 0.827.
* The updated 90% C.L. indirect bound on the tau EDM is |d_tau| < 1.1 × 10^{-18} e cm.
* The updated 90% C.L. indirect bound on the muon EDM is |d_mu| < 1.7 × 10^{-20} e cm.
* The 90% C.L. bound on the charm quark color EDM is |tilde_d_c| < 5.2 × 10^{-21} cm, using m_c = 1.27 GeV, m_u(m_c) = 2.5 MeV, m_d(m_c) = 5.4 MeV, alpha_s(m_c) = 0.38.
* The 90% C.L. bound on the bottom quark color EDM is |tilde_d_b| < 2.3 × 10^{-19} cm, using m_b = 4.18 GeV, m_u(m_b) = 1.8 MeV, m_d(m_b) = 4.0 MeV, alpha_s(m_b) = 0.223.

## Context

This work corrects and extends the three-loop calculation by Grozin, Khriplovich, and Rudenko (2008), who computed heavy lepton EDM contributions to the electron EDM but omitted the momentum-expansion term. The updated tau EDM bound from the ACME paramagnetic EDM experiment (2018) is now more than an order of magnitude tighter than the direct constraint from e+e- -> tau+tau- at Belle (2002), providing a benchmark for the upcoming Belle-II measurement. The results also update the muon EDM constraint from Ema, Gao, and Pospelov (2021) and provide new indirect limits on charm and bottom quark color EDMs from the neutron EDM bound by Abel et al. (2020), complementing constraints from the Weinberg three-gluon operator.
