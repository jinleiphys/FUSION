# LOFF and breached pairing with cold atoms

> We investigate here the Cooper pairing of fermionic atoms with mismatched fermi surfaces using a variational construct for the ground state. We determine the state for different values of the mismatch of chemical potential for weak as well as strong coupling regimes including the BCS BEC cross over region. We consider Cooper pairing with both zero and finite net momentum. Within the variational approximation for the ground state and comparing the thermodynamic potentials, we show that (i) the LOFF phase is stable in the weak coupling regime, (ii) the LOFF window is maximum on the BEC side near the Feshbach resonance and (iii) the existence of stable gapless states with a single fermi surface for negative average chemical potential on the BEC side of the Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

Using a variational ground state ansatz and comparing thermodynamic potentials, the authors show that the LOFF phase (pairing with finite net momentum) is stable in the weak coupling regime, the LOFF window is largest on the BEC side near the Feshbach resonance, and stable gapless states with a single Fermi surface exist for negative average chemical potential on the BEC side of the Feshbach resonance. These conclusions are conditional on the variational approximation and the comparison of thermodynamic potentials after solving the gap equation.

## Method

The authors constructed a variational ground state using a unitary transformation that creates Cooper pairs with a fixed nonzero total momentum q, generalizing the BCS ansatz. They computed the thermodynamic potential by taking expectation values of the Hamiltonian with respect to this ansatz state, including thermal effects via thermofield dynamics. The condensate function f(k) and thermal distribution functions were determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential, yielding gap equations and quasiparticle dispersion relations that were solved numerically for different coupling strengths and chemical potential mismatches.

## Key numbers

* Superfluid gap in the weak coupling BCS limit: z = 8e^(-2) exp(-pi/(|k_f a|)) in units of fermi energy
* Chemical potential changes sign at k_f a ~ 0.56, signaling the onset of the BEC phase
* Near the unitary limit (k_f a = 0.01), the ratio of condensate gap to chemical potential is Delta/nu = 1.195, and the chemical potential is nu/epsilon_f = 0.58
* In the weak coupling limit with fixed chemical potentials, the thermodynamic potential difference between condensed and normal phase is approximately (epsilon_f k_f^3 / 2pi^2) (2 delta^2 - z^2)

## Context

This work extends variational approaches previously applied to quark matter with charge neutrality (Mishra 2005, Mishra 2006) and cold fermionic atoms (Mishra 2005) to include LOFF pairing with a single plane wave condensate. It is complementary to the stability analysis of breached pairing by Gubankova (2005) and others, which treated chemical potentials and gap functions as parameters without an explicit ground state construction. The results enable systematic comparison of LOFF, BCS, and normal phases across the BCS-BEC crossover, and the authors note that the variational results for the symmetric case correspond to the saddle point solution of Randeria (1995).
