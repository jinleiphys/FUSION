# Nonperturbative Effects on Radiative Energy Loss of Heavy Quarks

> The radiative energy loss of fast partons traveling through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is commonly studied within perturbative QCD (pQCD). Nonperturbative (NP) effects, which are expected to become important near the critical temperature, have been much less investigated. Here, we utilize a recently developed $T$-matrix approach to incorporate NP effects for gluon emission off heavy quarks propagating through the QGP. We set up four cases that contain, starting from a Born diagram calculation with color-Coulomb interaction, an increasing level of NP components, by subsequently including (remnants of) confining interactions, resummation in the heavy-light scattering amplitude, and off-shell spectral functions for both heavy and light partons. For each case we compute the power spectra of the emitted gluons, heavy-quark transport coefficients (drag and transverse-momentum broadening, $\hat{q}$), and the path-length dependent energy loss within a "QGP brick" at fixed temperature. Investigating the differences in these quantities between the four cases illustrates how NP mechanisms affect gluon radiation processes. While the baseline perturbative processes experience a strong suppression of soft radiation due to thermal masses of the emitted gluons, confining interactions, ladder resummations and broad spectral functions (re-)generate a large enhancement toward low momenta and low temperatures. For example, for a 10 GeV charm quark at 200 MeV temperature, they enhance the transport coefficients by up to a factor of 10, while the results smoothly converge to perturbative results at sufficiently hard scales.

## Key claim

Nonperturbative effects, confining interactions, ladder resummations, and broad off-shell spectral functions, can enhance the radiative energy loss of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma by up to a factor of 10 for a 10 GeV charm quark at 200 MeV temperature, while smoothly converging to perturbative results at sufficiently hard scales.

## Method

The authors use a previously developed in-medium T-matrix approach to compute gluon emission off heavy quarks, starting from a Kadanoff-Baym equation and reducing it to a semi-classical Boltzmann equation. They define four model cases with increasing nonperturbative content: (1) Born-level color-Coulomb interaction only, (2) adding confining interactions, (3) including t-channel ladder resummation in the T-matrix, and (4) full off-shell spectral functions for both heavy and light partons. For each case they compute the gluon emission power spectra, heavy-quark transport coefficients (drag and transverse-momentum broadening), and path-length dependent energy loss in a fixed-temperature "QGP brick."

## Key numbers

- For a 10 GeV charm quark at 200 MeV temperature, nonperturbative effects enhance transport coefficients by up to a factor of 10 compared to the perturbative baseline.
- The baseline perturbative processes show strong suppression of soft radiation due to thermal masses of emitted gluons.
- Nonperturbative effects (confining interactions, ladder resummations, broad spectral functions) regenerate a large enhancement toward low gluon momenta and low temperatures.
- Results smoothly converge to perturbative results at sufficiently hard scales.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous T-matrix approach for heavy-quark collisional energy loss (Liu, Rapp, 2018) to radiative processes, and builds on their quantum many-body framework that successfully described QGP properties including equation of state, heavy-quark free energies, and transport coefficients (Liu, Rapp, 2016, 2017). It contrasts with standard perturbative QCD formalisms such as AMY (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002), ASW (Armesto, Salgado, Wiedemann, 2003), BDMPS-Z (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1996; Zakharov, 1996), DGLV (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000; Djordjevic, Gyulassy, 2004), higher-twist (Wang, Guo, 2001; Majumder, 2009), and SCET (Ovanesyan, Vitev, 2011; Kang, Vitev, Xing, 2017) approaches, which typically neglect nonperturbative effects. The results enable improved phenomenological modeling of heavy-flavor observables in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly at low momentum and temperature where standard perturbative expansions break down.
