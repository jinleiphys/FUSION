# Low Energy Continuum and Lattice Effective Field Theories

> In the first part of the thesis we consider the constraints of causality and unitarity for particles interacting via strictly finite-range interactions. We generalize Wigner's causality bound to the case of non-vanishing partial-wave mixing. Specifically we analyze the system of the low-energy interactions between protons and neutrons. We also analyze low-energy scattering for systems with arbitrary short-range interactions plus an attractive $1/r^α$ tail for $α\geq2$. In particular, we focus on the case of $α=6$ and we derive the constraints of causality and unitarity also for these systems and find that the van der Waals length scale dominates over parameters characterizing the short-distance physics of the interaction. This separation of scales suggests a separate universality class for physics characterizing interactions with an attractive $1/r^{6}$ tail. We argue that a similar universality class exists for any attractive potential $1/r^α$ for $α\geq2$. In the second part of the thesis we present lattice Monte Carlo calculations of fermion-dimer scattering in the limit of zero-range interactions using the adiabatic projection method. The adiabatic projection method uses a set of initial cluster states and Euclidean time projection to give a systematically improvable description of the low-lying scattering cluster states in a finite volume. We use Lüscher's finite-volume relations to determine the $s$-wave, $p$-wave, and $d$-wave phase shifts. For comparison, we also compute exact lattice results using Lanczos iteration and continuum results using the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation. For our Monte Carlo calculations we use a new lattice algorithm called impurity lattice Monte Carlo. This algorithm can be viewed as a hybrid technique which incorporates elements of both worldline and auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The thesis generalizes Wigner's causality bound to systems with non-vanishing partial-wave mixing and derives constraints of causality and unitarity for systems with short-range interactions plus an attractive \(1/r^\alpha\) tail for \(\alpha \geq 2\), finding that for \(\alpha = 6\) the van der Waals length scale dominates over short-distance parameters, suggesting a separate universality class; in the second part, it presents lattice Monte Carlo calculations of fermion-dimer scattering using the adiabatic projection method, with the hedge that the method provides a systematically improvable description of low-lying scattering cluster states in a finite volume.

## Method

The first part analytically generalizes Wigner's causality bound to coupled-channel systems with strictly finite-range interactions, then extends the analysis to systems with an attractive \(1/r^\alpha\) tail for \(\alpha \geq 2\), focusing on \(\alpha = 6\) (van der Waals interactions). The second part uses the adiabatic projection method with Euclidean time projection and initial cluster states to compute fermion-dimer scattering in the zero-range limit on a lattice, employing Lüscher's finite-volume relations to extract \(s\)-wave, \(p\)-wave, and \(d\)-wave phase shifts; the calculations use a new impurity lattice Monte Carlo algorithm that combines elements of worldline and auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations, and results are compared with exact lattice results from Lanczos iteration and continuum results from the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation.

## Key numbers

- For the van der Waals case (\(\alpha = 6\)), the van der Waals length scale dominates over parameters characterizing short-distance physics of the interaction (no explicit numerical value for the length scale given in the text).
- The expansion of the shift \(\nu\) in the order of Bessel functions for van der Waals wave functions is given as \(\nu = \nu_0 - \frac{3}{2^{8}\nu_0(4\nu_0^2-1)(\nu_0^2-1)} p_s^4 + O(p_s^8)\), where \(\nu_0 = (2\ell+1)/4\).
- The coefficient \(y_\ell(p_s)\) for van der Waals wave functions is given as \(y_\ell(p_s) = -\left[ \frac{\Gamma(\nu_0)\Gamma(2\nu_0-1)}{\Gamma(\nu_0+1)\Gamma(2\nu_0)} + \frac{\Gamma(\nu_0)\Gamma(2\nu_0+1)}{\Gamma(\nu_0+1)\Gamma(2\nu_0+2)} \right] \left(\frac{p_s}{4}\right)^2 + O(p_s^4)\).
- Wronskian results for single-channel wave functions include \(W[s_{0,\ell}, c_{0,\ell}] = -1\), \(W[s_{0,\ell}, c_{2,\ell}](r) = -\frac{r^2}{2+4\ell}\), and \(W[s_{2,\ell}, c_{2,\ell}](r) = \frac{r^4}{16\ell(\ell+1)-12}\).
- For the coupled-channel delta-function shell potential test, the scattering matrix is parameterized with nuclear bar phase shifts \(\bar{\delta}_\alpha\), \(\bar{\delta}_\beta\), and mixing angle \(\bar{\varepsilon}\), with relations between eigenphase and nuclear bar parameters given by \(\sin(\delta_\alpha - \delta_\beta) = \frac{\sin 2\bar{\varepsilon}}{\sin 2\varepsilon}\), \(\delta_\alpha + \delta_\beta = \bar{\delta}_\alpha + \bar{\delta}_\beta\), and \(\tan 2\varepsilon = \frac{\tan 2\bar{\varepsilon}}{\sin(\bar{\delta}_\alpha - \bar{\delta}_\beta)}\).

## Context

This work extends Wigner's causality bound (Wigner, 1955) to coupled-channel systems and to interactions with an attractive \(1/r^\alpha\) tail, building on prior analyses of causality constraints for short-range interactions. The derivation of van der Waals wave functions follows the approach of Gao (1998) and Gao (1999), and the lattice Monte Carlo calculations using the adiabatic projection method enable systematic studies of few-body scattering in nuclear effective field theories, with the impurity lattice Monte Carlo algorithm providing a new computational tool for such calculations.
