# 1/Nc Expansion in QCD: Double-Line Counting Rules and the Undeservingly Discarded U(1) Ghost

> The 1/Nc expansion is one of the very few methods we have for generating a systematic expansion of QCD at the energy scale relevant to hadron structure. The present formulation of this theory relies on the double-line notation for calculating the leading order of a diagram in the 1/Nc expansion, where the local SU(Nc) gauge symmetry is substituted by a U(Nc) symmetry and the associated U(1) ghost field is ignored. In the current work we demonstrate the insufficiency of this formulation for describing certain non-planar diagrams. We derive a more complete set of Feynman rules that include the U(1) ghost field and provide a useful tool for calculating both color factors and 1/Nc orders of all color-singlet diagrams.

## Key claim

The standard double-line counting rules for the 1/Nc expansion in QCD, which substitute SU(Nc) with U(Nc) and discard the associated U(1) ghost field, are insufficient for correctly evaluating the 1/Nc order of certain non-planar diagrams; the ghost contributions must be retained to recover the correct color factors and 1/Nc ordering for non-planar diagrams, even though they are subleading for planar diagrams.

## Method

The authors explicitly calculate color factors for non-planar quark-gluon vertex diagrams at O(g^5) using the properties of SU(Nc) Gell-Mann matrices, revealing a discrepancy with the standard double-line counting rules. They then derive a revised set of Feynman rules that include the U(1) ghost field, represented as a single dotted line with no color indices, and demonstrate its application by decomposing gluon propagators into a U(N) double-line part and a U(1) ghost part using the SU(N) identity (t^a)_j^i (t^a)_l^k = (1/2) δ_l^i δ_j^k - (1/(2N)) δ_j^i δ_l^k. The 1/Nc order of any color-singlet diagram is then computed as O = N^(L - C/2 - G), where L is the number of closed color index loops, C is the number of gauge couplings g, and G is the number of ghost propagators.

## Context

This work extends the foundational 1/Nc expansion formalism of 't Hooft (1974) and the double-line notation reviewed by Manohar (1998), specifically addressing a known limitation where the U(1) ghost is commonly ignored. The authors demonstrate that for non-planar diagrams, such as those encountered in dressing the quark-gluon vertex for the quark Gap equation (Matevosyan, 2007), the standard rules fail and the ghost contributions become essential. The revised rules provide a practical tool for calculating both color factors and 1/Nc orders of all color-singlet diagrams, enabling correct large-Nc power counting in non-perturbative QCD studies of hadron structure.
