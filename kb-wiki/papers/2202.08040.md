# 1-jettiness with jet axis at $O(α_s)$ in jeep inelastic scattering

> We present $O(α_s)$ analytic predictions for event shape 1-jettiness $τ_1$ distribution aiming measurements in deep inelastic scattering process at future Electron Ion Colliders. The result depends on conventional variables $x$ and $Q$ as well as on $τ_1$ and is relatively compact and easy to implement for numerical calculation. Three different choices of axis, with respect to which $τ_1$ is measured are considered in the Breit frame. The first is the one optimally adjusted to minimize $τ_1$ and the second and third are taken from anti-$k_T$ and Centauro jet algorithms defined with a jet radius parameter $R$, respectively. We find that the first and second give the same result at this order and are independent of $R$, while the third depends on the radius. This fixed-order result provides a nonsingular contribution to be combined with a singular log-resummed contribution to give the full spectrum in $τ_1$ space and also shows how fixed-order and resummation regions change as a function of $x$ and $Q$.

## Key claim

The paper presents analytic $O(\alpha_s)$ predictions for the 1-jettiness $\tau_1$ distribution in deep inelastic scattering, finding that the 1-jettiness axis and the anti-$k_T$ jet axis yield identical results at this order and are independent of the jet radius parameter $R$, while the Centauro algorithm gives a result that depends on $R$; this fixed-order result provides the nonsingular contribution needed to combine with singular log-resummed contributions for the full spectrum.

## Method

The authors computed the cumulative cross section for 1-jettiness in deep inelastic scattering at leading order in $\alpha_s$ in the Breit frame, using three different definitions of the jet axis: the 1-jettiness axis (which minimizes $\tau_1$), the anti-$k_T$ algorithm axis, and the Centauro algorithm axis. They derived analytic expressions for the nonsingular parts of the structure functions $F_1$ and $F_L$ by performing fixed-order QCD calculations for two-body final states, separating the singular (logarithmic) and nonsingular contributions, and also extracted the next-to-leading power (NLP) corrections in the small $\tau_1$ limit.

## Key numbers

* The maximum value of $\tau_1$ for the 1-jettiness axis is $\tau_{1,\max} = \frac{1}{2}\theta(-x+\frac{2}{3}) + \frac{1-x}{x}\theta(x-\frac{2}{3})$, which depends on the Bjorken variable $x$.
* For the Centauro algorithm, the critical value of $z$ where the two particles merge into a single jet is $z_c(R) = 16/(16+R^2)$, and the region IV exists only for $R \le 2$.
* The NLP corrections for the 1-jettiness axis contain terms of order $\tau$ and $\tau\ln\tau$, with the quark nonsingular $B_q^{\text{ns}}$ including a term $\tau\big(\frac12+3\ln\tau\big) f_q(x)$.
* For the Centauro algorithm, the leading nonzero NLP correction to $\delta B_q^{\text{ns}}$ is $-\tau\big(-\frac32+2\ln\frac{R^2}{4\tau}\big) f_q(x)$, showing explicit dependence on the jet radius $R$.

## Context

This work extends previous analytic calculations of 1-jettiness in DIS, specifically the $\tau_1^b$ version studied by Kang et al. (2014), by considering the $\tau_1^a$ version with jet axis determined by different algorithms. The results provide the nonsingular fixed-order contributions needed to combine with resummed singular contributions for precision predictions at future Electron-Ion Colliders (EIC and EicC), and the NLP terms are essential for improving subtraction accuracy in higher-order perturbative QCD calculations using N-jettiness subtraction methods developed by Boughezal et al. (2015) and Gaunt et al. (2015).
