# The Weak Decay of Hypernuclei

> The nonmesonic weak decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei is studied in a shell model framework. A complete strangeness-changing weak $ΛN \to NN$ transition potential, based on one boson exchange, is constructed by including the exchange of the pseudoscalar mesons $π$, K, $η$ as well as the vector mesons $ρ, ω$, and K$^*$, whose weak coupling constants are obtained from soft meson theorems and SU(6)$_w$. General expressions for nucleons in arbitrary shells are obtained. The transition matrix elements include realistic $Λ$N short-range correlations and NN final state interactions based on the Nijmegen baryon-baryon potential. The decay rates are found to be especially sensitive to the inclusion of the strange mesons, K and K$^*$, even though the role of kaon exchange is found to be reduced with recent couplings obtained from next-to-leading order Chiral Perturbation Theory. With the weak couplings used in this study the rates remain dominated by the pion-exchange mechanism since the contributions of heavier mesons either cancel each other or are suppressed by form factors and short-range correlations. The total decay rate therefore remains in agreement with present measurements. However, the partial rates which are even more sensitive to the inclusion of heavier mesons cannot be reconciled with the data. The proton asymmetry changes by 50% once heavier mesons are included and agrees with the available data.

## Key claim

The nonmesonic weak decay of Λ hypernuclei is studied using a full one-boson-exchange potential including π, K, η, ρ, ω, and K* mesons; while the total decay rate remains in agreement with present measurements, the partial rates cannot be reconciled with the data, and the proton asymmetry changes by 50% once heavier mesons are included and agrees with available data.

## Method

The authors constructed a complete strangeness-changing weak ΛN → NN transition potential based on one boson exchange, including pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) and vector mesons (ρ, ω, K*), with weak coupling constants obtained from soft meson theorems and SU(6)w. The transition matrix elements were evaluated in a shell model framework using spectroscopic factors for 12ΛC, with realistic ΛN short-range correlations and NN final state interactions based on the Nijmegen baryon-baryon potential. General expressions for nucleons in arbitrary shells were obtained, and the decay rates and proton asymmetry were computed for 12ΛC.

## Key numbers

- Free Λ hyperon lifetime: about 260 picoseconds, decaying into p π− (∼64%) and n π0 (∼36%)
- Λ mass excess of 176 MeV converted into kinetic energy of final-state nucleons emerging with momentum of about 400 MeV/c
- Harmonic oscillator parameters: bΛ = 1.87 fm and bN = 1.64 fm, adjusted to experimental separation energies and the 12C charge form factor
- The parametrized ΛN correlation function uses parameters a = 0.5, b = 0.25, c = 1.28, n = 2, yielding deviations at most 10% from full numerical correlations
- The weak coupling constant GF mπ2 = 2.21 × 10−7
- Empirical weak constants for pion: Aπ = 1.05 and Bπ = −7.15
- The average NN correlation function parameter qc = 3.93 fm−1

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to the ΛN → NN decay mode, including early one-pion-exchange calculations by Adams, heavier meson exchange studies by McKellar and Nardulli, and the quark-based model by Kisslinger. The authors note that most previous investigations were performed in nuclear matter, whereas this study uses a shell model framework with spectroscopic factors for 12ΛC. The calculations enable future comparison with experimental data from BNL and KEK on hypernuclear lifetimes and branching ratios, and the proton asymmetry measurements from KEK using the (π+, K+) reaction.
