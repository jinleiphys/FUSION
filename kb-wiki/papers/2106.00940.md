# Bottomonia production in p+p collisions under NRQCD formalism

> In this work, we present the calculation of the production cross sections of bottomonia states using Non-Relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics (NRQCD) formalism. The direct production cross-section of a resonance can be factorised in terms of short distance Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) cross sections and long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) under NRQCD. We use a large set of measured $Υ$(nS) production data at Tevatron and LHC energies in both central and forward rapidity regions to extract the LDMEs with better precision. The feed down contributions from the higher states including the $χ_{b}$(3P) state are taken into account for the LDME extraction. The formalism provides a good description of the bottomonia data in wide transverse momentum range at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors present leading-order (LO) NRQCD calculations for bottomonia production cross-sections, claiming that a LO analysis is useful because it is "straightforward and unique" and has "excellent predictability power for unknown cross sections," while acknowledging that NLO QCD corrections can enhance the colour-singlet cross-section by approximately a factor of two, with only slight changes to colour-octet cross-sections.

## Method

The authors factorize the double differential cross-section for bottomonia production into short-distance QCD parton-level cross-sections (calculated at LO for 2-to-2 processes) and long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) extracted by fitting experimental data. They use CT14LO parton distribution functions, a bottom quark mass of 4.88 GeV, and include feed-down contributions from higher states including the chi_b(3P) state. A combined fit is performed over a large set of measured Upsilon(nS) production data from Tevatron (CDF at 1.8 TeV) and LHC (CMS and ATLAS at 7 and 13 TeV, LHCb at 7 TeV) in both central and forward rapidity regions.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass m_b = 4.88 GeV used in calculations (with a variation to 4.77 GeV for uncertainty estimation)
- For Upsilon(3S): M_L(b\bar{b}([^3S_1]_8) -> Upsilon(3S)) = 0.0547 +/- 0.0007 +/- 0.0036 GeV^3; combined LDME M_L(b\bar{b}([^1S_0]_8,[^3P_0]_8) -> Upsilon(3S)) = 0.0054 +/- 0.0005 +/- 0.0021 GeV^3; chi^2/ndof ~ 4 for combined fit
- For Upsilon(2S): M_L(b\bar{b}([^3S_1]_8) -> Upsilon(2S)) = 0.0400 +/- 0.0016 +/- 0.0023 GeV^3; combined LDME M_L(b\bar{b}([^1S_0]_8,[^3P_0]_8) -> Upsilon(2S)) = 0.0405 +/- 0.0018 +/- 0.0029 GeV^3; chi^2/ndof ~ 3 for combined fit
- Colour-singlet matrix elements used: M_L(b\bar{b}([^3S_1]_1) -> Upsilon(3S)) = 4.3 GeV^3; M_L(b\bar{b}([^3S_1]_1) -> Upsilon(2S)) = 4.5 GeV^3; M_L(b\bar{b}([^3S_1]_1) -> Upsilon(1S)) = 10.9 GeV^3
- A minimum p_T cut of 8 GeV/c is applied for the fits

## Context

This work extends previous LO NRQCD analyses of bottomonia production (Domenech 2000, Brateen 2001, Sharma 2012) by incorporating the most up-to-date datasets including LHC 13 TeV CMS data and including feed-down from the chi_b(3P) state, which was not considered in many earlier studies. The results are compared with NLO analyses (Gong 2010, Gong 2013, Feng 2015) that show significant variation in extracted LDMEs; the present LO analysis provides a consistent set of LDMEs with improved precision that can serve as a reference for comparison with NLO calculations and enable predictions for bottomonia production at future collider energies.
