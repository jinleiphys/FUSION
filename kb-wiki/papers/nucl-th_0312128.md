# Hot Nuclear Matter in Asymmetry Chiral Sigma Model

> In the frame work of SU(2) chiral sigma model, the nuclear matter properties at zero and finite temperature have been investigated. We have analyzed the nuclear matter equation of state by varying different parameters, which agrees well with the one derived from the heavy-ion collision experiment at extreme densities and reliable realistic(DBHF) model at low density region. We have then calculated the temperature dependent asymmetric nuclear matter, also investigated the critical temperature of liquid gas phase transition and compared with the experimental data. We found that the critical temperature in our model is in the range of 14-20 MeV.

## Key claim

The modified SU(2) chiral sigma model, with higher-order scalar self-interactions added to achieve empirical incompressibility, produces an equation of state that agrees with heavy-ion collision data at high densities and with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) results at low densities, but only for specific parameter sets; the critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition is found to be in the range of 14–20 MeV, depending on the chosen incompressibility and effective mass.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field approach based on a SU(2) chiral sigma Lagrangian where the omega meson mass is generated dynamically via spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. They add higher-order scalar field terms (up to quartic) to control the nuclear matter incompressibility, and include a rho meson term for asymmetric matter. The five parameter sets (I–V) are fitted to saturation properties (binding energy −16.3 MeV, density 0.153 fm⁻³) with varying incompressibility (210–380 MeV) and effective mass (0.8–0.9 M), and the equation of state is computed at zero and finite temperature using Fermi-Dirac distributions for nucleons and anti-nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³; binding energy per nucleon: B/A = −16.3 MeV.
- Critical temperature for set I (K=210 MeV, m*/m=0.85): T_c = 14.2 MeV, with P_c = 0.14 MeV/fm³ and n_c = 0.035 fm⁻³.
- Critical temperature for set II (K=300 MeV, m*/m=0.85): T_c = 16.8 MeV, with P_c = 0.22 MeV/fm³ and n_c = 0.044 fm⁻³.
- Critical temperature for set III (K=380 MeV, m*/m=0.85): T_c = 20.4 MeV, with P_c = 0.36 MeV/fm³ and n_c = 0.051 fm⁻³.
- For set II, the critical temperature decreases from 16.8 MeV at α=0 (symmetric matter) to 11.2 MeV at α=1 (pure neutron matter), without rho meson.
- Symmetry energy coefficient fixed to a_sym = 32 MeV, giving c_ρ = 4.66 fm².
- At T = T_c for set I, the energy per particle is −2.04 MeV; for set II, 4.8 MeV; for set III, 13.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SU(2) chiral sigma model (Sahu 1993, Sahu 2000a) by adding higher-order scalar terms to correct the unrealistically high incompressibility found previously. It compares the resulting equation of state with the DBHF results from the Bonn A potential (Li 1992) and with the NL3 parameter set of the nonlinear relativistic mean-field model (Lalazissis 1997). The critical temperatures are compared with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions (Li 1994) reporting T_c = 13.1 ± 0.6 MeV, and with prior theoretical values from the original Walecka model (18.3 MeV, Serot 1986), nonlinear RMF (14.2 MeV, Furnstahl 1997), and DBHF (15.0 MeV, de Jong 1987; 8–9 MeV, Baldo 1995). The results enable further study of finite nuclei and matter at extreme densities and temperatures, with work in progress (Work 2003) to verify the model's validity.
