# Real photons produced from photoproduction in $pp$ collisions

> We calculate the production of real photons originating from the photoproduction in relativistic $pp$ collisions. The Weizs$\ddot{\mathrm{a}}$cker-Williams approximation in the photoproduction is considered. Numerical results agree with the experimental data from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We find that the modification of the photoproduction is more prominent in large transverse momentum region.

# Real photons produced from photoproduction in $pp$ collisions

## Key claim
The photoproduction mechanism (including resolved photoproduction) provides a significant higher-order modification to prompt photon production in relativistic proton-proton collisions, but this modification is weak at small transverse momentum and becomes prominent only in the large transverse momentum region, especially at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors calculate the invariant cross section for real photon production from photoproduction and resolved photoproduction in $pp$ collisions using the Weizsäcker-Williams approximation for the photon spectrum emitted from charged partons. They combine this with leading-order QED Compton scattering ($q\gamma \rightarrow q\gamma$) for photoproduction and QCD subprocesses ($q\bar{q}\rightarrow g\gamma$, $qg\rightarrow q\gamma$) for resolved photoproduction, using parton distribution functions for the proton and the resolved photon, and apply $P_T$-dependent $K$ factors to account for next-to-leading-order effects of direct and fragmentation photons.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV), the modification from photoproduction relative to direct plus fragmentation photons is approximately 12% in the region 5 GeV $< P_T <$ 16 GeV.
- At LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5500 GeV, $pp$ at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV), the modification from photoproduction is almost 33% in the region 20 GeV $< P_T <$ 200 GeV.
- The $K$ factors for direct photons are $K_{dir.\gamma}$(10 GeV) $\sim$ 1.5 for both RHIC and LHC.
- The $K$ factors for fragmentation photons are $K_{fra.\gamma}$(10 GeV) $\sim$ 1.8 at RHIC and 1.4 at LHC.
- The momentum scale used in the strong coupling constant is $\Lambda_{LO} = 0.2$ GeV, with $Q^2 = 4P_T^2$.

## Context
This work extends the photoproduction mechanism, previously important in $ep$ deep inelastic scattering at HERA (studied by various authors in the 1990s and 2000s), to relativistic $pp$ collisions at RHIC and LHC. It builds on prior calculations of leading-order prompt photon production from QCD Compton scattering, annihilation, and fragmentation (e.g., Aurenche et al., 2006) and next-to-leading-order contributions. The results enable more accurate modeling of prompt photon backgrounds in $pp$ collisions and suggest that photoproduction contributions must be included for precise comparisons with experimental data at high transverse momentum, particularly at LHC energies.
