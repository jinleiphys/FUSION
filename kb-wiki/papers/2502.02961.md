# Extraordinary nature of the nucleon scalar charge and its densities as a signal of nontrivial vacuum structure of QCD

> It is widely known that the nucleon scalar charge is proportional to the pion-nucleon sigma term as one of the important low energy observables of QCD. Especially interesting to us is the physics of the nucleon scalar charge densities. This comes from the fact that the corresponding operator has the same quantum number as the physical vacuum. It indicates unusual behavior of the the nucleon scalar density as a function of the distance $r$ from the nucleon center. Namely, it would not be reduced down to zero at the spatial infinity but rather approaches some nonzero constant corresponding to the vacuum quark condensate. Naturally, this unique nature of the nucleon scalar density in the position space also affects the corresponding density in the momentum space, i.e. the corresponding parton distribution function (PDF) as a function of the Bjorken variable $x$. This PDF is known as the chiral-odd twist-3 PDF $e (x)$. We argue that $e(x)$ is likely to have a delta-function type singularity at $x=0$, and that the appearance of this singularity can be interpreted as a signal of the nontrivial vacuum structure of the QCD.

## Key claim

The nucleon scalar charge density in coordinate space does not vanish at large distances but approaches a nonzero constant equal to the vacuum quark condensate, and this behavior implies that the chiral-odd twist-3 parton distribution function \(e(x)\) contains a delta-function singularity at \(x=0\) whose existence signals the nontrivial vacuum structure of QCD arising from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author uses the chiral quark soliton model (CQSM), a low-energy effective theory based on the instanton-liquid picture of the QCD vacuum, to compute the nucleon scalar charge density in coordinate space and the light-cone correlator \(E(z_0)\) that defines the twist-3 PDF \(e(x)\). The model treats quarks moving in a self-consistent hedgehog pion mean field, with the nucleon built by filling the valence orbital and all negative-energy Dirac-sea orbitals; ultraviolet divergences are removed by a double Pauli-Villars subtraction that also reproduces the empirical vacuum quark condensate. The asymptotic behavior of the light-cone correlator is extracted from the CQSM calculation and compared with that of the unpolarized PDF correlator.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon scalar charge \(\bar{\sigma}\) from lattice QCD (Alexandrou et al.) is given as a sum: \(\bar{\sigma}(u+d\,(\text{conn})) = 20.4(1.6)\), \(\bar{\sigma}(u+d\,(\text{disc})) = 3.04(59)\), \(\bar{\sigma}(s) = 1.00(13)\), \(\bar{\sigma}(c) = 0.175(36)\).
* The pion-nucleon sigma term from the same lattice calculation is \(\Sigma_{\pi N} \simeq 41.6\,(3.8)\,\text{MeV}\).
* In the MIT bag model, the scalar charge satisfies \(\bar{\sigma} < N_c = 3\), which is too small to match the empirical sigma term.
* The CQSM calculation shows that the Dirac-sea contribution to the light-cone correlator \(E(z_0)\) approaches a nonzero constant as \(z_0 \to \infty\), while the corresponding correlator for the unpolarized PDF \(f(x)\) approaches zero.

## Context

This work extends the earlier perturbative-QCD suggestion by Koike and Burkardt (2002) that a delta-function singularity exists in \(e(x)\), and the nonperturbative origin was traced to infinite-range scalar quark-quark correlations by Schweitzer (2003) and by Wakamatsu and Ohnishi (2003). The paper contrasts the CQSM predictions with the naive three-quark MIT bag model (Johnson, 1975), which fails to reproduce the vacuum condensate behavior, and with recent lattice QCD results (Alexandrou et al., 2020) for the scalar charge. The analysis enables future lattice QCD studies of the quasi-PDF version of \(e(x)\) via space-like correlators, as proposed by Ji (2013), to test whether the same infinite-range correlation appears in a first-principles framework.
