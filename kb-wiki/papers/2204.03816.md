# Emergent phenomena from centre vortices

> Quark confinement is perhaps the most important emergent property of the theory of quantum chromodynamics. Herein we review some key aspects of centre vortices in SU(3) lattice gauge theory. Starting from the original Monte Carlo gauge fields, a vortex identification procedure yields vortex-removed and vortex-only backgrounds. The comparison between the original `untouched' Monte Carlo gauge fields and these so called vortex-modified ensembles has provided a variety of results that support the notion that centre vortices are fundamental to confinement in pure gauge theory. For the first time we perform direct numerical tests of the response of centre vortices to the presence of dynamical quarks in SU(3).

## Key claim

Centre vortices remain identifiable and exhibit the same three-peaked centre-phase distribution in SU(3) gauge theory with dynamical fermions as in pure gauge theory, even though the global centre symmetry is explicitly broken by the quark fields.

## Method

The authors apply Maximal Centre Gauge (MCG) fixing to both pure-gauge and dynamical-fermion SU(3) lattice configurations, then project the gauge-fixed links to the nearest centre element of Z(3). This produces three ensembles: original untouched configurations, vortex-only configurations (the centre-projected links), and vortex-removed configurations. They compare the phase distribution of the link traces before and after MCG projection on both pure-gauge and dynamical ensembles, using the PACS-CS (2+1)-flavour full-QCD ensembles at a light pion mass of 156 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The dynamical-fermion configurations use a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice volume with a renormalisation-group improved Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively improved clover fermion action.
- The lightest pion mass studied on the dynamical ensembles is \(m_\pi = 156\) MeV.
- In pure gauge theory, the vortex-only field recreates approximately 2/3 of the untouched string tension and approximately 2/3 of the infrared strength of the untouched gluon propagator.

## Context

This work extends the extensive body of pure-gauge centre-vortex studies (including static quark potential investigations by Langfeld (2003) and Trewartha (2015), hadron spectrum studies by Trewartha (2017), and gluon propagator analyses by Biddle (2018)) into the unexplored territory of dynamical fermions. It provides the first direct numerical tests of centre vortices in SU(3) with dynamical quarks, enabling future investigations into how the vortex mechanism for confinement and chiral symmetry breaking is modified by matter fields, and addressing the long-standing question raised by 't Hooft (1977) of whether centre symmetry remains meaningful when quarks are present.
