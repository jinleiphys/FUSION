# Studying Maximal Entanglement and Bell Nonlocality at an Electron-Ion Collider

> In this paper, we propose to test quantum entanglement and Bell nonlocality at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). By computing the spin correlations in quark-antiquark pairs produced via photon-gluon fusion, we find that longitudinally polarized photons produce maximal entanglement at leading order, while transversely polarized photons generate significant entanglement near the threshold and in the ultra-relativistic regime. Compared to hadron colliders, the EIC provides a cleaner experimental environment for measuring entanglement through the $γ^\ast g \to q\bar{q}$ channel, offering a strong signal and a promising avenue to verify Bell nonlocality. This study extends entanglement measurements to the EIC, presenting new opportunities to explore the interplay of quantum information phenomena and hadronic physics in the EIC era.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can test quantum entanglement and Bell nonlocality via the photon-gluon fusion channel, claiming that longitudinally polarized photons produce maximal entanglement at leading order and transversely polarized photons generate significant entanglement near threshold and in the ultra-relativistic regime, but the experimental feasibility depends on the ability to separate longitudinal and transverse photon contributions, which the authors acknowledge is challenging.

## Method

The authors computed the spin density matrix for quark-antiquark pairs produced via the photon-gluon fusion process at leading order, separately analyzing longitudinal and transverse virtual photon contributions. They derived the spin correlation matrix C in the helicity basis of the center-of-mass frame and evaluated the concurrence and Bell nonlocality measure as functions of the quark velocity beta, the angular variable z, and the photon virtuality parameter alpha. The measurement strategy relies on the self-analyzing property of weak decays, where the angular distributions of decay leptons or baryons (from heavy quark or hyperon decays) encode the spin correlations of the parent quark-antiquark pair.

## Key numbers

- Concurrence for longitudinal photons: C[rho_L] = 1 (maximal entanglement) at leading order for all kinematic configurations.
- Nonlocality measure for longitudinal photons: N[rho_L] = 1 (maximal violation of Bell inequality) at leading order.
- For transverse photons at the production threshold (beta = 0), the spin correlation matrix becomes C_ij = -delta_ij, corresponding to the spin singlet state.
- The entanglement condition for diagonal correlation matrices with nonpositive elements reduces to D = tr[C]/3 < -1/3, where D can be measured as D = -3 <cos phi> from the angular distribution of decay leptons.
- The Bell inequality violation condition is N[rho] = mu_1 + mu_2 - 1 > 0, where mu_1 >= mu_2 >= mu_3 are eigenvalues of C^T C, with maximally entangled states achieving N = 1 and the quantum bound B <= 2 sqrt(2).

## Context

This work extends entanglement measurements from hadron colliders (where ATLAS and CMS have observed entanglement in top quark pairs at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV) to the EIC environment, addressing the lack of theoretical studies in electron-proton collisions noted by Afik (2025). The photon-gluon fusion channel at the EIC offers a cleaner experimental environment compared to the LHC, where the quark annihilation channel complicates Bell inequality tests. The results enable future studies of decoherence in eA collisions at the EIC, where entangled quark-antiquark pairs traversing nuclear matter could probe nuclear medium properties, and suggest extensions to ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC.
