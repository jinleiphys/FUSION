# General Tensor Structure for Inclusive and Semi-inclusive Electron Scattering from Polarized Spin 1/2 Targets

> The general structure of semi-inclusive polarized electron scattering from polarized spin-1/2 targets is developed for use at all energy scales, from modest-energy nuclear physics applications to use in very high energy particle physics. The leptonic and hadronic tensors that enter in the formalism are constructed in a general covariant way in terms of kinematic factors that are frame dependent but model independent and invariant response functions which contain all of the model-dependent dynamics. In the process of developing the general problem the relationships to the conventional responses expressed in terms of the helicity components of the exchanged virtual photon are presented. For semi-inclusive electron scattering with polarized electrons and polarized spin-1/2 targets one finds that 18 invariant response functions are required, each depending on four Lorentz scalar invariants. Additionally it is shown how the semi-inclusive cross sections are related via integrations over the momentum of the selected coincidence particle and sums over open channels.

# General Tensor Structure for Inclusive and Semi-inclusive Electron Scattering from Polarized Spin 1/2 Targets

## Key claim

The paper develops a fully general, covariant formalism for semi-inclusive polarized electron scattering from polarized spin-1/2 targets that is valid at all energy scales, from nuclear physics to high-energy particle physics, but the 18 invariant response functions required are each functions of four Lorentz scalar invariants and the decomposition into helicity projections of the virtual photon is frame-dependent even though the full contraction of leptonic and hadronic tensors is Lorentz invariant.

## Method

The authors construct the leptonic and hadronic tensors in a general covariant way using kinematic factors that are frame-dependent but model-independent, and invariant response functions that contain all model-dependent dynamics. They decompose the problem into four sectors based on polarization combinations (unpolarized, electron polarized only, target polarized only, both polarized) and separate symmetric and anti-symmetric tensor contributions, yielding 18 invariant response functions. The formalism is developed in a general frame to allow transformation between the target rest frame, the collider frame, and the photon-target center-of-momentum frame, with explicit connections made to the conventional helicity-component representation of the virtual photon.

## Key numbers

* For semi-inclusive electron scattering with polarized electrons and polarized spin-1/2 targets, 18 invariant response functions are required, each depending on four Lorentz scalar invariants.
* The 18 responses sub-divide into two sets of nine according to their properties under parity and time-reversal.
* For the fully unpolarized case there are 4 symmetric invariant responses; for electron polarized but target unpolarized there is 1 anti-symmetric invariant response; for electron unpolarized but target polarized there are 8 symmetric invariant responses; for both electrons and target polarized there are 5 anti-symmetric invariant responses.
* In the Extreme Relativistic Limit for electrons (ERL_e), the contraction of leptonic and hadronic tensors reduces to 6 classes of response: 4 involving symmetric contributions (V_L W^L, V_T W^T, V_TL W^TL, V_TT W^TT) and 2 involving anti-symmetric contributions (h V_T' W^T', h V_TL' W^TL').
* The photon longitudinal polarization is defined as epsilon = [1 + (2/rho) tan^2(theta_e/2)]^{-1}, where rho = -Q^2/q^2 ranges from 0 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Donnelly (1985) for inclusive electron scattering to the semi-inclusive case with polarized spin-1/2 targets, providing a general covariant framework applicable from nuclear physics to high-energy collider physics. The development enables connections between non-relativistic models valid only in the target rest frame (such as for polarized 3He) and relativistic formulations needed for collider kinematics, and provides the mathematical structure for relating semi-inclusive cross sections to inclusive ones via integrations over the momentum of the detected coincidence particle and sums over open channels. The paper explicitly discusses applications to charged pion production from polarized protons and to polarized 3He(e,e'p) reactions.
