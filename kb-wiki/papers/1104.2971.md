# Non-Perturbative Relativistic Calculation of the Muonic Hydrogen Spectrum

> We investigate the muonic hydrogen 2P^{F=2}_{3/2} to 2S^{F=1}_{1/2} transition through a precise, non-perturbative numerical solution of the Dirac equation including the finite-size Coulomb force and finite size vacuum polarization. The results are compared with earlier perturbative calculations of (primarily) Borie, Martynenko, and Pachucki; and experimental results recently presented by Pohl et al., in which this very comparison is interpreted as requiring a modification of the proton charge radius from that obtained in electron scattering and electronic hydrogen analyses. We find no significant discrepancy between the perturbative and non-perturbative calculations, and present our results as confirmation of the perturbative methods.

## Key claim

The authors find no significant discrepancy between their non-perturbative relativistic Dirac-equation calculation of the muonic hydrogen 2P_{3/2}^{F=2} to 2S_{1/2}^{F=1} transition and earlier perturbative calculations by Borie, Martynenko, and Pachucki, thereby confirming the adequacy of the perturbative methods at the level of precision required to interpret the experimental result of Pohl et al. (2010).

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically in quadruple-precision Fortran for the muon wave functions, including the finite-size Coulomb potential (using an exponential charge distribution for the proton) and finite-size vacuum polarization (Uehling potential convolved with the proton charge distribution). They iteratively refined the eigenvalue for each state until the normalized discontinuity at a central matching point fell below the required tolerance, and validated their solutions using the virial theorem. The transition energy was obtained by taking differences between converged eigenvalues for the relevant states, with the dependence on the proton charge radius extracted by fitting energy shifts calculated at various values of r_p to a cubic polynomial.

## Key numbers

- Lamb shift (2S_{1/2} to 2P_{1/2}) from Dirac equation with point-Coulomb and point-vacuum polarization potentials: 205.1706(5) meV
- Lamb shift from Dirac equation with finite-Coulomb and finite-vacuum polarization potentials: 205.1822 - 5.2519 ⟨r_p²⟩ + 0.0546 ⟨r_p²⟩^{3/2} meV
- 2P fine structure splitting from Dirac equation with finite-Coulomb and finite-vacuum polarization potentials: 8.4206(5) meV
- 2S_{1/2} hyperfine splitting from Dirac equation with finite-Coulomb and finite-vacuum polarization potentials (using the contact-term method): 22.7690(5) meV
- 2S_{1/2} hyperfine splitting from Dirac equation (using the Borie method): 22.7640(5) meV
- Estimated numerical error in eigenvalues: less than ±500 neV, corresponding to a proton rms charge-radius uncertainty of approximately 0.05 am (5×10^{-5} fm)

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic calculations by Borie (2004, 1982, 1975) by providing a fully non-perturbative Dirac-equation treatment of the muonic hydrogen spectrum, including finite-size effects in both the Coulomb and vacuum polarization potentials. The results confirm the perturbative calculations used by Pohl et al. (2010) to extract a proton charge radius of 0.84184(67) fm from their measured Lamb shift, which differs by 4.9 standard deviations from the CODATA value of 0.8768(69) fm. The authors note that the finite-size dependence of the 2S hyperfine splitting (not parameterized in the Pohl et al. analysis) contributes a correction of 0.0087(5) meV to 0.0100(5) meV, representing 2.8-3.2% of the 0.31 meV discrepancy, and that the dependence on the choice of proton charge distribution shape (exponential, Yukawa, or Gaussian) appears extremely weak.
