# Dynamical and invariant supersymmetry in the fermion pairing problem

> We argue that fermion-boson mapping techniques represent a natural tool for studying many-body supersymmetry in fermionic systems with pairing. In particular, using the generalized Dyson mapping of a many-level fermion superalgebra with the SU(2) type of pairing we investigate two kinds of supersymmetry connecting excitations in the systems with even and odd particle numbers: dynamical supersymmetry, which ensures a unified classification of states for both even and odd populations, and invariant supersymmetry with actual degeneracies of states within the same supermultiplet. Dynamical supersymmetries based on the dynamical algebra of the type U(K/2Ω) (where K and 2Ωdenote the number of fermion-pair and single-fermion states, respectively) naturally arise in the bosonized description of the system. Conditions for invariant supersymmetry are determined in a restricted case of bilinear supercharge operators.

## Key claim

The paper argues that dynamical supersymmetry in the fermion pairing problem, which unifies classification of states in even and odd fermion systems, naturally arises from boson-fermion mapping techniques and is always restricted to the form U(K/2Ω) ⊃ U_B(K) ⊗ U_F(2Ω), meaning it conserves boson and fermion numbers separately and should be referred to as dynamical boson-fermion symmetry. In contrast, invariant supersymmetry with actual degeneracies between states of different particle number is shown to be difficult to realize microscopically and depends critically on the precise nature of the fermionic interaction.

## Method

The authors apply a generalized Dyson mapping to transform the fermion superalgebra of a many-level pairing system with SU(2)-type pairing into a boson-fermion description. They analyze both noncollective pairs (single-shell bifermions B_k†) and collective pairs (C_μ† formed as linear combinations of single-shell pairs) and derive the mapped Hamiltonian and physical subspace conditions. The conditions for dynamical and invariant supersymmetry are then examined in terms of the parameters E_k and G_kl of the original fermion pairing Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The number of fermion-pair states is denoted K and the number of single-fermion states is 2Ω, with the dynamical superalgebra being of type U(K/2Ω).
- The number of single-particle states Ω gives a total fermion capacity of N_F ∈ [0, 2Ω] fermions.
- For collective pairs, the condition K/Ω ≥ √(1 + 1/Ω + (Ξ/Ω)²) − 1 typically yields K ≪ Ω when Ξ ≪ Ω, where Ξ is the number of states in the pairing-active subset.
- In the noncollective mapping, the physical subspace projector restricts each k-shell to at most one boson (n_k = 0 or 1) and at most one ideal fermion (N_k = 0 or 1), with the combination n_k = 1 and N_k = 1 forbidden.
- The overlap of the condensate state with the physical subspace is given by |∑_{k1≠k2≠…≠k_{N_B}} η_{k1}η_{k2}…η_{k_{N_B}}|² / ((∑_k |η_k|²)^{N_B} N_B!), which is less than unity for 1 < N_B ≤ N_F/2.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic analysis of Cejnar (2001) on collective superalgebras in the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) to study both dynamical and invariant supersymmetry in fermion pairing systems. It clarifies the distinction between dynamical supersymmetry (as observed experimentally by Metz, Jolie et al. in nuclear data) and invariant supersymmetry (proposed by Jolos and von Brentano), showing that while dynamical boson-fermion symmetry is firmly established from the nuclear interaction, invariant supersymmetry with actual state degeneracies remains hypothetical and difficult to realize. The analysis enables future microscopic investigations of whether realistic nuclear Hamiltonians can support invariant supersymmetry conditions.
