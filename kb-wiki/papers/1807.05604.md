# Chiral Magnetic Effect in Isobaric Collisions from Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD)

> The isobaric collision experiment at RHIC provides the unique opportunity to detect the possible signal of Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in heavy ion collisions. The idea is to contrast the correlation observables of the two colliding systems that supposedly have identical flow-driven background contributions while quite different CME signal contributions due to the 10% variation in their nuclear charge and thus magnetic field strength. With the recently developed quantitative simulation tool for computing CME signal, the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD), we demonstrate that a joint (multiplicity + elliptic-flow) event selection is crucial for this purpose. We further propose to use the absolute difference between RuRu and ZrZr events (after using identical event selection) for detecting CME signal and make predictions for the correlation observables.

## Key claim
The isobaric collision experiment at RHIC can detect the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) only if a joint (multiplicity + elliptic-flow) event selection is applied to ensure that RuRu and ZrZr events have identical flow-driven background contributions, because conventional centrality-based selection leaves a few percent level difference in elliptic flow between the two systems.

## Method
The authors used the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework, upgraded to an event-by-event tool (EBE-AVFD), to simulate millions of collision events for both RuRu and ZrZr systems at 40-50% centrality. They applied a joint event selection cut requiring identical multiplicity (64 < Nch < 96) and elliptic flow (0.05 < v2 < 0.25) for both systems, then computed the absolute difference in correlation observables (γ and δ) between the two isobar systems for varied CME signal strengths controlled by the initial axial charge density n5/s.

## Key numbers
- The 10% variation in nuclear charge between Ru and Zr leads to a 10-25% relative difference in projected magnetic-field-strength-squared between the two systems.
- With conventional centrality selection, the relative difference in eccentricity between RuRu and ZrZr is at the few percent level (approximately 1-3% depending on nuclear deformation assumptions).
- With the proposed joint event selection, the relative difference in eccentricity is reduced to approximately 0.1%, while the magnetic field difference remains at 10-25%.
- The analysis focused on 40-50% centrality with event selection cuts of 64 < Nch < 96 and 0.05 < v2 < 0.25.
- The initial axial charge density range suggested by AuAu collision analysis is n5/s ~ (5-10)%.
- The number of collected isobar events at RHIC is about 100 times more than the AVFD simulation sample, giving experimental statistical error bars about 10 times smaller than the simulation results shown.

## Context
This work extends the quantitative modeling of CME signals using the AVFD framework developed by Shi et al. (2017) and addresses the background subtraction problem identified by Bzdak (2009) in the γ-correlator analysis. The proposed joint event selection method and absolute difference observable build on the isobaric collision concept proposed by Skokov (2016) and the R-correlator method developed by Magdy, Lacey, et al. (2017, 2018). The results enable experimental analysis of the recently completed RHIC isobar run (2018) to detect CME signals by providing quantitative predictions for correlation observables.
