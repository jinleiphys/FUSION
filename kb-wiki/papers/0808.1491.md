# The three-flavor chiral phase structure in hot and dense QCD matter

> Chiral symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature and quark densities are investigated in the framework of a linear sigma model with N_f=3 light quark flavors. After the derivation of the grand potential in mean-field approximation, the nonstrange and strange condensates, the in-medium masses of the scalar and pseudoscalar nonets are analyzed in hot and dense medium. The influence of the axial anomaly on the nonet masses and the isoscalar mixings on the pseudoscalar η-η' and scalar σ(600)-f_0(1370) complex are examined. The sensitivity of the chiral phase transition as well as the existence and location of a critical end point in the phase diagram on the value of the sigma mass is explored. The chiral critical surface with and without the influence of the axial U(1)_A anomaly is elaborated as a function of the pion and kaon masses for several values of the sigma mass.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in hot and dense QCD matter, studied within a three-flavor linear sigma model in mean-field approximation, is sensitive to the poorly known sigma meson mass; the existence and location of a critical end point in the phase diagram depend on this input parameter, and the chiral critical surface is mapped as a function of pion and kaon masses for several sigma mass values, both with and without the axial U(1)_A anomaly.

## Method

The authors used a linear sigma model with three quark flavors (N_f=3) and derived the grand thermodynamic potential in mean-field approximation, neglecting meson fluctuations but retaining quarks as quantum fields. The model parameters were fixed by fitting to vacuum pseudoscalar masses (pion, kaon, eta, eta'), decay constants (f_pi, f_K), and the sigma meson mass, which was varied between 400 and 1000 MeV. The nonstrange and strange condensates, in-medium meson masses, and the chiral phase diagram were then computed as functions of temperature and quark chemical potential, with and without the axial U(1)_A anomaly term.

## Key numbers

* For a sigma mass of 800 MeV, the nonstrange condensate starts at 92.4 MeV and the strange condensate at 94.5 MeV at zero temperature and chemical potential.
* The pseudocritical temperature for the nonstrange condensate at zero chemical potential is approximately 181 MeV (for sigma mass 800 MeV).
* At zero temperature, the light quark Fermi surface is reached at mu = 300 MeV (light constituent quark mass), and the first-order chiral transition occurs at a critical chemical potential mu_c ~ 352 MeV.
* The strange constituent quark mass is predicted to be approximately 433 MeV for a light quark mass of 300 MeV and Yukawa coupling g ~ 6.5.

## Context

This work extends a previous two-flavor linear sigma model analysis (Schaefer, 2006) to three quark flavors, incorporating the axial U(1)_A anomaly via an effective 't Hooft determinant term. It contrasts with lattice QCD simulations that use the imaginary chemical potential method, which for three physical quark masses find no critical endpoint (de Forcrand, 2003; D'Elia, 2004). The results enable systematic exploration of how the poorly known sigma meson mass affects the chiral phase diagram and the location of the critical end point, and provide predictions for in-medium meson masses that can be tested by future heavy-ion experiments at FAIR.
