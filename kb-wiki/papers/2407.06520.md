# Unveiling the Secrets of Vortex Neutron Decay

> Investigation of decay and scattering processes of particles in a vortex state offers a novel and promising approach for probing particle structure. Our study reveals distinct properties of vortex neutron decay, which deviate from those of classical plane-wave neutron decay. We present the energy-angle distributions of the final-state electron and antineutrino in unpolarized vortex neutrons, as well as angle distributions integrated over their energies. Notably, we provide theoretical calculations of the decay behavior of neutrons with varying vortex cone angles and initial energies. We propose that identifying the vortex state of the initial neutron can be achieved by analyzing the angular and energy distributions of the final-state particles, introducing new degrees of freedom for studying weak interactions and neutron decay kinematics that have been previously overlooked in particle physics. This timely investigation takes advantage of recent advancements in vortex neutron preparation and analysis, opening up new avenues for exploring the fundamental properties of matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the beta-decay energy-angle distributions of a vortex (twisted) neutron differ from those of a classical plane-wave neutron, and that these differences depend on the vortex cone angle and the initial neutron energy; however, the decay width does not depend on the intrinsic orbital angular momentum (OAM) quantum number of the vortex state because the OAM phase factor cancels when the squared amplitude is averaged over the initial state.

## Method

The authors construct an unpolarized Bessel vortex state for the initial neutron, which is a superposition of plane-wave components with fixed energy, longitudinal momentum, and transverse momentum modulus, distributed uniformly in azimuthal angle on a cone. They then compute the differential decay width for the tree-level beta decay \(n \to p + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e\) by averaging the plane-wave decay width over the azimuthal angle of the vortex neutron's momentum, using the standard four-fermion interaction with the Fermi constant \(G_F\) and the chiral operator \(\gamma_\alpha(1+\gamma_5)\). The integration over the vortex azimuthal angle is split into two regimes depending on whether the electron energy lies below or above a threshold defined by the cone angle and the electron scattering angle.

## Key numbers

* Neutron mass \(m_n = 939.57\) MeV, proton mass \(m_p = 938.27\) MeV, electron mass \(m_e = 0.511\) MeV (rest masses used in the calculation).
* Fermi constant \(G_F \simeq 1.166 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* For a moving plane-wave neutron, the total decay width is \(\Gamma_{PW} = \Gamma_0 / \gamma_n\), where \(\gamma_n\) is the Lorentz factor and \(\Gamma_0\) is the stationary-neutron total width.
* Numerical results are presented for neutron speeds \(\beta_n = 0.1\) and \(0.5\), with vortex cone opening angles \(\theta_0 = 0.1\), \(0.5\), and \(1.0\) rad, and fixed electron scattering angles \(\theta_e = \pi/20\) and \(\pi/3\).

## Context

This work extends the study of vortex-state particles in high-energy physics, following prior theoretical developments on vortex-state collisions (Ivanov, 2019) and decays (Zhao, 2021), and builds on the experimental preparation of vortex neutrons (Clark, 2015; Sarenac, 2018, 2019). The results enable the identification of the vortex state of an initial neutron by measuring the angular and energy distributions of the final-state electron and antineutrino, introducing the intrinsic OAM as a new degree of freedom for studying weak interactions and neutron decay kinematics. The authors note that the analysis is simplified because the initial state contains only one vortex particle, so no interference occurs, and the decay width is independent of the OAM quantum number.
