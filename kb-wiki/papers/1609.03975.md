# van der Waals Interactions in Hadron Resonance Gas: From Nuclear Matter to Lattice QCD

> An extension of the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model is constructed which includes the attractive and repulsive van der Waals (VDW) interactions between baryons. This VDW-HRG model yields the nuclear liquid-gas transition at low temperatures and high baryon densities. The VDW parameters $a$ and $b$ are fixed by the ground state properties of nuclear matter, and the temperature dependence of various thermodynamic observables at zero chemical potential are calculated within VDW-HRG model. Compared to the ideal HRG model, the inclusion of VDW interactions between baryons leads to a qualitatively different behavior of second and higher moments of fluctuations of conserved charges, in particular in the so-called crossover region $T\sim 140 - 190$ MeV. For many observables this behavior resembles closely the results obtained from lattice QCD simulations. This hadronic model also predicts nontrivial behavior of net-baryon fluctuations in the region of phase diagram probed by heavy-ion collision experiments. These results imply that VDW interactions play a crucial role in thermodynamics of hadron gas. Thus, the commonly performed comparisons of the ideal HRG model with the lattice and heavy-ion data may lead to misconceptions and misleading conclusions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of van der Waals (VDW) interactions between baryons in the hadron resonance gas model yields a qualitatively different behavior of fluctuations of conserved charges in the crossover region (T ~ 140-190 MeV) that resembles lattice QCD results, implying that the commonly performed comparisons of the ideal HRG model with lattice and heavy-ion data may lead to misconceptions and misleading conclusions. The authors hedge that the quantitative agreement is not perfect, and that the VDW interactions between baryons are modeled in the simplest way possible, assuming all baryon pairs have the same VDW parameters as nucleons.

## Method

The authors extend the ideal hadron resonance gas (IHRG) model by including both attractive and repulsive van der Waals interactions between all pairs of baryons and between all pairs of antibaryons, using the VDW parameters a = 329 MeV fm^3 and b = 3.42 fm^3 fixed from the ground state properties of nuclear matter (saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and binding energy E/A = -16 MeV). The VDW-HRG model treats mesons as non-interacting, neglects baryon-antibaryon, meson-meson, and meson-baryon VDW interactions, and solves the grand canonical ensemble equations numerically to obtain thermodynamic observables and fluctuations of conserved charges at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy of nuclear matter ground state: E/A = -16 MeV
- VDW parameter a for nucleons: a = 329 MeV fm^3
- VDW parameter b for nucleons: b = 3.42 fm^3
- Critical point of liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter: Tc = 19.7 MeV, μc = 908 MeV, nc = 0.07 fm^3 = 0.45 n0
- Crossover temperature range at zero chemical potential: T ~ 140-190 MeV
- Minimum of speed of sound squared in VDW-HRG: T ~ 155-160 MeV
- Net-baryon kurtosis χ4^B/χ2^B turns negative in VDW-HRG at T > 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends the ideal hadron resonance gas model by incorporating van der Waals interactions between baryons, building on previous formulations of the VDW equation with quantum statistics for single-component systems by Vovchenko et al. (2015). The results contradict the interpretation by Karsch (2013) that hadrons melt quickly above 160 MeV, and challenge the claim by Bellwied et al. (2013) that flavor hierarchy in χ4/χ2 ratios traces back to deconfinement. The model enables future studies of net-baryon fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions as possible manifestations of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, and suggests that thermal fits to hadron yield data should account for VDW interactions.
