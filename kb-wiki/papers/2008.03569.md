# Exploring the Collective Phenomenon at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Based on rare fluctuations in strong interactions, we argue that there is a strong physical resemblance between the high multiplicity events in photo-nuclear collisions and those in $pA$ collisions, in which interesting long range collective phenomena are discovered. This indicates that the collectivity can also be studied in certain kinematic region of the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) where the incoming virtual photon has a sufficiently long lifetime. Using a model in the Color Glass Condensate formalism, we first show that the initial state interactions can explain the recent ATLAS azimuthal correlation results measured in the photo-nuclear collisions, and then we provide quantitative predictions for the long range correlations in $eA$ collisions in the EIC regime. With the unprecedented precision and the ability to change the size of the collisional system, the high luminosity EIC will open a new window to explore the physical mechanism responsible for the collective phenomenon.

## Key claim

The authors argue that high-multiplicity events in electron-nucleus (eA) collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can exhibit collective long-range correlations, provided the virtual photon has sufficiently low virtuality (Q^2) and small Bjorken x_B, such that its QCD fluctuations produce a hadron-like partonic state with a lifetime longer than the interaction time with the target.

## Method

The authors use a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with dilute-dense factorization, treating the virtual photon as a hadron-like projectile with a Gaussian Wigner distribution for partons in transverse coordinate and momentum space. They compute two-particle azimuthal correlations by evaluating the background average of two dipole scattering amplitudes, including 1/N_c^2 corrections that convert dipoles into quadrupoles, and then Fourier-transform to obtain the elliptic flow harmonic v_2. The model is first compared to ATLAS photo-nuclear ultra-peripheral PbPb collision data, then extended to predict v_2 in the EIC regime by varying the photon virtuality Q.

## Key numbers

- For the ATLAS photo-nuclear data comparison, the model uses parameters Delta = 0.5 GeV and Q_s^2 = 5 GeV^2, the same as in prior pA studies (Zhang 2019, Zhang 2020).
- In photo-nuclear collisions at the LHC, the typical photon virtuality Q is of order 30 MeV, and the parton transverse spread B_p is set to 25 GeV^{-2} due to color confinement.
- For EIC predictions, the authors assume Lambda_QCD <= Q < 1 GeV and set B_p = 1/Q^2 (or 4/Q^2), with Q_s^2 = 4 GeV^2 fixed.
- The ATLAS UPC data used for comparison had an integrated luminosity of 1.73 nb^{-1}, while the planned EIC integrated luminosity is 10 fb^{-1}/year.
- The model is applicable only in the low p_perp regime and for low Q^2 where the ratio Q/Q_s is small.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based initial-state explanation of collectivity in small systems, previously applied to pA collisions by Mace (2018), Zhang (2019), and Zhang (2020), to the new domain of electron-nucleus collisions at the EIC. It directly addresses the mixed experimental signals from LEP e+e- collisions (Badea 2019), HERA ep collisions (ZEUS 2019), and ATLAS photo-nuclear PbPb collisions (ATLAS 2019, Aad 2021). The predictions enable future EIC measurements to distinguish between initial-state (CGC) and final-state (hydrodynamic) interpretations of collectivity, and the authors note that a new event generator for gamma*A collisions based on Mueller's dipole evolution (Bierlich 2019) is under development.
