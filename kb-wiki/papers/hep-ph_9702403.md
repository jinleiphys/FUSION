# Thermal Effective Potential of the O(N) Linear sigma Model

> The finite-temperature effective potential of the O(N) linear σmodel is studied, with emphasis on the implications for the investigation of hot hadron dynamics. The contributions from all the ``bubble diagrams'' are fully taken into account for arbitrary N; this also allows to address some long-standing issues concerning the use of non-perturbative approaches in (finite-temperature) field theory.

## Key claim

The Hartree bubble-resummed thermal effective potential of the O(N) linear sigma model is renormalizable for arbitrary N when all bubble diagrams are included with correct counting and symmetry factors, but the renormalized theory is only consistent as an effective low-energy description with a finite cutoff, because the bare coupling becomes negative above the Landau pole.

## Method

The author uses the CJT (Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis) formalism to compute the finite-temperature effective potential by resumming all bubble diagrams (the Hartree approximation) for the O(N) linear sigma model. The dressed propagators are obtained from gap equations for the sigma and pion masses, and the ultraviolet divergences are isolated in the tadpole and one-loop contributions. Renormalized coupling and mass are introduced to obtain finite expressions, and the resulting renormalized gap equations and effective potential are written in terms of renormalized quantities plus terms proportional to the bare coupling that vanish in the infinite-cutoff limit.

## Key numbers

* The tadpole integral contains divergent contributions I1 = Lambda^2/(8 pi^2) and I2 = (ln Lambda^2/mu^2)/(16 pi^2), where Lambda is the ultraviolet momentum cutoff and mu is the renormalization scale.
* The renormalized coupling lambda_R is related to the bare coupling lambda by 1/lambda_R = 1/lambda + (2/3)(N+2)/N * I2, and the renormalized mass m_R^2 by m_R^2/lambda_R = m^2/lambda + (2/3)(N+2)/N * I1.
* For N=4 (the physically relevant case for hadron dynamics), the large-N approximation may be too drastic, but the Hartree approach is renormalizable for arbitrary N.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigation of the ultraviolet structure of the Hartree thermal effective potential in a two-scalar-field theory (Amelino-Camelia, 1996) to the O(N) linear sigma model. It addresses long-standing issues raised by Dolan and Jackiw (1974) and Kirzhnits and Linde (1976) regarding the resummation of bubble diagrams at high temperature. The paper enables future studies of the chiral phase transition in hot hadron dynamics, particularly for the O(4) linear sigma model relevant to ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and provides a way to estimate the effects neglected by the leading-order large-N approximation.
