# A simple qualitative description of EMC ratios μ^A for 0.2 <~ x <~ 1.5 and some sample calculations

> We study EMC ratios on the basis of a relation between Structure Functions (SF) for a nucleus and for a nucleon, which is governed by a SF f^{PN,A}(x,Q^2) of an unphysical nucleus, composed of point-nucleons. We demonstrate that the characteristic features of EMC ratios μ^A are determined by the above f^{PN,A} and the SF of free nucleons. We account for the positions of the points x_{1,2} in the interval 0.2 <~ x <~ 0.9, where μ^A(x,Q^2)=1 and also for the minimum x_m in that interval. We similarly describe the oscillations in μ^A for Q^2 <~ (3.5-4.0) GeV^2 in the Quasi-Elastic peak region 0.95 <~ x <~ 1.05 and for its subsequent continuous increase up to x\approx 1.4. Finally we compute μ^A over the entire range above for A=^4He, C, Fe and Au and several Q^2 values. The results are in reasonable agreement with both directly measured and indirectly extracted data.

## Key claim

The characteristic features of EMC ratios mu^A(x,Q^2) across the entire range 0.2 <~ x <~ 1.5 can be qualitatively understood from the properties of a single structure function f^{PN,A}(x,Q^2) of a fictitious nucleus composed of point-nucleons, combined with the structure function of free nucleons. The authors claim this provides a simple description without requiring medium modifications of nucleons or exotic mechanisms, though they acknowledge the approach is heuristic and the computed ratios show only reasonable agreement with data, not perfect precision.

## Method

The authors use a previously postulated relation connecting nuclear structure functions F_2^A to the weighted nucleon structure function F_2^{<N>} via the point-nucleon structure function f^{PN,A}(x,Q^2), which is a covariant generalization of the non-relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory for inclusive scattering. They compute f^{PN,A} from many-body density matrices constructed from nuclear ground state wave functions, using exact calculations for light nuclei (A <= 4) and approximate methods for heavier targets (C, Fe, Au). The EMC ratios mu^A = F_2^A/F_2^D are then computed by separating the nuclear structure functions into nucleon-elastic (NE) and nucleon-inelastic (NI) components, with the relative importance of each component depending on x and Q^2.

## Key numbers

- EMC ratios in the classical regime 0.2 <~ x <~ 0.9 show deviations from 1 of |1-mu^A| between approximately 0 and 0.15-0.20, with little A or Q^2 dependence
- The second crossing point x_2^A where mu^A = 1 occurs at approximately x ~ 0.85 for A >= 12, with x_2 larger for ^4He than for heavier nuclei
- For Q^2 <~ 3.5-4.0 GeV^2, the minimum at x ~ 1 reaches a depth of approximately 0.35 for A >= 12 and approximately 0.50 for A = 3,4
- EMC ratios in the deep quasi-elastic region 1.05 <~ x <~ 1.4 reach maxima of order 4-7 before leveling off
- Computations were performed at fixed Q^2 = 3.5, 5.0, and 10.0 GeV^2 for targets ^4He, C, Fe, and Au over the range 0.2 <~ x <~ 1.5
- Average separation energies used: 20.2 MeV for ^4He, 40 MeV for C, 45 MeV for Fe and Au
- The slope s^A(x ~ x_1) of the EMC ratio near the first crossing point is approximately -0.3, nearly A-independent

## Context

This work extends the covariant GRS approach previously applied to inclusive scattering data (Rinat and Taragin, 2003; Rinat, Taragin, and Viviani, 2003) to the specific problem of EMC ratios, building on a first version that reasonably accounted for mu^Fe in the classical region (Rinat and Taragin, 2002). The paper contrasts with the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) approach, which the authors note has not converged onto a unanimously accepted understanding, with some authors concluding Fermi averaging and binding corrections do not account for data (Smith and Miller, 1995; Arrington et al., 2005) while others reach opposite conclusions (Akulinichev et al., 1985; Oset et al., 1995; Poles and Thomas, 1999). The approach enables predictions for the currently running JLab E03-103 experiment on D, ^3He, and ^4He targets, which will test the predicted Q^2-dependence of mu^A and the gradual fading of the minimum at x=1 for increasing Q^2.
