# Azimuthal angle distributions of neutrons emitted from the $^9$Be($γ$,$n$) reaction with linearly polarized $γ$-rays

> The electromagnetic transitions of $^9$Be with linearly polarized $γ$-rays are calculated by using the $α$~+~$α$~+~$n$ three-body model and the complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation; the azimuthal angle distributions of the emitted neutrons are investigated. We calculate the anisotropy parameter as a function of the photon incident energy $E_γ$, and discuss how sensitive the anisotropy parameter is to nuclear structure and transition modes. The result suggests that the azimuthal angle distribution of neutrons emitted from the $^9$Be($γ$,$n$) reaction with the linearly polarized $γ$-rays is useful to identify the resonances in the final states even if it is not clearly observed in the cross section.

## Key claim

The azimuthal angle distribution of neutrons from the \(^9\)Be(\(\gamma\),\(n\)) reaction with linearly polarized \(\gamma\)-rays is sensitive to nuclear structure and can identify resonances in the final states that are not clearly observed in the cross section, but the anisotropy parameter for \(1/2^\pm\) resonances is suppressed due to limitations on the \(z\)-component of the total spin.

## Method

The authors used an \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) three-body orthogonality condition model (OCM) with the KKNN potential for \(\alpha\)-\(n\) interactions and a folding potential for \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) interactions, including a phenomenological three-body potential. Final scattering states were described using complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS), which combines complex scaling with the Lippmann-Schwinger formalism to treat three-body continuum states. The electromagnetic dipole transition matrix elements for linearly polarized \(\gamma\)-rays were calculated for both \(E1\) and \(M1\) transitions, and the anisotropy parameter \(b\) in the azimuthal angle distribution (proportional to \(1 + b \cos(2\phi_n)\)) was evaluated at a neutron polar angle of \(\theta_n = 90^\circ\).

## Key numbers

* The calculated cross section reproduces experimental data below \(E_\gamma = 16\) MeV.
* The \(5/2^-\) resonance is obtained at \(E_x = 2.43\) MeV with a decay width of approximately \(3 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV; the anisotropy parameter for the \(M1\) transition has a maximal value at \(E_\gamma = 2.42\) MeV corresponding to this resonance.
* The \(5/2^+\) resonance is obtained at \(E_x = 3.04\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 0.323\) MeV; the anisotropy parameter for the \(E1\) transition has a maximal value at \(E_\gamma = 3.2\) MeV corresponding to this resonance.
* The \(3/2^+\) resonance at \(E_x = 4.69\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 1.44\) MeV is not clearly identified in the cross section but produces a minimal value in the anisotropy parameter for the \(E1\) transition at \(E_\gamma \sim 4\) MeV.
* The \(3/2^-_2\) resonance at \(E_x = 4.65\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 1.18\) MeV has a minor contribution to the cross section but produces a maximal value in the anisotropy parameter for the \(M1\) transition at \(E_\gamma \sim 5\) MeV.
* Above \(E_\gamma = 6\) MeV, the cross section is dominated by the \(E1\) transition into non-resonant continuum states, and the anisotropy parameter for both \(E1\) and \(M1\) transitions gently changes with positive values.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Agodi (1957), who predicted azimuthal angle distributions proportional to \(1 + b \cos(2\phi)\) for (\(\vec{\gamma}\),\(n\)) reactions but did not include nuclear structure dependence. The authors apply their \(\alpha + \alpha + n\) three-body model, previously used to reproduce the \(^9\)Be(\(\gamma\),\(n\)) cross section quantitatively (Kikuchi 2016), to calculate the anisotropy parameter. The results enable identification of resonances in \(^9\)Be that are not visible in the total cross section, such as the \(3/2^+\) and \(3/2^-_2\) states, and suggest that the anisotropy parameter can distinguish between \(E1\) and \(M1\) transitions in the non-resonant continuum region above 6 MeV.
