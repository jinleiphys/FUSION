# Generalized parton distributions of gluon in proton: a light-front quantization approach

> We solve for the gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) inside the proton, focusing specifically on leading twist chiral-even GPDs. We obtain and employ the light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) of the proton from a light-front quantized Hamiltonian with Quantum Chromodynamics input using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ). Our investigation incorporates the valence Fock sector with three constituent quarks and an additional Fock sector, encompassing three quarks and a dynamical gluon. We examine the GPDs within impact parameter space and evaluate the $x$-dependence of the transverse square radius. We find that the transverse size of the gluon at lower-$x$ is larger than that of the quark, while it exhibits opposite behavior at large-$x$. Using the proton spin sum rule, we also determine the relative contributions of quarks and the gluon to the total angular momentum of the proton.

## Key claim
The authors compute gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) within a light-front quantized Hamiltonian approach that includes both a valence three-quark Fock sector and a three-quark-plus-dynamical-gluon Fock sector, finding that the transverse size of the gluon at lower longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) is larger than that of the quark, while at large \(x\) the opposite behavior holds. These results are obtained at a model scale of \(\mu_0^2 = 0.23 \sim 0.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and depend on the specific truncation parameters and model confining potentials employed.

## Method
The authors solve the light-front mass eigenvalue equation using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) with a Hamiltonian that includes QCD-inspired interactions and model confining potentials in both transverse and longitudinal directions. They truncate the Fock space to include the \(\ket{qqq}\) valence sector and the \(\ket{qqqg}\) sector with one dynamical gluon, using two-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis functions for transverse degrees of freedom and plane-wave basis functions for longitudinal motion. The gluon GPDs \(H_g\), \(E_g\), and \(\widetilde{H}_g\) at zero skewness are computed from the diagonal overlap of light-front wavefunctions, and the impact parameter space distributions are obtained via Fourier transform with respect to transverse momentum transfer.

## Key numbers
- The model parameters used are \(\{m_u, m_d, m_g, \kappa, m_f, g_c\} = \{0.31, 0.25, 0.50, 0.54, 1.80, 2.40\}\) with all values in GeV except \(g_c\), obtained by fitting the proton mass and electromagnetic properties.
- The gluon contribution to the total angular momentum of the proton is \(J^z_g \approx 0.066\) at the scale \(\mu_0^2 = 0.23 \sim 0.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), accounting for approximately 13.2% of the total \(J^z = 0.5\).
- The \(u\) quark contributes \(J^z_u = 0.473\) (94.6%), and the \(d\) quark contributes \(J^z_d = -0.039\) (\(-7.8\%\)).
- The transverse squared radius of the proton model is \(\braket{b_\perp^2} = 0.473\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), about 10% above the experimental value of \(0.43 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) from Dupre (2016).
- The truncation parameters are \(\mathcal{N} = 9\) and \(\mathcal{K} = 16.5\), with harmonic oscillator scale parameter \(b = 0.70\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends previous BLFQ calculations of parton distribution functions and form factors (Xu 2022) to the computation of gluon generalized parton distributions, providing a nonperturbative approach complementary to lattice QCD (Alexandrou 2020) and Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Tandy 2023). The results enable comparison with the light-cone spectator model (Tan 2023) and provide predictions for the transverse structure of the gluon that can be tested at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider. The computed gluon angular momentum contribution of about 13% at the model scale is lower than the 37-38% reported by lattice QCD and DSE approaches at higher scales, reflecting the need for QCD evolution to higher momentum transfers.
