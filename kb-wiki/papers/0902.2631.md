# Mass parameters for relative and neck collective motions in heavy ion fusion reactions

> Mass parameters for the relative and neck motions in fusion reactions of symmetric systems $^{90}$Zr+$^{90}$Zr, $^{110}$Pd+$^{110}$Pd, and $^{138}$Ba+$^{138}$Ba are studied by means of a microscopic transport model. The shape of the nuclear system is determined by an equi-density surface obtained from the density distribution of the system. The relative and neck motions are then studied and the mass parameters for these two motions are deduced. The mass parameter for the relative motion is around the reduced mass when the reaction partners are at the separated configuration and increases with decrease of the distance between two reaction partners after the touching configuration. The mass parameter for the neck motion first decreases slightly up to the touching configuration and then increases with the neck width, and its magnitude is from less than tenth to several times more than the total mass of the system. The mass parameters obtained from the microscopic transport model are larger than the ones obtained from the hydrodynamic model and smaller than those obtained from the linear response function theory. The mass parameters for both motions depend on the reaction systems, but the one for the relative motion depends on the incompressibility of the EoS more obviously than that for neck motion.

# Mass parameters for relative and neck collective motions in heavy ion fusion reactions

## Key claim

The mass parameters for relative and neck motions in heavy ion fusion reactions, extracted from the microscopic Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model, are larger than those from the hydrodynamic model and smaller than those from the linear response function theory (LRFT), with the relative motion mass parameter depending more obviously on the incompressibility of the equation of state than the neck motion mass parameter.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model to simulate head-on collisions of symmetric systems at incident energies of 1.1 times the Bass barrier height (and 1.0 and 1.5 times for one system). The nuclear shape was defined by the equi-density surface at half normal density (ρ = 0.5ρ₀), and the distance between centers of mass R and neck width Δ were extracted from the time-dependent density distribution. Mass parameters were deduced from the ratio of conjugate momentum to velocity for relative motion (M_RR = P_R / Ṙ) and from the kinetic energy relation for neck motion (M_ΔΔ = 2T_ΔΔ / Δ̇²), with the crossing term neglected based on the weak temporal correlation between Ṙ and Δ̇.

## Key numbers

- For R > 1.8R₀, the mass parameter for relative motion M_RR approaches 0.25 of the total system mass (i.e., the reduced mass); for R < 1.8R₀, M_RR increases with decreasing distance.
- The mass parameter for neck motion M_ΔΔ ranges from less than one-tenth to several times the total mass of the system (A₁ + A₂).
- For ¹³⁸Ba+¹³⁸Ba at E_cm = 350 MeV, the neck width Δ increases from about 0.5 fm to about 7 fm as R/R₀ decreases from 1.9 to 1.6.
- The incompressibility K_∞ for the two force parameter sets used is 144 MeV (IQ1) and 175 MeV (IQ2), with ρ₀ = 0.165 fm⁻³ for both.
- For ¹¹⁰Pd+¹¹⁰Pd at E = 1.5V_B, M_RR is roughly equal to the reduced mass when R/R₀ ≥ 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the study of mass parameters beyond the static treatments of the hydrodynamic model (Werner-Wheeler method, used by Nix (1967) and She (2002)) and the linear response function theory (LRFT, used by Adamian (1999, 2000)), which give conflicting descriptions of fusion dynamics for heavy systems. The ImQMD approach provides a dynamical, self-consistent calculation where all collective degrees of freedom evolve simultaneously, testing the validity of the static approximations. The results enable more realistic modeling of fusion paths for superheavy nuclei synthesis, where the choice of mass parameter qualitatively changes the predicted fusion mechanism.
